    Dual Pictures of UFO taken by Pedro Avila on July 17th 2004 in Mexico using 2 8mm HandyCams, one without IR filter and one with IR Filter.
    1. Use old (before August 1998) Sony Camcorder with Super NightShot Plus / Nightshot feature: The easiest way to film a UFO is to use a Sony camcorder with Super NightShot Plus or NightShot feature. The camcorder must be issued before August 1998 because after that Sony weaken the IR feature. You can try Sony CCD TRV series (eg Sony CCD TRV65).
    2. Create your own cheap IR Filter : The cheapest way is to use positive film that has not been photographed but has been exposed or processed. So buy a positive film (not negative film) and then directly to go to a photo service to process the film. When asked why you want to process film that has not been used just answer that you want to make an IR filter . Now the processed film is a ready to use IR Filter that you can use on the camcorder you have. Note the IR filter should be used on camcorders that have the Night Shot / Night Vision (0 Lux) feature.
    3. Buy an IR Filter : If you already have a camcorder with Nightshot / Night Vision feature all you need to do is to buy an IR filter.  If you have the money you can buy Hoya IR Filter, Hoya has very good quality IR Filters but they are expensive and most of the time out of stock. If you are low on budget you can buy the cheaper Opteka X-Ray IR Filters . You can use the IR Filter on SONY 0' Lux (NightShot) Camcorders, PANASONIC 0' Lux Camcorders, SHARP 0' Lux Camcorders, and SAMSUNG 0' Lux (Nite Pix) Camcorders. You can use these tables for compatibility reference.
      • How to choose IR Filter Size : You need to know your camcorder lens size. You can use these tables.
      • How to choose IR Filter Blocking Wave Length :
        • 1000nm (for bright, sunny lighting conditions)
        • 900nm (for cloudy, overcast conditions)
        • 830nm (for bright, indoor lighting conditions)
        • 780nm (regular & low indoor lighting conditions)
