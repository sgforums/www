    Sweating is a mechanism to lose heat. It is the most efficient way to lose heat. Depending on your built, the amount of exercise you were doing and the type of your clothing, the amount of heat built up may be different. The amount of perspiration needed to bring down the temperature may be different. Another point is that the other person may be sweating the same amount, but depending on the body surface area and clothing, perspiration may evaporate quicker and so you not notice it. In dry, desert conditions, sweat evaporates very fast and you may not even notice you are sweating. Third, some drugs eg. anticholinergics inhibit sweat production by blocking the muscarinic cholinergic receptors in sweat glands.
