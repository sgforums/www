    The gastrocolic reflex

    The gut is connected through extensive autonomous nervous system whereby plexuses of nerves swerves through the entire gut to help in peristaltic movement of the food.

    The stomach (gaster) is connected to the large bowel (colon) by one of these intertwined nerves and as food enters the stomach, this pressure reflex will stimulate the nerve to activate food movement not only in the colon but also in the small intestines (gastroileac reflex)...
