    Yes, this is known as referred pain. Embryologically, certain organs develop from similar origins and receive the same nerve supply, and their information sent to the brain is represented in a fixed area. The organs once fully develop will be at separate anatomical location though it may be quite nearby.

    A pain initiated by the sole organ confuses the brain and it will interpret it as a stimulus coming from the other organ which is embryologically related hence you complain of pain in an unrelated site...
