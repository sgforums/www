    Sneezing is to clear the uppermost portion of the airways (the nasopharynx) while coughing is for the lower airways. If a particulate is trapped in the lower airways, we don't need to take in more air (as this if it happens, which is impossible, this will dislodge the particulate and makes it travel even deeper into the lowermost portion of the airways), hence coughing, which does not involve a series of inspiration, sometimes, only one is enough to generate enough energy or momentum (the distance from the particulate to the mouth is further) to bring out the irritant. Given this fact, you should be able to think out why sneezing requires a series of inspiratory effort.

    Both are served by different cranial nerves from the brain.
