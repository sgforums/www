    That's right... oxyhemoglobin is bright red in color, and when it loses the oxygen to cells it becomes darker red.

    Because artery is located deeper to veins, it is sometimes hard to withdraw arterial blood if you don't know physiology and a little bit of anatomy. ABG (Arterial Blood Gas) measures and quantifies the amount of saturated oxygen in the blood and let us know whether the patient is hypoxic or not. A brighter red blood on withdrawal will let doctors know that they have punctured the right vessel.
