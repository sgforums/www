    It's the alveoli, the terminal structure in the lower respiratory tract which is involved in gaseous exchange, collapses on expiration and expands on inspiration.

    The structure may collapse permanently if the bronchus proximally is obstructed by a lesion, which could be a tumor (lung cancer), a foreign body (organic or non organic) or a simple tuberculous lesion.
