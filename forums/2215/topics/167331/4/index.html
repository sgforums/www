    Yeah... you are right... neutrophils can be up to 40-70% in the body's normal population and can rise up much higher under bacterial infection. Lymphocytes can be between 10-20% and much elevated in a viral infection.

    Monocytes is about 5% and is raised in chronic infection.

    Eosinophils and basophils are each lesser than 1% under normal condition, but raised if there is parasitic infection or allergy (eosinophils). Basophils elevation signifies more significant pathology such as blood malignancies.
