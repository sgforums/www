    If your bleeding is like your normal period and has occurred at the usual time of your period, then there's nothing to worry about yet. If you get a normal period the following month (with effective barrier contraceptive), then you can set your mind at ease...you are not pregnant.

    However, if your menses the following month is late or abnormal in some way, it will be worthwhile to do a pregnancy test. Postinor has a 85% effective rate, which means that it can fail in a significant number of cases nonetheless. What I would be more concerned about is the possibility of an ectopic pregnancy....vaginal bleeding may not necessarily mean there is no pregnancy.

    So I suggest you watch your next menses and do a pregnancy test to set your mind at ease..
