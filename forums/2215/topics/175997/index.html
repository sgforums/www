    The thing is, just because the people who give health advices here are not professionals doesn't mean that they're quacks. The way I see it, there's nothing wrong in sharing some information which you may have from personal experience or from reading of articles.

    In addition, while not everyone here may be a professional, there are people here with at least a working knowledge of first aid, CPR, and the like. They're not qualified to give a full medical opinion, but they can give suggestions and their personal viewpoints on all things medical.

    If anyone cares to know, I was a combat medic specialist in the SAF. I'm not a doctor, and I'm not qualified to give diagnoses. However, I'm allowed to make my own evaluations out in the field and put forth my opinion on what the casualty is suffering from. For example, if the casualty seems to be suffering from a broken lower leg, I'll note down 'suspected tibia/fibula fracture' for the MO to further evaluate.

    Heck, once I got a soldier come up to me to say that he had problems urinating. After looking through the symptoms, I suspected it was more than just an ordinary urinary tract infection and suspected gonorrhoea instead. I had him sent to the medical center, who sent him on a clinic specialising in STDs (the one at Kelantan Road). My suspicions proved correct, and it really was gonorrhoea.

    What I'm trying to say is that in this place, people may give medical opinions. Just because I give my opinion doesn't make me a quack. If anything, it gives the patient an idea of what he's suffering from and what he should do.

    That said, this forum should just serve as a means to educate and inform people on health matters. Any postings here should never be taken in lieu of true medical evaluation. While I felt that the guy was indeed suffering from gonorrhoea, I still had him evacuated to the nearest medical center anyway. Bottom line... if you're ill, you should still go to your doctor for a full evaluation.

    I hope the moderators can come up with disclaimers or something.

    Hmmm.

    OK, I'm not going to flame here, I'm just going to put my points across.

    Re: Aunt Agony and SAF Forums. To some extent, as fymk has said, Aunt Agony is exactly that - it's a whinge spot in the same vein as the agony aunt columns that originated in newspapers, not a counselling service. Keep in mind that Miss Manners falls under the agony aunt category, and that can hardly be considered as counselling. As for the SAF Forums, is anybody going to conduct an airborne drop based on what they learn from the forum? I think not.

    The difference here is the tendency for people to indiscriminately act upon medical advice regardless of the source or context. All of us on an individual level will of course say, "I'm not that stupid", but the article that fymk posted a link to shows that we cannot count on everybody to be so wise.

    As such, we have an obligation to treat this sort of thing responsibly, beginning with disclaimers.

    For what it's worth, we're all subject to the same rules. I've had people from Agony Aunt PM me and ask me serious questions, and I always act according to my professional obligation to let them first know the limits of what I can tell them. More often than not, I refer them on to people I know in the clinical or counselling psychology fields - I'm an organisational psychologist and I'm not going to even pretend to be able to do my clinical or counselling peers' job.

    It's very simple: Primum Non Nocere - first, do no harm. If there's any chance that someone might act upon information that may not be correct, action must be taken to prevent this from happening, be it the use of disclaimers, referring people to qualified professionals or witholding information. It's the only responsible thing to do.
