    I have been away for just two weeks for CNY and another flame war started?

    Haven't had time to read all the comments (no point reading those in a flame war...bad for the heart and mind anyway). Many of the negative comments I read are not worth repeating...lots of angst, anger and even arrogance....but I do not want to re-start flame wars. Here are my views:

    As I previously PM to alexkusu when the idea of such a forum came up, I suggested a disclaimer of sorts, but not for the reason some of you might think. To those who talked about disclaimers on websites, you may not realize that often they are not worth the paper they are printed on. Having a disclaimer does NOT protect you from litigation if someone suffers harm from your advice.

    In a negligence suit, you have to prove a duty of care, dereliction of duty, damage and causation. If you receive medical advice from a website of the Mayo Clinic about your specific condition from one of its employees, you might reasonably suspect that a doctor-patient relationship exists. A disclaimer is useful in this context to inform the viewer that the website does not acknowledge such a relationship. However, it does not prevent you from arguing that having Dr ABC from Mayo Clinic telling you your condition requires a particular treatment constitutes a doctor-patient relationship, regardless of what the disclaimer says.....and you may indeed be right.

    In a forum such as this, when the participants are anonymous and even as some critics admitted, they do not know if the moderators are health professionals, how is one going to argue that a doctor-patient relationship exist in the first place? I have said that I am a medical doctor but how is anyone to know?

    As I have said before during the earlier flame war involving renorenal, a forum such as this is purely for information and entertainment. Nobody should or can successfully claim that a doctor-patient relationship exists simply because XYZ advises a particular course of action in a thread. Incidentally, one would have to sue Sgforums.com rather than some phantom entity who can dispense advice under a multitude of clones. Interestingly, I have not seen any disclaimers by Sgforums.com; and I would certainly put one up if I were Jason or Fireice. Not so much for fear of negligence suits (one would be laughed out of our courts) but the very real possibility of defamation (which is no laughing matter in Singapore).

    Back to the reason I suggested a disclaimer so long ago...I thought it was a good idea just in case there are naive persons as suggested by certain critics, who might think that they can substitute the need to see a doctor by getting free consultation through cyberspace. To the cheapskates who might be tempted to save some money by not seeing a GP, the disclaimer would warn them that they would not have the full legal protection of a doctor-patient relationship by relying on information on the Web. In other words, the disclaimer is not placed to avoid being sued, it is to tell the patient that chances are that you will not be able to seek legal redress without a proper consultation in a doctor's office.

    Incidentally, why would doctors and lawyers give free consultations when we should be earning good money for them? I am not allowed to advertise or I would leave my practice address here

    This is the form of a disclaimer I had in mind. If the other moderators are in favour, we can put it up. It has no legal effect but serves to inform, which is after all, the purpose of this forum as well:

    "The information and materials on this forum are provided by the moderators and other users for general informational purposes only and not for the purpose of providing medical advice or giving a medical opinion on any specific facts or circumstances. Your use of this forum does not create a doctor-patient relationship between you and the moderators or any other users."
