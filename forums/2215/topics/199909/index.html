    Some people have more teeth than usual, and some people have fewer teeth.

    These are cases of hyperdontia and hypodontia respectively. However, these are extremely rare occurences.

    Most of the time, these extra teeth do not grow out from the gums hence the possibility of further dental problems or even causing the delay of other nearby teeth. Such extra teeth are most commonly found near the incisors.

    And ermm, they're more common in Asians.

    The milk/primary teeth hold the right spaces for the secondary/permanent teeth. If a child loses a baby tooth prematurely, nearby teeth may tip or move into that empty space, causing crookedness.
    Hence, it is extremely important to take care of one's baby teeth, even though one day they will eventually fall out!
