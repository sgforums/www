    Well, it can have more reasons.
    You either don't brush your teeth regulary (atleast twice a day) or you use a wrong tooth brush, usually a hard one. Hard tooth brush hurts the gums and there you have the problems. Use a soft tooth brush, it's not true that a hard tooth brush cleans the teeth better then a soft one.

    Other reasons - using one tooth brush for a long time. The tooth brush is the home for the bacteria from your mouth, from the air and if in the bathroom is also the toilet, the fecal bacteria are also there because when you flush the toilet, the micro drops from it are sprayed about 180 cm around the toilet. Buy a new tooth brush atleast every 3 months and when you are sick, buy a new one after you recover.
    Keep your tooth brush in a dry place, not on the basin.

    Brushing the wrong way - left right movements are wrong. Brush with circular and up and down movements.

    Use an antibacterial mouth wash atleast twice a day.

    You can develope a parodontotis, I would suggest you visit a dentist to check if it is already so far.
