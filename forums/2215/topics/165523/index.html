    Pain and stiffness of the neck may result if the lump is big enough or uneven enough to have encroached onto one of your cervical nerve sitting and jutting out from the upper neck region (C2 and C3). This will increasingly be evident if you also experience symptoms of numbness and pain below your ear level, the sides of your neck and perhaps the collar bony region. However, the symptoms may not be present altogether, but if you feel worried about it, may I suggest you to a radiologist, get a skull and cervical X ray done to check for any possibilities of nerve compression or irregularities of the cervical vertebraes.

    The skull X ray will rule out any possibility of lumps that are depressed into the skull, which may coompress on the meningis and causes pain without any external pressure (if you have this problem that is).

    Most patients are curious, and this is often encouraged as they will be more aware of their problems, but not over-aware, hence seeking treatment in future at an earlier stage which enhances the medical or surgical intervention which may be fatal if detected late.

    When doctors say there is no problem, it means that it is not going to affect any of your quality of life (QOL) or your activity of daily living (ADL) in anyway.
