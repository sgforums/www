    The causes are numerous and you may need to see your doctor for a proper diagnosis.

    The possibilities (not exhaustive) include:
    1. Physiological: related to male hormones which gives the typical male balding pattern.
    2. Fungal infection: very common and may itch as well. A scrape for fungus will be useful.
    3. Autoimmune: alopecia areata has a typical, sharply demarcated scarring hair loss pattern
    4. Lichen planus: an inflammatory cause of hair loss. A biopsy will demonstrate it
    5. Systemic disease eg. SLE (systemic lupus eruthematosis): more common in women6
    6. Trauma: mainly in women with long hair and vigorous combing. a related psychological cause is trichotillomania (an urge to pull hair out)
    7. Others: including nutritional deficiency, drug traetment eg. chemotherapy, toxins and idiopathic (we don't know why)
