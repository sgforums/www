    1 lumps on the underside of the penis :

    As renorenal said - Human Papilloma Virus, and also Molluscum contagiosum,
    which is a benign viral infection of the skin causing small skin-colored bumps. It's caused by a pox virus.

    2 sores on the mouth :

    Caused by the Herpes Simplex Virus (HSV-1) which causes mouth and lip rash, blisters. There is also HSV-2 which causes itching rash on the genitals, it's called the genital herpes. Either strain of this virus can cause either rash, though 75% of genital herpes is caused by HSV-2.

    3 anal sex will lead to what std?

    HIV/AIDS,

    Chlamydia (bacterium Chlamydia trachomatis which causes various sexually transmitted diseases. Chlamydia is currently the most common STD),

    Syphilis,

    Hepatitis B and C (you can be vaccinated against Hepatitis A and B),

    Gonorrhea (caused by the bacterium Neisseria gonorrhoeae) which may cause abdominal pain, vomiting, fever, sore throat (pharyngitis), arthritis, skin lesions, and discharge from the eyes, anal and rectal itching, anal discharge, painful bowel movements, and painful anal sex may occur. Gonorrhea can affect the prostate and testicles in men. In women, it can cause pelvic inflammatory disease.

    Chancroid, which is a highly infectious bacterial disease caused by Haemophilus ducreyi that affects the skin and mucous membranes of the penis, vulva, urethra, and anus. It produces painful irregularly shaped ulcers.

    Sure, I can give detailed comments, but though I have "attacked" mom's books to search for the particular harmful strains, haven't found them. So it stays just by the comment.

    There are more than 30 different strains of HPV, some of them referred to as high risk types, can lead to cancer of a woman's cervix or penile cancer. Other types that are not likely to cause this infection are designated as low risk.

    All warts are caused by a strain of HPV. Some types of HPV cause genital warts whereas other types cause common skin warts. Genital warts can develop on the cervix, penis, or in the vaginal or rectal area. They look similar to warts that might appear on the hands, feet, or other parts of the body. (Warts on the
    hands and feet are not transmitted to the genitals.)
    Genital warts, like warts on other parts of the body, are dry and painless, firm and rough in texture, and usually grayish or skin color.

    High risk strains of the virus are linked to cervical changes in women and could possibly lead to cervical cancer. High risk HPV strains are those that are least likely to cause genital warts. Two of these same high risk strains are those that can cause penile cancer in men or anal cancer in those individuals who engage in anal intercourse.

    So you still can have HPV though you have no warts. That's even worse becuse this strain of HPV can cause cancer.
