    Originally posted by ndmmxiaomayi:
    DHF very serious liao, still got Dengue Shock Syndrome? What is it by the way?
    DSS is a complication of DHF... when you lose excessive blood due to hemorrhage, you will lose your plasma volume (or the water content to be frank in the body). The blood pressure and pulse rate will try to compensate immediately by vasoconstriction (constriction of blood vessel) and increment of pulse rate (tachycardia) to maintain perfusion of blood to vital organs such as the brain, heart, lungs, kidneys and adrenals to name a few.

    Once the blood escapes from its blood vessels to its extravascular (outside the blood vessels) space, the patient will be subjected to hypovolemic (low in volume) shock, which may render the patient to coma and then followed by death shortly...
