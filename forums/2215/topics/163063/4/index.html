    Originally posted by Master -_-:
    What does it mean by " Kidneys activating the vitamin D "? What is the significance of the renal function ?
    Hi Master, glad to hear from you again

    Right, vitamin D can be taken orally or it can be activated under exposure to the sun (sunbathing).

    Vitamin D or better known as calciferol starts from its root of synthesis in the skin. The resulting metabolite known as cholecalciferol will make it's way to the liver to get conjugated (become water soluble). The liver will add a hydroxyl group to its 25th position making it a 25-hydroxycholecalciferol (this is basically chemistry).

    Once this happen, the 25-HCC will make its way to the kidneys, and the kidneys will decide to either activate it by adding another hydroxy group to its first position, making it 1,25 - dihydroxycholecalciferol (1,25-DHCC) or making it inactive by adding it to the 24th position making it 24,25-DHCC.

    The activated vitamin D will then continue on with it's function after it leaves the kidneys. One of it being aiding calcium absorption from the gut...

    Once the kidney goes conked out, the ability to activate 25-HCC will not be able to work properly or might fail altogether. That is the sole reason of taking Rocaltrol (1,25-DHCC synthetic) or 1-alpha (1-HCC) for patients who have kidney failure...
