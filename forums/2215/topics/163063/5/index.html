    Diabetes --> There are two types... Type 1 and Type 2

    Type 1 - causes are more towards autoimmunity, infection and genetic

    Type 2 - causes are more towards hereditary, environment and obesity

    The more commoner ones in our Eastern countries is Type 2 which the onset starts after 40 years of age (there may be variants)...

    With the causes listed, the modes of prevention is based on avoiding the causes...

    It has been proven through many research papers that in potential Type 2 diabetics, prior to them developing symptoms, they carry a potential gene for insulin resistance (insulin helps in absorbing glucose into muscles or fat cells, with resistance, glucose is not absorbed and left in the circulation for a long time till it gets excreted in the urine, normally, the urine should NOT have any glucose in it giving rise to a urine dipstick test for glycosuria)...

    If you have a strong family history of diabetes, it is vital that you start caring for your body now by taking less sugary foods or carbohydrates, start taking up exercises - the best being 3 sessions per week with 1 session lasting for 30-45 minutes, the best is still running or jogging). Reduce your weight at all cost if you are overweight or obese.

    Do not start smoking or drink alcohol as these are compounding factors. Remember that diabetes coexist with a lot of other diseases such as high blood pressure, kidney failure, heart attack, stroke and peripheral vascular disorders.

    Get your blood checked once every 2 years or 3 years for evidence of glucose if you do not have any evidence of diabetes. If you do, you may want to get it checked more regularly.

    I will not be talking on Type 1, as it is not as common here, but suffice to say that diabetes in Type 1 is part of a group of autoimmune disorders. The person afflicted with these genes may present otherwise with thyroid problems and other endocrinal problems (adrenal glands).
