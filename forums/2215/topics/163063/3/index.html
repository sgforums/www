    The first question involves a lot of controversy, but I believe that with the recent compilation of types of arthritis, none of it seemed to fit in the showering thing...

    Rheumatoid Arthrtitis - known to have been linked to the presence of the gene HLA DR 4 and is more commoner in females by 3 times to 1. It commonly affect those who are in their 5th decades of their life.

    Osteoarthritis - a joint failure, degenerative and is due more to wear and tear over the time with age. Vigorous exercise, manual labor, occupational demands, sports demands, trauma, accidents and other joint disorders may lead to this condition.

    Gouty arthritis - more of a failure in excretion of uric acid by the kidneys which leads it to accumulate or due to excessive formation of uric acid (through the breakdown pathways of nucleic acid - RNA and DNA) or through excessive intake from the diet of offals (inner organs), alcoholic beverages or seafood.

    Seronegative spondarthritides - known to have been linked to a gene called HLA B-27 rather than other causative factors.

    Infectious arthritis / Septic arthritis - main cause is through blood transfusion (that has not been properly screened for infection), dialysis, sharing of needles or rheumatic fever.

    So far, I do not know of any true causes of arthritis that can be linked to taking a cold bath at night, though it may cause arthralgia (joint pain) rather than arthritis (joint inflammation).
