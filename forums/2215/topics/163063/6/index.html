    Famotidine is a Histamine Type 2 Receptor Antagonist (H2RANT) whereby it inhibits one of the potential chemical (histamine) in the body that has the ability to stimulate gastric acid production.

    They are widely used for problems like gastritis and also for the treatment of peptic ulcer disease.

    Other medications of the similar group as famotidine includes cimetidine (which can cause enlargement of the male b.reast - gynaecomastia) and ranitidine.

    Stomach ulcers are caused by an imbalance between the protective (defensive) mechanism in the gastric lining and offending agents.

    The typical offending agent that may causes harm to the gastric lining is the acidic environment in the stomach. Under normal conditions, this offense is countered by the bicarbonate-mucus barrier (an unstirred layer) which lines the gastric epithelium. This barrier prevents any further damage that might have been caused to the vital and functional cells in the stomach.

    The remaining defensive mechanism is the availability of the tight junctions in the cells that prevent both the acid and H. pylori from entering into the cell and causing a breach in the lining (ulcer formation). The blood vessels in the stomach also channels protective prostaglandins into the gastric epithelium helping in regeneration of damaged cells.

    Some other offending agents include chronic NSAIDs (aspirin, ibuprofen, ketoprofen and diclofenac) intake, alcohol, duodenal reflux of bile juice, helicobacter pylori infections, smoking and steroidal medications.

    The sole method of relieving an ulcer is to first diagnose it through an endoscope (OGDS - oesophagogastroduodenoscopy) inserted from the mouth and into the esophagus to view it in the stomach. Once diagnosed, a sample of the ulcer tissue is taken to culture for the presence of H. pylori (the sole and common infective cause for peptic ulcer disease).

    Treatment relies on 2 antibiotics and 1 protective agent for the ulcer. The common practice is to treat with Flagyl (metronidazole) + Amoxycillin + Omeprazole or any of the H2RANTs medications mentioned in the previous post.
