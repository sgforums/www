    Originally posted by alexkusu:
    this one sounds terrifying... especially when the liver is concerned. What are the resulting consequences and the possible causes for this? is it genetics?
    there are many causes so to speak, the commonest being viral hepatitis infection where the liver cells (the factory that produces such enzymes) are destroyed by these bugs, hence the accumulation of the bilirubin compound can bring changes to the sclera (eye white) color...

    the others include thalassaemic patients or sickle cell disease where blood cells get destroyed prematurely at a rapid rate, and the enzymes cannot cope with the workload, hence jaundiced...

    there is also a possibility if the bile ducts are obstructed (the site of excrement for the bile juice), can be by tumor, gall stones or by some certain autoimmune disorders...

    One can classify the causes of jaundice into prehepatic - hepatic - posthepatic... hepatic meaning liver, and the pre and post notes the location of the problem..
