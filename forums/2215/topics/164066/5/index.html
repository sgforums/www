    Originally posted by renorenal:
    I think nowadays, especially in Malaysia (I cannot speak for Singapore), the govt is very strict with vaccination and we would have yearly visits from the community health center's nurses in the schools.

    We would be granted checks for vision, hearing, heart murmur screening and testicular descent (if detected especially at 6-7 years old, surgery is still feasible, but not at a later age, the reason that surgery is not done early is that the testes may come down on its own, but at a later age, no) before giving vaccination.
    The School Health Services in Singapore also provide health checkups for children in primary and secondary schools. For polytechnics, I don't think they provide. For JC, I am not sure because I have never been to a JC.

    The common ones are height/weight and eyes. Only children at ages 6-7 and 11-12 are offered hearing checks. Similarly, only children at ages 6-7 and 11-12 are offered vaccination. The only exception is for 15 years olds. Those who have not had Hepatitis B vaccination, family who are carriers of Hepatitis B and those who have taken the vaccination at a young age and it has worn off will be given the vaccination.

    Depending on the child's medical history, he/she may have to go for additional checkups at Outram Park or by the doctors who come to their school. Usually heart murmur screening is not done unless the child has a history of it. It is done by doctors from School Health Services. If serious conditions are detected, these kids are usually send to the respective specialists. For example, diabetes.
