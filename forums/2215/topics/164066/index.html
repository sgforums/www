    Primarily what happens is that you need a blood vessel to get clogged up before a heart attack can actually happen...

    This blood vessel in the heart is called the coronary circulations... there are two main branches that comes out from the heart outlet (the aorta)...

    Hypertension, Diabetes and High cholesterol levels are well-known to destroy the blood vessels. I shall list this here:-

    Hypertension - the jet pressure of the blood flow continuously over time tears the fragile vessel wall leading to trauma and slow repair may cause clot formation (which is called thrombus)... but since hypertension is chronic, the continuous trauma may still happen onto the clot and burst the clot even further leading to another clot formation on top of the old clot... this is called superimposition of clots...

    So slowly but surely, the whole artery will get clogged... symptoms appear once the diameter is clogged up more than 70%

    Diabetes and high cholesterol levels - occur together as the body metabolism is screwed up... what happens here is that the cholesterol levels are so high that it gets accumulated within small arteries and damage the vessel wall... there is a LONG pathological process here which I will leave to oxford mushroom...

    Smoking - is well known to cause sticky platelets which their function is to clot up or plug broken vessel wall... sticky platelets will cause them to occur together much closer and have a raised tendency to form thrombus excessively if there is a damage of the vessel wall...

    NOTE that most patients present with all diabetes and hypertension together... i hardly see any that only have one factor coz diabetes can also lead to hypertension... some ppl who have hypertension alone may develope kidney infarcts, heart attack and strokes as well.. silently coz hypertension is silent... with no symptom presentation...
