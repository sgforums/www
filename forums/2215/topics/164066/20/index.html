    Shingles normally occur in the very young and the very old for the immunity in the young is not fully developed to fight off infections yet and on the other hand, the immunity for the very old has already declined.

    Boosting up immunity ought to be done prior to an infection, not during an infection as it will be rather pointless. Once into the course of disease itself, you should grant them food in the fluid form, porridge, oats and milk if possible to reduce the pain on chewing and biting.

    Yes, in shingles, the pain can be so severe as it affects the nerves in the head, neck and the body itself, it courses through the nerve root from the spine in the body and follow it right to the midline in the front before terminating. By right, it should not cross the center for the nerve has already terminated prior to reaching the midline. Though there may be some forms of complex shingles which may give rise to such condition.

    Headaches and increased sensitivity is rather normal as the involvement is generalized. Be sure to note that the blisters do not start popping out on the eyebrows and the nose itself, for these are early signs which will progress to the involvement of the eyes that may lead to blindness... Such condition warrants aggressive treatment with acyclovir.

    You may want to read about Herpes Zoster Ophthalmicus (search on the net), this is one of the dangerous complications and warrants and emergency treatment. Otherwise, the course of the disease may take from 4-6 weeks depending on the individual to recover from the illness.

    Is your SO taking any antiviral medications for his illness or just the pain killers? It has been shown that such antivirals such as acyclovir terminates the proliferation of such viruses by preventing its reproduction hence leading to a shorter course of illness. It may also help in relieving pain and is a welcome sight for the patient.
