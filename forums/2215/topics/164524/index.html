    Notifiable Disease #1

    Silent Killer = Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)

    This is perhaps one of the most fearful disease to acquire, even though most people feel they are fine with it, since it manifest typically no symptoms at all, even though, there may be a slight headache, which people attribute it to stresses from daily work and family life.

    They are only aware of their own problems when they go for an unrelated health check-up which the doctor may pick it up if he were to do a BP check. However, the patient will be required to come for 2 more times for a BP check to confirm that this is a true hypertension and not just due to a sudden change because of not sleeping enough, just ate or having a hard day.

    Most remain undetected and becomes uncontrolled till it manifest above 210/120 mmHg where malignant hypertension steps. The blood pressure will continuously peak and tears through small blood vessels in the brain leading to stroke, the heart leading to heart attack and the kidneys, leading to acute shutdown of both of it.

    Get your BP checked yearly if possible if you are at risk or health-conscious. A family history of hypertension creates a favourable atmosphere for the next one in the family to get it as well...

    Notifiable Disease #3

    Chronic Bronchitis

    Smoke - huff - puff - huff... like a chimney all the way and you will notice that if you are saved from lung cancer, you are most likely to get chronic bronchitis when you turn older.

    Such is the norm of the content of cigarretes which burns through your airway's defensive mechanism, both in the trachea and bronchus and in the bronchials and alveolus. Mucus gets plugged up cumulatively over time, and inflammatory cells step in in an attempt to repair the injured cells by the cigarette.

    The whole place burns up and gets even worse with the repair as the fragile site of the respiratory center is not meant for excessive damage and handling.

    Chronic bronchitis is somewhat like asthma, but the only thing that differentiates them is that asthma is reversible, while chronic bronchitis is not. This can lead to respiratory (lung) failure over time, and if very severe, it may even stop your heart from functioning.

    Other complications include blood cells going up (polycythaemic) making it extremely viscous in the blood vessels and recurrent lung infection (lost defensive mechanism). Your heart will be enlarged and goes into failure (pumping harder and because of fluid accumulation).

    One will have trouble sleeping at night, and typically finds it hard to live on.

    Notifiable Disease #4

    Ugh! Headaches - Migraine

    Lasting from 4-72 hours per episode and 1-2 attacks in a month, such a throbbing headache, typically described by patient as having pain only at one particular side of the skull reflects it's nature of attack. One would often get the attacks in an enormous amount of stress, cigarette smoking, alcohol, lack of sleep, taking foods that trigger it, hormonal changes during menstruation or puberty, certain medication, bright light or loud noises.

    It is uncertain as for the cause but is typically classified into two types, classic migraine and common migraine, in which the sole differentiation is that those with classic migraine experience an aura before going into an attack...

    The aura can be visual or sensory, visual being able to see zig-zag lines, dotty vision and flashes of lights, and sensory being feeling numbness over the site of pre-emptive pain.

    When you know you are going to suffer an attack soon, try to calm down yourself, get away from your work-desk or whatever that has been giving you the trigger factor. Sleep in a silent and dark room, most people find this the best way to get off it. There is typically no cure for migraine, but there are medications to relieve your symptoms.

    Otitis Externa

    It's best to leave your ear alone and not dig it with cotton buds as this will only cause trauma to the ear drum.     Notifiable Disease #7

    Haemorrhoids (Piles)

    This is a rather common problem especially nowadays. Increased body weight and prolonged straining in the toilet are two of the main causative factors that exacerbate this condition.

    The vessels (veins) in the anal mucosa are collectively clumped underneath and under normal condition appear as soft bulges which provides the cushioning effect for the stools to be passed out. Characteristically, there are three of them located at 3, 7 and 11 o clock positions when the person is lying supine.

    Under great pressure and compression from straining of stools (from constipation), the veins will lose its flexibility and the elastic muscles that holds them in location will also give way. The veins will balloon outwards from the normal soft bulge and pushes it out further more making the small bulge into a big unsupported lump.

    Depending on severity, the lump will be forced to escape from the anus after pooping and fresh red blood will be passed out, often noticed by the person through wiping with toilet tissue or in the toilet bowl. If it is minor, the lump may return to its normal location by itself, but if it is major, it may not.

    If you are suffering from this condition, it's best to seek a GP's help to acquire certain cream and medication that aids in the vessel's integrity and prevent bacterial infection of the perianal area. Start to switch to soft diet, reduce intake of red meat or anything hard. Drink more fluids, take more fiber and don't retain poop for a long time as this will only exacerbate the condition. Exercise often to reduce weight.
