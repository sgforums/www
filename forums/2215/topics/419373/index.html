TCM practitioners are not specialists: Khaw

  • Clivebenss
    2 Jan 11, 20:11

    By Shaffiq Alkhatib | Posted: 02 January 2011 1734 hrs

    SINGAPORE: Health Minister Khaw Boon Wan says it is wrong for Traditional Chinese Medicine or TCM practitioners to claim they are specialists.

    That's because Singapore currently doesn't have a specialist register for such practitioners.

    Mr Khaw was commenting on recent letters issued by the TCM Practitioners Board to errant TCM practitioners who claim to be specialists.

    The TCM Practitioners Board was set up under the Health Ministry in 2001 to register and regulate the sector.

    Mr Khaw said the authorities will tighten and monitor the situation.

    "The TCM Board makes its position quite clear. We don't have a specialist register yet, so how can you call yourself a specialist? If everybody starts calling themselves specialists, that may be misleading to the consumers. That, I think will be unethical. So, anybody who claims themselves to be specialists obviously is misleading the consumers," he said on the sidelines of a New Year celebration at Sembawang on Sunday.

    Mr Khaw also said it will take time before the Board can look at registering specialists.

    "Even for Western-trained doctors.....it took us many years before we do the specialist register. I suppose if that is also the way for Chinese medicine to develop, we can consider that. But I don't see it as something in the short or immediate term," he said.

    Besides registration, the Board also regulates the professional conduct of TCM practitioners.

    - CNA
