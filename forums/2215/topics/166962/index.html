    Precisely...

    The more concentrated the hemoglobin (Hb) is the higher the chances of the patient developing cyanosis. Whereas the lesser the Hb (anaemia), the more unlikely the chance of developing cyanosis.

    Cyanosis is formed through an accumulation of the blue pigmented body - carbaminohemoglobin if the patient is deprived of oxygen for a long period of time. If there is more hemoglobin (polycythaemia) in the patient, more carbon dioxide packs into them easily, hence increasing the chances of cyanosis more.
