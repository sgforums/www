    The noise you hear sounds like snapping tendons. Snapping tendons around the hip is more common and more likely benign. Snapping tendons around the knee is unusual. You might want to read this report of a patient with subluxating biceps femoris and tendon snapping around the knee. (Am J Sports Med. 2001 Jan-Feb;29(1):93-5.) You might also want to see an orthopaedic surgeon or sports medicine specialist with an interest in knee injuries.

    Sounds like you've got osteoarthritis of the knee.

    You might need an arthroscopic joint debridement to 'clean' your knee joint.. it involves the insertion of a scope into your knee joint and removing all the loose bodies and protruding bits of bone from the bony ends, that should help relieve your symptoms. A knee realignment surgery might help too.

    Why don't you consult an orthopaedic surgeon for an opinion? These are bread-and-butter cases to them.
