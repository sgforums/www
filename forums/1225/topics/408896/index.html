    Dating events now too costly

    WITH the merger of the Social Development Unit (SDU) and Social Development Service (SDS) into the Social Development Network (SDN) and the privatisation of events to accredited agencies, I am wondering if it is an initiative to encourage singles to get hitched or to deter them?

    As a single, I was daunted by the high fees charged by the accredited agencies as compared to the reasonable prices before.

    It seems to me that the regulation of prices and service levels need to be relooked or tightened.

    An event can easily cost $40, be it a movie date, dinner or high tea. Not many can afford to spend that much, especially those with low income looking for a soul mate. Why aren't prices subsidised to boost the marriage rate?
