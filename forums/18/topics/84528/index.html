    Gory cigarette packs aimed at curbing smoking in Singapore (DPA)
    Source: Khaleej Times (ae), 2004-06-15

    Intro:
    Every time smokers in Singapore reach for a cigarette, they will see graphic colour images of a cancerous lung, a dying baby, a brain oozing blood or a patient on his death bed in Singapore's latest drive to cut smoking, a newspaper said on Tuesday

    Starting in August, the six images to be printed on cigarette packages will also include diseased gums and a family suffering secondhand smoke in silence, The Straits Times reported.

    Tobacco companies were given a year to implement the new rules after they were passed in parliament in August.

    Below the images will be written health warnings such as "Smoking can cause a slow, painful death" and "Smoking harms your family."
