    It is not really that people are not interested in the topic.

    How do you distinguish between an erotic movie with real sex and a pornographic movie with a storyline? There aren't many movie-makers who would want to cross the line and blur the distinction between erotica and pornography. Any explicit depiction of sexual intercourse is most likely to garner an X rating in the U.S. and an automatic ban in many other countries. Not only would it make the movie difficult to market -- most, if not all, X-rated movies go straight to video -- but it'd also not look good on a serious director's track record. That probably explains why there are so few "serious" movies out there which contain such explicit scenes.

    And why would audience want to see an erotic movie with real sex? They are watching it for the storyline, for the effects, for the acting, for general entertainment, not really to satisfy their voyeuristic desires to see people actually copulating. If they wish to be stimulated by explicit sex scenes, there is always adult entertainment for that purpose in the privacy of the home. Why would they want to watch something that is "2-in-1" but usually fails to deliver on both accounts?
