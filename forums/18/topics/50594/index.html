    Read the Straits Times this morning.

    A MASSIVE manhunt was mounted on Monday after a 23-year-old woman was sexually attacked while jogging at one of the Macritchie Reservoir's forest trails.

    More than 100 police officers and dogs were poured into the area, and elite reinforcements kept arriving till late on Monday.

    Advertisement

    She had been found naked, except for her jogging shoes, by fellow runners around 10 am on Monday. She was bleeding from her head, face and lips.

    The hunt was centred at the area outside the Singapore Island Country Club (SICC), which bounded by Upper Thomson Road and Lornie Road.

    The victim was described as being of medium build, had long hair and was fair-complexioned.

    One of the joggers who found her alerted the club's security personnel, and the police were called immediately.

    The woman is now in a stable condition in hospital, according to police.

    The trail where the woman was attacked is off a private road behind the SICC. Usually used by members of the club to reach the golfing greens, the trail leads to the Sime Road end of the Macritchie Reservoir.

    Police have not classified the case, pending results from a medical examination.
