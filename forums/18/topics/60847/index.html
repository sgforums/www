    You should set aside about 30% of these income to invest. Depending on how much risk you wanna take, you can invest in penny stocks(short-term) or blue chips(usually long-term). Also, do not only invest in one sector. You should diverge and invest in a basket of stocks from the different sectors to minimise risk.

    However, if you are not so much of a risk-taker, you might think about investing in bonds or unit trusts, which may take a longer period before you see your gains but is much steadier, meaning even if you lost, it wouldn't be as heavy a penalty to pay as if you invest aggressively.

    There are also various insurance policies that are useful to investors. You might want to check them out. Or you may want to seek a financial advisor for advice for saving, investing, and insurance.
