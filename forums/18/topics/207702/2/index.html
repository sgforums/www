    Originally posted by Dogtor:
    Folks just to update.

    Getting infected with HIV is not a death sentence in that it can be controlled.

    Unfortunately, there are many medications to take and comes up to thousands of dollars a month - equal to dialysis treatments probably.

    When I was in Medical residency in the US in early 2001, there were many patients suffering the complications of HIV infection which is Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndromes, particularly opportunistic infections - PCP, Toxoplasma, etc. all fairly easily controlled with antibiotic prophylaxis.

    With the advent of HAART (Highly Active Anti-Retroviral Therapy), I saw the number of such cases fall rapidly over 3 years.

    The medical center I worked in was a major NIH-funded HIV research center. Most patients will have medications funded by private health insurance, medicare, or medicaid (for very poor or disabled). Also, because it was a major research center, many drugs companies would be performing clinical trials and thus, many medications would be funded through them. Therefore, there was NO-ONE who had HIV infection who could not get treatment!!!

    That aside, EDUCATION on unprotected sex, barrier methods of contraception are needed to halt the spread of disease.

    Also, better funding of HIV treatment is required, with better health insurance coverage or funding sources so that Singaporean patients can also enjoy medical care that equals those enjoyed by citizens of other industrialized societies. Once the drug companies know that there's money to be made (ie, people will pay for treatment); more money gets poured into research, and then more gets funded for studies, especially in Singapore, and ultimately, Singaporeans benefit.
    Singaporeans can have access to low-cost anti-retroviral drugs from Thailand...Action for Aids can facilitate the purchase...As for clinical trials, pharmaceutical companies would rather hold it in Thailand, where there is a far larger number of HIV infected patients.
