    The original concept of MLM is actually a good thing.
    The business model is that it takes away advertising cost(which incidentally makes up a huge part of the cost of product you buy, be it the shampoo you buy from the supermarket, or the watch from some jewellers), and depends on word of mouth by satisfied customers, which is by far a more convincing way of selling. The aim is to take the money intended for the super-rich advertising firms, media tycoons and spread it amongst the consumers. However, perhaps due to the high infrastrutural cost of doing business in SG, the most successful product from MLM tends to be higher end/cost froducts, where there can be enough profit margin to spread to its members. Its simple mathematics, a product which costs $10 each can perhaps earn the seller maybe $3, its simply impossible to entice ppl to wanto sell for the company.
    Similarly as more and more MLM company sprung up, there are a dearth of products available to sell, and ppl start competing another for the same dwindling group of customers. Gradually the only way to differentiate their product is by the business or 'taxation' models, and soon the business model is actually the product they are selling, some MLMs even dun even bother to try and sell the product itself, just its business model. The method of selling has also been altered, to one bordering on deceit, given the notoriety of the original MLM companies, which has often been likened to pyramid selling(Which is different and illegal).
    To summarise, MLM can be likened to Marxism or Communism, with is a wonderful model but due to the practical application had fallen short of the ideals.
