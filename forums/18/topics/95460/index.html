    Originally posted by shade343:

    In simple terms, there are enough evidence or proof that one is guilty of some crimes. This is used in criminal cases. And there is no other way someone else did it, or some other circumstances happened, except this one - that you are the one who is guilty.

    However, the judgement is still arbitrary, plus the court also relies on arguments of the lawyers and many other factors.

    So even if you are innocent, but proven guilty by these 'factors', too bad for you.

    You will have heard of wrong sentencing by courts, where they overturned judgements on innocent people who were imprisoned for long term.

    Thats why some US states are against death sentence, because there will be no return if any error or new evidence to the contrary turn up in the future.
