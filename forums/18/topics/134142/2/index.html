    Let me suggest a combination that worked:
    Communication + being non-judgmental, Compromise + patience

    Communications + non-judgmental
    Communicate how you want to be loved to the other party. Contrary to what you think, your partner may not know actually how you want to be loved. Ask your partner how he/she wants to be loved. You maybe loving your partner the way you want loved, not the way he/she wanted to be loved.

    In your communications, do not judge nor draw conclusions.

    Compromise + patience
    Through your communications, you will discover differences between the ideal situation and the actual situation. Let's call this "tension". The best way to reduce this tension is to lower the ideal, and raise the actual at the same time. This is call compromise.

    Compromise will mean change, and changes sometimes takes place very slowly. Do not rush things and expect things to go your way at the blink of an eye. In fact, some difference will never be entirely resolved. Couples who lasted a life-time found their way to "agreed to disagree".
