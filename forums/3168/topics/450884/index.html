Plans for future of former Iluma mall revealed

  • QX179R
    18 Apr 12, 14:41

    SINGAPORE: CapitaMalls Asia (CMA) has unveiled details about the S$38 million makeover for its mall Bugis+, which was formerly known as Iluma.

    CMA had acquired the mall in April 2011.

    With the revamp, CMA says shoppers can look forward to new fashion tenants, including UNIQLO -- which will open its largest store in Singapore at close to 20,000 square feet -- and a standalone MANGO TOUCH store.

    The mall will also be home popular brands like Japanese fashion label Lowrys Farm and South Korean food chain BonChon Chicken.

    Leading online game service provider Garena will also set up its first gaming experience store in Asia at Bugis+, CMA said.

    Other international brands at Bugis+ include Pull&Bear, Sephora, Bershka, Aeropostale, Celio, New Look, Denizen, Adidas Originals, Quiksilver and Dockers.

    CMA added that over 80 per cent of the mall has been pre-committed.

    Bugis+ is expected to be completed by July 2012, but CMA says 90 per cent of tenants on the first two floors will start operating in June.
