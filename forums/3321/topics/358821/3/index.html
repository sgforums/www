    So irresponsible to transport workers unsafely in wet weather

    http://singaporeseen.stomp.com.sg/singaporeseen/viewContent.jsp?id=79376

    This lorry carrying more than 10 passengers in rainy weather was spotted by STOMPer Sekarang, who says that the company in charge is very irresponsible to transport their workers in such unsafe conditions.

    The lorry was spotted travelling along Dunearn Road in morning rush hour traffic on July 15.

    As it was raining, the workers had to hold onto canvas and cardboard pieces while sitting unsecured at the back of the vehicle.

    In an email, STOMPer Sekarang says:

    “On July 15, at 8.11 am, my family and I were on our way to work along Dunearn Road (flyover beside SCGS), when we saw this Toyota pickup (GBB 5373 G) overloaded with about 12-15 workers.

    “It was raining heavily and the road was slippery so one had to drive during the morning rush hour with care and consideration.

    “The workers, crammed into the pickup, were huddled against one another, holding onto drabs of canvas and flimsy cardboards to shield themselves from the rain.

    “Like many pickups ferrying employees in their back, this pickup subjected its passengers to safety risks. The workers had no proper safety harnesses to secure themselves to the vehicle.

    “This is a clear breach of road safety regulations, especially in light of the weather and unsafe road condition.

    “Employees are entitled to be protected by their employers in organised commutation for work purposes.

    “Under these circumstances, they have the right to travel safely on public roads and at the very least, be properly sheltered from elements of the weather which can compromise their health -- and consequently, affect their performance at work, particularly if it requires physical rigour.”
