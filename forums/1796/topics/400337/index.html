    The Siamese cat gets its title from the old name for Thailand. Thailand used to be known as Siam until 1939, and briefly again from 1945 until 1949. Natives of Thailand used to be called "Siamese", and this breed of cat, believed to be descended from the sacred temple cats of that country, were introduced to Britain and America as "Siamese cats" in the 19th century.

    The Siamese cat was one of the first breeds of cat recognised by cat clubs as being of distinctly Oriental origin.

    Its country of origin, Thailand, has several native breeds of cat, so the Thais themselves do not call the Siamese cat by that name (all their native cats are technically "Siamese" cats!). Instead, the breed that we know as the Siamese is known in Thailand as the Wichien-maat, meaning "moon diamond".

    The word "Siam" itself is said to be derived from a Sanskrit word, "Syama", which means "brown", or "dark".
