    Background
    The Company was incorporated in Singapore on 27 December 1967 to undertake the development of a 4-star hotel and shopping complex known as Apollo Singapore Hotel which opened for business in February 1972. On 30 September 1975, it acquired public status via a public issue of 4 million shares of $1 each. The Company's principal activity is that of hoteliers and investment holders.

    In 1981, the Company diversified into property development through its wholly-owned subsidiary, Apollo Properties Pte Ltd. In 1983, the Company through its subsidiary, Apollo Center Pte Ltd, developed the Apollo Centre, a 7-storey commercial building. In 1994, it acquired the Furama Hotel Singapore. In 1996, it acquired a 4-star hotel in Sydney and renamed it Furama Hotel Central, Sydney. It also has a 13 percent interest in the 872-room 5-star Grand Hyatt Hotel, Taipei.

    In 1999, the Company entered into a management agreement with Accor Group of France. Apollo Hotel was renamed Novotel Apollo Singapore.

    Background
    The Company was incorporated in Singapore under the name of Colonel Sanders Kentucky Fried Chicken Pte Ltd on 22 September 1973. On 1 February 1983, it was converted into a public limited company under the name of Kentucky Fried Chicken (S) Ltd, operating the fast food franchise.

    In 1986, after transferring the fast food business to a subsidiary, it became a holding management and investment company, under its present name. In 1992, it divested the Kentucky Fried Chicken business. In 1993, it wrote off a substantial part of its losses by reducing the par value of its shares from $1 to $0.20 followed by a rights issue. In 1996, it divested its paper trading business. In 1997, it divested its poultry processing business in China. In 2002, the Company wrote off $78,020,000 of its losses through a capital reduction exercise which reduces the par value of the Company's share from $0.20 to $0.01 each.

    The principal activities of the Group now are the franchising of the Shakey's restaurant system in the USA. The Group also engaged in IT development and Telecommunication services. The Company is an investment holding and management services company.

    Background
    Mandarin Oriental Hotel Group is an international hotel investment and management group operating 22 deluxe and first class hotels and resorts worldwide including four under development in New York, Washington DC, Hong Kong and Tokyo. The Group has equity interests in most of its properties and net assets of approximately US$1 billion at 31 December 2003. Mandarin Oriental now operates some 7,500 rooms in 12 countries with 12 hotels in Asia, 6 in The Americas and three in Europe.

    Mandarin Oriental's aim is to be recognised as one of the top global luxury hotel groups, providing exceptional customer satisfaction in each of its hotels. This will be achieved through a strategy of investing in facilities and people, while maximising profitability and long-term shareholder value. The Group regularly receives recognition and awards for outstanding service and quality management. The growth strategy of the Group is to progress towards operating 10,000 rooms in major business centres and key leisure destinations around the world.

    Background
    The Group had its origin in 1935, when Mr Ow Kim Swee, father of the Group Chairman, started his tongkang (wooden lighter) business under the name 'Hai Sun Hup'. As a small tug and barge operator, he provided lighterage facilities for ships anchored in the harbour and at warehouses along the Singapore River.

    In 1970, Hai Sun Hup Co (Pte) Ltd was incorporated to take over the operations of the sole proprietorship. Since then, the Group has expanded from sub-contractor to sub-agent to agent and shipowner.

    The Company was incorporated on 25 July 1977 under the name of CK Ow (Pte) Ltd. It became a public limited company on 10 November 1989 under the name of Hai Sun Hup Group Ltd.

    The principal activities of the Group consist of investment holding, ship owning, chartering, ship management, shipping agency, terminal operations and ancillary marine services, freight forwarding, hotel owning and management, travel agency, trading, property investment and development. The principal activity of the Company is that of an investment holding company.

    The Group owns 10 four-star hotel and five-star hotels in Australia and New Zealand under its own hotel brand name called Stamford Hotels and Resorts.

    The Company changed its name to Stamford Land Corporation Ltd wef 10 September 2001.

    Background
    The Company was incorporated in Singapore on 28 August 1999 as an investment holding company under the name "Thai Village Holdings Pte Ltd" to hold the entire share capital of Thai Village Sharksfin Restaurant Pte Ltd and Thai Village Overseas Ventures Pte Ltd. The Company changed its name to Thai Village Holdings Ltd on 6 April 2000 in connection with the Company's conversion to a public company limited by shares.

    The Group's business started as a partnership in August 1991 when Lee Tong Soon and Kok Nyong Patt started the first Thai Village Sharksfin Restaurant at East Cost Road. This restaurant has a built-in area of 1,800 sq ft and seating capacity for 90 customers.

    The Group has grown into a chain of 3 restaurants located around the island with a processing plant in Tai Seng Avenue in Singapore. The Group has also established regional presence in Indonesia and in China. In Indonesia, the outlets which are located in Jakarta and Surabaya stemmed from franchising and service agreements respectively. Presently, its Chinese branches are located in Shanghai, Kunming, Qingdao and Beijing. A processing plant has also been set up in Dalian. It also has a network of 14 franchises, located in Tianjin, Quanzhou, Wuxi, Fengxian, Nanjing, Ningbo, Guiyang, Changzhou, Wuhan, Xian, Suzhou, Fuzhou, Zhangjiang and Yangzhou.

    In 2002, the Group entered into a Master Franchise Agreement with MK Restaurants, Thailand's largest steamboat chain.
