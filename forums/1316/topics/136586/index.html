    After Mobile Suit Victory Gundam finished airing in Japan in early 1994, Bandai celebrated Gundam's 15th anniversary by trying something new: free the franchise from the continuity of the the Universal Century (and sell more model kits). Thus, the alternate universes were born that are the settings for every TV series following Victory Gundam. Premiering in 1994, G Gundam was the first alternate universe series, and perhaps one of the most controversial. Directed by Giant Robo's Yasuhiro Imagawa, this series takes a vastly different approach to the Gundam saga. Instead of having wars between the colonies and the Earth, a fighting tournament is held to determine the ruler of the colonies. Called the Gundam Fight, it is held once every four years by the United Colonies Federation. Each country has its own representative Gundam (some of which are really ridiculous looking), and the Gundams fight on Earth until only one is left. Set in FC 60, this series chronicles the 13th Gundam Fight and stars Neo Japanese Gundam Fighter Domon Kasshu, who is in the fight as much to win as to fulfill a personal quest. The series has aired on Cartoon Network and is available on DVD. Please note that the names of various mobile suits have been changed for the American release. The reviews here follow the original Japanese names devised by Sunrise.

    Oh my, it has been 11 years ago (July 94') when G-Gundam was first aired in Japan. Funny it seems, that I first got to know of the series not by watching it but instead, I was enamoured by the heroine of the series - Rain Mikamura. She was on the cover of Animage ( April '95). I'm not so much into the Gundam Universe although I'm familiar with the Mobile Suit series (Amuro Ray, Char Aznabble et al). The endless and needless wars, the suffering....

    However, G-gundam quickly changed all that. the first series for tha Alternate Universe - FC Century. It's the story's theme that captivates me the most - of valour and honour, overcoming personal struggles, friendship and devotion and most of all, the power of LOVE. The concept is quiet a novelty, some called it to be "The Street Fighter Of Gundams", with so many different moves, the funny looking gundams, nationalities (Neo America,Neo France,etc). Every week, we get to see how Neo-Japan's fighter, Domon Kasshu (with Rain), hops to one location after the other, looking for a worthy adversary - before heading to Neo-Hong Kong for the 13th Gundam fight Tournament.

    Along the way, he met with his future counterparts - Chibodee Crockett from Neo America, Sai Saichi from Neo China, Argo Gulskii from Neo Russia and George De Sand from Neo France. Ultimately, they form the new Shuffle Alliance after the original members perished in their bid to save them from DG (Devil Gundam)cells. Hence, the insignias on their wrists - Domon (King of Hearts), Chibodee (Queen of Spades), Sai (Club Ace) , George ( Jack diamond) and Argo (Black Joker).
