    General and Technical Data

    Model number: GAT/A-01E2
    Code name: Buster Dagger
    Unit type: mass production long distance artillery mobile suit
    Manufacturer: Earth Alliance
    Operator: Earth Alliance
    First deployment: 5 July C.E. 71
    Accommodation: pilot only, in standard cockpit in torso
    Dimensions: overall height 17.81 meters
    Weight: max gross weight 80.7 metric tons
    Construction: unknown
    Powerplant: ultracompact energy battery, power output rating unknown
    Equipment and design features: sensors, range unknown; laminated armor (improved type of heat-resistant ablative gel DPX-30)
    Fixed armaments: 350mm gun launcher, mounted behind right arm, can be combined with 94mm high-energy rifle, hand-carried in use; 94mm high-energy rifle, mounted behind left arm, can be combined with 350mm gun launcher, hand-carried in use; 2 x 220mm triple missile pod, mounted on shoulders; 2 x ES01 beam saber, stored on backpack; hand-carried in use
    Optional hand armaments: none
    Technical and Historical Notes

    The GAT/A-01E2 Buster Dagger is a simplified, mass production version of the Earth Alliance's prototype GAT-X103 Buster Gundam. The Buster Dagger is originally designed to serve as a Striker pack for the GAT-01A1 Dagger, but it is later redesigned as a full mobile suit. Built on a Dagger frame, the Buster Dagger features the same laminated armor that protects against beam weaponry. The Buster Dagger is equipped with the same combining guns of the original Buster Gundam. Its missile launcher is smaller, but it adds a pair of beam sabers, a feature lacking in the Buster Gundam. The Buster Dagger is rolled out late in the war, and one unit is assigned to ace pilot Rena Imelia.
    Miscellaneous Information

    Pilot: Rena Imelia

    Original mechanical designer: Yoshihiko Machida

    Model number: GAT-X105+P204QX
    Code name: Lightning Strike Gundam
    Unit type: prototype all-purpose multi-mode mobile suit
    Manufacturer: PMP (Propulsion & Machinery Progress)
    Operator(s): Earth Alliance (Atlantic Federation); Orb Union/Three Ships Alliance
    First deployment: C.E. 71
    Accommodation: pilot only, in standard cockpit in torso
    Dimensions: head height 17.72 meters
    Weight: unknown
    Construction: unknown
    Powerplant: ultracompact energy battery, power output rating unknown
    Equipment and design features: sensors, range unknown; Phase Shift (PS) armor; hardpoints for mounting Striker packs; additional battery packs
    Fixed armaments: 2 x "Igelstellung" 75mm multi-barrel CIWS, fire-linked, mounted in head; 2 x "Armor Schneider" combat knives, stored in hip armor, hand-carried in use; Type 70-31 electromagnetic cannon, mounted on forearms, hand-carried in use
    Technical and Historical Notes

    Although the GAT-X105 Strike Gundam is initially designed to use three Striker packs, several more are designed later on. One of these designs is the combined GAT-X105+P204QX Lightning Strike Gundam. The Alliance seeks to increase the Strike Gundam's operating time by 150% through the use of a new Striker pack, but the first design proves to be a failure. Later on, Orb's defense company Morgenroete steps in and designs the P204QX Lightning Striker. The Lightning Striker features an improved power pack design, along with extra cooling systems and battery packs. In addition, the Lightning Strike Gundam is armed with a Type 70-31 electromagnetic cannon, which can be stored as two separate pieces on the Strike Gundam's forearms. The Lightning Striker pack is assembled in late C.E. 71 on the Kusanagi after the fall of the Orb Union. After its construction, the Lightning Striker pack is destroyed in combat during a test flight.
    Miscellaneous Information

    Pilot: Mu La Flaga
    Appearances: SEED-MSV (Gundam SEED Mobile Suit Variations)
    Original mechanical designer(s): Junichi Akutsu (Strike Gundam principal design), Kunio Okawara (Strike Gundam cleanup), Takuya Sasaki (Lightning Striker principal design), Yoshihiko Machida (Lightning Striker cleanup)

    GAT-S02R N Dagger N



    General and Technical Data

    Model number: GAT-S02R
    Code name: N Dagger N
    Unit type: special operations mobile suit
    Manufacturer: Earth Alliance
    Operator: Earth Alliance
    First deployment: unknown
    Accommodation: pilot only, in standard cockpit in torso
    Dimensions: unknown
    Weight: unknown
    Construction: unknown
    Powerplant: ultracompact nuclear fission reactor, power output rating unknown
    Equipment and design features: sensors, range unknown; Mirage Colloid; 17 x multipurpose launcher port
    Fixed armaments: Mk315 "Stiletto" rocket-propelled anti-armor penetrator, stored in hip armor, hand-carried in use; 2 x DES-G07D+ anti-armor sword (set includes long and short swords), mounted on hip armor, hand-carried in use; "Hakenfaust" piercer lock, mounted on left forearm; DFH-S2026 "Schildgewehr" offensive shield system (mounts 70mm high-energy blaster), mounted on right arm; 2 x anchors, mounted inside hands, can be fired out on a line
    Optional hand armaments: none
    Technical and Historical Notes

    The GAT-S02R N Dagger N is a special operations mobile suit based on the GAT-01A1 Dagger and GAT-X207 Blitz Gundam. In its model number, SO stands for "Special Operations," while the first N is for "N-Jammer Canceler" and the second N is for "Ninja." The N Dagger N is equipped with a nuclear engine, as well as a Mirage Colloid stealth system. The development team was officially dissolved in C.E. 72 upon signing of the Junius Treaty, which prohibited use of nuclear engines and Mirage Colloid. However, work actually continued as a secret "Ninja Works" project (hence the second "N" in its name). Multipurpose launcher ports distributed around body are usually fitted with grappling wires. The N Dagger N is armed with a "Schildgewehr" offensive shield system similar to the "Trikeros" system used by the Blitz Gundam. The "Hakenfaust" piercer lock is similarly inspired by the Blitz Gundam's "Gleipnir" rocket anchor. In addition, the N Dagger N is also armed with an anti-armor penetrator, as well as a pair of anti-armor swords and hand-mounted anchors. On October 2 C.E. 73, two N Dagger N units are involved in the Earth Alliance's surprise raid on ZAFT's Armory One PLANT, in which three new Gundam mobile suits are stolen. However, the two N Dagger N units are intercepted in space by PLANT test pilot Courtney Hieronymus' XMF-P192P Proto Chaos.
    Miscellaneous Information

    Appearances: DESTINY-MSV (Gundam SEED DESTINY Mobile Suit Variations; Mobile Suit Gundam SEED DESTINY ASTRAY
    Original mechanical designer: Kunio Okawara
