    Bandai & Uniqlo team-up to sell Gundam apparel

    Bandai put out a press release announcing their collaboration with clothes retailer Uniqlo for the sales of Gundam T-shirts. Starting in mid-March, Uniqlo stores nationwide will carry a Gundam 00 T-shirt + FG Gundam Exia (white version) set for 1,500 yen aimed at kids while at the end of April, the first of several MS Gundam design T-shirts for men will also go on sale for 1,500 yen.

    The limited edition colorless Exia plamodel is a tie-in for a Gunpla Coloring Contest for kids. Prizes will be handed out in 4 categories (Bandai prize, Uniqlo prize, Kerokero Ace prize, and Gundam 00 prize) and winners will be announced at the end of June on Gundam Perfect Web and in Kerokero Ace.

    Hobby Japan, Hobby Magazine and Gundam Ace for this month are out. There's a bunch of new information that's now revealed.

    Vedabots confirmed!:

    In the new chapter of Gundam 00P, it's revealed that Gundam Meister 874 is a humanoid created by Veda to interact with humans to better understand humanity. This was 15 years ago.



    In the new chapter of Gundam 00F, it is revealed that Hanayo (the catgirl Haro hologram) is actually Gundam Meister 874's current form. It is also mentioned that Gundam Meister 874 now has "brothers and sisters" all over the world in strategic places, and their exact locations are not known.



    It should also be noted that Gundam Meister 874 has the same eye processing that Tiera and Ribbons does. Her flashback to her creation also shows the same location as the end of ep20.

    for Veda Access Levels and CB hierarchy:

    The highest access level of Veda is Level 7. This is where the Gundam Meister data is stored. CB Observers are only granted an access level up to Level 3, while Sumeragi has access to up to Level 4. Nena Trinity gained access to the Level 7 data through a backdoor, not through standard access. Ribbons and Tiera should normally both have access to Level 7. Also, the Observers meeting that took place in ep16 was underground in some AEU facility.

    for Gundam Nahdeel's Trial System and Transam Mode:

    The Trial System that Nahdeel is capable of was originally part of it's prototype unit - the Plutone. The Core Fighter that was part of the Plutone's design is not carried over to any of the third generation suits. The incident with the Trial System and the Plutone killed many of Chall's comrades.



    Next month's issue of Hobby Japan will showcase the "Transam Mode" in detail. Gundam Ace also has a sneak peek at the pin-up art that will be in the coming Newtype. It shows Exia in a normal suit posing with Exia behind him. Although the artwork in Gundam Ace is in black and white, the coloring of Exia is very different from usual. Could this be the Transam Mode?



    It's also noted that the GN-X units are indeed the final mobile suit designs by Ebikawa this season. They're the mobile suits that he mentioned in the last interview which would make a large impact on the second half of the series.
