    An interview with Director Mizushima
    - The story arose from my desire to portray a situation in which the way the characters distinguish "justice" and "evil" changes depending on their respective positions.
    - Trinity was deployed as a necessary counterbalance to make clear the justice on the protagonists' side.
    - In episodes 23 through 25, the Meisters will pay the price for everything up until now.
    - The first season will depicted absolute destruction.
    - Episode 25 will go up to the point at which rebirth begins.
    - The flow of the second series will come from the question of what kinds of things happen at a time when the world is changing.
    - In one sense, what 00 must do once is draw out non-naive conclusions on the protagonists' part. The core of the second season will be how they make their appearances if they live to the very end, and what, in the midst of that, their conflicts have led them to discover.

    - In the second season, the fact that "Setsuna and co. entered CB because they despaired in the world" will become evident.
    - Lockon and Saji are utterly unlike each other, but the overlapping of their circumstances (being victims of terrorism) makes their existences closer together and so forth.
    - I was aware that there was a focus on Graham's eccentric way of expressing himself.
    - I feel that the second season must become a more emotional work. For example, mecha action. "00" is presented as stoic, as opposed to a super robot-type action series, but even then it should be possible to give it more theatricality by doing things like making scenes more flashy.
    - In episode 25, I'd like you to look forward to seeing it head in the direction of having a variety of characters go, "Eh, this is how things'll end up?" Among them are characters whose positions which change tremendously. In ep. 25, the worldview of the second season will come out, and things like "Why is he/she here" and "He/she was alive?" will also happen.
