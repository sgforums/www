    Ok... I've been really busy the whole of last week, so I really didn't have the time to sit down and do this. I will try to find the time in the coming days. To warm myself up to this task, I will begin with the Forward Left/Right position. I will refer to the left, but as you all should know by now, the mirror image will apply to the right:

    1) When Left Back has posession of ball, drop deep slightly to provide option. If given the ball, can play simple pass to Centre Left or others around him.

    2) When Centre Left / Centre Mid has possession of ball, give option to left flank. If given the ball, can make run down the flank to cross / cross from deep / play ball back to Centre Left.

    3) When attack is played on opposite side, to make run into the box to receive cross. If ball is played to Centre Forward, to provide option to Centre Forward.

    4) When team loses possession, track oppponent right-back. Get back in front of him and look out for penetrative runs. Be prepared to give option to midfield and defense when possession is won back.

    5) When defending corner on our left, to take out short corners. When defending corner on the right, to stay up front for counter attack (please try to stay at least 5 metres away from opponent).

    As you can see, the Forward Left / Right position involves the defense, midfield and attack, so all (even keeper) should understand his role too. Let me know if there's anything you're unsure of.

    Ok, the next position to be covered will be the Centre Left / Right position. Again, I will be referring to the Centre Left position and the Centre Right will be a mirror image:

    1) When Left Back / Centre Back has posession of ball, drop to provide option. If given the ball, can look for Forward Left to play ball to the flank, through the centre to Centre Forward, or play simple pass to others around him.

    2) When Forward Left has the ball on flank, to stay open behind him and provide option for pass back.

    3) When Centre Mid has the ball, to provide option for simple pass.

    4) When attack is played on opposite side, to drop back slightly to centre of midfield.

    5) When team loses possession, to cover midfield with 2 other midfielders. Provide support to left side if opponents attack on left. Provide support to centre if opponent attack on right / centre.

    As mentioned, the main role for the midfielders is to dominate the midfield in BOTH attack and defense. They will provide support to both the defenders and forwards.

    As what Bernard said, please feel free to share your thoughts and concerns on the various positions in this formation. Don't keep me wondering if anybody else on the team is reading any of this stuff! In fact, feel fre to talk about ANYTHING in this forum.

    Ok, next position to cover will be Centre Back. The roles will generally apply to both Centre Backs. As usual, I will refer to the left side:

    1) When we have goal kicks, to stay open and provide option to Keeper.

    2) When Left Back / Centre Back has possession of ball, to drop back to provide option with front facing opponents' goal (for obvious reasons!). If given ball, can play simple pass to players around him in defence / midfield.

    3) When Midfielders have the ball, to stay open behind and provide option for simple pass.

    4) When attack is advancing deep to opponent half, to push up offside trap to near halfway line, taking note of opponent strikers / wingers.

    5) When team loses possession, track opponent strikers. Similiar to fullbacks, be in position to defend, then defend like dog or die trying.

    6) When attack coming down left side, be prepared to cover Left Back in the event he gets beaten.

    While both Centre Backs will apply the above, one will control the defence and play deeper than the other. The one controlling has to constantly decide on when to play offside vs defend deep and control defence accordingly. If repeatedly under extreme pressure, to call for support from midfield.
