    Well, in FA's position, going tactical may not be the most exciting thing to do, but it is the wisest. Rather than chasing for the lead, his key priority is to ensure that Kimi gains as few points as possible, so there's no percentage in gunning for the lead, putting himself out and losing 10 points to Kimi.

    Last week, Kimi was on the charge, and those Macs were stunningly faster than anybody else, so FA made the smart move in avoiding a tangle. Even if he did try to hold Kimi up, Kimi would eventually have gotten at least fourth anyway.

    I don't think Trulli can hold FA off for long. Spa's got a few good overtaking spots, so I expect that FA can move up, and if not, it'll be done in the pitstops. The real question is whether or not the Renault's fast enough to take on the Mac. Montoya's eventually going to have to make way for Kimi, but I doubt he'll make it easy since he has his own position within Mac to protect. Best outcome I can see for FA is JPM giving Kimi a hard time and FA taking advantage of it.
