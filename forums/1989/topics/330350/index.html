  • Kuali Baba

    Venue: Autodromo Nazionale Monza, Monza
    Laps: 54
    Race start: 2000 HRS SIN

    The temple of speed and the home of the Tifosi (Ferrari fans) is the final European round of this seasons championship. It boasts the highest average speeds, and the atmosphere is simply stunning. Every team brings a special low-drag package to this track, where downforce levels are run at the absolute minimum. Thus, the cars are always twitchy and on the edge, but it is a trade-off for top speed, which can reach 360 km/h.

    As expected, power is also a key factor to getting around quickly, engines are heavily stressed, and those drivers who have to run the same engines as they did at Spa will be disadvantaged. A car that is able to ride the chicane kerbs very well is also a very big plus. A one-pit stop strategy is possible, but the top 10 in qualifying will probably go for 2.

    Hamilton leads Massa by 2 points after he was demoted after the Belgian Grand Prix, and he will be determined to fight back and improve on his 2nd place from last year. Raikkonen will not make it easy for either driver even though he is 19 points adrift. Look out for surprises from Toro Rosso, Toyota and BMW.
