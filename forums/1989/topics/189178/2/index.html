    Tsutomu Tomita to step down at Toyota


    Toyota team principal Tsutomu Tomita is to stand down from his position at the end of June.

    He will be replaced by Tadashi Yamashina, who is currently Toyota Motorsport's vice chairman.

    The team said that Tomita's departure was part of a standard policy of staff rotation.

    "As is normal for Toyota senior executives following a long overseas assignment, he will return to Japan in the middle of the year where new assignments await him with the Toyota group," said a team statement.

    Yamashina paid tribute to the man he will succeed.

    "Tomita san has been involved in a number of Toyota's motorsport activities throughout his career, including Formula 1 from its very beginning," he said.

    "He has been one of its central pillars over the last two decades, and I am both proud and pleased to succeed him.

    "Tsutomu is part of Toyota's F1 heritage and we will remain in close contact with him."

    Tomita described his F1 stint as "one of the most challenging and exciting periods of my career."

    He believes that he leaves the team in good shape.

    "I am looking forward to celebrating Toyota's first victory in Formula 1, which I believe will happen sooner rather than later - based on the technological capability and competent team of people which has and is being established in Cologne," said Tomita.

    BMW drops Friday testers


    Sebastian Vettel

    BMW Sauber has abandoned its policy of running young drivers on grand prix Fridays 'until further notice'.

    The team had used test driver Sebastian Vettel in opening practice in Melbourne and Sepang, but race drivers Robert Kubica and Nick Heidfeld felt that this had cost them critical preparation mileage.

    Under the 2007 regulations, team can only use two cars in the Friday sessions, so any test driver appearances must be at the expense of a race driver.

    Team boss Mario Theissen admitted after Bahrain - when Vettel's other commitments meant Kubica and Heidfeld had a full day of running - that BMW might rethink its driver plans, and today he confirmed that the testers would have to stand aside for now.

    "As announced, following the overseas races we have been looking closely at the driver line-up for the Friday of the GP weekends," said Theissen.

    "Until further notice, the two race drivers will be on track for the whole of Friday."

    Williams is the only other team to have used a Friday tester so far this year, but with Kazuki Nakajima committed to the supporting GP2 event, the squad is also expected to use its race drivers all day in Spain next week.

    Although Spyker had also intended to give its four test drivers some Friday practice opportunities during the season, it has stuck with racers Christijan Albers and Adrian Sutil to date.

    Key proposals are:

    Engine efficiency

    Limit engine power by imposing a maximum energy flow rate. There will be few restrictions on the engine cycle, which can include turbo-charging and energy recovery. This could lead to a gain of at least 20% in thermal efficiency.

    Drag

    Allow moving aerodynamic devices, which will reduce drag by more than 50% and allow a 40% reduction in the power required to maintain current speeds.

    Energy recovery
