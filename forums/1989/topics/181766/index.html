    Qualifying

    Phase One

    Kimi Raikkonen will start from the back of the grid in Sunday's Bahrain Grand Prix after the Finn lost his rear wing at the end of Turn 10.

    The McLaren star's suspension failed while he was travelling at around 140mph which tore apart the rear of his car.

    While Raikkonen recovered what was left of his McLaren to the pits, the red flag was brought out to clear the wreckage with just five minutes left to run in the segment.

    This created a wild scramble as many of the top runners had yet to set a lap time and under the new rules the slowest six cars are eliminated at the end of the segment.

    With more or less the whole field leaving the pits at the green light together, there was always going to be some unhappy faces and this time they were at Toyota as Ralf Schumacher missed the cut.

    Also now out of the session are the Midlands of Christijan Albers and Tiago Monteiro and the Super Aguris of Yuji Ide and Takuma Sato.

    Fernando Alonso was fastest of all ahead of Jenson Button, Giancarlo Fisichella, Nico Rosberg and Juan Pablo Montoya.

    Phase Two

    World champion Fernando Alonso left it very late in the second 15-minute segment to ensure that is in the final shoot-out, by setting the fastest time of the weekend so far.

    The Renault star, and team-mate Giancarlo Fisichella waited until the last four minutes to go out and bang in their bankers.

    Splitting the blue cars was the sole remaining McLaren of Juan Pablo Montoya.

    Nick Heidfeld made the cut with 10s to go as he went fourth ahead of the Ferraris of Felipe Massa and Michael Schumacher.

    Jenson Button changed his set-up midway through the session to go seventh ahead of Christian Klien, Mark Webber.

    Rubens Barrichello, off the pace all weekend, ended the session on the bubble.

    Those that missed the cut included Jacques Villeneuve, Nico Rosberg (who spun on his final lap), David Coulthard, Jarno Trulli, Vitantonio Liuzzi and Scott Speed.

    Final phase

    Michael Schumacher has equalled Ayrton Senna's record of 65 career pole positions by setting the fastest time in the final phase of qualifying for the Bahrain Grand Prix.

    As we predicted after free practice, Ferrari still had something left in the bag as Schumacher and Felipe Massa scrapped out the top slot between them.

    In fact Massa, was just 0.04s slower than the great man in the end.

    Jenson Button was third fastest having never seemed to have the pace of the two Ferraris, although it's possible that he and world champion Fernando Alonso could have been on slightly higher fuel loads.

    Juan Pablo Montoya had a quiet sesssion but banged in a quick lap at the end to go fifth ahead of Honda's Rubens Barrichello.

    Excellent individual performances from Mark Webber and Christian Klien saw them go seventh and eighth.

    Giancarlo Fisichella dropped to ninth in the final shuffle, while Nick Heidfeld blew his best lap by going wide onto the sand.
