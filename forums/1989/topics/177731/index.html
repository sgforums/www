    BERGER RETURNS WITH TORO ROSSO
    Last Updated: Thursday, 09, February, 2006, 11:57

    Former Formula 1 driver Gerhard Berger has bought 50% of Scuderia Toro Rosso, the team created out of the Minardi sale to Red Bull owner Dietrich Mateschitz.

    The 46-year-old Austrian, who quit F1 as BMW's motorsport director in 2003, has long been linked to a return to the sport as a team principal.

    As part of the new deal, Berger will have a hands-on role in the running of STR and will "be available in an advisory capacity" in regards to Red Bull's entire F1 involvement.

    "I am aware that my Formula 1 know-how is very much valued by Red Bull in general and by the Italian Toro Rosso team in particular," he said.

    Under the terms of the agreement, 50% of Berger's haulage and shipping company will fall under Mateschitz's control.

    Berger recently expressed interest in running his own team after 2008, when Max Mosley's new low-cost Formula 1 comes into effect, but the former Ferrari and McLaren star has also been connected to Red Bull since the 1980s.

    In fact Berger was the first sportsman to be sponsored by the drinks company, which now runs multi-million dollar promotional programmes in a variety of sports worldwide.
