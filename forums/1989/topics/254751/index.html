    Hamilton fastest at Ricard test (Day 1)


    Championship leader Lewis Hamilton continued his excellent form to set the fastest time on the first day of this week's Paul Ricard test.

    The teams used the twisty 2D configuration at the High Tech Test Track in France as they worked on their set-ups for the forthcoming Monaco Grand Prix, a race Hamilton believes he stands an excellent chance of winning given his Formula 3 and GP2 successes at the track.

    Hamilton was a a full 1.3s clear of the competition, with Ralf Schumacher second for Toyota and Renault test driver Nelson Piquet Jr third.

    The Brazilian was only fractionally faster than Mark Webber, Nico Rosberg, Spanish Grand Prix winner Felipe Massa and Honda's Christian Klien.

    James Rossiter set the ninth best time on his first appearance for Super Aguri.

    Spyker plans to share its car between no fewer than five drivers during the four day test, with Malaysian Fairuz Fauzy in action today.

    Although the weather was dry all day, gusty winds made life difficult for the teams.

    Lewis stays on top on day two


    Lewis Hamilton remained on top on the second day of this week's test at Paul Ricard in France.

    The McLaren driver improved on his Monday time by 0.085s, with his 1m05.614s lap putting him a tenth clear of Barcelona winner Felipe Massa's Ferrari.

    The title rivals were followed by BMW's Robert Kubica, the flying David Coulthard's Red Bull, Takuma Sato impressing again for Super Aguri, and Renault's Heikki Kovalainen.

    Champ Car star Sebastien Bourdais could only manage the 10th fastest time as he re-acclimatised to Formula 1 machinery on his second appearance for Toro Rosso.

    The teams were all working on their set-ups for the Monaco Grand Prix, using a tight and twisty configuration of the specialist test track that was designed to replicate the demands of the Monte Carlo street circuit.

    They focused particularly on tyres, as Bridgestone will use introduce its new super soft compound for the first time at next weekend's race.

    The test continues until Friday, but the drivers will switch to a different circuit layout for the remaining two days as they turn their attention to perfecting low-downforce settings ready for the Canadian Grand Prix, which follows Monaco.
