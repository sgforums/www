    The British rookie later increased his margin to nearly 0.4s with a soft-tyre run at the end of the session, clocking a best time of 1m21.880s.

    Lewis Hamilton set himself up for a crack at pole position for the Spanish Grand Prix by topping the times in final practice on Saturday morning.

    The 22-year-old rookie pipped team-mate Fernando Alonso by 0.079s as McLaren maintained its narrow advantage from Friday.

    Remarkably, it was the third time in his four grand prix outings to date that Hamilton has been fastest in Saturday practice.

    For most of the session Ferrari ran the silver cars closest, but Felipe Massa and Kimi Raikkonen slipped back to sixth and seventh amid the usual frantic scramble in the last few minutes of the session.

    The two BMW Sauber drivers made big improvements at the end to slot into third and fourth places, Robert Kubica edging out Nick Heidfeld and finishing just 0.131s behind Hamilton.

    The surprise of the session was David Coulthard, who hauled his Red Bull up to fifth.

    Massa was the first of the big hitters to set a benchmark, going fastest with a 1m22.214s after 20 minutes.

    He was immediately unseated by Alonso, who managed a 1m22.178s and then a 1m21.893s Â– to the cheers of the expectant Spanish crowd.

    But Ferrari soon fired back as first Raikkonen shaved a few thousandths off AlonsoÂ’s time and then Massa moved the bar to 1m21.672s.

    With a little over 20 minutes remaining, Hamilton took on a set of soft-compound tyres and upped the ante to 1m21.233s, which would stand as the fastest time of the hour.

    Aside from the BMWs and Coulthard, Anthony Davidson was the other big mover in the dying minutes Â– continuing his run of impressive Saturday practice performances.

    The Super Aguri driver finished eighth quickest ahead of the Williams of Nico Rosberg and Renault duo Heikki Kovalainen and Giancarlo Fisichella.

    Jenson Button was mired down in 17th, four places behind his Honda team-mate Rubens Barrichello.

    Massa spoils Alonso's Spanish party


    Qualifying 1

    Lewis Hamilton got his bid for a first career pole position off to the perfect start by topping the first knock-out qualifying session at the Circuit de Catalunya.

    But Ferrari's Felipe Massa got within a quarter of a second despite a ragged moment under braking on his fastest lap Â– suggesting the Maranello squad is ready to do battle with McLaren this afternoon.

    Home hero Fernando Alonso was third fastest ahead of Nick Heidfeld (BMW Sauber) and Heikki Kovalainen (Renault).

    Both Super Aguris comfortably made it through to Q2 but there were several surprises among those who missed the cut.

    These included Toyota's Ralf Schumacher and Williams' Alex Wurz, as well as Red Bull's Mark Webber.

    The Australian's RB3 was struck by a suspected hydraulic problem, while Scott Speed (who had shown well hitherto this weekend) was sidelined by transmission gremlins.

    Drivers knocked out: Ralf Schumacher, Alex Wurz, Mark Webber, Adrian Sutil, Christijan Albers, Scott Speed

    Qualifying 2

    Ferrari brought a halt to McLaren's dominance of the Spanish Grand Prix weekend in second qualifying, as Felipe Massa set the pace and team-mate Kimi Raikkonen finished third fastest.

    Delivering on the potential he had shown in Q1, Massa edged out McLaren pacesetter Lewis Hamilton by just over 0.1s.

    Fernando Alonso was shuffled back to fourth place, precisely 0.2s shy of Massa's time.

    Next up were the BMW Saubers of Nick Heidfeld and Robert Kubica, ahead of Red Bull's David Coulthard who made it through to the top-10 shoot-out for the first time this season.

    Jarno Trulli (Toyota) and the two Renaults were the others to make the cut, while Nico Rosberg (Williams) missed out by 0.3s.

    Super Aguri had a nightmare session, as Takuma Sato ground to a halt with a technical problem and Anthony Davidson had a pair of off-course excursions.

    Drivers knocked out: Nico Rosberg, Rubens Barrichello, Takuma Sato, Jenson Button, Anthony Davidson, Vitantonio Liuzzi

    Qualifying 3 (Pole Shootout)

    Felipe Massa shattered the dreams of the huge Spanish crowd at Barcelona on Saturday by denying Fernando Alonso pole position for his home grand prix.

    The Brazilian produced a superb final lap to beat Alonso's benchmark time by an infinitesimal three-hundredths of a second and claim a remarkable third straight pole.

    Q3 built up to a thrilling climax as the four big hitters from Ferrari and McLaren went head-to-head.

    Alonso had been fastest during the fuel-burning phase and he lived up to that with his first hot lap, eliciting a raucous cheer from the grandstands as he clocked a 1m21.661s.

    Team-mate Lewis Hamilton was first out for his final run but was unable to match Alonso's benchmark despite a tidy and committed lap.

    That and Lewis' comments afterwards suggested that Alonso was carrying less fuel and was on an aggressive strategy in a bid to snatch pole.

    The ploy looked like working when Kimi Raikkonen could manage only a 1m21.723s, splitting the McLarens.

    But then Massa uncorked a stunning 1m21.421 to knock Alonso off the top spot. Try as he certainly did, the world champion couldn't quite match that on his final flier.

    Robert Kubica took fifth on the grid for BMW Sauber, while qualifying specialist Jarno Trulli demoted the other F1.07 of Nick Heidfeld to seventh.

    Heikki Kovalainen, David Coulthard and Giancarlo Fisichella rounded out the top 10.
