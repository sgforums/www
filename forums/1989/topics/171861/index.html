    Organisers of the Nurburgring - the 2006 European Grand Prix venue - have offered to share the German Grand Prix with its financially-troubled rival Hockenheim.

    The Nurburgring's general manager Walter Kafitz claims both circuits could bolster their finances by becoming the sole German F1 outlet in alternate years after it became clear earlier this week that Hockenheim is facing an operating loss of 1.5 million euros in 2006.

    Germany and Italy are the only two countries to have two races on the Formula 1 calendar.

    "That is at least an idea. It has already been welcomed of Bernie Ecclestone," Kafitz told the Bonn newspaper General Anzeiger.

    Kafitz's opposite number Georg Seiner told the Mannheim Morning newspaper that he wouldn't rule out the proposal.

    "We looking into all the options, but we will not make any rash decisions", said Seiner, who added that any such considerations would have to take into account Hockenheim's contract with the FOM to host the German Grand Prix until 2008.

    "It's true that the track is in financial problems," added Kafitz.

    "It used to be possible to make a lot of money from hosting Formula 1 races, but these times are now past. We are not attracting the same amount of fans as we were in 2002 when we had 350,000 spectators for a grand prix at the NÃ¼rburgring."

    "He has said before that he wants Formula 1 races in as many countries as possible."
