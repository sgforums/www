    This monster of a circuit is the longest on the calendar and one of the most challenging. Winding through the forests in the Ardennes mountains, it is a firm favourite among the drivers. It has been likened to 'a drive home in the country', as several awesome bends simply follow the natural contours. The names of Eau Rouge, Blanchimont and Pouhon are legendary.

    This event was absent from the 2006 season as the bus stop chicane underwent a revision, the pit straight was...erm...straightened, and the facilities were upgraded.

    The track sets itself up for exciting racing as it is one of the few where a driver in an inferior car can shine. Most memorably in...

    ...2000, Mika HÃ¤kkinen passed Michael Schumacher and a backmarker (Zonta) simultaneously in one of F1's greatest overtaking manoevers.

    ...1991, Schumacher made his debut at this track, qualifying 7th before retiring on the starting grid with gearbox trouble. 4 years later he braved the rain on dry tyres to take the win, after starting 16th.

    ...1998, David Coulthard triggered a 14-car pile-up. After the restart, he and Schumacher collided while the former was about to be lapped. This paved the way for the Jordan team's (now Spyker) first win, which was also to be 1996 champion Damon Hill's last.

    ...2004, Kimi RÃ¤ikkÃ¶nen, in an inferior and damaged car, also passed Schumacher for the lead and set the fastest lap at the finish line.

    A medium downforce set-up is used to give a good compromise between speed on the straights and that in the fast bends. Setting up the ride height is also tricky - greater ground clearance favours the sudden gradient change of Eau Rouge but affects the rest of the lap. The weather also presents another challenge. Due to the circuit's length, it can be wet at one end and bone-dry at the other. The teams that think on their feet quickly stand to gain the most.

    RÃ¤ikkÃ¶nen won the last 2 Belgian Grand Prix, but he is not 18 points behind the championship leaders from McLaren. Massa's retirement at Monza put him a further 5 points behind. At McLaren, Alonso is now just 3 points behind his teammate after the Spaniard won the Italian leg.

    Spa is expected to suit the Ferraris more than their homeground as it is fast and flowing. The McLarens rode the kerbs of Monza better but they will have none of this advantage in this race.
