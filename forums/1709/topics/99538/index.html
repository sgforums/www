    25 Oct 04, 15:03
    Toshiba, Hitachi, and NEC have come out with thier own methods of liquid cooling their own range of laptops. They run a fluid line over a wide area behind the LCD panel to cool the fluid and pump it through an area in contact with the processor. Their main aim of using liquid cooling is to make the laptops less noisy with the reduction of fans, although a cooler processor is said to run better.

    For a home-based cpu, if sound isn't the main consideration, recommend the use of sufficient fans and good ventilation is more than good enough. If you really want to go all out, come out with something that ducts air through a R-134A evaporator coil, but then you'll need a compressor with a pump and thats where it'll turn noisy again.
