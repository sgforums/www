    I like the Citaro because 1) It's WAB. Which means added accomodation for PIWs. And easier entry for the elderly and the handicapped. 2) It's got a better fuel efficiency rating. 3) It's (at the very least) stronger than the KUB. 4) I've been to Deutschland before and the Citaro handled really well.

    But what really matters to many commuters in Singapore is 1) The strength of the A/C. 2) The amount of seats on the bus. 3) How fast the bus goes. 4) How "swee" the bus looks.

    And the reasoning behind either side's conclusions:

    1) Because the Citaro is a WAB bus, it definitely has to compromise on seating space to accomodate PIWs. Therefore its only a balance between PIW space and normal passenger space.

    2) Who cares much about fuel efficiency rating, other than SBST/SMRT?

    3) Even though the Citaro is stronger than the KUB, its not as strong as SMRTs' O405s.

    4) The A/C on the Citaro needs work, while (in my opinion) the KUB's A/C is overdone.

    5) The SBST Citaro (according to my friends) looks plain "disgusting" with its colour scheme and placements. On the other hand, they agree that the SMRT Citaros look plain sexy. Strangely enough, the KUB rated as the most "sexy" bus of them all, ahead of the OC.

    Each side has their own perceptions on the bus and I think its just a matter of which features of each bus they prefer.
