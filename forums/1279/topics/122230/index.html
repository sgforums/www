    CONGRATULATIONS!

    Hope u like ur stay there. Basically if you want to find out more on HK buses, go this website: www.gakei.com/hkbec
    Other than tt HK has 3 major bus operators: KMB, NWFB,Citybus
    These 3 companies use a variety of buses & they do swop buses often thru sale to each other coys. NWFB & Citybus are related, i tink owned by a coy called Chow Tai Fook.
    NWFB uses Dennis Dragons, Neoplans, Dennis Tridents, 10.3M,12M respectively.
    Citybus fleet has VOs,Dennis Dragons10.3M,Man buses, SDs like Dennis buses on sv6,6A,6X,260,12M,25A
    KMBs buses are the newest: TRansbus Enviro500, VOs,VSOs, Dennis Dragons... click on the above website for more...
    Fyi, they dun have an interchange like ours, their int/terminals is by the roadside for most of their svcs. However, for major places like Admiralty Center, they have a central parking space but the parking arrangement is like a roadside terminal...
    KMB serves mainly Kowloon side but for svcs with prefix '1','6','9' cross harbour svcs, they serve HK island side also.
    This goes the same for Citybus, NWFB which serves mainly HK island side...

    The websites are: www.citybus.com.hk for citybus & nwfb
    www.kmb.hk for KMB routes

    BTW if u r in HK, drop by the Star Ferry near Tsimshatshui, go to the KMB stand, there are a lot of KMB bus models, in addition, if u present ur passport, u get a KMB souvenir.

    Airport buses A11,A12,A21,A22 run by Citybus. the buses have luggage storage area on the lower deck. The seats on the upper deck have foot rest & blinds can be lowered to keep out the glare. Whatmore, the buses also give a short commentary when u r on Tsing Ma Bridge and also announcing the nus stops.

    Citybus website will show you the various buses Citybus utilises.

    The 100 plus ex-SBS LAs were purchases by Citybus to meet the shortfall in their fleet after they were awarded the Franchise routes. All are phased out liao.
