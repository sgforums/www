    Nice pictures VBZ. I was in Switzerland for 4 months and took lots of bus and train pictures. Luzern has quite a large number of Citaros. Besies Citaros, there are also Mercedes O405, O405N and O405N2. However, there isn't any O405G, which is quite surprising. As far as artics are concerned there are Volvo B10MA, B10LA, Scania L94UA and MAN NG313.

    The trolleybuses are an interesting sight in Luzern. The oldest trolleys, made by Siemens-NAW-Hess, have achieved 1 million kilometres and have a golden plaque for their feat. There are artic trolleys and normal 2-axled ones, which have the means to attach a wagon behind it. Weird but unique. The newer white coloured trolleys are also made by Hess and they are all artics. I didn't get to see the new 23 metre bi-articulated trolley as it came after I have left. I would've love to try it.

    Zurich is also a bus haven. Not much Citaros here for the Neoplan Centroliner is the dominant model. There are rigid ones, artic ones and even midis! I got my first Neoplan bus ride in Zurich. It was a wish came true. I also managed to find a few MAN Lion's City buses but didn't manage to ride them.

    Zurich has trams in addition to trolleys. There is only 1 model for the trolley. The Mercedes O405GTZ, which is, in fact, a trolley bus version of the O405G. Riding the tram is an enjoyable experience. There are 3 types of trams, the oldest being made in 1968-69. The latest one has a slick and stylish design by Pininfarina.

    While I was in Switzerland, I lived in Rotkreuz in Kanton Zug. Zug is not as large as Luzern or Zurich but there is a fair mix of buses. One interesting model is the mid-engined NAW BU5-25. This bus uses Mercedes parts and in essence, it is a O405 with the engine moved to the centre like the B10M. There are artics and rigids, which can be attached with a trailer. Also available are the Citaro/Citaro G, some of which with Hess bodywork. My favourite is the Scania N94UB in 11m or 9.5m lengths. There is also the 17m, 3-axle Scania L94UB and at least 1 example of a normal rigid L94UB (can also be attached with a trailer), but these are not many and are only out at specific times. The bus which reminds me of home are the Mercedes O405s for they sounded just like the ones in Singapore. They have O405Gs with W4E boxes and of course, normal rigid O405s, but there is at least one rigid O405 which I rode having a turbocharged engine, much to my surprise! Another surprise is a short wheelbase Volvo B10M and a Volvo B10L, both of which can only be found at Cham Station.
