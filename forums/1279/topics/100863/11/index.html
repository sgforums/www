    Those shopping centres in Kulai and Kota are a bit too far, bus fare wise, need at least RM 3 per trip, not really worth the trip, in my opinion.

    Kompleks Lien Hoe is at the Eastern side, located slightly after Crystal Crown Hotel, 1km from Holiday Plaza. However, the shops in this shopping centre is more or less like Holiday Plaza but a smaller scale and NAC. Meanwhile, there are other big shops include "the store" and "ocean" supermarket/dept store, nothing much to buy though.

    Bus fare wise, it will be RM 1 per trip. Get down at Wisma Daiman and cross the road using the overhead bridge.

  • purplecrazed
    8 Sep 05, 12:50
    Well, Gelang Patah is very developed now but the interchange is right into the "kampung".

    You have to drive straight in until you passed the 2 schools, the chinese Chinese school called Ming Terk will be on your right and the other Malay school on your left. After you passed the schools, you'll come to a traffic light and the interchange will be right in front of you.

    CW3 has no problems picking up passengers punctually from Larkin, Tmn Perling or Tun Aminah but their major weak point is not knowing how to coordinate the buses so that upon reaching GP, the bus that has the permit to enter S'pore is already there waiting for us, instead of us wasting our precious time hanging around or "stoning" in the bus.

    Many times we ended up having to wait very long at GP for the S'pore bus to arrive OR waiting for the next bus coming from Tmn Perling/Tun Aminah so that more passengers can fill-up the S'pore bus. The same goes with going home. Eventhough we're already in our respective buses (whether it's Tmn Perling/Tun Aminah or 666), yet we have to wait for the next S'pore bus to arrive in order to fill up our current bus. If it's 15mins wait it's still forgiveable, but sometimes we wait 30-45min. Then another 10mins to hit back the highway. They cheat us of our time and money.

    But since 2nd Link has no crowd and jam, you might as well make GP your very own pick-up point, arrange for someone to drive you there and also fetch you home from the interchange.

    I think Johor cab drivers should start making Gelang Patah interchange their next hunting place.
