    Bandaraya in Malay language means town, so the whole area is known as Bandaraya Johor Bahru or rather Johor Bahru City. There is no specific area or corner or arena called Bandaraya.

    There are buses from Larkin to Masai, Kulai and Kota.

    But in Kotaraya, only the bus from the West side will call at there, therefore you can only get buses to Tampoi, Skudai and Kulai.

    777 goes to Ayer Hitam, but will passed by Kulai. But 777 is not the only bus to Kulai. As for details of more bus routes in JB, you may like to refer to page 1 and 2 or this thread.

    777 goes to Ayer Hitam, Machap, Spg Renggam, Kulai, all the west side places. 777 only ply between Ayer Hitam and Larkin.

    39, 133, 133A, 200, 208 all goes to Masai. Only 39, 200 and 208 start their journey from Larkin, to JB city centre, then head towards Masai. 133 and 133A will start their journey from Masai, making a loop at JB centre city, and head back to Masai. I remember that there is also Causeway Link sv from Larkin to Masai, but does not loop at JB city centre, its either 55 or 66.

    If you are referring to Jaya Jusco Permas Jaya, this shopping centre is in Permas Jaya, not Masai. Masai is a small town beside Pasir Gudang there. Permas Jaya is housing estate, about 10 km away from Masai town, but still under Masai region. So you cannot take Masai buses to Permas Jaya.

    For going to Permas Jaya, can take Maju sv 10, Causeway Link sv 22 and Transit Link sv 123. You can take these buses from JB, at the bus stop adjacent to City Sq, opposite Caltex Petrol Station. Alternatively can take from Post Office also.

    From JB city centre, Permas Jaya will ply thru the Tax Free Complex ( Kompleks Bebas Cukai ), followed by Jalan Pasir Pelangi, Bakar Batu and you will cross a high bridge, where you can see Senoko Power Station in Singapore, when landing at the other side of the bridge, you will enter Permas Jaya, the bus will continue straight, until you see MacDonalds' at the left side, at the traffic light junction of MacDonalds', you should be able to see Jaya Jusco. The road name is Jalan Permas Jaya Utara.

    More info can click the following:
    http://www.jusco.com.my/juscohome/html/profile/storeinfo/jusco_pj.htm
