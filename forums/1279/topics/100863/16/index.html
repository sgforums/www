    Don't know if you're noticed, the B10M used by SBST doesn't have a flat floor between the axles unlike those used by private operators with coach bodies. As a result, not practically possible in the SBST version to extract any extra standing space compared to say an O 405, whose flat floor between the axles presents no such constrains in being able to take out some seats in exchange for more standing space.

    The O 405 & 18.250 i consider as having lower c.g than the B10M & 18.280, implying when fully loaded (say standing to the doors etc.) i'll logically expect the O 405 & 18.250 to be more stable dynamically than the B10M or 18.280 since these have the disadvantage of their payload acting from a higher elevation from the road level.

    I notice there's no other model in Singapore that's as similar to the MAN 18.250 as the M-B O 405 here. Whether it be the GVW, chassi dimensions, dashboard, rear axle hub designs & even the closeness of the engine capacities.

    Over the past decade in Malaysia, there's indeed been an enormous amount of 'upgrading' done on the outstation coach sector there. In terms of hardware & service. In hardware, many higher end new bus-chassi were introduced in Malaysia either the same time or ahead of Singapore. The quality of Malaysian coach bodies also improved noticably such that they now on par if not superior to bodies from Singapore coach body builders & some like MTrans known to export to UK. In service, nowadays you have '1st class' whereby the driver & attendant, somewhat over-dressed i'll say, in suit & tie to serve food onboard to passengers & greet them while standing by the door when they get down etc. You don't get much chance to use such service within Singapore though !
    Of course ticket fares for coaches are far more expensive than on short distance citybuses.

    In the shorter distance urban bus sector, understandbly Malaysia has 'traditionally' lagged behind Singapore in both hardware & service, in quite sharp contrast to the long distance coach sector mentioned above.
    I attribute that mainly due to the cheap fares which doesn't give much incentive &/or makes it impossible for operators to upgrade both hardware & service levels to match Singapore's levels. BUT the good news is that, judging from operators like Handal Indah being around now, the hardware especially & slowly even the service standards are gradually being jacked up.
    It seems to me that HI has the financial reserves to withstand somewhat more risks than some of the other 'poorer' operators around such that they can afford to charge fares not very much higher than the others in the short distance urban routes despite their quite big fleet of new or brand-new vehicles of newer generation models which Singapore has largely still not upgraded to !

    Of course, the bigger cities in Malaysia like KL & JB would be the most likely places to have any new citybuses being deployed while the out-lying & more backward smaller towns would continue to have the lousier junks for the forseeable time being.
