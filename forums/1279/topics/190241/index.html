    On Page 46 of TODAY 11/05/06

    Letter from reader Damon Tan

    If so, they should deploy aircon buses on long routes popular with students

    I refer to the May 8 reply from Transitlink, "Fare concession rests on other factors too". Ms Kathryn Lau said that teritary students do enjoy concession privileges through monthly concession passes.

    However, I would like to point out that these concession may not be fully enjoyed by some students.

    It is common to see students squeezed into sardine - packed non - airconditioned buses plying between housing estates and teritary insitutions. Take service 151 from Hougang to the National University of Singapore via Ngee Ann Polytechnic. The journey takes more than an hour. Imagine being trapped in a furnance for that long.

    Yet we see mostly airconditioned buses being used to ply feeder service routes, in which most of the commuters spend less than 15 minutes on the buses.

    One possible reason for this disparity that I can think of is, as Ms Lau has said, that the public bus and train service are provided on a commerical basis. Hence it makes more sense for the transport companies to have airconditioned buses plying feeder service routes for which most commuters pay the full fare.

    It is not difficult to see that it does not make much economic sense for air - conditioned buses to ply the so - called 'teritary insitution' routes, as the majority of the commuters are students who pay $52 for the monthly bus concession pass that allows unlimited travel on basic fare bus services.

    In this case, if the subsidy is granted to the transport companies was based on the concessions that are granted to students travelling on airconditioned buses, are the students then fully enjoying the concessions supposedly granted to them?

    Perhaps the relavant body could shed some light on how the transport companies are subsidised for the concessions granted to students - TODAY
