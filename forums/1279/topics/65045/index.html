    well, u might wan to visit it one day since you are quite familiar with YI and should pass by it quite often.. the list are permanent buses on the routes, but there could be exceptions because:

    1. there could be one or more of the perm buses is/are down so a mid-term sub is taking its place.
    2. drivers, though fixed to a route, rotate their duties so they drive different buses every week.
    3. feeder svcs at YI do have special (S) shifts covering only the peak hours in the morning and afternoon. this is also known as "swing" or "split-shift" to some forumers. 804's duties codes are usually 804S20x.
    4. trunk route drivers do go for an additional run on feeder services sometimes as part of their duty so there will be more buses going around during the peak hour on feeder svcs.
    5. TIBS buses do not follow simple AM, PM and S shifts.. there is also the "three-quarter" (TQ) shift from 8am to 8pm which is enough to cover the demand during off-peak and peak day hours.
    6. not all buses are on the service all the while even though it is considered to be permanent. they are mostly on the service 6 days a week or sometimes 5 days so there are enough spare buses to go around in the depot. after all, machines need rest too.

    the list comes in the form of work calendar.. look out again the next time you go down.. at the opposite board of the work calendars is the notices.. there is one indicating schedule change implementation on 29th dec '03.. i will go down to check if it is removed, though it should not be so before 29th dec 03.. as for O405s on Svc 852, they have permanent duties on svc 852 on weekends usually.. for exact shift details might have to wait for the new work calendar to be up..

    as for Svc 852, the O405s are perm for holiday schedule.. for normal schedule, usually DAF and Lances take its place.. now some DAFs are starting to come back to Svc 852, but major changes are in place from monday, 29th dec when holiday schedule ceases..
