    I know it can be challenging to keep accurate loading on stops where there is high alighting/boarding. I used to face this challenge quite a bit before. Here are a few tips what you could do:

    Let's take for example sv 154 during AM peak towards Eunos. At Clementi MRT, you will typically have 35-40 pax alighting (from HDBs/condos along AYE), at the same time you will have some +50 pax boarding towards Ngee Ann.

    This is what I do:

    Take row 5 window of upper deck.

    When the bus arrives to stop, I count the people who come close to door to board. Your concentration needs to be on people boarding at same time with one eye and your fingers you count pax alighting. Everytime 10 pax alight, deduct from those boarding.

    Try doing it few times, you will find your own trick. I won't say it will be accurate, but it is still a good estimate +/- 5 pax. 

    I have validated this by then taking the count at King Albert and going to lower deck and verifying my count v/s those actually in bus. It has been pretty accurate 2-3 pax +/- but in overall scheme of things, it's not much! Let me know if this helps :)

    The part that I have NOT BEEN able to master yet is loading calculation on bendy buses. Extremely tough.
