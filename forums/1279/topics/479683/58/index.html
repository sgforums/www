    156 - only two bus came in 1 hour observaton duration and that too had no standees (around 30 pax on board)

    186 - spotted 1 DD and 2 SDs... all 3 buses must have had around 20 pax on board

    ** Overall, all buses leading to Toa Payoh direction had modest loading and no issues with loading. 153 to me was surprising that it seemed to have the highest loading among all services that called during that time followed with 132 and 154.

  • BusAnalayzer
    24 Sep 15, 11:56

    Observation of services at Whitley towards PIE (BPJ direction) from 18.00 - 19.00 hours

    190 - buses came at an interval of 03-05 minutes (16 buses) with many DDs bunching as well. In the duration I observed, no SD came but I saw 4 SDs in the opposite direction towards NBR. I would say at least 2 DDs were packed to door, where as all others could be in the range of 80-100 pax. 1 bendy that came during the period also was packed to door but had DD right behind that took the pax. So overall 190 is doing great with a high frequency and people are able to board well now. Take note that apart from the frequency, there was not one bus that left Whitley stop without standees.

    700 - spotted 3 SDs in the duration of 1 hour compared to around 16 buses on sv 190. Surprisingly, the buses came to Whitley stop with maybe 40-50 pax on board only. So SDs managed the loading very well. Definitely 972 has tanked its loading. So it is mainly the Petir road residents who take 700 these days. Absolutely manageable with SDs

    960 - spotted only 5 buses in 1 hour duration... that to me seemed very less.. also to note, not even 1 of them was a DD... the 5 buses were 3 bendies and 2 SDs... and all 5 of them had standees but none of them were packed to door. Most buses in opposite direction were DDs towards Marina Center.. I was confused. So are the DDs deployed for Woodlands Road industrial estate towards BPJ to cater to the high loading there? Or maybe it is just the hour I observed didn't have DDs towards WRI... maybe has before 6pm and after 7pm. Also frequency seemed lower than expected but overall no complain because buses were not overly packed. I think also to do with 190/972 alternatives

    972 - spotted around 11 DDs... buses came at frequency of 02-07 mins... Every time the Steven road signal turned green... either a 972 came or 190 or both... 972 loading is lesser than 190 as expected but has on average 60 pax loading.. Most buses were around the range of 55-60 pax loading, there were few that came in after 07 mins that were packed but none packed-to-door. Clearly when you stand at Whitley, you notice that even if 190 or 700 calls, passengers wait for 972 as direct option. Didn't see that with 700 and 960. 

    ** Overall such a huge improvement to BPJ services. I just can't imagine how the loading was managed before DDs came on 190 and 972... Well done on this sector to manage the loading :)
