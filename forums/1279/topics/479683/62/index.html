    In my opinion, this is how I think the loading would be like on bus service 258:

    For the section between Pioneer MRT Station and Joo Koon Bus Interchange, it is likely that throughout the day, the loading is very low - usually zero, sometimes a handful.

    For the section between Pioneer MRT Station and Jurong West Street 82, there should be around forty people taking it during peak hours. During non-peak hours, this number may be as low as... less than five. The connection of 258 to Pioneer MRT Station may encourage some of the people to change to take 258.

    • Assuming only street 81 and 71 people use this connection, there should be around 5 people from Street 71 and 81 making the switch from 243 (if frequency is around six minutes) to 258.
    • The connection of 258 to Pioneer MRT Station would encourage many people at street 81 and 71 who take 179 or 241 to change to take 258, thanks to the unreliability of 179 and the difficulty to board 241 at Pioneer Road North. Looking at how the people who take 179 at street 81 to get to Pioneer MRT Station can actually fill up a double decker bus, there should be at least half a single decker bus size of people making the change to take 258; around 30 people from Street 81.
    For the section between Jurong West Street 82 and Boon Lay Bus Interchange, there will always be at least around five to ten people taking it.
    • As the people there will take whichever bus (181, 243W, 258) that comes first, a double-decker bus on 258 should be around 50% occupied during peak hours, when it goes to Boon Lay Bus Interchange.
    • When 258 returns to Joo Koon, the loading between Boon Lay Bus Interchange and Jurong West Street 82 may be up to half a single decker (thirty) during regular hours, and around single decker (seventy) during peak hours. 258 may take away around half of 243G loading.
    Overall, bus service 258 should be able to make enough revenue to recover the costs.
