    On Sunday, 15 Mar 2015, I took sv 122 from New Bridge Rd to Commonwealth MRT. I can't remember most of the details, but here are some general observation:

    - Interestingly, the peak loading was before Tiong Bahru MRT. At least 10 pax boarded at one bus stop along Tiong Bahru Rd, and before the bus reached MRT it already had 20+ pax on board. Seems that other services had not called for a long time. Only 1 pax boarded at Tiong Bahru MRT but most of the pax onboard alighted here, and in the rest of the journey the bus never had more than 12 pax onboard.

    - I saw some new flats/condos under construction along Alexandra Rd where there are not so many services, so I guess one of the reasons why sv 122 plies Tiong Bahru MRT instead of Redhill is to connect this sector of Alexandra Rd to Tiong Bahru MRT, shopping malls etc. (If not they could easily divert 122 to Redhill)

    - Some people were waiting along Kay Siang Rd in the opposite direction (towards NBR). As for the direction towards Commonwealth, no boarding or alighting.

    - Quite a lot of people boarded 122 towards Commonwealth along Margaret Dr, most of them asking BC if this bus goes to MRT. However, I'm not sure if they will still take 122 if they know that it does a big loop at Commonwealth Dr before reaching Commonwealth MRT. There were some boarding at Commonwealth Dr, and I assume they are heading towards MRT only (if not they would wait for 195).

    Sin Ming residents prefer to board at interchange where there's 410 and 52, rather than at Bishan Rd where there's 52 only. For St 22 residents, most ppl at Circle Line MRT exit or Junction 8 will prefer to board at interchange, and would only cross the overhead bridge and board at Bishan Rd if they're at North South Line MRT exit or at J8 closer to Bishan Rd. 

    Also, pax at St 22 have various svcs heading to Bishan MRT/Int, and mostly prefer to take 13, 52, 54 or 88 to Bishan MRT which is faster and more direct than taking 410G to Bishan Int.

    This is why 410W has higher demand than 410G, since pax from Bishan Int to St 22 is greater than pax from St 22 to Bishan Int.
