    Service 307 was set up by then-SBS as a townlink service.Townlink 307 had Double Deckers and MK3s. It is a merger of Service 301, which is the current 307 Teck Whye route, and Service 303, current 307 CCK St 62 route.

    When Tibs first took over, it was indeed a pampered service. It received bendies, and frequency was good. That only lasted for around 3 months. It was probably because Tibs was trying to popularize itself with residents of CCK.

    So, in 2000, two months after the launch of the BPLRT, Tibs shortened it and converted it into a Feeder Bus Service. It only served CCK St 62. Then came the pre-2001 elections, a lot of Teck Whye residents complained to the MP and thus, the MP told Tibs to reinitiate the route. however, tibs only reinitiated half of the Teck Whye route. During this period, frequency was getting worse and bendys were no longer deployed.DAFs were deployed.Also, the direction of the service was changed, it went to teck whye first before going to St 62.

    In late 2001, Tibs then initiated the whole of the old 307 route and on the press release, it was written as "Enhancement of 307" and introduced it as a new intratown service. During this time, only onece a bendy was deployed and then all the CACs started coming into 307.

    Now, 307 is one of the most unfairly treated service. With a frequency of 15 minutes (although smrt claimed as 12 mins), 307 receives the dirtiest CACs of all CAC 0405. Well, what to do? it's the fate of it. I know all these as I have been taking 307 since it's SBS days...

    I guess some of you may not be able to understand or to be enlightened due to ignorance to certain facts:

    1. Sv 190 and Sv 307 come from Kranji Depot. It's rather a small depot and hence it could not park many bendy buses.

    2. Sv 190 must have a mixture of BOTH bendy and 12 metre buses, so as to have a better mix of buses, and not to constraint others services which require bendy buses too.

    3. NOT all the times, Sv 190 does have very very bad frequency. It is pretty good already. Sometimes, the 12 metre would come by, instead of the bendy buses because it's either they are on their way to New Bridge Rd Terminal, else they are on their way to CCK. There's a flow for the buses, whereby it actually follows the timetable being done up by the related department.

    4. I suppose Sv 307 has to go through very very tight roads. That's why it may not be able to have bendy buses, or Kranji Depot does not have enough to supply for Sv 307.

    I hope that after such explanations, some of you here could stop thinking very lame or childish reasons about why such things happened.
