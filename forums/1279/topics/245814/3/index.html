    SBS Transit launches campaign to stop assaults on bus captains

    SINGAPORE: There have been 18 cases of assault on bus captains in the first six months of this year, compared to 14 for the whole of last year.

    SBS Transit says its bus captains have been slapped, kicked, and in one case, punched repeatedly after he declined to hold up a busload of passengers for one man who wanted to finish his cigarette at the bus stop.

    To protect bus captains, a Stop Bus Captain Abuse campaign has been launched.

    Posters will be fitted on the interior panels of SBS Transit's 2,800 buses to encourage commuters to report such assault cases to the police.

    They will also be put up at bus interchanges and terminals. - CNA/yy

    Opening Of New Access Road To TPE From Tampines Road

    To facilitate the road construction works at Tampines Road near the Tampines Flyover, the traffic between the Tampines Expressway (TPE) and Tampines Road will be diverted permanently to a new access road between Defu Flyover and Tampines Flyover on 23 June 2007.

    Motorists are advised to take note of the following changes when traveling to and from the TPE from 23 Jun 2007:-
