    Hi guys,

    What you will notice is that while the Citaro features the same drivetrain as the OC 500 LE (OM 457 hLA engine, ZF Ecomat 2 or Ecolife transmission), there is a lot that is different.  The axles are made by ZF instead of in-house at Mercedes-Benz (which the OC 500 shares with the Integro), the braking system is more sophisticated, and the Citaro chassis features independent suspension.  It is a higher quality product all round.  The ZF driving axle not only sounds nicer, but transmits more power to the wheels.  The dash and associated instruments are also completely different.  The Citaro and OC 500 are therefore almost entirely different products.

    Regards,
