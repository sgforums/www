    Personally, I think the Jurong Region Line (JRL) will be underground.

    Firstly, elevated viaducts need to maintain some distance away from buildings. So far, we know that the JRL will run along two densely-populated areas, namely Jurong West Street 64/75/82 and the "gap" towards Choa Chu Kang MRT Station. The former is only 35.60 m to 57.32 m wide, while the latter is only around 28.04 m wide. It's hard to visualise how JRL could run elevated along those areas.

    Secondly, the line will run in Jurong Lake District and Jurong Innovation District. These are growth areas where land is precious. I do not think the government would want the JRL to run elevated across those areas and occupy the air space there.

    The information about JRL being "elevated" is probably a distraction, or a fabrication.

    (likewise for other information such as number of stations and the interchange stations)

    It is likely that the public, including the "transport enthusiasts", knows little about JRL.

    Which is good, since contractors are to abide to the agreement of not disclosing the information to the public.

    Thanks for reading. :)
