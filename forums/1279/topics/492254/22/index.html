    IN CASE 88 is shortened to AMK (as some forumers have said), I feel there might be room for this service:

    Service 71: Ang Mo Kio Depot - Changi Airport (Loop)

    https://drive.google.com/open?id=1DaQfIDrOkTftVIS9xOT-_fUWfbM&usp=sharing

     

    - Follows current 88 route from AMK Ave 10 to Toa Payoh, and then use PIE

    - Direct service for Ang Mo Kio, Bishan and Toa Payoh residents to Changi Airport

    - Calls at Jln Toa Payoh and Jln Kolam Ayer bus stops for transfers (e.g. 23 to Tampines / 966 to Eunos, MP)

     

    Limitations:

    - Cannot use DDs

    (Alternative route is to terminate at Changi Business Park so that DDs could be deployed, although not sure of the demand there)

    - Unable to call at ITE bus stop directly for Bishan/TPY students (but within walking distance like 45)

    With the view that the DDs on svc 117 are best utilised along the Yishun Ave 2 - Sembawang sector, I was thinking if the terminus of some routes can be swopped over to better match capacity to demand. So here are some ideas:

    1. Swop the terminus of 103 and 117 as such:

    • Svc 103: Serangoon - Sembawang (takes over svc 117's route to Sembawang from Yishun MRT)
    • Svc 117: Punggol - Yishun (terminates at Yishun like 39, 85)

    That way I think it can be justified for the revised 103 to have a boost in frequency and/or capacity, whereas svc 117 can use a full-SD fleet. Also it might facilitate Go-Ahead to take over 117 (so that they can have 2x Punggol-Yishun routes) ...

    2. Or alternatively, swop the terminus of 85 and 117 (85 goes further to Sembawang, 117 cut back to Yishun)

    Svc 85 already has almost full fleet DD, which would be useful for the future when Canberra estate is more built out. As with above, 117 can use majority SD fleet (if not full-fleet). Also this arrangement results in 2 bus operators at Sembawang and Yishun Interchanges (for now at least).

    Thoughts on the above?
