    ok... i translate part of it for u guys.

    To improve its bus fleet, SMRT buses will be refurbishing over 730 buses in the next 11 to 12 years. 100 of them are expected to be refurbished by the end of this year.
    Mr Lee Seng Kee (vice president, SMRT buses) also mentioned that SMRT will be ordering 132 new buses to replace its ageing fleet. 66 of them will be bought in the second half of next year, while the rest will be bought by 2011. These new buses will be zero-stepped to facilitate the boarding and alighting of the elderly and those who have difficulties getting around. They will also have a space for a wheelchair for the elderly and the disabled in wheelchairs.
    On another note, SMRT is planning to provide 11 premium bus routes in the future. "We will also look into extending premium services to serve the evening peak hour," says Mr Lee.

    ADD-ON: The rest of the article talks about the development of SMRT's real-time information system, which monitors the positions and timings of the buses through GPS. The article talks a lot about how this system helps the operators know the conditions on the road and thus rearrange bus journeys in case of accidents/breakdowns/traffic jams/bad weather/etc.
