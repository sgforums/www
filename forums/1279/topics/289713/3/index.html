    Yup. 138 previously was from Yio Chu Kang Ter (Ave 6, then turn right to Ave 5). It was later amended to originate from Ang Mo Kio Int, and used Aves 5 and 8. Then, 138, 265 and 267 were the only 3 services to right-turn into Ang Mo Kio Int saw-tooth berths. Though it was faster, people didn't take it to Ang Mo Kio Int as feeders were still cheaper then.

    159 was toa payoh - ang mo kio (loop), not the other way round. From Toa Payoh after Ave 1, it turns into Ave 8 (like 22/24) and heads into the Int. After letting pax alight, it immediately heads to 159 saw-tooth berth to pick up berth (hence the loop), then exits at Ave 3 before heading to Aves 6 and 1 (like 133/136) before heading back to Toa Payoh.
