    When Mercedes-Benz E-series transmissions "lose their gears", they're in a condition called "limp mode". Put quite simply, the transmission's computer has detected (correctly or incorrectly) a fault with the transmission and will only allow forward movement in hydraulic (as opposed to lock-up) 2nd gear. Quite often, restarting the bus using correct procedure will fix this problem.

    ZF Ecomat transmissions commonly suffer a problem where the transmission does not lock-up at all, or sometimes there will be no lock-ups in particular gears, especially in 2nd. In this condition, the transmission will rev the engine much higher than normal before upshifting and during steady transients, the engine will appear to "free-rev" (i.e. sound like it's being revved in neutral). There is also the characteristic feel of "less gearchanges" in this condition. As far as I know, the only way to rectify this problem is with an overhaul of the transmission.
