    It has been a prob with SMRT Buses for a few years, but they are doing fine.

    Many ZF Habits had this slippage, but are also rectified. There are some oddball cases, such as TIB 469P and 495M slipping 2nd time after a fix.

    Currently, TIB 466Y, Habits 1023K (?), 1099S and 1139K Habit has slipping gears.

    So many WLDEP buses have been fixed, like TIB 1000B, 1061A, 1063U, 1064S, 1066L, 1067J, 1070Z-1074M, 1077E, 1089X, 1091M, 1104K, 1105H, 1110S (the one burnt down), 1111P-1113J, 1130J, 1138M, 1141C, 1143Y, 1169Z (2nd batch), 1187X (Volgren). The situation is rather bad with older ZF Habits, oddly the box and Citaro lookalikes are ok.

    Most ZF slippages start with tardiness during 1st to 2nd gear. Then occasional slipping, characterised with sudden jerks and gearbox "grinding". The full blown is when gears appear to change, but revs higher than usual plus the sound of engine in neutral when accelerator is released during motion.

    Yup, 1132D had this problem for a looong time. I wonder 1131G has been fixed.

    Jerks are seldom though. Gear crunching (abrupt transmission from 2nd to 3rd due to tardiness) and a audible knock when 2nd gear is engaged (when slipping signs occur) are more common. When slippage finally happens, there are surprisingly no jerks, the transmission appears to be even smoother!

    When the Volgren 1187X had slippage, the entire Woodlands Int can hear the revving when it leaves a berth as though something is stuck. It was fixed in less than 2-3 months, probably due to the noise.

    I have only seen a Voith breaking down, that is TIB 1193C. The same tardiness when switching to 2nd gear, and over 2 weeks of neglect the gearbox gave up completely. The last night before the bus went for repairs, it ran only at 1st gear for the full feeder journey.

    I feel SBST fleet is better in this area, although i take them less frequently. Except for early Leyland buses that are starting to age.
