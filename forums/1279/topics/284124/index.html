    I guess many of our fellow bus fans or commuters have experienced this common problem while taking the buses in other bus services not only 147.

    I was drinking my drink while waiting for my 147 at Hougang Central to take it to my home in Serangoon North. I took 20 mins or more to finish my drink while I saw 4 74 buses, 2 153 buses, 2 89 buses, 3 27 buses picking up passengers from their respective berths. 147 haven't come at all. So, I walked around with my drink in the interchange and realised that there were no 147 buses resting at the interchange at all. After After drinking for around 16 mins, a bus 147 with bus rego 7313 came back, followed by 9216 5 mins after that.

    7313 came around 5 mins after it was parked in the int. 9216 never parked in the int at all.

    And the most interesting thing is that both buses came its berth at the same time.

    7313, followed by 9216. The BC was grumbling to another BC that they hadn't rested enough and a guy at the office asked them to drive towards Clementi as they were behind schedule.

    I am just wondering whether is that what SBST deals with late arrivals of buses into the interchanges by sending off two buses 7313 and 9216 together on the roads towards Clementi when they were behind schedule.

    It is considered very tough driving service 147.
    1. Route has 35 stages from Hougang to Clementi.
    2. Encounters frequent traffic jams along Jalan Besar to Bras Basah Rd
    3. I have researched 3 yrs ago for 147, it takes around 2 hrs for a bus to travel from Hougang to Clementi. I am not sure how long it takes for now but what I know is that it is definitely longer than 2 hrs due to high number of vehicles on the road now.

    Coming to the topic of bus bunching, it will tend to occur for the following reasons.
    1. If a bus is behind schedule by x mins, it will have to pick up y more passengers due to the x mins lapse for every bus stops making an assumption that every bus stop generate the same amount of passengers every minute. More time is wasted in picking up passengers and plus if the bus is full, time is wasted in telling people to move into the rear and so on and so forth and the BC will be waiting for the people to squeeze into the bus and to ensure safety before he or she closes the entrance door. Hence, bus bunching occurs.

    2. Traffic conditions.

    What I can suggest is knowing that 147 is a service that often experiences the problem of bus bunching which can be so severe up to a point where I can see at IRIS, waiting time for the next bus to arrive is 28 mins, 4 mins, 2 mins, 4 mins, 2 mins, 2 mins, spare buses should be left at the int so that in case of anything, freq of the service is ensured. Anyway, the mins here represents the distance between each other after a bus passes by a specific bus stop. This is taken during the Mid - Autumn Festival organised at Chinatown on 15th of September 2007.

    Just a thought. And besides, is the thing in bold a common way to so called curb bus bunching problem?
