    I believe that you don't get my point. It is not that I complain about 1 bus 3 mins. Even if 1 bus 10 mins, it is fine with me as long as the freq of the bus is as stipulated in Transitlink Bus Guide or even the bus guides stated at the bus stops.

    Let me use my above example to explain the problem of bus bunching. A bus service with 6 mins freq which I consider is rather gd.
    Due to an event or any unforeseen circumstances, unfortunately 6 buses are clustered together. Max waiting time will be 36 mins.
    It can be 36, arr, arr, arr, arr, arr in IRIS for the most extreme cases.

    Or, it can be 28, 1, 2, 3, 2, 1 for probably the next extreme case.

    Anyway, assuming a passenger comes to that bus stop, wanting to take this bus service every minute.

    After 15 mins, there will be 15 passengers waiting.
    After 27 mins, there will be 27 passengers waiting.
    So, when the first bus arrives, there will be 1 passenger that has waited for 28 mins, 2 passengers that has waited for more than 26 mins and around 7-8 passengers that has waited for more than 20 mins.
    18 passengers will have waited for more than 10 mins.

    Plus don't forget that this is just a bus stop. If there are 10 bus stops which is as hot as this, 180 passengers will be waiting for this bus service alone for more than 10 mins. In that case, do you think that bus bunching is gd?

    So, in actual fact, the freq of that bus service seems to be longer in the eyes of the commuters although it is the same. I can understand that these problems exist, our BCs are doing their best to drive the bus and explain to passengers about the long wait. It is just that, I hope someone be it government or the company can do something about this problem.

    I feel that bunching is a problem that wont go away so soon. Other than traffic conditions, there are many other factors. For eg, buses in Singapore dont exactly get the 'right of way' on the roads. How many cars drivers actually notice the 'give way to buses' sign at the back. Some of the newer buses now dont even have the sign. As a result, buses have to wait up to a minute to get out of bus bays/slow down to change lanes etc, causing unnecessary delays. I believe this does not happen in Germany.

    On another note, I believe one-off incidents shouldnt be taken as a general condition of the bus service. Delays during Mid-Autumn Festival should be expected. A few years ago, I took 145 at about 8pm on National Day from lavender to TP. 3 buses bunched together and all chionged to TP, let the passengers alight, and immediately left for the next trip again, w/o resting. Most prob, the buses will bunch all the way until Bn Vista. No one will take this as a typical service standard of 145.
