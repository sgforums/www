    Ang Mo Kio Interchange

    The old interchange has been around since the late 70s. It was closed with effect from 24th March 2002 for redevelopments in the AMK Town Centre. It's relocated to the junction of Ang Mo Kio Central 2 and Ang Mo Kio Ave 8 since then. The new interchange is expected to begin operations in mid 2007.

    Bishan Interchange

    This interchange was opened in 1988/1989 and has been around ever since.

    Hougang Central Interchange

    The old interchange was opened in 1994 due to growing developments of Hougang Town. However, due to the construction of Hougang MRT Stn, the interchange was split into two - HGN Int and HGN Ter in late 1998. Hougang Interchange was located in front of Hougang Plaza while Hougang Terminal was located next to Blk 851.

    They were relocated back to the current site in 2001 due to the completion of Hougang MRT Station.

    Punggol Interchange

    This interchange was opened on 30th November 2003 by Rear Admiral Teo Chee Hean and has been around ever since.

    Serangoon Interchange

    This interchange was opened in 1989/1990 and has been around ever since.

    Toa Payoh Interchange

    The old interchange has been around since the late 70s. It was closed on 31st May 1998 for redevelopments in the Town Centre. It was relocated across Toa Payoh Lor 6 at Toa Payoh Town Park for 4 years.

    The New HDB Hub complex and Interchange were opened on 19th May 2002 by Yeo Cheow Tong @1100 hrs. (I was at the opening ceremony)

    Opened in mid-80s. This interchange was meant to serve Northwest Singapore at that time as CCK & BP did not have any int back then.

    Bukit Panjang Inter
    Opened in 2000.This interchange is a temporary one. Permanent one to be intergrated with BP station of Bukit Timah MRT line.

    Choa Chu Kang Inter
    Int opened in 1989 in conjunction with the completetion of the BT Batok MRT extension.This int was extended in 1994.(From 'I' shape to 'L' shape) The walls of this int have seen a lot of changes such as the exchange. The 2nd Townlink in S'pore...

    Woodlands Regional Inter
    Opened in 1996 in conjunction with the Woodlands Line. On that day, Woodlands became a regional Centre.

    Oh yah not forgetting that Toa Payoh Interchange is the first Aircon Interchange in Singapore..

    Sengkang Interchange

    Before the opening of this interchange in Sengkang Square on 10th Jan 2003, there was a temporary one located at the junction of Compassvale Rd and Compassvale Walk. It was opened in late 1999 just after SBS took over operations in Sengkang. It ceased to exist on the same day that the 2nd Aircon Interchange in Singapore opened.

    Woodlands Interchange
    This interchange was opened in the early 80s @Woodlands Ctr Rd to serve the Marsiling areas. Most of the services moved to the current Woodlands Regional Interchange on 10th Feb 1996 and 10th March 1996. Since then, Townlinks 911 912 913 were called here as a looping point enroute to Woodlands Regional Interchange.

    The fomer interchange was then closed down in early 2003 and the Townlinks were made to loop around Woodlands Ctr Rd.

    Woodlands Regional Interchange
    This interchange is the first underground interchange in Singapore with the largest number of berths (16). It was officially opened on 10th Feb 1996 and i believed that that was the day when interchang operations just began.

    Boon Lay Interchange
    Officially opened on 1 July 1990. Most of the services were from the old Jurong Interchange. Services at that time:
    30, 154, 157, 178, 187, 192, 196, 197, 198, 199, 206, 240, 242, 244, 246, 247, 248, 249, 250, 251, 252, 253, 254, 255, 257, 852, 951

    Jurong East Interchange
    Started out as a terminal in 1984 along Jurong East Ave 1. Buses park at the pair of bus stops in front of the church. First services were 79 & 333. Present interchange was opened in June 1985. Services at that time:
    7, 76, 79, 97, 98, 147, 333, 334, 335

    Clementi Interchange
    Officially opened on 16 November 1980. Svs. 78 was also inaugurated on that day. Services at that time:
    74, 78, 176, 185, 186, 191, 209, 282, 284

    Bt Merah Interchange
    Officially opened on 14 February 1981. Services at that time:
    14, 16, 44, 50, 63, 125, 139, 153, 184, 185, 187, 272, 273, 274

    Ang Mo Kio Interchange (old)
    Opened on 17 August 1980. Svs. 77 and 134 were also inaugurated on that day. Before the opening of the interchange complex, buses park along Ang Mo Kio Ave 8. Services at that time:
    25, 74, 77, 132, 133, 134, 136, 138, 159, 162, 165, 166, 168, 169, 261, 262, 265

    Ang Mo Kio Temp Int
    Too small for a relatively large Ang Mo Kio Town, services quite good except need a link to northwest / western MRT corridor.

    Bishan Int
    Only 5 services utilizing it, needs more services although Bishan is quite a small town.

    Boon Lay Int
    Quite big, dirty, and noisy. Needs expansion to accomodate the large numbers of JIS buses there. Or maybe can reroute some JIS services to Jurong East or Bukit Batok?

    Bedok Int
    Big, busy, and dirty. Needs upgrading. Very well linked. Perhaps even the best linked of all interchanges.

    Bukit Merah Int
    For a quiet neighborhood, the Int is relatively busy. Large parking space but not enough berths / space for pax.

    Bukit Panjang Int
    Good sized interchange. But needs more links than only to western / city area.

    Bukit Batok Int
    Quite quiet, but nice interchange. Can do with a few more services.

    Choa Chu Kang Int
    Not bad an interchange. Just needs more services.

    Clementi Int
    Too crowded, cramped and small. Dirty and very bad on rainy days as well. Needs the most upgrading out of all interchanges!

    Eunos Int
    Huge but a real ghost town. Needs much more services. Maybe some Bedok services can be shortened to Eunos?

    HarbourFront Int
    Pleasant interchange, quiet and bright. But needs more space / berths.

    Hougang Ctrl Int
    Now very busy and bus parking always crowded since the closure of Hougang South, but a bright and spacious interchange.

    Jurong East Int
    Big but very underutilized. Bright, spacious and clean.

    Pasir Ris Int
    Quite small but just sufficient for the number of services there. Maybe it needs expansion if more services are diverted / created @ Pasir Ris.

    Punggol Temp Int
    Nice interchange, but underutilized. It could do being the perm interchange of Punggol already. Very quiet.

    Sengkang Int
    Small, dark and cramped. Far cry from Toa Payoh.

    Serangoon Int
    OK interchange, quiet and lots of space, but a little too dark.

    Tampines Int
    The best or second best (to Woodlands) of the "Big 4" (Woodlands / Tampines / Bedok / Boon Lay), busy and bright and reasonable space. Berths too few for the large number of parking spaces available though, maybe its because most services in Tampines do not need too much space. Maybe to make more berths they can change the 3 fixed berths (23, 8, 81) to a set of 9 queue lines (3 lines per berth).

    Toa Payoh Int
    Aircon, bright, spacious, and very well connected. I daresay its my favorite interchange. Very good for a relatively small town like Toa Payoh.

    Yishun Int
    Lots of space, not too noisy, and bright. Nice interchange. Its connections to the rest of Singapore is OK.

    Woodlands Reg Int
    A little too big for my comfort, but it has lots of space and bright enough. Good interchange. I like the basement design also.
