    Driverless bus rolls out of interchange and onto road
    Straits Times, The (Singapore)
    Home
    February 1, 2008

    AN UNMANNED bus which rolled backwards out of its parking bay at the Toa Payoh Interchange and onto a nearby road tripped off a chorus of honking from motorists on Wednesday evening.

    On board were a bus driver and two of his passengers.

    The bus, service 155, had its engine switched off and had been parked in its berth at about 11.20pm, when the bus driver got out of his cabin to help a commuter and her child look for a missing bag on board the vehicle.

    SBS Transit said the driver failed to engage the handbrakes fully, so the bus rolled out of the berth and towards Toa Payoh Lorong 6.

    After some 60m, it came to a stop when its rear wheels mounted the pavement in front of Toa Payoh Gardens.

    The sight of the reversing bus on the road bewildered motorists, who honked to warn others.

    One motorist, Ms Neoh Chong Eng, said in an e-mail to The Straits Times: 'I heard a loud continuous honking from the vehicle next to mine. I had to stop to let this strange reversing SBS bus cross the road.'

    She said that it was only after the bus had come to a stop that she saw a child, followed by a man and a woman on board, running to the front of the bus.

    SBS Transit's director of corporate communications Tammy Tan said the bus driver 'may have been doing a good deed at that point, but he should have been more careful with the brakes'.

    She added that disciplinary action would be taken against him.

    No one was hurt and no damage was done to the surrounding property or bus.

    SUJIN THOMAS
