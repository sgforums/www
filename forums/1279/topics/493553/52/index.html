    Originally posted by sgbuses:

    12e and 147e are long overdue routes. This is the type of all-day limited stop services that have been popular in Perth and Adelaide in Australia and helps commuters shave time off their journey.

    All-day limited stop bus services that run parallel to train lines are also very popular in Hong Kong, where the MTR, buses and minibuses compete against one another for market share by offering fast and direct services.

    It is good to see that Singapore is heading in a similar direction, where SBS transit and Go Ahead are competing with SMRT by introducing 147e and 12e.

    If SMRT and SBS Transit are unwilling to introduce express bus services running parallel to their MRT lines, other operators should come in to compete, so that the winners are the commuters. (I am eager to see Go Ahead introducing express services from Punggol to compete with SBST's NEL.)
