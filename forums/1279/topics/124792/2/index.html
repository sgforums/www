    okies, to give u ppl more details.

    because the 2 CNG buses now running in Singapore are still under Volvo warranty, SBST has been sending the buses to Volvo mechanics for servicing regularly (SBST mechanics won't do anything or else warranty may be void). cos SBST and CNG bus drivers were complaining that the bus has "loss of power" and "loss of torque" issues, the Volvo ppl have been overhauling & modifying certain parts of the bus and getting feedback to improve their B10BLE-CNG buses.

    while i'm not sure which parts have been modified, i believe it includes the gas reservior tank (slow flow of gas to the intake?), ZF transmission and the engine itself. and i must say the performance of the bus has improved since. if they have found the solution for the "loss of power" issue, they may apply it to the other 10 CNG buses, which are now being kept in depots to rot.

    my personal analysis could be that the reservoir could not supply gas fast enough when the engine needs more gas at high rpms, leading to a "suffocating" sensation when the bus is trying to speed. but this has since improved.

    note that after each modification, SBST will have to collect data on fuel consumption rates cos it's not all about performance but also being cost-effective. thus, a definite word on the launch of the remaining 10 buses will not come soon. imo, the time frame for the launch could be jul 2006 - jan 2007.

    however, there is another source that says SBST may want to sell the 10 buses though i don't think the possibility of such a deal is high.

    6 of the buses are parked in slbp and 4 (2990E, 2991C, 2996P & 1 more) in bbdep.
