    So much about the children's stories (as i'll call it as) about triple-decker buses !

    Now for something which is REAL, in REAL use & which therefore, of course DO exist..... these are NOT triple-deckers, but ARTICULATED DOUBLE-DECKERS instead !

    While in Europe some years ago, i actually saw such a vehicle, a light-blue colour Van Hool driven to London from Holland.

    What it looks like ?? Actually you take say an O 405 G & add another floor on top then it becomes articulated double-decker ! Passenger capacity of course much higher than articulated single-decker but NOT exactly double maybe just 1.5 times, which still works out much more than on rigid double-deckers.

    But i've not yet come across any articulated double-decker for citybus use though. But technically this is POSSIBLE since such a design of omnibus already existed for some years as coaches instead of citybuses. Basically do away with the luggage compartment, change the type of seats & create more standing areas downstairs & there you have it...articulated 'jumbo' double-decker citybus !

    So, this instead is what's really possible instead, NOT triple-deckers.
