    E shift should be Extended Shift. Driver does AM peak till around 9am, 10am. Gets a 1.5 to 3hour Swing. Then continues again from 11am, 12pm onwards till end of PM peak.

    T shift are Three-Quarter Shifts. Driver usually starts in later AM peak and continues all the way to PM peak with no Swing.

    For S shift, yes, they are supposed to drive the same bus (unless got step backs where driver changes bus). But sometimes, if something happens, they might send another bus in the PM split shift. Like maybe they sent the original bus to cameo on another svc because of a breakdown.

    3. Citybuzz, Chinatown Direct services, 192 - 194

    as to what time they jump, they dont exactly jump every single trip. For these services, they will definitely jump during meal breaks. As for other times it depends on whether there's a T shift guy in the middle somewhere which requires drivers to jump every single trip (eg S82). Most of the jumping occurs during the day - especially from the start of the morning meal break to the end of the evening meal break in cases of weekends. For weekdays, they jump during the morning peak until the end of the morning meal break, start again at the start of pm peak / evening meal break and ends at the end of pm peak / evening meal break.

    Sometimes split shift drivers jump during their break at the depot. Depends on the service.

    Only exceptions to the above rule is service 92 and 36. Service 92 jump during meal breaks and during normal breaks. Service 36 jump every single trip until the last bus.
