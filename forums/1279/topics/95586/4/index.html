    Anycase the original standard engine for the Citaro is 12 litres, like the O 405 before it. But noteworthy about this newer 6.4 litre engine is that it's of new generation design with multi-valves, charged-cooling with advanced management electronics such that despite it's small size the output can be rated @ 280 BHP with Euro2 emissions levels ! This can be viewed as adequete as Euro3 buses are not yet the norm in Singapore. Of course if they want the latest Euro3 or coming Euro4 engines, then i suppose they revert to using the 12 litre engines rated @ say 250 BHP for rigid bus deployment & say 300 BHP for articulated bus.

    By the way, their new 6.4 litre engine does compare very well with almost any other the Euro 2 engines of similar or even larger capacity in performance. 280 BHP from only 6.4 litre how many other engines produce so much from so little ?? Currently very few.

    Going by my personal experiences just yesterday taking Handal Indah's route 22 from Permas Jaya to JB city in their new M-B with new generation engines, i'm quite optimistic such new generation engines although small in cc the acceleration, power torque outputs can match or exceed older design engines of even double the cc !! There's limits to what can be done to gearing to boost performance perhaps, beyond that i'll say engine performance & engine efficiency takes over. The 'push from behind' was strong, quick & fairly silent as the bus climbed up the slope near the new CIQ complex under construction, mainly only the turbo whine with hardly much gearbox noise from the manual gear (suspect to be M-B original gearbox with 5-speeds) in fact i'll even say this is more what should be expected from the buses in Singapore instead !
    Those ZF or Voith gearboxes used in Singapore on the whole i find them too noisy & harsh !

    Yes, they have a 6.33L engine. hseochin can also confirm this, I'm sure, since he is also pretty specced up on the buses. I personally find the build and finish of the Citaros pretty bad...a Custom Coaches CB60 body would be much more suitable, with the lower plastic content. The suspension is a little harsh, compared to the O.405, but the B12BLE is a vast improvement on previous Volvo air suspension. Even the Hino RM197K has a tiny 6.8L engine. The O.303s with the Mannheim bodies are very nice, and being a firm believe in the V8 engines they produce, I am disappointed at the lack of V8 option in the Australian market for the O.500RF coach chassis. I am even more disappointed at the lack of hub reduction, although the hypoid rear axle will make a similar (though quieter) whirring noise.
