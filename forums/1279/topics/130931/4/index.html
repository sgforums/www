    A long time back (somewhere in the early 80s), MacPherson Estate can only be accessed by Merpati Rd and the slip road to and from Pan Island Expressway. Circuit Link has not been constructed and the link between Paya Lebar Rd & Circuit Rd was a small lane by the name of Lorong Bengkok, which is still existent today, except it has been converted into a no-through road.

    Buses which used to terminate and passed by the estate used the green plate and the white plate. For buses that started its route from the estate, green plates means that the buses are going "out" (same as St Michael's services) and white plates means that the buses are terminating ("in"). For services 135, 155, 158, different plates means going towards different directions.

    If you noticed the sgwiki bus guide for 1979-80 (http://www.sgwiki.com/Bus_Guides_1979-80), u would have noticed that all the services that plied all entered via Merpati Rd and exit via Mattar Rd.

    svc 60 (MacPherson - Shenton Way)
    svc 61 (MacPherson - Ulu Pandan)
    svc 62 (MacPherson - Rumah Tinggi)
    svc 63 (MacPherson - Alexandra)
    svc 64 (MacPherson - Ulu Pandan)
    svc 65 (MacPherson - Lower Delta)
    svc 135 (Toa Payoh - Upp Changi Rd 13.3km)
    svc 154 (MacPherson - Jurong)
    svc 155 (Toa Payoh - Upp Changi Rd 13.3km)
    svc 158 (Toa Payoh - Chai Chee)

    svc 40 was amended from its previous route of Chai Chee - Prince Edward to loop at MacPherson Estate, making it one of the 1st services not to use the green/white plate system.

    When Circuit Link was opened, svcs 60, 135 & 158 was amended to ply the new stretch. Slowly, as bus services from the estate began to be relocated (svcs 61, 62, 64, 65, 154) or withdrawn (svcs 60, 63), the green/white plate system still continued on. For those buses which still uses the metal plates, the green/white colours are still around and can be seen on 135 and 154. However, colours are as per normal for buses using the new plastic side destos.
