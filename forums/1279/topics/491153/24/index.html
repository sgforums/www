    Originally posted by SBS8676Z:

    I would say that 851 is one of the most well treated service all these while.

    In late 1994, Tibs started rolling out new air-conditioned 0405 fleets and 851 was the very first to get them.

    In 1996, Tibs rolled out its first zero step buses (NAC Scania Elbo) and 851 also got most of them.

    In 1997, Tibs started rolling out new bendies into its fleet and 851 was also among the first to get capacity upgrades then.

    In 2000, Tibs rolled out brand new Habit bendies and 851 was also among the first to get them.

    In 2008, SMRT started rolling out new Merc OC500LE WAB fleets and 851 was also one of them getting new fleets. 851 was also among the first few SMRT service to go WAB.

    However when came to double decker fleets, 851 couldn't get them initially was probably due to Yishun interchange as well as AMDEP infrastructure constraints.

    Compared to 854, dun forget that 851 actually started bendies much earlier before 854 started getting them (that was in March 2000). When come to double deckers deployment, 854 got to get the DDs first, probably because 854 had too many old bendies and they need some of these new double deckers in order for 854 to go WAB then.

    AMDEP now is fully free from rooftop infrastructure because of BCM.
