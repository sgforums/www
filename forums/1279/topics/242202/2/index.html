If a new service is introduced which duplicate some of its own existing route, the number of passengers will drop on its existing services as some passengers will turn to take this new service. If there is no route duplication, they will expect you to take the existing service and transfer to another service somewhere else. At the same time, SMRT has to incur expenses for new service unless the service is a very profitable one to breakeven the expense of which SMRT will think it is quite unlikely to happen. Since this is the case, there will not be a need to buy new buses as using existing old buses in its fleet will do. This can result in maximizing their profit.

    What SMRT is trying to do currently is play around with its existing bus services like changing frequency, amending the route, introducing short trips and introducing fast forward. They will only introduce new feeder/short haul services if the area is being ply by only this new service.
