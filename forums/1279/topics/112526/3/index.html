  • The_Bus_Guide
    14 Apr 05, 01:41
    The driver does that because the pressure in the air reservoir is low. By revving the engine, it will drive the air compresser to build up pressure in the reservoir. The air is supplied to many components of the bus such as the suspension, the doors and, most importantly, the brakes. Buses run on air brakes. Contrary to popular belief, in an air brake system, the air is used to release the brakes. If there isn't enough pressure in the brake cylinder, the bus will be moving with its brakes applied causing serious wear to the brake drum. In some cases, the bus couldn't move at all.
