    For Hong Kong, this is also known as the Ground Transport Centre, where buses, trains and taxis to all parts of HK starts from here.

    In this case, when some HK airport buses does not terminate there and runs as a loop (very common among the "S" shuttles), the bus would drop passengers outside the PTB lift lobby---and the passengers were to take lifts into the arrival/departure hall.

    Otherwise, other buses (especially the notoriously expensive "A" Airport buses) would run up to outside the departure hall---and drop passengers there---before they head for the GTC terminus.

    sorry for being a little off-topic...
    Just a comparision of Sydney & Melbourne public transport to/from the International/Domestic Airports.

    For Sydney, passengers who uses the railway stations at Sydney's International & Domestic airports are charged premium fares (abt several dollars extra). Either travel by train or taxi. SMRT Trains doesn't impose any premium fares to/from Changi Airport stn.

    In Melbourne, only special express buses shuttle between the Airport & City (Spencer Street Railway Stn) at regular intervals ($13; acurate as of Yr 2003). These buses call at both International and Domestic Airports. Either take the Express bus or a taxi (average fare bet. city & airport ~$35).

    I think that's what make Singapore's already defunct "Airbus" a failure, because there are still other cheaper bus travel options (SBS svcs).
