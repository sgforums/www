    SBST had in mind of getting the DM3500s in service 160 & 170 since 2001 or 2002, shortly after all the DM3500s had been registered.

    SBST had in mind of getting the KUBs in service 160 & 170, shortly after all the Changi Airport services had went fully KUBs. Beginning October 2009, those earlier batch buses went to Johor Bahru, and in January 2010, they were tested at Queen Street Terminal. They only started deployments in April 2014, after the registration in October 2013.

    As the bus package goes nearer (160 & 170), LTA will have to liaise with SPAD to register up to 100 Citaro buses. These needs a Malaysia speed limit sticker, SPAD contact hotline number, the company telephone number and voice announcer that says "Do not leave your belongings unattended."

    Ulu Pandan Bus Depot will have a separate room for bus captains driving JB-registered buses, such that they only need card tapping at all times, as a form of identification. In order to drive JB-registered buses, newbies must scan extra thumb. Passports will sure run out of pages, and is troublesome.
