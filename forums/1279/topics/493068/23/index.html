    By right, public transport should have the very basic; no frills. Like the budget airlines.

    I don't understand why they want to have USB charger, television screen, third door, etcetera.

    All these additional features may add on to the costs, and cause the transport fares to increase.

    To prevent that from happening, we may want to charge extra for the additional services.

    For example,

    - Exit via third door, charge additional 50 cents.

    - USB charger, charge $1 per use. Pay and activate via QR code, using mobile phone app.

    As for the TV screen, that one can only recover from advertisements, and other sources of revenue.

    As for the questions asked in SGforums, most of them can be explained with "data analysis" and "economies of scale".

    Don't worry about the bus capacity. The demand is always constantly tracked by LTA, via some kind of data analysis system. If the demand exceeds a certain threshold, they will increase the capacity. That's the beauty of technology.

    For more information, research on "Internet of things (IoT)".

    By the way, this is "Power Engineering", not "Computer Engineering". Computer Engineerig is about robotics and automation, whereas Power Engineering is about observing trends and providing adequate capacity.
