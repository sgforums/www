    NUS/NTU bus services need improvement

    I feel that the bus services in NUS and NTU are not adequate. Only 179 and 199 penetrate into NTU, and NTU students can only go to NTU via Boon Lay only. As for NUS, the west side (e.g. Arts, SDE, Engin, etc) has access to a number of buses such as 96, 151, and other services serving Kent Ridge Terminal. However, the east side (science, medicine, etc) has very poor accessibility as it is only served by 95 (the frequency of 95 sucks and the bus is always overcrowded during peak hours). Student can take 97, 197, 198 or 963 and alight along AYE, but the walking distance between the bus stop and the nearest building is far.

    If you notice, the buses penetrating into NUS and NTU are all operated by SBS... If I'm working for SMRT, I will probably steal some market share and improve the company image by introducing new routes...

    For NTU, there should be a service to Choa Chu Kang via PIE and KPE, or a service to Bukit Batok via PIE, Bukit Batok Road and BB West Ave 3. This will facilitate students and staffs living in the North and Northwest and eliminate the need for them to travel all the way to Boon Lay and "squeeze" with other people on 179/199.

    For NUS, I would love a service between Kent Ridge and Harbourfront via Kent Ridge Crescent, Lower Kent Ridge Rd, AYE, Alexandra Rd, and Telok Blangah Rd (especially after the opening of Vivocity... LOL). Maybe this service can extend westwards from Kent Ridge to Boon Lay via AYE, or northwards from Kent Ridge to Woodlands via Clementi Rd, PIE and BKE, or combine with 189 to form a service from Bukit Batok to Harbourfront... Or the service can extend eastwards from Harbourfront to Marine Parade and Bedok South via ECP, or extend to Shenton Way and combine with 70 or 130 or 162 or 400 or 402....

    If SMRT or SBS is too lazy to introduce new services to NTU or NUS, the least they can do is to improve the frequency of 95. I have been suffering on 95 almost every day for the past 3 years and so far I never see any improvement... Currently, the frequency of 95 is around 8-15 minutes, which can be unbearable and unacceptable during peak hours... (Unlike 179, 95 cannot use double-deckers due to low bridges along Lower Kent Ridge Rd.) I feel that SBS doesn't seem to understand the NUS lesson timetable very well. Lessons normally start at 8am, 9am, 10am, 12pm, 2pm, 4pm and 6pm, and students tend to reach Buona Vista MRT 5-30 minutes before the lessons. So SBS should improve the frequency to 3-4 minutes during 0730-0800, 0830-0900, 0930-1000, 1130-1200, etc etc, and they can stick to their normal (slow) frequency outside these times...

    Recently, NUS introduced a free shuttle service between the Kent Ridge Campus and the Bukit Timah Campus (BTC1). Although BTC1 is fast and it is free, one thing I don't like about BTC1 is that it skips Lower Kent Ridge Rd (science, medicine, computing, NUH). Hence, if I want to go to the Bukit Timah campus from science faculty, I need to take a bus to Central Library and change to BTC1... It will be nice if NUS can amend BTC1 to serve Lower Kent Ridge Rd, or NUS can extend service C or D from science to Bukit Timah campus. Haha.
