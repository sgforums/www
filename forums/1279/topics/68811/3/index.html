    My friend (wdl_962), would like to clarify with all of
    you that after some quering from the PTC through
    online feedback, they stated that SBST and SMRT are
    FREE to operate into each others' territories and that
    THERE ARE NO SUCH RULES such as 2:1 towns like you all
    mentioned. One of the feedback replies is as follows:

    Dear Mr xxx,

    We refer to your email dated xx xxxx 04 on the above
    matter.

    Please allow us to share with you that SMRT Buses
    (formerly known as Tibs) was first allocated the
    northern sector of Singapore in the early 80s. The
    objective was to introduce healthy competition and
    allow benchmarking of bus operators' efficiency, cost
    effectiveness and service levels. When Sengkang and
    Punggol were developed, Tibs was allocated the
    responsibility to provide bus services to residents in
    these two new towns.

    However, with the award of the NEL to SBST Transit
    (SBST), Sengkang and Punggol towns were reallocated
    to SBST so that it could better integrate MRT, LRT and
    bus services in the Northeast corridor for the benefit
    of the residents there.In exchange, SBST had to
    relinquish Bukit Batok and Choa Chu Kang towns in the
    North-West sector. Bukit Batok and Choa Chu Kang towns
    were selected on the basis that the exchange would be
    equitable to both parties. Several factors were
    considered in the town selection, for example
    ridership figures and geographical proximity to Tibs'
    area of bus operations.

    Both SBST and SMRT Buses are assigned separate areas
    of responsibility for bus operations. Each is
    responsible to plan and put in operation their
    respective bus network and services to meet the
    mobility needs
    of commuters in their own area of responsibility. But
    this does not restrict the operators to operate bus
    services into other operator's territory and to use
    the terminal facilities there. There are also many
    examples
    of operators running their services from their own
    areas to common corridors such as CBD and Jurong.

    For your information, PTC in 2002 announced the
    opening up of feeder bus services in HDB towns for
    competition to ensure that commuters enjoy an
    affordable bus service that continues to improve over
    time(please refer to our website at www.ptc.gov.sg).
    PTC will strive to enhance the effectiveness of our
    regulatory framework including introducing more
    competition in the provision of bus services where
    appropriate.

    We hope the above address your concerns. Thank you
    for your feedback

    Yours sincerely,
    XXX

    Dear Ms XXXX

    Suggestion to reroute bus services

    We refer to your above feedback on 21 May 04 and our earlier
    acknowledgement.

    Our planners have carefully studied your suggestions to reroute our
    bus
    services and have reverted with their findings as follows :

    Service 111 was cutback to operate between Ghim Moh Terminal and
    Marina
    Centre to avoid duplication of resources since the Serangoon Road
    corridor is served by the NEL. At the same time, the change provided
    commuters from Commonwealth Avenue, Alexandra Road and Tanglin Road
    with
    a direct connection to Marina Centre. We thus have no immediate
    plans to
    integrate it with Service 23 as this would result in duplication as
    well
    as the loss of links with Marina Centre.

    For the suggested integration of Services 153 and 274, part of the
    proposed route along Alexandra Road and Queensway is presently
    served by
    Service 195 which also provides residents of Depot Road with a
    connection to Commonwealth and Queenstown Stations. As such, the
    benefits of such a merger is limited.

    Service 123 provides circumferential links to the Orchard Road
    corridor,
    Bukit Purmei, Tiong Bahru, River Valley at one end and Bukit Merah
    Central and Queensway from the other end. Service 36 on the other
    hand
    operates between Changi Airport to loop at Tomlinson Road. As such,
    there is no scope for the integration of Services 36 and 123.

    As for Service 502, we are presently reviewing its routeings with
    the
    plan to extend its coverage.

    Thank you for your feedback and we seek your understanding on this
    matter.
