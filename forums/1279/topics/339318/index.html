    3 Dec 08, 15:34

    Hi SMRT,

    The frequency for Service 966 is poor. With frequencies averaging 13 - 16 minutes during the AM Peak and up to 20 minutes during off peak hours, it is very frustrating for a lot of commuters, especially during the AM peak hour where a lot of commuters need to wait so long to take this bus to work / to school. 

    Many a time, I have seen commuters not able to board this service along Pending Road and Petir Road.

    This is in particular 630am to 700am (in Bukit Panjang Area) where a lot of CJC students need to get to school and working adults travelling towards the North East / East side of Singapore to work. The bus is cramped to the doors and often bus driver skips these 3 bus stops. 

    Another time was 800am to 900am (in Bukit Panjang Area) where the bus is cramped to the doors and commuters have to wait up to 45 minutes to board the bus (Because the bus skips their stop) and they end up making backup plans, such as taking 700 to cross over the bridge at Whitley Road and transfer to other SBST services to make their way to Jalan Toa Payoh and the East / North East.

    I personally have experienced it on 3 occassions while travelling to Toa Payoh on 966.
    I was waiting for 966 at the last stop (Blk 202) along Petir Road.
    18/11/08
    Missed the 815am (Bus was full and skipped my stop)
    Missed the 830am (Bus was full and skipped my stop once again)
    At 840am, I decided to board 700 instead, and transfer to 105 to get to Jalan Toa Payoh.

    25/11/08
    This time round, I became smarter and walked further to Blk 223 Bus Stop along Bukit Panjang Road
    Took the bus at 825am. Had to stand but I managed to board the bus.
    The bus skipped the Pending Road, Petir Road Bus Stops all the way to Jalan Toa Payoh.

    01/12/08
    My last bad experience was today. Wanted to travel to Jalan Kolam Ayer. Boarded at Blk 202 Bus Stop. Reached bus stop at 1241pm. Bus came at 109pm. I waited a total of 28 minutes for the bus. Isnt the frequency supposed to be at most 20 minutes during off peak hours? 

    I hope SMRT can look into this matter. Perhaps by deploying 2 more buses during the peak hour to smoothen the load and also improve overall frequency for Service 966. Bus is usually quite packed with commuters during peak hours, even for the Woodlands Bound 966 during PM Peak from 500pm to 1130pm.  

    Thanks & Regards,
    Mr. Lee Jun Yang
    Frustrated Commuter
    ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Dear Mr Lee
     
    We refer to your email below.
     
    We wish to inform you that we are aware of the high demand for service 966 and have been monitoring the service closely. Thus, with effect from 14 December 2008, we will be adding an additional bus to cater to passengers demand during peak periods.
     
    While we regulate the frequency from the interchange, factors such as adverse traffic conditions and heavy passenger loading may lead to prolonged waiting time at bus stops.  At SMRT, we continuously monitor our bus services and conduct site surveys on a regular basis to ensure that our services are able to meet the demand on the road. We will look into ways to optimise our resources and still able to obtain the efficiencies of each service. Please be assured that we will continue to monitor our bus services and make adjustments when necessary.
     
    Mr Lee, we apologize for the experience and thank you for highlighting it to us.
     


    Yours sincerely
     
     
    Gin Noy (Ms)
    Senior Officer, Customer Relations
    Corporate Marketing and Communications
    SMRT Corporation Ltd
     
    For more contact information on SMRT, please click on this link http://www.smrt.com.sg/contact_us.html
