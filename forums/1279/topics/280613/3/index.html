    1 Oct 07, 23:06
    Today managed to camp at BNL for 1.5hours, from 16.30 till 18.10 to see the operation. Here's what I think of the new service

    From passengers point of view

    Those people who board at BNL are severely disadvantaged. The frequency is reduced by more than half. The biggest gap I observed was 8 minutes from 17.30 onwards. The 16.00 period was still rather alright, with frequency at 4 - 5mins.
    At one point, the queue stretched from the new 179 berth (shared with 157, 199, 252) all the way to the 174e berth!

    However, those from NTU and those along Pioneer Rd Nth/JW St 63 benefit!
    Buses are not so unevenly loaded now, with the load being spread out among the buses. And the people along Pioneer Rd Nth/JW St 63 are able to board 179 buses.
    Previously, they had to face 4 or 5 full to the door 179 buses, before another 4 or 5 empty buses come bunching together.

    But heavy bus bunching is still present today...
  • stooper
    1 Oct 07, 23:26
    From operational point of view

    The timing and arrangement is EXTREMELY complicated! There are much more variations than just 179 and 179A!

    But first, about 242 and 255, they have tried to keep the timings of these services the same, so majority of the buses on these 2 services still depart on the same timings as the old timetable. The part severely changed is the 242 or 255 crossing over to 179 part. 9187L will do 179A for 2 or 3 trips, while the 255 buses will do 179A too (not sure how many trips).

    Back to 179, firstly, it seems that the buses doing 179A in PM peak are those which don't x-over to 242. But I'm not so sure whether this is completely true.

    There are actually at least 3 types of 179 services. (4th one unconfirmed yet)

    179 - one loop around BNL
    179 - down trip from inside NTU
    179A - standard departure from Hall 1
    179A - (unconfirmed) down trip from inside NTU

    Moreover, buses aren't fixed to just one type of svc.
    For example, 9092B did at least 2 trips of 179A in early PM peak, then switched to down trip of 179, then did pure 179 rounds.
    However, there are buses will do the reverse, like 9111E, 9128H, 9131Y! They do pure 179 rounds first, and then do 179A rounds later on, from 6pm, 7pm onwards! (as observed by brotherchen)

    I have no idea why SBST don't want to leave the 179A buses as they are, and the pure 179 buses as they are, so BC don't need to keep changing destos.

    Buses doing 179A consist of both A/P and S shift buses. E shift not so sure.

    But the biggest joke I think is the timesheet arrangement. 179 I believe has the strictest timetabling of all services.
    179 now has 9 regulating points on the timesheet for a 30 - 38min round! This is compared to other svcs like 334, which was 3 fare stage points for 45mins, 333 with 5 points for 50min, 255 with 7 points for 55min.
    It's rather ironic since even today, the BCs were chionging like crazy, ignoring the timesheet.

    The timesheets are now super long, only being able to fit 6 complete 179 rounds on one page. I think there may be a 6 - 8 page long A/P shift timesheet., or maybe a 4 page long E shift timesheet! Maybe someone can try and take a look.

    So the 179 arrnangement is super complicated, requiring all buses to carry 179 and 179A front destos, 179 and 179A AM peak, 179A PM peak side destos. So lots of destos required! Maybe 242 or 255 too!
