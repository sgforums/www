    Yes the oil pipe/similar parts leakage is much of a concern as well...

    Not only 'batch one' Habits, but 'batch two' (and also other variants of O405) have seen such problems as well.

    Engine overheating is also one issue. Some bus captains identify the cooling units as 'fans' and when the water temperature goes up they noted that 'one of the four fans are not working', and performance of the vehicle would decrease once water temperature goes above 90 degrees. Vehicle will not move off due to safety catch once its around 100 degrees. Also known as 'boil water' in Mandarin.

     Correct me if the following information is not correct:

    For the Mercedes vehicle (noted on 'box' bendy), there is a warning lamp at the left most, top corner of the main cluster of lamps in the dashboard. If there are any thing wrong with the dials/system, the lamp would light up (check lamp?). There would not be audible tones.

    For Scania (noted on L113CRL), there is a lamp and continuous single tone warning.
