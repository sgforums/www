    Ang Mo Kio Ave 1, 3, 6 & 8: Red roof tops and green metal rectangular bars.
    They are the first bus stops in Singapore to be revamped since the orange-white bus stops era. A total of 100 bus stops, or 50 pairs, in the CBD and AMK were selected for the new bus stop design trial, which had included fans & even water coolers powered by solar panels. The additional features were eventually found to be a waste of public resources, and the solar panels were too expensive to install yet ineffective & inefficient in generating power.

    Ang Mo Kio Ave 4, 5(Bef CTE) & 10(Sth), St 11, 13, 52, 53, 54, 61, 62, 63, 64: Red roof tops and yellow metal bars and circular bars unpainted.
    These are the first-generation design by JCDecaux after opinions were gathered regarding the initial trial design.

    Ang Mo Kio Ave 3(Opp Big Mac Ctr), 10 (Nth), Ave 5 (Aft CTE): New bus stops. Grey ones.
    These are the second-generation design by JCDecaux.

    Ang Mo Kio Ave 2: Orange and pale yellow stripes (1980s type of bus stops)
    They are bus stops that are not upgraded yet.

    Ang Mo Kio Ave 5: Pair of bus stops (Opp/Bef Ind Pk 2) Unique design. Silver/Blue Green/Yellow bus stop.
    They are most probably a on-trial design or built by the landlord of a nearby industrial building as part of the overall design to fit into the colour scheme & asthetics of the building.

    Bus Stops finding report in the North West. Dated 22/5/2004.

    Bukit Panjang: Red roof tops and with brown walls. Same structure as AMK bus stops.

    Choa Chu Kang: Have they been repainted? Grey paint has ripped off from some bus stops and blue patches hae appeared. Grey rounded roof top with structure like the pair of outstanding bus stops along AMK Ave 5.

    Bukit Batok: 2 types of bus stops. One type is the new bus stops in majority neighbourhoods in Bt Batok. Another type seen along Bukit Batok West Ave 2 (bef Bukit Batok Ctrl). Same structure as the bus stops of AMK St 52/53/54 except the bus stop is flat roofed and light blue-green based walls.
