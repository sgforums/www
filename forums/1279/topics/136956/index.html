  • hseochin
    14 Jun 05, 22:55
    I won't consider those Nice2 as 2-storey, as the lower floor is of standing height only for small children, not regular adults !

    Nice 2 entire fleet i'll say is '1.5' storeys coach, where the overall height is only slightly higher than a high-deck single decker coach.

    For true double-decker in more recent times in Malaysia , i'll consider those used with MAN 24.460 chassi with 3-axles. Both floors are of adult standing height here.

    I believe why double-deckers didn't catch on in Malaysia was because of the sceptism from some people in Malaysia then that the (likely mostly British) double-deckers had inferior road handling capabilities compared to single deckers, something which i do believe is still the case with modern double-deckers, simply due to their higher c.g. over single deckers. Of course in the double-deckers of today, these have greatly improved active safety margins with the increasing use of electronic air-suspension control, ABS, ASR etc. which i won't expect to find in a Leyland Atlantean !!

    So slowly now you see the re-emergence of the double-decker in Malaysia. although so far not yet in citybus operations again. With better buses available today, i won't rule out KL having double-decker citybuses again, with MAN being a probable choice.
