    Memories for 803 were the most complete of all Yishun Services because I used to take it everyday.

    Before the farecard trial, service 800 looked something like this:

    - TIB231H
    - TIB232E
    - TIB243Z
    - TIB250C

    During and after the farecard trial, service 800 became:

    - TIB245T (spare bus with validator, not perm 800)
    - TIB246R
    - TIB247M
    - TIB248K
    - TIB249H
    - TIB250C

    I dun remember anything about service 801 until someone can give me hints...

    Service 802 included TIB's 39Y, 41P and 42L initially, but later it became:

    - TIB44G
    - TIB154Y
    - TIB157P
    - TIB233C
    - TIB235Y
    - TIB243Z (since 800's farecard trial)

    I have mentioned service 803 already, so shall not repeat them here.

    Service 804... its history was also very complicated, but it was the most spoilt service of its time in Yishun, second only to 851, 852 and 854 back then. It started with some obscure RK176's (I saw TIB69K and TIB115K), but quickly sucked up all the best UD's from services 167 and 850 very soon:

    - TIB198X
    - TIB234A
    - TIB311K
    - TIB312H
    - TIB314C
    - TIB316Y
    - TIB317U
    - TIB318S
    - TIB319P

    When TIB's 311K to 317U became CAC, 311, 312, 314, 316 and 317 were replaced by:

    - TIB281M
    - TIB282K
    - TIB283H
    - TIB284E
    - TIB289S

    Thats what I think happened to 804.. chronology and/or order not sure but the rego's are correct.
