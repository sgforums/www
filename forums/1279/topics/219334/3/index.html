    Buses and trains are built to last, about 30 years at least. While we have a 17 year lifespan for public buses, theres no such restriction on trains. The C151 are still working well despite being 20 years old due to excellent maintenance by the SMRT crew. In other less fortunate countries, trains of such age would be a nightmare to see, let alone ride. SMRT has already taken the initiative to refurbish the C151 and spent a lot of money too. Why they didn't use the money to buy new stocks, you may ask. Well, phasing out and bringing in 77 trains is no walk in the park.
