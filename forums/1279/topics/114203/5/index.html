    a example of Mk IV DM3500's.

    2689B-2788Z: $190k - $200k
    2789X-2838M: $182,322

    how come DM3500's are cheaper? the reason lies behind the fact that they were delivered (and registered!) in bulk - they were registered en masse on x'mas day 2001. and the total cost of these 50 DM3500's is jus nice: $9 million.

    see the power of bulk purchase? interestingly, most buses of the same type, if identical, tend to get cheaper if u buy more and more. but there are few such exmaples - bus companies will tend to increase cost by retrofitting buses with better equipment and adding other chassis components to fine-tune the buses to meet their operating requirements, thus this savings is often off-set or even results in more expensive but better buses.

    also, i'd like to claify the 50% margin. it's tat between a propotype and subsequent mass purchases. if u see the above example, the savings is only about 5%, though $10k per bus is a lot.
