    The N113 is a rear engined chassis, no doubt. But the engine is mounted in a vertical-transverse position. In other words, the engine position is similar to that of the Olympians.

    On Scania chassii, the first letter denotes the engine position. AFAIK, Scania only has rear engined models.

    N = vertical placement, transverse driveline
    L = tilted at 60 degrees to the left, longitudinal driveline
    K = vertical placement, longitudinal driveline

    The 2 or 3 digits that follows denotes the engine capacity and the chassis generation.

    eg.
    113 = 11 litre, 3rd gen
    94 = 11 litre, 4th gen

    The last 3 character denoted the chassis type

    eg.
    CRB = single deck/coach, right-hand drive, normal floor
    CRL = same as above but with low entrance

    For the 4th gen chassii, only 2 characters are used

    eg.
    IB = intercity chassis
    UB = urban chassis
    UA = urban chassis, articulated
