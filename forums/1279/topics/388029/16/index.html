    More bus trips, bigger buses

    TWENTY heavily-used bus services will get more buses and bigger buses from Monday. The North-East MRT line will also get 120 more train trips per week.

    Transport Minister Raymond Lim announced this during his visit to the Distance Fares roadshow at Boon Lay Bus Interchange on Saturday.

    These efforts to ease crowded buses and trains followed the recent Budget debates, during which Mr Lim had said the Land Transport Authority (LTA) will work with the operators to improve capacity on busy bus services.

    'These improvements underscore our collective commitment to enhance both the bus and rail services for the benefit of commuters, wherever possible,' said Mr Lim.

    The twenty bus routes will get 71 additional trips a day. Double decker and bendy buses will also be deployed instead of the usual single-decked short buses.

    However, these measures will only kick in during what the operators termed as 'peak of peak' hours. The 'peak of peak' period - an half-hour duration anytime between 7am and 9am, and between 5.30pm and 7.30pm - varies depending on the bus routes.

    Of the 20 bus routes, half of them are run by SBS Transit and the other half by SMRT.

    -- ST
