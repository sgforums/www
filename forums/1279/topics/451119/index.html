    Managed to find copies of it kept at library...

    Seems like it's kinda of the basics of the bus ops today...

    1) Bus makes should be kept to 3 for easier maintenance. The bigger the bus, the better. Auto transmission is preferred.

    2) In the report, OMO was alrdy mooted.

    3) SBS should follow KMB's practice, allow full bus ads.

    4) SBS should have a sizeable pool of mechanics, similiar to the headcount in KMB.

    5) Busy bus stops should be staggered whenever possible.

    6) Buses were poorly maintained, proper depts should be set up. In fact, some NSFs were posted to SBS to repair buses in the early years. *But this issue however, was more stated in the report written by SAF (Singapore Armed Forces).

    7) Overcrowded bus depots: fire hazards.

    Ironically, one of the GTO was Mr Mah Bow Tan (Engineer in MINDEF).

  • SBS8533C
    23 Apr 12, 05:50

    Full bus ads in Singapore were banned by the ROV in the past. In the 1980s, ROV had ordered SBS to remove a full body ads on a double decker bus as it is banned. Only desto-sized, side & rear panel adverts were allowed.

    When the ROV regulations are relaxed, SBS begun putting full body ads on double deckers, after a few years, the front body must retain the company's livery as compared in the past, whereby full body ads cover the entire body, including the front.
