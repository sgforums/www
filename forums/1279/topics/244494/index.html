    25 Mar 07, 01:31
    Sv. 99
    In the beginning (198 - Mercedes O305, short period. Replaced fully by Leyland Olympian 2x by mid-year.
    1989-1990 - Leyland Oympian 2x fleet replaced by full MB OF1413 with the occasional MB OF1417, possibly on cameo. No DDs during this period.
    1990 - Return of the DDs with about half of the fleet comprising of Leyland Atlanteans (Metsec/L-type/R-type). The other half were made up of Volvo B57. Occasional cameos of Volvo B10M Mk-II.
    1993 - Old Leylands phased out, only the R-type remained. Svc became PAC with the AC fleet made up of Volvo B10M Mk-III.
    1995 - ADDs made its debut with the Volvo Olympian 3x.
    1997 - Atlanteans withdrawn. Remaining NAC buses were worked by Volvo Olympian 2x.
    1998 - Svc became FAC. Fleet now comprised of Volvo Olympian 3x and Volvo B10M Mk-IV Strider. B10M DM3500, B10M Mk-III and Leyland Olympian 3x are also frequently spotted and sometimes a Volvo B10M Mk-II CAC will make an appearance.

    Service 178 when converted to PAC service in 1995 had MB0405 OAC buses, together with occasional Nissan U31 SCN CAC (TIB311K-TIB317U) and Hino HT328K CAC (TIB380K-TIB389L). DAFs were eventually used too. The NAC Nissans were withdrawn from service 178 when this service was a few to be converted to FAC in Feb 2007. At that time service 178 used Nissan U31 RCN OAC, MB0405s, DAFs and Hino HT328Ks from KJDEP if I am not wrong.

    When WLDEP came into control in 1998/1999, Scania WAs entered into service 178's fleet. DAFs were eventually withdrawn after WLDEP gained full control on service 178. The NAC Nissan U31 SCNs reappeared on service 178 once again after service 178 revert back to PAC in Sept 1999, the NAC Scania ELBOs were also deployed on service 178.

    In year 2001 onwards, service 178 then became a full Scania fleet with occasionally OAC Nissan cameos. This deployment lasted till 2004 when all the Scanias were eventually replaced by Nissans (OAC and the new additions of CAC Nissans from CSS). In 2006 service 178 became a full Nissan fleet.
