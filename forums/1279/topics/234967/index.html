    1/2 right only.
    Intercooler is not merely a devise. It's something like a radiator.

    For a turbo intercooler: When the exhaust propels the impeller to turn, it in turn rotates the turbine connected to it. When the turbine rotates, the air is sucked in faster thus improving the introduction of fresh air and expelling of exhaust fumes. When the air is sucked in, the air is too hot and thus hinders good combustion. Thus the hot air runs across a circulation of coolant in pipings/fins to reduce the temperature of the air. This helps to give better combustion even though with force fed air.

    There're cheaper ways to make force fed air. SRAD (Suzuki Ram Air Direct) a very old way of using the air gushing through the air pipe when the motorcycle runs. The gushing air is forced through an air filter before feeding into the throttle body & into the engine. In this way, the air pipes facing the front whereby it collects the gushing air when the motorcycle is moving becomes a form of turbo. But because there's no hot air, the intercooler is not needed.

    For a gearbox intercooler, coolant is circulated inside pipings/fins while gearbox ATF is outside the pipings/fins. In this way, the coolant which has an advantage of going to the radiator to dissipate heat collects the ATF heat and dissipates it at the radiator.
