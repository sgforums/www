    Err, you're partially right on this, but it's not the only reason too. 

    Basically, from what I noticed, it boils down to several factors:
    1) the no. of WABs vs non-WABs in the perm fleet (need at least 60% WABs in the fleet to hit the quota)
    2) whether the BCs are already trained for Persons-in-Wheelchair (PIW) ferrying etc.
    3) whether the stops along the route are able to pick up PIWs (although nowadays LTA like to give exceptions to selected bus stops if the bus stop is not suitable for PIW boarding)
    4) whether the Interchanges themselves are capable of PIWs boarding or not.

    And point 4) is actually the most critical factor why many of the svcs recently aren't granted WAB status yet. (I'm talking mostly on SBST ones, SMRT apparently live by their own set of standards for WAB statuses).

    Best eg would be Sv 8 and Sv 81 which share the same type of head-in berths at Tampines Int. Granted that Sv 8 still needs 1 more WAB (17/29 = 58.6%), while Sv 81 already passes that quota (12/16 = 75%). But the main issue with them not getting the WAB status even though they've gotten their KUBs for years, lies with the current head-in berths not being PIW-friendly (after all, PIWs board from the rear doors, not the front doors). 

    So yeah, just some food for thought, I believe that all the 4 reasons I mentioned play a collective role in determining when they would grant WAB status to the services. Maybe there's other reasons for consideration which I left out, but my point is, no one reason is the sole source of the issue. Cheers.
