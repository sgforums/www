    Originally posted by tintinspartan:


    KJDEP can do anything with their S-shift buses. If the bus meant for the s-shift that 1H was on just now not available due to various reasons, they could just deploy any bus to relief the other bus. For example, SMB1H did 67 or 970 in the morning and went back depot. Starter may just anyhow put it anywhere for the PM-Spilt. It isn't necessary to deploy the same bus that did the AM Split to do the PM Split. Ends up, it come out on 172 as what it happened today.

    'Anyhow' should not be used, however, appropriate usage by the starter in the depot is what counts.

    Being tactful in words is important. No company does things 'the anyhow way'.

    Based on operation viewpoint, the bus have to be suitable for the BC, a bus that the BC is trained on, then he can take the bus out. If the BC is not trained in the OC then it would not be feasible for him to take it out.

    Waybills bearing the same bus of the AM section of split can be assigned back to the same BC when he collects the bus at the depot in the PM section of split, if it has not been taken earlier or has defects demanding repairs.
