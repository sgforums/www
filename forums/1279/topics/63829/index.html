  • foxtrot1985

    Another TIBS bus caught fire today...
    ~~~
    SINGAPORE : A Trans-Island bus driver and his passengers escaped unhurt after the vehicle caught fire on Wednesday along Riverside Road in Woodlands.

    Raging flames engulfed feeder service number 903, leaving only its frame.

    The driver had noticed smoke coming from the engine at about 8am.

    He immediately stopped the bus and evacuated some 60 passengers from the bus.

    The blaze got so large that it even spread to the bus stop.

    SMRT, the parent company of TIBs, said they were investigating the cause of the fire.

    This is the second time a TIBS bus has suddenly caught fire this year.

    It is unlikely any part will be salvaged. Reconstruction is worthwhile only when the fire damage is confined to one area e.g. the engine or when the damage is largely confined to the area above the floorboard.

    As evident from the TV footage the fire had spread to the faceplate area, which means the flames burnt through the whole length of the underside. The only thing probably left is a very brittle and powdery chassis backbone, anything that came with the chassis are probably destroyed, including the wirings, transmissions, aircon units, suspension, articulator etc which add up to be pretty much 80 or 90% of the bus' value.
