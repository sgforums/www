    Ever since SBST started 168 and 161 in Woodlands, the service by TIBS had been forced to improve as well. 969 and 965 are the consequence of the 2 services. The intoduction of VSOs had also forced TIBS to deploy better buses, such as Volgren 0405Gs on 969.

    Do you think that if SBST is allowed to operate in Yishun, the same improvement would also occur?, since the TIBS operations in Yishun is passable at best. According to the PTC, there is a regulation that for every x households, there must be a trunk service.

    However, in the Nee Soon East constituency, the largest SDC in Singapore, the only trunk is the rather pathetic 520 through the lower half of the Ring Rd. after Avenue 9. However, most of the blocks are clustered in the part between Ave. 9 and Ave. 11, but there are only 2 feeder services.

    However, 806 does not even operate during Sundays and PH, citing 'low pax deamnd'. This results in tremendously long queues during Sundays and PH for 804, yet the obstinate TIBS, after so many years, still cite the low pax demand.

    I feel that a trunk passing through Yishun Ring Rd. between Ave. 11 and Ave. 9 by SBST may be in order, and would force TIBS to improve their services, despite the PTC restriction which doesn't even really hold now about the control area. What do you'll think?

    This is because svc 804 have to make a U-turn along Yishun Avenue 1 to go back to the interchange. The moderate, but fast traffic with speeding trucks and cars make it difficult for buses to turn into the L-shaped thing to reverse, and the length is insufficient to accomodate Bendys.

    FYI, TIBS did try deploying Bendys on 804 in 1999, and I happen to be on board when the bus made the U-turn. If you think that the 3 point reversing at Harbourfront is serious enough, this is worse. Basically, the bus driver missed the narrow entrance to the L-shaped thingy and had to reverse, and effectively dammed the road, and after some hair-raising struggle, he went into the L-shaped thing, but ended up stuck there. If I remember correctly, there is hedge or poles at the end to prevent overshooting. The thing is designed for a 12m 0405, not a 17+m 0405G. Thus, there is that trailer hanging out in the road and caused a bit of hold-up. I dun know how the driver did it before my trip, but I guessed he attempted an articulator-breaking turn. Anyway, I think the bus behind got the inspector with him to and helped to direct traffic and the Bendy driver while he reverse on the whole road. It took 10 minutes before the bus got going to the next bus stop. After that, 0405Gs are history on 804.

    However, there are still Bendys deployed on 804 today, especially in the afternoon, using 806 Bendys. The route is similar to the old 804A, where the bus expressed from the interchanged to the Blk 356 bus stop to pick up its first passengers, and follows the normal 804 route after that.
