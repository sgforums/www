    bus interchange versus bus terminal.

    I do not know if anyone has realised this. The terms "interchange" and "terminal" are quite same-same, but different.

    same-same: both have bus interchange facilities, a bus bay, bus interchange canteen, etcetera.

    different: "terminal" not air-conditoned, and less boarding/alighting activities. "interchange" air-conditioned and more boarding/alighting activities.

    Based on changi business park bus terminal and the upcoming tuas bus terminal, it seems that the term "terminal" only means a facility that has limited or less boarding/alighting activities (than a "interchange") and it is not air-conditioned.

    It seems that this is the new definition of "terminal", unlike in the past when a terminal means a container office, some parking lots and some portable toilets.

    but of course, as now is still the transition phase for all bus interchanges to become air-conditioned, "terminal"s and the old/temporary "interchange"s tend to look alike.
