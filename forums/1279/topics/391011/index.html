    Believe it's not only to save fuel. It also is a rule by LTA that buses are to only run at a maximum of 60km/h. But personally, some services need to run so they need to go at a faster rate. I've ever boarded 858, 965 and 969 buses going at 60km/h, and the rides are horribly pathetic and some SLs complain they will be late for their schedule. Safety comes first, but their timings are also important. I see 61 buses on Sundays running like crazy just to meet their time, and I can hear the speed limiter for the AMDEP 61 rigids going off often till the SL needs to jerk the bus. 

    Basically, the slow acceleration of buses can be due to fuel saving and also the scheduling of buses. The bus companies either give too much or too little time to the bus services. For SBS Transit, because they've got plentiful supply of buses, most of their services travel at a slow speed, which explain for their slow acceleration. As for SMRT, some services go slowly to not overshoot the timings or else the SL needs to answer. And others fly like crazy just so that they meet the timings and so that their buses do not get overloaded.
