    From my personal experiences riding the M-B O 405 over 15 years, i find that overall the O 405 provides the best travel experience among the SBS fleet of current vehicles.

    The issue of abnormal, jerky gearshifts i've also experienced but these are strictly the exception rather than the norm. Bearing in mind the large population of O 405 in SBS fleet (approx. 700 units) the absolute no. of units with such gearbox problems of course seems 'many' but one must take into consideration their large population here !

    I do recall taking the O 405 brand-new (SBS 82 P on route 76) 15 years ago & the vehicle was slowly climbing up Boundary Road with full-load of passengers & the gear change was 'seamless' i.e. cannot even detect the noise or vibration when the gear changed & only the drop in engine speed confirmed the gear changed !! That's the 'normal' standard of M-B gearbox behavior which also blends superbly with the excellent suspension ride comfort, which to this day ranks with the best & unsurpassed.

    I also do recall taking an O 405 this time 15 years later just a few months back
    (route 133 using Duple-Metsec body) this unit also demonstarted the 'normal'
    M-B standard of 'seamless' gear-change mentioned above !

    The majority of times i've taken the O 405 over the past 15 years..the gearchange performance was somewhere in between the 2 'extremes' i.e. between 'seamless' to jerky etc. but MOSTLY smooth enough to be 'inperceptable' i.e. overall still smooth & certainly not inferior to the other makes & models of buses here, irrespective of age.

    Therefore what i can conclude is that maintenance & driver competence also do play very significantly in the gearchange performance & consistent top class maintenance (which i'm honestly NOT sure was always given !) do utimately make the difference between you get a smooth ride or a jerky one !

    The O 405 is no doubt a fine class citybus, 1st world standard & still there even in Germany nowadays in service BUT to achieve that, sufficient maintenance must be given with no corner cutting !

    For info, this issue of jerky gearchanges i've also experienced on the Saab-Scania N113CRB here. Those were more 'violent' though not noisy. I observed some drivers tend to release the accelerator @ the 'wrong' time (presumably because they scared they gone too fast !) just when the gear changed so that
    the gearbox seemed 'undecided' which gear to stay in etc. hence the jerkyness.
    I do believe if they held on the ride would have on the whole been smoother with less obvious gear changes !

    In those cases, i observe the engine idling speed becomes too low whenever the air-con compressor comes on, thereby causing unneccessary noise & vibrations from the engine. I'll then blame it on the idling speed being set not taking into account the effect of an air-con compressor in-use, like in cold countries without air-con. I'll expect the idling speed to be raised a little (even if that wastes little more fuel) so that when compressor comes on, the resultant engine idling speed does not fall below the original minimum level !!
    Then no abnormal noise or vibrations. With high mileage use, settings may have changed through normal wear & tear so require re-setting like you tighten some screw & it becomes loose after few million km, so you need to re-adjust & re-tighten etc.

    If not due to air-con compressor effect, then i believe is due to gearbox's idler shaft not taking away the excess torque while the bus is stationary, thereby 'passing it back' to the engine, resulting in an effect like some malfunctioning exhaust brake which 'forces' the engine to run below the required idling speed, thus causing excessive noise & vibrations from the engine. Modern automatic gearboxes like on the O 405 are known to last 1000000 km or longer between servicing/replacement so maybe time to renew the gearbox or least give it it's due overhaul !
