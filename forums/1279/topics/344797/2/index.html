    The additional 2 slots are given P shifts only, 19P & 20P, they don't have A shifts for the slots. Means driver takes bus direct from SLBP to start work. Perhaps service_238 can elaborate on what's the reason behind in adding P shifts but not fullday slots.


    Sometimes adding P shift doesnt mean that night demand is more than the day. Dont forget in the day there's the S/E shifts to cover. In a sense, if you put the morning S shift and the P shift together - you get a full day slot less the period between 1100-1500hrs.

    PM S shifts in this way helps in the A/P meal break and the run time during pm peak, given that frequency stays the same and run time difference is not very significant. That is why sometimes for some services during morning peak the S shifts has no driver due to shortage but there's a driver during PM peak due to A shift drivers doing OT.
