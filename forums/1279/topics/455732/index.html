    3. Stepless aisle 

    The aisle is stepless for safe and convenient movement within the bus

     In order to meet the increasing passenger demand, bus operators in Singapore have tried hard to introduce buses with huge capacities: The introduction of 12m air-con double-decked "Super Bus" of SBS Transit is a remarkable success. Nevertheless, road conditions are sometimes so harsh that the allocation of double-deckers to certain highly demanded routes is not applicable. In this case, articulated buses could be one of the best solutions: SBS Transit and its major competitor, TIBS, brought in articulated buses in 1996. Mercedes Benz O405G first came to service with TIBS in March 1996, followed by SBS Transit's Volvo articulated B10M which started service in late April in the same year. 

        SBS Transit's articulated Volvo B10M, which is regarded as "Asia's Longest Bus", is 19m in length. It is capable of carrying 147 passengers, with 54 seating capacity and 93 standing capacity. The power is from a Volvo THD102KF engine, rated at 245bhp, posterior to the front axle. It is then transmitted through a ZF4HP590 gearbox to the second axle which drives both the tractor and trailer. It is worth noticing that there are two options for the third axle on the trailer: The first one is a steered trailer axle which is able to support 8 tonnes of load at maximum. It gives the best performance on steering. The second one is a rigid trailer axle, which means the load can be up to 10.5 tonnes at maximum. SBS Transit finally adopted the first option. Note that the location of axles of articulated Volvo B10M is different from that of MB O405G.

        After a series of tests and practical services, SBS Transit finally decided to introduce more air-con double-deckers to strengthen its fleet. The first and the only articulated Volvo B10M of SBS Transit, SBS998Y, used to serve on Service 290 before its cancellation. It is now frequently seen on trunk route 18, which is a loop service between Tampines and Bedok Interchange. It has been painted in the new livery of SBS Transit since early 2003.
