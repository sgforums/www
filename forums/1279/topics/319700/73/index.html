    Aluminium coaches drive Gemilang's rapid growth
    By Sim Bak Heng
    Published: 2008/11/24


    COACH builder Gemilang Coachwork Sdn Bhd's investment in aluminium technology is paying off handsomely as orders for their lighter and more durable coaches are rising. It more than tripled revenue to RM100 million in the financial year to October 31 2008, from RM30 million a year ago.

    Its impressive growth was due to a factory expansion completed in April this year, and the increasing need for fuel-saving coaches by bus companies.

    Aluminium-made coaches are lighter by between half and one tonne each than conventional coaches, which weigh 10 tonnes each. They are also more durable as they are rust-proof and can go longer distance.

    Gemilang managing director Pang Chong Yong said the firm imported the Australian technology, the first and only one in the country, in early 1990s and fine-tuned the technology with a UK-based firm.

    "We linked up with Alusuisse Aluminium of Switzerland in 2000 and became the first and only licence holder of their aluminium technology in coach-building in the Far East. "They supply us with the technology and material while we design and build our coaches," he told Business Times in an interview.

    Singapore makes up 80 per cent Gemilang's market while the rest comes from Malaysia and Hong Kong. Plans are afoot to penetrate Taiwan and China.

    According to Pang, the company is keen to supply buses to RapidKL, the state-owned bus operator, which currently sources its vehicles from China.

    Gemilang started in 1989 by building bus chassis in Bandar Baru Uda in Johor Baru. It shifted to Seelong in Senai five years later and started making factory, school and tour buses. Since then, it decided to specialise in bus body development and has its own design team to make coaches ranging from single deck to one-and-a-half deck coaches.

    Prior to the regional economic downturn in 1997, the company built coaches solely for the local market but poor demand forced it to look overseas. It clinched a RM25 million deal with Perth-based Bus & Coach International Pte Ltd in 1999 to build 120 buses, and this earned the firm a reputation for being the first local bus builder to win a contract to build coaches for the Aussie market.
