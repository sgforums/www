    24 May 04, 08:53
    I vividly remember a very weird advertisement on DDs back in 1992. On one side there were words like "I SEE", "I HEAR", "I LIKE" etc. and on the other side were the words "NOW U CAN" with a big red letter U. I was scrathing my head trying to figure out what it was all about. It was not until much, much later that the words "PANASONIC - NOW U CAN" came out. Geez.

    And who can forget the "CARRIER MAN CAN" ad. This ad was the first to be painted wholly on a single-deck bus. There were 2 buses, I think which had this. One was a Scania on sv. 16 and the other was a Mk-II B10M on sv. 174. These 2 buses created such a confusion that it was featured in the papers. People thought they weren't SBS buses and so didn't take them. That is why the front of today's buses were left unpainted.

    On Atlanteans, there was one ad I liked which was for MAS. It had the caption "We're going to Kuching, are you?" and had lots of cute cats on it. (Kuching is Malay for cat). Another one I liked was a blue ad for ESSO which had a picture of a tiger.

    The B10MD was painted a couple of times. Once, it's bottom half was light blue and the top half was white. It was for celebrating Singapore's 25 Years of Nation Building cum Volvo's 50th Anniversary. Another time it was black and white emphasizing that "Transport Is Vital - SBS Makes The Difference". Then it was all black with an ad for "Sony Trinitron". The last time I saw it, it was red and white for "Indocafe".
