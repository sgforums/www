    Erm, I think you're looking too far ahead. Currently what most depots are concentrating on is on beefing up the WAB fleets for already-certified WAB services, because of complaints that WAB buses do not arrive during scheduled WAB slots. 2, 7, 12, 14, 21, 130, 196, 198, 232, 268 are just some of the services that have received KUBs replacing non-WAB buses. It's up to individual depots to decide how to deploy their new buses, some choose the triangle method (For example, EEV goes into Sv3 & Sv19 replacing Euro IV buses, which then go onto Sv12) or direct deployment (Can't think of any example yet but there will be some soon). Just because certain depots are taking in massive numbers of EEV doesn't mean they have immediate plans to clear off old buses. You've got to look at depot size constrains too. You know why AM/BR only takes in EEVs a few at a go, then stop? Their depot is too packed.

    I'm not saying what you're saying is wrong but your picture is too big. Look at current situations first. Who knows that in the future those non-WAB buses that were replaced eventually replaces outgoing buses.
