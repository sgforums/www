    School kids, the elderly etc. who don't use private cars it's 'implictly' assumed that these lot are catered for by others who drive a car be it parents, friends, helpers etc.

    Of course no situation would ever be 'perfect' so there'll ultimately be some above who would unfortunately be 'left out' ! The ideal goal i'll say is to minimise people in such situations but this being an imperfect world there'll always be some in this boat.

    In this real world NOTHING would ever be perfect. I do NOT believe such a state can/would ever be achieved anywhere BUT people can progress closer to perfection, that's all. So i don't believe in such a thing as perfect public transport system.

    However that does NOT mean complacency can set in with no bothers to make improvements ! My central point has always been in making relative comparisons among the various alternatives to public transport WITHIN a certain country & comparing it to another such relative comparisions from another country, irrespective whether that other place is overall more 'advanced' or not. This way, differences in income levels & purchasing power of money etc. would have been taken into consideration & 'cancelled out' as such, leaving more clearly the 'nett picture' for people to figure if indeed improvements can/should be made in their own countries to make up for any shortcomings when compared this way with another country's.

    The bottom line would be like say the cost of taking bus here is so much & the cost of taking the alternative forms of transport here would be so much. (In the 1st case) If the costs of the private alternatives seem cheap compared to the bus within the same place, then i'll consider it as being less of a case for complains/improvements. However (in the 2nd case) if the costs of the alternative transport seem expensive relative to the bus within the same place being evaluated, then it's more of a case for complains/improvements ! Having understood these, therefore that's why i consider places like Malaysia, Australia, Germany in case 1 while Singapore i have to put under case 2 !!!!
    If private car costs in Singapore are as cheap as in these other places, then only i place Singapore under case 1 i.e. then less case for complains/improvements !

    As for any increases in bus fares, i've already written number of posts since quite long time ago in this & perhaps other forums saying why such a situation need NOT develop. That's i'll classify under another subject area so i'll not repeat myself here.

    Since 20 years ago the articulated double-decker existed, but in coach form i.e. only seated passengers. But i don't know of any ever used as citybus with standing passengers.

    The 'problem' of double-decker design i'll say (whether rigid or articulated) is that the c.g. tends to be on the high side, obviously not so good in active safety aspects ! So while the single-deck articulated bus can be relatively more dynamically stable & almost as swift & flexible like a snake, the double-deckers tend to be more top heavy, directionally clumsy as such hence more dynamically unstable in comparison.

    Unless there's some breakthroughs in automotive technology (likely achieved thru high-tech electronics in the suspension system i'll say) which reduces/eliminates the inferior stability up to now inherent with double-decker body designs, then i don't believe there's likely to see articulated double-deckers as citybuses with seating & standing passengers on both floors !
    The most might be standing & seating on the lower deck with only seating no standing on the upper deck, but in that case then the overall passenger capacity on even an articulated double-decker may not reach your 300 figure.

    On the more practical front, i notice the new M-B CapaCity 4-acle single-deck articulated (purpose built) city-bus can take in around 200 passengers in Europe i.e. expected with the more generous European allowances for seating spaciousness, implying that if such allowances were somewhat reduced to account for the 'smaller' physical size of Asians here, then the passenger capacity can be increased somewhat beyond 200 to 1 driver ratio ?!! making it the closest to your 300 figure compared to any other design of city-bus currently here.
