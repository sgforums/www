    In Beijing here, though the frequency is much better than singapore for quite a large number of routes, packed bus still occur, sometimes because people do not want to move in too. Most buses in beijing have a conductor on board so they will shout and shout fiercely for people to move in. Although most buses do not allow boarding from the back  door, some people will just squeeze in from the backdoor. Some of their NON WAB buses have lesser seats than our scania k230s also. Evading fare is not really an issue here as some conductors dont care when the bus is crowded and there are hundreds of ways to cheat fare, ranging from an adult using a student concession card (paying SGD 4 cents per 12km or less trip) and not tapping their card when they board for certain service where the driver is at the front door and the conductor is at the back door controlling traffic, where the driver think you will buy the ticket from the conductor and the conductor is unsure if u have tapped your card (For real crowded cases like 190 960 case)

    There was once i was at some rather rural area near a tourist attraction, there was an accident that occupied the whole road and when the police finally managed to clear the jam, i saw 2 sv331 (Full bendy fleet) passing by fully packed after 30 minutes of wait (considered very long already) and when the third 331 came within 2 minutes, the conductor opened the door, although it was really packed and me n my fren just pushed very hard and squeezed the others like sponge to get on board (It was really really packed and my friend was leaning on the door with 1 leg on the ground only and from the next stop the conductor did not stop already after request by other commuters until Summer Palace stop when some people alighted.
