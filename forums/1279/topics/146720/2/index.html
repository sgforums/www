  • hseochin
    14 Aug 05, 03:55
    The O 306 is an original M-B bus i.e. with 1st party M-B bodywork, not some 'rojak' type lousy body from some unknown name 3rd party bodybuilders.

    The chassis used in the O 306 here tend to be OF 1413.
    So the O 306 existed same time as OF maybe even OH as well.

    What i believe is that in the earlier years, the M-B's in Singapore tend to be sourced from Germany mainly & it was only around 1990 that C&C launched the OH series sourced from Brazil instead. OF is front engine OH is rear engine bus-chassi i.e. no M-B bus body comes with it !

    But M-B's were produced from Brazil for many decades before then & OH series does not seem more recent then OF or O 306 as such. In Singapore they appear to be newer models only due to their late introduction here.

    So i don't think O 306 was superceded by OF or OH series, although it's obvious the later models in the OF & OH (like the OH 1627 L) are clearly light years more superior in specifications than the O 306 from the 1970's !

    What you mean by Malaysian made bus-chassis ?
    You mean chassi imported CKD into Malaysia & assembled there ?
    Or chassi parts itself manufactured in Malaysia as well ?

    Don't know about Dennis Darts but Lances in Singapore i'm told are Euro-2 compliant. For Lance, engine should be Cummins, charged-cooled about 10000 cc & 245 BHP with 2-valve head.

    MAN 18.250 i consider it as chassi version of the SL 252 therefore since it's entry level low-floor chassi with rear mounted engine, it ought to use low profile tyres to get the most from the lower floor height. I notice HI doesn't use low profile tyres on its 18.250 when it's so similar to O 405, which of course can use both tyre sizes with low profile tyres being the original choice if the bus bough with original M-B bodywork. Same expected with MAN SL 252 etc.

    For MAN 18.280 as used by HI, because that's normal high floor height type of chassis, i see less point of using low profile tyres although should be technically still possible if wanted.

    If the Volvo B7R is intended as coach chassis, then that normally implies a high floor height chassis, which then doesn't make that much point to bother much about lowering steps since you'll end up climbing many more steps than a proper citybus to get onboard ! Also, if the tyre size unchanged then the 1st step height would not be reduced. If low profile tyres used, then your 1st step height is lowered but still overall quite a high height to climb to get onboard.
