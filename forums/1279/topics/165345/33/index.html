    Originally posted by stooper:
    What does Lay Up Expiry Date mean? Does it mean lay up until that date, then can see on the roads again? Lay up means no road tax too, right?
    lay up mean that the vehicle is temporary not in use. No road tax is being paid during the lay up period. So the bus cant be on the road.. it need to be park at a designated area/depot and cant be move around without permission.

    i think some parts of this bus might be damage and since it is almost reach its life span, so SBST might not want to replace the parts. So it put SBS3731E in lay up status and might use those spare parts from this bus instead. Just like those NAC Scania.

    Originally posted by chickenlittle2:
    wat is plug door?
    Plug doors are swing out doors. They function like normal doors, but the door swings out instead of pulling in and are normally installed at exit doors.

    The buses in which you can sight a plug door have famous single decks like SBS 2888T, a Scania L94UB plying on Express 518 and is kept in custody at BNDEP. Another bus which spots this is SBS 2754X, a Volvo B10M MkIV DM3500 kept as a spare at AMDEP.

    Double deckers also hae this kind of door configuration. All Dennis Tridents, VSO's and B9TL's spot a kind of plug door. No other buses had this kind of door.

    SMRT buses have majority of the bendys fitted with plug doors.

    Anything wrong with this information, please help correct it. Thanks.
