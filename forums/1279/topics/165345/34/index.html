  • Scania
    15 Dec 06, 14:02
    Originally posted by choww:
    What does 2A , 81A, etc stands for in the timesheet?
    SBS Transit
    Regular shift A is for AM shift, P is for PM shift
    Split shift, S is for split shift which morning split ends around 11am to 1pm, E is for morning split which ends around 7:30am to 9am.
    N-Night shift
    T shift-Three Quarter shift, starts in mid morning and ends in the evening.

    SMRT Buses
    Regular shifts, AM and PM
    Split shifts, S1 and S2 (S1 is like SBS Transit's S shift, while S2 is like SBS Transit's E shift)
    TQ- Three Quarter shift (like T shift)

    SBS Transit examples: 1A, 5P, 46P, 50A, 81A, 21S

    Where for services with white and green plate, green plate would be 1A... while White plate would be 50A.... .

    80 something ones were explained

    SMRT buses:

    Examples: 190AM11, 190PM11, 851S150

    Where, 190AM11 and 190PM11 AM and PM shift drivers are having the same block for 190. (11 is the block number)

    Blocks: Blocks are basically somewhat like the sequence of buses (roughly, but not neccesary always)
    SBS Transit: Blocks are assigned to a bus, except services where drivers are allocated with a perm bus.
    Sw-101
    Se-801
    Se-901

    se/w-1XX are for Non Aircon DDs
    se/w-8XX are for Aircon SDs
    se/w-9XX are for Aircon DDs.

    and the trend is like this

    se- East Distriict
    sc-Central District (now East District after the former East District disappeared, so amdep and brbp is now se)
    sw-West District

    I believe there may be some timesheets still with jr-XXX, which are for Jurong Depot services, now with slbp.
