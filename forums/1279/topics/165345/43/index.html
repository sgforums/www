  • TWS4793
    7 Jan 07, 13:55
    Originally posted by XTransbus:
    how do you check if a bus is an SP or Cameo? i took a nac n113 on friday and they got write 'spare' on near the opening there you need to open to change the desto. Is that a spare bus?
    Yes. If they wrote 'SPARE', that means the bus is a spare. SBS 0061A, SBS 0063U and SBS 3671U are just three examples. Behind the desto cover, they wrote SPARE, and that means they are spares.

    But if it's a permanent bus, say my favourite bus, SBS 3721J, they normally put a 'Sv' followed by the number of the service. So if you're to consider my favourite bus, then it's written as 'Sv225'. Anywhere, the operators can write. For double deckers, they write it at the top right han side at the driver's seat, so anyone entering the bus can just see the permanent service number.
