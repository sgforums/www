    Hougang Central Interchange started ONLY in the year 1994.. Before that, it was Hougang INt (Hougang South Interchange). When hougang Central Interchange opened in the year 1994, Upp Serangoon Rd Terminal was closed down and all services were diverted into Hougang Central Interchange.

    During the construction of the NorthEast Line in the year 1997, Hougang Central Interchange was then being affected. A temporary interchange was built beside the Hougang Plaza. At the same time, the original Hougang Central Interchange then became Hougang Central Terminal as the Temp Int couldnt fit in so many services. After the completion of the NEL, Hougang Central Terminal and Hougang Central Interchange then reunited and they remain on the current site ever since.

    Originally posted by Y_Shun:
    Oh okay, then how about Services to Hougang South Int which is now shut down? Thx
    In 1985, the first services in Hougang (South) Int were 85, 97, 111, 321, 323 and 329. Before the interchange was opened, 85 and 111 terminated at Upp Serangoon Rd (10.5 km), which in somewhere between Valley Rd and Jln Rengkam, near Glad Tidings Church. 97 was a new service which went to another new interchange at Jurong East.

    In 1986 came 54, which loops at what is now Eunos Int/MRT. More feeders were added; 322 and 327.

    In 1988, 54 was integrated with 51 and that is how 51 came to Hougang. New Aircon service 118 to Orchard Rd was introduced. 329 was withdrawn and 328 was introduced.

    In 1989, 62 was extended to Hougang from MacPherson. 132 was extended from Ang Mo Kio. By this time, the services in Hougang Int were 51, 62, 85, 97, 111, 118, 132, 321, 322, 323, 327 and 328.

    The MRT rationalisations in the early 1990s saw services 85 and 118 withdrawn but the rest of the trunk services remained, along with a new addition, 151.
