    Went to Joo Koon Int today... Unfortunately all the gates are closed so I cannot enter the int and see the routes of 99, 253, 256, 258 etc. (I wonder how someone here managed to enter the Interchange previously)

    Anyway, there are 2 entrances: one along Joo Koon Circle (only for direction towards the west) and one along Benoi Rd (only for direction towards the north, after 192's stop). The only exit is along Joo Koon Circle, but a unidirectional slip road across Joo Koon Circle (near MRT) is being built so that buses can turn to the right. 
    From what I see when peeping through the entrance at Benoi Rd, the layout of Joo Koon Int is quite similar to Eunos Int, except entrances and berths. There are 2 alighting berths and likely 3 boarding berths. Although most of the signboards in the int are completed, berth allocation is not done yet and I didn't find service numbers on any of the signboards. But overall it seems that the interchange is ready to open on 5th September. 
    The FairPrice hub is already in operation with a food court, a Warehouse Club, some small shops and food outlets, so I feel that Joo Koon Int will still have some passenger flow during weekends, instead of being empty or even not in operation during non-peak hours (as previously suggested by some forumers). There was even a family walking to Benoi Rd to take 192 towards Boon Lay Int with shopping bags. Plus, even JIS are having a considerable amount of passengers during weekends nowadays.

    On the other hand, here are my speculations about JIS affected by Joo Koon Int:
    255 will be split into 255 and 256, one looping at Gul Circle and the other looping at Benoi Cres.
    254 will be split into 253 and 254, one looping at Tuas West and the other from Boon Lay to Joo Koon plying current 254 route (as there are quite a lot of unique sectors).
    As for 257, one of the routes will be from Joo Koon to Pioneer Sector 2; whether the sector from Boon Lay to Joo Koon will remain depends. It can be a new service from Boon Lay to Joo Koon, but I hope that's not the case; alternatively, 99 can be made to ply Jln Ahmad Ibrahim (where current 257 plies) and a new feeder can be introduced in Boon Lay plying 257's route, eliminating the need of current 257's Boon Lay - Benoi Rd sector. Then the last JIS can be a new service from Joo Koon to Tuas South (where 182 doesn't ply - I feel this is the only place that needs a new JIS).
