    Bus Service 258 is new. Many people still do not know that there is this bus service. Give it a few weeks, months or years (up to three years). As more people know about this bus service and the new bus connections that it provides, the loading will increase.

    This reminds me of bus service 185 (Soon Lee - Buona Vista). When bus service 185 was launched in (late-) December 2006, only a few people knew about it. Although bus service 185 complements bus service 99 and connects to Westwood, many people at that time continued to take bus service 99, or make transfers at Boon Lay Bus Interchange. It is only after a while when more people became aware of bus service 185 and began taking bus service 185, that bus service 185 became a heavily-utilised bus service it is now.

    I am sure the new bus service 258 will be well-received and well-utilised. It is only because not many people know about it yet, that is why the loading on new bus service 258 is still so low.

    When bus services 182 and 182M are amended to terminate at Joo Koon Bus Interchange, the people at the affected sector of Upper Jurong Road will have to use bus service 258 for bus connection to the MRT (at Pioneer Station) or Boon Lay Bus Interchange. This is partly why there is bus service 258.

    The other purpose of bus service 258 is to relieve the extremely high loading on bus services 243G and 243W during peak hours. These bus services had been enhanced regularly since around year 2010 when bus services were reviewed quarterly. Hopefully, with the introduction of bus service 258, there is no longer the need to enhance bus services 243G and 243W again.

    The bus connection between Pioneer MRT Station and Jurong West Street 81 is only one of the reasons why bus service 258 was created.
