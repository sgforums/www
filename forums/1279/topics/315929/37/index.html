    Originally posted by Scania:

    CNG OC500LEs obviously do not have a diesel engine in them, they have a M447hLAG CNG engine instead of a OM457hLA/OM906hLA diesel engine.

    Only the OM 457 hLA and M 447 hLAG engines are available on the OC 500 LE now.  The OM 457 hLA is available in 220 kW / 299 PS (there is apparently a demonstrator circulating in Australia with the larger 260kW / 354 PS rating) and the M 447 hLAG is available in either 185 kW / 252 PS or 240 kW / 326 PS.  Both CNG and diesel engines displace 11.967-litres.

    For operators who are after a low-entry chassis with a smaller engine, the Brazillian O 500 U (also known as the OH 1830 LE) is available.  This has a model OM 926 LA engine which develops 225 kW / 305 PS (de-rated from 326 PS maximum).  This engine displaces 7.201-litres but with it's unusually high torque output (1200 Nm @ 1200-1600rpm), this engine actually easily outperforms the old OM 457 hLA Euro 3 engine rated at 185 kW / 252 PS, as I found out yesterday.

    Regards,

    Dave
