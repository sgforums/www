    Originally posted by pagani zsnda:

    I thought that these buses are fitted with hydraulic retarders ... they are not dependent on engine speed (if i'm not wrong). That would reduce the volume of the downshift "cat call".

    Hydraulic retarders are fitted only inside gearboxes, not engines and most often, all Auto gearboxes used by bus have them. Edit : Engines uses a separate secondary braking system. This system is known as exhaust brake or sometimes, jake brakes.

    Engine speed is used to govern the bus speed, together with gear ratios. Just like a manual car, when you go downhill, its unwise to engage in high gear ratios, as the car would most likely roll down faster than you can stop it.
