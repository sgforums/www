    Originally posted by JaSoNhSu92:


    what ECU stands for?

    ECU = Engine Control Unit.

    The ECU is basically a computer that controls all of the functions of an engine.  Functions such as fuel injection, valve timing, boost pressure (if engine is fitted with a turbocharger or supercharger), ignition (only for spark ignition engines, e.g. CNG), throttle (also only for spark ignition engines), and emissions controls such as EGR and SCR are all controlled by the ECU.  The ECU relies on feedback from numerous sensors scattered around the engine.  These sensors are especially important if low emissions is a requirement.

    The TEMIC ECU on the OM 457 hLA engine installed on the OC 500 LE chassis communicates with the other sub-systems of the bus (e.g. transmission) over a 24-volt Controller Area Network (CAN) bus.

    Regards,
    Dave
