    Integrated public transport hub at Boon Lay to open in December (CNA):

    SINGAPORE: Come December, Boon Lay residents will have a new air-conditioned bus interchange.

    Up to 60 buses, 31 boarding-alighting berths and retail outlets will be seen at the upcoming Boon Lay Integrated Public Transport Hub (Integrated PT Hub).

    The bus interchange, built over 20,000 square metres, is the Land Transport Authority's (LTA's) biggest to date.

    The interchange is linked to Boon Lay MRT station via Jurong Point 2 shopping centre.

    Ong Teck Chuan, deputy director of public transport promotion, LTA, said: "Commuters can look forward to a comfortable and seamless transfer between the bus interchange and the MRT station. And at the same time, they will have the added convenience to pick up some grocery or to do some shopping."

    All entrances and exits of the new interchange can be accessed by wheelchair users. And it takes about three minutes to walk from the furthest bus berth to the MRT station.

    But the 150,000 existing daily commuters will have to wait until December for it to open.

    Mr Ong said: "Principally, we are really thinking of the students who will be taking their year-end exams. I think this is one of the reasons as to why they have scheduled it at the end of the year."

    LTA said six more Integrated PT Hub will be built over the next 10 years, with those at Serangoon and Clementi expected to be completed in 2011.
