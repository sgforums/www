    A Bus not a sports car (TODAY 20/5/2008)

    Reckless manoeuvres bybus driver almost caused a serious accident


    Letter from GERALDINE CHAY MEI FONG


    ON FRIDAY at around 10.45am, my husband and I were in our car in the middle lane of Thomson Road, towards Marymount Road in the direction of Ang Mo Kio, near the Old Police Academy. There were no cars in front of us. Suddenly, the driver of an SMRT-Tibs “bendy bus” (Service 851) accelerated from the bus lane and cut into the middle lane ahead of us to avoid some road works in the bus lane. The bus almost hit the left side of our vehicle, where I was seated. The bus driver’s rash actions forced my
    husband to suddenly apply his brakes and we had to come to a stop in the middle lane for quite a while to wait for the bus to pass. If we hadn’t stopped in time, I believe
    a serious accident would have taken place. My husband couldn’t swerve into the right
    lane to avoid the bus as there were other vehicles in that lane. The bus driver could
    have also caused a pile-up if the traffic had been heavier. The bus driver had no qualms about endangering the lives of other road users and that of his passengers. He should have stayed in his lane and made sure the road was clear before changing lanes. I would like an explanation from SMRTTibs for the actions of bus drivers who
    drive their buses like sports cars.
