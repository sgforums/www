    Back to the topic on destos, I have floated this idea around in my mind for a while now. It might not be implemented anytime soon, if ever, but  do hear me out.

    In the 1970's and 1980's, buses had a front small desto in addition to a side desto and the main front desto. This system was discontinued a long time ago but persists on a few exceptional bus routes (those that ply Bukit Purmei Avenue, for example). What I'm thinking of is to have a small desto, either in the form of a small plastic sign or an LCD/OLED screen, that indicates the type of bus route, whether it's terminating, green/white plate, and so on. To do this, Singapore's bus system will have to adopt the "Local/Express" nomenclature - "Local" buses referring to basic bus routes in our system (i.e. trunks, basic plus, feeders, and intra-towns), and "Express" referring to our express and Fast Forward routes. The destos will be of different colours for different situations, which I will list below:

    • For Local routes, the desto will be white-on-black, with the word "Local" on it. 
    • For Express and Fast Forward routes, the desto will be white-one-blue, with the word "Express" on it. This is done for Fast-Forward routes mainly to avoud confusion and mix-up between the local and Fast Forward (Express) version of the route, with the latter charging higher fares.
    • Premium routes will bear a white-on-purple desto, with the word "Premium" on it. Underneath it, the fare for the route and the notice stating that standing on buses on such routes is prohibited will also be stated.
    • Shortworking trips and alternate "M"routes will have a black-on-yellow desto, with the destination of the trip or the extra/different areas served stated on the desto. For example, the desto for shortworking trip 27A will indicate "Blk 147 Tampines" on it's travel towards Tampines, while the desto for route 182M will indicate "Skipping Tuas Checkpoint" (or it could read "Tuas Checkpoint" with a diagnoal bar over it to visually indicate that the route will not be going there). This is to avoid any confusion between the full and shortworking/alternate version of the bus route.
    • Green/white plate routes will obviously each get their respective green and white desto. The desto will indicate - in travel order - one road or landmark it will pass by before it's looping point, which will also be indicated. For example, route 410G may have...

    Bishan St 11

    Sin Ming Ave

    ...on it's desto, while route 410W may have something like...

    Bishan St 22

    Sin Ming Avenue

    ...on it's desto.

    • Terminating intra-town and Premium routes will have a white-on-red desto that reads "Terminating" for terminating intra-town and premium routes. This system is already being used on such premium routes, and intra-town routes use a similar "T" plate system; These two systems should be standardised to maintain consistency.
    • For routes that serve the same bus stops in both directions (eg. the Serangoon Gardens and Bukit Purmei routes), the destination of the direction could be indicated on the desto. Two colours - red and white - for each respective different direction could be used. So, for example, route 136 heading to Punggol could use a black-on-white desto that reads "Punggol", while the Ang Mo Kio ones could have white-on-red destos that read "Ang Mo Kio".

    The system may sound complicated, but is actually quite simple to understand and could help lower or prevent cases of route mix-ups. Note how I used colours to indicate the type of bus route, as well as it's directionality and status in some cases, which could help the illiterate or partially-literate (for example, those who understand only Chinese) to help better differentiate the bus routes.
