    Things to note when using ez-link card
    2 June 2007

    WE refer to the letters by Mr Paul Teh Seng Toon, ' Review procedures on retaining ez-link cards ' (The New Paper, 16 May) and Madam Telma Gomez, ' Driver could have checked photo on card ' (The New Paper, 18 May).

    We would like to take the opportunity to share that when a concession ez-link card is tapped at the bus card-reader, commuters will hear two beeps and see a green light if the card is successfully read.

    However, if no beep tone is heard or a red light and a series of five beeps are produced by the card-reader, this indicates a problem with the card where the transaction cannot be successfully conducted.

    The bus operator reserves the right to retain the card for investigation and verification.

    This is for the protection of ez-link card users in general, as the card may be tampered with, misused, replaced or refunded.

    Concurrently when this happens, a message will be shown on the bus captain's fare console to guide him on what he should do.

    Bus captains are authorised to check, verify and retain if necessary, all cards and passes for investigation purposes.

    This includes cards that have been reported lost, stolen or have expired and are no longer valid, as well as those that are misused by persons who are not entitled to concessionary travel.

    The use of ez-link cards is governed by the regulations and published conditions as indicated on the back of the card.

    For details, commuters may also visit www.transitlink.com.sg.

    Bus captains are trained on the proper procedures of retaining cards and he or she will issue a retention slip to the cardholder.

    After investigation by the bus operator, the card will be sent to TransitLink for cardholders' collection.

    Cardholders may call TransitLink Hotline 1800-2255 663 after five working days to check the status of the cards.

    In Mr Paul Teh's son case, we are pleased to note that he had since obtained a replacement card.

    For the case cited by the writers following the report in The New Paper of 13 May (of a bus driver who was allegedly attacked after he retained a student's ez-link card), we wish to explain that the student had earlier reported it as lost/stolen and a replacement card was then issued to her.

    To avoid misuse of the card, it was blacklisted on the system, so the bus captain proceeded to retain it.

    Misuse of concession cards, if not addressed, will indirectly affect other fare-paying passengers as they may then have to bear a larger burden of the cost of public transport.

    We thank the writers for their feedback.

    - Kathryn Lau
    Assistant corporate communications manager
    TransitLink Pte Ltd

    - Tammy Tan
    Director
    Corporate communications
    SBS Transit Ltd
