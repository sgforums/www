    I would say its a nice idea. However, who is going to fund this project? A brand new prototype built from scratch and involving such machining and requires 3D engineering precision drawing, would easily draw up a 4 figure sum. Main factor: Cost.


    If bus companies are willing to fork up some money for a trial, then this could be worth trying out: Modifying the door mechanism on the front door, such that the opening of the shelter would be in sync with the doors. Reason: By keeping majority mechanical, you reduce the risk of breakdowns by up to 50%. Electronic sensors, actuators(motors and cylinders), are super costly and expensive to maintain.
