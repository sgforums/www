    Originally posted by nenepokey:

    this is nice. anyway, it's a N310UA6X2 chassis, with Scania DC918 Euro IV engine and ZF6HP602C transmission.

    Finally saw an interior of a N series (5th generation) Scania. N series have the engines installed transversely, similar to the DDs (excluding Dennis Tridents). Thats why it can be low floor.

    Don't get me wrong, buses with longitudinally installed vertical engines can be low floor too, just like the Tridents, MAN Lion City and Volvo B9Ls. As for the MAN Lion City, theres an option for vertical and horizontal engines(both installed longitudinally). However, those with vertical engines will have less seating space as compared to the horizontal ones because the engine pertrude slightly into the passenger compartment. Just some technical 'tidbits' for you guys.
