    Morning in Innsbruck. Interior(or rather, front) of the Citaro G cabin. ivb.bus corp owns a lot of Citaros and the articulated Citaro Gs here in Innsbruck. Buses are both quiet and have really smooth(though on the slow side) acceleration.

     

    Took the Swarovski Kristallwelten(Crystal World) shuttle bus from Innsbruck Hauptbahnhof Autobusplatz to the Kristallwelten place in Wattens, out of Innsbruck proper. Frequency of the bus is 2 hours, from 9a.m to 3p.m, departing Innsbruck Hauptbahnhof Autobusplatz. Picture was taken before boarding the shuttle going back to Innsbruck Hauptbahnhof Autobusplatz.

     

    The mountains behind are the picturesques ones when travelling on the train from Zurich to Vienna/Salzburg — the Alps.

     

     

     

    Reaching Innsbruck Hauptbahnhof, I took the train to Salzburg Hauptbahnhof. Unlike Innsbruck(with the exception of dedicated tram lines), Salzburg is dotted with overhead power cables for trolley buses(Innsbruck has them for trams). Salzburg is the last Austrian city before the border with Germany.

    Picture was taken on the other side of the Salzach River from the Salzburg Hauptbahnhof, near the city fortress, Festung Hohensalzburg.
