    Originally posted by AdAptAliS:

    Don't know where to post this.

    Saw one on those machines that tows trains? (those yellow coloured machines that uses the mrt track usually parking at depots) passing by Serangoon Station heading for Harbour Front.

    Previously a stationary train was at Potong Pasir also heading for HBF.

    such way maintenance locomotives (made by Bombardier i think) are common on both NEL and SMRT lines. fret not, it came from Sengkang Depot after the last southbound train to do checking of rails for excessive grinding, misalignment etc. in fact, if u are going home to Serangoon/Hougang/Sengkang/Punggol on the last train, u may be able to see the locomotive move into the opposite platform. tis happens every time i'm at dhoby ghaut.

    Then, a stationary train was at Sengkang Station heading for Punggol. Caused my train, heading for Punggol, to use Platform A, (heading for HBF). I didn't know they could change tracks like this cause it was using Platform B when bypassing Buangkok Station.

    First time i alighted NEL on the "wrong" platform. Pretty strange.

    there is a NB to SB crossover just after Buangkok NB. these special rail configurations that allow trains to change tracks are called crossovers. try standing at the front console of NEL train and see the crossovers after Buangkok. and anyway, the stationary train at Platform A was probably terminating revenue service at Sengkang and heading home to Sengkang Depot.

    All happen between 2342 and 0003.
