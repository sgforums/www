    it seems like there is some misconception on how shortworking trips operate.

    1. if the bus terminates at its terminating point, but starts somewhere along the route, then a SWT number is not needed. in short its known as downroute. There are many services that has this. they include but not limited to services 2, 9A, 15, 21, 27, 28, 30, 31, 33, 45, 52, 53, 54, 55, 57, 60, 69, 70, 74, 80, 83, 87, 98, 99, 100, 131, 154, 161, 174, 190, 195A, 196, 228, 238, 240, 243, 960.

    2. SWTs are designed to help a specific sector at a specific timing. There has to be a group of commuters that the SWT is catered for. It is usually at the expense of at least 1 full route bus/trip for this SWT.
