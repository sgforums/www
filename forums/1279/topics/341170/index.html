    Remember a similar such thread created by me last year?

    As 2008 comes to a close, we want to look back at the significant events that have occured in the bus scene.

    If you know of any such events, you can post here. The following categories are available as a starting point for you to recall:

    1. Events (Accidents, Breakdowns etc.)
    2. Redeployments
    3. Cameos

    For a start, I shall reflect on what have happened in the year 2008. The most memorable...

    Events

    1. Introduction of new Premium services by various bus companies.
    2. Introduction of Service 128, Singapore's first rail-duplicating service in nearly two decades (7 Jul).
    3. Introduction of SMRT's newest bus in 8 years - the OC500LE.
    4. SBST receives Volvo B7RLE and MAN 18.240 for demo purposes.

    Redeployments

    1. Service 197's transfer from HGDEP-controlled to BNDEP-controlled (3 Mar).
    2. Massive depot and service transfers between SMRT buses and depots (23 Mar, Jun).
    3. Complete withdrawal of Volvo B10M Mark 2s (6 Apr) as well as the Scania N113CRB NACs (9 Dec).
    4. Double-deckers introduced to more services, especially Service 151 (4 Aug) and 225 (18 Feb).

    Cameos

    1. SBS 8031J on Service 5 (11 Oct)
    2. SBS 8009A on Townlink 222 (30 Apr)
    3. B9s on Service 5
    4. SBS 9676S on Service 107M (13 Dec)
    5. SBS 8389C on Service 147

    To the rest, continue to add in more! I'm listing only a few examples so that there will be ample talking point for all.

    Yishun Interchange Summary Report 2008

    Notable cameos around Yishun
    1) 0960L on Sv806
    2) 0959S & 0961J on Sv811
    3) 0484U, 0565U & 0595H on Sv859
    4) 0364H on Sv811 - Happened once in February & once in September
    5) 0365E on Sv812 - Happened once in January (Tomato head drive PM shift)
    6) 0365E on Sv800 - Happened once in September
    7) 1257C on Sv806 - Happened once in October
    8) 0364H on Sv171 - Happened twice in October
    9) SMB 20C on Sv812 A/P - due to 1207Y breakdown
    10) 3-5 appearances of OC500LE on Sv853
    11) 1 appearance of OC500LE on Sv804

    Duty adjustments for services
    1) Sv804 adjust headway & runtime
    2) Sv806 add one fullday & adjust headway, original perm bus SMB 7S
    3) Sv969 & Sv812 adjust headway, Sv969 add 1 S catered to cross Sv812 PM S.

    Transfers
    1) 0437G, 0479K & 0816X to WLDEP (ex-811)
    2) Lots of CAC merc from ex-851 to WLDEP
    3) 0772M & 0781L to WLDEP (ex-171)
    4) 1072T & 1076H to KJDEP (ex-YIBP SP) replaced by 1171R & 1241Y
    5) Sv859 handed over to WLDEP, ex-fleet either went to KJDEP (0490B, 0762S & 0821E but the first two were later transferred to WLDEP) or Sv106 (1171R, 1237K, 1238H & 1241Y but the first and last two bendies were replaced by 1037X & 1097Y which came from KJDEP later)
    6) Yishun RG takes charge of AM 190 & AM 106
    7) Sv852 handed over to WLDEP, ex-fleet mostly went to Sv985 (after which 0938C, 1009C, 1010Y & 1014L were transferred back to WLDEP to perm Sv852)
    8) Sv171 partial fleet handed to WLDEP, ex-fleet mostly went to KJDEP (after which some of the original perm buses under AMDEP that went to KJDEP were transferred back to WLDEP to perm Sv171)

    New buses
    1) First placed on Sv851 (SMB 4A), more added in (SMB 19H - SMB 23U)
    2) Sv806 gets SMB 7S as fleet addition, thereafter bus given to Sv811 in exchange for TIB 0479K and then replaced again to TIB 1221E
    3) Sv811 gets SMB 7S - SMB 10G replacing the box mercs, after which SMB 33R goes to Sv811 also to fulfill 50% WAB status
    4) Sv171 gets SMB 24S - SMB 27J replacing 1 OAC merc, 2 CAC merc & 1 Volgren merc
    5) With addition of 969S107 cross Sv812 there have been a few OC cameos on both services, mainly: SMB 3C, SMB 4A, SMB 7S, SMB 8P, SMB 10G & SMB 20C

    Accidents
    1) TIB 1138M involved in notorious KUB accident with SBS 8074K, bus was doing 67S201 on that fateful morning and was returning to depot when accident happened along YCK Rd

    Others
    1) Yishun Int slated to go for upgrading works soon (in the near future) to an air-conditioned hub
    2) More PRC & Malaysian SLs are put into Yishun RG as standbys usually driving Sv853/Sv854/Sv855
