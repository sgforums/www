    I did a so-called 'trial run' through the route with my dad last week in his car. The new CIQ in Johor is pretty mordern but the bus acess lane to the CIQ is shared with cars. There are three lanes leading to the new checkpoint from Woodlands direction. 1 for motorcycles, 1 for cars and 1 for bus and cars combined.

     

    I would say that the new route would take up 5 - 8 minutes more if traffic flow is good. So, a few fleet additions should be introduced. For Today, Sv 950, Sv 170 and Sv 160 were in a big mess going through JB Sentral Bus Stop heading towards their respective endpoints in JB thanks to the car jam going into Singapore.

     

    The JB Sentral Bus Stop is located near the current Johor Bahru KTM Station. JB Sentral is not yet completed as they are just starting to lay the tracks that would take the trains up to the JB Sentral platforms as it is above-ground.
