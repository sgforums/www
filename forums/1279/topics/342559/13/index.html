    Just my 2 cents here.

    This entire post is not directed to anyone in particular, but rather, a community as a whole. If you feel offended by the nature of my posting & certain keywords, I sincerely apologise in advance because I have no intention to do so.

    I feel saddened that in a bus community like Singapore, it's mandatory to have proper sunlight / absolutely no obstacles in bus photos, failing which the person gets a super harsh lecture and probably gets skinned alive virtually.

    Just look at the Hong Kong bus community. People post photos of a VSO with flower pots blocking the sides of the bus. People post photos of a Trident that has no proper lighting. People post night shots using free hand (and abit grainy). People post photos of bus in wierd angles (some even tilted). People post night shots that have the sides looking super dark.. But do people there lash at those who do not follow so-called proper protocol in taking photos?

    Yes having proper lighting conditions makes your photo look nicer. It's an added bonus, not a must-have. What matters more is that the bus is there; the registration plate is shown, the service number & route details are shown too as well as what's inside the bus. Is it a crime to have obstacles in your photo? Is it illegal to have the wrong lighting conditions in your photo?

    Alright. People probably say that since they can take photos with super-good quality, so why not the rest. Please. Different people have different styles of photo-taking. So what if your photo looks "nicer" than the rest; please respect another person's photo-taking style. If there is no Sun for 7 days, will you NOT take photos for 7 days? W/O sunlight and you will die because your photos will turn out "horrible"?

    I'm not suggesting that we follow Hong Kong's way of treating fellow bus enthusiasts, but as we are from an Asian culture, humility is what makes us stand out from other cultures. Mutual respect is important here. I'm sure if everyone treats another with some basic respect, the entire community can be much more fun-loving and enjoyable. Of which I seriously see no point in having this senior-junior barricade theory, because it's totally rubbish. Does it mean that a senior knows more than a junior? Does it mean that a junior must learn everything a senior teaches them?

    I have shot photos for quite a number of years, and perhaps the bus community is a place where I hear the most funny and restricting protocols, really...

    In photography as a general term, there should not be too many rules to restrict how a photo should be shot, and there are no hard and fast rules either. There may be some tricks like 'rule of thirds' and so on, but they should be only regarded as tricks and not how you should be governed to make your pictures look nice.

    I have tried to share with some folks around me to try these tricks commonly used in commercial photography, but I feel being shunned every time. Bus must be well lighted, bus must be in the center. I even got flamed by god-knows-who about a Scania N113 being posed in the 'rule of thirds' with a battery powered rubbish collector. What is the constant refusal in accepting new ideas?

    Maybe no one has seen my potential, maybe people do not know me well. I feel sad again and again when these commercial tricks were put to waste just because there was a trend set about how a bus photo should be taken.

    Then again, I am not the best. But experience on the field is important. While you guys have shot bus photos on the street for the past number of years, I have shot on community levels all the way to international levels. Its only recently that i have joined the bus theme, and its also recently that i have left it... Retired for good until I can find what i should shoot, instead of grabbing a thousand-dollar camera just to shoot something on the streets and call myself a 'street-photographer' which any uncle or auntie can do.

    I have ever thought of the fields that I can head after this. Studio? Macro? Or even a theme like capturing people at work in different jobs on the society. Who knows...

    Maybe I have reached the level of reckoning, that competition with a fellow photographer is no longer of importance; we all have our own styles. I cannot compete with another bus fan's style, neither can I compete with other commercial photographers out there, we are simply on different fields rather than levels.

    I welcome anyone to approach me to learn more about the different ways that you can shoot a photo. To round up, share something that was shot in the past...
