    This odd location makes it generally awkward for northbound services - that is, if they are also to serve BPJ MRT.

    Were we to have a BPJ - Yew Tee service (as you've suggested here), the BC's would have to travel the stretch of Woodlands Rd before they arrive at their first stop at BPJ station along Upp Bt Timah Rd, then loop around Bt Panjang Rd and continue the journey back down Woodlands Rd. The same journey again when they return, end at the same stop, and head back to the bus park. It is still somewhat troublesome and inefficient, especially during non-peak hours, because the route has to serve the MRT station twice.

    Which is why I still somewhat support extending a nearby short trunk like 177, THEN add short trips with moveable start / end points to reduce this redundancy.

    • The morning peak can have a short-trip start directly from the bus park heading towards KJE, then end at BPJ MRT (UBT Rd), and then immediately loop back to the bus park, if not Woodlands Rd & Yew Tee for the next round, until the end of peak hours when it will return to the bus park.

    • The evening peak would start from BPJ MRT (UBT Rd) (seeing that it's difficult to start from the stop opposite), and end at Woodlands Rd just after KJE.

    • In both cases, the BC driving the short-trips would be able to return to the bus park to rest.

    • At non-peak hours, the route would just continue down UBT Rd / Hillview Rd and to Bt Batok, providing comfortable transfers to other services.
