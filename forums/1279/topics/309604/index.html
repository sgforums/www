    Anyone who says they waited for 14 hours only is crap. I'm utterly disgusted when i realised that they waited at least a day. I went to talk to one of the drivers and one of them said they waited from 9am on Tuesday (4/3/08) until 10am on Wednesday (5/3/08) and they were still stucked in SG.


    Anyway don't worry. Don't listen to people who exclaimed that they never eat for 14 hours and above. That's not true. They have people to help them tapao food for them to eat (obviously its their friend, not people from our government whatsoever.). However, i saw alot of them ended up eating maggie mee. Alot of maggie mee wrappings were strewn on the pathways. This is pathetic. The drivers were earning a living for their families, not on army training. Absurd!

    You guys may think its just jam only right? No. Before barricades were set up, drivers cutting queue was a common scene. At one junction near the Checkpoint [ (not the main juntion that links to the causeway) Lets call this Junction A], a nicely done grass patch was totally ruined. The trucks cut queue by driving over the grass patch and kerb, digging up the mud as a result. The entire area stank. At one point, a small portion of the (what you guys call it? I can't rmbr lols.) was demolished by the trucks.


    Tempers rage when drivers cut queue. On Tuesday (4/3/08), a tractor trailer went to cut queue near Kranji MRT. One of the waiting drivers behind got so mad that he came down and smashed the windscreen of the truck that had cut queue. TP came to take over the situation. At Junction A, drivers were cutting queue as well. Drivers behind came down to confront the situation. Most of them were indian drivers. One of them then said in Tamil "Whoever cut queue don't ever let them cut. If they still insists, smash their side mirrors off!". Once the trucks get moving, drivers forced their way thru that there were barely inches to spare between the trucks, in an attempt to force off those who cut queue.

    It doesn't end there. Most of the trucks were old trucks. Old trucks meant dirty exhaust. Yeah i mean real dirty exhaust. Whenever the trucks started moving and trottles up, the entire stretch of road becomes a choking hazard. The road gets covered in thick white or black diesel fumes. At night, it looks as if theres fog. Sad to say, those are poisionous fumes from the trucks.

     

     I went there since Wednesday (5/3/08) morning 8am to check out the condition. As i went further inside, so many drivers were blaring their truck horn in frustration. For BusFans here, you know the loudness of the K230UB horn right? The trucks' horn were twice louder than that. Not only 1 trucks is sounding its horn. At least 4 were sounding. Imagine the noise and chaos. Imagine trucks sounding their horn repeatedly and those toxic, thick black diesel smoke. Its the most chaotic scene i've seen with my eyes. Even in the night some trucks still blare their horns.

    Not to forget that the length of the jam totals up to almost 4km (estimated).

    This is real chaos man. No joke.
