    In my opinion, personally, I feel that 983 is very likely to have High-Capacity buses deployed on it. The projected rider-ship on 983 may be high, but the actual rider-ship really have to wait and see when the service begins in the last week of April 2015, and when the MRT Downtown Line opens in the first quater of 2016. 

    Along the interim route of 983, Ave 4 already have route 300, so high demand may not come from there. Ave 5 maybe, because it provides a shorter connection to CCK MRT than route 301, but then again, Ave 5 still largely under construction. So, maybe now not yet wise to have high-capacity on route 983.

    Next time when 983 is extended to Bukit Panjang, there may be a surge in rider-ship on route 983 because people staying at Choa Chu Kang Avenue 4/5/7 may want to take MRT Downtown Line at Bukit Pajang MRT Station.

    To anticipate it, we can deploy High-Capacity buses on it when the MRT Downtown Line opens. After a while, when we know how the actual rider-ship is like and how the travel patterns is generally like in Choa Chu Kang with DTL in the area, then fine-tune the deployment of buses on 983 and/or create a new bus route that complements 983, and/or create a new bus route that provides connection to DTL from other parts of Choa Chu Kang, or even, Bukit Gombak.

    Really, we have to wait and see how the situation is actually like when the MRT Downtown Line opens, before we can really discuss which bus routes should have high capacity buses, or which areas need new bus routes.
