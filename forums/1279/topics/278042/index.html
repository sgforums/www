    Originally posted by newcomer:
    maybe u can touch on why some ppl think bus spotting is boring? i mean there're so many things out there with better spot value like birds, planes, watever. but why of all things common, the bus?
    It really depends on the individual, his likes and hobbies.
    For me, although I do identify different types of buses, I am not really into the name and type of buses and gearboxes that they are using. Like for example, at a bus stop near Dieppe Barracks with 167, 169, 171 and 980, if one identifies a bus coming from a distance and it is a merc, there is a high chance that either 169 or 171 is coming. If it is a bendy, it is high chance that the bus will be 169. This gives me or everyone with an interest of spotting buses an advantage.

    Besides, the sounds of the buses are unique. Even with buses of the same brand and type, the engines sound may be different due to external factors.

    I am more into bus routes. There are so many ways to get to different places in Singapore and I can say Singapore is not so bad in terms of bus networks although there are definitely areas to improve on. I like to read Transitlink Bus Guides because it is a pocket sized book, with colourful labels and the details are clear. Besides, different bus services have different demands as can be seen by the dds and bendys deployed on selective services. It is rather interesting to identify the hotspots that create the demand.
