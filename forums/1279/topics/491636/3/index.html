    Let's take a good look back in history at the links available between Paya Lebar MRT and Marine Parade Rd/Terrace if that's the case.

    Since Paya Lebar MRT station opened on 4 November 1989, let's start the chronology from Year 1989.

    Year 1989 - Year 1992

    Only sv135 offered the connection between Paya Lebar MRT and Marine Parade Rd when the MRT station first opened. The situation was possibly way worse back then than right now. While sv135 do have Leyland Atlanteans back then, they were of similar capacity to present air-conditioned single-deck buses (approximately 80 pax) to cope with the passenger demand, which probably just started building up as residents of Marine Parade first started to adapt to using Paya Lebar MRT as a transfer point to return home/get to work or school.

    Year 1992 - Year 2005

    sv76 was diverted from Ubi Ave 2, Eunos Link, Jln Eunos, Still Rd and Still Rd South to serve Paya Lebar Rd, Tg Katong Rd, Amber Rd and Marine Parade Rd (between Amber Rd & Still Rd South) to alleviate the passenger demands of sv135. However, air-conditioned double-deck buses had yet to be introduced until 1993, which means that sv76 could be using the same 80-pax non-air-conditioned double-deck buses.

    I wonder if the commuters along Ubi Ave 2 had complained about the loss of a direct link to Marine Parade Rd. Eunos Link and Jln Eunos didn't have its lost links to Marine Parade Rd replaced for about 8 months until the introduction of sv55, while Still Rd and Still Rd South remained served by svM4 to Marine Parade Rd, Marine Cres and Marine Terrace.

    Year 2005 - Year 2016

    sv43 was introduced as the third connection between Paya Lebar MRT and Marine Parade Rd to help out sv76 and sv135, which also resulted in its extensive duplication with sv76. Operating with a full fleet of B10Ms, double-deck buses were only introduced on sv43 from 2012, which meant that sv76 was the only service with air-conditioned double-deck buses of the three connections along this sector for about 7 years after sv43 was first introduced. The Marine Cres/Terrace connection was only offered from 2011.

    Year 2016 - Present

    sv134 was introduced as a replacement connection for sv76, which was diverted to Eunos Int. sv43 was marked as "the alternative" with albeit poorer frequencies, while sv134 itself, as with a few other BSEP services, also had erratic frequencies. Commuters were confused with the changes between sv76 and sv134 as the goodwill ambassador deployed was not conscientious in informing commuters of the changes and the BCs had to be the ones keeping the commuters informed.

    The Verdict

    Which era was the worst? 1989-1992, 1992-2005, 2005-2016 or right now?
