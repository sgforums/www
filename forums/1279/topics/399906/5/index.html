    The Victories are strictly speaking not quite Leyland. However, the Vikings carried the Leyland badges too even though they came from Albion.

     

    Ashok Leyland was an Indian offshoot of BL, now independent. It still produces the Viking based on old-school blueprints.

    British Leyland wasn't sold to Vovlo, British Leyland became Rover Group which then hived off its bus and truck businesses by selling them off. Rover then focused on cars and through some means disintegrated even more to a state that it is hard to really find any quintessentially British car manufacturer now (MG Rover was sold to the Chinese).

    It really showed how big and filmsy British Leyland was - the diseconomies of scale did it in, and Margaret Thatcher was determined (and largely succeeded) to end such ineffiencies in the British economy (by disbanding and privatising that large number of previously nationalised (and merged) companies), which saw the number of strike days decrease and better quality products being produced.
