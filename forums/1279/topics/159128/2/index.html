    Extract from Today 29th Oct 2005

    Not always cost effective to have most direct bus route
    Letter from LOH CHOW KUANG
    Secretary, Public Transport Council

    WE REFER to Mr Benny Tien's letter "Are bus operators taking commuters for a ride?" on Oct 20. .
    The Public Transport Council (PTC) is an independent body that regulates bus services and also bus and train fares in Singapore.
    .
    PTC is required to balance commuters' interests with the long-term viability of the public transport operators, and to ensure that the costs of public transport provision are managed and fares remain affordable to the general public.
    .
    Basic bus operators (SBS Transit and SMRT Buses) are responsible for providing a comprehensive and efficient network of daily scheduled services within their areas of responsibility.
    .
    In particular, the operators are required to comply with the quality of service standards stipulated by the PTC and to seek PTC's approval to introduce a new service or to amend or withdraw an existing service.
    .
    To safeguard commuters' interests, the PTC has from time to time directed the bus operators to revise or withdraw their proposals for bus route changes.
    .
    The PTC has also required the operators to provide bus services to less developed areas even though the services may not be profitable.
    .
    Mr Tien has asked why some trunk services take longer routes.
    .
    We wish to clarify that the directness of bus routes was measured using a proxy, by comparing the one-way, terminal-to-terminal distance of a bus route with that of the shortest route by car.
    .
    In practice, most commuters do not travel from one terminal to another.
    .
    While the average one-way distance of trunk bus services is about 18km, the average travelling distance of bus passengers is only about one third of this.
    .
    Public transport caters to the masses and passenger demand and trip distribution vary widely across the island.
    .
    It is therefore, not always practical and cost effective to provide every commuter with the most direct bus service.
    .
    Route deviations are sometimes necessary, to better serve the commuters in certain areas.
    .
    In fact, commuters often request for an existing bus service to be diverted closer to their origin/destination.
    .
    In the absence of a direct link and where a transfer is necessary, the existing fare structure facilitates this by providing for a transfer rebate to minimise the cost of making the transfer.
    .
    We assure Mr Tien that we will continue to ensure the bus operators improve on the current bus network.
    How they ensure this??? Talk is cheap.
    .
    Mr Tien and members of the public may direct any further queries or feedback to PTC's feedback e-mail address at ptc@www.ptc.gov.sg.
    .
    We also invite them to visit our website at www.ptc.gov.sg for more information.
    .
    We thank Mr Tien for his feedback and the opportunity to clarify.
    Letter from LOH CHOW KUANG
    Secretary, Public Transport Council

    Tourist-targeted buses should be ad-free
    Letter from Ericia Tan

    .
    I REFER to the letter, "Can Passengers 'see' through ads on buses?" (Oct 27).
    .
    I agree with the writer that the ads on buses are taking up a greater proportion of the bus windows. While I applaud the bus operators' efforts to be creative in generating secondary revenue to keep fares low, it can be exasperating to tourists wanting to sight-see using public transport ation.
    .
    Recently, I took the CityBuzz service C3 to Little India with my friends from overseas who wanted to see the light-up for Deepavali.
    .
    As it is a bus for tourists, I expected the windows to be "ads-free" to allow a good view of the outside. However, the bus was wrapped with advertisements and passengers sitting near the back rows where the advertisement stickers were pasted could not get a good view of the sights. It certainly did not help that the darkness of the night added to the low visibility.
    .
    The bus was not crowded then and we could avoid sitting by the panels covered with advertisements. But what if the bus was crowded? I urge the bus operator to look into this matter.

    So contradicting, they deployed the best buses for this so called tourist sight seeing buses, yet covered it with all the advertisements...

    .
