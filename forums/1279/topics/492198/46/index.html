    Originally posted by Seveneighteightsix:

    I do miss the days when our bus fleet's were so varied and filled with many different models. However, in this cost-efficient world it's probably just better to consistently large batches of one model.

    Yes, having the same model in large quantity is more cost effective.

    This is called "economies of scale".

    When the order is large, Manufacturers and Advertising companies usually offer substantial discounts, as producing in large quantities lowers the unit cost of production. This enables the bus company to procure the products and services at lower prices, and as a result, they are able to generate higher revenue.

    When there are only a few models, Technicians can become specialised in handling the buses. As they become specialised, they tend to make less mistakes, and thus productivity increases.

    With higher revenue and productivity, banks and financial institutions would be more willing to provide financial support to the bus company, at lower rates.

    ^^ Which is why Go-Ahead only wants B9TLs and Citaros, and they outsource the advertising to Moove media.

    However, the lack of communication and coordination in the very beginning worked against them.

    Hopefully, things are better now, because there are indeed economies of scale to be reaped from Go-Ahead's way of operating and managing the bus services.
