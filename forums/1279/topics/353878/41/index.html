    No, I do not work for SMRT. I met my ex-colleague who used to work for SBS, but now working for SMRT. He was part of the crew who were transferred to SMRT when SBS relinquished the Bukit Batok services many years ago.

    The reason for the better acceleration is due to the lower 1st and 2 gearings, on top of a more torquey engine.

    As a former driver, I will say every bus is good. It is the LTA which imposes speed limit regulations. Of course the depot has to comply. But I know some bus drivers have got the depot mechanics to adjust the speed limiter. We used to call "kuui you" in hokkien, meaning to disengage the speed limiter.

    Most buses can attain 100km/h without problem. Look at the M'sian overland coaches, many are not well maintained, yet you see them hitting 100km/h. It is the speed limiter that is the problem.
