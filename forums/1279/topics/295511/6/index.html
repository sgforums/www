    I did not have much time to take pictures, but here is a pic of their new WAB in Hobart, Tasmania. It was taken in Sandy Bay area.



    The other thing interesting is that they use Volvo articulated bus there, with an ancient bodywork and it really sounds like the strider. Guess its the gear box. They also do use the Scania 113 there.

    They have got a tourist bus called Red Decker. It works like Hippo tours where you can buy a day pass and hop on and off. The Red Decker company uses the old Mercs and Leyland double decker NAC bus we used to have.

    Also, even their old buses, all of them have got the kneeling function.

    Lastly, the quality is a bit lousy because I used my phone.

    I am in Melbourne, Victoria now, I'll see if I have time to get more pics.

    P.S. Quite a number of buses here are equipped with the Volgren CR221 bodywork here, and their buses can really speed because there is no limit. For example, the skybus low floor Scania articulated bus I took from the airport was travelling at 110 km/h for quite a while from Melbourne Tullamarine Airport to Southern Cross station if you know where that is.
