    Indeed, read the following news:

    +++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++

    KTM hit by thefts, thieves even tried to take coach
    Sean Augustin

    KUALA LUMPUR, Tues.
    --------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Thieves are getting bolder. Stealing signalling and electrification equipment on railway tracks has become commonplace, but earlier this year authorities foiled an attempt to use a crane to steal an old railway wagon in Ipoh in October.
    KTM is losing RM3 million every year to thieves, who mainly sell the items to scrap-metal dealers.

    They have stolen cables, metal clips, brackets, base plates and the metal doors to 25kv circuit-breakers.

    Unable to patrol the length of the railway tracks, its managing director, Datuk Mohd Salleh Abdullah, said today the company had been offering RM1,000 rewards to informers.

    In the first 11 months of this year, there were 170 cases of track equipment and 49 cases of signalling and electrification equipment being stolen.

    This was almost double the loss over the same period last year, despite measures taken by the company such as changing copper or aluminum cables to galvanised steel cables, installing anti-vandal fasteners, and increasing patrols by its auxiliary police and track staff.

    He said the rising incidence of such thefts was due to the rise in metal prices.

    Such activities had disrupted rail services, sometimes causing hour-long delays.

    Salleh said KTMB would continue to work with the Transport Ministry to curb such incidents.

    "The ministry should lead the way for the sake of the public," he said.

    He added its efforts against theft had led to retaliation. "Sometimes when we apprehend vandals or thieves and report them to the police, people from the same villages throw stones at our trains."

    Until October, there had been 43 cases of trains being pelted with stones, he said.

    Source: NST Online.
