  • sgbuses
    10 Aug 09, 20:12

    I can only think of one explanation - the database controller chip for LAWO SICMA may not be backward-compatible with existing LLE flip-dot controllers.

    If this is indeed correct, this is probably what happened :

    - Depot staff in long-term wants to standardize all future EDS to display route number on right. For legacy EDS, they would keep in existing format as they might not want to reprogram everything all over again.Â  (Because it involves mini LAWO, Hanover, Mobitec ELBO and Twin Vision/Transit Media EDS with different databases)

    - New SICMA EDS replaces a single unit. Depot staff is unable to retrieve data from an existing SICMA EDS controller in time with number desto on left, or it might have been lying around in another depot. In a rush, he creates some common destos on the software and downloads it into new single unit and puts the unit back into service until they can retrieve the data from an existing one or another depot.

    - In other cases, they don't have an existing unit either, but have a spare OC lying around. In that case, data is synced from the OC to the new unit until they can retrieve from an existing replaced SICMA EDS. Hence the case of desto number on the right.
