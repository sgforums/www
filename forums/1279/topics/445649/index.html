Buses move slower as daily ridership increases

  • QX179R

    SINGAPORE: More people are commuting by bus, as ridership grew by 16 per cent from 2.9 million a day in 2007, to 3.45 million per day in 2011.

    But it also means that buses are moving slower.

    Minister of State for Transport Josephine Teo, who provided this update in parliament on Tuesday, said measures like full-day bus lane extensions have helped improve journey speeds along heavily-used areas by seven per cent on average.

    Another possible measure is to cross-train more bus drivers.

    She said: "Each driver, perhaps being just familiar with their own route that they drive on a regular basis, is also familiar with several other routes. This measure obviously takes time to implement because there are so many drivers and apart from their usual duties, you've got to find the capacity for them to also be extracted from their regular service in order to go for the training."
