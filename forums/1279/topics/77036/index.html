    For me, SBST as they organise their fleet of 2000+ buses better. It is NOT true that SBST drivers drive slower, there are plenty of examples out there.

    TIBS give the impression of being direct is because of the geographical location of the towns they control. Bt Batok and CCK are basically land locked areas. Woodlands is accessible easily as there are a lot of empty non residential developments surrounding it. The real problem comes for Sembawang, it has to pass thru either Yishun or Woodlands.

    However, for SBST, the towns that they control lie mainly on the MRT lines. This means that the fastest way to go to city is to bypass all the towns in the process, and as it is a residential area, the there are more traffic lights and pax and school zones. Hence, it gives the impression that SBST is slow and winding.

    Its a pity that CSS is going off soon.....the buses are very nostalgic, and wads more, the fares are very cheap and non rip-off at all.

    People who want to go to SAFRA Tampines can just take Sv 23, Sv 65 or Sv 67, the three most direct routes.

    Sv 28's big loop around Tampines is quite profitable, it connects the Ave 9 & St 32/34 areas to Bedok and beyond. The Ave 9 area is only served by Sv 29 (with its lousy frequency and how many ppl want to go to Changi Village?) and Sv 293 while the St 32/34 section is only served by a single Sv 291.

    Sv 28 remains quite profitable, despite its very winding route. I was once at the bus stop at Tampines Ave 2 and as many as 10x more people went for the Sv 28 VO than the Sv 8 MK4.
