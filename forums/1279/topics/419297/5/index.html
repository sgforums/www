    From iTODAY:Bukit Panjang commuters crowded off service 966

    Letter from Ho Kwan Oi | Jul 14, 2011 8:05 PM

    On Monday morning at 7.30 am, I went to the bus stop before Pending LRT station to wait for bus service 966 which would bring me to my workplace at East Coast Parkway. The bus came but was overloaded with people. Obviously all those waiting could not board the bus. Five buses of service 966 passed by without stopping because the buses were overloaded.

    At 8.15 am, I boarded the sixth bus that came along but it was filled to capacity. Bus service 966 starts from Woodlands Interchange and passes through Woodlands and Marsiling estates. How on earth do Bukit Panjang commuters board this service when all buses are full? It caused me to be late at work on Monday morning.

    Now the transport operators seek fare increases. Singapore is flooded with foreign workers and we have to struggle with them to get on to buses. Operators have to deal with this problem first and fare increases later.

    Would the authority consider creating a service 966A or B which will operate from Bukit Panjang Interchange to East Coast Parkway from 7am to 8.30 am (just like service 190B from Chua Chu Kang Interchange to Dhoby Ghaut station)? This will help solve the problem for Bukit Panjang commuters.

    Reply

    Plans in place to further enhance Service 966

    WE THANK Ho Kwan Oi for his feedback on Service 966, "Bukit Panjang commuters crowded off Service 966" (Today Online, July 15). We would like to share that plans are in place to further enhance capacity for the service, including deploying new buses.

    Since February this year, we have made two rounds of enhancements to the service by adding more trips and buses to increase capacity and improve frequency.

    Service 966 operates at a frequency of 8 to 10 minutes during morning peak hours. We are mindful that wet weather, peak hour traffic, accidents, heavy passenger loading and inconsiderate roabehaviour can cause delays, and would like to take this opportunity to encourage passengers to move to the rear of the bus to allow more to board, and motorists to give way to buses in bus lanes.
