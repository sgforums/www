    History of Bendy Bus deployments

    TIB838H was first introduced on service 171 as a demo in March 1996.

    When service 960 was introduced in July that year, TIB838H was then withdrawn on 171 to serve on 960 instead.

    TIB838H was somehow transferred to service 176 somewhere in 1997.

    When TIB849B and its gang came in, it was then deployed mainly on services 82, 176, 180, 184, 812, 851, 856, 903, 912, 913 and 960. Service 160 (now 980) also received Bendy cameos once a while during sundays & public holidays.

    Also remembered that some bendies were also deployed on the new Rapidbus 700 when introduced in 1998. It was only for a certain period of time.

    In 1999, when Tibs exchanged Sengkang/Punggol/Jln Kayu service in exchange for CCK services, the bendies were then introduced on service 190 and 300, later on service 67 and 188 for trial run.

    Tibs then bought in another 200+ bendy buses (Hispano, Habit and Volgren models) in between 2000-2001, more services introduced Bendies during this period.

    Up-to-date info on Bendy bus deployments in yr 2006:-

    Perm full fleet: 180, 184, 300, 302, 800, 804, 806, 812, 856, 900, 903, 912, 913, 960, 962, 964

    Perm partial fleet: 61, 67, 106, 169, 172, 176, 187, 188, 189(??), 190, 700, 811, 851, 854, 859, 901, 911, 963, 969

    Cameos (during higher demand periods): 857, 980

    Seen before since year 2000: 171, 178, 307, 365 (now 945), 367 (now 947), 858A, 920, 985

    I think it should be around 2002, sv 300 & 302 received full bendy fleets.
    Mostly Sv 300 & 302 used Hinos, DAFs & some MB O405 buses before the 2 svcs uses bendies.
    Service 300 started operating as a PAC with bendies as AC buses and NAC MB0405s upon handover to TIBS. The NACs were later converted to CAC and were transferred to other services. The newer bendies like Habit and Hispano were then introduced to make up for the MB0405s, hence having a full bendy fleet. I think is somewhere in 2000-2001.

    As for service 302 and 307, it was operated with MB0405 OAC and NAC for a start, later NAC ones convert to CAC in 2000. There were some bendies as cameos. I remember got one time when 302 and 307 operate with full DAF fleet for a short period of time before reverting back to MB0405s. 302 was later replaced by full bendy fleet in 2002-2003 due to popular demand, whereas 307 remain as a full MB0405 perm fleet.
