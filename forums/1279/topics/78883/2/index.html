    Service 21 was introduced in 1993, plying between Pasir Ris int and Lor 1 Geylang ter. In 1994, merged with service 4 (Tampines - St. Michael's)

    Service 23 originally plied between Tampines int and Tomlinson Rd (loop), shortened to Rochor Canal Rd after MRT went to Orchard.

    Service 27 originally plied between Tampines int and Changi Airport PTB, once extended to Pasir Ris int but later shortened back to Tampines with the extension of service 53. In July 1999, extended to Sengkang int. In Dec 2002, extended again to Hougang Ctrl int.

    Service 28 orginally plied between Tampines int and New Bridge Rd ter, withdrawn in 1992. Re-introduced in 1994, plying between Tampines int and Bedok int. In 2000, merged with service 152 and extended to Toa Payoh int.

    Service 29 originally plied between Tampines int and CACC. In 1993, trips were truncated to loop at Changi Village terminal after 1930 hrs. In 1994, amended to loop at Changi Village terminal with the re-introduction of service 19.

    Service 30 was amended to terminate at Boon Lay int in July 1990, with the closure of Jurong int. In June 1992, amended to ply ECP and Fort Rd.

    Service 31 originally plied between Tampines int and Stadium Blvd. Merged with service 149 in 1993.

    These 2 services were etched in my memory during my 4 years of living in Jurong East St. 24 (1984-198
    76 (OMO 40c) - Clementi Interchange to Jurong East Ave 1 (loop)
    76 was the first service to Jurong East New Town. It was introduced in 1984 and provided a vital connection from the infant town to the nearest developed town (ie. Clementi). When Jurong East Interchange was opened in June 1985, sv. 76 was amended to start from Jurong East and loop at Clementi Interchange. It was withdrawn by years' end because there were other services (namely 7, 147 and 333) which shared its route.

    79 - Jurong East Terminal to Bras Basah Rd (loop)
    79 got the dubious honour of being the first trunk service in Jurong East. It was also introduced in 1984 and mostly followed the route of 147. In fact, its demise was due to its integration with 147. 79 made a comeback in June 1985, plying from the new Jurong East Interchange to Empress Place, its route similar to that of 97 today (ie. via AYE and Alexandra Rd). The second demise of 79 was caused when 97 (which originally plied via Clementi Rd and Pasir Panjang Rd) was amended to ply 79's route instead. Seemingly attached to Jurong East, it made its second comeback there in 1993, this time going to Boon Lay Interchange. Its present route came after an amendment made in 1994.
