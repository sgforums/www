How to be a good bus-spotter: A guide

  • tintinspartan

    I'm making a guide here for those who want to take up bus-spotting as a hobby.

    First Thing you might do is research. For example, you want to ride a Volvo B10M Mark II. Go to sgwiki and research on route deployments. See if your desire-to-ride bus is on which service. Consider the route pattern. If you want to ride a bus on a highway, take all the service numbers that your desired buses are in. Scout the route out on SBS Service number enquiry or SMRT Electronic Bus Guide.

    Things you need:

    A good bus-spotter would never go out on an empty stomach. Have a sumptuos lunch but don't eat too much. If you are bus-stopping after work or school, have a meal first before you head out. You don't want to have an empty stomach waiting for a desired bus. Bring a snack or two for reserves in case you get hungry waiting.

    Next, is judging what time it will arrive. If you are riding a desired bus on a low-demand route, it's frequency will tend to be long (27-59 mins). If you are riding it on a high-demand route, the frequency would be about 5 - 12 minutes. If there's a bus timetable at the bus stop, refer to it.

    Bring a camera. If you want to take a picture of the bus, you might want to use a high quality mobile phone camera. It will no cause suspicion among others. If not, be brave if you are taking it on a SLR camera or digital camera.

    Lady luck. Sometimes, luck could help you. Sometimes, your desired bus may be on another service, let's say your regular neighbourhood service filled with Bendy buses. One day, you see your desired bus making an unexpected cameo on it. Cameo is a bus lingo meaning, 'a spare bus which replaces a faulty perm bus. Some service timetables require a certain bus to do 2 services back to back.

    Choose a comfortable bus stop. Wait for your desired bus to arrive. Just don't choose the wrong bus stop at the wrong time. If you do, the bus captain will be very suspicious.

    Be a curious fox. Try to predict which bus would come out first. If you see your bus just arriving at the bus interchange, see what service it's on and spot other buses which are on that service. If you spot that bus was on that service only, you could wait or have a toilet-break. If there's two to three buses, you can have a comfortable meal without rushing. It depends on the service frequency. Try to time each bus departure on the service. I recommend 5 minutes for feeders and 10 minutes for trunk services. low passenger demad services could be timed at 15-25 minutes.

    Get a concession or season pass. Use the advantage of unlimited rides benefit. This would save money from wasting it on fares for bus-spotting.

    If you have all this, you're on your way to become a good bus-spotter.

    well it depends on what type of bus spotter you are..

    you can spot

    1. any bus rego of that model
    2. a specific bus rego
    3. a cameo (combination of both 1 and 2)

    Cameo spottings be it rare or not are a good gauge of your spotting skills, spotting buses of a certain model serves as a value added thing in your spotting skills.

    say for example you receive information or you spot a bus in the mid afternoon around 4pm and at that moment you are not able to spot the bus immediately. So what you do is to calculate the time taken from that spot to the interchange or looping point, and back to the other interchange.

    and timing is very important, 4pm is a common time for split shift buses to gear into action. So whether the cameo is on split shift or full day depends on your estimation as well as whether you know who drives the bus. Always assume the bus is on split shift. This gives you more kick into spotting that bus asap as well as snapping the bus.

    Last time i used to estimate the timings and all but now with iris its so much easier to spot buses, be it B9 or not. In addition, e-timetables on the sbst website helps also.

    1) Spot buses whenever you go, no matter where you are. I do that while in another bus, MRT or even walking down the road or to the escalator (in WRI). I don't camp in interchanges because I am still schooling and have much more stuff to do. Regos can be seen on the MRT, but if you are not sharp-eyed or have fast reaction you would get the numbers wrong (like me, especially SBST regos)

    2) I agree that knowing the timing is good, especially for those with has one type of bus in its perm fleet (Example is svc. 911). If you happen to live in a flat right beside a bus stop or road you can just listen to the gearbox sound to differenciate them. Tried this on Service 900 perms and it works, but you would have to remember the order of gearbox sounds if you don't bother to look down. (reminds me of remembering, Voith, ZF, ZF, Voith one day :lol

    3) Recognize buses by ads. Example TIB1049L is the only Habit with Ban Leong Pawnshop side ad. so remember it for easy reference when it cameos

    4) Take a notebook along to write down the numbers. use your memory you will make your brain suffer, though I do that frequently

    5) Beat the Boredom. Take some friends out with you so that you can chat while spotting cameos. Just don't talk till you forget your motive there.

    6) Don't spot at the bus stop with like 1 service on it. Go to the one with the most services on it. For me when I go home from school I don't take 911 from the bus stop outside because I know the next nearest bus stop serves every single service coming out from WRI in that direction. The best thing to do is to go to the bus stop outside the interchange

    7) Want to relax while spotting cameos? Go to a bus stop where a coffee shop is just right beside it. You can drink coffee, eat roti-prata while sitting down spotting cameos

    For photographers: Try not to capture the Bus Stop in the shot. Looks obstructing. For beginners maybe they would like to go to bus stops where there isn't a shelter and is just located beside shophouses. The metal pole which shows all the services denotes the bus stop, however these bus stops may be extremely crowded so be careful. These type of bus stops are still around in the Aljunied area
