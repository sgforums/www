    The 1st air-con SBS service was 168, which plied from Ang Mo Kio to Orchard Road.

    However, the first SBS service to ever get an air-con bus was 143. The bus was the Leyland National II which was a demonstrator (or trial) bus. It lasted for a short time before the bus was sold overseas.

    Before SBS was formed, there were already air-con buses, but these were very rare and I do not have any exact details on them.

    19 Feb 04, 20:08
    The first SBS air-con bus, a Leyland National 2, came in 1982. It was put on trial for a short period on sv. 143 (Toa Payoh - Jurong Rd 16.2 km).

    Svs 3 and 168 had existed since 1980, both were NAC in the beginning. When SBS bought over 8 Mercedes O302 buses from SABS, they were put on SBS's first air-con service in 1984. This new service was called AIRCON 168. Route details was the same as the normal sv. 168 except that AIRCON 168 operated from 0630 - 1645 with no operation on Sundays & PH. And it charged according to the air-con fare structure.

    Sv. 3 was also converted the same way as 168. In sv 3's case, the whole fleet was converted to AC and renamed as AIRCON 3. So, the first SBS service to be full AC right from its inception was actually AIRCON 118.
