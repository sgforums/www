    One very nice question,

    Giro-link is a form of payment for public transport. It enables a person to automatically top up their Ez-link cards whenever their card value reaches 0 or negative through their POSBank/DBS account. On the MRT/LRT, the fare gates are connected to the Giro system, in the past, Giro-link was restricted to fare gates which are connected, these gates have blue coloured stickers to denote them being Giro fare gates. The question is, how does Giro-link work on buses, especially in the past during the farecard days?

  • ^tamago^

    All the while GIRO deductions are not immediate, and bank accounts with insufficient balance to proceed with the GIRO transaction may be blacklisted, and the farecard/ez-link card will be disabled. In the past, probably GIRO-enabled fare gates require extra hardware or extra manpower to upload the GIRO data of auto top-up so not all faregates have it.

    For GIRO farecards, same thing, at the end of the day, all the GIRO data with every other trip data will be uploaded and processed. The media used to store and upload all these is the black diskette at the left side of the driver's console.
