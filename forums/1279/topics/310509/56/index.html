    Originally posted by TIB537B:


    Then how come the bulge wasn't that distinctive?

    If you are referring to the DAF buses, well, consider this.

    The 1990 DAF SB220 buses had two-stepped entrances and exits (much like their similarly-bodied Mercedes MB O405 colleagues).

    the 1994/5 (?) DAF SB220 buses were basically the same buses, but the floor height was reduced on this version - they have one-stepped entrances and exits (like the Dennis Lance buses).

    Higher floor translates into more space underfloor to position the mechanics of the bus. The 1994/5 version had a lowered floor, which meant less space for the innards of the bus to be positioned underneath. I could guess that the radiators for both versions of the bus were similar in size, but with the lowered floor in the 1994/5 version, there simply wasn't enough space underneath for the radiator (after factoring in the other mechanical parts, most likely). I guess they had no choice but to design a bulge in the front to accomadate the radiator.
