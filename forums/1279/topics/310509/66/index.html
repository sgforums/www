    All SBST MB O405 use 3 speeder W3E/ 110 gearbox, and therefore the selector is normally "3". If "1" or "2" is selected, then the bus is running on first gear or second gear. Before the 60km/h limit, at "3", the MB O405 can attain a top speed of 90km/h.

    The MB gearbox should come with a kickdown feature for better acceleration. When the BC steps to "max", it is supposed to hold onto either the first or second gear slightly longer, which resulted in better acceleration. Many BC do not know how to apply this kickdown feature because every MB accelerator comes with a further step action to activate this kickdown. You should hear a "tock" sound if it is really pressed to the max.

    As for the effect of gearbox going to neutral then to a higher gear at low speed. It is due to the mechanics at the depot having selected a non-kickdown switch (economy mode). And so when the BC release the pedal and step again (most probably out of frustration), it goes to the higher gear.

    I have not driven a MB with a Voith gearbox, so can't tell how it works.
