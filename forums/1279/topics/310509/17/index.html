    Originally posted by loki_chaos:

    sure...my 2nd question is not answered yet...^^


    Mostly by nearest interchange.

    However, there are some exceptions:

    Eunos is under BRBP instead of HGDEP before.
    Serangoon is under HGDEP instead of AMDEP.
    Bishan is under AMDEP instead of BRBP which is closer.
    St. Mikes is under AMDEP except 131 which got their parking lots transferred to BRBP.

    The reason why the above 4 are true is due to the NEL Rationalisation a few years back, and hence the wierd depot controls.

    Some other services have exceptional depot controls too. Example is 198. It's under BBDEP full control instead of ARBP/SLBP partial because BBDEP is somewhere in between the end points of 198.

    If you have other individual services that you're not sure about, feel free to drop a PM. :)

    I would say that it is due to the reorgnaization of the districts (possibly due to the need of more efficient operations). The previous East district (controlled by hgdep) ceased to exist and had to be split, and there was a need to have a more balanced amount of labour and other resources between the districts. so the decision was made to reorganize Serangoon under hgdep from amdep, Eunos under bram from hgdep, and because of this, Bishan went from bram to amdep, with a slight increase in travelling times (it takes 8-9 mins from amdep to BSI instead of 4 to 5 mins from BSI to bram (using St 11 and gate on the centre median of Braddell Rd which ceased to exist))
