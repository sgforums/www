    7 Aug 08, 22:37
    Originally posted by Acx1688:

    Let me rephrase: eg [SBS2706K]sv36 starts its normal daily run till evening then return to BN. refill then start again as sv518 so that when its run ends, it's among the last few buses to arrive at depot to facilitate easy maneuver out of BN the next morning for its morning shift


    ok let's say if 2706K is on the split shift on 36 that doesnt jump bus, then the daytime operations is as per normal. The other case is that 2706K went back to depot because there's not enough P shift drivers for 36 and hence someone did the 5.5h OT shift.

    The reason why it started as 518 is because one of the 518 buses broke down and a replacement bus is needed. Since 2706K just returned to depot the starter or the driver himself may conveniently use the bus as his replacement bus. This is currently the only reason for a bus to hop to another service under these circumstances.

    Another case is ad-hoc crossover at the interchange because a bus is out of service due to accident/breakdown. There was once the 238 split shift indian driver did 90 using his perm bus 3693G because there's either (i) not enough drivers doing PM S shift for 90 or (ii) one of the S90 buses broke down.

    The other ad-hoc xover at interchange is when the driver change buses. say 3614K was doing P shift on S90 and broke down, driver swap with S238 3693G as 3693G is going to end shift and return to depot. So instead of 3614K driver going back to depot to change bus, he can just change with an S shift that is going back to the depot. Saves time though service disruption is inevitable.

    I hope you get what I mean.
