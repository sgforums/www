    Accurately speaking the engine is mounted, on a tilted angle 30 degrees NW to SE (as picture illustrates below). '30 degrees' is used by Scania to illustrate the engine placement as well, as it is 'raised' 30 degrees from a flat position. Nonetheless 90 - 30 = 60 which is also a correct representation. In that case, 60 degrees from upright.

    The main reason for this is to reduce compartment intrusion for lower entry (one-step or less) buses, at that time in Europe. Do note that this engine placement cannot be done to any existing upright mount engine, lots has to be done especially the engine oil pan, which enables the engine to suck up engine oil at a 90 degrees angle. Hence there is a 'out-of-shape' oil pan if you get to see the engine, you will understand how it looks like.
