    Originally posted by stooper:
    Bus handbrake use air lah, not like car use a mechanical form.

    Yah... When the BC starts the bus for the first time in the day, he has to step on the accelerator to build up the air pressure in the air tank. (The air pump is connected to the engine.) When the bus brakes, part of the air is used to brake the bus. When the bus runs, the air tank is replenished again because of the running engine.

    Most heavy vehicles (buses, lorries, etc) use air brakes. Beside braking, the compressed air has another function - to inflate tyres if the tyres go flat... (But people seldom use the vehicle's air tank to inflate tyres. It's very tedious, as I found out when I learned driving in the army...)
