    Handal Indah to spend RM5mil on new buses to expand services

    BY ZAZALI MUSA IN JOHOR BARU
    THE owner-operator of Causeway Link bus service, Handal Indah Sdn Bhd, will invest some RM5mil on 10 new buses next year for its Kuala Lumpur, Ipoh, Butterworth and Haadyai routes.

    Managing director Lim Han Weng said the company would acquire two new buses each month beginning January, increasing the number of buses serving long haul routes to 13 by the end of next year from the present three.

    Â“The company will start plying the Ipoh and Butterworth routes in April, followed by that to Haadyai in May,'' Lim told StarBiz in a telephone interview from Singapore yesterday.

    Lim said the company was targeting travellers from Singapore who wanted to go to Kuala Lumpur, Ipoh, Butterworth and Haadyai by bus.

    He said the company was in a better position to capture these travellers as the Causeway Link buses also ply the Johor Baru-Singapore route.

    The company has 45 buses for its services ending at the Queen Street and Kranji bus stations in Singapore, ferrying about 15,000 commuters daily.

    The company started the Causeway Link Express from Johor Baru to Kuala Lumpur last week, with three trips daily.

    Lim said although there were 50 bus companies plying the Johor Baru-Kuala Lumpur route with 100 trips daily, the route was still lucrative.

    He said apart from Kuala Lumpur, Ipoh, Butterworth and Haadyai, the company also had permits to serve the Johor Baru-Malacca route.

    According to Lim, the company would apply for permits to serve Kota Baru and Kuala Terengganu, and hopefully begin services before end of next year.

    Â“The routes are lucrative, considering that there are a large number of Kelantanese and Terengganu people who are working in Johor Baru and Singapore,'' Lim added.

    Sources:The Star
