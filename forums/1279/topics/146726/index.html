    Ride On Selected SBS Transit Bus Services To Enjoy Discounts On Mooncakes And Admission Fees To Lantern Safari

    http://www.sbstransit.com.sg/whatsnew_01September2005.asp

    In celebration of the Mid-Autumn Festival, SBS Transit has partnered with Tong Heng and the Chinese Garden to offer discounts on mooncakes and admission fees to the Lantern Safari for commuters of selected SBS Transit bus services.

    Tong Heng moon cakes
    From 2 to 18 September, passengers of Chinatown Direct Services namely CT8, CT18 and CT28 as well as CityBuzz Services namely C1, C2 and C3 will get $3-$6 off per box of moon cakes from Tong Heng. To enjoy the discount, all that the passengers need to do is to collect three bus tickets from any of the Chinatown Direct or CityBuzz services or show their CityBuzz pass when purchasing moon cakes from Tong Heng.

    This special promotion is exclusively for SBS Transit and is only available at Tong Heng Chinatown branch at South Bridge Road.

    Please refer to the poster attached for more details.

    Chinese Garden, Lantern Safari
    From 3 to 25 September, passengers of Service 154 and Service 355 can approach the Bus Captains of these two services for a $2 discount coupon. Upon showing the discount coupon at the Chinese GardenÂ’s ticket counter, passengers will enjoy a $2 discount off the admission fee.

    One passenger is limited to one discount coupon (while stocks last). For more details on the Lantern Safari, please refer to the website www.cjgardens.com.sg.

    Updates are also available in our website, www.sbstransit.com.sg.

    Temporary Bus Service Diversion - Chinatown Mid Autumn Festival 2005

    http://www.sbstransit.com.sg/whatsnew_02September2005.asp

    Chinatown Mid Autumn Festival 2005
    On 4 and 18 September 2005, bus services 2, 12, 33, 54, 63, 80, 124, 143, 145, 147, 166, 197, C2, CT8, CT18 and CT28 will be diverted to ply via Upper Pickering St and South Bridge Road as New Bridge Road and Eu Tong Sen Street (between Upper Cross Street and Kreta Ayer Road) will be temporarily closed from 3pm to 11pm for the Chinatown Mid Autumn Festival

    Temporary Bus Service Diversion - Mizuno Wave Run 2005

    http://www.sbstransit.com.sg/whatsnew_02September2005-1.asp

    Mizuno Wave Run 2005
    On Sunday, 4 September 2005, bus services 112, 113, 115 and 119 will be diverted to use Upper Serangoon Road as Hougang Street 21 (towards Hougang Avenue 1) will be temporarily closed from 6.30am to 9.30am for Mizuno Wave Run 2005. On the same day from 5.30am to 11.30am, due to the temporary closure of the bus stop at Kovan Hub, bus services 112, 113, 115 and 119 will call at the temporary bus stops outside Kovan Hub along Hougang Street 21, with the exception of the diversion period between 6.30am and 9.30am that Services 113 and 115 will skip bus stops along Hougang Street 21.
