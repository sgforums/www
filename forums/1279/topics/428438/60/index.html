    Originally posted by nfshp253:

    Maybe SBST should set up small Mercedes, Volvo and Scania service centers in their depots with full-time mechanics approved by the respective makes so that servicing will be better done and completed quicker. Especially so for the Citaro since it’s an integral bodywork!

    Most of the time its not because of the busy mechanics, but the lack of availability of parts instead that cause the delay.

    It's not surprising if it takes a month for replacement parts ordered to arrive (esp our euro buses), because air freight is very expensive and generally the parts will have to travel by sea. It therefore depends very much on the distributor or bus co. to build up a buffer of parts available for use.

    A possible reason why smrt tends get repairs faster is because they already have a shortfall of fleet to use, so money is more willingly spent to recover buses than sbst which has much more spare fleet to use. And also because of willingness to retrofitting with other compatible parts, e.g. tib700x's front, tib1238's front, some of the lance's unique rear engine cover, etc.
