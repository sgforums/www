    FYI, the engine of the O405 is also placed horizontally. Actually, the reason why horizontal engines are used on both B10M and O405 is not because of having a lower centre of gravity like what Subaru claims, instead since the engine is horizontal or "pancake", theres no need for extra space/room for underfloor equipment, which explains why B10M has a horizontal engine. If the B10M were to have a horizontal engine, the platform height would be so high due to the need for space for underfloor equipment that its platform height would almost same to those tourist coaches.

    Buses that are fully low floor, but uses vertical engines, has the engine placed in transverse position, in order to enable full low floor. An example is the N113CRB (altho not low floor), its engine was placed in transverse position, meaning engine is parallel to the axle. Another example of low floor buses with vertical engines placed in transverse position is the B9TL.

    The MB O405 was never intended as a coach. In the beginning of the 1980s, the German Ministry of Transport promoted the development of a new modern city and intercity bus. In response, Mercedes-Benz presented the 11.5 metre long O405 citybus in 1983. Production for the series started in 1984. The O405G was presented in 1986. The low-floor versions, O405N/O405GN were presented in 1989. These were further improved in 1993-94, by having its seats in the front section lowered so that it is attached to the floor and not on platforms like its predecessor. The new variants are called O405N2/O405GN2.

    The B10M, on the other hand, was developed as a multi-purpose bus. It is suitable for city, intercity, suburban or interstate services.

    There was a thread about the weight of buses and I had posted the unladen weights of a B10M and an O405 (sourced from the SBST webpage), which are:

    B10M Strider: 10370
    Mk III : 10300
    WA O405: 9550

    It is clear that the B10M is not lighter than the O405, nor does it have a light chassis (unless you consider 10 tons as light?) as claimed by our chicken friend. So, I would suggest our friend to stop embarrassing him/herself further. The superior man, recognising his faults, corrects them.
