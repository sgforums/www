    I have a feeling that both routes 400 and 402 will be withdrawn in the near- to mid-future, to be replaced by other bus routes. Quite likely, route 30 will be rerouted to ply the new Marina Bay downtown once East Coast Parkway is realigned; there is also a high chance routes around Marina Centre and Shenton Way will be extended into the new downtown, especially after the bridge linking from Marina Centre to Marina Bay is completed.

    Some good possibilities:

    • Routes 162 and 400 are merged and extended to ply into Marina Bay, looping near the Marina Barrage.
    • Same goes for route 162M, but it will enter Marina Bay via the new bridge linking Marina Centre with Marina Bay.
    • Alternatively, both routes 162 and 162M will have their looping/terminating point swapped, but will now ply through Marina Bay in both cases.
    • Routes 133 and 402 merged, and will loop or terminate at the new cruise centre.
    • Route 30 is definitely in for a reroute - likely it will run into the new downtown core and also serve Marina Bay MRT station, before going onto the future Sheares Avenue and climbing back onto the East Coast Parkway.
    • High chance of a new, temporary dedicated bus route for the Marina Bay Sands I.R. before the Downtown Line MRT extension opens.

    Moving further into the future, I expect that Shenton Way bus terminal and Marina Centre bus terminal to be closed down and replaced by a new bus terminal or interchange in the Marina Bay area, with all bus routes currently at these two places extended to the new bus terminal. Alternatively, both bus terminals could continue to exist, but the bus routes they serve will be swapped as routes terminating at Shenton Way gets extended to Marina Centre via Marina Bay, and vice versa.
