  • SBS3004X
    14 Jul 16, 17:49

    Another problem on 43 is 'pax hogging', a more serious form of bus bunching.

    This is how it works:

    2 buses on the same bus service arrive together. The first one is SD and the second one is DD.

    A crowd of pax board the SD, not noticing the DD. This allows the DD to overtake the SD. But it can also cause the SD to be full.

    But if the pax all board the DD, it slows down the DD and the SD may move off first if it is late. The SD will eventually be full if there are enough pax and the cycle repeats until the buses reach the terminal. 

  • SBS3004X
    14 Jul 16, 17:55

    However, bus hogging can be more serious than above scenario.

    If the SD is very close in front of the DD, but not near enough for the BC or pax to see each other, the pax will all board the SD. If there are enough pax, the SD will be full and the DD will be empty.

    Just go to YCK Rd or Buangkok and see how often it happens. It happens in 50% of all bunchings between 43/M.
