    Hey SMRT, we need more buses in Bukit Panjang


    STOMPer Lee says demand for buses far exceeds supply in the Bukit Panjang area. Services 184, 190, 700, 960 and 966 all leave commuters stranded regularly at peak hours there.

    Lee explains the situation:

    "Every morning, Bukit Panjang commuters face problems boarding SMRT bus services 184, 190, 700, 960 and 966.

    "Service 966 is the worst among the five. It makes a total of six stops here, but by the time it reaches the second bus stop, some commuters are left stranded to wait for the next bus.

    "An average of ten commuters are left stranded on average to wait for the next bus which comes every 10 - 16 minutes, according to SMRT website.

    "Many students also face problems boarding 184 in Bukit Panjang, where it is common to miss two to three buses from 7am to 9am.

    "Matters are made worse with the recent cuts in the SMRT fleet.

    "Similarly, services 190, 700, and 960 heading to the CBD also face the same problems, with commuters left stranded even though buses are packed to the very last step before moving off.

    "The problem is certainly not caused by commuters' unwillingness to move behind, but the insufficient number of buses plying the routes as demand simply exceeds supply.

    "I hope SMRT can do something about the frequency of its buses during peak hours."
