    Originally posted by dO|pH|n3 a.k.a JaMiE:
    Is it true that they only charge $1.90 for the maximum fare regardless of no.of trips taken?

    What if I took 5 trips in a single day and each trip cost more than $1
    1st Qn: No.

    2nd Qn: It'll cost you $1.90 plus the unrebated fare of the last trip, provided the first 4 trips meet the criteria of a "journey" (read below).

    To clarify this interesting point (this is, of cos, interesting to any bus spotter who is not on a bus pass!), let's see an excerpt of the latest definition (not that it has changed) of the Unlimited Bus Transfers system from the recent Bus Fare Amendment notice:

    "Continue to enjoy unlimited bus transfers for a journey at only $1.90... We guarantee a maximum fare of $1.90 on our basic bus services when using the ez-link card."

    The keyword here is a journey, which is up to 4 bus trips that all meet the eligibility criteria for transfer rebate:

    1. From MRT/LRT to bus, bus to bus, or bus to MRT/LRT, you must transfer to the next service within 45 minutes of exit from MRT/LRT gate or from tapping the card on the bus exit card-reader.

    2. NOT RELEVANT BUT INCLUDED: For all concessionary travel on buses or trains, the time allowed for the next transfer will also be 45 minutes of exit from MRT/LRT gate or from tapping the card on the bus exit card-reader.

    3. The first, second and third valid transfers on a journey must be made within 2 hours of the first boarding on the same journey.

    For the third point, in a gist, it means you must board the 4th trip within 2 hours from the time you board the 1st trip to qualify for Bus Fare Cap and each alight-to-board transfer should not take more than 45 minutes.

    Now you know there's no such thing as unlimited bus transfers for the whole day, but you have to take these buses in a string.
