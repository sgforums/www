    TIB 1215 Z has a "next bus stop" display that is fully working! Saw it on my ride on sv 67 from Bedok to Choa Chu Kang.

    Now i realise how the system benefits everyone and not just tourists: What if it's a crowded bus and you're travelling through some place which the view from the windows is quite the same for a few stops (Geylang Serai or Geylang Road for example) ? The next stop info is quite useful in this case to tell the stops apart.

    The display of the bus stop info text depends on its width. AFAICT, the full bus stop name as written in the Transitlink guide is used.
      [*]If the text is shorter than the display screen's length, it is bolded and no scrolling is used.[*]If bolding the text will cause the text to become longer than the display, it is not bolded and no scrolling is used.[*]If the text is longer than the screen even when not bolded, it will be bolded anyway and scrolling is used to display it in full. Text that is scrolled is always in bold.

    Unfortunately, only the front carriage display of 1215Z has the bus stop info. The one in the rear section displays the old stuff: Day of week / date and time.
