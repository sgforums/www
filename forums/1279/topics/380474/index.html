    Originally posted by Fryderyk HPH:

    Oh~!

     

    No wonder... I set it to 1600, thinking that the high the ISO the better the EDS would be captured... So the lower it is the better?

    ISO is a measure of the camera's sensitivity to light. in the daytime, using a lower ISO will give you less grainy photos.

    if you want to shoot a Euro IV K230UB and get the full EDS, your shutter speed needs to be 1/60 or slower. if not you will get a blanked out EDS. which means you need to do a really good panning shot or the bus has to be stationary. but at such a low shutter speed the camera will be very sensitive towards hand shake, so your hands have to be steady too.

    just to add on.

    F value is also dependent when taking photographs of a slower shutter speed. the higher the F value, the clearer the photo is, but the longer shutter time u will need to compensate for a picture which is not underexposed. 

    most compact cam users will experience problems in terms of taking shots of K230s under strong sunlight conditions since the F ratio is at F/8 max and trying to get a shot of it at 1/50s, F/8 will still end up with an overexposed picture.
