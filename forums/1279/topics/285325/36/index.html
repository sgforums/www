    From the broadly 2 approaches i know of, i see it as where one incurs the cost as ultimately the overall costs (monetary & otherwise) can turn out the same.

    Those Euro-4 diesels which don't use additives are very fussy on fuel, they only accept the more expensive lower sulphur diesel, which also is very limited in supply nowadays & only some fuel stations stock them.

    Majority of diesel fuel (especially in 3rd world) is the cheaper higher sulphur grade which remains widely available. So a noteworthy point about those diesel engines which use the additive approach is that they are less fussy on fuel i.e. they can drink the cheaper more easily available grade of diesel only that one needs to stock up on some additives, which only take up a small proportion in volume compared to the diesel fuel used.

    Of course one spend a bit on additives (like buying some extra cans of motor oil, brake fluid, radiator coolant or even WD-40 etc.) perhaps keep some always on hand when traveling to more ulu places. Then just look for the usual diesel station for refilling the 'normal' cheaper diesel instead of being limited by the relatively fewer low sulphur diesel refueling stations around the world !

    this post is for people who ask me "why 63"?

    i will generally refer to work processes in the European countries.

    the scania k230Ub Singapore is seen as a crush loading bus model. that is it allows for much standee room in order to crush load the bus until its capacity is over 100 people. much more than normal for a typcial 12m single deck bus

    Also the bus is best deployed on start-stop services. this mean the bus hardly reaches top gears then has to call at the next stop. such bus services genrally go through busy throughfares in a city. as u can tell, the Scania k230Ub singapore cannot hit a top speed of 60km/h. So it is best to put on start -stop service. typical according to european standra , start-stop services buses travel at average speeds of 22lm/h to 25km/h.

    so why?

    1) Scania k230Ub is a crush loading type of bus. service 63 during peak has crush load. but yet during off peak not much people take. thus the issue of little seats here cannot be an argument for not allowing the bus on 63

    2) the bus is best suited for start-stop city services. one fine example is 63 whereby there is simply no express sector on h service. almost every stop has alighting n loading activities

    3) many senior citizens live in macpherson and Rumah Tinggi ( source sv966 )
    the travel pattern for these senior folks is to go from rumah tinggi est to chiantown.
