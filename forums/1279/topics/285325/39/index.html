    topic: lets use words to describe SBS8033



    start>
    well first fact is gemilang does export bus to perth Australia. so they do have perth bus like blueprints. this bus should be using some elements of those blueprints.

    however i had been shown an interior of a custom coach K230UB pix so i will use that as reference.

    the first two paragraph just says the 8033 uses a more Australian styling inside the bus. On the outside the bus has a funny groove at front. which looks like its collecting dust. ah choo. grey grey de. other than that like gemilang front.

    The Australian k230Ub fixes the equipment up ways . that is instead of the bed like structure u find in front covering up the space, they lift everything 90 degrees clockwise and fit it up. this means the equipment box is now tilting into an oblong shape. this means more space to fit seats. HOWEVER 8033 did not fit any seat. oops

    the air-con panelling adopts a more volgren look (translates to more australian look) and is not in a beige colour but purple. teh furniture panel covers are in a nice greenish dark blue colour.

    there is the normal two step to the rear but u will find the rear seems a bit more sloped than usual k230UB. i put that down to the fact that the Custom Coach K230UB i saw have 3 steps. and to ease our step climbing burden i guess gemilang and Scania Singapore decided to do away with he third step and use a incline slope instead. its very a sharp slope compared to th others k230Ub flatter slope if u stand there.

    the seats used are the same as SBS9888Y that VSO de seats.

    the bell has a dweep sound before the usual ding dong. maybe circuitry between wheelchair bell and norrmal bell got tangled up or maybe is a leftover from a possible Australian-type bell fitting.

    in all one word to describe 8033

    gemilang perth bodywork around there.
