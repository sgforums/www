    AFAIK, none of the buses I showed had a Gemilang bodywork. The Dong Feng buses are bodied by MTrans and the rest, I believe, are CBU.

    There are several bus companies in the State of Pulau Pinang, which comprises of Penang Island and Seberang Perai (formerly Province Wellesley). The red and white buses are from Transitlink. Although they have services into Penang Island, their base is actually at Bukit Mertajam. The bus network of Rapid Penang covers the whole of Penang Island as well as nearby towns on the mainland such as Butterworth and Perai.

    Before Rapid Penang, the largest bus operator on the island was the Penang Yellow Bus Co. However, after operating for 58 years, it suddenly stopped running on 1 Jan 2004 leaving hundreds of commuters stranded as the bus network fell into disarray. Attempts by the State Government to improve the bus network failed so they turned to the Federal Government seeking a revamp similar to what Kuala Lumpur went through. The Federal Government agreed and so on 31 July 2007, Rapid Penang was launched.

    The remains of the Penang Yellow Bus can still be seen on the road. The buses now operate as the Milan Bus Company. However, their passengers have dwindled as commuters would prefer the new and modern fleet of Rapid Penang.
