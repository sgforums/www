    Originally posted by cck_190:
    What about Choa Chu Kang, didn't it have any terminal???
    There wasn't any terminals in the area of present day Choa Chu Kang New Town. Apart from Teck Whye Estate, the rest of the area was still undeveloped. However, there was a terminal along Upp Bt Timah Rd near the Bt Panjang Circus (now the junction adj to Ten Mile Junction) in 1980. Svs. 178, 181, 193 and 200 terminated here. Do not confuse this terminal with the other Bt Timah Terminal located near Ewart Circus (Beauty World).

    The terminal near Bt Panjang Circus was closed around 1983. Svs. 178 and 181 were extended to Woodlands Inter., Sv. 193 was cut short to end at Princess Elizabeth Ter. and sv. 200 was made to loop at Jln Anak Bt.

    Originally posted by thots:
    What was the name of the terminal? Is it Upper Bukit Timah Terminal? Than how about the location of Bukit Timah Terminal?
    Bus Terminals in the 1970s and 1980s were named after the road where it was located. I have an old picture of an SBS Metsec bodied OF1413 which was on sv. 181 and its desto simply states "Upp Bt Timah Rd". I didn't know there were 2 Upp Bt Timah Rd terminals until I checked Bus Guide 1980. One was called "Upp Bt Timah Rd (near Ewart Circus)" and the other "Upp Bt Timah Rd (near Bt Panjang Circus)".

    The "Upp Bt Timah Rd (near Ewart Circus)" terminal was later given the name Bt Timah Terminal. Ewart Circus used to be at Hindhede Dr, which leads to the Bt Timah Nature Reserve. The old Bt Timah Terminal area is now occupied by the Court's store.

    Ok, I am basing my findings on the 1983, 1984 and 1988 street directories.

    In 1984, there were 2 roads, Upper Changi Road and Upper Changi Road East which are linked together at one point. There is a split just after Somapah Road, near Changkat Changi Primary School there. There is also no presence of Simei Road at that time. Only Sunbird Ave existed then. See the image below.



    I assume that Service 9 entered the right side of the split roads, thus Upper Changi Road between Bedok and Changi Point. Either that, or it turns right at Somapah Road (note the small road between Upper Changi Road and Upper Changi Road East with an arrow) and left again at Upper Changi Road (similar to current Service 2). However, this is just my presumption.

    For further information. By 1988, this split road and the small Somapah Road were gone, to be replaced by current day Simei Rd which is still under construction then (shown as u/c inside the 1988 directory).
