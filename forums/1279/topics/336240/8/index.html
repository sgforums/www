    The true story about Service 70
    Things you might not know
    1. It was formed from Paya Lebar Bus Service Route No. 1 (RAF Seletar to Guillemard Rd). The route remains unchanged until 11 Apr 1971, the major re-organisation of bus services.
    2. Service 70 was under ABS, under the Eastern Group. Its route became Jln Kayu (RAF Seletar - Lor 5 Geylang)
    3. Its route starts from Seletar East & West Camp (inside RAF Seletar) then via Jln Kayu, Yio Chu Kang Rd, Upp Serangoon Rd, Upp Paya Lebar Rd, Sims Ave and terminates at Lor 5 Geylang.
    4. Service 70 extended its route from Lor 5 Geylang to Prince Edward Rd in 1977. But in 1978, its route inside RAF Seletar was also cut back to terminate at Jln Kayu Ter. Hence, SBS introduced 2 new set of routes - 214E and 214W. 214E served the Seletar East Camp (Picadilly Rd) & 214W served the Seletar West Camp. It was probably that Service 70 demand along the Seletar West Camp on certain timings were poor and therefore 214W had to serve Seletar West Camp on that particular timings that has adequate demand.
    5. When Prince Edward Ter closed in the late 1980s, it was laterally transferred to Shenton Way Ter.
    6. It was extended to Yio Chu Kang Ter in 1992, when Service 73 amended.
    7. In 2001, Service 70# extended to loop at Suntec City and Service 70 extended its route to Yio Chu Kang Link & Boundary Rd.
    8. Service 70 used NAC buses (VO2x & Scania N113) until end 2003, when the service gets a FAC Status.
