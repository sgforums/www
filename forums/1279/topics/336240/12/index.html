    Toa Payoh bus services in 1982:

    Overview:

    Bus services
    Toa Payoh in 1982 basically had trunk services serving the whole town, these bus services go one big round (or sometimes rounds) in Toa Payoh, acting as feeders, before going out of Toa Payoh to their destinations. The only feeder service, 230, serves the then relatively new Lorong 8 Toa Payoh Estate.

    Road system
    As seen from the map above, there are two roundabouts in Toa Payoh, one of them is the more famous (cos its near the town centre) Toa Payoh roundabout at the junction of Lors 1, 2 and 6, the other is a bigger one located at the northern part of the town, at the junction of Lors 1, 6, 8 and the slip road (now renamed as part of Lorong 6) leading to Toa Payoh North flyover.

    Also, there wasn't a signalized junction where Lor 4 and 6 meet near the sports complex, and vehicles from Lor 4 cannot turn right into Lor 6 westbound, vehicles who wished to do so had to U-Turn in front of SAF Reservists Association (shortened to SAFRA), which is the reason why the U-turn is so big-its meant for buses u-turning there.

    Also note that CTE wasn't completed at that time, and there wasn't a flyover at Kim Keat Link, so vehicles entering Toa Payoh from the westbound Jalan Toa Payoh have to use Toa Payoh South flyover (it was dual-directional).
