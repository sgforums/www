    let me explain on how a bus from one depot bus can cameo on a timeslot that belongs to another bus from another depot. This applies to dual depot controlled services.

    As you know buses are prone to breakdowns, accidents, traffic jams and what have you. In any event of any of the above happens, drivers in the service are instructed by the Operations Control Centre (OCC) or the timekeeper at the bus int/terminal to 'cut time'. it is basically cutting your break time (NOT meal breaks where its mandatory) and not the running time.

    say for example:

    Service 143 controlled by brbp and slbp.

    Order as follows-
    brbp, brbp, slbp, brbp, slbp, slbp

    Breakdown occured on the 1st slbp in the order.
    Revised order would be-
    brbp, brbp, brbp, slbp, slbp

    so far so good?

    let's say at Jurong East the departure times in the first order are:
    1400 1411 1421 1432 1445 1458

    Due to the absence of the slbp bus which was supposed to depart at 1421, the buses behind and possibly (if notified early) the bus in front will have their departure times adjusted.

    Revised in the second order as follows:
    1400 1414 1428 1442 1456
    OR
    1400 1411 1426 1443 1458
    OR
    any other combination as long as frequency is maintained or remain constant

    If departure time for the last bus in the order is 1456, the bus after that might suffer 1/2 min in cut time. If its 1458, schedule returns to normal.

    so now you know how a bus from one depot bus can cameo on a timeslot that belongs to another bus from another depot?
