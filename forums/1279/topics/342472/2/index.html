    Ok, the maps below are from Upper Bukit Timah Rd all the way connecting to Upper Jurong Rd (the junction of Jln Bahar/Jln Boon lay)











    Well, one thing I should emphasise on older Singapore Street Directories of the 1980s or even earlier than that, certain landmarks like churches (places of worship), cemeteries, community centres, markets were not listed on the sectional maps. Though the community centres and markets were listed on the indexes of the Street Directory, but the maps listed C.C. (for Community Centres) due to some space constraints. Some churches (or places of worship) were not even listed on the indexes and the map simply put "Church" on it. Another thing to point out is the old Bulim Chinese Cemetery, on the street directory itself, it was an un-named cemetery and basically it stated "Chinese Cemetery" on the map. However, if you check the bus routes of 174 & 175 in the Bus Guide 1985, you would see one of the bus stop landmark "Os Bulim Cemetery" of the Jurong Rd column (i.e. after the "Aft Corporation Rd" landmark).

    Speaking about the old Bulim Chinese Cemetery, as according to the map, it was linked with Hong Kah Drive, Jurong Rd Track 28, 30 & 32 as well as Lorong Kabong, which is made up of the entire land, although its a short distance linked from other cemeteries located in Choa Chu Kang Rd. However, Bulim Chinese Cemetery can also be accessible through roads that serve Kg Tengah (which is Jln Berseri) and drive through Hong Kah Drive from Choa Chu Kang Rd as a shortcut.

    When the Tuas Terminal at Pioneer Rd was cloased & demolished, the route of 175 was shortened to operate at Bt Batok Int and it was withdrawn later, means that it was never shortened to loop at Upp Bukit Timah Rd or extended to Lim Chu Kang. At the same time, Prince Edward Rd Ter was also closed and thus services operating from Prince Edward Rd were re-routed and called from Shenton Way Ter

    Service 175 was introduced a number of times. On its 2nd introduction, this service plies from Bt Batok to Jurong West St 91 and loop and it gets withdrawn again.
    In its 3rd time, it was reintroduced as Lim Chu Kang Ter to Upp Bt Timah Rd (Loop) service, using part of 172 & 206 routes, and before handing over to TIBS in 1999. Service 175 was re-numbered to 975 by SMRT in 2005.
    Later in 2007, SBST re-introduced 175 as a Clementi to Geylang Lor 1 Service.

    Total no of intro: 4

    One thing you might not know...
    Service 175 was originally Upper Jurong Rd End - Prince Edward Rd service in the 1970s, with a short amendment to Jln Ahmad Ibrahim.
