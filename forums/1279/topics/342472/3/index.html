    Originally posted by Khairiano:

    Service 172 and 175 (now 975) used to ply Choa Chu Kang Terrace. Choa Chu Kang Terrace was in the past a part of Choa Chu Kang Rd before it was re-aligned to connect with Ave 2 and re-named.

    The 2 stretchs of Choa Chu Kang Rd that no longer exist are the stretch that crossed over KJE and the stretch that ran through Blocks 460, 462, 462A, 465 & 469. There is a bridge that cross over Sungei Peng Siang beside Blk 460, this is the bridge where service 172 and 175 used to ply on before the re-alignment.

  • tranquilice
    25 Jan 09, 01:35
    Originally posted by Rk 2010:

    There used to be a road called old clementi road in the 80s. is it true? and is it true that the Jurong Bus Park was the then Jurong Interchange where service 154, 165,196 and 197 terminates at?


    Yes, the old Clementi Road was a branch off Clementi Rd that ran somewhere near the Sunset Way estate. Clementi Road was called Reformatory Rd before it was re-named. Clementi New Town was named after Sir Cecil Clementi Smith who was the Governor of the Straits Settlements from 1887 to 1893.

    Jurong interchange was closed when Boon Lay interchange opened in July 1990, the interchange was then converted into a bus park before re-located to Soon Lee Rd.
