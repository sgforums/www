    Do you guys know of any trivia about buses in Singapore that others might not know or have some misinformation about? It can be regarding anything - the buses, engines, interchanges, and so on.

    Let me start by sharing one that I know:

    Bedok Bus Interchange is not only the oldest bus interchange still surviving today, it is the only bus interchange to receive/once received all of the following:
    - SBS Transit, SMRT, and CSS buses, all in one go (the only other interchange to have this now is Bukit Merah Bus Interchange)
    - bendy buses from both SMRT and SBS Transit (Sadly, SBS Transit had sold their bendy buses off to New Zealand. The only other bus interchange that had this honour is Tampines Bus Interchange)
    - buses that are powered by both diesel and C.N.G. (Only Toa Payoh, Serangoon, Eunos, and HabourFront bus interchanges - besides Jurong East and Boon Lay - can claim this)
    - having both single-deck and double-deck low floor buses (Pasir Ris, Toa Payoh, Sengkang, HabourFront, Jurong East, Serangoon, and possibly Eunos bus interchanges are the other interchanges that can lay claim to this)

    No other bus interchange can lay claim to all four "honours" at the same time .

    Here's one that really will get you digging into the history books, and one that most people would had never known about...

    The first air-conditioned buses in Singapore were bought in not by SBS, not by TIBS, and not by any other private bus company, but by...

    Singapore Traction Company (STC)!

    Yes, STC brought a fleet of Isuzus (if I'm not wrong, their last mass purchases before they went belly-up in 1971), and two of the buses (registered STC 1 and STC 2, if I'm not wrong) were fitted with air-conditioning. These two are, rightfully, the first air-conditioned buses ever in Singapore, and also the first air-conditioned public buses in Singapore. The fate of these two buses after the fall of STC is pretty much unknown. Whether it survived long enough to become part of SBS' (now SBS Transit) first fleet of buses is unclear, but if it did, then it could also rightfully claim the title of SBS' first air-conditioned buses in it's fleet, way before the air-conditioned OF1413s, CAC "Baco" Victories, the demonstartor B10M with air-conditioning (all appearing around the mid-1980s), and so on.
