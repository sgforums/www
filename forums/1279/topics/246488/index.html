S'poreans find public transport satisfactory: LTA survey

  • SMRT Buses
    4 Apr 07, 22:39
    Eight in ten commuters said public transport services in Singapore were "satisfactory".

    Of the 1,000 public transport users polled in a survey conducted by the Land Transport Authority (LTA), 94 percent said they were satisfied by the level of train services.

    Buses got a lower rating - only 77 percent said they were satisfied.

    According to LTA, the main reason for this is because the trains enjoy a dedicated right of way.

    It is also comparatively faster and more reliable.

    91 percent said they were satisfied with the locations of train stations and bus stops.

    But one thing that most were unhappy about was waiting time.

    Only 68 percent said they were satisfied.

    Responding to the survey, SBS Transit said it is encouraged by the survey findings and aims to improve its level of service.
