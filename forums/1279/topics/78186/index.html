    Originally posted by cck_190:
    What I mean is that before the name SBS Transit was out, Superbus had their own livery, with the Superbus logo. Now, the dds are not even called Superbus
    When the Superbus came out in 1993, they had their own livery which was different from the conventional double-decks (Although the one which was built in Falkirk came painted in the normal design.) 997A (Super-Long Bus) had its own design and so did 998Y and 999U (the 2 Bendys). Even 9888Y and the Tridents have their own design. This was not the first time a different livery was used on 'special' buses. Back in 1980, the Leyland Leopard and Leyland Olympian demo buses had a special beige livery with the old SBS logo and pink over red stripes. The Metsec bodied MB OF1413, when it first came out, had its top half painted white and the bottom half red.
