    What for ?
    What's so good or superior about Korean buses over these European makes ?

    I know the Korean makes tend to be cheaper, as usual, but in a place like Singapore which is desperately trying to improve its quality of public transport, i really see NO room for these cheaper but more backward & lousier grade of junks to exist here !
    The land here is too expensive to house them & the commutors here do deserve much better !! Even in Malaysia they buy new M-B, Scania hardly Daewoos etc. nowadays ! They know what's it like the Korean makes from their existing fleets.

    Just look @ those Kia 'mini-buses' used by some private transport operators here, very obvious to me like they took a truck chasis with truck cab & cut out the backside & there you have a so-call 'bus' ! What sort of ride comfort can one expect from such suspensions ?!? Also listen to their (ancient) diesel engines & note how noisy, rough & harsh it still is ?!!? Even the newer Japanese diesels are better than those Korean makes which don't use the M-B diesels !
    Having tried the M-B Sprinter mini-buses with their state-of-the-art cDi diesels, immediately notice the 'great divide' between that & the Kia's e.g. ! Performance, silence, comfort, safety etc. don't expect the Korean lot to match or exceed almost any of the German, French even Italian makes around here !!!!

    So frankly, these Korean makes are more @ home in 3rd world markets. Cannot afford to have them in Singapore.
