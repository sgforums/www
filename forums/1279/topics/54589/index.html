    Well, buses or part of them will break down from time to time and the percentage is on the rise as now there are more air conditioned buses on the road.

    I agree that bus companies should call the bus back for maintainence. However, whether they have enough buses to cover all the buses back at the depot is one problem.

    As for 12m buses and single deck buses, it is easier to for the bus captain or service captain (Tibs) to be aware of the problem. However, for double decker buses and Bendy Buses, the problems often lie with the upper deck and the trailer where the driver seldom goes to.

    Sometimes even for a normal bus, the driver seldom go to the back of the bus and most of the time the bus has it's engine off or the bus is stationary so it's almost impossible for them to note the problem.

    To solve this problem, the best way is to feedback to the respective bus company and / or driver about the condition of the bus. You can also approach staffs of each bus company at respective interchanges. However, please note down the date, time, service number and the bus registration number. These are few important information for the company to call back the involved bus.
