    For me, i think almost all of you should have already know about the infamous 1st August TIB648P accident on 985.

     

    Firstly, driver was speeding like around 60 km/h along Kallang Industrial Park area. Only 3 people was on the bus. Then, the driver tried to brake as there was a red light but a van was right infront of the bus. The driver braked but it was too late.

     

    The bus hit the van at around 20 km/h. Damage of the van was horrible but the bus suffered dents and a shattered windscreen. The van driver and the bus driver argued at who is to blame for the accident. A duo of SMRT Inspectors arrived by chance only 2 minutes after the incident occured as they were doing their regular ticket check on a Sv 853 UMW Lance.

     

    The inspectors looked for witnesses and they asked me for evidence. The driver alerted OCC beforehand about the incident. I was taken on board the bus for interogattion with the 2 SMRT Inspectors. I told them that the bus was at fault since it was a red light and the bus was trying to stop from 60 km/h. The bus was driven to the Geylang Lor 1 Terminal to contact the OCC by phone.

     

    It was later decided to bring the bus back to depot for repairs. The bus was driven back to depot with the windscreen shattered and missing a side mirror. I continued on with my regular programme.

     

    The bus was repaired within 2 weeks and is back on service. The driver is currently driving TIB677E on 985 perm A shift.

    My experience wasn't an accident or a breakdown but a technical error on the bus itself.

    It was a few years back already....

    I was taking a MB O405 (WA) svc 97 to Selegie Road's Peace Centre for tuition. The bus had problems with the doors at the rear. Every time the bus driver pressed the door closing button, the doors would close but the "Door Closing" sign remains and a loud shriek that accompanies the sign persisted. As a result, the BC had to reopen the door and close it about 3-4 times at each bus-stop that the passenger alighted. Until the bus reaches AYE, after 3 to 4 times, the bus remained stubborn and the loud high-pitch sound persisted even when the BC reopened and closed the doors after 5 times. As a result, he had to leave his seat and manually close the door. As passengers, knowing that he had to drive and close the rear door after the passenger alight, we decided to exit from the front door since then.

    Here's one accident I can clearly remember. The most prominent, especially since the accident took place on the last day of CSS's revenue service on Friday, 27 April 2007.

    So it was about time for the last CSS bus to depart from Bedok Interchange (CSS 0195K). I was standing at the 608 berth waiting to see it depart.

    Timecheck: 1928 hrs. While I was waiting for that bus to leave, I suddenly heard a loud bang coming from behind me. I turned and I saw that there was an accident involving SBS 0420Y (26) and SBS 2803M (32). Both buses went back into the bus bay.

    On closer inspection, I immediately saw that the windscreen of SBS 0420Y was damaged. It's front cover had also disloged as well. I couldn't see what's at the back of SBS 2803M, though there seemed to be completely no damage to the bus (I saw this bus cameoing on Express 518 the next day). Some SLs and the transit security were seen hovering around SBS 0420Y and there were about two people taking photos of the damage.

    The passengers of Service 26 were transfered to the next available bus, SBS 0135X. I forgot what happened to the Service 32 passengers.
