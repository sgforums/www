  • wirelessed
    21 Nov 06, 23:12
    Currently, the most efficient mode of transport between the extreme eastern and western parts of Singapore is by MRT.

    However, the journey is very time-consuming with many stops in between. For example, travelling from Pasir Ris/Tampines to Jurong East/Boon Lay by MRT will take slightly more than an hour. Travelling from Punggol/Sengkang to Boon Lay takes close to an hour.

    For Singaporeans working in the west, such as Tuas, Jurong Island, and the military and home team camps, travelling is a bore, taking up to 1.5 hours each trip, including Feeder Bus transfers to MRT and vice versa.

    SBS Transit and SMRT should considering competing for bus routes serving between the west and the east/north-east of Singapore.

    Currently, both bus companies have similar but diverse expressway routes serving Woodlands/Yishun and Tampines/Pasir Ris/Bedok, and also Woodlands/Yishun and Punggol/Sengkang/Hougang.
    (e.g. 161, 168, 85, 39, 965, 969)
    It will be beneficial to consumers if they extend this kind of competition for routes between Tampines/Sengkang and Choa Chu Kang, Jurong East and Boon Lay.

    Also, LTA should also consider constructing a bus stop on the PIE between Stevens Road and Adam Road exits for the easy transfer of such buses and existing buses plying the expressway, such as 190, 960, 966 etc. With this bus stop, a passenger going from Choa Chu Kang will be able to have a smooth journey to Eunos/Marine Parade by 2 bus transfers, instead of multiple transfers from MRT and bus.
