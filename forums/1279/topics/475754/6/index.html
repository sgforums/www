    Originally posted by SMB128B:

    Yes agreed, but if only HK can follow SG's distance fares and have exit tap outs on their buses...


    That is something I don't hope to happen with HK anytime soon. HK currently do have partial fare and tapping out on some services, but implementing distance fare would in my opinion cause the entire system to collapse into sg's less than functional state.

    What makes HK buses fast and efficient (although it may cost a little more for long distance services) is that they run on a stepped fare scheme, with little transfer rebates.
    The huge implication that this brings is that on long distance trunk services, (e.g. any of the XHT services), there would be almost no pax boarding after halfway as comparable fares on "local" services is a dollar or two cheaper. This greatly reduces the travelling and runtime of long distance trunk services as pax would only alight, whereas in SG most of the trunks would act as feeder halfway through the route, causing a significant slowdown.
