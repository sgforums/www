    Trains: SMRT trains:

    They are in this sequence: 3XXX,1XXX,2XXX,2XXX,1XXX,3XXX(the 3 rear cars are + or - 1 digit)

    Third generation: Kawasaki Nippon Sharyo(black cars), black in color with unique sound and with baggage racks. Cars: 13XX,23XX,33XX. Bulit 1999 to 2001.

    SBS Transh*t: Alstom(also the manufacturer of the TGV high speed trains in Frace, Frace company) black in color, ugly looking. IMPORTANT NOTICE: SCARPPPPPPPPP THEMMMMMM. they have 5 digit numbers: 7XXXX
    There are a few cash trolley locomotives with serials 330X, which are usually fixed to 3 other cars from any other first generation KNS trains to form the 4-car train.. usually comes out in the afternoon.. duty codes usually are 9XX (i saw 966)..

    duty codes for EWL are usually in the 00X-04X range, NSL is usually 05X-08X, CAL is 14X range, NEL is 3XX range.. special duties (incl. peak-hour trains going back to depot after duty but not during) use 9XX..

    NEL cars use the serials 710XX, 720XX, 730XX.. the arrangement of the cars in NEL is such that for some AA, 710AA is the first car, 720AA is the second and 730AA the last car of a 3-car set whereas SMRT use (from first to third) 3XXX, 1XXX then 2XXX..

    another info is that the black trains use ZF gearboxes.. from the infamous incident when the new trains were retracted for a period from service due to faulty gearbox from the manufacturer..
