    I think you have a few errors here... Reasons:

    1. You need to check the layover time at both terminals. I know that you have only observed the layover time at Punggol Int, but please keep in mind that the layover time may be different at the other terminal (Upper East Coast in this case). Take 10 as an example. Service 10 buses layover at Kent Ridge Ter for around 10 mins - 30 mins (night time), while the layover time at Tampines Int is around 20 mins. Service 188 buses (SMRT) layover at HarbourFront Int for 10 mins at most, while these buses can layover as much as 20 mins at Choa Chu Kang Int.

    2. T (or TQ for SMRT) shift(s) do not have so many trips. A T shift combines the number of trips from a A shift with one or a few trips from a P shift, thus the 'three-quarter' name for the shift. Hence, I know that a Service 43 T shift does not have 7 trips, but about 5 trips. If you notice the timesheet, you will see that most of the time, a T shift will start from T1 (Punggol in this case), but end at T2 (Upper East Coast in this case), unlike an A/P shift which starts and ends at T1 or T2 (depends on depot allocation).
