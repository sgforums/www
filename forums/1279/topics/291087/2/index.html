    I went to Changi Airport's Terminal 3 today. Some things I noticed are:

    1. The barriers used before entering Changi Airport's Terminal 3 is the same as those used in Terminal 1; in this case, a crash barrier (without the 'teeth').
    2. As mentioned by 'sbst275', it's indeed a long way into Terminal 3's basement. It takes about a minute to get in there.

    So you might ask, what's in there? Why is it so long? From my point of view, I would think it's either the row of shops or it could be a place for loading/unloading goods (I remember seeing one).
    3. The signs for Scheme B's Service 646 and 651 are at the berths, but I'm not sure whether both services go there. I didn't see any Scheme B buses going there.
    4. When going from the bus terminal to the lift or escalator, the walking distance is pretty quite long too - about a two to three minute walk into the building and then having to go up the lift or the escalator. I had to walk past untiled pieces of carpet just to get to the bus terminal and back so that I could go back to Terminal 2.

    Do note that some info that I provided may be inaccurate. To know more about Changi Airport's Terminal 3, you can take a bus there by yourself.
