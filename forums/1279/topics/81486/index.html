    it should be noted that closed stacks are not available at JRL, nor at any of the reference sections of the other 2 regional libraries, namely WRL & TRL. tis is due to lack of space at JRL. meanwhile the closed stacks collection is kept at some NLB warehouse. thus, bus guides before 1979 are not available.

    another book on the same shelf worth mentioning is the Singapore Bus Handbook 1.

    the JRL Singapore Collection shelves is located at Level 3. operating hours of the library are 12noon to 9pm daily.

    I went to the library yesterday to finsh my work on the 1982 Guide (which I did). The library is truly impressive. And you're allowed to bring your bags, laptops etc. to the reference section. So those with laptops will have a less ardous time copying the bus guide by writing (like me). About eating and drinking, try not to do so. The library is not a makan place. Besides, you might get the books dirty. And please only take what you need. There are others who might want the books for real research work. On that note, after you're done, please return them to their original locations. These small, fragile booklets can get lost easily. I'm glad the guides have settled in the Westside. You sure it's just temporary? I mean the NRL section at Jurong East is nicely spruced up. Seems silly to dedicate a a whole floor for the NRL section only for it to be removed in about a year or so later. I'll return next Sunday. Oh yeah, if you plan to stay in the library for long, bring along your jackets/sweaters. It's bloody cold in there. I was shivering by the time I'm done.
