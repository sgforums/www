    TIBs, SBS makes official exhance of bus services
    ---------------------------------------------------------------------
    Sunday, July 25, 1999

    The first exchange of bus services between Trans Island Bus Services and Singapore Bus Services was made Sunday.

    TIBS took over Service 190 in Choa Chu Kang, and handed over three of its services in Sengkang to SBS.

    The exchange of services follows the appointment of SBS to operate the North-East MRT line and the Sengkang and Punggol LRTs.

    Mr Yeo Cheow Tong, Minister for Communications and IT said: "This is a significant milestone because it represents the first step in the process of converting our public transport system in Singapore into a multimodal system and this will enable the operators to optimise on the resources that we have put in place either in terms of the MRT infrastructure or the bus infrastructure and to enable them to optimise the usage of the system for the benefit of the public."

    Mr Yeo said with such a partnership, the multimodal operator will be able to ensure easier transfer should a breakdown occur.

    The process of integrating bus and train schedules will start with the operation of the Bukit Panjang LRT and the process will reach its full extent when SMRT and TIBS complete their merger.

    Meanwhile the exchange of services between the two bus companies will be carried out in three phases, and will take between 12 and 18 months to complete.

    And to show its commitment, TIBS has invested $57 million on 143 new buses for its new services in Choa Chu Kang.
