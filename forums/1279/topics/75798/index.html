    It seems no one gives the lo' down of these 4 buses. So, here I go.

    Pic 1:- Leyland Olympian ONTL11/2R. This was one of the prototype Olympians (Chassis no. B45.07) It was featured at the 1980 Commercial Motor Show in England, showcasing Walter Alexander's latest R-type bodywork. It was sent to Singapore as a demonstrator where it was registered as SBS5396B in May 1981. Interesting livery. The white was slighly off and the stripe was actually pink over red!

    Bus returned to England and was acquired by City of Oxford Motor Services. The front was replaced by an ECW model, along with the tropical sliding windows. In 1997, the bus was acquired for preservation at the Oxford Transport Museum in Long Hanborough.

    Pic 2:- Volvo-Ailsa B55-61 with Alexander AV bodywork. This was the only front-engined DD in Singapore. New in 1981.

    Pic 3:- Nissan Diesel 4R94. New in 1962 and formally owned by Singapore Traction Company. Ownership passed down to UBC/ABS/ABC, when STC went bust in 1971, and subsequently to SBS in 1973.

    Pic 4:- The one and only Renault PR100.2 (SBS30P). Used from 1986 to early 1990s.
