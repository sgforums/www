    They cannot be much dirty, but its mostly the locations of the depot, how the bus captain treat the buses and so on that affects the situation.

    I cannot confirm whether WL depot still parks their buses outside, but even in overnight parking like BB Interchange, some bus captains leave the bus doors open for the whole night. Thus insects of all sizes can enter and make nests/reproduce and do all sorts of things in the night. The main cleaniness is not really by the company but down to the individual employee who does not care about the cleaniness itself. How many buses are not cleaned everyday? How many AM shift thinks that the PM shift BC will wash the bus for them, and the other way round? That is the reason why buses can be so dirty...

    In certain services, S shift buses are always clean and well maintianed, have the least problem and accidents, because the BC feels attached to the bus, and its always under the care of one person during the day. No matter if it gets to another service in the weekends, the bus will become clean again when Monday arrives.
