    Okay, now my mighty collection of maths-bus questions with some minor edits. (I don't expect those who have did to do again.)

     

     

    1)   A and B are two trunk services. When A stops at a bus stop in Bishan St 22, B stops at another bus stop directly opposite the bus stop A is at. Both A and B terminates at Yio Chu Kang Term. Is the sum of service A and B a SBST service?

     

    2)   X, Y and Z are three trunk services. X is the smallest number and Z is the biggest number. All of them terminates at Jurong East Int and they are SBST trunk services. They do not ply into the city but they ply out of the western region of Singapore. After you have gotten the answer, take 100 away from X+Y+Z. The answer is now called G. Is G a SMRT bus service or a SBST bus service? 

     

    3)   K is an SMRT service and L is a SBST service. Find the product of K and L by using the hints provided. Then divide the answer by 6 and then minus 189. Now, the number is called BBC. What is 1 quarter of BBC? Is the answer a SMRT bus service or a SBST bus service?

    -         K and L duplicate each other for more than 20 bus stops but does not terminates at the same Int or Term.

    -         K was once an SBST service.

    -         L has permanent KUBs deployed and K has permanent OCs deployed.

    -         Both of them do not use expressways.

    -         Imagine that TIB1P is a permanent bus of K and SBS88H is a permanent bus of L. Note that they will never cameo on other services.

    -         TIB1P is at a bus stop. Behind TIB1P, there is SBS88H at that bus stop too. Then, TIB111A and SBS88H leave the bus stop. TIB111A turns right and head to an Int in the eastern region and SBS88H turns left and head to an Int in the western region.

    4)   P, Q and R are three Jurong Industrial Services terminating at Boon Lay Int. They only serve 1 MRT station. If P is 6 when added until a single digit and Q has the most number of fare stages of all, find half of R. Let’s name half of R, ZAZ. Round off ZAZ to the nearest hundred. After rounding off, is the number a service that terminates at Serangoon Int?

     

    5)   J, K and L are three trunk services that allow passengers to get into Changi Village Term. After J and L leaves Changi Village Term, they only meet each other at 4 particular bus stops. K has the most number of fare stages but does not ply into the city. If L passes by Loyang Point, what is the sum of J, K and L?

     

    6)   D, E, F and G are four trunk services that terminate at Ang Mo Kio Int. All of them ply into the eastern region of Singapore. D, F and G use the same route to get out of Ang Mo Kio. Thus, D, F and G are separated from E for a period of time. When E reaches Sims Ave East, D can be found at the road too. If G is a looping service, what is the sum of D, E, F and G? Is the sum a bus service number?

     

    7)   M, N and O are three trunk services that ply Jurong West Ave 5. N has the least number of fare stages and M is 5 when added until a single digit. What is the terminating point of O?

     

    8)   C, D and E are all premium services to Fullerton Rd. When added together, the sum is 1672. All of them serve different towns. Both C and E uses CTE to reach Fullerton Rd. The towns they served also have at least one CityShopper Services. The sum of C and D is 1103.
    If E is the second largest SBST premium service number, find the sum of C and E. Then minus D from the sum. Is the answer a premium service too? If yes, where does it ply?
     

    9)   H, I, J, K and L are SBST premium services that serves the western region of Singapore. The starting point of J is the furthest from the city out of the five services while the starting point of H is the nearest to the city out of the five services. K and L serves a same road but different bus stops. The fares of H, I and L is $3.30 while the fares of J and K is $3.60. H is the smallest SBST premium number. L passes by IMM Building while I passes by The Japanese School. All of them starts at 08:00 except for I and J. What is the sum of H, I, J, K and L?
