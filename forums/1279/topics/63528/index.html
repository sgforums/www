    Svc 107 did ply Upp Serangoon Rd, but i can explain why you don't seem to recall as so.

    Before 1986 (and probably also 1987 but not 1988 ) motorists along Upp Serangoon Rd can only travel to Serangoon Gardens and Serangoon North via Braddell Rd and Lor Chuan, or via Yio Chu Kang Rd. This is because the current Serangoon Central area was not developed yet. In other words, Upp Serangoon Rd then still existed as it is now, spanning all the way to Punggol Rd, but there just wasn't Serangoon Ave 3, Serangoon Central and Boundary Rd to connect to Serangoon Gardens and Serangoon North from upp Serangoon Rd like we do now. So they have to use Braddell Rd or Yio Chu Kang Rd.

    Svc 107 (and also Svcs 101 and 106) plied via Lor Chuan and Braddell Rd on its way from Serangoon Gardens to Upp Serangoon Rd and vice versa. Thus, the actual sector of Upp Serangoon Rd plied is only between Braddell Rd and MacPherson Rd/PIE, and then, i believe, was only cemeteries, a hospital (Youngberg Memorial Hospital), a few factories (Singapore National Printers etc) and a private estate. So, you may not have remembered as clearly, since there isn't a prominent landmark there.

    Regarding Svc 156, i could not tell you the actual route as i do not have the info now. However, if u r interested, try visiting National Reference Library Singapore Resource Centre section at Central Library Level 3 and get a Bus Guide that is between 1979-83. It is at the very last shelf, call number is 388.3221.

    As for Svc 136, the old route from Ang Mo Kio up to Upp Serangoon Rd (End) is unchanged since its introduction in the 1970s. It is probable the route of Svc 156 duplicates that of Svc 136. But the bus system then is different from now. Then, SBS tries to provide direct links whenever possible between places to make it fast, cheap and convenient, thus duplication is common. Now the MRT forms the backbone of the Singapore Public Transport system, and thus new ideas of transferring instead of direct links is introduced and duplication is now a taboo.

    NOTE: () denotes non-stop sector.

    Serangoon Gardens bus services in 1971
    -----------------------------------
    Associated Bus Services (Eastern Group)
    ----------------------------------
    72: Serangoon Gardens Estate - Tanjong Rhu/Rhu Cross via Paya Lebar Rd and Tanjong Katong Rd
    103: Serangoon Gardens Estate - Commonwealth Ave/North Buona Vista Rd via Yio Chu Kang Rd, Jln Besar, River Valley Rd and Margaret Dr
    103A: Serangoon Gardens Estate - Commonwealth Ave/North Buona Vista Rd via Lor Chuan, Braddell Rd, Jln Besar, River Valley Rd and Margaret Dr
    156: Toa Payoh Lor 1 - Upp Serangoon Rd via Serangoon Gardens Estate

    Singapore Traction Company
    ----------------------------------
    100: Serangoon Gardens Estate - Prince Edward Rd via Yio Chu Kang Rd and MacPherson Estate
    100A: Serangoon Gardens Estate - Finlayson Green (Loop) via Yio Chu Kang Rd and MacPherson Estate (weekday peak service)
    101: Serangoon Gardens Estate - New Bridge Rd/Neil Rd via Lor Chuan, Braddell Rd and Jln Besar
    101A: Serangoon Gardens Estate - New Bridge Rd/Neil Rd via Yio Chu Kang Rd and Jln Besar
    102: Serangoon Gardens Estate - Prince Edward Rd via Lor Chuan, Braddell Rd and Jln Besar
    102A: Serangoon Gardens Estate - Prince Edward Rd via Yio Chu Kang Rd and Jln Besar

    Northern group was operated by United Bus Company (UBS) (170 to Johore, 171 to Queen St etc) while western group was operated by Amalgamated Bus Companies (ABC), the smallest of all in terms of number of routes.

    Serangoon Interchange was moved to Serangoon Central in 1986-87.

    SERVICE 102 (Serangoon Gardens Estate - Prince Edward Rd):
    [1974] Serangoon Gardens Way, Lor Chuan, Braddell Rd, Upp Serangoon Rd, Bendemeer Rd, Jln Besar, Bencoolen St, Bras Basah Rd, (Nicoll Highway), Connaught Dr, Fullerton Rd, Collyer Quay, Raffles Quay, Shenton Way and Prince Edward Rd.
    And return via Shenton Way, Parsi Rd, Anson Rd, Robinson Rd, Market St, Cecil St, Collyer Quay, Fullerton Rd, High St, St. Andrew's Rd, Stamford Rd, Orchard Rd, Dhoby Ghaut, Selegie Rd, Serangoon Rd, Upp Serangoon Rd, Braddell Rd, Lor Chuan, Serangoon Gardens Way, Chartwell Dr, Burghley Dr, Berwick Dr and Serangoon Gardens Way.

    NOTE: Introduced before 1971. Withdrawn between 1976-1978.

    And return via Commonwealth Ave, North Buona Vista Rd, Holland Rd, Queensway, Margaret Dr, (Tanglin Rd), Alexandra Rd, (Delta Rd), River Valley Rd, Hill St, Stamford Rd, Orchard Rd, Dhoby Ghaut, Selegie Rd, Serangoon Rd, Upp Serangoon Rd, Yio Chu Kang Rd, Serangoon Gardens Way, Tavistock Ave, Hemsley Ave, Kensington Park Rd to Serangoon Gardens Circus.
    NOTE: Was operated by Associated Bus Services (ABS) before it was amalgamated with other bus companies to form Singapore Bus Services in 1973 and had its route extended to Jln Kayu (End).

    SERVICE 105 (Serangoon Gardens Estate - Prince Edward Rd):
    [1974] Serangoon Gardens Way, Yio Chu Kang Rd, Upp Serangoon Rd, Bendemeer Rd, Jln Besar, Bencoolen St, Bras Basah Rd, (Nicoll Highway), Connaught Dr, Fullerton Rd, Collyer Quay, Raffles Quay, Shenton Way and Prince Edward Rd.

    SERVICE 318 (Serangoon - Serangoon Gardens Way (Loop)):
    [1988] Serangoon Interchange, (Serangoon Central), Boundary Rd, Lor Chuan, Serangoon Gardens Way, Lor Chuan, Boundary Rd, (Serangoon Central) and Serangoon Interchange.
    NOTE: Introduced in 1986-87 when Serangoon Interchange shifted. Then, SBS 315 plied straight to Serangoon North Ave 1 via Yio Chu Kang Link/Rd. Integrated with SBS 315 in 1988.
