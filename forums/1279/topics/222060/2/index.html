  • The_Bus_Guide
    27 Nov 06, 13:50
    Sv. 1 was withdrawn about 15 years ago and this number was never re-used. For me, I have seen and rode this bus before. Sv. 1 had a limited operating hours. It ran from 5:30 AM to 8:00 PM and didn't run on Sundays and Public Holidays. Its route started at Changi Village Terminal and followed Sv. 2 until Crawford St. From here, sv. 1 went on via Beach Rd, Connought Dr, Collyer Quay, Raffles Quay, Shenton Way, Cantonment Link, Cantonment Rd, Outram Rd, Tiong Bahru Rd, Zion Rd, River Valley Rd and finally terminate at Delta Fringe Car Park or Delta Terminal. The car park is located between Gan Eng Seng Sec Sch and the MOE Examinations Branch. It can still be seen today but its terminus (crew rest area cum office) was demolished a few years ago.

    FYI, CSS also had a sv. 1 before. I'm not sure about its route details but it went through Orchard Rd and terminated at Marine Terrace. The Mercedes O309 minibus was used on this service and I hated taking this bus with my dad to go to my maternal grandmother's house; preferring instead, the Volvo B57 of sv. 16. My dad preferred the former because it was cheaper and to a certain extent, faster but it was always packed like sardines and very uncomfortable.

    In the past, when 51 terminated at Hougang South, 30 was longer as 30 had 45 fare stages & 51 had 44 fare stages. But now, 51 terminates at Hougang Central, and I think both are equally long (with 45 fare stages).

    Svc 10 used to have 44 fare stages too, and 30, 51 and 10 used to be the "top 3" longest routes. But 10 was shortened to Kent Ridge and lost the "top 3" status. 30 & 51 also lost the "longest route status" thanks to Express services like 502 & 506 and SMRT services like 858 & 989. (I kinda miss the good old days when 502 went to Sengkang and 506 went to Changi Airport...)

    Of course, in terms of travelling time, 51's "Top" status is undisputed.
