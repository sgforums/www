  • hseochin
    28 Aug 05, 03:25
    In Singapore, i'll definitely select the M-B O 405 G with Hispano Habit bodywork !

    As far as bus-chassi is concerned, the O 405 G offers 1st class suspension ride comfort, purpose-built low height gentle inclination ergonomic dashboard (instead of truck dashboard like some others !), excellent turn-table design in terms of function, aesthestics & finish. Not surprising since in Hispanos case here, that's original M-B hardware !

    As far as bus bodywork is concerned, overall it's the Hispano Habit because of its solidness of construction (hardly any rattles, crackles & squeaks coming from the bodywork part unlike the overall not-so-good ones like Volgrens & Duple-Metsec etc.), superior levels of fit, finishing & style.

    However, Hispano can do better by improving on the finishing & aesthetics of the roof-top ! Do not assume that all buses here are only 1 deck high & people cannot see the rooftop of the Hispano buses !

    On the bus-chassi side, select a quieter type of air-con compressor instead of the loud drony types now used. I notice the interior noise, vibration & harshness levels are so much lower only when the air-con compressor is off !
    Transmission NVH levels seem lower than on some O 405 here, but i believe it can be further reduced if they had used the M-B gearbox instead. I've riden on some O 405 via TransitLink in JB with almost no whinning, grinding etc. transmission noises ! Overall their O 405 are quieter than those here, although theirs not so well maintained as those here.

    I'm not professor though, as i find the world of engineering & business more appealing than academia.

    Steps on OH 1418 of course much higher OR more steps compared to O 405.

    Because O 405 is a low-floor citybus while OH series of bus-chassi are the usual high height type more suited for 3rd world markets with poorer road conditions so they allow more ground clearance hence higher floor height.
    Also OH is more for use with coach rather than citybus bodies, where the higher floor enables the space underneath to be used as luggage compartment.
