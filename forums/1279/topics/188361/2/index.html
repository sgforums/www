    The seats of those SBS Transit Volvo MKII and MKIII buses, their earlier single-deck Walter-Alexander-bodied buses, as well as those SMRT Nissans (and some of the seats on their bendy buses) always give me trouble with their tiny legroom for my long legs. The Walter-Alexander-bodied MKIIs and the seats behind the rear wheel of the Nissans are the worst - I think even children may have trouble with them. I really don't understand why then-TIBS didn't use the old seat configurations found on those older Nissans (the non-air conditioned ones and the older converted air conditioned buses without E.D.S.es) - they have a bench seat over that wheel, instead of trying to squeeze a forward-facing seat with such a high step.

    As for those with wide legrooms, I have to say all versions of the MK4 buses score the best, especially those with TV Mobile installed. The seats behind the second TV Mobile screen (immediately behind the exit door) is so generous I could even cross my legs over there and still have room to spare! I have nicknamed them "The Lounge Seats", as I always love to grab those seats, cross my legs, and just enjoy the view from the window (those Strider- and DM3500-bodied versions have especially wide windows), watch TV Mobile, or just read something to lounge around. It's almost luxury .
