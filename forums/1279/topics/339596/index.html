    I am sure everyone here knows the difference between trunk service and feeder service.

    I am puzzled about the classifications of some SBS bus services, like 96, 179, 181, 194, 199

    96 serves between Clementi Interchange and NUS, looping at Kent Ridge Terminal. NUS is not a town.

    179 ply between Boon Lay Interchange and NTU which is located within Jurong West extension estate.

    181 ply between Boon Lay Interchange and Jurong West Ave 3 which is also within the Jurong West extension estate. Total number of stages is 8 (on return trip), 4 on single trip.

    Whereas 242 and 243 are classified feeder services with similar distances and fare stages. 240 serves longer distances and its total fare stages is 15 (return trip), and 7.5 on single trip.

    194 is plying only between Boon Lay interchange and Jurong Bird Park, less than 4 stages on a single trip.

    Trunk services charge based on fare stage or distance whichever applies. It is unfair to the 181 commuter to pay stage based fare whereas a 240 commuter pays only a flat fare regardless of the distance.

    Any comments?

    The initial plan was to extend 181 to Clementi, making it a trunk service. However since the load is so good, SBST decide to introduce a new service 185 instead of extending 181.

    246's main purpose as an industrial service is to serve the Chin Bee area. The Corporation area is an extension with the opening of the Boon Lay Int.

    Correct me if I'm wrong about this part:-

    There are actually 2 types of feeder services, namely the Residential Feeder Service and the Industrial Feeder Service. I think we're so used the calling feeder service & JIS that we forgot what the actual terms mean. Any service that don't serve communters between the Interchange & its connecting HDB areas cannot be classified as Residential Feeder Service, hence 96, 179, 199 & 194 are trunk services because their destinations are not HDB estates.

    The Industrial Feeder Service used to have a flat fare. It was renamed as the JIS with the introduction of distance-related fare on industrial services, dropping the term feeder.

    Back then service 241 operated from Jurong interchange. Feeder services from Jurong interchange were numbered 24x. When the services at Jurong interchange were relocated to Boon Lay interchange, service 241 was shifted to Jurong East interchange and later it was renumbered service 331 as feeder services from Jurong East interchange are 33x.

    Feeder services 378 and 379 were formerly 820 and 821, which were formerly 214E and 214W. When SBST took over services 820 and 821 from Tibs, it renumbered them to 378 and 379 as Jalan Kayu terminal is near Sengkang where the feeders are numbered 37x.
