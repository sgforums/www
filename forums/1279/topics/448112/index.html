    Some points to expand,

    2.1 There's some feeder services that generally only have 1 way loading during any time of the day. These services can should attempt to link to 2 different mrt stations if not already done so, to share the load of other services. Such an example is 811 which started plying khatib mrt not too long ago.

    2.2 As per what sbst275 has mentioned, routes should not be so long. What's the point of displaying the destination on the EDS when its not going to bring the passenger there in relatively acceptable time compared to taking a train, say <30% more?

    3.1 Bus stops with more than a certain amount of services plying, should either be extended significantly or staggered as per how the two stops outside ibis hotel at bencoolen street was. This is so that when multiple buses enter the bay, no bus should have the tail sticking out of the bay onto the road.

    3.2 As much as possible, feeder services should try to use stops that are not on the main road, or bus stops that are served by few trunk services. This is so that feeder services does not delay movement of trunk services, as feeders have much looser timetabling.

    4 More enforcement would be good, there's a european country (which i unfortunately forgot) that installs cctv on the front of the vehicle just to capture any cars that violate the bus lanes. The footage is then reviewed by staff, and those who intruded into the bus lane given fines. This could also be done for those inconsiderate drivers who parks in the bus stop.
