    New shuttle service to popular market and hawker centre for Tampines residents

    By Audrey Tan

    Residents in Tampines can now look forward to hearty breakfasts at the popular Tampines Round Market and Hawker Centre without having to squeeze on crowded feeder bus services. Instead, they can take a shuttle bus that will ferry them there for $1 per trip.

    It will ply the route between Tampines Street 32 and 33, near Ngee Ann Secondary School; and Tampines Street 11, where both the Tampines Round Market and Hawker Centre and the Tampines Changkat Community Club are located.

    The bus will stop eight times along the route, including outside the Tampines Interchange. Payment must be made with an ez-link card.

    The shuttle service, run by private operator Republic Express, will operate from 6.30am to 9.30am and 5.30pm to 8.30pm on weekdays. On weekends, the shuttle will operate from 6.30am to 10.30am at 15-minute intervals. It will not operate on weekend nights as there would be low demand.The shuttle will help reduce the congestion of existing feeder bus services in the area, especially bus service 291.

    * source Sunday Times May 4

    MR SAMUEL Ng's letter ("One gap LTA should've plugged"; Monday) mentioned that the Land Transport Authority shot down calls for a shuttle bus service in Tampines because it was "not viable".

    This is akin to the situation in the Kranji countryside.

    I have given feedback to the transport authorities about how the community of farmers and farm employees in the Kranji countryside has been neglected. There is no public bus service to the farms, and members of the Kranji Countryside Association have been paying for a private shuttle service for almost 10 years.

    Each time we make an appeal to the LTA or Transport Ministry for support of the service, the reply is that the Government cannot offer backing as the service is "not viable". I have stressed repeatedly that the farmers are not asking for a full subsidy or even a full-service public bus. We are happy to fund part of the service, which needs only to be a minibus operating at 11/2-hour intervals.

    Farms, visitors and the Government should all chip in to make this service viable and useful for the farming community in Kranji.

    With the rising costs of doing businesses, farmers are facing increasing pressure to sustain the expensive private shuttle service.

    The LTA should not assess appeals based simply on financial viability. Special communities - such as elderly folk in Tampines, needy Singaporeans and even poor foreigners - should be supported based on need.

    - See more at: http://www.straitstimes.com/archive/friday/premium/forum-letters/story/bus-services-offer-support-based-need-20140509#sthash.8iPlDU51.dpuf
