    For those who want to record sounds, here is some advice.

    1. Sit somewhere around the rear driving axle (for rear-engined buses) or towards the centre of the bus (mid-engined buses).  This will ensure that you record a good mix of transmission and engine noises.
    2. Hold the recorder preferably in the aisle between the seats but if that's not possible, at least hold it so that seats and other fittings do not block/reflect sounds in an unwanted fashion.
    3. Avoid buses with bad wind draughts, open doors/windows/roof hatches, or loud airconditioning systems.
    4. Avoid peak periods and busy bus routes due to the likelihood of "talkers".
    5. This is important for those with MD recorders: manually set the recording volume to a low level.  Otherwise you might end up with a digitally "cropped" recording, where loud noises become distorted.  Many MD recorders have an "AUTO" volume setting which can totally ruin a recording by randomly varying the recording volume level.  AVOID!
    6. Do not move a muscle during recording and make sure your phone is turned off.  The Sony ECM-DS70P is an extremely sensitive microphone that will pick up the slightest of noises.

    Regards,
    Dave
