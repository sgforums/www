    Singapore, with its expensive private car costs & land constrains, there's simply no room for lousy buses here ! Unfortunately the finer class buses & coaches do cost somewhat more but utimately that'll have to make more sense to have still.

    The OH bus-chassi range is what Mercedes-Benz is known to consider as 'export chassi', which i'll take it to mean as chassi of a spec level no longer 'suitable' for German or 1st world use so it's 'downgraded stuff' mainly targeted for 3rd world markets instead ! So no surprise if the instrumentation e.g. on the OH 1318 is of the old generation type seems unchanged in 30 years, eventhough they updated the engine to include turbocharging for this model !!

    BUT from personal VERY positive experiences on the OH 1625 L & OH 1627 L (both use similar 15 litre V8 engines with twin exhaust pipes) i find these models in the OH range are still of 1st world standard, as far as passenger ride comfort goes ! The air suspension is 1st class & engine noise vibration harshness levels (are lower than all the others here i've riden on !) When i say the NVH levels are low, i refer to NVH levels when crusing on the highway which are impressive considering they don't have any engine encapsulation @ all ! Otherwise they're only somewhat noisier than others externally when accelerating off from stationary, which is understandable in view of its big capacity V8 naturally aspirated engine.

    A bus or coach with V8 engine is to me never 'cheapskate'. Many operators still seem reluctant to buy such vehicles because they think it's not worth their while to pay the higher price for them ! I of course think otherwise & am in full support for V8 coaches, whenever the class of service offered is high enough to justify their usage !!

    The lower end models of the OH range like 1318 are of course nowhere as satisfying & i've encountered many instances of ill maintained vehicles with squeaky (spring only for this model !) suspensions with lousy 'donkey' engine air-cons which add much to the unwanted noise & vibration levels !
    But when compared to those Hinos also with spring suspensions only, i'll still take the 1318 anytime over those, largely to due to the superior suspension ride comfort from M-B's spring suspension !

    The MAN 18.280 is a high floor bus-chassis, like all the M-B OH & OF series.
    Therefore i'll consider as NOT optimally suitable for citybus operators like SBST etc. Instead such models more suited for intercity, outstation or tour coach use where boarding height is not so 'critical'. For citybus operations, the norm is low floor buses instead like the Citaro, O 405 etc.

    The next nearest choice to the O 405 or Citaro i suppose can be the MAN SL 262 or variants of this (like the SL 252 which i believe is used by Intrakota in KL) with purpose built low floor chassi & low profile tyres & O 405 like gentle angle & low height dashboard ergonomically optimised for citybus operations.

    In Singapore i know of 1 MAN (suspect is 16.240) used as tour coach while SJE does have MAN with automatic gearbox but mostly theirs is manual gear.

    What i've been saying is that high time the world class bus & coach makers like Mercedes-Benz, MAN, Saab-Scania etc. supply their integral PSV bodies in CKD form to Malaysia & do local assembly there (like what has been happening last few decades with M-B or BMW cars) either via their local dealers or sub-contract such works to the Malaysian bus/coachwork builders !! This way you can then get real original quality integrally built buses or coaches (similar quality standards like the cars) for sale both in Malaysia as well as for export to Singapore maybe even to further markets on a global basis. That way it can be possible to cut down somewhat on price but not on quality. Definitely better than the status quo where you only tend to see good chassi but mated to 3rd party bodywork very often of suspicious quality & 'inconsistent' design themes with respect to the models of chassi used !! A rojak situation giving the buses & coaches some real identity crisis which is definitely not desirable in the long run as buses & coaches are PSV's not goods vehicles !
