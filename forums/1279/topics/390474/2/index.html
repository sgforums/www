    A small sharing here of how a job of a bus captain (or bus driver) is like overseas in Japan.

    http://www.jrbuskanto.co.jp/recruit/driver.html

    JR Bus Kanto, recruitment for route bus drivers 1: throughout its operation sphere, 2: Tsuchiura area operations (similar to recruiting for a single depot/based at a single depot). In this advertisement they are particularly looking for people for post 2, perhaps of new routes/change of employment scheme.

    All drivers recruited from this advertisement are contract workers (å¥‘ç´„ç¤¾å“¡) and enjoy benefits of the company as a full time worker.

    Job: Route bus driver (basically as a bus captain here).
    Employment scheme: Contract worker, tests for advancement to full staff given after training.
    Requirement: 2nd type large vehicle (equal to Class 4 here, they can get this license class just by taking tests at driving centers, anyone above 20 can obtain) without demerit. No questions asked about age and job history (unlike local companies).
    Pay: Monthly 250,000 JPY and above, bonus twice annually, pay set by the company. Daily pay of 9000 JPY and above, able to earn above 200,000 JPY.
    Working hours: Depends on route, changes monthly (this includes long distance express bus routes).
    Benefits: Health insurance, retirement fund insurance (like CPF), employment insurance (more like basic insurance type), accident insurance.
    Holiday: Determined by the company, 4 days off for 4 weeks, special holidays available (6-day work week).
    Work locations: Shizuoka. For Tsuchiura, experienced applications will have bonus, aged people welcomed.

    Note: Brackets are added information not translated.

    250,000 JPY is SGD 3294, 9000 JPY is SGD 141 as of post.

    Considering the large amount of routes you may drive (from several kilometers to hundreds of kilometers depending on type), 6-day work week, this 3000 SGD would be hard-earn money. Minus tax and such perhaps left with 2600 to 2800 SGD.
