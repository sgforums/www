    the tertiary concession BCP is $52 and the TCP is $45. the HCP, which allows u unlimited trips on buses and maximum 4 cost-free rides on the MRT/LRT system, costs $97. for a 30-day month, the minimum current cost of rides u have to take on buses or MRT/LRT or both per day to make the pass feasible (break even) is $1.73, $1.50 and $3.23 respectively.

    the benefits of getting concession passes for tertiary and NSF only lies in the ability to buys these concession passes. they could save u a great deal if u take a lot of bus trips everyday (like us bus fans) or maybe u jus have to go to a lot of places everyday. i used to chalk up more than $100 per month as a NSF when taking buses but now it's a flat $61, a savings out more than $50.

    when u board a bus, u tap it like any ez-link card. the special thing, no fare is deducted - it jus shows "PASS USAGE", even if u have $0.00 in ur card. same thing with MRT, except it shows something like "RIDES LEFT 3". all ur rides will be free in that month, unlimited for bus and 4 rides a day for MRT/LRT/NEL.

    for final yr students, the decision is solely dependent on whether u need the pass to save on ur transport costs. unless u stay very near (spend up to $0.84 for a single trip from home to sch), getting a bus/train/hybrid pass will save any tertiary student a lot of money. even if i can only use it for a month, i'd gladly take it, needless to say for 12 months.

    ur concession card can still function as a normal adult ez-link card after u've graduated. of cos, the difference is only the fact that ur face is on it, and if u lost ur card u can get back the last balance recorded on the ez-link system and disable the card with ur I/C at any TransitLink office.

    Originally posted by kuekj:
    does this mean that for myself, when i grad from jc, i still can use that card, dun need to purchase an adult card while waiting to go ns?
    u're in J2?

    technically when u finsh your As, your Concession will expire at the end of this year. Thereafter it will function as a normal adult ezlink card (with NO concession pass) for the 6 months after that. However, by then u'll be NS and soon u'll get your NS ezlink card.

    To the rest:
    When u buy HCP, u CANNOT buy BCP and TCP. Let's say your HCP ends on 27th of the month, when u buy your nx BCP/TCP/HCP, it will take effect from 28th of the month. This is due to overlapping of the concession.

    However, when u are on BCP/TCP and it ends on 27th of the month, your next BCP/TCP/HCP shall only start on 28th. Same concept as earlier para.

    If you're on BCP/TCP and your nx pass u wanna buy is TCP/BCP respectively, the effective date can be as early as you buy the pass as there's no overlapping of any concession.
