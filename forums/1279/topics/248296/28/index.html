    Note: I was supposed to have replyed to your earlier post.

    To know more about BNDEP perms, this requires experience, simply because of BNDEP's laziness to update the stickers/markings. The first thing I would always see in the morning is at least one BNDEP bus. But it cannot be relied on just one person alone. That's why there are others who know BNDEP buses.

    All the five buses you mentioned are spottings by others not me actually.

    Some examples (adding on to your list):

    Former perms, markings that were simply striked off
    SBS 0069D (33 BNDEP) Former SP BNDEP. Even during it's days as a spare bus the 'SP' marking was not present.

    Former perms and markings that were left to fade
    SBS 0039R (SP BNDEP) Was former 229 BNDEP, but marking was still there.
    SBS 0074M (42/68/SP BNDEP) Was former Service 19 (and TP1?) perm. The two markings, 'Sv19' and 'TP1' are still there.
    SBS 9462S (168 BNDEP) Though it had a Service 168 cut out service guide but it still has the 'Sv21' marking if I'm not wrong.
    SBS 9554K and SBS 9616R (Both SP BNDEP) Both have faded 'Sv196' markings.

    Do correct me if you find mistakes in the info. I don't do bus spottings as frequently as you (bus fans) do.
