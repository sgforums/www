    Originally posted by BusSpeeder:
    What is the difference between SMRT Scania L113CRL ELBO and the WA Strider? Is it due to its windows?
    The difference is in the bodywork. ELBO is a Greek company while Walter Alexander (WA) is based in Falkirk, Scotland. The ELBO bus have no AC in the beginning. That's why they have those sliding windows. The windows are sealed (technically) when the buses were converted into AC with a roof top unit. They have a stepless entrance although there is 1 step at the exit.

    The Strider buses have AC but their AC unit an underfloor one. Due to this, the Strider buses don't have a stepless entrance. I think there is also a difference in the number of seats; the ELBO having less seats than the Strider. (I can't confirm this)

    Originally posted by SBS9806J:
    Mercedes-Benz O405
    Scania N113CRB
    Volvo B10M Mark 2

    What is/are the difference(s) of the WA bodies on the buses above?
    All of them have the same type of bodywork, the "PS" type. It is actually a modification of Alexander's "P" type for the Singapore market, hence the "S". It proved to be so popular that Alexander put the "PS" type into their range.

    The differences you see to the bodywork is due to the chassis itself, mainly the position of the engine and its components. The Scania N113 has a vertical-transverse engine at the rear and an intercooler. So, the rear seats have to be put higher to clear them.

    From the front, apart from their respective manufacturer's sysmbol, the N113 and O405 looked the same. On the B10M, however, there are openings to let the air flow into the radiator.

    The uniqueness of each kind is found at the rear. Although the shape is the same, each of them have a different style of tail lights. The B10M is arranged horizontally, the N113 vertically and the O405 is made of 3 squarish shaped lights arranged in a row.
