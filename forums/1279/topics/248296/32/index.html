    Originally posted by TWS4793:
    On the top extreme left corner of most of SBST timesheets, there are numbers and letters (example: 54P).

    From what I know, there are several kinds of shift types. P, A and S are some of them.

    Can someone explain what the shift number and the several shift types mean? And P=Permanent and S=Split? Then what's A?
    Regular shifts
    A=AM
    P=PM
    Split shifts:
    S=Normal split shift, with no meal break
    E=Split shift that starts 2nd shift usually in late morning and has one meal break
    Others:
    T: Three quarter: Usually starts shift at 9 to 10am and ends in the evening. However, some services have T shift which starts at 6+am and ends around 3+pm
    N shift: Night shift

    SMRT:
    AM=AM shift
    PM=PM shift
    S1=See S shift
    S2=See E shift
    TQ=See T shift
    For NR, they use PM shifts.
