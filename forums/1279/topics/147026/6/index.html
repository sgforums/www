    The standard gearbox is M-B 6-speed manual, which i think is cheaper than an automatic. Maybe they targeting this chassi @ a wider market so they put the lower cost features as standard unless the customer wants an 'upsize' ?
    HI does use ZF automatic, but mainly on those with kampong roof bodies & as for the contemporary body types (like on CW3) they use both types of gearbox.
    The automatic gearbox is as usual noisier than the manual, with much whinning during acceleration but becomes as quiet as the manual once final gear reached.

    BTW, even the O 405 did have manual gear option although the standard item is automatic it seemed.

    From my observations & sensing, there seems to be 3 different engine & chassi models of such M-B new generation (i.e. with 4-spoke steering-wheel) air-suspended charged-cooled bus-chassi in use in Malaysia. Hi may be using 2 out of the 3 models. Transtar the 3rd model with Mtrans coachwork, where the engine sounds more MAN-like & with single tapered-down exhaust pipe as on the Travego & newer OC 500 series. HI's models the single-exhaust pipe is straight out on the rear-right instead & with 2 different sounding engines & 2 different dashboard instrumentation panels.

    The model with the higher end dashboard they only use on the JB-KL express which comes with the more 'resonating' engine. I believe that's the IBC 1632. The other model with the more commonly seen dashboard (also as used by Transtar & few others) they use on CW3 & few other routes within JB. This i believe is the CBC 1725. I won't rule out that the OC 500 may also be in-use in Malaysia now. OC 500 i've been informed is a very 'broad' description which encompasses many models like 1830, 1832 maybe even a 1840 etc. Nowadays the Malaysian market for M-B bus-chassi is moving higher end to the over 300 BHP class.

    A likeable thing about HI is that they give the driver the chance to fine-tune the air-con settings such the level of coolness is more suited to the situation @ hand i.e. not being excessively cold till condensation occurs which mists up the windows & causing discomfort rather than comfort ! Most HI buses i've been on the air-con is set in the 'ok' range & not over done. Only a few cases (like many cases here unfortunately on SBST) were the air-con set ridiculously cold that the driver doesn't know how to adjust & wears jacket instead !!

    The 3 steps height about same as O 405 & the floor is largely flat, from the entrance till the rear axle where it then rises a little with a raised platform below the seats, also similar to on the O 405. This i classify as entry-level low floor citybus. It's not ultra low-floor though because low profile tyres not used & not zero steps.

    What model ? This uses HI's own bodywork but with the headlamp & rear lampset taken from the O 580 i.e. Travego coach ! They try to copy Travego's front grill but omited the fog-lamps eventhough the 'imprint' was 'stamped' on the bumper.
    The M-B bus-chassi used should be CBC 1725 in the Malaysian way of describing the model but i believe the more complete model description would be O 500 /1725-CBC i.e. this model from the new generation O 500 series, which it seems how the Australian & Brazilian markets know it as. I believe it's essentially the same as OC 500 series as known in other markets like Germany. I was told 'OC' means 'omnibus chassi' so if they just say 'O 500' then may result in confusion since technically that can mean an integraly constructed coach with original M-B bodywork + chassi. There exist O 404 in chassi form but O 404 tends more associated as an original M-B coach i.e. with coachwork & chassi all from M-B. So i suppose in later time they devised this term 'OC' for the generation after the O 404 like O 500 etc. to make clear OC 500 is only bus-chassi (without the M-B bodywork) for those buying this way. But it seems in Australia & Brazil they tend not use the term 'OC' so they refer to the OC 500 still as O 500 etc. but knowing the background their O 500 cannot possibly be an integral coach or bus but only in bus-chassi form instead.

    The engine model used in CBC 1725 i note is OM 906 LA, which is basically the same engine model as on the Citaro which uses it in OM 906 hLA form i.e. flips it flat instead of standing vertical on the chassi. Other differences seems to be difference in 1 valve per cylinder i.e. in CBC 1725 it's 24-valve engine but in Citaro only 18-valves. Overall looking @ this engine & noting the specs., i see the highest output from this 6374 cc model is 279 BHP with Euro-3 emission standard, which seems quite impressive in view of the small cc. [Volvo in the B9TL would use a much bigger cc engine to match this, which doesn't sound so efficient to me ?!]

    But, with other factors kept constant, a Euro-3 rated model should be more expensive than Euro-2 rated model eventhough otherwise largely the same thing. So they likely specified it only to Euro-2 level for the same engine in CBC 1725 for Malaysian use to keep the price down. But nice to note the potential of having Euro-3 from the same engine + with higher power output as well.
