    More On: SBS/SBST models
    SBS has and did release scale models[mostly 1:76] to commemorate special events, eg 25th Anniversary, launch of SBS Transit...
    For the 25th Anniversary in 1998, they asked Corgi to produce the SBS "Superbus", a 3 axle double decker with route 25[SBS9426Y] and the 25th Anniversary decal pasted on buses windscreen in 1998.
    Corgi came out with the model but it is a pale and incorrect version of the actual Superbus in Singapore, there were major errors:
    1. Seating arrangement of the bus was that of KMB's version
    2. The top deck window is not "wrap around"
    3. No coinbox, farecard machine...

    http://www.orientalmodelbuses.co.uk/Volvo/Olympian/43219m2.jpg

    More On: SBS/SBST models
    Subsequently, SBS decision to go into rail operations on the encouragement by government to be bi-modal operators in Singapore led SBS Transit to be set up.
    SBS was retained in the new entity as the operator says Singaporeans have grown familiar with the name.
    So, SBST launched a commemorative set comprising of a Twin Pack[sv28] and a Single Pack[sv88]
    Creative Master Northcord was tasked to deliver and boy did it deliver
    A specially created mould was created for this project, this time, the 1:76 scale model closely resembles what is running on Singapore roads.
    1. Coin boxes, EZlink reader, farecard machines
    2. Correct seating arrangement
    3. Wrap around windows on upper deck
    4. Grab bars on front of upper deck and many more...
    5. TV mobile
    Authentic to detail beauty

    http://www.orientalmodelbuses.co.uk/Volvo/Olympian/sbs1m2.jpg
