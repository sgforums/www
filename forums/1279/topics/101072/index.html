    Originally posted by SBS9845X:
    What is hub reduction anyway?
    heavy vehicles like buses and trucks have hub reduction to help them increase the torque (rotational force) applied to the individual wheels so as to increase performance. note that tis is a common feature found on most modern heavy-duty vehicles.

    note that for gears, power = torque Ã— speed. by conservation of energy, power developed by an engine is constant. by further reducing the gear ratio (which means reducing the rotational speed of the wheel by the gear ratio) of the wheel at its hub, more torque is developed to help push the vehicle forward.
