    Currently, every trip you make, the fare comes with boarding charge and distance component. when you make a transfer, a nominal amount (transfer rebate) is returned to you as an offset against incurring boarding charge.

    The new fare system will base your fare totally on the aggregate distance travelled regardless of the method of travel. Hence, you can change buses like as if you have a bus pass and not incur more as long as you're travelling through the same route (and hence the same distance).

    All trips requiring only EWL, NSL, BPLRT, SKLRT, PGLRT and basic bus services (trunk, feeder, Townlink and Jurong Industrial Services) will only need to pay the standard distance-based fare.

    A premium of up to 25Â¢ for adult card users will be incurred on top of the distance fare for any part of the journey that requires NEL and CCL.

    A premium of 60Â¢ will be payable for express/FFw services on top of basic distance-based fare.

    You may post your questions here at http://buses.sgforums.com/forums/1279/topics/397082?page=5 and we will help you to estimate the fare you have to pay under the new fare regime.

    meaning your total travel distance is travelled by pt A to pt B be it bus - MRT - bus, bus - bus or MRT - bus (MRT - bus - MRT and taking of same service within 5 transfers & 2 hrs would not be considered).

    Say I take Sv 506 from Bt Batok Ctrl and transfer to Sv 8/ 26/ 31/ 90/ 142 at Lor 6 Toa Payoh to Jln Toa Payoh/ St Andrews Village.

    Current: 18 express fare stages + 2 fare stages => $1.85 + $0.69 - $0.50 rebate = $2.04.

    New: Approx 14.4km + approx 1.6km = 16km Express fare => $2.09 or $2.13. This is subject to the new 'fare stage' where ez-link would display km marking instead.
