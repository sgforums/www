    Wow! This is a tall order! I'd suggest you get a copy of Mr. Mike Davis' new book. The answers are all inside; a real treasure trove.

    OK, which one do you want to start first? How about the Albion Viking? This was the first major order of new buses by SBS. They were of the EVK55 type, which was an improvement over the earlier EVK41 type, with dual air braking system. 255 buses were ordered altogether, 155 of which were EVK55CL (9813mm length) and the rest being the longer EVK55CUL (11790mm length).
    All were bodied by Soon Chow with ribbed side panels. Seating capacities varied but in general, the EVK55CL could carry 41 while the EVK55CUL 55 passengers.

    Both variants were powered by a Leyland O.401, 6075cc diesel engine with manual clutch and 4-speed constant mesh gearbox. (The Albion Viking didn't have crash gearboxes as I previously thought). The Vikings were introduced from 1975 - 1977 and were fully withdrawn by 1992.

    Leyland was the leading bus manufacturer at that time. The earlier orders of Leyland chassis was so that there would be a conformity of parts between the various models, a practice that still carry on today, albeit with the company which 'swallowed' Leyland.

    There wasn't any different batch of 2-axle Leyland Olympians. All 200 units were introduced from 1986-1988. The different registration grouping was to denote which depot the bus belongs to, which is not practiced today.

    There's only 1 batch of 2-axle Volvo Olympian; 100 of them introduced in 1994. There are, officially, 3 orders of 12m Volvo Olympians. The first 200 units were introduced from 1994-1995. The next 201 from 1996-1997 and the remaining 70 in 1998. Our local bus enthusiasts classify them into the various 'batches' for easier reference as each 'batch' has their unique differences.

    ONTL11.2R and AN68.2R are the chassis codes for the Leyland Olympian and Atlantean respectively.

    ON = Olympian
    AN = Atlantean
    TL11 = Leyland TL11 engine
    68 = Leyland O.680 engine
    2 = 5639 mm wheelbase
    R = right hand drive
