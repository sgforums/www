    Originally posted by newcomer:
    for crying it out loud, i think bus spotters are rather nerdy ppl in general.

    but tat's juz my honest opinions. come at me if you will.
    Very straight forward and unhypocritical.

    I don't spot buses, but I have interest on the technical aspect of the buses as I'm working in a German automotive engineering MNC. Even though I don't take public transport.

    Everyone will have a hobby. I find my colleagues who are obsessed with golf boring. They can spend one whole weekend in JB trying to conquer the golfcourse. Stay overnight and try to correct their mistake and re-do it again the next day. I find it a waste of money and boring. What's so nice about trying to get a ball so small into a slightly bigger hole in a big plot of land. But to my friend it is an attractive sports which trains and test an individual's patience and thinking capacity.

    I enjoy motorsports. Riding my bike or drive my car in the race circuit. Going round and round trying to beat my previous lap with a faster timing, trying to overtake the next guy I can see at the same time prevent the guy behind from overtaking me. Some people find it stupid running around in circles. Wasting tyres and fuel trying to see who's faster. But I find that it is an attractive sport whereby one's endurance towards pressure & capability to think in the midst of speed and pressure is brought to the test.

    So everybody views beauty differently. One needs to fathom the beauty of the activity in order to appreciate it. Otherwise, it is just a waste of time to that person.

  • TIB1224Y
    4 Dec 07, 10:56
    I think my friends and schoolmates find the hobby alright. Many years ago they would ask why would bus and transport be so interesting, but after some time they know that its good to know more about their own transport system and follow. Some who do not have full interest in transport went on to do their own research and helped lots when they were doing projects. They also helped spread the message out and make some other of their friends interested in transport as a whole. Although they may not know as much as some of our folks here, they have their own share of knowledge, at least they do not have to rely on the taxi when getting around.
    As for many folks here who may have the same experience, you are a roving transport guide. Friends and schoolmates ask you for directions and so on. If you gave them a good answer and details to the route they would definitely come back and ask again. This is a good way to know more people too; people are the best advertising tool. Many of my secondary schoolmates still contact me if they got lost or need to go somewhere quick but not giving the money to flag a cab.
    I always find it good to spread the message out and tone down the general feeling that bus/transport lovers are nerdy and dumb, bad at studies and so on. This general feeling is definitely wrong; I have met local and overseas bus/transport enthusiasts who do not look dumb and nerdy at all. They just put their time wisely into their hobby, spend time with friends and family, and sometimes adopt another hobby to supplement the time and balance life.
