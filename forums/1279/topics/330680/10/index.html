    Let me help you enlighten why some services (812 for this case) need PM Peak xovers but not AM Peak.

    During AM Peak, all perm buses of the service are out in full force - the A S1 and S2 shifts. Break times are minimal and the frequency is met.

    However, during PM Peak, the variable factor to consider is the P shift BC/SL's Meal Break. They tend to occur just before and during PM Peak period.

    Assuming run time & frequency (i.e. 10min) during peak hours is the same, when 1 P shift BC/SL goes for meal break during PM Peak, another bus needs to cover it due to the adherement of the 10min frequency.

    On the ground you may not feel much difference as the frequency is about the same as AM Peak but the fact is there's an extra bus running on the route thanks to P shift Meal Break.

    And that is also the reason why many commuters find PM peak harder to wait for a bus than AM Peak, assuming no xovers to cover frequency for PM Peak.

    That's how Meal break xover buses came about.
