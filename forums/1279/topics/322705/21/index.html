    Originally posted by 28_29_293:

    So Svc 36 all days need how many buses?


    if u notice that's always 2 S 2 T and 2 more Ps than As.

    Thanks to each trip having to jump, the process goes this way..

    If there's 11 A shifts, 10 of them will take bus from depot, the 11th one hitch a ride from depot to CGA to wait for jump bus to start. The 11th A shift driver will start from the bus that came from Tomlinson Rd in the morning.

    2 S shifts will also take bus from depot in the morning. After the AM Peak trip, both S shifts will take a meal break - meaning jump 2 bus instead of 1. This is when the 2 T shifts comes in. The T shifts will be the last to have the meal break after their 1st trip. the 2 S shifts will end the morning portion at CGA.

    The jumpbus continues until the T shift finishes its final trip around 1630-1730. 2 P shifts will take over from the 2 T shifts. That is why there's 2 more P shifts than A shifts. As to how the P shifts settle their mealbreak, that's when the PM trip of the 2 S shifts come in. The 2 S shifts will start and end their PM portion at CGA.

    Jumpbus continues till midnight. 1 P shift driver will end at CGA without taking any bus back. That P shift driver will handover the bus to the driver who is in front of him for him to do last car 36A. Don't worry the P shift driver will be able to hitch a ride from the first bus that off service from CGA..

    Long story cut short, to calculate the no of buses needed for S36, Count (A shifts - 1) + (P shifts - A shifts) for full day buses. The excess dont do whole day. That formula is not a tested and proven method..
