    Originally posted by One Harmonius Blend:

    about S107, does every service has it?


    No. It depends on the number of S-Shifts a service get. The first S-Shift the service got would be S101, subsequently S102, then S103 etc. Also to take note is that there is S201, S202, S203 etc. S1 operate during peak hours, while S2s operate during school peak hours

    However for SMRTB if a duty code contains 5 in the 2nd digit (E.g. S151, S252), it normally means that the duty does a crossover to another service. 

    For Sch-H Week it just means holiday timesheet. It will revert to normal after the school holidays
