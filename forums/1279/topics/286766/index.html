    Im relatively new to this forums and I find that there are many bus lingo in this forums which may be hard to understand for a newcomer. I took quite some time to 'decipher' some of the phrases too. I started this thread so the more common phrases can be explained. Maybe this thread can be made sticky or sth so other newcomers can easily refer to this before it is buried into the mass of inactive threads after a few weeks?

    Some of those I know

    TS = Thread starter
    SD = single deck
    DD = double deck
    SP = spare
    NAC = non aircon
    FAC = fully aircon
    PAC = partially aircon
    EDS = Electronic display for bus svc number at the front of buses, mainly on SMRT ones
    JIS = Jurong Industrial svc
    BIH = bus info hub (businfohub.com)
    cameo = buses that appear on a route for a day to take over perm buses that may be under repair etc
    rego = bus licence plate number
    desto = the plastic plate with the bus svc number seen at the front/back of buses
    cross-over/x-over = buses that serve more than one service every day
    skyliner = ads that juts upwards from the side of buses

    Some that I still dont really understand
    CAC = converted aircon (?)
    troll = spammer (?)
    OAC
    UD

    Im not sure whether all these are correct. Can some senior members check and add other phrases. A short summary of the different bus models would be nice here too.

    partial Air-con bus is a 90s catch phrase. then they used it to describe a bus service.

    a bus service is called partial air-con service if half its fleet is air-con bus and other half is non air-con bus. a common situation in 90s.

    today still got partial air-con services

    like bus 93, 74 or 151.

    the short form is PAC.

    CAC: Converted Air-con

    this one use first used in the late 80s when SBS converted non air-con leyland Victories to air-con buses. in Essence u can say they "mod" the bus in race car lingo if u want.

    today some of he Scania are CAC. so are some mark 2s. on the SMRT side many merc are CAC. even the ex CSS UD many are CAC.

    OAC - original air-con

    essentially coined in 2000 . then in 1989 Scanias came as non air-con and air-con variants. in 2000, SBS converted some NAC Scania to Air-con bus. so to differentiate the CAC scania,, the orginal air-con scania was called.
