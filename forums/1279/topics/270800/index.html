    Why is it that Germany's bus system can follow a given timetable with at most 5 minutes difference?

    Why is it that Singapore's buses can come 3 buses in a row, and not well spread out?

    I'm talking here about buses that do come in 20 minutes interval. I have personally seen at HarbourFront interchange: 3 bus 188 coming in one after another, and 10 mins later, 3 bus 963 coming into the interchange one after another.

    We all understand that it may be the fact that drivers drive at different speeds. This is something that can be worked upon. The drivers could drive at speeds which would allow them to reach a certain bus stop/check point at a certain time. This way, the bus timings would be kept rather constant.

    With our "high-tech" GPS systems installed in the bus, I would suppose it wouldn't be much of a hassle too if we install a small device to inform the driver whether he should drive faster or slower to the next bus stop.

    What do you all think?

I rmb LTA introduces a rule that the traffic lights will show green all the way along a road with many traffic junctions for the convenience of motorists. Imagine this, for most SBST drivers, after the traffic lights turn green, the bus will accelerate and stop at a bus stop. Then, it will accelerate again. However, by the time when it approaches the next traffic junction, the traffic lights will be turning red.
