    25 Oct 09, 21:46

    Managed to catch the Yutong today after camping at Bedok Inter for about 1.5hours.

    The engine do sound like european buses that we all are used to. The ride was smooth and does not jerk much, also notice that Entrance door comes with the door closing signal. 

    Air con wise, it is generally cold despite the bus being packed and the hot afternoon weather. However, if you are seating at any of the 4 seats at the back, it can be quite stuffy and squeezy. There is also a big gap between the seats and the window and it feels kinda awkward. 

    Verdict: 7.5/10, Would not be surprised if SMRT decide to buy more of the Yutongs

  • SMRT BUSES LuvER
    22 Nov 09, 16:58

    My review for the Yutong

    Rating: 8.5/10
    Overall:
    - Even though the A/C vent is un-adjustable, it still feels very cold and not stuffy
    - Seats definitely better than OCs
    - Transmission sound is audible, but softer than OC
    - Vibrates lesser than OC
    - No jerkiness; better than batch 1 OC
    - No more 'TSHHHH~~' sound when opening and closing of doors
    - Door Closing announcement softer than OC

    Bad points:
    - Seat colour seemed abit plain
    - Rear left ez-link card reader is abit higher up than the one on the right; but reachable.
    - Seats on the rear are cramped since there is an 'awkward' space between the window and the seat of the rear
    - Bus Stopping chime kept on 'pressed' when actually no one does; (is it fixed already?)
    - Side-facing seats on the front could have been better if its forward-facing.
