    Hi ZYX, as far as i know, starting from the B10M MKII, there were 4 airbags per rear axle, that includes the CRBs and O405s. Even the O305s have them.

    Anyway, the airbags aren't there to absorb bumps. Its meant to absorb vibrations and small jolts. Bumps are absorbed by the shock absorber, which were placed side by side with the airbags. 2 per front axle and 4 per rear axle.

    That said, the pressure inside the airbag also cannot be too high, otherwise it would become so stiff that its practically "no suspension". It has to work side by side with the shock absorber to produce a smooth ride.

    Anyway, I've ridden an old sch merc at malaysia before, on both paved road and unpaved road. And yeah, its a great merc suspension, until my bus rode over a huge pot hole. The entire goes "poom!" and shook everyone violently, but suprisingly tho, nothing of the bus suspension or axle is damaged. Still can cruise on highway smoothly like its brand new.
