    toa payoh is at the centre of the jams. but think of it.. you live in toa payoh you endure 1 part of the jam. if you travel beyond toa payoh there's part 2. Here are some examples that involves going thru toa payoh

    Work: Clementi/Pandan
    live: anywhere east of paya lebar rd

    to get to work hit PIE using Jln Eunos, Bedok North Rd or Paya Lebar Rd. you get 1 jam near Woodsville. the next jam is slightly before BKE exit thanks to the speed camera, next at the Anak Bt exit and then Clementi Rd.

    to go home its Clementi Rd, Dunearn Rd/PIE and then whitley is slightly better and then jam again after the Toa payoh exit. and it will only ease up when you exit at eunos. travelling beyond that would have smoother traffic.

    If bartley rd east is anywhere to come by, morning gives you the airport rd/kaki bt exit heavy traffic + braddell viaduct jam + lornie heavy traffic + adam rd entrance jam. evening gives you lornie exit jam + braddell Rd CTE exit jam.

    Another case is

    Work: buona vista/nuh/alexandra rd
    live: serangoon/hougang/sengkang

    morning if in sengkang you get the KPE heavy traffic, or CTE if you can afford. Serangoon will get heavy traffic at upp serangoon rd turning to braddell. Those on KPE will exit PIE (if wanna avoid ERP at ECP) and u get a jam there, then slightly smooth traffic till adam rd exit. Those on braddell will get jam on the braddell viaduct and lornie heavy traffic. Then you get farrer rd flyover jam till the holland rd underpass. then smooth till queensway/portsdown ave junction. Those at alexandra have to endure 4 cycles of the alexandra rd/queensway junction then after that its heavy traffic to alexandra rd offices. Those at bnv/nuh have to endure the AYE sth bnv exit jam.

    evening those from bnv/nuh have to endure the nth bnv entrance to aye before exiting at portsdown to the ORR. alternatively they can use c'wealth ave which is slightly better. those from alexandra have smooth traffic until the queensway underpass. then its jam until the empress place/farrer rd junction then its smooth flowing until lornie road. those taking the PIE will get the jam from adam (heavy traffic) till toa payoh exits. If heading back to hougang and using upp serangoon viaduct will have to endure the woodsville exit jam. those going to kpe will endure the jam from toa payoh exit till pie entrance itself. hitting KPE thereafter is slightly better. Those who proceed onto lornie will get heavy traffic then slight jam at braddell rd cos of the cte braddell exit. then its smooth as well as upp serangoon rd.

    in short: live in east, work in east. conversely, live in west, work in west. jams are lesser that way.

    you no need to reply, but just to give you an idea of the daily peak hour jams.

    Thank you for reading.
