    I would guess a ZF EcoLife 6-speed gearbox as SMRT has preferred ZF gearboxes (as seen in the NL323Fs). I really hope it's the EcoLife though, as the Voith can sound very boring and doesn't seem to have acceleration as good as the ZF.

    If the full torque rating (1800 Nm) is to be utilised, the only two gearbox options would be the ZF 6 AP 2000 B (EcoLife) or Voith D884.5/D884.6.

    Remember that just because an engine is rated at 360 PS / 1800 Nm doesn't mean that the full load power / torque is available in all gears and under all conditions.  Modern gearboxes reduce the torque of the engine in lower gears in order to preserve ride comfort and to ensure longevity of components.  Articulated buses also have turntables with sensors that progressively signal the engine control unit to limit the torque of the engine depending on the angle of articulation.
