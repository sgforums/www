    http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/72/MAN_NG_363_Lion%27s_City_G_3456_Marki.JPG

    MAN Lion's City GL (5 windows for the front part of the bus):

    http://www.st.gallerbus.andkat.ch/visuelles/fotogalerien/man-lions-city-gl-a23/data/images/man_lion's_city_gl_03.jpg

    SMB388S (5 windows for the front part of the bus, no small window):

    http://sphotos-b.xx.fbcdn.net/hphotos-ash3/32630_542955802411565_1922953790_n.jpg

    Conclusion: A24 could be 18.75m long, OR it could be a 18m chassis with a modified GL configuration (Note that the 5 windows are not equally sized, seems like Gemilang made it look like the GL by having no small window, as for the trailer it doesn't have the 2nd wheelchair slot, which is also part of the GL's original configuration).

    The difference in length only applies to the front part of the bus as both G and GL have the same trailer length.

    What do you all think?

    There is no G or GL in 388S, the G and GL indicators are only used on the integral Lion's City from MAN with model code A23. The designation by MAN of A24 shows that it is a chassis form of the A23.

    Since 388S is also not a integral unit from MAN, but instead built by Gemilang with the design of MAN Lion's City series, we cannot compare the seating configuration or the installation of the glass panels directly. That would be comparing apple to oranges.

    Also note that the design by Gemilang has shifted the exit doors of the rear section forward by 1 seat, unlike the design from MAN.
