    Originally posted by Bus Stopping:

    The gear ratios on the transmission are fixed and can't be changed. What could be changed is the final drive ratio on the drive axle. Final drive ratio determines the maximum speed and effective torque of the vehicle.

    Correct.

    On the Citaro with 275/70R x 22.5 tyres, there's a choice of 5.27:1, 5.77:1 and 6.21:1, giving road speed of 99km/h, 90km/h or 84km/h respectively with a Voith DIWA.5 gearbox, or 117km/h, 107km/h or 99km/h respectively with a ZF-EcoLife gearbox.  This is assuming that the OM 906 hLA engine is used.  Because the OM 457 hLA peaks out at 200rpm lower, the road speeds offered by the above final drive ratios will be slightly lower.
