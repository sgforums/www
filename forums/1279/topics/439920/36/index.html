The Suntec-Orchard area is one, the Marine Parade that area also. 

    If 36 gets Citaros, yes it will be faster and less lethargic on the expressway stretches. But the time made up on the expressway sectors would be lost once you hit those bus stops with such heavy pax traffic. The KUBs are just faster in loading everyone onboard to be honest due to its wider aisle and larger standing area below the steps. 

    The higher roof of the KUBs also makes it easier for the BC to see if there's any alighting pax who is trying to get out but stuck in the bus. I've encountered many BCs driving the Citaros which are packed who either: 1) Missed the stop where pax already pressed the bell long ago (no 'Stopping' warning light on dashboard for Citaros, KUBs have), or 2) stuck at the bus stop for twice the time needed in a KUB because everyone's head is blocking the BC's view of seeing who exactly haven't alighted. And yes, that is with CCTVs fitted at the same positions as the KUBs on the Citaros. 

    And if I'm not wrong (I'm not too sure about this), but the KUBs are more suitable for 36 too because 36's rest time at CGA (that is its only terminating point) is slightly shorter than the other airport services with Citaros (24 at AMK, 53 at Bishan). Not to mention the jumpbus system of 36, the BC of the previous 36 bus usually jumps straight onto the next 36 bus that arrives around 5-10mins later to take over & drive straight away, rest for the BC yes but no rest for the bus practically. You probably won't feel it from inside or outside the buses accurately, but the Citaros actually run hotter than the KUBs. That's why if you put Citaros on 36, they might tend to overheat faster?
