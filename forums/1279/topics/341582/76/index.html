  • SBS 1000U
    4 Jun 11, 15:17

    An end of era indeed...

    Farewell to SBS/SBST Mercedes Benz O405 - 1990 to 2011...

    They have seen through the eras and evolution of the SBS fleet for the past 21 years.

    - Last services and deregistrations of the old generation of single deckers, like Mercedes Benz OF1417 Hawkes, DMs and OF1413 Soon Chows, Albion Vikings, Volvo B57s, Leyland Victories and Mercedes Benz O303 and Leyland Victories air con buses, and 1st generation of Leyland Atlanteans DM and BACOs.

    - Era of introducing Volvo B10M air con buses, from MK3 to DM3500

    - Introduction of air con double deckers, Leyland Olympians and Volvo Olympians batch 1

    - Introduction of EURO compliant buses, from EURO 1 to EURO 5.

    - Last non air con double deckers introduced, Volvo Olympian 2xles. They are also the last model of NAC single deckers.

    - Introduction of the last midi bus, Dennis Darts and deregistration of other midi buses like Nissan Civilians and Mercedes Benz midi bus.

    - Introduction and withdrawal of CityLink Direct services, AirBus and Sentosa shuttle buses under SBS Leisure.

    - Introduction of EDS in SBS fleet, with the Super Long bus SBS997A as the start, and other noticable demos, like SBS1688K, SBS2888T, SBS998Y, SBS999U, SBS9888Y, SBS9889U, SBS9810 and Dennis Tridents.

    - Conversion of cross boarder service 170 from NAC to FAC status.

    - Conversation of non air con buses to air con buses, Mercedes Benz O405 DM, Scania N113CRB WA, Volvo B10M MK2 DM and WA.

    - Introduction and withdrawal of bendy buses demo, Volvo B10MA 60 artic, SBS998Y and Mercedes Benz O405G, SBS999U.

    - Introduction of non step entry buses, first with the Volvo B10BLE diesel, SBS1688K, second with the Volvo B10TL, SBS9888Y.

    - Introduction of CNG buses, Volvo B10BLE CNG.

    - Last DM bodied double decker bus, Dennis Tridents.

    - Withdrawal of last old generation of NAC double deckers, Mercedes Benz O305, Leyland Olympian 2xles and Leyland Atlanteans

    - Corporate change, SBS to SBS Transit

    - Introduction of WAB buses, with the start of the CDGE Volvo B9TLs.

    - Mordernisation of SBST fleet, with the start of Scania K230UBs, with full EDS, WAB and using ALCOA rims.

    - Full withdrawal of first generation of modern SBS fleet, Volvo B10M MK2s and Scania N113CRBs.

    - Introduction of China buses, SunLong and Higer and also hybrid and electric buses.

    - Introduction of Wright buses in SBST fleet.

    - Introduction of SBST 3rd fully built up integral European bus model, Mercedes Benz Citaro O530 Evobus. First being the Leyland National 2 demo in the 1980s and second was the Renault PR100.2 demo. If I include the Japanese, will be the Nissan U31 NAC and air con buses and Nissan and Hinos in the 70s and 80s.

    Thank you for serving us, Mercedes  Benz O405, for the past 21 years. You are a realiable and comfortable workhorse for SBS/SBST during this period and is one of the most influential model in the SBST fleet. Goodbye...
