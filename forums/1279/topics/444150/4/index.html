    Let's put it this way:

    Its no coincidence that SMRT has a lot a lot of problems.

    Bus problem.

    Train problem.

    Back-up problem.

    And perhaps even worse, PR also problem.

    The corporate structure needs a major shake-up to change all of that, but the govt has remained silent of this.

    SBST has problems too. But SBST has the advantage of economies of scale in the bus sector, and its NEL has been fairly reliable and its PR is certainly much better than SMRT's.

    And seriously, the bendies should just go. They take up space. Plenty of them. If you look at SBST's purchases, they made much more sense than TIBS (even after taking into account the hodge-podge fleet caused by slow expansion). SBST rejected the bendies for very good reasons (reasons people have raised time and again here). TIBS decided to be different and SMRT is paying some of the price.

    SMRT could have moved aggressively to end the nonsense.

    They didn't.

    Worse. They went the opposite direction and concentated on retail and advertising while the core businesses languished.

    In many ways also, it is also the system that is at fault. The govt has to keep mum about SMRT's CEO because it needs to build confidence in investors about its promise of "free business environment". Yet ironically, Singapore is not a free market with little govt intervention. Far from it in fact. While this model has its advantages, the bad stuff can be seen in crises like these and you can see that the govt has its hands tied if it wants to carry on this model which it clearly intends to.
