What did you do during bus spotting?

  • TWS4793

    Share with us on how you would normally do your bus spotting while on the roads. Points to consider are:

    - What preparations did you make prior to your start of bus spotting
    - Where did you start off from; where do you like to go to
    - What did you take for your bus spotting (e.g. Camera, Water Bottle)
    - How do you dress up for a bus spotting

    Or any other relevant points which are of significance to you.
    ====================
    For me, I would always have a system of doing bus spotting in mind. A few days before my actual bus spotting, I would always look at the map and the Transitlink guide so that I know the fastest solution to get to a particular place (with sufficient hot spots that bus photographers would target). Of course I would also ask questions here in this forum.

    A few hours/minutes before going out, I prepare my stuff needed for my bus spotting. I carry along a black pouch (which I normally hang it around my neck), with important things such as my camera (for taking photos of buses), my handphone (to contact with other bus fans/my parents should something happens on the road) and a small A6 size notebook, which fits snugly into my pouch. I also bring an umbrella should there be rain, and extra items such as extra batteries and memory cards (will be adding in some first aid kit to my pouch soon). Before I go out, I ensure that I have sufficient water to last me for the whole journey as I do not normally bring a small water bottle - it adds to my load.

    I would normally start with a Service 225 bus, if I'm lucky enough I may be on SBS 3721J, or I may be on other buses. I whip out my notebook and pen before the bus comes (if I have time), and I tap my EZ-Link card without having to do the troublesome way of having to take out the EZ-Link card to tap. On my notebook, I would usually write all the three usual bus stuffs such as cameos, new adverts and rides. Sometimes I even record the behaviour of our bus fans or other people who may be hindering the bus operations/saying about our buses. When I reach my desired place, I take out my camera and start taking whatever I can.

    Out on the roads, the situations may suddenly change unexpectedly. In handling this, if I miss the bus, I just let it miss and have a go on another day. Sometimes I receive news (say rare cameos) from other people. If I have the time and I am at another place where the particular bus is out of reach of my area, I would call up my parents and request permission from them to go to the particular place. If not, I have to leave it alone and I just let myself regret for not taking picture of that bus. It happens to me most of the time, one example is SBS 8018Z cameo on 58. I was in Tampines when I received the message in the afternoon. I thought of asking my parents to let me go to Pasir Ris to take photos of the rare cameo but they did not allow me to go. In the end I just regretted of not taking the opportunity to have a photo of it (But anyway I'm still young).

    When I come back home I take my 225W again, sometimes it can be Service 17. When I come home, I take a rest first before starting to sort out my spottings of the day.
    ====================
    Different people do different things. What is yours?
