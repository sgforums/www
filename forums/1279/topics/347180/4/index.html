    continuing from the thread by SBS7382C, locked by ^tamago^ and request to continue here:

    Why do we hate the new scania so much?

    This is due to alot of reasons: (pls note that i dont dislike KUBs)

    1. SBS Transit bought too many of a single model and all of them are bodied by Gemilang. Some are thinking that maybe some can be bodied by another company. Or maybe SBST could buy some KUBs and some of other model/brand (ie. B7RLE). My answer for this point is that SBST had to do it for being cost-efficient. Buying in bulk will save cost and it will facilitate maintainence.

    2. Many have bad experience with the Scania N113CRBs. Noisy, bad vibrations are some of the reasons. So stereotyping occurs where the KUBs are as bad. And that so happen they took some "lousy" KUBs which further prove their own point.

    3. When the Batch 1 KUBs came into revenue service, many problems did occur like vibrations, jerking etc. I agree that at then, it was really a problem. But subsequently when the other batches came in, the problems started to subside, althought it still exists. My point is that the jerking problem will not be solved immediately as it all depends on the BC's experience and judgement on how much pressure to apply on the brakes and accelerator.

    One point to note: Why does it take so long for BCs to get used to the accelerating and braking system of the KUBs?

    For older buses, upon releasing the brake, the bus will move alittle. However for the KUBs, the bus does not move after releasing the brake, so a longer time is needed to adapt to this system.
