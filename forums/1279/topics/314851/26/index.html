    Originally posted by nwfb23:

    Germany manufacturers have an industrial standard within them. It is common that both Mercedes-Benz and MAN use same type of wheel and rims

    They had the VÖV and StÜLB standards for city buses and intercity buses respectively, of which O.305s and SL200s were of the same VÖV standard and O.405s (and Variants), SL202 were of the VÖV II standard, but the standardization ended in 2000. From then on, Germany bus manufacturers developed their own range of buses. The reason why you still see a lot of similar parts on German buses now is that current designs are still largely influenced by the buses produced in the VÖV and StÜLB standardization period.
