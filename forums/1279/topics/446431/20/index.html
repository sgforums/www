    Originally posted by TIB783G:

    How I'd wish TIB300S-361R could stay, I haven't seen them yet - Not sure if it is due to the intake of the bendies that make them go.

    In the past, the lifespan for every public buses are only limited to 12 years. TIB1E to TIB310U were cleared off after reaching the lifespan of 12 years between 1995 to 1997. That was also the time when Tibs rolled in several aircon buses (0405s/Scanias/DAFs) and upgraded all the bus services to PAC status.

    TIB311K onwards were initially planned to go off in 1998, however that was when LTA allowed extension for another 3 years, hence their lifespan was extended till 2001. It was by the end of Dec 2001 when the last such old Japanese NAC buses were totally cleared off (last Hino HT238K NAC was seen on service 175 - LCK to Upp Bt Timah).

    There we also a few Hino HT238Ks that were also transferred to CSS in late 1997. All the NAC ones were phased out together with Tibs ones in 2001, whereas the AC ones (former TIB38x series) stayed till 2004.
