    Back in 1995, BPJ was still a developing estate while SKG was not even developed. Furthermore, Tibs pulled out quite a few services following the WDL extension (but were forced to partially reinstate 950). The takeover of the northern region (from BPJ toSKG) in a way was to "compensate" for losing some WDL routes when the future MRT extension was open.

    Also note that PGL was hived off from SKG and given its own route group in 12/2003 with SBST in charge. Even though SBW developed earlier than these 2 estates, it still doesn't have its own bus interchange (but could have it by Christmas). In a way, SBST seems to be doing a better job with north-east routes. It is likely Tibs preferred a slow approach to SKG and PGL (like what they did in SBW) so as not to overtax themselves, but the government's decision not to have a subway monopoly spoilt all their plans.

    857 Withdraw/integrate with 169

    950 Withdraw

    952 Withdraw
    SS7 Withdraw
