    The last ever notice about NAC/PAC/FAC conversion that SBST put up was in Nov 2001 when service 170 was converted to FAC. No notice was given to services like 32 82 103 158 etc when NAC buses were deployed to these services, reason was that some AC buses were being pulled out to be deployed on the newly introduced services in 2005.

    But in 2006 notice was put up for some JIS services whereby ADDs were introduced, for the purpose of the bus fare structures.

    Anyway for now that all the NAC single deck buses are already phased out, it is rather obvious that all the services in the north and eastern areas (like services 6 and 35 which were used to be NAC) are definitely FAC fleets now. Unless SBST have decisions to transfer some NAC VO2x to BNDEP.

    WAB status is a bit different. SBST will have to do some experiments on those bus routes before deciding whether to make the service a WAB route or not. Nothic that there's no such things like partial WAB or fully WAB. Most of the WAB services still have a mixture of WAB and non WAB fleets in them.
