    In PTC's term, which is the ofiicial term, Sv 400, 401 and 403 are Trunk Services, while Sv 408 and 409 are basic-plus services.

    They tried to convert 402 into basic plus (ie flat fare), but wasn't successful. The Express (including Fast Foward) services and 926 are also other example basic-plus services. All CSS services are supplementary services, and I do believe if any of the operators want to operate a CSS service right now, they can do so (provided PTC gives green light).

    Summary of public bus service types (not including charters, shuttles)
    Basic
    -Trunk
    -Feeder

    Basic Plus, any bus which has a higher fare than basic services, but can still operate without restrictions, just like a basic service.
    -Flat Fare services
    -Express Services
    -All the "direct" services

    Supplementary
    -City Shuttle Service
    -Scheme A (Passenger lorry service, withdrawn circa mid 1970s)
    -Scheme B
    *Both scheme A and B buses/lorries are required by ROV to have the red "P.T." sticker behind the bus, although nowadays it isn't enforced properly.

    Premium
    -Shenton Way
    -City Shopper
    -Neighbourhood to Town Centre/Transport Interchange/CBD in a territory which does not belong to the bus operator operating the service
