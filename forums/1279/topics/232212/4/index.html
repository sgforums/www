    OK, so ABS can also be translated as Air Bag Suspension, but the whole world in general refer to ABS on vehicles as Anti-lock Braking System. ABS uses wheel speed sensors to determine if one or more wheels are trying to lock up during braking. If a wheel tries to lock up, a series of hydraulic valves limit or reduce the braking on that wheel. This prevents skidding and allows you to maintain steering control.

    OK, so ABS can also be translated as Air Bag Suspension, but the whole world in general refer to ABS on vehicles as Anti-lock Braking System. ABS uses wheel speed sensors to determine if one or more wheels are trying to lock up during braking. If a wheel tries to lock up, a series of hydraulic valves limit or reduce the braking on that wheel. This prevents skidding and allows you to maintain steering control.
    Anti Brake Lock System.
    ABS is a monitoring system for prevention of wheel locking during hard braking.
    The wheel sensors use a pulse generator principle. Consists of a toothed ring located at the axle ends or within the axle. The analogue Voltage o/p from these sensors is transmitted to an ECU located in the vehicle cabin.
    ECU is fed on how fast each wheel is rotating. At the point when the wheel is about to lock up, the ECU outputs a signal to reduce braing force to the affected wheel. This is done by controlling the air through a modulator (for pneumatic brakes) or Brake fluid (DOT3,4,5.1) instead of air through a modulator (for hydraulic brakes).
    Modulator valve is a solenoid controlled valve capable of dumping air/fluid being delivered to the brake chamber/caliper. This dumping of air to the slave pump (brake chamber/caliper) moemntary and is immediately followed by recharing the slave pump. The cycle of this dumping and feeding can occur many times within a second to give the wheel brake and release, brake and release effect known as ABS.
