    Requirements when taking photos of buses:

    a) Perfect weather and sunlight/light source. With this, your photos are brighter and easier to be seen.
    b) Suitable places to shoot. Taking photos of buses cannot just be as and when you like (unless you're taking for leisure). Bus photographers normally have a strategic place to take photos of buses (e.g. Chinatown, opposite New Bridge Centre). Take at the correct place without major obstructions and do also take note of shadows being cast on the road. If there are, go to another place to shoot.
    c) No parts of the bus are cut away. Make sure that we can see the whole bus (which includes the side and front with the rego seen properly). Also make sure that the angle is of a suitable ratio of front/back to side.
    d) Correct setting of camera settings. ISO (which is how sensitive the camera is to light) must be set to somewhere a bit low during the day and higher if it's a night shot (must be accompanied by a tripod or a flat and smooth surface). Make sure that you do not use any digital zoom or else it spoils the photo.
