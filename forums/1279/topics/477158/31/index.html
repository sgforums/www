    While we can suggest long inter-town routes for all we want, under LTA's hub-and-spoke model it is unlikely that such new routes will be feasible unless they offer significant time savings or convenience over the MRT. From Sembawang to CCK/BBT, MRT would be faster than this proposed route unless you're taking this service direct without any transfers, which is unlikely since it mostly connects residential areas.

    Between Yishun and Yew Tee/CCK/BBT, most ppl would be heading towards industrial estates, schs like ITE College West or the army camps along Sembawang Rd (Khatib Camp, Chong pang camp etc) or at Yew Tee (Kranji Camp II/III, Mowbray camp). Currently, 171 provides a North-Northwest connection between Yishun and Bt Panjang, serving Sembawang Rd and allowing for transfer to other svcs at Bt Panjang. When DTL is opened, 171 section from BPJ to Orchard/Marina Ctr can be removed, and the route extended to CCK and/or Yew Tee/Bt Batok, providing more inter-town connections. At the moment, I don't think a separate North-Northwest bus route is needed, especially since the general link is already provided by MRT.

    As for short, regional connections e.g. Yishun-Sembawang or Yew Tee-Bt Batok, these can be better served by short routes within the housing estates themselves or between adjacent estates. I agree with BusAnalyzer that Yishun Ring Rd can have more bus svcs. Maybe 850E can be operated full day to Marina Centre or looping at Suntec, with a peak hour variant to Shenton Way. 860 may be extended to serve the new flats coming up at Canberra in future and terminating at Sembawang, providing another link between Yishun and Sembawang. 103 can be extended from Seletar to Yishun, though may not go via Yishun Ring Rd since it's a bit longer. Most direct route would be via Yishun Ave 11 and Yishun Central.
