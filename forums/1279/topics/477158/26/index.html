    Well, Mr Pervertedboy is correct. There is no doubt that this extended 181 is no shorter/faster/cheaper alternative to the combinations of bus services that people in Jurong West take to travel from one part of the town to another. Since there is no incentive, there is likely to be minimal demand for it.

    To think about it, I think it is better to have it as an entirely new bus service, rather than make it an extension of 181.

    Since this bus service would only be of high demand during peak hours and the crowd would move in a particular uniform direction, I suggest this bus service only operates during peak hours and runs only in one direction.

    To optimise resources, we can simply modify the route of 240P and extend it at both ends to Bulim and Tukang respectively, and make it a new peak period bus service altogether.

    1. Bulim Street 
    2. Bulim Avenue
    3. Corporation Road
    4. Boon Lay Avenue
    5. Boon Lay Drive (along River Valley High School)
    6. Boon Lay Drive
    7. Corporation Road
    8. Boon Lay Way (Lakeside MRT Station)
    9. Yuan Ching Road
    10. Yung Ho Road
    11. Jalan Tukang
    12. Tukang Innovation Drive

    In the morning, this bus service starts at Bulim Street and ends at Tukang Innovation Drive. 

    • * People staying along Jurong West Avenue 3 can walk across the grass patch (there is a pavement linking Jurong West Street 22 and Bulim Street) and take the bus at Bulim Street, to get to Jurong Junior College, Lakeside MRT Station, Yuan Ching Secondary School, Jurong Secondary School or the Tukang Innovation Park.
    • * People staying at Boon Lay can use this bus service to get to Lakeside MRT Station, Yuan Ching Secondary School, Jurong Secondary School or the Tukang Innovation Park.
    • * People working in Tukang Innovation Park can alight at Lakeside MRT and take this bus to their work place.
    In the evening, this bus service starts at Tukang Innovation Drive and ends at Bulim Street, for people to go home.

    If we were to have the two alternatives suggested by Mr Pervertedboy - which I think is more feasible - we actually do not need this bus service to go to Bulim in the morning.
    • * People staying in Pioneer and Gek Poh can take 172, 181, 185 and 199 to go to the industrial areas in Bulim, especially if 181 is extended to only loop at the industrial area, as suggested by Mr Pervertedboy.
    • * People staying in other parts of Singapore can take 181 at Boon Lay Bus Interchange to go to Bulim Industrial areas.
    • * People staying in Boon Lay can actually just walk to the industrial areas in Bulim. Right now, there are already people staying in Boon Lay walking across the new HDB and crossing the road to Saint Joseph's Home and Corporation Primary School. Hence, contrary to Mr Pervertedboy's suggestion, I do not think bus 49 should wind around Boon Lay before going to Bulim. Instead, it should go directly to Bulim Avenue from Jurong West Avenue 1. 
    • * People staying at Hong Kah and Taman Jurong can take bus 49 to Bulim industrial area.
    In short,
    1. A short extension of 181 to loop around Bulim Industrial area,
    2. A simple modification (to skip a bus stop at Jurong West Avenue 1 and go directly to Steet 41) and a short extension of 49 to Bulim Avenue (directly from Avenue 1),
    3. And the new single-direction peak period bus service I suggested
    is about all that is needed to provide the bus connections in Jurong West that are not available at this moment.
