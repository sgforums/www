    Hi. I would like to propose an amendment to a new route I suggested on last Sunday. Ultimately, this route is meant to provide the intra-town connection between Pioneer and Taman Jurong, provide the bus connection to Lakeside MRT Station from Hong Kah and Boon Lay, and enhance various bus connections across Jurong West. This amendment takes into account and avoids the 400m from Boon Lay Bus Interchange, to reduce duplication of resources while strengthen its coverage in Pioneer area.

    One challenge while drawing the route is to ensure that the length is not too long for a Singaporean trunk route. Originally, I wanted it to ply in both directions all the way to Jurong West Street 93, then loop at Street 91. However, after noticing the route is already at 10km mark at Street 93, I decided to make it loop there instead. Nonetheless, it does not defeat the purpose of providing intra-town connection to Taman Jurong. Secondarily, this trunk route may relieve the loading off Boon Lay Bus Interchange, because people may change to alight at Lakeside and take this service home. To connect to Lakeside MRT Station, I would suggest creating a new pair of bus stop at where a taxi stand used to be located, at the transformer/power plant/etc. building area.

    The revised length of this route is around 20.51km. Coincidentally, the number 205 is not taken. Therefore, I would like to suggest calling this new bus route 205.

    Dear Sir/Madam,

    Good Afternoon.

    Today, I would like to suggest something which I have been thinking of for around six years now. This is about Pioneer MRT Station. Finally, after six years, I have finally found a way to re-route 243 to Pioneer MRT Station - without any lost connection!

    Well, I think so la...

    This involves re-routing 243 AND introducing a new feeder.

    1. New 243
    2. Length = 8.54km
    3. Boon Lay Bus Interchange
    4. Jurong West Central 3
    5. Jurong West Street 64
    6. Jurong West Street 75
    7. Jurong West Street 82
    8. Jurong West Street 81
    9. Jurong West Avenue 5
    10. Pioneer Road North
    11. Jurong West Avenue 4
    12. Jurong West Street 61
    13. Jurong West Street 62
    14. Jurong West Street 65
    15. Jurong West Street 63
    16. Pioneer Road North
    17. Jurong West Avenue 5
    18. Jurong West Street 81
    19. Jurong West Street 82
    20. Jurong West Street 75
    21. Jurong West Street 64
    22. Jurong West Central 3
    23. Boon Lay Bus Interchange


    Obviously, this would remove the direct bus connection from bus interchange to N6. To make up for the loss, as well as to anticipate increase in demand for bus services from a few new flats in the area, I would like to introduce a new feeder bus route: 245.
    1. New 245
    2. Length = 3.5km
    3. Boon Lay Bus Interchange
    4. Jurong West Central 3
    5. Jurong West Street 62
    6. Jurong West Street 61
    7. Jurong West Avenue 4
    8. Pioneer Road North
    9. Jurong West Avenue 5
    10. Jurong West Street 71
    11. Jurong West Street 61
    12. Jurong West Street 62
    13. Jurong West Central 3
    14. Boon Lay Bus Interchange

    The total length of new 243 and new 245 is around 12.04km. 
    The current 243 is around 10.2km in total. 
    This can be regarded as a new 1.84km bus route; 12.04km minus 10.2km.

    In addition, I would like to propose a new set of feeder bus network at the original Jurong West area - Boon Lay, Hong Kah and Taman Jurong. In this network, there are three routes, each plying only in one direction. Total length of the three routes is 16.26km, which is 0.86km longer than the current 240. This can be regarded as a new 0.86km bus route; 16.26km minus 15.4km.

    Each hosted by a new roadside bus terminal. Ideally, I would want the bus to go round and round without terminating, somewhat similar to the feeder bus routes at Yishun. As in, the bus stop for a while at the roadside bus terminal, engine turn off, all the components turned off, while passengers still inside. Nonetheless, a comprehensive review should be done to determine the best location for roadside bus terminals, because the only group of passengers that have to stay inside the bus while it is parked at roadside bus terminal should be those travelling intra-town, not those travelling to/fro MRT station.

    Alternatively, you may want to adopt my ideas for Boon Lay, Hong Kah and Taman Jurong, and come up with new route suggestions.

    I have created a map on Google, showing a visual representation of the above route suggestions. It can be found at the following link: https://www.google.com/maps/d/edit?mid=z0EIBCs5wxHI.kVffSYQ8JxwM

    Thank you for taking time to read this reply.

    Regards,
    PB / JWR
