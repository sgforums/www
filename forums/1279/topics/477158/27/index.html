    Well, it is good to have a direct bus connection to JIE from CCK/BP.

  • Personally, I feel that this service is more of a complement than a substitute of the current 194, since this route's focus is more on JIE than on JBP.
  • It may be better to have it as a new service instead of an extension to 194, since the current 194 goes directly to Jurong Bird Park from Boon Lay Bus Interchange, while this service does not.

    • For the JW-CCK section,

    • Since there is already 172 at Jalan Bahar and Jalan Boon Lay, I think it is more rationalized to have the bus ply Pioneer Road North instead, and enter the PIE at the earlier flyover (Nanyang Flyover).
    • This will enable people staying along Pioneer Road North to also have bus connection to CCK.
    • With 172 already providing bus connection from CCK to Boon Lay MRT/Jurong Point, there should not be a need for the bus to ply Boon Lay Way.
    • From International Road, the bus can straightaway enter PIE via Pioneer Road North.

    For the CCK-BP section, 

    • I think it is better for the bus to ply 
    • Teck Whye Crescent, Teck Whye Avenue, Jalan Teck Whye, CCK Road, Upper BT Road and Petir Road to arrive at Bukit Panjang Bus Interchange. 
    • This will provide bus connection to TW Crescent and Jalan TW, that do not have any bus service.

    Hi mr busAnalayser, yes economy of scale is very important. Thus when only HDB flats near soo teck LRT are up few years ago, they did not open soo teck LRT. They leave it closed. Instead, they made sbs 43 stop at one more pair of bus-stop along Punggol way at soo teck LRT. They also increased sbs 43 frequency which was used as a feeder service to get home from Punggol mrt. As time goes by, sbs 119 is extended from sengkang to Punggol to serve this group of people at Punggol sapphire along Punggol field.  

    Situation has changed now and since more flats have finished, the possibility of re-routing sbs 43 is not very feasible. Introducing a bus service soley for these residents will come much later after the plan to finalise fhe roads is up. Meanwhile, to keep holding back the West LRT from collecting service revenue is also not feasible since flats around the 3 stations are ready. Thus they decide to open finally after more than 14 years. Meanwhile in view of low crowds, they make it uni-direction and also reduced frequency of 8 mins. This is what they do to other developing areas in sengkang west initially.
