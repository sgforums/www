    Sorry your proposed changes just dont make sense to me. So much of route change for heavily popular services - IMPOSSIBLE to be ever implemented.

    some benefits I can think of:

    [related to Pioneer MRT Station]

    • residents at blk986-990 (Jurong West Street 93) would finally have feeder bus connection to the MRT network. To go to MRT, they can take the proposed new 243 to Pioneer MRT Station. To return home, they can alight at Boon Lay MRT Station and take the proposed new 243 home. Boarding and Alighting would be at the same bus stop (#22461), which is another plus point because Pioneer Road North is a very busy road and the barrier in between makes it quite impossible for anyone to cross the road. Likewise for residents staying along Boon Lay Way (further away from Jurong West Street 63). These residents do not have feeder bus connection to MRT. The proposed new 243 would provide for them feeder bus connection to MRT.

    • Pioneer MRT Station is actually nearer than Boon Lay MRT Station, for residents staying at Jurong West Street 61, 71 and 81. However, there is no bus connection (excluding 257) from there to Pioneer MRT Station. 179 and 241 are the only bus services available for them to go to Pioneer MRT Station but only those staying along Pioneer Road North have access to them. Besides, 179 and 241 are usually already packed when they arrive at bus stops #27329, #27309 and #22459. So there is not really a reliable bus connection to Pioneer MRT Station. The proposed new 243 would provide a reliable bus connection to Pioneer MRT Station from Jurong West Street 61, 71 and 81, and make Pioneer MRT Station accessible to residents staying in the inner areas away from Pioneer Road North. This would enable more residents (staying nearer to Pioneer MRT) to benefit from Pioneer MRT Station, and help to reduce crowding levels at Boon Lay Bus Interchange.
    [related to enhance bus connectivity within the area]
    • The proposed new 242 would use the current alignment until the junction at the Telecom building, where it would turn and use the current 243G route and loop at Jurong West Street 81. While providing feeder bus connection to Street 81 from Boon Lay Bus Interchange, the proposed new 242 also provides Street 81 residents with bus connection to Jalan Boon Lay and the cluster of temples along Jurong West Avenue 4. Currently, only Street 91 and 72 residents have bus connection to these areas. (Street 65 residents can get their bus connection to these areas when bus route 49 is extended to Boon Lay Bus Interchange via Jurong West Avenue 2/4 and Jurong West Street 64, which is the shortest route.) Street 72 residents seems to have an excess of bus connection to Jalan Boon Lay and Jurong West Street 76 (cluster of temples). Since there is already bus 99, the section of 242 can be given to Street 81 residents, so that they can also have bus connection to these areas - rationalisation. 

    • The re-routing of 181 would increase its coverage to residents staying along Jurong West Street 72 (nearer to West Grove Primary School) so that they can have bus connection to Gek Poh Shopping Centre and to Jurong West Avenue 3 - rationalisation. Meanwhile, the re-routed 181 would provide a faster feeder bus connection to Boon Lay Bus Interchange for residents staying along Jurong West Street 72. 

    • The re-routing of 241 would enhance bus connection between N9 and N6.
    Regarding your concern, rest assured that most people would not be affected by the changes to the feeder bus network.

    In the Gek Poh area, the changes would help to spread out the crowd to more bus stops in the area, so that while 2 bus services complement each other to serve the area, the bus stops would not be so crowded and the roads would not be so congested, especially during peak hours. Also, the re-routing of 181 would increase the service's coverage to areas nearer to West Grove Primary School and connect residents staying at Jurong West Street 72 to Wenya, while provide them a bus connection to Gek Poh Shopping Centre.

    In Jurong West Street 81, although residents would have to commute on a longer feeder route to Jurong Point, they can have a bus connection to the cluster of temples at Street 76 and Jalan Boon Lay (where they can have faster bus connection to other areas, via transferring to the trunk bus services at Jalan Boon Lay). Meanwhile, with the proposed new 243, their feeder bus connection to MRT would either remain the same or improve. Hence, every Street 81 resident would benefit, except those wanting to go to Jurong Point and/or Fronteir Community Place.

    We should see this as new BSEP routes being introduced in all parts of Jurong West Extension, although half of it is actually re-routing. 

    As I have mentioned, the weakened bus connection (eg. to Jurong Point/Boon Lay Bus Interchange), loss of some bus connection (actually, it is quite minor, especially since there is 185)(eg. to Pioneer Mall/Jurong West Primary School) and other bus connections that would still be unavailable can be provided by changing the Trunk bus network - especially when the new Joo Koon Bus Interchange opens in March 2015, which may see some Trunk bus routes nolonger going to Boon Lay Bus Interchange and new Trunk bus route(s) would have to be created to compensate loss of Trunk bus connections.

    These changes to the Feeder bus network would improve the feeder bus connectivity to MRT for Jurong West (Extension) residents and improve the bus connectivity within Jurong West (Extension area); enhance the bus services in Jurong West (Extension area).
