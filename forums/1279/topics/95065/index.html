    No, and that is the most funny thing about how the government tries to explain non-dublication and confuse the public.

    If we were to base this on basic demand and supply principles, and assume that all bus companies here are profit maximising entities, then dublication will actually benefit consumers either way. When two companies offer the same bys route, they still have to take into account actual demand along it, which obviously dosent neccesarily multiply by two with two routes. The demand is shared, and they will have to adjust and reduce their frequencies if need be in response.

    If they were to do the uneconomical thing of offering an increase in frequency despite low demand, and try to charge higher fares, then a price war can result, with each companuy under-cutting the other to gain more passengers. The result is that both comapnies end up having to keep their prices low anyway. In some cases, they might get into a cartel and both raises fares to the same level, undermining the basics of open competition, and which I assume is the main problem you are refering to. Of coz, both are only possible provided there is no governmental intervention and the like.

    But of coz, I was refering to the current state of affairs here, with governmental control over certain aspects such as pricing. It almost always helps to have more then one route to take from point A to B, which I assume most of us would have discovered in our daily journeys.

    competition for the sake of competition (esp. in a small market like ours) is self-destructive.
    the tv stations may be one example with both bleeding millions over the past four years. for a business, the bottomline (profits/losses) is the priority.
    another example would be the low-cost carriers...tiger,airasia, jetstar asia, valuair (even though not strictly lcc), thai airasia, merpati, lion air......good for consumers initially, but from the examples in europe, unsustainable in the long run and leading to company closure and job losses. lccs are willing to undercut even the cost price of a seat to "compete" with rivals. probably strategically competitive, but financially ruinous.

    having said that, i think the present situation is just nice...both smrt and sbstransit are still making profits *overall*. we still have some choice. my mom remembers a time when there was NO tibs...people were simpler then..they just wanted to get from point A to point B...without arguing over whether there was a monopoly or not, or competition for the sake of competition.
