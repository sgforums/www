Buses in Bangkok

    I went for a holiday to Bangkok and just got back a few hours ago while I was typing this. It was a revelation indeed. I've never expected Bangkok to have such a wonderful array of buses. At first glance, you'd be forgiven for thinking there are a few bus operators in the city. Although there are a number of liveries, the public buses are all controlled by the Bangkok Mass Transit Authority (BMTA). The BMTA, operating under the jurisdiction of the Ministry of Transport & Communications, is charged for providing bus services in Bangkok and the nearby provinces. Different colour buses denote different type of service and charge different fares. They are as follows:-
    1. Regular bus (4 baht): Cream-Red
    2. Regular bus (5 baht): White-Blue
    3. Expressway bus (5.5 baht): Cream-Red
    4. All-night bus (5 baht): Cream-Red; from 2300-0500 hrs
    5. Aircon bus (8-16 baht; distance related): Cream-Blue
    6. Bendy bus (10 baht): White
    7. Euro-2 bus (10-20 baht; distance related): Yellow-Orange
    8. Microbus (3.50 baht; plying main road): green
    9. Microbus (3 baht; plying lanes): purple

    The bus models for:-
    1. Mitsubishi Fuso & Hino AK
    2. Hino
    3. Same as 1
    4. Same as 1
    5. MB O404 (I think), Hino AK, RK
    6. So, Singapore is not the only ASEAN country with Bendies. I never expect to see Bendies in Bangkok. Buses are MB O405G but I there is another unknown model.
    7. The cream of the crop. New, bright and clean. Looks like Hispano buses and colour is like CSS. Several models viz:- Hino RU1J, Daewoo BH115, MB O404 and Isuzu.
    8 & 9. Reminds me of the old CSS minibus or KL's Bas Mini. Not as pleasing as its bigger counterparts.

    Don't ask me about routes. Destos are in Thai.
