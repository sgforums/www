    Have you ever ride on a bus in a temperate country? I'm sure you haven't, or else you would know. Heaters are installed on the floor. Why, you ask? Common sense will tell you its because hot air rises. There is no point installing a heater above your head because then, only the ceiling would be warm. The vents above, even in Europe, are for the air-con.

    In Switzerland, the buses have both, a/c on top and heaters below. The heater would be turned on in winter and the air-con in summer. In spring/fall, it is enough to just open the ventilation pods and the few small windows. However, if you're stuck in a jam, it would get very stuffy inside.

    Originally posted by Scania N113CRB luver:
    You cant really say about Habits . That vent is originally designed for heating , supplying warm air into the bus in cold countries .
    Wrong. The Habit was designed from scratch based on TIBS's requirements in 2000. During the launch of the Habits in 2000 (where the public were given free joyrides on them around the neighbourhood), the TIBS staff mentioned about the dilemna they faced when designing the aircon vents as both the person & the aisle seat would to control the aircon for themselves. Unfortunately, the aircon vents have to be located above the window seat due to design constraints, so they decided to use the unique vent design that regulates the flow of aircon rather than the direction (when it worked).

    However, the plastic wheel mechanism isn't exactly durable and breaks easily leading to all the uncontrollable rectangular holes now (esp with SMRTB's questionable maintenance)...
