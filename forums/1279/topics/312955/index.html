    In the news video I could make out the last bus being drove away a DM Trident, parked just next to the ten buses that are in the picture. At that time it was a 'hot' situation and the flames were already burning the second bus from the left, originally, all the lots were full. The staff I believe did the best they could, maybe not as fast.

    Generally in vehicular fire its best not to move any vehicles near it, that means sadly those vehicles will have to say bye bye as well, until the fire is under control or is already put out. This is all due to safety procedures. Aircraft fires are also the same. Money in this case is not a concern as piority is given to staff and fire brigade to do their job. Buses should only be moved away when the fire is still in its premature stage, or, when its totally put out.

    If I put myself in the shoes of a depot manager, I don't mind if ten twenty or thirty buses got burnt rather than one of my staff die because of moving a bus away from the fire. I will 'kenna' lawsuit for putting a staff in danger and even manslaughter, but if buses are burnt, at most I lose my job.
