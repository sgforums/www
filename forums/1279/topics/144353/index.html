    Dear forumnites, it's time to wake up and sober up.

    I will not entertain any replies or clarifications with regard to this. If you do not like it, leave this forum. If you think it does not apply to you, take note and read on, but a reminder that you will take whatever consequences that might arise from your actions. I'm buying you time here, I'm putting up my last-ditch effort to save this forum, so if you are against this, we will proceed to close the forum down.

    Recently I have received many complaints about how the standard and quality of discussion of this forum has dropped. You might not know but many fellow bus fans, local and overseas, have detested the people partcipating in the discussions here.

    This Singapore Buses forum is (or was) one of the top bus enthusiasts' forum for Singapore. A Google search on Singapore buses will quickly locate us in the top ranking. We used to attract a lot of quality discussion; everyone who knew SgForums.com were impressed with our work, our enthusiasm, our sheer amount of information, research and data. With that, we were one of the Top 10 forums. Our highest ranking was 6th placing, only behind Cars forum. That was the amount of dedication that we had put in. That was before many members left this place due to the diminishing quality of discussion.

    And I dare to say that then, for every post we had, it was a worthwhile one. I myself can spend one whole week working on a single reference post. I could have clocked many posts for one long post, with my moderator's account, but I didn't. Other posts would come in for suggestions & comments to improve the reference. All of us pulled in and helped each other. We shared knowledge. That was the sheer purpose for the existence of the forum.

    Now, when foreign bus enthusiasts wants to know what Singaporean bus enthusiasts are doing, they do a search and they step into SgForums.com. They see this mess that we have, and left quickly. I ask you, of the so many topics created in our forum, how many are necessary? How many are only for chatting purposes? How many are duplicates? And how many of these are the works of regulars who post in this forum?

    Imagine you had never seen this forum. Clear your mind. Now you load the page and you see all these topics. The quality of discussion. Beyond the stickies, there is nothing well worth reading. Who would be interested about your fondness of a particular service? Who would be bothered to read a thread whose title simply reads "Svc 91" or "Service 107"? I give you the benefit of doubt. I opened up the topic, only to see unimportant chats about a particular service. I give up on the forum and leave.

    Looking through the topics is enough to shun any geninue bus enthusiast off. Do you have an idea what it takes to be a bus enthusiast? Have you seen the web pages of what other bus enthusiasts have done? Have you seen the amount of dedication in it? Have you visited other bus forums around the world and see how real hobbyist discussions take place?

    Do you have any idea that what you are doing now is tarnishing the reputation and image of Singaporean bus fans, given the popularity of Singapore Buses forum? Do you have the passion and enthusiasm to be called an enthusiast, and have pride in your work and be recognised as a "bus fan"? Do you have what it takes to be a bus enthusiast? If you do not have, step out of here or go act like a professional bus enthusiast.

    I will make this clear: Singapore Buses forum is a professional and specialised forum. This is not your playground where you may post as you wish. Chatty posts will not be tolerated. I will not tolerate anyone who wants to make use of the forum to increase their post count, or worse, to help increase the rating of the forum. I would like to highlight to you that this forum does not need your chatty posts to increase its rating. Yes, we don't need you here, please get out. Go, create a forum of your own and try posting every minute there. Or better still, go create a script to do that if you are skilful enough. Make it to the top! Then I'll congratulate you and ask you the whole purpose of doing this. Do you feel glorious being at the top from the 'feat' you've managed to pull through? Even if SgForums.com decide not to remove this erroneous ranking, you'll feel ashamed to be on top. This I reaffirm, we do not need your ratings. We would rather not be in the Top 10 or Top 20, but to offer quality posts and discussion that will make people come again and again.

    I urge you to come up with constructive posts. For the regulars, why come here to ask for your directions when you can have an online bus guide & street directories handy, and since you are online in the first place? Even better, as regulars, shouldn't you be the ones providing the answers? Does your enthusiasm not be enough to give you the will power to flip through a bus guide or street directory to familarise with places? If this is so, then I don't think you have enough enthusiasm to be a bus enthusiast. Wouldn't it be shameful for a bus enthusiast not to know his bus routes well?

    Be hardworking, go source for more information when the need arises. Do not sit there and ask "Why?". Why not start a topic with information rather than questions, and go research on more information that you can share with others? Or ask for more inputs? You'll be full of satisfaction and pride from the work you have done. Be innovative, not crappy. Recent topics on KL and JB bus routes are a good starting point. That is also the reason why I'm supporting them wherever I can. For the KL thread, I sourced for an online map and spent one whole night to improve it using a primitive Microsoft Paint which everyone have. That night, I slept at 2am. The image is clear but huge (>1Mb); I could have easily given up on the project with my 56k connection but I see the dedication and want to push the flow. Every alteration means a 20-minute work, 10 minutes to upload, another 10 minutes to review. It is tough work but this is the dedication that has guided my work here.

    Why do I not see this level of enthusiasm here from those who call themselves "bus fans"? Those who are active here (minus those long-serving members and mods), you will be the third generation of bus fans. One day, you will be the representatives of the bus enthusiasts community in Singapore. You have a heavy responsibility. We are already over that stage; we have done our fair bit of contributing to the society and community. Now it's our turn to pass down the batton. Sooner or later, one of your people will become moderators and take on a more senior role. But before that happens, you have to act like a moderator, mentor and leader.

    I dare say that this Singapore Buses forum is the online bulletin board that has helped improved the profile of the bus fan community and popularized bus spotting as a hobby that is not "weird" and limited to a few. We have a role to play; this forum did not exist simply as a place for discussion. This forum has come a long way and I am sure it has been great help to many people who are interested in buses to know the latest trends and happenings, and to understand how we work. Surely, seeing the state of the topics list, that is not reflective of what we are doing, right?

    Thus, I will like to highlight a few points for all:
    1. No flamming. Too many a times we have people who are involved in flaming wars due to misunderstandings. I do not wish to see this happen again. Everyone who post here should adopt a learning attitude; be prepared that there may be errors in your posts. Learn from it. Those clarifying, please do so with some tact and courtesy. Remember that we are not doing correction, but simplying doing a kind reminder. Humans will err, and there is no point in hurting your fellow bus enthusiasts or the forum by being right. Have mutual understanding and being gentlemanly.

    If both sides of an argument believe they are right, then please, stop and think. Think you are definitely right? Bring some evidence with you, may it be photos or references. Not sure? Then maybe it's time to do some research and clarify for all's sake. Everybody in the forum is reading, it's not something between 2 persons. Have the forum in mind.

    2. Watch your creation of topics. I don't wish to see multiple threads being created for a single topic. Already, for the 2 new services 57 and 58, I see one for Service 57, one for Service 58, and one for both of them? Is it necessary? Do we need to check 2 threads at a time for discussions on a single matter? As a reference, from now on, a single topic shall only be created for one new service. An example, 1 thread for Service 57 and 1 thread for Service 58 is enough. The press release will be reflected under the appropriate sticky. Full stop. Nothing else will come out. We need to be organised, clear, neat and lean.

    3. Naming of topics. Another issue is the vague way topics are named. Citing as an example, how would I know that you are referring to the new signboards in place at Bishan Interchange, which might indicate a new service there, if you simply put the topic name "BISHAN BUS INTERCHANGE"? Long topic names are fine, go ahead. The longer the better, as allowed, if you need to make yourself clear.

    I hope to see some improvement by National Day, or the sooner the better. I am working very hard to save this forum. This will be my last major assignment as a bus enthusiast so I hope I won't be disappointed.

Our hobby is one of it's kind, it enables people of different age groups to mingle together, to learn and grow. Instead, perhaps contributed by generation gap, alot of misunderstandings were present. Sure, I don't blame those who doesn't read our rules and regulations, but I certainly won't welcome those who test the system.

    I was once a very active moderator, trying to fend off any flaming, vandalism and anything that would degenerate the forum. But it was too much that I decided to stop. Apparently it shows that some just do not have the self discipline and be considerate.
