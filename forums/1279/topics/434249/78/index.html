    Why is SMRT Buses retiring their old buses even though some still have quite a long lifespan?

    The guess is that it's probably due to the 2020 full wheelchair accessible fleet, and if some of the old TIB-era buses are left to run to their full 17 or extension to 19 years, it would be hard for SMRT Buses to transition to a full WAB fleet by 2020 due to the space in depots (given the replacements cannot come in until the old buses are gone). Unlike SBS Transit, SMRT inherited a younger TIB fleet and doesn't have the luxury to extend all the models.

    Also, by bringing in the clean Euro V zero step wheelchair accessible buses like the MANs sooner, it also benefits all the passengers in general in terms of environment, comfort and accessibility - and also the wheelchair bound as more wheelchair accessible bus routes can be introduced quickly.

    Info from BIS~
