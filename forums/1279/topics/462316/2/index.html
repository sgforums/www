    [Mercedes Benz O405G/Hispano] 17 years have passed, and it is time for the retirement of the very first articulated (more commonly known as bendy) bus introduced into service as a demonstrator in Singapore. The last day of service is 12th March 2013.

    It was airflown in by the local Mercedes Benz agent for Trans Island Bus Service (TIBS) ahead of Singapore Bus Service's own articulated buses (Volvo B10MA, later followed with a Mercedes Benz O405G), with a bodywork that follows the earlier Hispano "Box" O405s, and aptly inherited the "box bendy" nickname. It did its maiden service on service 171. 

    It was registered in the middle of the Scania L113CRL ELBO bodied buses between TIB833X to TIB848D - TIB838H for its auspicious numbering of 3 and 8. Farewell, you have made a great history with the production vehicles following with O405G with Hispano (MK I, II and Habit) and Volgren bodywork.

    Seen here is the vehicle on its last permanent service 180 with its original Hanover flipdot signage that was used on the earlier Hispano bodied CACs O405s.
