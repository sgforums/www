    Singapore Shuttle Bus (SSB) was set up in 1975 because of the "Park and Ride" Scheme introduced by the government. SSB operates the City Shuttle Service (CSS) which plied from the fringe carparks to the CBD zones. In 1976, CSS services were extended to the public housing estates. Today, SSB provides regular bus services to and from the housing estates through the CBD and later merged with former TIBS in 1985 which is now SMRT now.     AFAIK, CSS was a joint venture between SSB (now part of SMRT group) and NTUC COmfort. They used Merc O309 minibuses painted in different livery. SSB's buses were painted orange, while NTUC Comfort's ones were blue, as some old photos suggest. The Comfort routes were withdrawn before I became aware of my surroundings. I only remember the time when CSS services were renumbered. By then, Tibs had been in control of SSB for a long time.

    It was a mistake to withdraw 604 as it provided a quick link between Chinatown and Mountbatten/National Stadium area. With new developments like Suntec City coming up in subsequent years, the need for a cheap and direct connection with other parts of the CBD became necessary. Chinatown now has C2 to Suntec City, but it doesn't come cheap, though.
