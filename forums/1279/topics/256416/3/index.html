    Putting the main subject (in this case the bus) off-centre is distracting. When someone look at the photo, they will not get what it is you are taking. By putting the subject at the centre, one look, BAM!, ah...a Scania 113 on 225. Also, there are too many objects in the photo and, to a non-bus fan, it is unclear which one you are focusing at.

    If you want something unique, look for ways to enhance your subject. Try different angles. Reffections, either from a puddle or from the glass window, is good too. You can also try to enhance the background. Get an MRT passing behind, for example. Try not to get things blocking, like poles, fences and lamp posts.

    let me say a word of fairness...
    as a media student

    rule of thirds is subjective. In this picture, you can see that the main focus is the yellow rubbish vehicle and the bus. the eyes starts from either the bus (due to its size) or the rubbish carrier (due to its colour)

    the way this shot was taken can often be seen on posters, advertisements etc.

    now the flaw,
    many would notice that there are too many distracting things in the background. that is one.
    number two, why many find it distracting, place an imaginary dot at the bottom half. the picture is overweight on one side. its like a see saw and the pic is not balanced.

    if there were lesser distraction, rule of thirds would work damn well.

    but as he mentioned, its abstract. actually, NOT.
    he is just following one of the rules that was created ages ago.

    for the other pictures from other forumnites, it is straight to your face and that the main focus is only the bus.
    means that the pics only have value to a certain target audience, the bus enthusiasts..

    elsewhere, it would just be labelled as "jus another bus picture"
