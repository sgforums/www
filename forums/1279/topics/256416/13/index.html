    Mistakes in the picture (spotting own mistake is important in learning)
    1. Too many traffic, lols, must pick the correct timing.
    2. Bus too short exposure time. This must rely on luck, and if BC speed off, the whole frame is 'wasted'.
    3. Lens flare from street lamp. I didn't bring the flare cap out, so must remember to bring next time...
    firstly,
    this photo is fine if the subject or theme of it is on "A Bustling City' or something to that connotation. if the subject is on buses then u cant open ur aperture with F8 as it will require a very long exposure time of 8 sec (that u used), with that high volume of traffic.

    if the subject is on the bus then u have to use a MUCH shorter exposure time of 1 or 1.2s. Use F8.0 with long shutter only if u want the 'star' effect of the bus headlight of which where u referred to as 'lens flare'. Its not lens flare.

    that is the aperture blade of ur camera creating that effect. this occurs when u set to a very small aperture. (F5.0 or F6.3 onwards in diff scenarios)

    with that, i should have also answered the cause of ur point 3.
