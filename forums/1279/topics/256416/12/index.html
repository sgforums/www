    Hi, very nice shot, lols, I love the way it shows the power of the VSO, great comparison!
    And nice to some of the pther pics that include the low factor, like 73R, also good, and a good mindful thing about being low is also safety, and the proportion of the front section and the sides.
    Some interesting thing is also that in photography, there are also known as golden hours. You may think it as heck, haha, I did ever thought so too, but the golden hours are 8-10am and 4-5pm (1600-1700hrs). The times are when the sun is in its inclination stage, just position yourself back facing the sun, and you can find the subject you are facing in brightly lit. This depends on the location you are at and the weather that day. If the weather is bad and cloudy, hmm, you might want to reconsider. And I love night shots too, just be mindful of you ISO rating (I normally don't exceed 800, my camera only works well within that), and your speed settings. Maybe you want to experiment with light streaks (long exposures) too, if time and camera (and parents) permits...
