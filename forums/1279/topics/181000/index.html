    Real-time bus info soon
    (TNP, 4/3/2006)

    SBS Transit, a subsidiary of transport giant Comfort DelGro, is expected to call a tender for a real-time bus information system in mid-year.

    The company hopes a prototype of a system, which will tell passengers the exact time their bus will arrive, will be ready by early next year.

    The system will also let commuters know why a bus is running late and whether it could be caught in a jam during the journey.

    SBS Transit and the Land Transport Authority earlier shelved plans for similar systems because of high costs.

    But satellite-tracked automated vehicle management systems, of the sort which will form the backbone of the project, are now sophisticated and cost-effected then before.

    This real time bus arrival information system is running hand in hand with the AVMS. Growth Venture (a subsidiary of Singapore Technologies) and LECIP Technologies were offered the tender to provde installation of the AVMS system in February this year. 4 temp staffs were employed by SBS Transit in Mid March 2006 to carry out AVMS readiness checks on all 2700 SBST buses, currently, Growth Venture is carrying out rectification works to AVMS systems with problems identified. The AVMS system is expected to be ready by May 2006.

    The AVMS system, otherwise known as Automatic Vehicle Management System is a full fledge fleet management system which allows the SBST HQ to monitor bus deployment and unforeseen road conditions. When Bus Captains meets any emergencies or faces any troubles on the road, the AVMS systems doubles up as an Emergency Communication System for the BCs to directly communicate with the HQ. The AVMS system is running on GPRS technology instead of the latest 3G technology due to cost constraints.

    The Real Time Bus Arrival Information System tender is set to be open after the AVMS system is ready for use. Currently, extensive usability test is carried out on service 88.

    Hope this information is helpful =)
