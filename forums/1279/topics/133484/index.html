Most comfortable seating locations on different bus types ?

  • hseochin
    23 May 05, 08:41
    1. Rear engine bus (both rigid & articulated); around 1 m from front axle

    2. Front engine bus; around 0.5 m from rear axle

    3. Mid engine bus; around 0.5 m behind rear axle towards end of bus

    4. Mid engine articulated bus; difficult to decide here ! but i'll settle for
    within 0.5 m from turntable !

    5. Older bus with seperate engines for vehicle & air-con, also difficult to decide
    but i'll settle for middle spot !

    I note that generally the overhead mounted air-con units often used nowadays tend to be less noisy & vibrasive than the olden time 'donkey' engines mounted underneath the floorboard. Best if they positioned it @ the middle of bus so then there's some 'quiet' spot @ the front end in a modern rear engine bus !

    In my perception of comfort, i take into account suspension ride comfort besides the noise vibration harshness levels & from personal experiences find the above locations 'best compromise' seating / standing locations on a bus for
    travelling comfort. In front engine or mid engine buses, the suspension ride comfort @ the rear section especially @ the end can be terrible, whereby you get hard spine-jarring rides easily even when going over bumps @ low speed.

    Any experiences different from mine or better locations i'm also interested to know !
