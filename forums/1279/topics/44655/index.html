NEL Fares Has Been Made Public

  • Superbus
    9 May 03, 01:44
    The fares are already out and it will be 5 cents to 25 cents more expensive than current SMRT fares. No increment to students, child and NSMen fares.

    10 cent discount will be given for commuters during the first 3 months of operation. However SBS Transit do not expect any profits for the first 2 years of operation, and even said that it will not break even for the next 2 years of operation. The discount will cost SBS Transit $2 million. SBS Transit has proposed higher fares in order to make up for the losses.

    On a side note, there wasn't any mention of bus service rationalization. Though there was a mention of SBS Transit 'keeping a service from Dhoby Ghaut to Sengkang, so as to provide an alternative to the NEL'. Which in my opinion is that we would see Service 85 operating for a longer time than previously anticipated.

    Cheers and warm regards
