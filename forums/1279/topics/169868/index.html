    Originally posted by SBS 9631X:
    But how come the VO3x(Batch 2 and 3)sometime sound more like they got more than 4 gears?And what speed of the gearbox do the Scania N113CRB have?
    that is due to the torque covertor locking up at the first gear, which causes a sensation akin to a gear change but is actually not. this means, it stays in the first gear longer. this is a trait of the ZF 4HP500 gearbox with the torque convertor set to lock at first gear.

    there are also ZF 4HP500 gearboxes which lock-up at the second gear (e.g. Mk IV Strider, Dennis Lance, B10BLE, B10BLE-CNG), which takes advantage of a more powerful driveline to increase take-off acceleration and to enhance inter-city start-stop operations.

    whether the torque convertor locks up on first or second gear depends on the engine. the series of SMRT O405 and Mk II's have both types of configurations depending on the tuning of the engine.

    it's very hard to pinpoint over text how to differentiate. we might need a outing soon then we can teach.
