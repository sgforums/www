    Originally posted by SBS1908B:
    Low pitch rumbling? There are also some B10Ms which emit a lower pitch also but increases in pitch as the bus accelerates, the pitch is not dependant on rpm but seems to be more dependant on speed of wheels. Is that the hub-reduction axle? What is its anyway?
    A hub-reduction axle is the big cylinder found on the rear axle of the O405. Inside, there are gears immersed in oil. B10M do not have a hub-reduction axle. The hub-reduction axle makes a "wooo wooo wooo wooo" sound when the bus is at speed.

    The newer generation O405s have quiet hubs and they are most of the time drowned by the much louder engine noise. However, due to the age of SBST's O405s, the hubs have become louder. An O405 with a full load can also make the hubs 'hum'.
