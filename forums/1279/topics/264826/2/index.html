    Close shave for triathletes, bus driver did not stop to check

    A group of 20 cyclists training for an upcoming Ironman competition had a close shave on the road, when all of them fell while trying to avoid a TIBS bus and one of them was nearly run over, said STOMPer Edmund Oh.

    "Routine morning training ride turned tragic for a group of triathletes training for the coming Aviva Iron 70.3," Mr Oh said in an email to STOMP on Saturday (14 July).

    He said the accident happened at about 6.50am on Saturday, along Sembawang Road, 50 metres before Dieppe Barracks.

    "A TIBS bus Service 169 (TIB 1055) moved out of the bus bay without checking blind spot.

    "His action caused a group of 20 cyclists to brake or sway to the side to avoid crashing into the bus.

    "Unfortunately, one of the cyclists could not react fast enough. He lost control and fell in front of the moving bus.

    "Fortunately, the driver managed to stop the bus in time. Otherwise, with another metre or so, the fallen cyclist would have been run over and crushed to death by the bus," Mr Oh said.

    He is also concerned that the driver did not stop to offer help.

    "A few minutes later, the driver drove off without even checking on the condition of the cyclist or rendering first aid to the injured cyclist, as the bus should have a first aid box," said Mr Oh.

    "Could the relevant authorities please explain whether the action of the driver is a common procedure practised by the company?

    "Should drivers involved in an accident simply drive off without even checking on the condition of the injured party or rendering first aid to the victim?" he asked.

    STOMP is contacting TIBs about this.
