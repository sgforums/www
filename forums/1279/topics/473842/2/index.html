    Damn fail eh u. I think you need explanation. 

    So now, there are two types of buses we're talking about. Here, I show you what the terms mean:

    B9TL: The type of bus. The model. Made by Volvo in Germany. This is the core of the bus. Made to the standard of purchase for bodywork. 

    Wright: The bodywork provided by Wrightbus in UK. This is the Wright version of bodywork applied onto the B9TL. Aka the round front kind of bodywork you can see on Singapore roads now. 

    Gemilang (bodywork): The bodywork provided by Gemilang in Malaysia, which has also supplied the KUB and OCs etc. with their bodywork. It is also the company that has offered the Gemilang bodywork for the B9TL mentioned in this topic. 

    So now you know what all there mean? It's either you say Gemilang B9TL or Wright B9TL. NOT Gemilang Wright, since Gemilang doesn't make bodywork for Wright body works, nor Wright Gemilang bodywork since they are in no ways related in the production of the bodywork mentioned in this topic. Hope this helps you get your facts right.
