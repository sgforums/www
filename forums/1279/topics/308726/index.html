    I went to the Singapore Airshow today. Situation still looked like the same as yesterday. I'll elaborate on the getting-out-of-site part since that is the worst part where so many people are getting out of CEC.

    1. The snaking queues started from the entrance of the main exhibition hall onwards, going towards south before U-turning back towards north and into the shelter. It took me an hour just to get onto the shuttle bus.
    2. Most of the show visitors wanted a cheaper way of getting home, thus queues for the Pasir Ris MRT Shuttle was ultimately long. Even with more than hundred bendies deployed, the queue is still long.
    3. The pick-up point for the Pasir Ris MRT shuttle can only allow boarding for one bus. Why not two? Why did SMRT and the organising committee not kill two birds with one stone?
    4 The Expo MRT Shuttle saw fewer people because it costed more. I was one of them who took that bus back.  (And the damn thing is that TIB 0479K came before TIB 0465A. I wanted to take TIB 0465A since it's someone's favourite bus. But I was forced onto TIB 0479K instead. .)
    5 At the time when we were about to leave, a millitary plane was being towed, hence resulting in a major pile-up at the bus bay and delays. The organisers seem to have quite poor planning again.

    But again, such events are unpredictable. The organiser should improve the situation in the case for the next Singapore Airshow - coming up in 2010.
