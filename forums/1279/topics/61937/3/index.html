    Sorry, it's swing nose crossing (SNC), not single nose crossing, though it's also logical to call it single nose crossing..

    Definition of Swing Nose Crossing: A set of points incorporating a moving blade in the Common Crossing that is moved according to the lie of the points. This eliminates the `gap' in the running rails and makes for a smoother ride and reduces wear.

    This type of crossing is found almost everywhere in the MRT network.. The central 'V' shape rail can actually swing to both ends (it is movable).. So imagine the 'V' as a different colour (red?) from the main rails in ur head.. Now the 'V' rail can swing towards the straight track or the curved track so that the train will follow the direction 'V' is swung towards.. for live anmation, visit Jurong East middle platform and wait at the side towards Marina Bay.. the crossing changes every few minutes (when train jus left and before train arrives at platform) and then u see the corresponding movement of the trains when they pass through the crossing..

    Common (or diamond) crossings are only found at Changi Airport Terminal Station where the trains change track to go to either platform.. it is the same as 2 SNCs placed together, meeting in the midddle and passing through each other at 90 degrees.. when the trains pass through the intersection it is particularly loud.. try noticing it the next time u go there..


    Swing Nose Crossing at bottom left, Common Crossing at top right

    A middle overrun tunnel/track is simply an additional third tunnel/track in between two tunnels/tracks connected by crossovers.. they can hold a train temporaily while it reverse or jus park there awaiting instructions.. they can be found between these stations: Yishun - Sembawang (track), Farrer Park - Little India (tunnel), Woodleigh - Potong Pasir (tunnel).
