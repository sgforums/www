    While a welcomed improvement, commuters Channel NewsAsia spoke to gave SBS Transit the thumbs down for the way the delay was handled. 

    They still raised questions over the announcement of the delay earlier in the day.

    The morning peak-hour crowd at Hougang Station was told to expect an average of 20 minutes' delay for service in both directions. 

    But for some commuters, the wait was as long as 50 minutes! 

    Over at Chinatown Station, there were similar frustrations that the wait was longer than announced.

    "If it's going to take more than 10 to 15 minutes, just be honest about it so I can make an informed decision and not be so late...Don't keep promising it's going to be 10 or 15 minutes (delay) and it turned out to be 40 minutes," said a commuter. 

    Another commuter said: "I am from Sengkang and it took me like one hour and 20 minutes" compared to the usual "30 minutes".

    One passenger, Mr Toh told MediaCorp that he was late for work and ended up leaving his job earlier than planned.

    "I come late, at around 9.20am...late for around 20 minutes...start arguing (with the boss)...because of this thing...suddenly, I lost my job! Because of the MRT (delay). If nothing happens, I (can work) until Monday..."

    In the afternoon, commuters were told to brace for more delays - of about 30 minutes - after SBS Transit found another fault, this time on the signalling system.

    By the time the evening rush hour kicked in, some commuters said they would have to wait for multiple trains before they were able to board one because the station was packed. 

    One commuter also said he waited for about 10 minutes before he decided to take a shuttle bus.
