    KMB Introduces Express Route 268X

     

    KMB announces the introduction of a new express route, Route 268X [Yuen Long ←→ Jordan (To Wah Road)] with effect from Saturday, 13 September 2014. The route will go via West Kowloon Corridor, bypassing Mei Foo and Sham Shui Po, with a journey time from Yuen Long to Mong Kok of approximately 25 minutes (the actual journey time depends on traffic conditions). The full fare is $14.20.
     
    Departures of Route 268X will leave from both Yuen Long West Bus Terminus and Hung Shui Kiu (Hung Yuen Road) Bus Terminus for Jordan during the morning peak period, and from Jordan for Hung Shui Kiu during the evening peak period. Passengers can enjoy discounted fares on other routes (including Route 68X) when interchanging at Tai Lam Tunnel.
     
    Departures of Route 268X from Yuen Long West Bus Terminus will go via Hi Lee Path bus stop, Tung Lok Street bus stop, Sun Yuen Long Centre, Tai Lam Tunnel and West Kowloon Corridor to Mong Kok and Jordan Monday-Saturday (except public holidays). The departure times will be 7:10 a.m., 7:22 a.m., 7:34 a.m., 7:45 a.m., 7:55 a.m., 8:05 a.m., 8:15 a.m. and 8:25 a.m. Monday-Friday, and 7:10 a.m., 7:25 a.m., 7:37 a.m., 7:49 a.m., 8:01 a.m., 8:13 a.m. and 8:25 a.m. on Saturday.
     
    Departures of Route 268X from Hung Shui Kiu will go via West Kowloon Corridor to Mong Kok and Jordan Monday-Friday (except public holidays). The departure times will be 7:35 a.m. and 8:00 a.m. Departures from Jordan to Hung Shui Kiu will be at 5:20 p.m., 5:40 p.m., 6:00 p.m., 6:20 p.m., 6:40 p.m. and 7:00 p.m. Monday-Friday except public holidays.
     
    Route 268X will replace the morning special service of Route 68X (from Ma Wang Road to Jordan). Passengers travelling to Jordan may take Route 68X or new Route 268X (from Hung Shui Kiu) as an alternative. Also with effect from 13 September, the starting point of the morning service of Route 968X [Ma Wang Road (Shan Shui House) → Quarry Bay (King's Road)] will be relocated to Yuen Long West.
     
    For more information, passengers may call the KMB Customer Service Hotline on 2745-4466, or browse the KMB website at www.kmb.hk. Complete route details will also be available on KMB and LWB Mobile Apps from 13 September.

    KMB concerns about the “Railway Development Strategy 2014”

     

    • KMB is concerned about the “Railway Development Strategy 2014” announced by the government.

     

    • KMB expects that comprehensive consideration is given by the government to broader multi-modal transport-related issues in tandem with the development of the railway network. KMB recommends in particular that the government should thoroughly plan the coordination between rail and other mass transport systems, including buses, so that all modes of transport may continue to have room for sustainable and healthy development thereby ensuring that Hong Kong citizens have a sufficient choice between different means of public transport. A well-planned multi-modal transport system also safeguards the functioning of the city in case of any major service disruption or sudden surges in demand.

     

    • It is also KMB’s hope that the government would announce the details of the railway projects as soon as possible and ensure that the congestion problems on the roads created by railway construction work is managed properly to minimize disruption and delays to other modes of transport. Other stakeholders, especially the operators of road transport systems, should be consulted as soon as practical with a view to reaching mutually acceptable solutions.
