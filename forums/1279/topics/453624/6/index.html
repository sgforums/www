    Man who pushed woman off bus tells TNP that he has clinical depression

     

    REPORT: AMANDA PHUA

     

    WALLOWING in self-pity or simply trying to tell his side of the story?

    While some netizens have reacted angrily to Mr Alex Ong's various online posts on why he pushed an elderly woman off an SMART bus at 3.15pm on Tuesday, he says he was trying to explain his actions.

      The New Paper reported yesterday that SMART bus service number 167 was along Upper Thomson Road, before Thomson Plaza, when Mr Ong got into a heated quarrel with the 76-year-old woman and eventually pushed her out the rear door of the stationary bus.

      A few passengers then stepped up to help the elderly woman, who appeared unhurt, though visibly shaken.

      Mr Ong later apologised to the woman.

      He contacted TNP after the report to try to explain his side of the story.

      Referring to comments posted on video sharing site YouTube, social networking sites Facebook and Tumblr, he said: "I replied to some, but there are so many and some are very hurtful. I feel like I'm going to explode already.

      "It's so stressful. I know I have done something wrong but I have already apologised. (By putting up the online posts), I am not looking for sympathy.

      "Now people are blaming me for everything."

      He said he will be putting his blog on a month-long hiatus to aloow things to blow over. His blog was still accessible at 7pm. An hour later, the site asked for a password before allowing access.

      Mr Ong, who has been unemployed for more than six months, claims to have been diagnosed with clinical depression since 2009, and obsessive compulsive disorder (OCD) and Asperger's syndrome since he was in primary school.

      He said he is now taking one type of tablet medication daily for his depression and OCD only.

      Mr Ong said he has not watched the video making its rounds on social networking sites.

      "I don't want to watch it. The contents are not making me happy. It's unfair to judge based on what happened in the video.

      "Who can think rationally when angry? If you can find one person who can (do so), I will give you all my money.

      "I just wanted her physically out of the way to continue my journey. I'm very sorry that she bruised herself."

      He wrote on his online posts that he was feeling stressed from his long unemployment and the difficulties he faced in applying for a job. He told TNP he left his last job after two months because he didn't like it.

      "I am looking for jobs now, and I'm planning to reply to an email about a job offer. But I don't know what they are thinking of me now."

     

     

    Growing up unhappy

     

    Mr Ong said he dropped from Express stream in secondary school to the Normal Academic stream, then went on to take his GCE O levels as a private candidate.

      He has plan to further his studies in a local arts school, but has not done so because of financial problems.

      He also claimed that his growing up years were unhappy, leading to his depression.

      Said Mr Ong: "I went to the police today over a threatening comment. The guy sent it over a private message on Facebook. He said he will punch my face if he sees me.

      "That got me very worried. If it gets worse, I will file a complaint with the magistrate court."

      He apologised repeated over the 20-minute long interview.

      He said: "It was a somewhat bad day. I feel emotionally annoyed at times.

      "I'm not in contact with the lady, but I'm still worried if she's okay, I didn't ask for her number and she didn't give it to me either.

      "I didn't know how to explain the past few days. I'm emotionally unstable."

      Police have classified the case as voluntarily causing hurt and have called Mr Ong in for questioning.

      He may face charges, depending on the outcome of ongoing police investigations.
