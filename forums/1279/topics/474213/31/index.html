    Random quiz:

    Services A and B share the same bus interchanges. From Interchange C, they will follow each other until Road D where A turns right and separates from B. A and B will travel totally different routes, but will meet again at Road E. Then, they will separate again (B will turn right while A will go straight) and only meet again at Interchange F.

    What are:

    - Services A and B?

    - Interchanges C and F?

    - Roads D and E?

    Clue: They are SBS Transit services and A is dual depot controlled, while B is single depot controlled, though B's depot is one of A's depots.

    There are these 2 services, A and B. Service A run mostly parallel to MRT Line D while Service B run partially parallel to MRT Line E. 

    The two services would meet near MRT Station F, which is the interchange for the two MRT lines above. They will travel together until Avenue G here they will split up and head to their respective interchanges. The immediate stop of B after splitting up serves an MRT Station, H, on Line D. MRT Station H is one stop after an interchange station on Line D. 

    Warm-up question: Identify A and B (and all the rest of the unkowns if possible, it would show how much you comprehend this question ).

    Question: What is common between these two services' fleets?

    Service A has a sister service, service C, which run nearly entirely parallel/identical to A, and is also similar in a few other ways besides the routes they take.

    Bonus question: Identify C.
