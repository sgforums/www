    Ok When it comes to this question, what you're gonna have to do is to spot unique features and phrases of this question. 

    Which highlight the possibilities? What are the possibilities?

    Nearby depot: CGBP from BNDEP, WLBP from WLDEP. 

    Interchanges that has two sides of exits: Tampines Interchange, Woodlands Regional Interchange. 

    Then, when it mention two sides, it means that the services can never possibly be the ones that goes by the same side. 

    Possibilities: 38 (only remaining svc in Tampines int that is controlled by BNDEP) vs 31, 10, 65 etc. (Tampines)

    169, 187, 856, 963 vs all other WLBP svcs (with respect to the sides) (Woodlands)

    If you work it out, none of the services that exit through the UOB side actually fits into this question. So Tampines is out. 

    What about woodlands?

    Possibilities: 858, 962, 964, 965, 966. 

    964 and 966, besides their own corresponding interchanges, never really pass by any interchanges, so they are out. 

    No services goes from Woodlands to Sembawang, so 962 is out too. 

    Now 858 and 965. Passes by Yishun Int. Only one service goes from Woodlands to Yishun, 856. 965 does not ply Sembawang, so 965 is out. 

    So answer is A: 858, B: 856, Road C: Canberra Rd, Road F: Yishun Ave 2, Interchange E: Yishun Interchange, MRT Station D: Sembawang MRT Station.
