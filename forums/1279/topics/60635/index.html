    As for buses, there should not have been such a thing called "strong-hold interchanges" in the first place. It stifles competition, and keeps everyone hanging in between the status of being a market controlled, yet regulated economy (which will never sustain). Cut the cords loose entirely, and sit back and watch them slay each other.

    I am sure that within a few days of reform (yes, a bitter medicine which these behemoths of a GLC need to take, quoting from PM Goh), they would find tons of fluff called "red tape" in their own companies. I can even guarantee it!

    Singapore is too small to allow a "middle-of-nowhere" system of transportation, and really, do the PTC need to worry about there not being enough bus services when complete free competition is not instituted?
