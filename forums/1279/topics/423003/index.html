    This is HG Int on a typical morning at 0825. I was standing in line waiting for 151 as usual. Someone please justify how such a queue is even possible for this relatively low demand sector of the 151 route (relative to Toa Payoh - Ngee Ann Poly stretch)

    The only explanation was that the 151 frequency in the mornings is about 20 odd minutes.

    Next picture:

     

    On board 918D at the 2nd stop. Bus was full by then and went on to skip the next few stops, much to the despair of those living in HG who badly needed the lift to work.

    And what happens when the bus reaches NUS? It arrived with 3 other 151s, all of which never started from HG. This is really nothing short of appalling IMO. One of them started from Macpherson area, while others probably started from Toa Payoh and Bukit Timah etc.

    BC slowed to crawl at Bukit Timah to reduce the bunching up, but that wasnt simply possible with all of that happening. So 151 has a strange paradox of chionging at off peak hour and crawling at peak hour, producing a variability in route time of 50 minutes to 2 hr 15 minutes (jam included).

    I might also add 151 has a horrible record of missing stops. Case in point: Today, this woman pressed the bell and stood at the exit. The bus slowed........................and went past the bus-stop. She screamed at the BC but he went on the next stop along the expressway, some distance away!

    This scene plays out about half my rides, I'm not exaggerating!

    In short: I really dunno what SBST is doing despite so many complaints about 151's frequency issues. Is it improving? It is. That much I acknowledge, but certain route sectors are clearly neglected.
