    Originally posted by ^tamago^:

    only buses that have hit their 12-year lifespan (or about to) will have them installed. that means all the o405's and lances are auto-included in this scheme.

    these buses fall under the old lifespan scheme of 12 yrs previously, and depreciation costs are accounted for based on a 12-yr lifespan. so once the 12 yrs is over, the buses have no depreciation costs at all. this makes it easier to upkeep by chartering them out on contract in exchange for a stable revenue enough to just maintain the buses, while those buses which have depreciation as a factor of costs will remain on public revenue service which can cover the higher costs. all these are only possible provided SMRT has the facilities and cashflow to stable both the new buses they're acquiring and these older buses which can be used for chartering.
