Wenya St (Bulim St) is about 150 metres before making a inverted P-loop (q). on the left side of the loop is a building (no name) (now, the name of the building is Kintetsu World Express (S) Pte Ltd ; 27 Bulim St Spore 649175). At this end of the loop, it's facing diagonally the junction of jurong west st 22/25 & ave 3. [when facing that junction, that loop is at a north-east direction].     to some extent, the section of Bulim Avenue (after Bulim Street) resembles Jurong West Avenue 4 (towards Pioneer Road North) - also a road that slopes at a gradient, although the gradient here would smaller than that over at Jurong West Avenue 4. 

    It seems that in this second phase of construction of road infrastructure in Bulim, the purpose is just for the bus depot to be developed.

    The remaining section of Bulim Avenue will be constructed, along with the first section of Bulim Drive - to allow for the developement of the new bus depot. 

    I think only the section of Bulim Drive that is required for development of the new bus depot will be constructed. 

    I think the other section of Bulim Drive will only be constructed in future, when the road alignment is finalised.

    According to the plan released by the Housing & Development Board (HDB) when they released a site at Westwood Avenue for developement of Executive Condominium Housing, it seems that there will nolonger be a junction next to the existing compound of Westwood Secondary School - where, according to the Masterplan, Wenya Drive (Bulim Drive) and Jalan Bahar were supposed to intersect - even though there are provisions for it.

    I guess the plan now is for the western section of Wenya Drive (Bulim Drive) to be re-aligned to slightly northwards to the future boundary of Westwood Secondary School, north of the future Indoor Sports Hall.

    I doubt the alignment of Wenya Drive (Bulim Drive) is still the same and has been shortened to just end at the intersection with Jurong West Street 25, because just one minor arterial road (Bulim Avenue) is not enough to serve the anticipated high traffic in the future industrial area. There definitely has to be at least two minor arterial roads connecting the industrial area to major arterial roads. So I guess there will still be an intersection of Wenya Drive (Bulim Drive) with Jalan Bahar, only that it will be re-aligned northwards to accomodate the expansion of Westwood Secondary School's compound and WWSS's upgrading.

    but then again, this may not be true. I am only speculating.
