    Wenya... is actually a messed up place. It was supposed to be a HDB area but somehow, the government changed its mind and decided to halt HDB developments there and re-zone it to be an Industrial area, without considering the HDB developments already at Wenya. As a result, there are now many complications that has to be settled to combine the HDB developments with the new industrial developments.

    The "new"(at 2012) plan for Wenya was to construct 2 new Minor Arterial (Category 3) roads - Wenya Avenue and Wenya Drive - and 2 new 21.4m-wide roads.

    • The new roads already there were constructed last year under "Proposed construction of roads, drains and sewers at wenya (phase 1)" - consisting of the 2 new 21.4m-wide roads and a section(until the inner 21.4m-wide road) of Wenya Avenue.

    The Road plan for Wenya shown in the 2013 URA Master Plan is a different one. It seems that the Road plan for Wenya has not yet been finalised; there will be more changes to the Road plan for Wenya and construction of the new road network would probably continue only when everything is settled and things are finalised.

    For instance, in the 2012 plan, the new Wenya Drive was to be constructed next to the existing compound of Westwood Secondary School. However, Westwood Secondary School needs to expand its compound to accomodate an Indoor Sports Hall and additional blocks.

    • Similar to Jurong West Primary School, the existing compound of Westwood Secondary School does not have sufficient space to construct new buildings. There is a need to expand the compound; a portion of the land adjacent to Westwood Secondary School is required. This affects the road plan of Wenya.

    According to the 2013 Mater Plans, there seems to be some demand to include Civic & Community Institutions and Places of Worship in Wenya, which explains why the road network is a different one. The expansion of Westwood Secondary School's compound is going to change the road plan again.

    This may explain why the construction of the bus depot has not yet started, as the new Wenya Drive might be part of Phase 2 construction of roads, drains and sewers at Wenya and there has to at least be an extension of Wenya Road to the future junction before the constuction of the bus depot could begin.

    Nonetheless, since azharjj mentioned that , as of yesterday, there were "tipper trucks going in & out to a deep forested area that is now being cleared", I suppose Phase 2 (or part of it) has already begun and construction of the new Wenya Bus Depot should start soon - at around June this year - when Phase 2 (or the part of it needed to facilitate construction of the bus depot) is completed.

    Meanwhile, at Jurong East, construction of the new Big Box building started in March last year and right now, the "shape" is already there. Based on this, I guess the new Wenya Bus Depot would require around 1.5 years to construct, since it should be "smaller" than Big Box and would require less time to construct.

    If start at around June this year, Wenya Bus Depot should be ready by the end of 2015. #justsaying

    The site opposite Jurong Junior College and east of Wenya Avenue is designated for 'Transport Facilities'. Since there is already this proposed bus depot at Wenya Drive, I suppose it would house only trains, which I believe are for the future Jurong Region Line.

    There could have been an integrated depot housing buses and trains, with either a train depot underground and a bus depot on surface, or a bus depot on surface and a train depot above the bus depot. Instead, the bus depot at Wenya is along Wenya Drive, at the other end of Wenya Avenue; the bus and train depots are located at separate sites. Unless, there are plans to build another bus depot at the site, but would it not cause Jurong to have an excess amount of bus depot infrastructure? 

    Apparently, there are neither plans to build an underground train depot nor plans to build a bus depot at surface and have a train depot above it, and that only a surface train depot would be built at the site. This is hint that Jurong Region Line is a LRT because it might be due to the financially unviability (being more costly to build and maintain) of an underground LRT depot, in contrast to an underground MRT depot, that is why the train depot would be on surface and not underground. This is also hint that Jurong Region Line is elevated because the train depot would be on surface instead of underground and there is (probably) not enough space for trains to travel between the surface depot and the tunnels underground. 

    +By the way, regarding the alignment of Jurong Region Line, back in 2012, I heard my peers say that there is going to be a MRT station (very) near to Westwood Secondary School. I shall not reveal more (I do not want to get into trouble with the authorities.)
