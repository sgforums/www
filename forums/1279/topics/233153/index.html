    PTC to allow more premium bus services for niche market

    SINGAPORE: Bus commuters will soon have more premium services to choose from.

    The Public Transport Council (PTC) has revised the premium bus service scheme to allow more operators to come on board.

    Chairman Gerard Ee said that the PTC hopes the scheme will complement the basic public transport services and provide more choices for commuters.

    The revised scheme will take effect from next month.

    Under it, the PTC may grant more licences to operators that offer premium bus services.

    This is to cater to commuters who are prepared to pay more for a superior level of bus service, such as a more direct and comfortable journey.

    According to the PTC, a recent bus passenger satisfaction survey showed that one in five of the respondents who took buses indicated a preference for better service at higher fares.

    Feedback from the bus operators was also taken into account in the review process.

    COE-exempted buses (that is omnibuses or school buses) may be used to operate premium bus services.

    Premium bus services have to meet the following requirements:

    (a) operates only with air-conditioned buses;

    (b) All passengers must have a seat; and

    (c) Fares are at least 1.5 times the equivalent fare of basic public transport services.

    - CNA/ir
