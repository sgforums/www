    SBS3812E of Svc 13 also had VLS enabled, but it appears it is running an older version of VLS, like most SBST buses.. for one thing, the readers were never disabled (what's the point) but the fare stages were updated when it detects that it is approaching the bus stop.. it works by detecting constantly and repeatedly the location of the bus along its route (as stored in the IDFC) and changing the fare stage if it believes it is approaching the bus stop along the route.. this is lousy as there is no point in doing this anyway..

    this old way and version of VLS makes the IDFC give out a rather inaudible, high-pitched and very short "beep" if it is detects the bus as being off the route and the IDFC displays "VLS OFF ROUTE".. and from the amount of beeps heard on buses running the old and not updated VLS versions (mostly SBST buses and the "beeps" come on whether VLS is on or not) it is apparent the old way of verifying whether the bus is on its route repeatedly is a bad idea since the incidences of the GPS not receiving the satellite signal if it comes under a tree or other obstacles is causing a lot of "VLS OFF ROUTE" beeps as most bus spotters have noticed.. so the mystery of those irritating IDFC beeps is finally solved..
