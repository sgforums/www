    SL 334 and SL 97 seems to have well-maintained fleets. (including the crossovers, and 97e)

    BB 99, the VO3x Batch IIs are OK. 9225K and 9227E are in sorry states. No way for comparison.

    SL 52/AM 52, both seem ok. When SL 52 got their first buses, they all seemed to be in sorry states. Today, most of them improved. AM 52 also got an improvement. However, don't look here for a broken speed limiter.

    BB 156 is in a sorry state.

    BR 143 looks pretty much OK.

    AM 162 looks average at best. Recommend me 2804? Ok. I'll try. 2801 to me is average at best.

    BN 66 is disgusting. "Burnt" Striders, "burnt" MK IIs, what more?

    HG 80's maintenance looks pretty decent. Whether it's truly ok is a different story; I haven't really taken them.

    HG 105 is ok, but not what you call impressive.

    The DTs on HG 62 seem ok. Controllable.

    HG 27, in the times I've taken, don't seem to be very well-maintained. Fuming air-conditioner, and fanbelts, but the fanbelt problem is not very serious on HG 27.

    My best-maintained criteria:
    1) structural integrity of the bus.
    2) ability to CONTROL. (not speed. Speed then crash?)
    3) The presence of a speed limiter (if it's broken, what maintenance is that?)
    4) The amount of patchwork done on the bus. (if there's a lot of patchwork compared to ordinary flaws, it shows the depot is effective in patching, even if the colour may not be correct. Recall the R on reuse and R on recycle?)

    With regards to flaming, why flame? There're people around who are living in the very estates which you're criticising about, but you don't see them getting their face black and bombarding you with barrages of questions on "Why?"

    My residence allows me to experience a little of Soon Lee, Hougang, Bedok North, Bukit Batok and Ang Mo Kio. You can say negative things about other buses. Just accept it and consider them. That's all.
