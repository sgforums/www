    The hex (#) sign is not called sengkang in Malay. It is called dwi pangkah (lit. double crosses). The hyphen or dash (-) is called sempang. Sengkang in Malay means to have something (eg. a fishbone) stuck in your throat.

    Jalan Minyak>Oily Road
    Eunos was named after a prominent Malay leader, Eunos Abdullah. He was responsible for the opening of a Malay Settlement in the area which now bears his name.
    Telok Blangah - Telok means "bay" in Malay. Blangah (or belanga) is an earthenware pot.
    Tanjong Kling - Tanjong means "headland" in Malay. Kling was a term used long ago to refer to Indians (even today but to a much lesser extent as it was considered to be impolite). It was derived from the name of a powerful Indian state called Kalinga.
    Bukit Purmei - Bukit means hill and Purmei (or permai) means peaceful.

    The (-) is also called Sengkang, hence the phrase "Kosong empat sengkang enam empat tiga" or "Zero four dash six four three" (#04-643).

    Other meanings of Sengkang are; long plank to lock a door (in olden days), an object to keep a mouth opened and walking with the legs wide apart.

    May I also note that the pronounciation of Sengkang in Malay is "Sng-kaang". I believe the Chinese pronounciation of Sengkang is "Sayng-kaang", right?
