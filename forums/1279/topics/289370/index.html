    Originally posted by chocolates-xed:
    i understand the diffrences bet mk III and IV but mk II CAC and mk III is hard to differentiate
    the windows u must get used to the differences.

    the front, Mk II has a windscreen divider at the middle while Mk III doesn't have. Mk II's radiator grilles and desto "black" area are thinner and wider as well.

    behind, Mk II has three depressed lines at the top while Mk III does not have.

    if in doubt, u can always see the rego. a 3-digit one is a Mk III while a 4-digit one is a Mk II.

    Me add on:

    1) The rear lights are a give-away, the MKII with horizontally-arranged lights and the MKIII's being vertical.

    2) The presence of 3 covers on the roof of the MKIII for the ventilating fans (should be the function?). The MKII CACs lack that. Moreover I believe except for maybe 1714T (?), no other MKII CAC uses the Sutrak AC unit nor the Fuji one which the MKIIIs use.

    3) The corners of the windows of the CAC MKIIs are curved because the ledges once accomodated sliding windows. The MKIII's is a 90 degree one.

    4) And of course the noise they make, this one I don't think I need to describe to you.
