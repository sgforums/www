    don't jump to conclusions. you read up more before posting such crap here.

    for those who are still unsure about the Scania chassis labelling system, here it is.

    for example, K230UB 4x2

    first letter - engine positioning.
                       K: longitudinal engine mounted behind rear axle
                       N: transverse engine mounted behind rear axle
                       L: longitudinal engine mounted behind rear axle, inclined 60-deg leftward
    L-series engine is no longer available now.

    middle 3 numbers - horsepower output of engine (eg. 230, 270/280, 310/320)

    last 2 letters - type of bus
                            UA: articulated bus, urban usage
                            UB: normal bus, urban usage
                            UD, double-decker bus, urban usage
                            IB: intercity bus
                            EB: long distance coaches

    well, the "4x2" in this example simply means that, total 4 wheels, with 2 wheels being driven. hence, to determine whether the bus is a 2-axle or 3-axle, you've to check with this system.
