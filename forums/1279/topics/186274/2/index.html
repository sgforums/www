    Please do not compare braking systems of different vehicles as one thing. 2 wheeled vehicles do not have problems of unbalanced braking compared to axled vehicles.

    Our latest buses run on front disc and rear drums. To brake efficiently, the driver relies on the bus heavily. If the workshop failed to do up the buses properly, can land the driver in deep shit. Of course, if any accidents take place, everyone in the line of fire will get part of the bullet.

    Brake linings, air leakage from H/brake valve, footbrake valve, quick release valve, brake booster efficiency, foot brake lever calibration contribute to lousy braking. And contamination of air system can also lead to air leakage.

    A bus carries a massive weight and thus braking it requires both technical and driver's skills. In 2 wheeled vehicles, there is no such thing as brake imbalanced. In axled vehicles, if the brakes are imbalanced, the vehicle will swing either side when braking. Sometimes as serious as to hit the vehicle in the next lane. (That's why all my personal vehicles I insists on disc brakes, lower chances of unbalanced braking and easier maintenance; lesser headaches) Being much lighter, bicycles and motorcycles requires much lesser braking force. But for a bus it means much more than that.
