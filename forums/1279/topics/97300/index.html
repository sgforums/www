    A bit of elaborations on certain buses:

    Volvo B10M MK II - Though old, it has huge amount of torque, and no speed limiter in them (somehow)

    Volvo B10M MK III - Not all of them are that sluggish. Some can be really speedy. That really depends on who the driver is

    Volvo B10M MK 4 DM, WA Strider - Same type of engine, good power, but screeching fan belt can cause people to pull off their hairs.

    Volvo B10M MK 4 DM 3500 - The most quiet type of B10M MK 4 so far. Good pickup and good speed. However the gearbox might not be suitable at all times

    Leyland Olympian 3 Axle - Don't ever think that they are old and slow. In fact, they can be quite good speeders. Again, this depends on how the driver manipulates them.

    Volvo Olympian 3 Axle - 92/93 series can speed, as they seem to be ligher in some manner. Some of them between 94-96 sries may be sluggish, but performance wise is kinda average.

    Volvo Olympian 2 Axle - Lightweight, fast, and windy. If it's roof-less, it can be a convertible.

    Scania N113CRB - Oh my god! Accleration can really win other models, just by the step on the accelerator. However, the gearbox can be jerky at times, creating rough rides in between journey.

    Mercedes Benz O.405 - One of the primitive batch of Single Deck Air-Con Buses. Engine can be really powerful at times.

    Dennis Trident - Out of the double deck range, this fella has better acceleration. Might be due to its horsepower.

I have sat on a MkIII (svc 89) that overtook an SMRT O.405 on TPE and I have sat on a VO3X (9619H) that overtook a Trident on TPE and if that is not enough, I have sat on a LO3X that sped up the ramp to TPE and cut straight into the middle lane of TPE, overtaking 2 SMRT O.405s and a Mk4 DM3500.

Another driver who has a good schedule will generally roll along so as not to catch up with the bus ahead of him. They have schedules to follow and they generally want to follow according to their schedules.
