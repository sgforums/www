    PIS (Passenger information System)

    A screen inside the bus that displays information to passenegrs. most commonly next bus stop location. Displays may also tell passengers of jams or diversions.

    Screen can be LED or LCD

     

    A very advanced PIS will be the

    GDS (Graphic Display System)

    As its name suggest the GDS will show graphics, including maps or conenctivity info or the bus stops info on a schematic diagram like train line.

    The GDS also can show what time the each bus service or even Metro or train  arrives at next bus stop

    anything that does not look like a LED screen or LCD screen is a GDS.

    and for display systems otuside the bus..

     

    GDS only applies for bus.  For train line maps on screens they are still called PIS.

    EDS (Electronic Destination System)

    the EDS acts exactly like a bus destination infromations creen. in Singapore it shows service number, via places and destination.

    In taiwan it shows from location to location with service number

     

    In Hong Kong it displays the to destination and svc no. a few hong Kong buses have LCD EDS.

    In European countires EDS are simplier with just destination and service number.

    EDS come mostly in LED. either single colour format or many colours.

    In hong Kong it is mandatory for EDS to be in yellow words n black background.

    In Singapore it is usually ornage words in black background.

     

    i have yet to see any operator use a reverse format of black words, bright background although this is possible to set in most EDS.

     

    common EDS systems are Hanova and MobiTec.

     

    EDS are not only for bus. they cna be found for trains as well and LRTs. in taiwan trains have EDS showing which direction train goes to.

     

    future?

    With the advent of Organic LCD technology, soon EDS or PIS may convert to OLCD or OLED technologies.

    Wheelchair ramps / lifts

    Wheelchair ramps are fitted on todays modern buses.

     

    there are many kinds of ramps

    Flip out ramp

    Flip out ramps when not in operation lay flat on the bus floor. Desgined to be a flap lying on a base support, the flap part  flips out and extends the ramp system into a 2 ramp part. One still on the bus and the flap out to the kerbside.

    the Mannual Flip out ramp is the most common in Singapore.

     

    the auto flip out ramp is the most common in hong Kong.

    Slide out ramp

    the slide out ramp is normally enclosed underneath the bus base. when needed to be used the ramp slides out and then hinges down to the kerb.

    One step ramp
    Ramps fitted on one stepped height buses. Ramps can be a pull out ramp from the step to the kerb level , a very long ramp.

    or a two section ramp with a sharp incline. one ramp to cover the step to the lower step and then another ramp snaps into place to form the final part that extends from lower step to kerb.

     

    one step ramps can be found in many Japan buses.

    Wheelchair lift

    the most primitive type of ramp is the lift. here the PIW rolls into a box like thing nad it lifts the PIW ontot hte bus deck level. normally used on coaches with high flooring for luggage .Eg mercedes travego. or even the disbaled freindly vans.



    There are two main types of Wheelcahir ramps

     

    Auto (motor) ramp

    quite obviuso how these work..hahah

    or mannual ramp

     

    In Mannual ramps are hand operated either using levers to pull ramps (mannual flip out) or grip holds to pull ramps (slide out pull ramp) .

    U31 SCN and RCN are same model of K-U31 N (wheelbase 5.5 meters) under the Nissan Diesel U/UA series. SCN and RCN are not proven to be different in any way and hence they can be named under the same model. However, U31 RCL would come under the K-U31 L which features a shorter wheelbase of 5.1 meters. The U31 model was produced until 1993 as an export model for Singapore; production discontinued 1984 for Japan market and succeeded by P-U/UA32.

    K- is the suffix for Showa 54 (1980 AD) Emission Regulation.
