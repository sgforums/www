    Did you know...

    1) That the B10M MKIV DM3500s' suspensions are not passive and do auto-level while on the move and at rest. It's simply less obvious than the later buses.

    2) Also, its ride-height can be adjusted, unlike the other B10Ms. It also has kneel function, but that has been disabled apparently.

    3) The Voith buses have NBS function and do employ them.

    4) Also, contrary to popular belief, Voith does employ lock-up clutch. It engages just before the transition from 1st to 2nd, making it less obvious than the other gearboxes. Notably, this reduces Voith's flexibility for on-road conditions because the clutch engages/disengages ONLY during that transition.
