    Originally posted by jayh272416:

    true but i thought residential trunk/feeder services should get priority for WAB, not these. previously i heard of 94 gerting WAB quite early too... it's quite weird.

    There's 3 important criteria before a service can be converted into WAB status,

    1. 80% of the bus stops along the route must be wheelchair friendly, and the interchange MUST be wheelchair friendly.

    2. Frequency/schedule of WAB buses used, at least every alternate bus is WAB friendly, or as per scheduled. (this is from what i remember, do correct me if im mistaken)

    3. LTA consultation and approval

    As you can see, sbst/smrt can only control #2. LTA does the upgrading of the bus stops and interchanges, so if the infrastructure does not support it yet then even feeder/trunk services that should have priority will have to be put on hold.

    Of course, as you can see, the very crucial ones that ply hospitals are being pushed for WAB status first, then of course there's the important routes, and finally the "filler" routes.

    "Filler" because there's no urgency to get it to WAB status, but since the infrastructure and buses are already there, why not just make that into WAB status and the overall % of WAB services by the co. would have "filled" up by a certain percent.

    Of course, this is my view of how i see the issue, so feel free to voice your thoughts.
