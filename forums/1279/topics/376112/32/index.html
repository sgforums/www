    Originally posted by TIB770T:

    The proper way of calculating the checksum is as follows:

    Say you have a rego: ABC1234 _ where the blank represents the checksum to be solved.

    Ignore A, and concentrate on the other characters.

    For postions B & C: The alphabet in positions B & C carries a value, which corresponds to their postion in the English alphabet, ie A=1, Z=26

    For postion B, Mutiply it by 9, For position C: Mulitply it by 4

    For positions 1, 2, 3 and 4, the weights (ie the number you need to multiply) are 5, 4, 3 and 2 respectively.

    Then add every number up.

    Then divide the sum of the numbers by 19.

    Then use the remainder to determine the check letter:

    0: A
    1: Z
    2: Y
    3: X
    4: U
    5: T
    6: S
    7: R
    8 :P
    9: M
    10: L
    11: K
    12: J
    13: H
    14: G
    15: E
    16: D
    17: C
    18: B

    And you will get the rego.

    Say you have SBS9455 and you don't know the alphabet:

    Mulitply the value of B with the weight: ie 2 (value) x 9 (weight) = 18

    Then mulitply the value of S with the weight: ie 19 (value) x 4 (weight) = 76

    Then muliply 9 with 5, 4 with 4, 5 with 3 and 5 with 2, you will get 45, 16, 15 and 10 respectively.

    Add all the values up, 18 + 76 + 45 + 16 + 15 + 10 = 180

    Divide 180 by 19, you will get a remainder of 9.

    Check with the corresponding alphabet: 9 = M.

    And there you get it. SBS9455M.
