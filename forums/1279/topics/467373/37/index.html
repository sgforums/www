    It was mentioned before that Jurong East Bus Interchange will be rebuilt by year 2020. Considering Boon Lay Bus Interchange took three and a half years (mid-June 2006 to mid-December 2009) and Bedok Bus Interchange took three years (end-December 2011 to end-December 2014), the construction of the new Jurong East Bus Interchange should start by next year (2016).

    At this moment, the new road near Jurong East MRT Station/Bus Interchange looks almost ready to open. I believe a land parcel along that road will be released for sale this year, to develop a mix-land use development comprising of the new Jurong East Bus Interchange and some other developments.

    Since this is a new bus interchange, I hope the government can take this opportunity to construct a new Jurong East Bus Interchange that has around sixty (or seventy/eighty, etc.) parking lots, so Jurong East Bus Interchange can take over the role of Boon Lay Bus Interchange as regional bus interchange. Many long-distance bus routes (eg. 154, 30, 178, 198, etc.) at Boon Lay can be shortened to Jurong East and a re-drawing of bus networks can compensate the loss of links (or even improve the bus connectivity in the area), since not many people take buses to travel intra-town when MRT can provide a faster travel. Besides, Jurong East is the regional centre of Jurong, not Boon Lay. { Or maybe not now, since now these long-distance buses provide intra-town connection from other areas to Jurong. Maybe when Cross Island Line opens, then there is no need for such bus connections, that such routes can be re-routed to terminate Jurong East Bus Interchange instead. So, for now, I feel that the upcoming new bus interchange should have a large number of parking lots to anticipate the possibility of it becoming the regional bus interchange of Jurong in future. }
