    • SMRT will have a new bus depot at Bulim Drive in 2015. Hence, seeing a SMRT bus (in new livery) driving at Jurong West Street 81 may mean that there will be a new SMRT bus service at Jurong West Extension.
    • Recently, OneMap included 'Bulim Avenue' and 'Bulim Street' into the website. I have not went to that area to check, but this might indicate that the 3 new roads are now open for the general traffic. 
    • With Bulim industrial area opening up, there is a likelihood that there will be new bus services - in the form of extension of existing bus route and/or introducing new bus route - to Bulim Avenue and Bulim Drive. As SMRT will have a new bus depot at Bulim Drive, LTA may create a new bus route that originates from the new bus depot, to complement existing bus route 181 and connect Bulim industrial area. It is very likely that there will be another bus service complementing 181 to serve Bulim Industrial area, as the industrial developments would probably all be multi-storey and there will be larger-than-average number of people seeking employment in Bulim Industrial area, such that just one bus service is not sufficient to serve the area.
    • Since 2009 when Pioneer MRT Station, there has been a consistently high demand for bus connection to Pioneer MRT Station from Jurong West Street 61, 71 and 81. There can be a new bus service that can have the secondary function of providing bus connection to Pioneer MRT Station.
    • By 2015/2016, there will be a new healthcare facility at Jurong West Street 93. In near future, there will be a new hawker centre and a new polyclinic at Jurong West Street 61. A new bus service can be created to serve these new developments.
    • The appearance of a new SMRT bus at Jurong West Street 81 could mean that there will be a new bus service starting soon. Bulim Bus Depot, however, will only be ready in 2015. Other than Bulim Bus Depot, there is no bus depot near Jurong - except Kranji Bus Depot, nor any bus facility that has space to accomodate more bus services. Hence, there has to be interim measures taken for a new SMRT bus service to be introduced at Jurong West.
    • In the interim, the buses for this new SMRT bus services would probably be supplied from Kranji Bus Depot, before the Bulim Bus Depot is ready to supply buses for it.
    • As this bus service originates from Bulim Bus Depot, yet Bulim Bus Depot would only be ready in 2015, in order for SMRT to run this bus service, there has to be a temporary roadside bus terminal for the buses to park and for the drivers to rest. Hu Ching Bus Terminal is along a two-lane road that is rarely utilized and has no bus service plying it. Based on this, the temporary roadside bus terminal would most likely be located along a road that would hardly be used. With that, I guess there will be a temporary roadside bus terminal constructed at the short-road (not the main road) that is also named Bulim Avenue, for this new bus service.
    • I guess the rest of Bulim Avenue and the future Bulim Drive will only be ready next year. Hence, in the beginning, this new bus service would probably only go to Bulim Avenue (the minor road) and terminate there, where the temporary roadside bus terminal would most likely be located at. In 2015, when the Bulim Bus Depot is ready, this new bus service will then be extended to ply more of Bulim Avenue and terminate at Bulim Bus Depot.
