    Introduction

    The lush green livery was chosen by a public vote organized by the Land Transport Authority to signify as the new unified livery as public buses in Singapore transition to the Government Contracting Model (GCM).

    All public buses would eventually be in lush green livery, regardless of operator. GCM operators are already in the process of repainting their fleet to lush green livery.

    The process for incumbents to transition to the new livery could begin as early as September 2016 (after BSOL renewal and negotiations are completed).

    Purpose

    The whole process may take years, as it was the case in Melbourne and Sydney when they transitioned to the PTV and TNSW liveries respectively. This thread is to consolidate all new observations of public buses repainted into the lush green livery, as well as any related discussions to the new livery.

    Fun facts

    1. The winning margin for lush green was 144 votes out of 57,505 votes cast. Perhaps canvassing by bus lobbyists on social media was enough to influence the outcome to become lush green?

    2. Blue was originally one of the options (similar to the DTL ad), but it never made the final vote.

    3. LTA was forced to spend extra money sticking a bright red livery onto an ordinary new A95 as a decoy, after bus lobbyists leaked the results of the winning colour when they published photos of the MAN A95 concept bus.

    4. The lush green livery currently appears to be applied inconsistently across operators.
