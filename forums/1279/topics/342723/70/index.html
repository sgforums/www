    Bus: SBS 7444H
    Service: 25
    Date: 10 Dec 2009, Thursday
    Time: 1916 hrs (at time of witness)
    Location: Bedok Interchange
    Nature of Breakdown: Unknown

    Details:

    1. SBS 7444H on Service 25 was attempting to reverse out of Berth A27 at Bedok Interchange when the bus stalled and stopped in the middle of the road.
    2. All passengers alighted and were ushered to wait at the berth. They subsequently boarded SBS 9432D.
    3. Destos were down at time of witness.
    4. Mechanics and "Green Men" were seen deployed to the scene to assist the bus captains.
    5. Service 16 and 33 were similarly affected by this incident. SBS 8340S was attempting to move out but was unable to (delayed departure), but slowly inched it's way out later on.
    6. SBS 9432D of Service 25 and SBS 0854D of Service 16 also experienced difficulties trying to reverse out. SBS 9432D had to do a V-shape manoeuver after reversing while SBS 0854D simply reversed but had to look out for SBS 7444H (the breakdown bus) to ensure it did not hit the rear of the breakdown bus.
    7. Other arriving Service 16 and 33 buses simply had to make use of the other berths to park their bus.

    15th December 925 am (0925 hrs) at Hankyu Railways Minami-Senri station, a passenger route bus of Hankyu Bus (subsidiary of Hankyu Railways) collided with a pedestrian bridge (2.5-meter height) near the taxi stand. Out of the 8 passengers on board, 5 male and female passengers of 48 to 74 years old suffered injuries to their face and head.

    Suita Police is investigating the case from the bus driver (58 years old), suspected of dangerous driving causing injuries.

    Hankyu Company says that the driver saw a sign saying 'Priority to pedestrians' and thought that there is a detour. The company also commented that 'It is likely that the driver made a mistake, we apologize to the injured (passengers) and we will do our best to prevent such accidents again'.
