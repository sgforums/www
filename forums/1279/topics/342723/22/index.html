     32 hurt in bus collision

    Eyewitnesses said the accident happened at a bus top in front of a 7-11 store and next to the Fragrance Hotel at about 10am. -- PHOTO: STOMP
        View more photos

    THIRTY-TWO passengers, including foreign tourists, were hurt when a Night Safari bus hit an SBS double-decker on Selegie Road on Tuesday morning.

    The injured passengers were sent to Singapore General Hospital, Tan Tock Seng Hospital and Alexandra Hospital.

    Eyewitnesses said the accident happened at a bus stop in front of a 7-11 store and next to the Fragrance Hotel at about 10am.

    Mr Supaya Michael, 46, who was standing outside the convenience store, said he heard a loud crash, followed by shattering of glass.

    He said the SBS bus had to swerve out of the bus lane to drop two passengers as the bus bay was blocked by two waiting taxis.

    As it was moving out, it was hit by an oncoming Night Safari bus carrying more than 20 passengers. The SBS bus - Service No. 56 - then mounted a kerb, slammed into a tree and smashed the railing. It also brought down a signpost.
