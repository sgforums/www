    Daughters request for SBS to pay for transportation of body

    The daughters of a 66-year-old woman who died after being hit by a SBS bus in Sengkang on Tuesday have requested for SBS Transit to pay the bill for transporting her body to Malaysia for the funeral.

    However, SBS Transit has asked Kenix Lau, 33, the younger daughter of the deceased Chan Ah Yong, to submit a proposal to their management. The company could not be contacted on Wednesday for an update on the family's request, according to the Straits Times.

    Chan’s funeral will take place in Seremban in Malaysia, where her husband and two sons live, ST reported.

    Chan, a Malaysian on a long-term visit pass here, had stayed at her daughter’s flat in Block 232 flat for nearly three years.

    On Tuesday morning, Chan was involved in a fatal accident while crossing the road at the junction of Sengkang East Way and Compassvale Road.

    She was just 350m away from her daughter’s flat in Block 232 when she was hit by a SBS bus 159, which was making a right turn at the junction.

    The elderly woman was dragged under the bus for about half a minute before the bus driver stopped the bus after being alerted by other motorists. She was pronounced dead at the scene.

    A 14-second Youtube video, taken from a car in the area of the accident,  had garnered over 300 views as of Wednesday morning. It has since been removed from the site.

    A resident from a HDB estate nearby said that after the accident, the bus driver was seen on the phone and shouted at the passengers to leave the bus.

    The bus driver involved in the accident has been suspended from work and is assisting the police with investigations, ST reported.
