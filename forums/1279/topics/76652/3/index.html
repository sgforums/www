    Nicoll Highway collapse: SBS buses continue to be diverted

    SINGAPORE: Five SBS Transit bus services - number 10, 14, 16, 70 and 196 - will be diverted because of road closure along Nicoll Highway.

    Buses will continue to be diverted along Mountbatten Road, Geylang Road, Kallang Road, Jalan Sultan, Beach Road and Stamford Road until further notice.

    Service 401 which operates on weekends and public holidays will also be diverted along the same amended routes starting this Saturday.

    On the return direction, bus services will be diverted to ply Bras Basah Road, Beach Road, Jalan Sultan, Sims Avenue, Geylang Lor 1 and Mountbatten Road.

    Bus services 11, 16 and 401 travelling within the National Stadium area will also be detoured.

    For all diverted services, there will be no change in fares from the longer road diversion. - CNA

    Any history about Nicol Highway? I remembered that it was built in 1956 and also known as Merdeka Bridge then with a lion statue.
    NICOLL Highway, which stretches from Suntec City to the National Stadium, was officially opened on Aug 17, 1956.

    It was a celebrated achievement on the island as this was a highway that ran mostly over reclaimed land.

    Costing S$9.5 million at the time, the highway included a span that ran over the Kallang River. It was then the longest pre-stressed concrete bridge in Southeast Asia.

    The highway was named after former Singapore Governor John Fearns Nicoll, and designed by Mr RJ Hollid-Bee.

    SBS Transit Press Release

    Update On Temporary Bus Services Diversion Due To The Closure Of Nicoll Highway

    Based on the information gathered from the authorities, it is expected that the affected stretch of road along Nicoll Highway will be closed for an extended period for reconstruction.

    As a result of this road closure, five SBS Transit bus services originally plying along Nicoll Highway and in the vicinity of affected area namely, Services 10, 14, 16, 70 and 196, will continue to be diverted to ply along amended routes that were earlier announced until further notice.

    In addition to these five SBS Transit Services, Service 401, which operates on weekends and Public Holidays, will also, starting this Saturday, be diverted to ply along the same diverted route with the five services, namely Mountbatten Road, Geylang Road, Kallang Road, Jalan Sultan, Beach Road and Stamford Road before resuming the original route towards the city. In the return direction, the service will be diverted to ply Bras Basah Road, Beach Road, Jalan Sultan, Sims Avenue, Geylang Lor 1 and Mountbatten Road.

    For routing within National Stadium area used by Services 11, 16 and 401, there will be slight detour to the routing although all existing bus stops will continue to be served and will call in the same sequence.

    For all diverted services, there will be no change in fares arising from the longer road diversion.

    Bus Captains of affected services are being reminded of these changes. During this period, we have deployed Traffic Inspectors and patrol cars at the diverted road junctions to assist the Bus Captains. Notices have been put up at bus interchanges and at bus stops along the affected roads. Similar notices are also displayed on the bus windscreen of the above services to inform commuters. Commuters travelling to and from the affected road can board and alight at the bus stops along the diverted routes.
    During this period of diversion, commuters may experience some service irregularities as we expect higher traffic volumes along the diverted routes as well as changes in the travelling pattern of our commuters. To cater to these changes, nine more buses have been added to the affected services to improve the headway and capacity. We wish to seek the understanding from commuters for any inconvenience caused.

    Source: http://www.sbstransit.com.sg/whatsnew_21april2004.asp
