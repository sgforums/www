    MTRC launches safety campaign

    12-02-2010
     
    The Mass Transit Railway Corporation has launched a campaign aimed at preventing accidents at station platform gaps. This came as the corporation revealed that there were 195 accidents last year - down four percent from the figure for the previous year. Many involved children and the elderly getting their feet caught in the gap. A psychologist from the Chinese University of Hong Kong, Winton Au, who was commissioned by the Corporation to find out why the accidents were happening, said that misjudging one's footing was the most common cause.

    Buses collide in Shing Mun Tunnel

    17-02-2010
     
    Twenty-five people have been injured - six of them seriously - in a collision between two double decker buses inside the Shing Mun Tunnel. Both the buses were travelling westbound.

    Transport officials say that according to initial investigations one bus rammed into the back of the other as it braked suddenly to avoid hitting a car.

    The injured were taken to Yan Chai and Princess Margaret hospitals.

    The accident forced the closure of the Tsuen Wan-bound tube of the tunnel for hours.

    Both double-deckers belong to Kowloon Motor Bus. Acting deputy commissioner for transport, Albert Yuen, has ordered the company to submit a report on the accident.

    The accident is the second in two days involving public buses. On Monday, 13 people were injured in a crash involving two buses.

    MTR fares to rise 2%

    26-03-2010
     
    The Mass Transit Railway Corporation has announced its first fare rise for 13 years. Travellers will have to pay an extra 2.05 percent from June. The increase was based on a complex adjustment system . Last year, the mechanism showed that fares should rise by 0.7 percent.     Meanwhile, the Transport Department is handling applications for fare increases from the operators of 38 franchised minibus routes. Most are in Kowloon. In the past two months, the government has endorsed rises of up to 60 cents on 11 minibus routes.
