    Still to early to tell.. Should give it a month or 2 before we can even closely estimate how the ridership is like.

    1. Some people don't even know these services exist even if it's in their areas.. These are usually the people who are so comfortable with routine and are skeptical with change.

    2. Secondly, some people are taking trips on these buses for novelty's sake. You know.. trying out new things.. normal human curiousity...

    3. People who aren't sure how to connect to these buses. What I mean is.. some people need more than 1 bus to get to their destinations.. Currently they're taking one bus, changing to another.. maybe a few transfers, before they get to their place. These people aren't sure if they can take a better route by taking some other bus and then transfering to any of these new buses (or the other way round) and make their trip shorter and / or cheaper.

    4. And lastly, people who're already making use of these new routes, but might decide to revert to their old routines after they find that it's not better or even worse.

    Just my 2 cents.
