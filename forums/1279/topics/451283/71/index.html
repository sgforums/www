    Originally posted by SBS3004X:

    Why is HGDEP in West District when it is so far from the west of Singapore?

    Basically before 2004, there were 3 districts, East, Central and West. East comprised of hgdep (district HQ) and bndep, Central comprised of amdep (district HQ) and bram, West comprised of bbdep (district HQ), jrdep and jrbp, of which jrdep and jrbp shifted into the then new depot at Soon Lee (and the district HQ shifted to slbp). After NEL opened, a lot of routes from hgdep were cut and the then East district became significantly smaller in terms of routes controlled.

    They then decided to reorganize the company and that saw the old East district spilt and closed as a cost centre. To even out the responsibility, hgdep went to West district, and bndep went to Central district, which was renamed to East district. By the end of the whole thing, Serangoon Int went from amdep to hgdep control, Eunos went from hgdep to bram control and Bishan went from bram to amdep control. And the remaining services under hgdep control at Bedok Int (7, 9, 66, 197) went to bndep (87 was not affected as it was considered a Sengkang service), and 39 switched from bndep to amdep and back.
