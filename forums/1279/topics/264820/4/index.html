    Originally posted by sbst275:
    SMRT's biz is very attractive to SBS sia
    Very true.

    The obvious case is when SMRT cutback their bus routes. Just count the number of buses they withdraw from service or those that suffer route cutback over the past few years.

    The other not so obvious case is when SMRT cutbacks their last bus operational hours. Ever noticed their trunk buses end earlier and earlier? Instead of ending at 0000 or 2345, It was brought forward to end at 2300 or 2330 now. As such, people who needs to travel late at night naturally transfer to alternative SBS buses which end about half an hour to an hour later.

    As such, SMRT is literally handing business to SBS.

    Whereas SBS is expanding its market share by introducing more bus services, SMRT seems to be scaling back.
