    I don't understand the negativity associated with the B5RH Hybrid.  There are certain constraints that had to be taken into consideration in its conception.  First of all, it is not possible to use a regular belt-driven air-conditioining system with a hybrid bus, for the simple reason that the engine belts would not be moving when the engine is switched off at idle.  Additionally, a manual gearbox (or in this case, an automated manual) is required because of the characteristics of the iSAM system, i.e. the diesel engine requiring to be 'push started' during the first gearchange after a 'battery powered' standing start in order for it to resume normal operation.

    Sure, there are certain spects that I don't like, for example the high floor height at the rear of the bus, but that is something that probably isn't changeable given European preferences for integral buses (as opposed to an OEM-supplied chassis with third-party bodywork) these days.
