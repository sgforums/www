    Gear slipping I believe is when the gears somehow make the engine run and change gear at higher RPM, akin to a Formula 1 car, just that one is a technical feat while the other is a technical fault!

    W3E, if I am not wrong, have a lock up gear as well, but would always occur on the first gear. A lock up is something like retaining the gear step, bringing the RPM close to its highest power output (torque on the lower end, horsepower on the higher end).ZF gearboxes have lock up on first or second gear. So far ZF and Mercedes gearboxes have the most notable lock up sounds. Whereas for what a lock up in gear can achieve, I am not sure. Some says its for retaining power (which I supported), others is for saving fuel.

    One of the few champions for gear slipping and recommended is TIB516L, not repaired since its gearbox failure in 2005.

    Gear slipping can lead to the increase wear of the gearbox components and higher fuel consumption.
