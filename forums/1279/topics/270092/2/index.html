  • SBS3625D
    28 Jul 07, 17:29
    If you asked me in the past, I would have said........0405! Now I would say......

    0405
    Chiong ar! I love it's quick stop-to-moving lag-time. Roars off from start! Not so good on expressway. But the W3E gearbox on the 0405 is simply shiok, especially if it jerks!

    B10M MKII
    Neat pick-up speed, especially WA NAC off-loaded MKIIs. I haven't seen any bus pick up faster than 1 empty 74 MKII WA yet. The bus simply shrieked it's way to 65 in just 5 seconds flat! Down-side, it's ageing, ie less appealing.

    B10M MKIII
    I'm partial towards vVoith so the MKIII is a great bus to me! Make no mistake, the MKIII can match the MKII in pick-up speed, and can definitely beat the MKII in top-speed, if the BC is willing. I've seen numerous cases of the MKIII racing the MKII although the result is always a draw. The initial pick-up is always led by the MKII, but the pick-up of the MKII tends to plateau from 3rd gear and that's where the steady Voith of the MKIII allows it to catch up. Another ageing bus though, so it's not that appealing now, although it produces less screw-loose noises than the other B10Ms (exterior). Lookswise, like the MKII, simple. So it's not bad.

    B10M MKIV
    I always use the MKIVs as benchmark for performance, and all I can say is.......if, the ZF MKIV drags it's 1st gear, causing the lock-up to engage in 1st instead, the MKIV is easily the fastest in pick-up for B10M. Otherwise, it's pretty much the same as the other B10M. Ok pick-up, ok top-speed. Their performance is pretty boring because it isn't "slow", nor is it "too fast".

    Conclusion: that's my answer lor!
