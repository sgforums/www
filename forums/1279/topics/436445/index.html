And its body is pretty sturdy, the suspension is still the same (brace position required over humps!).........

    I think each bus has its own definitive advantages, and the MKIII is a good example of quiet reliability.

    All the Volvo B10Ms. I love them all. If only the fact that SBST can't remove the seats.

    And the K230UBs aren't that bad also. I like the seats, the good aircon, its generous legroom and it can be pretty zippy too if the BC wishes. Truth is, if the bus jerks excessively, vibrates excessively....half of it can be attributed to the BC. As my favourite BCs demonstrate, the K230UB hardly jerks when you:

    lift your foot off the brakes gradually,

    apply power when starting up gradually to prevent jerky lock-up,

    apply more pressure as the bus moves to 3rd gear to reduce unnecessary vibration at low rpm,

    brake with consistent force so that the gearbox can shift smoothly downwards.

    When you look at the list, aren't these the standard things you were taught before you got your driving license?

    2 KUB rides ago, I encountered a terrible E4 KUB with an equally terrible BC who jerked the bus, kicked-down whenever he could (even on small roads), stomp on the brakes immediately after hard accelerations. Small wonder the bus was making all kinds of sound.

    But of cos, the argument could also be that the MKIII suffered worse treatment but it hasn't taken to all these problems. The MKIII is probably the last SD to still see BCs opening the door before the bus stops.
