    184: Bt Merah - Withdrawn. Replaced by a new 198.
    188: Jurong West St 91 - Replaced by Service 99
    197: Geylang Lor 1 - Extended to Bedok Interchange

    199: Nanyang Technological Institute - Service became 2 services, 179 and 199 respectively.

    Minor route change for 30 via Teban Gdns Rd in the direction of Boon Lay.
    98 was made to loop at Jalan Ahmad Ibrahim (like 98A). Later integrated with 247 to form current route.
    165 was amended to Clementi
    184 was merged with 176 to form present 176 (Bt Panjang - Bt Merah)
    188 still terminate at Jurong Bus Park, which was next to the demolished Jurong Interchange. Withdrawn in 1991.
    198 was amended to Jurong East in 1991. In 1993, it was amended to its present route Boon Lay - Bt Merah. On the same day, 197 was amended to Jurong East.
    179 was an entirely new service to cover the loss of 199 when the latter was amended to NIE.
    When 206 was withdrawn, 172 was amended to Boon Lay while a new service 175 was introduced.

    Mid to late 90s

    901, now 911.
    902, now 912.
    904,905, 906.
    These 3 svcs were withdrawn due to low demand. 2 of them merged to form 913.
    Also 900. Withdrawn due to same reason. But unlike those 3 which lasted only a few months, 900 was actually quite old. 900, 901, 902, 903, used to use the NAC Hinos, then they switched to MB0405s, those were the days when Woodlands was still quite ulu, hence the MB0405s often sped and your hair could have blown away on the NAC MB0405s! 904, 905, 906 used NAC MB0405s throughout their short lives.

    911 was partially aircon, and it brings back good memories of myself on the occasional DAFs on 911. And then it was the cameo MB0405Gs that provided the excitment.
