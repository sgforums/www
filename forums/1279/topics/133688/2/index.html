  • hseochin
    6 Jun 05, 04:53
    Air is released from usually the front air-bellows of the air-suspension so that the bus's boarding/alighting height is reduced, to make it easier for people to go up/down the bus. I'll say such feature mainly makes sense on low floor citybuses only with zero or few shallow steps.

    Volvo B10M i don't consider it an ideal citybus due to its higher step/boarding height, higher floor level & no low profile tyres used. Furthermore, with the engine placed so near to front axle, if there's any kneeling @ the front entrance & there's a hump on the road underneath, then i think the resulting ground clearance can be too little & the engine may be hit. If the engine's right behind which tends to have higher clearance from the road (as in the case of purpose built citybuses which i don't consider the B10M to be) then perhaps kneeling may be used.

    Therefore i'm not surprised if there's no kneeling function as standard on Volvo B10M.
