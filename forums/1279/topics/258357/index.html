    Normal paper won't work. Its too flimsy. You need something more rigid, like a drawing block, for example. To make your model colour last, and to make it more easy to clean, paste a sheet of plastic over your printout. There is a kind of plastic which is sticky on one side and you can cut it out to size. The plastic will also give more strength to your paper model. However, be careful not to create 'bubbles'. After that, cut out the various parts, fold and paste them together. It takes lots of practice and patience to get the lines to match accordingly. Failing which, your bus may be lopsided or crooked.

    I used to have several paper buses in my collection. Some people in here have seen them. A few were crushed by my young cousins while the rest eventually became encrusted with dust. I have no choice but to 'retire' them. However, I still keep the templates in my PC. Perhaps one day, I will find the time to build again.
