    Opening of Bishan MRT Station and Integrated bus services

    Bishan Services Then: 56, 57, 58 and 59

    Originally Bishan Interchange had service 56, 57, 58, 59.

    Service 56 plays between Bishan and Toa Payoh (Loop),
    Service 57 was like the present-day service 410 except it looped at Sector a Sin Ming.
    Service 58 plays between Bishan and Marina Center except it went by Bendemeer, Lavender and Victoria Street.
    Service 59 plays between Bishan and Jalan Kayu.

    In late 80's or early '90s, service 58 and 56 were integrated to form service 56 playing between Bishan and Marina Center, with the original service 56 route intact, and service 58 route was withdrawn.

    Service 57 was converted into Townlink service 410 when Bishan St 22 from Marymount Rd to Bishan Rd opened in 1992, and had green/white plate service. In 1995, service 410 was downgraded to a feeder service. Service 57 was re-introduced on 31st July 2005 plying between Bishan and Bukit Merah.

    Service 59 was cut short to Ang Mo Kio in early 90's. Eventually TIBS took over all the services in Jalan Kayu and service 59 was extended to Sengkang in 1997 and renumbered it as service 866. In July 1999, SBS took over service 866 and it became today's service 86 in 2000, solely operated by Hougang Depot.

    Service 55 originally terminated at Ang Mo Kio Terminal but was extended to Bishan via Ang Mo Kio Ave 10 in 1994.

    Service 134 was cut short to Bishan from Ang Mo Kio in 1992 due to poor demand. Service 105 was rerouted with service 104 to swap roads, with service 104 serving Bishan MRT and service 105 going by Lor Chuan. In 1993 however, SBS decided to integrate service 104 and service 134 to form today's service 54, which also goes to New Bridge Rd. Service 156 was then introduced to bridge the gap from Seranggon Interchange to Bishan MRT. In 2000, service 156 was extended to Sengkang.

    Bishan Services Now: 53, 54, 55, 56, 57, 58 and Feeder 410
