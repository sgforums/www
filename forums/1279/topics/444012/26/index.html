    I think the sluggishness has more to do with the gear ratios on the new Voith Wrights compared to those CDGE B9s that have Voith gearboxes. The newer Voith gearbox on the Wrights just doesn't seem to allow the turbo in the bus to spool up enough to propel the bus forward before cutting off, the revs are being cut off earlier than what I see in the CDGE Voith B9s.

    You can tell that the difference in the turbo boost and revs just by listening to them acclerating out. I mean yes, the official engine specs from Volvo will say the B9TL engine will rev to a certain RPM before cutting out, but the Voith gearbox seems to cut off the revs in each gear much earlier than the ZF Wrights or the Voith CDGEs.
