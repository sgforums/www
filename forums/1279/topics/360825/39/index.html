    Waiting for bus at Woodlands Checkpoint? Imagine being in an oven

    http://singaporeseen.stomp.com.sg/stomp/sgseen/what_bugs_me/263446/waiting_for_bus_at_woodlands_checkpoint.html

    STOMPer Traveller was standing at the bus queue at Woodlands Checkpoint and noticed that there were no fans around for the comfort of commuters.

    He says:

    "These pictures were taken near the commuters' waiting area for SBS Transit service 170 at the Woodlands Checkpoint.

    "There is no fan provided near the waiting area and waiting for the bus can tax your patience to the limit.

    "These makeshift metal barriers were tied together with red and green ropes and they looked very messy and untidy.

    "On weekends when there is a big crowd it makes sense to have a 50m long barrier to ensure that passengers queue up properly while waiting for the bus. However, on week days especially during the non-peak period, it makes no sense to make passengers walk the 50m zigzag line to board the bus.

     

    "I saw some young men climbing and jumping over the metal railings. When there is no crowd there should be a short cut through the long barrier to ensure faster boarding.

     

    "I asked the bus inspector why passengers had to walk 50m to board the bus when there was no crowd and he told me that the barriers were put up by ICA and SBS Transit has no control over such matters.

    "One sultry day I saw a baby crying non-stop and the mother was trying hard to pacify the wailing baby.

     

    "Ceiling or wall fans should be provided to ease the agony of commuters."

    Mother ignores rules and warnings while feeding toddler in bus

    http://singaporeseen.stomp.com.sg/stomp/sgseen/ugly_commuters/265304/mother.html

    This mother was spotted feeding her toddler in the bus despite the no-eating or drinking rule. STOMPer Lookout, who also warned her about the bumpy bus ride, was disappointed by her actions.

    Aside from the no-eating rule, the sender was also worried that the bumpy ride might cause the child to vomit or feel uncomfortable while eating.

    In an email dated October 11, STOMPer Lookout says:

    "I was on bus 196 (sitting at the far back of the bus) going to Parkway and just a few stops before reaching my destination, I noticed a lady with a child, probably about three years old board the bus.

    "They sat at the back of the bus where I was sitting.

    "As soon as the bus started moving, I saw the mother taking out a plastic bag and opening a container. I did not notice what she was doing until I saw her feeding her child.

    "To me, she should not be doing this as firstly, you are not allowed to eat on the bus and secondly, feeding the child on the bus isn't the right thing to do as the bus is quite bumpy at times.

    "The child might not feel comfortable and he might feel giddy and vomit.

    "So I actually politely told the mother, if possible, to try and feed the child when they have alighted at their destinations but they just ignored me.

    "I could not do much as I had to alight from the bus."
