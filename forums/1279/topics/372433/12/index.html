    In the early days, there was a bus company called Tay Koh yat. Tay Koh yat started to expand beyong providing bus services to the Sembawang British naval base areas and opened up a new bus terminus @ Bartley Rd and bradell Rd Junction.

    and so came the introduction of Service 10 and Service 10A by tay Koh yat.

    Service 10 ran roughly like this, bartley rd, braddell rd, lornie rd, adam rd, farrer rd, holland rd, hollabnd V, nth bouna vista rd, and terminate at the junction of pasir panajng rd and south bouna vista rd.

    Service 10A ran like this: bartley rd, braddell rd, lornie rd, adam rd, farrer rd, queesnway, and loop at queesway circus. (at the jucntion of Queensway and alexandra rd)

    i think u cna see that tay Koh yat service 10A eventually got retained during the great bus numbering + great bus company reogrnaisation and merger era.

    this service was assigned service number 93. And by then the international airport was at paya leabr and the bus was called 93 because all airport bus services started with a digit 9 (except 94 whcih was funny and terminalted at Kolem Ayer est)

    How svc 980 came about.

    I think by now u know i really liked this Tay Koh yat Bus company,

    Thsi concerned tay koh yat very first services. the british naval base was opened in 1933 in the Sembawang area as a precaution measure to WW2.

    as a result new road was built called sembawang rd connecting to the then existing cross island going nroth rd called upp thomson rd. a Bus service provider was needed. tay Koph yat say "me me me me"

    tay Koh yat gave services 1 was a feeder service. which i not care.

    the base was so big, many areas of exit/entry are defined.

    In town a bus depot was set up along Beach rd beside the sea 9chamring). the bus depot further down was a bus terminus called Hoi haw Rd Bus terminus. in later years it is called Crawfurd St.

    TKY Svcs2 ,  3 and 5 were the service that ran along SBW rd, Upp Thomon rd, thomson rd, bt timah rd and down to beach rd.

    3 and 5 are belvied to serve from the West end of the base, that leaves svc 2 presumbly serving from the eastern side, running along inside the base, 9today the road which ran is similair to Admialryt rd).

    so we concentrate on service 2.

    later service 2 and many other tay koh yat services were invovled in the great 1969s numbering excercise and bus company merger thing.

    tay koh yat became part of UBC.

    and UBC svc 160 was born doing TKY svc 2.

    musch later UBC 160 became SBS 160. as part fo the plan to unify the all bus companys to one entity came to be realsied in 1971)

    SBS 160 went on to eb trasnferred to a new entity bus company called TIBS.

     

    it became TIb160 in 1983.

    much later SMRTbus came about and the then 160 was already became svc 980 as a renumebr excercise.

    Originally posted by Rk 2010:

    How did the current service 151 route came about?


    Introduced in 1993. Route was between Hougang South interchange and Kent Ridge terminal.

    There were 3 amendments to the route:

    1) Plied Defu Ave 1 (duplicated former service 323 between Hougang South interchange and Defu Ave 1) before amended to ply Hougang Ave 3 between Tampines Rd and Defu Ave 1.

    2) Amended to ply Kent Ridge Crescent to serve NUS and skipped Clementi Rd between the 2 junctions of Kent Ridge Crescent.

    3) Extended to Hougang Central interchange in Feb 2004 when Hougang South interchange closed.

     

    Original route:

    Hougang South interchange, (Hougang St 21), (Hougang Ave 1), Tampines Rd, Defu Ave 1, Hougang Ave 3, Airport Rd, MacPherson Rd, (Bendemeer Rd), (Whampoa North), Serangoon Rd, Jalan Toa Payoh, Whitley Rd, Bukit Timah Rd, Clementi Rd, Kent Ridge terminal.
