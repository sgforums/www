    THE HISTORY OF BEDOK INTERCHANGE & ITS SERVICES

    Bedok Intercahnge, commenced its operations on 25th February 1979, marks the another milestone of its new bus interchange serving the new Bedok New Town. It was generally the first bus interchange to have a colour coding berth (Red/Yellow/Green), which makes boarding easier for passengers and to identify the correct berth to board a particluar service from Bedok Interchange. Sooner, the trend for the new colour coding berth were introduced at later stages, i.e. Tampines & Jurong East

    It used to have 5 berths at a start, with 1 berth reserved for Alighting, there wasn't any end-on berths (like the current Interchange layout). However, in the later years, the Bedok Interchange was expanded with the end-on berths to accomodate the existing Chai Chee Terminal services, on which the terminal was acquired by the HDB for housing developments. (Please refer to the Shifting of Chai Chee Terminal services to Bedok Interchange mentioned at the previous pages)

    ITS CHANGES TOOK PLACE THROUGHOUT THE 30 YEARS OF OPERATIONS OF BEDOK INTERCHANGE
    3 - Was converted from semi-express to trunk service in 1985 and later on amended to Jurong East. Within the few years, it was one of the few service that had gotten the Air Con status. [service withdrawn due to TrainsitLink network integration exercise]
    4 - Was extended to St Michael's Terminal in the 1980s, but was integrated with sv 19 and extended to pasir Ris.
    6 - Was withdrawn in 1983 and integrated with service 3.
    7 - Was extended to Jurong East Terminal in 1984, but was cutback to Clementi with the extension of the MRT Line to Jurong East
    8 - Was amended to serve ECP as an express sector and was later amended to loop at Marina Centre before continuing its trip via ECP. [service withdrawn]
    9 - Was cutback from Lor 1 Geylang to Bedok Interchange.
    13 - Was amended to Bedok Interchange when Chai Chee Terminal closed. It was re-routed and its termination points was Yio Chu Kang & Eunos
    14 - Was cutback from Changi Village to Bedok Interchange, and integrated with Service 92.
    16 - Was amended to Bedok Interchange on 19 Aug 2007, previously loops at Marine parade Rd
    17 - Introduced to replace 227. Bedok - Pasir Ris
    18 - Introduced as Bedok to Tampines St 22 (Loop). When Tampines Int opened in Nov 1987, service was amended to Tampines Int.
    19 - Introduced as Bedok - Pasir Ris service. It was merged with Svc 4 and withdrawn in 1989.
    20 - Extended to Bedok Int (this service did not start from Chai Chee anymore, when Bedok Int opens) It was withdrawn due to the MRT Rationalisation
    25 - Slight amendment in the 1980s. It was amended to Ang Mo Kio Ave 2/3 , Eunos Link & Jln Eunos.
    26 - Introduced as Bedok to Bendemeer Rd. Extended to Toa Payoh Int in the 1980s.
    28 - Introduced as Bedok to Tampines in 1995. It was merged with 152 and extended to Toa Payoh
    30 - Amended to Boon Lay Int on 1 Jul 1990, and amended to ECP & Fort Rd in 1992.
    30e - New FFw service from 19 Sep 2005
    31 - Extended to Tampines Int
    32 - Extended to Buona Vista
    33 - Service introduced as Bedok to Kent Ridge with a special peak period (Alternate buses will go to Geylnag Rd (Kent Ridge direction) and Still Rd (Bedok direction)). The Special Peak Period of sv 33 was withdrawn in 1989.
    34 - Introduced as Bedok - Changi Airport Service. Withdrawn on 14 Sep 2002
    35 - Introduced as Bedok - TMFT service
    38 - Introduced as Bedok - Tampines service (covers former 220 and 290)
    40 - Introduced as Bedok - MacPherson service (loops at MacPherson Estate when the terminal closed)
    41 - Introduced as Bedok - Bukit Merah service (integrates with 197 and withdrawn)
    60 - Introduced as Bedok - Eunos Service (covers former 226 and 385). On 7 Nov 2004, it was enhanced to cover Bedok Reservoir Rd and introduced the White & Red plates.
    66 - Cutback to Bedok Int in 1991.
    69 - Introduced as Bedok to Tampines service in Sep 1995, with the opening of TP Tampines Campus.
    87 - Introduced in Sep 1999 as Bedok to Sengkang Service.
    152 - Merged with Service 28 and withdrawn
    158 - Introduced as Bedok - Toa Payoh Service. It was later amended to Serangoon instead.
    168 - Introduced as Bedok - Woodlands Service in Apr 2001. First SBS service to Woodlands after 17 years
    196 - Amended to Bedok Int, previously it was Marine Parade.
    197 - Extended to Bedok Int, previously it was Geylang Lor 1
    207 - Service introduced as Bedok - Upp Changi Rd in Nov 1980, it was later withdrawn.
    219 - feeder service from Bedok - Bedok South Rd. (Renumbered to 229)
    220 - Feeder servie from Bedok to Bedok South Ave 3 (Merged with Service 290, to form 38)
    221G/W - Feeder servie from Bedok to Bedok North Ave 3 (Merged with 221 and 229 to form 222)
    222 - Townlink Service merged with 221 and 229
    224 - Feeder service from Bedok - Chai Chee Rd circa 1992 (service was withdrawn after 1 year)
    226 - Feeder service from Bedok - Bedok Reservoir Rd. Merged with 385 to form 60, thus 226 was withdrawn.
    227 - Feeder service from Bedok North Depot to Bedok Int (Loop); but its looping was anended as Bedok - Bedok North Depot (Loop)
    228 - Feeder service from Bedok - Bedok Reservoir Rd
    229 - Fedder service from Bedok - Chai Chee Dr (merged with 221 to form 222 and withdrawn)
    229 -  Feeder service from Bedok - Bedok South Rd
    401 - Special service amended to Bedok Int with the clousre of Chai Chee Ter. In 2003, extended to Orchard Rd and a year later, loops at Fort Rd, returning to its present route
    402 - Special service from Bedok Int to SAFRA Resort, on trail basis. Service was withdrawn.
    506 - Express service shortened to Bedok, but on 12 Nov 2006, it was amended to Upp East Coast Ter.
    854 - TIBS Service from Yishun - Bedok in Jan 1987.
    CSS 8 (608) - CSS Service from Bedok - Bukit Merah, last CSS service till its demise on 28 Apr 2007

    WHY 219 WAS RENUMBERED AS 229? A FAQ.
    With the withdrawal of the old 229, the service 219 from Bedok to Bedok South Rd becomes 229, to complement the 22x series of the Bedok feeders, as the usage in the modern era of the 21x looks odd.
