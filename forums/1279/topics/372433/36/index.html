    Some things you might not know...
    - SBS 56 was the first Bishan service from Toa Payoh and loops at Bishan St 11. (It was introduced on 22nd Dec 1985, not 1986)
    - SBS 57 did terminate at Buona Vista Ter for a very short while before its withdrawal on 19th April 1987. (not 1988)
    - SBS 210 & 218 were both withdrawn on 26th April 1987 as the service was under-utilised. That was when SBS 29 was made to serve Netheravon Rd.
    - SBS 410 was introduced on 3rd May 1992, as part of the TransitLink's integration effort that provide links from Bishan to Sin Ming. (Not June 1992)
    - TIBS 853 do not start off with an alternate route when it was introduced on 20th January 1987, just 2 days after the introduction of TIBS 854. It was then the alternate service for Suns & PHs was introduced later on 21st June 1987. (Not started off as dual service)
    - TIBS 857 was introduced when both TIBS 161 & 850 were withdrawn on 20th Sep 1992. (It was actually NOT introduced in 1993)

    Originally posted by ^tamago^:

    you know the reason behind the sunday variants right? :)


    Generally some points about the Sunday & PHs route:

    Take Service 181 and 850 for example, many residents from Woodlands & Yishun do not work on Sundays & PHs - they might take a day off to do shopping at Marina Square as one reason, the basic reason behind the Sunday & PHs schedule is to provide a direct linkage from the 2 HDB towns to Marina Centre without having to transfer extra bus services.

    As for Service 853, it is exactly the same reason, just like 181 and 850. For 853, this service will serve Marine Parade via Mountbatten Rd, Amber Rd, Marine Prade Rd, Siglap Rd.  Although for some who wants to go to East Coast Park, they would take another bus 401 at the vicinity of Marine Parade which goes to East Coast park or walk from Marine Parade through the underpass to East Coas tPark.

    Suggestions to request for more bus services is not the cause of the introduction but it was introduced accordingly to provide the necessary satisfaction and the needs of the residents.
