    Trivia

    A 16 is a service that plies a road with a height restriction.

    B 111 is a service that makes a loop in the city.

    B 111 adopts a jump bus system and all of A 16 and B's 111 buses originate from one similar depot/bus park.

    A, 16 B, 111 C, D and E along with some of the other alphabets will all ply this road, but C and D do not call at the same bus stop at all along this road.

    C and D will meet again at a cross junction but when C and D meets, they will turn right from opposite directions.

    D is a loop service that does not share ending point with anyone (A-M) but M will pass D's ending point 

    E will cross intercept C at 2 road junctions before meeting at the road where A-E meet. E is also known to meet C at the opposite direction of E after the 2 cross-interceptions.

    E also plies a highway and expressway.

    F will meet with B 111, J and K altogether at this road for only 1 stop.

    F, G and H will enter this road at the same time and ply all the bus stop on this road before leaving this road at the same time.

    F is known not to meet any other service (A-M) thereafter.

    G and H share one ending point that all the other services (A-M) do not.

    H meets many services (A-M).

    I is a long loop service known to intercept J for 4 stops at the first 10 minutes of its journey.

    I and M is known to intercept H on both directions but H will only call together with I and M on 1 stop on 1 direction and none for the other.

    J will meet A 16 when J turns right onto this road. A 16 and J will then turn right together at the next junction and separates when J turns left out of the next road while A 16 continues going straight. A 16 and J do not call at any bus stop together.

    J and K's service numbers have 2 identical digits, one of which is the last one. example, sv143 and sv543.

    L meets all services except E.

    L is known to intercept D for at least 6 stops before splitting and meeting again for at least 6 stops.

    H, B and L are the only SBST Trunk known to ply this road along with M.

    M shares one ending point with L and the other with A 16 and C.

    M will intercept with I for many stops at one point of time and 1 or 2 stops at the next time they meet.

    A to K are 11 basic services operated by either SBST or SMRT. A-G have DD while H-K do not.

    A has B9TLs. A goes to only one train station and goes by an industrial area. In additon A meets B and H at a road but B and H do not call at the same stop together.

    B has SLBP control. B has only one perm batch 1 KUB and several batch 2s.

    C ends at the same int/ter as B in town T1. C has the longest routing in town T2 which is the other end.

    D uses the same int/ter as A. There are two more A/D meetings, both near MRT tracks, however the two svcs do not call at the same stop except the int/ter. D also meets E 185 at one of the two meetings.

    E 185 goes from a depot to a terminal.

    F is a square number. F shares an int/ter with D. F will meet D again near M1 Station and duplicate until M2 station. They then split up and do not meet again.

    G is a prime number. It meets F for two stops in either direction in town T3. They also meet again in the central area of singapore.

    H goes between two terminals. From one end, after two left turns, H meets F. H uses an expressway and goes within walking distance to F's route from the other end of H, which is also served by E 185.

    I 82 used to go to the city area but was shortened. Currently I 82 makes a u turn and does not serve any NSL or EWL stations. I 82 meets D at a junction.
