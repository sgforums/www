    Customers who blow a chicken rice seller away
    Letter from Joan Teng

    I agree with Ms Yvonne Goh in her letter, "The tango of service" (Aug 2). To receive good service is to be a nice customer first: How simple is that?

    I have been working every Saturday and Sunday at my brother's chicken rice stall for three years. The customer experience has been shocking.

    Some arrogant customers throw money at us and make us run after it as the wind blows the notes away. Some get angry and spew vulgar words when we tell them to join the queue.

    Once, a customer yelled over his $2 rice dish: "I am giving you business, it is only right that I demand that you treat me like a king!"

    This perennial discussion about service is actually not about service; it is truly a reflection of how we Singaporeans treat each other every day.

    Some people lack respect for those we think are inferior or less educated than us.

    Also, I've found that some Singaporeans have preconceptions of those who work in the service or food industry Â— that they are not on par socially with those who require the service.

    I salute my bother, who is patient and still greets some nasty customers with a smile. His belief is that it will melt their heart and, some day, they will treat him more humanely.
