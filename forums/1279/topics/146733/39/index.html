    MPs from West Coast GRC ready for General Election: Lim Hng Kiang
    By Joanne Leow, Channel NewsAsia

    Trade and Industry Minister Lim Hng Kiang said Members of Parliament from West Coast GRC were ready for the General Election.

    Speaking at the launch of the West Coast-Ayer Rajah Town Renewal Exhibition, he said there had been a lot of work on the ground by the PAP since the last election.

    Mr Lim who is also an MP for the West Coast GRC said: "In West Coast we have not seen very many signs of the opposition activity. We of course will be very happy if they will come and contest in West Coast, because we have all the supporters and all the grassroots leaders and all the MPs who have worked very hard over the last 5 years."

    West Coast-Ayer Rajah is undergoing a major renewal exercise.

    Almost 50 precincts have been slated for or have completed some form of upgrading work and 9 sites have been selected for the Selective En-Bloc Redevelopment Scheme.

    The exhibition showcased upgrading works and new developments which are in the pipeline for the West Coast-Ayer Rajah area.

    Residents there can also look forward to more market and hawker centres being upgraded, improved school buildings and park connectors.

    And at the end of this year, there will also be a newly constructed sports complex in Jurong West with a covered pool and 3,000-seat stadium.

    One of the major new developments is the new Clementi Town Centre which is modelled on the one in Toa Payoh.

    It has a 40-storey building and an air-conditioned bus interchange and library.

    Mr Lim said: "For the next 5 years, it's better integrated with the national plan, so it's not just the upgrading plan within the town council, but also fitting in with the transportation plan of the MRT, with the parks, and with the social facilities."

    "Because many of the residents are getting older so they need nursing homes, they need healthcare facilities, they need to improve the mobility of the residents. So we are making changes to make sure the walkways are pedestrian friendly, elderly friendly."

    Those living here can also look forward to 5 Circle Line stations running through West Coast and Telok Blangah and 2 new stations on the MRT extension at Boon Lay. - CNA/ch
