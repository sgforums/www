  • Yusry
    27 Mar 04, 00:19
    Originally posted by delonchew:
    Just to clarify with you. I believe heavy vehicles all use leafsprings for their suspension system. Is this correct? As for MacPherson struts, they are more commonly found in cars or light vehicles and it is normally mounted on the front.
    Yes. Leafspring forms the basic suspension structure of most medium - heavy vehicles.

    In the case of buses and possibly other medium weight vehicles, the leafspring are supplanted by air bellows - which gives rise to "air suspension". This particular leafspring cum air bellow suspension is not as stiff as purely leafspring suspensions.

    I believe struts are still used for the steering axle.
