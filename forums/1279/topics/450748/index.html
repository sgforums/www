    Lui Tuck Yew: Two bus companies will stop operations if govt did not inject public funds to buy more buses for them

    The two bus companies will stop operations if the government did not inject public funds to help them buy more buses to meet growing demand, said Transport Minister Lui Tuck Yew, who is sounding more and more like a spokesperson for SMRT and SBS Transit with each passing day.

    Mr Lui was confronted with an angry resident during a community event in the constituency of Cheng San-Seletar who asked why the government chose to spend $1.1 billion dollars to purchase a fleet of 800 buses for the two bus companies when their services have not improved for the last few years.

    In response, Mr Lui replied:

    “If the government did not inject more funds, the operators will have to increase bus fares by as much as fifteen cents to cover the operating costs.”

    Mr Lui explained that the $1.1 billion dollars will be used to subsidize the commuters’ bus fares and not to ‘compensate’ the bus operators, adding that they will be forced to fold up and stop operations if the government did not do so.

    Both SBS Transit and SMRT are partly owned by Singapore’s giant sovereign wealth fund Temasek Holdings either directly or indirectly through its many subsidiaries.

    Temasek Times
