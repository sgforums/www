    Originally posted by SBS9828X:
    1800/min? isn that the revs wen the pedal isnt stepped on?
    Depending on the engine, idle speed can be anywhere from about 500 r/min to over 1000 r/min. Idle speeds can also fluctuate due to the constantly changing power demands from air-conditioning, compressors, etc. CNG and LPG engines (particularly ones that rely on venturi mixers for fuel metering) are particularly sensitive to abrupt changes in power demand and therefore have a tendancy to vary their idle speed considerably (i.e. revving up and down when stopped) - differences can be greater than 100 r/min and can be quite audibly noticeable.

    Today, bus engines, whether diesel or CNG-powered, run at their best between 1000 r/min and 1700 r/min ("Green Zone" - best fuel economy, peak torque, and maximum turbo boost are achieved somewhere during the above engine speeds). Rated speed (engine speed where peak power is achieved) is usually around 2000 r/min. Of course, all this above depends greatly on the engine manufacturer, engine displacement, etc.....
