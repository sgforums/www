    As I look back at the email replies, I found one which explains it all on why there is no direct bus route to any of the two neighbourhoods.

     

    "We seek your understanding that public transport evolved from a multitude of needs arising from the residents in area they served. Hence public transport are planned to meet most of these needs in an efficient and effective way while keeping travel time reasonable."

    "For example, Service 181 besides connecting residents in Jurong West Avenue 3 to the Boon Lay Interchange, also connect them to Gek Poh Shopping Centre in Jurong West Ave 5."

    "Similarly, Service 242 connects residents along Jalan Boon Lay to schools in Jurong West Street 72 and Jurong West Avenue 5."

    "Given that buses serve a multitude of needs, it may not be possible for all buses to take the shortest route to all the destinations since it is just one of many destinations that they are serving."

    I like this route because it provides the bus connection from the Boon Lay Bus Interchange to Jurong West Street 24, and links Jurong West Street 24 to Jurong West Avenue 4.

    However, this bus service only moves in one direction. People can only travel from Neighbourhood 2 to Neighbourhood 7, but not the other way round.

    I feel that the route should have a looping point while the rest of the route should be in bi-direction, for travel from Jurong West Avenue 4 to Jurong West Street 24. This would encourage some loading throughout the day at most sectors of the route.

    Otherwise, this bus service would only have some loading from Jurong West Avenue 4 (in the morning) and to Jurong West Street 24 (in the evening) during peak hours. During regular hours, the section to Jurong West Street 24 may have little loading, while the other sections may have only a handful or even no loading at all.

    Five years ago, when I joined the forum and was only active at the threads about Boon Lay ITH and PTC list of approved bus routes, I ever suggested that we should have bus services travelling from other areas to the Nanyang Technological University, to relieve the loading on bus service 179.

    Back then, there was the practice of the SBS Transit and the SMRT having separate areas of responsibility. I suggested having bus routes travelling between the Nanyang Technological University and Choa Chu Kang, but then I realised that it was not possible as both areas were under the responsibility of different operators.

    Today, bus operators operate in whichever areas they have bus service at, and bus routes can go anywhere. We can have bus routes between the Nanyang Technological University and Choa Chu Kang.

    I like your 973.

    Firstly, by connecting the Nanyang Technological University to Choa Chu Kang and Bukit Panjang, it provides an alternative to bus service 179 that only connects to Jurong West.

    Secondly, by using the expressway, it provides a faster bus connection than bus service 172, for travel between Jurong West and Choa Chu Kang - although it does not ply Jalan Boon Lay.

    Thirdly, this bus service provides a new bus connection between Bukit Panjang and Choa Chu Kang, and Joo Koon. If the travelling time from Bukit Panjang and Choa Chu Kang to Joo Koon is about the same or shorter than that of the fastest route (eg. via the MRT) currently available, this bus connection may be heavily utilised.

    Thanks to the excellent bus connections that it provides, this bus service may experience medium to mid-high usage throughout the day. It could have a fleet made up entirely of Double Decker buses, and still have at least 50% loading on each bus.

    However, during peak hours, there is traffic congestion at Pioneer Road North... Well, bus lanes could be the solution.
