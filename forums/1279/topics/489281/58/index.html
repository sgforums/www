    After yesterday's conversation with a few members here in SGforums, I thought about a possible merger of BSEP new services 49 and 258.

    I took out a pencil and some papers and made some sketches, with reference to a map. It was quite a bold move. As much as possible, I tried to optimise by having the fastest possible route - without compromising on the objectives of both bus services 49 and 258.

    The result is a nineteen kilometre route between Joo Koon and Jurong East. As it turns out, it is possible to merge bus services 49 and 258 without making the route too long to manage.

    The route from Joo Koon to Jurong East is as follows,

    1. Joo Koon Bus Interchange
    2. Joo Koon Circle
    3. Benoi Road
    4. Upper Jurong Road
    5. Pioneer Road North
    6. Jurong West Street 63
    7. Jurong West Street 61
    8. Jurong West Street 71
    9. Jurong West Avenue 5
    10. Jurong West Avenue 3
    11. Jurong West Street 22
    12. Jurong West Street 23
    13. Jurong West Avenue 2
    14. Corporation Road
    15. Jurong West Avenue 1
    16. Jurong West Street 41
    17. Jurong West Street 51
    18. Boon Lay Way
    19. Corporation Road
    20. Corporation Drive
    21. Tah Ching Road
    22. Yuan Ching Road
    23. Jalan Ahmad Ibrahim
    24. Ayer Rajah Expressway
    25. Jurong Town Hall Road
    26. Jurong East Central
    27. Jurong Gateway Road
    28. Jurong East Bus Interchange
     the route from Jurong East to Joo Koon is the same, only in reverse direction.

    I skipped Jurong West Street 42 and 81, Yung Kuang Road and Yung Ho Road (ie. Corporation Drive), Jurong West Street 64 and 75, and Jurong East Street 11 and Boon Lay Way.

    The reasons are as follow,

    - Jurong West Street 42 already has the most direct route to Jurong East, provided by 334; there is no need for the connection to Lakeside MRT Station.

    - Part of Jurong West Street 81 already has 179 providing the bus connection to Pioneer MRT Station. The other parts can continue to have 258, at bus stops along Jurong West Avenue 5.

    - Corporation Drive already has 98 providing the bus connection to Jurong West Street 51 where the market is.

    - The route of 258 along Jurong West Street 64, 75, 82 and 81 duplicates route 243.

    - As a Jurong West resident myself, when I go to Jurong East walk walk, I usually patronise JEM and Westgate, sometimes Big Box, JCube and IMM (via the link bridge)... Anyway, the bus stops along Boon Lay Way do not connect to most of the landmarks. As such, I feel that we should skip Jurong East Street 11 and Boon Lay Way.

    Do let me know how you think about this route suggestion on merging two bus services 49 and 258 into one.

    If you have an idea on how to improve this route suggestion, please feel free to share here.

    Thank you and have a nice day!
