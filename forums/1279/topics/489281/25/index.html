    Brackets before road names denote the number of bus stops. Bold road names denote proximity to MRT stations.

    • [1]  Bt Batok Ctrl   [NS2]

    During the first trial, 177 will operate during off-peak hours only (unless eventual demand necessitates otherwise). During peak hours, it will instead run these short-trips:

    • 177A: (AM peak only)  from Woodlands Rd (44051; aft Senja Way) to Upp Bt Timah Rd (44029; Bt Panjang MRT Stn). Approximate route outlined in blue & orange;

    • 177B: (PM peak only)  from Upp Bt Timah Rd (44029; Bt Panjang MRT Stn) to 44051; aft Senja Way) to Woodlands Rd (44069; aft KJE). Approximate route outlined in blue.
    • After first stop, makes a non-stopping loop around Petir / Jelebu / Bt Panjang Rds before calling at the second stop at Woodlands Rd (Junction 10);

    • 177C: (AM & PM peak)  Bt Batok Int - Gombak Dr (MINDEF) - Bt Panjang MRT (loop). Equivalent to current peak-hour 177 route.

     

    This suggestion makes the most out of a current service (including fleet, boarding berths, etc.), taking into consideration:

    • (1)  The urgent need for a Yew Tee - Bt Panjang MRT service when DTL2 opens;

    • (2)  The current unfeasibility of nearby interchanges (CCK too crowded, BPJ inefficient due to route drawback, esp. for the entire day), thus choosing Bt Batok as the most suitable interchange to start / end the main route, but utilizing Bt Panjang bus park as a resting point for peak-hour short-trips;

    • (3)  High demand between specific sectors during peak-hours, thus the introduction of fluid short-trips.

    • (4)  The need for more direct transfers to other buses from Yew Tee town (such as 61, 157, 506 etc.), thus the continuation to Bt Batok.
