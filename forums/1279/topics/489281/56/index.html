    Proposed route amendment of service 857:

    • Yishun Int
    • Yishun Central 1
    • Yishun Central 2
    • Yishun St 61
    • Yishun Ring Rd
    • Yishun Ave 2
    • (usual route to Suntec City)

     

    This is way overdue -- a trunk service serving St 61, with proper two-way links to Yishun Polyclinic / KTP / Yishun South CC / Khatib MRT station. Now that Ave 4 gets a bidirectional service (805), St 61 should deserve one too. This will benefit mainly neighbourhood 6 residents and Chung Cheng High students.

    The extension will add about 1.1km to the overall distance (two-way) and about 5-8 minutes to the journey time.

    Two pairs of bus stops are skipped -- mainly only long distance travellers will be affected, as there is still 855 to Yishun Central 1 and 854 to Yio Chu Kang Rd. Considering the effort of transferring for commuters at Ave 2 versus those at St 61, the amendment is worth it.

    The only concern is whether the road turns support double deckers. (Alternatively 853 or 855 can ply this route, if their fleet can support the additional load.)

     

    Possible follow-up: extension of 859

    • Sembawang Int
    • (current route to Yishun Ave 5)
    • Yishun Ave 5
    • Yishun Central
    • Yishun St 61
    • Yishun Ring Rd
    • Yishun Ave 4
    • Yishun Central
    • Yishun Ave 5
    • (current route to Sembawang Int)
    • Sembawang Int

     

    With 805 and 857 providing more direct links to Ave 4 and St 61 respectively, demand on east 812 is expected to fall, so this section can be given to 859. This frees up half the fleet of 812 for other deployments in Yishun, which can be distributed across 805, 857 and 859.
