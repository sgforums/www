    Speaking of Siemens, Keretapi Tanah Melayu Berhad (KTMB, aka Malayan Railway) will be getting new Siemens locomotives which are scheduled for delivery in August. These locomotives are built in China by the Dalian Locomotive and Rollingstock Works.

    Did you know that KTMB also have Kawasaki locomotives? They were built in collaboration with Toshiba and were delivered in 1985. Named the 24 Class, they are the most powerful KTMB locomotive until the arrival of the German Blue Tiger in 2003. Powerful they may be, the 24 Class have been plagued with reliability issues.

    Originally posted by SBS1908B:
    Can someone teach me each train type? I would be glad if you can.
    Well, I'll share what little that I know. Ok, first up are the pioneer batch of trains. They are delivered from 1986-1989. There are manufactured by Kawasaki Heavy Industries in collaboration with either Kinki Sharyo, Tokyu Car Corporation or Nippon Sharyo. The most abundant type, you'll most often ride on one.

    Next up are the Siemens model delivered in 1995 for the Woodlands Extension. They are the ones with a black strip at the doorway.

    Lastly are the black trains delivered in 2000-2001 for the Changi Airport extension, made by either Kawasaki or Nippon Sharyo. Different from the rest, very easy to spot.

    Technical details are unknown and very hard to find.
