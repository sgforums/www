    Non-basic bus services are launched by SBS Transit and SMRT Buses to cater to bus commuters who are awaiting from faster and more convenient bus travel to reach their destinations.

    However, are they really effective in doing so? Share your views and opinions on non-basic bus services based on 4 factors.

    Factors to highlight a non-basic bus service

    1 Frequencies - Do buses arrive at the bus stops in time and depart from bus stops / bus interchanges / bus terminals in time?

    2 Affordability - How are the fares of these bus services similar or different with basic bus services?

    3 Efficiency - Are buses able to transport their commuters to their destinations in time and are buses able to divert easily during traffic jams?

    4 Comfort - Are buses available with seats and are the seats comfortable?

    I would like to clarify something.

    SBS Transit and SMRT Buses can actually deploy buses into some non-basic bus services. 

    Since Service 401 uses buses from Service 196 and Fast Forward 196e, SBS Transit can actually implement crossover slots for buses from Service 196 and Fast Forward 196e.

    SMRT Buses should also implement crossover slots from Service 925 or Services 177 and 925 to deploy buses on Service 926.

    Premium services 589 and 598 can be handed over to Bus-Plus, since its Toyota Coasters are deployed in Bus-Plus services such as Services 541 and 547. The Lentor Estate and Teacher's Estate bus services, as well as SMRT Link, can all be handed over to Bus-Plus.

    Some weekend buses can be deployed on Service RWS88 (SP/RWS88) from KJDEP to better control the bus service.

    Nite Owl and NightRider services can also get some buses since they operate on a Friday, Saturday and eves of public holidays basis.
