    30 Jan 09, 13:17
    Originally posted by jayh272416:


    talking about this issue, why do they have to press out a coding? why cant they simplify things and let the drivers just press out the service number itself?

    They started it out wrong in the first place....TIBS, that is. And there may be hardware limitations, for e.g., the driver has to press a 3 digit on the EDS panel and he can't press 2 digits.

    Also, on older EDSes, the route number and the destination are seperately coded. They aren't together like on the OCs.

    On the OCs, if I'm not mistaken, say if I wanna press out 851 twds Yishun, I'd press for e.g. 111, then if I wanted 851 twds Bukit Merah, I have to press 112.

    On older buses (i.e. before OC generation), if I wanna press 851 twds Yishun I have to press 111 and 112. Then if I want 851 twds bukit merah, i'd have to press 111 and 113. That's why you have stuff like 859 towards Marina Ctr.

    They won't revamp the system.

    1. Cost

    2. Old drivers whom are used to the (current) system

    3. The basic theory of - if it ain't broken, don't touch it.
