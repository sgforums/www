    Actually, why I pointed out the ex-HG 27 B2 E4 KUBs in the list was because, I've the thinking that those KUBs that were ex-perm HG 27 had their gearbox "tuned" somewhat differently compared to the other E4 KUBs you see running around on the roads.

    The rev limiters on those ex-HG 27 KUBs don't cut in that early as the others, so therefore they can engage "kickdown mode" more easily than the other E4 KUBs without the usual jerkiness. It's as though HG has done something specifically back to those KUBs back then when they were perm HG 27 and played with their ECU mapping or something.
