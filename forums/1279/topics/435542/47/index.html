    Did you know.. that the most unique Jurong Industrial Service (JIS) in Boon Lay is Service 246? Although it is classified as a "JIS", this service mostly plies through Taman Jurong & Jurong West estates instead of industrial places in Boon Lay. It acts like a feeder bus instead. This is one of the few surviving "feeder" services in Singapore that residents could enjoy boarding a Non-Airconditioned (NAC) bus to and from home.

     

     

    Did you know.. that this particular bus (Leyland Olympian), registered as "SBS9168S" was the last ever Leyland Olympian bus chassis built in the world? It might just look like an another ordinary bus to the public, but it hides the most valuable history to the minorities that have been working in Leyland Co. Ltd in Britain. The company had been closed down in year 1993. It currently operates on Service 154, that operates from Eunos - Boon Lay.

     

     

    Did you know... that services 111 & 100 terminating at Ghim Moh Bus Terminal would have to do a 3-point turn near a carpark before ending its service? This terminal also retains its old bus stop sign.

     

     

    Did you know.. that Chinatown Direct Bus Services CT8 & CT18 are the only two daily bus services that ply on the tunnel sectors of CTE (Central Expressway)?

     

     

    Service 91 is the only service that loops at a bus depot (Ayer Rajah Bus Park - ARBP) at a daily basis. This service used to ply at a carpark, and had to bypass 2 carpark gantries; but that sector was closed earlier this year.



    SBS Transit's Bus Service 284 holds the shortest bus feeder route in Singapore. This bus service only serves a total of 3 bus stops (excluding interchange), which is within walking distance from the bus interchange/MRT station in Clementi.
