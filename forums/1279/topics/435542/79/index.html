    The smiley buses during the Trans Island (TIBS) era! =)

    The Volgren CR221 bodied Hino HS3KRKK (right) has a modified Volgren front to set its buses to be different from the then-SBS's CR221 bodied Volgren bodied B10BLE (1688K) and L94UB (2888T). When it was newly introduced into service, the Hino had a smile pasted at the front of the bus and (if not wrong, the only type to have this smile?) which was removed some time later.

    This modified Volgren front bodywork would be used on the Mercedes Benz O405, O405G and two Dennis Lance chassis.

    Later on, TIBS purchased the next fleet of O405G bendy buses (left) and is said to have wanted the front to be based/licensed from the Citaro (Gen 1), though it was unsuccessful and hence TIBS & Hispano created a modified front based on the Citaro and included the smiley motif as the Citaro's "front grille" design similarities - earning the nickname "Smiley bendy".
