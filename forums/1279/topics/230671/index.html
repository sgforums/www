    (1) CNG need to pump gas at Jurong Island. Only the split Shift people are arranged to pump the gas. So only services with split shift services can possibly accomodate a CNG. Only SL has CNG. Obvious reasons. SL is nearest to Jurong Island.

    (2) Not everyone can drive a CNG bus. Only those trained are able to do so.

    (3) CNG bus basically has the same anatomy as other buses. Except that the means of carburetion is different. It is by means of gas instead of liquid fuel.

    (4) There are 8 gas tanks on top of the CNG bus in the SBST buses. Each tank can store up to a maximum of 200bar which is 200 x 10 to the power of 5Pa.
    Diesel is measured in Litres while CNg is measured by Bar. For better illustration, the atmospheric pressure on Earth is approx 1bar. The brake booster spring if dislodged from the booster can kill people and lodge itself up into the ceiling of an air-craft hangar. This is the amount of spring power it has. And it takes 6 bar to compressed it. imagine 33 times more of such power compressed inside the tank.

    (5) I feel that CNG bus sux operationally due to refuelling/carburetion process. If I have a choice I hope that these buses never existed in SBST.

    (6) CNG buses scores very well for emission. Giving us no headache on emission tests.

    (7)Why it stalls during e-brake. It's applicable to any combustion engine just that it's more prominent in the CNG bus. Because when the vehicle is moving at speed, the curburetion is consistant. Air and fuel mixture is optimum to keep the engine moving. But when you suddenly jam brake, you're forcing your engine to suddenly slow down from high RPM. In a manual vehicle, if you dun clutch in quick enough, the vehicle confirm stall. In auto, if the gear dun release the clutch to N the engine also stalls. But the bus will still maintain in 1st gear. So the carburetion must adjust to suit the situation mechanically to give in ample fuel when the BC lifts his foot off the accelerator to the brake. Thats why sometimes it stalls. You drive any other car it's the same. This is the problem of the TPS (throttle positioning sensor)

    Nadi Putra

    The Daewoo CNG buses (most of the time) that run on a particular service will have a livery with the matching line (laluan) colour and seat scheme. The new buses were commissioned in batches since last June.

    Services running are Laluan 100, 200, 300, 301, 302 and 400. Fares are 50 sen per ride and services run at 30 mins interval during off-peak and 20 mins interval during peak hours.

    The services go to different parts inside Putrajaya, including residential precincts. All services (except Laluan 301) goes to the roundabout known as "Parcel ABCDE", which is the ring road serving all 5 parcels of land comprising the various "smaller" ministries, each parcel with its own colour scheme. So don't be alarmed if it seemed like it's going one big round if u're going to Putrajaya Sentral or Alamanda, which are the usual next destinations.

    The only 2 bus terminals in Putrajaya are Putrajaya Sentral (ERL and inter-state) and Terminal Bas Presint 1 (local).

    RapidKL only has limited participation here with 2 services (426 and 427) running inside Putrajaya, using Dongfeng buses. Fares are $1 but you get a day pass that entitles you to ride all local shuttles (green in colour). But at Putrajaya Sentral, E1 goes to KL Sentral while T43 connects you to Kelana Jaya LRT and Damansara (where The Curve, IKEA and Cineleisure is). There is also a T42 to Subang Jaya and Sunway.
