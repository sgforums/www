    Originally posted by carbikebus:

    You mean d20 engine ranging from 320/360 and 400 hp all use just a chip??

    All variants of the D2066 within the same Euro emissions level are the same engine.  For Euro V/VI buses and coaches, the lowest rating is 280 PS / 1250 Nm and the highest is 400 PS / 1900 Nm.  All variants are the same, with the same 6-cylinder, 10.516-litre configuration, the same twin-stage turbocharging, the same common rail injection system, and so on.  The difference in power and torque comes down to engine management differences and maybe (but not necessarily) differences in the fuel delivery system (e.g. larger injectors).  It is absolutely possible to significantly derate a 400 PS engine simply by controlling airflow (turbocharger wastegates) and fuel flow (injection timing and quantity).
