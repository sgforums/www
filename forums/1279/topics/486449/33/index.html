  • SBS351M
    28 Apr 16, 23:30
    Originally posted by SMB315C:

    In a nutshell, LTA has screwed the entire bus fleet for GCM operators..

    They should have purchase new A22, A95, (E500 if applicable), B9TL & O530 buses for those respective companies instead of handing some units over from SMRT & SBST.. Those (SMRT & SBST) buses can be deployed to other services to meet / improve WAB quota or improve the bus services frequency..

    Even the registration of buses under SG plate is really really messy..

    At that point in time (2009 to 2012), it was very clear to LTA that the bus network was not coping well with the old licensing regime where bus companies were losing money (ie bus side of SBS and SMRT), and that disatisfaction with bus services were increasing. Something had to be done fast to solve it, and while the contracting model looked attractive then, LTA needed the time to study and understand the new model. So the immediate action then was to launch BSEP where buses were bought by SBS and SMRT, which both had experience in buying buses. This has now changed with a department in LTA in charge of allocating the assets to different companies.

    If LTA had bought more buses now, there would be a lot of wastage, you will have excess buses running around, running in an uneconomical way. Yes, the companies are no longer affected by revenue risk under the contracting model, which means LTA can provide a level of service higher than that in the old regime, however, it is still subjected to LTA's budget, which is basically influenced by the government's fiscal policy, which means excess resources that are not justified will still not be ideal under LTA.
