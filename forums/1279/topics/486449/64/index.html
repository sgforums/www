    Either that or SBST is just plain fucking stupid... I'm not surprised if it is so LMAO

    Production and delivery does not work the way you think, manufacturers and customers have to agree on a delivery schedule. The most important thing on deciding the delivery schedule from a customer's point of view is finances (cashflow etc), bearing in mind that payment is made from the customer to the manufacturer or dealer usually only when the bus is delivered.

    What this means is that, the customer will have to decide between accepting a "rushed" delivery schedule with a heavier impact on cashflow vs accepting a slower delivery schedule which has a lesser impact on cashflow. 

    The customer will also need to consider whether any existing assets are reaching end of life, and the space and rent required to store the new (replacement) assets. Not using a perfectly usable asset until end of life is wastage, therefore, companies will only replace them when they come up for renewal. This determines the delivery schedule for the new equipment.

    In short, its not that Volvo is inefficient, its just that the contract most likely doesn't require them to produce at whatever rate you as an armchair expert think is suitable.

    Welcome to the real world.
