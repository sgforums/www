    Yesterday evening 6 pm at Haw Par Villa station, I spotted a double decker train - with that I mean 7 buses back to back. This was on the opposite stop to where I was, so could see all the buses.

    30 - Boon Lay (seated upper and lower deck with a few standies)

    10 - Kent Ridge Terminal (empty - nearing last stop - so okay)

    51 - Jurong East (50 pax)

    51 - Jurong East (20 pax)

    30 - Boon Lay (20 pax)

    143 - Jurong East (5 pax)

    143 - Jurong East (5 pax)

    This is during evening peak. It is such a waste of resources when buses bunch and the BCs just snail behind the buses ahead rather than creating some distance in between.

    Out of curiosity, I checked through iris the next bus arrivals. 

    30 - 09 minutes
    51 - 23 minutes
    143 - 18 minutes

    My argument is that if you see a bus of the same number ahead, slow down so that you at least create a 3-5 minutes gap between the next bus. In this case, the first bus becomes slow, the 2nd bus becomes fast, creating a huge gap for the 3rd bus. 

    If a 3 minute interval is created, you at least carry more passengers coming to the bus stop in the 3 minute interval.

    Your thoughts? And your bus bunching experiences - post here!!
