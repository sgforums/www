    It is obvious that the new buses, bought using the money for BSEP, will be deployed to this new BSEP bus service; The buses to be deployed on service 49 would be new Mercedes Citaro buses and new Volvo B9TL Wright double-deck buses. 

    As mentioned earlier, the length of the new BSEP route is 10.7km. Judging from experience, it would take around 70 minutes for a bus to complete a trip on route 49. On the assumption that the service runs on 10-15 minutes frequency, around 5 to 7 buses is required. 

    Taking reference from 98: the shortest frequency is 4 minutes per bus. This is perhaps the shortest frequency on 49 as well, since it complements 98, to a certain extent. Thus, the maximum number of buses that may possibly be deployed to 49 is 18. 

    For a start, the frequency of BSEP service 49 is probably the same as that of the other BSEP services when they just started - 10-15 minutes - which means that around 5 to 7 buses would be deployed to this service. Over time, if the demand for this service grows and more people commute on this service, it is likely to run at a reduced frequency of down to just 4 minutes, with a maximum of 18 buses deployed to this service, 2.6 times the maximum number of buses deployed now as this service just started.

    Based on where the Taman Jurong Bus Terminal is located, it is very likely that the buses for service 49 will come from Soon Lee Bus Depot (roughly 3km straight-line-distance away), via Pioneer Road North, International Road, Yung Kuang Road, Corporation Drive and Hu Ching Road.
