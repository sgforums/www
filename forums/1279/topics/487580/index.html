    NEW BUS DEPOT AT SUNGEI SELETAR TO SUPPORT BUS FLEET EXPANSION UNDER BSEP

    News Releases
    30 Jan 2015

         The Land Transport Authority (LTA) will be building a new Sungei Seletar bus depot, off Yio Chu Kang Road beside the Seletar Expressway (SLE) and Sungei Seletar Simpang Kiri river. This is to accommodate the growing bus fleet that will support higher service standards under both the expanded Bus Service Enhancement Programme (BSEP) and the Government Contracting Model (GCM) for the public bus industry in Singapore.

    2.   The LTA is calling a tender today to invite contractors to submit their cost proposals to build the Sungei Seletar bus depot.

    3.   When completed by third quarter 2017, the Sungei Seletar bus depot will accommodate about 500 buses and will be equipped with facilities for daily bus operations, bus repair and maintenance, bus parking, offices, rest area for bus drivers and canteen. An access road will also be built to serve the depot. The bus depot will be handed over to the Public Transport Operator for outfitting and preparatory works before operations can commence.

    4.   Besides bus depots, the government has built bus parks to support the additional buses introduced under the BSEP. Since 2012, two bus parks[1] have been built to support BSEP, while two other bus depots[2] are under construction.
