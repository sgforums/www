Bus Driver Who Does Not Know His Route

  • rainee
    6 Apr 08, 09:42

    Yesterday I wanted to go to this place called Orange Grove Road which is somewhere near Orchard. I do not know where this road is located, I only know I can take bus no. 132 and 105 from the bus stop near Far East Plaza. So when a bus no. 132 arrived at the bus stop, I hopped on it and showed the map of the place to the driver. I asked him whether this bus goes to this particular place (the RELC building at Orange Grove Road) and he said yes. Of course I believed him and went on to take the bus.

    After a few bus stops, he stopped the bus and told us that we had reached the destination but we would have to walk in through the pathway opposite the bus stop. We got down at this bus stop at Orchard Boulevard at around 3.55pm and walked down the pathway he had pointed out. We walked one round and still couldn't find the place we were looking for!

    We reached the bus stop at Orchard Boulevard again after one big round and when we looked at the map in the bus stop, we were shocked to note that Orange Grove Road is actually a few stops before Far East Plaza. Which means we shouldn't have gotten into the bus and should have crossed the road and took the bus opposite the plaza instead. But why did the bus driver told us it is okie to take his bus and caused us to waste money and time looking for it in the wrong place?

    And this is not the first time either. Last time when I went up a bus in Woodlands and asked if the bus driver knows where is the Woodlands Sports Center, he replied to me that he doesn't know even when his bus is supposed to be passing through there. Is it common for bus drivers nowadays to not be familiar with the routes they are driving through anymore?
