    In the early days in 1971, that was when the 4 bus companies were reorganised. Service 197 was born as Crawford St to Shipyard Rd service (noticeably most of the services in Jurong used to terminate at different teminus in the Jurong Industrial Estate area), used to operate by ABC before merging to SBS in 1973. However, this service has to make a U-turn at Tanjong Kilng Rd facing the National Iron & steel Mills factory.

    Sometime in end 1977, at that time Jurong Interchange was opened, several bus services in Jurong had to terminate and call from Jurong Interchange as part of the integration and fragmentation of all services terminating at one terminus. By then, the service was extended to Geylang Fringe Car Park Terminus (now known as Geylang Lor 1 Terminal), making it to be known as Geylang - Jurong Service.
    When Jurong Interchange ceased operations in 1990, Service 197 was amended to Boon Lay Interchange and then eventaully become shortened to Jurong East Int. On the other hand, it was also extended to Bedok from Gylang Ter in the mid-1990s.
