    okok make very clear here

    Technically they are called Guy Victory mark 2s. Leyland ate up Guy so is become Leyland Victory.

    the dash use Leyland component. in fact look like lo3x dash if u see old picture.

    The Guy Victory mark 2 were chassis built in United Kingdom Britain and then ship to Singapore for Soon Chow .

    a certain batch of the Guy Victory came ckd for soon chow to build with British Aluminium Company (BACO) bodywork parts for Soon how to see blue print follow n build.

    So got soon chow one n BACO one.

    Then much later in 1988 SBS decided to ask Soon Chow to convert some BACo n Soon Chow Victory to air-con one. thus one of the first few CACs done here.

    Soon Chow basically stripped the bus back down to chassis n rebuild the body from scratch using a familiar coach like bodywork of that time to re build the bus.

    about 14 or 15 units were CAC. the rest carried on as metal rattling on-air-con noisy buggers till late 1993 when all were phased out at one go.
