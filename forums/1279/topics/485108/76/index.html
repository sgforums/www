    Third bus package put up for tender

    SINGAPORE: A third bus package under the Government Contracting Model was put up for tender on Tuesday (Jun 7), the Land Transport Authority (LTA) announced in a news release.


    The Seletar bus package comprises 24 existing services and two new services, which will be announced later. The bus services will be operated from the Ang Mo Kio, Yio Chu Kang and Yishun Bus Interchanges, and will be supported by the new Seletar Bus Depot located off Yio Chu Kang Road, which is scheduled to be completed by the third quarter of 2017.


    The existing bus services in this package are: 24, 70, 76, 130, 133, 135, 138, 162, 261, 262, 265, 268, 269, 800, 803, 804, 805, 806, 811, 812, 850E, 851, 852 and 860.


    The package consists of about 420 buses, growing to about 500 buses in 2023 in tandem with new developments and projected growth in ridership, LTA said.


    The successful tenderer will also operate, manage, and maintain the Ang Mo Kio and Yio Chu Kang Bus Interchanges and the Seletar Bus Depot.


    Both local and foreign bus operators can bid for the right to operate the package. All tender submissions will be evaluated through a two-envelope process for quality and price, with more weightage given to the quality proposals submitted by the tenderers, LTA said.


    The operator which successfully bids for the package will operate the bus services for five years, with the possibility of a two-year extension for good performance.


    “The implementation of the bus services in the Seletar bus package will be carried out in two tranches from the first half of 2018, to allow sufficient time for the operator to build up its resources, such as recruitment and training of bus captains and technicians,” LTA said.


    The first bus package – the Bulim package – was awarded to London-based Tower Transit after 11 bids were received. The second bus package – the Loyang package – was awarded to another UK transport operator, the Go-Ahead Group, after 10 bids were received.


    UNION IN DISCUSSIONS WITH AFFECTED STAFF

    To safeguard the welfare of bus employees, the Public Transport Tripartite Committee will engage various stakeholders during the transition, LTA said.


    In a Facebook post on Tuesday, National Transport Workers’ Union (NTWU) executive secretary Melvin Yong said the union has been working closely with the committee to ensure that the concerns and feedback from the affected bus captains are taken into account.


    The union, together with LTA, SBS Transit and SMRT, has conducted more than 10 engagement sessions with affected staff, he added.


    “Through these sessions, we addressed their concerns, clarified their queries and sought their feedback regarding the latest changes,” he said.


    “While we will continue to engage the relevant stakeholders to ensure that workers are able to have a smooth transition to the new model, we also urge all bus operators to continue to work with the NTWU. This is so that we can all enjoy a harmonious, constructive and win-win industrial relations climate that will benefit both companies and workers.”


    UK-based bus operator Tower Transit indicated interest in the latest bus package. Mr Andrew Bujtor, MD, Tower Transit Singapore told Channel NewsAsia: “We most certainly have our sights set on the Seletar package. There are significant economies and efficiencies that we can unlock by operating on a larger scale. We’re certain this will allow us to offer LTA a very competitively priced bid backed firmly by our local experience of running what will soon be a fleet of about 370 buses and 26 services.


    We’ve brought innovation to everything we’ve done in Singapore, set new industry standards in many areas, and now we’re focused on delivering the best possible service in order to present the best possible case for Tower Transit to be awarded the Seletar package.”
