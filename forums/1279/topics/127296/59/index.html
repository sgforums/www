    I seriously don't see the need to take so many bus guides at once, even if you are a collector of them.

    If you want to collect them, fine, but don't take stacks of it. You are depriving the people who really need them for information. Not only that, but since money is spent on printing these guides, which are then provided free-of-charge, you are indirectly making the bus companies lose more money, and in turn, give them an excuse to raise fares.

    You might not know it, but these bus guides can be useful to even the kind of people who don't seem to need them. For example, I once wanted to go to the Salvation Army thrift shop in Changi (not the one in Changi Village, but somewhere near) from Changi Village. I used the bus guide on board to find out the fare stage number of the stop I need to get off at (after enquiring with the bus captain), and I sat near the ez-link card readers to check on their fare stage numbers to make sure I get the right stop (which I did). If I didn't have the bus guide, I could have needed to constantly ask the bus captain how many stops is it to my destination (thus distracting him from driving), or to have to count the number of bus stops myself, which can be inaccurate at times depending on whether you count in the destination stop as "1 stop" or not.
