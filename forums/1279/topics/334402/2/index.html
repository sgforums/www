    Seriously, even though I think the level of graces of Chinese nationals overall is just about as bad as that of a Singaporean overall, if you factor out the more educated, younger people, you certainly have a people who are even worse than Singaporeans. And I regard Singaporeans as scrapping the barrel in this case.

    I believe much of this can be attributed to them growing up in their closed-off society for much of their lives. Don't forget China is a Communist country, and it was only until recently that China started to open up like this, and their people could (almost) freely migrate to any place in the world. Having lived in their bubble for so long has definitely turned them into the ignorant, ungracious people they are now infamous for. To them, the open world we live in comes as quite a hard culture shock to them, and having been brought up for so long used to having one opinion or habit while being closed off from other alternatives have robbed them of their adaptiveness to our world. When confronted with alternative viewpoints and notions, they can only react with rejection and arrogance, unable to accept that such alternatives exist and that only the viewpoints and notions they know of can.

    I don't want to put the blame entirely on the government though - it seems like Singaporeans often complain of their jobs being robbed by foreigners, but we ourselves are shunning jobs that we complain are being taken away by foreigners because we whine about it not paying well, being too difficult, tiring, etc. In some ways, we only have ourselves to blame for our own ricebowls being taken away because of our own laziness, and our never-to-be-fulfilled lust for that imaginary highly-paid job that does not demand hard on our minds and bodies.
