  • 16/f/lonely
    8 Sep 08, 21:51

    I wonder if SBS815S is still as good as its 89 days. That bus's acceleration is one MKIII that whacks the shit out of you.

    SBS9580J.....not sure of its current status. As a BRAM 88 permer, overtaking other vehicles became child's play with its well-tuned engine. As a AMDEP 72, I took it once and it was disappointing, I'm hoping it's because it was because of the BC.

    SBS9423E, for the most part of the 2000s as a BRAM 88, this bus's lock-up clutch was never repaired and served as a studying tool on the effects of lock-up power-loss for me. Just took it a few days ago on 161, now repaired finally, it definitely proved to be another "shiok" VO.

    SBS9171G, also not sure of current status, but as AMDEP 161, was pretty well-known to be one speed-monster. The "shiok" factor went up when at one point in time it had its gearbox tuned to the same as a VO.

    SBS9159T, who knows what they did to multiply this LO's torque. Even with a light-pedal, this bus stays in first gear for only micro-seconds after lock-up engages because this bus practically flies after the turbo-lag.

    List is not exhaustive!
