    Bus fires normally are due to the following:
    (1) brake seizure- Volvo running on Z cam systems are extremely prone to jamming.
    (2) Turbo Chargers- The turbocharger is jammed or lack of lubrication from the EO directly tapped from the oil filter.
    (3) Electrical faults- Common in all sorts of vehicles.
    (4) fuel leakage- Petrol engines will be very prone to fire if fuel lines leak. But diesel merely causes smoke with the current bus operations. To burst into fire with diesel is not easy.
    (5) ATF leakage- ATF is combustible even though the temperature required to burn it is very very high and almost unlikely in normal conditions. The Exxonmobil ATF requires up to 200DegC to burn as demonstrated by SBS Technical Service Team before. But as ATF is a very commonly circulated fluid in the buses, for it to leak onto hot surfaces and cause smoke mistaking it to be fire is common.

    I am not saying that there will not be other possibilities but they are really too low. (1) to (3) are the most common. (4) and (5) are not common. In fact I have yet to hear about diesel and ATF causing fire on buses.

    Intercoolers have nothing to do with fire or not normally. There are basically 2 intercoolers in the buses. One is the turbo intercooler which helps to cool down the force fed air into the combustion chambers as turbo induced force fed air also carry the hot air into the combustion chamber. The high air temperature does not execute combustion as effective. The hot air is circulated around the coolant in 2 different passages. Therefore transfering the heat to the coolant which in turns dissipates it at the radiator.
    The other intercooler is the gearbox intercooler whereby the ATF that is used to operate the gearbox requires to be cooled constantly. Therefore an intercooler allows the ATF to circulate around the coolant in 2 different passages. So that the heat can be transferred to the coolant which will dissipate off at the radiator.
