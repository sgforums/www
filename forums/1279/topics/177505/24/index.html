    I prefer to call it DESKPAPER (an old joke I made on a technology forum I used to go to a long time ago) .

    Anyway, back to the topic on the Volvo B9TL. I had the chance to ride this bus last Sunday (SBS 7302J - the one with the commemorative livery), and I have to say, this is one beautiful bus. Acceleration and deceleration was smooth, and the air-conditioner is top-rated (very much like SBS 9889U's). The engine also sounded quieter and better, very unlike the Volvo Super Olympians (which was too loud and low-pitched, like some kind of a bull). The interior was also very well designed, striking a perfect balance between a modern design and a functional design. The colour scheme - with it's hues of yellows, greens, and gold - was much better than SBS 9889U's, whose hues of blue and purple, while sleek and cool-feeling, was rather overwhelming. I also like the new ez-link Card readers' colour scheme, with it's two tones of not-too-contrasting grays (p.s. I saw one such reader installed on a particular SMRT Mercedes MB O405 bus a few days ago, seemingly replacing an older spoilt one - seems like whoever are making them decided to use a new colour scheme). Leg and head room was great - my long legs had no problems on several seats I tried, and I can actually stand up straight - all 1.8 metres of me - on the upper deck! Not since childhood times had I managed to do this on a double-deck bus.

    Exterior-wise, I find not too many problems with it. In fact, it looks very sleek and stylish, and the so-called "one-piece" windscreen manages to look like it's fabricated from a piece of glass, even though it's really three joined together. Viewed from a certain angle, these buses even exudes an air of nostalgia - they somehow look and feel like those "classic" style of buses from the 1950's and 1960's Singapore. Maybe it's the deeper-than-usual roofline at the front...

    One gripe though - the upper deck front windscreen is just too dark, and distorts the outside world too much. I have never seen something like this before - and it's just headache-inducing. The upper deck front row seats are usually my favourite seats on double deck buses, but not this one.

    Also, I don't know if this is a problem isolated to just the particular bus I took, but the exit door alarm sounded like a botched-up version of the ones we hear on the Super Olympians. Until I can take other such buses, I want to believe that this problem is isolated to this particular bus only.
