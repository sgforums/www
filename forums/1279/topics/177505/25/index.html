    You see... One thing that I learnt in SBST. The money that's used to buy the buses are our hard earned money. This money is siphoned out of our earnings to invest in tools that strengthens customer service.

    Every cent that's used to spend on the buses are paid by commuters. Every step we take that involves money must be justified. We don't enjoy raising fares for nothing. That's why steps are to be taken progressively. Not to jump and risk losing our balance. Don't buy unless we need it.

    The room for profit expanding is very limited for SBST. So every step must be carefully planned before we actually 'step' forward to make a commitment.

    Alas, not all my colleagues practice the art of cost control. Some of them jump into deals of purchase but didn't realise that they're losing more than they gain only much later.
