    Currently, there is 99 providing direct bus connection from Yunnan (Nanyang and Gek Poh) to the Bon Lay and Hong Kah subzones and 185 to the Wenya and Hong Kah Sub-zones of Jurong West. From the Hong Kah area, there is 99 to Boon Lay and the Extension area and 185 to Wenya and Yunnan (Nanyang and Gek Poh) – Yunnan (Nanyang and Gek Poh), Boon Lay and Hong Kah are well inter-connected by direct bus.

    Wenya has weak direct bus connection to Boon Lay subzone: there are bus routes to Boon Lay but only to the Western end of Boon Lay. There is totally no direct bus connection to Taman Jurong from Yunnan (Nanyang and Gek Poh) and Wenya. Whereas for the bus connection to the Gek Poh and Jurong Point areas, 181 has already been proposed to be re-routed to loop around Jurong West Avenue 3, Jurong West Street 22, Jurong West Street 23, Jurong West Avenue 2, Wenya Avenue, Wenya Drive and Jalan Bahar, when the Wenya Industrial Estate is developed.

    As such, I would propose a new bus route primarily providing connections from Wenya to Taman Jurong and from Yunnan (Nanyang and Gek Poh) to Taman Jurong.

    This bus route to Taman Jurong can also be extended to the upcomg Tukang industrial area after going through Taman Jurong. 

    1. Joo Koon Bus Interchange (providing direct bus connection to the Joo Koon Industrial area from the Jurong West Extension area)
    2. Benoi Road 
    3. Upper Jurong Road 
    4. Jurong West Street 93 (providing bus connection to Nanyang Neighbourhood Centre from the blk986-990 estate) 
    5. Jurong West Street 92 
    6. Jurong West Street 91 
    7. Pioneer Road North 
    8. Jurong West Avenue 5 
    9. Jurong West Street 81 (providing bus connection to the Nanyang Neighbourhood Centre from the Street 81 section of Nanyang Constituency, which is currently the only one without a bus link to the Nanyang Neighbourhood Centre.) 
    10. Jurong West Street 82 
    11.  Jurong West Avenue 5 
    12. Jurong West Avenue 3 
    13. Jurong West Street 22 
    14. Jurong West Street 23 (shortest option to go through Wenya yet still serve it sufficiently well) 
    15. Corporation Road (Providing bus connection to Taman Jurong from Wenya and Yunnan [Nanyang and Gek Poh]) 
    16. Jalan Tukang (avoiding the jam at the section of Corporation Road from here onwards to the expressway) 
    17. Tukang Innovation Grove 
    18. Tukang Innovation 
    19. Drive Jalan Ahmad Ibrahim (new industrial area. So might as well serve this industrial area and loop here, while also to avoid jam) 
    20. Corporation Road (going in the opposite direction as the evening jam along that section along Corporation Road) 
    21. Jurong West Avenue 2 
    22. Jurong West Street 23 
    23. Jurong West Street 22 
    24. Jurong West Avenue 3 
    25. Jurong West Avenue 5 
    26. Jurong West Street 82 
    27. Jurong West Street 81 
    28. Jurong West Avenue 5 
    29. Pioneer Road North 
    30. Jurong West Street 91 
    31. Jurong West Street 92 
    32. Jurong West Street 93 
    33. Upper Jurong Road 
    34. Benoi Road 
    35. Joo Koon Bus Interchange 

    Total distance = 24.4km  

    I’m not sure if 24.4km is too long for a Trunk route. Meanwhile, bus route 99 is 25.7km long and bus route 98 is 26.4km long. 

    Nonetheless, although it is a long route, it does provide many bus connections that are currently not available, providing bus connections to 3 Industrial estates, Neighbourhood centres and many schools where there are currently no bus connection to them from various parts of Jurong West, etcetera. In addition, it would also complement 99 and 185 to a small extent. Generally, it would improve the inter-subzone connectivity within the Jurong West Planning Area. 

    This route I suggest may not be the perfect route yet but I believe if we go along this line, we should be able to formulate a bus route that is the perfect version of this suggested route.

Even if want to collaborate, have partnership, share resources, joint project, etcetera. , I’m sure they have their own private vehicle to go to each other’s factory. I believe the Public buses’ role in Industrial areas is (solely) to ferry people to their workplace from nearby bus interchange(s) and if possible, from their homes. Otherwise, the other role is to ferry people to the amenities in the Industrial area, such as food court, retail outlets, etc.

    Taking reference from the bus stop at Pioneer MRT Station, which can only accommodate 1 more bus service, it can be concluded that a normal bus stop is designed to accommodate up to 5 bus services. Including 98M, there are already 5 bus services running along Corporation Drive – full already. Meanwhile, Corporation Road and Yuan Ching Road have only 1 bus service running on the majority of them.

    Maybe an alternative would be to re-route bus route 246 to go further into Chin Bee Drive, and ply along Third Chin Bee Road and Chin Bee Avenue, and then return to its original route at Quality Road. This would skip that 1 pair of bus stop at Quality Road but there is bus route 79 to make up the loss for residents at South Taman Jurong.

    Actually, regarding Taman Jurong, I ever had a thought that perhaps bus route 240 should be split up as it might be a bit too long (15.4km: even longer than bus routes 243G and 243W combined) and does not really serve as a feeder service for Residents of Taman Jurong. There isn’t really a feeder bus service to Taman Jurong from Boon Lay Bus Interchange. The shortest/fastest bus connection to Boon Lay Bus Interchange available at Taman Jurong would perhaps be service 30, 154 and 178 – all of which use Boon Lay Way to go to Boon Lay Bus Interchange.
