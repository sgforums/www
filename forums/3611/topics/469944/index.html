    Getting married in a church is too common and boring for these couples, who chose to make theirs memorable and exciting

     

     

    Karen Lloyd and Steve Vailes dressed up as skeletons for their wedding in Bristol, England.

    Fran Calvo, left, and Monica Fraile celebrate their wedding in an aquarium in Malaga, Spain.

    Bride Erin Finnegan and groom Noah Fulmor, both of New York, are helped by Zero Gravity Corporation co-founder Richard Garriott, right, and Bryan Rapoza as they seal their wedding with a kiss during the first weightless wedding

    This couple were wed on a platform high above the ground, and then launched themselves over the side.

    Rick Harker, left, 64, and Marlene Hoar, 55, kiss as they rappel down the side of a skyscraper in Vancouver, British Columbia. The couple married as they descended the building

    'Til death do us part: Tony and Angela Lightowler pose for wedding pictures after renewing their marriage vows during Whitby Gothic Weekend in Whitby, England.

    Bonnie Cam and Jon Nguyen pay for their 99 cent wedding ceremony at a checkout at a store in Los Angeles. The 99 Cents Only Stores chain paid for weddings for nine couples on Sept. 9, 2009.

    Tsen Hai Sun and Hay Gy Sun, hanging by their hair beneath a helicopter, get married in 1979 in Germany.

    A robot named I-Fairy acts as a witness at the wedding of Tomohiro Shibata, left, and Satoko Inoue in Toky

     

    Thousands of couples marry at a mass wedding ceremony hosted by the Unification Church in South Korea. The Unification Church, founded by the Rev. Sun-myung Moon in Seoul in 1954, performed its first mass wedding in 1961 with 33 couples.

    This couple look like they came straight out of Tim Burton's 'Corpse Bride' movie.

    Kanjana Ketkaew, right, with scorpions on her gown, poses with Bunthawee Sengwong, with a centipede in his mouth, during their wedding in Thailand. Ketkaew, 35, holds a world record for staying in a plastic cage with 3,400 scorpions for 32 days. Her husband holds a world record for staying in a cage with 1,000 centipedes for 28 days.
