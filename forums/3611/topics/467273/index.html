Air Freshener Made From Cow Dung

  • NeverSayGoodBye

    Two high school students from Indonesia have created an air freshener from cow dung. 

    Orange News reported that students Dwi Nailul Izzah and Rintya Aprianti Miki made the environmentally-friendly fragrance from fresh cow manure. 

    It is said to have a “natural fragrance” of herbs and is “good for human health” because it does not contain any harmful chemicals found in other aerosol products in the market. 



    To create the freshener, the girls collected fresh dung from a cattle farm and left it to ferment over three days. 

    Next, they extracted the water from the dung and mixed it with coconut water—after which, the mixture was distilled to remove impurities. 

    The end product was an air freshener with a “natural aroma of herbs from digested cow food”. 

    Meanwhile, Dwi Nailul Izzah claimed his scientific work was explained about waste droppings (feces) into liquid cow pengharuman room with natural fragrance of herbs. "Fragrances room that we produce pure natural smelling like cow feed plant, not supplemented with chemicals in order to smell fragrant," he said.

    In his work, the two students were also made economic studies regarding market air freshener products are. "This room fragrances healthy because it contains no harmful chemicals, such as" benzo acetan "products like fragrances on the market, and also economical because the cost of production is very low, at $ 21 thousand to 225 milliliters packaging," he said.

    Meanwhile, for air freshener products on the market price of Rp 39,900 for a 275 gram pack.Therefore, it will be filed for a patent on the work, as appropriate the work of two students at the site Director of Intellectual Property Rights (IPR) has been no similar product patents.
