Farmer in Scotland created Panda Sheep

  • NeverSayGoodBye

    THIS wee pet lamb bears an uncanny resemblance to Scotland’s most famous animal attraction.

    A farmer has created a breed of sheep to celebrate the arrival of the Edinburgh Zoo pandas.

    The first lamb was born on April Fool’s Day – but proud Jill Noble insists it is no joke.

    And after the failure of Sunshine and Sweetie to mate this week, she could pass on a few tips on successful reproduction.

    However, Jill is refusing to reveal the breeds of the parents.

    She said: “We are keeping that to ourselves. Let’s just say there is a bit of Icelandic as well as Scottish.

    “We are calling it the panda breed. We do sell Icelandic lambs as they look very nice so I imagine there will be interest in this breed.”

    Jill, of Carlops, Peeblesshire, breeds horses and cattle and has a 120-strong flock of sheep.

    She added: “Everyone is very amused and impressed by the lambs.

    “And the sheep certainly showed the pandas a thing or two about producing babies.”
