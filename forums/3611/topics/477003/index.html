    Korean artist JeeYoung Lee's work is turning heads after a French gallery displayed several pieces of her mind-bending work. 

    Lee creates the unbelievable pieces in her small studio – the space measures just 3 by 6 metres – over the course of weeks and even months.

    "The moment required to set the stage gives her time to meditate about the causes of her interior conflicts and hence exorcise them; once experienced, they in turn become portents of hope,"

     

    It might seem hard to believe but the images she snaps of the scenes she creates contain absolutely no photo editing of any kind. Lee draws inspiration for these unique self-portraits from "excerpts from her heart, her memory, or her dreams."
