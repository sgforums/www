    Hanging precariously off the side of a mountain in the Swiss Alps, the wooden structure is certainly not for the faint hearted

    It might not look that safe - but at 7,000ft this shed boasts some of the world's most stunning views.

    Hanging precariously off the side of the mountain, the wooden structure is certainly not for the faint hearted.

    It was built to house the operator of a chairlift in the Portes du Soleil resort in Switzerland.

    And with views across the stunning Alps Mountain ranges there are definitely more boring places to work.

    Real estate agent Florin Biscu, from Romania, bought the floating shed during a recent trip to the ski resort.

    He said: "When the fog rolls into the valley the shed appears as if it is floating on thin air."

    But the shack is soon to be demolished and replaced by a more modern, sturdier structure.

    Despite its rickety appearance, Florin says it is resilient.

    He added: "It is safe inside, just a little cramped. It is strong enough to accommodate one person, but no more.

    "I was very curious to know why it was there. It is very rustic. It looks like the home of Charlie Chaplin in the movie 'The Gold Rush'."
