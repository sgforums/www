    Lions are native to Africa, not South America. But here at the Safari Park Zoo, the only park in Latin America where tourists can see from a short distance six lions in a two-hectares large open area, the lions are the biggest draw.

    The roles, however, are reversed. Inside the safari, tourists travel in a cage-like vehicle, and the lions are free to examine them (with hungry curiosity, perhaps) and walk atop the cages. It's a rare kind of thrill and, hopefully, entertaining for both parties.
