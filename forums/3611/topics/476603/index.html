    3D paintings are so yesterday. Immerse in ‘4D’ outdoor street art on the steps of Wisma Atria.

     

    Art isn’t only found in galleries.

    Dutch artists Ruben Poncia and Leon Keer have brought their art to Singapore’s Orchard Road in the form of a 63 square-metre 3D painting outside the Wisma Atria shopping mall.

    The anamorphic street painting, which depicts iconic buildings from major shopping cities around the world such as the Empire State Building, Eiffel Tower and Petronas Twin Towers, took over a month to design. Completed on November 21 after five days of work, the art work gives viewers the illusion that they are looking down at a strange, colorful city, when seen from the right angle.

    To enjoy the masterpiece in '4D', visitors can download a mobile application and scan the 3D artwork, which allows them to watch animated snow fall on the landmarks via their mobile devices and tablets.

    A smaller 40 sqm art piece on the second level of Wisma Atria.

    Commission by DBS, this 150 sqm art piece was on display outside Raffles City shopping mall in April.
