    Originally posted by DeadPoet:
    How to become a Psychologist in Singapore? I am really keen to be one leh.
    DP, the requirements for registration as a Psychologist in Singapore are:
    1) A Master's degree or Doctorate and;
    2) at least 400 hours of supervised practice and;
    3) full membership of the Singapore Psychological Society

    I finished my doctorate in December 2002, but I went into market research after that (there's good money in statistics), so paying about $400 a year to be a registered psychologist in Australia didn't make any sense. However, by the end of this year it looks like I'll be back in the psychology business, so I've just dropped off my application. Besides, I needed to apply within two years, or my qualifications would need further assessment.

    I don't do any of that clinical stuff, though - my D.Psych's in Organisational/Industrial Psychology. By the end of my undergrad years, I figured I didn't want to spend my days listening to someone whine about how daddy didn't love them enough. Besides, why chase a dysfunctional and jobless client up for fees when you could just as easily make 2 grand a day?

    Don't let me put you off, though - this is just my personal perspective. I've got plenty of friends who are clinical, neuro and counselling psychologists, and I respect them for the talented people they are.
