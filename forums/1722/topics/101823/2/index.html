    Exactly, bratpig, and that's why I invited people to post their experiences in the thread starter.

    To expand on the point, look at the example of psychology in the US. The field came into wide public acceptance after the work of psychologists such as Charles Spearman during World War I. As a result of work such as the introduction of psychology into the military selection process, the American public came into direct contact with psychology, and since that time psychology has become widely accepted and recognised in American society. Similarly, in other countries such as Australia, the practice of psychology introduced during the world wars paved the way for the field's acceptance in society.

    Of course, Singapore has not had such an experience, and my observation is that psychology is not as widely recognised and practiced here as in other countries. Indeed, ignorant fools here think that psychology is exclusively associated with mental patients. Rather than simply reaching the conclusion that Singapore is pretty much psychologically unaware, I was asking for experiences that reflect that Singapore is gaining awareness of the field.
