    My personal theory is that what is not said is more important than what people say. I find that analysing what people say, and juxtaposing it against what they would be expected to say along their obvious line of argument, shows up gaps that reveal what they're afraid of.

    As a very generalised rule, I find that most people, maybe four out of five, like to show off what they don't have. This, of course, is a central factor in how banks operate.

    Also, at the end of the day, the motivation for people to do things can be encapsulated in one simple question: "What's in it for me?".
