    The behavioural perspective is often a bit too simplistic IMHO - it often assumes a linear cause-and-effect relationship.

    In this case, I don't think the theory adequately addresses the issue of jealousy for women. Going by the sociobiological perspective, tending to the children would exclusively be the woman's role, and the male's commitment in the bond is therefore irrelevant.

    Certainly the territorial instinct plays a part in jealousy, but it's more complex than that.
