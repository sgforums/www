    Great food:

    Thai fried rice ( khao phad ) at the restaurant ( probably GP88 ) beside/opposite the Petronas gas station in Gelang Patah. Opens only at night. 3.50 ringgit. There is at least one similar Thai fried rice stall at Nusa Bestari, but have not tried it.

    Penang laksa, Johor laksa, mee rebus etc at a Malay stall at Gelang Patah night market every Tuesday. Very good business. Seem to be sold out within an hour. About 2.00 ringgit per packet.

    White-color fried (or is it steamed?) rice vermicelli (bee hoon) at various Malay stalls at Gelang Patah night market every Tuesday. Nicer than most Chinese styles. 1.00 or 1.30 ringgit per packet.

    Yes, 777 is nice, I am a frequent visitor of this restaurant It is an open space restaurant concept, advise you all to visit it on Saturday as most of the nice stalls will be open on that day. The restaurant is also located opposite to the night market on Saturday, so after dinner you can take a walk there to find more snacks and food But come earlier, as most tables are snapped up around 8pm...

    Another place which is nice is a western food in Taman Melodies, nearby to Holiday Plaza...but it is not easy to find this place...just go behind Holiday Plaza and roam around...the name is Lemon Tree Restaurant, the chicken chop and spaghetti is highly recommended, most of my colleagues praised them to the heavens lolz...including me myself as well

    Another gem near Holiday Plaza is a duck/chicken stall, but I dunno how to go there yet, only been there once and my colleagues drove me there de...

    Another nice place is near the Pelangi Mall, it is a noodle soup place, very famous it seems and has appeared in newspapers before...When you make the U-Turn to go to Pelangi Mall, turn into the first turning you see, right after the bus stand, then go all the way in, and you will see a restaurant at your left...that's the place...

    If you would like cheap Japanese food, got one near Leisure Mall, go during the weekdays and you just need to pay RM 10 for a set lunch which has a soup, chawan mushi, main dish (different dishes depending on the day you go there), rice and dessert consisting of yam jelly and fruits...you have to drive past Leisure Mall...forgot which turning already lolz...I just know it is after Leisure Mall, and it is located on the second floor...the first floor is a shop called Kim Kopitiam, also highly recommended...actually that area has a lot of very very nice food, if you can find it...

    Will write more here once I find out more...every Tuesday is my lunch out time with my colleagues and they always bring me to nice places to eat
