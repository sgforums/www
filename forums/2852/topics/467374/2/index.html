    Initially, I depended alot on own transportation. I was driving a Malaysian registered car and I would park the car for free at any area in JB. However, later on I decided to purchase season parking in Plaza Seni in JB near the JB checkpoint because for security and my own safety. After parking my car, I would then commute in and out of JB and Singapore by public buses. I find it faster for buses to reach JB because the bus lanes to JB is quite clear of other vehicles in comparison to car lane. I used to commute in and out of JB and Singapore by KTM train too in the past when the railway station at Tanjong Pagar was still open and run by KTM. Its even easier and cheaper to travel by that means in the past...it only cost me only RM123 for a to and fro season pass a month. I would travel by KTM train all the way to Tanjung Pagar Railway Station and go to my workplace from there. So before KTM train closed its operation at Tanjung Pagar Railway Station, I managed to ride the KTM train for the last time and it was a historical train journey for me. Well, now I don't depend on my car to commute to JB eversince it brokedown three years ago. Intially, I felt like patah kaki or broken legs for not having my own car. I started and learned to take public buses from Skudai to JB and then to Singapore and vise versa going home to Skudai. I need to walkout to the mainroad at Skudai which will take me about 15 to 20 minutes walk and at the mainroad there are many buses for me to take to JB. In the past, when I was still working full time in Singapore, it would take me about 2.5 hours to reach my workplace. Now, eversince I go into semi-retirement and do part time work in Singapore, I don't travel much to Singapore and I don't have to rush. I take my time. I'm much more relax and happier now. About applying Malaysian citizen probably not but PR I don't mind. Its better to hold a Singapore passport because you can travel to most countries without a visa.

    I think Malaysia is like any other country - there is a little higher crime rate. I been driving and exploring whole of malaysia - down most of the road and area. It is very safe as long as you are street wise. Do not dress too flashy, drive expensive car, wear expensive watch, carry too much cash in wallet ....etc Do not go to quiet places, lane, alley or restuarant or go to quiet shopping center. In shopping center - avoid quiet corners and quiet toilet. In car lock your door. Do not service your car or wash car there - usually car theft - are your key been duplicated. Do not pump at quiet petrol stations. When driving or walking - be aware if you been followed - if so go to crowded places for protection, like police station, shopping center, where there is lot of people, if need ask for help from security or staff. I once have been followed before by a red small car, i purposely turn a few turn to confirm that . For this type of case, alway keep to the big road with lots of car, keep turning around in the city big road with lots of cars - soon he be left behind in a traffic light. Ensure the tail is totally lost before you proceed to your destination.
