    Surprisingly, my dad had left early for work today and took the car with him, leaving me no choice but to take the bus for 2 consecutive days.

    Being half an hour early before a morning class may prove to be a costly mistake because no one would like to bask under the hot morning sun and beaded droplets of sweat cascading your face.
    Still, i persisted to meet her first.

    Seconds ticked by and the heat became intolerable, i decided to take a cool cup of Coke to quench my thirst and cool down my body.

    "Ker-plunk. 10 cents. Ker-plunk. 20 cents. Ker-plunk. 30 cents."

    "Damn! I am short of 10 cents,"i muttered silently as i search both the pockets of my jeans.

    "Ker-plunk.Swoooooooosssshhhh"

    "There you go.10 cents"

    A familiar voice protruded heavenly to my tender eardrums as i was still searching for the much-needed 10 cents.

    I turned around and there she was. The goddess of my love. The guardian of my dreams. The ink in my printer. The blood in my veins.It was Isabelle.

    "Errr. Thanks. You just arrived?" i asked trying to mask the embarrasment earlier.

    "Yeah. My dad sent me here before going to work. What about you?" she asked me temporary blinding me with the glow from her pearlies.

    "My dad? Lets not talk about him. He doesn't care about my family. Hey! Thats the teacher. I think we better go in now" i answered rather emotionlessly."

    It was fun having her as my classmate and new friend. We helped one another and even shared some secrets though we knew each other for only 2 days.

    "Hey Isabelle. I'm just wondering whether you are free tonight? I kinda wanna ask you out for dinner", i asked after finally mustering enough courage.

    "Sorry Andy. I have plans tonight with my family. Some other day perhaps", she replied.

    "Its ok. Maybe you can treat me to dinner first. Haha.", i teased.

    The whole day went great and alas, it was time for us to go our separate ways once again. I remembered about the dinner Dad had asked us to go and immediately called my brother so could meet him first before going to La Creatuix De Pignon at Shenton Way.

    We arrived at the restaurant rather early as there were not many patrons yet. I asked the waiter for the reservations made by my dad and was shocked to see him requesting for a table of 5 despite having only 3 people in our family.

    We were ushered to our seats and waited rather patiently for my dad and his guests to appear. Never did we expect playing arm wrestling in a posh restaurant would be a great idea and a great time killer.

    "ANDY!!!"
