    anyway just some general guidelines that i've picked up about portraiture:

    - keep ur DOF shallow (big aperture) to isolate ur model form the background.

    - avoid unflattering perspective distortion, that is try not to use wide angles, nose will look bigger than the rest of the face becos it's closer to the lens.

    - take note of shadows, use reflectors and/or fill flash to get rid of them.

    i maybe using a SLR, not really the FM2, any suggested aperatures and shutter speeds?
    Generally as what jOhO said,

    -shallow DOF
    Open your aperture as big as possible, eg f/2.8
    Too shallow DOF also bad 'coz later in the picture eyes sharp but nose and ears blur?

    -use telephoto lens, eg 200mm
    Compress your model's features; especially those with wide faces as well as keep a comfortable distance from your model!
    Also crops away ugly background...

    A note though: at such long focal lengths, your camera shake will be greatly magnified!!! beware! so best to bring along monopod or tripod loh... Take note of your exposure time... for 200mm a rough idea will be 1/f, where f=focal length, where in this instance, 1/200sec!

    Wide lenses should generally be avoided unless you want to take your model in her environment... but background must be nice lah
    But dont make her look like those cute puppies from THE DOG with
    distorted perspective... keke... unless for creative effects...

    -use auto focus + metering rather than FM2 la
    Allows you more time to compose the picture; personally I prefer to learn about composition first then the exposure and focusing... up to you

    -time of day
    If take 12noon (afternoon sun) the light is too harsh... beware of ugly shadows (especially 'eyebags' ) Hide in shade and take loh
    Use reflector...

    -film
    I use Fuji... how about you?
    Anyways, get ISO400 for general case la... dunno what time you taking pic and what kind of weather also...

    Email me if you need help
