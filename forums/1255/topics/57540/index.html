    Originally posted by the Bear:
    what are the advantages of an SLR to the normal cam?

    ADVANTAGES only or both?

    ok i give u both. according to wat i know/experienced b4:

    adv of DSLR over digicam:

    - flexibility: able to buy different lenses to suit different needs.
    - lower noise at equivalent ISO (due to bigger sensors, NOT bigger megapixels)
    - MUCH BETTER auto focus speed and MUCH less shutter lag. i'll give u an example of shutter lag: i was shooting hummingbirds at jbp, framed my camera to wait for the bird to face me or look at me. then when he did, i snapped, and guess wat? he's not in the frame!! flew away already. that's what u call shutter lag, not to mention u have to deal with reaction time as well.
    - SHIOK rating is much higher when u feel the shutter slap and the sound (but that's just a personal fetish of mine!)

    disadv of dslr over dc:

    - price much higher. u dun just worree about ur body, u worree about lenses, filters, TCs, extention tubes. investing in a SYSTEM more than a camera
    - big and heavy compared to digicams
    - not able to use the LCD screen to take photos (very important for many pple who like to frame using their screens, for me it doesn't matter i use my viewfinder all the time)

    eh.. i think that's all i can think of for now.

    hmm cold shoe?? never heard of that leh... but anyway a hot shoe is one of those things on top of ur cam that u can stick an external flash on. of course u can fit on a whole studio set up with lights and stuff thru that too but let's not get into that..

    the ISO rating is a sensitivity to light. the higher the number, the more sensitive to light. so film rated at iso100 will need more light than film at iso200. needing "more light" basically means having a longer shutter, or a bigger aperture, or both.

    the thing with iso is that the higher u go, the more grain u'll get (for film) and more noise u'll get (for digital). for dslrs, u can go up to iso800 with minimum noise.. for DCs if they even have an iso800 setting, they'll be so noise u probably could hand paint the picture urself better. ahhaah okokok exerggerating abit lar..
