    Taken with Nikon D100. At West Lake, Hangzhou, many of such boats can be seen patrolling the lake transporting goods and people across this iconic locale. This particular boat saw me photographing him, so he paddled the boat near me. Taken in the evening when the sun was setting.


    In Shanghai, many of the shops there are hidden unceremoniously behind stone walls and wooden doors. This one shows an eatery, although the food cannot be seen. I wanted to capture the rickety buildings there when I chanced upon this scene. We held eye contact for a while, as the local peered on the foreigner. Taken with a Nikon D100.


    Taken at Wuzheng, China. This is an unusual scene, as it portrays the state police sharing a light moment with one of the peasants of this small water town. The peasant seemed rather intrigued by technology, oblivious to me and my camera, allowing me the privilege of capturing this picture. Taken with a Nikon D100.


    Hangzhou, China. The many pagodas that dot the city are less powerful than this one, which stands proudly over the iconic West Lake of the city. Much of the tourism life revolves around this lake, making this particular pagoda a well-known one in Hangzhou. Taken with a Nikon D100.


    Suzhou, China. One of the highlights of my trip there was a performance held at a historical theatre. I forgot the name of the show, but it was about the history of Suzhou and required payment to enter. I was particularly stunned by the use of laser technology in the show, and even though photography wasn't allowed, I knew I had to sneak a shot like that. Taken with a Nikon D100.

    ---------------

    I hope you enjoy this series of 'not-so-recent' shots.
