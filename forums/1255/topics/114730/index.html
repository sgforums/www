    This is a pictorial account of stuff I saw or went through my trip from 31st Dec 2004 - 24th Jan 2005.

    31st Dec - 2nd Jan


    I took this in the afternoon as we were making our way to Meulaboh. This ocean took the lives of many, yet it is so beautiful...


    That's a US Carrier. Abraham Lincoln if I'm not wrong... we passed by them near the Bandar Aceh.


    The whole tank deck was filled with these. Cactus mineral water slogan - Life Goes On


    Upon arrival, this was the first craft off the boat.


    And the recce team zooms off to find a landing site. We didn't get to land till the next or following day.


    That's lunch! In a microwaveable paper packet.


    The thing to pull when you fall in the water.

    I woke up the following morning with the sound of the chair rolling across the room and smakcking against the wall.
    I had no idea what was happening, the boat was rolling from side to side pretty heavily.
    Unknown to us, there was actually an earthquake around Banda Aceh measuring 6.2 on the Richter scale.
    This picture shows the waves hitting shore. I initially thought there was a wall there. I was wrong.


    Notice how the swells makes the ship look so small.


    The chinook lands! And it rained suddenly during their few minutes here.


    Leaving 207 to 209 after we collected spares. Turns out that I won't get on board the other ship in another 2 hours.

    I thoroughly enjoyed this trip. For the first 14days, I didn't know why i was there for. After helping the people, I felt we've all done something meaningful to help the people there.

    What we've done is just provide relief. What they need now is to start rebuilding. I was surprised to know there are people (NGOs) who volunteer as an individual, it makes me feel happy to know there are people like them around.
