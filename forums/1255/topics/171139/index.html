    27 Dec 05, 22:31
    Cameras capture light to form photographs.

    The camera is able to capture more light when you use the flash.
    As a result, the shutter speed, speed at which the image is registered onto the sensor, can be faster.
    If the shutter speed is faster than you shaking your hand, you will not get a blur picture.



    Therefore, if you have a camera which manual settings, you can choose to shoot photos at high shutter speed to minimize blue, if you have shaky hands

    when the flash turns on, as parka mentioned, more light enables a faster shutter speed. but it's kinda the other way around...

    BECOS the cam knows there'll be more light, it will fix ur shutter speed at a "handholdable" speed, which is "industry standard" at 1/60s.. then the rest of the light needed will be supplied by the flash (provided 1/60 is underexposing the shot). if 1/60 is enuff, or even over exposing the shot, and auto camera will not fire the flash, of if the flash is forced to fire, it will stop down the aperture (i won't go into that here).

    so, there would be times when u get a clear pic, the flash fired, but it's still dark. that's becos the flash is prob at it's max, and can't go any brighter. wat u can do then is (for auto cams) just one thing, GO CLOSER.

    for manual cameras, u can set ANYTHING you want. u can have a slow shutter with flash and get some different effects with slow sync and stuff like that.
