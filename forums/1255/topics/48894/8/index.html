  • the Bear
    16 Jun 03, 11:06
    slowly, slowly

    don't form an opinion on it quickly... mostly, lots of things go into appreciating red wine... firstly, you need good stuff...

    a good red needs some time to "breathe"... a bit of oxidising... some speed up the process by pouring it into a decanter... some swirl the wine in the glass (that's why you see the people "playing" with their wine)

    appreciate the stuff by having a clean palate... smell the aroma of the wine first.... take a small sip and sort of (for want of a better description) "suck the stuff through your teeth" letting the wine cover your entire mouth... enjoy the taste, aroma, the tannin, the fruity flavours... all that

    after some trial and error of experience, you'll kinda develop a taste for it, knowing your likes, dislikes, and what is good and bad stuff
