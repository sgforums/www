    oh.. totally forgot about this thread.. hope this isn't too late.

    1. i would post only photos that i think were nice. in this series, their generally taken from the same spot so i'd just choose a couple to post up.

    2. when u look at the photos, where does ur eye focus on? the trails? the sparodic lights? the barrier on the left of the frame? (in a couple of the photos). i find that the photos are unable to hold attention becos there's just so many things to look at.

    3. traffic trails are better taken when there aren't so many distracting lights around, generally speaking. of course, usually where there's traffic there's street light. try a higher vantage point like on top of an over head bridge and when there's more traffic. also, u need some REAL streaks, which comes from having more traffic, if not some of the photos look like the road, with something blur going thru it.

    4. there are some really wierd spots near where the lights are, i can't tell if it's flare, or dust. take note of that. sometimes flare just can't be helped, so it's good to take note where light sources are in ur frame.

    5. i'm sure u used a tripod, but i've seen sharper photos from ur cam. did u use a cable release or the timer so that ur finger doesn't move the camera when u depress the shutter?

    ok that's that...
