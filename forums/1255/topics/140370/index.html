    yeap u answered ur own question

    i think u should also know why the longer the focal length, the more prone to shake, at a given shutter speed.

    the rule-of-thumb is that u shouldn't go slower than 1/focal length. meaning, if you're on 200mm, u should shoot at or faster than 1/200. but dun forget, with digital, there's a 1.5 crop factor, so at 200mm u should really be shooting at 1/300 shutter, which is pretty fast and not always practical.

    there's where VR comes in. it is SUPPOSED to improve your shutter by at least 2 stops. so where u shake at say 1/125, u'd be able to go down to 1/30. it's hard to believe, but it works somewhat. anyway the reason for this lens purchase is more of the sharpness at 2.8 than the VR. but the VR (vibration reduction) is extremely helpful for smokers like me, who aren't very steady at the long end. just try to remember ur m16 firing days, breathing techniques, elbows tucked in etc.. same applies for long lens photography.. and anyway this isn't even considered long yet..

    or.. use a damn monopod/tripod.

    dun forget that camera shake isn't the only thing that u worree about when u have slow shutters. u also have to worree about whether u wanna record movement in your SUBJECT. so what if u can shoot at 1/4 at 500mm without shaking at all? the bird/person/flower-in-the-breeze will be totally blur if it moves within that time frame, which is highly likely.
