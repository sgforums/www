    whereas, for example, the tamron 70-300 f4-5.6 would mean that the widest you can open at 70mm is f4 and as you increase the focal length to 300mm..it will stop down to f5.6.     another example would be a constant aperture 2.8..for example a 70-200 f2.8..at any focal length, the aperture can open up to f2.8..and stopped down to f22 or f32..

    Originally posted by bismarck:
    stop down to 5.6 u mean the max u can go is 5.6 ?
    then why is it that my cam can achieve a constant f2.8?
    yah..means at the tele end the max it can open is f5.6

    for digicam..they are all made in a way such that they can stay at a constant aperture (of f2. throughout all focal lengths or they go down by 1/3 or 1/2 a stop to f3.2 or f3.5 at the tele end..

    its a all-in-one package. lets say..for a digicam, if at 10x zoom, the widest it can go is f5.6..and you can't increase ISO to above 200 for noise/grain will be very obvious, then chances are your shots will be blurred (handheld).

    cos even if its very sunny, at ISO of 100 and aperture f5.6, to get a shutter of 1/400 and faster to "stabilize" your shot at 10x zoom is quite difficult..

    and then..there are lenses with constant aperture of f2.8 throughout the whole range of focal lengths for SLR too..but they will be very very costly lor..for example, even a third party 70-200 f2.8 will cost >$1000
