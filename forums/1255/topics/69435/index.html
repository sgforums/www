    Perhaps you guys can tell me more about your adventures and preferences in photography? I find shooting negs very forgiving, because of the negatives' tolerance for overs and unders, while slides gives great color rendition, it is less forgiving in metering errors - over it by a 3rd of a stop, and things will turn out white-washed. Under it likewise, and details will also be lost in dark areas. So, shooting slides requires spot on metering of subjects. But with a digital camera, one can first test exposure values by taking a shot with a digicam, before committing a shot in slide. Because the digicam's metering principle is same as slide - +EV brightens, -EV lightens, while in negs, it is opposite.
