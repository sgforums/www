    (trying to learn some gongfu here )
    ahha maybe u should ask mother nature that.. it's just like that! no need to do anything one.

    i call it the "twilight hour"

    the sky looks very very very dark blue to the naked eye during that time everynight (in sg it's about 730-745 zhun zhun) and when u take a shot it comes out slightly brighter, becos of the long exposure.

    just take a shot as u would any other night cityscape scene... typically with a long exposure of 4-30 seconds depending on ur aperture.

    for us digicam users, it'd be around 4-8 seconds at F8 max (cos our cameras can't reach a smaller aperture)

    note: small aperture (small hole) equals BIG F number...

    generally speaking:
    small hole (big F number), more focus area (greater of depth of field) for landscape shots.
    big hole (small F number), less focus area (shallow depth of field) for portraits (background will be blur)
