    There's also Davidoff at Hyatt.

     

    If you're not used to cigars, Cubans (e.g. Montecristos, Cohibas, Romeo y Juliettas) may not be the best start because they can be too strong. Some Dominicans are pretty smooth and mild, and I would recommend Santa Damania Coronas.

     

    If you are going to Davidoff, though, try the Davidoff 2000 – it's milder than the Grand Cru No 3.

     

    Apart from mildness, there's also size. For starters, try a ring gauge between 38 and 42, and a length between 5.5 and 6 inches.

  • Gedanken
    16 Feb 09, 18:55

    Cigars aren't as easy to light as cigarettes - they're a lot denser.  You can use a butane lighter to light them, but a better way is to use a cigar match.  Hold the tip of the cigar over the flame and roll it so it burns evenly across the exposed end, till you get about 1-2 mm of ash, and then puff on the cigar before putting the flame out.  The cigar can go out especially if you smoke it slowly, so be prepared to relight it.

    And you don't tarik with cigars - you're just supposed to taste the smoke.
