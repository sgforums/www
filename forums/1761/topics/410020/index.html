    Watch out Hello Kitty... there's a new cat in town: Angel Cat Sugar set to become new icon

     

    By LIZ THOMAS
    Last updated at 4:09 PM on 3rd September 2010

     

    She is one of the biggest stars in the world, her brand makes £3 billion a year, but it seems at 36 Hello Kitty is the latest victim of ageism.  

     

        The iconic cat, which has been a favourite with little girls across the world since the 1970s, faces competition from a younger model.

     

        Angel Cat Sugar is the new toy on the block and is set to take the UK by storm when it launches later this year. 

     

    Deadly rivals: Hello Kitty, left, is being usurped by young upstart Angel Cat Sugar

     

        Merchandise featuring the new character is expected to be a must have on children’s Christmas lists.

     

        Already a huge hit in the U.S. and Japan, stars such as Lady GaGa, Dannii Minogue, Tess Daly and even Gary Lineker have been seen with Angel Cat Sugar toys or products.

     

        The new character looks similar to predecessor Hello Kitty, which is a white mouthless cat often seen with a bow in her hair.

     

        Angel Cat Sugar is also white but features a crown on her head…and has a feline mouth.

     

        Designed by Yuko Shimizu, the original creator of Hello Kitty, the new product range includes stuffed toys, umbrellas, bags, shoes, stationary and crockery.

     

        It originally debuted in Japan a few years ago but its popularity has spread to the US and now executives at Sanrio, which own both characters, are in final talks with high street stores here to sell Angel Cat Sugar in the UK.

     

        A cartoon character, global fashion icon and megabrand, Hello Kitty is one of the most bizarre success stories in modern business.

     

        Created in 1974 the little cat character which first appear on a vinyl purse, came with only a limited back story – she was born in London and liked cookies.

     

        But after more than three decades she has transformed into a £3 billion a year global empire featuring products such as dolls, clothes, lunch boxes, stationery, kitchenware, bikes and even a plane owned by a Taiwanese airline.

     

        Last year Halfords credited the popularity of Hello Kitty bicycles as helping achieve their best Christmas sales yet.

     

        But, sales in Japan have been shrinking for over a decade prompting fears her popularity could soon wane worldwide.

     

        Executives have been searching for a new star and potentially have found a new one in Angel Cat Sugar.

     

        More than 10,000 products are sold worldwide each week worldwide.

    Importantly, this time the character has a mouth, which means she will translate well in films and television series – as she will be able to speak.
