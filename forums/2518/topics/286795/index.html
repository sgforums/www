    World Cyber Games Means

    1. a global tournament in which sport is conducted within the medium of cyberspace, also known as e-sports.
    2. a rapidly growing international olympics in which nearly one million players complete against one another for the title of world champion in separate events.
    3. a gaming industry conference drawing industry leaders and technology experts as speakers and
    attendees to share ideas about the present and future of e-sports and their cultural relevance to society.
    4. a worldwide youth festival inaugurated in 2000 and held annually.
    5. the only single e-sports event organizing competition from six continents.
    6. the largest cyber-gaming event serving what is becoming the largest sports market in the world.

    The Procedure of Host City Selection

    The Procedure leading to the selection of the Host City for the World Cyber Games each year is determined after a careful evaluation process. Proposals are submitted by potential host cities, and visits are made to each location to inspect the proposed venues.

    The evaluation criteria for potential host cities is divided into four main categories : location/financing, availability/suitability of facilities, event infrastructure, and promotion. The following are the weightings given to each category.



    Each category is judged upon a number of sub-criteria that are all related to the information that is outlined in the following guide to manual preparation. The sub-criteria are judged on a 1-5 scoring system, with 1 being the lowest favorable grade and 5 being the highest favorable grade.
    These sub-criteria scores are combined and multiplied by their corresponding weightings to produce a final overall score. To qualify for the final phase of host city consideration, a minimum score of 75% must be achieved.

    The visitations to these final qualifying cities shall serve to eliminate any widely varying consideration factors, and streamline the final evaluation scoring process.
    This evaluation process is done internally by the WCGC, and results are strictly protected from public view to protect the privacy of the candidate host cities.

    Thus, the host city for the World Cyber Games is selected each year after consideration of a comprehensive range of factors to maximize the experience, impact, and enjoyment of all participants involved.

    WCG Ranking is decided cumulatively by the performance in WCG Grand Finals and WCG Pan Championships.
    1. General
    1) WCG ranking is updated once a year after the Grand Final of the year by WCG Committee.
    2) WCG ranking is based on the total of all points. If two or more nations have the same points total "3. Ranking Decision in case of tie" will be applied to break the tie.
    3) Applicable tournaments are WCG Grand Final and WCG Pan Championships. All WCG Grand Finals are included in the rankings, while WCG Pan Championships are covered from 2006 when Europe, America, and Asia Championships were held.
    4) Any records of cheating, warnings, and/or disqualifications may be reflected in the rankings as decided by the WCG Committee.

    2. Detailed Points Breakdown
    1) WCG Grand Final Point
    Gold Medal (50 Points), Silver Medal (30 Points), Bronze Medal (20 Points)
    2) WCG PAN Championship Point
    PAN Championship points are weighted based on the number of participating countries and their GF performances.
    - PAN Euro : 15% weighted, Gold Medal (7.5 Points), Silver Medal (4.5 Points), Bronze Medal (3 Points)
    - PAN America : 12% weighted, Gold Medal (6 Points), Silver Medal (3.6 Points), Bronze Medal (2.4 Points)
    - PAN Asia : 10% weighted, Gold Medal (5 Points), Silver Medal (3 Points), Bronze Medal (2 Points)

    3) Performance Point
    This performance measure is only for the Grand Final tournaments. Only players who advanced to the Single Tournament are eligible.
    - 4th Place : 6 Points
    - Round of 8 : 2 Points
    - Round of 16 : 1.5 Points
    - Round of 32 : 0.5 Points

    4) Win Ratio Point
    This performance measure is only for the Grand Final tournaments. The win ratio is calculated only for the Full Leagues.
    - 100% Win Ratio : 1.5 Points
    - 50% or higher, but less than 100% Win Ratio : 1 Point
    - Over 0%, but less than 50% Win Ratio : 0.5 Point
    - 0% Win Ratio : 0.1 Point

    5) Weighting by Year
    This weight is applied to only Grand Final medal points.
    - Current year : 100%
    - 1 year ago : 80%
    - 2 years ago : 60%
    - 3 years ago : 50%
    - 4 years ago : 40%
    - 5 years or more ago : 30%

    3. Ranking Decision in case of tie
    1) When the tie occurs, Grand Final points are compared to decide the ranking.
    2) If the Grand Final points are same, the number of gold medals, followed by silver and then bronze medals are compared.
    3) If number of all medals are same, the latest Grand Final scores are compared.
    4) If the tie is still not resolved, Pan Championship performances are compared.
    5) If the tie is still not resolved, same ranking is applied, and the tied countries are listed in alphabetical order.
