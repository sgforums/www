  • koosong
    27 Jan 05, 10:42
    Singapore E-Commerce still has a long way to go especially for the retail sector.

    I had placed an order for an Acer LCD display on-line with this company, Skylet Computer Corporation Pte Ltd at www.skylet.com. I had received the acknowledgement from this company via email mentioning the payment terms. In other words, my order was logged on with this company.

    When I called them for the confirmation about the delivery date, only then did they inform me about their mistake in pricing and thus would not honour my purchase order. The company offered to give me a discount from their list price. Now here's the bite, the price offered by the said company for the item is more expensive than the listed price offered by the manufacturer! I was taken aback by the offer.

    Anyone would expect a better discount from them. If the transaction can not be honoured, why put them on the web in the first place. The reason for e-commerce is to save time and to shop at my convenience.

    In my telephone conversation with them, I was told that the acknowledgement from the company on my purchase order isn't a business transaction contract. Also, I was reminded that under the Terms and Conditions, "Prices, specifications and availability are subject to change without notice......". Thus, there is nothing I can do. Now I end up having to pay for their mistake by paying the difference in amount for the equivalent specification display as I promised it to someone.

    In this respect, I wonder by stating such Terms and Conditions favourable to the company, will it allow leeway for unscrupulous organisations to obtain customers' data through on-line transactions? Also, will they use this opportunity to solicit for business?

    After this experience, I may not want to undertake anymore on-line transaction for local retailers.

    For a contract to be valid, there must be four things: offer, acceptance, consideration and legality. When you response to their pricing, you are offering to buy from them at that price. They can reject you or counter offer. However, since they have acknowledge the offer and possibily accepted your offer. The consideration is the price of the product and since this is a legal transaction, ( no illegal things like gambling ), this contract is valid.
