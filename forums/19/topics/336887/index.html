    You can try Mutiara Damansara where The Curve, Cathay Cineleisure Damansara and SEA's biggest IKEA are at, but getting to that area is not easy without a car. Meter fare from KL to Damansara is RM12. But when coming out, taxis at IKEA will charge a ridiculous fare of RM17 just for the 10-minute ride out to the nearest LRT station at Kelana Jaya.

    But you can choose to wait for the IKEA shuttle to and from Kelana Jaya LRT if you plan your journey well.
    From bus stop opposite Kelana Jaya LRT Station to Ikano Power Center: 9.00am, 10.00am, 11.30am, 1.00pm, 3.00pm, 4.30pm, 5,30pm, 7.00pm, 8.00pm and 9.30pm

    From Ikano Power Center to Kelana Jaya LRT Station: 9.30am, 11.00am, 12.30pm, 2.30pm, 4.00pm, 5.00pm, 6.30pm, 7.30pm, 9.00pm and 10.30pm

    The latest entrant to Bukit Bintang is Pavilion KL, but it will feel very much like a Singaporean mall. Upmarket goods which you can find in Paragon will be there, but the basement boasts familiar fare like Food Republic, J.CO Donuts, M.O.F. Izakaya, Pepper Lunch and Crystal Jade etc. Sakae is also there at Level 5.

    Recently I discovered the Japanese enclave of restaurants, right behind Pavilion KL on the way to KLCC, at Wisma Cosway.

    Midvalley now has a new wing selling upmarket products too (emulating Pavilion KL) called The Court.

    Other than that, there isn't anything new liao. Maybe you can try Berjaya Times Square at Imbi if you haven't gone there.
