    Abstract:

    In the business world, misrepresentation is a common ground for one party to raise against another party. A simple example is where a business retains a computer programmer to create a program relying upon the programmer's claimed expertise in that field. If the programmer is unable to create the program contracted, the business may wish to terminate the contract and seek a refund of fees paid. In doing so, the business is alleging misrepresentation by the programmer.
