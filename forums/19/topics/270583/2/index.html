    for those having frequent disconnections during bulk data transfer from the 2wire 2700 modem (regardless of its sub model) it is likely not due to the following causes:

    1] operating system
    2] programs running
    3] your line problem
    4] interference

    those people (minority) that have this 'disconnection problem when transferring bulk data' are probably running a wireless connection from the 2700 modem. it was reported that the 2wire 2700 modems (all series) have wireless connectivity problems with certain 802.11 (wifi) chipsets.

    you can do a simple test (if you're currently on wireless connection to the 2700 modem) by running a speed test @ speedtest.net. if you're on the 10Mbps service and your speedtest result (on wireless) shows you 5 to 6Mbps means you're most probably using a 'non-fully-supported' 802.11 chipset.

    these 'non-fully-supported' 802.11 chipsets are not 100% compatible with the 2wire 2700 modems. constant bulk data transfer will cause temporally disconnection of the DSL link. within minutes, the modem will do a reconnection and everything resume normal. if the constant bulk data transferring activity is still pressent, the modem will again disconnect and reconnect again.

    Solutions:
    1] DO NOT use wireless connection, use wired lan.
    2] Change the modem to a non 2wire 2700 modem. (not applicable for those on MIO voice, as there is currently no aftermarket digital voice ADSL modems available)
    3] Gamble your luck, try other wifi chipsets (do not ask me which chipsets work, as i have YET to meet a working one ...)

    thank you for reading this post. i am in no way related to singnet, starhub, pac net, microsoft, 2wire, etc ... feedback is not required ... thanks once again.
