    Not claiming to be an expert on hairstyling...but here is a tip i picked up form my stylist friends along the years..

    If you have an exact look that you would like to achieve, you will need a precise cut to acheive that look....and for that you will need to visit a hairstylist who is skilled in creating texture/lengths/face structure and understands your hair.

    Anyways, from just looking at the picture you provided, if you have wispy curly ends, you will need to blow dry (when hair is damp) str8. Perhaps even use a "leave in straightener" while blow drying....or even get a hairstylist to trim the ends to give it that razor edge look.

    I have the same problem you do....blowdrying it straight with a little wax already applied in solves the problem for me.

    what i did was to:

    1) apply moving rubber (the pink one) onto my hair and make it evenly everywhere

    2) use my hands to brush my hair from front to back, bottom to top, with the sides also brush back

    3) apparently, at this stage, the hairstyle looked fugly, so i gave up and went to bathroom to wash my hair with water and didn't use shampoo as there wasn't enough time and i was late for work

    4) use the towel to dry my hair

    5) look into the mirror and got shocked by the effect, which is something like water's motion combined with the power of the remaining wax

    6) simply press the sides a bit neater and the top a bit down

    7) spray a lot of super-hard hair-spray to lock-on the little amount of wax

    for the bottom part of my back's hair, i follow the style of the photo, together with sideburns.
