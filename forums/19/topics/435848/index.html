    What is Manuka Honey?

    Manuka Honey is produced by bees that collect nectar from the Manuka Bush or Tea Tree (also known as Leptospermum Scoparium) indigenous only to New Zealand.

    Bees gather the nectar from the flowers of Manuka Tree.

    In the hive, they add an enzyme called glucose oxidase to the nectar in order to preserve the honey. 

    When Manuka Honey comes into contact with body fluids, the glucose oxidase enzyme slowly releases the antibacterial hydrogen peroxide.

    The levels of hydrogen peroxide released are not high enough to damage tissue but sufficient enough to affect bacteria.

    Benefits of Manuka Honey

    Manuka Honey has specific properties that contain antibacterial, antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits.  These benefits can be used to fight infection, speed up healing process and help the human body fight off any ill effects caused through excessive bacteria

    The honey can also speed up the healing process for fresh wounds.  It creates moisture that allows new skin to grow much quicker than it would on its own.  On top of this, the anti-bacteria properties prevents infection, allowing the previously damaged skin to repair itself.
