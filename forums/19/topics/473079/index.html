    Spectators at the Bay Grandstand will hear the roar beneath their feet as the cars race past the picturesque waterfront stretch and drive underneath the Bay Grandstand, a signature feature of the Singapore circuit.

    This section of track has proven to be an Achilles' heel for many of the drivers as it requires them to negotiate four angled turns (16 to 19) in quick succession. In 2012, Narain Karthikeyan lost control of his HRT in his Marina Bay Street Circuit debut and crashed into the barriers at Turn 18 in lap 32, triggering the first of two Safety Car appearances. In 2010, another HRT fell foul here; Bruno Senna rammed his HRT into Kamui Kobayashi's stationary BMW Sauber that had hit the barriers earlier.

    The Bay Grandstand is perhaps best known for the notorious 2008 Nelson Piquet Junior incident that took place after Turn 17, in addition to bungled moves a year later by Romain Grosjean at the same location.
