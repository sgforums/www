    A stimulus is a change from the normal condition of the internal environment. Receptors detectthese changes in the internal environment. Impulses from the receptors are sent to the control centre or regulator, triggering an appropriate response to restore internal environment to the norm. A response counteracts the changes and returns the internal environment to the norm. This may be behavioural, such as moving into the shade avoiding the heat of the Sun, or physiological, such as an increase in the blood glucose levels. This response to return the internal environment to its normal levels is known as negative feedback.
