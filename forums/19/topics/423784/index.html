    Smart Play
    Aesthetics aside, why should you go old-school? Why hand a child a set of blocks when they can simply log onto a prefabricated, virtual world?
    For one, blocks offer open-ended play and only require a child’s imagination to use. Simple things, they can be used in increasingly complex ways as children grow. Unlike other toys, they can be taken apart and put together in infinite ways — creating impermanence and allowing creativity to soar.
    Children can learn such skills as sorting, matching, ordering, language, logic, spatial relations, recognition, organization, planning, and balance from the simple letters, words,
    numbers, and pictures.
    Blocks encompass all types of play — active, quiet, solitary, social, creative, dramatic, and manipulative. They also develop children’s motor skills, give them a grasp of natural physics, and stimulate problem solving skills. This is how vital pathway connections are made as little brains develop.
