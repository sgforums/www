    Well, since I'm at it and in the mood to write, I'll share with you how we planned for the coming one.

    1. One year ago, during our casual converasation, a friend of mine and and I talked about where we would like to visit. Mount Kilash came to mind. Mt Kilash is located at the western region of Tibet, near Ladhak.
    2. We got interested and started reading about the place. We found that there were 5 routes there. Via Kangar - Xinjiang (China), via Sikimot (Western Nepal, shortest but more rugged and hostile), via Qinghai - Xinjiang (China), via Kathmandu (Nepal), via Chengdu (China) and many others. Some were out of bound to foreigners.
    3. We narrowed down to Kathmandu and Chengdu as the rest of the places either requires a longer time or is hostile. Then we decides to go via air or overland.
    4. A drive thru Kathmandu requires a shorter time but we had wanted to see some site at the North. Therefore flying into Lhasa would be a perfect choice. Furthermore, if a new comer going to Tibet have not seen Potala Palace, it would be like going Beijing without visiting the great wall. Not that Potala is the grandest place, there are many place more beautiful than it but Potala is well known. Furthermore I would love to see Lhasa after 22 years.

    1. Next alternative was via Chengdu. We would love to drive into Lhasa via Chengdu but it will take us 12 to 14 days to reach Lhasa. It would be nice but time and money was a factor and Chengdu have daily flight into Lhasa.
    2. Then we started to work on an itinerary for both Chengdu-Lhasa-Mt Kilash-Lhasa-Chengdu and Kathmandu-Lhasa-Mt Kilash-Kathmandu. Our itineraries took us many ding-dong (more than 10 discussions and emails) before something concrete came out.
    3. By then, we would have worked out the time required (in days) for the trip. Next, we started searching for air tickets cost and timing(Chengdu to Lhasa is everyday but Kathmandu to Lhasa is Tue & Sat), a travel agency to work with in Kathmandu and Chengdu 
    4. The travel agency is to help you with the Visa and Tibet Permit. Without them, you can't even step into Tibet. By this time, you can almost fixed a date and an estimated budget.
    5. We have worked out that it is cheaper to travel by Chengdu and would be the best because my friend and I had intended to stay back in Lhasa for another week to do some more treking. We have to think about those who do not have the luxury of time too.
