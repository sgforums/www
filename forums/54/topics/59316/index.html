    Project Name: Project Clay: Moulding Senses

    Project Clay is a pottery, design & marketing expedition to Siem Reap, Cambodia.
    To help build a vacation training school that would provide an opportunity to the disadvantaged woman in Cambodia, under the new Non-Government Organisation (NGO), Pro-lerng Khe-mai (name is written in Khmer), to learn a life skill, such as weaving, sewing and pottery.

    This expedition, Project Clay, will require about 20 volunteer artists, designers or marketing major individuals and will run for approximately 18 days. The team will be led by an Expedition Leader and a Facilitator, and is supported by Singapore International Foundation (SIF).

    Project Clay aims to construct the platform where pottery skills can be learn. By building a training centre that may house the different stages in pottery making, as well as a furnace, promoting pedagogy of pottery. The team will provide basic designs (logos and patterns etc) for the handicrafts made by the ladies and branding the potteries, so that it is marketable in Cambodia.

    Besides the above mentioned, volunteers are also required to plan and carry out interactive activates involving the locals to raise their interest in the new NGO. Conditions will be basic as the team will be staying on-site among the villages and cultural exchanges are expected.

    As part of the preparation, volunteers are expected to commit their time in the pre-expedition activities and planning and other necessary meetings, which will run in October through the expedition. Other prerequisites of volunteers are:
