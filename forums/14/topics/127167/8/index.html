    My point of view.

    1) MLM is not all about recruiting. Although the power of it is so. Unlike insurance or property, MLM offers you to build your own sales team and manage them instead of selling alone. At the end of the day, products still must be sold. If not, where do you expect the money to come from. MLM does not equal to pyramid selling.

    2) MLM is just a marketing tool. Through words of mouth, the publicity of a product is spread widely into the market, thus allowing the manufacturer to penetrate the market while cutting down on steep advertisements and promotions cost. The manufacturing company in turn brings down her profit margin and shares a cut of her profits with her independent distributors. Thus through mass and volume, the manufacturing company also gets a fair share of the rewards.

    3) MLM is definately not a get rich quick scheeme. The ultimate aim of every networker is to source for a group of loyal consumers and creating a residual income from his/her faithful consumers' repeat sales.

    From a book.
