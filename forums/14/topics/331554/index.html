    In business it is about selling, increase sales, increasing revenues and net profits. So how do MLMs derive these profits from?

    There are 2 sources:

    1) From your own pocket (buy the product yourself or pay for an expensive membership)

    2) From the products you sell

    So before you join, you have to ask yourself these questions:

    Product: Do you like the product? Would you buy it and use it yourself? Do you foresee people buying it from you? Is it a good product? Would there be repeat customers? Is the product superior or even close to similiar products elsewhere? Is it too expensive?

    I hear all sorts of nonsense that the product is not important, it is about the system blah blah... If not where the net profits come from? From your pocket? Or you cheating others to sign an expensive membership so that you could have a payroll? Or convincing others to buy a lousy product that they would not even use and spoil your friendship?

    In a sustainable business, it is all about the product. No sales means you are in trouble.

    And please do the math, if you think there are too many sales people around you, you will get a smaller pie of those profits. Makes sense right?
