    This is what i think how it works....

    The company collects approx. S$ 25,000.00 as a membership fee from the new members. In return, the company promises you, as a new member, that you can use any of their authorised resorts for a number of free days per year. They have resorts in australia and a few countries for their members to stay. However, they dun own the resorts. They also rent the rooms in bulk thus got special rates.

    So, for instances, the company got 10,000 members but only got 100 rooms in this particular resort for the whole year. Therefore, it is 1000 member per room for the usage of 1 year.

    Their booking system is first come first serve. Whoever comes first, get the room with their desire dates. If there is another member that wants to use the room for the same dates, he/she will not get it as the room is already booked. That's if say all the rooms get booked at the same time with the same dates for occupany.

    In the event of peak periods, most of the members wants to use their entilitments, therefore only a small group of members will get to stay at their desire resorts and dates.

    However, if the room is unoccupied during a off peak period, the room will be sublet out by the resort management to other tourists. The rental made from the subletting out, will be discounted from the fees paid by the time share company.

    I hope this clear your doubts.
