Tips on Choosing the right MLM

  • JulianW
    22 Dec 06, 22:15
    1. Unique Product. Is it a new product category? Or old? Is the product patented, or patent-pending? Or not? Choosing a company of New Product Category ensures that you do not face a negative price pressure. For example, many companies are doing callback and voip services- OSMS, ICENTEL, REDCOM, ESMEGEN- so they have no choice but to compete on price in a technology (I believe) will be made free. Also, don't bother picking something that competes on quality. For example, whether Melaleuca beats Amway is debateable, but people will always remeber than Amway came first in its Household Product category.

    2. Realistic Payplan. 50% payout? 65%? 90%? There's a difference between high compensation, and high compensation DISTRIBUTION. Well, a good company will provide you with many ways of earning. And more importantly, it must be achieveable quickly, with the correct incentives to make you want to achieve it. Look out for Fast Start Commissions, Good Retail Profits of at least 20%, Car Funds, Expense bonus, Profit Sharings, Team Volume Bonuses, Leverage Matching Bonus, the more the merrier. Many companies like to dangle a huge bait, but leave you to starve as you try to grab it. One company even claim to pay 91% (but in actual fact, pays in Ringgit!). Never be fooled by numbers. If in doubt, ask a neutral!

    3. Financially sound company. Are they financially secure? How many countries are they in? It is not so much an opportunity cost to join a company that is already in several countries, than one that is only in one country. Start small, end small. Start big, end big. One of the most prolific startups launched simultaneously in 12 countries! The big boys are all backed by some huge venture capital fund or another.

    4. Good management team. Are the top management experienced just locally, or internationally? What results did they bring their previous companies? Have any of these team members been a marketer themself? If so, such would clearly be a "for the marketeer" kind of company.

    With all these said and done, a good company must possess all four qualities. Not simply a combination of two or three of them. Not sure if your evaluation is good? Ask! [/b]
