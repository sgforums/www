    Like all industries, there are good and bad companies.  Think it will be unfair to brand the whole industry as bad if there are a few bad companies.

    Arthur Andersen is from the auditing industry but we cannot say that the whole audit industry is bad because of one company.

    Enron is the energy company and yet we cannot say that the energy industry is bad because of one company.

    Lehman Brothers is with the banking and think you will know what I want to say.

    My take is to join a MLM company, it is to see if you will still use the products when you take out the compensation plan.  If you will not take the products when you take out the compensation plan then don't join.

    The other are just like what most MLM people will tell you:

    1. How old is the company?
    2. Does the company manufacture their own products?
    3. Do they have a system in place where you don't need any superstar?
    4. Are there success stories that you can see and learn from?
    5. Is it for all generations, be it an 18 year old boy or even a 60 year old man?
    6. Are the products consumable and something that can address a problem?

    These are some things that I believe will constitute a good company.

    Hope it helps.

    Eugene Goh
