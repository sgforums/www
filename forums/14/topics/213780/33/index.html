    Just my 2cts worth,

    1)  I believe the MLM business model works, but the general public might not be able to accept it. It also depends on the way how your company projects itself, how they recruit new members.  Having said that, i have friends who succeed in MLM and others who have failed.  Personally, i have been invited to Network marketing/MLM since i was still studying.  Even though i believe in the business model, i just don't feel right gaining from my downline.  That's why when my friends invite me to talks and to their office, i willingly oblige but at the end of the day, its guilt that keeps me from joining in.  Especially when i know there are people who throw in money but have not profitted from it.

    2)  I agree that some black sheeps might cause all the ruckus for VE/SE or whatever it is called BUT the company have to be responsible for who it is recruiting.  You do not just take any ah mao or ah kow happily as long as he buy your product or go to your talk or is he your best friend.  My Amway friend already gave up recruiting me but something he told me let me think back, :"not just askinng you to join this company, if you like you can just try the products.  We do not have advertisement and distributors like Walmart or other companies that's where we cut down costs."  Its that its the only way to compete with Walmart, by keeping the money within the MLM company and using all your own products.

    But when i look at the price... Sorry still a long way from Walmart...  A word of caution if you are trying to join MLM, do take note how/who you approach to recruit or promote your product.  My friend from Amway was a close friend in JC but when he 1st joined, he called all of us(his classmates) .  I felt sad when during our gathering he wasn't here and a friend asked," XXXX is not here?  Did he call you regarding blah blah blah"   and someone replied, " I better not answer his calls."  That was when i hope he was there to see how his sales tatics could affect his relations.
