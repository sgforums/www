    to add on a bit more, the law of agency applies in this case

    most of the time, when you sign up for a MLM scheme, the form usually will state something like 'an independent distributor is not an employee, representative nor an agent of the company'

    meaning distributors words are not legally binding, if they say something wrong, you cannot hold the company responsible

    which give rises to many cases of misrepresentations and inconsistent remarks

    thats why i dont always suggest distributors, especially errant ones, to speak up especially if they are not backed up by evidences

    another issue to look at is conflict of interest

    an analogy i found in an investment book is that of a stock analyst who gave positive reports about certain companies through the media, and its later found out that he invested in those shares as well

    a distributor already has an interest in whatever scheme he is promoting, and that should be taken into account whenever you invest into any scheme, same when we see distributors speaking through publicly available medium such as sgforums, we should take note too
