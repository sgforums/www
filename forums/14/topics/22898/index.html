    Originally posted by ahpeng:
    Isn't the above true of any job that we take??? Be it MLM or not?
    Erm.... in MLM, for every item you sell, a portion of the sale proceeds goes to the people 'above' you who recruited you. Hence its better for you to recruit people to sell, than to sell the product itself - when people sign up under you, you get a cut from their sales even when you sell nothing yourself.

    Essentially, something is wrong when more effort is spend on getting people to sell the product, than to sell the product itself. Even the act of signing up profits the people at the top when you pay joining fees and buy supplies to sell to others.

    Unless you got in at the very beginning, it is pretty likely that your efforts will only end up profiting others much more than yourself.
