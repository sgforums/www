    Amway,

    They are the No. 1 for 3 things:-

    a) The big brother of MLM and long history from U.S. Used to be NO. 1 in Malaysia too but taken over by Malaysian Company now.

    b) They have the lowest commissions payout in relations to their products price and sales.

    c) They are No. 1 in training. The term BRAIN-WASHING in MLM originated by this Company.

    You may ask me, if they pay the lowest commissions, how could they are still so successful ?
    The answers:
    Because their training and brain-washing is strong, so strong that people in Amway will not leave even if they don't make money; and they are good in looking for new bloods and young people who do not have much exposure to MLM.
    In fact, I don't consider them as successful if you look at the range of products they have, meaning their sales volume per product is relatively small.
    In Malaysia, Amway is so difficult to do that they are thought to switch to a "pyramid" method and will not reveal to a prospect that it is Amway.
    In Singapore, people are more intelligent and many MLM Companies start together, so I think Amway will not be as successful here as compared to other countries which they started when there is less competitors.
    My advice is don't join Amway unless you want to learn something from them and take it as a stepping stone, but beware, don't be brainwashed and start telling all your friends you will never leave Amway and that they are the best. This is how they teach you inside. I have seen many people said that and eventually find themselves very shameful to join other Companies and ruin their own future in MLM.

    a) Whenever I mention MLM, what came to my father's mind and other people is Amway. Amway is used as a punchbag for many people in MLM or outside MLM. Amway is always used by many non MLMers as a "gauge of the industry" although this gauge is rather wrong.

    For those inside MLM, especially Singapore companies, I seen many distributors "bashed" Amway as reasons why its better to join as "pioneers" in local start up. Their points are not wrong, but be cautious, whether its good to start local company or an international one, its best to consider all factors.

    b) Experienced is true in the first two points. But as for the brainwashing part, so far I seen one person who worked in Amway for 11 years and only earning RM2000.
