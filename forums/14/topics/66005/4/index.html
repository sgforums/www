    Once upon a time, multi-level marketing was a legitimate business which provided a way for small companies to get their unique products to consumers in small towns and rural areas which had no access to these products. At this time, the products sold themselves, and the multi-level aspect was a way of giving a small reward to those who had worked hard to build the organization. But the focus was always on the product.

    Today, and especially with the growth of the internet, it is possible for consumer to get about whatever they want at competitive prices. There is simply no real need for distribution Â“systemsÂ” as there once was, and indeed the focus of all the programs is not on the products they sell -- which are usually either bogus or are available somewhere else to the public at the same or lesser prices. Instead, the focus now is solely on recruiting new people to either buy into the program or else to buy products that are grossly overpriced (i.e., a $1 bottle of Â“herbal shampooÂ” for $26), with the idea that those people will recruit additional people who will also buy into the program or themselves buy the grossly overpriced products.

    Thus, today just about ALL of the multi-level marketing programs are scams. In todayÂ’s internet economy, there is simply no need for multi-level marketing or the overpriced products that they sell -- meaning that the only thing they are selling are memberships in anticipation that future memberships will be sold in the future, which is the classic definition of a pyramid scheme, and thus a fraud.

    The poster boy for all MLM 's companies is Amway, the oldest MLM which was founded in 1956. The Amway website claims that it has annual sales of US$6.2bn sold through some 4m distributors (meaning each distributor had average sales of US$1000 annually).

    Four years after Amway, Sam Walton founded Wal-Mart, a conventional retailer, in 1960. Walmart reported sales of US$256bn (41 times larger than Amway), employed some 1.3m people and profits of around US$8bn. Wal-Mart is the LARGEST company in the world in terms of sales, larger than BP, Shell, Texaco and almost 10 times the size of Microsoft.

    The battle for MLM has been lost where it matters most-in the marketplace.
