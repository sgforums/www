    They just drew up a list of all the 'buzzword' industries and claim to be in it.

    Well I'm not saying anything that a 100 other people have said, but anyone who is the least bit familiar with the industries know it's not so simple.

    Biofuels? That a high risk investment. Even of you make the right bet, it will take another 10-20 years before people get weaned off oil.

    There has been a re-emergence of support for nuclear power recently. Did the price of uranium shoot up? Not really. Even if they started planning for the reactors now, it would probably take another 10 years before the power station goes operational.

    IPTV? You gotta be kidding. IPTV requires huge amounts of CAPEX and generates very little returns. You need to deal with regulatory bodies. You need to pay for censorship. You need to pay the content providers. You will face the cable companies who may already have exclusive content deals locked up.

    But wait! Wireless is the answer! We'll use wireless to carry IPTV so we have low infrastructure costs! Using wireless to deliver IPTV is still unproven and it still costs a lot to build out your base stations/cell towers.

    The most successful iptv deployment right now is probably PCCW. They had several advantages including a large, pre-existing customer base to market to. Singtel is probably trying to follow that model with Mio. Incumbent telcos and cable operators have advantages that EmWhatever does not have. Look at Pacific Internet. Look at Qala. Both of them bought WIMAX spectrum to help overcome the face that they don't own infrastructure. Has PI launched a WIMAX service? Qala has launched but do you hear much about it? Why hasn't Qala launched an IPTV service over WIMAX? Don't you think they would do it if they thought they could make loads of money?

    Calling card margins are thin and there's pressure from free services like Skype. Also there are regulatory hurdles. Some countries don't allow VoIP service. Those that do require a license which raises your costs.

    They claim that EmBeyond provides, among other things, a complete range of consultancy and provisioning service to Hotel Managers/Developers. Hotel chains are very picky about who they work with. Hospitality is a very niche market with a few specialized players. Micros Fidelio pretty much owns the market for Property Management Systems. HIS is like AMD to Micros' Intel. Then there are the specialized HSIA/DVoD/In-room Entertainment players like Maginet, Guest-tek, Lodgenet. Financing these projects is another big challenge.

    Even if they had the expertise in all the industries they claim to be in, would customers be willing to buy products and services from them? They need to prove themselves and that won't be easy. It means doing deals that lose money at the beginning to gain market share. It means paying top dollar to pull expertise from established companies. It means a big cash drain.
