  • Phaze
    14 Nov 07, 21:34
    You guys are wrong when you say that MLM is good when done right.

    MLM is a fundamentally flawed business model. Even when you do it right, it's built into the system that a vast majority will fail.

    This is why the average monthly gross income for "active" IBOs in Quixtar was $88. This is typical. And it doesn't even account for costs - the time spent selling the product and other expenditures. Not forgetting that most distributors buy their own product. Most distributors actually lose a lot of money.

    There are tons of articles out there that explain the fundamental flaws of MLM. I will discuss 2 of the flaws.

    The first is market saturation. When you recruit a downline, you are really recruiting a competing salesperson. In a normal company, people are hired to do sales but hiring stops when you have enough salespeople to serve the market. MLMs have no such controls.

    The second is that MLMs are very inefficient. What is "passive income" as defined by MLMs? "Passive income" means you sit back, do nothing while your downlines sell. People who collect commission while doing nothing is very inefficient.

    Let's take a look at this Businessweek article:

    http://www.businessweek.com/smallbiz/content/apr2006/sb20060419_388805.htm

    "On a $25 grocery order, how much profit do you think we make?" he asks a group of new cashiers and stock clerks. "$12.50, $7.50, $2.50, or 75 cents?" Most are shocked to learn the correct answer is 75 cents -- and suddenly they understand the importance of watching costs.

    That works out to an average margin of 3 cents on the dollar, or 3%.

    Think about what this means. It means that as a reward for distributing goods, Walmart earns 3%. How much are all the distributors in an MLM rewarded for each product sold? Go sum up the percentages. And why would anyone want to pay such inflated prices? And do you really think it is the road to millions?
