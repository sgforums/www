    Okay, time to answer the question.

    MLM = Multi-level marketing, a marketing strategy that is based on direct selling of products to consumers.

    The seller gains a percentage of the profit made through selling the products.

    The product, basically, regardless of the claim is not the key.

    The key is the consumers. They direct their sales at friends, relatives and past associates.

    Usually the %age of the profit earned by the seller is fixed by his position, the introducer (the person who introduce the seller) gains a %age of the profit equivalent his profits for his position minus the introducee's. And this list goes on until a certain level where a person who will gain the remaining profits for the base sales.

    Thus, assuming A sells the product. A is supposed to gain 5% for the sales. This is direct profit for the seller.

    B introduced A and B is supposed to get 15% for his sales, B will gain (15-5)% of the profit.

    C introduced B but C is at the same position as B, so C gains zero profit, but still accumulates points (which will help upgrade the position)

    D introduced C and D is at the highest position. D gains (35-15)% of the profit.

    This is called indirect profit for D and B.

    As D has achieved a certain position in the company, D will gain profit from the pool equivalent to his position say he gets 10pts.

    The total pool pts is 251, D will get from the pool, 10/251% profit for the sales for the month.

    That is pool profit.

    The products, thus, may be sold at a price triple to four times its original manufacturing cost depending on how the company decides the pool, gain and sales.

    The credible part of this system is that, it is indeed a fast scheme to wealth as long as you're the first few top seller.

    Your network, of introducees, will actually, work to increase your sales volume.

    The problem with this system, sellers may be overly concerned over sales and thus vent their frustration on family, friends who had taken up their so called precious time listening but refusing to purchase.

    Ok, so think of a franchise.

    A corporation, let's say McDonalds sells a franchise or a store to someone
    who wants to own his own business, let's name him AL.

    Al pays McDonalds 2 million for the rights to own the franchise. And in turn
    Mcdonalds tells him where Al's store will be and also gives Al a box of instruction of how to prepare food and the guidlines for marketing and advertising. In addition,
    Al has to buy all of his supplies and food from corporate at wholesale.
    Al's job is to get customers and make money. However, Al also has to turn around and give McDonalds Corporation a royalty, some corporations are up to 40%.

    So, Al has to get enough customers to pay Mcdonalds first and then himself. It takes about 5 years.

    McDonalds has thousands of franchises all over the world collecting royalties from all of them.

    What if Al went to his friend and said "hey, I have this great business with great burgers you should do it too". His friend does the business too, and also pays McDonalds their royalties. Do you think Al gets a bonus for finding them a new business owner....NO!

    Al and his friend get customers, pay their royalties and Mcdonalds is happy.

    And then Mcdonalds comes along and says "hey Al, you have a lot of business on this corner. We are going to open another one across the street, do you want to buy it? Unfortunately, Al doesn't have enough to buy, so McDonalds turns around
    and sells it to someone else, cutting Al's profits in half!!

    In network marketing you are the corporation. You start your business just like Al did, however your cost is a fraction of a franchise, you find customers who may or may not be friends and family because that is who you know.

    You also buy product at wholesale and when you buy a big amount of product
    you get a bigger discount.

    Some of your customers will like the products so much that
    they will want start their own business. They pay their distributor fee
    at the fraction of the cost of a franchise. And when they do, guess what, you get to earn royalties
    from all of their sales just like McDonalds does, because you introduced them
    to the product.

    You do not have any territories or limits. Your income is dependent
    upon how many customers you have and if you've help other people
    start their business you get paid on that as well.

    If you put those same products on a store shelf, you have
    to consider all of the middle men involved. EVERYTHING
    is sold at a higher than manufactured cost whether
    it's on the store shelf, sold through franchise or network marketing.
    It's just another business model.

    Take a look at this video on the top middle of this web page:
    It will give you an explanation of the MLM business model.

    http://www.anmnow.com/streamofincome/index2.shtml

    You will find out that MLM is the most ethical way to do business.

    Enjoy!
