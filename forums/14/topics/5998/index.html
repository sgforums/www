Are human beings typed or unique?

  • rae-lyn
    5 Apr 00, 16:48
    Today I took the MBTI. For those who don't know what that is, it's the Myer-Briggs Type Indicator, which technically tells me who I am, what I'm good at...etc.

    I thought it was quite good stuff, initially. It described me to some accuracy, told me my strong points and my weaknesses, how I could improve upon them and my most suited career paths. Yet upon reflection, the indicator just shows that we are a classifiable type. That none of us are individuals, that we are all of a certain character type. Yes, there are some who are more extreme in their type than others, but still, we are all one of the 16 different types.

    That got me thinking for a while, that I've always believed human beings to be superior, that we all have different characters, mindsets and moral standards. Yet this test begs to differ. Perhaps in a small sample group, there are several different types of people. But all over the world, we have 5 billion divided by 16 character twins. That scares me a little, to know I'm more common than I think I am...
