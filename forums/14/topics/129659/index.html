    [OK, i must admit at this point that i am not a singapore lawyer, and i have not done much research into Singapore law.]

    But i believe the UK's Unfair Contract Terms Act, and the Sale of Goods Act 1979 are currently applicable in Singapore. I do not remember the relevant sections, but i seem to remember that indemnity clauses need to meet a reasonableness "test".

    If you are looking into the law, i suggest you look into those 2 statutes. AFAIR, they are the more important statutes where one is dealing as a consumer, and where exclusion clauses are concerned.

    Further, in my uninformed opinion, it is a settled principle of law that no one can exclude the courts jurisdiction.
