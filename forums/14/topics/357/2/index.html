    Of course I would strongly encourage anyone to sign on, only if they like the job. That's the key. No point signing on and regretting and fretting about it.

    If your concern is about remuneration, I'm sure SAF will pay sufficiently well with their SAVER scheme. Moreover, during times of recession, what better alternatives do you have? In Taiwan, the enlistment numbers increased tremendously because of the economic problem.

    But then again, it really depends which vocation you are in and how fit you are. Combat and technical vocations are among the highest. If you are highly unfit, don't sign on unless you intend to stay stagnant in your ranks.

    These are the few things that you need to pay attention to, if you are intending to sign on with SAF
