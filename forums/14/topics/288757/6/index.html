    Dispute with Singapore Business Pages over advertising contract
    I HAD a very terrible experience with Singapore Business Pages (SBP) recently.
    My father received a letter from what he thought was Yellow Pages asking to verify his company's particulars. He signed it and faxed it back.

    A week later, we received a tax invoice from SBP, asking us to pay $4,547.50 within seven days. The letter that my father had signed and faxed was an advertisement contract and is supposed to be valid for two years with one payable edition per year.

    We wrote in to request cancellation and explained that we had no intention to take up the advertisement and that we could not afford to pay the fee.

    Despite various e-mail, letters, faxes and tele-conversations with them, they insisted that they will only allow us to cancel the contract if we pay a termination fee of $1,062.50.

    My dad works alone, running a small firm and is illiterate. He does not understand the terms and conditions stated in the letter. The logo on the SBP form was square and yellow, very similar to the logo of Yellow Pages. The terms and conditions stated therein were in very fine print. The amount was also in small print and the currency was written as 'SGD' instead of just a dollar sign ($), of which the recipient might probably have noticed if it was written as $4,250 instead of SGD 4,250. The entire contents of the letter were very ambiguous and misleading.

    Since the publication is not even out yet, I wrote in to them again requesting them to cancel the contract without imposing any termination fee but I am willing to compensate them by paying a nominal fee of $100 for the inconvenience caused.

    We also do not see why we should pay since we did not engage their services. No artwork, logos, company's description, et cetera, have been supplied to them for the so-called 'advertisement'.

    In spite of all the counter proposal and explanations, we received reminder invoices and a final reminder letter from SBP saying that should we not pay up the full advertisement fee of $4,547.50 within seven days from the date of the reminder (which is due on Sept 25), they would hand the matter to their legal adviser to recover the full invoiced amount, including legal fees and interest.

    We tried to seek help from Case but it is not able to handle it because this is a business-to-business dispute. We went to Singapore Mediation Centre and, unfortunately, they cannot come into the picture unless both parties agree to go down to see the mediator to resolve the dispute.

    Today, I found out from the 'Legal Assistant Blog' as well as some other websites that I am not the only victim. In fact, many have lodged complaints against Singapore Business Pages (also called Asian Business Directories Pte Ltd). Attention was brought to Case, the police, the Commercial Affairs Dept, Singapore Mediation Centre, commercial fraud centre, et cetera, by different victims. But it seems they cannot do much about it.

    I really hope the authorities can step in to prevent this company from doing the same thing to more business owners.

    Cindy See Seow Hwee
