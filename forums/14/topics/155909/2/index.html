    I think the rule of thumb should be: if you are not convinced, don't join in.

    However if the risk is small (eg US10 a month), would you consider taking that risk to see if you can make it? Sometimes its all about making calculated risks if you have done as much research as you can but havent found all the factors or have that niggling doubt because you don't know what question to ask.

    Because of high joining costs, MLM participants sometimes or more likely most of the time are pressured by themselves to recruit more people to recover their cost. But the real cost of this is the potential loss of friendships. IMagine your friend alwas bugging you to join his mlm. The MLM fails in human economics.

    Thus, I think that if you want to join an MLM, consider if you will be pressured to recover your costs (weigh in the factors of high startup cost and recurring costs) and if you will feel pressured, its likely your upline will be feeling pressured too. So put yourself in his shoes. I think this is one way to find a good MLM but I am open to comments.

    Don't be swayed by people who claim they can make so much money in a month. Do more in depth research. Its good that the company has such good testimonials. The difficult part is when you join, it may takes a long long time for you to reap the income that the claimant once claimed he had. So join only if you have the strong will to wait it out. The higher the return, usually the longer the wait. After all its not some get rich quick scheme.

    Last time, it was buyer beware. Now, it is joiner beware.

    As i previously posted some guidelines to joining that you may want to follow, though its my own opinions.

    Good luck to you all in achieving your financial goals, regardless of the path you take.
