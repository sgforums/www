    Speaking of MLMs...

    It's really nothing but just money making based on subtle conning, cheating there and about. The one that truly profits, are the ones on the top, the pioneers.

    I've seen countless products offered by MLMs, most being health products. And being a Health Science major, I can only say that these products are at worst, harmful (in the long term), to being harmless...and benefit-less, to beneficial - at astronomical prices.

    Products I've come across so far...

    Protein weight loss plans
    -> Based on a pure protein diet, up to 80% calories from protein. All meals are replaced with 'meal packages'. Total amount of calories offered from the meal replacements are usually less than 1800kcal/day. This is by far the most harmful product offered. Why? Do your homework.

    Ephedra supplements
    -> Another weight loss product, sometimes used as an adjunct to intensive weight/physical training. Ephedra was previously pending to be banned by FDA. Whether or not this substance is harmful in typical doses remain to be seen. A powerful stimulant, similar to caffeine. Long term use could lead to anxiety disorders.

    Obsolete/bogus supplements (chitosan)
    -> Some MLMs still sell products with claims that have already proven to be obsolete. Chitosan is one of them.

    Probiotic supplements
    -> This range of supplements do have documented evidence of some health benefits. Can serve as a form of preventive medicine to gastrointestinal diseases. Alternative forms are widely available; dietary interventions can remove the need for probiotic supplements. Such as Yakult, baker's yeast, whole bread, fiber intake. Very expensive, and perhaps unneccessary.

    Water purifiers
    -> Local water supply is by far some of the safest compared to other nations. Boiling your drinking water can kill most bacteria while most bacteria are harmless to the body. What else do you need to filter from your drinking water?

    Cosmetics
    -> MLMs have a tendency to promote cosmetics that are made from certain essential oils, fatty acids, 'exotic oils' that could promote skin health. While most based on the Omega fats with sources from salmon, whale, peanuts oils could be harmless, these cosmetics could cost more than your Elizabeth Ardens. A small range of products based on exotic oils could be dangerous - due to allergies which are not uncommon. Some oils could contain chemicals that people are allergic to.

    After seeing so many products that are either dangerous, to of no use, to products with such high costs...how then I bring myself to sell these things to people? I have yet to see anything that is worth selling, much less be absorbed into the MLM industry.
