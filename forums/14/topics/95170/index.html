    441 has pointed out the section of the law I regularly referred to when I talked about ntiparis in the other topic.

    Please note that as there is no precedent of any contravention of the amendment, this section, as well as other sections, has not been debated or clarified in the court..yet.

    Lack of specific industrial regulations and standards in MLM allow many scams to exploit this section of the law.

    Let me open this section for debate.

    Take nti for example.

    By right, you are not supposed to purchase anything to join the company, nor do you receive commissions upon your downlines payment of registrations fees. The first action leads to the contravention of this section, while the second action point out the trait of a pyramid scheme.

    When I first entered nti for a presentation, I was asked almost immediately to purchase the products in order to join the company. Many of us have noted this, by referring to the replies in the other thread. Remember that you are always asked to join in for $288, products included?

    This become a norm, join the company, pay 288 bucks, join the company, pay 288 bucks...untill its almost impossible to distinguish 288 bucks as product + registration fees, or registration fees alone...

    Someone told me recently, in nti recent 'trend' in shifting its membership policies and refund agreement, its began to state that $35 is the registration fees.

    All these lead to few questions. Why do I have to purchase the $288 set? Why cant I pay just $35, and choose to purchase or sell any of the products in any combination I choose? Why must I buy even a set of anything together with the registration fees just to join the scheme? And fulfilling these criteria to join the scheme, my upline began to receive commission immediately, based on company policy of members purchasing the 288 bucks set immediately!?

    This is uncannily like frontloading and earning direct from a newbie registration fees as whole.

    Secondly, I stated this once, and I will state this again, a company exist to sell its goods or services Another form is investment, where in simple terms, you invest to provide capital for the company (legally of course). Its like lending people money, and you will receive interest as returns, in investment, its called dividends upon company profits. That company of course use your money in the operation of selling its good or services.

    As many can see, the observation is that nti aggressively recruit. There is really less emphasis on the products. Some agents blatantly said products doesnt matter. I know MLM is also about earning great income, but you DO NOT forgo selling things.

    Going back to the law, nti does not legally break the law. It has goods, it has cleverly differentiate registration fees and product purchase. But it does not resolve the issue of the agents agressive recruitment nor the policy of tying up products purchase with registration fees and participation. It does not hide the fact many agents agressively recruit people, and sales are made thru this recruitement, and the monthly $50 input.

    Remember that aromatheraphy and other stuff nti sell are what we called oestantious good, or luxury good. Being highly priced and without any obvious beneficial value, how are some of the agents able to sell and earn so much, especially when their target market seems to be teens without much money? Some, without batting an eyelid, could tell you that it all comes down to recruitment, not products.

    All these actions lead to one thing. There is a lack of a lot of physical evidence in terms of company written documents, and the existence of good makes it hard to clamp down on nti because of this very section of the law. Unless you got hard evidence that members acquire benefits directly and explicitly from recruitment, you cant go banging on the police door.
