    What I read from the above post seems to be over exaggerated. I have been an active MLM business owners in 2 companies but what I quite agree is the tactics employed.

    Firstly I want to clarify some points:

    1) First understand that MLM is a business. When one is in business, one has to do some marketing.Therefore, on the business standpoint, its just part and parcel of business practices. Even working in a company when meeting with potential clients, it is purely social etiqueete.

    2) Secondly, its a people business. Hence, relationship building is necessary. However what so differentiate from other business models is its unique word of mouth sharing of its services and  products. This enables clients or potential customers to be well - informed about the products that are introduced. But before I went on, allow me to relate a personal account of my own story:

    I remember once i 'm having acne issue and so I went to a department store to find possible solution to my problem. The sales lady would brought me to a list of products and begin to provide recommendation. Since they are professional, I trust their advice and it turns out to be a nightmare. It does not work at all and it is expensive.

    Then I meet up with a network marketer. I was surpised when they began to analyse my issue and even show me the cause of my acne and how I should remedy it. It was then I realize the difference between a network marketer and a salesman.

    A salesman will sell a product that they never used themselves and their objective is to sell the product and earn a commission. While a network marketer is in a teaching and consulting business. Since their income comes from repeat orders of products, it is necessary to provide excellent and professional services with an uphold of integrity. It is their own business, do you think they will ruin it? A salesman on the other hand is working for somebody. Think for a while, who will give more value added in terms of quality and information to resolve your existing problems.

    I agree that they may be some who resort to gimmicks for profitability, and not offering what clients truly required, but certainly MLM is a legitimate business and is even part of the core curriculum of Harvard business school. In fact, I seen lives were changed after taking MLM, some from the scorn of society to becoming the prince of the world.

    When I think about it, who will give the less fortunate a chance to proof themselves in a realistic world where qualifications is necessary. Yet network marketing is a building people business and great friendship and bonds were built up as a result. Its more than a business, perhaps in my opinions, it has become a reform center. Why?

    I have seen familiess who came to an almost broke up to a restored relationship through network marketing. I have seen people who have lost confidence in themselves changed and had become great leaders. In fact, many who become successful lives a life of giving and service to others. They share what they have, their knowledge, expertise and support. In addition, the support and help from network marketers becomes a strong pillar of support for those in adverse time. People of diverse backgrounds stood out to help one another, sometime in the process brings about the concept of sacrificial giving that is lacking in modern society. In the corporate world, most backbiting occurs in office politics. Who will sacrifice to lend support to the 'slower' colleagues? And how many suffer in silence inspite of their selfless giving to their company ended up in a letter of dismissal. In fact, last week, while working in my mnc company, I was shocked by a dedicated worker who even willingly to forsake his lunch to complete an important task is scolded by his superior and are even made a remark of being not serious in his work. When I saw how upset he was, I feel so sorry for him. These I did speak out from what I truly saw and personally experience.

    It may be pure generalisation that it occurs in all companies but statistically, it appears 80% are not happy in their work and hope for more time with their own family, wishing for financial independence and being able to live their dreams. The question is who will give them opportunity and how can they obtain it? Companies giving you more time, provide you with financial freedom and ......? If there is such a 'lobang' let me know. In my opinion, what network marketers offering them is a golden opportunity to turn their life around, and certainly their motive is not to take advantage of as perceived from the message that conveys earlier. It appears from your letter that MLM is bad, but can you on the other hand lead these disgruntled souls out and provide them twith solutions....Some even with health issues and if the products of a network marketing company can help them, why not. In fact, I have personally research and having been in the industry as a network marketer and being a worker in coporate world, network marketing, I discover that mlm forms a social platform where human are able to come to a personal level where social interaction is possible given the fast-paced and virtual world that society has become. It is healthy and in addition, a perfect abode for solace....where there is no competition, but encouragement....and good products that solve our problems.

    Finally, before jumping to conclusion, and before making any criticism, unless you have alternative solutions for the less fortunates, enterprising and people who want to change their lots in their lives, please think first before throwing a wet blanket over them, dashing their hopes and dreams and missing some opportunities.

    The worst thing that can happen in life is someone without a dream and one without a hope. Don't kill their soul with a pure remark for words are powerful weapon. Therefore be wise in your words for one never knows how your words can influence and affect the lives of others.
