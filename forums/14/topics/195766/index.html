    16 Jun 06, 13:57
    Well, that is quite an interesting spin on a topic as diverse as MLM. However, it is important to keep in mind that there are MLMs that actually work. Firstly, your statement about "the top leader does not sell anything" is applicable for other companies other than MLM. The main idea of a proper MLM is both the distributor and the buyer both use the product and the distributor-buyer relationship is supposed to be that of a recommendation and I guess if your product is good, there is no harm in the distributor buying it. However, if the product is grossly overpriced even to the distributor and is of questionable quality, that is where the problems start creeping in.

    I agree about the value for money part. When people do MLM, they forget that they are in the Sales line. Market share, market value and market forces and competition must feature in the marketing of the product. The product in question must be of a certain standard, be of a reasonable quality and be priced competitively compared to competitors. It is ok to attend seminars and lectures on a product IF it is conducted free of charge AND the focus is on helping you explain the product, not to motivate you to recruit more people.

    MLM has been around for ages. If it was a profitable as it claims, we would have seen the rise of MLM as fortold since the 1970s. Why till today we do not see professionals and top management leaving their posts and proceeding with a career in MLM? When people talk about having cars, lets just do a comparison. How many people in MLMs actually own cars? How about those working in big companies? Most people holding a steady job with decent qualifications drive cars too.

    If they say they are pioneers, they should state what kind of pioneers are they talking about. A special plan/compensation system? Remember, pioneers are innovators in their field and from what I can see, the MLM industry is far behind in any new products on the market thus far.

    There are two sides to a coin, except in this case, the MLM industry has been falling face down on itself time and time again, weighted down by its many years of false promises and unethical marketing tactics.
