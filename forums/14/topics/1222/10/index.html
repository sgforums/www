    Firstly MLM is now legal in Singapore and there are decent people in Singapore earning some income from it. This is better than being jobless. So the problem is not the 'MLM' companies per se. According to the regulating authorities, the crux of the matter is 'people must not be cheated of their money'. So for those of you who put down at 'face value' MLM, I think we need to relook at it from the standpoint of 'another job'. Just like in the old days when everyone considered the 'insurance man' as con man, but now the image has improved and all of us know the importance of being insured.

    Having said all this, the industry is not without it 'black sheep'. Even reputable MLM companies have 'wolves' within, this is expected. LIke we have good cop and bad cop, too.

    So everyone of us must protect ourselves, family and friends by being more informed and vigilant about opportunities thrown to us.
    The test must always be:

    The financial status of the firm and individual of the MLM companies

    Their products and its efficacy

    and their compensation plan if you are joining.

    Every reputable firm has a financial report and nowadays you can check the web if they claim they are in the Stock Exchange.

    Asayo is a strange breed and from the description here, I would say it smells trouble.
    If you have committed money, it is pointless to gripe about it. File the necessary claims through lawyers or direct to small claims.

    Psychology tactics is always used in recruitment but like brothers here have done, keep to the point and if it does not meet your requirement drop them.

    Fair trading act is here so all of you must get your case to them and collectively it can be effectively be dispensed.

    Going to CAD is only when there is an obvious case of cheating. This may not be so, especially when you, in your own accord have agreed to the delays in stock or compensation.

    If we collectively strive to be informed, sharp and clear in our rights, you can eliminate to some extent such 'black sheep' of industry.

    Just my thots.
