    Oh man... that's just too funny.

    "the moment you say a product or idea is good or bad to someone, it is considered multi level marketing."

    As other people pointed out, word of mouth advertising, or leveraging on social networks, is not multi-level marketing. Of course, you are free to define multi-level marketing as the equivalent of word of mouth advertising, but that's not how MLMs operate.

    What differentiates an MLM is the compensation plan.

    So to use the movie analogy, an MLM is when you get paid a small commission whenever you recommend someone watches a movie and the person actually watches it. Furthermore, when the person you convinced to watch the movie convinces someone else to watch the movie, you also get a cut. It's the multi-level compensation plan that makes an MLM an MLM.

    The multi-level compensation plan makes your cost of sales very high.

    Why do companies try to leverage on word of mouth advertising or social networks to promote their products? It's because word of mouth advertising is very effective relative to the cost.

    For example, in order to market the Wii, Nintendo decided to specifically target Alpha Moms. Alpha moms, as defined by Nintendo, were community leaders who were influencers in their local communities. Nintendo gave them Wiis and helped them organise parties where the alpha moms invited other moms to experience the Wii. So for a relatively small investment of money, Nintendo was able to reach a much larger audience of mothers. The alpha moms were not compensated for each Wii sold, nor were they compensated for recruiting other moms to promote the Wii.

    Perhaps before you post, you should reflect on whether what you say is a valid analogy.
