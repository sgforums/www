    My this is indeed an unfortunate incident and i give my heart felt condolences to the families involved.

    Well, back to topic, I myself have been through the Enyouth recruitment process as many of you here have exprienced or read about in this thread, and I would like to give my 2 cents about it.

    Basically, I have no qualms with any MLM/SLM companies, how they want to run the management of the company is entirely up to the top people.

    But the main issue at hand is about ethics.

    In business there is no right or wrong but it is all about ethics.

    Is it ethical to "innocently" bring one's friend down to have a meal, when one's motive is not to catch up but rather to get the friend to participate in a supposedly good scheme ?

    Is it ethical to tell first time distributors to get "selling" exprienced, by using persuasive means to sell a.k.a share of joy of the product to one's close relations ?

    The above are just 2 examples which are are legally correct but just unethical in my personal opinion.

    Also, I would like to add this as extra information for anyone who is below the age of 21 and was under certain influcences made to sign a contract.

    I cannot exactly remember which statute this is in, bad memory on my part, but if you are below the age of 21, any contract that you sign is void if you can prove that the contract is not beneficial to you.

    But, i guess most companies would not bother with the hassle of bringing a person to court, plus imagine the media attention it would attract it a case like this happens, way too much bad publicity in my personal opinion.

    So, even if they pressure you to make payments, you can choose not to and they basically cannot do anything about it, if they go to the police it would most likely be brought to court (also cannot remember which court it is), and any sensible judge will question the need of a person below the age of 21 needing to buy an item ridiculously in my opinion priced at over $1,500. Anyway, the judge would also consider the atmosphere of when you signed the contract.

    Anyway, just some food for thought might have gotten a little carried away, sorry for the long post, and for anyone that wants to join any MLM/SLM companies, good luck to you all, because like any companies around the world, there can only be so many high flyers, do you make the cut ? Are you street smart enough ?
