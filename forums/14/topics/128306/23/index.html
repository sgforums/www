    ultimately the true mark of a long running MLM company can only be 3:
    1) product loved by consumers (not distributor driven)
    2) enduring philosophy of the company
    3) Unique Selling Proposition

    a lot has been said that products are delivered to the consumer direct without middle man.
    so the savings on the distribution channel is reimbursed as rewards to the distributor and his network for his hard work.

    theoretically a MLM product should not be more or less expensive than a product on the shelf of a supermart,
    however, experience tells us that many MLM products, shampoo, soap, cosmetics, etc, are 2 to 3 times more expensive than found in retail stores.

    the main reason is due to the compensation plan.
    in order to pay for the many layers of distributors, and due to the exclusivity of the products (and therefore a restricted consumer base), the products are marked up inevitably.

    this aspect really defeats the concept of MLM as a consumer-direct model of marketing.
    it still works as a marketing method, but the products are much more expensive.

    ... unless the products are uniquely different, and no substitutes can be found, then it can command a premium.

    ... and if the MLM company is its own manufacturer, it would reduce cost of production (unless a OEM party is cheaper and more efficient, eg. in China), otherwise, licensing 3rd party products to sell will increase the cost of such products.
