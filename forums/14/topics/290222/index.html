    Rise and Fall of the Empire

    Beset by all sides by people screaming for refunds, there were scenes of chaos today at beleaguered MLM company Sunshine Empire's Toa Payoh HDB Hub office as investors, spooked by recent media reports and ongoing investigation by CAD into the company's suspicious business practices, rushed to withdraw their funds from the company.

    Many were left fuming as the company had recently issued a memo on their webpage claiming that bank transfers from their company bank to investors accounts would take 14 days instead of the normal 7.

    Queues had stretched out of the company's office out into the lift lobby where this reporter was forced to interview several participants as many of the company's staff rushed helter-skelter trying to reassure panicked investors. There were emotional scenes as many people broke down in tears after being told of the company's imminent closure.

    "It's not right", some of the people, as young as 17, lining up at the counter to process their refund, said. "My parents invested almost all of their life savings in this scheme, now they can't even withdraw their funds".

    Many investors have threatened legal action against the most visible people responsible for this farce, including the company's mentor and adviser, as well as the company's president. At press time, they were not available for comment. A junior manager of the company sneered, "do whatever you want, you can't do anything to us, we are united! Our managers are all driving luxury cars and making 5 and 6 figure monthly salaries! Come sue us if you dare!"

    The attitude of a rat with nothing else to lose, standing on board the bridge of a sinking ship. Yesterday the king of the world, today the sinking of the Titanic.
