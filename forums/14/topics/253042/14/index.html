    I believe there should be tighter regulations against these errant MLMers

    So far the law only deals with medium, mean and mode income distribution of MLM distributors

    There should be tighter laws requiring them to prove income so that they wont mislead prospects

    I believe income proof per se is not regulated in the sense that you want because simply,

    "what one person can earn does not equate to what the next person will earn";

    and it is still considered a private affair regarding one's personal income - no one in any other industry will let on their earnings in published manner too...

    and if such "proof" were allowed, it may draw even worse conclusions that earning this sum is a "guaranteed" when we know that is not the reality etc...

    In fact the MLM Act stated very clearly that where any actual or potential income or benefit that can be earned or accrued via the mlm business must have fair records of max, min, median, avg and mode records that are duly audited by an auditor for each financial year. That means, if any person in the mlm business wished to claim income, they must ensure that those figures aren't plucked from the air...

    Singapore being a pro-enterprise economy, I don't think the govt will step in to make stricter controls as they would rather self-regulation in the market. But we'll see if there are any impending changes if too much unregulation harms the market eventually...

    Hope i make sense...
