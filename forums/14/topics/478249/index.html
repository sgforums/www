    Whether you're into network marketing or conventional marketing, the fact remains the internet SHOULD play an important part of your campaigns. 

    Obviously all of the hotel meetings, 3-way calls, and all the other MLM type stuff works. But it can rapidly burn up your time with tire kickers. 

    An automated system online will free up your time, plus you can offer training sessions to your team that you record once and ask them to watch. 

    This means you can enjoy your vacation on Bora Bora while someone in Chicago learns all about your techniques. 

    But when it comes down to marketing, there are a few fundamentals worth bearing in mind. Frank Kern outlined these in his book "Convert", which is well worth getting hold of.

    Far too many people thrust their opportunity down people's throat, and the internet has made that even easier. Truth is, 90-odd percent of these marketers won't be in business in a year's time.

    So it helps to position yourself and gain credibility. This takes time, and all too frequently people just can't be bothered. 

    You also need to identify someone's problem and offer a solution. Oddly it's not always about the money, but there could be other factors there that need exploring. 

    Share stories - your story - and seek ways to help people. After all, Zig Ziglar said something along the lines of: If you find a way to help more people, you will be richly rewarded. 

    So what techniques can you use? 

    Facebook is an immense resource, and chatting to people will help build your influence. 

    YouTube, Vimeo, and DailyMotion are other great resources that you can use. Cut a video a day saying who you are and what you believe in. You don't even have to mention your business, because you're becoming influential so people will follow you. 

    Write blogs. These are important because they'll be working 24/7. With hyperlinks leading back to your landing pages, you can pick up prospects even when you're sleeping. 

    Traffic exchanges. These are a good way to get eyeballs on your site, and there are loads out there such as Clixsense, Neobux and so on.

    Personally I use MyAdvertisingPays.com because it does as it says, keeping my online advertising costs down. 

    You can always look for places to place banner ads for your business. These usually cost money, and so it's worth shopping around to find a good deal. People who write e-zines (electronic magazines) for your target client base would be a good place to start looking.

    Just Google your niche and "ezines" - I typed in: make money "ezines" - and this got 471,000 returns.  

    Finally, you can always buy leads. The Warrior forum has plenty of people selling solo ads or offering lead exchanges. 

    Don't go overboard at first. Buy a few, see how it works, then tweak your message if you need to. When you hit a sweet spot, scale up! 

    Anyway, I hope this has given you a few ideas about where to get started. 

    To your success, 

      ~Steve :)
