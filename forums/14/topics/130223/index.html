There are so many avenues out there where i think people can be taught to understand what this biz is really about and not to blatantly condemn people like me who just have a stronger desire to pursue my dream.

    8 May 05, 04:56
    I disagree.

    To first critisize MLM, you must understand how it works.

    The structure is not the same as other corporate organisations, where you must have certain attributes to be a prospective employee of the company.

    Most MLM companies market their products thru mass distribution thru the avg joe. The crux lies then in the training. Its how you manage to train up your workforce of distributors. Unfortunately, this is not largely successful even in major legal MLM companies. The bad customers service and lack of professionalism left bad impression on many prospects and customers.

    Few other misconceptions including labelling ANY direct selling company as MLM companies when they clearly are not. In fact, so far, all direct selling companies that do stringently recruit salesman to do direct sales, and paying them commissions instead of basic wages, are not really MLM. So far i have not encountered one yet. and in anyway, such stringent recruitment would defeat the aims of MLM, which is more towards mass sales and referrals.

    Therefore, the crux would lie in training.

    To add, there is one forumer in sgforums who is a psychologist and human resource consultant himself, who has been doing work for companies in this region and Australia.

    He stated once, that sometimes, its not the host of experience or skills you have that will get you a job in that company. In many instances, its rather that you are skilless and its easier to train someone from a clean slate. It will be then easier for you to adapt to the organisational culture and goals.
