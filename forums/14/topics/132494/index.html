    Not all MLMs are fraudulent. You just need to be introduced to the right one, with the right kind of support.
    2 main things you need to look out for: Product and Commission Plan.

    Product:
    1.Price-Is it reasonable? Exorbitant prices like $367 for a magnetic pillow will instantly turn people off.
    2.Marketability-which group of people will this product appeal to? Suppose it's $500 for a bottle of miraculous slimming pills, only the highly desperately or gullible will be interested.
    3.Credibility- Is this product really useful? Does it live up to the qualities it boasts of? Do your personal research on it and NEVER be sweet talked by anyone in that business already.

    Commission Plan:
    1. Binary system: Are the returns from direct sales reasonable? Are the leverage bonuses realistic? The conditions for a 'flush'.
    2. Fineprints: Things like x0.8 for exchange rate fluctuations, x0.9 for shipping charges are detrimental when overlooked.

    MLM in actual fact, is one of the popular and more cost effective marketing strategy used in developed countries like America. The reason why it has been made taboo in Singapore is because of the small population and people who try to introduce MLM to the community often resort to unscrupulous means to gain sales. I wont name them, but it's all over the forum.
