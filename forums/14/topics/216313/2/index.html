    My problem with the binary compensation model is three fold:

    1: You are paid on the "Matching Volume" which means the lowest volume leg is the deciding factor. When you get a cheque, there is always a feeling of "Leaving Money on the Table" (not healthy for motivation).

    2: With the explosion of Powerlines using the binary plan it has magnified the fact that when people realize that they have to build there very own leg (powerline) starting from 0. It is a slap in the face, and then a slap in the face for everyone that comes on board. So the fact that the powerline has 100s of members is irrelevent, people feel deceived and that is because they were.

    3: The promised or marketed payout normally does not work mathematically, so read the qualifications carefully, there is normally a clause that states "A maximum of X% will be paid out in Commissions". It may never affect you, but I find it to be deceptive marketing.

    With that said, there are some good honest companies that have done very well with the binary comp plan.

    If you realize what to watch for, and are ready to "balance" your legs, and feel your team would be motivated to do the same, Good Luck.
