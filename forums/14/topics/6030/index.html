    its perfectly legal. just tat it encourages pp to look for other pp to recruit other pp to...(tend to infinity). while recruiting, the new fella pays a sum of money (say 1.4k to purchase 3 mths supply of health food (for 1 person/ i.e. 1 person spends 400+bucks on health food per month!) when this happens, a portion of this sum goes to the person who recommends...basically everyone on top gets a cut.

    this way, it encourages a person to keep recruiting till the ladder is established before he lays back and do nothing and expect an income... such, although gives passive income, is actually rather unhealthy mentality. the presence of a product is the only thing tat prevents the collapse of this business practice during saturation; and after establishment, the unfair distribution will exist forever, benefiting the top and to the detriment of the bottom. how to achieve equity? start another ladder, hopefully with the bottom pp on top this time. but chances are this will hardly happen as those on top will be tempted to start another chain with their spare cash and time.

    one major weakness in this marketing strategy is that it seems (personal opinion) that the company is not interested in selling the product but more interested in recruitment of pp to recruit... (tend to infinity-saturation). their repeat sales in fact come from this distribution chain (as a distributor is a consumer himself) the repeat sales figures of 80% etc are misleading as they are not from pure consumers. to quote them; an establish and saturated mlm business has 20% distributors and 80%pure consumers; at the rate (esp in the beginning) its more like 0% consumers and 100% distributors; this is a dangerous situation as they are not testing the real consumer market. moreover the enthusiasm of distributors towards the product probably has a bias component as they have to "like" the product before recommending to someone else... its their own business/career at stake after all.

    the products are usually very expensive so that the pp on top of the ladder get better cuts. but this also means during actual sales it will be hard to workout. (distributors aside, as they are supposed to profit from selling, not lose)
