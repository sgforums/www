    This is a good test for whether a network marketing company is legitimate. Ask yourself: "would I buy this product, at this price, if not for the business opportunity". If you can't honestly answer "yes", stay away. Another similar test, if you are already doing NM is: "If I were to quit the business right now, would I still continue buying these products that I'm now selling?" Again, if you can't answer "yes", it might be good to get out of the business.

    A legitimate NM company will have real products bought by real people at real prices. An NM company's products need not be the cheapest around, but they must offer good value for the money you are paying. A sign of a good NM company is that there are consumers who are interested in the products but have no interest in doing the business. These consumers have simply decided not to purchase, say, health supplements from GNC or Nature's Farm, but from the NM company.

    Hope this is helpful for those who are looking at the industry.

    'If the startup cost is substantial, be careful! The start-up fee in multilevel companies is generally small (usually for a sales kit sold at or below company cost). CAUTION: PYRAMIDS OFTEN DISGUISE ENTRY FEES AS PART OF THE PRICE CHARGED FOR REQUIRED PURCHASES OF TRAINING, COMPUTER SERVICES, PRODUCT INVENTORY, etc. These purchases may not even be expensive or "required," but there will be considerable pressure to "take full advantage of the opportunity."

However, little or no effort is made to actually market the products. Instead, money is made in typical pyramid fashion, from recruiting. New distributors are pushed to purchase large and costly amounts of inventory when they sign up

    Truthfully, there are pro and cons with every marketing plan. Binary itself is not perfect, neither is it lousy.The main features of it are

    Explosive growth - Less strain on recruitment
    Simple? system of building network
    Minimal sales

    If you engage in binary, and you have less friends who are willing to join you, your upline could put people under you, giving you less strain on recruitment. People on top puts people under them or their downlines in "two by two" style. So in the end you could have explosive growth.

    Usually people worry about building network in MLM. Binary allows you to put two people under you, and they each have two guys under them.Simple, less hassle

    There is nothing wrong in the distributors being the end consumers of the products. Even if your customers are willing to buy products, but not doing the business, they found out that once they have people placed under them by you,(becos of binary system needing to be balanced) and they start earning money unwittingly, they could be enticed to join too.Provided they have joined as members inf irst place to enjoy maybe the discounts offered to members for the products.

    Now here are the 'cons' point

    You need to do balancing act with your network. Binary system says you got to have equal number of people or sales volume on both sides of your network to be entitled to enjoy compensations. If they safeguard against this by giving you time to build your network, fine. But it doesnt matter. Balancing the network is a constant worry since binary is explosive and ongoing. For novice, its a stuggle.

    Pyramid can employ binary plan to withold money from you this way.

    Since your upline put people under you, and their uplines put people under you, a good company should have a reliable honest system to enable people to track their network. Most of the time, you do not know the people under you, since they are your friends; friends. You cannot possibly track their sales volume or downlines manually, since you cannot "see" ,unlike other MLM plans.

    Dishonest company can put up a bogus system to let you think you have many people under you, or maybe you thought this month sales wasnt good, when in reality, people under you could be selling their stuff like hotcakes......You wouldnt know.

    Minimal sale? Provided the products are good and readily saleable and consumable. You wouldnt want to buy a certain amount of products every month and just keep them at home. But if you can just buy just to enable you to meet the maintenance requirement, either for membership or to reap the commissions from your downlines, why would you make the effort to sell or resell the stuff.
    This can be the downfall of many MLM companies engaging in binary.

    One good thing about binary for disguised pyramid is that while its explosive, it can keep many people from knowing the company too.Sound conflicting? If I were to approach many people to put directly under me, many people would have heard of my companies. But binary is explosive for downlines too, and goes two by two, so not so many people would be approached, but you can build the network deep.Sound chim maybe for new guys, but MLMers should know what I meant.
