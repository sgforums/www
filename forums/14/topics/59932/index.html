    Hi Bear
    I start with your second question first. MLM products are not cheap. Personally, I would be suspicious of quality products that are cheap. But they are CHEAPER than it would be sold through retailers.

    Lets say a Coke cost price cost 10 cents.
    It would move from manufacturer to company, then regional distributor, national distributor, wholsaler, retailer and lastly consumers. Let say each of these guy mark up the price by ten cents to earn their profit, including the manufacturer. So we calculate to be 70 cents when it reach consumers. Lets not forget to add media advertising cost, let say 10 cents. So total 80 cents.

    In MLM, products move from manufacturer to company to distributors to consumers, each one marking up also by ten cents, so it will be 40 cents price for consumers. We cannot add agvertising because there are none. This is the simplest way to explain the MLM distribution system.

    Lets say Pepsi is a lousier brand, but it cost 30 cents. If people ask me why Pepsi can be so cheap, I would tell them "Look, Coke is a quality brand.By bringing it direct to you, our company eliminated 40 cents.'

    Greetings, the below info is meant for those who can see the potential of MLM as a legitimate business/ marketing strategy.

    The conversation regarding the compensation plan of ntiparis spurred my interest to participate again. :p

    Hmmm... As of today, from my own personal knowledge, there's currently 4 main compensation plans in the industry. 1) Stairstep Breakaway 2) Matrix 3) Unilevel 4) Binary (latest compared to the previous 3). Each of these individual plans has their pros and cons. But since the emphasis is on Binary Plan, allow me to share my 2 cents worth.

    One of the common misunderstandings revolving around Binary Plan is that it's a money game, get rich quick scheme that should be avoided at all costs, by any networkers. I must confess that I had that perception before, but after reading through some books and mags, it brought a whole new insight to my belief system. Also, the misunderstanding was brought about by many unscrupulous companies that employed the explosive growth of the Binary Plan in the past. To the extent of overpaying to distributors that ultimately results in the closure of such companies... This sure sounds like a pyramid scheme!

    If anybody wants to realise the benefits of Binary, you must look at it from a neutral point of view without any bias tendency towards other plans. One unique aspect about Binary plan is that it focus on 2, no more or less, income centres as your frontline. Which is beneficial if you were to take into account the amount of focus you can employ, thereby improving your effectiveness, as compared to other plans which recommend more frontlines eg 3 and more at one time. Also, commission will only be rewarded if one can balance the volume their 2 income centres. Let's say for eg, my LH group achieved $1000 sales volume, RH group $500, I'll not get anything if the company commission payout is at $1k. Until my RH group can achieve the remaining sum. It propels the upline to focus on helping the weaker side.

    Other than that, additional sales volume, sponsoring performed by the upline group MUST be added down the list. So those along the line will benefit together from it. Profits are shared among them. Hardly noticeable in other plans, whereby , upline sales volume will normally be absorbed by he, himself. That's when one WILL see EXPLOSIVE growth in their group, thereby giving Binary plan the bad name of money game... Also, there's no Breakaway from the group! Which is beneficial for many networkers who often have a hard time of re-establishing a new group all over again.

    However, some cons include no max. cap for the amount one income centre can earn. Some Binary companies employ the tatic of grossly exaggerated figures one can earn with no limits! Please be careful then, because based on figures, it will shutdown in a few months time.. Reason? Over payout of more than 100%. Imagine monthly turnover to be $1k but commission payout of 1.2k... So legitimate Binary MUST include a max cap. Also, some companies practises flushing of unbalanced sales volume. From previous eg. 1k & 0.5k will be flushed if you can't balance it!! A good Binary company MUST safeguard against this. Another point to note for any plans is the safeguarding against extreme front loading!! I've heard of many 'reputable' companies asking new distributors to pump in thousands of dollars for the inital investment... *faint*

    One more thing to note is the accountability of people, because of the explosive growth, it makes accountability of uplines and downlines confusing... Imagine I bring in a friend but he's placed 10 levels below me.. That's when you must find a good company that employs a good system! A good MLM company should employ such a system whereby your presense is not absolutely essential of the success of your group! Talk about time freedom.

    Reason why Binary looks like a pyramid because of the idealogy of income centres being added down the group. Also because of it's explosive growth since 2 is the max for frontline directs. But please bear in mind that income centres MUST also include sales volume, not solely on sponsoring! Which means there must also be sales/ customers etc. Guess those people whom some of you met dunno what they're saying, if not careful, they'll have authorities coming after them. However, this is something very contradicting, because MLM is a business, in which sales should be accomplished without door to door retail sales. If people focus solely on looking for customers, they're just doing direct selling & not employing the power of leverage... if they focus solely on sponsoring, it's pyramid... a balance is the best from my point of view of cos.. I guess it's safe to say that the people you all met merely meant personal sales is minimised but not totally eliminated.

    Binary can fulfil much explosive growth as compared to other plans, but the ideology is the same. The same amount of effort applies! Not just because it employs a different concept, it should be 'condemned' by many networkers alike. If you do look at it carefully, the emphasis meant growing 'deep', unlike other plans which go 'wide', as to which is better, I leave it to individuals to decide!
