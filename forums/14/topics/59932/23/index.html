    All I can say is this.

    MLM do emphasise on income earning too. When human greed or foolishness kicks in, even if the company emphasise or is trying to sell its products, members may override such system and focus on the benefits of huge income potential.

    On the other hand, companies may use products as cover up, I will point out again and again, when a business is set up, its sole purpose is to sell products or provde services, no matter what marketing system. The product or service cannot be secondary or bonus to the earning potential.

    When a company management, without considering its distributors, tries to focus on 'huge income potential' and use nice words such as 'investments'. it plays along the line of money game, where people 'invested' in a money system, churning out money to uplines, causing downlines who 'invested' to wait hopefully for the next downlines 'investments'. Distinguish this from the income earned from genuinely trying to sell products, where you are offering value for money products, and even without recruiting, you benefit.

    nti, in my opinion, has led many people to believe in few myths.

    - Registration of company = Legality

    Nope. When a company is registered, it only complete a few legal obligations and formalities. Frauds and scams are later found out and investigated by CAD

    - Great income potential, plus distributors really earning a lot = Legality.

    Nope again. Earn based on what? I would ask. Read my first few paragraphs.

    - Boss invested a lot into the company, even setting up overseas branch. Therefore, he intends it to be fair and aboveboard.

    Another myth. Relate this to the scams of past years. Many has registered as business, and set up posh offices at Shenton Way and White House. Refer to the commodities scams of 1994 period. If returns negate huge initial investments, why not? Sophiscation will the keyword.

    Lastly,unlike dcx, (paiseh), I will not take a neutral stand, Consider the impact of such companies. Truly the attractiveness of nti is that many do earn initially. But troubles sets in the long term, when such great income earning is not supported by considerations such as value for money products, lacking legal support, flawed management etc etc.
