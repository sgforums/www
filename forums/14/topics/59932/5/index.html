    How to tell good MLM firms from the bad

    I REFER to Mr Leow Zi Xiang's letter, 'Dangers of multi-level marketing' (ST, Dec 30).

    There are both legitimate as well as suspect multi-level marketing (MLM) firms, also known as network marketing companies.

    When assessing an MLM company, you should ask yourself this question: 'Would I buy those products at those prices if not for the opportunity to make money?'

    In other words, do you believe that the products give you quality corresponding to the price paid?

    If you think the products are grossly overpriced, then others are also unlikely to buy the products if the hope of making money were not attached.

    A good sign of a legitimate MLM company is that it has a significant base of consumers who are happy to buy and use its products without wanting to be in the business of MLM.

    But even good companies may have doubtful distributors representing them. You do not want to be associated with a dubious company or distributor.

    Some red flags signalling these:

    # The products are grossly overpriced

    # You are told that you must immediately spend a large sum of money on products which are beyond what would be reasonable for you to consume or sell within one or two months. This is known as 'front-loading' and is frowned upon

    # The distributors are reluctant to talk about their products or answer questions about them, emphasising only the marketing plan or commissions.

    Good MLM firms and distributors are willing to give information about both the income opportunity as well as the products.

    # You are told that it is very easy to make money. On the contrary, MLM is like any other business and needs hard work, perseverance, good service and good products.

    Anyone who tells you that MLM is easy money is to be viewed with extreme suspicion. MLM is simply an alternative distribution channel for manufacturing companies.

    The money used to pay commissions is the money that would otherwise have gone to pay retail staff, rental, renovation costs for shops, advertising costs, profits for wholesalers, profits for franchise owners, and so on.

    There are MLM companies selling quality products at fair prices and good distributors who are honest about the business opportunities. Avoid the bad ones.

    TAN SOON MENG
