    Greetings all,

    First of all, a very happy new year.

    I realized that my very first posting on the subject was rather vehement, and could possibly seem as an overly emotional flaming of NTI Paris. I now clarify, however, that I am not tarring all MLM companies in a single brush stroke. I will concede that there are MLM companies which concerns as much about the products that they are selling as the customers they recruit, and that perhaps many of the posters on these message boards do belong to such companies.

    That said, I am still very much against the marketing policies of NTI Paris (if they even deal with "marketing" in the first place). The spate of articles in the Straits Times forum page should be sufficient to prove my point. I would like to draw attention to the interesting fact that among the 4 letters, only 2 were accusatory. And both pointed to NTI Paris.

    I disagree that Mr. Leow's Dec 30 article was a result of "lack of knowledge" in the plan. Many of his accusations are exactly the ones that my friend and I experienced in NTI Paris. It would also be naive to attribute such hard-sell tactics to a few rogue agents because it takes considerable effort from the ENTIRE company staff to organize and present the seminar. If NTI Paris started in July last year, and they still do not have the sales kits for their members six months later, can you blame me for being suspicious of their motives? If they follow the binary scheme, would they not experience an exponential growth in members? And there are no plans made for such an outcome? I don't think you can only point the finger at the "greedy agents." Quite simply, the way in which the employees conduct their businesses is a true reflection of the company strategy itself, the strategy formulated by oh-bow-down-to-cult-leader James Phang himself. I would also like to add that simply because a plan is legal in no way absolves the company of any crime if the methods of recruitment and product distribution are pyramidal in nature.

    timandra has noted that ".. given such a painful lesson they (NTI Paris) learned for this few days, I'm sure they'll change their approach." Judging from the current behaviour of the company's agents, this may not be happening. My friend who invested an equally large sum of money tried to retract his investment, only to be told that he could not do so. (No surprises there) Furthermore, he was asked to continue with the hard-sell tactics used by NTI Paris agents on him to recruit more members into the fold and recoup his "losses." Nothing was mentioned about the advertisement and sale of aromatherapy products. Well, somebody please convince me that the aromatherapy products are not a front, albeit a rather exotic one, that hides the true nature of the company's dealings. I am not amused. Neither are the teenagers who have thrown in thousands of their/their parents' hard-earned money into the venture.

    I would also like to point out that hellodoggy's earlier claim "time and especially his paycheck will show all" is not exactly correct. It is not how much you earn that counts, but how you earn that much that counts more. If a fat paycheck is indeed the overriding motive, then we might as well go into the drug industry. Similarly, I am furious with NTI Paris because when the entire scheme collapses around them (especially for the members who joined late), the people who fleeced the most wealth are the founders of the company, and not the ones who have been conned into investing later (such as our friends).

    To laurence and tibrig, don't worry I won't sit by and watch the ship sink with my friends on it. I will be talking to a police officer friend tomorrow regarding the legality of the recruitment methods/scheme. I urge you not to sit and wait for things to happen too. I shall attempt not to be cliched, but only by our combined efforts will we bring down the scourge that is NTI Paris. And get your friends out of their mire.
