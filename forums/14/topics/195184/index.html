    As a company, it has failed many parties, mainly its agents who have contributed time, money and effort to its cause, believing they have made useful contributions. How many young and impressionable agents quit their studies to do NTI full time but are now secretly regretting their decisions? How many people left their careers or better paying jobs to do NTI full time but are now only earning a fraction of what they used to earn? And the worse thing is that some of them have to overdeclare their income tax and make themselves more in debt just to keep up appearances.

    It has failed its customers, by raising prices and providing overpriced and substandard products. Aromatherapy, Skin Care, so-called e-mall, ladies underwear and country club memberships and now entrepreneur education. I really wonder at the quality of the instructors giving the training and who accredits the quality of the courses. A training course and certificate is useless if it does not have the proper accredition. A certificate from NTI cannot even be used outside NTI in the MLM industry. So what is the value of the training you receive there? How can a company provide such a chaotic match of products and services I cannot understand. Trying to be a jack of all trades it ends up a master of none. The agents tasked to market their products and services don't know if they are coming or going one day or the next.

    Every business MUST have a well-defined one, three and five year plan at least, and not rush headlong into any project which on the surface seems profitable but potential pitfalls are unknown. Before one project is complete they rush into another. Losing perspective is a dangerous way to lead a company. Failing to deliver on promises and failing to pay distributors on time is also bad. Failing to deliver products to customers on time is even worse.

    How did a small company with big dreams degenerate into the cancer it is today? Is there still hope for this company?
