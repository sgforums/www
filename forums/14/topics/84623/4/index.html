    Just want to note on ur first and third point.

    Singapore MLM industry is wholly unregulated. The only "regulations" are the MLM Act, changed in 2000 and 2001.

    An MLM company need not to be registered. There is no provisions for such action. Besides, u mistaken the function of DSAS, which only admit members who abide by its refund policy and rules on hard pressure selling. Meaning, any company can be MLM if it want to, just register for business as per Company Act and open its doors. DSAS members are direct selling (including MLM) companies who abide by its rules, which place a lot of emphasis on refund policy and hard selling. DSAS members are supposedly more ethical in its transactions than any other non DSAS members.

    Note: All MLM companies are direct selling companies, but not all DS companies are MLM

    On ur third point...

    If they use a third party as an intermediary, it defeats the purpose of direct selling. Please note: direct selling is an evolution from traditional form of selling such as retailing.

    When a company posseses a product it wishes to sell (good or bad), it has the options of many marketing channels, including selling thru wholesalers, retailers or using a network of professional salesmen, or ordinary distributors as in MLM, SLM.

    Therefore, it chooses the marketng channel it see as the most effective way to market its products. A chosen marketing mode cannot reveal how good a product is, until we purchase it and use it.

    Do note also, many statutes such as the Fair Trading Act, Advertising Act, Sales of Goods Act arises from unfair or overclaimed products advertising by business, many of them retailers and other marketing intermediaries.

    Just my dua sens.
