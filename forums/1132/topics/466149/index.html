    The body of 21-year-old Elisa Lam, the Canadian undergraduate who had gone missing during a stay at the allegedly haunted Cecil Hotel on Jan 31, has been found in one of the hotel's four water tanks.

     

    According to the Los Angeles Times, police confirmed her identity through body markings yesterday (Feb 19) after she was found by a maintenance worker investigating complaints about low water pressure.

     

    STOMPer A.N. contributed CCTV camera footage showing the young woman acting strangely in the hotel elevator the night before she went missing.

     

    In the video, Lam could be seen pressing buttons for multiple floors in the lift before stepping outside suddenly. She then looks quickly to her left and right before moving her arms around wildly.

     

    According to the authorities Lam had intended to travel to Santa Cruz as her final destination, which is about 563km north of Los Angeles.

     

    Police investigators say Lam remained in daily contact with her family in Vancouver until she went missing, and never checked out of the hotel.

     

    She was last seen by the hostess and desk clerk, who confirmed that she had been staying at the hotel alone. They also said that they had not seen her with anyone else while she was there.

     

    Hotel Cecil is known for having been home to serial killers like Richard Ramirez, alias the 'Night Stalker', who was convicted of killing 14 people, as well as Jack Unterweger who committed suicide after being convicted for several murders involving prostitutes.

     

    Goldie Osgood, known as the 'pigeon lady of Perishing Square', also was found choked to death in her room in 1964. Her case remains unsolved.

     

    These deaths have led to the Cecil Hotel acquiring a reputation for being haunted.
