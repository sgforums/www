    In our modern era, many people are not connected to their soul, and only think and live using their conscious mind. That part of us is only concerned about personal survival and protection. That is why in many modern relationships that fail, the reasons are often due to 'surface matters' such as lack of money, job security, good looks, high status, etc. The natural survival instincts of people are to find a partner who can offer the most benefits to provide the best possible life.

    When one is connected to his/her soul, they will be feeling genuine unconditional love for each other, regardless of what challenges exist in their relationship.

    To connect with and feel one's soul, we need to truly live from our heart, not with our mind. The conscious mind has a 'built-in logic/program' that will automatically think of how to gain the most benefits for ourselves at all times. If we're still a hunter or farmer, that sort of thinking might be useful. But when we use that mode in our relationships, it is very hard for our relationships to last, and will be unable to stand the test of time and life challenges.

    When things go badly, eg. husband lose his job, husband lost his legs in an accident, husband is stuck in low-paying job while other men are doing better and better, wife suddenly has cancer, wife put on lots of weight after childbirth so is no longer pretty as before, etc, that is when many people's conscious mind start prompting them that they are no longer receiving sufficient benefits, and causing a problem for themselves, so it is time to move on to greener pastures.

    If everyone can start learning to manage their conscious mind, so that it does not attempt to control every aspect of their lives, it will really change their lives for the better. We all need our conscious mind for things like work or studies, but when it comes to matters of the heart, we need to make the effort to use our soul to feel, not our conscious mind to think.

    By regularly quieting our conscious mind when it is not needed through simple breath meditation or quiet self-reflection will help train our conscious mind to relax and stop its auto-controlling mechanism. When our mind is at its quietest, that is when we can connect to our soul, our true essence, the part of us that truly understands Unconditional Love. :)
