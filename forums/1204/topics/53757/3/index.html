    dear diary,

    have you ever woke up one day and suddenly feel that whatever you have done is meaingless? i mean not all of it of course, some of it though.

    tracks and roads are built to be used, no doubt. with the never ending lands beyond the horizon, it is unlikely that one can cover every patches.

    weeding out the first patch of grass and laying the first tar could be difficult. but the thought of what can be harvested kept you going.

    people wont start using the road if the lands covered is too short. as such, you gathered a few friends to increase productivity.

    as the days go by, the distance is stretched. people and vehicles avoid the muddy area and began using the road.

    tired but fulfilling.

    before long, you and your comrades covered a substantial area of the town.

    one fine day, your colleague shares his thought over lunch, "hey, as much as i enjoy laying the road, i think it is about time we show some of the users how easy this can be done. with more manpower, we can continue with this direction, while the others can continue with that."

    "i think its a brilliant idea.", you said.

    the next morning, you stepped out of your house with high hope, greeting every users who are using the road and began to share your idea.

    "no. i just want to use the road. i am not going to dirty my hands with those tar."
    "nah. as much as i enjoy using the road, i wanna spend my time on something else."
    "do i get paid?"

    negative answers brought you back to earth. none of them were moved even after you showed them how easy these can be achieved.

    bearing that disappointment, you shared the feedback with the rest of your colleagues upon return.

    "never mind. i guess let us just continue and see how it goes. hopefully, they will see how easy it is and will choose to join us some days", one of them said.

    having no other alternative, you just went on with what you did right on the very first day.

    one fine day, one of the your trustworthy counterparts came to you, "say, i dont think this is working out. i worked my ass off working on these roads and it seems to me there is no end to it. furthermore, it kills me to see the mere few of us working on it. i mean i got a day job to cope with, u know?"

    "i guess i gotta split here. thanks for the company, mate. until the days when these road users become more initiative, i'll be spending more time on my personal agenda." and off he goes.

    you cant force him to stay. after all, he aint paid a single cent for doing all these.

    one week later, another two of your mates felt tired as well and decided to leave too.

    within a month, many have followed suit, leaving only a miserable few.

    so what are you going to do next? are you going to do likewise?

    www
