    Dear Diary

    Can't sleep. Tired, but sleep won't claim me.

    I've got all these ideas and thoughts just churning around in my head. Thoughts about nothing in particular. Random ideas, jumping here and there, like a ping-pong match inside my mind. I'll think of a question, answer it, then argue against myself. No, I'm not insane. Just trying to sort out the thoughts in my head.

    But when separate ideas suddenly click, it's like getting hit by lightning. Like a jolt; a sudden burst of inspiration. And then I just have to go with it. Once in a while, the inspiration will result in a short story or an intriguing poem. In some cases, even a conversation.

    Maybe if I stop thinking, I'll finally get some sleep. But I can't. I'm always thinking. Most of the time, I can even control my dreams. They say you can't really do that because it's all your subconscious. But I can control what happens in my dreams. Like they're just more of my stories, I can create the plot. (sounds crazy)

    Workz starts soon. Very soon. Less than a few days. Then there'll be less time for thinking -- free thinking, anyway. More methodical thinking. I'm good at that too; I can be extremely logical when I put my mind to it. But I like being creative more. Letting one thought lead to another, and finally making sense of something because I lead myself to a different perspective.

    That's probably why I love get myself busy. Because workz force me to think less.Then again too much workz physically and abstractly will stress and exhaust oneself, and that's probably why I hate it to. Sometimes it's forces you to stay inside the box, and it won't let you think your own ideas. At least not without having evidentiary support behind your idea.

    And because of all this, not too many people get me. I think it's because I scare them. I mean, if I was one of them, I'd probably be scared by me, too. Someone thinks she understands what's going on inside of me. She seems to think it's just the whole career turmoil thing that she went through. But let's be honest, I'm not exactly a typical guy. I mean, sure, I do have some of the career turmoil happening, but that's just a small part of it.

    That's not to say I'm unique, or an anomaly. No, I'm sure there are plenty of other guys out there going through what I am. It's just that I feel like everyone around me sees me as someone else. That's all. But not in the typical way, either. It's in a much bigger picture that I feel like I'm just not understood. I think a lot differently that most people. It's just really difficult to explain.

    Maybe I can get some sleep now, but I doubt it........

    wilI
