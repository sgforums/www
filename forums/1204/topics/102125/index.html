    The HSBC TreeTop Walk (HSBC TTW) is the highlight of an 11 km-hiking route in MacRitchie that brings you through different stages of mature secondary forest. The first of its kind in Singapore and in the region, this free-standing suspension bridge connects the two highest points (Bukit Peirce and Bukit Kalang) in MacRitchie and offers a birdÂ’s eye view of the community of plants and animals that live in the forest canopy. The total length of the walkway is about 250m and its height from the forest floor varies, with the highest point at 25m.

    Besides providing another avenue for nature recreation for Singaporeans, the HSBC TTW will also play an important role in forest canopy research, an area many researchers were not able to get into because of lack of access. This bridge will help to facilitate surveys and plant identification work and further our understanding of how forest ecosystems work.

    Frequently Asked Questions about the HSBC TTW

    How do we get there?
    The HSBC TreeTop Walk is accessible from MacRitchie Reservoir Park via MacRitchie Nature Trail. The walk will approximately be 4.5 km (2-3 hours walk) from the reservoir park. The HSBC TTW is the highlight of an 11 km-hiking route in MacRitchie that brings you through different stages of mature secondary forest. We are also currently exploring possibility of providing other access points.

    Getting to MacRitchie Reservoir
    By Bus
    Take bus nos. 74, 93, 130, 132, 156, 157, 162, 165, 166, 167, 605, 852, 855 or 980. Alight along Lornie Road at the bus-stop in front of MacRitchie Reservoir Park.

    By Car
    Entrance to the MacRitchie Reservoir Park car park is along Lornie Road.

    The 11 km-route in MacRitchie is a loop trail, with trails beginning in both directions from MacRitchie Reservoir Park. From the bus-stop and carpark, you can either walk left and pass the zig-zag bridge to Lornie Trail or right to pass the fishing ground to MacRitchie Nature Trail to begin.

    What is the opening hours ?
    9.00 a.m. Â– 5.00 p.m. (Last entry to walk is 4.30 p.m.).
    Note: The HSBC TTW is closed on Mondays for maintenance and research.

    Do we need to pay?
    There is no admission charge at the moment.

    How many people can the HSBC TTW accommodate?
    For the comfort and enjoyment of all visitors, we will be restricting visitor numbers to 30 pax at any one time as well as visitor flow. The traffic along the walkway is one-way. Therefore, visitors will enter via the ranger post at Bukit Peirce and exit at Bukit Kalang and continue on to the Petaling Trail.

    What can we see up there?
    Instead of arching their necks to observe the canopy, visitors are now able to catch a glimpse of the vibrant Â“high lifeÂ” of the forest at their eye level. From this high vantage point, nature enthusiasts will be able to have a closer observation of the animal community in the canopy or take a peek at the flowers or fruits of tall trees and climbers and their pollination activities

    Are there any resting stops along the 11km-route?
    A Ranger Station is located at the cross road of the 11 km- hiking route. It serves as a convenient resting point, with basic amenities such as toilets and a water cooler, and an information kiosk for users. There are also two shelters at the 2.8 km and 5 km mark along the route.

    The ranger station will be occupied during normal working hours. Visitors who need assistance outside the office hours can contact our 24-hours ranger number at 64685736.
