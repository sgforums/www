    thanks. interesting points u got there.

    first of all, my legal understandings tells me that the photographer owns the copyrights to the pictures, as long as the pictures taken are decent and not doctored in the wrong way.

    it is not wrong to have other attendees taking pictures beside the appointed photographer. the reason for not encouraging so are:

    1. be it new or old faces, we do not know the integrity of everyone here. the reason for restricting to only a couple of photographers is a mean of possible damage control.

    even for joho, i share my thoughts with him when he first joined me for sentosa trip, equipped with his first nikon camera.

    i even requested to browse thru some of the pictures before he went home with it.. not because i doubted his intention, but to make sure people see what they want to see.

    2. many of u have seen his work. thus, i can safely say that joho is one of the most skilled photographer around this joint and is armed with the most appropriate equipment.

    and since he is already doing the job he does best, i thought others would be better off enjoying themselves than to be snapping away.

    3. and the distribution part would be easier if there is only one source.

    as for your other suggestions:

    - i believe the educating part has already take place for quite a while. the policy around here is that if u didnt attend, u wont get the pictures. period.

    to some, we even emphasized about distributing the pictures to the right person before we pass them the link.

    - when we start to vet the participants list, it means the situation is getting really severe.

    it mean that we have to openly unwelcome certain individual. fortunately, we have not come to this stage yet, and we hope we dont have to.

    - i dont know about the last suggestion. firstly, the issue here is about people distributing photos to people who are unauthorised.

    secondly, it is not a case of stalking as well.

    lastly, i dont know how private can we go when it comes to beach activities.

    nevertheless, i do agree that "soft approach" should adopted here as well. it would be done on a case-to-case basis.

    for now, we are just trying to lay out some of the appropriate guidelines.
