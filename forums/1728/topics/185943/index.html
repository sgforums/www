    In the Buddhist approach to life the value of work is deeply embedded. The Buddha taught that actions bring results, whether for good or ill. He also taught that nothing just happens by itself - all things are conditioned and each thing as a cause. To work hard and conscientiously has beneficial consequences, whether this is working on spiritual practices or cleaning the streets. Idleness, lethargy, dissolute behavior are decried by the Buddha as unwholesome.

    Agan and again, the Buddha urges us to act - to meditate, to do good deeds, to build good relationships with others. Indeed, it is notable that the Buddha's last words were 'strive on with diligence!'.

    I decided to add on this thread to maybe mention something that I've observed so far from the few organisations that I'm affiliated with.

    I realised that politics does exists in these organisations, where the committess basically wanted the policies to benefit themselves rather than the organisation. One instances that I came across was that this committee decided to that all activities organised by the volunteers must obtain the approval of the committees, despite the venerables's have agreed to go ahead, and when it comes to big events such as venerables visited Singapore, the committees are always absent, citing busy work schedules etc.

    Another example is another committee even set high entry level for the volunteers, citing that they must be of certain tradition etc, despite knowing the volunteers number are dwindling fast.

    From these few examples, I noticed that sometimes, people join the management committee of a religious organisation, or any voluntary organisations because they want to gain control and ensure the events work towards their advantage.

    Citing the incidence that I've encountered in the two different committees, I have nothing but disappointments, but I stayed on because I do have friends inside who kinda "suffered" under the hands of these committee unknowingly.

    This makes me ponder on the qualities of these so call committee members who preaches dharma, but doesn't seems to be practicing it. Of course, I have met good committee members who are dedicated to serve for unconditionally for the work of dharma propagation. However this will have a long term adverse impact on the progress of Buddhism in this society. Pretty much disappointed, but luckily my advising venerable is constantly encouraging me, and teaching me on different ways to handle it. If not, I'll probably ended up in IMH sooner that I expect.
