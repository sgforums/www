    What is emptiness?

    Usually people associate emptiness as nothingness. In Buddhism, emptiness is not nothingness. Just like non-attachment is not detachment. It is not getting attached to anything while at the same time able to enjoy it without having to do without it. Ok before I confuse u, let me simplify it.

    We get attached to things we like or love. For eg. a dog, family, friend etc. So when we lose it we get upset. non-attachment means not to get attached to things as we know that all things are impermanent. But some may miscomprehend non-attachment as detachment. They think since getting attached to such things will cause unhappiness, they rather do without it. This is an extreme of one end. So Buddhism advocates the middle path. We understand that the nature of all things are impermanent. Nothing lasts forever. So we can accept it when something goes away for good as a way of life and appreciate it n enjoy it while it lasts.

    Since we now know all things r impermanent, all things are originally empty of inherent nature. True a chair is there. It is always there. U see it. I see it. What is meant by empty of inherent nature means it appears as what we see, to put it simply.
