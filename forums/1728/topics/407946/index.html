    hi Rooney,

    Understand why you don't accept my definition of praying... i will try to give you an explanation but if you still can't accept it then it's alright. A diversity of views makes life interesting isn't it?

    There are many levels of meaning to 'prayer'. What most people think of is the most external meaning...

    When we say pray to Buddha, we have to examine what is 'pray' and what is 'Buddha'.  Buddha is not a form only as Diamond sutra says. The ultimate Buddha is within, emptiness, nature of mind etc.. So when we say 'prayer', prayer can be full of attachments, based on a want, or it can be wantlessness.. It can also be separating, or it can be merging with Buddha, so it is the same as meditation. 

    Even if we talk about the most external level of meaning, prayer is also alot of hard work. Don't think only meditation is hard work... but actually the hard work is actually the same as sincerity and devotion as weychin says...

    yes can. Everytime we chant, we end by dedicating the merit to all sentient beings to attain Buddhahood. Dedicating for all sentient beings seal the merit and make it increase greatly.
