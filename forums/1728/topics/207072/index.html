    Anyone can go to heaven as long as you have good karma. Right, as long as you accumulate good karma through the practise of '10 virtuous deeds' and keeping the 5 precepts so as not to commit bad karma. Going to heaven is not difficult. And you mentioned you read many books related to Buddhism, Dalai Lama and Zen practise, I am very interested to know which books have you read..? Do share with us. Thanks.

    Secondly, whether you lead a simple life of a monk is entirely up to you, it is not necessary that you have to lead a life like a monk in order to practise. Many great lay practitioners have a thriving business and enjoys good life, although they would not be too attached to luxury etc and are usually generious in giving what they have. Also as you should have known, the teaching of Middle Way is core in Buddhism. Middle way can apply to alot of situation. It first started when the Buddha realised that neither indulging in sensual pleasures, nor his practise of ascetism, could lead to Enlightenment. He stopped all these and sat under the Bodhi tree and attained enlightenment. So the Buddha taught that we should practise the middle way, that is, not become extreme. So in your case, it translate to, not indulging too much in luxuries and sensual enjoyment, but also we have to make a living and have what is necessary. You don't have to live like a hermit.

    The habit of smoking and clubbing is also detrimental to our Buddhist cultivation, and all such self-serving habits/'habit energy' must be eliminated through our practise. It is also part of purifying our minds. In fact my shi fu says, the very least a Buddhist practitioner must accomplish is to eliminate our self-serving habits.

    5 Precepts and 10 Virtuous deeds, as I mentioned earlier, is only a means of accumulating good karma and also to prevent accumulating any bad karma but it does not lead to enlightenment. It is very important, it is a good practise, but it will not lead you to liberation.

    Buddhism is different from any other religion because it emphasizes that we must purify our minds. Our mind is the source of all good, all bad, all thoughts, all afflictions, all sufferings and so on. We must treat the problem at the root. We must eliminate the root of suffering before we can find the unconditional and eternal Bliss of Nirvana in our lives. Buddhism is a culture of bliss, as it is the way of living in perfect bliss in the midst of Life through the cultivation of dharma. The Buddhist practise of mindful awareness allows us to watch our every arising thought, and not become misled by these thoughts into further samsaric cycles. Since you have read many 'Buddhism, Dalai Lama and Zen' books, I think you should have a rough idea of what I am talking about.

Because as far as I know, although I do not claim to have lots of experience, you can only find true love if you date someone and your relationship becomes deepened as a result.

In Theravada Buddhism, there is no tradition of vegetarianism. Monks accept whatever the lay has to offer unless it's not suitable for eating (i.e rotten, or some other kinds of animals that are not usually eaten)

    In general, when a Buddhist eats meat, he should make sure that he eats meat according to the '5 pure meat' principle:

    See entire article here:
    Five Clean Sources of Meat

    (1) That he did not slaughter the animal personally
    (2) That he did not instruct others to slaughter
    (3) That the slaughter was not committed for his sake
    (4) That he did not witness the slaughter
    (5) That he did not hear the cries associated with the slaughter
