    Life (Self) is nothing other than the continuous flow of the Now Moment.
    The Now Moment ceases as it arises. This moment must completely ceased
    and serves as the CAUSE for the next moment to arise.
    Therefore Self is a process of series Self1, Self2, Self3, Self4, Self5, Self6...etc
    A fixed entity 'Self' does not exist, what really exists is a momentary Self.
    Under deep meditation, one is able to observe and sense the karmic and mental factors from moment to moment,

    When the karmic and mental factors subsides, it is known as "The True and Only (and Inherently egoless) Conscious Light (Itself)".

Emptiness is also all-encompassing. For example when you pour water into a space in a cup, this space will not reject the water. At the same time this space is not diminished.

    The property of encompassing whatever you encounter is none-other than Compassion. When you develope metta, arent you developing a capacity of acceptance for yourself, ppl around you, situations? Broaden this and encompass the whole universe

    Your Buddha nature is also the Nature of Emptiness. True emptiness is not completely empty. You still need awareness to realise this. -- Surangama Sutta.

    Your Buddha Nature is wholesome in itself. Words like Emptiness, Compassion are its properties, not itself entirely. This way you won't be confused.

    If one sees thoughts as empty as in absence or a void, then it is false emptiness.

    Thoughts are just conditioned arising - one thought arise is Yuan Qi, and conditions the other thought to arise and is constantly changing - ungraspable. These are conditioned dharma and empty of inherent existence.

    Treat thoughts like heart beat, walking, and breathing. For example when you walk, you do not keep thinking "I want to walk.. i want to walk..." it is the most natural thing. There is no thoughts, and no self walking.

    Similarly thoughts are just conditioned phenomena, do not arise another mind on top of it, just like your heartbeat. Do not make it an imputed reality out of Fa (dharma/phenomena).

    When we do not impute reality, it is mere Pure Awareness, neither is there seperation of inner and outer.

    It is every time when a condition and cause arise, contact is made, perception arise almost immediately, that is the problem. Therefore the immediate Buddha Nature is not known.

    Think about it - did you first HEAR the sound, or KNOW the sound?
