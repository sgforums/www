    Hi there!

    The word "karma" basically means "action" in sanskrit. Karma is basically actions we do over and over again which are governed by the "law of cause and effect." The law of cause and effect (karma) can be simply put into three categories. Firstly is karma of speech, secondly is karma of thought, thirdly is karma of body. Therefore, every good or bad action of speech, thought, or body simply has a corresponding result either good or bad depending.

    An example would be someone hurts you, and you are infuriated. You then yell back and scold him/her for hurting you. Firstly, the reason he/she has hurt you was a result of past karma which has now ripened and come into fruitation. When you yell back at the person, you are now planting a negative seed that will ripen towards the future, thereby resulting in suffering. The things you do, are the cause, the results are the effects. Therefore, the future is the result of past karma aswell as being seed of future karma.

    That is why one of the goals of buddhists is to attain the purity of mind that enables one to make wise choices and avoid errors and mistakes that will in the future cause us suffering.
