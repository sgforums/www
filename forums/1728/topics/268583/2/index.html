    In reality, Anatta is taught in order to 'negate' our false conceptions, but no other propositions is given to establish another 'self' apart from 'not-self'. If someone teaches a 'self' apart from 'not-self', he is holding onto the eternalist teachings of the Hindus.

    On the other hand, if we say 'No-Self', which I often use this term, some of us may think that it is saying that the Self is non existing, the nihillistic understanding.

    Actually, the point Buddha is trying to make is that the separate, constnatly existing Self acting as an Agent (observer, doer, thinker, etc) cannot ultimately be found, and 'self' is merely just transient aggregates arising spontaneously out of conditioned arising. If the 'self' cannot ultimately be found, it is not right to say whether the self is 1) existing, 2) non-existing, 3) both existing nor non-existing, 4) neither-existing and non existing.

    One who still thinks that 'self does not exist' as if self is an entity that do not exist, is still holding on 'a thicket of [false] views':

    There is the case where an uninstructed, run-of-the-mill person... does not discern what ideas are fit for attention, or what ideas are unfit for attention.     So 'the material self, the conscious self, the metaphysical self' or etc etc 'selves', all these are relative truths. That is to say it is as true as saying 'you are TheGoodEarth, I am An Eternal Now'. When we communicate, with inevitably will need to use conceptual models to communicate and understand things.

    There is no problems with this, but due to our ignorance we posit ideas that our conventional self has inherent existence, has permanency, and is truly 'ourselves'. When we begin to think like that, we become attached to everything, to our material self, conscious self, etc. We are bonded.

    Now back to Anatta. As I said earlier, Anatta (no-self, noself, etc) is solely about the ultimate reality. It is a Dharma Seal, a Characteristic of Phenomenon. All samsaric phenomenon exhibits three characteristics: it is Impermanent, Suffering and No-Self. All phenomon existence exhibits these characteristics of Ultimate Reality at all moments.

    Therefore, that is to say, our material self, conscious self, metaphysical self, and so on, are ALSO of the nature of Anatta, and Anicca and Dukkha (No-Self, Impermanence, Suffering).

    Which is to say, ultimately speaking, there is NO such thing as a 'Material Self' or 'Conscious Self' or 'Metaphysical' that is inherently existing, and in reality they are just made up of the 5 aggregates, transient and ever-changing, arising out of conditions. There is ultimately no 'Self' that can be found in these transient aggregates! There is no fixed self, only a momentary Self1, Self2, Self3, Self4, ceases as it arises, conditions the next rebirth. Only when we come to the realisation of the Dharma Seals through intuitive experience (and not mere theoretical understanding), will all our bonds and dualistic attachment naturally drops.

    Therefore in Buddhism, if you want Liberation, you have to practise Insight. You have to observe moment to moment these characteristics displaying themselves in moment to moment reality. You have to be very aware at all moments.
