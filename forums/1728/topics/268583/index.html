    1) The actions of raising his hands are natural phenomena?
    It depends on what you mean by natural. Some people think that 'natural' means actions arising without intentions, while unnatural are actions that arises out of our intention. That is seeing things dualistically. Actually both intentional and non-intentional actions are natural in the higher sense -- they are conditioned arising, and that is perfectly natural as it is, but it must not be misunderstood.

    My taiwanese teacher also talk about walking as being natural, what he meant is not that somehow your walking is somehow controlled by some natural forces.

    What he meant is that there is completely no need for a sense of doer or dualistic volition involved in walking, it is so natural that you never gave it a second thought, and that is possible to be natural because in the first place the sense of a doer or there being a 'controller' of an action is an illusion.

    A sense of a doer, controller or the necessity for you to "think" (here I mean dualistic thinking, thinking in terms of a Subject and Object) your walking (i.e "I want to walk, I want to walk, I want to walk" ) in order to walk is absolutely unnecessary, because whatever arises, arises spontaneously and naturally as part of conditioned arising at that very moment. It is not caused by a self, there is no self and no doer.

    Means whether intention, or thinking, or action, all these arise SPONTANEOUSLY and NATURALLY with no agent, thinker, or doer.. it just self-arise out of conditions.

    The reason why you think there is a doer/self/thinker is because you review the previous thoughts and find a coherence and chain them up together, causing the impression of a 'doer' or 'thinker'. This is called dualistic thinking. In reality reality is spontaneous and disconnected.

    That is why in Buddhist practise we must not dwell in meaning, content and dualistic thinking, we must let go and plunge into moment to moment experience with naked awareness to experience/BE the arising and ceasing as it is moment to moment. If you can do that, then that very moment is unborn, because you no longer see time or the birth and death of a 'self' or entities.

    So back to topic, whatever arises, whether there is intention or no intention, arises spontaneously and is not caused by any Doer. This is a profound truth and easily misunderstood.

    From what I understand, even if I were to accumulate merits, it is still "I'm accumulating merits." which will only led me to higher rebirths and not out of samsara as there is still a recognition of "I" in doing good deeds.

    What should be my motivation when accumulating merits and what sort of seeds should I plant so that my projecting karma will be towards liberation?
    Very simple. There is a difference between leaking-blessings and non-leaking-blessings. Leaking blessings may be good deeds but tainted by not keeping the 5 precepts, or that it may be good deeds done with certain agenda/wish behind it, some desire for some good outcome from that doing. There is still attachment to the 4 forms will performing the virtuous deeds. That blessing resulted will be small and will 'leak away'.

    If you are liberated constantly, one who like in Diamond Sutra said, does not hold onto the form of a self/doer, of an other, of a sentient being, and of age/time. At that time whatever good deeds you do are not done due to any agenda, or due to craving, and there is no dualistic illusions or the four forms. Then the merits will be immeasurable.

    So when you are doing good deeds or doing anything, practise the dharma, and do not give rise to the four forms, do not give rise to dualistic thinking or craving. When doing good deeds, just do it without any extra thinking, and practise the Dharma.

    http://www.jenchen.org.sg/understa.htm

    Real merits

    Merits are consequences of the non-orignation and non-cessation of the mind. During the Tang Dynasty in ancient China, Emperor Liang Wu-Ti asked the patriarch Bodhidharma, "Venerable One, I have built many monasteries for the well being of the Sangha and I have also performed many virtuous deeds. What merits have I accumulated? Bodhidharma answered, "You have no merits." Why is that so? It is because when Emperor Liang Wu-Ti performed those acts of giving, he was attached to them. Thus, even though he had performed many virtuous deeds, because he had not cultivated the purity of his mind, he did not attain "non-origination and non-cessation". Therefore, his actions can only be considered as cultivation of blessings, not merits. Without liberation there are no merits. On the other hand, when there is liberation, all virtuous deeds will reap merits.

    Within, to keep the mind in a humble mood is Kung; and without, to behave oneself according to propriety is Teh.
