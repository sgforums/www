    14 Oct 04, 23:58
    Thai Buddhism can be considered one tradition of the Theravada stream.

    The term "Human Vehicle" has originated since Buddha's time, it's been mentioned many times over centuries by different masters, but only recently has masters like Master Taixu who brought Human Vehicle into mainstream.

    "da cheng jing tu zhong and nan chuan xiao cheng fo jiao" - doctrinal difference, and difference in path.

    While the "Hinayana-ists" practice Self-Liberation method, the highest attainment is the attainment of "Arhatship", or sainthood. However, the Mahayana practitioners commit Bodhisattva vows and practice Great Compassion, liberating both the inner sentient being and the other sentient beings. Only through the practice of Bodhisattva can one attain Buddhahood. The Hinayana practitioners may turn to the path of Mahayana at some point of their cultivation.

    When I speak of "Hinayana" and "Mahayana", I would not discriminate them by just the traditions and sects. There are people who attained arhatship within Chinese Mahayana and Vajrayana, and there are those who wish to practice the Bodhisattva path in Theravadin too, since the Bodhisattva concept is present in the Theravadin.

    The Mahayana Buddhism focuses more on the metaphysics compared to the Theravadin, I suppose. The Mahayana Canon and the Pali Canon (theravadin uses the Pali canon) are also different sets of scriptures... but basically, all authentic buddhist scriptures never leave the 3 Dhamma Seals:

    1) Impermanence
    2) Non-self
    3) Nirvana

    Most importantly, I think a practitioner should stick to their path, practice 1 dharma door, and not run around Singapore to look for different dharma doors but not enter them deeply. This is a common mistake of many people in the modern age when information technology and modern transport is predominant.
