    From what I understand Eskimo Buddhism is a "bridge" belief between the orient and the occident. The term 'Eskimo' designates the Inuit, inhabitants of the top of the planet. These people are orientals yet they "live" in North America (Canada and Alaska) implying that the traditional Buddhist belief system may be bridged by the migration of these people via the solidness of the northern ice flows.

    Buddhism embraces several non-modern principles and shuns wealth and wealth generation as an example.

    Eskimo Buddhism seems to represents the 'accomplished' individual who has no reason to live impoverished in a modern value system YET wishes to contribute and identify himself as a Buddhist.

    The Eskimo Buddhist Mantra is as follows;

    I am 'Eskimo Buddha' and therefore am

    healthy,
    happy,
    lucky,
    prosperous,
    honest,
    fit,
    altruistic,
    ethical,
    brave,
    selfless,
    loving to my fellow child, woman and man,
    positive,
    a leader,
    simple
    and continue to cultivate wisdom.
    Clearly widely applicable and desirable virtues
