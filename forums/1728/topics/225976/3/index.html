    Hi January,

    Just to share what I felt from reading this thread.

    Firstly, I think you seems to have something against the belief in past lives. Buddha through His experience and insight, sees that life is a continuum. Life neither start out-of-nowhere nor ends abruptly. Nibbana should be the primary objective of a buddhist, but of course, knowing that Nibbana is not easy to attain, as buddhists, we gradually train and cultivate ourselves, to condition us to achieve this Supreme Bliss. It may take one, two, or even many lifetimes ahead of us to achieve that.

    But a journey of a thousand miles begins from your first step (I think it's a nice quote from Lao Tze). So to prepare us for that, Buddha, during His 45 years of propagation had explicitly taught His Teachings. And Buddha's Teachings are very practical, because rather than praying and seeking for external divinity, we seek inwards to the purification of the mind. For our actions and speech originate from our thoughts.

    Now you may ask again so how is past lives practical? Well, to the best of my knowledge, Buddha never instruct anyone to be immerse and dwell into their past lives. When Buddha mentioned past lives, usually His own or some others, His objective was to teach the audience the Dhamma. Buddha was a very skillful Teacher. He often used analogies that best suit to His audience.

    Buddha taught issues like how to handle one's anger, one's craving and so on. I find that these are very practical issues, in fact, many leading counselling and psychology fields did look into the buddhist scriptures in support of their role in mental modelling.

    For Buddha had said that the Past is gone and Future yet to come only the Present is what that matters. Buddha often emphasized that we should 'live in the present moment'.

    So my feeling is that you have somehow misunderstood from some of the, perhaps, misinformed buddhists, that made you have such strong case against past lives.

    There may have meditators who claimed to see their past lives, which could be true. That simply shows that they have achieve a very profound focus of the mind that they could recollect their past lives. But recollecting one's past lives is not a meditator's objective. I think you could have misunderstood on this part.
