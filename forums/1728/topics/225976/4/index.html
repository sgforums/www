    Good morning January,

    Just to share some of my views again.

    Firstly I believe you have a huge misunderstanding on the idea kamma. Much of the misconception is that you do good, begets good rewards; do bad begets bad rewards. This is often a mixture of the buddhist idea of kamma into mainstream folk-deity-beliefs. Kamma has somehow transform into another force that is judging people. Which is not true at all.

    Kamma in Pali means "effect". Kamma is one of the five universal laws that the Buddha had discovered. Venerable S Dhammika had written a book sometime ago titled "All About Buddhism" which explains very clearly the idea of kamma. Now I don't have the book with me so I'll try to explain kamma as much as I can recall and understand.

    The whole kamma theory (that you had named) is a process. A process of cause, condition(often people forgets about this factor) and effect.

    Now say if this person is handicapped, say due to an accident. Then the cause could be either, he was not mindful of the situation he is in, and given another condition that there was a driver who is not mindful as well, and another supplementary condition is that they cross path. Now the effect will be that this person to be injured and unfortunately turned handicapped. Kamma took place in this scenario is not because anyone had done anything exactly bad, but lack of mindfulness in their action. Now if the former person and the driver would have been more careful and mindful, the cause has changed, even the condition remains, that the two would meet each other, but perhaps the car stops at red light and this person happily crossing the road, no one is injured.

    This is kamma.

    If my parents have cancer, then probably due to the lifestyle they have led for decades that gives cancer a chance to develop. Take my father for example, he started smoking since the age of 11 but he had quitted smoking when he turned 56 due to an operation. Though he had stayed clean from smoking for near 4 years now, I won't be surprise if (touch wood! touch wood!) he would be caught down by lung cancer. 45 years of hardcore smoking and we can imagine how much damage had he done to his own lungs. Now smoking becomes the cause and the body is the condition. And due to biological law (one of the five universal laws) if the body reacts in a negative way then cancer develops which is the effect.

    Yet another example of kamma.

    Now if my friend is in trouble, and if I really do treat him/her as a friend, then I should try to help to the best of my abilities. This is what friends are for! Even in the Pali Canon, I can't remember the title but I think there is a short discourse that was said by the Buddha in regards to friendship. How should a friend try to influence his friends in a positive manner. And supporting one another.

    The Buddha, motivated by the compassion He had for the world, that He generously spread the Dhamma to people who are fortunate enough to hear from Him (cause). And a large group of this people who became the Buddha's disciples made efforts by been diligence, hardworking, and do a lot of propogation work (condition) which eventually even people living in this time, though may not hear from the Buddha personally, are able to receive the same Dhamma as well (effect). Given the compassionate feature in Buddhism, all the more we should encourage and applaud UNICEF for its good effort in helping poor countries.

    No offence but I think your understanding of the kamma theory IS rubbish.

    Literally kamma takes place in anything we do. We make a small and harmful lie here and there to boast our own ego now and then. Well it may seems relatively harmless, but it slowly and gradually conditions your mind in "getting used of lying". Which may eventually lead you to big problem. Now this is what the Buddha caution us about, not to allow us in getting used in these bad habits. Now if a person is habitually short tempered, probably this creates an impression in the 8th conscious and in our future rebirths, we may have the tendency(note:it's not determined) of becoming a short tempered person again.

    So my understanding of Buddha's teaching about past lives, is one of the explaination on some of our very hard-to-kick habits (be it good or bad ones). Or probably these habits are due to our upbringing from our parents or some incidents that happened between us and our friends which made great impact.

    Whichever the case is, the Buddha in His 45 years of ministry was more interested in improving our quality of living by upgrading our spirituality. Kamma definitely is not a determining factor but rather a conditioning factor, changes can take place and these changes, as what the Buddha had taught, should be for the better.

    Sad to say, as Buddhism spread to many regions and mixed with various culture, some may seems to have been "degraded" to mere superstitious. But I am interested in what Buddha had taught and not non-buddhistic practices, I won't want to waste my energy and time on those things.

    I think you should too. If you genuinely cannot accept kamma and past lives, then move on, Dhamma is a practical thing and I believe given your high intelligence you can certainly find something that is really useful and beneficial to you.
