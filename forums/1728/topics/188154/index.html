    when the thought of Buddha appears in one mind. Just let it be. Know it. No further action is required. Hence there is no need to

    1. Kill it
    2. Reckon it as Mara
    3. Give it up

    You may find commentaries in scriptures from both Mahayana and Theravada scriptures -- "Inaction with regards to all phenomenas". In summary to be firm as a mountain no matter what happens.
