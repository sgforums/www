    Religion is defined as the praying or worshipping of god or the system of beliefs in a divine being.

    From the above definition, Buddhism is not a religion. So, you might ask what is Buddhism if it is not a religion

    Religion is started by mankind because of fear of the unknown and a supernatural being higher than mankind. Early homo sapiens hunt for their food and slept in caves and mountains. They do not understand natural disasters like volcanic eruption, earthquakes, tsunamis and the like and thought that a divine being higher than them punish them. So they sought refuge and pray to the imaginery divine being known as god in the hope that the god protect and do not punish them. This is how god concept and religion came about in the early civilisation of mankind.

    It is out of ignorance and fear that religion was born.

    If the definition of "religion" is the unquestionable belief and worship in a supreme entity, with the obligation to carry out rites and rituals, then Buddhism is definitely not a religion. It is beyond all conventional definitions of religion - for Buddhism encourages intelligent doubting and believes in the potential supremacy of the individual. Rites and rituals are seen as ceremonies that help guide and inspire us. They are important in that sense, but they do not give us Wisdom and True Happiness. Buddhism is thus called a religion only out of convention.
