    Hi

    What do you guys think of the potential of Buddhist Uniformed CCA groups in spore schools. Primary and secondary schools to be exact.

    From what little I notice, Buddhists are increasingly looking forward to other religions, esp Christianity in devising methods to spread the Dhamma. This is what we have currently

    1. Youth Ministries / Divisions belonging to specific Buddhist centers
    2. Buddhist Societies (BS) in tertiary institutions

    If we look at Boys Brigade, Girl Guides and Pari Rovers they are fundamentally still Christian and for more or less, religious studies are still conducted within these groups. We can learn from them the good points and model it to something that is more Buddhism

    So why not a Buddhist Uniformed Group? It will open a new channel for Buddhism to spread into spore youths. And hopefully it can make its way into non-Buddhist schools and appeal to non-Buddhist to come and learn the Dhamma. And eventually make it internationally available

    According to a survey by the Malaysian Buddhist Missionary Society the No.1 reason that cause the decline in Buddhist population is "Lack of opportunity to study Buddhism". Followed by "Passive Missionary Work". The survey targeted Buddhist youths age b/w 20 to 25
