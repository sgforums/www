    In Buddhism, it is ok to wish for reasonable improvements to our lives as I mentioned before, but to cling to anything at all, even hope, can mean sufferings. To cling to hope, desire and craving will ultimately lead to disappointment, and whatever one is attached to will eventually pass away, whether is money, or people, or whatever.. because of its impermanent nature. The nature of samsara is dukkha, it is full of suffering. We can never find true bliss and contentment in Samsara and our endless seeking within it.. we can only find Bliss in Clarity and Liberation through practising the Dharma. Everyone in the world is hoping for something. Big money, good relationship, good life, etc. This seeking is neverending and suffering is also neverending. And our desire is never satisfied... after you get one thing, you desire something better than what you have... there is never contentment. The Buddha taught: Contentment is the greatest wealth.

    Therefore in Buddhism we should give too much attention to our hopes for the future... it may sound depressing but it is not. It is being realistic. I'm not saying we must give up all hopes. Neither am I saying we should not plan for our future. We should plan for the future but our attention should be in this very moment, whatever we are doing right now including planning, and not attached to any outcome. This is the wisdom of non-doing. It is not doing something as a means to an end... it means to be present with whatever you are doing right now. Being content with this very moment.

    Therefore what I am saying is that we should not pay too much attention to something that is unpredictable, illusive, something that is only imagined in the mind. Rather we should live in the moment because that is what Reality is. Like Eckhart Tolle said the quality of our consciousness affects our future and I think that is very important.

    Lastly, true Buddhists do not just venerate the Buddha and honour the teachings. We must PRACTISE the teachings so that we can gain enlightenment ourselves, that that is the most important thing for a Buddhist. We must practise, gain liberation, attain the highest bliss of Nirvana. That is the only true lasting bliss that is NOT conditioned by anything, it is not something that can be lost. It is the bliss of Clarity.

    Originally posted by mancha:

    It is this hope that sustain many human in times of tribuation. This is the essence of any religion.
    Unlike all religions which offers hope of salvation as some place out of our planet somewhere in the future or after you die, Buddhism is the only realistic teaching that teaches that true salvation is in attaining insights/wisdom and clarity in our very lives... and not some future place. (All 'places' in samsara, under time and space, are conditioned, impermanent, and is of the nature of dukkha/suffering)

    It is possible in Buddhism to awaken to our unconditioned reality, clarity and bliss in this very life, through Dharma. If you awaken to this unconditioned reality, you remain unaffected by any sort of tribulations that is happening, even if the whole world is going to be destroyed. You are like the mirror (our Buddha Nature, our pure awareness) that reflects everything without getting engaged nor attached to any appearances. All appearances arise and passes instantaneously (impermanence), leaving no trace in the mirror. The mirror neither rejects unpleasant appearance nor pursues pleasant appearances, nor attaches to any appearances. The mirror just reflects, non-judgementally... The mirror is the reflection itself. Our Buddha Nature is Luminous-Emptiness inseparable. By cultivating awareness we get in touch with our true nature.

    The reason why we are suffering is because we are identified with being a separate self, which is an illusion. We think there is a dualistic relation between Self and the world, and therefore we try our best to get what is pleasant (fundamental attraction) and get rid of the unpleasant (fundamental aversion). We are constantly trying to fulfill the sense of self... so we keep perpetuating the sense of a seperate self and split.. and we become so attached and do not know how to let go. We did not realise our true nature as the non-dual mirror mind, that there is only Oneness and the whole universe is of the same all-pervading essence which is luminous-emptiness... we do not realise there is no duality, no inner and outer, no subject-object separation. If we awaken to the truth, there will be no suffering, there will be no constant striving nor aversion and the mind will be still and blissful.

    Like I said.. the bliss of Nirvana is undescribable and is not conditioned by anything, Nirvana is beyond time and space.
