    See http://www.jenchen.org.sg/vol7no1c.htm

    Excerpt:

    5 types of offspring

    It is normal to expect kinship between parents and their children. Loving parents and filial children are the basis for a warm and harmonious relationship. However, there are exceptions to this norm. According to Buddhist sutras, there are five types of conditions under which people are born to their parents:

    1. They are reborn into the family to pay back their indebtedness to the parents. For example, the child is filial, obedient and diligent in his studies or at work. Regardless of the financial situation, he would provide for the needs of his parents.

    2. They are here to seek the return of what their parents owed them in the past. This might be due to the parents owing unsettled matters or debts to the child incurred in their past lives. Thus, not only does the child not bring fortune to the family this life, but the parents have to spend a lot of money on him. For example, the child may be sick very often thereby causing the parents to incur a huge amount of medical expenses, or other situations where the parents have to spend a lot of money on him and driving them to financial difficulties. Some of them may even cause the family to break-up or the death of family members. Some may become incapacitated and cause the family to run into huge debts or even bankruptcy. These types of children are reborn to their parents to seek the return of their dues.

    3. A child may be born into a family to return the benevolence that his parents had accorded him in his past lives. In this lifetime, everything is smooth sailing for the child and he is successful in his studies and career to bring honour and pride to the family. Hence, he is here to repay kindness of his parents.

    4. They are here to seek revenge on their parents. Perhaps there might be hostility between the two parties in their past lives. The parents might have hit the person; insulted him, ill-treated him, or treated him unfairly. He could not forgive and forget. For example, he would beat up his parents, ill-treat them, or even to take their lives in return for what they owed him in the past lives. Hence, he is reborn into the family to seek revenge.

    5. They merely borrow the mother's womb to be born into this world. The great sages and virtuous men, monks and nuns, the Bodhisattva path cultivators and the Buddha belong to this category.

    If there is hostility between parents and their children, the parents have to sincerely repent and perform meritorious deeds in the name of the children. Parents should not bear hatred and anger but to treat them with compassion and loving-kindness. Then, the situation would slowly improve and the animosity would soon dissolve.

    Some children may be born into a family with defects, or with illnesses that cause the family to fork out a huge sum of money for hospital bills and other medical bills and cause other unhapiness in the parents' life. In many cases it could be that the child has intention of seeking revenge in some form due to past hatred. In such a case we should not stop the child from being born into the world because it is our karma and the child's karma. He is here because of his and our karma, and by allowing him to be born, and taking care and giving him love, the karma will be dissolved in this lifetime and we will no longer have the 'Yuan1 Qing1' relationship with the child. We must have confidence in the Buddhadharma and practise it in every moment of our lives, and not let our external situation affect our inner well being. Let this be a test of our cultivation. After all we must know the ultimate and inner purpose of our lives should be to attain wisdom, bliss, enlightenment through dharma cultivation. Human life is too precious.

    But I do not know what kind of situation that you or your friend is having, so I would advise you to see a wise Buddhist venerable to discuss this matter.

    There is another thing I have to add to the article I wrote a year ago. As I said if we have already aborted a child, we must practise repentance every single day, and also every day we must dedicate all the merits we have accumulated through our lives to the child. We must practise that even to a child who has defects, and is still unborn. Or even when the child is already born. Sometimes certain situations may change for the better.

    My dharma teacher gave an example of her own. Before she became a Buddhist, she always wished to have a child. And she couldnt have a child. So she went to a temple to pray to a spirit deity (that belongs to the spirit realm) for a child. I cant remember what she was told, but very very soon she became pregnant. But not soon later she had a miscarriage. She was quite sad and when she met my master (that was when my master first came to Singapore), she asked my master why she had a miscarriage. My master only replied four words to her, "Lai2 Zhe3 Bu4 Shan4" (the one who comes is not virtuous). It was only years later that she realised that the child that was to be born came for the lower realm, the spirit realm, however the child could not resonate with her consciousness and then left. If we have 'brightness' in our mind, non-virtuous entities may not necessarily have to come. So during pregnancy one should chant and practise repentance and dedicate merits for the child. But whatever the outcome, we must accept it and do our best for the child and shower our love for him/her.
