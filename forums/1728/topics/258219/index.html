    hi ching74, sorry yeah i borrow your thread to post some info for sharing.

    (abstracted from the newsletter)

    1st storey:
    100 Dragons Hall

    100 dragons hall on the 1st floor of the buddha tooth relic temple is designed according to buddhist temple of the tang dynasty. it is solemn and dignified with a hundred buddhas enshrined on both sides of the hall.

    Avalokitesvara Hall

    behind the 100 dragons hall is the avalokitesvara hall. a beautifully handcrafted bodhisattva avalokitesvara sits upon an elaborated lotus throne appears before your eyes. along the sides are the zodiac protectors, surrounded by smaller statues of cintamanicakra avalokitesvara.

    2nd storey:
    Tripitaka Chamber and Exhibition Hall

    an exhibition hall is located on this floor. enriching religious art exhibitions featuring calligraphy, paintings and sculptures will be held here regularly.
    also situated on this floor is the depository of buddhist texts. this is where the maha-prajna paramita sutra is housed.

    3rd storey:
    Buddhist Culture Museum

    an impressive collection of buddhist artefacts is displayed on the third level of the temple. the featured artefacts encompass and embrace all three branches of buddhism and tells the events in the life of the buddha.

    Sacred Buddha Relic Chamber

    "sariras" are formed due to the cultivation of discipline, meditation and wisdom. they represent the blessings of the most divine nature. the museum of buddhist relics houses the sariras of buddha, his disciples and eminent monks from various countries, for faithful believers to look at with reverence.

    4th storey:
    Sacred Buddha Tooth Relic Chamber

    the sacred buddha tooth relic chamber is the main reliquary of the buddha tooth relic. the gold sacred buddha's tooth relic stupa or reliquary is the center of attraction in this magnificent room. the buddha mandala above this stupa and the gold floor tiles enhance the room. only the sangha have access to the inner chamber to conduct the daily services. public can venerate and observe the daily sevices from the public viewing area.

    Roof:
    Ten Thousand Buddhas Pavilion

    as you arrive at the roof level of buddha tooth relic temple, you will step into a splendidly spacious pavilion surrounded by a sea of fragrant orchids where 10 000 buddhas are enshrined and worshipped. the varieties of orchids planted in the pavilion are named after the maha theros (venerable senior monks) of the various temple.

    in the center of the 10 000 buddhas pavilion is a gigantic cloisonne vairocana buddha prayer wheel. cylindrical in shape, it is a religious artefact commonly used by chinese buddhists. the rim is embossed with esoteric vairocana mantra. a large number of dharma sutras are placed inside. every turn of the wheel represents a single recital of the scripture and mantra. the wheel is a convenient way to recite the sutra.

    Mezzanine Floor:
    Dharma Hall
    Eminent Sangha Wax Museum
    Ancestral Hall (ksitigarbha bodhisattva)
    Lotus Heart Tea House
    Dining Hall
    BTRTM Tang Shop

    Basement:
    Theatre

    Hi,
    in Japan the only two "buddhas" known to use crowns are Dainichi (Vairocana) and Maitreya. However, to distinguish the two further is that Maitreya Bodhisattva does not sit cross-legged. Also if i do not remember wrongly the statues were cast in mainland china, but it is true that the present-day Japanese Buddha statues are a remainder of Tang Dynasty statues, as opposed to the Ming/Qing ones we see in most temples today.

    The Guanyin statue is the Ru-yi Guanyin, quite popular in Japan and even till today, is still the main statue enshrined at the famous Rokkakudo temple in Kyoto city.
