    Precious Relics Exhibition

    Venue Sponsor:
    Ngee Ann Cultural Centre
    Ngee Ann Auditorium
    Teochew Bldg. 97 Tank Road

    Duration:
    21 to 27 November 2005

    We are pleased to finalized the exhibit as mentioned below. H.E. Beru Khyentse Rinpoche has arrived on the 10 November in Singapore. We have taken all the relics photos and placed it in the photo gallery.

    1) Conch Shell of Naga of Namtso Lake
    This precious Conch shell originally belonged to the Naga of Namtso Lake. She offered this conch shell to Ah Tsa Salay, one of the twenty-five disciples of Guru Rinpoche. He then offered it to Dakini Yeshi Tsogyal, consort of Guru Rinpoche who left it in the Samye Treasure Box. After the Cultural Revolution, Beru Khyentse Rinpoche received this precious Conch shell of the 7th century.

    2) Atisha's skull piece
    This piece of skull was found from the famous Atisha's Tara temple. One of the old senior lama found it and offered to H.E. Beru Khyentse Rinpoche in 1982. This bone is over 1000 years old.

    3) Lord Atisha's Relics
    H.H. the 14th Dalai Lama in appreciation of H.E. Beru Khyentse Rinpoche's restoration works of the Namgyal Stupa presented two Lord Atisha's relics (it is more than a thousand years old) to him. Currently, one of these relics has been placed inside the Namgyal Stupa, Nyethang.

    4) Tsa-tsa of Lord Atisha
    Over a thousand year ago, Lord Atisha (982 - 1054 CE) himself made one hundred thousand pieces of Tsa-tsa. This Tsa-tsa was kept in a stupa. This stupa was completely destroyed during the Cultural Revolution (1966 to 1976). A Senior old monk found this Tsa-Tsa and offered it to H.E. Beru Khyentse Rinpoche in 1982 at Nyethang Village, West of Lhasa, Tibet, China (Rinpoche's birth place).

    5) Milarepa's Meditation Belt Piece
    This piece of meditation belt cloth belonged to the Great Yogi Milarepa (1052 - 1135). It was found in Tsurphu's treasure box.

    6) The 1st Karmapa Dusum Khyenpa's Hat Piece
    This part of 1st Karmapa Dusum Khyenpa's (1110-1193) hat was offered by Drupon Dechen Rinpoche to Beru Khyentse Rinpoche in Tsurphu.

    7) 2nd Karma Pakshi's Hat
    This piece of fur from the hat belonging to the 2nd Karma Pakshi (1024-1283)is found from the large Buddha's statue as well. Along with this, the 1st Karmapa Dusum Khyempa's cloth piece of the hat was found.

    6th Karmapa Finger Bone

    9) 8th Karmapa Mikyo Dorje's Waist Belt Piece
    Druppon Dechen Rinpoche also found in the Tsurphu treasure box and offered to Beru Khyentse Rinpoche a waist belt cloth piece belonging to the 8th Karmapa, Mikyo Dorje.

    10) 13th Karmapa's hair

    11) 14th Karmapa's cloth

    12) Tibetan Bowl made of Zab Wood
    This special bowl is made of Zab wood and covered with silver. It is able to prevent food poisoning as the Zab wood will be broken upon contact with poison. It belonged to the 15th Karmapa Khakyab Dorje (1871-1922). It is more than a hundred years old.

    13) His Holiness the 16th Gyalwa Karmapa's own Gampopa Hat
    In 1955, at the age of nine Beru Khyentse Rinpoche was formally recognized and His Holiness 16th Gyalwa Karmapa offered his own Gampopa Hat to him. Miraculously, H.E. Beru Khyentse Rinpoche could wear the hat back then and now without any alteration.

    14) 16th Karmapa's long sleeve shirt

    15) Mother Pill of 16th Karmapa
    The mother pill is only made by Karmapa himself. In his life, 16th Karmapa only made the mother pills four times. This mother pill was made in the final round in 1973 at Rumtek. This mother pill has miraculously produces baby pills as well.

    16) Karmapa's Vajra and Bell
    After the Cultural Revolution, an old lady found these Karmapa's Vajra and Bell besides an old creek in Tsurphu. At that time Tsurphu Monastery, the main seat of Karmapa was destroyed.

    16) Karmapa's Vajra and Bell
    After the Cultural Revolution, an old lady found these Karmapa's Vajra and Bell besides an old creek in Tsurphu. At that time Tsurphu Monastery, the main seat of Karmapa was destroyed.

    17) The Three Jewels
    A large Buddha statue built by the 2nd Karma Pakshi in Tsurphu was destroyed during the Cultural Revolution. Three jewels were found inside the Buddha statue. These jewels are more than 800 years old. Two of which are the Zee stone while the other one is a crystal.

    1 The Nine-point Vajra (Samye Dorje Fence)
    The 9 point Vajra was part of the fence built by Guru Rinpoche in Samye Monastery in the 7th century. This nine-point Vajra was miraculously created by thunder & lightning. This was found in a
    holy mountain area and it is more than eleven hundred years old. It is about 8" in length and very heavy. The metal has a special quality that includes all metallic properties.

    19) H.H. Pema Karpo's Damaru
    This Damaru is about 600 years old and made of ivory. It belongs to the Kunkhyen Pema Karpo (1527 Â– 1592), head of the Drukpa Kagyu Lineage. He was the first incarnation of H.H. Drukchen Rinpoche.

    20) Golden Ganesh Painting
    The picture of the Golden Ganesh is painted by Sangye Won with the same gold ink (Dakini Dorje Tso's miraculous urine).

    21) Buddha Vairocana painting
    This painting by Sangye Won, made in the gold ink portrays a picture of Buddha Vairocana. This painting is 1000 years old.

    "Great Master Sangye Won had expected a very special guest to come and instructed his attendant to give this guest a warm welcome. The attendant waited for a long time for this special guest. After sometime, an old lady who looked beggary visited and asked for a pot from the attendant. She urinated in it and gave this pot to the lama telling him to offer it to the Rinpoche. Naturally the lama was quite angry with such rude offerings. He immediately scolded her and threw the pot away.

    Subsequently, the attendant reported the matter to his Rinpoche. Great Master Sangye Won explained to him that the old lady was a Dakini Dorje Tso and asked him to go back. He found that the urine left in the broken pot had become gold ink." (Source: H.E. Beru Khyentse Rinpoche)

    22) Diamond Sutra
    This scripture was written with gold and silver ink by Great Master Sangye Won, the founder of Taglung Kagyu in Rewo Che Monastery. The gold ink is not ordinary gold as it was the Dakini of Dorje Tso's urine miraculously transformed into gold. It was offered to the Great Master who used this gold ink to write the scripture of Diamond Sutra. It is more than a thousand years old.

    24)Namchak Vajra

    25) Mani stone carved by Mahasiddha

    26) Jigme Puntsok's father pill

    27) 1st Jamgon Kongtrul Rinpoche's cloth piece
    "Jamgon Kongtrul Lodro Thaye was one of the most brilliant stars in the galaxy of scholars and siddhas from Tibet , the land of snow. Predicted by the Buddhas, he was the crown jewel in the rime (non- sectarian) movement of Buddhism in Tibet."

    2 1stJamyang Khyentse Wangpo's cloth

    29) 1stJamyang Khyentse Wangpo's Bone
    This is a precious bone of the Great Rime Master Jamyang Khyentse Wangpo.

    "The 1st Jamyang Khyentse Wangpo is a great master of the non-sectarian lineage holder of the Tibetan Vajrayana Buddhism (1820 - 1892).

    Jamyang Khyentse Wangpo was the emanation of great Bodhisattva Manjushri. Although he had attained Buddhahood from the very beginning, he continued to appear in this realm of beings as a Bodhisattva to liberate the last of the beings from the sufferings of the cyclic existence." (Source: www.khyenkong-tharjay.org)

    30) 1st Chokyur Lingpa's cloth
    "Chokgyur Lingpa. (1829-1870). A treasure revealer and contemporary of Jamyang Khyentse Wangpo and Jamgon Kongtrul. Regarded as one of the major tertons in Tibetan history, his termas are widely practiced by both the Kagyu and Nyingma schools. Chokgyur Lingpa means 'Sanctuary of Eminence'."

    31) Guru Rinpoche's Clay Statue
    The three Great Rime Masters: Jamyang Khyentse Wangpo, Jamgon Kongtrul and Chogyor Lingpa with other high lamas performed a Grand Guru Rinpoche Puja. This Guru Rinpoche's (Kutsab Dewa Chembo) clay statue was made during that time.

    32) Small Tsa-Tsa moulded from Thunder Lightning
    This was found in Changri Demar mountain of 16,000 ft high, facing the front of the Tharjay Monastery in Nangchen, Drogshok area.

    33) Gold Pleated Pen Holder
    A Tibetan-style bamboo pen holder which is made of iron with gold pleated design is over three hundred years old.

    34) 1st Shamarpa Drakpa Senghe's Bone Piece
    This bone piece of the First Kunzig Shamarpa, Khedrup Drakpa Senge, (1284-1349) (Red Hat Lama of Tibet) was offered by Druppon Rinpoche to Beru Khyentse Rinpoche.

    35) Iron Plates / Armour of Ling Gesar
    These two small pieces of Iron plates / armour, belonging to Ling Gesar, the famous and victorious Warrior of Kham area, Tibet are thousand years old.

    36) Patrul Rinpoche's cloth

    37) Ju Mipham Rinpoche's bone

    3 Lama Jamyang Gyaltsen's cloth piece
    He wore this clothing when he attained rainbow body. He was a Sakya lama from Taglung Monastery.

    39) Iron Stamp
    This old stamp is made of iron with gold pleated design. It has Mongolian letter and style and is more than a hundred years old.

    40) 1st Beru Khyentse Rinpoche (1895 - 1945) Porcelain Statue
    This 1st Beru Khyentse porcelain statue made in 1941 in China is very life like. The head was broken during the Cultural Revolution and was put back without a dent.

    41) Great Terton Migyur Dorje's cloth
    He was a terton who found the Padma Benza text.

    42) Old Brocade with Dragon Design
    This is also known as Khandro Tinthak (meaning: weaved by Dakini) and is more than seven hundred years old.

    43) Gold Thangka of Lord Buddha
    This thangka is about approximately over 1000 years old and portrays a picture of Buddha cutting his own hair.
