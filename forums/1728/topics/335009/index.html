  • soul2soul

    Theravada POV, rebirth is determined by rebirth-relinking consciousness upon dissolution of the current life. It means, the state of mind upon death is important as it will determine the next destiny as we all have our almost infinite stores of Akusala (bad/unwholesome) kamma, all competing to enter the mind-door at death. Unfortunate people who commits suicide (mostly) are in bad state of mind, maybe too distraught with loss, grief-stricken, extremely depressed or in anger. These unwholesome state of mind, will most likely enter the mind door upon death and invokes the dying consciousness, hence rebirth-linking consciousness. However, not necessarily they will appear at woeful places, as appearance in such places need also the supportive kamma. It's best we don't speculate too much on people who die committing suicide, but to pass our merits to them when we perform dana or other meritocarious deeds. It's a good blessing to be able to pass away peacefully, as our state of mind will most likely be wholesome, therefore increasing the chances for rebirth in good places.
