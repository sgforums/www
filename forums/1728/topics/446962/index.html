    90 seconds of emotion

    Karen Koenig, psychotherapist and author of the Rules of Normal Eating, recently wrote a post about what Dr. Jill Bolte Taylor calls the "90 second emotion rule." The idea is that it takes 90 seconds or less for each emotion to be automatically triggered, surge through the body, and dissipated. Once the emotion (chemical component) is gone physiologically, it then becomes a choice to continue as she puts it the "neurocircuitry" to run or not. In this sense, we become more attuned to the emotion in the current moment versus continuously cruising on autopilot. We become aware to what we are feeling, learning to yes, choose whether to take it, leave it , or change it.

     

    http://betweenlivingandexisting.blogspot.com/2009/08/90-seconds-of-emotion.html

    Experiences has certain characteristics that one realised from insights. These characteristics are no-selfness, non-duality and emptiness. When afflictive emotion have arised, awareness of no-selfness, non-duality and emptiness are momentarily lost. I understand this as Distraction.

    However, even at these moments of distraction, the characteristics are still of no-selfness, non-duality and emptiness. When in distraction, experiences are not realising/feeling these characteristic at that moment. At such times, the experience seems solid... although really it is empty. At such times, there feels a self is experiencing the affliction, although really there is no-self. At such times, there feels as if there is the affliction and the one feeling the affliction, although really there is non-duality.

    This is what makes liberation possible ?
