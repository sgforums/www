    Extracted from E-sangha

    Hi. I have read "LiaoFan's Four Lessons" recently, so let me endeavour to address your query from a layman's point of view. In a nutshell:

    * All men have their respective paths to walk in life, with the final destination being death.

    * Along this path, there will be happenings of ups & downs, haves & have-nots, endowments & depravations, gains & losses; just like you will find pumps & potholes, uphills & downhills along a common road.

    * The happenings on the path of life could take the forms of being borne into a good family or otherwise, being blessed with intellect or otherwise, attaining a high education or otherwise, getting a good job or otherwise, marrying or remaining single, blessed with filial children or otherwise, becoming a millionaire or otherwise, being stricken with fatal illness or otherwise, living to a very old age or otherwise -- this is one's DESTINY.

    * Such destiny is a realization/ripening of KARMA (i.e. volitional actions) that a person has accumulated as imprints in his consciousness from previous lives or from an earlier part of that person's current life.

    * LiaoFan taught that past karma is not fixed, whether in form or in intensity, but can be lessened in intensity or even completely overriden by one's volitional actions in this current live. For example, LiaoFan was originally destined not to have a son, but through genuinely repenting for his past actions and performing numerous merits, he succeeded in turning around this destiny and was blessed with a son.

    Therefore, one's destiny can be changed by "fighting" past karma with current karma. Past karma will only run its course of ripening without change IF one does nothing about it in current life. Kapish??
