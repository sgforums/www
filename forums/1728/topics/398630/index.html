    Agree to Disagree - Conversations on Conversion is an e-book offering practical tips to fellow Buddhists so that we can say "no" compassionately to religious proselytism. The e-booklet contains simple and effective responses that Buddhists can use when approached at work, in school, in public places, in hospitals and even at home. 

    If you know find this information useful, please help us to email our link, www.conversion.buddhists.sg <http://www.conversion.buddhists.sg>  out or link to your fb, twit or blog, so that we can reach out to fellow Buddhists. 


    More about this project
    We are a group of Buddhists from the counselling, human resource, social work and teaching professions. We have developed an e-book with practical tips to fellow Buddhists so that we can say "no" compassionately to religious proselytism. We decided to do this project after the Prime Minister raised a concern regarding the maintenance of religious harmony in Singapore at the 2009 National Day Rally. He pointed out that the aggressive pushing of one’s religion onto others can prove detrimental to our society and highlighted the importance of maintaining racial and religious harmony through tolerance and restraint, as well as preserving the common space that all Singaporeans share.
