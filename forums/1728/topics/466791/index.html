    Date: 13 March 2013 Wednesday
    Time: 7:30pm to 9:30pm
    Venue: Poh Ming Tse Temple
    Topic: "I Wish I Can Delete Unwanted Memories" - The Meditation Approach

    Synopsis:
    Have you ever wished you can delete unwanted memories? How do you handle uncomfortable, disturbing or painful memories that comes to mind at the most unexpected moments?
    What does it take to ride smoothly through the blips in life? Join Venerable Tenzin Priyadarshi in this talk on the meditation approach to the "good, bad and ugly" memories. 

     About the Venerable Tenzin Priyadarshi

    The Venerable Tenzin Priyadarshi is the Founding Director of The Dalai Lama Center for Ethics and Transformative Values at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, a non-partisan collaborative think tank focused on interdisciplinary research and programmes related to the development of human and global ethics. He received his undergraduate degree (summa cum laude) and was also an Integral Honors Scholar (studying Philosophy and Physics), and has a graduate degree in Comparative Philosophy of Religion from Harvard University.  Venerable Priyadarshi serves on the board of several philanthropic, humanitarian, and educational organisations. He lectures internationally on subjects ranging from philosophy, science, ethics and religion to socio-political thought.
