    As my Master said, we can pay our respects to our ancestors.

    It doesn't even have to be your direct family's ancestors, but anyone who has done great deeds, deserved to be respected, etc... you can pay your reverence and respects to them.

    This is NOT about worshipping them, and not about asking for favour... this is about paying our respect and remembrance of these people for their noble deeds.

    Of course though, we should not pay reverence or honour the evil, criminals, murderers, and so on that has harmed others...

    Praying to the dead will *not* make them suffer less in their afterlife... depending on their karma, they may be reborn in higher realms like celestial or human realm, or the lower realms.

    However, nothing can benefit them (whether offerings, burning paper money, or praying) in their afterlife... the only way we can benefit them is to dedicate merits to them. Also see: http://www.jenchen.org.sg/vol5no1a.htm

    As for praying to our ancestors for favours, honestly speaking, it doesn't work this way. Because if our ancestors have been reborn in higher realms or human realm... it is already a different lifetime.. they have moved on. Similar goes to being reborn in lower realms like hell or animal realms...

    If your ancestor has turned into a ghost, he himself is undergoing suffering in the ghost realm... so how can he possibly give you much blessing when he himself does not have much blessing?

    So, it is advisable not to be too superstitious over these things.

    We can pay our respect to ancestors, but it is not really good to be superstitious or dependent.

    Jhana beings are always in a state of absorption in jhanic bliss. In the 4 upper jhana planes, their consciousness is simply in a state of expansive absorption. They experience the bliss of formless absorption.

    After death, of course they will continue their cycle of rebirth because they have not been liberated from samsara yet (except one particular plane in the 4th jhanas where anagamis are reborn to attain arhatship, those arhats in the 4th jhana planes have transcended samsara)

    Even if you attain the 8th jhana, the plane of neither perception nor non perception, you will still be reborn after death and possibly be reborn in the lower realms. That is why rebirth in celestial plane is unreliable, and dangerous.

    We should not seek rebirth in celestial planes, but seek to transcend rebirth, transcend karmic birth. Then we can experience the bliss of the deathless.

    Four Modes Of Birth
    Buddhism states that there are four modes of birth - namely :
    egg-born beings (andaja)
    womb-born beings (jalabuja)
    moisture-born beings (samsedaja)
    beings having spontaneous births (opapatika)

    This broad classification embraces the 4 modes of birth of all living beings.
    Birds and oviparous snakes belong to the first division.

    The womb-born creatures comprise all human beings,
    some devas inhabiting the earth,
    and some animals that take conception in a motherÂ’s womb.

    Embryos, using moisture as nidus for their growth,
    like certain lowly forms of animal life, belong to the third class.

    Beings having a spontaneous birth are generally invisible to the physical eye.
    Conditioned by their past Kamma, they appear spontaneously,
    without passing through an embryonic stage.
    Petas and Devas normally, and Brahmas belong to this class.
