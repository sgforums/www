    Tsewong Rinpoche, head of the Sangye Teng monastery in
    Bhutan, is a living master of Kagyu & Nyingma Chöd lineages,
    and the dharma heir of PeGyal Lingpa. Having spent 20 years in
    meditative retreat, he is a realized Dzokchen master who embodies
    the limitless compassion and fearless wisdom of Chöd.

    Lama Jinpa is director the School of Chö. Having completed
    the 3 year Tibetan retreat under Kalu Rinpoche in 1989, he has
    practiced holistic medicine for 33 years and studied dharma
    for 28 years. Currently authoring several books on Chöd and
    5-element healing, Jinpa is dedicated to this authentic lineage.

    Lama Pema Tenzin is a master of meditation and ritual, sacred dance and art and has built several stupas in America with Rinpoche.. The brother of Tsewong Rinpoche, Pema began studying dharma at the age of six under his father, a renowned yogi. In demand at dharma centers throughout the US for his various skills, he is an accomplished thangka artist, specializing in creating Healing Mandalas
