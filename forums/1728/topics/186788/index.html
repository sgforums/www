    'Trees', 'Humans', 'Sky', are but labels. They are empty of seperate, inherent existence. It is a usual mental habit in most humans to immediately label something and think of it as Real with Substance. But the very 'thingness' is an illusion, it is due to our attachments to forms. Because all these phenomenal existence are conditioned, they do not exist 'by themselves', but are interdependant. They are impermanent, meaning they do not last even a single second - it is subject to change and is fluctuating. If we are to grasp on Forms, we will miss their essence.

    All is undifferentiated Awareness, Buddha Nature/Dharma Nature. Be raw & undefine. Look at the colours, feel the air & experience the presence & be totally absorbed into the experience. Awareness is that flow of experience. There is no self, only this mere seeing.

    Have you ever looked out of your window, and marvel at the sceneries? If your mind is clear at the moment, and you become so completely absorbed in it, there is no longer any thoughts arising, it no longer seems you are looking anymore. You become the seeing - and everything seems so vivid and clear in its entirety. That is Bliss. I believe everyone has such experiences now and then, even I do sometimes.

    When you become enlightened, you go BEYOND theories. Enlightenment is direct, intuitive, clear. It is BEYOND doubts. There is without a single doubt remaining after enlightenment. Theories is just another concept, another mind perceptions. And all these are rooted in Ignorance. If we are to be enlightened, eventually we will have to throw away all these.

    Buddhism is not about theories - in fact I would say it is not even a philosophy, but beyond philosophy.

    Detaching From Thoughts

    Ven. Jue-Hsing

    A scholar once asked, "Buddhism seems a great theory, and I find it useful. Is Buddhism a Philosophy?"

    "Master Shen-Kai once said 'Buddhism is not Philosophy but beyond Philosophy,'" I replied.

    "Why so?" The scholar asked.

    "Philosophy is a system of thoughts based upon logical reasoning." I answered. "However, Buddhism is about realizing truths while detaching from thoughts."
    The Perfect One is free from any theory, for the Perfect One has understood what the body is, and how it arises, and passes away. He has understood what feeling is, and how it arises, and passes away. He has understood what perception is, and how it arises, and passes away. He has understood what the mental formations are, and how they arise, and pass away. He has understood what consciousness is, and how it arises, and passes away.

    Therefore, I say, the Perfect One has won complete deliverance through the extinction, fading away, disappearance, rejection, and getting rid of all opinions and conjectures, of all inclination to the vainglory of I and mine.

    - Majjhima Nikaya, 72
