    Buddha never denied dying, did he? He taught the Impermanence nature of all conditioned phenomena. He taught everyone to let go of attachments because of the impermanent and ungraspable nature. Impermanence is one of the 3 Dharma Seals. There is nothing abstract about the truths that the Buddha taught. Buddha has three bodies. Nirmanakaya, Sambhogakaya, Dharmakaya. When Buddha appears in Nirmanakaya (his physical form with 32 major marks and 80 minor marks) it is appeared to us as a temporary form. But the Dharmakaya is unconditional and eternal. We all have the same unconditional Buddha Nature although we have not realised it and are therefore still illusional.

    Nirvana is Unconditional - therefore beyond anything that can be defined in time and space. In order to 'enter' Nirvana all attachments and illusions on the Conditional must be let go of. 'Desire to be enlightened' is also a desire, but a necessary first step. Gradually as one practises he will also let go of such thoughts and simply remain in Naturalness - what you already are from the very 'beginning'. It is not forming a new perception, because all perceptions are still rooted in ignorance. Enlightenment is simply direct seeing without the layer of illusions that most of us still have... not learning something 'new'.

    Also, what did the Buddha not answer? He actually already described the formation and cessation of the world system without a creator. He just did not explain in terms of Creator, that doesn't mean he kept quiet.
