    The Great Hanshin Earthquake (阪神・淡路大震災, Hanshin-Awaji Daishinsai?), or Kobe earthquake as it is more commonly known overseas, was an earthquake in Japan that measured 6.9[1] on the Richter magnitude scale and 7.2[2] on the Japanese seismic intensity scale[3]. The focus of the earthquake was located 16km beneath its epicenter, on the northern end of Awaji Island, 20 km away from the city of Kobe with a population of 1.5 million. This area is in the middle of the fault zone of the Pacific, the Eurasian and the Philippine tectonic plates.
