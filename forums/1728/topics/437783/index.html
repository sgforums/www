    The Chokgyur Lingpa Foundation’s Second Annual

    Sadhana Ritual Course
    Trinley Nyingpo & Zabtik Drolma

    October 17 – November 17 2011

    From Oct 17 – Nov 17 2011, the Chokgyur Lingpa Foundation will be hosting its second one-month long Sadhana Ritual course. This year, the course will concentrate on the sadhana practices of Trinley Nyingpo and Zabtik Drolma. (For more information on these practices, see below.)

    Aim
    The aim of the course is to provide students with an opportunity to learn in detail about both the meaning and practical aspects of Vajrayana ritual practice.

    Content
    During the course, students will be instructed on the meaning of development stage practice in general and the visualisation stages of these two sadhanas in particular. Students will also be taught the different melodies and mudras for the practice; how to make the related tormas; play the vajra and bell, damaru, and drum; how to perform the feast offerings; and how to act as the shrine master and chant master.

    Teaching
    The teachings will be given in Tibetan by Lama Tenpal Gyatso, one of Ka-Nying Shedrub Ling monastery’s senior monks, and translated into English. Teachings will be held at Pal Kagyu Shedrup Tashi Dhargay Phuntsok Ling monastery (aka Riwoche monastery), Tinchuli, Kathmandu, which is just five minutes walk from the Boudhanath stupa.

    Classes will be held for approximately five hours a day, six days a week, and all of the participants will also have the chance to perform the practices together each day. Aspects that are common to the two sadhanas will be taught together in the same class, while those that are particular will be taught in separate classes.

    At the beginning of the course, His Eminence Tsikey Chokling Rinpoche will kindly bestow upon all participants the necessary empowerments and oral transmissions. At the end of the course, participants will perform the practice together with Rinpoche.

    Materials
    All necessary practice texts, as well as tea and snacks, will be provided as part of the course.

    Lama Tenpal Gyatso has been a monk at Ka-Nying Shedrub Ling monastery in Boudhanath, Kathmandu for almost thirty years. He has successfully completed a traditional three-year retreat under the guidance of Kyabje Tulku Urgyen Rinpoche and has also served Phakchok Rinpoche as his attendant for more than twenty years. He taught the Sadhana Ritual Course in 2010 and also teaches occassionally in various Rangjung Yeshe Gomde and other dharma centres around the world.
