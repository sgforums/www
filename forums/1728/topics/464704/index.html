  • Weychin
    22 Jan 13, 03:01
    For ornamental fishes, most are assumed to be bought for display, another category are fishes bred as fodder for ornamental fish such guppies, mosquito fishes and gold fishes, prolific breeders. The feral population of guppies and mosquito fishes are quite successful you see them everywhere, even in sea water! Goldfishes no so much, but I have seen some condos with water features that keep fishes as mosquito control, perhaps you can convince someone to release them there! As for farm bred seawater fishes, it is a little more tricky, as we cannot tell how much survival instinct each fish has. If the wish to release seawater fishes remains, search for food fishes indigenious or feral species already coexisting with local fauna. Never release them in a pristine environment, fresh water or sea! First a stressed fish does when released is to seek cover to hide and a fish will need to feed after it settles down! So cover and food source are essential when releasing animals!
