    I'm surprised there is no one posting anything about your own sufferings. Do you have problems in your life? Understand what causes the problem.So many people had focused on enlightenment discussion that fails to address the powerful force of repentence.

    If you have no courage of confession of your wrong-doings (perhaps that breaks the law), I suggest that you repent in front of Buddha (or in your mind) and vow never to commit it again. The great compassion of Buddha is willing to relieve the sufferings of those who commit it and offers ways and means to lessen the bad karmic effect on our suffering. If you repent with your heart and mind, not only will the bad karma be lessened, you will slowly be healing until you recover. This thread is for those who have picked up the courage and wants to confess, repent for the wrong-doings and are "awakened". You need not necessarily confess. But those who have past experience should contribute. Buddhism offers a very good effective means to relieve the sufferings in our human world.

    If you still do not know the root to the problem, perhaps, saying it out will let members discuss what the roots are. However, I believe the 'awakening' have to be done by yourself.     Causes of Sufferings

    Buddha had said all sufferings originated from roots of evil: greed, hatred, ignorance. I have no greed and practise generosity. But I suffer from hatred and ignorance. The causes of my mental disease originated from killing a chicken when I was a boy scout during my teenage times. It struck me as I was chanting today and practising mindfulness. In fact, I cut the throat of the chicken, which makes the chicken cry in pain, his own hatred that causes my own sufferings of mental disease today. When I realize this, I cry it out lout in compassion and ask for Buddha's forgiveness. How can I make such a grave mistake of killing under ignorance? What forces me to carry out such killing?

    I have also killed insects (cockroaches, houseflies). I can still remember when I was a teenager. The mirror suddenly falls off while I was bathing in my bathroom (not sure why) and it cuts my stomach (luckily, it did not cause me to end my life).  You can associate this with revenge minds from those I killed.

    Although some masters say that mental disease was a result of bad karma resulted from hatred, greed or spoiling dharma services in my past life, I believe this killing the chicken is the root to the causes my disease. Having been "awakened" to this wrong-doing, I vow never to engage in any form of killing and will start practising life liberation and vegetarian today.

    If you don't believe in the Buddha, you will have to believe in karma. When the bad karma ripens under the right causes and conditions, it will lead to sufferings.

    Repentence, Precepts and Vows

    I repent for all wrong-doings in my life, be it be acts of killing, sexual misconduct, bad speech and other wrong-doings.

    I've taken the 5 precepts:

    No killing

    No stealing

    No sexual misconduct

    No lying (or even bad speech)

    No intoxicant.

    I vow to cut off all evils.

    I vow to practise all goods

    I vow to help save all sentient beings from all sufferings.

    I have undertaken to practise vegetarian for the next of my life (if possible) and refrain from killing.

    May I dedicate the merits of knowing this karma (roots to the suffering) to all sentient beings.

    May I practise loving-kindness and compassion.

    @yhjow,  keep up this strong faith you have with the Three Jewels and you will have great protection from all the Buddhas and Bodhisattvas that abide in the ten directions.

     

    All of us have created number-less causes to be born in the lower realms which produce the greatest obstacles to achieve realisations and create the greatest suffering.  Thank you for sharing your experience and wisdom on this.   It is really inspiring to hear how you gradually overcome your difficulties, subdued the fear within and took charge of your life.  I can see you now have clear faith in the Three Jewels.

     

    Karma is definite to be experienced, so every negative action that have been created will definitely bring its result, no matter how long it takes, until it is purified.  And, from one negative karma, one has to experience the result over and over again for so many lifetimes.  In this way, samsara has no end!

     

    Another purification method is the Practice of Prostrations to the 35 Confession Buddhas and Prostrations to the 7 Medicine Buddhas, which is followed by reflecting on emptiness of each of the negative actions, remembering that even negative actions do not truly exist from their own side.  They arise in dependence on causes and conditions and are merely labelled by the mind.

     

    I personally find this practice beneficial as it helps to discipline the body, mind and speech.  If time permits, one can add the 21 long Vajrasattva mantras and the Praise to Avalokiteshvara.  This way, the accumulation of merit and the power of purification are increased.  It was difficult when I first started because you have to really focus but after a while, you will feel the benefits.  I experienced better control of my body, mind and speech.  I felt lighter, calmer and developed better concentration than before.
