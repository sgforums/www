    Ratnasambhava

    The name Ratnasambhava means "the Jewel-born One" or "Origin of Jewels." The three Buddhist Jewels are the Buddha, the Dharma and the Sangha.

    In Chinese he is called Bau-Shing-For.
    In Japanese he is called Ho-sho. (Fig 1)
    In Tibetan he is called Rin-Chen-Jung-Gnas. (Fig 2 & 3)

    He is a Dhyani buddha within vajrayana buddhism residing in the south quarter of a mandala. Ratnasambhava is found throughout all four tantra classifications most notably in the Anuttarayoga class. He rules over the element of earth and embodies the skandha of feeling or sensation. His consort is Mamaki, and they represent the transmutation of the poison of pride (spiritual, intellectual and human pride) into the Wisdom of Equality, one sees all beings and the Buddha as having the same nature. Tibetan Buddhists teach that with the Wisdom of Equality one sees all things with divine impartiality and recognizes the divine equality of all beings.

    Ratnasambhava's mantra is Om Ratnasambhava Tram. His body is yellow in color and the right hand is placed in the mudra of varada - supreme generosity. It is the gesture of giving or charity, which portrays him offering compassion and protection to his disciples. He is peaceful in appearance, with one face and two hands. The animal that upholds Ratnasambhava's throne is the horse, denoting impetus and liberation.

    It's great to have posting introducing the esoteric schools.

    Just wish to add a word of caution that in the esoteric paths, it is extremely important for a Dharma student to obtain the transmission and oral instructions by an authentic lineage holder, before one could embark on the chanting of mantras, visualization of deities or other practices.

    The reason for the necessity of the oral transmission and instructions is that the esoteric path is extremely special and it is easy for one to misunderstand the practice or venture the wrong way. As such, it is important for one to gain the proper instructions and permission from an authentic lineage holder.

    I know that a lot of these pictures, mantras and visualization instructions are now freely available in bookstores and on the internet. But I just wish to protect the Dharma and the practitioners as much as possible by stating this. I'm not stating it on my own, but this has been expressed by many of my Dharma teachers.

    If one feels inspired by the esoteric path and is interested to learn more, I would really encourage one to actively seek guidance from qualified Dharma teachers.
