    Dear bohiruci,

    I'm not sure which Buddhist association do you mean. So, I'll state their names (then & now) & affliation with years of formation so as to verify :

    SGI -

    Singapore Nichiren Shoshu Buddhist Association, formed in 1972, affliated to Nichiren Shoshu as Soka Gakkai was a lay org of Shoshu. Renamed Singapore Soka Association (SSA) in Jan 1992 due to split between Shoshu & Soka in Dec 1991 & become SGI branch in Singapore.

    Nichiren Shoshu -

    Shobo Nichiren Shoshu Singapore, formed in 1991 during Soka-Shoshu split. The Shoshu branch in Singapore itself split into another 2 org's later till 2003, i.e. Nichiren Buddhism Association & Nichiren Shoshu Hokke Buddhist Association. All 3 org's merged again in 2003 as Nichiren Shoshu Buddhist Association (Singapore).

    Nichiren Shu -

    Lotus Sutra Buddhist Association (LSBA) formed in 1986 by ex-Soka members. Independent & not affiliated to any Nichiren school but loosely in contact with Shoshu. Renamed in Shobo Nichiren Shoshu Singapore in 1991 as above till 2003 & reverted back as LSBA due to question of correctness of Shoshu doctrines with breaking of affliation & becoming independent.

    Gained contact with Nichiren Shu in early 2004 with questions cleared by NShu. Officially affiliation with NShu in 13 Nov 2004 & things continue ever since.

    Status present : Lotus Sutra Buddhist Association (LSBA) is the Nichiren Shu branch in Singapore from Nov 2004 & are Sakyamuni Buddha based (therefore in the mainstream).

    So, bohiruci. If you mean LSBA, it is the above. What it is now in 2006 is totally different from 1997 then. I hope you will visit them again to see for yourself.
