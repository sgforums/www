    BUDDHISM FOR ABSOLUTE BEGINNERS 2006

    Venue: Buddhist Library

    19 May 2006 (Friday 7.30pm to 9.30pm)
    Relevance of Buddhism
    The relevance of Buddhism to the individual and the world.
    Speaker: Professor W Chandima

    2 June 2006 (Friday 7.30pm to 9.30pm)
    What is Wrong?
    The Buddhist perspective of life's problems as outlined in the Four Noble
    truths.
    Speaker: Prof W Chandima

    9 June 2006 (Friday 7.30pm to 9.30pm)
    The Buddha
    Biography of Buddha Gotama and his contributions to the world: the
    significance of Buddhahood.
    Speaker: Mr Wee Sin Tho

    16 June 2006 (Friday 7.30pm to 9.30pm)
    The Remedy
    The practical solution to life's problems prescribed in the Noble
    Eightfold Path.
    Speaker: Mr Jeffrey Po Gim Tee

    23 June 2006 (Friday 7.30pm to 9.30pm)
    Ethical Living
    Morality as embodied in the Buddhist precepts
    Speaker: Ven B Dhammaratana

    30 June 2006 (Friday 7.30pm to 9.30pm)
    The World As It Is
    The Buddhist view on the characteristics of existence.
    Speaker: Mr Wee Sin Tho

    7 July 2006 (Friday 7.30pm to 9.30pm)
    Life After Death
    Concepts of Karma and Rebirth.
    Speaker: Dr Wong Weng Fai

    14 July 2006 (Friday 7.30pm to 9.30pm)
    A Jewel With Many Facets
    The many facets of Buddhism reflected in the different traditions.
    Speaker: Dr Tham Weng Yew

    21 July 2006 (Friday 7.30pm to 9.30pm)
    Reorienting Our Lives
    The meaning of becoming a Buddhist.
    Speaker: Mr Jeffrey Po Gim Tee

    Course Fee: $40.00 (members)/$50.00 (non-members). Please register at the
    Buddhist Library counter.
