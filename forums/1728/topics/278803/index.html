    Welcome to Life Without A Centre.

    Here, we speak of another possibility: that there is only ever the present appearance of life, with no individual at its core who could ever escape even if they wanted to...

    The message of nonduality is utterly simple, utterly obvious, utterly ordinary. As simple as the sound of the rain splish-splashing on the roof, as ordinary as going to the toilet...

    Life Without A Centre is not about taking on any new beliefs, nor getting rid of old ones. This is not about "giving up the search" or "transcending thought" or "dropping the ego". No, this message is simpler than any of that: it's about life as it already is. It's about the possibility that this (what is happening presently) is all there is, and that the person who seeks, who suffers, who longs to be free, has no more reality than a presently arising thought. That is, "you" are just a belief, arising now.

    What we are talking about is nothing less than the death of the individual self. But this is not something that an individual could ever bring about, not a goal that "you" could ever reach. And of course you don't need to: already, the "I" is just a fiction, already there is absolute freedom: freedom from the burden of self, freedom from the burden of having to choose, freedom from the burden of being a "person in the world", with a heavy past and an uncertain future.

    And so the clarity and liberation sought for a lifetime reveals itself. And it is clearly seen, by no-one, that the extraordinary was always right here, present in and as the utterly ordinary.

    There has only ever been this obvious present appearance of life. There has only ever been aliveness, Oneness, Being, playing at being a "world", playing at forgetting it was only ever playing.

    And the spiritual search only ever brought us here: to hear that none of it ever happened in the first place.

    This message is about liberation, right here, right now, in the midst of this very life. A life lived by nobody. A life without a centre.

    - Jeff Foster, UK, August 2007

    What is Nonduality? - Reflections on the utterly obvious....
    by Jeff Foster

    This is the end of all hope, because it is the end of a future, and what is the future but a hope?

    And with the end of hope comes the death of the one who hopes. But this death is not something a person could ever bring about, for what is the attempt to bring this about but the Hope of all Hopes?

    And so, the end of all hope is death, death of the individual, death of the one who hopes. And death looks like this:

    Breathing, the heart beating, sounds in the room, sensations in the body.....

    In fact, death looks a lot like life, except now there is nobody there living that life, just life itself playing out spontaneously, of its own accord, in its own time. This is a life lived by nobody, and yet there are still faces, places, feelings, ups and downs. Although now the ups are equal to the downs, pain is equal to pleasure, the most excruciating suffering is equal to the greatest joy. Because with the death of the person comes the death of all opposites, all opposition, all duality, which is to say that everything now exists in perfect balance (as it always has done). And yet, there is nobody to know that balance, nobody who could name it, nobody who could even speak of it even if they wanted to.
