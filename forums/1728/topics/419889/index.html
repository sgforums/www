    In terms of the morality aspect, there can be a lot of similarities. As a matter of fact, Buddhism and in fact religion itself can't monopolize or set itself as the sole arbiter of morality - society already imparts moral teachings with or without religion. Therefore as you can see, morality, even though foundational to Buddhism, is not owned by Buddhism and in fact not owned by religion. What is special about Buddhism isn't the foundational aspects such as morality. We might as well say 'moral education is a foundation of Buddhism' - that does not mean however it covers the entirety of Buddhism, since it is only the mere foundation. That said, without the foundations such as five precepts and other aspects of morality, you can't progress far in other aspects like samadhi and prajna.

    But in terms of other aspects like samadhi and especially prajna (other religions do lead followers to samadhi but only Buddhism leads followers to the realization of Emptiness), Buddhism is different.
