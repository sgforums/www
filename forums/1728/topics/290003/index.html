    Originally posted by Spnw07:
    Questions about marriage:

    1) Is Buddhism against marriage or for marriage? My personal and limited understanding so far is that Buddhism neither encourages or discourages marriage among male and female or among the same sex.
    That's right. Marriage is an individual choice. Furthermore, marriage is a very cultural thing. It differs from culture to culture. Buddha doesn't speak so much on these things. As for same sex marriage, this is another topic. Though Buddha never spoke about marriages, I do not think he encourages this. But we do not discriminate them.
    2) As a buddhist, should I tell myself not to get married cos I will be subjecting myself to another endless cycle of rebirth due to lust or craving?
    No. It is really your choice. You can get married. But of course, you must continue to practice the dharma. Unless you want to be a monk, then you shouldn't get married.
    3) As a buddhist, is it preferable to choose a future partner of the same faith, so that one, we can agree and practise together on many common aspects of the faith; two, we can help each other to prepare a proper Buddhist funeral when either one passes away?
    Ideally, yes. But if you all can maintain harmony inspite of religious difference, then it is good. There are lots of families with differences in religious views but they also live harmoniously. It all depends.

    It can be argued that since you are willing to marry a Christian spouse, you must have accepted to some significant degree, the concept of a creator God and Christians see dating/marriage as one of those good chances to help you embrace Christ.

    But from a Buddhist point of view, funeral preparations can become very sensitive and even quarrelsome affairs when the religious beliefs of both spouses are different. For example, when I die, can I reasonably expect my wife to chant the name of a Buddha or to do merits in my name by donating cash or donations in kind to a temple or the Sangha? Can I trust her not to try to bring a Reverend father to convert me to Christianity while I'm still breathing my last breath or has just passed away? Technically, she's not wrong, as she believes that she is doing all this for my own good, cos to her Christianity is the only good and true faith.

    Though I perfectly understand my choice of a spouse is a personal one, I want as much information as possible from both laymen and Buddhists about this subject to help me choose wisely.
