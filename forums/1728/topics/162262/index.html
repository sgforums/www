    Apart from this powerful 'energy', it is possible to receive blessings from Medicine Buddha. I would like to relate (I have posted this before) one true story:

    http://buddhism.sgforums.com/?action=thread_display&thread_id=147300

    Originally posted by An Eternal Now:
    Speaking of Medicine Buddha... I roughly remembered a true story I was told over a year ago, by a Buddhist friend who experienced it himself. I can't really remember the detail now as it has been a long time.. but anyway...

    This guy had some problem and needed to go for brain surgeries several times... Before he went, he went to a foreign lama who came to Singapore to teach the teachings on Medicine Buddha. I can't remember which Lama, but I know the name starts with S. Anyway, during some kind of ritual (or maybe not ritual, something else), everyone was sitting in a circle. The lama walked to him and pulled him out of the circle, and told him he got (Karmic) Obstructions, and he needs to practice well.

    Soon, he went for the surgery (or is it not? i cant remember)... I believe he had been practising.. and then he slipped into a coma. For 4 days he remained unconscious, until the 4th day, the first thing he remembered was seeing a figure of a man, he done quite some description but i really cannot remember it anymore. After awaking from it he asked the doctors/nurses whether someone came to visit him. They said nope, nobody had visited him when he woke up. It was later he visited the lama again, and the lama told him the figure was a manifest of the Medicine Buddha, and he was saved by the Medicine Buddha. Later he went for several surgeries and succeeded. I still see him sometimes these days.
    Even though your cousin has not taken refuge, it is also good to chant for him the Buddha-name or mantra of Medicine Buddha. However, you receive most of the benefits of the chanting rather than him. There is a saying... when you chant, 6 parts goes to u, 1 part goes to person (you intend to chant to benefit). It is best for him to chant or at least recognise your chanting. And it is also important if possible, that he takes refuge in the Three Treasures, Buddha Dharma Sangha. However if he does not believe in Buddha it may be very difficult, unless he meets a good venerable who can convince him.

    Anyone who experience giddiness and eye pain should check their windows Refresh Rate and always enable V Sync during gaming especially if your monitor is CRT

    How to change refresh rate... u right click on ur desktop, go properties, then from there go settings, click on advanced, the go monitor. then from there u can see ur 'screen refresh rate', how many hertz... basically it is how fast the screen refreshes itself. if it is low, then it will certainly make a person feel giddy and hurt the eye after some time, and when playing games, always enable 'v sync' if there is such an option.

    If yours is a CRT monitor, chances are you can set your monitor refresh rate to as high as 85, and if yours is LCD, probably 70.

    and remember v sync, if possible, cos some games set the refresh rate back to 60.
