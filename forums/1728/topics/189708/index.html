    And denominations and sects is not a problem in Buddhism actually. Because we generally see denominations and sects in a different light. As long as the teachings of each 'sect' follows the guidelines of the 3 Dharma Seals it should be OK.

    And also, it is a common view among Buddhists that there are '84000' dharma doors in Buddhism - that Buddha taught not only one dharma door for enlightenment but many different kinds of dharma doors - a person only need to focus on one to attain enlightenment. For example zen focuses on meditation and koan and the pure land school focuses on chanting Amitabha. Different patriarches open up different sects according to the needs of the times and focuses on different aspects of Buddhism. And after all, there are enlightened people from all the different schools of Buddhism, so I think this sectarian thing is totally unnecessary.

    But of course, there is a history of sectarianism in different times and different parts of Buddhism, but much of it I believe is resolved. As for now, there is very little sectarian tendencies among Buddhists, but you can say perhaps a very minority of them continue to prejudiced in some ways.

    Regarding 'heresy', see The Four Seals

    Originally posted by _wanderer_:
    The entire Buddhist teachings can be summarized in the four seals (in the Theravadin tradition, it is more commonly known as the three seals). Anything that sounds Buddhistic but does not contain the 4 seals is not Buddhism. As such, the 4 seals are often used as a reference for people to distinguish what is Buddhism and what's not.

    The 4 seals are:

    1. All compounded things are impermanent.
    2. All afflictive emotions are painful.
    3. All phenomena are empty; they are without inherent existence.
    4. Nirvana is beyond extremes.

    (there may be other slightly different versions due to translation)

    Read a very good teaching on the 4 seals from the following url:

    [b]Buddhism In a Nutshell: The Four Seals of Dharma
    - Dzongsar Khyentse Rinpoche

    http://www.shambhalasun.com/Archives/Features/2000/Mar00/4Seals.htm

    [/b]
