    This is something that many people have different opinions. But generally speaking, don't think that it is limited to any particular tradition, we do not encourage homosexuality.

    We don't actually believe in 'sin'. In accordance with my dharma teacher (I'm from Mahayana anyway), in Buddhism, in the sense that there is no absolute evil person. Everyone is a human, but they are conditioned by their way of thinking... so if their way of thinking can be transformed, the person can be transformed. We should not condemn homosexuals or bisexuals but if possible provide them with counselling/guidance and encouragement.

    I recall my dharma teacher speak about how homosexuality is a misidentification of our gender, and advised us to remain as what our gender is... that is not to engage in homosexuality, male shldnt seek male and female seek female and so on.

    And no matter what we cannot run away from the fact of our gender.. even if we become transexual. Transexuals generally have a very very difficult lives for them... and would usually tell you not to walk that path unless you can tolerate a lot a lot of pain.

I didn't knew that was true, but when I searched the internet there are actually quite many research that supports this statement that homosexuals usually have shorter lifespan. Some people say isn't homosexuality a 'freedom of love'... but we should understand that freedom have responsibilites and we shouldn't destroy ourselves and the body that our parents gave us...

    That said, unlike some other religions, we do not label and discriminate gays as 'sinful people' or such. We do not hold a sense of prejudice against them. But if it is possible we should try to encourage them to transform (but never never condemn them)
