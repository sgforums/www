    Meditation according to Ganges Mahamudra

    The "Ganges Mahamudra" was taught by Tilopa to Naropa; Naropa taught it to Marpa, who translated it into Tibetan. This short instruction is considered the source text of all Kagyu doctrine in general and especially of our tradition of Mahamudra.

    Mahamudra is an approach to meditative practice that can be used by all. It is an approach that engulfs any practitioner with tremendous splendor and is very effective and easy to implement. This is especially true in the present time.

    ABOUT PALCHEN RINPOCHE



    Sonam Zangpo Palchen Rinpoche was born in Nangchen, Qinghai. He is recognized by Jamgon Tai Situ Rinpoche as the reincarnation of Sonam Zangpo Rinpoche. The previous Sonam Zangpo Rinpoche was the Lama of late Kyabje Drubwang Sangye Tenzin Rinpoche, who had spent his life in retreat.

    Since young, the present Palchen Rinpoche followed Sangye Tenzin Rinpoche to Japa Monastery, where he received many precious empowerments, scriptural transmissions and oral instructions. At a later period, Jamgon Tai Situ Rinpoche bestowed him with the Bodhisattva vow and the Three Levels of Ordination.
