    I have heard that there are fake dharma books circulating now.

    I got no sources yet and I have not read any of these books currently. The content usually mix with some dharma words which sound very buddhist-like but it mislead people.

    I thought about it and I generally list some of the common characteristics:

    • excessive self-praise- the writer can just praise and praise himself / herself excessively, almost to the state of divine being

     

    • self-proclaim encounters with Buddhas and the Bodhisattvas- like any other writers, he/she will claim that they are "enlightened" and have seen or even talked to Buddhas etc.

     

    • supernatural powers- they claim to have "healing" powers, or the ability to change your luck etc

     

    • monetary rewards- they just want your money. Enough said.

     

    • total devotion- they like devotees who don't question them but to believe them wholeheartedly. Usually likes to mix with fears e.g. bad karma if you do not do what he / she says

     

    That's what I can summarize. Most phoneys like to deploy such "tactics".
