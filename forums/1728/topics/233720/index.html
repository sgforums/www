    Originally posted by marcteng:
    how does it relate to buddhism?
    She has experienced some degrees of the luminosity (clarity, awareness) aspect of our Buddha Nature, however, without deep insights into no-self, she will fall back into a sense of self witnessing the world. Her experience is known as Witnessing Consciousness, there is a subtle form of dualistic Witness detached from all phenomena, witnessing everything.

    The 'no-self' she talked about is just a losing the sense of 'personality', not the true Buddhism's No-Self in the sense of the insight into the illusion of subject and object. Neither has she experienced fusing (stage 2), as fusing into everything is a stage of being fully authenticated by all things. Even after experiencing fusing, it can remain a stage that can be entered and left, only Stage 4/5 onwards will there be pathlessness due to the insight that our buddha nature is never separated from the manifestation/phenomena, and as a result the fusing will not be a temporary experience that can be left, but will be without entry and exit.

    Therefore her experience is not 'complete' in Buddhism. See Thusness's reply in this thread. Also see Thusness's six stages of experience, http://buddhism.sgforums.com/?action=thread_display&thread_id=210722&page=3, Suzanne's experience would be Stage 1.
