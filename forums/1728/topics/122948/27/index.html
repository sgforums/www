    A famous General went to see a renowned Zen master and asked him: "Buddhism mentions the six realms. Have you seen any? Bring me to see Hell then."

    The Zen master replied: "Who are you? "

    To which the general proudly answered: "I am the general of the emperor of China. "

    " What?! Such a stupid person like you can be a general?" The Zen Master shot back.

    Hearing this, the general who was now fuming with anger, drew out his sword and shouted: " I kill you!"

    The zen Master calmly said: " I have fulfilled your request, General. You are now in the Hell Realm."

    The general was embarrased and immediately dropped his sword to the floor and apologised profusely to the Zen master.

    " You are now seeing the realm of Heaven as well." The Zen master smiled.

    ~ A Zen Story

    ... Manjushri, after my extintion, in the Evil World of Five Turbidities, all living beings are born without spiritual penetration of the past lives. So they do not know the rotation of cause and effect. When they temporary obtain the human forms, they feel that they should fully enjoy themselves presently.

May be he enjoys slandering others, or he may make use of his powerful, honourable and wealthy disposition to threaten others' lives. He will give rise to all kinds of evil thoughts.     ~ The Dharani Sutra of The Buddha On Longevity

    One day a man of the people said to Zen Master Ikkyu; "Master, will you please write for me some maxims of the highest wisdom?" Ikkyu immediately took his brush and wrote the word "Attention."

    "Is that all?" said the man. "Will you not add something more?"

    Ikkyu then wrote twice running: "Attention. Attention."

    "Well," remarked the man, "I really do not see much depth or subtlety in what you have written."

    Then Ikkyu wrote the same word three times running: "Attention. Attention. Attention."

    Half-angered, the man demanded: "What does the word 'attention' mean anyway?"

    And Ikkyu answered gently, "Attention means attention."
