    Just for sharing

    I have also experienced the obstacles in learning and practising the dharma. While in the process of overcoming these obstacles, one can still learn and practise the dharma.

    For example, i was sharing a cab with my friend to attend a chanting ceremony in a temple at 7pm. We were worrying that we would be late so we took a cab down but then there was a traffic jam. My friend was anxious and disappointed, she had told me that whenever she attended such buddhist ceremony, she will face some obstacles.

    From another perspective, one can learn to deal with this sort of crisis with a calm and peaceful mind by practising mindfulness. We can practise to be mindful of our mental state; learning to overcome habit of agitation, worrying ,grasping and other unwholesome mind state. We are already practising the dharma now.

    Wisdom is required to distinguish: If there is something we can do about it, we do but if there is really nothing we can do about it, then we should learn to accept that. Even when we can't have what we want, it's okay. No need to be grossly upset over it and try to make the best out of it.

    Back to my story, It ended up that we had made a blunder. The ceremony was actually at 7.30pm and not at 7pm! Hahaha.. Even if we are really late, there is really nothing we can do. It might be just bad karma but my point is we can still take the initiative to practise dharma at every moment, even now, be mindful while u type or reading tis.

    so if you think you don't have the affinity with venerables, patiently acquiring the affinity then. If you think you do not have the merits, patiently accumulating the merits. It is already a blessing that you get to know buddhism in this lifetime.

    Sharing dharma is also a form of practising dharma. Of course u have to be mindful of what dharma and how and whom you are sharing to.
