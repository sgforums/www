    Ten Doubts about Pure Land
    By Tien Tai Patriarch Chih I
    Translated by Master Thich Thien Tam
    http://www.geocities.com/sinweiy/TenDoubtsaboutPureLand.html

    Question 3

    All the Pure Lands of the Buddhas of the ten directions have equal qualities and virtues. Their Dharma Nature is also the same. Therfore, the practitioner should meditate on all the virtues of the Buddhas and seek rebirth in the various Pure Lands of the ten directions. Why should he specifically seek rebirth in the Pure Land of one particular Buddha (i.e., Amitabha)? Is this not contrary to the truth of "equally in seeking rebirth"?

    Answer

    All the Pure Lands of the Buddhas are, in truth, equal. Nevetheless, since the majority of sentient beings in our world generally have dull faculties and defiled, scattered minds, it will be difficult for them to achieve samadhi, unless they concentrate exclusively on one realm.

    The practice of constantly focussing on Amitabha Buddha is the "Single Mark Samadhi". Because the Mind is exclusively devoted to one thing, the practitioner achieves rebirth in the Pure Land. In the Sutra Rebirth According to One's Vows, Buddha Sakyamuni was asked by a Bodhisattva, "Honored One! There are Pure Lands in all ten directions. Why do you especially extol the Western Pure Land and urge sentient beings to focus continuously
    on Amitabha Buddha, seeking rebirth in His Land?"

    The Buddha replied, "Sentient beings in this Saha World generally have polluted, scattered minds. Therefore, I only extol one Pure Land in the West, focussing their Minds on a single realm. If they meditate on all Buddhas, the scope of attention will be too broad, their Minds
    will be lost and scattered and they will find samadhi difficult to attain. Thus, they will fail to achieve rebirth in the Pure Land.

    "Furthermore, seeking their virtues of one Buddha is the same as seeking the virtues of all Buddhas -- as all Buddhas have one common Dharma Nature. That is why to focus on Amitabha Buddha is to focus on all Buddhas, to be born in the Western Pure Land is to be born in all Pure Lands."

    Thus, the Avatamsaka Sutra states:

    "The bodies of all the Buddhas / are the body of any one Buddha. / They have the same Mind and the same wisdom. / They are also equal in power and fearlessness."

    The Avatamsaka Sutra further states:

    "It is like the full moon, round and bright, its image reflected in all rivers and ponds. Although the reflection is everywhere, there is but a single moon. So it is with ... (the Buddhas). Although they appear in all realms, their bodies are non-dual."

    In summary, based on these examples, the wise will understand the truth that "one is all, all is one". When this truth is grasped, concentrating on one Buddha is precisely concentrating on all
    Buddhas.

    Question 4

    There are many Buddhas and Pure Lands in all the ten directions. Even if sentient beings in this world have polluted, scattered minds and dispositions, so that focussing on many Buddhas makes it difficult for them to attain samadhi, why should they not recite the name of any Buddha, as they wish, and seek rebirth in any Pure Lamd, in accordance with their vows? Why concentrate specifically on Amitabha Buddha and seek rebirth in the Land of
    Ultimate Bliss?

    Answer

    Common people lacking in wisdom should follow the teaching of the Buddha rather than acting arbitrarily on their own. This is why, from time immemorial, Pure Land practitioners have all diligently recited Amitabha Buddha's name.

    What does it mean to follow the Buddha's teaching?

    During his entire preaching career, Buddhs Sakyamuni constantly enjoined sentient beings to focus on Amitabha Buddha and seek rebirth in the Land of Ultimate Bliss.
    This is mentioned in such sutras as the Longer Amitabha Sutra, the Meditation Sutra, the Amitabha Sutra, the Lotus Sutra, the Avatamsaka Sutra ... In numerous sutras, the Buddha constantly urged us to seek rebirth in the Western Land. This is not only true of the sutras; in their commentaries, the Bodhisattvas and Patriarchs unanimously
    advise us to seek rebirth in the Land of Ultimate Bliss.

    Moreover, Amitabha Buddha possesses the power of His forty-eight compassionate vows to rescue sentient beings. The Meditation Sutra states:

    "Amitabha Buddha possesses 84,000 signs of perfection, each sign has 84,000 minor marks of excellence and from each minor mark 84,000 rays of light shine forth, illuminating the entire Dharma Realm (cosmos) to gather in, without exception, all sentient beings who practice Buddha Recitation. If any sentient being recite His name, there will be correspondence between cause
    and response, and he will surely be reborn."

    Furthermore, the Amitabha Sutra, the Longer Amitabha Sutra, etc., teach that when Sakyamuni Buddha preached these sutras, the Buddhas of the ten directions, numerous as the grains of sand in the River Ganges, all "extended their tongues to cover the entire universe", bearing witness to the truth that any sentient being who recites Amitabha Buddha's name shall be assured of rebirth in the Land of Ultimate Bliss, thanks to the great, compassionate vow-power of the Buddha.

    We should know that Amitabha Buddha has great affinities (causes and conditions) with this world. As the Longer Amitabha Sutra states:

    "In the Dharma-Ending Age, when all other sutras have disappeared, only this sutra will remain for another hundred years to rescue sentient beings and lead them to the Western Pure Land."

    This demostrates that Amitabha Buddha has strong affinities with sentient beings in this defiled world.

    Although one or two sutras have, in a general way, urged rebirth in other Pure Lands, this cannot be compared to the fact that numerous sutras and commentaries have earnestly pointed out to the Land of Ultimate Bliss as the focus of rebirth.

    The Great Dharma King Amitabha Buddha

    by Venerable Master Hsuan Hua

    This four characters: A Mi Tou Fwo (Amitabha Buddha) means inconceivable, so it says,

    The Buddha Amitabha is the great Dharma king,
    universally gather all various faculties of living beings to the West.
    Day and night, hold his name, with sincerity recite it.
    At all times, in contemplation, think upon it well.
    With one herat unconfused, you realize samadhi.
    when all creation's void, you'll enter the Lotus Land.
    Suddenly enlightened to the non-production, the Buddha appears in person.
    A wonderful enlightenment fruition is naturally attained.

    This eight verse praises Amitabha Buddha as the great Dharma king. Heng Chang wrote me a letter and addressed me as " Venerable Master Dharma king", Is master a Dharma king? that is not for sure, but Amitabha Buddha definitely is a great Dharma king, so his name is Amitabha Buddha. Amitabha is a Sanskrit word, which means" limitless life" , also means" limitless light". Limitless life is symbolizing his unlimited blessing, and limitless light describes his unlimited wisdom, so it means both blessing and wisdom are unlimited.

    The blessing is completed, the wisdom is completed. With both blessing and wisdom are perfected, he is named the Buddha of limitless life and wisdom. Limitless life can cause all living beings also to obtain limitless life. Limitless light can make all living beings to attain limitless light too. Why is it? Because Buddha is not selfish, he did not say," AH! only me can monopolize the limitless life, you can't have it.", or he won't say, " Only I have this limitless light, living being can share none of it.".Whoever recites the name of Amitabha Buddha will gain the limitless life. Whoever recite Amitabha can gain limitless light. The unlimitless life and light could be obtained by everyone. Hence the words "Amitabha Buddha" are inconceivable, so he is called great Dharma King. Amitabha Buddha is the king within Dharma, not only he is the king within the Buddha Dharma but is a great Dharma King, so you see how great he is! those who recite the name of Amitabha Buddha have good roots; Those who have no good roots will not encounter this dharma door. How simple this Dharma door is! All you have to do is reciting. Just by reciting the name of Amitabha Buddha, you can become Buddha, which without spending money or effort. As long as you can recite it, you too can attain limitless life and light. Why could we gain this just by reciting ? Isn't this a bit too much of a bargain for us to be able to learn this dharma door without making any effort!

    The reason for it is because in the past lifes, Amitabha Buddha cultivated all kinds of dharma doors without success. He had cultivated this and that dharma door for several great kalpas without being able to obtain Buddhahood. He had been precticing various dharma doors, and had undergone bitter experiences. Because of this, he then made the vow, " Since I had undergone such a difficulty while I was cultivating the Buddha Dharma, I want to make a vow to cause all living beings to accomplish Buddhahood easily, I don't want them to go through the same hardship as I did.". So Amitabha Buddha made forty-eight vows,which universally gather all living beings . All living beings of the ten directions, as long as you can recite " Na Mo A Mi Ta Bha Buddha", the name that I accomplish the Buddhahood, then I will assure to take you and to become Buddha. If you don't become Buddha, then I myself neither will become Buddha nor attain enlightenment.
    Amitibha Buddha had made the forty-eight great vows, therefore, any living being of the ten directions, whoever could recite the name of Amitabha Buddha,has deeply tied up the dharma condition with him. Once the dharma conditon has been tied up,Wao, you will be in the West before you are aware of it. So it says, " Universally gather all beings of various faculties ". Yet you still don't know how did you make it to get there. You may wonder, " Gee! I did not buy any airline ticke to come here-the West? I did not purchase any train, or boat, or bus ticket. I buy none of it and I come to Western Ultimate Bliss Land already.". Don't you think that you got a great deal of it? Amitabha Buddha in the former life had made a great effort and cultivated many ascetic practices to be able to become Buddha. Now, we just only need to recite the name of Amitabha Buddha, and we can go to the land of Western Ultimate Bliss. Don't you think that it is a good bargain? There is nothing more expedience, more convenience than this, it is truly" easy! easy! easy! you could realize the patriach's mind on the tip of myriad grass." it is too easy, it is extremely easy. So it is said," universally gather all living beings" , you could see that we here all have a connection with Amitabha Buddha like radio. If you recite Amitabha Buddha, your shadow will be reflected on the radar of Amitabha Buddha. If we say it more advanced, it is like T.V., we send a fax to him. Amitabha Buddha will know and catch the wave whoever recites his name,that person's mind telegram will be sent to Amitabha Buddha. Isn't it really wonderful? So it is said, " universally gather all faullties of living beings to the West."

    "Morning and night, hold his name, with sincerity recite it": To recite the name of Amitabha Buddha day and night. In movement and stillness, you recite his name. While moving you can recite and change the motion into stillness; while still you can recite and change the still into motion. When there is neither movement nor stillness, at this state, your telegram to Amitabha has gotten through and you have received his response. "hold his name" means you concentrately hold the name of Amitabha Buddha- Na Mo A Mi Ta Bha Buddha, Na Mo A Mi Ta Bha Buddha..., don't lose it, don't forget about it, remember it by heart. That is what it means by " morning and night, hold his name, with sincerity recite it."

    Recite it single-minded and sincerely without false thoughts. You don't think how is China nowadays, how is Japan rescentantly, how is Mahatma Gandhi, the prime minister of India,right now. What for to think of all these worthless matters? If you do not strike up all these false thoughts, you are singlemindly and sincerely in recitation. To recite and uphold the name concentrately to the point that you were ask," what is the day of today?", You don't know. "what is the date of tomorrow?' You don't know.To recite till not knowing anything, then you arrive at a miraculous state.

If you are able to learn to be stupid like this then it's not bad at all.

    Vow To Be Born In The Western Pureland

    To end birth and death in the world is not easy.
    Only that you will not be born in the Land of Ultimate Bliss,
    once there, you will never turn away

    by Venerable Master Hsuan Hua

    Any question you want to ask? To understand this problem, one has to understand Armita's day in his previous life before enlightenment when he practised all kinds of dharma doors, none of which was easy to accomplish. He therefore made a wish saying since all beings of the ten directions practise buddhism(dharma) so difficult as the way I did, I now make 48 vows, each of which is to lead them to my land. What is the advantage of being born in my land? It is to see the Buddha when born as the lotus blossoms. This is not the birth from the impure conditions of parents but is transformed as lotus when being born in the Land of Ultimate Bliss, forming a body within it and this body will only emerge as a man, no woman. It is therefore no women in the Land of Ultimate Bliss.

    When born in the Western Pureland, one is out of the cycle of birth and death, and thus go beyond the realm of the six paths in this cycle, and in his life he dwells in the Buddha land, he will never turn away. It is definite that one will become buddha when born in the Land of Ultimate Bliss, so the 48 vows Armita Buddha made, each is to lead and guide living beings to become Buddha, this birth is different from the birth and death of the lay people. In this very life in the Land of Ultimate Bliss, one will never fall into the three evil destinies, never withdraw, this is the condition that would determine one be a Buddha in the future. So everyone should recite and seek to be born in the Pureland. Unlike this saha world mingled in sufferings and evils. It never like that. It is a place free if sufferings but joy.     "Medicine Master Vaidurya Light" is the name of this Buddha. Tathagata is one of the ten titles of all Buddhas. "Past Vows" refers to the vows to achieve Bodhi that Medicine Master Buddha made before he became a Buddha. If we sincerely make vows, we will certainly reap the fruits of those vows. They won't be in vain. Vows are also a form of karma. If we make good vows, we will reap good results; bad vows bring bad results. Each person must make his own vows of his own initiative. Once we have made vows, there will be a driving force pushing us to accomplish those vows. Medicine Master Buddha made wholesome vows in his past lives, so he accomplished wholesome karma and reaped wholesome results.

    His vows are wholesome because he made them for all living beings. He didn't vow, "When I become a Buddha, I'll enjoy my blessings and forget about other living beings." Having attained the greatest happiness, the Buddha wants to share it with all beings. He perfected his wholesome karma by practicing the Bodhisattva path in life after life. He made a great Bodhi resolve to benefit, enlighten, and rescue all beings. Forgetting themselves and thinking only of living beings, Bodhisattvas make wholesome vows, create wholesome karma, and reap the wholesome result of Buddhahood. When they become Buddhas, they are not arrogant. A Buddha is just the same as the other living beings, except that he has wisdom. He has truly left confusion behind and returned to enlightenment
