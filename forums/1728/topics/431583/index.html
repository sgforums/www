    Hi All Brothers and Sisters in Dharma Any of you know of others who are terminally ill or going through illness though not terminal type can contact me-inbox me your handphone. I can arrange for bed-side support group for the illed. He or she will not be alone to face this path. We will walk the path with he/she. Below is an article on what our support group does. The Bed-Side Support Group. (BSSG). The family goes through a very stressful period when a member is facing the dying process. The process may start with the discovery of a terminal illness; it is followed by the death and ends with the wake and the funeral. The Bed-Side Support Group helps families through the process by comforting both the dying and the living. When the grieving family is facing the dying process, CURE and HOPE must be replaced with CARE. The medical professionals take care of the body but the mind needs to be cared for too. Old wounds of the mind can reappear and quite often the pain of the mind can hurt more than the pain of the body. Volunteers at the BSSG provide counseling or just a pair of listening ears. A person may be bedridden but meditation empowers him. Learning meditation enables him to control his own state of mind; an untrained mind can constantly regret the past and worry about the future. Knowing that there are people in the community who are your friends in your need warms the heart. Chanting provides comfort and sharing the Dhamma helps provide answers to many questions. Many do not like to talk about death but it is a process everyone must go through. If well prepared, the process can be peaceful and joyful. Illness is uncomfortable but we have all experienced some form of illness before. Dying is a change of phase of our life. We too have all experienced changes in phases of our life, from childhood, adolescence, young adult, middle age to old age. The change of phase is a natural process. Every phase of our life ends, the end of one phase is the beginning of another phase, a better phase. There are some elements in the society who would take advantage of the grief of families to proselytize, often resulting in turmoil and agitation within the family. Others would capitalize on superstitions to sell their services, resulting in hard earned savings being spent on items to be burnt or buried. The BSSG operates on a strictly by invitation only basis. Volunteers are dispatched when calls for bedside support comes through the Buddhist Fellowship. We have volunteers who are knowledgeable in funeral arrangements and we also provide chanting and memorial service at wakes and funerals. With Metta
