    I believe there are people who think that practitioner of Buddhism are somewhat pessimistic. Be it through some chinese dramas, or people that they see or hear in their daily life... I absolutely disagree on this.

    A lot of people might have heard of "Fang Xia" - Letting go, a commonly heard term in Buddhism. I have been practicing this since the day I have a better understanding of it through my Australia's Shifu. I find it very useful as it helps suppress my negative thoughts and worries and turn me away from being pessimistic.

    Before I make a forward step to get to know more about Buddhism, I was a pessimistic guy who habour lots of worries, I hate crowds and seldom join people. This really makes my life dull. I used to put my own viewpoints into understanding Buddhism and hence causing me to make a lot of wrong assumptions. But now thinking back, I am really grateful to a friend who brought me into Buddhism. I am now cheerful and easy-going, and I cannot believe I can have so many friends. Many of my doubts in Buddhism (that I tried figuring out myself) have been cleared after seeking advise from my Shifu.

    A lesson that I learn: "Always seek a Shifu rather than trying to understand Buddhism in our own viewpoint." Trust me, they are very friendly and approachable. I believe if I did not make that very first step, I would still be having the same misconceptions on Buddhism.
