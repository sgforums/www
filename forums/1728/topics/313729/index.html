His body is nourished by food: his spirit is nurtured by teaching and experience. Therefore both flesh and spirit are related to conditions and are changed as conditions change. Blossoms do not appear independently, nor does a leaf fall of itself, out of its season. So everything has its coming forth and passing away; nothing can be independent without any change. It is the everlasting and unchanging rule of this world that everything is created by a series of causes and conditions and everything disappears by the same rule; everything changes, nothing remains constant.

    Here are some excerpts from a article by Ven. Fazhao published in Vaidurya vol.27 touching on Karma, hopes it can helps to clear some doubts.

    ' 'Karma' is not necessarily something that happens in the future. A person with heavy karmic obscurations would have fear in his heart. A person filled with fear would naturally not be healthy physically. He may even have hallucinations resulting in terror. '

    'Karma arises from the mind. When karmic obscurations manifest, an illusion is produced which leads us to experience the fruition of karma. From past causes to present results; presents causes to future results, Buddhism studies the present thought and the development of future cause and effect. Not only does it study the future, but the way to transform past negative karma. People who know the law of cause and effect and put effort into making positive changes will be healthy both physically and emotionally.'

     

    'From the Buddhist viewpoint, fame and gain, status and glory are the positive results of past virtuous activities. If one has not practised generosity in the past, even if one accquires wealth in this lifetime, it will not stay. Causes, conditions, fruition and results are not fatalistic but a message for all to put effort into practising virtue, wisdom and compassion.'
