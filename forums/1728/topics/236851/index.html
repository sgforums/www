    Ya, I think we shouldn't take dreams seriously. They are usually caused by strong imprints in the 8th consciousness.. whether they have any meaning I think it is not necessary for us to seek an answer..

    If you want to continue to be a devoted yogi, generally you should never cling to dreams. If you do, you will eventually expose yourself to the influence of the four maras. If your dreams are positive, do not have any expectations. If we are filled with hopes and expectations, even positive things can turn negative. If your dreams are negative, don't take them too seriously. Learn to see negative dreams as illusion, not real. Then, although a dream seems negative, because we realize that it isn't real, it becomes a positive thing that prepares us for further development and realization in the spiritual path. This is the practice of a yogi.

    -- from The Life of Gampopa by Jampa Mackenzie Stewart, just published by Snow Lion Publications.

    Erm there are many many kinds of ghosts in Buddhism (see: 36 Kinds of Ghosts)... in general, some people have bad karma and after their death their bad karma cause them to be born in the ghost realm and they suffer a lot of different kinds of sufferings. (some become hungry ghost, etc)

    There are also some people who are so attached to their life, relationship, possession and so on, after 49 days (the period where the antarabhava can be reborn) they also turn into ghost. But generally they are not the kind of ghosts that have very bad karma.

    Still, both of them belong to the 'ghost realm' in Buddhism. The three lower realms are: hell, ghost, animal. The three higher realms are: Humans, Asuras, Celestial/heaven beings (devas).
