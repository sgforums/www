    Not all 'ghosts' from the dead of human. Some are spirits of various domains and tertiaries.

    If you attended some Dharma event, you'll notice the offering to Naga and a small offering left outside the hall or place of assembly.

    Vajrayana has protective recitation that are very forceful towards all (Dharma) disturbing activities, similarly in Mahayana and Theravada. These are not aim at 'spirits of deceased human'.

    Most Ven. Jing Kong in one of his teaching session mentioned he encounter various disturbing 'activities' while he was in Korea.

    The logic is, human does not has the ability to break through the Bardo or 中阴身 unless, while alive, this person has some form of practices that makes him aware or having a strong bond with protecting deities (can be of good or bad) that carries him to live in another dimension (for a certain period only).

    Accordingly, whenever after you did chanting (in any tradition), you should dim your room light (not the shrine light) and leave some food offering for any visiting 'friends'. They are drawn by your chanting or the Dharma that is recited. If they found delight, they will actually stay and becomes protecting deities - for the Dharma activities. That is one reason why Vajrayana did not allow non-filled Buddha statues.

    That's why if you have a dedicated chanting room or chanting area, you find it to be most peaceful for reflection and meditation.
