    A passage from "Words of wisdom" from venerable Shi Fazhao:

    "According to a sutra, in the previous life of the Buddha, he was scolded by 500 "scolding experts" who followed him and scolded him whenever he went. The Buddha did not have the slightest anger, only compassionate observation. His practice of patience finally won him supreme enlightenment. This show that patience is an important quality in attaining Buddhahood."

    I know it is not easy for people like us to have such kind of patience, but see it this way: if even Buddha can ecountered such aggressive stalkers, then it is natural that we will meet with difficult people once a while. So relax, who knows? Maybe we are one of the 500 "scolding experts" so now got "toasted".
