    There has to be a uniform understanding/definition of the term religion. Otherwise we will have difficulty keeping to this thread. The dictionary meaning of religion is belief system. In previous thread I elaborated on belief system and its elements. Those are my POVs.

    I think aside from the moral teaching, central to every religion is the worship or veneration of a 'special' being that is beyond human. Whether this being is a spirit, creator-god, cosmic or reincarnation of something, whatever it is called, and whether its existence is proven or not. It could also include the worship of elevated or exalted humans whether alive or dead.

    Do not be confused by one's affinity to a particular philosophy or morality. There are many great philosophers and moralists who are respected and quoted but never exalted to a higher being or worshipped.

    In this regards, I don't see the three PMs as having any religion although they may professed to be 'nominal' this or that. A free-thinker or non-believer is not against any religion per se. Also, it doesn't mean they don't have a philosophy or they don't have any moral!

Many innocent people were massacred or tortured or raped!
