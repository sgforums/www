    Honestly, I am bad at counselling.

    But I think since this is an inevitable situation then you should just accept it as it is, focus more on your work, go with the flow, the causes and conditions.

    As my Taiwanese teacher said, "Gen yin yuan dui kang de ren shi zui ben de" (Those who react against causes and conditions are the most foolish)

    Or as Byron Katie said, "When I argue with reality I lose - but only 100% of the time."

    Reality is just what it is, and it is only our thoughts giving meaning and interpretations and reactions to them. Don't react to your mental stories, if they arise, don't pay attention to the content of the thoughts, the thought will subside on its own. Then the ideas of gain or loss wouldn't hold.

    Since you practise chanting, I think doing more chanting will help to calm yourself.

    Just my 2 cents

    You are "attached" to a certain view. You are assuming that your view is "right" "correct" and "the best" view point.

    This is why you are "miserable". Meditate and "see" the reality of the situation. Meditate all the possibilities, all the what ifs...

    What if you get the position and you are not happy?
    What if you get the new position and you are very happy?
    What if you get the new position and you are so happy with it?
    What if you get the position and you fall sick?
    What if you get the position and the company goes into a crisis?
    What if you get the position and the company becomes very successful?
    What if you get the position and Singapore goes into a crisis?

    Meditate all the possibilities, all the what ifs... you will be able to imagine all the possibilities better than me because you are in the situation...

    Then after you "see" all the possibilities... write them down and meditate on how you will react to all the what ifs.... and all the possibilities of how you can react to all the possible what ifs...

    If you are willing to do that, you will soon free your mind... the result will surprise you... it is not what you think...

    Most people when find themselves in a difficult situation, would naturally start to think of all possible negative outcomes which may or may not come true. As one grows older in life, there are bound to be more such harsh situations. Here is when the Dharma comes into play; it helps us to face reality, see it clearly, take it on, let it go and move on. Easily said than done? No. Unless we are pessimistic or see Reality as our enemy.

    So, if we are the types that the mind tends to take a life of its own and run wild with negatives thoughts( which is not uncommon, that is why most of us must practice harder ), my DIY solution is to prepare ourselves mentally and physically with the worst scenerio that may come in the situation we are in now, then when it finally come, we are all ready and will be in a better position to shoulder the outcome or even better still, solve it. Once the preparation and decision are made, leave it aside and give the best we can offer in our job or study and carry on as usual with our life, meaningfully ( by doing more good deeds or study and practise the Dharma, yeah, chanting also).

    If we see life as unfair to us whenever a undesirable ( as we percieved to be) outcome hit us, then we have forgotten the law of cause and effect. It is exactly the law of cause and effect that we encountered situations in life, and it is through the law of cause and effect that we can change the situation and/or take it on bravely. My 0.002 cents.
