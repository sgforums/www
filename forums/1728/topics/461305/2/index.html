     From my understanding, there is a difference between FD ç¦�å¾· and GDåŠŸå¾·...

    FD can only be used in future lives. GD's results can manifest within this life and used at the end of one's life to go to Pureland.

    One of the main deciding factor of what positive deeds/ practice becomes FD or GD is one's motivation.  If the motivation is to leave samsara and attain liberation for all sentient beings, then it can become GD.  If it is a selfish or worldly motivation (ie to gain worldly benefits, like good health, fortune, relationships etc), then it can  become FD.

    FD and GD is not just a kind of merit bank account accumulation. But depends on your thought from moment to moment.  If you give rise to a worldly motivation or a wish to enjoy worldly stuff in your next life, if your motivation degenerates in this way, all your past GD can be transformed instantly to FD.

    There are also possibilities of wiping out your FD/GD easily with negative thoughts, wrongful actions etc.  Therefore, the concept is not one of accumulating only but it should be viewed also like a state of mind.  People with great GD automatically have a state of mind that is in line with the Dharma teachings.

    Many people practise for many years mistaking these very important concepts, that is why there is often reversal or lack of progression.  For Liang Wu Di, i heard that eventually he died of starvation in that very life. That may be because he had FD, not GD. FD to be used only in the next life.

    This also explains why the old Chinese masters always emphasized for us to appreciate our merits æƒœç¦�ï¼Œwe should not waste our merits easily in this life and squander it on all kinds of enjoyment.  The past masters were very careful with waste to ensure that they had sufficient merits and time to do practice and attain realisation.

    Buddhists are often questioning themselves or asking others why it is that someone do a lot of good things but they still have so many miseries and problems.  One of the reason is the difference between FD and GD.  Someone may do alot of positive things, but the motivation is very weak.  It may be for improving his worldly life, cure sickness, improve wealth etc.  But there is no mind to seek liberation.

    The other reason of course is that a practitioner who seeks liberation must be prepared to return all his karmic debts.  There is no way to attain Buddhahood without returning all your debts.  

    The workings of karma are very intricate and accurate. Anyone who is wise will have the highest respect and care for cause-and-effect.
