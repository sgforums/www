    The whole idea is being filial, respectful and faithful to one
    teacher (if not the fewer the better). If it coincides with His
    teaching, I accept, if not I reject. Filiality, be it parent or
    teacher is the root of all achievement. It is also a form of
    restriction to us against desire. It's not that other teachings are
    not good. The thing is about rooting the basics and getting the root
    correct. When one had that abilities of differentiating good and
    evil, then you can go to learn about other things, if not one can be
    easily move by desires. This is the ancient method of Chinese
    teachings. Another word, too many teachers can be confusion.

    You know how Master Chin Kung did when he seek a teacher, Master Li.
    Within the first 5 years he had to follow a strict regulations.
    Firstly is to listen only to Master Li's teachings, other then that,
    he is not allow to listen to other teachers' teaching. Secondly,
    without Master Li's permission, he is not allow to read other
    teachers books. Thirdly, whatever Master Chin Kung had learned in the
    past, all are to forgotten. From these restrictions, Master Chin Kung
    had felt that his 6 consciousnesses had been purified. From such a
    purification, listening to dharma talks develop insights. Within a
    few years time, there's a sign of improvement, so Master Chin Kung
    added another 5 years. Now, not even the 8 winds can move his
    samadhi. Furthermore, when he read other's peoples' book, listen to
    conventional ideas, everything is seen as they truly are.

    The root of Buddhism understanding is filialty. Filial also to the
    path of enlightenment.
