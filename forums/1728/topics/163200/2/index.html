    The most common meditation done in Buddhism is the Anapanasati Meditation, meaning the meditation on mindful breathing. Being aware and focused on breathing. As I have mentioned it is always best to learn it directly from a qualified teacher. Even chanting can help if you can concentrate well on the chanting. But if you can do meditation regularly it will be good.

    Awareness/mindfulness is not about the mind or about the thoughts. It is about our innate awareness nature. If something is happening out there, or something bad is happening, but you can continue to remain simply aware and not led into wandering thoughts, then it is awareness. Even if you have wandering thoughts, you are completely aware (and therefore let go). If someone wishes to tempt you into something that is wrong, and you are aware, then you will not be tempted. If you are aware, you will not steal, get into fights/arguments etc. If you are not aware, you start to steal, you get into fight/arguments, that is called 'ignorance', not knowing. The word 'Buddha' actually means Awareness/Awaken. Therefore it is important that we practise awakening/awareness.

    Why is that so? From the 1st list, other than the sutra on Amitayus which focuses more on other-power (but is also not lacking in self cultivation instructions), all other sutras were related to self-power, self cultivation. Seems like you have not read much yet.

    Second thing is the Mahayana canon is vast and includes all those scriptures in the Pali canon. Not all of them were listed in the list. Other than that - the Mahayana canon includes plenty of rich and sophisticated teachings and methods to enlightenment which are not found in the other canon.

    All teachings are taught according to the conditions and needs of sentient beings as I mentioned... also depending on what kind of capacity the person is. If the person is suited to practise the Shravakayana, then the Buddha will teach him the teachings leading to self-liberation. If the Buddha sees that someone has the seeds of practising the Bodhisattvayana, the Buddha teaches the Bodhisattvayana (i.e Mahayana) teachings, which is to liberate himself and others and attain Anuttarasamyaksambodhi (the perfect and complete enlightenment), Buddhahood.

    We must know that although the Buddha taught 84000 dharma doors to enlightenment, all dharmas are ultimately equal in nature. All teachings are Buddha's convinient means of conveying the truth to different people.

    Subhuti asked, "With what esoteric implication do you say so?"
    "I say so because all dharmas are equal by nature."
    Subhuti asked, "If all dharmas are equal, where are such dharmas as the stages of Sravakas, Pratyekabuddhas, Bodhisattvas, and Buddhas established?"
    Manjusri answered, "As an illustration, consider the empty space in the ten directions. People speak of the eastern space, the southern space, the western space, the northern space, the four intermediate spaces, the space above, the space below, and so forth. Such distinctions are spoken of, although the empty space itself is devoid of distinctions. In like manner, virtuous one, the various stages are established in the ultimate emptiness of all things, although the emptiness itself is devoid of distinctions."

    Actually I want to add something.

    Not say all sutras have totally different teachings - but the Buddha teach in a different way because of the different 'yin yuan'. So there's a saying: Fa3 Wu2 Ding4 Fa3, Ding4 Fa3 Fei1. What does this mean? In simple terms, the Dharma is taught in an unfixed manner, therefore it is so flexible and "alive" that it separates from all attachments, and is expounded according to the needs of a person. Some people who are attached to certain views or thinkings will have dharma-attachments (fa3 zhi2), so very important is to get rid of all attachments. Thus learnt from my dharma teacher.

    One time a monk asked Zhaozhou "Does a dog have Buddha-nature or not".
    Zhouzhou answered, "No."
    Another time a monk asked Zhaozhou "Does a dog have Buddha-nature or not".
    Zhaozhou answered, "Yes."
