    I used to have this question some time ago.

    In order for one to be born in western pure land, following are the requirements:
    1) Take refuge of 3 triple gems
    2) Vow to reborn in pureland
    3) Keep 5 precepts, filial, ten good conducts, bodhicitta
    4) Have certain level of meditation practise

    The 1st 2 are rather easy to achieve, but the 3rd and 4th one are relatively difficult. How does one know if he can reborn in the pureland.
    If one fail to "migrate" to pureland, will his karma resulted from his diligent practise towards pureland automatically allocate a "cultivation friendly" place for him.
    I don't want to go to heaven, I may use up all my merits there. I want to go to a place that is suitable to continue cultivation for example, human realm or tushita heaven.

    The late master yinshun encouraged his disciple to vow to go to tushita heaven bcoz of the lower entry barrier and most importantly, it is a heaven that one can listen to dharma spoken by maitreya bodhisatva, who is also the next to be buddha.
    Western pureland is still better, but we should have contingency plan, isn't it?

    "Those who desire to be born in that land must perform the three meritorious

    acts:

    first, they should attend dutifully to their parents, honor the work of teachers

    and elders, with a compassionate mind refrain from killing, and cultivate the

    ten virtuous acts; second, they should receive and uphold the Three Refuges,

    keep the various precepts, and refrain from breaking the rules of conduct;

    third, they should awaken the mind that aspires for Enlightenment, believe

    deeply in the principle of cause and effect, read and recite the Mahayana

    teachings, and encourage those who practice the way. These three acts are

    called the undefiled acts."
