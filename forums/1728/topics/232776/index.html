    Don't worry about wanting to stay as human or going to heaven.. If you practise the Four Immeasurables, next life you will be born with good conditions, luxury, etc. A Buddhist should cultivate all the good mental qualities, cultivate blessings, wisdoms, and conditions. If you really practise hard as a human.. even if you are reborn in Deva realm you will still get to know the Dharma. Why? The seeds of dharma is deeply rooted in your consciousness, and you have a strong karmic affinity with the Triple Gems -- Buddha, Dharma, Sangha. The Dharma is also taught in the heavens, there are a lot of records in Sutras of Devas attaining Stream Entry or higher. There are many Bodhisattvas teaching there as well. In Tushita Heaven especially, Maitreya Bodhisattva is teaching in the Tushita's Inner Courtyard, until it is time for him to come to Earth to attain Buddhahood. There are many famous practitioners throughout history who aimed to be reborn in Tushita's Inner Courtyard to continue their practise and learning from Maitreya Bodhisattva.

    If you are practising the path to Arhatship, aim to attain at least Stream Entry (1st stage, and at most 7 lifetimes before Arhatship) this lifetime! If you are practising the path of Bodhisattva, aim to attain at least 1st Bhumi this lifetime! Then your path is somewhat "set" already It is not all that impossible, there are quite a lot of enlightened people even today, some of them are even posting in the forum. But lastly don't be satisfied with whatever attainment and continue to full enlightenment.. hehe..

    No, attaining first jhana does not necessarily mean we will be reborn in first Jhana realm. I also have certain jhanic experiences but I really doubt that I am heading for heaven now

    Why? Firstly, Jhana experiences are temporary meaning after you're out of your jhana, life goes on as normal. In the human realm it is impossible to function in the world at the Jhanic level of consciousness, except in deep meditation. If you go to the Jhanic realms in the afterlife, you will be able to sustain the level of jhanic bliss throughout your life as a Brahma.... but NOT in the human realm.

    What this means is you can cultivate very high jhanas and continue to do evil things (although less likely for a mind with stability). This will probably remind you of Devadatta, Buddha's cousin. He tried to kill Buddha four times (and failed) and tried to create a schism in the Sangha. He also mastered the Jhanas and psychic powers, but because of his heavy karma he still had to fall into hell for his wrongdoings.

    The Buddha was omniscient.. so the question is why would he allow devadatta to enter his Sangha and create all these sort of problems? The reasons was that the Buddha did indeed foresee all these, however, he knew that by allowing Devadatta to enter the Sangha, he will be able to attain Pratyekabuddhahood after his "visit to hell". So the Buddha prophecised that Devadatta, due to his jhanic attainments and cultivation, will be able to attain Pratyekabuddhahood in the future.

    Then how to attain Jhana rebirth? Firstly, the basics must be there -- you must avoid all bad karmas, keep the 5 precepts, do the 10 virtuous deeds, practise generosity, be filial to your parents, also practise the mental qualities like the 4 immeasurables, loving kindness and so on... I also suspect at the time of death, you must be in the state of jhana to begin with.. then there is a likelihood that you might be reborn in the jhana realms. That means, not only must you attain jhanas a few times, but you must master the art of entering jhana easily at any given moments, this requires very strong samadhi/samatha skills.

    Lastly.. I am not too sure about the practise of being reborn in Tushita Court as I do not practise that... perhaps someone can fill you in.

    As concentration improves, it is as though the mind Â“seesÂ” the first jhana and grabs on to it. The attention is focused narrowly, as though one were looking at a small area of this page. This state can be quite a relief from the pain and discomfort of sitting meditation and can temporarily quiet the mind somewhat. As with all the concentration states, it is generally quite easy to concentrate on something that is very enjoyable. Thus, attaining the first jhana is really, really important.

    Indeed, just to add, liberation and enlightenment is a result of a form of "awakening", it is not about attaining anything, or any states, because enlightenment is imageless. Any mental state is still within the realm of consciousness, as long as there is no awakening to the truth of no-self, emptiness, there is still dualistic consciousness, 7th consciousness.

    To put it in another way... enlightenment means awakening to the truth of what always IS. It is literally waking up from the dream of what never existed, into the real... but yet reality is not any "thing", being of the nature of emptiness itself, ungraspable. Just a form of wisdom.
