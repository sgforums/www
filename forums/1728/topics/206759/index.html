    Chiggala Sutta
    Samyutta Nikaya LVI.48

    "Behold, bhikkhus, suppose that this great earth were completely covered with water, and a man were to toss a yoke(wooden log)with a single hole into it. A wind from the east would blow it west, a wind from the west would blow it east. A wind from the north would blow it south, a wind from the south would blow it north. And suppose a blind sea turtle were in the great earth and came to the surface once every century. Now what do you think: would that blind sea turtle, rising to the surface once every century, come up with his neck right through the single hole of the yoke?"

    "It would be a very rare occurrence, lord, that the blind sea turtle, rising to the surface once every century, come up with his neck right through the single hole of the yoke."

    "To be born a human is also a very rare occurrence. Likewise a very rare occurrence for a Tathagata [The Buddha, literally 'one thus gone'], worthy and rightly self awakened, to arise in the world. Likewise a very rare occurrence that a doctrine and discipline expounded by a Tathagata is taught and flourishes in the world. Now, this human state has been obtained. A Tathagata, worthy and rightly self awakened, has arisen in the world. A doctrine and discipline expounded by a Tathagata is taught and flourishes in the world.

    Blind Turtle in Search of a Floating Wood

    In the Mahaparinirvana Sutra, the Buddha used the metaphor of a blind turtle in a vast ocean to explain how difficult it is to be reborn as a human being and at the same time to have the chance of hearing the Dharma.

    Suppose there is a small piece of wood floating on a vast ocean. The wood has a small hole the size of which is just enough for the head of a turtle to pop into. There is a long-lived sea turtle in the ocean. Once every one hundred years, this turtle comes out from the bottom of the ocean and pops his head into the hole of the wood.

    To be able to hear the Dharma is just as hard as for the blind turtle to encounter the small piece of wood on a vast ocean and let its head go through the hole in the wood piece.

    "Behold, bhikkhus, therefore you should persevere to realize as it truly is, 'This is dukkha [unsatisfactory/unpleasant/unhappiness, always changing and falling away] This is the origin of dukkha. This is the cessation of dukkha. This is the path leading to the cessation of dukkha(stress).'"

    Soil on the Buddha's Fingernail

    In the Darukkhandha Sutra, the Buddha once scooped up some soil with his fingernail and asked his disciples which was greater, the soil on his fingernail or that on the whole earth. His disciples answered that the soil on his fingernail was so small that it was incomparable to that on the earth. The Buddha then said that in the same sense only a few beings were able to secure human form whereas far too many beings retrogress into the hells, hungry ghosts and animals.

    USNISA VIJAYA DHARANI SUTRA

    Thus I heard, at Bhagavan (World Honoured One) was dwelling in the city of Shravasti at Jeta Grove, in the Garden of Anthapindada (Benefactor of Orphans and the Solitary), together with his regular disciples of twelve hundred and fifthy great Bhiksus and twelve thousand Maha Boddhisattva Samgha in all.

    At that time the devas (heavenly beings) in Trayastrimsa Heaven were also having a gathering in the Good Dharma Hall. Among them was a devaputra by the name of Susthita who was, together with the other great devaputras, frolicking in the garden and courtyard, enjoying the supreme wonderful bliss of heavenly life. Surrounded by devis (female heavenly beings) they were extremely happy; singing, dancing and amusing themselves.

    Soon after night fall, Devaputra Susthita suddenly heard a voice in space saying, "Devaputra Susthita, you have only seven days (earth's time) left to live. After death you will fall into the hells to undergo more suffering. Only after fulfilling your karmic retribution will you be reborn in the human realm, but to a humble and destitute family; while in the mother's worm you will be without eyes and be born blind."

    On hearing this, Devaputra Susthita was so terrified that his hair stood on ends.Tormented and distressed, he rushed over to the place of Lord Sakra; bursing into tears and not knowing what to do, he prostrated himself at the of Lord Sakra, telling Lora Sakra of what had happened.

    "As I was enjoying the frolics of dances and songs with the heavenly devis, I suddenly heard a voice in the space telling me that I had only seven days of life left, and that I would fall into Jambu-dvipa after death, remaining there in the animal realm for seven successive lives. Following that I will fall into the various hells to endure more suffering. Only after my karmic retribution has been fulfilled will I be reborn as a human, and even then I will be reborn without eyes in a poor and humble family. Lord of Heaven, how can I escape from such suffering?"

    On hearing Devaputra Susthita's tearful appeal, Lord Sakra was greatly astonished and thought to himself, "In what seven successive evil paths and forms will this Deveputra Susthita undergo?"

    Lord Sakra immediately calmed his mind to enter Samadhi and careful observations. Instantly he saw that Devaputra Susthata would undergo seven successive evil paths in the forms of pig, dog, jackal, monkey, python, crow and vulture, all feeding on filth and putrescence. Having seen the seven future rebirth forms of Devaputra Susthita, Lord Sakra was shattered and filled with great sorrow, bit could not think of any way to help Susthita. He felt that only the Tathagata, Arhat, Samyak-Sambuddha could save Susthita from falling into the evil destinies of great sufferings.

    Thus, soon after night fall that very day, Lord Sakra prepared various types of wreaths, perfumes and incense, adorning himself with fine deta garments and bearing these offerings, made his way to the garden of Anathapindada, the abode of the World Honoured One. Upon arrival, Lord first prostrated himself at the feet of the Buddha in worship, before circumambulated the Buddha seven times clockwise in worship, before laying out his great puja (offerings). Kneeling in front of the Buddha, Lord Sakra described the seven successive rebirths in animals realm with details of his subsequent retribution.

    Instantly from the usnisa (crown of the head) of the Tathagata sprang out multiple rays of light illuminating the world in the ten directions; the light then returned, circling the Buddha three times before entering His mouth. The Buddha then smiled and said to Lord Sakra, "Lord of Heaven, there is a Dharani known as the Usnisa Vijaya Dharani, it can purify all evil paths, completely eliminating all birth and death. It can also liberate all miseries and sufferings of beings in the realm of hell, King Yama and animal, destroy all the hells, and deliver sentient beings to the good path."

    Lord of Heaven, if one hears this Usnisa Vijaya Dharani, all the bad karma incurred from his previous lives should cause him to fall into hells will be destroyed altogether. He will instead acquire a fine and pure body, and will clearly remember the Dharani wherever he is reborn, from one Buddhaland to another, from one heavenly realm to another heavenly realm. Indeed throughout the Trayastrimsa Heavens, wherever he is reborn, he will not forget."

    Lord of Heaven, if someone at death's door recalls this Dharani, even for just a moment, his life-span will be extended and he will acquire purification of body, speech and mind. Without suffering any physical pain and in accordance with his meritorious deeds, he will enjoy tranquility everywhere. Receiving blessings from all the Thatagatas, constantly guarded by Devas and protected by Boddhisattvas, he will be honoured and respected by people, and his evils hindrances will be eradicated."

    Lord of Heaven, if someone can sincerely read or recite this Dharani even for a short of time, all his future karmic retributions which would lead him to suffer in the realm of hell, King Yama and hungry ghost, will be completely destroyed and eradicated without leaving any trace. He will be free to go to any of the Buddhas' Pure Lands and heavenly palaces; all gateways leading to the Boddhisattvas' abode are open to him unobstructed.

    " After hearing the above discourse, Lord Sakra immediately appeal to the Buddha, "For the sake of all sentient beings, may the World Honoured One please gives a discourse by which one's life-span can be lengthened.

    At that time, the four Guardians of the World--the Catur-maharajas circumambulating the Buddha three times, and most respectfully said, "World Honoured One, may the Tathagata please explain in details the way to uphold this Dharani."

    The Buddha then told the Four heavenly Kings, saying, "Please listen attentively, for your benefits as well as for the benefits of all beings with short life-span, I will now explain the method to uphold this Dharani. During a full moon day, one should first bathe oneself and put on new clean clothes, take only vegetarian food and recite this Dharani a thousand (1000) times, this will cause him to lengthen his life-span, and be permanently free from the sufferings of illness, all his karmic hindrances, including those which could cause him to suffer in hell will be completely eradicated. If birds, animals and other sentient beings hear this Dharani once, they will be reborn in the impure and gross body forms once their lives ended."

    The Buddha continued, "If someone suffering from a serious disease hear this Dharani, he will be free from it.     1. I undertake the precept to refrain from destroying living creatures.
    2. I undertake the precept to refrain from taking that which is not given.
    3. I undertake the precept to refrain from sexual misconduct.
    4. I undertake the precept to refrain from incorrect speech.
    5. I undertake the precept to refrain from intoxicating drinks and drugs which lead to carelessness.

    And the 10 virtuous deeds are as follows: Refrain from killing, stealing, lying, sexual misconduct, frivolous and meaningless talk, tale bearing, abusive speech, covetousness, ill-will and perverted views.

    And more importantly... we should cultivate wisdom and one day be free from sufferings and the cycle of birth and death and attain the highest bliss of nirvana.
