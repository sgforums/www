    Hi James, Buddhism does not practise worshipping of gods... it is a common mistake and misunderstanding that Buddhist worship gods. That is a Taoist/Chinese folks practise.

    Anyway... Monkey God, as far as I know, is a fictional character. The Journey to the West book is a work of fiction written around the 16th century. It is NOT the real account of the journey to India to get the scriptures by Tang Shan Zhang.

    Goddess of Mercy is actually Avalokitesvara Bodhisattva... In Mahayana Buddhism, the Maha Bodhisattvas are also important role models for us to learn from. Avalokitesvara Bodhisattva represents Great Compassion.

    Guan Yu is a worldly dharma protector. He used to be a very well known warlord Liu Bei who died in war... basically he became a spirit and met a Buddhist venerable and later he vowed to protect Buddhadharma for 3000 years. There is a story about this.... but I can't remember the details now. Does Sinweiy know? We do not worship Guan Yu, but we pay reverence/respect because of the noble work of this dharma protector.

    I'm not sure about Songgo though...

    True Buddhist teachings also teach people to change from bad to good - and not only that, purification of the mind. This is something that most religions do not focus on. The ultimate practise of Buddhism is in the liberation and purification of the Mind.. because the mind is the source of so many things. Good/evil, is all got to do with mind too. Miseries, afflictions, are also in our mind. Enlightenment is only possible with Pure Mind.
