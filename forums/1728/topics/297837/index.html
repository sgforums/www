    Just posted something regarding the non-duality of cause and effect as dependent origination in E-Sangha:

    What is the meaning of the word "conditioned" in "All conditioned things are impermanent." My uncle asked me and I said "Those that arise from a cause." Is that correct?
    That is right, but more accurately, it should be said as 'All that arise, arise dependently', or 'cause is INSEPERABLE from effect'... you cannot separate them at all. Cause and effect arises spontaneously. Take one out, and the other goes with it.. this arises, that arises too. Take electricity (i.e cause) out, the scene in the cinema screen (i.e effect) goes with it. Inseperable.

    The Buddha taught the principle of Dependent Origination as such:

    When this exists, that comes to be;
    With the arising of this, that arises.
    When this does not exist, that does not come to be;
    With the cessation of this, that ceases.

    Since everything is dependent arising, inseperable from conditions, nothing has an independent, persisting existence (i.e inherent existence), lacking any permanent substance, and hence is Empty. Whatever arise are momentary, spontaneously arising appearances inseperable from conditions, without any persisting objective reality.
