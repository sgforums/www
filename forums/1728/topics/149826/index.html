    Originally posted by laurence82:
    Is Nirvana a level?

    For me, its an Exit from the endless cycle, a state, rather than a level...
    Nirvana is beyond any kinds of 'states', anything that can be perceived and is within Space and Time is still not Nirvana. Therefore it cannot be considered as a level.

    However it is not something that one will suddenly "experience" one day in meditation, it requires long term practise, not only in meditation but in the living. Constantly we are mindful/aware, constantly we are purifying the mind.

    In the self-liberation path, Arhant path, even after one truly realises non-self, it is only the first stage called Stream-Enterer. The Stream-Enterer shall attain Arhantship/Nirvana in no more than 7 lifetimes, and is destined to be reborn in the 3 higher realms and not fall into the 3 lower realms. There are altogether 4 stages of sainthood, the 4th is Arhant.

    For the Bodhisattvas, after realising emptiness he begins at the 1st Bhumi stage, and must perfect the 10 paramitas before attaining Buddhahood. There are 10 Bhumis, each bhumi is linked to 1 paramita.
