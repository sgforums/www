    Tsultrim Gyamtso Rinpoche’s Teaching Program
    (Teachings will be conducted in Chinese, English translation provided)
    A qualified teacher who will illuminate the wisdom light of your mind
    with the Dzogchen teachings of pure lineage.
     

    21st (Sat) Nov 2009
    7.30pm-9.30pm
    The Teaching and Recitation of Guru Rinpoche Mantra
    Rinpoche will give a teaching on how to engage in the actual practice of Guru Rinpoche in accordance with Guru Rinpoche Yoga of Dzogchen lineage, and lead us in a short session of practice of Guru Rinpoche while reciting Guru Rinpoche mantra.
     
    22nd (Sun) Nov 2009
    10am-5pm
    One-Day Meditation Retreat with progressive guidance in accordance to Dzogchen instructions
    During the the meditation retreat, Rinpoche will give a progressive guidance on the actual practice of Dzogchen in accordance with Longsal and Longchen lineages of Nyingma tradition, to teach on how to develop practice continuously on the nature of mind, including to teach and fine-tune the methods of meditation, to rectify the problems encountered during meditation, to correct the mistakes in the practice of Tsa, Lung Thigle ( Nadi,Prana and Bindu), and to give the instructions on how to engage in practice through the three doors (Body, speech and mind). Dzogchen practice is non sectarian and all the actual practices of the four traditions can be contained into one practice.
     
    7.30pm-9.30pm
    Public Talk: Respecting Life - The Power of Compassion and Wisdom
    Rinpoche will talk about how to respect life through the development of wisdom and compassion, to love our own lives and also to cherish and protect the lives of others, and to embrace all beings in the world like our own family members.
     
    28th (Sat) Nov 2009
    7.30pm-9.30pm
    The Teachings and Recitation of Bodhisattva Kshitigarbha Mantra
    Rinpoche will give a teaching on how to practise Bodhisattva Kshitigarbha, its visualisation and the benefit of reciting Bodhisattva Kshitigarbha Mantra. Rinpoche will also lead us in a short sessioin of practice of Bodhisattva Kshitigarbha while reciting his mantra.
     
    29th (Sun) Nov 2009
    10am-5pm
    One-Day Mani Recitation & Chenrezig Empowerment
    Rinpoche will endow a Four-armed Chenrezig Empowerment, teach on the visualisation of Chenrezig, and lead us in the actual practice of Chenrezig while reciting Mani mantra. Rinpoche said that everyone of us have deep Dharmic connections with Chenrezig, it is very beneficial for us to recite the Mani mantra. It will help us recite Mani mantra more effectively when we have received the empowerment of Chenrezig.
     
    7.30pm-9.30pm
    Public Talk: Returning to the Homeland of one's Mind
    In our daily life and work as we encounter more and more pressure and depressions, do we remember the peaceful homeland of our mind? Rinpoche will talk on how to generate the Buddha Dharma in our minds and naturally eliminate our afflictive emotions and pressure, so as to return to the homeland of our mind.

    A Brief Introduction to Tsultrim Gyamtso Rinpoche
     
    Tsultrim Gyamtso Rinpoche was born in 1975 at Pema in Qinghai, China. He was endowed with the name Menchog Gyalbo by Khenchen Oshe at the age of three. Osel Rinpoche recognised him as a reincarnated Tulku and named him Tuden Rinchen Nyima when he was eight. He was ordained and received his novice vow at the age of 14.
     
    At the age of 15, Rinpoche went to Seda area in Sichuan and relied on Khenpo Pema Choeying Kyabdal ï¼ˆKhenpo Choekyabï¼‰ who was regarded as Milarepa of the time and observed very pure precepts. In six years, he studied many sutras and commentaries on Vinaya (Precepts) and received many empowerments and instructions of Nyingma tradition from Khenpo Choekyab.
     
    At the age of 21, Rinpoche came to Larong Five- Sciences Buddhist Institute. From Choeje Jigme Phuntsok, he received extensive teachings on different sects of Tibetan Buddhism, including Nyingma, Gelug, Kadampa, Kagyu, Sakya etc, and studied the teachings of Mipham Rinpoche and Choeje Jigme Phuntsok.
     
    At the age of 26, following the prophecy of Wisdom Dakini, Rinpoche came to Yachen Monastery in Sichuan. When he met Jamyang Lungdok Gyaltsen Rinpoche (Lama Achuk Rinpoche), tremendous devotion arose in his mind. He then served Lama Rinpoche perfectly in the three ways of pleasing the guru, and received many profound teachings from him, especially the empowerments on luminous essence of the mind and instructions in accordance with Longsal and Longchen Nyingtig of Nyingma tradition. He practised the pith instructions given by Lama Rinpoche day and night. His practice and realisation has been recognised and highly praised by Lama Rinpoche. He became one of the eminent heart disciples of Lama Rinpoche.
     
    Lama Rinpoche recognised him as a reincarnation of Jigmey Gyalwey Nyugu, he said, “Jigmey Lingpa was one of the heart disciples of Longchenpa and his lineage holder was Jigmey Gyalwey Nyugu. Tsultrim Gyamtso Rinpoche is the reincarnation of Jigmey Gyalwey Nyugu. I give this authentic prophecy to him who possesses the wisdom of knowing all as they are and as they appear. ”
     
    From the age of 18, Rinpoche started turning the wheels of the Buddha Dharma both in Sutrayana and Mantrayana to Tibetan and Chinese devotees. For the past eight years, following the advice of Lama Rinpoche, Rinpoche has transmitted the teachings of Dzogchen (Great Perfection) to many local and foreign devotees in more than ten countries and regions, including Brazil, Germany, England, South Africa, Japan, South Korea, Malaysia, Singapore, Hong Kang and Taiwan.
     
    Rinpoche yearned to undertake retreat in cave for life like Jigmey Gyalwey Nyugu. However Lama Rinpoche instructed him that he should not stay in solitary practice and that he should compassionately and extensively give the teachings of Dzogchen to sentient beings instead. Therefore to fulfil the wishes of his guru Lama Rinpoche, Rinpoche has wholeheartedly transmitted the teachings of Dzogchen to the disciples with karmic connections all over the world.

    Nyung Nay at ABC
    Weekend of 12 - 14 Dec 2009

     

    Once again, we are organising this fasting retreat at Amitabha Buddhist Centre.
     
    Nyung Nay is a powerful Mahayana practice that helps us to generate strong compassion toward all sentient beings, wishing to free them from all suffering by relying on the compassionate qualities of Avalokiteshvara or Chenrezig. 
     
    Through the practice of fasting, making prayers and prostrations, the Nyung Nay practitioner purifies obscurations and accumulates vast merit, and can strengthen the resolve to achieve enlightenment for the benefit of all sentient beings.
     
    The Nyung Nay practice is thus a perfect practice opportunity for those with the Mahayana motivation.
     

    Our residential Nyung Nay will be held as follows:
     
    Check-in  : 11 Dec (Fri), 7 pm
    Check-out: 14 Dec (Mon), 11 am
    Venue:       Amitabha Buddhist Centre
    Fee    :      Member $60 / Non-member $80 (to covers costs for meals, offerings, accommodation and utilities)
     

    As an absolute minimum, participants should have taken Refuge and the 8 Mahayana Precepts.
     
    Due to space constraints, please note that the number of participants at this event is limited to 45 places only.
     
    Registration will be on a first-come-first-served basis. Should there be an overwhelming response, priority will be given to the following applicants:

     
    1. Those who have taken a Great Initiation of Chenrezig and need to complete a Nyung Nay commitment within a stipulated period
    2. Those who have been advised by their teachers to do Nyung Nay
    3. Those who can stay behind to help with clean-up after the retreat ends on 14 Dec 09.

    If you are keen to take part in this retreat, please fill in the Registration Form by clicking on the link REGISTER HERE by Saturday, 28 Nov 2009.

    ABOUT PALCHEN RINPOCHE

    Sonam Zangpo Palchen Rinpoche was born in Nangchen, Qinghai. He is recognized by Jamgon Tai Situ Rinpoche as the reincarnation of Sonam Zangpo Rinpoche. The previous Sonam Zangpo Rinpoche was the Lama of late Kyabje Drubwang Sangye Tenzin Rinpoche, who had spent his life in retreat.

    About  H.E. Sangye Nyenpa Rinpoche
    The 1st Sangye Nyenpa Rinpoche, Tashi Paljor was the root lama of Mikyö Dorje, the 8th Karmapa. The present 10th Sangye Nyenpa Rinpoche was recognized by H.H. 16th Karmapa, who saw through His undiluted wisdom eye the birthplace, the name of the parents, the year and sign of birth and thus gave clear indications. Nyenpa Rinpoche was born in a family of practitioners; Sangye Lekpa and the mother Karma Tshewang Choden, who resided at Guru Rinpoche’s temple, the Tiger Nest Pharo Tagtsang in Bhutan. He was invited to Rumtek Monastery where he was enthroned by H.H. Karmapa and given the name of Karma Palden Rangjung Thrinle Kunkyab Tenpe Gyaltsen Pal Sangpo. At the age of 5 he started his studies, writing and reading as well as the outer and inner sciences relying on H.H. Karmapa, H.H. Dilgo Khyentse Rinpoche and other great masters. In particular he received from H.H. the novice and Bodhisattva vows, many empowerments of the highest Yoga Tantra, instructions on Chagchen Da Ser (Moonbeams of Mahamudra), Marig Münsel (Eliminating the Darkness of Ignorance), Chöku Tzubtsug (Pointing the finger at the Dharmakaya) etc. and thus was introduced to the ultimate realisation. He completed 10 years of studies at the Nalanda Institute in Rumtek and obtained the title of an Acharya. Thereafter he was teaching for 3 years at the institute. Nyenpa Rinpoche is one of the most learned Rinpoches in both philosophy and tantric rituals. Presently he lives in his monastery Benchen Phuntsok Dargyeling Kathmandu, Nepal giving teachings to the monks and other disciples. The rest of the time he spends in retreat.
    For more of Rinpoche and his monastery, please visit http://www.sangyenyenpa.com/
