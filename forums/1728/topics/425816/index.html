    I shall use the sense of sight and conceptual thinking,
    Usually we perceive and and our conceptual thinking reacts
    in relation to "I", our conceptual thinking serve as judgement
    on how to react, based on our stored attraction and aversion
    to things and events. Normally, we skip our focus between 
    these two and assume it is one flow of thought. Sort of 
    looking out looking in. 
    Normal day to day awareness can be visualized as sensory 
    perception and conceptual thought balanced on a see- saw.

    Thoughts can be classified as collected or scattered, collected 
    usually means our concentration is focused or the ability to 
    comprehend and follow sequence and shift of events.
    Scattered thoughts usually means the inability to assemble
    rational thoughts or systematical of dealing with issues at 
    hand. 
    Our consciousness also have and agitative and dull states.
    And exaggerated example would be akin drinking to much
    caffeine or some kind of stimulants and drowsy sluggish 
    reaction one gets when taking depressants like alcohol or 
    cough syrup.

    Then there is awareness when one is the watcher, the "I" of
    the conceptual thinking, where two distinct processes are going,
    much watcher watching a movie or a nondescript dream, there
    is certain deja vu kind of feeling, somehow really not down to earth.

    Then, there is another perception during sitting in which  awareness
    of outer phenomena is a collective of central, peripheral and
    auditory and tactile sensation.  Thoughts arises and but seems
    rather minor and inconsequential as one falls and another arises.
    The expansiveness and brilliancy of the collective perception
    makes normal sensation seem dull and heavy.
    Absence of emotion is silencing and clear, like been in a noisy
    public transport and then going in a sound proof round, and
    from wearing old scratched  and  blurry glasses now you you
    got new ones.Or the acuteness of sensation like minute beads 
    of perspiration oozing out as the gentle breeze blows across the
    forehead, or the waft of your own bodily odor. 

    Now back normal day to day practice, just "being", is being
    bringing your state of consciousness to being centered on 
    the see saw,being aware but not indulging in either. Not
    indulging means having a light touch, not reacting strongly
    or jerky, not getting overly personal. When negative emotions
    arises, the sensing of quickening the tensing of jaw muscles,
    and tightening of chest,  bringing attention to anger, 
    acknowledging and then that too will pass.

      
    The more  we "work" at it , the more easily we can return to
    back to this equilibrium. Working mean being not going back
    to previous habitual patterns. It becomes less trying. And
    each time we go back to this equilibrium, we start afresh,
    it ok when let go of our "stories", we start at square one and
    and not find it bothersome.

    Yes. Good insight. Not only is there no one flow of thoughts... There is no continuous movement of things... there is nothing constant or substantial at all to our experience. Walking from here to there, there is in fact no here and there much less a person moving from here to there, there is only this, then this, then this... but there is no The Here either. The sense of there being The Here or The Now is another illusion. Here, now, thought, sight, etc, all ungraspable, dream-like, bubble like. Then it is realized and experienced that there is no continuous flow of thought, only bubbles manifesting and popping (self-liberating) moment by moment tracelessly. There is nothing truly there! Truly groundless, truly traceless, truly free.

    Through contemplation, after the initial insight of anatta, there can be a further insight where we discover that all phenomena are disjoint, unsupported, and self-liberating, and arising without an agent. Just this one thought, one sound.... completely distinct from a previous moment of thought, yet interdependently originated as a process.

    Anyway here is something by Thusness that may be of relevance here (and as suggested here, mindfulness must complement with right view otherwise the insight into non-dual and anatta will not arise):

     

    Hi Kim123,

    Just to add a lil, see whether it makes sense to you.

    As a start it is almost not possible not to feel dualistic.  An observer observing the observed is our ordinary experience and it will appear that this is an experiential fact.  Therefore we should not rush into anything but just simply recognize the ‘cause’.  The cause that made us see in such a way is termed ‘ignorance’.  Try to understand ‘ignorance’ not as not knowing but a form of knowing instead.  See it as a very deep form of ‘dualistic knowing’ that we have taken it to be truth.  We then proceed to overcome this wrong view in 2 steps; one by strongly and firmly establishing the right view to replace our existing ‘dualistic and inherent view’ and second, practice seeing in bare attention to lessen the grip of views. Practice being bare in bodily sensation till there is a very strong clear mirror feeling in bodily sensation.  Then with the right view, non-dual will dawn.  Without the right view, it will most likely turn into a mirror reflecting phenomena experience.

    Practices can take decades and often quite frustrating and challenging during the journey.  But have faith, be patient and have confidence, all effort will proof worthwhile eventually.

    A simple summary I use to help my practice:-

    When there is simply a pure sense of existence;
    When awareness appears mirror like;
    When sensations become pristine clear and bright;
    This is luminosity.

    When all arising appear disconnected;
    When appearance springs without a center;
    When phenomena appears to be on their own without controller;
    This is no doer-ship.

    When subject/object division is seen as illusion;
    When there is clarity that no one is behind thoughts;
    When there is only scenery, sounds, thoughts and so forth;
    This is anatta.

    When phenomena appears pristinely crystal;
    When there is merely one seamless experience;
    When all is seen as Presence;
    This is non-dual Presence.

    When we feel fully the unfindability and unlocatability of phenomena;
    When all experiences are seen as ungraspable;
    When all mind boundaries of in/out, there/here, now/then dissolve;
    This is Emptiness.

    When interconnectedness of everything is wholly felt;
    When arising appears great, effortless and wonderful;
    When presence feels universe;
    This is Maha.

    When arising is not caged in who, where and when;
    When all phenomena appear spontaneous and effortless;
    When everything appears right every where, every when;
    This is spontaneous perfection.
     
    Seeing these as the ground of all experiences;
    always and already so;
    This is wisdom.

    Experiencing the ground in whatever arises;
    This is practice.

    Happy journey. :)

    Originally posted by An Eternal Now:

    What do you mean by 'sensing but no referencing'? How is it different from pure experience.

    Let me use an example, imagine you are listening to a lecture,

    sometimes you will listen and you will pause, trying to take stock
    of what the lecturer is saying so far, in segments.
    However, there will be moments where you give your bare attention'
    with intent and intensity,taking the whole of his narrative without 
    pausing, at that moment your full attention is focus on his speech
    in it's entirety.
    Your are still you, and he is still he, but at that length of time,
    you are fully immersed in his speech.
    Usually, we take information by bits, building up point of reference 
    as we go along, hoping to make sense of things bit by bit, building 
    up a conceptual idea or around a concept, trying to fit it to our 
    understanding, This is what I mean by referencing.
