    Personally I would recommend audio-visual or reading materials according to the person's level of exposure to and/or understanding of any Buddhist concept. I would also recommend based on the person's liking or preference for topics taught by a particular Venerable.

    Unless I know, I will not dare to carelessly recommend. I will have to talk with that person more often to know.

    For example, some who prefers Zen to Pureland, I will recommend the Heart Sutra, Patriarch Hui Neng and Diamond sutra, etc to him/her.

    It is also preferable that these materials, audio-visual or text, are watched or read with the guidance of an wise, patient and compassionate fellow Buddhist or Venerable. This is to prevent any accumulation of doubts or wrong views in any person while or after having listened or read about Buddhist concepts.

    I would personally recommend beginner Buddhists to start reading books on what is the Triple Gems, what is the Five precepts. Find books, VCDs or DVDs, that explain these in a simple way and at the same time, gives us clear guidelines on how to apply them in different real-life scenarios.

    As I have not yet come across a greater variety of books and cds that meet the above criteria, I cannot recommend any specific title to any of you here at the moment.
