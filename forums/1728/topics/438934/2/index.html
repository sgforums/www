    @ Sabrina

     

    According to the Buddha, we are the makers of our own destiny.  We alone are responsible for our lives.  We created everything.  All our grief, perils and misfortunes are of our own creation.  We spring from no other source than our own imperfections of heart and mind.   We are the results of our good and bad actions committed in the past under the influence of greed and delusion.  And since we ourselves brought them into being, it is within our power to overcome bad effects and cultivate good natures.

     

    Many years ago, a close friend came to me for advice.  I am just a lay Buddhist practioner, not any great master.   Her friend had experienced something similar to the extent that the being appeared in front of family members which spooked them.  Of course, there were also many strange things that happened which I will not go into great length.

     

    I advised the family to do purification themselves.  They had difficulties.  To cut the story short, I recorded my own chanting of The 100-syllable Purification Mantra of Vajrasattva for them to play it in their house instead. I also gave them a picture of the Vajrasattva Buddha and the verses for them to chant along with the recording.  I was told that the being has since left and their home has been peaceful since.  There were no more strange sightings.

     

    I believe that spirits that haunt a place have been there longer than you or I think they exist.  Perhaps, we could be the one disturbing them.  Perhaps, they were the first occupants of the place before us.  Perhaps, it was their home before we came.  However, do not jump to any conclusion that they are here to harm or not to harm us.  Do not attempt to communicate to them as this will bring more harm than help.

     

    Do not be scared Sabrina.  Seek refuge with the Three Jewels (Buddha, Dharma and Sangha) and once you have taken genuine refuge, you must develop strong faith that the Buddha will protect you and your family.  Do not believe in any master who claimed that they have clairvoyance but does nothing to offer good advice or genuine help.  There are many con-artistes out there who out-perform actors/actresses.

     

    Taking refuge in the Buddha is a confident acceptance of the fact that one can become fully enlightened and perfected as the Buddha was.

     

    Taking refuge in the Dharma means understanding the Four Noble Truths and basing one’s life on the Noble Eightfold Path.

     

    Taking refuge in the Sangha means looking for support, inspiration and guidance from all who walk the Noble Eightfold Path.

     

    Sabrina, if you believe this, doing this, one becomes a Buddhist, and thus takes the first step on the path towards enlightenment and liberation.  This is the refuge whereby one is freed from all suffering.  If you take refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma and the Sangha, you will be free from fear and trembling.
