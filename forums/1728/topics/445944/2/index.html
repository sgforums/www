    it's only natural to see such diverse interpretations on the same subject (ie. old men sitting...) because that's the reality of how individuals interpret and give meaning to things.  we can only interpret and appreciate things from our respective (karmic) circumstances and nothing outside of it.  

    a person with more advanced spiritual practice may gain valuable realizations from a mundane observation like old men sitting around, whereas a person with no spiritual practice will have more mundane interpretations like poverty, health.. etc. neither is really more right or wrong because their respective (karmic) circumstances only enable them to interpret as such.  so you can say both are "right" because that's what they are able to interpret given their own circumstances.

    nature (or karma, ç¼˜.. etc) is not really interested in right or wrong, only what can be manifested (or perceived, interpreted by intellect.. etc) based on what the current (karmic) circumstances allows.  i guess that is why as enlightened as the Buddha is, he still has to "work within the system" and cannot simply "give" you enlightenment if your (karmic) circumstances does not allow it.
