    Firstly he did indicate some of his details of his next life if im not wrong, as well as having stated his Bodhisattva vows to continue being born again here to save sentient beings. (cheng yuan zai lai) Next, my taiwanese dharma teacher (another one, my master's dharma successor) have visions of him and his family and his life, so he does update our students time to time of his development.

    Next, Buddhists believe in rebirth. All samsaric beings are trapped in the cycle of rebirth in samsara... which is the world of sufferings, consisting of six realms. There are three lower realms: Hell, ghost, animals, and three higher realms: Humans, asuras, devas. One is being reborn according to his karma, whether wholesome, unwholesome, or pure karma. Generally one can maintain higher realm rebirths as long as he keeps his precepts, does 10 virtuous deeds, being generous, filial and so on.. whereas one enters the lower realms by breaking precepts, doing evil, etc.

    One who attains enlightenment, nirvana, is freed from sufferings in this very lifetime, and is no longer trapped in the cycle of samsara, become freed from all sufferings of samsara and attain the highest lasting bliss of Nirvana. This brings about the question "then how come bodhisattvas and Buddhas come back to samsara"? They do not come back because of their karma, they come back because of compassionate vows to save sentient beings. Arhats are also liberated, but because they walk the path of liberating only himself, they do not have the great vows to save other sentient beings. One who practises the Bodhisattva way will eventually attain Buddhahood. Though these enlightened beings appear in samsara to save sentient beings, they are actually at bliss and does not suffer... though they still manifest Birth, Old Age, Sickness and Death.
