    There are two sources of Bodhisattva vows:-


    Two sets of Buddhist vows

    There are mainly two specific sets of vows that Buddhists take in formal settings nowadays, although every Mahayana aspirant is encouraged to make individual vows too.

    One set is from the Brahma Net Sutra and followed in China, Japan & Korea & the rest of East Asia.http://www.purifymind.com/BrahmaNetSutra.htm

    The other is used as an expanded set of rules for those aspiring to become Bodhisattvas, by the Tibetan & Mongolian Mahayanists of Central Asia:

    http://buddhism.kalachakranet.org: Bodhisattva Vows
    These constitute secondary vows:-
    32. On Harming Sentient Beings
    A disciple of the Buddha must not sell knives, clubs, bows, arrows, other life-taking devices, nor keep altered scales or measuring devices. He should not abuse his governmental position to confiscate people's possessions, nor should he, with malice at heart, restrain or imprison others or sabotage their success. In addition, he should not raise cats, dogs, foxes, pigs and other such animals. If he intentionally does such things, he commits a secondary offense. 

    From what I understand secondary downfalls are unhelpful as compared to root downfalls.

    On casual observation, one would have seen the applicability of the two
    different set of vows, both are agarian cultures, one predominantly on
    agriculture, the other, pastoralist.

    Owning or keeping pets have may have two unhelpful causes,one of  overly

    attachment, the other, indifference, leading to  impersonalisation.
    People often have fuzzy lines between ownership and relationship with
    animals as pets. Also perception of different member of the family differs.

    The type of pets we keep also depends ability to cope with demands of the 
    relationship. Cats and dogs have different bondings as do breeds.

    There is a need to consider the pet as a member of familly, as obligations
    to towards the animal is different if compared to an infant as inclusion to
    the family, even though the baby is tougher to raise. Thus when bringing 
    a pet requires commitment and thus much thought. Some pets are more
    adaptive and some families are  more accommodating, however, the reverse is 
    also true. Animals also provide companionship for the childless. 

    I am a sucker for animals, as a kid, I would enjoy playing with animals, feeding
    chicken, rearing fishes. I would try adopt feral dogs and cats, only to be told to 
    return them back.

    One thing about loving animals is you develop empathy and responsibility over
    other sentient beings, you learn to be aware of them as being an individual,
    capable of emotions and idiosyncrasies.  Also animals are capable of returning
    our affectoins and also capable of being naughty. And also responsibility too, 
    there was a British documentary on how to prevent their cats from bringing home
    prey which the cats brought back to their owners.

    Another is the impermernance of life,  as pets are inevitability of shorter lifespan
    than us humans. The stronger the attachment, the more the loss is being felt.
    Now, the question is, do you wish to expose or shield your kids at an earlier
    age than necessary? Death is is something is dawned slowly on us.

    Keeping fishes, note plurality,  as there is less connectivity with them, although
    i may be wrong, but, we live in two different environment, and also it is so very 
    easy to flush them down the toilet when they die. However, it is also possible to observe individuality in each of them, even though they don't communicate with you
    like non aquarium pets.
