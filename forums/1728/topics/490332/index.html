    Despite the inclusive nature of Buddhist, Polytheist such as Daoist, Hindus etc., if we still have sense of self, we naturally develop a sense of being who or what we are, in that we(believe) are innately right, we also may well have develped a sense of what we are not being wrong. Not perfect, generally right. Ruffled this sense of unspoken assertion, you get defensive, maybe even agressive. It is this indignance that I would label as self righteousness.

     

    Whether we consciously or subconsciously perceive ourselves as innately more superior, or that we see ourselves as just nomal, average and perhaps,  socially and morally more acceptable. Or maybe them ( the other religion or creed) think they are superior, thus worthy being subject to our disdain or they are just morally or socially depraved(acts in name of God and Religion), thus are entitled to be subjected to general disapproval and disgust. Nett result is actually the same.

     

    I shall repeat again, nett result is the same. We treat and subject people and their beliefs against the yardstick of how different they are from us. We are so used to comparing and commenting on others, how bad or evil they are.

     

    When we convert or subscribe ourselves to a higher belief or creed,  we are automatically insinuating that our previous position are inferior? Think about it for while, what is wrong about that sentence? If I being a better person today, are'nt I then a lousier person yesterday?!  What then about my peers who were my peers, my equals, yesterday?!!

     

    Very too often, when we assign the negative act to the person, the person  become negative, or evil.  

     

    In order to satisfy our self righteousness, we lump the negative act/s and person as one inseparable, a single entity. The evil is unpardonable, and to hold accountability, the person. We demonize, because as such we've become self righteous, we've no better than those we dispise. We are just like what they are, HUMANS.

     

    It is the unsatisfactory state of being humans that we seeking answers in religion.

    Antidote? Develop no self, during the path, cultivate loving kindness and practise compassion and generosity. And while we are it, be humble and do not hung up on imposing uprightheousness. Just practise the right conduct and ground yourself with that. Self righteousness is simply an adversness in reaction to one's self.

     

    If you are practising the Bodhisattva's Way, So you cannot allow the indulgence of looking at others with revulsion or disdain. That's the surest path to hell. With compassion comes the path of skilful means, we adapt ourselves to the situation.

     

    So do not cringe if you see a shaven person in kassa robes spewing intolerance and hate, and people actually supporting him/her. That's just some pretty unhappy persons holding one unhappy person in awe. Are they practising tolerance, loving kindness and compassion? So are they practising Buddhism? So what is it that make us Buddhist(practitioners)? Are we really immune?

     

    But old habits die hard, so perhaps we'd like to think we are better, in fact we are not that much better, we only think so. So a little humility is in order

    I would like to point out the difference between righteous and self righteousness, also hypocrisy. 

     

    Righteous: characterized by uprightness or morality; morally right or justifiable; acting in an upright, moral way
    (Synonyms include good, honest, fair, right)

    Self-righteous: confident of one's own righteousness, esp. when smugly moralistic and intolerant of the opinions and behavior of others
    (Synonyms include sanctimonious, pharisaical)

    Hypocritical: a person who pretends to have virtues, moral or religious beliefs, principles, etc., that he or she does not actually possess, esp. a person whose actions belie stated beliefs; a person who feigns some desirable or publicly approved attitude, esp. one whose private life, opinions, or statements belie his or her public statements
    (Synonyms include deceiver, dissembler, pretender, pharisee
