    Locals win S'pore Open

    By Lin Xinyi

    Women's badminton doubles pair (from left) Shinta Mulia Sari and Yao Lei celebrating after their win yesterday. It is the first major women's doubles title for Singapore, and the first time any Singaporean individual or pair has won a Super Series title. -- ST PHOTO: MUGILAN RAJASEGERAN

     

    SHINTA Mulia Sari and Yao Lei yesterday ended the Republic's 20-year wait for a local player to be crowned champion at the Singapore Open.

    The host nation's top women's badminton doubles pair, unseeded at the US$200,000 (S$277,000) Li-Ning Singapore Open, upset fifth seeds Kim Min Jung and Lee Hyo Jung of South Korea 21-17, 22-20 in the final yesterday.

    The closest any local player has come to triumphing in this event was Ronald Susilo's runner-up finish in 2002.

    The win is the first major women's doubles title for Singapore, and the first time any Singaporean individual or pair has won a Super Series title.

    The Super Series is the second tier of tournaments on the Badminton World Federation's (BWF) calendar, behind competitions such as the Thomas/Uber Cups and the World Championships.

    Kim and Lee are unranked in the world standings because they were paired together only recently.

    Source: ST
