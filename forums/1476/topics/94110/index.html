    Being an advocate of new world, new decisions, new options and new choices, I will have to say the following (a little too ideal but still can be managed):

    1) Firstly, love is a two-way process as such I feel it might be better if
    - If there is a conflict between two decisions, always follow the rule of having equal share of the outcome. i.e. Today, your decisions. Tomorrow, my decisions. Until then, you have to try to accomodate one another's way of life even in the future.

    2) Secondly, money is an external factor in love. It cannot be used to subject another to one's will. There will be a time when the mine can run out of gold. Moreover, we must understand we're no longer in a time phrase where might alone earns the keep. Men are no longer the sole breadwinner of the family, so we must adopt the practice of sharing burden. Thus, it is no longer important to remind ourselves who is footing the bill.

    3) Thirdly, love is unconditional. Please throw away the old view that men at work, women at chores. Women are not items, possessions and slaves or decorational vases. They have their own views and ideals such that we cannot avoid and should not force them to accept roles we expect them to. However, it changes when other responsibilities are more critical like and we might need to adjust to suit the needs to give up the wants:
    - Having a handicapped family member that require constant care
    - Having children, or aged family members that are not capable of handling themselves
    Thus, in situations similar to the abovementioned, a consensus must be reached between both parties.

    4) Last, love is between two parties, not the whole world. Your own decisions and calls are more important than anything.
