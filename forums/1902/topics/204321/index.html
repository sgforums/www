Rafflesia Flower

  • Gordonator
    10 Aug 06, 21:37
    Named after Sir Thomas Stamford Raffles, the Rafflesia Flower is the world's largest single flower and is found in Southeast Asia in the Malay Peninsula, Borneo, Sumatra and Kalimantan, West Malaysia and the Philippines.

    The flower has no leaves and hardly any stem, just a huge speckled five-petaled flower with a diameter up to 106cm, and weighing up to 10kg.

    The flowers smell like rotting meat, thus it is also known as "corpse flower" or "meat flower".
