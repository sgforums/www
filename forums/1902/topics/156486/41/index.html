    An elderly Pakistani earthquake survivor stands outside his damaged house destroyed by the October 8 earthquake in the village Lari in Khagan Valley, 250 Km (155 miles) northwest of Islamabad November 6, 2005. Barely a house is still standing in Pakistan's earthquake-shattered Khagan valley and in a few weeks time it will lie under a blanket of deep snow. The official death toll from the devastating earthquake that hit North West Frontier and Pakistani Kashmir stands at more than 73,000, with another 1,300 killed in Indian Kashmir.The Khagan valley settlements are about 6,000 feet up (2,600 metres) and are usually carpeted in deep snow from late December until spring.
