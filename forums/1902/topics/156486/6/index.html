    Helicopter earthquake missions



    Introduction

    Helicopters are playing a vital role in the relief operation after last week's earthquake centred in Pakistani-administered Kashmir.

    It's dangerous work, one helicopter has already crashed killing all on board. And bad weather has left them grounded at times.

    Chaklala airbase at the garrison city of Rawalpindi bursts into activity at 0630 every day.

    The helicopter operation has already saved thousands of lives, the army says.

    Text, photos: Aamer Ahmed Khan

    International effort

    By sunrise, only a few out of the 50-odd helicopters at Pakistan's disposal remain on the ground.

    It is an international effort. As well as the Pakistani helicopters, there are Chinooks and Blackhawks from the United States and other helicopters from Afghanistan, France and the International Committee of the Red Cross.
