    Sea slugs (Family Onchidae)

    Onchs are gastropods but lack shells. Their gills are modified for breathing air. They are hermaphrodites with complicated reproductive organs to ensure cross- but not self-fertilisation.

    Mud lobster (Thalassina sp.)

    This common but rarely seen crustacean plays a key role in mangroves. It creates the characteristic volcano-like mounds of mud in the back mangroves as it eats-and-digs in the mud. These mounds provide living spaces for a variety of other mangrove creatures.
