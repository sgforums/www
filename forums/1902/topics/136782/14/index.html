    Acorn worm (Class Enteropneusta)

    These coils often seen on the sand have been processed by the Acorn worm. Acorn worms live in burrows in the sand, processing sediments for edible bits. At low tide, they stick their backsides to the surface and eject the cast.

    File Snake (Acrochordus granulatus)

    File Snakes have loose granular skin that they use to grip their fish prey. These snakes are found in coastal habitats and have valved nostrils that they can close when they are underwater, They give birth to live young. These snakes are gentle and, like most snakes, will not bite unless they are disturbed or picked up. If you see one, just watch it as it goes about its business.
