    Butterfly fishes are usually associated with reefs. Often, a few are trapped in pools at low tide in the coral rubble area. They have long tube-like mouths which are used to suck up coral polyps and other titbits.

    Scorpionfish (Family Scorpaenidae)

    Scorpionfishes lie motionless on the bottom, their colour and body shape
    mimicking rocks and other innocuous objects. They suck up unwary fish
    that come within range by suddenly snapping open their huge mouths.
    They have poisonous spines in their dorsal fins, which cause pain but not death.
