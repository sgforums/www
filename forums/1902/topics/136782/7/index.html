    Sponges (Phylum Porifera)

    A sponge is a simple animal made up of a few types of cells. These cells are largely independent of each other and only loosely held together. A sponge can almost be considered a colony of cells. These cells do not form organs so a sponge does not have a mouth, digestive system or circulatory system!

    Common sea pen (Pteroides sp.)

    This sea pen is commonly seen in the seagrass lagoon. Sea pens are colonial animals belonging to the same group as corals, sea anemones and jellyfish. The central stalk is one animal, while the 'feathery' portions are other animals. Their common name comes from their resemblence to feather quill pens used in the past.
