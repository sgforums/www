    Brittle Star (Order Ophiuroidea)

    Brittle stars are echinoderms and related to "normal" seastars. They get their name from their ability to drop off their arms, for example, when grabbed by a fish. A new arm will regrow.

    Seastar (Class Asteroidea)

    Often called starfish, sea stars are not fish. They are echinoderms related to sand dollars, sea urchins, sea cucumbers and brittle stars. These sea stars are more commonly seen at night in the seagrass lagoon.

    Common sea star (Archaster typicus)

    Common sea stars are sometimes seen one on top of another. The male is usually smaller and on top, interlocking his arms with the female below. Their sexual organs do not meet as they practice external fertilisation, releasing eggs and sperm at the same time. This behaviour is believed to ensure more effective external fertilisation.
