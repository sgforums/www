    Carpet Anemone (Stichodactyla sp.)

    Carpet anemones harbour symbiotic algae called zooxanthallae in their tentacles. The algae carry out photosynthesis and share the food they produce with the anemone. In return, the anemone provides protection and nutrients. It is the algae that give the different colours to Carpet anemones.

    Carpet anemone (Stichodactyla sp.)

    A carpet anemone looks much like a mushroom. It has a large oral disk covered with tentacles, with a mouth in the centre. Below, buried in the ground, is a thick cylindrical body column that ends in a pedal disk to anchor the animal down.
