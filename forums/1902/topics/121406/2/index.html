  • cornyfish2000
    9 Mar 05, 18:18
    Wow, congratz!

    Hmmm.. but personally i've never spawned angelfishes before leh, so no first-hand experience to share.

    But i read before that there's basically a 50-50 chance that the adults will eat up the young, depending on the individual fish and also especially when stressed/hungry.

    Have the eggs hatched? If i were u i'd probably remove the adults once the eggs have all hatched and are in the 'wriggler' stage. Of course, u can't have your filter on or else the little babies will all end up sucked into the filter, but do maintain the water well-aerated with air pump. The adult angelfishes usually do the job of keeping the eggs well-oxygenated by fanning over the eggs.

    Oh, also try to keep the water as clean as possible by removing any dead baby fishes and stuff.

    Lastly, u'd have to feed the baby angels.. Usually people feed baby fishes with baby brine shrimps which u can purchase at most aquariums.. I think they might have some other specialized foods for these babies too, can try asking the aquarium shopkeeper abt that..

    Good luck and keep us updated!
