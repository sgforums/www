    Sables, also known as Martes zibellina, is a graceful carnivore of the weasel family, found in the forests of northern Asia and highly valued for its fine fur. The sable population is not threatened yet, so they are not in the endangered species list. However, the numbers are fast dwindling because they are vigorously hunted for the fur. A sable fur coat can fetch up to USD63,000.
