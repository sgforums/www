    1. check the water temp. If you had a heater installed set it to lower range @ appr.28-29 . However, some spawn are able to live at room temperature.

    2. The water must be clean before any spawning as it will occur within days

    3. Keep the parent with the spawn until they are free swimming before removing the pair. The chance of the spawn to live is higher as the parent will look after them for the initial 2 week after they hatch
