    Late night in Luton



    Open all hours?

    While the debate continues about what effect new extended licensing laws will have, what happened in the Bedfordshire town of Luton the night the new measures came into force?

    Along the Park Street strip drinking past 11pm is already commonplace, with closing times staggered until 3am to avoid everyone piling onto the streets at the same time.

    Business as usual

    Barman James Fox works in the Bat and Barrel. The pub already has a 3am licence as it has a function room.

    They have applied to open between 10am and 5am and expect to hear in January if they have been successful. Even then, they expect to mostly stick to 10am until 11pm opening hours, as they do now.

    "We'll just lock the front door and if anyone else wants to come in they can knock on the side door," he said.

    Image overhaul

    In the past 18 months Luton has made efforts to deal with drink-related crime and disorder.

    After it was voted one of the country's worst towns it decided to try to clean up its image with the Luton Safe scheme.

    Licensees now regularly meet to decide whether certain culprits should be banned from the town's bars and for how long (up to 12 months).

    On the look-out

    For John O'Reilly, landlord of The Castle, the new laws mean he will open for an extra hour-and-a-half so as not to lose business to the late-opening pubs. As to whether it will cut drinking, he doubts it.

    Will it cut drink-related trouble? Luton's solution is to have town centre bar managers communicating with walkie-talkies to keep a close watch on pub-goers.

    Know your limits

    Out on the town are Louise Brown, 26, and Richard Hall, 24, who both work for a major brewery firm.

    Perhaps unsurprisingly they are glad of the extra pub time.

    "It's about choice. It's about being able to spend more time in the pub with your friends," said Ms Brown. "I don't think I'll be drinking more. I know my limits, I think a lot of people do."

    Mr Hall agrees. "We work hard, we don't finish work until 8pm, so now it will be more relaxed."
