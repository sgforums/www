    New community

    Nearly a year after the tsunami hit southern Sri Lanka, a new community has emerged out of the ruins in Palana village on the Indian Ocean coast. With the help of the Belgian government, the Solideal Southern Coast Rehabilitation Trust, a local NGO, is building 50 two-storey houses - 20 of which are already complete.

    Many families have already begun moving into the houses, while others are waiting to be handed possession in a few weeks.

    Swept away

    On 26 December 2005, the village of Palana south was swept away by the tsunami. More than 300 people lived in this settlement - 75 of them were killed. The survivors were put up in transit camps set up across the road.

    Most of the people living here work as farmhands, while others are employed in the fishing industry. Almost every family lost somebody in the tsunami. Now, nearly a year later, they are trying to rebuild their lives.

    Future plans

    Once complete, this settlement will be a big improvement over the previous one, say the residents. The plans include building a pre-school and a health clinic. There is even a plan to build a recreation hall with a library. Built along the key coastal highway, many think the future of Palana is very bright.
