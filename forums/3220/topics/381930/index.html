    Neighbour accuses me of trespass when I confront him about mess after wedding

    idThere were dirty plates left in STOMPer Maggie’s mailbox and this was after she confronted her neighbour about the mess he left behind at the block void deck.

    The STOMPer later called the police after he threatened her.

    In her email to STOMP, she recounted:

    “One of the resident held his wedding on Nov 15 at Block 12 Marsiling Lane.

    “After he left, he also left a bunch of garbage lying on the floor.

    “Also, I have found plates with food and cup in my letterbox. My letterbox is faulty, so when I swung it open, the terrible foul smell just ran into my nose.


    “My intention was to have a peaceful talk to the host of the wedding and ask them to clear up the mess since it was left over by their invitees.


    “I manage to know from one of my neighbours living in the same block that the host is from Block 10.

    “I talked to the owner but he was accusing me of blaming them and refused to clean up the mess.

    “He threatened to call the police if I didn't leave.

    “So instead of him calling the police, I made the call first.

    “Police arrived but was unable to talk sense to the man. He told the police that I had tried to trespass his property and accuse that I had pinpoint him as the culprit which is untrue.

    I had videotaped part of their conversation.

    “The police have given me an assurance that the Town council will look into the matter and ensure that cleaner will be sent to clear the rubbish.”
