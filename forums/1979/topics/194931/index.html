    Rhasta
    Range: 500 | | Move Speed: 275 | Primary: INT
    Str: 16 + 1.6 | Agi: 16 + 1.6 | Int: 21 + 3
    Damage: 42 - 49 | HP: 454 | Mana: 273

    Pros:
    1. Ranged hero; which means farming would not be as difficult as is the problem of other heroes.
    2. One of the best, if not the best disabler among all dota heroes.
    3. Strong Early and Mid Game (a con if not played properly later on).

    Cons:
    1. Very very fragile, too low hp pool.
    2. Slow movespeed, 2nd in the game after Techies.
    3. Needs practice if you want to own with this hero.

    Skill Descriptions:

    Forked Lightning - Calls forth a cone of lightning, hitting multiple enemy units for damage.

    Level 1 - 75 damage to 3 targets.
    Level 2 - 150 damage to 4 targets.
    Level 3 - 225 damage to 5 targets.
    Level 4 - 300 damage to 7 targets.
    Mana Cost: 95/ 105/ 135/ 160

    Voodoo - Transforms an enemy unit into a random critter, disabling special abilities.

    Level 1 - Lasts 1 seconds.
    Level 2 - Lasts 2 seconds.
    Level 3 - Lasts 3 seconds.
    Level 4 - Lasts 4 seconds.
    Mana Cost: 110 / 140 / 170 / 200

    Hero Shakles - Magically binds a target enemy, so that it cannot move or attack and deals 40 damage per second. Channeling spell.

    Level 1 - Lasts 2.5 seconds.
    Level 2 - Lasts 3.25 seconds.
    Level 3 - Lasts 4 seconds.
    Level 4 - Lasts 4.75 seconds.
    Mana Cost: 110/ 130/ 155/ 185

    Mass Serpent Ward - Summons 8 immobile serpentine wards to attack enemies. The wards last 30 seconds, deal piercing damage (200% damage to creeps, 50% damage to heroes and 35% damage to buildings) and are immune to magic. Each ward has a range of 600 and has 0 armor type Heavy. With Scepter Rhasta can summon 12 wards.

    Level 1 - Deals 39-43 damage, 135 hp each.
    Level 2 - Deals 54-58 damage, 150 hp each.
    Level 3 - Deals 69-73 damage, 150 hp each.
    Mana Cost: 200/ 350/ 610

    Question/Answers:

    1. Why max out Forked Lightning First?

    Forked Lightning is the only single nuke that Rhasta can make; it is essential for farming and harassing hero, hence a good finishing skill and essential in getting good items, remember in dota money is power, otherwise heroes such as KOTL and Techies would be one of the weakest.

    2. Why Shackles First before Voodoo, isnÂ’t Voodoo essential for ward trapping?

    I believe that Shackles is more powerful than Voodoo, especially early game with the 40/sec damage and the longer duration of disable. Plus, the main player in ward trapping early on is cyclone, so no problem there. I would learn 1 level of Voodoo though, in place of Shackles if there are Heroes with crazy channeling spells, like Crystal Maiden, Sand King or Enigma in my lane just to stop them from Casting their ultimates.
    Note: if you want to practice ward trapping, then you can learn voodoo first, but in competitive games, always max out shackles first before learning voodoo.

    Techniques/Strategies:

    Things to Remember During Early-Game:
    1. Get a void stone ASAP.
    2. Complete the Voidstone into a Euls.
    3. Harass your enemy lane partner with Forked Lightning. 2 to 3 forked lightning would force your enemy lane partner to go back to the fountain. Do it again and again and rely on your Voidstone/Euls for the mana regen.

    Things to Remember Durnig Mid-Game:
    1. Be the most dominating player in the game, by hero hunting and farming in every oppurtunity possible. Make the enemies know that you are to be feared. Kill those enemies who outlevel you by doing the cyclone+wards+shackles combo.
    2. You can also deal with 2 heroes at once.

    Things to Remember During Late-Game:
    1. Never ever ever go solo, always go with a team mate.
    2. Should circumstances provide you a 1 on 1 battle, then do not be afraid, you have unending disables. Thier life could only last so much...

    HOW TO USE THE "WARD TRAP"

    Now the question, just how do you encage those wards to a moving target? Answer: Practice. Just remember these three things.
    1.) The larger the collision size, the harder it is to trap him.
    2.) The nearer you are to the hero, the easier it is to trap him.
    3.) It is almost impossible to trap heroes who are in 500 range of you, especially if you did not reduce his collision size. The casting range of wards is 400 I think.

    Now there are four ways of pulling off a successful ward trap, and that is:

    1. Manual Ward Trap - Manually and without any help from any skills and items. This is almost impossible to pull of unless with luck. I usually TRY to do this early game when I have no cyclone or voodoo yet. To pull it off, click on the spot where you think the hero would be (it is easier to trap ranged hero since they donÂ’t move while attacking, and heroes that are near you, in about 300 range or less). However, most of the time the enemy wonÂ’t be trapped, but whatÂ’s important is you follow up with a shackle. So that even though they may not be encaged, they would still be shackled near the wards.

    2. Cycloned Ward Trap - This is the easiest to pull off, even noobs could do it, just cyclone the enemy and exactly on the point where he is flying cast the wards. Should some misclick or other circumstances made you miss, you could cover the opening yourself by going to that spot.

    3. Voodooed Ward Trap Â– This one is easier than Manual WT and harder than Cycloned WT. First voodoo the hero that you wish to trap (know that heroes have the smallest collision size when hexed, thatÂ’s why its easier to trap them). Should the voodooed hero be too shocked to move, then simply cast the wards at his feet. Experienced players would move in erratic paths to make your ward trapping more challenging. If this happens, try to predict its movement and cast the wards. If you miss, thereÂ’s always shackles !!

    4. The Unexpected Ward Trap Â– Lastly, this is when youÂ’ve ward trapped an enemy due to some event not intended in the normal course of using Rhasta. For example, youÂ’ve picked up an Â‘invisibilityÂ’ power up or tree ent gave you natureÂ’s guise or your allied hero just stunned the enemy and his position is saying Â“please trap meÂ” Anyway, given these unusual circumstances, make the best out of it. If you got an Â‘invisibilityÂ’ power up, take your time and cast the wards on the enemy when he is alone or when they are only 2, shackles the other one. If you see medusa running away and your allied Sven just storm bolt(ed) her near you, then do it quick.
