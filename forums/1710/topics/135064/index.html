Analysts see ComfortDelgro as better bet than SMRT

  • sbst275
    1 Jun 05, 19:26
    SINGAPORE : When news broke that both public transport operators had won an overall 2.4 percent fare hike effective July, share investors began favouring one company over the other.

    The Public Transport Council had said the fare increase would raise revenue for each operator by S$13 million to S$14 million.

    As a result, SMRT shares surged 6.8 percent on Monday, but gave up 2.7 percent on Tuesday.

    In contrast, Comfort Delgro was more muted, gaining 0.6 percent yesterday, but giving it all back today.

    Still, analysts believe it is Comfort Delgro that is headed for better times, and they believe SMRT will be left behind instead.

    Analysts say SMRT's 6.8 percent share surge on Monday was overdone, and smacks of a knee-jerk reaction.

    OCBC Research went so far as to downgrade SMRT from "buy" to "hold".

    OCBC says the stock has already risen 20 percent in one month, and at Tuesday's S$1.07, is near its fair value of S$1.13.

    NetResearch Asia believes SMRT has already overshot its fair value of just 98 cents.

    Said Kevin Scully, managing director of NetResearch Asia, "SMRT is probably fairly valued already. My fair value is 98 cents. It's currently about 8 to 10 percent overvalued. And given that interest rates are rising, and it's a yield play, you will probably see it move closer to the one dollar level."

    Said Gabriel Yap, senior dealing director at Phillip Securities, "For SMRT, my fair target is S$1.18. That is based on the earnings potential for this current year, as well as the price to book valuation."

    Analysts favour Comfort Delgro for its more diversified earnings base -- it has its taxi business, owns 70 percent of bus operator SBS Transit, and operates the Metroline in the UK, with businesses in China and Vietnam.

    Brokerage house Kim Eng is also keeping a "buy" on ComfortDelgro, with a fair value of S$2.18.

    It says its group dividend is also attractive at 5.3 percent.

    Kim Eng believes the impact of fare hike is unlikely to be significant to ComfortDelgro's group earnings in fiscal year 2005. - CNA /ct
