    Why cap prize money for SEA Games gold medallists?

    I am puzzled by the incentives given to gold medallists at the recent SEA Games. Why put a cap on the prize money for individual golds? Take swimmer Joscelin Yeo as an example. After winning six gold medals - five individual and one team event - she can claim the prize money for only three individual medals, with the cash award for the second and third medals being less than that of the first.

    This is really a negative incentive for any sportsman to give his best after winning a third individual gold. These athletes should be rewarded handsomely, considering the time and hard work they have put in. How many more medals can they get once age catches up with them? It is no wonder that parents are not encouraging their children to become professional sportsmen.

    Spare a thought for the athletes. The money they get from winning could be their retirement funds after their sporting career is over. Can the authorities be more generous?

    Brian Koh Chin Mong
