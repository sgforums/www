    Local woman dies after giving birth to twins

    A 44-year-old woman has died two hours after giving birth to a pair of twins at Raffles Hospital.

    The deceased, Sun Li Juan, was admitted to the hospital last Thursday for bleeding as a result of a low-lying placenta.

    In a written response, Raffles Hospital told 938LIVE that Madam Sun continued to bleed while in the ward.

    As there was a continuing threat of premature labour and in the interest of the twinsÂ’ safety, a Caesarean section operation was carried out on Saturday.

    The hospital added that during surgery, massive bleeding was encountered and emergency blood transfusion was immediately started.

    Relatives of Madam Sun were also requested to assist in replenishing the blood supply stocks, a customary procedure according to the hospital.

    Madam Sun eventually died after she developed an acute blood coagulation problem arising from massive bleeding and transfusion.

    But in comments made to MediaCorp News, Madam SunÂ’s family had reportedly queried if the hospital had enough blood supply.

    Her husband was quoted as saying that the hospital told him it would need to seek approval from higher management if more blood was needed.

    He then questioned if the saving of lives actually needed approval.

    Some 200 relatives and friends had been mobilized by the family to donate their blood to Madam Sun.
