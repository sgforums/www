Take public transport during countdown festivities

    Expect large crowds and heavy traffic around the Marina Bay and Esplanade areas on New Year's Eve for the Marina Bay Singapore Countdown.

    Police are advising spectators to make use of the public transport system that day.

    938LIVE understands that public transport operating hours will also be extended till 2 in the morning.

    For safety reasons, auxiliary police officers will be regulating the number of persons into Esplanade Bridge, Esplanade Waterfront Promenade and Merlion Park.

    The footway along Benjamin Sheares Bridge will also be closed to the public from 6 PM on 31st December to 1 AM on 1st January due to possible concerns of overcrowding.

    Deputy Assistant Commissioner of Police, Lau Peet Meng with this advisory.

    "We have worked with the organizers for a smooth flow of people and traffic. Nonetheless, we will expect some congestion and delay. We advice the public going down for the events to be patient and calm, and to follow the signs and directions that we put up. Please look for directions given by the marshals and the police officers at the scene. For those who are driving, we ask the motorists to not stop when the fire works start as this will pose a danger to the motorists behind them. Police will not hesitate to take action against their vehicles on the road to watch the fireworks display, causing obstruction to other drivers."

    For those of you who want to avoid the crowds, there are seven recommended free public viewing areas to catch the fireworks displays.

    These include the Singapore Flyer, Esplanade Waterfront, Merlion Park and One Fullerton.

    For more information on the road closures around the Marina area on New Year's Eve, please visit the website www.marinabaycountdown.sg
