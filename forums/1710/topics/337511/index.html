    AVA to test Crystal Geyser spring water after Japanese recall

    SINGAPORE: The Agri-Food & Veterinary Authority (AVA) will be testing American bottled mineral water "Crystal Geyser" for pesticide residue as a precautionary measure.

    This followed reports out of Japan that a company had recalled eight million bottles of Crystal Geyser after consumers complained that it smelled like insecticide and medicine.

    A company spokesman from Otsuka Beverage Co Ltd was reported as saying that no abnormalities were found in the water after the company received 75 complaints about its smell.

    AVA added that Crystal Geyser water is imported from the US only by United Airlines for their use in flights and 30.6 tons have been brought in so far this year.

    It is otherwise not available for sale locally. - CNA/vm
