Cedric Foo quits Minister of State post

  • sbst275
    23 Apr 05, 20:44
    SINGAPORE: Cedric Foo will relinquish his post as Minister of State for Defence and National Development at the end of the month.

    He has requested to return to the private sector and Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong has agreed to his request.

    Mr Foo will remain as Member of Parliament for West Coast GRC and Chairman of SPRING Singapore.

    Mr Heng Chee How, Minister of State for Trade and Industry, will be concurrently appointed as Minister of State for National Development from May, while continuing as Central CDC Mayor.

    In a statement, the Prime Minister thanked Mr Foo for his contributions as a Minister of State over the last three years.

    The statement from the Prime Minister's Office said that while in the Defence Ministry, Mr Foo had led the productivity and innovation movement and paid special attention to the career needs of SAF Warrant Officers and Specialists.

    As the president of SAFRA and chairman of ACCORD, Mr Foo had worked closely with NSmen and their employers, community groups and the public to strengthen commitment to Total Defence.

    The statement added that while in the National Development Ministry, Mr Foo had co-ordinated the work of the Town Councils to ensure that HDB estates were well managed and maintained, and developed the programme to restructure HDB retail shops.

    Mr Foo also helped to revamp and grow the construction industry, and to promote the building and consultancy expertise overseas.

    Mr Foo joined politics during the last general election in 2001.

    He rose up the ranks and was appointed Minister of State three years ago, at the same time as other new MPs like Dr Ng Eng Hen, Dr Vivian Balakrishnan and Mr Raymond Lim. - CNA/ir
