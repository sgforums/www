Sentosa Monorail to be replaced by express trains

  • sbst275
    12 Mar 05, 07:50
    SINGAPORE : The 23-year-old Sentosa Monorail is going to make its last journey next Tuesday.

    In its place will be a S$140 million express train project, which will connect Sentosa to the Harbour Front MRT station within 6 minutes.

    Rosminah Aziz says the monorail is her second home.

    She has been driving it since the very first day of its operations and she says she will miss the experience dearly.

    She said, "The best part in my life....when the guest come back one year later and they look(ed) for me and they still remember me....I feel very sad you know, three-quarters of my life I have been driving the monorail...I'm meeting a lot of people."

    In its place - a state of the art express train system which is Sentosa's biggest infrastructure investment since 1972.

    The air-conditioned train will depart from Harbour Front MRT station every three minutes.

    Its first stop will be Sentosa Station, where passengers can connect to a network of buses.

    The next stop will be the Merlion Station, next to the Merlion Statue.

    And finally - Palawan Station - which will stand next to the beach.

    And it takes just six minutes for the 4.3 kilometre journey.

    Construction is already 70 percent complete.

    The Sentosa Express will start running in November next year and will be able to carry up to 8,000 passengers an hour.

    In the meantime, Sentosa will have added bus services to cater to visitors.

    The island is currently undergoing a S$3 billion rejuvenation project to update its appeal.

    The island hopes to attract 8 million visitors annually by 2012. - CNA
