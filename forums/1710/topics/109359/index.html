    Singapore feels tremors from massive Indonesian earthquake

    SINGAPORE: Tremors were felt in parts of Singapore early Sunday from a massive earthquake that struck Indonesia's Sumatra island, the local meteorological services division said in a statement.

    The tremors were "mild" and caused no damage or casualties, an official told AFP.

    Tremors were felt in Toa Payoh, Clementi, Beach Road, Siglap, Meyer Road and Tanjong Rhu.

    Police said they received about 13 calls from these areas, but no accidents were reported.

    The National Environment Agency confirmed the tremors were caused by an earthquake that occurred off the west coast of northern Sumatra, about 950 kilometres from Singapore.

    Indonesian officials measured the quake at 6.4 on the Richter scale, while the US Geological Survey said it registered 8.9. - AFP
