    Indonesian maid, toddler found dead in Tiong Bahru

    SINGAPORE : Two loud thuds and screams shook a quiet neighbourhood near Tiong Bahru on Saturday.

    Residents looked out of their windows to find two bodies at the foot of an HDB block at Kim Tian Place.

    It was a grim sight for many residents who gathered near Block 120 Kim Tian Place after they heard a commotion between 2.30pm and 3pm.

    They saw the body of a 21-year-old Indonesian woman among the bushes near the block and a dead five-month-old boy just metres away.

    They are believed to have fallen from the 23rd floor of the block, where the baby's grandparents lived.

    A neighbour said: "We heard two loud screams and two loud thuds...my husband went out to look, went to the window, we tried to look, we thought it was a car accident."

    Another neighbour said: "From (the) balcony, I heard one loud thud followed by another. I looked down and did not see anything. Later I saw a middle-aged man crying loudly."

    Investigations revealed that the Indonesian woman is a maid employed by the baby's parents.

    The five-month-old boy dressed in a white shirt and pink pants was pronounced dead at 3.15pm, while the Indonesian maid dressed in dark brown t-shirt and checked bermudas died 10 minutes later.
