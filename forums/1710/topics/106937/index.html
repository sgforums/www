Salons-filled Far East Plaza the place for haircut

  • sbst275
    12 Dec 04, 20:16
    SINGAPORE: It's quite possibly Singapore's hairest mall!

    With a staggering 60 salons, Far East Plaza is fast becoming the place for a haircut.

    There are over 100 hair stylists and thousands of styles, techniques and colours to choose from.

    Prices for a snip and style start from as low as $15 and reach to over $100.

    Laurence Lee, creative director of salon It's Hairy, said: "I've been here for about nine years. There are more salons right now catering to street style fashion. So this is a place I think people are turning to to look for something that's up and trendy."

    One customer who goes to Far East Plaza for a haircut is 20-year-old Thipa.

    She's got a spunky new hair style, with highlights to match - a pretty radical makeover for Thipa.

    "It has the reputation for being a cool place to cut hair. I don't know, I hope my mother doesn't scold me because she didn't want me to cut my hair short!" she said.

    "Got a lot of fashion," said another customer on why she chose to go to Far East Plaza for her haircut.

    "It's cheaper here and more trendy," said a third.

    "Quite 'in' actually," said a fourth.

    "Younger, funkier, they follow the Hong Kong and Japanese styles, so they're more fashionable," said a fifth.

    The place is not only for the young and hip, but also for many working adults, like 35-year-old Patricia Chng who paid about $200 for a state-of-the-art ceramic perm.

    With a growing number of salons at Far East Plaza, competition is keen and many are competing on price and service.

    Kenneth Chan, creative director of Klearcut Hairdressing, said: "The competition here is quite tough. Most of the new hair salons couldn't make it after one or 2 years, so they close down." - CNA
