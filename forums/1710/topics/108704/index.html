Police looking for 9-year-old girl who went missing in the P

    SINGAPORE : Police are searching for a missing nine-year-old Filipino girl, Kaye Charish Mae Patino, who was last seen in her home at Block 119, Potong Pasir Avenue 1 Wednesday morning.

    They are also appealing to the public to help find her.

    Kaye is the third in a family of five and studies at Beng Wan Primary.

    According to her mother, Kaye would normally watch television in the morning after she gets up.

    But lately she told her mother that some men had been talking to her.

    Conchita Patino, Kaye's mother said: "My daughter told me that one Indian man was calling out at her. So I asked my daughter why they were calling her. She said he was just calling out for her. And she'll run up and tell me about it. Then there's a Chinese man who's been coming to sell something for three days now. I told them we are not interested, but he is still coming here."

    Police say efforts by their officers and the girl's guardians to locate her have so far been unsuccessful.

    Police are continuing with intensive searches and men from the Special Operations Command have also been deployed, combing some construction sites in the Potong Pasir vicinity.

    Valentino Patino, the girl's father is appealing to the public for help.

    "Whoever saw my daughter please contact us, give us a call or tell to the police," said Mr Patino

    Police say Kaye is 1.2 metres tall with shoulder length black hair.

    She was last wearing a red t-shirt and blue shorts.

    Anyone who knows her whereabouts can call the police hotline 1-800-255 0000.

    - CNA
