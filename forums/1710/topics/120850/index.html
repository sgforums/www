    SINGAPORE : The Workers' Party wants the casino decision to be put off till the next General Election - so that people can decide on the issue at the ballot box.

    Its Secretary-General Low Thia Khiang, who is also MP for Hougang, said this at a public consultation exercise on Saturday.

    He said the decision to open a casino would have implications for society as a whole, and so a wider consensus is needed.

    While the Workers' Party has yet to decide on its stand, Mr Low said he is against the casino idea.

    Many views were heard during the two-hour long forum - which discussed the economic impact and social costs of having a casino. - CNA
