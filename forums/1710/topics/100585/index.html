    SINGAPORE : 22-year-old vegetable seller Took Leng How has been charged with the murder of 8-year-old Huang Na on Monday.

    He was accused of causing her death on 10th October between 1.40pm and 10 pm at Block 15 of the Pasir Panjang Wholesale Centre.

    The Malaysian, clad in a red t-shirt and dark shorts, was calm as his case came up for mention in the Subordinate Courts this morning.

    He's being represented by lawyer Chung Ping Shen, who was hired by his family members late yesterday.

    His parents are expected to come to Singapore over the next few days.

    Also in court today was a visibly upset Huang Na's mother, Madam Huang Shuying.

    She was accompanied by 2 friends.

    Took, who fled the country on 21st October, surrendered to Malaysian police on Saturday and brought back to Singapore the same night.

    Just hours after being questioned by Singapore police, the badly decomposed body of a girl, believed to that of Huang Na's, was found stuffed in a box, in thick undergrowth at Telok Blangah Hill Park.

    Huang Na, a China national, had been reported missing since 10th October. - CNA

    Crowd packs courtroom for glimpse of accused in Huang Na murder case

    SINGAPORE : About a hundred people turned up at the Subordinate Courts on Monday to catch a glimpse of Took Leng How, the man who has been charged with the murder of 8-year-old girl, Huang Na. But many were turned away, as the court was packed.

    Took made a brief three-minute appearance in the dock, wearing the same red polo shirt, dark blue bermudas and slippers that he did last week.

    The Court heard that investigations were not complete yet and the accused was soon led back into police custody.

    That means even Took's lawyer was not able to meet his client to take instructions. Those who didn't make it into the courtroom, however, managed to get a view of Took's parents, who came from Penang.

    They left the court straight after they heard that their son's case would be mentioned again this Friday. Took's father had earlier told Malaysian press that his son was innocent, or he would not have turned himself in to the police. - CNA
