Genting International to launch IPO on Friday: sources

  • sbst275
    1 Dec 05, 20:06
    Gaming operator, Genting International is expected to launch its initial public offer on Friday.

    According to sources familiar with the deal, Genting will be offering some 1 billion new shares at 35 Singapore cents each, up from 700 million shares estimated earlier.

    The new shares will make up about 21 percent of the company's enlarged capital.

    Some S$350 million will be raised from the IPO.

    The share sale is part of Genting's move to switch its primary listing to the Singapore Exchange from Luxembourg.

    Potential investors tell Channel NewsAsia the institutional book for Genting is at least 7 times subscribed.

    Several strategic investors are believed to have been roped in.

    Temasek Holdings is reported to be one of them but the investment company declined comment.

    Genting International owns the UK's Maxim's Casino Club.

    It also has a 20 percent stake in UK-listed Stanley Leisure, with which it also has a joint venture to develop casinos in Britain.

    Genting also owns 29 percent of betting clubs operator London Clubs International

    Genting is in the running for the integrated resorts project in Singapore.

    It is also looking for opportunities in Macau and elsewhere in the region.

    Trading in the counter is expected to begin on the SGX mainboard on December 12. - CNA/ch
