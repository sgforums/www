    DPP insists it will contest MacPherson

    SINGAPORE : The Democratic Progressive Party has insisted that it will contest the single-seat constituency of MacPherson, which has also been staked by the Singapore Democratic Alliance.

    In an e-mail to the media late Friday night, DPP Chairman Mansor Rahman pointed out that the DPP has given way to the Workers' Party, which wants to contest in the East Coast GRC.

    He added that the SDA itself has won agreement to contest three other single seats -- Chua Chu Kang, Potong Pasir and Yio Chu Kang.

    As such, MacPherson is the only seat the DPP is contesting.

    The opposition parties met on Friday night to work out an electoral pact, which however left the issue of MacPherson unresolved.

    After the meeting, SDA Chairman Chiam See Tong told the media he believed the issue could still be settled amicably.

    However Mr Mansor Rahman's e-mail left no doubt that as far as the DPP was concerned, it was contesting MacPherson -- whatever the SDA might say at subsequent meetings.

    In past General Elections, opposition candidates in three or four-cornered fights generally lost their election deposits.

    In the coming elections, the election deposit has been set at S$13,500. - CNA /ct
