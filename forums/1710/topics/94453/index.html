    SINGAPORE : The first bus of SMRT's new service number 950 to Johor Baru, Malaysia started on Sunday, running from the Woodlands Regional Interchange to JB's Kotaraya II Terminal.

    The service also marks the end of the monopoly held by rival operator SBS Transit, which runs service numbers 170 and 160 to Johor Bahru.

    Advertisement

    In the past, Woodlands residents headed for JB needed to take a shuttle service, followed by a short walk before getting to the Woodlands Checkpoint.

    But now service 950 delivers them straight to the Checkpoint within 15 minutes.

    And residents say it is not only more convenient, but also saves them half the bus fare.

    There was even a surprise souvenir gift thrown in for the maiden trip's first passenger.

    Bus 950 will make about 100 trips every day, and SMRT expects the daily passenger load to grow beyond 2,000.

    "There are enough passengers for all this services, and we provide a convenience for our passengers, especially those in the north and northeast," said Tan Hup Foi, deputy CEO of SMRT. - CNA
