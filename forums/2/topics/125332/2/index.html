    The factory dump valve or compressor bypass valve minimizes compressor surge as you described. A BOV is different in that instead of recirculating the air back into the intake, it is vented to the atmosphere. Neither system has any anti-lag properties by themselves.

    For a car running on any fuel metering system other than MAF, running a BOV is not a problem. On a MAF-system such as the Evo however, you will get into a momentary rich condition since the air which has been vented is no longer in the same proportion as the injected fuel.
