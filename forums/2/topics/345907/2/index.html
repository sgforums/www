    You can switch from gear four to gear two, bypassing the gear three, if you see that the corner you are taking is very tight.

    From there, you will feel the car engine or your car lurching which means the engine is trying to slow the car down.  You can also try to help the engine by braking lightly.

    Further my first post:

    I used to have to drive different cars and get use to them on really short notice,  Like 5 minutes checking the car I am assigned on to the day, and 15 minutes driving it to make sure I can adjust to the car and that its got no problems.

    My bad habit, was to leave my left hand on the gear knob, or resting on it.  With only my right hand on the steering wheel.  And after some time, you will know the position of the gear stick and dont have to worry about where it is.

    And with your focus on the road, you will find that you can gauge what speed you are going and roughly what gear depending on how smooth the engine sounds to you.

    So you dont have to have your hands on the gear sticks anymore.
