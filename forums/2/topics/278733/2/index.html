    Jap car.. would be good if u have a clearer target of what car u might wan, but i'll try to give u an idea of the costs.

    Insurance, depends on your age, gender and if u have owned a car b4. My guess is your insurance will be about 2 grand.

    Petrol, dependent on how much you drive and how you drive and price of petrol itself. My guess, about $300 a month

    Assuming price of car is 60 grand, a 10 year loan will be about 600/mth, and about 900/mth for a 7 year loan. Interest levels will adjust that figure a lil bit.

    Typical service intervals for jap cars are 1k, 10k, 20k etc etc. Major service would be 40k, 80k, 120k, if you drive that long. Servicing costs would also vary, but would be on an upward slope the longer you drive. It would also differ, when you choose to service @ the AD or an external workshop. 1k, 10k, 20k, should cost you in the region of $250 each.

    Air filters costs a few dollars from stockists. Fuel filters cost slightly more.

    Tyres, I prefer to change them @ 40k or 2 yrs, whichever comes first. Costs of tyres would depend on where you get them, the size, and which brand and range you get. Do not cut corners on tyres tho. They are the only things that prevent you from meeting the hard concrete barriers when u corner or another car's backside when you E-brake. 15 inch rubbers should cost you about 100/pc and upwards.

    The above is by no means exhaustive, but I'm tired and kinda brain dead so it's as far as I can contribute for now. When you have a better idea of your finances and which car your interested in, come back, tell us, and more information maybe come forward.

    Enjoy shopping!
