    Swenn, I suppose the problem lies with the instructor more then it lies with you. Driving is something that takes a lot of practice, time and experience to get used to. It is very normal for a new driver to feel confuse and loss when trying manipulate something he/she is not familiar. Situation will be even worse if there is person beside you keep scolding you. The more you get scolded, the more you will be de-moralized, and the worse you will perform. It you are taking a driving course from driving school, suggest that you check with the clerk if you can change to another more patient instructor. If you are learning from some private instructor, fire him and get another one. As a general guide for novice driver, try to adapt the following habits when making a turn.

    1) Always check your blind spot before you turn. Look out for
    motorcyclist.

    2) Try to turn slowly and adjust your wheel accordingly. If there's a
    vehicle infront of you, try to stay inline with that car and position
    your car with it.

    3) For now, do not change gear when you turn. Change it only
    after you finished the turn.

    4) Release the wheel gently. The car will self centre by itself.

    Hope this helps.

    car drifting to the right could be due to u taking ur eyes off the road for too long. maybe due to u checking the mirrors for too long, or looking down while changing gear, so watch it. don't keep ur eyes off-road for more than 2 secs.

    one way to counter wide turning is to turn ur car to the curvature of the kerb. once u see ur car drifting too far off from the curve then u steer in more(gauge yourself a clearance to prevent rear wheel from kissing kerb). but of course be prepared to return in time as the path straightens. for right-turn at junctions, try to start ur turning pt earlier, then keep as close as possible to the centre line.

    for the clutch, release it slowly to the biting pt and Hold it there for a Sec before releasing fully. this should prevent the jerk caused by clutch throwing. haha the accelerator is the most sensitive pedal, so u gotta be gentle on it. learn to balance the 2 flank pedals, ur gear changing will improve surely.

    these are problems we all learners meet, just have to analyse and piece up the solutions, surely can fine tune wan... performance will improve with every lesson, as experience builds up...

    be proactive! cheers~
