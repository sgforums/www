    larger capacity does not always equate higher fc.

    think of an engine as a muscle, and fuel as nutrients/chemicals which moves that muscle.

    compare a big muscle (bigger cc) and small muscle (smaller cc), and assume that each is as efficient in converting energy per unit size. a bigger muscle COULD move a bigger load and consume MORE nutrients at the same time. likewise, a smaller muscle COULD move a smaller load and consume LESS energy in doing so. on the other hand, it is also possible for a big muscle to move a small load and consume less energy compared with a small muscle which has to haul a huge load.

    but now, if i had the same big muscle which is injured, and is not able to convert energy as efficiently as it would (as in the case of a poorly designed/ poorly conditioned engine). now, it would consume more energy to move the same load compared to a perfectly healthy smaller muscle (as in the case of engines equipped with vvti, vtc, vvtl-i, vtec, i-vtec etc) .

    it is therefore an inconclusive statement 2 say that bigger cc equates higher fc.
