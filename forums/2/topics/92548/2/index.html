    In my opinion, owning, driving, and maintaining any kind of vehicle in Singapore is a costly undertaking. But those of us where owning a vehicle is a necessity because of business or work, this predicament is inevitable.

    I drive a commercial van, and it being a van, its body is ultra thin. Like a soft-shell crab. As such, parking in public places is hazardous. Repairing any kind of damage however superficial will run into the hundreds - regardless of the type of vehicle you own. Hence, my painstaking Standard in choosing a parking lot is as follows:

    Standing Policies that Greatly Reduces Risk of Scratch or Superficial Damage

    >> Always keep a disposable camera or digital camera in vehicle
    >> Park in bright of well lit areas ( corner lots ) and or near heavy human traffic
    >> DO Not park under any tree ( except young and short ones )
    >> DO NOT park over or near dried grass or shrubs
    >> Always in corner lots, and 1 inch away from the kerb and 3 or more feet away from the next empty lot or parked vehicle
    >> Never park next to another comercial vehicle, or a group of motorcycles
    >> Never park at or near carpark entrances or exits
    >> Where there are no corner lots in MSCP, use only lots next to a pillar
    >> Never park under HDB blocks
    >> Never park outside schools or school's basketball or sport fields
    >> Never park near trolly bays in shopping centers
    >> When parallel parking, park in last lot with kerb behind with rear wheels 1 feet away from kerb
    >> Never park in secluded carparks, or empty level of MSCP
    >> Park away from garbage or combustible garbage nearby
    >> Never park in a lot, where you regularly see a vehicle parked in
    >> Never park next to a vehicle with a "Just Passed" triangle sign displayed

    Following the above rules/Policies ensures you need not have to spend money on incidences to your vehicle.

    Disclaimer:
    While the above standing Policies I follow ensures that all vehicles I owned were scratch-free since 1992, I shall not be held liable for any misfortune befalls anyone who adopts and follows these Policies.
