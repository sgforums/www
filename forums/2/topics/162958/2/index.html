    COE tingy is a very complex issue.
    Firstly, when car dealers post their price for a car, they would have a rough indication how the coe trend over the next few bidding would be like. They can based it on the volume of booking in the auto industry. 1 bid,3bids, 4bids are all gimmicks to entice buyers to pay more if they want the car earlier. So if coe is at all time low, car price are fixed then to cater for a resurge in the COE over the next few months.

    Secondly, COE bidding is done usually by the sales manager with no inputs from SE. He/she will bid based on the stock situation and colour availability so that his port charges will not be excessive. i.e first in first out basis.

    Thirdly, top up is all a ruse or marketing strategy for the weak. If you stay firm you will eventually get the car at the price you have decided on.

    For the case of the new sunny model coming next year, it will most likely fall into cat B as the japan model is also 1.8l and 2 l similar to the new civic. The car is substantially longer about 4.6m whereas new civic is about 4.5m. But in terms of look the new sunny loses out to the civic, it looks like a scaled down cefiro.

    My views only
