    The Jeep you're referring is the one by Chrysler? The showroom is at Leng Kee Road, behind the Hyundai showroom. Or if you're referring to those 4WD vehicles, which there are some pick-ups which also has off-roading capabilities. I think Ford and Isuzu have those 4WD pick-ups. Not forgetting Land Rover as well.

    There should be some off-roading clubs out there that occasionally hold expeditions up north. Maybe just do some searching on the net.

    Not sure what catagory jeeps fall under. There are some registered as private passenger car, while others as commerical vehicle with the "G" plate.

    Generally it is never advisable to speed in a jeep. The tyres are not meant for high speed travelling and if you can recall some years back, Firestone had to recall their All Terrain tyre series because of reported tyre blowouts while travelling on the highway.

    Fuel consumption is of course dependant on your driving style. If you buy a Jeep Wrangler with a V8 engine and always show off its grunty exhaust note at traffic lights... you get the idea.

    If i were to get a SUV to go off-roading, i'll take the Toyota Fortuner. So bloody high, can clear almost anything.
