    9 Oct 04, 17:53
    Hello Jadedguy,

    This is the 3rd time I'm offering my advice to pple interested in COE-ed ESi. Same advise. Dun buy. My previous experience with an 8 year old ESi was nightmare. Don't follow my footsteps by buying an even older ESi. Overheats easily and best thing is you cannot spot this problem easily during test drive. A thorough wash of the radiator can suppress the overheat problem for up to 3 months so even if the dealer gives you 2 week warranty after sale, you won't experience the problem so soon... But when it comes, you are stucked in a situation which you faced selling the car at almost no value due to the current sitaution of the 2nd hand car market. Heed the advise of the guys, go for younger Civics if you must really own a Civic. And yes, you can definitely find a car that suits your taste with the depressed 2nd hand car market now.

    All said, I must still give credits to the ESi. Powerful, spacious and quite good FC despite its power. I enjoyed the power as much as I dreaded the experience of seeing the temp needle shoot up while cruising. If the dealer says no to test driving on highways then really no point buying. Surely something is wrong and they are trying to hide it. Since your main aim is to get from point A to point B just for classes, get a more decent car that suits your budget in the long run. If you are able to provide the 80% of the cost, I don't see why you can't pay the showroom a visit...

    As a new driver you got to balance between paying more for the insurance for a new car or paying more for the road tax for a COE-ed car. That's why we recommend you go for "middle-aged" car of 4~5 years origin. Not so siong on road tax and insurance; kinda like a compromise between 2 extremes. Styling may be important but ultimately, proper budgeting and not burning a hole in the pocket should be the final factor in your decision.
