    Clutch slip not necessary at high speed of the car.. but rather high speed in the RPM. Just imagine... 7000 rpm, how fast is it rotating.

    Normally from 7000rpm, increase 1 gear up will drop it to around 4.5 to 5k rpm.
    When rotating at such a high speed, sometimes normal clutch will miss or slip due to insufficient grip.

    Racing clutch or dry clutch, reduces the possibilites not 100 % maybe 99 %.

    If you are a normal driver.. no point chaning it to dry clutch... unless you always chiong and see the redline on the rpm.
