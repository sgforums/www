    is there any proof or write up that states u should always put ur transmission into neutral so as not to overwork the engine?

    there are merits to both practices i presume.

    constant engaging and disengaging between "D" and "N" causes the torque convertor additional stress because of the fact that it runs on a fluid coupling effect. ie; the harder u step on the throttle, the more fluid gets forced into it and the more forward motion it generates.

    at idle, the amount of thrust generated by the torque convertor as a result of the engine's speed is minimal but optimal for move off. if u engage "N" at this point, the pressure build up in the torque convertor is relieved. when its time to move off, and u reengage "D", the pressure will take time to build up to turn the convertor again to generate the same forward force.

    now, u dun need a phd to understand that this constant changes in pressure using the fluid coupling effect and the effective fluctuations in the rotational speeds of the mechanical parts will result in pre mature wear and tear.

    likewise, one can argue that the engine will be "stressed" by constantly working to provide this pressure build up to keep the torque convertor under such conditions. but IMO, if its anything thats the weaker link here, it's gotta be the automatic gearbox.

    i've seen more breakdowns in auto gearboxes but hardly in any of the engines running them. which goes to show how well informed some drivers of automatic transmission are in this aspect of care for their gearboxes.

    what is "N" for exactly? i can only think of one logical question to ask in answer to that. try pushing ur car in "P" when it breaks down.
