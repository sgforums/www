    As a habitual practice, I always look for and choose to park in corner lots - parallel or reverse.

    For parallel parking lots, I look for the last one that is either with a curb behind, or a wall or pillar behind in a basement carpark or MSCP. I will park and leave about 3 to 4 feet space behind, so that if someone parks really close to me in front, I can stiill work my way out later. Always make room for yourself to leave later.

    In reverse parking lots, I park as close to the curb or wall as possible, but just enough for me to get out if the wall is on the driver side. Creating a lot of space from the lot next to you, makes provision for people who swing their doors open hard, thus causing a dimple of the side of your car. It sucks. But there are people out there like that.

    I once experienced a Merc parked next to me when I just got out of my car. As I was about to leave, when the rear passenger door swung open and banged against the side of my car. The driver looked at me apologetically smiling and apologizing, saying that they are children, and it didn't make any dents.     I didn't kick up a fuss, I inspected my car, then walked away without smiling nor saying a word. The family was Malay, the driver was a man, probably the father. I could hear him scolding the children as I walked away.
