    Original tyres are 155/** R13.

    I think its better to increase size of rims but keep the overall wheel diameter same right? Meaning go up one inch on rims but get 1 inch thinner tyres.
    Picanto's original tyres are 155/70 R13.
    70 being the profile height in percentage.
    The overall tyre diameter is 547mm (21.54"inches)

    If you are planning to upsize the rim (in this case, to 14"inches), the ideal new tyre diameter should be the same or not more than 2.5% of difference (recommended to ensure ride height and gearing ratio)

    You can try 165/60 R14 (KIA recommended)
    Overall Tyre Diameter for this configuration is 554mm (21.80"inches)
    Difference of only 7mm (1.28%)

    My current configuration is 175/60 R14
    Overall Tyre Diameter for this configuration is 566mm (22.27"inches)
    Difference of 19mm (3.47%)
    Advantage: Stable at high speed (over 100km/h), stabler cornering, grip'y feel of car.
    Disadvantage: Higher fuel consumption, sluggish pickup
    FC: By how much, in my case, around 20km as the fuel gauge touches the half tank mark.
    Sluggish: I don't really feel that, and if it does... does not bother me.

    I got the 175 instead of the 165 due to safely reasons... it's heavier, however it's definitely more gripping on the road, and read reviews that it's a good wet tyre too. By the way... grip means noisy.

    By the way, the brand and model of my tyres is:
    Yokohama A539 (cost $360 + GST)

    Hope this helps
    Vincent

    Just a bit of an info here to share:

    For those of you who had gotten your COE. Well, if you wish to cancel the sale, you can't. Legally, C&C will be able to sue you for default of the sales contract. Even if they don't, besides forfeiting your deposit, you will also need to buy over the secured COE that is already in your name, becos it is not transferable, in the exact amount it was bidded for, in cash. I think this is partly because C&C has already paid the S$10,000 deposit for the COE bidding on your behalf.

    Anyway, this is what I understand from my SA. Don't know how much to believe. But a thought did occur to me. It might be better for those who need the car on time to get a written agreement that the car will be delivered on the exact date or earlier, or else you will have the option to cancel the deal if they failed to deliver on time. This might be a way to protect your interest.
