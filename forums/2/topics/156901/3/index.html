  • ditzy
    14 Oct 05, 02:37
    Use super soft in the front, and medium at the back. Gear ratios tune to power at low revs. Brake bias max at back for oversteer and 0 at the front. Camber angle set to 3 degs max front and 2 at the back. Ride height all the way down. TCS and ASM all off or you can forget about ever oversteering. The r34 is nicely set AWD with 50/50 power distribution to front and rear wheels so no need to bother. One more thing, best is the car don't have more than 500bhp. WRXs are more tricky to set. How the car will drift you have to experiment till you find the comfort zone. I had to play around alot with the settings till I could find my comfort zone. Now I can drift just by jamming the brakes and tapping the throttle. I using GT3 and mountain circuit mostly.

    Originally posted by ceecookie:
    u know hw to tune for NFSU 2?
    Just turn off the traction control. Tune the settings like how I did for GT3 above. Maybe it'll work. But I know there is one setting that is not available in NFSU 2. You cannot specify the amount of torque going to the front or rear wheels, because in GT3, the AWDs drift better. To get into a drift, you want to lose traction at the back and usually a FR is a natural at it, and in a drift, you need power to the front for full control. That's why the high grip tyres and best a 40/60 power distribution front and back respectively. And above all, practice until you find something ideal to use.
