    you are right to say tt new car prices is "low" as compared with 2nd hand... especially so now with the 100% financing scheme.

    given a choice btw a new and 2nd hand car, wat would you choose?
    Your choice would most likely be a brand new car, the same choice as other singaporeans facing this question.

    resale market also depend greatly on many other factors, so if you really wanna calculate how much you can get for your car after you have "loose interest" in it, then why not look at the scrap value. That would be the lowest price tt you will get for your car...

    Thus assuming you get the Saab 9-3 with an OMV of 30k and COE of 27k, after 3 years, the scrap value of the Saab would work out to be 48k. For a price of 130k now and its worth of 48k in 3 yrs, the difference of 82k means the car cost you around 27.3k, not taking into consideration your car loan and interest payable.

    consider it from this angle and see if you think its worth it....
