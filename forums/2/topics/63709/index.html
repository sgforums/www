    Used to drive my dad's second hand proton saga, then drive a 2nd hand 96 model Mit Lancer , and currently driving a new lancer bought last year.
    Having driven 2nd hands cars before, i am pretty clear in my mind that i would want to drive a brand new car. you never know what happened to the car previously when you buy second hand .... the dealer will tell you "oh the owner wants to upgrade" or wants to leave the country, but they will never tell you that the previous owner had crashed the car and quickly want to dispose of it to innocent first time buyers...sad, but true fact of life here.


    Can let you know my honest opinion about lancer since i am driving one. Essentially, no problems is getting you from point A to point B. Cabin is relatively spacious ( think more voluminous than Vios or City) for its class. The engine for my model is GDI and is quite fuel economical ...can hit about 12.8 km/L. However think the toyota corolla 1.5L VVTI engines can hit about 14-15 km/L ! I confirmed with at least two frends who are driving them.

    The continuos variable transmission CVT results in a really smooth transmission but it takes away the power during acceleration from halt.
    The cabin noise is definitely much reduced, but i think the corolla is better noise insulated than lancer...had drove someone else's corolla ...really quieter.

    In any way, i have not regretted purchasing the lancer as i think it looks more sporty than corolla and it serves its function as a means of tranportation for me, and has not given my any problems so far.

    Other cars you may want to consider is honda civic -- has driven my frens Civic VTI-S 1.7L ....whoa , acceleration is fast and handles well too. Of course, more expensive and consumes more petrol.

    hello Grief Actually your experience with driving shouldn't severely affect your choice of car... i guess what's most important is to drive carefully yah? Nothing shld happen in that case... and if anything does happen, it'd be an insurance issue rather than a new car/old car issue. A new car is definitely more worth it than a used one, unless you are on a tight budget and would want to get more car (metal/size) for your buck. People used to buy 2nd hand to avoid the mandatory 30% downpayment for new cars. Now that is gone, and also how new car prices have dropped, it's definitely more worth it to get a brand new one.

    Made in Thailand shouldn't be a problem, as many forum users have said due to the AFTA, more Japanese manufacturers will be assembling their SEA cars in Thailand. The City/Accord (City is a 5-door Jazz) Vios/Corolla/Camry are built there already. Major parts and the designs still come from Japan, but labour, component, logistics costs are lower.. so why not?

    Mazda 323: The replacement model's coming in next year. These are leftover stock the local dealer's trying to clear. It's up to you whether you fing this worth it or not.

    Hyundai Matrix: Low price for a 1.6l car... though i reserve my comments on Korean cars, I believe it's under-powered and the rear seats are tad too small.

    Nissan Sunny: Another popular car, though I agree with you that the rear looks ugly. It's supposed to consume a lot of petrol, if that's an issue with you. Actually rear drums aren't bad ideas, they are just cheaper than disc brakes.

    Proton Wira Avoid at all costs! An early 1990s Lancer and if you are looking for reliabililty, don't look at this car.

    Manual or auto? This is really up to personal preference. If you'd like a manual, my advice would be to borrow a manual car and drive it around for one day to gauge the feel. Hah, as for not wanting to drive a manual after using an auto, well it depends on whether you'd want to or not! An auto is definitely easier to drive, but I still prefer manual cars... heh.

    Good luck with your choices!
