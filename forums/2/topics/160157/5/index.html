    For a moving car to flip, there must be two forces.
    (1) One that stops the car "the hinge".
    (2) One that lifts the car "the lift".

    Just like opening the booth door of a hatchback car. You need both:
    (1) The hinge (built into the car).
    (2) The lift (your hand).
    Without either one, the hatchback will never flip up.

    In this accident....

    (1) The "hinge" was probably provided by the black BMW, which stopped the MX-5 head on.

    (2) The lift was probably provided for by the kerb. But if the MX-5 was peacefully rolling over the kerb, it will probably not flip over.

    (3) So when the MX-5 went over the kerb, it must have gone over with great enough speed to lift its body up.

    (4) One force stops the front, one force lifts the back into the air, the car flips. ie. probably speeding lah.
