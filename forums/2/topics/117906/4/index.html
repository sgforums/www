    Originally posted by Newdriver:
    thks wolf. i hv a question here. wat happens if the space outside the parking lot ie, the spot where u position yr car before reversing, is narrow and you can't tilt yr car to 45-degree w/o hitting the cars opposite the intended lot to park in? do i then need to use the 90-degree position and full lock steering when reversing? so far, i hv not experienced this yet but i know sooner or later i will.
    In this case, if you cannot tilt 45 degree, you will never be able to do a 90-degree position to turn even if you full turn the steering wheel. You are kind of restricted by the angle you can turn, and can turn steering wheel as much as the angle allow only.

    This type of case, you will have to do a few reverse & forward. This mean you have to be handly with your forward and reverse gear. What you do is to do very gentle 20 degree reverse or as much as the angle allow, move the tail in to the space, the trick is yoru car tail corner position . You just have to move forward, and do another 20 degree reverse or as much as angle allow. Frankly in some narrow car park, even experience driver , may need as much as 3 reverse to do it, but to other it may seem very fast, because they forward and reverse fast as they are so familiar with their gear and they are familiar how far they can push each reverse angle each time. For you take your time, check to ensure the gear is in reverse or forward carefully, and do it slowly, you will get faster with each time.

    Don't rush it, i have seen people accidently mess up, putting the wrong gear reverse instead of forward, and bang right into a wall.
