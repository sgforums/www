    Geez guys you really went after poor Kenneth there. I'm sure his intentions were good but his over exuberance got the better of him. Anyway it looks like I've completely highjacked this thread which I apologise for as that was not my intention.

    muwrx - I have done a couple of tracks days and I really enjoy the camaraderie of like minded people. I don't do them very often though as they are hard on the car and greatly increase your maintenance costs, but they are a hell of a lot of fun! Driving the car everyday is one of the greatest pleasures of owning such a toy. Whether I've had a good day or a bad day, one thing is assured; my day will be a little bit better when I press that red starter button. I must confess that I been known to take the long route home on occasion. 200km/h in 4th gear? That would be breaking the law surely! Oh and before anyone asks, the fastest I've taken it is 290km/h; I just won't say where haha. Parking on a slope; simple. If it's in gear it isn't going anywhere. Starting on a slope is no different to a stick shift, and unlike an automatic it will roll backwards (into a Honda City) if you let it.

     

    ceecookie - I guess I should be concerned about the rising fuel costs but at the moment I must admit that I'm not, simply because I view it as a fact of life. We actually all benefitted from unnaturally low oil prices for many years and the recent rally is more about playing catch-up than anything else; if oil hits $200pb then I might change my mind though. Generally speaking I fill up no more than once a week and only ever use premium, usually Shell V-power with a full tank costing about $140 after discount. You can never expect to blend in when driving a Ferrari and I actually get enjoyment when people stop and want to take pictures of the car, and will generally try and accommodate as much as I can. Two recent examples spring to mind. I was parked outside a hotel and there was a bus full of Indian tourists readying to depart. As I went to leave one asked if he could take his photo with the car, and before I knew it the entire bus had disembarked cameras in hand. 30 minutes later nearly every one had had their picture taken whilst sitting in the drivers seat; and it makes me smile to think that that was probably one of the highlights of their trip to Singapore, and proud that I could make that happen for them. On another occasion I was at home when my doorbell rang and 2 teenagers were standing at my gate. They asked if I'd mind if they came in and took photos of the car, which of course I obliged. The look on their faces when I told them to push the starter button made my day as much as theirs. Probably the most annoying thing is people asking "how much?"; it just seems to be the first thing out of most peoples mouths.

     

    Cheers
