    Originally posted by Exige60:


    Not sure too...but like the others said, this car was probably on test after servicing but still I don't see the need for them to hang the trade plates while testing a serviced car...anyone here can help?


    trade plates are used to cover insurance purposes... car with trade plate attached means that car at that point of time is under company cover. anything happen, company settle, not whoever is behind the wheel. of course only authorised personnel assigned by the company is allowed to use that plate lah. Each trade plate has a certain amount it can cover and it's usually used for higher end cars, etc Jaguars, Bentley, RR, Ferraris, AM and such. Others companies have designated drivers (usually technical advisors or senior mechanics doing QC test drives) with insurance covered under company name, but the limit is lower than given for trade plates. If you notice, trade plates are also used on brand new cars not registered on the roads yet; no car is allowed to be driven on the roads w/o insurance.
