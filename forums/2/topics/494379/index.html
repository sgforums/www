6 things to consider before buying your first family car

  • FireIce
    1 Aug 17, 11:22

    The quest to finding the right car is never an easy affair. Much like a vacation, careful planning and research is required before purchasing your very first family four-wheeler. But to help make that decision a little easier, we’ve compiled six vital tips to help you find out which vehicle best complements your family’s needs.

    Type of vehicle

    A family car is more than just a vehicle—it’s a comfort chamber, an amusement park, a grocery mule, a moving diner and a protective metal bubble. Whether you’re looking for a hatchback for extra storage, or a spacious 6-seater to transport your kids to school. The goal is figuring out what best suits you and your family, because as much as we love the sleek look of convertibles, coupés and child seats simply don’t mix.

    How big is your boot

    Depending on how much space you need, your family car should have plenty of storage space for the stroller, your week’s groceries, and even the family dog! SUVs and hatchbacks offer great storage potential for all your shopping sprees. Foldable backseats are another way of instantly increasing your boot space. Look to invest in vehicles that offer storage solutions like this.

    Safety first

    Looking for a safe and reliable ride should always be at the top of the list. Cars these days are often equipped with state-of-the-art safety features and electronic driving aids that guide you on the road. Side-curtain airbags and a 5-star Safety Rating is also a MUST when ensuring the safety of your loved ones and cargo.

    Comfort and convenience

    Whichever car you go for, you’ll want it to give you and your family a smooth and comfortable ride.

    Does it come with enough leg room? Is there limited head space? Are your kids bouncing off their seats every time you run over a speed bump? These are all important questions to ask yourself before making your purchase.

    Convenience is another factor parents should consider—especially when you’ve got your squirming toddler in one arm and a heavy bag of groceries in the other! Simple conveniences like automatic doors or hands-free liftgates can make all the difference in your daily routine.

    What about family-friendly features?

    Examine the layout of the vehicle’s interior to ensure there is ample room for multiple child car seats, find out what material the seats are made of — and more importantly, if it would be easy for clean ups. Having power sockets for charging or even an onboard DVD player will definitely keep the kids occupied and in their seats on the way to grandma’s.

    Think green

    More car trips means spending more money on fuel and more CO2 emissions into the atmosphere.   Save money (and the environment) by choosing a car that can give you the best mileage per litre.
