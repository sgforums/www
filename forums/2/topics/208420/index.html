    The general rule of thumb is to try to take as short a loan as possible - preferably 3 years. Max. 5 years. Because most people sell their cars after 5 years anyway.

    NEVER take a 10 year loan - I know lots of people who get trapped by it. As many people have already said: Financing for a car can be a very tricky trap!

    But first, threadstarter, you must realise that when you take a loan, the credit company/bank will ask you for documents showing your income, etc. They must know that you can pay back (logically, they can't loan money to someone without a stable and sufficient income).

    Hi again,

    Thanks for sharing.

    No doubt you are bonded and guaranteed a job. But you see, the first problem still remains. The credit company will ask you for you pay slip....or your CPF contribution for the last 6 months, etc.

    Credit companies assigns loans based on what you're earning.....not what you're gonna earn.

    I suggest that the best way now is to call up the credit company or car agent, etc....to ask if you are able to take a loan in your position right now.

    You might want to know that there have been cases recently where buyers have given a downpayment to reserve his car. Then after he couldn't find a credit company which will give him a loan (although he had a job). He lost his thousands of dollars of deposit.

    I know the urge to own a car and I don't mean to burst your bubble.....but some down-to-earth sensibility is very important for cases like these.
