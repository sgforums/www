Have you heard of the Ferrari Limo?

  • sjohnson

    Created from the body of the world class Ferrari 360 Modena, the limousine stretches nearly 20 feet long and seats 8 passengers.  The limo boasts 400 horsepower, can reach 60 miles per hour in less than 6 seconds, and has a top speed of 170 miles per hour, making this vehicle the fastest limo in the world. The limo was unveiled in September 2007 at the European Limousine and Chauffeur Show, held in the United Kingdom. 

    The black carbon fibre exterior turned more than a few heads at the show and its sleek styling is unmatched by any other style of limousine.  The interior mini bar was shunned in favor of extra seating and 9 foot gull wing doors.  The limo is stretched using hand built carbon fibre which allows the vehicle to retain its strength while still keeping it lightweight, less than 160kg more than the original car.  The limo also has a 3.6 litre V8 engine that is still visible behind a rear glass window. (source: Ferrari Limo)

    But now news had circulated that Ferrari says this is a Fake Ferrari limo.

    Representatives from the Italian automaker have reportedly threatened the creator of the Ferrari limo, Dan Crawley, with a lawsuit if he fails to remove all of the Ferrari symbols, names, and distinguishing marks from the vehicle.
