    Depends on what you look for in your car..

    Honda Vti,
    Not the monster performance the older Civics were known for, but still a decent performer. Handling has been firmed up thankfully wif the latest facelift. My dad is driving the Malaysian model with the 2.0l iVTEC.. nice and torque-y albeit without the old prominent VTEC 2nd cam kick of the older B16 engines.

    Subaru Impreza,
    Boxer engine sounds nice
    AWD handling is best in its class
    1.6l engine lacks grunt to do chassis and 4WDtrain justice

    Mit Lancer
    AVOID LIKE THE PLAGUE
    GDI engine not suited for local petrols
    GLX engine is dunno how many donkey years old (appeared waaay before MIVEC or GDI.. abt Evo 4 timeframe!!) but rehashed for the Singapore market!!
    CVT transmission makes the whole equation even worse!!

    Vios
    Light
    Relatively torquey (esp wif such a light chassis)
    Good potential
    but really need to firm up the handling and suspension
    cachet of made in thailand?

    I drove the Focus hatch for a year and a half. Engine lacks grunt in the low end, but that car handles incredibly well... out of the factory. Nothing changed in suspension etc.

    Took me almost 3-4k worth of mods on my next car just to get it to handle as well as the Focus.

    I think the manual transmission version should help compensate for the lack of power.

    But we should be seeing the new model soon (the current Mazda 3 and Volvo S40s are all based on the same chassis as the upcoming Focus).

    Some people find the new Focus New Edge styling to be softened a little. I think it looks nicer. Current front end looks a little dated.
