    hey joe, this is for u:
    T
    he long plastic with TWINCAM 1.0 writing is a must for DOHC engines (incidentally, it's called an Intake Runner). A lot of people don't understand the fact that DOHC engines need long intake runners to take full advantage of DOHC. Something to do with maintaining a high pressure air buffer while the intake valves open and close. Here's some reading material to understand this fully:

    DOHC/Twincam:
    http://auto.howstuffworks.com/question562.htm

    Tuned Intake Runners:
    http://auto.howstuffworks.com/question517.htm

    Therefore, I would suggest sticking an adaptor to the end of our stock intake runners, and attaching an open pod there (don't forget to remove stock filters la ) for maximum performance from an air filter. Unless you're going turbo of course...heheh. This way, you'll also get fresh air from the left side of the grill, away from the hot radiator.

    There are a lot of intricacies and tricks to intake systems. For instance, it is beneficial to have the intake air moving as fast as possible into the cylinders. This increases the turbulence and mixes the fuel with the air better. One way to increase the air velocity is to use a smaller diameter intake runner. Since roughly the same volume of air enters the cylinder each cycle, if you pump that air through a smaller diameter pipe it will have to go faster.
