    Car washing 101:-

    1. Wash off the dust/dirt with water (best is if you can hose it off using those high pressure sprays)

    2. Clean the car using sponge and car shampoo. Make sure sponge doesn't have bits of dirt sticking to it as that will scratch your paintwork. Always start from the roof and work downwards.

    3. Rinse off the car. Again, if you have a high pressure spray, that's the ideal.

    4. Dry the car. Make sure the cloth you use is clean with no dirt stuck to it.

    It is the dust particles/ bits of dirt on your car/Sponge/Cloth that causes the scratches. Make sure you get rid of them first.

    Just to add on, try not to wash your car in the hot sun or in hot hours (10am to 4pm).

    The soap will dry before you finish soaping the rest of the body. This will leave ugly soap marks.

    While wiping dry, use a chamois cloth and have a bucket of water to rinse the cloth every now and then, this is to rinse off any dirt/debris that could be collected while wiping dry.
