    13 Feb 06, 23:10
    CTE stretches from north (YCK) to south (city area) hence the name CTE, central expressway. it is on the right side of the central catchment aread AKA bukit timah natural reserve.

    PIE stretches from west (jurong side) to east (changi airport), hence the name Pan Island expressway.

    BKE is on the left side of the Bukit timah natural reserve.stretching from PIE
    Eng Neo to Woodlands, it runs very near bukit timah, so i believe tat's how it got its name

    SLE is on the Northern part of singapore, also the north part of the central catchment area. it brings woodlands/sembawang to the rest of the singapore linking with BKE and CTE

    AYE is the counterpart of SLE, running on the southern part of Singapore. i'm not too sure how AYE is because i hardly use it

    i aren't too sure about TPE, KJE and the rest. but if you look at the map of singapore with the expressways, there's a certain pattern and the names will help you remember how the express way system run

    All the expressway are suppose to be interlink.

    Can go round singapore.

    SLE->TPE->ECP->AYE->KJE->BKE->SLE(again)

    you can go round and around of singapore.

    CTE and PIE are the so-call middle intersection which links it to the other expressway.

    CTE can link to = SLE, TPE, AYE, PIE
    PIE can link to = CTE, BKE, ECP, TPE
    BKE = KJE, PIE
    SLE = KJE, BKE, TPE, CTE
