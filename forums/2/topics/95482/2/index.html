    Some more usage for highbeams, it is generally practiced by taxi drivers and truck drivers to communicate or alert other drivers of your presence (also used in EU, USA and other western countries).

    For example, when you are making a right turn and a taxi going straight ahead (opposite side) but you try to proceed to make your turn.. may be you assume that he is stoping or turning left, in this case he will use the high-beam (just a single flash) to warn you that he is proceding straight ahead.

    Another common usage is in freeways to alert someone cutting into your lane with bad judgement of your speed. I noticed that people usually horn but it is useless because your horn is very feeble on freeways (remember most drivers turn-on their stereo, horning is a very bad idea). You can double flash to indicate that it is difficult to slow down, and prefer to proceed first.

    you should also use it when travelling along sharp bend on two way roads to alert the drivers on opposite side.

    There are many more usage, and commonly practiced in EU and USA, try searching for usage in google.
