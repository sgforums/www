  • redDUST
    1 Mar 08, 23:01

    i think we all need to exercise patience. the road is for all users. i ride my roadbike a couple of times a week. when i ride alone, i am very defensive on the road. when in group, there's slightly more safety in numbers. however, it all boils down to mutual respect to one another between the cyclists and the motorists. I dun zig zag, i stay on the left lane most times.

    the reason why some cyclists occupy the left lane is becos there are some motorists who tend to pass the cyclists very close to them, the draft can cause the cyclist to lose slight control if he/she is not careful. i have personally encountered some drivers who drive pass me within inches on their vehicle almost on purpose. occupying the lane will almost certainly force the motorists to filter out to the second lane to overtake.

    when i am driving, i have an enhanced awareness of these cyclists and give them the space they need. i can say most are responsible cyclists. there will be a few pinheads who want to show off, but there is still no reason to endanger their lives.

    if you encounter those in a big group, i.e. 10-50 of them, just overtake using the outermost lane. in a bigger group, it is inevitable some cyclists sway out as it is not easy to ride in a group of varying ride skills.
