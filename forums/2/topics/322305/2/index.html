    Well, the majority of people having these old plates are usually their very own treasure which they had when they bought their first car.

    Either that or that it was passed down by wealthy merchants in the past to the next generation and it goes on.

    A fact - the older the number plate, it will appreciate in value and many such owners would not want to let these number plates be transferred despite the high intangible valuation given.

    The older the number plate, (numbers that are affixed to these rare series) and which are on the road are typically bidded numbers and it customary showcases and reflect's the owners's accomplishments.

    Take for example, I've got a a neighbour around 3-4 houses away which have so called established a tradition - they own an entire fleet of mercedes benzes marques which all carry the identical numbers, with the S and E series. It's more towards a status symbol and sense of identity among their entire family.

    Number plates which are on sale in the market bearing these rare affixes are usually not bidded numbers and are demanded by buyers who just want an old number plate.

    Well, as for my dad, we've had a few enquires before but of course - it's not for sale! Most probably it's going to be passed down to my sis. It also has had a fare share of being in the top three prizes of 4D Draw. 3293:)

    For my car number, probability that it will never come out is extremely high - given the 3 factorials of "2" in it. (I've never stepped into a 4D outlet and never will - coffer donations to me! :P)

    Hope it has helped you in gathering some information for your project study!

    Many of these old license plates were either scrapped off with their cars when the COE expired sometime in the late 1980s thus the vanishing of these "vintage" number plates. (This mainly applies to non bidded numbers)

    For the bidded series, they usually are not let up for sale and are often passed down from one generation to the next. Profit from the sale of the number wouldn't be a primary thought to the owner as well, somethings cannot be bought with money. Especially something passed down from perhaps your late parents.

    And yes! A number 1 plate exists. EZ 1 Z. This guy stays somewhere around Hume Avenue in a Black Mercedes S Class. (Provided this guy has not switched car yet)

    The next closest would be the owner of EA 5 Y aka the acronym EASY of a Olive Green Lexus LS460. (This guy frequents the Guan Yin Hood Cho temple at Bugis so you might want to spot him there on Sundays)
