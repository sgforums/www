    Rule of 78 Loan Calculator

    http://www.reidtax.com/rule78.html

    Do you have:
    1) In cash, at least enough liquidity to survive 12 months (retrenchment proving) including servicing of debt and expenses? Typically 6 months salary

    2) No more than 30-40% of NET monthly income servicing debt? (including mortgages, loans, credit card bills)

    3) A retirement plan, and regular contribution of your balance income to that plan?

    If you satisfy all of the above conditions, how do you work out the affordability of purchases?

    Simply find out how much you can afford to pay each month to bring your debt servicing to 40%. That's the MAXIMUM installment you can afford to pay each month.

    The maximum downpayment you can afford determines the price of the car you can buy. Obviously you can load up to 100% financing if you wish, but calculate the interest impact first and make sure you understand the trade-off in cost and the amount you lose on resale. I personally would not load more than 70% financing, but others think otherwise. Whatever you wish to pay down, remember you need to rebuild your cash reserves to survive retirement.

    Bottom line is, if you're contributing 40-50% of your net monthly income to savings via retirement plans, insurance, FDs etc, buy anything you want with the balance.
