    6 Jul 05, 23:05
    Hi all,
    The 'ding2' sound means the air in the tyre is as much as what was programmed when you first started. However if you started at 3000 psi then off course your tyres will explode. BTW 'psi' stands for pounds per square inch. That figure for our tyres is usually about 20-23 psi say. The metric equiivalent is 220 or 230 kps. If you check your tyres in a SPCstation you can see the difference as their pumps have 2 measurements. If you are travelling north to Msia, ie driving for hours on the N-S expressway, then you might increase it by 5-10%.

    But remember that overinflated tyres provide better fc and grip in turning. they also 'burst' rapidly and are a bit more difficult to control in the event it 'bursts'. Underinflated tyres give a higher fc but when they 'burst' will be much easier to control when driving. So don't overdo it either way. All my comments are subject to confirmation by the TC mechanics on Sat.

    Have you seen your spare tyre?. It meant to be used only at slow speeds and on a temporary basis, eg not over 50 kms

    For those who are interested knowing how to change their tyres, I suggest they meet earlier on Sat and arrange for a volunteer. It's dead simple.

    Toadie
