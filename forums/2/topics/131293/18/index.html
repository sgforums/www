    Madagascar,

    What is a door visor?

    I drove a car some years back with window visors. I assume they are those plastic things you wedge on top of the window on the outside. If you wind down your windows slightly, some air/wind will come into the car to cool it without disrupting your hairdo. Am I correct?

    If so I feel its a waste of time as the 'air' coming into the window is quite warm and will do little to cool the car. Further they make a lot of wind noise and cause unnecessary wind friction. This is the biggest disadvantage. This works well in cold countries as the outside air is cold and is just suitable to cool the interior. Its like driving a convertible in Sgp. Further if the quality is poor the plastic will discolour with age, otherwise it is just another example of a cosmetic mod.

    As for my experience I never used it and did not bother as it came with the car, even when it was raining. Hope this is useful.

    Toadie
