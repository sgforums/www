    Pump prices up by 5 to 9 cents on record high crude prices

    Petrol companies Caltex, Shell and SPC on Tuesday increased their pump prices by 5 cents to 9 cents per litre, to match rival Exxon Mobil's hike on Monday evening.
    Fuel prices have been going up and the reason for the latest hike - record crude oil prices due to fears that hurricane Katrina in the US could damage the country's refining capacity.

    Crude soared to almost US$70 per barrel.

    How would the hike in petrol prices affect motorists?

    "Save money by travelling less, this is the only choice," said a motorist.

    "I'm in sales. No choice, but I will plan my routes," said another.

    "I wait at the taxi stand, mostly. You cannot go around.....the diesel, so expensive," said a taxi driver.

    "Not really, I think it's still very much the same. I still drive as it is," said a fourth motorist.

    The relentless price hikes are taking its toll on private bus operators who say fuel costs are up 75% since the beginning of the year - from S$40,000 a month to S$70,000.

    But there's little else they can do as they've just increased transport fares by 15 percent in July.

    Taxi drivers say their fuel cost has also gone up by more than $300 a month in the last three months.

    Pump prices were fluctuating throughout Tuesday as petrol companies tried to match their rivals' in a highly competitive retail market.

    But they managed to keep the prices just under the psychological $2 mark, with standard discounts thrown in to soften the impact.

    Currently, grade 98 and 95 petrol were retailing at $1.927 and $1.844 respectively across the board, before discounts.

    Prices for Premium 98 grade petrol stabilised at $1.99 per litre.

    Shell increased its diesel prices by 5 cents to $1.22 but Caltex and ExxonMobil did not follow suit. - CNA/ir
