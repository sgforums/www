    if u notice resistance returning ur brake pedal depression, u probably have warped rotors or evenly worn brake pads. during braking, when the pads go over the uneven parts, it forces the calipers and consequently, the hydraulics and brake pedals away from the normal depressing of the calipers. this "return" pressure obviously messes up ur ABS sensors since they are pressure sensitive. they should result in very slight continous "nudges" against ur foot. my hunch anyway.

    When ABS intervenes, you will feel it in a way like th brake pedal is pulsing up and down. It concludes that you are applying too much brake pressure and thus the pedal pulses up to release some pressure. If you feel it pulsing and the ABS light is on but the situation is not an emergency and you're not doing an E Brake then I think your ABS has malfunctioned. This is what I think - correct me if i'm wrong. Whatever the case, go to your workshop.
