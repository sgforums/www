    I actually know a little. Why I can compare the power of Aveo and Getz is because I test-drove Aveo before and my friend Getz and Aveo. All the things I know about Aveo comes from reading from forum, My car forum. Because I seriously consider Aveo before to the point of almost booking it. Last info about its FC I found on the forum pull me back. That 12km/L figure is reachable, but you are going to get a lot of tailgater behind you. Especially at the traffic light, all those stronger cars behind will close in impatiencely and cause you to gas a little more. And that is how FC drops.

    A few things to take note if you want to conserve fuel. Firstly keep of lane 1 of expressway, and only drive about 80km/h max. Always slowly increase our throttle pressure, don't floor. Change to K&N filter, may or may not help. Don't not change your rims, unless you are prepared for the worst. I heard there is a method to control the gear you are in for Auto cars, but you will have to ask someone else. I driving manual.

    Not use crying over spill beans, Aveo is still a great car if you overlook the FC and a slightly weaker torque at low-end. From 3rd gear onward, it should be a breeze. And finally, this is what 1 aveo driver did to his ride. Aveo
