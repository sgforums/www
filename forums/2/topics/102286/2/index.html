    I wonder why people like to dump FC and power together.....

    also, the topic of FC is very variable...

    - the way you drive
    - the roads you take
    - the load you carry (incl how much u weigh)
    - rims/tyres size
    - transmission type
    - gear ratio
    - engine capacity/displacement
    - fuel tank size
    - injection/engine technology
    - alot more

    it's not just about "Brand A/Model A has better FC than Brand B/Model B".

    on the average, most cars clock about 9-12km/liters.

    even if you are on a picanto, if you carry loads, rev hard and drive in city most of the time. trust me, you won't get even near your famed 17km/liter.

    if you are on a matrix, if you drive well, you can get to 16km/liter. with mostly expressways and no traffic jams and minimal weight.

    even on a getz, you can easily hit 15km/l or more if you adjust your driving habits, plan..etc

    the M6 - which can achieve 100kph in sub 5 seconds. has a documented FC of 8km/liter.

    the 1000cc nissan mach, also is around the 13-14km/liter region.

    no one knows how well you will do in FC except yourself!

    I don't know about others, but the reason why I like to mix FC and power together is because if the car is weak, the driver have the tendency to floor more in order to keep up with the traffic. As we know flooring will increase your FC. Comparing cars like Vios which is lighter and thus a little gas could already set the car flying. There is also this thing about the percentage of the power of the engine got transfered to the wheel, I think got a technical term for that.

    Most car clock that amount. But with the introduction of cars like Jazz, people's expectation of FC risen. And cars commonly reported to have about 10km/L will be rated as a car with weak FC. There are serveral cases of people changing cars and clocking a very much different FC. Thus it is always a good guide to ask around for the general average that particular made is clocking. Don't see much Vios with a FC of 10km/L, do you? Then again, having a small cc car will generally yield a decent FC.

    As for the bad mouth part. Many was advised by friends who own that car themselve. I know 3 Getz driver, but 2 were frequent visitors to the workshop. As times goes on, news spread and usually it is the bad news. That is branding. It takes time to build, but a little shake will everything down.

    Conclusion? I still think Picanto is a decent car that can cruise easily at 90-100km and have a reputable FC. As for the reliability issue, please buy 1 and feedback to us in 3 years time. Thanks! I will contribute my part with Kelisa.
