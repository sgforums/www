Which car to buy survey .. X-trail or Forester or Airtrek?

  • nolimit
    25 Feb 04, 14:20
    I seek feedback from anyone who has experience in any of these cars and is able to give me their impression to help me to make a decision. I'm more interested in issues about owning the car that cannot be found in test drives, brochures or reviews.

    X-Trail 2.5 - spacious, sunroof option, reclining rear seat
    Forester 2.0 XT - performance, free revving engine, character
    Airtrek 2.0 Turbo - most performance, modern dashboard

    Assuming that:
    All are automatic transmission
    Moderate driving for better fuel economy 80% of the time
    Drive main roads 80% of the time, 20% expressways
    Keep within appropriate speed limits
    Drive to M'sia occasionally

    Survey - rank in order of preference under the following scenarios:
    (X = X-Trail 2.5, F = Forester XT, A = Airtrek Turbo)

    Longest suspension travel (driving over road humps)
    Most composed suspension (travelling over uneven road surfaces)
    Quietest cabin from road noise (not counting engine sound)
    Most comfortable seat after at least 1 hr driving (driver seat)
    Most comfortable rear seats after at least 1 hr journey
    Least vibration when idling in 'D' gear
    Least wind noise from A-pillar or side mirrors

    Best acceleration 0 - 60 kph at half throttle
    Best acceleration 60 - 90 kph in 4th gear (without gear changing down)
    Change up to 4th gear soonest at half throttle, city driving
    Most useable torque and gear matching for city driving

    Most composed while rounding bends (moderate driving, within posted speed limit)
    Smoothest gear upchange under brisk acceleration (3/4 throttle)

    Best able to live with it long-term (general conditions)
    Most worry-free in terms of breakdowns
    Cheapest in terms of repair and maintenance costs

    Generally most satisfying to own overall
    Generally most fun to drive overall
    Generally most rugged (least prone to rattling etc. after a few years)

    Thanks.
