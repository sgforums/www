    i also need to point out to you, that its not 100% accurate to say that if you change your rims to a smaller one, u'll get faster acceleration.

    generally, what matters most is the overall wheel size, meaning, the rim with the tyre fitted on. if you go down a rim size, but get higher profile tyres, it generally off sets the change in rim size, hence your speedo's calibration should not be affected either.

    similarly, if u go up a rim size, and get lower profiled tyres, your overall wheel diameters stays more or less the same. so for eg, if your car has 15" rims now with 195/65 R15 tyres, if you swap out for a 16 inch rim, u should be able to stay with 195/55 R16 tyres, maintaining your overall wheel size. in general, for every inch increase in rim size, u have to do a corresponding 10mm decrease in the tyre's profile, and vice versa.

    now regarding overall wheel diameter, if that decreases, it is true that your acceleration would increase. But it would also upset your car's suspension settings as the car's suspension was set up to respond best to running on that particular wheel size. a smaller overall wheel diameter might result in insufficient suspension travel, which might result in the underside of the car being damaged easily.
