    Hi all
    Classic payment allows 6 bids for COE non-guaranteed.
    Gold payment gives guaranteed COE, think the difference is $1,000.

    There are two levels of trim: Standard and Deluxe...
    The aircon is an automatic aircon. From reviews I read, it seems to provide a good flow of cool air to the whole cabin, since the temperature sensors are all over the car.

    The audio is an in-dash CD/mp3 player... no changer though. Yes, there are 4 disc brakes.

    The Malaysian cars come with traction control, which I don't think is available here something not usually seen in this level of car... and yah, it's not like Toyota's giving a discount for S'poreans (still damn ex ah!) M'sian cars are so expensive bcoz of high import duties levied on foreign-made cars... to protect their own car manufacturers...
    I think the M'sian 1.8l Altis costs SGD 54,000... about there, so still cheaper than S'pore.
    Actually the 1.6l version is available overseas as well. This Altis is also sold in the US.

    I've test-driven and ordered the 1.8l one liao... $80+k for a 1.8 car is a good deal, 136PS/ 171 Nm of torque for such a light car is cool as well though think most pple would be fine with the 1.6l....

    Alas... no manual trans! Will miss my manual car...
    Does anyone agree with me that the SEs at BM in Leng Kee do not provide good service?

    Anyways the engine is a chain-cam, which is more reliable than the standard timing belt. 90% of engine components come from Japan though I believe the engine is made in China. The auto trans is made in Japan, the steel comes from Japan and the rest are assembled in Thailand.

    Heh the Camry's replacement is made in Thailand as well. If the quality is there and the price is so much cheaper, why not?
