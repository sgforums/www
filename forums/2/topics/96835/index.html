    Shell used to give out some booklets on things to look out for in buying a used car. I am not sure if they still have it. Anyway, here's a few pointers:

    a. Handbrake - Without depressing the release button, pull it up and hear the clicking sound. There should not be more than 4 click in the process. Anything more than that indicates the handbrake needs a repair job.

    b. Clutch - Depress clutch fully. If you need lots of strength just to depress it fully or there is some strange noise in depressing it, the clutch system needs repair. With clutch fully depressed, engage 1st gear. Release till 'biting' point. It should be 1/3 of the way or close to 1/2. Anything more than that means the clutch is almost worn out. If engine stall at 1/3 or 1/2 level, it is also another indication. Next, with clutch fully depressed,engage 4th gear. Release gradually and the engine should stall immediately at 1/3 or 1/2. If it does not, the clutch is worn out.

    c. Car Body - Walk around the car. Look from every angle. Is there any misalignment or the body surface uneven? Look especially at the door seam and joint section. Look at front door and rear door. Are they aligned? Open bonnet and look at the welding spot. Non accident car will show factory welding spot(looks like crooked line but identical on both sides). Do the same with boot open and look at both side.

    d. DIY chasis/steering system test- At the wheel fender, btw the gap of tyre and fender, use the number of fingers to measure the distance. Let's say 4 fingers at right side should also gives you 4 fingers distance at left side. If it's not, chasis or steering system has been damaged before or worst chasis damaged.

    e. Water leakge test - During your test drive, request to go for car wash and during the wash, watch out for any leakage at the doors area. Next open the boot and see if water has entered the boot area.

    f. After test drive, check the area underneath and see if there is any dark oil patch(engine leakage) or greenish/blueish liquid stain(coolant leakage). pen the bonnet and inspect the engine area. Any oil leakge aroud engine block? If there is, engine casket gone or worst engine needs top overhaul. If exhaust emitting dark blueish smoke, another indication of worn engine. Open radiator cap(allow to cool down) and watch for any rust stain present or bubbles. This could spell radiator problem and may lead to overheating.

    The list is endless. Hope above helps.
