    ok la proper feedback. i used to drive a particular mpv with 4 aspec dBs. thereafter, I changed them to 2 x yokohama c.drive and 2x yokohama aspec dBs.

    either way, same reaction when one side contacts with a puddle of water. why? water is not like "air". when u hit water - its the same as hitting a wall. if a plane crashed into the sea for example, it would smash, break, explode, etc. - same as crashing into the ground. except that water = crash then sink.

    therefore when ur wheels contact a large body of water on one side, that side will be pulled back like as though there's an invisible force on that side.

    now i'm on 4 pilot sport tyres i think.. different car but same effect =) tyre threads that drain water away assume that it's an even layer of water on the surface of the road - not a sudden pile of water although the threads do mean different ways of impacting the water and might mean less force etc. =) the best way to counter this is to have a tire that "cuts" thru the water - like a ship's bow (the V shape at the front of the boat) but that's quite impossible. so the thicker your tyre, the harder the impact.
