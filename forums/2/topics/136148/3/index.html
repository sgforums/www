    I think there is no doubt that motorists should drive in the 2 left lanes at speeds up to the maximum of 90 km/h. In practice, I think all will agree that most vehicles are already going at the maximum speed in the middle lane. That means there is no reason for overtaking a vehicle in front at all, because he is already at the maximum speed. Does that mean all vehicles will therefore be using only two lanes, leaving the right most lane empty....since no one can overtake?

    Of course that does not happen. It does not make sense to leave an entire right lane empty because no one can legally overtake in that situation (unless you are driving an emergency vehicle). What then happens is that some vehicles will filter right into the right lane. That is allowed provided you do not go faster than the maximum speed of 90 km/h. In other words, you do not go any faster by using the right lane if you are already moving at the maximum speed in the middle lane. However, it is good for traffic flow 'cos you open up a space for the vehicles behind you going at lower speeds to move up to 90km/h.

    The right most lane is indeed to be used for overtaking only. However, even when overtaking, you must not exceed the maximum speed or you will be guilty of an offence. Whether or not the TP wants to enforce it when you are overtaking at 110 km/h temporarily to go past the vehicle in the middle lane at 90 km/h is up to him. But he is entitled to book you because you have committed an offence according to the law.

    So when traffic is heavy (it often is), all 3 lanes may be used by vehicles. If vehicles in the middle lane is already going at maximum speed, vehicles in the right lane CANNOT overtake and can only go at the maximum speed. In essence, the right lane then functions as a middle lane. If for some reason a vehicle in the middle or right lane wants to slow down below the maximum speed, it should filter left.

    By the way, if you are already travelling at the maximum speed in whatever lane but the path is clear in front of you, you are NOT road hogging. You CANNOT exceed the speed limit just because there are no vehicles in front of you. Speed limits are fixed based on road engineering concerns, visibility, road curvature etc...they are fixed for a reason.

    Originally posted by No_10_Tomas_Rosicky:
    Can someone provide any evidence that driving at 89km/h on lane 1 is a traffic offence or

    can someone provide an example of someone being booked for driving at 89km/h on lane 1????
    No, it is not an offence to do that unless there is an ambulance coming from behind. In that case, you will have to filter left and if any motorists in the middle lane refuse to let you in, they may will be booked.

    The right lane is intended for overtaking and emergency vehicles, but there is nothing in the law that makes it an offence to drive in that lane at close to speed limit if you are not impeding an emergency vehicle. In contrast, driving above the speed limit in any lane is an offence under the law (Sect 63, Road Traffic Act).

    In my knowledge after driving for 20 years, so far, I know the middle lane of an expressway are for those who are travelling at the prescribed speed of 80/90Kmh, and the left lane are for slow moving commercial vehicles and heavy vehicles. The right most lane however, fondly called Lane 1, is for OVERTAKING another vehicle in the middle lane. After you have overtaken it is expected that you return to the middle lane and drop back to 80/90Kmh or you will be deemed to be speeding by any Traffic Police that happens to spot you.

    There is NO mystery or complexity about what this or that lane is for. Just the above.
