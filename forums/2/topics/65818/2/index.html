    For saving fuel, unfortunately most of the touted "fuel saver" products are bullsh**.

    Next to your initial choice of car (factoring in the weight, aerodynamics, engine size, transmission efficiency, etc.), the rest of it lies in your driving habits and maintenance practices.

    Full throttle starts or take it easy? Slam brakes or rolling stops? Treating the roads as an ongoing open-air street race, or playing the entirely different game of trying to achieve maximum efficiency?

    For me, the trick on my EK3 is to get the Econo light to turn on and utilise 12-valve mode (VTEC-E) for as long as possible, and to try to use "VTEC Stage 3" as sparingly as possible ...

    Maintenance? Well that includes all the boring but important stuff - engine and transmission oil, checking the battery, tyre pressure.

    30 Jan 04, 16:31
    Did I mention tyre pressure? Yes I did.

    More on that. Picked up a few tips from the hybrid technology forums and local SHC forum :

    Inflate your tyre to as high as you are comfortable with, without blowing it apart (check the maximum pressure on yours, it's usually listed on the side - it should be quite high, like 300-350 kpa).

    Higher tyre pressures make for a slightly rougher ride but can contribute to 5-10% better economy.

    Recommendations I've seen include settings like (front-back) : 250-240, 240-230, 230-220.

    Oh, and remember to pump air only when the tyres are cold. *Not* the petrol station near your place when you reach home from work (for example).

    That's why there's one more metric which you can consider using : $/km. Or cents/km if you will because usually you'll get numbers like 9-10 cents or so.
