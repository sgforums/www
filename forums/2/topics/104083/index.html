    The 1st number's the width of the tire in mm. So in the 185/70R14 example,
    the tire width is 185mm.

    The 2nd's number's the height of the tyre sidewall, expressed as a percentage of the tire width.So in 185/70R14, the sidewall's 70% of 185mm = 129.5mm tall

    The R in 185/70R14 sez that the tyre's internal construction is Radial

    and the last 14 in 185/70R14 is the rim size in inches that goes with the tyre.

    Hope this helps

    Originally posted by slacker001:
    So if we want to change tires can we get those with different specifications ie different height of side wall? Assuming we dont change wheel just tires.

    How will different sizes affect performance and FC?
    Yes, you can get a different spec for the sidewall, provided that your wheelwell can accomodate the changes.

    If you upsize too much, the tires will rub against the wheelwell, esp. if the car is really loaded, going over humps. Font tyres may even rub when the steering is turned lock-to-lock.

    As performance, I think it will affect both performance and FC adversely, because the tire is now bigger. Your speedo and odometer accuracy will also be affected
