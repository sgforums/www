    a BOV vents off built up pressure when throttle is cut off. this prolongs the lifespan of the turbocharger by reducing the resultant pressure on the turbine fins, but it is not necessary for low or stock boost conditions. and it certainly does nothing to reduce turbo lag. if installing a few hundred dollar cheapo device can reduce turbo lag, why should ppl spend thousands of dollars on an anti lag management system?

    for most part of it, a poorly matched or improperly tuned BOV will wreck havoc on the MAP sensor readings in a turbocharged vehicle. u are lucky the evo is less susceptible to such probs. usually, its young punks who wanna show off and know nuts about BOVs that install them. high powered evos producing in excess of 700 hps are all running waste gates instead of ricey BOVs.
