    Allow me to make some correction. Yes the City is a 8 valve engine BUT it comes with the new tech iDSI engine configuration and CVT, coupled with 7 speed steermatic. Although max power is 88 kwh, it comes in max torque at 2700 rpm.

    iDSI stands for Intelligent Dual Sequential Ignition, meaning 2 spark plug per cyclinder as opposed to conventional single plug layout. That means City has 8 plugs instead of the normal 4. What this translates into is ultra high fuel efficiency and max torque at low rpm until redline at 6500 rpm. CVT is a high tech expensive stuff and what this translate in automatic gear is seamless, stepless gear changes. You dont feel the knee jerk effect at all and its smart fetaures is that it adapts to changing road condition and speed. Same pressure on the pedal but when you are hitting into a slight gradient, the rpm automatically rev up and adjusted back when you have passed the gradient. Thats the beauty of smart CVT.

    Read about international review on the City and find out more. Thats what I did before I chose the City.

    Last I heard China having waiting time of 6 months for City (known as Fit there) and 3 months in Malaysia. In Japan, it is a top seller in its class and oh yes, in Japan it does not belongs to the K-class catergory, which means small compact like the Nissan March.
