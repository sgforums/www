  • ditzy
    29 Dec 05, 11:53
    The reason I didn't choose any FR cars for drifting in the game is because of its physics engine. I chose 4wd cars because the problem didn't lie in entering the drift, but getting out of one. I required grip and traction on the front wheels to get it out, thus playing with the torque settings for the front wheels.

    The suspension I didn't really play around because those are the more touche settings I cannot seem to figure out, but lowering the spring height and stiffening should give a little bit of added handling. I wouldn't play with the dampers if I were you, they don't seem to affect anything. For the toe angle, the fronts should be 1.5 more than the back. Try not to exceed 4 for either front and back.

    LSD i din't mess around with it either because I'm not so pro. The 1.5 one works pretty well all round for me. I've only managed to tune one perfect drift car using the 22B, it is simply impossible to spin yet goes ultimate sideways and out when I need to.

    The 86 if you want my frank opinion is impossible to tune for drift, the rear wheels rev way to much, just can't find the oomph.

    Can't remember exactly, coz I left my PS2 in Oz, so I can't access it anymore. I do know the torque distribution was somewhere with 30% front. Super soft front tyres and medium at the back. Car only had 522bhp. Spring height all the way down, Rate all the way up. LSD is 1.5, gear ratio auto is at level 33. Brake bias max back, min front. Toe angle front was 3.5, back was 2.5. That's all I remembered. Different car needs different tunes to achieve the same effect because of the physics engine. Also how you configure the controller will affect the sensitivity of your throttle control. I use the x for gas, o for brakes. I tried tuning most of the FR cars and couldn't find any settings that suit me.

    The track I used most often for drift practice is the mountain one. There is a huge U just after the uphill. That is one place for you to practice throttle control. Remember, getting the back out is easy, getting it back in is difficult one.
