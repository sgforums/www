    I am having some of these mods on my ride now. Here are my feedbacks and comments:

    For the air intake mods, I find that there is an improvement in pickup and throttle response. However, some said that this effect is temporary, and that the ECU will override them after a while. Or some actually says that because you have gotten use to the effect, you will think that it is losing its effect, ahaha, cheem?

    Volt stabilisers dun think have much effect on smooth engine running, but should have more effect on getting better effect from your stereos.

    Crank pulley, I dun feel much effect. I checked the weight diff. Although, reduction in the weight is 30%, but bloody hell the stock pulley is just 1.8kg. I was thinking - how much engine power do you need to spin this thing? Hence, I feel that this probably explain why I do not feel much effect. But it does look nice spinning inside my engine bay. Mine is metallic blue.

    Clutch.... mine is auto , never tried, no comments,

    SAFC2 - optimises HP by maximising air/fuel ratio. But to benefit and to be safe, you will have to have it tuned via dyno. So do up all your other mods b4 fixing this up, else you will have to re-tune again each time you do a mod. Itz $200-$300 for each tune.

    Fuel regulator - Heard from comments it does help in overcoming the "flat" when you throttle from idling to 2000 rpm, by keeping the fuel pressure up. Haven't tried this yet, but comments does indicate that it is effective.

    But take note , mods for improving performance will worsen your fuel consumption.
