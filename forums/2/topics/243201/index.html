    Let me put the "other side of the case" forward. Not because I have decided one way or the other, but because I am interested in yours (and others) response:

    *Looks: Like comparing chalk and cheese. The Civic has an elegant, refined look. The Mazda 3 (especially with the spoiler, etc) has a sporty, sexy look.

    *Civic is a newer model: true, can't dispute this. This might impact on resale value in the future. In 3 years, a 2007 Civic will still be "current model" or similar, whereas the Mazda 3 will be a "has been".

    *Interior: matter of taste, some like the Civic's interior, some don't.

    Let me make some other points:

    *Subjective driving : many people say that the Mazda is much more of a driver's car than the Civic. I have test driven them both (though the Mazda I test drove was a 2.3, not the 2.0 one) and can confirm that handling, brakes, steering are better on the Mazda. It's much more fun to drive.

    *In Australia, the Mazda 3 is Japanese made and the Civic is Thailand made. This may or may not impact on build quality.
