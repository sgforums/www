  • Ali_Khl
    27 Jul 04, 15:40
    Upgrading to larger rims, especially in your case with two inches larger, can result in significantly slower acceleration times because of both the increase rotating mass (most effecting area in gaining/ losing weight is the wheels/ tires) and increased rolling resistance from resulting larger tires. So i would say 8.2 is about accurate, while a .7 second quicker time can be expected realistically from a stock car. If you want to regain your stock times with these large rims, you might need to push up power slightly via a replacement ECU or similar basic modifications.

    My quickest 0-100 km/h time was recorded recenetly with 29'000 kms on the odometer, at 8.5 seconds timed by clock, with two people in the car.
