    I am an altis owner since feb 04. Also grappled with some of the same issues when deciding. Here is why I bought eventually.

    main critique - made in Thailand .... implication being a inferior quality, likely to break down, fall apart, not as quiet etc.

    My reasoning : Merc is made / assembled in S.Africa, your Volvo is assembled in India, the BMW are also done in S.Africa. Lexus produes its cars in US now. Toyota produces Corolla in every continent now, Australia, Asia, US, Europe and Africa. No one has complianed that local produced Corollas are inferior. In fact, the Thailand plant is supposed to be the toyota plant with least defects per car internationally.

    Conclusion ?? : Look at the car for yourself. A toyota fan would say that the altis is the best. A honda fan would say that the altis is outdated. Look, drive and decide for yourself if you like the looks, handling and the interior.

    I decided after looking and test driving twice. The quality is definitely there. The most impt parts of the car, the transmission, the engine and body are Japanese built. Who cares it is assembled in thailand, except that the same quality is evident.

    On the contrary, I decided against, e.g. lancer cos the quality was very different ... try other cars like suzuki, kia etc to see ... simple things like sound of door closing ... or the feeling when you sit inside ....

    It is your $70,000 or $80,000. Whatevery you buy, u must make sure you are happy with your decision.....

    3. Transmission is smooth with CVT

    4. Leg room are good for tall pple

    5. I think the back is nicer as compare to Altris, Corolla and Civic.

    Bad Point.
    1. Engine are noisy especially when driving 90km/h and above

    2. Audio Unit are lousy especially when listen to radio (poor reception)

    3. Engine is old but improved version of old, trusty GLX

    4. Pickup is so-so only

    For me, to buy a car since its purpose is to ferry pple. The most important factor in order are as follows:

    1. PRICE

    2. Maintenance cost

    3. Fuel Consumption

    Cos I am riding a bike now, I prefer to get the above points to ease my pocket.

    For pple who prefer accel, styling and speed. I think should not be looking at 1.6 cos 1.6 litre saloon cars are not known to be accel 0-100km/h under 10 sec except for the Turbo version of Ignis and YRV.

    If comfort, accel, speed and look are what u looking for. 2.0litre sporty car like Galant, Audi A4 or continental cars are what you should be looking at.
