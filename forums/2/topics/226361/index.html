  • FD-Civic Boy
    31 Dec 06, 22:24
    You can get the specs at www.carbuyer.com.sg.

    But I can remember some figures offhand.

    The civic 1.6 gives a power of 125bhp. This is a notch or 2 higher than its Japanese/Korean competitors(the other 1.6s normally give 107-109BHP), but bear in mind it is also heavier. Personally, I feel that if u wanna race, get the 1.8 or 2. Or wait for the Type R.

    Accessories compared to 1.8? Besides the smaller engine displacement:
    - it lacks HID lamps, indash 6-CD changer(u get an Alpine singledin model),
    -Paddleshifts,
    - Steering-wheel audio controls,
    -Side mirror light indicators,
    - and Aluminum rims(u get steel rims, nicer looking, arguably).
    -1.6 gets electronic powered steering, compared to hydraulic for 1.8. (Note the 2.0 variant gets electronic steering too)

    Otherwise, mechanically, the 1.6 engine is very similar to the 1.8 variant.

    Overall, it is a good car. Great looks (beauty is in the eyes of the beholder). Quiet smooth engine. 5-speed auto transmission (competitors give 4!). Big on the outside and inside. Good build quality. Great suspension (independent wishbone!) Responsive steering.

    Originally posted by FD-Civic Boy:
    What is an e-throttle? Flipped thru the Civic catalogue and found nothing on it. Google only made me more confused. Mind educating me?
    E throttle means that your gas pedal isn't wire/hydraulically operated but elecronically.

    In a normal car, you stepping onto the gas pedal, pulls a wire than in turn pulls the throttle of the engine.

    E-throttle means your gas pedal is electronic, so it should have a potentiometer which sense how much you step and in turn sends electronic signals to the engine to how much it should throttle.

    That's why FD's pedal is like a foot-floor based pedal unlike normal cars where pedals are "suspended" by a metal bar.
