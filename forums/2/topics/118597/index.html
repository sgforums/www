    Get those cars 7 - 8 years of age, then you scrap of after 2 to 3 years. If budget still too low, then 17 - 18 years of age.

    Car sharing don't work as you need a car to drive to work

    OPC don't work unless you leave your house before 7am and leave your office after 7pm.

    I am not too sure about long term rental, but weigh in these factors to compare with rental rates, you should probably be able to see which is cheaper. owning or renting

    Road Tax - usually dun have to pay for rental
    Insurance - usually not included in rental rates, you should have an option to buy or not. check the excess
    Maintenance - Check with the rental company or person on this. If nothing is damage, you should not need to pay anything.

    Other factors like parking, petrol and instalments / rental rates is your cost for both rental and owning.

    Hope this helps
