    Hi,

    Theoritically, you're not able to get a new car cos the downpayment should at least be 20%. The car dealer might be able to do something if your downpayment is less tahn 20% but if you are talking abt 3k or less, I'm not too sure what they can do. Also, its not worthwhile as the interest of the car is gonna kill you. I would suggest you get a 2nd hand car somewhere near the region of 5 yrs old.

    Lets take a 1.5l Korea car for example (more budget), a 5 yrs old car will cost you ABOUT 32k for example (Will be cheaper if you buy direct from owner rather than go through dealer). And if you downpay 3k, you'll loan abt 29k for 5 yrs. Thus it'll work out, take that the interest is 2.2%:-
    29000x2.2% =638 /per yr (interest only)
    638x 5 yrs = 3190/for 5 yrs
    So add the interest to your loan of 29000, it will be a total of S$32190 for 5 yrs.
    So 1 yr you pay 32190/5 = S$6438/yr
    Mnth will be 6438/12 = S$536.50

    Understand? If you base on 65k for a brand new car, and downpay 3k, lets say acceptable = 62000 loan. 10 yrs interest will be slightly higher, so lets say 2.4% (Me not sure, but not advisable to loan 10 yrs one, for a car, not worth it). Your calculation will be:-
    62000x2.4%=1488/per ye (interest only)
    1488 x 10 yrs loan = 14880
    14800+ loan = 14800+62000= 76880
    So 1 yr = 76880/10 = 7688
    1 mnth = 7688/12= 640.66
    But the total you pay for the car is 79880 for a 65000 car cause of interest.

    Base on your 65k car, if you get 7 yrs loan (will be slightly less than 10 yrs, so lets say 2.3% interest) will be:-
    62000x2.3= 1426
    1426x7= 9982
    Mnthly will be: [(62000+9982)/7]/12=$892/per mnth
    However, your total paying will be 65000+9982=74982 for the car, which mean you save S$4898 for the cars in 3 yrs, though you pay S$252.00 more.

    Get the calculation? Hope this example helps.
