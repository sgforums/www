My Impressions of E200 Kompressor

  • VBlaster
    27 Nov 04, 12:02
    Was looking to upgrade from my pevious gen Cefiro V6 2.0 and tested BMW 520i, Audi A6 2.4, Jaguar S Class 2.5, and Volvo S80. I ended up with Merc E200 Kompressor.

    BMW 520
    Very nice driver's car. I was actually set on getting this. Handling was great along the bends behind NUH. But the interior is very simple and not as luxurious as expected. Legroom was so so.

    Jaguar S Class
    Good engine and nice retro looks. Interior is luxurious and very British. Too retro and British for me... so

    Volvo S80 2.0
    Good 5 cylinder ligt pressure turbo engine. Interior is no nonsense, but not really luxurious. Handling was not as good as the rear driven options from Germany. Value for money option.

    Audi A6 2.4
    Just heard it's in this weekend, but I don't want to wait anymore. I like the looks of the A6, ad I'm sure it's a good choice.

    Merc E200 Kompressor
    I always thought Mercs design and drive were dated. I went to the showroom on the insistence of my wife with a bias against Merc, but surprisingly, I was slowly won over. First tried the facelifted C Class C200K, and its adept handling pleasantly surprised me. Next, we tried the E200 K. Legroom was generous, interior was luxurious, elegant and modern.

    Drive was a tad below BMW 520 (IMO), but much better than the S80. The Kompressor engine, although only 1.8l, is supercharged to give power and torque of a V6 2.4 engine. With Lancaster balancer and twinpulse, it's pretty quiet, although there is a sporty rumble from the Kompressor when pressed.

    Having driven it for a while, the drive is getting better. Probably it's adapting to my way of driving.

    The irony? I'm paying less road tax and fuel consumption than my old Cefiro V6 2.0![/b]

    E200 K has higher bhp and torque than Accord 2.4. It has about the same bhp as Cefiro V6 2.4, and higher torque.

    So yes, it has more than enough power to pull fully loaded. Of course, don't expect white knuckle driving experience as it's not meant to be that way

    E200K (1.8l supercharged engine)
    Power: 163 bhp 120 kW (5500 rpm)
    Torque: 240 Nm (3000 to 4000 rpm)
    FC: 11.6 / 6.1 / 8.4 litres/100km (city, extra urban, combined)

    E240 (2.6l V6)
    Power: 177 bhp 130 kW (5700 rpm)
    Torque: 240 Nm (4500 rpm)
    FC: 15.4 / 7.4 / 10.5 litres/100km (city, extra urban, combined)

    What you'll notice is that the 1.8l supercharged engine produces very respectable output, and more importantly, is able to produce its max power and torque at relatively lower rpms (the more useful range that we use day to day anyway).

    Also, FC for E200K is very good...

    The previous E200 K had a 2.0l supercharged engine that is pretty rough. The new 1.8l has the same bhp and better torque at lower rpm. It also has 2 new features: Lancaster balancer that balances the vibrations inherent in a 4 pot design, and twinpulse (2 sparks per cylinder for better combustion).

    Build quality of the new Merc E200K (and also the face lifted C200K) is impeccable. There are many subtle quality touches that grow on you. Like soft glow puddle lights on the door when opened, cabin lights that fade gently on and off, auto rain and lighting sensors that actually work well, soft ambient and floor/foot lighting... and I could go on for a long time...

    One point to note is: the system will adapt to your driving. The test car is probably not very responsive as it would undergo many different driving styles, with most being conservative as most test drivers are careful and not likely to floor the car. After 1 week of driving, my E200K is definitely more responsive, and kicks in easily when I prepare to overtake.
