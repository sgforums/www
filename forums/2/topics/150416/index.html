    1 Sep 05, 12:02
    A car in Singapore is both, a luxury and a necessity. Here's why:

    It's a Luxury because you would not have to arrive at work already sweaty and moody from rushing for seats in MRT and squeezing with other commuters.

    It's a Necessity because our Public Transport, though touted to be WORLD CLASS, still has plenty room for improvements - such as buses should arrive on time more often, and the LRT/MRT should breakdown less often. While Taxi drivers should stop playing with Peak Hours to pick up commuters, and drive safely instead of recklessly.

    Driving a car doesn't necessarily mean you can sleep longer and arrive early. Instead, it's the other way around; you need to plan your daily route, get up a little earlier to beat the rush hour jam, ERP, and get that parking space at your office. Otherwise, you'd be stuck in jam, get charged for ERP, and then arrive late to realize you can't find a convenient parking lot, then go see your boss's black face.

    Yes haviong a car is luxurious, comfortable and privacy assured, but it has it's own frustrations and stresses.

    Driving, needs a lot of pre-planning. Unlike taking Public Transport.
