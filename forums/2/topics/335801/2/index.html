    Brake pad compounds are made of varying materials, however, the heat integrity is only as good as the compound with the lowest melting point. A good example would be phenolic resin, used commonly as a binder in brake pad material. However, at temperatures of more than 450 deg c, the resin will char and evaporise, causing the entire pad to lose its structural integrity.

    So say a normal, basic brake disc made of cast iron (Fe, Si, Mn) has a melting point of more than 900 deg c, so tell me, do you think under extreme braking conditions which cause the brake disc itself to warp, scar, crack or melt, will the pad give way first, or will the disc give way first?
