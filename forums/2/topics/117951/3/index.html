    Originally posted by InSidiousGG:
    U wanna go fast without changing engines..
    1:body parts as bumpers, side skirts hoods and spoilers--- a must.. coz of downforce.. cut through air faster

    do u realise what a contradiction this is? "coz of downforce.. cut thru air faster"

    with downforce, there is drag. u cut thru air SLOWER. if u want to cut thru air faster, u need to minimise drag, hence u need to minimise downforce as well. want to go faster? giving your car more drag is definately NOT the way to go.

    Originally posted by InSidiousGG:
    2:Rims..most likely 17" and above coz larger rims means handling power and change in speed increase. tires of course..
    larger rims means they are heavier, unless u buy the expensive ones which are made of exotic alloys. heavier = slower. comprehendo?

    at least u got the "change in speed increase" part partially correct. "change in speed increase" can be summarised into one word: ACCELERATION. And if you get larger rims and increase the overall diameter of your wheel, assuming weight remains constant, your ACCELERATION will be SLOWER.

    as for tyres, do know that the grippier a tyre is, the higher it's rolling resistance. what does this mean? It means that if u put better tyres on your car, it will be SLOWER.

    Originally posted by InSidiousGG:
    3:Supension..Common sense..naturally it will increase.
    common sense? it will increase what? suspension does not actually increase bhp or speed in a straight line. it may only improve cornering speeds, and that is assuming it has been properly set up. it does not make your car faster in a technical sense.
