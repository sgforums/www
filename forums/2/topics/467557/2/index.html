    Song Seng Tze, 31, was charged in court on Thurs (Mar 28) with eight charges, seven of which are traffic offences including speeding, dangerous driving and driving under disqualification.


    Song, who is self-employed, is accused of driving at 190kmh from Punggol to Shenton Way, along Kallang-Paya Lebar Expressway, on Monday evening and failed to stop when ordered by a police officer along Tampines Expressway (TPE).

    The Straits Times reported that he then drove at high speed from to East Coast Parkway right up to Marina Boulevard where he hit a stationary police motorcycle and two police cars.


    Song is also accused of driving while he was under a 10-year driving ban imposed from July 2010; driving without insurance coverage; failing to stop when his car was involved in a collision with the police cars; and failing to render assistance.


    An eighth charge states that he was absent from his full-time national service duties at Singapore Civil Defence without leave from November 2011 to March this year. Bail of $20,000 was allowed. His next court date is on April 18.
