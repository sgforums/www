    Juz remember dealer's mark up the price (e.g COE $22k, car selling for $28k) for the body, but insurance company may not recognise that as the market value.

    Let's say you bought Third Party + Fire/Theft, and ur car got stolen or burnt to a crisp, I doubt the insurance will pay much or anything for the body. (Esp. if u spent money modding it, they dun recognise ur mods). So effectively, u are only getting ur COE rebate back. (Sian, mite as well dun buy insurance...if only...heh)

    So renewed COE cars are a bit 'risky' in terms of their value and for insurance purposes. But if nothing happen, the vehicle well-maintained, its actually a good buy compared to new car. One can opt for those more 'performance-oriented' cars like Civics or if can afford, an RX-7 why not. Most of us can't afford a new 'sports/sporty car', so one way is to get a renewed COE one.
