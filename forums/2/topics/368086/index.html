    Originally posted by coldzero:

    but why isnt the answer for question 3 C? C is also possible righht?

    Hi coldzero,

    We assume that the long vehicle is already on the right. Since it's a long vehicle, the driver would have long ago changed lane to the right way before he reach the U-Turn (as long vehicles hard to lane change).

    So to turn effectively when the long vehicle is in the right lane just next to the kerb, the long vehicle will need to turn left a bit before attempting to U-turn due to the wider turning radius.. Or else will mount kerb or pavement.

    If answer (C) was: "move left before turning right" then the answer can be accepted.

    U-Turns are all on your right, instead of left  = )

     

    Hope this sounds clear to you.
