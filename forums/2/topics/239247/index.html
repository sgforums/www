    from some website...:
    There was no RB30DET, only an RB30E - in Nissans - and an RB30ET - in the VL Holden Commodore. The lack of a "D" in the name means it's SOHC and 2 valves per cylinder. The conversions that are done with this engine involve fitting a twin cam RB head (from an RB26DETT or RB25DET, usually) to the RB30E or usually RB30ET block.
    There was a RB28DETT, which was found in the Nismo R33 Skyline 400R. The displacement was increased from 2.6L to 2.8L.
