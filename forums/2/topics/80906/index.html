    In a sense, I agree with you, but I disagree that the drivers of yore are necessarily better than today's. In fact I feel that today's drivers are better, skillwise, and possibly in terms of physical fitness as well. The reason is that although technology has improved to allow average drivers to be better, it has also allowed good drivers to be legendary which is simply not possible with older technology. In other words, technology has raised the bar and on the whole, everyone is improving.

    In the 1920s, it's not possible for a combat airplane to pull 9Gs because the airframe will simply come apart. Later on with the advent of better material engineering the plane can do 15Gs but the pilot will probably die first.

    So, in that sense, if you apply it to motorsports, it's similar. Using an old race car, with poor tires, suspension technology etc, it could probably only do a corner at 80 kmh, but today's cars can do that at 140 kmh, does that not raise the limit? If the driver exceeds the limit, a spinout is still a spinout, doesn't matter if you have electronic assistance or not, everything still has to obey the laws of physics.
