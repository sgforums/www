    Originally posted by HaYasHiBaRa:
    Wow. 21 replies overnight. Hahaha. Well I don't know much about the STi and the DCCD. Care to explain that? Car noobie here...
    DCCD is Driver Controlled Center Differential, a feature exclusive to STi. It allows the driver to control the power distribution between the front and rear wheels. i.e. it's possible to direct more torque to the rear wheels and have the car behave more like a FR instead of an AWD. There is an automatic mode where the traction control is left to the computer. Technically speaking this system is more difficult to implement than the SAYC on the Evo since the latter only controls how long before the center differential opens. In other words, the STi has even more electronic assistance than the Evo.

    Another example is AVCS, which is a Subaru-developed variable valve timing. The 4G63 engine in Evos do not have any variable valve timing.

    Yet another example is the USDM STi which uses electronic DBW throttle. The list goes on.

    So... to your friend who says Evo drivers can't drive due to electronic assistance, please go back and educate him. I would be very interested to know what's his excuse for back pedaling.
