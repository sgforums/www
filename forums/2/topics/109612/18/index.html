    My opinion is you don't need the TuffKote. I applied it some 15 years ago to my first brand new car. It was a nuisance. They sprayed some kind of 'oil' under the body and the inner parts of the door, even on the paint work. Thus you get your cleaning cloth often stained by the 'oil' and find yourself rubbing the 'oil' into other parts of the car body. Forget it. It's just a gimmick. The current range of new cars, especially from Japan/Europe/USA are normally already undercoated protected by the manufacturer against at least the salts they used to melt the snow. So don't worry. Our local conditions are not even that corrosive.

    The manual states that the car can use 92 octane but recommends 95 octane. I don't think 98 octane would fix your problem as it has higher octane levels meant for engines that are designed for it.

    Seems that after the diagnostics, the tech only 'suspect' contamination in the fuel. Logically, using higher octane petrol doesn't solve the problem.
