    Gaz, Heng has made a fine point, but it's an important one.

    Let's face it - how much F1 technology is actually applicable to road cars? Sure, the general concepts hold, and so we've now got active suspension and semi-automatic transmissions. On the other hand, I wouldn't hold my breath for a 19,000 rpm screamer with no mufflers hitting the roads any time soon.

    The fact of the matter is that in order to run at 19,000 rpm, the ignition, valve and lubrication systems are substantially different from anything you would find on a road car. Oh, by the way, you do realise that idle speeds for racing engines are much higher than on road cars' engines because they have no flywheel to speak of, don't you? Also, cooling systems in racing engines are built to tap as little energy from the engine as possible - there's no way an engine with such a cooling system would survive daily traffic.

    Anyway, before you get bored with these details, the bottom line is that not a whole lot of BMW's racing technology reaches their road cars, be it related to the engine or chassis - the environments for the two types of engines are just way too different. Heng's point is correct in that extrapolating BMW's F1 participation to the cars that you can buy is stretching it.

    As for the comment about not knowing much about racing, I'd really think twice before saying that to someone who not only actually races, but does motorsport engineering as well. Just a thought.
