    Hm, none (manual or auto) guarantees a faster or cheaper way.

    It really depends on your learning ability, whether you're fast or slow.

    It also depends on the school's availability of slots and whether you can afford to book all the slots you need all at once. If you can like fork out roughly about $2k at one go, then you can book all the slots at once, which means you can have lessons everyday and complete the whole course in less than 2 months. Then waiting for the test is usually 1 to 1.5 month for school.

    And yes, auto although easier to drive, it doesn't mean it's easier to pass. At BBDC, the passing rate of auto cars at first try is not really that high.

    Well, if you don't see yourself driving a manual car at all, then go for auto. As for manual, if you don't drive manual after you pass, very soon you'll forget how to drive a manual also.
