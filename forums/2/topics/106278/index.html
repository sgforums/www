    u will have to factor in the following costs :

    1) season parking ( if u stay HDB )
    2) parking coupons
    3) half/yearly road tax ( ard $500+ for 1.5/1.6l )
    4) maintenance ( normal servicing around $100 per 10,000km )
    5) monthly instalment
    6) once a year insurance premium ( $amt depends on yr age, occupation, marital status, NCB etc ..... )
    7) ERP/Summons/misc

    and of cos, the petrol cost.

    brand new Toyota Vios should not be a prob to maintain if u r earning around $3-4K, if u do not have any major commitments on hand ( kids, housing loan or other liabilities ). dun even think of buying one, if u draw less than $2K. its too risky and too much of a struggle when u factor in other things in your current life.

    its up to u to decide/plan your own spending budget/habit ...before u commit on a vehicle.

    juz my optimistic point of view.
    many forumers have many different way of views.

    If you can pay the HDB season parking (1 year), road tax (I year), insurance (1 year) all at the same and without having to beg or borrow money, then you could consider owning a car.

    There's servicing (much more expensive if done at agent) to be done. Those major servicings can really cost a dent in the pocket.

    There is not fixed minimum income to own a car. How well you can afford a car depends on how you spend your money, and how much you intend to save.
