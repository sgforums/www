    turbocharging a type S is not entirely without its merits. to begin with, the K20A3 engine has lower compression, less aggressive cam profiles and lower powerband. characteristics much akin to the turboed engines of late.

    problem is the total cost of ownership and legal risks involved. while honda engines have often been described as "bullet proof" in the automotive industry, turbocharging itself adds a whole load of stress on the engine internals. not only are u changing the entire volumetric efficiency of an engine dat was built for NA applications, u also incur all sorts of hidden costs in terms of maintenance (worn out clutch, gearings driveshafts) and reliability (engine blowing after sustained boost).

    trust me. it took almost half a decade after top fuel successfully created a 550 ps VTEC + turbo B series engine before local boys were able to run similar engines without blowing an engine along the way.

    can u live with the risks of getting caught ($500 fine at least) or blowing an engine?
