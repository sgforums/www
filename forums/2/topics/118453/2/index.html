    when NOS is used, there is an acceleration, hence G forces are felt. otherwise, when u're travelling at 200km/h, u won't feel anything if u're travelling in a straight line. but when u're driving at 200km/h and make a turn, there is lateral acceleration, so u feel the "cornering forces". when a helicopter makes a turn, u will feel the forces acting on your body as well.

    one thing tho, a car is much nearer to the ground, hence stationary objects whizzing by provides a sense of speed, even tho the body is physically experiencing no force at zero accelertion, while in a heli up in the sky, there are no near objects to provide the visual sensation of speed.
