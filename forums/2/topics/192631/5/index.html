    1. R18 is i-Vtec (cam phase +cam change)
    2. Cam phase is also know as VVTi(toyota/Suzuki),AVCS(subaru),VTC(nissan) it is actually cheap easy tech pioneered by Ford in 1962
    3. Cam change pioneered by Honda in 1980s
    4. Vtec patent ended abt 5 years back; till date only Porsche,BMW and Toyota has successfully adopted Vtec system in their cars.
    5. Toyota's "i-Vtec" is VVTi-L last seen in Celica SSII 1.8 Block. Now discontinued as it is too complicated and expensive to produce. VVTi-L now reserved in expensive Lexus range
    6. BMW's new inline 6 engine (R6) uses digital Vtec system; again complicated and heavy, they solved by incorporating aluminium alloy.
    7. Honda's relevance is that it is still the only car manufacturer to introduce true racing technology at affordable prices (unless you consider Porsche, BMW are cheap)
    8. Choice between SOHCorDOHC is just power, if you can get the same power from a SOHC then of coz SOHC. (Notice Honda's SOHC pumps out more power than most DOHC) DOHC also more complicated and higher FuelConsumption. Plus point for DOHC is more receptive to modding due to its extra head.
    9. overall comparing a BMW M3 with WRX STi/S2000/Evo not good; Price of 1 M3 can get you all 3 cars.
