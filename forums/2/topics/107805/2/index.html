    23 Jan 05, 20:28
    Curious about why people get so hung up on the changes between each generation of Evos.

    Generally, every year or so, every car company will try to refresh their line up of cars. Usually this involves a minor facelift of some sort aka new bumpers or new headlights or better trim levels.

    With the Evo 9, Mitsubishi has gone one step further by incorporating
    a. a MIVEC head on the 4G63 engine block to provide variable valve timing, providing a much flatter torque curve (vs std twincam on earlier models)
    b. a magnesium/titanium turbo (vs titanium on earlier models)
    c. improved aerodynamics on front bumper and rear diffuser on bumper
    d. rear spoiler gurney flap
    among others of minor but incremental improvements that will undoubtedly make the 9 faster than the stock 8.

    IMHO, this is going waay better than most facelifts.

    BTW, the Evo 1/2/3 were generally based on the same platform. The 4/5/6 also shared the same CP platform and it is not too far a stretch to imagine that the 7/8/9 will also share the same CT platform.
