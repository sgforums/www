    7 Apr 07, 08:48
    HIS name, picture and contact details have been splashed all over the Internet and newspapers.

    But the man who has been fingered as the driver of the fateful Mazda MX-5 has remained silent through it all.

    The New Paper called the main telephone line of his company on Wednesday and asked for him by name.

    The receptionist transferred the call immediately.

    The man subsequently answered 'Yes' when we asked for him by name. But when we told him we were from The New Paper, his tone changed.

    'Please don't call my office line,' he snapped.

    When we asked if he had seen the earlier press report on the recent Internet talk about the crash, he said: 'There are a lot of things wrong about it, but I don't wish to comment.'

    He then hung up.

    We also called the handphone number posted on the forums, but it had been disconnected.

    The New Paper team then went to his workplace in Tuas for a third try to clarify claims with him.

    In the company's parking lot was a yellow Fiat Punto HGT - the same model of car that his father had reportedly bought for him shortly before the accident.

    At about 5.45pm, three young men, none of whom resembled the picture of the supposed driver, came out of the building and got into the Fiat.

    One of the men, who refused to give his name, said he was a worker there. He got out of the car to speak to us while the other two drove off.

    He claimed that the man named on the forums had quit the company early last year.

    He also claimed that the supposed driver had sold the yellow Fiat to one of them.

    'The speculation is just making trouble for our car. Please (tell the public), don't tailgate our car, it endangers our lives,' he said.

    But when The New Paper told him that we spoke to the supposed driver that morning through the office line, he changed the subject.

    ZOOMED OFF

    At 6.15pm, the yellow Fiat returned to the factory complex. Twenty minutes later, we saw that it was driven to the back of the factory. A metal gate was pulled behind it to block it from view.

    Five minutes later, a medium-built man came out of the building with a group of other men.

    We could not see his face as he was wearing a motorcycle helmet with the visor pulled down, his head lowered.

    Then he got onto a motorbike and zoomed off as we flagged at him to stop.

    At about 7.30pm, the security guard locked the gates of the complex.

    The New Paper had also sent an online message to the driver via his supposed forum-user identity, but he did not respond.
