    Hi
    Yes, the monthly instalment is about right, assuming the 10-year interest rate is about 2.7% PA.

    Assuming the Picanto is about 43K,
    After downpayment, the loan amount is about $38700
    At 2.7% PA, the total interest you pay for borrowing this amount over 10 years is about $10449
    So monthly instalment is ($38700 + $10449) / 120 months = Approx $410.

    Just want to add my 2 cents worth. The fixed costs of the car such as the actual price of the car are things you can see. But there are a lot of hidden costs such as maintenance, petrol, road tax, insurance, carpark (season and outside), interest for the loan and the number of years you are going to loan that you must take notice of because these are the costs that are actually going to kill you. The time when you breakeven your loan is also important.

    If you are even going to struggle a bit in paying just the instalments for the car, then please do not get one. You might end up making a huge loss if you find that you can't upkeep the car after a short time and have to be forced to sell it due to the loan. Lastly, know what you are getting the car for. Plain transport or whatever. Consider how long you will like the car you want to buy, FC, leg and boot space and whatever you consider to be important and compare it with your budget. You can narrow down your choices fairly easily then. Hope this helps
