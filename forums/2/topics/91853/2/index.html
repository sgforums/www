    The thing to do in mastering clutch control, is the point where it catches torque. Then make a mental mark at which point in release ( angle of left foot in relation to ankle ) that torque-pull sets in. The other portion of clutch control is regulating acceleration with the right foot. Most engine stalls are due to nervousness, anxious and declutching too fast.

    The best practice is on a slope, and best of all, use a deserted HDB multistorey carpark and practice taking off on one of the up-ramps. Or find a gradual slope somewhere and practice clutch control. I see a lot of panic-stricken people ( especially those who just got their license ) in shopping center basement carparks, struggling to control their clutch going up the ramp.

    It is indeed, EMBARASSING.
