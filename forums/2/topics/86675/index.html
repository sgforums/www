  • pie
    22 Jul 04, 16:27
    For accidents with injuries:

    Call police, remain at the scene.

    For non-injury:

    i think the most important detail to take down is the other party's license plate, because everything else can retrieved from it. If possible, take the driver's IC details too.

    Then visit the nearest IDAC for them to record down the damages to your vehicle. I believe they will ask for your vehicle insurance details which you should have in your vehicle and should help you with your claim form.

    Finally, confirm with your insurer where the vehicle should go for repair, give them a call.

    Things not to do:
    1. Do not bother arguing with the other driver, absolutely nothing is accomplished

    2. Do not attack the other driver

  • pie
    23 Jul 04, 09:25
    The investigators will not really seriously consider both sides' stories because either one could be making false accusations, just as you said. Instead they will look for 2 things, the areas where the cars were damaged and the statement of any independent eye witnesses (meaning people who were not in your vehicle but saw the accident).

    Generally, the vehicle whose rear got damaged has a pretty strong case.....abt 80-90%. Damage to the side gives you about 50-50 chances of winning. These were the usual rates according to IDAC.

    As for eyewitnesses, if they are not related to you...probably stronger case.

    For your case since the other person hit you from behind, you should probably make a claim to his/her insurance.
