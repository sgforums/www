    11 Jun 05, 14:53
    Originally posted by wh|te_@ng3|:
    can someone enlighten me if itz better for 'p' driver (which is me) to join car sharing compared to car rental.. if let's say i dun really drive that often? coz i kinda confuse on the usage of cityspeed thingy.. thanx in advance..
    First, the difference is that sharing you can take the car, drive a few hours ( as little as one hour), return the car and end of rental. You pay for how long you use + mileage charges, not to mention the annual fee and joining fee that you have already incurred. Renting you need to rent the car for a min of 24 hours.

    The decision that you make should be from a basis of the following factors:
    How often (nt so impt) and how long (more impt) you want to drive per rental or sharing.
    (one day, few hrs, few days)
    How much are you willing to pay each time for time behind the wheel
    (Note that sharing might not be cheaper even you use the car for lesser time)
    How much distance that you think you might cover for the rental / sharing?
    Are you willing / Can you fork out a sum of money of a few hundred dollars to pay for the membership, joining and p plate fee to drive

    In sum, car sharing's convenience and flexiblity comes with a cost that may put rental company into a favourable territory depending on how you orientate your priorities.

    I agree with pqr that no many car rental companies rent their car to p plate due to insurance considerations. But not to forget that there are private rental who are willing to rent a car to you (it's legal only on weekends and PH). You will need to ensure that the contract drawn does not put you in a disadvantage position.
