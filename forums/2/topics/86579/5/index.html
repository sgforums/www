    I really dont think that you guys should get so worked over about a few statistics about the 0-100 km/h sprint...So what if the Legacy might edge the Airtrek T in this apsect, or vice versa? They are both well-balanced performance cars, with the Legacy being almost a larger, more relaxed deriviant of the WRX in terms of general performance, while the Airtrek a larger, more practical detuned Mitsubishi Evolution hosting very accesible performance. They are both very good cars for the money, considering that they both offer a fair amount of luxury for the price ; having driven the regular Airtrek (almost indenticle interior to the A. Turbo) while examined the new Legacy up close.

    About the UK Xtreme times for the Airtrek Turbo-R, out of general estimation for a four-wheel drive 250bhp 1.5 tonne car, a sub seven second 0-100 might be possible. From the way I see it the only thing that is holding the car back in terms of straight line performance is its weight (some 70kg more than an Evolution 8 ), Automatic transmition which drains some of the torque output and large frontal area and resulting drag coefficent, which works against the car more after 180 km/h which might have explained the highway story. Of course, there are also ways to remove the regular 180 km/h limiter for Japanese cars

    Just be happy with what car you have, and be glad to have one especially in Singapore where a buying a BMW 318 could buy you a M3 elsewhere.
