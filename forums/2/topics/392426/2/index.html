    Originally posted by linwei20:

    It is like that. Even if you go to Malaysia or Thailand, Road Tax and insurance you cannot escape.

    Motor Insurance premium (paid yearly) is dependant on:

    - which company you go for

    - what they cover you

    - your age and driving experience

    - Car make and model

    - Category of car (Sports, MPV, Saloon, SUV etc)

    - No of airbags (maybe)

    - Engine Capacity (CC)

    - No Claim Discount (NCD)

    - Age of car

    - Claims history

    - Comprehensive coverage or 3rd party fire and theft (this is only for paid up car, ie. car fully paid for).

     

    It all depends, but ranges from about $600 - $6000.

     

    Road Tax (paid half yearly or yearly), is dependant on engine capacity and age of car.

     

    For petrol cars,

    a typical 1.6 litre car road tax about 760 per year.

    a typical 2.0 litre car road tax about 1210 per year.

     

    For Diesel cars only (not inclusive of commercial vehicles such as lorries, vans),

    road tax is (original road tax for specified engine capacity) + (diesel tax of 1.5 times egine capacity).

    lets say 1.6 litre diesel car, road tax is 760 + (1.25*1600) = $2760 (indicative figures)

    2 litre diesel car, road tax is 1210 + (1.25*2000) = $3710 (indicative figures)

    Actually,

    from what i observe, RON 92 / 95 / 98 / VPOWER (Shell context) is all equally clean. The only different between 92, 95 and 98 is the octane number. This is achieved by additives, which have been blended into the fuel that we pump at petrol stations.

    Basically, VPOWER is also RON 98, just that they put in some special additives to make your car more powerful (tried before in malaysia, indeed a significant increase in power).

    For fuel efficiency, the usual recommended grade of petrol for most cars will be RON 95, unless you driving COE car (RON 92) or sports car (Porsche, Ferrari, Lamborghini), then you need VPOWER.

    Pumping 98 DOES NOT necessarily improve your fuel consumption. Just follow the car manual instructions and recommended fuel types and you will be good.
