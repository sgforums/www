    Petrol discounts are always temporary and unsustainable in the long run. What is needed is a permanent reduction in petrol taxes. With rising crude oil prices, the govt can and should reduce tax rates accordingly without a loss in tax revenue. The public's perception that high fuel price are caused by oligopolistic oil companies is inaccurate. Oil companies in Singapore are in competition against each other and the price of petrol will be at an optimal market rate without govt intervention. The most significant cause of the unreasonably high price of petrol is the exhorbitant tax rate imposed by the govt. Since demand for fuel is very inelastic, the govt will feel no pressure to reduce petrol taxes. When fuel prices increase, they simply collect more revenue.
