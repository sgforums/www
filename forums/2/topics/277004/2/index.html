    Our Ref: LTA/P&P/F20.016.000/7938/sl

    PUBLIC FEEDBACK ON ELECTRONIC ROAD PRICING (ERP)
    FEEDBACK NUMBER: 507946

    We refer to your email of 23 August 2007.

    Please allow us to explain that we do not rely on ERP alone to relieve
    traffic congestion. The Land Transport Authority (LTA) adopt an integrated
    package of measures which includes traffic engineering such as road
    widening and modifying traffic schemes.

    However, measures like road widening alone are not sustainable solutions.
    Over time, more traffic is attracted onto the widened road and it becomes
    congested again. This is what happened on the Central Expressway (CTE) and
    the East Coast Parkway (ECP). For example, LTA widened the northbound CTE
    by one lane between the Pan Island Expressway (PIE) and Ang Mo Kio Avenue 1
    in 2002 to improve traffic speed. But within six months, congestion built
    up again.

    Similarly, LTA added one lane to the eastbound ECP in February 2007 between
    Marina South and Fort Road and within two months, the congestion returned
    as more people use these roads to take advantage of the improved speeds.

    We would like to share with you that roads are designed to provide
    operation efficiency, safety, comfort and convenience to the motorists. In
    order to achieve this, the design of roads will have to comply with the
    road geometric requirements. This is a set of road design criteria, as well
    as the professional application of these requirements to suit the
    topography and site constraints. The design of the CTE alignments is well
    within these geometric requirements.

    Due to land constraints, it is not always possible to have exit slip roads
    linking to all the roads in each direction. Hence, traffic lights near to
    the expressway exits may be necessary to distribute the outgoing traffic to
    different roads.

    Our traffic light system is adaptive to traffic flows, i.e. the appropriate
    amount of green time given to each direction of the junction/ signalised
    intersection is based on the traffic demand of that direction. This means
    that if the traffic flow on the direction exiting from the expressway is
    heavier, more green time will be given.

    We have forwarded your feedback on road hogging to the Traffic Police (TP).
    Their patrol officers will monitor the situation and take relevant
    enforcement action against road hoggers where appropriate. TP will include
    your feedback in their road safety public education efforts.

    We thank you for writing.

    Yours sincerely,
    Celine Quek (Ms)
    Assistant Manager
    Community Engagement & Feedback
