    Another tree falls, narrowly missing passer-by by few seconds
    By Mindy Tan

    May 25, 2007

    HAD she walked past the tree a few seconds later, she would have been crushed.

    Miss Balavenda (left) escaped death by a few seconds when the tree (above) fell just after she passed under it. -- Pictures: JONATHAN CHOO
    Miss Balavenda's heart was still thumping half an hour after her narrow escape.

    She had taken the day off as she was moving to a flat in Block 1, from another block nearby.

    At about 4.30pm, as she was pushing a trolley full of belongings along the footpath near Block 1, Dover Road, the eight storey-tall Angsana tree suddenly came crashing down.

    It fell across Dover Rise, blocking the footpath and much of the two-lane road. Fortunately, according to a police spokesman, no one was injured.

    By 5pm, National Parks Board maintenance contractors had arrived with chain-saws.

    'I am still very scared. It just missed me, if not my life would have been taken,' said Miss Balavenda, 29, a hospital environment service worker from India, who is planning to get married next January.

    Miss Balavenda (left) escaped death by a few seconds when the tree (above) fell just after she passed under it. -- Pictures: JONATHAN CHOO
    'I am supposed to return home to see my mother in December. I am definitely going to the temple tomorrow,' she added.

    The bride-to-be, who has been working here for four years, tried to call her Singaporean boyfriend, but could not reach him immediately.

    At the time the tree fell, there were no other pedestrians around, and no cars on the road.

    Local residents also said there was no strong wind at the time.

    However, the tree trunk appeared to be ant-infested.
