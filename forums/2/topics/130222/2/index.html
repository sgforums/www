    The best and only remedy is; reduce humidity inside vehicle. Humidity levels in Singapore is generally very high, usually above 60% at the lowest. In days like now, we're experiencing up to 80% humidity levels. Therefore, the air inside your vehicle is more than likely to be the same as oustide once you open the door. It takes hours for humidity level to drop once you shut the door and place two packs of 700ml Thirsty Hippo packs inside. And even this, will only reduce humidty inside to around 50% or below. Obvisouly, more dry packs you place inside car, the lower the humidty level will become.

    Smell comes from airborne bacteria, which collects in your aircon, and the ducts. If humidty is low, these particles cannot adhere to the walls of the aircon ducts, but the internal coils cannot be helped apart from periodic cleaning or servicing.

    Buying and installing a mini air-revitilizer helps remove particles that're in the air inside your vehicle, trapping them inside the fragrant solution in the revitalizer. Currently I use GoodAire's Car Revitalizer which is simply working wonderfully well. Dust and dirt particles are effectively trapped in the solution, and I simply pour it away and mix up a new batch each day in morning.

    The 2 to 3 Thirsty Hippo packs ensure that the interior humidity is kept low while driving and parking overnight. There's even NO sticky feeling on my steering wheel. in the morning, everything inside feels smooth and dry, and no smell. I can even still smell the new car smell after one year of use!

    These Thirsty Hippo dry packs lasts about 3 months, and is relatively cheap. No smell can last long inside my vehicle. With the air-revitilzer running, it can process away any odour in under 30 minutes. You can bring a fresh pack of Nasi Bryani in, and the smell will disappear after about 15minutes. NO smell can linger - even fart.

    It's the the combination of the dry packs and air-revitilizer.
