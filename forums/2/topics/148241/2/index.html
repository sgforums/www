    Here is my advice -

    Firstly, learn all the theory about safety, road regulations and such. Learn to control you're vehicle and be cohesive in the way you handle it (this does not mean drive at snail pace through every corner you take). Simple enough and it won't take much effort if your determined to drive.

    Then -

    Dont be afraid to use the accelerator. For some reason, most folks who take their license test here, and most P platers here, drive as if their car has blown a head gasket. Its there for a reason, and the testors are really sensative about this - as long as you are under the limit, accelerating swiftly is actually a positive attribute rather than something you can get marked down on.

    If you want to pass you're practical ttest, remember this, and please for my sake don't forget this once you get your P's as i've had enough of sitting behind jokers driving at 50 on the highway with nowhere to go. Lets not make everday driving in Singapore more lifeless and slow-motion than it already is.

    And thats it.
