KIMI RECORD SEES OFF WILLIAMS

  • HENG@
    12 Jan 04, 09:06
    Last Updated: Sunday, 11 January , 2004, 20:27

    Kimi Raikkonen set a new lap record to end Williams' domination of testing at Jerez.


    The Finn, driving the new McLaren MP4-19, pipped Marc Gene to top spot by two-tenths of a second.

    That was in spite of the fact that he suffered a massive engine failure in the morning which limited his running to 32 laps.

    Ralf Schumacher was third fastest in the new Williams FW26 ahead of the second McLaren of Pedro de la Rosa and Toyota's Ryan Briscoe.

    BAR suffered a disappointing day with Jenson Button and Takuma Sato propping up the timesheets. The duo both completed less than 40 laps while Sato suffered yet another Honda engine failure.

    Sunday testing times from Jerez

    1 RAIKKONEN McLaren 1m16.125s
    2 GENE Williams 1m16.341s
    3 SCHUMACHER Williams 1m16.769s
    4 DE LA ROSA McLaren 1m17.240s
    5 BRISCOE Toyota 1m18.562s
    6 BUTTON BAR 1m18.575s
    7 SATO BAR 1m19.866s

    courtesy of www.itv-f1.com
