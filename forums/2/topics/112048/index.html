    OD is like this sexy name given to the highest gear in an auto gearbox used for cruising
    simply put, OD is like the fifth gear in a manual gearbox.
    Leave it on for high speed cruising.
    For high speed overtaking, turning OD off is like forcing the auto box to drop a gear, hence more power and RPM goes up.

    In heavy traffic, Doesn't matter whether you leave it on or off,
    coz not likely the OD gear will kick in at low speed, even if it's on.
    Prob just the regular 1, 2, 3 or 4 auto gears will be used only.
    IMHO, leave it on at heavy traffic. Save the hassle.

    Just remember, O/D on (default) is the 4th gear in a 4 speed auto.

    O/D off (light lighted up on your dashboard) means that you have only go up to 3rd gear.

    As for rev and fuel economy, think whether you want to engage the 4th gear or 3rd gear when cruising on the expressway.

    Another tip to remember is that the correct mode you should be in when cruising or in any other normal situation is when the O/D light on your dashboard is off (O/D off light off = O/D on -you have up to 4th gear of the auto gearbox!!!)

    As for when to use O/D off (3rd gear) is when you need power for overtaking
    (think of the situation when you need to shift from 4th to 3rd gear)

    Hope this clear your confusion.
