  • ShutterBug
    29 Dec 04, 09:17
    1) Take down license plate of 2nd party
    2) Approach (not apprehend) 2nd party
    3) Obtain particulars and IC number (you have NO authority to demand IC from 2nd party)
    4) If unco-operative, call Police
    5) If co-operative, work out where to repair car
    6) Have the damag evaluated on same day, and make claim from 2nd party after obtaining repair quotation

    The above is based on accidents wherein it can be settled mutually. Otherwise, it's a draggy drawn-out affair to involve insurance companies.

    I side-swiped by a Malaysian driver driving a S'pore reg commercial van; he own up his carelessness, and paid for panel beating costs which amounted to S$350.00. All went smoothly - I held on to his Malaysian IC throughout.
