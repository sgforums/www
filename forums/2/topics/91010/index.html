    26 Aug 04, 17:30
    Hi skywalker,

    The U turn I would say its not the best performance for its size (comparing it to a bigger size car that I have driven - Toyota wish) but it is adequate for normal U-turn, not talking about illegal ones! Can't remember how many turn on steering I have to make.

    The steering feels very light,don't really like this. Though during cornering, I can feel the car corners well. Can't compare to a focus, but its responsive enough.

    The engine noise can be on the high side. On the gear tranmission, tt has a hold button which holds the gear (so it cant automatically shift gear up) for overtaking purpose. I haved rev the engine to 6000rpm and you can feel that it can still be pushed further. This is definitely a plus point for drivers who doesnt have a heavy foot, a plus point for drivers who love to rev with auto cars!

    Apart from that, the luggage compartment, on the rear seat, you could get the seats up to free more space, lifting the rear seat up and it levels with the boot .Some cars, you cant do that. Makes me feel that the designers have considered the aspect of luggage space in designing MZ2.

    There are much more but I will have to write in on the next post. Back to work.

    Have a good day.

    Hi,

    Mazda 2 were also one of my choice, have checked out the lastest promotion of $300, and realised that it's only for the first 14 months, 15th month will be around $760 and $850++ for the rest of the months!!! Which is consider very high unless you are very sure you will be rich by that time.

    Have also checked the picanto and found the boot is too small.

    Beside that, I have also checked out some other models:

    Mazda 3 -
    Excellent body! But the engine imported here is only 1.6 with Auto which has made the car very underpower and fuel comsumption is high!

    Toyota Vios -
    Very soft body, can easily feel the body metal is warping when you gently push with your fingers. We bought a Sonata for my mother in law in brunei, same problem and the engine compartment is really cheap.

    Proton Gen2 -
    Very nice design and powerful engine, also very good in the handling as well. Just before wanted to book it, I read some news that a Gen2 just caught in fire after 4 days of ownership. And also found some very negative feedback about the materials used in the interior. like the door handle, handbrake and cheap plastic all over the place.
    http://www.suaramalaysia.com/archives/2004/08/gen2_on_fire_1.html

    Peugeot 206-
    One of my dream car! Top quality all over and Superior sales service! The SE explains every single point of the car and is very professional. But my wife doesn't like the small interior space. Sighh!!!

    Kia Cerato-
    Looking nice, but our company's Korean made Mercedes Van has given us so much trouble which has made me not interested in Korean made vehicle anymore.

    Toyota IST-
    Very nice design and good value in 2nd hand market, but priced at $76,000 which I don't think I can afford it.

    My final choice is - Daihatsu YRV
    Same engine used in Toyota Echo, looks equally nice as Mazda 2, lower fuel comsumption at 19km/L, and the most affordable car fully made in Japan.

    This is after few months of research for almost all of the cars below $80K.
