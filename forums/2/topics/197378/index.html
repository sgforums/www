    if u enter the S from the tail end of the S (turning right then left n finally exit on the right >> S )

    1. keep ur vehicle positioned more on the left (if turning right and vice versa)

    2. remember as u turn, ur front 2 wheels turn first and followed by ur rear 2 wheels which acts as a pivot. by this, i mean that if u are too near to either side of the S-curve, ur rear will hit the kerb/mount kerb which is immediate failure.

    3. when u steer, don't be panicky. try to steer slowly as u accelerate n clutch at the same time. one point to note (personally), is that u need to see ur front of the vehicle clearing ur side before u make a turn. it is very similar to a parking theory/practical when u are abt to come out from a parking lot.

    otherwise, ask ur instructor and go more rounds in the circuit. practise makes perfect.

    It's best if u practise and use your own judgement skill + some tips given to you earlier to get thru the 'S' course as it'll benefit you when ur out on tiny little road one day where you have to learn to squeeze.

    BUT if you're really desperate and juz thinking of how to get thru this 'S' course in your circuit to pass, here's the trick and tips:

    - from your driver point of view, focus on your "L" plate in front and at all times while driving in and out from the 'S' course, make sure your 'L' plate is always cruising on top of the outter (right side) kerb ...

    - if your "L" plate didn't touch the kerb in your eye point of view, your rear wheel could most prob hit the kerb, and if your "L" plate go beyond the kerb (hit grass patch area from your eye point of view) then most prob your front wheel will hit the kerb

    So as much as possbile, drive thru the 'S' course having your "L"plate on the kerb from your eye point of view, of course not literally ...

    PS: This wun work if your "L" plate is positioned on the extreme left or right of the front car, if it's position just on the right side on the car license plate, still within centre area, it'll do.

    Gd luck.
