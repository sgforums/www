    There is such a thing as Defensive Parking.

    Some set standard of measures, is better than none, when parking to avoid dents. When you park so close to neighboring vehicles, be prepared to be dented.

    Take corner lots, park as close as you can to the curbside, and make at least 3f or more space between yourself, and the neighboring vehicle.

    If the safe lot, or corner lot is far from where you're going, such as entrance to a shopping center, sacrifice the convenience of parking next to entrance. Parking in between cars always increases the risks of dents. The next car that parks next to yours, may be driven by an idiot who can't park to save his life. Or worse; ladies who park just so they can get out confortably from the driver's side, but leave a hair's breadth on the other side.

    I'm not saying this to insult the intellect of the opposit gender, but I am saying so from experiences and observations.
