    My thoughts on the Vietnam special:

    Top Gear normally set out to prove something, to express their views, or to conquer some challenges. They travelled to the north pole to prove a car could do it, they crossed Botswana in cars to prove you dont need a 4WD, They went to America to expose their culture and show that it’s not the car, it’s the relationship you build with it that matters.

    This time though, they just kind of … were there. The bikes weren’t tested at all, the only challenge was to drive across the country and, excuse the modern cliche, how hard can that be?

    As such, I found the guys trying too hard to make something of the journey, to give it some raison d’etre. I get it that Vietnam is beautiful and has a very powerful history, but why did they go there? I still don’t know what the point of the special was aside from … having a special? The cinemetography was there, the epic music was there, but I felt like there was no clear purpose driving it all.
