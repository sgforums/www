    taking 6 cylinders engines for examples...
    To put it simply, straight-6s are capable of higher horsepower ie, 800-1000bhp GTRs and Supras. The pistons travel just up and down.... 3 up 3 down at the same time. imagine a hammer hitting a nail... the greatest effect is when hitting directly above it.

    V6s has the advantage of higer torque and more cubical design; more compact than the long straight-6. Power-wise, why it doesnt develop as much horsepower as the S6 is cos the end stroke ends up at 2 places; the downstroke of one side of 3 cylinders goes to, say bottom left while the other ends at bottom right. the combustion energy is more spreaded out, unlike the S6 which is more towards a focal point, which is basically down straight. But it gives out more torque; each downstroke at is 2 location, from left and right... hope you get the idea

    Both types have their pros and cons, so you cant really say which one is definately better.

    Why BMW engines are more powerful is cos they have a complex electronic engine managment system, that continously makes adjustment to the cams and valves to give peak performance. The Mini One and Mini Cooper both uses the same identical engines, yet the One only produces 90bhp while the Cooper puts out 120bhp. The difference is the engine control unit, that controls how the engine runs. The One has a springy performance with better fuel economy while the Cooper has a more punchy feel.
