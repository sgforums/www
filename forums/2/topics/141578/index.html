    OMV (open mkt value) = A figure decided by Singapore customs based on the car's purchase price, freight, insurance, handling + other charges etc..

    ARF (additonal registration fee) = 110% of the OMV for cars registered after Mar 2004

    PARF (preferential ARF, aka scrap value) = 50% of ARF paid at the end of 10 years (if scrap earlier get more, refer to onemotoring website) or can directly calculate as 55% of OMV

    so PARF is NOT equal to OMV.

    Wif the OMV, which most car dealers put, you can then calculate the ARF and then subsequently the PARF.

    If the car belongs to the old school, I think its May 2002 and before or something, then directly 80% of the OMV is the PARF value at end of 10 years.

    fyi, for a new corolla altis, the depreciation is only about 5.2k if you drive for full ten years.

    however, if you sell your new car after 1 or 2 yrs, you will loose more than 5.2k a year (assuming coe remains at current level)

    if you intend to change car in two years time, you can consider the car above (base on price only) but bargain down the price. To me, it doesn't make sense to buy a 2nd hand car if its annual depreciation is higher than a new car.
