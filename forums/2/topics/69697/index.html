    My family had a volvo limo when i was younger, i didn't have an inkling of cars back then but i thought it was pretty decent. Then came the 80s-90s period where Volvo acquired the reputation of being the 'safest' car in the market. That was about the last i heard about Volvo i suppose.

    In recent context, Volvo made a gross and i do mean gross mistake in coming out with their s60-80 models which featured that 'chinese coffin' look (if you don't know what i'm referring to, take a closer look at volvos on the road, from the rear.)

    Currently, their SUVs are pretty decent, only matched by cayenne and RX300. But one model i realised that hasn't received enough fanfare is their s40 model. Something like what the IS200 is to lexus, a small sporty coupe type of car. I saw one on the roads the other day, which puzzled me cause i thought it wasn't brought in. It had the sports edition i've seen on the net, with a spoiler and sports rims and stuff, in black.

    Now sometimes, when we're overly exposed to japanese cars, good solid cars like the s40 gets ignored and glanced over. So for those who are looking for a 'young' chiongster or rather excecutive car with a budget of 100k to 125k, i highly reccommand this classy alternative to your IS200s and Beemer 3 series.
