    I am able to change gear around 2.5-2.8rpm for my kelisa and yet still keep up with normal traffic. Can be faster than most at the red light. I also just started diving manual after 4 years of non-driving. At first, I rev too. Then later when I saw how my bro handle my car, I tried to shift at lower rpm. But like most un-experience divers, you will experience engine coughing.

    This is my usual pick up routine now. 1st to 2nd, 2.0-2.5k rpm. 2nd to 3rd, ~3k rpm. 3rd to 4th, ~2.8k rpm. 4th to 5th, ~2.5krpm. Usually if I am the first car at the traffic light, I will be able to shift into 3rd gear before I exit the junction. Once cross, I would be in the 4th.

    I guess it is about the speed you shift your gear. Try to create a high enough acceleration so that when you clutch out (free gear), there is enough momentum to roll the car without losing much speed. And of course, don't take forever to upshift. When reach crusing speed, always use 5th gear even when you are only travelling at 60kph. Down-shift when there is a need to accelerate.

    Keep playing the gears and clutch. I have to, cause kelisa is really weak. Practices makes perfect, engine stall is common for the first few weeks, you will overcome it. Best if you can get someone experience you know who can drives smoothly to have a test drive on your car (the normal way, not reving one). I actually let 4 others drive my car during my "run-in" stage just to check out their driving method and learn from them.

    Hey rumie not an expert but i drive a stick too; normally what i do is to keep my eye on the speedo, not the tacho. and to use the lower gears to accelerate rather than stretch it in 3rd and 4th. i used to have that problem as i shifted up too early, resulting in sluggish moving off. try shifting up from 2nd to 3rd around 35kph, then to 4th at around 45-50. thus the engine should only be spinning around 2.3-2.6k before shifting. should give you a balance between FC and decent speed. don't worry about stalling, just try to rev it a bit more and release the clutch slowly, till the day you instinctively know where the biting point is. might i ask what capacity car are you driving? mine is a 1.6L, so you might need to adapt a bit. but dont try accelerating hard in 5th or 4th, it's slow and it strains the engine.
