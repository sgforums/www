  • Eckeck1996
    29 Jun 10, 21:05

    I have one before.... good grip and handling on the road. The drive feels good and the sensitivity on the steering is a class of its own. Compare to the SLK, the SLK is smoother and the drive is distinctive Mercedes - lots of comfort but no feel of the road handling/ground and power. You would probably not feel the difference if you were driving the C class and the SLK. For me, a sportscar needs to have the feel of the raw power when you need it and the porsche boxster S with 310 BHP provides this adequately.

    Most of the comparison for the boxster is always been with the Audi TT. For me, both cars are equally good but at the end of the day, a Porsche is a Porsche. The logo at the front of the bonnet tells all. Moreover, if the car is from Stuttgart Auto, you get to join the exclusive Porsche Club and to improve your driving skills, there is always the Porsche Driving School in Mount Cotton Australia.
