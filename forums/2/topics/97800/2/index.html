    My recommmended items:

    1: Suspension
    These are a must for good handling. Lower and stiffer is the key here.
    (Not too low unless you want your undercarriage to be covered in scratches )

    2: Wheels (aka rims)
    Reduce weight and (if design is selected properly) makes the car look great without overdoing it. Upsize it by 1 or 2 inches.

    3: Tires
    I find that this is one of the most-neglected parts of a car. Most boyracers do up their engines, only to find that their car's become too powerful for the stock tires. Selection of tires depends on your driving style, but always remember that the only part of your car that's actually in contact with the road are those nondesript rings of rubber wrapped around your rims.

    4: Brakes
    Another neglected part. Ventilated disc-brakes are the way to go. Get slotted rotors and six-pot calipers. Don't get brakes that are too heavy or you may end up having to upgrade your engine premurturely(sp).

    5: Strut bar
    Strengthens the chassis and prevents it from bending. If your pockets are deep enough, get one made of carbonfiber.

    From here on, you can upgrade your engine, but don't go overboard or even these upgrades won't help you from crashing.
