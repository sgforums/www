    4 Dec 05, 23:36
    Originally posted by Theatre Of Dreams:
    pressure = force/area

    hence it increases the temperature to increase the kinetic energy?

    or by forcing denser air in?
    i'll explain using more layman terms

    imagine air as tiny particles with lots of space between each particle.

    now, when pressure is exerted over a fixed vol of air, the spaces between each particle gets lesser, and the average rate at which they are colliding into each other within a smaller space increases.

    such "collisions" increase the kinetic energy of the air particles and they heat up as such.

    as air heats up, the particles try to distant themselves again to achieve equilibrium, and therefore, they expand.

    hence, if such compression/expansion were allowed to happen as it is, the engine would receive less denser air if the heated air passing thru the turbocharger does not get get a chance to become "denser" by allowing it 2 pass thru a intermediate heat transfer agent such as an intercooler, which helps to "stabalize" the movement of the air particles by taking away some of their kinetic energy. voila! more air particles per unit time reaches the engine.
