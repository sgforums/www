    Indonesia June vehicle sales down 50 pct

    JAKARTA, July 11 (Reuters) - Indonesia's vehicle sales fell 50.26 percent in June from the same month last year, as the industry struggled with weak consumer spending and high interest rates, an industry official said on Tuesday.

    Jodjana Jody, head of sales at PT Toyota Astra Motor, told Reuters that June sales volumes reached 24,564 units, recovering slightly from 23,022 units in May.

    June's volumes took first-half sales to 149,829 units, down 49.34 percent from the same period last year.

    The local unit of Toyota Motor Corp , the world's second largest car-maker, had said its June sales volumes were down 45.8 percent to 8,646 units.

    Toyota Astra Motor, owned by the country's largest automotive firm, PT Astra International Tbk , is the distributor for Toyota vehicles in the world's fourth most populous nation.

    Singapore's Jardine Cycle & Carriage Ltd controls Astra International.

    Indonesia's auto sector is suffering from a government decision last year to sharply hike domestic fuel prices to scale back costly fuel subsidies following high international oil prices.

    The country's automotive industry association has predicted sales would only reach between 370,000-400,000 units this year, but its chairman recently told Reuters it would be hard for the sector to book sales of 350,000 units in 2006.

    The forecasts were far below record high sales of 533,910 units posted last year, when interest rates were at a record low.

    Astra's shares have fallen nearly 3 percent since the start of the year, while the overall market has gained 15.2 percent over that period.

    COEs drop for big cars but rise for small cars and open category

    SINGAPORE: Results of the latest Certificates of Entitlement prices for vehicles are out.

    In July's second bidding exercise, COEs for Category A, or cars 1600cc and below, have gone up to $10,803. That was an increase of $602 from the last bidding exercise.

    In Category B, for cars above 1600cc, COEs fell to $10,600, registering a decline of $201.

    COEs in the Open Category were up slightly from $10,803 to $11,101, an increase of $298.

    There was a drop in the COE prices for Category C or for Goods Vehicles and Buses by $450 from $5,951 to $5,501.

    Motorcycle COE prices also came down by $19 to $881.

    The President of the Automobile Association, Mr Bernard Tay, commenting on latest COE results said though the Category A prices have gone up slightly, there was a drop in the number of bidders.

    He noted that "While car price cuts and some new models have stirred up buying interest in the last round of bidding for this category somewhat, it did not sustain that interest. Car market for Category B is also quiet."

    Mr Tay also observed that "existing car owners are hanging on to their cars as many can't sell their cars without having to top-up cash to pay off their loans. First-time car buyers are also adopting a wait-and-see attitude to see if COE prices will drop further." - CNA /dt

    LTA relaxes rules on private hire cars

    The Land Transport Authority has removed the 10-year statutory lifespan of rental cars.

    It has also lifted the rule that only new cars may be registered for private hire.

    This means imported second-hand cars and existing private cars may now be converted to or re-registered as private hire cars.

    The LTA says the changes will give rental car companies more flexibility when sourcing for cars.

    At the same time, the public will have a wider choice of vehicles for rent.

    The changes take effect from August 1.

    Previously, only new cars could be registered as private hire cars and they were subject to a 10-year statutory lifespan.

    If such cars were later converted to private cars, their lifespan limit continued to apply.

    LTA says it found that rental cars clock similar mileage as private cars.

    They also undergo the same vehicle inspection requirements.

    Thus the restrictions on the registration and lifespan of rental cars are no longer necessary. - CNA/ir

    Green Transport Week starts on Sunday

    It's not that hard to be green - that's the message for Green Transport Week which starts on Sunday.

    Into its fourth year, the campaign advocates eco-friendly modes of transport like taking the bus or train, cycling, walking or car pooling.

    These are ways that can reduce carbon emissions that contribute to global warming and climate change.

    The Singapore Environment Council would also like to see more Singaporeans buying fuel efficient and hybrid cars.

    Howard Shaw, Executive Director of Singapore Environment Council, said: "We are a nation of car lovers. We are not saying, 'leave your car at home, forget about cars'. Cars form a very necessary part of our integrated transport system. But what we are encouraging people to do is to choose more fuel efficient vehicles if you have to drive, maintain your vehicle so that it emits less pollution, have better driving habits and use the car responsibly. If you are going to travel less than two kilometres, you might want to consider walking or using your bike."

    This year's Green Transport Week will see a contest to spot fuel efficient cars on the road.

    Owners of cars with fuel economies of at least nine kilometres per litre and who are under the Fuel Economy Labelling Scheme will receive decals and stand to win fuel vouchers if spotted.

    And, SBS Transit will encourage more people to take public transport next week, by handing out free drinks at selected bus interchanges.

    It is also encouraging motorists to give way to buses to ensure a smooth ride for commuters.

    There will also be community activities beginning this Sunday, such as an "Eco Chase" which will see teams racing round the island via public transport.

    The first event on Sunday at Changi Village will see the launch of the third edition of the Green Transport Guide which contains useful tips to help motorists reduce fuel consumption and save money.

    Organisations and schools will also be able to pledge their support and submit their plans online for the Green Transport programme.

    Since rebates for hybrid cars were introduced, more Singaporeans have been interested in buying them.

    But while driving fuel efficient cars and taking public transport might be more environmentally friendly, Singaporeans Channel NewsAsia spoke to say cost and convenience are also major factors in their choice of transportation.

    To learn more about Green Transport Week, you can log on to www.sec.org.sg/gtw.

    BMW Group DesignworksUSA opens first Asian branch in Singapore

    SINGAPORE : The next BMW car may be designed by a Singaporean in future.

    The luxury car company is officially opening its first Asian design studio here on Friday and it already has plans to hire more designers from Singapore and Asia.

    From blue print to reality, designers at BMW Group DesignworksUSA have been making that happen for over 30 years in studios at California and Munich.

    The first design studio was opened in 1972.

    Their latest design flagship in Singapore, a first in the region, is poised to tap into Asian culture and grow Asian talents.

    "The client may want a specific view of Asia and the kind of products that are needed for this market and are accepted here may be a little bit different than in Europe and America. That might be an objective but I think generally, the design is universally accepted," said Nikolaus Von Saurma, director of the Singapore studio.

    The studio started operations early this year with just four experienced international designers.

    But plans are already in progress to hire from Singapore and Asia.

    "We're also looking at Singaporean designers that we train internationally in our studios in Munich and Los Angeles. We're looking at Asia as a whole for talent, so that we don't just have the background of this region but other parts of Asia as well," said Von Saurma.

    The studio will be officially opened on August 25.

    Nissan shares design experiences with Singapore experts, students

    SINGAPORE : Japanese car maker Nissan is on an Asia Pacific roadshow to exchange ideas with experts and students in the design industry on the models it is coming up with.

    It is all part of an effort to stress the point that design matters to the consumer.

    It is called the Imagination Factory - Nissan's processes of automotive design from concept right through to completion.

    With the support of Singapore's Design Council, Nissan has been meeting local and international designers to explore the many facets of design - something which has helped the Japanese car maker ride out difficult times in the 1990s.

    Nissan says its company philosophy is driven by three Cs.

    It says at the end of the day, whatever it does, it has got to be clear, consistent and creative.

    Alfonso Albaisa, Product Chief Designer, Nissan Motor Company, said, "It is really to understand the culture and the people. And it's really for us to understand how they feel about the market, how they feel about their life today and their dreams today, and we will try to work with that and create a car so that when they see it, they never imagined it, but they can't imagine living without it".

    Nissan is also looking into the impact of environment-friendly cars on design concepts. - CNA/ms

    Driven to frustration

    In September last year, sales manager Lionel Loh ordered a $70,000 Honda Stream from a parallel importer that promised delivery in the shortest time.

    After paying the deposit of $3,000, Mr Loh looked forward to getting behind his new set of wheels by late October or early November.

    But three months later, the 29-year-old is still waiting for his car Â— after being given various reasons for the delay.

    "First, the salesman said my car was stuck at the port in Japan, then the next time, he said it was still being manufactured," said Mr Loh, who went to the parallel importer Â— Car Resources Â— as he wanted a specific model of the multi-purpose vehicle.

    "I thought it would be quicker and cheaper going through a parallel importer but it turned out to be a nightmare," said Mr Loh.

    Last month, Kent Corp, another parallel importer came into the picture.

    It took over his case and told him his car was being inspected by the Land Transport Authority (LTA) and would be ready on Jan 15.

    Mr Loh has filed a formal complaint with the Consumers Association of Singapore (Case) to get back his deposit from Car Resources.

    To do this, he paid $25 to become a Case member and a small administration fee, and authorised it to act on his behalf.

    Last year, seven formal complaints were filed with Case against Car Resources and Kent, of which four have been resolved.

    The number is much higher if informal complaints, in which someone just calls or shoots off an email to Case, are taken into account. Last year, the association received 24 complaints against Car Resources and two against Kent Corp.

    In all, it received 41 complaints against parallel importers.

    The complaints related mostly to misrepresentation and late deliveries.

    For example, several clients claimed that the firms had informed them that the open market value of the car was higher than the one stated by the LTA. This information was not true, the clients said.

    Repeated calls to Car Resources went unanswered.

    When contacted by TODAY, Kent Corp general manager Vincent Wong, said there were delays for shipment of brand new cars and the company has resolved most of the complaints filed formally with Case against it.

    "Most of the cases have been resolved and there are some that are being resolved right now," he said.

    Customers who terminated the contracts got back their deposits and other money due to them, ranging from $427 to $3,000.

    Case executive director Seah Seng Choon warned that the act of accepting money from customers without the intention of delivering the goods "amounts to a criminal offence".

    However, consumers should first reason with the retailer or go to Case before taking more drastic action, he said. - /st
