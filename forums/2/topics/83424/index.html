    The first 1000km is crucial. The golden rules are:

    a. Do not over exert the engine, keep the rpm below 3000.
    b. Do not brake suddenly, gradually is the rule here as premature damage will be caused to the braking system and running in of brake disc and brake pad.
    c. Must change oil at 1000km.
    d. Apply gentleness to everything e.g. turning steering to the max and locking it and still forcing it turn is a no no.
    e. Gentle gear change, don't force. If manual, depress clutch fully.
    f. When you first collect your car, check the tyre pressure. Have it inflated to the manual guideline. My personal experience was the 2 rear tyres was only 135 psi, way below 220 psi as recommended.
    g. Check every fliud, oil more frequently as the hose, pipe may be damaged during the shipment, unloading, testing stage. The mechanics may even miss out on some pre-delivery items.
