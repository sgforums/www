    anyway... what car is it? maybe i can give you rough idea can change or not.

    basically if you use industry standard bulbs (H4, H3, 9008, 9006, etc), yes, you can change. this is mostly found in japanese (H4, H3) or lower end european (9008, 9006)

    if your car comes standard with a HID or Xenon module, then its no. because it's an entire module that generates 12,000 volts to create an arc (spark) in the bulb that has no filament. This modules are usually found on BMW, Lexus, Benz and are mostly optional.

    if you change bulb, look for a lower colour temperature rating, eg 3800-4200Â° K.

    your HID is usually above 5000Â°K

    but my advice to you, is you add fog lights. fog light bulbs are designed to permeate the fog whereelse a yellowish-white bulb isn't.

    Don't confuse fog lights with driving lights. Driving lights are those small ones you find at the skirt of the bumper. Fog lights are found at the grille although theres no harm moving it to the bumper.

    What LTA prohibits are the following,

    1. UNCOVERED fog lights unless they have met certain recognised standards.
