    my 2-cents worth of advice for those taking their tp..

    don't forget, don't ever forget, to check your blind-spots... this may carry small pts but it may bring a lot.

    n of course don't mount the kerb. that is immediate failure. (duh!!!)

    s-course, crank-course... open your eyes. if u must, don't just sit back. move your body forward to check left & right.

    in the circuit, listen properly.. sometimes instructor say go here or turn here. check if there is any arrow or sign that says whether can or cannot. sometimes they want to see whether you know how to read the signs but sometimes they see some obstructions in front so he wants to go short-cut (to finish the circuit faster).. thats y, check the signs then tell him "Eh, the sign say no left turn!" .... n see what he reply...

    there! my 2-cents advice..

    Guess it depends on your luck of getting a lenient tester...took my TP at SSDC in mid April this yr at the 2pm slot. As I was the only AUTO car on test for that slot amongst the 10 testees, I felt abit weird lor. The Chief tester gave the same speech as he probably does to everyone before their test..."Have confidence...blah blah...I passed my test on the 1st try...blah blah..." and so on.

    Then came the drawing of the Routes you were gonna take. Really like lucky draw but I can say that all the 10 routes aren't much diff...just have confidence and you'll make it through without much demerit pts. Just show the tester you aren't at all afraid and nervous..have confidence when moving off and turning, reversing and parking...and on the road. If you clear the circuit without much hassle, you're 95% there. I really feared the reverse vertical parking as that was my weak point. But as long as you show the guy in white that you know what you are doing and always always show him that you're checking for blind spots no matter wat..you'll pass

    In fact I did not expect to pass on the first try, after the test ended he lef me up to the de-briefing room and go over my test. He kept saying, "You never check your blind spot enough...blah blah....you almost hit that Land Rover in front of you at that junction...blah blah....too slow in moving off...blah blah..." but in the end he gave me 8 demerit points and pass me on my first try.

    Needless to say I was shocked and delighted at the same time, and let me tell you the feeling of watching that Safety video after you passed was great

    Basically just have confidence while driving during your test and show the tester you know what you're doing at all times. Good luck to those taking their TP soon
