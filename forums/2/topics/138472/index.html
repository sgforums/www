    I do not think the COE will be phased out since this is easy money, don't you think so? On the other hand, the PARF may be the one being phased out. In other words, only credit to LTA book account but no debit from the same account.

    Presently, we can take back 55% of the PARF derived from the OMV. If this PARF scheme is removed, it will mean the OMV is irrelevant for any new car price registration except for govt own tax computation. For a new car of say 60k of 14k OMV, maybe then the price will drop to 46k. However, dun expect to receive back a single cent (excluding the pro-rated COE) when the car is scrapped later, just as an example.

    If your RX8 has a OMV of 36K, maybe you could have it at <90k working out to be 9k depreciation per year for car body... sound more realistic?

    Also, i think ERP gantry is packed to the road tax calculation (i.e. using on road). With more ERP gantries will mean lower road tax in the future.

    Conclusion:

    With a lower new car price and lower road tax => everyone is happy that now new car is getting cheaper (however keep it in the garage to prevent paying ERP )

    (With more cars registering => more COE money gained) + more ERP gantry collection => LTA is happy that more money is available for them to improve on the SG road network and at the same time fulfil the govt needs allowing citizen to fulfill their dream of owning a car.

    Sound more logical?
