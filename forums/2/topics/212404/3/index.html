    It's more a case of knowing the torque curve on your engine. At different speeds you would leave the car in various gears to keep the torque from going too low. A classic case of torque being too low is when those ah ceks driving cabs go round a corner in third or fourth, when you can feel the taxi struggling to build up speed and the engine making a horrible noise.

    Keeping at the right revs is a lot easier if you have a tacho on your dashboard. I change gears according to it all the time - it was just a matter of delevoping the habit. If I'm taking it easy, I short-shift at 3000 RPM, and if I'm booting I wait till about 5750 RPM. There's no point redlining it either - by that time you would have gone well past the peak of the torque curve.
