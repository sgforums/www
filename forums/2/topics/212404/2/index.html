    That doesn't add up. By definition, engine braking is creating resistance from the engine by means of a reduced air-fuel mixture, i.e. the intake stroke takes more energy than the power stroke produces. Because the throttle is closed, it would not draw any more fuel than if the engine was idling. The only thing that might explain it is wonky fuel metering.
