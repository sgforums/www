    I would say, the average person will spend b/w 2k to 3k to learn driving with SSDC. The cost depends on:

    (1) How many lessons you need to complete all 33 subjects
    - average 20 to 30 lessons, depending on individual progress

    (2) When you take the lessons
    - peak $67.20 or off-peak $58.80

    (3) Whether you want to book a fixed instructor
    - extra $8.40 per lesson

    (4) How many revision lessons you want to take
    - revisions are done when you have completed all 33 subjs and have booked your test date, up to you to decide how many you want, depends on how confident you feel for the test.

    (5) How many attempts you need to take the test
    - each test itself will cost about $150 (test fee + warmup fee + vehicle rental), plus if you fail you need to take some more revision lessons

    As a guide for you, I spent about 2.3k:
    - 22 lessons to complete 33 subjs
    - 6 revisions
    - lessons mostly during peak hours
    - paid for fixed instructor for 20 lessons
    - 2 x theory lessons (took final theory with school, but passed basic before I enrolled)
    - passed on 1st attempt

    Other costs: Enrolment fees $100+, PDL $25, photocard licence $50, etc.

    Other things...
    I did Final Theory with the school, found the revisions on their computer system very useful. Just go for many, many revisions, by the time you take your FTT, the questions mostly see before one. Oh, also try to take your FTT after you have taken at least 2 or 3 practical lessons, it will help.

    Btw, there is no min. number of lessons required. Last time they set the min. at 25, now no need liao.

    And try to go for min. 2 lessons a week. In the beginning stages, preferably at least 3 times a week, else you will prob forget what you have learned in the prev lesson. For me, I took 3 to 5 lessons a week, from my 1st lesson to the date I passed = 2 months 1 week.

    U so from ssdc?

    S-course:

    First n foremost, u got to check ur blindspot b4 u turn in into the course... It is one of the safety check which will cost u 4pts if u forgot to do so...

    Usually is on the left hand side, very few occasion, go in by rite side... which ever side, jus turn ur steerin wheel to the dir lor...

    Full lock go into s-course... Maintain half clutch control... Very impt, go slow nev mind, no timin anywae...

    Keep ur car the center pt, u know the car in front, got 2 dots? Look lik 2 eyes lik tat? Round round circle de... The center is inbetween the 2 dots... Jus let the car center follow the rite side of the kerb... Adjust ur steerin wheel to ur right to follow it, gradually...

    U follow until reach the most curve bend, cannot keep the center in line wif the rite side kerb liao rite, u now adjust ur car again...

    Tis time, the 2 dots rite, use the right side tat dot, to follow the kerb on ur left... now u got to steer left.... Slowly slowly u be out liao...

    then stop, give way, etc... U should know liao le...

    Direction change:

    When u go in tat time, same thing, check blindspot...

    When u turn in, turn in nicely, mak sure tat ur car is in the center... or else adjust as u move inside the "waitin area" (So called lah...) Ur car very center, u quite safe ba...

    U rev gear, check the area tat u want to go in... the slot there... Mak sure no car, n don forget ur signal...

    u in half clutch, rev till the pt, hmm... the door knob tat area? The kerb hit tis pt liao rite... Roughly reach there liao hor, u quickly full lock to the right... Then u can go in, sui sui...

    Then u see ur side mirror, car about to inline wit the kerb, get ready to straighten, u judge urself tis lor... when to straighten the car... very hard to explain... U see roughly the car goin st, u turn 2 rounds to the left can liao lor...

    Then ur side mirror, in line wit the kerb at the side in front, stop... Done!
