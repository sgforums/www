    Some lessons learnt.
    1: Upon a puncture or vehicle breakdown, you are required to call the Toll Plus Highway number or using the SOS phone to inform them. They would then send their vehicles to place cones and other warning signs to assist you. Apparently this is required by Malaysian Law.

    2: Place spare tyre behind back of car in line with triangle sign, in this case the spare tyre prevented a direct impact on the car and missed my dad whom was squatting spanner in hand on the still attached punctured tyre when like a cartoon the car just disappeared in front of him.

    In event of accident,
    3: If you/passengers are not injured, retrieve your passports/valuables from vehicle and safekeep them, many 'vultures' will appear out of nowhere to scavage while you are distracted or helping the injured, although some are genunie passer-bys whom are really helping. If possible get women and children away from accident site if you have to stay with your vehicle, many cases of accident victims becoming robbery victims too have been heard...

    4: Call your insurance company and have them contact the local authorised panel contractor to have them tow the vehicle, the towing bill will then be covered by insurance. Verify the identity of the towing truck/company. Many tow trucks or unknown people would suddenly appear offering to tow your vehicle, do not let them tow your car away unless you want to have a hefty bill to be paid with cash on your own and troublesome 'release process' while you are still in a foreign country.
