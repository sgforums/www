    Hey there,

    Slacker - stock Evos come with CBVs - compressor bypass valves. You dun get the "psss" sound as the CBVs re-circulates the air back into the intake. WRXs - come with BOVs that gives you the release of pressure sounds. An EVO that has a BOV has done some aftermkt mods.

    Megatron - T-timers or turbo times, keep the engine running even tho the driver has removed the car key and is on his way to buy his cup of coffee. Main reason is when the car has been rev-ed hard, the EVO engine gets extremely hot. The engine actually uses the engine oil to cool the engine. Once the engine stops running, so does the oil flow. Hence, a t-timer is used.

    Cheers!

    I can vouch that the EVO 8 is fun. You can drive it like any normal car - and it can go like a mad monster if you so desire. Prepare for high insurance. Maintenance cost like any new car should really revolve around your petrol cost. If you drive everywhere like you need to get there the minute before, you can easily spend $400-500 per month. If you drive it like a normal car, you can keep petrol cost down to about $200 (i.e. like any 2 litre car). Modifications are not truly necessary - but if so desire, its easy to do so and there are many places that will gladly help out in this respect. Best thing about the EVO 8, its exclusivity compared to its closest competitor.
