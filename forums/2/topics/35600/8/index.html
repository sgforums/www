    Power is set at a conservative 280ps as before, but the Evo VIII gets a long overdue 6-speed manual tranny, adding ten more kilos to the transmission system. To maintain the same immense acceleration, the first gear's ratio is equivalent to that of an Evo VII. Second, third and fourth gears are of the same Super Close/Hi Ratio trans used in the WRC cars, while fifth has been set up lower than the previous 5M/T to match the three previous gears close ratio characteristics. The additional forward gear is set up for higher gearing than the Evo VII to save fuel in top gear cruising and achieve a higher top speed. Dials now sport a full scale 270km/h speedometer.
    Weight reduction was a key improvement with the new Evo VIII. A light weight full carbon fibre spoiler shaves 2 kg off the previous resin wing. ENKEI Alloy wheels similar to those of the WRC car are lighter by almost 1kg per rim. And even the engine on the whole has been lighten with lighter parts. The fuel tank however is larger by 7 litres for the GSR and 2 litres for the RS.
    Changes to the internal design of the AYC increases usable torque 1.6 times over the previous model, front and rear LSDs are now standard on the car. (Courtesy of www.ez-import.com)
