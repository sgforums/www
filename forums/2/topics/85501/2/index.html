    Actually you can control (to some degree) gear changes in an auto too. There is a sort of "biting-point" in an auto and if you hold the accelerator there, it drags a little... Then you either push down further, or release a little the gear will shift up.

    There is also a way to make the gear drop without rapid deceleration (ie, make your RPM shoot up).

    Race car drivers use a variation of these techniques in manual cars too to make their cars go real fast... inexperienced drivers tend to just floor the accelerator and wonder why their cars have no power (and why their fuel economy so bad).

  • mushigen
    15 Jul 04, 22:27
    In auto tranny, the torque converter still rotates, that's why you get the slight surge forward when you release your brake.

    On flat surface, this little force (or torque, to be precise) generated by the torque converter is enough to propel your car forward.

    However, on very steep slope, the downward force overcomes this torque. That's why the car slides backward lor.
