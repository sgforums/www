    Abstract from AsiaOne Motoring

    Bestsellers
    The popularity of five-door models is evident at every price point - $50,000, $60,000 and $70,000.

    But three models were at the forefront of this rear-end revival because of their spacious interiors and versatile seating - the Honda Jazz, Mitsubishi Colt and Hyundai Matrix. Together, they made up almost half of all Cat A hatchbacks sold from January to November 2004. Throw in the Hyundai Getz and the total proportion is almost two-thirds.



    Look out for
    In 2005, the benchmark will still be the Honda Jazz for its refinement. The Hyundai Matrix will be the other favourite because of its pricing. But these two ageing models are facing fierce competition and at all price points.

    The perky Mazda2, rakish Chevrolet Optra5 and the coming Suzuki Swift are revving their engines in the wings. The Chevrolet Aveo is also carving out a niche for itself as the 'cheapest of the premium minis'.

    Other models to look out for are the new Mitsubishi Colt Plus, an upsized version of the Colt. Along with the exciting Ralliart Colt Plus Turbo, this Mitsubishi will have more space and more power.

    Also interesting will be the budget mini segment, with the diminutive Hyundai Getz and Kia Picanto gunning to gain more market share with their cute looks and affordable pricing.

    Best buys
    The Hyundai Matrix will still be a good buy in 2005 because for a mere $60,000, you get mini-MPV spaciousness equal to a 1.6-litre sedan plus a more commanding ride height. But the most desirable are the more expensive Honda Jazz with its excellent handling, and the Mitsubishi Colt Plus Turbo for its sheer power.
