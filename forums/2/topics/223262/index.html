    Car smashes into wall of house, one passenger killed

    SINGAPORE: A car smashed into the wall of a house along Still Road just before midnight on Friday, leaving one dead, four injured and the driver arrested.

    Police said the driver was travelling along Still Road towards Eunos Link when he was believed to have lost control of his vehicle.

    The car veered towards the left side of the road, mounted a road kerb and hit a tree before colliding into the wall of the house.

    "When the car hit, the electricity went off, water pipes burst and water shot up. My kids were very frightened, there was boom! boom! bang," said Mary, the house owner.

    The passenger seated in the back left seat of the car suffered serious head injuries and was pronounced dead on the scene at about midnight.

    The driver, together with the front-seat passenger and two other passengers in the rear, were rushed to Changi General Hospital.

    "I tried to pull out one female passenger. She was screaming and saying it hurt in her leg and chest. I tried to pull out the other two female passengers but couldn't manage," said Mary.

    The driver was arrested but has since been out on bail.

    Police are requesting for witnesses to call their hotline at 1800-547-1818. - CNA/ir
