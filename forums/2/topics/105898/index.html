    YRV - Since it is more mpv like, it has a little more space and headroom. I don't know about the seat configuration, but the very least it should be able to fold out for more storage space. Yes, the engine is from toyota and although the engine make a major component of the car, it is not the only component. I guess it is whether the synergy of all the parts that give you the satisfaction. I agree that it is unique, but quite subjective. In comparision on unique-lity, i would rather go for March or the Ignis 1.3

    Vios - Very nice car and it is really powerful (in 1.5 catagory terms), mainly due to its vvti engine + 970kg weight and fuel consumption is good. Cruising is also comfortable. However, being a sedan here, it can only offer you what a sedan can offer space-wise. I do totally agree with you too that it is very common.

    March - March is marketed as a car with "so March (much) more intelligent" and "so March feature". As the name implies, it is "intelligent". Features features that can only be found in expensive cars, e.g a keyless entry keyless start, digital display, speed sensitive wiper. As for space wise, I am not sure it is offers as much as space as car of its class.

    Maybe you would like to test drive all of them first to find out how they perform on the road before making a decision.
