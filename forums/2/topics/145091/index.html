    Figures from the UK:
    For every 1% reduction in mean traffic speed, fatalities reduce by about 7%
    Lowering impact speed also reduces the severity of the injury. When a vehicle travelling at 40 mph collides with a pedestrian, nine out of ten are killed. Travelling at 30 mph, five out of ten pedestrians are killed. At 20 mph one pedestrian is killed, but nine survive
    For example Havant Borough Council which has imposed a 20mph speed limit on 20 miles of road has seen traffic accident casualties drop by 40%

    Reference: http://www.cfit.gov.uk/factsheets/03/#fn
