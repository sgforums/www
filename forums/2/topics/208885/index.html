    Petrol pump prices down by around six cents per litre

    Petrol pump prices went down by around six cents per litre on Tuesday, marking the first adjustment in four months.

    The four oil companies - Shell, Caltex, ExxonMobil and the Singapore Petroleum Company - all reduced their petrol prices.

    The oil companies say the adjustment reflects current market conditions.

    SPC says its Premium 98 unleaded petrol now costs S$1.857 per litre, Premium 95 unleaded S$1.774 per litre, and Super 92 unleaded S$1.742 per litre.

    Oil companies in Singapore last adjusted gasoline pump prices in late April when they raised the price of regular gasoline by around 10 cents a litre. - CNA/ir
