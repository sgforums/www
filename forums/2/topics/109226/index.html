    Hi
    My opinion on your requirements 1 and 2 (3 on feeling is subjective)
    Get a new car, they are low on maintenance. Now COE is cheap. And you don't have to worry about parts or their availability coz new cars are under waranty (their single most attractive point). Anything wrong, you can just drive to the agent and KPKB why like that, it's their responsibility to get the parts and have the car repaired without bothering you. Hassle-free. New engines after run-in are at their most efficient, coz every thing is clean.

    On your first dilemma,
    Whether is it relevant to own a brand new car or COE car, maybe you should look at the depreciation. Some old cars depreciates more than a brand new car of the same class these days, so since you're not a car enthusiast, maybe a new car makes more sense given the reasons above if there's no substantial savings in depreciation if you get a COE car. Note however, you may have to hang onto the new car for at least a few years if you don't want to suffer the huge depreciation of selling a young car as the 1st owner.

    Used, old cars can be really bad in terms of maintenance, depending on your luck and car, as much as one or two thousand within the first few months just the get the noises and rattles out and have it road worthy. The downside of not having warantee, is that if the workshop make a mistake on troubleshooting, you'll have to pay nonetheless.

    However, COE cars have their pros too : There are some cheap ones in a good condition. And since you can choose one which don't require as long or as big a commitment in order to break even as new cars, they allow you react better to changes in situations. Go for the commonly-available Jap ones in this case as their parts are cheap and readily available becoz they come in different grades, according to your budget.

    Hope this helps,
    Cheers!
