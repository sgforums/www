    Originally posted by p21:
    wat do you mean depreciation lower??
    take for example before Mar04 ( correct me if I am wrong ), the Parf is 65% of OMV. Thus if I bought the deluxe version at say $73188 similar to the price now, my Parf is approximately 65% of $16000 (OMV) = $10400, which works out to a depreciation of $73188 - $10400 = $62788/10 = $6278.80 per year.

    Assuming all else stays e same now.

    Parf = 55% of $16000 = $8800, so if you continue to calculate based on the same working above, you will realise the depreciation per year is higher.

    The only way for the depreciation to be equal to then is the Parf Value to increase to about $19000 which is pretty unlikely as the variation is somewhat too great.

    hope u understand now.

    Note though the calculation is based on use of car for 10years. Does not take into account if you scrap or sell your car before 10years.
