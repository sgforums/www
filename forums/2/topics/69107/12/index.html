    I think you can do the following:

    1) Go to One motoring to check out the number of the car exporter & call them to get an idea of how much more are they willing to pay for your car above the scrap value.

    2) Make sure tha the scrap value will not be more than the PARF value plus the OMV because the PARF cert can only be used to pay for certain portion of the car price. Check out at One motoring too for the details. You can still breakdown the PARF cert to smaller value and get your friends or relative to use them but would be a hassle.

    3) If you can get the SE to do all the things for you and pay you similar or lower by a few hundreds, you should let them do it. The first time I talked to the Toyota SE four months ago, she told me my car is scrap value only. One month ago, I talked to a Nissan SE and she told me she can give me $3K above scrap. When I called an exporter, was told I could get about 3 to 4K depending on the condition of my car if I would go down and let them view the car.

    All the above are theory only as I am contemplating of doing this with my 4 year old Sunny which has a $44K COE.

    Regard sun film LTA approval,

    I installed V-cool premiun after collection. LTA rule is 70% visibility for the front and 50% for the rear.
    I was also concerned about this rule. So, i asked the boss to use the same type of visibility detector to check on my the front and rear.
    Result is front is only 68%, which is fail as it is below the LTA rule. However, later the boss shows me a letter which was a reply from LTA. The letter explained that they give tolerance of -/+5% on the 70% visibility. He added that if LTA does question on this tolerance, he will then bring the letter to the LTA officer. Well, i think V-Cool did their homework before selling for 10 years in singapore. I am satisfact with them.
