    Hi, i will try my best to explain to you...here goes (may bore the rest to death )

    185 or 195: The tyre section width (W) in millimetres
    70: The aspect ratio in % (The height of the sidewall divided by the tyre's width) [H/W x 100%] = Aspect Ration %

    If you understand the above, say the 185/70 R14 vs a 195/70 R14 based on the above theory, you will realise the height-H will be different for both, thus if the Rim size stays constant (i.e. 14") the overall radius and circumference will change.

    to answer the question on what is "Pinging" and how it sounds like....

    it sounds like marbles in the engine, but ur ICE has to be turned off as the "ping" sound is very soft in my case, u actually need to concentrate to pick it up....

    "ping" could happen when:

    1. Lower octane fuel is used
    2. may happen with light throttle, usually with full throttle as what i described above when I do sudden depression of my accelerator pedal
    3. Compression Ratio too high & ignition timing too advanced (highly unlikely in our case)

    I think our ALTIS has knock sensors to counter the "pinging" problem...anyone can confirm if knock sensors are present?
