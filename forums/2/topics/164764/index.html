    Can I just add on....

    The philosophy of Honda and Toyota is totally different.

    Honda seems to be heading towards R&D, investing heavily in engineering and coming out with better product. Is it where the VTEC orginate from and if you looked at Honda cars, you can tell that the engineers ready thought through every details.

    At Toyota, it seems that the company is focusing on efficiency and reducing cost. This explains their manufacturing process does not only revolve round EOS but also comtemporary cost savings methods like Keiritsu, kanban systems as well, better known as JIT techniques. The savings are passed back to consumer by either offering a higher spec model at the same price with competitors or lowered price, or (usually) a combination of both.

    Taking the Vios and the City as examples, not only is the honda model more expensive on paper, it is also more expensive in terms of lesser equipment. The basic Vios 1.5E cost around $5 less than the basic City, but on closer examination, it seems the equipments are rather different too...among them are ABS, Alloys wheels, airbags, power output, etc...
