    In a nutshell, earth lines are suppose to improve electrical / electronics performance which can be translated in improvements in torque/ power / fc, idling and engine starting characteristic, better functioning electrical parts such as healights, horn and ICE.

    There seems to be more positive feedback on ICE performance and headlights,
    and not so much on significant power gained.

    My 2 cents:
    I may install this only later when the car gets older, when the voltage potential across the electricals may degrade. For now, I'll make do with the stock grounding earth lines; Though they don't look as sexy, but they'll do for the time being. I'd rather spend my $ elsewhere for now.
