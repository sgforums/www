    Ok while its right to say the SLR's engine is in front of the passenger compartment, I think one thing most people here have missed out, is that its mounted behind the front axle.

    That in effect, makes the SLR a mid mounted engine car. an MR. mid mounted is defined by having the engine anywhere between the 2 axles. Doesn't say where the passenger compartment has to be. Thats the reason why the SLR has such a long bonnet.

    For most exotics, the primary design concern is how well they drive, which is why most of them are mid-mounted. Secondary concerns like luggage space are often given lower priority. Most rich people who buy cars like these, have luxurious estates for longer get-aways, so there is essentially no need to pack in luggage space into exotics. Also, packing in empty space for luggage interferes with the packaging of the car, esp with aerodynamics and mechanicals. These cars are focused on performance, and are designed as such.

    Mercedes cars tend to be front engined, not just the SL. Its not exactly a tradition though. As we can see, the SLR has gone for the MR route.
