    And your rebuttal shows your prejudices.

    AYC makes a bad driver look good, but a good driver better. It affords traction where there was none in the first place. The AYC is an electronically controlled hydraulically activated differential, it is NOT a viscous coupling unit. It affords better control than even a "traditional" clutch/cam LSD since there are individual sensors for individual wheel speed, lateral and longitudinal g, brake and handbrake application, steering wheel angle etc so that the ACD+AYC combination knows what is expected of it all times.

    In practice, if you were to compare an AYC-equipped Evo with a non-AYC-equipped Evo on a surface with differing traction qualities, the one with AYC will have better grip and move off more smoothly with minimal tire slip. And it has been shown repeatedly that an AYC-equipped Evo turns in better lap times than a similar non-AYC-equipped Evo. All cars were driven by professionals, and the drivers were swapped around to minimize bias.
