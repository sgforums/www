    No la of course i did not pay it in full. I try to minimize my borrowings in general. Good thing about car loans is that it is fixed rate so as long as you lock it in at a good rate, you effectively create an arbitrage opportunity in a rising interest rate environment. I normally cap borrrowings at 40-50% of purchase price so that over a 3 year period, nett of depreciation, your upfront capital remains in tact or may be even higher when selling the car which can then be used to fund the upfront payment for the next car.

    Actually you will be very suprised that the maintenance is actually quite low (in terms of servicing cost). The petrol is not too bad as i only drive the car during weekends as well.
