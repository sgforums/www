    ok here's my 2cents worth

    build quality:
    sunny - made in japan, should be ok
    vios - thailand, not very good, seems to be a lot of problems here and there, check out the vogs forum
    city - thailand, but could be better than vios, why? cuz it's exported to japan as fit-aria. if it's good enough for japan, should be good enough for us, no?

    comfort/suspension:
    sunny - never found sunny to be comfortable leh..interior small for that kind of exterior size
    vios - typical toyota soft suspension, very uncomfortable if corner aggressively, cramped interior
    city - vtec has a sportier suspension than idsi, esp the new face-lift since it's been tweaked even further(the original vtec has already one layer of changes from the idsi version)

    engine:
    sunny - variable valve timing, but feedback from owners seem to be there doesn't seem to be any difference, you should check out sunny forum
    vios - variable valve timing, good power for its weight
    city - vtec, effectiveness is more than vvt in terms of power wise. vtec engines are also very fuel-efficient when operating below vtec kick in

    fc:
    sunny is infamous.
    vios - fc good
    city - should be close fight with vios

    looks:
    sunny if you like ah-pek look
    vios - facelift looks worse actually
    city - facelift looks much better

    price:
    sunny: made in japan at that price is good
    vios: cheap lah
    city: priciest, about same price as altis, but what you get is better cabin utilisation, big boot, efficient cvt(if auto), wheel base same as vios, better suspension.

    ignis sport is slightly different from the 3 here cuz it's 3 door(incovenient or sporty depending on how you look at it), no boot, dated interior look, small size, but you get a rev-happy engine, close gear ratio, long exhaust, sporty suspension, forged pistons and quite a lot of mods compared to a 'normal' car.

    for honda city, be sure to check out the in depth review at asia.vtec.net

    Hi,

    The FC for similar Japanese cars nowadays is not that different. And I've always said, that there are many other things which will burn your bank account faster than FC ratings. If you don't travel very much, its really not practical scrutinising FC ratings.

    Heavier doesn't necessarily mean safer. It depends on where the weight is - most of the time, its in the engine or gear.....or due to difference in body size. These do not necessarily have any influence on occupant safety.

    In my previous job, I used to work for a company producing victim extration systems in case of car accidents. In my training, I've cut up cars before at the scrap yard. Even the supposedly heavy US-made cars were just a thin sheet of metal wrapped around a metal frame.

    Occupant safety nowadays almost always means airbags (passive safety) - front, side, rear, etc. And airbags do not contribute much to the weight of the car. ie. weight is no indication of safety.

    For each car, there are crash tests. Different countries have different standards for these tests (frontal impact, side impact, etc). And most modern vehicles sold in Singapore have passed these tests.

    Here is a pdf document from the internet showing some crash test observations. You will notice that based on REAL-LIFE ACCIDENTS, the heavier, more expensive, more famous European cars are sometimes less safe than the so-called light Japanese "tin-can" cars.

    http://www.folksam.se/engelsk/trafik_eng/sakrabilar2005.pdf (See page 4).

    This reminds me of a rich friend who always went for European cars, saying that its because they're safer for his family.
