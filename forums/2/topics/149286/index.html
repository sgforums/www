    Car grooming can be a very expensive hobby if one just sent them in to agent for package grooming (usually pre-paid from $450 & above) once in a blue moon.

    Like all things, grooming is an everyday chore and contrary to myth, it only takes a few min each day to save you a bomb!

    Here is a simple 3 steps for everyday routine :-

    1) Wipe your interior of car everyday with a wet tissue (those that is popularly used by babies) after you start your engine and before moving off - (5min job)

    2) Inspect car exterior and look out for irritating bird's dropping, wipe them off with a wet tissue followed by a dry grooming cloth (those that car wash usually use... and it is in disgusting orange... ha) - (3 min)

    3) At the end of the day, remove / dust your interior carpet and wipe car again before your off the engine and call it a day -(5 min)

    Personally, I take car "grooming" or house-keeping, as a very serious hobby. I DON'T allow anyone else, however professional they claim themselves to be, to touch my vehicle.

    Yes, it can be time consuming, as does Golfing, Fishing, Mahjong, ..etc. But if it interests you, and you enjoy cleaning up your car and keeping it sparkling clean and showroom shiny, you will find the kind of time it requires.

    Periodic wiping of interiors is a good regime to follow, but externally, I don't rely on wiping (depends on what sort of soiling it is) as I rely on the layer of wax I lay down every 2 weeks. Waxing is very important in our climate due to acidic rain. Laying down a layer of wax every 2 weeks prevents dirt from adhering to surface, makes tar spots easier to remove, and protects your vehicle's paint surface from the acidic rains we get because of Sumatra's slash and burn die hard habit.

    Regular waxing also creates a pretty tough buffer against minor scrathes, making micro scratches and other types of accidental scratch, easier to buff over and fill with wax molecules. I typical waxing takes about 2 hours, including interior cleaning and shampooing adds another 2 hours. Yes, that's half a day.

    Ready to commit? If you love your ride, you will be ready to commit.

    Hi you all people who matains your rides.

    Once again, I re-emphasize that wax coating your ride, is VERY important in Singapore's climate. We get rain that is quite acidic due to haze from Sumatra, and also the factories in Tuas area emits by-products into the air and can aget absorbed by passing rain clouds.

    Take note that during each downpour or moderate rainfall, the rain water in a busy road or expressway looks or appear foamy each time a vehicle passes by. If you take a drop of it and allow it to dry on a glass plate, you will see there are many particles residue left behind after water evaporates.

    WAXING, is VERY important. Minimum, wax once every 2 weeks. The more layer of wax you overlay, the safer your ride's paintwork shall become.

    I notice that after waxing my ride, if it happens to rain the next day, I can feel the smooth waxiness of the surface where I waxed has been washed away. Normal rain, does not do that. If rain these days can strip away the slippery surface of a wax, can you imagine what rain can do to un-waxed rides? Our undercarriage is a different story, because of undercarriage rust proofing. But not topside.
