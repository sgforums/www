    There are many types of auto transmission. Traditional auto uses torque converter, CVT which uses belts and pulleys, and clutchless manual which is actually a manual trans with a mechanised clutch -- this is the DSG in the VW GTi, Selespeed in Alfa Romeo, and SMG in BMW.

    The F1 cars use the clutchless manual trans, which is actually faster shifting than a traditional manual. The BMW M5 can shift in 0.4secs, while the F1 cars shift in 0.2secs (but they change whole transmission after each race!). No way, the human hand can shift faster than that.

    The DSG VW GTi is actually rated faster than the manual version.

    Even the traditional auto trans can win races against manual. No 2 cars are perfectly identical. Manufacturing accept tolerances. Cars that are built close to the design specs will perform much better.

    In the Vios races in Pasir Gudang organized by the VOGS, an auto Vios driven by Lady-Rose (her nick) can win the guys with manual. Hers is mostly stock without mods.
