    Well, just heck care those people who tailgate you and pressurize you. If they are really so impatient, they will overtake you. So just drive safely, let them do the overtaking. 

    Added: Tip #2 from me: Always slow down when going up slope. Because you never know what's at the other side of the down slope. I experienced that before, on CTE. I maintained my speed of about 70kmh when going up slope at one of the bridges before AMK Ave 1 exit I think.     these r taken from my learner book::

    - 100% stop at the white stop line

    - don't turn left when the order sign says no left turn, likewise for right side.

    - mount kerb

    - unable to stop immediately at E-brake

    - change lane without due care

    - drive across the centre line

    - drive over double white line

    - exceed speed limit

    - never stop to give way at pedestrian crossing

    - stop directly on zebra crossing lines when left turn filtering.

    - never give way to traffic with right of way

    - form up incorrectly in lane direction

    - remain in junction and course obstruction

    - beating red light

    - improper action causing accident/ near accident

    - TP tester step on the break pad on his side

    - TP tester has to help u turn the steering wheel.
