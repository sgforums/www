    Firstly do you really need a car or do you want to buy one just cause your peers happen to have spent their life savings on a 10 year loan for a vios or picanto?

    Sometimes there isn't a need to buy one unless you really are into cars or your work/family requires one.

    I'd say you may want to consider doing your homework, find out the deal about OMVs, scrap value, the rise and fall of COEs, your preference for which car you may want to buy etc.

    By doing your homework i mean visit the OneMotoring websites, window shop at the 2nd hand car marts to get an idea of the marekt rate for a desired car, and probably go to certain showrooms to get one of their SE's to do a financial analysis for your budget.

    Heres where you can find a decent list of FCs for most cars
    http://www.mycarforum.com/reviews/?action=fc_table

    Personal owners' reviews on their cars
    http://www.mycarforum.com/reviews/reviews.pl

    New car buying procedures
    http://www.mycarforum.com/forum/General_C1/Resources_F58/New_car_buying_procedures_P425339/

    Seriously do the proper research and don't commit to a car too soon.
