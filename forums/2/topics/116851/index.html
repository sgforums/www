    I am a firm believer of you get what you pay for. While I think some korean cars look very nice and yet come cheaper than cars in their own category, I have to agree in terms of maintenance and performance, it may not be as good.

    In buying cars, you cant buy a perfect car. Buy a car that suits your needs most. Cars, whether Korean, Japanese or Euro fulfills different needs. There is no arguement on whether this car is better or not because different cars meet different needs.

Furthermore, in my first year of owning the KIA, I spend close to $1000 for maintenance and changing of parts believe it or not but with my Honda, everything still working fine.

    Thus my belief is that work on your priorities in buying a car and see which car fits your needs most. I always believe that there's a car for everyone.
