    Originally posted by tailslide:
    LTA approves HIDs based on specific models of vehicles, even if its on the same vehicle type. For example, HIDs are standard fixtures on the DC5 type R, but not so for the DC5 GSR. hence, if u fit HIDs on a GSR, it is not approved.
    But there are some cars, eg BMW/Mini, where their models have option extras that include HID retrofitting, approved by factory (done at the authorized dealer of course). I believe LTA's rule on the HID is based more on the factory's approved specifications for that model.

    quote from one.motoring. http://www.onemotoring.com.sg/publish/onemotoring/en/lta_information_guidelines/modification_guide/hid_headlamps.html

    "Qn: Can I retrofit my halogen headlamps with an after-market HID headlamp system?

    Ans: In general, we do not allow the retrofitting of HID lamp systems on existing vehicles, unless it is duly certified by the vehicle manufacturers.
    So if Honda has a HID retrofit for the DC5 GSR, then fitting HID in that is legal.... the retrofit kit has to be bought and done at the authorized dealer of course. Same for the BMW 3-series.
