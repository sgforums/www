    Originally posted by guitarman1:
    Go send your car for dyno testing and find out when your max horsepower come in. If lets say it come at 5000 rpm and your redline is 6500 rpm, no point dragging it to redline also wat.

    If lets say the dip in horsepower after 5000 rpm is VERY SHARP, for e.g at 5000 rpm your car pushing 130 bhp, then after that drop sharply to 100bhp at 5500, then you shouldn't drag over 5000.

    If the dip in horsepower is only very minimal, you might consider dragging higher a bit so that when you change gear, you are still within a good power band for the next gear (ie heading strongly towards your max power)

    E.g.

    4000 rpm : 105 bhp
    4500 rpm : 120 bhp
    5000 rpm : 130 bhp
    5500 rpm : 125 bhp
    6000 rpm : 100 bhp

    Just as a example, lets say this fella racing his car. Max bhp is 130 at 5000 rpm. Lets assume when he change gear, it drops around 1000 rpm. So if he drag to 5000 rpm and change gear, it will drop to 4000 rpm the next gear. From 130 bhp drop to 105 then start to climb again. He fell out of the power band as his car make power at around 4500 rpm only.

    If he changed his style and shifted at 5500 instead, although it drops to 125 bhp at 5500, he will land at 4500 rpm in the next gear which is 120 bhp and keeping within the power band.

    This is just an extreme example. Actually got many diff arguments but this is what I gather. Pls correct me if I'm wrong.

    Anyway, most cars have rev limiter, it should cut you off before the redline. For those modified civics (original redline is 8200 for the old DOHC VTECs), revving to 10,500 also engine should be able to tahan. Highest I've seen is 12,000 rpm but after that engine got problem.

    But of coz dragging 1st and mebe 2nd gear to 10,500 is "OK", but if higher gear revving to 10,500, engine may not tahan cuz it stays there much longer. 1st and 2nd is 'fast-fast job'. Rev there, change gear. 3rd still bearable. Later gears take so long to climb that your engine may not tahan revving so long at such high rpms.
