    Financial sense is important cos, the moment you buy the car, the value drops.

    You need to understand the base value of the car at the moment which you may de-registering it. This will be the PARF rebate:

    http://www.onemotoring.com.sg/publish/onemotoring/en/topwebwide/faqs/faq_deregistration.html

    Next is the "Body Value" which is variable from dealer to dealer, but definitely there is a highest price point which only known to the industry player.

    So in short, buying car for ordinary folks like me will be to consider:
    Loss = Buying Price - Rebate - Body Value (where available)

    Note that if the rebate paper exceeds the taxes of new cars, the rebate will be split. The remaining value is usually sold to those who can use it or to the agent at a commission which means additional loss. So try to understand the monies involved. Rebate is only for the purpose of off-setting taxes and has a expiry date.

    Cheers.

    Before you read what I have added, which may still be confusing, you can approach your agent to ask them to provide you the information or calculation until you can understand better before you commit to a decision.

    What you asked, 45k price is not enough. You also need to have the following information to calculate the annual depreciation of your purchase:

    1) OMV ( To calculate together with no.2 )
    2) Registration Date ( To know which rebate formula to use, OMV or ARF )
    3) COE ( To calculate the COE rebate at de-registration )

    The car price will be sold at a value higher than the immediate PARF rebate, so it will be the cost price, as you mentioned 45k.

    Method 1: Annual depreciation until COE expires
    SG Carmart has some Santa Fe, so take a look and do your calculations to the suggested annual depreciation average of 5000 until the expiry of the COE.

    Method 2: Annual depreciation until de-registration of car
    Now, lets say you are to de-register the car before 10 years is up since COE date, you can calculate the 5th year PARF rebate value.

    So from the moment you buy until the de-registration, you divide the loss over the time to get an annual depreciation for the duration of use of the car.

    Just to share also, which others has suggested in other topics

    1. How much the car has travelled: 20 000km in 12 months is a good average
    2. is the COE value is high, it just means higher depreciation for you
    3. is a new car a better alternative?
    4. is loan for used car and new car the same?
    5. when is the roadtax for this resale expiring? new car comes with 6 months road tax
    6. is the dealer covering the transfer fees for you?
    7. is paint job issue? BLACK paint is likely to be full of flaws after 1 year, swirl marks, scratches etc etc
    8. are you able to check if car is accident free?

    Writing down in a list and slowly checking it at the agent's and the car is best.
    No rush, so do it in a comfortable pace.
