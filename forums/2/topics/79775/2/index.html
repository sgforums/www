    I have to agree with you on this one, and hey, I am getting a red one by the end of this year too! will yours be the sedan or the hatch? mine will a hatch.

    btw, I have test driven the car with my friend recently, and I have to say It's one of the better cars I have driven, at least imo...

    I used to look forward to owns a Ford Focus manual hatch, but after test driven it again last week, I was even more convinced that the M3 is a better bet. Compared with the focus, it may not be able to keep up, in terms of 0-60 times, but the engine note sure sounds more sporty and enthusiastic; the Yamaha tuned Zetec SE engine of the Focus is too smooth for my liking (funny, cos that the Auto Version sounds better), so as in the over light clutch pedal and shift, not much robust feel. As the Focus looks became dated gracefully (it's still a great car to own nonetheless, since it was launched first back in 199, the M3 oozes much more styles, inside and outside.

    The M3 has the same or better handling capability as the current Focus, since it's adopting the new 2nd generation Focus C1 Platform, whch will be in the New Focus (launching end of this year), as well as in the new Volvo S40 and V50. However, the Suspension settings are done in Mazda's Hiroshima Testing Facility by Japanese Engineers, nothing wrong with that, I threw it into a third gear bend, and does not even understeers (just like the Focus), so not really necessarily that we need to have AWD as in the Impreza. High speed stabilty is another strong point for this car, as it obsorbs most of the bumps that we found on our roads. I tried doing hard braking on both the sedan and the hatch, and found that the hatch, with it's Front and Rear Disc Brakes really provides excellent stopping, it's not to say that the Sedan brakes will not that good, just that the hatch is better.

    I was quite amazed by the just nice level of insulation of this car, not too quiet as the Peugeot 307 (can doze off while driving), and not as noisy as the Koreans, Altis (no offence).

    Anyway, I have done a review of the M3 when it was launched here previously and it goes like this: ( no offence to anyone here, but just my own opinion )

    Mazda Axela as called in Japan, 1.5VVT with 115ps.
    Mazda3 as called in Singapore (and the rest of the
    countries, including Europe and US), 1.6VVT with 105ps
    (euro4 emission type)

    Other versions include 2.0 & 2.3 versions, which is
    not available here of course. US don't get 1.6 version
    as petrol to them is not a concern, US fuel prices:
    around S$0.27 per litre!

    Official Information:

    Official Mazda Axela Special Site
    http://special.axela.mazda.co.jp/

    Homepages

    www.mazda.co.jp
    www.mazda.com
    www.mazda.com.sg

    In my opinion:

    "A great Compact Car, which offers Japanese Reliabilty
    and European Looks and Feel"

    "Handles almost like the Alfa 147, and steers sharper
    than the Focus and Impreza, makes you wonder why you
    need AWD (as in the Impreza)"

    Looks aside as they can be very subjective.

    Pros:-

    Good implementation of Ford Focus/Mazda Axela/Volvo
    S40 & V50 C1 Platform, strong chassis.

    Solid and reassuring doors closing, even better than
    Honda Civic. Worst example: Corolla (improves
    marginally on the new Corolla Altis).

    Superb suspension setup (which is already a forte on
    the six years old Focus), inspires confidence even
    under hard cornering, superb handling with 16" sport
    rims/tyres. Stays firmly to the ground even during
    high-speed sections, superb dampening on uneven roads
    as well as humps (like the focus). Another good
    example: Impreza 1.6TS, worst example: Corolla
    (improves marginally on the new Corolla Altis, but
    still feels bouncy and floaty, as compared to the
    european suspensions setup. This type of soft
    suspension setups still appeals to the Americans, as
    they have quite badly cut-up roads).

    Minimal body roll, very well controlled even under
    hard cornering. Worst example: Corolla (improves
    marginally on the new Corolla Altis).

    Quick 2.2 turns lock-to-lock Steering (same as Alfa
    147, and sharper than Focus and Impreza), great for
    quick maneuverability and overtaking, also useful for
    parking (unlike the Corolla/Corolla Altis, which you
    always have to be busy turning the wheel, during
    turning and parking)

    well-weighted steering wheel (better on the hatch),
    pretty communicative, good road feedback information.
    Another good example: Ford Focus 1.6 Zetec, worst
    example: Corolla/Corolla Altis (lightly-weighted
    passive driving and minimal feedback)

    Activematic (Auto and Sports) Shifting is very slick.
    Auto Mode offers smooth shifting and good laidback
    cruising capability (that's how we normally drive).
    Sports Mode shifts eagerly to 7000rpm (when we want to
    have some fun), shift up beautifully with no jerking
    (unlike the Alfa 147 Selespeed, but then again the
    alfa is a proper 5-speed manual with automatic clutch
    actuators)

    Engine note sounds much like the Yamaha designed Ford
    Focus Zetec SE Engine, very sporty yet composed.
    Another good example: Engine still a forte with the
    Corollas VVTi, but does not sounds so inspiring
    through.

    Cons:-

    Acceleration sufficient on the sedan, but suffers
    fairly on the hatch, which is slightly heavier. Due to
    the heavier weight, offers better stability on High
    Speed Sections.

    Rear-door Roofline and headroom too low on the sedan,
    needs to be extra cautious getting in and out, tall
    passengers suffers at the back. No problem with the
    hatch.

    Engine note sounds a tad harsh upon reaching the max
    7000rpm revs, but then again we don't always push it
    so hard.

    Emergency Brakes on the sedan is a little not so
    effective as the hatch, as the rear are drum brakes.
    For cars with rear drum brakes, the Subaru Impreza
    1.6TS still tops.
