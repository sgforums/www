    I'm not a good driver, but this is how I learn.

    ok, first things first.

    Throttle control: depress your accelerator until your rpm(free/neutral gear) reach around 2500, then let go. Try not to look at the tachcometer while practicing, get use to the engine sound. Repeat until u get use to it.

    Moving the car: depress your clutch fully, engage 1st gear, depress accelerator until your rpm reach around 2500, very slowly release your clutch until you feel the car starts to move a little. Release(slowly) the clutch more to go faster. Repeat until you get use to it. note: don't hold the steering wheel too tightly, just place your hands there will do.

    Steering: hands at 10 - 2 position @ the steering wheel. When steering, try not to look at the steering wheel, feel you way through the bend/turn/corner/hairpin/u-turn. Use crosshand method when you need to turn more. Look at the direction you wan to turn at all times. Repeat until you get use to it.

    Shifting up gear: While moving, depress clutch fully, release accelerator completely, relax your right hand(just rest on the steering wheel will do), left hand shift to desired gear, depress the accelerator until rpm reach around 2500, slowly release the clutch until you can feel the car going a little faster, release more to go faster/need more power. Do all of these actions with you eyes glued to road in front. Repeat until you get use to it.

    Shifting down gear: Depress brake(right foot) slowly until you can feel the car slowing down, depress clutch fully(left foot), relax your right hand(just rest on the steering wheel will do), left hand shift to desired gear, slowly release the clutch until you can feel the car going a little more slower(engine braking effect), slowly depress the accelerator until desired speed. Do all of these actions with you eyes glued to road in front. Repeat until you get use to it.

    All of the above must be done with your eyes on the road in front, try not to look at any of the meters(unless checking for speed), steering wheel and gear stick. note: if you release the clutch or depress the accelerator too quickly(especially 1st, 2nd and 3rd gear), the car will jerk violently, and it'll look ugly from the outside.
