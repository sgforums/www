    Measures to Tackle Singapore's Traffic Problems

    At the recent Budget debate on Transport, Transport Minister Yeo Cheow Tong outlined two measures to ease vehicle growth in Singapore and increase vehicle speeds on expressways.

    The Transport Ministry has reviewed the vehicle growth rate and has decided to maintain the growth rate at 3% for another three more years from 2006 to 2008. However, it is not possible to retain indefinitely the 3% growth rate as the road network may not be able to support the vehicle growth. The total COE quota for 2006 would be set at 131, 127, down marginally from 132, 581 in 2005.

    The Minister also highlighted the worsening traffic situation during evening peak hours on expressways. Due to the success of introducing ERP charges on the northbound CTE during evening peak hours to improve traffic speeds, the LTA will study how to use ERP to improve the traffic speeds on other expressways during evening peak hours.

    Do you think restricting COE quota and introducing more ERP are solutions to Singapore's traffic problems?

    What other suggestions do you have to solve Singapore's traffic problems?
