    Originally posted by HENG@:
    think there is a distinction between just righ dampening and suspension supplenessand compliance and being overly soft. Too soft a ride certain would cause a need for air sick bags. A comortable car is one which absorbs bumps with a certain firmness, deals with undulations very effectively and yet is not overtly wallowly in its ride.
    It is always a trade off. A firmer ride provides better handling, better braking, better acceleration. If you go softer, the ride is more comfortable and pliant, but the body roll when taking turns becomes an issue (well at least it is for me)

    Of course, some higher end cars like the Volvo S60R, some higher end Mercs, Lotus and even Citroens have adjustable dampening but even then, it is stilla choice between a sporty/harder/firmer ride or a more supple/rolly ride.
