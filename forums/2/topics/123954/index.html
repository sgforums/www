    dumb that idea of remembering how many round to turn on your wheel. prior to right turn, ensure there's no pedestrian nor incoming vehicle. what's most important about executing it is, Keep To Your Own Lane. which means, no wide turning, no sharp turning.

    you use engine brake when you need to slow down. for example, red light in the distance, reaching a junction where you need to make a turn etc. you do that by dropping to a suitable lower gear, and leave all pedals untouched. of course, brake when you need it.
