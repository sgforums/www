    The autioneer did gave an invitation to treat and is not consider an offer to hold it. At any auction, any bid that follows is considered as an offer. It might be interesting to note that, the sale is complete when the auction closes or when the 'reserve price' ie buy price is met and hence going into a legal contract.

    In all fairness and equity, the defendant may put up a defence on Unilateral mistake only if the seller knows that the buyer is mistaken concerning the offer. As far as such ruling is concerned, equity follows the common law and regards such contract as void.

    If I'm the seller of the WRX, i won't be losing sleep over this.... why? Because Yahoo Auction is not considered a 'Traditional Auctioneer'. meaning transactions in Yahoo Auction are not recognised as auction sales by the Sale of Goods Act ( Chapter 393 of S'pore Statues). Therefore the seller is not bounded by law where for auctions, a contract is sealed at the fall of the hammer.

    In my opinion: The seller merely put up an offer to sell his WRX at a starting price of $1. The buyer responded by offering to buy at $1. At this point, the ball is in the seller's court. It is his rights to accept or reject this offer of $1 made by the buyer.

    Therefore, there is no breach of contract law whatsoever.
