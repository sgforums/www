    The chap who started the thread wanted suggestions before he went for another race. Here are some of them.

    "I find if you're driving a kart with rear brakes only, you can apply the brake slightly coming out of a corner. It's like a manual form of traction control just like the extra rear brake pedal fitted to the 1997 McLaren. It cuts down the wheelspin on the exit and will improve acceleration."

    "Places where you have understeer, especially tight hairpins, you should try throwing your weight around and stepping the rear end out. It keeps the engine reved and maintains momentem."

    "dont let the engine bog down....
    try to keep the rear wheels spinning by locking them into the bends, stepping the rear out, them boot it to keep the revs up and wheels spinning......"

    "As a rule, it's quicker to be slow in, fast out. You only gain a fraction by braking later and have to sort the kart out for the exit. Wheras brake a little earlier and carry more speed through the corner, you'll have more control and carry that speed all the way onto the next straight.
    Leave the last-of-the-late braking for overtaking manouevres!"

    go karts r largely RWD only. never heard of a FWD or 4WD one. Some sprinters have front brakes, but by and large, the sprinters I raced only have a single rear brake disc on the main axle. Since the rear axle is one solid rod, tat means there is no rear differential either. This makes things very very challenging indeed.

    another thing I would do is to hold both the brakes n accel at the same time. because my sprint kart has a centrifugual clutch, to get faster accel out of corners, I would have to keep my engine revs up while using the brake to control the rate at which the wheel spins.     Also... there is the question of your line thru a corner. there r several different lines, depending on situation. this is where racecraft comes into play.
