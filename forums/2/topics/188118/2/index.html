    For those nsf military drivers

    If his vocation is a driver, he will attend a class3/4 driving course. Which comprises of learning how to drive land rover (class3) for few weeks and 3/5 tonner (class4) for the rest of the course.

    Driving test is based on class 4 vehicles; supposedly still using 3 tonner.
    The practical test will comprise of public rd, just like what the civilian learners will go thru. However, they have no circuit test, they do have a chance to drive in a circuit with the land rover but not tested. They will only be tested parking with a land rover(angular and parallel) and a tonner(angular). Before the practical, they will have to go thru driving simulators too.

    Whereas for the theory part... theory comprises of MCQ questions... something like the civilian one but they compress both basic and advance questions together as one paper. Another part is HSP (highway situation problem)... use toy cars and explain given situations. HSP is usually the killer for many pple... need to repeat 1 or 2 times or even more cos many drivers are not very educated and inevitably, expressing themselves verbally will not be such an ease.

    This is what i could remember. oh ya... and testers are from army side too... very senior ranking ones... most used to be regulars. Very strict, fierce and will not compromise one... I heard of pple cannot pass for over 8 times..

    Passing military license is definitely more tedious than civilian ones just by judging the size of the testing vehicle.

    Some non driver vocations require military license too... however some only need class 3 ones...so they will just train on class 3....everything on class 3 loh.

    There are conversion courses as well...
