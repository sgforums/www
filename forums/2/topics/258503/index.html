    No la..i doubt so..torsion beam set-up is not a independent suspension set-up..the reason why independent suspension set-up like multi-link set-up is favoured over non-independent suspension set-up is because independent suspension can maintain more contact between the tyres and road..But of course, independent suspension system more expensive..

    That's why the Golf GTi uses the multi-link set-up at the rear, when there's more contact between road and tyres, the car has more grip and is more stable..the torsion beam is a cheap suspension system and is not as good as a independent suspension system at maintaining contact between tyres and road..so less stable..

    I think Honda use torsion beam on Type-R is partially to save costs..but to put it in a nice way, Honda claims that their torsion beam is sophisticated enough to feel like a independent suspension system..
