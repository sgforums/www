    anyone care to explain whats the difference between power, torque and engine cap?

    Power of an engine is divided into two figures..one is horsepower (bhp) and the other one is torque (Nm)..both are equally important for acceleration and top speed..but if you want fast acceleration, a car with good torque will be good..In the case of the RX-8's rotary engine, torque ain't that good as is notorious with rotaries..so if you want good acceleration in the RX-8, you'll have to go all the way with the gas pedal..which equals to high fuel consumption..

    But I would think the RX-8 will still outperform the Accord Euro-R because no matter what, the RX-8's Renesis Rotary still has more power overall..

    Engine capacity is the space where fuel and air mixture combusts in the cylinders..The space between the cylinders and pistons added up will be the total capacity of the engine..

    E.g.: If one cylinder has a space of about 500cc and the engine has 4 cylinders, it has an engine capacity of 2.0-litres..

    Higher engine capacity usually equates to more power because with more fuel and air mixture burnt, more power is produced..
