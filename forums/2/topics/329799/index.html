    Singapore Motorshow, Make-A-Wish Foundation to help children with cancer

    SINGAPORE : The Singapore Motorshow has teamed up with the Make-A-Wish Foundation to grant the wishes of children with cancer.

    The Motorshow, which is now in its ninth year, will start on September 26. But even before the event begins, it has already helped fulfil the wish of two children, by sponsoring their dream holiday.

    One was to Disneyland, Tokyo and the other was a trip to Lotte World in South Korea.

    Tickets to the Motorshow - which will be held at Suntec City - cost S$8. And organisers will donate a portion of ticket sales made after the Formula One race weekend to the Make-A-Wish Foundation. - CNA/ms
