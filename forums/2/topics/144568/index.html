    There's a lot of factors to consider.

    your current outstanding loan. Need to call your finance company to find out how much you need to pay to repay (they need to deduct the interest amounts for remaining tenor)

    that means what car are you driving

    Since your car was reg. in mar 2000, your parf rebate when you scrape your car will be your omv*120%. you can find out the omv value of your car in the log card.

    Suppose you scrape in Sept, your coe will still have 4.5 yrs, scrape value of your car can be calculated by :

    scrape value now = (omv x 1.2) + 17.5k[coe paid x 4.5/10 yrs]
    take an omv of 14k, then scrape value now will be abt 34.3k

    by the tenth yr, your parf rebate will be left with 14k * 80% = 11.2k [coe zero]

    annual dep. will be abt (34.3 - 11.2)k / 4.5yrs = 5.1k

    if you can sell yr car higher than 34.3k now, then your dep will be even higher if you keep your current ride.

    if i am not wrong, annual dep. of a new vois is slightly less than 5k now (thats if you spread it over 10yrs), so i think you can consider changing. I think the waiting time for vois is about 3 mths now.

    Hi loss,

    I just started to learn as well. I'll try to answer your question.
    But if anyone reading this has something to add, please feel free to add in your comments, so we all can learn something.

    (2) Go to www.onemotoring.com.sg >> Online Enquires >> PARF/COE Rebate
    Add up "PARF Rebate Amount" & "COE Rebate Amount" which will give you the base value of your car.

    (3) Yes. 55% of OMV will give you the value of the car @ end of 10 years

    (4) Take the selling price of the car to minus the (55% of OMV), then divide by the remaining years of the COE to give you the annual depreciation. Do the same for your car and compare the depreciation amounts.

    (5) no outstanding loan, easier to calculate. No loans to repay.

    Hope the above helps.
