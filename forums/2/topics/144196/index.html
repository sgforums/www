    Hi bro,

    Looks like you are experiencing backfiring.

    Having said your van is modded giving better performance, i assume that you are now running on a richer air/fuel mixture than stock. I also suspect that your exhaust might have a little leak.

    The exhaust leak is probably the cause of the bubbly sound you were mentioning.

    There are a few reason of backfiring.

    I suspect for your case:

    its when you release the acclerator i.e. to change gear or just backing off the throttle when cruising (especially at high rpm since yours is a van travelling at 80km/h), unbunt fuel due to the rich mixture (due to you accelerating) will still be momentarily present and not be fully ignited in the combustion chamber. It flows to the hot exhaust and if there is enough of oxygen i.e. maybe due to exhaust leak, there could be ignition of the air/fuel mixture in the exhaust, causing backfiring.

    Check out with the guy driving behind you, a momentary burst of fire due to the backfiring might occur from the exhaust!!!

    One way to fix this is to seal the leak in the exhaust (if there is one), run on a leaner air/fuel mixture (be careful, too lean and it will lead to a MISFIRE! which is BAD FOR THE ENGINE).

    2 possiblities.

    either ur fren installed an anti lag system onto ur van or retuned the a/f to make it run richer.

    option one seems unlikely since it'll activate each time u release ur accelerator (not juz at 80 km/h). seems like option 2 is the likely scenario. contrary 2 popular belief, its not the case that unspent fuel igniting upon contact with hot exhaust that causes the presumed secondary combustion. the fact is ur exhaust pipe/ extractors will never get any hotter then ur combustion chambers/engine block itself.

    the release of ur accelerator causes a momentary starvation of air molecules entering ur combustion chambers. owing to the nature of a richer fuel map, the secondary combustion is the result when blowby of unburnt fuel (not enuff air molecules to burn with the additional fuel) as ur exhaust ports open and the unspent gases in one chamber pushed out during the exhaust stroke suddenly gets a breath of fresh air (literally) when it meets with minute amounts of oxygen from other chambers during deceleration, causing an uncontrolled combustion and the "pop" sound u described, accompanied with an occasional display of flames spewing from ur exhaust tips.

    dun think u have a leak in ur exhaust. if u did, i'd be sounding like a harley during idle.

    Hi Kron,

    from what i understand, misfiring is usually caused by spark plug failure; the air/fuel ratio is out of balance to ignite; or loss of compression.

    With the air/fuel ratio too lean, there will be lean misfire. This can be due to insufficient fuel pump pressure, fuel pressure regulator, fuel injectors.... etc.

    Thus an example in a car with turbo, if too high a boost pressure is set, and the fuel pump and fuel injectors cannot handle the fuel required for the air/fuel mapping, it will misfire due to the lean mixture.
