    Base on what I've seen and heard, I'm more inclined towards it being allowed. I'm not sure if I can so surely say, like bro D16Z6 above, that it is "definitely" illegal.

    I know some people have discussed that CF bonnets fail to crumple but shatter instead upon impact and thus it is definitely illegal. But although that is logical, it doesn't mean it's the stand LTA takes.

    Just like HID. If you buy a DC5R that comes with HID from factory, it is allowed. But let say you get a DC5GSR with no HID and install your own HID, its technically illegal. But not logical what? It's the exact same model of car, just that engine and tranny different.

    So it's best to get an answer straight from them than to base on 'logic' and 'hearsay'. Some logical things may be illegal and some illogical things are legal.

    I don't wish to guarantee you on it, that's why I told you its up to you. But personally, based on what my friend went through, I believe CF bonnet is allowed and would use one myself if I had the money to spare and even bring it for inspection without changing it back to stock.

    Actually, it seems more like a safety issue than anything else. Carbon fibre is known to be harder than steel and whatnot, but the thing is, it also comes in different grades. Without going into the technical aspects of its design and structure, some grades of carbon fibre are better than others. Some might shatter, some will look like it's been torn away. The other thing which might be in LTA's list of considerations is the number of layers that are present in that particular product. The more layers probably = to safer for them cause it's going to be harder.
