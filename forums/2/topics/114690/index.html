    Just to add:=
    The intangible advantage of 1st hand car is the good smell of a new car, peace of mind warranty from dealers and the feeling of not owning a 2nd hand stuff.
    2nd hand car is cheaper in purchase price but does not mean that depreciation is always lower. While it is 90% true, there is still the remaining 10%
    A new car if you drive for 10 years average out depreciation might be lower than if you buy 2nd hand car at a lower price but spread thru a shorter.

    Simple illustration:
    (forget abt scrap value and things like that)

    New car purchase @ $70,000
    70000 / 10yrs = 7000 per year, $583 a month

    3 year old car purchase @ $50,000
    50000/7yrs = 7143 per year, $595 a month

    Thus 2nd hand cost less but depreciate more,
