    As for the pricing, 2nd hand car are priced base on the annual depreciation spread over remaining life-span. So, taking a new car of the same model and look at the annual depreciation spread over 10yrs, will give you an idea of the max you should pay for a 2nd hand car. [Actually, even if the annual dep. is the same, you'll still lugi less with a 2nd hand car when you sell in 3yrs time..... it's just the mental thing about getting a used car with a perceived annual dep that is tha same as a new car... ]

    As for a fair value, I guess it all depends on how much discount you think you should get for a used car vs a brand new car. As long as you think the savings you get is worth the risk of a used car, who is to say you are wrong
