    Watch out for the following fine print:
    - make sure it's all-cash deal with cashier's check
    - negotiate for the road tax rebate (the unused portion of the road tax is supposed to be refunded to the seller but most of the time, the dealer takes it without telling you)
    - make sure they don't charge you the 5% discount on the paper value (common tactic which reduces the net payment to you significantly)
    - get the insurance refund for unused time (the unused months is refunded to seller but this is sometimes left out because it's extra work for the dealer)
    - ONLY show the dealer the car AFTER you have removed everything you want (alloy rims, stereo, leather, etc.) because once they view it, the deal is as viewed basis, so you can't take them out after that
    - make sure when you give them the car, get a signed receipt that shows the time and day of handover. Otherwise, if any accident happens or it's involved in a crime, you're responsible.

    I learned all the above the hard way, after trading in 3 cars already...
