    lets assume both same car, same design, same tire, same road contdition only that one car fetch more ppl than the other to create the weight different.

    well, lighter car will actually oversteer 1st because quite simply, less weight pressing down on the wheels to keep it in contact with road surface. HEavier car has more inertia, so when the same steering force is applied to both cars, what happens is the heavier car will change direction more slowly, while the lighter car will change direction much faster. As a result, the heavier car will 1stly be sluggish, almost perhap it will understeer. Due to its inertia, and due to the extra weight on the tyres pressing it down into contact with the road. Esp in a car which has more people than another car of the same make n brand, most of this weight will go over the rear wheels, thus making it even harder to oversteer. In fact, common sense tells us if u try to take a corner too fast with a heavy car, u will go straight on off the road! That is understeer and it is the terminal opposite of oversteer.

    Hope this clears up any confusion u have about weight of a car and its implication on it oversteering.

    2 reasons.

    1) Assuming frontal impact, due to the lower curvature on the front of the MX5, the distance of its front wheels to the front, and the force carrying it into the curb, it will certainly mount the curb even if its clearance is low. Hence clearance is not much of a factor in this case

    2) If it was a sideways impact with the curb, then clearance is not even in consideration because if u consider a lightweight block of wood with NO clearance, hitting a low curb hard enough, will still tip over.

    For your case of talking about SUVs flipping, u're referring to a topple without striking anything else, occuring due to an oversudden change of direction, ie. flipping due to its high CG and inertia alone. In the case of SUV flipping, clearance does not play any major factor here other than adding to the CG of the SUV.
