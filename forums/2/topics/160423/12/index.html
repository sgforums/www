    I agree. drifting, or more accurately, oversteer can occur even at low speeds. And I do have to remind people that the MX5 can BE this badly smashed up not because of its speed alone, but the resultant speed during moment of impact. What do i mean by resultant speed?

    Normally when we think about a car hitting the wall, the speed at moment of impact is the speed of the car alone. But that is because the wall is stationary. Now imagine if a car is travelling at 40km/h, and the wall is travelling towards the car at 40km/h as well, resultant speed will be 40+40k = 80km/h

    Similiarly, resultant speed of the impact between the MX5 and the BMW will be the speed of the MX5 AND the speed of the BMW. I don't think its fair to say the BMW driver is speeding either. Might I remind many of u here that Possum Bourne died in 03 after his Forester hit another competitor's recce car head on during pre-event recce. As that was an official event, recce speeds were limited to 30km/h. But as it was a head on collision, the resultant speed was 60km/h, which was enought to kill Possum even tho he had his seatbelt on.

    I think, right now unless there is enough evidence to say that someone was speeding, we should all refrain from such baseless accusation. I AM very surprised to read that many of you in your frenzy to throw accusations, totally forgot about the physics of resultant speed.

    Of course just because the MX5 was hit on the side by the BMW doesn't mean it doesn't have any velocity in the direction of the BMW. velocity is not fixed in the same direction as the front of the car.
