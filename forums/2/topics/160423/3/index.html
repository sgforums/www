If you bang into them, they take speed off you. Its a law of physics which cannot be changed - when you bang into something stationary, you loose energy and speed. Its not like going over a smooth angled road hump (which is a ramp) - and even that causes you to lose speed.

    (3) If you are travelling at 50 km/hr before you hit the kerb, you will be MUCH slower than 50 km/hr after hitting the kerb. Conversely, if you are going at 50 km/hr (or any speed which can make you flip) after hitting the kerb, then you are probably going much faster than 50 km/hr before hitting the kerb.

    (4) If you are at X km/hr before hitting the kerb, and still at X km/hr after hitting the kerb, then something is wrong with your speedometer. Either that, or the kerb and tyres are totally and perfectly frictionless and has the ability to change the direction of force/motion (from horizontal to vertically up, then to horizontal again) with 100% efficiency, ie. no energy loss.
