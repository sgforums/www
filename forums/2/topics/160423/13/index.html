    Ok. 1stly many people keep overlooking the point that HANDLING DOES NOT EQUAL STABILITY

    Its simple. A heavier car will be more stable. U are wrong in saying that a heavier car will oversteer easier. It is incorrect in using momentum to explain this. U should be looking at inertia. Inertia is the force that resists change in velocity. Velocity is speed x direction. When u change your direction, even if speed remains constant, your velocity changes. Inertia is what resists change to velocity, and inertia is basically dependent on weight. So higher weight = higher inertia. U would need more force to make a heavier car change velocity(skid) because u would need to overcome the inertia.

    The MX5 is light. Good handling, but less stable. Lesser weight on the wheels, pressing the wheel down into contact with the ground. Besides, the MX5 has a short wheelbase. Cars with short wheelbases handle very well. But are more unstable. Everyone knows a go-kart's handling is excellent. But it is also very unstable. Doesn't take much for u to spin it by mistake. A car which handles well but which has to depend on mechanical means for most of its grip will always be more unstable compared to a car which handles less well. Don't have the mistaken impression that just because its a car which handles well, it shouldn't have spun or skidded easily at low speeds.
