    Originally posted by nullifi3d:
    By all measn research on it but don't try it on your own especially on public roads. Get a track instructor to teach you the fundamentals in a controlled environment. Wrong usage of heel and toe can result in fishtails.
    Fishtails.. unlikely lah..

    But agree with some professional coach can learn faster.

    For starters, practice your footwork 1st (stationary), then try it at lower speed on straight roads. Try brake and downshifting at the same time.. if you do not blip your throttle and throw your clutch while downshifting, the action will be rough and may unsettle the car. Try blip the throttle before throwing your clutch to achieve a smooth downshift.

    Action goes this way..

    1. Brake (into a turn)
    2. Clutch in
    3. Downshift
    4. Blip throttle while still holding to brake (this is where footwork comes in)
    5. release clutch

    Try performing step 2~5 within 1sec and at the same time achieve smooth downshift (not by releasing your clutch slowly coz in a race, your clutch will overheat in no time).

    How much to blip? say you are in your 3rd gear and traveling at 150km/h, 5000rpm. you brake and slow to 100km/h @ 3000rpm, at this speed, if you down shift into 2nd gear, your RPM should, say be at 6000rpm. This means you need to blip your throttle to 6000rpm before releasing your clutch.

    Hope its not too complex.. keke.. If still dun understand, try signing up for an adv driving course (hopefully they still teach this, coz nowadays all semi auto, computer blip throttle for driver).

    OK, having said the above, please drive carefully and responsibly on public roads.

    Cheers!
