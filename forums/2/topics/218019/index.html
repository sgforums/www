    The petrol basically referes to Octane Number ... it refers to the stability of the pertrol .. there was another thread on this earlier.. somebody actually replied scientifically.. you go search for that thread.

    Basically, as long you engine is build for that Octane Number, that's the minimum you should use. So for example a Vios is made to take in 95 OCT petrol,

    it makes no diff if you use 95 or 98. But if you use 92, it will cause pinging in your engine which ain't very good.

    Refer to your instruction manual to the minimum you should use.

    As for the bonnet of the car, in theory yes, it causes misalignment. But you are not driving an aircraft or a space shuttle. so it doesn't matter if it is misaligned so long you don't travel in speeds of 1000km/h

    One way to prevent such misalaignment is you "drop close" the bonnet.

    Most ppl will just close the bonnet and press down to latch it. This will cause the bonnet to arch over time.
