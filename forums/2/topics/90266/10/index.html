  • ed2004
    11 Nov 04, 14:54
    Originally posted by RENOMA13:
    Izzit....At least i tried..hee
    Anyway, hopefully something will come up later

    Btw, would like to share some comments after driving for 3 mths. Not sure if other ppl have similar experiences
    1) Very slow pick up.. Step on accerlator and still cannot rush off fast
    2) Feel that the car is tilting very badly when doing acute turning... Have to maintain speed at 50km whn doing my turns..haiz
    3) Hand brake seems to be less grip as time goes by. Have to pull firmly all the way up...
    4) Lastly, the creaking sound when u off your engine


    1) Automatic car does not take plain hard pushes. You have to find the G-Spot. i.e. light acceleration to get the momentum (about 0.5 - 1.5 secs) then floor it. I find that after installing Pivot Volt Stab and Z-Stab, my acceleration has further improved. www.z-stab.com

    2) If you do a lot of hard-cornering, have front camberkit installed. This does not turn your car into Skyline but greatly improve handling and reduce tyre wear and tear. Check this out with Riverview Auto (AMK), look for Kenny but appointment need cos REX and EVO always occupy his time slot. Superb skill and knowledge. I would also warn that rims/tyres selection play the more important part before this.....

    3) During regular servicing, you can ask the workshop to tighten up. Reduce the loose-effect by press-n-hold the knob before pulling up the hand brake i.e. dun pull up directly and hear the kekekekekekee.....I find the know-how and service quality is good at Riverview Auto. (www.riverviewauto.com.sg)Look for Vincent. Tell him Ed2004 recommended.

    4) Normal sound during cooling off. If you are worried, do check the flooring for fresh oil spill in the parking lot when driving off in the morning. I do regular check to ensure no oil leaks......

    Hope this helps.

    12 Nov 04, 13:31
    When increase the tyre, width and profile must be adjusted accordingly. Use this calculator to check and make sure diameter difference is keep at minimum.http://www.miata.net/garage/tirecalc.html On the left enter your current tyre size, On the right enter your new tyre size, You can then see if there will be any changes in your speedo.

    Experienced tyreshop will also advise you of the correct offset for respective car. This is very important.

    Whatever you do, stay away from the ECU or warrantly will definitely void.

    Z-stab will not void the warranty. If there is any worry, remove the gadget before going back to distributor.

    Struct bar - no reason why warranty should be void.
