    By Christopher Tan
    March 22, 2005
    The Straits Times

    Subarus with sirens on the way
    COMING soon to a highway near you: the fast and powerful Subaru Impreza WRX, decked out in orange-on-white livery and adorned with flashing blue lights.

    The cars will be the latest additions to the Traffic Police's fleet.

    After a public tender in December, which also drew bids from BMW, Volvo and Skoda, the decision to go for the WRX was made yesterday.

    The model, known for its powerful 225-horsepower engine, quick acceleration and stable all-wheel-drive technology, will be the first Japanese car to join the fleet, which currently comprises BMWs and Volvos.

    Subaru distributor Motor Image will supply 12 to the Traffic Police, with the first due late next month.

    According to tender award details on the government eBusiness website, they cost $618,000 - nearly half the cost of a similar order from BMW or Volvo.

    Motor Image chief executive Glenn Tan told The Straits Times: 'Being quick, the cars will also improve their response time, so they can avoid long car chases.'

    The WRX was the fastest and least expensive of the contenders in the tender, which included Volvo's S60, Skoda's Octavia RS and BMW's newly launched 320i - all sporty models.

    But Mr Tan, 27, said competitive pricing - the Subarus were 20 per cent cheaper than the Skodas, the next lowest bid - would not have been enough to clinch the deal with the police.

    'They have conducted extensive tests on the cars. The WRX is also used by the police in other countries, such as Italy and Australia.'

    Mr Tan said that the police purchase would go some way towards his efforts 'to reposition the brand', a reference to the view held by some that the WRX is for 'boy-racers'.

    Asked why they picked the car, and if the model's current image was a concern, the police said: 'We do not favour a particular model or make, and we choose our vehicles based on our needs and prices.'
