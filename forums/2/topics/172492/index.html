    WRONG.

    If you go Shell website and take a look, they will say there is absolutely no difference to use a RON 98 fuel if your car is designed to take RON 95.

    The only difference you will feel is if you use V-power with the others because of the additives. Otherwise V-power is just a RON 98 fuel with additive.

    Why there are different grades available is not for consumer choice but rather the wide range of cars we have here.

    A turbocharged vehicle and higher-end cars will need a higher ron (RON 9 because their engines are designed to combust fuel at a given interval. If the fuel combust lesser than expected to, it will not deliver the 'expansion of the gases" required and thus detrimental to your engine.

    As for you saying if your vehicle feels better after an oil change or any other fluid change. This is simply because you are using a new fluid.

    Fluids in your vehicle will collect grime and become more viscous over time. This gives the explanation why you feel your vehicle is smoother after a service.
