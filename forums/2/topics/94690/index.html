    CNA - Two badly burnt after minivan catches fire in AMK accident
    21 September 2004 2011 hrs (SST)

    http://www.channelnewsasia.com/stories/singaporelocalnews/view/107795/1/.html

    SINGAPORE : A man and woman were badly burnt when their minivan burst into flames after crashing into a tree.

    The accident happened near the junction of Ang Mo Kio Avenue 5 around 2pm on Tuesday.

    Just minutes earlier, the minivan was engulfed in flames, and a female passenger was trapped in the vehicle.

    The driver, though hurt, managed to escape.

    One eyewitness said, "I saw the burning, the fire. The girl was shouting. It's really really sad because the fire (was) so big...at one occasion when they tried to pull her, she shouted in pain."

    Another added, "The man (was) also burn(t), the leg and the hand (could not move), (he could just) just lie down only...but the man (was) already out, and then the girl (was) still stuck inside because of her leg...stuck with the seat."

    Eyewitnesses also said that bystanders tried to help pull the female passenger out, but the flames made it impossible to rescue her.

    Several workers from a nearby construction site also rushed to the scene with fire extinguishers.

    The driver suffered second degree burns over 40 percent of his body, while his female passenger suffered second and third degree burns over 60 percent of her body.

    Both were taken to the Singapore General Hospital and the cause of the fire is being investigated. - CNA
