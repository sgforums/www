    Started a thread on this due to no response to my query in this thread.

    Based on what the instructors at my driving school said, the correct method is to use right foot for both pedals with left foot on the floor board (for safety reasons).

    I was used to using both foot (lesser time spent between right foot shuffling both peddles) until instructors said it isn't safe. And these days even when I use only my right foot, trying to curb the habit, it gets extremely uncomfortable. So I'm garnering opinions from experienced drivers.

    Wikipedia says it is generally advised against because:
    1) In emergency braking situations, it is common for the driver to extend both legs in a panic reaction. If the right foot is on the throttle, this will cause unwanted and potentially dangerous acceleration.

    2) When the left foot is often used for depressing the clutch pedal this generally requires more force than operating the brake. A driver accustomed to applying this amount of force may unexpectedly slow down to an unsafe speed when attempting to use the left-foot braking technique.

    3) Most road users find it extremely irritating when they keep seeing the brake lights from other drivers for no apparent reason.

    4) When you depress brake and acceleration simultaneously, it will cause wear and tear.
