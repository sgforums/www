    I received the said offence today (25/07/06) for breaking law number 12(1) as printed on your "notice of parking offence". On the overleaf, 12(1) says "Failure to obey sign(s) displayed in a parking place.Â”

    This is utterly ridiculous. Firstly, I made an effort to look for signs when I arrived at this road (Ann Siang Road). It was not even a car park in the first place. It was merely a road. It had 2 lanes, separated by a dotted line - which means parking on both sides of the road are ALLOWED unless there are other lines drawn on either side. I was happy as this meant I had found a place to park and I thought the government was so helpful as to draw boxes to guide cars to park. However, to be on the safe side, I looked for signs and found a CARPARK sign. So I realised I had to pay to occupy these lots but I still decided to park here. I followed the vehicle in front of me and it stopped to park in an empty lot on the right that was ahead of me. I looked around me to my left and right for other empty lots. There were none on my left. At this moment, a vehicle vacated a lot on my right and waved at me to signal that I could occupy his lot. It was an adult male. I thanked him with a wave and, just like the car in front of me, I parked into the empty lot on the right. Directly in front of me was a lorry that I could only see the back of. In other words, he was facing in the same direction as me. The vehicle behind me was ALSO facing this direction. So were all the cars on the left.

    As a relatively new driver, I look to seasoned drivers for guidance. I parked properly and made sure my car was in the lot. When I got out, I even walked one round around my vehicle to make sure I was not obstructing traffic. I then promptly went to the ONLY sign and read it so as to know how much to pay for half an hour. There were NO OTHER SIGNS around. I promptly OBEYED the displayed sign in that parking place and put a $1 coupon on my dashboard. I then locked the car and left.

    To my horror, I found the said notice under my wipers upon my return. The offence was as I said - rule 12(1). Another line wrote that I was fined for parking inside a parking lot - against traffic flow 19 (*). This line is in itself ridiculous! How can I be "against traffic flow" when I'm "in a parking lot"! Parking lots can't possibly go against traffic flows! They're STATIONARY RECTANGLES DRAWN ON THE SURFACE! Furthermore, as I am a relatively new driver - I still have my basic theory fresh in my mind. There is NO LAW STATED in the theory books that you HAVE TO face a certain direction when your car is parked! I was taught that I could park head in or head out for vertical - it was my choice! Likewise for parallel - I was taught that parallel parking could be done both to the left and right! It didn't matter which way I faced my vehicle as long as I was in a legal parking spot / parking lot! How can I be fined for facing my car in a direction the URA did not like? Furthermore - most of the other cars were parked like this! I followed the example of the lorry in front and the car behind me as well as the car that vacated the lot and the car that parked before me! Granted - the lorry was facing head-to-head with another car. But what problem does that pose?? What is the logic behind this rule?

    On top of that - I was even accused of disobeying a sign - of which doesn't even EXIST! There was no sign that specifically stated that "Cars parked on this side of the road MUST face in ---> Direction. Or else FINE $50." If there was such a sign or anything likewise, I would have promptly made a LEGAL 3 POINT TURN - taking into account the dotted line in the middle of the road (which means u-turning on this road is legal). However, this would simply be another ridiculous thing to do as this would obstruct traffic flow! Which I believe is WORSE than what direction a vehicle faces when in a parking lot! Other than being aesthetically beautiful to see all vehicles on one side of the road facing in the same direction, I DO NOT SEE what logic could have possibly made this a law!
