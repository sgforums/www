    "We wanted you to feel the breeze"

    For the ultra-wealthy seeking splendid isolation from the cruel world outside, there's nothing - nothing - better than a Rolls-Royce Phantom saloon. And, like the moated castle or long fur coat which do the same job in other circumstances, it makes itself patently obvious.

    As if the colossal size and Parthenon chrome grille weren't enough, those who own these limousines oftentimes want their identity and taste clearly identifiable, what with their vanity plates and their chauffeurs, so they can elegantly disembark from the rear door in a haze of flashguns at the conspicuous end of the red carpet. But there's a paradox here.

    Once safely inside a Phantom, the isolation is visual as well as sensory. The drawbridge is up and they're hidden and anonymous. By the same token the view out is less than panoramic. The sights, the sounds, the air and the aromas of the outside world are blocked out as effectively as the bumps, knocks, noises and all those other rude sensations of motion.
