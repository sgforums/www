    The only thing AMERICAN about the Chevrolets brought in by Starsauto in Singapore is the badge! All the current cars in their line-up (Aveo, Optra) here are based on platforms developed by Daewoo (now renamed GM Daewooo) which is regarded as the 3rd largest KOREAN car manufacturer after Hyundai and Kia.

    That's why the Chevys here still exhibit the typical Korean traits:
    1) Poor FC
    2) Poor reliability
    3) Rough engine/transmission
    and hence poor resale value.

    It disgusts me that some Chevy owners here think they are buying a superior AMERICAN car compared to their Japanese/Korean/Malaysian counterparts. Even my American friend who's driven REAL Chevrolets says the pseudo "Chevrolets" sold here spoils the brand's reputation.

    Having said that, the quality of Korean cars have improved significantly from the old days and will only get better with time. The popularity of Hyundai & Kia, and now Chevrolet, has shown that Singaporeans are willing to sacrifice some quality for what they perceive as "value", so to each his own.
