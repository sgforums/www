    What do you wish to know? Most Evo 6 have been scrapped due to the high paper value. As for Evo 8, the yearly depreciation is >10K+. IMO the later generations are not any worse off to drive, just that the earlier cars were more visceral. By that I mean that the suspension setup becomes softer but the AYC has been improving with each iteration.

    The Evo 6 is relatively light, net weight of 1280 kg, but the AYC program which was introduced with Evo 4 was not quite there yet. The Evo 8 is the first truly livable daily driver from factory, and the MR improves upon it. Do note that when purchasing such cars, especially preowned, you would have to do a lot of research into it, find out the car's history, find out what kind of mods have been done to it.
