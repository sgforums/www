  • babyek
    13 Feb 04, 14:29
    normally it depends on the make & model of the car u are getting
    also, how much of downpayment u can pay upfront and the no. of years of instalment payment.

    after getting ur car, the 1st mth is always very carefree becos, you would have paid the 1st instalment of ur car into your downpayment.
    u only start paying from 2nd mth onwards.

    every mth
    instalment
    petrol
    season parking (open space= $55 / multi-storey=$90)
    * if u pay by giro, there is a rebate of a few dollars *
    car park charges/coupons (parking at elsewhere)

    every 6 mths
    road tax
    (inclusive of radio licence and amt payable depends of year, cc of car)

    every 1 year
    car insurance (depends on amt insured, owner's driving experience, NCD (if have), occupation, age, etc)

    others expenses
    maintenace. wise to set aside 200-300 for unforeseen repairs which may occur if u are buying a 2nd hand car. usually 1st hand cars are covered by warranty for the first 3 yrs.
    amt saved will be used for changing tyres when the thread wear out

    i think u shud count how much u can set aside every mth, then decide on wat car u can buy... of course everyone has a dream car.
    but if u dun plan, u will end up feeling very stressed ...

    Hi,

    I'm driving a 12 yr old COE car.

    Including monthly instalments, road tax, insurance, petrol, parking, maintenance and other incidentals, I spend about $1,500 per month on my car.

    Of course petrol is a little on the high side ($400-500 per month) compared to most others ($150-300), and I do spend quite a bit each year on small upgrades (sound system, tyres, wheels,...).

    But it's the unforseen ones, like the changing of clutch assembly, alternator, radiator, water pump... that starts to accumulate. Not to mention the occasional "fender bender".

    In the end, despite all the excess spending, I actually have grown very fond of my car.
