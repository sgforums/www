    I assume u are talking about McLaren International the company?

    We have to remember that McLaren 1st and foremost was set up as a racing company. Up till more recently, their focus were on Formula 1. The McLaren F1 was conceived as a showcase and celebration of the company's design expertise and heritage in motorsports. And even then, im sure not many of you know this: the mclaren F1 was only conceived because of a delayed flight. The concept for the car was thought up back around 1990. Ron Dennis, Gordon Murray, Mansour Ojjeh and someone else whom I can't remember now, were on a flight back after a Formula 1 race. The flight got delayed and thus the 4 of them sat around in the departure lounge, and after a while, the idle chat got around to the topic of making a roadcar to celebrate the company's heritage and showcase their expertise. And that, was the start of the idea for the McLaren F1. The rest, as they say, is history.

    Anyways today, McLaren's company structure is diverse. There is the parent company McLaren Group, which comprises all the smaller McLaren companies. These companies are:

    McLaren Racing

    McLaren Racing, the company behind Team McLaren Mercedes, was formed in 1980 following the merger of Team McLaren and Project Four, Ron Dennis' racing company. Operating in the highly competitive and technological environment of Formula One, McLaren Racing comprises a multitude of administrative and engineering departments, ranging from vehicle design and aerodynamics, to Materials Science and the paint shop.

    McLaren Automotive

    McLaren Automotive was created in 2003, formally bringing together under one name the companies previously known as McLaren Cars and McLaren Composites.

    McLaren Automotive has two sites, one at Portsmouth and the other at the McLaren Technology Centre. The Portsmouth site operates as a world player in the design and manufacture of advanced composites, and is involved in production of the complex body shell of the Mercedes-Benz SLR McLaren programme.

    The site at the McLaren Technology Centre incorporates the state-of the art production line for the Mercedes-Benz SLR McLaren. Production of the car began in 2003.

    McLaren Marketing

    McLaren Marketing was formed in 1987, primarily to service the marketing and media requirements of McLaren Racing. The company's role has constantly evolved to serve the growing needs of other companies within and outside the McLaren Group, and McLaren Marketing now consists of eight groups working in concert: Business Development; Creative; Hospitality; Media and Communications; Special Projects, Filming and Photographic; Finance; Partner Management and Partner Programmes.

    McLaren Electronic Systems

    McLaren Electronic Systems develops and manufactures automotive control systems for many different motor sport series, including Formula One, World Rally and Le Mans. Its wide range of products and components are utilised by nearly 100 customers world-wide, including sales to most of the cars on the Formula One grid.

    The company provides the complete automotive control system used by the Team McLaren Mercedes team to deliver the optimum performance available from the engine and chassis. McLaren Electronic Systems has a team of over 100 highly qualified engineers and skilled technicians.

    McLaren Applied Technologies

    McLaren Applied Technologies Limited was formed in 2004 and is the latest company within the McLaren Group.

    The primary objective of the company is to work within a diverse range of industries and develop commercial applications for technology developed within the McLaren Group.
