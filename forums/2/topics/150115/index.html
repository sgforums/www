    Originally posted by Steph84:
    how come??? can explain?
    Here's the mechanical fundamentals behind it:

    Considering motion in a profile plane, the vehicle is a compound pendulum. If the front wheels strike a bump, a reaction will be felt by passengers unless the center of percussion is located at or near the rear axle. The reverse is true as the rear wheels strike a bump. A reaction will be felt unless the center of percussion is at or near the front axle.

    As a consequence, good vehicle design places the centre of percussion about one axle with the center of oscilation about the other.
