    ERP car park system has two integrated systems into it...

    One is the IU reader connected to a main computer for reading and detection of IU units in the vehicles and Cashcard IC chip, computation and data logging of entry time, IU serial number, Cashcard value, exit time, amount deducted...

    Another system is the auto-barrier system... The motor or actuator of the barrier will only activate upon a pulse signal sent to the controller from the main computer which will then raise the barrier...

    At the sametime, there will be an active IR sensor mounted which will send the IR beam, if the beam does not hit obstacle or obstruction based on a pre-programmed range of timing, the IC on the controller card will send a pulse signal to activate the barrier to lower...

    However, in these sensor based barrier systems, the IR sensors are often mounted at an angle to offset and compensate for the time to tigger the barrier to lower... In layman's term, it means that as long as the sensor detects obstacle or obstruction, it will not close till the obstacle or obstruction is cleared... Normally the offset angle is around 5 to 10 degrees... Enough for a second vehicle to tailgate close enough, but without kissing the bumper of the car in front, to "bypass" the barrier system...

    So in this scenario of the Rav4 driver, probably she knows this loophole and thus making it to her convenience...
