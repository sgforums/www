    The difference is in the octane rating (RON), 95 vs 98. The higher the rating, the more resistant is the fuel to premature detonation i.e. premature detonation = BAD NEWS FOR THE ENGINE.

    Each car has a minimum required octane rating which can be found in the owners manual. That would be just right. Having a higher octane rating than minimum required will not serve a better purpose for the engine. It will just burn a hole in the pocket.

    High performance car usually need a higher octane rating due to higher compression ratio from engine or forced induction i.e (turbo/supercharger).
