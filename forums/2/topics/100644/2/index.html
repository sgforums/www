    Uhhh, Heng, you seem to have missed the obvious here: the over-revving is occurring, but it's not upon acceleration - it's upon downshifting.

    Keep in mind that one of the regulation changes this year (the set which was suppposed to have been introduced in the middle of last year but which were delayed) include the banning of automatic downshifting.

    Rev limiters operate by limiting the number of firing cylinders - the loss of power prevents further increase of revs. However, when downshifting, what affects the revs is not the engine power (since fuel has been decreased by a closed throttle) but the torsional force transmitted to the crankshaft from the wheels via the driveshaft. The result: even with minimal fuel, the wheels turning at 100 MPH are still going to keep the engine at 17000 rpm in third gear (or 18700 at 110 MPH!). That's where your overreving is coming from, and rev limiters are of no use here.

    Automatic downshifting would have attenuated for this problem, but since they've been banned, forget that one. Drivers downshifting too quickly will break their engines.
