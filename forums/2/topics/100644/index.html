    And I've got a nasty suprise for you but engine failure CAN be due to the driver's fault. If you consider the fact that Taku had more failures than Button, then certainly driver factor is involved, despite what the team says. Button is known to be very smooth. Taku is less so. You may not know, but driving a car roughly, pushing it too hard, braking later etc, actually subject the crankshaft, engine, etc, to undue stresses. Its not just the engine's temperature increasing and decreasing during a pit. Let me show u a scenario:

    For a certain corner, Button starts braking 5m earlier. He is on the brakes gentler than Taku. He downshifts slightly later to allow for the revs to drop more than Taku does. Taku, because he brakes late, only can start downshifting later. He brakes from a higher speed which means his engine is at a higher RPM, and he has to downshift faster and sooner after he starts braking than Button. At highr RPMs, the engine is subject to more stress. So perhaps for those few seconds of downshifting, he subjects the engine to an extra, say 500RPMs of stress. 500 RPM may not seem like much, but as the RPMs increase, the stress and wear increase in a non-linear fashion. And then add to the fact that there are possibly 5 to 6 such corners in a circuit(not including the fast corners), and the fact that this situation is repeated over and over again. You can see why Taku's engine breaks.

    I am not being unfair in saying that Taku is still too rough on the car. Jacques Villeneuve, 97 F1 champion, is known to be a rough driver as well. When he was with BAR Honda in 03, the same situation was happening, in that he broke more engines than Button did. The key to Sato improving now, is that he actually smoothens his driving out, and he will definately improve.
