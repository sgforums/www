    Typically in an engine, the alternator, coolant pump and a/c compressor are all powered by the crankshaft pulley, with the belt running all around them. If you remove the belt, the engine will still start, but you see the battery light coming on in your instrument panel, meaning there's no charging... which means no power from the alternator. Why? cos no belt to transmit power to the alternator. same if you try to turn on the a/c... blower will still run, but it will not be cold, cos compressor not running. And then your car will overheat in a matter of minutes cos the coolant is not running. Get the idea?

    Today's car now usually have built in pressure control inside the a/c compressor... meaning it will not keep switching on and off to control the a/c gas pressure in the system; the compressor will keep running all the time. Older compressors rely on the switching on and off of the the whole unit to regulate the gas pressure inside the a/c system... hence you'll feel the compressor kicking in at times during driving or idling.
