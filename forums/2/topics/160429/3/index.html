    Dealers usually let only mature drivers test drive sports cars
    By Yvonne Cheong, Channel NewsAsia

    SINGAPORE : A test drive turned into a tragedy on Saturday, when a 24-year-old prospective buyer crashed the sports car he was driving.

    His passenger, a 22-year-old car agent, died in the accident.

    Some car dealers Channel NewsAsia spoke to say they only allow mature customers and those with years of driving experience to test drive such cars.

    The Mazda MX-5 was reduced to scrap metal after the test drive along Upper Paya Lebar Road went terribly wrong.

    Most car dealers say they usually would not allow prospective buyers to test drive such high-performance cars, as drivers are more likely to lose control of the vehicle if they are not careful.

    Besides, serious buyers don't usually test drive as they are well aware of the car's performance.

    Said Douglas Chua, a sales agent at World Of Wheels, "We do not allow the car engine to be revved -- max 3,000, 3,000-plus that's all. We have a designated what we call road for you to go. Young drivers tend to be impulsive so at this moment they feel like, I want to speed. They will just step on the throttle regardless of who is in the car."

    Dealers say they will also look at the customer's driving experience and stipulate they must be older than 28 years of age, before they can test drive.

    Others also require customers to sign a document, which states the car agent has the right to demand that the driver pulls over if he breaches any traffic regulation.

    But most customers say rules aside, what is more important is that the driver exercise caution and not exceed the speed limit. - CNA /ct
