    5 Apr 05, 16:22
    May I also add that anyone taking their TP test, don't get the 0815 and the 1145 slot, that's because 0815 => bus lane time plus loads of pple taking the PIE Eunos entrance.

    As for the 1145, you do the circuit for about 20 minutes, by the time you go out to open road, it'll be early 1200, where everyone in Ubi is going out for lunch etc..... changing lanes, especially if you get your test route at Route 6/7 (the one where you pass the Eunos flyover and past the PIE Eunos entrance),
    best of luck changing lane, it won't a normal 'no car just change lane',

    You'll have to moderate your speed so that you don't cut too quickly in front of the car behind and also change lane.

    Ubi, personally is one of the toughest but best place to learn driving.

    If you can pass Ubi Route 6 & 7, Orchard Road on weekends isn't too far behind.

    Like my private instructor said:

    Q:"Where is the Bangkok of Singapore?"

    A:" Ubi Road lor!"

    Hahha, yah, Route 6 & 7 are killer routes.

    I remembered I had 40 pple with me in the Ubi test room before my test.

    Out of 40 pple, only 12 pass.

    And only one lady who took the Route 6/7 out of six passed.

    Personally I think Route 1/10 (the route where you go through a big round through the HDB flats and then left turn on red light) is one of the easiest.

    The U Turn is easy and it's within the factory areas so changing lane is not difficult at all.

    6 & 7, can be a dud at times, if you're lucky, pple stay away from you and let you cut... although majority of the time lane changing on 6 & 7 isn't easy.

    You have to be aggressive and skilled, it's a challenge for some of us, but for all learning drivers, it's a much unwanted thing you ever want to do.
