  • Kuali Baba
    15 Jun 05, 23:42
    Montreal places a premium on the rear tyres because of the hard acceleration and severe weight shifts those tyres experience when the cars exit the slow bends (and hit the straights). Imola is similar in nature.

    The 'Ring has plenty of bends which require the driver to turn the wheels to full lock, hence the front tyres go through more punishment.

    Barcelona has an abrasive surface, no prizes for guessing that softer tyres wear out quickly there.

    Australia and Monaco are kind on the tyres, as they are slow and lack long straights and high-speed bends.

    Originally posted by finoq:
    wad happens if it rains..? the wet tyres?
    You seldom see wet weather tyres wear out like the dry weather ones because the corners are taken more slowly and braking distances become longer. The drivers do so to avoid aquaplaning or spinning the cars.

    However, the compound is very soft and heats up quickly once the track dries up. The tyres now become ineffective and are a liability.
