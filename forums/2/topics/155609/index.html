    in layman terms, any rim that are not standard items on ur car in stock condition are generally referred to as "sports rims" in local context. most car dealers only offer "sports rims" as options unless u are talking bout performance models such as the evo and rex where sports rims come standard.

    there are a myriad of aftermarket sports rims which vary in their design and method of production which eventually affects their cost. depending on the condition and aftermarket value of ur original rims, certain sports rim dealers may consider allowing u 2 trade in ur rims. dun expect good prices though. other option is to stash ur stock rims safely somewhere for u 2 fit back when u want to liquidate ur sports rims when u sell the car.
