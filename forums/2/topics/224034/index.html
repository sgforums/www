    Sales executive Chia Ye Nam, 35 was at the Block 36 Chai Chee Street carpark on 28 November at about 12:20 pm when he spotted these motorbikes parked illegally.

    What made matters worse was they belonged to parking wardens, and the bikes were parked on the double yellow lines.

    He walked on, and finally saw them sitting at the coffeeshop nearby having a chat and smoking.



    An indignant Mr Chia told STOMP:

    "I believe they must be good examples too!"

    Mr Chia wonders "who has the right to fine them."

    The Head of Enforcement Unit for United Premas, Mr Roy Yeo, has replied, and appended is his email to STOMP:

    We agree with you that our patrolling officers should behave professionally to uphold their public image. Following your feedback, we have reminded them not to group together to have their meals in any eating place so as not to cause any adverse public impression or reaction.

    Please accept my apology for the inconvenience caused and we shall assure you that disciplinary action shall be taken against our staff if they were found parking illegally.

    We appreciate your valuable feedback and your kind understanding in hearing us out on the clarification of our position.

    We have provided the particulars of the offending vehicles to HDB Bedok Branch Office for further action. Be rest assured that we do take disciplinary action against our staff if they are found parking illegally.
