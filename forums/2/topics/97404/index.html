    By Paul Anthony A. Isla, Reporter
    The vehicles were provided for testing by car manufacturers led by Toyota Motor Philippines, Honda Cars Philippines, Mitsubishi Motors Philippines, Columbian Autocar Corporation, Nissan Motor Philippines, Universal Motors Corporation, BMW Philippines Corporation, Viking Cars, and Isuzu Philippines Corporation. The run was sponsored by Total (Philippines) Corporation.

    The 60-car convoy traveled a total distance of 170 kilometers from the DOE Center in Taguig to Lipa City in BatangasÂ—and then back to the starting pointÂ—at an average speed of 60 kilometer per hour with the maximum speed set at 80 kph. All the vehicles had their air-conditioning system, headlights and hazard lights on.

    Energy Secretary Vincent S. Perez said that among the 60 vehicles, the Vios and the Crosswind proved to be the most fuel-efficient as both consumed a total of only 7.574 liters of gasoline and 8.683 liters of diesel, respectively.
