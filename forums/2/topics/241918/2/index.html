    I learned my driving from Ubi Comfort Driving Centre.

    I often noted down pointers from my instructors so that I could remember them when I was learning to driv.

    That's why I can still vividly remember them now. Just make sure the car that you want to cut into its lane can be seen on the rear mirror (the fuller the car on the rear mirror - it means it is further away from you from behind). But as long as it can be seen on your rear mirror, it is behind. If you are not so sure, then make sure you see the 'full' car right behind you before cutting into its lane.

    However, do make sure you check your blindspot because the car that's already beside you might not appear on both the rear and wing mirrors.

    It's really not that difficult. Practise and you will get better.

    Just tell yourself safety is foremost.

    I remember once I drove all the way to Still Road (way out of the learning route) simply because I was just too afraid to lane change in order to do a U-turn at Eunos Link. It's all part and parcel of learning oive.
