    There's a misconception that Ferraris are out of the reach of the middle class in terms of money.You can easily get a 348, 328 or 308 GTS or GTB models for below $150k.Mondial Ts and Mondial Convertibles, though rare, are available around that range too.

    I knew of a Dino 308GT-4 which was going for $70k and a 246GT for $60K.

    However, cost aside, the main hurdle to owning and driving a prancing horse is to possess a good knowledge of your car's ability and weakness.The older generation Ferraris need someone to keep it in tip-top shape, regular DIY maintenance is enough for the carburettor models, esp the 308 GTS.GTB and the GT-4.Do give them a blast now and then, because their engines are designed for high-speeds, so let them work out , so to say.

    It's not too hard to own a Ferrari, however most corporate guys won't be driving one due to fear of breaking some hierarchy in the office. Keep it in your garage as a 2nd car.It's a fun thing to have.

    Try a Maserati if you prefer a more normal road-going car.But the older Biturbo models are vanishing, and only a few Ghiblis are left in SG.
