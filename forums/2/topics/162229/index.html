    In LTA's regulations, they have classified modifications into three categories:

    (1) Allowed.
    (2) Allowed only with approval from LTA - case to case basis.
    (3) Disallowed.

    From what I can see, gullwing doors are not specifically mentioned in any of the above general guidelines (I've not seen the detailed guidelines, if there are any). I suspect that gullwing doors may come under category (2) above. LTA's general guidelines for car modifications is HERE.

    Anyway, the main purpose of these LTA directives is to ensure safety. And LTA has "question and answer" channel, via their feedback website HERE.

    There are some cars with factory-fitted gullwing doors in Singapore. One example is the Toyota Sera, pictures HERE.

    As for aftermarket gullwing doors, there are some workshops doing it here too - and they do advertise it publicly. One example is HERE.
