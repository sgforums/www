    A car's max speed depends on 3 things:

    Max power output.
    Its tyre rolling resistance
    Aerodynamic drag

    This is assuming that it is geared so that peak power in top gear coincides with the max speed, otherwise there is a small penalty.

    in turn, tre rolling resistance(TRR) depends on the resisitance coefficient, the weight of the car, and to a limited extent on speed. aerodynamic drag depends on the frontal area of the car, its drag coefficient(Cd), and the square of the speed.

    At very high speeds, aero drag is so much higher than the TRR that TRR can be disregarded.

    it would be simpler to calculate a car's max speed with known figures than to do it from scratch, because u need knowledge of the car's frontal area n CD, plus its power output.
