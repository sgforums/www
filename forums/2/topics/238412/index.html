    Dealers will not reduce car prices by same amount the COE has dropped, simply because they know many people will rush in. But this COE bidding has definitely set a new psychological benchmark and people expectations for COE will be set lower.

    Coupled with the fact that many people has bought their cars in the last one or two years with long loan tenures, they are unable to change cars in the next few years without incurring losses of up to 10k per year, made worst by the lower PARF rebates in recent years.

    Imagine the moment you receive your car key on day 1, the paper value of your new car would have lost 50%-60%!!! How to change another car in a short period without topping up easily another $20-30k???

    So down the road I won't be surprised to see average COE in the 7-8k region or even lower.
