    Cockroaches are hardy bugs. This fact is recognized world-wide.

    WipeOut can eradicate them over time if they're in your car. But a better remedy is still prevention:

    >> Avoid parking near rubbish dumps
    >> don't park next to lorries, vans, and other crummy old looking vehicles;
    german roaches can fly across to your car
    >> don't leave the door open too long when parked at the rear of some HDB flats where the rubbish chute doors are - at night, the ground around that area is acrawl with small baby roaches
    >> clean and spray shieldtox (make sure other brands you use do not contain petroleum distillate) externally around the perimeter of your car once every two months
    >> clean the interior of your car THOROUGHLY
    >> DON'T eat inside car if can be avoided

    Here's a known fact to share; roahces CAN BE introduced into your vehicle by ferrying things like old clothings, pots and pans that someone has kept a long time but never used, and old books kept in a carton box. Generally, if you ferry a lot of old things, baby roaches are potential stow-aways in your boot.

    Search the internet about cockroaches, and you will get to see just how small, a baby roach is.

    About these roach stations:

    Roaches are not intended to die in these stations. The stations contain an agent which when ingested by the roaches, will kill it when it crawls away, and remains "contagious" to surrounding roaches that crawls across the dead roach or feeds on the dead roach.

    If your car suffers a serious roach infestation, these stations may not be sufficient. You will need to fumigate your car first, then place WipeOut stations in your car.
