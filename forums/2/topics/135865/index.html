    Theres a diff between the stock ecu and an aftermarket ecu, in this case, you're most likely referring to an aftermarket one. They come with a flashy display that fits on the dashboard and tells you this an that.

    Modern fuel injected cars come fitted with a stock ecu which you normally have no access to which as said earlier, controls the timing, fuel in, etc.... The aftermarket ones are an auxillary unit which might in one way or another help the engine perform better by retuning its a/f ratio, timing etc....

    So in some cases, if not tuned properly, your auxillary ecu will funk around with the default ecu settings and give you more problems instead. Modern engines already strive to be more efficient in both emissions and power delivery, and therefore are in one way or another tuned to give you the best, thus the non-requirement for aftermarket ones. That is unless you're an enthusiast who seeks to play around and find the next best.
