The Trap of Owning a Car

  • StMichael
    26 Jan 04, 12:23
    I am in a bad situation now. Let this be a warning to all aspiring car owners as well.

    I want to sell a Suzuki Swift COE car I bought for 38k a year ago, as I am going overseas. I contacted the leasing company (SDL) and realised that I owe about $34k (after a year out of 7!).

    I looked through the classifieds and noticed asking prices for about $26k. This translates to a negative sale of about $8k. Which I will have to pay upfront to sell my car or the leasing company will not release the title .

    I tried negotiating but the company mentioned that they cannot allow me to pay by instalments as they are a leasing company and MAS regulations will not allow them to do that.

    Now I am stuck in a terrible situation. Nobody in my family drives or wants to buy a car. My credit situation makes it difficult for me to come up with the $8k. Now I am stuck with paying for a car that I do not use!

    Next time, I will be extremely careful about buying depreciating assets like cars!

    There were many things I learnt:

    In the early years of your loan, most of the money goes towards paying off your interest, just like housing loans. In other words, when you sell off too early, you will realise that a great deal of your principal is still around, to be paid off.

    I took a $38k loan (100%), and after 16 instalments of $587 a month, I still have about $34k. Doesn't take much maths to do the calculations. My Swift with COE expiring Jul 2011 has offers of $24k.

    That was the second thing I learnt - I suspect I paid too high a price for the car. Even assuming I knock off $3k for the dealer's profit, can a COE car depreciate $9k in a year?!?!?!? That was the price I paid for not checking out properly.

    I paid a fair price for the first car I bought in my life, only to be killed by the second car I bought. Goodness. Let this be a warning to all our friends out there - please be very careful. Buying a car is almost like having a child - you may find yourself stuck with it even if it kills you financially.
