  • ditzy
    19 Jun 05, 21:34
    '185' is the width (where the tread is) in mm

    '65' is the aspect ratio, or tyre profile or sidewall

    'R' means radial construction
  • ditzy
    19 Jun 05, 21:35
    The profile (or height of the sidewall) is expressed as a % of the tyres width. For example 185/65R14 means that the tyres profile or sidewall is 65% of 185mm (width). A low profile tyre is generally 50 or below and down to as little as 35 in the ultra low profile segment.

    The advantages of low profile tyres is that they give you greater control and better handling when cornering, however the trade off may be a slightly harder ride.
