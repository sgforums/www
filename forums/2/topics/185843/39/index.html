    If you were heading towards Jalan Eunos from Eunos Link, and you got caught in a incredible jam stretching from the junction of Bedok Reservior in the evening hours of 7-8pm y'day. Yep. That was my doing.

    I was heading to my friend's wedding dinner at Clarke Quay and was going by way of PIE from Ubi. Keeping to the right lane, I saw an object on the road, dead centre of the lane. As the vehicle in front had no trouble clearing it, I anticipated being able to clear it as well. (But in my heart, I was 60% unsure.)

    There was a tight screeching and chilling sound. I immediately stopped my car, put on the hazards and inspected the damage.

    The object resembled a crushed metal dustbin. It was stuck square under my carriage. And I could touch it, but not extricate it.

    I considered using my jack, but was uncertain if there was damage beyond cosmetic scratches. And I made up my mind early that it was best that a workshop could examine the undercarriage and advise me on any substantial damage that might have occured.

    So I called up TC's emergency line and gave them the go ahead to send a tow truck.

    Additionally, I placed my little triangle 15 metres behind my vehicle. The little triangle was a valiant sacrifice. It got run over within 15 minutes of placement.

    I waited in my car and thought about the next things to do. The tow truck took 1 hour to arrive. In the meantime, after my triangle got squashed, I stood outside the car, in the pouring rain to attempt guiding vehicles off to the next lane.

    My ride is still at TC Ubi. Their customer service has told me that they are checking for me, and thus far, no significant damage other than scratches. They will inspect further and will call me again to update.

    That's all for now folks. Any advice for follow up action?

    Sorry to read about your minor scrape. Didn't sound like fun at all especially given the rain and you were probably dressed for a wedding dinner! The ground clearance for the March is unfortunately lower than other cars (135mm compared to 160mm for Corolla Altis), so better try to go around rather than over things on the road. Good thing is that since it's a FWD, the only important thing below is the exhaust piping. They design the bottom to withstand hits from the road like stones and sticks, so the car should be fine.

    It's easier to remove items off the road when you're a pedestrian rather than motorist. When I see large stones, sticks, nails, and other things that shouldn't be on the road, I will pick it up and throw it off the road so it doesn't cause trouble to road users. Some people think I'm crazy when I do it, but I just hope that others will take 5 seconds from their walk and pick these things up. It will cause a lot more trouble if/when incidents occur.

    Hope you get your Air Blue March on the road again soon!
