    ANYONE READ THIS IN JUNE???
    Reviews

    Atten-shun. Forward March
    The style nannies at Nissan have worked their magic to spruce up this already adorable car

    By Lee Nian Tjoe - 24 June 2006
    The Straits Times

    Look at the March and the car seems to stare back at you through its big glassy 'eyes'.

    Among other minor changes effected during this mild update is a new palette of colours including Ice Blue, which is a lush, soft hue that reminds one of Smurfs.

    Viewed head-on, the new bumpers exaggerate the March's width.

    The ruddy cheeks look even more pinchable now. The distinctive double bubble silhouette still looks like a cross between a classic Volkswagen Beetle and a fluffy cloud.

    Matching the car's Ice Blue exterior, the designers quite literally 'blue' up the cabin.

    Everything inside - from the part fabric, part suede upholstery to the dashboard to even the wiper stalks - is coloured like blueberry-flavoured cotton candy, if ever there was one.

    Depending on the exterior colour, there is a beige-on-beige option for more conservative customers.

    In this 1.4E trim, the car is very well equipped, almost exceedingly so.

    Gizmos like powered folding mirrors, auto headlights, 15-inch alloy wheels, smart key and digital climate control with integrated air ioniser don't distract from the fact that this is essentially a really cool shopping trolley for four, with a rather tiny boot.

    Except that shopping trolleys don't tool around with nearly as much verve as the March. It leaps off the line effortlessly like a bunny in heat.

    The electric power steering feels like it's filled with helium but the turn-in is incisive, followed with good progression as the front-end starts to run out of grip.

    Its relatively generous wheelbase and soft suspension gives the car a cushy ride but manages to suppress almost all body roll and any mid-corner bobbing.

    Despite its camp (and some even say, frivolous) looks, this car drives well, better than the Honda Jazz and matching the Suzuki Swift.

    The March 1.4E is not cheap though. For the money, you could bag a Nissan Sunny with cash to spare. But, then, you can't cuddle a Sunny to sleep.
