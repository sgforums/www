    I'm still within the lock-in period for my car loan and will have to just wait until the end of the lock-in period. In the meantime, I'll just drool over any passing March and keep reading posts here.
    I previously drove a white manual Vios for 18months. Loved the car. Sportiest car for the money paid. Although it's only 100cc bigger engine, the power/torque is 109hp /141Nm vs. the March's 97hp /137Nm. The weight is about the same, although the auto version is about 50kg heavier than the March. In many developing countries (Thailand, Indonesia, Philippines) the Vios is the main taxi vehicle. That tells you a lot about the reliability, economy, and performance. I drove up Cameron Highlands twice, and was overtaking most other cars with ease especially when going uphill. The Vios' power to weight ratio is 110hp/ton and so for a new Camry that weighs 1540kg the equivalent power is 170hp (the 2.4liter Camry is 167hp). Add to that the manual transmission, lightweight body (less rolling effect when cornering) and all-round disc brakes (better braking), it is a very strong performer. The FC I got is about the same as I'm getting for the March i.e 12 - 13km/l but that may be because I drove the manual Vios like a sports car (16" wheels with Bridgestone Potenza GIII tyres, and KNN filter for better airflow).

    Frankly the main reason I changed (and took a big loss in exporting the car) was that my wife just passed her driving and I wanted her to be able to drive as well. She absolutely had no confidence with a manual transmission and barely passed the test (within 1 point). However the March is a very different car and holds its own in many respects.

    1. This is the easiest car to drive I have ever tried, and I've been driving for 18 years. It is so relaxing and low stress. The viewing angles from the drivers seat is amazing. You don't have to adjust the mirrors or strain your neck to reverse or parallel park because the windows/windshield are huge and the side mirrors covers a very wide area. This has to be tried to believe. The car was designed for Japanese ladies as a urban runaround car, so the power steering is extra boosted (you can make U turn with 2 fingers holding the wheel). The turning radius is 4.4m compared to 4.9m for the Vios, great for those not confident in making tight U turns.

    2. It has a great balance between power and FC. The 1.4 liter is not a race-bred animal but will certainly make you a lot more confident on the road than a 1 liter Picanto or even a 1.4liter Getz. The usual FC is decent at 12 - 13km/l. Drive gently and you'll hit 14km/l.

    3. The quality is in a different league from the Vios. Everything is put together much better in Japan. Fit and finish is tops. No funny rattles and noises that the Vios is famous for. The door shuts with a nice sounding thud. The switches click nicer and the plastics are soft instead of the cheap hard plastic in the Vios. The interior light dims gradually and when you pull your key out, it comes on (again gradually) to help you get out of the car.

    4. And finally, they really went all out with the colors! If you choose AirBlue, the interior (dashboard, steering wheel and switches, and seats) are also blue! You'll find unique colors not available on any other car (certainly not with the Vios) like Cream and Green Pea. It even used to have a Chocolate color.

    Well enough said. I sound like a car reviewer... Enjoy your decision making...

    My Vios is an auto-transmission car and I've been driving it 20mths now.

    I chose Vios because it was good value-for-money, powerful, well-known for good FC and reliable (becoz it's Japanese and Toyota). All practical, economical reasons for my decision. So far, my FC has been consistently 13-14.5km/l and yes, I do enjoy the power when I need it.

    However, ever since a relative got her March and having test driven it a few times, I've fallen in love with the smooth, understated elegance and cuteness of the car. It stands out proud and elegant amongst the crowd of Vioses, Altises and Hondas in any car park. It never fails to catch my eye whenever a March whizzes along the expressway alongside mine; whatever the color.

    The cute, front lamps, bright, radiant colours, cheeky curvey shape, regal height of the car all work together to command attention. Having sat in it, I've found that all fixtures and fittings are very well-placed and reasonably thought through. The ceiling cabin lamp is fitted within easy reach of the driver; the broad and wide dashboard area gives good space for tissue boxes or sunglasses. The shelf beneath the steering wheel lets you place car park coupon books or folded umbrella. The cabinet is wide enough to place necessities.

    The texture of the seats scream of quality and high-class sophistication. The ability to easily fold down the backseats to enlarge the boot is also so sensible. Not to mention the wide legroom for front passenger.

    Just like what you mentioned, Shooting_cloud, the steering is sooo smooth and easy that it's clearly a lady's car. All these factors have been tugging at my heartstrings and I'm just so near to deciding to sell off my Vios to get a March. *shy smile* So much for all the sensible reasons for retaining the Vios.
