    thks for the big welcome

    Lemme share with u my experience on my new marchie...

    When i first got my car..the car engine is pretty noisy. It irritates me a lot cos for a brand new car..i didnt expect it to be noisy. Seek opinion from my frens and they too find it a bit noisy. After my 1k service, they did some engine tuning for me..the noise get lesser...and now its pretty much silent...just the way i like it.

    At the beginning, my car didnt perform well too when it goes uphill esp going up the benjamin shears bridge...it cant seem to push itself despite the fact that i press on the accelerator. You can feel that it has no power.However when my car exceed the 2.5k mileage..things starts to improve a lot...i no longer feel that the car is dragging itself..it has gained power. And i no longer have to worry about hogging the road on the busy benjamin shears bridge.

    Wind noise from my right rear seat door is  exceptionally a lot too..so went to  tan chong to get the rubber thingy on the door to be replaced.

    Im not so sure about the other marchies...but my marchie seems to drag itself at 3rd gear for quite long..hence when im driving at 50-60km/h..you can feel that the car drags itself at 3rd gear..before it moves up to the next gear.

    For a small girl like me...i do feel big inside the car and hence it helps to exudes my confidence in driving the car on the road. Tried test drive other cars before...but they make me feel small inside.

    I think my marchie has yet to reveal her true performance.....only time will tell...

    I used to feel that the 3rd gear was draggy as well, but it's actually the strongest gear. I always drive up Sheares bridge on 3rd gear. It only feels draggy because it has the widest range, compared to 1st and 2nd gear. 1st gear changes to 2nd at 10-15km/h, 2nd to 3rd at 20-25km/h, then it's 3rd all the way till 65km/h, you can even use it till 85km/h before switching to 4th.

    Overall I try to be light foot, in view of the overall increase in fuel prices, and I'm happy to keep to my left. Once in a while, I step a little harder and wa hay!, my March still delivers the fun!
