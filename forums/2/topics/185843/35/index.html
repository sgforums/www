    Originally posted by nanomarch:
    Wat does this mean??? Is it mean that those purchase last bidding @ $46K got a good deal, they got the March for $46K (COE - 13K)? Please advice.
    Yes, exactly! But dealers has the option of returning you your deposit if they wish (after 6 bids). The dealer cost is about $46k, so the margin is extremely thin at $45,800. The cost is calculated as follows:

    - Customs Duty = 20% OMV
    - GST = 5% (actually 6% because GST also on Customs Duty)
    - ARF = 110% OMV
    - COE
    ==> Dealer cost = (2.36*OMV) + COE
    OMV for 14C is $13145, so you can do the math. Add a bit for their overhead and SE commissions.

    They squeeze some profit from your trade-in car, PARF/COE rebate, loan, and insurance:
    - Kickback from used car dealer, bank, and insurance companies
    - buy PARF/COE from market at 4% discount and register your car (you pay full price)
    - remainder of road tax on your car is supposed to be refunded to you, but usually dealers take it

    Dealers rarely return deposits. Toyota SE told me that Borneo has never done it ever. But I heard that Hyundai did on the Sonata recently when they priced the Sonata at $61k for the National Day promotion, and COE went up.

    I picked up my car 2 weeks ago. Check the basic stuff (lights, windows, keyless remote, horn, aircon). The most annoying part for me was that the car was dirtier than I expected. Stains on the dasboard, door, steering wheel, turn and wiper levers. Just check all round. If you installed solar film, be sure to check for bubbles.

    Remember to drive easy for the first 1000km. Supposed to be under 3000rpm if possible bit since we don't have the rpm meter, just take it easy on the accelerator.

    I've changed 2 things already which has made a big difference for me. The horn to a pair of Stebel Magnums ($15 but installation may cost $20) so now at least I have the bark if not the bite, and headlight bulbs. I bought the extra bright Philips bulbs (H4 is the right size) at the Motor Show ($36) and replaced the bulbs myself. The extra brightness can be seen immediately and makes night driving much safer. Totally LTA compliant.

    Congrats!!

    No problem at all with the questions. Getting your car is a very exciting time but opens up a lot of questions.

    You should get 1 keyless (intelligent), 1 hard key, and a key code. Mine is Comfort so that's my guess for Elegant. I saw this pile of keys for a new Latio nearby which also has intelligent key system. I thought that we should at least get 2 intelligent keys (Comfort gets keyless without "intelligence").     I haven't quite figured out which petrol discount is the best. I heard from friends/forums that the SPC discount is the best. I'm using EssoMobil petrol, and most people I speak to advise using the same brand if possible due to the different additives used by different brands. Apply for the Esso Speedpass (through Esso - check their website). It's so convenient since you don't have to queue to pay, and you get higher discount than queuing because you get discount from station and credit card (link your Speedpass to your Citibank credit card). Note that the listed price at the station can be confusing because of all the discounts that are given.

    Enjoy!
