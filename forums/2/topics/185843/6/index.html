    Long story again !

    Techron cleans the un-combusted petrol in your engine, maintains and give you better response. Vpower indeed gives you more horse power.... ! It depends on how you want to take care of your car and feed it with what goodies. I will use 98, techron and vpower it depends.

    1. Min 98, no point going for 95. (If you can afford a 60k car, what is a few cents ) dun understand why pple use 95 or 92 to torture your ride. It is never a long term solution for your engine to use 92 or 95. lets see after 3-5 years how you engine condition will be.... !

    2. 98 pump 20L ( abt 250 mileage + 18km more after amber light on. )
    - For normal driving mon to fri pump 98. (80% highway)

    3. Vpower 20L ( abt 268 Mileage + 25km more after amber light on. )
    - For some hard action and running around on week Ends pump Vpower.

    4. Techron 20L ( abt 260 Mileage + 25km more after amber light on. )
    - Every 2 weeks once I pump Techcon to clean up the engine.

    I am running on 16 inch rims 10 kg per wheel = 40kg more drag so my FC will be 11-13 km. Normally, every 3days pump once 98.

    I will pump twice of 98 - 20L = 3rd / 6th day , Once Vpower - 20L = 9th day, Once Techron to clean up on the 12 day and after which I do the same again and again. Is just my guide, most or some of you will know how heavy my car is curb weight at stock is 975kg + 110kg more = 1085 kg for sure my FC will be abt <12. ( 11kg per horse pull is bad, but no choice )

    I have been sharing with pple but some believe it is troublesome and not a good idea to pump only 20L/half tank. (1L = 1kg ) you think of the drag you are going through and also the amount of petrol needed to spin off the car. I had never pull full tank, this is not Melb or New York city. Petrol stations are every where on this island in between < 10km there is one petrol station. Why worry and waste petrol and money pumping full 41L. ?? that me ! again to each it's own. Is a trade off ! I call it ....!
