    Hi Velly, bare with me long essay.

    There were two issues mention abt March 1.4L in Wheels Mag (2 page article) and Motoring half a page, featuring on specs, power, design and etc some time from May-Aug 2004. surprising some how these 2 copies were no where to be found. in my library of Car magazines. "missing in action "

    Just a brief, of my 2cents worth of facts and experience:

    1. Model BK-12, 1.4L 4AT
    Engine: 16v DOHC
    Performance: 0- 100 km/h @11.5 sec
    Power to weight ratio: 98 hp / 975kg (9.94 kg per horse power pull at crank)

    Personal Experiences and Remarks: (Light weight, the car has sufficient torque, pick up response is good, engine is quite when you start it, a few occasions I dun even release my engine was on and I restart it again when I warm up the car in the morning) Is a little under powered when you climb the Benjamin S. bridge with full load. Trotler is pretty responsive for light footed drivers bcos the car has good torque for lower gears. (Factory suspensions are soft, good for tight city driving and turning, tamper rebound too responsive), (ABS: I rate it at 7.5 /10 rating with stock specs on braking power, but different tyres will give your different performance braking on the road and also brake pads / calipers). Steering wheel is light with integrated Electronic PS, easy to perform parking and negotiate tight corners. Easy city driving car.

    2. 2002 Europe Design car of the year.
    Personal Experiences & Remarks: Interiors: Good head room, very roomy and reasonable passenger space, front driver leg room is roomy for me.
    (2+2 adults acceptable, 2+3 is a little pack at the back ) (Jersey materials- seat covers (a little hot at times), bad marketing becos the car does not come with leathers) (Sound proofing-> noise slip through side doors and engine bay itself infront of the front driver area has thin layer of S.proofing from factory, the Roof: gets a little nosy when it is down pouring heavily) (There are wind nosy from the side above a certain speed and it is still acceptable)
