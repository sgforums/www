  • Shooting_Cloud
    8 Aug 07, 09:45
    Tyre pressure numbers are usually pasted on the driver's side doorsill in most cars. Can't remember off the top of my head.

    Front and back tyres require different pressure because the front is heavier due to the engine being there. I usually put in slightly higher (about 5psi), to get better FC, power, and less roll while cornering, but note that this can cause slightly uneven tyre wear and bumpier ride. Try different pressures to see what you like, but don't go beyond +/- 10psi.

    Just did my 10k servicing at TC and opted for Synthetic Oil. I love the engine performance right after getting fresh synthetic oil! It just purrs along and I could swear there is more power as the pistons are moving with less friction.

    TC charged me $64 as the top up. They used Shogun 0W-30 instead of Fastflo oil. When I asked about this, the Service Advisor said that Shogun is better (they got customer feedback that Fastflo makes the engine sluggish), but is about $20 more expensive. 0W-30 is very light and thin oil.
