    the sensible way to measure costs is to measure "value for money".

    i am no economy expert but buying a car in singapore does not make economy sense at all in the first place. and if our perceived "values" are what most typical sporeans' values are, ie, big space, fierce look, racing engine all for low costs, then the more choosing the Marches is not being sensible.

    if you can overcome the above, for the price of the marches, you are rewarded abundantly in the "values" some of us here deemed more important than the above mentioned. such as the super "cute" look you were after, the head turning and limelighted experience, the feel good factor, the award winning design, driving zippiness, reasonable fuel consumption, many bells and whistles, the wonderful marchies community (some of us call this cult group) behind you etc etc... some of these you cannot even experience thru owning many more expensive or bigger cars which in my opinion, are souless.

    the marches have been on the road for a few years now, i would like to advise potential buyers to check out other makes and newer models that are available today. only you can convince yourself that the march is right for you.
