    This is my first post.

    I have been reading this forum for some time, just to pass the time and for interest sake. However, this topic perked my interest.

    I drive a 350Z. Minimal finance. Before we go into a rich's man car, poor man's car concept, let's put it this way. It was a comfotable car for me, both financially and aesthetically. Simply, I like it, and it's well within my means. I could buy a Mercedes CLK or SL, but it would strain my lifestyle.

    However, I am going to buy a Tuscani for my girlfriend. Frankly, I do not like the car. Both in terms of looks and performance. BUT, she likes it. And frankly, I know of some people who like it. Simply because it is a "sports car" in general. 2 door, low profile, what else can it be to the general public. Yes, they may seem to be poseurs, but how many people, young or old, rich or poor, have dreamt of owning and driving a "sports car"? And if it makes their dream comes true just for a moment, who are we to criticise?

    I have heard many associates of mine pass the Tus of as a "poor man's sports car". Some of them are rich people. When you own a few ferrari's, $80k for a hyundai is ridiculous. However, most of them just drive a family sedan. They are working class people, yet they condemn their peers when they buy a Tus. But in todays context, where we work to support the family, and every cent counts, do you think it is feasible to buy a 2 door coupe? Thus, a Tus will only be to feasible to singles, or as a second car. Since generally, singles do not remain single for long in Singapore, averagely, it would mean someone young. And to be able to afford a car, any car in his/her early to mid twenties, is commendable. I need not further say that to afford a second car in Singapore is not easy.

    In conclusion, we each have our views. I tend to lean towards guitarman's view on this CAR, but that doesn't mean I have no respect for the DRIVERS.
