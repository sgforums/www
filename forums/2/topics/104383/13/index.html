    Rx7 is a 2 seater purely 'sports', or rather say drift machine. It's popular bcos of the handling, definitely not the top speed, may b this is the nature of rotary engine. They have been in many racing events for yrs, tht's how they built the fame & 350Z is so new, it's totally diff to it's predecessor which is powerful but lack of good handling.

    RX8 is a 2+2 '4 doors' car, diff weigth distribution, diff concept. Latest RX7 will come out in 2007 if i'm not wrong, so RX8 will never replace RX7, i think RX8 can consider as a family sports car oni. It's difficult to explain, but ppl who tend to drive fast, once they put their hands on RX7 & 8, they will feel the handling diff immediately.

    I like 350Z very much, but road tax & insurance turn me off. It's using VQ series engine which GTR35 going to use it in future as well. In fact, latest R34 in Jap racing events mostly change to VQ (or VG? I can't rem) series engine instead of RB oledi. Nissan has tuned down the 350Z due to the government restriction. Or else, with 3.5L engine, guess it can pump out at least 300+ HP. RB26DTT is capable of handling over 600 hp without internal mod, so i assume 350Z can make it as well, compare to stock 4G63 can handle around 400HP without internal mod, i think 350Z is sweet. Subaru engine i have no comments, not familiar, haha

    Okie, now we r toking bout the potential of the engine, back to Tuscani again. They need a lot of R&D & aftermarket support to live up the engine. Korean most powerful Tus can hit 800+HP so i dun think Tuscani is a ricer or poser car.
