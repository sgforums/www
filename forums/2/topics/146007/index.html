    Clicking when turning? A vast majority of the time it's due to a worn constant velocity (CV) joint, especially in FWD cars. It's not the drive shaft in itself, but the joint between the drive shaft and the wheel rotor.

    However, if you're saying that it's coming from the back, it could be that the wheel bearings are on their way out. However, the most common indicator of a worn wheel bearing is a scraping sound, and in later stages, a rumbling - try driving with the windows down and see if you can hear this when you're going in a straight line. If you do hear the scraping sound and it stops as you begin to apply the brakes, it's most likely the bearing.

    If it is a worn bearing, make sure you change it ASAP - the last thing you want is for it to let go completely while you're on the expressway - not only does it jerk the car around and make it uncontrollable, the heat from the friction will also boil your brake fluid and make it useless.
