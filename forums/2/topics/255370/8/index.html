    thx for yr compliment guys. the pics are fresh off the camera, no editing involved. yes, this is called a panning shot. a way of shooting whereby u point your camera at the subject u r shooting and follow at its speed. this gives a effect of a blur background and clear subject. it depends on the shutter speed ur camera is set at. it's easier to take such shots in the day as compared to the night as during the night, the shutter needs to be exposed longer to capture more light. the longer yr shutter is exposed, the tendency of having a blur picture is higher, including yr subject, unless u can point to yr subject very accurately, following the speed. i m still learning the ropes, give u guys a tip, try shooting these pics at low shutter speed in the day b4 going to try at night. night shots tend to be nicer looking if taken perfectly.

    these pics are shot with a DSLR camera, but normal camera also can do the same, even hp camera.

    will try to take more of such shots next time if the opportunity comes by.
