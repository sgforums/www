    Hi,

    1) for BTT and FTT, you can study from your own books and take the test. Whether you sign up through the driving school or go as a private candidate, there is no need to take theory lessons. I recommend you just pay the $5.65 fee to take the trial test. That would be sufficient. Theory lessons are mainly for foreigners who do not understand the language in the books. You MUST, at the very least, clear your BTT before you can apply for a PDL to take practical lessons, regardless of driving school or private candidate.

    2) No, you will not even be allowed on the circuit if you do not have a valid PDL. If you ask me, you should start sourcing for a good private instructor then contact him for lessons after you pass your BTT. Book your FTT right after you pass your BTT. Normally it takes quite some time until your FTT date. In between, you can continue taking practical lessons.

    3) Unless you are an extremely slow learner, go for private. That being said, ask around your relatives and friends to recommend a good private instructor to you. School allows you to clear lessons very quickly and religiously in a nice environment, but it comes at a steep cost. I spent close to $1800 for school and got my license within 5 months (failed the 1st test), whereas private will perhaps not even overshoot $1200, but might take longer. Not worth the money I guess.
