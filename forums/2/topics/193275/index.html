    You might not be able to go for 3rd party insurance. In the case of newer cars, the finance company will insist on you taking comprehensive insurance to cover for the loss of car in accidents/etc.

    There is no PARF when you scrap your car.

    As for body value, I don't believe you can get anything. Hell, you will need to pay for people to scrap the car for you.

    Do check out the TOTAL price you will have to pay, and get the dealer to write it all clearly in black and white. Call me discriminatory or whatever, but second-hand car dealers are not the most honest bunch of guys around.

    Lastly, if I were getting a car like the one you are aiming at, I'd be very worried about its reliability. Poor reliability = more downtime = more expen$es.
