    how ar.. if 1 side drift right corner...

    1: gather enough speed around 70-90km/h(4d gear)
    2:before u reach the bend.. clutch in, down to 3rd gear at least 3/4 steer to the right to have the turning momemton , tap brake + hard acceleration for the butt on the left side of your car to slide slightly, immediate after tap brake throw the clutch. Once you feel the whole car body starting to drift out, counter-steer to your left to bring it back to straight( if not, u will see ur car most likely to hit the left side due to the drift or over turn). Most likely got to counter-steer at least 3 times to get straight. (cos i steer 3 times)DO not let go of the acceleration because it is use to stablelise your throttle power after the throw clutch and oso to reduce the velocity gained from the drift.

    Notice: I try tis in heavy vehicle carpark from 1 row to another row and its big enough. It can be done.. but choose a corner which does not have holes(cozz alot of places have pothholes) and the corner must be slightly 1 sided slunted.cos i never try on flat road n i dun wanna try

    You will feel your body sliding towards ur left alot and the pressure of the movement is big... but if u chicken out at the last min when u enter the corner... congrates.. u can call for ambulance and tow car.

    To be on the safe side, dun try this on any normal salon cars. adjust suspension+4wd.. throttle power get higher. Coz i try on a rex '01 version wif some mod.

    Control your right leg. toes part on acceleration and hind foot for tap the brake. Took some times before i got use to it...

    U can try some basic turning corners first to get use to the feelings eg. turn @ 50km/h.

    It is something like throw clutch when doing U-turn except that its not at a fast speed and easier to steer and the velocity movement of the car is much more lower.
