  • TwinTurbo_Supra

    when u release the throttle of a car, the throttle valve closes. preventing more air and fuel to enter the engine's cylinders.

    however, the turbo would still be running, pushing air into the closed throttle valve. hence, there will be lots of pressure built up between the turbo and the engine. to release the pressure, a blow off valve is used to direct the pressurised air to escape to the atmosphere through another path.

  • ditzy
    3 May 05, 19:18
    Few sales people will take the time to explain that an externally venting (as opposed to fully plumbed ) valve confuses the A/F meter, as a result the ECU has no idea that air has been vented and thus causes a huge change in fuel mixture ( as the ECU thinks there is more air than there really is) resulting in a very rich mixture. That's why the standard one's outlet is after the A/F meter. This end result is funny misfires and flat spots.

    Plus you will consume a lot more fuel.
