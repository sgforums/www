    DIFFERENCE BETWEEN CLASS 3 AND RACING LICENCE, PLS READ!!!

    The difference is very simple. With a class 3, u can drive on the road. With a Racing Licence, you can enter official FIA sanctioned racing events. Without a class 3, even if u own a racing licence, u cannot drive on the road. Without a racing licence, even with a class 3, you cannot take part in official FIA sanctioned racing events.

    TURBO, SUPERCHARGED, NA?

    One of the most frequently asked Qs about cars is, what do the 3 terms in the title mean?

    Turbo:
    In layman terms, a turbocharged car, has a "fan" that is connected to another "fan". We shall call them fan 1 and 2. Fan 1 is put into the pipe where the exhaust gases from the engine escape from. These gases push fan 1 and thus cause fan2 to turn. When fan 2 turns, its creates a suctioning effect and draws more air into the engine for combustion. This increases the bhp of the car.

    Supercharger:
    A supercharger is similiar to a turbocharger. The difference is, there is only one fan. The fan is made to turn by drawing off a small amount of power from the engine. When it turns, it sucks in air, and acts in a similiar manner to a Turbocharger.

    NA:
    NA means Naturally Aspirated. Aspirate means to breathe, to draw in air. so Naturally aspirated means to draw in air nautrally. Thus a NA car draws in its air for combustion in the engine naturally, without any assistance from a fan of any sort. The air enters the engine as fast as u drive.

    Depreciation is the value lost over the years. To calculate depreciation, take the price you pay for the car minus the scap value (money you get back when you scrap the car after the COE expires). It can be summarised as below

    Depreciation = Price - Scap Value

    You can from there calculate yearly depreciation by dividing your answer with number of year left for the car. Yearly depreciation allows you to make comparison between cars

    As you can see, depreciation value depends very much on the price you paid for the car and scrap value, therefore not necessary new car will depreciate more yearly. Also other factor include the make of the car, current market price, etc

    Note: Do not confuse depreciation with loan repayment. They are different set of figures altogether.
