    Taman Sutera is closer to Perling interchange, which will remain congested until the current upgrade has completed. Taman Sutera Utama is close to the north-bound lanes of Jalan Skudai, which does not really make it close to the causeway, as the TUTA interchange where a U-turn can be made is susceptible to congestion during peak hours. Horizon Hills is about ten to twenty minutes further away from the causeway compared to Taman Sutera and Taman Sutera Utama. There is a plan to build a road linking Jalan Skudai and Nusajaya, making Horizon Hills significantly closer to the causeway, but just like any plan proposed by the Malaysian government, it can take a decade to materialize, if it ever materializes at all.

    Currently, CausewayLink CW3 is the only bus service that uses the second link. You can find some information on it here:

    http://www.handalindah.com.my/

    There was a Malaysian CIQ-Senai Airport bus service for a short while months ago, but it has been terminated, probably due to lack of passengers. Senai had too few useful international flights to make it attractive to Singapore residents.

    I do not travel from TUTA, so I do not know the bus fare for a journey from TUTA to Jurong East Interchange. However, the bus fare from Jurong East to anywhere along the route is 3.50 Sing dollars.

    From Jurong East, if you wish to go to TUTA, board CW3 at Jurong East, then switch to 666 at the Gelang Patah bus station. If you read The Straits Times, you should have seen a forum letter giving free but slightly negative publicity to the service a few weeks ago. The 3.50 Sing dollar CW3 bus fare paid at Jurong East already covers the 666 bus fare.

    The route might be changed a little at the end of July 2007, resulting in Taman Sri Yakoob to be a CW3 terminal. The schedule and frequency of CW3 is also being improved a little from the current 30-45 minutes a bus. You can find some posters on CW3 buses about this.

    THEY gave their victim the slip, but two snatch thieves ended up being bashed by a mob of 30 people when they returned to try to sell the victim her handphone.

    The two men had allegedly snatched the woman's handbag in Johor Baru on Wednesday, reported Guang Ming Daily.

    The angry family of the victim decided to lay a trap for the thieves.

    They called the woman's handphone - which was in the stolen bag - and made an offer to pay for the bag's return.

    When three thieves showed up on Friday evening however, the family and four friends used their car to prevent the suspects from escaping.

    The thieves fled, but the family gave chase, beat them up and smashed their car.

    When members of the public realised what was happening, they joined in the attack, using helmets and other makeshift weapons.

    One of the thieves escaped the mob, but the remaining two were beaten so badly that their faces and clothes were covered in blood.

    Four policemen arrived at the scene and put an end to the attack but, while waiting for an ambulance, the two suspects escaped, reported New Straits Times.

    Johor Baru police are now looking for the two men. A police spokesman said that they are investigating the case, and advised the public not to take the law into their hands.
