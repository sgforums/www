    Originally posted by TwinTurbo_Supra:
    so it breaks down into nitrogen and oxygen before being combusted?

    how does it break down so readily? i thought it is damn stable?
    neither oxygen nor nitrogen exists in their purest form as single atoms. under room temp, they need to share electrons in order to form molecularly stable compounds such as N2 and O2. N2O is stable in so far as temps remain relatively low. thats also why they are stored in pressurized containers.

    in the case of N2O, the low boiling point ensures that the compound vaporizes upon contact with the intake charge in which it is injected at the intake manifold. at elevated temps within the engine, the N2O particles break up readily to form their constituent N2 and O2 molecules. this further reduces intake temps which results in a denser charge and also helps to prevent predetonation. the higher influx of O2 available as a result means that more fuel can be squeezed into the chambers to create a stronger combustion.
