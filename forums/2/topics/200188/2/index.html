    there are generally 2 scenarios when u have flames coming out of ur exos. this phenomenom is usually accompanied by a "pop" sound as the flames are expelled from the exos tip. one is deliberately induced (as in an anti lag system) to prevent a drop in boost and the other is a result of engine management and exhaust set up. the flames are seen because during shifting, there is a sudden cut in the influx of intake gases when ur rite foot is lifted off the accelerator. the resultant drop in intake means there is significantly lesser oxygen to react completely with the leftover fuel in the previous combustion cycle which is in turn passed out as exos gases. as this fuel is unable to react with oxygen to produce the stable by products of combustion (CO2 and H2O) therefore, the extra fuel self combusts within the exos pipe thereby producing the flames.

    the effects are more pronounced in a straight thru exos system because the hollow pipe acts as huge "barrel" for the explosion to be channeled out (imagine a round coming out from a long barrel weapon such as an M16A1 rifle).
