    hey
    for those new 'P-plate' drivers. Its really easy to park parallel if you apply wad u;ve learnt from driving sch. Trust me. When I first started, I tried to dgo by estimation and judgment..and as expected..all go wrong!! Den a fren came up to me and tell me, jus apply the driving sch style. And it works perfectly

    Others might haf their own style of parallel parking, like going by 'feel' and stuffs. But for a start its definitely better to apply wad you;ve learn first

    This is how i go abt doing it for a 5 door saloon car.

    1) go beside the car in front of the lot you wanna park (be sure to keep close..abt half arm length from it)

    2)as you slowly reverse straight, look out for your Left/Right rear 'pillar' to get in line with the boot of the car in front of the lot. ie also the car beside you at that pt of time la. (Left/Right depending on which side of the road you parallel park. Basically its the pillar dat is nearer to the car beside you of course)

    3) Once it gets in line, do a full-lock till you see the inner wheels & kerb of the car behind you. (thru you side mirror)

    4)reverse straight (your car shd be 45 degree into the lot by now) until you see enough space in front of you to turn in your front. (you gotta steer in the other direction of course)

    5) reverse as much as possible. And do the minor adjustments if the lot is too small. Usually, you;ll get in with at most 2 reverses

    cheers
