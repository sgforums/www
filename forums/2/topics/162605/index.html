    The fuel guage is merely a device for you to GUAGE the amount of fuel in your tank. Its position relative to actual amount of fuel depends on the Calibration ... i.e. how the guage is set-up.

    I believe most cars now use electronic sensors to guage the fuel. Suppose your tank capacity is 60 litres.

    1. The manufacturer can set the sensor to full scale value when there is 60litres of fuel and set it at zero when there is still 20 litres of fuel in the tank. In this case, you will have approx 40 litres when the guage shows 5/10.

    2. Or, they can set it at FSV at 55 litres and zero at 10 litres, and your 5/10 will actuall equates to abt 33 litres.

    "Approximate" cos as someone mentioned earlier, the guage may not be linear.

    The trigger for the low fuel indicator is usually set some where between 1/10 to 2/10 of the full scale value. The balance fuel in the tank when the guage is a Empty is your so call "Reserve tank".

    For my car, when the LFI is triggered, I still have close to 20 litres in the tank [70l tank], so I know I can safely travel another 150km based on 9km/l.

    Try going to the pump to pump full tank once your LFI is triggered and that will give you an indication of the amount of fuel in tank for the trigger. From there, you can estimate the distance you can venture when you LFI is triggered based on your ave FC (km/litre).
