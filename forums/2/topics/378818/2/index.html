    Originally posted by linwei20:

    For slope, how to we find the biting point when the car is not moving?


    Couple of ways:

    1. As the clutch begins to bite, the engine RPM will drop. Watch the tachometer to see when this happens. 

    2. On a front-wheel-drive vehicle, as the clutch begins to bite, you'll notice that the rear of the vehicle will squat slightly due to engine torque. You should be able to feel this.

    When you notice one or both of these happening, release the handbrake, give the engine a bit of gas, and release the clutch smoothly all the way. Very simple, really!
