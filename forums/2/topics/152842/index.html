    Just in case you decided to be a little bit adventural. Here's some tips I got from a friend on DIY.

    *****************Frank of Kelisa Club Singapore************
    ......I started to spray my rims of my various cars from Charade to Viki to Colt and then Vivio. I tried "all sort of patterns of spraying" and finally I did a good one for my friend's Getz and the result its perfectly good until today!

    Step 1: The rim must be cleaned.

    Step 2: Put the rim under the hot sun before spray job commence.

    Step 3: Spray layer by layer of high temp paint onto the rim (min 3 layers) under the hot sun.

    Step 4: Spray layer by layer of the colour paint of your choice (min 3 layers) under the hot sun. Recommend to use RJ and not Nippon Pylon (Famous of cracking under high temp)

    Step 5: Spray layer by layer of clear paint (min 3 layers) under the hot sun.

    Important note:
    - Your skill of spraying determine the finish of your rims!
    - Notice that I did everything under the sun.

    No of cans require.....
    = 2 cans of RJ high temp paint, silver
    = 3 cans of RJ moonlight blue paint
    = 2 cans of RJ clear paint

    Total amount spent: S$35 only
