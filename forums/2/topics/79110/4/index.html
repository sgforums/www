    A lot of smack talking going around here without any good info.

    Here are some facts:

    Evo is a strong performer in Group N rallying and circuit racing in Japan. Current champion of Super Taikyu in its class (Class 2).

    http://www.gialla.co.jp/gialla/race.html

    For modified class, it's the current modified production car record holder in Tsukuba, held by HKS TRB02 Evo 8.

    http://www.hks-power.co.jp/topics/trb/trb.html

    In US, the Evo has consistently performed well against STi, despite the fact that the STi in the US has the 2.5l engine with more power and torque, DCCD, and Spec C suspension geometry. And remember that the USDM Evo has less power (271 bhp vs 280PS) and only a 5 speed transmission, which is really close to an Evo 7 RS spec.

    Evo in its inaugural year won the SCCA Pro Rally Championship.

    http://www.scca.com/News/News.asp?IdS=012F16-6516790&Id=135&~=

    In this year's Open Track Challenge, Evos captured the top 2 spot in T3 class, making a total of 4 Evos in Top 10, with 1 USDM STi in tenth position. The second place Evo was STOCK. In U3 class, it came in third. No STis there. In overall standings, Evo came in 10th while the nearest Subaru entry was 28th.

    http://www.opentrackchallenge.com/results/2004/index.htm

    In the recently concluded One Lap of America XXI, Evo came out tops again in its class (mid-priced sedan), and was 5th overall.

    http://www.onelapofamerica.com/History/2004/results/OVL_CUM.HTM

    Looking closer to home, even the local SMSA races are dominated by Evos. Ever wonder why?
