  • Ali_Khl
    3 Nov 04, 21:24
    About the Mitsubishi Evolution 8 overtaking a BMW E46 M3 at speed - Not as easy as you claim. The Evo uses a highly-strung turbo 2.0 litre, which although has power output in the 300hp range and a high torque figure of approximatly 380 Newton Metres. It also hosts 4WD, a feature that (when combined with high-revolution clutch-balacing) helps it attain a 0-100 km/h time in the high four second range. But if we look more closely at its top end permance, past the point where the traction advantage is cancelled out, then we see that although it is a very quick car on its own it cannot quite match an M3 in straight-line speed at least.

    The Evo 8 is primarly a road-going rally homoligation special, and because of its 'boxy' shape compared to the M3 and the front splitter/ rear spoiler combination and other downforce-producing components, the car is limited in its top end (ie above 100 km/h) acceleration. Its four-wheel drive drivetrain also induces road drag and higher rolling resistance as the front wheels are turning as well as the rear.

    The E46 M3 features not only more Horsepower (332), but also a lower aerodynamic drag coefficent (0.32) as well as much more top-end power (The M3's powerband stretches to 8200 rpm, with maximum HP in the 7500 range).

    Oh and its Lamborghini, not Lambourghini
