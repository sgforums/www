    In a fire situation, the first thing to do is to raise the alarm. then fight the fire, if possible. Then only call the fire/police dept.

    In this case, the alarm was raised immediately, which was a correct action by one of the young resident nearest the burning car. The other residents came out with their own fire extinguishers, but the flame was too intense for them to get near to effectively fight the fire. Furthermore there is the danger of a fireball explosion.
    Vehicular fires must be put down while it is just starting and smoking, with Class B extinguishers for flamable liquids/grease. For a conflagration, it best left to the experts, like our Civil Defence fire fighters.
