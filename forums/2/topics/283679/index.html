    You ever drive before?

    It is almost impossible to shift to REVERSE when you are cruising along the highway. Just for your info, the reverse gear and the 5 forward gears are not synchronised and this is done for a good reason too.

    If you have access to a manual gear car, you can try this, shift to REVERSE(when the car is stationery) and try to move the lever to 5th, you will notice this can be done very easily. But you will also notice you cannot shift back to REVERSE when the stick is in 5th (not synchronised). You will have to shift the lever to NEUTRAL and then you will be able to shift back to REVERSE.

    Also if the car is moving/crawling(5km/hr) along and you try to shift to REVERSE, there would be a large grinding sound since the clutch plate is still spinning (I kena before when I was in the carpark). Most auto manufacturers recommend resting the lever for at least 3 seconds in NEUTRAL before you shift to REVERSE. So just imagine if you do this at 100km/hr in 5th, the sound would be enough to put you off for trying any stunts.
