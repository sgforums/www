    lancer 1.6AT engine sohc...as always mit engine roars like a lion on high rpm. FC would not be that good. I using MT 1L=13km (average). AT is around 9-10km. 0-100 wound not be really that fast.. It took me 9secs+drag on the MT... AT i estimated 12-13sec. For the price its going now, it would be a good ideal to get a lancer. Lance are more for appearance wise if you know how to do it up. Interior is reasonable. If you are thinking about FC, it would be best to get a MT as you will be in better control of it.

    With some add-ins of parts or accesorries, it would have better performance.
    I using MT stock car, Gear1 = 0-40 gear2 = 40-90 gear3 = till 130. You will be able to achieve the above results if you drag till 7K rpm. I have seen 1 lance who change open pod filther+muff+stabiliser drag 0-100 in around 8secs. If you are looking for a normal sedan, it would be alright. But I personally find that the CS3 lance butt is a bit heavy. If you cornering at high speed, you can really feel the butt pushing out alot which causes the rear to skid. Overall ratings for it I would give a 7.5 out of 10

    Someone from one of the car forums commented that the auto transmission or throttle body easily breakdown. Its repair cost can be as high as $1.2-1.3k. It's running on SOHC engine and personally, I don't think it's good. But if you need it from point A to point B, then I guess it's ok.

    Other than that, it's a good and affordable Jap made saloon car.

    Anyway, no car is perfect and perfect car doesn't come with affordable price. So sometimes, got to give and take.

    Buy a car that as long as you're happy with it, meet your needs and within your budget. What others say may not be really important to you.
