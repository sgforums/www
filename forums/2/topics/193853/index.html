    S'pore cars going into M'sia likely to start paying levy in one month or two

    ---------------------------------------------

    Singapore cars going into Malaysia are expected to start paying the RM20 levy in one month or two.
    Malaysian Deputy Home Minister Tan Chai Ho said this on Saturday, according to Bernama news agency.

    He said that the levy would be enforced as soon as the amendment to the Road Transport Act providing for this is gazetted.

    The Minister added that he anticipated the levy could be enforced by the Immigration Department in one month or two.

    The amendment was endorsed by Malaysia's Parliament recently.

    The Minister said that the levy would be charged as the cars depart Johor and the quantum would be based on the number of entries and not the period of stay.

    "If a car stays in this country for a week, the levy is only RM20 but if it makes three entries in a day, the total levy is RM60," he said. - CNA/ir
