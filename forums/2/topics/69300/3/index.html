    Originally posted by shanhz:
    how much you spend every month on essential maintenance (eg engine oil, brakes, filters, tyres, etc, or bigger items like clutch lining, etc??)
    Hi,

    For engine oil, depending on usage and distance travelled, I spend between 50-100 for engine oil change. 50 for no name brands. 100 if I'm springing for Mobil tri-synthetic. They usually last me between 12-15,000km

    Brakes. I use Mintex brake pads for the front: $80 about once a year.
    My rear ones are drums, so it costs about $100 and lasts about 1.5-2 years.

    Filters. I just bought a HKS drop-in air filter for $50. it's washable, so I don't think I have to change it for quite a few years. Previously it was $20 every year (for paper filter)

    Oil filter. Original Nissan Oil filter costs me $12 per oil change.

    Tyres. I'm using Falken tyres, and although they're usually cheaper than other brands, my tyre size (205/45R16) increases the cost. I spend about $500 per tyre change (every 30-40,000km)

    Clutch Assembly, including pressure plate and clutch pad costs me $250 per change (labout inclusive). I usually change it once a year. Too much hard driving means that it doesn't last very long.

    Battery - $100 every 2 years

    Alternator - For a refurbished piece, it costs about $120 including installation - changed every 100,000km (4 years?)

    Radiator - I go to Malaysia for this. S$150 inclusive of labour.
    (For old cars, this needs changing at least once)
    Water Pump - $100 (same as Radiator)

    Powersteering - depending on what's spoilt, it could cost between $150 to $300. The harder you drive, the more often this spoils (2-3 years) If you drive normally, it will probably last 5-7 years without much incident.

    Suspension. I'm using aftermarket, comfort shocks (Monroe Sensatrac) $400 with installation (for all 4).

    Gearbox (normally neglected) I spent $350 for a "new" reconditioned one (including installation). This happens more often for COE cars.
    IF it's an automatic, repair costs starts from $500. And up to $3000 for a "newly" reconditioned one.

    I'll stop at that .... for now.
