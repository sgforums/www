    all the EVO series (from the first gen CD9A right up to the current CP9A) have their engines mounted in side-to-side transverse arrangement, so your info on the Evo V is incorrect.

    And the proper term for the Skyline's engine, is that it is mounted longitudinal, meaning "north-south", where the crankshaft is oriented along the long-axis of the vehicle, front to back.

    Another 4WD car with longitudinal engine was the European Ford Escort RS Cosworth, though its FWD sports model, the RS2000, had its engine mounted side-to-side tranverse (4WD of the RS2000 was also available, in the same configuration, but built in limited numbers)

    Yes FWD car can do drifting..but RWD car will be the more natural one at drifting..here's why:

    1) The only way to drift a FWD car is to use the handbrake to induce a drift..and by using the handbrake to induce a drift in a FWD car, the car will more likely lose control especially if driver is new..and once a FWD car is in a drift and the driver steps on the accelerator, the car will slowly get out of the drift instead of drifting even more..a FWD car's drift harder to control in general, though I might be wrong..

    2) RWD car easier to drift because most of the time, releasing the accelerator mid-corner will be enough to induce a drift, and once the drift sets in, step on the accelerator gently and drift will continue until driver applies enough opposite lock on the steering wheel and releases accelerator to bring the car out of a drift..RWD might sometimes be hard to control in a drift too..4WD also can drift by releasing accelerator mid-corner..
