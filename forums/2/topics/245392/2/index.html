  • Perception
    30 Apr 07, 00:33
    Its not wise to install a turbocharger into your engine. There are alot of factors to consider.

    - Brakes: as mentioned, you might need a better brake to deal with the additional power you get through the turbo.

    - Engine construction: The current engines you have might not be able to take the additional power. Basically this is what a turbo does. First we shall have 2 sides, the exhaust and the compression side.

    1.) The exhaust gas from the engine powers the impeller from exhaust side.
    2.) The impeller, connected using a shaft spins another impeller on the compression side. Note that the exhaust and compression side are sealed tight to prevent the exhaust gas from mixing with the air intake.
    3.) The impeller from the compression side gets the spinning power from the exhaust side impeller and thus able to force more air into the engine's combustion chamber. To cope with the additional air intake, your engine will tend to pump more fuel into the combustion chamber so that the air fuel ratio is proportional. So thats how you get your extra power.

    Thus if you install a turbo, the air intake and exhaust valves and many other components will be subjected to additional pressure and forces compared to what it is designed for. So it will basically shorten your engine's lifespan.

    Just 2 cents of my knowledge. Correct me if I am wrong.
