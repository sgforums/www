    I have registered a company before under my name (a Pte Ltd co.), which shares I have now sold to the Europeans, though I still run and manage it for them till this day.

    I'm thus only familiar with the owning/running of a Pte Ltd company. With a Pte Ltd co., you'll need to appoint auditors (though auditing is not always needed anymore), company secretary (can be yourself), book keepers (can be yourself), etc etc. In other words, you'll need to show your full set of accounts to the government - where/how your money comes and goes. If you don't profit in 3-5 years, there is a chance that ACRA or IRAS will look into your accounts, to see if the system is being "abused" (for lack of a better word).

    I don't have first-hand experience in the running of other types of companies (sole proprietorship, partnership, etc). I know you don't need to appoint the same parties as a Pte Ltd co., and are not subject to auditing....but has anyone really tried owning such a "ghost company" with no trading movements? Knowing how well-informed governments are where tax collection is concerned.....is there really such a simple loophole, to be able to just register a company and use its name to buy a commercial vehicle and keep it peacefully for years without being questioned?

    (I'm not doubting anyone's words that it might be possible....I'm sincerely asking for the sake of my own knowledge).
