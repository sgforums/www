    4 Jul 07, 11:17
    Simply put, imagine two disc. One is rotating because the engine is running.
    The other is pressed against the rotating plate, and therefore it also rotates.
    This is when the clutch is engaged, i.e. the clutch pedal is not depressed.

    When you step on the clutch pedal, the two disc separate, and therefore power is not transmitted to the drive wheels.

    When you slowly raise the clutch pedal slightly, the two disc slowly come together, until at one point, the two disc are just brushing against each other. That is the the biting point. You feel it. You sense it. You do not find the biting point by estimating the distance you raise the clutch pedal.

    The clutch pedal can be adjusted to have a high or low biting point.
