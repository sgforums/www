    Okay, there's traffic rules for all cyclists to conform to, otherwise, TP can issue them a warning or fine. You can read the rules in the basic theory hand book for Class 3 license, there is a section for the rules cyclist should obey, if you want further clarification.

    1) Its a must to cycle with the flow of traffice, going against the flow of traffic is illegal and dangers IE what if a motorcycle did that?

    2) Cyclists have to obey the traffic lights, ie stop at a red.

    3) Cyclists have to give way to pedestrians on a zebra crossing as well as on a pavement.

    4) Cyclists are NOT allowed to cycle on high ramps/ flyovers or under pass. The reason being, they will go TOO slow and have difficulty. This is illegal but I see cyclists who do so.

    5) Cyclists cannot cycle on HIGHWAYS like PIE, reason being, they're too slow and can obstruct traffic. IE the same ruling such for cars/motorcycles the slowest speed you can drive on a expressway is 40km/h, any slower you should have your hazard light on.

    6) Cyclists must wear bright colours and a MUST to have a bike light at night. I have encountered an Indian man cycling without his shirt on, you can understand that i cannnot even see him cycling on the road at all, no street lights even!

    7) Cyclists should flag or show their intention for turning right or left or coming to a stop, WHICH VERY FEW DO, ie basic hand signals applies to cars/motorcycles when your signal does'nt work, you learn this is from your basic theory handbook or from military hand signals, its the same.

    Cyclists CANNOT cycle on the fast/ overtaking lanes, they can however cycle on a middle lane if its a 3 lane road, reason being they might want to turn right at the junction.

    Please take note of these rulings to all cyclists.
