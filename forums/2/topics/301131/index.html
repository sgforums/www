    Generally, supercars are stock factory models delivering above 400bhp and sprints to 60mph in less than 4 secs in a somewhat coupe-like or chassis designed ground up with performance in mind. If a factory makes a 4 door sedan with a super engine means they are trying to appeal to the practical driver whom has loads of cash to spare. It also means they have compromised pure driver's pleasure to some form of practicality and marketability. These are not supercars, they are superbly-marketable cars. Modded cars are by nature in another category so we shan't discuss that.

    As far as i recall, Japan has not yet produced a stock factory car delivering such power and speed. Not any STIs, Evos, S2Ks, NSXs, RX7s, or any other high performance sports cars in the Japanese stable has acquired such levels of specifications until the current GTR.

    In other words, Nissan has by definition manufacured a "supercar" by virtue of paper specifications. You and he might not consider the GTR as a supercar, but i am sure those whom are fans do. The first Ferrari or Lamborghini weren't supercars either, but today they probably are due to their relentless pursuit along purist lines of delivering the ultimate high performance road vehicle.

    I daresay that Nissan has just raised the bar on supercar affordability by building one at such low cost. Or rather, offering one to the mass market at such a low price. If you can build a 200 grand supercar but some other smart alec need 800 grand to make one, who's the dumb one i wonder. Either its overpriced on brand value, or it simply makes you wonder what the Japanese could have included and given to consumers with a sticker price of 800 grand for a car. Premium leather seats? You mean only Italian cows can be made into leather? The gap is still in existence of course, but the bridge has surely shortened.
