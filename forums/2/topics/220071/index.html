    Is your car Auto or Manual. If Manual, its easier to climb if your car hasn't got so much power. Whether its auto or manual, Try to keep your RPM at 3K, it will give you an easy time up. Switch off the aircon during climb, it will give you some power, and also prevent overheating.

    Towards the peak, there's a really deep slope. Manual car with 1.5 or less CC will have to engage to Gear 1 or 2.

    When coming down, try not to use foot brake as you might burn your brakes. Engage in low gear, its ok for the RPM to pull to more than 5K if you need to. Don't be scare by the engine sound, Don't worry your engine won't break.

    Take it slow, don't rush. If you are not confident, just go half peak at Gotong Raya, park your car there and take the cable car up.

  • 4wheelsdriver
    17 Nov 06, 23:54
    Just came back from Genting. The road up Genting has improved alot. Some parts of the road going up has 3 lanes that make it easier to drive up. If you are using a manual car, use 2nd and 3rd gear. When near the top, the road is a one-way and has 3 lanes and this makes driving up easy. You do not need to stop and brake the car so frequently compare to the past where there are only 2 lanes and there are many cars infont of you and you also have to watch out for cars coming down. Use Shell V-power petrol when going up, it will provide extra power for your car when you are going up. I have tried with this petrol and found that it really gives additional power driving up the hill.

    When driving down take note of some of these precautions. Immediately after you start your car, before your start driving, always press the brake pedal a few times. Then move the car slowly and test the brake and make sure that the brake system is working properly. Do not press on the accelarator when the car is down gradient. Engage 2nd or 3rd gear and press on the brake to slow down the car if the car is travelling at above 40km/hr. Do not always press on the brake as this will wear the brake lining.

    The North-South expressway has also improved. Some parts of the expressway from Malacca to KL has 3 lanes and the road has been resurfaced. This makes driving much more comfortable. Beware of road works along this stretch as they are adding lanes and resurfacing the road.

    Happy driving to Genting and these few tips should help you to have a safe journey.
