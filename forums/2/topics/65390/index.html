    Yah yah it doesn't follow directly that a DOHC engine is better than an SOHC, there are many other factors involved.
    Yah Silvercab, there are many engines with both Variable Valve Timing and DOHC... think of the Corolla as an example.

    Yah Ah Wei DOHC means double overhead camshaft... two rods over the valves one to control the intake and the other for the exhaust.
    SOHC engines use one rod only.

    VVT-i is the name for Toyota's variable valve timing technology... basically it changes the timing of the opening and closing of the intake/exhaust valves of the cylinders in the engine. This changes the characteristics of the engine... torque, high end power blah blah won't go into the details...
    It goes by other names as well, VANOS (BMW) and VTEC (Honda) and Honda's VTEC is quite different from Toyota's VVT-i....
