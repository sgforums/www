    FC really isnt an issue if you just to and fro work. I travel from Simei to Boon Lay every morning to my office and its ard 35Km so per day only 70km travelled. So in 20 working days you may travelled around 1500 km (plus your own outside or work consumption).

    Thus a car which gives you 10km / litre will need 150 litres of fuel. Assuming you pump Esso/Mobil 95 which is 1.465 per litre, you would spend $219.75 per mth.

    In comparison a car which say gives you 12km / litre, you would spend $183.13

    In comparison a car which gives you 15km / litre (which getz and kelissa cant give you i believe), you would spend $146.50.

    Kelissa and Getz's FC is close to one another I believe cos I never read up on them, thus based on yout travel consumption, FC should not be a big consideration as the savings you may achieve per mth is not that critical. The lesser you travel, the lesser FC becomes important.

    For your reference, I travel around 3000+ Km per mth on my sales job and I use to drive a KIA Sephia and my fuel every mth $400+. Now I drive a Honda City Vtec, I spend less than $300 per mth. So with over $100 in savings per mth, FC should then be a critical factor.
