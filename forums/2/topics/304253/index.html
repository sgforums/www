    There is a reason why your instructor wants you to use the 2nd method. If there are cars in front and behind you, imagine the car dashing off when you release your handbrake

    Just step on the accelerator and slowly lift the clutch until you feel your car tilt forward a little, its actually easier to feel this on an upslope because you're already being pushed back against your seat. Release handbrake (your car should be balanced at this time), then accelerate slowly and move off.

    When you release your handbrake, don't panic and just keep your feet stationary, I think its just a matter of practice.
