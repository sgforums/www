    1) A singaporean cannot use a foreign license to drive in Singapore, this rule applies also for PRs and Employment Permit/Work Permit holders.

    2) Singapore law only allows ppl above 18 to drive in Singaproe. So regardless if you are licensed overseas, it is still illegal for under 18 to drive.

    3) Penalty for using foreign license to drive, first time is warning and confiscation of foreign license.

    4) Penalty for under 18 driving in singapore, owner of car gets into trouble and depending on the severity, it might be a criminal offence for the driver.

    5) So much so, your being licensed might be a mitigating factor in the sentence you receive. because a trained driver driving is better than an untrained driver driving.

    WHy would they not issue you a class 3? As mentioned, as of now, you can convert a foreign license to Class 3 after passing the Theory Test.

    The requirements are simple,
    a) have a foreign license,
    b) have an extract from the authorities that you have been driving for more than 1 year and that you were never banned from driving.
    c) Have a valid reason to be overseas (studying, working, etc). This is to prevent people from going overseas just to pass a driving test. Usually evidence needed are, School results, Exit Permits, Visas issued, etc.
    d) pass theory test
