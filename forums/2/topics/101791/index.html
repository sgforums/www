    Originally posted by Tayysj:
    from thailand onwards? which countries? n what's up with southern thailand? have you driven there before? including the countries after thailand?
    Nah, never drove pass Thailand before. Been to Betong in Thailand though.

    3 countries borders Thailand up north - Burma, Laos and Cambodia - none of which are friendly to people entering their country. You can forget about Burma, only accompanied tourist are allowed. Laos is equally bad if I remember correctly. Cambodia might be a possible route, then you will have to pass through Vietnam followed by China.

    China onwards, things may get wilder, possible routes are Kazakhstan, Kyroyzstan, Talistan, Pakistan - none of which are friendly. Bandits are not uncommon in those places I heard. Best bet might be Pakistan, but it leads to either Afghanistan or Iran. Neither of which is a country you would want to pass through. But then, no matter which route you choose, you have to eventually pass through either Russia or Iran. If you can survive that far, the rest of the journey would be peanuts.

    Essentially, the problems are
    (1) Getting a visa to enter a number of the countries enroute
    (2) Some countries are highly unsafe with bandits along the way

    Those are the main two. Of course there are others, but I think the rest are minor compared to the above two.
