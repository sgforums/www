    FC very much depends on your right foot and the route taken (expressway/city driving).

    It's not very fair to compare FC between sunny (1.6L), vios (1.5L) and city (1.5L). Definitely 1.5L cars offer better FC than a 1.6L car. But then, space, comfort and price of the 1.5L car may have to be compromised.

    You can't travel fast in S'pore even on an expressway. So why bother about which car can travel faster?

    If you're referring to "taking off", then may be there's still something to debate about.

    I was very concerned about FC initially but then after weighing the pros and cons, I ended up buying a not so FC friendly car but it's spacious, comfortable, within my budget and meet my needs.

    If you travel alot a day, then you should seriously consider a car that can offer you better FC. Else, why pay upfront more car loan with bank interest on the car that supposed to give you good FC as these cars don't come cheap?

    Anyway, though petrol price is hefty nowadays but then you don't have to pay any bank interest for it as it's not part of the car loan and end of the day, it's you that control the usage of your car.

    DonÂ’t underestimate the long term cost of FC differences.

    Consider FC of Car A is 10km/L and Car B is 12km/L. Looks an innocent difference of only 2km/L, but letÂ’s do a calculation:

    For similar mileage e.g. 12km, Car A will use 1.2L while Car B will use 1L of petrol, i.e. Car A needs 20% more petrol, meaning Car A will pay 20% more in petrol than Car B.

    If $50 per week is spent on petrol for Car B, that means Car A will spend $50 x 1.2 = $60

    In 1 week, Car A will spend $10 more
    In 1 year, Car A will spend $10 x 52wks = $520 more
    In 10 years, Car A will spend $520 x 10yrs = $5200 more

    Hence, fuel efficiency can easily make a difference of more than 10% of a carÂ’s price depending on usage. With petrol prices moving upwards in the long term, decide for yourself how important FC will be vs other attributes in the car you want.
