    send the BMW to VICOM or AAS and ask them to do a check for you. They have such service. They are the experts in detecting any minor damges.

    The fee is about S$120 and they can detect any accident repair b4, engine condiction ok etc.

    If the seller refused to have it vertified by VICOM or AAS, then obviously there are things to hide.

    As for 2nd hand dealer, I if the car is VICOM certified, check the dates of the certification. If the date is more than 2 months, get them to send to VICOM or AAS again.

    Do be warned that nowadays, there are many 2nd hand car dealers renting out the cars that are supposed to be sold. You can easily find them in ST classifieds ads.

    So be careful.
