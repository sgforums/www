    In my opinion, when the Avante was launched, it was sold at around $68,000. Given the current price, I feel that now is certainly a good time to purchase cars as COE premium have fallen so steeply.

    But do note that as car prices fall, market interest rates for loans have risen to around 3.18% as banks are tightening their loans requests. Also, although the car prices have fallen, monthly maintenance of car including the insurance, petrol, servicing, parking, road tax, ERP and other related charges remains more or less on the high side. You may only save on monthly instalments, if any.

    At low COE prices, COE rebates will also be low if you wish to change to another car in a few years' time. Hence, if you buy a car now, it would be wise to drive for longer periods of time to keep the depreciation low and make your expenditure worthwhile.

    Just my 2 cents' worth...
