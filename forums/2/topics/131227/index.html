Loading & Unloading Bay in HDB Estates

  • ShutterBug
    9 May 05, 18:46
    I post this topic, to highlight the lack of Terms or Rules governing the use of these Loading & Unloading Bays in HDB Estates.

    You all are likely to be familiar with the Loading & Unloading Bay in HDB Estates, there's usually a sign that says in proper and simple English "No Waiting & No Parking", and more likely than not, you'd see a motorbike, a van, a car, or even some rubbish from renovation occupying it.

    Now, I recently parked my vehicle in one, left it to collect some goods from an apartment to load into the vehicle. Returning 20 minutes later, I find a ticket title NOTICE OF INTENDED PROSECUTION, and $100 fine.

    A check via email with HDB detailed me a reply that states;
    The purpose of the loading and unloading bays are for motorists to park
    their vehicle temporarily when carrying out loading and unloading
    activities. Motorists have to move their vehicles to designated parking lot
    after completing such activities.

    I asked in reply;
    While I understand the standing rule is that, the Loading & Unloading bays are for motorists to park their vehicles temporarily when carrying out loading and unloading activities, but I wish to ask, how long is 'temporarily'? The signboard says No Waiting, or Parking, but it lacks indication of time frame allowed. Or perhaps, a good consideration would be the deployment of parking meters for loading and unloading bays? It would make a parking offence less arguable, and the infringment, more outright.

    So far and to date, I haven't got a reply.

    In my opinion, the rules governing the use of these Loading & Unloading bays are at best, rather vague and ill-defined. Hence this facility is like a fish trap; in that it is used only in the best of the public's knowledge - if your vehicle happened to be parked there, and you're not around, you shall be assumed to have committed an offence.

    In my suggestion to HDB vide email, I seeded the idea of implementing parking meters with integrated barriers controlled by time limit, and pointed out that this method would be beneficial to both the public and HDB. Although, I doubt that they'd consider my suggestion viable.

    In any case, let me hear your views about these Loading and Unloading Bay in HDB Estates. Note that at anytime, most Loading and Unloading bays are occupied by some vehicle - loading or unloading or not. Each incident when caught, whenever the parking enforcement units are deployed in that area, a sizeable number of tickets can be harvested.

    Is this FAIR to us?

    Tricks and decoys like leaving the door/s open will only tighten rules on the misuse of these LUL bays.
