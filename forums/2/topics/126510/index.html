    15 Apr 05, 13:26
    Just a few more thoughts on the difference between NTUC and CitySpeed

    1) NTUC group A should be compared with CitySpeed group B. CitySpeed's group A consists of Hyundai and Proton cars. Cars of such quality are not present in the NTUC offering at all
    2) From the answers given in the forum the only thing is clear: which company is cheaper for you depends on the intended use. Examples:
    a) for going to Malaysia NTUC charges 50% of the rate while CitySpeed $50. Which is cheaper? If you spend more than $100, then CitySpeed. If less than $100, then NTUC.
    b) Personal injury insurance is included in NTUC but is an additional $3 charge in CitySpeed.
    c) if you drive often, then NTUC's valueshare plan offers slightly lower prices than CitySpeed. If you don't drive often, then you need to question yourself whether it is a good idea to pay an annual fee for car sharing. Maybe you will it be better to rent instead.
    d) NTUC offers cheap diesel minivans (catergory U), which are not present in CitySpeed
    e) locations are not uniform. NTUC is underepresented in the west, while CitySpeed is quite scarse in the north
    g) etc.

    Conclusion: examine you intended driving patterns and make a decision. Things are not as simple as the prices you see in those advertised tables
