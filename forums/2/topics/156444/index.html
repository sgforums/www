    The exact reason is that HID operates via a ballast, which means to say, it takes some time to get started up and warm up to the temperature. If you notice cars with HID, the moment they switch on their HID, it will take a while to light up and achieve the intensity. Which means to say, if you install HID on your high beam, the moment you activate in emergency, e.g. to warn someone not to cut into your lane, it will have a retard reaction. So the guy in front will not see your high beam, and you'll cursing and swearing 'cos you run into him.
