    Penang Trip Report
    --------------------------
    The TP was out in force. Could not get past the toll after the immigration checkpoint (Msian side) on 2nd link before being flagged for speeding. Was told that my speed tagged was 136kph. Reckon it was a fair deal as I hit 160. "Paid" the settle now amount instead of settle later ;-)

    Good Points of 230JM
    ==================
    1. Nice V6 response when you floor the throttle. Nice sound too.
    2. The salesman was wrong. The limit is still capped at 180pkh although the speedo says max is 240. Instead of a cut off, it just stays at 180
    3. Air-con was very good even under the hot sun
    4. Passengers felt the car was very comfortable
    5. A lot of admirers as it seems this model is still not available in M'sia

    Bad Points of 230JM
    =================
    1. Insulation is very poor. Besides the car sounding like a zinc hut during a rain, the tyre noise from the Michelin Pilot Sport is very jarring.
    2. The driver seat is not as good as the old 520 BMW
    3. Steering is over sensitive. Slight movement causes car to move in response. Should put in some damping to cut off slight accidental movement
    4. Consumption is a bit on the high side. But then this is 2300 cc, so I guess it is expected to drink a bit more. I think about the same as my 520 BMW.
    5. Brake not so elegant as the one on BMW. But can live with it
    6. Car movement on winding roads is quite an affair. Even with better tyres you cannot take it too easy on the winding downhill roads near Ipoh/Taiping.
    7. 180 kph limit is a wee bit too much as the protons are doing 200 now.

    Others
    ======
    Going up North is tricky. Got lost trying to find highway out of KL. Ends up in Sg Buloh. It is not me only as a group of SG bikers were lost too.

    Anyway, all is well and we reach Sheraton in Penang at 4.30 pm and we started in East Coast Singapore at 6.10am. Delay was due to getting lost in KL and also due to trying to be a good boy doing 110 and 90 kph after the paying the "fine".

    Return was better as we left hotel at 6.00 am approx. and we were able to get to Kulai (had to run an errand there) at 12.30 pm. Average speed was 150kph. Paid another "fine" somewhere in Negri Sembilan - doubtful that they caught me - reason was the way they greeted me. Told me I was speeding but did not tell me what speed I was caught.

    Stopped for breakfast (some town in Penang) and lunch at the Ayer Keroh stop in Malacca. (3 other stops for restroom breaks)

    Re-fueled 4 times - total RM 180 - full when I left and full when I got back.
