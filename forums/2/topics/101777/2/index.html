  • Qitai
    16 Nov 04, 23:16
    Yeap, you can just expect your buyer to factor that in too, meaning you would have to sort of share the $17,000 to your buyer in the sense of a cheaper price to them. E.g. Normal car price is 60K for new car. OPC is 43K. After 7 years, normal car may sell at 25K, but an OPC may sell at 20K.

    But actually, the bigger problem I would worry about if I get an OPC is the number of buyers out there who are willing to buy it. Since it is not the mainstream, you can expect less buyer and thus it is harder to sell.

    Of course, there is also this option of converting the car back to a normal car before selling, but that means you will have to pay back the balance to LTA. In this case, the amount will be 30%*17,000 = 5,100 + administration charges.

    The last option would be to scrap the car pre-maturely just like what you will do for a normal car. In this case, you will have to payback the $5,100 as well for the unused OPC period in the form of reduced scrap value.

    Originally posted by benhur80:
    that is with reference to OPC car or normal car?

    if normal car can fetch 19K,how about OPC?any rough estimation done with respect to the same OMV and COE(but have to minus the 17,000 offset)
    If we assume the same OMV of 12K and COE of 23K, and assuming 17K offset, then the scrap value @ 7 years is 10K. So, perhaps 14K would be an appropriate resale value.

    But note that the above assumes stable COE value which is probably a very bad assumption. The resale value of a car is very much dependent on the price of a new car. And if a new car is a lot cheaper or more expensive, then the resale value is lower or higher accordingly. Most buyer do make a comparison of the cost of buying a new car compared to the cost of a resale car. And if it make sense to buy a resale car, then the demand will push up the resale value. During the time before 1997, I have even heard of stories of people earning money after using a car for 1-2 years, since the COE is raising rapidly during those period.

    Originally posted by X-Wolf:
    Anbody knows how to calculate the scrap value of OPC cars? Is the % PARF same as normal car? The $17k has to be pro-rated and paid back to LTA?
    PARF for OPC is same as normal car. The 17k rebate is used to offset COE, unless yr COE is lower than 17k, watever excess rebate will then be used to offset yr ARF which is tied in to yr PARF. Otherwise, no effect on PARF.

    No need to pay back LTA regardless when u de-reg car. Only need to pay back pro-rated rebate it u want 2 convert it back to normal car.
