    Originally posted by zenixth:
    From what I knew, the 17K rebate only applies to new cars. If you want to convert a second hand car to OPC, only the road tax rebate of $800 applies.

    INFORMATION FROM LTA
    -------------------------------------------------------------
    You can apply to the LTA to convert your normal car to an OPC. Upon receiving a letter from LTA, you can proceed to change the car number plate to red and have it sealed by an authorised Inspection Centre. To complete the conversion, you will need to submit the required documents as listed in the letter from LTA and pay the conversion fee of $100.

    You will be given the $800 discount on the annual road tax subject to a minimum road tax payment of $50 per year. No tax rebate on your COE quota premium or ARF will be given.

    If your car is de-registered before it is 10 years old and is eligible for Preferential Additional Fee (PARF) benefit, an additional PARF may be granted on top of the normal PARF benefit. This additional PARF is computed as follows:

    Lifespan of the car as a converted OPC (in months & days) x ($2,200/12)
    ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Essentially, I read that as you get more PARF value if you convert a normal car to OP car. And this amount is $2,200 per year. If you look at it over 10 years, you actually get $22K worth of rebate which is more than the $17K rebate.
