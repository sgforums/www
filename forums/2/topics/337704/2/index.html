    The reason why this month's COE price is $2 is because the number of bids submitted is around the quota, hence the number of bids is not large to cause a big jump in prices.

    Hence, I feel the most a car dealer can deduct is $5000 per car. Because if they cut too much, there will be a strong influx of customers, as a result, they might risk being unsuccessful in their first few bids. COE will go up and the cycle repeats itself.

    I don't really understand why we should be happy about this. Rather, the group of people who should be rejoicing are those who have bought their cars during the previous round of COE. Now that the COE has dropped, the dealers will refund the difference to them! Afterall, if we decide to buy now, the COE that we ultimately get will not be $2.
