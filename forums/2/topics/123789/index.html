If you travel a lot, you might want to consider the Vios, which is smaller, with better FC but more expensive.

    Okie, lets based on a 10,000 downpayment, inclusive of insurance. So lets presume your insurance is 2k, so technically you only downpay 8k. The kia cerato HATCHBACK (which is slight cheaper and i personally find it nicer, heh....) cost 56499 now. So with a 8k downpayment, your total loan is 48499.

    Yes, one thing to take note is that its not advisable for 100% loan, and worst still, 10 yrs loan is not advisable as well but if you got NO choice, then you go for 10 yrs loan. The reason being that the longer period you stretch, you'll get a higher interest to repay, and the earlier you sell your car, the more money you will lose cos of higher outstanding interesr. Hope this is clear.

    So with a 2.7% loan for 10 yrs, your interest will be $13094.73 in all plus your loan, which mean you got to repay 61593.73 in 10 yrs. So in a yr, you got to pay 6159.37 and a monthly of SGD513.28. This is just the loan itself. New car comes with 1st half a yr free road tax. So other things to take into consideration is the maintenance, parking and other costs like petol etc..........

    I do not look down on Korea car really but if its okie, go for a jap brand...... but then, with your stringent budget, believe the Korea car will work for you best. Another smaller sedan to consider is the Hyundai Accent or Kia Rio. Both consider small sedan, and as they are new, wun be LKK.

Like I say, it will be good if you work out other hidden cost. At this moment, believe no japanese car that is able to meet your budget at the moment.
