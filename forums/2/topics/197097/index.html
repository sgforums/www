    The only bad thing about learning to drive in Singapore is that you never learn how to park in a real situation. They use poles to guide you and then your instructor actually teaches you a method to "cheat" so that you can park nicely within the 4 poles with just one try... so as time goes by, you become an expert when it comes to parking within poles... just remember the code "turn the wheel 2 rounds when u see the first pole touch the side mirror.... and so on"

    And then when u finally pass, u realise that you dunno how to park at all.... i suggest that you learn to park nicely and straight with no cars on your left or right.... when u're good with tht already, try it with a car on your left and then right.... lastly, try it with cars on both sides... do this at a deserted carpark... rule of thumb is --> DO NOT DAMAGE OTHER PPL'S CAR!

    As for parallel parking, it's harder to find chance to learn.... never try to squeeze in to a small space if u're new to parallel parking..... never attempt to parallel park beside a busy road where a lot of cars will pressurize u to hurry up.... just try at some deserted place.... find a big space between 2 cars and try it....

    **USE YOUR OWN JUDGEMENT. GET A FEEL OF YOUR CAR'S DIMENSIONS AND RESPONSE. THERE IS NO SUCH THING AS "FOLLOW A PARTICULAR PATTERN AND GET IN NICELY STRAIGHT AWAY.
