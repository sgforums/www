    Do check out what the Tuff guys used for soundproofing and where. Different parts of the car, different effect. If you are talking about road noise, changing a nice set of comfort tyres might be more practical.

    Aftermarket sound proof have their limits and would never be able to achieve what branded car can. Few reasons involve, window glasses, extensive sound proofing coupled with sound damping, shielding for gaps to reduce wind noise, ......etc.

    Lastly, most people do aftermarket sound proofing for ICE.

    U can go to Chuan Sing (Serangoon Garden Way). Not too sure about the charges, but heard its not very cheap. But quality-wise, its definitely thumbs-up, at least from my friend who had his car done by them.

    Else, u can adopt these simple measures to reduce the road noise:

    1) High profile tyres (which means smaller rims)
    2) Tyres with less grip (grip and road noise don't really go hand-in-hand)
    3) DIY Soundproof of your car (using foam like Acumat, Dynamat or Bitumen) on the flooring and doors
    4) Spraying the wheel archs with 3M undercoat spray. Won't last long and u might need to touch up now and then.
