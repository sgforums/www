    The Full Review by Topgear

    The Cerato is the size of a Golf, a lot cheaper, and by no means hopeless. I say this right now because people don't always read new Kia stories right to the end. But, whether it's desperately uncool or not, this one does deserve your attention, because it shows just how far Kia has come lately. If it keeps going at this rate then by, ooh, a week next Tuesday, it'll probably be capable of building a Porsche 911 rival.

    But, for now, the Cerato woos you with its size and spec list. It's a 1.6-litre, with aircon, an in-dash CD that also plays your MP3 compilations, six airbags and brake-assist. Same-size rivals at this price are mostly poverty-spec 1.4s.

    It gets along pretty well, and has more than adequate grip for the power. The steering's pleasant and body control fine. There's a fair bit of noise of all kinds - Kia made the mistake of benchmarking against the Toyota Corolla, which is a pretty loud car itself. Cabin design and quality are a big step up for the Koreans, but the exterior styling tries a bit too hard, with lines and bumps all over the place.

    Kia is part of Hyundai (the Cerato is an Accent underneath) and between them, by 2010, they want to sell more cars globally than Renault and Nissan combined. To do that the cars will have to be better than this. But at this rate of progress, don't bet against it.

    in terms of handling, the ford focus is the unparalleled handler in its class.. it uses a traditional hrdraulic steering rack.. which gives much better feedback than an electric-power steering, used in the altis..handling wise.. the altis lags behind due to its more comfort-biased springs.. and due to the fact tht it has a torsion beam at the rear.. in contrast to the focus's multilink.. it doesnt' give as much variations in suspension geometry.. thus handling is not as good.. however, the altis's handling isnt as bad as most ppl think.. in fact, its turn-in response is quite good.. so lowering the car and fixing stiffer springs will help a bit..

    performance-wise, the altis beats the focus.. as it has variable valve timing.. which means optimised torque and power..

    the focus is going to be replaced soon... which explains the lower price.. but i still think its a better buy... since its built in europe.. so its quality is better than tht of the altis.. foci dont sell tht cheap usually.. so if u cant wait.. just go for it..
