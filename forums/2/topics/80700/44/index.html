    Insurance depend on age, most ex is 3k. Mine insurance is 3k for 21 yrs old. So I use my dad's name.

    My advice is loan 7 years, dont loan 10 yrs bcos the interest is too high and stretch for so long means that ur monthly instalment is low, so if you intend to sell abt 3 years later, you cannot sell bcos your outstanding loan is much higher than your car value. As a result, you may have to top up 5-10k to sell your car.

    Even if you dont intend to sell so soon, are u sure that these car can last 10 years? Maybe at around 4-5 yrs if u unlucky it starts giving u lots of problem until u have to sell it bcos the repair cost is not worth.

    I suggest u at least put 10-20k down payment, so that u can easily sell anytime without making much lost and putting more downpayment, u save on interest. This is a car with very good fuel consumtion, 15-18km/liter depending on driving style. So it a good choice. Dont rush into it. Plan ur financial properly b4 u commit.

    Also u cannot calculate ur road tax, insurance into monthly commitment.
    Say ur instalment is 550, carpark only 95, petrol 200, you need abt 900 per month. Plus car park parking, coupons, cash card for erp, you need abt 50 dollars.

    So you must confidently have abt 1k per month. And end of year, you must be able to come up with 2k for your insurance and 500+ for road tax. Beside all this, its better you have abt 5k set aside for emergency repairs dur to accident and doing up your cars.

    So all in all, at least 10k cash(down payment), 1k per month, 2.5k yearly and 5k aside for emergency. With this, you will be sure you wouldnt be worrying no money for instalment or pay for emergency repair if suay suay accident or need to sell cars abt 2-3 years later after u are sick of it.
