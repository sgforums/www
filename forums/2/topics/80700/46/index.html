    I don't know about the KIA Picanto 1.1litre, at least for now. My first car, in Australia was a 92' Sukuzi Swift 1.3litre (aka Holden Barina, in Australia). When I bought it, it was already more than 8 years old. It works like a wonder; I averaged at over 17km/litre, no less. I sold my car in 2001 to a friend and he is still driving it. I still get to drive it when I go to Perth. It's surprisingly smooth after 12 years. I believe manufactures don't make cars to only last ten years. You must remember, there are people in Australia that drive their car for over 20 years and it still work well. There are even cars that last and last... 50 years?

    Back to my first car; the technology on a 92 Suzuki Swift was old and was a carb model unlike most fuel injection models now (Picanto is electric fuel injection). Fuel injected cars seems to be more fuel effective. Newer technology also means more refined overall; from the engine to the tiny little components that aids the car's performance.

    It's how you maintain and upkeep your car from day to day driving. If that Suzuki was able to last the way it did... I think newer cars should stand a even better chance (unless the manufacture already decided to make cars for Singapore to last only ten years?).

    Hope this helps
    Vincent
