    Did you all know that the picanto price has increaed by S$2,000 (S$1,000 each time) since its launch, both times when COE increased (by less than S$1,000).

    Since then, COE prices have dipped by S$500 each time on the 23 June & 7 July biding, and by S$700 on the 21 July bid.

    That means COE prices has gone down by S$1,700, so effectively, the Picanto price should have dropped by at least S$1,700, or back to the launch prices of S$49,999 for manual and S$52,999 for auto.

    But C&C is taking that "act blur" approach & not reducing prices. With such huge profit margins now that COE prices have gone down, they are NOT :

    1) Delivering the cars on time to those who have secured COEs
    2) Bidding very low and NOT securing COE for those have booked their cars 2 months ago (probably another sales gimmic to have you buyers top up)

    I hope history is not repeating itself like the what happened to the previous KIA distributor who failed to deliver the cars and ran off with customers' deposits.
