    Uh.......not on the barge or ES300 for that matter. But in the IS200 the intitial pick-up note is a little raspier. Idle speed should be around 700+ rpm....too low could also result in some vibration.

    One more thing....try with your air-con compressor on and off...the engine noise most probably is the compressor. If so, it is better to have the air-con checked by BM technician......probably nothing but just to be safe....so u can get the 2nd dealer to rectify any problems before taking over the car

    Yes there will be some wind noise above 100km/hr.....perfectly normal. Additional insulation not really required....only adds uneccessary weight to the car.....rather when its time to change your tyres....look for a quiter model. The main road intrusion will be tyre 'roar'....can solve with careful tyre selection.

    Sports version.....no enhancement in power but think some skirting and 17 inch sport rims.....purely aesthetics.

    There are 2 variants to this model. The IS200 Lexus (rebadged) or the RS200 (Altezza).

    The Toyota Altezza is the Japanese variant of the European Lexus IS200.

    In Japan, the car is produced in two forms,

    The Inline-6 DOHC 24v VVT AS200, and the more powerful RS200 - with an inline-4 DOHC 16v VVTi engine

    The European market (including the UK) was not offered the more powerful RS200 variant, settling instead upon the Lexus badged IS200 - with the same I-6 (Inline 6 cylinder) engine and a set range of packs e.g. S, SE and Sport.

    The Toyota Altezza RS200 is available in Japan and as a UK import in both manual and automatic form, with the 6 speed manual pushing out 210bhp and the 5 speed auto box with e-shift gears close behind with 200bhp. The RS200 is the more sought after and therefore more expensive car of the two
