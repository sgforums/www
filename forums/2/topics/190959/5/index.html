    Originally posted by olala:
    knew u wuld say that,Hah
    but im the type who wuld begin prefer havign a hard time in the start but surpassing in the end
    oh no. trust me. u're surpassing nothing. u're not putting all emphasis on skills training.

    besides, i don't think u know what a hard time and an easy time in racing skills training is. u start in a sports car, i can tell u you won't be progressing very far. the power flatters your lap times on the straights, and masks any cornering mistakes. As a result, u're not learning as much as u should, not being forced to think as much as u should.

    If u want to progress fast, my challenge to u is to start training in something that doesn't have a lot of power(so no advantage on the straights), is very lightweight(so u have to make up all your time in the corners), RWD(emphasis on throttle control), no rear differential(more emphasis on throttle control), only rear brakes(easy to lock up and spin, hence emphasis on braking control) very short wheelbased(thus prone to snapping into a spin easily, more emphasis both on braking and throttle control).

    u start training in something like this, u will have a harder time than u ever will in a skyline, i promise u, and u will improve MUCH faster too.
