    In NA applications and even most FI engine up to medium boost levels, or even moderate amounts of nitrous, power loading is often the lesser of the factors that lead to failure - making up close to 0% of all NA engine failure. Combustion pressure in fact goes to offsetting some of the tensile load at TDC and for the rest of the power stroke, compressive loading is many times less than tensile.

    So in an small NA engine trying to match power of a larger one [only way to do it is to rev higher mainly], you should worry about rod failure, rod bolt failure, pin failure, and not burned or holed pistons.
