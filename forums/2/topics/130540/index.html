  • Apollo_sg

    CAI - supposedly a secondary pipe harvesting air fr low level into the air distribution system (normally open pod filter), in order to introduce cooler air in place of warm engine bay air.

    idea behind is cold air is more densier hence more air molecules will be combusted.

    but it is not as simple as this, you will lack power in low end, ie slow start. and also your car can adapt and more power = more fuel needed. needs to work with a piggyback ECU like SAFC-II, etc. tat is another story.

    -------

    lower spring is as the name implied, bring down the suspension level so you get closer to earth. hence less drag from under carriage.

    -------
    coilover also as name suggested, is coiling over the existing springs making them stiffer. good for cornering.

    -------
    if u noob, best not to touch anything since u r unsure. keep ur $$$ lock up in the bank vault n resist all temptations.

  • tailslide
    6 May 05, 00:54
    Originally posted by Apollo_sg:
    but it is not as simple as this, you will lack power in low end, ie slow start. and also your car can adapt and more power = more fuel needed. needs to work with a piggyback ECU like SAFC-II, etc. tat is another story.

    not exactly. normally, the difference in a/f caused by fitting a CAI on a NA vehicle will be compensated for by the ecu. unless u are changing major components in ur engine, the stock ecu will be more then enuff 2 handle a CAI.

    Originally posted by Apollo_sg:
    lower spring is as the name implied, bring down the suspension level so you get closer to earth. hence less drag from under carriage.

    less drag is only a quarter of the story. by lowering, u lower the position of ur center of gravity and induce more negative camber at the rear end (since integra is using macpherson struts in front, camber is hardly affected). the former gives u better handling, the latter will make ur car tend towards understeering. u also increase ur chances of grinding parts of ur car against humps, ramps and kerbs. both of which are not a desirable effects.

    Originally posted by Apollo_sg:
    coilover also as name suggested, is coiling over the existing springs making them stiffer. good for cornering.

    thats not what the term implies. coilovers simply refers to suspension set ups whereby the springs surround the shocks. in effect, even ur stock suspension are considered coilovers. u cannot alter the stiffness of ur existing springs unless u change them altogether to another set with a higher spring rate which also gives u a harsher ride.

    Originally posted by Apollo_sg:
    if u noob, best not to touch anything since u r unsure. keep ur $$$ lock up in the bank vault n resist all temptations.

    best advice i've heard so far. IMO, there's not much potential in the GSR engine to mod so u'd be better off doing more research before sinking ur precious money.
