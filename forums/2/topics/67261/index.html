    Hi again enginboi,

    Yr friend is rite. For most cases, using the 'D' is sufficient to drive an auto car ard.

    For me, I only drop gear from 'D' to '2' is when I need extra torque for the car to overtake a car change lane in tight situation.

    But I got to admit that its not good for the gear box if use it this way excessively. I got the habit of putting the car to 'N' upon red light also, which is also not good for the gear box. But so far, dun think all these had created any problems in my driving yrs till date.

    An auto gear box will normally last you 10 yrs without problem, if not, guess no one is willing to buy an auto car liao. To maintain it, just remember to change the automatic transmission fluid regularly, like 20,000km-30,000km once.

    So dun worry too much abt the spoilt gear box. Last but not leasy, yes its true that auto gearbox is more expensive than the manual one, and that's why they usually cost more than manual cars.
