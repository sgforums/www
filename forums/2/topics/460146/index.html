    There're basically 4 types of commercial vehicles.

    • G plate - Light goods vehicle, Goods + Passenger vehicle (double-cab pickup)
    Requires company registration to own. Goods + passenger vehicles have to pay 2x road tax (goods vehicle + pax vehicle), that's why its unpopular.
    For Land Rover Defender, if you its a 4-door version register under G-plate, will be considered as goods + pax vehicle.

    • Y plate - only for lorries or very big vans, can register with company or personal name
    • X plate - very heavy vehicle, can register with company or personal name
    • W plate - Engineering/Plant vehicle, if the vehicle only carries special equipment used for industry

    In short, two ways to register a Defender,
    1. G-plate as commercial vehicle and pay $500 lights goods vehicle (LGV) road tax, under company name.
    2. Private car (but defender is diesel powered, need to pay additional diesel tax; quite expensive). 
    Defender itself is expensive already, due to UK exchange rate + tax, that's why its rich men's toy.....unless you're very rich. Maintenance, repair and parts also very expensive, that's why even SAF decided to just change to civilian type Ford Everest - use and scrap also cheaper than maintain Land Rovers.
