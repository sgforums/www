    Turbo charging is fun but costly. Many aftermarket turbo installers forget one important part. Your engine block, pistons etc are manufactured to withstand certain pressures. Turbo charging increases the pressures to these parts and too much pressure will actually "blow up" your engine.

    To Turbo Charge your auto car, you need to strengthen the engine, reprogramme the ECU and better still change your gears. All the works will cost you $10k plus, but it will be a safer option than just slotting in the turbo.

    Supercharging on the other hand can achieve a better low-end result which in Singapore ( I fee) is more impt. Supercharging is safer and less hassle. After market kits are usually bolted on and can be removed for inspections within hours.

    Just my 2 cents worth. By the way, I really admire cars which are damn quiet and still let you smell smoke. Much better than those that make loads of noise but are left behind when the lights turn green.
