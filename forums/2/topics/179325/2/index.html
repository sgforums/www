    Actually not quite right.

    CC belongs to consuls. Consuls are people who have not attained the rank of ambassadors in their countries Foreign Service. But consuls in Singapore are usually because their country do no see the point of sending an Ambassadors to Singapore. So this position is usually vacant and thus the country is represented by Consuls instead.

    CD belongs to countries where representation are at Ambassadorial level.
    High Commissioners use CD. However, some Commonwealth Countries are not "entitled" to dispatch High Commissioners, hence some of them still use CC.

    While Ambassador's vehicle are typically S xx01 Cx, some countries have done away with this practice I guess for security reasons. Those I noticed either use a normal private registration or any SXXXXCX plate.
