    Some info to clear up the misconceptions in this thread

    SEP1 : belongs to president, "singapore elected president"
    SP1 : speaker of parliment
    SPF1 : comissioner of police
    SJ1 : chief justice

    the number plates: S1, S2, S3 up to S10 all belongs to the government. there are no end alphabets. they are on cars used to ferry visiting foreign ministers etc.

    the very first citizen owned number plates therefore are S11#, S12#, S13#
    they all belong to extremely rich families and will never be sold. you can see one of them at UOB plaza basement 1 carpark (sometimes it is shuttered shut), of course belongs to the Wee family

    when you see uniques plates on small cars, (eg EA4A), means that that family got many many nice number plates and just use the smallest car possible to hold on to the plate. EA4A belongs to the cheong family and they own international plaza, which is where you can see this plate

    there have been offers of up to $500k for some special plates, but when the owner is worth $20-30million, he is not going sell a plate for a few hundred thousand. and many of the wealthy believe that their personal number plates brought them luck.
