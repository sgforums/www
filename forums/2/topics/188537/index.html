  • Kr0n
    4 May 06, 02:48
    Erm, not really, tamago. I think you might have gotten it mixed up with a supercharger.

    In a supercharger (SC), it is dependent on the engine's rpm. This however, as tailslide has explained, does nothing to increase compression. I think you might have confused it with the resultant increased pressure in the combustion chamber.

    The Compression Ratio is that of volume of cylinder at bottom dead centre : volume of cylinder at top dead centre. This can't be increased/decreased just because a turbocharger or supercharger has been added.

    The turbocharger makes use of the pressure of hot exhaust gases to spin the turbine. This turbine sucks in the air, which is compressed during the suction process, resulting in its temperature rising rapidly. The air then goes through an intercooler, where it is allowed to cool before being sent to the engine.

    Pros, please correct me if I'm wrong.
