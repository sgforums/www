    ever tried "bleeding" ur brakes to see if the brake response is better? with time, air builds up inside the brake's hydraulic system resulting in a "spongy" feel associated with the need to brake hard to achieve the same braking effect. bleeding eliminates such gases built up by releasing such "contaminated" brake fluids and replacing them with new ones.

    brake pads, unlike tires, do not get "harder" with age. they do not suffer from the latent chemical changes with constant heating and cooling but will simply wear off with use.

    in any case, u should not be experiencing any friction on the disks if u are not stepping on the brakes. new pads need to be bedded in initially so u might be experiencing a temporary problem which should go off after 100 kms of driving or so.
