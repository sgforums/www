    ok.. there are several kinds of wheel alignment.

    1. Adjust toe setting only - what mech will do is to drive your car over a device that check your "Toe" setting. Vicom and other places where you do your regular inspection does this.

    2. 4 Wheel Alignment - This is more complete kinda alignment. It checks for several reading on all 4 wheels by attaching sensors to all wheels (usually optical - NIR or laser.. some older system uses mechnical means such as cables)
