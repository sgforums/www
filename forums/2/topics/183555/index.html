    Hi,

    Might be able to give some advise here......... hopefully it helps.

    I personally test drove all the 3 cars you mention before but not the Chevy. Anyway, no offence to Chevy owner but if you can afford it, try to go for a Vios or City. Esp so if you are on the road more (like in sales etc). A japanese car will definitely gives you more confident (of parts) and better FC.

    Maybe you will like to ask yrself some question 1st like........ is your car going to be auto or manual? You are going for power of fuel economy? Space important?

    I will summarised the three below for you....... cos got friends who drove Vios and the new G4 City. My comparison will be based on these three cars, the City i-dsi, vtec and Vios

    1) Space - City (in fact, much bigger than Vios).
    2) FC - City i-dsi (auto, approx 15km/l, due to 7-speed AT), (vtec auto, appox 13km/l).
    3) FC - Vios (auto, appox 12-13km/l), (manual, approx 15km/l)
    4) Power - City vtec manual ..... follow by Vios manual....... i-dsi weaker of all
    5) Price - Vios more affordable, most expensive is City Vtec of cos.

    So it really depends on idividual. If you want the most power with the cheapest price can get, the Vios Manual will be the one for you as its 10k difference as compared to the City Vtec.

    But my 2 cents, if you going for AUTO car, the City will be a better care to drive.

    I hope i can help with the Chevrolet but i don't really know abt the cars..... so can't really comment much. But then again, I do like the Optra5...... it kinda look good to me. But on long run, i believe the maintenance of these cars will be higer (due to poorer FC etc), so u really ought to think carefully.

    i-dsi will never be able to outrun a Vios for sure........ the bhp is too big a difference, and that the Vios is much lighter (900+kg as compared to City 1000+).

    The vtec is having 110bhp as compared to i-dsi 88bhp. A whooping 22bhp difference, which works out to abt 20% more bhp....... do u think u can feel the difference? The ans is definitely yes, esp so when u rammed the car harder. The price is 3-4k difference inbetween this two model. If you ask me, i will say that some pple will spend hundreds and thousands of dollars just to get another 5-10bhp out of his car...... but rite here, when you pay 3-4k more, u straight away get another 22bhp........ so i think its very much worth it. Of cos unless FC is really a concern to you.... else choose the vtec..............

    Any way fyi....... for 3-4k more, the vtec offers you the following

    1) 22bhp more
    2) built in atenna, i-dsi on the roof
    3) 4 disc brake, i-dsi 2 disc and rear 2 drum
    4) vtec black interior, or beidge........ i-dsi only beidge
    5) comes with sport rims (ugly, but can use them for trade in for abt $400SGD), i-dsi no sport rims
    6) Exhaust different, vtec one bigger
    7) illluminate speedo meter (overall nicer interior)
    rear seat diff......vtec one can fold down....i-dsi just two tray at the bottom.

    So with so many more things, esp (item 1)..... i seriously feel that its worth going for the vtec then i-dsi if you consider between these two cars. Anyway, my colleague is getting ard 500km-500km+ a bit for 35l of petrol.

    Hope this helps.
