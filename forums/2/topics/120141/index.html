    Now getting a car would be bond to it for 10 years, or 5-7 at least. Once you gotten a car, you will have to cut back on a lot of your expense. Not impossible, just a bit hard at first. Worse if you are taking 10 years loan.

    For a period of 10 years, better get a reliable "No Doubt" car that you will not grow out of, or not least you break even for the loan you are taking. Do a search on this forum, you might find a thread talking about Matrix selling off just after 1 year of ownership. That is a good lesson we can all learn. He took 10 years loan, have to top-up about 20k just to sell the car off. Do have a look slowly, no rush. Widen your choices so you can choose and compare.

    Car is a sure-lose investment that you have to hold on to to minimise loses. Remember that. Not advisable if you are not finacially stable.
