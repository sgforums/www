    Mindfulness of Breathing is one of the most common meditations, and that is the meditation that Buddha practised.

    However simple the practise may be, and even though there may be online guides for it, it is still better to meet a dharma teacher who can guide you, see your posture, and give clear instructions, and answer your questions. And the Right Motivation for Anapanasati practise is also very important:

    from another topic:

    In accordance to Buddhadharma, it is unadvisable seeking of any forms of spiritual powers as that may create a link to the lower realms.

    Powers gained by a Buddhist through dhyana (meditation) is absolutely different from those that has to do with spirits. However it shld be noted that (from the point of view of a Buddhist), we should never meditate with the intention for psychic powers, that is Wrong Motivation (google for 8 Fold Path), and may even easily enter a deviate path easily influenced by the Maras (see Shurangama Sutra). Psychic powers is only a byproduct and shld not be taken as an ultimate aim. May we avoid walking to a dark path.
