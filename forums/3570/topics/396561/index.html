    Japan offers 15 used coaches to KTMB for free

    2010/04/11

    KUALA LUMPUR: Malaysia will be getting 15 used-coaches from Japan for use by Keretapi Tanah Melayu Bhd (KTMB).

    Transport Minister Datuk Seri Ong Tee Keat said the coaches, which were still in good condition and offered free to Malaysia, were expected to arrive by June this year.

    KTMB had conducted a technical assessment of the coaches in Japan and found them safe for use here, he told reporters at a programme, "Borak-Borak and Sarapan Pagi" with occupants of the Pandan Perdana Low Cost Flats here Sunday.

    Ong said the coaches would be refurbished and used for the KTM Intercity train service.

    Early this year, the Transport Ministry cancelled the purchase of eight used Diesel Multiple Units (DMUs) valued at RM64 million from Spain after the train supplier failed to deliver the units according to schedule.

    The purchase of the DMUs was made to fulfill the pressing need of KTMB to accommodate the rising number of train passengers which had increased to 95,000 passengers daily. - BERNAMA
