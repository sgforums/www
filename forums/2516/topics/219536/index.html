    Also known as glue-sniffing, solvent abuse, solvent sniffing
    Inhalant abuse involves sniffing vapours or fumes of glue, paint thinner and similar substances.

    It affects the growth and development of muscles, nerves and organs and can lead to bone marrow problems. Sudden Sniffing Death (SSD) may result even for first time abusers. The abuser may die suddenly without warning. This is caused by rapid heartbeat which often leads to cardiac arrest.

    The effects of inhalant abuse are memory loss, inability to think, bruising and bleeding easily, damage to the central nervous system, liver and kidney damage, stomach and urinary pains, muscular cramps or coughing.

    Penalties

    Selling or offering to sell an intoxicating substance when it is suspected that the substance is for the purpose of intoxication - Up to S$5,000 fine or 2 years imprisonment or BOTH.

    Refusing a blood specimen test - Up to S$2,000 fine or 3 months imprisonment.

    Abuse of intoxicating substance - Admission to a rehabilitation center for up to 6 months or up to S$2,000 fine and/or 6 months imprisonment.
