    Regarding the canoeing/kayaking part

    Before engaging in the activities you're recommended to:
    1. Have enough rest the day before. Water sports is ten times more dangerous than most sports especially when you're out in the sea/ocean.
    2. Drink enough plain water so that you will not get heat exhaustion/stroke. This is also very important. Falling unconscious in the water is very dangerous.

    What to wear?
    1. Shorts and shirt. Ladies are recommended to wear clothes that are not revealing when wet. You'll definitely get wet unless you don't paddle at all.

    Why no slippers or shoes?
    1. Slippers can be easily lost in the waters. Sandals or booties are recommended.
    2. You don't want to damage your sport shoes do you?

    The life vest/jacket.
    1. Wear your life vest/jacket, tightening it to your body. It is for your safety. A capsized canoe, kayak due to strong currents can actually knock a person unconscious (especially the paddles).

    What to do when you fall inside the water or capsize.
    1. HOLD on to your paddle at all times, the paddle itself stays afloat.
    2. Remain calm and try to swim towards your canoe/kayak. It'll usually drift towards the shore.
    3. When holding on to the canoe/kayak, swim towards the shore.
    4. If your canoe isn't overturned, do not immediately try to climb up, wait for another canoe to come close to assist or you risk overturning it.

    How to swim with a paddle wearing a life jacket/vest.
    1. Move your arms in the same way as you paddle a canoe.
    2. Push yourself forward with your legs like how you do in breAst stroke.

    The paddle, left-handed or right-handed.
    1. Oftenly, people are confused with how to choose the correct paddle for them. This is more common for left-handed people. Place the paddle in pole position with the concave side of the bottom blade facing you. The concave side of the upper blade should be facing the direction where your master hand is.
