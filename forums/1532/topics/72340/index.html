    MAY I BORROW YOURS? 2 LOSE CELLPHONES IN RUSE

    TWO cellphones, worth about $1,500 and $250 respectively, were stolen in separate incidents in Tampines within two hours on Saturday night.

    In both incidents, the thief borrowed the victim's phone, after saying that his battery was dead. He then ran away with the victim's phone.

    The first incident happened at the void deck of Block 428, Tampines Street 41, around 9.30pm, while the second occurred around 10.40pm near Block 721, Tampines Street 71.

    The first suspect was described as dark-complexioned, about 17 years old, and wearing a green shirt with black trousers.

    In the second incident, the suspect is believed to be about 18 years old and 1.72m tall, with short hair and wearing spectacles.

    The police have not determined if the two thefts were carried out by the same person.
