    Arapaima...



    The arapaima is believed to be the largest freshwater fish in the world. While it certainly grows to a massive 10 feet in length and 450 pounds in weight (3 metres / 200 kilograms), its maximum size remains unknown - although a length of 14 feet (4.5 metres) has been widely quoted.

    With numbers greatly reduced by commercial fishing, it is now officially classed as an endangered species. Large specimens are therefore much rarer than they once were, and the exceptional giants of yesteryear are less likely to exist.

    Consequently the arapaima is only rarely caught by sport-fishermen. Throughout most of the Amazon it is considered impossible to catch on rod and line.
