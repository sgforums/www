    Every manufacturer has claims to "unique" core designs or whatnots... But I think the only company that is able to claim that is probably Mo-Rich. There is one other company out there with very different core designs, but is not as popular. Forgot which one.

    Everyone else has the same basic designs they just copy from others. The only difference is the cover/core match.

    I will always love Columbia because no other brand's coverstocks take so equally well to either shining or sanding. For plain versatility, this company wins handsdown.

    Currently, I use Track and I like what I have. They roll well and match coverstocks well with the cores. I have encountered very little problems with energy management with Track equipment, all other factors being equal.

    I would love to try Mo-Rich and Ebonite's offerings, but they specialise in asymmetric cores. So i'm out of luck there, since the only reason I wanted to try them was for their innovative core designs, especially Mo-Rich's (Ebonite's coverstocks don't impress me).

  • harmzuay
    16 Mar 03, 01:34
    the fingerhole is widened and it isnt smoothened out much
    Not bevelling the edges of thumb or finger holes is the common way to "teach" a bowler not to squeeze. My brother has been using sharp edges on his ball for years. Ultimately, the aim is to switch back to bevelled edges unless one gets so used to it that they stay that way. Some pros have grips that are naturally like this, though their names escape me now.



    Back to this thread's topic: Brunswick and Columbia indeed do not make balls with asymmetrical cores. If Columbia does make any (though none springs to mind now), those are probably rare forays into that market. But as far as I can backdate, i do not know of any Brunswick's that have a mass bias centre.

    Track bowling was for a long time a similar company - specialising in a range of very good balls without asymmetrical cores. Then they tried it once with the EMB (elliptical mass bias) ball and the response was so good, EMB because a series of balls. Since then, I think only the Freak series (designed my Mo Pinel of Mo-Rich) has such aggressive cores.
    Other from those two, Track is a "normal" ball making company.
