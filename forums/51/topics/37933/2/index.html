    Dry lanes...............hmm....what kind of dry lanes?

    1)28 feet light oil with clean backend?
    OR
    2)reverse block which doesn't really have any oil in the lane?
    OR
    3)After oiling and stripping the backend very dry kind of condition?

    Each of the above require a different type of game approach, so i would say there is no such thing as a ball or drilling that fits dry lanes, cos there are different types of dry lanes!

    All i could say is that the above 3 dry lanes have a similiar characteristics, ie u need to let the ball skid thru the heads. How much to skid depends on the oiling conditions. But if the ball starts to roll before it hits the mid lane, chances are u can start sparing pin 7(for right-handers) whilst playing outside line.

    Ehhhh be a semi spinner like me, then dryer lanes no problem. Then go home and cry on wetter lanes. Or learn to bowl like Ian.

    Anyway, I use a 4 x 4 layout for my dryer lanes on a pearlised reactive coverstock ball. This is a stacked layout with a further pin.

    The further pin distance from PAP allows the ball to take a longer time to roll, the pearlised cover assists in the skid as well. I chose a stacked layout for a more aggressive snap at the back (compared to an arc or roll layout). But keep the speed up on this drilling otherwise you'll visit Tom/CH's pocket more often than your own.

    Lastly, I chose to have the pin under the ring finger rather than above it, because I spin it more than most semi-rollers. So I get a little inherent skid compared to most of the others here. Except Jae and Hanz, of course.
