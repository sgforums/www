    It doesnt mean u have to walk a striaght line to the approach. the swing can also be adjusted towards the line u wanna play.
    eg. u wanna play 15 - 10. and ur placement is 5 boards and no drift. ur right foot should end on the 20 board. now, there are 2 ways to reach the 20 board from ur starting step.
    1) straight from 20 end at 20, armswing outwards towards the 10 board.
    2) or, walk a diagonal line towards the 20 board.

    it kinda depends on wat u are used to. i myself prefer walking straight down to the board i wan to reach.
    it doesnt really matter wat method u use as long as u reach ur target and deliver to the correct line.

    points to note.
    left hander, ur leftmost board is 1st board.
    right hander, ur right most board is 1st board. we assume as such.
    makes things clearer.

    hi player1,
    i'd like to share a few pointers with you. it might be useful but than again its my own view to this question that you.ve posted.

    1) firstly you have to take into consideration that you have no drift.
    meaning that you walk straight towards your target and do not
    drift. eg. (i stand on 20th board and finish at 20th board at the
    foul line)

    2) well if i remember correctly, it is 15ft from foul line to the arrow
    zone and it is also 15ft from foul line to the dots on the approach.
    so if you want to swing the ball out to 15th board (3rd arrow), for
    lefties, and finish at the foul line at centre (20th board), you are
    swinging outwards at a line of 5 boards, for every 15ft. which
    means you draw a line backwards and get 25th board. thats the
    line of the ball path.

    3) So now you know the line of your ball path, which comes to the
    next point, your shoulder span and standing position. if your ball
    has to travel or start at 25th board, than you have to stand say
    31th or 32th board as thats the span of your shoulder width.
    Also remember that you have to walk/face the direction of your
    alignment of your ball path.

    Try this out, it might help you. Remember you don't always have to finish square to the foul line. it be easier to finish with your shoulders perpendicular to the line of the target. Well it is posible to do elsewise but like i said this is the easiest way to start learning and you can move on to something more complicated when you have accustomed to this method.
