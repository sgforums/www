  • prataman
    8 Nov 02, 23:51
    Drilling a hook ball? That's a difficult question to answer.

    For the most part, a "hook" comes from the bowler's hand, and from the lanes. Any ball can hook on a really dry lane. Any ball will slide on a really really wet lane.

    A significant part of it also comes from what ball you are using. Drilling only accounts for a small small part of "hook" in the overall scheme.

    Personally, I feel that when a bowler learns to hook, a plastic ball is fine enough. After all, it's the technique that you're trying to master, and it really has nothing to do with the ball at an early stage.

    Just a point to note: It's not how much it hooks that's important, it's when it hooks.

    For a newbie, you really don't need to know all these technical terms. Your pro shop driller should be able to drill out a ball for you. You just have to let him know what you're trying to accomplish at this point. It'll be much better if you could actually bowl a couple of frames to show him your skill point so that he may better judge for you.

    I have to agree with Jae and Prataman. For beginners, do not bother yourselves with such deep concepts first. Instead, you'll be better served using all that energy and motivation to improve upon your individual technique on your preferred shots, be it hook, spin or something else.

    Many just are eager to get a new ball. Hey, i can understand that! Heck, even us "oldies" to the game are always itching to get new balls, eh guys??

    But far more important is to work on your technique. This can be done with a simple polyester/plastic ball or even a house ball. Rushing to get a ball may force you to settle into a style that the ball dictates, rather than one that you prefer. And once you pick up a bad habit, it is more difficult to get rid of than learning a good one.

    So for now, just enjoy the game with whatever you have, and leave the technical aspects until later (assuming that you're still playing then).
