    It is not legal to mark one's ball with anything during tourneys sanctioned by world tenpin bowling rules. Heavy ball bowlers too have to remove any sort of tapes they place on the outside of the ball to observe ball reaction.

    The reason markers are allowed in the ABT is because the ABT is not governed by the full set of rules. If they were, the taiwanese spinners would be disqualified for using scotchbrite pads to scuff their balls during matchplay.

    As for the full set of rules, I think Jae has a book. Or we can just search on the web.

    if u know your ball reactions well enuff, u will roughly know wat type of lanes u are dealing wif.

  • harmzuay
    30 Jan 03, 02:12
    I think everyone will agree that light ball spinners hold the advantage over heavy ball players in tight game series or matchplay format. In other words, in a straight shootout a spinner would have the advantage because a heavy ball player needs longer to find a line if there isn't one ready for them.

    Of course, this is dependent on many factors. But the general concensus is that spinners are very capable of high games, but even the best ones average about 220. Heavy ball bowlers play with a larger range of scores (i.e., lower low games and higher high games) but can average better over time, in the 240 region.
