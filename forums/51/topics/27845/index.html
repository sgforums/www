    Tifosi

    My apologies for the late, late reply. I was very busy a while back and thought that some other kind souls would provide you with the advice...

    To care for a cover usually means that you should have a regular cleaning regime. When you clean the ball is subjective to the conditions you have faced. The norm is, the more "worn" your cover looks - especially after playing on really dry conditions - the more it needs to be cleaned.

    Cleaners remove dirt from the microscopic pores in the cover so it regains as much 'grip' as possible. There are several types of cleaners on the market, and each ball company has their own range. But it doesn't matter which brand of cleaner you use - it's all down to preference and price.

    Another way of maintaining your ball is to send your ball to a proshop for "re-conditioning" once-in-a-while. Basically, this process restores your ball to its original condition, unless otherwise stated.
