    Let's analyse this. Spinners have advantage early in games bcos of their usually similar lines they play, and need only minor adjustment when they change lanes or when the conditions change. the shot they throw is straightforward and simple, no need for much calculations for breakpoints and such, whereas the hook ball bowler has his headaches from here. Hookball bowlers are different. they come usually in 3 tyoes. Crankers, strokers and forwardroll types. true crankers are power bowlers that if done properly, can give XXX even if the pocket is not hit well. given the power of the shot they throw, but the lane conditions will kill them fast if it is really very dry and the proper balls are not in hand. strokers and the forwardroll types are simplistic shots that give basically skid and flip. the shot looks nice but lacks the power of a cranker or sometimes, even the spinner if the lanes are not read right.
    All in all, wat u should play is really dependent on wat u can throw now. and how much committment u put into training.
