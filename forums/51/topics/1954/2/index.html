    yeah Deamon, I absolutely agree that Walter is a fantastic driller. In fact, i rate him as one of the best. I can't dream of getting my equipment fired up by anyone else.
    I just wanted to say that, it would be nice to adjust in slight moderations as well, say from 600 grit sanded to 800 grit. Walter adjusts the ball internally - drilling and core position, which does limit its effectiveness over a wide range of conditions. It would also help in gauging the box conditions of new equipment that comes out because i can compare them to what i have.

    I agree on the insignificance of top and side weights nowadays. These are all static weights and are only useful for numbers and tourney regulations. The dynamic weight of the ball is what matters, and that changes with each bowlers style. Unfortunately, it is not possible to measure dynamic weights because the ball is in motion. That's what makes Walter so good - he can accurately determine a balls dynamic properties while drilling.

    Wow! you're the first person I know to own the Tour Power. It's supposed to be a very good ball. I have some observations:

    Was your surface sanded? I think the box surface is quite aggressive, so it might not have been you.

    I agree when you say that you gave some room for a slower shotspeed after returning from injury and playing on synthetic lanes. However, you seem to have overlooked something:
    The fact that they carrydown quicker means that there'll be less head-oil. And a slow ballspeed means that you burn energy before your breakpoint, resulting in your pancake hits.

    From the description you gave, I assume you are using a leverage pattern, around 3 1/8" - 4 1/2"?? Does your layout look like this?

    o <-- Pin
    0 0 <-- Finger holes

    * <-- CG

    0 <-- Thumb hole

    Heehee. That's the best i can do to 'illustrate'. Anyway, if your ball is drilled to aid with leverage, then by breaking your wrist to achieve length, the ball is still burning up too quick. Not enough energy left to carry, even if the leverage assists in getting to the 1-3 pocket.

    Anyhow, don't give up on the ball!! I believe it's a good core/cover. Just need to tweak it for the conditions. And get well soon!!
