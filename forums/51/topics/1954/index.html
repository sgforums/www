    Hi fellas,

    Thought it might be fun to report on what your balls have been doing on the lanes. Be it new ones, old ones, whatever. I'm sure all us fellow bowlers are interested to know. I know I am. Alright. All that said and done, I'll start by introducing my personal "specs".

    Style: Hook bowler, the more the merrier
    Ball speed: Medium fast-ish (medium being Chuan and fast being Jae, Dinghua being rocket)
    Axis rotation: Close to maximum
    Revs: medium
    Master eye: Left, gotta aim 3.5 boards to the right of the board I wish to hit

    -------------------------------------------
    Went out to Changi last night. Friend was kind enough to lend me his last frame for a warm up shot.

    Whipped out my Adrenaline (15 lb 3 oz, label leverage with small axis hole for earlier roll, smacked with Ebonite Particle Polish) and on the lanes I went!

    I started by standing on about 22 and throwing to 14 at the arrows. Within 4 shots I realised the Adrenaline was too much for the lane I was playing. It had good head oil, but the lanes were dryer in the middle and carrydown was mild. The ball would and begin it's left charge too early.The edges were dry, so we had an "immunity to right gutter". Unless I threw a real deep inside line I knew I had to put some oomph on it to REACH the breakpoint to ensure at least a pocket hit. But hey, I want the ball to HOOK. I'm in it for the ego-boost. Stubbornness did not pay off. A few brooklyns and greek churches later, I looked up. 135. Save it for soupier midlanes, I felt.

    Time to bring out the Dynasty (Label leverage <-- surprise!! I drill that on practically every piece of mine). One fling of it, I knew it. It was almost the original Trimax in different colours! Only better! WIth head oil this baby could sail through LONG before making the hard left when the drys came into the view. However, this ball refused to read the dry in the midlane (unlike the black Matrix) but instead managed to hold till the backend. Had a bit of over/under tendencies with this one, though. Tried 2 frames playing a deep line, it would hold well and make the turn, but the hook's not continous and deflects. Found the line early, stood on 21 and aimed at 8 (ball crosses about 11-12) all the way out to about the 7 board, makes the turn and just smoothly eliminates the pins. Got in the groove and finished with 216.

    Ego took over. Out came the Adrenaline again. Now confident that I'd warmed up and game 1 was a fluke, I stood way out on 31 and aimed the ball to go over 17 at the arrows to break at 4-5. Boy it was beautiful. Tends to leave weird spares if hit light, but the half pocket hits that normally leave a 10 wasn't there.

    Finished the night with 135, 216, 182, 194.

    --------------------------------------------

    Dynasty: Carries excellent on high hits, deflects too much on light hits. Half-pocket hits leaves many pin 10s. Has a skid-hook-set motion. I believe this ball will NOT handle carrydown, but will negotiate through anything from light to light-medium in the heads to the midlanes. More forgiving than the original, but still overreacts a little in the dry.

    Adrenaline: Needs a small helping of head oil and at least med midlanes, but skids well for a low load particle/aggressive reactive combo. Powers up and arc-snaps impressively. This baby will handle anything between light medium to med heavy oils, with up to med carrydown. Best to hit light and half-pocket with this. Hitting slightly too high normally leaves you splits or a stone 9.

    You need a more forgiving ball? you seem good enough to deal with a small target area with consistency. Lots of people will vouch for that! Seriously, the power surge is the decendent of the origianl blue surge, which was itself a hook monster. Although both surges were reactive, the MICA loading in the coverstock made their reaction (and bite) closer to those of particles. In fact, the power surge was the ball before the black Chaos (essentially the Game + a Ti core). So, what you have is essentially two balls that are very similar in reaction to each other, in spite of the fact of the cover difference. Because of these cover differences, they will react differently on some surfaces, but for the most, they should be the same - differences will probably be due to drilling than ball.
    Sorry about the loooong post But i get carried away.
    Ok, i think you need a non-hook monster ball. Pearlised would be a good start for expanding your arsenal. Get a mid RG pearlised ball, and i'd recommend a length drilling. Label laverage if you need flare. I don't think you need it though - your track is already quite high. That should allow you to open the lanes up when it gets favourable or drier. Hope this helps.

    The messenger Ti is supposed to be a very good ball. But as usual, the usual answers surface: on any given lane condition/bowler style combination, anyone can have hold area. Conversely, the same ball in the hands of another or on a different lane might force a tight line.
    All being said and done, there are some balls out there that retain a large proportion of their 'advertised' capabilities over a wider range of conditions. Messenger Ti (solid/pearl); Super power (solid/pearl); Eraser, etcetera...
    Of course, this is only a guide. Bowler style plays a more than big part in keeping all those predictions accurate. Coverstock preparation factors too.
    And the Game is exactly the same as the black Chaos. The only difference is the name, price and the Ti core. The game was designed as a budget particle ball in the US. But as usual, by the time it gets here, the prices are the same... So the difference you feel must come from a different drill layout, or cover surface, or lane condition, or possibly a slight more control from the Game (because the Ti nugget slightly lowers the dynamic RG of the ball?) IMO.
