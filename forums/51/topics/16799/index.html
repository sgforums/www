    It's no harm wearing black or white. Just that teh sport isn't really well publicised with all the laymen out there thinking that bowlings a 'beng', or gangster sport; all bowlers guzzle beer in-between frames; or gamble (true for the older generation), et cetera... I think it's best for the sport that bowlers like us, fresh-faced and energetic, go out and right the image of bowling. It's as legitimate a sport as golf or billiards is. It's about time people started seeing bowlers as true athletes, and not people out to kill time and money chucking a ball at 10 pins.
    Just think golf: 3 years ago, it was an old man's game. Since Tiger Woods burst on the scene, parents now send their kids to golf lessons, no longer piano or ballet classes. The newspapers report teen-golfing sensations like they're going out of fashion... Mr. Woods showed the world that golf is power, elagance and poetry in motion. Now, if one spoke of liking golf, it is perceived as 'hip'.
    I think bowling needs a 'Tiger Woods'. And we might not be as good individually, but as a collective, we can help revamp the poor reputation of our beloved game. And don't have to be winning tourneys or representing the nation to do so (because not all of us here are that good). The idea is to show that bowling can be skill and form combined too; that we're not rich people with too much money to burn, but people passionate for the game. And every little bit helps.
    I know i've drifted a tad. But that's the idea. Perhaps something as simple as brighter, more varied colours at the alley would show the vibrant side of bowling. Afterall, most of us already debunk the myth about bowlers being all unfit and fat.
    IMHO.
