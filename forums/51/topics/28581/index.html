    Solitary

    First of all, Player1 got it half correct about the pin - it shows where the weightblock is. More specifically, it shows the top of the weightblock of a ball. You always see the cross-section of a ball in advertisement posters taht show the core of the ball. The pin is simply a mark on the surface of the ball to indicate that that is the top of the core, and hence how the core is positioned inside. This knowledge is important to drilling it properly.
    The pin is the dot that is differently coloured from the coverstock of the ball. It is usually around the manufacturer logo area and around the finger holes (to the right for righties and vice-versa).

    The CG is the centre of gravity. Well done.

    P1 was correct again about the top and side weights being just under the cover of the ball. These are imbalances of the left/right or top/bottom of the ball after positioning the core when drilling. For instance, if you had a see-saw with two persons on each end. One of them is heavier. The see-saw would now have "side-weight". That's a very, very simple view of it.
    If you want more detailed explanations, check the ball manufacturer websites like ebonite.com or columbia300.com. They have good technical sections.
