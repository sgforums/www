    Solitary

    Let's address your queries:

    Question: is bowling with 2 fingers a good reflection of what it'll be like with 3 fingers? cos i'm considering redrilling the ball to play hook.

    Bowling without a thumb is defenitely not a good indication of a proper thumb + 2 fingers grip. With the thumb inside the ball, you'll have more control and ballspeed. While its true that getting one's thumb stuck can cause problems, that's nothing that dedicated practice and proper instruction cannot overcome.

    Question: if i do change.. is a polyester ball suitable to learn?

    I believe Prataman has replied to a similar enquiry of yours in another thread. You can use a hard rubber ball and practice on the road outside your house - the important thing is that your grip is fitted well so that you can properly learn the technique.
    If you buy a ball that hooks, you may be able to look flashy for a while. But learn to hook it the correct way, and that'll remain with you for life.

    Question: will redrilling affect the ball in any way?

    I take it you mean ball reaction? Since you're now throwing a spin, the answer is a yes. Don't expect the two different drillings to be effectively interchangeable.

    finally.. i can't really visualise the thumb hole for a hook.. issit easy to get your thumb out? wun it get stuck?

    Getting the thumb stuck is something that even the best professional bowlers in the world have to contend with. Only practice can make one good at what they do.
    About visualising it... have you any friends who own hook balls? try holding their gear and feel it.
