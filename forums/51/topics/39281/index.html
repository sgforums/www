    I pose a second question:

    What do you fellas do at practice throw time? You know, the few minutes after the lights go on and before the league/tourney starts proper?

    ---------------

    As for me, I try to squeeze in at least 6 shots.

    1) Throw my favourite line to "check" overall condition. Which is something like foot (left inside for me) on 15, drift out to about 24 and aim at 14-15. (I say aim at because I usually shoot left off my target due to master eye issues)

    2) Second shot deep inside to gauge how opened the lane is. Hook early, olypmic skater... etc..

    3) Third shot in Jae territory to see how hooky the outsides are, or whether we have an OB. (out-of-bounds area)

    4) Pin 10. Feet on 35, aim at 20-21. (I don't drift as much when I'm standing far to the left)

    5) Compute all the info gotten from the first 4 shots, and test out the "educated-guessed" strike line. In the mean time, I should already try to figure out any MAJOR differences in the 2 lanes.

    6) Make any minor adjustments if any and try No.5 again, if possible on the other lane.

    And if time permits, go for at least another pin 10 try on the other lane.

    Asiaspin

    The book means that you look at a point on the lane throughout the approach and release. Many books use the arrows as references simply because they are there and most people find them easy to look at. You could be the minority.

    As long as you keep your head steady during the approach, you'll be fine. A head that keeps moving is detrimental to aim.



    During practice throws,

    my 1st ball is down the 10th board *try to*. Ideally it'll be 10 - 10 or further outside.

    my 2nd ball will be the next arrow, hence 15 - 15.

    if i have the time, the 3rd ball will go about 7 - 7.

    All three of the above shots are executed with lower speed and rotation than usual with a premium on keeping the ball in play so the ball reaction can be read.

    Then i repeat on the next lane.

    If i'm pressed for time, just the first shot will do. Or if there's another bowler who throws any of the above shots, then I won't have to throw any at all because i'll read off theirs.

    I adopt a 4 shot routine

    1st shot, try to clear the thumb. usually i dun do the full 5 step bowling. Imagine yr muscles are still "cold" and then suddenly take up a 15lbs ball and try to hook the ball. Imagine how much strain on yr shoulders? Eeven if u have warmed up alot, but the transisition frm a hand carrying no ball to one that is preparing to throw a 15lbs ball is simply too much. That's why i never attempt to throw the 1st shot. By throwing a lighter shot and clearing my thumb, i allow my muscles to stretch a bit, but not too much.

    2nd shot, pin 10
    Standard procedure, stand left and throw. if left to pin 10, means area ard 1st arrow backend is dry. if right of pin 10, gutter oily. I won't throw a strike shot in my 2nd shot as muscles is still a little tense

    3rd shot, pin 7
    SOP as above

    4th shot
    Strike shot. usually 2nd arrow as benchmark

    I do all 4 shots on the same lane. If i got both pin7 and 10, there is no need to repeat that on the other lane. So the next 4 shots in the next lane is on strikes. 1st on 2nd arrow, 2shot on outside line of 3 boards, 3rd shot is anyway bet 3rd and 4th arrow and last shot deep left, playing 5th arrow.

    The rest are all calulated guess. So when the game starts, the 1st shot is always my calculted guess of my strike shot. Can strke good. cannot strike, well at least i alrdy know how to spare on this lane. then move again. This time any adjusements are all minor of shifting boards to get the strike shots, until that area can no longer be playable
