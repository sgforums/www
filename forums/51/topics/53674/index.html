    Here's some info for raindrpo. It is not advisable to polish or sand your ball too often.This not only spoils the coverstock, it also causes the abll to shrink and warp. Trust me, as crazy as it sound,...its true.

    Don't worry too much about the scratches. What you do wanna do is remove the lane dirt. Use water and a clean cloth to remove the dirt. If the dirt is really stubborn, and won't come off, use 2 parts water and 2 drops of lighter fluid...or ball cleaning solution. Make sure the ball-cleaning solution is non-abrasive.

    Lastly, if you REALLY cannot tahan the dirt, than put the ball on a ball spinner and use a cloth and water to get rid of the dirt. Moreoever, you could use polish or ball cleaner, depending on the condition of the ball.
    The recommended frequency for polishing your ball is once every 30+ games or so. Seriously........

    nail polish remover dulls the surface slightly if left too long on the surface. best is to use the car polishing cloth to apply, then an absorbant cloth to wipe it off fast.

    another thing, to make the ball 'like new', the correct term is reconditioning. but a ball's life span is like 3 recons and thats it, have to look for another ball after that. what recon does is to take away the more minor scratches, therefore thinning the coverstock.

    and, if an alley gives u many scratches after bowling, probably that place isnt good to bowl. the lane surface or gutter or ball return may be causing those scratches. minor nicks and scratches are probably ok once in a while, but if you see it happening everytime u bowl at a particular alley, its time to switch places. otherwise, u're gonna end up with a really scarred ball.
