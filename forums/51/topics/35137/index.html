    Ladies, Forumites, Bowlers and Gentlemen in no particular order,

    The final details of the Forum Challenge have been settled and are all here for your perusal!

    Date: Saturday, February 15th 2003
    Venue: Jackies Bowl (542-B East Coast Road)
    Time: 6:45 check in, 7pm practice throw, 7:05 roll off

    *****PLEASE DO NOT BE LATE!!!!!!!!*****

    Cost: $27 per participant, to be paid when checking in
    Format: 5 qualifying games, top 5 to proceed to 3 round stepladder

    Bowlers participating:

    Eric Tan, Ng Ting Hwa, Sin Ker Han, Saffry Hoo, Regina Ong, Vernon Lee, David Goh, Tom Lim, Ian Wong, Ong Chong Han, Jay Soong, Jordan Loh, Tan Liang Cheng, Alexsius Pek, Lim Boon Kiat, Ong Chong Siang, Johan Shah, Jae Tay, Liong Chuan, Hayden Tan, Kazan Looi, Yeo Wee Siong, Adrian Yaw, Victor Loh, Ian Tan, Kevin Ng, Yvonne Chee, Andy Chin.

    Some points to note:

    General Info: We have 28 participants, over 12 lanes. To save time, the lanes have already been drawn for you, courtesy of the Jackies Bowl manager. We have been allocated lanes 1 - 12. Good luck to the corner lane people. If you wish to know your lane assignments, please let me know. Otherwise you can surprise yourself on the day itself.

    Lane Change: There will be just 1 lane change after 3 games. Players on odd numbered lanes will move 4 lanes to your left, and players on even numbered lanes will move 4 lanes to your right. So if you're on lane 7, you move to lane 3 after 3 games. If you're on lane 10, you move to lane 2 after 3 games.

    Playoff Format: The top 5 players will advance to a 3 round stepladder. In the first round, 3rd 4th and 5th placed qualifiers will have a 1 game shootout. The winner moves on to play the 2nd placed qualifier. The winner of the 2nd match plays the 1st placed qualifier to determine the tournament champion.

    Handicap: Ladies are given a 10 pin handicap per game. Please note, this handicap will count towards the high game award.

    Prizes: There will be a total of 11 cash prizes, for the top 5 qualifiers as well as for the 6th to 10th placed qualifiers, plus a high game award. The prizes for the top 5 will be based on their performance in the stepladders, whereas the rest will be based on the 5 qualifying games. High game award includes games played in the stepladder. Thanks to Deamon and his "goodie bags", I believe no one will have to go home empty handed.

    Tie: Should there be a tie game in the stepladder, the player's performance over the last 2 frames will be used to determine the winner. For example, player A hit 9/ and XXX for his last 2 frames, and player B hits X XXX for his last 2. Thus player B will "win" the tie breaker due to a better last 2 frames. Should there still be a tie, players will roll a 3 frame (8, 9 and 10) tiebreaker.

    Dinner: It's best to eat before you come. Or come early about 5:45pm or 6pm to eat (and digest) at the alley. Food's quite good. We start at 7pm, and will not finish anytime before 9:30pm, 10pm-ish. PLEASE try not to order food and eat during the games, unless you are really on the verge of fainting. It can be very distracting to your fellow lane mates.

    Having said all that, I hope I didn't leave out anything important. Otherwise, see you all at Jackies Bowl on Saturday and let's go have a helluva time. By the way, parking is free. And bring your cameras.

    Please post here or SMS me (96947062) should you be unclear about anything, or have any comments. Jordan, Deamon and myself will do our best to help.

    It would be great and most helpful if the participants (from this forum at least) reply to acknowledge that you understand the above details.
