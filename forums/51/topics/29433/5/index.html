    28 Dec 02, 11:42
    Player1,

    That equation will only work if you're playing against a scratch opponent, ie 0 handicap. Which isn't really likely, unless we're playing against 4 scratch bowlers, and/or we field 4 max handicap bowlers. Which is even unlikelier.

    The oiling is actually not heavy on the outside. In fact, it's a rather clean backend and thin outsides on league nights. You may be referring to your ball's late breakpoint, or failure to turn enough to the pocket.

    2 things:

    1) You favour an inside line, more often than not. You make your ball travel through the oil in the middle part of the lane first, so this extra sliding in the oil will cause your ball to sometimes skid past or go outside of the optimum break point.

    2) A Shock Zone was built for length and snap. Skid is already imbued into the coverstock, you certainly didn't need to help it by moving so deep so quickly.

    Perhaps it will be better for you to start outside and shoot a straighter line almost parallel to the gutter, and move in as necessary. Play what the lane gives you, rather than trying to overpower it.
