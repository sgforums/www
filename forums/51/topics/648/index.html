    Fighter
    If you're looking to work on a straight game first, then the bundle (shoes + bag + ball) packages of the various Proshops should be sufficient to get you off the ground. Most of them won't charge more than $230. Might be even less nowadays, i haven't been keeping an eye on prices for quite a while. :p

    Regarding the terms reactive and extra-length. i believe you're refering to Brunswick bowling's website - they are the only one's to use the x-tra length term. Anyway, they are not of any concern to your straight game.

    Reactive means the cover is more aggressive and 'bites' the lane surface more than a regular plastic ball. This translates into more friction and more hook. Extra-length is a non-universal term that refers to a ball that is designed to hook later (closer to the pindeck) than other balls. It's very subjective, so it's not used to evaluate the balls of seperate companies.

    Hope that helps.

    200-300 games?!? I think it's wiser to resurface more regularly. Even for a noodlearm, that's too many games - the oil would have set in the cover waaay before any process can help. And a ball is good for more than 2 - 3 'recons'.

    I have my gear recon-ed about once every 6 months. i play about 150 games?? if i use it often, then 4 months. Anyhow, your driller should be able to properly inform you of how many more recons one's ball can take. Walter tells me, for instance, that my Rage can take only about 2 more before it becomes illegal (too small) in diameter. Obviously, some balls DO react at different rates to reconditioning, but i think the more you put it off, the more has to be 'done' during the recon... more eventual damage. An analogy can be drawn with visiting the dentist.
