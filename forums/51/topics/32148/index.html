  • prataman
    6 Jan 03, 12:33
    Easy? I think it's a matter of perspective. And it also depends, I believe on what you wish to achieve in the sport of bowling.

    I admit bowling is easy in a way that people of all shapes and sizes can just pick up a ball and fling it down the lane. And score "well", too.

    But, let's put it another way. If my objective was just to keep a ball "in play", that is, hit the pocket safely each time, I'd be using a houseball rather than the reactives. I'd leave pins 5 and 8 all the time, and assuming I spare 8/ all the way, I could average 180+ with a houseball. But I believe I sure can't get to 200 avg with that ball. Even our best straight ball exponent forumite Deamon would be the first to tell you that if all he wanted was to hit the pocket all the time, he'd rather use a houseball or at most a urethane.

    Therefore I switch to reactives and aim for a bigger angle of entry, etc. The risk increases and if I dun crush the pocket, I leave more spectacular leaves than with a houseball. So now, on a good day I can avg up to 190+ and on a bad one go to 150+.

    But to do well in league and average 230+ like many of these people claim? Most of us would find it impossible. I for one don't find bowling "easy" to average a score which I aim for. But if wanted to score 170 avg ONLY as my bowling objective, sure it's damn easy.

    But the ballreviews people are mostly made up of very high average veterans, and loads of braggarts. (I shot 800 of of the box. Yeah, like that's a review, go tell someone who cares)

    For every person that average 200 and above, there are definitely many others who can't, and do not post in ballreviews.
