    That would work.

    Placing the index finger next to the middle finger reduces the tendency to turn the ball. Thus you stay behind it more.

    Another thing to do is to play around with the pinky position. Spreading the pinky (putting it far away from the ring finger) also helps you generate a tendency to stay behind the ball. Trick is to make sure you feel the pinky come through the ball as well as you deliver the ball. This guarantees a much straighter shot. Almost to a backup.

    I use that technique for my first ball when the heads and midlane are really dry.
