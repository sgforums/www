    Actually, there are a lot of people that don't understand the word "snap" in bowling parlance. There are many types of hook, each with it's distinct shape, be it a curve, arc, hard arc, et cetera.

    I often hear many people say, "... drill my ball to skid/snap...".
    It makes me want to cringe.

    Here's the truth, styles and hooks like Ian's are hard arcs. I think the only person in Singapore who can come remotely close to snapping a bowling ball is Shaun Ng. There is a very angled shape at the break point, not even a hard or sharp arc.

    That's just F.Y.I.

    Quantum balls were manufactured by Brunswick. So you may want to try their other offerings under different names such as the recent Fuze of Monster lines.

    Just keep in mind what Nick said: impact, revolutions and hook are only dynamic elements of the game that involve the proper matching of bowler style and equipment to the proper lane conditions. Under some conditions, even a houseball can provide all the hook, impact and revolutions you require.

    Shop for a ball with which you can do what you intend, not what the ball is advertised to do for you.

    Skid snap is a term I still hear James, my pro shop driller use. The lao-jiao bowlers will still prefer the old terms like finger weight... etc.. Skid snap is a drilling which maximises finger weight for skid and side weight for snap.

    Modern bowling drilling concepts focus more to the pin and mass bias (for asymmetrics) placements for ball reactions, rather than the old fashioned "side weight, thumb weight, finger weight" concepts.

    Coverstock surface is also the greatest influence on how fast your ball grips the lane, hence modifying the distribution of its energy release. For example, a pearl ball tends to skid through the front part of the lane and unleash it's energy in the backend. This may result in a "flip" motion.

    As what Ian said, Quantums are designed by Brunswick Technology Ventures (BTV), and I believe their last offering was the Denim Quantum, which PT currently has.

    I don't regard Remy Ong as a "snap" type of bowler. The way he releases the ball does not make it possible. He's a strong arc bowler.

    To make a ball skid and snap is to create a hook shape the americans term the "hockey stick" shape. For those who have seen any hockey sticks before, this is a very strong imagery. A strong arc may look awesome to asians, and because of that many of us who see a very aggressive hook simply call it a skid/snap shot.

    The fact is, the best of us are only strong arc bowlers. Shaun Ng doesn't get on television anymore. But i'm sure some of you know who zulmazran zulkifli is. On his form, he is what i regard as a strong arc bowler. Not a skid/snap.

    Of course, lane conditions allow for certain shots to be played. But personally, i believe the bowler is still the largest input into the shape of the subsequent shot. For instance, Robert Smith plays skid/snap whether or not he plays the first three boards or lofts over the left gutter. He is the exception, not the rule.

    Just to let you guys in on something:

    THE SKID-SNAP REACTION:- What is done here is that the pin is above or below the ring finger hole in most instances. Hence the entire span lies with the CG almost in the centre of the span. This creates finger weight OR the top weight will lie in the top half of your palm. This causes the ball to maintain its initial axis of roll longer("Fowrad-Roll") before the weight block flips hard to give the entire ball a "skid-Snap" reaction. It is the same as the label drilling which gives the ball the longest length, but strongest backend. NOTE THAT DRILLERS WOULD NOT NEED YOUR Positive.Axis.Point. or P.A.P. FOR THIS LABEL DRILLING MOST OF THE TIME. ----Personally my favourite drilling.

    Quantum balls were the technological heights of ball design 6years back. their main asset was their core which allowed a better pin action and rev even on inept bowlers.

    However, that was 6 years ago. since its a brunswick ball, (and i only use brunswick) i can only discuss along these lines, The 'new' series of Zone is out, namely the dangerzone 3,4 RedAlert series etc, these either come in the new surface of carbon something i cant remember what, and partical surface. these surefaces will allow a bowler to hook like never b4, but, only for the mid to low end rev bowlers. higher rev bowlers will find that unless the oil is mercifully 'thick', their ball will roll out at the pins.

    The fuZe line by brunswick, supposedly to take over the original Zone line, seems very popular, using symmertrical cores to distribute even hitting power. eg are the raging red, purple, igniter. they primarily used the same core (upgraded version they claimed) slapped a new name, but used the overall concept of their predecessors to market the product.

    the reason why quantum was so popular last time was becoz no other company had that level of technology to compare wif brunswick at that point in time. now, most of the major manufacturers have already caught up and most have their 'specialities' to promote their balls.

    brunswick = powerkoil, 1st company to use partical technology.
