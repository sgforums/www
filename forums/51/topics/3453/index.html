    Seriously, Jae.... if you really want to combat SKID, it's better to get urethane Balls or particles rather than reactives. Reactive balls skid more than anything in oil, just that they "react" more violently in the dry.

    Particles and even urethane have a higher friction in oil which will cause it to skid less. However, don't hope for a booming backend with urethanes, 'cause in this case they will have less friction than reactives in the dry. Err... particles are a different story, they simply grip harder anywhere, anytime.
