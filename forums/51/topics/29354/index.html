    Well, it depends why one would not want to represent the country.

    Many reasons may include: dislike of coaches, not wanting to play under the pressure of 4 million people (like the ladies table-tennis squad, who I think are doing very well to cope with all the mud slung their way)...

    Given the chance I'm sure most of us would, but for niggly little issues that are present. I, for instance, put a lot of weight into the coach factor.

    Back to the topic...

    I once asked myself that same question:
    "Why continue to train so religiously when I can no longer represent any institution?" - I had left JC (was in the school team) by then.

    But inside I knew i always had the answer. To this day I still play for only one reason: I love the sport.

    It's similar to my love for soccer, but yet not quite the same. Bowling isn't a team game - I can play as and when I like. I don't have to bowl with others or bowl well to enjoy the game. Simply the act of bowlng brings joy. It's a feeling of freedom and release (pardon the pun). Like once I stand on the approach, the entire world has shrunk down to just what i'm standing on and the 60 ft in front of me.
    That's why I often play just to escape the accelerated pace of life in our little island-state.
    I play to display my emotions during good times and I play to relieve the pain in bad times. It takes my mind away from other things for that little while that I'm concentrating on playing through 10 frames.

    I play for myself.

    I don't think passion has anything to do with winning. An individual can possess a passion for something they may not even participate in (e.g., handicapped people can be passionate about rugby, which they cannot play, et cetera).

    One can pursue personal goals in their chosen way. For you, that goal may be "playing to win". Others may be "playing to relieve stress" or "playing to meet friends"... One's goals will influence the way that person behaves and perceives all things that happen while in pursuit of that goal.

    Just like what P1 said - passion is not possessed by everyone, nor is every passionate person capable of winning. But when the two coincide, it simply means that the victory is so much sweeter.

    P.S. There are many reasons to like a sport. Some people I know only bowl because they are good at it. They don't play or give up the moment they cannot do well anymore. Others loved the sport from the time they picked up their first houseball - getting better only meant that they are able to enjoy what they love more.

    It depends on what drives you.
