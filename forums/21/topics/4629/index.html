    What is the Singaporean entertainment scene?

    Could it be the Youth Park every Saturday with it's cool gigs? How about the Boat Quay and Clarke Quay area with it's array of pubs and discos? Maybe the Orchard Road area as an avenue to drink, play and just kick back?

    Problem is, there is no definite area that is the playground for the Singaporean. There are so many different races and age groups to cater to that the need for specially manicured entertainment scenes for each are created. So which one represents the Singaporean entertainment scene then?

    But firstly, let me explain why I'm raising this point, trivial as it may seem. My friends and I are about to do aa 15 minute documentary on Singapore in a "Lonely Planet" kind of format and with just 15 mins to present our lovely country on tape it's quite a nightmare. We can't decide whether to film the nightlife at Boat Quay or hang out at Orchard Road on a Friday night.

    So perhaps you pertinent, more experienced party-goers could help me out a little and tell me, from a Singaporean point of view, what is the Singaporean entertainment scene? Or could we be so pathetically culturally impaired that we don't have a meaningful arts and entertainment scene at all?
