    COE prices fall for most categories

    By Melissa Aw | Fit to Post Autos – 2 hours 35 minutes ago




    Certificate of Entitlement (COE) prices have dropped for four out of five categories in the most recent bidding exercise which concluded on Thursday.

    Premiums for Category A (1,600cc and below) saw the largest decrease, as prices dropped from $73,501 to $66,809 — a difference of nine per cent from two weeks ago.

    For Category B (Cars 1,601cc and above), the COE premium is now at $88,002, down seven per cent from $94,502.

    Prices for the open category (Category E) saw the COE premium fall from $95,034 to $93,990.

    Category D (Motorcyles) also saw a tumble in prices, from $2,081 to $2,013.

    Meanwhile, only Category C (Goods vehicles and buses) saw their prices increase marginally by four per cent, from $57,001 to the current $59,334.

    Title : COE prices mostly down
    By :
    Date : 05 September 2012 1630 hrs (SST)
    URL : http://www.channelnewsasia.com/stories/singaporelocalnews/view/1224141/1/.html

    SINGAPORE: The Certificate of Entitlement (COE) premiums for vehicles were mostly lower at the end of the latest bidding exercise on Wednesday. 

    The COE price for big cars (1,601cc and above) closed down S$7,811 at S$80,191, compared with S$88,002 in the previous tender. 

    In the Open category, the premium ended at S$87,999, down S$5,991. 

    The COE price for commercial vehicles fell S$2,000 to S$57,334. 

    However for Category A (cars 1,600cc and below), the premium closed S$1,111 higher at S$68,000, compared with S$66,889 in the previous bidding exercise. 

    Meanwhile, motorcycle COE dropped S$212 to S$1,801.

    COE premiums end mostly lower
    by Woo Sian Boon
    Updated 04:32 PM Oct 03, 2012
    SINGAPORE - Certificate of Entitlement (COE) premiums for cars ended mostly lower at the end of the latest bidding exercise, which closed at 4pm today.

    The small cars category was the lone exception, witnessing a 9.5 per cent jump as the COE premium closed at S$69,000, compared with S$63,000 in the previous bidding exercise.

    The COE premium for big cars (1,601cc and above) fell by 9.6 per cent from S$88,500 to S$80,001. 

    In the Open category, where COEs can be used for any vehicle type but end up mainly for cars, the premium fell by 3.5 per cent to S$86,889. 

    The COE premium for commercial vehicles dropped 3.1 per cent to S$56,001, while the premium for two-wheelers increased by 3.2 per cent to S$1,912.
