    Cabby whacked because he didn't know directions

    Hedy Khoo | The New Paper | Wednesday, Apr 3, 2013

    SINGAPORE - At first, he was verbally abused.

    Then the passenger punched Mr Michael Tan's back while he was driving his Comfort taxi.

    When the 56-year-old cabby refused to continue driving and stopped on the Ayer Rajah Expressway (AYE) near Normanton Park around 1am on Sunday, the burly passenger rained blows on him before throwing him into a roadside bush.

    The relentless attack stopped only when passing motorists sounded their horns.

    It is the latest in a series of verbal and physical attacks on taxi drivers here in recent years.

    In this case, Mr Tan was not sure of the drop-off point and had irritated the passenger by asking for his postal code so that he could key in the destination on his Global Positioning System.

    Mr Tan ended up with lacerations on his face and back, scraped knees, and blood on his shirt.

    He has since made a police report and the incident is under investigation.

    Mr Tan said he picked up the passenger - a man who appeared to be in his 40s - on North Bridge Road, at the junction of Coleman Street.

    The passenger wanted to go to Chwee Chian View, near South Buona Vista Road.

    Mr Tan recounted: "I wasn't familiar with the location and asked him to give me his postal code so that I could use my GPS. He said he wasn't a 15-year-old boy and that he wasn't drunk so he could direct me there."
