    The HTC one X .. 

    Well, iirc it's running on a quad core snapdragon s4 processor - one of the first quad core android phones. (The Galaxy S2 in comparison is dual core)

    While I don't have the One x, I'm currently using it's sister that's available in Singapore - the Htc One V. 

    Pardon the fonts will post what I wrote in a post on G+

    It has a single core processor (from the processor data sheet it is most probably underclocked from 1.2ghz to 1ghz). A very nice aluminium unibody industrial feel to it too - feels like a brick

    What I do like about this phone is its 5mp camera.. Very quick app start up with no delays what so ever like the initializing messages (1 sec) - Thanks to htc image sense. Htc image sense is avaiable on all HTC one devices and is the software and an independent graphics chip that processes the images and colours of the photographs

    Very quick snapping of photos (no shuttle delay). It suits me quite well that I can just whip up my phone,press the camera app and quickly snap an image the first time - Knowing so well that there are no blurred images or images that are not taken properly due to me quickly whipping it back into my pocket before the shuttle actually snaps a pic - all thanks to it's F2.0 aperture and 28mm lens.


    I dare say the camera is best in it's class. (5mp shooters) - definitely great for microbloggers


    Now I will not be biased to say it's the perfect phone.

    It isn't. What in the hell were HTC thinking about having an inbuilt battery? - and the battery management software switches the phone off at 10% (Yea I do understand - switching off the device at a certain state of charge to prevent over discharging of the battery) - the problem could be solved best if it could first switch off wifi, then dim the screen to its lowest before giving a warning to turn off the phone. 

    I was pleasantly surprised that I am still able to switch it on after it switches off automatically and it's battery life from 10% to 0% lasted me 2 additional hours with constant texting.
