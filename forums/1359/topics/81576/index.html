    SINGAPORE: If you think it's impossible to type a 160-word SMS message in under a minute Â— minus predictive text and spelling aids Â— the gauntlet has been cast.

    It took Singaporean teenager Joshua Tay just 58 seconds to smash the world record for typing "The razor-toothed piranhas of the genera Serrasalmus and Pygocentrus are the most ferocious freshwater fish in the world. In reality they seldom attack a human".

    As the son of a SingTel employee, however, the 15-year-old     will not be eligible for the grand prize of the SingTel SMS Shootout on June 27.

    A total cash prize of $17,500 plus a mobile phone awaits the island's fastest fingers at the grand finals at Ngee Ann City Civic Plaza.

    Shootout hopefuls have until June 9 to register at www.singtel.com/shootout. Time trials will take place on June 12 at the SingTel Comcentre Theatre and outside Orchard Cineleisure on June 19 and 20.

    The official world record for typing the 160-character message is 67 seconds, set by Briton Mr James Trusler last September.

    Said Joshua, who clocked his feat at SingTel's trials for its own internal competition for staff and their families: "I was a little disappointed when I broke the record because I knew I could type faster than that by three to four seconds." Â— TODAY
