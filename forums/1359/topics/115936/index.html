    SINGAPORE : Japan's widely-popular i-mode mobile Internet service is coming to Singapore.

    That has been made possible by an exclusive tie-up between StarHub and Japan's NTT DoCoMo.

    StarHub's i-mode services will been rolled out in the fourth quarter - with NTT DoCoMo providing expertise in marketing and technology.

    StarHub also says it is on track to launch commercial 3G services in the first half of the year.

    43 million people in Japan use i-mode.

    And the reason is simple.

    You don't need to be tech-savvy; i-mode makes it easy for mobile users to make restaurant reservations, buy an air ticket, trade stocks, or do online banking.

    Terry Clontz, CEO, StarHub, said, "The experience in accessing data while on the move will be very consistent, very predictable. And of course, a broad range of different types of contents, that they will have available to them on the i-mode platform.

    "Our 3G launch is still planned for the first half of this year. We are still very much on track to do that. Once we introduce i-mode, 3G will allow us to offer i-mode services, at much greater speed."

    StarHub hopes i-mode in Singapore will help spur the development of local content providers here, given a pool of 170 million potential users worldwide.

    Mr Clontz said, "The ease of writing to the i-mode platform will attract a lot of content providers. And the good news is that those same content providers will be able to access the full i-mode customer base throughout the world. So there will be many incentives for them to write applications for the i-mode platform."

    Users of i-mode will need to use handsets designed for the service.

    StarHub's pricing details will be announced later.

    On a separate note, Mr Clontz said, "We believe that in 2006, it will begin to improve ARPU (average revenue per user) and enhance customer loyalty. So, if you look out, say 2 to 3 years, we will expect our data ARPU to be 25 percent of total ARPU."

    That is up 10 percentage points from the current 15 percent. - CNA
