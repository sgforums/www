    PETALING JAYA - A factory worker went through a night of terror when she was abducted and raped by two men - one of whom she had befriended a week earlier - and then gang-raped by six others during an eight-hour ordeal.

    The 35-year-old woman, who works in an electronics factory in Shah Alam, said she had been abducted from outside her hostel on Wednesday evening.

    Advertisement

    Two men - one of them known to her - had forced her into a car and taken her to a house in Puchong where they raped her. She was then taken to another place in Klang where a group of at least six men gang-raped her.

    Sources said the woman was believed to have been abandoned by the roadside in Klang.

    On Thursday, the woman lodged a report with police.

    Wednesday's gang-rape is the second in a week in the district. On Monday, a 14-year-old girl was gang-raped by six youths aged between 19 and 25 in Kota Damansara in the suburbs of the federal capital.

    Police later said they had arrested seven men in connection with the incident. -- The Star/Asia News Network
