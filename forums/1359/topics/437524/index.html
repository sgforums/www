    I am a M1 subscriber for their mobile phone service.

    And i am downright disappointed with the service that the telco provides man.

    Just earlier today, I've found some suspicious amounts on outgoing call charges and decided to make a call to the M1 customer helpline to get an idea of exactly what am i billed for.

     

    Guess what they told me? The CSO on the other side of the line said he wont be able to provide a breakdown of my bill unless i pay 5 bucks to request an itemised bill be printed out. And he is not able to look from his side into the system on the breakdown of my call charges.

     

    What the Hell ? The other telcos provide the breakdown of the number of calls and call durations on their bills. Why arent they charging extra for that? What makes M1 so special to be the only telco not to print the call duration on the bill and have to charge extra if the customer wants to see it?

     

    So if i am suspicious about my call charges, how does it makes sense for me to pay yet MORE to get another itemised bill just so i'm able to clarify on those charges?

     

    And if i paid to get this itemised bill, after looking at the breakdown, if i find that i have a dispute over the call charges , who will i have to call and speak with to clarify those charges?

     

    Bingo, the CSO on the M1 customer helpline !!

     

    This is where they really contradict their own asses man, if they cant access my calls breakdown, how in the blue hell are they able to verify charges with me if i have a dispute over it ?

     

    And if i need to pay more everytime, only then i can see my calls breakdown, it simply defeats the purpose of calling the customer helpline altogether, aint it?

     

    After much insistence, the CSO somehow micraculously was able to give me the call duration and charges of some of the longest calls i've made. Funny, didnt he say earlier he wasnt able to access my call details? And magically he can now all of a sudden? I am really getting suspicious of the type of protocols M1 practices. So they are not unable to access your call details, rather, they are unwilling to disclose it, unless you pay the designated amount to print the itemised bill for it. I cant think of another better term than the word 'cheapskate' to describe them.

    WHAT ??!! Being charged for an 1800 number ? Aint 1800 numbers supposed to be toll free? And M1 got the cheek to charge for that? Thats dah first I've ever heard man !!

     

    The CSO said that different telcos practice different policies and it is in M1's protocols to charge for 1800 numbers. Is he fucking sure of what hes saying? Because if somehow i found out that aint true, i'm gonna gather a bunch of M1 users with me to freaking sue for deceit.

     

    The CSO mentioned that basically only their customer care hotline was toll free and M1 customers will have to pay anything else they call.

     

    Now, doesnt that make M1 just a shameless money grubber? If thats the way they do business, what is there to procure customer loyalty to ensure the customers will stay and recontract with them?
