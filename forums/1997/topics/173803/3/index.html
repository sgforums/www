    The Homeward Bound Misery Wind

    When the exhaust has cooled,
    and the lights have dimmed.
    What do I do?
    Do I stand in silence?
    Looking back to the days
    where the night was a quilt
    and our voices
    were
    one
    patch
    out of many;
    radiating throughout the world
    from my tiny island
    to that old-town
    where you live.

    But after the DJ
    slows and stops
    and the wine runneth
    parched...
    I still find myself
    lying back
    in my bed
    and the only music
    is played by
    The Carrier Man..
    It's not a quilt,
    only a simple shawl..

    And pigs can fly

    There are some things in life we are certain about
    Stuff that we will never doubt
    Laws that the universe spin by
    Rules such as pigs can never fly
    But why is it that day
    I saw a pig flying by the highway?

    I thought I was sure of your love
    The sky is always blue up above
    No matter what the night will go away
    The sun will return for another day
    But why is it that day
    The darkness never went away?

    You said you loved me you said you cared
    But when it came to the test you never dared
    You knew you could had made me the happiest I'd ever been
    You knew my love was such a foolish thing
    You knew everything and more to say
    But still you went ahead and broke my heart anyway

    I'm such a fool I know
    But I really thought things went with the flow
    I thought that pigs could not fly
    I guess I was a fool over that guy
    I guess things went badly that day
    When pigs flew by the highway

    Oh, love of woman, you are known to be
    A passion sent to plague the hearts of men;
    For every one you bring felicity
    Bringing rebuffs and wretchedness to ten.
    I have been oft where human life sold cheap
    And seen men's brains spilled out about their ears
    And yet that never cost me any sleep;
    I lived untroubled and I shed no tears.
    Fools prate how war is an atrocious thing;
    I always knew that nothing it implied
    Equalled the agony of suffering
    Of him who loves and loves unsatisfied.
    War is a refuge to a heart like this;
    Love only tells it what true torture is.
