    Sad Life 04 May 1996

    i live a forbid life
    full of apprehension and lies
    pain, agony and morbid fear
    but who is willing to lend a listening ear ?

    i watch the world unfolds
    revealing friends and foes
    it echoes with hail and kill
    sadistic savagery make me ill

    i see through the masks people wear
    here, there and everywhere
    they treat you with false sincerity
    action so repugnant and devoid of humanity

    i witness the atrocities people commit
    slaughter, slay, murder, rape and other unspeakable things only
    an insane mind can permit
    bathe in blood and feed upon the dead
    a devilish and macabre mentality filled with hate

    i see the way life is ran
    my heart bled, ached and wrenched
    a crazy pattern stretching to the never
    neverending journey to forever

    i shout into space for an explanation
    the day Earth is born after an explosion
    unfortunately, all i receive is emptiness
    destroying any hope of finding a solution to this madness

    i just had to come to grips to the facts
    join the majority fad
    dreams and ideals are squashed
    perhaps to be relived slowly in later years with a sip from Orange Squash ?

    SUSHI FOR TWO

    I want to be the seaweed that rolls you up.
    Will you wrap yourself round my clumsy body?

    Can you stand those bright sea urchin eggs on
    me?
    Loving you I have to love them too-octopus,
    cucumber, crab fillet and all.

    Countless rice rolls of the past return to haunt us.
    Plain tea or sake? Feels like the choice before a
    thousand crossroads.

    Reaching for you where you are soft and chewy I
    hit the hidden spikes.
    Claws of the soft-shelled crab-like spider legs-
    playing for love?

    Shedding layers of clothes you stop as if
    shuddering.
    Nearing the coiled core is like touching some pain
    deeply buried.

    With no idea of how I taste my rawness drives you
    away.
    Your natural pungency, hot and mustardy, hurts
    me too.

    We fall silent, laid out side by side on the dish,
    like strangers.
    A word or two perhaps but my stomach feels
    queasy with old grudges.

    When love is no more evening meals are mere
    consumption of matter.
    When home is no more maybe only the soul of
    clams will give shelter?

    From different cities we came, with different
    winters behind us.
    We enjoy each other's bright hues but what keeps
    up apart?

    I chew slowly, digesting your deep sea fibre.
    You go still in the noise as I melt on you tongue.
