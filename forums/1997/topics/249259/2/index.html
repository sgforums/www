    Hi SB,

    I can't say that I can totally empathise with your situation, but I don't think it's a stretch to say that my experience comes close. Back during the time period Sec 3 - JC2, my family was having serious financial difficulties. Simply put, my dad quit his regular job to go into some business which didn't do all that well.

    You should have seen how stick-thin I was at that time. Where I'm 61kg now, I was a much lighter 46kg (in Sec 4) and 53kg (in JC2), and my height hasn't changed by much. I often had to make the choice between buying food and buying lecture notes. Guess which one I bought.

    And on top of that, my dad could actually accuse my mom of intentionally forbidding me from eating. And he would constantly jeer at me and look down on me for being 'slow and stupid'. Never mind that I was slogging through school while he was lazing in front of the TV half the time.

    And yeah.... I don't think I really want to go into how he would bully me every I got bullied... lots of bad memories. According to his twisted point of view, I got bullied because I was weak - and he was not proud of having such a weakling for a son.

    In the end, it affected me very badly - physically and psychologically. I had at times considered giving up and quitting school. But what stopped me from doing that was the notion that if I did that, he would win. And I had to have faith that things would get better.

    And luckily for me, things did get better. He finally woke up his bloody ideas and found a regular job. And he started to go easy on me - though at times, he would go back to his old self and try to put me down. And I entered the army - where I actually ate much better than at home. I gained weight during BMT in fact.

    I won't lie and say that things are perfect for me now - I've had to take out 100% loans from Mendaki and DBS to finance my tuition fees, and I've had to give tuition to my cousins for my pocket money. But on the bright side of things, it is still far preferable to the past.

    About your dad's loans.... what I can say is this. If there's one thing that I've learnt from this current semester, it would be this:

    Do NOT let irresponsible people make you feel responsible for their actions.

    It's high time for you to put your foot down and make it clear to him that his debts are his responsibilities and not yours. Go and get the rest of your family and present this unified stand to him. The only debts that you should clear are your study loans, which (as I can understand), are pretty substantial already.

    As a final word, remember that even parents can be wrong, and it would be up to you to help them see the error of their mistakes. On my part, the only truly slow and stupid thing I ever did in my life was to actually believe my dad when he said that I was slow and stupid. It just took me a long time to realise that.

    So now perhaps it's time to get your dad to realise that.

    God bless and good luck.
