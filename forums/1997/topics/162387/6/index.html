    Originally posted by alfagal:
    Dr Doc,

    so wat wld GPs usually do when a patient goes to him/her with regards to irregular menses? eg. skipped periods, or late menses
    If skipped periods or late menses, and the patient is sexually active, we would suspect pregnancy and proceed to do a Urine Pregnancy Test (which is highly specific after 5 days of conception with regards to the human chorionic gonadotrophin hormones)...

    If the irregular menses is chronic, an abdominal ultrasound will be done to reveal any presence of fibroids, ovarian cysts or endometriosis... If it cannot be viewed properly, a transvaginal (a probe inserted into the vagina) ultrasound will be done and a sample of the cervix will be taken (if there is intermenstrual bleed - bleeding in between menses) to rule out the possiblity of a malignant change of the cervical tissues...
