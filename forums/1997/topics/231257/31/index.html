    P = V x I.

    But also, V = I x R.

    So substitute the second equation into first equation.

    You will get P = I^2 x R.

    I prefer P = I^2 x R as P = VI can be misleading at times... especially when it comes to power losses in transmission cables. The V to be used is NOT the voltage of the electricity being transmitted, but rather, the potential difference across the cables.
