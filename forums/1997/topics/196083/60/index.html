    Mouth ulcers...the causes are too numerous to cover here adequately..can do one lecture. The most common are apthous ulcers, which are the common round ulcers with a surrounding white or reddish rim. The cause is idiopathic in most instances...meaning we don't know why. People have suggested viruses, but not proven in most cases. Viruses such as HSV (the commone herpoes simplex) and varicella-zoster (chicken pox) can certainly cause mouth ulcers but not the common apthous ulcers that we have. It is most likely autoimmune but the the honest answer is that we dunno why.

    The 'white circular thing' or can also be reddish ring around the ulcer is a zone of inflammatory cells (white) and vasodilation (red), often associated with some swelling. That's why it is painful...cos' it is an inflammatory lesion. As we teach our medical students, the classic signs of inflammation are rubor (redness), calor (heat), dalor (pain), tumor (swelling)..

    There is some disturbance in the immune system, possibly related to a viral infection or unknown insults, resulting in inflammation and breakdown of the surface mucosa.

    and while we're talking about aches, what exactly is muscle ache? the fibres are being pulled?? nerves perhaps? how do they subside. what (besides salonpas and yoko yoko) can help speed up the ache relieve?
    As for muscle ache, after badminton in this case, it is due to buildup of lactic acid that stimulates the nerves leading to sensation of pain. But if you do vigorous exercise without adequate warmup you can damage the muscle fibres...analogious to microscopic tearing of muscle fibres forced to hypercontact without adequate stretching ...damage to cells lead to leakage of muscle enzymes like creatinine kinase (rise after vigrous exercise) and stimulate release of various cytokines which stimulate the nerve endings and giving you sensation of pain. Obviously the buildup may take some time, so the aches can come later in the day or the next day...

    Cytokines and inflammatory mediators do have a half life and degrade with time...damaged muscles can also repair..and symptoms will resolve with time.
