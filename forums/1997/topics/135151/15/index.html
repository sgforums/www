    Jack knew a bit about Puddlemere. The football league was dominated by Puddlemere clubs United and Athletic. Their rivalry was fierce and intense. He also knew that United's president was distantly related to King Gregory IV. Jack was a supporter of Athletic and loved wearing their green-and-white hooped jersey around. His uncle had been Athletic's goalkeeper two decades ago and later became its sporting director. He was still with the club till this day.

    One day, Joe Albuquerque called the members of the Order to a meeting.

    "I've just received information that Rainee's agents may seek to sabotage the derby between United and Athletic and claim it was a terrorist act that we did," he addressed those present. "I will need everyone who can fight to go with me to the Puddlemere Oval. Krobeen, you are MacLaggan's heir so you have to fight for us. I understand that you have not finished your training yet, but the spirit of your valiant ancestor will protect you."

    "That's not fair!" exclaimed Jack, Rachel and Yan-xin in unison. "Krobeen is our junior in class, so why is he allowed to fight?"

    "Because he is the heir to the noble Half-blood Prince," Harvey replied. "It is his duty to fight. Kids like you have no obligation, so it's best you remain at the base. You can help Orelia in the kitchen if you want."

    The threesome would not back down, especially Jack, the Athletic supporter. "I've not been to a derby for ages," he protested. "I just have to go down to the Oval to cheer my team on."

    Fabrice stepped in to defuse the situation. "I'll talk to your folks regarding this matter," he said. "If they're agreeable, you can come along. If not, forget about it."

    So the matter was settled. The derby was not due to take place until two weeks' time. Athletic were second behind United on goal difference in the league tables. For the last 12 years, the league title had alternated between these two clubs. Chudley were the last non-Puddlemere team to win the league. The previous season, Tutshill had won the title, but were stripped of it after a match-fixing scandal was uncovered by the Quibbler, a trashy tabloid magazine. United were subsequently crowned champions.

    Joe Albuquerque, Fabrice and another Order member named Julius would be in charge of the whole operation. Harvey would keep an eye on the base camp due to his age and rheumatism. Albuquerque's men would form part of the task force, along with people from the rest of the cell groups.

    "I'll handle this," McGonagall said curtly. "In the mean time, here's a portkey for you all to use. Time is not on our side."

    Albuquerque and the others took hold of the portkey, and at the count of three, they were transported to the Football Association building. The once-opulent structure looked as if it had been the victim of an atomic bomb blast.

    The Order members saw the FA's chief executive been shackled and brought towards a cage by some Black Knives. Before they could make any move, they came face to face with the Black Knives' Chief of Staff, Lord Nelstar.

    "Hahaha, I've been expecting you," he sneered.

    "We have a mission to complete," Albuquerque retorted. "And you are in the way of our success."

    Nelstar drew his sword. "Kill me if you dare," he taunted the Order members.

    Before Albuquerque's group could respond, another group of Order members came running in their direction. "Cell group Charlie reporting," its leader announced. "This is Sadie O'Brien."

    Nelstar took one look at the two cell groups assembled before him and gave a start. He took out a whistle and blew three times. Immediately, several Black Knife assassins appeared from nowhere. "Let the fight begin," he declared.

    Immediately, a massive free-for-all broke out. The scene was like a war zone, with a mess of enemy combatants at each other's throats. The Black Knife fighters were no match for the Order of the Phoenix, and Nelstar had to admit defeat.

    He tried to run away, but was speared in the back by Krobeen. Nelstar turned around and came face to face with his attacker. "Krobeen... I have had... the pleasure of meeting your father..." he gasped. Those were Nelstar's last words, and he fell to the ground dead almost immediately.

    "The cage! Get the cage!" shouted Albuquerque. Everybody turned in the direction of the cage containing the CEO of the FA. It was locked. O'Brien removed her hairpin, slipped it into the lock and turned it. There was a click, and the poor man was free.
