    There ain't any much tips aside from the tips that some of the forumnites have gave earlier and the explanation that I have posted in the first few pages...

    Growth in height comes with proper nutrition whereas after your vertical growth ceases, horizontal growth steps in, and that you need overly proper nutrition...



    I have a friend who is a late bloomer, he was only aobut 130 cm tall when he was 17 years old, but recently when I saw him, he's 24 now, he was 40 cm taller... basically he started to grow taller, because it is his time of growth plus the suitable activities that he has adapted to and the food that he takes...

    Our growth is partially controlled by the epiphyseal plates in our long bones (humerus, ulna, radius, femur, tibia, fibula, metacarpals, metatarsals), and this in turn is controlled by sex hormones (estrogen and testosterone), which decides when to shut them up...

    Another hormone which has the capability to alter the activities of growth in the human body is the well known growth hormone (excess of it leads to gigantism while deficient in it leads to dwarfism). This hormone has been genetically modified and has given way to a new method of cheating vertical growth.

    However taking these genetically modified growth hormone may yield side effects (note that excessive endogenous growth hormone may also cause the same side effects). They include high cholesterol, diabetes, liver abnormalities, increased tissue stiffness, carpal tunnel syndrome, musculoskeletal disease, neuropathy, allergic reactions, pancreatitis, hyperglycemia, visual deterioration, headaches, vomiting, increased liver enzyme levels, increased sweating, pain in general and back pain specifically.

    These common side effects are due to hormone-hormone interaction. Growth hormone is highly known to suppress insulin leading to hyperglycemia and its related condition. It is also known to suppress many other hormones such as the thyroid hormone which leads to liver abnormalities. Extensive growth also will compress the fascial sheath in our body leading to a feeling of tightness. Such rapid growth will also lead to what we all know as growing pains, hence leading to all the aches and pains one would complain about...

    NOTE: The age that I have provided which was mentioned to be the age where growth ceases is only an average. There can be some people who may continue to grow up till 25 years old, but these are variants...
