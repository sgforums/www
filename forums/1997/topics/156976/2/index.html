    SINGAPORE : The Singapore Examinations and Assessment Board has apologised for a mistake in a question in this year's PSLE Mathematics paper.

    But students will not suffer from the mistake. The Board said it would award two marks to all students, irrespective of their responses.

    Shaun Loh, 12, and his friend were among many stumped by this year's PSLE Maths paper.

    One of the questions - question number 13 - required them to calculate the area of Rectangle C - based on given area of Triangles A and B.

    "I understood the question but could not solve it no matter how hard I tried. I tried to solve it by guessing the length and breath of the rectangles and triangles. I skipped the question as I knew I would not have time for the other questions if I carried on working on that one," he said.

    In a statement, the Singapore Examinations and Assessment Board said although the answer can be worked out using the given data, the question itself was flawed.

    The mistake lies in an inconsistency between the dimensions of the rectangle and the areas of the two triangles.

    Based on this data, "it is mathematically not possible to draw such a figure."

    Singapore parents have long complained about the PSLE Maths paper, which they say gets more difficult every year.

    But the Board's mistake in a PSLE examination question - believed to be the first-ever here - left many parents baffled.

    Lisa Lee, Shuan's mother, said: "At this point, I'm just wondering how this could have happened after many rounds of vetting and how this could be prevented in the future. And also I hope the examiners would be more lenient in marking the papers."

    The Board is giving two marks to all students - regardless of their answers to the question.

    Some tutors said the move was fair.

    Some schools also say students won't be too badly affected as students had always been advised not to spend too much time on multiple choice questions. - CNA/de
