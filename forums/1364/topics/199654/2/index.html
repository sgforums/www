    Last evening, i met up with Oceanos and Karkadann there again, so I can collect more intelligence for everyone who is interested.

    Oceanos and Karkadann both ordered the po-lo bao and ying yong, and i ordered Macau pork chop burger and HK milk tea



    The verdict?

    the supposedly crispy crust of the po-lo bao were not crispy. it was a total disappointment, as what Oceanos would say.

    They did agreed with me that the ying yong was good...smooth texture and a perfect blend of tea and coffee.

    As for the pork chop burger, 2 pieces of french loaf with lettecue, tomato, cucumber, slice of cheese and, of course, the pork chop.

    The pork chop was tender and juicy, not the very chewy type you might think it will be. One thing: you must eat it while it is hot...and beware of the tenderizing and juicy pork chop

    The HK milk tea. I would say it is as smooth as the Ying Yong...but as compared to the ying yong, the HK milk tea lose its glam. Maybe it is my taste bud, but I cant really tell the different between HK milk tea and the ying yong..I will pay more attention next time when i order ying yong

    Field Report #2

    Went to Ubi to look for Char Siew Rice but accidentally came across the shop selling Authentic HK food, so what the hack...eat first...Char Siew Rice can wait...

    On the menu, they have HK Steam Rice and herbal soup, but i decided to order something that one can immediately spot the difference : Wantan Mee...

    Nothing great about the shop, I think you might have problem spotting it, cos there were more than 4 coffee shops in the same area.

    Service wise, they are trying very hard to promote the Steam Rice together with Herbal Soups....after I placed my order, the lady went back inside to continue her chat until my noodles is ready

    The moment of truth...

    The wantan mee was kinda disappointing...cos i was expecting the HK size wantan...but instead i got the Singapore size wantan...haiz...and the chives are missing too...

    Soup was a bit too salty and oily...but the noodle was ok...not lumpy...the only comforting part of this field trip...

    I also ordered a glass of HK Lemon Tea... was hoping the authentic HK Lemon Tea....but when i got it...haiz...again another disappointment...tis the normal lemon tea (not those can trype) you would get from any kopi diam or kopi stall...the only difference is the spoon and more lsices of lemon in it...

    Maybe I should have tried the steamed rice with herbal soup or even the Hong Kong Hawker Noodles (Zhe-Zai Mian)...they might even tasted better...

    Well....maybe next time...

    But I would give a 2.5 out 5 for this field trip...

    NOte...the shop is located in the heart of Ubi Avenue...It is near the "Shop N Save", U wun miss it.
