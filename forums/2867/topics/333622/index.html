    i have helped a few people planned for their japan itineraries before. if it is your first time, Tokyo is a must visit. North-south-east-west of Tokyo, there are many sights. To the south we have Yokohama, Kamakura. To the west we have Mt Fuji and Hikone. To the north we have Nikko. To the east is Narita (where the airport is). To fully explore Tokyo, a week is best set aside to experience the best of it. I will recommend that you visit Tokyo in end-March to catch the Sakura.

    The other area is the Kansai area which consist of Osaka, Kyoto, Nara, Kobe, Himeji. You can give yourself 5 days to visit all these places. Best time to visit is during the autumn leaves period in mid-November. If you have more time to spare, can set aside another 2 days for Miyajima/Hiroshima.

    Please be warned that shinkansen is expensive. Unless you get the JR Pass. It depends on what you want from the trip. To see as much as possible but experience less. Or to experience as much as possible.

    I do not normally share my blog with strangers. But you can google search 'vincent travel' to get some info.

    cheers

    Japan is not as difficult as some folks put it out to be. Aside from the language you just need to understand the rail system. Transport is really not too expensive, the JR and the Tokyo Metro provide excellent routes. Further away, as someone else had already, purchase the JR pass.     Honestly, if you want to have budget accomodation, you should try the lodges/minshuku managed by Taiwanese people. They charge usually 3000 to 3300 per night. But it's cheaper if more people share a room. There're different places to visit for different interests: Nature and Farms: 1. Visit Nikko of course. (take Utsunomiya line if you have no pass and switch to Nikko line at Utsunomiya; and you can get the famous gyoza there too) 2. Visit Nara. (from Osaka, take Yamatoji line down to Nara; it takes 40 minutes) 3. Ibaraki prefecture: you can take tsukuba express from Akihabara and drop at Moriya to switch to Kantou railway which has quite a scenic farm scenery (I went to Shimotsuma because of the movie Shimotsuma Monogatari) History and Culture: 1. Kyoto (needless to say how to go about; you can take the bus system which has a flat rate of 120 yen I think) 2. Hiroshima (JR rail pass; it's 1.5 hours away from Osaka and you can go to the A-bomb dome via the tram i.e. the Hiroden) Anime/Manga/Electronics: 1. Akihabara (needless to say) 2. Nakano (perhaps cheaper ones there?) Most of Japan have Books Off which offers 2nd hand manga books usually at perhaps 115 yen? That is if you know Japanese. Seeing the beach: 1. Yokohama 2. Enoshima (it's nice to see the South China Sea; i took a budget route taking Shonan Shinjuku line down from Shinjuku to Ofuna before switching to Shonan Monorail) 3. Kujukuri beach (take to Chiba station via Sobu rapid line then change to Sotobo line before taking to Togane line) Shopping: 1. Osaka (they are half price of Tokyo) 2. Harajuku/Shibuya/Shinjuku (prices are a little steep) 3. Ginza (for branded goods lovers) I was in the Kanto area for 3 weeks and Kansai/Chugoku area for 1 week.
