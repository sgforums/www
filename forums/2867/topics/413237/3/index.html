  • Kuali Baba
    17 Oct 10, 21:48

    For a start you can be less vague about what exactly it is you want to see in each city. If there's only one attraction, is it worth making the trip there? As 7-11 pointed out, travelling will eat up a huge chunk of time. Places are much more spread out than in Singapore, so don't bite off more than you can chew. What else is there to do if circumstances ruin your plans, e.g. bad weather around Mt Fuji? Are you able to converse in Japanese, read signs and understand what people say?

    You can't win your parents' trust in just 2 months. You need to show that you're responsible first - you work for your own money, spend within your means, work out the budget for your trip and stick to it. Do your research, know exactly how you're going to stay in touch with home. Don't simply follow any stranger, and expect to be caught out by things like being unable to use your phone. You need to be able to look after yourself, because no one else will while you're away.

    Japan is really a good choice for a vacation. But things are not cheap there.

    Somethings you have to take note before your trips:

    - Where you wish to visit in Japan?

    - What kind of transport you need to take?

    The transportation system is far too complicated comparing it with Singapore MRT lines. Hence, it's essentil to do some research work before your trip.

    And bring more money. There are too many nice things to buy and too much delicious food to eat. And the accommodation may not be cheap too. For 3 week vacation, I will advise you to bring at least 10k SGD just for in case any emergency.

    And enjoy your trip!
