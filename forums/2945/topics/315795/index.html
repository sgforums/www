    Dear Residents,

     CRIME ALERT – THEFT FROM VEHICLE TARGETING LAPTOPS

     Recently, there had been an increase in the number of theft from vehicle cases where laptops were stolen at Geylang, Bugis, Beach Road and Kallang. It is believed that the culprit would loiter around the car parking lots to observe car movement.

     2.       After a vehicle owner parks his car and leaves, the culprit would approach the car to observe if the owner had left his laptop inside. If he finds a laptop, the culprit would smash the vehicle’s window to steal the laptop in a matter of seconds. In one case, the owner had left his car for only 10 minutes when he returned to discover his car window smashed and laptop stolen.

     3.       While Police are closely monitoring the situation and some arrests had been made, the crime is still occurring. It is important that car owners adopt the following crime prevention measures to prevent themselves from becoming the next victim:

    a.     Do not leave your laptop lying exposed in your vehicle. As far as possible, always carry it with you. Otherwise, it should be locked in the car boot;

    b.     Keep a record of the serial numbers of valuable items such as laptops, handphones (IMEI no.) and/or cash cards. These will help the police in investigations;

    c.     Install an anti-theft alarm in your vehicle for added protection.

     4.       If you have any information on the above cases or wish to report any suspicious person(s) loitering within the vicinity, do not hesitate to contact Singapore Police Force at 1800 – 255 0000.

     

    The above information has been reproduced with the permission of Singapore Police Force (SPF). Any feedback should be made directly to SPF at www.spf.gov.sg/feedback.htm.
