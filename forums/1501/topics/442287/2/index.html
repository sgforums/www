    The workers there are too terrified to testify, their livelihoods depend on this. All except two are foreigners on work permit, and I'm one of the two singaporeans. The only thing I can say for new job-seekers is to read the contract carefully, take note of working hours, days off, ot pay, ot conditions, job scope, termination conditions, and if possible, ask for no contract. Also, request for a few days of trial run at the job to see if it's suitable, chat with employees and gauge the vibe, all these before signing the contract. I actually had this done but I'm not very street-smart so it's not foolproof. And if they ask you to do things not stated in the contract or if you find something that doesn't tally, bring it up immediately or ask the mom about it. This means you have to be very familiar with the contract. You have to know the rules in order to abide by it.
