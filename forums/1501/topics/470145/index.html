Good or bad to increase re-employment age till 67?

  • sunnydays
    31 May 13, 17:02

    The labour movement yesterday called for the re-employment age to be extended from 65 to 67 to keep older workers on the job, as the resident employment rate reached a new high, driven by strong job creation and a higher number of female and older workers in the workforce.

    Based on an NTUC survey of 118 unionised companies conducted between July and September, about three in four companies do not cut the pay of older workers. This makes the case for the re-employment age to be raised, especially amid the tight labour market, the NTUC argued.

    "Given the generally smooth outcome, I hope the tripartite partners would consider further enhancing the upper end of the legally mandated re-employment age from 65 to 67," said NTUC Deputy Secretary-General Heng Chee How.

    "This will not only enable more older workers to earn income for more years, but would also help companies meet the challenges of a tight labour market and reduce reliance on foreign labour," he added.

    Observers agreed with the NTUC's suggestion, citing the shortage of workers in some sectors and Singaporeans' higher life expectancy, but they felt it may also cost employers too.

    "They need to look more at re-designing jobs for an ageing workforce," said Singapore National Employers Federation Executive Director Koh Juan Kiat. He added that firms have to be prepared to invest in re-training and upgrading for older workers.
