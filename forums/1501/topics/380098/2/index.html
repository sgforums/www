    I think there are some mis-perception on Gain City. I have been working in Gain City for 3 years and I would  like to clarify some of the missing truth in my personal capacity.

    Firstly, the OT claiming hours from the finance side are pre-agreed by the foreign employees and finance dept. during their employment negotiation as part of the terms and conditions on salary, applying of S-Pass, etc.

    Secondly, I have never seen or hearsay on any of our directors (MD or ED) shouting to any of our colleagues during my 3 years stay in Gain City, even till now. In fact, they are always polite and humble.

    Thirdly, on Gain City paying below market rate, there is no right or wrong answer to this. It is part and parcel of negotiation and market demand-supply situation. It largely depends on our negotiation skills and how keen we wanted the job.

    If we have accepted the job unwillingly, then we shouldn't have in the 1st place because they did not force us to accept. Obviously when we accepted the job offer, it might not be because we were satisfied with the pay but there and then, there isn't any other better offers around.

    So looking back, at least Gain City in a way has tide us over the period when we need a job to provide for our family while looking around for better ones.

    Fourthly, on the unmentioned of 1 year contract employment agreement, as far as I knew, most of the companies in Singapore are already implementing contract employment to encourage continuous performance driven result for re-contract of employment. Even civil sector are gradually, if not all, removing the permanent contract scheme to identify performance drivers so that their career path can be better map out by the respective organisation.

    On MC claims, we do accept private clinic claims besides polyclinic claims and the individual entitled claim amount was also increase from Y2007 to Y2008.

    Lastly, whether the individual HR or Finance manager has a character flaw or personality clash with the employees, the employees have avenues to address their grievances against their immediate superior. It is never the company intention for any of its middle management to mis-treat their fellow employees/colleagues which is against the company idea of creating a harmony working environment for all.

    Of course there are certain areas that we might have not done enough or may not be to the ideal of every employees but at least the minimum terms are provided. It is this minimum that allows the company to continuously provide a stable career and income for the about 500 staff and their families.
