    The worst employer is the Government Linked Companies.

    By manipulating the NTUC - which is run by a PAP Minister-without-Portfolio in the Prime Minister's Office - the Government has encouraged adopting the idea of short term employment contracts that are renewable every 3 to 5 years.

    The simplistic reason given was that a "short term employment contract" allows the employer the flexibility of adjusting manpower needs, without having to adjust the salary or CPF of the workers.

    Nothing was elaborated that when manpower is adjusted - the short term employment contract is no longer renegotiated, and the employer simply wash its hands without even needing to give a "Golden Handshake" to the "expired" contract worker - as the worker is not retrenched but its employment contract had expired.

    Does a worker without job need to be concerned about salary adjustment or CPF cuts ?

    While the interests of the Employers are considered, did the PAP Government give any consideration to the interests of those workers who have been adjusted out of their jobs when their "short term employment contract" reach its end-date ?

    Under such circumstances, while the PAP Elites give themselves an iron rice bowl, they expect the Singapore workers to be contented with our insecure rice bowl.

    The obvious winner in this setup is the Singapore Government - who is the biggest employer of Singaporeans.

Mom would even bake cakes and bak changs to give to his employees during festivals and such. New year dinners were compulsory 10 course affairs. During bad times, my dad made sure everyone of the employers were paid, even during bonus periods, even if we, his family had to subsist on porridge and vegetables.

    But now it seems such attitudes were lost. We all had gone wrong somewhere. SMEs were the loudest to scream for help to the govt, but where slowest to help their own workers.

    They would rather spend profits on their mistresses, first class travel, dine in the best restaurants, splurge on sports cars for their own sons than to pass some profits back to their workers. Worse still, some use hire and fire strategies, and push the blame on society, on workers being choosy or demanding.

    No worker seek to disadvantage others, nor do workers want to be taken advantage of. Similarly for bosses, thus a need to find back the balance. We dont need confrontational union representation, but workers sorely need protection against abusive employers, and may this thread be an expose of such unscrupulous bosses!
