    VIDEO: Lee Kuan Yew says it ‘cannot be helped’ that foreigners are competing with locals unequally for jobs

    July 24th, 2011 | Author: Temasek Review

    In other countries, foreigners are only allowed to take up jobs shunned by locals, but in Singapore, not only are they allowed to compete directly with Singaporeans for jobs which can otherwise be taken up by them, Singaporeans are now told by their supreme leader Lee Kuan Yew that they will have to accept it.

    For video: 

    http://www.temasekreview.com/2011/07/24/video-lee-kuan-yew-says-it-cannot-be-helped-that-foreigners-are-competing-with-locals-unequally-for-jobs/

    Speaking during the South Asian Diaspora Convention two days ago, Lee Kuan Yew was asked about the political implications of his mass-immigration policies to which he replied:

    “So you’ve got to accept the discomfort, which the local citizens fear that they are competing unequally for jobs. (It) cannot be helped.”

    He also claimed that China is ‘easier’ to govern compared to India as 90 percent of its people speaks the same language.

    At 88 years of age, Lee Kuan Yew is the world’s oldest living MP. Though he does not hold any ministerial posts in the Singapore cabinet, he still has five full-time and part-time private and press secretaries from the Prime Minister’s Office working for him.

    When asked if he will retire a year ago, Lee said:

    “You work as long as you can work and you will be healthier and happier for it. If you ask me to stop working all of a sudden, I think I’ll just shrivel up, face the wall and just that.”

    While Singaporeans are forced to compete unequally with foreigners, the PAP employs all kinds of dirty tactics to prevent its political opponents from competing with it.
