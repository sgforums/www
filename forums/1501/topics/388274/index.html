    Letter to PM Lee and Manpower Minister Gan Kim Yong on Courts employing Malaysians

    January 17, 2010

    Dear PM Lee & Mr Gan,
     
    I refer to the online report by local blog www.temasekreview.com (see article:
    here) that Courts Singapore will be holding a mass recruitment exercise across the causeway to fill up their Singapore operations’ positions with Malaysian only.
     
    I would like to know the Ministry of Manpower’s position on this matter.
     
    PM Lee, it is somehow good news to hear recently on some Government announcements that give native Singaporeans certain priority such as primary school balloting and revision of school fee for PR/foreigners. However, most of these announcements are simply superficial don’t really give a clear distinction between citizens and foreigners.

    During the early pre-independence days, the PAP Government under then-PM
    Lee Kuan Yew had campaigned for a ‘Malaysian Malaysia’ policy. Why after fifty years the PAP Government has sifted it ‘goal-post’ from a ‘Singaporean Singapore’ to ‘pro-Foreign Talent Singapore’ policy?  Is it because of such radical shift that caused companies to also adopt a pro-Foreign HR policy?
     
    Yours Faithfully,
     
    A native-born Singaporean – Tan Keng Hong
