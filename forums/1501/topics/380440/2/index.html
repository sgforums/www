    I don't think anyone can say anything bad about MDIS here because of the T&Cs of this forum. Also, MDIS always likes to say if anyone says bad stuff about them, they will sue - I have personally heard the sec-gen say this.

    However, as an ex-worker from MDIS, I have nothing but bad things to say about the company. I managed to 'graduate' from my contract late last year. The turnover rate is so high that they have to place so many restrictions on the position (such as 1 - 5 year contract, payment required if you leave before your contract is up - which is TRUE btw. For me, it was 3 months of salary, but couldn't afford so I had to stay on.) They tend to place employees in the wrong positions (eg. IT graduate doing scheduling and other ad hoc duties, geography/lifescience graduate doing admin jobs.)

    The working conditions are not up to standards and the facilities are not as cutting edge as they would have you believe. My computer monitor was so old that I could still see the refresh rate - staring at this from 8.30am to 9pm almost every day was a nightmare. People treat you like crap, as if your studies and previous work experiences don't count for anything. I got scolded for not being able to produce ridiculous amounts of analyses and have been shouted at in front of other colleagues for expressing my honest thoughts in my monthly report. My pay, despite being a degree-holder, was only S$1.8k, and no significant increments were made even though I was doing overtime and working to keep my portfolio from disintegrating. I was very upset as there was another staff in my dept who did not do as much work and she got just as much increment as myself. There is little/no staff appreciation.

    Come to think of it, I feel like I wasted 1 whole year of my life working there. It has not helped my resume as it has nothing to do with what I studied. Also I did not learn much from working there, other than using Microsoft Word/Excel, stuff I already knew before... I'd much rather be unemployed than work in MDIS.

    This is my perception of my time working there. No defamation intended, but if your company is lousy, this is what you should expect to hear.. isn't it?
