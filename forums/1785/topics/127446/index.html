    Escaped jaguar recaptured after half an hour
    By Suzanne Jung/Farah Abdul Rahim, Channel NewsAsia

    SINGAPORE : There was high drama at the Singapore Zoo on Sunday afternoon when a jaguar escaped from its enclosure.

    Luckily, apart from a bit of a scare, no one was hurt and the animal was recaptured half an hour later.

    It was business as usual after the incident at the Zoo.

    A visitor saw the jaguar escape from its enclosure at about 2pm and sounded the alert.

    Zoo officials say the jaguar was on the loose for some 30 minutes just outside its exhibit, within possible reach of visitors.

    All visitors were evacuated as a precautionary measure.

    The enclosure is now closed, as investigations are going on to determine how the jaguar managed to escape.

    But most visitors were none the wiser about what had happened.

    "Really? We did not know, they didn't tell us. They said nothing happened. They should tell us so we can be more careful and prepared, especially with children around," one visitor said.

    "If any animal escapes there should have some alarm system and we can go to one place to hide. Some people were frustrated; they didn't know what's going on," another said.

    "For them not to tell us was a good call, as they didn't want to panic people," said another visitor. - CNA
