    So Joe is 18 years old. He still lives with his parents and his grandfather, and walks to university everyday. Every morning before he leaves the house, his grandfather would say the same thing to him over and over again: "be careful when crossing the road, don't cross if the light is red, cross if the light is green, look to your left and right before crossing..." Joe was very irritated with those lame advice being repeated everyday. One day Joe could not take it anymore and bursted out: "KEEP QUIET LAH! OF COURSE I KNOW! SUCH A SIMPLE ADVICE!" and stormed out of house.

    Not long after that, his grandfather died. Joe was very sad. Joe always loved his grandfather, just that sometimes he was irritated by the nagging. So Joe tried to recall the moments he spent with his grandfather, tried to recall their conversations. But he couldn't remember any, except the red light green light advice. Now he realized that, giving that lame advice was the ONLY way his grandfather could have had a conversation with him.
