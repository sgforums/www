    What you expect in JC, depends on your personality

    You can choose to be a hyper funky person
    (sort of putting on a mask)
    goes around making friends and more friends, join lotsa activities, creating lotsa attention
    being over the age of 16, means more freedom from parent
    there are lotsa things u can do
    go pub, zouk chiong lolz...

    or if u choose to a 'chao mugger', everyday is study and study
    and top the school, and enjoy the prestige of getting labelled 'Smartie' in your school

    because you make it to JC, shows there is some standards in your academic achievement.... meaning u know how to study smart and not study hard
    and also that shows u know how to manage your time.
    Time managemet allows u more time for other activities !

    Widen your social circle, and 'selling' yourself, is the most important aspect to learn in real life.
    you can get all the As in your academic years,
    this doenst mean u will be successful in future.
    A resourceful person is a successful person.
    Being resourceful and know how to 'sell' yourself is crucial and definitely helps in your future career. Grades are just there, as an advantage, to add to your portfoilo.

    People management or what they call 'ren yuan' is something you should start observing and learn. That is what they call 'EQ'. Something that wont be taught in school, but left for you to develop on your own.
    How many times in secondary school, your heard stuff like " XXX dont like YYY... lets not talk to him/her blah blah....." Don't do stupid things to get yourself ostracise by people. But dont be a "all-pleaser", go around pleasing people, people say yes, you say yes. Know where is the limit.

    You can make many friends... but only a few are 'zi ji' real friends. They are those whom you can really talk to, and depend on when trouble arise.
    True friends are friends that last throughout your lifetime.
    Isnt it sad, if graduate from JC, but having no 'true friend'?
    Secondary and JC are the time that really nurture real friendship, because you spend most of your time with them.
    When you go Uni, everything is very individual and independent.
    Or worst if u join the workforce, everyone will be stepping on everyone,
    so that they can climb the corporate ladder...
    very sad, but its the truth.
