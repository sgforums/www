    The author, Mr Eugene Wijeysingha, in collaboration with Rev Fr Rene Nicolas, MEP, is to be highly commended for giving us a captivating and well-documented publication worth treasuring for posterity.

    For me it is not just another history book but a special one with personal significance. I could relate to some parts of the history with cherished memories.

    I remember the old Church of St. Joseph at Bukit Timah and the energetic church builder cum parish priest, the late Rev. Father Joachim Teng, whom I served as an altar boy for his Latin Tridentine Mass. I also served in the re-burial service of the French priests, among whom was Rev Father A. Mauduit, whose remains were unearthed from the tombs in the church and transferred to the cemetery nearby. This was to make way for the expansion of the old church.

    Two key events which I would never forget are the visits made to Singapore by Mother Teresa in 1985 and Pope John Paul II in 1986.

    I was fortunate to be able to hold the hands of Mother Teresa while I shared some brief moments conversing with her. Petite, frail and bent, she was a living saint radiating ChristÂ�fs love for all, especially the poor and destitute. I remember her message of love for the destitute and rejected in Calcutta which she powerfully shared with the crowds who turned up for her rally at a stadium.

    The National Stadium was filled to the brim with large crowds of people mainly Catholics. They were undaunted by the pouring rain as they patiently waited for the arrival of Pope John Paul II to celebrate Mass. It was a highly emotional moment and I felt very proud to be a Catholic as I witnessed the spirit of solidarity manifested by all Catholics present.

    I personally find the book inspiring and written with great relish. Catholics should get a copy and start reading it to acquaint themselves with local church history. Not to do it would be an enormous loss to them.
