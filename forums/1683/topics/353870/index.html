    How do i know whether GOD answers my prayer?

    Hey voice, I try to answer you from my own experience.

    There are many ways in which you could know that God has answered your prayer. The most obvious and perhaps the most common way is by getting what you prayed for at a later time(seeing what you prayed for being realized at some time in the future).

    There are times when God tells you that He grants your prayer whatever it is before you actually see it realized. For example when praying or asking for something (usually when one pray quite hard, under duress, or in times of need) the person would feel that God has listened and granted his prayer. The person usually knows and is so very convinced that God has listened and answered his prayer such that he would stop asking or praying for that purpose any longer. In fact when God answered  prayer in this fashion and lett he person know, he who prayed for it doesn't even need to do anyhting except to wait for it to be fulfilled. So when God answered before His realization took place, that person who prayed for it definitely would be so convinced so that there is no doubt about it(and it's not made up) that God has answered his prayer and be in a state of trust with God and await for His fulfillment.

    There are times but I believe it's quite rare that God grants our prayer in an instant nothing short of miracle you could say. That is you see the realization of your prayer or what you prayed for in a matter of seconds or minutes. This is no coincidence of course. God is all powerful and able to answer whatever we ask for in an instant. But this is rare though. At least it is rare enough in my case.

     The thing about praying and asking for something in prayer is that we should pray that it is God's will that will be done. But it doesn't mean that we can't have or can't ask for what we want but what we ask for must be in tune with what God wants. We can't force God to grant us what we ask for.

    God is not a vending machine or a wishing well. You don't always get what you want, but you will get what you need, and in due time. Sparing quiet time in prayer, reflection and contemplation helps you discern if you are asking for what will really help you grow and whether the way you've looked at the situation is adequate.

    We believe too that redemption through Christ is the sole way to eternal life. However, we leave it to Him to judge who will enter into Heaven. Some people may have never heard of Him before, other people may be doing His work unwittingly, and while there are false Christians He will reject.

    All we can do is to introduce others to the Word not through proselytising but by the example of our actions and our lives and engaging other people when they enquire. The Christian way of life challenges us to do more than just avoid being evil, but to love and serve with humility the world without falling prey to its ways. And anyone may respond to it at any time in their lives and be entitled to the same reward. We cannot judge properly who will be saved or damned by looking at their present response.
