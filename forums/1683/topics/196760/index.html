    22 Jun 06, 22:43
    1. I have never seen Psalm 23 used in this context. Especially for verse 4, the typical Catholic understanding is that this is a statement of hope in the Lord in times of trials and difficulties. How it is related to prosperity is completely beyond me.

    2. To say that one only enters the valley of the shadow of death due to sin is contrary to the Bible and to everyday experience. Take the example of Job. In Job 1:8, God describes Job as being faithful and good. Yet, shortly after that, God allows disaster to befall upon Job. Clearly, Job did not sin before the bad things happen to him.

    In terms of everyday experience, many bad things can happen to us due to the natural laws of the earth, or through the neglect and malice of other people. These things are allowed by God, as part of what Catholics call his 'permissive Will'. It has nothing to do with the sinfulness of the receiver of such bad things.

    Clearly, the pastor is making the same mistake that the 3 friends of Job made, and for which they were rebuked at the end of the book by God.

    3. The part about God being a shepherd to us who fail is certainly true. On our own, we are destined to fail. We are saved only by God's mercy, and we need to continue to ask for His mercy each day until we go home to Him.

    regards,
    obiterdicta
