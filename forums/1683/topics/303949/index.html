  • catinthehat
    21 Jan 08, 16:11
    okay "schola cantorum" means "school of singing" in Latin. Usually this term is applied to people singing Gregorian chant, and is shortened to "schola". Any church choir can call themselves "schola cantorum", and if their singing sucks, then they really need to attend a "schola musicae" (music school) first ... hee hee

    Now, there is nothing to stop people from organizing a mixed schola.

    Its just that the singers here think that a separate women's and men's schola will be better. A schola of mixed voices has different musical issues to tackle and it can get quite difficult. So we prefer to just keep things simple.

    Even in the mens' schola, there are tenors and there are basses. The tenors always complain the key is set too low, the basses always complain about the high notes (in Gregorian chant, the schola sets the key of the music).
