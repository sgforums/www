    By this time next year, Singapore could be a platform where leading international religious authorities come together to seek and promote greater understanding of each other's faith.
    Senior Minister Goh Chok Tong personally discussed this possibility with the Secretary of State of the Holy See, Cardinal Tarcisio Bertone at the Vatican City on Monday.

    The suggestion of an inter-faith dialogue was first mooted in June in Singapore during the visit of a special envoy from the Holy See.

    This is SM Goh's second official visit to Vatican City.

    And it comes at a momentous time in a troubled era, when a clash of ideas between religious extremists and the rest of the world is presenting a very real threat to global stability.

    2006 marks 25 years of formal diplomatic relations between Singapore and the Holy See.

    In celebration, the Vatican sent special envoy Cardinal Renato Raffaele Martino to visit Singapore in June where he met the Senior Minister.

    It was at that meeting that Singapore and the Vatican decided they would work together to promote peace and inter-faith harmony in the world.

    SM Goh said: "We initiated the Asia-Middle East dialogue, that's the result of our concern that there could be misunderstanding between the Middle East and other parts of the world. Singapore believes we have a rather good understanding, because we have Muslims and we are living in a Muslim region. But people in other parts may not have such a good understanding.

    "And then the worry is that misunderstanding may lead to clashes between Islam and other religions, which is unnecessary. This is actually a follow up of the Asia Middle East dialogue, this is more focused, this is on religion whereas Asia-Middle East dialogue is for economic, social, political and so on."

    The plan Mr Goh proposed to the Vatican is to gather four or five eminent international scholars from various other religions, like Islam, Buddhism and Hinduism, for an inter-faith dialogue in Singapore.

    SM Goh said: "The objective is not to criticise anyone, not to criticise the radicals and the fundamentalists, It's simply to get a positive understanding of people of different faiths. The cardinal was very supportive, he asked me to flesh out the idea, and then to send him the paper, to work on it. There's no hurry, maybe sometime in 2007 or 2008, we can have this dialogue at a high level. This is the plan which the cardinal supported."

    The Silver Jubilee of good ties between Singapore and the Vatican saw the commemorative release in Singapore of a book, and limited edition coins and stamps.

    But, if this Singapore initiative of an interfaith meeting takes off as planned, it could prove a truly memorable quest for dialogue launched out of 25 years of good relations between Singapore and the Holy See. - CNA/ch
