    If one accepts the doctrine of Original Sin, then it's possible. We all inherit Original Sin because we have normal mothers. Jesus however, is sinless. Since Original Sin can only be inherited, Mary must be free of any sin since Jesus does not have it. However, not only is Mary free of sin, she is also full of grace.

    We often ask our friends for favours, when we need our friend's friend's help. You know? So when we need the help of Jesus, we too can do the same and ask His Mother to help us ask. The prayers of two combined is more powerful than one person, don't you think?

    If we recall the Wedding at Cana, we will see the first case of Marian intercession and Jesus' first public miracle. Mary asks Jesus to help the Bridegroom who has run out of wine. She also tells the servants, 'Do whatever he tells you.' The same applies to us; Mary asks us to do whatever Jesus tells us to do.

    Of course, one can always go to God directly. We encourage you.

    And since it is All Saints Day, I'd like to remind that we can also ask the Saints for intercession. God's friends are my friends, and can be yours too!

    In summary, the Church honours Mary because:

    1) She is sinless
    2) She is full of grace
    3) She is the Mother of Christ
    4) She is the perfect woman

    Amen.

    Originally posted by breytonhartge:
    sgmac,

    how was mary savedin advance?
    Think of it this way.

    Person A and Person B are walking in the wilderness. There is a pit up ahead, of which person B is invariably in danger. Person A can save person B in two ways. he can allow person B to fall in, and then rescue him from the pit. Or, he can divert the path of person B and keep him from the pit all together. In both instances he has saved person B. So it is with God and sin. The normative way in which God saves mankind is by saving us from sin once we have been stained by it because of original sin and the sin we commit. However, He saved mary by protecting her from sin alltogether
