    On October 1960, Friar Fulgence Gross, long- time missionary in China, arrived for what would be the start of more than three decades in Singapore. later, he would recall the community that greeted him: "We had 2 Italians from two different provinces, several Chinese priests from various missions districts of China, a Brother from the Irish province and myself, whom you know as American but who spent very little time in the USA. The other fathers are degreed men, having studied in Spain, Germany and Belgium and received licentiates, doctorates, etc. from universities in sociology, labour and economics. And they are all working hard at writing books and tracts in the Chinese language."
    Gross worked with the other friars to translate the scriptures and church teachings into Chinese. He was also made the guardian of the community, worked with the parishes and conducted retreats in Malaysia and Singapore. he remained in Singapore after the Sociological Centre moved to Taiwan in 1970.
