    Amutha Valli ordered to pay S$372,000 in costs in exorcism case


    By Channel NewsAsia | Posted: 16 March 2009 2212 hrs
         
         
    SINGAPORE: The woman who lost her exorcism law suit against the Novena Church has been ordered to pay costs and disbursements amounting to some S$372,000.

    Disbursements include charges such as court filing fees and doctor's fees.

    Madam Amutha Valli Krishnan will have to pay the six churchgoers she tried to sue, more than S$300,000 in lawyers fees and disbursements.

    She will also have to pay over S$72,000 in disbursements to the Church and the two defendant priests, Father Simon Tan and Father Jacob Ong.

    Another sum of over S$92,000 - comprising largely of costs to get psychiatrists to testify for the defence - has been referred for taxation.

    A Supreme Court registrar will decide if the sums claimed are reasonable.

    Two defence lawyers, Senior Counsel Jimmy Yim and Mr Tito Isaac, have not asked for their legal fees to be included in the costs.

    But had they done so, their fees for the 32-day trial could have come up to between S$500,000 and S$1 million.

    - AFP/yb
