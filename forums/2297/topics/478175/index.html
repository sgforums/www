    hi, i am a student from nus and i went through the jc path like u. to be honest, i think u are making a mountain out of a molehill. i mean fine, you have your goals and expectations to meet. but trust me, many years down the road, your o level results or your jc wont really matter. or rather let me phrase it in a different way. whether you get into the course of your choice in uni will not depend on which jc you come from. rather, it would be how well you do in your A levels (and university interview for course like law, med and dentistry) and every jc student fight on the same level of place field. you can think about it this way. everyone starts at the same starting point during the "jc race". different jc means u are at different lane but ultimately, everyone runs the same distance. everyone got to put in the same effort so yup, go out and enjoy while you can. A levels is a tough nut to crack. your 2 years in jc wont be as easy as sec school. -TJC alumni
