    Extracted from http://www1.curriculum.edu.au/sciencepd/readings/elec.htm

    in this case, the outer electrons of the atoms in the Perspex rod are held more loosely than the outer electrons of the atoms in the cotton cloth.

    When these materials are rubbed together, it is more likely that some of the electrons in the Perspex rod will be rubbed off by the atoms in the cotton cloth.

    This means the Perspex will not have enough electrons to balance the positive charge of the nucleus. Therefore, the Perspex rod will have an overall positive electric charge.

    Tip for O levels:

    Just memorize which material are more prone to positive or negative charges. O levels just test those few materials mentioned in ur textbook
