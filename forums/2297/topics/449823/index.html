      What kind of exam, is it comprehension?

      It is a good start for you to check each unfamiliar word with a dictionary.   • wee_ws
    28 Mar 12, 19:43

    Hi,

      1. Read (quickly, joyfully) to get an initial idea.

      2. Read again (closely) to highlight unfamiliar words.

      3. Refer to the dictionary for their meanings and see if you could replace the original words in the passage with those meanings found.

      The more you carry out these steps, the more you become efficient in mastering english.

      One more advice: more haste, less speed :) It takes some time to strengthen one's command of language.

    Cheers,
    Wen Shih

    I find reading the newspapers works for me because it's shorter than books, straight to the point and the words can be utilized widely.

    With regards to the dictionary, I have a habit to just assume the meaning and continue reading. Only after I can make out the gist of the chapter do I go back to find out the exact meaning.

    Also, if you're writing reports using MS Word, just opening the thesaurus option can help in expanding your vocabulary.

    Watch more  British programmes.    Reason I mentioned British is because the English (in our education) in Singapore is based on British context.    Speak to people with good English.   If possible, speak to the native speakers.    You will benefit a lot.

    I get what you mean on checking the dictionary while reading.  I was a fanatic for English , checked every word I did not know whenever I read.  End result was I lost interest in reading.    On the positive note, it helped me improved on my knowledge of vocabulary.

    Getting yourself a good dictionary is important.    I recommend Collins Cobuild for Advanced learners.
