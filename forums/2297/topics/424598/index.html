    SBS n SMRT, yes school candidates must take SPA. Only private candidates can take the Practical Exam (in lieu of SPA). As a figure of speech, I always tell my students : You (ie. all JC students) can burn away all your JC's lecture notes and just use CS Toh's 'A' level study guide for the H2 Chem 'A' levels, it's enough. Of course, if you happen to come across any apparent contradiction between your JC's lecture notes and CS Toh's 'A' level study guide, feel free to ask about it here. I'll tell you on a case-by-case basis which version is better for you to use in the 'A' levels (in some topics, Cambridge will accept more than one version, for instance, the equation for formation of the nitronium cation during electrophilic aromatic substitution, the tri-iodomethane equation, etc). You're taking your H2 Chem & H2 Bio 'A' levels this year 2011 yes?
    Dizzyday, the Advanced Study guide combines the Chemistry syllabuses for Singapore H2 'A' levels + International Baccalaurate + Hong Kong 'A' levels + Cambridge International 'A' levels. So it's a useful book to have if you're keen to pursue a Chemistry based course in the Uni, but since you're not, his H1 'A' level study guide will suffice for your needs. You might as well buy CS Toh's H2 Physics and H2 Maths 'A' level study guides together, tomorrow. (Bonus : When you see Peter Soh (he *is* Ecogreen Technology) tomorrow, ask him about the Thomas Bond and Chris Hughes.)
