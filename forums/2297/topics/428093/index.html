    For Q3, the "trick" is to do the other series by visualizing the last term as the first. Then the arithmetic progression is just -d.

    So we get (k/2) [2a + 2d(n-1)] + (k-1)d] ---- *

    The other one is just the normal (k/2) [2a + (k-1)d]. ---- **

    Taking * minus **, you should solve your problem directly.
