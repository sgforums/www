    Here are some other stuffs to help you out:

     

    One of the reason why Poly is becoming more popular is because JCs are becoming more and more study-based, as you can see from the change of having 3 "A" subject to 3 H2 subject and 1 H1 contrast subject.

    What is more, many of those who come out of Sec school realise that what they are studying stuffs which they do not need once they enter uni, hence they will prefer entering poly and take up a course which they are more interested in.

    There is also the factor that "A-level" cert holders will be no better than an "O-level" cert holder if they cannot make it into uni, whereas a Diploma will help out in getting you a job.

    For employers, they will prefer a Diploma holder with working attachment experience over an "A-level" cert holder with no experience at anytime. Diploma holders whom they employ tend to have a diploma related to whatever business line they are in, accountants are expected to have an accountancy Diploma, and so on.
