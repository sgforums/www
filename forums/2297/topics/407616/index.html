    Yes, SBS261P is right. Three isoceles triangles are shown but (eureka!) their base angles are of the same size. So they are similar triangles. This is the main thing to see. For example, ABC and CBE obviously share a base angle at point B, while CBE and BDE obviously share a base angle at point E. Clearly then, the base angles of all three isoceles triangles are of the same size. After that it's easy ... compare similar triangles ... the answer is indeed 13.5.
