I am going to take H2 maths bio chem but still unsure whether to take H2 or H1 econs. Assuming that I am not aiming for scholarships, is it still more advantageous to take 4H2s/another H3 for admission into medicine? 

    On the other hand,if I am aiming for scholarship,would it be better to take 4H2s or another H3? 

    What other preparations should I do in order to increase my chances of getting accepted into medicine?


    If you handle it, take both 4 H2s + 1 H3. If not, the next best thing is to take 4 H2s + 0 H3. This is for a practical, 'play safe' purpose : in the unforeseen and unfortunate event that you don't get all As, your lousiest H2 score can be downgraded into a H1 weightage. As far as Medicine is concerned, it's far more important to perform well in your BMAT score, your multiple interview rounds, your personal statement and any outstanding achievement (you know, single-handedly saving an entire 3rd world village from going blind from some parasitic infection or getting cancer from some industrial pollution, that sort of heroic thing that separates you from all the thousands of other perfect A scorers applying into Medicine), than whether you take 4 H2s or a H3 subject.

    I've some links on my webpage that students intending to apply into Medicine may find relevant and useful : http://www.infinity.usanethosting.com/Tuition/#Medicine

    Posting Results for the 2016 Joint Admissions Exercise (JAE) and Joint Polytechnic Special Admissions Exercise (JPSAE)

    1The Ministry of Education (MOE) will release the posting results for the 2016 Joint Admissions Exercise (JAE) on Thursday, 28 January 2016, at 9:00 am. Results of the Joint Polytechnic Special Admissions Exercise (JPSAE) will be released on Friday, 29 January 2016, at 9:00 am. Applicants can obtain their posting results via the following channels:

    JAE

    • The JAE Internet System (JAE-IS) is accessible through the JAE website athttp://www.moe.gov.sg/education/admissions/jae; and
    • Short Messaging System (SMS) text messages sent to the mobile phone number provided by the applicant during registration.

    JPSAE

    • The JPSAE portal can be accessed at https://jpsae.polytechnic.edu.sg/.

    2Applicants posted to a Junior College (JC) or the Millennia Institute (MI) should report to these institutions on Friday, 29 January 2016, at 7:30 am. Applicants posted to a Polytechnic or Institute of Technical Education (ITE) course will receive further instructions via an enrolment package from the respective institutions by mail.

    Appeals

    3Applicants who wish to appeal for a transfer to another JC should approach the JC of their choice directly. The JC has the discretion to consider the application if it has available vacancies and the student meets its course cut-off point for this year’s exercise. Applicants who wish to appeal for a transfer to MI should approach MI directly.

    4Those who wish to appeal for a place in a polytechnic diploma course or seek a transfer to another diploma course can do so via the Centralised Joint Polytechnic JAE appeal system at http://www.polytechnic.edu.sg/jaeappeal, from Friday, 29 January 2016, 9:00 am to Tuesday, 2 February 2016, 4:00pm. The outcome of the JAE appeals will be released through the Centralised Joint Polytechnic JAE appeal website on Wednesday, 10 February 2016, at 2:00 pm. For more information on the appeals exercise, please refer to the Centralised Joint Polytechnic JAE appeal website.

    5Those who wish to appeal for a place in the ITE can do so athttps://www.ite.edu.sg/wps/portal/intake, from Thursday, 28 January 2016, 9:00 am to Wednesday, 3 February 2016. The outcome of the appeals will be available athttps://www.ite.edu.sg/admission/ft/results.html from Monday, 22 February 2016, 9:00 am to Thursday, 25 February 2016. Successful applicants are required to accept the offer by Thursday, 25 February 2016.
