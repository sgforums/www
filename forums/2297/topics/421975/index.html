    A ball of mass 200g is thrown vertically downwards from a height of 3.2m

    (a) Calculate the potential energy of the ball when it is 3.2m above the grounds

    PE =mgh

         =200 x 10 x 3.2

         = 6.4J

     

    (b) Calculate the speed of the ball just before it reaches the ground.

    (1/2) mv^2 =6.4

    0.5 x 0.2 x V^2 =6.4

    V^2=64

    V=8ms^-1

    (c) State the acceleration of the ball when it is falling .

    [Student tends to think the acceleration is 8m/s which is wrong.Because acceleration due to gravity is always constant ]

    therefore g =10m/s

     

    (d) Hence ,find the force acting on the ball as it is falling .

    F=mg = 0.2 x 10 = 2N

    [Note:when the acceleration of the ball is answered wrongly, naturally the answer of the Force acting on the ball will also be wrong ,as wrong value is used]

    Source:Ahmad Ibrahim Secondary School ,GCE O level Prelim Exam 2008
