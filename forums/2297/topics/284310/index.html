    Originally posted by yiha093:
    a 1cm3 sample of tin lead alloy has a mass of 8.5g. the density of tin is 7.3g cm-3 and that of lead is 11.3g cm-3
    calculate the percentageby weight of tin in the alloy

    sec 2 standard , cmon la guys
    let the percentage of the weight of tin in the alloy be a%.

    Percentage of the weight of lead in alloy = 100 - a %

    a/100 x 1 x 7.3 + (100 - a)(1/100)(1)(11.3) = 8.5

    7.3/100 (a) + (1-a/100)(11.3) = 8.5

    0.073 a + 11.3 - 0.113a = 8.5

    0.04 a = 2.8

    a = 70

    Percentage of the weight of tin = 70%
    Percentage of lead in alloy = 30%

    Then, check your ans back into the eqn.

    7.3 x 0.7 + 11.3 x 0.3 = 8.5 (proven)
