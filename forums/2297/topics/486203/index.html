    Working in biomedical or life science industry is not easy. If you have a normal basic degree or honours degree, you may not proceed far unless you do sales or routine lab work (a little like a lab tech). To do research, you need a real good PhD from a top uni from overseas (not NUS or NTU). Even there is a glass ceiling for Masters degree for local uni grads.

    My friend has a life science basic degree from NUS and was working in NUS lab. He is stuck there and the last I heard, he was looking for another job.

     

    Government agencies may not value a MDIS degree that highly, unless your degree is 1st class honours.
