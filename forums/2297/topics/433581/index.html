    Donkhead333 and SBS n SMRT are correct. Iodoform reaction only works on methyl aldehydes, methyl ketones and methyl alcohols (which are actually automatically oxidized to methyl aldehydes / ketones first, before the iodoform reaction occurs).

    The reason why the iodoform reaction as well as the 2,4-DNPH reaction works only only aldehydes & ketones, but not carboxylic acids, esters or amides, is that the latter group are resonance stabilized and are therefore less reactive (remember : the more stable you are, the less reactive you are) than the former group. The iodoform and 2,4-DNPH test requires a level of reactivity that only the former, but not the latter group of compounds, meet.
