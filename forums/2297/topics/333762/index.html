    Answers to eagle's question:
    i) A square is an equilateral quadilateral i.e it is a 4 sided figure whose sides are all of the same length. But all of its interior angles are 90 degrees.                          
    A rhombus is an equilateral quadilateral. But has only two sets of parallel lines.
    Hence interior opposite angles in rhombuses are equal and interior angles of
    a tranversal are not equal and add up to 180.                        
                                                                                                                              
    Rectangles are quadilaterals that have two sets of parallel lines.
    And also its interior angles are 90 degrees.
    A square is a rhombus since it is equilateral quadilateral.
    And an equilateral rectangle can be square.
    Therefore: Squares are rhombuses and rectangles.
    But not all rectangles are squares.
    b) (P excluding Q) + (R excluding Q). Or (P + R) excluding Q.

    Question

    Sets M and P are defined by

    M = { students studying mathematics }

    P = { students studying physics }

    Describe in words which students belong to the set

    (i)    M' intersects P'

    (ii)   ( M intersects P' )'

    Answer

    (i)     Students who take neither Physics nor Maths or both

    (ii)     Students who are not taking Maths only

    There are many ways to describe the required sets in words.

    So, the sentences can be phrased differently as long as the required sets are

    correct.

    To get the required sets, we will first

    (a)    draw a rectangle to represent the universal set

    (b)    next we will draw two intersecting circles, circle M for students studying

            Maths and circle P for students studying Physics

    (c)    Next, shade the correct portion for the required set

    (d)    Finally, we will write the required sets in words, using words as simple as

            possible.
