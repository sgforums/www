    That's quite a dilemma you've gotten yourself in.

    H2 Bio has more stuff to memorize than H2 Chem + H2 Phys *combined*.

    It's true that if you're the type who is easily influenced by your peers and social environment, MI requires you to have somewhat more self-discipline than some other JCs. In addition, it's a 3 year course, which means you'll spend : 1 year retaking + 3rd year at MI = 2 years longer than your peers.

    Your decision will hinge on your honest assessment of yourself on two points :

    1) Can you find the interest, effort and discipline to memorize all that stuff required of H2 Bio?

    2) Can you find the interest, effort and discipline to be self-motivated and focused regardless of your peers and social environment, for the next 3 years?

     

    Finally, are you certain that MI will accept you at this point in time? MI reserves the right to accept or reject any late-coming applicant (regardless of his/her grades), through several rounds of interviews with the vice-principal.

    Be sure you stay with your current JC until you've secured a place at MI. You don't want to end up not having a place at any JC as well as MI, and having to take private 'A' levels all by yourself.
