    #1 Infer in your own words

    #2 evidence from source to support your inference and explain if needed

    ok now for the 2nd part, the USEFULNESS

    example question for usefulness... How useful is this source in telling you...?
    How far does this source help u to understand....?   how comprehensive is this source in....?

    - always keep in mind what the source is asking, they source could be asking how useful is this source in telling u how successful is Singapore... but the Source can talk about WHY singapore is successful, and not HOW...  what i am trying to say is, pls find the RELEVANT information from the source, and sometimes it can be very tricky...

    - then u divide to 2 parts, one being the useful part, one NOT useful...   for example:
    Useful, this source is useful in telling me the perception of ...
     this source tells me about how singapore is built which makes it successful, evidence...

    NOT useful : from what i had learnt, this source did not tell me whether the ... has helped in singapore growth,

    also from source L also stated how singapore became successful, but the Source B did not tell..

    - u can also say that the source is NOT RELIABLE, THUS not useful in telling me the truth... since i cannot trust...
     ( this links to reliability)

    - basically, it is about the limititation the source has, what the source have, what it dont have... give evidence, can be from other source (cross-reference) or from ur contextual knowledge

    Note: to be honest, i am quite weak in this category, i always run a bit out of point to the reliability, so rmb to control urself, always link back to the usefulness.. it is mostly on how u phrase the question...

    ok now for the reliability part...

    example of questions : how reliable is this source..
     do u believe this source...?
     can u trust this source ...?


    things to look out for, for this type of question

    1) the source is giving FACT or OPINION?
         words like "i think", "i believe" are obviously indicating the source is made up from opinions...   words or sentence like " survery has shown...." or "The object has made.."  this type of sentence are obviously FACTS, it would be better if the source has evidence, and u should of cuz put the evidence..

    2) generalisation.... example: "the Singaporeans always...." or "Everyone..."
        if the source nv generalise at all, please also state that down, words like "10% of the..." or "name of someone...." is used..

    3) bias.... is the source bias? how can u tell? prove that the source is bias.. the source kept mentioning that this organisation do this and that... and give no evidence... and only talk about the good side of the other organisation...

    however, from what i have learnt... the other organisation also had done this and that...

    4) the purpose of the source... u can tell the purpose from the provenance, the content, the tone and many more... more are elaborated from the earlier post of other forumnites..

    5) cross reference... once again, u can use your own knowledge, or other source to prove or disprove the source is stating...

    NOTE: THE CROSS REFERENCE IS VERY VERY TRICKY    ... this is because the other source u are cross referencing can be telling lies... u can try proving the other source right, or match with ur contextual knowledge... but please do not go out of point and prove the other source instead proving ur original one...

    once again: i might be wrong..
