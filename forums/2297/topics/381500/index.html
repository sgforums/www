    1 What is the concentration of hydrogen ions in 0.05mol/dm3 sulfuric acid?

    I will present you my working. Not sure if I'm right, but please point out the error.

    H2SO4  ----> 2H+ + SO4 2-

    Assuming that volume of acid is 1 dm3,

    No. of mols of H2SO4 = concentration X vol

                                          = 0.05 X 1

                                          = 0.05 mols

    Comparing mole ratio, we know that no. of moles of H+ ions is 0.10 mol

    so concentration is 0.10 mol/dm3

    concentration in mol/dm3 = concentration of g/dm3 / relative Mr of substance

    concentration of H+ ions (in g/dm3) =  0.10 mol/dm3 X 1

                                                                 = 0.10 g/dm3 (final answer) - please verify

    the answer is correct, but not sure if my working is correct or not.

    I multiply by 1 since Ar of hydrogen is 1.
