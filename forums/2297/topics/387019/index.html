    Secondary 2 Maths question?

    Two quantities, x^2 and y, are in direct proportion. It is known that y = 60 for a particular value of x. Find the value of y when the value of x is doubled.

    y is directly proportional to x^2.

    Therefore, y = k x^2

    When y = 60,

    60 = k x^2                        - (1)

    When x is doubled,

    y = k (2x)^2

    y = 4k x^2                        - (2)

    Substitute your (1) into (2),

    y = 4(60) = 240

    It is just a simple simultaneous equation. Combine both to get your y value. Cheers!
