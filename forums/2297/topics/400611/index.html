    Dear Tanstan72,

    Geography, to me, is about understanding facts. In a geography exam, it does not matter whether how much the student writes but the quality of words written. A typical essay would contained serveral keypoints (between 5 ~ 7) about a certain topic. The crux in scoring in geography is to be able to convey clearly to the examiner that he/she knows the concept real well and able to reproduced the keypoints that the examiner is looking for.

    Instead of memorizing huge chunks of text, try asking your child to list down the 5 ~ 7 keypoints and use the 'linking' technique to link up those points. I.e. One keypoint links to another one.

    Regarding about tuition, I suggest you getting feedbacks from all tutors to look for signs of stress in your child. I myself is a tutor for many years and I have seen many cases where the child is simply too burn-out over the routinize day-after-day of tuition session without room to breathe.

    Your child math's and science's results have rooms for improvement, and encouragement should be given constantly. Instead of seeing the picture from the negative angle, I always suggest parents and my fellow tutors to view the picture 'positively' i.e. there are time for improvements and his results as of today is based on his pure effort, encouragements helps a long way!

    Firstly, IDENTIFY that pakistan is a LDC, MUST !

    Humans factors: River channelisation, river resectioning, embankments, and plantation and removal of vegetation.

    for each: 1 short liner of where it happen (Example) + 1 elaboration 

    State that river channelisation, resectioning, embankments are very expensive, certainly not a LDC will be able to afford

    And plantation and removal of vegetation, long term solution, but more cost effective, cheaper, LDC can afford

    And conclusion (VERY IMPORTANT TO SCORE A L3/8)

    Pakistan should use plantation and removal of vegetation, as it is more affordable

    But the real test of feasibility IS THE TEST OF TIME, 30 years later, the floodlands still protected, useful and feasible !
