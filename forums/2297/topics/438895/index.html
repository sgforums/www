    Turnitin is by no means perfect; after all some (original) basic sentences can be very common across many original works. Also, some common quotations and sayings (such as 'It's a small world after all') can't really be paraphrased without losing their meaning. These things can turn up as false positives during the plagiarism check.

    Just use your logic and know how to paraphrase ideas and concepts from your sources without copying them outright. Obviously you'll be asking for trouble if you copy full sentences and paragraphs without at least paraphrasing them.
