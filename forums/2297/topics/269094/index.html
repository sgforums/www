    Originally posted by starmoonsun:
    1) a unifrom capillary tube, closed at one end, contained air trapped by a thread of mercury 85mm long. when the tube was held horizontally, the length of the air column was 50mm. when it was held vertically with the closed end downwards, the length was 45mm. find the atm in Nm-2
    (take g=10, density of mercury = 14000kg/m^3)

    ans= 107000Pa
    Let atm = P1
    Volume of air trapped is proportional to length of air column

    When it is horizontal, only atm is involved
    when it is vertical, pressure of air = atm + pressure due to mercury column
    = P1 + 14000 * 10 * 0.085 (pgh)
    = P1 + 11900

    Hence, using P1V1 = P2V2 (Boyle's Law)
    P1 (50) = (P1 + 11900) (45)
    Solving the equation,

    P1 = 107000 Pa (3 sf)

    Originally posted by starmoonsun:
    2) A U-tube with 30cm of water is set up vertically and 12cm of olive oil is poured carefully into the left hand limb of the tube. it is observed that water will be pushed up in the other limb. the density of water and olive oil are 1000kg/m^3 and 920kg/m^3 respectively.
    A further 6cm of olive oil is added into the left hand limb. how much further will the water level rise in the right limb?

    ans = 2.76mm
    Check if the answer is 2.76 cm, not 2.76 mm. I'm rather sure it is 2.76 cm.

    Soln:

    U can draw a U tube, left side with oil and water, right side with water

    let the height of water at the left side = x cm
    thus the height of water at the right side = 30 - x cm

    Also, height of oil at the left side = 12 cm

    Using pgh,

    pressure at the bottom due on left side = 12 (920) + x (1000)
    pressure at the bottom due on right side = (30 - x) (1000)

    Equating both sides of the pressure, x = 9.48 cm

    Repeat the above for heigh of oil = 12 + 6 = 18cm
    you will get a new x of 6.72 cm

    Hence, the difference = 2.76 cm => the water level rose by 2.76 cm on the other side.

    --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Alternatively, you can use this method if you understand the concept

    when 6 cm of oil is added onto one side, the extra pressure will have to be balanced out; i.e. the pressure on the left side (oil side) will have to decrease by 3cm of oil, whereas the pressure on the right side will have to increase by 3cm of oil.

    height of water level that is equal to pressure due to 3cm of oil
    = (3 * 10 * 920) / (10 * 1000)
    = 2.76 cm

    Question:

    4) A hot water tank of heat capacity 6000J/K contains 120kg of water at 15 degrees celsius. what is the time taken to raise the temp of water to 50 degrees celsius using a heater coil of power of 2500W, given that the specific heat capacity of water is 4200 K/kg/K?
    ans = 7140s.

    Answer:

    Q = mct where t is the rise in temperature, so

    amount of energy needed for the rise in temperature of the water only, Q = (120)(4200)(50 - 15) = 17640000 J

    amount of energy needed for the rise in temperature of the tank only, Q = (6000)(50 - 15) = 210000J

    total energy required to be supplied by heater coil = 17640000 + 210000 = 17850000J

    since power = energy/time, time needed = energy/power = 17850000/2500 = 7140s (ANS)
