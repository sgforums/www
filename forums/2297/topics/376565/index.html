    Meaning

    illuminated object -  paper <---- >  lens  <------------> mirror

    <-no distance-                 >     12                  20

    mirror is replaced with white screen and illuminated object is placed 24cm away from converging lens.

    Q: how far should the white screen be placed from the converging lends such that a clear focused image can be seen on it

    (  ans is 24)furthermore, it stated that Focal point is 12cm . ( how derive that ?)

    Qb : upper half of lens covered with black cloth, stage how image is affected  ?

    my ans: only half of image formed.

    correct ans: image not as bright.

    how can ?  the light ray canot even pass thru the cloth how form the upper half of image?

    Key concepts to know: Parallel rays will converge at the focal point

    What happened: light travels from object through lens, reflected at mirror, go past lens again, and produce image at same point.

    Only way it is possible, object was at focal point, so go past lens ==> parallel rays, reflect back as parallel rays, then converge at focal point again. So focal length is 12 cm

    Then recall your different scenarios. Objects placed at 2 times focal length (2f) will produce an image at 2f. Hence, screen should be put at 24 cm

     

     

    Part b is a very common question.

    Image is less bright because light rays travel in all directions, and will still reach the lens... Any rays from any portion of the object will still be able to reach the lens, and converge to form the image on the screen
