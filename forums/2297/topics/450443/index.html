    I believe what your friend meant by opening up more is that you need to link further(you can use the who what when where why how questions to help you), for example:

    Fast food restaurants

    • What are the major restaurants? eg. mcdonald, long john silver, kfc, etc
    • How they manage to cook so fast? eg. deep frying, using frozen/process food
    • They share common cooking methods like the above.
      From here you can expand the above informations, like:
      Deep frying is bad for health due to the excessive use of oil in cooking, during cooking, the food soak up the oil, etc....
      Again, you see the keywords coming out(eg. oil), then you can continue opening up more things to say:
      Oil are divided into many types, such as butter, vegetable oil etc. It is also known as fats, fats have adv/disadv trans fats are bad for health
      What are trans fats? then exlpain again in your research.
    • Cooking methods, which are healthy/unhealthy?

    People of all ages

    • Diff age group need different nutritient levels, you can take a sample of mcdonald's nutrition facts label, and then since mcdonald deep fry things, you can take a nutrition fact label of kfc and compare here and there.
    • List wad nutrients diff age group need. Like teenagers need to eat more of _____ than elderlies.
      Again, take another fast food resturant's nutritient fact label and see if it meets people of all ages(even if they do, just say they lack a bit of calcium/vitamin A  etc)
