  • UltimaOnline
    14 Jan 15, 22:08
    Originally posted by Cheesesticks:

    Also I'd now like to ask for tips regarding subject combinations? I'm planning to persue law in uni and heard a wide variety of subjects is ideal, so i'm planning to take hybid in jc (h2 econs lit math h1 chem), However I've heard that it's extremely taxing! Slightly worried atm 


    For subjects like Chemistry, since it makes a big difference as to which Uni courses you can take (ie. almost all Uni Science courses require H2 Chemistry), it's advisable to take it at H2. H1 Chem is useless as a Uni prerequisite, it's just a filler for your Uni Admission Score.

    For subjects like Econs, since it makes no difference as to which Uni courses you can take, it's advisable to take it at H1 (and free the H2 slot for subjects like Chem). You don't even need to take Econs at H1 level (let alone H2 level), to be able to study it at Uni level.

    Of course, if you happen to (although I can't imagine why) much prefer Econs over Chem, then go ahead and take H2 Econs and H1 Chem (or better yet, take some other subject you prefer... Chem is one of those either-take-at-H2-or-might-as-well-don't-take-at-all subject).
