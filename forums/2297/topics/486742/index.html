    Hello,  I am also taking O level this year. Honestly speaking, I also find that O level English this year was quite unusually odd. I also have the same goal as yours - to pursue to JC next year. Because I was sec 4 NA last year, I also took O level English last year and I obtained a D7. Thus, I retake English this year as Sec 5. I was also quite depressed for the moment that almost affected other O level exams. In my humble opinion, these are the following that you should/should not do :

    1) Don't make assumption on grades. How do you know that your L1R5 is 15 pts? Just simply because you NEVER KNOW how English and others are going to be marked. This also comes in on how the nation-wide students perform (bell curve). Based on what I concern, quite majority of people saying O level English is not that easy. So YOU are not alone, we are on the same boat.

    2) What really matters is something that is currently still under-controlled. Don't because of languages that affect your performance on other subjects. Strive for other subjects to score them! At least you will have no regrets after O levels that you should have had done better.

    3) Be confident in yourself! Don't keep saying that you are "not in a good situation." You can make use of this opportunity to score others well to pull up your aggregrate points. You may expect something unexpected to happen - your English may be just up to par! You may be the top student! Just simply because you NEVER KNOW.

    4) Determination! Keep continuing to pursue our goals (since we have the common goal). The world is at your feet always! All the best for your upcoming Os.
