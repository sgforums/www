    I currently studying in Sec 4 and is taking my O Levels this year. I am taking A maths as one of my subjects and is struggling with it since last year. I have already done seven chapters for a maths and find that the concepts and workings in the questions are very confusing and hard to understand, especially the topics of surds and indices, logarithms and binomial theorem. Many of my classmates are also struggling in the subject and most of them have failed the end-of-year examinations last year. The problem is that a maths is a compulsory subject in my school that all students must study from sec 3 and that the subject is very unpopular among the students in the school. When I asked some of my classmates who failed a maths about the matter, they mentioned things such as "a maths sucks", "I hate a maths" and "A maths makes me have headaches". I am currently considering to drop a maths. I think that the school should not make a maths compulsory and should instead offer subjects such as literature, principle of accounts and design and technology. In conclusion, a maths should not be made a compulsory subject to all students and that they should be allowed to choose from at least two subjects (e.g. AM and POA), especially for the students who do not like mathematics. Do you agree? Tell me your views.

    I believe compulsory or not depends on the school

    I was very weak in maths since secondary 2.
    Because of this i was not allowed to take A maths. The reason given was that it will be too hard for us(everyone who failed E sec 2 maths) to cope.

    Eventually i took engineering maths and Higher Engineering maths in poly. It wasn't so hard after all. Engineering maths is very similar to A maths.

    If you decide to drop A maths, make sure your E maths is good. Be very familiar with complicated fractions with algebra, how to make subject of the formula, trigo, factorising, expanding, simplifying equationsetc.

    If you plan to study engineering, please take A maths. Because there are lots of equations used to represent behaviours of circuit diagrams, and most of which would need some basic understanding of A maths.

    Engineering maths covers things like: Differentiation/Integration, indices, the waveforms(y=mx+c, sin/cos, pi, etc), statistics, probability, complex numbers, matrices(i think e maths cover some basics), taylor series, fourier series.
