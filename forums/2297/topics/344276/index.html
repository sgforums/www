    As Vendettus said, and to add on, 1) Only if you get A1 for combined science, then they'll allow you to take Science subjects at H2 level. 2) If you want to take Bio, you should take Chem as well. 3) If you take any science subjects, they should all be at H2 level. H1 level science is useless as background prerequisite for Uni admission. 4) Chemistry (of the 3 sciences) is the most required background subject for applying into Science University courses. 5) Chemistry is the most enjoyable subject at JC level, definitely more enjoyable than Biology (which is a pity, Biology is interesting on its own, but at H2/H1 syllabus in Sg JCs, you need to memorize way too much, and usually without proper understanding, appreciation or enjoyment).

    Originally Posted by crucifiedx (on SgClub) >>> but if i go for the arts stream, are the courses in uni quite imited? <<< I replied : It of couse depends on what courses in the Uni you're interested in. Regarding the statement that "the arts stream in jc quite useless" compared to science, it's only partially true in the sense that in addition to the 'A' levels grade points (akin to L1R5 at 'O' levels) entry requirements, there is also subjects prior background prerequisite for particular courses. Eg. to study Medicine at Uni, you need H2 Chem, and either H2 Physics or H2 Biology. And generally, science courses in Uni are stricter and more demanding in their subjects prior background prerequisite, as compared to arts courses in the Uni. So you have to be clear on whether you want to go for Science or Arts in the Uni. If Science, you must go to Sci stream in JC now. If Arts, then you could go either Arts or Science stream in JC (but again this depends on exactly which Arts course in Uni you're interested in. Check out requirements in NUS website).
