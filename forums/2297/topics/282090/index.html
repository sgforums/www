    Originally posted by Deadly:
    A boy at the bottom of a 150m high cliff throws a tennis ball vertically upwards. A boy at the very top of the cliff drops a golf ball so that it hits the tennis ball while bot hballs are still in the air. The acceleration of both balls can be assumed to be 10ms^-2 downwards.

    a. With what speed is the tennis ball thrown so that the golf ball strikes it at the maximum height of the path?

    b. What is the position of the tennis ball when the golf ball strikes it?
    For tennis ball,
    the boy throws it upwards.

    s = ut + 1/2 at^2
    a = -10 if I am taking upward as positive.

    s = ut - 5t^2
    If you have drawn the displacement vs time graph of the tennis ball, it will be a curve, or frowning curve. The maximum height of the path of tennis ball implies the stationary point of the curve in this case.

    Hence,
    ds/dt = u - 10t
    when ds/dt = 0,
    u - 10t = 0
    u = 10t where u represents the initial speed of tennis ball.

    sub u =10t into s = ut - 5t^2
    s = 10t^2 - 5t^2
    = 5t^2

    This means that at v = 0 which is the maximum height, displacement travelled by the ball upwards = 5t^2.

    For golf ball,
    the boy drops it.
    u = 0
    a = 10
    s = ut + 1/2 at^2
    In this case, s is the displacement travelled by the golf ball taking downwards to be positive.

    s = 1/2(10)(t^2)
    = 5t^2
    This means that at time t seconds, the golf ball will travel 5t^2 downwards.

    Since the balls clash at this point, assuming that the diameter of the ball is negligible,

    5t^2 + 5t^2 = 150
    10t^2 = 150
    t = root(15)

    Initial speed of tennis ball = 10t
    = 10root(15)

    Position of tennis ball = 5t^2
    = 5(15)
    = 75 m
