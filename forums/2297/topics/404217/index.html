    The grades of H1 subjects are worth half as much as the grades of H2 subjects when calculating university admission points.

     

    The content of H1 subjects are half of that of H2 subjects, but of equal difficulty.

     

    GP and 2nd language are H1 subjects. Science subjects can be H2 or H1.

     

    In JCs (ie. school candidates) you must study a contrasting subject (eg. econs is a contrasting subject for science students), which can be either H1 or H2.

     

    Most students choose (or their JCs only offer such subject combinations) to have their contrasting subject as their H1 subject.

     

    You can download the syllabuses (both H2 and H1) for all the subjects here :

    http://www.seab.gov.sg/aLevel/syllabus/schoolCandidates/2011_GCE_A.html
