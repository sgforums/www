    No. of days required for 50 workers to build 20 chairs = 8
    No. of days required for 50 workers to build 10 chairs = 4 (since workload is halved)

    => No. of days required for 10 workers to build 10 chairs = 4 x 5 = 20

    I have another method but as usual slightly longer than eagle one.

    My method is to use productivity to solve it.

    Let a be the productivity of A
    Let b be the productivity of B.
    Let c be the productivity of C.

    (a + b)(10) = 1 (1 job so equal to 1)
    (b + c)(12) = 1
    (a + c)(15) = 1

    a + b = 1/10 --------------(1)
    b + c = 1/12 ---------------(2)
    a + c = 1/15 ---------------(3)

    Taking (1) - (2)

    a + b - b - c = 1/10 - 1/12
    a - c = 1/60 ----------------- (4)

    (1) + (4)
    2a = 1/15 + 1/60
    2a = 1/12
    a = 1/24

    From (1)
    b = 1/10 - 1/24
    = 7/120

    From (3)
    c = 1/40

    Let the No of days that A and B and C work together to complete the job be d.

    (a + b + c)(d) = 1
    (1/24 + 7/120 + 1/40)(d) = 1
    (1 /8 )(d) = 1

    d = 8
