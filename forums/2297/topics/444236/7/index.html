    hi guys just wanna share some stuff with u.

    i just did my a lvl last yr (2011) and i just wanna share my experience.

    firstly, try to choose a JC ( if you're planning to) that is near to your house.

    trust me, it's beneficial, because of all the late nights u will be up studying for ur tests and rushing thru ur tutorials, you don't wanna be late for school. you'll be so freaking tired that u cant even stay awake during national anthem. i have friends who are frequent late-comers and got themselves into UNNECESSARY trouble. im sure you wouldn't want that when you are so stressed up trying to understand what kinematics is or memorising the endless stack of protein chemistry notes. ( you'll know what they are when u enter jc )

    secondly, its all about your attitude. i had a friend who didn't take a maths, was from combined sci stream and he was very motivated and focused. initially we all looked down on him as he was an appeal student (thru cca)  but we were all wrong. my jc had this bridging course for those who didn't take a maths/pure sciences during secondary sch and he attended all lectures punctually without fail. true enough, he outshone all of us and we didn't know where to hide our faces. in my opinion, just get into a jc that u like, be motivated and determined to do well. most importantly DONT GIVE UP HALFWAY.

    and mjc's retain rate is not that exagerrating anymore. if i remember correctly, to be promoted to j2, u gotta pass gp, pass 1 h2 subject and something else i can't rmb. to me i think it's reasonable and achievable if u stay focussed. they are not so unreasonable as now they try to push u up to j2 so don't worry. just do your best. the school will do their part, and u have to do your part also.

    these are just the tip of the ice-berg from my experience during my jc life. wish u guys all the best in ur pre-u/tertiary education.
