  • UltimaOnline
    13 Jan 10, 13:08

    It appears many students are having a similar dilemma.

     

    On another forum thread, I just posted :

     

    The less time you spend travelling, the more time you have for your homework, study and for yourself. Besides, you'd have less risk of being late (eg. traffic jam) during the crucial exam periods.

    There is also another 'advantage' of going to a JC that's significantly 'below' your personal academic performance (as indicated by your L1R5), would you rather be the 'worst of the best' or the 'best of the worst'? (or put in another way, 'small fish in a big pond' or 'big fish in a small pond'?). Which would be more self-encouraging for you?

    Ultimately, it's all about personal self-motivation and self-discipline. Yes environment matters, but nowhere as much as personal self-motivation and self-discipline matters.

     

    Go for the JC that's nearer your home. Time is a luxury you can't afford to waste during your 2 short JC years.
