    Practice practice and practice. I would suggest go with the topical mastery before attempting other school's papers.

    Topical mastery is a type of revision where you solely focus on the topic that you're doing. Practice questions from the same topic and try to master the art of recording the entries. Once you understood the fundamental of that topic, you may now start to proceed attempting questions from the same topic from other school prelim paper.

    I have one POA teacher that paistakingly compiled 10 prelim papers from 10 school, and take her precious time to catergorized all questions I.e. if we were told to practice on Bank reconciliation, we will know which schoool and which questions to look for in the stack of paper.

    If you want to do well, you will need to attain mastery at each topic before proceeding to the next.
