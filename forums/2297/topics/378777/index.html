    (i) [HCl] = [CH3N2], since : 1) 25cm3 of base required 25cm3 of acid for equivalence point. 2) HCl is monoprotic and CH3N2 is monobasic. (ii) Kb = ( [OH-] x [conjugate acid] ) / [conjugate base] Since at initial, [OH-] = [conjugate acid], you get Kb = [OH-]^2 / [conjugate base] Hence you can find [OH-], hence pOH, hence pH. (iii) pOH = pKb for maximum buffer capacity. Notice that the question was much too generous in reminding you it was "a solution of maximum bufferring capacity". It was actually sufficient to simply ask, "Calculate the pH of the solution obtained when 12.5cm3 of HCl was added during the titration". Comments : This question is too easy for 'A' levels exam standard (and your Promos, and your Prelims, etc). Expect much tougher questions for the actual 'A' levels (especially considering the recent trend that papers are getting tougher every year - the 2008 exam papers for Chemistry was much tougher than the 2007 exam papers. Brace yourself for 2009 and 2010).
