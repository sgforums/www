    When will people learn? It's fallacious to say "MMA vs (a certain martial art)". Because MMA *isn't* a martial art, it's the *application* of *multiple* martial arts. So if say, Donnie Yen, were to fight in an MMA match, he would use a mix of the dozen or so martial arts that he's proficient in, including Wing Chun and Tai Ji (recall last month's "Tai Ji versus MMA" nonsense?), and of course, BJJ as well. That's point #1.

    And point #2, one-vs-one without weapons, BJJ is the ultimate martial arts (but quite useless if it's many-vs-one, Aikido would be a lot more useful for that). So it's correct to say that BJJ would beat Tai Ji or Wing Chun one-vs-one, but it's still nonsense to say it's "MMA versus Tai Ji or Wing Chun", coz MMA isn't a martial art, but a mix of multiple martial arts. So there.

    A polyneuropathy that involves shooting pains, numbness, and tingling in the hands or feet may develop. A neurologic syndrome called Lyme encephalopathy is associated with subtle cognitive difficulties, insomnia, a general sense of feeling unwell, and changes in personality. Chronic encephalomyelitis, which may be progressive, can involve cognitive impairment, brain fog, migraines, balance issues, weakness in the legs, awkward gait, facial palsy, bladder problems, vertigo, and back pain. In rare cases, untreated Lyme disease may cause frank psychosis, which has been misdiagnosed as schizophrenia or bipolar disorder.
