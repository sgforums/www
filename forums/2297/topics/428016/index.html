    Because H3 subjects cannot be used for calculating your Uni admission score, hence for the vast majority of JC students, H3 subjects are a waste of time. If you already find it hard to score As with your current H2 subjects, then taking up H3 subjects (which will be additionally time-consuming) will be an unwise act of self-sabotage.

     

    Only if you're already supremely confident of scoring all As (distinctions) for ALL your H2 subjects, then you can considering taking H3 subjects, which (assuming that you continue to score straight As in ALL subjects even after taking up the time-consuming H3 subjects), may help boost your Curriculum Vitae, and indirectly your chances of entering into top Uni courses and/or scholarship opportunities.
