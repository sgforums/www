    You don't have to register at ITE (or at any school at all, for that matter) to take O levels (or A levels, if you wish) as a private candidate. You can self-study (of if you can afford it, with private tuition to help you) for all of these subjects, if you don't wish to register as an ITE or some other private school candidate. There are, of course, pros and cons regarding taking O levels at ITE (or at any private school). It's up to you. And there's no age limit for taking O levels or A levels as a private candidate, no worries, whether legally (by MOE) or otherwise (you don't need to world to approve of, or agree with, or believe in you; you only need yourself for this).
