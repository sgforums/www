    Regards to the syllabus, almost every year there are minor (nothing to bother about) revisions to the syllabus and exam paper formats. The last major change was the Planning Qn for Paper 2.

    You can find out the answer to your own question, by comparing the 2012 and 2013 syllabuses :
    http://www.seab.gov.sg/aLevel/syllabusSchool_Info.html

    What should be more of a concern (to teachers and tutors, as well as to future cohorts of 'A' level students), is not the syllabus change, but the deliberate (and unannounced) increase in difficulty level of the H2 Chemistry exam papers (year on year), as well as the rumoured decrease in number of 'A' grades to be handed out from 2012 'A' levels onwards.

    See my post here :
    http://sgforums.com/forums/2297/topics/448684?page=1#posts-10535064
