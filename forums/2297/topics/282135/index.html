    For acceleration vs time graph, assume that the air resistance is directly proportional to the velocity of the feather.

    Air resistance is directly proportional to the velocity of the object. Imagine, as the feather is increasing speed due to the presence of gravitational force acting on it. However, in the presence of still air, as velocity of the feather increases, air resistance also increases. Hence, the resultant force decreases.
    Rmb F = ma. Thus, the resulting acceleration of the feather decreases until the resultant force is 0. In that case, I can say that the feather have attained terminal velocity. At this state, the gravitational force acting on the feather is the same as its air resistance.

    basically as the feather/object falls, 2 forces act on it. taking away from the planet as positive, the mass+ the gravitational attraction causes a decceleration force of F1=mg.
    the 2nd force is that of air resistance/friction which causes acceleration. that is related to the velocity of the feather/object by the equation F2 = -kv or -kv^2

    and the acceleration is determined by the resultant of the 2 forces. therefore as |v| (speed) increases, the F2 and the acceleration increases until equilibirum.
