    You can consider the following three suggestions (amongst many other possibilities) :

    1) Negotiate with your HOD and Principal to let you self study (with private tuition) Physics, and register for O level Physics as a school candidate (ie. taking the O level exam at the same time as all your other subjects).

    If the above fails, then :

    2) Register as a private candidate for O level Physics (preferably Pure, and preferably together with Pure Chemistry), and take it one year after your O levels as a school candidate (since you're not allowed to simultaneously be a school candidate and a private candidate in the same year).

    This option might appear to be a waste of 1 year (and hence most students won't even consider it), but hey, better to waste just 1 year, than to waste the rest of your life (studying and basing your career on a subject you don't enjoy).

    3) Transfer to another school right now (ie. mid-term), even if it means a 'lousier' school, as long as the new school allows you to take Physics. If no school will accept you mid-term, then you have no choice but to wait 1 more year and reapply to another school (or ur existing school?) only at the end of 2014, and repeat Sec 3.

    If you don't like any of these three suggestions, you could wait for other pple to advise you with other suggestions, or think of something else yourself.

    Hi,


    This is my maiden post in SGForums Homework Section. I thought I'll give a bit of background before answering your question.  I obtained a Bachelor's Degree from NUS back in 2005, and also advanced degrees from other institutions. 

    Your opening post tells me that you're indecisive; you wanted to pursue arts, then animation & design, and now engineering. I understand that most of us do not know what we want when we're young, but the frequency which you changed your mind is alarming. 

    Please, for the sake of yourself, keep your options open. Don't narrow down your options at such a young age.

    I encourage you to take Physics as a private candidate. It is essential for entry into Engineering. That said, Mathematics *IS* even more crucial. 

    The main question is not "How" to take Physics, but whether you *should* do it. Engineering is not an easy course. It requires good foundation in Mathematics and Physics. If you want to go into it, please be prepared to brush up your foundation and work hard in the future.
