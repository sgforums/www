    Hi,

    Although everyone is different, let me share my story in JC. Perhaps my J1 wasn't as filled up as you, but by the time I was J2, I'm pretty sure the immense stress I had was incomparable.

    In J1, I had 4 CCAs that time. In J2, I had to drop 1, and still have 3 CCAs. In addition to SYF, Maths Competition and Chess compeititon, I was in the national training team for Physics Olympiad. Not only that, during those days of S papers, I had extra lessons for both Physics S and Maths S. It wasn't uncommon for me to reach home past 9~10 pm everyday. Those were the days where my JC was a Buona Vista and I too stayed at Hougang. You can imagine the time I took back and forth school. I even had to tell my econs teacher I simply don't have the time to do her essay homework, and requested for an extension of a few months. Fortunately, she was understanding and allowed that.

    Coupled on to that, going from a "monk" school to a mixed school, I had girl problems as well.

    Looking back, I have to say that it was the best training of my life, that is, to handle the extreme stress with limited time. Stress management, time management skills were taxed to the max. And on hindsight, I have to say it serves me very well now.

    Sure, it is a big transition from secondary school to fast paced stressful JC life, but you have to learn to adapt, learn to manage it, learn to take it as it comes. That's what separates the wildly successful from the rest. I do know how stressful it is in JCs nowadays, but trust me, it wasn't any better in the past as well. So many people have been there, done it. You will be able to do it as well.

    It's only a short two years. Look and focus on the final goal: at the end of two years, you get a cert from TJC on top of your A level cert. And when you finally get your certs and As after all your hard work, isn't the success all the sweeter?

    Ganbatte!
