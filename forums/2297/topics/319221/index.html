  • Rhonda

    I think you're probably nervous, that's why you make these mistakes. 

    What level are you at?  Always read the question slowly and a few times, then break it up into the parts you need to tackle, and quickly scribble a quick outline of points that you will include to address the question(s). 

    And then as you write your essay, tick off those points.

    And ALWAYS keep a time-check!  Right before you enter the exam hall, you'd already know how many questions you're expected to answer, etc.  Allocate a certain amount for each section, each essay, and try to observe the time limits.  I used to remove my wrist watch and place it right at the front of my desk so I can tell if I need to speed up or not.

    Good luck!
