    Hi,

      More often, teachers develop questions to assess

      1. the students' ability to apply the usual mathematical concepts and skills;
      (see the checklists in http://www.sgforums.com/forums/2297/topics/356377)

      2. the students' ability to translate descriptions into mathematical expressions and relationships;
      (read about interpreting sentences in statistics in http://www.freewebs.com/weews/interpretingstatistics.htm)

      3. the students' ability to apply critical thinking in unseen (atypical) questions.
      (read about generalisations in http://www.freewebs.com/weews/ongeneralisations.htm)

    Hi,

      Sometimes, questions may be set to check if students appreciate the finer points of concepts.

      Let's look at an example from complex numbers:

      Verify that z = i is a root of the equation z^3 + (1 - 3i)z^2 - (2 + 3i)z - 2 = 0. Determine the other roots.

      In the second part of the question, the student may unwittingly start with z = -i being one of the other roots, without realising the fact that conjugate roots only occur in polynomial equations with real coefficients.
