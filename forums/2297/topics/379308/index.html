Studying Blues, anyone?

  • Audi
    19 Oct 09, 21:44

    The O level's approaching and I'm sure many of you here are frantically preparing for this major exam. Well, I must confess, I pretty much like studying and don't feel it's a chore since I've been a pretty much consistent worker in my 4 years of sec school. But has anyone of you ever "spaced out" in the midst of studying and wonder to yourself,"why am I studying so hard?"

    The realistic answer, to have a good future, of course. Then I'd ponder again, when I grow up, I merely want to get myself involved in things like humanitarian organizations, set up a tuition centre(maybe),  and do something i really like. My peers all see me as the great worker, who tries to strike a balance between hanging out with them and working real hard at school work at the same time. Of course, I do get quite remarkable grades in school. However sometimes I really want to "drop" all this studying obligations, go do something that is meaningful to me. No, I don't despise people who like studying because I love that myself. But I'm not very obsessed with that. I just enjoy learning, revising and of course, I must admit, there's a sense of personal glory. Till now, the main reason I can think of why I should still study is to secure a good future... a reason which I am not convinced of. I mean, studying is not just to get a good future, but reasons which I do not know of.

    Have you all ever felt this way? Haahaa, i know this's a weird question to ask. Best of luck for all your O's!
