    The term "waste gases" here refer to the fact that gases that must be allowed to leave due to thermal expansion and pressure differences - bear in mind that new air is constantly being pumped into blast furnace because additional oxygen and carbon dioxide is continuously required for this industrial process (to react with C to generate CO). In which case, the "waste gases" that must be allowed to leave naturally includes N2, CO and CO2 (and naturally some, but relatively little, of unreacted oxygen, H2O vapour, noble gases, oxides of nitrogen, oxides of sulfur, etc etc), even though these may not be "wastes" in the sense of the word. So, as far as this particular question is concerned (two additional relevant points : 'O' level questions are simplified, so it's far easier to simply ask the student summarily thusly, rather than an essay discussion on every subpoint; even Cambridge exam questions may be argued to have some aspects of flaw or fault in regards to interpretation), then the best answer would indeed be : CO, CO2 and N2. Although one might argue that oxides of nitrogen and sulfur are more polluting than N2, but these are not present in significant amounts. Bottomline : the question isn't actually asking "what are the waste gases from the process occurring in the blast furnance?" but is asking "what are the main chemical gases present in the waste gases released from the blast furnace?" See the difference?
