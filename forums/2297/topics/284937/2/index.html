    Exothermic reaction = heat is given out to surroundings

    This means that the internal energy of the products decreases but the temperature of the product increases if the energy given out is converted to heat.

    Outcome: Temperature of product > temperature of reactants
    Total internal energy of product < Total internal energy of reactants.

    Endothermic reaction = heat taken in from surroundings.
    The process reverses.
    Outcome: Temperature of product < temperature of reactants
    Total internal energy of product > Total internal energy of reactants.

    http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internal_energy
    Definition of internal energy

    Please don't confuse endothermic reactions by associating heat gained as in heat gained by the product so temperature of the product increases.
    Heat is actually taken in from the surroundings to increase the internal energy of the product.
