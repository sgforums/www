    4 Mar 15, 10:48
    Originally posted by Newcomer99:

    Hi! Does anyone know if our citizenship status will affect our chances of getting into a course? And also, I got As for my 4H2s (BCME) and E for my gp, A for pw. Do I still stand a chance for NUS pharmacy?? >< Thanks!! 


    Citizenship slightly affects your chances, in that there is a quota for Singaporeans vs Foreigners for each Uni course, and/or for each Uni. The Unis won't divulge the quotas, however. And the exact quotas may differ from Uni to Uni, and from course to course.

    Yes, you've a chance (albeit a small one) for NUS Pharmacy (unlike say, Law, which has strict GP requirements). But you'll be required to take a Qualifying English Test, if admitted into NUS.

    http://www.askadmissions.nus.edu.sg/app/answers/detail/a_id/24/~/failed-general-paper
