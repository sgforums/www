    Hi TS,

    speaking from an ex-GEP background, both Eagle and weews are right. I scored only 4A1s out of 9 Os' and only 1 A out of 4 A level subjects. But in other aspects like articulation or creativity, I may have an edge over my peers, honestly. Weews says it well that its more important to let your kid enjoy learning. GEP is overrated somehow.

    to answer your question, GEP needs to be prepared. But not in the way you would think... not in the mugging way. You can't -study- for the GEP exam a few months before, and you shouldn't. But I guess the following would help:

    -parents leading by example by actively learning

    -providing many interesting books to the child

    -encourage the kid to question

    etc.
