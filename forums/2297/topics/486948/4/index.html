    Regarding calculation answers, as mentioned earlier, some JCs and some other private tutors will probably make the full solutions available online sometime. Don't worry about calculation answers. As long as you showed clear working, Cambridge will give you some marks even if you made careless mistakes.

    "For peroxyethanoic acid I think perhaps the only difference being an additional O makes the distance to C=O further hence the dispersal of the negative charge conjugate base occurs to a lesser extent. Only difference and possible reason I guessed."

    No. As mentioned in a preceding post in this thread, the correct reason for the lower acidity of peroxycarboxylic acid, is that unlike carboxylic acid, the peroxycarboxylate conjugate base *cannot* be stabilized by having it's negative formal charge delocalized by resonance over 2 O atoms (as is the case with regular carboxylate conjugate bases). Stabilization by resonance delocalization of charges require conjugation, ie. sideways overlapping of unhybridized p orbitals, which is not present due to a sp3 O atom between the O- atom and the sp2 C atom.
