    For both Moonwalkers and Rynal, the correct answer for the enzyme catalyzed graph is :


    Moonwalkers :

    Q1 & 2 ) You'll get some marks, not full marks, but you'll get some marks. Exactly how many marks is impossible to say without looking at your exact submitted answer. But don't worry too much about this. Focus on your P1.

    Q3) Your equations are correct, but (assuming what you submitted is what you posted) you lose marks for the following errors :
    It's HI that's a stronger reducing agent than HCl, and not "I is a stronger reducing agent than Cl".
    "The statement H2SO4 is used against I2, then it will be oxidised to HI, and further oxidised to I2 again which would contaminate HI" contains 2 errors :
    firstly, the question describes H2SO4 being reacted with NaI, not I2; secondly, if I2 is converted to HI, then I2 is reduced to HI, not oxidized.

    Q4) To be technically accurate, it should be an additional electronegative, inductively electron-withdrawing "O atom" rather than "OH group". Afterall the H is present in both conjugate bases, it is the 2nd O atom which is present in HOO- but not in HO-. Cambridge may or may not award you a mark for this.
