    Just an experience to share.

    I was giving tuition during my undergrad days.

    There was a particular family which I remembered till now who was pretty nasty.

    They were a reputable family with a certain stand in society.

    But, they chose to default on a 300 bucks tuition fees which really surprised and kinda make my blood boil.


    I tried calling them, but they told me straight that they are not going to pay, I asked how could they do that, they told me how could I be a debt collector and collect from them, my own tuition fees?

     

    My mum even helped to call them, but their reply was bring me to the court if you can, I have the money and time to play with you. I have money to play, and to pay as well, but I just don't want to pay the tutor, what can you do?

     

    Besides, there are tutors out there who really does charge fees at a lower rate.

    I used to charge lower rates to students with financial difficulties; in addition to free books, materials, stationery, meals etc.

    Now, after grad, I turned to volunteering work.

    Ultimately, every teacher/tutor does wish to see improvements from their students, it's a satisfaction most will have.

    Fees/money maybe important, but a lower fee just rep that maybe we can tune down our lifestyle slightly.

    But all in all, it still depends on the tutor himself/herself to see how much he/she is willing to give.
