TS is still young and still has 6 long years to change his course and my best advice is to wake up and work hard.

    It is not that medical school is elitist and it only takes in students from top Junior Colleges and fine academic backgrounds. The selection process is extremely rigorous and mentally draining because this is what is expected of the profession. They are looking for people who have the mental capacity to withstand five long years of intense studying amongst many other qualities and hence academic record will be your first hurdle. Don't be fooled by thinking that you can get in as long as you have perfect or excellent A level results. Truth is everyone who applies will have a perfect or excellent A level results and each year only a small percentage gets through. The minute you apply, you are pitting yourself against the best in this country.

    Do think through it carefully because as said once you decide on this course your field of study becomes very specific. The life of a Doctor is not as glamorous, decorated or high living as you think.
