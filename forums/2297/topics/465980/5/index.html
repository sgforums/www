    Evening Donating blood is good. You can go to HSA to donate blood. Before blood donation, they will give some questionaires to answer. Here is what happens during blood donation: 1) They (the nurse) will give you a round object. This is to apply additional pressure if the blood flow is not enough. 2) They will use a cotton swab to swab the phlebotomy site (site of puncture), in this case, one of the veins on your arm. 3) They take out a blood collection set, place the collection bag onto a scale. 4) They insert the needle into your vein. At the same time, they will activate the scale. This scale is to measure the amount of blood being collected. Typically the amount of blood being drawn from your body is 10% of your body weight, no more than that. If not, you will faint. 6) After the amount required is collected, the scale stops and the nurses will come and remove the needle from your arm. 7) The phlebotomy site will be closed off with a cotton swab and a handyplast over it. 8) End of blood donation. The nurse will give you an armband and a meal voucher. The armband design is very nice and the voucher entitles you to free refreshments at the cafe next to it. Yup, if i remember anything wrong, please kindly correct me.
