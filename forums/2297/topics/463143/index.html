  • Lee.mingcheng
    31 Dec 12, 17:54
    The road will be very tough, considering you have dropped out of 4NA, but it's not impossible. :). You'll have to dedicate around 8 hours a day of revising at home and you gradually build up the hours (say, 10hrs 2 months prior to the o's). But it is much better if you go to a private school because you'll need some tips and notes to take on some trickier questions and if you solely rely on yourself, you might not be exposed to the curriculum fully, hence, I strongly disagree to self-studying. For science and math, the best advice is to keep doing the TYS because they always repeat exact questions (This year's Bio paper had more than 5 repeated MCQ questions). For Math, repeatedly doing the TYS is a good way to ensure at least an A2, you'll need to be extra careful to get an A1. I don't think it is very possible to get an A1 for Combined Humanities without a teacher's help as when the teacher marks your essay, he/she'll will be able to point out where you are lacking and if you self-study, you'll have no chance of identifying where the loopholes are in your learning. All in all, I believe it's best you still go to school, a private one at least.
