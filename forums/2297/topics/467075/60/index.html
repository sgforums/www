    "Symposium" and "Conference" are 2 words that have confused (and continue to confuse) many people due to their mutual interchangeability. I may be Science person, but I'm a Science person who has an understanding and appreciation for many other fields, including Language. The meaning and purpose of many words (in all languages, including English) evolve over time, based on their usage by society at any given point in time. As such, I would say "Symposium" is now best understood to be a subset of "Conference". The latter can be of any type (eg. business), while the former refers specifically to academic. If you're not sure which to use in a particular context, use the more generic term. This (playing it safe by using a more generic term) is generally recommended, including for instance, within H2 Chemistry (eg. if you're not sure if it's nucleophilic aliphatic substitution or nucleophilic acyl substitution or nucleophilic aromatic substitution, just say nucleophilic substitution).
