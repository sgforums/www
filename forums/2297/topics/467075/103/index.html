    The following compilation of 2015 Star Wars related commercials illustrate what a powerful cultural phenomenon Star Wars is (rivaled only by Star Trek, as far as the Sci-fi genre is concerned).

    In Singapore, Ip Man 3 almost immediately beat Star Wars 7 in local box office movie rankings. A couple of discussions on the Straits Times (including ST journalist John Lui, and a couple of forum letters written in) had their own theories on why this was so (eg. 1 letter claimed that many in the audience kept texting on their phone during Star Wars, but was glued to the screen during Ip Man, proves that Ip Man is a better movie than Star Wars), but all of them are rather off the mark. Let me explain.

    Star Wars as a cultural phenomenon (see YouTube video below) far surpasses Ip Man, essentially because (ie. the deeper reason for the attractiveness of Star Wars, is actually that) "The Force" is a psychically and spiritually seductive concept (which has some basis in reality if you understand spirituality, but that's a topic for another discussion altogether). Star Wars as a common interest brings together far more people across cultures and countries, rivaled only by Star Trek (Star Wars vs Star Trek, another interesting discussion for another time), than Ip Man ever could.

    However, martial arts exemplify personal skill, which is the deeper reason for the attractiveness of martial arts movies : you respect and admire the high-level skills displayed by the martial artists, most notably (arguably most successfully) exemplified by Donnie Yen, including in his Ip Man movies.

    This is the real reason why the audience was glued to the screen during the Ip Man movies, because the martial arts displayed represented and are consequential of, real and admirable (martial art) skills.

    Such skilled martial arts scenes were in contrast, missing in the Star Wars movies (which hopefully might change in the upcoming Star Wars movie starring Donnie Yen*), which is why the audience wasn't as glued to the screen in Star Wars movies, choosing instead, to text or tweet their thoughts on the Star Wars content during the screening.

    So it's not that Ip Man is better than Star Wars, it's that the martial arts in Ip Man is better than Star Wars. In terms of cultural impact, Star Wars still wins. Overall, they're both great movies, and I love them equally and uniquely, for their unique strengths and impact on human culture.

    (*Incidentally, Donnie Yen himself will be acting in the upcoming Star Wars spin-off : Rogue One, so there. Who says we can't have the best of both worlds? *grin*)
