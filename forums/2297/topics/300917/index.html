    Pay attention during lectures and tutorials. The temptation to switch off or skip them is very very great. Trust me, once you pay attention, your need to do self-revision will cut down by about 30% to 40%. Never just blindly copy what is on the whiteboard/projector screen. Listen to your tutor/lecturer and understand the fundamental concept.

    Of course, you can try practising. For me, the only subject that I practise a lot on is H2 Maths. At the start of my j2 year, I couldn't understand a single thing about Vectors and Complex Numbers. I just piah qns on these 2 topics for 3 months and somehow I manage to understand the questions better. Of course, doing prelim papers from other schools helps too *thinks back about the hours burnt in ang mo kio library doing prelim papers after prelim papers*.

    For physics, TYS is the most important. Period. Chemistry... Just go practice answering questions on chemical trends (esp chemical bonding). You need to write concisely and score on all the keywords. Of course, organic chemistry is mug+application, physical chemistry is really lotsa practice, inorganic chemistry is just pure mug (just mug and you are likely to score full marks on any inorg chem qns).

    Most importantly, have the will to go study. Discipline is what it takes. Well, actually, I started only in the second half of the year which is quite late. Besides, I also had to train to get that NAPFA silver (didnt pass a single NAPFA until my j2 year). So I guess it is possible to juggle various committments. Just be focused and manage your time well.
