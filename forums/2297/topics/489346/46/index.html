    The relevant formula is Î”G = - R T ln Kc

    Mathematically, JC students are expected to know that ln 1 = 0. So if position of equilibrium lies on the LHS, Kc < 1, then Î”G > 0, hence the forward reaction is not thermodynamically feasible (under standard conditions and molarities), the backward reaction is.

    Conversely, if position of equilibrium lies on the RHS, Kc > 1, then Î”G < 0, hence the forward reaction is thermodynamically feasible (under standard conditions and molarities), the backward reaction isn't.
