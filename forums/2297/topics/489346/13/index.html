    Originally posted by ArJoe:

    And does acid base neutralisation of phenol/ carboxylic acid and na+phenol/carboxylic acid/ alcohol requires heat? Or does room temperature works also


    Room temperature for all aqueous Bronsted-Lowry acid-base reactions, as Ea is very low for proton transfers to occur.

    In Inorganic Chem, SiO2(s) requires strong heating with concentrated or liquid alkalis to react, due to very high Ea as a result of the giant covalent lattice structure of SiO2(s).
