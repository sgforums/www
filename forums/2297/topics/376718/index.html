    There are some p6 maths qns that I encountered in my students' work that her other tutor solved using algebra, and I would solve them using algebra too. However, she has difficulty understanding as the p6 syllabus about algebra is is very basic. Therefore, I hope to see if there are any other ways to solve the qns apart from algebra.

    1) An art exhibition was held for two days. On the first day, 500 more men than women attended the exhibition. On the second day, the number of men decreased by 10%, and the number of women increase by 15%. The total number of visitors on the second day is 1680. How many females attended the exhibition on the second day?

    2) At 7am, Car A left Town X for Town Y. At the same time, Car B left Town Y for Town X. At 3pm, the two cars met. 5 hours later, Car A arrived at Town Y, while Car B was 150km away from Town X. What is the distance between Town X and Town Y?

    3) Alan and Barry have 480 marbles. Alan gave 2/5 of his marbles to Barry. Barry then gave 3/7 of the total number of marbles that he has to Alan. In the end, both Alan and Barry had the number of marbles. How many marbles did Alan have at first?

    Thank you! (:

    3.

    Amount of marbles each boy have at the end = 240

     

    Amount of marbles Barry have before giving some to Alan

    = (240/4) X 7 =420

    Hence Alan has 60 marbles then

     

    Amount of marbles Alan has at first before giving some to Barry

    = (60/3) X 5 = 100 marbles
