    Electrolysis : When protons from water are reduced to gaseous molecular hydrogen, what remains are hydroxide ions. So both equations (reduction of protons vs reduction of water) are in effect the same (though of course, will have different redox potentials, depending on the molarity, and thus availability, of the species reduced), and for aqueous solutions in which gaseous molecular hydrogen is evolved at the cathode, both equation variants are accepted at 'O' and 'A' levels. Liquefaction of Ammonia : The gas which liquefies first, has the strongest intermolecular interactions (in the case of ammonia, hydrogen bonding), and accordingly, the highest boiling point.
