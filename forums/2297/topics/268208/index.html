    I guess the correct statement is:
    unfortunately, friction is NOT negligible, the crate slides up 1.6m on the ramp, stops, and slides back down.

    If friction is negligible (usually the case for O levels), then we can use conservation of energy.

    initial kinetic energy = final increase in potential energy

    because the ramp is angled at 30 degrees and is 2.5 m long, the height increase is 2.5 sin 30 = 1.25m

    1/2 m v^2 = m (1.25) (10)
    v^2 = 25

    Hence v = 5 m/s
