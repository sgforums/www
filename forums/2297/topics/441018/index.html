    Hey dude! First of all, dont be too discouraged by your promo results, thats not the end of the world. You definitely have the potential to do well give your olevel results. Judging by your result, i guess you might be in one of the JCs that set notoriously hard papers? But that the shouldnt be an excuse not to do well. Your goal is to do well for Alevels.!

    I dont think its a wise decision to consider retaining based on these factors.

    1. retaining doesnt guarantee you will do well. Some become more complacement when they get retained and their results fall further.
    2. Seriously dude, spending two years in JC is more than enough for a few lifetime 
    I think you should consider your studying strategies like what  Darkness_hacker99  suggests. Let me share with you some tips which i found useful. In general right, you should always seek to understand your content well, no point memorising like in Olevels cause Alevel is more advanced than Olevels. Always clarify your doubt with your peers or subject tutor when you dont understand, i find that to be extremely useful. If you must get a study buddy whos around the same standard as you and try to help each other out and get regular + early consultations with teachers. Btw, sometimes its helpful to take the subject syllabus and see whether you can answer the requirements there, it helps you to consolidate what you know or dont know.

    For Econs: It is recommended that you do full essays on your own to practice the rigour you need in answering essay questions. Essays outlines should be 1/2-3/4 of a full essay to practice answering the question(its a practice to make sure you identify the correct points to write about and have a sense of what you are going to explain). For case study, must read questions and annotate both the extracts and questions.

    For chem: Its highly essential to understand the content, because they always repeats the same type of questions in different context(elements/compounds). The answers are quite similiar, but must modify to suit the question requirement. Once you get the hang of bullshitting(my teacher always says that) you can answer most of the questions fairly well. Basic mastery of content like colour of compounds is very important! For organic chemsitry, always understand the mechanism and not the reaction. A smiliar type of reaction with different functional groups that you never encountered may come out and you are required to apply what you have learnt to it.

    For maths: Understanding the significance of each step is key! You must know what you are doing. Read your notes and understand. Practice with understanding and dont blindly do questions like in olevels, it doesnt work this way with Alevels! Observation skills to see similiarity between parts is also important, make sure you stay clam and think!

    I dont take bio so i have no advice for this but i think the key is understanding rather than memorising.

    Thats all i have to say i guess. But dont waste your time repeating! Since you already know that you might be lagging, work hard for the coming year! no time to slack while you try to keep up with year 2 work and catch up with year1 work. The essential thing is to study SMART and i am sure you can make it! Dont be too stressed and All the best!

    P.s hope this helps.
