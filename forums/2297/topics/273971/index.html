    Sulphur dioxide IS acidic.

    Acidic oxides are those covalent oxides (eg SiO2, SO3, SO2, NO2) while basic oxides are mainly ionic oxides (eg MgO, CaO, Na2O).

    However, there are some oxides that are amphoteric (acidic and basic). At O level, there are only 3: Zinc oxide, Lead Oxide and Aluminium Oxide. At A level, ionic oxides with a high degree of covalency are considered to be amphoteric.

    METALLIC oxides are either BASIC or AMPHOTERIC.
    But the amphoteric oxides which u need to know for O-levels are just:
    - ZnO
    - Al2O3
    - PbO

    NON-METALLIC oxides are either ACIDIC or NEUTRAL.
    But the neutral oxides which u need to know for O-levels are just:
    - CO
    - H2O
    - NO

    So the rest will just be acidic oxides.
