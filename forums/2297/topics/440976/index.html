    In an argand diagram, the point A represents the complex number 2-3^0.5+i. If A, B, C are the vertices of an equilateral triangle taken in clockwise order, and these points lie on a circle with the center at the origin, find the complex number represented by B in the form p + iq giving the exact values of p and q i know that angle of B is 120 more than the angle of A and it is of the same length. But if i calculate the angle of A and sub the values to find B, i cant get the exact value. The answer writes b=e^(-2/3pie) x (2-3^0.5 + i) b= 3^0.5 - 1 + i(1-3^0.5) I dont understand the method used in the answer. Does anyone know? Or is there another method?

    Hi,

      This question is from RI(JC) 2010 paper.

      To obtain B from A via a clockwise rotation of (2/3)pi about the origin, we multiply e^(-(2/3)pi*i) to the complex number represented by A.

      I believe students are more familiar with the relationship between iz and z, where iz is obtained from z via an anticlockwise rotation of pi/2 about the origin. In fact, iz is equivalent to z multiplied by e^((pi/2)*i).
