  • ndmmxiaomayi
    19 Jul 07, 11:34
    1) 5x + 2y = 11 - (1)
    x + y = 4 - (2)
    From (2),
    x = 4 - y - (3)
    Substitute (3) into (1),
    5(4 - y) + 2y = 11
    20 - 5y + 2y = 11
    20 - 3y = 11
    20 - 11 = 3y
    9 = 3y
    3 = y
    Substitute y = 3 into (2),
    x + 3 = 4
    x = 4 - 3
    = 1

    Therefore, x = 1, y = 3

    OR

    5x + 2y = 11 - (1)
    x + y = 4 - (2)
    (2) X 2: 2x + 2y = 8 - (3)
    (1) - (3): 3x = 3
    x = 1
    Substitute x = 1 into (2),
    1 + y = 4
    y = 4 - 1
    = 3

    Therefore, x = 1, y = 3

    2) LHS = RHS. Unless you are at higher level, this is not possible.

    3) Same as question 2.
