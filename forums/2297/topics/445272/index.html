    Did your school provide a booklet on SPA guide for students?

    Besides the skills to carry out the instructions, you will need to be aware of presenting your data in a proper manner (usually in the form of a table.)

    Have proper headings in your table of data.

    Depending on your apparatus, take note of the degree of accuracy in your data.

    Common ones used are the burette (for titration experiments) and thermometer (for experiments involving heat).

    I'm surprised you mentioned that you weren't taught on report writing, a few practices similar to the actual assessment will be usually completed before the actual day.

    While the teachers can't tell you exactly what will be tested prior to the day, they can tell you the topics that would be involved.

    17 Jan 12, 19:52

    Many possible experimental errors for titration, it depends on the context of the experiment.

    1) If solution was prepared by dissolving solid mixture (to find out percentage mass)

    Possible error: mixture was not homogeneous (not uniformly mixed)

    2) Titration involving heat changes measurements

    Possible error: loss of heat to surroundings

    3) Titration involving "real-life chemicals" e.g. apple juices, toilet cleaner, baking powder

    Possible error: Presence of other reactive additives inside the sample

    Not possible to list out all possibilities, would have to use a bit of your logical thinking (which is purpose of SPA anyway....)
