    87.7% of A-level students obtain at least 3 H2 passes, a pass in GP or KI

    SINGAPORE: Results of the 2008 Singapore-Cambridge General Certificate of Education Advanced Level (GCE A-Level) Examination were released on Friday.

    87.7 per cent of the candidates obtained at least three H2 passes and a pass in General Paper (GP) or Knowledge and Inquiry (KI). This is a slight improvement over 2007's 87.5 per cent.

    The Education Ministry (MOE) and the Singapore Examinations and Assessment Board (SEAB) introduced the new indicator of "three H2 passes, with a pass in GP or KI" in the 2007 A-level exam.

    They said this is to reflect the intent of the revised A-Level curriculum, which emphasises both breadth and depth.

    Under the revised curriculum, students have to take at least three H2 and one H1 content-based subjects, of which at least one must be a contrasting subject.

    School candidates can collect their result slips from their schools, while private candidates will be notified by post.

    Source: CNA
