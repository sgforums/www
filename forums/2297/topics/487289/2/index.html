    I agreed with FireIce. It is not a good choice to compare using prelim and O level results.

    I have classmates scoring A1 for E-Maths but ended up C5 for O level.However, there are also some instances where my classmates got C6 for Combined Humanities for prelim but got B3 during O level.

    Comparing O level and prelim results is not a wise choice at all.Firstly, prelim results only takes in your secondary school while O level comprises of the whole Singapore. Next, the grades for every subject during O level each year is not standardlized. For example, in every school, A1 for English is 75 and it won't changed. However, for O level , A1 may not always be 75. It can be <75 if the paper is set more diffcult and 75> if it is easier.

    Mentioning about scoring badly, I'm unable to comment on these as you knew the best when you take the exam. Ask yourself if you have lots of "???" or  " Huh?" etc. From these, you knew that chances of making mistakes is higher.

    All I can only say is, we can't cry over a split milk. I retook English as well.
