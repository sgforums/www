    I will strongly advise against taking H2 Maths w/o A maths background. Some A maths topics are extremely important as the foundation for H2 maths

    Differentiation for A maths forms the foundation for differentiation for H2 Mathematics; basic integration taught by A maths leads onto H2 mathematics integration (standard forms, by substitution, by parts) which then leads onto area and volume of revolution. 

    If thre isn't strong foundation, it's very difficult to catch up as JC tends to assume knowledge of A maths when teaching. 

    That's said, H1 maths isn't that easy either, it's the same difficulity in terms of question per say (at times, H1 maths questions can be harder than H2, same as other subjects like Chemistry also) but half the breadth which fortunately gives you more time to adapt to the syllabus. 

    H2 maths is not a prerequistic for courses like law, business and etc, so ponder carefully your future before making any decisions

    However, if you are bent into doing H2 maths, I wish you all the best but spend these few weeks reading up on A maths (especially calculus and trigometry - ignore geometry).

    To Unknown Soldier and Bbujess (and all students applying into JCs), bear in mind that "appeals" give you a small chance to get in, *only* if your L1R5 score is *exactly* the same as the cut-off score for that JC. Eg. for a certain JC's cut-off score for science stream is 10, and you scored 10 for your L1R5, but didn't manage to get in, then you can appeal (even then, the chance is very low, because only if some student who got in, decides to transfer away from that JC, thus giving a vacancy, then you've a chance... even then you gotta fight hundreds of other appealing students with the same L1R5 score).

    So to both of you, hope for the best, but prepare for the worst (actually, the worst case scenario isn't all that bad), ie. after putting these desired JCs as your 1st and 2nd choices, put realistic JCs (particularly YJC, or even consider MI, which are more lenient in terms of A level subject combinations based on O level subject prerequisites) as your next few choices, and be prepared to enter these JCs.

    As a last last resort, there is always A level private candidacy. But it's too early to consider this last last resort for now (this would be more appropriate for students who for whatever reason, decide to drop out of JC, but don't wish to enter Poly). For now, seriously consider more realistic, lower-tier JCs. It's not that bad, as the A level papers at the end of the 2 short years are exactly the same, and your own determination matters far more than the school environment.
