    Can't help you much on the literature part but i can give you some tips on the ss component (since i'm ss/hist combi). SBQ only require your skills, on whether you get it correct or not, that ultimately rests on your interpretation. I'm sure you have mastered the skills of presenting your answers by now yea? Most importantly always answer to the question.

    Eg. Do you believe that the source...?

    Even though you know it's a reliability question, please DO NOT write "this source is reliable in that..." Definite wrong answer. Instead do only write "I can believe/cannot believe this source in...."

    Ah yes, my teacher says there's a trend that express students tend to do well in SEQ than SBQ because they study for the essay-writing component. On the other hand, NA students tend to score well for SBQ part because they "don't think too much as Express students do".

    Whatever the case, master the skills and explain logically. My SBQ can be fairly good at times but there sure are times when I misinterpret the source.
