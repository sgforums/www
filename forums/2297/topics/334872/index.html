    >>> Is it true that, in order to go to jc, I have to pass MT? If I dont, but hit the entry requirements for whatever jc, does it mean I still cant go? <<<

     

    I'll leave that to HipHop or some other Moderator or JC student to answer you. But from what I understand, as long as your L1R5 qualifies, you can be admitted into JC. But you still have to study and pass ('AO' level?) MT at JC level, while your peers study MT at a higher level in the JC.

     

     

    >>> If I currently take combined science, can I still take a H2 science subject in jc? (Other than Math) <<<

     

    This is subject to the individual JC's discretion. In other words, some JCs allow, some do not. But I expect most should allow (combined science at 'O' levels), as prerequisite for H2 science.

     

    Although (almost all) JCs no longer allow you to study a H2 or even H1 science subject, if you didn't study it at all (whether Pure or Combined) at your 'O' levels. They used to allow students to study Biology at H2/H1 even if you had absolutely no Biology background at 'O' levels. But it seems they (almost all JCs) no longer allow this.
