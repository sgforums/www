    i'm highlighting these mistakes not out of mockery; i mean well. it's important that you have a good grasp of grammar first. once you have it, your vocab will come naturally. you'll never need chim words for essays, because language is something to be understood, not flaunted.

    reading papers, magazines and books help because you get to learn how other people structure sentences. using them for your essays will help. personally, i think it's more effective than tuition.

    comprehension, as the name suggests, tests how well you comprehend things. answer whatever the question asks, never be afraid that your answer is too short. too many people plonk down irrelevant chunks and got themselves killed.

    good luck.

    I can tell you how to do well in English: read a lot of good English books, and make the effort to speak good English at every opportunity. When I say 'at every opportunity', I am saying that you should put in the effort to speak in proper formal English even when you're talking to your friends, and to not use shorthand lingo in your SMSes.

    While you're at it, put in the effort to write use proper English when you're putting up posts in sgforums. I'm not kidding.

    At all costs please do not bother trying to memorize lists of 'cheem' English words. That will not help you at all, and may in fact prove to be counterproductive. A poor understanding of those words may simply lead you to using them in the wrong context at the very least.

    At the very worst, you may end up writing an essay full of high-level words but riddled with simple grammatical errors - that doesn't reflect very well on you, does it?
