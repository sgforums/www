    Not to break your dream (some people will be against me for doing this) but sometime you will have to face the harsh reality of truth and I have to say the time is now. You mentioned "it's never too late to realise your dream" but likewise "it's never too late to realise it is a dream".

    You may ignore what I've said below since it will definitely be not pleasing to your eyes and you may not accept it but I think it's something you really should know and decide.

    Firstly - To become a lawyer, you need to be excellent proficiency in English since you will need to write contracts and stuffs. I think you should have no problem with this since you "boasted" about your command of Englishin your last post, right? So I think you gotten an "A", or at least a "B" in English for your N-level, yes? Personally, I think B is no good but well, if you have gotten at least a "B" then there should be no problem brushing up it up to "A".

    Secondly - Unless you can get into JC which I doubt you can with your current standard, there are almost no way you can do law degree in Singapore University. If you failed your N level, there no way your basic foundation of your current taken subjects (Math, Science, etc) can be good.

    N-level to O-level is no walk in the park. Been there, done that. The gap is pretty big. It is said getting an 'A' in N-level is equivalent to 'C' in O-level which I think is quite true.

    Granted, there is 1 classmate I know who got himself into one of the better JCs but that is after his continuous effort in getting top in the class everytime round and his effort paid off. Can you do that? Saying yes is easy but doing it is not.

    Thirdly - Don't say I never give you benefit of doubt, even if you do miraculously get into JC, whether is it by luck or seriously study your ass off everyday, you still have another hurdle to breakthrough which your GP paper (Very Important - You will need at least a B to be eligible). Even if you do well in getting an "A" for GP but screwed up all other subjects, you can forget about it.

    Lastly - Getting into poly will NOT get you to do law degree in Singapore. You won't stand a chance against the A-level holders. You will more likely stand a chance to do a degree (provided you get a GPA of 3.4 and above) which is related to what you are studying in poly.

    Looks grim to you? It should. Currently, there are a few options left, which I can think of, for you:

    1. Find some ways, don't care how you do it (go tuitions whatever), pass your N-level, score well enough for your O-level and get into JC. From there on, you are on your own.
    2. Find some ways, don't care how you do it, pass your N-level. However, you did not score well enough to be eligible for JC. Get into Temasek Poly, apply for Diploma in Law & Management. Get a good GPA (3.5 and above) for the diploma and try apply for law degree in NUS. If fails, no choice, apply for law degree in overseas university instead. Do note that not all overseas law degrees are recognise in Singapore, unless you want to remain overseas to become a lawyer then by all means, go for any of it. 
    3. Find some ways, don't care how you do it, pass your N-level. However, you did not score well enough to be eligible for JC. Get into Temasek Poly, apply for Diploma in Law & Management. Get an average GPA for the diploma and try apply for law degree in NUS. If fails, no choice, try apply for law degree in overseas university instead but also fail. Be happy you are able to get into law sector and become a lawyer's sidekick.
    4. Give up and let your dream remain as a dream. Think of a new dream which you like to do next and is within your grasp.

    Frankly speaking, you don't earn much being a lawyer in Singapore. However, it will be a different case if you are well-known. If you are think being a lawyer in Singapore earns big buck, forget it but if its your passion, then go on aim for it.

    Right now, you should repeat sec 4 and just focus on getting your foundations up and do well in N & O level. It's not too late to decide what to do after that. ITE is definitely not the route you should go for your dream.

    'O' Level Subject Fees are here :

    http://www.seab.gov.sg/SEAB/oLevel/2009GCEOSubjInfo.pdf

     

    Regarding private tuition, the fees vary widely. It will definitely be expensive for you because you will have to engage multiple tutors for the different subjects you're taking at 'O' levels. And you may need more than just once a week tuition, since a large part of the syllabus will be new for you. Unlike the usual practice for students studying in school, tuition won't just be supplementary, it will be your main source of learning the 'O' level syllabus.

     

    You should be aware that ITE offers 'O' level tuition classes on campus at night, for interested ITE students taking 'O' levels while studying in ITE. It will be a lot cheaper than hiring private tutors, especially multiple tutors.
