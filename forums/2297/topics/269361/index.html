    1)It is known that one gram of carbon from a living plant which contains a definite proportion of carbon-14 decays at a rate of 15 disintegrations per minute. a specimen which was formed from a living plant a long time ago shows 15 idsintegration every 4 minutes from one gram of carbon present in it.

    given that the count rate from carbon-14 begins to fall only when tree dies, estimate the age of the specimen. (the half life of carbon-14 is 5000 years)

    ans = 10 000 years.

    2) N atoms of isotopes phosphorus-32 undergo radioactive decay. it decays to a non radioactive sulphur-32. calculate in terms of N, the number of sulphur-32 atoms remaining at the end of the 60 days, given that the half life of the isotope is 15 days.
    ans = N/16
