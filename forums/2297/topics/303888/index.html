    Assuming normal atmospheric pressure, at higher temperature, the heat supplied to the system will disturb the intermolecular forces of the molecule (breaking H-bonds among water molecules). Thus, the molecules are farther apart, occupying larger volume.

    The intermolecular forces in liquid is far stronger than those in gaseous state. Stronger intermolecular forces -> stronger attraction among molecules -> molecules are closer to one another -> occupying smaller volume.
