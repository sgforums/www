    Here's a chemistry calculations question I wrote just the other day, to let my students (both 'O' and 'A' levels) enjoy a bit of fun with chemistry calculations. I challenge all 'O' and 'A' level students on this forum (including lurkers) to solve this question. If you can, post your worked solution here (as proof you solved it). If you're unable to upload your solutions, at least you still have the satisfaction of solving it yourself on paper. ~ A BedokFunland JC Original Calculations Challenge ~ 50cm3 of aqueous sulfuric(VI) acid of unknown molarity was mixed with 75cm3 of 0.45mol/dm3 of aqueous potassium hydroxide. 55cm3 of the resulting alkaline solution A was mixed with 80cm3 of 0.35mol/dm3 of phosphoric(V) acid. 90cm3 of the new resulting acidic solution B required 39.97cm3 of aqueous sodium carbonate of unknown molarity, for complete neutralization. In a separate experiment, 625cm3 of the same aqueous sulfuric(VI) acid solution required 192.3cm3 of the same aqueous sodium carbonate solution for complete neutralization. Calculate the molarities of the sulfuric(VI) acid solution and the sodium carbonate solution. Answers : [H2SO4] = 0.2 mol/dm3 [Na2CO3] = 0.65 mol/dm3
