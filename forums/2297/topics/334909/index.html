    Once again, I see this type of logarithm questions asked in school examinations...

    log3x-log327=logx9

    Which make me so glad that I always touch on the approach to such questions despite it never having occured before in the past 30 years in the O levels.

    Seriously, this type of questions stand a 0.1% chance of coming out so I always wonder why it is almost sure to come out in school examinations.

    One may argue it is good for thinking but if you want thinking... think up something innovative please.

    Almost every year, I am impressed by the way the O level throw up something new which teachers and tutors will not be able to predict and thus drill their students for. Which is why I never try to predict the unique questions and don't spend thing drilling my students on 101 type of unlikely questions. For unique and/or tough questions... it is better to work on the reasoning ability of the students and just hope that the question will be one that they can reason their way through.

    It make me very very glad that there has never been a single year that the O level have threw up more than 2 such questions as invariably I am bound to have one or two students who will get a B3 under the best of situations and 2 of such questions may throw them into the C region if they panick.
