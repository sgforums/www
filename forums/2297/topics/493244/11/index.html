  • UltimaOnline
    15 Jan 17, 21:42
    Originally posted by qixx:

    Just enquiring, is it very hard to get a degree for math in Singapore? Am i able to achieve that if I'm taking a combi of H2 econs math phys and H1 chem? Moreover, NIE would recognize such degrees right? 

     


    Yes, that subject combination is on track to becoming a Math teacher, no worries.

    If you can afford it, get an Honors Degree in Mathematics from NUS or NTU, paying your own fees. MOE will still sponsor your NIE Diploma education, and the contract bond is for a shorter number of years. You get to choose to be a Primary, Secondary or JC teacher.

    If your family got serious financial problems, you can apply for having a MOE contract, in which they'll sponsor your NTU Degree education, together with NIE Diploma education, and you'll be bonded for a longer number of years, and you can only teach Primary school and Secondary school, but can't teach JC.

    You'll have to check with MOE / NIE website for the latest updates, because it's been 1.5 decades since I was an MOE teacher, many changes to contractual agreements liao.
