    Originally posted by qixx:

    Hi there, sorry for disturbing but is it possible to explain a little about the grading system during A levels? I'm not quite sure cause like they said S grade for GP is a pass but is it still a pass? And what are the relevant scores for each grade kinda thing? 

    Hello

    S-grade is considered a sub-pass for either a particular h1/h2 subject but in technicality means you have failed the subject,hence implying the name sub-pass,which to me means 'borderline fail' in a sense.

    A particular grade for a particular subject gives you a certain number of points which you can use for your University Admission Score,with each H2 subject giving a max score of 20 points and H1 subject giving a max score of 10 points should you get an A for them.Any subsequent consecutive grades from B to E is 2.5 and 1.25points less than that of the previous grade respectively.An S grade gives you half the points an E grade will give you and a U grade gives you 0 points.
