    GCE O-level results to be released on Jan 11

     

    SINGAPORE: Results of the 2016 Singapore-Cambridge General Certificate of Education Ordinary Level (GCE O-Level) examination will be released on Wednesday, Jan 11. 

    In a joint media release on Thursday (Jan 4), the Ministry of Education (MOE) and Singapore Examinations and Assessment Board (SEAB) said students may collect their results from their schools at 2pm on that day. 

    Private candidates will be notified of their individual results by post. The result slips will be mailed on Jan 11, 2017 to the address provided by the candidates during the registration period, MOE and SEAB said. 

    Private candidates who are eligible for SingPass can also use their account to obtain their results online from 2pm on the same day. 

    Students who wish to apply for Junior Colleges, Millennia Institute, polytechnics or the Institute of Technical Education may do so through the Joint Admissions Exercise (JAE) using their GCE O-level examination results, MOE and SEAB said. 

    JAE registration will open from 3pm on Jan 11 to 4pm on Jan 16, MOE and SEAB said.

    Obviously you should go JC.

    You should only choose Poly if you're certain you've a passion for a particular industry, and are certain you will be working in that industry.

    Everyone else should go JC to keep their options open.

    Don't worry about the % of students going to Uni for each JC, because it's yourself which determines your success, not your JC. The only reason why there appears to be a correlation, is simply because best JCs (eg. RJC) get the most academically able students in the first place, with their most demanding L1R5 cut off points. Otherwise, the different JCs' teachers and environments don't make that big a difference at all.

    Retake your O levels as a private candidate (since your secondary school won't allow you to retake, correct?) in order to pass your English to be eligible for JC. But in the meantime, don't waste time, but start self-studying H2 Chemistry and other H2 subjects on your own.

    In the very worst case scenario, though it shouldn't have to come to that, you can always take A levels as a private candidate if you still can't pass your English at O levels and no JC will accept you (note that if you're a Singaporean guy with National Service obligations, you'll have to finish your NS before taking your A levels as a private candidate).

    General Paper isn't English. You can still do well for GP even if you failed your O level English. Many China students get A grade for GP every year. And there are many good GP tuition centers out there, that can help with your GP.
