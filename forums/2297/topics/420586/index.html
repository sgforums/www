    I am no F&N student, but previously I was reading Biomed stuff.

     

    Option 1)

    A)

    You need to know what are "food additives", why they are bad, how does it cause the "negative" effects, types of additives, why addictives are use, is there any alternative other than using food addictives.

    You will need to know what are "processed food" and the definition, which type of food falls under the category of processed food, give some examples of processed food that can be easily found in daily lfie. Is processed food "good" or "bad", discuss.

    Bonus: "Why addictive are commonly found in processed food?"

     

    B)

     You need to identify what are the so-called "healthy ingredients", why are classified as "healthy", roughly how do you tell between healthy and unhealthy ingredients. Give some examples.

     

    C)

    Preparation of meals. What are some of the recommended techniques of preparing food (for the health conscious people). Why do you chose them, and how will it affect the food (in terms of nutritional values) if you prepared it in this way. List a few techniques, and discuss the topic in nutritional values.

    D)

    Health concious people. What do you think health concious people are looking for in their food? Why do you think people will go for healthier food? List down examples of unhealthy eatings.

     

     

     

    The most important in Task Analysis is to look for key words and pick up the main points and elaborate from there. The usual way is to use the Who, What, Why, When, How technique. It may not apply to all, but usually you will develop from there.
