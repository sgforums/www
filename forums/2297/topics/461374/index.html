    Ask yourself once and for all: do you want to go to a local uni?

    Because regardless of what people may tell you, the JC route is still the more sure route to uni. If you think you can learn from your mistakes and work harder next year, and you are dead set on a local uni, then you should stay in JC.

    But if you think you will be better off at poly, then by all means move on to poly. Just take note that it would be harder to go to a local uni from there.

    Do NOT go to private uni except as a last resort, because they are generally held in lower regard by employers.

    Hi. Yes I suggest you search for A level H1 mathematics syllabus. You should be able to see the materials you will be studying for (the objectives, etc). As a general advice, I will say try not to drop H2 Math. I have a friend who used to fare really badly for O level math and she got 17 points for the L1R5. She got into a neighbourhood JC and still managed to get by with a B for H2 Mathematics. I will say you just have to do more practice. I know when school starts, it's difficult for you to do 1 Math paper per week alongside the tutorials. Why not try this, do ONE Mathematics question PER day from different topics. I am sure you will see the result in the long run. Mathematics is one of the subjects where you can get an A or B easily. To give you that added advantage, probably do ONE Mathematics question once your school holiday begins. This gives you an edge over the Year 1s right? My H2 subjects are Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics. Hmm 3 Arts subject is way too heavy. Most arts student I know only take 2 H2 arts subjects: Economics, Literature, Mathematics, History (for H1). So I'd suggest if you want, take 2 H2 Arts subject, 1 H2 Science subject and another arts as H1 if you like it. Choose according to your interests. The above is merely a suggestion based on what I have seen from my friends' subject combi.

    Hi, I would understand what you are going through. Don't worry. The humiliation part is only felt by ourselves. Initially,when I told my parents that I want to redo my Year 2, all my teachers, parents and siblings are against it. They were like,"Stop wasting one year of your life". But I told them that this is my life and I felt that this was the only right decision I could make at that time.Just like you, before I began my new semester, I was worried about how the new peers would think of me, etc. However such fears were unfounded. The peers were ok. I am in a retainee class so everyone in my class belongs to the 1993 batch. Sure, most of the teachers scolded us, checked our homework individually, etc. They did specifically "humiliate" our class during lectures as well. But deep down, the teachers want to help. The reason why they are harsh is because they assume most retainees are lazy, playful and uncompetitive in JC, to quote the words of one of my tutors. They take the hard approach because they dont want us to waste another year since, as I said, they assume retainees won't take the soft approach. However, along the year, you will meet encouraging tutors as well, who tell the class to never give up. There is nothing humiliating about it. No one will laugh at you. The only enemy of yours is your own pride and self-esteem. Deal with it. For me, I treat it as a rare opportunity that only a minor portion of each cohort get to experience. After all, only a handful out of the hundreds of students retain. Take it as a learning process. Retaining is only a small setback. If you can swallow down this "humiliation", you will go a tad further in life than the rest. Don't worry too much. If you need help, talk to a school counsellor. There is nothing shameful with that. All the best!
