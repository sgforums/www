    What Genie99b said. Good advice, certainly.

    Notwithstanding, at the same time, if your daughter is sure she would like to give the Medical School route another shot, she can try a route less conventional : for one year, do not go to either JC (arts) or Poly (non-biomedical), but instead self-study and retake O levels as a private candidate, *and* self-study A levels at the same time (since due to age constraints, she won't be allowed to take A levels as a private candidate till she's older).

    If all goes well, her O level results should improve sufficiently to re-apply into a JC which allows her to take Science. And meanwhile, her self-study of A levels would have prepared her well for her JC studies.

    Worst case scenario, she can always (whether at the time she's in a JC, a Poly, or neither) take A levels as a private candidate. If she manages to achieve all As as a private candidate, she can still apply into Medical school (both local and recognized overseas Unis).

    The above is for your daughter's and your own consideration. It's just one possible route. I mention it, not because it's necessarily any better or any worse, but only because it doesn't even occur to (or register in the minds of) most students and parents, that this (albeit unconventional) pathway could actually work.
