    generalise (e.g. my undies, bra, singlet ----> undergarments)


    leave out details (e.g. the scorching red ball of fire shining down... --> the hot sun)


    dun repeat (e.g. the busy street is full of people ---> the busy street)


    answer the question (e.g. the question ask u why man can be successful in their career.......... in general, men not only are diligent and intelligent, but they also have bad manner..... in ur summary, only take in the diligent and intelligent part, big appetite, dun say they got bad manner, leave it out)

    read sentence by sentence, ask urself this sentence answer the question anot... slowly.. take ur time... rmb it is wise to have at least 50 mins for summary... summary is there to help u, not to pull u down.

    Summary writing requires alot of practice

    first ,you must established what is the Point required for your summary

     

    Next you must strip away the lengthy detail (eg. scorching heat of the sun changed to hot day)

     

    then you need to link up with suitable conjunctions

     

    As a student who dun have much time

    I will suggest you write down all the necessary points and strip away the details

     

    if upon first counting , you have exceeded 20 words ,go back to your points and check if any points is irrelevant

     

    whilst on the other hand, when you short of more than 9 words to the word limit will imply you short of a point

     

    separate a comment from a fact and answer to the question requirement

     

    examiners is not out to fault you .but if you choose not to summarise properly(leaving so many details in the summary) , you wont have high marks

     

    Summary writing in gist is a shorter form of report writing as compared to your paper 1

     

    Recommended time for summary :45 mins

    10 mins jotting points

    10 mins summarising

    10 mins checking grammar

    remainining is polishing on conjunction and refinement b4 counting of word limits
