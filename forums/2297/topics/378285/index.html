    The purpose of an indicator is to indicate to you the volumes (assuming fixed molarities) of reactants used (titrant and titrand) to reach equivalence point, which is approximated by endpoint (ie. when a suitable indicator changes color). If after knowing the required volumes (hence moles of titrant and titrand may be calculated), you wish to obtain a pure sample of the salt at equivalence point, you must redo the titration without indicator. There is no shortcut, using animal charcoal or any other type of charcoal, or any simple method (ie. it's much less trouble to simply redo the titration), to separate the indicator from the salt solution at equivalence point. Uses of animal charcoal : http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Animal_charcoal
