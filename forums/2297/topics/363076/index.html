    The question is fun.

     

    Question

     

    Differentiate y = (x2)lnx

     

    Steps

     

    (1)    x = elnx    eg   eln 3 = 3

     

    (2)    xlnx = (elnx) lnx

            

            xlnx = e (lnx)^2

     

    (3)   y = (x2)lnx

     

           y = [xlnx]2

     

           Substitute xlnx = e (lnx)^2

     

           y = [e (lnx)^2]^2

     

    (4)  Using Chain Rule,

     

           dy/dx = 2 [e (lnx)^2]^1[e (lnx)^2] [2 lnx] [1/x]

     

    (5)  Substitute e (lnx)^2 = xlnx

     

    (6)  dy/dx = 2 [xlnx]^1[xlnx] [2 lnx] [1/x]

     

           dy/dx = 2 [xlnx]^1[xlnx] [2 lnx] [x]-1

     

           dy/dx = 4ln x [x2lnx – 1]
