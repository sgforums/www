    The answer for part 2 should be x^2+y^2+9x-y-6=0

    Because the center for circle A is (4.5, 0.5). Reflection of this point in the y-axis would be (-4.5,0.5) which is the center of circle B. The radius will remain the same.

     

    Where the original circle is (x-a)^2+(y-b)^2= r^2

    Center (a,b) and radius r.

    The image of the reflection of (a,b) in the y-axis would be (-a,b) and the radius would still be r.

    Giving you [x-(-a)]^2+(y-b)^2 = r ^2
