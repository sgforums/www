    This is an interesting topic. But while it has my interest (because I'm a Chemistry and Biology person), I will decline from advising you on this, because :

     

    1) It don't really make much of a difference what your choice is. Whatever you wish to do later (eg. medicine, researcher, etc), you can still do with any 2 of the 3 sciences at 'A' levels (but Chem is compulsory).

     

    2) It really is up to you, what your interests are (physics or biology), and whether you can cope with triple science or not (you know yourself best), and most importantly don't end up regretting a choice and say "it's so-and-so's fault. I shouldn't have listened to him."

     

    Hear from others.
