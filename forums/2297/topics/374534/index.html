    Zn(OH)2 is an insoluble ppt, but can form a soluble complex ion of tetrahydroxozincate(II) ion, in excess OH-(aq). NaOH is stable (as Na+ has relatively low charge density), and will not lose water to form Na2O. When excess water is evaporated away, you will get white solid NaOH(s). On the other hand, Zn2+ has relatively higher charge density, is more unstable and hence Zn(OH)2 will lose water when heated to form ZnO(s). Zinc oxide is yellow when hot, white when cold; the yellow colour arises from interactions in the crystal lattice, and not due to d-d* transition, since Zn2+ has no partially filled d-orbitals and doesn't qualify as a transition metal.
