    24 Nov 09, 18:05
    Strontium vs Calcium. Sr has one more electron shell than Ca, so do you think they are "nearly the same size"? The reason why Sr easily replaces Ca, is because they're both in Group II and have similar chemical properties. In the first step of the mechanism, the pi bond of the alkene is the nucleophile which attacks the partial positively charged Br electrophile. In the second step, the bromide ion Br- nucleophile attacks the cyclic bromonium/carbocation electrophile. The name given to the reaction is dependent on the initial first step, not the subsequent or second step.

    You're right that this question and its options leave something to be desired. (But it's certainly not the 1st time Cambridge gives terribly phrased questions/options.) Some MCQ questions you have to solve by elimination, since the 'A' level syllabus doesn't specifically study the bonding of hydroxyapatite.
