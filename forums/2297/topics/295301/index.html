    Na2O reacts immediately with water to produce NaOH.

    You will never get Na2O by reacting Na with H2O.

    Reacting Na with O2 will give you Na2O and Na2O2 (sodium peroxide).

    For the case of Potassium, K + O2 will give you K2O and/or K2O2 and/or KO2 (potassium superoxide). This property of peroxide and superoxide are only limited to group I metal except Li (due to diagonal relationship, Li compounds behaves more like Mg compounds)
