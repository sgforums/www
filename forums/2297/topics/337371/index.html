    Q1. Don't be confused by the fractions, just treat A as a 6-units figure while B as a 4-units figure >> now you'll understand why 1/6 of A equals 1/4 of B

    1a) By right A and B when combined should form a 10-units figure. However, since there is a overlap of 1 unit, the total area of the figure is only 9-units. Hence 8/9 of the figure is not shaded. Ans: 8/9

    1b) since the figure has 9 units in total, 1 unit = 162/9 = 18cm2 >> given shaded area = 1 unit = 18cm2 >> Ans: 18cm2

    Q2.

    1. When eight 50c coins are taken out, the total value is $4. Changing the $4 will give you twenty 20c coins.

    2. You took out eight 50c coins and put back twenty 20c coins, hence the new difference between the 2 type of coins = 20 + 8 = 28 coins

    3. Since the new ratio is 1:2, and the new difference is 28 coins, that means there are now twenty-eight 50c coins and fifty-six 20c coins.

    The amt of $$ in box = 28(0.5) + 56(0.2) = $25.20

    Counter-Check:

    Assuming you put back the eight 50c coins and remove the twenty 20c coins, there will now be 36 coins for each type. The total remains the same = 36(0.5) + 36(0.2) = $25.20
