    Two gas containers A and B have equal volumes and contain different gases at same Temperature (T) and Pressure (P). 

    Show that there are equal nos. of molecules in the two containers.

    You could also use the formula

    PV = NKT, where K is the boltzmann's constant, and N = num of molecules

    Thus, N = PV / KT
    Since P, V, and T are equal for both gases, and K is a constant, thus, N is equal for both gases (shown)

    You should know the formula for kinetic energy of a molecule. If I remember, it's 3/2 KT

    So, 1/2 mv^2 = 3/2 KT
    v = sqrt (3KT/m)

    This means, the heavier the molecule, the lower the velocity.

    Hence, in general, molecules in B should travel about 2 times as fast as molecules in A.
