    Answering to ThreadStarter's question, Yes. Galvanizing is the technique, Sacrificial protection is the mechanism.

    Quote from my Chemistry Textbook

    The common example of using a more reaction metal to prevent the rusting of iron, is galvanized iron. Galvanized iron is steel coated with zinc metal. The zinc protects the iron by corroding in place of the iron. Even if the zinc layer is broken or scratched, and air and water reaches the iron underneath, the zinc still protects the steel. The type of rust prevention is called Sacrificial Protection. The zinc is 'sacrificed' to protect the iron.
