    I just did more research, and came up with a figure of 9.6volt - 10.7volt for a car battery that is for all intents and purposes, FLAT.

    How: Leave your car overnight. In the morning, before you start your car, turn on headlights, including the high beam for 10secs. Turn off headlights. Now get a voltmeter and measure the voltage across the terminals. If <10volts, I'd be worried.
  • epsilon93
    4 May 04, 17:42
    It is very very difficult to tell when a battery decides to die. It will work fine one minute and die the next minute. This is especially true of maintenance free batteries. Just measuring the voltage does really mean much, what matters most is the battery's ability to source current.

    For a wet cell, it is a lot easier to predict since it will usually die slowly and gracefully. And you could also see the condition of the plates in the cell. They also last a lot longer if maintained well.
