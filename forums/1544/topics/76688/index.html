    Both the 318i and A4 are at the end of tis life cycle. A new model is definitely planned, probably to be launched in 2005/6.

    As for the S60, an interim facelifted model is planned for 2005 as well.

    The 93 is the youngest of the lot. So it will look "fresh" for many more years than the present other models.

    Price wise (base models), from lowest to highest, I think it will look something like this: 93, S60, A4, 318i.

    In terms of value for money, the 93 delivers. Just compare the list of standard features and you will know what I mean.

    The 93 and S60 have the turbo power advantage over the normally aspirated A4 and 318i. The transmission in the A4 is CVT, 93 is Sentronic, and S60, 318i is Automatic. If you like the choice of both automatic and "manual", it comes standard only on the 93.

    It is better for you to test drive all 4 models and make a choice based on your own value system.

    I love my 93.

    The fuel comsumption actually gets better with time and use. Also, your driving patterns play an important role. Highway better than city driving, as you know. I used to get as bad as 6.45km/L at 6706 on the odometer. With my latest fillup, I got 10.11km/L at 8718 on the odometer.

    Another thing that helps, I think, is the power. If you have more power upfront (like a turbo, or something special), you can very quickly get up to your desired speed (especially on the highway), then maintain "cruising altitude" all the way to your destination! This driving method is the best for minimising fuel consumption.

    Not sure if using a freer flowing air filter helps, but my inprovement came about rather recently, after making some changes.

    By the way, I don't get any subsidy from my company for fuel usage either. However, I do get a whole lot of thrills when the turbo whines, the air intake swooshes, and the butt dyno says "all systems GO, ready for takeoff"!
