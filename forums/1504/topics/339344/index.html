  • QX179R

    'Tiger Airways operator was rude and even hung up on me'

    Lynn had a frustrating time trying to cancel her Tiger Airways flight to Bangkok because of one particularly rude customer service operator. She says:

    "I would like to share my frustration and upsetting experience with Tiger Airways.

    "I bought an airline ticket from Tiger Airways departing on 15 December.

    "After reading the news that Bangkok Airport will not be open till at least 15 December, I called them on Monday, and tried for 11 hours before I reached them.

    "One of the operators told me if there was no news of further closure, then I could not change my flight destination. Neither could I get a refund.

    "Today, I called in again after reading the news that said the airport would be opened within these few days, but the protesters were saying that they might go back to the airport again.

    "I thought it was enough assurance that the flight would be cancelled, and customers could call in to change their destination.

    "The operator informed me that I could not do so.

    "Ok, so I asked her to check with her management, but she insisted that there is no way to contact them as all higher management do not have email addresses and direct lines.

    "Can you believe that?

    "When I asked for her full name, she said that she did not have a family name.

    "I told her I wanted to speak to another operator and she asked me why.

    "I told her that she was very rude, and she hung up.

    "Is this the correct way to treat customers?

    "Or is this the service standard of Tiger Airways?

    "I am extremely upset about the bad experience with them.

    "Why fly with a budget airline, when they don't care about their customers?"
