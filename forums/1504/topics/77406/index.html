    I'm not sure what plane-spotting means technically, but if you're referring to gawking at planes other than from the terminal buildings, then yes and no.

    Yes, it's possible to catch the planes during takeoff and landing from the perimeter fence. Usually we drive up to one of the emergency gates and hang around there. You can get really awesome views of the gigantic monsters of planes. Plus, the noise is really deafening, but it all adds to the experience.

    The downside is that it is illegal to crowd around the emergency gates for obvious reasons. I've been chased away by police at least once, but most of the time, nobody bothers you. Just don't visit the gate next to the airport fire station.

    Just grab a cab and get the driver to bring you to Changi Coast Road. Make sure he stays there throughout your adventure, or you'd have problems getting back to the terminal.

    SQ22

    There is a Viewing Gallery in each of the two terminal buildings. Unfortunately, these are public areas and are out of reach to the average transit passenger. I'm not sure if the Transit Lounges have designated areas for plane-viewing, but even if they do, I guess the views wouldn't be as great as those at the Viewing Galleries (just to give you an idea, each of the viewing galleries takes up almost the entire top floor of the building, and Changi Airport terminals are looooong). As for rooftop viewing, I'm not sure about that. Have you tried seeking help from CAAS? Visit www.caas.gov.sg and drop them a mail.

    PS: Why don't you go through immigration and spend your 5 hours outside the airport? Is there some concern with visa requirements?
