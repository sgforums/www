    ST Engineering displays QUOTE..

     

     

    Bronco New-Gen The Bronco New-Gen is the newest iteration of the battle proven Bronco All Terrain Tracked Carrier, and provides high protection and mobility with increased internal space but lower all up weight. The Bronco New-Gen's V-shaped front and rear cabin hulls are designed to be stiffer and lighter with greater spaciousness to accommodate various crew and equipment configurations. Its rear cabin is designed to be modular and can be quickly interchanged for a wide spectrum of missions. As the lightest vehicle in the family, the Bronco New-Gen offers growth potential in payload and protection to survive a multitude of IEDs and landmines.

     

    Robotic Mapping Vehicle (RMV) ST Kinetics' Robotic Mapping Vehicle (RMV) is an unmanned ground vehicle (UGV) with semi-autonomous capabilities, offering warfighters in high threat environments with dual advantages. First, it can traverse difficult terrain and obstacles, effectively collecting critical intelligence from inaccessible areas. Second, it is capable of providing real-time situational updates to the soldier, helping achieve sustained surveillance and reconnaissance objectives. Robotic Articulated-Wheel Vehicle (RAWV) ST Kinetics' third new exhibit is the Robotic Articulated-Wheel Vehicle (RAWV). Its suspension system allows it to surpass the mobility of conventional UGVs in such areas like crossing gaps wider than half the RAWV's width. The unmanned vehicle can function in both combat and transportation support roles, carrying equipment and supplies for dismounted troops to ease the logistics burden or carry out missions like fire or mortar-support, surveillance, and mine-detection as a "last-mile enabler" for the warfighter.

     

    40mm Day & Night Marker (DNM) Round ST Kinetics' latest innovation for effective peacetime training is the 40mm Day & Night Marker (DNM) round, developed to enhance the warfigher's combat readiness by allowing clear recognition of the 40mm round's impact after firing. The 40mm DNM round's impact, made visible via colour clouds is visible to the naked eye up to 300m, be it day or night. Under low light conditions, the round's luminous tracer and impact signature are also highly detectable through night vision equipment utilising either infra-red or thermal sights. above sources--- ST press release 1831

     

    Skyblade 360 Skyblade 360 draws on the technology and general configuration already developed for the Skyblade III, but the new vehicle is larger, with a wingspan of 9.8 feet as opposed to 8.5 feet, and a maximum takeoff weight of 20 pounds compared with 11 pounds. Performance is also improved, with a doubling in operating altitude to 3,000 feet, and an increase in range to more than nine miles. above ---sources--aionline

    IN THE PIPELINE:AUTOMATED ARMSKOTE AND WEAPON CLEANING SYSTEMS, by ST Eng

    IN THE PIPELINE: AUTOMATED ARMSKOTE AND WEAPON CLEANING SYSTEMS
    THESE systems could potentially replace the current laborious set-up in Singapore where soldiers manually issue and receive personal weapons, as well as clean and oil them.

    A soldier needs only to swipe a smart card for identification, and an automated carousel fetches his weapon and dispenses it like a vending machine.

    A robotic arm dips the weapon in various compartments to rinse, dry and oil.

    The system also uses ultrasonic frequencies to agitate a solvent, creating bubbles that kick the dirt off the surface of the weapon as well as hard-to-reach corners. Such technology is used to clean aircraft engine parts and silicon wafers.
