    Apologies the below mail is an expression of an incident that had make me totally helpless on how I can assist a visiting friend to Singapore, a country I am so proud of.

     

    This visiting friend who is a graduate has no criminal records in both China and Singapore was detained and deported based on immigration officer stringent assessment at moment of time which had no doubt keep Singapore away from overseas criminals on 23 May 2009 at approximately 10 pm via CA969.

     

    Feeling so excited with an overseas friend visiting me on a Friday night, had ended up being so helpless.

     

    She was first in Singapore during last Christmas and New Year period. And was impressed of Singapore night life, cleanliness, safety, friendly and caring people she met. This poor girl was so cheered up after the first visit and had gained back confidence in life whose beloved father deceased on the very same year. Unfortunately, these good memories she had had prompted her extension of each stay in Singapore though of her tight budget which I assumed may have resulted in today's incident. Praises at home for Singapore to friends with no doubts had prompted her to join friends on every possible trip. But in this incident, I could feel the sadness, helplessness and uncertainly when I managed to communicate with her just at moment when she was escorted to the departure flight back to Beijing on 23 May 2009 at 09:32 via CA976.

     

    My stay at airport from being so excited from 9pm, to being totally helpless in need of advised and assistance to a visiting friend till morning with her departure, with regardless of endless call to Changi airport immigration T1 East hotline at 62148010, which was not answered throughout the period, I was so helpless on how I can assist a visiting friend from overseas.

     

    Do admit my negligence to Singapore Immigration procedures practiced at Changi Airport . She, with a valid visa from Singapore Embassy in Tianjin China, a flight ticket to and return, and of a budgeted visit to Singapore with approximately S$4k in cash was rejected entrance due the frequency of visits which is less then 3 months from her last, as enlightened by an immigration officer at hotline in the morning at 8 am. I am sad and lost, if I had the knowledge, I would have advised her of this visit. Which had resulted in her future entrance to Singapore as advised.

     

    Even though of feeling down and helpless at the moment, I would like to praise and thanks the professional services provided by Changi Airport T1 information counter who keeps answering to my endless seek for help throughout the night and to Ministry of Foreign Affair of advises at time I am totally lost.

     

    I too sincerely apologized to Changi airport immigration T1 West hotline, Departure hotline and an unknown immigration department who had expressed great depressed with my endless call throughout the night begging for help and assistance.
