    SINGAPORE (AFX-ASIA) - Singapore Airlines' regional carrier, SilkAir,
    said it has firmed up the delivery of six aircraft over the next
    three years and expects to have a 16-strong fleet by 2008.

    No financial details of the purchase were provided.

    The new aircraft are three Airbus A320s and three Airbus A319s. The
    airline now has six A320s and four A319s.

    SilkAir added that it has further aircraft orders on option.

    The airline said it will be offering discounts of up to 40 pct of
    normal market fares and expects to make more such offers in the
    coming months.

    SilkAir said it took a bad hit from the SARS outbreak last year as
    revenue fell 19.7 pct due to a dramatic decline in passenger demand.

    However, it managed to bounce back with a strong third quarter to Dec
    2003, when it posted a net profit of 9.2 mln sgd.

    The company's cumulative net profit for nine months to Dec 2003 was
    5.3 mln.

    "We managed to get back into the black after we were hit by SARS in
    the first half (2003)," SilkAir chief executive officer Mike Barclay
    told a media briefing.

    "We have every prospect to be comfortably profitable for the fourth
    quarter (to March 2004) of this financial year," Barclay said.

    Barclay also said he sees minimal impact from the proliferation of
    low-cost carriers in the region as these airlines are unlikely to
    offer interline facilities and, thus, will not be able to draw on
    SilkAir's customer base.

    In contrast, Barclay sees potential competition from Valuair,
    Singapore's first budget airline.

    "Yes, I think Valuair will be more in our niche, but we don't know
    much about them yet. Although we don't think they are going to be
    interlining -- that will be the crucial difference -- but Valuair
    will be a competitor," Barclay said.

    Valuair, which plans to launch its services in the second quarter of
    this year, intends to operate a network within five hours' flying
    time of Singapore.
