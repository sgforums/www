    You folks must have heard of the last-minute denial of landing rights by the Indonesian government to Valuair. Everyone's guessing what was happening, and Valuair's bosses were reportedly baffled as to what had gone wrong.

    You see, Singaporeans are known to be organised and go by the book, so all the paperwork should have been in order. What could go wrong?

    My boss had a simple explanation. The airline folks forgot to give coffee money to the authorities in Jakarta. Civil servants are paid measely sums in Indonesia and they survive on undertable money.

    My boss should know. He travels to Jakarta frequently for business. In fact, on a recent trip with him, I had to carry some IT equipment in my suitcase and he prepared Rp20,000 in case the X-ray scanner folks made things difficult for me.
