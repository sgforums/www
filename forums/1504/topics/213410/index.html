    Airlines usually make a point of accommodating taller passengers in exit row seats to afford them more legroom. A taller passenger is also more likely to be able-bodied enough to assist in the opening of the door in the event of an emergency evacuation.

    One disadvantage of occupying an exit row seat on a long-haul flight is that passengers getting up to stretch their legs usually end up standing and yakking in the space immediately in front of you. This can be annoying if you're trying to get some sleep.
