    SIA announces wage freeze for management, shorter work month

    SINGAPORE: Singapore Airlines (SIA) has announced a wage freeze for the current fiscal year for all management level employees as part of its efforts to weather the current economic downturn.

    In addition, senior management will adopt a shorter work month from Wednesday, with lower level managers following suit from May 1.

    SIA said these are among the range of measures the airline is taking to address excess staffing capacity and reduced levels of activity arising from its capacity reduction of 11 per cent.

    It won’t rule out further measures to cut costs.

    SIA added that it is in discussions with pilots. — CNA/vm
