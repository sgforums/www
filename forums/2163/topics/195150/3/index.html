    Originally posted by Fatum:
    I think the reigning champion would be MG ....
    Perhaps not.

    I began living permanently in the US in '91, right after college, but made trips back to Singapore every year thereafter, right up to around 2000.

    The flights to and from Singapore were long, arduous and uncomfortable. My body never really coped well with the time zone changes, and for the first few days after my arrival, I'd be wide awake at night and barely able to function during the day due to fatigue, with the heat and humidity further sapping what little energy I had. I noticed that, as I grew older, it would take me longer to recover from jet lag on both the outbound and return legs. These annual trips to Singapore were more tests of endurance than restful and relaxing vacations, and I stopped going every year after 2000.

    My most recent trip there was in October/November 2003, and I stayed two months to make it worth the effort. It took me a full three weeks to synchronize my circadian rhythm with the local time zone. And as an added bonus, I caught some kind of bug that manifested itself as a throat irritation which didn't go away for almost half a year after that trip.

    I'll probably head back there again sometime in '07 or '08. But I'm not looking forward to it.
