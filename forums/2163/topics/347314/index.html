    Originally posted by jetta:

     

    Question: What do you do when an annual statement arrives after you've filed your taxes?


    Short answer: I usually ignore it.

    Long answer: You might have to file a 1040X (Amended U.S. Individual Income Tax Return), but first find out if the document that arrived late actually changes your taxable income enough to bump you into a different tax bracket and alter the amount ot tax you owe. If the change is favourable to you, you might want to consider if the reduction in tax is significant enough to justify going through the trouble of filing a 1040X. If the change is favourable to the IRS, then I would question whether it is large enough for them to actually bother with it.
