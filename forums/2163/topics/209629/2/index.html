    got a nice rainbow trout fishing ....

    Baked Rainbow trout with lemon and garlic sauce

    Ingredients:

    1 x medium rainbow trout (salmon would do just as well)
    basil
    black pepper
    salt
    spring onions
    parsley
    fresh lemon juice
    1~2 cloves of garlic, according to taste
    a large hunk of butter
    some bacon strips

    Recipe:
    melt the butter, mince the garlic and throw it into the butter together with the lemon sauce,

    line a large baking pan with bacon strips, rub pepper, salt, parsely and chopped spring onions onto the fish, baste it liberally with the butter mix,
    stuff a bacon strip into the fish, and lay a few more strips onto the fish to prevent it from drying out, pour rest of butter mixture onto fish

    heat oven up to 250 degrees, bake it till the bones fall away from the flesh ...

    voila ! ...

    If you got hong kong food stall - you go buy raddish cake to make CHAR TOW KWAY! MUWAHAHAHA

    Then u go to asian grocer to buy chai por and the sweet dark sauce (optional) and chilli (optional but I like to pound chilli padi for char tow kway!)

    Get spring onions, garlic , shallots, fish sauce ,sesame oil, peanut oil and eggs ( how much u want is also optional)

    Chop up garlic , spring onion, shallots, raddish cake up ( preferably in small squarish cubes)
    Heat up pan
    Add peanut oil ( just nice to swirl and oil the pan fully - less the better tho)
    Throw in garlic , spring onion and shallots plus chilli ( optional)
    Add a teensy bit of sesame oil
    Dump the pieces of chai poh ( about enough to cover quarter of a pan surface)
    when fragrant ....throw in all the pieces of raddish cake
    Stir for a while and then make a circle in the middle
    Add egg to the circle ( I usually add alot of eggs)
    break the egg up in the pan til yoke and white looks fairly mixed up , add fish sauce (I never measure - usually I use at least 4-6 shakes) and pepper for taste
    and then let the bottom of the egg cook.
    Fry until eggs are cooked well ( throw in ur sweet dark sauce - abit will do) ...
    add some spring onion again and fry til they looks semi lembeh

    SERVE!
