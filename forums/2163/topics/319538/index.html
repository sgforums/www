    Howzabout a thread for overseas Singaporeans to share images of their summer experiences and activities, eh? I'll start the ball rolling.

    The following pictures were taken at Lock & Dam No. 2 on the Mississippi River just outside the town of Hastings, south of St. Paul. This area, being part of a wildlife preserve, sees its share of migratory birds, and I managed to snap a few pictures of some egrets (I think that's what they are, but feel free to correct me if I'm wrong!) that happened to be passing by. Some of these pictures were taken at extreme zoom, so please pardon the grainy quality.











     

    This how the day started when we headed out to Hastings:

     

    Then, returning from the dam, the weather turned nasty. Very nasty:


    Bear in mind the previous image was taken around noon. We had multiple lines of severe thunderstorms move through the cities, dumping lots of rain and golfball sized hail. A good friend who lives in a 'burb slightly north of me reported that they were hit by hail that accumulated to a depth of 4 inches on the ground. Fortunately, my neighbourhood "only" got hit with torrential amounts of rain, and there were no reports of tornadoes even though the sirens were wailing.

    Another view of the storm to the north:

    By early evening, most of the storms had passed, and this was seen as we were heading out for dinner:

     

     

     

    Edited to add:

    Here's a picture of the hail accumulating on the deck of my good friend Dave's house during the hailstorm mentioned above:

    The depth of the hailstones covering the deck attests to the intensity of the storm (and it wasn't over yet when that picture was snapped!). I live about two miles south of Dave, but fortunately it didn't hail in my area.
