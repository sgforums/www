    SINGAPORE – BRISBANE (Meals on Board)
     

    Assemble at Singapore Changi Airport for your flight to Brisbane.

    BRISBANE – GOLD COAST (Meals on Board/Dinner)
     

    Upon arrival, head to Dreamworld and enjoy thrilling rides like Wipeout, Thunderbolt, and the 38-storey Tower of Terror. Visit Tiger Island and admire the big cats playing and swimming. Head for the Australian Wildlife Experience to see its resident animals like koalas and kangaroos in their natural habitats. Watch the Australian Farm Show for a look at the Aussie rural way of life. Check out the new FlowRider and watch the Flowboarding demo to see the experts ride the perfect wave. Also not to be missed is a thrilling ride on The Claw.
    GOLD COAST (Breakfast)
     

    Spend the day at the exciting Warner Bros. Movie World, known as “Hollywood on the Gold Coast”. Take the most advanced and thrilling suspended coaster ride in the southern hemisphere on Lethal Weapon or try the heart-thumping and thrilling ride, Superman Escape. Enjoy the Police Academy Stunt Show, or go on the scariest, spookiest, most spine-tingling rollercoaster ride on Scooby-Doo Spooky Coaster.
    GOLD COAST (Breakfast)
     

    After breakfast, you can spend the day at leisure. Join an optional tour to Sunshine Coast or spend the day at Sea World at your own expense. In the evening, take an optional tour at your own expense to Springbrook National Park to see Australia’s largest glowworm colony or see the new Australian Outback Spectacular which showcases the rugged outback, complete with daring stunts, wild horses and stampeding cattle. Enjoy an Aussie BBQ dinner while you feast on the action.
    GOLD COAST – BRISBANE – SYDNEY (Breakfast/Dinner)
     

    Depart for Sydney by flight. Upon arrival, embark on a city tour of Chinatown, St Mary's Cathedral, Darling Harbour and King's Cross. Continue your sightseeing at The Rocks, a charming waterfront precinct with beautifully restored buildings. See the famous sails of Sydney Opera House and Harbour Bridge. Make a stop at Mrs Macquarie's Chair for a superb bird's-eye view of the magnificent Sydney Harbour. See a vast collection of marine life at Sydney Aquarium. See the largest reef display at the Great Barrier Reef Oceanarium and come face-to-face with huge stingrays, sharks and schools of fish at the Open Ocean Oceanarium.  

    Note

    If you are taking an early morning flight to Sydney, boxed breakfast will be arranged.
    SYDNEY – BLUE MOUNTAINS – SYDNEY (Breakfast)
     

    After breakfast, visit the vibrant Sydney Fish Market, Australia's only working fishermen's market. Set off for the scenic Blue Mountains National Park. Enjoy the gorgeous scenery of the densely forested Jamison Valley and see the giant sandstone pillars called “The Three Sisters”. Shop to your heart’s content at Direct Factory Outlet (DFO) with many individual outlets and well-known labels under one roof. You can buy fashion apparel, footwear, accessories and more at discounted prices.
    SYDNEY – SINGAPORE (Breakfast/Meals on Board)
     

    If time permits, you can do some last-minute shopping before you transfer to the airport for your flight home

    Sea World and Dreamworld not really that worth it. Movie World is nice though. There is also a casino inside Conrad Jupiters Hotel somewhere in Gold Coast. But basically not much else to do in Gold Coast unless you like surfing or shopping or eating seafood. The small Ripley's there is not worth visiting. If you are in Brisbane, u can check out the parliament house.

    Sydney, start with Darling Harbour, there you can visit Maritime Museum (used to have a submarine there), Star City Casino, Fox Studios (can go for tours), Aquarium, Chinese Garden. Then you can move on to Sydney Opera House, next to it is Circular Quay where you can take a ferry to visit the zoo. You can also drop off from the ferry halfway to the zoo to visit Fort Denison which is in the middle of the harbour. Next to Circular Quay is The Rocks area which has a nice weekend open air bazaar. Also a few eating places there as well as the Bounty which is an old wooden ship that you can actually have dinners on as it goes around the harbour (not sure if it is still around). On the other side of the Opera House is the Botanical Garden.

    Near to Darling Harbour is Chinatown which is also near to several major shopping complexes. Don't forget to check out Queen Victoria building. It's a small shopping mall but very nice building. If you like red light district, there is King's Cross area where they have the annual Mardi Gras parade.

    Hunter Valley and Blue Mountains will take a few hours to reach by bus from the city. While you are in the Blue Mountains, nearby there is Jenolan Caves where you get a guide to take you through the caves. The lightings light up the caves pretty nicely.

    If you are in Sydney around Christmas, u can attend the Carols In The Domain. It's a free open air concert in a huge field. During New Year Eve, go to Opera House and watch the fireworks in the harbour as well as on the bridge itself.

    for 10 days, i would recommend a self-drive from sydney to melbourne, or the other way round. i did a sydney - adelaide self-drive last year during winter and it was a tremendous experience.

    depending on what you want to see, i am sure you will be able to sample a very diverse australia as well.

    australia is very easy to drive around. i would recommend that you call the tourism australia office to get some road maps to plan your trip. they are located at goldhill plaza. i popped by their office last year and their staff are generally very helpful.

    for accomodation, my recommendation would be youth hostel. it is a bit spartan, just beds and a simple table, but it works for me. i typically requested a room for my family, sometimes we get lucky, as we were given room with attached bathroom. what i like about youth hostel is most have a big kitchen that allows you to cook. we did a lot of self cook to save money, plus you can only eat out so many times...the YHA folks will also typically know where to go and what to see. you can check some B&B as well as they offer reasonable prices for their rooms.

    the key to a good hols down under are the following, imo,

    1. plan well, i.e. familiarize yourself on roads to take

    2. book your accomodation early, ideally thru' the web while in sgp

    3. try not to pack too many things on your itinerary. australia has so much beauty in the various location that sometimes it is just better to take an extended few hours to soak it in.

    4. rent an suv if possible.

     

    many years back, my and a few of my buddies drove from brisbane to byron bay, a hippie town by the coast. it was a 3.5hours drive, i think. it is absolutely a cool place to hang out. i read last year that byron bay is now very commercialized.

    Hi fatone,

    It really depends which parts of Australia you want to see.  If you would like to see only those popular ones in one place, then it might be worth hiring a car to look around.

    But if you want to stay at one place for a few days and fly to another, then just organise day trip.

    (1) Gold Coast - the different worlds

    (2) Sunshine Coast - Australian Zoo (Steve Irwin's zoo)

    (3) Sydney - Opera House, Harbour Bridge, Sydney Aquarium, cruise around the harbour

    (4) Melbourne - Great Ocean Road (12 apostles), Penguin Parade (Phillip Island), Vic Markets, Wineries

    If you can confirm the date you want to come, you can get domestic flight at pretty good price (www.virginblue.com.au or www.jetstar.com.au).  Same applies for accommodation (www.wotif.com.au).

    It might work out to be cheaper.  If you need any help, just let me know.  I am happy to help you to plan/organise.  The earlier you set a date, the cheaper everything will be.

    Enjoy planning! ;-)
