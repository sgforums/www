    While walking from the bus stop to the office this morning, I was at the traffic junction waiting for the pedestrian crossing signal to turn green. A bit of snow had fallen last night and I noticed that the road surface at the junction was shinier than usual, indicating a thin layer of ice had formed.

    One of the roads leading to the junction has a slight downhill grade, and just as the pedestrian signal turned green, a car that was coming down that road attempted to stop but locked up it wheels and slid on the ice into the junction. It did a very graceful 360° spin while sliding through the junction in slow motion. Amazingly, the car didn't hit anything and continued on its way without stopping after straightening itself, all in one fluid move.

    It was pure poetry in motion--the people waiting to cross the road even applauded and cheered after the car completed its manoeuver.

    Yup, winter's here!
