    Cold weather doesn't necessarily imply permanent snow cover. There has to be precipitation (i.e., snowfall) to provide that snow cover. It doesn't snow all the time in winter climes, and an occasional stretch of warm (i.e., above-freezing) weather can melt some or all of the accumulated snow on the ground.

    Ski slopes and resorts require a certain grade of snow on the ground. "Old" snow tends to compact down over time (from its own weight and the weight of skiers) and become hard and crusty--not ideal conditions for skiing. The artificial snow that's generated ensures that the slopes retain a layer of soft, powdery snow that is suitable for downhill skiing.

    The artificial snow is generated by blowing a fine spray of water under great pressure into the air. The cold air freezes the water in the spray, which then settles on the ground as "snow."
