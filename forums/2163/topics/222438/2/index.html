    Yeah, the transit operators here also use a "smartcard"-type thingie similar to Singapore's EZ-Link card, called the MetroPass. The fixed monthly fee of US$20 is automatically deducted from your paycheck, and you can use the MetroPass for unlimited rides on the buses and trains anywhere in the Twin Cities. Not a bad deal actually.

    Bus drivers are assigned specific routes on three-month rotations, so they get to know the regular passengers very well. The bus driver that day knew I was a regular rider with a MetroPass, so the 'misread' didn't bother him in the least.
