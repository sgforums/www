    Finally arrived in Huntsville, Alabama at around 2 a.m. Saturday morning. The Friday evening flight out of Minneapolis was delayed for over 2 hours because--get this--they couldn't locate the flight crew. Northworst eventually had to fly in an aircrew from Boseman, Montana, to take the flight. How's that for a completely screwed-up airline?

    Anyway, the flight to Nashville, Tennessee was uneventful, and the two-hour drive to Huntsville was boring.

    Alabama might be part of the same country, but you might be forgiven for thinking you're in a foreign land.     The next set of pictures was taken inside the vast system of caverns and caves at Ruby Falls, on the outskirts of Chattanooga, Tennessee. An elevator shaft takes visitors over a thousand feet underground, and the guided walking tour is a little over a mile distance.




    And the centerpiece of Ruby Falls: a 145-foot waterfall located 1120 feet undergound. Pardon the poor image quality--lighting conditions weren't ideal for photography, and using the flash just illuminated the water droplets in front of the lens.
