    Did backpack when i was a bachelor.

    Travel by SIA to Nepal and then backpack from Nepal to Tibet and Tibet to Nepal.

    Tibet was a hush destination. sleep in army camps, cheap lodge. Most memorable journey was the visit to the "Tian zhang" or Sky Burial. Had to bribe (packs of Marbolo Cigarettes) the priest and the deceased family to allow us to watch the ceremony. The deceased was bundled up in a triangle shape and the priest chanted the prayers. Then the priest cook up some stuff to attracted the vultures that was flying in the sky to decsend. The priests than started to cut up the body with a cleaver and smash up the bones with an axe. The priest than walk away and sat there to chant some more. The birds started to feast on the flesh and the smashed bone all in.

    We took risk to watch the ceremony as there was a law that allows the police to lock you up even if you are a foreginer.

    In order to prepare for the bagpack holiday, you need to read up on the destination really well, such as where to stay, where to eat, where to visit, the opening time of the place, arranging your own means of transport etc.

    The advantage of such holiday is the pace, price and to spend your time according to your wish unlike package tour. you also get to see places package tour don't bring you. You get to eat the ordinary food taken by their locals.

    Be prepare to travel by lorry, train, bus, bicycle and any other means of transport used by local. Backpack is interesting but usually you need friends who have the interest to travel this way.
