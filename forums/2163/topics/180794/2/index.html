    Originally posted by ceecookie:
    does cars have prob starting up during winter and/or stall more easily too?
    Not necessarily. Much depends on how well your vehicle is maintained.

    It helps if you switch over to a lighter grade of motor oil in the winter to help prevent the oil from thickening too much in the engine block. On exceptionally cold days, the oil may thicken to the point where the battery does not have enough power to crank the starter and turn the motor. However, one the engine has warmed up, it should operate normally.
