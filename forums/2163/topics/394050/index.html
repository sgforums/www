    I'm in Osaka now.

    The flight from Minneapolis was long. Too friggin' long. The leg from MSP to ORD on United was fairly uneventful. Had to take a train to get from the domestic to the international terminal at ORD to check in with Japan Airlines. Surprise! They upgraded me from Economy to Premium Economy. Woohoo! Nonetheless, a 13-hour flight is still a long flight regardless of seat comfort, and I wasn't really looking forward to another one hour flight to ITM after arrving at NRT.

    The flight got into ITM a little past 6 p.m. Took the airport limousine bus to Shinosaka Station and hoofed it to the hotel. Got my room, then headed out to get some really good ramen noodles at a joint called Hanamichi, which is supposedly one of Osaka's most famous noodle houses. It was a little on the expensive side, but well worth it, in my humble opinion. There is no shortage of eateries around the station and hotel, as I found out when I took a walk around the area after dinner.

    I'm setting aside tomorrow to (hopefully) recover a bit from jet lag. I'm hoping to head into the Namba district of Osaka to catch the Suzumiya Haruhi no Shoshitsu movie, and then perhaps bum around the Shinsaibashi area for the rest of the day. Then the heavy travelling begins in earnest on Saturday, when I head up to Tokyo for the weekend to attend the Tokyo Anime Fair 2010 in Odaiba plus do a little sightseeing on the side while I'm there (aka make a mad dash for Akihabara).

    Pictures to follow when I've had a chance to resize and upload them.
