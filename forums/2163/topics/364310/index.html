    Originally posted by vito_corleone:

    i'd just like to ask, if i have an sg drivers license, can i buy a car? cause school starts on monday and i can't get a CA license in time so i have to drive to sch with my sg license

    Go to the Singapore traffic police website and print out your Driving license certificate of merit

    You need to start calling up the insurance companies to get liability insurance coverage.

    You can buy a car without a liability insurance.. but you will not be able to REGISTER your car at DMV without it.

    And driving without a liability insurance in CA is ILLEGAL.

    Try Progressive Insurance. Your initial cost of coverage will be high due to your lack of in-country driving record and your age. Shop around for the best deal. Not all companies are willing to cover international drivers with no in-country driving history.

    Once you get the approval for your liability from the insurance you can go purchase that car of your selection.

    Once you have established driving history with your CA license under current insurance .. you request for a lower coverage costs from your current or other insurance company. Typically one year of driving is good.

    Liability insurance only protects you from legal liabilities if you get involve with an accident with another party.

    Coverage for theft and collision is a separate insurance option on top of your liability.

    So if you buy a disposable car.. you don't have to worry about the theft and collision because that is not a legal requirement.
