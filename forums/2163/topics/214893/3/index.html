    Saskatchewan Copes With Blizzard
    PDF
    Print
    E-mail
    Written by Vanese M. Ferguson - 600 Action News-Local First
    Wednesday, 10 January 2007
    The blizzard is going full force in Saskatchewan.

    Roads And City Services

    The Highways Hotline is reporting that all highways out of Saskatoon, Meadow Lake, North Battleford, Lloydminster, Kindersley, Biggar and Rosetown are temporarily closed due to zero visibility, blowing snow and drifting snow. Highway 3 east and west of Melfort is also closed due to the blizzard. Travel not recommended in the Prince Albert and Spiritwood areas as well as highways north out of Yorkton. The Prince Albert Airport is also closed.

    Dispatch at one of the cab companies says the wait for cabs depends on where you are - some streets are completely blown in and the vehicles can't actually get to the addresses in question.

    Jeff Balone, Saskatoon Transit manager, says they are no longer adhering to a schedule. Some buses are stuck and several roads in the city are now closed impacting routes. Balone says their goal is simply to make sure everyone gets home safely. Roads closed in Saskatoon include McOrmond Road and Kerr Drive have been blocked off so no traffic will be allowed onto Highway 5.

    Preston Avenue, between Circle Drive and College Drive is blocked off.

    22nd Street West and Diefenbaker Drive, which includes westbound traffic on Highway 7 and Wanuskewin Road and Adilman Drive for northbound traffic heading out of the city is also closed.

    There are numerous intersections that have now been closed.

    Those are Kenderdine Drive and Kerr Road, McOrmand Drive and Kerr Road, McOrmand Drive and Highway 5, Preston Avenue and Perimeter Road, Preston South and Circle Drive, Wanuskewin Road and Adilman, Warman Road and Circle Drive, Attridge Drive and Circle Drive as well as 22nd Street West and Highway 7.

    University and Schools

    Classes are not completely cancelled at the University of Saskatchewan.

    That decision is left up to the individual colleges.

    As of early this afternoon, classes are cancelled at the College of Education.

    If students need to know what's going on in their specific colleges they can call them directly.

    The SIAST Kelsey Campus cancelled classes Wednesday afternoon and evening. Services are expected to resume Thursday.

    As for the Public and Catholic School Divisions in Saskatoon, the schools remain open for now and meetings are being held to try to determine what to do from here on, but parents who want to pick up their kids can do so. YWCA is closing in Saskatoon on 25th Street and shutting down due to the blizzard.

    Once concerned mom phoned the newsroom and says Dundonald and St. Peter's Schools are virtually inaccessible due to snow and stuck vehicles and she says parents need to start making provisions NOW to get their kids home.

    The power has been out in a couple of communities West of Saskatoon for about 40 minutes now.

    Crews know about power outages in Sonningdale, Arlee and Struan but can't get there until the weather clears.     [/url]
