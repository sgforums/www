    The little hailstones could be ice pellets.

    Normally air gets cooler with an increase in altitude. Sometimes the air above can be warmer. In this case precipitation falls as rain (liquid droplets) through colder air that is at or below 0 C and the droplets freeze and what hits the ground is frozen raindrops.

    Another term you'll hear is freezing rain. That's when water droplets are already below 0C and still remain in liquid form. They then freeze on impact with a collecting surface, such as the wing of an airplane. Freezing rain is the usual cause of aircraft accidents involving ice
