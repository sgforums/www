    Update:


    June 18, 2007 07:01pm

    POLICE have named the man they are hunting over the fatal shooting in central Melbourne today.
    He is Christopher Wayne Hudson, 29, a Victoria Police spokeswoman said tonight.

    "No further details or a photograph of the suspect are available at this stage," the spokeswoman said.

    But the Nine Network tonight said Hudson was a member of the Hell's Angels motorcycle gang.

    The spokeswoman urged anyone who saw him to contact police or Crimestoppers.

    "Under no circumstances should anyone approach Mr Hudson," she said.

    One man died and two others were seriously injured when a man opened fire at the corner of William Street and Flinders Lane, as hundreds of people headed for work in the morning peak.

    A 43-year-old solicitor died at the scene, despite the efforts of paramedics to revive him.

    An earlier altercation at a nightclub turned into a violent confrontation on the corner of Flinders Lane and William Street at 8.15am (AEST) today, when two good Samaritans went to the aid of a woman being dragged from a taxi by her hair.

    Without warning, her attacker pulled out a handgun and, at point blank range, blasted the two men in the upper body before shooting the woman.

    Paramedics tried in vain for an hour to save the life of the solicitor.

    The second bystander, a man in his 30s, was tonight in a critical condition with gunshot wounds to the upper body.

    Extremely brave

    The actions of the solicitor who was shot dead were ones of "extreme bravery", police said.

    "It really strikes at the heart of all investigators to see somebody who's going about their daily life to be killed in such a tragic way," Detective Inspector Stephen Clark said.

    The Barcode nightclub on King Street was cordoned off as a crime scene and police were questioning a different man they took from there this morning.

    "We're not sure exactly what his involvement is," Det-Insp Clark said.

    Police were examining a handgun found at a nearby building site after the shooting, which is a similar type and calibre as the one the gunman used.

    A mobile phone found on Flinders Street was also being examined.

    Police are appealing for the driver of a taxi seen at the shooting.

    "I don't know exactly where the taxi fits in, but there was a taxi seen in the vicinity," Det-Insp Clark said.

    "There appears to have been an altercation between the male and the female at the taxi," he said.

    Earlier assault

    About 10 minutes before the shooting, the gunman assaulted a different woman a block away in King Street's nightclub precinct by either kicking or punching her in the face.

    That woman was also being treated in hospital today and, as with the other two victims, police were yet to interview her.

    "The victims at the moment are all receiving medical treatment. Once we get an opportunity to speak to them, we'll then do that," Det-Insp Clark said.

    Police did not know whether the two women knew each other or whether one was the gunman's girlfriend.

    Interstate authorities have been given information about the suspect, who was last seen on Flinders Lane.

    "It's the No.1 investigation at the moment and we've got plenty of resources dedicated to finding this man," Det-Insp Clark said.

    Distraught family

    The distressed family of the shot woman expressed their horror and sorrow for the other victims.

    The woman's mother, who rushed to her daughter's hospital bedside, offered her sympathy to the dead man's family.

    "My daughter is up there fighting for her life," she said.

    "I just feel so sorry for the other family."

    The family would not comment on the woman's links to the gunman. It is believed she had recently moved to Sydney and worked in tourism.

    A News.com.au reader said the gun was found later at a nearby contruction site. Police were "fairly confident" it was the weapon used. A worker at the site said a coat was also found there.

    Lockdown

    City buildings was in virtual lockdown this morning with thousands of people being told to remain where they were while the manhunt was on.

    The landmark Rialto Tower was briefly evacuated amid suspicion the gunman had fled inside, sparking a floor-by-floor search.

    Rialto managing director John Apps said the search came up empty-handed and that the gunman was then rumoured to have been seen running down St Kilda Road.

    The crime scene near Flinders Lane remained closed, but Flinders Street had reopened to traffic.

    The gunman was described as being about 30, about 183cm tall, wearing black jeans, a denim jacket, and with short brown hair.

    Some Telstra staff who work in the Rialto Building had been been offered counselling.

    The nearby AXA building was also locked down and customers and staff at a nearby cafe were told to remain inside.
