    Part of the museum at Point Defiance that chronicles the history of commercial logging in the region. Many of the machines and railway equipment used in the process were on display too. Very impressive!



    Approaching Bremerton (again!), with the mothballed warships visible across the bay. The three carriers in the image are the Ranger, Constellation and Independence (though not in that order). Of particular interest is the Ticonderoga-class cruiser at the extreme right of the image: it is the USS Vincennes, which gained notoriety for shooting down an Iran Air A300 airliner in the Persian Gulf in 1988.



    At Kingston, driving aboard the passenger/vehicle ferry, the MV Puyallup, for the short cruise across the sound to Seattle.



    Underway across the sound. We took multiple ferry rides during our time in Seattle, and I have to admit they definitely count as one of the highlights of the trip.



    Woohoo! Aboard the underground shuttle train at SeaTac Airport. The shuttle links the main terminal with two satellite ones.



    The Pacific Marketplace Mall at the SeaTac Airport. What's wrong with this picture? The sky is blue!!!



    Finally, the Northwest A320 that will take us back to (bleah) Minnesota. Oh, look at that! A blue sky!

    On this trip, I snapped well over 400 images; the ones featured in this thread are but a small sampling of them. Hope you liked them!

    Some more random images from the Seattle trip, with equally random captions:



    The Space Needle, viewed from the ferry. It was located far enough away from downtown as to be difficult to include in any skyline shots. And no, we did not visit the Needle as we were pressed for time.



    Another panoramic shot of the Seattle skyline from the ferry. The sun actually made a brief appearance for this shot.



    Some interesting businesses near Pike's Place Market. The Japanese characters under the left sign read porun fuirumu... porn film! A sneaky way to target a specific market segment without running afoul on the city's billboard ordinances.



    What the?.... A Yaohan in Richmond, BC??? Actually, it was located along No. 3 Street in the Asian enclave of Richmond. There was a killer food court on the ground floor that could have belonged in any major housing estate in Singapore. I had some fabulous Hong Kong-style wonton noodle soup there!



    Pike's Place Market in the foreground. It was a Sunday, and most of the much-acclaimed fishmongers were closed. Still crowded, though, as you can see from the image.



    Aboard the passenger/vehicle ferry for the one-hour cruise across the sound from Seattle to Bremerton. Did I mention that I really, really enjoyed the ferry rides? I really did!



    I can definitely see myself retiring in one of these seaside residences on Bainbridge Island in Puget Sound...
