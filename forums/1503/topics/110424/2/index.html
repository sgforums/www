    You guys remember what a haiku is? It's a very short poem with only three lines. The first and last line has five syllables, while the middle line had seven syllables.

    I had an idea: why not write a full-length poem..... where every stanza is a haiku?

    I initially start this piece by explaining what a haiku basically is. As I wrote however, I somehow lost touch with reality and started blasting them apes (i.e. some of my peers) who mock what I'm doing.

    To them poets are either show-offs or a freak of nature. I still can't believe that at their age (sixteen at that time) they can be so immature. Oh, well.... I can't hate them for that, can I? They're.... misguided, not evil.

    This isn't a good poem. But of course, as I said earlier, I lost touch with reality halfway through.

    A Different Poem

    A different poem
    Composed by yours-so-truly
    It's called a haiku

    Five on the first line
    Seven on the second line
    And five on the third

    Full of hope and love
    Packed with horror and mystery
    Filled with much intrigue

    I've had no regrets
    Investing in these strange words
    Expressing my thoughts

    Yet people still smirk
    At what I've been doing
    Ignorant are they

    If you're not a fool
    Please do remember that I
    Am not one either

    For I am writing
    Words that will make you quiver:
    A different poem.

    little red box

    i have decided to squeeze
    all our memories
    into that little red box
    with carving of fanciful fairies
    image of a fairy tale

    this little red box used to
    house both our engagement rings
    now only one remain
    which i will bring
    when i meet you there

    meanwhile

    i will keep our little red box
    closely by my side
    caressing and defending it
    furiously day and night
    never letting it down

    i will shower this little red box
    with tears and love
    as if it is our unborn child

    Caged

    It was born last night
    Among other sleeping cats
    In this foreign land

    Its eyes pounced in aghast
    Searching futilely towards the horizon
    In the direction of a faraway Kingdom
    Where Kings like him
    Roams freely and gracefully upon
    The ancient ground

    There
    They will bow to him

    But here
    Its birth was a terrible mistake
