    Void Deck Day

    The sun bears witness
    To the strolling cats
    And yelling children
    Who kick their feet
    Screaming like a lady robbed
    And refusing to board the bus.

    The plump Ah Soh waddles
    Around the void deck
    Leaving an invisible trail
    Denoting irritation
    Towards the patient Sun
    And his brother Humidity.

    Chattering away like leaves stirred,
    The three kids of 4th Floor
    Slump head-first on the stone table;
    Hands dealing out magic
    Â“Lightning Blast!Â”
    and their nimble fingers
    surpass even the noodle seller.

    One cat stretches and yawns
    The postman is here.
    Ah Soh has returned.
    The sun turns white
    And the smell of fried fish
    Waketh the little kitty.

    The sounds of basketball
    Bounces off the whitewashed walls
    And distracts the studying girl
    Who looks up
    And swoons at her man,
    But the Os are a jealous rival.

    The sun sets in a blaze
    And the rays light up
    Even the dark corners
    Of letterboxes and lift landings.
    A cat stands and stretches
    Her day has just begun.
