    Your First Hunch is Your Best Hunch

    Is it really true that Â‘your first hunch is your best hunch?Â’ According to a study by Stoffer, Davis, and Brown (1977) this may not be the case. By examining the studentsÂ’ answer sheets for evidence of changes such as erasures or crossing out the initial answers here is what they discovered,

    From wrong answer to wrong answer --> 15%

    From right answer to wrong answer --> 22%

    From wrong answer to right answer --> 63%

    Amazing! So when your teacher asks you to go through your answers again if you have the time, she knows what she is talking about.

    Afterthought

    This study was conducted back in 1977, i.e. almost 29 years ago. If we were to conduct the same study again, would we come to the same conclusions? I do not have the answers to this question but one thing is for sure, as humans we are generally unsatisfied with our first choice. We change our jobs for better prospects, better salaries, higher positions, etc. Our next girlfriend/boyfriend must be better looking, smarter, richer, etc. than the previous one. We must constantly reach for higher grounds, better living conditions, bigger TVs, bigger cars, etc.

    Is your next choice really going to be a better one? Will your next revision improves your poem? Will you really be more successful in your next job? How do you measure success? How do you know there wonÂ’t be a better person coming along your way after this one? When will it end?

    I know I am not making much sense here but let me tell you a secret (well now it is not a secret anymore); yesterday I turned down an opportunity to be promoted two grades above my current grade. Yes, a double promotion and I turned it down. Sounds crazy? Maybe. I have my reasons but I am not going to explain them here. I know what I am doing but to be on the safe side, will you pray for me?

    Afterthought II

    I have a friend, okay, had a friend whose philosophy towards life is very simple Â– always go for the better option. Whenever a better girl comes along he will dump his current girlfriend and go after the new one. When he completed his National Service, the same pattern repeated: always go for the better option. He bragged that he never remain in the same job for more than two years.

    I have no idea where he is now, what he is doing, or whether if he is married or still single. Maybe he is right in his quest to constantly seek for better things in life. But sometimes it is good to settle down, take a look around you and be grateful of what you have. There is always a better choice, but are you sure it is the right one for you?
