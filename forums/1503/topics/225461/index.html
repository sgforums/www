    My latest attempt at writing christmas poems.

    Christmas at the mall

    When we were just children,
    we used to marvel and covet
    the piles of presents hoarded under
    the lofty dressed Christmas tree
    in the center of the mall;
    we thought they were real,
    ....................................we thought
    they were free for the taking.
    When we got our hands on one
    and tore it open, and saw that
    it was just an empty cardboard box
    wrapped up in gold shiny paper,
    .....................................we wondered
    who would be so cruel
    as to falsely decorate Christmas.

    Santa under scrutiny

    Who is this Santa, who is this old man
    whose facial features is hidden by his overgrown beard?
    He appears every December with his signature ho-ho-ho,
    (who laughs like that except the sinister?) sits
    on a grand wooden chair on a stage and parents
    (whose senses seem to have gone on vacation) line up
    with their children, those innocent unscarred faces,
    to go to him and let the little ones sit on his lap!
    They stand below and watch the fat fuzzy manÂ’s claws
    grab hold of each child as he asks them for their name
    and what they would like for Christmas, then after 5 minutes
    of whispers and his hands around their shoulders or across
    their legs, he gives them a present from under a plastic tree,
    then he sends them back to their parents with doubtful
    looks on their faces. The ones that start crying as soon
    as they see him know better.
