    The Ivory Tower was a beautiful building. The constitution of the building consisted of stone slabs made of carved and polished marbles of white and silver stacked neatly together. Around the tower was a fortification fence made from the toughest bricks. The entrance, was guarded by two towers. Around the bricked wall was a moat and the only way to cross it was to enter through the drawbridge.

    "The Ivory Tower of the Academy of Spellcasting! Behold, the beautiful dream and magnificent libraries of knowledge. Here is the home of the druids, the shelter of the wizards, the oasis for all who thirst for transcending to a more knowledgable being! The Castle of Academy of Spellcasting, the Fortress of Gillania, the place where all who seeks power of unimaginable heights. This is Dep-Isharma, the legendary Ivory Tower whom all spellcasters yearned for!" the slender old guide proudly proclaimed.

    "If the people who lived here are powerful, they won't need the fortifications, " laughed one young lady. Her hair was copper red, swaying beautiful with the breeze. She was robed in grey and black and her fair complexion reflected the beam of the sun. Her eyes were black, but under the beautiful gaze of the light, it shone red and seemed to burn excessively. She was slender, her chin proudly tilted up such that the rest of the world seemed to fall below her feet. An aura of strength and confidence emitted strongly around her and her elegance was followed by every forceful stride.

    "Stand aside Telfis before you're swept by the flames of Master of Wizard's Pawns, Lady Viltarsis Incineray! Milady, your strong feelings may be a little undermining of the strength of those who reside there. For there may not be any in there who could best you in the game of Wizard's Pawns, but there may be many who might be able to stand against your might," said a druid, with greying beard and white hair.

    The druid with the greying beard was a man in his early seventies, his eyebrows were also grey and the man stood calm beyond the fixed gaze of the rest. His eyes were gentle, and dressed in the white robes, he would had been considered a priest if it was not for his rings and white oak staff. He moved so slowly forward that it seemed it would have took forever, yet he did it so as if nothing could push him back.

    "It's nice to see you again, pretty Viltarsis. Ah, Master Permissen Tidalfield, you are here too," said a young man. The young man's hair was slightly longer than Viltarsis's and his smile was striking. Although, Viltarsis's features were sharp, his chiselled features were more remarkable. He wore a blue vest over his green robe and had an shiny, reddish brown gauntlet which awfully stood him out. He cut through the crowd as if he was invisible and moved in exceedingly fast pace.

    "Stop shoving people around, punk! Your manners were somewhat lacking, Itaida Galeforce. My servants are human, mind you, human, even if they were my slaves! They are not dogs, not animals, and even animals don't deserved to be ill-treated under your hand! I'll chain you to my carriage if you bump into my men again," shouted the stout man.

    The stout man was a stocky figure. Slightly tanned complexion, the stout man looked as if he wore lard around his bones but in truth tucked beneath the thin fats were chunks of muscles. His black cape and leather shirt further protrude sideways. His eyes were small and his gaze was continuous and the pupils were always staring. Beautiful sharp fangs of monsters hung around his neck and he moved purposefully and each step were rooted strongly into the ground.

    "Simmer down, Demon Hunter Balist Powerstone. You could use plenty of your rage on more demon trophies," Permissen Tidalfield said, "Telfis, it's about time to show us to your master,"

    "Will do," Telfis answered.

    "The first time for Viltarsis and Balist here, ain't I right?" Permissen asked.

    "A most unfortunate situation," Balist answered, "I would had built another academy like this instead of the mess back at Yilde."

    "Don't listen to Balist, Vil, Yilde is an even more impressive stronghold. Maybe, it's just that too many collection of daemon skulls instead of books.
