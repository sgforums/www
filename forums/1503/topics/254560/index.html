Why did the chicken cross the road?

  • DeadPoet
    15 May 07, 16:54
    MPC version. Please feel free to add on!

    Q: Why did the chicken cross the road?
    A: Because it could not stop for death. (Emily Dickinson)

    Q: Why did the chicken cross the road?
    A: To wander lonely as a cloud. (William Wordsworth)

    Q: Why did the chicken cross the road?
    A: To see heaven in a wild fowl. (William Blake)

    Q: Why did the chicken cross the road?
    A: Because it is the road less travelled. (Robert Frost)

    Q: Why did the chicken cross the road?
    A: To see the cherry hung with snow. (A.E. Housman)

    Q: Why did the chicken cross the road?
    A: To seek a dimpled spider, fat and white. (Robert Frost)

    Q: Why did the chicken cross the road?
    A: Because it is white and there is a red wheel barrow across the road. (William Carlos Williams)

    Q: Why did the chicken cross the road?
    A: Because it is jealous. (Othello)

    Q: Why did the chicken cross the road?
    A: That is not the question. (Hamlet)

    Q: Why did the chicken cross the road?
    A: Et tu, Brute? (Julius Caesar)
