    Cheong Lee San's Choice

    Poem #14 : Structure of Punkd Reality

    His comments

    These 2 entries are getting my attention:

    Poem #14 : Structure of Punkd Reality

    Poem #07 : Oh girl, would you be...

    First, I am not sure if there is a typo in the title, or was it deliberate, but I am ready to forgive that. A title sets the mood for the poem, in my opinion, I am talking about poem # 14, "Structure of Punkd Reality"

    A measure of how much you like a poem is how you relate to it. I don't usually go for rhyming verse, but I think this one is well written. I am still smiling at "there goes my face, skillfully cut." I like the way the story unfolds, all in a breathless rhythm. simply done, but effective.

    As for poem #07 "Oh girl, would you be...", I think it's a quite well-written piece. The imagery is good, not too overwhelmingly sweet or mushy, as one would expect from such a genre. The first strophe already grabs one's attention, so much tenderness expressed in those words. My only gripe is, the following strophes could not sustain the power of the first. Overall, it's an enjoyable read, and I think the writer has chosen his/her words well.

    It's a hard choice, but I have to go for poem #14, as I enjoyed it more. Poem #07 is a very close second.

    General Comments

    Let me say something about the other poems. There are a few poems here which have the potential to be good works but which I feel are not fully developed.

    For example "Ever wondered in Paschendale". The writer writes about selfishness & lost loyalties in battle, and this is a new angle to focus on the poem. Talk about why this is so, the attritional trench warfare, the mud everywhere. and the senseless loss of live that the writer mentioned. A competent poem here, but could be much better.

    Same here for Poem #10, "To the Hills of Death". The problem here is the writer is trying to cram too many images into the poem. Think of a battlefield, for example, Hamburger Hill, imagine your emotional state there, write about it using strong words. War poetry can be beautiful, despite the ugly subject. Poetry is about using less words to describe a big picture.

    I would like to applaud all the participants for their efforts.

    Hari Kumar's Choice

    Poem #11 : Serpent in Disgust

    His comments

    First let me thank you for the opportunity to be one of the judges for your poetry competition. It is indeed an honour. I went through all the fifteen poems over the weekend.

    Before I give you my opinion of what I think was the best poem of the lot, I must confess that I don't think I am qualified enough to be a judge, since I have not formally studied poetry, or for that matter, English literature since I am basically a Technical person by qualification (I am an Engineer) and training. Though I admit I do have a passion for poetry and literature in general, as is evident from my works. Therefore my analysis of poetry is more holistic and comes from the heart rather than have the cold analytical/reductionistic precision of those with degrees in literature who slice up the poor poem into microscopable bits that are prodded and poked and further subjected to different tests.

    I look at the emergent idea in a poem and in the process try to understand the heart of the poet. I am not unduly worried about cliches and meters etc. And I am not impressed by the liguistic gymnastics that you find in some modern poems.

    That said, here is my favorite of the fifteen:

    Poem #11--Serpent in Disgust.

    Although I felt that the title could have been improved, the poem in general was well executed and quite topical. I liked how the poet has avoided mentioning China, which obviously is the subject here. I commend the poet in how cleverly he segued from the cardboard buns (Stanza 1) to the contaminated toothpaste (Stanza 2) and finally to the bad construction in China (Stanza 3). The poet has captured well the greed of a nation in too much of a hurry to change. The only other issue (besides the title) I had of the poem is the usage of "disgust" in the ending. "Serpent in Disgust" somehow does not work. Both in the title and in the ending.

    Regards,

    Hari
