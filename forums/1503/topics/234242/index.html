    As I fall deeper into the mist,
    I find shadows everywhere,
    Life as I had known them there,
    Were like the afternoon air

    Though I may be reminiscing again,
    I know what was there, was there,
    Turning the wheels of time,
    Was never worth the dime

    Life had gone by and all that's left are the memories. The 'afternoon air' is contrasted with the 'mist' from the first line.. One is past, one is present. Also, the 'mist' represents the things that I have done in the past and as ya'all know, the afternoon clears up the mist. So when i say that life as i had known was like the afternoon air, I am emphasizing that it's all gone..

    I hope it makes sense
