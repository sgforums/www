    Secret Box

    When we were made,
    in the same guise and form and shape,
    we were given each a secret box,
    empty from the beginning.

    Over hours and days and months and years,
    when a certain occasion strikes,
    happy or tinged with the blue of neglect,
    with no need for grammar we write them down
    on invisible slips of paper,
    and gently slip them in,
    then lock the box with a key whose location
    we quickly forget. Until the next unhappy event.

    The box is both sacred and profane.

    Then someone touches your life.
    Dark roses bloom in your secret box,
    unsettling the hinges. The key whose location
    you have forgotten sometimes finds its way
    into her hands. You are pleased. Why wouldn't you?

    Now the responsibilities are twinned,
    you slip promises into each other's safehouses,
    watch them grow and prosper with delight.

    It lasts only for a little while.
    Then doubt mists over, a slow cloak of frost,
    and the secret box seals shut.
    Impervious to desperate agitation.

    Then you turn to the other,
    in words you didn't know you possessed,
    you gently bid her leaving.

    She has no want to want your secrets,
    you have no desire to let her in.

    An inexplicable, fallible bind.

    Unless she accepts that you are whole and distinct,
    flawed just as she is. If not more.
    Your worlds may converge, sturdy as a handshake.

    Until then, the invisible walls of the secret box
    folds into itself, like a cold clenched fist, and waits.

    needy visibility.
