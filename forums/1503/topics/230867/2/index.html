    Flawless

    The burning sun stands smugly, hiding behind levels of cloud.

    Like how you fufill my senses, with your brilliant smile.

    Like how you caught my eye, with your flawless beauty.

    Walking The Line

    Thinking ahead, I bought myself a golden
    ticket, an original idea, walked and
    got in line like the rest, beholdened
    to a subscription letter in my hand.
    In front, the women, no -- ruddy giggly girls
    dither in the queue for one of your kisses,
    hardly aware that I've quietly reversed
    back in line, a once-removed missus.
    I don't mind (I lie) if you've had ten, twenty,
    even a hundred (God forbid!) since then.
    I've had my share, dismissed others easily
    from a line not unlike this. Sweet chameleon,
    so what if it ends where the sun doesn't shine?
    Despite the odds, I wish I were last in your line.

    Falling

    The hands of the clock
    have stopped,
    as if to mark the transit
    of inertia into permanent motion.

    The brownstone cracks
    from the brunt of disrepair.

    All is falling.

    In the grand scheme of things
    I fall so others may rise,
    though they remain
    where they are,
    the act of falling
    tricks the mind into accepting

    the way things that are
    and will remain. As a whole, unwhole.

    Just like the winds
    rushing to fill in an opening,
    a beginning
    that begins from what is already missing.

    I become the illusion,
    a comparative one,
    a contrasting one,
    a one that forms a stern letter 'I'
    braced for the weight of the sky.

    In giving myself up, I give away nothing.

    What is left of me
    is no longer right.
    So it moves to move away the excess.
    So it falls to fall behind the rest.

    When it falls.
    If it falls.

    All is false.

    In falling,
    the failings of life's delight
    rise up like the moon
    shrouded in fog, milky and unclear.

    Elementary

    A bastard heart is impossible to quantify.
    It defies logic, existing only in theory,
    a falsifiable fact. No equation is elegant enough
    to equalise this atrophied state.
    Like Schroedinger's cat, it is both alive and dead.

    How do you reason with a soul-ache?
    With a trembling half-smile. You pummel it
    back into subclavian caves, check its sell-by date,
    reach into the back of your throat and vomit
    one promise after the next.

    Bang. It explodes. A cracked bumper hanging
    on a solitary screw. A long slow honeymoon.
    How do you divorce a massive overdraft of
    statutes carved in stone? With an ice pick.

    This bone-hurt sings from the bones
    of your toes to the tip of your nose.
    It lives in a dreaming nightmare, it feeds off
    your sleep. The night wind kissing your lips.

    How do you quantify something so basic,
    so urgent in its quasi-solid state? You can't.
    A heartbreak, a bone-hurt or a soul-ache is
    completely like you, impossible to comprehend.

    The Prize

    The days be weary
    and greys be my companion
    I have seen the sun
    arched high in the sky
    the call of my brothers
    echo in the death and life
    which the desert calls
    the song of misery.

    I have eaten dust
    kicked by a tango of bullets
    I have dreamt of you
    while the smoke of flares
    covered me like a veil.
    Do you ever think
    that I might see you
    again?

    Today we launched at 0600
    the stars above Arabia
    winked at me.
    I entered the mosaic portal
    and I hefted my mistress.
    She gleamed and glistened
    I could almost listen
    to her sweet clicks.

    But something laid me
    down to the sweet earth
    and I saw a daffodil
    beside me.
    It glimmered and I shook.
    I reached for you
    our son, our future.
    The grey cleared
    I stand here,
    clad in peace.
    This is my prize
    my sweet reward.

    Blunt

    In so many aspects,
    in all manners that ought to be well,
    you were like a needle cast in silver,
    only blunt. Unkeen. Poised for piercing.
    Except you were blunt,
    and therefore cruel in your intentions.

    So easily you batter the coffrets
    that house a treacherous heart
    drunk on lost hopes and lost faith.
    And because you were blunt,
    the thick muscled walls cannot give parting,
    in spite of your ministrations.

    So they bruise.
    Patterned like the stars that mark
    the darkest nights; a tapestry of celestial tears.

    And because you were blunt,
    there is no pity in your cruelty,
    no gentleness in your touch.
    Can one blame the heart then, or blame you?

    A conundrum that brooks incomplete reproach

    save an unholy truth: you were blunt.
    And therefore the most memorable, the most unfleeting.

    Construct

    If God were an architect and Life,
    A denominator of base products --
    Are we then mere jellies in a vat,
    Existing for the next stabbing impulse?

    I am Superman with an invisible gun,
    My body is a rough of ones and zeros,
    With entrails gutted out to make room
    for pure, unadulterated vice.

    Save this peccadillo I am anybody:
    I could even be the one who gives you
    your new breasts or a next-to-discrete lift.
    Tear sheets off your face until there is none left.

    Will that be all, lovely Galatea?
    I'll sculpt you a brand new face,
    throw darts at your wild black heart.
    Strangle, silently, this Pygmalion construct.
