    The Modern Soul

    There was a time
    When doors stayed unlocked
    But this age isn't as kind.

    The once green fields of vast horizons
    Now only wastelands of mechanical pollution
    How could they have called this improvement?

    Where there was once the gentleness of sand
    There is only the coldness of cement
    I walk, in sadness and lament.

    I once sat on the branches of the oak tree
    Admiring the clear sky, enjoying the clear air
    Now, only thing left common is me.

    We move in an age so fast
    The old, the weak, the disabled are left behind
    Kindness, a monument of the distant past.

    So here is the modern soul
    Crying out to preserve our Earth bestowed
    Crying out to preserve our heart, once gold.

    As is

    It is as elemental as the air we breathe.
    The mere utterance of its solemn psalm
    brings not reassurance, nor depth nor anchor nor port;
    in fact it brings poor relief to both subject and conveyor.

    But it is as elemental as the air we breathe.
    A spoon struck against a crystal glass,
    a piercing ring through a forbidding gloom.

    The mere utterance of its unbidden psalm
    is not a plea, or a half-hearted demand.
    A cup runneth over, it demands to be sung
    like the melody it is always meant to be:

    at times, redolent in ascending thrills,
    at times, swollen in the valley of sorrows,
    suspended on a tension of toil and time,
    but never on a standstill, only an exacting pause

    like the air we breathe, undeniably elemental:
    Full in its complicity, though steadfastly true.

    A blank between parentheses,
    a prayer aloft on angel wings. It just is.
    Who alone will enter here? Every one and no one.
    Though it brings poor relief to both subject and conveyor
    it is as elemental as the air we breathe; take it, as is.
