    My beloved fellow migraine sufferers, just to let you all know that You Are Not Alone, with a poem I wrote.

    A War We Will Win!

    Like an army of slash hammers
    Pounding on our skulls
    Shattering them into a million pieces
    And trying to conquer us all

    Ravaging at us with such load
    How long more will it attack?
    Our brains might just explode
    Will it stop when our heads crack?

    We all know what it's about
    As we lay in bed and grunt
    To have our heads turn inside-out
    And outside-in and back to front

    Struck with such torment and strife
    Confiscated of our energy and joy
    No tolerance for light or any noise
    An enemy that has come to destroy

    Why does it come at all we ask
    It is indeed not needed anytime
    Gosh, it makes our life such a task
    Whatever we committed that was such a crime?

    The feelings that follow the initial sting
    Causing us emotions of sadness and frustration
    A pain so intense and so throbbing
    We do not deserve this unreasonable punishment!

    Begging for relief while feeling insecure
    At the most inopportune moment itÂ’s unleashed
    Each new phase we shall learn to endure
    We are not obliged to suffer this disease

    Vow not to succumb to this curse
    And to defeat the frightful migraine
    Or our lives might get even worse
    If we ever let it win the game!

    Searching for relief in our plight
    Bearing the pain of our constant chagrin
    We shanÂ’t give up without a fight
    For it is a war we will win!

    - Julyka H.
