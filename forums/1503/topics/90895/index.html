    Usually poems about locations & places of interest are not very popular among readers. (Imagine a poem about Jurong Bird Park or Science Centre)

    The main reason beings such poems are usually descriptive in nature. Some (those nasty critics) may even comment that such poems are basically tourist brochures written in poetic form. What some poets do is to use locations as a kind of background to write something more personal rather than an just an historical account.

    In view of this, I strongly suggest writing these poems as a kind of stepping stone to something more in-depth or as a form of exercise to shape your writing style.

    This is a good poem. Well organize, details, clear and informative. What it lacks is that personal touch that will bond the poem with the readers. I am sure you are on to something big.

    Just my two cents worth.
