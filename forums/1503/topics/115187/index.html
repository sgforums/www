    My Advocates' Complains

    Tell me something my man
    what is it with you these days;
    you have doubts more than the grains of sand,
    more than the hellish scorching glints of ray,
    more than the mortals on this rotting land 5
    or the autumn leaves that withers away;
    more than all the crows in my orchestra band,
    more than the elves in the Enchanted Woods far, faraway...

    Doubts? On what?
    Have I not let you into my abyss-deep cellars, 10
    or peer into my darkest chamber?
    Have I not let you take the reins on my mind's pillars
    and cast all the others asunder?
    Have I not jolly well fool around with you in your parlor,
    or ride with you on the highways of meander? 15
    Doubts? I doubt so...

    Well, you see not what everyone here observes;
    Your train of thoughts've gone real askew,
    very much getting on our nerves;
    Really, your misdeeds are not few, 20
    and such treatment is not what we deserve;
    think of how we refresh you daily like morning dew,
    how you have had divinely exciting times in our turf
    where everyday's joyous thrills are new...

    So I see what you mean my dear advocate; 25
    it's not that I'm shunning you or anything of that nature.
    Just that things have somewhat complicate,
    or rather simplify this mystifying, abstract picture
    which I have painstakingly perused for so long my mate...
    for in it lies the exceedingly crucial juncture 30
    where I can find the trident key to abate
    the raging storms that my mind's agendas so perpetually feature...

    Well then, however disappointed we may be,
    keep in mind we're always there,
    faithfully standing by in the deepest, coldest valley 35
    ever ready with our concern and care;
    with lanterns that never burns out easily
    to light your path and expose the craftiest snare;
    for whatever you desire, we aim to fulfill readily,
    for whatever you want destroyed, there's nothing we won't dare... 40

    Lots of love,
    Your Faithful Advocate(s)

    Christina

    Wish I could, if I have again the chance
    to sit in the same old chair,
    with my mind in a dance
    when out of the blue she came to me
    with a beam on her face so lovely
    into my hands she shoved a piece of paper
    an ad from a journal it was, for me to ponder
    "I know you love to write...
    it's a small writing school
    looking for people to groom, pretty cool"
    "Er...oh, yep, gosh...thanks, really,"
    was only what I could mumble
    darn I wish I had killed myself for that fumble.
    Well, that was just an instant of many others
    in which I did not dare to express much either
    call me a fool, I would not protest;
    instead, I pretty much detest
    myself for being such a coward,
    never dare to take a step forward...
    Anyway, it's all too late to say anything now
    for no shrub can be reaped
    from a land not plowed
    no autumn leaves sailing in the wind
    would ever stop and turn
    no wet embers can once more start to burn
    I can only keep her
    in this secret closet in my heart
    of which I hold not to fall apart,
    and wish her well in her blissful marriage life
    I wish her love , to live and thrive
    though my fire for her I shall always keep alive...

    That will be the day...

    That will be the day,
    when the moon shines no longer the intoxicating light
    when the birds' chirping becomes an irk
    when sunrise is no longer a beautiful sight
    when Spring resigns from his routine shift work

    That will be the day,
    when the rainbow no longer bends a classic arc
    when the waters of the stream gush so erratically
    when no leaves dance no more in the lushy greeny park
    when there lovers loathe to cuddle so intimately

    That will be the day,
    when my heart flutters no longer at the your smile so lovely
    when my ear tingles not a bit at your whisper
    when my chest warms not when there your head rests so cosily
    when my pale face flushes not as I let it your ebony smother

    That will be the day...
    that I say I don't love you...

    The Muahaha Song

    There's this little imp called Tralala
    bouncing here and there singing a looney song
    this song he named Muahaha
    yes, the looney Muahaha song...

    So here's how it goes...

    "Muahaha i go, as i see the guy in the Hugo Boss suit
    swaggering down Shenton Way,
    his haughty eyes hint of his pride in his pursuit,
    that meaningless pursuit of a so-called Elite Way;

    Muahaha i go, as i bump into this chick so voluptuous,
    all glitters of Tiffany's, her ivory arm clutching the renowned checkered bag;
    but like a supposed-to-be masterpiece ruined by an error so preposterous
    her other arm's around a confounded, lascivious old hag;

    Muahaha i go, as i overhear these four single ladies bantering
    of the hunks they drool over and dream about
    of men they need not, they concur though, so firmly
    when intense yearnings harbor in those lonely hearts, no doubt;

    Muahaha i go, as i see the pompous yuppie
    lavishly spending on designer brands and classy dinners
    so wanting of respect with his cheapened money
    bah, those cheapened money from his forefathers.

    So there goes Tralala,
    bouncing along
    happily singing his Muahaha song...

    the tramp

    the wretched tramp, slowly he trudges
    in the muddy path to NoWhere Land
    his tattered trench coat so artistically fudged
    it accentuates his story-telling tan
    he drags his feet forward as such
    like a leaking, blotching manuscript pen
    many a times he stops to sigh
    wondering when his journey'll end
    he looks at the eagles soaring high
    to fly he wishes he really can
    each step he takes, some pieces of him die
    like the colors that fades at the rainbow end
    how he wished of his sorrows he could cry
    but his eyes're as dry as Sahara's sand
    how he wished he could just drop dead and die
    but his life's not his own, he knows his stand
    O lonely tramp O wretched tramp
    why does he live on so wearily
    why does he drag his feet on anyway
    he knows not what's it to be happy
    he can never fathom the meaning of gay
    he will never know of peace and harmony
    of love and tenderness there's no need to say
    but relentless angst and agony
    shall always paint his skies so gray
    woes and blues are his company
    warmth and tenderness keep him at bay
    the devils spite him with sarcasm and mockery
    the angels from him with disgust shun away
    there's no meaning in this life anymore
    ah, anyway there may not be any before
    like a sailor overboard who'll never see the shore
    like a trapped child who can never find the door
    his future will be bleak and unsure
    there'll always be much to endure
    so very much to endure
    endure...
