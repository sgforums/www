    In Memoriam
    (Jan 2003-Oct 2003)

    I wore green
    The day you left.
    The falling rain
    Left me a note
    Of empty speeches
    And broken clauses.

    Where I had slumped,
    The evidence now gone.
    Vanishing times of yore
    And little fireworks
    Every time you called.

    Somehow the steely rain
    Entered my fortified camp
    And snuffed out
    The little flame
    That meekly burnt.

    I saw you
    Two months later
    No words filled
    The cold unflinching air
    Where we sat.

    And even the table
    Was like the Berlin Wall.
    And I could not
    Reach into your heart
    And replant my rose.

    History of my muse and I

    1.

    I remember the cold air
    on your face
    as you looked out
    and watched the raindrops
    fall like gems on a velvet cloth.
    I sat with you
    but you cried.

    My fingers in your moist palm
    entwined like fireflies.
    You held on tight
    but I felt no pain
    and I was content
    just being here.

    The sky was grey
    when we walked down
    and bought us some ice-cream
    but you smiled a little
    and I was glad.

    We cradled the tub of vanilla,
    you licked and I looked
    and the setting sun
    gleamed in your eyes
    whom I worshipped.
    But I still saw a
    solitary
    tear.

    Somehow I woke
    with the phone in my hand
    and your soft voice
    in my ear.
    I turned in a sea
    of grey masques
    and you had a plaid.
    Cute.

    Many afternoons
    I would stare at the clock
    2 meters above me
    as you silently pored
    over the politics of
    Siam and her Kings.
    Then we would go
    and get more
    ice-cream.

    2.

    Somehow, we never
    got out of your clouds
    till the day
    you huddled against me
    with the silent Boeings
    as witnesses to you.
    and I cradled you
    in muted silence

    We soared beyond
    the muted sky
    upon wings made of wax
    and I fell like Icarus
    why didn't you catch me
    why did you let me slide?
    My angel flew
    and I slumped.

    I heard the bad news
    surrounded by fish;
    and one bus stand
    out there in the South
    still bears my scar
    of immovable faith and love.
    And I saw the clouds
    looming.

    The thunderstorm's party
    wrecked me clean
    like a scouring brush
    leaving me with
    one-thirds of a coconut's
    hairy husk
    and no juice.

    I sallied forth to my crusade
    but the walls of your heart
    repulsed me a la Saladin
    at Al Quds.
    But inside
    you were pure of soul.

    I came out of my tunnel
    one dandy day
    and I saw you.
    No more wings.
    But I think I could
    make us a pair
    to fly above those
    grey clouds above the
    old school
    where
    we used to
    dream.

    Finger Food War Blurbs

    Another siren.
    Body parts covering
    The sandy lawns of a hopeful people.
    Freedom is on the march.
    Always.

    Swarthy men
    Led as dogs.
    Fed by teenage girls.
    Freedom of expression
    Unbridled.

    Pimply faces greeted
    The Reaper
    As he walked in the dust
    The Right to Life
    Raped.

    Two boots
    One rifle
    25 years returned
    to God.
    Freedom from evil.

    One President
    One coalition
    Unwilling in deeds
    Willing in voice
    Freedom of patronage.

    Fifteen years
    Same sandy dunes
    One family
    One choice
    God bless America

    Blurbs of the Green

    We sit around all day
    When our PC comes we pray,
    But he just smiles in glee
    For plain OOC dudes are we.

    The RPs all know my name
    Even the MPs had my picture in a frame.
    Silence and a yellow shirt they gave me.
    Did I actually slap my CO? Gee.

    My monitor is turned away
    My head begins to sway.
    Lunch is in fifteen,
    But tonight we're doing OT till Nineteen.

    I hear the 84mm fire off.
    In Neo Tiew's lallang my helmet I doff.
    I see my men sitting under the tree
    But I see more than one killer bee.

    The mynah perched on my head,
    Feels like it's crafted from lead.
    The rain fell for more than a day
    Yet my finger rests on the trigger bay.

    Today is one of my last trials
    My eyes are fastened on the dials.
    The sweet Australian air sings
    And I might get my first wings.

    I have come back for you,
    My long lost pal in blue.
    When I leave the green behind
    You shall turn pink in no time.
