    He lay in a field with his eyes to that azure sky, thick undergrowth bordered it's edges, a ridge on the side bordering the road.

    It was a place someone had been murdered, many years ago.And now, he has come seeking death- his own.

    he lay in the field, watching that impossibly blue sky, on a fair day. What is beyond the Earth,in a sky above concerned not one like him ,to whom into the earth was where he was going. And in that field he lay, only the passage of clouds gave sense of time.

    In that field, obscured from worldly matters, one that had seen better days- a school,a camp- but was now nothing,a blank,a field it was called. So did he think of himself and bemoaned the passing of his own days, the ones filled with laughter, when he loved and was loved in a world that followed time faithfully.

    Traffic sounded occasionally from the road, a couple of meters just over the ridge. It was no matter, nobody would stop.

    To kill one's self required a rational frame of mind, the composure and the will to execute the terrible deed was of paramount importance. He had none of that, he lived wanting to die but was unable to kill himself,so it was hell. But here in this field he lay, not acting was what was required of him.

    Thus he waited faithfully.

    Clouds amassed stopped moving, time froze in a single frame and it started to drizzle. Was it for her or himself that he cried?

    He lay there crying into the rain, something warm blew on his neck, a shaft of pain like light stabbed into his consciousness. and he was up, punching and kicking at that wet shaggy coat of fur, a yelp told him he had made contact and he ran... over the ridge into the path of an incoming car.
