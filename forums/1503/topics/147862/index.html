    Hi DP

    A quick follow-up exercise while the ideas are still "fresh" in everyone's heads.

    This is a follow up to the "metaphor game" ("Religion is like 100plus" etc) and to the Sylvia Plath poem "Metaphors" where she describes her pregnancy in 9 lines with just images.

    Basically the exercise is to create your own "list" poem based on your own past experiences, but to write using only images and metaphors (objects, impressions, sounds etc.)

    Step 1: Think about an important experience in your life. It should trigger some strong emotions - be it joy, or grief, or despair (the stronger the better, though they don't have to be darkly suicidal!). For eg a love affair, a breakup, the first death in the family, 1st child born etc. Remember what it felt like, looked like, tasted like, smelt like, sounded like etc.

    Step 2: Take some senses and objects from your room (either from Where ever you are now or from that important memory). Try and find a connection between them and your important experience. Actually the stranger the connection the better.

    Step 3: Write a poem with 9 lines. Each line should describe your important experience using only images, comparisons, objects, senses. Don't say "I fell in love that night". Say "A door I never knew was locked, fell open" etc. etc.

    Use Plath's METAPHORS poem as a guide to just how wild (and yet still relevant, and suggestive) images can be.

    Have fun!

    Alvin

    Ok forget accidents, make it even simpler (by making it stricter).

    Here's a topic:
    LOVE

    (good, bad, happy, sad, win, lose, too much, too little, top, bottom, up to you)

    Here are nine objects (feel free to vary or add):

    Television
    Wok
    Bed
    Mirror
    Telephone
    Kitchen knife
    Cup of coffee (or tea)
    Newspaper
    Underwear

    Now go write your poem: remember, each line is one description / comparison of love using one (or more) of the objects.
