Goldfish

  • peebrain
    22 May 06, 01:58
    Speak not of me, I am heard poorly
    like a goldfish, round-eyed, 'loft in brackened spit.
    Mediocre fins, familiars to Neptune,
    nap in certain seas; not bright, but gleaming,
    the first waves of light, dancing in striations,
    bathe sands merged in sub-meridian.
    I have but one fin and one tube, I am compelled
    to a swift delivery, a thief cavalier riding
    the hour of a stellar tide's decline.
    Here I trace sound paths along cracking glass,
    the lub-dub of the ocean, this way or that,
    the prodding beak of groupers keen so nearly
    the cracking plod of an unexercised heart.
    Speak not of me, if you were to say
    that I am a goldfish, round-eyed and swollen,
    unelemental, expiring in a somnolent flood.

    Sorry for the hiatus -- been busy of late. And I wanted to let this poem prove for a little while before I comment on your comments. To gain a better-rounded perspective, so to speak.

    The inspiration for "Goldfish" is my recent snorkelling/diving trip to Redang -- so dsnake1 hit the nail on the head.

    But more critically, I wanted to show how out of element the persona is -- she's in an environment that's totally anathematic (goldfish = freshwater, sea = saltwater) and she feels like she's fighting a losing battle, not really belonging anywhere, and doomed to remain on the surface.

    Hence the metaphors of salt, brine, and the imagery of the sea as something poisonous, and murky -- not to everyone, but just her, and people like her.

    Submersed in such an environment, she choses the role of the silent observer, a "thief cavalier" who must try her hardest to harvest the mysteries of the sea before her time runs out ("stellar tide's decline").

    As for the last line ("unelemental, expiring in a somnolent flood") -- it's a reference to how constant humanity is, and how the majority of those that go against the grain are forgotten and buried under the sands of time.
