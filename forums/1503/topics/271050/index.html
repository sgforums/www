    Poem #04

    Serenity
    By Delphian

    Life is transient like passing cloud,
    The world is but an emulous crowd,
    We are just creatures battling too hard,
    Given an inch, yet wish for a yard...

    Birds in air, for foods they fight,
    Trees on land, outgrow for light,
    Fishes in oceans, bite to survive,
    Animals attack, to stay alive...

    Humans trample, to climb the ladder,
    Forsaking God's Word," Love one another."
    Obsessing over luxuries & powers,
    Failing to stop to smell the flowers...

    Close your eyes & soothe your mind,
    Put your issues, all behind,
    Forget you're a stressed out worker,
    You're the child of a loving mother.

    Withdraw for a moment, come with me,
    Let life be of serenity,
    Touch the wind and feel the sun,
    Just for a moment, be "No one".

    Poem #10

    To The Hills of Death
    By outlaw-2

    The hills; the last section to conquer,
    The drums; beating, draining,
    Marching; from crisp booms,
    Marching; till with sludge called blood and mud.

    We raced; marching was hopeless,
    One too many; our fleet fell like flies,
    Blast of flames; we thought it came from hell,
    With tanks of death; one rupture will do.

    The field scorched brown; dominated with embers and warmth,
    Scalding soldiers; who had wielded the flamethrowers,
    To the crest of the hills; we reached,
    To our dismay; we had to scramble.

    Bullets rain upwards; defying Mother Nature,
    Blood poured down; all too similar like a shower
    A dead comrade lay next to me; I showed no expression,
    I had no time; even to fall into depression.

    We scampered; they chased,
    They shot; we fall,
    We retaliate; flames of torture came to us,
    Scotch, whiskey; our drinking supply became fuel.

    Howling; our dreaded voices echoed past the hills,
    Wails of misery; reverberated into my burning ears,
    Skin; wrinkled, charred,
    Extreme heat; our bodies gave up.

    A diabolical chuckle; I turned to look,
    A scorching nozzle; right before my eyes;
    Kill me; I begged,
    None; I was left to die, painfully, slowly.

    Poem #14

    Structure of Punkd Reality
    By Master -_-

    She's always there, come rain or shine,
    Gently tugging her wispy grey hair.
    Her hands dirty from sand and grime,
    Hands cupping up to the air.

    No shelter from the rain nor shade from the tree,
    Hoping to get a dollar or two.
    Positioned in the centre of a busy street,
    Her only possession was a rickety stool.

    I wanted to talk to her but there's something else,
    I hope she won't be offended by my help.
    To buy everything that she normally sells,
    To pierce through her sad history, i slowly delve.

    She looked at me with suspicious eyes,
    Fearing i would snatch her only gold coin.
    It's only right to break the ice,
    By asking her if she was fine.

    Alarmed, i was when she stood up,
    Mustering all but her remaining courage.
    Standing on the stool, her palms to the mouth, cupped.
    She shouted out loud till her face turned orange.

    What the hell, she said i was a thief?
    For trying to help, was it that hard?
    Dumbstruck i was, gritting my teeth,
    There goes my face, skillfully cut.

    I threw away my loose change to her,
    Walking away as fast as i could.
    This day i would not remember,
    I should have just continue eating my food.

    And there i was again, exactly a week later,
    Not there to exact revenge, i hope.
    But what amazed me, an auntie-hater,
    was that she repeated the whole same show.

    I have never been taken in by Nigerian scams,
    Nor Timeshare and MLM deals too.
    But well, I'll be damned,
    An old and wrinkled auntie made me the fool.

    Poem #15

    I wake up, drenched in cold sweat
    By Keii

    I wake up, drenched in cold sweat.
    From a horrible nightmare,
    melancholic and despondent.

    Why am I, inflicted with this pain.
    Pangs of remorse, repeating.
    Like a broken tape, playing.

    I reach in, rustling through my drawer.
    Through overdue paperwork,
    expired coupons and magazines.

    What is this, burning sensation?
    The warmth of leather, cozying.
    The coldness of metal, tingling.

    I take out, what seems to be a revolver.
    Locked and loaded,
    awaiting to claim it's next prey.

    When was it, that I acquired this?
    Might in my hands, empowering.
    Freedom to wield, tempting.

    I walk around, randomly releasing bolts.
    Into innocent bystanders,
    a momentary span of enjoyment.

    Who am I? Unable to control myself.
    Unbearable suffering, consuming.
    A sudden surge of pain, reacting.

    I fall asleep, oblivious to my surroudings.
    Too late to realise,
    how many lives I cruelly took.

    Where am I? Descending into darkness.
    Feels like eternity, relaxing.
    I found my solace, comforting.
