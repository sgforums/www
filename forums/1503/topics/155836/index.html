    Inside one of many small glass cells.

    I am trapped within.
    yet I am free.
    This white snake
    leads me towards a path
    both uninhibited and unruly.
    To you I stretch my palm
    but all I touch is glass
    and somehow
    I see you.
    Your breath stains the door
    and yet I am but in
    one white cubicle
    and not in that mosaic-tiled
    monstrosity with birth-barriers
    planted on the wall,
    a god of reality
    for all you worshippers
    and false priests alike.

    I am here
    And you are across the azure maze,
    a maze of my thoughts
    spiced by the anthem of Cuba
    which makes me just
    a tad bit morose
    Because I cannot tell
    nor confess but only hint
    I fear you to be a bird
    that flies the cage
    upon seeing the honest sky.
    I fear that you might
    leave on a road-trip
    and become a desiccated skull
    on the highway of my uncertainty.
    I fear that I might not
    Be able to love you.
