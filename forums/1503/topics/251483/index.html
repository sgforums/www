    Please read the following poems.

    Dust of Snow by Robert Frost (1874-1963)

    Version 1

    The way a crow
    Shook down on me
    The dust of snow
    From a hemlock tree

    Has given my heart
    A change of mood
    And saved some part
    Of a day I had rued.

    Version 2

    The way that a crow
    shook down right on me
    some snow, sort of like dust,
    from a high hemlock bough
    has given my heart
    a different feeling about things,
    and partly saved
    a day I felt had been wasted.

    Version 3

    How dear the ways of Natue! Lo, you crow
    Precipitated earthward, even on me,
    A pulverous silver essence, dust of snow,
    White benefactions of a hemlock tree;

    Bequeathing (legacy unto my heart!)
    Transfigurations of an erstwhile mood,
    Redeeming a jeweled modicum, wee part
    Of one diurnal unit I had rued.

    Taken from Western Wind: An Introduction to Poetry
