Emptiness

  • eskyhastofall
    26 Nov 04, 14:42
    Mistook a silver of light
    For the warmth of the moon
    How does one spend a long drawn day?
    Drifting in the depths of dreams?
    Or tucking time under the play of thoughts?

    I lay my hand on the softness of my cheek
    The tenderness, how long will it last?
    But the night is still a dusk away
    Although time is slipping from my grasp
    Like the fall of tears from a newly blinded eye

    Turned from the brown of a well trotted path
    Onto a green carpet that leads to a cliff
    Treaded softly on the bridge of hopes
    Lightly linked by tiny wishes
    A dying love, or perhaps a ring

    Today a strong wind blew
    Tomorrow may be dry and stark
    But my life I knew nought of
    Nothing to add to the whimsy bridge
    Those frail emotions that have strengthed all

    Hello deadpoet~~ you got the first stanza right. It's about the person, actually a girl, wondering what to do with her life. yes, she is young. she thought that the day has already gone and yet another night has arrived, instead it is still day and she has no idea what to do.

    In the second stanza, there is a paradox. she acknowledges that she is still young and to her, there is quite some time before she grows old. but time is moving fast and she feels she has no control over it. Yes, the night represent old age and the dusk, youth. you got it right again. but instead of lamenting over her aging, she is (as i would like her to come across as) simple recognising her youth and acknowledging that it would not last. she dont know just how long it will last bcos it is not for her to decide. she could die tomorrow and retain her youth, no one knows.

    as for the last line..."a newly blind eye"...i used this phase bcos a newly blind eye represents a regret. it means that the eye can see before, but now it has lost its sight. and it's when you lose something than you know the value of it and the tears come fiercer than an eye that can see. the girl is feeling aimless right now, but her youth is slipping away as time passes. sight is aliken to youth and blindness to old age. one day, the girl will lose her youth and she will then realise she should have treasure it
