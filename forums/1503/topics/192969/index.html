    Poem #9 Obilvion

    His comments

    Poem 9 is my choice for 1st Place. Poem 10 would be my choice for runner-up.

    Poem 9: Oblivion

    The poem went very well with the photographic image. I also enjoyed the way the poem examined the idea of oblivion from different angles - the old man being oblivious to the world; the world being oblivious to the old man; and the internal oblivion of the old man to everything including himself. The poem flowed well and the line breaks and overall structure of the poem were also aesthetically pleasing.

    Poem 10: Twilight

    This was another interesting effort. I liked the attempt to flesh out the poem with realistic details. To me, the poem was unfortunately spoiled by the fact that it was written in the first person, and the first-person "voice" just didn't feel authentic. That is to say, it didn't sound like what a poor old man sitting on a trishaw would say - "tar-stained lungs"; "the bureaucrats have to bury me"; "the city will not defeat me" etc. If the poem had been written in the 3rd person, then perhaps the poem would have worked better.
