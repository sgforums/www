    A friend of mine told me that he's getting married some time back... He really ain't prepared for the wedding but he thought he would go into it anyhow and see how things go... And if it seems that he might take if he takes it positively, things would just turn out to be quite positive for his marriage....

Who + who = Yes, and Who + wHo = No? Or is it that anybody with anybody would actually holds that certain level of chance for the relationship to work out for a specific period of time....? Non-specific period of time.....?

    If my friend thinks VERY POSITIVELY and is VERY COMMITED to make his relationship work, would it work....? Or would it really matters because it takes 2 hands to clap...? That his thoughts and actions alone is not enough but what his spouse do would matter to a great extent too....? So how many hands actually does it take to clap for the marriage or even a relationship to work.....?

    Still.... I finally thought.... In order for a relationship or even a marriage to work, it would take LOTS of EFFORTS... COMMITMENT from BOTH PARTIES.... Somehow I think the arena has changed... People are more open..... Given more options and more alternatives... More things to handle, more things to 'commit' to..... Life isn't all that simple anymore... Where family and marriage used to be the root support of many's lives... The level of commitment we devote to our relationship nowadays tend not be that at that 'sacred' level unless it's still something very new and 'honoured' by us....

    I must admit that even with LOTS of EFFORTS and COMMITMENT from BOTH PARTIES, things might not work out well and it might not be enough to overcome everything in this now complicated world... But instead, I thought this might be the bare minimum requirement for now....?
