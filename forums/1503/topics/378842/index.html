Hero

  • da-cheese-freakk
    14 Oct 09, 23:23

    When I used to visit, we’d play by the pool

    With blue gleaming drops – crystalline cool –

    Embracing our foots as we splashed and we laughed:

    Teen against child (lucky and daft)

     

    When I used to visit, we’d walk in the mall

    Your arms stretched across its brightly lit halls,

    Fountains, you’d note, and libraries too

    I’d watched enraptured, cheeks dabbed with hue –

     

    Once when I visit, aunt pulled me aside:

    “Your cousin is busy, hear now and abide –

    Five days a week, maybe holidays too

    He’ll serve in the army: there’s no time for you.”

     

    Sweat gushing as you limped to a side;

    Blank pupils as you’d press back – you’d hide –

    The thoughts contorting within that dark mind

    When you’d search for an answer unable to find

     

    Its way to the surface. And

    Each day you’d think of home from your base -

    Of your sweet girlfriend and life in those days

    Untainted by sorrow, free from this haze

     

    Of blinding thought-

     

     

    You won’t get a medal, you “aren’t that good”

    Two years of slopping around in the mud

    For the sake of the country, for the sake of us all

    Yet who will notice when you stumble and fall?

     

    You aren’t noble, ‘cause you were forced

    To join the army, to cry yourself hoarse.

    You didn’t sacrifice, ‘cause who will care

    for the hidden bruises and burdens you bear?

     

    Now when I visit, I silently stare

    At the hero braiding his young sister’s hair

    And the others around me: my friends and my peers

    Bracing themselves to face all their fears

     

    For the sake of the country, or perhaps the law

    Who cares? They’re still heroes, after all.
