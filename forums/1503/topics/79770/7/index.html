    Path alone

    I walk on this street haunted
    by the spirits.
    They don't talk, they
    just watch,
    lurking in the corners..

    With their eyes they glared at me.
    So silently
    I can hear the tick on my watch.
    So endlessly time seems to mock.

    Yet I can't struggle even if they lay their icy fingers on me..
    My heart pounding furiously
    towards a dream that leads down the dark alley.
    The audience seems everywhere but yet again
    it seems like just me.
    The windows and shops that I passed,
    showing a weird being smuggling himself
    across the building.

    He has no eyes. Dark is his heart and world.
    He has no legs. He mimics the cold chilling wind.

    Until memories bring him back again,
    on the spot he was
    glued to.
    For a moment he thought he was dead.
    Separated entity from his body.

    Yet they don't see him as one of them.
    Yet he is none.

    Yet he find no lodging from the long night.
    And the dark day which spells eternity.

    Yet he find no tears on his cheek.
    The face on which the howling wind would gladly steal the tears off him.

    He has no life, yet not dead.
    He is deprived, yet of nothing.

    On this street he walks,
    glued to the spot.

    He can run, he can panic
    like a hysterical man yet
    he is just not being himself.

    He is afraid of the dark,
    yet this is his world.
    His kingdom and his call.

    They longed for his touch.
    Cheering him on,
    bow upon his 'grace'.

    Midnight.
    Here comes a 'Cinderella'.

    A Long Walk from Home

    September, 17.
    A fence I lapped each year.

    Through time the field I walked,
    of empty timings I tried to clock.

    Of a big beautiful sky above me.
    All belonging but to myself.

    The grass which shines my boots,
    not a moment too long before me.

    As of muds and thoughts I dragged through,
    frustrations which accumulated.
    Suffocations which filled my lungs and
    drowned my senses.
    Too soon,
    I thought it's gonna be over.

    But still
    morning light hits me like a shovel on my back.

    Hurrying me on my next day's trip.

    The sky, the field, the fence ahead.

    Anywhere else soon?

    The days without me...

    While I was gone,
    I didn't know
    to whom I belong?

    A glass of dust or stainless steels
    waiting to rust?
    My sense of joy and humour
    buried so deep inside me...
    Mockery it is to the weeps befall me.

    Whilst I stood tall with the iron fist on hand,
    my tender fingers I shall not see
    too instantly.
    Of the time which have slipped through in between them
    into a pile of sand
    not in castle domains
    they stand.

    As I conform myself upon the beliefs that surrounds me,
    human breaths
    took off with my dirt that has failed miserably.
    Hungry groans with filthy hands clinged onto me like bees
    attracted to a drop of honey,
    on a plate in the sink.

    Though sunshine falls in,
    beholds a lovely scene.
    But surely such an ugly painting should not be nice.

    Through angry eyes
    and verbal fights,
    a sponge of foams shall drown the night.
    With murky drains and will to wipe,
    pretty;
    an empty dish of pride.

    is selfishness mixed with despair.
