    Memoirs of a Sleepy Isle

    Sometimes in my dawn,
    the smoke from the ants below,
    those in different colours and
    making different noises
    do waft up to the tiny glass
    windows;
    which encase my towering house
    in a pattern often
    repeated near and close to me.

    Imagine that we should all
    move from the humble earth
    to Valhalla's nest in mere decades.
    Colours and prints assail
    my senses without end.
    Those which were once likened
    to pigeon holes,
    now seem the jewels of the Orient
    albeit to the eyes of antipodes.

    We carried our kin amongst the wet swamps
    now the wet swamp is carefully
    nurtured in a laboratory
    which people do pay to see.
    And the tigers which grandfather
    shot
    are prized and guarded with care.

    The leaders of this nation
    stand warily amongst us men.
    Be it fear or distance
    no one can see through
    the coats of white and
    the poker faces in public debate.

    Have we exchanged a sleepy isle
    for one that is still sleepy
    but inside because of the steroids
    we have eaten to null our true nature?

    And the ants still crawl
    day in day out,
    almost on command
    like a Swiss clock, handmade
    but ironically not known
    for pragmatism
    which is labelled on the many ants that
    shuffle along the metalled road.
