    surreal sorrow

    The teachers frown my way
    exams are round the other street
    and its raining in my
    paradisical life

    all the sorrows of the world on my shoulder
    and i mope over midday meal
    at the kopitiam

    a boy my age looks me in my eye
    slang thick and learnt from the cradle
    asks me for my order with practiced
    cheer

    i answer in perfect english that i
    suddenly feel
    ashamed of

    his body is wiry
    not from gym
    he knows kopi-o from teh tarek and
    laughs with the coffeeshop uncles
    about the gahmen

    my order arrives in seconds
    and he balances four other cups
    to other tables
    joking with regulars
    trying (failing) to mimic with the ang mohs

    but he grins and
    tries

    he collects my empty glass and
    i chide myself for my
    surreal sorrow
    when he worries about the
    rising price of
    coffee beans

    older eyes smile from a
    younger face
    and he has my heart which
    weeps out for him

    but deftly, he opens an old textbook
    when business lulls, and his
    same-eyed companions
    leave him alone

    and he chides me silently, for he
    doesn't want any of my
    surreal sorrow
    (for what do i know?)

    This Last Poem Is For You

    Pain.
    Fills my heart with a wretched stain.
    Agony.
    it overwhems me entirely.

    obviously, we were never meant to be.

    Just today,
    I looked into your eyes and you looked back into mine.
    it was then that i thought of how our fates had entwined.
    was it because of some divine intervention by a higher entity?
    was he planning to end this in a simple tragedy?

    I turned away, leaving these questions unanswered.
    For some answers are better left not founded.

    Whenever I thought of how things were between you and me,
    hope blinds me from the harsh reality.
    I admit. I had deluded myself to believe things could work out,
    but there never was going to be any shower of love. Only eternal drought.

    My heart feels so empty without you filling the crowd.

    Honestly,
    I had seen it coming.
    But upon knowing the truth, I just kept running.
    Away and away and away...

    You were brimming with joy when I saw you hours ago,
    but I wasn't the cause for your euphoria, that I know.
    Apparently, I had lacked that extra something that he possessed.
    Maybe he had taken a step further? Maybe he had earlier confessed?

    All thanks to you, my life has become such a mess.

    It was then, I asked myself.
    How was it that I could hold back my tears?
    How was it that I could conceal it all from my peers?
    After all these years,
    losing someone precious is still one of my many fears.

    Come what may,
    "I had loved you" is all IÂ’m left to say.
    but after what I saw, I know that there's definitely no way.
    absolutely no way I could make known how I've always felt.
    how it was so true that your words made my heart melt.

    But you're never going to know this.

    I've always thought love is what makes people blind.
    blind to the extent that they lose control, or even their mind.
    but now I know,
    cupid's arrows never did undermine my logical thinking.
    I am solely to be blamed for my folly to continue assuming.

    Assuming that things will work out for the better in the end.
    Assuming that to you, I was more than just a friend.

    You want to know something?
    You had sparked my interest to write poems again.
    I only did it for you, never for the sake of fame.
    Just as you are the one who started it all,
    you're also the one who'll end it all.

    This last poem is for you.

    You, and only you.

    Yuhan

    His Song

    Softly he whispers in my ear,
    Gentle yet wistful, delicately clear,
    Lightly it danced on the mild spring breeze,
    Lifting the spirit with apparent ease.

    Tenderly he sings to my heart,
    Kind and loving, deeply passionate,
    Subtly it grew, like a rose it blossomed
    Glowing like ambers in the bosom.

    Ardently he croons to my being,
    Intense and devoted, zealously wooing,
    Fervently it burned with white heat on its flames
    Ablaze, it sets the soul on fire.

    Madly he tears through my mind,
    Quick and violent, vehemently blind,
    Suddenly it rents with the keenness of steel
    Slicing the heart like tender veal

    Coldly he left my empty shell,
    Aloof and distant, eerily hell,
    Silently it faded with the body grown weary
    Leaving naught but a scar in memory.

    An Appointment With Eternity

    Your eyes meet mine
    In a place where
    Language keeps a mistress
    To bear false tongues
    And paid witnesses.
    you look beyond me
    But there are parts of me
    Even well-paid spies
    Could not even swim
    In decadence.

    The captain smiles faraway
    As he fondles the trigger
    "A smoke?"
    "Why not?"
    I threw him a salute
    And the seas of the Med
    Engulf me.
    I saw the ages
    And Granny Smith
    Fall like toys
    Strewn in haste.

    I meet you
    In a grove.
    Of olive husks and a rusty sky
    And we embrace
    With eyes aglow
    In a purifying blindness
