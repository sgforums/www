    The winds that gently softly sigh,
    Seems to signal the end is nigh.
    Once I had joy, once I had bliss,
    All flows forth now like blood from a slit wrist.

    The silence is golden, the silence is heavy.
    The feeling of pain, it is a levy.
    For that game which guys and gals play,
    It can hurt, as clear as day.

    I think of her, I see her smile,
    For her I flew 10000 miles.
    The twinkle always in her eyes,
    The way she gave affectionate sighs.

    It's funny how life can be,
    Fall from light to darkness so suddenly.
    One minute you grin like a lout,
    Next moment you sob your eyes out.

    When all is finally said and done,
    That fact is still that she is gone.
    But, whenever I think of you,
    Always, a tear will fall, my dear French girl.
