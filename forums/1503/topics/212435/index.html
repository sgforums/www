    Just picked this up from the library today. Some totally fascinating poetry here. Hope you guys enjoy reading this one...


    "I'm beginning to know myself. I don't exist"

    I'm beginning to know myself. I don't exist.
    I'm the space between what I'd like to be and what others made of me.
    Or half that space, because there's life there too...
    So that's what I finally am...
    Turn off the light, close the door, stop shuffling your
    slippers out there in the hall.
    Just let me be at ease and all by myself in my room.
    It's a cheap world.

    I think these are feelings or state of dilemmas we've all felt at some point, or maybe many points in our lives To assert our own sense of self or follow the image of what others prefer us to be. The poet, Fernando Pessoa, thru his persona, Alvaro de Campos, felt this dilemma very keenly. The sense of being half of a person, or even nothing at all because he's so lost about who he really is.
    He says:

    Yes, I am I. I myself, just what I turned out to be after all.
    A soft of accessory or leftover,
    The foggy suburbs of my sincere emotion,
    It's me inside of me, it's me.

    (from poem #481- Introducing Alvaro de Campos)
