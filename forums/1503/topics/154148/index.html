    Poem #02: Ghost



    Ghost

    This is the food
    That I eat
    Nourishing my body
    Giving me strength
    To continue
    On this relentless journey

    This is the road
    That I take
    To reach my destination
    Wherever it may be
    Encountering
    Crossroads of every kind

    This is the mind
    That I use
    Figuring out an escape
    From this wreck
    My greatest asset
    My only true asset

    This is the man
    I hardly know
    Whom I have yet to comprehend
    All that is known
    Is that he is there
    He is here.

    This is the ghost
    Haunting me with its omnipresence
    The ever-present shadow
    Of an unforgiving past
    A darkness prescience
    A wound unhealing

    Somnambulism

    Into the unknown,
    I sauntered till the road of endless steps.
    But I have shown,
    I uttered in my own breath of regrets.

    Death denies me,
    As the blade danced across my jugular.
    The morning revives me,
    Across the floor with ladles and spatulas.

    Showered with sweat,
    I awoken with eyes bleeding from fear.
    Her absence i dread,
    The silence from someone i hold so dear.

    Amplified screams,
    It echoed across the void of my mind.
    Sombre dreams,
    I entered across the journey i may find.

    Will it end,
    I wonder as my vein, i slit.
    Death began,
    I prayed,for this asylum i may sit.

    Poem #05: His Song



    His Song

    Softly he whispers in my ear,
    Gentle yet wistful, delicately clear,
    Lightly it danced on the mild spring breeze,
    Lifting the spirit with apparent ease.

    Tenderly he sings to my heart,
    Kind and loving, deeply passionate,
    Subtly it grew, like a rose it blossomed
    Glowing like ambers in the bosom.

    Ardently he croons to my being,
    Intense and devoted, zealously wooing,
    Fervently it burned with white heat on its flames
    Ablaze, it sets the soul on fire.

    Madly he tears through my mind,
    Quick and violent, vehemently blind,
    Suddenly it rents with the keenness of steel
    Slicing the heart like tender veal

    Coldly he left my empty shell,
    Aloof and distant, eerily hell,
    Silently it faded with the body grown weary
    Leaving naught but a scar in memory.

    Poem #06: Our Story



    Our Story

    I turn away from the sunset to see,
    the last moments of his shadow leaving me
    His smile that once so gently engulfed my soul,
    With each of his unnerving frown;
    telling me its time to let go...
    the whole world seems convinced we were just not meant to be,
    But in spite of all our troubles...
    I truly believe only with him do my wings fly free....

    My wings have long turned stagnant;
    since that day he chose to go...
    I have traded them in return for a scar,
    etched and knifed into my broken soul
    My heart still beats and the sunset is still wistfully brilliant,
    But somehow the world has lost its shine....
    Just as my hollowed heart echoes for his return

    I have changed since then,
    with an outer crust of hardened expressions
    Its not that I no longer cared...
    but I do have to shield myself from the poisons of passion...
    My will has gone weak from the tears that I've cried,
    No more tears I promised myself;
    as I left my heart and humanity behind

    I still pray for him every now and then;
    for his safety and health in a foreign land
    And may him and his children grow to see the days,
    when a sunset shows a sorrowed glow.....
    Perhaps he will then see the wings I've lost...
    in the shape of the clouds with thunder as my hollowed echoes....

    Poem #08: When Daylight Comes



    When Daylight Comes

    When daylight comes,
    I long for you.
    You leave me at dawn,
    When I still want you around.

    At night in my dream,
    We are together.
    My arms around you,
    Your arms around me.

    Ah, what ecstasy!
    When we are together.
    A preview of life,
    When we are in holy union.

    I beg you to stay,
    but night grows old.
    You are Cinderella.
    One who leaves at dawn!

    The glass slipper she leaves,
    Is an awful mess.
    A mixture of spilt seed and spilt angst.

    When daylight comes,
    I long for you.
    Waiting for night,
    Waiting for another dream.

    Poem #09: Broken Wings



    Broken Wings

    Hidden in a corner street
    where she moved about discreet
    along with the strays of cats and dogs
    they were the forgotten child of god

    In litter bins she rummaged through
    will she find food she had not a clue
    and on knees she begged a passing girl
    food for the kitten next to her

    Then in winter where the harshest winds blow
    she sang herself to sleep in the cold
    a kitten small and curled in her hold
    we all have a guardian angel, so she was told

    and this winter was long
    the winds were strong
    she sang again to forget a hunger
    sounds she can barely whisper
    but a voice in her heart
    kept saying she must not falter

    Then one long night
    she stopped shivering
    In a light she saw a dove
    descended from heaven,
    with another pair of wings
    are wings for what she's worth

    Up to the stars she had wanted to soar
    but part of her wings she decidedly tore
    and covered the kitten lying there still
    A guardian angel ..she revealed
