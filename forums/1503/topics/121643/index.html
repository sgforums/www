The poetry and prose here are all parts of my personal experiences and record emotions, thoughts and events that happened in my life last year.     Poetry

    Stepping in the Puddles...of past regret and sorrows...

    The rain falls,
    The rain falls,
    The rain pours.

    The lonely traveller treks on the road
    Like a train,
    Doomed to its tracks.
    Alone, one-way.
    There is no sun no shadow no ending.

    The rain falls,
    The rain falls,
    The rain halts.

    Lonely puddles reflecting the rainbow.
    There is a shadow now; there is a hope.

    Colours in life,
    Pot of gold.

    The Lighthouse

    There was a child
    Whose life
    Was grown in rife.

    Deriving warmth only
    From Blood in Veins
    Which chained him like Reins.

    People found just ice
    In him but he could
    Find Nothing.

    Then came a searing Flame
    Which melt the Ice
    And Body purified.

    People found in him
    Beacon of Light.
    Still the boy found Nothing.

    Run, row, ride
    He cannot
    Fight.

    The Tide of Fate
    Will not abate,
    Cannot be sate.

    Lamentation: of God after Dinner

    My tongue it hurts O so much I feele,
    Sometimes bitter, acrid, pungent but never sweet
    As thy life slides downe myne gut;
    What was Ambrosia is now rot
    Which my belly-worlde, in it gall burne
    And you harbinger of sin, decay and
    Addiction, as Innocence is lulled to you;
    Rat to poison, moth to fire,
    Why thence dost thou tremble when thou bringst
    Quaking to the now gall'ess worlde
    Which desires salvation through doome;
    You indictment of Hope and Messiah of the deluded Despaired,
    Make my gullible worlde I created, wave of fooles,
    To put false faith and terrible trust leading to
    Them temulent, always blind;
    And you my childe-creation so effective
    That I too sometimes fail
    To detect thy Potency
    Which through age brings life,
    And I realize that thou is not
    Created from hapless bunch of grapes,
    But from the fallen Nine Sunnes.

    *The fallen Nine Sunnes refers to the Chinese legend of skilled archer Hou Yi, who shot down 9 out of the 10 suns from the sky. The 10 suns were tyrannical as they rose together, causing suffering to the commoners as they produced tremendous amounts of heat.*

    Monologue on Friendship: among Me, Myself and I

    Friendship is a strange thing.
    It can bring you up and down
    With effects so profound
    Which can soothe or singe.

    So then why do some have Lady Luck
    To have the better side
    With friends so bona fide
    While mine merely make my life fuck?

    I think I know the answer,
    "Friendship is like a coin, a nickel.
    I'm just plain unlucky that luck has been a monster
    To me, like Grim Reaper and his sickle."

    And on my death I am awake
    And in my folly I realize
    Friendship is not a bloody dice;
    It is neither luck nor fate.

    Satan came and said to me,
    "Friendship is a foolish trade,
    For vain love you pay with wretched pain.
    So why then do you pay the fee?"

    I smiled to the Devil and shook his hands.
    Maybe we could be friends!
    But God descended and he sighed,
    "Do not listen to Lucifer's lies."

    "For every pain you labor
    To give your friends the Joy,
    You must remember that true friends
    Also suffer to return the favor."

    Suddenly I'm left alone,
    In a strange but familiar zone
    Where I can hear Cerebus barking,
    And Saint Peter's plaintive calling.

    I sat down pondering which path to take.
    Not knowing where to go,
    Not knowing what to do
    For God and Satan both seemed fake.

    Is this treason
    Against my religion?

    No it's not.

    It's reason
    For my Zion.

    Fragile! Handle with Care! Do not Break!

    Serene is the trust I have
    In the mirror which holds my dream
    Intact.

    Blasted and broken now is the mirror
    That used to hold my trust
    Pristine.

    Plastered, cracked and fragmented;
    The trust within is slowly
    Leaking.

    Shards of sharp smithereens
    So abrasive like stainless steel sabres
    Slashing.

    Try as you may
    To fix it back,
    The mirror is imperfect.

    Each piece of glass
    Is a broken faith
    Reflecting countless a mocking face.

    The truth hurts:
    That the fatal thrust that shattered the glass
    Was fuelled only by my Over-trust.

    One-Winged Angel: Flying without Wings

    Lucifer with two Silvery Wings
    Came on swift Winds
    To a little prison cell,
    Where God dwell'd.
    He laughed.

    God binded by three shackles,
    Himself, his Son, the Holy Ghost;
    Lucifer cackles
    As all is lost.
    He ravished the Virgin Mother.

    This is doomsday;
    No moonlight, no sunray.
    Not eternal darkness, not infinite light;
    On-going is a transcendant fight.
    He is of no-concern.

    Oh Wingless Angel you sorry sod;
    Lucifer who sought the freedom that Power brings,
    Don't you see that to take flight from God
    You must first lose you angelic raiment: both Wings?
    He is Imbecile's incarnate.

    Flying without wings
    Or so he thinks.
    Halt Lucifer you stupid lout;
    Please wait before you bail out!
    He freezes.

    Flying?
    I say falling.
    Soaring?
    I say boring.
    He lost his wings.

    Then I turn to look at my fetters.
    How high can I fly with just one wing?
    Height doesn't really matter
    When I'm trapped in a labyrinth.
    God smirks.

    I am the omnipotent One-Winged Angel.
    True darkness from true darkness.
    Power over Lucifer the Falling Angel,
    Who in turn has power over God now in a hearse.
    God dies.

    Flying without wings?

    I have one;
    I'm in between.

    Unfinished Drama...Anticlimax

    Life without love
    Free like a dove.
    There's peace in the sky;
    Lift your wings and let us fly.

    But let's get back to reality.
    It comes crashing down on me.
    I wish I was not in love;
    It's a fatality.

    Alas, injurious love has shot me.
    Cupid the (not-so) lovely deitie.
    Oh, with love on it's tip;
    No different from poisons which kill slowly.

    Maybe this is what love is:
    A seething venom that tests
    Not about how long you can go without it,
    But about how long it takes for you to die from it.

    Love's labyrinth can come crashing down,
    But because I have you here with me,
    I'm safe and sound (or so I hope to think);
    God, please help me!

    Scrambling towards the exit door,
    Tightly hugging you as I run across the crumbling floor.
    The dastardly door has a lock,
    Every second's ticking by on the clock!

    My insides writhe from the love-toxin
    While my environment is reducing to nothing.
    But even a miracle elixir is no antidote
    And that door is probably no escape.

    Seconds left,
    Before an unsightly death.
    Adrenaline pumping,
    Damn parents are grumbling.

    Howabout an anticlimax?
    I'll give you one!

    Tribute to my Brother: Water is thicker than Blood

    Water is thicker than blood!
    Don't laugh! Don't scream and shout.
    Just hear me out!

    The wise men of the past are outdated;
    Here I am to herald a new relationship.
    Look at Genesis when God started.
    Even before light and dark divided
    The world was a watery wasteland.
    Water water everywhere; unseparated.
    Before the beasts and birds roamed, before man came,
    Before blood.
    The Atlantic, The Pacific, The Indian, The Arctic and The Southern,
    All bodies of water so immense and large.
    Unfathomable deep seas which are
    Mysterious, bottomless and lovely;
    What can match their purity?
    So now, let me pose you some challenges,
    To convince you of my theory.

    Find me a body of blood that can match the depth of the oceans.
    You cannot.
    Find me an artery that can match a geyser from the earth.
    You will not.
    Find me a vein that flows smoothly like a shining stream which breeds serenity.
    You shall not.
    Prove to me that blood holds more life than the marine seas.
    Impossible.
    Who says that parentage determines kinship?
    The ignorant.
    Who says that history determines future?
    The obstinate.
    Who says that blood determines closeness?
    The unfortunate.
    Who says that blood determines love?
    The retarded.

    Brothers we are,
    Not bonded by blood
    But by love; we can make
    Blood-ties looks weak.

    We can forge our own blood:
    Crystal clear and sparkling bright.

    Take my Hand

    Suicide can be the best thing in life-
    It's like reaching the destination early.
    Why wait impatiently for time to dive
    When you can bring yourself there surely?

    Dying isn't fearful; we all end there.
    It's the transition between living and dead;
    Between the walking and the laid.
    It's a happy medium so just and fair.

    Death is a great leveller that I agree.
    Plows through all pain,
    Mows down all names;
    Done so effortlessly without a fee.

    Suicide, Dying, Death- Please do not be mistaken
    That they are alluring.
    It's what's in between (birth and death) that compels the forsaken
    To take the shorter path and be daring.

    Let me let you in on a secret
    If you don't want to live a lifetime of regret,
    Just do what's right and end your life now.
    Lots of ways: You can climb up and fly down.
    Maybe that's not preferable, so you could try pills.
    But please do remember to take them to your fill
    If you do not want paramedics to pump them out.
    Alternatively you can hang yourself- makes you dumb so you can't shout.
    Or you can slit some major vessel
    But the bloody mess it makes is a hassle.

    If you feel hurt by his actions
    And you feel guilty that you couldn't do anything,
    Then let me offer you a solution-
    Just take my hand and fly with me.

    Maybe life in death will be better,
    As we cross the worlds and breach the realms;
    When and where nothing else matters.

    To all out there who care for me,
    Please do not bother with a funeral.
    If I didn't care about my mortal body
    And if I left behind my shell callously,
    Then why should you bother with it?
    What life is there left to live if one is dead?

    Why try to make me happy with a grand procession
    When nobody tried in my lifetime of perpetual depression?

    Since the birth of Death.
    He wrestles with the luggage that we brought.
    At least we get to die,
    He cannot.

    Afterall who will claim Death as his own?
    Who will want to create problems for themselves
    By taking the burden of Death?

    Wait.
    "Create problems for themselves."
    Maybe this is why our sciences seek immortality-
    We are eligible candidates to claim Death.

    Misery stems from my mother. She seems to nurture it dutifully, allowing it to sprout and take root on firmer grounds. She flits around my life like a light-footed snake; oh she is a special serpent (more special than the one found in the Garden), she has two feet where others have none. Come hither, dear friends and let us explore these chasmal jaws which house the tenacious fangs (blest with the ability to regenerate) which produce (like clock-work) more than just surplus toxin, but also enough malice, spite and bitterness that would make a nefarious malador of contempt towards my mortality. There is no antivenin for this venom. I could imagine how scientists tremble at the potency of this vile creature and its medical and scientific significance. But looking in retrospect, scientists would never have dreamt that such a beast could ever have walked this earth. She is the stuff of nightmares. Afterall, science only deals with the empirical and rules out the paranormal.

    Suddenly feeling disgusted at the state of the cathedral, he went back towards his bed, in hopes of looking for comfort and perhaps even shelter from the disease called age which plagued the entire structure. He found none as he found himself staring at his rectangular bed framed by four fragile-looking bedposts mirroring the rectangular hole on the brickwall besides his bed. It was framed by four other bricks, anxious to follow in the footsteps of their missing consort; rest was no longer comforting to him. He shifted his attention back to the cold sky which was shut and wished that its doors would yield to the rain once more and open. He did not want to see the diseased gargoyles nor the ailing structure. The rain could blind him temporarily from all that. More rain would not make a difference to how fast the cathedral sunk into the soft mud; the monolithic structure was crushing itself down by its own weight, contributed by time, desire and the moss. The rain would not obey his wish, neither would the dream of a spire. So he closed his eyes, blinding himself from what he chose not to see.

    He saw himself preaching, high up and speaking from the pulpit. But he was not in any of the churches in the city. He was up in the spire, a new spire which shone with lustre. A spire where the wood was varnished till it glowed like luminous marble and where the marble dazzled to mirror his reflection. The bricks were unadulterated of the lecherous lichens and moss and the gargoyles for once, actually looked healthy and happy despite their task of eternal damnation to safeguard this marvellous structure. His sermon echoed down the spire, just as the spire echoed perfection down to its foundation. Yet, there was something not quite right about the entire affair. It was peculiarly quiet, lacking the familiar ambience of a disgruntled yet ostensibly patient crowd. The people who so often complained. Were they all dead from the plague of reality? The tranquility was shattered as the familiar wind, which he had grown accustomed yet not agreeable with, howled into his ears, forcing his eyes open. The sudden outburst shocked him as it shocked his vision of those pristine walls which held the spire he so dearly wanted. The rude interruption did more than just shock him and his spire, but also the bricks by his bed. Eagerly, another brick fell onto the cold, stern floor, happy that its wish was finally granted. Joceline wished that the imminent decay of the cathedral would be as imminent as the construction of the additional spire. But it was only a wish. It was a long time since God last fulfilled one.

    *This was a piece of creative writing done to emulate a writer's style. The writer in question is William Golding, and the novel in question is The Spire. Obviously, it was an exam question for one of my A level literature prelims.*
