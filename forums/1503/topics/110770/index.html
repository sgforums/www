    Bleed, M. H Heng, 4th November 2004, 12mn?
    Title by hengzhj

    Like a spy clad in covert,
    Love stabs me with a stiletto.
    From back to front,
    My tunic drinks the blood I leak.
    If I think of you,
    The blade draws in.
    If I long for your caress,
    Anaesthesia but to no avail.
    May I love you,
    But never lust.
    If I sin, may this blade shatter and rust.
    And let me meet my maker.

    when daylight comes, M. H. Heng, 13 October 2004, 5 pm

    When daylight comes,
    I long for you.
    You leave me at dawn,
    When I still want you around.

    At night in my dream,
    We are together.
    My arms around you,
    Your arms around me.

    Ah, what ecstasy!
    When we are together.
    A preview of life,
    When we are in holy union.

    I beg you to stay,
    but night grows old.
    You are Cinderella.
    One who leaves at dawn!

    The glass slipper she leaves,
    Is an awful mess.
    A mixture of spilt seed and spilt angst.

    When daylight comes,
    I long for you.
    Waiting for night,
    Waiting for another dream.

    flowers

    i

    The flowers for you were never presented,
    i left them on my shelf.
    They will whither and dry up,
    Or haven't they already?
    Maybe they will live again
    one day when my courage blooms.

    ii

    The flowers for you were never presented,
    for my courage comes too late.
    Father Time waits for no man,
    and may I say no flower too.
    Because petals fall, and stems rot,
    and buds do not give birth.
    All my praying come to naught,
    my tears, are they of worth?

    iii

    If there was another ending,
    then let my faith increase.
    If trust in God unwavering,
    then I shall stop my erring.
    The flowers may not un-wither,
    but neither does love un-grow.
    So let the young plants blossom,
    and then I will confess.
    Grant to us a happy ending,
    as seamless destiny flows.

    Descent into sleep, M. H. Heng, 6 April 2005, 3 AM

    As I descend into the darkness of my sleep,
    I hear the sounds of the world vanishing.
    When I turn around,
    A door creaks open.

    There I will see you.

    From then on,
    Our smiles turn to kisses.
    From then on,
    Our embraces turn to climaxes.

    But at the apex of our unity,
    You vanish half way,
    I am left to release my seed helplessly,
    Thinking that you are still here.

    Once again you leave behind your glass slipper,
    Filled with my overflowed emotions.
    How I love you,
    My Cinderella.

    I long for you again,
    But would that be a sin?
    For now, I will remove the evidence of my crime.
    Lest I be mocked and shamed.
