  • the Bear
    24 Aug 04, 01:27
    Singapore's my home
    Even when I thought of leaving
    But I've learnt
    Happiness elsewhere may be fleeting

    I wander around
    To places and roads few travel
    I stop I smile
    Coz I can't help but marvel

    I stroll around
    Arab Street with its minarets
    Stop for some sheesha
    Or some of their fragrant cigarettes

    Places like Changi Village
    Call to me with their quiet serenity
    Reminding me of how we can be
    Quiet yet happy in our simple humility

    Our shopping malls
    Rival the best on God's Earth
    Oh the hustle and bustle
    Provides joy and mirth

    There is so much more
    For her citizens to do and see
    Yet so many fly off
    Seduced by promises and what may be

    But this citizen has found
    Contentment's elusive key
    For one is only as happy
    As one plans and decides to be

    So for now this soul
    Will call Singapore his home
    For happiness can be found
    Wherever he may roam
