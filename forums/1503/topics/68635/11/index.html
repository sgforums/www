    Daemon's Quill

    I am spurned by the chilling touch of the daemon,
    Whose lust for my blood would never be satisfied.
    Yet the quill has turned, filling much of the poem,
    Which must be the daemon's work personified.

    The tip was dipped into red crimson ink,
    And his fangs lingering near my neck.
    The daemon and its quill, a gruesome link,
    And his touch singling out near my back.

    The verse that roared inside my mind,
    Came out from nowhere.
    The curse that stood right behind,
    Was telling me to beware.

    Oh, what a time for me to have my plight?
    To listen to people grumbling about others,
    When these people had once regarded brothers?
    The clock strucked thirteen tonight,
    For the rain lost her red moonlight.

    The clock strucked thirteen tonight,
    Oh, who is to judge who is wrong or right?
    To listen to people grumbling about friend,
    Turned on each other into strangers in the end?
    The clock strucked thirteen tonight,
    For the joy had lost the cloud from her sight.

    The clock strucked thirteen tonight,
    Oh, forgive me for I could not shred any light.
    To tell on others when I'm as blind as the rest,
    For them I had never fought for with any zest.
    The clock strucked thirteen tonight,
    For the star has lost his mind in the blight.

    The clock had strucked thirteen tonight,
    Oh, all the zeros gone from here with flight.
    To one's demise again is a good way out,
    Dear friend, do I have any doubt?
    The clocked had strucked thirteen tonight.

    Ravaged

    Nails in my head,
    What holes they make.
    Blood flows over my eyes,
    I go blind, oh I go blind.

    The elephant told me where to go,
    What bus to take, I do not know.
    The numbers I cannot see,
    The elephant forgot to tell me.

    I saw my best friend the day before,
    Remains of a meal drips on the floor.
    Bare walls pale they mock and laugh,
    I won't see her till next year.

    7 days, 7 nights, 7 hours of light,
    The trees dance around me in fright.
    They sway and sway and stumble and tumble,
    Splinters of wood pierce my maimed eyes.

    My hands in front, I feel the way,
    I hear not the bus in the bay.
    The ground is cold, it's rough and hard,
    My legs the wheels on the bus they crush.

    Empty dishes lying cold.
    Bones in them long stripped bare.
    Devil girl she looks at me,
    Glint in her eyes and a grin on her face.

    I drag myself on the cold rough ground,
    Legs crushed, eyes blind, mind unsound.
    Grit n dirt fills my mouth,
    Searing sun burns my soul.

    The flash of light I cannot see,
    The stabbing pain I do not feel.
    When you live in darkness like me,
    You shall not know when death claims ye.
