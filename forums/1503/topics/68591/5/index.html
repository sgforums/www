    this was written when I helped to paint a house for an Indian family 3-4 yrs back

    The dilapidated house
    Lives a family of six
    An ex-convict father
    Their childhood seemed like a loner's road

    An afternoon passed by
    I helped work on the torn ceiling
    The dust and cobwebs
    A century's worth of untold tales

    I saw the laughter through the tears
    The pain behind those sunken eyes
    Smiles revealed on their faces
    Heart's in agony, so real...

    My heart aches for them
    For a father whose love was never there
    Struggles they faced
    I prayed it'll make them stronger each day

    My duty was done
    A makeover of the house
    One last glance,
    I walked away, heavy hearted..
    Pondering where their future lies..

    WIth a Variable Key

    WIth a Variable Key
    You unlocked the house in which
    drifts the snow of that left unspoken.
    Always what key you choose
    depends on the blood that spurts
    from your eye or your mouth or your ear
    You vary the key, you vary the word
    that is free to drift with the flakes
    What snowball will form around the word
    depends on the wind that rebuffs you
