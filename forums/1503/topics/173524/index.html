    Results Day

    He sits in his chair
    Waiting
    In utter trepidation
    Waiting for the time to come.

    The clock is on the wall
    Ticking.
    Ticking.
    With every second that passes
    It seems to get louder.
    And louder.

    He feels a tickle
    Ticklish.
    Should he answer the call of nature
    Once again
    For the umpteenth time?

    Five o'clock
    Oh, no.
    The time has come
    For the reckoning.
    And a reckoning there shall be.

    Sweat pouring down his face
    Shivering.
    Trembling.
    Hardly daring to gaze his eyes
    Upon what lies before him.

    Oh, my.

    The Succubus

    Black was the color of her long straight hair
    Red was the glow of her eyes
    Lust was what controlled her instincts
    As the Succubus prowled the dark streets in the night

    She wished for neither gold nor fame
    But for the man who slept in the small house
    Quietly she moved
    Yet heavily she breathed

    In the dim light of the full moon
    Her slim and slender shape was revealed
    In the semi-darkness she crawled up the walls
    As the man in the room stirred

    She reached the side of his bed
    And the man moved a little
    His eyes still closed
    His mouth open

    Undressing the sleeping man
    The succubus breathed in his scent
    Ah how handsome he was
    She thought

    Lust overcoming caution she reached down
    And as she straddled the man he seemed to groan
    Her hand caressed his muscled body
    But the man only snored

    As the dawn broke the man stirred once more
    And she leaned forward to kiss him
    The succubus turned to leave
    And gave the sleeping man one final glance

    To a bright young spark

    There is no doubt
    that your ideas are wonderful
    and if executed, will make
    the world ideal. However,

    the world is not ideal
    and thus, will not submit
    to ideas that require ideal
    morals and ideally,
    selfless individuals
    who will execute them.

    "Why not? Why not?" You demanded
    I explain, why cannot. You waved
    your hands about, frantic like the wings
    of a dragonfly. You paced, and skipped
    here and back, like a water strider
    searching for a mate.

    I cannot agree with your ideas, so you forced
    a bookmark into a closed book,
    and laughed.

    Valentine

    Because I have learned from you the art of maiming,
    I hang my fruit, sectioned and unwhole, in a bowl
    of shadows scalped from the deep of unyielding night.

    Because I have learned from you the art of leaving,
    I prance and strut, stage left and right, sidestepping
    the bark of the director's cut. Always, an invisible longing

    Because I have learned from you the art of loving,
    By association I have learned to maim and leave
    a pale moon ring, an onion, a scent that clings to a knife

    (like yours)

    Because I have learned from you to learn my lessons well,
    I carve my lessons in abstract. I write to you a valentine.

    I have begun

    I have begun to wear your habits like a second skin.
    The stars and nightwind are sworn witnesses
    to this shadow spectacle: invariably
    I gnash my teeth in throes of sleep.

    Shredding myself awake, with great familiarity.
    I catch myself: (I caught you when
    your petulant mouth refuses rest, when
    your searching hands turn clammy in my wake.)

    Beyond the span of dissolved silence,
    do you catch yourself muttering a exaltation
    that used to be mine, replicate twin-like
    gestures like Gemini in flux?

    I've learnt to bury this transference,
    my dirty pretty secret. Like an
    emollient unguent your essence worms gangrenous
    into liverspotted skin; A half-worn lyric,
    a half-healed thorn: you're all of them.
    I'm afraid (say it!) that you'll never be gone.

    Scab

    Fingers and nails, feeling the sides
    of a scab in idle pleasure --
    something about the hardness
    roughness that is your own and yet
    not your own; that edge
    that your fingernail digs into,
    the skin peeping from under is smooth
    and thin, ripples like a plastic sheet
    as you test its tenacity.

    Your fingers explore. The scab
    threatens to tear the new skin
    along with it if you insist.

    But no, you think. You think
    if you do it slowly, you could just
    pull the dry ugly pad off. The wound
    bleeds again. A new scab will form
    and this time, it will leave a scar.

    Portside

    The northern winds, once proud, once vengeful,
    have collapsed on themselves,
    shouldering a wrath keener then the sirens.

    It is their incumbent, their one sole heir,
    soaring towards a horizon, receding
    beneath the wings of the scarlet ibis,
    like hope -- faint tendrils, formless, yet substantial.

    On a painted boat glazed wet with intent,
    a lone traveller gazes beyond certainty,
    into the wild expanse of water and salt.

    Living, breathing , consuming air as if he were fire,
    wavering in a coat of black, blue and crimson
    without direction, for long they have ceased
    to whittle edges off stubborn whetstones,
    to lumber a finite crux to a point beyond flux.

    As the sails fold and billow, imperfect dreams rise and ebb.

    The Crying Tree

    Dumbly, you spread your arms, a silent "why"
    as if to question your wavering franchise,
    slake the futility of wild protuberant lust.
    I carve a careful girdle upon your virgin hips

    like a lover coaxing ecstasy, with beaded intent:
    fear changes the consistency of your fount.
    Your tears, hot and moist like bleached burnt sugar
    trickle tersly into thieving palms, waiting;

    infinite patience, in the veins of commerce
    I slat the boughs of your disavowed health,
    laughing as strangled screams drip from your
    descendent heirs, the ones that turn their faith

    away from trials of fire you turn your head
    as I suck your last tear, and turn away.
