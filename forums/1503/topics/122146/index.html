    the major categories are
    - Wu Yan (5 words per line)
    - Qi Yan (7 words per line)

    further break down into Jue Ju (4 lines), Luu Shi (8 lines, usually), Gu Shi (longer and can dun rhyme or even prose-style) and Yue Fu (singable poems)

    most prosperous period for chinese poems is Tang Dynasty tt time
    but can be chopped into 4 eras......each era having own poets and style and theme of poems
    1st era....Chu Tang....initial Tang.....still considered a warring state....so most ppl write about wars, family breakups.....tone usually gloomy
    2nd era.....Sheng Tang.....Prosperous Tang......war over, everyone living well...ppl generally happy......so the poems become more on kinship, frenship, scenery, travels.....
    3rd era.....Zhong Tang......mid-Tang....though not as good as Sheng Tang, but in general still ok...mix of cheers and tears for this period......
    4th era.......Wan Tang......late Tang......war eminent, Emperor cannot make it.....brings back the gloom again....

    after Tang comes Song.......where Song Ci takes over
    Song Ci are prose more than poems and they can get quite lengthy at times.....
