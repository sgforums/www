[Poem] The Battle

  • Aeolius77
    14 Aug 07, 00:41
    They sat down in the grid of grey desks, pens poised,
    minds a mass of colours:
    Red and blue and yellow with frustration, fear and eagerness.
    Grey with that dull sense of deja vu:

    That familiar vision of sitting at the grey table, badly vandalized,
    glazed eyes staring blankly
    at that piece of evil on the table.

    Of course in this epochal battle,
    each head, bent towards the table in concentration
    tells its own story of victory and defeat.

    Having had a hand in orchestrating this,
    by turns I prayed in intercession
    for clarity of minds,
    by turns I shook my head
    at their muddied foolishness.

    Young soldiers in battle, some poised for war, equipped, their mouths set, their belts girded.
    And yet still
    Some bewildered some crying inside some shivering some yawning some thinking of a love
    waiting outside.

    They played their chess now.
    I'd played mine awhile back, setting the pieces and the traps that
    I'd spent the last months warning them against.

    Now will these sheep learn from their wolverine tutor?
