    Song of Woe

    Along a vein bent by time and age,
    A long time I suffered a nagging ache
    As I struggled under the watery weight,
    I stretched hard and heard it break.

    A violent shudder went up my spine
    And all the way down that aching vein
    As I trembled from time and time
    My body swelled and surged in pain.

    Across the whole that ripple went,
    A quiet undercurrent, hidden within.
    Gathering force that is deadly to vent
    As it coasts, my agony begins.

    With mighty swipes and crashing blows,
    With a deadly rhythm the current pounds.
    Reaching for the highest point it goes,
    Amidst the desperate cries and frightful sounds.

    The rage is spent and the tide subsides,
    As my body is whole again
    For the devastation brought to innocent lives
    I now cry in vain.

    His Song
    Softly he whispers in my ear,
    Gentle yet wistful, delicately clear,
    Lightly it danced on the mild spring breeze,
    Lifting the spirits with apparent ease.

    Tenderly he sings to my heart,
    Kind and loving, deeply passionate,
    Subtly it grew, like a rose it blossomed
    Glowing like ambers in the bosom.

    Ardently he croons to my being,
    Intense and devoted, zealously wooing,
    Fervently it burned with white heat on its flames
    Ablaze, it sets the soul on fire.

    Madly he tears through my mind,
    Quick and violent, vehemently blind,
    Suddenly it rents with the keenness of steel
    Slicing the heart like tender veal

    Coldly he left my empty shell,
    Aloof and distant, eerily hell,
    Silently it faded with the body grown weary
    Leaving naught but a scar in memory.
