    Dust

    You can see the sinister
    crooked micro-fingers pointing
    at your lack of housekeeping.

    Bits of you falling apart
    inside,
    mingling with the wastes
    of a world falling apart
    outside, that enters through
    an open window from
    where you watch
    homebound crowds bargain
    for right of way with traffic lights
    and yellow boxes.

    Ignore the rapping on the door,
    pretend
    no one is in;
    you will not want a visitor
    to see the dust you breathe.

    (No-holds barred feedback welcomed. )
