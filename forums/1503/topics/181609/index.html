Angel Wings

  • peebrain

    I am the sole survivor
    of the red heat that swarmed into our town
    seventy years ago, before the bugles
    cracked in stunned abandonment,
    before the streets collapsed from the insanity
    of kinsmen running like yahoos with liquid skin
    pooling around their knees, eyeballs bulging from
    the simple physics of expansion.
    My business is words, yet there is none
    left to explain the vast
    penumbra of white amber black.

    I am the sole survivor,
    I am alone with the dead.
    Scraping congealed veins and muscles
    in strips, off cobble stones on streets where
    I had my first kiss, my second, my third --
    then rinsing them in a soup of lye and thyme.
    I hurl sated buckets over the bridge like
    showers of red roses trailing a bride's veil.
    From our withered hearts, perhaps
    a mass murderer lurks, waiting to strike,
    waiting to dig his bloody chipped claws
    into an artery, sever with a flick of his --

    I am the sole survivor,
    trapped in a deconstructed armoire,
    devious splinters reaching into hell
    to raise fire and brimstone to my knee.
    The sea is a boiling crimson,
    while vultures watch the skies hungrily
    with their putrid beaks. They mourn for what
    is denied to them: the scent of decay
    steeping in rot. They stuff promises
    in my mouth and cackle as I vomit
    them out onto my feet.

    I am the sole survivor,
    but penance will come in time:
    I fasten crude leather straps
    on blistered, mangled limbs,
    draw bloody curlicues on waxened feather sheaths.
    On my angel wings -
    protruding in stark relief - I rub
    salve dangerously, with simmering intent.
    As I stood tightly at the precipice,
    throwing my hair back as my last act of defiance:
    Everyone forgets that Icarus also flew
    before his fall. It is the same when love fails,
    and breaks Atlas's courageous back.

    I may have come to the end of my triumph,
    but this will not be my failing:
    In falling, I savour flight's sweet delight.

    --------------------------

    Something I wrote half a year ago.

    Sorry I wasn't able to reply earlier. Deadlines have a knack of putting non-work stuff by the wayside.

    I wrote this poem with a specific image in mind: Angel wings -- the shoulder blades of one's back. From there, the piece evolved to a post-apocalyptic exposition where I imagined a world so steeped in chaos, the only thing that makes sense any more is one's individuality, defiance and strength of spirit.

    That said, all of your interpretations make sense -- that's the beauty of poetry, innit?

    Love, like Liz said, represents the ultimate loss of control. It wields the potential to destroy as profoundly as it fills a person with immense courage to face the unknown.

    This poem alludes to that, for sure. But it is my aim (when I wrote this) to paint a picture that has its beginnings in abject desolution and bleakness, and ends with a salient triumph and optimism -- however small it is -- to remind us that there is light at the end of the tunnel.
