    My mind is like my foot
    So lost easily in the woods
    My foot is like a island
    So easily lost in the world

    Oceans surround my island
    Imprisoning me in solitude
    I'm not high on altitude
    Not even a lighthouse can help me see
    Beyond the vast ocean and sea

    Wait for the siren's call
    That's my last and only shore
    Until i find the shell broken to its core
    How can there be solitude without even a cage?

    The ocean pulls me under
    My mind tears me asunder
    Unfair the despair
    Lonely and the melancholy

    Might as well kill me
    Peel me from the skin
    Tear me from the rine
    Break me from the bones
    All detached and desolated

    I have a question for you, what do you want your poem to achieve? If you want your reader to simply understand your poem, I think to a certain extent you have achieved that. (I have read poems so abstract that none of the readers know what the heck they are talking about, luckily none of the poems posted here are like those)

    But if you want your poem to stand out, to be different from the hundred of thousands of Â“darkÂ” poems out there, or if you want your poem to leave such a deep impression on your readers that 10 Â– 20 years down the road they can still recall it, there is still quite a distance to go.

    When I look at a poem the first question I ask myself is do I understand what the poem is about? If the answer is no, why is it so? Is it because I am stupid or is it because it is too abstract? Most poems have no problem achieving the first level.

    At the second level I start to get picky. I focus on techniques, such as spelling, punctuation, rhythm, sound, image, etc. Here is where the conflicts arise. Usually I avoid commenting at this level because we need to have a certain understanding to communicate effectively here.

    At the third level, I look beyond the surface of the poem, and the techniques used and go for an in-depth analysis. If I manage to reach this level, usually I will remember that poem forever. Personally this is the most interesting level.
