    ============================================

    The Dance (2)
    A lone shade
    mutely waits, breathing
    snake-like wisps spiraling
    in the humid breeze.

    A nameless tune
    hummed softly, solo
    against the distant chorus
    of midnight traffic.

    The corner window
    on the top floor,
    singled out
    from the fluorescent parade.

    Twin silhouettes
    waltzes, on the lighted screen,
    tangled and intertwined
    in a liberated dance.

    A callous epitaph
    mourned to opened eyes,
    dissipating the warmth
    of the forgotten spectre.

    the poem basically describes a scene where a person/spirit of a person stands on the outside, looking in through a window that he/she is familiar with. However, instead of one person i.e. the partner, he/she sees two people inside.

    I made it a descriptive poem because rather than me telling the reader what the person/spirit is feeling, i would like to reader to imagine himself/herself as the protaganist and experience how it would feel if it happened to them...

    it is a little voyeuristic... but it's the kind of feeling where sometimes we just want to watch silently what our loved ones or partners are doing... that's the kind of feeling I'm exploring in this one...
