Nonsensical Poem #3: The Silent Killer

  • tare
    11 Nov 05, 14:41
    The door closes,
    All eyes are on the row
    Of numbers
    Taking turns to glow.

    Without warning,
    The silent killer emerges,
    Spreading its poison
    Among the group of strangers.

    The lady in powersuit,
    Her thick layer of rouge
    Fails to conceal
    Her face that is turning blue.

    One after another,
    Sting by the poison,
    Uneasy rufflings
    As impatience rises.

    With stoic faces,
    Pretending it's absence,
    Dignity of the guilty
    Temporarily protected.

    The door opens,
    Victims scramble out
    Gasping for air,
    Fresh air of freedom.

    Ode to the damn Beans

    Alas 'Twas the Beans I ate
    So I can't blame it all to fate
    They look so harmless in the noon
    Innocently sitting on my spoon

    Ketchup-laden those Beans were
    Now the objects of my Fear
    As they travelled uncontrolled
    their journey I find hard to hold

    via my airducts they speedily sped
    as towards the exit I hastily fled
    Made my way through shelves and aisles
    Through stationery, books and files
    Bewildered readers reading books
    giving me disgruntled looks

    Can't they see what I have done
    Trying to save them! Not having Fun!
    Before the escape of the Fume
    Before they meet impending doom?!

    Resentful, I paused amidst a throng
    of readers, and though it felt so wrong
    It was with pleasure, I released
    It was quite some time before I ceased

    I am afraid to say the suspicion came
    not at once, but all the same
    stealthy glances all around
    disgust measured by the pound

    But by then I am far away
    from the scene of the crime, you might say
