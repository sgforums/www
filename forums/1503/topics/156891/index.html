    This Last Poem Is For You

    Pain.
    Fills my heart with a wretched stain.
    Agony.
    it overwhems me entirely.

    obviously, we were never meant to be.

    Just today,
    I looked into your eyes and you looked back into mine.
    it was then that i thought of how our fates had entwined.
    was it because of some divine intervention by a higher entity?
    was he planning to end this in a simple tragedy?

    I turned away, leaving these questions unanswered.
    For some answers are better left not founded.

    Whenever I thought of how things were between you and me,
    hope blinds me from the harsh reality.
    I admit. I had deluded myself to believe things could work out,
    but there never was going to be any shower of love. Only eternal drought.

    My heart feels so empty without you filling the crowd.

    Honestly,
    I had seen it coming.
    But upon knowing the truth, I just kept running.
    Away and away and away...

    You were brimming with joy when I saw you hours ago,
    but I wasn't the cause for your euphoria, that I know.
    Apparently, I had lacked that extra something that he possessed.
    Maybe he had taken a step further? Maybe he had earlier confessed?

    All thanks to you, my life has become such a mess.

    It was then, I asked myself.
    How was it that I could hold back my tears?
    How was it that I could conceal it all from my peers?
    After all these years,
    losing someone precious is still one of my many fears.

    Come what may,
    "I had loved you" is all IÂ’m left to say.
    but after what I saw, I know that there's definitely no way.
    absolutely no way I could make known how I've always felt.
    how it was so true that your words made my heart melt.

    But you're never going to know this.

    I've always thought love is what makes people blind.
    blind to the extent that they lose control, or even their mind.
    but now I know,
    cupid's arrows never did undermine my logical thinking.
    I am solely to be blamed for my folly to continue assuming.

    Assuming that things will work out for the better in the end.
    Assuming that to you, I was more than just a friend.

    You want to know something?
    You had sparked my interest to write poems again.
    I only did it for you, never for the sake of fame.
    Just as you are the one who started it all,
    you're also the one who'll end it all.

    This last poem is for you.

    You, and only you.

    Yuhan

    apparently, my perception of poetry is very much...different. i guess i've not taken it seriously enough and appreciate it. here are a few more which i had done months back, let me know if it's any better, or worse in that matter. still, i appreciate the comments i've received. thank you for sparing your time.

    -Whenever-

    whenever i'm near you,
    my heart misses a beat or two.
    whenever you enter my view,
    the emotions i feel are nothing new.

    whenever i hear your melodious voice,
    ignoring you is not a choice.
    whenever you expel that particular scent,
    my mind goes completely blank.

    whenver i savour the delicacies you concoct,
    i am like 'oh my god!'.
    whenver you come over and say hi,
    i never want to say goodbye.

    whenever you put on that smile,
    my day becomes colourful, not dull.
    whenever you give me that wink,
    i simply cease to think.

    whenever you come to me for comfort,
    i'll give you everything but grief and hurt.
    whenever you need a shoulder to cry on,
    i'll be there, even if it takes till dawn.

    whenever you need a hand to hold,
    whenever you are feeling cold,
    whenever you have a story to be told,
    i'll be by your side, till you grow old.

    Now,
    whenever i'm all alone,
    whenever i'm all by myself at home,
    I wonder,
    when will you ever be more than a friend?
    when will you ever say 'yes, i can'?
    And,
    if you ever love me,
    when?

    -What If-

    What if the world we live in is an illusion?

    What if the feelings we conceive are all a delusion?

    What if all our moral values were merely for confusion?

    Everything is a matter of perception.

    -DayDream-

    I often have this daydream.
    I'm all alone at the bus stop.
    the rain, it just won't stop.
    there's no one else.
    just myself
    and the rain creeping in, with utmost stealth.
    there's no one else, no one in sight.
    I think to myself, this isn't right.

    it's getting dark. soon it'll be night.

    I look around,
    there's no one to be found.
    no one making any sound.
    I look down.

    My shoes are wet.

    Without a hint of her arrival,
    I still know it's her. It's undeniable.
    She's making her way into the bus stop. so says my gut.
    it's just like how she had walked right into my heart.

    unknowingly.

    gracefully.

    is this what they call destiny?

    well, i liked the idea and emotions behind 'last poem'. it's unique, a new take to the whole love-poem thing.

    it's a bit too long, and i think that some of the lines sound rather awkward ("Maybe he had earlier confessed? ", "I am solely to be blamed for my folly to continue assuming. " )

    some of the phrases were excellent though ("My heart feels so empty without you filling the crowd. /Honestly, / I had seen it coming. / But upon knowing the truth, I just kept running. / Away and away and away... " ). i especially liked the ending; it fit wonderfully.

    as for your other poems, 'whenever' seems rather long as well (or maybe i just dont have long attention span ^^). again, some of the prases can be improved on ("whenever you expel that particular scent," , "i am like 'oh my god!'. " ).

    i like your ending for 'whenever' as well ("And, / if you ever love me, / when? " ). again, it fits.

    'what if' was too short, but i think it has potential.

    'daydream' doesn't flow too well, i think. you were describing a daydream, and then you were talking about 'her', and then about destiny. i'm not sure where the daydream fits in, or if it's really important in putting across any ideas.

    in most of your poems, it sometimes feels like you're straining for rhyme. the words you use for the sake of rhyming don't fit too well, sometimes.

    but overall, i like the idea and inspiration behind your poems. keep on writing!
