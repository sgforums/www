    Poem: Elucidation.Beneath

    Even the upturned bikes
    Seem orderly enough,
    When your floors are children
    Of a standard grey heritage
    Whose DNA is cheaply auctioned
    Like a package for two
    Flying to over-clichÃ©d Bangkok.

    Your flowers bloom not from the sun
    But by the hand of ones
    Who never bounded in your joy,
    Much less know your domicile.
    But they spring up like rabbits
    Albeit no hutch they have here

    And the ones who call you
    Home.
    May one day be like rats;
    They who made the Titanic famous
    And bid that old man cry.
    Freedom for us saints,
    No taint of the white power,
    Not even a downy stick.
    If colour is chaos
    Let me wear thy shield
    Before white death freezes us.
    And we fear
    We fear.

    Seek that light
    As the talking cock crows,
    For posterity and Asterix,
    Respect and Obelix
    A healthy nest we must secure
    Or our young be damned
    From the suffocation of
    Our
    Leaden
    Hands.
    [/b]
