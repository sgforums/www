    9 December, Saturday (that's tomorrow!), at 3pm, we will be hosting Betty Warrington-Kearsley's poetry session where the English-born, Singapore-bred poet will be reading from her poetry collection "Red Lacquered Chopsticks".

    About Betty Warrington-Kearsley

    Betty was born in Cheshire, England, of a Singapore Chinese mother and English father. She was educated in Singapore where she was conceived and where she spent her holidays under the tutelage of her maternal grandparents in their family home in the Chinese fishing village of Telok Mata Ikan which now no longer exists. After graduating from St. Hilda's, then an Anglican mission school, she went to England to study physiotherapy. She returned to Singapore and worked with the RAF and the Australian Red Cross before heading back to England for further studies and experience. She then emigrated to Canada as an officer in the Canadian Forces Medical Services where she served for the next 24 years, twelve of them as the Forces' Chief Physiotherapist. After retiring from the Canadian Forces she worked with the Arthritis Society. She finally ceased her physiotherapy practice in 1996 to write, full time. Betty has travelled widely, visiting over 30 countries in Europe and Asia, Middle East and Africa, North and South America, and Australia - sometimes on business, sometimes family related, sometimes to explore and learn, and always with a notebook at hand. She lives in Ottawa but continues to travel at every opportunity. She also enjoys reading, walking, gardening, and sitting in the garden, in a timeless space between her Zen-like rocks, absorbing the spirit of "Life, the Universe and Everything" - in all its seasons. She writes, as her husband says, because there are things which must be said, and poetry is a most powerful medium. Always the student, Betty continues to study and hone her skills. While her works in progress include a memoir of her Chinese mother and life in the early days of Singapore, essays and short stories, she presently concentrates on poetry which she began writing in childhood. She has written of her experiences in Africa, South-East Asia, Japan, Chile and Canada, but mainly writes of humanity and the universal human condition.

    Her poems and other writing have won prizes and been published in Canada, the United States, United Kingdom and Japan. Her feature article won first prize in the National Capital Writing Contest in 2005, and in 2004 she was recipient of the Diana Brebner Poetry award and co-winner of the Ray Burrell poetry contest. Her poems were also short-listed in the 2004 Shaunt Basmaijan Poetry Award. Betty's debut collection of poems, Red Lacquered Chopsticks, was published by TSAR

    Publications in October 2006.

    Previously exhibited in Tokyo and Kanazawa, the launch of alternative Japanese T-shirt project SHIKISAI in Singapore (brought in by Crop Design) will be held with a two-week long exhibition at BooksActually.

    The exhibition features different possibilities of T-shirt design under the restrictions of "the black print on a white T-shirt". Additional details are hand-sewn by Japanese design-duo Noto-Fusai (Noto and Fujimoto) directly onto the T-shirt. These T-shirts will be for sale in the duration of the exhibition, exclusively at BooksActually. Surprise gifts of limited quantity will be handed out during the launch party.

    Launch party: 24 August (Friday), 8pm, at BooksActually
    Exhibition dates: 24 August (Friday) - 8 September (Saturday) 2007

    Venue: BooksActually / 125A Telok Ayer Street
    Co-organized by: Crop Design & BooksActually
