    Very nice pictures! Just a few minor suggestions that might be helpful:

    1. Try to use natural and/or ambient light to illuminate the subject. Using the camera's flash tends to create harsh shadows. I normally set up the subject and background next to a window, and use glossy white paper and aluminium foil encased in transparent DVD cases to bounce the light onto the shadowed areas of the figurine.

    2. A decent tripod really helps to eliminate camera shake, especially when the particular lighting conditions call for longer exposure times. Use the camera's delayed shutter release timer to take the picture, as this eliminates camera shake when pressing the shutter release button.

    3. For extreme close-ups, try experimenting with your camera's macro function.

    Looking forward to seeing the rest of your figurine collection!
