    Odex "Catching" Methodology
    As of now, the method that Odex utilizes for IP harvesting is very similar to MediaSentry's (The company behind IP gathering used by the RIAA) technique.

    Basically, they monitor file-sharing networks for infringements of the media. The gathering technique has been argued as "unlawful" and shouldnÂ’t be used as legal evidence, based on Dr. Johan Pouwelse' opinion, and was proven to be so on Dutch court[[5]].

    This is due to the fact that, in order for Odex or any other company to retrieve a list of IP addresses that are sharing a specific file through the BitTorrent Tracker, the company itself must be connected to the Tracker as well, and in order for the company to obtain a positively confirmation that you are downloading or sending through the P2P network, the company itself must be part of the sharing process through either sending to the illegal downloader or recieving the illegal material from the illegal sharer. Which means, the company itself is also breaking the law.

    Although Odex or any other firm can argue that they could have just connected to the Tracker to obtain the list of IP address without taking part in the uploading and downloading process through a customized BitTorrent client, it is impossible to prove that the IP in the list are all illegal downloaders or uploaders since Odex did not take part in the uploading or downloading process first hand. Which means, the ISP of the culprit must provide packets sent and received by the cultprit network to recontruct the media in question, which requires a network professional and a file recontruction expert, if the file happens to be destoryed in the "culprit's computer".

    Due to the complexity of P2P file sharing system, it is impossible to prove that someone has completed an actual download of a certain file. A simple analogy would be that:

    Mary (BitTorrent Tracker) is watching Joe (Uploader 1) and Tom (Uploader 2) passing half of their apples (bits and pieces of the file) from their individual basket (complete file) to Kelly (Downloader).
    Mary then announces to everyone who is part of the apple passing (network) that Kelly now has a full basket of apples.
    Jim (Odex), hiding in a corner, listening to what Mary is annoucing, suddenly declares that Kelly has stolen his special apple and decided to sue Kelly.
    Jim's (Odex) only evidence is an "announcement" made by Mary (Tracker).
    Kelly (Downloader) is unable to confirm that the apples she has in the basket are all "special apples" and not normal apples until she tastes them all. In Odex's case, both Kelly and Odex are unable to confirm that the media in question is really a real copyright infrigement media and not a random file with a similar name until they obtain and watch it.
    Mary (Tracker), the provider of the grey evidence, is somehow not involved in the court case.
    When Odex first sent out the legal letters, the time frame has allowed the illegal downloader to destroy the media in question. Solid evidence could therefore no longer be obtained, unless Odex is able to request the ISP to perform the above mentioned media reconstruction. Some argue that Odex should have just gone through the law enforcement channel and perform a house raid, but the problem lies in the fact that their so-called "evidence" is insufficient "evidence of infringement" in order for them to obtain a search warrant.
