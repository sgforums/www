    Oh my gosh, nobody replied to your topic. But anyway, it's a good one so I'll answer you. 1. Everywhere outside Japan, it's okay to be called an otaku. 2. I considered myself an otaku since secondary school because that is when my obsession started. 3. The otaku community in Singapore is growing still. But it's still not very significant until it can be reported as a phenomenon. 4. I don't have meetings with other otakus. Sorry, I can't answer this question because none of my friends are otakus. 5. Being a otaku is definitely a very important element of my private life I guess. 6. Almost every now or then, I'll go to comic connections or even kinokuniya for the manga or light novels. (I have only an interest in anime-styled light novels) 7. Anime and manga have very interesting storylines. The graphics are good but they play a secondary factor. In fact, in my opinion, graphics should not play a main factor because each anime/manga's graphics differ.
