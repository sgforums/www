    Faeries are known by many different spellings, the most common ones are fay, fae, faeries, fayerye, fairye, fayre, faery, fairy, sidhe, the 'wee ones', and kindly neighbours, to say the lest. It has been said that the wee folks do not like to be called fae, instead preferring to be adressed by good neighbours or wee folks... With all the mis-use of the word "fairy" nowadays, I wouldn't be surprised...

    Not every fairy comes with wings, wands and in the size of a butterfly... In fact, after the show Lord of the Rings, one could not help but be yet again mesmerised by the magic of these good folks, like the good old days of our childhood...

    What is here is a thread on these folks, and what is found over the internet, if you happen to have come across any resources that I happened to miss, and thought that it is all too good to not share, please, by all means, post here!

    Banshee - "Woman Fairy"; a spirit attached to certain families. When a member's death approaches, the family will hear the banshee crying. Not always terrifying. Always female and appear in a filmy, full-size human form.

    Bean-Fionn - (Ban-shoan) water woman, she is known to give directions to lost travellers but will, however, drown those who displease her, or who hurt and abuse children... lives beneath lakes, streams.

    Beansidhe - (Ban-she) Look above, banshee

    Bogles - Generally evil-natured Goblins although they are more disposed to do harm to liars and murderers.

    Brownies - Have traditionally attached themselves to humans and human households. Traditionally friendly and genuinely helpful.Brownie looks for deserving humans to aid, one who is humble, gracious, good to nature and with other people. He also hunts for a warm house, one which he could feel comfy in (meaning, no cats...)

    Buggars - A type of goblin, though in england has become a slang term for a bratty child. They are adept at shape-shifting so is hard to describe their appearance, considered to be very dangerous to humans, fortunately, they never leave the astral plane.

    Bwciod - (Bu-key'd) solitary and moves at high speed. Said to appear slender, and no more than a foot tall, with long pointy nose, slender fingers, huge flat feet and purple eyes that reflect no emotions. Once find his way into a human home, exorcism is the only known remedy to persude him to leave.

    Corrigans - Appears as repulsive hag by day, and blonde females by night. Men who see her by night are said to be never able to forget her, and pine away for want of her... Though legend states that if a man marries a corrigan, and genuinely loves her, and can love her in her day-form, she will become human and remain beautiful both night and day.

    Dwarfs - Are stocky, short and powerful. They mature at three years old and are grey and bearded by the age of seven. It is said they cannot appear in the light of day for to do so would turn them to stone. However, there are potions and spells that empower them to endure sunlight.

    Dryads - They ae spirits that dwell in the trees, preferably Oaks.The Druids turned to them for inspiration.
