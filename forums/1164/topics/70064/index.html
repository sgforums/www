    Sardaukar,

    u have a point, but in wartime, its not hard to de-humanise the enemy and to dis-regard all the considerations u brought up.

    out in the field, there's really no room for an overly-developed sense of morality... u watched band of brothers recently? the second episode i think... when the american soldier bayonets his platoon mate cos he thought it was the enemy...

    thats exactly the kind of pressure soldiers face in the field. u're tired, cold, wet and miserable. very scared. anything that moves makes u jump and u'ld rather shoot first and ask questions later. a joke during the gulf war was that the most dangerous fire was friendly fire cos its so much more accurate... and why's that so if soldiers are indeed, thinking clearly out field? cos they aren't... things are simply moveing too damn fast.

    besides, you would've also been instructed that everything in front of you is the enemy... and its a perfectly logical reasoning if u're advancing into hostile territory. if u don't react first, u'll be killed. the other guy would certainly not give a second thought to pumping u full of copper or lead.

    so in the field, u're not killing the enemy to protect your country, your're not killing somebody else's son, husband or father. u're neutralising a threat to u and your fellow soldiers.

    and because things moves so FAST out field, u have no time for a morality debate. in that context, it is a much greater sin if u let your men, or your comrades down by not taking out the enemy first.

    once the first shot rings out, your training and your instincts takes over... and thats not an exaggeration... nothing else matters. its just u, your weapon, and everything else that lays before u is a target.

    if anyone wants to be pure and not kill, they should do it in their own time in the confines of a monastry. out in the field, u have no business thinking that way because lives are at stake. not just yours, but many others as well.
