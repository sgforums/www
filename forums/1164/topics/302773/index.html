    Nah I disagree... it's good to have a soldier that can shoot well and not question orders when it comes to it, but at the end of the day it depends on what kind of balance you strike up. The soldier should not be the kind that questions each and every order but also not the kind to carry out bad orders that would spell disaster just because he was told to do so. I don't see any use for arguing for either extreme (ie. totally independent, thinking soldiers or totally obedient soldiers), it's a matter of balance.

    If you read Generation Kill, there are several instances in the Yank army where NCOs stood up to Commissioned ranks because the officers were making bad decisions, or even instances where they defied their superiors. But they always acted in the best interests of their men, and as it turned out they made the right decision. What we need is an army that holds all ranks accountable to each other, and not just "this is what you are told, go do it."

    I would certainly trust myself more if the dudes beside me were a lot smarter then if they simply followed all orders without question. Creativity in the battlefield is a great asset, and a grunt that can get the mission done without too much micromanagement from his NCO and commissioned ranks is certainly a very useful thing to have.
