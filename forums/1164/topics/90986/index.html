    I'm no Sino-apologist but I don't think they "over-reacted" at all. Their official rebuttals to our MFA's initial clarification attempt was completely valid; I scruntinzed closely then for logical fallacies, except in vain. If anything, IMO they asserted very compelling from their point of view - much like we did in our spats against Malaysia - although they obviously didn't know prior about our PM succession date.

    In the same manner, I think Singaporeans didn't comprehend the whole context either, unless they have long memories.

    PM Lee was valid when he said he wanna get a personal, updated feel of the Taiwan situation. But what's left unsaid is that Singapore-Taiwan has, deliberately or not, an ongoing "private visit" exchanges of once every 2 years or so. The ones I can most recall is from Lien Chan & company's 1998 "vacation" here onwards; LKY twice dropped by there in 2000 & then 2002, & now LHL flew over in 2004.

    China lodged complaints during each of the earlier trips, so I don't think PM Lee didn't prepare himself for the (expected) backlash this time around. I do think, however, that he mis-calibrated their anger, since - let's be fair - he had 12 years since his last visit in '92 to meet up with "friends" over there.

    I posited on another board that China cannot but acknowledge the facts that:

    (a) the PAP/Singapore Government has comparatively longer ties with the KMT than with the CCP,

    (b) Singapore politicians are chummy with certain DDP reps as well, even though they collectively seemed to prefer courting our opposition parties more.

    [For the latter reason, I suggested that PM Lee maybe shouldn't meet anyone higher than their Premier Yu Shyi-kun, rather than let Chen Shui-bian & Annette Lu (who scolded LKY back in Y2K, remember!) milk his presence to the max. But that would be rude to their hosts on our part, wouldn't it?]

    Along these lines, I don't really care too much whether China wanna indirectly test PM Lee's mettle, or use him to signal how hawkish they've become on the Taiwan issue, or whatever. What matters is that they appreciate, in restrospect, his sense of urgency, upon the revealing of the PM succession date. Even if they still reprove his "stubbornness", so be it. The rest of the world knows it's impossible to coordinate political timings when it comes to international relations.

    Thankfully, the dispute was conducted rather restrainedly at the official level - both sides postulated their stands clearly - without succumbing into the emotional bickerings ala online. For that reason, I expect things will blow over quickly. Let's move on.
