    It has to be either Rommel or Guderain for me

    The latter for his exploit commanding the 7 Panzer Division during the invasion of France and for his drive to Benghazi in 1941 during the African campaign. He helped create the legendary fighting exploits of the Afrika Korps and was considered the most competent German commander at the early part of World War 2. However, he probably did not do as well after the surrender of the Afrika Korps. His command of Army Group E and B in Greece/ Italy was forgettable. His tenuere as Army Group B commander saw him as commander of the Alantic coast and he probably did not perform as well due to conflicts with his superior, Von Runstedt, on the best way to defend the Palais and Normany beach head from an Allied invasion. Although no fault of his when the Allied made deep penetration into the beach head within days, he would have partial responsiblity for the debacle. He also predicted wrongly the site of the Allied invasion and placed large amount of armour and troops within the Calais Sector. Rommel was also considered a wilful commander by the German High Command due to the fact that the 7th Panzer broke off radio contact with it's higher HQ during the drive to the coast and Rommel had acted on his own direction instead of those from the Korps HQ.The legend of Rommel would probably attribute to his flair, leadership and chivalry towards foes. His flair and leadership made him a legend among the troops of the Korps and when he was forced to leave Africa when all was lost , his departure was not announced due to fear of a drop in moral in the Korps. He also cut his own men water ration in order to feed British POWs with enough water. However, what made this man legend was his supposed involvement in the plot to assissinate Adolf Hitler and dying under the orders of Hitler.

    The former was probably the most brilliant commander of tank units in the entire German Officer Corps during the second world war. Guderian can also be considered the creator of the combined arms and Blitzkreg doctrine. His work Achtung Panzer is basically one of the finest military doctrine book ever written. In commend of the XIX Corps in France, he was the commander that made the vital breakthrough at the Meuse that split the Allied forces into two (The Sickle Cut). Later in Russia,commanding Panzergruppe 2, he made the drive towards Kiev and bag 600 000 Soviet POWs when Hitler diverted attack from Moscow. However, this was probably his large great achievements during the War. As well as a brilliant war commander, he stood up to Hitler and openly showed his contempt for him and was duly relieve of his command in Russia. Guderian was later made the Inspector of Panzer Troops and the Chief of the General Staff where in that post, he had many heated arguments with Hitler and was once again relieved in 1945.
