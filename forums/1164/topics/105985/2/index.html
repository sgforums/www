    We got the F-5 not because it was dbl engined. More because the first series was nearly free under FMS. The subsequent ones were not free when the economy boomed and foriegn aid dwindled.

    F-5 was dbl engined because costs. Excerpt from "Northrop F-5/F-20" by Jerry Scutts, Modern Combat Aircraft.

    "One drawback foreseen was the use of a large conventional turbojet: not only would this require a large amount of fuel, but its cost would be high. This factor was one of the primary reasons for the cancellation of furthter work on the Fang. Tom Jones, then Northrop Planning Officer (later President and Chief Executive) felt that this was not the way the company should go. He pressed for a reassessment of the fighter programme, with more emphasis on keeping the cost down.
    At this point, Northuop's brainchild was helped considerably by the availability of very small turbojets being produced primarily for guided missiles. With very high thrust to weight ratios and dimension modest enough to fit into a very compact fighter airframe, these engines became the crux of the aircraft Northrop was plannning."

    The F-5 has the smallest engines ever. The engines start after the auxilary air intakes. Look at any F-5 taking off. It is that small.

    Due to same cost reasons, it have dry wings, eg all fuel is carried in the main fuselage.

    The end result, the smallest fighter supersonic aircraft that can take off from a grass strip. You need to respect this little bird as it is used for Agressor training. This is mainly dogfight without radar using mainly eyes.

    Cons, limited stores stations, limited radar capability.
