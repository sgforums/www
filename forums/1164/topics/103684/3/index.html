    The long and short of the whole supply thing is that...
    One weapon = one system to support it.
    Parts, ammunition, documentation, training, all that.
    All this costs MONEY.

    Two weapons = twice the cost.
    And if it uses a different ammo, ammo resupply must be counted in also.
    So consider this: a Platoon armed with 7.62mm MGs, 5.56mm rifles, 9mm/.45 pistols, 40mm GLs, assorted heavy support systems such as 84mm RR, 67mm LAW...

    Requires SEVEN types of ammunition resupply.

    The strain is already hard enough on the poor storemen and armourers, do you think they want more? Not to mention you need to factor in special ammo types.
    5.56 AP/Tracer, 40mm HE/Smk, 84mm RR rounds.
    *save me trouble*
    So with the above post as an example, let us examine what different parts exist between a M16S1 and an M4 carbine.

    Accordingly, the difference between
    Models 614S and 720
    are as follows.

    Telescopic butt-stock.
    Shorter barrel length.
    Various other small teeny-tiny things.

    But as it is, the two main difference mean that the armorers and storemen will have to maintain, fix, and indent an wholly different part stock than a unit which uses M16s.

    Furthermore, if the unit uses a different weapon for drivers/service personnel such as the MP5k or FN P90, a wholly different ammunition stock and part stock will need to be maintain.

    In simple mathematical terms.
    To support one weapon = 1 $.
    3 weapons = 1 + 1 + 1 = 3.
    That is 3 $.
