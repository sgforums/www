    The units that the starter said he has seen carrying such weapons are the people who have priority for issue of small arms.
    Of course we'd better issue carbines to our SOF people, after all, we wouldn't want them to have problems clearing a room, would they?

    But a driver?
    Hey driver, your job is to drive. Not to shoot.
    So if we issue you a bayonet you should be happy.

    Consider, is a medic more important than a driver in the great chain of things? Yes he is. But he gets issued a full-size weapon.

    The long and short of the whole supply thing is that...
    One weapon = one system to support it.
    Parts, ammunition, documentation, training, all that.
    All this costs MONEY.

    Two weapons = twice the cost.
    And if it uses a different ammo, ammo resupply must be counted in also.
    So consider this: a Platoon armed with 7.62mm MGs, 5.56mm rifles, 9mm/.45 pistols, 40mm GLs, assorted heavy support systems such as 84mm RR, 67mm LAW...

    Requires SEVEN types of ammunition resupply.
    These seven types of ammunition need to go through these processes.
    Requistioning and purchasing.
    Collating and packaging.
    Indenting and sending.
    Lastly, parceling them out to the troops as needed.

    The strain is already hard enough on the poor storemen and armourers, do you think they want more? Not to mention you need to factor in special ammo types.
    5.56 AP/Tracer, 40mm HE/Smk, 84mm RR rounds.

    Isn't it ironic that at the end the Army asks for money from civilians?
