    Originally posted by sgFish:
    wads NGFS?
    NGFS = Naval Gun Fire Support, i.e. supporting ground troops with naval guns, particularly in amphibious assaults. This is mainly a role for battleships with multiple 16" guns. We will never get a good ship for NGFS since you need big, long ranged guns and lots of ammo = giant ship.

    Originally posted by sgFish:
    oh and um can anyone explain to me the difference between LST, LSD and LPD? and are our endurance class ships correctly classified as LSTs?
    LST = Landing Ship Tank = the ship sails into the beach, the front opens up and tanks drive out.

    LSD = Landing Ship Dock = the ship has a dock which can launch smaller boats that carry ground troops to the shore.

    LPD = Landing Platform Dock = LSD with a platform for launching amphibious craft.

    Endurance = Rejoice 3-in-1 shampoo aka pow-ka-liao = The front is a LST, the back is a LPD, it has a naval gun, anti-air and anti-ship missiles and can launch helicopters. A very small combination of LST, LPD and frigate. Very rare and weird configuration, typical of Singaporean kiasu-ness.
