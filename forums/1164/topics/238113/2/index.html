    My personal opinion:

    Have seen both Flags of Our Fathers and Letters from Iwo Jima.

    Both are anti-war war films. Good.

    But...

    Unlike Saving Private Ryan, both films are terribly predictable and pretty boring. The characters are somehow quite distant and unreal. The good are overly good, the bad are overly bad.

    I also don't know how much real facts are in the films. I question how true is it that so many of the Jap officers were portrayed as insurbodinate to the CO.

    There was also a scene where a group of about 8 Jap soldiers were committing suicide. They were all standing very close together inside a cave. Then, one by one, each one took a grenade, pulled out the pin with their teeth (like all Hollywood movies), thumped the grenade firing mechanism against their helmet and then held the exploding grenade to their chest. Each time, the victim gets blown wide open, but those standing nearby are not only unscathed, but also unaffected by the blast and concussion even though they are in a small cave which must magnify all the sounds.

    So in a way, while both films are MUST WATCH for military buffs, I would rate it as being much much less entertaining than SPR.

    If SPR was a 10 out of 10, I would give FOF and LIJ about 5 out of 10.

    And ZERO for reality.
