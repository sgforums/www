  • specfore
    11 Sep 06, 20:23
    Anyway, on the subject of specs, in my years in NS and the reserves, I never got my specs broken. Only once did I drop my glasses on the Pasir Laba parade square ( this was not because of training but rather I took them off to wipe them and drop them by accident ..hahaha careless me !). Even then, there was only a small chip to a corner of the lens.

    I have been doing various things with the specs on... river crossing and swimming, heli- rappeling etc etc. Only a few times I have used one of those straps to secure the spectacles, e.g. during assualt boat training . Most of the time I carried a spare in my backpack.

    The only time I never had specs on was sparring during unarmed combat training. But I still "beat" my buddy in the 5 minute sparring session !

    The only time I was inconvenienced by specs was during very humid nights in the jungle and the lens fogged up everytime I took a deep breath. But that happen once in awhile.

    For those going to train in Australia or Thailand , or where the environment is really dusty... the good practice is to clean your multi coated hi -index lens is to flush them with a bit of water first, then apply a very dilute soap mixture and wash them under a tap. Do not wipe dry with a tissue but use a tissue and just sponge off the water. Because if you don't, which I learnt by trial and error, you will get many tiny scratches on your multi coating.
