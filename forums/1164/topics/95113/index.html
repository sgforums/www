    Yes the SAR21 does fit M-16 magazines which is known in NATO speak as the STANMAG.However,this applies only to the export version of the SAR-21.But what for you want the SAR-21 to use M-16 MAGs?The mags these days are made of polymer and is transparent and even has markings indicating how many round you have left and so on.Its lighter and not as fragile.

    5.56mm is a Nato standard round, period. But that does not include the magazine housing the rounds.

    As far as why the export version of the SAR21 can used the M16's mag? I would like to refer to the similarities of the U.S Bradly ARMOURED FIGHTING VEHICLES. Despite the word armoured in the name, the 1980s U.S version were mostly made up of light skin armour as compared to the export version to the IDF which had modifications to the vehicle. So whats the point here?

    The point is simple, as long as the manufactural is making weapons technology for its host country, its making a lost in profits. Therefore, to make up for the profits, attract buyers & to make a stand in the arms market, manufacturals would always modify the designs of the weapon to suit their intended buyers.

    Silencer for the weapon is not really a must since it also reduces the max effective distance of the weapon. However, in like of todays battle ground in urban warfare, having a silencer ensures the firer's hearing is not affected in combat, like disorientation, unable to hear in-coming communications, etc.
