RSAF successfully tested quick recall of its F16s from US

Next

    By S Ramesh (Channel NewsAsia)

    The Republic of Singapore Air Force (RSAF) has successfully tested how quickly it can recall some of its overseas assets like the F-16 Fighting Falcons from training detachments in the US.

    Mindef said four Fighting Falcons jets from its Peace Carvin III detachment in the US were redeployed home in the exercise.

    The aircraft left Luke Air Force Base in Arizona, transiting at bases in Hawaii and Guam before arriving in Singapore on Saturday.

    Two aircraft tankers (the KC-135R) also provided air-to-air refuelling support for the 17,000-kilometre journey.

    During each leg of the journey, the F-16s were refuelled between six and eight times by the tankers.

    The exercise was the RSAF's third in the long-range redeployment drills.
