    During WW 2 , the Germans used turrets from decommissioned tanks and fitted them onto concrete bunkers. Ended up as fixed gun emplacements. Which was not such a bad idea since at that time, there were no such thing as smart weapons (e.g. bunker busters or ATGW).

    They were difficult to kill but easy to get close by infantry, because of the turret's limited all-round vision. However, you needed a shaped charge to kill the bunker.

    If I 'm not mistaken, the idea of using turrets on bunkers was also adopted to a small degree by the Swiss and also the Syrians. But with ATGW and smart weapons, the idea of fixed gun emplacements is only good for limited situations.
