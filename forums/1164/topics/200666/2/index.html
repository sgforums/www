    I think that clerks should indeed write their memoirs. Many people have mentioned that they appreciated more the hardships of the commandos since reading my memoirs. Likewise, I think that reading the memoirs of clerks will give us a greater appreciation of clerks and their great work.

    I don't know much about the life of an army clerk. But it may be much harder than I have imagined. For example, a clerk may be given an order to photocopy a huge amount of material in one hour. That's great stress. If you ask a commando and a clerk to do this task, I am sure that the clerk can photocopy much faster and do a better job. Commandos can do parachute jumps and march 35 km or whatever, but give us a huge stack to photocopy in a short time and it may be very difficult for us.

    During my NS, many of my commando friends envy the clerks. But as they say, "the pasture is greener on the other side". Who knows if my commando friends were to be suddenly converted to being a clerk, they may wish that they were back in the commandos? Who knows if some of them may not be able to handle the pressures of being a clerk?

    I have respect for army clerks, but if I read their memoirs, I think I'll have an even greater respect for their works and sacrifice.
