    SINGAPORE: A container ship sailing through Singapore's waters has struck a large navy ship and collided with an oil tanker.

    No one was injured in the incident in the early hours of Wednesday.

    Advertisement

    No oil spill has also been reported in the collision which took place about 40 nautical miles east of Singapore.

    This is the second time in two years that a Singapore navy vessel has been involved in a collision at sea.

    Preliminary reports indicate the Liberian-registered container ship Merkur Bridge was overtaking the navy ship RSS Persistence when it collided with the oil tanker High Endurance.

    As it drew alongside the navy ship, Merkur Bridge suddenly altered course to avoid the oncoming High Endurance.

    But the container vessel struck the navy ship and then collided with the oil tanker.

    The oil tanker's engine room caught fire and a small hole was torn in its hull above the water line.

    The High Endurance then radioed for assistance.

    The RSS Persistence, which sustained minor scratches, and several other Navy patrol vessels responded immediately.

    The RSS Persistence was heading to Australia for a routine deployment, but was forced to turn back after the incident.

    The RSS Persistence is a locally-built landing ship tank which is used to transport personnel and equipment.

    It measures 141 metres from bow to stern - 20 metres longer than most football fields.

    The Maritime and Port Authority is investigating the collision but says that traffic through the Singapore Strait has not been affected.

    Maritime authorities have, however, issued navigational warnings advising passing ships to sail clear and to navigate with caution. - CNA
