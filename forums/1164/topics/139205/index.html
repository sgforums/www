    Well, when you think about it, it only makes sense to have an aircon in the cockpit.

    After all, the cockpit is airtight... no way is the pilot going to feel the rush of supersonic winds in his hair. Not to mention the amount of heat generated by the friction between the plane and the air at high speeds... it gets really hot.

    The last thing we want is to have a pilot knocked out in his cockpit due to heatstroke.
  • SlowPoke
    25 Sep 05, 01:38
    the so-called "aircon" on the F-16 is called the ECS (Environmental Control System). Primary function to provide cooling air to cool the on-board avionics, cooling the pilot is secondary. In fact it works too well in our humid climate, in the early days of our Block 52s, water collects under the ejection seat from all the condensation, after every flight we had to pump out the water, else the water either shorts out the comms matrix located under the seat or on the next sortie the pilot will get a wet surprise if he does a barrel roll.
