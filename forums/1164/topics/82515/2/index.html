    Oh, I see now. &, yes, the 3 Guards entrance was a quite long walk away to book in-book out, though personally I held that Amoy Quee Camp is comparatively farther (if your unit is sited within its deep reaches) ...

    The old 21SA used to operate self-propelled mortars mounted inside the M113 - we were spiritually more Armour than Arty. The explosion wiped out the whole of Alpha Battery's Detachment 4, killing one & rendering 4 permanent downgradees due to sharpnel injuries (the DC in the buttocks, the 2IC in the face, the layer's fingers blown off & another mortarman in the back). Only the driver escaped unhurt.

    The official investigation tracked it on the dead mortarman, who'd supposedly found a grenade & hid it from the end-of-exercise oath-taking. I recall how, with the rest of my unit personnel, the conspirational theorists (who speculated if the truth died with the dead) & lynch hunters (who wanted my BC & CO charged, let alone the Chief of Arty & other big shots who came down for the military funeral) were quickly put in their place - if only out of freaking respect for the departed.

    It was, overall, quite a difficult time for us NSFs to get over. My PTSD was actually triggered off by a couple of sporting accidents when I was in NUS, but back then it did cause deep, deep philosophical conflict like what is life, is NS worth it, etc. (Hokkien peng-style, since we were all drawing sub-$200 salaries ~smile~).

    As with the recent Commando incident, the foremost instinct was also to close ranks & seek simplistic comfort in some sort of transcendal comradeship. That has its place - I'm human myself - but it won't, & can't, hide the sinfullness of the deed. The dead mortarman was a great guy (one of the few Hokkien-peng taking the initiative to befriend the Air-Level arrivals) & I'm sure he didn't mean to cause what he did, but I couldn't look the other injured guys in the eye & not see their hurt or sense of wrongedness. I mean, I would've fought in any foxhole with him, but he was still a moron who caused serious casualties for an idiotic act.

    But what's done was done. I felt for our BC (who darted into the M113 after our medic froze) & hoped his career wasn't derailed - thankfully, saw his name a few years later in the National Day award list with a higher rank/staff appointment. I didn't ROD with my 21SA batch either due to the extra half-year & then NUS, but read in Pioneer Magazine that they won Best Reservist Unit one year - so that's a pleasant surprise too.[/u]
