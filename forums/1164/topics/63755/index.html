    KUALA LUMPUR (Malaysia) -- More than 480,000 boys and girls who are eligible to become the pioneer recruits of Malaysia's compulsory national service programme were kept on tenterhooks when the avenues to check on their fate were jammed on Tuesday.

    They have been told they could dial a hotline, visit a website or send a mobile phone text message on Tuesday to check if they've been selected randomly by computer but the government phone number was continually busy, the website was inaccessible and their text messages received no replies.

    A spokesman for the programme said at least six operators were on duty at the hotline, but there were 'non-stop calls'.

    One of the few who got through was Ms Goh Sue Anne, 17, who said she was close to tears because she thought she would be chosen. 'When I found out I wasn't, I was so happy, I was jumping around the house. I called all my friends and told them I wasn't chosen.'

    About 85,000 youths born in 1986 are expected to get the call up for three months of physical training that includes military-style exercises, as well as community service and classes on patriotism. The first batch start in February.

    Officials hope the plan will help bolster racial integration, curtail religious extremism and instill a sense of civic responsibility in mostly Muslim Malaysia's young people. -- AP
