    Air Force chopper deal likely
    31 January 2004
    By JOHN MYERS

    The Air Force is investigating a deal to buy Singaporean Squirrel helicopters - like The Square Trust rescue helicopter - to replace its elderly Sioux training fleet at Ohakea.

    Reliable sources have told the Manawatu Standard an evaluation is already under way that may see a number of former Singapore Armed Forces machines join 3 Squadron.

    A single average used Squirrel changes hands for about $US1million on the commercial market, but what's being worked on in Singapore is a government-to-government deal and they will probably cost less.

    The local helicopter community believes the Squirrel is an excellent machine.

    "As a light training and utility helicopter, I couldn't see there's another choice," Helipro chief Rick Lucas said.

    "New or second-hand is probably irrelevant as long as the component times are 'up there'," he said.

    "Squirrel is the benchmark of light utility helicopters in the world today," Mr Lucas said. Helipro flies a number of Squirrels.

    Square Trust rescue helicopter pilot Mike Hall, who flies a Squirrel every day, agrees.

    "Running costs per seat are about as good as it gets," he said.

    "It's very reliable and nice to fly. The Squirrel has hydraulic controls - a bit like power steering - and you can do a big day's work without too much fatigue."

    Australia flies Squirrels in a range of military roles.

    The sources say the deal looks good so far. It's understood a team led by helicopter replacement project chief Wing Commander Barry Nelson is in Singapore at the moment going over the helicopters.

    The Sioux and Iroquois machines have been in RNZAF service since the mid-1960s. Not only are they "old" in terms of their flying life, and increasingly hard to maintain, they are old technology.

    The replacement project is based on taking a broad-brush look at Defence's need for helicopter roles.

    The Squirrel would likely be regarded as a modern-role Sioux replacement, adding broad utility and surveillance capabilities to the training role.

    The Squirrel is a French design from the Eurocopter stable. It's a rugged turbine-powered machine able to carry seven people or more than a tonne slung from its load hook.

    It's quite fast in general-purpose helicopter terms, cruising around 220kmh.
