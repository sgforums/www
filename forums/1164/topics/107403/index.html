    PAS Youth wing slams air space pact with Singapore

    Wednesday, December 15 @ 10:59:38 MYT

    By REME AHMAD, The Straits Times Singapore KUALA LUMPUR, Dec 15 - The Youth wing of Parti Islam SeMalaysia (PAS) yesterday slammed Prime Minister Abdullah Ahmad Badawi's agreement to open up Malaysian air space to the Republic of Singapore Air Force, saying this could affect the country's sovereignty.

    "The central PAS Youth committee regretted very much the over-enthusiastic attitude of the Prime Minister to easily surrender Malaysia's sovereignty to Singapore purely to get funds worth only a few billion ringgit," it said.

    After his meeting in Putrajaya on Monday with Datuk Seri Abdullah, Singapore Senior Minister Goh Chok Tong said the Republic was ready to allow the release of Central Provident Fund money belonging to West Malaysians if Kuala Lumpur agreed to let Singapore's air force use Malaysian air space.

    The funds are believed to total $2 billion.

    The PAS statement, signed by its Youth chief, Salahuddin Ayob, said Kuala Lumpur should learn from the Pedra Branca episode.

    The island, off the north-east of Singapore, is 'claimed' by the Republic as its territory after Malaysia allowed it to build a structure there, he said. - zs
