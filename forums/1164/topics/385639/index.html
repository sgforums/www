    Gun shields are usually installed only after real bullets start to fly.

    In Vietnam war,   the first    M113    50 cal guns came without gunshields. These were installed once casualties of 50 cal gunners mounted quickly. Later, gunshields were also added to the 7.62 MGs at the rear fo the M113s when it became clear they prolonged  the lives of these gunners.   Ditto for other vehicles in Vietnam as gunshields were installed on every pintle mounted MG including those on trucks, tank commander MGs etc.

    This lesson is being learned again in Iraq & Afganistan where gun shields are fitted  to loader's MG on M1A1s, gunner MG  on Humvees &  Warthogs  etc etc

    It is actually a no brainer that gunshields shud also be installed on SAF vehicles and especially the commander MGs for our Leopards, Bionixs, Broncos  etc etc.
