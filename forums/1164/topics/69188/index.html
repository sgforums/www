    My vote would be for alignment with all three nations, even though the politics of such an arrangement would have to be handled carefully. While India and China are up and coming, I don't think the US would fall so far as to lose its military and economic influence in the forseeable future. Right now the safe hedge would be a good compromised between the big boy on the block and the up-and-comers.

    I don't think gratitude comes into play here. I believe Margaret Thatcher once said something along the lines of "There is no friendship between countries, only common interests" and Stalin said "Gratitude is a disease of dogs". If Singapore offers anything attractive to any of these countries, we may hope for favourable conditions when dealing with them, but trying to call in a favour is unlikely to work.
