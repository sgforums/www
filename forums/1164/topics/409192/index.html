    Problem with biological weapons is blowback, the user might end up suffering from its effects as well. Hence while highly terrifying and destructive, their actual strategic use within rational belligerents is thankfully more limited.

    In some ways they may be more destructive then nuclear weapons. You can deal with a nuclear attack if you know how to, a biological threat is self-replicating and is far more chaotic in its spread. Most rational military planners know this, hence while they have stocks of biological weapons, the use of them is limited, if ever.

    All bets are off however, if you are talking about biological weapons in the hands of a madman dictator with no grasp of reality.
