    Rafale Vs F-15T....Eurofighter Out!?!

    Dassault, Boeing still in Singapore arms deal race - report
    Mon Apr 18, 2005 08:28 AM ET

    PARIS, April 18 (Reuters) - France's Dassault (AVMD.PA: Quote, Profile, Research) declined to comment on Monday on a newspaper report that it and Boeing (BA.N: Quote, Profile, Research) have both reached the final round of bidding for a $1 billion contract to provide combat jets to Singapore.
    French newspaper La Tribune reported that Dassault and Boeing would be considered for the lucrative contract, but not Eurofighter, which the paper reported to be out of the race.

    Officials at Dassault, which is bidding to sell its Rafale combat jet to Singapore, declined to comment and noone was immediately available at Eurofighter a consortium of top European defence firm BAE Systems (BA.L: Quote, Profile, Research) , Airbus parent EADS (EAD.PA: Quote, Profile, Research) and Alenia Aeronautica.

    La Tribune quoted several sources close to the issue as saying that the island state, which wants to buy 20 combat jets, would announce its preferred bidder at the end of June or beginning of July with exclusive negotiations seen finalised at the end of the year or early 2006.

    Aerospace officials say the outcome of the bidding for the widely watched Singapore contract is tough to call.
