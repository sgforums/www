    Tawi Tawi, Philippines (June 18, 2006) - The U.S. Military Sealift Command (MSC) Hospital Ship USNS Mercy (T-AH 19) sets sail for Sembawang, Singapore, after its four-week stay in the South Philippines. Mercy is on a five-month deployment to South Asia, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The medical crew aboard Mercy will provide general and ophthalmology surgery, basic medical evaluation and treatment, preventive medicine treatment, dental screenings and treatment, optometry screenings, eyewear distribution, public health training and veterinary services as requested by the host nations. Mercy is uniquely capable of supporting medical and humanitarian assistance needs and is configured with special medical equipment and a robust multi-specialized medical team that can provide a range of services ashore as well as aboard the ship. The medical staff is augmented with an assistance crew, many of whom are part of nongovernmental organizations that have significant medical capabilities.     Singapore Armed Forces team to join US Navy hospital ship next month
    ADVERTISEMENT

    SINGAPORE: A six-man Singaporean medical team plans to join a US navy hospital ship during its humanitarian mission to Indonesia.
    The hospital ship Mercy is almost 900 feet long, has 1,000 beds and can provide medical and surgical treatment onboard and onshore.

    The 24 personnel from the Singapore air, navy and army toured the facilities on the ship and talked with the US medical staff.

    The treatment facilities include a dental unit, four X-ray rooms and an operating theatre.

    Some of the operations performed range from dental to Neurosurgery.

    The SAF team joining the ship next month will be working in various units of the ship.

    The team consists of 1 dentist, 2 anaesthetists, 1 surgeon and 2 medics and will be onboard for one month.

    The ship has a pharmacy and medical supply centre and can hold 5,000 units of blood. The medicines will be used in helping the needy.

    "We look forward to being able to contribute to the humanitarian effort together with the members of the military medical community of other navies. We look forward to providing both surgical care and dental care to the people in Indonesia," said Major Dr Tang Kong Choong, Republic of Singapore Navy, Medical Service.

    When they arrive in Indonesia, the patients will be flown in by one of the two helicopters onboard.

    The first stop for Mercy is Simelue, followed by Nias island, before heading for Bandah Aceh. - CNA /dt
