    UAE F-16 Blk 60 Details - Dubai 2001

    For the first time, Lockheed Martin has displayed a detailed model of a two-seat Block 60 F-16, confirming that at least some of the UAE's F-16Ds will be fully combat -capable version, similar to the specialiesd 'big spine' Block 50 F-16Ds now in service in Israel and Singapore. A significant change has been made to the basic Block 60 airframe- the planned undernose Integrated FLIR targeting System (IFTS) has been removed and replaced iwth a new podded FLIR[, mount on the intake hardpoints.

    According to Lockheed Martin, there were problems in achieving the required performance with the original IFTS layout: consequently the Northrop Grumman AAQ-32 targeting FLIR and laser designaotr has been repackaged in a new station. The original wide-area navigation FLIR housing above the nose has been retained, now sharing cooling with AAQ-32 pod. Changes have also made to the Northrop Grumman APG-68(V)5 agile beam radar, necessitating the inroduction of a brand new designation- APG-80.

    The first of 80 Block 60 'Desert Falcon' for the UAE is due to fly in August 2003 and three aircraft will be devoted to the four month flight test programme. Deliveries are sceduled to run from 2004 to 2007, with a phased three stage capablity introduction plan for that timeframe. The Block 60 weapons integration will be undertaken at Eglin AFB and in the UAE. According to Lockheed, the Block 60 aircraft will be armed with teh EGBU-24 Paveway III, GPS-guided Joint Direct Attack Munition (JDAM) bombs, the WCMD (Wind Corrected mUnitions Dispensor), the Hakeem family of powered stand-off weapons and 'other' weapons, which are known to include AGM-88 high speed ati-radiation missile (HARM), AIM-9M Sidewinders, and AIM-120 AMRAAMs (see Desert Falcon's AAMS, May 2001, p14)
