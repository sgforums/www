    Thailand's complicity goes something like this:

    Indochina was a French colony.

    France had fallen to Nazi Germany and is now administered by Nazi puppet called the Vichy French government.

    Thailand had long contested some of the areas under French control.

    Sensing France's weakness, Thailand - which had a modern army and airforce - attacked French Indochina including Laos, Cambodia and Vietnam.

    The war was not very large scale and each side suffered only about 400+ dead and wounded. But this war involved armour, air and naval battles. The French admitted defeat against Thailand's superior forces and asked for peace with Thailand.

    Japan, which already had military bases in French Indochina, came in to negotiate.

    In the end, Japan gained more than Thailand out of the peace deal. They expanded their influence in French Indochina.

    Japan also got agreement from Thailand to assist in Japan's invasion of Malaya. Though Thailand did not keep this promise, they also did not really try to stop Japan either.
