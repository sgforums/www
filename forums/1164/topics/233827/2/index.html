    The physicality aspects between the 2 sexes is a moot point that doesn't even need debate. Certainly nobody has ever claimed that the average female soldier would be able to carry as much or as fast as the average men.

    But not all sniper missions need all the equipment you mentioned where you need to survive weeks in the jungle after doing an Iron Man endurance race several hundred miles behind enemy lines.

    In modern warfare, given densely built up cities all over the world (which are way more valuable to fight over than jungle and wasteland), urban scenarios are much more likely occassions for snipers to work.

    After all, the assault rifle developed in WWII was precisely in recognition of this point. Also the bullpup rifle as a further development of that. Many of the latest sniper rifles can be offered in bullpup configs with polymer parts. They offer better characteristics than the rifles of old. Certainly snipers needn't carry as much ammo too.

    In such situations the weaker attributes of the female physiology is less of a hindrance - such as infections of the urinary tract, need to cover superhuman distances - since the resupply line is so much shorter. Psychological make-up wise, such as stealth, patience, calmness, attention to detail etc. need not be lacking in them.

    In fact, where brute strength and endurance is not needed, scientifically speaking, women have great characteristics for long term survivability - a higher threshold for pain (childbirth has been shown to be equivalent in pain to a gunshot wound), higher body fat for sustenance etc.

    WWII in Russia featured the urban rubble of Stalingrad was a prime example, where little effort is needed to get behind enemy lines (u can even let the enemy over run your sniping position). Similarly in the Balkan conflict settings of the towns/cities.

    Distilled to the bare basics, where its just the 1 x sniper, 1 x rifle and a small quantity of ammo, women snipers CAN be even better suited, certainly no worse.
