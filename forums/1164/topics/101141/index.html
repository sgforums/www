    Home-grown radar allows soldiers see through walls

    DEFENCE engineers said on Thursday that they are developing a radar that will allow counter terrorist forces and search and rescue teams to 'see' through concrete walls to detect movement

    DSO National Laboratories, Singapore's largest defence research and development organisation, has revealed that its 'through-wall radar surveillance system' has been successfully tested in trials since the project began in mid 2002.

    These involved scanning solid concrete walls some 30cm thick from various distances.

    Mr Tan Chee Seng, DSO's Director of Sensors, said the radar is being improved so it can scan thicker walls even when placed 100m or more away, yet portable enough to be carried on a soldier's back.

    Engineers are also tweaking the radar so it will be sensitive enough to 'see' whether a person is armed from the radar image.

    The few countries involved in such research usually prefer to keep the technology to themselves, Mr Tan said
