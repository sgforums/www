    The squad of 7 men searched the tree line carefully. 3SG Cheng, the Sect Comm, peered through his infra-red scope, and found what he was looking for. As the rounds whizzed over their positions, he quickly depressed a small button beside his trigger. In that moment, the target was marked by a small rotating cursor on the helmet mounted sighting system worn by his men. Once the men had the target sighted, eached pressed a similar button on their assault rifles, sending a "ready" cue back to their section commander.

    Once ready, he carefully pulled his trigger... making sure he made the first pressure a slightly long one for the network to send the "fire" cue to his men. In that instant, the rotating reticule turned solid red and stopped rotating. And the jungle was deafened by a roar of gun fire. Through the barrage of lead, some movement to his left caught his attention. Quickly sighting the area, he depressed the button and held it while quickly sweeping over the area of the movement.

    The new cue was sent over the Section Fire Control System. This time the cue was a blinking line across the area. It indicated the firing arc that was required. Again the same process as the 7 barrels open fired on the section of the forest.

    Checking his Engagement timer, 2.30 secs passed... more than enough time passed for the enemy to relay their coordinates for mortar shells to rain on them. Tapping another button on the side of his rifle, it sent the retrogade command down the network of 7. And immediately, everyman, unhooked a board from their back. Upon activation, the board hovered above the ground, the men mounted and made their exit. Before whizzing a away, a few deadly surprises were left behind. Hand grenades with an electronic timer fuze. They were set to go off 5 mins after they depart...

    At 20km/hr, the men were outside the range of the mortar fire in no time.
