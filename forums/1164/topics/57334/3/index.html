    Dear Duotiga,

    If you want to see where our economy is headed, just look at australia for example. Its the nearest developed country and a good model of what being a developed economy is like. (not to mention, the best statistics currently for a developing country)

    They are having their best year at this moment. Unemployment is at 6.2%, Growth rates at 3%, birthrate is flat, and they do have lots of commodities to export.

    So in 10-15 years time, Singapore becomes a fully developed nation, that looks like the scenario we are going to have. Are we stable socially and politically at unemployment of 6.2%? How about with the same kind of economic growth rates? We already reached that milestone of flat rate of population increase. However, we are still going to be without resources. Are we ready for that kind of challenges in the economy? Japan progressed very well right up to the 90s but now is in their 10th year of recession.

    So I can say that due to the changing face of our economy, we can't keep certain models constant. Military spending being one of them. And what are the other bad news heading our way?

    The first I can tell you right now is the CPF is in deep shit. Almost every western country's pension fund is in trouble. We will face ours very soon. Those with PR and those commuting from JB are only allowed to withdraw their CPF funds when they reach 55. We had our great influx of them in 1980s. (remember the dean of NUS who got slashed? He was a PR and worked 21 years in Singapore) He had 9 years to go before turning 55.

    So assuming that there are about 100k of them retiring at 55 (out of the 1 million foreigners). So by 2010-2015 timeframe, they will withdraw ALL their money. You work for 20-30 years in Singapore, I can guarantee that they will have at least S$100k in the CPF, if not heaps more. So 100k x 100k = S$10 BILLION will be withdrawn at that time. And as time goes on, more will be withdrawn as many more retire.

    Unfortunately for us, our population is not growing in size, so there is no growth in the number of workers replenishing the CPF. We will have the same problem as the western countries pension funds, and will be made even worse by the fact that these wothdrawers are not going to use their pension money in Singapore, they will take it back to China, India and Malaysia.

    Worse still, last year, the government announced that we can not depend on the CPF being enough for retirement in Singapore. That means EVERYONE who can, will take their money and go to somewhere cheaper. (And I don't even want to think about the property market when these FT sell their HDBs to buy houses)
