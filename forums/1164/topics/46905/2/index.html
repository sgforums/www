    Indonesia's military has sent three dozen British-made Scorpion tanks to Aceh province, a move which could upset London.

    Indonesia launched an offensive to crush the Free Aceh Movement (GAM), which has sought independence.

    Indonesian military officials said the 36 tanks arrived in the western province on Sunday and will be used to guard roads.

    The military has already used British-made Hawk fighter jets there.

    Britain sold the tanks and jets on the basis they would not be used for offensive operations against separatists.

    British officials have raised the issue of the jets with Jakarta. However, Jakarta has insisted there has been no breach because the jets were not used for bombing but for surveillance.

    A British embassy spokeswoman in Jakarta said any questions about the tank deployment had to be directed to London.

    Some 300 people have been killed since the Aceh military offensive began on May 19.

    - Channel NewsAsia
