    SYRIA LINK TO IRAQI TANK-BUSTERS
    By NILES LATHEM

    March 30, 2003 -- WASHINGTON - Iraqi Republican Guard storm troopers are using a sophisticated anti-tank missile system supplied by Russia and Syria against U.S. armored forces advancing on Baghdad.

    And U.S. military officials are furious.

    Pentagon officials told The Post last night they have confirmed that the system, known as the AT-14 Kornet, was used last week during a Republican Guard ambush of a tank convoy of the U.S. Army's 7th Cavalry near An Najaf. Two M1A1 Abrams tanks and a Bradley M2 fighting vehicle were destroyed, according to military officials and reports from embedded media.

    It was the first time ever that an Abrams tank was lost to enemy fire. Pentagon officials said the anti-tank system, which can fire with deadly accuracy from almost 2 miles away, is the most dangerous item on the list of military supplies that U.S. intelligence agencies believe are originating from Russia, Belarus and other former Soviet republics that are flowing into Iraq through Syria.

    The only known export of the AT-14 Kornet system was to the Syrian Army in 1998, according to U.S. intelligence officials and previous reports in military journals.

    Syria and Russia have both denied allegations they are helping arm Saddam.
