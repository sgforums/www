    Singapore unlikely to be offered revamped Rafale
    4-10 feb 2003

    Funding issues mean idustry cannot justify development of alternative export version

    Singaproe will probably be offered a basic Dassualt Rafale fighter incorporating the upgrades planned for the French air force and navy because the industrial team developing the multirole aircraft believe it will be too expensive to develop dedicated export versions.

    Dassualt, engine manufacturer Snecma and avionics and sensors supplier Thales are developing planned updates that will lead to the F3 multirole standard by 2007. The three say future export customers could benefit from these as part of any deal if they buy the rafale now. In-service rafale are deployed as air to air aircraft.

    Singapore will host the next major international fighter compettition with a request for proposal (RFP) covering 20 aircrafts and 20 options expected later this year.

    Denis Ranque, Thales chief executive, says the Dassualt industirial team will offer Singapore and others the updates destined for the French air force and navy.

    He adds that it is not worth the companies investing billions of euros in developing a version of the aircraft specifically for Singapore. However, Ranque says, if a country does select Rafale it would have an aircraft at the beginning of its 30-40 year service life with a series of planned upgrades already defined. Other liely competitiors are "at the end of the production line", he says

    Export Rafales are, however, offered with more powerful engines and more powerful radars. Dassualt says French government funding for all developments up to the F3 standard guaranteed.

    Regarding Singapore, Dassualt says: "Firstly, we don't yet know exactly what they want and secondly we do not know whether the French government would be willing to fund the development of a specific version."

    Singapore is expected to narrow its shortlist to three or four candidates from the current field of the Boeing F-15 Eagle and F/A-18E/F Syper Hornet, Eurofighter Typhoon, Lockheed Martin F-16 Block 60, Sukhoi Su-35 and Rafale before issuing its RFP

    As we have, in many other threads, discussed before, RSAF's requirements for the next generation fighters are to replace the A4 in the deep strike, penetration, mud moving roles, and F16 in the BVR A2A roles, while the F16s Vipers will replace the A4s CAS and battlefield interdiction roles.

    I believe the current F2 version offered by Dassault and the eventual F3 version (when and if available when theoretically RSAF gets them out of the production line) will fully meet these roles. However, the cavaet is that the F2/F3 version will only be fully equipped with French/European weapons, ie. if Singapore wants Rafales to be intergrated with all NATO compliant weapons, or more rightly in RSAF case, US and Israeli made missiles, then Singapore will have to pay for it itself. Dassult put it long and clear. It would not also create a special export version with other non-French requirements or integration of non standard equipments out of Dassault's own pocket. The French or the foreign govt will have to foot the development bills.

    The question now is, how would this news affect RSAF selection? Would this harm Dassualt's chance of its first foreign export of its priced fighter? I think Not. This is because Rafale, as it current stands in its current form (F1/F2), is already a very capable platform. The F3 will enhance the platform further, just like the Block 50+/60 Vipers, which are just phased upgrade of the current RSAF Vipers. RSAF can always enter into a contract with Dassault to have the option to go with the upgrade at a pre-determined priced. Of course, this will be factor into the selection criteria. Eurofighter Typhoon will have the exact same situation, so would the E/F Superbugs and the Russian SU-30s. Vipers and the -E Strike Eagles are the only two with such a conditions I believe. Block 60 Vipers are totally new built with all the new stuffs developed for UAE and funded by them. The -E version Strike Eagles have very minimal growth left in them because the USAF would not be putting a lot more money upgrading , what is an essentially a dying fleet. Their places will be overtaken by the new Raptors.

    Super bugs, the Su-30s, Typhoons and the Rafales are still relatively "young" and have lots of growth potential. I believe these are a essential attribute for RSAF when they come down to shortlisting the six. Once again, my money is on Rafale, Typhoon and the Super bugs, and in this order too
