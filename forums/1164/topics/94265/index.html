    Rifle: A firearm with a rifled bore, designed to be fired from the shoulder.

    Defined by length of bore and grooves in barrel, aka accuracy and rounds with strong penetrating power.

    Machine gun: A firearm that fires rapidly and repeatedly.

    Defined by the numerous repetition of shots within a short timeframe, thus usually not suitable to be fired without a mount or proper training.

    Sub-machine guns: A portable firearm that can fire rapidly and repeatedly.

    Defined by lightweight and low recoil (pistol rounds) with similar standards for repeated shots, thus making it safe for shooting without a mount.

    Assault rifles: Similar to rifles except it's capable of firing rapidly and repeatedly. Though that will be compensated with lower accuracy.

    Pistol : A small firearm designed to be fired from the hand and loads with magazines.

    Revolver: Similar to pistol except it's loaded through chambers with revolving cylinders.

    Handgun: A generic term for firearm that can be used with one arm.

    Musket: A smoothbore firearm designed to be fired from the shoulder using spherical pellets.
