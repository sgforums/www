    Originally posted by sidestep1984:

    Your information is erroneous. Just because an aircraft is less technologically advanced does not mean it's a "failure". Conventionally designed non-stealth aircraft will be around for quite a while more. Aircraft like the B-1B still have some "shelf life" left.

    While stealth is definitely going to be needed on the modern battlefield, an all-stealth force is rediculously expensive. Realising this, the USAF & other US services have formed a strategy where there remains a stealth first strike, followed by a conventional non-stealth "follow up" action.

    Thus, conventional aircraft capable of hauling larger payloads like the B-1B will still be around.

    Also, stealth is not the only decisive factor in the performance of a good combat aircraft. Afterall, even the Commanche programme got cancelled.
