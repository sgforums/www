    well, if you are a 2nd generation PR, which means that your parents were PRs, you would have to do military or other types of national service... could be in the civil defence or the police too..

    as for a career in the military, i think those who want a career in it had a headstart as they would have enlisted before they were 20 so you're at a disadvantage.. practically, it's better not to...

    i've heard of people volunteering to serve national service to "help" in their PR and citizenship application but that's about it.. heard..

    if it's about PR and citizenship, you can go check on your eligibility here => ICA Website

    good luck!

  • baikachuchu
    20 Oct 09, 11:47

    Even after your PR is granted,they might not definitely call you up for NS even though you are liable for it.

    As the Bear pointed out,NS can be done in the SCDF and SPF too instead of the army.Which you go to is up to the govt,you cannot indicate a preference.

    Career in the military?At your current age,you would be better off continuing your current line of work using your prior experience.When they call you up for NS,it is a part of your obligation,they won't set out a career path for you at the start.I believe you have the intention to sign on to the Army only as an officer and not as an NCO right?Even if you get enlisted into the army,they might not shortlist you to go for officer course and you might end up ording as an enlisted rank only(eg.Cpl).Once you do not qualify for OCS after your basic military training,it is unlikely that you will ever get to be a commissioned officer in the SAF.

    To find out more about army careers,visit this link:

    http://www.mindef.gov.sg/arc/default.asp

    It is not quite completed though since their faq is not up yet.
