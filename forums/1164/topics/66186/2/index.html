    HENG@, I never said anything about being aggressive to our neighbors. Of course peace is preferable under any circumstances. A war in the region would benefit no one. That is precisely why the SAF has to be so strong. Because we suffer from many strategic disadvantages, we must make up for them with technological, tactical and material advantages if the SAF is to be a credible deterrent. Any potential foe must realize that invasion of Singapore is futile, and that we have the armed strength to secure our interests in the region. If the SAF ever becomes too weak to be a credible deterrent, it will encourage diplomatic and ultimately military assault upon Singapore and lead to war. Hence, the stronger the SAF is, the more peaceful ASEAN will be. The conscript nature of the SAF helps ensure that she will not be used for illegitimate or aggressive purposes.

    VINSANITY, I don't think completely destroying the jungles of the peninsula is possible or necessary. You don't seem to realize how big even a small country like Malaysia is -- a side effect of living in Singapore too much, maybe? Burning everything down would require catastrophic levels of force. Anyway, I don't think it is necessary, since our armored vehicles have probably been designed to pass through a good portion of the terrain they will encounter. Either that, or their doctrine has been written to be suitable for their capabilities.

    After all, you don't have to slavishly follow the doctrine of Blitzkrieg or armored divisions, and burn down a whole jungle to do it. You create your doctrine according to your environment, then acquire equipment and train in accordance with your doctrine, revising the doctrine as circumstances change. If Blitzkrieg is impossible on the peninsula, then we will fight another way. Just because Guderian used it does not mean it is the word of God.
