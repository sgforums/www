    I am really old soldier era ! Even in my reservist in the 90s, I missed range for various reasons. But during my NSF time in the mid 80s, Mindef realised that the range test that was conducted was rubbish. It was composed of static shooting, eg at 300 m in foxhole etc etc. It was only one aspect of actual combat shooting, which everyone knows is mostly shooting at moving targets, on the move, behind cover and probably close ranges from 100m down to 20m.

    What I remember was that they introduced (we were the guinea pigs in 1983 -4) CTSS, Combat Tactical Shooting System. Meant to test every soldier from foxhole at 300 m , moving targets at 100m, down to "instinctive shooting" at 25m. All these in one session.

    I guess the shooting test has probably refined this today.
