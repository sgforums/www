    This is official from the Army Open House 2004,the School of Infantry Weapons have declared that the Austrian-made Styer SSG Sniper rifle will be phased out and replaced with the newer British-made Accuracy International PM L96A1 Sniper Rifle.

    The L96A1 is being touted as one of the best sniper rifles in the world and has a 12X optical scope and uses new construction materials.But the weight of the both weapons tilt in the favour of the Styer SSG as is weighs approx. 5Kg while the L96A1 tips the scale at approx. 8Kg.

    It has been confirmed that all trainees undergoing sniper training will be trained with the L96A1.

    Seems the new rifle has twice the telescopic power of the SSG69.

    Talk about SAR-21 sniper variant......extremely unsuitable for field snipers due to various factors:

    1) Auto chambering of rounds can damage projectiles and lead to inconsistent flight.

    2) Semi-auto bolt movement adds statistical inaccuracy during fire.

    3) Gas diverted for chambering adds to inaccuracy during fire.

    3) Automatic ejection of rounds may give away position.

    4) No need for multiple continous fire due to nature of deployment and mission. Less maintenance. Sniper rifles are less prone to carbon fouling.

    One may argue that the best semi-auto rifle in the world, PSG1, is non less accurate than the general bolt action rifles. One may want to think twice when your target is 800m away and you need an upper body shot to confirm a kill.
