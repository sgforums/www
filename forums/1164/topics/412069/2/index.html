    1 Oct 10, 01:43

    From the eyewitness reports it seems the Apache encountered a systems failure in flight that rendered a return to base not viable. From the spiral flight downwards path taken before the crash landing we can infer that the helicopter was either autorotating after an engine failure (gliding), or had a tail rotor malfunction, rendering forward flight impossible.

    The landing was probably not as hard as a crash, but perhaps more of the fact the pilot might have flared the helicopter just before touching down and causing a tailstrike... we can see from the still-extended wheel bogeys and general intactness of the fuselage that the impact to the forward part of the fuselage was somewhat mitigated, perhaps by the tail strike.

    It is difficult to infer travel of the aircraft following impact (it might have rolled forward) due to the lack of pictures in the appropriate angle to show ground marks, but if it did then it explains why the tail rotor was found a distance away.

    I do not think it's likely that the tail rotor snapped off in flight, the results of such a catastrophic failure is usually such that the aircraft is virtually uncontrollable, and certainly almost impossible to put down with such minimal damage to the forward airframe as indicated in the pictures.

    My analysis from the pictures.
