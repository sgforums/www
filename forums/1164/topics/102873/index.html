    Originally posted by react81:
    what about master sergent, first sergent, master gunnery sergent, sergent major, and sergent major of the marine corps?
    After Gunnery SGT (E-
    the Marine can go on to Master Gunnery SGT (E-9), which enables him to specialise in other fields.

    Alternatively he can go on to command, whereby he will be known as 1st Sgt (E-. After that is the rank of Sgt Major (E-9).

    1SGs in the USMC are usually senior NCOs of Coy sized units.
    Sergeant Majors get to be the "right-hand" of officers commanding far larger units.

    At the very very top is Sergeant Major of the Marine Corps, who quite literally sits next to the Commandant of the United States Marine Corps.

    Now is the picture clearer?
