  • Sardaukar
    23 Mar 05, 22:46
    My current view on NS is that the period of time that we serve needs to be lengthened.I'm currently an NSF training to be a combat engineer.My unit is 39 SCE.

    As an enlisted personnel,I will have to serve in the SAF for 2 years,a time frame which was the normal service time for men in the SAF in previous batches.However,the reduction of half a year and a uniform service time of 2 years for ever NSF,including specs and officers means that the strength of the army will be affected,especially the command cadre.

    As I understand it,the SAF already puts much resources into training these commanders during NS,but the shortening of service time reduces their length of active service.With less time for their training,I personally doubt that commanders will be of the same calibre as before.What assurance can be given that our national security,armed forces morale and operational effectivness won't be affected?Furthermore,is it cost effective to have to put the resources into training these commanders only to have them serve 2 years?

    I feel that we should return to the old system of 2.5 years for commanders and 2 years for men,but with provisions for commanders,in that benefits can be provided for those who wish to pursue further education before returning to complete their full service term,perhaps like lessened tax rates for their parents while doing university degrees.I feel that this is something which warrants further attention and would be of benefit to our army as a whole.

    24 Mar 05, 11:22
    Realistically speaking,I think 2.5 years was a good balance,and the minimum time in which we can train competent troops.However,due to manpower demands all around,the SAF has probably had to compromise this approach with regards to training time.

    Different and flexible approaches can be taken to still keep the NS time at 2.5 years,its just that we need some lateral thinking and the determination to carry it through.lI already suggested one approach such as earlier deferments with tax breaks for their families while NSFs defering their service undergo university studies.Other approaches can be devised that may be unconventional nature but still help to keep the training time there.

    I was the first batch to undergo the 9 week BMT,and I can say with confidence that it was rushed,everything was rushed.While everything can be compressed into 9 weeks,such as field camp,live range,sit test,BAC and the various other training components,the pace may not be enough to properly train soldiers up.

    I think we should really be concerned about this NS affecting our overall manpower quality.I feel this is a major concern and that is why I am highlighting it.

    While there may be no clear and present danger at this point in time,we know how mercurial ties can be with certain neighbours over the long term.Governments change.For these neighbours,there is less of a continuity in politics,especially with regards to certain foreign policy matters,unlike us where we have a certain continuity and long term outlook in our policies.

    It is for this reason that I feel the 2 years NS isn't suitable as it affects our strength at its core,the manpower.It is the people which make or break a military,and all the fancy technology we are getting is just that,technology which acts as a force multiplier that evens the odds at a strategic and tactical level,It basically evens the odds and enhances and expands our capabilities.However,with all this you need proficient training with skills and knowledge as well as the inculcated discipline as well as physical and mental endurance.And to impart all these qualities through training,you need time.

    With the 2 years,when you have invested the resources in training your people,they will have less time of active service,so it will be less cost effective overall,plus with people ORDing earlier,unit strength will not be kept at certain levels.Perhaps ways can be looked at extending the service of men(enlisted pers.) to 2 years 2 months and commanders extended back to 2 years 6 months.Perhaps for those who wish to serve NS even longer,there can be an option to extend your service length to another half a year or a year and a promotion to go with it ,though not necessarily a realistic option.

    This all therefore affects our strength.This policy orchestrated by PM Lee really needs relooking.This is a policy that I feel will compromise our overall security integrity.

    The only comparable citizen soldier armies that serve as long or longer than Singapore are those that have been in a constant state of war or war-footing for 40 years or longer; viz. Korea, Israel, Taiwan.

    The Swiss, as an alternate example, serve only 6 months upfront and the same sort of number of weeks/year as reservists, and their defenses were sufficient such that they held off the Germans in WWII from invading. The Germans, who during the cold war were front-line against Warsaw Pact countries, only serve 9 months active duty.

    2.0 years (let alone 2.5 yrs) is an extreme length given there is no clear and present danger and no state of war has existed in Singapore since the end of WWII -- accepting that Konfrontasi was really a skirmish between a still colonial Malaysia and a recently independent Indonesia. That long a service detracts heavily from fundamental developmental years in a young man's life and career, and is an unnecessary burden IMO under the current geopolitical environment Singapore finds itself.
