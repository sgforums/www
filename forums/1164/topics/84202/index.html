    1st phase RT is twice a week for 4 weeks( 1 weekday and 1 weekend), 8 session take test.
    Discharged if you pass.

    Otherwise 2nd phase RT(9-20 sessions), I think is thrice a week for 4 weeks(2 weekdays and 1 weekend), at the 16 sessions ,you will take the test again.
    Pass you will be discharged, if you fail ,you need to complete the 20 sessions(total 8 weeks).

    So after the 20 sesssions, you will be considered to have completed your obligation for the window before, but since you have not pass your IPPT during RT , you still need to book for IPPT for the new window.

    If you have passed your IPPT during the RT, it be will considered as you have passed your IPPT in the new window & even monetary award if you make the mark.

    For eg, at age 27th window, you have failed your IPPT after many tries, so you book for RT within 2 months after your 28th birthday, went for the RT, let's say you pass your RT's IPPT at the 16th sessions and be discharged. You are also considered to have pass your IPPT for your age 28th window.

    Can we defer RT if we have something on like night classe/work and is it subject to approval? Who shall we send the deferment letter to?
    Cannot escape. RT is now legally like ICT; your employer is required to release you for both under the Enlistment Act.

    Administratively, your NS unit is responsible for your RT. It'll book a progam slot for you if you didn't book one yourself, & will also access your application for deferment (medical chit, studies, etc.).

    If you're subsequently granted leave of absence, you must still do make-up training for each session missed, subject to a maximum of 2-3 depending on which RT Phase you're in. Any further missed sessions would render your existing RT program void, you being called back for investigations (maybe with disciplinary actions), & a new RT schedule rebooked for you.

    http://content.miw.com.sg/Mindef/Static/Ippt1/FAQs/rt_faq_index.asp
