    okie... lemme clear things up a little... okie, i've done all these in 2001, so whether if there had been any changes to the system, i do not know...

    Pre-enlistment: (if you expressed your interest in joining when you are in JC or Poly)

    1st stage - CPSS

    this is the stage where you go down to CMPB to get your computerised pilot thingy done...
    the test is mainly on your IQ, psycho-motor skills. the test is roughly ard 4 hours long...

    Pass or fail, they'll send you letter...

    2nd stage - Health Examination

    You'll be asked to go down to Aeromedical Centre at PLAB. A series of body checkups will be done... Things include an X-ray of your spine, heartbeat rate, eyesight, ears and nose checkup, dental checkup. They will ask you to go into a room to measure the length of your legs and hands. The height when you sit down. Roughly thats abt it.

    If you fail, you'll probably hear from them. If you pass, the next stage would come during your BMT.

    During enlistment: (if you indicated your interested in joining during BMT when the AFRC ppl come down for a recruitment talk)

    Okie the first and second stage are the same, just that the order has been switched. You'll go for your health examination first before the CPSS

    3rd Stage: (same for both pre-enlistment and during enlistment)

    Okie this is the interview stage where you'll be asked to to sit in front of a panel of interviewers which include a LTC (prob from FTS), a CPT (prob from AFRC) one more member and then 2 psychologist.

    typical questions like tell me more abt yourself and why you wanna join the Air force will be asked...

    Immediately, you'll be notified whether you are selected as a pilot trainee...

    after which, those who has to go to OCS first will go to OCS, those that is selected but din make it to OCS will go to Air Force School to wait for their chance to go to Tamworth for their airgrading.

    Hope this helps to clear the air
