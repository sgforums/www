    15 May 04, 17:50
    Well that's a myth propagated by fixed-wing jocks which has been very nicely taken up by the general public.

    Not saying that fixed-wing flying is easy, but flying a helicopter is much more demanding than a plane due to the fact that a helicopter is aerodynamically unstable on all 3 axis (take your hands off the controls for more than a few seconds and you can say bye-bye to stable flight; autopilot notwithstanding). It's another case of comparing apples and oranges, but the actual flying part, IMHO, is more demanding for a helo pilot.

    And yes, there are commercial helo pilot jobs available and I would believe they have it easy since former airforce pilots also have to compete with a huge army of commercial pilots who were never in the airforce.

    Just look how easy it was for the 9/11 hijackers to get flying instructions for commercial flight. As a civilian, you can get to be a commercial pilot without having to go through the airforce.

    As for airforce pilots, almost everybody wants to be a fighter jock, that's a natural mystique. There is a hierarchy of status within most airforces. Fighter jocks think ground-pounders are fighter washouts. Both think transport pilots are glorified bus drivers. While all think that heli-pilots are a combination of the above. Just let it be known, all of them have a place within the airforce.
