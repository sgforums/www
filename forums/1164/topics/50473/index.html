    The "GY" that you are talking about is some sort of optical director. I think you need to point the director at the target.

    The operator need to point the optical director (got powerful telescope) at the target. The fire control radar will be aligned together with the optical director and point at the same bearing. It will then emit electronic signal towards target.

    The Gabriel missle will launch and will be a "beam rider" and follow the electronic signal at the target.

    The Gabriel has a two-stage guidance system, beam rider at the inital phase, then at the terminal phase it will switch on its on-board radar at the nose to "seek out" the target, correct itself and hit the target.

    This above actually very very primitive. this is because In the past, electronics and computers not so high tech.

    The radar at the missle's nose is small and can only be used at short range. So the missle need to be guided initially. Nowadays, they will " program" the missle and which will fly a "pre-programed" path and when the missle is at the point of the programmed path where it is expected that the target is near the target the radar on the missle will switch on, find the target and home in. There is no need for initial guidance liao.

    The above not classified info. I think I read it many years ago in a book about SSMs.

    To try to answer your question about our "over-size" "Patrol Vessel".

    The other of our Patrol craft are only armed with 40mm or 76.2mm gun.

    I think the RSN have requirements for 6x ASW PV and 6x PC/PV (to complement and maybe utlimately replace the existing Patrol craft.

    Instead of getting two different types of vessels for the same role, they use a single basic design/ hull /Engine to fulfill both roles.

    They produce 12 similar ships but with two sets of roles. Although the PVs may seems over-armed, it might actually be cheaper than getting a seperate smaller type of vessel as a PV but have larger maintenance requirements.

    I think RSN quite smart in this regard. Very efficient.

    If we want to have a more numerous ASW force (to counter our neighbour built-up of submarines), can re-arm the PVs to ASW Vessel and buy another class of PVs.
