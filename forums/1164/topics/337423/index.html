    The initial reactions to F-35 cost figures are now just filtering in. Israel is stunned by the US$200m price tag for each of the F-35A CTOL (conventional) fighters. http://www.defensenews.com/story.php?i=3823104&c=FEA&s=CVS Earlier, some observers have suggested that the US$5b price tag submitted to congress for 25 units may have been a life-time cost basis but apparently, this may not be so. Whilst the combat capability of the F-35 is not in question, some would question the cost-effectiveness when 4 sukhois can be procured by potential aggressors for every F-35. What does this mean for the RSAF? Replacing 3 sqns of F-5s with 72 F-35s (24 plane sqn) will cost US$14.4b or S$21.6b to S$26b (at exchange rates of S$1.5 to S$1.8 to US$1). This will take a huge chunk of the military budget considering the current budget is only ~S$10b a year. Translated, this could mean stretching the procurement over a longer period or accepting a smaller number per sqn. Alternatively, this could also mean another 2% GST rate hike over the course of the next 15 years or 1% over the next 30 years depending on how much the procurement can be stretched. Replacing 3 sqns of F-5s with 54 F-35s (18 plane sqn) will cost US$10.8b or S$16.2b to S$20b (at exchange rates of S$1.5 to S$1.8 to US$1). Alternatively, the RSAF could continue procuring another 24-48 F-15s (@~US$2-4b) and 36-18 F-35s (@US$7.2b-$3.6b) for a total package of US$9.2b-$7.6b respectively. If the latter, the RSAF may even opt to take the more expensive F-35B STOL variant (~US$250m per copy = US$4.5b-US$9b). This will provide off-airfield capability as well as operation via the current endurance LPDs or a future LHD. Such a purchase could also provide significant defensive A2A capability in the situation when the airfields are non operational. Whilst 18 F-35s might seem small, it should be noted that regional airforces do not have significant numbers of sukhois either. 48-72 F-15SGs aren't pushovers either. The above doesn't factor-in inflation. Considering ballooning plane cost, Mindef/DSTA might decide it is more worthwhile just to bite the bullet and procure the F-35 as quickly as possible to prevent inflationary cost pressures that plague most US procurement programmes. Judging by past mentality, military spending is not something that the current govt will take a risk on. Singaporeans should expect to face more tax increases in the near future.
