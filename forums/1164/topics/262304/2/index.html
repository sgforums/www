    I didn't wanna get involved in this thread. But I guess, seeing the heated discussion and exchange of views, I decided to throw in my loose change.

    Civilians caught in war, or collateral damage? Summary Execution or plain and simple murder?

    They mean the same thing, just different perspective. None of them paints a pretty picture, and definitely theres no way to hide the tragedy that human life was loss.

    SGT-rex's view is simply put, war is terrible. And you have to do things that you don't really know whether is right or wrong at the end. A split second decision to protect your buddy, your friends, could well be an act of murder. You might accidentally shoot an unarmed civilian, or even an unarmed- but surrendered enemy soldier. But you might also have shot a potential insurgent who was strapped with explosives and a detonator in hand. The only way to find out is to risk either lose your buddy, your section mates - or - risk being labelled as a murderer of an innoncent. Its a very, boots on the ground view, coming from the perspective of a soldier on the ground.

    Chino, Dr Who, if I don't gather wrongly, have more legalistic and in a way a more "politician's view." It is a fair cry for justice, for the innoncents that have lost their lives from summary executions and human rights violations. There is no reason why an unarmed civilian or even an unarmed-surrendered- soldier, protected under conventions can be shot.

    Interestingly, both views, though at the extreme ends of the spectrum, are what keeps actions commited in war, in check. In the oddest of way, they work hand in hand, constantly checking on each in order to achieve the same aim. That is, to reduce casualties to a minimum.

    Adopting SG T-rex's boots on the ground view wholly, would lead to a situation of chaos and massive loss of civilian life, IF there weren't legalistic hawks to jump of them. At the same time, if any army was run by legalistic hawks who care more about following conventions and lawful actions over the safety of their own troops, then that Army is bound to take unacceptable losses, lose morale, the will to fight and its sense of purpose. And eventually, they will no longer be an army, but just a band of killers.

    When the Japanese invaded Singapore, many young Chinese men were beheaded, on suspect that they were part of the resistance movement. Or in modern terms, insurgents. That is precisely the scenario that i mentioned, where there are no legalistic hawks to check on the actions of the army. Massacres and other human rights abuses ensued.

    Call me a fence sitter. But I believe BOTH views are necessary as long as there are conflict and wars.
