    Can someone explain to me whats the difference between 44 and/or 55 calibre with the 120mm calibre? What exactly does the former measures? Thanks
  • sane_guy2
    18 Dec 06, 16:16
    120mm is the diameter of the round, and hence also roughly the internal diameter of the Gun barrel. 120mm/L44 means the length of the barrel is 44 times of 120mm = 5280mm.
    The 120mm/L55 has a barrel length of 55 X 120mm = 6600mm.
    The longer the barrel, the longer the range, because it allows for a longer duration of acceleration in the gun (i.e. force is applied on the projectile for a longer duration).
