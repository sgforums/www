    you do the tests and interview... but i tink your perfomance in bmt has some bearing as well.

    after completing bmt, your posting will be announced to you. if successful, then your posting will be "pilot trainee".

    you will then report to OCS to begin next phase of training. because now tri-service system, so officer cadets from all three services will spend the first phase of the OCS training together. It lasts 4 wks. this phase quite slack one lar, mainly classroom lessons, and PT. it ends with a summary outfield exercise. first time you will go extended outfield in the 4 wks you're there.

    then after tri-service term is completed, some of your fellow cadets will be posted to MIDS Wing, to continue training as Navy cadets, while you, as pilot trainee, will go Air Wing. The rest of the cadets will continue as Army cadets. I think you will proceed to Air School, which is not at OCS grounds, right after tri-service, then come back some to OCS sometime later.

    So, all potential pilots go through bmt first. still got chance
