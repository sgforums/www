    I think things in Singapore are great. They are not as good as the boom years of the 90's, but those were the exception, not the rule. I think that too many Singaporeans have grown to regard that sort of an environment as "normal". Many people on this forum are young, and your entire childhood (entire life actually) has been in the midst of an economic boom, so it may seem like the world is ending. It is actually just returning to normal. From the state of the economy, everything else springs.

    As for the political situation, things have improved a lot, and they continue to improve. Lee Hsien Loong will not have anywhere near the level of power his father enjoyed. The press is also much freer -- if you don't like a conservative newspaper like ST, read the liberal Today, or simply look around the Internet. The civil service has always been open to anyone -- it is not an aristocracy. Anyone can rise through the service, though your effort must begin in childhood at school.

    The military situation is as it has always been. No matter what, we are not a battle-tested force, and if all goes well that will remain so. Battle is the primary means of eliminating incompetent commanders, and we lack it. Fortunately, the SAF personnel system at least seems to eliminate useless officers before they reach COL, and bad NCOs before WO2 -- I have never seen a bad colonel, general or warrant (especially the last). Screw ups are simply a fact of life in any organization of hundreds of thousands of men, so I am not too worried there.
