    Shanghai_Grand ... nothing against you asking questions but can you be a bit more accurate in your usage of terms?

    I don't know if it was your original intention to ask about a rifle (as opposed to gun) penetrating armour but leading from that I'm not sure if your question on "AP round from a bullet" refers to small calibre bullets or large calibre rounds ...

    Anyhow, you may find the following link useful as a primer on basic munitions ...

    Ammunition resource from GlobalSec

    In any case, with regards to your original question on "some rifle that can pierce tank armor" ... There are but as some have mentioned, it depends on how you define rifle and how you define tank armour.

    If by rifle you mean a man portable rifle, then historically, there have been plenty of so-called "Anti-Tank Rifles", from small calibres such as the German 7.92mm PanzerbÃ¼chse to the Finnish 20mm Lahti. These were capable of various levels of penetration but had one common factor ... they were all facing obsolesence by the early years of WW2.

    (you can read more about WW2 ATRs from here http://www.antitank.co.uk/)

    There is a finite limit to how much armour these man portable rifles can penetrate and that limit was reached pretty much in WW2. Tank armour OTOH has progressed significantly beyond that.

    So comes the second part of defining "tank armour". If you're talking about an MBT like a Leo 2, then no way in hell. Unless you want to count in golden BB shots.

    OTOH, an AMX-13 is still considered a tank The armour protections on an unmodified AMX-13 can be penetrated by even 12.7mm SLAP (Saboted Light Armour Piercing) rounds.

    Modern 0.50in (12.7mm) sniper rifles are also sometimes called anti-material rifles. They also commonly have AP rounds available for engaging lightly armoured vehicles such as APCs, Armoured trucks, etc.

    Hope that helps.

    There are AP rounds in much smaller calibres than that ... AFAIK there is the M995 5.56mm AP round and the M993 7.62mm AP round. Also IINM, the standard 4.7mm round for the P90 is also of an AP design (if not designation).

    AP rounds for rifle calibres are usually denoted by a black tip on the bullet. They differ from standard ball rounds in that they usually have a steel (or harder material) penetrator embedded in the bullet core instead of a standard lead core.

    The 0.50in SLAP round is somewhat different in that it is IINM it is more an APDS (Armour Piercing Discarding Sabot) round than a pure AP round.
