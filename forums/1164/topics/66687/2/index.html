    If you choose to server NS, you have no choice in your posting. The govt decide.

    If you want to sign on as career soldier, it's 6 years. But you can break the contract after 5 years. 2.5 years is only for NS. If you choose to sign on, you can choose your career path. Example, you apply to be an Air Force technician or a signal specialist with the Army. You need not indicate your choice up front. You can decide during your BMT. During your BMT, you'll be able to make a better choice.

    I'm from IT as well. I know of ex-Army guys who sign on as signal specialists and subsequently became managers with Alcatel. So, it depends on what IT specialisation you're in.

    Please remember that if you serve half way and decide to quit, you'll be treated as a deserter. You can forget about coming back to Singapore forever because you'll end up in jail. This is worse than you not serving NS in the first place.

    Physical fitness: running is very important. Do it as often as you can. Run 5km regularly and you should be fine.
