    Two things that count against the M249 are its weight and bulk - 7.1 kg as opposed to the Ultimax's 4.9 kg (both without ammo). If you're trying to keep the other guy's head down, I don't know how much difference 750 rpm makes as opposed to 600. If you wanted heavy firepower, you may as well make the jump up to a GPMG.

    In terms of accuracy, the M855 and M193 ball rounds seem to demonstrate silimar groupings when fired at up to 500 mteres through an M16A2. From the following woubd profiles, the 855's additional 6.6 grains don't seem to make much of a difference either:




    For what it was designed to be, the Ultimax is an excellent weapon. It's light and small enough to be easily carried by a section gunner humping a load of gear through the boonies, and given the average size of a Sinagporean section gunner, the difference between an Ultimax and an M249 would be well appreciated.

    I don't know about belt feeding, but I'll certainly agree that the Ultimax's loading system could stand a fair amount of improvement, and I never cared much for that Tommy-gun style foregrip. Given the accuracy and low recoil of the Ultimax, a Picatanny rail would be a good addition to the design.

    Yeah, we had a company live firing at Safti where we used the drums. Mind you, while we indented ammo for the whole company, two thirds of us were attached out to sports teams at the time, so the rest of us ended up having to expend everything. Got about 400 rounds on the Ultimax and 1000 on the GPMG, and I couldn't smell anything but Cordite for the next week.

    The drum sat more snugly in the Ultimax than the magazine did - I'm not too worried about it dropping off. For the old top-loading weapons like the Bren, the key design consideration was the ability to load the weapon without having to lift it up off the bipod - a mate of mine served in the British Army in the late 60's and used the Bren, and he loved the ability to change mags without having to move out of firing position. Gravity's got nothing to do with it - if the magazine/drum isn't seated properly, it wouldn't feed properly regardless of whether it was on top of or under the weapon.
