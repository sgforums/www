    Think you mean "Conscientious Objectors", who may also include non-JW ppl.

    Jehovah's Witnesses are ppl who practice a "deviant form of Christianity" who refuse to perform any military service, salute the flag, or swear oaths of allegiance to the State. In 1972 the Government deregistered and banned the Singapore 'Congregation of Jehovah's Witnesses' on the grounds that its existence is prejudicial to public welfare and order. At the time, there were approximately 200 Jehovah's Witnesses in Singapore; now there are approximately 2,000.

    Singapore does not recognise the right of "Conscientious Objectors" not to serve NS, like in Germany. Objectors are court-martialled and sentenced to 12 months in military detention. They are then called up again and non-compliance results in further 24-month sentences. Differ from Detainees, they wear white Tee and PT shorts etc. and do General Duty work for their term instead of harsher DB.
