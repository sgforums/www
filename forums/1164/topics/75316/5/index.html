    When I said "if it aint broke don't fix it", I was refering to the SAR mags. Since some TAR makes can reuse the M16 mags, I don't see why the SAR (somewhat similar to TAR) can't reuse them. Even if they had to replace the mags, why use polymers that have to be reinforced on the outside instead of sticking to metal mags?
    That's because polymer mags are far more durable, stronger and most importantly, do not warp.

    It can be fatal in battle when you realize you can't load your magazine because you warped your metal magazine back then when you went prone. Metal as a material for a magazine is unstable, it expands considerably when heated, tends to warp and is vulnerable to environmental conditions. Polymers as a materal are far more stable. I also like the feature that you can see how many rounds you have left. No grabbing for a misplaced, four round mag by accident in the heat of battle. I also hate to die because my last magazine refused to load after I dropped it on a hard surface accidently.

    Also, I suspect the reason why STK made the SAR-21 use its own mags (hereby setting up a logistical issue) was because SAF itself announced it's intentions that it was going to totally replace the M-16 in due time.

    Granted the current polymer mags are not perfect, but they are certainly superior to the old metal mags, and with a proper magazine pouch most of your problems with drawing them will cease to exist.

    SAR-21 for Singapore
    An Overview of the Development of the SAR-21 Assault Rifle

    (The new SAR-21 is expected to progressively replace the M16 as the standard issue assault rifle in service with the Singapore Armed Forces.)

    The conceptual design phase of the SAR 21 (Singapore Assault Rifle for the 21st century) Started at Chartered Industries of Singapore (a member of Singapore Technologies) in 1993. Development activities over the first two years were mostly focused on feasibility studies and the search for viable design technology. The key aim for the project was to develop an assault rifle which should have significantly higher hit probability than the M16 (the current standard-issue individual weapon of the Singapore Armed Forces), while at the same time being more pact and offering a lower overall system weight (when fitted with an equipment aiming device ). The rifle should also be equipped with an integrated optical sight.

    The SAR 21 is a 5.56 mm caliber, gas-operated, Â“bullpupÂ” configured weapon system capable of firing in semi-automatic and full-automatic modes. The Â“bullpupÂ” configuration results in a shorter rifle without any reduction in the barrel length; indeed, the barrel length of SAR 21 is the same as in the M16, and therefore the inherent advantages (such as round dispersion and overall accuracy) that are barrel length-dependent were not compromised by the project goalÂ’s of a compact weapon.

    In addition to being lighter and shorter than the M16 it is intended to replace in SAFÂ’s service, the SAR-21 offers other significant advantages on terms of integrated day/night sighting capability, full automatic controllability, ease of maintenance, reduced recoil and additional safety features.

    The SAR21 is modular in design consisting of five basic assemblies: barrel, upper receiver, lower receiver, bolt and magazine groups (see drawing). The modular construction allows ease of disassembly/reassembly and maintenance. The operator needs only to disengage two takedown pins and the weapon may then be field stripped in a matter of seconds or reassembled in less than a minute without the use of tools. Once field-stripped, all component parts requiring operator maintenance, cleaning or inspection are readily accessible.

    Extensive use of modern and advanced engineering plastic materials is made for the weapon's upper receiver body, safety shield, hand-guard and lower receiver.

    Accessories include rifle sling, blared firing attachment, cleaning kit, bore sighting equipment and Miles attachment.

    OPERATION

    The SAR-21 is magazine-fed, and has a close-bolt type of firing mechanism. The weapon uses a long-stroke piston with the main spring well protected inside the guide tube. The bolt tube has two large locking lugs that rotate 450 to lock with the barrel extension. The SAR-21 incorporates the CIS patented constant recoil principle to balance the bolt group and the recoil spring force in order to improve full automatic controllability.

    Ejection is to the right and is accomplished by a rocking ejector. The cocking handle is fully ambidextrous as it is located on the centerline being protected within the housing scope mount when not in use.

    The SAR-21 fires in semi-automatic and full-automatic modes. The mode-of-fire selector is housed in the lower receiver, while the safety button is just forward of the trigger. The rifle uses a 30-rd translucent plastic magazine to enable the user to have a direct visual feedback on the number of rounds in stocks.

    The SAR-21 features an integrated 1.5x optical scope and a laser aiming device (LAD), both of which are factory-zeroed and do not require further field zeroing for engagement.

    The optical sighting axis is optimally positioned to suit the average firer. The LAD is specially configured to suit the weapon, and the activating button is positioned such that no loose cable is exposed. The LADS is powered by one AA-size battery which can last for 4 hours of continual usage. Both visible and IR LADs are available.

    As both the optical sight and the LAD are integrated to the weapon, stringent requirements were specified. Under normal condition, other than general cleaning of lenses, no other preventive maintenance is required.

    Human Factor Engineering

    In the SAR-21 design, human factor considerations were heavily factored during the development stage. HFE trials involving hundreds of soldiers were conducted which has resulted in the current profile of the butt stock, the cheek rest on the upper receiver, and the location of the safety button and the cocking handle.

    HFE also involves safety features. One of the main safety considerations in a Â“bull pupÂ” Rifle is that the firing chamber is much closer to the operatorÂ’s head than in a conventional rifle layout. To address this concern, the SAR-21 has been designed to provide the maximum protection possible by incorporating a built-in composite material Safety shield, metal reinforcement, positive large locking surfaces and by using a gas pressure venting hole in the barrel extension, to release the high pressure gas/blast.

    The Weapon Family concept:

    In addition to the standard-issue assault rifle, there are several possible variants of the SAR-21 to meet the varying requirements of different users and yet maintain high commonality of parts. This is achievable due to the modular concept adopted in its design.

    Main variants include:

    Sharp Shooter Variant
    This is a standard SAR-21 with an integrated 3x optical scope. Replacement of scope from 1.5x to 3x or vice-versa can be performed at unit level.

    Light Machine Gun Variant
    This variant comes equipped with a heavy barrel and bipod, and operates in a full-automatic open bolt firing mode. More than 60% of the components are identical to the basic assault rifle.

    SAR-21/M203 Variant
    This is a SAR-21 attached with a M203 grenade launcher with 9Â” barrel. The LAD is mounted on the quadrant platform.

    SAR-21 Picatinny-Rail (P-Rail) Variant
    This variant, featuring a sight-mounting platform in place of integrated optical sight permits the use of all types of optical, electro-optical or collimating sight as selected by the operator.
