    Ah yes, the J-10:



    According to this website (http://www.sinodefence.com/airforce/aircraft/fighter/j10.asp): "The original design of the J-10 was based on the cancelled Israel Aviation Industry (IAI) Lavi lightweight fighter. China and Israel started collaboration in the early 1980's and full-scale co-operation was underway by 1984. After the 1987 cancellation of the Lavi programme, its design was taken over by the CAC [Chengdu Aircraft Industry Corporation], and the IAI carried on with the development of avionic equipment..." (There's much more.)

    Another websource (http://home.iae.nl/users/wbergmns/info/j10.htm) indicates an earlier (cancelled) delta-canard incarnation called the J-9 designed by Shengyang Aircraft Industry Co. that was based on the Mig-23--and--the Viggen (a little hard to believe, actually; unless the Swedes are really a lot more 'neutral' than I imagined them to be--even in the 60s!)

    But there definitely are resemblances to the Lavi:



    The Lavi itself is, of course, much more obviously influenced by the F16. On the other hand, the J-10's intake is definitely Typhoon like; thought the slight 'hump' just behind the cockpit is somewhat Su-27-ish (below):



    In other words, there's a bit of everything here: zhong guo ren, yang yang do...yao!
