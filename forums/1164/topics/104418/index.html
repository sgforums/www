    I have the dubious opportunity to work on J I (go figure) where security is tight and go through the checkpoint every day.

    Most of those doing sentry duty are NS Men (Reservist), sometimes NSF together with CISCO etc. Most times spot them with finger "on" trigger; till someone wrote in and a change in "Standing Orders" put a stop to it. (although still spot sentries 'sitting down' at times).

    Having your finger on the trigger is ill-advised (as this is poor weapon safety). The M-16 has space on the pistol grip for all 4 fingers (your tumb wraps round other side duh!) as original design was for larger hands. The SAR 21, being local design is designed for asian users with a shorter pistol grip.

    The tendancy for your index/pointing finger to 'stray' onto the trigger is hard to suppress. Blame it on this small oversight, as well as economy on materials, .

    It may also be interesting to ask if all weapons basic design parameters pre-suposes that finger "on" the trigger; is more likely state than, finger 'off' the trigger.
