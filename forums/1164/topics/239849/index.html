    Regiment vs Battalion.

    In old day, SAF Infantry (SIR & Guard), AI & AR were organized in Regiment, other formations were Battalion.

    Reason being is Regiment strength around 900 troops whereas Battalion is around 500 troops. After the reorg & down sizing of SAF fighting troop, today Regiment may be history to SAF.

    Note: - Old day, 1 section :- 8 or 9 man depend which period
    1 PL :- 3x section
    1 Coy :- 3 x section, 1 x support arm section
    1 Regiment :- 4 x Coy, 1 support Coy, 1 HQ Coy
    1 Bdge :- 4 x regiment, plus various organized Coy/Bty
    support arm
    1 Div :- 4 x Bdge, plus various Battalion support arm
    As such, 1 Div is around 10K troop.

    Today, fighting troop down size to x3 (Support arm BN also down size as well). As such, 1 Div is around 7~8K troop.

    This is how I understand it:

    My guess is that in the old days, the term "Regiment" in SIR denoted a specific size of a unit.

    So 1SIR really meant 1st Singapore Infantry Regiment, 2SIR meant 2nd Infantry regiment and so on. And in the old days we literally only had a couple of regiments.

    But these days, 1SIR means 1st Battalion of the Singapore Infantry Regiment. 2SIR means 2nd Battalion of the same Singapore Infantry Regiment.

    So how big is a "Regiment" in Singapore?

    My guess is that the "Regiment" no longer denotes a particular size. It is merely some nostalgic hold on tradition to keep using the term "SIR" for our units.

    The term "Singapore Infantry Regiment" is more like the family surname and doesn't denote a specific strength. So every battalion in Singapore - irregardless of which Brigade or Division - is a part of the BIG Singapore Infantry Regiment family.
