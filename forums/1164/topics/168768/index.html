    For civilian use, the original M-16 gas system is probably good enough(?). Sure, it starts clogging up after a few dozen live rounds but a well-maintained civilian rifle should still be able to keep shooting with little danger of IA.

    Well, you could get a stock M4 or XM177 for US$950 on the open market?
    Oh, easily! And if you're willing to build one instead of buying it complete, you can make yourself a very nice M4 clone at that price.

    Complete firearms sold retail are subject to the federal excise tax (FET) which bumps up the final price somewhat. Building one piecemeal not only avoids that tax, but allows you to completely customize the final product to your specific requirements.

    More importantly (for me, at least) is the fact that you have total control over the quality of the build. All too often, I've come across factory-built AR and M4 clones that have glaring quality issues, like tilted front sight bases that require the windage adjustment on the rear sight to be maxed out to compensate (Bushmaster), incorrectly staked gas key screws atop bolt carriers (Olympic Arms), inconsistently applied finishes that cause the upper and lower receivers to be of noticeably different colours and/or textures (ArmaLite), extremely gritty triggers (Colt), etc.
