    During my course, there was a handful of non-Commandos and they were all regulars. However, my information is not up to date - I come from the days when it took 7 jumps to qualify.

    Be it freefall or static line, most of us took it simply as a way of getting from A to B, a glorified version of stepping of a three-tonner, as it were.

    CXnemesis, with the right instructors, commando training is far from hell. Certainly we did have some section commanders and platoon sergeants who ran around like psychotic poodles on Ecstasy, but they were not the norm and generally ended up making life harder for themselves than for anyone else.

    For the life of me, I cannot remember the name of the Special Boat Squadron officer in WW2 who said, "Are you TOUGH? If so, get out. I need f***ers with intelligence", but he summed it up very well. The training is not specifically designed to more physically demanding than that for other units (although it often works out that way), but, because of the tendency to operate in smaller numbers, to get the trainees to be more level-headed and so to fight more efficiently and with greater flexibility than those of other units.
