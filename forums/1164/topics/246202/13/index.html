    Movement along a Track
    CM06, you are Right, Moving along Tracks, barely have enough room to Zig Zag, even in the some parts of Taiwan (Don't know the place well, have not been to every part.) Movement is restricted within Tracks.

    In Asia, using large Tanks, is very restrictived in manoeuver space. Moving along a track, provide the Aggressor with a predicable/easy way to monitor the Armour movement.

    Zig Zagging
    Used in open terrain, not only for avoiding Artillery avoidance. But a host of others purposes, e.g but not restricted too:

    1) Gunnery Survival
    - Open terrain, Gunnery engagement, can be anywhere from 0.8 to 4 KM (Current Gunnery record, held by the UK Forces on a Challenger2)

    The sudden changing of directions, is in theory, should make the life of the Aggressor attempting to shoot at the Tanks difficult.

    Firing sequence
    Based on the course that the Aggressor assume that the Tank was moving, compute a Fire solution (can't think of a better term off hand).

    Pull the Trigger, and the round goes down range, flight time varies from 1-3 sec for Sabot, longer for a HEAT ammo.

    The round will hit a guess point that the Tank will be at after the flight time, but if a Tank change direction suddenly (Moving at a speed of 36km/hr, chose for ease of calculation) every 1 sec difference is 10 metres movement.

    Faster movement, bigger the error of aim, better chance of survival for the Tank.

    2) Throws up alot of dust, very useful feature.
