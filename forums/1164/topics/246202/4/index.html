    It depends on many factors, the difference in elevation between you and the target, the way the target is facing you, the properties of your rounds and many other things that affect the way your munition hits the armour.

    Obviously the optimal way to splash your target is to achieve a 90 deg hit with your warhead, but given that tank armour is sloped this is not often possible. If you fire straight on at a PT-91M's frontal hull armour (assuming the tank is flat and level with you as well), you will probably achieve much shallower angles of hit.

    There is a reason why armour is sloped.

    On the other hand, if you are splashing the tank from an elevated position, your warhead is going to hit closer to 90 degrees, and hence the penetration power will be increased.

    But the best is to go for the hull sides and turret, where the armour has virtually no slope at all, not to mention the armoured hide there is thinner as well. In any case it's foolhardly to directly engage an MBT in the frontal arc, given that's the most likely arc the tank can return immediate fire when you engage it.

    Flanking it and catching it in the side works better.

    Depends on the range, the huge CEP of rocket arty makes it useful for sending a lot of explosives in a very short time into a large area, however the actual mass of exposives it can send per unit area over distance is a lot less then tube arty.

    So while the forward HQ may take several hits, if they have been dug in properly they will most likely survive... unless the enemy is like the Yanks who can really toss plenty of rockets at you with impunity without the real need to shoot and scoot given they had already established superiority over their hapless enemy.

    I doubt the Malaysians will see the same situtation with their ASTROS, if the SAF's counter battery and RSAF does its job properly.

    MLRS is great for catching large masses of targets out in the open with minimal protection given its saturation effect, however it is a lot less useful when trying root out a properly dug in enemy force, in which tube arty, with its ability to consistently send HE to a smaller CEP would be far more useful.

    This is also very important in urban battles, where the landscape can provide plenty of cover from saturation attacks. It would simply take too many rockets to root out a properly established and dug in forward HQ at distance, not to mention a lot of the destruction would be wasted on nothing.

    In which case tube arty is a lot more useful at sending your enemy packing.

    Once again, different tools for different jobs. MLRS is great for sending stuff to an enemy that is advancing on you and lacks proper cover, this is when the ability of the rocket arty to saturate an area with explosives works the best.

    But properly dug in an harderned targets require more then the MLRS grid square removal effect, you got to send HE into a small CEP over a period of time.
