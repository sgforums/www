    No one will know, but it's possible to make some educated guesses:

    For example, APFSDS technology is reaching it's apex, that's why they have been suggesting alternatives to suceed it for some time now (ETC guns, larger calibers). There's a pratical limit to how much armour you can penetrate with a conventional APFSDS round from a gun of 120-125mm caliber.

    One limit is in the propellant, as chemical propellants have a limit as to how much bang they can produce (which has a pratical limit before it destroys the gun). But a greater limit lies in the penetrator itself.

    The design of the penetrator is prehaps the most important. The ratio of diameter to length, the type of material we make it out of and what have you not. Get the wrong length ratio and penetration will be inefficent. Get the material wrong and the penetrator might shatter and be ineffective.

    The problem with current APFSDS technology is that we are already reaching the very limits of what we can do with it from a 120-125mm platform. To get good penetration you need a very slim and long penetrator (high diameter to length ratio), but such a penetrator is only possible with exotic materials, and hence each round will cost a lot more. And this can only carry you so far.

    The yanks have some of the most effective APFSDS ammunition around, given they are the ones who are willing to spend a couple of grand with a single pull of the trigger and also the ones willing to use exotic materials like DU in their penetrators. And they are well aware of the limits of this technology. What we do know is that the frontal arc KE protection of their M1A1 HA (and onwards) series actually provide more protection then could be overcome by the pratical limits of APFSDS technology, that's why they worked on systems like LOSAT and the like to come up with new ways of defeating such KE packages, but nobody really followed up with it on their MBTs.

    In fact their next generation tube-launched KE technology is based on rocket assisted projectiles (more akin to LOSAT then conventional ballistic technology), they have reached the limit of what is possible with APFSDS technology just as how the limit with HVAP was reached before APFSDS was introduced.

    So back to the PT-91 APFSDS, this means that we can exclude the more fantastic numbers as pure fantasy and an attempt by the salesman to sell the round rather then actual reality, and the higher numbers as greatly suspect (unless the Malaysians are happy to buy their rounds at very expensive prices and deal with heavy metal toxicology issues), and focus on the more likely and lower estimates instead.

    I would treat "even 760mm RHA" as highly suspect, and see the lower numbers as more likely. Prehaps 450-650mm RHA is more in the ballpark.
