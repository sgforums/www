    Originally posted by Wosiu:
    Energy of blast is capable to change penetrator line of flight, in result, penetrator hit target own side. Impact still is big, so armour must be sufficient. Similar technology is in Israeli "Iron Fist" ADS, but not as advanced as in IBD/Akers Krutbruk product.
    AFAIK, the blast does not cause the "penetrator to hit target own side", but causes it to yaw somewhat of 8 degrees or so, which in the penetrators with high D/L ratios represents a great decrease in penetrating power. In rounds with lower D/L ratios this effect is less.

    But even if it strikes, it is still striking with a force that can possibly penetrate over 200mm RHA or even much more. So you still need to carry sufficent base armour.

    And of course this assumes the system works perfectly each and every time. We know passive armour in the path of projectiles won't miss, but ADS can and will have a failure to intercept rate, a reason why they can complement, but not replace armour.

    As I said, this light tank "convert" of the CV90 is impressive, but if you look on the same page, you'll realize the same company has also given the Leo2 their own souped up technologies.

    And of course, you still need armour to properly defend against non conventional threats like IEDs.

    And the autocannon question is still open... can the ADS defend against a stream of medium caliber rounds all arriving in a very short space of time? You still need armour at the end of the day.
