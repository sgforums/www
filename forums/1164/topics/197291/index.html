    You'll be hard-pressed to find full-auto weapons in private hands here in the US. Owning a full-auto weapon requires one to jump through a lot of hoops. The overwhelming majority of privately-owned self-loading rifles in this country are semi-automatic, not full-auto.

    A basic, no-frills M4 with a 16" barrel and plain iron sights can be purchased at a typical gunstore here for anywhere between $700 and $1000. You can save a lot of money if you are willing to buy the individual parts and build the rifle yourself; I've assembled many M4/AR-15 rifles for members of our gun club, and the total cost of parts for a typical basic M4-type rifle comes to around $550 (more if the member wants to add 'upgrades').
