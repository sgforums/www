    its simplistic to think that the more hardpoints an aircraft has, the more it'll be used. fighter bombers don't go into battle with the mentality that once they are in danger they would unload. mission planning is a tedious process. the type of weapons to carry for the mission depends on the threats. an a-6 never went bombing carrying sidewinders for self defense cos they are CAP protecting them. same goes for the prowler. if a hardpoint is available, it does not mean that it has to be utilised. f-4s in vietnam did not use the centreline hardpoint for the fuel tanks when they venture north cos they use wing tanks. so do they put a bomb there to maximise weapon load when doing CAP? the type of weapons carried affects the aerodynamics of aircraft. if the mission requires alot of agility. the pilot would want to keep the load at the minimum. the su-27 pilots which crashed at the ukraine airshow last year complained that the plane was carrying extra fuel that affects its maneuverability. a su-27 pilot would managed his fuel load such that when he reaches the hot zone, he had fuel only in his central tanks and the wings tanks are empty to get maximum agility. lift vs drag, thrust vs weight.
