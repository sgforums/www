    The rifle that the M16 was based on, the AR15, designed by Eugene Stoner, was a SUPERB weapon. In field tests, its performance was near perfect. United that were supplied with it didn't want to go back to the their M14s. In the early years of the VN War, a AR15 could fetch up to 500 bucks on the blackmarket (it was sold in the US for only $100). Such was its quality and performance.

    The problem is the AR15 was designed and manufactured by a civilian contractor, whereas the M14, the US military's standard rifle, was designed by the army. Naturally, the army didn't like having its rifled replaced by one that wasn't designed by them. In an era where politics was less than transparent, certain quarters of the US military establishment sought to sabotage any effort to replace the M14. And when they failed, they made certain changes to the M16 to ensure that it would offer sub-par performance.

    The 2 big problems were with the rifling of the barrel, and the type of propellant used in the ammunition.

    The brass believed that the M14's replacement should have its range. Being of smaller calibre, the AR15 naturally could not fire a round as far as the M14. The solution to that was to 1. increase the rifling of the barrel so that the bullet would spin more, be more stable in flight, thus travel a further distance. 2. use a propellant in the ammo that expanded more rapidly These 2 design features were thus added to the AR15, among others, and the M16 came into being.

    Not necessarily a bad thing, increasing the rifling of the M16's had a detrimental effect on lethality. The 5.56mm round was very light, but scientists had long discovered that the lighter a bullet was, the more devastating a wound would be. Bigger calibre bullets often passed thru the flesh because of the sheer momentum and mass. A 5.56mm round would tumble as it passed thru the flesh, causing a lot more tissue damage. By increasing the rifling, thus spin, the 5.56mm bullet would continue thru the body like a bullet of larger calibre, negating the inherent lethality it had.

    The AR15 was designed to be used with bullets that were propelled by IRT (I think). It was with such rounds that the AR15 performed perfectly, and was most impressive. The US military, however, had strong ties with companies producing rounds which used ball powder as a propellant. The M14 used such rounds, and various political and military forces worked to ensure that existing ammo suppliers would not lose their contracts.

    The problem with ball powder is that it burnt much faster, and was dirtier. The increased speed of combustion was good, because it gave the bullet a greater range, but ball powder rapidly fouled the M16's internal mechanisms, components that were designed for use with the cleaner IRT. That was the crux of why the M16 kept jamming - the wrong ammo was being used.

    I wouldn't say the AK47 is a superior weapon. Had the M16 been a wholesale copy of the AR15, it would have undoubtedly kicked the AK47's ass. Early field trials already suggested this. Unfortunatley, the GIs in Vietnam were saboed by a stubborn bureaucracy. The M16 was no AR15 although they looked similar.

    Most of the design problems were quite rapidly resolved, mostly because there was an immense amount of pressure on the govt from GIs, parents of GIs to do something about the unrealiability of the M16. Apparnetly, many GIs had written to their parents complaining about their M16s jamming. The pressure was enough for a House investigation committee to be convened to find out wtf went wrong. I believe the product of that was some heads rolled, and a 700 page report. Oh, and a better M16, of course. You can find it online if you search hard enough.

    But most now admit that it is a major design flaw of the M-16. This is because of its gas system where the gas is bled back into the bolt carrier to cause the bolt to go backwards. The problem with this is that there are a lot of moving parts in the bolt carrier. A slight buil-up of fouling immediately causes the mechanism to be sluggish and eventually IA.

    The AK uses a different system of blowback where such fouling from propellent waste does not affect performance.

    The US Army has learnt - after 30 years with the M-16. The XM-8 I think works with a piston where the gas is bled into the piston which then pushes the bolt. This increases performance like 100% as the piston is a simple tube and rod where fouling does not pose much of a problem.
