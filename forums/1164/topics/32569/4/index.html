    There are several problems with going at or near light speed:

    Firstly, getting to that speed. You can't acceletrate to 250,000km/s in any meaningful amount of time, for the accelerational forces will quickly turn you to mushroom soup. Even at an uncomfortable acceleration of 30/s2 or 3gs, it would take you three months to get to near light speed.

    Secondly, you can't really steer at that speed, your path would be pretty much a straight line, and since you are travelling way beyond the escape velocity of the earth, you would shoot off in a straight line into space (and reach the moon in a second) rather then follow the map of the earth.

    Thirdly, there are releativistic time dilation effects to worry about. (study special relativity)

    Fourthly, if you studied energy mass conversion, you would realize that you cannot exceed the speed of light. Even getting up to near that speed would require an exponential increase to infinity in energy required when approaching lightspeed. Short of something like warp drive, that would mean a complete revolution in modern physics, you could spend all the energy expended by the human race on a single hovertank to little purpose.

    Fifth, all the projectiles you fire would still travel below the speed of light, irregardless of even if you are at lightspeed. (why? study relativity again!)

    All in all, even if we did come up with things like deflector systems and mass dampers, and even warp drive, there is no meaningful reason why any land unit should be anywhere near luminial, or superluminal speeds.
