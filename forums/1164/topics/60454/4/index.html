    Like you said, Singapore was chosen as a training base primarily because of its accessibility and its strategic location meant it required defending. The training aircraft were based here since good bases abounded (Seletar, Sembawang, Tengah and Kallang) while training could be done over Malaya or Riau since they were all occupied territories. In addition, the old Naval Base was used as a main Repair Dock for IJN ships, and the Southern HQ here also required defending. Hence fighters were based here. The sea-lanes needed defending against Allied subs, so anti-sub aircraft units were based here.
