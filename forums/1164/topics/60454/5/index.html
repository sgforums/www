    More books for those interested in Singapore Military Aviation history

    Bloody Shambles Volume I/II by Shores/Cull/Izawa (Grub Street Books)
    The story of the air war over SouthEast Asia. Starts just before the war, and covers the air war over the Philippines, Malaya, Singapore, Dutch East Indies, Ceylon (Sri Lanka). Burma and the Darwin/Broome raids. Hardcover I just saw it at Kinokuniya yesterday (Sunday) and it costs about S$99 per volume. A little on the expensive side (I got mine for A$75 in Melbourne) but worth the read.

    Buffaloes over Singapore: RAF, RAAF, RNZAF, and Dutch Brewster Fighters in Action over Malaya and the East Indies, 1941-42 by Cull, Brian with Paul Sortehaug and Mark Haselden (Grub Street Books)
    Not read this book yet but will get it the first chance I got. Geoff Fisken DFC, who did the foreward, is a pilot who flew Buffalos over Singapore.

    Hurricanes over Singapore by Brian Cull (Grub Street Books)
