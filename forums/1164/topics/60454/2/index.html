    Originally posted by espanol_man:
    heard that there were jap Zeros in Singapore during WW2... is that true?
    Yes its true, records have shown that there were at least 4 Imperial Japanese Navy Kokutais (Flight Regiments) with various models of Zeros based in Singapore at different times of the war. One of those was a carrier-based Kokutai based at Seletar for crew training in 1943-44 and was decimated in the Battle of Philippine Sea in June 1944 (better known as the Marianas Turkey Shoot)

    Originally posted by superspitfire:
    Here's one fact I just found out...The Zeros never took part in dogfights over Singapore - that was done by the Japanese Army Air Force's Nakajima Ki.27.
    Actually superspitfire, thats slightly inaccurate. During the invasion of Malaya and Singapore the 22nd Naval Air Flotilla of the IJN has a small fighter unit attached to it (along with the bomber Kokutais that sank the Prince of Wales and Repulse) which consisted of land-based Navy Zeros. (tailhook removed). They did take part in limited missions escorting bombers and performing fighter sweeps over Singapore.

    You're right, however when you said that most of the air-to-air work over Singapore was done by the Nakajima Ki-27 'Nate'. Two Imperial Japanese Army Sentai (Fighter Regiments) were equiped with the 'Nate' during the invasion, along with 1 more Sentai equiped with the new (at that time) Ki-43 Hayabusa (Allied code-name 'Oscar')
