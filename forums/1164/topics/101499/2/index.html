  • Moxie
    8 Nov 04, 18:12
    Disagree that TTRL is anti-war. I can understand where Baer (& others) are coming from, but Malick is too visceral a filmmaker. The voice-overs, in particular, asked a lot of "cheem" questions but IMO they collectively alluded more to the vagaries of war. TTRL is, at worst, a meditation of military combat.

    Compare that with Saving Private Ryan, whose tour de force opening depicted the horrific randomness of battle, then contradicted itself with its self-conscious saccherine-sweet ending (suggesting some - abstract - things are worth fighting for). I remember feeling sold out, going WTF?

    [I stand corrected: Terrence Malick made TTRL, not Stanley Kubrick. The latter made Paths of Glory.]
