    yeah, thats quite a premium you paid.

    mine was like USD 320+shipping (HK prices), and that was 3 years ago, so by rights, the price should have dropped significantly in that time.

    All airsoft guns, aside from those made 20-odd years ago (which were made of proper metal) - and so classed as 'Classic airsoft', are incredibly fragile.
    The metal is not metal, but often an amalgam of different types of cheap (pot or pig) metal, and sometimes mixed plastic polymer and metal filings.

    I've seen many airsoft guns, even 'metal' ones simply snap in half because someone lent on them or something.
    They were never built to be used as rougly as real weapons in real life - and rightly so, because they were targeted for the toy and collector market first and foremost.

    The main areas of contention for realism is actually the width of the receiver, and the width of the handgrip.
    Its often alot wider than the real thing, and though they've managed to get round this by installing the correct width picatinny rail [if present], people who have handled the real deal can often tell the difference straight away.
    The reason for the disparity in widths is because the handgrip houses the electric motor [same as in r/c cars] and the receiver houses the gearbox and subsequently the springs and piston too.
    Obviously in the real deal the handgrip is hollow, but this is the best place for the motor angle position.

    not sure what i can suggest.
    AEG's are pretty much the last type of airsoft product to use as a target shooter..
    (bar, according to reports, TM's M14 series which is apparently the best shooting and most accurate AEG out of the box)

    it sounds like perhaps the inner barrel is slightly off - perhaps worth stripping down the front section, and have a look at how its held in [grub screw and tension usually, but watch for the hop up rubber, which is generally considered to be not as good as Tokyo Marui's rubber], or the outer barrel might need tightening [mine loosnes occasionally].
    i take it youre shooting at very short distances within your flat.
    alot of people, if they want better accuracy out fo their AEG's are aware that the standard innder barrel for AEG's is considered a bit wide - so the bb kind of lops aroun din the barrel before it gets out, decreasing accuracy in general anyway.
    alot of people then swap the standard inner barrel for whats called a tightbore barrel which keeps the bb on a more direct course.
    tighter barrels also increase the velocity by a fraction as well.
    longer barrels are also another way of increasing accuracy but at a much reduced fraction compared to other options.

    best thing is to speak to someone who deals with the ins and outs of airsoft guns to see what [and how much!] your options are.
    [i generally do not tinker inside my weaopns so i'm not the best to ask]

    As for power, bear in mind that CA and alot of other manufacturers outside of Japan generally tool up for the foreign markets.
    While TM always had to cater for a market where the playable guideline in the field was power equal or below 1J, and often the rating was a conservative 0.8J.
    There is a 1J law in effect in the UK and in a few other coutnries [US is a bit freer but i state dependant] tha means that these guns, when they were originally at their factory-produced power ratings, would have been illegal to use on the fields.
    CA, and a few others subsequently modified their production power ratings and lowered the power of their guns. to be able to be more appealing ot other markets outside of China and SEA in general.

    as for weight vs realism, thats a common misconception that if it is made of metal, it will be the same weight as the real thing. the massive internal differences means that the airsoft gun is alot heavier than its real life equivalent.
    TM's guns are plastic because the law in Japan says that replica guns cannot be produced in full metal.

    TOP's M60 and M249 were generally half the weight of the real deal.

    Star/Guarder's replica of the Troy Industries SOPMOD M14 is actually spot on, at 11pounds / 5kg's, which gnerally means it weighs a ton, and thats without accessories.

    This is why Systema's PTW presnal training weapon is not made out of real metal - it cant be as it would exceed the real weapons weight without any benefits of the real weapons strength in its materials.
