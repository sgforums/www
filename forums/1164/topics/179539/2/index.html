    28 Feb 06, 17:48
    Yeah, its the Systema PTW - Professional Training Weapon.

    Systema initially supplied LE units in the US for training, and once that was complete they sold commercially.

    The first few batches exhibited quite a few problems.
    Subsequent production [which was rumoured to have been canned due to poor sales] yielded improved products and now they are quite well regarded.

    Construction is made with Die-cast aluminium, which overall means weight is identical to the real steel.
    All dimensions are identical to real steel.
    (Airsoft AEG's traditionally had slightly incorrect measurements - partially because the gearbox and motors were wider than a real steel handgrip and receiver, amongst other reasons)
    Any plastic used on this is fibre reinforced meaning its much stronger than any equivalent Airsoft plastic/poly material.

    The differences to normal Airsoft is that its functionality and detail is closer to the real weapon.

    Although this is still an AEG, it stops firing after last round leaves the barrel, and the PTW is the only AEG that always cycle the gearbox when you pull the trigger (means you cannot 'just tap' the triger to let off a round or prep a round liek in Airsoft. You hit the trigger, rifle will fire, meaning you must always observe proper firearm safety procedure).

    The magazines used are real steel dimensions [Airsoft magazines are traditionally shorter]. Each Systema magazine has a follower that triggers a switch in the body to cut off power to the gearbox whenever the magazine is empty.
    Because of the follower design, it doesn't matter if you load 5 rounds or 50 rounds into the magazine (which holds 120rds - but you can low-load the magazines), the gun will still stop firing after you clear the mag.

    Instructors can choose to pre-load only 30 rounds of BBs into each magazine to simulate the actual weapon's capacity, and together with realistic rate of fire it even mimics how fast one might run out of rounds and needed to reload in a stressed environment.
    Beyond that, once you've inserted a fresh magazine you will also have to hit the bolt-release button (which internally resets the circuitry) to ready the weapon - again just like the real-steel.

    The sound is very different to an ordinary AEG - you cannot hear the gearbox 'turning over' to complete its cycle before firing a round, which you normally can in ordinary Airsoft AEG's.

    Realism breaks with the PTW when you field strip the weapon or pull the charging handle.
    The gearbox is a new improved split case design that allows the upper part to separate away with the upper receiver. This allows quick change of the spring unit, abit like how ICS and the newer Star AEG's offer quick change solutions.
    The charging handle does its job, but only has a small amount of movement to do its job.
    Hop-up can only be done by field stripping the weapon so you have access to a screw set in the upper receiver. This is one of the drawbacks of the PTW.
    I believe the FPS is adjustable - you can order different types of piston/spring units, but they come as complete units and not easily modifiable yourself.

    The other, main, drawback of the PTW is price. Because of what they are, and who theyre normally aimed at, their per unit cost is about US$1000.

    PTW's are currently available as M16-A2, A3, and M4-A1 (with Crane stock for improved battery performance), and can be bought from any major Airsoft retailer.

    Systema are currently developing SMG (MP5's) and handgun versions of their PTW system.

    hope that helps!
