    Msn at nehpyh@hotmail.com if you are interested to meet. Location is not an issue for me.

    I have the RAT3 (D2 steel) by Ontario because it is cheaper than RAT. However, if given a choice again, I would have gone for original RAT. It's not that Ontario is inferior - just that the handle is a tat short even for asian size (I'm 1.73m). Being short handle is good for utility but suffers a bit if you ever need it as weapon but whatever superior being out there forbids it. But I hope you get my drift.

     

    As for edge holding, I have not truely trashed it during my reservists but being D2, good but slight more to the brittle side by 0.000002% :p so take care when hammering it with a sledge hammer. Should perform fine for any other chores.

    I'm not sure if if you already had it but if you are getting your first or last 3" fixed blade, get the Fallkniven F1. Period. No other current fixed blade can win that. It'll last a lifetime and frankly you can frame up the rest of your other 3".

    Hi Lazy Sam,


    I dun mean to sound like your school principle but baliisong is not a 'knife'. It's a fancy toy that happens to have a sharp edge. A dangerous toy for adult.

     

    And if you are serious with your reputation and outdoor activities, you wouldn't want to bring a ballisong to camps. Peeps will laugh their heads off.

     

    For outdoor and survival activities, you need something sturdy with minimal mechanism cos you dun wish things to break while you are trying to cut chicken, chopping firewood, or releasing someone from harnesses gone wrong.

    For your case, I strongly recommend a simple and affordable Swiss Army Knife a.k.a. SAK.

     

    I'm not sure if you can find the model but you can go to Marina Square and there is this sport shop near GV and they have some SAK. Can bargain for a good price. Get something close to the link above.

    What is good about the one above is that it has most utility tools you need for camp. It's hi-grade stainless so you dun have to worry about rust. Also, it has thumb-assist so that you can open the blade with one hand.

    BUT DUN GO AROUND BRANDISHING THIS, I WARN YOU!

    Also to note, it's not how great or expensive or big your knife is....it's how you use it safely, responsibilty and learning the skill to care for it.

    Try to sharpen your kitchen knife until you can shave your leg hair and you are there. :p

    Something is wrong with the SAK link, but if you google One-Hand Trekker German Army you should be able to find it.

     

    For online purchase/ imports:

    Do refer to earlier posts on importing knives. But basically, it is possible except for knives which are considered contraband like ballisong, stiletto, double-edged cutlery, and mechnical assisted knives. Be careful and read up from Singapore Police Force website before you go retail therapy.

    If you need fixed blade knives, try Shears Technology http://www.sheares.com/ They are not cheap cos they need to make a living but generally you can get decent knives from them saving you the hassle of internet, credit card, ICA, and CID. They are of course helpful.

    There is a Rambo in everyone of us (similar to there is a Beng in everyone of us...), try to refrain yourself from getting any blade larger than 7". The bigger the knife, the less utility it becomes and more silly you look. Imagine sitting around camp with something sticking up your **&^@#$....
