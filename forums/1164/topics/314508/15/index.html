    Well for the benefit of those who're getting into this hobby of collecting knives for display or functional uses such as outdoor sports/overseas camping/ jungle trekking. Perhaps this might help you out. 

    Like a few, still new to this thread and forum, i have found the requirement of such knives for my overseas trips/treks, and sought information as to how to legally accquire them, and have across the span of this year now owned a few knives, which i've purchased via amazon. 

    I respectively contacted ICA, and SPF's arms and explosives licencing department, it takes a little while to get the answers you are looking for, because for one, the customer service call operating officer isnt' exactly very knowledgable regarding the info, and will often redirect you to his/her supervisor in charge who will either direct you from ICA line to SPF, or the other way around. So i just emailed my queries and it got forwarded to the right department and here's my reply. (below in diff font)

    As of now, i own 2 Gerber LMF IIs (infantry black), Bear Grylls Ultimate Survival Knife, 2 SOG Seal Pup Elite Serrated Tiger Stripe, and i'm eyeing the ESEE-5/6 by R.A.T. 

    The only time i had a huge delay in shipment was when i compounded my orders, namely a Gerber LMF II, Bear Grylls Ultimate Surival knife and a Gerber Prodigy Tanto, as i was ordering on behalf of friends who're joining me on the expedition. As such the bulk order got impounded by ICA for around a few days before it got delivered to me. I used Vpost, so i need not go down to cantonment road or whatever. Amazon usa to VpostUSA, VpostUSA to singapore, and that's where the inspection held it and delayed before i finally got my package. 

     

    I genuinely hope this helps.... this whole rough idea how the process goes.... so you'd know what to do! 

     

    cheers!

    And for privacy's sake I've removed my name and the officer's extension, though the gist of the message should be clear. 

    Dear ---------,

     

    Single purchase of survival knife for camping trips or diving knife can be approved but please be advised not to import knives with blades more than 30cm in length.


    Generally, import of survival knives will not require a permit but may still be subject to our inspection upon importation prior to the release of the item to the owner.  

     

    If item is shipped in, it would be detained by ICA officers and forward to us for inspection. Once approved, we shall contact yourself for collection.

     

    Please take note that Flick Knives are not allowed in Singapore but manual foldable knives are permitted.

     

    Regards,

    Han Teck Wee 
    Compliance Management Officer
    Arms & Explosive
    Police Licensing Division

    Shipping costs is dependant on how you wanna ship it, as amazon has limited shipping internationally for items, but predominantly ships to all of usa, i use Vpost to help ship from USA to Singapore. But within USA, as in from Amazon to the Vpost address in USA, that's another shipping point. 

    As for the amazon shipping within USA, prices vary on the speed of delivery, eg, 1day shipping, 2 day shipping, 3-5 day shipping or 5-8 days, you can check it up on Amazon, i know 5-8 tends to be free with purchases over 100 USD i think... but i stick often with 2 day shipping for around 20 plus USD. 

    When the parcel gets to VpostUSA, it will be weighed and sized up, before delivery price is stated, after payment only which will it then be delivered to SG. So pray that Amazon keeps the box small, and pray that the weight of your item is light. 

    So all in all, it's basically 2 couriers, one from amazon(whichever courier they use, fedex/USPS, etc) and Singpost(Vpost USA).

    If you however manage to buy from other sites which offer international shipping, then you'd save yourself alot of trouble, problem is prices tend to be very much more expensive with such sellers unless you know where to look, and customer satisfaction/gurantee of item's delivery and quality may not always necessarily be there. Eg, problems with goods and filing of claims will be a hassle as opposed to approaching larger corporations with such problems.
