    27 Jan 12, 21:39

    It's true, basically anything with a pointy edge can be used to stab, I got stabbed with a pen before (don't ask how, lol).

    Um, but basically the classic western tanto design (there are modified tanto designs) has a very strong piercing/penetration ability than many other blade shapes.

    In terms of combat usage, if you need to stab through thick protection, say kevlar. Then yes, personally I prefer my tanto-shaped blades because they do their job well and the thickness of the blade helps it maintain its strength during knife-fights.

    However, that is not to say other knife shapes aren't 'combat-ready', there are many combat knives that have a good combination of slicing ability and chopping ability, but do not fare that well in stabbing. Different uses, each design has its pros and cons and it really boils down to preference and situation.

    Haha, I really don't know how to explain it any simpler other than that double edge knives have 2 sharpened edges. Very common on daggers, bayonets, relatively straight knives (example: http://www.amazon.com/Elk-Ridge-Double-Hollow-ground/dp/B0044EGYV2/ref=sr_1_3?s=sporting-goods&ie=UTF8&qid=1338130622&sr=1-3 ), but I'm guessing that its not very common on a highly curved blade because honestly in my rational and personal opinion, it doesn't make sense to put 2 edges on a high-curve blade. That is not to say they don't exist though, sometimes you might get a full length sharpened edge on one side of the knife and a short sharpened edged on the other side. Or a plain edge on one side and a serrated edge on the other side. Or even comibnations. Examples: http://www.nauticexpo.com/prod/tigullio-dive/double-edged-diving-knives-26532-151281.html and http://216.38.63.83/products/index.php?id=1766 .
