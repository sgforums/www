    Barrel is shorter...and the muzzle velocity is lower. Aiming a carbine requires a bit of practice, since its shorter, and the barrel tends to climb a bit when shooting. The audible "crack" is much louder and sharper with the carbine, whereas the M16 goes "blam"...

    Of cos'....its easier to clear SOC with the carbine. Its a good compact weapon, meant for spec ops, scouts, cdos, armour crewmen, and anyone that requires an assault rifle sans length and weight.

    Originally posted by one-niner:
    ermm a rifle newbie..is the SAF carbine used by the scouts, tankees also known as the M4 or just a M16 carbine ?
    Nope - the M4 incorporates the key developments that came with the M16A2, namely a barrel with one-turn-in-7-inches rifling (as opposed to one turn in 12 for the M16A1), and the M855 round to go with the new rifling. The new rifling spins the projectile faster, making for higher accuracy.

    The carbines used by scouts etc. were the older models with the 1:12 rifling and the M193 round.
