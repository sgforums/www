Men who opt for neither are sent to open prison where they may work and study for a period of six months.

    Germany
    Germany has a mandatory military service of nine months for men. Women may volunteer and are allowed to perform the same jobs as men.     Clarencebk, I'm ex-Arty so here's my comment to your post; I dunno how utilitarian it was to other Formations, or if it's still relevant today, or whether it was different in the 1970's. But from my own experience, I've always interpreted that everything - inc. 2 or 2.5 years' conscription - is oriented around a feasible training period for operational status, which is (was?) chronologically 1.5 years for an Arty unit. That's the period when soldiers of various vocations would come together to work as an integrated whole.

    Deductively, it also meant that, as they initially assemble:

    - the gunners were 6-month-old soldiers, after 3 months mono-intake BMT & 3 months vocational training.

    - the Arty NCOs were 9-month-old soldiers, after 3 months Tekong BMT, 3 months section-leader course & 3 months vocational training (when the gunners were concurrently doing BMT). They would've already been posted in to train together with the gunners during the latter's own vocational training.

    - the Arty officers were 12-month-old soldiers, after 3 months Tekong BMT & 9 months OCS course.

    - the other non-NCO appointments (TAs, signallers, etc.) were also
    6-month-old soldiers, after 3 months Tekong BMT & 3 months vocational training.

    Of course, there's also "auxiliary" troops like drivers-medics-storemen-clerks, as well as regular officers/NCOs & both disrupters & makeupees, whose inprocessing dates can further complicate the status quo, but all in all the operational main body is formed around the 4 sub-groups above. The old "problem" was that, during my time in the mid-'80s, more & more of Group 4 - including myself - were A-level educated & thus, with an extra half-year to serve, didn't ROD with the battalion. The unit HQ didn't have room for all of us, so we got attached to anyone who wanted us: Training Cadre, other Arty units, overseas exercises, even just the COS-needy!

    But all of us did serve 1.5 years together, regardless of our subsequent fates as reservists. if the SAF is still using this chronological "formula", then IMHO they will have to make some major adjustments to the length of vocational-training & possibly leadership courses as well, so that they can get the timing down on the bulk personnel arriving to train for operational status.

    It's an entirely separate (logistical) matter to just possessing new-technology weapons, even as the latter can indirectly aid the efficiency of the former.

    Curious: Do the other non-Arty combat formations time their forming up & training schedule in the same way?

    P.S. The last 6 months of the said 1.5 years was kinda "lobo" for my unit; we'd passed our proficiency test for the 2nd time &, apparently, inherited the role of live-firing resource for any foot unit that wanted some realistic toppings during their SAFTI field exercises. But I remember we ultimately spent 2-3 of them months converting to our reservist artillery weapon too, so, no, it wasn't a complete waste of time.

    Me & my kakis used to wonder, as our ICT stints wounded down, whether the then-newly implemented 5-month BMT (or whatever) would've forced significant adjustments to training timetables for NS operational status.

    Until informed otherwise, I'm assuming this status quo hadn't changed that much since my own time in the 2nd half of the '80s - & that, even with the 2-year blanket conscription, the SAF continues to adhere that an NSF unit must train together for X years before they enter the reserves.

    I'm speaking more from the Arty viewpoint, but my feeling is it shouldn't negatively affect the individual training for NCOs & other ranks (BMT, vocational plus relevant specialist courses) all that much - the new technology can, on paper, save back up to a fortnight's duration in attendance. Of course, it'll also resolve the non-NCO, 2.5-year NS "displacement" for good.

    But I foresee the present 9th-month OCS term as a possibly major obstacle to adjust around; my knowledge is the cadets' schedules have always been jam-packed, so how much time is realistically saveable so that they can be posted out earlier if possible? Presumably, they're from the same Tekong intake as the Arty NCOs but - unlike the latter - they're comparatively 3 months behind in arriving to work with the gunners. Realistically, will they be mature enough to form chemistry &/or lead within a 2-year NS window, with or without a shortened OCS course?

    The other probable handicap, for me, is if the NSF operational unit must switch to another system (weapon, apparatus or technology) before they ORD. If its training calendar's already reduced by 3 months from, say, 1.5 to 1.25 years to reflect the 2-year conscription, then having to additionally schedule in a conversion timetable would cut the original training cycle further, to perhaps just 1 year (for argument's sake). IMHO That'll be unfortunate; technical proficiency is one thing, but unit cooperation needs more time to gel simply cuz it's human-oriented.
