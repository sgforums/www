    Malaysia rejigs national anthem to boost patriotism
    KUALA LUMPUR -- Worried the national anthem has lost its patriotic punch, Malaysia is revamping the somber, decades-old tune with new lyrics and a more spirited beat, an official said on Wednesday.

    Most Malaysians can recite the anthem by heart as it is sung during daily assemblies at many schools. However officials say young people no longer fully appreciate its lyrics, which include references to "the land where my blood flows", "our king safely crowned" and "citizens living united and progressive".

    Advertisement

    The proposed changes to the anthem, called "Negaraku" or "My Country," would be the most dramatic to the Malay-language piece since Malaysians began singing it when the country achieved independence from Britain in 1957.

    The anthem's title would remain unchanged but its opening line is expected to be changed from "My country" to "My Malaysia," said Mr Wah Idris, a prominent composer whom the government chose to develop the revisions.

    The changes -- which are still subject to approval by Prime Minister Mahathir Mohamad's Cabinet -- are likely to include a more rousing rhythm but no adjustments to the melody, which is based on a traditional Malay folk song, Mr Wah told The Associated Press.

    The remodeled version will be officially launched during National Day celebrations on Aug 31, the Utusan Malaysia newspaper reported, adding that the changes were "meant to inject a deeper love for the country".

    Officials have tried to update the anthem once before -- in 1992 -- to make it more upbeat. but the revamped version was criticised for sounding too much like a pop song, and the more stately version eventually returned to favour. -- AP

    Studying the lyrics of our national anthem, it's basically an exhortation for the people to unite for the common good. To be really honest, the meaning while noble doesn't inspire passion that comes from a rooted sense of identity and destiny. Below is the english translation:

    Come, fellow Singaporeans

    Let us progress towards happiness together

    May our noble aspiration bring

    Singapore success

    Come, let us unite

    In a new spirit

    Let our voices soar as one

    Onward Singapore

    Onward Singapore

    Please pardon me as I've not made a study of other anthems other than the star spangled banner so my comments are biased. Produced below is the first stanza of the star spangled banner"

    'Oh, say, can you see, by the dawn's early light,
    What so proudly we hail'd at the twilight's last gleaming?
    Whose broad stripes and bright stars, thro' the perilous fight,
    O'er the ramparts we watch'd, were so gallantly streaming?
    And the rockets' red glare, the bombs bursting in air,
    Gave proof thro' the night that our flag was still there.
    O say, does that star-spangled banner yet wave
    O'er the land of the free and the home of the brave?'

    Even at a superficial reading, you'll likely note that the song already speaks of a tumultous history (during the revolution against the brits). It gives people a sense of destiny when you remind them of how the country overcame incredible odds in the past. It also speaks of the best in its people as 'brave' and remind them to always cherish liberty.

    Having said that, i must say that 'Majulah Singapura' is my national anthem, and whenever its being played, i do feel tugs of patriotic feelings in my heart, in spite of the plain lyrics.
