    More on what Teo said

    "We are inside the range rings of more and more countries that have missiles of various kinds, so we have to take that into account when we design our future air defence systems -- our radars, our missiles, our air defence fighters," Teo Chee Hean said in an interview with the Straits Times.

    "We don't expect that they will be a direct threat to us. But the fact of the matter is that we are now within their range rings when, prior to that, we were not," he said without naming any countries.

    "So this is something which, if we're prudent, we will take into account."

    "At a higher level, what we see as changes to the security landscape are things like the proliferation of weapons of mass destruction, the proliferation of missiles with longer ranges -- ballistic missiles, cruise missiles," Teo said.

    Source: Agence France-Presse

    SINGAPORE, July 31, 2006 (AFP) - North Korean Foreign Minister
    Paek Nam-Sun will hold talks in Singapore on Tuesday, an official
    of the Stalinist country's embassy told AFP.

    The two sides will hold discussions on "bilateral cooperation,"
    said the official who hung up when asked for his name.

    Asked whether there would also be any mention of stalled
    multilateral talks over North Korea's nuclear program, the
    official said, "I don't think so."

    Paek's visit comes at the invitation of Foreign Minister George
    Yeo and will last until Thursday, Singapore's foreign ministry
    said in a statement Monday night.

    "This will be the first official visit by Mr Paek in his
    capacity as foreign minister," it said.

    Paek will meet with Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong, Senior
    Minister Goh Chok Tong as well as Yeo, it said.

    Analysts have said North Korea managed to alienate even its few
    friends after a defiant showing at Asian security talks in Kuala
    Lumpur last week where it snubbed a campaign to rejoin the
    stalled talks on its nuclear efforts.

    Pyongyang's main ally China led a campaign to breath new life
    into the stalled six-nation nuclear talks on the sidelines of the
    Malaysian meeting, known as the ASEAN Regional Forum.

    But despite the concerted push, for which it joined hands with
    South Korea which also favours a softly-softly approach towards
    its neighbour, North Korea was unmoved.

    US Secretary of State Condoleezza Rice on Friday also urged
    Pyongyang to rejoin the negotiations and said the United States
    was ready "at any time, at any place and without any conditions"
    to meet North Korea under a six-nation framework that began three
    years ago.

    But North Korea, which left the multilateral talks in November
    and caused further outrage this month when it test fired seven
    missiles, said it would not return until United States financial
    sanctions against it were dropped.

    North Korea said it was also considering withdrawing from the
    ASEAN Regional Forum, one of the few diplomatic gatherings it
    attends.

    The regime last week branded Rice an "imbecile" and rejected
    United Nations Security Council condemnation of its missile
    tests, saying they were part of its legitimate right to
    self-defence.

    Earlier this month Singapore appealed to North Korea not to
    repeat its "provocative" missile tests and called on the
    Stalinist state to return to multilateral talks.
