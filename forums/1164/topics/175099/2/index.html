    the sniper works with his partner, the spotter. the spotter assists the sniper in spotting the target, helps to calculate the direction of wind, the speed of wind, the range of target, angle of slant of the rifle, etc. the sniper then adjust the crosshair to compensate for the differences.

    snipers also do not usually use silencers. the silencers are used only in close range. putting a silencer on the sniper rifle will defeat its purpose, as sniper rifles are made to fire from long ranges. when the target is killed, the sniper and spotter will escape in the confusion. even if the enemy fires back into their direction, their normal rifle bullets are out of range to hit the sniper.
