    SINGAPORE -- A Singapore anti-submarine ship collided with a merchant vessel off Malaysia's southern coast near the disputed Pedra Branca, late on Friday, injuring eight people. Four others -- all women -- were still trapped in the damaged area of the warship on Saturday, the Singapore government said.

    Efforts were still ongoing to free the four women, who were sleeping in the back part of the ship at the time of the collision, the Ministry of Defence said.

    'They were in the sleeping quarters for female crew members at the time of the collision. The sleeping quarters are located in the aft of the ship where the most severe damage was sustained,' said Ministry of Defence spokesman Valerie Yeo.

    Eight people from the ship suffered minor injuries, and three of them were taken to Changi General Hospital for observation, the ministry said.

    The accident between the RSS Courageous and the ANL Indonesia, a container vessel headed for South Korea, occurred minutes before midnight off Pedra Branca, an islet claimed by both Singapore and Malaysia, the ministry added.

    A source said that the RSS Courageous was hit on its side and that one of the four trapped women was unconscious.

    'Navy divers assisted by the police coast guard are out there trying to free them. The right side of the ship is severely damaged,' said the source, adding that the accident occurred about 1.8 km north of Pedra Branca.

    The ministry did not say if the ship was in danger of sinking or whether it was in Singapore territorial waters.

    The 455-tonne RSS Courageous, commissioned in 1996, is one of six sub hunters in the Republic of Singapore Navy fleet. -- AP
