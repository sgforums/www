    Too much of a good thing is never good.

    The basis of our defence diplomacy is "my Enemy's enemy is a friend". This tied to our need for defence technology, training areas and ready markets for our products has made us travel far and wide, we have strange bed fellows as friends.

    The obvious friends in need: Brunei, Indonesia and Taiwan provide for all these last two 'needs'. On a more limited role is Thailand for training and limited markets. India and Australia provide training space. Whilst USA, Sweden, Germany(?), UK and France provide technology and training.

    Israel provides technology solely (obvious). Special mention is Myanmar which is buyer of some of our products. All these partners we keep 'sweet' but silent.

    So what's the flip side?

    What happens when an international issue has some friends on one side and others on the opposite side? Singapore's diplomats will have a ball of a time to keep the sides separate and walk the thin line between.

    We have been very successful by being netural and being an umpire for issues which we ourselves have little/no stake (i.e. as chair for discussions on G8 agri policies etc).

    How then? "Walk the walk", "talk the talk", Carry a big stick and hope it never comes to blows.
