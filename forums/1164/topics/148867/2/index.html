    The ASTROS II launcher is capable of firing rockets of different calibers armed with a range of warheads.

    The SS-30 rocket, caliber 127mm, can be loaded with 32 rounds per launcher and has a range between 9km and 30km.

    The SS-40 rocket, caliber 180mm, can be loaded with 16 rounds per launcher and has a range between 15km and 35km.

    The SS-60/SS-80 rocket, caliber 300mm, can be loaded with 4 rounds per launcher and has a range between 20km and 80km.
    In my uneducated opinion, the howitzer gun and the MLRS are not meant to replace each other. They may both do the same job, but they do it very differently.

    In terms of range, you can see the the bigger MLRS rockets have big range advantage over the howitzer.

    (Of course, arty is more accurate.)

    But the reason why the Germans in WW2 feared the Soviet rockets - called Stalin's Organs - is not range.

    It's the sheer firepower of a rocket barrage.

    One ASTROS MLRS launcher can fire off all 16 SS-40 180mm rockets within (estimated) 6 seconds.

    So 10 MLRS launchers can impact a target with 160 rockets within 6 seconds!

    10 155mm howitzer with 3-round burst capability can deliver only 30 rounds in the first 6 seconds.

    The initial 6-second barrage will catch most people before they can take cover.

    The MLRS will not have to reload and can move away to safety immediately after firing to escape counter fire or air attacks.

    However, the howitzer is capable of accurate, sustained fire on an objective whereas the MLRS is more suited for one-time-only saturation of an area. This is because a MLRS takes forever to reload.

    The Malaysians have the MLRS as it is a good weapon for destroying our airbases. I don't think they'll waste the expensive rockets on our HDB estates. The Malaysians may go to war with us but they are not terrorists.

    I don't think SG need MLRS. What we need to figure out is an effective way of countering MLRS rockets after they are fired towards SG.
