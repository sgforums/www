    Kenhor,

    I do not think you understand the concept of MAD. It only applies if in a conflict both sides are 100% assured of destroying each other. That is why the superpowers built enough nuclear weapons during the cold war to vaporize each other literally hundreds of times over -- so that no matter how many were disabled or destroyed by enemy action, enough capability would remain to assuredly destroy the other party in return. That is why it is Mutual Assured Destruction.

    We do not have a 100% chance of completely destroying Malaysia. We have a 0% chance of doing that. I think you overestimate our capabilities. We cannot have a strategy that relies on MAD. Comparing our situation to the standoff between NATO and WP is ludicrous. In my opinion, the whole "poisoned shrimp" idea was retarded from the beginning, but it was the best that we could do at the time. Why would an aggressor nation be deterred by the threat of injury if he could inflict death upon us in return?

    No, we must seek victory in war if we wish to deter the enemy. An aggressor must realize that he is likely to LOSE a war with us. That is the surest and most effective form of deterrence. The only war for us to defeat the enemy, and therefore deter him and prevent war is a forward defence doctrine. It is the most humane thing to do. If the temptation of war is removed from potential aggressors, then everyone will live in peace.

    As for the intimidating effect of our forward defence doctrine, I have three things to say:

    Firstly, our neighbors should and do realize that because of the conscript nature of our army, we can never be a force of aggression. We cannot annex another country. We cannot permanently capture and hold ground. We are not trained for counterinsurgency. Our neighbors only sometimes pretend to forget this for domestic political reasons. Regardless of how strong the SAF becomes, she can only be a defensive force. We cannot and therefore will not invade.

    Secondly, the SAF specifically lacks deep strategic strike capability. We do not threaten our neighbors with ballistic or cruise missiles. We have no strategic heavy bombers. We lack sea control platforms like cruisers and aircraft carriers. This means that the SAF is not a force of intimidation. We cannot use the SAF as a stick to make our neighbors do what we want.

    Thirdly, our neighbors are not extending the same courtesy to us that you suggest we extend to them. They threaten us with force or deprivation of water. They parade their military hardware. They try to make us feel insecure. We do none of these things, even to the point of keeping our military strength out of the public eye.
