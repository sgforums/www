    Originally posted by |-|05|:
    the SM1 looks slanted backwards because of it's turrent.....the whole thing moves when the gun needs to be elevated...not just the gun...
    The correct technical term is because the turrent is an oscillating turrent. Imagine two rather uneven shaped sauces placed rim to rim. Instead of a system of hydralic struts to raise or lower the gun, the two halves raise, lower and oscillate to raise or lower the gun accordingly.

    The gun is fed by automatic rotating magazine system with 6 rounds each, much like those of a pistol, the spent shell casings are ejected out of the rear of the turret.

    Pretty neat solutions to make it compact and light weight.

    Before any of you chumps shoot off your gob about being too light etc, consider this in any tank to tank battle, its mostly a hide, shoot, scoot and shoot from another hide position. Not the slug fest all small boys imagine tank battles are.

    Even the M1A2 is capable of scooting between hides at breakneck speeds.
