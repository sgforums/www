    Most pictures of the Ultimax that I have seen being used by foreign armies and mercenaries featured the 100-round drum.

    These people wouldn't be buying the Ultimax-100 only to use it as a Ultimax-30.

    Though I, too, had my doubts about the drum, I can only assume that it works well enough, for people like the Indos to have bought quantities of it.

    Interesting thing about belted 5.56 ammo. The AFP (Philippines Armed Forces) bought Minimis over the Ultimax and then found that belted 5.56 ammo was either too expensive or scarce since the Philippines didn't produce them. And 2 people on the Timawa site reported that they preferred the Ultimax over the Minimi as the Ultimax was lighter and more accurate, and tons cheaper. But most of all, it didn't require the production of belted ammo.

    But drums vs belt is a debate requiring its own thread and it's been done many times and on this forum, too.

    I agree with Dave's comment about hand-loading the drum but as someone pointed out, you can hand load up to 50rds or something.

    I'm sure Sullivan considered all the different drum systems before deciding on the current Ultimax system. No drum system had worked well so far.

    The Russians traditionally favoured drums but even they had given it up. They had drums for their PPD and PPsh-41 SMGs. They had drums for their RPK (SAW AK-47). The 75-rd drums were "heavy, expensive and hard to load" according to Modern Firearms. They were loaded the traditional way from the lip.

    THe RPD and German MG-34 snail drums aren't really drums as they nothing more than a container for the belt ammo.

    AS far as I know, the Germans never used drum mags except for one on the MP-18 SMG during the WW2.

    I also remember seeing pics of a HK LMG with a big capacity box mag with a transparent back.

    Now only the PLA (QBZ-95 LMG) and SAF (Ultimax100) operate drums as far as I can recall offhand.

    The US military had experience with 1 drum system in the Thompson SMG and was not satisfied with the Thompson drum. They also used the now-dead "pan" magazine in the Lewis Gun.

    So it will be a real surprise if the US military does decide on the Ultimax. It is often very hard to change deeply entrenched perceptions as we all know too well. Even though it was designed by Jim Sullivan.

    Will the Ultimax be phased out was a question I asked before. One of its main advantage of sharing mags with the rifleman is gone out the window with the STUPID decision to adopt non-M16 mags for the SAF SAR-21.

    Anyone who says a better M16 mag can't be made should realise that our old-fashioned SAF aluminium mags aren't used by most countries still using STANAG mags. HK have made stainless steel ones. Diemaco have their own etc etc.
