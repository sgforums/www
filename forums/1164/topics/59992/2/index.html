    Dr Goh Keng Swee's preamble when introducing the NS Bill in Parliament in 1967 stated:

    "I consider it to be wrong to believe that there is nothing we can or should do about defending ourselves, even though we cannot achieve complete invulnerability by our unaided efforts.".

    This, I believe, aptly sums up the intent of introducing national service when the Act was passed in 1967. The act, and by extension NS itself, was introduced with the express purpose of defending Singapore. Note that unlike the Army or People's Defence Force Acts of 1965, this pertains directly to conscription, and I think it is quite clear that conscription was introduced in Singapore with the intent of defence of Singaporean territory, not for overseas deployments.
