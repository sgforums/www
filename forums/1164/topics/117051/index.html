    New maritime assault capability to fight terror in Singapore's waters
    02 February 2005 1540 hrs (SST)
    http://www.channelnewsasia.com/stories/singaporelocalnews/view/130413/1/.html

    SINGAPORE : The Singapore police has launched a new maritime assault capability aimed at fighting crime and terrorism in Singapore's territorial waters, especially with the high volime of traffic at sea.

    In a possible scenario, a high-speed sea chase with assault boats circle in on a hijacked ferry at the Eastern Anchorage.

    Special Tactics and Rescue or STAR officers then jump onboard to save hostages and arrest the hijackers.

    The STAR officers have been training over the past 4 years to expand their role from land to sea to develop this maritime assault capability aimed at dealing with criminals on the high seas here.

    And the biggest challenge when making the transition was the sea itself.

    The Commanding Officer of the Special Tactics and Rescue (STAR) Unit, Arthur Law said, "Getting used to sea-sickness. a few of our guys didn't adapt operating at sea, we had a few cases where they vomited during the initial phases of our training, but after some time, they got used to it with determination."

    The officers trained with their counterparts in the US, UK and Hong Kong on the various aspects of maritime operations, from assault diving, vessel boarding techniques and close confrontations on vessels, to prepare for any situation.

    "It's not an entirely new role; we have our brothers in the Police Coast Guard and Navy, but the STAR Unit's primary responsibility will be in Singapore's territorial waters. I see this enhanced capability as giving the STAR Unit more confidence in managing any situation - whether dealing with hostages, piracy or terrorist-related." said Senior Minister of State for Home Affairs and Law, Assoc Prof Ho Peng Kee.

    This will add yet another level of security to keep the busy sea-lanes safe. - CNA
