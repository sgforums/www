    I'm not quite sure what you mean by this - are you saying that we would have two castes, one being the traders and the other being the warriors? In such a case, who would sit higher in the caste system?

    One key issue to be considered here is that Singapore is a nation of migrants. This has two implications. First, there is the question of whether Singaporeans will continue to be migrants - this could be a real concern, as evidenced by the famous "quitters and stayers" episode.

    Second, being comprised of a mix of cultures, rather than being made of an indigenous people, would we expect everybody to band together? The one example that comes to mind is America. On one hand, they all joined in to fight in WW2. On the other hand, look at the backlash in Vietnam.

    One difference between the two wars was the assumptions of the prevailing culture. In Band of Brothers, one of the interviewees (I can't remember who it was right now) said, "In my town, there were four guys who they killed themselves because they were 4F - it was a different time". In Vietnam, burning of draft cards was almost the thing to do. The other key difference is, of course, Pearl Harbour - the people responded to being attacked. In Vietnam, the situation was so far removed from the people America's participation in the war was seen as pointless.

    So as I see it, the questions for Singapore would be:

    a) does the culture provide the people with drive to get into the fight, in the right circumstances and;

    b) what are those right circumstances - could we be pulled into an offshore war that serves our strategic interests (perhaps another Domino Theory scenario)?
