    The M113 is not a wonder-vehicle, no matter what its strongest supporter on the web Mike Sparks says. Its armor protection is very weak, compared to modern IFVs like Bionix. If you uparmor it to the same level as the Bionix, it will be underpowered instead. The reason why the armor did well in Vietnam was because the B-70 RPG warheads the enemy used had a high dud rate. The aluminium chassis and armor of the M113 can also burn under the right conditions, releasing toxic fumes. There have been incidents where M113s (and older aluminium Bradleys) have had the whole chassis burned away, leaving only the melted steel tracks, engine and chaingun behind.

    At least newer M113s have the fuel tanks on the outside rather than inside -- the old M113s often ignited their crews when penetrated. Many American AIs in Vietnam rode on top of the M113 instead of inside for this reason, even though this exposed them to fire. Better to die of a gunshot wound than being burned alive. Are the Ultras modified in this way, like the M113A3?

    The M113 is definitely useful in "second-line" roles where they are unlikely to face enemy fire. They make good mortar carriers, medevac, command and even logistics vehicles. But if you are expecting to be shot at, you will definitely be better in a Bionix. The key is to use the right vehicle for the right situation. The Bionix is too heavy for some roles, the M113 too poorly protected for others, so just mix and match. I am glad to see the SAF is doing this.

    The problem with M113s nowadays is that there are other vehicles that are even better second-line vehicles. ST's Terrex and Bronco are two examples, and there are many foreign equivalents. The M113 used to be tough enough to be a front line vehicle, but the improvement in anti-tank weapons means its day is over.

That is the probable reason why there is such a delay in finding a replacement for the SM1 -- there may be no possible replacement at present.
