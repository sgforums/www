    A single MRLS vehicle launcher can fire a salvo of 12 to 16 "rocket" propelled explosive shells at one go.

    While a single artillery gun can only fire a salvo of 6 shots in one minute.

    Imagine the devastating power from a battery of MRLS, when compared to a battery of artillery guns.

    Is an artillery shell any more accurate than a "rocket" propelled explosive shell, when both are dependent on trajectory flight ?

    MRLS systems have been added into most armies, to work in tandem with artillery, and to provide saturation fire to suppress the enemy position, as well as to soften enemy objectives, or used as interdictory fire to block defined routes.

    It performs most of the tasks that traditional artillery does, and perform it with a higher degree of saturation fire - much as what "carpet bombing" is being done by bombers from the air, although at lower explosive yields.

    New munitions are added to make the MRLS even more deadlier, with cargo rounds also being added, as well as laser guided rocket propelled shells - with some of the shells having delayed fuse, and concrete busting effects.

    The present day MRLS is a far cry from the early "Stalin Organs" of WW2 vintage.
