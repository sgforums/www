    2 Feb 05, 00:11
    Hi Specops. I do agree with you to some extent that the British move to use the SA80 SAW variant was a bad idea, but I believe that the SA80 was an inherently flawed weapon to begin with. Even with extensive modifications, the stock SA80 performs much better today, but remains problematic and unreliable compared to other bullpup rifles.

    That said, the flaw of the SA80 SAW variant was not strictly due to its 30 round magazine, since the Ultimax itself is usually used with a 30 round mag as well. Many argue that the ultimax may be used with a 100 round mag, but neglect to mention that filling these 100 round mags require heavy machinery due to the powerful springs in the drums, which makes it difficult to do out in the field.

    I am not in support of the Ultimax being phased out. But then, it does have its drawbacks, with the drum issue being one of them. I admit the argument isn't straightforward tho. For one thing, one reasonable way round it is to modify the ultimax so that it can be belt-fed (easier said than done), but one has to admit there are certain advantages with a 100 round drum that a loose box with a belt of rounds won't have.

    My 2 cents worth.
