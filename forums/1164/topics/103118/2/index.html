    But, yes, there's such exploitable lobang with the present rank system for reservists (except, again, it won't be as a rote reward).

    I'm more of a traditionalist in preferring the old classification, which was basic but most sensible;

    CO/RSM -------------> OC/CSM -------> PC/PS -----------------> SL-HIC/2IC
    (MAJ/WO2-SSG) (CPT/SGT) (LTA-2LT/SGT-CPL) (CPL/LCP)

    IMO The current rank structure is too politician-influenced & rendered too complicated. For all its "more affirmative" & "visibly upgradable" rationale, I think it can create more bureaucratic pettiness for the military hierarchy. Eg. Soldier A, for all his accomplishments, can't be promoted to above the incumbent Soldier's B's rank without accompanying new job responsibilities.

    The post-'93 WOSE reforms that IMO concerned frontline reservists most were;

    (a) the WO, upon affirmed as the 4th "executive" commander (joining MAJ, CPT & subalterns), is now too senior for RSM postings. Its rank is now recognized as equivalent to CPT level.

    (b) the former NCO corps gained 5 separate ranks (MSG/SSG/1SG/2SG/3SG), & this is where reservists can get most advancements. A junior commander can definately make 2SG before he stands down; ditto 1SG for the more senior specialist KAHs. SSG-to-MSG isn't really a big deal; IMO it's more to offset against the new WO status, but what it does confirm is that the RSM now holds a Spec rank (albeit senior one).

    [Specialist-to-WO now requires attending a formal tri-service Joint-WO course, kinda the WOSE version of OCS, before getting promoted. Quite rare for reservists.]

    (c) enlisted men now gains the CPL & PFC along with the LCP. No rank-pulling allowed here; the hierarchy differentiation's more with between, say, HIC (head IC) & 2IC instead.

    Overall, if you think about it, the combat-unit appointments (see above) hadn't fundamentally changed, nor has the general partitioning of the rank & file:

    Officer corps -------> NCO/Specialist Corps ------> Enlisted men

    Regardless of specific rank, it still requires extraordinary circumstances for non-regulars to advance out of each scission.

    But there's no obstacle stopping Specs from being promoted within its "internal" grades, based on ICT performances & pragmatic unit needs. Ambitious reservists can maneuver themselves into filling in for missing incumbents &, if they continue to defer the next call-up too, then fight to be a permanent replacement.

    I have no problems either if the SAF elects to withhold the LTA promotions before ORD, & wait till after the 2nd ICT. The junior officers tend to mature more as NSmen than they did as NSFs anyway.

    Among enlisted men, CPLs have no business advancing to a leadership rank like 3SG as a peacetime "reward." &, for now, LCPs can't make CPL without the post-O Levels cert either. But, that's said there's nothing from stopping enlisted men from switching postings, vis-&-vis against unit needs. Combat-fit signallers & combat medics could all along be deployed to any Formation. Even for same-Formation troops, working with a 155mm howitzer is totally different from the Primus. Active-time PTIs (Specs, mind you) & MPs revert back to INF for reservist.

    What all these examples required is a short induction course, or at worst on-the-job training. That's how I became a mono-intake instructor, & how one Malay reservist colleague of mine successfully switched from driver to RP (after a few ICTs of persuading the MTO to release his services). But "other ranks" climbing to 3SG or CPL rank - forget it.

    What's your rank & vocation?
