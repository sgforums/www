    Nope - there are two VC-25A aircraft (SAM 28000 and 29000). Air Force One is only a callsign assigned to the aircraft the US president is on. If the S3B that Bush flew onto the Lincoln was not a Navy aircraft, it would have been assigned the callsign Air Force One as well.

    The old VC137 used for the job (SAM 27000 - the backup for 26000) changed callsigns in midflight. It took off as Air Force One when it was flying Nixon back to California following his resignation, but reverted to and landed as SAM 27000 in immediately after Gerald Ford was sworn in.
