    Beret colors depends on your vocation, unit and training.

    If you are engineer trained (dark blue beret), then posted to become pionner in Infantry unit, you become green beret. This is because pioneers are organic to an Infantry battalion. Like signallers (dark blue beret) who got posted to Gds unit, they must go through Gds conversion to become Khaki beret because they are organic to Gds battalion.

    Actually - Commandos and Guards berets are awarded to personel whom have successfully completed the respective courses.
    Hence, if a signaller (or other vocations) have completed the Guards conversion course successfully - he will have the Guards tab, and the Guards beret awarded for use.
    Members of the 7th SIB have an additional 7SIB cap badge backing on their berets - regardless of the colour of the beret (vocation).
    For the Commando and Guards beret/tab - as long as you remain qualified - the beret follows you - as it is an award, like any other qualification badges.
    I still have my original green Guards beret with the 7 SIB backing (used up to the mid 90s') and the current khaki beret without the 7 SIB backing, as I'm longer with 7 SIB.
