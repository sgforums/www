    28 Apr 05, 16:04
    Only problem with airsoft is that they all have a tendency to break down.

    The mechanism are usually too delicate to stand up to the impact of firing the weapon for long.

    When you buy these toys, make sure to go for Japanese models and not the Taiwanese ones.

    For pistols I had a Taiwanese-made 92-F (CO2) and it literally fell apart after a year or so. Right from the start it needed frequent tuning to the firing pin and replacement parts to the magazine gas valve.

    For long arm I had a MP5 Navy fixed butt stock replica with the recoil spring replaced for higher strength. This probably caused a lot of later problems.

    But this MP5 was accurate as hell up to a distance of 5m after I zeroed the sights. After 5m you have to compensate for drop.
