    Here are some of the battles, if changed, would have caused great impact and changes to our modern world. This is just some of the battles I have thought.

    (Please note I am not well versed in Asian histories, so for decisive battles for that region please feel free to add, same goes to those other battles that I have missed.)

    Ancient Age: 500 B.C - 476 A.D

    Qin Shi Huang unites the whole of China and became the first Chinese Emperor, a title that was to stay for 2000 years.

    Battle of Salamis - Athenian Navy defeated Persian Fleet, saving the whole of Greece from threats of Persian invasion

    Battle of Zama: Roman general Scipio Africanus defeated Hannibal at the battle of Zama, saving the Roman Republic from further Carthagian threat.

    Battle of Milvian Bridge: Roman ursurper Constantine defeated his rival Pagan Emperor Maxentius, making himself the first Christian Emperor of the empire, and eventually made Christianity offical religion of the empire.

    Battle of Catulonian field: Roman General Flavius Aetius, defeated Attila the Hun, saving the Western Roman Empire, indeed Europe from be conquered and ruled by Asian savages.

    MIDDLE AGES: 476 A.D to 1453 A.D

    Battle of Yarmuk: Eastern Roman armies (Bzyantines) crushed by the newly emerged Islamic horde at Yarmuk, Syria, which will open up the fall of Eastern Mediterrean and North Africa to Muslims conquerors.

    Battle of Tours: Charles Martel, the Frankish king defeated the muslim armies invading France from Spain, saving Western Europe from being overrunned by Islamic conquerors and preserving Christianity of Europe.

    Seige of Arce: The last Christian city in the holy land, was all the remains of the land won by Christian crusaders, fell back into the Muslim armies.

    Kamikaze: Not really a battle but more of luck, Japan is saved from invasion of the armies of Kublai Khan when a typhoon destroyed the Mongol Invasion Fleet.

    Battle of Orleans: Joan of Arc led her armies and defeated English troops for the first time and changed the tide of Hundred's year war.

    Seige of Constantinople: The once largest and most magnificent fortress capital city of the Bzyantine Empire fell into Turkish hands after a long seige, killing the last flame of the Former Roman Imperial glory and ending the middle age.

    THE RENAISSANCE AND AGE OF DISCOVERY: 1453 to 1527

    Note: This is difficult for me to conclude when does renaissance ends, I roughly put it at 1527, when Rome, the seat of Papacy was sacked by troops of Emperor Charles IV.
