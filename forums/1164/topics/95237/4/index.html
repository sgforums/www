    27 Sep 04, 13:57
    This book was listed as one of the recommended readings in the SAF Professional Reading Programme. The book review was written by Chan Kay Min and here is part of it:

    -------------------------------------------------------------------------------
    THE leaders of the Singapore Armed Forces bureaucrats or warriors? This is one poser raised by British political science academic Tim Huxley in Defending The Lion City -- The Armed Forces of Singapore.

    The author doubts the effectiveness of SAF in time of war, being led by regular officers, many of whom, he says, are essentially bureaucrats. Huxley is also unsure of the motivation and quality of conscripts and national servicemen should they face a professional, albeit less well-equipped and less well-educated, adversary.

    In his conclusion, Huxley warns against Singapore's overconfidence when comparing itself with its neighbours. "Singapore's defence planners need to beware of falling into the trap of overestimating their own armed forces' potential performance and under-estimating the capabilities of likely adversaries."

    While Singapore has technological advantage over its neighbours, it is of less use in situations of protracted political or military provocation serious enough to trigger mobilisation but insufficient to justify deployment of forces.
    ------------------------------------------------------------------------

    It is important for us to recognise our weakness while we work on our strength. The URL is as follows:
