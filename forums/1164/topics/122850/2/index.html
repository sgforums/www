    Ok.... Ok..... I do hear stories about the not -so-fit medics at the big medical centres (e.g. in the major camps). Before my time, I heard stories... like medics fainting when they saw a real serious casualty. But even within the same story, I heard that the MO, a NS captain, also got nervous and was under stress with the serious casualty. The casualty was a OCT who got blown up by a detonation cord ( I guess you may heard this story from the early 80s). But I heard from my medic friends that training has improved. There is attachments to hospital A and E departments to get them used to serious injuries.

    Yes, I was from the infantry in NS and a specialised unit in the air force in reservist. Have always had combat medics attached to me, in fact 2 medics when I was in the reserve unit. As I said, I trust them fully , not just their medical vocation but in their other tasks as well. We expect them to fight in the infantry platoon, because the enemy is not going to differentiate who is the medic and who is the infantryman.

    Sometimes the combat medic is "tail end charlie" in movement to contact. He and the PS hang back behind the platoon to cover everyone's backside. Very often the medic is actually the last man covering the PS 's backside.
