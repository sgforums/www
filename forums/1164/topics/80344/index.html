    25 May 04, 15:22
    I give a breif one:-
    1.) Don't need to bring duffel bag one (but I am suprise you keep one! As this item now requires you to return back to QM when your ORD)
    Things to bring back:-
    (Basically, just like going for a trip)
    a.) Your Army uniforms set. This includes, No.4, army socks, PT socks, jockey cap(if you have), beret (some units must have for parades) How many sets you have, bring back.
    b.) PT attire
    c.) Underwear
    d.) PT shoes, slippers
    e.) Toiletries.
    f.) Others (such as some shorts, t-shirts,etc)

    2.) Not sure about "Phase Date" though. Typo?

    3.) 1st ICT training varies from unit to unit. So this will be up to your CO.

    Basically, when you reach camp, there will be signs on where to go.
    Report in you No.4. Proceed to the Inprocess counter, after which you will be told which coy to report to and proceed to the coy holding area.

    Your head (be it PC, PS, etc) will bring you to your bunks to put your brang brang. After which, there is usually a parade, followed by a speech by your CO briefing you want to exepect in your ICT.

    The rest of the day will be up to the training schedule. Some I see are lobo on the first day, draw arms, do some cleaning up, go emart. Some go into training immediately. So depends on your unit.

    The above is what I call In-sync, which means you come back with your unit. There are also out-of-sync ICT where you come back as a small group or alone to do some prep work, or become the RSM slaves, etc.

    Looks like I'm a bit late with my reponse, but here's a perspective from an Arty reservist anyway. I finished my 13-year cycle back in '01.

    Yes, it's prudent to report with wearing your fullpack (helmet, SBO) & carrying a civilian bag (PT attire, personal equipment), though bring along an extra #4 set too (in lieu if booking out in a stinky uniform). FYI No need for a "standard pack" yet unless you have to do standby (in later ICTs); your unit will be issuing footpowder/kiwi/toiletpaper, T-loaning out ponchos/groundsheets/shoebrushes, & also collecting money to finance a BN fund for mealboxes/F&N syrup/makan for future CO functions, etc.

    Dump your barang in your car boot, or tompang with someone's on arrival. Much depends on which group you're reporting with, & I think this relates to the "phase date" you're referring to; we didn't have such phrases in my time. FYI My unit was split into 3, of which 2 groups come in either 4-7 days earlier (key personnel, S1 cell, etc. to prepare everything) or 3-5 days earlier (mostly technical people to attend refresher classes/training & sit for tests). There's no formal morning/last parades at this stage, hence some flexibility with booking in-out (with officers' tacit permission, natch).

    The last group is the main body itself, & you won't necessarily get a bunk immediately if you report with them. That's cuz the 1st day is usually busy with administrative & logistical stuff. So even as the various reservist i/c's scutter about taking over stores/rooms/vehicles/etc., the rest of you are liable to hang out in the canteen - all day!

    The process will become more efficient, though. By my 6th-8th ICT, everyone knew their jobs so well that we cut down the preparatory logistics to just 2 periods (10am-12noon) & the main body could move out for field training in the afternoon & run an IPPT in the evening! Better yet, most everyone would've built an informal contact network with the "early arrivals" after a couple of ICTs, to learn what the training programme is (esp. IPPT schedules!) & what to prepare for/when to bring in our barang. That must've contributed to cutting down our "high key" ICTS from 21+ days to around 14 as any.

    My unit always made it a point to allow book-outs on the 1st day - even during a very-tightly finetuned ICT schedule - as a nod for everyone to bring in last-minute stuff that was needed. As for discipline, the other camp people (inc. RSMs) will usually leave the reservists alone &, remember, your unit RSM has his own operational duties too (i/c of the regimental police) to stereotypically boss people around.

    There's no need to march point-to-point but, of course, long/dyed hair & wearing slippers (usually during 6am drawing of rifles) can get your asses chewed off.

    If anyone's to pull rank, it's usually the respective junior officers & junior sergeant majors - but more to set a tone (if it was SAF regulars who took charge during the NSF days) & to ensure everyone respect the hierarchy (you'll probably befriend them enough by the 3rd ICT to call them by appointment rather than "sir"). IMHO It's very much a citizen army in the reserves, with a high level of cooperation & a can-do spirit.

    Best wishes, Rotary79, from a fellow "gunner".

    BTW You won't be seeing the unit RSM as yet, except for muster parades. I know this, cuz I served with the BN Recce & Survey group & the RSM people usually follow us in field training.

    I believe your base is Khatib Camp; it was either that or Amoy Quee Camp during my time. Arty reservists usually chope 4 areas there - the garage side, the canteen, & the bunks or (when no bunks) the stadium grandstand seats. The officers &, when permitted, the NCOs get to enjoy the air con in their respective messes, of course.

    "Argh" (befriend) with your assigned group; you guys can "take cover" together as long as whoever's the i/c is contactable by whoever's higher in the "other rank" chain of command - that's NSmen for you! [Some of us were sneaking out of camp for lunch during the downtime, like maintainence days].

    Stay away from the School of Arty side, & note too if they're concurrently conducting a OCS course - the camp is usually more "on the ball" if they do.
