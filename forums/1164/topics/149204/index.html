    Singapore, Brunei air forces hold bilateral air defence exercise

    SINGAPORE : The Republic of Singapore Air Force and the Royal Brunei Air Force are conducting a bilateral air defence exercise.
    Called Exercise Airguard, it is the eleventh in the series, and is being held for the first time in Brunei.

    The 12-day operation, from August 15 to 26, will involve about 70 personnel from the two air forces.

    SingaporeÂ’s Defence Ministry says the exercise is an excellent opportunity for both air forces to strengthen their inter-operability and further enhance the close relationship between the two armed forces.

    The Chief of the Republic of Singapore Air Force, Major-General Lim Kim Choon, is in Brunei from August 22 to 24 to observe the conduct of the exercise.

    He will also call on Commander of the Royal Brunei Armed Forces Pehin Major-General Awang Halbi Mohd Yussof and Commander of the Royal Brunei Air Force Colonel Mahmud Saidin. - CNA /ct
