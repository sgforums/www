    I think even though these RTS games cannot realistically simulate commanding troops in actual combat, they do have instructional value.

    When gamers are playing, the are presented with tactical problems. Realistic to real conditions? not a chance. but on-screen, there's a objective to achieve. there's ground to be taken.

    Given so much resources, what can the gamer do to achieve that objective? What are the enemy base defences? How can it be overcome? Do i send in Col Burton to disable the power generators, and hence the gatling guns, to allow my armour to punch through? Should I send in air-strike instead? Do I station snipers around the perimeter to take out enemy troops from a distance?

    All these train a person's problem solving skills. In real ops, there are certainly many more things to do than just moving men around. But in pare it down to the most basic level, it's also about solving tactical problems.
