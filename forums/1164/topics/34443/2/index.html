    From Janes

    SINGAPORE - FLIR upgrade for Cougars

    The Republic of Singapore Air Force (RSAF) has enhanced some of its transport helicopters, equipping them for the search-and-rescue (SAR) and Combat SAR role, writes Robert Hewson.

    A new pilot's forward-looking infrared (FLIR) system has been acquired and integrated for the RSAF's Eurocopter AS 532UL Cougars, and the same system is also potentially available for its AS 332M Super Pumas. The new modification was glimpsed for the first time at the Avalon Air Show, Melbourne during February.

    Since 1999, an RSAF helicopter unit (126 Sqn) has been based at Oakey in Queensland, to take advantage of Australia's wide and open training spaces. There are currently 12 aircraft detached with 126 Sqn (a mix of Cougars and Super Pumas) and, according to RSAF sources, six of them have received the FLIR fit.
