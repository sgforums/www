    The Endurance class has the US Navy LPD Design, but the RSN prefer to call these vessels LST - Landing Ship Tanks.

    The helos used on these LST will be different from those used on board the new Frigates.

    The helos used on the new Frigates will serve as outer defense against submarines, as well as extending search and rescue radius of operation, and other missions included.

    The helos used on the LST will normally be of the heavy lift type, to ferry personnel and equipment to shore, or used as search and rescue as well.

    Although, similar sub-hunters can also be operated from the LST, it is unlikely to be used, as the Frigates will normally provide escort to the LST that will be carrying high value assets, and will need the wide array of defense system installed on board the Frigates.

    The new "Delta" Frigates that will be owned by the RSN, is a shorter version of the "La Fayetter" Class of Frigates built by DCN for the French Navy.

    With a smaller compressed size, it is doubtful if the RSN will select a naval helo of the size of a NH-90, let alone a 'marinized' version of a Puma - both of which are considered to be a medium lift helo.

    The naval helo will have its primary mission envelope to cover ASW, extended anti-ship attack role, extended air patrol, air-sea rescue, and if equipped for "Airborne Early Warning" role as well.

    The choice will be more in favor of the SUPER LYNX than the NH-90 if one compare the size and weight ratio of both helo, given the mission envelope to be performed.
