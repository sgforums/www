    The Republic of Singapore Navy (RSN) is investigating how the RSS Tenacious, a 141- metre-long stealth frigate, damaged an underwater cable at a Darwin naval base after the vessel’s anchor became entangled in a power line.

    The ship was anchored in Darwin Habour after being part of a six-day maritime exercise which ended last Friday.

    Australian news reports said the incident happened when crew members of the Tenacious attempted to pull up the anchor when it snagged a 66,000-volt power cable and raised it 30 metres from the seabed.

    Northern Territory’s Power and Water Corporation have engaged divers to investigate the extent of the damage and added it may take some time before the extent of the damage to the cable is known.

    General Manager of Power Networks Bertram Birk said in a statement that its divers will enter the water when visibility in the area improved.

    “It appears that strong currents are making visibility poor and not allowing us good images for diagnosis,” Birk said.

    “I am advised it may be Tuesday or Wednesday before the currents are more favourable.”

    The harbour master, who is investigating the incident, also added that the incident happened in a clearly marked “no anchor” area.

    According to a statement from the Ministry of Defence (Mindef) on Sunday, crew members of the Tenacious were informed by port authorities on Friday afternoon that the warship might be anchored “too close to some underwater cables”.

    Mindef spokesman Desmond Tan said in a Straits Times report: “The Royal Australian Navy Port Services advised RSS Tenacious to relocate to a new anchorage position.”

    He said that when crew aboard the frigate attempted to retrieve the anchor in the afternoon, they found it had become tangled with an underwater cable.

    He added: “RSS Tenacious immediately ceased the anchor recovery operation and decided to recover the anchor during low tide.”

    The anchor was eventually detached from the cable and retrieved after a 90-minute operation that was carried out in the presence of the naval base’s port authorities.

    The “Singaroo” is an the annual maritime exercise between the Singapore and Australian navies is now in its 14th year. This year’s exercise was hosted by the Royal Australian Navy.
