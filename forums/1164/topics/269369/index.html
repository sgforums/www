    Like I said, you can't specialise in military studies here.

    One route is to do a BA/BSocSci(Hons) in Political Science at NUS, specialise in the International Relations subfield to get your basic grounding in basic foreign policy and diplomacy, then go on to S.Rajaratnam School of International Studies at NTU and do a M.SC in Strategic Studies.

    The NTU side will offer a much more in-depth specialisation in areas like security studies, counter-terrorism doctrine and other areas like international political communication and economy.

    On another note, I had the honour of one of the top military historians of the Commonwealth teach my History of Strategic Thought module awhile back. He's taught countless generations of ADF officers, basic elements of classical and modern war philosophy and military doctrine.
