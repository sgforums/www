    It is the prevailing opinion among many of former conscripts and reservists in the SAF that the level of training,its toughness and its overall quality have decreased,leading to decreased troop quality,disclipline and competence.
    This goes not only for physical training but for vocational training as well.I am of the opinion that a shorter time frame of 2 years is insufficient for training people in such vocations as Guards,Commandos or NDU,which are our elite forces.

    We need to arrest this fall now and give more discretion to the PTIs,NCOs and PCs in regards to the training,to train their soldiers as they see fit,within limits that will test,harden and challenge our conscripts troops,before we lose our advantage over other armed forces within the region.

    We should take a hard look at Swiss,Israeli,Taiwanese and Korean conscription systems and their training methods and adopt them to suit ourselves,to increase the level of proficiency of our troops now and instil some sense of military tradition and pride in our prowess now rather than later.

    We are getting to civilised for our own good and I say that the military should go out there and make it perfectly clear to the civilian population that they are not a civilian corporation.They are a military force with the purpose of training soldiers and ensuring their welfare at the minimum as well as minimising the risk of accidents,which are always present.To that end,the training should be tougher and the length of time should be suited to the vocations.

    There should also be less emphasis on academic qualifications,for they do not a soldier or leader make,only a scholar,and we already have enough of those as generals.

    What I'm suggesting is an overhaul of the military system as a whole and more emphasis on proven abilities such as leadership and combat or peacekeeping experience as a premium for promotion rather than academic qualifications.

    @Singaporeans
    Already enlisting next year,probably Guards of Commandos.I'm hoping that all of what I've heard is misinformation,but if not,the SAF seriously needs to toughen the training and make it harder,for example perhaps looking at Russian Spetsnaz training methods and techniques and utilise them to train our Commandos,conscript and regular.

    We are surrounded by possible enemies,though friends as well for now,but we should always be prepared,and we need tougher training to increase the overall fortitude and quality of our troops to make up for less numbers while still maintaining the ability to multiply the effect of any of our attacks.Technology only serves as a force multiplier,but it doesn't win you wars.In any case,it is the human element that is more important,as consistently shown by the Israelis in their military history,or closer to home the Vietnamese whp prevailed against the Americans despite being outgunned throughout the war,through guile and guerilla warfare.

    We should also consider drafting of women,as it drafting by lottery of select female condidates from the population to serve in command echelons and combat support as well as logistics functions to make up for any manpower losses and to ensure some degree of self-sufficiency and self-reliability.

    Of course most of you don't want to be there.Understandable as you don't want to be restricted in your freedom.

    However,whether you like it or not,it is your duty and it is inevitable.You either accept it as a part of your reality and change your attitude to make the best of it and think of it as an exposure to the military system,a way to test your limits and challenge yourself,or you can rebel against it and just slack and be an incompetent,inproficient fool who is wasting an opportunity to contribute constructively to the security of society.

    As I have said and will reiterate,the armed forces is a military institution with the purpose of protecting the national interest and integrity as well as deter possible foreign intervention.We have succeeded very well in the deterrence arena until now,but with the current social situation,with increasingly educated servicemen,overprotective parents and an increasingly consultative government,the military is in for hard times in maintaining its standards which have dropped.

    The main problem from my view is that the military is pandering to a populist government.While it is all very well and fine to consult the population on matters related to civil,cultural,social and economic affairs of the state,we cannot allow such consultation to affect the military.It is the demands of overprotective parents that is degrading the operational capability and proficiency of the individual soldier,thereby affecting their unit.

    As a military institution,the SAF has a duty to defend our society,and to do this they have to resort to conscripting from among the civilian population and maintaining them as reservists after a tour of service,to maintain the numbers of an effective fighting force and deterrent force.To that end,it has to train troops that are physically and mentally resilient,a task it succeeded with in the past,thought less so now.This entails training,which has its risks.The responsibility of the SAF as a military institution is to look after the welfare of each individual soldier,for they are the most vital and important elements of the SAF as a whole.In looking after their welfare,they most they can humanly do is to minimise the risks presented in training by and providing medical personnel to treat injuries.Now,the present perception is that they are looking after the welfare of the troops,but at the cost of compromising on operational capability and ability,which should not be the case.A balance has to be struck between the welfare of our soldiers and the operational capability of the SAF.Currently,the balance seems to be skewed in looking after the welfare of the conscripts and troops at the cost of operational ability.

    The deaths of our fellow servicemen,while tragic,and preventable,were still accidents,and accidents happen.You can only minimise the chances of them happening.However,due to the political requirements necessitated by the accidents,it has resulted in the imposition of legal limits that hamper and restrict the judgement and authority of PTIs and other commanders as well as trainers in ensuring the maximum possible mental resilience,physical resilience and vocational proficiency of the individual soldier and/or unit is reached.

    There needs to be reforms undertaken to curb the further degradation of standards in the SAF and raise standards,and there needs to be a clear position taken by the army in resisting the pressure of the civilian population,to show its authority and to state clearly its role and purpose in Singapore.
