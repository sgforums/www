    Add-on to the counterpoints to Sardaukar's contention:

    1. As stated earlier, I don't think the passing grades for the various proficiency tests both on the individual (IPPT, SOC, marksmanship, unarmed combat, etc.) & unit (various training programmes, with RECON 1 as ultimate aim) levels have declined all that much through the decades. Neither as we've significantly lowered standards. Thus the "dropoff in caliber" just doesn't hold, certainly not at the rate we like to tout our operational readiness. What has changed between the generational batches is the level of regimentation, but that's more of a reflection of the educational/motivational backgrounds of the enlistees.

    2. I think the SAF is at least 15 years younger than any of the military forces you mentioned, so - for a "fairer analysis" - why not compare where we are today with, say, Taiwan's in the late '80s in terms of organizational maturity?

    3. Local conscript & reservist training form part of a bigger whole than Taiwan or even South Korea, whose reservists are IMHO more USA-like "weekend warriors". If anything, I think it's those countries that churn out soldiers more partly cause they have the population base. I won't underrate South Korea's patriotism - in fact I think the Northeast Asian peoples are the most fanatically nationalistic in the world - but I liken their military forces to formerly Iraq's: both have a core of superior troops, but the majority citizen armies are question marks. It doesn't help also that, like Taiwan, they conscript later & end up with a comparatively smaller window of peak unit performance than ours. [Believe me, there's a notable quality dropoff once part-time soldiers near age 30.]

    4. Taiwan - & to some extent South Korea too - still believes in these epic Napoleonic manuevers & attritional warfare. That's the way their armies have evolved, if you know your 20th C war histories (not just with the KMT, but also the CCP - even in the hard-terrain Korean peninsula). It's also why I stated them as "Garang Buta", cuz they do emphasize form as much as, if not more than, substance. Just check their showy parades & show-of-force displays, like last week's photo of them lining up 20+ cannons in a row & firing in unison. [Never mind a single shell or bomb can annihilate the entire deployment.] Singapore, by contrast, is located in a region whose forces are still trained mainly for counterinsurgencies. We were similarly inclined once - pre-Independence 1SIR & 2SIR - but have moved on to a more conventional outlook since, while maintaining a lot of "tactical" mentality that's associated with the earlier era. Brunei sends its kids to our OCS, rather than Malaysia's or Indonesia's, cuz it shares the same military philosophy & size limitations. Why do so, or recognize we're the best equipped in SE Asia, if we've supposed to slip badly?

    5. There's also the topography factor & corresponding deployment suitability. Taiwan's gonna face a defensive war all the way (with or without US planes/ships) if China invades. South Korea's ground-troop roles are secondary in importance with missiles already pointing at their cities. Singapore island is slated to be guarded by 2nd-line PDF soldiers, with our frontline troops expected to be forwardly aggressive. That supposition, along with the purported siege mentality, are the things we "share" with Israeli. But the Israelis, as pointed out, are repeatedly battle-tested, whereas our own present identity is - better or worse - still more at the deterrent stage.
