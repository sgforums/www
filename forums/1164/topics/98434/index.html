    There is a reason for historical accuracy, "accuracy"

    When we start to 'bend the truth' especially for historical based movies like Pearl Harbour, Braveheart or Saving Private Ryan (which is only based in part on the true situation of the NILAND brothers see http://www.101airborneww2.com/bandofbrothers2.html "SAVING SGT NILAND") we get somthing worst than a skewed perpespective of realty - falsehood.

    Take "Braveheart" so many facts were changed that you'd be suprised to learn that Isabella of France who marries Edward II (the king's son) was only 14 when William Wallace was executed (they never met).

    William Wallace was executed in 1305. Edward I did not die until 1307, and was in fairly good health up to that time (he was leading an invasion of Scotland at the time). Edward II did marry Isabella of France...in 1308. In France. Afterwards she came to England for the first time. She never met William Wallace or Edward I. Finally, her first born child, Edward III, was born in 1312. Unless Wallace had come back from the dead, there was no way for him to father that child.

    see http://www.nitpickers.com/movies/repository.cgi?pg=t&sp=i&tt=101073

    End result, more kids will remember Mel Gibson's version with its distorted facts than the historical figure.
