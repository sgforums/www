    The term 'light tank' is insideously misleading to the ill-informed of the reality of the capability of a 'light tank'. The correct term to use is 'tank destroyer'

    Properly defined, 'tank destroyer' is described, in essence, as an anti-tank weapon, usually gun-based, mounted on a mobile platform. The weapon may or may not be under armour, e.g. M-50 Ontos (6-barreled 106mm recoiless weapon system on air-droppable tracked chassis - 1950s-era). Even if under armour, the weapon may or may not be traversable/turreted, e.g. Jagdpather, Marder (German tank destroyers - WW2 era).

    Tank destroyers are solely designed to defeat enemy armour (up to MBT-class) but yet are not as protected nor as sophisticated as the MBTs. In this perspective, all 'light tanks' are in fact tank destroyers simply because they will never be able to survive a direct hit from enemy tanks.

    So I say this for the ppl who may be awed by the armour that we have or may be intending to acquire - the AMX13SM1, M8 AGS or any other 'light tank' that may be indigeously conceived - do not get too excited.
