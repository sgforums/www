    Cost of defence crippling: DSTA (reported in Today Feb 26 2004)

    THE cost of buying military equipment and maintaining military capabilities is rising and is becoming increasingly unaffordable for Singapore, warned senior officers from the Defence Science and Technology Agency (DSTA) yesterday.
    .
    "Unless this problem is tackled, we will not be able to afford many new systems," said Mr Soh Kong Pheng, the DSTA's deputy chief executive of operations, at the Asian Aerospace 2004 show.
    .
    One reason for the high expenditure is the cost of operating and supporting the equipment, which can be more than twice the purchase price, said DSTA deputy director Lee Wee Khong. Which means, for example, the planned purchase of new fighter aircraft, reputed to be worth $1 billion, could incur another $2 billion.
    .
    This is why the DSTA, Singapore's defence procurement agency, has told competing suppliers to minimise the up-front expenditure and suggest support concepts for spares and maintenance.
    .
    It has also tried to be cost effective by keeping price and technical proposals separate, so that the price is only known after the technical benefits have been assessed. "Then, the system with the best benefit-cost ratio will be selected," Mr Lee told industry suppliers.
    .
    Even low-value purchases take their toll on military expenditure. Items such as stationery, photocopies and electrical appliances as well as camp maintenance cost the Singapore Armed Forces up to $60 million a year. Â— Derrick A Paulo
