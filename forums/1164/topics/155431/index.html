    3 Oct 05, 11:14
    Kevlar is actually one of the more well-known and documented types of materials used for Ballistic Resistant Vest, or what is otherwise known as Bullet Proof Vests.

    Bear in mind, Bullet Proof Vest is actaully a mis-nomer, as you can always find a bigger, or faster bullet to defeat the existing armour around. The industry term is Ballistic Resistant Vest, or Body Armour.

    Kevlar is from the Aramid family, there are also other types of materials, such as High Density PolyEthylene (HDPE) and PBO.

    Weight wise, Kevlar is actually quite heavy compared to HDPE in terms of performance. However, it is also cheaper as material.

    There will always be confliscitng trade-offs if one is to design body armour as a LBV. Body Armour is meant to protect critical areas, therefore, there is generally coverage at shoulder areas and groin areas, which means greater impediment to agility and movement.

    Body Armour will be donn depending on the mission profile.
