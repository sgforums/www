    FN MAG (GPMG)

    The GPMG is much liked and favoured by all troops who have used in the past and is said to be one of the best and reliable 7.62mm General Purpose Machine Gun in the World.

    The MAG is belt-fed, gas-operated and fires from the open bolt position. It is air-cooled and its 21.4-inch barrel is designed for quick-change by the gun crew. In the ground version, i. weighs 231/2 pounds with an overall length of 49.2 inches. Its adjustable gas regulator permits the cyclic rate to be varied from 650 to 1,100 rpm.

    Well....are u a technician???

    Technically speaking, when you do a FFF of the GPMG, you do it with both barrels. What technicians do is to ensure that both the barrels of the GPMG sit in well and tight with the rest of the parts of the GPMG - i.e. body and receiver group.

    When you use another barrel from another GPMG to be used on a different one which it was FFF with, you get a possibility of a gap by which excess gas would rush out. There would not be a tight fit. Note that when u fit in the barrel, you need to slide in and turn in right-wards. Why? Take note of the lugs

    It's not a quality control problem.

    It's just that when you do FFF of the barrels against the bolt-carrier and the rest of the GPMG, it ensures that evreything is in place.

    It's not one-size fits all.

    Technicians need to do various steps in the FFF checklist to ensure that both barrels can be certified to fire with the MG it is assigned to.

    There are also different types of nuts, no. of turns etc. Just as your fingers are not of the same length, same goes for the barrels.

    A couple of mm make a whole difference, so that's where the barrel nuts (of the barrel) come in.
