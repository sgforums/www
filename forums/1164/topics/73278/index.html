    Body Armour is not Bullet-proof... Reason being that one can always find a round that is capable of penetrating the Armour...

    While a WWII Flak Jacket, weighing 14kgs, can only stop low-level Handgun threats. Newer Man-made Fibres, like Kevlar Vest are capable of stopping Higher-Level 9mm Handgun Threats at 4-5kgs.

    To stop Rifle rounds, you would require Ceramic Plates or Steel ones, depending on what is the calibre and type of round you expect to encounter. They tend to weigh 2-3 kg per piece. 2 pieces, 1 Front & 1 Back, means an additional 4-6 kg...

    To stop Fragments, you would hardly require Plates. Soft Kevlar is typically capable of those.

    Since the fugitives were described as possessing handguns, going in with Plates would be a bit of an overkill... not to mention very uncomfortable...
