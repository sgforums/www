    Actually, Naval Gunnery is not much different from land based Artillery. The Gun barrels are after all identical. So they are indirect weapons, except for the lower caliber guns.

    However, Naval Guns are expected to lob heavier shells for armour penetration which one does not need on land. Hence, they have to elevate their guns at the right angle to score a shot. An example would be the AP round the US used in WWII which weighed 1225 kg. Take a look at this: http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/16%22/50_caliber_Mark_7_gun. With shells this heavy, one must definitely account for the parabolic trajectory. I suspect these weapons have a reinforced frontal tip possibly similar to anti-tank projectiles back then. After all, battleship armor was simply lots and lots of steel.

    The shore defence guns are of the same type as the naval guns as well.
