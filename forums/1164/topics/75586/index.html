    Presently, the divisional ADA battalions of the air force provide mobile ADA coverage for the army. These are primarily missile-based systems integrated on board lightly armoured or even soft-skinned wheeled vehicles, operating in conjunction with sensor systems on separate platforms

    What is really lacking are dedicated mobile ADA systems (preferrable gun/missile armament) wih integrated sensor packages onboard unitary armoured, tracked platforms, e.g. Russian Tunguska, to shadow or even be organic to armour battalions. Such systems have dual secondary mission for fire-support against infantry/light armour/bunker role with their gun/missile armament, e.g ADATS

    Nonetheless, Russian missile technology in the SAM department is revered to be even more advanced and cheaper than the latest that the West can offer. Which I believe to be the fundamental case for choosing the Igla against the others, like US Stinger, UK Blowpipe/Starstreak, or even continuing with the costly Mistral, which isn't a pure MANPADs to start with. True, the Igla's targeting device may look crude, but it's still cheap and comparably effective. And that's what one would expect from a MANPADs that is meant to be shoulder-fired - easy to use.
