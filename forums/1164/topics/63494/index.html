    It is obvious that the best for the SAF cannot be compromised, and if Doctors are serving their internship in the SAF Medical Centres, surely the SAF Soldiers deserve a higher standard of medical attention then from inexperienced doctors.

    Surely the minimum rank of Captain for a Medical Graduate is far too senior for an inexperienced person undergoing training ?

    In most SAF Medical Centres, these young doctors act as any other General Practictioners attending to the simple daily illnesses - such as flu, cold, coughs, skin problems, aches, rashes, pains in the joints.

    As General Practictioners they are hardly specialists in any field of medicine, and have basic and limited knowledge that qualify them only as Medical General Practictioners.

    From their inexperience, they will need the immediate and close supervision of more senior SAF, or Reservist, or Civil Medical Officers to act as their Consultants.

    However, for this to work, the inexperienced NSF Medical Officer will need the patient guidance of senior Medical Officers, some of whom can be abrasive for the demands made on their limited and profitable time.

    It is not a question of fairness if we want to get the more experienced doctors in the SAF, even if "it means we haf to get them to resume and complete their ns liability much later in their careers".

    It is a question of serving the National Service mid-way in their medical training and being recruited BEFORE intership experience, and having the SAF recruit inexperience doctors; or allowing the Medical Students to complete their entire medical training that include 'internship' or 'housemanship' before qualifying as Doctors, and serving the SAF as such.

    The SAF and the soldiers will deservingly obtain experienced and truly qualified Doctors, who will be more then willing to complete their entire medical education before being called up, instead of being called up

    From two frightening medical incidences over the last five years, I am doubtful of the standards of the young and inexperienced NSF Medical Officers acting as General Practitioners, risking the lives of other NSF men as guinea pigs for the advancement of their own career experience and development.
