    You may not want to die but would you want to kill another human being?

    During WWII, there was a study set up to investigate human nature during a firefight. Up to 65% of respondents replied that they would not return fire when fired upon. They would cower instead. Only when encouraged to fire by a Sect Comm or Platoon Leader, do they blaze away. Again, they do not take careful aim and shoot off instead, to alleviate frustration.

    When the Sect Comm or Platoon Leader moves away, the men will just go back to a cowering position.

    Due to the findings of that study, range targets were changed to Figure targets. Prior to WWII, it was common practice to shoot at bull's eye kind of targets. Figure targets are believed to de-sensitise the shooter's brain.

    Again, note the reluctance of a typical human in a firefight. Group leaders and small-unit commanders make a big difference.
