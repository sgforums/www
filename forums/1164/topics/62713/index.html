    well i think i can answer you the best since i have been thru this phase of pilot training but din make it thru airgrading so i could onli stop after airgrading.

    There are 3 scenarios that can happen.
    First scenario, if you qualify for OCS after BMT, you would be posted to OCS to complete your Tri-Service Term. After which you would be posted to Air Force School to wait for your turn to fly to Tamworth which is an hour's domestic flight from Sydney to do your airgrading.

    Second scenario, you did not qualify for OCS but you manage to finish in the top few percentile in your cohort during BMT. You would get posted to Air Force School immediately after BMT. During your posting order, you would come under the category of RSAF regular. After which you would then wait for your turn to go to Tamworth for airgrading.

    Third scenario, you made an application for pilot training, but becoz of pending medical status after the health screening, your status as pilot trainee is not confirmed, hence you would complete whatever posting you had after BMT, following which if your medical has been approved of, you will then be taken out and sent to Air Force School for your turn at airgrading.

    There are other scenarios but these are the top common three. hope it did help you!

    airgrading isn't tat difficult if and only if you came from Youth Flying Club. lolx
    well airgrading is a hell lot of mental stress that the instructors impose on you to see how well you perform under pressure.
    You've got lotsa checks to memorise and instructors sitting next to you would scold you for the slightest mistake tat you make since safety is part of the air force culture and i am sure you want to be as safe as possible when you are flying in the air. and yes, you get to fly the plane...

    Roughly let me tell you wat airgrading syllabus is.

    Sortie 1 - The instructor takes you for a joy ride. but dun take it as a joy ride. you gotta start to remember ground features as this determines where are the boundaries for different training area. After which he will show you the effects of control; i.e how the plane would reacted to the control of the pilot. after which brace yourself for some black knight manouevres as this is done to see if you are prone to get air sick.

    Sortie 2 - the instructors takes off for you and you move off to training area for training and impart further knowledge of the gyroscope, the altimeter, the speedometer and etc. then slowly he would ask you to do the checks slowly one by one. Turning the plane and etc.

    Sortie 3 to 5 - basically is the same as sortie 2 just tat you would start to take off by yourself and do the checks required yourself without any prompting from the instructors.

    Sortie 6 - A pre test sortie. basically is sortie to practice all the stuff you have to do for your test.

    Sortie 7 - Test 1

    Sortie 8 to 15 - Dunno liao coz din make it thru test 1. lolx. but gotta do with aerobatics and high angle turning and such.

    hope this helped
