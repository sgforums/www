    Its been 5 years since I was a 2IC in BMTC2. Hope the below still applies.

    Normal BMT... Its the standard package, you need to complete and pass the following, IPPT, SOC and IFC. I shall not go into details.

    ORD'd a long time ago; my understanding was built from the days when they were all under 7 SIB: 1st GDS/SAFGU, 2nd GDS/8 SIR, 1st CDO, 10th CDO, School of Commando Training, & later 3rd GDS/7 SIR.
    Those changes were about 5 years before my time, at the start of the '80s or so. It came roughly together with the introduction of the IPPT system, if I recall.

    Browsed through some reference book & spotted the differences between then & now.

    Now :- HQ Infantry takes charge of, for both infantry & Guards, the training of its recruits (via BMTC), specialists (via SISPEC) & reservists (via ITC & BCTC). The CDOs, as well as OCS, are outside of this organizational structure.

    Then :- 7 SIB, while one of 3 "active" brigades, was very much the personification of "kings of combat." It had elite elements like the CDOs & SAFGU (along with 7 SIR & 8 SIR) & also, as I remember, took charge of ITD (predecessor of BMTC) training as well. Its sub-unit, the SCT, led the way in instructor training, particularly grooming NCO trainers who would mentor for the RDF conversion. The CDOs, thus, was very much a prominent "conventional" team player then.

    Also, the SAF emphasis of those days that all servicemen must go through the same BMT - Army, Air Force, Navy; combat, technican, service; elite, non-elite - was one thing that distinguished us from other armed forces, believe it or not. That's why I presumed there could be some flexibility in training-course continuation, esp. for new regulars (don't make sense if not). With the NSFs, it's certainly applicable, as said, for the top SISPEC trainees-to-OCS route.
