    The V-200 will not be upgraded in my opinion. It will be used until they cannot move anymore or until they know what to replace it with. Right now they are just being used as an anti-aircraft platform and easy troop carrying duties. You won't see them tackling rough terrain etc as it is a well-known risk.

    Why?

    The V-200 has a serious problem. It is known to flip over easily on inclines because of its high centre of gravity and the fact that it is a wheeled vehicle.

    That's why they were taken out of service as troop carriers and the job given to M-113.

    Furthermore, I think the 4 wheels do not give very good cross country performance and the vehicle can get stuck in small ditches etc. Most countries in the world now go for at least 6 wheels for armoured cars.

    Our Terrex for example has 8 wheels. The US Stryker also has eight wheels.

    Anyway, there is a great debate going on regarding wheels vs tracks, The US and Canada seems to be moving towards more wheels and some people feel this is a great mistake.

    (Just type in "stryker wheels vs tracks" and you can find a lot of debate for and against.)

    But wheels or tracks, all is in agreement that 8 wheels might be good, but 4 wheels definitely not good.
