    6 Jan 04, 09:26
    A forward pistol grip is not necessarily tactically superior to a normal rifle foregrip. The normal rifle foregrip is easier to go prone with, while the forward pistol grip is better when you are firing from a standing or kneeling position, particularly in CQB. Which one is better depends on what situation you are using the rifle in. It makes sense that the standard issue SAR-21 has a standard grip, since SAF infantry tactics involve firing mostly from the prone position. This photo is of an Army marksman who shoots in competitions for the SAF, where they might have to fire from the standing position.

    The mechanism of the SAR-21 is based on the Ultimax, which was designed with help from Eugene Stoner, designer of the M16. It was certainly designed in cooperation with the Israelis, who produced the TAR-21 at almost the same time. So the rifle has design input from American and Israeli sources, but it is definitely still a Singaporean design. There is no design that is not in some way based on others.

    The SAR-21 still has some teething problems, which I hope ST resolves soon. ST never gets anything perfectly right on the first try, but usually the third try is the charm. Look at the Ultimax Mk 1 vs Mk 2 vs Mk 3 for example.
