    Actually, I feel that the RSAF would upgrade our F-16 fleet to perhaps a near Block 60 standard, focusing on replacing current avionics.

    The APG-68(V)9 that equips the latest batch of 20 F-16Ds delivered in 2003-4 are already very capable radars and the 42 earlier F-16C/Ds could have their APG-68(V)5 sets upgraded to a similar standard. This would be more cost effective instead of procuring new APG-80 radars which constitute an extremely expensive option.

    The current F100-PW-229 engines are still modern engines and there is no need to convert to the F110-GE-132 which powers the F-16 Block 60.

    Thus the focus should be on replacing the current avionic fit with state of the art systems that would ensure a high degree of survivability in areas with hostile enemy fighters or SAMs.

    Compatibility with a wide range of other PGMs such as the Israeli SPICE guided bomb and Popeye missile would be considered to reduce our dependence on short range American Paveway LGBs and AGM-65B/G missiles.

    The 42 F-16C/Ds not fitted with conformal fuel tanks could be adapted like the last 20 F-16Ds to extend their operating range while carrying a heavier warload.

    With all these modifications, the RSAF should be in possession of a fleet of formidable strike fighters even before the F-15T enters service in 2008.
