    There used to be 3 broadly-defined expertises for GAs, aka TAs - Technical Assistant - in my time: firing-data (FD) calculation, recce n' survey, & arty-fire planning (i.e. assisting FO/FSO). The 1st two are taught at SOAY; the latter also touched on there, but IMO learnt more ad-hoc while at the active unit.

    I understand, though, the recce n' survey side has been phased out with the advent of GPS sophistication & also the newer Arty weapons.

    So, of the remaining two, the FD side is physically cushy but quite mentally taxing; you're vehicular-based but will be finger-tapping keyboard/calculator keys all the time.

    The arty-fire planning side can, I believe, be split into 2 further subgroups:

    - the HQ appointments where you're basically based in an air-con office in camp (generally tagging along for field exercises as BN Ops, though occasionally get attached to FSO/FO's too);

    - the line-troop appointments where you pack a signal set & follow the FO/FSO when they do support for the infantry exercises, which was a lot & usually on weekends during my time.

    The latter (as FO/FSO GA or TA) is the most siong, esp. if you're not even PES B in the 1st place. You could even end up going to Brunei for jungle-training, like I did!

    Tip: Unless you're garang or absolutely hates to work with numbers, you can try to persuade for a change from the "tougher" appointments during the initial getting-to-know-you interview with your commander. That's what I did, & even then I did an about-face come reservist (didn't fancy chiong suah with the infantry until age 30something).
