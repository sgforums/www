    S'pore fighter jet 'screeches' to emergency landing
    AN A4 Super Skyhawk fighter jet (like the one in the file picture below) from the Republic of Singapore Air Force suffered a mechanical failure and made an emergency landing at the Tengah Airbase yesterday afternoon.

    The plane was slightly damaged and the pilot was not hurt.



    The New Paper was alerted by a passer-by, who said he was surprised to see a plane looking like it was touching down without its landing gear out.

    He called and said he could not see what happened to the jet when it touched down because the airbase's runway was not visible from the public road.

    'I heard a screeching sound. There was no explosion and it just went silent after that,' he said.

    When contacted, a Ministry of Defence (Mindef) spokesman confirmed that the Skyhawk jet had made an emergency landing at 11.54 am yesterday.

    'It experienced problems during a routine training exercise,' she said, adding that the damage to the plane was minor and that the pilot was not hurt.

    Mindef will be conducting an inquiry to determine the cause of the incident, she added.

    Super Skyhawks have taken part in the traditional flypast at National Day Parades.
