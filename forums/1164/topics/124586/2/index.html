    But Iwant to add something abt ground pressure.

    Conventionally it is calculated by the weight of the vehicle / area of surface contact to the ground(i.e area of the track/surface contact ).

    But in reality , the pressure is higher at the point where the road wheels contact with the track, so the pressure is not evenly spread across the length of the track.

    Therefore, when a 50 tons AFV vs a 13tons AFVs of the same ground pressure, it does not mean that both have same tactical mobility due to same the ground pressure. It is likely the smaller AFVs wil be less likely to be mired.

    As for the experiences of Vietnam, MBTS was statistically less mobile than the lighter and smaller APC as reported.

    Narrow jungle tracks which are "created" by civillian jeeps and logging trucks etc are still frequently found in SEA. Likewise the bridges there might not be able to withstand a 50 tons weight.

    And there are plenty of rubber & palm tree plantations in MY , in which the trees are planted in such way to fully utilised the land which are quite narrow for big MBTs to maneuver.
