    I doubt that SG Govt. will approve such a setup. First of all, the control of explosives and research / building of any type of military and tactical equipment is very controlled and sensitive to them.

    Secondly, it will be hard to compete with ST for the local market, since they have a close tripartite relationship and special trust. ST and DSTA share SAF's secrets and special needs, that SAF will NEVER share their secrets with anyone else, not any commerical contractor. Every ST and DSTA engineer is specially selected and security screened. To compete with ST on this is impossible.

    On the international market, its worse, how do you compete against ST, Raytheon and much larger competitors with billions to burn for R&D?

    In Singapore, you can't do firings and tests without using SAF's range. How do you do that in the first place as a private contractor?

    Its extremely remote chance that it can ever be approved, not even having to study the commercial viability.
