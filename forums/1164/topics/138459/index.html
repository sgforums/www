  • tvdog
    23 Jun 05, 20:36
    Conventional, definitely.

    I don't ride in a armoured vehicle, while I did a lot of FIBUA and FOFO, I never felt the M16 to be too long. And I love the balance as the weight is very evenly distributed. And it's light and accurate. Also the conventional layout is good for buttstrokes and bayonet fighting.

    For the sake of saving a bit of length by switching from M16 to bullpup SAR-21, the tradeoffs are too much.

    Slow magazine change. Heavier. Odd selector switch location. No bayonet lug. No provision of good iron sights (optical scopes are prone to damage, fogging and fungus).

    M16 is one of the few weapons you can reload super fast using just one hand.

    To be really fast you should reload before your existing magazine completely empties. This eliminates the need to ease spring, re-cock etc.

    You can simply press the mag release so the existing mag with a few rounds left can drop out. While you're releasing the existing mag your left hand is already pulling out a fresh mag from your ammo pouch. As soon as the old mag falls out you can plug in the new one and continue firing.

  • Meia Gisborn
    23 Jun 05, 23:44
    One of the members in our gun club recently bought a Bushmaster M17S Bullpup carbine, and I had the opportunity to shoot it recently.

    As I recall, the trigger was pretty awful (it felt very "spongey" and had an indistinct break), and the pistol grip was raked a bit too far forward for my comfort. But it seemed to be pretty accurate (I managed a sub-4" group at 100 yards with a standard red-dot sight).

    However, I was exceedingly uncomfortable with the fact that the firing chamber was RIGHT NEXT to the shooter's head. On a conventional AR-15, the firing chamber is located at the front of the upper receiver, some distance from the shooter's face, where a chamber rupture (caused by an overcharged cartridge or obstructed bore) would probably not cause any injuries. On a Bullpup design, if the chamber lets go, I wonder if the shooter will get a faceful of shrapnel.

    MG

    1) not able to switch left to right..The AUG do have an left hand bolt ! but do not know if the SAR21 is capable of switching!.FAMAS can be swithc over to lefthadn also! and during tactical movement your strong sweep is easier but the weak sweep side you have to shift your body position much more.
