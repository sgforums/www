    Dave,

    Take a look at this website:

    www.ammo-oracle.com/body.htm#m855zero

    It explains the concept of barrel twist rate vs. projectile weight in great detail.

    The 1:9 twist in your 223 barrels should be able to properly stabilize the majority of 55 to 62 gr. projectiles, such as the ones you've mentioned, out to a minimum of around 300 yards. Heavier projectiles will require a faster twist rate to properly stabilize over greater distances.

    Hahaha.... you actually stumbled on one of the useful features of the sharpshooter scope. It is commonly used by the infantryman as a sort of spotter scope, since not all chaps are issued with the binoculars.

    By the way, it is not meant to be a sniper scope, since this is a different instrument altogether. The sharpshooter scope is just to enhance the infantry sections firepower by enabling some of the chaps to lay down more accurate covering fire. It is not meant to pick-off targets at long distances... but it enables the section leader to have more options for covering fire during an assault.
