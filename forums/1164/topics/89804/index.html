    To Sardaukar,

    I may be able to comment on some of the issues you raise because I am from an SAP schoool. The purpose of setting up SAP schools by the govt was to preserve the traditions of such schools and to build up a core group of younger Singaporens with an in depth understanding of the Chinese culture to engage an emerging China.

    Being immerse in the Chinese culture does not make one a chauvinist. In my school there are efforts to let us interact and understand the culture of other races. I have friend from other races in my neighbourhood, in tertiary institutions, in the army and in working life.

    Being the most westernised of all the races in Singapore, the Chinese are at the highest risk of losing their culture identity and hence the efforts to help SAP schools. The govt has also help other races through organisation such as Sinda and Muis.

    As for creating links with other ASEAN countries, there are several scholarship programmes that invite the brightest from these countries to study here. They will serve as a bridge to understanding between Singapore and other countries in future. As for our officers who are deployed to other countries they are given intensive cultural immersion programmes in the country they are deployed to.

    The immgration Issue.
    The primary concern in deciding whether a person is granted citizenship is his/her ability to contribute to Singapore. A world class researcher from any race from any country would be welcome in Singapore.

    The race does play a part in botherline cases. A person who is of ethnic descent from the major races in Singapore will stand a higher chance than someone who is Vietnamese or Filipino. Such a policy is maintain the balance and stability of the ethnic make-up in Singapore. Such a policy is not unique to Singapore but other countries as well.

    If the CDO take you on, then good luck. I'm not sure they conduct BMTs seasonally (every 3 months) like Tekong, though - so what if your enlistment timing is all "wrong" for them?

    The bulk of combat units are also filled by mono-intake people, so on paper a Telong/SBMT "outsider" can only be posted in via 2 ways: relevant vocation-training by the pertinent formation (inc. after going thru OCS or SISPEC) or more general vocation-training by service formations. It won't be as if you can, say, aim for 3rd Guards on your initial reporting day, unless you've signed on or something. Instead, your only input resources - as far as influencing your hoped-for posting goes - are during the PC interviews, & of course your course results, which'll entail your DIs' feedback.

    With the latter, I actually had 1st-hand experience after being attached to Arty Training Cadre, due to my extra half-year. My recruit battery had a few pai kia's, inc. 2 gangsters (inc. one bodily tattooed who came under my section) whom the instructors were informally told to mix together with care. I dunno if the word came down from ISD or whatever, but they turned out to be normal peng.

I have to confess that not all of us were 100% honorable in our judgement calls, even as we reached no consensus on whether posting out (to storemen-type appointments) represents a "reward" & retaining an underachiever is a "punishment". But generally we tried to be fair, & every evaluation was performance-rated with no racial shenanigans.
