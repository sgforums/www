    Lats pulldowns are of limited use because they do not precisely replicate the movement in a chin-up.

    The most effective way of doing it is to keep going until you can do no more, take a short break, and do it again, and to keep repeating this cycle until you get onto the bar and cannot complete a single chin-up.

    Success in chin-ups ?

    It is a combination of arm muscles, abdomen muscles, upper chest muscles.

    Something most do not believe is that lung power helps.

    Breathe in deep as you pull yourself up, and breathe out as you come down.

    Go through this exercise in a "dry run" and tension those muscle areas in an imaginery pull up and release, and it will help you half the way when the real pull up come along.

    It worked for me during the preparation stage to psyche myself up to the task.

    Most chin up bars sold for home use will have three components:

    one piece extendable bar with rubber suction on each end - ( the bar can be turned in any one direction to extend or shorten its length to fit any door way).

    two end caps - each with at least 3 holes

    one set of screws.

    Both the end caps must be screwed into the doorway, after the preferred height level is determined, and that both are at the same height.

    These two end caps will be where the end suction rubber of the extendable bar will be "held" in place.

    Without these two end caps, the suction rubber may slip, when one's full body weight is taken up by the chin up bar, and causing the bar and one's body to crash by the gravity pull.

    It will be painful when your knee or your toes crashed onto the floor, when the chin up bar slipped from its position without the end caps acting as an "anchor or holder cup".

    If you are taller then the height of the doorway allow for the chin up bar to be installed, you can always curl up your leg (by either adopting a kneeling position while hanging in the air, or taking on a knee up position when hanging), then pulling your entire body weight up.

    Try getting one at Mustafa, they sell two varieties (below or above S$20.00), and both with end cups to be screwed into the door jamb, or onto the wall of a narrow passage way.
