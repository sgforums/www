    Air Force head to join in T-50 trainer jet exercise
    SEOUL, Jan. 2 (Yonhap) -- South Korea's Air Force chief was to join this year's first midair interception exercise here later Tuesday in which he will pilot the country's domestically made air trainer jet himself, the Air Force said.

    Air Force Chief of Staff Kim Sung-il's participation in the drill is aimed at testing T-50 training aircraft before they are commissioned starting in February, as well as inspecting the air defense posture, it added.

    South Korea developed its first trainer jet T-50, which is able to fly at supersonic speed, in 2005 for the purpose of training elite pilots. It is also seeking to export the jet.

    "The T-50 fleet led by Chief of Staff Kim is scheduled to take off from the Air Force's base in Suwon (46 kilometers south of Seoul) around 2:40 p.m.," the Air Force said in a press release.
