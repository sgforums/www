    Hi, Dave!

    A good friend of mine has an FNP-9 (identical to the FNP-40, except chambered in 9mm) and I have shot it on many occasions. His FNP is box-stock and it has one of the best SA triggers I have ever come across in a DA/SA handgun. He was over at my house last year, and we measured the SA trigger break at just a hair under 3 lbs. FN must have some pretty good lawyers working for them! Plus, his FNP was incredibly accurate--I was managing offhand sub-3" groups at 25 yards using commercial 9mm ammo; I expect the 40S&W version will be just as accurate.

    (CDNN is selling the two-tone versions of both the FNP-9 and FNP-40 for just $409. That's a damn good deal!)

    I don't think you'll regret getting the XD-40 Tactical. I've had one since July of 2002, and its accuracy is easily on par with that of the CZ pistols. I've thus far shot well over 25,000 rounds of my handloads through it, and it has never, ever jammed or misfed. The SA trigger pull weight can be reduced with the simple replacement of the sear tension spring (I have mine down to around 2.8 lbs.), but reducing the trigger reset will require some metalwork on the trigger mechanism.

    Dave,

    The XD's have historically had a very crappy finish on them. You could rub the finish off with just your fingers! When my XD-40 Tactical was delivered in July of 2002, parts of the finish had worn down to bare metal from just moving around in the plastic pistol case. I called Springfield about it and they refinished the slide under warranty.

    Springfield finally responded to all the complaints about the finish when they introduced the XD-45ACP early last year. The XD-45ACP featured a very durable finish (called Melonite) on the slide, and Springfield adopted the same finish on the rest of their XD pistols manuactured from that point on. If you get an XD, make sure it was manufactured in 2006 or later. Alternatively, you could get one with a stainless slide (which, in my opinion, looks a lot sharper!).

    If you do decide to send your P-01 to CZ-USA for trigger work, let me know and I'll talk to my contacts there to see if they can give your job a higher priority.

    Dave,

    CZ pistols come from the factory with the magazine brake (#24 in the parts diagram of the manual) at the back of the magwell bent to press against and retain the magazine, preventing it from dropping free when the mag release is pressed.

    It's real easy to make the mags drop free: just remove the two bends in the magazine brake to straighten and prevent it from applying pressure to the rear of the magazine. You need to remove the brake from the gun to accomplish this:

    1. Make sure the gun is unloaded.

    2. Remove the grip panels.

    3. Decock the hammer all the way forward (not just to the half-cock 'safety' position).

    4. Drive out the magazine brake pin (#25).

    5. While applying upward pressure on the main spring plug (#22), push out the main spring plug pin (#23).

    6. Push and hold the main spring plug further up (this will require considerable effort as you're pushing against the main spring [#21]).

    7. Remove the magazine brake by pulling it up and forward into the magwell.

    8. Gradually release pressure on the main spring plug. The plug and main spring will fall out.

    You can now straighten the two bends in the brake, then reinstall it by reversing the steps above.

    Thanks!

    From a purely aesthetic standpoint, the Tacticals, with their 5" barrels, always seemed better proportioned to me. The longer sight radius probably makes shooting them accurately a tad easier too.

    Another nice thing about the Tacticals: they have uncaptured recoil guide rods and utilize standard full-size 1911 recoil springs, making it easy to fine-tune the recoil characteristics by using springs of different strengths. The XD-40 Tactical comes from the factory with an 18 lb. recoil spring, but I find it functions better with a 24 lb. spring (which is what I currently have in mine).
