    Originally posted by chanjyj:

    you trying to say that the M16 is still one of the most accurate military assault rifles? I thought our SAR21 would have surpassed it in terms of accuracy already, not to mention all the new rifles popping up
    He's not kidding you, not to what I know anyway.

    In the hands of a pro who knows how to shoot his stuff, the M16 is one of the most precise firearms ever, thanks to it's direct impingement system which simply has far less parts moving and shifting weight as you would get in a piston.

    However this is also it's downside, for it makes it quite a bit less reliable then other kinds of actions.

    In terms of overall performance for the grunt, the SAR21 is more accurate because of its 1.5x scope and egronomics. The disadvantage you get in using a piston system is more then made out for by removing the need to figure out the ghost sight picture that some of our guys simply cannot figure out. And of course this is subjective but I prefered the balance of the SAR to the M-16... I guess I like the weight of my weapon centralized.

    Of course, this is not even assuming that you can stick a scope on the M-16.

    In terms of MOA and group size in the hands of two equally skilled pros who know their stuff, you will probably get a better group with the '16 then the SAR. But of course this is raw, technical accuracy.

    However if you take everything into account, including the increased number of conscripts who can finally hit something with the SAR (thanks to the scope), as well as the ability to fire the SAR one handed and in strange positions that you might encounter in FIBUA with improved accuracy (thanks to it's centralized weight distribution), the SAR-21 presents an overall increase in combat accuracy for the grunt.

    Those two things are quite different, for example (though this is an extreme one), the rifles they use in sharpshooting contests are probably a lot more accurate then your military weapons, but you probably aren't going to chiong with those weapons or realize that that increase in accuracy really helps you out in your combat drills. You'll probably get more hits if you stuck with the military weapon.

    That said, different guns for different needs.

    But of course, the differences in technical MOA accuracy we are talking here (M-16 vs. SAR) are really marginal for all pratical purposes. You can still easily nail a first shot hit on a target at 300 meters with the SAR, and that's more or less more then what the average grunt needs. But factor everything in and the SAR is an easier and more forgiving gun to shoot in combat.

    This is certainly no AK47 vs M-16 difference in accuracy we are talking here.
