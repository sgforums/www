    Here are some facts to consider for people like us brought up to love the Ultimax...

    Belt feed looks complicated. But for our Ultimax, the complicated bits are hidden away inside the drum where you can't see.

    The Ultimax drum is problematic: Why else are they not issued for training?

    Comparing the loaded and unloaded weight between the belt-fed Spanish CETME Ameli and the Ultimax is revealing of the 2 feed systems.


    Ultimax:
    Empty: 4.9kg
    Loaded: 6.8kg (100 rounds drum)
    100 rounds in drum = 1.9kg

    CETME Ameli:
    Empty: 5.3kg
    Loaded: 6.85kg (100 rounds belt in combat box)
    100 rounds in belt & box = 1.55kg

    Weight of a single M193 5.56mm round = 11.79g

    So 100 rounds of M193 will be 1.179kg. This works out to:


    Ultimax drum alone weighs 0.72kg.

    Belt links and box weigh 0.37kg.

    For the Ultimax gunners 3 empty drums will weigh over 2kg. And these empty drums are deadweight he will be carrrying until he can send them back to HQ for reloading.

    And for the belt gunner, this 0.37kg weight are all disposable once the belt is fired off.

    This means that as far as ammo SUPPLY alone is concerned, belts are actually LIGHTER than Ultimax drums!

    If the belt feed mechanism did add some weight to the weapon, once mounted with ammo, both systems evens out, slightly in favour of belts.
