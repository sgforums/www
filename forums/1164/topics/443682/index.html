    Reuters reports that USN eyeing stationing "several" littoral combat ships (LCS) in Singapore.

    http://news.yahoo.com/u-navy-eyes-stationing-ships-singapore-013943985.html

    This was first publicly suggested by former secretary of defense Robert Gates during the Jun 2011 Shangri-La conference but no numbers were suggested then.

    The LCS carries modular mission packages which can comprise either mine countermeasures, anti-submarine or anti-surface (targeting fast attack crafts).

    Its far enough from China not to be seen as targeted at China. Singapore offers good/safe docking facilities with personnel hub transfers from Guam/Diego Garcia. Its also a lot nearer to hotspots eg Gulf or APAC region than US homeports.

    Each LCS carries twice the complement of helos than the Formidable frigates but can share maintenance due to commonalities bet the MH-60s and S-70s. The paucity in AAW is made up by RSAF air cover which would likely be networked-link. The LCS is capable of speeds twice as fast as the Formidables eg 50kt sprint speeds.

    2 classes of LCS have been completed with 20 more on order. Final number may exceed 50. This move will significantly boost ASW/MCM capabilities in this region.
