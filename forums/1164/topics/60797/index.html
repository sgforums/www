    KL buys Italian copter for US$75m
    KUALA LUMPUR -- Malaysia has signed a deal with Agusta SpA of Italy to buy 11 Agusta A109 light reconnaissance helicopters worth RM286 million (US$75 million) for the army's air wing, local media reported on Thursday.

    'We have waited a long time for this,' Defence Minister Najib Razak was quoted as saying by the New Straits Times.

    Advertisement

    He said the helicopter can be used for reconnaissance, security operations, limited fire support, command and control functions, air observation posts as well as search and rescue.

    The helicopters, equipped with 70-millimetre rockets and 20-millimetre machine guns, are expected to be delivered from Dec 2005.

    When the idea of an army air wing was first mooted the focus was on procuring attack helicopters, with previous contenders for the contract being Boeing's Apache attack helicopter and the South African Rooivalk by Denel.

    The contract includes technical support and training.

    Malaysia recently embarked on a major arms spree, agreeing to buy Russian fighter jets for US$900 million, French submarines for $972 million, British and Russian missile systems for $364 million and Polish attack tanks worth $368 million. -- AFP
