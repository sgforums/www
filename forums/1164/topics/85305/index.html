    If it's a terror act by fifth-columnists preceding an invasion force, I dare say a lot of NSmen will already be called back to camp by then.

    The thing is, you don't mobilize such troops on a whim; the respective divisional staffs would've put forth an approved plan - however preliminary - before the activation order goes out. & as long as the foe represents a conventional force - let's say on the orders of an ultra-nationalist or fundamentalist neighbor government - the Singapore government (& local populace) would've taken note of their foreign-policy aggressiveness on the outset.

    I remember when I was doing NS in '86-88, there was overt Malaysian hostility over a series of events - the Israeli PM visit, then GCK's comments about Malays in the army, then a supposed SAF assault-boat intrusion into their waters - which the SAF took seriously, certainly more than the subsequent complaints in the '90's about our planes flying into their territory. We raised our alert status to a high level & was prepared for any eventualities; I experienced the tension, even if it was essentially for deterrent's sake, cuz my unit was RECON 1.

    I expect the SAF will do a thorough review on whether operational readiness would be severely hurt by another such blackout; one area I had in mind - & I stand corrected if I'm wrong on this - is civil-resource requisition, which I believe is only done by open mob(?) under the existing system. They may need to introduce logistical contingencies in terms of recalling all the relevant vehicles.
