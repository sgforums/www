    Indulge me; I didn't elaborate earlier on how our standby forces would react, but here's my take anyway:

    1. The quickest-activated would be the RSAF & the DA BNs (both to secure air superiority), followed by the recalling of RSN fortilla out patrolling. This aspect of the battle could drag on for days, even as the rest of the SAF mobilizes.

    2. The standby ground-troops, while alert, aren't really that bountiful. They'll thus be deployed to dig in somewhere, but serve as frontline blocking positions against the perceived land invasion. Could be lots of scrambling around, depending on what intelligence the Joint Staff gets.

    3. The NSF outfits would reinforce the standby forces, ready to take the fight to the enemy. Ditto the NSman outfits who happened to be attending ICTs. I suppose I'd better give a shout-out to the OCS & BMTC/SISPEC batches as well. They'll be activated for battle; in particular, we'll need to withdraw the Tekong guys back to the mainland to prevent a siege there.

    4. The civilian reservists, upon reporting, would be utilized accordingly - even if only to help out in disaster relief or logistics distribution. Would this mobilization aspect be super-prompt? I'm not that optimistic, since the affected guys have to make serious choices - which in a worse-case mentality is akin to abandoning their parents, leaving wifes to fend for themselves, & possibly never see their children again. Won't put it past certain patriots joining the field-deployed personnel as pseudo-militia too, willing or enforced.

    ~shrugs~ That's how I foresee the SAF in such a crisis-management mode.

    If anyone's interested in how we'll respond to an invasion, I recommend the SAF "Fighting Fit" coffeebook issued about a decade back (way before Douglas Chia's & Wee Ee Mon's novels). It included a fictional short story that briskly namedropped, Tom Clancy style, our formations & equipment (both a bit dated - it had us fighting with SAR-80's) as well as local landmarks against a force attacking from the east.

    FWIW The narration centered around the Battle of Changi (IMO more like East Coast Parkway &, supposedly, Marine Parade) & more romantically, the Battle of Tekong (IMO more like Rocky Hill, with the recruits digged in & waiting for the GDS to heli-drop in as support). The tale ends with a killer punchline of a coda, which I won't reveal here.
