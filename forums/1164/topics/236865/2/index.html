    There's far more to procurement then simply getting the best and biggest guns at the moment... else you might end up like a certain northern country that has a mess of different weapons systems that is a nightmare to keep up with.

    One has to consider the politics, economics and what have you not of the weapons you are buying. You can get the best fighter jet but it ends up costing you a bomb and you realize you lack the skills and manpower to support it... you would find it a lot less useful then if you had used your brains a little and thought through it a little more.

    The merits and quirks of the SAR-21 has been discussed quite a lot in another thread, it would make for an interesting read once you get past some of the heated debate.

    But AFAIK, there does not seem to be any real reason to consider the weapon a flop once all things have been considered. The additional weight of the weapon comes from the nick nacks that in the standard variant and all things factored it the weapon isn't obese compared to it's other counterparts (in fact it is often lighter). As for bullpup versus conventional it is another debate altogether. I am a bullpup person though, and I like the weapon pretty much.

    But going for German rifles? Sure they are popular but given the way things currently are, there is no reason to sub out the SAR-21 for the H&K. I believe one of the things that made the SAR extremely attractive to the SAF was that it was extremely cost effective for the features it provides.

    As for the Igla, I think the rationale behind it went that it has a similar preformance envelope to the Stinger (which was also considered) but was a lot cheaper. Hence by using two Iglas together one gets a higher hit rate then the Stinger at a lower cost... but at a cost in mobility though. But Manpads knocking down Su-30s at high altitude and speed? I'm not sure any Manpad in the world can do it reliably.
