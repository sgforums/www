    11 Feb 07, 11:08
    A list of weapons that I feel should not have been bought:
    Of course, let me stress that its only a personal view. Some may not even count as blunders but of course we all strive for the best.

    SAR-21 Assault Rifles:
    A better choice would be to produce German G36 assault rifles under license. Not that I know about any teething problems associated with the G36. Has most, if not all of the capabilities of the SAR-21 and comes at a reduced weight. It is reliable, hard hitting and accurate. Easy for old timers like us to convert from the M16S1 due to the conventional layout too.

    Vastergotland SSKs:
    No doubt that the 2 upgraded Vastergotlands bought by the RSN are extremely capable boats. But a more straightforward approach would be to simply buy a class of 4 new-built Gotland class, keeping in mind that the 2 remaining Sjoormens need replacement before long and we would bear the burden of having to support an additional type of replacement submarine.

    Quad SA-18 SAMs on M113 APCs:
    A novel, low cost solution indeed. Probably effective against low level air threats but a more effective solution will be needed against higher level threats. A system in the class of the Russian SA-15 will be needed. (Not that I advocate buying Russian arms, but a more capable system with a full range of sensors and 8 missiles per unit is advisable)

    A reliable basic firearm is the most basic requirement for any army. Take the reliability of the Ak-47 which gave the US troops armed with M16s in Vietnam such a nightmare. We cannot compromise defence just to give our local defence industry more market power. Sadly, in the world nowadays, many of such factors are taken in account when procuring weapons.

    And the case of SA-18s being sufficient for our ground troops, it is prudent to note that even a single enemy warplane which gets through the screen of fighters armed with cluster munitions or even no frills HE bombs can kill more then a few of our good men on the ground. There is no excuse for having a top notch air force and a comparatively light SAM coverage for our troops.

    Imagine in a shooting war, when some of our comrades are killed by some lucky enmemy aircraft, we would be cursing at why there is no better SAM coverage. SA-18s are good against low flying helicopters and warplanes. They are about as useful as stones when pitted against Su-30s flying at high altitudes equipped with the latest jammers and electronic countermeasures.
