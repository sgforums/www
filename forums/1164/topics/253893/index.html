    FInally got me the SIG-556 rifle. its a derivative of the SIG-551 series but takes M16 Stanag mags , an M4 type retractable buttstock. I mount an ACOG- TA-01 NSN on it.. the fit and finish is quite good, not as good as the SIG550 series. trigger is smooth like butter.. CAn;t belief a production rifle can have such crisp trigger ! thanks to teh Swiss Engineering. RIfle is in 1:7 twist so it will stabalize the SS109 or M855 62 grain 5.56 ammo and not as well on the 55 grain M193 bullet. BArrel is 16".. rifle feels a little front heavy and with the ACOG it is more balance now. magazine release button is a little far.. compared to the M4. Handguard is Ballistic polymer with PIcatinny rails for your NINJA stuff!

    Will sight in and shoot it this weekend to see how it does.

    A lot of comments saying the SIG 556 looks like M4 or AR15 and looks ugly!... but I feel this is better design to the SIG551 series becasue the folding stock is way too long and you cannot adjust to your shooting preference like with body armor and SBO where you need to shorten the length of the stock.. M16 mag is another good thing than to stock up another magazine type. So! I do prefer the sleek design of the SIG551 series but do like the 556 for its funtionality! I still get the quality of SIG and reliability and yet a lot of familiar AR trades is in it! SO! I trade the good looks of the SIG551 for funtionality of the 556!

    ...you may remember that in SAF, we only co.ck with our right hand as SOP for M16...

    Can someone tell me about the SAR-21 drill? Co.ck with left or right?

    I agree it is actually more logical to co.ck with the left hand as the reloading steps are as follows:

    - RIght hand press mag release
    - Left hand take out empty mag
    - Left hand insert new mag

    Meanwhile, Right hand remains at pistol grip

    So, after inserting new mag with left hand, it is logically faster that the left hand now operate the charging handle. In fact, most HK weapons even put the charging handle forward of the magazine so that it makes it more stable process while the right hand holds the pistol grip.

    But for the M16, it would be very awkward to hold the pistol grip and co.ck as the grip and charging handle are so close together at the rear. So, inevitably, after putting in the new mag you have to change hand and use the right to co.ck.

    But for me, a HK forward left co.cking weapon will be unfamiliar at first.

    In the past, nearly ALL bolt-action rifles were right hand co.cking. Left-handed people now bi.tch about things not being built for them etc but with bolt-action, no left-handedness was entertained. The bolt charging handle was on the left side, period. Loading the rounds into the weapon was usually also from the left without exception.
