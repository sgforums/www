    Arguments in another thread that went out of topic prompted this -

    Officers versus Senior NCOs

    As the opposing camps had stated, there are good and bad officers AND Senior NCOs. I have seen them all, good and bad officers in my battalion, good and bad Senior NCOs (i.e. those holding CSM/BSM appointments and up).

    In my battery, all officers were respected except for the Battery 2IC, a young national service guy who was given the appointment immediately after his commission. He was not particular competent and liked to pull rank. The BSM on the other hand, was a regular and maybe 10 years older and much more experienced, and whom the men listened to.

    I have seen an officer cry, but never a Senior NCO. The Battery 2IC was so hated that none of the NCOs (except for the BSM, whom the Battery 2IC had never crossed sword with) would obey his orders - and thus the crying incident. As you can imagine, he totally lost all our respect since then.

    Any other incidents that inspire respect or disrepect that you have encountered?
