    Several reasons actually.

    The F-16 is a truly mult-role aircraft. Its very capable in both its A2A and A2G role. Just that the pilot may not be as capable.

    Especially when taking on the Wild Weasel role, or SEAD role, a single pilot will be thoroughly stressed when he has to handle : Flying of the aircraft, firing of the HARM or Shrike, checking the EWS for additional threat, and evading the attacking missile if any. All that while trying figure out how many more secs til missile impact. IE, calculating missile closure rate relative to his aircraft's orientation.

    So with 2 guys in the cockpit. The pilot worries about flying and evading the missile, while the WSO can worry about other threats.

    Of course, I think the most fun the WSO has is guiding of PGM via targetting pod, and recording some really cool BDA videos. Again, the Pilot worries about flying, under the guidance of the WSO to the release point, and the WSO keeps and eye on the bomb from there. Fun huh?

    2 heads are better than one.

    Think about it, some of the most established aircraft in the business of dropping PGMs comes with crews of 2. The Strike Eagle, F-4Gs, even the F-14D Bomcats.
