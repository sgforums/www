    Back in the early 70s, the shooter was taught to cover the black target with the foresight, lower the gun barrel till a small space show up between the top of the foresight to the bottom of the black target, then pull the trigger.

    This must be stupid, as the arm of the shooter will have to be rock steady so as not to create different space between the top of the foresight to the bottom of the black target.

    During the 80s, the shooter was taught to aim at the black target, with the top of the foresight at the middle spot of the target.

    This one seems to make more sense, but one has to remember the same approximate middle spot at 100m.

    Used the same approach during a side trip to the Cu Chi tunnels in south Vietnam, I tried firing the AK-47 at different targets located between 100m to 300m.

    Without the gun sight zeroed to my eye, I missed the first three shots - each shot cost US$1.00 a piece for some very old ammo with plenty of duds replaced at no extra cost.

    Making compensation to my aim by shooting low, I managed to hit the 100m target by ricochetting the round off the ground to the target; and seeing the dust burst, I compensated again to get better direct hits at the 100m targets.
