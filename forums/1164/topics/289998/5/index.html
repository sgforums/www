    Subsequent development saw the rifle being manufactured in Britain with its name change to FN SLR when used by the British Forces - as a 'Self-Loading Rifle'


    Above: The standard FN-FAL rifle


    Left to right: The 7.62mm NATO round, the 7.62 intermediate, and the 5.56mm

    The FN SLR was used during the confrontation period, and was a standard issue with our 1 and 2 SIR - when they were sent into active operations in Sarawak, as well as during the hunt for the Indonesian parachutist in Labis, Johor - {when one section was wiped out with only one survivor}.
