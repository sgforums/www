    Depends - is the vehicle suppose to perform the IFV or APC role?
    I would prefer the IFV to be tracked, like in the armour units. The IFV moves in with the supporting armoured infantry. The vehicle is generally heavier, better armoured, and is expected to fight at the front in tactical battles.

    The basic APC role can be done with wheeled amoured vehicle. Much better protection is provided than the 3 or 5 tonners used by infantry units, but still less than tracked ones. The wheeled APC provides transport, support fire (from a fire base) and vehicle patrols.

    Malaysia, for example, uses a lot of wheeled APCs to transport its infantry units. The wheeled APCs provide very good strategic mobility across the North-South highway. Only recently has the focus shifted to armoured units with tracked IFVs.

    IFVs and APCs perform different roles, and in many countries, both tracked and wheeled vehicles are used. Problems occur when wheeled APCs are used as IFVs, in my opinion.
