    "Fire for effect" essentially means no (more) marker rounds needed to adjust fire. The shells are landing on top of the targets & it's time to bomb their brains out!

    FYI There's different types of arty fire missions: registration fire, adjustment fire, fire for effect ("FFE"), harassment & interdiction ("H&I"), time on target ("TOT"), illumination ("illum"), et al.

    The arty officers who follow the infantry are either Forward Observers ("FO", Lieutenant rank) or Fire Support Officers ("FSO", Captain rank), assigned to COY & BN levels respectively. Unlike the Americans, our local versions have Technical Assistants ("TA") who carry the signal sets & may help plan/call the fire missions over the comms. I was both a FSO & FO TA during my NSF.

    A new bird is usually trained to adjust-fire - or with prior luxury time, registration-fire - before executing a FFE, but I suppose immediate FFEs will be the norm come wartime.

    "Fire at will"? That's for the M16's during range shooting, isn't it?
