    I don't see why they should use .357 or .44 Magnum. The 5.7mm cartridge was meant to pierce Kevlar vests, and it did that very well during the tests. I'll need some time to dig up the exact stats, but in any case, I think converting an FN P90 to fire Magnum rounds would not be very practical. First, like you said, the recoil would be a little too strong. Secondly, not only would you have to rebarrel the P90, you'd have to reinforce the chamber, bolt carrier, etc. to deal with the increased pressure of firing a Magnum round. Thirdly, you'd have to develop a caseless Magnum round (not very easy, mind you). Not to mention that the increased bullet weight means less rounds per magazine.

    Also, why would they want to use the P90 on the battlefield? I feel that in an urban environment, a P90 would be an excellent weapon, but in the jungle, or over longer distances a traditional assault rifle would be superior.

    And regarding the Swiss, I think they use the SG series (SG550/551/552). The Belgians probably use the new FN FNC rifle (also 5.56mm).

    Just my two cents.
