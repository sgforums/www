    Looking at the experience of France with the French Foreign Legion and Spain with the Spanish Foreign Legion,why doesn't Singapore embark on a similar venture to create a foreign professional soldiering branch dedicated to peacekeeping duties and operations that are politically sensitive in a domestic context?

    For now,I would only look at the experiences of the French Foreign Legion as they are currently the most established example,having been around since 1831.Its commissioned officers are drawn from the regulars of the French Army,while NCOs and enlistees are drawn from among its recruit pool which comes from overseas.

    This could serve as a good posting for our regulars to rotate through and receive a fair amount of overseas and possibly combat experience,and make it a thoroughly regular arm of the SAF.

    It also offers the option of increasing visibility and power projection capabilities in the world stage,being seen as able to protect our own interests overseas and the mutual interests of those countries we have ties with.

    It would also help provide an avenue for gaining citizens,since the 'big picture' demands that Singapores population grow in volume,so this opens up another avenue to do so.

    It also offers an option to test our weapons and doctrines in combat situations if they do arise and to help rotate combat experience back into the ranks,perhaps rotating personnel from this branch into TRADOC and the Guards/Infantry and Commando formations training branches.

    The contract could be of similar length as the FFL contract,though maybe 6 years is a more reasonable amount,with application for citizenship possible after 3 years.
