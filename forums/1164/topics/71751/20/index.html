    After reading some posts about composites, one could think you wrote about salt or sugar.
    They have shurely some disadvantages compared to metals, but in general they are lighter, more resistant against vibrations and especially in the high end sector don't tend to catastrophic breakdowns.
    Maintainance wise more critical are shurely the Al/Li parts used in the Typhoon. The Russians had large problems with Al/Li alloys, and they have the most experience with this material. But they are only used for leading edges, so that they are probably not welded, which caused most of the problems.
    Further more, composites fatigue at a much lower rate. One possible problem is that components made of composites often occupy more space then comparable parts made of metals.
    What I don't know is how delamination can be checked without destruction of the airframe. For corrosion are many methods known, so this maybe a plus for metals. Ultrasound scannings could be a solution, but here I am no expert, maybe someone else knows more.

    As nearly noone here seems to like Typhoon, I try to give some arguments in favour of it.
    It is supposedly the best fighter for following reasons: Best supersonic acceleration and agility (important for BVR angagements). The only contender beeing able to supercruise. Better radar tracking range compared to Rafale, comparable performance to APG 63. TVC already tested, intended for integration in tranche 3 (improves not only agility, but STOL capabilities, power output, reduces drag,....)
    For WVR engagements, Iris-T and ASRAAM are available. The Libelle flight suit is the most modern available.

    The man hours are phenomenal low, well below the 9 mh/fh from the contract.

    Many weapons from different countries are available for Typhoon, including U.S. weapons already used by the RSAF. It is the only platform armed with the most modern western ARM's, including HARM(USA), ARMIGER(Germany) and ALARM(UK).
    There will be three different AShM's available: Kongsberg NSM(Norway/UK), Harpoon(US) and (probably) RBS15Mk3 (Sweden/Germany).
    Nearly the same can be said about ALCM's and PGM's.

    For the Rafale and the Eagle only one source for weapons and spares is available. (The proposed F-15T has an different weapon's system, so Israel is not for shure a source). U.S. weapons available for export will be integrated in Typhoon. Examples are JDAM and the Paveway series. If the 4 European EF-nations don't get it, than Singapore won't get it, too. Additional, the four EF-partners integrate many different weapons. For example Germany uses the Taurus ALCM, while Italy and UK use the Stormshadow/ScalpEG which can be found on the Rafale, too.
