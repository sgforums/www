    We have done this ages ago.

    Eventhough the Brazilian company did not disclose the CEP, for a totally unguided rocket system like Astros 2 , it should have a Circular Error Prob. (CEP) of at least 1% of range.

    For example , the similar Chinese WS-1B is 1~1.5%, the Indian Pinaka is reported to be abt 1~2%.

    Eg, at range of 89km with 1% CEP of range, the Prob Error (PE) is 89km x 1% =890m.

    CEP was actually based on the Gaussian distribution, where 50% will fall within 1PE, 32% will fall between 1~2 PE ,14% will fall within 2~3 PE and lastly 4% will fall bewteen 3~4 PE.

    Since the actual CEP of Astros is unknown, but such unguided system at best is having 1% CEP, so give it a benefit of doubt , say 1%.

    Thus at maximum range of 89km , the Astros will have :

    50% will fall within 890m from the target,
    32% will fall within 890m~1.78km from the target,
    16% will fall within 1.78km~2.67km from the target,
    and lastly will fall within 2.67km~3.56km from the target.

    Remember, 1% is the best CEP for such unguided rocket system, the Astros 2 could be much worse.
