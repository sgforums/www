    Actually - to be a.nal - a 7.62 x 51mm NATO gun is technically not the same as a .30 gun.

    .30 refers to the WW2 US 30-06 rifle/MG round.

    .30 also refers to the WW2 M1 carbine.

    And the 7.62mm NATO is different from all the WW2 7.62mm rounds even though the calibre is similar.

    .308 Wincester is the other name for the 7.62 NATO calibre.

    So in order to avoid confusion, 7.62 NATO is not referred to as a .30.

    There is also a WW1/WW2 MG commonly referred to as the .30 cal and that's the M2 Browning's little brother the Browning M1919.



    The .50 continued to be called that because neither the gun nor the ammo had changed since its inception.

    For the same reasons of avoiding confusion, the Soviet Dshk is referred to as a 12.7mm HMG, the term .50 is seldom used when referring to the Soviet HMG. Even though the calibre is the same diameter, the rounds are different.
