    I've asked this question here before, but never got any useful answers.

    Some even insists that the AMX-13 had no room for a .50 to be mounted. I refuted this wild guess by posting a pic of a foreign AMX-13 with a turret roof-mounted .50 - with room to spare.

    We may call the AMX-13 a light tank but when you actually climb onto it, you realise how big it is externally.

    The only satisfactory answer I got from one post was that the AMX-13 never operate alone, and will always be supported by other IFV like Bionix or Ultra. And these support vehicles will have plenty of CIS 50, 25mm cannons, SAMs etc. What the AMX-13 3-man crew should concentrate on is using its 75mm gun on targets that require this firepower.

    Still, I am pro putting the CIS 50 on tanks. There is already a GPMG mounted co-axial to the 75mm. Unless the reason is that there are no more room to store enough ammo for the .50.

    Most of the time the stated purpose of the .50 on the turret roof is air defense. This is when SHTF and you're alone, you have something to shoot at enemy helicopters. Or if you're out of 75mm ammo and you see a enemy IFV...The CIS 50 can fire Saboted Light Armour Piercing rounds which would be useful against lighter vehicles.

    But this is all moot as we are now getting Leo 2s. So it will be interesting to see if there will be a CIS 50 on the Leo turret roof.
