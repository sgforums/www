    So do not underestimate the potential value of Russian style MBTs used by potential adversaries against us. Unfortunately our own military is still too steeped in the Western style of warfare tactics to allow for a radical paradigm shift in their own doctrines.

    Originally posted by spencer99:
    As for the weight of MBTs being too heavy for roads in the region, this is a peacetime consideration only. In war who cares!

    Er, no, it's not a peacetime consideration only. If the roads collapse under the weight of your tanks, your vehicles' mobility will be appreciably reduced, and if the ground beneath the road has been extensively excavated for urban architecture like pipes, electrical and telephone lines, etc, you may find your tanks getting stuck in the ground!

    Obviously, this will be less of a problem with respect to, say, roads in rural areas, but in urban or suburban areas, where the ground has been hollowed out quite a bit to make way for considerable underground construction, it would be a real issue.

    And then of course, there's also the problem of bridging - most bridges in this part of the world, which is dominated by rural terrain, are simply not designed to bear the weight of 40-50 ton MBTs.

    In Europe and the Middle East (the theatres of war in which most MBTs have been designed to operate), this is not really a problem because there is enough open terrain such that MBTs can forego the roads and just travel cross country. In SE Asia, which is mostly forested, this is clearly not an option.
