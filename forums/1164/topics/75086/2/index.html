    The utilization of unmanned units in future engagements is not a matter of if we can do it, but a matter of it we want to. It is certainly not far fetched, non of the problems we have with them today are due to any fundemental flaws in their operating concept, but a matter of time when technology improves, they will be solved.

    In the end, why do we want to devote a number of resources towards getting unmanned technology? Because it offers a ton of advantages in battle. A UAV can be replaced rapidly, and in many cases cost less then the SAM that shot it down, but to replace a recon scout who had to scout to forward locations just to get a weak view of the enemy is a lot more expensive, harder, and drains our labour pool.

    Micro UAVs can even be one of the tools used by our recon scouts to find out what's ahead of him, it will certainly improve their line of sight.

    It's far from far fetched, it's like saying using missiles to arm our aircraft is far fetched because we have to create a whole new class of workers to handle their related issues. No, when you do the math, the cost is more then worth it.

    In the end why do we want a networked army with unmanned eyes? Because the army that can see through the fog of war better, and diffuse the information along to the right people faster, will be the one who will ultimately respond faster, make better and more informed desisions, and outwit a slower, blinder opponent, which as anyone can see, will be a great advantage for the side which holds these cards.

    In a battle soldiers would certainly like to know where that sniper is, or were that machine gun position is instantly over a network via imaging software instead of somebody risking their lives to relate that information by voice, or better, using such networked technology to take out the threat without even having a LOS to it. Like send a non-LOS missile tied in to an aquostic or microradar sniper detection system to a sniper nest without even exposing yourself.

    Worth it? Certainly. We should not shy away from developing these technologies because of the cost, but put more resources into them to make sure when cruch time comes, we are as far forward of the field as we can.
