  • panda_eyes
    23 Jan 04, 12:23
    Nobody knows for sure who will make it to become a pilot, or to pinpoint all the characteristics required to be a good pilot. Having the right attitude (ie willingness to learn) and having the aptitude (hand-eye coordination etc.) are important factors that instructors screening pilot trainees look for, but more importantly you need to have the interest in flying. Unfortunately no one truly know whether they will have a genuine interest in flying until they have tasted flight itself.
    At the end of it all, it is a combination of factors that determine if anyone makes it as a pilot. No one knows for sure what they all are, that's why they label it the X-factor.
