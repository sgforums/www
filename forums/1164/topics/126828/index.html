    possibly for any other country, but not for the united states and anyone who works with her in joint ops.
    each singular role platform can perform to its best capabilities that one role - and be focused on it without having to worry about anything else that a multi-role would have to.

    multi-role is cost-effective, but not always combat effective.
    singular role also means you travel in a package - and multi targets are harder to engage than just one..
    imagine a multi-role platform has to roll in, take out the aerial defenses before it can get to the target, cope with aerial combatants, and then do it all again on its return journey.
    a singular-role platform can go in and just focus on say bombing the target, while another knocks out all the aerial defenses while several more keep aerial combatants at bay - that way theres a higher percentage of aircraft coming home, and of the mission being a success.
    Wild Weasel missions in the Vietnam war proved that this kind of concept was effective - before, when a mulit-role aircraft had to everything, it was basically being tied down because it was trying to do too much - and it became a much easier target for doing so..

    example package: BUFF or Lancer goes in, with maybe two bombers, two 15s for fighter cap, 16s carry harms for air defense suppression [modern day wild weasels], and a couple of 15s and 16s for extra bombing.
    the bombers can get on with their role and concentrate on dropping more ordnance than any of the other multi-role aircraft put together..and dont have to carry other ordnance, because the 15s and 16s are taking care of that..

    multi-roles are good for smaller ops, maybe less lethal ops, and surgical strikes, and they do well for countries that cannot afford everything that goes
    with the modern mixed bomber package that the us model has [and has been shown to work very effectively].

    Not to say that the us doesnt operate multi-role aircraft effectively - they do - these are fielded faster and more effectively than any of the bomber fleet could, simply because their role doesnt cover say what a 15 or 16 or even an 18 does, they can be on-station and doing whatever thats required of them fast.
    but get them on a multi-bomber mission dropping huge amounts of ordnance, maybe over a wide area? get the BUFFs and Lancers [and perhaps now the B-2's] for that..
