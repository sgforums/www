    IMO You should be safe if you're a junior commander whose name gets included within the main-body roster, & the whole unit gets redeployed to inactive status after your training cycle. Not being commissioned, I do not know if you'll get extraenous call-ups for doctrinal updates & maybe shooting competitions & stuff.

    Recycling happens for 2 reasons: if you're a newbie who joins an older reservist unit & thus must compete his training cycle with another outfit, or if you're a KAH. The training cycle for KAHs is 16 years, not 13; that's why COs & RSMs & S1-4's don't stand down even after they've completed every ICT with the main body. In any case, if you've noticed, they don't tend to be from your NSF batch either; they appear to serve according to a different timetable.

    Post-training cycle, you can opt to serve on under the ROVERS scheme, but has to commit initially 3 more years. The NSPC is supposed to contact such eligible reservists after 12 years on whether they want to carry on.
