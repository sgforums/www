    2 Feb 03, 00:38
    Not every unit in the SAF is sloppy and slack. If yours is, your officers are killing you with kindness. But it depends on what kind of slack you are talking about.

    The discipline of the SAF in general is considered very good. Note that unlike what you see in movies, every army in the world has sloppy and slack parts. US Marines, SAS commandoes, everyone. The vast majority of soldiers are lazy and afraid to die. The vast majority of units neglect training and are totally SNAFU. This is perfectly normal.

    What counts is whether at the vital moment they will perform. When your unit needs to push during an exercise, do you follow orders and push? Do you possess the basic skills needed to fight? If you do, it makes you better than the average soldier, and makes up for all the chao genging, admin fuckups and other day-to-day stupidity at camp.

    The SAF trains its men very well, better than the US Army, though not as well the USMC IMHO. The same CDOs you see sleeping in camp all day (they seem to have the most senang camp life) regularly perform better in exercises and tests than US SEALS and Special Forces. The same softie pilots at our airbases regularly top the class at Top Gun.

    So just focus on your training. The army around you is better than you think it is, and as long as you do your part we will all pull through in a war. Make sure you know your stuff, and have the ability to use this knowledge. If you are an infantryman (like me), make sure you can use and care for your weapons and know how to move in a firefight. Build up your stamina, and learn to be alert at all times. Confidence in your training, condition and equipment will build courage. It may save your life someday.

    Regarding our armor forces vs Malaysia's: I will believe they are getting MBTs when they actually get them, and I see them moving and firing. They have a tendency to buy equipment they are unable to support or effectively use, especially in the airforce. When the purchasing decision comes from the politicians rather than the armed forces, the chance of such a screwup is multiplied.

    Even if they do get all of these MBTs operational, I do not think they are that much of a threat. MBTs need a lot of support to operate effectively, and without this support they are useless. I seriously doubt that Malaysian logistics are up to the task of keeping an MBT force fed with gas, ammunition and maintenance. Or that their engineers are ready with enough breaching and bridging equipment to prevent the force from being bogged down by mines or destroyed bridges and slaughtered from the air.

    In addition, contrary to what many on this forum think, these tanks CAN be defeated by our AMX13SM1s and even our LAWs, let alone Spikes, Hellfires, mines or DPICM. You need to be much more careful with shot placement, which is not hard at the close ranges of likely battle. There isn't a tank in the world yet that can take a hit from an anti-tank weapon to a hatch, the engine grille without brewing up. There isn't yet a set of tracks that can take an ATW hit without resulting in a mobility kill. Even a hit that fails to penetrate may result in a vision kill. Tanks are far from invincible. They need to be deployed in sufficient numbers to be effective, since they will suffer attrition in battle no matter how thick their armor. The number that Malaysia is buying is too small.

    And of course, we DO have MBTs of our own, more than Malaysia will even after this acquisition.
