    That's the problem with sub-launched missiles. How are you going to target them? The sub cannot "see" that far while it is submerged, and if it wants to get targetting data from other ships or satellites, it must come close to the surface and put up a UHF or VHF antenna, which reveals its position. While submerged it can only use LF radio, which has bandwidth of about 10 bits per minute. After the sub launches the missile, its position is revealed to everyone within radar range, not just ships within sonar range for the case of a torpedo, which is suicide. No matter how you cut it, sub-launched ASMs are suicide for the sub. Land attack cruise missiles and ballistic missiles are OK, since their targets don't move, but missiles meant to engage moving targets -- no way.
