    Ok...

    To clarify tt,

    IPPT and SOC. There seems to be a common misconception that IPPT and SOC has to be passed before selection into OCS. I've seen ppl who have NOT passed SOC and got in. One has to clear IPPT to be certified as graduating from BMT. That is the criteria. So OCS has no criteria which overwrites this basic rule. As for SOC, I'm not too sure about it...Maybe someone in BMT would care to clarify?

    SIS!??!. It's called the SITest aka Situaional Test. This tests the recruit's judgement of scenarios painted to him, and it really is a litmus test for whether someone is really cut out to lead, or just there to follow instructions.

    Recommendation. Yes. Firstly, during the 3x PC/PS interviews (initial, during and after), you HAVE to state your desire to undergo OCS/sispec training. On top of that, there are the peer surveys which see how one ranks in his plt, then his coy, then the school. This is because, when all figures are consolidated (SITest Results, Field Camp Results, ATP, SOC, IPPT etc) they will be ranked and the top 1% (I think) will get into OCS, followed by NSPI, SISPEC, etc...
