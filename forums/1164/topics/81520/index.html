    Originally posted by Langley:
    Not sure about this but think officer can sign on for 3 years, NS included. There should be other schemes where one need not sign on for ten years that long. Can consider if really need money.
    Negative. One does not sign three years including NS, meaning serving an extra 1/2 year after your NS liability. Rather, the 3-yr contract for officer starts from the day he/she commisions and runs for three years after that, making it a total of slightly over 4 years.

    The current scheme for officers is the SAVER scheme. It's not a long term contract but there is still a minimum time (about 5 years?) you must stay, after which you can choose to leave after giving one months notice.
