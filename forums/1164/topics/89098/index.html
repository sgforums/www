    there's no such thing as a "master freefall" course. a master freefaller is merely someone who's done the requisite number of freefall jumps (somewhere around 25 for master, iirc. i think i saw it somewhere recently; i'll try and check).
    unless you mean the advanced military freefall course. for freefall, there's only that and the military freefall course.

    the yellow backing means he's an instructor ( not a jumpmaster as someone said ). jumpmasters don't have any special distinction on their badges.

    red ranger = singapore ranger course
    yellow = us ranger
    the camo ranger badges are the same for both local and us courses, difference being that for both no.3 and no.4 badges, the us ranger tab is worn on the right sleeve while the local ranger tab is on the left sleeve (same as for foreign badges).

    to expound on that a little the difference between a 'normal' parachutist and a commando parachutist is that commandos ( and divers too ) go for module II of the bac which is the combat load phase. 'normal' parachutists are only trained to jump in clean fatigues - hence there isn't really any practical reason for them to undergo the course except to fulfill the confidence course requirement for regs.
    the red backing actually denotes the combat-load phase; but since generally only commandos do that, its akin to being a 'commando parachutist'.
