    OZ Defence Dept outlines benefits in Singapore defence training
    [http://www.abc.net.au/news/newsitems/200506/s1396010.htm]

    Australia's Defence Department says training in Australia by Singapore's Armed Forces offers important benefits to Australia and the region.

    The department was giving evidence to a parliamentary hearing on a five-year extension of Singapore's right to conduct military exercises in the Shoalwater Bay Training Area near Rockhampton in central Queensland.

    The proposed treaty allows Singapore to deploy up to 6,600 troops for a maximum of 45 days a year.

    A deputy secretary of the Defence Department, Shane Carmody, says Singapore uses sophisticated technology and is a high quality exercise partner for Australia.

    "With inadequate training areas in Singapore, the use of the Shoalwater Bay Training Area allows the Singaporean Armed Forces to hone their military skills and maintain their technologically advanced capability," he said.

    "This technology benefits Australia by making Singapore a more effective coalition partner - for example in the five-power defence arrangements - and [as] a contributor to regional security."

    Mr Carmody says Australia does not have the same defence training links elsewhere in South-East Asia.

    He says not many others in the Association of South-East Asian Nations could offer the same level of sophisticated military technology as Singapore.

    "Within the agreement, over 6,000 troops can be deployed," he said.

    "It is a very high-end exercise and so we have not considered inviting anyone else, mainly because I think, there's no-one else that is operating quite at that level and this relationship does provide us the opportunity to do something special for Singapore."
