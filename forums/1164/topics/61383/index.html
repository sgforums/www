Australia set to upgrade defence force

  • IAF
    7 Nov 03, 23:59
    CANBERRA -- Australia's military has outlined a 10-year plan to buy new tanks, ships and helicopters to defend against terror threats, the government said on Friday.

    A strategic review in February recommended making the military more mobile and able to operate in South-east Asia and the South Pacific in response to new worries about terrorism and weapons of mass destruction.

    The new equipment will ensure the military can 'defend Australia and Australian interests in an uncertain and complex environment,' said Defence Minister Robert Hill.

    He would not reveal the expected cost of the purchases, planned over 10 years.

    Mr Hill said the army will replace its ageing Leopard tanks, and was considering three options: the American M1 Abrams tank, the British Challenger 2, or the latest German Leopard.

    The army will also get more helicopters for ship-based land attacks.

    The navy will get three new destroyers and new missile systems for its frigates. It will also replace its main troop carrier and a helicopter troop-lift ship with larger vessels.

    The air force will start retiring its 40-year-old F-111 fighter jets in 2010, five years earlier than planned. It will also replace it AP-3C Orion maritime patrol planes with more modern aircraft. -- AP
