    Originally posted by moca:
    Dave & Meia

    I'll assume you are both US citizens. So what kinds of background checks before you can carry a concealed firearm? Similarly, how do you qualify to own automatic weapons?
    Background checks are only part of the overall process required for a private citizen to obtain a carry license for a handgun. The application process can vary from state to state; I can only describe what the procedure is like for the state of Minnesota.

    Citizens and permanent residents (a.k.a. "green card" holders) can apply for a carry license. You first have to attend a state-sanctioned firearms safety course. Such courses typically cover the situations and circumstances under which you are legally justified in using deadly force to protect yourself. The more practical aspects of daily carry pertaining to the proper use of holsters and concealment techniques are usually also covered. Candidates are also assessed on their firearms handling competency and have to complete a live fire exercise in which they are scored on accuracy. The successful completion of the course results in the issuance of a certificate that is valid for one year.

    The applicant then has go in person to the county sheriff's office to fill in an application form for a carry permit. The certificate is attached and a $100 "processing fee" paid. The sheriff's office then performs all the necessary city, state and federal background checks, and if they come back clean, he has to issue you the carry permit within 30 business days by law.

    Minnesota legally changed its carry permit process from "may issue" to "shall issue" back in 2003, after a long and drawn-out lobbying effort by gun rights advocacy groups that lasted many years. "May issue" means that the issuing authority (in most cases, the county sheriff) can deny your application despite your having met all the legal requirements; "shall issue" means that he is legally obliged to issue the permit if your meet all the necessary requirements.
