    F/A-22 Raptor gets green light
    The U.S. Department of Defense has given the OK to Lockheed Martin Corp. to start full-rate production of the F/A-22 Raptor stealth fighter.

    The aircraft is built at LockheedÂ´s (NYSE: LMT) Marietta aeronautics facility and should reach initial operational capability in December 2005 at Langley Air Force Base, Va.

    "The Under Secretary of Defense (Acquisition, Technology, and Logistics) [Michael W. Wynne] approved the full rate production capability of the F/A-22," according to an acquisition decision information paper released by the Department of Defense on April 18.

    This F/A-22 program milestone follows initial operational test findings in February and March by both the Air Force and the Department of Defense, which judged the aircraft to be "overwhelming effective" in its performance. Air Force pilots will be able to dominate any engagement with the Raptor and the F/A-22 will provide protection for troops no matter where they are, to an extent never before possible, Bethesda, Md.-based Lockheed said.
