    THis have been discussed in the tank thread.

    there are various versions of the sweddish IFV.

    * CV9040 - The original model with a 40mm Bofors autocannon
    * CV9030 - Export version with a 30 mm Bushmaster II autocannon. (Adopted by Norway, Switzerland and Finland)
    * CV9035 - Armed with a Bushmaster III 35/50 cannon. (Adopted by the Netherlands and Denmark)
    * CV90105 - 105 mm smoothbore tank turret/gun.
    * CV90120-T - Has a tank turret armed with a smoothbore 120 mm gun.
    * CV9040 AAV - Anti-Air Vehicle, fitted with radar and 40 mm AA gun.
    * CV90 Forward Command Vehicle
    * CV90 Forward Observation Vehicle
    * CV90 Armored Recovery Vehicle
    * CV9040C Made for UN peace keeping forces with add on armour.
    * CV9056 Trial version equipped with the Bofors RB56 anti-tank missile. Not able to carry any infantry. Project cancelled.

    I think 105 mm is the optimum calibre because it is still in widespread use and is / was a NATO standard,. That means there are a lot of rounds ( with very wide range of specialized & versatile ammo ) out there available. Also, the latest ( although developed many years back ) 105 mm APFSDS rounds are still very potent against MBTs.

    If our future LTs are primarily for fire support, recon etc 105 will be ideal as it delivers a lot of firepower for these roles and also retains an anti MBT capability. Also there is some development of new 105 ammo types ( eg those developed by Israel recently ) which expands the capability of the 105 further.

    Putting a full capability 105 on a small & light Bionix chassis will be tough. Probably need to change & strengthen the chassis design somewhat to accomodate a 105. No point stressing the existing design if it means stretching design limits - remember the Sheridian LT with the 155 gun ?
