    Insufficient evidence to suggest sniper shot at school: Police

    THOUGH there are unexplained "small holes" on damaged windowpanes at Chestnut Secondary School, police have found no bullet holes or any other markings on the walls of the school.

    Police spokesman Victor Keong issued this clarification in response to Today's front page story titled "Shots fired at secondary school".

    Singapore Police Force Assistant Director Keong also clarified that "to date" there was "insufficient evidence to suggest the presence of a sniper", and that no air gun pellets or projectiles had been found at the school.

    Today had reported yesterday that students of Chestnut Secondary had not been informed of a series of six shots that had been fired at the school since April. The latest incident, which happened on Tuesday, had damaged a windowpane on the third floor.

    Principal Chia Mee Lan had also told Today that all necessary precautions had been taken to ensure the safety of students and that teachers had been told not to let students sit too close to windows until police find the culprit.

    Police said yesterday that they were still establishing the cause of the damage to the windowpanes.
