  • Moxie
    1 Nov 04, 15:55
    There's none, even if you aren't harkening for a Tom Clancy-level parallel. Douglas Chia & Wee Ee Hon ("Ko Island") are the only local writers I can think of whose recent publications dealt with some sort of SAF sensibility. There was also this NS account by an author whose name currently escapes me, with "Air Level" featured prominently in the title - will let you know on both tomorrow. Plus, I posted before that Mickey Chiang included an engagingly "realistic" short story that opened the SAF "Fighting Fit" coffee book of 1990.

    IMO The best localized narratives - not necessarily fictional - are still those which depicted the Emergency Years. In particular, I recommend a couple: Noel Barber's "War of the Running Dogs" &, my favorite, F. Spencer Chapman's "The Jungle Is Neutral". (Found $1 copies of both at a Bras Basah bookshop this year.) Of course, the SAF didn't exist then & most of the dramatic action took place in the Malayan jungles.
