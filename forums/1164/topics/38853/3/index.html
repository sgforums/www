    INAUGURATION OF PEACE VANGUARD DETACHMENT

    Deputy Prime Minister and Minister for Defence, Dr Tony Tan officiated at the inauguration ceremony of the Republic of Singapore Air Force's (RSAF) Peace Vanguard Apache Helicopter detachment in Marana, Arizona, United States today (Singapore time). The inauguration of the RSAF Apache squadron marks a milestone in the development of the SAFÂ’s attack helicopter capabilities.

    The Peace Vanguard detachment was initiated in Marana with the assistance of the Arizona Army National Guard in October 2001. The Peace Vanguard Programme gives the RSAF Apache squadron valuable access to firing ranges and training. The Apache squadron will enhance the Singapore Armed ForcesÂ’ (SAF) operational capabilities and advance its efforts to build an integrated fighting force. This will also enhance the SAF's ability to deal with a spectrum of non-conventional threats.

    The Peace Vanguard detachment currently has 8 Apache Longbow Attack Helicopters (AH-64D) and 118 RSAF personnel. The training with the US Army will enable the RSAF Apache squadron to attain the level of operational readiness required of equivalent US Army Apache units.

    The RSAF received its first AH-64D on 17 May 2002. The AH-64D Apache Longbow helicopters, equipped with targeting radar and a formidable array of armaments, are deployed as frontline tank busters and reconnaissance platforms.

    RSAF detachments in the US reinforce the longstanding and close defence relations between Singapore and the United States. They have helped to develop inter-operability and enhance understanding between the two countriesÂ’ armed forces.
