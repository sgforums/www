    Yup, the watch isn't any good if it's not going to stay on your wrist. Compare the Luminox to real military issue watches like the milspec Rolex Submariner - the Sub's solid springbars are welded to the lugs so there's zero chance of them coming off, and when it's on the proper NATO strap, even if one of the strap's loops arounf the springbars gives way, the other still holds the watch in place.

    I seriously doubt that the Luminox would pass the testing that the Royal Navy used on the Sub in the 1960s.
  • specfore
    19 Dec 07, 22:04
    I am not from CDO or equivalent "tough" vocation. But I found that if you have a light weight watch, it will survive bashing quite a lot.

    During reservist, I used a Swatch diver's watch. The fact that it was light weight means it can stand a lot of g-shock. And the fact that it was waterproof to 25m helps in normal infantryman's water crossing, wading in sea...etc etc. It also has a large luminous face which helps at night.

    The problem with a heavy complex watch is that it will not stand g-forces if it was not properly designed for it. Worse still if it is an automatic ( not a light quartz mechanism).

    Afterall, a second hand Swatch on eBay now costs about $50. So if it breaks, get another.
