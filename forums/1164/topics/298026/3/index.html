    Hmm, pre-owned Tudor? The Tudor Subs are all getting long in the tooth, so if you do get one make sure you get it serviced before putting it through its paces, and I'd recommend putting it on a NATO strap just in case one of the springbars gives way (not that I've ever seen a Rolex/Tudor springbar do that). You may want to consider getting a Hydronaut instead since it succeeded the Sub and would be newer, requiring less maintenance.
