    For those going to AA by public transport, one way is to take the SBS shuttle bus from Singapore Expo. The pickup/drop-off point is next to the MRT station. The fare is $2 cash (no ezlink), and it includes the return trip to Expo from Changi. The bus takes approximately 20-25 mins each way. Expect to wait for a bit while the bus passes thru the security checkpoint.

    Plane addicts disappointed with show

    http://straitstimes.asia1.com.sg/singapore/story/0,4386,237301,00.html?

    Who they are: Low Jer Wei, 35, systems engineer; Sean Chua, 34, product designer; Philip Koh, 31, accounts manager.

    What they do: These hardcore aviation addicts call themselves wingnutz, devour aviation magazines, meet at hobby shops on weekends and own hundreds of aeromodels. They've been to every Asian Aerospace show, or nearly all.

    Which was the best?

    Philip: 1994. The Russian Sukhoi aircraft did a very beautiful cobra-like manoeuvre. And the United States also showed off everything it had.

    Sean: 2000 - the French aerobatic team was here.

    How about this year?

    Jer Wei: Disappointing. The Typhoon is only a mock-up. Langkawi's airshow last year had a longer aerial display and more stunts, planes flew closer to the ground.

    Sean: Too little flying. Singapore should have contributed F-16s and Skyhawks since we are the hosts.

    Why come on a trade day?

    Sean: The exhibitors would have kept a lot of things from the public or gone home by this weekend. Trade days aren't so crowded and I want to take close-ups of the French Rafale and Mirage fighters for my research.

    Which of the three fighters on Singapore's shortlist would you pick?

    Philip: Boeing's F-15. It's more rugged.

    Jer Wei: Rafale. It's proven in service with the French air force and navy.

    Sean: The Typhoon, if I had to choose from the three, because it's a Generation 4 fighter with a seven-tonne payload. But I'd prefer Russia's Sukhoi Su-30 Flanker. Underrated, very rugged and pure titanium.

    Any tips for airshow visitors?

    Jer Wei: Use sun screen. Tour the static exhibits outside in the morning or evening. Cool down in the hall in the afternoon.

    Sean: Don't drive. I parked in Tampines and took a bus here. Bring a hat, binoculars, digital camera and lots of water. Watch the aerial display from Changi Beach.

    Philip: Don't bring your girlfriend.

    -- Goh Chin Lian
