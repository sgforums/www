Furthermore, if our comms network is jammed or down, u may end up with fire from the 120mm mortar only, which is lacking in range. Furthermore, it will be faster and more efficient to get support from BDE as it is a shorter chain of command. Look at the recent Afghan war, rockets are widely used by the Taliban, which proves you do not need special skills to operate these equipment, which is a gd news for our conscript army. By not having a large MLRS like those in MAF, u do not need extensive machines and manpower to load them, as small calibre can be easily loaded by small frame Asian. No doubt shorter in range for smaller calibre, we could use these rockets to create a final 'shock' on the enemy terrain before the deliberate assault. These rockets may not be accurate, but the intensive, continuous saturated attacks can make the enemy 'balls to shrink in fear'.

    I think the main advantage of rocket arty over gun arty is that you can put down a larger volume of fire within a short span of time.

    I think it's disadvantages are lower accuracy and not suitable for sustained bombardment.

    For the terrain around our region, I think it is difficult to find open wide terrain where you can catch the enemy troops in the open. So the opportunities for short intense fire looks low.

    For close fire support, as an infantry men, I wouldn't want rockets to do the bombardment. Drop on me how?
    But if we want to dislodged dugged-in soldiers and in urban area, we need accurate and sustainablement bombardment.

    If the issue is not enough capacity for fire missions, then why not just add more guns instead of more rockets? You can add more guns to division, regiment or battlion levels. Wouldn't adding different firing platform and ammunitions only complicate the logistics more?

    The only need I can think of choosing rocket arty over gun arty is guided munitions. Such as if there is a formation of tanks found on the move, then the rockets can be launched to dispense guided sub-anti-tank munitions againts the tanks.
