    Depends on unit. Generally speaking... because the training programme is packed into shorter ICTs (compared to the old days ... pre-1990), it can be quite hectic. Plus nowadays, there is less emphasis on discipline (haircut, dress code). But does not mean you don't get a scolding or some kind of retribution from the seniors.

    Maybe those you saw were in storeman vocations ... there are off-duty periods. Or maybe the commanders are not managing their men's time properly. When I was doing reservist, our CO let the men off in the afternoon, on the understanding that they book in by a certain time. So they can go and visit the nearby shopping centre and do whatever they need to do outside and not lounge around in the camp and cause embarrassment.

    For the officers and specs, there are briefings and meetings, so not so lucky. However, we get our time off in other periods , e.g. when the men are doing some in-camp admin, that is supervised by the regular cadre in the camp, and does not require our presence.

    All boils down to the management of everyone's time.
