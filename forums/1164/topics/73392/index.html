    WITS on our LMTV/HMTV

    The existing Light and Heavy Maintenance Task Vehicles (MTVs) have certain drawbacks such as insufficient lifting capacity, insufficient carrying capacity and the lack of power tools. They were unable to support the current operational requirement efficiently and thus, created a maintenance capability gap.

    The WITs had identified two suitable platforms from the present fleet of SAF 'B' vehicles to be modified for use as MTVs. The facilities and accessories for the MTVs were also identified. Two prototypes MTVs, based on the FIAT 3-Ton Vehicle and the U1300L Unimog, were developed with sufficient on-board lifting capability, enhanced diagnostic and payload capacities necessary to operate independently for prolong periods in the forward battle area.

    It is a vehicle for the field maintenance battalions to provide fix-forward repair as well as battle damage assessment and repair capability in support of the combat, artillery, engineers and armoured forces in the forward areas. Besides, this current fleet of in-service vehicles, which are about 10 years old, are still in good condition and will not require major maintenance until 10 - 15 years later.

    In order to calculate the cost avoidance, the WIT then used a similar platform from the US Army as a basis for comparison. Based on the total number of MTVs needed, the cost is approximately $90 million for the new buy. The cost incurred for this project is approximately $40.898m, resulting in a cost avoidance of $49.102 million.
