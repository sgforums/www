    I can offer some personal input; my brother just made LTC recently, at age 37. He was a NSF commissioned officer, but didn't sign on until after he ORD'd & have completed his NTU degree course. It was interesting tracking his career; he wasn't a shooting star by any means, but got promoted at roughly the "right" stages. There's a underlying professional momentum affecting all regulars, & the status perception is being "condemned" if they don't attain a certain rank by a certain age (esp. prior to getting the 1st crab.)

    Finished OCS - 2LT
    Before ORD - LTA
    Mid-20s - CPT, usually with a command stint at the company level or equivalent
    29/30/31 years old - MAJ

    Up to this rank, the promotions road is generally consistent & predictable; a regular officer would also tend to finish his 1st bond commitment by the end of this period. Thereafter, there's more of a "politics" element that come into play, since there's only so much available appointments for MAJ or higher, & a candidate would have to compete with the scholars who're on the fast track. It also takes place more in the staff-officer (not combat-) arena, so your track record of requisite training courses is also a big factor. My brother also used the opportunity around this stage to finish a Master's degree in Public Policy.

    As for Lee Hsien Loong - & also the other Big 4 military scholars/politicians (George Yeo, Teo Chee Hean, Lim Hng Kiang, Lim Swee Say) - I think their rapid SAF achievements have more to do with the fact that Singapore was trying to create a professional officer corps as quickly as possible from the early 70's onwards. It's a credit to them that they didn't fail the test.

    Prior to that, the pioneer officers were either remnants of the SVC & SMF - 1SIR/2SIR (& national service in general) were initiated with self-government in the late 50's, rather than independence in '65 - or commissioned with the new SAFTI-trained batch. The military in the early days was also PAP-controlled; flip through the pages of the Singapore Artillery 100th Anniversary book & you'll see Goh Keng Swee reviewing troops - in military dress as a "Honorary Colonel"!

    Thus, there's fast track for today's SAF scholars, & there's a more-urgent fast track for their predecessors 2 decades ago. Here's a link of that era's progressive batch: LHL was the launcher of the program in '71; the other quartet were chosen 2 years later (though it was TCH, being career naval officer, ended up as the least politically experienced today).

    http://www.scholar.mindef.gov.sg/ourscholars_succ_intro.asp

    Even as all senior officers should complete a CO stint with a combat unit at some point, those who are scholars aren't always required to do this "linear-ly". In other words, they may be posted to attend staff-officer courses (local & overseas) earlier in their careers; the implication being MINDEF or the SAF taking a long-term view of grooming. That's partly why the non-scholar officers sometimes feel 2nd-class & shortchanged, after being bypassed for promotion or reposting in favor of the high-fliers. So much for meritocracy ~lol~
