    MINISTER FOR DEFENCE OBSERVES INTEGRATED SAF EXERCISE

    The new Minister for Defence, Rear-Admiral (RADM) (NS) Teo Chee Hean, observed a Singapore Armed Forces (SAF) exercise on Pulau Sudong today. He was accompanied by Minister of State for Defence, Mr Cedric Foo, Senior Parliamentary Secretary for National Development and Defence, Associate Professor Koo Tsai Kee, Permanent Secretary for Defence, Mr Peter Ho, and the Chief of Defence Force, Major-General Ng Yat Chung.

    Codenamed Vigilance, the exercise featured the recapture of an offshore island which had been taken by an enemy force. The Minister for Defence first watched two commando platoons rappelling from RSAF UH-1H and Super Puma helicopters and landing on Pulau Sudong using Zodiac boats. This was followed by infantry troops, M113 armoured vehicles and Light Strike Vehicles disembarking from waves of RSN fast craft to secure the beachhead. The Army forces then advanced inland to recapture the airstrip from the enemy. At the airstrip, RADM (NS) Teo saw a Chinook helicopter airlifting light artillery to support the operation. Two Super Puma helicopters also delivered additional logistics supplies to the SAF task force while the Chinook evacuated simulated casualties.

    Exercise Vigilance involved regulars, Full-Time National Servicemen and Operationally-Ready NSmen, and demonstrated the SAFÂ’s operational readiness and proficiency in conducting integrated operations.
