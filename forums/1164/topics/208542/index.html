    I actually think it is a great idea to pack a loud hailer for combat commanders.
    So many times during mission that I wished I had one. I also wished we have whistles ('like the Viet Cons'). Yes, we have radio sets. But when you are out in combat fire and manoevre, I tell you, the last thing that happen is things go the way they are planned. It is always very chaotic. People all over the place, lost, missing, not in order. Even basic but effective means of communication will work wonders then nothing.

    When things are so chaotic and people are spread out, shouting is norm. You shoot for the concern of your men, to keep them in formation, to keep them safe, to keep the unit safe. If you don't keep your unit in formation, you risked yourself or your men being killed. No prize for keeping quiet and all composed.

    As a combat leader, I will also not order to shoot, even if I know the area is suspected to contain enemy. This is because in actual combined unit fire and manoevre, it is always very confusing. People get lost. You get lost. So there is a chance that friendlies will stray or you stray into other friendlies' areas. Combat intelligence is also cannot be fully relied on for your lives. We are talking about taking a fellow section/platoon/company's mates live we may take, if we just fire without clearer identification.
