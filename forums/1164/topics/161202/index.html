    Yes I know that. But after NATO decided on a standard round to use, there are certain specifications to follow, such as grade of gun powder etc. Thats why its known as 5.56 or 7.62 NATO now. FYI 7.62 is not purely an MG round. It was the standard round for assault rifles until USA pushed for NATO to adopt the .223 as standard because that was what their newly designed M16s were chambered for. In the past, it was commonly used by rifles such as the M14, H&K G3, FN SLR/FN FAL etc.
