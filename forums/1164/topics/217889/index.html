    I have been using a Garmin E Trex standalone GPS for many years. It is purpose built for GPS only and is extermely reliable and accurate.

    Go to Google Earth, put the cursor on a spot in Singapore, read off the coordinates & key it into your GPS unit and bingo, when you are physically at the actual spot with your GPS unit, you are dead on target plus minus 30 m max using the coordinates keyed in.

    Despite the adverts, overhead foilage can disrupt the weak GPS signals.
    If you are a serious geocacher, stick to a GPS from Garmin or Magellan. They are cheap and cost about S$200 to 300 for a basic model.

    The US Govt spent more than US$ 20 billion to put up the system and you only spend S$ 200 to know your exact position anywhere in the world. This is a real bargain !

    I am not sure if a GPS receiver is a viable proposition in Singapore. I have an older Magellan GPS 315 handheld unit that I use for navigation when I go hiking and flying here in the US. However, when I bring it with me on my trips to Singapore, I find that, due to line-of-sight obstructions from dense foliage and tall buildings, I'll be lucky to achieve a lock even on the minimum two satellites required for a 2D fix.

    Besides, does one really need a GPS to get around in Singapore? It's not as if there were vast tracts of uninhabited and unexplored lands on the island.

    (It would probably make more sense if you intend to acquire a GPS receiver for use outside of Singapore--say, on a drive to Malaysia.)

    I am using Garmin "Etrex Venture". Bought in Sim Lim Square 3 years back. About SG345. Works great. Only compliant is when I am in another country, the first time boot up & search for signal is very very slow. About 5~10mins. After the first time to "Set-in" the country, it's alright. For subsequence usage. Then back to Singapore needs to set again. It's fully automatic to set. Just stupid waiting only.

    My main usage is fishing. Recording of sight-seeing place. It's mounted on my bike sometimes. Usage of "Way-Point" pretty good. And all major towns are shown up well advance if travelling with. But no map for this model. Don't think GPS with map is useful also. As long as you know the direction & track. You always home.

    In fact it provide me too much informations liao. Like ETA, ETE, stopped time.... All these I would not use.

    GPS can never be use under shelter. It needs clear line of sight towards the sky. If on budget Garmin Geko 201 is about SG250. Already enough information for getting around & back.
