    in my opinion i think there is a minimum number of aircraft that any airforce should possess in order to wage an air campaign effectively. look at the US for example, they are currently being stretched very very thinly. and looking at future procurement 183 F-22s as their main bet for air superiority worldwide isn't enough.

    looking inwards towards our region or for most countries, unless you need force projection then probably the requirement for self-defence would mean that the numbers of aircraft or assets needed could be scaled down.

    but look at it this way, in any engagement you would expect losses. so let's say you load up an F16 or even a F35 with its full load of air-to-ground weapons. maybe you're expecting say 6 or more SDBs for example for a minimum of 1 bomb per target. if that aircraft gets shot down unfortunately then how? the other aircraft on that mission might have targets of its own to hit so you're already like 6 target kills less.  its like loading all your eggs in 1 basket. i think its more prudent to spread out the loads over slightly more aircraft so that loss of aircraft in an engagement would not render your mission ineffective?

    if you look at the US, when they go on a typical mission its like 4 aircraft tasked for Sweep, maybe 4 or 8 aircraft for strike, another 2 for SEAD, 2 more for rear sweep or escort.. thats already about 12 to 16 aircraft just for one mission to guarantee enough bombs to hit the target and usually you're looking at several targets within the vicinity, real case scenario would prob be more. of course we're only talking about legacy fighters like F15s, 16s and 18s and the rest of the conventional type of aircraft we have. F35s would be a significant asset to any airforce but you do still require a significant amount of them as their inner payload isn't that great, and if you want to add pylons outside to carry more then it defeats the whole purpose of them being stealth. :S
