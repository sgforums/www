    Interestingly, the gangsta-style shooting technique has been used by users of the mauser C96 pistol at the turn of / early 20th century, esp. soldiers of the Chinese warlords. Fired on automatic, the pistol has tremendous upward kick, causing the firer to miss the target further with each successive rounds. To get around this problem (sort of) the pistol is fired sideways, turning the upward jerk into a horizontal one, giving a nice spread of bullets. Useful if firing against massed enemy.
