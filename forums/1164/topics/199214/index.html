    The M16, M16A1 and M16A2 are military assault rifles with full automatic or burst-fire capability.

    The civilian version is the AR15 which lacks the full auto or burst feature. It is capable of only semi-auto fire (i.e., one round discharged per trigger pull).

    Externally, both versions are identical (except for an additional position on the selector switch of the M16 that is labeled BURST or AUTO); internally, the mechanisms are somewhat different. This page describes and illustrates some of the major differences in the internals of the AR15 and M16.

    AR15 (ARmalite 15) is the original name for the M16 before the US Military adopted it and designated it the M16.

    The ArmaLite Division of Fairchild developed the AR-15 in 1959. Fairchild licensed the rifle to the Colt's Patent Firearm Manufacturing Corporation, and Colt's assumed the AR-15 trademark. Early Colt rifles were marked "Colt ArmaLite 15."
