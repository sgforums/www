    Fitting Mistral on top of a MBT is technically not a problem however tactically, it may not be a good idea. The main role of MBT in the battlefield is to provide firesupport for the other friendly elements and basically that is to neutralise the enemy's fire. It will be too much work for the tankees if they will to provide firesupport and at the same time deal with air threats, thus tactically i dont see a need for the Mistral system to be fitted on a MBT.

    If the armour columm require air defense, it can be provided in different levels such as divisional AD Singapore is currently practising. Anti-aircraft weapons will not be mount on MBTs as i said it is tactically unwise to do so but it is mounted on delicated vehicles such as the V-200. A number of V-200 AD vehicles will be supported by a P-Star radar mounted on another vehicle and a number of unit of V-200 AD vehicles will provide air cover for the division. It is reported that they did experimentally mount Igla missiles on M-113 or similar plateforms, well to be frank, i dont know the result but it could be a possible solution.
