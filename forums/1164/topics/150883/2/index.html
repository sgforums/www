    I have not done reservist for almost 9 years cos living overseas. So I don't know if PRC-77 still in use.

    A heavy but tough signal set might be OK for signallers.

    But I was a rifleman platoon runner, which means I have to route march, charge hill, FIBUA, FOFO with the PRC-77. Sometimes all in one night.

    All the while I have to keep up with my PC, whom is leading from the front. On top of this 14kg weight I have to carry the same weapon and ammo load as the other riflemen plus a long antennae which weighs almost another kg.

    Though I was very fit but I remember one embarrassing occassion where my PC had to pull me out of a comms trench as we were assaulting all night and by daybreak we were all but dead tired.

    So it's useless to have a tough, heavy radioset unless you have a really, really tough runner carrying it.

    Another bad thing about being runner is that the first enemy sniper bullet will be for the guy with the radioset. It is harder for enemy snipers to identify who the officer is. That's why in Vietnam some soldiers carry their radio upside down with the (short) antennae pointing down.
