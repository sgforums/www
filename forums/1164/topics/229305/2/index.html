    The T-34 is actually one of the best tanks of WW2 - in the same way the AK-47 is the perfect assault rifle.

    Item by item, the T-34 may lose out to the best German tank - the Panther - for example. But the T-34 is cheaper to build, and easier to maintain, and as a result there are a LOT of them.

    (It's a bit like comparing the excellent FN FAL against the crude AK-47. The FAL is accurate and powerful and works reliably, except in the desert. But the recoil is hard, full auto is uncontrollable, ammo is big and heavy, it is expecnsive to manufacture and too big and heavy for smaller troops to carry. The AK-47 solved all those problems with the trade-off being accuracy.)

    Even though Germans are superior in every way, they simply couldn't win if you pit 5,000 superb Panthers and crews against 10,000 good T-34.

    The Americans and British had excellent modern tanks towards the end of the WW2 which were equal to the German Panther and Russian T-34.

    But the tank they said won the war was the less than perfect M4 Sherman simply because there were so many of them and it was also an easy platform to upgrade - much like the M-16.

    So, again, the best equipment does not guarantee victory.
