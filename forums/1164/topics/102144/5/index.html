    First, the "sudden bright light" scenario is exactly the situation that monocular NVGs are designed for - the eye that is not looking through the NVG is still good.

    Second (qualifying this with the caveat that this is the theory), one of the original aims for instituting NS in Singapore is the diffusion of military-trained personnel throughout the population. The scenario quoted above may not be entirely unrealistic. As in the case of United 93, people can be capable of surprising things when the outlook is grim enough.

    Third, the scenario hinges upon the terrorists getting what they demand - that's their Archilles heel and that's why pre-9/11 hijackings were a losing proposition. Think Entebbe, Mogadishu and the Achille Lauro - time was on the good guys' side and they didn't need to be in a rush to storm in. In order to get what they wanted, the terrorists would have to leave their prepared positions, and that's where they're weakest. The scanario is therefore moot - why try to hit them at their strongest point?

    Fourth, just to entertain the idea of a storming at that time, flashbangs aren't the only pre-storming method of incapacitating Tangos, are they? The train's got speakers built in and can be used in place of the flashbangs' "bang" effect.
