    The military specs for testing lens is based on the standard set in MIL-STD-662F 1997 and MIL-PRF-31013

    The ANSI standard is also widely followed in high velocity impact standards test. Both are reputable standards and all lenses that are fit for military ops must meet or exceed them. It is to my understanding that the US Gov. (so is Australia) is moving towards the use of ESS goggles for military ops and SWAT. However, alot of existing military supplies which were issued earlier are still using Oakley's M frame.

    In Europe and Israel, I've been told that the troops use NXT, but I haven't been able to confirm. Just for information - The patented NXT urethane technology was originally developed for military application as ballistic armor by SIMULA, Inc., a leader in the field of lifesaving technologies for human protection. This project began in the early 1990s when SIMULA was awarded a contract with the U.S. Army to develop a variety of lightweight armor systems. At the time, state-of-the-art materials for armor designs were polycarbonate and acrylic, which would not meet the new requirements of the U.S. Army related to weight, impact resistance and durability. With diligent research, NXT was born.

    In all honesty, I think governments do not rely on a single supplier and they like to have choices and only awards short to mid-term contracts. This is why you may see a lot of brand cross-overs, where different groups of forces may use a certain brand over others. I do not see a single company having a competitive edge over others and for defence spendings, I think cost would be more relevant to the planners as long as the standards are met or exceeded.
