    Unless you want to employ a fully-auto 0.5 Browning HMG at vehicle checkpoint on Singapore, your next less "lethal" and "intimidating" weapon is a shotgun loaded with solid-shot. There are solid shots designed to engage on-coming thin-skinned vehicle frontally. They can penetrate the radiator without losing much velocity and shatter the engine block, stopping the vehicle in it's track with a few well-aimed rounds.

    You might have seen demo where the full-metal jacket 5.56mm round and 9mm round penetrate the doors of ordinary sedan and maiming the subjects in the cabin. Those shots are fired at the side of the vehicle, a broadside. To hit the driver from the front is a different story. The 2-litre engine block is in the way and the slopped wind-screen may deflect off the puny 5.56mm and 9mm rounds. You don't want to take chances when you engaged an explosive-packed sedan with a suicide bomber behind the wheel. He/She might have rigged the vehicle to crash in the event that he/she met a "pre-mature" death. You want reliable stopping power from well-aimed shots in a crowded and flammable environment.

    Gun designs in the 60's and 70's are built around the cartridge. In terms of gun design, we have reached a plateau in gun performance i.e., design-wise, we have max out on ergonomics, weight, reliability and etc.

    So, if you want to design the next revoluntionary kinetic energy based assault rifle, please start on the cartridge.
