    Originally posted by kotay:
    I'll hazard a guess ... what you eyes saw was a depleted Orbat as a result of no-shows/deferments/downgrades for ICT. Your ATEC would most probably have given you the closest to actual Orbat ... from an outside observer's POV.

    From what I know of PDF Orbat, which isn't much, some of you guys are still formed up in the traditional 1980's Orbat. 9MS, Coy MGs (not Plt), 4 coys, etc. Do note that most Military formations, these days, are basically working in multiples of 3's

    3 Sect = 1 Plt
    3 Plt = 1 Coy
    3 Coy = 1 Btn
    3 Btn = 1 Bde
    3 Bde = 1 Div

    To complicate it slightly, you add in the HQ elements and support arms.
    I enlisted in 82. Before that it was 9 men per section, 3 section per PL, 3PL for 1 Coy and the Infantry Regiment consist of :-
    Trooper Coy x4
    Support Coy x1
    HQ Coy x1

    4 Regiment = 1 Bde
    4 Bde = 1 Div

    during my NS day, the section was 8 men:-

    Section :-
    Group 1 x3 :- IC(M16), M203, M16 (heavy barriel)
    2 x2:- M16, M16(training in map reading)
    3 x3 :- 2IC(M16), M203, M16(heavy barriel)

    PL :- section x3
    Coy:- PL x3
    Regiment :- Trooper Coy x4, Support x1, HQ x1

    4 Regiment = 1 Bde
    4 Bde = 1 Div

    Still in memory, alway have 1 reserve strength for all attack mission.

    Just before I was going to ROD, 1 section became 9 men(added a sharp shooter), Regiment, Bde & Div format still the same.

    Don't know when, it become 7 men per section & only have 3 trooper Coy for the BN(formally was Regiment) & every level become x3

    Note :- in 80s infantry eg 3 SIR is 3rd Singapore Infantry Regiment (strength is around 900 men) now is infantry BN ( around 500 men)
