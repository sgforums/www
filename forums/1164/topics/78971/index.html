    Defence Minister Teo Chee Hean got an update on the Republic of Singapore Air Force's training detachment in the United States when he visited the F-16 detachment in Phoenix Arizona on Sunday.

    The training, under the Peace Carvin II detachment, is ongoing at the Luke's Air Force Base.

    Rear Admiral Teo also met US Armed Forces personnel as well as leaders of the local community there.

    The RSAF has been training in the US for more than ten years and has four training detachments there.

    MINDEF says training in the US has helped the RSAF enhance its professional skills as well as the level of inter-operability between the armed forces of both countries.

    Singapore and the US have extensive and multi-faceted cooperation between their defence establishments. - CNA

    TEXAS : Defence Minister Teo Chee Hean visited Singapore's Peace Vanguard Apache helicopter detachment in the United States on Sunday.

    Rear Admiral Teo tried his hand flying one of Singapore's Apache Longbow attack helicopters based in Texas.

    He praised the air force personnel from the Apache detachment for becoming operationally ready after a five-and-a-half month stint there.

    He said overall he was very impressed with what the detachment has achieved in the past two-and-a-half years.

    Singapore plans to bring the Apache helicopters back by 2006.

    Admiral Teo said: "The Apache Longbow moves us along the path toward transformation. That means information dominance, precision weapons and also network-centric warfare.

    "The Apache Longbow has a radar, it has got guided missiles and it has got data link between the helicopters.

    "This means that it can outsee, out-manoeuvre, outshoot enemy tanks. So you do not really need a tank to fight a tank anymore. You can fight it asymmetrically with a helicopter." - CNA
