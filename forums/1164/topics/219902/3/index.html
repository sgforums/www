    Thats actually SubCommand:Akula, Seawolf n 688i.

    Its like Janes 688i in most aspects except that u can command a choice of these 3 submarines. There is only 1 campaign, and the 3 submarines recieve a variation of these missions. For example, the first mission as the AkulaII, u have to egress from port undetected, while in the Seawolf or 688i, your objective is to locate the AkulaII leaving port, and track it to deeper waters before egressing to your release point.

    The campaign is a modernised cold war scenario. As a game, and a simulation I rank it pretty highly in terms of playability. However, u need to know a few submarine warfare tactics or some of the missions would be a bit too challenging. But of course, as a game, there is always "broken" aspect. You can launch any weapon u like, even if its a drastic overkill.

    Personally, its quite easy to get the hang of the game as long as you are patient and don't shoot off torps easily. I usually like to scout out SAGs carefully, fix everyone of their positions before launching ALL 8 tubes at them. Followed by a whole 10 minute of running perpendicular at flank speed when they detect my torpedoes coming at them. If you ever try the game, just remember, FLANK speed and DECOYs are your FRIENDS. It helps you shoot ppl and not get shot! =D

    ANTI-SHIP Missiles ARE NOT YOUR FRIENDS. Cos they give away ur position AND are usually ALWAYS shot down. Hence I never carry and TASM or Harpoons. Only Mk48s, UUVs and TLAMs.
