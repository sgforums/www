    Have large ships mean nothing without the ancillary systems.

    Thailand has an aircraft carrier, its' so expensive to maintain, that it is kept in port all the time. Size isn't everything. UK explored deploying the Harrier on large container hauling merchant ships like the Alantic Conveyer (later sunk by exocet). It was deemed that such deployment was impractical and had marginal benefit.

    Problems like refueling, repairs, routine maintenance plus navigation equipment, weapons systems support (targeting data entry) etc make deploying aircraft on an LST a fool's dream (or a cockmanie idea dreamt up by airchair genrals).

    with a V/STOL aircraft runway length is not an issue but without the support, deployment will spread your assets without corresponding benefit of coverage, depth or sustainability. Even an off-site airfield on land takes up a lot of precious assets and preparation. The key to air operations is tempo which is either sustained by closeness to target, loiter time, haulage (for bombs) and turnaround time. The biggest secret in RSAF is aircraft availability and sortie rate.

    This thread sound like a previous experiment to deploy our 155mm on another platform (before the current Primus program) which failed spectacularily.
