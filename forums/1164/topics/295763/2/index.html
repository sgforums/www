    Yes, of course the M16 has been proven in battle to be a reliable weapon. However air crew men/pilots have limited space to swing their weapons around. In situations such as urban warfare, a standard assault rifle may also hinder movement and give away location.

    This explains the development of sub-machine guns like the MP5, however reliable, accurate and situation proven they are, use 9mm ammunition which doesn't work against bulletproof vests. This partly led to the development of PDWs which use special ammunition able to penetrate the NATO standard body armour.

    The CPW is targeted somewhat along that lines, to be able to provide the compactness of a pistol but accuracy and firepower of a rifle.
