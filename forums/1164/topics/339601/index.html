    lets just say we cant comment on operational doctrine.

    but do think of it this way.

    a barbed wire / wire obstacle there means that the enemy is trying to block that route of access from you. it is designed to make you reroute your way around and take longer time/different way to attack you.

    you could either

    1) breach the wire obstacle

    2) go around the obstacle

    either way, both will take time. so its a no-gain situation for the attacker. irregardless of operational doctrine, obstacles still take up time. and no two obstacles are the same, so while doctrine may say breaching, actual ground observation may call for something else.

    really depends on context.

    there are plenty of ways to cross barbed wire, its just not many of them are avaliable on the web too.
