    Italian copters may be Army Air WingÂ’s first buy
    Leslie Andres

    LANGKAWI, Sept 30: THIS yearÂ’s Lima will see a deal being struck between the Government and an Italian company for the procurement of the first component of the Army Air Wing.

    The deal is at the end stage of negotiations and an agreement or Letter of Award is expected to be signed before the close of Lima '03.

    "There will most likely be a ceremony and it will be announced soon," said Italian Embassy Defence Attache Capt Adriano Sarto.

    The Government is negotiating the purchase of 11 Agusta A109M light observation helicopters as the first component of the air wing.

    When the idea for an Army Air Wing was first mooted, attention was focused on the purchase of attack helicopters.

    The Apache attack helicopter from America's Boeing and the South African Rooivalk by Denel were the front runners in the race for the contract.

    Now that the deal for the A109M is to be concluded soon, it is unclear whether the Government will go ahead with the purchase of attack helicopters.

    The A109M is capable of carrying light armaments and can be used as an attack helicopter besides its surveillance capabilities.

    Another idea being mooted is the possibility of upgrading the Royal Malaysian Air Force's aging Aer-macchi MB339 jet trainer aircraft.

    "The upgrade would be done in association with Malaysian industry players to bring the aircraft up to the level of the aircraft which the RMAF may get from the Royal New Zealand Air Force," said Sarto.

    The Government recently announced the leasing possibility of 16 MB339s from the RNZAF to complement its existing fleet of aircraft, used as lead-in jet trainers for RMAF pilots.

    Another Italian company set to make an offer to the Government is Alenia Aeronautica.

    Sarto said the company is offering its ATR42 maritime surveillance aircraft, in wide use by the Italian Coast Guard and Customs Department.

    "This is in keeping with the proposal to set up the Malaysian Maritime Enforcement Agency," he said.

    Sarto said Italian companies were keen to maintain a close relationship with Malaysian companies and the Government.

    Alenia Marconi, an Anglo-Italian joint venture, has a Malaysian partner in Comlenia Sdn Bhd, which has the contract for the maintenance and support of the Royal Malaysian Navy's four new Laksamana class corvettes.

    Airod Alenia Technology Malaysia, meanwhile, is a Malaysian-Italian joint venture company that holds the contract for the maintenance and support of Malaysia's military and civil land-based radars.

    Russia-Malaysia defence cooperation

    (09/30/2003 -- 19:03GMT+7)

    Moscow, Sept. 30 (VNA) - Malaysia has been Russia's leading Southeast Asian partner in military and technical cooperation over the past 10 years, said Victor Komacdin, Vice Director of Russia's arms export company, Rosoboronexport.

    The Russian Vice Director made the comment at the International Maritime and Aerospace Exhibition 2003, which opened in Langkawi, Malaysia, on Tuesday.

    Malaysia signed a deal to import many Russia's Mig-29 fighter jets in 1994. A Russo-Malaysian joint venture enterprise was established in 1995, specialising in maintaining and modernising aerospace products and technology imported from Russia.

    A protocol on mutual understanding in defence cooperation, defence technology and defence industry, signed between Russia and Malaysia in 1999, was a milestone in the countries' military cooperation.

    Russia signed a contract to provide "Metis" and "Igla-1" missiles to Malaysia. Russia also signed a deal with Malaysia for the delivery of 18 Sukhoi SU-30MKM fighter jets, during President Vladimir Putin's visit to Malaysia last month. The deal marked a change in Russo-Malaysian military technical cooperation.

    Russia gives priority to promoting military technical cooperation with other countries. Last year, the country exported 4.8 billion USD worth of military equipment.-Enditem

    Copter handover formalised

    LANGKAWI: Malaysia yesterday took delivery of the Agusta Westland Super Lynx 300 multi-role helicopter during a ceremonial handover at Tanjung Malai here.

    Royal Malaysia Navy chief Laksmana Datuk Mohd Anwar Mohd Nor signed the acceptance agreement on behalf of the navy while Agusta Westland managing director Alan Johnston signed for the company.

    The signing was witnessed by Prime Minister Datuk Seri Dr Mahathir Mohamad, who later boarded a Super Lynx on static display, to take a closer look at the flying machine.

    Of the five which have been delivered, three are undergoing trials. The helicopters will be based at the Lumut naval base.

    Earlier, Dr Mahathir graced the maritime section of Lima 2003. With him was Brunei's Sultan Hassanal Bolkiah.

    The Prime Minister and Sultan Hassanal Bolkiah watched a fly-pass of aircraft and a speedboat demonstration before touring the exhibition hall.

    Dr Mahathir then conducted a fleet review of 46 naval ships anchored offshore.

    RMN Receives First Super Lynx 300

    LANGKAWI, Sept 30 (Bernama) -- A European aircraft manufacturer, Augusta Westland, Tuesday officially handed over the maritime offensive helicopter "Super Lynx 300" to the Royal Malaysian Navy (RMN) here.

    The high-tech helicopter, the first of six ordered by the RMN at a total cost of RM700 million, was handed over at a signing ceremony between the Controller of the Royal British Navy, Nigel Guild, and RMN Chief Laksamana Datuk Mohd Anwar Mohd Nor.

    Also present at the ceremony, held in conjunction with the Langkawi International Maritime and Aerospace (LIMA) exhibition here, were Prime Minister Datuk Seri Dr Mahathir Mohamad and Defence Minister Datuk Seri Najib Tun Razak.

    The Super Lynx 300, the most modern offensive helicopter in its class, will be piloted by Lt Komander Ajazi Jamaluddin and assisted by technical officer Lt Zamri Ismail.

    The Super Lynx 300, equipped with digital glass cockpit, 360-degree scan radar, electronic support measures and forward-looking infra red for night operations, are expected to run from the RMN's F200 frigates like KD Lekir and KD Lekiu.

    The helicopters, with a maximum speed of 175 knots, will also be equipped with high-tech weapons, including the guided missile "Sea Skua", and torpedoes.

    Bofors Defence Asia To Produce Guns For RMN And Asian Region

    LANGKAWI, Oct 2 (Bernama) -- PSC-Defence Technologies (PSC-DT) and its foreign partner, Bofors AB of Sweden, have agreed to finalise the establishment of a joint venture company which would be named Bofors Defence Asia Sdn Bhd.

    PSC-DT in a statement Thursday said that the new company would be Bofors hub in Asia and be given the license to manufacture the company's weapon spare parts for Malaysia and in the longer term for the Asian region.

    "Bofors Defence Asia will be able to provide full integrated logistics support for all Bofors clients in Malaysia as well as in this region. This will certainly enhance the operational availability and reliability of Bofors guns with the Royal Malaysian Navy," it said.

    PSC-DT, a subsidiary of Penang Shipbuilding and Construction Sdn Bhd and Bofors AB had earlier in the day signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) at Awana Porto Malai to pave the way for the cooperation.

    The new company according to the statement, would endeavour to supply the new generation 57mm Mk3 guns and smart ammunition for future naval programme with a significant part of the production to be manufactured and assembled in Malaysia under license.

    "Bofors Defence AB, through PSC-DT will provide assistance and facilities to produce Bofors smart 3P ammunition for 40mm and 57mm Mk3 gun in Malaysia," it said, adding that this effort would contribute greatly to the development of self reliance in the local defence industry.

    Currently RMN's warships use various calibre of Bofors guns with two of its latest vessels, the KD Lekiu and KD Lekir choosing the 57Mk3 guns as their primary guns.

    Government talking to Boeing to buy Super Hornets

    LANGKAWI: Malaysia is negotiating with Boeing on the possibility of buying 18 F/A-18F Super Hornet fighter aircraft to replace the present batch of F/A-18D Hornet jets deployed by the Royal Malaysian Air Force (RMAF) at its Butterworth base.

    Defence Minister Datuk Seri Najib Tun Razak said there had to be lengthy discussions as the purchase would involve high cost.

    The Super Hornet is considered among the best fighter jets in the world and is currently used by the United States Navy and Marines.

    If Malaysia reaches a deal with Boeing on the purchase of the aircraft, which saw action in the Iraqi conflict, she will become the first other country to have the Super Hornet.

    Malaysia Purchases 11 A-109M Surveillance Choppers

    LANGKAWI, Oct 3 (Bernama) -- Malaysia on Thursday agreed to purchase 11 A-109M surveillance helicopters by Italian firm Agusta Spa, a subsidiary of Agusta Westland, for the Army Air Wing Unit.

    The Letter of Agreement was signed by Defence Ministry secretary-general Datuk Subhan Jasmon and Agusta Westland vice-president Bruno Spangollini at the Mahsuri International Exhibition Centre here.

    The ceremony was witnessed by Deputy Prime Minister Datuk Seri Abdullah Ahmad Badawi and Defence Minister Datuk Seri Najib Tun Razak.

    When met at Awana Porto Malai, Najib said the helicopters were worth in total RM480 million (US$75 million) and they were expected to be delivered in two to three years.

    "The purchase of the Agusta A-109M is to make the Army Air Wing operationally stronger," he said.

    Najib said the helicopters could be equipped with various weapons suitable for surveillance, anti-tank and search and rescue operations and advanced training.

    He said part of the purchase agreement involved the transfer of technology where Agusta Spa had agreed to set up a helicopter maintenance centre in the country.

    Aside from the Defence Ministry, two of the helicopters would also be used by the Fire and Rescue Department, he said.

    Meanwhile, the Ministry also signed a purchase agreement worth RM24 million on spare parts for its warship A244-S torpedoes from Whitehead Alenia.

    Another agreement with local company Mutiara Dominasi Sdn Bhd involved the purchase of the Action Speed Tactical Trainer simulator worth RM24.3 million which would be used for training by Royal Malaysian Navy personnel starting 2005, Najib said.
