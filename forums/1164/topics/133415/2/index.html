    Anyway, before we can even convince the US Army to adopt the SAR21, even in field trials, ST must really convince our local operators that they can solve three main issues.

    First issue, the scope fogging problem.This is very intriguing because scopes have fogged up, been replaced with new ones, and yet the same problem occurs time and time again, often within months.An Infantryman cannot be told his weapon cannot even take moisture or water occasionally, and the downtime caused by this problem is pretty bad.

    Second issue, the LAD.Granted, it is integrated and poses less chance of this item falling off due to vibration from intense firing, however, in-field replacement is a pain and nightmare.You cannot do such a replacement in battle, and to take this weapon and send it back to rear lines is something that cannot be allowed in battle.

    If you have detachable ACOG or Trijcon sights or floating red-dot reflex(which incidentally is better than a visible laser) on your MIL 1913 rail, repair or maintenance issue will be much easier and roving maintenance teams can reach the frontlines and find that they do make a difference.

    Third issue is the magazine release catch.This is a niggling design flaw that operators have often feedback to DSTA that it poses a barrier to combat reloading.And seconds are lost when you have to transfer one hand to steady the gun while the other depresses the button to release the magazine and viceversa to load a fresh magazine.

    These issues aside, the great thing going for the SAR21 is the center of gravity.I do not know if these flaws will be addressed for the next batch of rifles.However, I had the chance to handle the SAR21 MMS Rail version at Asian Aerospace last year and this enhanced model, though vastly heavier, seems like a better compromise.



    I do know that STK has offered a version of the SAR21 with no LAD,but with a front grip.This version was used by the SAF peacekeepers in Timor Leste.Another export version has the MIL 1913 rail on the upper handguard.Perhaps these might stand a chance in the US market, however I thnk they would have solved the three issues before they decide to enter into the fray.
