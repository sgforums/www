Malaysia's Keen to WORK with Singapore at Pedra Branca

  • Atobe
    12 Mar 03, 22:10
    Today's Straits Times reported the Malaysian Chief of Navy as saying that Malaysia is keen to work with Singapore to enhance the safety and security in waters off Pedra Branca.

    This is made more urgent considering the recent accident between two merchant vessels in the waters off the disputed island.

    "He said the busy sea route near Pedra Branca was prone to accidents.

    We have always considered Pulau Batu Putih as belonging to Malaysia, and we are responsible for ensuring security. Even though we are not stationed near Pulau Batu Putih, we will enter the area to maintain security should anything crop up,' he was quoted as saying by Sin Chew Daily"

    There seem to be some change towards a more diplomatic tone; which could lead to some possible co-operation in the waters not within the immediate vicinity of Pedra Branca; and allowing for joint co-operation of the entire general area in the vicinity of this disputed island.

    There has been a long fraternity of co-operation between the two navies, as well as the peoples of both countries.

    Any co-operation between the two Navies will not influence the outcome of the judgement of the ICJ proceeding to decide on the ownership of this island.

    With co-operation as a first step,. this might lead to a further mutual respect towards each other's interests.

    This change of tone during the Malaysian DPM Badawi temporary position of being the Malaysian Prime Minister (in the absence of Dr Mahatir who is on vacation), can only provide a good impetus for further improvement of ties between reasonable peoples from both sides.
