    If I am not mistaken, the range is when the aircraft is flying in L/D Max.

    That is, high altitude for low drag, throttled to most km per lb of fuel burnt.

    More weapons, more drag+weight, lesser range.

    It doesn't mean the the F-15 is inferior if its range is measured in such "optimal" conditions, without combat load. For another aircraft to carry the same amount of combat load, it would probably have half the F-15E's combat range with the same load.
