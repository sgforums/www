    Correction. no such thing as halfway decent shape during preservation. You need to service them daily, weekly, monthly, yearly, weekly engine runs and functional checks to make the systems work on ground condition. In fact they keep them as war reserves but they have to perform these tasks to keep them ever-ready in hard shelters/hangars. They need to be 100% airworthy. Perservative wrapping is just to prevent corrosion and only work in dry climatic condition but not in tropical conditions due high humidity. Say after 15 yrs in storage condition in desert with engine and main component removed, then corrosion is minimal. Inorder to make it in flying condition, it has to go thru a complete 'refurbishment' on the airframes, structure and control systems then buildup for completeness and flight tests. So there are two different conditions for preservation of aircraft in airworthiness status and carcass storage
