    Singapore joins US-led programme to develop new generation stealth fighter
    Singapore will be taking part in the system design and development phase of a new generation of advanced stealth fighters under a US-led programme.
    Its Defence Ministry and the US Department of Defence today signed a Letter of Intent which paved the way for Singapore's participation in the Joint Strike Fighter or JSF Programme.

    MINDEF said, this has the potential to meet the RSAF's longer term operational requirements for a multi-role fighter.

    The stealth fighter will also have enhanced combat survivability, precision engagement and lower cost maintenance.

    Singapore is the first Asian country to join the JSF Programme.

    Other partners in the collaboration include the United Kingdom, Australia, Canada, Denmark, Italy, Norway, The Netherlands and Turkey.
