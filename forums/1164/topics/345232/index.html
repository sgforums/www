    If memory serves, the AWB that was in effect from 1994 to 2004 limited the capacity of new magazines manufactured during the ban to a capacity of 10 rounds. Magazines with capacities of over 10 rounds that were manufactured prior to the ban were still legal; unsurprisingly, these so-called "pre-ban" or "high-capacity" magazines went for a premium during the ban.

    Unfortunately, there is talk of the Obongo administration reinstating a similar or more restrictive version of the AWB sometime real soon, and this is fueling the current run on firearms in this country and driving prices into the stratosphere. The prices of AR-pattern rifles, for example, have more than doubled, and there are currently chronic shortages of ammo at the retail level.
