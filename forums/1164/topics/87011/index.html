    26 Jul 04, 15:07
    It's not anything new; I went thru the experience while at NUS between '89 & '92. The "arrangement" mentioned earlier is informally termed - I dunno about official terminology - as "being in an IHL unit" (Institute of Higher Learning). One implication is that the relevant CO must design his ICT schedule around the local vasities' term holidays, nominally March-June back then, for the 1st 4 years or so of the training cycle (until the relevant NSmen presumably graduate).

    There's why I suggested in a previous thread that you could technically end up doing 2 ICTs in a calendar year, with one being a "make-up" one. The window of opportunity is already limited with the IHL thing, then there's also IHL exams & national holidays like CNY & Hari Raya. A March ICT could thus be pushed forward to April or May, but be counted as the previous SAF workyear's.

    Dunno about RT; it was a different regimen then. Also, all of us were still quite young/fit to pass the IPPT during the ICTs.

    As for mob manning, it's not any more inconvenient compared to out in the working world. I stayed in the hostel, without a personal TV or table phone but I carried a pager - in other words, roughly same-same with the non-office workers out there - & I recall my unit even particularly "arrowing" the IHL students to be Area I/C's too! (We could claim cab fares with a silent mob activation.) The word on the streets was - is? - that mobilization recalls tended to happen on weekends, so it's really not much difference with the rest of the NSmen.

    What I can vividly remember, however, was being part of a contingent to perform for this SAF Tattoo event. It "counted" as one ICT; we ended up burning 9 consecutive weekends in parade rehearsals. That means booking in at 7:30 on Sundays (latter-ly Saturdays too), hanging around the camp until 15:30 before being transported to the National Stadium, & not booking out until 23:45 or later. Luckily, I was largely vacationing as a NUS student then, but it was super-siong for the other NSmen, having to sacrifice their weekends time & again after a long workweek ... & being "imprisoned" in camp for half the time - with the canteen unopened, only training-shed concrete for naps, no proper bathrooms for washing up or showers, & no gradual winding-down after each parade "high" prior to the new workweek.

    The numbness & disgruntedness boiled over into a memorable argument during the final sending in of rifles - partly cuz the IHL men were to out-process a day earlier (start of new school term), but some also jumped queue in desiring to catch the last bus/MRT, & others wanting to return home to catch a soccer-tournament telecast. In the end, the SI cell people didn't even stamp my participation stint into my reservist booklet for that call-up.
