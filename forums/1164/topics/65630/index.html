    The problem with carriers is that they are going increasingly senile as weapons systems. Political statements aside, it can be noted that carriers are by themselves, extremely vulnerable systems that now require an inordinate devotion of resources to their protection, just so they can do their job of launching aircraft to carry ordinance to their targets.

    In our waters, carriers will be all the more vulnerable to conventional, and unconventional attack. We will have to devote tons of resources just to protect the carrier, and make sure that we have an infaliable shield against attack... all this just to launch aircraft (when it be done so from land)? An attack on a carrier that fails means that the carrier gets to live another day, but a defence that fails means the carrier is destroyed. The pros and cons are just not worth it for us. That is to project airpower from a ship, the costs required to maintain and protect this ship far exceeds that of land bases.

    America has the cash and power, and even their carrier battle air group remains a largely ineffective weapon in pratical. In most modern wars they had fought, they barely dropped 1/3 the munitions that were dropped by all air strikes, and all this was done at far higher cost and risk (carrier aircraft suffer higher attrition rates). The carrier is more of an American political weapon then actual weapon of war that they can afford with their seeminGly limitless defence budgets... but do we?

    The conventional carrier as we know it today will not be very useful in the smart-munitioned wars of the future. Maybe a hybrid arsenal-UCAV carrier type stealth ship might be envisioned that's far lower in maintaince and more capable of standing on it's own. But that's speculation on my part.

    On the other hand, we should be seriously looking into the technology of hypersonic smart munitions that rely on sensor networks to find, and strike their targets anywhere on our map.

    A hypersonic missile fired from anywhere in our turf can find it's way towards any potential target within it's operational range in a matter of minutes, if not even less, and destroy the target. A carrier can at best move a few hundred yards in the same amount of time to get it's planes into range, during which it is extremely vulnerable and has to be protected at all costs, and then there's also the factor of losing aircraft that cost you so much to put on a ship you have to protect with a good portion of your defence budget that can be spend getting these very smart munitions... When you do the math, it's far, far cheaper to plink point targets with future cruise missiles.

    In the end, if we want to plink targets with our navy, it's far cheaper, and more effective to mount these munitions on our new and future stealth craft, and drive them around the south china sea anniliating Orang Utans in the jungle during our precision test firings.

    My guess? The politics of the future will be cruise missile politics rather then carrier power projection...

It works the same way in the future world of hypersonic smart munitions.
