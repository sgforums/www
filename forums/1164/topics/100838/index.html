    Was checking out the Straits Times and read article on the US' progress in Afghanistan & Iraq (World, page 16). Nothing new about the articles but the photos were noteworthy. Apparently, some combat units seem to have already started wearing the Digitized Desert Camouflage uniform.

    For those unfamiliar with digital camou, it's the new camou pattern first invented by the Canadians and later the US. It comprises of overlapping small squarish shapes rather than the current curvy or leaf like shapes that most armies' use. It supposedly makes it harder to differenciate from its background as the squarish (pixel) shapes tend to confuse the human eye.
