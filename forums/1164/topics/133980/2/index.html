    Another very funny thing about my unit:

    we were issued with short 20-round magazines!

    At first I couldn't believe it and I thought it was a temporary thing.

    But then when they issued us with the (then) new Israeli-type chest (bra) webbing, they came with pouches for 20-round magazines only!

    A few years earlier when I was in the Guards unit we were already issued with the chest webibings and they all came with 30-round mag pouches.

    So I was quite shocked as my whole life I had never used 20-round magazines. These were like Vietnam era, only brand new. Or very well preserved and unused.

    The officers wouldn't comment or don't know. I asked one trainer and all he said was "we have a warehouse full of them".

    But it's a damn funny situation. The SAW gunners were given 30-round mags but M-16 users all had 20-round mags. I felt very humiliated by this and until today did not understand the reason. Logistically, it made no sense to have 2 types of mags and webbings for such a small armed force like ours.

    Sure, we were issued a lot of old and obsolete equipment like the 12kg PRC-77 radioset I carried, but since we were an assault force and not a static defence force, these 20-round mags were a joke.
