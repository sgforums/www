    Originally posted by LazerLordz:
    So this would essentially mean that you guys would be in more danger from criminals with Tec-9s and stuff like that right?So in what way is the ban a bad thing?
    For a few reasons:

    1. The AWB banned rifles that had a detachable magazine and two or more of the following features:

      - A folding or telescoping stock
      - Pistol grip
      - Bayonet lug
      - Flash suppressor, or threads to attach one
      - Grenade launcher

    Banning any of these feature does absolutely nothing to reduce the lethality of the rifle. This aspect of the AWB was merely a 'feel good' measure aimed at making rifles look less menacing.

    2. The ban restricted only the manufacture of 'high-capacity' magazines during the time it was in effect, not the sale of said magazines. In the case of the AR-15, there were literally hundreds of thousands of military-surplus 20- and 30-round magazines manufactured prior to 1994 that were easily available right through the period of the ban. The same applied for high-capacity pistol magazines made prior to 1994.

    3. Criminals, by their nature, tend to ignore laws, and are unlikely to abide by those restricting the magazine capacities of their guns to just 10 rounds. In addition, the vast majority of gun-related crimes commited in this country do not involve firearms subjected to the restrictions in (1) above.

    4. Crime stats show that the AWB had essentially NO effect on the rate of gun-related crime in the the US.
