    Not a ghost story - but definitely a sad one.

    10 years ago my friend used to work for URA in charge of all the old abandoned warehouses along the rivers.

    He'd get keys to go into the warehouses and we'd take a lot of bw photos - a hobby.

    One time he had the keys to a 2 storey shophouse. The entire neighbourhood is now abandoned and boarded up.

    Upon entering, we saw old-fashioned shop glass display cases.

    There were some damage here and there but we still discovered that an unusually large amount of stuff was left behind. From the look of things, it looked like this house had been abandoned more than 10 years - during the early 80's.

    This is what we discovered in the house.

    A plastic bag full of ang pows containing brand new $1 notes - the "bird" series. By then, they were already phased out more than 10 years.

    Entire bw photo collection of the family that used to live in the house. Pictures of one of the son's wedding at the house where the entire neighbourhood turned up. And then their honeymoon in Cambodia - before the Vietnam War! I still keep this photocollection at home, most of them probably 1960's vintage judging from the cars and fashion. There were no colour photos.

    In the kitchen we found their kitchenware etc still left behind.

    Upstairs, the big picture emerged. All the beds in the bedrooms were still there as if someone still slept there - mattresses, pillows and sheets.

    Just about everything not precious was left behind.

    An art-school portfolio bag with an entire art-school porfolio. While this may be worthless to most people, this is a student's 3 years' worth of art school work. I know cos I'm in advertising.

    In the wardrobes, all the family's clothes including underwear were still intact. In one bedroom the roof has collapsed somewhat and there was the dried up skin and bones carcass of a dead cat on top of a bedside cabinet.

    Cheap badminton rackets etc. Anything not resellable.

    In conclusion, to say the family left in a hurry was an understatement. 2 items that were more precious than money is the photo collection and the art school portfolio.

    The ang pows tells us they left during CNY.

    We can only speculate why they did not come back to collect the rest of their stuff. It was especially spooky to look at the photos of the place when it was so different compared to the sad desolation that we found.

    If there was a way to contact that family I would galdly return their photo collection. It must mean a lot to them.
