    Well psykor, that's the thing - I started in the old camp and ended up in the new one, and saw jack squat.

    In the old camp, 5 Coy had painted a devil's head as a mascot, and apparently a few odd things started happening after that - rattling chains at night and all. Apparently, the guys tried getting rid of the painting by painting over it, but the new paint job kept peeling off. I've seen the peeled paint and the mascot under it, but that hardly counts as a ghost.

    In the new camp, a number of skeletons were discovered when the excavation was being performed at the site of the swimming pool. It appears that these skeletons were from WW2. The day after the swimming pool was first filled up, two dead frogs were found floating in the pool, and cracks had developed in the pool floor.

    Okay, stories from my company mates?

    First, I was from 2 Coy, just after the batch where the three guys dies in the helicopter abseiling accident. During my BMT, one of the blokes said that he woke up one night to see three figures dressed in full battle order, carrying abseiling ropes in their shoulders. This fellow was living in the bunk that the three guys had lived in, but none of the recruits knew that at the time.

    About a year into our service, a passerby called Battalion HQ and complained that three men were abseiling down the side of our company line.

    During my BMT, one of my section mates was killed in a motorcycle accident. The night after the funeral, one of my platoon mates woke up to hear someone singing outside the barracks. My late section mate had often led the cadence when the platoon was marching or running.

    During Chinese New Year, the Indian guys in my company were put on guard duty, and so had to book in the night before the duty. Apparently, a number of them heard noises from the armskote, and it sounded like the chains were being pulled through the trigger guards of the rifles. They went down to check, and the armskote was locked up.

    Like I said, I never saw any of this for myself, so please take this as simply a set of accounts that I have related.

    As for guys who have seen things, I had one of those in my company. It becase the running joke when we would be doing a tactical movement, and this guy would go "Wait!", and the whole company had to skirt around ghosts that only he could see!

    Here are one of mine own first-hand exeprience, much less frightening, during my NS 20 years ago.

    First one: taking a course at a camp in Alexander Rd that's next to a JC - St Andrew's or something, can't remember if it's an Engineer camp or something.

    At night, 4 chaps decided to play Ouija Board. They use a coin and draw up a piece of paper with the alphabets, numbers, yes/no so the spirit they call up can answer their questions. And a circle for "home".

    Started a about 9pm in the bunk on a square folding table. Being the kiasi and skeptical cynic, I decided to just watch.

    One of the chaps was a very playful fellow. So when, sure enough, the coin started moving, I was sure it was him pushing the coin.

    All their questions were answered promptly. ...Spirit was a girl. One of the chaps will have a gf this year etc etc... But when it was time for supper, the spirit was reluctant to go to the space marked "home".

    (In this kind of game, you cannot lift your finger away before the spirit goes home or you'll sure kena a lot of problems from the said spirit.)

    Again, I thought it was the playful fellow moving the coin. Eventually it did go "home" and all of us went for nightsnack.

    After nightsnack at the canteen, they were back at it in the bunk. This time, the coin barely moved, but it did move.

    Part 2 of the game was very slow going. The spirit didn't answer any questions instead just moved randomly - and painfully slow - over the board.

    Midnight, lights out. Still at it, and the spirit refused to go home. Started feeling cold, being only an observer, I was free to get up to switch off all the fans.

    Everyone else went to bed.

    0100 hrs, 0200 hrs, still at it. This time I was quite sure no one was kidding around. I can sense all were nervous as heck as the coin is still moving slowly and randomly.

    It began to get very cold even with the fans off. They, including myself, started to feel a chill. I got everyone as well as myself wrapped in blankets. In Singapore.

    They were all very quiet and one guy already had dark rings under his eyes. Quite a sight, four of them sitting in the dark wrapped in army blankets with their fingers on a 50c coin. Almost funny.

    By 0300 hrs I cannot tahan anymore so friend or no friend, I went to sleep.

    The next day they were very quiet. Eventually the spirit went home at about 4am, they told me. The guy with the dark rings under his eyes said he was seeing things in the bunk some time during the game. One of the things he saw was a child playing under one of the bunk beds.

    Whatever it was, my conclusion was that something did move the coin and it wasn't any of the chaps. No matter how much of a prankster you are, you won't want to sit with you finger on a coin for almost 5 fxxking hours.
