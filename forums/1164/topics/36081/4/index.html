    Headlines

    Defence Paul Lewis/Washington DC

    Singapore's JSF move raises A-4 contest fears

    Singapore's decision to join the Lockheed Martin F-35 Joint Strike Fighter (JSF) development programme as a security co-operation participant(SCP) is raising concerns among rival manufacturers that it will overshadow the competitve selection in the near-term of a replacement for the air force's McDonnell Douglas A-4SU Skyhawks by 2007.

    Singapore becomes the 11th nation and the first from the Asia-Pacific region to commit to the JSF. It has earmarked $50 million, which provides access to data, modeling amd country-unique system studies, but does not give programme office representation.

    While the agreeement to join the system development and demostration(SDD) phase does not commit Singapore to ordering the F-35, its defence ministry acknowledges "JSF is a potential candidate to meet Republic of Singapore Air Force's longer-term operational requirements for a multirole fighter". This concerns some companies expecting to bid for Singapore's 18-24 aircraft A-4SU replacement programme.

    Observers say Lockheed Martin's hopes of selling Singapore additional F-16C/D Block 52s would be boosted if the nation were to defer a decision on adding anotehr aircraft before the F-35 is ready. Singapore already operates 49 f-16s and will begin taking delivery of another 20 Block 52s from December.

    JSF has already undermined fighter competitions in Australia and Netherlands, which have both joined SDD. Singapore's long-term goal is toreduce the number of different fighters in the air force's fleet from the current F-16, A-4SU and upgraded Northrop Grumman F-5S/T to just two types.

    Rival manufacturers are pinning their hopes from Singapore needing a more modern air superiority fighter and the F-35 being available for export before 2015.

    Contenders include Boeing F-15T and F/A-18E/F Super Hornet, Dassualt Rafale, Eurofighter Typhoon, F-16C/D Block 60 and Suhkoi Su-35. Singapore is to expected to shortlist two to three aircraft in May before issuing a request for proposals and Marketing final fighter selection in February next year.
