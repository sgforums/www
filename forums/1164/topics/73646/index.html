    the F-16 vs Mig-29 thing was covered in a 1995 issue of CODE ONE, the lockheed martin mag. i lost tt issue some time ago... but IIRC it all started when a USAF F-16 sqn went to play DACT in decimomannu, italy vs luftwaffe Mig-29s.

    of course, the article was biased towards the F-16 (it was a LM publication after all). but the general tone was that F-16 was superior in almost all practical aspects: radar, avionics, ergonomics, HOTAS, cockpit visibilty, turn rate, agility, engine life, efficiency etc etc... but its big handicap was the lack of an off-boresight HMS-aided WVRAAM. R-73 simply thrashed AIM-9M. one quote i remember was from an F-16 pilot: "everytime we got close to a fulcrum, we'd be banking hard and dumping flares...."

    that was a very interesting issue... it also had a pretty comprehensive article on the early development of the missile we know today as the AIM-9X. it was called the Boxoffice program back then... they actually had a thrust-vectored, off-boresight, HMS-aimed AIM-9 prototype back in 1995, took them so long to put it in production.
