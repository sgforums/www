  • dkhoo
    23 Jun 03, 09:15
    The main advantages of the SAR21 to me are the reliability and the accuracy.

    Most SAF soldiers have only used the M-16 family rifles before and think that they are "reliable" weapons. They are not. They are one of the most unreliable assault rifles in history. You need to keep it very clean for it to work. A rifle which you need to clean at least once a day is not reliable. An average AK-47 can be buried in mud for months and be completely red with rust and still fire perfectly on full auto the first time you pull the trigger. That is reliable. I have not used the SAR21 for long enough to make a full judgement, but it is definitely a huge improvement on the M-16 type weapons in terms of reliability. No stupid little pins and bolts to lose while cleaning the rifle either.

    The accuracy of the SAR21 is also really good. You have to shoot it quite differently from the M-16. Don't try to predict the sight's movement with your breathing like you would with the M-16. When the dot is on the target, just squeeze the trigger right away (just like a computer game). Breath control is not so important, just finger control. Grip the rifle tightly against your shoulder since it has a tendency to jump forward rather than up (for me at least). If you do this, the rifle is damn good. No need to line up rear sight with front sight and wait to breathe in or out and all that crap, just put dot on target and shoot. It takes a bit of practice if you have been trained with the M-16 but I can now engage the range targets faster and more accurately once I got rid of the M-16 mindset. New recruits who have never fired the M-16 apparently did better on the SAR21 than the M-16, so it is easier to learn (though I have no confirmation of this). No need to zero is a big big bonus. The backup iron sight on the SAR-21 is crap though, no question.

    The main disadvantages are the controls and deployment attitude.

    The fire selector is in the stupidest possible position and the safety is too hard to push in one handed (maybe the rifles are still too new). Magazine change is also very difficult, since you have to reach almost into your armpit, the magazine release is not near your firing hand and is also hard to press (again, maybe too new). The cocking handle is weird though it works OK, and needing to cock after magazine change slows everything down even more. They seriously need to improve all of this.

    The SAR21 is currently so precious. Cannot drop it. Cannot stamp the butt on the ground. No oil on the sight or laser. No this. No that. 1206 if you do anything wrong. Etc. No wonder there is no bayonet stud -- who would dare to have bayonet training if the crabs and stars think the rifle is made of glass? It is as if the thing is a new baby everyone is cooing over. Once the rifle is no longer new and fascinating this should change. Less headaches for me.
