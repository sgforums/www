    Well, if the CO & S1 are due "back on sept 1st", then they should already be available locally this weekend to boost your case.

    Seeing your MP is a bit late; by the time a change of orders funnel down the chain of command, it'll likely cause some other poor soul to receive the 1-year attachment ... at an even more last minute.

    IMO You need to contact the appropriate higher people who can directly aid you in arranging an operational contingency. Is your Chief Clerk, or any Manpower-savvy colleague, around to guide you on the channels?

    An online search suggests something called the PMC (Personnel Management Centre) to be seemingly supervising overseas postings, but I've not be able to locate their contact number or source. You might try SAFPSC (Personnel Services Centre) to see if they could refer you on:

    http://www.mindef.gov.sg/dmg/des/psc/contactus/index.htm

    Here's supposedly how the SAFPSC & PMC liase with each other to ensure the affected servicemen's next of kin are visited regularly during their period of absence from home:

    http://www.mindef.gov.sg/dmg/des/psc/welfare&serv/carevisits/nokgrant.htm

    I also think it'll help your case if you could persuade a colleague to replace you beforehand, albeit at a late hour. My own indirect experience (also for Cresdendo) wasn't with a lengthy posting like yours - more like for a fortnight or something - & Div Arty left it to our CO, who left it to our BC, who left it to the two of us corporals on who should go. I verbally pleaded with my friend to "take one for me", since I had over a month of off-in-lieu days to clear just before ORD. Thankfully, he said yes, so there was no hiccup. If you can line up a replacement yourself, I don't think the higher-ups care who precisely flies over, as long as someone does.
