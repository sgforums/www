    You are totally wrong, if someone really shoot a pistol before, he/she will know how hard it is to shoot it, and hit the target.

    For shooting a rifle, we have what we call 3 point contact. You have the bulk on shoulder, 2 hands on rifle, so 3 point contact, the weight is well distributed and center of gravity is ok. The recoil is assorbed mainly by the shoulder.

    Now come the shoot of pistols. We have what we call 2 point contact. You have 2 hands on the pistol. the center of gravity is in fact pulling you forward. The pistol fully loaded with 12 to 13 rounds is close to 1 kg. Holding it steady, is not as easy as it seem. Even is the pistol is zero, forsight and hindsight which is nearer to each other, so much margin for error, a slight alightment error will end up the bullet flying way out. The recoil is much stronger, as it don't have a butt like a rifle which help absorb the recoil, recoil is totally assorb by your hands. A rifle which is have 3 point contact ( tripod ) super firm. A pistol is 2 point contact ( bipod) not as good.
