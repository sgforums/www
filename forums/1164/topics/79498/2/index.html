    That where you are wrong, the safety in tigger system was build just right and suit our army. I had the chance to shoot a few models of glocks, can say they are totally great. In fact they will be more suitable for our army (in the case of limited pistols training for most officers).

    1) When you have not so well-train folk, the problem come during action, it is a time which is life and death. Usually there is a lot of cases of no-well trained people pulling the pistol out and pull the trigger just to realise they forgot to release the safety and then panic trying to release it. This shock and delay can result in disaster. That is why glock design the safety into the trigger system.

    2) The light weight work to the advantage. It is lighter, which make it more easy to carry, aim and shoot. Make a lot of different for a less well train shooter to aim and shoot.

    3) The glock build their pistols so that the parts can be interchange with the same type of glock. That mean if you have 6 x glock 17, you can stripe them and mixed up the parts and reassemble them, they still all can work, which others cannot.

    If someone play play and shoot themselves, then some unit is lucky enough to get rid of some idoit officer. If he/she cannot even trust to hand a load gun, he/she should not lead. First rule, never put your finger on the tigger till you ready to fire.
