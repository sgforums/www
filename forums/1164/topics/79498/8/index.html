    I say the FN P90 won't be available to SAF signallers as it is an expensive weapon being an import, as is its 5.7mm ammo. This weapon, if you even have a slight inkling of its internal feed system, you will realise why I say it is a delicate and complicated design.

    The bullets are arranged tangent to the barrel. So when the round is travelling from the magazine into the chamber it first goes into a rotating "turntable" where it is rotated 90 degrees before fitting into the chamber and then fired.

    The magazine is not spring-loaded so it is said that the rounds inside could get "jumbled-up" if the weapon is dropped. This has stopped many countries from adopting this weapon even though it has been around for many, many years.

    The bullet itself is almost as big and long as the 5.56 bullet we use. I'm not sure about its weight. But its cartridege case is alot shorter and thus it is less powerful than our 5.56.
