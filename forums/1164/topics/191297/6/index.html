    Building the 1/32 F-14 day 2



    Ok, so basicly i joined the burner sections and the 2 halves of the rear section of the fuselage together.



    There was a molding error during the production of this kit. A whole section of plastic went missing on the tail pipe of the aircraft. Ver dissapointing for a tamiya kit. Not a problem though, I can easily rectify it.

    First i cut out a piece of plastic card and made the rough shape that i wanted to fill as shown in the picture.

    After that i poured superglue on to the card, and sprinkled talcum powder on the super glue. It causes the super glue to harden very quickly and at the same time, turn it brittle, allowing me to sand it down to shape.

    I added one layer after another of superglue/talculm powder mix, until the layers are flused with the kit's surface.

    After that i used a grade 320 sand paper to shape the fabricated part.

    So this is the outcome.



    I sprayed the surface with some paint to see if there are any cracks or gaps to be filled. If there are, i add a drop of super glue on the crack and sand over with a grade 320 sand paper.



    All looks good now.

    we are using 3 tone grey. as far as i know, there are no tamiya equivalent yet.

    the four-color scheme has FS 36118 (gunship gray) for the major portion of the upper surfaces; FS 36320 (dark ghost gray) for the radome; FS 36270 (neutral gray) for the vertical stabilizer, near the cockpit, the ventral fins and the top of the intake structure; and FS 36375 (light ghost gray) for the underside. Although this is the RSAF scheme, I have seen at least one picture of a USAF F-16 painted the same way.
