    try arts friends. Ask for water slide decals. I find their decals thick though. For printing, best is to use laser jet. After that spray dull coat.     Generally, i think it will be better to use hobby paint brishes. They are generally finer than those arts and craft paint brush. Of course don't compare those from arts friends. Some of them are super high quality. I am not exactly a hand brush painter, i only own one paint brush, that is the extra fine one, for painting instrument panels. You may have to ask manager433 for tips on how he handbrush.

    As for paint, if you intend to do more models after this, it is ok to keep some paint bottles around. If not , you can try the humbrol series of enamal paints, small little cans, not much problem with hand painting over small surfaces. Just that you have to control the amount of paint and thinner used. Use very thinned paint.

    Anyway, here is a website that gives a very good tutial on hand brushing. You may find it useful.

    http://www.ttfxmedia.com/vallejo/cgi-bin/_modelis.asp?p1=ing&p2=modelcolortecnicas#modelcolortecnica01
