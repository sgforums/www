    Ok this is the mega update. I kept alot of pictorial stuff but i didn't put them online.

    I started out with the landing gears. The Trumpeter company made the Su-27 with landingears that have spring suspansion. Which was cool. To make the spring suspension actuate, the landing gear is made up of many hinges. Which is not so cool, because firstly, it would make the structure weak, and secondly, the Trumpeter plastic is very soft. The hinges broke very easily.

    But the temptation to build the hinge overcame me, and I had the build first ask questions later mindset.

    I started out by replacing the plastic hinges with metal wires.

    Apologies for the blurry picture, I took it too close to my camera.

    I started by drilling a 0.5mm hole on each of the linkage





    After which, a wire is pushed through the holes.



    A couple of holes and wires later, I got this. This is the main landing gear of the flanker.



    Here's the front landing gear. Apologies, I didn't have the build process for the front landing gear. I was really cursing and swearing so much, I forgot about photography.

    I attached the landing gears to the fuselage and see if it could stand. Well it was wobbly, but everything seems to hold.

    After that i proceeded to perdorm enamel washing on the aircraft.

    I started off by spraying Mr Super clear gloss on the aircraft. This performs to functions.

    1. It protects the underlying coat of enamel paint.
    2. It is easier to remove excess paint on gloss then on matt.

    After that I started out by painting a thinned mixture of 1 part black to 7 part thinner on the aircraft. Focus on the panel lines and rivets.



    Let the paint dry then add 1 drop of thinner.(I used turpentine here) On a piece of tissue paper and gently wipe it down in the direction of airflow.



    This would be the result.



    The right side is the one that was washed while the left side hasn't been washed yet.

    Well i only showed you the elevator. Didn't take the full image of the aircraft. So apologies on that.

  • CenturionMBT
    29 Jul 06, 00:14
    finally, after all that washing, I did the decaling.

    Took me the whole of yesterday and today. As mentioned previously, The plane was sprayed gloss. It has one added function of helping decals adhere to the surface. So you wun see any silvering.

    I used two solutions to soften the decals, Microset and Mr Mark Softer.



    The rule of thumb for decaling, is to cut away the excess backing film, I.e cut as close to the image as possible.

    After that, dip in water for 10 seconds and place it on a paper towel for about 60 seconds. This allows the adhesive on the decal to melt, but not totally dissolve, as in the case of leaving it in a tub of water.

    When the decal film as come off, add some decal setting soluction (microset) on the surface and and "float" the decal to the position you want. Leave it there for 1 to 2 minutes then press down with a damp tissue paper.

    For cases where you want to see rivet lines, or you have to go on very tight contours, add decal softening solution ( Mr mark softer) Beware, it is pretty strong, and it might crumple your decal. So "paint over your decal, count to 10 and press it down with a damp tissue paper. You should see the underlying details.
