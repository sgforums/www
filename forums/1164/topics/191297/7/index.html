    F-5 & F-14 Update



    Ok Previously you saw the F-5 cockpit. So this is how it looks like when fixed onto the fuselage.

    One thing good about this hasegawa kit is that the fitting is really good. But too bad it is using protruding panel lines instead of recessed panel lines. So 1/2 a day is spent on rescribing.



    The forward fuselage is fitted onto the rest of the body. Hardly any gap filling is needed. Almost like you take some cement, some super glue, throw it into the box, shake it a little and a full plane comes out.



    An image of the rear end of the F-5. One side of the main wing is fitted on.



    Image of the other side.



    The F-5 is more or less done. Haven't painted, nor have I added landing gears.

    Next on to my F-14 project.



    painted the upper side with medium gray, underside with light ghost grey and canopy sides with dark ghost grey. However, after spraying i find that there itsn't much difference in paint colour. Well it looks good anyway. Added some post shading on areas where i have over applied paint or in areas which requires touch up.

    Sorry about the lack of image for this one. There was a moulding line on the canopy which spoiled the whole thing.

    I use an old image of my F-16 for reference.



    Basicly i sanded it off with a grade 2000 sand paper. After it has been sanded flat, you see a misty milky white area on your canopy. Fret not. Add some tooth paste, buff it down with a piece of tissue paper untill it is semi shiny. After that, use tamiya polishing compound and buff it again. Voila, the canopy is crystal clear without the moulding line.

    Btw, I just found a new method of filling gaps. Add the tamiya putty onto the gap, pour nail polish remover onto a ball of cotten wool and wipe it down. You will find that other than the gap, there shouldn't be any putty anywere else.
