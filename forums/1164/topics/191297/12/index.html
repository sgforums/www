    20 Jun 06, 00:08
    Wah..lots of questions. Will try to answer all...

    Black Aces: Personally, for the 1/48 AAR probe I would grab a Hasegawa 1/48 A-4 Skyhawk (it offers both the cranked and straight probe) and cannibalise one of the refueling probe ends. Then get the correct diameter copper/brass tubing and bend it to shape. Superglue the copper/brass tubing to the probe. Next scratchbuild the AAR probe mount (scrap plastic like sprue will do fine) and cut/sand to shape. For reference use this pic

    Unidentified: To build 1/72 RSAF CH-47SD, get Italeri/Revell (same kit by different companies) MH-47E Chinook. Its the ONLY kit that provides the enlarged fuel tanks used by our CH-47SDs. You'll have to leave out a lot of the SOF gear found only on the MH-47E though. The ACH-47 CANNOT be used because of the smaller fuel tanks.

    For C-130, best option is the Italeri 1/72 C-130E/H

    As for A-4SU, the Hasegawa 1/48 "A-4SU" is riddled with so many errors I wouldn't even bother. Might as well get an A-4C and scratchbuild the modifications. And get the Horizon ModelTech 1/48 Singapore Scooters decal sheet. Last time I checked Marina Square and Far East Plaza hobby shops got stock of the decal.

    Finally, 1/72 Super Puma. Italeri's AS532 Cougar is the way to go. Check out this article(link). Watch out the side fuel tank sponsons though! Its been a LOOOOOONNNGGG time since I last saw a RSAF Super Puma using the small landing gear sponsons w/o the flotation gear.

    Also, forgot to mention about the Super Puma. DO NOT get the Italeri AS332 Super Puma kit. Its actually a AS332B (which I've never seen in RSAF service despite some reports we have 4). To build a RSAF Super Puma use the Italeri AS532 Cougar kit which has the lengthened fuselage. Although you still need the AS332 kit to rob the flotation gear/larger landing gear sponsons which comes in the Italeri AS332 kit but not the AS532!
