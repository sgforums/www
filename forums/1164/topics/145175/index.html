    Hi all,

    A thought that came across my mind while reading ST's article on proposed joint air exercise for Singapore and M'sia to protect the straits is how well prepared and/or equipped our navy or even air force is to deal with such terrorists and/or piracy threats. I mean, we have had extensive discussions about our overall capabilities to deal with clear offensive moves by nation states but how about this clear and present danger.

    I understand that most of our naval patrols is partly hampered by the extent of our territorial claims along the straits but if we have better cooperation among the littoral states, how prepared are we to deal with such situations. Forgive my ignorance in this but I guess we are definitely better armed than the pirates etc but what about their tactics and ability to hide/knowledge of the straits etc. It is similar to how the Brit police have to learn new tactics from Srilankan police and Israeli police in dealing with suicide bombers.

    How would sea-air coordination work? I have been exposed, I guess, from movies and own experiences of how land-air exercises work but with the sea-air aspect, it would seem very interesting to discuss abit of this dimension of warfare especially against unconventional enemies.

    Comments anyone?
