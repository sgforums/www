  • Moltke
    12 Dec 05, 11:28
    WW2 is always THE WAR for me, because of the sheer global scope of the war and the lineup of combatants - from Europe to North Africa to Asia. The only true global war. WW1 was by and large too slow-paced - trench warfare/stalemate on the Western Front, and negligible Asia/Pacific action to speak of.

    WW2 is also the last "interesting" war in that there were several "great powers" contending for supremacy, and also because there are so many alternate outcomes that might've been. What if the Germans didn't invade Russia, or declare war on the USA, what if the Japanese had not attacked the US etc etc.

    After WW2, it became a "boring" bipolar world, with just the USA and the Soviet Union. Honestly, modern wars are soooo boring. Whereas WW2 had breathtaking campaigns with so much color/feel and dozens of divisions stretching for months - I find the Russo-German war absolutely fascinating - nowadays you get little inconclusive skirmishes or the US military simply flattening its opponent in an overwhelming concentration of steel and fire. Absolutely boring
