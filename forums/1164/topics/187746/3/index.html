    The iron sights on the SAR are good to 100 meters, i tried it at a life firing. Only issue is that they can't be zeroed, but it isn't a problem if you have a weapon that shoots reasonably straight.

    Problem is they probably won't be that useful at night, unless something was done about it.

    And the LAD is quite useful, if you use it in the right way. I seriously doubt anybody would leave it on and chiong with it, but it is fantastic for FIBUA where you can point-index a target far away, turn on your laser for a burst to get your aim right and squeeze off a shot without ever having to narrow your field of view to the sights... it can be a lifesaver in combat, reducing the attention needed to take aimed fire.

    Problem is don't believe all the bull about it being factory zeroed, virtually every SAR after some time develops it's own quirks that requires normal zeroing. And the LAD needs to be zeroed as well, and to a certain range at that.

    Don't have much issue with the trigger squeeze, except that it feels mushier then the M16... but I still could hit targets 300 meters away, and double taps aren't a problem. The M16 squeeze is nice to have, but not really necessary.

    A real issue is the reload time, and I am wondering why they don't bother to give it a proper ease spring, given the fact that when you bang the weapon in open bolt hard enough, it would eash spring itself. Going for the charging handle is quite a hassle, and lowers considerably the loading time.

    But all in all, I found the weapon a more reliable shoot then the M16, as much as the M16 reminds me more of a oil and metal gun. The SAR spits out metal that can kill, gives me things the M16 cannot provide, and that's good enough for me. I feel a lot of the compliants come from expecting M16 qualities from the SAR when it's a totally different weapon entirely requiring a different mindset. Going about the SAR with an M16 mindset means you never make full use of the weapon, and unless you make full use of it, how can you judge it fairly against the other?
