    Don't forget that Singapore owns a sizeable merchant marine, especially NOL's fleet of sizeable container ships.

    During a POT, these civil resources could be requisitioned and employed as what the Royal Navy calls "Ships Taken Up from Trade" (STUFT). Look at how the ill-fated Atlantic Conveyor was used as a helicopter carrier during the Falklands/Malvinas War in 1982. The Brits have had some success using the Royal Fleet Auxiliary Argus as a helo carrier too.

    Closer to home, check out how the US Navy has used some of its maritime prepositioning ships (MPS) ro-ro ships as helo carriers..... with Seahawks as their air group.

    If you have a good hull form, it's not rocket science converting it into a flat top for V/STOL aircraft or helos. Many carriers that served during WW2 were converted from merchant ship hulls, examples include the Bogue-class and Kaiser-class escort carriers, which served with distinction, and assorted Imperial Japanese Navy types Junyo, Taiyo Chuyo, Unyo etc that had a poorer combat record due more to tactical shortcomings and inept operational deployment rather than their design limitation.

    And who said a V/STOL aircraft needs to be manned....... Harriers are yesterday's story.

    P.S. Speaking of STUFT.... interestingly, the hull form of the Endurance-class ships appears to have been based on the Tropic-class 400-TEU ro-ro/lo-lo commercial vessels, Tropic Tide and Tropic Sun, built by STEM.
