    First we should limit our discussion to the 60-70 surplus JAS 39A.

    The difference between A and C is that A version has Swedish software and lacks OBOGS (onboard oxygen generation system),
    inflight refueling system, Coulour screens, and is not NATO compatible (datalink). And a bit weaker wing that gives
    less flight hours with maximum load.

    on the +side we still have a 4th gen airplane with
    - Low accusitions costs
    - Low operating costs
    - Easily uppgraded
    - Adapted to conscript ground crews
    - 800m take off and landing
    - can operate from simple roadbases
    - Swing role

    So a guess would be for 1 billion USD you could get 60-70 surplus JAS 39a (about a third of the acquisition price)
    and then pay for uppgrading the software and datalink (Tech transfer) This way you could have a a 2 type airforce with the F15sg
    in the Strategical long range role and JAS39A for tactical defence of Singapore in Air to Air, Seastrike, Recon and CAS roles

    Further uppgrades planned in Sweden for the A model was
    # larger, 150 x 200 mm colour MFDs
    # enhanced warning and countermeasure systems
    # reduced radar signature
    # an imaging IR sensor, IR-OTIS, in a ball in front of the canopy which can be used to search for, track and imagine airborne targets as well as searching for and identifying ground targets, this in conjunction with the helmet mounted sight, it also will function as a FLIR navigation sensor
    # further developed PS-05/A radar
    # upgraded computers and databus
    # new data cartridge
    # a new IR AAM missile, IRIS-T
    # helmet mounted display, Guardian by Pilkington/Cumulus (Denel)
    # enhancements to the RM 12 engine consisting of a new turbine, improved flameholder and new FADEC by Lockheed Martin, General Electric and Volvo Aero Corporation
    # new navigation and landing system

    Acording to http://www.canit.se/~griffon/aviation/gripen/
