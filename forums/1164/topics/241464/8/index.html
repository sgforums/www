    No, you got it wrong there. Graphite lube is a proven form of DRY lubricant, its not wet. Its used in industrial machinery too. Its carbon powder based and slides because of the molecular structure of the carbon being in sheet/layer form.

    LET, your understanding of SAW U100/Minimi impress me.

    As a Junior Member, sorry for been so blunt. This Thread is For SAR-21. there are some issues with the fellow and it's being resolved.

    SG combat concept for SAW differs from that of Other Countries (Very Normal)

    U100 is mean for Assault Operation, be it 100 rd Mag/30 rd Mag. Both serve the Purpose very well. Nice, Narrow and light weight, the length can be reduce, but reducing the barrel lenght, range is affected. Look at the Butt, some modifications can be done.

    Explanation,
    Dependent on Unit SOP (Infantry and Armour Unit differs on this also):
    A typical "Text Book" single contact, takes the SAW about 160-200 rds are used, either 2 100 rds Mag or 1 100 rds, and the rest 30 rds Mag. With the "Fresh" clip inserted before the final assault.

    Minimi, if a problem is encounter with that Ammo Box(200 rds), it take slight longer to replace as compared with U100 100/30 rds system. Minimi 30 rd Mag replacement timing is similar with U100 system.

    Belt Feeded Problems
    1. Weight
    Minimi, there are 200, 100 and the 30 rd version. Some will argue that the 200 rds is enough ammo for a single contact. This I would agree, but the price/penalty is the weight of the whole weapon (As what Let had mentioned).

    2. Cleaniness of Ammo
    Mag cover the Ammo, with less leakage as compare with belt system.

    Minimi is a fine weapon when used in a Defensive Operation. Long Belt of Ammo can be linked up to provide protection capability, without the need to replace the ammo box.

    In a Defensive position, (I play alot of this as Aggressor Simulator) In a Well kept Gun, rarely need to open up the weapon to reload, shot till tens of rds left, shift the next box over, link up, and continue to cause "Misery".

    No need to worry about Dirt/Sand fouling up the Link and Jamming the weapon.

    More rds can be put thro a Minimi, than a U100, partly due to the extra barrel supplied, and the Gas system.

    In an Assault situation, I prefer to use a Mag system, as the ammo can be kept cleaner, and less chance of fouling.

    3. Aiming
    In an Unsupported Position, U100 wins this area Hands down.

    In a Tripod position Minimi is Far Superior Accuracy, no U100 Tripod is issued at the current ORBAT.

    Little Secret
    In some unnamed countries (X and the Union Jack Fellow) GPMG ready to use ammo for Infantry, are kept either in a special Box Mag or , a cloth sleeve. just to keep the Ammo clean and prevent jamming.

    Agree with Let
    U100 serve SAF purpose better. Minimi is nice to have, if it can be easier on the weight department.
