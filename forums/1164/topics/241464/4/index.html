    Woah, woah take a chill pill guys... it's obvious we need to see both sides of the issue sensibly. I'll just give my two cents worth from my experience in coaching the greenhorns to shoot.

    Shooting is not a skill that comes naturally to everybody, however it is possible to train somebody to become a good shooter. I believe most people fall into this group. The make of the firearm as well as the features can make this job easier or tougher.

    However, there are indeed some people that no expense of effort can transform them into reliable shooters. For some reason they just can't seem to grasp the fundementals... and certainly not quickly enough in the timeframe we usually have in the two to three months of BMT (not all of which is spent on BTP) to transform them into good shooters.

    The SAR-21 certainly cuts down on the number of non-combat effective shooters by turning marginal shooters into competent ones. Usually these are the ones who for some reason can't figure out how to get a proper sight picture with iron sights. So that improvement for the SAF is certainly quite material.
