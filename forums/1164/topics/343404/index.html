    Originally posted by ktk:

    woah nice piece u have there.

     

    what upgrades? care to share?

    Upgrades from the original bone-stock SA GI Mil-Spec 1911-A1 include:

     

    1. A skeletonized aluminium match trigger with adjustable take-up and overtravel.

    2. An LPA adjustable rear sight.

    3. A titanium firing pin (in place of the regular steel one).

    4. A two-piece full-length recoil guide rod.

    5. A tighter barrel bushing.

    6. A slightly lighter hammer mainspring.

  • Meia Gisborn
    8 Jan 09, 00:54
    Originally posted by wonderamazement:

    what would a firing pin made of different material do to the entire weapon?

    The titanium firing pin weighs about half as much as a regular steel one. The reduced weight and consequent lower intertia allows it to strike the primer faster after the hammer hits the firing pin, thereby slightly reducing the delay between trigger break and cartridge ignition.
