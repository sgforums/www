    "Plastic guns" is merely a colloquial reference to firearms that have major components made out of composite materials; the USP would fit that definition since its frame, as you've noted, is polymer.

    Out of the dozen-or-so polymer-framed pistols in my collection, the only one I would consider somewhat uncomfortable to hold is my Glock 34. It is a very accurate gun, but the blocky grip with its excessively raked angle requires me to adopt a different and somewhat unnatural hold when I shoot it.

    The most comfortable and natural-pointing plastic gun in my collection is also the one I carry: the CZ 100.
