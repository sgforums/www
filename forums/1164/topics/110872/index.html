    I have a Khukiri and I don't feed it blood each time I draw it (though it is a nasty piece of work, it has a forward CG which makes any stroke frount heavy resulting in a deeper cut).

    The legend is that during WWI (yes, 1914-191, many people who meet the gurkhas first time were curious about their Khukiri, asking them to show the blade etc. Soon standard reply was "cannot, bcos need to feed blade blood everytime draw blade" was used to discourage such casual requests. There are some (me included) who believe that knives have spirit which demand respect and the Khukiri has one of the more nasty versions. FYI Giant Khukiris are used in a festival to decapitate a bull with one blow!

    I have seen gurkhas in action during my NSF. Dressed in British DPM camo, carrying M-16 and full pack, an entire platoon can walk by without as much as a sound even before you know it. I've seen them do "PT", more like SOF selection, full pack runs, in tempo and at IPPT pace!!

    They are tough and dependable, so much so that my ex-company wanted to hire a few to provide security for our plantations in Indonesia. They are the bodyguards of choice for the rich and famous in Asia, the Sultan of Brunei has two battalions. Singapore has Btn plus. Guarding Lee Dynasty and Singapore Mint etc.
