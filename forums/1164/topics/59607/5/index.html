    I was, and still count myself as, a Commando. I will state definitively, from the perspective of someone who was there in the days of the old barracks, and of someone who more than survived the training that far exceeds your SERE course in intensity and duration, that the actions of the trainer were wholly unacceptable.

    Captain Roger Courtney said to his batch of SBS recruits in 1942: "Are you TOUGH? If so, get out. I want f***ers with intelligence". That is the essence of the unit - the macho crap is only a corollary.

    The distinction between the Commando ethos and that of other units is that every commando is expected to think for himself and act upon his judgement, rather than running, getting into prone position, letting off a few shots and waiting for further orders. Anything else that we do is simply an extension of that principle.

    As such, the training is meant to impart that faith in one's individual judgement - this leads to the impression of poor discipline, as tripwire has pointed out, and also to the actions that we tend to take, which are executed only because they are necessary to get the job done - not to impress anyone.

    It follows then that the instruction provided must be as professional as possible. I expect that, more so than any other unit, the operating principle reads, "Leave your testosterone at the door". Anybody deviating from that principle is untrue to the Commando ethos.

    I will therefore entirely disagree with you that "we have to be wussies and cry out for heads to roll for unauthorized training". That is simply against the standards of Commandos, and for that heads will indeed have to roll.
