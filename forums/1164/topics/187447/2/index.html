    April 26, 2006 15:28 PM

    Thirteen MOUs, Agreements Signed At DSA 2006 Today

    PETALING JAYA, April 26 (Bernama) -- A total of thirteen defence memorandums of understanding (MOUs) and agreements were sealed between local companies and their foreign counterparts at the Defence Services Asia 2006 exhibition and conference here Wednesday.

    Among the biggest MOUs and agreements signed involved Airod Sdn Bhd (Airod) and DRB-HICOM Bhd's wholly-owned subsidiary, Defence Technologies Sdn Bhd (DefTech).

    DefTech, the main supplier of defence vehicles to the Malaysian Armed Forces, signed strategic collaborations with five foreign partners for the supply of field vehicles, weapon systems, spare parts and technology transfer to the local defence industry.

    The first agreement was signed with MOWAG GmbH of Switzerland for the supply of its "Piranha" family of armoured-wheeled vehicles (AWVs), to appoint DefTech its local industrial partner for promotional and marketing activities, locally integrate and ultimately produce the AWVs, transfer of technology and after-sales support in Malaysia.

    The second agreement, with Ruag Land Systems of Swizerland, is for the marketing, promotion and local assembly of the Ruag Land's 120mm mortar systems.

    The company also signed an agreement with Thales Communications of France to collaborate on promotion and integration of Thales vehicle communication and electronic systems for DefTech's products.

    Another agreement with FN-Herstal of Belgium is to collaborate on promotion and integration of FN-Herstal weapon systems with DefTech's products.

    The last agreement was with FNSS Defence Systems of Turkey for the supply of spare parts and technical services for the Adnan ACV300 armoured infantry fighting vehicles that are in service with the Malaysian Army.

    Airod signed two agreements - technical services and basic ordering - with Pilatus Switzerland for support of the Royal Malaysian Air Force's (RMAF) PC-7 MK I and MK II aircraft.

    The agreements were sealed following the signing of letter of intent between the Malaysian Government and Pilatus last December to purchase nine additional PC-7 MK II to meet RMAF's requirement for training aircraft.

    Airod has been providing comprehensive maintenance, repair and overhaul services to RMAF's fleet of PC-7 MK I aircraft since 1997 and PC-7 MK II since 2001.

    The signing of the agreements with Pilatus strengthens Airod's reputation as the preferred choice for aircraft maintenance in the country.

    Other agreements include:

    CASS Technology Sdn Bhd and United Arab Emirates-based TagStone to explore opportunities in integrated radio frequency identification technology solution for the intelligent weapon control system in UAE; and,

    The collaboration between Satang Jaya Sdn Bhd and South Africa-based Denel (Pty) Ltd's optronics division to establish a maintenance and repair facility to handle warranty and after-sales services for defence observation and surveillance systems.

    Deputy Defence Minister Datuk Zainal Abidin Zin and ministry's Secretary-General Tan Sri Subhan Jasmon witnessed the signing ceremony.

    -- BERNAMA
