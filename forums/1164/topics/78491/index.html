    Basic is you must be 1st medically fit 1st.

    2 ways you can ensure you get into OCS

    1) One study real hard. A very good result will ensure you a place in OCS, even if you are not that fit.

    2) The other is for those result not too good but still a pass. Than you still have a chance, you go in the good old hard way. You have to be fit, mentally suitable ( answer surveys correctly ), show leadership, common sense, recommended by Platoon instructors, preform well in situation tests.

    Just be yourself, the instructors can see who carry balls, acting for them to see. That is why you see some expected to go in will not.

    It is good to make known your intension of wanting to be a guard officer in BMT interview with your Platoon commander. At the end of BMT, select infantry as the choice instead of support.

    If you are chosen for OCS ( infantry ) also make know your choice is guard unit. After OCS (infantry) , you will need to go for convertion course for Guard, before you can become a guard officer.

    Life as a officer is not as elegant or fun or galmour. First you have to go through hell in OCS. After that you still face a lots of stress or responsibilities and the same time trying to earn the acceptance and respect of your man and sgts.

    Hope this help you.
