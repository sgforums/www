  • Unsung_Hero
    9 May 04, 14:08
    You stand a good chance of selection if you have poly or jc education and:

    1) Above average fitness viz IPPT and SOC times
    2) Show maturity and some leadership ability, especially during evaluation period
    3) Inform your PC and OC (if you get a chance) that you want to go to OCS, and ask them to recommend areas you need to improve on when you have your interview session.
    4) Platoon-level best recruits (usually three identified by PC who then vie for company best recruits) are always earmarked for OCS.
    5) Also doesn't hurt to be more on most of the time.

    Even if you are supposed to go to Police or other service branch, as a platoon/company best you will most likely get re-routed to OCS, which has first call on high potential pes A cat trainess.

    And generally, yes it helps if you were in NCC and a good performer there, not because your record necessarily follows you, but you have an advantage in some basic skills over the other recruits.
