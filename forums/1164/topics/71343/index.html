    Originally posted by kopiosatu:
    most of the time i hear about the aircraft just dumping fuel into the sea... not sure if burning was part of it.
    You're right, dumping extra fuel is part of the procedure when aircraft have to make an emergency landing, but burning fuel is certainly not part of it.

    The Dump and Burn made by the RAAF Pig at AA is part of their display routine. It is done by simply dumping fuel and lighting the afterburners. Mix open flame with a stream of volatile avgas streaming out behind the aircraft and voila! Dump and Burn!

    Because of the location of the Pig's fuel dump (lower fuselage between the exhausts - see attached pic), it is one of few planes able to do this safely. Still its a forbidden stunt in the USAF (when they operated -111s) and only practiced by the Aussies
