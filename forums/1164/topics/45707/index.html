    Sukhoi jet deal set to be sealed

    BY ZULKIFLI ABD RAHMAN
    KUANTAN: Malaysia is expected to finalise a RM1bil deal with Russia to buy a fleet of Sukhoi SU-30MK multi-role combat aircraft soon.

    Talks between Malaysian and Russian officials on the purchase of the aircraft have been going on since 1997, when Malaysian officials expressed interest in creating an RMAF squadron comprising an all-new aircraft to complement the air defence roles of the F/A-18 Hornets and the MiG-29 aircraft.

    The two-seater Sukhoi equipped with Russian and European avionics, would be similar to the SU-MKI aircraft used by the Indian Air Force.

    The aircraft can fly at Mach 2.35 and has a service ceiling of about 18,000m.

    It can cover 3,680km and be equipped with several types of missiles and laser-guided bombs.

    Defence Minister Datuk Seri Najib Tun Razak, who will receive a courtesy call by Russian counterpart Sergei Ivanov today, is expected to make an announcement on the deal.

    The sources also said the RMAF might buy several F/A-18E Super Hornets from the United States to complement the Sukhoi.

    Purchase of Russian jet likely

    Tony Emmanuel

    KUALA LUMPUR, May 18: Malaysia is likely to make a positive announcement on the purchase of the
    Russian-manufactured Sukhoi Su30MKM multi-role fighter jet for the air force.

    This was the conclusion aviation observers reached following the ar-rival of Russian Defence Minister Ser-gei Ivanov at the Kuala Lumpur International Airport today. He is on a three-day official visit and is scheduled to call on Defence Minister Datuk Seri Najib Tun Razak at Wisma Pertahanan tomorrow.

    "This is a positive indication that the Su-30 is the choice for the Royal Malaysian Air Force," the observers told the New Straits Times.

    A Russian Embassy spokesman said the minister's visit was at Najib's invitation and several meetings have been scheduled over the next two days.

    They are the fifth Malaysia-Russia joint committee on defence co-oper-ation and the third Malaysia-Russia defence industry co-operation (working group).

    On whether there would be any announcement on military hardware procurement, specifically fighter jets, the spokesman said: "Any announcement would be made at the appropriate occasion."     The Sukhoi MKM is a variant of the Flanker customised to Malaysian specifications. A similar variant, the Su-30MKI, is currently in use by the Indian Air Force.

    Sources disclosed that another item expected to be discussed is the scheduled visit of Russian President Vladimir Putin.

    "While dates are tentative, plans are under way to schedule the visit before October," the sources said.

    On the purchase of the Flanker, the sources confirmed that the air-craft was under evaluation by the Defence Ministry. The proposal to buy the Sukhoi Su30MK was made in the 1990s but shelved due to the Asian financial crisis.

    Revived three years ago, it was the second arms procurement negotiated on barter trade terms between the two countries.

    In 1994, Malaysia purchased 18 MiG-29 Fulcrum fighter jets for a total of US$600 million (RM2,280 million). Besides the MiG-29, USmanufactured F/A-18D Hornets are also in service with the air force.

    Malaysia eyes 18 Russian Sukhoi fighters

    Malaysia is planning to purchase 18 Russian-made Sukhoi Su-30MK fighters in a deal worth US$900 million, and the contract will likely be inked this year.

    In a news conference on Monday, Defence Minister Najib Tun Razak explained the move as part of Malaysia's modernisation programme.

    Hi people,

    I think before we start a flame war across the (Johor) straits, lets examine what this purchase implies in terms of
    1. balance of power in the region
    2. RSAF responses
    3. RTAF responses
    etc.

    I think there are a few things we can learn from this purchase, one being that Malaysians (and Indonesians) are more willingly to purchase Russian made equipments regardless of logistical issues.
    On paper, SU 30 MK series version is very potent. It has all the flight characteristics of the SU27 plus the additional thrust vectoring... (cannot confirm if this is true on the RMAF MKM version but certainly true on the Indian MKI version). The avionics suite may also include the French Thales stuffs. All these make the aircraft the most sophisticated fighter in the region.

    So the question now is "What will the 18 SU-30 MKM do to the balance of power in the SEA region?" My guess is that, all of a sudden, you will see RMAF sticking a claim to be the most advanced air-force in the region. They would probably claim that they are equal if not better than both RSAF and RTAF.

    So what will be the responses from these two air-forces? I don't really know. But as for RSAF, the next generation fighter, presumedly a Euro canard, should theoretically match, if not better the SU-30. Both RSAF and RTAF Vipers, in my view, can still be upgraded with the latest Active Array radars, request US to release the Amraams and match the SUs in BVR shoot outs. I also believe that Vipers can still hold their own, when properly employed, in dogfights. I also see the needs of RSAF to give each Viper a targeting pod in the form of either the Israeli made Litening II/III or the Sniper XR pod.

    I also believe that the skills of the pilots, plus the logisitic maintenance of the aircrafts will determine who is stronger at the end of the day.

    For RMAF to acquire, firstly the Mig29, then the F-18Ds, then now the Su-30, and later the F-18E/Fs, not to forget the Hawks too, man oh man, it will definitely be a logistical nightmare.

    On the other hand, I also see the needs for RSAF to carefully choose the next gen fighter. I would say that Rafales and Typhoons would be my choice given that the Strike Eagles and the Super bugs do not present much advantage over the Sukhois.

    Just my 2 cents.
