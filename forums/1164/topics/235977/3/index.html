    That'd be the SdKfz 234/4, the only model (8 wheeler SdKfz 234 hull) carrying the 75mm Pak 40 L/48 AT gun, the other models that carried 75mm guns carried the 75mm Kwk 37 L/24 which is essentially a low velocity infantry support gun. It must be pointed out though that the 75mm armed armoured cars were relatively uncommon, comprising only about 300 out of approx 4,000 armoured cars produced (all models). Most common main armament was still the 20mm autocannon. Most famous,IMO, was of course the SdKfz 234/2 "Puma" with the 50mm Kwk 39 L/60.

    In any case, the Pak 40 was not mounted on the SdKfz 234 for the purpose of allowing the recce force to engage the enemy. The decision to mount the Pak 40 (resulting in the SdKfz234/4) was the decision of Hitler himself ... supposedly as an emergency measure to increase the number of mobile anti-tank guns. Something the Germans were in dire need of in the late war period.

    This meant that the SdKfz 234/4 was no longer a recce platform per se but more a Pakwagon, or mobile/SP AT gun. If anything, the conversion to mount the Pak 40 resulted in the SdKfz 234/4 losing the distinguishing feature of most SdKfz recce hulls, ie. the additional rear facing driver position. Useful for getting the hell out of dodge when you make contact, a vital ability for a recce platform
