    Legally, confirm if you've officially volunteered, or "got volunteered" to extend your reservist stint. There's this SAF scheme called ROVERS, or Reservists On Voluntary Extended Reserve Service, which requires an initial 3-year commitment for the qualified who're "keen enough"; find out if your status comes under this category. You may be able to opt-out if you weren't partial to making the decision yourself in the 1st place.

    I don't think a medical downgrade can achieve what you want, unless it's for something truly crippling. Presumably you're already Category Z, in which case they'll likely reclassify your PES status & let you continue your military appointment - i.e. they're separate issues & not necessarily causal-linked. I mean, they don't even redeploy soldiers with hypertension to the reservist CQ/rations people nowdays (as they would during NSF). Instead, they're excused from IPPT, but otherwise carry on as usual with their kakis trying to lessen the physical workload & ensuring they take their medicine.

    I think income ceiling can a bigger "deterrent" by comparison; do you make enough money that the SAF couldn't affordably call you back unless they have to?

    Continue pushing both your senior officer & employer, in order to concretely reflect your unhappiness. If your boss values your work, he'll sign countless deferment requests if need be - & the 1st line of obstacle isn't actually MINDEF or the SAF, but your CO or equivalent (who'll acede to or reject your deferment). Who knows, there may not be an alternate make-up stint available if you're a KAH with a higher-level outfit.

    Michaelteo, I sympathize with your predicament but now that the facts are established, I don't think you're in that great a quagmire that you made it out to be. Had me misconstrued there for a while ....

    Basically, you can't escape these 2 truths:

    - you're still on operational-ready status, unlike your other reservist cohorts who're being "stood down" & being reposted to, in reality, a non-active unit (PDF). Yes, that's depressing - blame it on the roll of the dice, that's all. It's not really different from a corporal who finished 6 ICTs after joining in midway, then had to serve 3 more ICTs with another fresh BN due to his NSman liability.

    - KAHs are supposed to go for upgrading courses, period. Medical downgrading isn't going to change this, unless your fitness's reclassified down to PES C9L2 or lower (I think).

    Accordingly, I don't think you should think too far ahead & then calculate on a risky, but ultimately ill-advised move. In some ways, I'm speculating instead that you can actually maneuver around with the hand you've been dealt with.

    1stly, going on Medical Board takes time; I don't think you can just make an ad-hoc phone call for a review. Rather, you'll have to report sick in your next call-up (ege. SCSC course, or FFI checkup) & then ask the MO to forward your documents. In other words, there's a bureaucratic process to go through, & they'll formally board you when they're ready. Until that date, it's likely you won't be excused from your present circumstances (as long as it's not overly physical-taxing). By all means, see how far you can get out of this means, but don't expect the moon immediately.

    2ndly, it's a bit SAF-backstabbing to suggest this but I think aiming for a deferment can help your case big-time. Think about it: you only have X years left in your 16-year window, & SCSC courses aren't organized impromptu. How many chances left to go for CO training if you can get yourself "postponed" from one preparatory attendance due to civilian factors?

    [Skip the next part if you're already posted out or on a holding list, completely at the mercy of where the SAF could send you to.]

    3rdly, I dunno if it's deliberate but my own experience indicates that it's both prevalent & preferable that an unit's KAHs recyle on TOGETHER to the new outfit, rather than breaking up the team & leaving each on his own. Thus, your CO's longevity can affect your expected promotion, in the sense that he may not formally "stand down" (& may even re-upp under ROVERS) until your own window is ending. There's a chance you may never climb higher than S3/2IC despite your new qualifications, or do CO for just 1-2 ICTs before the ROVERS people contact you. [As for suddenly being faced with 3+ week ICTs again, see "Truth 1" above.]

    Take it one step at a time. Even if your worst nightmare comes true & you end up commanding a SIR unit, there's always the "delegate-down" leadership style - instead of hands-on - though you'll probably be stuck with a "condemned" rep ~smile~

    Don't think I can help much more; maybe other posters can offer something ...
