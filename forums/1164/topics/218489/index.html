    The PC-21 is quite unlike any turboprop trainer made in the past. With its swept back wings, strengthened fuselage and much more powerful engine, it is capable of maneuvering at close to jet speeds. Its performance exceeds the S-211 in many aspects and will give some of the other basic jet trainers a run for their money. Avionics wise, it is equipped with an open architecture which can accept upgrades easily as they become available. It costs considerably less to operate than a jet and overall, I think the RSAF made a good decision regarding the purchase of this capable trainer. Pilatus has a long history of making great aircraft and the PC-21 would be no different.
