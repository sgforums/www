    Originally posted by khaiseng:
    not really, booster charge is used to "boost" the det wave along the det cord when the distance between charge is more than 2om (i think)
    For the life of me I can't remember what the distance between boosters is supposed to be, but you're essentially right. The actual explosive charge is detonated by a shock wave and not heat. The shock wave is provided by the PETN in the det cord as it detonates, but as it burns along, more force is directed outward than along the length of the cord. As such, the shock wave diminishes along the length of the cord, and the detonation of the boosters is used to increase the shock wave, a top-up, as it were.
