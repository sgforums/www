    With the SAF being built up around NSF it will have a strength of over 300,000 men and women - including Regulars.

    It will allow for more effective Command & Control for MINDEF HQ to deal through a few Division Commanders, rather then micro-manage a large number of Brigade Commanders from MINDEF HQ, or some other Campaign HQ; and leaving more Battalion Commanders to co-ordinate with each other at a lower subordinate levels in the field.

    It will only lead to confusion and communication breakdown during Battle.

    If one Platoon consist of 40 men, a Company with 3 Combat Platoons will have 120 men PLUS its own Company HQ strength of another 40 personnel - totalling 160 men.

    A Combat Battalion will have 3 Combat Company PLUS Battalion HQ and Logistics will have approximately 600 men and women.

    A Brigade will have 3 such Combat Battalions, PLUS Attached Specialist Support Platoon from the various Specialist Arms - that will include Combat Engineers, Artillery, Signals, Medical, Logistics.

    All the primary and sub-groups within a Combat Brigade will need careful micro-management, and the Brigade Commander will be assisted by his HQ Staff.

    The Division Commander's Role will be to see the broader picture of its assigned sector of the War Zone, and will co-ordinate the activities within his War Zone with that of the Other Zones.

    All of which will contribute to the entire effort in winning the War under the guidance and Command of either a Campaign General, or directly under the Command of the SAF Chief of Defense Force, aided by the respective Chiefs of the respective Services (Air, Army and Navy).

    When one understand the structure of the chain of command, it will be difficult to see the rationality of trying to remove the Division Commander's role in this Order of Battle.

    The role of the Division Commander will be required, when a War of several fronts is being waged, across great distances.

    If ever a Division Commander's role is no longer deemed necessary, a great reliance will have to be made of modern day C4 and I equipment, to allow a greater directional control of the Brigade Commanders from the Campaign HQ.
