Pearl Harbor disabled a large chunk of the Pacific fleet less the USS Langley was reduced to delivering P-40s to reinforce the island of Java against Japanese invasion - it was lost here. CV-2 Lexington was lost in the battle of Coral Sea, where the two fleets battled to a draw, where the US lost a fleet carrier but prevented the Japanese from reaching their objectives.

    With the hindsight of history we know that this was the turning point, for the next battle at Midway was the beginning of the end for the Japanese. Still the US lost the Yorktown at Midway, the USS Wasp to a submarine in September. and the Hornet at Santa Cruz. The sinking of the USS Enterprise was the ONLY operating US carrier in the Pacific, from late October to early December 1942.

    So despite the Japanese losing carriers at Midway, it was far from a foregone conclusion.

    As far as the Royal Navy was concerned, after the disastrous Force Z (Prince of Wales and Repulse) defeat, remnants of that fleet escaped to Java, combined with a cobbled-together force comprising of Dutch, British, Australian and Australian ships, known as ABDA. No carriers and hardly enough stuff to prevent Japanese landings on Java and in Bali. The Japanese went as far as to bomb Darwin though no landings were made on the Australian continent.

    The Japanese also took their carriers toward India, flying raids on Ceylon, intercepting merchant shipping, and sinking the British HMS Hermes in the process. Shortly thereafter Japan's efforts turned back to the Solomons and trying to invade Guadalcanal and Papua New Guinea.
