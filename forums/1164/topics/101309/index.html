    I read 2 translated novel/accounts written by former Vietcongs/NVA.

    Can't remember the names. My favourite one was written by a former NVA. Had the word "Bugles" somewhere in the title.

    You think the US soldiers had a hard time in Vietnam? Their tour lasts 1 year or something...

    The NVA fought till they die, too badly wounded or if they are lucky enough to survive, the full ten years or so of the entire conflict.

    They, too, were demoralised. They didn't fight for ideology. They fought to stay alive and for their buddies.

    They, too, took drugs to keep themselves sane in the jungle. But they didn't buy them, they knew which plants in the jungle to smoke or chew to get high.

    After the war, the Vietnamese veterans also went home to a mixed reception. Even though they are victors/heroes, the soldiers in the end were the losers. They got discriminated by potential employers who feel they are too difficult to employ as workers being more used to fighting and killing. The civiilan police targeted former soldiers as potential criminals. They lost their girlfriends to other people who didn't go to war etc. Basically the same shxt deal the US veterans got.

    I used to think the communists in Vietnam were the bad guys but afterwards, I feel they were just fighting for their freedom and way of life.
