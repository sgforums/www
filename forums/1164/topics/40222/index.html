    Malaysia buys Norwegian ammunition

    NAMMO AS of Norway and SME Ordnance SDN. BHD. (SMEO) of Malaysia have signed a long-term agreement covering co-operation on production of 25mm ammunition for the new Malaysian Armoured Combat Vehicle, ACV 300. The Malaysian Army has chosen the unique Nammo Multipurpose ammunition 25x137mm MP-SD-T MK2 and the US designed W-APFSDS-T ammunition for use in the ACV 300.
    Nammo is co-operating with its strategic partner, General Dynamics Ordnance System, GD-OTS, for the deliveries under this agreement.
    The co-operation between SMEO and Nammo AS covers transfer of technology and know-how from Nammo to SMEO, supplies of equipment and components, and assembly of complete rounds in Malaysia. The co-operation will give significant production and delivery possibilities for Nammo and deliveries will start already end of 2002.
    Nammo?s President & CEO Edgar Fossheim expresses his satisfaction with the signing of the long-term agreement adding that the Malaysian Defence Forces will get the best ammunition available in the world market for their ACV. 300.
    SMEO and Nammo already have a similar agreement giving SMEO and the Malaysian Defence forces access to Nammos 12,7mm Multipurpose ammunition through local co production by SMEO.
    Nammo views the signing of the agreement covering 25mm ammunition to be a very important step in establishing itself as a long-term partner for SMEO and the Malaysian Defence Forces.
    Both companies have expressed their intention to further develop the relationship to also include other ammunition requirements and to work to increase the Malaysian content of the co-operation.

    The "ULTRA" in the SAF are upgraded to the M-113 A3 standards, with Detroit Diesel Engines producing 275 hp (previously of A2 standards and firing on 212 hp Petrol Engines) and changed Allison Auto Transmission following the A3 upgrade programs available from United Defense LP

    Ref:
    http://www.fas.org/man/dod-101/sys/land/m113.htm

    While the ACV300 produced by FNSS Defense System of Turkey has the same M113 platform but upgradable according to several different upgrade kits available.

    The ACV300 manufactured for the MAF has its engine uprated to 300 hp Diesel also from Detroit, as well as matching Allison Transmission Gear Box.

    Full air-conditioning and NBC protection is available for the ACV300, but nothing indicates if the MAF version will include this.

    To all intent and purpose, the ACV300 would actually be identical to the improved version of the M113A3 developed by United Defense LP

    Ref:
    http://www.army-technology.com/contractors/armoured/fmc_nurol/index.html

    The basic hull of the Ultra and the ACV300 comes from the M113 which is a Aluminium box; with add on laminate steel plate for armor protection, and composite materials available to both types.

    See the following site for the FNSS work in Turkey.

    http://www.army-technology.com/contractors/armoured/fmc_nurol/index.html

    While the ACV300 will probably be equipped with the Bushmaster 'sharpshoote' cupola armed with a 25mm gun, the ULTRA feature an Overhead Weapon System featuring a 25mm Gun without a cupola, with the gunner inside the hull of the APC/IFV.

    .http://www.stengg.com/land/la_automotivegrp.html#Tracked

    According to Janes Defense:
    [Quote]
    Malaysian vehicles
    Late in 2000, Malaysia placed an order for 211 vehicles in 10 variants based on the ACV 300 version with a 300 hp Detriot Diesel.

    M113 upgrade programme
    FNSS Defense Systems is also offering to upgrade the M113 family of vehicles into the latest A300 configuration which includes a 300 hp 6V-53T Detroit Diesel engine coupled to an Allison X200-4 automatic transmission.

    According to FNSS Defense Systems, the A300 configuration provides higher mobility, higher internal volume, improved cooling performance in extreme conditions and better survivability. A full NBC and air conditioning system is also available in this configuration.
    AIFV equivalent suspension, steering, fuel and electrical systems are available in M113 A300 configuration with various other optional equipment to meet different customer requirements.
    [/quote]
