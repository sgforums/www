    The Malaysian Army's recent purchases have puzzled me. The T-84 from Ukraine and the Russian T-90 from Russia are considered more potent MBTs than the Polish version of the T-72. I was expecting the T-90 to win the Malaysian MBT competition, considering the fact that the Indian Army already operates the type and could well pave the way for much defense cooperation given the selection of the Su-30MKM by the MAF which is almost identical to India's Su-30MKI.

    The selection of the Turkish ACV series of IFVs have also been puzzling. The selection of a platform based on the classic M113 which has almost reached the limit of its developmental life does not augur well for the long term. Perhaps the decision was influenced by the fact that the Malaysian alrady operates the K200A APC, itself also based on the M113. The BMP-3 would have been a better choice, given its compatibility with the growing number of Eastern Bloc equipment in Malaysian service as well as offering greater firepower and lower ground pressure.

    The purchase of the Pakistani version of the Chinese Red Arrow 8 ATGM, Russian Metis-M(AT-7 'Saxhorn') ATGMs, British Rapier FSC SAMs, Pakistani Anza Mk 2 SAMs, Brazilian Astros II MLRS and South African G5 155mm howitzers, amongst a host of other equipment acquired from at least half a dozen countries, would present the Malaysian Army with an inconvenience to maintain at best, and a logistical nightmare at worse.

    Of course the above views are purely my own. I am writing from a neutral point of view in which the recent purchases of our Northern neighbours are evaluated from a 3rd party. Any differing views are welcome.
