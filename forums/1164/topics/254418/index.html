    Also you must realize the dirty little secret of armours with ceramics like Chobham armour and its second gen incarnation Dorchester is that over time their CE protection might degrade due to cracks forming in the ceramic layer due to internal stresses caused by the tank going over rought terrain or metal expansion and contraction. So it requires some upkeep to make sure the protection level is still there.

    A reason why despite the effectiveness of ceramics, some other armour makers still decide to go with other packages for the protection requirements, such as the perforated Tungsten/Titanium package of the Leo 2A4 and the like.

    In any case one can see why the Brits decided to keep news of the penetration under wraps... you don't exactly want such news reaching the insurgents or the parties that are equipping them until you've figured out the proper reason why this happened, and the solution to it.

    On paper the Challanger 2 ought to be the most CE protected tank in NATO, if not the world.
