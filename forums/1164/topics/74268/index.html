Jakarta plans compulsory military service

    High school graduates may undergo basic military training for two months

    JAKARTA - Indonesia's Defence Ministry is drafting a Bill that would make it compulsory for the nation's youth to do military service.

    The Ministry's Director General for Defence Strategy, Major-General Sudrajat, said that under the Civilian Participatory Defence concept, the youths would be recruited and trained to support the army's strike force.

    He said that Indonesia needed more defence personnel on the ground.

    The ministry, he said, was not focusing on maritime defence for now, despite the fact that Indonesia is an archipelagic country.

    'We are drafting the legislation on national defence, that mandates us to regulate the use of the Indonesian military (TNI) as the main component of the state's defence, and the use of reserves and support units,' Maj-Gen Sudrajat said over the weekend.

    He said that another aim of the programme was to boost national character-building and 'implant nationalism' among youths.

    Based on the proposal, which is being discussed by the ministry's officials, the draftees - high school graduates of both sexes aged 17 years or above - would be obliged to undergo basic military training for two months.

    With its population exceeding 215 million, Indonesia has around 400,000 military personnel, most of whom are in the army. But the country also has 17,000 islands.

    Maj-Gen Sudrajat, however, conceded that the proposal would need further discussion, especially regarding funding.

    'I'm not sure whether the state could implement such a programme in the next five or 10 years.'

    He added that the military has been capable of overcoming the threats the country had faced thus far, suggesting that there wasn't really an urgency.

    Indonesia imposed mandatory military service in the 1960s when the country fought the Dutch over West Papua.

    At that time, Indonesia spent huge sums to buy weaponry from Russia, including tanks, MiG jet fighters and trucks. However, it continued to lack military personnel.

    -- The Jakarta Post/Asia News Network
