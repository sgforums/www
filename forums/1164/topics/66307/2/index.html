    All infantry in Iraqi Freedom 2 are issued body armour, the interceptor vest capable of stopping a 7.62.

    Did it work? Well the facts out are as figures.

    The death rate, despite heavy fighting was only SEVEN a day, the lowest rate ever for major combat in recorded history. Of all the allied soldiers killed, most of them were killed by hits to unprotected parts of their body (face, neck and legs) and almost no deaths were caused by torso hits (the most common cause of death in combat). Only in unusual cases where the bullet entered by an odd angle and bypassed the vest or a hit by a large caliber weapon was a torso injury caused.

    By comparism, most of the iraqi wounded the allies treated suffered from torso injuries which effectively put them out of the fight. In many cases an allied soldier was hit by a bullet and knocked down, and back in the fight the next instant. Does body armour work and save lives? The answer is yes, and it works very well.

    Go back further in history and note that many of the rangers who were wounded or killed in the battle of the black sea (black hawk down incident), were so because they opted to remove the composite plates in their ranger body armour to save weight, that would have saved them from an AK 47 round. Most telling was the case of Sgt. Casey Joyce, who was shot from the back by an AK 47, the bullet ruptured his heart and he died. He had left out his back plate and the round penetrated his vest, ironically, to embed itself in the front of this vest, where he did keep a plate. This incident was reproduced in the movie, if you saw it.

    Body armour in military applications works, and saves lives. And not wearing them during peacetime does not make sense, as we need to train out troops to get used to using them.

    Why neglect using body armour and sustain a 70 percent higher loss of life? So many people needlessly lost because they did not wear their protective equipment. People who could had fought on and given the enemy trouble, people who have families and loved ones.
