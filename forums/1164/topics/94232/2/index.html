    SE Asia doesn't need it; even Australia hasn't commissioned another since its last one delisted in the '80s. The reality is no regional country has extended overseas interests to finance a AC; the various countries' existing airbases & naval bases are operationally appropriate enough, augmented by the ASEAN & FDPA arrangements.

    Like other posters on this board, I can foresee strengthening/upgrading of the purer maritime forces (both individually & collectively) though. But, in Singapore's case, my feeling is we'll choose to bolster the naval bases more instead, if only to faciliate better the American & Commonwealth taskforces that occasionally visit our shores (& indirectly act as a deterrent).

    That's basically been our historical load to carry, even as we sought to protect ourselves militarily post-independence. The whole pre-WWII "Singapore Strategy" - i.e. the building & upkeep of the British Naval Base at Sembawang - was that it's ill-affording to station a permanent fleet within the region to protect colonial interests, but a local facility can be established from which operations can be launched.

    The new Changi Naval Base - cost between $33m-$35m - should thus be viewed from this perspective, vis-a-vis the regular FDPA exercises that partly launched from the facility.
