    Covering that amount of body would certainly not be a lightweight body armour system.

    Interestingly, the technology and expertise of body armour has largely been spearheaded by the Law Enforcement agencies, rather than the Military.

    Body armour can be made to specification to whatever threats you would like. Typically, the heavier the threat round, the greater the protection. But the trade-off is in determining your threat. If you set your protection level too high, you are going to get a bulky and uncomfortable piece of equipment that the individual soldier is not going to use most of the time.

    If you set it too low, than it is just extra weight that doesn't perform and worse, you offer your soldiers false confidence in wearing it.
