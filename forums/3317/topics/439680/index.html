Let maids rest, urges Archbishop

    SINGAPORE: The head of the Catholic Church in Singapore is calling for a rest day every week for domestic workers, the latest in a chorus of voices here urging for time off for maids.

    Addressing the 200,000 local Catholic population to mark the World Day of Migrants on Oct 30, Archbishop Nicholas Chia noted that some employers here may not trust their helpers, feeling they would "get into trouble" if given time off.

    Employers may also feel they cannot cope without help to take care of a child or elderly parent, he said.

    But in the message published on the archdiocese's website, he called for respect for one another, especially for those living under the same roof.

    "If you enjoy having a day off during the week, would it not follow that your domestic helper would also enjoy and appreciate this 'luxury'?" he said.

    He pointed out that while one in six families employs a domestic helper in Singapore, only 53 per cent of maids receive one day off per month, and about 12 per cent give their helpers one day off per week.

    Archbishop Chia said migrant workers must be paid fair wages, given adequate food and ample time to rest each day, and the ability to reunite with their families on a regular basis.

    He also called for the "disturbing trend" of foreign wife abuse and trafficking of women and children in Singapore to stop.

    In June, Minister of State for Community Development, Youth and Sports Halimah Yacob said Singapore should consider legislation requiring employers to give their domestic helpers a rest day every week, to help reduce issues such as stress and overwork that domestic workers are facing.

    -- TODAY
