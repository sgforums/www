SG needs to be careful not to bend to external influences

    SINGAPORE: Deputy Prime Minister Teo Chee Hean said Singapore needs to be careful not to bend to external influences indiscriminately.

    Speaking at the National Community Engagement Programme Dialogue on Saturday, Mr Teo said that as an open society, Singapore is always subject to external influences.

    He noted with the widespread use of the internet, many people are increasingly forming online groups.

    They are also finding common ground with others who may live in different countries with different social contexts.

    Thus, Mr Teo said it's important to be mindful about absorbing teachings or practices which may not be appropriate for Singapore.

    These will threaten the country's social cohesion, and risk Singapore's social capital.

    He believes that if one group or religion pushes hard to have its views and beliefs applied to society, others will also want to do the same.

    So, practices must be adapted to suit Singapore's local context, and be aligned with its social norms.

    Mr Teo observed that with an increasing trend of greater religiosity, there is an increasing tendency towards assertiveness and projection of religion into the public sphere.

    He stressed it's therefore even more important than before that people respect the beliefs and views of others.

    In addition, they must not be tempted by the cloak of anonymity offered by the internet to denigrate the faiths of others, otherwise social relations will deteriorate rapidly.

    Mr Teo also reiterated key principles and values that have allowed Singapore to grow and prosper as a multi-racial and multi-religious society.

    These include ensuring a common space where Singaporeans of all creeds and races can interact together, keeping religion separate from politics, and taking a proactive and practical approach, when handling sensitive issues.
