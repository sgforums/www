    CHC $13m bond purchase 'made no sense'

     

    External auditor Sim Guan Seng yestersday said he was concerned when City Harvest Church (CHC) bought a $13 million bond from Xtron Productions.

    That purchase was later amended and raised to $25 million.

       The managing partner of Baker Tilly said he had questioned the purchase of bond from Xtron, which used to manage pop singer Sun Ho, the wife of CHC founder Kong Hee.

       He also said the arrangement, in which Xtron used the bond money to buy a Riverwalk property and lease it to the church "didn't make sense to me".

       When asked by Deputy Public Prosecutor Christopher Ong why he had concerns about the purchase's commerical rational, Mr Sim said buying the property directly would have saved the church in legal fees and made things clearer from the audit view.

       But church board members told him that religious organisations could not buy commercial property, so he asked them to seek legal advice to confirm.

       Mr Sim also said that the client-auditor relationship must be transparent.

      E-mail exchanges showed Xtron's out-sourced accountant Serina Wee discussing with church people matters that did not concern them.

       Kong's No.2, Tan Ye Peng, also asked her to arrange for "off-the-record meeting" with Baker Tilly's then managing partner Foong Daw Ching.

       It seemed they did not want the auditors to know certain things or show a link. Said Mr Sim: "It means they are trying to hide something."

       Kong and five deputies are accused of misusing more than $50 million of church money through sham bonds to further Ms Ho's singing career and covering it up.

    - Koh Hui Theng

     

    News, The New Paper, Tuesday, January 21 2014, Pg 4
