    SINGAPORE: To celebrate the Lunar New Year, it is usually customary to feast on "yusheng", a popular dish symbolising prosperity in Chinese culture.

    To mark this year's festivities, hotels and restaurants in Singapore have introduced new varieties of the dish.

    Some chefs have come up with a Japanese-inspired version of yusheng, and they expect the variety to sell well, given many Singaporeans' love for Japanese food.

    Another version uses fruits to give the dish a lighter flavour, and its makers said this variation was created to appeal to the tastebuds of youths.
