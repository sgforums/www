Guys who study in a single-sex school may affect marriage

  • QX179R
    1 Dec 11, 19:00

    STOMPer anson highlights a 2009 survey on marital success in which 17,000 adults were interviewed. One of the findings of the London Institute of Education survey was that men who went to single-sex schools were more likely to be divorced -- and depressed -- by their early 40s.

    There was no significant link for women, but the survey suggested that there was a "statistically significant positive association between single-sex schooling and wages for women". Another possible conclusion was that, "boys learn better when they are with girls and they actually learn to get on better."

    The findings of the research were presented by Professor Diana Leonard, from the university, at a conference at the Perse School for Girls in Cambridge.

    Wrote the STOMPer:

    "This study shows that boys taught in single-sex schools are more likely to be divorced or separated from their partner than those who attended a mixed school by their early 40s.

    "However, those who stayed together were just as likely to be happy in their relationship as men educated in mixed schools -– when asked to rate the quality of their marriage on a scale of one to seven.

    "The research covered 17,000 adults who had been taught in a range of institutions from private boarding schools to state comprehensives.

    "The majority had been brought up in day schools."
