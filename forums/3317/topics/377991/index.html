    Sydney court finds Tiwary guilty of double murder of Singaporean flatmates

    SYDNEY: An Australian court hearing the re-trial of Ram Tiwary on Tuesday found him guilty for the double murders of his Singaporean flatmates Tan Poh Chuan and Tay Chow Lyang.

    In September 2003, Tiwary's two Singaporean housemates were found brutally murdered in Kingsford. All three were engineering students at the University of New South Wales.

    Tiwary, who was on a Singapore Armed Forces study award, was arrested seven months after the murders, when he tried to leave Australia for Singapore.

    In 2006, Tiwary was convicted of both murders and sentenced to life imprisonment without reprieve. However, Tiwary was granted a fresh trial at the court of appeal last year.

    Tiwary claimed at the first trial to have slept through the murders. At the retrial which started five weeks ago, he changed his story, saying that he had hid and barricaded himself in his room.

    However, the evidence of bloodstains on Tiwary's hands, shorts and feet pointed to his guilt.

    It was also found that Tiwary's baseball bat was used as a murder weapon. In this trial, Tiwary was represented by Senior Counsel David Dalton, who made several fiery addresses to the jury.

    The jury of eight women and four men at the Supreme Court of New South Wales deliberated for close to 17 hours but could not reach a unanimous verdict.

    The state of New South Wales can accept a majority verdict of 11-1 after a minimum of eight hours of deliberation.

    The judge ruled that sufficient time had passed, and after a brief recess, the jury delivered the guilty verdict.

    Later, a resigned Tiwary - now 30 years old - was led away in an armoured truck, back to prison in Lithgow, three hours northwest of Sydney.

    The court meets again for sentencing on November 13. Each of Tiwary's two murder charges carries a maximum penalty of life imprisonment. Tiwary's family did not comment on the verdict.
