    Three Singaporeans have been arrested for being involved in a $300,000 drug operation. 

    Two of the three men are suspected drug traffickers and were caught with 1.2 kilogrammes of heroin. 

    They also had drugs like ‘Ice’, Ketamine and ‘Ecstasy’ tablets. 

    The Central Narcotics Bureau also seized two Singapore-registered cars and cash of more than $22,000.
     
    CNB officers caught one of the suspected drug trafficker at Minto Road. 

    They subsequently arrested the other suspect at the junction of Sims Way and Geylang Road. 

    The third male suspect was arrested at Mattar Road. 

    The trio, aged between 42 and 49 years-old are all unemployed. 

    If convicted, the two drug traffickers may face the death penalty.
