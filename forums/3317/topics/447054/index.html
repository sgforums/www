  • QX179R

    KUALA LUMPUR - The cattle company owned by the family of Cabinet minister Shahrizat Jalil on Friday admitted it purchased several luxury condo units, saying they were investments made during a lull in its cattle business.

    The National Feedlot Corporation (NFC) said in a statement that it had bought the condominium units, including three in Singapore, as investments to generate returns on its RM250 million (S$104 million) government loan.

    'The property market is sound and secure, more protected and less volatile than others,' chief executive Wan Shahinur Izmir Salleh, Ms Shahrizat's son, said in a statement on Thursday. 'NFC retains the prerogative to invest the funds in the best interests of the company.'

    He was swiftly pilloried by critics in Malaysia's opposition, who pointed out that the soft loan from the government was expressly meant for cattle farming.
