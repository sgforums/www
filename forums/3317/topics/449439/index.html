SG woman jailed for life in Thailand for ordering murder

  • QX179R

    Diana Goh Li Fun, a Singaporean woman, has been given the life sentence in Thailand for organising a professional hit on a former Canadian model.

    She is now serving her sentence in Nakhon Si Thammarat prison.

    Diana told the police that ex-model Francis Alex Degioanni, 34, was her business partner who reportedly borrowed millions of baht from her, then refused to pay her back as they had no written loan agreement.

    She claimed that because of this, she had to contend with monthly interest payments of around 100,000 baht (S$4,300 at the time), and Thailand website Phuketwan.com reported that she borrowed 1.5 million baht (S$61,500) to hire a hitman to murder Francis.

    On Feb 19 2009, the victim's 34th birthday, he was going to meet his friends who had prepared a surprise birthday party for him.

    Two men shot him eight times while he was sitting inside his own car in front of his home office before fleeing on a motorcycle.

    Diana and five other people were arrested, and the two gunmen were identified as Somchai Mingjaiyen and Suwan Simet.

    Sumon Sumen, Diana's friend, was arrested for helping to plan the murder and putting the money for the gunmen in a bank account.

    Eakapon Lakrod, Sumon's friend, helped in the planning by observing the victim's behaviour, as well as deciding on the place of execution.

    Netnapa Witayatamsopon, another of Diana's friends, was also accused of planning the murder and of finding the gunmen.

    Phuketwan.com reported that Goh and four accomplices were sentenced on Dec 29, and a sixth case against an alleged accomplice is still pending.
