    TAIPEI, Taiwan (AP) - Japan has agreed to give Taiwan fishing rights in waters near a group of Japanese-controlled islands in the East China Sea that are also claimed by China and Taiwan.

    In a deal signed on Wednesday, Japan agreed to let Taiwanese boats fish in a part of its exclusive economic zone near the islands. They are known as Senkaku in Japan and Diaoyu in China.

    A Taiwan government statement hailed the deal as enabling the sides to jointly tap the rich resources there.

    Japan's compromise could be seen as a way to prevent Taiwan from forming a united front with China in the dispute, though Taiwan has also disputed China's territorial claim.
