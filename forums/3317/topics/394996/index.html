    Cathay Pacific staff mocks S'porean accent and English on public website


    STOMPer Christine is upset with how a Cathay Pacific staff was mocking a girl for her Singaporean accent, saying that she had awful English, and posting the comments on a public website.

    Said the STOMPer:

    "An overseas friend of mine alerted me to a current campaign by Cathay Pacific.

    "A purser with Cathay is recounting how she was inspired by a Singaporean girl whom she met while studying in USA.

    "An excerpt of her comment is as follows: '....even though her English was horrible with a strong Singaporean accent...'

    "Not sure about the rest of you but I was rather livid when I read that. Firstly, I think Singaporeans are English educated and when the situation calls for it, we are capable of speaking proper English.

    "I have lived overseas for the last 10 years and never had a problem because I always made it a point not to speak Singlish unless I am with Singaporeans.

    "Secondly, I am amazed that Cathay saw it fit on a professional level to post such a comment on a global campaign.

    "I am Singaporean and I am not ashamed of my Singaporean accent or speaking in Singlish with my Singaporean friends.

    "I have never lost the accent although I have lived away for so long. It makes me feel closer to home everytime I get to speak Singlish with people who understands me. Like it or not, Singlish is part of being a Singaporean.

    "Makes me wonder if I ever take a flight with Cathay and the staff is the Purser on the flight, would she then judge me for my 'bad' English?

    "Or perhaps she will congratulate me on my good English although I am Singaporean?

    "I cannot help but wonder, in the even of an emergency, would she be able to understand and help me despite my Singaporean accent?

    "No prize for guessing. I will never travel Cathay again. I think I am better off travelling with an airline that respects my accent."
