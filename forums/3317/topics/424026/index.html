    NEARLY half of Singapore's residents are not happy with the chilly indoor environments they deal with regularly, due to low air conditioning temperatures.

    This is according to a poll of over 450 people in Singapore conducted by WWF Singapore for Earth Hour 2011.

    As a result of the low temperatures, many have faced health problems, such as flu, cough and dry skin. The poll was set up to determine how comfortable people are with air-conditioning temperatures in the country and to help encourage individuals and organisations to set their air-conditioning temperatures at 24ï¿½C or higher.

    Some 52 per cent of respondents felt that cinemas were too cold; offices and schools followed closely with 40 per cent indicating the same. Almost half of the poll takers compared the temperature in their office/school to 'Autumn in Seoul' (where you could find the temperature ranging from 5ï¿½C to 15ï¿½C), while one in four likened it to 'Winter in Siberia' (where it could range from -23ï¿½C to -45ï¿½C).

    To further encourage energy saving, WWF Singapore plans to launch the '60+' iPhone application, which enables users to 'check-in' their planet-saving acts to earn badges and achievements as well as share their achievements through Facebook.

    The Government has been driving efficient use of energy as a key strategy to mitigating emissions in the country.

    -- ST
