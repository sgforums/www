    Probe of ex-MFA senior official raises eyebrows

    SINGAPORE: Mr Lim Cheng Hoe's suspension from duties and investigation by the Commercial Affairs Department (CAD) over alleged improper claims for expenses abroad came as a surprise to some who had worked with him.

    Mr Lim was a familiar face in overseas ministerial and presidential visits as the chief of protocol with the Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MFA).

    "For him to do this doesn't make sense. He's waiting to retire," said a source, who declined to be named.

    Apart from the fact that Mr Lim held a senior position, it is understood that the reimbursement policy for claims at the MFA are fairly stringent. For example, for overseas visits, hotel rooms are assigned to specific individuals along with the duration of stay. A guest list and receipt are to accompany claims for meals.

    The protocol chief is usually accompanied by two staff officers, who would be the ones settling payment for the meals.

    As for dining engagements that take place in Singapore, restaurants would usually send their invoices directly to the MFA.

    Mr Lim, who was awarded the Public Administration Medal (Silver) in 2009, is the only official being investigated in this matter.

    Responding to media queries, the Public Service Commission (PSC) said that Mr Lim has been interdicted with effect from June 21.

    The case was brought to the PSC's attention on June 19, the same day that the MFA suspended its 38-year veteran. Mr Lim's case was referred to the CAD the next day.

    The PSC added that it did not receive any other case of improper claims made by civil servants in the past year.

    Civil Service disciplinary proceedings would be initiated if there are adequate grounds to suspect an officer of misconduct, the PSC said.

    But it would not say whether such proceedings would be started against Mr Lim.

    "For misconduct that involves criminal charges, Civil Service disciplinary proceedings concerning those charges will only take place after the court conviction. This ensures that the outcome of the court proceedings is not prejudged, and that the officer is treated fairly," it added.

    The MFA, which earlier said that it was "very sad that this has happened", said on June 21 it is reviewing its procedures to see what additional safeguards may be necessary for the approval of expense claims.

    A ministry spokesperson said: "As investigations into this case are ongoing, we cannot comment further and we should not pre-judge the outcome of the investigations on the causes of Mr Lim's transgressions."
