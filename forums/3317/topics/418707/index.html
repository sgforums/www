Emergency landing in Romania after Singaporean ill

  • QX179R

    BUCHAREST: A Royal Dutch Airlines flight made an emergency landing in Bucharest on Tuesday afternoon when a pregnant Singaporean onboard fell seriously ill.

    The 28-year-old Singaporean was travelling with her husband to Amsterdam for a holiday.

    Their vacation turned sour 10 hours after takeoff from Singapore, while they were flying over Russia.

    The woman reportedly felt very sick and pilots decided to land in Romania.

    Henri Coanda International Airport spokesman Valentin Iordache said: "At about 5.50am local time, a message was sent to the airport to await an emergency medical landing here.

    "The plane landed at 6.48am. Medical emergency crew from the airport got in the plane and tried to give first aid to the passenger".

    The woman had almost no pulse and blood pressure and was immediately rushed to Romania's Elias Hospital.

    Doctors discovered she was suffering from extrauterine pregnancy, and had to remove part of her uterus in order to save her.

    She is now clear of danger, and doctors say she will still be able to bear children.

    Elias Hospital's Dr Alexandru Filipescu said: "The patient is now feeling very well, as compared to the state of shock when she was brought in yesterday. She is balanced and she has regained all of her functions".

    The woman's husband, who did not want to be identified, said his wife underwent a medical scan before the trip and was assured she would be able to cope.

    He said: "The scanning showed that things were okay, so we (made) preparation and (took) precautions.. It's not a thorough check-up, but the pictures showed that it's quite okay, that's why".

    He said they would stay on in Romania for observations, if necessary.

    -CNA/wk
