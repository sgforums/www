Woman says 'S'poreans too weak" sparks outrage online

  • QX179R
    19 Apr 12, 12:06

    A woman sparked a massive outcry on the Internet with an insensitive remark about the death of a full-time national serviceman (NSF) who collapsed during training on April 17.

    The NSF, 21-year-old Private Dominique Sarron Lee Rui Feng, died after he experienced breathing difficulties during a training exercise on Tuesday.

    After news of the NSF's death was reported online, the woman, Ms. Zheng Huiting who is currently pursuing a bachelor's degree in fine arts in Australia said in her Facebook post, "Singaporeans too weak? LOL (laugh out loud)."

    A screenshot of her post went viral yesterday after it was reposted on websites and online forums. Netizens were quick to condemn her actions, with many calling her 'a disgrace' and 'disrespectful'. As of last night, the post on hardwarezone had garnered more than 80,000 views and 900 comments.

    STOMPer AngryBird, who came across the woman's posting, wrote:

    "How could a 21-year-old girl make such an insensitive comment?

    "Should let her undergo some NS trainings to see if she still can LOL or not!

    "It's so insensitive of her to say what she said."

    Yesterday, Ms. Zheng posted an apology on the hardwarezone forum. She also added that her first thought was that 'Singapore should have better measures as to who are able to take physical exercises'.

    She has since shut down her Facebook profile page, blog and Twitter account.

    Defence analyst Ho Sun Huang called Ms. Zheng's remarks 'ill-timed and inappropriate' but cautioned, "The death of a citizen soldier and national service are emotional issues that should not be taken lightly, but with the Internet, it is easy for people to say things too quickly without thinking about the repercussions."

    Meanwhile, the wake of the NSF, the elder of two siblings, is being held in Tampines Street 11. A military funeral will be held on Saturday.
