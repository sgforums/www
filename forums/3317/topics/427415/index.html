SIA stewardess says SIA discriminates Taiwanese employees

  • QX179R
    9 Apr 11, 16:37

    STOMPer Buay Tahan was furious to see this 'ungrateful' SIA flight attendant from Taiwan complaining about SIA on the Taiwan news. She claimed that SIA promised a salary of S$3.8k that turned out to be only S$1.3k.

    In the news clip, the woman, who was named Miss Liu, claimed that SIA deceived the public with promises of a basic NT88,000 (S$3,800) pay when she only received NT30,000 (S$1,300).

    The woman also claimed that the treatment of Taiwanese employees was inferior to that of Singaporean employees, stating that the latter was able to fly to worldwide destinations, but the Taiwanese attendants could only fly to regional destinations and had to return within a day.

    According to her, Singaporean SIA stewardesses could transfer to ground duties after 15 years but Taiwanese stewardesses could not. Also, there were no pregnancy benefits for them.

    The STOMPer commented:

    "An ungrateful Taiwanese air stewardess alleged that SIA's salary of NT88,000 was actually only NT30,000 after some deduction and commented that there is a huge difference between the benefits of a Singaporean and a Taiwanese stewardess.

    "I wonder why SIA have to go overseas to search for air stewardessed when there are plenty of choices available in Singapore.

    "Now, it is a 'foreign talent' that complains about the company on the broadcast media."
