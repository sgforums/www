    SATS' Q4 net profit up, wins Aussie contract from SAF

    SINGAPORE: Singapore Airport Terminal Services (SATS) says its fourth quarter net profit rose 10.2 per cent to S$46.5 million.

    The improvement came on the back of higher revenue, which gained 19.6 per cent on-year to S$390.6 million.

    SATS says its revenue rose due to the full consolidation of its Singapore Food Industries unit and higher aviation revenue.

    For the full year, SATS' profit stood at S$181.2 million, an increase of 23.5 per cent over the previous year.

    Revenue also jumped by 45 per cent on-year to S$1.54 billion.

    Better performance reported by the firm's units in Indonesia and Hong Kong saw overseas contribution grow by 89 per cent on-year to S$41.9 million.

    Going forward, SATS expects to see an improvement in activity levels in this financial year.

    That's because with the economic recovery, airlines will gradually reinstate capacity with increased flights and cargo throughput.

    Separately, SATS says its wholly owned Australian food logistics subsidiary, Primary Industries Queensland, has secured a 224 million Aussie dollar contract.

    It won the 5 plus 5 year logistics support contract from the Singapore Armed Forces for its Shoalwater Bay Training Area in Australia.

    Under the contract, SATS will provide a full range of services, including catering, supplies support and transportation.

    SATS says the new contract is not expected to have any material impact on its earnings for the financial year ending in March 2011.
