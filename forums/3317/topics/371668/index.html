    Boy falls from 9th floor, suffers just fractured ankle

    SINGAPORE: Saved by the bushes - that's probably what happened to a three-year-old Korean boy who survived after falling nine floors from his apartment in Singapore on Monday.

    Goldenhill Park along Mei Hwan Drive is five years old and has all the facilities expected of a modern condominium. But one simple device was missing from the apartment rented by the family of Park Sihu.

    Window grilles were not installed in all the rooms and this resulted in Sihu falling down nine storeys. According to Sihu's mother - who declined to be interviewed on TV - the boy was playing in the study room where he is not allowed inside because the window does not have grilles.

    When the maid found him missing in the apartment, she went downstairs and found the boy crying amongst the bushes. The maid carried him back to his home where paramedics treated him for abrasions and cuts.

    He was then taken to the KK Women's and Children's hospital in a conscious state. Apart from a fractured left ankle, doctors found no concussion.

    Sihu has two sisters aged 10 and 12 years.

    The family has lived in the apartment for one and a half years. Sihu's mother told Channel NewsAsia that she will ask the landlord to install window grilles in the apartment.

    - CNA/ir
