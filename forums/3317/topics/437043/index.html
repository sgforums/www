Maid spikes employer's soup with menstrual blood

  • QX179R
    29 Aug 11, 15:50

    (THE STAR/ASIA NEWS NETWORK) - China Press reported on a domestic maid who mixed her menstrual blood in soup she cooked for her employer in Singapore because she was upset over not being allowed to use the telephone.

    Apparently, the 38-year-old maid put a soiled cotton pad into tea bags used to make soup. Her 40-year-old employer known only as Tan said the maid was initially hired by her aunt and had been a diligent worker.

    Thus, Mrs Tan decided to hire her to take care of her 89-year-old grandmother who was living with another relative. However, she claimed that the maid eventually became lazy and spent much time chatting on her mobile phone till past midnight. She said the maid even invited men to the house when the relative was not around.

    Mrs Tan claimed that due to the maid's negligence, Mrs Tan's grandmother once fell down and fractured her pelvic bone. The relative then prohibited the maid from chatting on the phone. Subsequently, the relative found a teabag filled with cotton pads tainted with blood in the kitchen and told Mrs Tan about it.

    -- ST
