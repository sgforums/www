    SINGAPORE: The Singapore Flyer, the world's largest observation wheel, has been closed since 1pm Sunday.

    The Singapore Flyer says the ride was shut after a cable dislodged, affecting the air-conditioning system.

    It believes the recent thunderstorms and torrential rains may have caused the malfunction.

    Restoration works to fix the air-conditioning are in progress, and the Flyer is expected to resume operations on Wednesday, July 21.

    Those who have purchased advanced tickets can either get a refund or return on another date.

    S'pore Flyer resumes operations after 2-day closure due to air-con glitch

    SINGAPORE: The Singapore Flyer - the world's largest observation wheel - resumed operations at 2pm Tuesday.

    The Flyer was closed on Sunday afternoon at 1pm after one of its electrical cables supplying power to the air-conditioning systems was struck by lightning.

    Thereafter, the management evacuated the some 200 passengers and stopped the wheel.

    The Singapore Flyer says the air-conditioning repair works have now been completed.

    It says the Flyer also has a lightning protection system.
