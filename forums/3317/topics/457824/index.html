    SINGAPORE: A 25-year-old man has been charged with attempting to lure a girl into committing an indecent act.

    Tan Ji Beng allegedly sent a text message to the 14-year-old girl, asking her to show him her breasts.

    The incident took place in June this year.

    The bespectacled man, who was dressed in black, told the court on Wednesday (29 August) that he plans to plead guilty.

    The court has issued a gag order to protect the girl's identity, as she is a minor.

    Tan will next appear in court on September 5.

    If found guilty as a first-time offender under the Children and Young Persons Act, he could be jailed five years and fined $10,000.

    GEORGE TOWN: Deputy public prosecutor Lim Cheah Yit will file an appeal today against the Sessions Court's decision to bind over 22-year-old electrician Chuah Guan Jiu for three years to be of good behaviour after he was found guilty of statutory rape.

    Lim said: “I'm disappointed (with the judgment). It had been a consensual act between the two parties but the girl is still a minor.”
