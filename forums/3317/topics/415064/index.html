    TAIPEI - TAIWAN is considering imposing chemical castration in a bid to stem sex crimes by repeat offenders, senior minister said on Wednesday.

    'We call it 'chemical treatment' ... the government will carefully handle the issue if a public consensus is reached,' Interior Minister Jiang Yi-huah told parliament.

    Personal safety issues will be on the agenda of the island's first government-sponsored women's congress scheduled for March 2011 aimed at promoting women's rights and gender equality, he said.

    Calls for chemical castration mounted after a case of a sex offender who allegedly molesting two girls just 17 days after being released on parole while wearing electronic monitoring anklet, local media said.

    Another offender, convicted of sexually assaulting three girls, reportedly admitted before his release last month after serving an 11-year jail term that he feared he would commit the crime again.

    Taiwan could become one of the few governments in Asia to legalise chemical castration and such a move would require parliament's approval, officials said.

    -- AFP
