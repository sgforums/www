    Singapore Medical Association censures two doctors for professional misconduct

    The Singapore Medical Council has found two doctors to be guilty of professional misconduct.

    The first is Dr Zubin Medora, a general practitioner at Medora Centre for Aesthetic Medicine located at Camden Medical Centre. 

    The 37-year-old has been censured for misrepresenting himself as a trained and accredited plastic surgeon when in fact, he was not. 

    He had also performed liposuction on a patient leaving the patient with deformities and scars. 

    Dr Medora was fined $10,000.

    The second case involved Dr Tay Tiong Hum from Tay Clinic at Block 20 Chai Chee Road. 

    He had inappropriately prescribed Benzodiazepine or valium to his patients. 

    Long term consumption of the drug may lead to drug dependence. 

    He had also failed to properly document his patient's diagnosis, symptoms or conditions. 

    Dr Tay was suspended for 3 months and fined $1,000.
