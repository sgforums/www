Unruly passenger on SIA flight fined $15,000

  • QX179R
    19 Jan 10, 11:05

    An unruly passenger who created a scene on board a Singapore Airlines flight last month was fined a total of $15,000. 

    Eustonian ship engineer Vadim Beljanin was on board a flight from Frankfurt to Singapore in December when he was drunk and disorderly. 

    According to court documents, Beljanin was seen drinking from a bottle of whiskey which he had brought onto the flight. 

    A few hours later, Beljanin was drunk and was annoying passengers. 

    He tried to kiss another passenger, repeatedly punched the LCD monitor until his fist bled and assaulted an air steward. 

    It took three stewards to restraint him, and the plane made an unscheduled landing at Dubai Airport to drop off the accused. 

    Total costs incurred by Singapore Airlines because the unscheduled stop at Dubai, extra fuel and other damages? 

    Almost $50,000. 

    Before sentencing, District Judge Low Wee Ping said the seriousness of the offence called for a stiff fine. 

    Beljanin, who was unrepresented, had pleaded guilty to two charges of being drunk on the flight and imperiling the safety of the aircraft earlier this month. 

    Under the Air Navigation Order, he could have been fined up to $20,000 and jailed for up to 15 months on the first charge and fined up to $100,000 and jailed up to five years for the other. 

    It is not known if the $15,000 fine is the stiffest sentence handed out so far. 

    Three years ago, Briton Gareth Gaudion was fined $5,000 for being drunk and molesting an air stewardess during a Singapore Airlines flight from London to Singapore. 

    He also shouted at other cabin crew and also verbally abused Minister Mentor Lee Kuan Yew, who was on the same flight.
