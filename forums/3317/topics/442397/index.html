    SINGAPORE: Singapore's former Minister Mentor Lee Kuan Yew has proposed a S$100 million bilingualism fund to enhance the teaching of mother tongue languages to pre-schoolers.

    Speaking at the launch of his new book, "My Lifelong Challenge: Singapore's Bilingual Journey", Mr Lee said the money will be used to expose pre-schoolers to both English and their mother tongue in the first few years of their life.

    Mr Lee also said it will also be used for key projects to raise the capability of schools and the community in promoting bilingualism.

    The Lee Kuan Yew Fund for Bilingualism will supplement efforts by the Education Ministry.

    To kick-start the fund, Mr Lee will autograph 200 special edition copies of his latest book which will be sold at a minimum of S$10,000.

    He will donate the proceeds of the signed copies - of at least S$2 million - to the Lee Kuan Yew Fund for Bilingualism.

    Mr Lee will also add another S$10 million of his own money to the kitty.

    In addition, businessmen Philip Ng and Ong Beng Seng have donated S$5 million and S$2 million respectively.

    President of the Singapore Chinese Chamber of Commerce and Industry, Chua Thian Poh has pledged S$1 million.

    The Khoo Teck Puat Foundation has also pledged S$1 million.

    Mr Lee's three children will collectively donate S$200,000.

    In 1980, one in ten primary one students came from predominantly English-speaking homes.

    This proportion grew to nearly six in ten in 2010.

    The new book by Mr Lee tells the story of his 50-year struggle to transform Singapore from a former British colony into a nation, where everyone who knows English also knows his own mother tongue.

    In it, he also reveals why he did away with vernacular schools in spite of violent political resistance, why he closed Nanyang University, why he later started Special Assistance Plan schools, and why he continues to urge all ethnic Chinese Singaporeans to learn Mandarin.
