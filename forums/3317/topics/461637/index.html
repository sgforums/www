Foreign spouses may help reduce foreign labour dependence

  • QX179R
    19 Nov 12, 19:40

    SINGAPORE: Mr Yeo Guat Kwang, chairman of the Migrant Workers' Centre, suggested Singaporeans' foreign spouses could help reduce the country's dependence on foreign labour.

    He made the proposal at a Deepavali celebration for foreign workers on Sunday.

    "At the moment, all our employers are looking to other countries with the help of intermediaries to bring in foreign workers. But in fact, in Singapore, many locals have foreign spouses," said Mr Yeo.

    "We will also request for MOM to provide the similar level of training support for the foreign spouses to help them because they give us Singaporean children, and they are with Singaporeans," he added.

    Mr Yeo said the Manpower Ministry has already implemented schemes to make it easier for foreign spouses to work in Singapore.

    When they apply for work permits, they are not subjected to quotas, restrictions from country source, and they do not pay any levies.

    Separately, Mr Yeo said Singapore should aim for a foreign workforce that has quality and not quantity.

    He urged the Ministry to set a qualification requirement for those seeking work permit renewals.

    He said a better-skilled workforce would be more productive and cost-efficient for the economy and better-educated workers would also be less prone to abuse.

    He said that over the last three years, he has seen a large increase in the number of distressed foreign workers - from 1,200 to 1,500 a year. Many are low-skilled and vulnerable.

    - CNA/xq
