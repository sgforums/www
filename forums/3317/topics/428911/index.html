ComChest seeks to raise S$55.7m this year

  • QX179R

    SINGAPORE : Community Chest (ComChest), the fund-raising arm of the National Council of Social Service (NCSS), wants to raise S$55.7 million in funds for its financial year beginning April.

    The amount is barely a 1 per cent increase from the S$55.2 million it raised last year.

    The group said it needed the money to fund 158 social service programmes run by 70 charities.

    Abandoned children and those from poor families, as well as troubled youth, continue to be the group's main beneficiaries, with a funding budget of S$27.3 million for this year, or 49 per cent of the total allocation.

    The group has set aside S$12.7 million for the disabled, S$10.1 million for financially distressed homes, and S$5.6 million in aid for the elderly.

    It is also setting up its 38th service centre in Telok Blangah Way as part of its efforts to make social service more accessible to the needy.
