    SINGAPORE: REACH Chairman Dr Amy Khor said the government will hear all views and suggestions, but not all can be taken into account.

    She was speaking on radio about the national conversation on the future of the country.

    She said: "I think that the key really is to close the loop. Not all suggestions can be taken into account, of course. We will hear all views and all suggestions, but as in all things, there will always be trade-offs.

    "There has to be balance because there are diverse views but what is important is that we close the loop. So, I think if suggestions are not taken into account, we should explain why they cannot be taken into account."

    Singaporeans are also giving their take on the national conversation, online. Some said that it is timely for the country to rally together and define what "Home, Hope and Heart" means. Some have also asked how the government can make the conversation a two-way and collegial one.

    Others, however, asked how different this exercise will be from the existing government feedback portal, if government policies have reflected Singaporeans' views, and whether leaders are ready for "hard truths".

    -CNA/ac
