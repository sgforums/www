CPF maintains rate on Special, Medisave accounts

  • QX179R
    15 Jun 12, 13:08

    SINGAPORE: Central Provident Fund (CPF) members will continue to enjoy a risk-free interest rate of four per cent on their savings in the Special and Medisave accounts from July to September.

    The CPF Board said this is in line with the government's announcement in September last year to maintain the four per cent per annum floor rate for interest earned on all monies in the Special and Medisave accounts, and the Retirement Account until the end of this year.

    Savings in the Special and Medisave accounts currently earn either four per cent or the 12-month average yield of 10-year Singapore Government Securities (10YSGS) plus one per cent, whichever is higher.

    The interest rate on Special and Medisave accounts is adjusted quarterly, based on interest rates on 10YSGS over a preceding 12-month period.

    The average yield of the 10YSGS plus 1 per cent, from 1 June 2011 to 31 May 2012, works out to 2.72 per cent.

    CPF Board said the interest rate on Special and Medisave accounts payable to CPF members from 1 July to 30 September will thus be maintained at the current floor of four per cent.

    - CNA/al
