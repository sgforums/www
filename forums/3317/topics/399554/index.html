    A Factual Appraisal Of The 'Holocaust' By The Red Cross  :  The Jews And The Concentration Camps

     

    There's a survey of German concentration camps which is honest and objective, Report of the International Committee of the Red Cross on its Activities during 2nd World War, Geneva, 1948.

    The Red Cross ( ICRC ) applied the Geneva convention to gain access to civilian internees held by German authorities but was unable to gain any access to Russian camps. The millions of civilian and military internees held in USSR, whose conditions were the worst, were cut off from international contact or supervision.

     They distributed food parcels to concentration camps in Germany. (Vol. 111, p. 78). The ICRC established contact with camp commandants and a food relief programme.

    The Report states "9,000 parcels were packed daily. >From the autumn of 1943 until May 1945, about 1,112,000 parcels weighing 4,500 tons were sent to concentration camps" (Vol. III, p. 80). In addition to food, there were clothing and pharmaceutical supplies.

     

    ### The ICRC complained that obstruction of their vast relief operation for Jews came not from the Germans but from the tight Allied blockade.

    ### The ICRC praised the liberal conditions at Theresienstadt up to April 1945. This camp, "where there were about 40,000 Jews was relatively privileged" (Vol. III, p. 75).

    ### From information gathered by the Committee, this camp had started as an experiment by leaders of the Reich ... These men wished to give the Jews the means of communal life in a town under their own administration with almost complete autonomy. . . two delegates were able to visit the camp on April 6th, 1945." (Vol. I, p . 642).

    ### The ICRC complained that it never succeeded "in sending anything to Russia" (Vol. II, p. 62).

    ### The same situation applied to German camps after "liberation" by the Russians. The ICRC received a flow of mail from Auschwitz until Soviet occupation, when internees were evacuated westward.

     

    ###The most important aspect of the Red Cross Report is that it clarifies the true cause of those deaths that occurred in the camps.

    ### Says the Report: "In the chaotic condition of Germany during the final months of the war, the camps received no food supplies at all and starvation claimed an increasing number of victims.

    ### Alarmed by this, the German Government informed the ICRC and discussions between the President of the ICRC and General of the S.S. Kaltenbrunner gave decisive results. Relief could henceforth be distributed by the ICRC, and one delegate was to stay in each camp ..." (Vol. III, p. 83).

     

    ### It is important to stress that delegates of Red Cross found no evidence at camps in Axis occupied Europe of any policy to exterminate the Jews.

    ### In its 1,600 pages the Report does not mention a gas chamber. It admits that Jews, like many other wartime nationalities, suffered rigours and privations, but its complete silence on the planned extermination is ample refutation of the Six Million legend.

    ### So far as the genuine mortality rate is concerned, the Report points out that most of the Jewish doctors from the camps were being used to combat typhus on the eastern front, so that they were unavailable when the typhus epidemics of 1945 broke out in the camps (Vol. I, p. 204 ff)

    ### Claims that mass executions were carried out in gas chambers disguised as shower facilities. The Report makes nonsense of this allegation. "Not only the washing places, but installations for baths, showers and laundry were inspected by the delegates. They often take action to have fixtures repaired or enlarged" (Vol. III, p. 594).

     

    ### Volume III of the Red Cross Report, Chapter 3 (I. Jewish Civilian Population) deals with the "aid given to the Jewish section of the free population," and this chapter makes it plain that not all Jews were placed in internment camps, but remained as part of the free civilian population.

    ### In Slovakia, where Eichmann's assistant Dieter Wisliceny was in charge, the Report states that "A large proportion of the Jewish had permission to stay in the country, and Slovakia was a comparative haven of refuge for Jews. Those in Slovakia were in comparative safety.

    ### While the law of May 15th, 1942 brought the internment of several thousand Jews, these people were held in camps where the conditions of food and lodging were tolerable, the internees were allowed paid work on terms almost equal to those of the free labour market" (Vol. I, p. 646).

    ### Large numbers of the 3 million or so European Jews avoid internment altogether and emigration of Jews continued throughout the war via Hungary, Rumania and Turkey.

     

    ### Many Germans died of typhus. One account of a boy aged 12 described how he had typhus. He was in a hospital ward with dozens of other typhus victims. He said a gas bottle was wheeled into the ward and Zyklon gas was released in an effort to kill all lice, etc, while the patients were in their beds.

    ### Zyklon gas was supposedly claimed by the Jews to be used in the gas chambers.

    Absurd Cremation Claims

    Cremation specialists confirmed that thousands of corpses could not possibly have been cremated every day throughout the spring and summer of 1944 at Auschwitz, as commonly alleged.

    Mr. Ivan Lagace, manager of a large crematory in Calgary, Canada, testified in court in April 1988 that the Auschwitz cremation story is technically impossible. The allegation that 10,000 or even 20,000 corpses were burned every day at Auschwitz in the summer of 1944 in crematories and open pits is simply "preposterous" and "beyond the realm of reality," he declared under oath.

     

     

    Gassing Expert Refutes Extermination Story

    America's leading gas chamber expert, Boston engineer Fred A. Leuchter, carefully examined the supposed "gas chambers" in Poland and concluded that the Auschwitz gassing story is absurd and technically impossible.

    Leuchter is the foremost specialist on the design and installation of gas chambers used in the United States to execute convicted criminals. 

    In February 1988 he carried out a detailed onsite examination of the "gas chambers" at Auschwitz, Birkenau and Majdanek in Poland. In sworn testimony to a Toronto court and in a technical report, Leuchter described every aspect of his investigation.

    He emphatically declared the alleged gassing facilities could not possibly have been used to kill people. Among other things, he pointed out that the so-called "gas chambers" were not properly sealed or vented to kill human beings without also killing German camp personnel.

    Dr. William B. Lindsey, a research chemist of 33 years at Dupont Corporation, testified in a 1985 court case that the Auschwitz gassing story is technically impossible. Based on a careful on-site examination of the "gas chambers" at Auschwitz, Birkenau and Majdanek, he declared: "I come to the conclusion that no one was willfully or purposefully killed with Zyklon B [hydrocyanic acid gas] in this manner. I consider it absolutely impossible."
