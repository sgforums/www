    SINGAPORE: Two Singaporeans are among this year's Forbes' top 100 World's Most Powerful Women.

    SingTel chief executive Chua Sock Koong returns to the list, coming in at 74.

    She was left out of the list last year.

    Temasek Holdings CEO Ho Ching is at number 76, down four places from her spot of 72 in 2011.

    German Chancellor Angela Merkel retains her number one spot, followed once again by US Secretary of State Hillary Clinton and Brazil's President Dilma Rouseff.

    The youngest on the list is 26-year-old Lady Gaga who came in at number 14.

    Britain's Queen Elizabeth the second is the oldest at 86.

    She's 26 on the list.

    Forbes says the women who made the list are all ranked by money, media presence and impact.
