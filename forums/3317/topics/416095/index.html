    S'pore water polo team disrespects national flag with suggestive trunks


    STOMPer zarzarboy feels that the suggestive design of these swimming trunks for the Singapore water polo team at this year's Asian Games disrespects the national flag.

    The STOMPer commented:

    "When I saw the swimming trunks worn by our water polo team at the 2010 Asian Games, I question if such a suggestive design is necessary.

    "The design incorporates the Singapore flag, with the crescent moon and stars provocatively placed near the groin area. The picture says it all. 

    "And when asked by The New Paper if they had sought approval for the design, team manager Samuel Wong told The New Paper, 'No need'.

    "But the Singapore Arms and Flag National Anthem Acts clearly states that there must be approval from the Minister for Information, Communications and the Arts to use the flag as part of any costume or attire, provided there is no 'disrespect'.

    "I feel the design is very disrespectful to our national flag.

    "Why don't we attract attention by getting a gold, rather than coming up with such an inappropriate design?"
