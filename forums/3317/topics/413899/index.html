    By Shaffiq Alkhatib | Posted: 25 October 2010 1515 hrs SINGAPORE: A Singapore Armed Forces corporal was jailed six weeks for giving false information to the police. Mohd Alfia Misnan, 29, made the 999 call on December 26 last year at the Pasir Ris Food Court. He did so after a friend, former police Staff Sergeant Shah Rizal Samsir, also 29, instigated him to do so. For his role, Shah Rizal was last Friday jailed for three months. The ex-policeman phoned Alfia on December 25 last year, asking him to make the false report as he wanted to teach another policeman a lesson for supposedly being rude to him. The SAF regular agreed to help him. He followed Shah Rizal's instructions and made a call at around 1am, telling an officer on duty that he had spotted a policeman arresting a Bangladeshi at Lembu Road in Little India at around 9pm on December 25. Alfia claimed that his wife saw the policeman taking out the Bangladeshi's wallet before helping himself to the items inside. Because of this false accusation, 28-year-old Staff Sergeant (SSG) Ng Keng Lin was investigated for theft. The court heard that SSG Ng and his colleagues had indeed arrested three Bangladeshi nationals at Lembu Road on the night the so-called theft. While at a lock-up however, one of foreigners reported that money was missing from his wallet. Shah Rizal exploited this piece of information to seek revenge against SSG Ng. For agreeing to help his friend make the false report, Alfia could have been jailed up to a year and fined a maximum of S$5,000. -CNA/wk
