    HONG KONG: Busty Hong Kong model Jessica Cambensy is out on bail after Hong Kong police charged her for theft on Monday, following a four hour-long visit to a police station in Tsim Sha Tsui.

    Police alleged that Cambensy, the spokesmodel for a large lingerie company, had taken HK$600 (S$98) which a forgetful woman had left behind at an Automated Teller Machine (ATM) in Tsim Sha Tsui.

    According to the police, the forgetful woman realised she had not taken the money she had withdrawn from the ATM and immediately returned to collect her cash, only to find the money gone.

    She then called the police and investigators found that the woman's HK$600 vanished after Cambensy used the same ATM to withdraw money some time later.

    This incident happened on July 30 last year.

    Police said that they only decided to charge Cambensy on Monday because it took them some time to gather enough evidence of her alleged theft, as security cameras did not show her taking the money.

    They added that it is considered theft if an individual holds on to another's lost property and does not report it to the police.

    Cambensy, who was on her way to Taiwan to shoot an action film, appeared calm when Hong Kong media caught up with her at the airport on Tuesday, but soon broke down.

    "I am very sorry if I hurt anyone.

    "I am very sorry. I wish to say more but I cannot. I am really sorry to everybody," said the 23-year-old model in a trembling voice, before she started to weep at an airline counter.

    Her surprised assistant immediately stepped forward to comfort her and send her through to the departure lounge.

    Cambensy is expected to return to Hong Kong in November to deal with the matter.

    -CNA/ha
