    SINGAPORE: A 55-year-old man has been sentenced to 12 years' jail for sexual offences that included outraging the modesty of primary school girls.

    Ng Ban Keong had pleaded guilty to three charges of outraging the modesty of primary school girls and one charge of sexual penetration on February 2012.

    He would carry a thermometer, surgical gloves, or what seemed like a stethoscope to make it look like he was a doctor.

    He committed sexual offences against pre-pubescent girls over a period of six years from 2004.

    In mitigation, defence counsel Subhas Anandan pointed out that his client is a first offender who has a heart problem.

    Mr Anandan said Ng, who is married with two children, had pleaded guilty at an early stage. He added that Ng was suffering from erectile dysfunction at the time of the offence. This led to Ng's sexual frustrations and caused him to do things he normally would not have done.

    Mr Anandan also said his client is remorseful and asked the court to show compassion and sentence Ng to 10 years' imprisonment.

    But the prosecution stood firmly on its ground, saying in its submissions that Ng preyed on young girls as they are vulnerable. As a deterrent, the prosecution asked the court to give Ng 12 years' jail.

    For outraging the modesty of a 10-year-old and two 11-year-olds, Ng was sentenced to five years and three months' jail.

    Ng was also convicted of forcing a nine-year-old to perform oral sex on him on October 26, 2009 at Sims Drive. For this, he received eight years' imprisonment.

    The district judge has ordered three of the sentences to run concurrently, bringing Ng's total sentence to 12 years.

    Earlier, Ng arrived at the Subordinate Court building with a mask and cap. He looked calm as he sat in the dock, listening attentively as the sentence was read out. Meanwhile, some of his supporters were seen teary-eyed as the sentence was read in court.

    It is understood that the defence may appeal against the sentence.
