HDB residents urged to interact with neighbours

  • QX179R
    22 May 12, 11:23

    SINGAPORE - Residents should step out of their flats and interact with their neighbours, and take part in community activities to build friendships, urged Minister of State (National Development) Tan Chuan-Jin.

    As the Housing and Development Board (HDB) kicked off the first event in its inaugural Community Week yesterday, Mr Tan said forging community cohesion is a key priority of the HDB.

    "Homes are really about the community. It is about family, it is about the cohesion, the friendship, the relationships that exist between neighbours, between neighbours on the same floor, floor to floor, block to block," he said.

    "And that is something that HDB is interested in. It is not just about the brick and mortar of building buildings."

    Mr Tan was speaking at the launch of Heartland Youthoria!, an exhibition to celebrate and encourage youth involvement in housing estates.

    A section of the exhibition allows youths to express how they feel about their housing estates. This includes a wall of drawings depicting good deeds performed by neighbours and Lego models of what students think is their ideal community.

    Another section allows students to make a pledge on how they plan to improve their community.

    Heartland Youthoria! kickstarts the Community Week by the HDB - a week-long initiative to build better ties between neighbours and build stronger communities.

    Heartlanders can look forward to a line-up of activities, including a student race across five HDB towns and the first vertical walk in an HDB estate.

    Community Week is part of the overall strategy of the HDB's Community Relations Group, which was formed in 2009 to look into building community ties in the heartlands.

    A key initiative by the group has been the launch of the Heartland Ambassador Programme, where students are trained to spread the word on community living and being good neighbours.

    To date, some 600 students have been trained as ambassadors.
