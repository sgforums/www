Young leaders wanted: PM

  • QX179R
    4 Jul 10, 00:02

    PRIME Minister Lee Hsien Loong wants to build up a pool of young grassroots leaders to better serve a changing community.

    Speaking at a grand dinner to celebrate the 50th anniversary of the People's Association (PA), Mr Lee reminded 5,000 grassroots leaders of the need to bring in new members and steadily hand over leadership positions to a younger generation.

    'It is not always easy for senior grassroots leaders who have spent many years building up their teams and looking after their residents and areas to let go,' he said on Saturday night. 'But all of us are serving the people, and timely self-renewal will make sure that the people get the leadership and service which they deserve,' he added.

    The PA has been on a search for young professionals to join its ranks since new rules from 2003 required grassroots leaders to step down at the age of 65. It has not done too badly on this count: Slightly over one in six - 5,300 - of the 30,000 grassroots leaders are aged 35 and below. And 1,500 of the 4,000 new grassroots leaders recruited last year fell into this category.

    Mr Lee admitted that it was a challenge drawing in young professionals who lead hectic lives, but noted that many constituencies had succeeded by organising activities to entice them first to take part and then volunteer. In his speech, Mr Lee outlined how the grassroots network played a critical role in Singapore's history and paid tribute to its many volunteers for their efforts.

    The PA's activities, he added, are directed at two strategic objectives: to strengthen the link between the Government and the people, and to build strong communities all over Singapore.

    -- ST
