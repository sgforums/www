    Former television actor, Mark Chow Yee Sze has been fined $6,000 for molesting a woman on 10 January 2007. 

    Chow was found guilty of the offence last month. 

    He molested the 27-year-old former pub operations manager while she was asleep in her office in the wee hours of the morning. 

    Chow who's 30, had touched various parts of her body- including her chest, buttocks and left thigh. 

    For doing this, Chow could have been sentenced to up to two years' jail, fined or caned. 

    District Judge Sarjit Singh did not give him a custodial sentence. 

    That's because the judge said, the touch to the chest was fleeting and the others were done over the woman’s clothes. 

    Chow has to pay part of the fine, $2,000, today, otherwise he could spend up to six weeks behind bars. 

    During the trial, the court heard, the woman made a police report after viewing a CCTV recording of the incident. 

    The footage of what Chow did in the office was also shown in court. 

    Chow's defence was that he was merely trying to wake the woman up. 

    His lawyer Subhas Anandan had then pleaded for leniency. 

    He said, Chow had some mental problems as reported by a Changi General Hospital psychiatrist, and was still being treated. 

    Other than this case, Chow has seven other outstanding charges against him and four of them were for molest. 

    He allegedly outraged another woman’s modesty in 2006. 

    It’s believed Chow also committed three acts of criminal intimidation with toy guns at the Institute of Mental Heath on 26 November last year. 

    The hearing for these cases will be held later this month.
