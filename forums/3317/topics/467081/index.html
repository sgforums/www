  • FireIce

    A man was killed and 12 other people are injured after an accident between a lorry and a car along the PIE in the Simei area this morning (Mar 15).

     

    The Straits Times reported that 12 injured victims, consisting of 11 men and one woman, were taken by SCDF ambulances to Changi General Hospital.

     

    The 12 victims were all on the lorry.

     

    STOMPer Tay saw the aftermath of the accident at about 7.30am. 

     

    He said: 

     

    "There was a traffic accident along the PIE towards Changi Airport, in the Simei area. I saw the accident when I was jogging on the overhead bridge. 

     

    "One person was killed in the accident, as you can see from the body covered up with white cloth in the photos.

     

    "A lorry full of workers had overturned, apparently after a collision with a black car. 

     

    "The accident caused all lanes to be blocked."

     

    STOMP is contacting the Police for more information.

    Indian worker killed in PIE crash 2 months before getting married

     

    The construction worker from India was just two months away from starting a new life. He had plans to go home and get married and open a fish farm.

    But yesterday, Mr Rajesh Mahalingam, 29, was killed as a black sedan crashed into a lorry he was in, injuring 10 of his co-workers. The accident happened at the Pan-Island Expressway (PIE) towards Tuas, before the Simei exit.

    The remaining 10 people in the lorry and the car driver and his female passenger were taken to Changi General Hospital (CGH). A hospital spokesman said five of the people involved in the accident were discharged yesterday evening.

    Mr Rajesh’s younger brother, Mr Kalaiyarasan Mahalingam, 24, said a mutual friend told him about his brother’s death at about 8.45am yesterday when he was at work.

    “I cried. I only have one sister and one brother. Just that one brother, he is gone,” he said.

    Read the full report in The New Paper on Saturday (March 16).
