    Police arrests teen who makes online bomb threat on school and police station

    Police said it has arrested a 19-year-old youth who made bomb threats online on a school and a police headquarters.

    The statement from police came after a STOMP posting about a STOMPer who came across the teen's online posting, in which he threatened to bomb Xinmin Secondary School and Ang Mo Kio Police Station.

    Police said in its reply:

    "In response to media queries, Police confirm that a 19 year-old youth has been arrested in connection with bomb threat postings made on social networking websites. Police investigations are on-going.

    "The Police take such threats seriously. Under Section 45 (a) of the Telecommunications Act, any person who transmits or causes to be transmitted a message which he knows to be false or fabricated shall be liable on conviction to a fine not exceeding $50,000 or to imprisonment for a term not exceeding 7 years or to both."
