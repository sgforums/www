    A FOUR-DAY hearing into three sets of appeals by leaders and supporters of the Singapore Democratic Party started in the High Court yesterday.

    The three sets of appeals were over separate cases of illegal assembly and taking part in an illegal procession.

    But SDP chief Chee Soon Juan succeeded in getting one set - over the distribution of fliers in Raffles City in 2006 - adjourned to be heard at a later date.

    Dr Chee, his sister Chee Siok Chin and Mr Gandhi Ambalam were fined $1,000 in December last year over that case. But all three served the one-week jail term in lieu of paying the fine.

    Yesterday, the hearing centred on the illegal assembly and procession held at the driveway of Parliament House on March 15, 2008. This stemmed from a rally organised by the SDP to demonstrate against the rise in prices of basic goods and services here.

    Dr Chee had applied for a permit to hold the rally but was rejected. Yet the rally went ahead, with the SDP and its supporters chanting slogans and holding placards. Members of the SDP were fined $900 and $1,000.

    -- ST
