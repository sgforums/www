    MOE likely to fire Mas Selamat's niece

    SINGAPORE: The niece of terrorist Mas Selamat Kastari, who is in jail for her role in his escape, will be facing disciplinary proceedings by the Ministry of Education (MOE).

    MOE said in a statement that it will initiate the proceedings against Nur Aini Asmom, who is a Malay language teacher, with a view to dismiss her from service.

    MOE said it takes "disciplinary action, including dismissal" against teachers who have been charged and convicted in court for a criminal offence.

    It was revealed on Monday that Nur Aini was sentenced to 18 months' jail for harbouring a prisoner of the State.

    Home Affairs and Law Minister K Shanmugam revealed in Parliament that Nur Aini helped Mas Selamat following his escape from the Whitley Road Detention Centre in late February 2008.

    It was Nur Aini who persuaded her mother Aisah to allow Mas Selamat into their flat in Tampines.

    Nur Aini also helped Mas Selamat to destroy material evidence -- such as his Whitley Road Detention Centre-issued attire.

    The next day Mas Selamat left the flat, but not before Nur Aini applied make-up on him and secured a tudung or headscarf over his head to disguise him as a woman.

    She also gave him supplies, and a map of Singapore.

    Nur Aini's mother and father were also jailed for their roles in harbouring Mas Selamat.

    Mas Selamat freed, deported to Singapore

    UPDATED @ 02:38:47 PM 24-09-2010
    September 24, 2010
    The JI leader will be handed over to Singapore authorities. — Reuters pic

    KUALA LUMPUR, Sept 24 — Jemaah Islamiah’s Singapore leader Mas Selamat Kastari, who was captured in Malaysia last year after escaping a Singapore jail, has been handed back to Singaporean authorities, an official said today.

    “We can say that Mas Selamat has been handed over to Singapore authorities,” said Jamilah Taib, a home ministry spokesman.

    “We don’t know when the handover took place and will release details as we have them,” she added.

    It was reported earlier that Mas Selamat had been freed from the Kamunting detention camp and would be handed over to the Immigration Department for deportation to the island republic today.

    Mas Selamat escaped from Singapore’s Whitley Road detention centre on February 27, 2008 and was captured by Malaysian police in Kampung Tawakal near Skudai, Johor on April 1, 2009.

    He was then detained under the Internal Security Act (ISA) for two years.

    In a statement on Friday, Police Special Task Force Director (Operations/Counter Terrorism) Datuk Mohamad Fuzi Harun said the home minister had cancelled the detention order against Mas Selamat. The order was cancelled at 10.30am the same day.

    It is not known if Mas Selamat’s sudden release and deportation was discussed at Monday’s meeting between Prime Minister Datuk Seri Najib Razak and his Singapore counterpart, Lee Hsien Loong.

    Malaysian authorities, with intelligence from Singapore, had tracked him down last year to Kampung Tawakal in Skudai, Johor.

    He had been living in the village of about 100 people for almost half a year before police ambushed him in a dawn raid last April.

    A Singapore Internal Security Department assessment is that “he remains defiant”.

    Mas Selamat was understood to have been undergoing a rehabilitation programme that includes debating with religious experts on Islam.

    Mas Selamat was born January 23, 1961 in Kendal, Central Java, Indonesia. He is linked with JI and is a suspect for a plot to bomb the Singapore Changi Airport in 2002.

    Previously, Mas Selamat was detained by the Indonesian police in February 2003 to aid their investigation of several bombings in 2001 and 2002. He was then using the identity of Edi Heriyanto with fake passport, and was subsequently jailed for 18 months in 2003 for immigration offences. It was during this time he acquired his permanent limp, as result of a botched escape attempt from a high floor.

    His claim of infamy started with an escape from Whitley Road Detention Centre of Singapore’s Internal Security Department on February 27, 2008. He was deported to Singapore after an arrest was made in Java, Indonesia on January 20, 2006 for using fake identity card while visiting his son there. It then spurred what is considered the biggest manhunt ever in Singapore — involving several units including the Singapore Police Force, Gurkha Contingent, Police Tactical Unit, Singapore Guards and Armed Forces Military Police Command.

    The manhunt was called off 17 hours later without success, and it wasn’t until April 1, 2009 that Mas Selamat was arrested by Malaysian forces. His arrest was undisclosed under the Internal Security Act (ISA) in order to root out his network in Malaysia, and was only reported by Singaporean media on May 8, 2009.

    JI’s activities are centred around the establishment of an Islamic State in Southeast Asia, including Indonesia, Malaysia, the southern Philippines, Singapore and Brunei. It was founded on January 1, 1993 by Indonesian leaders Abu Bakar Bashir and Abdullah Sungkar in Malaysia, while escaping the Suharto Government’s persecution. Since then, JI has initiated contact with al-Qaeda, Taliban, the Abu Sayyaf Group and Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF) among others.

    The militant organisation has been involved in dozens of attacks and bombings, the deadliest being the Bali car bombings on October 12, 2002. Its suicide bombers killed 202 people and injured 240 more in two attacks in the tourist district of Kuta. Several members of JI were arrested afterwards, and Imam Samudra, Amrozi Nurhasyim and Mukhlas Ghufron were executed by firing squad on 9 November 2008.

    Other attacks suspected or claimed to be carried out by JI include the 2003 JW Marriott hotel bombing in Kuningan, Jakarta, the 2004 Australian embassy bombing in Jakarta, the 2005 Bali terrorist bombing and the 2009 JW Marriott and Ritz-Carlton hotel bombings. Many other JI important figures such as Noordin Top, Azahari Husin, Hambali, Abu Dujana, and Dulmatin, have since been killed or captured, mostly by Indonesian anti-terrorist squad.
