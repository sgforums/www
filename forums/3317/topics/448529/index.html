    Do you know what your maid is really up to on her days off? Domestic helper Rezzilyn Lodia Vinegas had kept her relationship with a foreign worker a secret, until the couple was found dead in a hotel room in Geylang.

    The bodies of Vinegas, 33, and her 22-year-old Bangladeshi national lover Md Sumon Md Lutfar had been discovered by a receptionist on Sunday night (Mar 4) in a bloodstained hotel room. Both had been found with slash wounds on their limbs.

    Vinegas had been working with her current employer for 15 months and appeared to have had no problems with her work. Her facebook page however, was full of unhappy postings on love and relationships.

    Police have classified the case as unnatural death and investigations are ongoing. It is not yet clear if the case is a murder-suicide or double suicide.

    STOMPer Fiona wrote:

    "This tragic death proves that just because your domestic helper appears to be content with her work does not mean she might not have personal problems you might not be aware of.

    "It must have been a tremendous shock for her employer and friends to discover that she had had a relationship she had kept under wraps, which only came to light because of their deaths.

    "It goes to show that employers here often do not know what goes on behind the scenes in the lives of their domestic helpers. They are people too and have private needs and wants. Just like us things do not always go according to plan and terrible things might happen.

    "If you have a domestic helper in your home, you might want to try to get to know her better, so that you do not get faced with such a horrid shock one day. It would also do many of us good to treat our maids with more respect."
