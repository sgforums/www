    STOMPer Caretaker was outraged to learn that some teachers were making fun of a Primary 5 special needs boy on Facebook..

    According to a New Paper report, an allied educator (AED), whose job is to give teaching and learning support in school, made jokes about a special needs boy's condition on his Facebook page on July 1.

    Several other teachers from the school also joined in with insensitive remarks.

    The special needs student drools occasionally and mucus flows from his nose because of his condition. The boy also has difficulty sitting still and AEDs are assigned to take care of such students.

    STOMPer Caretaker siad:

    "Such disgusting behaviour! It's really unacceptable.

    "You would think that an AED, someone who is supposed to be there to take care of the needs and welfare of students, would be more sympathetic to this kid's situation.

    "Don't be an AED if you're going to complain and rant about your job.

    "Their comments were so hurtful. If I were the parent of the boy, I would make sure the school did more than counsel the teachers.

    "The fact that other teachers joined in worries me. These people are supposed to be teaching children moral values in school as well. How can they do that if they themselves behave like that?"
