    Malaysia on the hunt for 20 escaped Afghans

    KUALA LUMPUR - A manhunt has been launched in Malaysia for 20 Afghan migrants who escaped from a high security detention centre and may be planning to travel illicitly to Australia.

    The Afghans escaped on Sunday from the holding centre at Kuala Lumpur International Airport, in the second such incident in five months. The group cut through metal grills and climbed over a fence to escape.

    Home Minister Hishammuddin Hussein has threatened to remove two of his senior officials - including the Secretary General and the Immigration Department chief - if investigations showed it was an inside job or the work of syndicates.

    "Their heads will roll if the probe shows they did not do whatever was necessary to prevent this breakout," he was quoted as saying by The New Straits Times.

    Police believe the Afghan migrants entered Malaysia planning to escape to Australia via Indonesia, said Selangor police chief Khalid Abu Bakar. "We believe they are still in the country. We have plugged all our exit points. They will probably try to leave the country by sea."

    The men had been held for over a month at the centre. 20 officers on duty when the breakout occurred have reportedly been suspended until the probe is completed.

    In March, 12 Afghans and four Myanmar nationals fled from the same detention centre. None was recaptured.

    Anti-human trafficking activists have said Malaysia is used as a staging post for gangs moving people from Afghanistan and Myanmar to Indonesia and Australia. AGENCIES
