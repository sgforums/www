    City Harvest Church's weekend service sees large turnout despite probe

    SINGAPORE : It is the first weekend since news broke that individuals and companies linked to City Harvest Church are being investigated by the Commercial Affairs Department.

    Thousands of churchgoers attended the service on Saturday.

    Charismatic church leader Kong Hee may have been nowhere in sight, but it is service as usual, as thousands packed the Singapore Expo Hall One.

    Before the sermon began, Pastor Derek Dunn thanked the congregation for their well-wishes and support. He stressed that church operations will not be affected by the ongoing investigations.

    Pastor Derek also mentioned Mr Kong and said the man is doing well. He added that Mr Kong is taking a rest this weekend, so that he can continue to assist with investigations.

    Churchgoers have thrown their support behind their pastor and board members.

    One churchgoer said: "I have full faith in my pastor, and also the church board because most of the time, when we do things, it is quite transparent already.

    "When we are doing missions in other countries, he will tell us how much we have spent, what are the items that they sent over."

    Another added: "We have been praying for the leadership, and we hope everything will be ok. The church is very united, I think everything will be good."

    When asked for an update on the probe, the police maintained that it is not appropriate to comment further at this juncture, as investigations are still on-going.

    - CNA/ms

    New Creation explains

    THE New Creation Church assured churchgoers yesterday that its business ventures were formed not to spin money for the megachurch, but were essentially driven by the need to house a growing congregation of 20,000.

    It also disclosed that Senior Pastor Joseph Prince stopped taking a salary from the church late last year, but relies on book royalties and other income.

    Deacon Jack Ho said he sought to ease the hearts of churchgoers in revealing this, in the light of extensive media reports and rampant online discussions of investigations into alleged misuse of church funds at City Harvest Church.

    In his address, the deacon dwelt on the rationale for the church's ventures.

    Deputy assisting in probe

    PASTOR Tan Ye Peng, a founding member of City Harvest Church (CHC) and Senior Pastor Kong Hee's deputy, is among those assisting the authorities in an investigation arising from complaints about the alleged misuse of church funds.

    The church in Jurong was told this during the Chinese language service yesterday. Mr Tan, who heads the Chinese section, was not present.

    Pastor Joseph Ang, who led the service, told the congregation that both Mr Tan and Mr Kong were resting so they could continue to assist in the police investigation.

    Assuring the congregation that both men were well, Mr Ang thanked church members for the encouraging messages sent to their leaders over the week.

    'Whatever happens, let us stay united,' he said.

    Seventeen individuals are involved in the investigation by the police and the Commissioner of Charities.

    -- ST
