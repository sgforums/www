    Four more charged with having paid sex with minor SINGAPORE: Another four men have been charged with having paid sex with a minor. This brings to 48 the number of men charged for the same offence so far; the others were charged on Monday. In February, reports said 80 men were assisting police in the online vice probe. Former Singapore Environment Council executive director Howard Shaw is accused of paying S$500 to the underage girl for sex in October 2010 at Hotel 81 on Bencoolen Street. Also facing one count is Chua Ren Cheng, a former teacher. The 31-year-old had allegedly paid S$750 for sex with the minor in December 2010. The others charged are 40-year-old Swiss national Juerg Buergin, a former UBS employee, and 49-year-old businessman Edward Whistler Goh Ngian Meng. Both are accused of paying S$450 to S$650 for the girl's services twice, sometime between September 2010 and November 2011. Earlier this week, 44 men, including nine civil servants, were charged in court with similar offences. They allegedly procured the sexual services from an online vice ring. On Monday, several defence lawyers took issue with the charges as they did not include the name and the date of birth of the girl involved. The prosecution has now included such details in the charges of the four men. On Wednesday evening, the Attorney-General's Chambers said it is amending the charges against the 44 men, so as to include the name of the girl involved. However, the AGC will also be applying for a gag order, to protect the identity of the girl. The AGC said when the charges were first tendered and accepted by the Court, the defence and the prosecution shared the objective of minimising the risk of disclosing the girl's identity, whether inadvertently or otherwise. This would be achieved if the girl's name was not included in the charges. But some lawyers now said they prefer the previous practice of naming the girl, but to impose a gag order at the same time. Lawyers also felt the defence could be prejudiced if the girl was not named in the charge, despite the fact that the accused persons and their lawyer would have known who the girl was.     The couple got married last November and appeared on the cover of local high-society magazine Singapore Tatler in February for a feature on "Great Romances". They had met six years ago when Xue was a contestant in a Miss Earth pageant, where he was a trainer and judge.

    Not much else is known about his 26-year-old wife, but Yahoo! Singapore understands that she is from China and had graduated from a local polytechnic sometime in 2004. Thereafter, she worked as an operating theatre nurse in a local hospital. She was also a part-time model and was featured in local men's magazine FHM in 2007.

     

    This is also Shaw's second marriage; he was said to have divorced his ex-wife about seven years ago and they have two daughters aged 11 and nine.

    On Thursday, friends of the former Singapore Environment Council (SEC) executive director contacted by Yahoo! Singapore painted a picture of a man who is down-to-earth and passionate in driving various environmental movements when asked about him.

    Li-Ann Wee, a doctor who has known Shaw for more than 20 years, described him as a "gentle, unpretentious, intelligent and thoughtful man".

    Shaw joined the SEC, an independently-funded, non-governmental organisation, when it was started in 1995. Last April he joined the Halcyon Agri Corporation, an investment holding company with businesses in rubber plantations and the offshore and marine sectors, as senior vice-president for corporate social responsibility.

    But those close to him continue to express shock at his involvement with the case. A prominent socialite, who has known the Shaw family for 25 years, said "it is out of character. Howard is a very good man, down-to-earth and ordinary".

    Wee said she was "shocked and speechless" when she found out about the case on the news on Wednesday night. "It is unexpected because he is such a devoted father to his two daughters".
