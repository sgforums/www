    Dad, uncle guilty of abducting, hurting NSF

     

    A FULL-TIME national serviceman (NSF) who continued seeing his 14-year-old girlfriend over her family's objections in 2009 was abducted by her father and uncle and taken to a cemetery in the dead of night.

    There, Mr Lee Poh Kiang, now 19, was assaulted by six men, one of whom even held a samurai sword to his neck and threatened to finish him off. The girl's father then warned him never to contact his daughter again before letting him go.

    Charged in court, the father and uncle claimed trial last year. The hearing lasted more than three weeks and on Thursday, they were convicted by District Judge Sarjit Singh of being part of an unlawful assembly armed with deadly weapons that hurt and abducted Mr Lee.

    Her father, 43, and uncle, 38, who cannot be named to protect the minor's identity will be sentenced on April 21, 2011. The other accomplices, who are unknown, are at large.

    Defence counsel Subhas Anandan told reporters that his clients intend to appeal against the convictions.

    Another charge of perverting the course of justice has been stood down. It is alleged that when Mr Lee reported them to the police, the father and uncle are said to have taken him to a karaoke lounge, plied him with drinks and hostesses and tried to get him to change his police statements

    Dad, uncle jailed for beating teen's boyfriend

    THE father beat up a full-time national serviceman (NSF) in a Choa Chu Kang cemetery for allegedly leading his 14-year-old daughter astray.

    Her uncle - the dad's younger brother - was also involved in the assault two years ago that featured a baton and a samurai sword.

    Yesterday, the two men were each jailed a year and eight months by District Judge Sarjit Singh for being part of an unlawful assembly that had attacked Mr Lee Poh Kiang, now 19, with deadly weapons, hurting him, abducting and taking him by force to the cemetery.

    The judge observed that the attack was 'an emotional response' by the two men who thought they were protecting the girl from bad influence. The duo were convicted on April 7 after a three-week trial.

    Asking the court not to impose a crushing sentence, defence counsel Subhas Anandan said the two men sincerely believed Mr Lee had instigated the girl to run away from home.

    They also suspected that she had sniffed glue and had sex with him. The lawyer added that these suspicions resulted in the two men setting upon the NSF to dissuade him from continuing the relationship.
