S'poreans flock to US, Europe amid weaker currencies

    SINGAPORE: Europe and the United States are amongst the top travel destinations for Singaporeans keen to take advantage of the weak euro and US dollar.

    Travel agencies at the NATAS Fair which opened on Friday, said the two continents, together with Japan and Korea, are expected to be their best sellers.

    This, even though air ticket prices remain more or less unchanged, said Tradewinds Tours General Manager Sheldon Hee.

    Mr Hee said: "Air ticket prices have always been variable. You always see promotional prices in the market and these are always competitive. What has really made the difference right now is that the prevailing exchange rates for all these destinations, in particular Europe and the US, has made day-to-day expenses, on food, or shopping a lot more attractive than it would have been, even two years ago."

    Travel sales to Japan are stabilising five months after the tsunami in March, with some agencies saying they expect business to return to or even go beyond pre-tsunami levels.

    Eddie Ung Kwan, manager of JTB Travel Saloon, said: "The earthquake crisis in Japan is over and the radiation threat outside Fukushima is low. And it's near the end of the year, so people are keen on holidays."

    With uncertainty continuing in the global economy, NATAS CEO Robert Khoo said some may be more cautious about travelling, but most Singaporeans will continue to travel at least once a year.

    "To a certain extent it does affect directly. But Singaporeans being Singaporeans, they will always want to go out and enjoy themselves, at least once a year. Maybe they will cut the number of trips this year but I'm sure they'll still travel," said Mr Khoo.

    According to a NATAS survey on visitors at its last fair, 62 per cent of respondents said they make two to three trips a year, while 12 per cent said they travel more than four times a year.
