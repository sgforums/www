    SFI promised to improve the quality of “DOG” food for YOG volunteers, but did they?

    TEMASEK REVIEW

    Since our publication of an article on the “DOG” food which YOG “volunteers are being fed with in contrast to the buffet spread the YOG athletes and officials are enjoying, a massive outcry has erupted in cyberspace forcing the caterer for YOG Singapore Food Industries (*SFI) to improve the food for the volunteers.

    Replying to media enquiries, a spokesperson for the YOG committee said: “We are aware of the feedback (on the meal) and have taken immediate action. We will work with the caterers to continually improve the meal standards,”.

    YOG volunteers' food

    Accordingly, volunteers for the YOG can expect a reasonable improvement in the quantity and quality of food served to them as promised by the YOG committee but that was not to be.

    One of our readers who is a volunteer at ECP for YOG emailed this photo to us (which was taken yesterday) of the ‘improved’ food.

     He has this to say about the ‘improved’ food:

    Imagine after working one whole tiring morning and you just need a simple, tasty meal to perk you up. And we get this crap? It did not taste good to start with.

    HOWEVER, THE MOST LAUGHABLE PART WAS WHEN I ASKED FOR A LARGER PORTION OF THE CHICKEN AT THE COUNTER.

    Guess what they told me “Sorry… this is standard sized”

    Apparently from the photo, the quantity of food have improved with the addition of 3 small pieces of what looked like ‘fish cakes’ or ‘toufu’ but the quality of the food leaves little to be desired.

     

     

    John Doe

    *Singapore Food Industries (SFI), a subsidiary of the Singapore Airport Terminal Services (Sats) had been awarded a $5.5 million catering services contract by the YOG organising committee. Its contract covers meals for up to 7,000 athletes, officials and volunteers at the Youth Olympic Village.
