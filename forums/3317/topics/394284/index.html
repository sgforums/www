    44-year-old man on trial for allegedly sexually abusing 4-year-old boy

    SINGAPORE: A 44-year-old man is on trial in the High Court for allegedly performing a sexual act on the young son of a good friend.

    Peter Lee Chee Soon is accused of committing the crime on the boy when he was four years old.

    When cross-examined by Deputy Public Prosecutor Isaac Tan, the boy who is now eight years old, told the court via video link that Lee performed the alleged act on him in Lee's flat in 2005.

    The primary three boy also used two anatomically-correct dolls to demonstrate to the court the sexual act that Lee had allegedly performed on him.

    And when questioned by Lee's lawyer, Wee Pan Lee, the boy testified that nobody had taught him what he should say in court.

    He also said that he had never seen the two dolls before he was cross-examined on Monday.

    Other than this alleged offence, Lee also faces five other charges which have been stood down for now.

    DPP Shahla Iqbal told Justice Kan Ting Chiu that Lee is alleged to have used criminal force to outrage the modesty of the boy's elder sister sometime between 1997 and 1998.

    She was about six years old at the time.

    It's believed that Lee had also committed a similar act of crime on the boy's aunt sometime between 1994 and 1995 when she was below six years old.

    The plump and bespectacled man is also accused of being in possession of 65 obscene films in 2007.

    Lee is now out on bail of S$80,000.

    The hearing continues on Tuesday.

    Judge ticks off lawyer of man accused of molesting young son of good friend

    SINGAPORE : The lawyer representing a 44-year-old man on trial for sexually abusing an eight-year-old boy was ticked off by the trial judge for asking the victim complicated questions during a cross-examination.

    At the High Court on Tuesday, Justice Kan Ting Chiu told lawyer Wee Pan Lee that his questions were "difficult to answer (and) can be misconstrued as an acceptance ... he is a child and even I have difficulty understanding".

    Mr Wee, who was acting on behalf of Peter Lee Chee Soon, had earlier asked the boy via video link about the number of times he had been left alone with the accused.

    Lee, a taxi driver, is on trial for allegedly performing fellatio on the boy, who was four years old in 2005.

    On the advice of Justice Kan, Mr Wee broke down his questions to make them simpler for the Primary Three student to understand.

    As a plump, bespectacled Lee sat in the dock taking down notes, Mr Wee disputed the charges, saying the former had been asked to look after the boy on three occasions and not six as claimed by the family.

    At no time was Lee alone with the boy during the time of the alleged offence, as Lee's mother was also present in the flat in Yishun, the lawyer said.

    Furthermore, Lee did not perform fellatio but had instead "accidentally" bitten the boy's penis, said Mr Wee, adding that at no time had his client intentionally taken off the boy's trousers.

    "Not correct," the boy said through an interpreter, looking visibly tired from the day-long questioning.

    Lee originally faced five other charges which included outraging the modesty the boy's sister, now 18 and aunt, 20.

    These charges have, however, been stood down.

    He is now out on bail of $80,000. If convicted, he could be jailed for life, or jailed up to 10 years and fined.

    The hearing continues on Wednesday.
