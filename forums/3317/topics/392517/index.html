    Assault: MFA in touch with Saudi official

    The Ministry of Foreign Affairs, MFA, says it has been in touch with the Saudi Ambassador here over the case of an alleged assault by a Saudi Arabian Embassy official on a condominium resident. 

    A spokesman, responding to media queries said the Ministry has emphasised the importance of observing the due process of law in Singapore and asked that Mr Bader and the Saudi Embassy cooperate fully with the Singapore authorities. 

    A Magistrate's Complaint was filed recently against Mr Bader I F Al Balawi, Third Secretary of the Saudi Embassy in Singapore. 

    According to reports, the complainant, Mr A Jaafar, a 37-year-old senior executive, alleged that Mr Bader attacked him on Jan 9. 

    He had told the Saudi official not to park at a space reserved for the handicapped at Glendale Park condominium in Hillview Avenue. 

    The spokesman said the Ambassador has given an assurance that the Saudi Embassy respects Singapore's laws and regulations. 

    It would extend its full cooperation to ensure that the facts of the case can be established and the matter handled within Singapore's legal processes. 

    The spokesman added that ass the matter is before the Attorney-General's Chambers now, it is not proper for MFA to speculate on what steps will be taken.
