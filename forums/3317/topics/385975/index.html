Dangerous driver arrested

  • QX179R
    25 Dec 09, 21:14

    Traffic Police have arrested a 20-year old driver following a high-speed pursuit along Seletar Expressway. 

    One of two officers who were pursuing him was also hurt after his motorcycle crashed into some railings during the chase. 

    The officer suffered back injuries and was sent to the National University Hospital where he received outpatient treatment. 

    The officers were patrolling along the expressway at around 1 this morning when they spotted the suspect driving past the speed limit. 

    They went after him and signalled for him to stop. 

    But he continued speeding and tried to shake them off by weaving in and out of the lanes. 

    Police say, the driver made a sudden sharp swerve along Woodlands Avenue 2, and this caused one of the officers to lose control of his motorcycle, leading to the accident. 

    Undeterred, his partner continued pursuing the suspect who even drove against the flow of traffic along Woodlands Street 31. 

    The driver finally pulled over at Woodlands Avenue 1 and was arrested for dangerous driving. 

    It's believed he was also driving without a license. 

    If convicted of dangerous driving, first-time offenders could be jailed up to twelve months and fined a maximum of $3,000. 

    First-time offenders found guilty of driving without a valid license could be jailed a maximum of three months or fined up to $1,000.
