    SINGAPORE: Minister in the Prime Minister's Office, S Iswaran, has urged Singaporeans to learn to integrate and be part of a larger society.

    He said this on the sidelines of a racial harmony event in his constituency, West Coast GRC, on Sunday morning.

    Mr Iswaran was responding to questions from reporters on the recent investigations involving some leaders of City Harvest Church.

    To a question on how this has affected Christians and non-Christians, Mr Iswaran replied that legal action was taken against individuals, not against any particular church or religious group.

    He stressed the need for ongoing dialogue, not just across ethnic or cultural groups, but with different religious groups as well.

    "One of Singapore's basic tenets has been that we view multi-culturalism as a basic organising principle," Mr Iswaran said.

    "We have got to make sure that we continue to keep that a very vibrant part of Singapore, where (when) people from very different cultural and religious backgrounds come here, they feel free to practise, pursue their faith, pursue their religion, but being a part of a larger Singapore society and integrate in that manner."

    - CNA/wm
