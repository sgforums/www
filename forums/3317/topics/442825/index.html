NSP discusses way to benchmark Ministerial salaries

  • QX179R

    SINGAPORE: Peg the salaries for political appointments to how much the majority of Singaporeans have benefitted from the Government's policies.

    This was one suggestion raised at a forum organised by the National Solidarity Party (NSP) Sunday.

    NSP member Ong Wee Min, who floated the idea, said salaries should also be pegged at a "comfortable level" before they are bench-marked.

    The benchmark, he added, should be based on a multiple of the median salary of Singaporeans.

    Mr Ong also said ministerial salaries could be made up of basic and bonus components.

    He suggested that the basic annual salary could be fixed at S$1 million.

    In addition, the bonus could be based on a basket of Key Performance Indicators or KPIs.

    Asked about Mr Ong's suggestion to peg salaries at a "comfortable level", former presidential candidate Tan Jee Say said it could include factors like lifestyle.

    Mr Tan, who shared his views at the forum, also said that the variable bonus component in the salary should be abolished as he was concerned that it could introduce biasness in policymaking.

    The NSP will collate the ideas from the forum and issue a party stand on the matter of political salaries.

    The suggestions come as a Government-appointed committee finalises its review of ministerial salaries and expects to announce its recommendations by this month.
