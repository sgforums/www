    As the murder trial of celebrated music producer Phil Spector (above) goes on in Los Angeles, the dark side of the 67-year-old was heard in court through his ex-girlfriend Dorothy Melvin.

    Ms Melvin was the first witness in Spector's trial for the murder of actress Lana Clarkson more than four years ago. She was called by prosecutors to boost their point that Spector had a history of pulling guns on women.

    Ms Melvin told jurors that during a vodka-soaked confrontation with Spector in 1993, he struck her twice in the head and tried to make her strip at gunpoint.

    'I was sobbing and I said 'Why are you doing this Phillip? Why are you doing this?' Iwas crying. I was terrified,' Ms Melvin, a former manager for comic Joan Rivers, told jurors.

    But during her cross-examination by Spector's attorneys, Ms Melvin conceded that she never pressed charges and maintained contact with him for a decade afterward.

    Prosecutors say Spector, best known for his innovative 'wall-of-sound' recordings, shot the 40-year-old Clarkson dead in the foyer of his mock castle outside Los Angeles as she tried to leave at around 5am on the night of 3 Feb, 2003.

    CONSUMED BOTTLE OF VODKA

    Ms Melvin, who dated Spector for several years, said she spent a 'lovely' night at his Pasadena home on 2Jul, 1993, and that he was charming despite consuming nearly an entire bottle of vodka.

    But after falling asleep on his couch, she said, she awoke to find Spector pointing a revolver at her Mercedes in the driveway and demanding to know where she had gone.

    Once inside, Ms Melvin said, Spector accused her of 'snooping' and stealing from him and ordered her to strip, back-handing her in the head again when she refused. Ms Melvin said that after she gave Spector her jacket, he told her to leave.

    But she was unable to drive away because the gate to his driveway was closed.

    'Then I saw Phil coming down the driveway and I heard the pump of a shotgun,' she said. 'He was screaming, 'I told you to get out of here.' And I was screaming, 'The gate won't open.' '

    Ms Melvin said that after Spector opened the gate, she drove out and called the police, who escorted her back to the house to retrieve her purse.

    The court day began with Spector's attorneys telling the jury during their opening statement that Clarkson, star of films such as Barbarian Queen and Amazon Women On The Moon, had shot herself while under the influence of alcohol and painkillers.

    The trial, being televised live, is scheduled to resume on Monday after a day off yesterday, and is expected to last for up to three months. Defence lawyers have not said if Spector, who has shunned the public eye for decades and has described himself as battling depression and internal 'devils' will testify. -- Reuters
