    Originally posted by yellowflip999:

    I store my trains and route files in openBVE\Train and openBVE\Railway respectively.

    Well, <openBVE> is your installed openBVE directory and \Train to store trains? O, also can, but by default, openBVE gives us the Data folder, which I used it as it is.

     

    Eg of mine installation of route:

    D:\openbve_stable\Data\Railway

    where D:\ is my D drive and openbve_stable is the filename of the downloaded openBVE application file (In .zip).

    And trains:

    D:\openbve_stable\Data\Trains

     

    For Flip, according to the post above:

    OpenBVE\Railway, is the routes installed where OpenBVE refers to installed directory of the openBVE. This is where your openBVE.exe is located.

    (Tip: So mine openBVE should be D:\openbve_stable)

    OpenBVE\Trains, where the Trains are installed.

    Do note that the train files simply cannot put on Railway folder, and Routes cannot be installed on Trains folder. Even the routes in Railway folder has a subfolders rule to go abide by one, or else the simulation wont work.
