    Well, if dan474 does not know how to control everything, it can be found on your controls. The basics are essentially:

    F6 (Open door)

    F (Push Reverser Forward by a level)

    V (Push Reverser Backward by a level)

    Well Reverser has 3 levels, namely: F,N,B, which means Forward, Neutral and Backwards respectively

    Z (Push up power notch by one level)

    A (Push down power / brake notch by one level until Neutral level)

    Q (Push up brake notch by one level)

    For power notches, in our trains, is basically P4B3, meaning 8 notches inclusive of neutral where higher a number, the powerful the power / brake notch is for more powerful acceleration / brakes respectively.

    Eg: If you are now accelerating on P3, and wishes to brake, you have to Q it but if you would like to decreases the accleration rate, than you need to A it to P2 or even P1 as keyboard button A does not brake the train if it is on Power notch mode. Similar applies to brake / deaccerlation as well
