    2: How do you guys know the speed limit for each track sector?


    The speed for negotiating crossovers should be 40 km/h, cruising speed for stations positioned very far apart with moderate curves (between Khatib and Yio Chu Kang) can be taken at top speed i.e 77 / 78 km/h through long straight level sections and may drop to the 60-50s or even 40s upon sections with sharp ascending curves like the southbound section from Yew Tee to Kranji. Most of the time, SMRT makes use of gravitational acceleration (eastbound between Commonwealth and Queenstown where the train will accelerate to approx. 60 km/h before reaching its maximum permissible speed for that line section down the gentle slope). For stations with very short distances apart in the NS and EW lines, the travelling speed should be 60 km/h
