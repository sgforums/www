    To further on Veyron's explanation, I repeat:

     

    You can use Mighty Minds 2010 Street Directory, as suggested by one of the train fans, which is verified by me, for the route details, how curve they should be etc. But there are no details on X Overs, when should the train goes up slope and down

     

    However, I will provide a tip on where are the X overs based on today CCLD:

    Marymount - Bartley skipped due to one can check youself. (Later)

    Bartley - Tai Seng:

    Immediately after Bartley, there is a crossover towards right (Call it Middle 2) which leads to KCD. Middle 2 can also crossover to Middle 1, which is where the Bartley Slidings terminates (I got board DNB before, I can attest) The reason why I call Middle 1 and 2, is because, you can check with Ang Mo Kio style, when approaching BSD.

    Note: As the sector is not included CCLD, what is the rest is I dunno.

     

    Diagram:

     

    But tip:

    Today CCLD again board DNB along with many people, who did not managed to alight on time (Heard a staff complain on only 28 sec to close the doors, while previously was about 40 secs, but that caused delays)

    What is after Tai Seng is that it is similar to Bartley, except in Bartley slidings, the train must go to M1 via M2 before terminates, but Tai Seng slidings does not need and can do either.

    Image:

     

    Between Tai Seng - Paya Lebar does not have a crossover, except when approaching Paya Lebar, it has such diagram:

     

     

    Between Dakota and Mountbatten has no crossover.

    Between Mountbatten and Stadium got a crossover similar to one in Farrer Park & Little India, another middle track between stations with dead end on each end. There are entrance and exit points for either side (Clockwise / Anticlockwise).

    Diagram --- I wont draw at the moment. Unless you request it.

    Between Stadium to Promenade has no crossover, but Promenade is a stacked platform. Hence, it has double platform levels (Upper and Lower platform) and for Marymount bound, the doors open on the left while Dhoby Ghaut's door open on right.

    One will curious why stack? According to my findings: The opposite side, is actually an platform, with temporarily hoardings, like before CCL after NEL's Dhoby Ghaut. When will the other side platform open and use, I seriously wonder. There IS a tunnel there, but no tracks and 3rd rail. I can confirm that becoz I peeped through the end's wall. (So taking DNB does not help) But the reason why stack is because for both direction, it has a super sharp right turn (For Dhoby Ghaut bound) and a striaght crossover, which is a dead end. I guess that will be used for Marina Bay Extension (There are 31 CCL stations, so far, the last 2 -- Bayfront and Marina Bay will open in 2012).

     

    Diagram:

     

    Since CC4 is a stacked platform, then for both directions, it will be at exact spot, but at different levels (Marymount bound is the lower level one)

     

    Between Esplandae to Bras Basah has no crossover, until approaching Dhoby Ghaut, which is an X shaped crossover. For that, refer to Willis CGL 0.5 or above should be able to do the trick. The overrun in Dhoby Ghaut does not led to anway, similar to Marymount, it is a dead end, instead of X shaped or whatever crossover, at Harbourfront (NEL) slidings.
