    Hello? Do you know that it is very hard to think of a route to make? It takes alot of creativity to make up station names and designs you know? You think you just find a random word in a dictionary and just smack that word as the station name? You have to mix and match words to make up one station name. As for station designs, you have to think very hard to make one you see? Fictional routes are like that, it's very hard to think of new elements in the route.

    And also, SGBVE won't sound unrealistic even though the operator name doesn't sound very nice. In fact, it makes the route more unique. Only one line, so what? It's at least better than nothing.

    No one cares if any other train doesn't fit into the station or anything as the station is designed to fit its designated train. Any other train which goes in, it's just for fun, and no realism is to be expected.

    Please, put your shoes into the route developers' before saying such things.

    The above letter was written by Willis Chong, he appears to have problems posting this on SGForums, therefore I'm posting this on his behalf.

    As for me, all I can say is, I'm already having tons of trouble thinking up of fictional names that are sensible and are not dumb. Names like "Sillytown" or "Thundervale" do not make the cut for realism.

    Fictional routes are tough to make, I can't say I'm experienced in creating them. You guys don't have any idea how much time I spend sitting and thinking up of names. After the station names are done up, then here comes the station architecture part. You don't just think I jump straight into the creating of the roofing of the station in CSV, do you? I actually spend an hour designing a station. All stations on the Blackton Line have a particular design behind them, and I painstakingly take an hour to draw the plans out. I already have done up 4 station plans (which are Townsville, Science Park, Benoi and the future-to-be station between Benoi and Museum).

    My words may sound harsh here, but recently there has been a sudden surge in fictional cities springing up. It isn't as easy as you think to design a city's transit infrastructure. The original plans of Harbourvale City were far from what they are today. There was no such thing as the Kegstone Line, City Line or even the District LRT.

    A fictional city comes a long way from scratch. The hugest difficulty about fictional routes, is to create a fictional city from elements from the real world. Some of my textures are actually real world textures and they are used in many different ways. For example, an image of a road viaduct in Singapore can be used to texture a metro viaduct in Harbourvale.

    My routes place an emphasis on station design, because honestly I think that is the only component that separates your routes from other typical fictional routes.

    Once again, I apologise for the harsh words. I typed this in reply to Bngauw's comment, "Look, he doesn't even have a station design."
