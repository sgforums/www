    Good job on the research!

    Zheng Yi sect, or orthodox oneness, founded by Celestial Master Zhang Daoling was the first sect of Taoism and it was the time when Taoism was officially a religion.

    Zhang Daoling had recieved vision from Tai shang lao jun in Sichuan and thus formed the religion based on Tao Te Ching and incooperate folk customs and oriental traditions.

    Other sects rises up later on, such as Linbao sect, shang qing sect etc..but subsequently, they all classified under Zheng Yi. Zheng yi can refer to the particular sect, or it can also regard as a branch.

    Quan zhen, too, can be consider a sect, or also a branch. Cos under quan zhen, there are sub sects too.

    Zheng Yi focus on talismanic, rituals arts etc.. Quan zhen is more monastic, mediatation.

    But both are still part of Taoism, and are orthodox sect. In Northern china, most sect are Quanzhen, in southern china, except hk, and including se asia..mostly are zheng yi.
    Quanzhen sect in Singapore is still new and not too much. although i personally belongs to quan zhen.

    No matter what, all taoist sect respect San Qing, highest divine and regard tai shang lao jun as the incarnation of the great Tao.

    something to take note..

    Dao jia, or rather the Taoist school of thoughts, is a philosophy sch of thoughts that is founded by Lao Tze, based on Tao Te Ching. Zhuang Zi is the sucessor of Lao Tze.

    Dao Jiao, or the Taoist religion, is a faith established by celestial master zhang daoling of han dynasty based mainly on the teachings of 'dao jia' and merging of other oriental custom and traditions.
    On a spiritual side, he recieved the divine message from Lord Tai shang laojun. divine form of Lao Tze. Avatar of the Tao.

    The practice of Taoism however, had long been carrying on far before the forming of the Taoist religion. thus, the history and age of the religion(taoism) has a few diff type of explaination. depending on how we see it.
