    It has been some time that I wish to really write about the so-call Three "Laws" which I have formulated, and why I decide to adopt them for the time being. The first disclaimer for me, is that they are just "laws" or rules, you may call it, and they are not meant to be dogmatic or even be fixed for the rest of my life. These are not silly little principles which I have to abide at all times, but nevertheless, it keeps my sanity at check. Since I am not a very good guy, I need some rules to check my honour at times. Since rules are meant to be broken at times, I would perhaps end up breaking one of these laws but that does not mean I actively seek to break it. I will try to abide it at all times, and would break it if there is circumstantial reasons to believe that it should be violated. The second disclaimer is that I would say that I don't have a rational reason in writing these three laws, but perhaps stem from a personal perspective on my way of life.

    Let me state the three laws in the following order and discuss about them individually :

    Law 1 : Never go after attached or married women.

    This law is straight-forward and obvious, and of course, not all men abide by it. Why do I adhere myself to such a law? The first and foremost reason, is personal : I do not want to see another guy feeling the same way as I did, when my ex-girlfriend was "snatched" away by another man. I know how it feels, and how much it hurts and pains me at that moment of time. In short, do not unto others if you do not want others to do the same thing to you. The second reason is more about being honourable since the Chinese has a saying that a gentleman should never take away a loved one from another. My self-reflection tells me that I am not a gentleman, but I just merely to try to keep myself in tabs.

    Of course, there are so many occasions where I ought to violate my first law, because I have an unusual affinity with attached and married women, since they love hanging out with me or date me out casually, as I am mostly harmless to them. I feel comfortable with them around to guide and advise me. Well, many times, I feel tempted to break the first law, yet the living thought of bringing someone a horrible situation is still fresh in my mind, hence I decided not to do it, and in that aspect, I feel that my conscience is clear. It is perhaps a tragedy in a long run or a comedy in a short run, to never be able to impress single woman on the good side of me. I have given up trying in that light, and decided that all women whom I am to know in the future are attached. That saves a lot of trouble, trying to get away with blind dates which ended in disasters or in futile state.

    Law 2 : Beauty x Brains x Availability = Constant

    Well, this law is very analogous to Newton's 2nd law of motion, only with one catch : the interpretation of women. Originally, it was circulated on the net with the original equation : Beauty x Brains = constant, which suggests women are either bimbos, or they have brains but no beauty. I did not agree with that judgement, and decided to modify the equation with an additional factor called availability, since I have met women with brains and beauty but they are no longer or rarely available at that point of time when I met them. Of course, the hardest problem was to determine the value of the constant. Various estimates have been given by using population statistics arguments, but the attempts failed because it is very difficult to quantify them. Sometimes, I ponder if it is the inadequacy of intellect as one might put it which makes so many women worry too much over trivial things. I don't think so, and I believe it is the triviality of soul - they really care about little things than about big ones. I suppose that it is one of the cynical lessons one learns in regard to most women, that they care more (or as much) for a smaller picture than for a big picture. I don't think it is intellect which is lacking in them but proportion in desire.

    Law 3 : What u give to a gal is never equal and opposite.

    Sad but true, and be realistic. It does not work that way. I have learned that thru many failed attempts. Yet, I still prefer to be the one giving. As I put it, love is not about possessing but about devoting your heart and soul to someone. Sometimes, I seek not to compel, but I felt that I lost out a lot, because I was not insistent enough. Perhaps, the secret key in winning a gal's heart is to be insistence and persistence together. Still, I will try to do my best to court a gal as much as I can, until I know that she has hit the "attached" status. Hence this law is to tell myself that I should not expect anything from trying to court someone. Of course, no expectations would mean no disappointments.
