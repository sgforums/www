    Straits Times - STOMP
    10 Jun 2007

    Ambulances are pivotal when it comes to saving lives, but this does not mean they do not get into accidents themselves.

    Eric, 27, a customer service officer, was startled by a thunderous bang this morning (10 June) at 5.30am at junction of Bedok Reservoir Road and Tampines Avenue 1.

    A hatchback had crashed into an ambulance that was on its way to Changi Hospital.

    The car was turning, while the ambulance was going straight.

    Both vehicles were not aware of each other and collided.

    The impact of the crash was so large that the ambulance was pushed onto the middle kerb, while the hatchbackÂ’s bonnet was severely damaged.

    Eric said he saw that the airbag on the driverÂ’s seat in the car had been activated and the driver was stuck in his seat.

    Luckily there was no casualty in the ambulance at the time of the accident, just a few other ambulance personnel, who were unharmed.

    According to the police, both drivers sustained minor injuries.

    Police told STOMP on Monday that the driver of the car has been arrested for driving under the influence of alcohol.

    The alpha involved in the accident is QX109T, Alpha 231.
