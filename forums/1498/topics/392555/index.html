Vegetation fire at Tampines

  • QX179R
    3 Mar 10, 23:41

    A large vegetation fire broke out in an open field along Tampines Street 72 at 5.34pm today.

    It took some 30 fire fighters nearly four hours to put out the blaze.     

    The Singapore Civil Defence Force says the fire has been brought under control and no one was injured from the fire. 

    It adds that the present dry spell experienced in Singapore has seen outbreaks of grass, lallang and vegetation fires. 

    In the first two months of the year, SCDF responded to 147 cases of such fires. 

    However, the figures remain relatively low as compared to the peak period last year. 

    The same period last year saw 341 cases of such fires.

    SCDF adds that should current dry and hot conditions continue, occurences of such fires may increase. 

    It's appealing to the public to help minimise such fire occurences by not throwing lit cigarette butts and matches onto grass patches or fields. 

    Other preventive measures include not disposing rubbish in fields as such items act as fuel. 

    It also advises home owners with grass compounds to keep grass trimmed and watered.
