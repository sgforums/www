    New SCDF chief affirms commitment

    SINGAPORE: The new commissioner of the Singapore Civil Defence Force said the SCDF remains committed to carrying out its roles and functions.

    Mr Eric Yap took over on Wednesday as commissioner in place of former chief Peter Lim, who has been suspended following investigation by the Corrupt Practices Investigation Bureau into allegations of serious personal misconduct.

    Mr Yap said SCDF will continue to provide prompt emergency services to the public, and enhance its operational capabilities.

    He added SCDF looks forward to working with its partners and the public in advancing its initiatives, programmes and community outreach efforts.

    He said he acknowledges the contributions of SCDF's Operationally Ready NSmen, and the strong support of its partners and members of the public, in working with SCDF to keep Singapore safe and secure for all, and looks forward to their continued support.

    - CNA/wk

    SCDF chief formally appointed

    SINGAPORE: The Singapore Civil Defence Force (SCDF) has formally appointed its new Commissioner Eric Yap Wee Teck.

    The Change of Command Parade was held on Thursday evening at the National Service Training Institute.

    The event was officiated by Mr Tan Tee How, Permanent Secretary from the Ministry of Home Affairs.

    Mr Yap's appointment comes after former commissioner Peter Lim was suspended following on-going investigation related to serious personal misconduct.

    Speaking at the parade, Commissioner Yap said he is heartened the officers and units remain united and steadfast in carrying out their roles as professionally as they have always been.

    "The quest to protect and save lives and property is a continuing journey," he said.

    "I am confident that the resilience we have built as an organisation and the strong ties we have forged with our partners and the community will enable us to face the challenges ahead.

    "As Commissioner of SCDF, I look forward to working with all of you. Your continued support is vital to SCDF."

    The parade was attended by some 600 guests including MHA senior directors, senior officers from Home Team departments and Operationally Ready Nsmen.

    - CNA/wk
