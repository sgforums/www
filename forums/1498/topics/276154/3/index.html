    Originally posted by joe_cole:
    really? Ok.. that's comforting to hear. I really hope to be in ERS man. But training seems a bit harsh. And i surely hope i can go thru it with ease and not to mention pass the IPPT and SOC so that i can POP with a sergeant else it will be a wasted 6 months. haha
    well, truthfully, not everyone pass out. there will be ppl who drop out, who gets injured along the way and have to ooc, some die, some fail ippt or the final exercises..

    there will be a few major milestones to overcome.

    Presentation Skill - just presenting any topic u want for 20 mins. I taught the class how to play a guitar.

    Footdrill Commands - how to give basic footdrill commands. turn left, turn right, turn back, turn front, U-turn, march here, march there..

    IPPT - minimum Silver (I so proud to have attained it, because I never passed IPPT b4)

    SOC - A pass of completing it in 09:30 mins.

    BAPT - Doing some gym exercises (pulling weights, brisk walking, cycling at a certain speed, and endless ladder) while wearing the full fire fighting gear , and the BA set (got time limit to wear everything, or else fail). then going through a rat maze filled with traps in the darkness and smoke, to find your way out before your air tank whistle blow (which means u left 10 mins of air). So even if you're totally tired, you have to force yourself to control your breathing, so you won't consume too much air.

    Final Exercise - You have to take on the role of either first Sect Comm, Intelligence(I forgot the proper term for it) or DO, and use whatever you learn to lead your 40 fire fighters to fight fires in different scenarios. There's oil tank fire (need alot of brain power and planning, calculate wind direction etc), ship fire fighting, 2 room fire, 3 room fire, underground rescue mission, ktv fire, flashover, Chemical Agent incidents, Mass casualty, Suicide, Flashover.

    the skills you'll pick up are:

    how to carry 3 lengths of hoses, how to lay hoses, how to recover hoses, how to operate hydrants, how to set up BA, how to use the hose sling, how to operate rescue equipments, how to transport casualties, CPR, how to bandage wounds, how to use medical equipments, how to set up ladders, how to operate the pump and primer, and other vehicles, impulse gun., long layon, rappelling. etc.

    Basic Task Manual (BTM): various drills, you'll hear S1W3 alot.

    How to tackle Flashover fire (the most exciting experience), the fire is 1000 degrees.

    Very exciting I tell you.

    We will dance where the devil lives...
