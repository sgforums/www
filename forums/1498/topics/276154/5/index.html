    As psk_ash says it really depends on the individual lah.. depends on where your interests lie. If you're into fighting fires(or playing with fire? lol) and doing the heavy lifting in rescue operations, then go ERS/FFC. If you're into blood, gore, and "saving lives", then go medic. If you're into simple non-physical work, go clerk.

    Let me add some points which is relevant, from what I've observed-

    - Clerk doesn't mean no stress, cos you're under the big shots/officers and there may be politics.
    - Seems like most guys would rather go ERS/FFC because it's "siong" and is "more manly".
    - To me, as long as you're posted to station it's going to be more or less fun, as you'll be seeing real action; not many ppl have such opportunity.
    - However not everyone will be able to handle such "real action", as it can be intense.
    - Firefighters have less calls then medics, thus ambulance work can be exhausting.
    - You should have character and a sense of responsibility if you're posted to station, if not, I got nothing to say..
    - Lastly, there are other vocations, and the decision is not up to you anyway! ahaha
