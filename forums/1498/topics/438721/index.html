    Wednesday, 28 September 2011
    Fire at 1, Pulau Bukom

    SCDF received a call at 1318hrs about a fire at a manufacturing facility on Pulau Bukom. 2 Fire Engines, 2 Fire Bikes, 1 Red Rhino and 8 support vehicles were dispatched.

     

    Upon SCDF’s arrival on the island, Pulau Bukom’s in-house fire-fighting team was already fighting the fire with 8 water jets. SCDF deployed two additional large water jets to the ongoing fire-fighting operation.

     

    Fire was brought under control about an hour from the initial notice of the incident.

     

    At the time of this news release, (a) fire fighting operations are still ongoing and (b) there are no reported injuries.

    3 more explosions heard at Bukom around noon on Thurs

    Three more explosions were heard around Pulau Bukom at about 12 noon on Thursday, and the SCDF told The Straits Times that two of its fire engines were badly damaged by the fire at the Shell refinery there.

    One fire-engine sustained partial damage.

    The blaze, which began at about 1.15pm on Wednesday, appear to have re-started and witnesses saw a 'fire ball shooting into the sky around the time of the explosions.

    Some laboratory staff and executives are still at the refinery but their office is far way from the pump room where the fire is.

    The SCDF has confirmed that there has been a surge in the fire at the manufacturing facility. The fire is still contained within the bund wall.

    Boundary cooling operations are still being carried out to prevent heat exposure to the nearby storage tanks.

    Thursday, 29 September 2011
    Fire at 1, Pulau Bukom: Update 3

    To recap
     The fire involves petroleum products from pipes in the tank farm at a manufacturing facility measuring 150m by 50m. The fire is contained within the bund walls.

    As mentioned in the SCDF News Release sent out at 2030hrs on Wednesday, 28 Sept 2011, as a safety precautionary measure, all non essential staff from Pulau Bukom had been evacuated from the island.

    Current SCDF Operations
     The situation is now under control. However, fire fighting operations are still underway at the affected area. There are currently about 100 SCDF fire fighters fighting the fire with 6 fire engines and 13 support vehicles. About 250 essential Shell personnel are also on Pulau Bukom.

    A total of 21 water jets are currently being used to carry out boundary cooling operations. This is to prevent heat exposure to nearby storage tanks. Concurrently, SCDF is closely monitoring the temperature levels of the storage tanks surrounding the manufacturing facility.

    Resources Deployed :
     2 Fire Engines, 2 Fire Bikes, 1 Red Rhino and 8 support vehicles

    Fire at 1, Pulau Bukom: Update 6

    This is an update to yesterday’s News Release which was sent at 2340 hrs on Thursday, 29 Sept 2011. The fire at the manufacturing plant on Pulau Bukom was extinguished at 2118 hrs on 29 September 2011.

    Shell declares force majeure as Pulau Bukom situation stabilises

     

    Published on Oct 2, 2011

     

    Shell has confirmed earlier media reports that it has declared force majeure on some of its customers, in a press statement released on Sunday.

    Force majeure is a common clause in contracts that frees both parties from liability when an extraordinary event or circumstance beyond the control of the parties occurs.

    In this case, the declaration means that Shell is unable to guarantee supply to its customers, and excuses the oil group from contractual agreements, explained Mr Victor Shum, managing consultant at energy consultancy Pervin and Gertz.

    Mr Lee Tzu Yang, chairman of Shell Companies in Singapore said in a press statement: 'We confirm that force majeure has been declared on some of our customers. We continue to be in discussions with our customers to address their supply of product needs and to minimise any potential impact to them.'

    On Sunday morning, the Singapore Civil Defence Force (SCDF) began a phased withdrawal of its fire-fighting team.

    As of 6.30pm, it had formally handed over the pump-house area, which was the site of the fire, back to Shell. The SCDF, however, will maintain a small presence on Pulau Bukom as support and will fully withdraw once the situation is stable.

    Mr Lee said the oil group is now investigating the cause of the fire to prevent a repeat of the same incident.
