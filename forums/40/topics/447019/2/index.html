    Hi dude...sorry for not being able to reply early as I've had the impression that everything was settled on your part thus I wasn't paying any attention to the thread. Nevertheless, here I am.

    I hope that your budget is doing okay right now.

    The thing with your casing is, if you've noticed in the specs, they could accomodate the Radeon HD 5970 card's length. Meaning 300mm (30cm) length max. should be okay.

    Unless your HD 6870 is longer than that (which I don't think so...maybe around 24cm more or less...you ought to check though), you must look closely again on the specs of your card choice and adjust accordingly by purchasing the right card for the right mobo and casing. I hope you are clear on this. Don't rush into buying anything if you still have questions unanswered or doubts.

    For your graphic cards, Sapphire is already good choice made from the start. If it's all boils down to that $$$$ again, I guessed you should lower down your expectation for the gaming experience that you'd look forward to.

    Life time warranties are not always as it seemed to be. If the card is fried due to normal wear, not even the after-life time warranty could save it. 3 to 5 years warranty should suffice and justified for commercial sake.

    My choice goes to Sapphire. If yours' aren't, it's okay though just look around for new choices, that's all...no worries.

    Again for your mobo, I really want you to calm down on this and go through the process of re-evaluation. Do you have any other types in your mind? As I've said, do not rush on something you are unsure of.

    Changing mobo because of ventilation is not a bad idea, as long as it fits your budget then go for it. If you happened to choose another mobo, could you do away with the integrated graphic (on board graphic)? Do you think you really need that? After all you are going to submit yourself to Crossfire in near future.

    Hope this would shed some light.
