    Lena Park's 5th Album is out!

    Check out the reviews below..

    It is said that when Confucius discovered music he forgot the taste of meat. Confucius is not the only inspired soul, because though he had the courtly music of the Odes, we have the beautiful, sensual and captivating tunes of Park Jung Hyun (Lena Park). Her latest album has dropped and there really is only one word to encompass it: Heavenly. Not only does she take her God-given voice and incorporate it perfectly with the music, but she accompanies it with such diverse and sensual lyrics that the listener is pulled out of their reality and thrown into the world of Lena Park.

    Her greatest accomplishment with this album is that it is the first album in which she has made significant contributions to the production and cultivation of the album. And if this is any indication of her talents beyond her wonderful voice, then she will have an even longer and more prosperous career than initially thought. We were given a brief insight into the album with the release of her Japanese single, which the Korean version falls on this album, "Moon." The song was brilliant when first released, and it complemented by every other song on this album to elevate it even higher.

    Every one of her albums is known for telling a story. The placement of the songs on the album is crucial to really understand what Lena is trying to convey through her music. This album is no different. This latest album, 'On and On,' is a story of redemption, of new beginnings, and of changes. She begins the album with a simple 'new age' meets choir concert song that signifies the curtain being raised and the show beginning. Then with the start of her second song, "Beautiful You," it is as if she appears to us and the audience is drawn into the world. It begins the rising action to the album, to the performance of a lifetime, and as each song passes the passion and intensity only increases. Each song possesses a distinctiveness that distinguishes it as original and as a 'Lena Park Classic.' This song, though a bit more 'popish' than traditional Lena, it is exciting, colorful, and a cultivation of relief and brilliance all tied together. This song closely mirrors a later song, which signifies the falling action in her album and performance, "If It Is Today," that also has a very exiting flavor to it and that marks a changing point in the album.

    It is easy to comment on every song individually, but the truth is that it is almost impossible to talk about the album without any one of these songs. Each song adds to the album a new feeling and a new point of view that was once kept hidden in Lena's mind, in her soul. Through her magnificent voice, ever present and ever captivating, the music comes to life and surrounds us; we can see the vibrant colors surround our bodies as we interact with them. We can have many artists that sing ballads, rock, pop, and any number of musical genres. But what makes an artist truly talented and truly brilliant is their intuition. From any given album we can remember maybe a few songs and remember vaguely the feeling of the album. Yet with Lena Park, and a selected number of Korean artists, we always remember the message; always remember the feeling, because we are part of it, part of what she creates. She possesses a craft that many know of, few can explain, and even fewer have. Confucius was wise to say that music is innate in all of us, that it lives and affects us all. Lena Park really does epitomize this wise sage's words in ways that many will experience, but few can master.

    Grade: A

    BYJ fans could live one year with official diary 'Joon 2005'

    The diary with BYJ's photos will be issued in Japan. In the afternoon on 11th, BYJ's agency in Japan, IMX, made a public notice that "we will sell diaries, second in a series of official BYJ products, for members of BYJ's internet site in Japan."

    On the last page of the diary where one writes personal profile, they placed BYJ's photo and made possible for fan who owns the diary to wirte personal profile in the remaining space. It is the compact sized and total of 256-paged in volume. The price is about 1,890 yen (about 19,000 won).

    The cover of the diary produced in leather has 3 colors, red, orange & kakhi. Also holidays of Korea and Japan are shown simultaneously and all the writings are written in Korean and Japanese at the same time so that it allows Japanese fans to study Korean languages. The diary could be used from March this year to March next year.

    They will accept application from coming 15th until end of April through homepage, and as they do not limit the quantity, quite a lot of sales amount seems to be recorded. It will begin sales from the 23rd.

    S.Korean Dramas Make Up 25% of Foreign Programs in China
    news posted 03-03-05

    Productions from South Korea accounted for a quarter of the foreign dramas and movies shown on Chinese television for the past two years thanks to the South Korean pop culture boom in that country, a state-run tourism promotion agency said Tuesday.

    According to the Korea National Tourism Organization (KNTO), China aired 359 episodes of South Korean dramas and movies in 2003 and 2004, which made up 25.4 percent of all foreign dramas and movies shown nationwide during the period.

    Fifteen films and 17 dramas, consisting of 15-20 episodes each, from South Korea were broadcast during the two years, the agency said.

    Among the foreign dramas and movies, the number of South Korean productions ranked second to those of the United States, which had 487 episodes of dramas or movies or 34.5 percent of the total. It was followed by Singapore, the United Kingdom and Japan with 8.9 percent, 7.9 percent and 5.5 percent respectively.

    The KNTO said it plans to draw more Chinese tourists to the country via the South Korean pop culture boom, also known as the 'hallyu' (Korean wave), and carry out film commission business, which helps local movies or dramas from China and Southeast Asia to be filmed in South Korea.

    "Last year a TV drama, 'Winter Sonata,' attracted as many as 270,000 foreign tourists to South Korea along with tourism income of 300 billion won ($298.2 million)", said a spokesman of the KNTO.

    'Hallyu' began in China in 2001, spread to Southeast Asia and has recently made a strong impact on Japan with the broadcasting of popular South Korean TV dramas like 'Winter Sonata'.
