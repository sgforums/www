    Indeed it is.

    So darryl's drawing is correct. That is the general layout and position of CG, MB, and pin that you would use to induce early roll in a ball.

    Don't worry too much about the pin position, bowler. The fact of the matter is, for asymmetrical cores, the MB location matters much, much more than the pin's. Even for normal balls, pin position only really plays a large part of a bowler's game at the very highest level. Most ball drillers in Singapore simply don't bother too much about pin position for the everyday, casual/recreational bowler.

    I don't know you or your style, but I hope all this information helps you.
