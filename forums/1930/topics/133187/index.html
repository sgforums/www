    If you've bowled before, and feel that you like the game and would like to do better, than investing in better equipment is ESSENTIAL. If you only like it in a very casual way, then it's not really worth it. Teri-Yaki is right in that regard.

    Bowling is an extremely expensive sport compared to your usual basketballs and soccers. Just lace up your sneakers/boots, get a $20 ball and go!

    TEAM FOS will be arranging weekend bowling sessions again soon, and do join us if you're free. Sometimes it's the company you bowl with that would fire up your interest and enthusiasm in the game, and makes you want to do even better. Also, we usually have equipment that we wish to let go, and you can get it at a greatly reduced price, rather than getting ur own.

    Inspiration can is a wonderful thing. Like in soccer, you see Zidane and you wanna dribble like him. You see Jordan in bball and you wanna do all his moves too. If you bowl with us, you see Daryl's revs and maybe you want to tune it up like him. You see Jae's simple yet effective style and maybe you want to emulate his consistency. You see Hanz's funky flamboyance and maybe.... err no, I don't think you want anything to do with that
