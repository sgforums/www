    I got some thoughts to share after reading some posts regarding ball drillers that simply amazed me. To me, the bowling business in Singapore is stil very immature. Sometimes when i am bowling at certein bowling centres, i will notice some bowlers that wan to improve their game by purchasing a personal ball, so i will see them walk into the pro shop and buy a ball which they like or can afford, very often a tornado. I will often wonder in my mind that it never come across these customer's mind if the ball driller is really good enough anot.

    Technology in bowling nowadays is really amazing, with new balls like XXXcel, The One Hammer Hawk and so many others. now ball drilling is not just about drilling 3 holes in the ball. Why do you think you are paying $300 plus for a high end ball?? First of all the technology of the ball makes it good, the core, the coverstock etc. but many of you dun realise that in order for the ball to perform, your ball driller need to know how to map the ball correctly!!!! The fact is very clear, most ball drillers and pro shops in singapore are not up to the mark!! Alot of them are not even qualified ball drillers. This is partly due to the education level of these ball drillers. How do you expect a primary school educated person with lousy english to be able to understand terms like Radius of Gyration, Mass Bias, coeff of friction, differential, positive axis point and many many more?? and mind you, these terms are all related to good ball mapping.

    I know some of you feel very passionate about your own ball driller, but i really dun see why ball drilling cannot be discussed openly. Unless you dun really care about what you are paying for. I see many people with 3 or 4 high end balls that are drilled all the same way, ie the pin and pap remains the same!!! that is really a waste of your money.

    So i do advise new bowlers, dun go to a pro shop and buy a ball just because you think the ball is cheaper there or because the guy inside looks charismatic. Make sure the guy really know his knowledge about balls really well. Otherwise you are not getting your money worth. Ask him a few simple questions, like how the mass bias position affects ball reaction, how the balance hole position affects the tie line of your tracks, and frankly, if you already have an existing ball and he doesnt even ask to see the ball tracks of the ball, he definitely is not a good ball driller.

    firstly you should read wat is written on the bottle of spray, it should write wat is contained inside the bottle, usually is ball cleaner. if it says "polish" then you bought the wrong spray. if it did not write anything, dump the spray cos god knows wat is inside. then buy a proper ball cleaner from pro shops, like energizer cleaner from ebonite, or cleaners from storm, more trusted name.

    next regarding your question on ball's erratic behavior, well, if ur ball release is constant, then wat is affecting the behavior of your ball will be the oil pattern, i.e long oil or short oil, heads break down, amount and type of oil the bowling centre used etc, the ball may hook from left to right and back to pocket again on a dry lane, but when using the same ball on a oil flooded lane, the ball may just skid all the way down straight without hooking. you just have to understand that ball hooks only when it finds friction!

    dun rush into asking the ball driller to make your thumb hole bigger, this is a very common problem when people buy new ball, they will tend to squeeze their thumb during ball delivery, which causes friction, and the end result is the thumb swell and get stuck. so try to practice confidence with your ball, dun grip or squeeze your thumb too tightly, relax your thumb. alternatively you can use high latex tape to tape the top half of your thumb(the side with thumbnail). Do not tape the whole thumb!!! that is not recommended. it will reduce friction and hence prevent swelling.

    the reason we should not open the hole too big is this, when the hole gets bigger, we tend to grip even tighter for fear of dropping the ball, it will cause further swelling and in no time u will have to enlarge your thumb hole again
