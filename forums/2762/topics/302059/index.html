    Oolong tea is sometimes referred to as semi-green tea. Unlike green tea, oolong tea is fermented; however, the fermentation process is stopped before the leaves begin to change color as they do with black tea. As a result of its production process, Oolong tea combines the health benefits that are found in both green and black teas.

    One of the chief health benefits of Oolong tea compared to green and black teas is that it contains polyphenols, which act as antioxidants. Antioxidants help to keep free radicals in check, which have been linked to a host of health problems. There is evidence to suggest that the free radicals are linked to some cancers, signs of aging, and cardiovascular disease.

    What Are the Health Benefits of Oolong Tea?
    The presence of polyphenols has itself been linked to many health benefits. It has been found to reduce the presence of markers associated with inflammatory diseases, including coronary artery disease. In addition to polyphenol, Oolong tea also contains two other antioxidants. It has more catechin than black teas and more thearubigin than green teas.
