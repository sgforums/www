    This is what I think....

    Physics:
    1) Jump at 45 degrees to the horizontal for maximum distance in projectile motion.
    2) Use the momentum of your arm swing to go further. Timing is important

    Technique:
    1) Swing your arms. Jump when the momentum is forward, i.e. lift off when the arm is at the most front. As mentioned earlier, timing.
    2) Bring your knees very close to your body when you jump. This is to allow a longer landing time, so you land a bit further. Do some lower abs, butt and hamstring training. Need butt and hamstrings to be flexible, so stretch before jumping
    3) Remember to bend your knees a bit before jumping to allow the explosive power of your thigh muscles to come into effect. I have seen pple jumping without even bending knees much... Dunno why...
    4) Do glutes/butt activation exercises (means just warm it up by contracting it and relaxing it).
    5) Look far ahead. Aim for 250. Like that easier to reach 216. Dunno why... Psychological perhaps?

    Training:
    1) Do HIIT. You lose body fat plus training explosiveness of thigh muscles. Great for SBJ as well as 2.4 km and shuttle runs. Might as well this than frog jumps or skipping as it helps with 3 of the stations. Losing the weight can also help you in your chin ups.
    2) Play basketball?
    3) Do weighted jumps if you are well-conditioned enough (at least I do that in gym with an empty barbell [~20 kg]).
    4) Do back squats. Train up strength as well.
    5) Do stretching exercises for butt, hamstrings before sleeping the few nights before.
    6) Do leg raises (hip training)
