    The following stress checklist has been done by me and my team for a project last semester. This is compiled and summarized from a survey of 150+ people, of which 70+ came from sgforums.

     

    This checklist includes the affective, physical and behavourial symptoms of stress. It is designed as a quick indicator if your stress level is high. It helps you to recognize some symptoms that determine your stress level.

    To use the checklist, assign a number from 0 to 5 to each statement below, 0 being never happened, 5 being having happened severely.

    1. I do not have enough time for leisure or taking care of personal needs
    2. My workload is extremely heavy and demanding
    3. My working hours are long and irregular.
    4. I have too many responsibilities at work, including those not under my area of work.
    5. The working environment is very competitive
    6. I have difficulty communicating with boss or co-workers
    7. I have not yet adapted to the work culture/environment.
    8. I do not have enough say in decisions that affect me
    9. My superior monitors me too closely
    10. My work situation is unclear; there are too many people to satisfy

     

    1. I get easily irritated
    2. I feel anxious easily
    3. I feel constantly tired
    4. I cannot concentrate easily
    5. I feel restless
    6. My sleeping patterns changed
    7. I have low confidence
    8. My eating habits changed

     

    1. I experience flashes of anger at minor things
    2. I suffer from nervousness
    3. I get the feeling of helplessness and hopelessness
    4. I feel frustration
    5. I am depressed
    6. My health changed for the worse

     

     

    Score
    0-30    Good!
    Congratulations, your stress level is very low! It is at a healthy level.
    31-60    Average
    It would be good to lower your stress level.
    61-90    Bad
    Modify your lifestyle to reduce your stress.
    91-120    Dangerous
    Immediate action is required to reduce stress!
