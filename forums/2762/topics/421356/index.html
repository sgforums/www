  • fudgester
    31 Jan 11, 10:47

    Correct. Doctors and nutritionists advise that your weight loss rate should be no more than 1kg per week.

    Think about it: 1kg of fat requires 7000 calories for you to burn it all up. So in fact, should you lose 1kg per week, only part of it is going to be fat, unless you happen to be a trained professional marathoner.

    Focus more on losing fat than on losing weight. Eat a sensible diet and have a sensible exercise plan to go with it. Small, sustainable goals are easier to achieve than large, massive goals.

    And be patient. You didn't gain all that fat all at once, so you shouldn't expect to lose it quickly, either.
