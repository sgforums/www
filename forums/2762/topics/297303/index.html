    you meant you had a stitch? I assume this when you say you do sit ups to resolve the issue as stitches usually occur near the tummy area.

    The stitch will be there if you have not been exercising for too long. What you can do now is to go at a slower speed whenever you encounter a stitch. A good way would be to put a hand on the stitch, then breathe deeper as you jog, and jog at a slower pace than before.

    When you jog more and more, you will start to find that the stitch will occur later and later into the run, ie after 200m, after 1km, etc... It will be quite fast before you realise that you don't get the stitch anymore

    What is your goal in jogging? If it is speed, you should do interval trainings. If it is fat loss, you can continue jogging, in addition to some form of weight training.
