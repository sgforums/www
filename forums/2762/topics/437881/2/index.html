    Hope this will help you.

    Since you have done army, I would assume that you would have passed the 2.4 in your younger years.

    One of the things that you might want to consider is that your body needs to be conditioned to be able to take the strain for the 2.4km run. So if you do not have a regular exercise routine, you might want to start with shorter distances & make sure you know your limits.

    Jogging will not give you the time needed to pass the 2.4 run.

    You need to develop a wider stride that you are comfortable with, but still at a relaxing pace. When you look at how good long distance runners do it, they look as if they are brisk walking, and they have very wide strides with their legs slightly above the ground and their breathing is very slow & consistent. Relax you hands and use them to give you momentum forward.

    So you would need to breathe in through your nose and out through your mouth. And if you find that you are breathing in & out from your mouth, it means that you can't go very much further.

    Lastly, when you feel you can't go any further, make sure you open up your stride and sprint the last 50 to 100m, and do not stop but continue walking when you reach your finish line until your heart beat slows down. ( Please don't do this if you have not been exercising regularly and eating well !!! ) - this sprint will definitely improve your timing in the next run because you are breaking the limit that your body can take. Make sure you stretch well before and after runs. Set targets for yourself, may be run 2 rounds and sprint 100 m and then progress to run 3 rounds and sprint , then 4 rounds and sprint....

    You will find that it may seem harder to have bigger strides initially & may take you some time to feel comfortable with the bigger strides but this change will give you better breathing to cover longer distance & better timing too.

    This is the same concept with swimmimg free style, you need to stretch your hands & reach further forward to strengthen the muscles of your arms to be a good long distance free style swimmer. And because you are reaching forward, it gives you more time to take slower & deeper breaths, but if you have small strokes, you would need to breath faster and will run out of breath after 1 to 2 lapse.

    May be you can consider swimming or cyclying to improve your breathing, becasue running can have a higher risk to injury to our legs as we get older.

    Lastly, when you are running, just think of something else when the pain comes, like an encouraging phrase, as for me I would repeat this in my mind constantly" I can do all things through Christ that strengthens me"...

    Not sure if this will help you. I am not a professional trainer, this is just my personal method.

    All the best, and hope you pass your 2.4 soon.
