    Aerodancing 4 is all about realism. If you love realism, you'll dig that game. You can activate landing gears, air brakes, release fuel, activate afterburners etc... You can take off and land at your own will. For the terrains, you can even shoose different weather such as raining weather, night, evening etc...

    The weapons are even more realistic! For SRMs etc heat-seeking missiles, then cannot be fired head-on at the target. SRMs requires heat-signature which are only effective when the enemy's back is facing you! Also to add, when an enemy fires a missiles at you, countermeasures such as "flare" or "chaff" will be deployed automatically.

    There are also helicopters to fly and big cargo planes such as the US-1A flying boat or the Kawasaki C-1 Transport.

    In the cockpit view, pull a hard turn, you'll see the pilot experience a "Black-out". Just like real-life, a "Blackout" occurs when the pilot pulls too much Gs. 1 G is equivalent to the weight your standing on the ground. When entering a turn, Gs tends to increase. You might hit 5 - 6 Gs. If your 50Kg, hitting 6 Gs would mean your 300kg in the turn! Blood will then rush to the bottom of your body, thus causing a blackout.
