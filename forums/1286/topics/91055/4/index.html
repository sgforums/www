  • Lim Wei Ming
    4 Feb 06, 22:15
    I myself use ATI card (A 1st generation x800pro) but there are a few things that make Nvidia cards slightly better than ATI.

    First, there is the Pixel Shader 3.0 support on most of Nvidia cards ranging way back to the 6600GT. ATI's X8 series of cards only have up to shader 2.0b.

    Second, Nvidia's Forceware drivers seems to be a tad more stable than ATI's Catalyst which is proven in most games already.

    Third, there is much less to choose from Nvidia. There are only the core chipsets of 6600, 6800 and the 7800 with some variation of the cards GT, GTX, GS etc... which makes it easier for consumers to choose. While ATI has the X800, X850, X600, X700, X1300, X1600, X1800, X1900 and probably more to be coming soon. And i'm not even going overboard by including the PRO, XT, XL, GT0, GTO2, GT, XTPE, and the XTX.

    I'm not saying ATI is crap but more rather they are releasing sequels and sequels of crads just to compete with Nvidia. Look at what happen to the X1800XL & the XT. Probably just 2 months after their release, they released the X1900 which left the owners of the X1800 series of cards stranded. This doesn't benefit end-users at all.
