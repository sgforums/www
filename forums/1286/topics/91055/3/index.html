    18 Feb 05, 02:16
    If you are planning to do an upgrade, you'll need to have some basic info about your computer first.

    1. Is your PC a branded one, etc... Dell, IBM...? If it is, then it be wise not to even open the case. Most brand name PC manifacturer have propertery hardware that you might not find elsewhere...

    2. You need to know what type of motherboard you have, the type of RAM slots, expansion ports and stuff... certain motherboards support only a certain type of RAM size. You'll need to refer to your motherboard manual for this... another way is to simply extract your current RAM and bring it to the shops.

    3. I assume you have an AGP port. (Identified as a dark brown slot slightly offset and above the PCI slots...) Good budget cards to recommend are ATI's Radeon 9600 256MB series which you can get for below $280. Then have decent performance for its price range and then do not require a molex power connector.
