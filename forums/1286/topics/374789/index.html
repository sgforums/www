[Impressions] Aion: Tower of Eternity

  • NEWater
    8 Sep 09, 19:50

    've been on this closed beta and open beta for the past few months, and here are my impressions so far:

    - Typical Korean architecture. You have basic classes, and oh my god you get a job upgrade. In a break from the stupid grind requirement that Lineage II demanded from players, it's an "Ascension" by level 10.

    - Extremely irritating "sticky targeting", and very impossible to try to dictate range and pull, especially when in melee.

    - Impressive server performance so far. Ten minute server restarts have lasted for ten minutes instead of the "We are going down for an hour" which end up lasting for 8.

    - Aion Generica. So far, this game hasn't innovated, nor evolved much beyond the typical Korean spectrum. It caters especially to the Asian player who gets lost when put in an open world setting and needs to be pushed down a path. As a liberal-minded individual this annoys the heck out of me. I've spent two years in uniform being forced down a path and I'm not amused by being made to follow a road again by a game that's supposed to entertain me.


    - Boring class design. Played all four classes, gave all eight advanced classes a go. None of them impressed me. 

    - Spectacular music. The scoring is the most inspired I've heard so far for a MMO, certainly a trend NCSoft picked up from Lineage II and hasn't failed in that department, and still continues to impress.

    - Has a PvP and PvP ranking system that has some interesting ideas, but yet falls short of expectations

    - Last point, and I always make it a point to never emphasize graphics. The engine runs well, decent framerate, and has some interesting animations. But still, very much generic Korean generica.


    Overall, not a game I would take my wallet out for. It has some interesting ideas but the overarching Korean generica just made me bored. I don't really understand the hype surrounding this MMO. Next, please.
