    Details for the StarCraft II PC game were finally released to rapturous applause from about 10,000 fans packed into the Olympic Park gymnastics stadium in Seoul Korea on Saturday.

    The special event was part of the Worldwide Invitational (WWI) gaming tournament organised by StarCraft's developer, Blizzard Entertainment. South Korea is Blizzard's single largest market and this is the third year in a row that the company has held the WWI in this country.

    In Australia and around other parts of the world, Blizzard is better known for its other title called World of Warcraft Â– a massively multiplayer online role playing game that's widely regarded as the most addictive game around.

    World of Warcraft currently has more than 10 million active subscribers around the world, 100,000 of these in Australia.

    Yet, StarCraft's popularity in Korea is unrivalled. It's practically a national sport where tournaments are televised during prime time and top professionals are paid big money to play.

    The original StarCraft was released in 1998 and to date has sold more than 9.5 million copies worldwide. It's a real-time strategy game that pits three species (Terran, Protoss and Zerg) against each other.

    Players assume the role of one of the races and battle others online or in a network environment.

    The sequel has been highly anticipated. Blizzard's presentation to the stadium crowd began with a teaser trailer before launching into a demonstration of the new features in the game.

    Few details were available before the announcement, with Blizzard going to great lengths to keep the project a secret.

    Rumours of a fourth race being added to the game proved to be just that. Blizzard decided to keep the existing three to keep the focus on balance and game-play.

    The announcement was followed by local pop music and dance acts with all the ceremony of an Olympic event.

    Blizzard is expecting to attract about 50,000 visitors over the course of the two-day event that includes hot air balloon rides, concerts, tournaments, a merchandise store and of course, plenty of gaming.

    Simon Tsang is attending the Worldwide Invitational as a guest of Blizzard.
