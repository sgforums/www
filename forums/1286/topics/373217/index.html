Verdict: Sudden Attack is utter rubbish

  • NEWater
    25 Aug 09, 04:19

    There can be some players who will cry that "Oh noes, you SA bashers haven't played the game, so you don't know anything lololol".

    I already have, because I had no choice but to train for it for some competition coming this Saturday, so here's the critical flaw that leaves me goddamned perplexed on why people are still

    a) playing this game
    b) taking this goddamned game seriously

    Other than the game being ridiculously successful by the virtue of being free and because of being free, pandering to the baseline Singaporean who will shiver in his boots at the prospect of PAYING FOR ANYTHING (but then subsequently shelling out enough money in microtransactions to buy a real game, or maybe for a spanking new computer), there's nothing here that should appeal to any real gamer other than how brain-dead simple this game is.

    Standard Counter-Strike fare with some kind of psuedo-MMO mechanic to it. Buy guns. Grind exp/points for guns. Guns expire. Get conned repeatedly to buy more @cash, etc.

    The critical flaw of this game, is that it on purpose, makes your mouse tracking go off. Even if you could execute a perfect lateral mouse movement, the bloody game will make your crosshair/cursor wobble up and down, and up and down again! This has been tested with the following mice:

    Microsoft Sidewinder Mouse
    Razer Mamba
    Logitech G5

    This is not at fault of my hardware when the same results have been reproduced on all three mice that I own, and don't be telling me that I "lack skill" when I have to compensate for poor game design.

    How could anyone take this goddamned game seriously, except for the typical, tasteless Koreans who supposedly made this game their "#1 FPS" to begin with?

    Conclusion:

    Rubbish game. Asiasoft ought to be hanged, quartered and fed to the dogs for popularizing such a toxic game to the masses.
