    The aim of these kinds of games is to recruit top martial arts talent to be trained for special operations.

    In Mushihimesama all of the boss spams patterns of purple bullets. The higher the boss the more patterns it has at its disposal to spam at random. Ordinarly a normal person with a good memmory or someone who spends alot of time playing this game will be able to finish this kinds of game in one token. (i.e even tho the patterns are random, once the pattern starts, the bullets fly in a fixed flight path, they don't home in on the ship and chase it around. Thus once you identify what pattern the boss is spamming it is a simple matter to move to the safe point. Although the pattern can be spammed from different starting points, the safe point in relation to where the boss spammed the purple bullets will always be the same.)

    HOWEVER, this game is special because at higher difficulties (most notably the last boss) the boss spams each pattern not only randomly, but at increasing speed. The last boss spams patterns within very tiny fractions of a second of each other faster than the normal mind can track. It is only the subconcious mind that is fast enough track the patterns and beat the system.

    For a top martial arts exponent who has trained his subconcious to a high level, this game is cake. However the fact that they are releasing such a game to the public means that the aim is to recruit someone who is blessed with a highly developed subconcious mind and a natural fighting genius.
