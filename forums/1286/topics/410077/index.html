    There are 2 ways, but either way will cost you money.

    The standard US server way, buy the dual pack battlechest, WoW and Burning Crusade pack at 60+ bucks, and the WOTLK separately at another 60+ bucks. Anticipate Catalysm coming in november, probably going to release at 90+bucks. If you pay monthly thru credit card, it'll cost you S$20-22 bucks regardless the days you got play or not. If you pay for 3-6 months in advance they charge you cheaper monthly rate, but you have to pay that 3-6 months one shot. Take note tuesday nights are maintenance days, so no play, and even after you finished installing WOTLK, there is still almost 5gb of patches you need to download thru the patcher before u get up and running to the game. So if you bought all 3 packs up to WOTLK on tuesday, don't expect to play till wednesday after finishing patching.

    The taiwan server way, I am not so well versed but my colleagues are playing their WoW that way. Basically, they got the  JustWoW client up to WOTLK from somewhere free. But game time still need to pay, using game time cards bought from some local website they showed me. If I remembered its 50+ bucks for 3 months, and depends on stock. The interface is chinese, but that is easily overcomed by using a font pack again from somewhere i dunno. I was told that since I have the existing US server version, I just needed to mod some values to switch to taiwan server, and buy game time to start playing, but I have not got into it since I am busy with BFBC2 at the moment.

    The reason for them to play on the taiwan server is mainly because people on these servers are more objective and into the game to level up and gear up asap. And also the fact you don't give blizzard so much money everytime they come up with an expansion. If you want more information on playing on taiwan servers, go google for it, I'm not really keen to switch at the moment.

    Btw, I pay using credit card monthly, because I want the option to suspend my account as required. Also I don't really know where to buy the game cards.
