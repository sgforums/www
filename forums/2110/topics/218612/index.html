    tribalfooball.com - November 03, 2006

    Chelsea are launching a shock bid for exciting Tottenham winger Aaron Lennon.

    The Times says Chelsea will make a discreet inquiry about the England winger's availability when the transfer window opens on January 1, but are more confident of securing his signature next summer.

    Lennon has 3 years left on his contract and it will take a fee in excess of 20 million to persuade Tottenham to sell, with Chelsea hoping to use Shaun Wright-Phillips as the makeweight in the transfer.

    Frank Arnesen, Chelsea's head of development and scouting, has convinced the club's board that Lennon will emerge as one of the world's most exciting talents and that the time is right to move for the England winger.

    Lennon would more than double his 25,000-a-week wages if he moved to Stamford Bridge, but whether he is prepared to sacrifice the virtual guarantee of first-team football remains to be seen.
