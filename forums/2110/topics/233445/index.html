    Chelsea midfielder Claude Makelele has poured cold water on claims of a rift in the Blues dressing-room - saying the reports are "lies

    There had been rumours that the French midfielder and striker Didier Drogba had met with boss Jose Mourinho to discuss Michael Ballack's (lack of) contribution since his arrival at Stamford Bridge in the summer.

    But Makelele is quoted in the Daily Star as saying: "These reports are lies. Most people who know me, and who know Didier, would say we are not the type of players to talk in the papers.

    "It's daft. Michael Ballack knows that we have solidarity, we're all together, the whole squad, the whole team, so there's nothing in that."

    Makelele has also played down talk of a crisis at the club - despite a six-point deficit to leaders Manchester United after just one win in their last five Premiership games.

    "It's true we're going through a very difficult time," said Makelele. "We're second in the Premiership, in the Champions League, semi-final of the Carling Cup, in the FA Cup. So for Chelsea it's not that bad a period.

    "We'll get back all the injured players, that's quite a few players, and then we'll be competitive to fight until the end of the season for the title."
