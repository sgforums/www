    CECH EYES UNBEATEN SEASON

    Chelsea keeper Petr Cech believes The Blues have the talent this term to go the whole Premiership campaign unbeaten.

    The champions suffered just the one defeat last time around, following their surprise 1-0 loss to Manchester City, and command a 100 percent record this season after nine games.

    Cech believes it is possible to follow in Arsenal's footsteps after The Gunners went the 2003/04 campaign without one Premiership loss to their name.

    "I think it's possible, but the main thing to do in an unbeaten run is to play well and be first at the end of the season," Cech told Sky Sports News.

    "Last season we won the Premier League and reached the semi-finals of the Champions League.

    "I think it was possible even last season to go through unbeaten because we were the better side.

    "We'll try our best this season as well and then we'll see."

    Michael Essien, who has been a revelation in England this term, is not hell-bent on going the season undefeated, he just wants Chelsea to retain their Premiership crown.

    "The first thing is The Premiership. To defend our title and then try to go further and win the Champions League, if it's possible," said Essien.

    "Anything can happen in football. We just have to keep focused and do our best."

    Joe Cole has been one of the main beneficiaries of Essien's arrival and he believes the former Lyon man is the best athlete in the modern game.

    The former West Ham man is full of praise for the Ghanaian's strength and pace.

    "He is just the best athlete for a footballer I have ever seen," said Cole.

    "He has got everything - strength, pace, he can run all day, he is clever.

    "He is a great player. I have not seen anyone to match him for that. He is fantastic."

    - credit: James Pearson, Sky Sports
