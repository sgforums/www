    Villa boss O'Neill: More to Mourinho than Abramovich chequebook
    tribalfooball.com - September 29, 2006

    Aston Villa boss Martin O'Neill insists there's more to Chelsea counterpart Jose Mourinho than Roman Abramovich's chequebook.

    O'Neill, whose Celtic side were beaten by Mourinho's Porto in the 2003 UEFA Cup final, said: "Jose has proved himself to be a top class manger. He proved himself in Portugal and he is proving himself at Chelsea.

    "While we all think we'd like the best players available, you still have to go and manage them and he's proved capable of doing it.

    "Chelsea are a terrific side. They are going for a third consecutive league title, have not lost at Stamford Bridge for so long, and they have set such a high standard. They make winning games a habit.

    "I have nothing but admiration for the top managers in the game - Jose, Sir Alex Ferguson, Arsene Wenger and Rafael Benitez. They have all done it."

    O'Neill does not believe new Villa owner Randy Lerner will splash out money in the same way as Chelsea counterpart Roman Abramovich, but insists he has no complaints about the London club adopting that approach.

    He said: "I've seen some quotes from people trying to stir it up a bit with Mourinho about me saying 'we are not going down the Chelsea way.'

    "What I said is I don't see Randy Lerner adopting the same approach. I didn't say the Chelsea way was wrong."
