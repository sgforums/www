    MOURINHO GETS CHAMPIONS LEAGUE WISH

    Jose Mourinho will have been enjoying a wry smile after the Premier League fixture computer handed Chelsea four home games following their Champions League fixtures.

    The Portuguese coach had forecast that rivals Arsenal would be handed more home games than the Barclays Premiership champions after their European games but the Chelsea boss got it wrong.

    Instead it will be Arsenal manager Arsene Wenger who will be feeling distinctly unhappy after the fixture computer handed the Gunners an away trip after every one of their seven Champions League games prior to Christmas.

    But Manchester United got the best result as all six of their Euro ties are followed by home games at Old Trafford in the Premiership.

    Chelsea will go in search of their third successive Premiership title with a home game against Stuart Pearce's Manchester City.

    The Blues then provide the opposition for Gareth Southgate's first home game in charge of Middlesbrough which will also see Chelsea come up against former centre-half Robert Huth for the first time.

    But, while Chelsea are in action at Middlesbrough, Arsenal's away match at Wigan and Liverpool's home game with Newcastle are postponed for Champions League qualifiers.

    The big weekend during the early part of the season will arrive on September 16-17 when all the Champions League participants play each other - Chelsea take on Liverpool and Manchester United face Arsenal.

    Premiership new boys Reading, Sheffield United and Watford all have tough opening day games.

    Reading face Middlesbrough at home while Sheffield United have the daunting task of trying to prevent Liverpool getting off to a good start at Bramall Lane.

    Watford face a difficult trip to Everton and then entertain West Ham and Manchester United but Watford chief executive Mark Ashton says everyone at the club cannot wait for the action to begin.

    Ashton told Sky Sports news: "We look forward to going to Goodison Park for the first game in the Premiership.

    "It's fantastic, there are no easy games, we're fully aware of that. West Ham followed by Manchester United is fantastic and we are eagerly looking forward to it.

    "The manager (Adrian Boothroyd) is going through the list of games. It gives him an opportunity to do more detailed preparation. It just makes everything so much more real, we can't wait.

    "The interest is building, the World Cup makes things more interesting and our fans can't wait. Season tickets have gone well, commercially things are moving well and it's anticipation all round."

    Coach Adrian Boothroyd added: "Including me, we've never been (in the Premiership) before, of all the players there's only Jordan Stewart who has.

    "It's going to be an adventure from the very start I'm sure. I'm really excited about all the fixtures and I can't wait to get started."

    But Boothroyd is adamant he is not going to ditch the players who took the club into the top flight for the sake of 'sexy' signings.

    He said: "One thing I'm not going to do is forget the players who got us into this position in the first place.

    "I'm not into signing sexy names, I'm into signing players who fit into the culture and the way we work here. We've all got to go up a level, but if you don't get these challenges you don't improve."

    - credit: Adrian Curtis, PA Sport, article from Sporting Life
