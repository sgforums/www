    Chelsea senior players urged Mourinho to go for Richards
    tribalfooball.com - December 27, 2006

    Chelsea's senior players are behind the club's push for Manchester City defender Micah Richards.
    The Sun says Richards made his full England debut against Holland in Amsterdam last month and Blues boss Jose Mourinho canvassed the opinion of John Terry, Frank Lampard, Ashley Cole and Joe Cole about him on their return.

    He received glowing reports and was told Richards is the real deal.

    While he may have waited until the summer to buy him, the injury to John Terry and his team's defensive frailty has forced the issue.

    Former Chelsea favourite Pat Nevin reckons it is the ideal solution to the club's defensive deficiencies which have seen them ship six goals in their past three league matches.

    Nevin said: "It makes perfect sense for Chelsea to sign Richards now. He's got great ability and he's unique among the current crop of young British players in that he is probably the only one who could sign and play every game.

    "It's a move which suits everyone right now given Chelsea's problems, Richards' ambition and I imagine that City will probably need the money to add to their squad."
