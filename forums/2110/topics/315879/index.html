  • Sumone3
    29 Apr 08, 01:00
    Been so queit lately around here. Anyway, just my own thoughts on the match that passed. It was a deserved win for Chelsea who were the more spirited side of the two and could easily have gone 2 up at least within the first half and I think both should have been scored by Joe Cole. Man U seemed contented to try and play for a draw which is probably why they lost. Man U seemed content to just be holding the ball for most of the time and although they tried to catch Chelsea on the offside on a number of occassions, their holding on to the ball didn't match with this. A turning point in the game was when Vidic left the pitch injured because you could see how Wes Brown couldn't cope in central defence and Vidic was so much stronger in the air. The first goal came after some good play by Drogba who held off a Man U player to cross the ball in for Ballack. And it required a mistake by Chelsea for Man U to score and despite scoring, I still feel Rooney is not cut out to be a lone striker. Didn't see the supposed handball by Carrick so can't comment, but referee did make the decision based on linesman's call. To just comment on one other flashpoint in the game, The New Paper mentioned about how Ferguson felt the challenge by Ballack on Ronaldo should have been penalty, but seriously how could that be a penalty? This kind of pushing and shoving happens very often at set pieces and the ref would at most stop the set piece from being taken and have a word with the players involved. But if Ronaldo had fallen over? Well.... Let's just be glad he didn't, otherwise a lot of pointless arguing would have gone on. Nice to have Chelsea win as a tribute to Frank Lampard's mum, it makes the title race that much more interesting. Man U is in the slighlty better position as Chelsea is behind on goal difference and has to face a Bolton side that is most likely fighting for premiership survival and would throw everything at Chelsea with nothing to lose. It's going to be an interesting last two matches.
