Ballack tells Chelsea boo-boys: Give me time

  • ghimpheng
    16 Dec 06, 05:42
    tribalfooball.com - December 15, 2006

    Michael Ballack accepts he can do better at Chelsea.

    Booed by Blues fans during the 1-0 defeat of Newcastle this week, Ballack said: "Of course I can play better.

    "It's important to remember I have only been here for three months. It's not easy for players from abroad to fit straight in.

    "It's a question of getting used to the style, of fitting in with the players, and that takes time. It's the same with any change of job.

    "And being dominant isn't necessarily just about scoring and making goals. It's about how you fit into the team, because that is very important too.

    "Take Claude Makelele - he's a very strong player and not necessarily the most spectacular, but his contribution to the side has been immense.

    "The team has many strong characters, who all want to play well and be noticed. There are not just five or six top players in the side, there are 14 or 15 world-class players.

    "It's difficult for the coach to try and make everybody happy. We just have to carry on, make it through and finish the job."
