    KIMBAP

    Kimbap is rice rolled up in seaweed and is a popular choice for Korean lunch boxes and picnics. The basic ingredients are seaweed and rice, but it can be filled to its seams with as many as 10 ingredients, such as like carrot, cucumber, spinach, meat, kimchi, pickled radish, egg, tuna, fishcake, cheese Â… the combinations are endless!

    The kimbap looks very similar to the Japanese futomaki but kimbap uses plain rice and sesame oil for flavouring.

    SAM GYE TANG

    Sam gye tang is, simply, chicken soup with ginseng. This is a very nutritious, invigorating dish eaten on hot summer days to beat the heat Â– restores heath, the mind and boosts physical stamina. A healthy dose of Korean ginseng, jujubes, gingko nuts, glutinous rice and garlic are stuffed inside a young chicken. The chicken is then tied with strings or 'sewn' up and boiled. Served with salt and black pepper on the side, cutting the chicken open will release the wonderful aroma of ginseng!
