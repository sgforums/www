    NAENG MYUN

    Naeng Myun is buckwheat noodles in cold soup, a dish that's great for hot weathers. Some folks believe good naeng myun should taste savoury, the chewy nature of the noodles and tanginess of the beef broth combining to contribute to its thirst quenching, refreshing taste. Pyongyang Naeng Myun and Bibim Naeng Myun are other versions of Naeng Myun.

    Don't know if it was the US Army who invented it but perhaps you're referring to Budae Chigae? It's Army Base Stew that evolved during the Korean war. Apparently there was so little for the people to eat they had to rely on left-overs from the US Army restaurants, distributed in the streets like pig feed - spam, hotdogs, etc. Whatever they could get their hands on were just mixed together and boiled in a potful of water.

    The version offered in restaurants now use ramyeon noodles , meat, sausages, Korean rice cakes, hot chilli paste ... apparently Spam's necessary if you want the authentic taste!

    GUJEOLPAN

    Gujeolpan, known to many as cuisine of Korean royalty during the Chosun era, is a dish thatÂ’s named after the container itÂ’s served in. Gu means nine, a lucky number to the Koreans, and jeolpan refers to the divided sections of the container. Unlike most Korean food thatÂ’s served in ceramic or brass containers, the gujeolpan is usually made of wood, with more elaborate versions inlaid with mother-of-pearl. The eight surrounding compartments of the gujeolpan are filled with a ingredients like abalone, vegetables, mushroom, beef, cucumber, etc - the selection varies depending on the occasion. The centre compartment is reserved for the thin pancakes.

    Gujeolpan is a great dish to serve as an appetiser, even an accompaniment for alcoholic beverages when entertaining guests. Usually geon Gujeolpan is the version served for accompanying drinks, with the compartments filled with Â‘dryÂ’ ingredients like nuts, beef jerky, dried prawns, etc.

    How this dish is eaten Â… place small amounts of three to four different ingredients on a pancake, sprinkle with soy sauce or mustard, roll the pancake around the ingredients.
