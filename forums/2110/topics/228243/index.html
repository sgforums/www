    Terry back for Chelsea in just eight days
    tribalfooball.com - December 29, 2006

    Chelsea captain John Terry could be back in action in just EIGHT days after back surgery this week.

    The Mirror says the England skipper was recovering in hospital in Bordeaux after the 30-minute keyhole operation.

    But Terry, who will fly back to London today, hopes to be back running by Monday and could even declare himself fit to play in the FA Cup third round clash with Macclesfield on January 6.

    It was discovered that a piece of the bottom disc in Terry's lower back had fallen off and was putting pressure on his spinal cord.

    The surgeons used keyhole technology to pick up the fragment of cartilage, meaning only a 2cm wide incision was needed, hugely shortening the recovery period.

    Terry will miss just the two Premiership games with Fulham tomorrow and at Aston Villa on Tuesday if all has gone well.

    Terry stuns Chelsea staff with training return
    tribalfooball.com - December 29, 2006

    Chelsea captain John Terry is back in training just 24 hours after undergoing back surgery.
    The Blues skipper reported for duty yesterday, even telling shocked manager Jose Mourinho: Count me in for Fulham.

    The Premiership champions play their local rivals today - although Terry will not be risked despite his amazing recovery.

    Terry was in France on Thursday night for his surgery on a disc in his spine.

    He flew back to London yesterday morning and was driven straight to Chelsea's training ground in Surrey.

    The England skipper ran for an hour before a strenuous swim in nearby Cobham to keep up his fitness while his surgical wounds heal.

    Blues doctor Bryan English said: "The instructions from the surgeon were to run the day after surgery. That is great for us and great for John.

    "We are not going to do anything with John that is going to cause him problems.

    "I won't be drawn into what game he will be back but I am sure people will draw their own conclusions."
