    CHELSEA 2-0 LIVERPOOL

    Chelsea's quality of finishing proved the difference as they beat rivals Liverpool to move 15 points clear at the top of the Premiership.

    William Gallas put Chelsea ahead when he diverted in a close range shot after Ricardo Carvalho's brave header.

    Liverpool created some promising openings but failed to take them.

    Hernan Crespo drove in an angled shot to seal Chelsea's win before Reds keeper Jose Reina was red-carded for shoving Arjen Robben in the face.

    The two teams were meeting for the fourth time this season and predictably the familiarity resulted in the usual cagey start.

    But it was Liverpool who wrestled the early superiority of the game after creating the first chance.

    Steven Gerrard whipped in a corner to the near post where the unmarked Jamie Carragher failed to catch his header properly from six yards and the home defence cleared.

    Liverpool were troubling the Chelsea defence with their balls into the box and Sami Hyypia headed wide from another corner while Peter Crouch nodded over from a John Arne Riise cross.

    And, after Crouch again headed over again when Hyypia nodded a free-kick across goal, Chelsea took advantage of their visitors' generosity.

    Ironically, the goal came from their first corner with Gallas swivelling to turn Carvalho's header into the Reds goal.

    Joe Cole should have done better than put an angled rive into the side-netting after dispossessing Stephen Warnock.

    Liverpool were once more left breathing a sigh of relief after Reina spilled John Terry's header with Crespo being ruled offside after sliding in the loose ball.

    The visitors pressed for an equaliser with Gerrard seeing his well-struck drive parried by keeper Petr Cech.

    But any hopes the Reds had of levelling were all but ended when Crespo was played on side by Warnock and lashed in an angled drive.

    Liverpool's misery was compounded when Reina was sent-off for a push on Robben.

    Robben seemed to make more of the incident than appeared necessary as he fell to the ground before a stormy clash between both sets of players.

    But it did not affect Chelsea as they eased to their 50th Premiership win under manager Jose Mourinho.

    CHELSEA REFUSE TO LET ROBBEN ROW LIE

    Chelsea took another step closer to their first league and cup double with a comfortable victory over Everton, but their performance was once again overshadowed by a war of words surrounding Dutch winger Arjen Robben.

    Robben, accused of getting Liverpool goalkeeper Jose Reina sent off during Chelsea's 2-0 Barclays Premiership victory over the Reds on Sunday, put the Blues on the road to a fifth round tie against League One leaders Colchester with the opening goal against Everton.

    Robben's theatrical over-reaction to a push in the face by Reina has since been condemned by Liverpool coach Rafael Benitez and a host of professionals, both past and present.

    But this time it was Chelsea who refused to let the matter rest with both Jose Mourinho and Steve Clarke raising the temperature before and after Wednesday night's fourth-round replay against the Toffees.

    In a one-sided affair, first-half goals from Robben, Frank Lampard (penalty) and Hernan Crespo put the tie beyond Everton's reach and although Mikel Arteta reduced the deficit in the 72nd minute from the spot, Chelsea captain John Terry restored their three-goal margin two minutes later.

    But the fallout from Benitez's comments refused to dissipate with Mourinho claiming the Liverpool coach had deliberately deflected attention away from his side's defeat by focusing on the antics of Robben.

    Clarke, sent to face the media after their victory over Everton, backed Mourinho's assessment that Chelsea do not receive the credit they deserve and made a veiled reference to Reina's conduct.

    "When I was playing I liked to think I could keep control of myself on the pitch," said Clarke. "I didn't make really crazy tackles and I didn't do stupid things. I tried to play in control all the time. If you lose control on the pitch you are asking for red cards.

    "You can't say the game hasn't changed from 20 years ago when I was still running around. I remember touching some players on the face 20 years ago and they would go down just the same.

    "The stick Arjen got was unjustified and we can't understand the criticism we get. We deserve a lot more credit. On Sunday we beat one of our big rivals for the title and we got no real credit for it because of comments made at the end of the game. It is not fair.

    "Everything else was spoken about except how well we played to win the game."

    Everton boss David Moyes accepted his side had been well beaten but said their cause had not been helped by the loss of goalkeeper Richard Wright with an ankle injury in the pre-match warm-up.

    It forced Moyes to play 21-year-old rookie Iain Turner in his place.

    Moyes said: "We did much better in the second half but losing the goalkeeper in the warm up was a setback. You don't want to lose anybody, especially a goalkeeper, when you have named your side.

    "We have taken x-rays of Richard's ankle and there is no damage there but it was like a balloon in the dressing room.

    "The second half performance was more like us but it is disappointing to lose goals to set-pieces."
