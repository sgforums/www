    Well you can start by adding some work on your posterior chain! You have only upper body work there, and they're all pushing movements, so basically you have 1 horizontal and 1 vertical push, working your chest/triceps/shoulders. Where's everything else?

    Things you could add are squat, deads, chins, rows. Of course, don't do them on the same workout on every workout, but mix them up for a more well rounded, balanced program that has a mix of vertical/horizontal push/pull movements, as well as hip and quad dominant movements.

    Some examples of these movements:

    Vertical push:
    Standing military press
    Seated shoulder press
    Push press

    Horizontal push:
    Flat Bench press
    Incline bench press
    Decline bench press

    Vertical pull:
    Chinups
    Pullups
    Lat pulldown with the torso straight

    Horizontal pull:
    Bent over barbell row/pendlay row
    Bent over dumbbell row
    T-bar row
    Cable row

    Quad dominant:
    All kinds of squat, but start with the basic back squat first

    Hip dominant:
    All kinds of deadlifts

    On top of that, you may add some assistance exercises for the smaller muscle groups like biceps, and also core work and general physical conditioning work, but don't over do it. The big lifts listed above should take up the majority of your time and effort.

    Also, don't forget proper warmup protocols(dynamic, static, prehab)
