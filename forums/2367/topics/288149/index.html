    some people will say depends on what exercise

    But I don't agree. Breakfast is the most important thing in the morning. It kick starts your metabolism for the day. Your body has already fasted since the dinner the day before (unless you eat supper, or take a cup of milk before sleeping like me, or take casein protein supplement like Macro_Simone). This is why breakfast is "Break Fast".

    Sure, you will lose weight, but it may quite likely be more muscle mass than desired. Muscles are the one that help increase your resting caloric expenditure, and by losing it, your metabolic rate will drop, and make it harder for you to lose fats. This is another reason why diets do not work well, especially if there's a yo-yo effect. Each time you diet extremely, you lose both fats and muscle. You will then need to eat less and less to maintain the weight loss, which is clearly not desirable. Your amount of nutrients intake would not be sufficient.
