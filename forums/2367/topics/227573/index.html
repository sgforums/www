    As you know - diet is number one - and don't full into the beleif that cutting calories is the answer.

    Here are just some suggestions on diet:

    - Determine metabolic type

    - Set the correct ratios of macro nutrients (protein, fat, carbohydrates).

    - Keep a nutritional diary

    - Increase water consumption

    - Remove all grains from diet

    - Remove all dairy products from diet (excepts raw, organic products)

    - Eat More Frequently (6 x day)

    - Limit intake of carbohydrates to moderate to low GI

    - Increase intake of vegetables (can include some vegetable juice)

    - No root vegetables

    - No fruit juice

    - No processed meats (bacon, ham, etc.)

    - No soft drinks

    - Whenever possible, eat organic foods

    Now with the training - if you want to maximize fat loss and limit muscle loss (actually find the my clients gain muscle) I would suggest the following:

    Metabolic Strength Training:

    - Whole body training Â– Compound exercises (you can split movements over two days)

    - Undulating Periodization (Reps/Tempo)

    - Superset upper/lower body exercise

    - Superset push/pull exercises

    Then fishish of the training session with:

    Metabolic Circuit Training:

    1. Interval Training

    2. Finishers

    3. Complexes

    4. Combinations

    5. Power Circuits

    Using one or more of the above.

    Don't use lighter weights.

    Very basic example

    A1. Squats
    A2. Bent Over Barbell Rows

    B1. Deadlifts
    B2. Bench Press

    C1. Alternating Lunges
    C2. Chins

    D1. Romanian Deadlifts
    D2. Military Press

    Workout One

    Sets: 3
    Reps: 6
    Tempo: 2-1-1-0
    Rest: 60 sec (between supersets)

    Workout Two

    Sets: 3
    Reps: 9
    Tempo: 2-1-1-0
    Rest: 30 sec (between supersets)

    Workout Three

    Sets: 3
    Reps: 3
    Tempo: 2-1-1-0
    Rest: 90 sec (between supersets)

    Note the reps/rest ratio

    This is just a basic example
