    Hi all, i was reading some of the past entries and this question came across.

    There is a common misconception that size = strength. This is normally so as you tend to see the bigger dudes lifting more weights than the smaller ones do.

    Actually strength is measured by a ratio. I will use a simple bodyweight : strength ratio. Meaning if you are actually 60kg and can deadlift 120kg, your strength to body weight ratio is 2. This makes you seriously very strong for your size!

    2 guys who are of the same size need not be as strong as each other too!

    Being able to do loads of pull ups does not make you strong! it just means your have good muscle endurance....
