    Don't do the upright row. That's a bad one for the shoulder. You can do either bent over barbell row, or 1-arm dumbbell row over a bench. Just make sure your back is neutral in both cases and squeeze your shoulders inwards at the top of the pull.

    1)Arch your back like a cat.1 trick can be to tilt your neck back.

    2)Relax knees and do NOT lock them.

    3)Bent over 45 degrees(There are many differeniating views on the angle but you should be back to feel the muscles worked at that angle.If not you can bend over somemore)

    4)Pull to your naval.

    5)Use a mirror to guide your form.(Side view)

    6)Practise form with light barbell(discretion)first.

    7)There are two grips.One is pronanted(Standard double overhand), another is supinated(curl grip).The latter is called a Yates row if i am not mistaken.
