    I just want to check if I did it right.

    I haven't got a foam yet, but I found this thing at home. It is something to train grip I think. It is shaped like a ball, size around a palm minus fingers. It is softer than a tennis ball, yet harder than foam, so I guess it should work.

    What I did:
    I put the ball thing on a hard floor. Then I laid down on the floor with the ball between my upper back and the floor, and I let as much of my body weight lie on the ball as possible. I could feel it pressing into the muscles, and there was some pain. Pain is like when you did a heavy back workout, then when it is tired, and someone helped u massage by finger pressure that type of pain... It's like pain, yet comfortable type of pain. Some parts of the muscles could be much more painful. At some parts where it is rather tired, I stretch the back muscle a bit and felt the pressure deeper in the muscles. I put my bodyweight on the ball through that contact part for about a minute before switching to another part of the back.

    After effect was my back felt less tight then when I tried it. It certainly felt better.
