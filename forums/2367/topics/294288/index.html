    there is such a thing called yo-yo dieting, which is very bad. What happens after dieting is that you lose muscle, and your body brings down your basal metabolic rate (resting rate). Thus, your body become more "efficient", and burn less overall calories. But do we want that? The moment we bring our diet back to normal, we gain even more weight, because now total calories intake exceed total calories expenditure more than before.

    Then if you continue to diet again to lose weight, this cycle will continue and continue, and at the end, you will end up either eating almost nothing, or being much fatter than before.
