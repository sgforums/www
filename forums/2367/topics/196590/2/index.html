    Here's a technique for those struggling to do pull-ups (it was first taught to us by an Army Seargent during BMT, and later I started teaching my secondary school students this technique) :


    At the moment of pulling up, jerk your legs/knees upwards towards your
    chest. (NOT fully shown in photo).

    Note that this technique is for the purpose of doing the maximum number of pull ups (eg. when you've maxxed out your capacity, or you're a beginner just struggling to do pull-ups), and is NOT for the purpose of isolating muscles (eg. biceps, lattissimus dorsi, etc) in bodybuilding.

    So if you're a beginner in pull-ups still struggling to do your first pull up, go ahead and use this technique to help you gain confidence and workout your pull-up muscles (esp. biceps), until you can do your first 'standard' pull-up (ie. without jerking up your legs/knees).
