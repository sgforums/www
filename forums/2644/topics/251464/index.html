    BEIJING: Americans must obey Chinese laws and regulations while in China, the government said on Friday, rebuking five US tourists for holding a banner supporting Tibet's freedom near Mount Everest.

    Foreign ministry spokesman Liu Jianchao condemned the "separatist activities" of the tourists in Tibet, saying China has been in contact with the US government over the issue.

    The Americans had unfurled a banner calling for the independence of Tibet on Wednesday. The tourists have been ordered to leave the country.

    "The Chinese government and people cannot tolerate any attempt to split China," the spokesperson added.

    "The Chinese government has asked the US to remind its citizens they must obey Chinese laws and regulations while visiting China," Liu said.
