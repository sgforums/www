    In the past, the procedure is to queue up physically at the office located at National Stadium to ballot for the fields. Now, it is done thru' the internet. It is definitely cheaper to book the field and arrange the games ourselves. However my concerns are; How successful are we in getting the fields and how successful are we in getting opponents? For those of us who have been arranging games for the team, it's quite tedious and pressurising at times. With the current momentum of our team, I'm quite confident to say that all of us look forward to our weekly game.     For convenience and other reasons, I am in favor to leave the hassle to the lobang referees to see to all the arrangement and keep us informed of the details.     [b]From the discussion yesterday, the final decision is NOT to participate in the coming league.

    I hope none of you feel disheartened about the decision.

    As mentioned, by joining the league, it would probably result in the following changes:

    1. The objective and mindset of forming this team will probably change and progress to be more competitive focus. That differs from our current objective of having plain fun and enjoying the game. Different ball game altogether.

    2. If we join the league, some players may not get to play as much. Will every single one of you be prepared not to play at all for certain matches? Eventually, some may stop coming or drop out. That's not what I want.

    3. Possible conflicts in fielding players, style of play, formation etc may arise . Currently, I think our team has not mature or settle down to effectively manage such issues.

    4. If we join the league, there will be a possible need to train and improve on our current team. Are we ready for the added committment?

    Nevertheless, unless something changes along the way, we may join some league in the future. For the time being, if any 1-day competition comes by (7-A-Side), we can still participate . So heads up Reds , cos' soccer is in our blood and we will continue to play every week.
