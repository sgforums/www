    I feel that the most important thing in shooting is consistency-- As long as your bullets always land in the same spot, you can then slowly adjust your aim so that the spot will be where your target is. So, during zeroing, you must take note of two things 1) Your grouping is tight 2) Your bullets hit the target (i.e. weapon is zeroed). IMHO, 1) is the hardest to get right-- but once you can master 1) even after a short run (which the ATP requires) then you should be fine. Anyway there is 4 components that can help you--butt pressure, trigger pull, breathing and weapon cant. Personally weapon cant is the hardest for me to get consistent, so you gotta find out which is most difficult and practice if possible.

    Your CSM/Weapon spec should have a booklet/sheet of paper that indicates where to aim on your target for a weapon zeroed at a given distance (even if they don't have it, they should know and can teach you), and how to lead moving targets. This will be very important especially at 100 and 50 meters--go ask them for it and you won't be disappointed.

    Clean your weapon! I didn't get my marksman because I had to use someone else's rifle for the night shoot (my LAD died) and it IAed.

    Good luck!
