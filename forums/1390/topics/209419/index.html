  • Quirinus
    10 Sep 06, 00:41
    Allow me to give you a more comprehensive answer...
    1) There is no such thing as a female citizen volunteering for National Service. At the present time, females are not required to enlist under the Enlistment Act for National Service and neither will the SAF accept such a volunteer. The only way a female can enlist is as a Regular Servicewoman who has signed a contract with the SAF as a career. As far as I know, no female volunteers for National Service have ever been enlisted into the SAF.

    2) Even if your friend wants to sign on with the SAF as a career, she will need her parents to sign a statement as part of her contract if she is under 21 years old.

    3) Future civilian pay will not be affected since as a female citizen, she is not eligible for NS or required for NS mobilisation etc.

    4) There is no such thing as a "female NS OCT". However, Regular female OCTs do participate in the same Commissioning Parade as their male counterparts.
