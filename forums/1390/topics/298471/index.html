    Local woman dies when 3 Singapore servicemen's car hits hers

    DAVID L. TEIBEL
    Tucson Citizen

    A Tucson-area woman was killed Wednesday night on the Northwest Side when a car crossed the centerline, striking her vehicle, a sheriff's communications supervisor said Thursday.

    Jacqueline Faircloth-Bair, 64, was taken to University Medical Center, where she was pronounced dead shortly after the 8:15 p.m. crash, according to the report.

    The Pima County Sheriff's Department report stated:
    Faircloth-Bair was westbound on West Sunset Road, about a mile west of North Silverbell Road, when a car driven by Ming Teo, crossed into oncoming traffic and collided with her vehicle, the report said.

    Teo was taken to a hospital for treatment of minor injuries; his two passengers were not injured.

    The three are Singapore Air Force members in Arizona for military training.

    The report did not say whether citations were issued in the fatal crash.
