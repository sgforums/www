    It's detailed in the "Shoulder To Shoulder" NS Journal book they sent out to every serving citizen a couple of years back. Visit a library if you can't borrow a copy yourself.

    The physical examination decides your PES classification. It's modelled on the British PULHEEMS system...

    P - physical characters (height, weight, etc.)
    U - upper limbs
    L - lower limbs
    H - hearing
    E - eye, right
    E - eye, left
    M - mental state
    S - stability

    ...plus one additional checkup item (dental condition), according to the book. The procedure's also supposedly made more transparent, with enlistees told of their "problem areas" on the spot & a health counselor available nearby for follow-up enquires.

    The aptitude test is officially called MAPAS (Manpower Aptitude Assessment). It's computer-administered & seeks to assess the cognitive ability to process information quickly & accurately: memory, speed of response, abstract reasoning, ability to follow complex instructions, ability to solve mathematical problems, mental spatial ability, etc. I understand it can take quite a while, but there's no reason to get panicky over it.
