In the process, Colonel Childers ordered a massacre of the demonstrators whom he claimed they fired shots at the US marines. The conflict was videotaped but the evidence was destroyed by the politicians to prevent another Gulf War. Enters Hays Hodges (Tommy Lee Jones), a second-grade attorney who has never practiced law in court before. Back in the Vietnam, Terry had saved Hays' life and it is time the latter return his favor.

    The director keeps the audience guessing whether Terry had massacred the demonstrators who are actually women and children or had he killed those who happened to arm themselves. A veteran in film-making (The Exorcist, The French Connection), William Friedkin holds the truth up to the very last minute which "What Lies Beneath" should have done. (Blame the trailers!)
