  • Lord_Nazrul

    Originally posted by Genius1111:

    What's an OC runner?

     

    If a person is already in a vocation which can accept E9L9 personnel, then there won't be any changes. 

    Even if MMI "demands" a change of vocation, the unit may appeal and send the serviceman to the camp MO to be certified fit to remain in the original vocation.

    Do note that "revocation" does not mean "change of vocation" in standard English. "Revocation" is the noun form for "revoke". 

    A unit Medical Officer has no power to go against a medical board's decision.A unit can coerce an Mo to do so but the MO cannot do anything about it.If they really want it to be so,another medical board has to be held and unit MO has to justify.If the person is Perm E9L9,i can assure you that sure you that making him remain in the same cbt vocation or even upgrading him would be rejected summarily with the MO being reprimanded.Trust me,i've seen it many times and it is very awkward to see a CPT being scolded or 'taught'  medicine by the Chairman.All the other MOs in the room could only keep quiet or look away.
