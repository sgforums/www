    So serious meh? Depression during ns doesn't mean depression forever rite? I tot can treat one... Just because the poor guy got depression during ns (maybe his parents passed away or something) he'll be stigmatised for life? Then the employer must be damn unreasonable...
    Why unreasonable? Treatable & curable are 2 separate things. The likelihood is that the person would've displayed unpredictable behavior even before he got enlisted (withdrawal, truancy, anti-social, unmotivated, etc.), to the extent of regularly seeing a shrink on the side too.

    As a illness, you don't "suddenly" get depressive, nor is it "suddenly" curable. It's not certifiable (or downgradable) based on hearsay either; there must be persuasive evidence that it's led to prior abnormal behavior that renders him ineffective to serve NS. & it's gonna be a lifelong condition too, affecting future employment prospects cuz the distressed person can't consistently cope well with daily activities.

    Depression is basically a "mental illness" but not Woodbridge-psychotic like schizophrenia. Rather, it shares the uncontrollable feelings afflicting those suffering from anxiety/eating disorders or obsessive compulsive disorder. No doubt, a coward can keng all he want, except it's self-defeatingly delusional to leave medical notes on record & then try to persuade future employers that they're all false.
