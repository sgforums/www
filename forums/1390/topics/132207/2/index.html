    A good officer is one who knows when and what to do when required - thinking officer/soldier.
    - doesn't falter when a clear decision or direction is needed in a very short time (command and control).
    - thinks like his men and put himself in their shoes when there is work to be done.

    Rank is what you wear, respect is what you earned.

    The soldiers know very well which PC, OC, CO they want to be with when the button is pressed!

    Care for others (troopers) and others will care for you (commander). A soldier is willing to help his officer clean his rifle if the officer is willing to help clean the platoon's GPMG.

    I once read in Pioneer a story when Lim Neo Chian was still the Cdmr of 3 Div. He route marched with his division in SBO helmet rifle. He was not in the GP car or landrover.

    In any case, I feel that it is improper for an officer to ask his men to clean his rifle for him even though he is not free. Since he is an officier, he is paid more and therefore should do more, even this means giving up his sleeping time to clean his own rifle.

    I think, sometimes, people forget that we are in national service. We go to the army to serve the country and not to serve the rank that someone wears. May be this is why some NS units are better motivated that NSF units.
