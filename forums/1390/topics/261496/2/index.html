    RE: Maintaining Boy/Girl Relationship while in NS.

    Q:
    1. Is it possible to maintain a stable, worry-less relationship while serving in the NS?

    Yes. Depends on your vocation. If you have to stay-in all the time, busy the whole day with work, trainees etc etc, then its a little hard to maintain the relationship. But if your gf loves you, and vice versa, it can actually work.

    2. What is the percentage probability of having a big quarrel, or worst, a breakup with my gf while serving in the army?

    % of having big quarrel: 75%
    % of break-up: 50%

    Both dependant on how you're able to manage the problem and communicate. But ifyour gf loves you, and vice versa, no wall is to high to climb.

    3. Any advice on maintaining and managing BGR while in the army?

    Communicate with her EVERYTIME you have the chance. Assurre her that you'll always be there for her when you book out of camp and that while you're in camp, the only thing that drives you through the day is the thought of listening to her voice after a day's training. <----YOU GOT TO MAKE HER REALISE THIS AND UNDERSTAND.

    Understand her problems. Let her know that even if you're at camp, doesn't mean she must everytime listen to you rambles and complaints; it is worst for her since she's in the outside world, there's more and harder things to face - your world is merely green, sedia & senang diri.

    Don't be selfish. Don't control her. Don't worry about her being unfaithful or etc etc.. cause if she want to play-around/be unfaithful, she ain't worth it. If she loves you, and vice versa, nothing is hard to manage.

    4. Will you still feel like going on a full-day date during weekend breaks?

    This is the most important thing to maintain the relationshp. As selfish as this may be, spend time with her as much as you can. If she love you, and vice versa, both will come to a compromise.

    5. Can we be excused or request for FALL OUT if we cannot concentrate in training due to BGR problems...

    NS teaches you to become a Man; someone who is able to prioritise things; compartmentalise matters, problems, issues. DON'T let BGR problems affect you in camp and DON'T let things in camp affect your relationshp. Fully grasping this, can really benefit you A LOT.

    6. What are the consequences of AWOL (Absent without official leave)?

    ..need I say more? Clean bars lah!

    I can't say I have the best knowledge; but I can say I went through 2 and a half yrs of NS, maintained a relationship throughout and this august will be our 4 years anniversary.

    I always tell this to my ex-trainees who came to me for BGR problems:

    Do you think she love you? or

    Do you KNOW that she loves you?

    And of course the famous quote:

    "If your girl leaves you when you're in NS, she's not worth to spend your entire life with. "

    My 2 cents worth.
