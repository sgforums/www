    G is Gauge and it is English/American Non-SI/ISO way of measuring things.

    Whenever Gauge is used in measure, it is usually related to a standard weigh or dimension.

    For example, in weapons, a 12 gauge shot gun means.... the amount of lead shots that would total to 1 pound. Meaning, 1 pound consists of 12 lead projectiles of the same diameter.

    So 10 gauge will equal to 1 pound therefore, the diameter is bigger thus heavier therefore only 10 will make 1 pound.

    So on and so forth....
    to explain why 16G is bigger than 22G, you will need to know what standard needles use. therefore you will need to first find out what 1G needle is... therefore 16 G is 1/16 of that.

    This kind of measurement is found usually in US/UK.

    Other gauges that might interest you..

    In electrical cables, the standard is AWG
    In Textile, the english yard count.
    In Air Cons, the BTU

    so on and on and on......

    Because the "A-series" paper follow the ISO standard. In paper, there are the A, B and C standards. Followed by American Letter/Legal size.

    The ISO 216 standard states that the A0 is one square metre paper with a ratio of 1 is to Square Root of two. A1 will be halve the length retaining the height.

    A2 will be halve of A1's length retainined the height.. A3 will be half of A2 same height and so on.

    As for photographs, it is just a standard where every increment you add an inch to the dimension...

    4R = 4 x 6 5R = 5 x 7 and so on.

    Please note the "R" standard seems to be found in Asia only
