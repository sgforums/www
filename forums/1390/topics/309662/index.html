    My brother had signed on as an AEO after graduating NTU in the early '90s. It was the natural route for engin grads targeting the RSAF, although in his case he was already a commissioned officer during NS. I didn't follow his career that closely during the early years, but my impression was that 'farmer' AEOs would mostly specialize in the logistical side of the service arm. Presumably, my bro had been trained as such at AETI after signing on; I know he'd ultimately went on to do a Masters in Public Admin (or something like that) on the side. He's currently a LTC, not a high flier but has always held deskbound appointments over most of his career. He'd rather quickly made CPT - about a year into his contract - as I recall. BTW, like with all regulars, one's vocation won't be as significant as the command branch he belongs to (the SAF equivalent is 'formation'), eg. combat, Air Defence, manpower, manpower, etc. I'm doubtful such regulars will be that literally hands-on as you think either. That'll be more the jobs of NCOs & NSFs while, for officers, presumably only during their course time. If you have to go through OCS first, you'll attend AETI during your Professional Term after finishing the earlier phrases with the other NSF cadets. There'll likely be further technical upgrading courses too, as your career progresses.
