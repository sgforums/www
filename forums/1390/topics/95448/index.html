    I met some Isrealis a few years back. They also have the same IPPT yearly tests for their reservists. We can take heart that we are not the only ones not very fit. They have the same problem motivating their reserve troops to exercise regularly.

    But the issue that is important, as I learnt in reservist, and also hearing the combat experienced Isreali friends, are that we must keep up our endurance limits on our legs. Those in the infantry will know that if you can walk for miles and miles and go on little sleep and can carry on with the mission, this will make the soldier. The next important thing is to strengthen the back and shoulder muscles for humping loads around. The rest we can be weak in (e.g. shuttle run, broad jump...).
