    Was from ADA previously. Now going backin june to resume my service. Based on what i remember, (my training was back in 2005) you will be reporting to AFS for i think 1 week for basic training... Nothing much there... Just Air Defence concept blah blah blah... that time my operators doesn't even need to stay in... 8 to 5 basically... Wing Commander (ADA) now used to be S3, DAG. He's a good man... dun worry....

    After that, depending on your luck, you will be posted to Chong Pang camp or Lim Chu Kang. Those in LCK ah will have a good life... Their weapons are like those super humongous one... Then need a lot of time to set up.. Those in Chong Pang camp one more jialat.. Even though system is easier but you will have many enciks walking around.... so don't try anything funny.

    Was from 18DA. So took one batch of operator before. Training phase last for 3 months. Basically u will be learning again Air Defence Knowledge. Air Craft recognition will be drilled into u... U need to recognise the air craft from 1 KM away from various views. Preparation for operations, u will to be well-versed in that cos normally when u prep, the officers are at the mess... hahaha...

    Physical wise, get ready for lots of chin ups... An average operator can do abt 17 chin ups... so thats the standard...

    Now too much of out field for my guys... Just a pre-summex and summex. Rooftop deployments are quite common... Meaning u carry all the equipment up to the rooftop to deploy... i almost died on that... haha... Manpacking.... Carrying all the equipments and walk for prolong distances... I died on that one... But really nothing much after that... Book out is on Friday evening... My guys booked out at 3.

    After your training, you will be attached to the same unit normally and work with your instructors who are usually PCs or 2ICs... Life is really good after that... Dun worry.... Hmmm... But that is in 18DA, in other units in Chong Pang also quite similar... LCK one i not too sure liaoz... have to see the intake...

    And good luck...
