    Haha, every time BMT POP results come out, Stellazio would be answering questions from new AOS trainees. Let me contribute this time round. :)

    Training

    Air Operations Specialist (AOS) is the NSF version of the ASOX. We would undergo a 5 to 8 weeks stay-in Basic AOSX/AOS course (though i heard due to space crunch at AFTC, some would stay out) at AFTC, C3 school. Don't worry, the instructors there are world class, pass rates of the course is 100%.

    After that we would be posted to either aircraft SQNs, Tower/BCPs, SImulators Training Centres or HQs as Ops Specialist.

    This is a stay-out vocation. but the working hours at fighter SQNs and Tower/BCPs can be long and graveyard. Expect heavy responsibilites and get caught in some office politics. Those posted to be HQs and Simulators would be luckier with fixed working hours.

    Promotion (assuming passed IPPT if not medically excused)

    • 8mths into service: CPL
    • 4mths after CPL and completed 1-week Intermediate AOS/AOSX course: 3SG

    Skill/Vocation Badge

    Currently only the regulars get to don the AOSX vocation badge. The NSFs currently wear only an empty No.3 unform and sometimes get mistaken for a clerk. ;( There is ongoing rumour that this is going to change as part of a RSAF uniform revision exercise. (whether will it be the same cool badge on the left or a newly designed one, is yet to be confirmed)

    Conclusion

    A NSF vocation as an AOS can be challenging and rewarding. Sometimes, you may even get to go overseas as part of a RSAF exercise/deployment. You would also get to see behind and appreciate the immense resource and secret-edge techonolgy our world class airforce employ. You will also get to befriend and personally know the pilots/WSOs behind our planes. Or would you see the dark hidden side of the RSAF? To all AOS trainees, all the best as you embark on this tiring but exciting journey! :)
