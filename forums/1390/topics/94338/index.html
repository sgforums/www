    Mono intake refers to those who do their BMT within the combat outfit itself; they traditionally takes in those with O Levels qualifications & below, & will thus do a 2-year NS (not 2.5 years).

    Don't look down on them. These guys very salt of the earth & will generally form the main body/line company personnel from the get-go, rather than the officers & NCOs & various support staff who get posted in from elsewhere (after their own leadership/vocational courses).

    These guys are also those who'll likely know their (penciled) vocations the earliest, following the 1st CMPB medical checkup. Unlike the Tekong batches , the mono-intake Training Cadre also basically has complete influence on which among the recruits to retain on permanently into active-unit status. I've had the experience of making the latter recommendations before.

    The BMT programme's more or less the same - weapons, physical training, IFC, marksmanship, grenade throwing, swimming, drills, etc. - but one thing my charges didn't went through, but which i did in ITD, was water-obstacle crossing. Field training was also all done in mainland Singapore.

    Yeah, all Commandos will make the corporal rank (2.5-year NS) at the least, mono-intake notwithstanding.

    1 question, does mono intake BMT at dippe barrack, home of the 1st Guards mean u will be a guardsman after POP?
    Only after a subsequent Guards Conversion Course, to earn the tab. Mono-intakers also do vocational-training, but generally not at the relevant Formation School like the Tekong "grads."

    I dunno what's the current system, but for the old - Arty - format it was that the inarriving BC, BSM (both regulars) & Arty NCOs (10th-month NSFs) who conducted/trained together with the men during this period. The NSF officers & non-specialists would only be posted in about 3 months later, so that on paper everyone can technically ORD together 18 months on & enter the reservist ranks as a unit.
