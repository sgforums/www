    I suffered almost the exact same symtoms as you. I seem to be ok during BMT, then when i got posted to a combat unit I started to feel like you do... extremely moody, homesick, miserable, don't feel like socializing with my platoon mates etc etc.

    For no reasons also I started to reminisce about times gone by my primary school days etc... even missing my grandma who departed eight years ago and whose death I thought I have long accepted... I generally wallow in self-pity... to the extent that I was very reckless during training in the hope that I will be injured and downgraded.

    In retrospect and reflecting inward, I am not used to the regimental (and hectic) routines of a combat soldier and that is causing me a lot of stress.

    I often ask myself why I dont feel this way during BMT, and I manage to know the answer after a while.

    So how do I get out from this vicious cycle? I am also not sure. But despite myself, in time I started socializing with my platoon mates (those in combat will know that its next to impossible NOT to socialize with your platoon mates... given the amount of time you spent togather lol). That fostered some kind of comradeship and give meaning to the things I do in my unit. The things you and your platoon work towards, the pride when you have made some accomplishments (like being the key platoon in securing the best infantry unit for my unit, watched by minister somemore), and the punishment you all have to go through when you screw up (defaulter's parade anyone?)

    These kind of activities slowly but surely washes out the negative emotions and miseries in the early days of my posting... and by the time we finish all 3 overseas training, I am a proud member of my unit and my vocation...

    Hope that you will overcome your depression in time... Just my 2 cents, but I think socializing with your platoon mates would be a very good start!
