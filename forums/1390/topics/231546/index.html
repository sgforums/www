    Originally posted by Friar:
    1) What usually happens when I see the psychatrist at the MMI?
    2) Are cases like mine common?
    3) What usually happens after the initial visit at MMI?

    All replies are greatly appreciated in advance.
    1). The psychiatrist will give you a thorough psychological evaluation. I'm not sure how, but I suppose it'll be through interviews and such.

    2). How common? Hard to say. I've seen and met a number of patients diagnosed with depression when I was doing SAF Psychiatric Ward duty, but I don't have any hard numbers.

    3). That will be up to the discretion of the psychiatrist you meet. Depending on how his evaluation of your psychological condition, it can range from anywhere between issuing of medications and light duties to downgrading.

    Be sure to tell him that you're seeing a private external psychiatrist as well.

    Whatever the case, seeing your MO and getting an appointment at MMI is a step in the right direction. Keep your hopes up; depression is a condition that can be treated.

    Good luck.
