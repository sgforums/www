    Hi Friar, I've seen your thread and i believe that u are suffering what many ppl call BMT blues. I have also been through this phase in my BMT. But i have a more positive mindset, you must have a goal you hope to achieve when serving NS or else you would just think its a waste of time.

    Aim high!!!
    Like aspiring to go command school. When i was in BMT, i told myself i wanted to go SISPEC. Many of my platoon mates made fun of me by calling me SISPEC but i accepted that instead. In fact, they are are the ones that pushed me to go through whatever my instructors did to me. In fact, 1 instructor from another platoon was very pissed when other started calling me SISPEC and he started dishing out punishments on me. Many of myplatoon mates picked on me whenever they had the chance. I endured it and through this, it made me stronger and more determined. I decided to be proactive in whatever my instructors asked us to do.

    Volunteer your services!!!
    Do not be afraid!!!
    Although it might attract the jealousy of many of your platoon mates, but, in the end, u will gain their respect if you can overcome anything they dish out to you.

    Towards the end of BMT, I had earned the respect of many of many platoon mates who i have seen some of them breaking down and crying in bunk. Some of them have broken up with their girlfriends and some of them couldn't take the stress.
    Don't let this affect you!! You must tell yourself if you break down and cry yoursef, you will be like one of them, who are weak-minded and afraid.
    During the peer apprasial, many of them told me they had voted for me to go command school.

    Be confident!! No matter whether you made a mistake, be cool about it. Don't try to cover it up as it will make you seem hypocritical. Just be humble and modest but also at the same time proactive in whether tasks u have to do.

    With determination and aspirations, i believe that you can do it too like many of us who have gone through the fruitful years in National Service.

    ( By the way, i just finshed my NS, i still think about the good old times. The bonding with my men, the jokes i shared with my colleagues.... Those are unforgettable. Im going to Australia to study end of this month, but i will never forget to bring my army photos and memories with me there.)
