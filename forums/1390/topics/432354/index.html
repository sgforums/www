  • vonlichenstein

    You should not report it to the police, unless he threatened you outside of camp. Anything that happens in camp falls under military police jurisdiction

     

    If he threatened you in camp, inform your immediate superior officer. Don't exaggerate anything, just tell it like it happened. Try to get a witness to back up your claim if possible (if no one else heard it then you have no choice). If you have no witness, it makes it difficult to prove your case, but whatever you do don't lie.

     

    If your immediate superior takes no action, and your fellow NSF continues to threaten you, tell your superior that you wish to bring it up one more level, and inform his superior. BE OBJECTIVE at all times, and be sure that you do not appear to be trying to get him into trouble. He has no grounds to stop you, assuming you were objective and truthful throughout the process. Technically you can keep going up the line till the situation is rectified. But I trust that your officers will do the right thing.

     

    If all else fails, and the threats from your fellow NSF persist, call the SAF helpline. Such abuse should never be allowed or condoned.
