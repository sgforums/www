    I think that the army has progressed from being a huge tekan session to a professional and task-oriented one where there is less emphasis on breaking the soldier but rather building him up and giving him proper support to fulfill his duties properly.

    I don't think there is anything to be afraid of. I was nervous, anxious and yes, a little excited. And we all went through it together. It was a lot of fun. When I received my posting to 30SCE (field combat engineers), my mother cried because her hairdresser told her it was one of the worst vocations. But we all survived, didn't we? A few scratches here, some insect bites, rashes, etc and bruised ego from all the tekaning, but really, we all came out better for it.

    Remember, what can they do to you? Tekan you? So what? They can't kill you! (especially after the recent spate of deaths, there are more precautions to prevent more mishaps). If you think a certain order given to you is needlessly dangerous or will result in serious injury or death, bring it up to your superiors.

    In short, be smart, but have fun. It's a once in a lifetime opportunity! Guys in other countries don't even have a chance to hold a rifle. You have an opportunity to shoot lots of rounds, throw real grenades, fire grenade launchers and rockets, learn martial arts, get fit, blow things up and get paid for it!
