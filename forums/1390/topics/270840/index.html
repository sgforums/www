    Dont worry too much. There arent many water activities in army actually. For BMT, they will separate u into swimmers and non-swimmers. For non-swimmers, you will go for swimming lessons that are conducted in medium pools, not deep pools, which means your feet can still reach the bottom of the pool. And if you think you really have phobia of water, you can tell your platoon commander. I have some friends who got away with this excuse, provided you are convincing enough and your platoon commander is taken in by it.

    After BMT, you wont have much chances of water activities, unless you are being posted to the more elite combat forces where they have things like 2km swim, river crossing, diving etc. But even if you really sway sway kenna posted to such a unit, dont worry. All the combat water activities come with inflated life jacket. You will never drown unless you didnt wear the jacket properly or the jacket is damaged.
