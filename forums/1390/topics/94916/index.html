    "Fit for field duties but only in Unit HQ or rear areas."

    Tim Huxley mentioned in his book that the PES classification was finetuned down to let some previously-graded as combat unfit to pass muster as combat-vocationable, but who will be attached to the HQ or rear echelon - eg. HQ Signaller, Camp Medic, etc. - rather than to join the line troops. It supposedly freed up half of the affected 7,500 cases or something to become operational soldiers, albeit with reduced IPPT liability.

    I think it's the Pes C1L2 grading which serves this purpose.
