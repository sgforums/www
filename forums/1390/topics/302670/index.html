    Legacy of mono-intake system. When NS was first introduced, the Hokkien peng have to be posted somewhere, preferably together for communication's sake (many were really pai kia & druggies in those days). There was also political sensitivities about deploying Malays in combat roles.

    NSFs was then deployed to the SPF & SCDF (then only a fire brigade) from the mid'70s onwards, after the Robinsons Fire (1972) & the Laju Incident (1974). Then came then Total Defence Plan, & its constant finetuning through the years saw increasing specialisation & professionalisation of its personnel.

    At SCDF, firefighters got more modern equipment, merged organizationally with the civil defence proper (largely a Construction Brigade in those days -- yeah, really sai kang work!). Progressively, highly skilled paramedic rescue & hazmat training were also introduced.

    At SPF, changing security needs saw it expanding beyond law enforcement & constabulary work, for example to be deployed with the KINS units these days.

    The Malays who were diverted there (for sensitive reasons) have performed well enough in building a solid legacy (a different reason altogether) to maintain the status quo, so to speak. Kinda like "ain't broken don't fix it" mindset, the difference being those who make it to A Levels or Poly can now get sent back to combat, by way of BMTC.

    It's still early days, but I think the more we see NSFs doing Police KINS work, the more it's racially irrelevant to highlight their SAF combat counterparts anymore.
