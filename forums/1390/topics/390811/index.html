    What is an Exit Permit?
    The Ministry of Defence requires all NS liable persons to comply with the exit control regulation for security and mobilisation purposes. The reporting and Exit Permit requirements may vary in accordance with age, National Service status, duration of trip, purpose of visit and destination:

    Who should apply for an Exit Permit?

    Pre-enlistee:
    If you are between 11 and 16 and a half years of age, you do not require an Exit Permit for overseas travel. However, if you are above 16 and a half years old, and plan to travel for more than 3 months, you are required to apply for an Exit Permit. Relevant documentary proof is required to support your application.

    Full-Time National Serviceman and Regulars:
    You are required to apply for an Exit Permit as long as you are travelling out of Singapore. You must otain approval for overseas leave or ensure that you have not been scheduled any duty during the period of overseas trip, before applying for an Exit Permit through the Internet/Intranet. Once your application for Exit Permit is approved, an eEP number will be given to you for reference. No hardcopy Exit Permit will be issued.

    Disruptee:
    If you have disrupted from full-time NS for local study and would like to go overseas, you are required to apply for an EP.

    Operationally Ready National Servicemen:
    You are only required to apply for an exit permit if you are travelling out of Singapore for more than 6 months in a single trip.

    (NSmen: If your trip is more than 24 hours but less than 6 months, all you need is to contact MINDEF Notification Centre via MINDEF eServices Centre at 1800-3646333 to notify your trip. You may also visit www.miw.com.sg or send SMS (97646333) to make your notification.)
