    Originally posted by joie:

    Why is it wrong to set up a clothesline in field camp? Because it's completely against field discipline, that's why. If you can even ask this question, I think you answered your own question about completing the syllabus, because this is as basic to soldiering as the three R's are to schooling.

    All right, since your instructors sucked at their jobs, let me explain the idea of field discipline to you. The two objectives of of field discipline are:
    1) to leave no trace of your having been in a location
    2) to be ready to move at any time.

    Here's a little-known rule that your instructors seems to have left out of the syllabus: if the enemy can FIND you, he can KILL you. Surprising, isn't it? Leave traces of your existence in the field, and you may as well lay down a welcome mat for him to come and get you.

    So, how does this relate to laying clotheslines out in the field? First, you're effectively putting up a flag for the enemy to find. Second, the toggle rope or comms cord that you're using as the line leaves marks on bark - it's not a natural occurrence, and anybody patrolling will know for sure that another human being has been there. I may be overly optimistic in assuming that your instructors taught you rubbish discipline, but the same principle applies - a trail of rubbish in the field leads right back to you.

    Second, you need to be ready to move at any time. The enemy is not going to make an appointment to pop you a new rectum in your forehead. It's going to happen quietly and suddenly, and if your men are making sure that they get their 7 hours' sleep, the first you're going to know about it is when you feel the pinprick of a stiletto just as it enters your brain stem. If you're lucky, you won't get one of our guys, and instead get someone who prefers to shoot the enemy. In that case, you're extermely lucky if you're on your feet before the tenth man gets turned into mincemeat by the enemy's MG.

    Where does that situation leave you? You've got your rifle and SBO and maybe, just maybe, your full pack - sans clothes. Chances are that if you're feeling comfortable enough to set up a laundry at your LUP, you've also left all manner of equipment out of your pack. You have to bug out of there because an enemy company is overrunning your position, and you're left with less than half the equipment that you came in with - equipment that you may just need to get out.

    THAT is why you don't set up a clothesline in the field!
