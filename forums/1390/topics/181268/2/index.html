    You must fully understand your ROE card before firing any shots. ROE card is to protect you not confusing you. I know some rules stated in ROE card is confusing. Like the situation that thread starter stated just now. What happen if the "handgun" is real? ROE card says that "You may use lethal force if you or your buddy or your camp is under hostile threat." In the situation mention above, pointing a handgun towards your direction is a already a "hostile act". You felt threaten, you felt your live is in danger, you may shoot.

    But please take note above the threat level. I believe ROE card also states that "Use ENOUGH force to stop the threat." Lets say a guy is holding a parang charging at you from 50 meters away. You are threaten but NOT live endangering although "hostile act" has taken place because he is still 50 meters away. You may fire at his legs or choose to use you CCT which your have been taught. But pointing a handgun at you from 100 meters is a totally different case. Thats why you must act accordingly to the ROE card.

    Also take note about "hostile act" and "hostile intend". A guy holding a parang strolling around aimlessly is a "hostile intend" because he hasn't take any action against you or your buddy or the place you are required to protect. In this situation you should comms back to your higher HQ at the same time gives a proper warning. Point your weapon (DON'T CORK) and challenge him by shouting "STOP" if he stops, ask him to put down his parang, move 10 steps to either left or right and prone down. If the suspect didn't stop challenge him again and if he fails to comply, you MUST give a final warning which is "STOP OR I'LL SHOOT" at the same time cork your weapon. If he fails to comply and RUN away, YOU DON'T SHOOT. Running away means that the threat is gone.

    If suspect is pointing a handgun, there isn't a need to give a warning because the situation doesn't allow you to do so.

    Me had one near-firing situational encounter before when me was serving my fulltime service. It happened in a NS Camp in Clementi area in 1996. Me remembered that me was the only rifle-trained guard on duty that day and the rest of the guards were on truncheon-and-shield. There was an armed fight within the camp between a SSG and 3SG as the SSG used a 20cm blade knife to attack the 3SG.

    DO called up Guardroom and activated Guard Cmdr for Riot Turnout. Me so sway, been the only rifle-trained guard to draw 5 rounds and load into rifle (but never cocked yet) and ran all the way to the Specialist's Mess while trying to fix my bayonet to the muzzle.

    On arrival, me saw the 3SG on the ground and the SSG with the weapon in his hand. DO instructed Guard Cmdr to execute, shouting at the SSG to drop his weapon, or else he will be shot. The SSG didn't heed the warning and charged towards my Guard Cmdr. Immediately, DO shouted: "On guard! Shoot at limbs!" Me stunned for a while and quickly cocked my weapon and "Bang!". One shot out and my shot hit the SSG's thigh, but he still charged towards my Guard Cmdr. DO shouted again: "Shoot at body!". Another shot was fired and the SSG dropped onto the ground. "Guard! Move forward and secure!" DO said.

    Me moved forward and kicked the weapon away. The SSG was moaning in pain as my shot hit his right shoulder. When me looked back, me saw my Guard Cmdr was still in shock, maybe due to the sudden turn of event.

    2 rounds out of 5 were enough to earn two months of regular visits (almost became a daily event for me) to SAFPU and MINDEF Gombak for statements and investigations!
