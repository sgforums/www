    first of all, welcome!

    and glad u haf a positive attitude towards ur ns too.

    i frm inf. so wat i can say here is frm my limited knowledge.

    there r 2 types of bmt - mono intake or bmtc.

    mono-intake - u go to ur unit for bmt. hence u dun haf a choice wich vocation u land in. it mabbe gds, inf, cdo... watever. but once u r there u r there for ur entire ns. unless o cos if accidents happen, etc.

    for bmtc, i guess u can state ur interest, but im not sure how much tt wil influence ur posting after bmt.

    if u kanna posted to gds, cdo frm bmtc, u wil go there and go 4 their conversion cses, details of wich u haf to find out from others here. after tt u r one of them.

    or u may go to advanced schs like sispec and ocs.

    frm there, o cos u may get posted to gds or cdo, etc after ur cse too. and similarly u go 4 their conversion cses den.

    as for ppl signing on, u wil go bmtc for ur bmt, and den either go to the unit, go sispec or ocs, depending on the appointment u sign on to. after tt den u get posted to one of the units for tt vocation and go 4 conversion.

    hope tt helps.

    some basic commands - good to noe now

    1. samula - do again!
    2. se-di-a - attention
    3. se-nang-di-ri - at ease
    4. ke-ka-nan pu-seng - turn to the right
    5. ke-ki-ri pu-seng - turn to the left
    6. ke-bla-kan pu-seng - turn to the back
    7. dari ka-nan - take dressing to the right; commonly used with 9
    8. dari ki-ri - take dressing to the left; commonly used with 9
    9. je-pa-ja-lan - march in quick time(dunno if tts the exact meaning);
    10. han-ta-ka-ki je-pa-han-ta - march on the spot
    11. lim-peh-ka-nan - shift to the right (while marching)
    12. lim-peh-ki-ri - shift to the left (while marching)

    the commands below are for arms drill.
    the weapon is called - sen-ja-ta. hence all the commands below will be followed by sen-ja-ta

    11. ru-so - port arms
    12. ju-lang - high port
    13. tu-ron - down arms
    14. ho-mat - salute

    due to my failing memory, i have forgotten quite a number of commands, such as running. i am not so sure if tu-ron and ju-lang were interpreted correctly or not! please come and add-on or amend!
