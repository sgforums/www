    22 Jan 05, 12:16
    Originally posted by ironpeasant:
    Hi i'll be enlisting early april. Just a few qns:

    1) Is it recommended to start training prior to enlisting? I'm PES A. Been doing 50 push ups, 4 km runs, 12 pull ups, 50 sit ups every 2 days. Is this enuff?

    2) Is it true that a high percentage of JC ppl enter OCS?

    3) For OCS, guards, cdos etc. Do the recruits opt to enter or is by selection?
    of course its recommended to start training to prepare yourself before enlisting. this way you wont suffer as much while in there. what you mentioned should be more or less enough. but you may wish to vary your training, perhaps to increase the running distance or speed, or vary your statics every now and then. the purpose is to ensure your fitness level increases generally and comprehensively.

    the percentage of a cohort going to OCS is more or less constant. the jan and apr cohorts, which are the JC cohorts, should have more or less the same number of persons going to OCS as the other cohorts. hence to say that a high percentage of JC students enter OCS is not quite right but not wrong too.

    for OCS you would have to indicate your preference for command positions during your bmt. as for guards, you may state your preference to your PC but in the end its up to the requirement and your PC. and CDOs are different - they are enlisted directly to CDO, go through CDO BMT, and CSLC. only those who have performed remarkably would then join OCS midway through the course.
