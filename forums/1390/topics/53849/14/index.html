    Enhanced BMT
    10 weeks
    This course trains combat-fit commander-potential recruits in the basic military skills. These recruits usually go on to do Specialists or Officer courses to become commanders. The Enhanced BMT has additional modules to train and select these recruits. For those who failed to achieve the NAPFA test silver award, they are required to undergo a 6-week Physical Training Phase prior to the Enhanced BMT.

    Standard BMT
    8 weeks
    To train recruits meant for combat vocations such as those who will be assigned to units as signallers, combat medics and military policemen, etc after their BMTC. The training is similar to that for Enhanced BMT, less the modules on training and selection of commanders. Similarly, those who failed to achieve the NAPFA test silver award are also required to attend a 6-week Physical Training Phase before attending the actual BMT.

    Modified BMT
    7 weeks
    To train recruits of lower medical fitness for assignment to selected service vocations after completing BMT. Training objectives and intensity are lower to reflect the lower combat fitness of this group of recruits.

    Obese BMT
    16 weeks
    The objective is to reduce the weight of the obese recruits. More time is devoted to progressive physical training. Those who have the necessary capabilities will be selected for further leadership training in OCS and SISPEC.

    Induction Program
    1 week
    The objective of the induction program is to familiarise recruits with the organisation and culture of the SAF. This program is catered for recruits with serious medical conditions.

    Women BMT
    2 weeks & 8 weeks
    The 2 weeks course is for women in service vocation, while the 8 weeks course is for those in combat vocation.
