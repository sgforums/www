    Low rope can juggle between strength & technique, unlike the ROC version which must demonstrate arm power all the way.

    To "cheat" more with technique, crunch "fetally" as much as possible whenever lifting your lower body upwards. Then knot the rope quickly with your foot, to minimize the strain on your stomach muscles. Straighten your body immediately, before repeating the whole process again.

    The regular sit-ups in PT lessons can already improve your endurance here, so no "need" for extra push-ups "help". Just master the technique & try to hit the top with the minimum knots (3?) decisively every time. Any extra knot will only lengthen your timing & tire you unnecessarily.

    Low rope is technique....its about the locking of the rope....so that u can "step on it".

    Think about this....when u jump....ur feet must get as high as possible, to lock the rope, such that when you can rest on the rope and stand up straight, grab higher.

    As you grab higher....now hold the rope with ur hands and swing ur feet as high as possible and lock the rope again, then 'stand on the rope again'...

    You do NOT pull urself up with the rope....most of our arms are not that powerful, its about locking the rope and standing up again and again.
