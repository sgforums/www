    10 Mar 07, 10:16
    Nonsense.
    The purpose of having Guards is to have a corps of infantry capable of air mobile operations. It's true, Guards are better trained in some aspects and have a reputation of being "elite" but if you think that one brigade of active Guardsmen and a couple more of Reservists Guards can tackle an entire front, you must be crazy.

    The purpose of the Infantry is always to take and hold ground. Only boots on the ground can conquer terrain and this is precisely why we have so much infantry. If you read about conflicts raging round the world today, you'll find that the Infantry are bearing the brunt of the work especially in areas of Urban Terrain (you can't clear a building with a tank).

    In Singapore, the Infantry are not well recognised and they are not as glamorous as Guards/Commandos. In my opinion, the Infantry is one formation that loses out on the better talents who opt to sign on because the best always aim for the fancy stuff like Guards, Commandos & Armour. However, the latest initiatives within the Infantry like POI, Urban warfare and the new Combined Arms Initiatives (I can't elaborate since it's still Classified) will go some way in raising the profile of the Infantry.

    What ever the case, if we ever go to war, it won't be the Guards, Commandos or Armour that raises the flag of victory, it'll be the infantry: the poor bloody, muddy, unglamorous, unappreciated infantry.

    The standards of Infantry here are quite decent. We are not as fit and probably not as disciplined as some other countries but we know some lessons that our neighbour's guys take for granted e.g. We travel at night and as much as possible we keep off roads. Sounds simple but many of our neighbours don't follow these rules. A road-bound infantry who travel in the day are easy targets. The reason they don't follow these rules are lack of Night Fighting Equipment, lack of Navigation equipment (GPS) and basic navigation training for non-officers.

    In times of real war, the Guards and Commandos will be "blazing forth first" but their job is to maneuvre and fly around or behind the enemy and attack them from the flanks or from behind. It will be the Infantry and Armour that maintain and fight the bloody battles at the Frontline. The infantry will move in to secure terrain, get Infantry Pioneers to take out mines and other obstacles and let the Armour move through. Infantry will bear the brunt and we will do the dirty work and yes, we are the most vulnerable but we are also the most needed. The enemy can kill off the Guards and Commandos and we'll still survive, but if they kill the Infantry, the front collapses and it's Game Over.
