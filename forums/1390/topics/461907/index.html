    Specialist Cadet School (SCS)

    3-Term Programme
    The Specialist Cadet School (SCS) trains selected soldiers with leadership potential from all formations in the Army to be commanders.

    Although trainees enter SCS with very different expectations, they all agree on one thing when they graduate - training was tough but rewarding.

    Trainees at SCS first undergo the Foundation Term during which they are taught basic infantry skills to command an infantry section. During the first week of the Foundation Term, trainees are also presented with the Specialist Cadet Rank.

    After the Foundation Term, trainees are sent to the various formations for specialised training in their respective vocations during the Professional Term. Trainees who are selected for the Infantry vocation will remain in SCS for their Professional Term.

    Finally, under the Combined Arms Term, all trainees will return to SCS to share with each other on the different experiences and skills that they have learnt during their Professional Term.

    Command Teams and Faculty Teams
    At SCS, there is a strong focus on character and leadership development as well as imparting essential soldering skills. To achieve these goals, SCS trainers are organised into Command Teams and Faculty Teams.

    Command Teams will stay with the same company of cadets throughout their term in SCS. This provides them the time and opportunities to fully understand and mould the cadets in their leadership and management skills.

    The Faculty Teams help to strengthen the cadets' breadth and depth of understanding in their war fighting skills. Each team consists of instructors with different areas of expertise such as weapon handling, navigation and signalling. With the Faculty Teams, the cadets are able to gain a full picture of our Army operations.

    The MO has all the power to say if you're fit/unfit. Because if he certifies you fit and something happens after that, he'll be responsible. Having that responsbility gives him the power (that's why he wears the CPT rank) to declare you unfit/OOC if necessary.

    You need to complete BSLC to get CPL. Otherwise you will leave SCS as a PTE.

    You might get posted to become an admin supervisor or CQ, etc. which will still get you a 3SG in the end. or you might end up as a clerk, cameraman, whatever....

    Postings are anybody's guess, its according to manpower requirements at the MINDEF level, wherever needs people, you're just a name they fit into the next vacancy.
