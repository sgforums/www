    Wah, very wayang leh!  Does Mediacock know you're available?

    It's been dog's ages since I did the VA but the basic idea's still the same.  Just go, follow the instructions, do the tests and that's that.  The last thing CDOs look for is a hero because garang kings are just hazardous to the men around them.

    I wouldn't sweat the fitness issue too much - the army operates on the principle of, "you can be trained".  I've seen seriously unfit guys make it to the beret, and I've seen JC sporting heros wash out completely.

    If they figure you're going to gut out the training without turning on the waterworks and becoming a blubbering mess, you're off to a good start.  If you can follow instructions but at the same time be able to think for yourself, all the better.  All they're looking for is someone who can keep his head screwed on right and be competent at a job that can be a trying experience at times.
