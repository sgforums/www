    RSAF pilots and those who have been through the application please come in!

    I am currently JC 1 student, and it may be too early for me to ask this, but nevertheless i hope my doubts can be cleared.

    Like many teenagers who yearn to be an airforce pilot, i applied and joined the Singapore Youth Flying Club (SYFC) to learn flying. I have read widely that getting a PPL can skip the airgrading course in RSAF and boost the chances of getting in. I know it is extremely strict and difficult to make it to the PPL phase. Due to study committments (as i am in JC not poly, since the poly students get more flexible timing and more time to fly), i was behind time in phase 1 and was unfortunately phased out after completing sortie 6. The instructor told me that i had potential but due to their timeframe, SYFC won't let me continue as i was 5 sorties behind my batchmates who made it into phase 2. By that time, the next batch are already overtaking my batch. Thus, i was very disheartened and i fear that it may affect my chances of becoming an RSAF pilot. My instructor told me to tell the interviewers that i was stopped from flying due to time and he will tick the box which says i have the potential to be a pilot.

    Anyone that only completed phase 1 of BFC, successfully become a pilot? I really hope that my experience in SYFC would not backfire and in turn led to me not getting selected as i only managed to complete phase 1. I really wants to be a RSAF pilot.

    Hey! I was also in the exact same situation as you. I applied for syfc and joined it a few years back. However, I only managed to complete phase 1 due to their strict timeframe, I was too slow haha. I was very disappointed and demoralised when i got phased out but my instructor still gave me a good testimonial and tick the potential to be a pilot box. Like you, i wanted to be an airforce pilot so during my last semester in poly i applied for the pilot voccation and i have thankfully managed to clear my compass,interview and medical.

    Anyway, the point is you don't have to worry so much. Completing only phase 1 in syfc does not ruin your chances of getting selected. However during the pilot interivew they are definately going to ask you why you only completed phase 1 in syfc. They key is to be humble and admit your mistakes. So all the best and good luck!
