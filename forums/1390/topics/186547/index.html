    Depends on your appointment, the country you are going, and if the country you go have pre-agreed arrangements.

    THree general modes of travelling overseas representing, SAF.

    1) Travel by nominal roll,

    Receiving country will receive a copy of your photo, and id details. Just present yourself at their airport, they will just stamp your in/out endorsment in SAF's copy of the list.

    2) Travel by Passport

    Just like any other travel. Usually for countries where SAF do not have regular training with OR that country's law do not provide for such arrangements.

    Some service personnel in the frame who will travel by nominal roll will travel using passports, this is in case there is emergency they will need to leave without the others.

    3) Diplomatic Passage

    SAF will go thru Mindef and Foreign Affairs who will request admission of servicepersonel into that country. Usually for last minute incidents (eg, Tsunami).
