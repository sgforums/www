    I think this is showing up the shortcomings in having a conscripted army.

    Look at how the British Army is mostly volunteer professionals, with career soldiers. Even the reservists are 'voluntary'.
    Look at how Russia is scaling back its (once-massively) conscripted numbers to rebuild with professional soldiers.

    With a force composition of (apparently, going by the threads in this forum) so few professionals, perhaps it is time for sg to look at what the advantages and disadvantages are of a conscripted v professional military force.

    With a professional volunteer force you always know that they would be at the peak of their abilities at all times (ergo, be able to operate under harsh conditions with all types of gear, without complaining incessantly to their mothers).
    Those that can keep at their peak even after service, often retain their skillsets via the Reserves (Territorial Army - British equivalent).
    This means the Military always has a pool of very highly skilled and capable forces, and they do not have to worry about 'skinny, doctors-note-carrying lambs' that do more to hinder (or, indeed, embarrass it) the service than to augment it.

    Conscripted armies are good for cannon fodder and paper tigers.
    A professional, full-time force is much more of a imposing image on the battlefield to any conscripted army that dares oppose them.
    Time for sg to have a rethink?
