    Not sure what's your exact context here. You're talking about active time or reservist? Or are you saying CQs, MT line, Signal Stores & Medical Centers, etc. cannot have a ranking NCO over their men?

    If you dislike rear echelon personnel, hey, join the club. But those departments are also run militarily, & taking charge as a (formal) IC is the foremost job of a 3SG, regardless of vocation.

    CPLs don't make 3SGs per se. Lemme bring up some historical perspective here. Back in the mid-80s, apocryphally, some higher-ups disliked the trend of Air-Level enlistees making CPL, thereby becoming of equal rank with the SAFINCO-trained ones. A new rank, Corporal First Class (CFC), was introduced to supposedly redress this -- except it never took off on the ground. I believe one of the original intentions was to make every "proper" NCO a CFC, but it turned out to be rear-echelon people who got promoted most instead. Complicating matters, the latter new CFCs were often Departmental 2ICs or equivalent, a subordinated role which became associated with the rank. The design of the rank itself, which is similar to today's CPL one, proved to be controversial too after it became popularly coined as "two-&-a-half-stripe ", insinuating a cursory contrivance (despite its good intentions). The feedback must've been conspiciously negative, cuz not soon after the SAF announced a complete revamp of the NCO rank hierarchy (which I'm sure might've been tied up with other, larger policy changes).

    If you're advocating to bar non-combat troops from making 3SG, then it won't work cuz the next-lowest rank (CFC) isn't a NCO rank. Yet your QM or your MO needs someone principally-trained to help him run & manage his people. Acting-IC appointments do happen fairly regularly in reservist, but they tend to be temporary & in any case don't fit into any acceptable military chain of command.

    As for "indiscriminate " NCO promotions in reservist, what you're discovering is what I (& others) did earlier: it's a different system from active time. Only the KAHs climb up in rank now. For what it's worth, in our local reservist context, your CQ or Chief Clark or MT Sergeant has a more vital role in the unit's overall functioning than your usual Platoon Commander. Hence they get promoted, while the company-line LTAs don't. Hence they get called back earlier & more often than the main body, where the PFCs/CPLs/LTAs/3SGs report for ICT together &, as a group, don't get promoted throughout their entire cycle.

    But I reckon even disappointed reservists will take these quirks in stride. By all means, cosy up to your commander if there's an opening. Even if you're willing to use up the maximum 40 days recallable each year, do you really want to risk getting recycled to a newly-ORDed unit when you hit your mid-30s? That's what KAHs are open at risk to, esp. officers since they're legally obliged to serve until they're 50! (If you're that chao siao on, why didn't you sign on as a regular in the first place?)

    Here's a bet: You'll laugh back someday at this initial disillusionment. I remember doing two men's work during one ROC (my buddy deferred that ICT) &, afterwards during the debrief, jokingly asked my commander about putting me up for 3SG. We laughed it off, he then reminded me about the KAH thing, & predicted I won't be as enthusasistic after a few years. Turned out to be true cuz after the 6th ICT or so, when everyone is nearing their late 20s & have heavier job, spousal & familial responsibilities outside. My unit visibly declined as a result, which was coincidentally when a number of our KAHs were identified for re-coursing for future redeployments (i.e. recycling). The rest of us were mightily relieved, & looked forward to standing down instead. No more callups or IPPTs once posted to the MINDEF reserves, regardless of age & legal obligations.
