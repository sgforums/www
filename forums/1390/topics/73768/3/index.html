    Hi, noticed that this thread has gone quite silent for a while. Am new here, but would like to add some comments.

    Personally, many people have told me that they recieved letters from Commandos and they simply rejected them. But really, not that I am doubting them or anything, speaking from experience, when Commandos send you a letter, you HAVE to go. It's called a Vocational Assignment (VA). It's stated in fine prints that you will be charged if you don't turn up. The penalty is either a sentence or a fine, or both. I don't know what letter these people I have talked to claimed to have gotten, but I don't think that is the real letter if you can ignore it.

    And also, once you are selected, you CANNOT say "no thank you, I am fine where I am". There is no such thing as rejection once they put you down on their list. But then again, there are always exceptions in SAF. Anyone would like to offer their insight?
