    On 26 Mar 1991, Singapore Airlines Airbus flight SQ 117 took off from Subang Airport in Kuala Lumpur with 129 passengers and crew onboard at 1815 hrs. Four passengers who claimed to be Pakistanis took control of the plane when it was enroute to Singapore. The plane landed and parked at Changi Airport's Runway One at 2215 hrs.

    Even as the plane arrived, an Executive Group of high-ranking officials from various organisations including MINDEF, Ministry of Home Affairs, Ministry of Information and the Arts, the Singapore Airlines and a negotiating team were activated.

    Demands

    The hijackers claimed to be members of the Pakistan People's Party. They wanted the release of former Pakistan Prime Minister Benazir Bhutto's husband and other PPP members detained in Pakistani jails. They also wanted the plane refuelled so that they could fly to Australia.

    On 27 Mar 91 at 0230 hrs, the plane was moved to an outer tarmac. The hijackers pushed two SIA stewards out of the plane, injuring them. The injured stewards provided vital information, which assisted the eventual storming of the plane.

    Countdown and Rescue

    The hijackers issued a five-minute deadline at 0645 hrs after which they threatened to kill one passenger every ten minutes if their demands were not acceded to.

    On the countdown to three minutes, the order was given to storm the plane. Elite Commandos stormed the plane, killing four Pakistani hijackers and freeing all 118 passengers and 9 crew. The rescue of SQ 117 was over in just 30 seconds and ended at 0650 hrs. None of the passengers and crew were hurt.

    PM Goh's Commendation

    Prime Minister Goh expressed his appreciation to everyone involved in the rescue, the officials from various ministries, statutory boards and SIA. He praised them for their swift and efficient handling of the crisis.

    CPT Stanley Lim, the pilot of SQ 117 and Supt Foo Kia Juah, the leader of the Police negotiating team each received the Public Service Star for their role in the rescue. The SAF Commandos were awarded the Medal for Valour while the members of the unit and members of Police negotiating team were awarded Presidential Certificates of Commendation at a separate ceremony.

    With all due respect, Sean, your view is problematic in that it not only is based upon 20/20 hindisght, but it also attempts to compare apples to oranges.

    First, of course, we have the hindsight issue. Yes, we now know that they had firecrackers and knives, but what would you have been thinking at H-Hour, standing outside the aircraft door and not knowing what you were going to find in there? That, I propose, makes the difference between the force line boys and the rest of us.

    Second, SQ117 and Thunderbolt were entirely different missions altogether.
    - 117 took 55 seconds to complete; Thunderbolt took 58 minutes
    - 117 took place entirely within the confines of the aircraft; the Thunderbolt hostages were in Entebbe airport, where there was more space to work with
    - 117 took place within the space of a day; Thunderbolt stretched over 8 days, during which the terrosists had time to grow complacent, especially since they had the support of the Ugandan troops

    It therefore would not be appropriate to say who did a better job since the two missions were vastly different.
