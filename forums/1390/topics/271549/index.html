    Aiyah, Oakley salesman sometimes also screwed-up. He thinks that all Oakleys are Milspecs, and anyone who puts that on becomes Tom Cruise. Not all models have the same ballistic resistance grade, especially those you picked from the shop.

    Just look at the thickness of the polycarbonate lens and don't let anyone bluff you. Compare it to ESS, Wiley-X lens. I have seen demos of the sales guys bending the lens over without breaking. The technology to date does not allow for a thin lens to have high impact resistance, that's why helmet visor shields are so thick for better protection. It needs to be at least 2.XX mm (not so sure what is that magic number) to meet the prescribed military specifications.

    Also, Oakley is famous for its optical clarity, and the thicker the lens get, the visual clarity will reduce.
