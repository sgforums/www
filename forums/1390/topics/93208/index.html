    18 Sep 04, 23:53
    As I recall, one of the animated discussions my reservist kaki's had during training downtime - plenty of time for them when out in the field - was whether SAFPU could be considered an local elite force, in the same mold as Commandos, Guards, Navy Diving Unit, RSAF Black Knights, etc.

    There wasn't a consensus. Obviously, many of its sub-units have extrajuristical military powers - eg. MPs, SIB investigators, anti-riot squad - & as such SAF soldiers, regardless of rank, held them up with appropriate respect. But many of us also thought that its precision-drill unit created a negative perception, in the sense that the public thought that's all they do (beyond the "discipline master" niche within army camps).

    I also believe that, come reservist, a lot of them were individually redeployed to become PDF troops, & thus "looked down" upon by soldiers with frontline, ops-ready combat outfits.

    But, for BMT during my time, SAFPU was definately one of the postings the better-performing recruits could aim for (besides OCS & SAFINCO/SISPEC).
