    One thing good about NS is that you'll meet many different types of people and learn how to handle "workplace politics"... (e.g. boss-employee relationships, tricks to score points, backstabbing colleagues and competitors, forming alliance and teams, etc etc) After going through NS, you will be more cautious of people around you and you'll be able to protect yourself better when you go to work. (Of course, you won't say all this if all your superiors and colleagues during NSF are very nice to you. For me, I have "kena" lousy officers, cunning platoon-mates, people who steal things from bunks, people who backstab one another in order to score point, etc.) In NS, if you stay high-profile, you will get a lot of arrows. If you accomplish too much, people will be envious and they will find ways to backstab you. But if you stay low-profile, people will think you are slacking and they will also arrow you for sai-kang. So it's important to find a balance in order to have a comfortable life during NSF.

    On the other hand, one thing bad about NS (for me) is the long duration. I served a full 2 and a half years, and I spent most of my time doing nothing productive. (Nothing productive for me, but a lot of things for SAF... haha) Now, NSF is reduced to 2 years, but it's still too long for me. Also, another bad about NS is that after NSF, we still have to go through reservice... so the "nightmare" drags on after the completion of NSF...
