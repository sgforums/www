    Air Operations Systems Specialist
     
      

    The Challenge

    As an AOSS, you'll play a unique dual role in:

    1) Command, Control & Communication

    Coordinate all airborne missions, including search & rescue and transport operations
    Provide assistance to distress calls
    Execute deployment of our air defence weapon systems

    2) Technical role

    Maintaining, troubleshooting & servicing highly sophisticated equipment, including state-of-the-art communication systems, radar tracking and sensor systems.

    The Requirements

    As an Air Operations Systems Specialist, you should be:


    medically fit

    Your eyesight should be:

    normal colour vision
    not more than 500 degrees

    Academically, you should have:

    A relevant Engineering Diploma in Mechantronics, Microelectronic, Computer Network Tech, Electrical, Electrical and Computer Control Engineering, Electronic, Electronic and Computer Engineering, Computer or Telecommunications; OR Higher Nitec in Electrical, Electronics Engineering or Information Technology; OR Nitec in Electronics (Instrument, Video Technology or Digital Equipment), Electronic (Wafer Fab).

    If you are a student or are planning on studying at the ITE/Polytechnic, you should check out the Polytechnic Sponsorship Scheme or ITE Sponsorship Scheme.

    u want to know what AOSS do?if you pass your checkup, you'll be posted to airforce school to start your training.. you'll be posted to the technical side for a few weeks for a short course, after that your basic aoss course will begin and last for about 8-10 weeks.
