    2 and a half weeks out of actions is quite a bad situation for you.

    You see, in order for a recruit to pass out from BMT, there is a certain set of criterions to be fulfilled, like going through a certain course, going through your range, etc. By missing out on your range, you could fail to meet these criterions. You could however compensate for these by going for makeup range, etc.

    When a recruit has OOC, he's immediately told or at least informed as soon as possible. When that happens, he becomes like a helper around the company for the platoon until the end of the course. He does not have to participate in the actual activities, but his help is required in preparing the stores, or some other minor stuffs.

    Depending on your condition and availability of the course you will be retaking, you could be immediately goes into a new BMT course once the current one is finished, or a few months later. There are some who pass their BMT close to the end of their 2 and a half years. If you are not posted to a new BMT course, chances are you will be sent to a unit as a man, not the combat type, but the helper type.

    Hope this is helpful.

    2 wks of att C almost certainly means OOC.

    next - depends on if ur med status is going to be affected. MO will prob need to see you after your long MC to endorse ur MC and at the same time, review ur case.

    if no change, den u will wait for next bmt.
    if there is a need for change, den it will depend on the med review.

    in the meantime u will prob be staying at the company line doing saigang. there is a possibility of being posted out to unit to do saigang, although i would say the chances are slim - it happens if u r given a temp downgrade (say, pes c for 3 months)

    as for avoiding recourse, the answer is no, there is no such way unless u get downgraded. and the hearsay about making one finish the course just to OOC b4 POP is just tt - hearsay.

    hope this helps. nothing much to fear or worry about. take life in stride, one day at a time.
