    I used to be in Pes Bp, and the sergeants realise that we are different from Pes A and B. Your training will differ from them in the sense that you guys do things at a gradual pace, rather than going all the way right frm the start. I would suggest to TS to start jogging 2km 2-3times a week first. Later on, increase the distance, and probably ur jogging speed. If possible, go to the gym 2 train up for ur chin-up, because a lot of Bp folks cannot do chin-up at all. Ask the instructor there what euqipments can be used for this purpose. Even if before NS you cannot do chin-up, at least u will definitely lose some weight.

    Originally posted by R3SsH|n:
    start some fast walking then progress to slow jogging without stopping..

    since u pes Bp..they probably will make u run till there is no tml...
    This is a myth for PES BP training. Being obese (mild / moderate / morbid), the recruit is subjected to be more prone to heat stroke, dehydration then normal recruits, plus their physical fitness wouldn't be that good. Running like no tomorrow will only endanger the recruits. Progressive training is planned. Instructors will most probably encourge their BP recruits to do OTOT training after dinner if there's no strenuous activities the next day.

    BP training program is targetted at losing weight, not totally on fitness training. That is why their exercises have lesser varieties but more repetitions.
