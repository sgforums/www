    Ehz, actually what can be said to be true is that while there are avenues for soldiers/sailors/airmen/marines to go from "green to gold" (Enlisted to Commissioned) in the US Military.

    The main forms of Officer Ascension programs in the US Military are still the Service Academies (USMA, USNA, USAFA - Each Cadet/MIDS taking four years to earn his/her dip+degree+commision[US citizens only]), Officer Candidate Schools(much shorter, I'm not certain of the length) and ROTCs...(not sure about the Arti officer part though)

    An Enlisted, if having proven him/herself can actually join any of the above (less ROTC)to earn their commissions together with other Cadet/MIDS who are fresh (no previous military experience..)

    With all due respect, such arrangment ensures a steady mix of experience within the junior ranks of the officer corp but I suppose time is a luxury we can't afford.. At least not to have ALL our officers rotate thru the basic ops level before moving them up...
