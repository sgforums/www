    Why no one mentioned the LAT pulldown machine ?

    Okay first thing first. To clarify things the proper way of doing chip ups is to grip the bar shoulde lenght apart using the over-graft method. Overgraft grip is palms facing down , while undergraft is palms facing up.

    To properly execute a chinup, you have to use your back muscle, commonly known in the gym as your lats. the secondary muscle group used are the forearm muscle for gripping the bar and biceps for the final curling motion of ur arm.

    Therefore pushups does not help you doing chinups UNLESS your using the undergraft method which is not recommended as one of the SOC obstacles, the Swing Trainer requires you grip bars, and i have not seen one that can move through the bars using the undergraph method.

    So to train your chinups.

    1) Try hanging from the bar in sets of 3. this is to train up your fore arm muscle. which will help u grip the bar better.try to time your hanging times. and slowly you can progressively hang longer and develop better strength.

    2) Try to go to the gym and use the LAT pull down machine. i could not pull a single chinup back in secondary school, and only after spending like 1 mth on the machine, i could do 3 chinups.

    3) if a gym is not avaiable or convienet, go to the fitness park and try to pull chinups 3 times a wk. this will help you train your muscles needed to do the chin ups.

    gd luck .

    agree w one-niner.. I would recommend using the LAT pulldown machine in the gym for those who can't do chinups yet. Cos its specfic (ie train the exact muscles used for chinups) and progressive (you can start with lighter weights and slowly strengthen as you go along)

    Do about 3 sets of 10 pulldowns at about 80% of your max, everyday.. increase after a couple of days. initially, u'll feel the strain in your muscles but once you get used to it, then it'll be ok.

    If no gym ard, doing Flex arm hang is also ok.. hang for 8-10 sec, do 5-8 sets..
