  • specfore
    18 Mar 05, 21:06
    Within the MPs, there are sub-vocations.... one of my friends was with a investigative support unit. You get to visit camps to follow-up on incidents of a "criminal and serious" nature, e.g. such as shootings, murder, fraud, big time theft , any crime that goes beyond the CO's jurisdiction..... Any vacancies are usually reserved for A level or poly grads , since there is a bit of paper work and tech stuff involved.

    There are other MP units that are more mundane.... e.g. guard units. Here, you have to learn to live with the boredom. There have been a few cases in the good old days, where MPs have accidentally discharged their pistols , when they were playing with them while bored at their post. So be careful and responsible.

    I think you are preliminarily assessed based on your education and background before you go into MP training. The interview is then used to screen out a few more.

    My cousin was in the "guard" unit type MP. He was not A level / poly batch .... but he was tall and presentable... so ended up a MP at mindef HQ.

    My friend, was A level, later university.... he was in the plain clothes unit, which did investigations. He is definitely not the type who would want to be a MP guard ... if you know what I mean. Actually he went to SAFINCOS (SISPEC) and then posted to the MPs. Got to do a lot of interesting things... and more or less 8 to 5 job... except when there was a case to follow-up or visits to camps at nights.
