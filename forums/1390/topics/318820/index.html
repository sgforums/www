    the bond is 10 years. you haf to wait til cmpb sends u the medical screening letter then u can apply. the grading is on the spot. once u are certified pes b or a just go online and apply. they wld get back to u in one week and will ask u to go down for the compass test. the compass test is about psychomotor skills. its a test which wld also find out more abt your personality which wld be used to get to noe u, and to size you up during interview. the key is to be honest. if u pass the compass test, they wld call u up in 3-weeks. if not, u wld receive a letter. if u get nothing, call them. if u pass, most probably u wld la, they will schedule u for an interview date before the pilot selection board. during the interview they wld ask you random questions. wad they are looking for is ppl who can adapt to military life, face shit, mentally sound and haf leadership qualities. they wld most definitely zero in on some screw ups in ur life ranging from school to yfc, and see how u answer. cope wif the things they throw at u. be courteous and patient. they can be very very nasty and mean and say things abt u which isn't true. juz be honest, polite and cool-headed. btw, do research on the rsaf intensively. if u pass the interview, you'd be given a civil service personnel form to fill in, a surety form for ur guarantors and u'd be given a date for ur aeromedical. aeromedical part is straightforward. results wld be out on an avg of 3 mths. and when u've passed that, you've cleared the selection phase. when u go in bmt, u'd haf to qualify for ocs on ur own merits. and after that u'd be called down to mindef to sign your trainee contract. you'd report to AFS when the posting orders are out. training will thence commence. yup. i hope i've given u the info u needed dude. if this is your dream...go for it. don't live life regretting having not giving wad u want a shot. all the best. =)

    about the aeromedical, your biodata wld be taken, x-ray, blood, urine, eyesight, ears, nose etc. very standard stuff. its pretty much like the yfc and the cmpb test. only thing is...they are very strict. strict meaning, its harder to clear a station. yup. on the day of ur aeromedical, do not go if you are feeling unwell or you haf a block nose. read the instructions carefully when you are there. bring along your glasses, dun wear contacts, drink plenty of water.
