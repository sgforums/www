    Originally posted by crosshairs:
    The reason why most of the snipers I know like to load round by round through the ejection window, is due to ........ ok I think that is somewhat sensitive information which I shall not divulge.
    I wouldn't consider it sensitive information. It's common knowledge amongst the precision rifle shooting community here that single-loading a cartridge through the ejection port of a semi-auto rifle eliminates the possibility of the cartridge being damaged when it is automatically fed from the magazine.

    The projectile of a cartridge fed from a magazine strikes the feed ramp as it is being pushed out of the top of the magazine and into the chamber by the advancing bolt. This has the potential of damaging or distorting the projectile and thereby adversely affecting its accuracy. The loss in accuracy might be negligible, but might mean all the difference between winning and losing a marksmanship competition when the spreads between the best and worst shots are measured in fractions of an inch.

    Single-loading through the ejection port also allows a longer cartridge that otherwise would not fit into the magazine, to be chambered and fired.
