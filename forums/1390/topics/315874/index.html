  • edwin3060
    29 Apr 08, 17:26

    Most of the new equipment has been 'tailored to local needs', or to drop the euphemisms, they have been downgraded to cut costs. When first fielded, the new LBVs and Field packs were made by Force 21 and they used better materials and had more QC. Now they issue the ones made by D&G, and frankly they are more prone to failure. The waterbags were originally from Source, a reputable Israeli brand, now also made by D&G and leak more often. The SOGs that are issued have fewer tools than the original version-- IIRC Sheares supplies both types, but the full version that I bought costs SGD 126 while the issued version costs 74 credits, which theoretically equals to SGD 74.

    Some of the cost-cutting makes sense, but some don't-- for example, the rate of water bag failure/leakage is ridiculous. The Source water bag that I was issued at OCS is still going strong 2 years later, but D&G water bags hardly last 1-2 months. I'd recommend everybody to go to a sports store or beach road and get a Source or Camelbak water bag instead-- you may have to spend some money, but at least you won't suddenly lose your water and potentially get  your No.4 and everything in your field pack wet :(
