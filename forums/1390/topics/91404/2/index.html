    The trainees do learn how to set up the various traps to catch small animals and birds. During the survival phase, they are required to set up such traps too. So it isn't just a learn and forget thing.

    Learning how to kill quill is just one of the skills that the instructors impart to the trainees. They will also be taught how to kill other animals such as a rabbit, chicken and sheep... but of course, the latter part do not need to be executed (else the killing would be too much). Therefore, the trainees are just required to know how to and properly execute the killing of a quill.

    I do agree with you that it would have been good if the trainees are told to catch their own quills instead of the birds being given to them. But we must understand that time is also a constraint... Given enough time, everything can be taught properly, but the cadets have only three weeks timespan in Brunei, of which they still need to carry out other trainings.

    Then again, there will be this issue of whether the Brunei authorithy would be happy if they learn that every time a JCC course is held, there will be that number of quills (or other animals) less in their jungle. Therefore, it is the best choice that the SAF provides for this themselves.
