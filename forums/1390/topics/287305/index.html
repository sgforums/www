    I meant prior vocational training. NSF clerk all the way, right?

    Reservist units have an S1 cell as part of their composition but, as far as I know, they're only in charge of ICT in-& out-processing during peacetime. (Plus rotating shifts like duty clerks.) They tend to get called back the longest for each stint but overall it's a good life: their own reservist offices, do mainly paperwork, can sometimes wear vest & slacks or even PT kit.

    I don't think, say, a 35-year-old will feel out of place, so to speak -- assuming in the first place that the SAF is willing to pay out his $3k-$4k civilian wages just to have him back to do clerical work! ~lol~

    In wartime, the S1 cell is supposed to help out in POW management, among other things. But I don't think reservist clerks practice for this much during ICTs.

    My old outfit churned a lot of clerks over the years too, as I recall, but not sure whether it's because they do a shorter cycle.
