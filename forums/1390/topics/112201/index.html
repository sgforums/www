    Originally posted by Moxie:
    - tracing back, it would appear that the SAF 1st sent the mobilization order down the ranks on 28th Dec: the 1st C130 plane flew out that day, the choppers/medical teams/SCDF personnel would fly out the next 2 days (to 3 countries), & the LST fully loaded a day later.
    The sequence of early events can be spelt out more fully:

    Dec 26: news of the earthquake and the tsunami broke, the SAF and SCDF were put on alert to deploy the usual sort of disaster relief teams (SAF medical teams and SCDF DARTs--typically 25 strong each; and the C-130s to bring them there).

    Late Dec 26, early DEc 27: the enormity of the disaster became known, the SAF put more of its forces on standby. The RSAF had its C-130s, Fokker 50s, and helicopters flying within 48 hours of being activated to ferry relief supplies.

    Dec 28: SAF decided to send a LST, personnel worked 24 hrs/day to get the ship prepared from cold start.

    Dec 29: 2 DART teams and 8 helicopters dispatched to Indonesia and Thailand.

    Dec 31: after 72 hours, RSS Endurance sets off, expecting to be in the disaster area in another 36 to 48 hours to start work.

    Jan 2: RSS Endurance arrives off shore Meulaboh: that's under 120 hours or five days--three to prepare and two at sea--from the decision to deploy to commencement of actual relief work.

    http://singaporerelief.blogspot.com/2005/01/saf-relief-deployment-to-indonesia-and.html
