    Well, I don't know whether to say your batch luckier or mine. But sure as hell I would give anything to be able to book out on a Friday night. It would be pure ecstasy. Alas, no such chance.     If given a choice I would opt for early bookout and more siong rather than late bookout and less siong. Friday bookout makes a lot of difference! Imagine we could still meet up later in town or on Sat with the thought of book-in quite far from our minds.

    I have to disagree with you though on tekan. I think tough training makes for good men and soldiers. Well as long as it is not done overboard. I have seen how men who went through relatively easy training became out of control, unmotivated and ill-disciplined when they turned operational. And believe me it it very difficult to get anything done during training or exercise. And this would mean late to return stores, late to go back bunk to rest and ultimately late to book out. It is very frustrating and painful to go reservist if your platoon mates are like these.

    Don't get me wrong, I am not a siow-on person. I am in a combat unit, so everyone needs to work together to get things done. If not ultimately, we are the ones to suffer. I am lucky that my current platoon mates are a good bunch to work with, making it a joy for me to go back for reservist. We subscribe to the belief that we slack and work at the appropriate time.

    Anyways, I hope you have a much better time during reservist. I have I think about four more cycles to go before hanging up my gortex boots.
