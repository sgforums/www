    Found it. It's called "Boy Meets Tekong: NS Guidebook for Muslims", issued by Perdaus & MUIS in 2000. Try contacting them for a copy.

    The middle half of the booklet is subtitled "Islam for the Ops Ready", preaching a pre-prayer checklist (timetable, $1 compass, water bottle, poncho, etc.), how to perform ablution, how to pray fast (proper technique & focus) & pray "smart" (combination prayers, etc.), plus clarification on fasting during Ramadhan as well as halal/haram food. All within practical context, or army style - so to speak.

    I didn't answer you directly prior cuz I thought maybe the SAF has a new directive but, from the gist of the fairly-recently publication, I don't think things have changed much since my time in the '80s. In other words, the military respects your religious obligations but won't go out of its way to overaccomodate them.

    Instead, the booklet encourages Muslims to be disciplined & faithful (when not enough time, water is scarce, too tired, dirty uniforms/terrain, missed prayers, etc.). At the same time, it advises that the seeking of permission to pray must be tactful, in order not to interrupt training or cause inconvenience to others.

    It's more than Friday prayers. Muslims are also encouraged to do their 5 daily prayers - Subuh (morning), Zohor (noon), 'Asr (afternoon), Maghrib (sunset), 'Isyak (night) - even when in the Army, including out in the field. More often than not, you don't see them performing them unless it's by happenstance. My own Tekong platoon had a pious Malay, but he was very discreet & did his prayers faithfully, but very quickly, during training breaks.
