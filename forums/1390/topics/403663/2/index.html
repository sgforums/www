    NSman diagnosed with acute grief reaction after girlfriend's death


    By Shaffiq Alkhatib | Posted: 24 June 2010 1654 hrs

    SINGAPORE: The boyfriend of a girl who fell to her death from an 8th floor ledge of an HDB block in Bedok was diagnosed with acute grief reaction after the incident.

    22-year-old national serviceman, Low Chen Sheng even had suicidal thoughts and was admitted to Mount Elizabeth Hospital for treatment from June 9 to 12 last year.

    In a coroner's inquiry, the court heard that 17-year old sales assistant Candice Goh Hui Yi died in the incident that took place Block 519, Bedok North Avenue 1 at around 4am on June 7.

    Mr Low lives in one of the units there.

    Ms Goh had a heated quarrel with him just hours before her death.

    This led her family to believe that Mr Low had pushed her off the ledge following a dispute over some 700 dollars which she had withdrawn from their joint bank account.

    Mr Low denied this and said that he did not touch Ms Goh prior to the fatal fall.

    Instead, he was the one who administered cardio-pulmonary resuscitation or CPR on her after she fell.

    When cross-examined by Deputy Public Prosecutor, Ruth Wong, Mr Low said Ms Goh had previously threatened to commit suicide on more than one occasion.

    The court heard that there were occasions when she would sit on the ledge after they quarrelled and Mr Low believed she did that to "scare" him.

    The case has been adjourned to July 5. - CNA/jy
