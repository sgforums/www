    To TS, recourse depends on your unit and the MO, also luck plays a part.

    If your unit lack of manpower, your unit won't send you for recourse even if you up pes.

    Also if you up pes doesn't mean 100% need go recourse, you have to go through a medical FFI for bmt pes C recourse by the MO.

    As I said luck plays a part, some MOs are kind or in a good mood for the day, they will ask you if you want to recourse. If you don't want then they will fail the FFI.

    All in all, if your unit don't want you and die die want you go recourse, they will pressure the MO to pass you.

    After finishing BMT there is a chance not to return to your unit as a clerk, but instead posted to a vocation and new unit. But if your ORD date is nearing, there is a very low chance to recourse, or a new vocation or new unit. Low chance doesn't mean no chance though, as I have friends ORD during their vocational period.

    If your goal is to slack in NS, then try not to even upgrade your pes. If you are at pes C9L2 permanent, then leave it as it is. If it's 6/12(6months) then when the time is up for a PES review, then

    1) Drag time, say busy and no time to go medical centre for FFI

    2) say cause of former injury still affecting you, make appointment with specialist or whatnot, possibly best scenario book apointments that is a far date away e.g. 2-3months, which is normal for govt specialists. Why? refer to point 1.

    By the time, it would already mean ORD and there would be no point.

    Although I have heard recourse bmt are really slack, but that is just a rumour.

    Good luck in your NS quest.
