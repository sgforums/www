    It's okay to enquire about certain vocations; I'm sure the posters here can help you on this. But no need to reinvent the wheel, you know? Plenty of people have gone through what you're desiring (further studies), so be practical & pick up pointers from their examples. Depending on your past academic results, you can:-

    - apply for subsidied education, not just Govt grants but also the self-help groups, etc;
    - apply for scholarships, with or without accompanying bonds;
    - find a sponsor, either personal or organizational, to underwrite you;
    - parlay an internship or job attachment into something permanent;
    - join the industry first & obtain relevant qualifications to complement your working experience (not vice versa);
    - self-support part-time studies (usually night courses or distance learning) for a new career line, & so on.

    Don't be too naive if you've never really worked before, or have no idea what a military organization or the NS experience are like. No point signing your life away if you're not that garang about your career yet, cuz none of the service arms shall treat you like a job-hobber who works just to collect (save) the pay. If you really want to plan that far ahead, do so only when employed & are savvy with your workload & expenses.
