    then if i excuse live range during bmt , what do I do as a storeman ?
    Eh, how come never go for course first? Traditionally, it's only one week's training there, siah.

    Most likely tio company-line storeman, at the unit itself. The department consists of just you & your CQMS (or RQ, at the HQ level), though any future downgraded troops shall likely be permanently attached to you too.

    Not only stay in (unlike your counterparts posted to the supply bases), but kena operational life all the way too (along with the rest of the unit). You'll sleep with them & follow/service their training programme, standbys, etc. though, hierarchy-wise, the QM is offically your OIC.

    Of course, in your case excuse IPPT, SOC & range where applicable, as well from field EXs. Instead, you'll just be preparing the water stations, or sending rations, during these occasions. Very busy life; I won't be surprised if you tio key-personnel status for mob mannings & every ICT (once you enter reservist).

    If they're really enrolling a new intake, then I reckon you've got it casual for the next 6-7 months. (Casual, not idle or switch-off.) The logistics side, even down at the company line, takes on a more behind-the-scenes role as the mono-intakers go through their BMT & then trade course. Your job shall be mainly to deliver the makan/water punctually & to organize regular changing of bedsheets, things like that.

    The downside is zero nights-off during their BMT (must serve night snack, mah) & maybe only limited ones during their course time too (I dunno if they allow book-outs as per the trainees at the formation schools, whenever there's no night training). "Combat" storemen can usually build a rapport with the Hokkien peng during this period, though, vis-a-vis the Tekong ones who are subsequently posted in as commanders & men.

    Once everybody is posted in, the unit shall then train together progressively (COY & then BN EXs) to pass the proficiency test & become operational & be eligible for standbys, etc.
