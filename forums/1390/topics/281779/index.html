    GDSman sounds right, if it's from 1984. Can't really tell if the logo image is depicting a firer wearing camo utilities (eg. tucked out) though the baggier trousers suggest so. He's clearly the old-style steel helmet, though.

    Mass issue of the camo attires only began in 1985, the year I enlisted. In the preceding two years or so, only certain units got to wear them -- non-standardized too, as I understood, as the SAF experimented with different designs. Logically, it must've been the infantry first &, amongst them, the elite troops most of all. The rest of the SAF, still in their tucked-in, starched/ironed Temasek greens, must've then recognized these camo wearers as "special" forces.

    Don't believe? Check the old news photos of the Cable Car Tragedy (1983). I recall, too, browsing through a photo album some retired MAJ donated for the OCS bunk display at the last AOH. It had some early '80s Temburong shots where all the instructors wore "jungle" fatigues & a perm staff in Temasek green. There were also SATO graduation pixs from the period, with different officers from different formations wearing different attire (even different camos).

    Speaking of the latter: Lee Hsien Yang was in it. If I'm correct, he was identified as being from Armour (which I didn't know previously).

    And at that time the camo uniform was of low quality they faded after washing.
    Those faded uniforms were prized among servicemen. ~lol~ More so the next-generation camo pattern, which had a brighter shade than the version which the SAF settled on later. Everybody tried their best to hang onto both sets, for their "veteran" look. Eventually the faded ones was kit-exchanged out at the Enciks' insistence -- the colour became more gray than green, as the picture shows -- & they even caused MPs to take any still-stubborn wearers' names down at the end (outside Amoy Quee), as I recall.

    But the other design remained popular throughout the reservist days.
