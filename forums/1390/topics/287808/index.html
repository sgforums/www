    All military vehicles can go past 50km/hr, the max speed for a rover i'm not sure but a 5Ton can go up to 110km/hr if i didn't remember wrong.

    Rover and most of the old manual vehicles speed is controlled by the SWD which will beep whenever the driver goes above the speed limit and if the counter jumps, the driver will get into trouble cause its a sign he speed for a considerable distance. Some drivers who like want to speed sometimes will disconnect the SWD but once discovered i think its going to be alot of extra if not even charge.

    5 Ton speed limit is program into the ECU so its harder for drivers to just speed with the 5 Ton, there are ways of cause which i know but thats something confidential and the last thing we need is any drivers knowing the way to do it...

    Speed limit of 50 is set by SAF i would say more for protection of the drivers and other road users as many SAF drivers are not very experience and going slow gives them more time to react. In war all such devices and programs to control vehicle speed can be easily disabled to let the vehicles reach their respective max speed.
