    OC = Officer in Commanding, normally responsible for a Company.

    CO = Commanding Officer, normally responsible for a Battalion/Regiment, Command School or Training School. May sometimes be known as a Comd instead of CO

    Comd = Used for a training facility, Brigade or Division

    Hope this answers your question, titles and names may differ for each service or country.

    Master, besides Commandant SCSC and CCOs, can't think of any other times this term is used.     In a Formation, Section Heads are considered to be on par with OCs and Branch Heads are on par with COs.Thus, OCs can be CPTs or MAJs and COs can be any rank from MAJ and above.There are LTA(NS) who are OCs too and CPT(NS) who are COs.It's confusing but just remember that an OC traditionally is a Company Commander and a CO is a Battalion Commander in the most common intepretation.

    Commander is usually reserved for Formation/Division Chiefs, e.g 3rd Div Commander , CGO(Chief Guards Officer), CMEO(Chief Maintenance and Engineering Officer).

    Camp Commandant is usually used to describe the person in charge of camp logistics and maintanence.He can be of any rank, most common would be MAJ and above usually retired too.Some are DXOs.The only exception is Commandant SAFTI, where the officer is the Chief Of SAFTI MI, usually a BG.
