    1)What shd i expect for NS in SCDF?
    Lotsa training, with more likely to get activated & deal with real-life crisis situations during NSF (compared to the SAF).

    2)Why am i posted to SCDF? Is it randomly chosen ? (btw i m Pes A)
    I suppose so. Put it this way: direct enlistment to the SCDF, similarly to frontline SAF units, can be generally grouped under the "mono-intake" system (which takes in only enlistees of combat-fit or -equivalent fitness). Tekong & BMTC isn't the be-all for NS.

    3)I heard tt ppl who are posted to SCDF mostly are ppl who are blacklisted by the govt. (eg, ppl hu cun be trusted to hold a gun or a rifle) ?
    No blacklisting. They do screen familial backgrounds of some enlistees, but the Government needn't suspect you if you're at least a 2nd-generation Singapore citizen & has gone through the national education system.

    4)Is there ani chance of being a SCDF senior NCO during NS? (coz i read frm sumwhere tt most of the ppl hu hold impt appointment are SCDF regulars)
    Only junior officers & NCOs for NSF aspirants, I'm afraid - how to get promoted so quickly inside just 2 years? You can advance higher during reservist, usually as KAHs (Key-Appointment Holders, same as for the SAF) & the bulk of SCDF's total strength lies in the reserves (like for Army).

    btw is it true tt after mi trainin when i m posted to mi unit, i can go home everidae after mi shift hr?
    Depends on where you're posted. Unlike other countries, our SCDF version is paramilitary; it's part of Total Defence. So it's not just firemen & paramedics; the SCDF also has DART specialists, Hazmart (Hazardous Materials) experts, rescue COYs & BNs, construction BGE, training schools, supply & maintainence bases, all of them with relevant command structures. Active strength is around 5k but total strength (with reservists & volunteers) number in the tens of thousands!
