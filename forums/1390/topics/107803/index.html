  • Moxie
    18 Dec 04, 20:26
    i heard there's a 9days outfield or something..
    Quite normal, lah. Local ICTs can spend 11-12 days out in the field too, compared to the longest (5 days) I've spend when active, excluding Ex Lancer.

    When overseas, the main logistic obstacle is that the deployment grounds are very far away from base camp, & thus it's quite impractical to systematically organize details to go back to bathe during the midst of training.

    Usually the RQ & CQ will plan for regular changing of uniforms (every 3 days or so) as an alternative, while commanders also have the prerogative to arrange for ad-hoc showering with nearby farms & such.

    I had a 4 days R & R in ROC following some trainning. I had just finished covering 2 groups of trainees as an MO. Definitely worth looking forward too after being in the field for about 30days! Of course there was the run of the mill guided tours but it was really until the nights that were really enjoyable. I remembered bring my medics to the night markets and later to pubs and nightclubs. Of course there was suppose to have the 2359(i think) curfew but well.......... you know.

    Anyway the most "exciting" occured when 6medics and 2 signalers had followed me to a pub in Taipei. It was "late" so officially we were suppose to be back in the hotel. Anyway I remembered there was this table of 3 local men who were getting very "pissed". In fact they were harrassing the waitresses there and getting fairly rowdy. Sensing potential trouble I told the guys that we should settle the bill and head off to some other pub. It was then that some other waitress tried to convince us that there was no problem and that we should stay. Unfortunately those 3 guys on the next table overhead the incident. They started shouting at us saying that we were unreasonable to leave because them. Well I asked all the guys to just stand and leave anyway. After most of us had left the premises. I noticed one of my medics still being harrassed by the waitress. I remembered promptly going back to get him out. This was when all hell broke loose! My medic recieved one on his cheek and I got one blow to my face! Trying to avoid further confrontation, the 2 of us backed off. Unfortunately those idiots actually continued to chase the 2 of us. For some reason, I managed to run to the other guys but my medic could not. In fact he was cornered and being shoved around. This is when I kinda thought enough is enough. I proceeded to order the rest of the men to go and aid our guy. So 9 guys in total against 3...... you kinda can make out the rest. Anyway eventually the 3 Taiwanese fled when they knew they were grossly outnumbered. The incident created quite a scene and even the local police showed up Fortunately the incident had passed and we just pretended nothing had happened and walked.

    I remembered after thinking what drama from a potentially enjoyable night. The next day I mentioned the incident to the other officers and WOs. Of course they started teasing me and even asked me why I had not called them! They were all testosterone charged and said they would have all showed up with their "parangs" and stuff Oh well........

    dunno abt u guys, I remember our first exercise in lancer, it was a section TOPO and I was 84 so with the company HQ section.

    so anyway we were asleep on a very steep knoll somewhere in the jungle and into the second day of the TOPO exercise. Our company 2IC who was the commanding officer for our section as well a real TOPO KING was getting worried that he couldn't find his way, we searched & searched for it the whole of the previous day but couldn't even find our first checkpoint while other teams were already starting to search for their second or last checkpoint, we could all see that it was starting to be a real embarassment for him. So once it was daylight on day 2 he roused us up from our sleep to have a quick breakfast and set off asap.

    After breakfast he looked around and found me to be the most convienient to ask so delegated me and another of his runner to collect all the water bottles then go down the hill to fillup all the water bottles for everyone. I just had my powder bath and was feeling very cool and non-sticky was abt to settle down for a smoke and this prick was asking me to run down the hill to get water. Why couldn't we all go down and get the water since we would be going down anyway I argued but he glared at me and insisted.

    So being a chao lance corporal and he an officer, no choice we collected all the water bottles into a huge black trashbag and down the hill sweating again we went.

    At the foot of the hill was a winding river bed at least during monsoon and rainy season it was but otherwise it was a dirt road. We found a small pond of trapped clear water abt the size of 3 bath tubs so we started topping up our own bottles first.

    Then my friend who was the runner suggested we take a bath since it was so hot. So I agreed and took off everything, there we were 2 naked guys swimming in the middle of nowhere, if a wild sunbear suddenly appeared we going to be his lunch.

    anyway after a short bath my friend said he had to take a sh!t and so he did, after he sh!tted he went back to the pond to wash his ass again ... then only we started filling up all the other water bottles.

    I remember after exercise cut and while we all stayed back on the base waiting for the sand barge to arrive to be shipped out, we did all sorts of things to busy ourselves as it was really boring.

    We broke open the C-rations and starting using solid fuel to cook instant noodles, you could see small cooking fires with clusters of people huddled around outside their company barracks cooking.

    also there were people catching multicolored cicada and there was even a small auction for it, I think the highest offered for a turquoise colored one was $450 bucks.

    Then someone found a scorpions nest and people started catching them first with rifle cleaning kit pincers after that 1 guys used his bare fingers and everybody followed and posing for photo shoot, I think back now .. really fool hardy.

    But the most interesting of all was using the radio. The telecommunications on the base was that of radio airwave. We would fiddle with our small radio dials to tune to the correct frequency to evesdrop on base personel making calls home. Sometimes you could hear really mushy conversations going between a man calling home to his wife.

    It was fun.
