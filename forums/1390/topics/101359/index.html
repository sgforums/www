    My guesses:

    No more re-vocational training, if you've already been formally trained in your present one.

    Same unit? Depends on needs, I guess. Don't see why your current outfit shouldn't get 1st shot at retaining your services. It'll help if your Signal Officer or Specialist fight for you.

    Yes, you'll do reservist. The best lobang you can hope for is to be out onto a holding list - i.e. no unit - & maybe they'll forget you. Much depends on the timing of your ORD: if it's together with fellow combat-unit personnel, you'll likely join their reservist roster. If it's an earlier date or if you're with a higher-up outfit (or a training school), then it's a matter of demand & supply. Could end up a newbie joining a lao chiao unit in the middle of their cycle, or a long-forgotten lao peng suddenly posted to a newly-ORD'd unit years from now.

    As for reservist duty, you and I PES C9L2 people are in some sort of a grey area.
    I dunno about nowadays but there was no grey area with the C9L2 personnel in my reservist arty unit. They inprocessed along with the main body, then was basically confined to camp when the unit moved out to the field. Most of them just stayed in the bunks, reporting sporadically to a rear-echelon NCO or the BN office to show that they haven't sneaked elsewhere.

    Very switched-off life for them during ICTs, though I've heard 2nd-hand that the cooks also moved out sometimes (for a field-kitchen delpoyment) & the S1 cell too (for a field-administrative tent). Even the armourers got bored & volunteered to travel with the MT-line repair vehicle ....

    I understand that some of the vocational tasks have been outsourced to the civilian side now, but not all. As long as your skills are still operationally useful & needed, they'll call you back - even if you end up as "lobo" 80% of the time.
