    I must admit there is this myth around RSMs, which encourages other ranks to fear them. But they can be human too. Also, life is such that it is a small world and things can turn out strange. Let me tell you my story...

    I was a NSF platoon sergeant. As PS, you get to do BOS duty in camp. During one of the duty days, I was reported by the Camp DO for something (can't remember what). Instead of taking action against me, he decided to inform the RSM to deal with me, which I guess is a smart thing, since all the other ranks fear the RSM more than a regular captain (sometimes !).

    So I was asked to see the RSM the day after the duty day. It was the first time face to face with him, and he had the stern kind of no-nonsense look. Anyway, cut a long story short, I was given 3 extra BOS duties ! Which I took gladly, in order to be out of his office, and escape his stare and his scolding, as I was a rather green 19 year old PS !!!

    Story does not end here.... I finished my NS and went off overseas to study. Got my degree and got a job. First day at work , I passed the security guard at our company's main gate. Guess who was the company security supervisor??? ! It was my RSM !

    Anyway, cut another long story short, we became quite good lunch friends. I did remind him during a chit-chat session, that he gave me 3 extras before, but he conveniently forgot who I was ! In recent years, when I bump into him (I am not at the same site anymore), I noticed he had started to address me as "sir " ! Strange, but I guess it is because I have reached the senior mgmt. ranks of the company.

    SMALL WORLD !
