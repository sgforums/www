    almost all psychiatric cases (or p-case in short) will be given within PES C to E only, depending on light to mild to moderate ones. the severe ones even get PES F, which is medically discharge from any NS duty, meaning no need to serve...

    they will NOT even give you a rifle and 5 live bullet rounds for guard duty to prowl the camp area, they will give you a stick baton and a rattan shield for self-defense, so it's much less than having to risk a risk of giving you some 30 live bullet rounds for live rifle range since you have had attempted to commit suicide. because later they all scare the slight possibility of you may relapse at any time to point, aim and shoot people at the live range.

    Problems you need to overcome

    1) How SAF view ur prison sentence. With the problems in the past few years, soldier misbehaving, with the recent "soldier steal car for joy ride", I am not quite optimistic of ur chances.

    2) How SAF grade ur medical assessment. Depends on ur asthma problem, if ur asthma will act up periodically even without medication, chances are still low. Remember, u are aiming for combat vocations, so u dun expect ur platoon to stop just because ur asthma acts up, the army cannot function this way.

    3) How SAF will consider ur mental health. SAF have its own psychiatric department,  you will still need to get thru them as well, these are medical officers (doctors) who ususally also work in IMH, so dun get surprised if u see a IMH doctor u know, now wears a uniform and u have to salute him.

    Lastly, if u decide to hide all this information from them, just be prepared to get back fired and you end up in Detention Barracks (SAF's prison, yes, SAF have its own prison) for lying and withholding information. BTW, if u commit an offence as a soldier u will need to face two court (Civilian and Military), so if u cannot adequately control what you do during ur depression, I strongly suggest against joining SAF, u will suffer even more.
