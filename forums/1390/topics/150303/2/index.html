    Been sometime since I pass out, didn't realise that Ex Long Walk has been chopped up so much. During my time in 90s, my team started in Mandai, walk around there and Sembawang, pass by SLE (under construction at that time), Woodlands, Marsiling and Kranji area, walk alongside railway line and cross Kranji Dam to Neo Tew, Lim Chu Kang. The average time taken for each team to complete was 24 hrs. My team came in at 27 hours. Started on Friday morning, finished on Saturday afternoon. Based on an average of 4km/hr, the total distance covered was somewhere between 90 to 100 km. While it isn't a route march, it is still a considerable distance to be covered. Chaffed thighs and big blisters on foot was the norm at midpoint and end point of Long Walk. At times, we even have to fast march. This is because we were walking on the main axis as we did not want to take the big detour.

    Wonder if the recce trooper course still go overseas? My coy went to Thailand for 3 weeks for the final phrase. As usual navex are plenty. My team pushed off at about 1100 hrs at 40 degrees, came back to Sai Yok Camp the next day at about 1400 hrs. So all in all, we did the 90+ km navex twice.

    Anyway, kudos to all those who did all sort of navex and route marches (whatever distance).
