The NS social survivalist question thread

  • Puutan
    22 Mar 06, 18:42
    Serving in the army is something almost every singaporean male has to go through, and the training is, although toned down by many degrees to appease our small island community after many unfortunate military related events, is still demanding upon the recruits physically and mentally.

    But i believe that there is another hazard in National Service. The social environment.

    As a pre-enlistee, i have heard of cases where recruits, NSFs and regulars who fail to adapt wisely to their surroundings, and ending up either performing push-ups, staying back in the detention barracks on the weekends, being discriminated against and labelled the black sheep of the platoon...... etc.....

    So, what social guidelines can we follow to prevent ourselves from having suffer the consequences of irresponsible behavior in the army ?

    I only know of a few advices: Don't be an "Indian Chief", Do whatever you want to do, but don't get caught, Don't trust anybody, Don't volunteer for guard duty and don't be enthusiastic about it..........

    My purpose for this thread is to collect many guidelines to form a library of prudent rules for those people serving in the army, who are going to serve their national service and hopefully do a huge favor for those soon going into the army or are in the army to lower the frequency of kenna screwed or punished. Thanks for your contributions and your time taken to list down these guidelines.[/b]
