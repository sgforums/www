  • blitzonic
    15 Aug 07, 21:30
    Actually, there's no need to confuse him further. NS is NS, aka consciption or active-duty. Reservist is reservist, but in our context called NSmen cuz they're ops-ready & not reserve-duty. Reservist is not "part time NS."

    If the TS is asking about reservist pay, well, the SAF has a system called MUP (make-up pay) claims. All one has to do is file for compensation of his lost wages (monthly pay divided by number of days called back). Fraudulent claims are chargeable & always checkable (eg. CPF & income-tax records). If you're studying or unemployed & didn't file a MUP, then the SAF will pay you only the equivalent service pay.
