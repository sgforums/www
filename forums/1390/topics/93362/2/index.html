  • Moxie
    12 Sep 04, 02:06
    Gordonator is correct.

    The 1st phase of promotion comes via a leadership course:
    - pass out from OCS (9 month course) = 2LT.
    - pass out from SISPEC (3 month course) = 3SG.

    The Other Rank's get "promoted"... from REC to PTE, siah.

    Other Ranks can then advance to PFC & LCP with good performances. If they're serving 2.5 years, they will climb to CPL. It's step by step; you don't get double-promoted unless it's for an extraordinary/heroic act.

    2LT's become full LTA's just before ORD. Not sure about NCOs - I belong to the old rank system - but it's logical to think certain 3SG's will become 2SG's if they're a KAH (Key Appointment Holder).

    In reservist, it's all based on combat appointments, esp. KAHs. Accordingly, such-empowered LTA's are promoted to CPT's, & 3SG/2SG's advance to as high as 1SG. MAJ's & SSG/MSG's must attend SCSC & SAFWOS courses respectively before becoming reservist CO's & RSM's.

    As for "White Horse" labelling, it's mentioned in Parliament that the system was discontinued in 2000. In any case, it has nothing to do with promotions. Supposedly, it's only for medical classification & combat/non-combat vocation assignment. Briefly, a tagged White Horse enlistee must be personally certified by the top medical CO before he could be graded PES C or delegated a non-combat position. MINDEF acknowledged, however, that the policy could've been corrupted in actual practice by certain "bootlickers".
