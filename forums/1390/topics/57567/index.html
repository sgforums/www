    The Army Medical Services
    The Medical Arm of the Army
    HQ AMS is the SSSO HQ to the Army on medical matters both in terms of scope and extent of the functions performed. The roles and functions are :

    a) Medical Specialist Staff advisor to Chief of Army on all medical matters.

    b) Command and control of the Combat Surgical Hospitals.

    c) Command Soldiers Performance Centre and define its research policies.

    d) Supervise all Army medical centres.

    e) Supervise the operational readiness of all Army Medical units.

    f) Develop medical support doctrine and procedures for the Army and supervise their implementation.

    g) Develop training standards, systems, and syllabi and ensure the professional training of all Army Medical units and vocationalists.

    h) Develop medical support plans in conjunction with Army operational plans, approve medical plans of subordinate units, and ensure the execution of Army medical plans. Responsible for all health matters in the Army.

    The History of Army Medical Services

    The Early Years
    The seed for the formation of SAF Field Hospital was sown in the late 1960s when MINDEF felt the need to establish a mobile hospital between the forward Medical Coys and the civilian hospitals. The objective was to provide resuscitative surgery to casualties so that they could last the journey to the civilian hospitals. As the SAF Medical Services was relatively young in 1960, Singapore Volunteer Medical Officers of the Singapore Medical Association were recruited into the SAF to assist SAF Medical Services in the formation of the First Field Hospital.

    Army Medical Services Motto
    - Life First
    The motto emblazoned on the scroll proclaims "LIFE FIRST". This is the essence of the arduous duty and challenge that has confronted medical forces labouring on the battlefields of war & peace throughout history. Remaining steadfast to this mission will mean the alleviation of pain and suffering, and the safeguarding of the Army's vital fighting strength.

    Army Medical Services Insignia

    The Snake
    The Army Medical Services logo is made up of a snake entwined around a sword, the latter resting upon a motto-bearing scroll.

    The snake, or serpent, has traditionally been associated with the art and science of medicine. Its origin can be traced to Ancient Greece where temples devoted to worshipping the god of medicine, Aesealapins, abounded. These temples served also as hospitals and in each was to be found a circular pit containing a snake. Retrieved by coiling the serpent from around a wooden rod, it was believed that applying the snake's forked tongue to the diseased body brought about healing.

    The Insignia
    The sword that is central to the logo replaces the rod and is the recognised symbol of the Army. It is raised in righteousness and strength, unsheathed in readiness to do battle. The entwining of the snake evokes the intimate bond between the Army's Medical Services and its fighting forces.

    The Colours
    The colours used remain true to those of the SAF's earliest Medical Services logo, namely maroon and gold. The color of the scroll hearkens to the flag of our predecessors, the British Royal Army Medical Corps, whereupon the colours of dull cherry, blue and old gold are to be found
