    Mod's Note : Weapon safety is an important issue in every Army, and the SAF is no exception.

    More importantly, BMT is the first place that many recruits come into contact with live weapons, at the age of 18-20 generally.

    Please feel free to share your tips on weapon safety.
    --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    I've been noticing a lot of chatter related to M16 parts.

    Here's what I'd like to advise you guys. Whatever parts you may get at Beach Road, is not range-certified nor may it stand up to safe usage in your exercises.

    If you lose your pins, go to the armskote specialist and work something out with them. Normally they are quite understanding. It is BMT, and your instructors will understand if these things happen, of course they will yell and curse and throw you extras.

    But do understand, a rifle is a lethal weapon, and a rifle requires working, rated parts to function safely.

    This is a safety issue affecting not just you, but for your fellow mates as well. The parts in your rifles have been tested for metal fatigue and structural rigidity before being certified for live-firing usage.

    I don't really want to see a thread about dead recruits and exploding rifles here.

    Thanks.

    There's something which I left out.

    Which is the cleaning of the Riffle.

    Anybody who's riffle isn't clean, when it comes to Ammo Inspection, anybody's Riffle found to be in unpleasant condition (dirty) might face disciplinary actions.

    From what I heard before I book out, people who don't clean their riffles properly will face Confinement.

    Don't think that while during usual cleaning riffle session, we can avoid letting the sergeants check our riffles by cleaning it slowly and waiting for the time to return back arms. (You can only do this when there's no Ammo Inspection)

    Should there be Ammo Inspection, those who didn't clean their riffles properly, better make sure it's being clean or else confinement. Thus cleaning of riffle/bayonet/other weapons which are issued upon must be taken seriously.

    During field-camp or Situational Test or any other trips which requires the weapon to be with you: You have got to prepare oil (recommended) bottle with you to oil the weapon exterior to prevent it from rusting.

    Simple steps like oiling is a good practice. (That is if you don't want to waste your time at the end of the trip to de-rust the weapon which is very time-consuming.)

    11 Nov 07, 14:22
    This is one issue I hope all BMTC instructors will take note.

    What you call "elephants in the barrel", are likely to be pitted spots and eroded rifling spots. Further "scraping" by use of the "christmas tree" will only serve to erode the rifling of the barrel and potentially render the rifle unfit for use.

    Armskote specs please do not leave your oil containers in the rain. Your CLP will be as good as useless.

    As for everyone out in the field, please bring two bottles of weapon oil and oil your M16S1/AR-15 rifle's exposed metal parts like your gas port cover, barrel, sight tip frame (Not the white illum sight) and the area around your carrying handle.
