  • vIn.Warrior
    26 Nov 05, 19:07
    1) Apply THICK layer of kiwi black on the surface
    2) Using a cloth or cotton, spread the THICK layer (in rotation) evenly but don't shine it.
    3) Use lighter to heat the layer of kiwi.
    4) You will realise the heated kiwi will melt and will SHINE.
    5) After the melted kiwi shined, it cools down and becomes very blur
    6) By using a new part of the cloth/cotton now, dip it in water (squeeze dry) and gently take SMALL portions of kiwi.
    7) Now, using rotation manner, polish the boots and results will be SHINY.
    8 ) To further enhance its quality, repeat steps 6-7 by adding SMALL portions of kiwi on the cloth/cotton again and polish until boots reaches its maximum SHINY "stage".

    That's how we used to do it with the Number 1 boots, although I understand they've got the shiny vinyl type these days.

    In any case, at the end of stage 2, let the Kiwi dry first. Applying a number of thin layers over each other and letting each layer dry before applying the next works better than using a big swag of the stuff.

    Also, if you run the flame over the Kiwi just long enough to melt it, it won't damage the leather. Also, keeping the flame on one spot too long makes the melted Kiwi run in streaks, and you get a lot of clumping. Zippo lighters burn at a lower temperature than the butane sort and work better.

    As for the shining part, you get even better results with cotton swabs (no Kiwi) under a slowly running tap.
