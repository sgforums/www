    If it's less than one year overseas for work, you can only apply for deferment. (The system may require you to do a makup ICT if there's a vacancy & leftover workyear time available, take note.)

    If you've already received a SAF 100 & it clashes with your stint, then apply to defer accordingly through your unit. Upon approval, you'll receive the requisite exit permit & thereafter ensure that your passport reflects your absence from local shores.

    If you haven't receive a SAF 100, then apply for the exit permit as per normal (3 months or more away) online or by post/fax. In turn, your unit shall be informed & won't call you up until it receives evidence you've returned home. You can renew or extend your exit permit from overseas.

    Sorry, but you're liable for IPPT now. No more lobang if you can't take it during an ICT. Now the initiative is on yourself to book a session within the one-year window prior to your birthday. If you fail or default the test, then you're required to book a RT slot too, although your unit shall often be the one to do the latter for you. Non-compliance is a chargeable offence, so try to downgrade or stay overseas if you wanna siam IPPT totally. Good luck!
