  • KoolKool
    14 Feb 07, 10:32
    Be positive ......... there are cons.

    Age 18 - with reasonable good A levels, get a Local Training Award (no need for President Scholarship).
    Sign-on for officer corps - sure get into OCS. When signing on, you choose between specialists and officers, so if you choose officer, you must get into OCS.
    Age 19 - enter University (NUS) after OCS. 2LT (then LTA) pay while studying for 4 years.
    Age 23 - Platoon Commander appointment for 1-2 years
    Age 24 - one staff tour in HQ
    Age 26 - OC for 2-3 years (CPT- MAJ)
    Age 28 - Staff appointment in HQ

    If you start your part-time MBA programme at age 24, you can complete it by age 28, assuming that you are kept very busy sometimes. As early as you start your MBA programme, indicate that you are leaving the SAF to your superior and your load will decrease. But do your part, be it the minimum.

    You can leave the SAF below the age of 30 armed with an MBA. Your peers will be slogging in the civilian sector all these times, and probably cannot make MBA without a lot more sacrifice than you.

    In terms of work experience, you may lose out a little bit. But you know what ? Most SAF officers are able to do well in studies because there is no financial burden ...... full salary while studying.

    This route is meant for those who wish to be financially independant, or whose family will be stressed to finance their children through University.

    And not to worry too much about the next employment. In the past, there are fewer SAF officers in the civilian sectors, and most people go through NS without making it to Officership, and lack the understanding what these officers are capable of. Now, there are more and more ex-SAF officers who will be willing to employ another ex-SAF officer because they appreciate the values and attitude.
