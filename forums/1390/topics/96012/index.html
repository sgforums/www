    Hey, we're from the same "family". FYI My batch created/voted the Deadly Accurate motto for 21SA back in '86-87.

    Signal Storeman is basically like a 7-11 job for signal equipment. The duties are pretty routine/mundane: T-loaning in/out, maintainence, sometimes helping to set up the sets for HQ Land Rovers (if not enough Signallers). It's rear-echelon but, being very much of a support-service niche, it'll often mean waking up at an unearthly hour or complying with last-minute requests or staying back on weekends - all in order to facilitate the battery-line personnel.

    There should be at least 2-3 of you, from my former experience anyway, assisted by a Signal Sergeant/Specialist & a Signal Officer. All of you will come under the S4 department in HQ Battery.

    I understand, with some outfits nowadays, the HQ Signal people may also be in charge of the unit computer system, especially as troubleshooters.

    Perhaps the other posters were referring to something else, but my own experience (both active & reservist) suggested that the signal store & MT-line office are the most "happening" areas for rear-echelon people. By comparison, it's hard to locate the optical storeman or kit-exchange guy or maps-room rep - none of whom ever updated their movement charts regularly.

    MT line personnel must work in vest-&-slacks, at least. The signal storemen I've encountered seemed always in PT kit & could escape things like muster parades, maybe due to the autonomy accorded by the signal officers (usually quite lobo too, & ended up "contributing" elsewhere as regular DO &/or "DY QM"). Perhaps they've already resigned to booking out less frequently & also often later than the other guys, cuz the premises was often turned into a mini-recreation room on the side. Hi-fi radio, magazines, portable fans, snacks, chess sets, etc. were all stocked & replenished ....
