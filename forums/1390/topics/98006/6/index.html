    Originally posted by iveco:
    Just curious, what route does an ADA specialist take? What happens to them after BSLC?
    haha, i guess i'm the correct person to ans this, since i'm a ada spec....

    anyway, after your bslc, u will be posted to air force school, in short AFS. This is where you will spent another 9 weeks of training on your specialised weapon, it depend on the intake as well. If you are a regular, aim for the I-hawk, better career ...but i don't think u can choose that.

    so after 9 weeks of specialised weapon training (i-hawk, 35mm AA, Mistral, RBS), the regulars will embarked another OJT technical training where they are being trained by the senior techs. Once you pass the course, then u will be posted as a detachment specialist (section leader) and usually, the best performance will be chosen as Flight Sergeant Major.

    if you are a NSF, then after the 9 weeks of training, you will also be taught some light OJT training too. then service your weapon on a weekly basis and play some war game (wait and shoot game) and till u ORD loh

    Originally posted by Gordonator:
    check out the chevron design on the parade square at the new SISPEC, signifying the 3SG rank. cool.
    SISPEC has two squares. A proper Parade Square with seating for spectators and what they call Chevrons Square, which is the square with the chevron design. Chevrons Square is a smaller parade ground in front of the HQ block.

    Personally I think it's a shame they didn't make the chevron design more prominent at Chevrons Square. The square is tiled using the common red concrete tiles seen at parade squares and public parks all over Singapore. The chevron design is simply created by using common blue-gray concrete tiles... because of this most don't even notice the design.
