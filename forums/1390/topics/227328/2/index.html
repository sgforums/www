    just my reflections after 2 weeks.

    Wa lao... 2 weeks only and u give it a death sentence! You judge too quickly. SAF does have it's merit. Go talk to those who have gone through SAF and you may realise that those who go through positively will gain from it and these ppl often do also look forward to the reservist period. Go through it negatively and u will just waste 2.5 yrs of your life!

    As u grow up, u will realise that life is harsh and not a bed of roses. Life is also not about individualism but is mainly about group and community. There is always the contradiction that what u want may not be what the group wants and it is usually the leader who dictates the direction and goals. The position of a follower and that of a leader is also very different. If you want the army to be what u think should be, then u must be a leader (a small one at least) in the organisation and u got to prove yourself to be one with qualities that the organisation wants. If you do not aspire to be a leader in that organisation, you will have to FIT IN as a follower. You can be a good follower or a bad follower. A bad follower in army gets punished often and he will not be a happy one. This is in general is the principal with which I believe is how most organisations function.

    Of course the values and goals with which in organisation looks for can be vastly different because these are what that will bring it to meet it's goals better. U are intelligent and surely know what are the goals and values in the army. The fear and regimentation is not senseless and has a strong purpose of instilling strong discipline. The pain is necessary for training of great endurance. And pride will come when there is a achievement together.

    In any case, I am sure u will go through and perhaps may even be the officer u want to be because I think u already got 1 trait of a leader and that's the ability to analyse. But that is just one trait and with which u have to further use in analysing the situation during training. Of course, there are also several more impt ones that determines whether u can be a officer.
