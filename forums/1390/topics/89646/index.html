    I must say my experience of combat medics have been mixed. My camp had one who fainted and was himself casualtied as he hit his head on the floor, when a recruit accidentally shot 2 instructors with one M-16 round (this story the next time). The instructors were not dead but were shot in the leg and hand respectively. Minor injuries to a trained nurse in a hospital A&E ward.

    The funny thing tho' is that those whose civilian life or education background involved something to do with their hands (e.g. car mechanic, cook) are actually very good medics in the field. Somehow they can think straight and work fast and accurate with their hands ! And can work under stress.

    One of my friends, who was a infantry section cmdr, got converted for reservist, to become a medic sergeant/ senior specialist, and was transfered to a medical battalion (not exactly combat medic in the platoon). He told me that they are definitely nervous ... human nature. They spend some time as part of medic training in the A&E ward of hospitals to get exposure to injuries and death. But still they are nervous when treating a real casualty. THis is normal. There are "kabra-kings" everywhere, even in the combat vocations. Who can say that they will not be frightened under fire???
