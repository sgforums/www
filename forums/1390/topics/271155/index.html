    The only quite effective tactic I can think of during NS time were dental appointments. Particularly those with teeth broken (or worn down) enough to regularly kena a loose filling or crown. My CSM was quite strict with anyone reporting sick during IPPT or SOC day, etc., but usually gave benefit of the doubt the rest of the time. (Those with appointments must travel to another army camp, so he really couldn't do much.) The chao kengers were also wise not to raise his suspicions. They got to disappear for half a day during these occasions!

    For reservist, "easy": Hypertension. Don't even have to be the real deal. Just don't sleep the night before, or go work in a really stressful job. The MO can only take your blood pressure, perhaps ask about your background, before writing out an excuse chit. (Your own civilian doctor is supposed to monitor & treat you long-term.) I should stress that this won't work in active-time, when there'll be no rotating MOs to pass the buck around, nor frequent FFIs & medical checks for consistent follow-ups. Also, note that you're playing with your own health (if you're really ill).
