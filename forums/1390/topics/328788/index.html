    29 Aug 08, 23:17

    I belong to the "old " army period.  Back then, there were rather strict rules on the use of messes by the various ranks. There was the officers mess and the WO and SGTs mess. If you are not of the correct "rank" and you were not specifically invited to go to the other mess, you will be told off.

    There were various reasons for this. Firstly, the mess was where people could hide and relax in the comfort of their own group, without causing embarrassment. It was also the place where the "chief" of the group, e.g. the CO of the battalion, would be able to inform his officers of his private and personal opinion of certain issues. Likewise, for the RSM in the SGTs mess to shed his usual hard stance, and talk to the NCOs about issues.

    I have been to many other foreign countries' mess and there is a certain code of conduct  practised in there. I have been to the US Navy officer's mess and eating there is like eating in a 5 star restaurant and you are supposed to behave accordingly. That is part of the training and discipline and culture. I have been to a British air force officer's mess and this was when you could bring your partner or wife there. And then again, because of the females present, you were supposed to behave accordingly. Again, it is part of the culture and tradition. 

    There were no table soccer or video rooms in those messes. This type of activity were housed in another area and this was open to all ranks. The mess ( officer and NCO) were areas where people could dine in peace, relax and entertain friends.
