    Witness denies being appointed safety officer
    NO SAFETY officer was appointed for the prisoner-of-war training conducted on Aug 21 last year, said Lieutenant Shashi Kumar Nagapah, a commando officer who had helped in the dunking of the trainees.

    Course papers changed: witness

    By Khushwant Singh

    A DISTRICT court heard yesterday that although the course documents listed Lieutenant Yap Kwong Weng as the supervising officer, Captain Pandiaraj Mayandi (pictured), 33, had put his name down in that appointment hours after a trainee died.

    Lt Yap testified that he had been the initial supervising officer of the combat survival training course on Pulau Tekong in which Second Sergeant Hu Enhuai, 19, died after being dunked.

    But on Aug 21 last year, the day of 2nd Sgt Hu`s death, Lt Yap attended a full-day Work Improvement Teams (Wits) session in HDB Hub in Toa Payoh Central and Capt Pandiaraj assumed the role of supervising officer.

    Lt Yap told the district court that he had gone home at 5.30pm after the Wits session and Capt Pandiaraj had called after 7pm to ask him to return to camp.

    While driving to his camp in Changi, Capt Pandiaraj called again to say a trainee had died and new administration instructions had to be printed out. The supervising officer was changed from Lt Yap to Capt Pandiaraj, the safety officer was changed to Lieutenant Divanandhari Ambat Chandrasekharan, and the administration specialist changed to Staff Sgt Tan Tian Huat.

    Asked by Deputy Public Prosecutor Christopher de Souza why the changes were made, Lt Yap said: Because Capt Pandiaraj was supervising officer on the ground that day, and secondly, the supervising officer had the authority to dictate the appointment holders. Lt Yap also told the court that he had helped Warrant Officer S Balakrishnan, 45, the course commander, in preparing the paperwork.

    - Noel Cheah (Chief CDO Officer) & Ho Kian Soon (Chief Instructor, School of CDO Training) stripped of their appointments & redeployed. While not sacked or demoted or lose their pensions, they now have a black mark on their service records. [IMO Bye-bye to National Day awards.]

    - NSFs & NSmen who die in service will now be compensated as regulars: their families will get, on top of the usual compensation, also a lump-sum pension of at least a year's salary of a regular soldier, plus welfare grants, dependents' pension (depending on financial circumstances) & group insurance payouts.

    - increasing Senior Commander visits & audits by Service Inspectorates to ensure training is actually in accordance with lesson plans; surprise checks on safety regulations has reportedly doubled since 2003.

    - medical coverage during training is judged adequate, but training centres will now have resuscitation equipment & personnel have been given extra medical training.

    - looking to engage external safety auditors & benchmark safety standards against other armed forces.

    - "80%" of safety improvements drawn up after last year's 3 training deaths put in place by May 2004, with the Chief of Army challenging his men to have zero fatalities.

    - parts of the machine gun that recently exploded & injured 4 servicemen were put on display at the Army Safety Exhibition, to drive home the safety message about what can happen when procedures are not followed.
