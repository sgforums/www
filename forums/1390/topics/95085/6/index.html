    Commando officer says he tried to stop dunking of trainee By Dominique Loh, Channel NewsAsia

    SINGAPORE : A commando officer tried to stop the dunking of 2nd Sergeant Hu En Huai during the ill-fated Combat Survival Training Course.

    Lieutenant Kong Wai Kheong is the first witness in the trial of four commandos blamed for Sergeant Hu's death.

    He testified that he tried to intervene during the ill-treatment of the trainees.

    Lieutenant Kong could have saved 2nd Sergeant Hu, but he intervened a little too late.

    The commando lieutenant did not see the entire dunking, and only intervened after Sergeant Hu had been dunked a few times.

    Lt Kong said: "The moment Lieutenant Divanandhari pulled the deceased's head out of the water, I immediately told Diva to stop dunking him into the water.

    "I noticed that the deceased was in a bad shape. He reacted differently when he was pulled out of the water. He was extremely weak. He tried to breathe hard and his lips had turned pale. He did not struggle as a normal person."

    Lieutenant Kong, a Platoon Commander at Hendon Camp, had sent seven men from his Strike Reconnaissance Team to attend the combat survival training course.

    He was at Pulau Tekong to observe their progress.

    Although Lt Kong is not an official instructor for the CST course, he was there because his own men were attending the course.

    In his own words, he was there to assist the other instructors from station to station.

    During cross examination, Lt Kong admitted he had helped hold down the legs of some trainees as they were dunked.

    When asked why, he said it was to prevent the trainees from getting injured as they thrashed around. - CNA
