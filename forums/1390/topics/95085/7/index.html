    From the straits times interactive

    TRIAL ON SOLDIER'S DEATH
    Trainee who died was fit for training: Doc

    THE doctor who found Second Sergeant Hu Enhuai fit to go through combat survival training said his assessment would be the same even if he had known the course required trainees to meet the highest fitness standards.

    This is because 2nd Sgt Hu had passed the standard medical examination for soldiers taking part in combat training, said Dr Ong Song Ching.

    The question arose yesterday because 2nd Sgt Hu had been given the lower PES B grade in 2001 - and the course requirements specify that trainees must be PES A.

    The PES, or physical employment standard, status came up in the trial of four commandos charged with rashly causing the death of 2nd Sgt Hu by repeatedly dunking him in a tub of sea water.

    Dr Ong, now with Singapore General Hospital, had examined 2nd Sgt Hu on Aug 6 last year before the soldier went for the course, which started on Aug 13.

    Yesterday, defence lawyer N. K. Rajarh asked Dr Ong if he would have certified 2nd Sgt Hu fit for the course had he known trainees are required to be PES A.

    From a medical point of view, he said, it was irrelevant whether 2nd Sgt Hu was PES A or B as long as he passed the FFI criteria, referring to Fitness For Instruction, a medical check-up procedure in the Singapore Armed Forces (SAF).

    He also disagreed that a doctor must know the 'extent of the physical regime' that trainees undergo in a course before certifying a soldier fit for it.

    'I disagree, because the purpose of the FFI is to exclude any medical condition that would prevent the soldier from participating in SAF combat training in general,' said Dr Ong.

    Knowing the contents of the course would not have altered his assessment, he said.

    The reason for 2nd Sgt Hu's PES B status - a 'less flexible' Achilles tendon in his left ankle - would not have affected his ability to go through the course, he added.

    Another witness who testified was a commando, 2nd Sgt Lui Yong Fook, who was among the trainees hospitalised for breathing in salt water.

    He said he had expected to be 'forcefully pushed down' in water, having seen it in 'the movies, it's normal POW training'.

    The trial continues.

    Singapore : Lieutenant Ng Chin Fong, the first of the 4 commandos on trial to take the stand, has told the court that he can't remember dunking 2nd Sergeant Hu En Huai, the trainee who died and he is very sure that he did not dunk Captain Ho Wan Hou, who is the other survival course trainee who nearly drowned.

    Lieutenant Ng says he could not have been the one who plunged Captain Ho in a tub of water during the Combat Survival course.

    The reason, he argued, is the way he dunked each trainee. He would use one hand to grab the back of the trainee's collar and the other hand to lift the trainee by the back of his waist.

    Captain Ho had testified that the trainer dunking him and had used one hand to hold his mouth and nose open.

    Captain Ho had also told the court that the trainer who held his mouth and nose open wore half gloves with his fingers exposed.

    But Lieutenant Ng says he wore full gloves with his fingers covered.

    As for Sergeant Hu, Lieutenant Ng says he can't remember dunking him, nor can he remember any trainee collapsing after the water treatment.

    When asked about the CST lesson plan, Lieutenant Ng told the court he made several attempts to obtain the document but was unsuccessful and therefore, has never seen it.
