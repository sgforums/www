    Recruits at Basic Military Training School are now each equipped with two pairs of the new full leather combat boots, which are more comfortable, durable and cost-effective than both the old leather boots.

    Introduced in June this year, the full leather combat boots have an ergonomic design similar to the Gore-Tex waterproof hybrid boots. They are made entirely of soft, lightweight leather with a strong, tight fibre-structure to increase durability and improve comfort.

    The new boots are not waterproof as they do not contain the Gore-Tex membrane. This means that that they are close to 30% cheaper than the Gore-Tex waterproof hybrid boots, making their use more cost-effective for Our Army. Nevertheless, for soldiers who are exposed to frequent field training under different weather and terrain, such as those in Infantry, Guards and Commandos, Gore-Tex waterproof hybrid boots are still issued.

    REC Chen Wei Jing said, Â”I have a friend with the old leather boots. The new boots are definitely much softer and more comfortable. My friend gets blisters easily especially when he does running. I have not had any blisters so far with these new boots.Â”
