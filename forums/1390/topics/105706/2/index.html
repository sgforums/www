    There is actually a few generation of combat boots I've seen.

    My elder brother did his NS in the 70s' - his boots had a seperate leather toe cap in the front - like some of the civvy shoes. They had to polish it till it shine. He change those old boots in the 80s', while in the reserves - to the ones with the rough "pimple" like leather - no need to shine anymore - just kiwi.

    I wore those same boots as my elder brother during my NS, until I too, changed them for the lighter smooth leather ones in the mid 90's, while doing my ICTs. These were good pair of boots - lighter, and no need to break them in (season). I wish I had them for SOC during NS. The new soles were easy to clean as well.

    Recently, after 2000, the SAF introduced the gore-tex ones. I've not bought them - don't know how good they really are.
    But I'll stick to my old leather boots. To me - its "proven" in a way - considering all the weight I have to carry.

    And now, I read - to save costs - recruits are going back to leather boots again.
