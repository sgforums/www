    hm.. to get water INTO our boots actually require quite some effort - my boots never got soaked except when there was river crossing or when the swamp was knee deep. yes, once the boots got soaked, it grew so much heavier and nasty...

    the old leather boots were more resilient than the goretex ones, and certainly resistant against conc and barbed wires. they were also better for parades, since they were easier to polish and shine, and sounded better when used for drills. but of course, for runs like SOC and SEOC and normal runs in boots, the goretex would be favourable due to its weight. as for support, again the leather boots did better.

    the purpose of shining one's boots is the same as maintaining one's turnout, and this is part of being disciplined. this is especially emphasised during parades when one's turnout is supposed to be tip-top. of course, the extent of the shine is debatable - some of us have alot of pride in our turnout, and would insist on nothing less than a shine that would reflect sunlight and better still, reflect our faces, while others would only go for the bare minimum required and do a brush shine. in the end, the RSM would define the required standards for the unit.
