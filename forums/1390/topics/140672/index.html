  • Gedanken
    6 Jul 05, 19:51
    Nope, but chances are that you're going to train longer hours, and you're definitely going to book out less than the Tekong boys.

    In my day, lights out was typically 0100-0130, and reville was 0445. Don't know if that's changed. Every day starts with a run between 3 and 5 km long, and that's if you're not being turned out at Changi Beach.

    Basically, the syllabus is standard - it's just that's you'll be trained harder than at Tekong. Not that it matters - if you make sure you don't get injured in training, it's perfectly survivable, and it's been getting easier over the years.

  • quivalen
    7 Jul 05, 00:41
    previously, cdo bmt was conducted by NSF leaders (3SGs) which - combined with the lack of the safety culture brought about by recent incidents - led to training being akin to what Gedanken described. over the years, it gradually got softer though it was still significantly tougher than tekong bmt.

    however there've recently been experiments with using regulars from the CRF as trainers a few intakes ago, and right now bmt is conducted by CTW - i.e. ranger cadres. no need to worry though - by all accounts it's still getting more slack - rather, no tekkan for ridiculous reasons which was still happening as recently as 03, which coincides with the last intake which used NSF trainers. but since then its gotten less and less shiong.

    so i wouldn't worry very much if i were you. the fact that ranger cadres are conducting the training is no cause for alarm, truly. ranger treatment is just a mode they can switch on and off - most of them are really quite nice people.

  • Ponders
    10 Jul 05, 10:27
    Infantry based mono-intake BMT (SIR and GDS) = BMTC BMT

    Same syllabus. Same goes for OCS SAF (W) BMT.

    Only difference, Mono-intake BMT is supervised by fresh OC/PCs with the only experience people the PS and Sect Comds.

    Mono-intake BMT tend to be based on "what we want our men to become" type of standard.

    whereas BMTC BMT is, "just follow the syllabus, meet the minumum and F-Off to your next posting".

  • Fatum
    13 Aug 05, 16:26
    eh, Par si ris camp ? ....

    During my time it was HENDON camp, (year 2000) been away from Singapore a couple of years now, has the camp moved ? ... our trainers were NSFs grabbed from other companies who've turned ops, (cpls, 3rd sgts), platoon sgts were mostly regulars, longer ? nope, same as tekong, diff is that the SOC has no concertina wires cos you'll be doing 5 click soc later on ... (10 click if you go leaders course) ...... the really fun stuff will start after BMT

    but i guess i have no right to say much though, as i never got the red beret in the end (downgraded, loosen shoulder tendons, dislocates easily ...) since you're going in, have to warn you about taking care of yourself, don't be afraid to sound out if you have a (physical) problem, the trainers will insinuate that you're chao-kenging for sure, and reporting sick at hendon is like going into a concentration camp (during my time, the MOs were fuckers who treats everyone as a chao-keng case until proven otherwise, and will ask the medics to drip you for anything to give them practise...) push yourself, do your best, but be mindful of your body's signals ... don't end up like me, not being able to play tennis or badminton anymore, and sometimes wakes up in pain because a shoulder joint pops out at night ...

    On the other hand, remember that what you achieve in your 2 (2 years now already ? ... ) years will stay with you for the rest of your lives, even though i can put my head up high and say i truly did the best i could (9.00 2.4, not the best of course, but respectable even there ... ) and it was something unforseenable, ... i still count not getting a red beret one of the big regrets of my life ..... This goes out to everyone going into BMT too ... there will be times when you're tired and dirty or got stuck on weekends etc .... and you'll start comparing how good a life others have in other camps, vocations, etc ..... just remember that years down the road, all these will be treasured memories,.... make them all good ones .... don't have any "what ifs" in your 2 years of wearing number 4 .........
