    let me answer the first question first... "Why are Guards called Guards? "

    In the past, the sorveign's personal body guards are elite soldiers, their selection is more stringent, some of them have to serve in normal line regiments and do well before they are put into the guard units. Also their training/pay etc are supposed to be better. And the unit is suppose to be better motivated and have higher combat value then normal line units.

    For eg, Napolean's Imperial Guards are all veterans of many campaigns. B4 Waterloo and after he came back from exile in Elba, all the french line regiments have to nominate 30 of their best men to join the reformed imperial guard. And he used them as his "knockout" punch in a battle, reserving them until the last moment.....
