    No, what you're referring to is called the mono intake. All the SAF frontline units (SIR, SAR, SA, GDS, CDO, SCE) muster their fighting troops this way, & these guys stay together from BMT right through reservist, forming the main body of the unit. The Tekong trainees will join the unit only after their commander (OCS, SISPEC) or vocational courses (signals, medics, drivers, storemen, etc.) courses.

    BTW This system was one of the reasons why Malays were excluded when NS was first implemented, cuz it was more practical to staff all the Hokkien peng (the majority of each intake) together for combat duty instead. That the NSF "segregation" endures to this day was partly cuz the Malays consequently built a solid legacy in the Police & SCDF -- "if ainÂ’t broke, donÂ’t fix itÂ” -- although, with improving academic qualifications, they can also be posted to the combat side (as officers & specs) now.

    Armour peng traditionally quite garang. Dunno if they still do it now, but once upon a time only they & OCS conduct boxing as a PT activity! Also, before the SAF restructurings, there used to be a big rivalry between the SAR & SIR formations (i.e. "heavy (mechanized) infantry" vs. "light/line infantry") in the old days.
