    In regard to the ORD FFI, It is usually a very brief examination. Think about it, it is to check if the personnel is fit for ORD... I mean everyone should be fit for ORD. The main reason for the FFI is mainly 2 fold

    1. To ensure that the serviceman has no outstanding medical conditions that needs follow-up
    2. To ensure that a perm PES status is given. No PES D.

    As to the rumor that the MO will take the opportunity to upgrade the person, don't think anyone is that free to create additional work for himself.

    Think I forgot to intro myself, I was previosly an NSF MO doing staff work and hence worked closely with the senior commanders and got to know the policies behind all the medical directives

    Dear dustbuster

    Your medical board is probably a board in absentee type, in which your medical officer will present his petition to a senior medical officer for consideration.

    Few things possible
    1. Medical boards fully booked for months and hence your case has not come up for review.
    2. Your medical officer has forgotten about your medical board and hence no medical board for you condition has been arranged

    What I would suggest would be to ask the senior medic of the medical center when you medical board is scheduled for. Medical officers are always ORDing but the senior medic is the one regular person around
