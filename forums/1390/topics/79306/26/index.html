    Presumably because there is nothing wrong with urine/blood sugar (to exclude diabetes mellitus) and urine osmolality (to check for diabetes insipidus). Since your symptoms persist although nothing wrong is found, they are trying to see if perhaps you have a mild form of diabetes insipidus, due to the lack of anti-diuretic hormone (ADH).

    ADH is produced during water deprivation and its effect is to inhibit urine formation, to conserve water. One cause of frequent urination is due to the lack of ADH. The water deprivation test is to see if your ADH level rise as it should following water deprivation. If you have a mild form of diabetes insipidus, there is a defect in ADH production and this rise will be absent during water deprivation.
