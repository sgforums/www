    most of the time, the medical board will highly endorsed what ur specialist written in the memo or report, which is to be submitted to ur reservist medical centre.

    there r minority cases where if the chairman of the medical board (a MAJ doctor) can e.g. board a particular case with e.g. c2l9, when the attending medical centre nsf CPT doctor recommend e.g. c9l9, which is according to the pes bible. but all these r based on the degree of the conditions as deemed. i cant say much.

     

    a routine normal medical board is conducted with a chairman (at least a MAJ), and 2 nsf CPT doctors to ensure a fair rule on the pes.
