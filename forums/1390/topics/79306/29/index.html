    Osgood Schlatter is very common, particularly in the NSF age group. If all cases of OSD are downgraded, the army will lose alot of combat fit soldiers (unfortunately, recruiting foreign talent to fill the vacancies is not an option ) Your MO will be very reluctant to recommend a permanent downgrading and the board president will be extremely reluctant to downgrade as well. They will most likely hope that with time, your condition will get better (as it does in many people). After ORD, your liability of 2- 4 weeks a year during reservist time is not too bad and most people with OSD can cope with it.

    If you want to get a downgrade, your best bet is to go shopping for a private specialist who is willing to write a recommendation to that effect.

    The SAF is used to handling OSD and sports injuries that are considered far too trivial for the hospital physiotherapist at TTSH. You must understand that a place taken up by you for having some pain when you squat may mean that a patient with a stroke may not get a slot for his physiotherapy which will be delayed. That's why we used to send soldiers with these conditions for physio at the soldier performance centre (not sure what they call it nowadays). In any case, as you admitted, physio did not help your dad much either (whom I presume has the same condition).

    During my time, OSD cases do not get downgraded at all. I was considered combat fit even though I was cripppled with a birth defect (club foot) but had an operation to correct it when I was older. By the time an X-ray was done after I got gone through BMT and OCS with a persistent limp after every SOC, my ankle joint is completely deformed. That's just life! The army is certainly more welfare-oriented nowadays..

    Thank you for your time.
    First, the spondylolysis is a separate and different problem from mitral valve prolapse (MVP). Some people think that having two medical problems can be additive in some way and you can downgrade further. You cannot.

    Second, if all you have is MVP, it is unlikely that you can be downgraded further given that you are already C2L2perm. Some cases of MVP can be associated with mitral regurgitation and you may need to have an echocardiogram to assess the severity. However, since your GP only mentioned MVP but not a murmur, I doubt it but you should have it checked by a cardiologist. As you are now a reservist, you have to pay to see your own specialist. If you also have a mitral regurgitation that is severe enough, there is a possibility of Pes E but I doubt it based on what you have written.

    Go and seek a specialist assessment. If you indeed have MVP, you should inform your dentist so he can put you on prophylactic antibiotics if necessary.

    routine med board is for ur standard uncomplicated medical board
    psy board is for p-cases where psychiatric personnel are reviewed and given new pes or extension of the same pes.
    pd board is for ur service injury percentage of permanment disablity. like ur insurance policy determining ur payout.
    specialist board is for complicated cases needing senior hospital specialists/senior regular MO (ltc and above rank) to review ur case and board u at MMI (NUS).
