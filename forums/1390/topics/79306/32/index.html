    With regard to the Uric acid - like I mentioned, it's not always confirmatory.
    Some additional tests are, for example, x-rays, joint aspiration or even MRI.
    The only confirmatory test for gout is actually to aspirate the affected joint/ (e.g. the ankle) which involves poking a needle into the ankle to suck out Some fluid Sorry we doctors do painful things to our poor patients.

    With regard to the downgrade - the medical classification Centre will convene a medical board to decide each case.     Nevermind it's your 8th ICT -your case will be considered on its own merits.

    hi, so sorry for the late reply...
    the first part about being colorblind: this is considered medical-in-confidence.
    it cannot be written on your ORD cert or you can Sue SAF for that!
    you should declare the fact that yoU are colour blind to yoUr future prospective employer if it's relevant. But SAF cannot make this their business.

    You're right - x-rays cannot show disc problems and an MRI is the investigation of choice in this case. The Cobb's angle was originally defined based on x-rays but the so-called 'scout' scan of the spine includes images that are very Similar to the routine x-rays. Which means the angle can be estimated on the Scan. What could the pain be due to? Well, muscular strain is Still the most common, but Sometimes it Could be something more Serious.
    For example, A pSoas abscess, tumour, Spinal cord or nerve root compression etc... But don't worry too much - and by the way MRI can pick all these other nasties out!
