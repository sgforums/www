    24 Oct 04, 01:13
    9 months' OCS to get commissioned as 2LT. 5 months' SISPEC to get 3SG. 2LT will advance to LTA when near to ORD. That's basically it for NSFs.

    Come reservist, the CPT & 2SG (or higher) promotions will depend more on unit-appointment designations.

    For regular officers, my impression is you'd better make MAJ by age 29-31, & then LTC by age 35-37, or else the "condemned" label or peaked-out "ceiling" will be increasingly pressured on you. Of course, the SAF hierarchy is pyramid-structured - the higher you climb, the more limited the vacancies available.

    LHL's status is different; he's a pioneering 1970s SAF scholar from the days when the military was trying to professionalize its officer corps. Such high-fliers still climb relatively rapidly nowadays, but not as quickly. For example, there's an online MINDEF news release from 2000 that said "LTC Hugh Lim U Yang & LTC Soh Poh Theen [are] among the youngest LTCs to be promoted to COLs at the age of 35", as a hint of the SAF promotional pace.
