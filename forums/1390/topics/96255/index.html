    Tekong... 1980s... doing my BMT. Section ambush on cross-roads near the old village school in the north of the island.

    8 or 9 of us lined up in typical ambush formation. The corporal in charge of training was a few meters behind the ambush formation. We were all settling into the ambush drill...

    Suddenly, a high pitched scream pierced the night. The scream was half human , half mechanical . And it was REALLY LOUD and PIERCING. So much so that all the section stopped whatever we were doing and broke the ambush drill by sitting up in our positions looking around. I looked back at the corporal as he was more or less directly behind me. He was brushing his teeth at the time of the scream. In the darkness of the night, I could see the white of his Colgate in his open mouth. He was frozen mid-way in brushing his teeth. (Because it was BMT training, there was a nominated recruit who was acting section commander, so the "real" corporal can be non- tactical...).

    At that time, we were not really scared. It was just that the scream was unnerving. The corporal decided to pack up and told us to execute a quick tactical withdrawal from the ambush site. We then went back to camp. Believe he reported the scream to the OC and camp DO, and cited it as a valid excuse to break off night training. It was also a Thursday night !

    Later , wild stories started to circulate, most were untrue. WE DID NOT SEE ANYTHING, but we just heard this frightful half human scream, which at that time puzzled us more than frightened us.

    Of the recruits in this section, a number signed on later and some of them are still serving in the SAF. Bet they remember this episode.

    then the footsteps get louder and it sounds like 2 person instead of 1. then suddenly the footsteps stop and the bed 1 recruit can feel that the figure outside is at the window looking into the bunk. he didn't open his eyes as he scare the sergeant will find him not sleeping.

One is that there are civilians still hanging around Tekong. This was certainly true during the 1980s when there was a very small kampong near the old jetty. Anyway, the screams and cries we hear could be the villagers playing the fool with the soldiers. I am not sure if in the 1990s there were still villagers around.
