  • specfore
    8 Feb 05, 18:46
    2 camps spring to mind....

    1) Nee Soon Camp in the 1980s.... when the old SBMT was there. I was based there so I have some affiliation. The old british barracks and officer mess are unique architecture in Singapore. Also, I found in the guard room at the main gate, old British Army documents. They were guard duty hand over and take over sheets. They were quite old... they were still listing down "drummers and runners". Presumably in the old days, probably before WW 2, the guard detachment had drummers attached on duty to beat the drum as a signal of some sort (muster parade?).

    My platoon office was high-ceilinged , with a large balcony and much of the structure was old, including the furniture !

    There were also bungalows behind the officer mess, which was unofficially a "no - go" area for SAF personnel. The New Zealand army had their families housed there and they had their own MP patrols. But on duty once, I had the chance to take a look on a few ocassions... they were the grand old 2 storey bungalows with high ceiling and a bit of perankan architecture. Nice. Nice women there too !

    Wonder what happened after the re-development of Nee Soon Camp? Can someone elaborate?

    2) Dieppe barracks in the 1990s.... when I did my reserve duty. The New Zealand army just handed over to SAF. We moved in and had the luxury of air-con rooms / bunks to sleep in. Quite modern and not so fascinating. It was the "luxury" that impressed me.

    Hi Gordonator

    Nice to know there is someone there in Nee Soon. During my time, Transit Road had those "mama" shops selling cheap goods. A year or so after my ROD, they built better shophouses for those guys. I think they are still there, as during my reservist in the 1990s, I went to a camp nearby to draw ammo and passed by Nee Soon.

    I was in SBMT 2nd Wing and my office was in Echo Coy near the Muslim cookhouse. I wonder if it is still there? Facing the Officers' Mess. One day, when I was in the platoon office, gazing out of the balcony, I saw a old British couple (man and wife... around 60 years old). They were walking along looking at the buildings. I guessed he was an ex-British army serviceman. I went down to say "hello" , since they were already loitering outside the company line. He told me he served there in the 1950s and actually Echo Coy barracks was the cookhouse staff barracks, which explained why we had some stuff we found in the building. He was quite amazed that in the 1980s , very little had changed. Some new training sheds were built but not much changes.
