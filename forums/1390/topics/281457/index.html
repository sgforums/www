  • blitzonic
    24 Sep 07, 13:29
    I kena NDP both years during my NS. The first time was marching contingent, & also performing item later in the parade. The second time was mobile column (M113 SP mortar). I also kena one during reservist, a big-scale Military Tattoo at the Stadium on SAF day.

    Actually, my NS run-ups were quite humdrum. For weekday rehearsals, we only moved out at 3 pm or so; the rest of the day were as per normal (follow the unit training programmes). For weekend ones, we were allowed to book in after lunch -- but counted as a full-day "burnt" each time.

    The reservist ones were more siong: book in by 8am, draw arms, wait around in training-sheds for half a day (no bunks, & canteens closed on weekends), then wait again at stadium until 5pm (when the proper rehearsal started), R.T.U by 11pm-plus, wait for DO to clear arms, & then hope no need to pay midnight cab fare. Repeat same process next day or, if it's a Monday, crawl back to work. Nine f*cking weekends!

    On the parade itself, note that what you see on TV can be deceptive. For example, the marching contingents often stand at attention for almost 80 minutes -- a looonnng time -- without being able to see the VIP arrivals, the flypasts etc. A lot of the contingents do sporadically talk among themselves on the field, as a result. They catch up with dinner outside during the performance items, before returning in for the finale.

    But, I must say, it was incredibly exhilarating standing in the middle of the field during the NDP climax, with the musical crescendos, the crowd screaming & foot-stomping, & the fireworks display. You really felt what those Malaysia Cup teams must've felt whenever they scored a goal.
