    There will be 2 things together, the road show and a recruitment fair.

    It is a must go.

    The road show will tell you more about e-prep. You will be given some credits which you can use to "purchase" courses to study. Some of these courses are certified. Note that you will have to pay 10% of the course fee by cash.

    The recruitment fair is as it sounds - a recruitment fair. There will be some companies, other government sectors such as MOE, SCDF, SPF...., some foreign universities, so on and so for. If you have are not furthering your studies, and want to seek a job with a reasonable pay, you can try signing for anyone of those - but do not make such a decision hastily, since you are going to "sell your soul" for a number of years.
