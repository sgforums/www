  • blitzonic
    13 Aug 07, 16:39
    I assume a regular officer who does not re-up will be in his late-20s, & holds at least a CPT's rank. He's definitely behind his civilian peers, who will be priming to enter middle-management at that point in their careers. But I don't agree that his only options are entry-level jobs or, to be more specific, that he'll necessarily be leaving the military "blind" as per before.

    MINDEF now has a DES (D'eXecutive Services) entity that helps facilitate training their regulars for civilian employment, namely the Career Transition Branch (under DES-SAFPSC) for senior officers & the Centre for Management Development (or DES-MCMD) for junior officers.

    [While confirming the details, I just found out MCMD even has a E-Prep scheme for NSFs too, with e-credit subsidies available to offset course fees. For now, the individual service branches coordinate for their wospecs, under various MOUs like SAF-ASPRI, RSAF-AAIS & RSN-ASMI.]

    The junior officer won't likely have time for part-time studies while with an active unit. But if he's diligent, or ambitious, he could forseeably do so -- possibly even disrupt his bond -- when attached to the school-trainer side.
