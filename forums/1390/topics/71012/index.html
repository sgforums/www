    Guardsmen are proud of their esprit de corps and fighting capabilities. Adopting the motto "Ready to Strike", they operate on the principles of lightning fast speed to capture objectives or intercept enemy incursions into Singapore.

    To understand the Guards Specialist vocation better, Army News spoke to SSG Ow Boon Hong who is currently an Operations Warrant Officer in 7 Singapore Infantry Brigade.

    What is the job scope of a Guards Specialist?

    Basically a Guards Specialist handles operations in three dimensions Â– namely in air, land and sea. For example, we heli-rappel, patrol on Light Strike Vehicles and conduct coastal operations.

    What training does a Guards Specialist undergo?

    All Guards Specialists undergo a five-week Guards Conversion Course (GCC). Besides the intense physical training including a coastal swim test, a Guards Specialist will learn skills specific to the Guards formation in the course. Some of these skills include weapons proficiency, heli-rappelling and jungle survival.

    Guards form the frontline troops. What mental preparation is needed to assume this role?

    I think the important thing is the attitude that our Guardsmen have. We know that we are prepared and that we will be able to deliver results. This gives us the confidence to carry out our operations.

    And the Guards training is conducted in small groups; this builds up the cohesiveness between the Guardsmen. This is how we become a closely knitted family where the commanders and the men share a quality relationship. There is mutual respect and everyone supports one another.
