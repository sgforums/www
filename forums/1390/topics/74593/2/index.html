    Just to add on to mfscrewu's suggestions. Try to buy a pair of spandex tights (cycling tights) and wear them before putting on your no. 4 pants. This would prevent chafing on your thighs when your pants get soaked with sweat. Dun forget to powder your crotch beforehand though...

    I was also a scout signaller last time, so here's some suggestions. Make sure that your signal set and frame are strapped on tight. This would prevent it from rattling against your back when you do your fast march. If you are carrying spare antennas, tie the lower bottom of antenna case to the signal set ruck, and see if you can switch over to whip antenna, while keeping the collapsible one in the case. If its possible to use the whip antenna, try to tie down the antenna to your SBO by bending it down. Put a couple of smal rubber bands on the right side of your harness (or left, depending on your preference). I know we are not supposed to do it, since the antenna might snap, but just be careful and not flex it too much....But the whip antenna thing depends on your PC or team leader.

    If you are putting the signal set in your full pack, try to position the radio comfortably so that it doesn't rattle. My trick was to position the spare batteries in such a way that it pushes the signal set against the back of the fullpack, and used my jacket to fill up the space and as a cushion.

    Try to adjust your SBO and full pack so that they ride high on your shoulders and not around your hips, cos' it'll really hurt after awhile.

    Last thing...hang in there bro!! You can do it! And welcome to our fraternity...

    Good for you! You are coping well with the stresses of scout training. Yeah, signallers carry the heaviest load around...what to do? You are the commo lifeline of the team....

    There's little more you can do to reduce the stress on your shoulders, unfortunately...the best thing to do is to make sure you distribute your load...i.e. spread the things around in your full pack or ALICE pack in such a way that both shoulders would carry about the equal burden. Another way you could ease the burden is to buy some thick foam padding and tape them on your ruck's shoulder straps.

    As for the combat rations, pass some to your SAW gunner/basher, and put the rest in your ruck. If possible, you could even put one or two in your thigh pockets. It really depends on your preference. If you want a warm meal of say, glutinous rice, then sandwich one of the ration packet in-between the radio and the backboard of your fullpack, or even better, your ALICE pack....after you work up a sweat, the heat from your body, plus the signal set would transfer to your ruck, and accordingly, to your combat ration...quite nice!!
