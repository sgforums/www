    my coy first to book out but we got lion's trail and i seriously underestimated the weight of things

    tried to be smart by loading all my admin/PT kits, 1 water bottle(compulsory) CV clothes, CV shoes etc

    almost broke my back trying to carry it.

    honestly, the night before, my section was in a total mess as we tried to sweep the place clean of dust etc. so that we wont tio late book out during area inspection.

    though the fans were on, we were all perspiring and calling out for each other to speed things up and i believe nerves were frayed.

    when i reached home, i found my room, com, keyboard, mouse, lcd so foreign.

    feel as if i had to re learn typing and my mouse which used to fit my hand to a T now feels like a rock and my lcd screen made me feel as if my eyes were having problems.

    anyway NS,
    honestly i feel the things to bring to NS list is somewhat long and i wish to add more stuff to it like waht to expect, how to fix up SBO, field pack, helmet etc, because my first week was a super stressed one as we all scrambled to fix them in our limited admin time, unfamiliarity with those things.

    anyway my sgt told us tht they cant really punish us during first 2 weeks until first book out, after which,,,,it's all hell break loose.

    somehow i feel as if some of the sgts cant really....lead ?

    eg, 1 sgt was reading a manual on attending to injured platoon mates just before the lesson itself and he was reading frm the manual as he thought..

    really inspiring lesson....

    for the SBO, 2 sgts gave 2 versions of how to tie some of the straps and we were terribly confused because of it.

    tying the camo to the helmet was another source of stress for us because we tried for days before getting close to the standard required for the helmets.

    then suddenly on the second week, we had more and more admin time but the sgts started to tekan us for stuffs and shouting at as anyhow.

    for Battalions specs to go BMTC to train recruits... it's not an official policy.. but since units like BCTC, BMTC have lower priorities in specialist deployments, they face constant shortages to the point they have to borrow specialist from units to help alleviate the problems temporarily.

    Also units that have already ORD'ed, but they have too many regular sect comd and they have been selected to be Platoon Sergeants. Since one unit only need 13-16 PS, the left over regular sect comds will do their "ps tour" in bmtc.

    as far as i know, they will be PS in bmtc but will not count as serving the PS tour. they will still go thru the Basic qualification courses as normal... only later.
