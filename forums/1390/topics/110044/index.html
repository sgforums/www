    2 Jan 05, 14:56
    I think some kind of formal identification is still needed, unless the troops are wholly confined to camp during their overseas stint.

    For ROC, I remember my reservist unit organizing temporary (military) ICs for everyone going over, & that meant putting aside time after inprocessing to queue up for photo-taking & stuff. It was during the mid-90s, so I've forgotten whether we specifically exchanged in our 11-B's for them.

    For us, the ICs served primarily as gate passes over there, though in reality IMO they weren't all that effective. We were all walking in & out whenever possible, if only to jalan-jalan around the rustic neighborhood (only base personnel phoned for taxis to go to Touliu). I also found their sentry posts to be quite senang: portable barriers as opposed to camp gates, & no sidegate for controlled screening - in fact, the Taiwanese guards were generally unresponsive whenever we flashed the ICs at them (while in our PT kits) from 15, 20 metres away.
