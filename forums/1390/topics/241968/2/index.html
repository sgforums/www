    While I'm not entirely familiar with the inner mechanisms of the Singapore army, it is not hard to understand where the author's coming from.

    Think about it : in most developed countries, the leaders of the respective armed forces are schooled in military academies preparing them for a career in the military (and that includes the world's leading military force, that of the US) and not one in another domain, only to be deployed in the military later on.

    While one might draw parallels that the likes of Colin Power has transited from military leadership to a top post in a civilian administration, it doesn't alter the fact the system, by and large, trains individuals for that particular field (you don't see top US generals having been schooled at Harvard and Yale Law School for instance) - in essence, anyone with ability in a given field can rise through the ranks to the highest post of a given field in the US. That, unfortunately, can hardly be said about Singapore - let's face it, Colin Powell would never have become a general if he'd been born in Singapore since the posts of generals are reserved primarily for the academically-inclined who might not have the slightest nuances/acumen for the military... To better get a grasp of what the author is driving at, try imagining having a Harvard Law School graduate administer treatment to you if you had cancer...
