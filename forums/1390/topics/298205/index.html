  • SBS3625D
    20 Dec 07, 22:37
    Yes. Navy NSF.

    I was sent for helo fire-fighting because I'm from a frigate which is due to receive helicopters.

    Basically as a seaman, besides knowing FFDC (fire-fighting damage control), now I have to learn to fight helo-fire in the event of a crash-landing.

    Helo-fire is a class B fire which means water is practically useless in fighting it. For that reason, we have foam gun in the hangar, and the heli-deck and hangar door is fitted with AFFF (Aqueous Film-Forming Foam) which is far superior in combatting such fire.

    The course basically gave us procedures how to approach a downed helo, what to do in the event of a crash to prevent a fire. And best (or worst) of all, we had to don fearnought suit (aluminised suit) to combat aviation fuel fire which was easily the worst fire I had been thrown, and rescue casualties (if any) from the helo.
