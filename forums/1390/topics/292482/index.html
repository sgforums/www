    Depends on what he sign on as....the different scenarios I can think of is...

    Specialist (Sergeant and then slowly promote to Warrant officer)
    A professional career, very demanding job.

    Officer
    Equally demanding, longer hours than specialists, but higher prospects, higher pay, highly respected.

    Special Forces
    Elite people from SOF (Special Operations Force) or NDU (Naval Diving Unit), these are highly trained "professional killers" in a way, with even higher pay, prestige, but much more dangerous job.

    Specialists can crossover to officers midway, its possible, though rare.

    Time - The Sacrifice

    A military person is under orders, when order comes, he cannot discuss and see whether he should obey - he has to follow. His schedule and time is at the mercy of his higher superiors - even if he is an officer.

    Normal days is like normal office hours outside, 5-day work week, except some Saturdays got to join whole unit for outdoor running.

    Sometimes may have to go a few days of continuous training, etc, which you may not be able to see or contact him.

    Whenever sent overseas for training, can be min 2 weeks to 2 months overseas, with few chances to contact.

    The Reward
    If he's an officer, you can look forward to a military wedding, with all his fellow officer friends all dressed in white and drawing their ceremonial swords on your wedding day.

    His pay will also be very stable and good, if he's an officer, confirm you can buy condo by the time he reach 35. and he can definitely buy a good car within 2nd year of service.

    At end-of-contract and retirement, he also gets a lumpsum which is quite handsome.

    Danger
    Training and operations is relatively safe with alot of safeguards and systems. There're also comprehensive insurance schemes, just in case something really happens. He'll be well taken care of.
