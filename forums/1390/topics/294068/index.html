    DB has recently been renamed to CC or correction center or something like that if i didn't remember wrongly. Regardless, its just the military equivalent of a jail. The amount of days spent in CC will depend on the offence and how high ranking is the officer who sents you there. Duration of over a month is not uncommon if its the CO or higher.

    All NSF gets 14 days of leave a year and besides that, you get off on all public holidays and return to camp like any normal working adult on the next working day. You can earn extra offs etc if your unit happens to burn some of your weekends but in most cases, its easier to burn weekends than claim offs.

    Try to slowly learn the NS lingo .... you should have also received an introductory recruit's handbook.

    BMT is always "stay-in" whether in BMTC (Tekong) or "mono-intake" (at the combat unit itself). I'm not familiar with the exact timetable for Enhanced BMT, Modified BMT or PTP (Physial Training Phase) BMT. But the standard one, if the system hasn't changed: first two weeks shall be "confinement" (no weekend bookouts), then a Parent's Visit Day (relatives can bring sinful food in to feed you). Thereafter, you can book out during the weekends & may also occasionally get "admin time" (no night training). The reverse can also happen: "burning" a weeknight or weekend due to extra lessons or something.

    If you're enlisting on Dec 15, it appears you may enjoy a super-long weekend ahead with CNY (Chinese New Year) due next year on a Thursday & a Friday (7-8 Feb). New Year's Day & this Xmas are also public holidays, both on Tuesdays. Not sure if you can go overseas without an "exit permit" during BMT; better check with your commanders. Hang in there, the SAF usually schedules a few days of "block leave" at the end of BMT, so as to process all the recruits' vocational/leadership postings & also to synchronize the enrollment at the various units.
