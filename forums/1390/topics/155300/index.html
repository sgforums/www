    Well... I am from the latest batch of bmt enlistees...

    Here are some latest information and experiences in BMT

    IPPT tests

    There will be 4 ippt tests to be conducted throughout your bmt, but only the last one will be the real test.

    1st Test: IPPT Categorisation Test
    2nd Test: IPPT Diagnostic Test
    3rd Test: IPPT Trial Test
    4th Test: IPPT Test

    Physical Training (PT)

    There will be a number of different exercises to train u all up.
    Most of the exercises are carried out once a week. ST and AGR might will be conducted usually 2 hours.

    1) 5BX
    2) CPT1
    3) CPT2
    4) AGR
    5) 30:60
    6) ST

    5BX
    STands for 5 Basic Exercise. You will be required to do this 5BX every morning ( yes every morning) right after you revielle.

    1) Jumping Jacks (10-20 counts)
    2) Alternate Leg Raise (10-20 counts)
    3) Crunches (10-15 counts)
    4) Push-ups (10-20 counts)
    5) Burpies (10-20 counts)

    You will also required to run and jog for a distance around 200m-400m after the 5bx.

    CPT1
    Combat PT 1. It is a warm exercise you will need to do for most of the PT exercises. Consists of 10 different exercises.

    1) Rear Lunge
    2) Bend and reach
    3) High-jumpers
    4) Squat bender
    5) Wind-mill
    6) Rower
    7) Prone rower
    Push-up
    9) Forward Lunge
    10) Pectoral Stretch

    CPT2
    Combat PT 2. It is a set of exercises to train your abs and your limbs.
    COmbat PT 2 take less than an hour to complete. Usually being carried out before PTI arrive for ST or other exercises.

    1) Sit-ups (20 counts)
    2) Push-ups (20 counts)
    3) 3 Climbing drills

    Climbing drills mainly trains your chin-ups and SOC. 3 sets of climbing drills: CHin-ups, straight arm pull and hang leg-raise.

    AGR

    Ability Group Run. Recruits are divided into 4 groups based on the timings of the 2.4km run in the IPPT categorisation test. 20mins non-stop run, with timing within 400m.
    for example Group A will run 400m in 2 mins 20 secs for 20mins, and Group B will run 400m in 2 mins 30 secs for 20mins....

    30:60

    Another exercise to train for 2.4km run. 30:60 means Sprint for 30 secs and jog for 60 secs. Usually recruits will need to complete 6-10 sets of 30:60. Very similar to the "interval training". Progressively, 30:60 will be increased to 60:120.

    ST
    Strength Training. 2 sets of exercise drills. Dumbbell drills and medicine ball drills. Recruits will need to do 10 dumbbell exercises and 5 medicine ball drills. ST will train more on your upper limbs and also lower limbs.

    Water Parades

    Try to train yourself to drink half water bottle in 1 go.... Some of you will get bloated after it.

    Finger Power

    A neglected strength. Some companies put less attention to it. Your fingers power and grip are very important tightening things. Fixing and attaching water bottle pouches to SBO really need finger power. You will need to secure straps to the MAXIMUM, in order to reach the standard of a goood SBO. Attaching camo net to your helmet also requires finger power. Making sure the net is tightly fitted to the helmet, needs tremendous strength and skill. My fingers are really swollen after fixing these items.

    A Basic Day in BMT
    0530: Reville ( Wake up!! )
    0545: Fall in for 5BX
    0600: Breakfast
    0630: Area Cleaning
    0730: PT lesson / IMT
    1000: Combat lessons
    1130: Lunch
    1245: Combat Lessons
    1500: PT lesson / COmbat lessons
    1730: Dinner
    1900: PT lesson / Lectures
    2030: RO
    2100: Coy Admin
    2130: Admin
    2230: Lights Out

    Inspections

    Standby Area: Inspection of your bunk for cleanliness. A bit of dust can get you 20 pushups or 30 Jumping Jacks.

    Standby Bed: Inspection of your cabinet and bed. Messy or dirty earns you the famous knock-it-down-20.... or the latest phrase "Half-Left-Down 20".

    Standby Book: Inspection of your notebook. Instructors want to see what notes and important points you have taken down throughout your lessons.

    Standby Fieldpack: Inspection of your famous green school bag. All items must be in the field pack and in the correct arrangement.

    Standby SBO: Inspection of the black tapes and things on your SBO. 1 missing L-torch or earplugs get your triceps train better!

    Standby Universe: The KING... INspection of EVERY thing you have... All your pockets to be inside out, SBO pouches opened, duffel bag opened. Mostly to be conducted for theft or missing items cases.

    For most part of BMT, the exercises is divided into 2 categories.... Strength training and runs.

    so the training will alternate between days.

    so some weeks will be monday wednesday and friday (strength days), tuesday, thursday, saturday (run days)

    strength days is where you do ST.

    Run days will be the AGR and the 30:60. Beginning it will be at least one of this 2 for alternate days. later on you might have 2 in a day.

    They now use CPT 1 as warm up for most of the exercises unless the activity you are warming up for is IPPT, SOC or anything conducting officer thinks its streneous.

    Attire, it is singlet and shorts for the beginning but it will progress to SBO attire. Strength training will eventually end of with a run in SBO attire in traning for SOC

    Ok, IN THEORY, the exercises are supposed to be alternate days, but due to public holidays, or some other events, tranining program may change till that strength training and runs will be on the same day.

    Also, IN THEORY, these are the only forms of physical training so authorised by G6ARMY. But focusing on these training only is not sufficient usually.
