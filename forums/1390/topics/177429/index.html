    As far as I know.... according to what BMTC Comd said before..

    As long you have taken some form of post secondary school education, you are eligible. They will not go to lengths as to find out what degree/diploma you have.

    What you studied will not affect your posting either UNLESS you are a regular.

    In general,

    highest qualification O level = mono intake BMT.
    Highest qualification O level but attempted some post secondary = BMTC BMT (leadership package).

    Attempted some post secondary can be anything like took one year of poly and drop out.

    The problem usually comes during which the person has a foreign qualification. BUT it is general accepted that Grade 12, IB and diploma qualification are eligible for BMTC BMT.

    At least the case now is quite clear cut. Because I've known cases where people "accidentally" served 1/2 year less in the past.

    12 Feb 06, 09:08
    Private certs are recognised but only to a certain extent.
    If you have a private cert, SISPEC is probably the highest you can go. OCS will be out of bounds to private cert holders.

    If you are talking about signing on then conditions are much more stringent. Private certs are not recognised at all so if you sign on your next highest qualification will be your secondary school education.

    Frankly, the private cert area is still not that well established. With the recent problems regarding closure of private schools due to financial reasons etc... the government is probably not going to be accepting such certs in the near future. Commercial firms accept on a case by case basis. Usually, it's better to get a private cert as an add-on to your normal ite/poly/uni education and not as a stand alone thing.
