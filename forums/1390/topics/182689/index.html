    Is it possible to have a mature discussion on racial bias in the SAF? I sure hope so....

    There are NO Malays in Armour. There are Indians and Chinese and ahem... "others" I was surprised that some Armour camps didnt even serve Muslim food at the cookhouse. Now if anything, THAT should be a big indicator that there is simply no need to cater for Muslims' (no matter the race) dietary prejudices, because if you're a DECLARED Chinese-Muslim or Indian-Muslim, you will NOT get sent to Armour. Or Military Intelligence. Or etc...

    We really should not brush this issue under some rug. Most people are aware of the need for security, especially with respect to the largely Muslim population of the neighbouring countries. The possibility of the Muslims within the SAF turning into some sort of fifth column in the event of a conflict is one that most people, no matter the race, would scoff at. However, the racial restrictions still remain, and people should be aware of them. If you are a Muslim in the SAF, you will NOT be treated the same as others.

    Is this really so bad though? Many countries practice negative discrimination based on security reasons, for entry into some branches of the security services. On the other hand, there is also the phenomenon of positive discrimination, so you see, for example, women getting favourable treatment and lower standards. Such things happen in many countries, not just Singapore.

    One thing that many Muslims dont like to acknowledge is that Islam preaches a universality of brotherhood, so in theory, a Muslim in one country should not take up arms against another. In practice, the fairly recent phenomenon of nationalism has succeeded pretty well in making such a value one of theory alone. Of course, the more ancient ideology of tribalism did not prevent Muslims (and other adherents of universal type religions/ ideologies) from merrily hacking each other to death. But is this a risk national leaders would be willing to accept? That there would be those among their own armed forces that would be swayed more by religion than by nationalism? After all the Abrahamic religions, and many others besides, claim to supercede all other callings including nationalism.

    Sorry for the hijack... but I just dont like it when some people act as if racial bias in the SAF either does not exist or should not be discussed.
