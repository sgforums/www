    The SAF Counselling Centre (SCC) was first set up in 1977 to provide drug abuse treatment and rehabilitation services for servicemen with a drug abuse problem. Since 1982, its services have expanded to include providing a general counselling service for all servicemen and women with problems as well as other personal enhancement programmes.

    SCC's mission is to enhance the psycho-social well-being of MINDEF and SAF personnel by providing a range of preventive, rehabilitative and developmental services, thereby contributing to Singapore's defence capability.

    Runs a 24-hour SAF Counselling Hotline Service for SAF servicemen and women and their families.
    Provides individual, couple and group counselling services for SAF personnel for a wide range of concerns.
    Runs rehabilitation programmes for self-confessed drug/inhalant abusers and servicemen under detention or supervision orders for drug/inhalant abuse.
    Recruits, trains, and supports volunteers who are SAF Paracounsellors.
    Provides counselling courses and other people-centred self-development programmes for SAF personnel
