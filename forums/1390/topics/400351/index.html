    Source: www.mindef.gov.sg/nsmen

    Deferment refers to a postponement of a specific In-Camp Training (ICT)

    NSmen from the same unit are called back for ICT at the same time as the SAF operates in cohesive units and it is important to refresh the skills of each NSman and ensure that they operate well as a team. The absence of an NSman from ICT will affect the training and operational readiness of the NSman as well as his unit. For these reasons, MINDEF exercises flexibility in granting deferment but such cases would have to be carefully weighed against the impact on operational readiness.

    All NSmen will inevitably have work commitments and it should not be a valid ground for deferment. In general, all NSmen are given ample advance notice of their NS call-ups. This is to allow sufficient time for the NSman to inform his employer as well as to allow the NSman and his employer to make adjustments and reprioritise his work responsibilities during his period of absence from office. The advance notice given serves to minimise the opportunity cost to employers when their employees are called up for NS training.

    Nevertheless, unit commanding officers may grant deferment on a case-bycase basis, taking into consideration the training requirements, the NSman's proficiency level, attendance records and past performances.

    Using MyDeferment in NS Portal

    Q: How do I apply for deferment?
    A: You need to login to MyDeferment via the NS Portal www.ns.sg and apply on-line.

    As an NSman, you are expected to submit your own deferment application. Information provided by your employer or other sources can be attached as supporting documents to your application. As an NSman, you are expected to submit your own deferment application. Information provided by your employer or other sources can be attached as supporting documents to your application.

    Your NS commander may take the following factors into consideration when deciding on your deferment application:

    • New employment or newly established business
    • Examinations / Internship Attachment
    • Own Marriage, ROM, Honeymoon
    • Compassionate grounds (e.g. wife's delivery, serious illness or death in the family)
    • Other valid reasons, supported by documents


    If you will like to find out more about the training programme for your upcoming NS training, you can go to MyUnit in the NS Portal. MyUnit serves as your unit's electronic notice board where information or updates on your NS training or your unit are posted.
