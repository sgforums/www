    Actually there is a VERY good reason why sprinting and sudden changes in direction are important in the military. When you sprint from cover to cover, you are using the muscles that are important for shuttle run. If you take the time to train, about an hour a day 3 times a week, you'll save yourself a world of grief. In fact, you will find in the field, that aerobic fitness (which you need for 2.4) is seldom really needed.

    And the image of army regulars as being a bunch of overweight, overpaid losers who cant survive in the outside world is sadly mistaken. The ratio of overweight regulars to fit regulars isnt even 40/60... its less than 25/75. Yes, this 25% does give the rest of us a bad image. But the Army cracks down hard on them. In case you didn't know, the performance bonus for Regulars is also affected by our IPPT results, and our BMI.

    And as for pay... a job in the Civil Service will usually pay about the same, with equivalent qualifications. A teacher with a degree from NUS and a diploma in education from NIE (which is NORMAL, not exceptional!!!) for instance, would earn the same than, or more, than an LTA with an equivalent degree from nus. Im using the rank of LTA cos this would be the rank a uni grad officer would be at, when his teacher counterpart has just finished NIE.

    And as for not being able to survive outside the service... Ill tell you if I ever decide to leave, how im coping!

    contrary to popular belief that EOD teams only dispose terrorists' bombs, SAF EOD teams are capable of doing 2 things. they are:

    Explosive Ordnance Disposal and Improvised Explosive Device Disposal.

    the former being rendering safe unexploded ordnance left behind after WW2 or during wartime when the ordnance fails to detonate.

    the latter is disposing IEDs in LIC (Low Intensity Conflict aka terrorism).

    if an unexploded bomb is at a vital military installation, the military has to call the police to come and dispose the ordnance? in an AO that's not in the mainland?

    btw, bombs in this case dun mean those really minute grenades. we are talking abt tank rounds, motars, artillery rounds, aerial bombs and BIG stuff like that.

    so, does the HK police do EOD, or just IEDD?

    also, just because theres peace now, doesnt mean that there will be peace in the future. conventional and unconventional threats to the nation must be dealt with pre-emptively, in this case, making sure that the country has the soldiers to fight a conventional/unconventional war, or as a deterrant force.
