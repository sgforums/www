One way would be to set up a fifth university solely for full-time national servicemen, based on the model of Amercian and Canadian colleges which allow students to do certain credits of their univeristy programme and transfer their credits over to say NUS or NTU or any other foreign universities come the end of their term of service. I believe doubts will be raised as to feasibility of this vis-a-vis to our nation's defence, especialy for those in the combat side. However, I believe that with shrude man power management and streamlining of operational and training needs, with the innovation of new technologies, our men will be afforded the time to do their studies while serving. Furthermore, we aren't talking about a regime of a full degree, but rather credits that will allow soldiers to attain academic knowledge while serving their nation. Of course it'll be tough, but it'll certainly bridge the knowledge gap which most NSFs face after a two year haitus from academia.

    Not fogetting soldiers who aren't persueing a univeristy degree. Individuals who are thinking of persueing a poly diploma or an ITE or O-level cert can also benefit from this.
