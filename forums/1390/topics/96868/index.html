    IMO It's actually only degrading if the DI forces you to use your own toothbrush.

    Objectively, what's wrong with cleaning the loo with such a tool, as long as the latter is disposable? It's not like the SAF provided such basic supplies all the time either; a lot of the equipment (esp. pails, but also extra brushes) were traditionally "scavenged" - sometimes brought from one's own home or paid for out of one's own pockets. My active time even saw a spell where the unfortunate area-cleaners had to rely only on hoses to clean out the filth every morning.

    I understand that some camps outsourced such duties now, but toilet cleaning - even as a punishment - doesn't carry the humiliation of a corrective work order. What if a DI asked you to use a cloth to wipe the surfaces of toilet bowls? Repulsive or not, that is precisely what's being done by chambermaids & civilian cleaners.
