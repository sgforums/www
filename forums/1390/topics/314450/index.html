    Hi there Did the memo state the recommendation that you have to be downgraded? If not, the medical board will probably just go by the book and assess you accordingly. Chances of downgrading would be alot higher if the specialist state that explicitly. Why not go to a pte specialist and request for a specialist report instead? It is alot better than a short written memo as specific details will be documented. Of course you have to fork out a sum of money for that (few grands). I've a friend who suffered from stress fracture. He was temporarily downgraded by SAF until a further review was made with a specialist. The specialist issued a letter and requested for a permanent downgrade eventually. As expected, his wishes came true. Just some food for thought.

    I definitely suggest also that you go to see a private specialist and get them to recommend you for downgrade. The MO is very unlikely to go against the specialist's word. Firstly because if something happens to you or your condition gets worse, that MO will look very bad for rejecting the specialist's advice, and secondly because now someone who is an experienced dermatologist has given his opinion, the MO has a reason to follow that and is no longer forced to just go by the book. Remember just like everybody else, MOs want to avoid trouble and will not want to risk their careers over a simple downgrade for an NSF.
