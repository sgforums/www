    Singing also has important physical effects. When you are forced to sing at regular intervals, it forces you to control your breathing and maintain your pace, so you don't hyperventilate. This is especially important for overweight recruits. It is also a good indicator for your buddies, as if you can't sing that means you are overexerting yourself or suffering an injury, then they can voice out to your medic. Many physical trainers at gyms outside will tell you that when you are doing normal brisk walk/jog, a good indicator is that you should be able to carry on a conversation while doing so - if you are breathing so heavily and gasping for air that you can't speak, it means you should take it slower. Singing helps to do this in an army situation.
