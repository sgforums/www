    very simple. just take leave and go for your studies.

    the predicament here is your CO will never openly say it's OK to go for studies during your NSF period (even though on leave). It's written in black and white liao. i believe your CO is not that heartless to hold people back, he wouldn't gain credit or promotion from such minor issues. but just to cover his own ass, he has to make a stand to follow the directive.

    i'm sure everyone know this common saying in SAF "wanna do anything, just dun get caught." beneath this saying is alot more depth to it.

    give you an example.

    when i was in taiwan for excercise, the ninja vans are all over whenever you're outfield. SAF rule is we are not allowed to buy from them. But we also cannot stop them from selling, coz it's their land, their country, their livlihood, who are we foreigners to prevent them from making a living in their own country right?

    ok so they sell, and we reservist and active NSFs buy discreetly from them. our CSMs, RSMs also never stop us, just close one eye. they even tell us not to buy or eat the food so openly, just be more discreet. but the official ruling still apply - not allowed.

    so why you might ask?? it's just one of those many official "cover backside" rulings any government agencies will have. I mean what happens if SAF personals were to kenna food poisoning? kenna bird flu? who is going to answer?? that is when the cover-backside rule comes in effect. you kenna food poisoning, your own pasar. dun expect SAF to compensate you for food poisoning when ur parents come complain, write letters to papers yadda yadda... COZ the directive already given not to buy from ninja van liao.

    OK, back to your studies issue. take a look from the above example. it's a general workstyle SAF (or any government stats board) have for years. just take leave, dun ask questions, dun kaypoh, dun ask for rights, dun ask for MP help, dun write to press... just quietly go for your studies.

    when you start working in the corporate world, this similar cover-backside standing rule issue will always be around. wanna do anything, just dun get caught.
