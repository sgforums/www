    No.1 Ceremonial Uniform
    White tunic. Formal occasions: parades & ceremonies, commissioning, and even funerals. All ranks, both genders. Pants for females. 

    No.2 Mess Kit
    This is worn during cocktails, mess dinners or evening functions. Red bow tie. Functions where male civilians wear tuxedos  and female civilians wear evening gown.

     

    No.3 Service Uniform
    Worn in less formal functions, where civilians wear short sleeves open neck. Of cause, for clerks and appointed administration staffs too.

    No.4 Uniform
    As we all already know. I think RSAF and RSN has coveralls for No.4

    No.5T
    "Modified" version of No.3 Service Uniform. Extended full long sleeves worn with a tie. During formal functions with civilians who will be wearing shirt and tie or lounge suit.

    No.5J
    Coat and tie functions. Most oftenly worn by the ADCs (Aides-de-Camp) and Honorary ADCs. Also for formal functions like PM Swearing-In etc.

    No.5J (Enhanced)
    All of No.5J with the addition of full service medals on the bush jacket

     

    Best of knowledge.
    Correct me if any.
