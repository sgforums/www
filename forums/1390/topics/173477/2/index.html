    Agree with you.

    we need to have equally proficient executors.

    Look at operations in Afghanistan and Iraq, we could learn a lot from this.Network-centric warfare is useless without grunts who can act on the info given and knock down doors, chiong the back alleys, doing basic infantry and FIBUA work.For every UAV guiding the Brigade Combat Team, the soldiers in the team must still face danger and they must still learn values such as patience and determination, something that a unmanned vehicle cannot apply decisively into war.

    Warm bodies are also needed to hold the ground.No amount of JDAMs or LGBs can defeat a nation, nor liberate a town, or else the Israelis would have long defeated the Palestinians in Jenin or the West Bank.And your foot soldiers must have the right capacity to deal with hardship and adversity.Stand-off weapons and capabilities are first-strike measures, designed to break the enemy's morale and disable their defenses.But to hold ground and push on, you need competent forces who can work on their own.

    None of that advanced soldier system is useful without a good soldier inside.We are the software, the hardware is nothing without the software.And if the software is not well-written (i.e trained in basics well) and debugged vigourously(i.e tough but professional training), then any implementation will merely bog down the system, making pen and paper much more faster(i.e less developed armies beating us).

    Safety is of top priority, which cannot be denied(i.e no programmer wants to ruin his software).It is possible to improve confidence in the SAF and also improve the quality of the soldiers we produce.Whatever they learn today, will protect them in times of conflict.This is not propaganda, this is common sense.The skill of shooting and combat sphere awareness is something not all human beings know or are skilled in.

    Let's see how it goes
