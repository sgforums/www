    Wah, now so "on" ah? Throughout the '90s, only the KAHs, officers & a few diehards wore their ranks in my outfit. Even the 3SGs didn't bother after some time.

    Not being rebellious or bo-chap, to clarify. It's more cuz reservists stay outfield much longer than active troops, possibly for more than a week. Whenever we changed No.4s -- particularly the borrowed uniforms our QM/RQ managed to requisition -- who cares about donning rank, or nametags for that matter, if they can't be fastened?

    At least, nametags could be velcroed in the old days &, of course, officers have their epaulettes. Ultimately, the KAHs ended up wearing their ranks sewn onto those cloth chinagraph holders (worn on the biceps). Guess it was a cumulative improvision among many units over time, cuz they were bought/donated & kept in the CQ stores for each unit ICT.

    Thus, how to wear the new patches? Safety pins?
