    The Army Logistics is made up of HQ Supply & Transport (HQ S&T) and HQ Maintenance and Engineering Support (HQ MES).

    HQ Supply & Transport

    The primary role of HQ S&T is to plan, command and provide the Supply and Transport support to the Army during peacetime, transition to war and during wartime. HQ S&T is to act as Senior Specialist Staff Officer (SSSO) HQ as well as Vocational Advisory Authority (VAA) for Supply & Transport Vocationalists in the Army.

    HQ Maintenance and Engineering Support

    The primary role of the Maintenance and Engineering Support Formation is to monitor and control the available maintenance resources within the Army and facilitate optimal utilisation of these resources on the maintenance of all vehicles, weapons and electronics systems in the Army. It also ensures that equipment are repaired as quickly as possible with the available maintenance resources and achieves this through establishing the appropriate maintenance policies and by constantly applying information technology (IT) and system engineering.[/img]

    THE HISTORY OF ARMY LOGISTICS

    A reorganisation of the Logistics formations was carried out in 1994, resulting in the closing down of HQ Army Logistics Command (ALCOM) and the formation of the HQ Supply & Transport (HQ S&T) and HQ Maintenance and Engineering Support (HQ MES).

    History of HQ Maintenance and Engineering Support (HQ MES)

    HQ MES was first formed in Sep 1984 as HQ Maintenance. In Mar 1994, HQ MES was officially inaugurated as a SSSO for maintenance and engineering matters in the army. Units under MES are 3, 6, 9 Direct Support Maintenance Battalions (DSMB) and Ordnance Engineering Training Institute (OETI). HQ MES actively looks into harnessing IT to enhance her responsiveness and effectiveness. It is the first army formation to attain the prestigious ISO 9002 award in 1995, therefore it is committed to provide quality service to her customers.

    History of HQ Supply & Transport (HQ S&T)

    Before 1990, 4 logistic formations, namely: Supply, Transport, Maintenance and GS Command, were under the command & control of G4-Army. In the year 1990, the Supply formation and the Transport formation were integrated to become the Supply & Transport Formation and came under the command and control of HQ ALCOM. A reorganisation of the logistics formation was carried out in 1994 where HQ ALCOM was declared defunct while HQ Supply & Transport (S&T) and HQ Maintenance & Engineering Support (MES) were formed to come under the command of G4-Army.

    ELECTRONICS



    Technology is evolving at an accelerated pace. And Military Technology will always be on the leading edge.

    Making the best use of sophisticated defence technology is what gives our Army its edge over potential aggressors. Whether it's tanks, weapons, or communications and information systems, our Army relies on all these to maintain our ability to fight and win.

    That's where the Electronics Specialist comes in - by maintaining our electronics systems in perfect working order, you give us a crucial military advantage. Working on a wide range of high-tech equipment makes everyday a fascinating experience!

    So if you're an "electronics junkie", this is the right career for you!

    ARMAMENT



    What if the infantryman's rifle misfires in the heat of the battle? What are the consequences of a tank's turret gun systems malfunction during an attack?

    Why do these incidents occur?

    Join the Army as an Armament Engineering Specialist, and you'll help find out, and prevent them from happening again.

    You'll keep abreast of the latest development in armament science. You'll monitor field reports, test results, and see that all our weapons function at their peak. If technical problems arise, you're expected to quickly find a solution.

    You role is crucial because you make sure the Army's guns, both big and small, work as designed. After all, what would an Army be if it didn't have guns?

    AMMUNITION



    Ammunition provides our combat forces with the firepower to defend our interests. Therefore, our Army attaches great importance to its management.

    This makes you an integral player in the Army.

    As an Ammunition Engineering Specialist, you'll handle all kinds of ammunition and explosives, from tiny 9mm bullets to massive 155mm Howitzer rounds. You'll also act as a technical advisor on all ammunition surveillance, maintenance and safety matters.

    All the guns in the world aren't enough if they have no bullets to fire. And your specialised knowledge of ammunition and explosives makes sure that we have the means to back us up.

    AUTOMOTIVE



    Modern military vehicles are perfect examples of increasingly complex and sophisticated systems.

    As an Automotive Engineering Specialist, you'll be the master of some of the biggest and most powerful vehicles on wheels! Be it an all-terrain tank, a versatile land rover or a speedy motorcycle, you'll keep them in peak running condition.

    As military vehicles are so important in our modern Army, by maintaining them in top-running condition, you give the rest of the Army the power to move smoothly and quickly to our targets.

    There are 3 types of Automotive Specialists:

    Class A - Armoured Vehicles (eg. Bionix)
    Class B - Wheeled vehicles like 5-tonners, land rovers.
    Class C - Heavy equipment like bulldozers, bridging equipment.

    You will perform tasks from maintenance, quality servicing and inspections to higher level of engine repair, gearboxes and vehicle overhauling. You will also provide field support and repairs alongside the troops.

    As an Automotive Specialist, you may also be groomed to be Workshop IC, Platoon Sergeant, Company Sergeant Major (CSM) and other staff roles like Maintenance Management Warrant Officer or Project Warrant Officer.

    Consider yourself a "mechanical nut"? Well, here's your chance to live your dreams and more.

    As a Military Engineering Officer, you'll face challenges and solving problems that few are qualified to tackle.

    You'll lead your team in the forecasting of resource needs, as well as the acquisition and management of vital military resources, so that our Army can function like a well-oiled machine. At the forefront of technology, you'll know how to make the best use of sophisticated defence technology to give us the crucial military advantage.

    LOGISTICS OFFICER



    As the saying goes, "Good Logistics is Combat Power".

    Our Army needs outstanding Logistics Officers to ensure that it has the resources it needs to work efficiently.

    As a Logistics Officer, you'll learn to juggle multiple roles - as a Supply Chain Manager, Logistics Information Systems Manager and Resource Manager.

    You'll tackle complex logistics issues and ensure that essential support is delivered to the right place at the right time. Well-versed in Information Technology, you'll use your specialised knowledge to improve our Logistics systems. What's more, you'll be responsible for an inventory worth millions!

    On top of this, you'll be in charge of training your men, maintaining morale, and looking after their welfare. Using your management and leadership qualities, you'll ensure their readiness, and that they provide the crucial support that will help our forces accomplish their missions.

    A leadership role in one of the world's best logistic teams can be demanding and complex. As a reward, we offer excellent career and promotion opportunities that promise new challenges and an enviable lifestyle. Our first-class training will make you a highly valued member in any organisation.
