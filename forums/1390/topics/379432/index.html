    Let me just say this: you got hammered really good not because you asked questions. It was because of what questions you asked, and the mentality you displayed in asking those questions.

    Regardless of whatever you may think, the army experience is 25% physical and 75% mental. You can be at the peak of your fitness, but it all counts for nuts if your mindset is not in the right place. I've seen Rambo-fit fellas go kaput because of the wrong mindset. I've also seen Winnie the Poohs who came out stronger, fitter, smarter, and more mature than ever before because they had the right kind of mindset.

    In the previous thread, you told everyone that you were thinking of keng-ing, you were gloating about going to OCS and you were declaring that army had made us all more mature but not smarter. Had you stuck to that kind of mentality, you would have already lost 75% of the battle before even stepping foot into the army.

    All I can say about winning this 75% battle is this: have an open mind, and think positive. There is seriously no point in thinking about things like whether you are going to Guards Recon Platoon or so on when you haven't even received your formal enlistment date and location.

    Instead, just take it as it comes. Army life is what you choose to make of it. If you choose to think positive and strive to get something out of it, you will find that army is a worthwhile experience.

    Have an open mind, and remember this phrase: we are all in this together. Do not only think about yourself. Your platoon and section mates are all in there with you, and they are the only ones you can really depend on. You are also in this with all of us, for we are among the people who have walked the path thatyou are about to embark on.
