    I OOC during my first BMT due to a leg injury, but I was not downgraded or anything. After you OOC, you will still be post out (most likely) to another school to learn other trades. For me, I go OETI and become armourer aka gun repair man. If your hand consistently has problem, very likely you get posted to log school or other become clerk or PA. For me, I got back to BMT because I wanted to and had volunteered for it. But lots of my peers never went back at all.

    What are grounds for OOC? Previously if you were excused or light duty for more than 2 weeks, you are considered OOC.

    For your case, I thought it will be considered OOC already.

    Injured ligament is tricky. It is very hard to determine whether is it really grounds for downgrade and it all depends on whether the MO gives you the benefit of the doubt. I've seen many different cases where some got downgraded and some didn't.

    Even myself is suffering from damaged ligament at my left ankle. I previously saw a specialist and he didn't bother too much other than prescribed painkillers, but he did recommend me for temp downgrade. In any case I got downgraded for a bad fracture for my right index finger due to training accident, and all the more so this left foot injury was overlooked. It appears that sometimes leg injury you see SAF specialist is better than you see outside specialist.

    Till today, I suffer great discomfort if I walk too long or stand with too much weight. I guess that is something many of us have to live with.
