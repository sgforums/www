    Change parade has to be the absolute craziest. It was already banned during my time, but legends abound on how creative the tekaning could be - since that's precisely the end in itself. Now we could only get a glimpse of it in those Taiwanese "Reporting, Sir" film comedies ~lol~

    The swimming instructors during my Tekong days were very inventive too - lucky for me cuz I couldn't swim then. It was, like, 45 minutes of exhausting circuit training & 10 minutes's actual swimming for those who would have no problem passing the test. Not so much super-siong but all the complementary humiliation left the trainee disoriented: "Recruit, you so blur you must've pa chiao with your left hand instead of your right, yah?" "Yes, Instructor!"

    Those by PTI's & during range butt-parties came with the territory, I guess.

    The most sadistic has to be the UC instructors, though. Them buggers never hazed or shouted at you; they just conduct their training under the blazing sun (meaning your palms get burnt on the hot asphalt), or subject you to knuckle pushups (to beget the "required" calluses), or stretch you into very-painful postures during warm-up sessions. Even the officers cannot escape; it was the only occasion when I witnessed my CO involuntarily plead for mercy.
