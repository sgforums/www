    I found the following info online, from a "All-Terrain Personal Load Carrier" design competition by DSTA for Poly students back in 2001. Re-edited to fit the format here:-

    Conventionally, military personnel are given a backpack to contain their combat/field/personal supplies. Military personnel wear/carry these backpacks by slinging-around-and-over their shoulders. Items contained within the pack include:-

    Item / Quantity / Unit Wt (kg) / Total Wt (kg)

    Weapons
    1. SAR-21 / 1 / 3.98 / 3.98
    Ammo
    2. Ammunition magazines / 6 / 0.46 / 2.76
    3. Smoke grenade / 1 / 0.62 / 0.62
    4. Fragmentation grenade / 2 / 0.18 / 0.36

    Basic Combat Load
    5. Includes 2 water bottles, bayonet, toggle rope, entrenching tool, camouflage and the weight of & webbing & belt of the Skeletal Battle Order / 10.16 / 10.16

    SUB-TOTAL (Skeletal Battle Order) / 17.88

    6. Combat Ration (24h) / 1 / 2.00 / 2.00
    7. Standard Field Pack /1 / 7.19 / 7.19

    SUB-TOTAL / 9.19
    GRAND TOTAL (Full Battle Order) / 27.07

    *The Full Battle Order would include the Skeletal Battle Order, Combat Ration & Field Pack.

    *The Field Pack consists of a set of uniform, ground sheet, mess tin and toiletries.

    *The existing standard backpack measures 300 mm (width) x 400mm (height) x 200 mm (thickness) and weighs 9.19 kg (when packed full).

    http://www.dsta.gov.sg/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=1866&Itemid=401
