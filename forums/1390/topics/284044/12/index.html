    CPX = Command-Post Exercise. It's a form of "skeleton" training with basic equipment & only minimum personnel, usually conducted during all combat vocational courses & also without the main body at the unit itself. Can take place within camp environs (eg. parade square or auditorium) or in real outfield. Usually only day-long, so that can sent back stores by book-out time.

    Dunno what TCS means, though one online reference alludes to it as "signaller + driver." Guess it means the typical commander or trainer/controller Land Rover, lah. Both "zay chia" (sit vehicle), except one is tactical while the other can roll up canopy & the driver/signaller sleep a lot (i.e. whenever not on the move).

    "Bns" was the quoter's short-form for BN (battalion).
