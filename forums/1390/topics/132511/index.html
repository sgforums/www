    What makes a good Armament Specialist

    1.Understand the weapon system you specialise in.
    2.Knowledge Management is important, you and your team should always treat cases of weapon failure as a databank to learn from.
    3.Understand that your role is vital to combat service support for our frontline troops.
    4.Quality Assurance.A well-repaired weapon is better than a weapon half-fixed but rushed out for collection.
    5.If your duties are desk-bound, make sure maintenance reports are as accurate as possible, and if it is not, make sure your superiors know why.Sometimes, it's not your fault.
    6.Treat every damaged weapon with caution, you never know if there is a round unexploded in the chamber.
    7.At the scene of an investigation, make sure that no one oversteps your authority concerning weapon issues.
