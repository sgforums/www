    Wow, really?

    My Tekong BMT was long, long ago - almost 2 decades & counting - & they only recourse OOC cases due to injuries or illnesses back then.

    The official line was (is?) that recruits must attain "a certain standard of proficiency" in IPPT, SOC, marksmanship & swimming, while learning non-test skills like IFC, weapon-handling, grenade-throwing, bayonet-fighting & marching drills. He's arrowed to redo BMT only if he's missed out, for some reason, on an intolerable amount of those instructions/trainings.

    The DI's would push him very hard - even "scare you" - about the IPPT & SOC benchmarks in particular. But fail those tests, as well as for CTSS, & he'll likely just be assigned a less garang vocation (likely still combat & frontline, though) in those days, that's all. BMT was never meant as the be-all; he's going to run & shoot again with his active & reservist outfits.

    Thus I doubt the "fail-BMT altogether" threat, unless the SAF's changed the doctrine & has set aside enough money/logistics to facilitate a costly retraining endeavor like rifle marksmanship.

  • Si Geena
    4 Nov 04, 13:52
    Its pretty hard to fail the range.

    For those that fail it during the company range, they will most likely be "ta-baoed" (packaged food) to other companies do join them for the range. Basically, all the companies will try to help one another and pass their "ta-baos".

    So if you are lucky, your company is amongst the first to do range. In the condition that you fail, you will "ta-bao" to the next company for their range test. If you fail again, you join another company again. Simple.

    There are also what we call "conditional pass" for the BMT, i.e., we pass even though the trainee has failed 1 of the 3 critical units of IPPT, IFC, BTP. Most of these conditional passees come from the fail IPPT category. Fail BTP makes up a small category.

    In any event, magic rounds do the trick.

    Your instructors try their best to pass you. Nobody likes to look bad...
