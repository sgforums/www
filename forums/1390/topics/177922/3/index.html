    Let's just say I learnt many things, but given a choice, I don't think I would want to re-live it all over again.

    One important thing, to me, if you get along well with others, and never show your weakness or act ego/garang, you will be safe.

    Your unit may be either heaven or hell, but it's hard to get posted out.So, make the best of it and give thanks for the small incidents of blessings.

    You might ORD with the ability to spot backstabbers and also realise that when your 1st Sergeant is yelling at you, it's not because he hates your guts, but because you endangered everyone when you failed to clean up the oil that you spilled on the floor.

    Remember, in the Army, not everything is taken literally. Be wise and learn to filter out what people yell, and what they really mean.
