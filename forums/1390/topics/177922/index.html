    haha I just finished my BMT field camp, you can read about it in another topic.
    physically NS is very tolerable i guess. I guess it may be tough at times but definitely not over the limit. Why? there are always weaker guys than you.
    For me whenever I feel tired during route march I just look at the smaller guys in my platoon, those <50kg who are carrying the same damn field pack, and I just feel much more energetic.

    mentally? NS is definitely challenging. Gotta find your own motivation and direction and be independent. GOtta tell yourself it is worthwhile serving 2 years of shit while others continue their studies or work in higher paying jobs. gotta tell yourself ure doing it for the country. Gotta force yourself to do more so that your platoon mates can do less( or rather, do more even when your platoon mates decide to slack)

    But of course im not the most experienced here, but these are my thoughts from a month-old recruit. As for tekhaning and one-one screwing by commanders, just remember that tough times dont last, tough men do. The only serious enough punishment you get is Detention in DB. unless you kill someone or intentionally steal stuff esp millitary equipment or go AWOL, you wont kena, lah.

    I will be your best example.

    2 weeks before my enlistment, I started to worry about life in the army. Heard of tekaning, hardship through the BMT. Started to miss the hobbies I have done, my friends, family. Losing the freedom to go places I want.

    3 days before enlistment, I totally broke down. I started to lose interest in my hobbies, things i used to enjoy. I kept thinking of the life in army, fearing I am unable to cope with it.

    Things started to change during the enlistment there. You will see similar, worried faces beside you. Soon you will have friends who will walk through the hardship with you. YOu will get used to the army life within the 1st week. Nothing to be feared of.

    Soon I realise my fears and worries were totally redundant. It is not as hard as what you think. You still get free times to chat and play with your friends.
    I can assure you, training there is very real;istic and progressive....

    Just voice out your feeings, i can try my best to help you

    few tips for BMT

    always stay low profile

    dun be the first one to volunteer anything

    dun give suggestion

    dun try to gey kiang n try to motivate the whole platoon

    if they wan volunteers for guard duty, n they tell u everyone gotta do at least once, its bs, dun volunteer

    during pc/ps interview, dun bother to feedback abt any 3sg cos it aint going to work
    its jus for paper sake, u wan u go direct to ur CSM or Orientation Officer or SAF hotline if u really feel something is wrong

    bring lotsa lotsa food, cup noodles, cookies, these are prized possesions in tekong

    few tips on posted unit

    for the 1st half a yr, do the same also, try to be friendly to everyone, know who are the gd guys and bad guys, from then onwards, no need for us to teach u
    cos u be almost a "laojiao" soldier liao, u know how to play wif the system, or know how to play rank, and know who to go to overwrite ur upper rank

    when i say play rank, i dun mean u use ur rank to order pple to do ur thing, or to siam ur way out, i mean use ur rank to protect urself

    no need to go about carrying balls, jus try to be friendly, ur true friends will emerge, who knows ur gd friend will actually turn out to be the Commanding Officer of the unit (this is wad happen to my friend, CPL, but CO's best billard buddy)

    wen r new bird in anywhere, sure will get bully, dun worry, things will get better, everyone who gone thru NS sure will know

    tips for pre enlistees

    unless ur parents going to support u thru NS, pls save up lotsa n lotsa money, few hundred aint gonna get u by in singapore

    pls stop ur bad habits of betting soccer or wad, ur few hundred aint enuff to cover ur transportation or food

    if u have a gf, u gotta decide btween her and ur stay in unit. then work ur way towards it

    if u wan to be in a stay out unit, the preparation gotta start now, dun wait until tekong or wad den u start to regret
