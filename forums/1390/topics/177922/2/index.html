    I completely agree with Ah_Tee, this is exactly what i would tell anyone who wants to go through NS. The thing is the stupid guard duty thing. If u follow excatly what is written , u should not be doing any guard duty. Like me, i have never done any guard duty on tekong before and this is possible if u know how to stay low. A few more points to add on to the list by ah tee

    1. forget about makng bosom buddies in BMT.They are only with u for at most 3 months.after POP, everyone will scatter like sand and communication will be futile. This is when the bastards will appear to try to get OCS. If u wan to survive BMT, look out for ur own safety and fuck everyone else. If u go around like mel gibson in we were soldiers, i tell u, ur name will be the first for sispec and other nonsense. Stay as low as possible.

    2. Flunk ur SOC and SIT like mad but borderline pass ur ippt and marksman. If ur SOC is extremely fucked up, do u think they will send u to sispec where they train platoon leaders??at most u go SIR, but it is also quite lobo and not yet BMT. Of course if u go 2 sir then good luck to you.

    3. Make use of the SAF hotline and PC interview to let them know of ur present situation. Say things like u miss ur girlfriend and that u wish to be a medic where u can learn life -saving skills and etc..Express ur desire to learn things and life will be better.

    4. Be very careful of backstabbers. They can even be ur buddy so if they even try to play punk with you, play them back during the PC interview by saying that he wishes to go sispec or 2 sir. Be a bastard to those who bastard u discreetly and u can laugh when they get posted to sispec while u get posted to medic. the look on their faces was priceless. If u wan to sabo, sabo a few dun sabo the whole platoon

    5.The best friends are found in your unit, learn who ur true friends are and u will sail through smoothly.

    6. No matter what, still be careful of backstabbers, they are everywhere.

    A lot of garang guys here will say that i am kenning here, but honestly if what i am doing does not harm anyone else. why should it matter to you? Survival is imporatant and it is during ur NS where it prepares u for society. NS makes u a man by making u used to shit in society. Take note of what is said and good luck to ur NS!

    12 Feb 06, 22:39
    MANY people have gone through NS successfully. if it had been a treacherous venture, how would that have been possible?

    it is unfortunate (but not unexpected) that only those with the more negative and vehement opinions of NS voice them out - those who are neutral or have positive experiences tend not to.

    NS, is, after all, a short duration in your life; a time when you are supposed to do something that you may or may not want to, with a different groups of strangers, and the things may be tough on both the mind and the body, which, again, may or may not be common stresses for youngsters at the age. with this suite of stressors coming in a package, plus the fact in my second para, it is no wonder that youngsters are anxious about NS.

    but as we can distill what NS is all about, one can be assured that with the right attitude and outlook, one will complete it with not much difficulty. and it can even serve to provide some lessons for working with big groups of strangers in challenging tasks too, and others, too.

    which is why people say youngsters mature with NS. it can serve as a test of character if taken in positive light.
