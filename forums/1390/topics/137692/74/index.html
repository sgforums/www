    Just wondering is there cases of going to early, maybe a day or 2, for enlistment? what will happen?

  • Audiophile
    27 Jan 10, 11:01

    @tarutaru, I am previously from BTMC as a commander. 

    Anyway, there are cases where people enlist early. Mostly it is because there has been a mistake on the part of the administration of enlistment or the soldier was re-posted to another company but the notification cannot be sent to him.

    If this is the case, then, they will still host the recruit in whichever of the companies that can accomodate him, usually a company that is having an intake on that day. The recruit will stay there and then be sent to his company when that company finally has an intake.

    However, if you make a mistake on your own part and misread the enlistment letter, they will probably turn you away at the ferry terminal, as there is a check there and there are computers and all to register you.

    b00mz, Sorry, just read your post again.

    If you only got REGISTRATION letter, first up is the mdical examination.

    They'll draw blood, check your height, weight, hearing, eyesight, take an x ray and make you cough to see how your balls react under coughing conditions. 

    Next, would be the MAPAS test. I don't really know what is its exact purpose cos specifications on it are Secret probably. But it is obviously able to measure your IQ. And many speculate that it can give your aptitude for various vocations. People believe that through th MAPAS test SAF will chart your entire path throughout NS. Like if you are qualified for command school or which vocation and specifically if you have the mindset to go into commandos or the Naval Diving Unit.

    And you can't really cheat on the MAPAS test, cos if the results are inconsistent, they will ask you to come by and take it again. Sometimes as late as in BMT. But usually before.

    Just do it as per normal, as it is really tedious and not worth doing a second time.

    At the end of it, you will know your PES status and you have to wait for a letter telling you of your enlistment.

    The mapas test won't tell you the result. But as it is, to be a clerk, you need to be pes c, d or e. Though there are some rare pes a and b personnel getting clerk positions.

    And noone outside the SAF's Applied Behavioural Science Department knows how the MAPAS test works. So there's no real way that you can cheat your way to get a clerk result.

    Aside from the obvious IQ questions, there are some random questions where there are no real right or wrong.
