    www.mindef.gov.sg/imindef/publications/cyberpioneer/news/2009/January/08jan09_news.html

    Pioneer batch of SAF recruits in new combat uniform
    Posted: 08 Jan 2009, 1030 hours (Time is GMT +8 hours)
    Report by Gail Wan
    Over 1,000 fresh recruits from the Singapore Armed Forces (SAF) Basic Military Training Centre donned the new digitally-pixelised camouflage uniforms as they booked out from Pulau Tekong Camp on 24 Dec 2008.

    They are among the first SAF servicemen to wear the new No. 4 combat fatigues, that aims to increase the survivability of the soldiers in varying terrain.

    The Army collaborated with the Defence Science & Technology Agency to develop an enhanced camouflage pattern which generates a 'quivering' effect that reduces the eye's ability to discern recognisable shapes.

    The creation of a unique micro-pattern within the larger macro-pattern allows the soldier to better conceal himself at both short and long distances.

    The new uniform material is also treated with Near Infra-Red (NIR) capabilities so the pixelised design loses none of its efficacy when viewed through night-vision devices. In fact, it is more effective at night.

    Besides the improved camouflage effect, the new uniform fabric is highly permeable and water-absorbent, enabling better air flow and greater heat dissipation. It also dries at double the rate of the old uniform.

    To ensure a smooth operational entry of the new uniform into service, they will be phased in over the next three years, with recruits, active servicemen and some Operationally Ready National Servicemen (NSmen) already receiving them.
