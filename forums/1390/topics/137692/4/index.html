    18 Sep 05, 02:30
    Originally posted by bent:
    can someone describe a typical day in life for a recruit in BMT?

    e.g. wat time wake up up, the activities, the lights out?
    Enlistment Day: Loads of Admin stuff: Forms to fill up...etc.

    2 weeks confinement: Friendly commanders.
    After your 1st bookout, things changes.
    1st bookout less time to enjoy since after you book in is Fieldcamp, some things to buy from Beach Road Army Market (such as L-Torch, Range Card, China Graph, a Black Trash Bag, 4 Umbrella Pins, Comms Cord...etc.)

    Then,
    80% of the time is:

    Wake up (Reville) @ 0530
    Lights out @ 2230

    Activities: Exercises, Exercises, Exercises, Lectures, Punishments, Footdrills, Clean Rifle, 'Heads out', AGR, 30:60

    New Warm-Up introduced : Combat PT

    ------------------------------------------------------------------------
    Route Marches (3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 16, 24) [km]
    Field Camp
    Situational Test
    Range
    Hand Grenade Throw (HG)
    Basic Assault Course (BAC)
    Basic Close Combat Training (BCCT)
    Battle Innoculation Course (BAC)
    Individual FieldCraft (IFC)
    Standard Obstacles Course (SOC) (Lessons + Rundown)
    IPPT Diagnostics, IPPT Tests
    Some Personal Admin Time (Free Time)
    Guard Duties (see if you're lucky: Weekday or Weekend)
    Report Sick

    Before POP: Games Day, Recruits Night, OC Night, POP Rehearsal Parades

    PS: Most people only bathe once a day (after RO at night). Fieldcamp and SIT Test: Snake Powder Baths.

    Originally posted by LazerLordz:
    C'mon..spill the beans What exactly is AGR and 30:60?
    dont tell u

    ok lah, dont cry

    agr stands for ability grp run
    based on the 2.4km running times, recruits are grouped into 4 categories and made to run 4 laps of 400m(i think, either that or 4 x300m) within a given amt of time.

    how much time each grp is given is based on how many points they get for 2.4km

    eg
    those who can attain 2+ points and abv are led by a PTI and given only 9mins ( not sure abt the times)

    those who fail by 10-20secs are given slightly more time

    those who fail by 2-3mins( clocking ~13mins) are given up to 11-12mins to run.

    30:60 is a stamina training run(havent taken part in it as i was on att b status)

    either u run for 30s then slow jog for 60s then repeat for 20-30mins...
