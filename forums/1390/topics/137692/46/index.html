    Originally posted by brdn:

    hello, i was prescribed xanax to take by my psychiatrist after my medical and psyc check up (which i got assigned c1l3 for), so the saf doesn't know that i'm on medication now. i'm enlisting into bmt soon and i was wondering if i have to declare it/if i will be able to take it during bmt as it causes drowsiness. thanks!

    Yes,please do declare it.


    Bring a memo from the doctor who prescribed you your medicine along with your medicine on your first day.

    Your PC will conduct an interview on the very first day of your enlistment...or at least the next day to find out about your current condition.When you go for it,please sound out your medical condition and your need for the medication.Emphasize to your PC that the medication will cause drowsiness.

    They will most probably ask you to bring along your memo and medicine to the medical centre for the MO to see.From there,the MO will advise your commanders as to how best to go about it.

  • baikachuchu
    28 Jan 09, 03:16
    Originally posted by brdn:

    hi, thanks for the reply. however, i don't have a doctor's memo as my psychiatrist didn't write me one. what should i do then?

     


    If there is still some time before you enlist,say about a month or so,try calling up MCC @CMPB at 63731340/1341 to inform them about your medication and ask them directly if you can bring along your medication or what's the procedure you need to go through.

    If you do not have time to contact them,then get your psychiatrist to write a memo about your current condition and the need for your medication.Tell him/her that it's for the army and they will understand.

    I recommend contacting CMPB since they already graded you based on your condition.Keep your psychiatrist's contact details on hand when you call them as they might ask you for it so that they can refer to him/her about your condition if they need to.

    Remember to sound out about your medication during your first day of BMT too even if you were given the all clear to bring along your medicine before that.
