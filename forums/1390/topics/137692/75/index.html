    Originally posted by supremebull:

    Just helping my brother ask...

    If he misses the 24km route march, will he still be regarded as having completed or passed BMT? Also, is the 24km route march a veto factor for consideration into command school?

    Hi supremebull, I was previously a commander in BMTC.

    To get a conditional pass for BMT, the trainee simply needs to get 2 of these 3 things.

    1) Attend Field camp

    2) Attend Range

    3) Pass IPPT.

    Passing IPPT is also a requirement to attend Command School. If he fails any component like IPPT or not participate in fieldcamp or route march, he may be called back to do these components during the block leave. Though this is not necessary. 

    He can simply get 2 of the 3 to get a conditional pass. Nothwithstanding, the trainee is still subject to the recommendation of his Platoon Commander and Officer Commanding as to whether he deserves to get a condittional pass.

    A conditional pass will make the service man be exempt from re-bmt.

    The outstanding training things that the trainee has missed will be convered again in the next unit or command school. Especially command School.

     

    Lastly, with respect to what ForbiddenSinner said, it is untrue as far as i know.I have known recruits to miss 24km AND 16km route march and still go to OCS and SISPEC. Though it is recommended that he does try to the best of his ability to participate. If he is excused medically then that is ok. It should not affect the commander's appraisal of the trainee. 

     

    SO in conclusion. Can miss 24 km, pass BMT and go Command School.

    Originally posted by Forbiddensinner:

    "As for your 2nd question, if he fails to complete 24km due to severe heat injuries and such, I will say that it will affect his chances getting into command school."

    Its not that i want to argue. But medical reasons will only be reflected in the trainee's FFI. 24km is usually after the FFI. 

    Usually even if a trainee has a likelihood of heat injuries, he will only be tagged for closer observation. If it is really severe, his PES status will not be A or B as needed for Command School (unless he is signing on for a Logistic Officer regular and is approved).

    I have complied documentation for my trainees at the end of the course, there is no place to indicate such details except in the FFI which is handled by a medical professional i.e. Medical Officers, not commanders.
