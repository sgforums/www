    17 Mar 08, 11:54

    I don’t know how true is this guy’s story because I do not know him personally.

     

    I was an ex regular Sgt in the SAF for 6 years. ORD for 3 years already. Was offered to extend my contract many times. But till this day I have not a single day regretted the decision to leave.

     

    I was swindled into SAF thinking I can become technically trained and exposed to this aspect when they came up with the Combat-Tech Scheme. Being able to cross train definitely appeal to the mass recruits as it will serve as a leverage for the recruit at the end of the contract. But merely 3 years later the ‘terms and conditions are subjected to change’ took place and somehow the scheme was scrapped. The premium plan replaced the JPSDS and of course it didn’t appeal to me. Those who wanted to continue may convert to the Premium Plan and stay for 10 years but those who do not want will continue as a pure combatant and leave on the 6th year.

    While working in the SAF, I encountered many other more nonsense. But a BG who took the efforts & stood up for his men. Because of his timely intervention, I was spared with further nonsense from the SAF so called ‘code of conduct’. Got converted to become a technician and this 3 years plus of exposure in this industry helped to form the foundation of the breadwinning career I have today in a German MNC. Up till today, I still keep in contact with the BG who left the SAF also around my time.

     

    Everyone in the SAF is pissed with the system. But unfortunately, what the SAF we see on TV are attributed to the selfless sacrifice of our comrades. And these sacrifice are sometimes very severe.

     

    In short, I can only describe the SAF management philosophy as ‘dirty politics’. And there have been countless of people who were victimized by this underhanded management style and naturally many people who attempted to test the system ended up getting what they deserve.
