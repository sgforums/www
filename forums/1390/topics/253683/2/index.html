    Your concern is with "A" and "B" Veh, and I definitely agree with you.

    Mine's with armament systems, which makes it more vital that our tech specialists have the rank to stand on equal footing, or at least possess adequate authority to argue their case if a weapon is unfit for deployment.

    Furthermore, weapon certification and ammo issues are always , eventually decided by NSFs.

    The MSCC should be tweaked into a PS-level course instead. There's no gain to be had to reduce the authority of armament specialists when their intake is usually soldiers with some mild medical problems that already make them non-combatants in the first place.

    The issue of using a combatant-related ranking system to apply to technical vocations is a simplistic look at a very large maintanence planning and enforcement issue.
