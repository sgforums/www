  • blitzonic
    24 Dec 07, 15:06
    Don't worry about the physical aspect. You'll continue to get to exercise regularly en masse at the unit -- for, what, the next year & a half? The tekan shouldn't be as great as in BMT anymore, though your commander may now & then improvise extra PT lessons for those less fit. I know, cuz I kena arrowed extra SOC every morning, when everyone was doing area-cleaning! But the purpose always fizzled out whenever the unit had other operational priorities. Still, my timing cut down to 8:06 at the end (from BMT failure, mind you, cuz I'd never tried the monkey bars in my life before).

    Chin-ups, you must get into a habit of training during your free time, over & above what everyone do at the pre-cookhouse rituals. Cultivating the self-discipline won't be easy, cuz there's a lot more autonomy & regular nights off in unit life.

    If you've only just finished BMT, then you're likely only attending trade course at Stagmont Camp. Thereafter, you can be posted almost anywhere, even to a GDS unit if your Pes status qualifies. Or sit vehicle all the time, if lucky. Signaller is quite a ubiquitous vocation, though course time can be very academic & technical.
