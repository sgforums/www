  • Moxie
    13 Sep 04, 17:32
    1. Rain. I'm serious.

    2. Mosquitos. Hence, powder baths & insect-repellent application before sleeping.

    3. The cold. You'll experience it even in Singapore, when out in the field. It usually hits at about 4 or 4:30 in the morning, when daily temperatures reach the lowest. You'll usually wake up needing to piss as well, given how your body's self-heating process creates a full bladder.

    3. Scorpions. Snakes aren't a big problem, maybe in Brunei but then you're taught to spread sulpher around your sleeping-area circumference prior. But many soldiers like to remove boots & let their feet "breathe" at night when sleeping, & in turn scorpions like to climb into the footwear due to the warmth inside. Bites can & do occur locally, when disoriented or sleepy guys forget the checking drill when waking up for stand-to or night-guard, etc.

    Notes from an old soldier...

    No the forest/ jungle does not scare anyone at all. The first night out, you will go to sleep hearing strange noises from insects (mosquitos most common !!!) and the occassional animal rustling the dead leaves on the ground. You are mostly too tired to worry about wild animals/ ghosts/ whatever. Even if you have room in your mind to think about other things, you will mostly think of:-

    1) What yr girlfriend is doing at this time in civvy life
    2) whether your PC or PS or Sect Comdr will come around and steal something of yours to test your alertness
    3) Guard/ sentry / OP duty shifts ... depending on the reason you are outfield... training or exercise/ mission.

    Just bear in mind that it is cold in the early morning, when the temperature can dip to 22 deg. and the morning dew can wet your clothes.
