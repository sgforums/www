    First thing, first. Are you a NSF, regular or NSmen?

    I dont think you will be compensated for the case, unless you are an NSmen?

    If you are a NSF or regular, you rest, operate and rest until you are cured. Even my regular friend who broke his spine during an air jump accident. He was paid like a few hundred dollars from the SAF. He rested, operated, downgraded and then changed a new vocation. 

    For NS men, it is your own insurance that is paying you (provided your insurance cover military activites. That includes you get choked by a fish bone in the camp).The SAF insurance would only pay you if you need to use your own limb for work. For an accident plan, it would be medical bills + months of not working only. 

    For your case, you will just get operated and sew back the ligaments together. I don't think there would be compensation for your temporany disability.
