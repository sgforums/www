    SAF runs security drill for standby unit

    By David Boey
    Defence Correspondent

    WITH the holiday season in full swing, the Singapore Armed Forces (SAF) remains on the alert for possible terrorist threats in the run-up to the New Year.

    The SAF activated its standby force of army soldiers yesterday morning in a homeland security readiness exercise on Jurong Island.

    Though it was only an exercise, live ammunition was issued to standby force soldiers 'to deal with any possible incidents', said a Ministry of Defence statement.

    The army has a 'standby force' of soldiers armed and ready to move into battle 24 hours a day, every day.

    During the exercise, standby force personnel provided reinforcement to soldiers from the Sixth Battalion, Singapore Infantry Regiment (6 SIR) - the SAF's first 'protection of installations' battalion.

    The additional firepower was evident from the heavy presence of troops around the island as armed soldiers went on vehicle patrols and prowled the fenced borders of petrochemical and chemical plants on the island.

    Such drills are often held to test the army's vigilance and operational readiness to deal with homeland security situations, said Mindef.

    Major Kong Kam Yean, 6 SIR's commanding officer, said: 'In today's climate, the army places a lot of emphasis on homeland security threats.'

    The exercise allowed for better planning and interaction in deploying the additional force to beef up security on Jurong Island, he added.

    In yesterday's readiness exercise, troops simulated protection of industrial facilities on Jurong Island against possible attack by saboteurs.

    Mindef said soldiers were responding to a scenario where a 'terrorist group' was planning to make a 'forced entry to blow up an unspecified chemical plant'.

    It added that increasing patrols and surveillance at key installations would help 'deter and prevent' terrorist groups from 'carrying out their intended tasks'.
