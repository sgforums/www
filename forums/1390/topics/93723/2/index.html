    Actually, I consider Air Defence Arty to be a good gig if you can get in there. They've now renamed it DA Battalion, but as the former SADA it was considered "combat", albeit physically "2nd-line of defence combat"* - but also too operational-ready to be lumped together with the more generic PDF forces.

    One viewpoint then was that they get the soldiers who're legitimately combat-fit, but lacks the ideal physical characteristics for army delpoyment one way or another. But educational qualifications also play a big factor, given the sophisticated weapons (SAMs, radar, AA guns) they have to operate.

    They also have these FDS's (Field Defence Squadrons) who're basically infantry troops protecting the air bases, though I'm not sure how they're presently organized with conscripts. It used to be that some PDF reservists were redeployed there.

    Overall, it's comparatively a small force - only 2 "active" DA Battalions if I'm correct - & thus may not recruit NSF's en masse if they replenish via the ORD method.

    *Not really true, cuz their standby status is supposedly very super-on for everyone (inc. reservists) due to the aerial theatre they're expected to war in & also due to the sensitiveness of their equipment. But I heard that life isn't as physically exhausting for them when compared to army.
