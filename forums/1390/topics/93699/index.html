    From www.miw.com.sg

    What if I fail my IPPT within my 1-year window?Â 

    A:
    NSmen who failed to pass their IPPT at the end of their 1-year window will have to book for a 8-week Remedial Training Program within 2 months after the closure of their IPPT window. The booking system is the same as the IPPT Booking System. Failure to book for RT within the specified duration may result in the NSman being disciplinary dealt with for Absence Without Official Leave (AWOL).

    The RT will be conducted at the same 3 FCCs, i.e. Khatib, Bedok and Maju Camps. The 8-week non-residential RT program comprises of one weekday and one weekend session weekly.

    IPPT will be conducted at monthly intervals at the 8th and 16th sessions. NSmen who pass their IPPT are immediately discharged from the RT regime. Only IPPT Passes will be taken as the IPPT result for the current IPPT window. The NSmen will also be entitled for monetary incentives during the RT IPPT if they meet the required standards.

    Attendance Attendance for all RT sessions is compulsory. NSmen who are absent from any RT session without a valid reason will be summarily tried for AWOL and given a make-up training for the session. Absentees are liable to be fined or sentenced to detention.

    Make-Up Training NSmen who are unable to attend a scheduled session regardless of the reason will be required to attend a RT make-up session the following week, e.g. NSman who misses a weekday session will have to attend 2 weekday session and 1 weekend session the following week.

    Deferment Applications for deferment from RT are to be sent to your parent unit at least 3 weeks before the commencement of RT programme.

    We hope that the above answer meets your queries.

    Should you require further clarification, please contact MINDEF eServices Centre at 1800-3646333. Alternatively, you may also drop us an email at esc@miw.com.sg.

    Did the letter inform you that you're attending a summary trial or court martial?

    Maybe events haven't reached the trial stage. You're alleged to have committed an offense - that much for sure - but the unit CO has to conduct internal investigations before he can decide on disciplinary actions. For appropriate review of evidence, he needs to hear your viewpoint - & mitigating factors (*hint*).

    I have no idea how formal this preliminary hearing is like but would advise you to be honest. Maybe he'll let you off with a sober warning & another chance; he's a citizen soldier himself & has no underlying motive to mess up your personal life/career.

    If a summary trial is convened, it's incidentally not as big a deal as a court martial, but note that the disciplinary officer can still sentence you to up to 40 days in DB &/or a fine. You'd better read up the SAF Act - esp. the provisions in Part III ("Military Offences") & Part IV ("Summary Trial by Disciplinary Officers") for an idea of what you'll be formally in for.

    To clarify further, the "LOCATION" address on the letter isn't at the SAF Court-Martial Centre, isn't it? It's either at your reservist camp or at one of the Formation units - correct?

    If so, then I think you'll kena a summary trial, with the matter to be resolved "internally" rather than outsourced to the Court Martial Centre (which involves a real life district judge-cum-NSman). Don't quote me, but I don't think you'll be formally charged as AWOL either. Instead, it'll probably be along the lines of "failing to attend IPPT" or equivalently more minor - esp. since the latter offence is fairly frequent with reservists.

    Prepare a persuasive mitigation - work overload, family burdens, recent injury, overseas trip, et. al. - with preferably documentary evidence & then be mightily penitent on the day. Maybe they'll hear your plea even before convening the trial; you'll hopefully cop a fine (or just a formal warning) as a resolution. Whether it's $50 or more will depend on the weightage of the evidence, your military rank (higher rank = bigger punishment) &, I guess, your disciplinary officer's "kind" heart as well. He has the discretion powers; the SAF rules decree only "summary trial & then redo RT" for IPPT offenders.

    Cheers.
