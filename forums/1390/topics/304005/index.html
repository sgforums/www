    Google for Heng Chun (or Hengchun) Camp. Plenty of feedback of the ATEC experiences there. Even found a detailed blog of the infantry exercises conducted: http://hongjun.blogspot.com/2005/09/roc-training_15.html

    BTW, ATEC itself traditionally takes place in Kenting (almost on the southernmost tip of Taiwan). Hengchun town is listed as 7km away, according to one online source. If you get to R&R, it'll probably in Kaohsiung or Tainan at most. Don't hope for Taipei, unless you're with Arty ~lol~

    The chore of the advanced party as described sounds like something what my reservist unit once did, which was to "movement south" a whole BN of 155m howitzers to support an infantry EX. Bladdy journey took a whole day from Touliu (middle of Taiwan), quite taxing for the drivers who had to drive continuously for 3-hour stretches mostly on expressways & in strict convoy at that. Then they must negotiate the off-road tracks & the mountainous terrain is quite something else. So be careful either way, esp. if there's heavy rain & winds at the same time (typhoon-related).

    The camps themselves were quite spartan in my time, compared to those in S'pore. They'd even practiced water-rationing then, though it wasn't as bad as in Brunei. Yet IMO the S'pore part of the camps was quite "atas" compared to how the Taiwanese NSFs were living -- most vividly contrasted (for me) being "our" individual shower cubicles vs. "their" dank public baths (everyone scoop water from the same trough like you see in their old military comedy films).
