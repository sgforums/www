    poly student can go into OCS. chances rather high in fact.

    but then, officer no big deal.

    really.

    no i am not a spec. i am a sign-on officer, currently still an officer cadet.

    the best officers are those who don't pull rank and don't push over those specs below them. as an officer, you have to earn your respect, not demand it (which sadly, some actually demand). are you really willing to serve in order to lead? that's the only question that i pose to you. not saying that you are not good enough for ocs, but the mentality needs a slight tweaking.

    i can't blame the "officer-better-than-spec" impression which ppl have. on the contrary, i say both can excel equally. in fact, i respect certain specs and warrant officers more than some officers.

    all you need is the mindset that "i want to be an officer because i believe in leading by example. i want to influence others", not because there's more prestige or pride involved.

    because at the end of the day, at the battlefront, your officer-ship, or the lack of it, is not going to confer you bullet immunity.

    as long as you have the correct mindset and excel in the tasks required of you, you will have one foot inside OCS.

    good luck.
