    I think yours is a still-evolutionary one. The version I learnt in the mid-'80s was mostly improvisional, built around the same 4-line stanza with the 3rd & 4th lines repeated.

    For the latter, I sung it in Tekong as "Hey-ho, AH! In-fan-try, AH! / Life is siong in In-fan-try, AH!" When I got posted to my Formation, we obviously changed it to "Artillery, AH!" though, with my unit's subsequent mono-intake, I taught my recruits to sing it as "B-M-T, AH!"

    The opening 2 lines could be about any gripe, but always beginning with "Got no time to ..." & didn't necessarily have to rhyme. Usually sung during road marches, though we also sung it during 3-tonner rides (to keep all awake) & during the more-primitive Tekong booking out (lots of waiting around). Usually each peng took turns to come up with 2 lines & the others then echoed him, which could make for frequent giggles when the wit - or low-brow jibes - sunk in.
