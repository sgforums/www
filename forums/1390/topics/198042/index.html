    The current 7 men section was a result of shortages in manpower.

    Prior to that, the batch before my NS intake had 9 man sections. Also each MG team had 3 men, and each 84mm crew consisted of 3 men. The battation had 4 front line companies plus the support and HQ companies.

    There will always be pros and cons a smaller unit. With a larger unit, your first man reaches the FUP but your last man is still 1KM behind for example. With a smaller unit, movement is faster but there is a general shortage of manpower.

    Doctrine is also different. The US Marines with more manpower is expected to cover more ground or frontage width. For us I guess, its a smaller area, or covered at a brigade level for larger areas.

    I always thought that the SAF would mechanise the infantry and guards units with basic APCs - considering the small 7 men section. In general, only mechanised or armoured infantry units are found with smaller 7-8 man sections around the world. I think only Finland (??) has the same 7 man sections for infantry units. Until now, our infantry and guards are mainly foot infantry with some soft skin vehicles.

    I've been on exercise with the US Marines in tekong a long time ago - and yes they sometimes do fire and movement at whole section or whole platoon level.
