    I don't think so, not to my knowledge. Certainly not via the bureaucratic-promotion method, though I do recall this NCO who got an on-the-spot "battlefield" commission during the Hotel New World Collapse (for his ad-hoc manpower organization on the ground). It was mentioned in the papers.

    MINDEF seems to have concrete plans to expand the capacity of the WOSE Corps though, under the 3G Army umbrella. From their website:

    "Over the years, the job responsibilities of the WOSEs have expanded beyond the traditional duties of regimentation and discipline. WOSEs are instrumental in implementing policies set out by MINDEF, and are involved in areas such as manpower, logistics, intelligence, training and operations. Today, many WOs are holding appointments previously held by commissioned officers."

    That was 2001. Since then, it's introduced a new career structure & remuneration package under the the New Partnership scheme, promoted a bunch of them to SWO's & - just recently - broke ground to build the new SWI next to SAFTI MI (& which will succeed the existing SISPEC & SAF Warrant Officers' School). They're going to take the professionalism to another level; maybe it'll teach staff-officer type courses for its own.

    But, no, I don't believe there's any actual commissioning "conversion" yet.
