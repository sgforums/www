    I learn this from my Brunei Military Police friends...

    First get 2 brand new kiwi.

    Use your finger and DIG a CHUNK of kiwi on to your boots. Then spread it over the surface. Then use your Ring Finger and massage the kiwi chunk onto the surface. Do it in circular motion. Do it till you cant see the grain of the leather.
    The boot will become MATTE BLACK.

    Then let it dry... Do the same for the next boot.

    After that, open your NEW kiwi. Use a soft cloth or orange cloth, dab the top of the kiwi. Your objective is to remove the top shiny oil covering the kiwi. Then rub it on to your dried kiwied boots...

    Rub till it shines.

    Try not to stare at your boots so long as the shine might cause temporary blindness. Use a pair of shades or goggles if you need to !!

    Alternatively, you can try the method used by the British Household Guards.

    "For those looking for the ultimate bulled toecap finish, beeswax still provides the best possible foundation for all commercially available black/tan shoe polishes. This 63 g bar of guaranteed pure yellow beeswax is the ideal first stage. For those new to using beeswax, we recommend the following basic approach - Step 1: Pack wet newspaper into the inside of the boot; Step 2: Heat the toe cap with a hot hairdryer or similar (be partcularly careful if using naked flames etc.); Step 3: While the leather is still warm (but not too hot!) rub two or more coats of beeswax into the toecap; Step 4: Polish away excess beeswax using your 'on' brush; Step 5: Before the leather has cooled, use your finger to apply your choice of black/tan shoe polish to the toecap; Step 6: Continue to bull as normal. "
