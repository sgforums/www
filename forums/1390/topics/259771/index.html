    9 Jun 07, 18:31
    oh i know about this!

    basically its really up to demand. they need about 20 every 1 or 2 years. to replace instructors that ORD. and not every batch they conduct the selection. it just so happens that during my batch, they decide they'll scout for 20 new candidates.

    im in this april's bmt batch and they were auditioning for instructors and i went for it too!

    basically i dont think they need high qualifications of martial arts, though it'll really help.

    in auditions, they dont ask for much in technique wise. i think all they want to see is aggressiveness which is really really dumb. all you have to do is shout at the top of your lungs and throw moves that are really aggressive. ie: you do 720 hook kick perfectly also they dont care. you not aggressive enough, you fail.

    they also look out for more practical moves, instead of fancy taekwondo kicks which are more common in traditional styles of martial arts. in simple terms, street fight.

    the camp is the same as the PTI. first 3 months of training, stay-in. whole day do martial arts training, which includes weapon training. end of day sure whole body bruise. after 3 months will be dispatched to camps to train people in bcct icct acct, 8-5 job, just like pti. rank will be corporal.

    but hey, if you're trained in taekwondo like me, you wont like it cause they train and teach in no.4 pants and boots. and you'll soon realise how annoying it is to have 2 heavy boots and stuffy pants restricting your execution of kicks

    hope that helps!
