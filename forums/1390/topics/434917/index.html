  • Papayaman
    26 Jul 11, 22:31
    I am a regular now and have many friends who have gone through different education routes in their SAF career. Let me map out some of the routes you can take as a diploma holder thinking of signing on. Route 1: Sign on as a diploma holder as serve as a officer with diploma as your highest qualification. You will be placed into scheme A, which is the lowest paying officer scheme. I'm not sure how long the bond is, you can check it out online or call mindef recruitment. Route 2: Sign on as a diploma holder and get into a recognized local university, NUS, NTU or SMU, or any recognized overseas universities, Australia, US, UK or Canada etc. As long as you get a place in any recognized local or overseas universities, mindef will give you the Local Study Award (LSA) which provides you with school fees. Mindef will only give you the amount equivalent to local university rates, so if you're thinking of studying overseas, you'll have to top up with your own money. The LSA bond is 4 years. Route 3: Sign on as a diploma holder. If you did well in poly, you can try for the SAF Academic Scholarship (SAS) Local or Overseas. SAF will then pay for your university. If you get the SAS Local and want to study overseas, you'll have to top up like the LSA. If you get the SAS Overseas, then Mindef will pay your full school fees for an overseas unviersity and give you monthly pay. The bond for SAS is 6 years may it be local or overseas. Route 4: If you do extremely well in Poly, you can try to get the SAF Merit Scholarship (SMS). This is more difficult to get. if you want to know more about it you can ask. Hope this helps!
  • Papayaman

    Oops i forgot to add. If you decide to sign on with your diploma without going to get your degree, you'll be placed into Scheme A. If you have a degree from any recognized university both local or overseas, you'll be automatically be placed in Scheme B. If you get the SAS Overseas, SMS or SAFOS scholarships, you can go for an interview to try to get into Scheme C. If you fail the interview, you'll be placed into Scheme B.
