    19 Sep 08, 16:04

    Haha get ready for one of the most shiong frontline combat vocations in the SAF.

    Which unit? AFAIK most likely 42 since they should have just completed their BMT phase....

    I'm not sure of the exact difference between Armoured Infantry and Infantry in the SIRs now because of the mechanization going on over in the SIRs units now....

    Yes you will be an infantryman, but one that gets to sit (and of course, sleep) in the BX. You'll need to dismount and walk and bash through the jungle like an infantryman if need be though.

    When you go to your new unit, join your new company, meet your new platoon mates, there are a few options for you. You can be a BX driver, just a normal infantry trooper or a BX gunner. BX drivers will go for their driving course while the troopers and the gunners will go for their trades course (i.e. learn weapons and what-not). After trades course you will receive your black beret and will continue on to infantry training (like fighting in a section and a platoon) and after infantry training, armoured infantry training. Usually BX gunners have it good since they do not need to dismount and walk like the troopers.

    Men life, should be 5 day work week. Friday book out Sunday night book in. Most probably shouldn't have first week confinement unless your unit is damn screwed up. Bring all your issued items when you report there for there will most likely be route marches, and you will need a stand-by condition field pack (i.e. packed for field camp/route march) anyway.

    Compared to infantrymen, less xiong outfield but more xiong admin, since you will need to help your driver and your vehicle commander (VC) take care of the vehicle and all the shit that comes with it (i.e. equipment, maintenance etc.). After outfield exercises....be prepared to work overnight and not sleep until vehicle is settled.

    Good luck...heard that your unit may be going to India and to Australia next year.
