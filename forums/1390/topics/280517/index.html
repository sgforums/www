    If anything, duty personnel used these duties to catch up on their rest in my time ~lol~

    Particularly on weekends & public holidays, when my unit COSs & clerks would regularly bring down their mattresses, portable burners & even TV sets down to the office. Those doing guard-duty were less comfortable but no worse off: "do two hours, sleep four hours", loh! By nighttime, we were more than adequately rested for the duty shifts.

    Even the weekday "day" camp guards got reasonable slack time. Sure, doing sentry was dull as hell, but it's physically effortless. Off-duty, you're free to nap all you wanted in the guardroom bunk too.

    IMO Things are only siong for the guard commander & 2IC, cuz there's only two of them to rotate over the 24-hour stretch. (In contrast, the ordinary guards usually rotated in 3 details.) Their sleep was further disrupted cuz the GC had to physically walk around the camp once past midnight (to check on the night prowlers), & the 2IC must stay up to man the guardroom when he's away. Things got so bad (2-3 hours' sleep a night) that my Enciks would later give these duty NCOs the rest of the morning off, training needs permitting. It's the latter duos who need the extra rest, not the others ...
