    Originally posted by Resentment:

    as training is gradual, more on stamina, endurance building. very less army technical stuff like fieldcraft or m16 rifle technical handling, only to be covered in the actually bmt phrase (9 weeks).

     

    e.g. route marches within the 13 weeks from 2 to 4 to 6 to 8 to 12 to 16, and then to 24km.

     

    jogging to train endurance (Ability Group Run), alternate sprint;jog to train stamina (30;60, 60;90)...

    In fact PTP batch is better compared to direct BMT for those who passed NAPFA. 4-6 weeks of mindset preparation, as well as some time for you to set up your SBO, helmet and everything. Just purely physical training during this period plus some drill practices too, no drawing of arms yet. Whereas those direct BMT intakes will straightaway set up their SBO and draw arms upon enlistment.
