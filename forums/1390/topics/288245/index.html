    1) I am going to NUS for undergrad studies after this. Will i get called back for reservist?
    Depends on which outfit you're posted to. If it's a combat one (i.e. becomes ops-ready after NS), then you'll almost certainly obliged to do reservist during your semester vacations. Either with this unit or, if not, probably with the next-ORDing one. My old experience in Arty was such that every other batch is designated an "IHL" unit (Institute of Higher Learning), meaning a number of its commanders & men are full-time varsity students & as such the CO must plan his first few ICTs around their off-term breaks. The troops will do the IPPT during these ICTs, but can tio RTs & mob-manning/recall-IC duties all year around.

    In turn, the ex-Poly grads join/stay with the non-IHL unit, though it must be said that this overall tertiary "segregation" isn't a blanket practice either.

    If you don't end up thus frontline, then it'll depend on your appointment in the rear outfit. A MT-line posting, for example, is quite hands-on & can get you redeployed anywhere they need you. That said .....

    2) Also, after i graduate from NUS Faculty of Dentistry, i have a 4-year bond with the Ministry of Health that i have to serve. Will SAF call me back for reservist if I have to serve a government bond?
    ....I agree with the others that you'll likely be converted. It's a highly-trained profession you're studying for, & the SAF will likely take advantage of this. (Yes, bonders must liable for reservist.)

    3) After i finish serving my bond, i plan to move overseas and work overseas. Will the SAF call me back for reservist? And if SAF does call me back, and i cannot come back due to work, what happens?
    Once your exit permit application is approved, you'll be basically disrupted from reservist & repost-able back to the holding list (more so the longer you're away). But bear in mind that your NS liability won't end until you turn 40 or 50 -- not sure the age limit for dental officers -- & you may be called up again upon your return (even if at an age when your peers have already finished their cycle & have stood down).
