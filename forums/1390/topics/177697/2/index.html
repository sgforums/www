  • Incedrius
    11 Feb 06, 00:19
    If you *strongly suggest* to your MO that you want to take the King's shilling, he would most probably back down just enough to grant you a temporary status of PES D. During this time, go to a civillian doctor and get yourself certified fit. For example, if the reason you PEC C now is cos u went for an operation last time, return to your surgeon and beg him to give you a clean bill of health.

    The important thing is that from now on, you CANNOT report sick cos of problems related to that. For example, if you had say back surgery, and got your surgeon to certify you combat fit and are now PES B, if you report sick for back injury after an Airborne jump (which can happen to even PES A ppl...) it will be viewed as YOUR fault. So think VERY carefully.

    Anyway, if you're really in it for the 'adventure' you can always sign on for the 3 years contract (Officer) or try for the Premium Plan for Spec. Something short-term where you still have *some* say about your vocation. People going for the adventure should try short-term careers so as to have some influence over their posting, yet not be stuck for too long in a career they are patently unsuited for but did not realize it before signing the dotted line. At least you can maintain *some* enthusiasm for the length of 3 years!

    In your current PES status, you will not be able to undergo Sniper training.
    My advice is for you to undergo a few months of NSF first.

    If you still feel like a combat vocation is your cup of tea then ask your PC or OC about the Service Fit Volunteer Scheme. When I enlisted last year, I read that under this scheme people with lower PES status can get a waiver and proceed on to BMTC and Command School (and combat vocations like Sniper) if they are deemed able to survive the specific rigours of training.

    Now, this might be a bit dangerous and it's up to you to assess your own physical condition before you do anything. Also, depending on your physical condition, you might not even be accepted under the sceme. I'm only telling you that this scheme (as at early 2005) exists.

    If you have further enquiries, you might want to direct them towards CMPB.
