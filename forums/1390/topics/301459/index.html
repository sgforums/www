    Singapore News // Weekend, January 5, 2008

    Soldiers down with upset stomachs

    AT LEAST 118 Singapore Armed Forces (SAF) servicemen at Pasir Laba Camp came down with gastrointestinal disease on Thursday. Symptoms included diarrhoea and in some severe cases, vomiting.
    .
    Today understands that they were struck down with the illness after they had apparently consumed meals on Wednesday at the camp's cookhouses. None of the camp's cookhouses have been closed while investigations are ongoing.
    .
    As a standard practice, food samples are taken from meals consumed by servicemen and kept for three days before they are disposed of. Samples from Wednesday's meals are now being tested and the results will be known on Sunday.
    .
    Mindef spokesperson Colonel Darius Lim said the 118 SAF servicemen suffered mild symptoms of gastrointestinal disease. Appropriate medical treatment and rest in camp were accorded to the affected servicemen, he added.
    .
    Pasir Laba Camp at Jurong is considered the home of SAF Warrant Officers and Specialists, housing the School of Infantry Specialists (Sispec) among other units.
    .
