  • blitzonic

    Looks like you're posted to a laojiao unit, if mhcampboy's info (3 ICTs served) is correct. The bad news is the unit is probably ready to take things to the next level - i.e. overseas-training stints - now that the reservist commanders are comfortable taking charge. In Arty, for example, we went to ROC for a month each during our 4th & 6th in-camps, with the unit performance peaking during this period (& thereafter plummeting for the rest of the training cycle, as heavier civilian-career, spousal & familial responsibilities took hold). Hmmm, funny that you're not following a newly-ORD IHL unit (it's usually the latest one or the next available one). Best to ask around when you report on whether your posting is permanent or just filling in (for someone who's deferred). On the 'IHL Blackout window': BTW Industrial training & internships during varsity vacations still don't 'count', as far as I know. Your employers must still release you for ICTs if they overlap.

    If the unit's only done 2 ICTS (presumably with a low-key year in between), then I suppose it's still fairly new enough to take in the undergrad-pengs. *shrug* How often do they designate IHL units in infantry anyway? In my time (start of the '90s), it was kinda like every alternate batch in Arty. So those Air-Level NSFs who were serving the extra half-year would follow the next ORD-ing unit for reservist, & it was in turn designated accordingly as IHL. Quite systematic in that sense, cuz the formation only has 4 active units to rotate with (a 5th, 24 SA, doesn't "count" cuz they're radar trackers). Infantry, of course, has 6 active units.
