    [afei - manners please, don't hijack the thread.]

    1stly, anything but PES D, E & F is technically considered "combat-fit". PES C itself doesn't rule out frontline combat (eg. see C1L1 below), but it does advocate posting to only certain operational vocations.

    2ndly, the PES status for A, B & C refers more strictly to participation in IPPT. The L-Code (Land Deployability Code) refers more to where you can be vocationally deployed.

    So:-

    PES C - Fit for some operational vocations (Modified BMT)
    >> C1 - Required to take IPPT but can be excused up to 2 static stations
    >> C2 - Do not have to take IPPT except Regulars (who take the AAFT)
    >> C9 - Not required to take IPPT

    >> L1 - Fit for field duties including front-line duty
    >> L2 - Fit for field duties but only in Unit HQ or rear areas
    >> L3 - Fit for operational duties at Bases only. Not fit for field duties/exercises

    Both C1L3 & C2L3 postings would be something like camp medic or signal-storeman if you're sent to a frontline unit. On the whole, C9 personnel have more chances to go to a rear-echelon outfit like those at BDG- or DIV-level or to a MINDEF desk job. But a lot of L3 personnel also end up with combat-support/logistics (supply, maintainence, transport, medical, ordnance, etc.) under the DISCOM umbrella.
