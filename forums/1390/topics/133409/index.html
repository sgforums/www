    if you are a regular... a degree makes a difference.

    if you are NSF..... A degree just makes you qualify for Enhanced BMT.

    Where you go for posting after BMT really depends mostly on your BMT performance.

    i would say 95% of people who went to OCS are primarily based on your bmt platoon commander's recommendation.

    Your BMT PC is usually a younger chap who doesn't know much about life's experience and is supposed to qualify people who are eligible to be SAF's leaders.

    Most of the time, his recommendations are based on his PERSONAL perspectives with input from his NCOs.

    So be in his bad books.... you are done for.

    In fact, having studied overseas (thus living some time outside singapore), may at times lower your chances at 'sensitive' units/postings. I have seen people who simply just can't go some places in SAF (i don't wish to elaborate).

    Seriously.... your pre-enlistment history has very little bearing on your posting post-bmt.

    How your education background will affect is most probably impressing your superiors in BMT and hopefully they think highly of you... and thus recommending you to OCS.

    Really no offence, but there are simply too many degree holders (honours or not) nowadays. If you are talking about 80s and early 90s, your degree as an NSF may help... but now people have differing perceptions due to the commonality.

    Trust me. I'm a degree holder (double major FYI with a high GPA), i'm an NSF.... i'm not an officer.... and best... i'm in BMTC. So i know how things work around here.

    As for getting 9-5 posting (which does not exist, you mean 0730 to 1730?), it's really luck.... your domestic circumstances may help if you appeal to your superiors and show you are under extenuating conditions. Even granted, it is not an official arrangement but rather a personal arrangement between your direct superior and you.

    Meaning, your superior reserves the right to revoke the arrangement for any reasons.
