    Add on slightly, I'd say that the right thing to do is to let your direct superior know, not necessarily all the way up to OC/Branch Head level by yourself.

    He or she is your "people manager" and is supposed to handle your case to the higher-ups.

    By letting your superiors know, it allows them to monitor you and ensure you are deployed according to your strengths and weaknesses.

    However, different units, different strokes.

    To clear up certain misconceptions which may result in serious accidents, I'd like to remind all NSFs, especially fresh enlistees not to mistake letting your superior know about your medical issues, with getting permission from them.

    Note: Going to the MO is a soldier's right, enshrined by the General Orders of MINDEF. He or she does not require permission to do so
