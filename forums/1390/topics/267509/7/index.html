    Eunice Olsen looked hot in her cheongsam. Irene Ng (my MP) looked like she just got laid too ~lol~

    Very impressed with the overall coordination this time around, from my vantage point (Pan Pacific). Two new sub-service branches (RSN & the police equivalent, PCG) were prominently featured for the first time, & the Marina Bay setting wasn't like just having a mini-fortilla sailing past a few years ago. No idea if they've switched around the show items, but otherwise I can appreciate just how difficult the coordination was, given the heavier emphasis on hardware (more actual performances this year, & not just forming a mobile column) & on a largely watery-cum-"riverbank" setting at that.

    The parade sequence never fails to rouse me. Those troops in the contingents are always mostly NSFs, albeit more & more "specky" (wearing spectacles) ~lol~ through the years. Looks like floating stadium will change how the contingents form up for the next 5 years too. In my time, we must stay motionless for over an hour (before the Prime Minister made his arrival). All in all, it would be one & a half before the GOH finished its march-past! (Quite gruelling -- though it's a lot better than the '70s when the ENTIRE contingents must march part.) Likely on an empty stomach too; I recall my unit getting to eat a quick dinner post-parade sequence, before filing in again as part of the finale item.

    DAMN PROUD TO BE A SINGAPOREAN.
