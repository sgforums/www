    Yeah, I know but it should be interesting. Seriously............it's very do-able as long as you've got your groove 'cos the drill requires one to start cold.

    I checked some past timings from a year ago when I sat in a class with this instructor who's full-time job (whether you believe it or not) is to train the FBI's HRT team and he called the following times out (I noted them):

    One shot, down zero, from HIGH ready - 0.90
    One shot, down zero, from LOW ready - 0.75
    Splits - anywhere from 0.25 to 0.30 (his splits were 0.15 to 0.20)
    One shot, drawn from concealment - 1.55

    So, based on these basic numbers, if you take drill # 2 (for example - Double Tap fr Low Ready, 1.35 sec), my goal should be:

    0.75 + 0.30 = 1.05 seconds

    Drill # 3, Rhythm (never heard this term being used); 6 shots at one target:

    0.75 + (0.30 x 5) = 2.25 seconds (which still beats the 3 sec mark)

    The major problem is in Drill # 4; One shot, speed reload, one shot from Low Ready. I never can reload a single stack 45 as fast as I can a double stack mag. If I'm lucky, I'll hit 1.5 seconds.

    All that said, like I've mentioned, if you ain't got your groove for the day, you're screwed. Here's a very good example - in the first video you will see a competitor shoot a stage with 3 targets (2 shots each); the first you have shoot thro' the car (target outside of co-driver side), the 2nd is in front of the car and the 3rd is a swinging target right next to the 1st.

    The reason why I included the 1st vid is becos' you get to see the complete (almost) set-up. The 2nd vid is of myself shooting the stage. Now, before I even shot, I had to mentally calculate how long it was going to take me to shoot this stage. So in my head, I 'see' the following:

    I open truck door, draw and shoot first shot - 2 secs
    Second shot on 1st target - 0.3 (remember my splits?) secs
    Transition to 2nd target for first shot - 0.5 secs
    Second shot on 2nd target - 0.3 secs
    Transition to 3rd target for first shot - 0.75 secs becos' this is a swinger
    Second shot on 3rd target - 0.3 secs

    So, in a perfect scenario, I should be able to shoot this stage in a total of 4.15 seconds with 6 shots. Unfortunately, it never is perfect 'cos I shot the last target 3 times to be on the safe side (remember it's a swinger?). And guess what? Total time was 4.14 seconds. Watch the vids.............
