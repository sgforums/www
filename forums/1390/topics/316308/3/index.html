    There's no answer to which vocation is the best for women. It all depends on the physical abilities and preferences of the individual. Women are allowed to joined all vocations, be it pilot, naval diver, guards, armour, etc (except for commando as so far it is the only unit that does not have any female in it). You are given the option of choosing your vocation upon expressing interest to sign on. However, your acceptance into the vocation will be based on interview/test with the specific vocation. (Therefore it is not true that you will get what you want) As for the difference between officer/specialist, they both have different job scopes. To simplify it, officers basically manage the troops in a more macro level, whereas, specialist is on the ground working together with the troops and training them. So it once again boils down to your personal job preference, as both roles can offer great satisfaction (No flaming guys... cause you know it can be true). BMT for women is almost the same for males except that IPPT standards is lower and there is no need to go through SIT-test (This explains the shorter BMT period). Moreover, since the cohort for women BMT is always much smaller than the males, training can be completed in a shorter time as compared to the males. (Think conducting a lesson for 20vs100 people). Other then that, the women BMT offers the same program as the male, including individual field craft, weapon handling, basic close combat training, drills, physical training, battle inoculation course, road march, first aid, national education, etc.
