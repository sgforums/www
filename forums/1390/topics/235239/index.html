    Should you be on MC, it's best that you contact your unit S1 to make necessary arrangement... Been on 3 days MC might not put you so-called Out-Of-Course, but you might still be able to complete the rest of the ICT... Unless you took it for granted and didn't report back after the 3-days MC...

    As for your previous offences, they can be taken reference during your summary trial in absence without official leave from ICT... For example, should you be convicted and punished with sentencing to DB of seven days in previous trial, you can be tried and convicted for more than seven days of DB...

    When SAF sends something to you : Proof of sending is sufficient.
    When you send something to the SAF : Proof of faxing is not proof of receipt.

    I suggest you go to your fax machine and print out the activity report, showing that a successful fax did go through to your unit. This will unlikely save you, but will be a mitigating factor that you did fax, but your unit has a screw-up system of tracking and follow-up.
