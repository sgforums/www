  • Culture counter
    6 Mar 11, 10:17

    Those who are undergoing courses at STC after 4wks of modified Tekong BMT are Pes C personnel who are allocated service support vocations because of their PES status which do not qualify them for combat or combat support vocations.

    Basically these NSF personnel would know their respective allocated vocations after 4wks at Tekong, and they would be taking up courses according to their allocated vocations at STC.

    Whether "research assistant" or "intelligience assistant" or "supply assistant" or whatever, they are nothing more than a branch of ASA (Admin Support Asst), in other words, just clerical support staff who are posted to support admin work on the various brances of service, be it intelligence or research or stores supply or what not. They would be trained to be aware of the services that they are to support, that's all. Simply clerical staff.

    6 Mar 11, 16:28

    0530 Reveille

    0545 Fall in

    0600 5BX

    0615 Breakfast

    0715 Standby area/bed

    0730 First parade

    0745 Lessons start

    This will be your routine for basically every day of your life there. I spent quite a number of days literally doing nothing in an air-conditioned room when there was supposed to be lessons. The original courses for each of the vocations are much shorter than the five weeks allocated for this vocational BMT, so there will be plenty of time wastage. Your LBV and helmet will be collecting dust under your bed until the final two weeks where they will make you go through a useless Vehicle Immediate Action Drill and if you're lucky, a mock firefight with blank rounds and smoke grenades during the rehearsals and the Passing Out Parade itself.
