    Supposedly computer logarithms, lah.

    I taught an Arty mono batch before, so can answer that part (but dunno how common the practice is across the SAF). Those who tio my unit intake, the BMT instructors get to wholly decide -- recommend -- who to retain after POP.

    The combat-support weapons were more labour-intensive in my time. My active-time outfit numbered about 125 ex-mono intakers (mortarmen & M113 drivers), of which 10-15 were "extra" troops to offset any ensuing downgrading cases, detention-barracks cases, SAFSA attachments, etc.

    The new BMT batch conscripted an extra BTY, making it about 200 recruits in all. So my estimation is that about 65-70 percent of them were retained for Arty trade course, & a slightly-lower percentage thereafter for operational life. The rest were presumably sent back to the CMPB computer for other vocational deployments. Never found out where they ended up, though as a reservist I ran into two or three later on at Amoy Quee. (They became storemen.)
