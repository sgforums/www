    Think about it this way. If you don't sign on, u probably wont have to worry about regretting it. If u sign on, you have to worry about regretting it.

    Which one saves u trouble?

    The most important question one should ask yourself is, "What do you see yourself doing 10 years down the road?" Or what do you WANT to see yourself doing 10 years down the road.

    Still doing ground work? Be in middle management? Or climb to higher management? Or sit at home earn passive income? Does the SAF advancement structure, rewards and renumeration help you to realize these?

    If not, why bother?

    Uncle Cabbie is probably quite sad about what happened to him. And rightly so. However, what is to say he would not have encountered the same situation working in the private sector? Would private sector companies not reduce his pension?

    The only problem with what happened to him was that he came out of the SAF with LIMITED skills. He was a naval diver, and now can only be a dRiver cos he didn't upgrade himself with more private sector relevant skills. "Self-acclaimed" management skills are a dime a dozen for those coming out of the force. Even the employers are smart to realize that officers and specs leaving the force are not necessarily good managers to begin with in the first place. Why? Cos too many lousy ones have spoiled the market already.

    So if you are regular, please take time to think of how to upgrade yourself. Take certifications on networking or whatever u enjoy. Be prepared for whatever unforeseen circumstances that may arise and jeopardise ur career in the SAF. Besides, if SAF are indeed teaching right, you'd have already been cultured to think that way already.

    My 2cts.
