    Both schools now follow the same curriculum, if that's what you're asking. But the BMT system wasn't always like this. There's a wiki entry that summed it up quite well but to briefly delineate out their "direct" lineage:-

    BMTC Sch 1 (Tekong) <- 2 BTS (Tekong) <- ITD Camp 1, 3 (Tekong) <- ITD (Sembawang)

    BMTC Sch 2 (Tekong) <- 3 BTS, 7 BTS (Nee Soon) <- SBMT (Nee Soon)

    The BMT programmes were different with each entity but their common link is that they all trained non-mono intake combat recruits. SBMT also trained lower-PES enlistees for future service vocations (drivers, cooks, storemen, etc.) in its time. During the BTS period, the individual BDEs took charge so both the Tekong & Nee Soon recruits had a quasi-mono intake look then cuz 7 SIB was GDS while 2 SIB/3 SIB were INF.

    Socks Policy : Changed in 2004 when new CO/Comd agreed that its stupid to issue Sch 2 recruits white socks and not use it.

    Soap Policy : Ok to use it, no more "wayang" stand by bed. Just don't drop it in shower.

    Mosquito : School 1 more mosquito because nearer to jungle. Any fumigation attempts have not really got rid of mosquitos.

    Xiongness Policy : BMTC is a ISO-9002 certified entity and BMT program is ISO-9002 certified. Any degree of xiongness is hence the discretion of OC/CSM. Otherwise "in theory" xiongness is the same. Warrant Companies tend to be "more slack" not because they are, but rather because they are experieced in training and hence impart to you what is needed and more organised.

    Google Earth Picture : Not update, but if you log into Google Earth nowdays it will show the land reclamation progress. The jetty is like in a Bay now.

    Repainting : BMTC was entirely repainted as of earlier this year. no more bright pastel colours.

    Batches : First half of the year generally JC batch since they would just finished As. Second Half is Poly. This only apply to BMT with Leadership Package. PES C/E BMT is mixed. Twice/Thrice everyyear there will be a batch for O level or less, but typically warrant companies.
