    My cousin is a NDU Warrant officer who was part of the naval contingent Singapore sent to Iraq. He was onboard the Landing Ship Tank (LST) that was dispatched, I believe it was the RSS Endurance. The mission of RSS Endurance was to protect the crucial Al-Basra oil terminal.

    I have gleaned insights from his operational tour of duty there. He and fellow NDU divers would regularly go out out on the Fast Craft Equipment Personnel (FCEP) boats to check local vessels bypassing the area for security reasons. Obviously, this is not just another routine Coast Guard job one might expect the police here to do against intruding Banglas. His MP5 submachine gun was locked, loaded and live, ready for any troublemakers when they board vessels for cargo inspection.The operations are real-time and real-life. Can you imagine if they came across suicidal insurgents on boats, intent on a one way trip to blow up the oil depots or at the very least, sabotage it? It would cause major damage to Iraqi infrastructure.

    So there you have it, I believe Singapore troops have played a small part in this Gulf war, a small capacity in the rebuilding of Iraq. The information I provided above is to my knowledge, public domain.

    To partly answer the question of exposure to combat some have been clamouring about, I will try not to step beyond the realm of public domain - mods please delete anything deemed unsuitable. My NDU cousin has a pretty big mouth hehe. Well, if one were to use logic to link the dots, do not forget that the Singapore Armed Forces maintains very close links with that of foreign militaries such as the US and say, Israel. Will it be so unfathomable if Singaporean combat observers have tagged along for say, Merkava tank rides in Gaza to witness Israeli military action against Hamas or Fatah or whoever first-hand. Or perhaps, hung behind US infantry combat patrols in various Iraqi cities.

    I truly believe that the SAF is not so virgin to combat scenarios as they would like the public to think. Imagine the public outcry if one of our blood were to die in a crossfire in an event that had no relevance to our national security, especially if they were not there in a peacekeeping capacity but rather as.. peaceful observers.
