    The SAF is trying to keep every1 as high PES as possible. You have to understand that some people have flat foot, but they are pronated normally. Hence they can be Pes B.

    If the specialist has recommended a lower Pes then most likely the SAF mo will just follow and put it up to medical board according. After all, he is just a small fry in the medical hierarchy he have no say. Most likely he will send to see the SAF orthopedic specialist for a second view.

    Just keep on visiting the medical centre, whack with Physio and MC until your medical condition is resolved. 

    If it is bad enough, then every time you take IPPT you will need report sick, and whack with pain killer.

    If incorrect walking or running motion, the joints might be worn out more quickly, so it will result is pain, swelling as symptom and eventually it will leads to arthritis later (might not be old). So take care.
