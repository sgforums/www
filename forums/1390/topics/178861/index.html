    Sometimes i wonder why in SAF we wear helmet.

    US did ballistic tests on their PASGT helmet (supposedly SAF's helmet is based on this).

    They concluded that it will not stop a direct 5.56mm hit, much less for 7.62.

    Shots from a 9mm do not produce consistent results but in general it stops the bullet to some extent but it cannot stop multiples shots.

    They are however effective in blocking shrapnels or high velocity debris (resulting from blasts). This are most likely situations in arty, mortar or grenade attack.

    They don't mention anything on Claymore blasts.

    So in a combat situation. if your helmet can't stop a rifle bullet why wear?

    And if your helmet is supposed to stop shrapnels and debris... how about the rest of your body? because in such situations.. there will be lots of debris flying and hitting other parts of body anyway. So why wear?

    To give us a fake sense of security?

    Originally posted by Ponders:
    They are however effective in blocking shrapnels or high velocity debris (resulting from blasts). This are most likely situations in arty, mortar or grenade attack.

    I think you just answered your own question with this.

    When you're at the receiving end of an artillery barrage, you would really be glad that you have a helmet.

    In fact, if I remember correctly, the military helmet was invented on the battlefields of WWI. In the past, they used to wear useless cloth caps. If I'm not wrong, it was a French soldier who ingeniously put a metal pot over his head to help him survive the shrapnel of a German artillery bombardment.

    In addition, even if it can't stop a direct hit by a 5.56mm round, it can still deflect an indirect hit sometimes. If the round hits the helmet at an angle instead of head-on, there's a chance that the round will be deflected.

    The effects are also psychological. I sure as heck would feel a lot better to have a helmet on when the bullets are flying all around me.
