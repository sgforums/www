    Well....let me try to answer your questions with facts.


    To be a RSAF pilot - of any kind, you need to be either Pes A/B. Cause afterall they're combat commissioned officers, got to be reasonably fit. In case you eject and gotta try to survive in hostile territory, you also need to pass your IPPT and able to qualify for OCS.

     

    Yes, there're many ex-RSAF pilots in SIA. They even have a scheme to help them transition over. There's no such thing as 'more dangerous'. Every plane has their handling limits. An airliner can't manoevre like a fighter and it can't overspeed, if not it will disintegrate and breakup in the air. Airline pilots have to be type-qualified to each type of aircraft and be 'licensed'. Military pilots need to go for training and type-qualification.


    No, I don't think a fighter pilot can just takeover an airliner and fly it just like that. The visual cues, systems etc. are very different. They can probably 'fly' it like, can control the stick and throttle, operate the ILS, but things like landing etc. have delicate and specific settings.
