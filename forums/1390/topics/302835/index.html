    From what I understand, most person is deemed to have High blood pressure (HBP) if the blood pressure is consistently high over a period of time. A lot of things may caused blood pressure to be raised temporary, for example being excited, nervous, exercise, sex or some form of illness may cause blood pressure to rise. Even the lack of sleep may raise blood pressure. Flu and fever are some of the things that might cause blood pressure to raise but if after you recovered from it and your blood pressure remains high, then you might really have HBP. Most doctors will not rush to prescribe HBP medicine once they got a HBP reading, more likely they would ask you to return on a later date to take the blood pressure again. Only after a period of time of high readings, how long is something for the doctor to decide, HBP may be diagnosed. So better leave it to the trained doctor to decide if you have HBP than to self conclude of having it.
