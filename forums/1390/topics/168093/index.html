    Hi guys! I'm a Warrant Officer in the US Army and am interested to know more about Warrant Officers in the Singapore Army.

    In the US Army, Warrant Officers are Technical Officers specialising in a particular field. We have the status of Officers, are saluted and are members of the Officers Club. There are five ranks. On qualifying from Warrant Officer Candidate School (WOCS), WOs are initially appointed (i.e. receive a 'warrant') at the rank of Warrant Officer 1 (WO1). When they are promoted to Chief Warrant Officer 2 (CW2), they receive a Commission from the President of the United States. The more senior ranks are CW3, CW4 and CW5.

    (see the attached link http://usawocc.army.mil/whatiswo.htm )

    Do your Warrant Officers have similar roles and status to US Warrants or are they more similar to British and Australian WOs

    Our warrant officers are soldiers, who worked their way up from NCOs (we refer to them as specialists) to WOs.

    They usually have lots of experience in their specialisation, and often take on roles usually filled in by officers. Some are platoon commanders/leaders in training schools, and most of them will have an opportunity to serve as regimental sergeant majors.

    As mentioned, they are not saluted as they are not commisioned by our President. Warrant Officers do have an opportunity to convert to become Commissioned officers, but few would. Doing so usually means a pay-cut as their Warrant officer rank pay is somewhat higher than the equivalent Officer rank pay.

    US Warrants on initial appointment as Warrant Officer Grade 1 receive a Warrant. They receive a Commission on promotion to the next Warrant Grade, Chief Warrant Officer Grade 2. As you say, WOs are specialist officers, mainly in technical fields but also aviators. Warrant Officers in most Military Occupational Specialties have reached a minimum rank of Sergeant (E5) before applying to become a Warrant. The exceptions are aviators who can apply as civilians.

    Sergant Majors are the most senior NCOs in the US Army (E9s). There are three ranks, Sergeant Major, Command Sergeant Major and the Sergeant Major of the Army who is the senior enlisted advisor to the Chief of Staff of the US Army. It seems weird that your equivalent ranks (WOs) are members of the Officers Club.
