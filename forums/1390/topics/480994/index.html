    Wed, Jul 13, 2011
    AsiaOne

    A NSF recruit from the Basic Military Training Centre (BMTC) on Pulau Tekong was picked up by the Police Coast Guard after attempting to swim towards Singapore.

    The incident happened last December.

    The recruit made his attempt at night, and it is unclear how far he swam before he was spotted.

    A Mindef spokesman said the man was found on the Kechil Land Link, between Pulau Tekong and Pulau Tekong Kechil.

    Mindef said the 18-year-old recruit had been disciplined, but did not comment on whether he is still in active service.

    For leaving the camp without permission, the recruit could have been AWOL (Absent Without Official Leave), an offense punishable with a jail term of up to two years in the detention barracks.

    Sources told The Straits Times the recruit was a student from Raffles Institution.

    All Singaporean males and Singapore permanent residents are required to serve national service of two years upon reaching the age of 16 and a half years.

    Most recruits are sent to the BMTC during the first phase of their training.

    Swimming is an essential life skill, especially for Singaporeans who live on an island. Chances are also high that any soldier in operation will see some time in the water. To ensure that our soldiers are able to manage this, we train our soldiers to be water-confident. We build up their stamina through aqua jogging training. By correcting their technique with practice, they stay afloat better and improve their overall physical fitness at the same time.

    This swimming training not only helps to ffn overcome any obstacle in life. The recruits will also use these swimming skills out in the field as they have to cross bodies of water while keeping their rifles dry.
