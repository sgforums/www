    now that ive booked my rt at maju camp one of the days is tuesday 1800-2200hrs. what if on that day my lessons ends at 1830 at sim, can i still make it for the training?do they always start on time at 1800?is it ok to be late?and how long will the training usually last?
    Yes, can report late though I suppose it's better to play safe for the first few &, from inside, gauge the grace period. But your employer or educational institution is obliged to release you early to attend RT under the law, so no lobang on that front. You're supposed to make your own arrangments with them.

    Yet I've seen people come as late as 6:30 to Khatib before in the '90s, from my vantage point at the canteen during my ICTs. The variation of the reporting times were inevitable for a number of reasons: eg. access via the back gate from nearby Khatib MRT may/may not be banned (depending on routine orders); access via the main gate is 1/2-hour longer if commuting from from Yishun MRT (171 bus) or from Thomson direction (evening hour-traffic along the long Sembawang Road); the FCC in-processing is quite impersonal (compared to last time when must fall in together); at least half a hour at the start is set aside for warming-up anyway, etc.

    From what I hear, they don't always exercise the full 4 hours anymore -- esp. on weekends. (The schedules are available at their website.) Anyway, the training is supposed to be quite progressive & unhurried, unless it's IPPT day. Really, Khatib was more like a fitness park every evening too, with other people playing football or jogging inside the stadium & the RT people wearing anything they wanted.

    IMO Only really siong if you live far away & don't drive, or if you work shift hours. Or if your body always "nua" (weak) after exercising.

    They always start late at Maju camp, usually around 6.15-6.30pm. You can still go and "book in" but usually they will give u a green tag, which means u are late. Usually, that would mean that when people are collecting their tags and ready to go home, the PTI would ask u go run around the parade square 2-3 times, after which u can return the tag and go home too.

    on weekdays, the training will usually be over by 8.15pm or even earlier by 8, and on weekends, 10+am/6+pm(depending which schedule u choose for the sunday one). They usually do the same thing, warm up, go run 3km, then take a break, then go do circuit/gym/pull up, rest a while, then cool down aka go home liao. But since u are in Phrase 1 currently, that would mean the PTI IC or the 2IC would spend sometime talking to u after the exercise, telling u what u can do to pass IPPT. If u kenna the PTI IC talk, less than 5 mins settle, if u kenna the 2IC, its 10minutes and above of talk.
