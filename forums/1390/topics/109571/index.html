    I understand the 13-year cycle remains. To change that would screw up the operational rotation of active & reservist units, something which I sense even the new base 2-year NS system may not necessarily impact.

    Under the old training cycle, it's at least 9 high-key ICTs in 13 years. Under the revised version, it's at least 7 high-key ICTs for the 1st 10 years, then a trio of 2-day BITs between Year 11 & 13.

    Realistically, "siam"-ing IPPT's only for the early 30somethings & older. As said, those who completed the full ICT cycle with their unit will be posted together to the MINDEF reserves, & thus no more IPPT (usually after 33-35 years old). Those who were on holding lists, or recycled on to younger outfits, are liable for IPPT unit they're 40.
