    sgf:

    What they're saying is correct, you just have to interpret correctly. BTW, the explanation is on the back of the Pay Claim Form.

    Treat Rank pay as a fixed amount but MUP as a variable amount with the following caveat - Total Amount Paid will not exceed Civilian Pay for the period claimed. Which means MUP will never equal Civilian Pay. IINM, the payments into your bank account will also show this. Reimbursements will come in 2 seperate deposits ... one reflecting amount due via Rank Pay and the other being the balance due via MUP. Add this 2 up equals your civilian pay for the period.

    Everybody else:

    Not sure if I read some of the post correctly but there seems to be some queries on the status of Ex-Regulars and also for reservist to be paid at Regulars' Salaried rate (Did I read you correctly ... "then why ICT work not given a salary")

    Regulars are in the Military on a long term contract, so they get an advantageous pay rate. Regulars don't do 'Reservist' as long as they remain regulars, hence no question of MUP for them for reservist duties. When they are no longer 'Regulars', they become like all of us, liable for reservist call ups. NSFs, NSMen are more like short contract workers or part-timers. Our per diem pay 'allowance' will of course be lower than the salaries of a full-timer. So Ex-Regulars will likewise be paid allowances based on a NSF Rank pay scale (part-timer what!).
