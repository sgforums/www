    But the thing here is same camp doesn't mean same priviliage.
    Stay out, nights out is usually set by the unit you are in. Sometime even Company line policy also different (A coy personnels can stay out, B coy cannot, etc).

    It also changes with change of CO or even RSM. I quote my experience, during my NSF days, there was a RSM change, "Xin Guan Shang Ren, 3 ba huo" the new RSM grounded all nights out and review all stayout personnel status.

    Trust me, after sometime you will not want to stay out already, too troublesome, wake up eariler, rush to take public transport, rush to camp. Sometime unit got ops that you work till very late, by then you don't even feel like stay out.

    Suay thing is late, then will kena left, right, centre. No use giving reasons such as traffic jams lah, etc cause the army don't believe in this kind of stuff, they just shoot "why don't you just leave eariler or wake up eariler?" then you lan-lan already.

    Lastly, now only 2 years, blink of any eye, you ORD already, so why trouble yourself over stay-in or stay-out.

    $1,200 a month isn't luxurious but should nevertheless be enough for familial expenses. I take it that the poster, with his prior part-time job status, didn't appear to be a significant income contributor either (though every penny set aside helped).

    IMO It's best that he seriously evaluates his own money management. Even if it's legal, getting a part-time job can only be on an informal basis - like tuition - since most civilian employers would shy away from hiring military conscripts. There's also the travel expenses of regular booking in & out to consider, unless he stays near his camp.

    If he wants to continue contributing to the familial funds, there's the SAF Financial Assistance Scheme to consider:
