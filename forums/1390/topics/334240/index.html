    This is what i did. But please note i was a old green duffle bag era. Green duffle bag can fit 2 army rations boxes (the carton boxes army rations was packaged in). Before you start, line your duffle bag with SAF's trash bag (the thick transparent one). Then fill up 2 ration boxes with your loose items that do not fit into the standard 7 pack items. e.g your remainder 3(*) sets of Number 4, mess mug, water bottles, nicely packed SBO, and spares you may need during your ICT. (* why 3 sets because 1 in 7 pack item, and 1 outsde to wear to report to your ICT, so 3 sets left) Put this 2 boxes at the bottom of your duffle bag, but tape them up to seal them. on top of your boxes, put your field pack (with 7 pack items) and boots, and the topmost will be your helmet. Inside the helmet, put a Thirsty hippo upright. Before closing the duffle bag, cover the first liner with a 2nd SAF trash bag inverted. On top of the second trash bag, type out a list of items that you DID NOT include in the duffle bag BUT NEED to buy when you are called for ICT. these are things with expiry dates or other reasons (beret/headdress you wear to report, batteries, lighter, slippers/shoes you still want to wear during in CV times) After all these is done, close up your duffle bag, LOCK it up or seal it with tape. This is to guard against your brother or some maid opening up your duffle bag to take things out with your knowledge. BUT PLEASE NOTE, WASH and DRY totally ALL items. Any damp or dirty items, is going to grow mushroom. leave your water bottles open in the box. Replace your thirsty hippo say.. once every 4-6 months.

    I hope the quality of the Black Duffle Bag has improved. When the Black duffle bag was first issued June 1 2004, we oversaw the issuance. End of day, I have half a platoon with either snapped buckles, torn fabric or damaged zippers. More irritatingly, ST Log said it was enlistee's rough handling, CSM said it was enlistee's rough handling. OC said not his problem go ask CSM. Enlistee REFUSED to use credit to replace. so for almost 2-3 months, they were using torn duffle bags. Until a few things happened, a) COMD BMTC saw recruits "hugging" duffle bag to prevent things from falling. b) Some recruits parents who work in the Civil Service wrote inter-department emails, protesting the lack of attention to kit replacement. c) S4 finally spent some time to ask us to write a report of all damaged kit bag. There was supposedly an "improved" second batch, but the same problem still exists.
