    The National Security Guards [NSG]

    established under the National Security Guard Act of 1986 (Act 47 of 1986), is India's premier counter-terrorist force. The NSG provides security to VIP's, conducts anti-sabotage checks, and is responsible for neutralizing terrorist threats to vital installations. Its missions include engaging terrorists in specific situations, responding to piracy in the air and on the land, and rescuing hostages in kidnap situations.

    Research and Analysis Wing

    The RAW was formed in 1968 and carries out India?s external covert intelligence gathering. The RAW has espionage agents in India?s neighbouring countries especially in Pakistan and China. Although its budget is not known, some estimates peg it at $ 160 million. It is believed to employ around 10,000 agents.
    [edit]

    Intelligence Bureau

    The IB was established in 1947 to assume responsibility for India?s domestic security. The IB is shrouded in secrecy and there is little public information concerning its main operations. What is known is that the IB is responsible for background checks on appointees to diplomatic posts and judges before they take oath, and on civilians who apply for HAM radio licences.
    [edit]

    Joint Cipher Bureau

    The Joint Cipher Bureau works closely with the IB and RAW. It is responsible for cryptanalysis and encryption of sensitive data.
    [edit]

    All India Radio monitoring service

    The AIRMS monitors all radio broadcasts in India as well as all foreign radio broadcasts which can be received in India.
    [edit]

    Joint Intelligence Committee

    The JIC is entrusted with the tasks of coordinating intelligence between all government agencies, civilian and military.
    [edit]

    Signals Intelligence Directorate

    The Signals Intelligence directorate is part of the Indian army and runs the Central Monitoring Organisation which has a large number of stations in the country.
    [edit]

    Aviation Research Centre

    The ARC is the SIGINT (signals intelligence) arm of the RAW. It has stations in Dehradun, Tissukiya, Assam, Chakratta (near Cuttak) in Orrisa, and at Palam Airport, Delhi. It reports to the Director General of Security, RAW. ...
    [edit]

    Directorate of Air Intelligence

    The DIA is responsible for air and satellite reconnaissance missions.
    [edit]

    Directorate of Navy Intelligence

    This is the Indian Navy?s intelligence arm

    Directorate of Military Intelligence

    This is the Indian military?s intelligence arm and primarily focuses on the intelligence needs of the Indian army.

    Special Service Bureau

    This is a Special Operations unit with responsibility for reconnaissance behind enemy lines
