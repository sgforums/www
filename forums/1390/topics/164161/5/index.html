    On radio 93.8FM today an ex SAF manpower officer called in and said that under the enlistment act, any defaulter who did not register for NS can be fined up to $5000 or jailed or both, but it is for the courts to decide the punishment.

    IF the person has already registered, then he falls under the SAF act. And if found AWOL, also carried fines and possible jail terms. He didn't say clearly which was more harsh, the civilian law or the SAF legislation.

    He also then said that if the person who did not register for NS is caught or surrendered himself, he would have to serve the remaning liability of his NS. For other ranks it is up to the age of 40, and for officers up to age 50.

    In this pianist's case, he is already 49 and hence need not serve NS as he is beyond the age requirement

    What this tells me is that I would advise my son not to serve NS. Don't register. Stay away from Singapore till he is 41 years old. In the meantime obtain a foreign citizenship, and when he is aged 41 he can return to Singapore, pay $5000 and get Singapore citizenship and still keep his foreign citizenship (in essence dual citizenship with no NS served!). Isn't that fantastic?
