  • pkchukiss

    For me, PTP was one of the most slackest time I ever had in my 5-6 months so far in the army. Your schedule will go something like this:

    0530 Reville
    0545 The 5 BX (Power PT if the Duty officer is feeling siao on...)
    0600 Breakfast
    0630 Area Cleaning
    0700 Stand-by area if your duty sergeant feels like it
    0730 First lesson (ET run/circuit/gym/)
    1020 Nothing essentially (labelled as "OC Disposal" or "Fatigue Duty, a.k.a. Sai Kang)
    1200 Lunch
    1300 See 1020
    1500 ET run/circuit/gym/drills
    1800 Dinner
    1930 Night training (usually extra training your sergeants have in store for you)
    2045/2100/2130/2200 C.R.O (Last Parade) Do whatever you want in your bunk
    2230 Lights out

    My record C.R.O was at 1900, but they used it to conduct a fire drill... Record latest was 2330 (but that is during BMT phase)

    Having said that, your instructors usually will take advantage of the free period to conduct stand-bys upon stand-bys upon stand-bys. So, whether you get to rest depends on them...
