    The only case I've heard where the chap was free to go, was an American citizen, whom the US Embassy here raised a huge stinker for. This is just hearsay, btw.

    Singapore does not allow one to give up their citizenship gained by birth unless he is 21. And NS Act comes into force when you are aged 16.5 for registration, and 18 for enlistment. This lag time of 3 years makes it impossible for one to apply for Australian citizenship, or even claim it as your primary citizenship because it is usually seen as the 2nd citizenship by our authorities, and therefore illegal to hold on Singapore soil.

    This is why some army mates of mine had to come back and serve once they finished high school in either Australia or US. In TS's case, I believe he is under the old legislation where if you post a bond and apply to defer your NS till post-overseas tertiary education is complete, you're safe.
