    The original rationale for confinement was to get new enlistees used to a regimented way of life without the distractions of a civilian life.

    However, for my BMTC batch, only the guys from my company and another got to taste the confinement almost towards its fullest (I could only book out on Christmas Eve, after 14 straight days on Tekong). Those who enlisted in January were lucky -- they had Chinese New Year break a few days after their enlistment, so essentially they were not confined.

    During the entire confinement period, I was actually quite comfortable, thanks to the support my section mates gave me. Without them, I might now have survived that 14 days. I cried on Parents Visiting day. It was simply too overwhelming, not seeing both my parents and the simple sights of my familiar surroundings.

    When I booked out on the 24 December 2003, I was reeling from shock. I was not used to seeing so many vehicles on the road. In fact, I nearly forgot about them, and crossed the road as if I were on Pulau Tekong...
