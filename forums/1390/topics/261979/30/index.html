    Hi Ssk,

    I will try to reply you your questions as much as possible as I had already out for quite some time.

    For CDO BMT, it was 2.5mths during my time in Hendon. Duration should not be too far from here.

    To put simply, CDO BMT (Recruit to Pte)--> CDO BCT (Pte to CPL)--> VT--> BAC--> CDO Fighter.

    VT -> Vocation training like Signal, Demolition/Boatman, Sniper, Weaponman, Medic and Section Leader Specialist (SLSC) course. From SLSC, you will passed out as 3SG. Among here, 15-20 will be chosen for OCS.

    Other CDO vocations will remain as CPLs. During my time, we got our CPL after CDO BCT training. Remembered CPL rank was handed to us after a 35km road march ended in Changi beach soaked in sea in middle of night.

    Warrant officer can be obtained if you are a specialist regular. Unlike NCC, no NSF can be Warrant officer for their 2.5yrs stay.

    Not sure abt chances of getting to OCS from Tekong BMT but you can calculate out of 250 recruited in CDO per batch, only 15-20 will get selected for OCS. Volunteer more during your BMT/BCT may help if you want to increase chance of getting into OCS. Ultimately, it is your attitude counts.

    You will need at least a A level cert or Diploma to get into OCS.

    To apply for CDO regular, you can apply before or during your NSF period. I remembered 1 of my mate signed on before entering NSF. No special benefit for him. We went thru same shit. In fact, the instructors whacked him more than us to make sure he has the cut of it. Like wht I said earlier, if you had signed on before or during NSF, plus you have the attitude and relevant cert, you can be selected for OCS. Pls bear in mind, OCS is mainly for Infantry officer. Once passed out from OCS, officers still need to go thru a CDO officer conversion course (COCC) before become a CDO officer.

    Hopefully I had answered most of your enquires.
