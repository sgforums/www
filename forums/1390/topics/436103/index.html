    for service medics, what i heard from others is that the course is about 3 months and stay-in. you will study 1st aid, basic medical theories and then take a couple of tests such as 1st aid, CPR and AED. and of course, the highlights is the need to poke 18G-sized cannula needles ("yakult straw") into your arms' veins for at least 10 xÂ  intravenous (IV) practices provided if the IV needles are "one shot, one kill".

    as for what to bring basically, you will need to bring just 2 or 3 sets of uniform as it's stay-in on a 5 days workweek basis and your fieldpack as you need to carry some books/ tutorial notes like a schoolboy to study the medical knowledge.

    lucky for you, service medics are excluded from outfield trainings because you are to work in camp medical centres, therefore there is no need to set up tentage for a mobile field clinic station like combat medics.
