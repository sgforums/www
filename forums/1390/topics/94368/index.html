    Hiz,
    Vehicle Technicians are called ATS - Auto Technician Specialist.
    Technicans are trained in 3 types; A, B and C.
    A is Armour, B is normal on road vehicles and C is for heavy hydraulics, i.e Forklift

    The course used to be a 6 months course for Armour Technicians consisiting of Phase 1 - Theory and Phase 2 - Practical. But from what I know, it is reduce to only practical in order to shorten the course and trainees are given CD-Rom to bring home to study. The course is conducted at OETI and is a stay out course though one still need to perform the regimental guard duty.
    When I attended the course 2 years back, we were taught first on the M113 Ultra, AMX-13 and then the BV-206. You will definitely taught and need to know how the engine works, including the fuel line, batteries, etc. The road system, especially the track is one of the most important too...
    This course is a 4/5 grade course, so the rank will be a 3SG. Normally for regulars, after a year of getting their 3SG rank, they will be promoted to 2SG. As for the CPF deductions and stuffs, I am not too sure...
    As for other courses with regards to Bionix, Bronco and RCV, you may be send by your unit to attend them...

    Yah, they are 2 different things.

    Being an Armour Technician, you will either be deployed to a combat unit or a base, depending on your PES status.

    If you are in a combat unit, this is further spilt into FMT (Forward Maintenance Team) and MMT (Main Maintenance Team). FMT are under the company's strength and will be in a RCV during exercises. During outfield, may be required to camo the vehicle. FMT also are on the constant move with the company though they lagger at the 'rear' with the OC and stuffs when they reach their designation and are only deployed out when needed.
    As for MMT, they are under HQ (Workshop) strength and may not be out in a vehicle during exercises. Those who are 'arrowed' to be on the move will be in a Bronco (3 ATS, 1 Comms Tech + 1 Turret Mech) and attached to a company to assist the FMT.

    For base technicians, I am not too sure.

    By the way, technicians also need to apply camo sometimes... (At least in my unit...)
