    This is strictly my opinion.

    Unless you need the money, don't carry on with it.  I'm not sure what the new schemes are like these days, but if you have the opportunity to flexibly plan your career, switch around, you should value it.  Cos once you are contracted, you lose all that flexibility.  

    If you still cant decide what you wanna do in life, don't jump into a military contract just because of that.  Take a few more years to study and upgrade yourself while figuring that out.  Or just start off a job with whatever training qualifications you have first and take your time to think through.

    One of the biggest traps that people whom I know have signed on said they fell into was "time."  They didn't really weigh out the decision carefully until they had went through the interview and were on the verge of signing.  This period which you are in is when a lot of my friends just decided "f-ck it, the pay is good anyway" and penned a contract which they regretted. 

    Like I said, unless you NEED the money, don't do it.
