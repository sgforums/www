    Channel NewsAsia - Saturday, September 1

    SAF unveils its Leopard tank at Army Open House

    SINGAPORE : The Singapore Armed Forces unveiled its latest acquisition, the Leopard tank, at the Army Open House on Saturday.

    The Open House is being held at the Pasir Laba Camp and provides what Second Minister for Defence Dr Ng Eng Hen says is an opportunity for Singaporeans to see where the defence dollars are going.

    The Leopard tanks will eventually replace some 300 SM1 tanks in the SAF.

    Dr Ng says: "They are obviously more technologically capable because they are of younger vintage and they will maintain our combined arms division, maintain our armour capability and part of our 3G transformation as well."

    However Dr Ng stressed that at the end of the day, it is not just the machines but the people factor that will be the the decisive factor when it comes to winning wars.

    The Army Open House is being held as part of 40 years of National Service celebrations in Singapore.

    It is an opportunity for Singaporeans to connect with the army and to find out for themselves the extent of development Singapore's army has undergone all these years to keep the country safe and secure.

    Dr Ng says: "We want to have the open house so that they can understand where the defence dollar is going. They can understand what preparations are being made to defend this island and their homes.

    The Open House is on till Monday. - CNA/ch
