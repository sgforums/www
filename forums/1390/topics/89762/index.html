    17 Aug 04, 12:12
    I was enlisted in Mar 1983; did BMT at ITD (Infantry Training Depot) at Tekong.

    One fellow recruit in same batch from another coy (Charlie) died when doing 16 km route march. Sad -- guy was from HCJC, only son. The incident had been covered extensively on forums before, and has generated many stories/"sightings" thereafter. Still remember seeing the CPLs practising the funeral drill, carrying a cupboard in the parade square (at camp 1, as no camp 3 yet nor the new "resort" at jetty).

    Recruit life then was different. We had to take RPL to Tekong, under hot sun & rain. Had to walk all the way from jetty to camp 1 & vice versa for weekly booking in & out. Only time we had the luxury of taking 3-tonners was when we had the ali-ba-ba bags (i.e. posting in/out). Go to 100m/300m range -- walk. Go to Botak hill or rocky hill -- walk. Go to reservoir for watermanship -- walk.

    Memories ...

  • specfore
    26 Aug 04, 17:16
    Hi, newbie here.

    I have also just reached 40 and given my demob papers. In another sense, I was also lucky, as I disrupted from reservist in 1995 and have been working overseas since.

    My reservist ICT frequency was bad... I faced 3 ICTs (average) per calendar year, plus the odd mobex or so. An example of how frequent I got called in is the fact that after about 3 -4 years of ICT, I received my Good Service Medal !!! Others in my unit, including my signaller received on the same occasion, the other one (the red one signifying 10 yrs of service !!!).

    Anyway, had great memories of NS days and reservists ICT (the camaraderie, even among fellow NCOs and our officers and the men). In fact, during reservists, the junior officers (LTAs and CPTs) and NCOs (2SGs, 3 SGs) had a lot in common, and we were often booking out together for makans and shopping etc.
