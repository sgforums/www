    Formed in October 2004, the 1st Battalion, Military Intelligence (1 MI Bn) is now ready for a spectrum of Peace-Time Contingency and Counter-Terrorism Operations and field intelligence gathering. Throughout this past year, the pioneering batch of soldiers from the battalion has been through extensive and realistic training to achieve these capabilities.

    To recognise the efforts of the soldiers, as well as to showcase the readiness of the battalion, the 1 MI Bn Inauguration Parade was held on 29 September at Lam Kiong Landing Site. Chief of Army, MG Desmond Kuek was the Guest-of-Honour.

    The battalion is at the forefront of Army Intelligence transformation, heavily experimenting in new technological systems and weapons. This was evident in a demonstration by the battalion at the parade featuring its Mini-Unmanned Aerial Vehicles and Intelligence Surveillance Teams.

    2LT Joshua Ng said, "Since we are the first to use much of these equipment, we have to implement our own operating procedures and doctrines."

    Regimental Sergeant Major of 1 MI Bn, 2WO Gilbert Chew said, "As an intelligence unit, we have to be constantly proactive, disciplined, observant and put in effort to collect as much information as possible. My men are capable and I am proud of them." -- ANI
