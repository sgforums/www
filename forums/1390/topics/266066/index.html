    Stand by universe? ~lol~ What a wonderful description.

    I did the pack-two-sets-of-everything too in my time. It's not kiasu, but very practical as you will find out in time.

    For BMT right through vocation course, including OCS/SISPEC if applicable, the regimentation will usually prompt the enlistees to set aside one set of items as "permanent" displays ~shrug~

    Once you get into the unit (presumably combat side) & it becomes ops-ready, you'll do proper week-long standby quite regularly & must prepare a standard-list fullpack for these periods. For convenience, the majority of troops packs the required stuff into different "parcels" (ziplock bags) &, during the downtimes, leave them untouched inside the cupboard.

    I found a list of the items online. Most will be issued to you, except perhaps the toiletries. Not sure if things are still as strict now but, during my ITD time (1980s), even personal stuff like plastic mugs & torches & shaving kits must be "standardized." What happened was my Ptn Sgt collected money from all the RECs, bought the stuff at the Tekong gift shop, & distributed the plastic bags to us like the Primary Schoolers & their textbooks. No negotiation, no pick-&-choose, & any stuff you brought in yourself was "rejected."
