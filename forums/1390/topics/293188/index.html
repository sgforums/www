    Frankly, I can't comprehend the combat applicants' mindset too. Technical or logistics, yes. (Same goes for the other service branches & the paramilitary organizations.)

    But with combat, I guess it has more to do with the immortality sentiments among the youthful, although today's generation also conceivably (pun) consists of some ex-"army brats". Perhaps they have prior romantic notions of the SAF as like the US Marine Corps or French Foreign Legion too. (If so, the more astute should aim to sign on with local elite forces -- any branch -- cuz the ethos they seek does exist with those small & professional units.)

    As long as we're in peacetime -- & prospering -- the military remains an attractive career choice, particularly for officers or those well-qualified, with guaranteed job security, material benefits & the chance to recycle on later with GIC companies if they stick it out.

    Yes, the first 6 years tend to be backbreaking with the operational demands at the BN level. But, thereafter, life does become more of an office-hours routine as you get promoted, posted on to the BDE or DIV or Formation HQ level, & can delegate down. That's the usual hierarchy structure of any military organization, what.

    The other complaint mentioned in your post -- which IMO is the main alienating factor -- is the bureaucracy nature of the SAF, which isn't surprising since we aren't on a war footing. As such, it also partly operates as an mammoth administration bureau like any government agency, with the same propensity for organizational structures, red tape & office politics. Except it can be more regimented, being military. The combat-inclined regulars must live with the system, or quit altogether.

    I would beg to differ on several issues mentioned by the original poster. Firstly, the situation he describes would occur regardless of the organization that he is in. Does that SSG think that such petty tyranny and politicking only occurs in the SAF? Besides, any assertion about a vicious cycle is pointless because in the civilian world, the first big mistake you made would get you fired anyway.

    Next, please remember that the SAF only has 1 Bn of CDO and 2 Bn of GDS. Not everybody wants to go there, and not everybody who can make it in will make it in. The ultimate goal of the SAF is to defend singapore, and to do so, it has to have competent people in EVERY vocation. While individual choice does play a part, organisational needs must triumph. This is the same in every company that you want to work for, except that in the case of commercial companies, if your needs don't match theirs, they just don't hire you. =)

    Fact of the matter is, not everybody can tahan the long and shitty hours that are part and parcel of being in the military. The pay is getting better, and regardless of the pay, it is sufficient, and if no savings are made that is a fault of the individual and not the organisation.

    Also, I would think that the military is NOT an attractive career for everybody when the economy is booming, as shown by the recent pay increases that were announced.

    Yes, the airforce and the navy are different from the army--in that they are more glamorous. But the differences are getting smaller and ultimately you still need all three services to defend Singapore.
