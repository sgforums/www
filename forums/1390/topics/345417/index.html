    Let me clarify some stuff that hasn't been clarified here. At least this was what happened to me during my time.

    1) Once the camp MO say you OOC/OOT, it takes effect immediately, once you show the letter to your coy people (ie. PC, PS etc...)

    2) Yes you will stay in camp to do sai kang (manual labour, clerical work (as mentioned)) until you get your posting, which will normally come 1-1.5 months after you officially OOC.

    3) As I answered earlier, you won't be reporting to another camp straight after you OOC, posting takes some time. SAF not that efficient. And what you heard isn't 100% true, not even close. You may not necessarily be a clerk but your vocation will not involve excessive physical activity for sure.

    4) Yes, but only when your C1L2 status is confirmed. As far as I can deduce from what you are saying, it is still pending medical board and your status will probably only be confirmed a sometime later, few weeks - months perhaps. And, even when it is confirmed, it will be quite long before you are actually scheduled to go for your BMT recourse. Took me 6 months, I'm C2L2. For some, it could even be a case of after the recourse, its time to clear leave already.

    5) Yes, but don't be surprised if you don't become one or enjoy 8 to 5.

    6) Other vocations could be storeman, driver, medic (the ones at medical centre), mechanic or armourer (possible, my armourer at my unit was a C9L3).

    7) Depends on the unit you are posted to. Most likely you will need to go for at least 1 ICT (in-camp training). If you have a serious spine problem, make sure you get a permanent excuse from the MO so that other people cannot make you carry heavy load.

    8) & 9) No to both

     

    1 more thing to add. Personally I feel PES C1L2 is a fairly shitty pes to be in. If possible, at least try to get C2L2. Coz C1 generally still have to take IPPT, just excuse a few stations. And even if you excel in your remaining stations, you cannot get any financial reward. Therefore it is just a waste of your time...
