    I wore it as part of a Guard of Honor for a fellow gunner who got killed in the line of duty; under the tragic circumstances I'd rather not have the opportunity at all, frankly.

    The No. 1 uniform isn't as glamorous to me. We drew it out of our unit store & they stank stale (even after laundrying) & needed a lot of improvisory tailoring (inc. ranks & ill-fitting collars). What's worst of all is the requisitory drills that come along with the attire, to be conducted by a RSM, no less. Imagine marching around the parade square for countless hours a day & for many days, trying to get used to the unfamiliar drill commands & "SER-MU-LAH! SER-MU-LAH!" all the time.

    The NDP contingents go through very much the same process, except they kena burnt weekends along the way too. So the visual appearance on the big day always has its context to me, cuz I recognize how much sacrifices were made to prepare for the occasion.
