    As a medic spec, I've done a number of duties at the SAF ward at Alexandra Hospital. Seen plenty of such cases before.

    Among some of the more notable cases I've encountered:

    1. There was one Malay REC who went into breakdown during BMT. He said that he had asked a bomoh to place a curse on his poly lecturer before he enlisted. If I remember correctly, he had also supposedly placed a curse on one of his instructors during BMT.

    He now regretted what he did. He went into breakdown mode over the fear of God's wrath for what he did. As a fellow Muslim, I gave him some advice regarding repentance and God's mercy.

    2. One REC was warded over a suicide attempt. He went up to his instructor with a knife and threatened to slash his wrists. His commander taunted him and dared him to do so. In the end, the REC dropped the knife.

    In my POV, what the instructor did was absolutely wrong - you should never, ever take a suicide threat lightly, and worse still, challenge him to do it. It could have gone terribly wrong, leading to the REC slashing himself.

    Reverse psychology does not necessarily apply to all situations.
