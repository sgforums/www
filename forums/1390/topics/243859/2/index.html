    What's with the rolling eyes?

    The nature of the current Commandos' work leads back to the role of the original Commandos of WW2 - close quarter combat, silencing of sentries and such. A properly sharpened bayonet could do the job, but the material and design on the Fairbairn-Sykes is close to ideal for the job. Being primarily designed as a stabbing weapon, the narrowness of the blade allows it to slide more easily between ribs. The shape of the handle also allows easier switching of the knife's position from tip-out to tip-in. And sorry, storywolf, but the F-B isn't balanced enough for throwing.

    In any case, killtuna is half-right. The stilettos are kept in the armskote and rarely, if ever, drawn out, but they are in fact issued. For daily use, I replaced my bayonet with a Gerber Mark II as a fighting blade, and I carried a Swiss Army knife along as a utility knife - it was a lot handier than any kukri or gung-ho rubbish Jungle King.

    At the end of the day, it's a tool, as much as a changkul or an L-torch is. No big deal unless you're a glory hound, and there's precious little space for that at Hendon.
