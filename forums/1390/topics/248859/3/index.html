    21 Apr 07, 19:09
    Originally posted by Skibi:
    [b]Commandos are generally better than normal soldiers because they are trained to keep things simple and stupid. In a high-stress combat situation, the simplest method is often the most idiot-proof and efficient method. Just do the minimum required to achieve the objective.
    Not quite. While the simplest solution is often the best one, you can't take that as an assumption. The most effective solution is one that suits the situation and is reasonably simple to execute, but you don't necessarily fall back on automatic drills.

    Case in point: I was in a four-man recce team on a BTEC exercise against a Guards unit. As the last man to cross a track, I just started making my way across when three Guards Rovers came round the corner. I dived behind the bush where the rest of my team was and the Rovers stopped on the other side of the bush. We were most likely outnumbered, and the simple and stupid thing to do would have been to get out of Dodge right quick. On the other hand, if the Guardsmen decided to come after us, it would all be over.

    I sent two guys a few metres to the right while I and the remaining team member laid down fire, and I started shouting, "Section 2, right flank!". The other two guys waited a few seconds and strated laying down their own fire. Once we finished one magazine each, we headed for the hills. After getting about half a click away, we checked back just in time to see an umpire pop smoke, simulating arty.

    What I pulled wasn't the simplest solution, but I still reckon we'd have been done like dinner if we simply ran. By scamming the other side into thinking they were facing a platoon strength and getting them to delay by calling arty instead, we bought enough time to get out without getting hassled.
