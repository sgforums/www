    Originally posted by sgboy2004:
    if u NS(LTA)...u still wear back your NS rank, not the NCC one lah
    I think there has been a change in policy, spoke to a former NCC Staff Officer who has since retired....My understanding is that .... In the past if you were a NS Officer and as a teacher, got NCC as your CCA, you get to retain your NS rank completely; in other words you wear your Singapura rank and your NS formation berret. If you're commando, you wear your red berret but change the crest to NCC crest. This is so even as when you are a trainee in the NCC Officer Training Course.

    But now,

    During course, regardless of past NS background, all trainees will wear trainee ranks, similar to service term OCT, i.e. 2 white strips but with the word NCC instead of Singapura. After course, what rank each NCC officer will be depends on his NS rank. If the officer is LTA(NS), he will get LTA(NCC). If the person is a 3SG(NS) , he will start off with 2LT(NCC).

    Subsequently, if an NS Officer gets promoted in his SAF rank, he will receive a smiliar promotion in NCC. Let's say a LTA(NS) gets promoted to CPT(NS), he will similarly get promoted from LTA(NCC) to CPT(NCC). But a promotion within the NCC ranks doesn't earn you a promotion in SAF ranks lah..

    Yet to hear of any NCC Officers being promoted beyond to the ranks of COL(NS) and above. Not too sure of this situation if it ever happens.

    Hope I didn't get any facts wrong
