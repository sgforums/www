    It's much easier than it sounds, seriously.

    I think maybe the most shiong part of your field camp will be the route march there, and then the shellscrape digging. You'll enjoy BIC even though it's quite tiring crawling all the way because it means return to coy line, lol

    Actually there is a lot of waiting, and just sitting around doing nothing, because there's so much to plan and do on the commanders' side, and only so many commanders to go around.

    You'll learn group movement and attack, and get to fire blanks. Might be fun, because I heard from some of my friends that their group 'missions' were really improvised and 'exciting'. But in my case there was too much to learn on the spot, whole section ended up cocking up every single drill and getting punished for that.

    There is night training i.e. night walks so on and so forth, but I think it's just an excuse for tekan when the commanders start doing funny things in the darkness like steal equipment or mislead recruits, then punish the platoon for not sticking together.

    In fact for my field camp I was sleeping much earlier each night as compared to back in coy line. And it varies from coy to coy, but we managed to take a 'shower' on the 4th day at Rocky Hill camp. Really helps boost your morale.

    All in all I really enjoyed my field camp, actually. But it will be a different story if it rains, so do pray that it doesn't rain during your field camp.
