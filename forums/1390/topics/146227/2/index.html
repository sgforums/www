    Originally posted by EXCO:
    The SAFTI State Colours has the same design as the SAF State Colours, except that it has an added scroll beneath the SAF emblem. The scroll is inscribed with the words "SAFTI MI".



    The SAFTI State Colours is on the left, observe closely and you will be able to see the scroll in blue. It is only paraded during OCS Commissioning Parades (as far as I know...).

    In the past, many units had both the State and Regimental Colours (Ie. 1st CDO BN). After a revision of the Colours system, all unit State Colours were retired, but the SAFTI MI State Colours was left untouched. The unit State Colours look similar to the SAFTI MI State Colour, but the scroll would be inscribed with the name of the unit.
    There is no SAFTI MI State Colour... Regimental Colours are awarded to the Formation or Units about five years after its birth. They bear the appropriate crests or logos of the respective Divisions that they belong to. State Colours are given to Services and SAFTI MI for being an international institution.
