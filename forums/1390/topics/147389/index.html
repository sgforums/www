    Hi everyone,

    I'm contemplating signing on with the SAF (army) since I completed my degree (overseas uni) last month. But a few of my guy friends who were NSF or currently serving strongly advised me against it. One of them reckons it's pointless for a girl to join as she will be discriminated against and because of her gender she won't go far. What is your take on this?

    It sounds almost too old-fashioned to think that way, and there is just so much cynism coming from our NSF. When I asked him if it was just the NSF who feel this way, he said there are regulars who also hate their jobs in the army. I am greatly interested in the military and this is a great opportunity for me to serve my country. My friend said there's a female regular in his camp who said the same thing (she wants to serve her country) and he just doesn't believe/understand her. Is there anyone here who signed on because they are interested in the job or because you'll like to serve the country?

    I know that at the end of the day I make my own decisions, but I'll really like to hear from what you guys have to say. Because I've been reading your posts for a while and I really admire your maturity. It's a fresh break from the "singapore sux", SAF-bashing, unconstructive comments that I usually hear. hopefully I'll be able to get some insight from everyone reading this thread.
