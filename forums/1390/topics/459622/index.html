    The Combat Engineers
    The Combat Engineers are an unique breed of professionals who are versatile and creative. Equipped with state-of-the-art machinery, the Engineers possess both brawn and brains for the daunting challenges that await them in peace and war. As Combat Engineers, we specialize in Mobility, Counter Mobility and Survivability operations such as Fortification, Clearing of Enemy Obstacles and Demolitions.


    Mobility
    The Combat Engineers overcome obstacles in the path of our advancing forces to give them freedom of movement and manoeuvre. Logistic supply lines are kept open by the combat engineers to enhance the sustainability of operations.
    Counter-mobility
    The combat engineers lay minefields, demolish bridges and routes and set up booby-traps to slow down the enemy.



    Survivability
    To protect our troops against enemy fire, the combat engineers use their heavy plants and demolition skills to construct trenches, weapons emplacements, command posts etc.



    Skills

    Field Engineers

    Field Engineers undertake crucial mobility, counter-mobility and survivability tasks to support the Army in its operation. Equipped with highly mechanised and modern equipment, Field Engineers are the ones who clear obstacles in the paths of our advancing forces to ensure mobility, construct obstacles in the paths of the enemy forces to counter their mobility and erect field fortifications to ensure the survivability of our own forces.

    Armoured Engineers

    Equipped with the most advanced machinery, Armoured Engineers require a significantly shorter time to clear complicated obstacles, ensuring the armoured forces' intrinsic high mobility and manoeuvrability.

    Armoured Engineers work together as a team to pave the way, removing all obstacles by means of heavy lifting and dozing. Protected by thick armour, the Armoured Engineers initial fear by virtue of their size.

    Bridging Engineers

    Bridging Engineers provide the transportation means to project combat troops and vehicles across rivers and water obstacles to engage the enemy.

    Bridging Engineers enhance the mobility of our forces by their specialised skills in operating state-of-the art equipment in the form of float bridges, rafts and assault boats. Bridging Engineers are highly skilled in both land and water operations.

    CBRE Engineers

    The CBRE Engineers comprises of the Chemical Defence (CD) Engineers and the Explosive Ordnance Disposal (EOD) Engineers. They are equipped with state-of-the-art equipment to respond to any Chemical, Biological, Radiological and Explosives (CBRE) threats. Visioned to be a world class operationally – ready and technologically advanced CBRE Force, they provide specialist CBRE advice and support throughout the spectrum of operations in peace and war.
