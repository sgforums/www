    Erm, many of the posts here show a lack of understanding of the way things truly are.

    For starters, the SAR-21 is actually marginally lighter then the M-16. It weights 3.98kg while the M-16 is 4.06. I dunno where you got the 2kg plus weight for the M-16 but it's quite off.

    Also, better balance and egronomics make the SAR-21 easier to handle and shoot. It is far more reliable, hardly IAs, and breaks down into two main modules in the field within 12 seconds for cleaning, try that with your M-16.

    Also, there is far less cleaning with the SAR, construction is polymer for most of the rifle, hence problems with corrosion is far less then the M-16. The SAR is also far more durable and can be slammed several times onto hard concrete and still function. Try that with your M-16 again!

    Also, once you learn how to use the 1.5x sight, SAR-21 is far, far easier to shoot then the M-16. As a matter of fact, rate of W.O kings dropped dramatically when shooting the SAR, opposed the M-16 iron sighting.

    In conclusion, the SAR actually proves to be generally a superior infantry weapon to the M-16, it seems that most of the bad press about it comes from people who have not had much experience with the rifle and those who are deeply engrained in viewing the world of weapons through the M-16 mindset.

    If anything, the M-16 has had the history of being one of the lest reliable and most hated firearms when it was first introduced, go figure those who can't sleep without it. The SAR is clearly superior.

    If anything, the M16's weight is forward heavy, as opposed to the centralized CG of the SAR-21. Leaverage plays a larger role in egronomics then sheer weight. This means that despite being lighter in weight, it is actually harder to handle and aim the M16 as it requires quite a bit more strength and cordination from the left hand.

    I don't think many modern rifles are made to be front heavy like the M16. The general theory now is that either centralized CG (sar-21) or rear weighted rifles (TAR-21) are easier to point index and use.

    Centralized CG allows the SAR-21 to be easily held and fired one handed, if need be. By focusing the weight onto the right hand, the left hand is left with far less weight to adjust and aim the rifle, allowing quicker snap point indexing and easier aiming.
