    Will talk about a typical day in Navy(if no sailing)

    Reach by 8.30am.

    8.30am, go OC cabin muster, then see if he got anything to see.
    9.00am, go back Cabin, take a rest.
    10.00am go walk around ship, pretend to be busy.
    11am go office, check emails(work emails, not personal ones)
    11.30am LUNCH!!!
    1pm, go my radio rm, and test-check the equipment to check everything is in working order
    2pm. Check with my Coxn see if he wants me to do anything.
    2.30pm, Check with my OC see if he wants me to do anything.
    3pm, go gym, e-mart, tea break or whatever. In short, SLACK!!!
    4.30pm, Go home!!!

    Take note that my actual going home time is not 4.30, but only when there's nothing to do, and there's no sailing the next day.

    If got sailing next day..
    8.30am-Reach ship, sianz feeling
    9am-Go dining area talk to NSmen, do in-processing etc, issue them cabin keys, life jackets, gear etc
    10.30am-Load ration onto ship
    11am-Find up which NSMen appointment are Radio Operators, talk to them, tell them what are expected of them during sailing trip. Tell them how to operate the equipment.
    11.45-Go do up sail plan, let OC sign.
    12.30-Lunch
    1pm-Go office send sail plan
    1.30pm-Go back ship, NSmen do shipboard drills, but have 2 stay besides NSMen see what complaints/queries they have, and try to help them.
    3pm-Go check who from SQN sailing with us, and finalise NSmen nominal roll with the NSmen officers.
    3.30pm-Army vehicles prepare to be put onboard ship.
    4pm-Send nominal roll, and call base see if they got receive.
    4.30pm-NSmen all go home prepare next day sailing. Me check that all cabins for them are in order.
    6pm+++-All vehicles onboard. Inform base about it, and go home. Or if past 7pm liao, then stay onboard ship and sleep.
