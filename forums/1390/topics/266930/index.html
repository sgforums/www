    GSOC stands for General Staff Operations Centre. It's one of them command/control entities that come under MINDEF control, I believe, rather than the SAF's. All the service branches -- Army, Navy, Air Force, PDF -- have such headquarters at the top of their hierarchy. As already posted, those working at that level are called G-3 staff.

    Such structural definitions are quite common in many militaries:
    Personnel = G-1 (or S-1 at the BN level)
    Intelligence = G-2 (S-2)
    Combat Ops = G-3 (S-3)
    Logistics = G-4 (S-4)

    There's also G-5 & G-6 at the staff-officer level, but no idea what they do. Beyond reading in the papers about so-&-so office-holder receiving a National Day Award, that is.
