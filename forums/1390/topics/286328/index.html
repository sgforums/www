    MrJacky, are you in Sec. 3 now or you have a kid in Sec. 3?

    Basically whatever the army needs you to learn, is not found in things at the Secondary School level. So its abit irrelevant.

    Officers
    To be a combatant officer a Polytechnic diploma of any discipline or an 'A' level certificate plus you must be combat fit, and you meet the situational test requirements at BMT, then you'll be selected to be an officer.

    Officers who qualify for university education, will also disrupt mid-career after they get their Lieutenant rank to continue with their degree. Nowadays to be a high-flying officer and reach Lieutenant-Colonel and above, you probably need a degree. Unless you're super super good officer.

    Nerds don't qualify as officers

    Contrary to popular believe, the army does not just look at paper.
    I've seen scholars with straight A, combat fit, but they are nerds. They signed their letter of intent, but at the end of BMT, they were deemed unsuitable to be an officer, because of their lack of leadership skills or the skills to be adaptable to situations. SAF officers are more than just nerds.

    SpecialistTo be a specialist (a sergeant to warrant officer) rank, you need a minimum of 'O' levels, although most specialists nowadays are Poly Dip holders. For specialists, as long you're combat fit and have 'O' levels, automatic acceptance.

    Specific vocations
    Unless you're aiming for special vocations, such as you really want to rise up to be the TOP engineer for Singapore armour, or the TOP engineer for Chemical warfare....then of course you may need a degree or diploma in that, which will mean you need to have the relevant subjects at 'O' level to qualify for those subjects later on.

    Or if you wish to be an engineer in the airforce or navy, you definitely need physics at 'O' level to take Electrical, Electronics, etc. disciplines of engineering later on in tertiary education, in order to qualify as an Engineering Officer.
