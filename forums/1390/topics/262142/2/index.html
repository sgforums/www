  • Kuali Baba
    20 Jun 07, 01:47
    You have to decide whether you want to bein the Army, Navy or Air Force, an officer or a specialist (non-degree holders don't go very far with the former), and then there are several different vocations that you have to choose from. Remember you'll be in this for at least 10 years once you sign on.

    What should you expect? Annual IPPT and range firing, duty this-and-that, parades, half marathons, projects, exercises, audits galore, performance ranking and (depending on vocation) attachments to SAF camps overseas.

    You'll have to use up your leave every calendar year. There are benefits you can claim from but if you quit early you have to pay back, with interest.

    You'll get jaded easily if you go in with the images from boyhood, the advertisements or the movies. It can get very political among colleagues and with difficult superiors. Heed the advise of the others and experience NS first, because you'll have one year from your enlistment date to decide.

    1 ) real comradeship... cheong together albeit against an imaginery enemy

    2 ) no one wanna work
    3 ) rank/appointment talks

    and a lot more.

    but in a nutshell, i'd like to say that money can be earned somewhere else, but your lifespan is limited. wat u gonna do once SAF used u and spat u out? without experience in anything other than Army... how?
