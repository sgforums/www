    hey, i dunno if someone else have posted it but for the benefit of the people here and those going to enlist. I will intro to you what the new BMT Syllabus is all about.

    OLD BMT: 6 weeks PTP, 10 weeks BMT.

    NEW BMT: 4 Weeks PTP, 9 weeks BMT. (with effect from Dec 2004 for PES A/B, PES C recruits still retain their syllabus)

    No more, interval training, circuit training.

    New... Combat PT, Ability Group Runs, 30:60 and strength training.

    Environmental changes: The training program now is packed than the past because a 16 week syllabus is squeezed into 13. So much so that BMT stuff are taught during your PTP phase. So during the PTP phase, you will learn partially technical handling with some companies conducting the tests during PTP.

    There will be no more parent visiting day and it is instead replaced by thurs to staurday enlistment and a guaranteed friday book out 2 weeks later.

    However, if during your PTP/BMT there are any public holidays, chances are that you may burn an additional weekend during the fieldcamp week. Meaning you book in on Monday and book out the 2nd saturday.

    Less emphasis is placed on SOC, with only 1-2 full run downs scheduled.

    Gym training is now own time own target.

    DUe to the COmbat PT syllabus, physical training now tend to be draggy. you usually start like at 0730 hrs, and it finishes around 1030hrs. very boring.

    any questions???

    Combat PT: a warm up exercise comprising 10 different workouts. it is to be used as a warm up if the activity you are warming up for is not streneous (IPPT, SOC is considered streneous) THis is supposed to be working out the muscle groups that is essential for army training even after BMT.

    ABility group runs: the company is sub-divided into groups of 4 according to their ability to run 2.4 km. these groups will run for 20-30 minutes according to their group ability. e.g. a person in group A, i.e. able to run 2.4 under 12:09, will run 400m in 2 mins 15 secs for 20-30 minutes. This is a progressive training, the lap time for 400m will decrease eventually and overall run time will increase.

    30:60: this is similar to interval training, but rather than sprinting for a distance, you will be ask to sprint for a duration. So you will sprint for 30 seconds, rest 60 seconds. You will start with 8 repetitions and gradually increase. The lap time will increase to 60:120 later in BMT.

    Strength training: This comprises 15 different workouts, involving dumbells (10) and medicine balls (5). This will train up your core and upper limb muscles groups.
