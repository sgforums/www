    Physical Employment Status (PES)

    PES A - Fit for all vocations
    PES B - Fit for most vocations
    PES BP - Fit for 5 months full BMT (applicable only to obese recruits)

    PES C - Fit for some vocations
    C1 - Require to take IPPT but excused up to 2 static stations
    C2 - Do not have to take IPPT but Regulars need to take the Alternative Aerobic Fitness Test (AAFT)
    C9 - Not required to take IPPT for all

    L1 - Fit for field duties
    L2 - Fit for field duties but only in rear areas
    L3 - Fit for base duties only

    PES D - Temporary unfit for grading and pending further review
    PES E - Fit for Administrative duties only;
    E1 - Able to participate in simple activities
    E9 - Unfit for any form of physical activities

    L9 - Suitable for sedentary duties at bases;

    PES F - Medically unfit for any form of service.

    i would like to know if my new pes status will require me to acquire a new vocation, or if i could still continue as a driver.
    I doubt the SAF will re-train you again, unless the system's undergone a complete policy makeover since my time. It involves just too much coordination/logistics to underwrite for such infrequent casualties. My guess is you'll get redeployed elsewhere within the same unit, subject to vacancy (CQ, mess boy, et al). That way, you're nobody else's "problem" since you don't have to be posted out.

    That's what happened to my Arty mate, also a M113 driver. He ended up following the rations truck - not as switched-off as you think - all the way through reservist service. [BTW In reservist, a downgraded soldier won't even get redeployed, internally or kicked out, unless he's really a hard-up case; the most they'll do is excuse you from IPPT & formal parades & stuff. That's my experience, anyway.]

    "can my csm force me to continue as a combat driver regardless of my pes status? or is he prohibited from doing so according to the policies of the saf."
    No, it's outside of his control, since the downgrade is done by MMI. Talk to your BC or PC, once your medical center has validated your new reclassification.

    "why am i given a temporary downgrade only?"
    No idea; flat feet are evaluated on a case-by-case basis, I guess. It could be 6 or 9 months next time round. Maybe your MO can explain the diagnosis, once he's able to read your new medical docket.
