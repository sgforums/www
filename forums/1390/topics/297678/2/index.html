    Originally posted by Icemoon:
    Why doesn't 1 CDO make the training mandatory for the troopers then?
    You're missing the point, Icemoon. The Ranger course and the Ranger battalions are two related but entirely separate things.

    Commando LRRP companies already have LRRP training and all companies are trained in the strike role (I don't know if the distinction exists following the changes in the NS structure, though), so the ORBAT-related issue is already addressed.

    On the other hand, as the others have already said, the Ranger course is more of a leadership course. The Commando section leader course can be seen to be a more basic and scaled-down version of the Ranger course, and is in fact conducted by Ranger cadres (or it was the last time I checked).

    Like others have pointed out - US Ranger units and ranger course are 2 different things (with 2 different tabs as well).

    Members of the US Ranger units must pass a 4 week Ranger Indoctrination Program (RIP) before posted to one of the US Ranger battalions.
    http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ranger_Indoctrination_Program

    The US Ranger school on the other hand, conducts a 9 week leadership course - http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ranger_School .

    Being professionals, most, if not all, US Rangers take up the 9 week Ranger school course in order to progress in their military careers (hence 2 "ranger" tabs on their uniforms).

    The roles of the US Ranger units are similar to our own commando unit. US Rangers are commandos - British commonwealth countries use the term commandos, whereas, the US choose the word "Rangers" instead. More details on the naming can be found in the history of US Ranger units formed during WW2.

    And please - not all units named "rangers" are special warfare/forces units.
