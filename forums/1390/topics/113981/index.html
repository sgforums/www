    From mindef.gov.sg :-

    379 officer cadets (OCTs) from the Singapore Armed Forces (SAF) were commissioned as officers in a parade graced by President S R Nathan at the SAFTI Military Institute this evening.

    A total of 379 cadets from the Officer Cadet School (OCS) were commissioned - 325 from the Army, 40 from the Navy, and 14 from the Air Force. Four officer cadets from the Royal Brunei Armed Forces were also among them. Seven of the SAF cadets received top accolades as Sword-of-Honour winners.

    The parade marked the completion of an intensive 42 weeks of training, which included a four-week tri-service term where officer cadets of the three services trained together. They were trained and assessed in physical fitness, military knowledge, and leadership. The newly commissioned officers will be assigned combat, instructional or staff appointments in the various units of the SAF.

    Also present at the parade were Minister for Defence, Teo Chee Hean, Minister in Prime MinisterÂ’s Office and Second Minister for Finance, Mr Lim Hng Kiang, Minister of State for Foreign Affairs and Mayor for North East CDC, Mr Zainal Abidin, Chief of Defence Force, LG Ng Yat Chung, and Chief of Navy, RADM Ronnie Tay, Members of Parliament, grassroots leaders, residents from the neighbourhood, NCC cadets, and students from junior colleges. Such public involvement and strong support underscore the importance of National Service in nation building, and the SAF as a foundation for SingaporeÂ’s security, survival, and success.
