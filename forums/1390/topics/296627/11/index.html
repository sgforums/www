    I got a friend formerly from 1SIR and he said its fill with school dropouts, gangsters etc.
    Arbuthen? But there's also the old stereotype, lah. Nowadays the majority have at least N levels, ever since VITB became purely post-secondary & their technical students switched to the Normal Stream. The dropout rate is very low now, about 3-5% only.

    There's always a few pai kia in every mono intake, but more teenage street-corner types, lah. They'd feared BMT (& confinement!) as much as the other recruits. I had a few in the Arty batch I taught in '88, & it was surprising who turned out to be the real criminal later on. One was fully-tattooed & turned up at CMPB with an incredibly-gelled yellow mane -- really nervous when he ended up in my section -- except he turned out to be a pushover. Another was a incorrigible loudmouth, whining all the time -- but the most rebellious he got was sneaking cigarette breaks after lights out, or walking with his No.4 unbuttoned after bookout. It was the latter's buddy-deputy, your classic 2IC type & quite leery during BMT, whom I found out in the newspapers a few years later to have been jailed for running a pirated-VCD ring!

    There were all types in my batch: N Levels, O Levels, ITE, proper apprentices, ex-dropout menial laborers & illegal peddlers (copy watch, sir?) as well as some rich men's sons. Really a motley crew.
