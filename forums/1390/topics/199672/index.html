Lately i've been feeling regretful and disappointed

  • Puutan
    11 Jul 06, 16:30
    I really needed to write out my feelings about what i have felt about my eventual national service enlistment in 10 days.

    Frankly speaking, i am quite disappointed in myself.

    As you might know earlier from my earlier topic, i have a PES status of C1L3. That means i am a non-operationally fit serviceman who could only perform duties at bases only.

    And i've been asking around about what type of vocations i would be most likely assigned to and i have grown to despise the single word: clerk.

    Maybe i am being a little stupid , but i believe that i could be capable of doing something more. Something that would be worthwhile doing and decently challenging. I'd really like to learn how to handle a rifle and learn the basics of infantry movement but i don't think i'll go through such lessons during the course of my national service. Maybe i am feeling this way since i just don't think that office based jobs or desk bound jobs aren't what i like.

    I talked to my friend who has signed up as a naval officer, he told me that the chances of me retaking my BMT under a different PES status would be very slim as alot of paper work is involved. He even told me to go through another medical examination to get it upgraded but there's too little time for that.

    But i have no choice but to go through my modified BMT and give it my best effort, even though i would feel a little bad because i wouldn't be combat trained as a national serviceman.

    So, as i promised i'll try to log onto the SAF forums and update all of you of my experiences and the experiences of other NS men while in my time in camp.

    Sorry, i just need to voice it out, because i really feel this way.

    i was once pes e, while all my peers were pes a or b. you can imagine how isolated and "oustanding" i felt. i couldnt understand a single thing they talked about when we were out. i had thoughts like you then too - that i'd rather be "normal".

    well. luckily where i was serving as a clerk, the env was great - the people there were closely knit and treated me very well. i realised also that i do contribute my part as a clerk.

    when i was eventually upgraded and went through the normal ns, all my friends have either ord-ed or were at advanced stages of ns.

    i was grateful for the clerical stint i had - it really allowed me to see ns from an entirely different perspective. i was glad to be back to "normal" too, and appreciated that more.

    the bottomline is - understand what really is bothering you - the desire to serve in a more physical setting, or the desire to be like most of the other people, including your peers?

    also, dont ever belittle what you do, and instead aspire to learn as much as possible, and contribute in whatever ways you can. how your ns experience turns out to be is very much defined by your outlook and attitude towards it. the nation would very much appreciate your contribution in best of your abilities in your own capacity, whatever it may be.

  • fudgester
    13 Jul 06, 21:11
    Well, I've got nothing much to say, for I believe that every one else has pretty much said what I wanted to say.

    But just let me add on a little something: PES C or not, when you're in the SAF, every single person has a part to play. You may end up as a clerk who helps to plan duty rosters for the officers and the specs. You may end up as a service medic who helps save lives in a 24-hour medical center.

    In any case, since you're not yet in the army, you're not really in a position to gauge how good or bad your experience is going to be. I've seen far too many people who aim of being rangers, snipers, commandos, etc only to get disillusioned during their BMT and hoping that they became clerks. Heck, I know of cases of fighting fit men who smash their little fingers with a hammer in the hope that they can get downgraded.

    In the end, the grass is always greener on the other side. We have clerks who wish they can be chiong sua warriors, and we have chiong sua warriors who wish they can be clerks. No one ever gets completely satisfied.

    So why think so negatively? Ever heard of the Pygmalion Effect? Your experience will be negative only if you want it to be. The best thing is to go into NS with an open mind. Since you're gonna be stuck in there for the next two years, you might as well make the most of it.
