    SINGAPORE: The Republic of Singapore Air Force (RSAF) celebrates its 40th anniversary this year, the highlight of which is an Open House.

    Defence Minister Teo Chee Hean opened the two-day event at the Paya Lebar Air Base on Saturday.

    Crowds packed the air base the moment the doors opened, with excited aviation enthusiasts heading straight for their favourite machines.

    The RSAF has come a long way since its humble beginnings in the late sixties. It is now an advanced and integrated third-generation air
    force.

    That's also the theme of this year's Open House, which aims to educate the public about the RSAF's milestones in defence capabilities and operations.

    Visitors can get up close and personal with the aircraft on static display. They can also get hands-on experience by hopping into a F-15 Strike Eagle fighter jet simulator.

    Visitors can also win rides on aircraft like the Fokker-50 and Chinook helicopter.

    And if you missed the aerial display at the National Day Parade earlier this month, you can catch it at the air force Open House.

    The Open House is on till Sunday, from 9am to 7pm.

    A commemorative book and a set of stamps have also been launched to mark the RSAF's 40th anniversary.

    - CNA/ir
