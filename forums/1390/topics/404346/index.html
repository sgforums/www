  • fudgester
    5 Jul 10, 10:46

    If you really want to sign on, let it be for the right reason, that is: you truly like life in the armed forces, and you have a true passion for what you're doing.

    I always tell people to go for BMT first before making a decision to sign on. The reason for this is simple: your perceptions can change overnight once you've had a taste of military life. I know of previously-bochup people who got turned on after BMT, and previously-gungho people who got jaded and disillusioned during BMT. You simply cannot tell whether or not you have the passion until you have a taste of it.

    Money? That should be the last reason for signing on. I know of regulars who got seduced by the money only to regret later on.

    Glam? Signing on for the glamour is just as bad a reason. Sure, it's nice to have a few chocolate bars on your shoulders, or a red beret on your head, but the weight of responsibilities is far more massive.

    For now, I would say to enjoy your youth, for your time to serve will come soon enough.
