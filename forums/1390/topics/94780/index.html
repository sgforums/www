    I think it's fair to say that you should be recorded as to have failed the IPPT, so be prepared to go through RT. I dunno which specific person informed you otherwise, but the source to clarify with is your reservist unit. (Not necessarily your actual S1 cell, but certain regulars/NSFs who do the administrative work on behalf of your CO - see the address/contact no. on the letters they send you about mob mannings etc).

    More generally, I think what you highlighted was one of the structural problems that created IPPT/RT "skippers" under the old format, possibly for the present one as well. Essentially, we were instructed time & again that the ICT IPPT - pass or fail - was the OFFICIAL result, & any other test you book/took in your own capacity was of "no count" if the latter also occurred during the same workyear.

    A few years of these frequent reminders, & it's bound to cause a few of the more daring reservists to skip the individual callups for IPPT, even risk rescheduling a couple of makeup RT sessions, if they know they would also be attending ICT soon.

    Citizen soldiers are human, after all. I presume that's partly why my CO threw the book only at the recalcitrant "skippers", & tended to give other such offenders more the benefit of the doubt.
