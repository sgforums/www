    Man jailed for glass attack on girlfriend

    He will also receive 12 strokes after carving his name on her chest and brutalising her with a fluorescent tube; judge says acts were 'hideous'

    By Selina Lum

    A MAN who carved his name on his girlfriend's chest with a shard of glass, then brutalised her with a fluorescent tube and burnt and kicked her, was yesterday sentenced to three years and five months in jail and 12 strokes of the cane.

    In mitigation, Pravin's lawyer said his client felt remorse.
    Pravin Kumar, 23, had pleaded guilty on Wednesday to two charges of causing hurt with offensive weapons to the woman between 8.50pm and 9.30pm on Sept 14 last year.

    Two other charges were taken into consideration.

    The customer services officer had admitted hurting his 25-year-old girlfriend after arguing with her at the fifth-level staircase of a multistorey carpark in Choa Chu Kang.

    In his mitigation plea for Pravin yesterday, Mr S.S. Dhillon said his client had taken his girlfriend to the National University Hospital after hurting her.

    The couple made up after the incident and continued to have a relationship for a 'substantial period of time', he said.

    He described the incident as a 'lovers' tiff gone out of hand'.

    The lawyer said that on that evening, Pravin had suggested they have a talk.

    His girlfriend had apparently stayed somewhere the week before without telling him where she was. She had also sent him an SMS message, saying that she wanted to break up with him.

    Pravin asked her to engrave his name on her chest because he wanted her to prove her love for him in a tangible manner, said the lawyer.

    He noted that the woman only suffered minor injuries and was not warded. The medical reports showed that the letters engraved on her chest were merely 'superficial scratch imprints', he said.

    Mr Dhillon said his client was remorseful and stressed that he was a first-time offender.

    However, district judge Salina Ishak described what he had done as 'hideous' and a degradation to women.

    'You left a mark of crime on her,' said the judge, adding that the brutal attack had left the woman emotionally scarred.

    Pravin is appealing against the sentence.

    Eight men in court over sex with teen

    One pleads guilty to sex with a minor and gets a year in jail; he got to know 14-year-old on Internet relay chat and met her later for sex

    By Elena Chong

    EIGHT men appeared in court yesterday - all for having separately had sex with the same underage girl last year.

    THE OTHER SEVEN MEN
    YESTERDAY, the cases of the seven other men were adjourned for their lawyers to make representations to the Attorney-General's Chambers.

    The seven accused are Yeo Kai Kee, 22, who is also accused of stealing $150 from the girl, Lim Wai Keat, 33, Zhou Jing Xiang, 18, Lee Hwee Kang, 23, each of whom faces charges of twice having had sex with the girl; Ho Kok Leon, 29, Goh Yeow Kwang, 23, and Li Tze Kit, 20.

    They were released on bail of $6,000 each and will appear in court again on July 10.

    Aged from 18 to 33, they hold various jobs, though some were in national service or even studying at the time.

    All were said to have committed the offences sometime between January and July last year - at hotels in Joo Chiat and Geylang, a park, and Housing Board staircase landings.

    In the end, only one, salesman Robin Heng Koon Huat, 24, pleaded guilty.

    He said he had come to know the 14-year-old girl through an Internet relay chat channel and admitted to twice having had sex with her at a Bedok park early last year.

    He was sentenced to 12 months' jail and a $6,000 fine on each of the two charges under the Women's Charter. The jail sentences are to run concurrently.

    A third charge of having sex with her at his home was taken into consideration.

    The cases involving the other accused men, whose lawyers were present in Court 26, were adjourned to another day.

    A day after Heng got to know the girl, now 16, over the Internet in January last year, he called her on her mobile phone.

    Heng, who knew that she was under 16 and a student, met her that evening at the Bedok Reservoir Park.

    They went to a hut there and had consensual sex. Sometime between January and February last year, they met again at the same place and had sex.

    The girl made a police report on Aug 26 stating that she had had sex with 'multiple' partners.

    In passing sentence, district judge Wong Keen Onn said: 'The High Court has clearly pointed out that public interest warrants a deterrent sentence to protect members of the public against those like-minded men who seek the medium of Internet to meet young and immature girls and sexually exploit them.'

    The maximum penalty for having sex with a girl under 16 is five years in jail and a $10,000 fine.
