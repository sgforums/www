    Worshipping 'Datuk', is a SE Asia Chinese customs. and those Chinese who worship these Datuks ARE Taoist. Folk Taoists to be exact, but nevertheless still falls under the umbrella of Taoism.

    Why?

    It is because ONLY Taoism can 'tolerate' and 'allow' the worship of other divines and spirits, as Taoism believes that the whole cosmic is full of spirits. If it is worthy of worship, a good spirit, promote goodness(which is the way of Tao) then Taoists can worship them, provided these Taoists understand the logic and do not lost the Taoist identity.

    Thus, Taoist worship tiger spirit, Datuk, Mountain deity, earth deity etc.. all of them fall under the same catagory.

    Datuk is a malay and of course a 'malay spirit'. however, it is the Taoist who worship them and thus does not concern the Islam religion. Datuk is a person, who had previously done alot for the society, a reputable person in the district etc..

    If it is to be explained in Taoist term, Datuk falls under the level of 'tu di gong' aka Earth Deity. A spirit governing the district and can bestow blessings.
