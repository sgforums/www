    When work become hetic, it will always help if you list down the thing you have to do. You can then see the actual amount of task you have to do. Your mind tends to snowball the amount.

    When colleagues get cocky, get humourous with them, you are still a rookie remember. Later when you grasp your work they will treat you better.

    Watch out for dumping. A noob who do not know his job scope, will always be a victim to job dumping. Learn and "help out", but be wary that grey area jobs do not become your responsibility by default.

    You are still not used to the work enviroment and work flow, things will "normalise" when you get used to the job, and have settled into your place in the scheme of your workplace.

    Then when a newbie comes, guess what will you be doing.

    Just bear it for now.
