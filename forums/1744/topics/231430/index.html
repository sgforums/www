    A day after I voiced a delicate issue (diplomatically) during a team briefing about the team's uneasiness over her emotional style of micro-management, my middle-aged female manager ran thru my office phone records and picked out some recorded conversations. On the pretext that I mishandled a customer (a manager call), she served me 1 verbal warning, 1 written warning and 1 final warning all within the same day.

    She then wrote a 2-page letter to the higher execs about why they should consider terminating my employ and spoke nothing of it. The day after, she went on long leave. Although my teammates all tried to stop it by speaking with the higher exec, basically, it was my word - and my teammates' - over the manager's, and since the accusations in the letter were too shocking, the execs decided not to risk it and terminated me on the spot.

    My colleagues tendered their resignations soon after.

    Interestingly, before this incident, I had full attendance over 1 year & won our team several excellence awards for customer compliments.. I was hired elsewhere with a better salary in no time.. and I've had no problems since.

    I'm a working woman too, and my mother was highly successful in her career. That's why I was particularly disappointed in my lady boss's actions - she confirmed every stereotype people have of female bosses. I now hold her as the perfect example of what I should not be, if/when my career takes off. =p

    Forgiven.. but not forgotten.
