  • charlize
    4 Aug 09, 15:55

    This is very common.

     

    Not only that, it depends on timing of you joining the firm at which point in the economic cycle.

     

    An example - a fresh grad graduates in 2004 when the economy is still just recovering from the SARS crisis and gets a job at 2K a month.  She works for the company for 3 years and gets a promotion and pay increase to 2.5K in 2007 (25% increment).  In 2007 the markets are booming.  Another fresh grad joining the company in the same department gets a starting pay of 2.5K at a salary that is on par with the person who has worked in that department for 3 years.

     

    See the disparity?

     

    That is why HR departments will always tell u to not reveal your salary to other colleagues because they know the wide disparity of salaries among workers will force them to pay more if salaries are known openly in the company.

     

    It's the same for higher ranking workers in similar jobs.  Sometimes, lower ranking personel could be drawing a higher salary than higher ranking personel in the same department.  Sometimes the HR will be nice and adjust the salary accordingly but if they can save the company money, they will.
