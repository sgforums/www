    11th October 1968

    Apollo 7 launched

    Apollo 7, the first manned Apollo mission, is launched with astronauts Walter M. Schirra, Jr.; Donn F. Eisele; and Walter Cunningham aboard. Under the command of Schirra, the crew of Apollo 7 conducted an 11-day orbit of Earth, during which the crew transmitted the first live television broadcasts from orbit. The crew also successfully performed a docking maneuver with a lunar module as preparation for the future manned lunar landing, which was accomplished by Apollo 11 in 1969.

    13th October 1812

    Sir Isaac Brock saves Canada

    During the War of 1812, British and Indian forces under Sir Isaac Brock defeat Americans under General Stephen Van Rensselaer at the Battle of Queenstown Heights, on the Niagara frontier in Ontario, Canada. The British victory, in which more than 1,000 U.S. troops were killed, wounded, or captured, effectively ended any further U.S. invasion of Canada. Sir Isaac Brock, Britain's most talented general in the war, was killed during the battle.

    17th October 1973

    OPEC ENACTS OIL EMBARGO

    The Arab-dominated Organization of Petroleum Exporting Countries (OPEC) announces a decision to cut oil exports to the United States and other nations that provided military aid to Israel in the Yom Kippur War of October 1973. According to OPEC, exports were to be reduced by 5 percent every month until Israel evacuated the territories occupied in the Arab-Israeli war of 1967. In December, a full oil embargo was imposed against the United States and several other countries, prompting a serious energy crisis in the United States and other nations dependent on foreign oil.

    Sydney Opera House opens

    After 15 years of construction, the Sydney Opera House is dedicated by Queen Elizabeth II. The $80 million structure, designed by Danish architect JÃ¸rn Utzon and funded by the profits of the Opera House Lotteries, was built on Nennelong Point, in Sydney, Australia.

    Famous for its geometric roof shells, the structure contains several large auditoriums and presents an average of 3,000 events a year to an estimated two million people.     Jeff Goldblum born



    Actor Jeff Goldblum is born in 1952 in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. Goldblum studied with the prestigious Carnegie Mellon theater program while still in high school, but rather than attending college he went directly to New York to pursue acting. He immediately began landing stage roles.

    He made his first film appearances in the mid 1970s. His gangly build and quirky looks landed Goldblum a series of offbeat roles in movies like The Adventures of Buckaroo Banzai Across the Eighth Dimension (1984) and Earth Girls Are Easy (1989) (the latter also starring his then-wife, actress Geena Davis). Goldblum and Davis divorced in 1990. Goldblum also received top billing in such hits as Jurassic Park (1993) and Independence Day (1996).

    28th October 1967

    Julia Roberts born



    Actress Julia Roberts is born in Smyrna, Georgia. Her parents ran an acting workshop in Atlanta, where she began studying as a teenager. She made her onscreen debut at age 21, appearing in Satisfaction and Mystic Pizza in 1988.

    The following year, she won a Best Supporting Actress Academy Award nomination for her role as a young diabetic mother in Steel Magnolias and later a Best Actress nomination for Pretty Woman. In 1993, she shocked the public by marrying singer Lyle Lovett, but the marriage ended quickly.

    Other Roberts films include My Best Friend's Wedding (1997), Notting Hill (1999), and Erin Brockovich (2000), for which she won the Best Actress Oscar. She married cameraman Danny Moder in 2002 and gave birth to twins on November 28, 2004.

    Winona Ryder born



    Winona Horowitz, later known as Winona Ryder, is born near Winona, Minnesota. Her hippie parents raised her on a commune and her godfather was the counterculture guru Timothy Leary. In 1990, she starred in Edward Scissorhands with Johnny Depp, to whom she became engaged, but the couple later broke off the engagement. Ryder won an Oscar nomination for The Age of Innocence (1993).
