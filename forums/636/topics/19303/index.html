Please inform me personally if i have offended you or you wanna gimme constructive criticism @ alweek@pacific.net.sg

    My idea/vision is for all MC members to be more profesional in some aspects. My approach to CS is not to take it too seriously. I know several people who approach the game too seriously (as if it's their life) to be a good CT/T and all this bullshit. Please lighten up if you think you fall in this catergory. I really enjoy going to competitions and outings as i get to meet up with my online friends who i meet every night on CS but never seen before. It is also often at these outings and such that i get to observe many "interesting" characters. Just take a look around next time you go to a LAN shop. You know the type, those arseholes posers who scream loudy, curse loudy, screw other people who can't play as well, taunt others when they frag them, and the list goes on... Please folks, it's just a damn game. So what if you're a headshot/sniping/mp5 king or whatever crap in CS? so how does that carry over into real life? just a dose of reality people... i dun think these same people can proudly proclaim to the real world that they're such kickass CS players. What i mean is, invest time in CS, but invest time in something worthwhile that you can be proud of outside of CS as well. To further elaborate, i know some folks who are real good in CS, but they have neglected many aspects of the real life until their studies/personal friendships etc are damn screwed up... so where do you think these people are gonna be in a year's time when CS fever eventually cools down?

    Another thought when going for outings/competitions... When you are put in such situations, people get to see you. not like when you're at home and can change your nick and be as cocky as you like and people still dunno who you are. When you play CS in a shop, you are physically there. People can look at you and make a judgement about you. This is both a good and bad opportunity. Try to take the opportunity to improve MC's professional image by not misbehaving too much. I'm all for having fun when we go out, shouting "Sooong boh?" to Toppy and the rest and what we normally do but when you play clan matches etc, follow basic sportmanship rules like shaking hands, winning or losing gracefully, give compliments, etc.. dun just finish the match and disappear etc. At the least if the rest of the team is lazy, the leader or older MC guys should make the effort to talk to the other teams leader. This goes a looooong way in improving MC public relations, by being a bit more sensitive to the opponent.

    Ok that's enough ranting for the morning. i'll continue this post as i think of more stuff regarding "discipline" and such.
