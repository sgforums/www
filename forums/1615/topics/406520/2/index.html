    You should never skip a meal. In fact, to lose weight effectively, you should be eating about 5-6 small meals a day. Eat a lot of fruits and vegetagles, a lot of protein and very little saturated fat. Avoid fried foods and anything with high-fructose corn syrup. It also has to be a change in the way you eat, not just something you do for a month and then that's it. On Sunday, or another day of the week, eat whatever you want. You'll get the carbs you need to feel satisfied and will not have to worry about gaining your excess weight back.

     

    Like another poster said, it's about burning more calories than you're taking in. You should be incorporating weight or other resistence training into your exercise, as it actually increases the amount of fat you burn. If all you do is cardio and run yourself into the ground, with no weight training, you'll burn muscle before you burn the fat.

     

    I just read in Muscle & Fitness magazine about a university study that compared runners who ran at least 30-minutes a day, at level 5 (on a scale of physical exertion of 1-10), and another group of runners who ran 10 minutes on level 5, took a ten minute break, ran another 10 minutes on level 5, and did it again for another round. They both lost the same amount of calories. What does that mean? Your cardio doesn't have to be you running forever. Just break it up into quick 10-minute spurts where you give it your all, then take a break.
