    Chinese Pear with Almond Soup
    -------------------------------------

    Formula milk may cause heat and phlegm to develop in the toddler and this soup is devised to counteract those effects. This soup can therefore be given to the child quite regularly. Chinese pears are refreshing and cooling. Together with the sweet and bitter almond mixture, they have the effect of alleviating heat and soothing the lungs. The cooked pears are tender and delicious. Children love them!

    Ingredients :

    4 Chinese pears (approx. 400g)
    10g sweet and bitter almond mixture
    200g spare ribs
    100g lean pork
    1.7 litres of water
    1 teaspoon of salt

    1. Wash and cut the pears into quarters or big wedges. Core, but do not skin. Soak in a light salt solution for a minute and drain.
    2. Scald the spare ribs and lean pork in boiling water for 3 minutes. Rinse and drain. Rinse the almond mixture.
    3. Bring the 1.7 litres of water to a boil in a pot. Put in all ingredients except the salt, cover pot and boil for 15 minutes over high heat. Lower the heat and simmer for 2.5 hours.
    4. Add the teaspoon of salt before serving. Serve .
