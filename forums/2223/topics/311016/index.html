Parking lots

  • GIB
    15 Mar 08, 10:37

    In the northern part of the USA, in many large cities, there are
    problems related to lots of snow and parking spaces in front of
    people's houses. What happens is that somebody will park in a nearby
    parking lot, then work for hours to shovel out a car-sized space in
    front of his house, so he can park his car there. When he is finished
    clearing a space, he goes back to the lot to get his car. If, when he
    returns home, he finds that the space has been taken by some other car.
    The individual can become very upset.

    Although most people do write nasty notes and place them on the
    windshield of the offending vehicle, sometimes the police have to get
    involved. In these cases, tempers flare, and the individual vents his
    wrath in somewhat more violent means. Serious damage has been done in
    these cases.

    One time, however, a fellow found a creative solution. Instead of
    writing the usual nasty note, he got out his garden hose and watered
    the automobile down. He watered the car, very, very thoroughly. The
    water, of course, froze solid.

    When the owner returned, instead of a car, he found a car-sized
    popsicle. The note on the car read: "You want the space? Here, it's
    yours until spring!"
