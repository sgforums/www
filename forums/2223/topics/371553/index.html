    Lawyer (to the witness): "Were you acquainted with the deceased?"

    Witness: "Yes, I was."

    Lawyer: "Well-acquainted?"

    Witness: "Very well-acquainted."

    Lawyer: "Was the deceased in the habit of talking to himself when he
    was alone?"

    Witness: "I don't know."

    Lawyer: "Come now, you pretend you were well-acquainted with the
    deceased, and you don't know??"

    Witness: "I never happened to be with him when he was alone."
