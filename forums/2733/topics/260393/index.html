    Ever wondered why the "bombs" are so devastating ? Infact , this is how atomic bombs are equally devastating . The atomic bombs dont explode on the ground . Instead , they explode in mid year . This is so that the explosion can be maximized . How ? Ok .

    When the bombs explode in mid air , the energy will travel in all sorts of direction . On the ground , most energy only travels upward and sideways ( parallel to the ground ) . The ground absorbs part of the energy . However , when the bombs explode in mid air , the energy thats travelling downwards , will hit the earth and bounces back into the atmosphere . Hence , the energy is "reflected" and multiplied . Same goes to the "bombs" in these videos .

    If you have noticed , the bottles dont touch the bottom of the bucket or the pool , just like what happen to the atomic bomb . When the bottles explodes , the energy that's travelling downwards gets reflected at the bottom and travels upwards instead , hitting the surface of the water . The cycle repeats itself several times , hence the energy is multiplied . Thats why when you watch the second video , when the bottle explodes , ripples and shockwaves can be seen on the water surface , even before the air (CO2) hits the surface . If such energy is strong enough and happens at sea, it can cause a Tsunami .
