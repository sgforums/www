    Yup I understood your point of view and indeed there is recurring expenses for those UT or ILP.
    But both of these has it pros and cons if you look closely enough. Both of these instruments cater for different kind of investors' profile.

    As for the charges, of course, Stocks wont have the recurring charges as you are doing every thing your own and at your own risk. ILP/UT on the other hand, is generally for part coverage and part investment and should be invested for med-long term growth and is managed by Full time - fund managers (monthly charges) (they need to feed their family too) and one time sales charges is paid to the advisors for their services and doing the paper work for you. If you met with mishap (death) at any investing period, for ILP a full amt plus profit or full amt (if u make a loss for your fund invested in) you invested will be paid out to your immediate family unlike stocks.

    I'm not saying stocks is not good for investing in but it is generally too complicated and higher risk than UT or ILP.

    As for savings plan, it is cater for long term (20-35 yrs) and for a specific purpose in order to make a better return than putting your cash in banks. If you really study, the proceeds (return) from these plan is generally very much higher than the bank savings account...
