    First of all, to all those who claims that it's great to be single I just want to ask if you have been attached before? No, I'm not for being attached or being single. But sometimes we just have to be more convincing in the way it is expressed. There has to be a comparision in our views.

    From my point of view, (In case you are wondering, I'm 31 and yes I was attached, two-timed, in a fling, had ONS, did the chase, was chased after, in a three way r/s, dumped, dumped others, married and divorced before I wrote all these.) Being Single or not, is not very much about whether you are happy or not. You should be asking what you really want.

    There are some of us who wants to be single, but that might be because you have been in so many bad r/s that being single is the way for you, but have you ever asked yourself why you were never happy in a r/s? It could be due to the fact that you never really knew what you wanted in a r/s and thus the many unhappy r/s. Many of us just go into a r/s of convinience, meaning we are in a r/s because there is someone we like and that someone happens to likes us too. But is what we like really what we want?

    I believe that people can be happily attached, we just need to be attached to the right person. If you are looking for freedom, then find someone who can give you that while in a r/s. If you are looking for closesness, then go find someone who wants to stick like glue. There are no absolutes.

    Of course there are some of us, who can only really be happy when we are single, then go ahead and be that, it is no crime, there is enough man and woman out there to help make the nation population grow, you can contribute through other ways.

    The point at the end of the day is simple, you really just have to know what you want. But you can never know unless you either meditate for 10 years under a Bodhi tree or go through enough r/s to find out.

    And yes, I'm single, but I'm still cannot be 100% sure I will be happier this way.
