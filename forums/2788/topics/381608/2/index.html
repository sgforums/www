    For the sake of your relationship, you have been putting up with his mother, but it is unlikely that things can last like this forever. What I will suggest is for the two of you to rent a cheap room for the two of you to live together in for the time being.

    As for the future, you need not worry too much about it, and do let time take its flow. Housing is definitely an issue, not only for the two of you, but also for most newly-wed couples as well. I have to sincerely apologise here though, for I am unable to give you a really good solution or suggestion when it comes to such housing issues.

    As for your wedding night, you can actually try out alternatives to the traditional banquets or restaurant dinners.

When he got married, his friends seek the aid of some fellow Buddhists and even nuns, and everyone did everything they could to help him out. The Bungalow which is used as a Buddhist Lodge by the nuns was used a wedding night dinner celebration area, and all of us did whatever we could to help him out. One of his friend become his chauffeur and used his lorry as a wedding car, some of us bring our own bottles of soft drinks and food ingredients and cooked for his wedding dinner meals, a beautician help out his wife, some of the odd-jobs workers help to bring tables and chairs...etc. Knowing of his finance situation, we refuse red packets with money, and accepted those with chocolate coins instead.

    For your honeymoon, the two of you can leave the child in your mother's care for a few days, and have a simple trip to Malaysia or Indonesia.
