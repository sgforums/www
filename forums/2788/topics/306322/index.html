    John Forde wrote an interesting article in Early to Rise newsletter on [Can Music Make You a Better Writer?]. Georgetown University researchers says that music and language use the same type of memory. Not only that, but we unconsciously learn the "rules" of what sounds good in both music and language in the same way. Research from the New York Academy of Sciences says that playing music can make you smarter and beef up your immune system. "How they explain why so many musicians seem to go nuts or die young, I don't know." said Forde.
