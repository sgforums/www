    Would open to me, like a dendrobium in the summer?

    Drench me in your love, trap me with your allure.

    Extract my shy and hiding self,

    and you've taken me off the dusty shelf.

     

    The dancing fire in your passionate gaze

    cuts me like a sharpened razor blade.

    I don't deny it drinks my resistance,

    with a smile seemingly incandescent..

     

    Stroked by a passing, playful gust,

    we're just strolling, both of us.

    Walking the river banks, you hold me close,

    and whisper softly, "Don't let me go,"

     

    A spoonful of love, I've been fed.

    Like a newborn's mother, I know you're there.

    The vaults in the depth of my heart,

    will house no other, but you, dear lover.
