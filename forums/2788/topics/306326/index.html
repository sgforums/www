    Researchers at the University of California, Berkeley, said women who sniffed a chemical found in male sweat experienced elevated levels of an important hormone, along with higher sexual arousal, faster heart rate and other effects. In a study of 48 female Berkeley undergraduates, researchers tested the women's hormonal response to androstadienone, a male chemical signal found in male sweat, saliva and semen. To gauge this response, researchers measured levels of the hormone cortisol in the women's saliva after they took 20 sniffs from a jar of androstadienone, which smells somewhat musky. The cortisol levels of those who inhaled the androstadienone shot up within 15 minutes and stayed up for up to an hour. The women also reported improved mood, higher sexual arousal, and had increased blood pressure, heart rate and breathing.
