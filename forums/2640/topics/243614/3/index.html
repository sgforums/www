    I remember starting off with an old Sony S50 P&S camera back in 2001, that time was the start of the digital trend in photography.

    As far as my experience goes, cheap equipment only restricts photography in terms of difficulty, not ability. If you were to take my DSLR away and give me the Sony again, I think the only difference it would make is to affect the quantity of great shots, or "keepers", rather than affecting the quality of my "keepers".

    So I don't think what inextremo says is true. Basics begin not with the type of equipment, but the mastery of your creative eye.
