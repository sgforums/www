    Normally, quick tempo and direct/long passing comes together and also slow tempo and short passing.

    Of course you can mix and match. What I observed in 7.0.2 is that the players will still tend to mix it around quite a bit. Remember, you're only giving them instructions, not playing for them. You can't control how they think like in real life.

    The range of closing down means how far up the field will your team start to pressure the other side. Therefore, normally, it's low closing down and deep defensive line and vice versa.

    But of course, you can have a high defensive line and normal closing down. No problems with that. Since defensive line means how high up the field your back 4 or whatever will go.

    Thus, this shows how deep the game can be.

    I observed that when the strikers cannot score, it's because either their goalkeeper too on form or the second one, which you can remedy, is because their central defenders defend too well.

    For the second reason, note down your striker "Jumping" stat and the defender's "Jumping" stat. Note that if you're using a target man, long pass system, your striker's "Jumping" must be higher than the defender's "Jumping" stat, otherwise, the defender will keep head off your long passes, thus breaking up your attack.

    This "Jumping" stat, in my opinion, is the most important stat in the game. Unless you're playing a short, ball-to-feet system. But even then, your players will still play high balls from time to time, which is where you want your strikers to be able to head it.

    Originally posted by glacer:
    Erm CoolMyth, does height play a factor when it comes to aerial battle?
    Of course it is to a certain extent.

    But what I observed is that tall people generally have high "Jumping" stat and shorter people have low "Jumping" stat. Note "Jumping" and "Heading" are two different things. One is to jump and one is to head. (Duh!)

    In short, if you want to choose a target man, choose someone with a high "Jumping" stat.
