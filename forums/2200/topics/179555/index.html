    If you know anything taylor's series and maclaurin series, you will realise that
    e^z=summation (n=0 to inf)[z^n/n!]

    So when you expand
    e^iz = 1 + iz +(iz)^2/2! +(iz)^3/3! +(iz)^4/4!+ ...
    =1 + iz -z^2/2 - iz^3/3! + z^4/4! + ...
    =(1-z^2/2! + ...) + i (z- z^3/3! + ...)
    =cos z + i sin z

    so e^i*pi = cos pi + i sin pi = -1 + i*0 = -1.
