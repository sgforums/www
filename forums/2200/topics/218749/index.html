    I gather from the that training schedule, there'll be frequent injuries especially the goalkeepers.

    Perhaps I may post my training schedule. It may not be the best around but it server its purpose by getting the players to improve well on their attributes and at the same time minimizing injuries.

    Here are the Normal Schedules for different Positions:



    Where GK is for goalkeepers
    DF Central is for your central Defenders
    DF Wings is for your WingBacks/DL & DR
    MF Defensive is for your defensive Midfielders
    MF Attacking is for your attacking Midifelders
    MF Wings is for your fast&pacey Wingers
    ST is for your strikers while SHOOTING is for strikers who can't seem to get in the goals for a period of time.

    Try to set these schedules during in-season and vary the intensities according to the number of matches and period of the season. Training intensity should peak towards Mid-season (but should NEVER go to "very Heavy") and go easy as the season approaches to an end. Putting them on "Heavy" or "Very Heavy" for prolong periods of time may invite frequent injuries.

    ..continued..

    And if your players are:

    1) suffering from injuries
    2) recovering from injuries
    3) just recovered from injuries,

    do set them to these few schedules:



    LIGHT is for those having injuries but the status box is YELLOW.
    INTENSE RECOVER is for those just recovered from a long-term injury. NOTE: Do not put him on this schedule for more then 2 weeks; it'll may just injure him again and make him worst!
    FITNESS is for pre-season time; set ALL your players to this schedule during pre-season and friendlies.
    SET-PIECES is best during mid-season or the week before that important game. Set everybody to this schedule.

    Just my two-cents worth. Having a proper schedules and spending time on actually tweaking and editing your schedule may take lots of time; but it can actually benefit the team and your players on the long run. Good luck!
