    Okay, long time never did this kind of thing but it's actually quite easy.

    From diagram, you know PQ = QC and BP = (1/2)QC

    So let BP be 1 unit --> QC = 2 units = PQ

    ---> 5 units = 30 cm ---> 1 unit = 6 cm

    Hence,

    PC = PQ + QC = 24 cm

    BQ = BP + PQ = 18 cm

    Therefore,

    Area ABQ = 1/2 * 16 * 18 = 144 cm^2

    Area CDP = 1/2 * 16 * 24 = 192 cm^2

    ---> Area of shaded portion = 144 + 192 - 42 = 294 cm^2 which is your answer.

    And as for the discrepancy in answer, actually if you draw out the rectangle, you realise that the area of the shaded portion isn't really 42 cm^2.

    That is, the entire diagram has inconsistent dimensions. Your method is correct and the 294 is also "correct" but because the dimensions are inconsistent, you'll get different answers.

    I'll assume this is a primary school question. No usage of algebra and stuff.

    Through close observation, you can see that the first digit and the last digit always adds up to 102 (2 + 100)

    Take those two out and you see the same thing (4 + 98 = 102)

    So, you just need to find the number of terms in this series. Therefore,

    Number of terms = 100 - 50 (100 terms from 1 to 100 minus away the odd numbers. Note that there are equal number of even and odd terms from 1 to 100) = 50

    Hence, the answer is 102 x (50/2) = 2,550. (Remember you're "pairing" two numbers together to make 102)

    In sec school, this is simply just summation from n=1 to 50 (2n) and we have a formula that says summation n terms = (n/2)(n+1).
