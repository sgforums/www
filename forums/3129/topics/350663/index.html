    I applied for NUS Law and Business Double Degree back in my time... think it was the first year it was introduced. Would have chose that if I had remained in Singapore, because it gives you two degrees and offers you more job scope next time I guess. SMU, if I'm not mistaken has a Law and Business Double Degree as well. You can look at this option if you are undecided between Law or Business.

    For Law: I would think NUS is a better choice, because it's a bit more select, shall we say? I'm not too sure, but I have heard that SMU does take in some of the NUS law reject. These might just the ones I've heard though....However, looking at your grades, perhaps you might consider a bond free local scholarship? For NUS, if you are taking law, you have to get the NUS GLobal Merit Scholarship I think, and that is really hard to get. To be honest about it, I don't think B for GP holds you in very good stead. BUT, to get funded for your studies at SMU is much easier... they give a whole range of scholarships and dont seem too selective about it... so it's up to you really.

    And by all means try to apply for NUS medicine, I think your grades are alright enough.

     

    If you are going for Business, go for SMU Business. I think they have marketed themselves well and my friends get to go on plenty of business study trips etc which I think enriches the learning process. And Business is all about marketing isn't it?

    I would suggest putting NUS choices in this order:

    Medicine

    Law and Business

    Business

     

    And smu:

    Law and Business

    Business and Accountancy

     

    If you are considering NTU, Accountancy and Mass com are the better degrees there.

     

    Good luck.
