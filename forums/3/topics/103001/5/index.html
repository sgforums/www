    This is how I prepare for my runs.

    The night before:
    Re-lace shoes and pin competition chip onto shoe
    Put socks and shoes next to door
    Pin number tag on shirt and put shorts near number tag
    Put money into pouch and put beside number tag.
    PUt a disposable bottle of water next to number tag
    Drink some water if felt dehyrdated.

    The morning:
    4:30am Wake up immediately go kitchen to start boil water
    Drink some water while in kitchen
    brush teeth, shower and put contact lens and pee
    4:45am put pasta into boiling water
    while waiting for pasta to cook. change into gear and drink water
    5:00am Eat pasta and drink isotonic drink and water
    5:10am Call for cab
    5:15am Pee again and then go re-check gear before putting on shoes

    Typically I take water, milo and pasta. Just plain pasta without any sauce. I find that it although it is can make me feel a bit full when I started running it will keep me from going hungry after a long time... I am a slow runner so for Full M will probably take about 5 hours. That means that at 11am I will probably still be running. So really need to eat something.

    I have been eating this before my runs and also before other races so not a problem. I wouldn't recommend people to try for the first time during the Stand chart marathon as pasta can really be very filling!

    1 Dec 04, 17:13
    Can share your game plan.... below is mine:

    Drink at every drink station. Drink isotonic when available.
    At downslope don't run too fast and give stress to my knees
    Keep feet close to ground be efficient in running
    Don't waste energy and weave to pass slower runners. if necessary round slower until the crowd thin out.
    Focus on some point ahead of me and then run at constant speed towards it.
    Don't get too worried about timing. just keep running.
    Run at planned pace and do not get carried away and run too fast.
    If knees pain, try to slow down and lessen pounding and hopely will go away.
    If cramping will slowly run and walk to nearest drink station and then drink.

    Breakup race to 4 phases so that will not be overwhemed.

    Phase I :1st 5km
    slowly run and warm up. no walking... run at constantly pace

    Phase 2: 5km to 15km
    Warmed up already. Will tend to want to run faster so don't run too fast. Walk 1 min 2 drink station

    Phase 3: 15km to 30km
    Will start to tire. Must continue to be determined. mental energy start to come into play. Body will start to ache. But have to endure. I must ensure that I keep hydrated and start to cool my head with water as sun is up.
    Walk 1 min for every 1 or 2 drink station

    Phase 4: 30km to 42km.
    Take the powergell. This is the unknown phase. never run so far before. Don't really know what it is like. But should be very tired. Have to continue to perserve no matter what.... take walking breaks but must be disciplined so that after walking break resume running... and give myself targets like I must run until the next drink station.....

    Drop out if:
    a. If very giddy or heart start to pound too fast.
    b. If cramp still cannot recover after prolonged rest
    c. If so tired that running at a stride that will cause strain and injury.
