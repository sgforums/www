    Coach Marco van Basten claimed his decision to omit Mark van Bommel and Ruud van Nistelrooy from the Holland squad to face the Republic of Ireland in a friendly next Wednesday in Dublin was because he had more trust in other players.

    "At the moment we think other players are better," said Van Basten.

    "We want to go on with the selected group because we have more trust in those players."

    Both van Nistelrooy and van Bommel would appear to be paying the price for poor World Cups.

    Van Nistelrooy, who moved to Real Madrid from Manchester United this summer, failed to shine in Germany as Holland crashed out in the second round to Portugal.

    Van Nistelrooy was left on the bench for that match after severe criticism from van Basten.

    Van Basten omits Ruud from latest Holland squad

    Former Manchester United striker Ruud van Nistelrooy has again been left out of the provisional Holland squad to face Bulgaria and Albania in next month's Euro 2008 qualifiers.

    The Real Madrid forward has yet to feature since losing his starting place in Marco van Basten's line-up during the World Cup.

    New Liverpool signing Dirk Kuyt and recent Blackburn acquisition Andre Ooijer are both included.

    The Netherlands take on Bulgaria in the Vassil Levski Stadium in Sofia on October 7 before meeting Albania in the Amsterdam ArenA four days later in the final qualifier of 2006.

    Van Basten will confirm his final squad on September 29.

    Provisional squad: Maarten Stekelenburg (Ajax), Henk Timmer (Feyenoord), Edwin van der Sar (Manchester United); Urby Emanuelson, John Heitinga (both Ajax), Tim de Cler, Kew Jaliens (both AZ), Giovanni van Bronckhorst (Barcelona), Andre Ooijer (Blackburn Rovers), Khalid Boulahrouz (Chelsea), Joris Mathijsen (Hamburg); Wesley Sneijder (Ajax), Stijn Schaars, Demy de Zeeuw (both AZ), Nigel de Jong (HSV), Denny Landzaat (Wigan Athletic); Ryan Babel, Klaas Jan Huntelaar (both Ajax), Robin van Persie (Arsenal), Arjen Robben (Chelsea), Dirk Kuyt (Liverpool), Jan Vennegoor of Hesselink (Celtic).
