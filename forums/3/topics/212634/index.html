    Hector Cuper has been linked with a shock return to Inter Milan.
    Last night's horror show against Bayern Munich, which saw Zlatan Ibrahimovic and Fabio Grosso sent off in the 2-0 defeat, has heaped more pressure on Inter coach Roberto Mancini.

    Reports of a fractured dressing room and a lack of defensive know-how has the Milan press suggesting Inter patron Massimo Moratti is ready to rip up the Mancini experiment and go with an established, experienced coach.

    Cuper, Sven Goran Eriksson and Claudio Ranieri are the three names being mentioned this morning.

    Crespo angry with Inter Milan teammates
    tribalfooball.com - September 28, 2006

    Hernan Crespo has told his Inter Milan teammates they can't afford to implode like they did last night against Bayern Munich.
    Crespo was angry with his teammates after Zlatan Ibrahimovic and Fabio Grosso saw red in the defeat.

    "The more experienced players need to bring a little bit more calm to the locker room and assure there's no need to tear our hair out. If we can end games with 11 men on the field, then obviously it'd make things a bit easier for everyone," commented the Argentine.

    "The fact we lost 2-0 at home and ended up in nine men is negative. We know we made mistakes tonight and were undisciplined, but we can start again with the knowledge we are strong and that these things shouldn't happen any more."

    Crespo had perhaps Inter's best scoring opportunity just after Ibrahimovic was sent off, but fired straight at Oliver Kahn.

    "We pounced on a mistake and the ball didn't bounce kindly for me, so the 'keeper was able to make a save. Clearly we didn't play too well today and now there's no room for error, we must win the next three matches."

    They now face back-to-back ties with Spartak Moscow and must win both those encounters or potentially crash out of Europe altogether.

    "We're also taking on Sporting Lisbon at San Siro and must try to get nine points before we go to Munich for the final match. We've still got a good chance of progressing in this competition," insisted Crespo.
