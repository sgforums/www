Wengers reluctance to open his cheque book

  • Quincey
    28 Aug 05, 00:46
    As a Arsenal Fan, I'm getting a little irritated and yes feeling a little uneasy on our prospects for the season. Firstly, since Patrick Viera was allowed to leave the club (which I thought was a horrible mistake in the first place) Wenger has refused to find a natural replacement for him. Instead, he choose to rely on two youngsters Febregas and Flamini, two youngsters who are hot propects for the future, but definitely far from that of the Viera mould. It is evident in the last few matches that Arsenal was sorely lacking the prescense Viera provided, that killer pass he so often provides that breaks the backs of defences. Just look at the two previous Premiership matches, against Newcastle they looked really out of sorts until Jenas got sent off. Again against Chelsea, they dominated, but the attack often crumbled when they reached the last third of the field. Thumbs up to Gilberto Silva though who tried really hard to emulate Viera's role, but fact the matter is that the man is not Viera. Of course, the departure of Viera is only the top of Arsenal's problems. The state of their defence is worrysome, the sight of Drogba muscling his way through to goal is just disgusting to watch. Again, Mr Wenger has choosen to rely on youth to troubleshoot the defensive problems plagueing his team. I just cannot comprehen Mr Wengers policies. Yes, bright young talents they are, but they are sorely lacking experience and big match exposure to be handed the mammoth task of replacing a figure like Viera or shouldling the defence.

    I understand that Arsenal is running on a shoe string budget with the Emirates Stadium being a liability on their finances. That is why when Viera was being sold, I accepted it knowing that the club needed money to bring in more players and that Viera only had 2 years left in his contract. However, since his depature, Arsenal has only brought in one player (Hleb, who I must say is a really good buy). That just isn't enough. Wenger needs to couple his "Go Youth Policy" with a reasonable transfer policy to mantain an efficient balance. I'm not going into specifics as to what Arsenal need to become the team that is able to via with the likes of Chelsea and Real Madrid for domestic and european honours, I think what change is needed is rather evident for us the fans to see.
