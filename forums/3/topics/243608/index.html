    CHELSEA striker Didier Drogba and United's Cristiano Ronaldo are battling it out for top spot in the Barclays Premiership player rankings.

    Drogba, who has scored 29 goals in all competitions this season, is heading a tight race for the top spot in the Actim Index, the Premiership's official statistics.

    Following the last round of Premiership fixtures before the break for the FA Cup quarter-finals, Drogba headed the Index by 15 points from Ronaldo.

    With Chelsea midfielder Frank Lampard 15 points further back from Ronaldo the pressure is on if either of the top two players in the rankings lets the standard of his performances drop.

    Lampard netted twice as Chelsea came back from 3-1 down to draw 3-3 with Tottenham and earn an FA Cup quarter-final replay to keep his good form going.

    Everton's Mikel Arteta occupied fourth spot in the rankings on 457 points after the last full round of fixtures before the break for the FA Cup, with Aston Villa's Gareth Barry rounding out the top five.

    Manchester United's Wayne Rooney, Tottenham's Dimitar Berbatov, Liverpool's Steven Finnan, Blackburn's Benni McCarthy and Rooney's team-mate Rio Ferdinand completed the top 10.
