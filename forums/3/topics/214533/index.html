    Bolton Wanderers striker Nicolas Anelka has had a pop at new manager Sam Allardyce's tactics.

    Anelka, labelled the Incredible Sulk for his constant whingeing, has yet to lose with his fourth English club as Bolton have climbed to third in the Premiership.

    But despite claiming he has matured at 27, the former Arsenal, Liverpool and Manchester City forward still could not resist a moan about Allardyce's no-nonsense tactics - and his deployment as a sole striker.

    "I have started to re-find the rhythm of the English league which I know by heart and I love," he said. "I am playing alone up front which is not what I prefer. Our game is based on 'kick and rush' so I don't get the ball much.

    "At the start, I was supposed to play with Kevin Davies. But the team is winning. We annoy other sides and, if we don't score much, we concede even fewer goals. We press a lot, so as far as fitness is concerned I am at 100 per cent."
