    In the midst of all the fervent on just about every other aspect of the World Cup, just thought I'd create a thread for football fans to share the biggest upsets they have witnessed. To keep things in perspective, it would be nice if people only post their thoughts based on the matches they've seen(and also give your reasons for your choice, folks) and not regurgitate some titanic feats from football annals. I'll start things off with the 3 biggest upsets I've watched from the World Cup...

    1. Cameroon 1-0 Argentina ( Italia '90 )

    This has no doubt got to be the biggest of those I've had the privilege of watching. An unheralded side against the much vaunted Maradona-inspired world champions which proved to be the harbinger of the arrival of African teams as a force to be reckoned with at the World Cup.

    2. Norway 2-1 Brazil ( France '98 )

    I don't think too many people remember this game, but this was certainly something special. Norway shocked what was then the reigning world champions whom many felt were invincible - one of the most entertaining(in the last 20 mins anyway) giantkilling feat at the WC where success wasn't founded on defensive play.

    3. Senegal 1-0 France ( Korea/Japan '02 )

    This was virtually a replay of the 1990 opener - France, defending world and european champions, came unstuck against another Africa debutant just when every man and his dog had put his mortgage on a French whitewash.
