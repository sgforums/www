    Portsmouth coach Tony Adams wants four wins from their next four home games.

    Harry Redknapp's right-hand man believes West Ham, Reading, Fulham and Watford are all beatable. The quartet visit Fratton Park over 36 days - starting with West Ham on Saturday.

    Tough-as-they-come visits to Chelsea and Manchester United are also in that spell.

    But Adams insists one characteristic needs to mark all Pompey's performances: toughness.

    He said: "I see our forthcoming home games as definitely winnable. I think what we tried to do at the start of the season was to be tough to beat, whether we played Manchester United away or West Ham at home.

    "That's because nothing is easy. Teams are either fighting for their lives, going for the league or going for Europe. There's pride and there's money wrapped up in there, too.

    "We want to do our stuff. To be resilient. To work hard as a team. To try to pick up as many points as we can. Hard work is a constant. Right from the off, we want to have a right go at teams."
