    The Ivory Coast international has revealed that the aspersions cast at him this season are behind his decision to want to leave Stamford Bridge.

    A number of incidents have led some to label Drogba a 'diver' and the 28-year-old claims he no longer enjoys plying his trade in The Premiership.

    The Â£24 million signing from Marseille believes the constant scrutiny has had a negative impact on his performances and has now revealed his preference for a move to Serie A giants Milan.

    "It's not a joke. I want to move on and avoid all the pressure and scandals here," he told The Sun.

    "Those things hurt my feelings. I want a place I can play freely without any drawbacks.

    "You should know what a player goes through with so much pressure."

    Lyon have already confirmed their admiration of the player and Drogba has said he will only move to a club who can offer him UEFA Champions League football, with Italy a possible destination.

    "I admire AC Milan. But anywhere I go, the Champions League will be there waiting for me," added Drogba.

    Friday May 19 2006

    Didier Drogba wants to quit Chelsea and leave the Premiership behind because he is sick of being labelled a cheat.

    The Ivory Coast striker, who is going to the World Cup this summer, has been involved in a number of incidents in the last couple of seasons which has led to him being labelled a diver.

    Now the 28-year-old, a Â£24million signing from Marseille two seasons ago, has had enough. "It's not a joke. I want to move on and avoid all the pressure and scandals here," he told The Sun.

    Drogba added: "Those things hurt my feelings. I want a place I can play freely without any drawbacks.

    "You should know what a player goes through with so much pressure."

    Drogba said he will only move to a club in the Champions League and is eyeing up a move to Italy.

    "I admire AC Milan. But anywhere I go, the Champions League will be there waiting for me," said Drogba.
