    Paul Dickov has been released by Blackburn after he rejected the offer of a new contract at Ewood Park.

    The veteran striker, who scored seven goals last season for Mark Hughes' side, is expected to sign for Manchester City in the next few days after he decided not to take up the offer of a new two-year deal.

    Both Sheffield United and Everton are also believed to be interested in the player but a return to his former club looks the most likely.

    Rovers' chairman, John Williams told the Lancashire Evening Post: 'Paul has been released by the club after he decided not to accept our offer of a new contract.

    'I don't think there was any problem with the terms we offered him, it was more of a football decision.

    'We'd like to thank Paul for his efforts over the last two years, and we wish him all the best for the future.'

    Arsenal defender Sol Campbell is a target for Turkish club Fenerbahce, according to reports.

    Campbell controversially joined Arsenal from arch-rivals Tottenham on a free transfer in 2001 after allowing his contract to run down at White Hart Lane.

    Skysports.com can confirm Tottenham are keen on signing Ivory Coast international Didier Zokora.

    Zokora looks set to leave St Etienne in the summer after agreeing a deal to move on.

    There is sure to be no shortage of suitors for Zokora with the player impressing a number of sides with his performances for club and country over the last few years.

    Arsenal and Manchester United have been linked with Zokora in the past, but the player's agent Franck Belhassen has confirmed Tottenham have joined the chase for the powerful midfielder's signature.

    "There is interest from a number of clubs in Didier and Tottenham are one of them," Belhassen told skysports.com.

    "At the moment he is away with the Ivory Coast preparing for the World Cup and we will wait until after that before deciding his future.

    "We have a deal with St Etienne that he can go this summer for the right price.

    "The World Cup is a very good opportunity for Didier to show clubs what he can do and we will see what happens.

    "Tottenham is a very interesting club as they are an improving club with a good manager like Martin Jol.

    "There are also big clubs in France and Spain looking at Didier and he wants to play at a good club."

    Zokora has previously expressed an interest in playing in England and Tottenham could firm up their interest in the player after next month's World Cup finals as they look to build on last season's fifth place finish.
