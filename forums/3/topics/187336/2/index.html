    YOU ARE TO BLAME, JOSE
    FA CUP SEMI-FINAL: Chelsea 1 Liverpool 2 FROM OLD TRAFFORD

    The Special One turns into The Tinkerman to wreck Blues' Double dream
    Martin Lipton Chief Football Writer

    JOSE MOURINHO has basked in the accolades, revelled in the confrontations, encouraged the hostility.

    But in football, as in life, you reap what you sow.

    And as Mourinho fatally undermined Chelsea's Double dream by tactics that handed the initiative, the momentum and ultimately a place in the FA Cup Final to Liverpool, it was not just the Merseyside fans who were laughing at the Special One.

    For all the moaning of Mourinho, the bitter truth he will have to accept is he, and he alone, was the architect of his team's downfall.

    Out-thought and outfought, outsmarted and out-grafted, there could be no denying Liverpool deserved the win.

    But it was Mourinho's Ranieri-esque tinkering that brought about defeat. Chelsea's machine was purring again in recent weeks, taking them to the brink of back-to-back titles. Yet instead of sticking with what was working, Mourinho was simply too clever for his own good.

    Paulo Ferreira has hardly featured at full-back recently, yet at Old Trafford he was employed on the right of the midfield diamond, simply proving another Blue shirt for a rejuvenated Harry Kewell to skate past as well as the hapless Geremi.

    And at the tip of the quartet, Mourinho used Michael Essien, whose power and tackling ability was neutered in that position and who did not have the imagination to create anything.

    It meant Chelsea, lacking width with three wingers on the bench, were able only to see a narrow channel through the middle, allowing Xabi Alonso and Mo Sissoko to block them off with as soon as they crossed the halfway line.

    Complaining "every crucial decision" went Liverpool's way was an over-statement by Mourinho, although only Graham Poll saw John Terry's tackle on Luis Garcia as a foul.

    But it was not Poll who ordered the Chelsea wall to fracture and allow John Arne Riise's shot to squirt into the bottom corner.

    And while Terry then got above the Norwegian defender to nod home an "equaliser", it was with the clear assistance of both hands on Riise's shoulders.

    Likewise, it was Didier Drogba, no one else, who squandered two great chances at 0-0, the second when two yards offside but not flagged, and Ferreira and William Gallas who allowed Garcia to score the winner. Drogba made partial amends to breathe life into the game but it was only when Mourinho threw out plans he devised in expectation of a dour battle, sent on Joe Cole, Damien Duff and Arjen Robben and used Terry as a centre-forward, that Chelsea almost took the game to extra time.

    Rather than blaming himself, Mourinho turned his fire on the three substitutes, suggesting they had forced the selection and tactics. Cole fired over the bar from close-range and Mourinho said: "I don't know why Joe Cole's form has dropped, but it's the reality.

    "At the same time Duff's had a lot of problems in his tendon. He was playing and training but with a lot of problems in his Achilles. He lost form because he couldn't feel good. Robben dropped too when he was suspended for four weeks. When he got back into the team against Birmingham he lost form. That's the reality.

    "The team wasn't getting results. This kind of player is very individual in their game. If they're good they can win matches for you - if they're not good they lose possession of the ball, the team loses balance and compactness.

    "I made the decision because of that. We got three consecutive victories playing with two players up and we were happy with the way we were. You can think what you want and write what you want." It was an open invitation, but so was a game-plan Rafa Benitez and his side exploited to the full, with Mourinho's insistence Liverpool have "no chance" of winning the league next term more sour grapes.

    Benitez, though, got it right. Kewell looked the player he once threatened to be, Garcia buzzed and sparkled and Jamie Carragher and Sami Hyypia were immaculate at the back.

    For Benitez, it was another chance to reiterate his pregame jibe that Roman Abramovich, rather than Mourinho, was the reason for Chelsea's success.

    "Chelsea will spend money," he said. "The key of this last three or four years is Roman Abramovich. No other team in the world can spend this money. It gives them a big advantage. Chelsea have a good team, good players, good manager, but also an owner with a lot of money."
