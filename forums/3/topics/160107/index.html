    Serie A: Milan end Juve's perfect season start

    AC Milan ended Juventus' 100 per cent start to the season as they romped to a 3-1 victory over the Serie A leaders in the San Siro on Saturday evening.

    First-half goals from Clarence Seedorf, Kaka and Andrea Pirlo helped the home side in to an unassailable lead, and although David Trezeguet pulled a goal back in the 76th minute, Milan saw out a crucial win.

    Brazilian forward Kaka scored the goal of the match, smashing home a volley from inside the penalty area after Pirlo's free-kick had been only half-cleared.

    The result cut Juventus' lead at the top of the table to just two points, with Milan second, six points ahead of city rivals Inter in third.
