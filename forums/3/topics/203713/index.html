    With so much discussion on football in this section, just wondering if anyone out there actually has any athlete - from individual sports past or present - in mind whom you'd consider a great athlete(and why do you reckon they're great?). To kick things off, I'd start with 2 of my personal favourites :

    Roger Federer (Swiss), Tennis - Quite simply, the most naturally gifted tennis player I've ever seen and I don't think it's an exaggeration to suggest he plays tennis from a different planet. Just an absolute delight to watch when he's in the mood and with 8 Grand Slams under his belt(Sampras holds the all-time record with 14 Grand Slams) at just 24, it surely is just a matter of time before he cements his place as the most successful, if not the greatest, tennis player of all time - so long as he keeps his motivation and stays injury-free.

    Ayrton Senna (Brazilian), Formula 1 - Although he doesn't hold too many records in terms of drivers' title won, his premature death did not help his cause in this endeavour. Nevertheless, for all those fortunate enough to have lived to watch him race, I don't think it's hard to see he was a class apart from most of his peers wherever he raced. While it's debatable as to whether Michael Schumacher will still have been as successful had Senna survived the fatal crash at Imola in '94, I don't think anyone will dispute the flamboyance, charisma and sheer class Senna brought into F1 is something that has not been seen since.
