    All Fuqua. Welcome back.....

    I borrowed Jeff Galloway's book from the library.... he emphasized about the run/walk technique... he specifically said that one should walk during the early phases when you don't feel tired and not walk when you feel tired...apparently walking uses a different set of muscles and let your running muscles rest while u r still covering distances by walking..he had a lot of computation on speed and formulas to show that you are better off walking a bit.... There is a lot on training for marathon as well. I think his book is more practical and the language simple. there are some other marathon books which i picked up which are too complicated!!!

    if you wait until tired then walk.... too late.... that is the theory... I read that after my SBR 1/2M and didn't really have a chance to put in practice until the Stand chart full M. And I find that at the initial stage I sort of go with the flow with everyone runnings.... kind of hard to walk and have everyone overtake you. So I run and forgot about my game plan.... I think I only start taking walking breaks at the ECP at which time I am already tired.....

    I think the program will work.. but need practice and discipline.....
