    Big jump in number of cases involving illegal pellet guns
    By Jessica Yeo, Channel NewsAsia | Posted: 09 April 2007 1941 hrs

    SINGAPORE: There has been a big increase in the number of people arrested for trying to bring in illegal toy guns.

    Police said there have already been 21 cases this year, which is a significant jump, compared to the 38 cases recorded in the whole of 2006.

    In the latest case, a Singaporean man will be charged in court on Tuesday with importing pellet guns illegally.

    The 58-year-old man was caught at Changi Airport's Budget Terminal when he was returning from China on 14 February 2007.

    When his bag was screened, he did not declare that it contained 20 sets of pellet guns (also known as airsoft or BB guns) and five bottles of pellets.

    Police said some countries treat these guns as toys, but they look real and can cause damages and even injuries.

    Most airsoft guns are detected at the airport and at other checkpoints where they need to be declared and surrendered.

    Police warned that those convicted could face a fine of up to S$100,000.

    They could also be jailed up to three years and fined a maximum of S$10,000.[/b]

    We had a good time in JB- some of the JB teams heard that we were coming up to play so they all turned up hoping to get a game with is- one team was hoping we'd coach them.

    The trip was mainly to scout the field - only 2 of us Red Sevens went and we brought 3 kids and a adult friend of mine came along. The ground was good no mud or scuffing despite the rain the night before. New Sup'air Bunkers - new Spyder Pilot house markers which shot pretty straight.

    We met alot of JB players who were very nice and friendly- they came and introduced themselves. I guess we have a new place to practice and we have plenty of ready sparring partners.

    Only draw back is that the place is pretty laid back- bunkers were not set up by the time we got there at 11am and safety....was not that great. Paint wasn't cheap too.
