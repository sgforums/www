    Chelsea boss Mourinho scoffs at Brady jibes
    tribalfooball.com - September 16, 2006

    Chelsea boss Jose Mourinho has laughed off the jibes from Arsenal coach Liam Brady.

    Arsenal youth director Brady predicted trouble for Chelsea in keeping all their superstars happy and wants the champions to suffer a crisis.

    But Mourinho said: "I really hope they do think that way.

    "I'm happy when other people from top teams are waiting for our mistakes instead of being worried about their own. That is a very passive way of being competitive. Instead of wanting to be the best, it is wanting the other to be not so strong.

    "I'm not waiting for Manchester United to make a mistake at the top - I wait for my team to improve by working."

    Yet Mourinho still expects bitter rivals Arsenal to finish in the top four despite their worst start in Premiership history.

    And he insists they will not be out of the title race even if they lose at Old Trafford tomorrow and drop 13 points behind the Premiership leaders.

    He said: "Last season's top four still have the conditions to finish top four again.

    "But if somebody sleeps and has a really bad Premiership season, like Liverpool two seasons ago or Arsenal the last time, someone else can jump into a Champions League slot.

    "This is just the beginning of the season and points are very important, especially for Liverpool and Arsenal who are coming from behind.

    "If they lose again this weekend they start being in a difficult position. But I don't believe a loser can say on Sunday 'It's over for us'."
