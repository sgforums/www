    Arsenal boss Arsene Wenger is planning showdown talks with Thierry Henry and is expected to tell his captain he will not play again this year.

    Wenger refused to discuss at length Henry's absence following Arsenal's 3-0 win over Tottenham, but did hint the pair are not seeing eye-to-eye at the moment.

    Despite carrying a slight neck injury, Henry believed he was fit to play in Saturday's north London derby and was upset when Wenger informed the striker he would not be playing.

    "It is very difficult to speak about that because first I want to sit down with him and tell him what I expect from him and where we go when he has recovered," he said.

    "I'm not saying that I'm happy or unhappy, but he is not ready to play. He is tired, he needs to recover fully, and when you don't play for a while you need to take time to prepare for your return. He cannot expect to walk straight back into the team."
