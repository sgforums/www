    nope....

    for karate is 9 steps to black :

    White belt
    Blue belt
    Blue belt plus Yellow tip
    Yellow belt
    Yellow belt plus Green tip
    Green belt
    Green belt plus Brown tip
    Brown belt
    Brown belt plus Black tip
    Black belt 1st dan

    for taekwondo is 10 steps to black :

    White belt
    White belt plus Yellow tip
    Yellow belt
    Yellow belt plus Green tip
    Green belt
    Green belt plus Blue tip
    Blue belt
    Blue belt plus Brown tip
    Brown belt
    Brown belt plus Black tip
    Black belt 1st dan

    for Judo is 6 steps to black :

    White belt
    Yellow belt
    Orange belt
    Green belt
    Blue belt
    Brown belt
    Black belt

    as for the time taken for each sports to attain black blet is about 3 years to 6 years.

    The practice of demotion for Judo does not apply in Singapore. In fact, it is also very difficult to get demoted in Japan where Judo is founded. You do not get demoted simply by not winning enough compeitions or randori but committed serious disciplinary infringements. Even so this is quite unheard of but it do exists.

    As for the number of years to get to shodan (black belt), 3 years is possble but provided you are very committed to the sport. If you are doing it leisurely, then it would take a good 6 years or more depending on your commitment.
