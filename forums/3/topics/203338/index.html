    Villarreal coach Manuel Pellegrini is resigned to losing midfielder Marcos Senna to United.

    The El Madrigal boss says negotiations over the 30-year-old's transfer have not been concluded, but accepts it is an opportunity Senna can not turn down.

    "In the case of Marcos, nothing is 100 percent finalised," explained Pellegrini to Marca, the daily Spanish sports newspaper. "I understand that this is a great opportunity for him but, for us, it is going to be a great footballing loss if he does end up going. If he leaves, I'll be happy for him but I will be sad for the team."

    "I am very happy for him because he deserves it," Pelligrini added. "He is a player who has proved his class and I have always found it very strange that nobody came in for him sooner as he is a great player."
