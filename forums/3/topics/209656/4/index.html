    Chelsea boss Mourinho grateful to finally work with players
    tribalfooball.com - September 16, 2006

    Chelsea boss Jose Mourinho was grateful for a rare few days to coach his players.
    The Chelsea boss said: "It was one of the few days we've had work tactically, properly on movement and co-ordination in the team. We had a very good session.

    "Next week we will have two or three days like that because we play Liverpool on Sunday and Fulham on Saturday.

    "This is what we need for the new players to feel better inside the team.

    "We have to wait but while we are doing this work and waiting for time to work, if we can do it and not lose a lot of points that's perfect."

    Chelsea, beaten 2-1 at Middlesbrough, have slipped three points off the pace set by early league leaders Manchester United but the champions can move eight points clear of Liverpool with a win on Sunday.

    Mourinho said: "If you don't lose points now and your best time is still to arrive we are very positive.

    "This is a time to improve and not to lose points.

    "If, in two months time, we are playing fantastic but we are 10 points behind Manchester United it's not good.

    "But if, in two months time, we are playing fantastic and winning match after match and the distance between us and the top of the league is one, two or three points, that is a very good situation."

    Chelsea's Shevchenko seeking Istanbul revenge
    tribalfooball.com - September 16, 2006

    Chelsea striker Andriy Shevchenko wants Istanbul revenge today against Liverpool.
    Shevchenko suffered heartache in the epic 2005 Champions League final, in Istanbul, missing the vital penalty when the Reds came from 3-0 down to beat AC Milan in a shoot-out.

    The Merseysiders then wrecked his Chelsea debut in last month's FA Community Shield, when the Ukrainian scored but ended up losing 2-1.

    Shevchenko, 29, said: "In my first game for Chelsea, we lost to them and now it's important to win.

    "I scored but my team lost and it is important to win in this game.

    "It's a difficult game - Liverpool are a big club and a good team."

    Chelsea paid Milan ?30million for the services of Shevchenko and such a huge price tag demands close scrutiny.

    The striker has scored twice in his first six games for the Blues but has not arrived in England with a bang.

    This week he conducted his first interview in English for Chelsea TV, stayed behind for extra training and joined John Terry for a game of golf but it is on the pitch where he is desperate to impress.

    Shevchenko said: "I feel okay. My performance is coming and it's very important to work now for the future.

    "My aim is to win the fans over with the quality of my game and my goals."

    Liverpool's Benitez cool on Agger, Hyypia decision
    tribalfooball.com - September 16, 2006

    Liverpool boss Rafa Benitez was non-committal yesterday over whether Dan Agger would keep his place ahead of Sami Hyypia for today's match at Chelsea.

    Finnish defender Hyypia is facing his toughest challenge yet.

    Outpaced in the Mersey derby last weekend, he found himself suffering the rare ignominy of a place on the bench in the Champions League against PSV Eindhoven in midweek, while 21-year-old Agger eagerly grabbed his chance.

    Agger cost ?5.8million last season and saw the latter half of that campaign ruined by injury, but he also had a fine game against Chelsea in the Community Shield success.

    That suggests he could again get the nod on Sunday when Liverpool face Chelsea at Stamford Bridge.

    Manager Benitez, as usual, was non-committal, saying: "Danny was ahead of Sami in midweek, and he may be ahead of him again. But you must not forget the seven years of outstanding service and experience Hyypia has given to this club."
