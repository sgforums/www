    Man City boss Pearce questions own methods
    tribalfooball.com - September 21, 2006

    Manchester City boss Stuart Pearce admits he's looking at everything, including his own coaching methods, to arrest the slide of his team.
    His full-strength side were embarrassingly dumped out of the Carling Cup at League One Chesterfield on Wednesday.

    That made it 13 defeats in City's last 16 games as callers to a local radio station began to call for Psycho's head.

    Pearce said: "I am bitterly disappointed by the result - it just wasn't good enough.

    "We will just have to work hard on the training pitch to get through this. I feel like I work hard as it is but I will just have to work harder.

    "And I am not going to blame others. I will take the stick."

    Pearce has even admitted that he may have to have a quick rethink at how he approaches his whole preparation for games.

    He said: "I am going to have to look at the coaching methods, everything. When you have bad results you have to analyse what you are doing."

    Chesterfield had won only one game in seven before meeting City.

    Yet Spireites' match-winner Derek Niven, who hit a stunning volley in the 2-1 victory, said he could sense City's nerves.

    He even admitted surprise that Pearce's side surrendered so meekly.

    Niven said: "You expect more aggression from a team managed by Stuart Pearce.

    "He is the sort of person who looks for his team to close down and pressurise sides - yet that is what we did to them.

    "We knew they had been struggling and we sensed their nervousness and took advantage. We got on top of them and got our just rewards.

    "When a team is not playing well you can pressure them into making mistakes."

    Chesterfield boss Roy McFarland added: "I have been in football 40 years and, at the final whistle, the first person I felt for was the opposing manager.

    "There is a lot of pressure on Stuart at the moment.

    "I wish him well but he has got a lot of work to do."
