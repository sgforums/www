    Just wondering if there are any experienced runners here who can share some tips so that i can get a gold a napfa. Don't wanna go ptp man. I'll share some of the few tips i have found useful for myself.

    1. Take large strides when you run. Don't just focus on running. Focus on covering as much distance in a step as you can.

    2. Take in large and deep breaths. Not mandatory but i always inhale twice successively with 2 steps and exhale on the consecutive 2 steps and repeat.

    3. Keep a good posture, relax your hands, don't clench your fist.

    So far these are all i know. I'm not a good runner yet. SO might wanna hear from some 9 min or 10 min runners in 2.4km run.[/img]

    Here's a couple that helped me,

    2.4 is as much of a test of mental strength as it is a test of physical endurance.
    Always fix your eyes on the furthest possible point ahead, be it a tree, a car or any object. It helps to keep you mentally objective and also prevents your head from dropping. Keep some sort of rhythm going inside your head to prevent your brain from going to sleep.
