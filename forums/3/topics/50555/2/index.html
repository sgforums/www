    For training, going for interval training is usually the best.

    This is where you sprint 1 lap around the track in as short a time as possible (say 1.20 min), take a short 30 sec rest, and then sprint another lap. Yes your lap times will get more and more as you are more shagged, but your stamina will increase.

    Build endurance also by going for long distance runs at a slow pace.

    Train 3 times a week for about 2 months should be good.

    You know, the mental aspect of the 2.4km can really shave off the time. But a PTI warrant officer once warned us (when I was a regular) that using mind-over-matter tricks are dangerous. Your heart cannot do it, but your mind is forcing your system to function beyond its capacity. He has personally experienced an NSman who collapsed and died after IPPT because he was challenged by a fellow NSman (I think he was insulted or something).

    It also happened to me before. I had just recovered from fever the previous day but still went for IPPT anyway (it's the last chance I had for my medal as the year is closing). I really felt weak, but I knew I could do it (I've got a medal the previous year), so I pushed myself. I vomitted, collapsed and was sent to the medical centre immediately after the run. Still no medal, though I finished .

    Use mind-over-matter only when your heart and body can take it. Never use it to substitute an untrained and unfit body. Your family and loved ones would prefer you to be alive than get that medal (it's only a matter of pride!)
