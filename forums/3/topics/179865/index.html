    Arsenal's Wenger rubbishes Real Madrid Cole link
    tribalfootball.com - February 25, 2006

    Arsenal manager Arsene Wenger has played down speculation that left-back Ashley Cole was set to join Real Madrid.

    Wenger said: "We have beaten Real Madrid and now my whole team is going to play in Madrid! The speculation is frustrating, but I cannot stop it, so it is better I do not worry about it. I feel he is more committed. His attitude has never been a problem to me this season.

    "He was always highly focused and committed. In fairness, he was very unlucky because he had a very good start to the season. Unfortunately he got his injury."

    He added: "He signed only a one-year extension because we had this problem to give him a pay rise and you can only do it by extending your contract.

    "He wanted one year, if he had wanted three years, I would have given him three years. We settled down for that and to see where we go. But he still has two years to go from the end of the season - for me, it is not a real problem, frankly. At the end of the season he has two years, so we will start maybe in September or October."

    Real Madrid president Martin: The global project will continue
    tribalfootball.com - March 2, 2006

    New Real Madrid president Fernando Martin says he will continue predecessor Florentino Perez's philosophy of marketing the club across the world.

    In his first interview yesterday, Martin told Real Madrid TV: "We are agreed on the global project. There are some differences between us, but many more similarities.

    "We are going to strengthen the great things we have developed and fix those that haven't worked. This is the continuation of the project that Florentino set out and now we must rejuvenate the most important part of that - the football team.

    "The players need affection and understanding.

    "Affection in the sense to ask them why this has happened, to understand what has happened until now but being firm in what we want in the future.

    "The players must now understand they need to fulfill the commitments they have made to the club."

    Martin will meet the players on Friday in a dinner meeting which will also be attended by the first team coaching staff.
