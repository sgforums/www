    Pedigree wise, Scholes is a more cultured player.

    Scholes started off as a striker, in a way an understudy to Cantona. He however gave Sir Alex a lot of headache by seizing his chances when coming on as a substitute. Gradually, SAF played him in a more withdrawn midfield role which he excelled in. His predatory skills in the penalty box was a result of his early development as a striker. Very cool finishing.

    As for Lampard, from his days as a midfielder in West Ham to his current role in Chelsea, he is more effective than spectacular. And there are easily many other effective English midfielders.

    Scholes for me any day.
