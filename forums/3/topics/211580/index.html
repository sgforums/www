    Pre-Match News

    Bolton skipper Nolan: We'll win it for Sam
    tribalfooball.com - September 23, 2006

    Bolton Wanderers captain Kevin Nolan says they'll beat Portsmouth tomorrow for manager Sam Allardyce.

    Angered, but galvanised, by the attack on their manager's integrity, Nolan says the players are hoping to give him something to cheer at Fratton Park.

    "We want a positive result for the gaffer because he deserves it," he told the Lancashire Evening Telegraph.

    The Bolton players held a team meeting yesterday, when they assembled for the first time since the programme was screened on Tuesday night. They emerged determined to turn a negative portrayal of their manager into a positive reaction on the field.

    And, Nolan says their determination has been matched only by the support they have had from the fans.

    "We are all fully behind the gaffer," he said, "and I would like to thank the fans, on behalf of all the players, for the support we've received from them over the past few days. They've been absolutely fantastic.

    "Now we're hoping to give Sam something to cheer about. Hopefully, we can get him through this tough time.

    "We are a family at this club, always behind each other, even at tough times.

    "But the important thing now is to do our talking out on the football pitch, starting on Monday.

    "There's certainly been no adverse reaction here. The gaffer took training yesterday and I think it was good for him to get back on the training ground, because he knows he's got fantastic support from the lads and all the staff."[/color]

    Post-Match News

    Bolton skipper Nolan: Winner for Sam
    tribalfooball.com - September 25, 2006

    Bolton Wanderers matchwinner Kevin Nolan says his goal against Portsmouth last night was for gaffer Sam Allardyce.
    Nolan ended Pompey's unbeaten run with a 25-yard volley to bring some cheer to Bolton boss Allardyce, named in the Panorama bung allegations.

    And the midfielder said: "That win was for him. It was a dogged performance, that's what we're all about.

    "It's been a tough week but it shows the unity we have, right through the staff from the manager."

    Allardyce admitted the game was more stressful than usual. He said: "Every Premiership match is emotionally exhausting but this one was a little more with what's gone on this week."

    Allardyce briefly lost his cool when he was asked live on TV if he had ever been offered a bung. Sky Sports presenter Jeff Stelling posed the question and a clearly-rattled Allardyce paused before saying: "You're out of order asking me that."

    Pompey would have gone top if they had won and were hoping to equal Chelsea's record of six clean sheets at the start of a season. It was their first defeat of the season but boss Harry Redknapp admitted he felt sorry for Big Sam.

    Redknapp, who was also named in the BBC programme, said: "I feel for him big time. It was different for me but I could sense standing on the touchline beside him that it has got to him."

    Allardyce added: "Jussi Jaaskelainen made some great saves for us and I am very proud of the players.

    "I can see why teams don't win here often and that's our first away win since last December. It's a record we weren't proud of but we have got rid of it now."
