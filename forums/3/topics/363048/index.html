    Daily Express, 6 June 2009

    Malaysian junior swimmers added another seven gold medals and one new record to their first day tally of 11 gold medals, when the second day of the 33rd Southeast Asia (SEA) Age Group Swimming Championship events ended at the National Aquatic Centre in Bukit Jalil, Friday.

    After completing Friday's events, the Malaysian contingent raised its medal tally to 18 gold, 16 silver and 10 bronze medals while Thailand maintained top spot with 28 gold, 21 silver and 23 bronze medals.

    Singapore are third with 10 gold, 10 silver and 14 bronze medals.
