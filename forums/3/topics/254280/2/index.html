    With relegation virtually assured, Leeds entered a Company Voluntary Arrangement (administration) on 4th May 2007, thus incurring a league imposed 10 point deduction which officially relegated the club to the third tier of English football. [19][20]. - Wikipedia
    Note the word "virtually". As alluded earlier, the 10-point penalty was enforced after Leeds decided to call in administrators after the penultimate game (but before their final game) of the season where they still had a theoretical chance of survival.

    Had Leeds not called in the administrators after their penultimate game, they would have had to beat Derby in their final game by a sufficiently huge margin, coupled with Hull losing their final game by a margin large enough for Leeds to escape the drop on virtue of a better goal difference. A tall order by all accounts, but not mathematically impossible.

    That said, Leeds had probably pursued the more prudent option of calling in the administrators before the season ended so as to ensure the 10-point deduction isn't carried through to next season.
