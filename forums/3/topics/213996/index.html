    Arsene Wenger says he is desperate to end Arsenal's recent barren spell in the league after two years of chasing Chelsea.

    Since Jose Mourinho's arrival in English football The Gunners have struggled to be competitive in the league, finishing 12 points behind their London rivals in the 2004/2005 season and suffering from a huge 24 point deficit last term.

    However, the Frenchman says he will never accept any excuses for failing to win silverware, adding that he is determined to get his hands on the Premiership trophy for the fourth time in May with his young side.

    "I do not think you can be happy without winning trophies - somewhere it would raise doubts about your ability to win things," said Wenger on the club's official website.

    "Now I am ready to win another one [championship] because the last two years have been very difficult.

    "I always feel you can do better in life and I regret every wrong decision I have made, and every time we have lost.

    "Overall we have had a consistent level of performance and we are proud of what we have done.

    "But I believe as well the duty of a manager is to always think you could have done better and how can you do better in the future."

    Arsenal full of young leaders - Wenger
    tribalfooball.com - October 06, 2006

    Arsenal boss Arsene Wenger insists he has several future captains in his young squad.

    Driven on by inspirational skipper Thierry Henry, the Arsenal boss feels his current squad has the most potential of any he has managed during the last 10 years at the club.

    Wenger believes having so many men capable of taking a game by the scruff of the neck can only be beneficial to the progress of the whole side.

    The Arsenal manager noted: "We do have some younger players who can take leadership in the team, although at the moment we have three experienced captains in Henry, Gilberto and [Jens] Lehmann.

    "In the generation behind, there is Kolo [Toure] who could be, [Cesc] Fabregas certainly in a few years, [Philippe] Senderos as well. They have this kind of potential one day.

    "I feel also that a big team is made of many leaders."

    Wenger: Arsenal can become Europe's dominant club
    tribalfooball.com - October 10, 2006

    Arsene Wenger is hoping to turn Arsenal into the biggest force in European football.

    The Gunners boss has admitted that he could never have foreseen the success he has enjoyed in London when he first took charge of the club ten years ago.

    But he is still ambitious and would like to see Arsenal progress even further.

    "My project is to dominate the English league and to dominate Europe," he told France Football. "Nowadays Arsenal is a club superior to what I could have imagined at the beginning.

    "When we built our new stadium we talked about a capacity of 50,000, then 55,000, then 60,000. Now we are asking ourselves already if it is too small.

    "That could seem pretentious, but I believe that if you don't give real ambition to a club it will not take off."

    He relishes the freedom to make his own decisions, rather than be strictly governed as he might be at a club like Real Madrid.

    "At Arsenal, I even have more freedom than a lot of other English clubs," he explained.

    "That is why I refused opportunities like Real Madrid.

    "I am like an animal that has a lot of freedom and then you want to keep him on a leash."
