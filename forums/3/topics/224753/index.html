    Man City boss Pearce wants rivals to condemn their divers
    tribalfooball.com - December 10, 2006

    Manchester City boss Stuart Pearce is demanding rival managers follow his example and condemn their own players if they dive.
    Blues striker Bernardo Corradi was sent off by referee Graham Poll in the last minute of Saturday's Manchester derby at Old Trafford after the official decided the Italian had dived in the United box in an attempt to win a penalty.

    Poll showed a second yellow card to the former Parma striker, which will result in a two-game ban as Corradi has already been dismissed once this season.

    However, rather than defend his player, as Sir Alex Ferguson did in similar circumstances last weekend when Cristiano Ronaldo won a crucial spot-kick at Middlesbrough, or claim he had been unsighted, Pearce confronted the issue head on.

    Corradi can expect to face the wrath of his manager in training early next week but, as the game tries to clean up its image, Pearce believes it is time his fellow managers adopted the same approach.

    "Why should I condone what happened?" said the former England left-back.

    "I expect my players to stay on their feet rather than make the referee's job difficult by forcing him to make a decision - then easy because he sent him off.

    "Bernardo went down too cheaply and I will be talking to him about it this week.

    "But let it not just be me who stands up and gives an honest assessment of what I have seen and be critical about something one of my team has done. It would be nice if some of the other managers did the same.

    "Probably 19 other Premiership managers would give you some cock and bull story about what happened. I am not prepared to do that.

    "We all want a bit of honesty, so we should all come out and say something if one of our players goes down. Let's all do something about it."
