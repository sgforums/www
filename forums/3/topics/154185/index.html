    Personally i also think muay thai trainings are quite similar anywhere - running, skipping, lots of punching etc.

    1st need to beef up cardio endurance to stay "Fighting Fit". I find burpees pushup is a good form of training too. Try doing 3 mins skipping + 1 mins burpees and see how many times u can achieve. Learning correct punching techniques, squarts...ducks etc also important lah.

    Having a good "teacher" is most essential. Hard to find these days, all those gyms charge u 1 arm 1 leg. In this aspect, I'm lucky to have a personal teacher trained in Boxing, TKD, Judo, Hapkido, Karate, Wushu for more than 30 years. I still go gym for weight stations and cardio machines. But all other martial arts/fitness trainings now are taught by him. We trained hard just to build foundation. A typical training consists of several sessions of skippings (10 mins each), at least 500 punches and 500 kicks on each leg..
