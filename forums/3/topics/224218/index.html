    Gilberto Silva - plays down Henry absence.

    Gilberto Silva insists Arsenal should draw inspiration from their Old Trafford heroics without Thierry Henry as they prepare for Sunday's Barclays Premiership trip to Chelsea.

    Henry missed the 1-0 triumph at Manchester United in September with a foot injury but the Gunners still outplayed Sir Alex Ferguson's side with Emmanuel Adebayor firing the winner.

    The Arsenal skipper is unavailable for the Blues clash because of a neck problem that could keep him out until the new year but Gilberto insists victory at Stamford Bridge is still possible.

    "We are doing our best without Thierry," said the Brazilian midfielder, who has taken the captain's armband in Henry's absence.

    "Thierry is very important to us because he's the skipper. But we are doing best and have shown that we can play without him.

    "Against Chelsea he might not play so we need to do our best without him and I hope we do the job well.

    "We won at Old Trafford and, more recently, against Tottenham when he wasn't there so we can win at Chelsea without him."

    It's a real tough ask for Arsenal, especially without striker Thierry Henry. One of Arsenal's problems on the road has been playing against teams that are physical like Chelsea, who eventually will break them down.

    Arsenal boss Wenger hopeful Djourou will make Chelsea clash
    tribalfooball.com - December 07, 2006

    Arsenal boss Arsene Wenger is hopeful defender Johan Djourou will be fit for Sunday's huge clash with Chelsea.
    Djourou suffered a hamstring injury in Wednesday night's goalless Champions League draw with Porto but his chances of featuring at Stamford Bridge are good.

    William Gallas, however, underwent a scan on the thigh problem he sustained in training last month and could be sidelined for longer than expected.

    "William Gallas is a big question mark, Johan Djourou is a small question mark," Wenger told Arsenal TV Online.

    "All that will be assessed Friday morning or Saturday morning at the latest.

    "I'm reasonably optimistic for Djourou and a little bit less for Gallas. William had a scan today.

    "That will tell us whether we can go further with him or he has to slow his recovery down."

    Arsenal are already missing Kolo Toure for the Chelsea clash through suspension.

    No date has been set for Thierry Henry's return, with the French striker possibly out until the new year with his neck injury.

    Czech winger Tomas Rosicky will spend two more weeks in the treatment room because of a muscle tear between the hamstring and groin.

    Chelsea's Cole: I know I'll be hammered at Arsenal
    tribalfooball.com - December 08, 2006

    Chelsea fullback Ashley Cole admits he expects a difficult return to former club Arsenal on Sunday.
    Cole made an acrimonious switch to Chelsea in the summer after a saga that included a "tapping up" row and a savage attack on Arsenal in his book.

    England defender Cole said: "I have to be honest with myself. I think I am going to get booed badly.

    "I am very well aware that some fans are very angry."

    Cole insists he did nothing wrong in making his move from his boyhood club.

    He said: "Some people are going to agree with what I have done and some are not. But I didn't move for money. It was a point of principle. I didn't feel I was wanted.

    "In any other job, if someone works for one company then goes to work for another it's not a problem.

    "But if you do it as a footballer you are greedy and leaving for the money. I'm not going to say it's unfair because footballers have a great life. But it's not easy.

    "I think I am going to get stick when I go back there. Yet if I stayed at Arsenal I was still likely to get abuse.

    "All through last season I had to deal with it and it's made me stronger and hopefully it can keep making me stronger."

    Gilberto so proud to be chief Gunner
    Gilberto has voiced his pride at being handed the Arsenal captaincy - and draws confidence from the faith shown in him by boss Arsene Wenger

    The Brazilian midfielder has taken the armband while Thierry Henry recovers from his neck injury and will lead Arsenal in Sunday's Premiership clash at Chelsea.

    He said: "I'm enjoying the captaincy. It's a lot more responsibility for me. I'm very proud of myself for being captain of Arsenal.

    "It shows Arsene has a lot of belief in me. I try to help the team, even when I'm not on the pitch, so that they can do the best they can.

    "Most of the time the players respond to me on the pitch when I talk to them."

    Arsenal will face Ashley Cole for the first time since his controversial move to Chelsea on transfer deadline day.

    Cole is no longer a favourite with Gunners fans following the publication of his autobiography, 'My Defence' - in which he is highly critical of the north London club.

    But Gilberto insists there is no ill-feeling at Arsenal for the England left-back.

    He said: "We haven't thought about Ashley Cole, just about Chelsea - because it will be a hard game.

    "Ashley will want to do his best, and we will do our best to win the game. I haven't seen Ashley since he left us."

    Arsenal secured a goalless draw with Porto in Wednesday's Champions League clash to finish top of Group G.

    They will resume European action in February, and Gilberto knows expectations remain high after the Gunners reached last season's final.

    He said: "When you achieve something or you go very close, the pressure is a lot greater than before.

    "Beforehand, no one expects you to win - but because we got to the final last season, there is a lot of pressure on us.

    "But the team is ready for it, because we are growing game by game and getting more mature.

    "It's up to us to keep this good thing going and cope with the pressure."

    Wenger: Arsenal have gone backwards compared to Chelsea
    tribalfooball.com - December 10, 2006

    Arsenal boss Arsene Wenger admits his team has gone backwards in recent years compared to the Chelsea juggernaut.

    Ahead of today's derby, Wenger said: "Chelsea have become better in recent years.

    "It is a side that has been slowly growing and maturing. All their players are between 26 and 32 so they know how to handle big games and be efficient.

    "Three or four years ago it was a young side with Lampard and Terry but they are better now. They have also had an injection of good players and they have a good manager. That has made a difference.

    "You can only go with the table and think that we have regressed a little bit in comparison but we have rebuilt now and we have huge potential.

    "We got to the Final of the Champions League without losing a game and we had a good run after Christmas. That is why I feel the team is ready to take off again."
