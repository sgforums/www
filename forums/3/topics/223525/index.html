    Ronaldo antics enrage Southgate

    Ronaldo receives a pat on the back from Sir Alex Ferguson

    Middlesbrough boss Gareth Southgate has labelled Cristiano Ronaldo a serial diver after his display in Manchester United's 2-1 win at The Riverside.
    Television replays showed there was no contact between Boro keeper Mark Schwarzer and Ronaldo when the winger won United a 19th-minute penalty.

    "Ronaldo has a history of it," Southgate told the News of the World.

    "Our keeper has done everything to get out of the way, but the lad has gone down once again."

    Interview: Middlebrough boss Gareth Southgate

    Asked if he felt cheated by Ronaldo, Southgate said: " Yes - it's as simple as that. I cannot see it was a penalty for love nor money.
    "He did it again afterwards with a free-kick against George Boateng."
    Ronaldo should have been yellow carded on both occasions

    After watching television replays, Manchester United boss Sir Alex Ferguson said the penalty was justified.

    "Schwarzer is trying to avoid it, but would Ronaldo have scored? I think he would, but anyway it was a penalty kick."

    It is not the first time Ronaldo's sportmanship has been questioned.

    Liverpool captain Steven Gerrard labelled the winger "a disgrace" for his part in Wayne Rooney's sending-off at the World Cup.

    Gerrard was furious when Ronaldo got involved after Rooney appeared to stamp on Portugal defender Ricardo Carvalho.

    "Sadly a dark side stains Cristiano Ronaldo's game," wrote Gerrard in his autobiography.

    Southgate wants action against Man Utd's Ronaldo
    tribalfooball.com - December 03, 2006

    Middlesbrough boss Gareth Southgate wants action taken against Manchester United's Cristiano Ronaldo over the way he won his team's penalty on Saturday.
    Southgate, whose troops crashed 2-1, raged: "Something has to be done because it is happening with that lad too many times.

    "People will say this has always been around. But it seems to be a handful of players at the centre of it.

    "It makes referees' jobs so difficult. I can't comment on the referee's decision because I don't know what his view of it was.

    "He felt from the view he had there was contact. But it is clear for everyone to see there wasn't.

    "I guess that is the first time I have been really angry as a manager. When there has been an injustice it makes you feel that way.

    "I am in the fortunate position where he isn't one of my players. It is not for me to comment."

    United boss Alex Ferguson stuck by his initial view that ref Chris Foy was correct to award the spot-kick.

    He said: "I've just seen it and it looks as though the goalkeeper didn't touch him.

    "But it's the intent - what could he do? He's going to be carted anyway. I think it's a penalty."

    Southgate (right) reckons the decision was a crucial moment - even though James Morrison cancelled out Louis Saha's spot-kick only for United's Darren Fletcher to head the winner.

    The Boro boss added: "I've probably said as much as I should have about someone else's player but I do feel aggrieved it gave us a mountain to climb in the game.

    "My players have given everything and you never know how a game is going to pan out.

    "But the first goal in any game in this division is crucial and we were playing well until that point. It knocked the stuffing out of us."
