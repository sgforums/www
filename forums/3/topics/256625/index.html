    French Open Fun Facts
    o The French Open is officially known as Roland Garros.

    o Roland Garros is named after its stadium, which is in turn named after the World War I French pilot.

    o The aviator, Roland Garros is known as the world's first fighter ace, the first real fighter pilot.

    o The French Open began as a national tournament in 1891. In 1925, the tournament opened itself to international competitors with the event held on a grass surface.

    o Red clay was installed when the new stadium was built.

    o As of 2006, the last six French Open men's singles championships were won by men who did not win any other Grand Slam tournament.

    o In 1968, the French Championships became the first Grand Slam tournament to go "open", allowing both amateurs and professionals to compete.

    o Bjorn Borg has the most singles titles at the French, with 6. Chris Evert holds the record for the women, with 7.

    o Michael Chang is the youngest singles champion. He was 17 years 3 months in 1989 when he beat Stefan Edberg 6-1, 3-6, 4-6, 6-4, 6-2. The youngest women was Christine Truman at age 18 139 days.

    Actually, that's the sad inaccurate reality of the ATP rankings. Basically, rankings are determined by the points accumulated from tournaments each player chooses to play without taking into account the surface on which a tournament is played on. Hence, it can be easily concluded a claycourt specialist actually has a massive edge over a grasscourt specialist simply because there're more claycourt tournaments than grasscourt ones on the ATP calendar (and therefore, easier for the claycourt specialist to chalk up points).

    Roddick has a proven pedigree on both grass and hardcourts which explains his formidable ranking. That said, he's hardly competent on clay, but I don't suppose the French Open organisers could ignore his overall ATP ranking.
