    Chelsea assistant boss Steve Clarke heaped praise on winger Arjen Robben after the Dutchman's performance in last night's win over Everton.
    Manager Jose Mourinho believes Chelsea's win over Liverpool was unfairly overshadowed by the debate over Jose Reina's red card - for pushing Robben - and Clarke backed his boss.

    Liverpool boss Rafael Benitez had accused Robben of 'play-acting' to get Reina sent off but Clarke insisted that the criticism of the Dutchman was unreasonable.

    Clarke declared: "The stick he got was unjustified but I thought his performance tonight was great. he has been playing very very well for us in the last few weeks and he continued that against Everton."

    Chelsea won 4-1 to book a fifth-round tie against Colchester.

    "The criticism we get we just can't understand," said Clarke.

    "We deserve a lot more credit than we actually get. The game on Sunday was a game we won 2-0 against one of our big rivals for the title, and we got no real credit for it because of comments made at the end of the game. It is not fair.

    "All we can do is play well every game but we don't create a siege mentality.

    "If you just take the game at the weekend for example, there was not a lot talked about the football - the way we had beaten one of our top rivals.

    "A team that everybody was saying could challenge us for the title and we beat them again.

    "At the end of the game everything else was spoken about except how well we played to win that game. I don't think Chelsea get enough credit for the way they perform, week-in and week out."
