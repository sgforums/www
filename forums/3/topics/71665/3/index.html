    tips for all beginners,

    Go for high lob

    Hitting a lob really high is a smart defensive move for a number of reasons:

    - High lobs buy time: Defensive lobs are usually hit when you're out of position and trying to gain time to recover. Lofting the ball higher keeps it in the air longer and gives you more time to get back to the center of the court.

    - High lobs are difficult to smash: Since the ball gains speed as it's dropping, high lobs travel faster than shallow lobs. Only players with excellent overheads will attempt to smash high lobs before they bounce. Most players will wait for the ball to bounce and hit a ground stroke rather than an overhead.

    - High lobs go deeper: The higher the lob, the deeper the ball will land. And deep lobs can be trouble for lots of recreational players. You may be surprised when you find out how many of your opponents are ill-equipped to handle high-bouncing lobs that land deep in the court.

    - High lobs will make a beginner more time to position him/herself for the return ball.

    The above tip is one of the simple trick for a real beginner to try out for a shot with the intermediate players on the court.

    Hi all ,

    share some basic tips for beginners

    Grid (holding of racket handle)

    Holding of the racket is important before you start the game. It really helps to control the ball coming towards you. Always lock your wrist while holding your racket and swing your racket below the coming ball ,whether your are using backhand / forehand.

    Positioning

    When your opponent give you a return ball , do not look at him/her but to see for the ball coming towards you and get really to hit it.

    Remember always hold your racket(both hands) facing parallel with you and get really for the incoming ball(forehand/backhand)
