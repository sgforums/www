    Green light for Allardyce
    February 08, 2006

    BOLTON manager Sam Allardyce will be given the green light by the club chairman to accept the England manager's job if it is offered to him.

    Allardyce is one of the front-runners to replace Swede Sven-Goran Eriksson, and has said he would love the job.

    And despite hoping to hold on to his coveted manager, Bolton chairman Phil Gartside has affirmed it would be an honour if the England offer came Allardyce's way.

    Gartside said: 'I am flattered Sam's name has been linked with the England coaching vacancy.

    'Should the call come from the FA, it would be a very proud moment for Sam Allardyce, Bolton Wanderers and me personally. Until such time, Sam is 100 per cent committed to his important role at Bolton.'

    Bolton are currently seventh in the Premiership. - PA Sport.
