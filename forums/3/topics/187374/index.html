    Milan suffer triple injury worry

    MILAN, April 22 (Reuters) - AC Milan's preparations for Wednesday's Champions League semi-final return at Barcelona suffered a blow with three players taken off injured in the first half of their Serie A match at Messina.

    The six-times European champions said later on their website that Brazilian defender Cafu had also picked up an injury at Messina and that all four players would be examined on Sunday.

    'We will see how they are on Sunday because at the moment we can't say anything,' said Milan coach Carlo Ancelotti.

    Brazilian Kaka and Italy defender Alessandro Nesta were both substituted with injuries and then midfielder Massimo Ambrosini, who had replaced Kaka, also had to go off in the 28th minute of Saturday's match.

    Milan were already without striker Filippo Inzaghi who has been struggling to recover from a virus which kept him out of the 1-0 first-leg defeat by Barcelona at the San Siro.
