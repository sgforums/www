    Gavin Hylton (Jamaica), Nigel Webb (Jamaica) and Darryl Strachan (Jamaica) defeated competitors Qi Wang (Fiji), Richel Sen (Fiji) and Rikesh Padarath (Fiji) in the gruelling four-rubber match.

    Spinning and smashing to win

    In only its second appearance at the Commonwealth Games, competition in the Table Tennis events is proving to be fierce, with many winners at Manchester returning to defend their titles.

    The Singapore Women's team, a hot favourite, was a force to be reckoned with, defeating the New Zealand team 3-0 in a fast and furious clash.

    The match saw the world's number three, Jia Wei Li, challenged early and taken to four games by spirited New Zealander Yang Jiang, who won the first game 11-9.

    In the Nigeria versus Sri Lanka match, Ganiyat Ogundele (NGR) was surprisingly taken to five games by Ruhansi Wijekoon (SRI), but won the fifth game easily to take the match 11-2.

    Meanwhile, Canada showed its team's dominance after comfortably accounting for Uganda 3-0. The class of Mo Zhang overshadowed her opponent and inspired the team to go on and win convincingly.

    In their match against Fijian the experienced Malaysian team, headed by Lee Wei Beh, was evident after a convincing 3-0 victory. Beh was confident in her opening match and showed signs of what she has to offer in upcoming matches.

    Wales' Naomi Owen, Bethan Daunton and Siwan Davies won their games respectively, beating Mauritius 3-0.

    England opened its campaign with a resounding victory over Vanuatu. England's Joanna Parker, Kelly Sibley and Georgina Walker each won their games against Vanuatu's Priscila Tommy, Anolyn Lulu and Kerry Mok respectively, carrying the team to a 3-0 victory.
