    Tonight, Korea will face Iraq.
    Even if the Iraqis win or lose tonight's semi final, they have my respect. We have to understand that their country has been ravaged by years of war and uncertainties. Yet they can still produce a quality team. It's not easy, especially when some players even lost their relatives in the war.

    Football gives them renewed confidence. Let's pray for peace in Iraq. They have a great future.

    Korea was in similar situation in the 40s and 50s. After the Japanese surrendered in 1945, there was the Korean war (1950-1953) that divided the country. Tens of thousands of people died.
    The war-torn Republic of Korea (unofficially known as South Korea) was one of Asia's poorest countries in the 50s and 60s but still managed to produce a team to qualify for the World Cup in 1954 and won the Asian Cup in 1956 and 1960.

    Therefore I believe football can also give hope to the Iraqi people as well.

    Peace to Iraq. End to war.
