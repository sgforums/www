Curbs faces Ashton wait

  • mcfadden
    19 Dec 06, 20:06
    West Ham United striker Dean Ashton is unlikely to return to action until well into the New Year, as he struggles to recover from a broken ankle.

    The 23-year-old has not featured this season after being injured while training with the England national team and new Hammers boss Alan Curbishley admits that his star man is some way off a return.

    He told the Evening Standard: "I'm told that Dean will not begin serious training until the end of January or even early February."

    Meanwhile, Curbishley has challenged Carlos Tevez to prove that he deserves a starting spot, having been left on the bench for the 1-0 home win over Manchester United.

    He added: "He understood and I've said to him that the players who didn't play will have to show me something in training, because I need to see them perform.

    "With the Christmas period they are going to have to show me in training, or when they get their chance, because there are no reserve games."
