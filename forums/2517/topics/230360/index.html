    Srnicek to stay with Magpies

    Pavel Srnicek has agreed to stay at Newcastle until the end of the season.

    The veteran goalkeeper was drafted into St James' Park as cover for Shay Given and Steve Harper last September, initially on a three-month deal.

    But Srnicek is now set to remain with The Magpies until June, before sitting down with club bosses to review his situation.

    "We have agreed that I will stay until the summer, then we will see," he explained.

    Srnicek accepts that his career may come to an end when the season finishes, and he is eager to make a few final appearances for Newcastle.

    Srnicek returned to the Magpies side after eight years away as an 87th-minute replacement for Shay Given in the 3-1 victory over Tottenham before Christmas, and he admits it was an emotional moment.

    He has played just one full game since, the 2-1 defeat to Bolton on Boxing Day, but he is enjoying his time in the North East.

    "Maybe these are the last six months of my career, maybe not. I don't know," he said.

    "I would like to make some more appearances. I did not expect to as I came here to cover my colleagues' backs, but then I appeared in two games.

    "The first one was fantastic. The whole of St James' Park stood up and cheered my name. It was breathtaking, I can't even describe it, but I felt like if I was in seventh heaven.

    "Fortunately I managed not to concede within those three minutes and we won 3-1.

    "The second one against Bolton was not as successful, we lost.

    "However, if I don't get any more chances to play, I will not be unhappy. These two games have been something extra."

    Martins stuns Spurs in thriller

    Obafemi Martins inspired Newcastle to record a 3-2 victory at Tottenham Hotspur in a game that was the very definition of a five-goal thriller.

    The Magpies looked destined for defeat in the face of some determined and inventive attacking football from Spurs, but summoned the necessary character to record their first win in four games and move into 11th place in the Premiership after instigating a superb turnaround.

    Martin Jol's side had already seen Paul Huntington reply to Jermain Defoe's opener when Dimitar Berbatov seemingly struck the decisive blow on 54 minutes, but Martins awoke from his slumber to transform the game in the space of 90 seconds.

    The summer signing from Internazionale rifled a stunning effort past Paul Robinson on 72 minutes before then playing in Nicky Butt for the unlikely winner in what was a non-stop game at White Hart Lane.

    Spurs' irresistible attacking play forced Shay Given to make a series of saves within the first 15 minutes as the North Londoners threatened to overwhelm their Geordie opponents.

    Steed Malbranque and Berbatov were both denied by the Magpies' stand-in captain, but Given reserved his best save for Defoe when he somehow clawed the England striker's downward header off the line.

    However, on 14 minutes Spurs made the inevitable breakthrough after quarter of an hour of relentless attacking, and whilst their efforts certainly merited a goal, the strike itself was highly contentious.

    Malbranque found acres of space down the left hand side and as he flicked the ball across goal Defoe simply slotted past Given from close range despite appearing to be in an offside position.

    Inside two minutes the scoreline changed again, but remarkably it was Newcastle, with their first chance of the game, who silenced the previously jubilant White Hart Lane crowd.

    Young defender Huntington, following in the footsteps of David Edgar who scored from left-back against Manchester United, drilled a shot in off Paul Robinson from a tight angle after his header from a set-piece had only bounced back off Antoine Sibierski at the back post.

    The goal came like a bolt from the blue and knocked Spurs off their stride, but Malbranque tried to recapture their swagger when he lofted the ball just wide from nearly 35 yards after Given had rushed out of his box to clear.

    In an open, attacking game that was a superb advert for all that is best about the high-octane Premiership, Sibierski and Martins both went close, whilst Given continued to sparkle at the other end as he got down quickly to block Aaron Lennon's effort.

    In the last five minutes of the first half Berbatov began to exert more influence on the game with two more shots at Given's under-siege goal, and the efforts continued to come thick and fast as Defoe tried to chip the shot-stopper from 25 yards before fizzing another shot wide.

    Both sides had adrenalin coursing through their veins following a breathless first 45 minutes, and tempers boiled over after the half time whistle as Pascal Chimbonda and Butt became involved in a physical confrontation which saw yellow cards issued to both players.

    Given had also become infuriated with the antics of Berbatov and Chimbonda as he tried to take goal-kicks, and as both teams trudged down the tunnel both sets of players started jostling to add to the drama of an lively evening at White Hart Lane.

    Young Magpies midfielder Matty Pattison went close with a header from James Milner's deep cross early in the second half, but Spurs got the second goal their efforts clearly deserved through Berbatov on 54 minutes.

    The Bulgarian's bouncing volley evaded both Given and Solano, and came immediately after the Peruvian had produced a fantastic sliding block to deny Teemu Tainio.

    Soon after, Defoe blasted a shot from outside the box as Given was again forced to stem the tide of Spurs' attacks, and Tom Huddlestone joined in with a shot that flew only inches wide after taking a deflection off Dyer.

    However, inside two minutes The Magpies turned the game on its head and Martins was the catalyst with a superb finish on 72 minutes.

    After playing a square ball to Dyer the Nigerian striker drifted out to the left side of the Spurs box, and after receiving the ball back from his colleague hit a stunning shot into the top corner that seared past Robinson like laser-guided missile.

    The former Internazionale man was instrumental in Newcastle's third just a minute later, as he played a neat reverse pass to Butt who slid his shot past the dumbfounded Robinson for a barely believable lead given Tottenham's previous dominance.

    Jol threw on Robbie Keane in a bid to find an equaliser, and the Republic of Ireland international quickly saw a goal-bound effort blocked by the arm of Solano as The Magpies rode their luck.

    Berbatov also headed just wide and Defoe went closer still when he rattled the post with a venomous shot after 80 minutes, but despite their multitude of chances Spurs could not take what would have been a deserved point in a game that will live long in the memory.

    Roeder: We deserved the win

    Glenn Roeder insisted that his under-strength Newcastle side fully deserved their dramatic 3-2 win at Tottenham Hotspur.

    The Magpies defied a lengthy injury list and some superb attacking play from Spurs to twice come from behind and take all three points at White Hart Lane.

    Martin Jol's side had previously been imperious at home, losing just twice all season, and Roeder was delighted that his young side showed their strength of character by orchestrating an unlikely comeback.

    "Those boys in there fully deserved the three points here," Roeder told Sky Sports.

    "It's good to be with your players when they have won in the manner they did. To come from behind at White Hart Lane is very difficult.

    "There are young boys out there today, the three or four of them who have come through the academy.

    "It has been a tough baptism for them over the past six or seven weeks with all the injuries and overall they are coping very well and I thought today they were absolutely tremendous and so were our senior players."

    Newcastle's goals came from Paul Huntington, Obafemi Martins and Nicky Butt, and Roeder was full of praise for the goalscorers as well as goalkeeper Shay Given who kept The Magpies in the game with a number of saves.

    "Shay Given, undoubtedly in my opinion, is the best goalkeeper in the Premiership," Roeder added.

    "He is up there with [Petr] Cech. It is a matter of opinion but it is Shay for me.

    "Nicky Butt, I can say now, has been in bed with a heavy dose of flu for five days, has lost nine pounds in weight before the game.

    "I don't think many players could get off their sick bed and play the way he played, he was magnificent, and of course Oba Martins scored the goal that showed you what Oba Martins is all about - unstoppable."

    Solano to snub Copa America

    Newcastle wide-man Nobby Solano has claimed he is not prepared to play for Peru in the Copa America this year as he is disillusioned with his country's Football Association.

    The South American nation are still without a manager ahead of the continental competition which will be held in Venezuela in June, and Solano has revealed his frustration at the working practices of some officials within the national setup.

    The Magpies star, who has been playing in a right-back role in recent weeks, says he cannot countenance sacrificing his pre-season to play in the Copa America if the FA continue to suffer from inertia.

    "The officials of the FA are the only people responsible for all the bad things that happen to Peruvian football," Solano told El Comercio.

    "We can't talk about doing serious work at the national team when we are five months away from the beginning of the Copa America and they still haven't found a coach for the team.

    "I think that the officials have to be more responsible for once in their lives. They need to have clear objectives and if they don't reach positive results then they should all leave the FA. Peru hasn't participated in a World Cup since 1982.

    "All the teams are starting to get prepared for the Copa America but Peru isn't doing anything.

    "In addition some teams are even already thinking about the South American qualifiers for the World Cup 2010. If for the qualifiers the atmosphere is better at the FA then I will play for Peru."

    Solano has also had his say on some potential candidates for the job of managing Peru.

    "My opinion is that the FA has to name a new coach quickly. He must be Peruvian. There are many," Solano added.

    "Just for naming some there are coaches like Julio Cesar Uribe, Roberto Mosquera and Juan Carlos Oblitas. I am sure that any of them can do good work."

    Roeder: It was a disaster

    Newcastle United manager Glenn Roeder has admitted his side's 5-1 thrashing by Birmingham City was a total disaster.

    The Magpies had been favourites to see off the Championship side in their FA Cup replay at St James' but were not at the races and were humbled from start to finish.

    James Milner had just given the home side second half hope, but Steven Taylor's red card sparked a collapse that saw Blues run out 5-1 winners.

    Roeder was stunned by his side's display following their 3-2 win at Tottenham last weekend and is determined to put things right against West Ham.

    "I couldn't be more shocked, that would be an understatement," stated Roeder. "That was the same starting line-up that went to White Hart Lane and won 3-2.

    "I have to really apologise to our fantastic fans for having to sit through that. I had no idea a performance like that was going to come.

    "The first goal that we conceded, which was very poorly defended, summed up the 90 minutes which was a total disaster for us.

    "There are no excuses. The team that we put out I felt would have been good enough to win. It's an inexcusable performance.

    "It's my job to make sure we come out fighting against West Ham on Saturday."

    Taylor was given his marching orders for hauling down DJ Campbell as the last man and Roeder had no qualms with the dismissal.

    "I'd like to be able to make a better comment if I've seen it on the replay, but he appeared to be clean through and Taylor's brought him down," he added.
