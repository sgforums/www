    Clichy out to prove point

    Gael Clichy aims to prove to Arsene Wenger that he is the long-term replacement for Ashley Cole.

    Clichy has become a regular fixture in the Arsenal backline this season, filling the void left by Cole's acrimonious summer transfer to Chelsea.

    "But now I am just enjoying the time I get to play on the pitch."

    Gilberto: Gunners still a threat

    Arsenal vice-captain Gilberto believes a win over Manchester United on Sunday could be the springboard to an unlikely title challenge.

    A victory at Emirates Stadium - in the game that is televised live on Sky Sports 1 - would still leave The Gunners 12 points shy of their pre-eminent rivals.

    However Gilberto is aware that Arsenal squandered a similar advantage four years ago and the Brazilian midfielder is refusing to concede defeat in the Premiership title race.

    "We need to take it step by step. To change our situation we have to win all 15 games we have remaining," Gilberto said in the Daily Star.

    "Sunday will be very important for us. If we win, who knows what might happen. Maybe they could slip up, like we did in the 2002/03 season.

    "We were 12 points ahead and then we dropped point after point and they caught up with us and won the league.

    "But first of all we have to make sure we win our games, because unless we do that nothing can happen."

    Gilberto will miss the crunch game due to suspension after being shown a red card for lashing out at Robbie Savage in the 2-0 win over Blackburn Rovers.

    The holding midfielder admits he is still frustrated at the nature of his dismissal and has accused referee Rob Styles of making a serious error of judgement.

    "What really frustrated me was I was kicked by Savage when I was on the floor after the referee had blown his whistle and stopped the game," Gilberto added.

    "It's strange I get sent off after being kicked while I was on the floor when the game has stopped, yet the person who kicked me only gets a yellow card.

    "I don't know who makes the rules but I think the referee made a big mistake."

    Fergie expects Gunners revival

    Sir Alex Ferguson feels Arsenal have struggled this term on account of the upheaval caused by their move from Highbury, but the Manchester United chief fully expects Arsene Wenger to mastermind a revival.

    Ferguson and Wenger have enjoyed a fierce rivalry for a decade and the Scot turned his attentions to Arsenal's current crop ahead of their meeting on Sunday, live on Sky Sports 1.

    The Red Devils chief feels Arsenal overachieved in reaching last season's UEFA Champions League final, but does not believe that they are suffering a hangover from their European near miss.

    "I don't think the Champions League final has anything to do with it, because I think it was a bonus them getting there," Ferguson told the Daily Mirror.

    "Nobody expected them to get there, it was a surprise and I think they would recognise it themselves because they were a young team and had no record in Europe, so they did exceptionally well to get there.

    "So rather than it being a negative it should have been a positive because they'd overachieved.

    "This season they've got young players, and a change to a new stadium may have something to do with it.

    "A change of environment, a different type of pitch, there's a lot of things can go into it. But they've got quality and you always expect quality to eventually surface.

    "There also seems to be a good camaraderie about them, there doesn't seem to be any break in the ranks."

    Cesc vows to get even better

    Cesc Fabregas believes there is still room for improvement in his game, despite the praise being lavished on the precocious Spaniard.

    The 19-year-old has been in superb form this season, pulling the strings in Arsenal's midfield with an exquisite range of passing.

    Fabregas's performances have coveted many column inches and reportedly also the wish list of Liga giants Real Madrid.

    But the player is determined to keep his feet on the ground, admitting even that his game is not without its flaws.

    "I am just 19. The good thing about being young is that you've got so many things to learn," Fabregas told the Daily Telegraph.

    "Even Thierry [Henry] and Ronaldinho - they can still learn. That's the thing about football. You cannot be perfect.

    "Sometimes you lose the ball, they will take it off your feet.

    "I need to improve my left foot a lot, improve the defensive side, my heading as well. That has come on lately but physically I'm not the biggest man in the world.

    "Even when people were talking about Real Madrid, I think I could have played better.

    "So when you hear all the praise, you just try to forget it."

    Sol relief after nightmare

    Sol Campbell endured a traumatic final season at Arsenal, but the Portsmouth defender believes he has returned from that nightmare a better player.

    Campbell's performances in defence have been instrumental in Portsmouth's rise up the standings, capping a major turnaround in the player's fortunes.

    The 32-year-old's career appeared to be on the ropes after he walked out of Arsenal's 3-2 defeat to West Ham last February but almost 12 months on he is delighted to have proved people wrong.

    "I know what people were saying, that it was the end. To overcome that takes a lot of strength within yourself," Campbell told the Mirror.

    "Whether people always realise that or appreciate it is another thing. When you go through times like that, you have to look at yourself and ask the question: 'What do you really want out of football?'

    "Football gives me great pleasure. I want to win, I want to play good football. I love the challenges."

    Campbell admitted public speculation over his future was a trying experience.

    "It's fair to say I wasn't always smiling last year but I am now. For any professional footballer who has a 20-year career it's not always going to be roses, is it?" added Campbell.

    "You do sometimes go through difficult times. It can happen in any industry. I understand it doesn't happen so publicly for them but throughout my period in football I've shown that kind of inner strength.

    "When you have that kind of experience that I did, you've got to re-evaluate everything around you. You look at what you are doing in your life and realise that some things have got to go and other things have got to stay.

    "It's not about showing other people that I can still do it, that I haven't lost what made me a player. It's about me inside, about who I am as a human being."

    Lehmann unsure of future

    Jens Lehmann has yet to make a decision on his Arsenal future but believes he can continue at the top level for another couple of years.

    Lehmann's current contract expires in June but he has been given no indication by Wenger about whether he will be required next season.

    The German number one is 37-years-old but he feels his fitness is very good and that his career is not quite drawing to a close yet.

    However, he concedes that he does not know where he will be plying his trade next term.

    "To be honest I don't want to think about it at the moment," said Lehmann.

    "I might make a decision next week, I might wait longer.

    "I have only missed one game for Arsenal through injury - and that was when I had a cold before the league game against Liverpool.

    "I feel I can play for another one or two years and anyway I will know when it is time to stop."

    Flamini: We knew Utd would tire

    Mathieu Flamini has revealed Arsene Wenger predicted Manchester United would wilt in the closing stages of Sunday's defeat to Arsenal.

    The Premiership leaders took a 53rd minute lead through Wayne Rooney, but succumbed to a late Gunners comeback as Robin van Persie and Thierry Henry scored in the closing seven minutes.

    Flamini admits United made it difficult for Arsenal, but acknowledged a half-time pep talk from Wenger ensured The Gunners would never give up.

    "During half-time, the coach told us," Flamini told L'Equipe.

    "He asked us to remain focused and strong because Manchester will give up physically in the last 20 minutes.

    "He said: 'They are not the same anymore after 70 minutes'. Once again, the coach was right!

    "We have to be honest, Manchester played very well and we had difficulties to play our game. They countered us very well.

    "But Arsenal is not only a team that plays nice football, it is also a team that never gives up."

    Flamini replaced the suspended Gilberto Silva in central midfield and the Frenchman was happy with his contribution in the 2-1 win.

    He added: "I believe I achieved a good match. It is not a secret that I prefer to play in the midfield.

    "The coach trusts me. Competition is very tough at Arsenal, you have to fight during each match.

    "But that's just like that in a great club and I enjoy it."

    Henry: Victory was special

    Thierry Henry could hardly contain his glee after netting a fine headed winner against Manchester United at Emirates Stadium on Sunday.

    Arsenal's captain was on the periphery of much of the action in a pulsating capital contest but his one major contribution proved to be vital, as he scored Arsenal's winner three minutes into injury time.

    After Wayne Rooney had broken the deadlock early in the second half, with a rare header of his own, there seemed only likely to be one winner but Arsene Wenger's side dug deep and ground out a result which opens up the title race.

    Substitute Robin Van Persie, whose afternoon was later ruined in breaking his fifth metatarsal, grabbed Arsenal's equaliser eight minutes from time before Henry, not for the first time, stole all the plaudits with a brilliant winner.

    The Frenchman admits he is not used to scoring with his head but he was not complaining, after what proved to be the most dramatic of conclusions to a fascinating contest.

    "I scored with my head so it must have been pretty special!" he told Sky Sports.

    "I wanted to make sure to at least make it difficult for Edwin (van der Sar) and I got the ball in the back of the net.

    "It can't be better against Manchester United than to be 1-0 down and come back right at the end and win the game.

    "A win against Manchester United is always special but especially so with the way we did it today.

    "Doing the double over them is always a pleasure."

    Wenger admits Diaby doubts

    Arsene Wenger has welcomed the imminent return of Abou Diaby to the Arsenal starting XI, whilst revealing that he feared for the youngster's career following his serious injury.

    The French midfielder is in line to make his first start since May in the Carling Cup semi-final against North London rivals Tottenham after recovering from a fractured and dislocated ankle.

    Such was the severity of the injury - sustained in a challenge with Sunderland's Dan Smith - that Wenger even pondered taking legal action against the Black Cats player.

    Now The Gunners boss has conceded that he feared for Diaby's future after the horrific injury which left him sidelined for nearly nine months, but the former Auxerre starlet is now back stronger than ever in North London.

    "I had a big doubt in my mind that he would come back," Wenger told the club's official website.

    "They told me first that the broken ankle was a very serious one and they needed advice from the surgeon, who was only optimistic after the surgery.

    "I am very happy because he not only came back, but he looks even stronger than before.

    "It is only his first game back and we must be patient - but he can be a great player and I am sure he will be."

    Diaby will start against Tottenham in the Carling Cup tie, live on Sky Sports 1, and Wenger is pleased that his availability will help to add some steel to a midfield which has looked lightweight at times since the departure of former captain Patrick Vieira.

    "I feel we missed him because he gives you something that the other players do not have. That kind of Vieira presence in the physical challenges," said Wenger.

    "In some games you want him in there, also in the air. When you go to a place and the ball is 'up there' all the time it is very important to have a guy of his size on the throw-ins, set-pieces and long balls forward.

    "They are not similar sorts of players at all really - but in the style of run and behaviour and especially in the strength of the challenges he reminds me of Patrick.

    "After that he is a different type of player."
