    H2 Maths students often make this mistake, practise this question to learn how to use the correct concept correctly.

    Question

    A shopper buys two kinds of vegetables in a shop. The mass of potatoes and the mass of onions are modelled as having independent normal distributions.

    The mass of potatoes has a mean mass of 3 kg and a standard deviation of 0.2kg. The mass of onions has a mean mass of 1 kg and a standard deviation of 0.05kg.

    The price of potatoes is 50 cents per kilogramme and the price of onions is $1.20 per kilogramme.

    Find the probability that the total cost of the vegetables lies between $2.50 and $2.80.

    (Hint : X1+X2 +X3 = T BUT T IS NOT THE SAME AS 3X).
