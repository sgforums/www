    No offense but reading some posts in this thread, it's no surprise taxi companies like CDG can more or less do as they please, be it how they treat drivers who may owe them rental or made some mistakes, or how they can send COE prices rocketting with newer vehicles, set new fares etc.

    Whatever you think or consider them, there's no doubt they are still tops in marketting themselves and creating a positive image to the public and unsuspecting new drivers.

    I'm saying everything they do is wrong or has an ulterior motive, they definitely have some plus points. But as long as they can continue to lure newbies to into hirers, they are in a Comfort zone.

    I'm saying that all newbies should be hirers, but like in any job, you'd normally expect to start slow or from an easier learning point and gain knowledge along the way. You won't expect joining a new firm to be put directly incharge of running the show when you've got no prior experience.

    Anyway that's my view, and to each his own I suppose.
