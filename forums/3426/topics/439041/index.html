    Published on Oct 3, 2011 http://www.straitstimes.com/STI/STIMEDIA/image/20111003/CNG.zainaljamari.jpg The green vehicle rebate scheme for new vehicles that run on compressed natural gas (CNG) will be extended by another year to Dec 31, 2012. -- PHOTO: ZAINAL JAMARI The green vehicle rebate scheme for new vehicles that run on compressed natural gas (CNG) will be extended by another year to Dec 31, 2012. The scheme, which accords buyers of CNG cars and commercial vehicles a 40 and 5 per cent rebate respectively on their additional registration fees, was to end by Dec 31 this year. The Ministry of Finance said on Monday that it decided to extend the scheme by another year after feedback from the public requesting for it to expire together with a similar scheme for other green vehicles, namely electric and petrol-electric hybrid models. The green vehicle rebate scheme was first introduced in January 2001 for electric and hybrid cars, as such cars were deemed environmentally friendlier. As such vehicles were substantially costlier than conventional models, the scheme sought to narrow the price difference to encourage their usage.
