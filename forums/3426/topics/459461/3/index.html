    The TD will alway be in the lower scheme of paid structure against inflation, why? because the increases in rental and fuel will hamper TDs income.

    For last few years, the average inflation at 5.5% is making TD cost of living even harder, so for a real wage increase, which many companies had increase 7 to 10% this years is to first, curb inflation and at the same time increase paid, so given an 8%, which I get increased even as a contractor, you take off 5.5% inflation, our real increment is only 2.5%, this is call real wage increment, if a company increase 3%, it is not an increment, it is actually a lost in term of inflation.

    So for TD to really overcome the inflation, they need to earn at least a 5.5% more per day of their usual income, and that is only the breakeven, they need to do more adding another 3 to 5% more income per day of their normal, totally about 8 to 10% more of the usual daily intake inorder to sustain a real income increase.
