  • JoeRaj
    31 Jan 12, 21:18

    Just my 2 cents worth. I was a driver in the 90s and now again for the past 2 years.

    My first advice to you: Don't be fooled by the sales pitch made the various training schools or road-shows. All taxi companies earn their bulk from renting out their taxis.

    Their primary aim to get ppl to lease them, that's why they 'offer carrots' especially to newbies, who don't know much about or anything about their structure and system and also have this new zeal to drive, so they are always targetted first.

    Ask yourself why are the companies so keen to offer carrots of up to 2k a year? Why not offer them to their existing hirers? Ask yourself, is 2k really that big amount? Can you recoup that 2k (in a year) if you don't take up their offer? The answer is invariably yes.

    2nd advice therefore is learn to walk before running. OMO gives you freedom and the keys, but it's a burden around your neck. What happens if you fall sick? What happens if something came up and you can drive for a few days or more? You'll find that in the end the companies main goal is collect rent, if you are arrears, vehicle will be repossessed and for companies like CDG, there will be a blackmark and you can never be a hirer again.

    You have up to 6 months maybe a year where the carrots are offered, so why the rush? A better solution is find someone in your class or a friend/relative and then hire a taxi. 1 of you take the carrots and share between both. Both can drive, both can share benefits, yet neither will be burdened as much as an OMO.

    Lastly, TD is far from what you've experienced in working life. I thought no problem, I was cop, drive patrol car 8-10 hours all shifts, how different can TDriving be?

    First be prepared for some back pain, sitting long hours and driving is no joke, even today after many years, my back sometimes hurts and I feel lethargic despit more than enough sleep.

    But you're young I suppose and can overcome this, but still there will be enormous pressure, to concentrate on the road, find pax etc etc. It won't be easy but not impossible either.

    I forget to add, ditch the crown and go for an automatic. I drove in both eras, auto and manual, but 8 hrs or more of manual is no joke, especialy for people with little experience of driving them. Pay a little more and go for auto, why wear your knees out as well? How many ppl on the road own manual cars these days? Why do you want to be in the minority? There must be something alluring to drive auto than manual right?

    To end, you don't have to follow all the advise given here, choose the ones you think suit you best. Don't be discouraged and yet don't take it as a challenge that you must prove some of us wrong in our advise. Advise is just that, advise. Not an order, so be yourself, yet consider what advise suits you or what that can suit you with some changes to suit your style, or marry some advise given into something you prefer.

    Good luck
