  • bowah
    1 Aug 12, 13:52

    Learning of routes is understandably not simple for us, our brain memory is not so good as scholars mah, if our coconut head is good, still driving taxis meh??

    But then, as slow learner, we still can learn slowly. Most important are the bigger routes.

    Start with Expressways, study each closely, each exit lead to where and how you can use it to your advantage. This is the most critical of it, like barltey east out from KPE, what from south, how you use it, from north, where can it lead you to, spent some time per day to study each, and you shall be able to master it. Beside some changes of roads, roads are dead, you are alive, it up to you to decide turn left or right.

    GPS is a good tool, but overly rely on it, get you not far, before long, it become a must for you, without it, you cannot operate and lack confident,

    Unless you really at lost, then use your GPS, once you know the route to a particular place, once you got a chance, try to navigate that place again without GPS , in this way, you learn faster and more confident. In another word, GPS is spoonfeeding, once spoon removed, you cannot get feeding, we do not like to see this happen.

    Once you know route well, you definitely are more confident and relaxing, instead of tense and more stress, when free at home, try to question yourself, from Bukit Merah hoyfatt road to Raffles hotel, which way, do it in your brain, then from Raffles to CCK, which way, this are call mind routing, you think in silence, just like you are driving in the cab thinking in silence the option of the road, the best is to discuss with pax, he or she may give you the best road
