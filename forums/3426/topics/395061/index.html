    I'm not very sure, but from Queen Street (that's near Bugis) to JB, it's $40 for each taxi to take off. You can go alone ($40) or split it among other passengers, which would be $10 for each person. As long as the taximan is paid $40 for the trip, he would take you along. We usually wait for other passengers to come along, so we pay $10 per person. If you think that $20 from Changi to Bugis is worth it, then $60 would be a fair price to pay. If not, you're better off taking the MRT from Changi airport to Bugis MRT and walking a bit to the Queen Street station to grab a cab. If you have luggage (and a lot of it), it's better to go with 2 or 1 more person (but you have to make your own arrangement with them, like you pay $30 and they pay $10). If you're willing to cram yourself up with more people, go ahead. Your bigger luggage would be in the boot anyway.

     

    Fetching people back from JB is different. The price is RM15 per person from the Kotaraya II terminal, or, RM60 per vehicle. That ends at Queen street, though. If you need to cab back to Changi airport, you can easily find a taxi waiting along or you can take the MRT.

     

    Hope this was useful!

     

    (I take the JB-SG taxi service quite often)

     

    Edit: Oh, I realised you're a taximan. Hmm. In this case, I think you shouldn't bother because you're better off in SG. The taxis we take are usually Malaysian yellow cabs, and I reckon the operational costs are way cheaper compared to the OC of SG taxis. You have to take into account the toll charges, the jam and the time taken. A typical, non-jammed, non-peak hour journey to JB takes about 40 - 45 minutes from SG. With the jam, you can double that amount. Bear in mind that with the non-peak hours, you also need to wait a while for passengers to arrive...
