    A train trip starting at Simei and ending at Bishan would take 46 minutes today. But soon, it will take you only about half an hour for the same trip.

    From April 17, the second phase of the Circle Line (CCL) from Bartley to Dhoby Ghaut will open to commuters. With the opening of the CCL, commuters can expect to save more time travelling.

    According to figures released by the Land Transport Authority, the amount of time saved can go up to 52 per cent (for a bus journey from Serangoon to Paya Lebar).

     

    The 11-kilometre CCL stretch comprises of 11 stations - Tai Seng, MacPherson, Paya Lebar, Dakota, Mountbatten, Stadium, Nicoll Highway, Promenade, Esplanade, Bras Basah and Dhoby Ghaut.

    Various entertainment and recreational facilities will also be made more accessible with the opening of the CCL. For example, arts lovers would be able to alight at Bras Basah Station to visit the Singapore Art Museum

    The new stations will start operations almost a year after the five CCL stations from Marymount to Bartley opened to the public last May.

    An LTA spokesman said that about 200,000 people are expected to use the 16 stations everyday, from Dhoby Ghaut to Marymount.

    The new MRT line is also expected to bring more crowds to the businesses in the surrounding areas.
