    Soba noodle with duck

    January 9, 2009 by Kokotaru   
    Category Japanese

    Comment

     

    Kamo nanba soba (é´¨ã€€ã�ªã‚“ã�°ã€€ã��ã�°ï¼‰is the Japanese name for this noodle with duck. The noodle is very simple to make. Just like other noodle soups cooked in Japanese style, the taste is very mild, lightly sweet, lightly salty. You can feel the fragrance of the broth, which is the mixture of dashi stock and Japanese soy sauce.

    I used dashi granules, made in Japan brand, and mix them with water proportionally according to the package. It is more convenient to use dashi granules. In fact, in Singapore, it is not easy to get the konbu and katsuoboshi to make the dashi at home. Also, within my limited budget, it is more economic to buy and use the dashi granules. If my financial could be better, of course I would go for the self-made "fresh" dashi rather than using the granules.

    With this noodle soup, either the normal soba or the green tea soba (Cha soba) is fine. This time I used Cha soba.

     

     

    Ingredients (serves 4)

    - 300g duck breast fillets

    - Scalion (or spring onion), as needed

    - 340g dried soba noodles

    - ground pepper

    * Broth:

    - 1.5 lít dashi stock

    - 2 tbs dark soy sauce

    - 1 tbs light soy sauce

    - 1 tsp sugar

    - 1/4 tsp salt

    Cooking:

    - Trim the fat and rinse the duck breast well, reserve the fat, cut the meat into thin slices.

    - Chop the scaliion into 4cm length.

    - Heat the duck fat in a frying pan, add the scallions and sauté lightly. Set aside.

    - Combine the broth ingredients in a saucepan and bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 10 minutes. Add the sliced duck and continue cooking until heated through. (1 minutes) Set aside.

    - Cook the soba until al dente following the direction on package. Divide the noodles among 4 serving bowls. Top the noodles with duck and scallions. Pour over with the broth and sprinkle with ground pepper as desired.
