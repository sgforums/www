    Traditional dragon boat race begins in Okinawa
    NHK -- May 04

     

    An annual dragon boat festival has gotten underway in Japan's southernmost prefecture of Okinawa.

     

    The "Hahrih" boat race is a traditional festival that originated in China. It is held to pray for good fishing and safety at sea.

    The 40th Naha Hahrih race started on Saturday in calm waters at Naha Port.

    Rowing teams from local junior high schools took part in the morning rounds. Each race involves 3 teams consisting of 32 rowers each in "Haryu-sen" dragon-shaped boats.

    The teams pulled hard on the oars to a cadence set by a bell-ringer on each boat.
