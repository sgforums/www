    Ramen is a very popular food in Japan.
    There are many kinds of ramen in Japan which differ depending on the area.
    Here in Wakayama, "Wakayama Ramen" is called "Chuka-soba"by the locals.
    There are 2 main kinds of soup commonly served in Wakayama.
    ã€�1ã€‘Shoyu (Soysauce) 
    Shoyu style ramen originaly began to be eaten in Wakayama around the old streetcar stations that were located in central Wakayama.
    The soup is dark brown but tastes light and simple.
    This flavor is the predominant flavor of Wakayama Chuka-soba.
    ã€�2ã€‘Tonkotsu Shoyu (Pork bone and Soy broth) . Tonkotsu Shoyu style soup has a rich and smooth taste, intertwined with the flavor of pork and soysauce.
    Wakayama is well known throughout Japan for its speciality, Tonkotsu Shoyu Ramen.
    Both kinds of ramen have thin and straight noodles.
    Usual toppings include : pickled bamboo shoots (Menma), green onions,fish cake (Kamaboko), hard-boiled eggs (Nitamago) and slices of roasted pork (Chashu).
    Recently, In addition to these two main flavors, one can now find many other unique and unusual kinds of Ramen here in Wakayama.
    Each restaurant specializes in a particular kind of ramen, so give it a try !

    ITINERARY
     
    Day 1 Singapore âœˆ KanSai (Osaka)
    Check in at Singapore Changi Airport for your flight to Osaka
     
    Day 2 Wakayama Castle/ Mt Koyasan / Okunoin / Kongobuji / Minabe
    Wakayama Castle: Built by Toyotomi Hideyoshi Shogun in 1585.
    Mt Koyasan: A mountain earned a reputation as a cool retreat from summer heat, with a lot of temples & National monument, (also success to registered as World Heritage) here.
    Okunoin:  This is the heart of Koyasan, with its many famous gravesites. Visitors can see wooden talismans specially placed there by the devout.
    Kongobuji:  Where visitors can see religious artifacts, shoji screen paintings, as well as a beautiful rock garden.
     
    Day 3 Engetsujima / Senjo-jiki / Sandan-peki / Kushimoto marine park / Taiji Whale museum
    Engetsujima: An uninhabited rock isle with round sea caves opened in by ocean wave.
    Senjo-jiki: A huge multi-tiered rock tat thousand “tatami” mattress floor created by the erosion of sofa sandstone by raging waves.
    Sandan-Peki:  Three-tiered wall within 50-60 meter high precipilous rock walls extend kilometers from south to north, will take a lift down to the bottom of the rock & enter into the natural sea-cave tunnel. Kushimoto Marine Park: Here you can experience the 6.3m depth undersea world of Kushimoto.
    Taiji Whale Museum: The biggest in Japan, with thousands of information exhibiting the whales lifestyle and the hunting of whale within 400 years history in Japan.
     
    Day 4 Nachi Taisaya / Nachi Waterfall / Pearl island / Futamigaura
    Nachi Taisaya:  The most famous shrine in the region visit by many pilgrims, which has been registered as one of the “World Heritage“ by 01 July 2004.
    Nachi Water Fall: The most common place visited by tourist with a tallest waterfall at height of 133 meters.
    Pearl Island:  Where you can see how to raising and breeding the culture pearl by the famous Mikomoto Pearl Co.
    Futamigaura:  Also called “Meotoiwa” or Wedded Rock, considered to be male and female represent the primal couple. They are also deemed husband & wife and the rocks were joined in matrimony by sacred ropes called “Shimenawa”.
     
    Day 5 Ise Shrine / Okage yokocho / Iga ninja mansion / lake biwa / Kyoto
    Ise Shrine: Built in 690A.D., consists of two separate shrine, which dedicated to the sun goddess, the mythical ancestor of the Japanese imperial line while the outer shrine is a god of the harvest.
    Okage Yokocho: A re-created Meiji Era village with teahouse, restaurants, and shops selling Japanese candies, traditional toys and folk crafts.
    Iga Ninja Mansion:  See how Ninja disappear suddenly, run on the water and climb like a spider etc.......
    Lake Biwa:  The biggest fresh water lake in Japan
    Kyoto:  The ancient capital of Japan for more than 1000 years. Place that you found traditional Japan culture.
     
    Day 6 Fushimi taisha Shrine / Arashiyama train / Japanese tea Ceremony / Osaka underground street / ShinSaibaShi / Dotonbori
    Fushimi Taisha: A shrine of the Japanese “Shindo” pray for the God of Harvest and the  speciality of this shrine was stand with thousand of “Torii” lead up to the hill which s a red color gate always located at the main entrance of the shrine.
    Arashiyama Train: we will take the antique ride along the best scenic spot of Kyoto Arashiyama.
    Japanese Tea Ceremony: Experience and try the traditional Japanese tea on the spot. Osaka Underground Street: Biggest underground shopping area in Osaka selling latest fashions.
    Shinsaibashi: Biggest modern shopping street in Osaka
    Dotonbori: A street of gourmet where you can try the famous “Kinryu Ramen”, “Takoyaki’ & “Okonomiyaki” both are the most famous and popular cuisine in Osaka.
     
    Day 7 Osaka -> Singapore
    Free at leisure until transfer to airport for your flight back to Singapore. Have a nice trip.
