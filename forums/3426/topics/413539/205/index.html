    The Chrysanthemum Festival - a traditional autumn event in Kagoshima

    A wide array of flower sculptures and arrangements, including award-winning works of the flower competition held by the Kagoshima Prefecture Chrysanthemum Association, chrysanthemum towers, figures and floats are being displayed in the garden of Sengan-en.

     

    Event Period       : 1 Nov (Thu) ~ 25 Nov (Sun)

    Venue                  : Sengan-en

    Opening Hours    : Opens everyday 08:30~17:20 (from 1 Nov ~ 15 Mar)

    08:30~17:30 (besides the above-stated period)

    Ohara Matsuri - A Dance Festival Attended by 20,000

     

     

    Ohara Matsuri, a dance festival held at Tenmonkan in downtown Kagoshima, is attended by about 20,000 people. It is also the largest festival of its kind in southern Kyushu. Dancing in unison to traditional music such as the Ohara-bushi (a popular tune of Kagoshima) and the likes, participants come dressed in traditional Yukata (summer style Kimono), Happi (festive overcoat), and some even wear home-made costumes.

     

    "Hana Densha (Flower Trams)", which are essentially street trams decorated with colourful festive lights, are parked in the dance area to brighten up the festive mood. Spontaneous participation is also greatly encouraged! So what are you waiting for? Come join in the Ohara Matsuri!

    Date:

    Pre-Festival        2 Nov (Fri); 18:45~21:00

    Main Festival       3 Nov (Sat/Culture Day); 10:20~17:00

    Venue:

    Along the tram line in Tenmonkan, Kagoshima City

    Getting there:

    15 minute walk (towards Tenmonkan) from JR Kagoshima Chuo Station / Take the city tram and alight at Takamibaba station

    The end of Edo period (19 C.), with the appearance of the ships from the great western powers in Japanese waters, Japan faced an external threat. SHIMADZU Nariakira, the lord of the Satsuma clan promoted modernization and industrialization projects on the larger scale. He constructed an industrial complex in Iso on the outskirts of Kagoshima, and named it “Shuseikan”. After his death, the machinery factory was constructed by the people who succeeded his dying wishes in 1865. At now, it becomes the main building of historical museum”Shoko-Shuseikan”, and is specified for the important Cultural Property.

     Keihan (chicken rice).  Although we would usually associate the Keihan with the Amami Oshima Island (as the latter is the place of origin), the broth of the Keihan that we will introduce today is actually made from the Ingi chicken of Minamitane Town of Tanegashima Island, So we are actually introducing the Tanegashima Keihan.  The way it is being eaten is similar to that of the Amami Keihan, where chicken broth is added to rice topped with

     chicken, omelette strips, muchrooms, ginger strips, shredded seaweeds and sesame seeds. The uniqueness of the Tanegashima Keihan lies in the Paitan (white coloured) soup, where the umami of the Ingi chicken mixes perfectly with the salt flavour of the soup, bringing out not only the salty taste but also a little sweetness. Of course, the traditional way to eat the Keihan is by topping the rice with all the ingredients (as mentioned above), but do also try the soup by itself.

    Rice paper butterfly

    This is one of the large-size butterfly species in Taiwan . Due to its size, it flies low and slow and is thereby dubbed “Dumb butterfly “in Chinese. It could pretend “dead” in your hands when caught. Interesting indeed! Its larvae or caterpillar feeds on the dogbane family tree (Parsonsia laevigata) and it has a shiny, golden colored pupa or chrysalis.

    Welcome to Toucheng Leisure Farm!
     
     
     
    We are a recreational and educational leisure farm located in the heart of the woods overlooking the Pacific Ocean near Toucheng City in Yilan County at the northeast coast of Taiwan.
    For more than twenty years since the establishment of the farm, we have continuously received and accommodated visitors from Taiwan and abroad and given them a unique opportunity to discover the beauty of Taiwan’s untouched nature, experience the traditional farm life and learn about environmental sustainability as well as more ecological ways of living to lead a healthier life.

     
    Lying in the wooded mountains of Yilan, Toucheng Fam (indicated in the map by a red square) can easily be reached by train within ninety minutes from Taipei or, since the completion of Hsuehshan Tunnel within an hour by car. Visitors arriving in Taiwan at Taoyuan Interational Airport (indicated by a blue circle), can transfer to Taipei by bus (ca. 50 minutes) or ask us to organise a pick up service.
    On more than 100 hectares of land passed by three streams of crystal-clear water, we cultivate all kinds of vegetables, fruits and more for visitors to eat and pick at will, and keep various animals to observe, feed and touch.
    The farm’s grounds further house a winery producing excellent spirits from grapes, kumquats as well as plums and running an exquisite restaurant with an impressive view on the farm and the endless green mountains.
     
     
    Always adhering to our principals of sustainability, preservation and love of nature, we also offer a wide range of leisure activities such as traditional rice planting, bamboo crafting using bamboo grown on our farm’s grounds, kilning and baking, river trekking, T-shirt printing and many more.
     
     
     
    Every year, we accommodate more than 60,000 guests in three apartment houses each different in arrangement and style. We offer various combinations of activities suited for all age groups and according to the length of stay, whether it be just a day or even a whole week.
     
    Furthermore, we offer special programmes for group travels, such as graduation trips, summer camps, or educational school trips. Large groups cannot only choose from our wide range of activities and arrange their schedule based on their liking, but supported by us can also develop ideas of their own and implement them into their plans – only imagination limits possibilities!
     
     
    But the farm is also an ideal spot to stay in while exploring the various scenic spots in all of northeastern Taiwan and our staff will gladly introduce the sights surrounding Toucheng Farm and help guests by organising one-day trips from the farm.
     
    By our continuous efforts to let visitors of our farm experience the traditional farm lifestyle, grant them the opportunity to touch and be touched by nature and reestablish the contact with wildlife that we think has long been neglected in our modern world, we aim to foster a culture of environmental understanding and awareness.
    We invite you to enjoy the homeliness of life on our farm and encounter nature in a fun way so that our environment be conceived as an exciting and enriching wellspring of energy.
     
     
    Be it by your active learning about or environment or simply leisurely relaxing and forgetting about the troubles of everyday life close to nature – we would like to welcome you to enjoy staying close to our great nature and encourage you to carry the desire to preserve this great gift bestowed on us beyond the borders of our farm. Our commitment is to the preservation of nature we strive to become a mirror of this very principle.
