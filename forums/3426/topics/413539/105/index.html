    Sea Cucumber/Common Sea Squirt: Hoya/Mahoya

    The Common Sea Squirt, very often called Sea Cucumber is neither a coral, seaweed, shellfish or whatever.
    It is an animal of its own class.

    In its natural habitat, already a prey to many marine predators, it has become rare because of the extensive catch by humans.

    Its natural colour is whitish out of the water while (see pic above) Hoya rasied by humans are of a deep orange colour.

    We are just in the middle of its season, May.
    They are mainly raised in Miyagi Prefecture while natural ones are caught in Iwate Prefecture.

    You have to cut it open to reach its edible part.

    It can be eaten raw, slightly boiled or pickled.
    It is said to be the rare sea animal combining the four tastes: sweetness, saltiness, sourness and acidity.

    For a better view of its insides.
    It is called sea squirt, because it is mainly filled with sea water which can be expelled at will.

    I personally appreciate it as sushi nigiri, although it is a bit of an acquired taste!

    Birds of Japan

    Okinawa Rail
    This flightless bird is endemic and restricted to the northern subtropical forest of Okinawa island, the forest of Yanbaru.
    Steller's Sea Eagle
    Massive sea eagle with bright yellow large bill, breeds from Kamchatka and shores of Sea of Okhotsk and winters in northern Japan. Large flocks can be seen in eatern Hokkaido in winter.
    Red-crowned Crane
    Large white crane with red crown and black forehead, neck. and tertials. Locally common resident in eastern Hokkaido but very rare elsewhere.
    Bonin White-eye
    The Bonin White-eye is a small yellow and grey bird endemic to the Hahajima of Ogasawara Islands. 
    Japanese Wood Pigeon
    The photo is the subspecies C. j. nitens (which used to occur on the Ogasawara and Iwo Islands) is almost certainly extinct.
    Japanese Woodpecker
    This medium-sized woodpecker is endemic to Japan. Inhabits evergreen forest and desiduous forests. It is also well adapted in urban areas, sometimes seen in small parks in Tokyo.
    Japanese Pigmy Woodpecker
    Small-sized woodpecker restricted to East Asia. Well adapted in urban areas, oftern found in small parks of Tokyo.
    Mountain Hawk Eagle
    Large uncommon eagle, resident in deep mountain forests
    Northern Goshawk
    Northern Goshawk, beautiful hawk have very wide range, nothern Eurasia and America.
    Japanese Wagtail
    Very common but endemic to Japan. It is similar to the white wagtail but face is black.
    White Wagtail
    Most common wagtail widespread throughout Eurasia.
    Green Pheasant
    It is the National Bird of Japan and also endemic.Common residents from Honshu to Yakushima, often found in cultivated fields and river banks.
    Copper Pheasant
    Reddish brown pheasant. Male has very long tail. Endemic to Japan and inhabits in mountain forests.
    Grey-faced Buzzard
    Summer migrants hawk. Main diet is frogs. Number is declining because of change of agriculture, however we still can observe thousands of grey-faced buzzards migrating to south in some place of Japan.
    Common Buzzard
    A Common Buzard caught a frog in February. Widely distibuted across template Eurasia including Japan.
    Japanese Night Heron
    This small heron has reddish-brown upperparts, dark crown and chesnut head. Breeds only in Japan and winters in Phillippines and southern China.
    Malayan Night Heron
    A male Malayan Night Heron menaces other male in breeding season. Distribute widely throuout southeast Asia but only in Yaeyama islands in Japan.
    Oriental Stork
    Large waterbird once common but become extinct in 1970s. in wild. Few birds come to Japan in winter.
    Japanese Accentor
    Japan endemic dark coloured songbird. Inhabits subalpine areas. Easier to observe in winter when they come down and stays in bushy grasslands.
    Japanese Robin
    Breed in Japan and south Sakhalin. Male has a bright dark-orange face and breast. 
    Japanese Yellow Bunting
    Breeds only in Japan and winters in south China, Taiwan and Philipines. Uncommon summer visitor.
    Japanese White-eye
    Flowers of Japanese Apricot tree attract Japanese White-eye, a common nectar feeder. 
    Japanese Grosbeak
    Large finch with very large bill. Common resident in Japan.
    Hawfinch
    Medium-large finch that breeds in northern Eurasia from Europe to northern Japan. Common winter visitor to open forests.
    Ruddy Kingfisher
    Reddish Kingfisher, uncommon summer visitor northern Japan and fairy common residents in southern island. The races of southern islands have purplish-red back.
    Eurasian Coot
    Widespread in Eurasia even from Europe east to India, northern china and Japan. Also to north Africa and Austraria. White bill and black body.
    Common Moorhen
    Widespread in temprate and tropical Eurasia. Has striking red frontal shield and yellow tipped red bill.
    Bull-Headed Shrike
    The most common shrike often seen in cultivated field in Japan.
    Great Tit
    The Great Tit caught an insect in the snow. Great because of its abundance throughout North Eurasia. A black tie like band on the berry is the characteristic of the Tit.
    Daurian Redstart
    A common winter bird found in cultivated fields and house yard.
    Meadow Bunting
    Very common bird. You'll see the bird almost always if you go to grasslands in Japan. 
    Saunders's Gull
    Small gull with black head and white eye-ring. The total population of the gull is said to be small. Some winters in Japan. 
    Black Headed Gull
    Medium-small sized gull often seen at inland water of Tokyo in winter. Usually the gull's head in Japan is white because the birds has black head only in breeding season. 
    White-throated Needletail
    Large dark swift with needle-like tail tips. However, the needles is not visible in the field because they fly so fast.

    Black Faced Bunting
    There are two races of Black Faced Bunting in Japan. This photo is personata, has dark greenish head. Breeds in maouontain area of Honshu and winters in warmer areas. Common in city parks in winter. 
    Blue Rock Thrush
    The Blue Rock Thrush breeds widely in southern Europe and northwest Africa, and from central Asia to northern China and Malaysia including Japan. 
    Dusky Thrush
    Abundant winter visitor to cultivated fields and open forest. 
    Alpine Accentor
    The Alpine Accentor is a small passerine bird found throughout the mountains of southern temperate Europe and Asia at heights above 2000m. It can be seen often hiking in Japan Alps. It winters more widely at lower latitudes 
    Grey Heron
    Grey large heron distribute to many counties. 
    Large-billed Crow
    One of the two common crow inhabits in Japan, characterized by thick black bill with down curved mandible and peaked forehead. 
    Carrion Crow
    One of the two common crow inhabits in Japan, slightly smaller than the other with rounder head. 
    Brown Booby
    he Brown Booby is a large seabird of the booby family, Sulidae. Their heads and backs are black, and their bellies are white. 
    Mandarin Duck
    Very beautiful duck breeds only in Korea, Taiwan, sourthern china and Japan. Found in mountain streams. 
    Baikal Teal
    Breeds only in north-east asia. Winters in south-west Asia. Uncommon winter visitor in Japan. 
    Common Teal
    Male has dark chestnut head with grassy-green patch on eyes. Breed across north asia and common winter visitor to freshwater lakes and ponds in Japan. 
    Common Pochard
    Medium sized diving duck with males having reddish head. Common winter visitor in Japan. 
    Greater Scaup
    Medium sized diving duck. Common winter visitor in Japan. Often form a large flock. 
    Tufted Duck
    Medium sized diving duck with males having black head with long crest and white flanks. Common winter visitor in Japan. 
    Spot-billed Duck
    Breeds in Japan and temprate Asia. Abundunt on freshwater lakes. Have dark bill with yellow tip. 
    Northern Pintail
    Males have long tail and grey body. Common winter visitor to lakesand coastal shallow waters. 
    Northern Shoveler
    Medium-sized duck with spatulate duck. Common winter visitor to lakesand coastal shallow waters. 
    Eurasian Wigeon
    Male has reddish-brown head with golden-brown forehead. Common winter visitor to lakes and shallow coastal waters. 
    Little Grebe
    Small grebe, common resident often found in ponds and lakes. Widespread in tropical and temprate Eurasia. 
    Great Crested Grebe
    Large grebe with black crown and white face. Common winter visitor to coastal waters in Japan. Small number breed in northen Japan. 
    Black-necked Grebe
    Small grebe, common winter visitor to coastal waters. 
    Northern Lapwing
    Large plover with striking color pattern and thin crest. Breed across temprate Eurasia, common winter visitor to sothern Korea and Japan. 
    Eurasian Oystercatcher
    Black and white wade and orange-red bill. Uncommon winter visitor but number has increased in recent years in Japan. 
    Black-winged Stilt
    Has very long pink leg. Widely distributed through Eurasia, Africa and Austraria. 
    Oriental Turtle Dove
    The most common dove, resident in wooded areas, parkes and gardens. 
    Pallid Harrier
    Normally, distribute in Europe and winters in Africa. This juvenile male Pallid Harrier was recorded for first time in Japan in year 2009.
