    SWEET POTATO TYPES

     

    Today, there are upwards of 40 potato types being used for making Shochu.  Similar to grapes in wine, breweries strive to highlight the best attributes of a specific sweet potato type, to be delivered with each sip.  Breweries distinguish themselves from others by establishing a hold as specialists with certain ingredient type or production method.  The three most popular sweet potatoes specifically used for Shochu production include the following top three.

     

    koganesengan

    This variety was born in 1966 in Kyushu, and is the most popular sweet potato in Kagoshima Prefecture.  It’s a very starch-rich crop, very sweet, and is delicious to eat.  Shochu made with refined Koganesengan has the fragrance of chestnuts.

     

    joywhite

    JoyWhite was the first Japanese sweet potato specifically created for Shochu production.  Introduced in Kyushu in 1994, the name appropriately reflects its light skin and white pulp nature.  The unique, not-too-excessive starch quality produces elegant-smooth finish and a fruity-citrus aroma.

     

    beniazuma

    Beniazuma is an early creation, born in 1984 in Ibaragi Prefecture located in central Honshu.  It’s a strong plant, fast-growing with an abundant yield.  When baked, it becomes a sweet and delicious treat.  Beniazuma produces a rich and sweet Shochu, characterized by a silky sweetness that expands.

    Located southernmost in Kyushu island, the Osumi Peninsula faces the Pacific Ocean to the east and the Kagoshima Bay and Kagoshima Peninsula to the west. It is connected to the active volcano, Sakurajima, which sits in the middle of the Kagoshima Bay, sporadically putting on volcanic spectacles .

    Something like two thirds of the peninsula are forestland, luring outdoor activists and nature-lovers alike.

    Those whose interests in nature are directed heavenward may want to consider visiting "Kihoku Tenkyukan Astronomical Museum" in the northwestern part of the peninsula. The location was selected by Environment Agency (now Ministry of the Environment) as the best star-watching site in Japan for four consecutive years (1991-1994).

    A couple of dozens of kilometers south from the neck of the peninsula is the elongated artificial lake, Osumiko. The dam lake has a circumference of 10 kilometers, and is a favored site for viewing cherry blossom in spring, purplish hydrangea flowers in early summer, and crimson foliage in autumn.

    Further south still, around the center of the peninsula, is "Osumi Koiki Park,"an open-air recreational facility with an auto-camping site and a go-kart racing track.

    A dozen or so kilometers north west from the park is "Kanoya Naval Air Base Museum," standing right next to the Self Defense Forces air base. Dispalys are mostly centered around kamikaze special attack corps. A replica of Zero fighter airplane, put together from two downed planes, is also on display.
