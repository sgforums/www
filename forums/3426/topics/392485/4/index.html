    Hallo my dear cabby uncle friends, how are you all? From the postings here seems that all are doing well. J

    I am back online and will try to pick up from where I left and continue sharing my experiences with you all. Sorry if some are a bit outdated or irrelevant now. Actually looking back at  it, I am amazed at how different a person I am now compared to just over one month ago. Not just different at the way I ply the trade, but also at how I look at life. Driving taxi sure is an eye opener to me, almost like seeing a parallel second universe that I did not know before.

    Anyway, before I bore you all more to death, as they all say, let’s the story continue……..

     

     

     

    Wednesday, 3 March

     

    Monday was not good day for me.

    I saw Poolie face that he did not believe me when I told him that. My “cousin” Kenny even nearly choked on his kopi when I told him, he said he was so busy until no time to break, not even have time to buy cigarettes

    I started at my bro’s fave place (and by now my usual starting point) near my house at AMK. Not many taxi but not many pax either. After abt10mins waiting an aunty carrying those plastic bags get in “Ah Di, Woodlands Dr 40 please”.  Later on she told me she only take cab that day because it was going to rain.

     

    Reached Wld, drive here and there difficult to get pax, until got a teenage malay couple flag me “Uncle, Bukit Batok please”, ouch another one of my “favourite” place.

    Bukit batok even worse, drive around for good 15 minutes cannot get pax, until got 2 malay women “Unlce two places. Woodlands and Yishun”.  Phui, if this goes on when am I going to get to CBD?? Damn tulan, until I called my wife and asked whether got any extra dinner portion for me. So go back first and have dinner with my family (a nice change though J ).

     

    After dinner and played with my kids for a while then start driving towards town again. Near Novena got a PRC flag my cab “Ma Li Si Tar”, wahlau eh this continues for the whole night. Even when I decided to heck care and just drive to CBD, from LP the girl throw me Hougang. On second thought, maybe I should have driven further and join the queue at 1RQ rather than LP, and maybe it is better. But it was 8+ nearly 9pm and I was afraid the office crowd have all already gone home by then.

     

    Damn suay lah that day. Monday, drive 5.30pm – 2.00am, collected only $180+ and I drove 267km. Bad bad bad day, my worst in this 2 weeks driving.

     

    Even yesterday (Tuesday), starting late at 6.00 and sat around for 2.5hrs chit chat with the cabby “uncles” I can still collect $200+.

    +++++++++++++================+++++++++++++++++

     

    Continuing from the above story, next is about my biggest tip so far.

    It was after midnight on a weekday when I took a 50+ y.o. uncle from RWS to Hougang (Avenue 8 block 600+). During the trip I asked whether it was his first time there, he just laughed at me and said it was his fifth (and this is when the casino is opened for just less than two weeks ??!!). He said he still need to work the next morning so he has to go home. He seems to be cheerful so I knew he was winning.

    After further talk he told me first time he lost a few hundred, then second time earn back few hundred, third time win one thousand, fourth time two thousand, and that morning he won nearly four thousand playing roulette !  WTF, some people got all the luck. At that time I nearly ask him to be my godfather and bring me along next time he go casino, but on second thought I am scared later I got addicted then how??

    Anyway, when reaching destination, meter was $24+. He gave me some $10 notes and said “ You keep, you keep”. At that time I thought he gave me $30, after he got down I counted it was actually $40.

    So this is my largest tip so far. Narrowly beating the previous record of US$10.

    That one was from a late fifty ang moh I picked up from T3. He was a nice guy, chose to sit in the front passenger sit and chit chat with me during the trip. A consultant for the LV IR coming down here for biz trip.

    When reaching the hotel at the Marina area he whipped out his corporate credit card and then also said “If I give you ten bucks will it be any good for you?” Of course it is!. Any kind of appreciation of our hard work is most welcome J Thank you sir!

     

    Musing : Is it such coincidence that the bigger tips I got are casino related? Is this a trend to come?

    Thursday, 11 March

     

    I picked up a woman pax (around mid forty) from Science Park 1(along the Sci Pk Dr) wanting to go to Singapore Casket (Lavender) at around 20.30pm on a weekday.

    Me : “No problem. Let’s go through AYE then CTE then through Balestier”

    Pax : “ Why are you taking a big round to go there? The last time the driver go through Holand Road, Orchard, then Newton there”.

    In my heart I thought “WTF?!?!  Going that way will take through at least one hundred and twelve traffic lights!!”  But what came out from my mouth was “Sure can…. No problem”. Damn. I am such a hypocrite (correct spelling??) bastard. J

     

    To be frank at that time (and even up to now) when a pax tell me a destination, I could only connect from pick up point to destination, and if there are few options can only agar which one is the best way to choose. I still don’t know exactly which way is shortest  (see further comment  below)

     

    Moving along Buona Vista she blurted out “ Maybe we can go down Portsdown….”  WTF?!? Just now did not want to go AYE now want to go Portsdown. But I just pretend I didn’t hear that and continue.

     

    Then this is the best part. When reaching Holland Rd, she said “Do you know. If we go down Clementi Rd and then Bukit Timah there (she meant Dunearn) will it be faster?” I nearly say “You want to go through KJE Mam?” but managed to say “Don’t think so lah. I think last time the taxi driver uncle got the BEST way”. 

     

    You know how sometimes we get a little bit of delay at the traffic light in front of the Shaw House? We wouldn’t even call it a jam, but from the backseat suddenlay came “Uncle, just now if we went straight to Grange Rd and come down here maybe it is faster?” Wahlau, honestly at that point of time I nearly say ”Mam, shall we stop the meter here and then I let you drive my cab to Lavender?”. But of course, all drivers in Poolie group here are courteous drivers one and will never loose temper like that.

     

    Coming to Newton Rd she said again “Do you know how to go to Balestier from here? I can’t remember how the driver go the other day” Of course lah. It is just straight and you get there what. I was thinking, maybe the uncle driver brought her through Thomson Rd and then the brighter side of Balestier before continue down the dull side of Balestier and Lavender??

     

    Before reaching the traffic light in front of Singapore Casket (I was in the right lane, mind you) she told me to just stop before the red light. I wonder why is that woman so control freak and stingy??? 

     

     

    So guys, remember. If you happen to pick up a bespectacled, straight short haired, mid forty 75%  woman pax from along Science Park Drive, you have been warned. This woman had been trained by the best uncle driver in Singapore !

    I think there are five levels of drivers, based on the route knowledge :

    -          newbie – when told a destination will go “Ah? Where is that place ah?”. Really no clue, and I have to admit sometimes I still fall into this L

    -          beginners – know the destination and different routes to get there, but have no idea which one is shortest way. This is the level I am now.

    -          intermediate – can tell the shortest way to get to a destination. Sometimes can also give shortcuts.

    -          advanced – not only know the shortest way, but also can tell, depending on certain time of the day, what is the fastest way to get there. I think most of you guys belong to this category.

    -          guru – not only can tell the shortest/ fastest way, but can tell exactly how much is the difference in the meter $$$ by going through those different routes.

         Our Mr. Eating -Walking-Talking GPS Poolman could tell me that day “Wow, going that way at least the meter got $2 more” when I asked about a certain route. But story about this later lah.
