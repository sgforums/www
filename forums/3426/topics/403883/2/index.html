    Bankruptcies are categorized in 2 classes,

    Self-Declared... or kena-ed sult by creditors...

    Self-Declared cases don't get discharged easily... (at least) not until you fulfilled all obligations listed by OA.  ( if you dreamt of getting the easy way out of your financial debts and ENDs all the calls, letters by creditors....).

    Kena-ed suit to bankruptcy -

    After the creditors have tried all means to recover their loans to you, one of the creditors will have to file a court summon and also place a "Deposit" with OA, before the court can filed a bankruptcy suit on you....

    The "good" thing about this class is that you may get a discharge if OA recommends to court (after 5 years) that you have meet all requirements that have set out by the statutory board.

    You are required to pay monthly instalment to OA (Origin Assignee). They will handle all your financial debts.  You also need to apply to OA if you require to travel overseas. 

    Try you best to stay out  (of bankruptcy)

    If a bill have been passed on by the govt, (thread by one of the forumer), there is hope to avoid bankruptcy. 

    Try all other means...talk to your friends... don't get too depressed... It is just a financial mistake.... It is never the end of the world... Life goes on... !!!!

    Good Luck !!!
