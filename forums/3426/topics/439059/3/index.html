    Here is my view on the MDT vs walkie issue:

    It is true that the walkie is very efficient and fast. It allows real time communication between caller and driver. Because it is based on public broadcasting, all taxi drivers who are on the same channel can hear it. This is where you got the biggest disadvantage. There are many taxi drivers who because they are unable to press the PTT button in time, they lost the job to another colleague. So out of frustration, they will mark the successful bidder and complain to the walkie group IC if the successful bidder is late even 1 minute for his job. So you bid 5 minutes and OTS only 6 minutes later. This loser TD will complain and try his best to get you suspended. These people who complain would rather invest their time and energy into 'policing' members who flout the rules than to bid for another job. Some even go so far as to go to the pickup point and see if the successful bidder made it in time.

    MDT is nowhere as efficient as the walkie. It is also not real time. But it has got the big advantage in that only you and the operator knows who got the successful bid. So you dont have to worry about TD politics. In TD trade, there are plenty of politics, especially in walkie groups. If most members like you, it is because you belong to some committee, or you got plenty of durians to share. In the MDT job bidding, if you cannot reach the destination in time, you can always call the pax and inform them. Yes some pax will not be happy. But remember you only got to contend with the pax and the operator. For the walkie bidding, you must contend with fellow TD as well, in addition to the pax and caller. For MDT jobs, as long as you dont 'no show', the operator is quite alright. By the way, I only bid for $45 and $50 durians on the MDT. The MDT strategy is the best when you got these time of durians. No doubt the levy costs $9.63 including GST, but in the end, you are able to grab more durians per day compared to the walkie. For the walkie, to be able to grab even 2 durians everyday requires considerable skill. And because you are that good, there will be plenty of 'sharks' on air observing you and will make a complain for the slightest mistake made to get you suspended.

    For normal cabs, I would say the walkie works rather well. LV is a good choice. Definitely better than the comfort MDT. But for limo groups, it may or may not be the best option. It all depends on your personal chemistry within the group, and also on the type of limousine you are driving.

    Good luck and happy hunting!
