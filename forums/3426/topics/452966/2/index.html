    Certainly, better grade of diesel will give you better performance, each grade go thru a process, it is like filtering the dirt away, and make it more pure, being pure, it will not leave much residue in your engine piston, piston rings and liner.

    However, most cab are built with  a diesel heavy duty engine, the liner wall are more rough and piston rings are more thicker.

    The fuel efficiency failed in a sense or so called less powerful is when the piston rings and the liner wall interference get bigger, chinese say, lau hong, instead of full combustion power, the burnt diesel escaped thru the gap between the rings and the liner, this happen after a period of 2 to 3 years on the road, more black smoke come out of the exhaust is due to such problem, wasted diesel and yet no power.

    The remedy is to change piston rings to bigger size in diameter to close the gap, you hv to remove the top engine block cover, remove all fuel injector, crank the engine mannually, to raise the piston above the liner, then change, at the same time, flush the engine clean.
