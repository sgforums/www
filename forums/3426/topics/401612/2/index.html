  • WizQ
    11 Jun 10, 14:26
    Originally posted by WizQ:

    You want difficult? Mr. Auto, Mr SMS and Mr iPhone booking.
    Any brothers here want to share how you tackle these situations? I will share my take on these three types later.

     

    As mentioned earlier, not everyone is like that millionaire CCK cabby, who now is enjoying live in the Carribean, can afford to reject all calls.                   I would just like to share my simple strategy with these calls.

    Auto booking.  Many of the staff call from their office just before they come down. Once recorded, the next call will be listed as auto booking (without name). You need to invest some time to familiriase with the "pick up" loactions. Ie, which building has what big foreign companies (foreign banks, finance firms, law firms, etc) and if you got calls from these buildings, most of the time it is "safe".      

    Sometimes you can even tell what type of pax and their type of residence from the number the call are made from. For example, if call from 6890xxxx, you can tell the age of pax, their rank in the company, method of payment (all will pay by eVoucher), etc, Believe me, your experience will tell.

    SMS booking. Again, need to see which buildings they are calling from and most of the time it is a lone pax. They just use sms for the convenience.

    iPhone booking.        This is the type I don't like and will reject.  These people using iPhone booking are usually the "smart aleck" who will "no-show" as they will grab any empty cab, even though they have made the booking. Worse is that they don't bother to cancel.

    If they don't "no show" (that is, they cannot get empty cab before we reach the pick up location), usually they are the Desmond Lim or Raymond Tan type, which will bring you to Komade's favourite places (Sengkang and Punggol).

     

    Anyway, I hope Professor Poolman would come back from the Carribean soon and teach us more on taxi driving. Seriously I learnt a lot from him.
