    Thank you "

    Fatal accident - temporary disqualification until convicted in court, then made permanent. Ban likely 3-5 years for 1st offender.

    (There is a difference between suspension and disqualification, suspension is when you go over the points limit, your license is suspended but will be given back. Disqualification means no license)

    Sec 66 (1) Road Traffic Act - maximum penalty raised from 2 to 5 years for reckless driving causing death.

    If cause of death due to carelessness likely fine from $5000 to $8000. Since 2 victims, likely 2 fines.

    If cause due to dangerous driving, likely fine + imprisonment, 6 weeks to 3 months.

    If cause to reckless driving, imprisonment of 5 months and upwards.

    Factors: If repeat offender, ie; faced a charge of dangerous driving under sec 64 before, higher penalties and  jail sentence.

    If act was due to total disregard of life or other road users - stiff sentence (reflected in raising of maximum penalty)

    If visibility was poor, road condition was wet, driver was driving within speed limit, pedestrians or other party was reckless, a fine and disqualification is the usual tariff.

    But if visibility was bad, road wet and driver sped above speed limit, this is aggravating factor, jail term of 6 to 8 months for 1st offender. Since got 2 fatalities, consecutive sentence likely.

    It all depends on the circumstances of the case and whether the driver has any previous convictions for dangerous, careless or inconsiderate driving.
