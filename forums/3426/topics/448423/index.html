Are our cabs poorly maintained???

  • SBS 1000U
    4 Mar 12, 20:32

    Last week, I went to Changi Airport T3 McDonalds' to have dinner. I sat by the window seat near the road, admiring the rows of cabs driving into the taxi stand.

    But I noticed that a lot of cabs have some exterior faults or shortcomings. There are a few common ones that I have noticed.

    - body dents

    - body scratches

    - black soot on body and rim covers

    - missing rim covers

    - peeled off body advertisements

    - patches of different colored toned body parts

    - sooted front headlamps

    - missing logos

    The best maintained are the SMRT Chrysler 300Cs. The worst goes to SMRT Toyota Crowns and Nissan Cedrics.

    Many of the Transcab Toyota Crowns have patches of paintjobs which seems to cover some wordings on the cab before.

    For missing rim covers, Transcab Toyota Wish, Comfort and City cab Sonatas have the most. And seems that all SMART VW Tourans have missing rim covers.

    Sooted front headlamps, many older Mercedes Benz E class cabs have this problem.

    May I know do cabbies take care of their cabs images or is it up to the company??? But I do see a few cabs are well maintained, some even add things like rim covers, chrome linings,etc.

    What are your views???

    It's up to the companies, they are the vehicle owners. You talk about exterior wear and tear, I'm more interested in internal maintenance. Many companies even the biggest and 'best' do not always maintain their cabs in tip top or road worthy conditions.

    Even after complaining, they do a lackadaisical job. What they preach in their handbook and what actually transpires, are 2 different things. Drivers have to really be persistent and demanding before things can get done. Companies, even the 'top 1' are more interested in making money from rentals than having them in workshop for repairs which means a loss of rental income. They really try to get the cabs out before 7pm, so that they don't lose out having it stuck overnight, although the repairs weren't done properly or still persist.

    Another factor is of course FTs being employed who have little experience in maintaining cars and are forced to learn from trial and error (even after many months are still learning!), or who simply are more interested in pay than proper work.
