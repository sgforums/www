    For the 1st 2 months, I do not dare to go inside CBD because of those CBD rules i dont understand so i dont want to go inside and get fined,and do not dare to take calls, i drive almost 16hrs daily and able to get about 160-200 gross, after deducting rental and cost, i get about 30-50....

    Learning the basic routes gives you more confidence to venture further.....

    More importantly, find someone who can click with you, to show you his path of journey, find a mentor, a friend, a buddy......

    Seriously, if i had not know about taxi forums, i could have given up too......i used to have doubts....how come other taxi drivers so nice to give advice meh? My existence could meant another competitor, less business for them......why should they help a newbie grow......just let them rot lah.......Just ask what you wanted to know....most will be glad to offer their advices......as you broke the ice and eventually meet up some during kopi sessions, then you find more joy and the motivation to continue.....

    All the best to your new career!

    I'm also a newbie... driving only for about 3 weeks nia... so I'm not expert enough to give advice... juz want to share my experience.

    1. I'm a Road Idiot (è·¯ç—´).... so whenever I'm in doubt.... I Always ask pax. Most pax are really willing to direct you to their destination.

    2. When I got questions... I come here and ask the experienced and super helpful TDs... They sure help you one... like those good fellows that had oready replied to your thread.

    3. I also try to PR with other TD while waiting for pax... ask them questions relating to taxi... so far they also quite friendly. I even ask how to secure the boot with the rubber string if the boot cannot close fully.
