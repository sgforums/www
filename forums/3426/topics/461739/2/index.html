    Yes it's good to have friends or join a group, but also can still be lone ranger, if you know your rights and what to do.

    Maybe this comes with experience, but really it all boils down to common sense, and maybe even 'dollars and cents'.

    Think of yourself as working by the hour, every hour you should earn $x, don't do things that will deprive you of this, unless you want to, say you wanna eat or rest or do some other stuff.

    Avoid trouble where you can, got many examples in this forum what to do, whom and where to avoid, what calls to take or not to take, what pax to avoid.

    Remember this service we provide is not like a shop where your card is rejected, you can return the goods and not buy it. This is like a restaurant, after eating your meal, you must pay for it. Here the payment is made after the service is provided, so no matter what is must be collected unless there is good reason not to: you went the wrong way or wanna help an old or handicapped person.

    For CDG, very simple either use the cashless receipt or call the operator and inform your issue. Let them advise you and follow it, because no matter what if you follow, CDG will pay you back, either from the pax or themselves. I'm sure other companies will hv some policy about this.
