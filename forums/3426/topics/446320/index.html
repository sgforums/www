Some taxis may be fitted with cameras

  • QX179R
    30 Jan 12, 12:09

    In-vehicle cameras which capture what happens in front of a vehicle may soon be installed in some taxis here.

    SMRT Taxis is exploring the idea of outfitting its fleet of more than 3,000 taxis with the cameras, which can record footage usable as evidence that could well protect the driver in the event of an accident.

    TransCab expects to have the cameras in all its 4,300 taxis by year's end. The two cab operators jointly run more than a quarter of the 26,000 cabs here. The largest cab operator Comfort Delgro, which operates more than 15,000 cabs, could not provide information on this matter on Sunday.

    SMRT Taxis director Tony Heng said of the usefulness of the cameras in disputes over accidents: 'The footage will clearly show what happened and which party is at fault. The device could also encourage better driving habits.' He said SMRT Taxis was evaluating a tender for the supply of these cameras.

    -- ST
