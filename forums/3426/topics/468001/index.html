    Last option jobs means nobody wants, right? Consider the following stats:

    1. There are about 45,000 active taxi drivers.

    2. There are more than 90,000 TDVL holders. Although it could be argued that half of these people hold the licence precisely because it could be their last choice option, then why did they choose taxi licence? Why not go for the bus driver's licence, or other class 4 or 5 licences?

    3. Every year, more than 9,000 people apply to take the TDVL course, which represents an increase of some 30% over the last 3 years. And this number is expected to rise in the future.

    4. LTA has increased the minimum fleet size for an operator by 100%, from 400 to 800. They must be quite confident that there will be demand for this increase from the TDs.

    The minimum requirement is an O or N level pass in English. This has raised the bar somewhat. There are quite a number of people who simply could not pass the exams despite numerous attempts. They make many attempts to retake the exams, paying for each retest. They can easily be a security guard or a cleaning supervisor or a bus driver. There are many other options for these people, but why die die want to be a TD?

    I dun think all 45,000 active TDs are desperados. The huge numbers hardly suggest that it is a last option job.

    As for Locals, TD was cultured out of no option because during the 80s and 90s when economy was booming, driving TD is a last option, meaning you must be someone who cannot make it even at a booming economy, and that culture had in a way transmitted down till now, coupled with those born with silver spoon si gina (kids), we are still very much under the scope of being the lower rung of the society.

    But then, now matter how peoples perception on us, the most important is still yourself, and that is ME, Me must have confident, discipline,steady, smile often and be happy, care for me family and good buddies. Once that is reached, we are no different or even better than a CEO status, because CEO is still a labour, we are the boss.
