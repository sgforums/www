    STOMPer Celine was shocked when a Caucasian man who boarded her cab refused to pay the fare. The passenger was apparently frustrated that the STOMPer was talking on the phone while driving.

    The STOMPer claims that she was talking on her hands-free set and even helped the passenger load his luggage inside the taxi boot.

    Celine recounts this unpleasant incident:

    "On 11 August 2010 at 9.32am, a Caucasian man boarded my taxi from Changi Airport Terminal 3 to go to Changi Village Hotel.

    "I helped him load his luggage inside the boot all by myself. He didn't offer to help even though I am a woman.

    "During the journey, my handphone rang.

    "As it was an urgent call, I apologized to the passenger and told him that I needed to answer this call but the passenger kept quiet.

    "After which, I answered the call on my bluetooth hands-free set.

    "At Changi Village Hotel, I again assisted the passenger to unload his luggage.

    "At this point of time, he grabbed his luggage and shouted that he will not pay the taxi fare as I was on the phone throughout the journey.

    "Despite my countless apologies and explanations, he continued to insult me and refused to pay the taxi fare of $13.80. Thereafter, he walked into the hotel to check-in.

    "Shocked by his actions, I called Comfort and the police. I informed him that I had called the police.

    "The passenger mentioned arrogantly that he had "no time for my nonsense" and left. There was nothing I could do to hold him back.

    "Police arrived and approached the hotel staff to enquire about the passenger but they said they were unable to help without a written letter from the police.

    "Traumatized by the incident, I proceeded to file a police report. However, I was told to lodge a complaint at the Subordinate Courts, who in turn directed me to go to the Land Transport Authority and Comfort.

    "I spent the entire day filing complaints and seeking redress but nobody helped me, just because he was a foreigner.

    "Where’s the justice?"
