    16 Apr 13, 08:46

    Let me give u an example,

    The piston rings are worn (make of hard material), the liner (cylinder make of cast iron) is oversized, in between the clearance is 1.0mm, so every cylinders will have fuel escape in between the rings and liner (cylinder) wall, and that is call fuel wastage.

    So, no matter how you clean or put better diesel or change engine oil also no use, you will find the same problem happening, high consumption of fuel, yet no power because of the leakage.

    And then the mechanic removed the piston (upper engine removed), he never measure the cylinder, replace new piston rings, about 3 to 4 rings for one piston depend to type of engine, put it back, however, there is still a leakage due to liner (cylinder worn), and that new rings can only reduce the leakage maybe by 0.5mm, and still leaking, still you find high consumption, tho not as much as before.

    What mechanic should do is measure the liner (cylinder bore), mannual hone it, replace the piston ring with the next available oversize one so as to reduce the clearance to 0.05mm, that is 5 micron.

    And then you see a high performances with less fuel consumption
