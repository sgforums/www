    Motoring @ AsiaOne

    Caught on video: Cabby deliberately rams into car on ECP

    Cabby who was ferrying a passenger turned dangerous and rammed a car after he was high-beamed at. -AsiaOne

     
    AsiaOne

    A cabby who deliberately rammed his taxi into another car a few times while on the East Coast Parkway (ECP) has been unwittingly caught on video.

    According to the other driver, the incident just happened in January this year. The entire incident was recorded down by the driver's in-car camera.

    He only recently uploaded the video onto his Facebook account. At the time of writing, the video was shared 193 times on Facebook.

    In the video, the driver flashed his high beam at a taxi that moved into his lane. Twice, the driver turned on his high beam.

    This caused the cabby to suddenly apply his brakes. The driver claimed on his Facebook that sudden braking in the hope to cause an accident was "standard taxi skill".

    Even though the driver flashed his high beam, the SMRT cabby did not give way. In the end the driver switched lanes and overtook the taxi.

    About 35 seconds into the footage, the SMRT taxi suddenly re-emerged from the left side and cut into the driver's lane. Judging from the video, the cabby seemed to have rammed the car twice before moving off.

    The taxi also seemed to be carrying a passenger in the rear.

    The other car stopped and was stationary for more than a few seconds on the ECP, causing other traffic to slow down.

    The video then showed both vehicles pulling over to the side of the road.

    The driver who also warned people to be wary of taxi drivers who purposely crash into other motorists' cars so that they can get into an accident and file an insurance claim.

    He added that it was thanks to his high-definition camera that there was evidence showing that the cabby rammed into him.

    The cabby could have claimed from his insurance if he did not have footage of the accident, the man said.

    He also added that he had already logged a complaint and sent a copy of the video to the Traffic Police and the Land Transport Authority.

    A Stomp reader who saw the video said she was disturbed that a taxi driver was driving aggressively and ramming a car on the expressway.
