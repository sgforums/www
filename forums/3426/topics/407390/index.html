    T2 is by far the quietest terminal for inbound arrivals after midnight.
    Three flights on a Monday morning, two from SQ, and one from Shenzhen Airlines.

     

    The situation at T2 is worsened as arrivals can be at T2 or T3, and the cycle so happens that most of the morning departures occur from T3, which otherwise would inbound for T2. So planes park over at T3 overnight, instead of at T2 and having to tow them across the bridge for their morning departure.

     

    The two SQ flights, one from BKK and the other from Shanghai PDG, have tremendous amount of connecting pax. Only a few would come out to the taxi stand, while the rest stay in the transit area or head over to the T3 transit area for sleep for their onward connections which would mostly be the first flight out in the early morning(mostly to Australia for the BKK and PDG pax, and Europe for the BKK pax).

     

    Shenzhen Airlines uses a 737 to SIN, and carries only at most 150 pax per flight, and thus the lack of traffic.

    Large planes and many arrivals/departures does not really mean a lot of people coming out of the transit area. A lot of these people transit to onward destinations.

     

    Maybe you all notice by now, the 5p.m to 7p.m arrivals and departure madness by the airlines in T1 is a façade.

     

    The Qantas - British Airways - Qantas - Jetstar - Qantas - Qantas - British Airways - KLM - Air France - Qantas - Finnair(soon to be) combo yields "a lot of pax". But almost none of them "come out".

     

    I still think the best would be if you all target the AirAsia arrivals @ T1. Not many people connect to other flights on AirAsia or other carriers in SIN, they usually do that in their hubs of KUL, BKK and CGK Jakarta. The traffic is almost always point-to-point in SIN, and not a hub operation. To a certain extent, Jetstar also.
