    Originally posted by He:

    Does anyone know why Sonatas seem to catch fire easily? i saw afew "black" sonatas already.


    Korea Cars have a weak spot in term of cooling system, car like Sonata, every 2 to 3 years, you need to be care about cooling radiator timming, thermostat and alternator. Fuel distribution is not a problem, but the cooling timming and alternator play a major role. My advise is that when servicing after 2 years, you ask for cooling and radiator flushing service and alternator check. And also thermostat, which is a sensor, it can break down leading to irregular cooling water supply or no supply at all.

    For those on the road with sonata, if you notice you aircon sometime cut off for no reason, you better check your engine instead of aircon system. Because sonata automatically cut off aircon when the engine is hot, need more energy to run, and when the delay of cooling water (due to timming) running into the engine make the engine hot, then when it run in, it cool down the engine and your aircon suddenly work again. Most people do not know, they thought aircon problem, so change air filter, put gas, flush cooling system, change alternator etc but end up is the cooling radiator problem, sometime it can be as small as a problem of changing the radiator sensor, which cost only about a 100 buck w/workmanship. Of course another indicator is the temp indicator on your panel, most of you see a slight below the half way mark while driving all day, then as it goes up abit, you dun bother, but abit is a few hundred degree hotter, so even abit, you need to pull in to service centre and check. However, guess most cabbies are working hard to earn money and forget to look at the temp indicator, once the engine caught fire...that is it.

    When fire, please run away from you car, you life is more important. Car can cover by insurance of your company, but you got to live to drive another day.
