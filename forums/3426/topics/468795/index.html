    SINGAPORE: The Ministry of Health (MOH) said Thursday that healthcare institutions in Singapore are on heightened alert and are prepared for the eventuality of a possible H7N9 avian influenza case, given today's globalised travel patterns .

    The MOH comment comes after the first confirmed H7N9 case in Taiwan was reported.

    The Health Ministry said that as of 24 April, seven individuals have been investigated for possible H7N9 infection. All have tested negative.

    It added that it will continue to monitor the situation closely for further developments to ensure that public health is safeguarded.

    MOH also issued a health reminder to travellers going to affected areas that have reported H7N9 cases.
