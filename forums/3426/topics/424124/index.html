    4 Mar 11, 21:44

    Won't be easy to find a Comfort/Citycab taxi willing to do this job. SCGS is notorious for it's traffic jams around 6.45 to 7.15am. Assuming you'll want your child to be dropped off inside the school compound, getting in already takes 10mins. Getting out another 10. During this time driver cannot accept any booking cos there is no way he can make it to the pickup point anywhere in the vicinity within 20mins. So it means about 30mins empty. (30mins during peak hours roughly equates $20 collection)

    Bookings in the area coming like crazy, but can see cannot eat. I have a semi-regular customer. Not so bad (Nayang Girls High at Linden Dr), by the time alight around 6.45am. As Pool says, hard to do charity, I'm paid almost double of meter + booking to do this job. Didn't ask for it, but was offered this amount as the customer understood the traffic situation around this area at this time. This amount roughly compensates my opportunity cost. Obviously must provide tip top service. I would already be on scheme B prior to picking her up cos once meter cleared, bookings fly in non-stop. But mostly can see cannot eat. The traffic lights at Dunearn/Adam/Farrer junction requires at least 5-7 mins to clear. No way to accept any booking unless it's on the way like Adam Park or College Green. The other side like King's/Queen's/Coronation/Duchess is almost impossible to reach within 10-12mins. If all bookings cannot accept, then either turn left to Adam which leads to nowhere or turn right to Farrer which leads to more jams. Going straight is usually not a bright idea as it leads to SCGS! Basically it's around 20-30mins without a customer if I send pax to schools in this area in the mornings. As peak hour kicks in at 7am, heartbreak hotel if stuck in there.

    If not on scheme B, in less than 1 min 3 bookings rejected and placed on scheme B for 1 hour. Then really no calls once out of this area. At least if on scheme B, I can switch back to scheme A once out of this area.

    Wish you luck finding a cab.
