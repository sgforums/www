  • BlueRubbishKia
    13 Jan 12, 18:41

    Sorry everyone. This would probably me my last post. Even though everyone has bear with me for countless times in the past. I do beseech everyone to bear with me one more time.

    I have been in quite a number of trades in my short life time. I am proud to say. This has been my happiest period so far in my work life.

    As I mentioned before in my first post, I actually spent a year peeping through this forum. Before I “peh” the steering wheel. Even after I started to “peh” the steering wheel. I still continue to read past postings from selected few (TCL, Poolman, ALN & Komade).

    Some people “stalk” people. I “stalk” people’s postings. Here’s the thing. From day one (1st June) until today, I have NEVER had an OBK day nor earn peanuts. Dream start it was. But by the end of the 1st month, how many dead (yusof ishak) I had at the end of the shift, didn’t matter to me. Everyday, I remind myself how fortunate I am (no parangs & very little liabilities).

    There are times the first half of my shift sucks, drive until pek chek. But I will tell myself when Komade said before “Relax & Do No Give Up [Komade Trademark]”. So I bite the bullet and soldier on. True enough, until today when that happens, during the second half of my shift, I always press meter and MDT until finger “sung”. Thus, end of shift still huat.

    My happiest day on the road was Ah Khoon’s Day 142 OTR Sunday. No surcharges at all! I am guilty as charged of persuading Ah Khoon to do 2 days work in one day. I was so Happy [Poolman Trademark] that day, not because that day huat. Of course, i got huat (Sunday still Huat big time). But because of 2 things that made me happy and kept smiling during that day, got buddy (ah khoon) to talk and tease (my paxs onboard hear me tease him also laugh non-stop) while driving and also willing to break with me (Chinatown Astons…steam [ALN Trademark]). Second thing was, after returning to base and parking at the 3rd storey of the MSCP, I forgot to off the MDT. While filling up the taxi log book. PING! Tio Call Liao!! [TCL Trademark]!!! Yes, I gian peng and no discipline. Picked up pilot to AP. Fare plus tips more than covered our dinner at Chinatown Astons. Si beh Steam…..

    My happiest day not on the road was the YCK makan session. I met and become even more humble after talking to so many “lao gui”. Only thing I regret is not doing my best to know their nicks or names (especially the humble guy who drives an i30 wearing a pink polo t shirt).  Everyone was so forthcoming in sharing their views and experiences. I worked for quite a few blue chip companies in my life. Not one company has ever remotely offered me such an experience.

    My happiest incidents/sightings on the road was sounding my honk like a mad man at 2 taxis on different occasions. First occasion, CKtan (along Eu Tong Sen Road). My paxs onboard see my so happy at honking and waving at him until they actually asked me should they wave at him too. I replied them “Yes, please do so”. Second occasion, BCL (along Hougang Ave 3). I flashed at him, he moved to his lane. I go head to head with him, he wave to say pai sei. I started to honk at him. He turned and see me wind down the window and tease him. I moved forward, my pax asked me “your friend huh?”. I replied “No!”. He is my taxi school principal. My pax then replied “no wonder he drive so steady”. People flashed, he quickly reacted and waved to gesture sorry. Pax say he first time see driver so courteous and considerate. On both occasions upon seeing them, immediately, I like macham meet “fortune god”. I press meter and MDT until finger “sung”. The worst was after meeting BCL, I remember when I met up with the day shift gang for break. They see me very “pek chek” and I never say hello to them. I quickly queue up to buy coffee. After a sip of coffee and a smoke, I slowly cool down and told them “Not OBK but zai buay liao!” Please stop saying “school principal idle”. When I saw him, he had eagle eyes with  one hand on the steering wheel and the other hand was “romancing the dead “yusof ishaks” nor did he have a messy hairdo.

    My most unhappy day was one of the days last week. I went to a Caltex station to deposit money at the OCBC atm. While doing the transaction, 3 day shift “lao gui” of the same taxi co as me were blasting their earnings away. It was so loud that the nearby private car drivers actually stopped to look and listen. I felt so disgusted! Here I am, having just met with 4 gloomy faces with each having a big OBK morning peak during my morning break. There they are blasting their jiak buay liao earnings. No, I did not have an OBK day too. On contrary, I was tempted to print my “receipt” and stamped it on their faces!

    I come to this trade as a nobody and newbie. I will leave very soon as a nobody too. Like Mercabs say : Why don't i give others a chance". The reason for my post is that I just wanna tell everyone to try to be happy and see open. The days when I was happy, was the days I most huat. I never had a strategy; I only just drive and try my best to be happy. I never had a fare runaway or an OBK day. Countless times I had to play taiji with paxs who tipped me because the only money I ever wanted is the amount on the meter.

    Lastly, please trust me when I say that a person in a cyber world is totally different when you meet in the real world. My classic example is hammerhammer8888. Clementi after peak hour easily can find someone along the way to his base. But he put busy and "turbo” to bedok to meet us for morning break. Please try to get to know a person in real world. You never know how much difference it can be, rather then slamming each other. Many paxs do “look down” on us, does it do anymore good to “look down” on each other?

    P.S. The pax I picked up that morning i sighted BCL on the road, he actually threw his smoke and ran across a junction to get into my taxi. He kept thanking me for stopping even upon reaching his destination. I can understand it is a mundane job, an unappreciative job. I totally agree that the biggest injustice is how this group of multi- talented cabbies who I have come to respect over time end up in this trade? But you know what? A nobody like me and my pax still respect you a lot. It may not mean much. But it is not nothing.
