    Joe's initiative is commendable indeed. We can see from the news that many are complaining against Taxi Drivers rather than speaking up for them. I would like to add my views on strengthening your proposal. When sending an email, you would expect the authorities to at least give your proposal a thorough thought rather than bouncing the email through a few departments and replying with a diplomatic answer to brush your concerns aside. Always think from the perspective of the stakeholders (government, transport operators, etc) when you present your case. Offer alternatives and basis on why your proposal is feasible or sustainable. Present your case from the pros and cons before making your recommendation. For instance in rental reduction, if you are running a business and someone walks up to you and ask for a discount citing the difficulties in various components, what would your response be? That is an important process to think through because the authorities will need to justify this to the transport providers and ultimately the commuters who might bear the consequences of the rental reduction. Let me explain why the commuters will be affected, a lower rental might lead to lesser cabs plying the road because now they would need to drive less to recover their cost, on the flipside, more taxi drivers might enter the trade due to lower overheads and that might lead to higher availability for taxis. Now you are writing to the authorities which has repeatedly said that they would not regulate prices and leave it to the market forces to decide the pricing mechanism, there are various ways to strengthen your case. Workers Party had proposed on nationalising public transport (which has been gunned down by our transport minister), the way of structuring and presenting the proposal is done quite well, which you can probably take cue from. Even though the proposal was not implemented, it was a good start as both sides had a fair share of deliberating the pros and cons. That proposal would be a good platform to continue the debate should there be a strong demand for nationalising public transport in the future. Similarly, we all hope that Taxi Driversâ€™ voices will be heard and considered in the email which you will be writing.
