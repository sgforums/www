    Finally there is another suggestion, that we compile a list of suggestions and then submit it in writing.

    That's possible of course, but would be quite tedious, because I would have to compile them, use my own words and present them in an email.

    And since we are writing to a Minister no less, such emails must have substance and offer clear and concise plans, with a proper justification for them.     We cannot offer suggestions to improve our welfare, our takings, making life easier for us or whatever, and then as after thought think of the commuters. That would never cut any ice.     Currently the system is made to look after the commuters, followed by the companies and then taxid rivers at the bottom. But in reality, it's the companies that seem to enjoy the primary benefits (profits year after year) despite rising costs etc.
