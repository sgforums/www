    Hi everyone,

    Nice to meet you all. I am a local Singaporean Android developer and I have just released my new Singapore Cab Pro app onto Google Play. You can search "Singapore Cab Pro" on Google Play for my app.

    This app will help you with booking (both SMS and by phone numbers) and it also comes with a taximeter which is guaranteed to be accurate within 1 dollar compared to the actual one on the taxi.

    I hope you can get the chance to try out this app and let me know what you guys think about it. I also hope that this app is also helpful to all the taxi uncles out there to use a backup taximeter in case the one on the taxi fails.

    Please and please: I am not really try to spam or anything. This is more like an announcement. I hope I didn't break any rules here. If I do, I hope you guys can let me know before locking or moving my thread and I will comply accordingly. Thanks.

  • BiDuSoft
    19 Sep 12, 10:36

    Thank you for all your replies, criticisms and feedbacks. I appreciate them all. The point of this app is mainly to break down the surcharges for taxi users. Taximeter is mainly a side function. I know there are quite a lot of taxi users (even local Singaporeans) get confused by the surcharge system in Singapore.

    This app will show you what kind of surcharges that will incur during your trip. It uses both GPS and AGPS as a backup in case the taxi goes through a tunnel or through area where there is no GPS signal.

    I really didn't mean any disrespect, it's simply a taximeter and I tried to utilize all the location capability on the phone to make one since I haven't seen any Singaporean developers made one yet so I thought that it would be a good idea to make one.

    This app also has SMS booking and phone book of all the taxi companies so it's not purely about taximeter.

    Once again, I am a young and inexperienced local developer, I really just wanted to contribute to the local community by making an app that I thought everyone can use. I really didn't mean anything else. I hope I didn't offend anyone with this app :(.
