    Have more taxis on the road

    A RECENT letter in the press said that a taxi driver refused to help a reader load her child's pram into the boot. This shows that cabbies are either poorly trained or do not care about providing good service as they have more than enough fares.

    Recently, I was at Lucky Plaza and saw a long queue at a taxi stand, made up mostly of tourists. There was nary a taxi in sight.

    Poor service from cabbies would leave tourists with a bad impression of Singapore.
    If Singapore is to improve its attractiveness as a tourist destination, it has to tackle this issue once and for all.

    I was in Hong Kong and Taipei recently and hailing taxis in both cities was a breeze, even in the rain.

    More taxis should be allowed on the roads until supply meets demand. This would increase competition among cabbies and not give them the luxury of picking their fares.

    MR YAP NGIM CHOON
