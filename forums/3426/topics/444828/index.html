    Wear of seat belts is compulsory. 1 unfortunate case highlights this. I think it was 2009 or 2010 at Bishan St 11 x Braddell Road. Taxi with rear seat female passenger travelling along Braddell with right of way.

    A car or van driven very fast tried to enter Braddell without giving way hitting the taxi square at the rear. The female passenger later died in hospital. Naturally driver of car/van charged in court and jailed, but the taxi driver also hauled up, because investigations revealed the pax wasnt wearing her seat-belt, onus on driver to ensure this said the judge. He got fined and license revoked.

    So this is something to consider, just like the 5 pax theory, the seat belt issue is the same. If accident involving your cab and the passenger is injured or worse killed, you will also be held responsible, plus obviously an insurance claim against either you or the cab company. Of course passenger will never get full amount because he/she also liable for not fastening, but there's no such thing as 100% in 90% of accident cases.

    Even if you had right of way, but knocked down a pedestrian who ignored a red light, courts will still award maybe 10-30% to the victim, because onus is always on you to keep to the speed limit and look out for pedestrians.
