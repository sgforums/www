    My personal opinion: I am a MBA holder. I can understand and concur with both Bro onewaytransfer and Bro BCL point of views. Take a scenario, If we are a successful own boss or a Cxx in a highly recognised company, then likely we will comment that we based on our capability not certificate to be successful. Even senior manager (with MBA), their basic salary also tagged with MBA market value. So why am I driving taxi? Because I was first to go when come to retrenchment. I had been in the top and bottom of my career. But what can I do? I still need to put food on the table everyday. I need to pay water and electricity bill.... and HDB instalment... etc. Sometimes during driving I will start to reflect why am I driving taxis after studied so hard for a MBA and was a AVP before. Then will start to feel demoralised and regretted with negative thinking. It is really miserable for a ex-high-flyer to drive a taxi. To think it positively it is taxi driving that help me bring food home for the past few months. I appreciate taxi-driving because it keep me afloat when I am at the bottom of my career. Thru tax-driving, I see and learn the dark-sided of human beings (pax) which I never visualise before in my corporate world other than office politics. I had improved in my defensive driving, thanks to our hungry drivers' driving behaviour and many "ninjas"(jaywalker wearing black) running across the road from nowhere at night. I am still looking for a suitable job because I need CPF to pay for my HDB loan and medisave for my parents/family. Meanwhile I hang on to taxi-driving and appreciate every single food I bring home everyday.
