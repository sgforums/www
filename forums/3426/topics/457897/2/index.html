  • BlueRubbishKia
    2 Sep 12, 09:28

    Read this thread a few days back, have to say it brings back a lot of memories.

    I was a Mickey Mouse driver when i first started out. Although, i did feel i kind of knew most major roads and E-ways during that time, i wouldn't "die of a horrible death". 

    Of course, i did spend a considerable amount of effort to read through "right leg" and "left leg" posts.

    So i started, without a street directory in the cab (wasn't intentional, my hirer and night relief didn't use it). The only thing i rely upon was the google maps on my iphone(thank god i had no trouble spelling out the street names).

    Then it happened, during the morning peak hours, i dropped someone at the fourth most "ice cream" place for day shift drivers. I didn't have any thoughts on where to go next, just wandered aimlessly.

    Suddenly, there was a pax running after my cab. As expected, the pax was unfashionably late for work. I knew the fastest way but i didn't know where was the nearest E-way entrance and the exact drop off location was.

     

    The pax was really nice and kind to guide me along. Upon arriving at the destination, i fumbled with the NETS payment. The pax told me two words "jia you" before alighting.

     

    That was my turning point, from then on, i put in every effort in knowing my routes. Three reasons. One, i can only be new for so long, not forever. Two, if "right leg" and "left leg" can do it, why can't i. Three, i don't want to disappoint another pax.

    Someone has posted a lot of threads and posts, which to be honest, i do not agree with most and i find it to be crap for the rest. But i for once do agree with his this post (statement).

    What i can only say is the phrase "i don't know" does more harm than good to oneself in the long term. Then again, i am a nobody and if the others in the trade who still carry on with this phrase in their minds whenever they start their engines everyday, so be it.

    Although i drive a normalsine, there are 2 types of grease i carry and use occasionally. Light duty for locals. Heavy duty for non locals. People who know me well, have heard in real time over the phone when i used the grease. No prizes for guessing who has the louder or only voice in my cab.
