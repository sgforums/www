    3/4-tank rule for CNG cars from Sunday

    NEW DUTY: With effect from Jan 1, owners of CNG cars will have to pay a duty of 20 cents per kg of CNG they buy at any of the six CNG kiosks islandwide. (PHOTO: THE STRAITS TIMES)

    Owners of cars which run on compressed natural gas (CNG) will soon have to pay more for their fuel. They will also have to comply with the three-quarter-tank rule when travelling into Malaysia.

    A 20 cents per kg excise duty on CNG will be levied at all gas pumps from Jan 1, a move announced in Budget 2009.

    CNG car owners will still pay less duty than petrol-vehicle owners, who pay 41 cents per kg in duty for most grades of petrol.

    The three-quarter-tank rule, which currently applies only to petrol vehicles registered in Singapore, will also apply to CNG cars from Jan 1, said Singapore Customs yesterday.

    Owners of CNG vehicles have been informed of these changes by mail.

    The Government implemented the three-quarter-tank rule in 1991 to discourage motorists from topping up in Johor, where petrol is cheaper.

    Once CNG becomes a dutiable item, a licence from Singapore Customs will be required for the manufacture of CNG.

    The manufacturers will have to account for the quantity of CNG produced, dispensed and sold, and will be subjected to periodic audits.
