    Surcharge can solve ferry terminal's taxi shortage

     

    I REFER to the Sunday Times report "Goods by day, people at night" (last Sunday), and would like to highlight a problem that passengers at the Tanah Merah Ferry Terminal face.

    There is a shortage of taxis at the ferry terminal, as cab drivers usually prefer to head to the airport where they can earn a surcharge when they pick up a passenger.

    Hence, touts appear, as there are business opportunities there.

    The ferry terminal, like the airport, is an entry and exit point of the country, where people are required to have passports to enter and leave.

    Hence, I hope the authorities will consider imposing a surcharge - similar to that of the airport's - at the ferry terminal, to encourage taxi drivers to drive there to pick up passengers. This would also eliminate the problem of touts.

    Cheong Tiong Hua
