    STOMPer Melissa is outraged by the behaviour of local cab drivers these days. She claims that they are becoming increasingly rude and choosy: preferring to pick up passengers only at certain locations and time. This is based on two separate incidents that she faced recently.

    She writes to us:

    "Not too long ago, cab drivers made their views known that they were afraid of their rice bowls being affected with increasing fares. Unfortunately, it seems to me that whilst our economy is doing well, cab drivers are getting cocky. I'm writing as feedback towards my unpleasant experiences on Sunday (Dec 5) and Saturday (December 11) at Marina Barrage and Jalan Sultan area respectively.

    "On 5 Dec, I was at the Marina Barrrage for an event. When my friends and I were leaving the area at around 6pm, we noticed that the cabs dropping passengers off, did not pick up any new ones. We decided to walk out and flag for one along the way. Strangely, many cabs, without passengers, drove past us with the green 'TAXI' sign brightly lit! There were some who saw us waving and accelerated away.I strongly feel that these cab drivers wanted to pick passengers from Marina Bay Sands,in order to charge the surcharge and maybe earn some tips from casino patrons.

    "On 11 Dec, my family and I waited for an hour for a cab at night, walking from Ophir Road to Jalan Sultan. At least 10 cabs drove past us, without any passengers inside. However, a cab driver driving a 'Comfort' cab along Victoria Road really takes the cake. At the traffic light junction, he had 'TAMPINES' flashed on his sign. We flagged the cab and asked him to bring us to Bedok Road. We even told him the shortcuts. Without muttering a word, he stepped on the accelerator and stopped 25m down the road to pick up two ladies. That was extremely rude of him. Of course, I need to add that the taxi hotlines are always busy! Are they under-staffed?

    "When I grumbled to my friend about the local cab situation, he highlighted the exact same thing he experienced on the night of ZoukOut.

    "Are cab drivers openly displaying their arrogance by choosing their passengers? How is it that rude cab drivers are allowed on our roads? Will I continuously be disadvantaged because I don't hail cabs in the city and don't have blonde hair?"

    "I hope that Comfort cab will provide us with a satisfactory answer. I believe that I'm not the only frustrated passenger around."
