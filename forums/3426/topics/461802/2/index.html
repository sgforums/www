    Hi alpacino, This is my first post and I am a newbie taxi uncle. I shared the same working life problems as u except that I am 5 yrs older than you. Some of the advices here are worth considering, like being a relief first and then work yourself up from there. When I first registered for a cab, my plan was to go OMO, after going through posts here, I got myself a relief driver and is thankful for the advice I received here and the decision I have made. After about 10 days of manning a cab, let me tell you it is really tough driving a 12-hour shift for guys our age. For your info, prior to this, I have been driving for the past 40yrs averaging 45 to 50k km per year. Casual driving is very different from driving a cab. Casual driving counts for nothing in driving a cab. Casual driving is more forgiving. I am also a route idiot. I bought myself a search friendly GPS. Paxs now are different, before you could reach for your GPS, they already have the route plotted out in their mobile phones - meaning they are very receptive to this mode of routing. Another advice from the forum worth taking note of is, asking pax for directions. Do it. they will directed you willingly. Alot that are written here by old birds come from their many years of driving experiences. Seperate the woods from the trees and go for it and adjust as you get along. What is my take for my mere 10 days of cab driving? I am doing fine. No the 7k of earnings as reported, but enough for the 3 daily meals and some. For a newbie hirer with a relief like me, it is nice knowing that half your rental is already in the pocket when you start out each morning. PM me, perhaps we can sit down for a chit chats between two TD wannabes.
