SmartCab errant taxi driver(SHB9064B) who overcharge

  • JYTG
    10 Aug 11, 15:36


    On 3rd Aug 2011, I flagged a SMARTCab white Mercedes taxi (SHB9064B) outside my home.

    The metered fare was about $11 but the final fare on the meter was $14.40 after the driver hit the final charge button on the meter.

    However, the driver insisted that I pay $18.50. I queried on the "final final amount" which he insisted; which was different from the final metered fare but his reasons were hardly comprehensive. He was also quick to reset the meter when I queried the inflated charges. Not wanting to get into an unpleasant argument and as I was also late for my appointment, I asked for a receipt. He managed to print a copy but with just the fare amount paid.

    I subsequently wrote to SmartCab to determine if I was overcharged and the followings were my correspondences with SmartCab.

    Before Smartcab's last response, I actually protest that the driver was only given a verbal warning as it was his first reported offence. But how many cases have gone unreported by unknowing or unsuspecting passengers? Also if he was brave enough to cheat a local, how many foreign visitors have fallen prey to his dishonesty.

    I can understand the verbal warning if it was a genuine mistake but this was a deliberate attempt to cheat and even after being queried, he was unfazed and proceeded to justify the inflated fare with non-existence charges. In addition, he was not remorseful after SmartCab initial investigation and only admitted when the receipt was faxed to his company.

    My question is why did SmartCab being a public company endorsed such dishonesty amongst its drivers and willing to let them romp the street to cheat the public? Isn't it the responsibility of a public company to protect the public?
