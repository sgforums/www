    Actually most of the olden road names based on kampong names and also names of the person who own the piece of load that go thru the road. Some roads are named after what industry is there at that time.

    Kreta Ayer Road, Kreta is car in malay, Ayer is water add together car water. Why car water? check out the wiki explanation: http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kreta_Ayer_Road

    McCallum St is named after  Major H.E. McCallum, Colonial engineer in the 1890s.

    Ann Siang Hill  was the site of the house and estate of Chia Ann Siang (è°¢å®‰ç¥¥; 1832–1892), a wealthy Malacca-born Hokkien Chinese sawmiller.

    Telok Ayer St  TheMalay name refers to "bay water" because Telok Ayer Street was the coastal road along the bay.

    Sago St The street received its name because during the 1840s, there were numerous sago factories on the street. pith of the rumbia palm and made into flour that is used to make cakes

    Club Street got its name from the various Chinese clubs sited on this street.

    Spring Street The street was once a source of water. Spring Street was opposite the Maxwell Market (now Maxwell Food Centre) and got its name from the spring which fed the well there. In the old days, water was drawn and transported by bullock carts at Spring Street.

    Tampines  The town is so named because in the 1900s (decade) a large forest of Tampines trees were there

    Jalan Kayu means "wooden road" in Malay. One version for the road name's origin is that firewood used to be stacked on the roadside. Another version is that the muddy laterite roads leading to the rubber estates in the area were made passable due to logs of wood that used to cover the muddy roads, hence the term in Malay jalan kayu.

    Holland Road and Holland Village were named after an early resident in Singapore, Hugh Holland, who was an architect.

    Kallang is an old Malay reference to the orang laut (people of the sea), called orang biduanda kallang, who lived in the vicinity of the Kallang River and Singapore River.

    Until today, I'm having problems with woodlands: drives, ave, street, ring, crescent, circle??!!

    Drives in woodlands

    woodlands drive 6X(means u acces from woodlands ave 6)

    woodlands drive 7X( means u access from woodlands ave 7)

    woodlands drive 1X (means u access from woodlands ave 1)

    okay...u can determine which side is woodlands dr 60? 61?

    even drive nos on one side, odd ones on the other.

    Ring,crescent,circle

    Avenues in Woodlands

    Woodlands ave 1,3,5,7,9 is the east-west running road .

    ave 1 is the one closest to SLE, serves HDB block number 3XX and 4XX

    ave 3 is located at western woodlands(marsiling MRT that side), serves blk 1XX and marsiling estate near checkpoint.

    ave 3,5,7 think of them as the wings of causeway point ave 3 on the west(marsiling HDBs ,woodlands blk 1XX,3XX,4XX) and ave 5,7 on the east(blk 5XX,6XX,7XX,8XX).

    ave 9 runs on RP that side and it serves blk 7XX.

    ave 1,3,5,7,9. Just rmb ave 1 is the closest to SLE followed by 3,5,7, then ave 9(RP).

    ave 2,4,6,8,10,12 same principle but they are north-south running road.

    ave 2 starting from SLE, the ave 4,6 and so on until ave 10,12 which is next door to sembawang.

    Enjoy speeding on ave 12 btw..

    Streets in Woodlands

    eg. Woodlands street 11,12,13. they are called st 1X. street 1X means the first number of the block number they serve...eg. st 1X serves blk 1XX,st 2X serves blk 2XX ,st 3X serves blk 3XX and so on.

    AMK,Tampines also same.

    CCK dun quite follow the system.

    Bedok though have names in street but completely different system esp. in bedok north.

    Rings,crescent,circle

    woodlands rings serves block 6XX which the area itself is more closer to SLE woodlands ave 12 exit. they are surrounded by woodlands Dr XX which i mentioned eariler on.

    woodlands circle serves blk 7XX likewise they are surrounded by woodlands Dr XX.

    same for woodlands crescent which is more nearer to sembawang that side.

    U all go study the online SG map regularly take note of patterns in the numbering of HDB blocks. Usually the run either clockwise(so far only Yishun having this pattern) or counter-clockwise.
