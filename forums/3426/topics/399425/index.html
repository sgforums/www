    This is my analysis of the fare structure.

    1. Assume we remove all extras and leave only a standard fare like what other countries do. Now if the minimum fare for Cedrics and Crowns is $5.00, then Chrysler starting fare must go up proportionally. If this happens, heartland passengers will kenna screw because suddenly it becomes more expensive to do HDB town transfers, or even short hops.

    2. CBD surcharges do not affect most people because most office workers can claim taxi fare if got overtime. Those CBD workers who cannot claim taxi fare usually will not bother with cabs at all, unless it is those "uncle 4 stops please (MPV 7 stops)" kind of pax. For those people who complain about expensive taxi fare, I really wish to see how often they take taxis. They complain so much but only take taxis once in a blue moon. If the CBD surchare was removed, the end result will be that CBD workers will not have any taxis during ERP operational hours. All the taxis will suddenly have no incentive to come into town during peak hours. Now the town passengers kenna screw.

    3. If we remove $3 MBS surcharge, it will be fine even if we got a flat fare taxi structure. But bear in mind the 2 problems above.

    4. If we remove $3 RWS surcharge, the gamblers there may have to call taxi because taxi uncles will PNG from Vivo, Harbourfront and St James. In the end, solve one problem, create another problem. We call that lampa pa lan.

    5. If we have a flat fare structure, I believe all extras must be removed, including airport, expo, seletar, 50% midnight and 35% peak hour. Otherwise what justification is there to jack up starting fare from 2.80 to 5.00 ? If that happens, there will be very few taxi drivers in airport, seletar, expo. After midnight, taxi uncles might as well go home and sleep unless they want to chiong SATS vouchers. Peak hours might as well go lim kopi to avoid jams. In the end, heartland and town passengers kenna screw again.

    6. So in the end, do you think Raymond Lim or Gerard Ee will agree to a flat fare structure? Based on my analysis, they will face a massive public protest if they give green light. So like it or not, this taxi fare structure is here to stay. Uniquely Singapore!
