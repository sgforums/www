[ERP] Rates to go up at 23 gantries across Singapore

  • Poolman
    26 Oct 10, 16:35

    Rates across 23 Electronic Road Pricing (ERP) gantries around the island will go up from November.

    This is part of the Land Transport Authority's quarterly review of ERP-priced roads and expressways, and may be the biggest single number of gantries affected this year.

    Motorists passing through 23 of these gantries around the island will have to pay more from November.

    The affected gantries include two along the ECP at Fort Road and KPE slip road, where rates will peak at S$4.

    Rates at 11 gantries in the CBD will also go up by S$1 to S$3 while another nine gantries in the Orchard area will increase by S$1 to S$2.

    Rates for other gantries remain unchanged.

    The ERP is an electronic system of road pricing based on a pay as you use model and according to the Land Transport Authority, rates are adjusted to manage traffic flow.

    LTA added that ERP pricing also helps to keep traffic flowing within the optimal speed range, which is 45 to 65km/h on expressways and 20 to 30km/h on arterial roads.

    Motorists that Channel NewsAsia spoke with were mixed in their reaction.

    "The increase is too sudden. Maybe they should do a so called survey to go around and check whether this is amount is justifiable or not," said one of the motorists.

    "If the traffic is already heavy every morning, the gantries, if increase by a bit, I think the traffic will be a bit more smooth," said another motorist.

    The next ERP review will take place in November for the December School Holidays.
