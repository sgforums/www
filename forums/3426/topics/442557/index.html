    ON NOV 20, a Sunday, I felt unwell while at Orchard Road and decided to take a taxi home. I tried booking a cab via the ComfortDelGro iPhone application many times but there were no taxis available for booking.

     

    I tried calling for a taxi without any success until my cellphone went flat. I then joined the long queue at Orchard Cineleisure where the taxis that drove up were mostly 'on call'.

     

    I noticed that the drivers of some cabs that came in had decided to call it a day and did not pick up another fare while others specified their destinations. Finally, 11/2 hours later, I got into a cab.

     

    When I told the driver about the hard time I had waiting for a cab, he said there were fewer taxis on the road because it was a Sunday and there were no peak-hour charges. Furthermore, he said, as it had rained earlier, cabbies chose not to drive.

     

    Unlike MRT trains or buses which run on schedule, rain or shine, taxi drivers work at their own time. So, adding more taxis on the road will not, in itself, meet demand.

     

    Taxi companies should address the issue of shortage when there are no peak-hour surcharges and when the weather turns bad. They should instil a certain degree of responsibility in the cabbies.

     

    Li Meixuan (Miss)

    Allow me to address a few points on this issue and also, what the future lies for TDs after the 2007 restructuring of fares.

    First, as some said, increase the fares of taxis and perhap scrap all these surcharges, more will join and even drive on Sunday, fair enough, because

    Based on the Labour Force Survey conducted in June, the median monthly income from work of full-time employed residents – Singaporeans and PRs – increased by 8.3 per cent to S$3,449 this year from the previous year. And this does not include the employer's CPF contribution, high Medical insurances, annual leaves, few month bonuses, MCs and all other perks in employment which can total up to $4 to 5k.

    So, if taxis driver are earning below that amount, you are consider under lower wage income group. And by this Decemeber, the median income will rise further.

    Secondly, taxis driver must harder to get above the median wage, and most are getting older by the years, it is not easy to drive, physically, can be stressful, it is not a 8 hrs job with 2 hours rest in between, if you do that, you surely fall far far below that median wage structure. For that kind of fares as of now, you do not attract people to be TD

    And thirdly, with an influential workforce, meaning a educated workforce, most youngs will not even think of TDs, and that further reduce the TD strength, as TD get older, they have to rest more, enjoy life more and relax more often. Remember TD are overnite businessman from an unemploy condition. And as businessman, it is all up to individual on the way they do business.

    That gives us a picture of a diminishing TD in near future, so unless the fares is to rise everyyear to match the median wages, the cost of living for TD is going to suffer, from high inflation to rentals and fuel, and that would reduce new intake and more throw keys, therefore the authority, have to look into it seriously, unless they got other plan, which is recruit foreigners, PRC or Indians to be TD here, then, that will be the end of TD for singaporeans.
