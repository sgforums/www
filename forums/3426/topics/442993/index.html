    Welcome Gagger

    I am currently using Garmin GPS

    opt for the singmal map....most accurate

    I am already quite an expert using GPS after being tekkan by the GPS several times

    after that you learn from mistake

    do not solely rely on the GPS...use it as a guide

    normally route wise, you will have 2 GPS guiding you

    one is mounted on the windscreen dashboard...the other is seated behind you

    opt for shortest dist in the option but do not follow what the GPS directs you

    for example for travelling in expressway, the GPS will anyhow direct you to exit

    for example if you are not familiar with Tampines,

    press GO only when exiting the highway towards Tampines

    GPS will direct you the shortest dist to the destination

    If you press too early....the GPS will announce loudly

    "re-calculation"...."re-calculation" if you do not follow what the GPS directs you

    then you look at the rear mirror, you will see pax looking uncomfortable

    Going thru the TVL course, you should have a Sg Map - one side shows city map the other side shows map of whole sg. Study this map carefully, the entrances and exits of all major expressway, where the expressway will lead you too, how the expressways are linked. For example, woodlands can go via SLE, BKE, and bukit timah road....This map is the most useful in my opinion, more useful than the road directory

    Using Garmin GPS has another advantage, if pax says blk 124 bedok

    you just key in "124"....124 bedok will appear for you to select

    I learn most of my routes thru pax.....so dun be afraid

    go out and drive and just learn from mistake

    This is how experience comes by
