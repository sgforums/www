    Diesel engines use compression to ignite fuel, thus no spark plugs required. In petrol engines, spark plugs will be in used during idling as sparking is required to ignite fuel. 

    In neutral or at park gear there's negligible difference in fuel consumption, when in drive gear it'll consume more during waiting. Disengaging from drive gear to neutral during waiting will improve in overall fuel consumption but will lead to more wear n tear to gearbox especially when one engage to park gear. Some European cars will automatically disengage gear to neutral to idle during waiting time at traffic lights to be more fuel efficient but will not engage park. 

    Depending on driver's concern...  Whether to save fuel or save on maintenance. Hope this clears up our newbie's question :)
