    Same suspect in two cab robberies
    Posted: 16 December 2009 0651 hrs

     
     
    Photos 1 of 1

    A CCTV image of the suspect in the two taxi robberies. (Photo: Singapore Police Force)
       
     

     

     

    SINGAPORE: Police are appealing for information on a man believed to be able to help with investigations into two taxi robberies in August.

    He is believed to be in his late 20s to early 30s, about 1.7m tall, fair, with shoulder-length black hair. Anyone with information can call the police hotline at 1800-255-0000; information will be kept confidential.

    On Aug 23 at about 9.35pm, a 34-year-old taxi driver picked up a man in front of a coffeeshop at Hougang Ave 1. Along Kim Chuan Road, the passenger grabbed the driver from behind, placed a chopper against his neck and demanded his valuables.

    The victim complied and was told to alight. The suspect drove off in the taxi.

    A day later at 11.10pm, a 43-year-old taxi driver picked up a passenger – believed to be the same man – along Punggol Road. While they were travelling along Baker Street, the passenger told the cabbie to stop the taxi and, wielding a chopper, demanded his valuables.

    The victim, too, was told to get out of the taxi which the suspect fled in. According to both victims, the suspect wore a surgical mask.

    Besides urging taxi drivers to be more vigilant and adopt crime prevention measures such as installing a CCTV in the cab, the police also advised them not to resist if they are confronted by a robber, and to note the suspect's features and activate the distress alarm where possible.
