    May 9, 2011 - Straits Times

    By Adeline Chia

    NATIONAL Solidarity Party's Marine Parade team went on a thank-you walkabout in Serangoon and Macpherson on Sunday afternoon.

    The star of the show, as usual, was 24-year-old advertising executive Nicole Seah, who was dressed in a cowl neck pink top with camel harem pants and gladiator sandals.

    Her other team mates, team leader Ivan Yeo, 63, businessman Cheo Chai Chen, 60, project director Spencer Ng, 31, and warehouse assistant Abdul Salim Harun, 29, were dressed in orange T-shirts.

    The group scored 43.4 per cent against the People's Action Party team led by Senior Minister Goh Chok Tong, and the afternoon walkabout was to 'express our heartfelt gratitude for the residents of Marine Parade GRC', Mr Yeo said.

    Ms Seah, whose more than 90,000 'likes' on Facebook means she has surpassed Minister Mentor Lee Kuan Yew as the most popular Singaporean politician online, was the biggest draw during the brief event.

    Supporters asked to take a photograph with her, while other well-wishers told her that she should contest in a single seat ward in the next election.

    May 9, 2011

    What voters want next

     

    ONE myth this General Election has debunked is that Singaporeans care only about bread-and-butter issues and not loftier ideals like providing a check against the Government, political diversity and democracy.

    As an Aljunied resident, it is clear to me that the People's Action Party has run the constituency well, but yet faltered in the face of the Workers' Party's call for a First World Parliament.

    While the PAP has led us to a First World economy, can it now facilitate Singapore's transition to a mature democracy with diverse voices?

    Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong's post-election reference to parties who can work together offers hope that the Government will guide, rather than block, this process.

    While the PAP may lose more seats in the transitional process, Singapore is larger and more important than the PAP. So Singaporeans are watching whether the PAP can 'play fair' going forward.

    The opposition has done well but it needs to grow to the next level. If it wants to win the trust of the voters and obtain a third of the seats in the House, which will make the opposition an effective check, acting as an alternative voice alone is not enough. In the next two or three general elections, the opposition must show voters that it can run the country.

    Workers' Party leader Low Thia Khiang rose to Minister Mentor Lee Kuan Yew's challenge to win a GRC. Can he now rise to MM Lee's next challenge of forming a shadow Cabinet?

    There seems to be a nucleus of an alternative leadership whose members are spread out within different parties.

    Apart from Mr Low and fellow WP MPs-elect Sylvia Lim and Chen Show Mao, there are the Singapore Democratic Party's Dr Vincent Wijeysingha as well as the National Solidarity Party's Mrs Hazel Poa and Mr Tony Tan Lay Thiam, and the Singapore People's Party's Mr Jimmy Lee and Mr Benjamin Pwee, to name a few.

    Can they work together and take on the PAP?

    Gerald Kok
