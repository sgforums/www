    Vote opposition and you may get a weaker govt: Ng Eng Hen
    By Karamjit Kaur
    Education Minister Ng Eng Hen (centre) greets residents during a walkabout in Bishan-Toa Payoh GRC. Dr Ng cautioned Singaporeans that by voting for the opposition, they may unintentionally weaken the Government and perhaps even cause a change in Government. -- ST PHOTO: MUGILAN RAJASEGERAN

     

    EDUCATION Minister Ng Eng Hen on Saturday cautioned Singaporeans that by voting for the opposition, they may unintentionally weaken the Government and perhaps even cause a change in Government.

    He urged voters to realise this because 'freak results' can happen, as they have in other countries.

    Speaking during a walkabout in Bishan-Toa Payoh GRC on Saturday morning, he said the system already allows for alternative voices in Parliament.

    If Singaporeans go to the May 7 polls thinking that more opposition would help further their interests, the outcome could be 'dangerous', warned Dr Ng.

    'Can a weak government deliver to him better transportation, better homes, better healthcare? And if you vote just to weaken the government, how can that help you, your own interests?' he added.
