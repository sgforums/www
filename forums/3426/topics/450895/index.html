    This is a judgement made by CJ Chan Sek Kiong in a case involving a taxi driver. The driver had noticed that his passenger had left property totalling 11k in the front seat after dropping him off in front of his house. The TD then 'seized' the property and sold it. He got 3 weeks jail. The CJ stated:

    A taxi driver is in a special position vis-à-vis his passenger. The taxi driver provides a transport service to the passenger for a fee and a passenger, in purchasing the service, not only entrusts the safety of his person but also custody of his property to the taxi driver during the journey. If the taxi driver finds lost property in the taxi, he should return it to the passenger if he knows who he is and where he lives. If he does not have such knowledge, he should place the goods within a reasonable time with the taxi company. At the very least, a taxi driver has a legal obligation not to take his passenger’s property, and in my opinion, this duty should be enforced strictly and vigorously.
