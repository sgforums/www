    Moral of the story, more mrt lines doesn't necessarily mean better connectivity if commuters can't fit into the platform. Since time immemorial, there's demand for personalize transport and it'll continue. During ancient times we have human sedan, then came the horses and we have horse carriages and eventually came automotives. Ford made it affordable for the masses and many would have thought that'll spell the end of many coachmen. To fill the void of personalize transport, coachmen evolve into what we call today taxi drivers.  

    Should the new mrt lines make a heavy dent to your income, those that are not serious will throw keys first followed by botherline drivers. Cabbies generate so much income for taxi coys, do u think they will sit idle and die with us? Imagine there's 20-30% idle cabs lying at their yards, storage in itself will kill them not to mention loss of revenue. They will have some brainstorming sessions with LTA/NTA and what have you to come out with some measures to entice drivers back. Eventually the only way to entice drivers back will be more carrots which is fare increase. Gov already spelled out our transport roadmap for the future, which is less private cars n encourage more to take public transport. Taxi is an essential part of our  public transport system plan. 

    To all my beloved brethrens who are worried, I'll say let's not worry too much and do what you need to do and that is just drive.
