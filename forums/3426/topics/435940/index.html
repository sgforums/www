    There is some unwritten notices or will never be wirtten for the commuter, but it is a good general knowledge to know,

    1) Which route you prefer - when asked, don't get annoyed. "uncle" only follow standing order to protect both interest. There are just too much argument arise that is unnecessary and most importantly, it is not leading to any good for both parties when destination are not further affirm with which route.

    2) Shortest and Fastest Route - When you give such instruction, it is equal to "go by your way". Such instruction are telling the driver to drive to THE BEST OF HIS KNOWLEDGE, his knowledge is not equal to your knowledge or GPS knowledge.

    3) Go by xxxx expressway - there are many routes to lead to that expressway, and there are alternative exit to reach your destination, be precise.

    4) Get your route ready - Do your homework first, find out your desirable route and translate your preferences precisely to the driver.

    Too troublesome? When the fares will trouble you, it is that troublesome.
