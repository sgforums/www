    Drunk man bashes cabby over $20 fare

    By Elysa Chen

    SWOLLEN and bruised eyes, cuts on the lips, and bruises on his head and all over his body - a cabby suffered these injuries just for tapping the shoulder of his passenger and asking him for the fare.

    And not only did the taxi driver fail to get the $20 fare, what ensued was a 10-minute chase as he desperately tried to flee from his passenger, who was in a drunken rage.

    The cabby, Mr Ong Bon Lai, 63, ended up being brutally punched and kicked by the passenger, a man half his age.

    The assault, which occurred at about 4am on Saturday, stopped only when two other cabbies who saw the incident rushed to Mr Ong's rescue.

    Mr Ong had earlier picked up two men at the Esplanade and driven them to Petir Road. One of them, a 29-year-old, was drunk.

    When the man and his friend got off the cab near Block 201 at Petir Road, in the Bukit Panjang area, Mr Ong tried to get them to pay the fare.

    However, the man's friend walked away while the man was throwing up at the side of the road.

    So Mr Ong tapped the man's shoulder and said in Mandarin: "Give me the taxi fare."

    But the drunk man swung around suddenly and hit Mr Ong in the chest with his left fist.
