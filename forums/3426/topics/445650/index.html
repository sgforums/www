SMRT, Smart Cab & Prime Taxi fail in booking svc standard

  • QX179R
    17 Jan 12, 22:08

    SINGAPORE: Three taxi companies - SMRT Taxis, Smart Cab and Prime Taxi - have fallen short in their services for radiophone booking.

    This is based on the latest quality of service (QoS) standards by the Land Transport Authority (LTA), covering areas such as answer rate, cater rate and the time taken to answer incoming calls and confirm taxi, as well as the time passengers waited for the taxis to arrive.

    Between October and November 2011, SMRT Taxis failed in the call answer rate.

    Smart Cab and Prime Taxi failed in the cater rate, waiting time for despatch centre to confirm the taxi from the time the call is answered, and passenger waiting time for the taxi.

    In response to queries from Channel NewsAsia, SMRT Taxis' acting deputy director of Customer Relations, Eunice Lui, said the taxi operator did not meet its call answer rate during the two months because of "capacity issues" with its phone lines.

    She said SMRT Taxis has raised the problem with the telco.

    She added that they're working to resolve the problem.

    SMRT Taxis is also working on more automated booking channels for customers to book its taxis with ease and to improve standards.

    Smart Cab and Prime Taxi were not available for comment.

    The other categories of the QoS standards are safety and taxi drivers' conduct.

    In safety, TransCab, SMRT Taxis, Smart Cab and Prime Taxi failed for accident rate.

    As for taxi drivers' conduct, Prime Taxi failed in the offence rate of its taxi drivers.

    Transport Minister Lui Tuck Yew said in parliament on Tuesday that his ministry will look closely at the service quality of taxis and see what needs to be improved.
