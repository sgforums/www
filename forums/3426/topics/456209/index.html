First 9 Months as OMO TD.

  • Centori1965
    25 Jul 12, 23:20
    I have been silently following this forum for the last 9 months. It is really nice to see fellow TDs sharing their experience with newbies. I became a TD in Nov 2011. I have this to share with fellow TDs here. Why i became a TD i think it's easy to guess. I am 47 liao. I started as a relief for 1 month to see if I am suited to be a TD and also to ease the burden of having to bear full rental as a newbie.. Thereafter, i became OMO. Initially, i had to drive 14 to 15 hrs to earn what i needed to earn. I used to get frustrated when cannot get pax. Today after 9 months, i drive about 12 to 13 hrs and earn the same amount as when i first started. I no longer get frustrated as eventually i will find pax. Whether street PNG or Taxi Stand. I start at 7am and home by 8:30 to 9pm every nite except Fri. Fri I drive longer (usually up to 12:30am to take at least 1 midnite trip) to cover for Sat rental so I can stay home Sat. Sat nite, i drive abt 4 hrs to cover for Sun rental. Sun itself i also drive few hours (usually 4 hrs) in the evening to earn a bit. Basically, Sat n Sun i stand-down. Personally, i see this slightly shorter driving hrs for same $$ as an improvement. Plus I dun need to sacrifice family time. I am well aware of the pitfalls of OMOing. However, I think I am managing it quite well at the moment. Whether I can last, nobody knows. Live each day as it comes. At least now when i sleep, i dun have to worry abt project deadlines, powerpoint slides, statistics and all that corporate shit. Not only TDs suffer from health problems. Where I worked, I have heard of 2 deaths of colleagues who collapse at their workstation without regaining consciousness. My boss was hospitalized twice due to heart attack. Thats all. Thanks for reading. I was wondering, if i should join LV?? Any opinions?
