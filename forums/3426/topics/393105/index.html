    Taxi drivers use walkie—talkies to share information & increase earnings

    Channel NewsAsia - Monday, March 8
    Taxi drivers use walkie-talkies to share information & increase earnings

     

    SINGAPORE: As a result of the economic downturn last year, taxi drivers say they have been earning less overall.

    However, some are finding new ways to up earnings by sharing information on assignments.

    Cabbies engaged with passengers are now using walkie—talkies to pass on new assignments to fellow drivers.

    Even though taxi drivers’ earnings have gone up for two consecutive quarters last year from S$166 to S$180 per day, this is still less than what they were earning in 2008.

    Taxi drivers such as Lee Say Keong have set up the network with 14 others.

    Normally, they get their assignments from taxi companies, which charge 50 cents for each call answered.

    Now they save on this amount, which translates to about S$30 more earnings for each driver monthly.

    "Some other people might think it’s a very small amount, but for (us) taxi drivers, it’s very important. We don’t have CPF, we don’t have any incentive, and we don’t have any other bonuses," Mr Lee said.

    Besides sharing information, the system also allows them to look out for one another.

    — CNA/yb
