    Economic is not as good as last time, so you tends to see a downward trends in your earning, unless you put in more hours, but that will be extra work and stress.

    When expatriates first arrive here, they spent happily, then end up monthly see less money, after some time, they become like locals, and spent like locals, so you will see less tips, and also less flagging for cab when there are MRT and buses.

    But not to worry, our land is still a haven for most rich peoples and certainly, a good tourist spot, and as we increase our main population to 6 millions plus the ever increases of tourists and those foreigners' relatives visiting our PRs, new citizen, WP holders and expatriates here, transportation industrial is still very much a booming one both short and long term in concern.

    However, thriving in Taxi driving are alway compensated by the ever increase of rental and fuel prices with newer cabs, and that is where TD earning is still very much in stalemate whereas Taxi companies are booming with good annual profitable reports.

    The end result is therefore, for a TD, who is the frontline customer servicemen, the driver, the user and at the same time, the renter and the fuel user, would end up only having a faster vehicle, better seating, more comfy interior, but still have to hold steering while taking in almost the same earning if not less.
