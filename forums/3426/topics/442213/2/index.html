    Experiences and skills are 2 different perspectives, experiences come with years of driving, the know where, how, what and when, skill is more on the driving, which can be train similarly in a few months, like defensive driving, but experiences take on individual differently.

    I am personallly very delighted in taxi trade because of it comaraderie spirit, as a freelance, unlike all other jobs I worked and still working, most do not expose their experiences and skills to newbies, to them, newbies kill their jobs. When i downloading new cars at west coast, newbies goes blur as to where to park and how to go up back ship, end up only downloaded a few cars, and the boss paid by each car downloading. When I go ship repair, or as dock master, newbies have hard time moving around the ship and do even know when and what to do with engine and where to repair, open, close, and so on. But once the newbies start to learn the hard way, just like me during my newbie times, the old ones get outcasted for being slow and lots of complaints.

    However in taxi trade, it is a complete 180 degree turn, fellow taxis teach each other and newbies to get adapt, which is good for a kick start. Bravo to all brothers for the selfless contributions and advices.
