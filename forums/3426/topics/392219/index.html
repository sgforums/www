  • Sgcabby
    28 Feb 10, 20:10

    1. Hirer or Relief

    Unless I fail my primary school maths or I really went bonkers, I find that there is almost no figurative (means numbers-wise, quantitative/not qualitative) difference between becoming a Hirer and just staying a Relief. It all boils down to just a matter of personal preference, habit or lifestyle..like some prefer cool nights, some prefer daytime clearer, some prefer weekends or alternate days.

    2. Queue or Dont Queue

    There is again almost no difference, except some prefer to stay put save petrol enjoy aircon read papers do some housekeeping, some prefer to skip boring queues rove around try luck.

    3. Short Distance or Long Distance Pickup

    I also find there is almost no difference whether a passenger hop on and says go 3 blocks down the road, or wants to do a cross country stamp passport. Actually its all just up to the cabby's own mentality, in his/her head, whether he likes (accepts) it or not. Sure Long Distance sounds nice to everyone, but treat it as an unexpected surprise, not an expected norm, that makes Life alot easier. My mindset to queue is to guarantee pick up Any Passenger, not to guarantee pick up a Long Distance Passenger. I left that to Lady Luck BEFORE I decided to join the queue.

     

    Maybe I am still relatively new at this (or maybe my experiences are different), but honestly the above are what I had observed repeatedly and can feel quite confidently about. Do share your experiences if they are different, but these are still just my personal opinions.
