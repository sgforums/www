    Singapore: A scheme to raise the healthcare savings of taxi drivers in Singapore has kicked off.

    Under the scheme called 'Drive And Save' (DAS), principal taxi hirers who contribute 50 cents a day into their Medisave Accounts get to enjoy a co-contribution of another 50 cents from their taxi companies.

    All this adds up to $30 a month in the taxi driver's Medisave Account, which totals around $360 in Medisave contributions for each principal hirer each year.

    In addition, eligible members can even receive some $1,800 in the form of Workfare Income Supplement (WIS).

    According to the NTUC, the Medisave co-contributions made by the taxi companies and Workfare, will incentivise drivers to contribute regularly to their Medisave Account and save for their future healthcare needs.

    Describing taxi drivers as an important group of self-employed persons, the
    Chairman for the DAS Working Committee, Seng Han Thong pointed out that the DAS enhances the social safety net of principal hirer taxi drivers, who are an important group of self-employed persons.

    "Medisave is one of the key benefits that all working Singaporeans enjoy. Taxi drivers are no different from other Singaporeans and should enjoy Medisave contributions as well" he said.

    "After several years of lobbying by the various taxi associations, we are heartened that our taxi drivers can also enjoy Medisave contributions from the company from which they hire their taxi" said Mr Seng who is also NTUC Assistant Secretary-General.

    A Memorandum of Understanding was signed last July between the National Taxi Association and seven taxi companies (CityCab, Comfort, Premier, Prime, Smart, SMRT and Trans-Cab), to realise the healthcare savings scheme.

    Encouraging taxi drivers to be part of DAS, Mr Yang Ban Seng, CEO (Taxi) of ComfortDelGro & CityCab, said his company is setting aside $2.8 million a year to match the Medisave contributions.

    This, he said, is on top of existing benefits for that ComfortDelGro & CityCab drivers already enjoy.

    As for Trans-Cab, General Manager Ms Jasmine Tan said, "currently, our company is giving cash benefits to our taxi hirers ranging from $400 to $700 per month. The CPF co-contributions by the company under the DAS scheme will be on top of the existing cash benefits.

    "We hope this will help to lighten the financial burden of our taxi drivers in building up their Medisave to cover for their medical needs and their families too."

    The scheme is expected to benefit 25,000 principal hirers.

    The seven taxi companies will collectively co-contribute more than $4 million a year to the drivers' Medisave accounts.
