    For those completely never drive before and now starting as a TD, GPS is sure to be helpful but not a dependable tool as yet.

    I take into an analogy of cooking, a completely new cook will cook a lousy soup, because he followed the book too closely and may had missed either on some ingredient or the temperature settings. He gets commented, scolded, or even criticised, if he takes it as a part and parcel of a learning journey, he move on and cook again, and again until perfection or near perfection.

    In TD, the first GPS are the pax, unlike delivery or bus, when destinations are pre planned, TD journey is not pre planned, this you have to understand, as newbie, there will be sudden shock and stress because of new directions in every pick up or call up. For every road lead to Rome, but which is the best is also critical and for that matter, the paxs are the best peoples to ASK first.

    Taxi courses, no matter what \

    practical or theory, they never teach you how to ask the paxs, how to gel with the paxs, how to get into the groove with the paxs ending up with a nice journey. They teach you more on the rules and regulations, the road directions, prominent locations and discipline. Eg from TP to Regent hotel, the road is TP ave 5, PIE< steven<orange groove and so on..but what if you are from CCK?, so, would say, the course is not flexible, it gives no room for creative thinking.

    GPS is best use when paxs do notknow the way, you also do not know the way, stop a while, go thru it, then on meter and move on without looking at it.

    Lesson 1, ask pax first, if he or she knows the way, best,
