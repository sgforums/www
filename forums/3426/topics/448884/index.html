  • JoeRaj

    Put simply I think, if the accident is clear cut and the TD isn't at fault, and the other party and their insurance  co, will agree to a quick settlement, then the camera can be useful to verify this.

    But if TD at fault or not at fault, but the process is gonna be long because other party has some 'excuses' and neither he nor his insurance will settle quickly and easily, then the camera ' tak pakai' in the insurance company's view.

    And the bottomline line really is, the camera's images or recording is only valid, if the case goes to court and the court agrees to admit it into evidence. If it doesn't go to court then it's only an investigation tool, that also if TP and the insurance company agree to it.
