    Cabby's story - This Article came out on ST forum Today. 

    'We have to give ourselves a break as and when necessary so we do not fall sick.'

    MR SID CHIA: 'I am a taxi driver who has been working for the past 15 years and I sympathise with Miss Li Meixuan who had to wait 11/2 hours for a cab ('Trying time waiting for a cab'; Wednesday). Unfortunately, it is true that on Sundays, there are fewer taxis on the road as most drivers take time off to be with their families. Cabbies are self-employed and we adhere to the rules laid down by the Land Transport Authority and taxi companies. Like other businessmen, we have to take care of ourselves first. We do not enjoy medical benefits or medical leave. We do not have our daily rental waived when we fall sick, unless we are hospitalised. So we have to give ourselves a break as and when necessary so we do not fall sick. As for not driving on rainy days, it is a no-brainer - accidents are too costly. And all cabbies are given the option to pick up or not to pick up passengers - as long as they have a legitimate reason.'
