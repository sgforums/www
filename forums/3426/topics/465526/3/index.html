    Of course that driver made some mistakes by never on the meter etc but 1 thing stood out in the judgment (and even on appeal to the High Court - judgment upheld), the Law is very clear, only authorised agencies can charge a booking fee, in this case - the taxi company upon approval by LTA. No private agreement whether lesser or more tan the normal fare is allowed.  A taxi driver can give a discount or not collect any fees, if he wants to provided it's between him and the pax directly, not thru  an unauthorised 3rd party - this case the hotel. But it has to be less not more and must on the meter reflecting the actual fare before discount.

    Does LV have approval to charge this extra from LTA besides the normal booking charge, since after all the cabs are driven from those belonging to companies who have rules and approval only for the normal booking fee?
