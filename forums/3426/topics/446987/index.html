  • Bingjames
    10 Feb 12, 15:01

    Want to know why people protect their turf(rice bowl) like vulture?

    I learnt it the hard way.

    I used to run a food business. At the peak, I got a factory and 3 outlets and supply to hotels and restaurants too. 

    I was even selected for the Singapore Day, where the government gathers the very best of hawkers food to a certain destination of the year selected, to let Singapore living overseas, working, studying or migrated there. To let them have a taste of local food, to entice them to return home.

    Just imagine, the very best of local food. I am rubbing shoulders with the best kuay chap, best laksa, best chilli crab, best fried kuay teow.

    This is the highest form of recognition in my eight years career but also leads to my downfall.

    While I am there for 10 days, I assign the daily work to a China worker whom I trusted and taught him all my secret recipes.

    As this China worker is going home after I return from my trip, I wouldn't mind him using these skills to earn a living in this home town.

    Only when I came back and he went home did I found out that while I am away, this guys sold the recipes I taught him to my very own customer, my major customer.

    Just for $800, I lost a 5 figure revenue every month and cannot find another one to replace this, drag on for 6 months and incurring more debts and eventually forced to cut lost when the landlord of my factory locked up the premise for non payment for rent for just one month.

    Have been down for a good 3 years and had real difficulties trying to come up again until this TD job.

    I used to be very generous in this sharing aspect as I always feel like I want to be the same as Macdonalds, you can copy for all you want, I will always create new and better ones - as long as I am around. I learnt my lesson the hard way.
