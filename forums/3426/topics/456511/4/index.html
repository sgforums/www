    If you drive on a one lane road below speed limit, it is not road hogging. If you drive on a one lane road above speed limit, it is speeding.

    If you drive on a two or three lane road, the outermost lane (Lane 1) is for overtaking only. You can drive below the speed limit, or at the speed limit, on Lane 2 or Lane 3. No road hogging here. If you drive above the speed limit on Lane 2 or 3, it is speeding.

    If you drive above the speed limit on Lane 1 to overtake a vehicle following the speed limit on Lane 2, it is not speeding. If you remain in Lane 1 at the speed limit or even above the speed limit, it is road hogging + speeding.

    Lane 1 is for overtaking only. Even if speed limit is 90kmh and you are travelling at 100kmh or more on Lane 1, is considered road hogging or to make it more clear, Lane 1 obstruction.

    Move to lane 2 to avoid obstructing other drivers driving way beyond the speed limit at 180 kmh or higher. Let them pass you.
