    SINGAPORE: One-cent coins are no longer in circulation and most goods and services are charged in whole figures to reduce the hassle of consumers not having enough loose change for payment. But that has not been the case when it comes to cab fares and some passengers are calling for more transparency. That's because taxi companies in Singapore differ in their guidelines on fare calculations. ComfortDelGro has automatically programmed its cab meters to round down the fare to the nearest five cents, while SMRT encourages its drivers to collect the full fare by accepting cashless payments. Some passengers Channel NewsAsia spoke to said they have encountered instances when their fare was rounded up to the nearest ten cents. But most cab drivers said they charge a few cents lower than the actual amount to avoid disputes with passengers. - CNA/ck http://www.channelnewsasia.com/stories/singaporelocalnews/view/1188530/1/.html
