    Ziyi snubbed by boutique
    By Diana Othman
    Gan acknowledged that Zhang generally believes the services in Singapore to be 'fabulous' and 'everyone is so polite and thoughtful.' -- PHOTO: ALAN LIM

    IT SEEMS that even superstars can't get the star treatment once shops close their doors.

    International film star Zhang Ziyi was turned away by a high-end boutique while shopping in Singapore two weekends ago as it had closed at 6pm, at about the time Zhang and celebrity hairstylist David Gan walked up to its doorsteps.

    This incident and snippets of Zhang's three-day visit to Singapore over the Hari Raya Puasa weekend were revealed in Gan's blog on Sept 23.

    Gan said Zhang was down town to dine, shop and relax after completing a shoot for a programme on the National Geographic in Borneo, Indonesia.

    She and Gan were touring Singapore and over that weekend, had gone shopping at the Hilton Shopping Gallery on Orchard Road.

    Wrote Gan on his blog: 'They rejected an international celebrity because of a technicality? I'm not saying that they had to let us in because Ziyi is an international star but rejecting her would give the brand and the service industry in Singapore a bad name.'

    Gan then went on to suggest that the staff at the boutique should be trained in hospitality to improve 'communication' and service to customers.

    He added that the management should also 'choose and train floor staff properly before sending them out to the real world.'

    But Gan acknowledged that Zhang generally believes the services in Singapore to be 'fabulous' and 'everyone is so polite and thoughtful.'

    'But...there's always that 1 per cent in 100 per cent that would bring down a good name of the entire industry,' he added.
