    Zoe names baby 'Brayden'

    By Yvonne Kwok
    MEDIA REPORTER
    ZOE Tay claims she is not superstitious, but when it comes to choosing a name for her baby boy and taking care of herself, the actress is leaving nothing to chance.

    'I'm not superstitious and my husband also said not to be superstitious, so that's why during my pregnancy, you didn't see me buying 4D or doing any investments,' said the MediaCorp artiste yesterday.

    She gave birth to a 3.61kg boy, her firstborn, on Feb 24, two weeks early.

    She said her husband, air force pilot Philip Chionh, 37, had picked an English name of Celtic origins for their son - Brayden, which means bravery.

    But Tay, 37, is still mulling over several Chinese names a fortune teller had suggested based on the boy's time and date of birth.

    Besides consulting a fortune teller on the child's name, she also believes in traditional confinement practices prescribed by Chinese custom.

    She has not bathed since the delivery and keeps her bedroom windows closed and room draft-free.

    She even refrained from having any air-conditioning despite the recent heat wave.

    She believes this will prevent water retention as well as ward off any aches and pains that may occur later in life.

    Tay, who wore a mint-green Marni dress and looked radiant, was speaking at a press conference held at The Pines.

    She said an Indonesian masseuse is massaging her body back into shape using jamu, a traditional Indonesian herbal medicine.

    Tay, whose mother and a confinement nanny are staying with her to help take care of the baby, said: 'A friend came into my bedroom and told me it was like an oven but even then I still wear socks so that I don't catch a chill.

    'I've been sweating a lot every day, but I wipe myself off regularly with a towel and change my clothes at least 10 times a day.'

    She dismissed talk that she was superstitious and relying on old wives' tales, saying: 'We shouldn't disbelieve what old people say. I'm still Chinese after all and we are different in mind and body from Westerners.'

    Tay, who gained 15kg over the course of her pregnancy and hasn't weighed herself after the delivery, is also relying on a special post-natal girdle her husband gave her which she says will trim centimetres off her belly.

    'I've worn it for a few days and even though I still have a bit of fat around the belly, it's really good for compressing the tummy.'

    Although it is still early days, her managers are already in talks with several companies for potential endorsements.

    Though she has agreed to endorse milk powder Similac Follow On for babies aged six months and over, she said she nurses Brayden every three hours and will continue to breastfeed him for as long as she can.

    She hopes her next child will be a girl, although she is not sure when she will have another child or how many more she will have.

    'I don't know if I will have my next child next year, but I can't wait too long because I'm quite old already.'
