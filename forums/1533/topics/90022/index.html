    2046 is too late

    LONDON - Hong Kong film-maker Wong Kar Wai's new movie, 2046, is officially not finished yet.

    It will not be closing the Edinburgh International Film Festival as planned, organisers said.

    A version was screened at the Cannes Film Festival in France in May, but Edinburgh organisers said the final print would not be ready by closing night on Aug 29.

    'Ultimately, this is just one of the pitfalls of working with eccentric artistic geniuses,' said festival artistic director Shane Danielsen.

    Mr Hamish McAlpine, managing director of distributor Tartan Films, told the Guardian: 'Having seen a recent edit in Hong Kong, the film is getting better day by day.'

    Wong, acclaimed for such films as In The Mood For Love, is notorious for working without scripts, then editing until the last possible moment.

    2046, described as a sci-fi follow-up to In The Mood For Love, stars Tony Leung Chiu Wai (inset), Faye Wong, Zhang Ziyi and Takuya Kimura.

    Rescheduled to close the festival is Untold Scandal, a South Korean remake of Dangerous Liaisons by director E.J. Yong and starring Bae Yong Jun. -- AFP
