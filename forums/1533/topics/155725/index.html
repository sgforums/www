    Official Website

    Flying at 40,000 feet in a cavernous, state-of-the-art E-474 aircraft, Kyle Pratt (Foster) faces every mother's worst nightmare when her six-year-old daughter Julia vanishes without a trace mid-flight from Berlin to New York. Already emotionally devastated by the unexpected death of her husband, Kyle desperately struggles to prove her sanity to the disbelieving flight crew and passengers while facing the very real possibility that she may be losing her mind. While neither the plane's Captain Rich (Sean Bean), nor Air Marshal Gene Carson (Peter Sarsgaard) want to doubt the bereaved widow, all evidence indicates that her daughter was never on board resulting in paranoia and doubt among the passengers and crew of the plane. Finding herself desperately alone, Kyle can only rely on her own wits to solve the mystery and save her daughter.

    Comments on the movie from other forums...

    I've heard plenty of bad reviews on Flightplan, and went into the theatre with low expectations, but the movie really turned out to be pretty interesting.

    Starring Academy Award winner Jodie Foster, she plays a bereathed mother who at 30,000 feet in the air, loses her child on board a flight to America from Germany. And nobody on board can remember seeing her child. And to make things worse, her child was never recorded to have ever boarded the plane. Throughout the movie you begin to question her sanity, and start to draw suspects from the people on the flight. Jodie Foster brings a lot of emotion to her role, so much that you feel her anxiety and hurt. I'd label it a cross between The Forgotten and Red Eye, a movie you'd definitely like to catch if you're into a thrilling mystery.

    I give it 3 and 1/2 over 5 stars.
