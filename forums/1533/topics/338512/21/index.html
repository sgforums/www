    "The Little Nyonya" to be sold at television trade show in Cannes
    By Pearl Forss, Channel NewsAsia | Posted: 20 March 2009 1822 hrs

     
     
    Photos 1 of 1

       
     

     

     

    SINGAPORE: Singapore's hit TV series "The Little Nyonya", produced by MediaCorp, is going to France.

    The series will be sold to international broadcasters at the world's largest television trade show MIPTV in Cannes at the end of March.

    The series showcases the region's Peranakan heritage and stars Qi Yuwu and Jeanette Aw, who will also be in Cannes.

    The show has already been bought by broadcasters in Cambodia, China, Malaysia and Hong Kong.

    The effort at MIPTV is spearheaded by the Media Development Authority. A total of 10 companies will be there, with 41 television titles for sale or distribution.

    One title expected to do well at MIPTV is the cartoon "Dinosaur Train". It is made by Singaporean production house Big Communications in collaboration with The Jim Henson Company, which produced "Sesame Street" and "The Muppets".

    "Dinosaur Train" has already been purchased by broadcasters in Norway and Canada. TV stations from France, Australia and Germany have also expressed interest in this 40-episode animated series.
