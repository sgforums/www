    Review by New Paper - 18th November 2004 (by Wendy Teo)

    Mighty Zoe shines again

    How apt it is to get the da jie(big sister) of Caldecott Hill, Zoe Tay, to play the da jie of the underworld.

    She is an Ah Lian pirated CD vendor who is the "best" in humble Balestier, but is trained in martial arts and can spew durian seeds like a sharpshooter.

    In the new Channel 8 comedy My Mighty In-Laws, Zoe plays the terror of Balestier Yao Meimei, whose Phua Chu Kang-cloned dad died when she was very young.

    Meimei's elder sister Jiaojiao(Lin Mei Jiao) is her direct opposite, meek, fragile and a doormat.

    When Jiaojiao is stricken with an illness, she begs meimie to get married to the soft-spoken Dacheng(Rayson Tan) as a dying wish.

    After Meimei reluctantly agrees to her request, Jiaojiao, however, recovers miraculously.

    But tragedy strikes when Dacheng goes missing after trying to save a child from an accident.

    Inspiration for this comedy obviously came from the Korean comedy My Wife is a ganster, where a mobster leader is forced to marry a meek man because of her dying sister.

    But, instead of spiralling into copycat hell, the plot similarity ends there.

    My Mighty In-Laws is a quirky tale of Jiaojiao trying to "legitimise" herself when cupid strikes a second time.

    Zoe remains the most credible leading actress here, thanks to her willingness to sacrifice her looks and her glamorous image, to take on "ugly" roles with panache.

    Other actors have tried but failed, either because they are too self-conscious, or because they simply don't have that capability yet.

    Meijiao is also a hoot as the gentle Jiaojiao, who swoons at the mere sight of a potential suitor.
