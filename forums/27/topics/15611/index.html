How time flies

  • ken
    25 Dec 00, 13:04
    Hi to all my fellow AJcians who are lkg
    at my msg.

    I'm from the batch 34/87. So u can imagine
    how "old" i am now. I still reminisce those
    years as a carefree teenage, those first pangs of "puppy" love, skipping lectures and all those silly things that teens do to rebel.

    Life as an adult brings me independence and
    freedom, but it also brought me a lot of other things. I worry about where my "career" is heading, financial burdens,
    etc. To be frank, in times of despondence &
    despair, it was my happy childhood and those silly teenage years that sustain me. Looking
    back, i'd not have done a lot of things if
    i could do it all over again.

    So to all those out there who moans abt
    the college, yes, to a certain extent, the college sucks. esp now. it neither has a
    strong sport & esprit de corp culture nor
    sterling performance in the exams-always 6th
    in position and not moving an inch up... this is called stagnant. But then, yr life is what u make out of it. Enjoy your 2 years in AJC, it can't be all that bad. 2 years (in fact, it's even shorter)is a a short time and will passed by u in a twinkling of an eye. I should know. Study hard but play hard too. Get to know yr classmates better (i regret not knowing mine well enough).Very soon u will be initiated into adulthood. And
    a lot of other burdens will come yr way. So
    seize the day and live yr life in college to the fullest. Remeber u decide whether yr life in AJ is going to be a memorable one or
    a forgettable one - not the principal, not the sch... if the sch sucks, play a proactive role to jazz up with activities ... liaise with sch authorities etc.

    Your Senior
    YMH

    26 Dec 00, 23:15
    hi! im also a senior, from class of 95 (we donated the benches near the pond..hee..)

    me in NUS, graduating in half a year's time...NUS is fine, but i miss my days in AJC more. U are rather much alone in NUS, and u dun get to see the same faces so often...u have to find r own clique or else u are pretty much alone. I miss the days in AJ, when we have AJ carnival, sports day, swimming carnival, cross country..etc...yes, we may moan and grumble when we had to participate, but thinking back, i really miss those times...

    Its true that there is really nothing great abt AJC, neither excelling in sports or studies, somewhere always stuck in the middle...but i thk wat i miss most abt my JC days were the friends...they really made a difference to my time in school. its diff now, in the Uni, where people go about doing their own stuff, not much of 'comrade-ship', we are all rather individualistic now, quite difficult to find a group of kakis to have fun after school or even during school time.

    No matter how bad school was, my friends' presence always made school bearable

    The 2 years in AJC was gone so, so fast...its really up to each person to appreciate what u have now...i thk watever JC u go to, there is aways something sucky abt it...bt try to stop the grumbling and appreciate the moment now...it will be gone forever once u grad, and believe me...2 years is very very short a time...

    So, those still studying in AJC...enjoy youselves..wish i was in ur shoes man...
