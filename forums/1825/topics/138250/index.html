GIs' Murder of S. Korean Woman Assailed

  • ctstalin
    21 Jun 05, 21:53
    Pyongyang, June 18 (KCNA) -- A south Korean woman was killed by a U.S. army truck at a road intersection in Tongduchon, south Korea, a few days before the third anniversary of the murder of two schoolgirls by a U.S. armored car. The truth about the case has been brought to light. According to it, a representative of the Democratic Workers' Party and members of civic organizations in charge of the case conducted an investigation into the case. In the course of investigating the case, keeping in touch with the relevant police station, they have found out the fact that a GI on the truck saw that woman before the incident took place and its driver, too, felt something wrong before stopping the truck.
    This is quite different to the statements made by the two GI assailants at the outset of the investigation that they did not see any woman nor their truck hit her.
    Commenting on it, a Minju Joson news analyst today brands the case as a thrice-cursed brutal murder deliberately committed by the GIs present in south Korea. The news analyst further says:
    This case clearly proves once again that the U.S. imperialists are a group of murderers regarding human-killing as their job and pleasure with whom the Korean nation can never live under the same sky.
    The U.S. imperialists have kept south Korea under their occupation for more than half a century, inflicting all sorts of misfortune and sufferings upon the south Korean people. Not content with it, they are now seeking to bring a nuclear war disaster to the entire Korean nation.
    The reality proves that without getting the U.S. forces withdrawn from south Korea, is it impossible for the south Korean people to be free from misfortune and sufferings and for the Korean nation to avoid such nuclear war disaster.
    All the Koreans should turn out as one in the courageous anti-U.S. struggle to drive the U.S. imperialist aggression forces, a group of blood-thirsty man-killers, out of south Korea at an early date.
