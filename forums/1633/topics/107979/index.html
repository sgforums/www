    Man Utd vs C Palace preview

    Manchester United have injury concerns in attack ahead of Crystal Palace's visit to Old Trafford, live on Prem Plus.

    The Red devils' expensively assembled four-pronged strike-force could be reduced to just one member in the worst case scenario for manager Sir Alex Ferguson.

    Louis Saha, who scored in the Carling Cup victory over Palace last month, is definitely out with an ankle injury.

    Ruud van Nistelrooy is undergoing a scan on Friday on his troublesome calf complaint and is rated doubtful for the teatime tussle.

    Ferguson also has concerns over the fitness of Alan Smith who picked up a knock to his foot during Monday's 1-1 draw at Fulham.

    That could leave Ferguson with just Wayne Rooney as a striking option and it would be interesting to see if the England ace is allowed to lead the line against Palace.

    Kleberson is also missing with an ankle injury, while Cristiano Ronaldo and Gabriel Heinze are reportedly on a mid-season sabbatical.

    Heinze's absence, although it could be delayed until next week, would open the door for John O'Shea, while the likes of Darren Fletcher, Liam Miller and David Bellion will be hoping for a chance with Ronaldo back in Portugal.

    Palace, like United, will ring the changes from the team which was beaten at Old Trafford in the fourth round of the Carling Cup.

    Iain Dowie's selection problems are not as severe as his opposite number, with Wayne Andrews aiming to shake off a flu bug.

    Tommy Black is definitely missing with a broken leg, along with Nicola Ventola, but Gonzalo Sorondo could return to the squad after a hamstring injury.

    The Eagles' sole victory away from home in The Premiership this season was at Birmingham, when Andy Johnson's breakaway goal settled the match.

    Dowie is likely to again deploy Johnson as a lone striker, with Palace operating with a five-man midfield designed to frustrate the hosts.

    BIG-MATCH FACTS

    MANCHESTER UNITED     are unlikely to feel too generous to the less well off this Christmas, when fourth bottom Crystal Palace visit the Red devils, who are fourth top. Indeed, while victory will see Sir Alex Ferguson take closer order on the top three, they cannot climb any higher before the festive break. Going into this game United have won exactly 300 matches and scored 950 goals in Premier League history.

    The eight times champions were on course for a fifth successive Premiership victory when Pape Bouba Diop equalised for Fulham with three minutes remaining at Craven Cottage on Monday. But United have only been beaten once in the League since their opening fixture. They've undefeated in any competition at home, have gone six Premiership matches without a loss and have picked up 14 points from the last 18 available, rising from seventh in early November to a top four berth now. However they're still nine points off the pace, and four points behind champions Arsenal.

    United have only dropped two points in six Premiership meetings with Crystal Palace. They have won three times as many League games as they have lost against the south London club (18 to six), and hold a 100% home Premiership record against them.

    CRYSTAL PALACE have been slipping steadily while United have been moving in the opposite direction. Indeed the form of Iain Dowie's side is the antithesis of the Red devils. The Eagles have not won in six Premiership games, claiming just three points from the 18.

    The Londoners visit the Theatre of Dreams hovering above the drop zone on goal difference. They've won just three Premiership matches under Dowie, and finished all square five times. Another draw here will bring their all-time total of top flight stalemates to 150.

    The Eagles have lost all five previous League visits to Old Trafford without scoring a single goal. They have only won twice in 15 visists in their entire history. The most recent of those was 1-2 in the top flight on 9 December 1989.

    Man Utd vs Crystal Palace

    Sir Alex Ferguson is willing to write Ruud van Nistelrooy out of Manchester United's Premiership title push for the next two months to ensure he is ready for their assault on Europe.

    Van Nistelrooy has been sent for a scan on the calf problem which first surfaced six weeks ago and has kept the Dutch striker sidelined for the last five games.

    Despite the rest, Ferguson is no nearer learning the extent of the injury and is fearful of causing further damage which may affect Van Nistelrooy's Achilles.

    And though his side's title ambitions are hanging by a thread following Monday's 1-1 draw at Fulham, Ferguson is looking towards the mouth-watering Champions League clash with six-time champions AC Milan in February as the time he really wants Van Nistelrooy available.

    "Ruud's injury has not cleared up," admitted Ferguson. "There is a bit of concern because he is the best striker in the business and we want him back, particularly with the European campaign coming.

    "Hopefully, we will have a better idea once he has seen the specialist because at the moment we just don't know and that is concerning."

    With Van Nistelrooy in the side this season, United have won seven of their nine Premiership matches. Without him their record is just two wins from eight.

    The 28-year-old's contribution in Europe is even more startling, with 36 goals in 37 European appearances.

    But, while his presence will be required if United are to stand any chance of finding a way past Jaap Stam and Paulo Maldini, Ferguson is more than happy to let seven-goal Alan Smith lead the line against Crystal Palace.

    Ferguson was thrilled by Smith's effort at Craven Cottage, including the tackle on Sylvain Legwinski which set up his goal.

    And although the former Leeds man has picked up a foot injury in training, he should be fit to face Iain Dowie's men.

    "Alan has been a real hero for us," said Ferguson. "Only the old fashioned type of forward gets that kind of goal. Forwards like Thierry Henry, Ronaldinho and Ronaldo can manufacture goals by beating men - but there are very few who can create one with a tackle like that.

    "I can't be speak more highly of him for what he has done and he won't be phased by having to lead the line."

    With Gabriel Heinze and Cristiano Ronaldo handed a long-awaited break, Ferguson is committed to making changes, with John O'Shea and Darren Fletcher favourites to fill the vacancies. Kleberson (ankle) and Louis Saha (knee) are definitely out.

    Dowie accepts his Crystal Palace players must show United the "ultimate respect," but maintains they will not be in awe of their star-studded opponents.

    For Eagles fans, trips to such venues as Old Trafford are what they have been dreaming about since their six-year exile from the top flight came to an end last season.

    However, with his side now struggling to keep out of the relegation zone following a winless run of six Barclays Premiership matches, Dowie is determined to keep the players focused on getting what would be an unlikely positive result rather than just enjoying the occasion.

    "What you have got to do is pay people the ultimate respect," the Palace manager said.

    "We hope at our football club we are a very respectful team - we won't be talking out of turn about other teams or managers, that is not what we do.

    "In the end, it is 11 against 11. They are individuals, people - whether they are better than us at football is for other people to judge."

    Dowie may stick with a lone striker role for Andy Johnson and pack the midfield, despite Palace having drawn blanks in their last two outings.

    Wayne Andrews, though, is one attacking option open to Dowie having shrugged off the flu following an impressive debut against Blackburn

    With the games coming thick-and-fast over the next two weeks, Dowie admits being able to keep out of the relegation zone come the turn of the year would give everyone at the club a lift.

    He reflected: "It is an important boost. We need to push on, we have got to make sure that whatever curve ball the season throws us, we are able to deal with that."

    Uruguayan defender Gonzalo Sorondo came through his run-out for the second string unscathed and is likely to be in the squad following a lengthy absence with a hamstring injury.

    Tommy Black, however, is set for several weeks on the sidelines after breaking his leg during a reserve game against Arsenal.
