    Wenger names young squad

    Arsenal boss Arsene Wenger has been true to his word and named a young and inexperienced squad to travel to Old Trafford in the Carling Cup.

    The Gunners head for Manchester on Wednesday with the same faces that have disposed of both Manchester City and Everton in previous rounds.

    Arsenal's second string have looked imperious against Premiership opposition in the competition thus far but will be without one of their more experienced heads, Pascal Cygan, in midweek.

    A back complaint has forced the Frenchman to the sidelines and his absence will be a major blow for Wenger, as he looks for an experienced senior figure to guide his youngsters.

    Exciting prospect Gael Clichy returns after an ankle injury, while Ryan Smith, who injured his knee ligaments against Everton in the previous round, also makes a comeback.

    It is expected that Dutchman Robin van Persie and Jermaine Pennant will be the only two familiar faces in the Arsenal line-up.

    However, United boss Sir Alex Ferguson has also indicated that he will be fielding a side largely made up of inexperienced youngsters and those on the periphery of first team football.

    Man Utd vs Arsenal

    Manchester United manager Sir Alex Ferguson believes the Carling Cup is the perfect competition to test the character of his rising young stars.

    Considering the fractious nature of the club's relationship with Arsenal there is little doubt their mental strength will be stretched to the full when the teams meet in the quarter-finals of the tournament.

    It is the first time Ferguson and Gunners boss Arsene Wenger have faced each other since October 24 in a Barclays Premiership match which was riddled with bad blood and accusations.

    There was talk that Arsenal players had thrown pizza and soup at Ferguson after his team ended Arsenal's 49-match unbeaten league run.

    Given the average age of both sides in the Carling Cup, lemonade and ice cream is more likely to be on the menu this time around.

    Of the side that defeated West Brom at the weekend, only Wes Brown is expected to remain in the United starting line-up against Arsenal.

    Ferguson expects his team to rise to the challenge.

    "You always learn about your young players in the Carling Cup," he said.

    "It is the area where we try to test them. We test their character, we test their temperament, we test their ability to play at the top level. It is great for all young players here.

    "Of course the game will be competitive. It is the quarter-finals of the League Cup, so you can expect a competitive game. It doesn't matter who you play against.

    "We have a chance to get into the semi-final and everyone wants to get there.

    "Whoever wins has a great chance of getting to the final."

    Ferguson will stick with the squad that defeated Crystal Palace in the last round, bar the injured Louis Saha, and give a rest to the majority of his senior players ahead of the league match against Southampton.

    He could give starts to promising youngsters Giuseppe Rossi and Chris Eagles.

    Rossi, 17, has impressed at junior and reserve-team level since his arrival from Parma in the summer and might partner Alan Smith in attack.

    Meanwhile, defender Pascal Cygan has added to Arsenal's injury problems after being ruled out of the clash.

    Cygan was expected to be one of just a few experienced figures in the second-string Arsenal side, but he has joined the likes of Gilberto Silva and Edu on the sidelines with a back problem.

    Full-back Gael Clichy returns after an ankle injury, while there is a surprise inclusion for Ryan Smith, who injured his knee ligaments against Everton in the previous round, and Frankie Simek is back after a loan spell at QPR.

    But Robin van Persie and Jermaine Pennant are perhaps the only two well-known names in what will be a very inexperienced line-up at Old Trafford.
