    Reports from Skysports
    Real Madrid coach Jose Antonio Camacho has offered to resign, just four months after taking the post, according to reports in Spain.

    Camacho was handed his second stint at the Santiago Bernabeu helm in May after Carlos Queiroz was sacked after just one season.

    But Camacho has endured a lacklustre start to the new season and has already tendered his resignation to Real president Florentino Perez, according to Cadena Ser radio station.

    The surprise 3-0 defeat to Bayer Leverkusen in the UEFA Champions League in midweek prompted heavy criticism of the team.

    Saturday's 1-0 reverse at Espanyol failed to silence Camacho's detractors and he has now offered to quit his position.

    Speaking after the defeat to Espanyol, Camacho accepted full responsibility for Real's current patchy form.

    "I like to play football and I have not seen that Real Madrid have done that," said Camacho.

    "The coach is the person in charge of all the games that are lost, that is my reading of it."
