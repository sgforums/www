    Individualism/collectivism has little, if anything, to do with the behaviours described by the OP.  That aside, despite Hofstede's (1980) study of IBM offices worldwide demonstrating a cultural difference in individualism/collectivism, the typical American "USA! USA!" chant at sporting matches is evidence against Western individualism.  Also, Hofstede is a psychologist, not a sociologist.

    A much more appropriate explanation is Henri Tajfel and John Turner's (1979) Social Identity Theory (or if you want to go further back, Leon Festinger's (1954) Social Comparison Theory) , which addresses the "us-versus-them" mentality, and explains how and why outgroups are identified and descriminated against.
