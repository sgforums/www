    You could be suffering from Carpal Tunnel Syndrome. Although the name suggests something scary, it's basically compression of a nerve at the wrist.

    Excerpted from Wikipedia:

    ...The first symptoms of CTS may appear when sleeping and typically include numbness and paresthesia (a burning and tingling sensation) in the fingers, especially the thumb, index, and middle fingers...

    ...For prolonged handwriting, use a big pen with an oversized, soft grip adapter and free-flowing ink. This way you won't have to grip the pen tightly or press as hard on the paper. Take frequent breaks. Every 15 to 20 minutes give your hands and wrists a break by gently stretching and bending them...

    Take care.

    It's not just about how long you study..... it's what you absorb that's more important.

    No point studying unproductively for 8-10 hours and absorbing nothing in the process. Might as well just study productively for 4-6 hours and absorb a lot more things into your mind.

    You study two hours at one go? Don't. Cut down to perhaps one hour periods. Take a short 15-minute break between each one-hour session.

    I've read studies suggesting that the human brain can probably only productively take in no more than 40 minutes of continuous information without rest. So just study in one-hour periods.
