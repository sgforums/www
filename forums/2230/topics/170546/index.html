    Diploma in Pharmaceutical Sciences

    About the Programme

    The DPHM leverages on the expertise of the School of Applied Science from offering the pharmaceutical science track in the Diploma in Biomedical Sciences. The programme, launched as a full diploma from AY06 is augmented by a greater focus on pharmaceutics and pharmaceutical chemistry, and the introduction of a course in Complementary and Alternative Medicine.

    The programme provides you with the opportunity to follow the Pharmaceutical Science track to better prepare for the mainstream pharmaceutical industry, or specialise along the Complementary and Alternative Medicine track to participate in the increasingly important alternative medicine sector.

    DPHM offers two specializations from Academic Year 2006:

    1) Pharmaceutical Science
    2) Complementary and Alternative Medicine

    Pharmaceutical Science Specialization

    The healthcare sector in Singapore is a growth industry. Knowing how medicines work provides a coherent approach to build a career in the healthcare service sector.

    Building upon the necessary abilities and skills to appreciate life processes, the modules that define this specialisation will enhance your understanding of the role of medicines in society so that you will be better able to fit into the various pharmaceutical service sectors. You will be able to participate in the knowledge intensive pharmaceutical industry; from the marketing of medicines, a career in the medicine dispensing community to ancillary clinical research support.

    Complementary and Alternative Medicine Specializations

    Complementary and Alternative Medicines which includes Traditional Chinese Medicines (TCMs) are becoming extremely popular. In 1998, the National Institutes of Health of the United States established the National Center for Complementary and Alternative Medicine. In Singapore, the NTU has started offering a double degree in Biomedical Science and TCM. The Ministry of Health regulates Traditional and Complementary Medicines under its Health Regulations Division.

    Lifestyle changes from rising affluence are driving the alternative lifestyle industry which includes the marketing of nutraceuticals and the provision of alternative therapies for health and wellness. This track prepares you with an appreciation of medication management to produce positive health-outcomes from the perspectives of both western medicine and alternative traditions, providing a strong foundation to engage this growth sector.

    Programme Aims

    The course will equip you with:

    1) A pharmaceutical science foundation.
    2) An introduction to the discipline of pharmacy, from drug discovery and development, the science of compounding and dispensing drugs, regulatory approval to pharmacy practice.
    3) An appreciation of complementary and alternative medicine.
    4) A rigorous introduction to major technological advances and emerging fields.
    5) An appreciation of the pharmaceutical industry, its best practices and societal considerations.
    6) Entrepreneurial knowledge of how pharmaceutical companies operate.

    In addition, the PBL approach to education will equip you with life-long learning skills, enabling you to apply and adapt your training according to circumstance, build on the foundations you acquired as a student, hence making you a real knowledge practitioner long after graduation.

    Future Career

    As a pharmaceutical science graduate, you will be able to find employment in scientific and biomedical research careers, as well as diagnostic and therapeutic development. Opportunities are also available in the healthcare sector, pharmacies and pharmaceutical companies. This programme prepares graduates with clinical research and regulatory affairs knowledge that are valuable to contract research organisations, pharmaceutical and biopharmaceutical manufacturing companies. Technical knowledge about how medicines are regulated will allow more entrepreneurial graduates to value add to the growing alternative medicine industry. You are also equipped to further your education, for the programme equips you with a broad science foundation.

    Diploma In Aerospace Avionics

    About the Programme

    Singapore is currently one of the most comprehensive aerospace maintenance, repair and overhaul (MRO) hubs in Asia-Pacific. This sector constitutes about 90% of the $4 billion aerospace industry and employs over 11,000 people. Singapore will continue building its capabilities as an international one-stop centre in aerospace avionics to serve both regional and global markets, supported by the rapidly growing air travel in the region.

    In response to this burgeoning industry, RP has launched the Diploma in Aerospace Avionics (DAA) to take advantage of the immense opportunities in the Asia Pacific aerospace industry.

    Programme Aims

    This programme aims to develop knowledgeable and enterprising young professionals who will be able to apply their skills in the field of aerospace avionics technologies, in particular, aircraft electrical systems, instrumentations, avionics and sensors. They will be adept in the art of problem solving and become effective learners in the ever-evolving arena of this high-tech industry.

    The programme will provide a well-rounded education that comprising both technical know-how and general capabilities. Discipline and specialisation-specific modules will equip our graduates with a solid understanding of aerospace avionics. General modules will ground them in innovation, creativity, entrepreneurship, as well as train communication and life-long learning skills.

    Future Career

    DAA graduates can look forward to excellent career prospects and ample opportunities in the aerospace as well electronics industries. Their potential employment opportunities are available in the more than 100 aircraft MRO companies and companies that engaged in design, development, manufacturing and testing of aircraft flight components or in-flight entertainment systems. They can be employed as assistant or associate engineers in diverse areas such as product design and development, equipment testing, technical marketing, applications support, and manufacturing as well as technical support personnel.

    Graduates may also choose to pursue further education and to this end, all our diploma programmes are designed to meet the requirements for advanced standing in local and overseas Institutions of Higher Learning (IHLs).

    About the Programme

    The Diploma in Integrated Events Management (DIEM) prepares students for professional practice in this industry. Students will learn to leverage technology to create, manage and innovate the processes of events management. They will learn to seize business opportunities by honing their entrepreneurial skills and forge ahead with their customer-focused, service-minded and technology-driven solutions.

    There are 3 specializations under DIEM.

    1. Meetings, Incentives, Conventions and Exhibitions
    2. Performing Arts
    3. Leisure & Lifestyle

    Meetings, Incentives, Conventions and Exhibitions (MICE)

    Graduates of the MICE specialisation track will support the development and growth of the Corporate Events Management sector. This sector is expected to grow substantially with the building of the new Integrated Resorts. Students will learn to address various issues and the best practices in marketing, organising and executing corporate events.

    Performing Arts

    Graduates of the Performing Arts specialisation track will support the performing Arts sector which is already experiencing tremendous growth with Singapore being a popular venue for major International Performances. Students will learn how to manage and host various arts events including mega international performances.

    Leisure and Lifestyle

    Graduates of the Leisure & Lifestyle specialisation track will support the exciting Leisure and Lifestyle segment, with organised events ranging from sports and recreation, trends and fashion, adventure themed to social and community based. Students will learn to organise, market and execute a variety of events effectively.

    Programme Aims

    The DIEM aims to produce graduates who can support SingaporeÂ’s positioning as the leading event organising hub in Asia. It seeks to educate students with relevant industry knowledge so they can hit the ground running with an innovative and enterprising mindset to challenge industry norms. It aims to groom graduates able to help Singapore attain and maintain its leadership position in this industry within Asia.

    Future Career

    The Events Management industry is expected to grow substantially in tandem with SingaporeÂ’s positioning as a global city. Singapore plans to grow its sports, art and entertainment sectors. Major infrastructure development such as the Esplanade and the Integrated Resort would generate the demand for professional and skilled workforce in this sector.

    Graduates join the events management industry as junior executives, working in a wide array of service sector (private and public) from meeting, incentive, convention, conference, sports, health, leisure and performing arts.

    Graduates may also choose to pursue further education and to this end, all our diploma programmes are designed to meet the requirements for advanced standing in local and overseas Institutions of Higher Learning (IHLs).

    Diploma in Sports & Exercise Sciences

    About the Programme

    This new Diploma in Sports & Exercise Sciences (DSES) aims to support the growing needs of the sports and wellness industry. It equips students with specialised knowledge and relevant skills, enabling them to help people keen to achieve overall wellness and athletes striving for peak performance. With a problem-based learning paradigm, the course adopts a multi-disciplinary approach in addressing the differing challenges and issues in sporting scenarios. Students will gain an appreciation of how organised sports today is increasingly dependent on well-run administration, management and sports science functions.

    There are two specializations for this diploma. They are

    1. Sports Performance
    2. Exercise & Fitness

    Sports Performance

    The Sports Performance specialisation option will support the sports development (Sports for Life) and Sports Excellence industries. This track explores the sports sciences with biomechanics, nutrition and psychology subject areas.

    Exercise & Fitness

    The Exercise & Fitness specialisation option will prepare graduates for the Sports for Life, health promotion, wellness and fitness industries. This track delves deeper into fitness & health and exercise prescription & programming.

    Programme Aims

    The DSES aims to support the needs of the growing sports and fitness/health industries. It seeks to adopt a multi-disciplinary approach in the study of various sports and exercise science areas. Students will be equipped with specialised knowledge and skills relevant to sports performance and exercise/fitness settings.

    Future Career

    DSES graduates will be able to pursue supervisory or account managing portfolios in the sports and wellness industry,

    Graduates of the DSES Sports Performance Specialisation may find jobs in the following types of companies and organisations: Singapore Sports Council, National Sports Associations, golf & country clubs/resorts, sports & recreation clubs, fitness centres/gyms, schools & institutions, private sports coaching schools, sports service providers (outsourced partners with SSC and schools), and so on.

    Graduates of the DSES Exercise & Fitness Specialisation may find jobs in the following types of companies and organisations: fitness clubs/gyms, health & wellness centres, HR departments in organisations (especially in workplace health promotion), golf & country clubs/resorts, sports retail shops (such as nutrition stores, apparel stores), fitness equipment & supplies companies, fitness & health promotion service providers, hospitals & physical rehabilitation centres, child development centres (motor/physical development), and so on.

    Graduates may also choose to pursue further education and to this end, all our diploma programmes are designed to meet the requirements for advanced standing in local and overseas Institutions of Higher Learning (IHLs).

    The DSES curriculum is designed to prepare students for national professional certification examinations. Specifically, the Sports Performance Specialisation is aligned with the SSC National Coaching Accreditation Programme (NCAP), while the Exercise & Fitness Specialisation is aligned with the SSC Fitness InstructorÂ’s Certification (FIC). This is to ensure that graduates of the DSES are industry-relevant and professionally ready when they complete the respective courses.

    Outstanding graduates will be able to pursue a university education in sports studies locally or overseas at institutions such as Physical Education & Sports Science Academic Group (National Institute of Education, Nanyang Technological University), Loughborough University, and University of South Australia.
