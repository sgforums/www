    there are 4 disney theme parks in the world,

    The orginal DisneyLand in Anaheim, California (near LA)
    DisneyWorld in Orlando, Florida
    Tokyo Disneyland in Chiba (near tokyo)
    Euro-disneyland in France, near Paris

    They're building another in HK

    I've been to 3 of them, except Eurodisney. (I was in paris but did not go to eurodisney)

    Disneyland is great if you've got abt 1-2 days to spare and you are visiting the west coast of the US. some great rides are space mountain, indiana jones adventures, pirates of the caribean, etc.

    Disneyworld is actually a theme country. It has 4 theme parks, (the magic kingdom, epcot, disney/mgm studios, animal kingdom), 3 water parks, a shopping area, and many resort hotels. You need abt a week to see everything. It has it's own transport system, fire station and ambulances, etc. I had a wonderful experience there. If you have the chance, try to stay in the resort hotels there. Not only do you get to go to the theme parks before they open, you'll also get to enjoy the atmosphere there.

    Tokyo disneyland has a new addition, disneysea, with water theme rides. the bad thing is that most of the narration, ride explainations, etc are in japanese. Unless you know japanese, this is going to lower your enjoyment of the rides.

    I've never been to euro disneyland, but I've heard that it's the poorest of all the parks. Every thing is in french, and the cast members ( what disney call's their staff) are not very frendly. Besides when you are in france, there are better things to do than go to a theme park, right ?

    I would highly recommend that you try to go to 1 of the theme parks if you have the chance. It makes you feel very young and happy, like all your childhood coming back

    Other interesting and excellent theme parks : sea world, universal studios, and the less interesting ones : WB movie world in gold coast

    If you have any questions, feel free to ask me. I'm a theme park junkie
