    Fiona answers some questions posed to her:

    1. What advice would you give to those who are still students but inspired to be a celebrity like you?
    - posted by moleow on 10/29/2002 2:51:11 PM

    Fiona:

    Chase that dream! But with your head screwed on tightly on your shoulders and realistic goals. Take up lessons to improve and hone your craft and embrace performing as a passion and art. Always remember fame is not everything and that there is a lot of hard work involved. All the best.

    2. What makes a perfect date?
    - posted by colin on 11/7/2002 1:07:35 PM

    Fiona:

    Right chemistry (The perfect and right person), barrels of laughter and fluttering of the heart, running conversation that sparks Technicolor fireworks and comfortable silence. And oh, did I also mention drowning in the person's gaze?

    3. Hi,Fiona, just would like to know if you were surprised when you managed to secure a place in the top 10 most popular female artistes despite the fierce competition in the category last year? Secondly, were you disappointed for not being able to see your name in any of the awards nomination this year? Finally, wish you all the best for the top 10 most popular female artists award.
    - posted by Long on 10/30/2002 12:55:51 AM

    Fiona:

    Yes, I was very surprised and honoured to be in the Top 10 as well as the Best Newcomer, especially since I've only joined MediaCorp for 6 months and have so much more to learn and grow. No, I'm not disappointed that I'm not nominated this year 'coz I haven't actually done any production this year. And thank you so much for your support and encouragement. The Top 20 Most Popular nomination award should go to you!

    4. Hi Fiona! I'm a great fan of yours and I really enjoy watching your shows! I've been waiting for you to be the current star! How did you feel when you clinched the Best Newcomer Award and did you expect it to be awarded to you? How did you get into the entertainment circle? Have you ever thought you would be a star when you grow up? I've read that you explored Hong Kong (is it true?) and I think that was brave! Anyway, I'll be waiting for the debut of My Genie ll and will always support you! :-)
    - posted by sue ann on 10/30/2002 4:33:32 PM

    Fiona:

    Honoured and stunned by the whirlwind of events that happened in the short 6 months! I stumbled upon entertainment really. Was a holiday thing for fun that started with War Diary and one thing led to another. I've never imagined I would end up in entertainment, I had always embraced the hope of being in a power suit as a lawyer or a teacher of some sort, apart from the days that I had wanted to be a nun or a loving mother kissing the chubby cheeks of her darling babies that is!

    The Hong Kong stint was amazing. It taught me so much and let me grow and get to know myself better. My independent streak shone forth then. But no, I didn't work on the movie industry; only the modelling arena. Once again, thank you so much for your continual support and love. Hugs.

    5. Hi Fiona! Your bubbly character in "My Genie" and "City Beat" is really natural. However, I know that you are rather mature in real life. How do you manage to stay so cool? At 20, what things do you set out to accomplish in before 25? All the very best, Fiona!
    - posted by Betty on 10/31/2002 1:57:58 AM

    Fiona: I love you already! Yes, I'm glad you know and celebrate the fact that actors are not an extension of their screen characters. What I want to do before 25? Have furious fun and learn as much as I can too. Retire at 25 and enjoy life in itself! As you know Betty, as cliche as it sounds, being true to yourself works wonders. It'll help you juggle with life with much ease and collected calm!
