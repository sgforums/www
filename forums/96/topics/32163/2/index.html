    Dead Singapore navy servicewoman identified as CPL Goh Hui Ling, 22

    By Lee Foong Ming

    The body of the servicewoman recovered from the RSS Courageous has been identified as 22-year-old Corporal Goh Hui Ling, a graduate in Electronics and Computer Engineering from the Ngee Ann Polytechnic.

    Corporal Goh joined the Republic of Singapore Navy in 2001, MINDEF said in a statement.

    Advertisement

    Navy divers recovered her body at about 4 pm Saturday, sandwiched between the bunks in the women's sleeping quarters.

    She was one of four servicewomen trapped in the flooded aft compartment of the Republic of Singapore Navy's RSS Courageous, which was involved in a collision with the Dutch-registered ANL Indonesia late on Friday in the eastern Singapore Straits near Pedra Branca.

    It is believed she is the first woman to die while on military duty in the history of the Singapore Armed Forces.

    MINDEF and the SAF have extended their deepest condolences to CPL Goh's family.

    Intensive efforts are still underway to locate the three remaining servicewomen who were also in their sleeping quarters at the time of the collision.

    The operation involves draining the water from the extensively damaged RSS Courageous, which is now berthed at the Changi Naval Base.

    Deputy Prime Minister Dr Tony Tan said Saturday that search and rescue operations at the scene of the collision near Pedra Branca will also continue.

    But he has cautioned Singaporeans to be prepared for the worst.
