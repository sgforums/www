    According to Singapore Law, there is only one basis for divorce - that the marriage has broken down irretrivably.
    (section 88(3) of the Women's Charter)

    To establish this, one of five "facts" has to be proven.

    1. Adultery
    Meaning: ...where the court deems that an opportunity is present for a sexual relationship with the opposite sex (other than the spouse) to take place.

    2. Unreasonable Behaviour
    Meaning: ...physical abuse, alcohol abuse, excessive gambling (nagging not counted)

    3. Desertion for 2 years
    Meaning: ...where absolutely no news has been heard from the spouse (even after placing ads in papers)

    4. 3 Years Separation With Mutual Consent
    Meaning: ...where no conjugal relationship exists (separate lifestyles)

    5. 4 Years Separation

    If the irretrievable breakdown is established, the court will order a "Decree Nisi".

    This is also a period where the court hopes that things can still be worked out between both parties. The Singapore Court is family-oriented.

    After custody of children, maintenance and other ancillary matters, such as distribution of matrimonial assets, are dealt with, a final order called a "Decree Absolute" will be made.

    Once it is declared, the marriage is considered void, and the divorce proceedings are complete.

    But do take note: A marriage is a solemn and sacred event. Do not get registered for marriage for the wrong reasons. In the eyes of the Singapore Law, you are considered a "couple" when you signed your names at the ROM, not when you hold your wedding banquet or 'jin cha' to your parents.
