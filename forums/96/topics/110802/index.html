    Yah, your time will come, young man. I am 48 this year.
    When I went to school in the 60s, I have to sing the
    Negara-ku the Malaysian National anthem b4 reverting
    to our present one.

    I came out of NS in Oct 1975. Many of u were not born yet,
    or some of u may be in your diapers.

    I have lived a fantastic life...like a roller coaster with ups and
    down.

    I went thru' quite a number of job changes during those boom years
    of the 80s and 90s.

    I started work as a Technical Officer at PUB after NS; for 2 and a half
    years until 1978. In 1979, I enrolled in the Institute of Education
    and came out in 1981. I was an educator for 16 over years in govt schools.

    Those were my glorious and happiest days of my youth. I was an active
    sportsman, school runner, basketball player, badminton player and
    recreative swimming.

    Becos' of my restless spirit and a subconscious desire to see the world, I decided to make some forays into the pte sector.

    I was a Sports Officer for SPH for 2 and a half years b4 leaving to join
    SBS as an Admin Officer for 2 yrs. Also worked in the defunct Emporium
    Holdings as a Personnel Officer for about 2 years b4 joining Temasek Poly
    as a Corporate Officer for 1 year.

    Now my life seems to have taken a full cycle as I am now back in the
    education field as an educator.

    I have a tumultuous and roaring time when I first visited Chiang Mai,
    Bangkok, Sungei Golok and Haadyai in 1979 and subsequent
    years as a cadet teacher. That year alone I visited LOS 5 times; lasting
    from a week to 3 weeks; courtesy of the schools' long vacations.

    I have my share of good fun.... and many moments of unforgetable
    memories.

    Some of my varsity colleagues in SPH passed away in their 30s,
    and early 50s. So not everybody live to see their CPF...

    So young men and women, enjoy your moments on this planet
    while it last, cos we may never pass this way again......

    Cheers!!! :-)
