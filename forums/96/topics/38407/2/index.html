    here's my riddle... was posted in another thread in this forum, so some of you may already know the answer. It comes in two iterations, the second one harder than the first:

    Iteration 1:
    You have ten bottles of pills. ONE bottle contains poisonous pills. the poisonous pills are totally identical in shape, size, color, smell etc to the normal pills, except that they are 0.1g heavier. A normal pill weighs 1g (therefore a poison pill weighs 1.1g).

    You have an electronic weighing machine capable of 0.1g accuracy. How do you weigh the pills in ONE READING (ie, u cannot put the pills on and remove one at a time) and determine which bottle contains the poisonous pills?

    Clue: Assume u have unlimited number of pills in each bottle.

    Iteration 2:
    You still have ten bottles of pills. Now a random number of bottles contain poisonous pills. The poisonous pills have the properties listed above. How do you use ONE WEIGHING again, to determine which bottles contain the poisonous pills??
