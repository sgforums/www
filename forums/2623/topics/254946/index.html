Djourou confident for future

  • turbo_drift

    Johan Djourou is confident Arsenal's young guns can make a leap in quality and fight on all fronts next season.

    Arsene Wenger has now presided over two trophyless seasons in succession as he looks to build a new team in North London, and a club that became accustomed to regular silverware in his first eight years in charge are now suffering something of a drought.

    Summer signing William Gallas has put the club's recent failings down to an over-reliance on their young players, but Djourou - who at the age of 20 made 21 Premiership appearances this season - believes the less experienced stars at Emirates Stadium are ready to signal their growing maturity next term.

    "We have guys like Cesc [Fabregas], Denilson and Justin Hoyte, who have all shown when they have played that they are ready for it," said Djourou.

    "Next season is going to be a really good season because we have the quality for it.

    "We have had a couple of injuries, with Thierry (Henry) and William Gallas, which was a bit of a blow for us - but now if everyone is back, we will have a chance for everything.

    "It is also up to the others to show what they can do to replace those players, and I think we can take that step for next season and try to do it."

    Djourou impressed in the first half of the season when injury robbed The Gunners of a number of defenders, and the Swiss international admits he was pleased with his input this term.

    "I am very proud of my season. I am only 20 and I have played a lot of games in the Premiership," he said.

    "That is all good for my experience and I hope I can keep going now, to play even more."
