Bendtner issues warning

    27 Sep 07, 12:07
    Arsenal striker Nicklas Bendtner has revealed that confidence is sky high in the Gunners' dressing room.

    Arsene Wenger's men are currently in outstanding form, having put together a string of six consecutive wins.

    Their latest triumph came on Tuesday, when a youthful Arsenal side recorded a deserved 2-0 victory against Newcastle United.

    The talented Dane, who scored the opener against The Magpies, has hailed the club's footballing ethos.

    Pass and move

    "All through the club, including all the young players, everyone knows what it's all about and the way to play football," Bendtner said.

    "We play passing football all the time in training. We are all used to it and can all do it and we showed that again.

    "At the moment the confidence is really, really high. The win over Newcastle was just another showing of what the club is going through at the moment.

    "We are doing everything really well. Everyone is really happy and wants to play for the club."

    Colossal Adebayor

    Arsenal face West Ham United on Saturday and Bendtner is aware that the powerful Emmanuel Adebayor is likely to return to the starting XI.

    But the 19-year-old has praised Adebayor's incredible performance during the 5-0 thumping of Derby County, and is happy to bide his time while he waits for his next opportunity to impress the boss.

    "We have a strong squad at the moment. Adebayor has done really well against Derby," he added.

    "I am going to hope for my chance. Anything can happen. There will be injuries as well.

    "If I am involved, great. If not, I will keep working hard."
