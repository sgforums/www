    Ok so we slipped up. As many have said, rather Sevilla away when we have already qualified than a league game which is far more important. I am quite glad we got a poor performance and our first defeat out of the way.

    Let's take the positives then: Bendtner was absolutely oustanding in a game where we underperformed and I think he warrants a regular start now. I'm sure Wenger can see this. His hold up play and passing is superb, he just needs to work on his touch.

    I think Denilson was chosen in midfield because he is more technical than Diarra, who I think Wenger sees as a player for the English games. What I like about Denilson is that he gets stuck in and shows a lot of heart when the chips are down. He is our youngest midfielder and can only get better.

    Traore was very disappointing and looked out of sorts defensively, which leads me to the main culprit, Phillipe Senderos.

    Somebody needs to take different coloured crayons and draw the following points on the board for him:

    1) The aim is to get the ball FROM the opposition, not give it TO them

    2) Now and again, it would be a good idea to win a header

    3) Your legs are used for running as well as standing or falling over

    4) You can't actually PICK THE BALL UP when it is in play!

    I think it's very harsh to criticise the manager when he clearly has the bigger picture in mind. It's also clear that the main reason we lost was Senderos. He sends negativity throughout the whole team and makes the MOST basic blunders.

    A top class defender can't be as prone to errors as he is and I think it must be all that time on the fringes with Pascal Cygan that has harmed his career. That is the only explanation I can think of why he has gone backwards.
