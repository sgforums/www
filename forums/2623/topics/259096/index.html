Cesc's future tied to Wenger

  • turbo_drift
    5 Jun 07, 21:41
    Cesc Fabregas has stated that his future at Arsenal remains firmly intertwined with that of his manager, Arsene Wenger.

    Amidst talk of interest from Real Madrid in both men, and with Thierry Henry strongly linked with Barcelona, Fabregas has further admitted he could be forced to consider his future should some high-profile figures leave Emirates Stadium over the summer.

    The departure of former vice-chairman David Dein has been the catalyst for unrelenting speculation about Arsenal's crown jewels, even if Wenger has publicly stated he will not be leaving North London in the summer.

    Fabregas also signed an eight-year deal in October to signal his commitment to the club, but Arsenal's youngest ever player and goalscorer has warned that if his manager leaves, he may also reassess his options.

    "My future depends on Wenger," Fabregas told Marca.

    "Now I am happy at Arsenal but if the coach leaves the club and also other players I would have to think again about my position.

    "Wenger has shown much confidence in me since my arrival in London and that is key for a player."

    Fabregas has made no secret of the fact that he wishes to return to Spain at some point in the future, having been plucked from Barcelona's youth setup at the age of 16 without making a senior appearance in his home country.

    Barca's rivals Real Madrid are believed to be great admirers of the young Catalan, but Fabregas has not given any serious thought to ending his English adventure just yet.

    "I believe the interest of Spanish clubs in my services but I have signed a deal with Arsenal," Fabregas continued.

    "It is normal that any time I decide to return to Spain but I still do not have a concrete date."
