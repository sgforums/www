    Malouda Wants Out Of Lyon 14/05/07
    France star Florent Malouda has expressed a desire to leave Lyon after claiming he is being tracked by leading clubs in England and Italy.

    The left-winger shot to prominence last summer following his strong displays in France's World Cup campaign, which ended in defeat to Italy in the final.

    A number of big European clubs were reportedly interested in the 26-year-old after the finals in Germany, but he stayed at Les Gones for this season and has just collected a fourth league-winner's medal with the club.

    It appears he is now ready to leave the Stade Gerland in search of a new experience.

    "I am going to make it very clear, I really want to try a fresh challenge," said Malouda, who moved to Lyon from Guingamp in 2003.

    "Big clubs abroad, both English and Italian, have made known their interest in me and are preparing to enter negotiations with OL.

    "Last summer, after the World Cup, I was already being tracked, but they weren't the same clubs (as they are now).

    "I have played the game with Lyon, I haven't asked for a departure clause in my contract, but I am expecting a little in return."

    Malouda, who is a regular for Les Bleus under coach Raymond Domenech, has four more years left on his current deal at Lyon.

    "I know I have a long contract," he added in French newspaper Le Progres.

    "But I might not have the same possibilities next year and OL could make a good financial deal out of it."

    France left-back Eric Abidal has already outlined his determination to leave Lyon this summer, while negotiations with goalkeeper Gregory Coupet and captain Juninho over extensions to their contracts are ongoing.
