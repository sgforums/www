    Barca won't discard Henry

    Barcelona have not discounted making another bid to sign Arsenal captain Thierry Henry in the summer.

    Reports in the Spanish media on Saturday claimed the Liga champions were plotting another raid on Emirates Stadium for the striker.

    Speaking after Barca's 2-0 win at Real Sociedad, sporting director Txiki Beguiristain suggested the club could lodge an offer for Henry.

    But Beguiristain also conceded that the Catalan giants may opt against revisiting old ground, following last summer's failed bid for the Frenchman.

    "Now it is the moment only to speak about our race for La Liga," Beguiristain told Spanish journalists.

    "I have to admit that the French player has not been discarded.

    "It is a subject that is necessary to evaluate.

    "If the future looks very good and brilliant, we do not need to look at the past."

    Henry, though, has revealed he sees his future with The Gunners, providing Arsene Wenger stays as manager following vice-chairman David Dein's recent exit.
