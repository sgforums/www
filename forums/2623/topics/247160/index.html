Wenger: I can't believe it

  • turbo_drift
    8 Apr 07, 15:26
    Arsene Wenger could not believe that Arsenal lost 1-0 at home to West Ham United.

    Bobby Zamora's lob in first-half injury-time condemned The Gunners to a first defeat at Emirates Stadium.

    But Zamora's goal was out of context with the rest of the game as Arsenal dominated for long spells, only to be denied by the woodwork twice and also a string of saves from Robert Green.

    Wenger was happy with his team's overall performance, but was left to rue another game in which his players missed several opportunities.

    "It's unbelievable," Wenger told the website. "I think we created at least 10 chances today - not half-chances, but clear-cut chances.

    "It's difficult to understand.

    "It was one-way traffic from the first to the last minute. I feel everything went against us.

    "The fact we missed early chances meant there was always more pressure on the next one who had the chance.

    "In the end I couldn't believe that nobody could put the ball in the net."

    There was also a question mark over whether Zamora was offside for the winning goal, with Wenger reluctant to commit himself on the issue.

    He added: "I thought from the outside it looked offside, but I haven't seen it again yet on the video."
