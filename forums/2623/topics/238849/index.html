    The first domestic silverware of the English season will be settled on Sunday when Arsenal's young guns are pitted against Chelsea's expensively assembled superstars in the Carling Cup final.

    The first ever all-London final in England's second cup competition is gearing up to be a memorable affair, with no love lost between the two sides in the two-and-a-half seasons since Jose Mourinho's arrival in English football.

    Arsene Wenger has angered the two-time Premiership champions with accusations of 'financial doping' under Roman Abramovich's reign, with the Frenchman's own antidote being a carefully cultivated youth programme which has paid real dividends this season.

    Arsenal's emerging stars have secured memorable victories over West Brom, Everton, Liverpool and Tottenham en route to the final, but a meeting with Mourinho's quadruple-chasing side will be the ultimate test of their talent.

    Whilst cracks have appeared in Chelsea's formidable facade this season, they remain a daunting prospect and will give no quarter in their bid to match their success of 2005 - Mourinho's first trophy in English football.

    The Blues have disposed of Blackburn, Aston Villa, Newcastle and Wycombe on the way to Cardiff's Millennium Stadium, and personal pride - along with Mourinho's ongoing spat with Wenger - means Chelsea will be keen to hand Arsenal's kids a footballing education.

    However, they will have to do so without inspirational captain John Terry after the England star damaged his ankle ligaments in Wednesday's UEFA Champions League game with Porto.

    Khalid Boulahrouz's dislocated shoulder means the backline will have a makeshift feel to it again, with Michael Essien expected to slot in alongside Ricardo Carvalho.

    Ashley Cole has an outside chance of facing the side he left under a cloud in the summer, but the left-back may take a place on the bench after marking his comeback from knee ligament damage in the reserves in midweek.

    Chelsea's 1-1 draw with Porto also saw Arjen Robben pick up a muscle strain so the Dutchman is doubtful for Sunday, with midfield companion Joe Cole a long term absentee due to a foot injury.

    John Obi Mikel could accompany Michael Ballack, Claude Makelele and Frank Lampard in midfield, whilst Andrei Shevchenko will be hoping for another start alongside Didier Drogba after claiming five goals in his last seven games.

    Arsenal's starting XI is much harder to pre-empt, although Wenger has insisted he will keep faith with the players that have taken The Gunners to the final as he chases the only domestic silverware to have eluded him in England.

    That means William Gallas will not play against his former employers, with captain Thierry Henry, Freddie Ljungberg and Jens Lehmann (suspended) also watching from the sidelines.

    Wenger may have selection problems in defence as Emmanuel Eboue is out with an ankle problem, whilst Gael Clichy (foot) and Justin Hoyte (hamstring) require late fitness tests.

    Clichy's participation is in some doubt, meaning 17-year-old Armand Traore could well start the biggest game of his career to date.

    Wenger is expected to garnish his side with more than a liberal sprinkling of experience so the likes of Cesc Fabregas, Kolo Toure and Emmanuel Adebayor could all start in Cardiff.

    However a substitutes' role would be harsh on young hopefuls such as Denilson, Theo Walcott and Jeremie Aliadiere who have started every game in the competition so far.

    If Wenger's proteges are to overpower their London rivals then Julio Baptista could be the key after claiming six goals in the cup already this season, but even The Beast will be aware of the monstrous task facing his young team-mates.

    Arsenal have demonstrated a deep reservoir of talent though their Carling Cup campaign this season, but it remains to be seen whether Chelsea's all-conquering side will prove to be a bridge too far for the young guns.

    On his Cup Final regrets...
    "I felt we had an outstanding performance and my only regrets are that, one, we had the chance in the first half to kill the game and we didn't do it. We should have done more with the chances we created, especially with the final ball. Two or three times we had clear opportunities and the game should have been beyond Chelsea. Another regret is that we lost our nerves with seven minutes to go and we must always keep our nerve. We should have taken the free kick, that was the best way to come back. My third regret is that the referee made some very bad decisions, vital decisions which you cannot afford to make in a game of that importance. The equaliser for Chelsea was offside and Adebayor was clean through without being offside. That is minor for [the media] but not for me."

    On losing another Final to a late goal...
    "Overall I am happy with the performance. We did not lose a game until the final part of the Final. It's a repeat of the Champions League last year where we did not lose until the final part of the Final."

    On the stoppage-time brawl...
    "I am very disappointed because it did not reflect the quality of the game. I know the media will speak more about that than the quality of the game but I think people will keep in their brain that they saw a good football game today and we are proud of that. It was a brawl, a few punches were exchanged but not only from our side. It was just tense, both teams wanted to win, Kolo was pulled back, the ball was kicked away."

    On why he went onto the pitch...
    "I went on to make sure nothing major happened. I wanted to calm things down. Basically I am not strong enough to do that. I overestimated my power a little."

    On why his players must learn from the incident...
    "We have had a great team spirit since the beginning of the season in this squad. The young players have responded every time and my regret today is that we had enough energy and mental resources to come back but that [brawl] killed our last chance to come back to 2-2. After that you don't get into the game any more. I feel until now we are more lambs than wolves. I think in a young squad you need aggression in a positive way but you want to control it and not lose our nerves. We will learn from this."

    On his youthful midfield quartet...
    "It is a learning process of course. They are intelligent, they are very young and they produced an outstanding performance. In the midfield we had Walcott, 17, Denilson, 19, Fabregas, 19, Diaby, 20. The average in the centre of midfield was 19 and they marshalled the middle of the park in an outstanding way against world-class players."

    On whether Arsenal's best chance of silverware has gone...
    "We wanted to win the trophy but we are full of hope with what we have produced in the Carling Cup. We have a good chance in the FA Cup and the Champions League. "

    On his approach to the Carling Cup...
    "I had my vision of this competition and I stuck to it until the last minute. I don't regret any second of what I did. "[/b]
