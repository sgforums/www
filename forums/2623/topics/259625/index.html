Adebayor hits back at critics

  • turbo_drift
    8 Jun 07, 16:49
    Emmanuel Adebayor has responded to criticism of his finishing by insisting he has improved since joining Arsenal and will work even harder next season.

    The Togolese striker produced a number of inspirational performances last season but was frustrating in equal measure as he missed a lot of good opportunities.

    He accepts that he could have scored more goals but claims there is no need for fans to worry.

    "I need to work on my finishing, but I'm not so s**t in front of goal," he told Four Four Two magazine.

    "I was a good player when I joined Arsenal but I'm better now.

    "I'm learning loads, I have more maturity and I feel I'm getting better and better.

    "I've missed a lot of good chances but I'm working hard to improve my goal record."

    Adebayor is looking to learn from Thierry Henry and has consulted his Arsenal team-mate for advice.

    "You can't just watch Thierry 100 times and copy it, you must communicate," he explained.

    "Thierry tells me where and when to run, how to score one on one."
