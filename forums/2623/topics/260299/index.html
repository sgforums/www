Wenger makes Gunners vow

  • turbo_drift
    11 Jun 07, 19:22
    Arsene Wenger has vowed to stay at Arsenal for next season.

    The Frenchman's future, along with that of several players, has been the subject of intense speculation in recent weeks.

    The departure of Wenger's close ally David Dein in April raised doubts over his future at Emirates Stadium.

    However, Wenger insists he will be at Arsenal next season as he still has one more year left on his contract.

    "I have a contract for one more year here and I will complete that contract," Wenger told the News of the World.

    "I have never broken a contract in my entire career and I will not break a contract now. So I can tell you categorically that I will be at the club next season.

    "And next summer I will sit down with the board and see."

    Wenger also insists there is no need to panic despite growing rumours over Thierry Henry's future.

    A number of top clubs across Europe are eagerly watching Henry's developments as the striker has hinted he is unsure of his long-term future.

    Wenger says he will not rush into the transfer market to bolster his side as he only wants to bring in top players.

    "There's absolutely no need to panic. We finished fourth in the table - level on points with Liverpool - and we have a very young side which, I believe, can improve again this season," added Wenger.

    "We will bring in new people only if they are exceptional players."
