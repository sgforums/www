    *Aston Villa v Arsenal preview*

    Two in-form sides meet at Villa Park on Saturday evening as Aston Villa host leaders Arsenal.

    Martin O'Neill's side have won their last four Premier League outings, including a 4-0 midweek win at Blackburn, while The Gunners are yet to suffer defeat in the league this season.

    History is against Villa, with the Midlands outfit going 17 league outings without success against Arsenal, a run spanning nearly nine years.

    Villa's last league win against the North Londoners came back in December 1998 when Dion Dublin netted twice in an unlikely 3-2 success after trailing 2-0 at half-time.

    However, Arsenal travel to Villa Park on the back of a rare loss after slumping to a 3-1 defeat to Sevilla on Tuesday despite taking the lead through Eduardo da Silva.

    Striker doubt

    O'Neill has a near fully-fit squad to choose from with only star striker Gabriel Agbonlahor a doubt to feature.

    The England Under 21 ace limped out of Villa's thumping win over 10-man Blackburn on 85 minutes with a gashed ankle.

    Should Villa's six-goal leading scorer miss out then Marlon Harewood, who scored off the bench against Rovers, may be given the nod.

    Key midfielder Cesc Fabregas is a major doubt for Arsenal having limped out of the loss at Estadio Ramon Sanchez Pizjuan with a hamstring injury.

    While Cesc is touch-and-go to feature at Villa Park, manager Arsene Wenger is hopeful of having Gael Clichy (thigh) and Alexander Hleb (hamstring) at his disposal.

    Backline

    Wenger is likely to change his backline with William Gallas, Bacary Sagna and Clichy coming in for Philippe Senderos, Justin Hoyte and Armand Traore.

    Emmanuel Adebayor will return in attack, although it remains to be seen whether he will be part of a 4-5-1 formation with Hleb or Tomas Rosicky for support, or in a 4-4-2 alongside Nicolas Bendtner or Eduardo.

    French midfielder Mathieu Flamini (thigh) is available after missing out against Sevilla but star striker Robin van Persie (knee) is still a few weeks from returning to fitness.
