    *Liverpool v Arsenal preview*

    Something has to give when Liverpool and Arsenal meet at Anfield on Sunday.

    The two Premier League title rivals boast the only remaining unbeaten records in the top flight this season and Arsenal are currently on a run of 12-straight wins in all competitions.

    Whichever way the game concludes, one statistic will become a thing of the past and both managers will be hoping for the three points for varying reasons.

    Liverpool manager Rafa Benitez will be desperate to bounce back from the midweek UEFA Champions League humbling at the hands of Besiktas, while Arsenal boss Arsene Wenger will be keen to rubber stamp his young side's title credentials in their first meeting of the season with one of the Premier League's 'big four'.

    Welcome back

    Benitez will hope to welcome back Fernando Torres, who has missed the Reds' last two games through injury, while Xabi Alonso could return to the centre of midfield, after the Spaniard featured as an unused substitute in Turkey on Wednesday having recovered from a fractured metatarsal.

    If Daniel Agger (foot) remains sidelined, 34-year-old veteran Sami Hyypia - who has scored two own goals in as many matches - should keep his place in the heart of Liverpool's defence.

    Harry Kewell, who has been out injured since August, could be set for a place on the bench for Liverpool after making a goal scoring return for the reserves on Thursday.

    Having destroyed Arsenal with a classic hat-trick at Anfield last season, Peter Crouch will also be desperate for Benitez to hand him a piece of the action against the Gunners.

    Demolition

    Meanwhile, Wenger is unlikely to shuffle his pack too drastically to the side which demolished Slavia Prague 7-0 in the Champions League on Tuesday.

    It had been suggested that Arsenal - who currently sit two points clear at the top of the league - would stutter following the knee injury to Robin van Persie, but the Gunners seem to keep improving.

    The increasingly impressive Theo Walcott is expected to keep his place in attack alongside Emmanuel Adebayor and the magical midfield duo of Cesc Fabregas and Alexander Hleb should continue to pull the strings, while Mathieu Flamini will remain the Gunners' unsung hero.

    However, Philippe Senderos will remain on the sidelines with his ongoing back injury.
