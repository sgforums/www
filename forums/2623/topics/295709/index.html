    Southgate: Boro won't fear Arsenal

    Arsene Wenger's men arrive on Teesside on Sunday having not lost a Premier League game all season.

    Southgate was at Newcastle on Wednesday evening to see Sam Allardyce's men muscle their way to a hard-fought point against the free-flowing league leaders, and while he admits his side are unlikely to take the same approach, he is adamant they cannot afford to let their opponents play.

    He said: "They have had a tough week with two games where they have been stretched physically and mentally - and we have to make sure the third is exactly the same.

    "Arsenal a couple of years ago did go unbeaten, but eventually, those records go.

    "We have the opportunity to be the first to do that this season.

    "But you have to be at your very best to get something against them. They are top-quality players.

    "I love the way they play football - but we cannot stand and admire them, we cannot afford to give Arsenal time on the ball to play because they have the ability to destroy you if you do.

    "We have got to go and do a job against them, and that's what we are intent on doing."

    Skipper George Boateng, like his manager, is relishing the opportunity to test himself against the Gunners once again, with Boro have taken four points from their last two meetings on Teesside and returned from the Emirates Stadium last season with a draw.

    However, the stakes are perhaps even higher than usual this weekend, with Southgate's men having slipped into the bottom three despite last Saturday's 1-1 draw at Reading.

    Boateng said: "Our position has made it even more important, and the fact that Arsenal are in first in the league makes it also very important for them because they need to win their matches.

    "But we are all very well aware of the fact that we need to get points.

    "People will write us off against Arsenal because they play very good football, but I watched them against Newcastle and I think there are opportunities for us to explore.

    "As much as it is a must-win game for us, it is also a must-win game for Arsenal."

    Southgate could have keeper Mark Schwarzer back from a broken thumb and midfielder Julio Arca is available once again after recovering from a medial ligament injury.

    However, it could be his defenders who find themselves under the greatest pressure as striker Emmanuel Adebayor attempts to get the visitors off to the same sort of start his superb fourth-minute volley handed them at St James' Park in midweek.

    Southgate said: "He gives them a threat aerially in the box, which they did not have before.

    "He was very impressive, certainly in the first half an hour, at St James' the other night, but that's for us to deal with and make sure we get the better of him."
