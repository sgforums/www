Vieira hints at Henry exit

  • turbo_drift
    3 May 07, 20:10
    Patrick Vieira believes Thierry Henry's future at Arsenal could be dependent on Arsene Wenger.

    Henry is regularly linked with a move away from Emirates Stadium, despite the French front man's insistence that he has no plans to leave.

    Following David Dein's exit from his vice-president role, Wenger's future with The Gunners has been cast into doubt with the pair having enjoyed an excellent working relationship.

    Vieira has hinted at a possible departure for Henry, who has four years remaining on his deal he signed last summer, especially if Wenger departs Arsenal.

    Henry's France international team-mate feels the 29-year-old striker must do "the best for himself" after almost eight years' service to the North London club.

    "Thierry is in the position where he is asking himself what he wants to do and I think it's normal," Vieira said at a Ford 'Feel Football' event in Milan.

    "He has been at Arsenal a long time working with Dein and Wenger and he would consider his future if these two people who built the current Arsenal were to leave the club.

    "I think Thierry will try to get the best for himself but if he was to stay it would be great for the club."
