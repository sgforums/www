    Having seen off Alex Ferguson last week, Sven-Goran Eriksson faces the second major test of his managerial reign at Manchester City on Saturday when his table-topping side visit Arsene Wenger's Arsenal.

    City's hastily-assembled side lead the Premier League with a maximum nine points after an opening day win at West Ham United followed by home victories over Derby County and champions Manchester United, surprising even their most optimistic fans.

    Former England coach Eriksson believes the mystery factor may now be starting to wear off. "I think for the first two games a lot of people didn't know about some of our players, Petrov maybe, Elano and Geovanni," the Swede told the club's Web site (www.mcfc.co.uk).

    "But we will not have that advantage anymore because the managers show videos about the opposition from the previous weeks. I know that Arsene Wenger will do it so I don't think we will live on surprising people anymore."

    Arsenal goalkeeper Jens Lehmann will be out of action for at least two weeks after aggravating an Achilles injury.

    The Germany shotstopper was suffering from the complaint before Germany's 2-1 friendly win over England on Wednesday and his involvement in the match only served to exacerbate the problem.

    The 37-year-old will now be sidelined for the weekend's crunch game with early Premier League leaders Manchester City.

    And he will also miss the second leg of Arsenal's Champions League qualifier against Sparta Prague and their home clash with Portsmouth at the beginning of next month.

    Gunners' boss Arsene Wenger said: "Jens is out and he will miss two weeks, I think. Maybe more. I don't know.

    "He is going to see a specialist in Germany. He had this injury before but it has got much worse."

    Lehmann has come under fire for a series of high profile errors so far this season, namely gifting goals to Fulham's David Healy on the opening day of the new campaign and to David Dunn of Blackburn a game later.

    But he will now have a chance to recuperate away from the spotlight, with Manuel Almunia ready to step into Wenger's starting XI. Summer signing Lukasz Fabianski will act as understudy in Lehmann's absence.
