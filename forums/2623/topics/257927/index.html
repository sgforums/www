Van calls for continuity

  • turbo_drift
    31 May 07, 10:41
    Robin van Persie is convinced Arsene Wenger does not need to make wholesale changes at Arsenal this summer.

    The Gunners came up well short in the Premiership once again, with Manchester United and Chelsea proving to be streets ahead of their nearest rivals.

    Arsenal suffered from injuries to key men, but many fans have suggested Wenger needs to add some experience to his squad to complement the raft of youngsters coming through the ranks in North London.

    Van Persie, who missed a sizeable chunk of the season along with Thierry Henry, is convinced Arsenal are on the cusp of mounting a challenge for honours and does not see a need to spend big this summer.

    "We played some fantastic football but there was something missing with the finishing," Van Persie told the Daily Mirror. "It's hard to work out why the ball wouldn't go in.

    "We have been very unlucky this season.

    "I'm sure that if we keep playing the way that the boss thinks, the way most of the players think, we will be successful - maybe next year and certainly within two years, as long as we stay together."
