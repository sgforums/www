Gallas committed to Gunners

  • turbo_drift
    30 Apr 07, 13:24
    William Gallas has insisted he is looking forward to helping Arsenal win trophies next season.

    Reports surfaced last week that the Frenchman was unhappy at Emirates Stadium and could leave the club in the summer.

    However, he has stressed that he still believes he made the right decision in joining Arsenal from Chelsea last August.

    And he is looking forward to a bright future alongside The Gunners' crop of talented young players.

    "I know why I left Chelsea and I don't regret it," he told The Observer.

    "I will be happy for Chelsea if they win the Champions League or FA Cup and I will try everything to win trophies at Arsenal next year.

    "The challenge of coming here was to play with the youngsters.

    "It's different here to Chelsea and Man United, where they only have big stars.

    "At Arsenal we have a lot of young lads with a lot of quality who are quick to learn and if I win something next year I will enjoy it so much."

    Arsenal have had a disappointing season in terms of results but their style of football has earned rave reviews.

    Gallas believes his team-mates will learn to adapt their approach to different situations as they get more experienced and will be rewarded with more success

    "Sometimes it is not just about playing well, you have to play with your mind," he explained.

    "Sometimes you have to play long ball and fight hard, especially when you go away and have maybe 25,000 people against you and you receive a lot of kicks."
