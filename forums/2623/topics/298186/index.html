    Ramos: I don't fear Arsenal

    Juande Ramos is unlikely to be scared by anything in tomorrow's north London derby after his most terrifying moment in football.

    The Tottenham boss was knocked unconscious when a fan threw a bottle filled with cement at his head when in charge of Sevilla in their cup derby with Real Betis last March.

    Ramos was out cold for 11 minutes, had to be carried off the pitch and there were horrific scenes as paramedics frantically tried to revive him.

    The incident happened nine months ago and, although he cannot remember much about it, Ramos is unlikely to feel intimidated by facing Spurs' bitter rivals Arsenal at the Emirates Stadium.
    Advertisement
    Click here to find out more!

    "I was knocked out but I want to forget that now. Somebody threw something at me, it hit me on the head and knocked me out," recalled Ramos.

    "I don't expect it to ever happen again, not this weekend or next weekend. I hope it never happens again on a football pitch. The following week I was absolutely fine. The match was heated but I never could imagine something like that could happen. I don't remember anything after being struck, nor what happened on the pitch."

    Ramos is used to fierce rivalries from his days in charge of Sevilla, but in north London the fixture has suffered in recent years due to the Gunners' dominance.

    Tottenham have not beaten Arsenal for eight years. And Arsene Wenger's success has only turned up the pressure on Spurs bosses. Glenn Hoddle, Jacques Santini and Martin Jol have all paid the price for failing to break the Gunners' domination.

    Ramos is the latest in a long line charged with stepping out of Wenger's shadow and bringing some silverware to White Hart Lane. But that task does not frighten Ramos even though he knows his progress so far, after two months at the helm, will be gauged by how his team fare against their neighbours.

    "I don't understand the correlation between the problems previous managers have had here and the success of Arsenal," said Ramos.

    "We cannot worry about what other teams are doing, we need to focus on ourselves.

    "There's a big difference in the league table at the moment, but we are not as far behind as we were before. Little by little we will close that gap and that will come by getting results.

    "We know it's a number of years since we beat Arsenal, but we have to try our best to get the right result.

    "Arsenal play good football, are very attractive to watch and are the leaders.

    "We are playing very effectively at the moment in terms of what we need to get results. We are playing well, more positively and are improving, which shows in the results. "I don't think it's a psychological barrier at all.

    Arsenal have a great team with a big squad and that is the reason, but whoever is the best and most powerful team on the day will win."

    Ramos believes he is introducing the attacking flair football that Tottenham fans want and brought him so much success at Sevilla.

    He added: "I don't like to compare ourselves with Arsenal and myself with Arsene Wenger, even though I congratulate him for the job he has done.

    "In the last few games we are showing we are getting to grips with the style we want here. At Sevilla I had a way of playing with a certain type of player so we would like to create that again. But the most important thing is results and getting points."

    Ramos has yet to fully master English, although he says neither he nor new England boss Fabio Capello - who he regards as a close friend - will be held back by the language barrier.

    Although he reckons it also has its advantages, saying: "I can't understand a word fans are saying!"

    *Wenger - Berbatov is quality*

    Arsenal boss Arsene Wenger admits his side must be on their guard when they come up against Tottenham Hotspur striker Dimitar Berbatov.

    Top of the table Arsenal host their fierce rivals at lunchtime on Saturday, and The Gunners manager is wary of Berbatov's quality.

    "It seems, especially in the last two or three games, that when Berbatov plays well, so do Tottenham," Wenger said.

    "The quality of Tottenham's performances recently can be linked with the gain in form of Berbatov.

    "His intelligence, his technique and the quality of his movement have impressed me. He is one of the Premier League's better strikers."

    Ramos

    Wenger went on to back Spurs boss Juande Ramos - who he believes will bring success to White Hart Lane.

    "I think Ramos can achieve something at Tottenham. He has the experience and plenty of resources," he continued.

    "He has the chance to do it because Tottenham already had a good team before he came - they have finished fifth for the last two seasons."
