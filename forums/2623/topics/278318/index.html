Thomas - My game will improve

  • turbo_drift
    7 Sep 07, 19:39

    Jay Emmanuel-Thomas

    Having made two appearances for the Reserves already this season, Jay Emmanual-Thomas hopes that 2007/08 will be a major stepping stone in his promising career.

    The versatile 16-year-old put in a man-of-the-match showing against Portsmouth on Monday, dominating the midfield in a way Arsenal fans will have likened to former captain Patrick Vieira.

    "I do my fair share of the work at both ends," Emmanuel-Thomas told Arsenal.com. "I am more of an attack-minded midfielder though, scoring or setting up others."

    The midfielder finished the 2006/07 campaign with a last-minute equaliser against eventual champions Reading on only his fourth appearance for the second-string. Following his impressive start to the new season the youngster fully intends to keep adding to his tally for Neil Banfield's side.

    "I'm happy to be in the Reserves, but I can still play for the Under-18s," said Emmanuel-Thomas. "As long as I keep playing at this level though, and against stronger opponents, my game will get better."
