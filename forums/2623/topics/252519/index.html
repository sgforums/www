Eboue confident for next year

  • turbo_drift
    5 May 07, 11:42
    Emmanuel Eboue believes next season will be Arsenal's year after a below-par campaign which will yield no silverware.

    Arsenal are way off the pace in the Premiership and although the young Gunners reached the Carling Cup final, Chelsea came out on top at the Millennium Stadium.

    But Eboue sees an extremely bright future ahead, and expects a fully-fit squad to be able to challenge on all fronts next term.

    "We still have a young team, we are still learning - but we are doing so very quickly," the Ivory Coast defender said.

    "We saw this season that we are now beating the big teams like Manchester United and Liverpool and drawing at Chelsea. We just lack a bit of consistency.

    "I am confident in our squad, in the work we do with the coaches, and in the football we are playing.

    "We want to win trophies for the club and our fans. We were close last year in the Champions League and this season in the Carling Cup. We learnt from those two defeats

    "We are all looking forward to next season because we know it will be our year.

    "Do not forget as well that we had lots of players injured this season. If we had our strongest team available throughout the season, it would have been different."

    Midfielder Cesc Fabregas also hopes to see progress next season, despite a fantastic campaign for the 20-year-old.

    "On a personal level, I am happy - but as football is about what the team achieve, I cannot be satisfied," said Fabregas.

    "If the team has not achieved everything it could have achieved, that means I should have done more." a
