Hleb: Arsenal Must Keep Flamini

  • felirox
    22 Feb 08, 11:52

    Arsenal midfielder Alexander Hleb says it is essential that the Gunners agree a new long-term contract with Mathieu Flamini.

    Speaking to Setanta Sports after Arsenal's 0-0 draw with AC Milan in the Champions League - a game in which Mathieu Flamini again impressed, his team-mate Alexander Hleb stressed the importance to the Gunners of the dynamic France international midfielder.

    Flamini helped to subdue World Player of the Year Kaka and Italian playmaker  Andrea Pirlo as Arsenal fell just short of beating the European champions in their last 16 clash at the Emirates.

    The game confirmed that this season Flamini has emerged as one of the leading holding midfielders in Europe, though players like Cesc Fabregas, Emmanuel Adebayor and Hleb himself tend to receive more media attention.

    Remarkably Flamini, who joined Arsenal rom Marseille, nearly left the club for  Birmingham last summer. But his long-term future is still unresolved, with his contract expiring at the end of this season and Juventus said to be monitoring his situatuon closely.

    Flamini though would like to stay in North London, and Hleb wants Arsenal to do whatever it takes to keep the services of the 23-year-old.

    "For me it is very important, I think for Arsenal too," Hleb told Setanta Sports. "He is my friend and he is very important.

    "Mathieu was fantastic, really. He ran everywhere where the ball was.

    "I think he had a fantastic performance and he killed [Gennaro] Gattuso. I like his game and he is very important to us."

    Although the Gunners dominated the second half against Milan, they had to settle for a goalless draw. The return leg in Milan will be tough and the tie has swung in favour of the Rossoneri, defending champions.

    For Arsene Wenger, who has yet to win the Champions League, his team's participation beyond this round of the current campaign looks to be delicately poised.

    An away goal at the San Siro would change the balance dramatically, and Hleb is confident that this team can repeat the club's earlier European heroics - at the San Siro against Inter in 2003 (when Arsenal won 5-1) and at the Bernabeu against Real Madrid in 2006 (a 1-0 win) - and qualify for the quarter finals.

    "It was difficult [at home to Milan] but I have belief in myself and Arsenal and I believe we can get through to the next round," said Hleb.

    "I think we can win in Milan but of course we need concentration and to give 100 percent to get through at the San Siro."

    Arsenal came agonisingly close to taking a lead to Milan with them, Adebayor's late header cannoning off the crossbar to give Carlo Ancelotti's side a reprieve.

    Asked what he thought when he saw the ball strike the woodwork rather than the back of the net, Hleb replied: "I don't know, ask him, it is better!  I think he needed to score but he was unlucky and that is football."
