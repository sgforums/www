    Dane happy with his progress at Emirates

    Arsenal striker Nicklas Bendtner has rejected talk of a rift with team-mate Emmanuel Adebayor and says he is enjoying life at the club.

     

    Reports of unrest between Bendtner and Adebayor have continued ever since their on-pitch scuffle during their Carling Cup defeat to Tottenham in January.

     

    Speculation over the relationship between the pair intensified over the weekend after Adebayor refused to celebrate with Bendtner after his last-gasp equaliser against Aston Villa last weekend.

     

    Gunners boss Arsene Wenger was quick to play down any problem between the pair and Bendtner says he gets on fine with Adebayor.

    Going great

    "Things are going great at Arsenal - I feel bloody good here," Bendtner told Ekstra Bladet.

     

    "Things are also good with Adebayor. What happened against Tottenham is now forgotten. I do not think of it any more.

     

    "I feel I have had a good season. It has gone well almost every time I have played - whether it has been from the start or as a substitute.

     

    "I have made a difference and that is what I'll continue to do."
