    WE ARE NEVER GUNNER GIVE UP

    Arsene Wenger saluted the greatest comeback of his reign as Arsenal manager as the Gunners recovered from a two-goal deficit against Bolton to keep alive their Premier League title dream.

    And they pulled off their great escape – coming back to win 3-2 - with only TEN men after Abou Diaby was sent off in the first half.

    Wenger said: “I can’t remember a better comeback because we were 2-0 down with 10 men and with half an hour to go.

    “It’s a great credit to the team and I am very proud of the performance. We got a big blow last week and responded.”

    Diaby was shown a red card for a dangerous lunge on Bolton defender Gretar Steinsson. Wenger admitted: “I was not upset with the sending-off because his foot was a bit high. I feel it was a more protective tackle than an aggressive one. I don’t complain.

    “This was a real mental test because it seemed like everything was going against us. Even at getting back to 2-2 we knew that wouldn’t be good enough. We had to continue to believe and show mental strength.

    “In the last 30 minutes we had chance after chance and it was all Arsenal but we got a bit of luck with the third goal.

    “We did absolutely everything to win the game and we got a very important victory.”
