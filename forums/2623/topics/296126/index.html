    *WENGER CALM OVER EURO KNOCKOUT STAGE*



    Arsene Wenger is prepared to shoulder his share of the blame should Arsenal not win their Champions League group - but warned other teams would not be "especially keen" to face the Gunners in the last 16.

    Following defeat in Sevilla with a much-changed side - which ended a 28-match unbeaten streak - Arsenal must now beat whipping boys Steaua Bucharest at Emirates Stadium on Wednesday night and hope the result in Prague goes their way if the Barclays Premier League leaders are to overhaul the Spaniards and secure top spot in Group H.

    That would mean seeding for the knock-out stages - and avoiding other likely group winners such as Real Madrid, Barcelona, Inter or AC Milan.

    However, Wenger - who must serve a one-match touchline ban following his sending off at the Ramon Sanchez Pizjuan Stadium - reckons the prospect of facing Arsenal, who reached the Champions League final in 2006, would not be so appetising either.

    "Of course I want to finish top of the group, but if we do finish second, nobody would be especially keen to play against us," the Gunners boss declared.

    "I feel we gave up a big advantage [in Sevilla], and yes that was partly down to my selection - but I had many players on the edge after the international games and I had to make a choice.

    "I try to find the right balance between not losing and keeping a strong team.

    "But I should not even have played [Cesc] Fabregas, and I ended up losing him to injury."

    Barcelona playmaker Deco feels the Gunners could mount a real challenge in Europe this season, and Wenger would not be overly concerned to face one of the other heavyweights when the knock-out format begins.

    He said: "I feel for us maybe it is not necessarily a disadvantage to play the big clubs. At least the big clubs will play as well.

    "Last year, we lost against PSV Eindhoven, who refused to play in the first and the second game, just waiting for one counter attack. Yet we struggled more than we had done in the season before against Real Madrid.

    "Maybe you play against Real Madrid or Barcelona, and you say 'what a Hell of a task' - but then a different problem you face because they will come out and play."

    Wenger firmly believes his current side have the burning ambition to succeed.

    However, the Arsenal manager is cautious to predict whether the latest crop can emulate the 'Invincibles' of 2003-04 and go a whole domestic league season without defeat.

    "We have different qualities now," Wenger said.

    "Maybe we are not a strong as we were impact-wise, but we are more mobile and technically different, more creative - that is now what we want to show."

    The Arsenal boss added: "We will try to go as far as we can, but we have only played 15 [Premier League] games.

    "If we had played 25 or 28, then I would have said yes, but it is too early.

    "I see the hunger and togetherness, the desire to do well - but for unbeaten? We are [still] too far [away]."
