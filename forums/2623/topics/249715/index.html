Cesc ready for a rest

  • turbo_drift
    21 Apr 07, 13:13
    Arsenal's Cesc Fabregas says he will use the summer break to recharge his batteries.

    The 19-year-old has made more appearances than anyone else for Arsene Wenger's side this season - 46 in all competitions.

    But his performances have not suffered, earning the midfielder nominations for the PFA Player and Young Player of the Year awards.

    However, as the season draws to a close the Spanish international - an ever present for Arsenal in the Premiership this season - has picked up a virus making him a doubt for Saturday's North London derby at Tottenham.

    Fabregas admits he has enjoyed playing such a key role this season, but is looking forward to taking a break so he can come back even stronger next season.

    "For the last three years now I have been playing about 60 games a season, including international matches," said the Arsenal star.

    "For me that is great, because it means I have been fortunate with injuries and I feel fine physically, which is very important.

    "This season there has been an extra pressure because there have not been as many occasions when the boss could rest me, but I am more than happy to play as many games as possible.

    "I will get away in the summer. I will go somewhere with my girlfriend, and also away for a break with my friends. The main plan is to rest.

    "We have got a couple of qualifiers with Spain in early June, but after that what I will do is rest, rest, rest and rest.

    "I will have my feet up on a beach somewhere and hopefully be ready to start fresh and strong next season."
