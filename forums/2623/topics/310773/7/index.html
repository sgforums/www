    Arsène Wenger: 'A draw is not good enough'

    On the result...
    "It's a very disappointing result today for us because we dropped eight points in the last four games. When you're in the race for the championship, especially when you have the opponents we have, that's a big disappointment but we have to keep belief, spirit and focus for the next game."

    On Mido's red card...
    "Funnily enough I don't think there was any intention there from Mido, I maybe didn't see him, but he hit him on top of his head because he just headed the ball at the moment he wanted to play the ball. I don't think that... personally I didn't see any bad intention from Mido. I believe the referee gave the red card when he saw the injury, because he was bleeding on the top of his head, but it was very difficult to assess that he wanted to hit him on his head, I don't think so."

    On Clichy's injury...
    "He's bleeding, they will see after - he's in the shower now and we'll see after if he needs stitches or not."

    On suggestions that the Boro goal was offside...
    "They say that the offside situation is judged every time the ball is played, but of course the ball wasn't played to him, spirit-wise he was offside."

    On Adebayor's goal being given offside...
    "Yes, the referee explained to me that the intention of our player was to play the ball to one of our players, who was offside, so the deflection of the ball doesn't count anymore. I didn't know that but, at least I learned something today."

    On the damage done to Arsenal's title hopes...
    "I don't know, we'll see that at the end of the season, but of course today we were after three points, but we'll see at the end of the season. A draw when you chase the championship is not good enough."
