    Arsenal's new Polish keeper Lukasz Fabianski says he has no intention of playing second fiddle at the club behind Jens Lehmann.
    He was bought from Legia Warsaw by Arsene Wenger as one for the future but he wants that to come as quickly as possible and he has warned both Lehmann and Manuel Almunia that he intends to be number one.

    "Lehmann and Almunia are very good - but I am not afraid of anyone," he told BBC Sport.

    "I am confident of my skills. I want to be first choice at Arsenal as much as they do.

    "My message is clear - I am ready to fight from day one."

    "Of course I can wait a little bit for my chance, but I am not coming to Arsenal just to be there.

    "I want to become a regular first-team player. Hopefully I will do it from the very beginning, because I am ready for this."
