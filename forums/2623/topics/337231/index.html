    ARSENAL’S much maligned defence could ALL start for France against Uruguay next week.

    That’s the view of French boss Raymond Domenech, who says Gunners quartet William Gallas, Bacary Sagna, Gael Clichy and Mikael Silvestre are all in the reckoning to face the South Americans on Wednesday.

    Ex-Manchester United star Silvestre, 31, last played for Les Bleus in the 2-0 win over Togo in 2006 World Cup.

    But solid displays for Arsene Wenger’s side have seen Domenech earmark him for an international recall.

    Domenech said: "Mikael is playing again and he brings experience at centre back.

    "That will allow us to put the Arsenal defence together, so at least nobody will criticise us."
