    Undertaker donates $50,000 to needy kids

    UNDERTAKER Roland Tay first learnt about The Straits Times School Pocket Money Fund from the children of poor families for whom he had provided free funerals.

    'I would ask the children how they got by at school for food and they would say 'school pocket money fund',' said the boss of Direct Singapore Funeral Services, who has organised several free funerals for the needy and murder victims like Chinese national Huang Na.

    Yesterday, Mr Tay, 60, and his wife Sally dropped by News Centre in Toa Payoh to donate $50,000 to the fund. This sum is half of the $100,000 he received from the grateful widow of a man for whom he provided a free funeral recently.

    On Monday, he received an envelope containing a thank-you card and a cashier's order of $100,000 as a token of appreciation from Madam Lim Sian Hua, who was inundated with donations after her husband was hit by a train.

    Mr Tan Jee Suan, 46, died after he fell on the tracks at Chinese Garden MRT station on Oct 18. His impoverished family's plight drew about $500,000 in public donations.

    Mr Tay, 60, donated the other $50,000 to five other charities. The couple dropped off cheques of $10,000 each to five groups that serve the handicapped and elderly sick.

    The $50,000 donation is the single biggest individual donation to the pocket money fund since it kicked off its new fund-raising year last month.

    The fund, which has seen a dip in donations compared with October last year, hopes to raise $2.7 million for its 2007 drive, so as to help 9,000 schoolchildren from low-income families.

    'It was a timely coincidence that we could do something meaningful to help,' said Mr Tay.

    'This fund helps keep poor children in school. With education, they can improve their lives.'

    The Straits Times has raised $17 million since 2000, when the fund was set up. It provides children with $30 to $50 a month in pocket money.

    To donate, call 6319-1016 or e-mail stspmf@sph.com.sg

    To apply for funds, call 1800-838-0100.
