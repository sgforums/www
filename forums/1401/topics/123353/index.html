    seriously, you wanna know the scariest "real life" moves which got incorporated and they act it out in 'rasslin?

    triangle chokehold: a variation used by Tazz in his Tazmission.. actually a judo chokehold called the Kata Hajime... basically closes the carotid triangle of the jugulars and the throat.. causes unconsciousness in minutes... may cause brain damage.. others include "sleeper", "cobra clutch", "Dragon Sleeper"...

    arm-bar submission: may easily rip your shoulder out of socket or dislocate elbow

    knee-bar submission: may dislocate knee

    anklelock submission: may break ankle and cause torsion fracture in shinbone

    then you got the scary slams..

    Tombstone Piledriver: done wrongly will break the neck of people.. ask Stone Cold Steve Austin who took a bad one from the late Owen Hart.. in fact, most of the piledrivers are scary..

    Vertabreaker: Vince McMahon outlawed it in WWE coz it looks fricken dangerous and it is..

    Powerbomb: Paul Wright aka Big Show got his neck almost broken when Kevin Nash slipped up.. there is no way for the victim to not get the wind knocked out of him according to Mick Foley in his book "Have A Nice Day"

    Michinoku Driver: like the Piledrivers, only less protection for the guy taking it..

    Dragon Suplex: double-underhook belly to back suplex.. the guy taking it has no defense and lands on the back of the neck...

    Death Valley Driver: what Cena's FU used to be.. where a fireman's carry turns into a driver instead of a slam... looks damned scary and can be dangerous if not done right.. Perry Saturn used to do it very well..

    Brain Buster: something like a vertical suplex but guy taking it lands on his head

    Steiner Screwdriver: a vertical suplex turned into a piledriver

    Burning Hammer: a torturerack reverse Death Valley Driver

    Ligerbomb: something like a powerbomb but when guy taking it is upside down, he's driven into the mat...

    there are many more.. all if done with malice, can kill or maim a person outright...
