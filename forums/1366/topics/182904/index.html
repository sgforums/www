    Originally posted by coffeeortea:
    i agree, i have encouraged her to consult a psychologist, she is very reluctant though.
    I am not sure what kind of psychological problem your friend have, but if it is depression, it is best she seeks help as soon as possible. My cousin has mild depression 4 yrs ago and he kept it from us. When we found out (after 1 yr), he insisted that he kept it under control and it was ok. His guardians chose to believe him 'coz they did not know very much about depression. This problem drags until now. Last year, his condition grew so bad that it became early psychosis. He became suspicous of everything/everyone and treat everyone badly. His condition grew bad that we had to send him to IMH. The consultant of IMH told us if he was treated early, it might not turn into early psychosis.

    Consulting a psychologist is not really something to be ashamed of. They are there to help you. If money is the issue, ask your friend to go to a polyclinic and get a reference letter to a hospital that has Psychology department (Mount E or NUH...not sure Gleneagles have or not). That way, your consultation charges will be minimized.
