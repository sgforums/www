    Avalokitesvara, the Protector of those born in the year of the Rat

    Those born in the year of the Rat have, as their protector, the thousand-armed-thousand-eyed Avalokitesvara. In each of Avalokitesvara's hand is an "Eye". "Thousand" here means inexhuastibility and perfection. The thousand hands symbolise Avalokitesvara's great Compassion & skilful means. The thousand eyes are a symbol of His Wisdom and Perfect Vision. According to the Great Compassion Dharani, Avalokitesvara has the ability to benfit all living beings and to bless all to have harmonious relationships, wealth, abundance, success, longevity and fulfillment. Those born in the year of the Rat who carry with them the seed syllable of Avalokitesvara would be able to enjoy happy relationships and good career prospects.

    Akasagarbha, the Protector of those born in the year of the Ox and Tiger

    Those born in the year of the Ox & Tiger have, as their protector, Akasagarbha. Legend has it that this Bodhisattva kept His vows so purely and was so dilligent that His merits and wisdom is beyond measure. Akasagarbha's ability to benefit all sentient beings is without measure. He is like a wealthy man who helps suffering beings joyfully. He is thus worthy of the highest respect & offerings. Those born in the year of the Ox and Tiger who carry with them the seed syllable of Akasagarbha would enhance their luck & wealth.

    Manjushri, the Protector of those born in the year of the Rabbit

    Those born in the year of the Rabbit have, as their protector, Manjushri Bodhisattva who symbolises virtue, auspiciousness & joy. Manjushri is sometimes known as the Dharma Prince wieding the sword of wisdom subduing hordes of demons and cutting off all worries and delusions. His left hand holds a green lotus which bears the scroll of the Heart sutra whuch symbolises the wisdom realising the true nature of all things. Manjushri has made 18 kinds of great vows for Buddhahood. Those born in the year of the Rabbit who carry with them the seed syllable of Manjushri would be able to enhance their wisdom, career, marriage and dispel all obstacles.

    Samantabhadra, the Protector of those born in the year of the Dragon and Snake

    Those born in the year of the Dragon and Snake have, as their protector, Samantabhadra who is known as the King of vows. Samantabhadra is engage actively in His vows. The Lotus Sutra states whoever prays to Samantabhadra with faith would be blessed by Him & other great Bodhisattvas. Due to His great wisdom and merits, Samantabhadra has the ability to bestow long life. Those born in the year of the Dragon and Snake who carry with them the seed syllable of Samantabhadra would be able to dispel all obstacles and enhance their career prospects and wealth.

    Maha-sthma-prapta, the Protector of those born in the year of the Horse

    Those born in the year of the Horse have, as their protector, who Maha-sthma-prapta is known as Great Strength Bodhisattva. According to the Sutra of Limitless Life, Maha-sthma-prapta Bodhisattva perceives with His wisdom and lead all sentient beings out of the three lower realms.Thus He is considered to be the foremost in wisdom. Together with Avalokitesvara, he attend Amitabha Buddha. Amitabha, Avalokitesvara and Maha-sthma-prapta are known as the Triple Saints of the West. While Avalokitesvara symbolises Compassion, Maha-sthma-prapta symbolises Wisdom. Those born in the year of the Horse who carry with them the seed syllable of Maha-sthma-prapta would be able to pacify dangers relating to natural disasters and weapons. Beside that, It dispel all obstacles of this lifetime and enhances career prospects and wealth.

    Acala, the Protector of those born in the year of the Rooster

    Those born in the year of the Rooster have, as their protector, Acala Buddha. Difficulties are pacified as they arise. The vow of this Buddha is, "Whoever sees my form will generate Bodhicitta; whyoever hears my name will cut off delusions and practise virtue; whoever hears my speech will gain wisdom and whoever knows my mind will gains Buddhahood." Acala Buddha's Compassion is evident. Acala Buddha is a wrathful manifestation. This serves to subdue evil beings and cause beings on the Path to develop unshakable faith and Bodhicitta. Those born in the year of the Rooster who carry with them the seed syllable of Acala which grants them protection and perseverance on the Path. This would enhance their luck and wealth.

    Amitabha, the Protector of those born in the year of the Dog and Boar

    Those born in the year of the Dog and Boar have, as their protector, Amitabha who is known as the Buddha of Limitless light & life. Amitabha is the patron Buddha of the Western Pureland. Those born in the year of the Dog and Boar who carry with them the seed syllable of Amitabha would enhance their wealth and help them to meet with favourable conditions and transform potential difficulties into auspiciousness.
