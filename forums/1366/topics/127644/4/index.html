    Hydrodamalis cuestae
    Dugong Seacow

    Dugongidae

    Description

    Timeline: Pliocene Epoch

    Hydrodamalis cuestae is an extinct species of sea cow, distantly related to the living dugong and manatee. It was an aquatic herbivore and a member of the mammalian order Sirenia. It lacked teeth, probably lacked finger bones, and was very large. Similar to Stellar's sea cow, H. cuestae may have reached a maximum body length of about 9 meters (30 feet) and probably weighed close to 10 metric tons.

    The sirenians spread throughout the tropical and subtropical waters of the early Eocene Epoch, from Japan to Baja California. Their populations peaked during the Miocene Epoch. Today, the dugong (Dugong dugon) is the only surviving member of the Dugongidae family.

    In This Region

    Fossil remains of Hydrodamalis cuestae have been collected from Pliocene-age sandstones in Chula Vista and San Diego, California.

    On 23 May 2000 Pat Sena, a field paleontologist with the San Diego Natural History Museum, discovered a complete skull of the giant Pliocene sirenian (sea cow) Hydrodamalis cuestae at a construction site at Otay Ranch in Chula Vista.
