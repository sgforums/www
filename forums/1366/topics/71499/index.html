    ~*~Shining Tribe Tarot ~*~

    The Shining Tribe Tarot is the personal creation of well-known tarot author Rachel Pollack. Pollack was inspired by tribal and prehistoric art from around the world to draw these pictures and place them within a tarot context. They arose from her encounters with sacred art, stories and places. Each card shows the image in a gold-colored frame with the number and name at the bottom. The suits are aspects of nature: trees, rivers, stones and birds. The royal court cards have been removed completely, replaced by the Place, Knower, Gift and Speaker cards of each suit. The Shining Tribe Tarot was orignally published as the Shining Woman Tarot, after the figure on the World card (see left). Pollack changed the name to honor tribal wisdom and to recognize the "tribe" of tarot lovers the world over. This deck comes with the companion book The Shining Tribe Tarot: Awakening the Universal Spirit (360 pages). Pollack describes each card in detail and also offers a vision of the tarot written in her usual lucid, inspiring style.

    ~*~Ancient Tarot of Lombardy~*~

    This Ancient Tarot of Lombardy deck is a reproduction of a neoclassical deck published around 1810 in Italy. The original was printed from fine metal engraving plates and hand-colored. The name of the cardmaker F. Gumppenberg is given on the ace of cups (coppe). Tax stamps of the period can be seen on the ace of swords (spade). The card titles are in Italian, and majors are numbered in Roman numerals except for the Fool and Death cards. Numbers for the minor arcana are given in the upper left and lower right corners. The suit symbols follow the Spanish style in that the indiviudal wands (bastoni) and swords are shown separately on the numbered cards. In an interesting reversal of the norm, the Lovers card shows a woman between two men. This deck is published by Lo Scarabeo of Italy.
