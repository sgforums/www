    Beowulf is a 2007 fantasy film directed by Robert Zemeckis. The film is an adaptation of the Anglo-Saxon epic poem Beowulf and was made using a special technique of digitally enhanced live action (utilizing, among other technologies, an advanced form of motion capture). Beowulf will be released on November 16, 2007 and stars Ray Winstone, Anthony Hopkins, Robin Wright Penn, Dominic Keating, Alison Lohman, John Malkovich, Crispin Glover, Brendan Gleeson and Angelina Jolie.

    Based on the epic poem, the film is about a man who comes to Heorot in order to rid it of Grendel, the monstrosity who terrorizes it. What he does not expect is Grendel's Mother.

    While some of the film remains true to the original poem, other elements, such as the dialogue, the emphasized hubris of Beowulf and Hrothgar, the perceived hedonism in Heorot, the span of time before Beowulf fights the dragon, Grendel's motive for attacking Heorot, and the relationship between Grendel's mother and Beowulf deviate from the original poem.
