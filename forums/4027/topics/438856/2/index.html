    1. The money you paid is to LGM Pte Ltd.

     

    2. LGM is only a marketing company and once the Private Limited Company closes down, it is almost impossible to get back your money from the local Singaporean Director as he/she is protected by limited liabilities which are the nature of Pte Ltd.

     

    3. LGM sells services from CVC, not on CVC's behalf.

     

    4. Thus, CVC is not responsible for your contract which is with LGM.

     

    5. For Cash Back, CVC in Spain may not pay you back the amount or is pro-rated to a few hundred dollars as they claimed that LGM did not pay them the money they collected from you.

     

    6. For records, the cash rewards forms indicated that CVC do not have to answer to your queries regarding the cash rewards.

     

    7. Many others marketing companies also sell services from CVC and have closed down.

     

    8. Many others new marketing companies have also took over the records from these companies and have also made claims such as termination with refund or switching over with additional amount etc.

     

    9. According to SG authorities, these marketing companies did not break the law and not many actions could be taken against them.

     

    ==========

     

    Companies are setup by people who want to earn money in the same concept to an employee looking for a job.

     

    If you believe that there is a possibility of an employee who seek a job in a company, work for the company, does not take ANY pay and yet pays the company a sum of money, then the same concept of a company which claims that they can do things for you free is possible.

     

    If they request that you pay them first, then they fit into the company that is looking for a profit, here you would want to be careful on the legal representation that they claim they can do.

     

    You might want to check with the authorities before making payment.

     

    Also, there is a consumer protection system in place for you to recover your money (read the fine prints in your contracts, or at times, not even there, so ask what the time frame you are allowed to terminate is)

     

    For termination, time and actions are crucial; any amount of delay is proportional to the reduction in probability of you getting your money back.

     

    Pro-active is the work here. Use the laws to protect yourself.

     

    A point to note, scammers are highly intelligent personnel, probably with a higher than average IQ, and knows the social, legal system well enough to manipulate it in such a way to avoid any legal implications.

     

    The only two ways to play their game is 1. Do not get involve in the first place. Frankly, only your own desires, which are constantly exploited by them, will be the cause of your wrong decision. 2. Play their game with a better understanding of the laws then them and outwit them at every junction and tactics they throw at you.

     

    These people are not actors, they are just employee of the company who told them what to do, so usually they are very real in their emotions, thus allowing you to believe in them, remember, they get paid when they make money (work) for the company.

     

    Good Luck.
