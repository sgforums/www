    Hmm.... cavitation bubble....

    For that to occur, the shrimp's limb had to be moving so fast that the pressure in the water flow surrounding the limb would drop below the vapour pressure, allowing for dissolved (cavitation) bubbles to come out of the solution.

    When cavitation bubbles collapse, very high local temperatures and pressures can result.

    In extreme cases, they can damage turbines and impellers..... in less extreme cases, cavitation bubbles are in fact used in ultrasonic cleaning equipment.
