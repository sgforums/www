    OK, visited http://www.dove.com/real_beauty/news_library.asp and here's the driving force behind their recent ads :

    "What is real beauty? Join the debate...

    "A new definition of beauty is needed. One that's not defined by unrealistic media images. Let's widen the definition of beauty and inspire women to celebrate themselves. ... ...

    "Our mission : To make women feel beautiful everyday by widening today's stereotypical view of beauty and inspiring women to take great care of themselves.

    Apparently, when Unilever first published the ad with six women of various ages, sizes, and race in the UK, there was some sort of 'media furor' and it even 'sparked a debate on society's narrow definition of beauty, particularly concerning everyday women with real bodies'.

    Dove even commissioned a global study, 'The Real Truth About Beauty : A Global Report' where Unilever partnered with research firm, Strategy/One in collaboration with Dr Nancy Etkoff and the Massachusetts General Hospital / Harvard Program in Aesthetics and Well Being and Dr Susie Orbach of the London School of Economics. The study participants were some 3,200 women from 10 countries.

    "The study's most startling find, according to Dove, is that only 2% of the women surveyed consider themselves beautiful, and only 9% feel comfortable describing themselves as attractive. Some 49% responded that women who are more beautiful have greater opportunities in life. And, while they allege the media consistently bombards society with images of idealised women, the majority of respondents strongly agree beauty can be achieved through attitude and spirit. What's more, 85% said every woman has something beautiful about her.

    Now, that's food for thought, and fodder for discussion!
