What the Colour of your Phlegm Means

  • Rhonda
    30 Jan 08, 08:14

    This is not exactly a very inviting title, but I dedicate this thread to our poor fudgester, who is complaining of coughing up clear phlegm.

    Did you know that the colour of your phlegm is an important indication of :

    -the sort of infection you are having, or even if it is an infection at all;

    - the type of treatment that is most effective for your condition; and

    - the severity of your illness? 

     

    So, here's a quick summary of what your phlegm means :

    White / Pale / Clear :  May be a viral infection and antibiotics are not necessary.

    Yellow / Green Phlegm : Bacterial infection and your body is currently fighting hard. Antibiotics may help.

    Brown : Usually from smokers but may indicate the presence of blood, in which case, go to the doc!!

    Red : Blood.  See a doc stat! 

     

    Disclaimer : This information is not intended to be used as a conclusive diagnostic tool.  Please see your doctor for comprehensive treatment!

    Originally posted by dumbdumb!:

    yeah i heard green phlegm means got infection. i hate phlegm la.. but its normal to keep producing it right?

    Phlegm is actually made up of the dead cells of the foreign body that's launching an invasion in your body.  The white blood cells attack these foreign cells, and they get expelled as blobs of green or yellow phlegm.

    And to answer your question, it is not 'normal' to keep producing it all your life.  But, if you're ill with, say, the flu, then yes, during the course of your illness, it is 'normal' to have that, but as you get well, it should slowly subside, and then disappear altogether.
