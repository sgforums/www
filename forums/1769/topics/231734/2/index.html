    Aussie experts to drive review of Singapore's public transport system

    The big job of reviewing Singapore's public transport system Â— from the nuts and bolts to the service standards Â— has gone to the Australians.

    In its first move to rope in an external consultant, the Land Transport Authority (LTA) has appointed the Australian arm of United States-based consulting firm Booz Allen Hamilton for the comprehensive study.

    Over the next six months, the company will review Singapore's current industry structure and regulatory framework for its bus and rail systems.

    And in the process, some fundamental questions could be answered.

    Madam Cynthia Phua, a member of Government Parliamentary Committee for transport, thinks a comprehensive review of the system cannot escape the question of whether the Singapore market is big enough for two public transport operators.

    "Does having two public transport operators allow for competition in terms of efficiency and profitability?" she told TODAY.

    Mooted by Minister for Transport Raymond Lim in October, the idea of appointing external consultants will allow LTA to explore more options.

    These could include best practices in other cities, identifying areas for improvements, and proposing measures to strengthen the system, the LTA said on Thursday.

    "The consultants will add value to the review process with their international experience while providing an external perspective to current viewpoints, standards and systems," it added.

    The Singapore public transport system sees about four million commuter trips daily. The LTA aims to increase the share of commuters from the current 63 per cent to 70 per cent over the next 10 to 15 years.

    For this to happen, chairman of the Public Transport Council Gerard Ee told TODAYy that timing and scheduling are critical.

    "For more public commuters to switch to public transport, reliability is key and ultimately factors such as waiting times, travelling times and scheduling matter," he said.

    Mr Ee said the review could look into road design and development so that bus routes can be more efficiently mapped out Â— for example, dedicated bus lanes can be considered.

    Booz Allen Hamilton is expected to submit their findings to the LTA in the middle of this year for review and evaluation. - TODAY/st
