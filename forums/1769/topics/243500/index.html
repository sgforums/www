    A few weeks ago, honeymouse, mousehubby, myself, Bear's sis, and another Singaporean friend packed ourselves into two cars and drove all the way to an Apple Orchard somewhere in the nether regions of Ringwood to pick buckets of apples!

    Before we embarked on our labourious session, we made sure to fill our tummies first! First stop - a lovely Dim Sum Lunch!



    In the background, you can see honeymouse poised with her chopsticks, waiting patiently for me to finish snapping my photo before digging in!

    Next, we started the long drive to the Apple Orchard! Under the sweltering hot 'n' dry heat, I almost fell asleep in the car during the ride. Luckily, I wasn't driving!

    Finally, a welcome sight!! The road that seems to go on forever and ever...



    Yes, it was a huge, huge Orchard with, I think, more than 10 varieties of apples growing in plots of land! I knew I should've taken a photo of the blackboard in the barnhouse showing the different types of apples that are ready for the picking on different months! Hmm... there were Granny Smith, Golden Delicious, ... what else har, honeymouse?
  • Rhonda
    19 Mar 07, 15:55
    It was a really huge orchard! The apple trees were neatly planted in rows. Actually, I expected the apple trees to be much taller than they were. I thought we had to climb up ladders to pick our apples but nope... they were quite short, only a bit taller than us.



    Only the apples in season during that time could be picked. We were allowed to pick and eat as many as we liked! I think mousehubby managed to chuck the most apples there. I forgot my digicam and had to walk all the way back to the car to get it and by the time I returned, there were two buckets filled with Royal Gala apples already! They were really easy to pick, if they are ripe, you just hold them, lift them up a little and gently give an upward twist and they'll fall off readily!

    These were the apples in season at the time we went :



    Look at this branch, heavy with ripe apples, ready for the picking!!



    Sadly, there were lots of apples on the ground, slowly rotting away. What a waste!
  • Rhonda
    19 Mar 07, 15:56
    Despite the drought, and the Stage 3 Water Restrictions in Victoria, the orchard and various other farms are permitted to irrigate their crops for a fee. That's why it's so green here.



    In the background, you can see how parched the earth is. The sun here is really strong and has been scorching a lot of fields so you don't see a lot of green fields now. The situation is aggravated by the drought. We sorely need rain... in the right areas, esp the water catchment areas.

    Just now, whilst in K-mart, there was news on the radio saying that one of the reservoirs here have enough water to last Victoria for only 60 days! The newsreader also commented that though we are in such dire conditions, the govt still has yet to do SOMETHING about it!

    And finally, ta-daaaah!! My very own darling little windowsill garden! My little babies! They are succulents and are drought-friendly and they are real survivors and difficult to kill! Good for people like me, who don't have a green thumb!
