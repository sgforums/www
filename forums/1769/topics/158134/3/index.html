    I think before we get carried away, maybe reading the ROM website itself would help.

    As it is clearly stated on the ROM website, the solemnisation at ROM can take place at those fancy rooms provided by Love that Binds or the basic rooms that are provided by the government. The number of basic rooms is limited (from what I can remember getting married there), so it is highly possible that demand outstrip supply.

    If the situation is that bad, the constructive thing to do is to write to either Feedback Unit or the press to ask ROM to allocate more rooms for use by people who don't want to pay extra to Love that Binds.

    I am writing this because there appears to be some insinuation that the online service is somehow rigged to favour the higher price option. This is unfair to those people who implemented the system. As the system was most likely outsourced to a private sector vendor for implementation, if there was something amiss, people in the IT industry like myself would have heard of it.

    Err actually I felt that to register ur marriage at ROM is very ...
    industrial. Pop in.. say your words... pop out. A bit like being on those production belt.

    It is much more convenient to just call up a JP (out of the huge list provided) and do it at the venue u want, the time u like...

    The cost of inviting a JP is really up to you. They do it as a volunteer work. I just gave a small angpow for good luck and a little gift of 'flower tea'. Popular JPs help to make the atmosphere more 'fun' but they are very difficult to book. I got a friend to recommend me one -beams at friend- and he was pretty good too. If you want, can pass you his contact.

    I did it at Changi beach so venue is free. So the only other thing I paid for was food. Actually parks are great. Just find those with ready made shelters and get a friend to 'chope' it for you lah!
