    Tremors felt in parts of Singapore as quake hits Sumatra
    By Hoe Yeen Nie, Channel NewsAsia | Posted: 25 November 2007 1309 hrs

    SINGAPORE: Tremors were felt in several parts of Singapore on Sunday morning after an earthquake struck off the western coast of Indonesia's Sumatra.

    Singapore's Meteorological Services said a magnitude-6.5 quake hit Sumatra in an area 560 kilometres away from Singapore at about 10.51am.

    Indonesian meteorologists put the quake at magnitude 6.2.

    The National Environment Agency (NEA) said there have been reports of shocks in Kampong Glam and Whampoa.

    Callers to the MediaCorp News Hotline also said they felt tremors in the Central and Eastern parts of the island, including Farrer Road and Katong.
