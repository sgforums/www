    I'm surprised our resident Missy hasn't addressed the topic.

    I'm also quite surprised also that many of you said milk.

    Milk can cause bowel irritation in people with lactose intolerance. What this means is that these people lack the ability to break down lactose (the sugar which is found in milk). As sugars is the primary source of nutrients for bacteria (which can be found in large numbers in the large intestine), they would grow "out of control" and give you problems.

    Most gastric pain is caused by acidity in the stomach. The stomach, being one of the main organs for the digestion, is naturally highly acidic (pH 2-3) due to the secretion of hydrochloric acid by group of cells lining the stomach lumen. Acid production can be stimulated by the presence of food, irritants (such as spices) or even the thought/sight of food.

    Acidity is usually neutralize by the food. However, if the stomach is empty, prolonged acidity results in irritation of the stomach lining and formation of stomach ulcers.

    Tea is acidic and thus not recommended on an empty stomach. Tea also appears to stimulate acid production (and that's the reason why Chinese tea consumed after a rich meal can aid digestion).

    Milk, though acidic (pH 5), is less acidic and can neutralize the acidity in the stomach. So it's often taken to alleviate symptoms of gastric irriation/pain. Doctors often prescribe milk of magnesiun (magnesium carbonate) for the same purpose.

    Other spicy foods mentioned can also worsen gastric pains as they can stmulate the secretion of more hydrochloric acid into the stomach lumen.

    Basically avoid acidic or spicy food when your stomach is irritable.
