    If you're really interested to learn salsa, just go for the classes. You don't need kakis to take lessons together. The whole thing about salsa that makes it different from latin or ballroom is that you don't need a fixed partner.

    To see what salsa is all about in Singapore, head down to Union Square at Amara. It's on the 2nd floor and is open daily except Sundays. Ladies Night on Wednesdays so more girls that night.

    If you want to dance socially, I recommend that you take lessons from schools that have their brochures in Union Square. Not every dance school teaches you the correct thing. A lot of schools teaches choreographed moves that you cannot execute on a social dance floor with strangers so choose the right school. Ask the right people who actually dance salsa socially and not someone who took lessons but never dance at all. If not you will not get the satisfaction and confidence which is supposed to come from dancing salsa.

    Usually there are more girls in a class than guys. It is harder for the guys to learn initially but overall the guys become better dancers in the end due to the technicality requirements from proper leading and experience. Most people who take the classes are single to start off and I have seen many who end up as couples and getting married.

    Singapore have a small active salsa community and every year we have our own Singapore International Salsa Festival. This year it will be in November. It can only keep going on from the growing number of salseras and salseros.

    It's not difficult to learn salsa. If you can walk, you can salsa. I have been dancing salsa for slightly over 2 years and have always wondered how good it will be if I had started earlier and younger. Don't wait anymore if you really have the intention to start.

    Don't worry about kakis. I started it alone. Now the whole Union Square is my kaki. And if you're shy, don't worry, I'm always around to help :)
