  • Rhonda

    There are so many of us animal lovers in Club 30.  Thought it would be interesting to share funny anecdotes with each other here.

    I'll start the ball rolling by talking about my cat, Shona.  I unofficially adopted this tuxedo stray cat off the streets.  It used to seek warmth by lying on a neighbour's doormat and would run away each time it saw me coming home after a late shift.  I started to feed it, gradually gained its trust, eventually persuaded it to come indoors to be fed, and now, 1 yr and 1 mth later, it is a collared cat with its own feeding bowl and cat bed.  It's still a part-time pet cause it loves to gallivant outdoors in the 'hood and only comes for dinner and occasionally, for b'fast and lunch. 

    So... this is what my cat has taught me -

    For trusting me enough to let me stroke its underbelly - I am trustworthy.

    For liking to snooze on my lap and getting angry when I fidget just a wee bit so I can't move once Her Royal Highness lies on my lap - I am disciplined.

    For giving me a dirty look when I turn on the noisy cooler when it gets hot, so I can't use it at all cause it disturbs Her Royal Highness - I can conquer all physical discomfort and suffering.

    For looking at the offerings in the food bowl distatefully and giving me a snotty look - I can do better.

    For running away when I approach her sometimes, and then running after me when I turn and walk away - I will not get what I want all of the time.

    For being able to distinguish her different meows to know what she wants - I am a good communicator.

    For pausing at the doorway, surveying my apartment and then turning her head to give me a haughty look full of rebuke - I need to tidy up my messy apartment. 

    For coming up to me and me only, refusing everyone else in the block - I am 'special'.

    And lastly, for following me out on the streets and escorting me a fair bit (for a cat) - I am loved and will be missed.
