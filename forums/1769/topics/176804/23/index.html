    I woke up not too long ago. Somehow, not feeling well. Body feels warmer than usual inside, and nose feels a tad stuffed-up. Uh oh... Foreboding...

    So, apparently, I was a staff there and I decided to go check on the snakes. Took a lift up there, walked towards the huge enclosure, and had the SHOCK of my life!! The snakes had become HUGE!! Three times the length of an average human, and their girth was like the waist of a petite girl's! The enclosure was SWARMING with them, and they were all white snakes with yellow markings on them. Some of them had three or four heads!

    I quickly walked past, wondering how in the world had this enclosure become like that, then it struck me that perhaps, all the staff had 'forgotten' about the snake exhibit since it was located at the rooftop. I turned to a colleague and we headed towards the staircase on the other end, wondering what to do, when suddenly, the snakes started to slam themselves against a part of the metal mesh... and then, the metal mesh gave way!!! They spilled out of their enclosure and started to eat up people!! We ran for our lives!!

    Then, the snakes entered the building and caused great mayhem! Lots of folks were eaten up. They swarmed the city, eating up everyone in sight!

    The entire dream was a lot of running and close shaves and escaping and all that. There was even a mad escape via car! Ended up, I rounded up a few folks, ran into a building that was also overrun by the snakes, but we managed to barricade ourselves into a room with windows where we could watch the snakes outside whilst we waited for our deaths...



    Then, I woke up.

    Found an interpretation of 'Snake' in dreams :

    "Snake

    To see a snake or be bitten by one in your dream, signifies hidden fears and worries that are threatening you. Your dream may be alerting you to something in your waking life that you are not aware of or that has not yet surfaced. The snake may also be seen as phallic and thus symbolize dangerous and forbidden sexuality. The snake may also refer to a person around you who is callous, ruthless, and can't be trusted. As a positive symbol, snakes represent transformation, knowledge and wisdom. It is indicative of self-renewal and positive changes. "
