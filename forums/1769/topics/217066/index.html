    Originally posted by dragg:
    how do you draw a line in the first place?

    That's exactly what I meant by the govt should empower senior/supervising govt officers with equitable discretion.

    To list what falls within equity would be to go against equity itself. The very essence of equity is natural justice - i.e. doing what is inherently right.

    Off the top of my head, I'd say -

    Those who deserve it - single parents, senior citizens etc
    Those who need it - families/singles with no viable means of survival (i.e. impoverished)

    Generally, a good number of cases from the former, would also fall within the latter.

    What is needed is freedom within administration, that is defined by certain perimeters and guided by specific rules.

    In any case, not to leave the topic..

    I still think that singles should not qualify for any housing subsidy. I would not like to pay tax to help some kids leave home.

    Singapore is not a welfare State - and it should never be.

    However, in line with my proposition of procedural fairness and equity, I would say that situations can arise whereby singles might qualify for subsidy.

    And just for the sake of keeping those (singles) who applied and got rejected shut - the implementation of ombudsman, and administrative tribunals with possible avenues of judicial review to the supreme court.
