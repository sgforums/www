    After moving into this apartment (my first apartment) about 10 years ago, I didn't have any money for renovations. All my renovations were forced ones....

    I didn't realise that one side of my bathroom was made of gypsum board, covered with bathroom wall tiles (to cover up some pipes inside). One fine day, I was washing up, doing my usual song and mini dance routine under the shower....I slipped and my upper back slammed into the gypsum wall. Since it broke the wall, I had no choice but to replace the gypsum with an artifical marble slab. Contractor charged me $1000 for it!! I still think I got cheated.

    Then there were the rotting kitchen cabinets. The washing machine dumped water into a pipe below the kitchen floor. When the pipe got clogged, soapy water would rise from the manhole (in the middle of the kitchen floor) and flood the whole place. In time, this caused the kitchen cabinets to rot. Replaced it finally, at a cost of $1800 (not too bad....done by a contractor who was a family friend).

    Then there was the bedroom. When I moved in, it was covered with this thick dirty and dusty carpet which made the bedroom extremely stuffy. Sick of it, I tore out a corner with my own hands to see what was underneath. To my surprise, it was parquet. A bit scratched up, but still, it was parquet. I tore up the whole carpet, used my hand-held drill as a sander (rotating sandpaper) and manually went through the whole floor to even it out (damned tiring for the back), after which everything was covered with fine sawdust. Then I gave it two coats of glossy varnish and it turned out well - could see my reflection on the floor! Cheap.....just the price of sandpaper and varnish. If anyone needs to varnish their floors, please let me know....

    I also changed the kitchen and bathroom taps, replaced the toilet flushing system, and replaced the bathroom mirror and shelves myself. Cheap...just bought the stuff, carried them home and fixed them myself. The only problem was when I was walking around Geylang looking for the taps (there are many bathroom wares shops there). When I walked out of the shop, I happened to bump into a friend whose first sentence was "oh, you frequent this place too?".

    Thats about all the renovations I've done to my apartment.

    Er....I've done some mini renovations to my face too, if anyone is interested?
