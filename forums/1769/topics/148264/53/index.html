    ISO is a paper-wasting exercise, IMO. For the sake of death man, basically, ISO9002 records on paper, a set of standard procedures that your company say it will do in its operations. Auditors will check documents and interview staff to ensure co is actually doing what it says it will do before awarding the standard. ISO is a big thing esp in Europe - some co won't do business with non-ISO certified co.

    Experienced it in my ex-co, and in some instances, it actually slows down the work, eg previously I could called up my supplier and verbally asked him to do an urgent job for me and he would start immediately. With ISO, you have to issue purchase requisition first, get it approved, followed by issuing the purchase order, get it approved, send to the supplier before your supplier can start the job for you. All bec. the co's ISO standards said that this is the procedure the co will follow, so action must follow also.
