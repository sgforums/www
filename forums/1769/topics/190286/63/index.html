    Good news: personal property tax can be itemized as a deduction on Schedule A of Form 1040.

    Bad news: property taxes are usually a percentage of the municipally appraised value of your home. Cash-strapped municipalities are finding creative ways to generate more income, including inflating the appraised value of your home beyond the market worth in order to collect more property tax.

    Moral of the story: when you receive your Notice of Proposed Property Taxes at the beginning of the year, pay careful attention to the line items that the city feels adds value to your home. For example, if the notice lists your home as having a finished basement when in fact you don't, make sure you have the city do a reappraisal. They did this to me once.
