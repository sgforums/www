    Have you ever wondered how long you can keep foodstuff in the fridge/freezer for?

    Well, here's a list that you might find helpful! Please note that the period you can keep the following food refers to the number of days AFTER you've opened the package the food came in. Needless to say, if the Expiry Date is already up and you still haven't opened the package, discard it!

    Before proceeding though, be sure that you get a thermometer and ensure that your fridge temperature is about 1 to 4 deg C, and the freezer temperature -15 to -17 deg C. Using the numeric gauge in your fridge, ie. the turn-button with markings from 1-5 or more, is not accurate!


    DAIRY PRODUCTS & EGGS
    Fresh Milk - 5-7 days
    Butter / Margarine - 2 weeks
    Cheese Spreads - 3-4 weeks
    Eggs - 3 weeks
    Hard Boiled Eggs - 1 week
    Ice Cream - 2 months in freezer


    POULTRY AND MEAT
    Chicken or turkey, pieces - 1-2 days in fridge, 9-12 months in freezer
    Chicken or turkey, whole - 1-2 days in fridge, 1 year in freezer
    Ground meat or stew - 1-2 days in fridge, 3-4 months in freezer
    Lamb, roasts or chops - 3-5 days in fridge, 6-9 months in freezer
    Pork, roasts or chops - 3-5 days in fridge, 4-6 months in freezer
    Pre-stuffed pork and lamb chops or chicken breasts - 1 day in fridge
    Sausage - 1-2 days in fridge, 1-2 months in freezer
    Ham slices - 3-4 days in fridge, 1-2 months in freezer
    Hotdogs, luncheon meats (unopened) - 2 weeks in fridge, 1-2 months in freezer
    Hotdogs, luncheon meats (opened) - 3-7 days in fridge, 1-2 months in freezer
    Cooked, leftover meat - 3-4 days in fridge, 2-3 months in freezer
    Leftover gravy and meat broth - 1-2 days in fridge, 2-3 months in freezer
    Cooked, leftover poultry - 3-4 days in fridge, 4-6 months in freezer
    Cooked, leftover chicken nuggets or patties - 1-2 days in fridge, 1-3 months in freezer


    SEAFOOD

    Fresh lean fish: cod, flounder, trout, haddock, halibut, pollack, perch - 1-2 days in fridge, 4-6 months in freezer

    Fresh fatty fish: mullet, smelt, salmon,mackerel, bluefish, tuna and swordfish - 1-2 days in fridge, 2-3 months in freezer

    Shrimp, crabmeat - 2-3 days in fridge, 4 months in freezer


    FRUITS
    Here's the list for fruits. Basically, the faster you consume your fruits, the better! And gee, I didn't know that there are folks who'd freeze their fruits!
    Apples - 1 month in fridge, 8-12 months in freezer
    Apricots, grapes, nectarines, peaches, pears, plums - 3-5 days in fridge, 8-12 months in freezer
    Berries, cherries - 2-3 days in fridge, 8-12 months in freezer
    Grapefruit, lemons, limes, oranges - 2 weeks in fridge, 4-6 months in freezer
    Guavas, papayas - 1-2 days in fridge, 8-12 months in freezer
    Kiwis - 3-5 days in fridge, 4-6 months in freezer
    Mangoes - 8-12 months in freezer
    Melons - 1 week in fridge, 8-12 months in freezer
    Pineapple - 2-3 days in fridge, 4-6 months in freezer

    ...Continuation

    FRESH VEGETABLES

    Carrots - 2 weeks in fridge, 8-12 months in freezer
    Beans, broccoli, peas - 3-5 days in fridge, 8-12 months in freezer
    Cauliflower - 1 week in fridge, 8-12 months in freezer
    Cilantro, parsley - 2-3 days in fridge
    Corn - use immediately for best flavor, 8-12 months in freezer
    Green onions - 3-5 days in fridge
    Celery, cabbage, chilies, green beans, peppers, tomatoes - 1 week in fridge, 8-12 months in freezer
    Greens: kale, mustard, spinach, chai seem, kai lan - 3-5 days in fridge, 8-12 months in freezer
    Lettuce and salad greens - 1 week in fridge
    Mushrooms, okra - 1-2 days in fridge, 8-12 months in freezer


    BAKED PRODUCTS
    Bread, yeast - 6-12 months in freezer
    Muffins, rolls, quick breads - 2-4 months in freezer
    Pancakes and waffles - 1-2 months in freezer
    Cookies - 4-6 months in freezer
    Unbaked dough - 2-3 days in fridge, 6 months in freezer
    Angel and sponge cakes - 4-6 months in freezer
    Cheesecake - 3-7 days in fridge, 4-6 months in freezer
    Fruitcake (NOT the fresh fruits with cream sort! ) - 1 year in freezer
    Layer cake (butter cream icing or plain) - 6 months in freezer

    For PASTRIES: Store at room temperature. Best used within 1 to 3 days
    Danish Pastries - 3 months in freezer
    Doughnuts - 3 months in freezer
    Fruit pie - 1-2 days in fridge, 1 year in freezer


    If you find that any of the foodstuff listed above does not have recommended storage dates for BOTH fridge and freezer, then it means that the one that's left out is NOT the recommended option either due to food safety, or quality issues.

    Remember, this list only applies IF your fridge temperature is in compliance to the recommended one. I'd recommend you get an alcohol thermometer instead of a mercury thermometer, for use in the fridge and freezer.

    These are just guidelines and if you do not follow proper storage practices, these storage times do not apply!

    Part II!

    Seasoning your wooden chopping board

    Before using a new wooden board, season it to prevent staining and absorption of food odors and bacteria.

    Purchase a bottle of mineral oil that is edible and tasteless. Vegetable and Olive oils cannot be used because they can turn rancid.

    Pour the oil into a container and warm the oil slightly. Using a soft cloth, apply the warmed oil in the direction of the grain, allowing the oil to soak in. You'll need 4-5 coats for this initial seasoning.

    After each treatment, wait about four to six hours and wipe off oil that did not soak into the wood (oxidation or hardening of the oil will take approximately 6 hours).

    Re-oil the butcher block monthly or as often as needed.

    Oiling the chopping board will seal the grain against bacteria. An oil finish helps to prevent the wood from cracking or pulling apart at the seams.
