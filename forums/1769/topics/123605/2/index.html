    Originally posted by shade343:
    Popular and Borders sucks. The Books are super expensive...I recommend you go Brash Basah area there. Da Vinci Code $10
    Bras Basah bookstores have a very limited range of books. They're good for textbooks and popular fiction but if you're looking for non-fiction books, it's still Kinokuniya and Borders that reign.

    Besides, the bookstores at Bras Basah are mostly set up for quick sales, not for casual browsing. I can spend half a day or more at Kino or Borders but cannot imagine doing so in Bras Basah.

    In defence of Popular, I'd like to highlight that at times, they do have non-fiction books at really good prices. For instance, I just bought a boxed set of a Yoga Mat, a Yoga instruction book with full-colour photos on every page, and a VCD for only $39.90. (Or was it $29.90?) I also recently bought a Forensic Science Encyclopedia for just $8.90. Stuff like that...

    Popular also brings in cookbooks and recipe mags from Malaysia and Hong Kong that aren't available elsewhere.

    After a few months, my favourite bookstore is still 'ddddddrrrrrrrrrrr.....(drumroll)... QIANG!!!' Kinokuniya!

    They carry a wide selection of books of all genres but I'm really impressed with the range of non-fiction books they carry. Of course, they could do with yet more titles on their shelves but it's really impressive enough.

    Borders also carry a wide range of titles and I think their cookbooks section has far more titles than Kinokuniya's. However, Borders does not have any privilege cards nor loyalty programs, and their magazines are generally priced dearer than those at Kino so Kino still wins hands down. When it comes to fiction though, there's this '3 for the price of 2' promotion at Borders that's attractive.
