    I don't quite know how to put it but I kinda have trouble believing that two patches placed one on each foot can remove toxins from our body. I've been pondering over this and I'm no genius but I do have some thoughts on this.

    The kidneys and liver are the organs that deal with toxins in us. An external instrument that can do a somewhat equivalent job would be a dialysis machine. It has a filter and also uses the principle of osmosis to draw out unwanted elements in our bloodstream, then channels the 'purified' and 'cleansed' blood back to the body.

    A patch that you stick on a localised area wouldn't have the ability to draw out stuff from your bloodstream, I think. It would have to exert quite a large osmotic force as it would have to go through the epidural, subdural and dermal layers before reaching the blood vessels. From a medical standpoint, I kinda have difficulty thinking that a patch would be able to achieve all this.

    Drug patches are different since it works on the principle of diffusion. It's easier to diffuse into our body than for osmosis to happen cause of the pressure involved, I guess.

    Maybe, the patch can draw toxins and substances out locally but I'd find it hard to think that it can achieve that for the entire body.

    Just my two cents' worth...
