    After listening to the CD a few times, I think that whilst Loxian is plain gibberish, it's appropriate in giving an ethereal, out-of-this-world feel to the songs. It does give an 'elfish' feel to it, that LOTR magical feel, and I think that's why they took creative liberty with using Loxian on a few of the songs in her new album.

    Afterall, when we listen to Opera or J-pop, K-pop, or even the recent Numa-Numa song that's not in English / Mandarin, don't the lyrics also appear gibberish to us?

    But language has never stopped anyone from appreciating a piece of music, and conveyance of mood and ambience need not hinge on language and understanding alone - music permeates straight to the soul and speaks the language of the heart and soul.

    So, Loxian or no Loxian, I applaud their creative direction in this album.
