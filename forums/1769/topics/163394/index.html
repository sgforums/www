    Just woke up with a start because I was dreaming of working in an American MNC and there was this new expat colleague from the US here with his wife, both working in the same firm. The man turned to me one day and confided that his marriage is on the rocks and that they're heading for divorce. Funny I'd dream of such a thing...

    Maybe, it was cause after night duty this morning, a colleague was talking to me about her failing marriage and impending divorce. She also told me to be very very careful when choosing a marriage partner and to be doubly vigilant and keep both eyes wide open when choosing a mate, but after marriage, to keep one eye closed to his faults and what-not.

    Scary... more and more couples around us are resorting to divorce as a final option to end unhappy marriages. What's happening? Is it cause they both made the wrong choices right from the start? Is it cause we now have a thwarted and warped concept of love and marriage? Or, is it cause we are now more self-serving, impatient, and no longer long-suffering?

    Sometimes, I'm really glad that I'm still single! At least, I won't have a partner who'll give up on me and want to walk out on me. That would surely rip me apart!

    I think in this day and age when women are financially independent, the 'long-suffering' on one partner's part cannot work in the long term, unless it's a minor issue which can be forgiven or ignored.

    And yes, making a marriage work is hard work, and not just about the living happily ever after through rose-tinted glasses. Getting married is just the beginning, and right after that comes the hard work of making things work, and as both live together, adapting to each other and their families, the expected obligations, etc. Suddenly, you're thinking and making decisions not just for yourself anymore, but for your other half as well, and sometimes considerations/sacrifices too have to be made to cater for what his family might think or expect.

    I believe communication cannot be overrated - I find it's always the key to building up the close bonds and furthering mutual understanding of each other (their changing needs and expectations) as both develop and grow throughout the marriage. Sadly, I find that it is usually the breakdown of communication and/or the lack of such a mutual understanding which leads to the beginning of the end of the marriage.

    Just my 2cts.

    Children think in simple terms and I guess if they see their parents going through a divorce, and the nasty fights and what-not that occurs before the divorce is finalised, they might form the conclusions in their little innocent minds:

    - love is conditional and can be withdrawn;
    - you have to work hard to earn someone's love and stay loveable;
    - did I do something wrong to cause mommy and daddy to fight and be unhappy?

    When kids are growing up, child psychologists believe that it's very important for them to grow up in a stable, secure and loving home, not a tempestous one where mommy and daddy are constantly at each other's throats, or both are totally bo-chup about each other. They're at their formative years where they build their self-esteem and basic concept of the world and relationships at that time, so seeing such a scary thing as mommy no longer loving daddy or daddy abandoning the family for another woman can totally shatter them to the core.

    Children need to grow up in a loving home... they shouldn't have to be subject to such psychological and emotional trauma.
