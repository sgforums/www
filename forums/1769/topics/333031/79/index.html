    The Age of Greed

    It seems like ages since the last time I wrote something in this blog. It’s not that there’s nothing to write about, but the times we are living in seems so depressing that I guess I’d rather play Warhammer than write something that will only make me angry.

    This is the age of greed that we’re living in. Everything that is wrong with the world is the result of greed. Money is not the root of evil, rather it is the love of money that is the root of all evil. Environment devastation, melamine in dairy products, the collapse of gigantic financial institutions and the relentless hikes that we Singaporeans have to bear with on a seemingly monthly basis- it’s all the result of greed.

    I think the age of greed was officially inaugurated the moment Gordon Gekko uttered the immortal phrase- “Greed is good” in the movie Wall Street. Whereas greed used to be part of the seven deadly sins, these days it seems more like the defining characteristic of the business and political elites. Everywhere you see, it seems like only the greediest motherfuckers will make it to the top. Bank CEOs, Presidents, Prime Ministers and hell, even CEOs of charity organizations. You couldn’t swing a dead cat without it hitting some greedy fucker these days.

    I am tired of it.

    I am tired because my country seems to be governed by the principle of greed. How else do you explain the rounds and rounds of pay increment for our millionaire politicians while the income of the middle and lower classes stagnate. How else do you explain the rounds and rounds of price hikes we have to endure- in good times or bad times by our GLCs while they post yearly record profits.

    In our parliamentary sessions, the only one who seemed to care about the poor is Lily Neo- who has to shout just get the rest of her compatriots to hear her out. Contrast their disinterest with that of their passionate arguments for more ministerial fair hikes.

    Greed is inherent in all men in varying degree. Nobody starts a business without wanting to make a handsome profit. I can accept that. But sometimes the profit motive can get out of control and that is the time when we need the government to step in to regulate businesses so that it don’t get out of control. But here in Singapore, where we have the same small group of people incharge of both politics and big businesses, when push comes to shove, how certain are we, that the politicians will stand on the side of the people.

    Thus far, I’ve yet to see one person stand up in parliament and speak against these prices hikes and fair hikes. Instead what we have is them speaking in favour of and justifying those hikes. Forgive me for naively thinking these politicians are supposed to be working for my welfare.

    Here we have so-called union “leaders” telling us pay increases is not the answer to inflation and their increasing the pay of the lower classes will only lead to more inflationary pressures. Meanwhile state owned and controlled power companies with billion dollar profits are allowed to increase their electricity tariffs by a whopping 21%. Not content, the public transportation companies and telecommunication companies are also following suit.

    So pardon me, while I take one minute to process this- so all these hikes are not going to lead to even more inflationary pressures?
