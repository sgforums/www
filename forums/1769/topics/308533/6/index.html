    There are essentially three kinds of cuffs - barrel, French and convertible.  Barrel cuffs are the sort that you most commonly find, and these are the ones that close with buttons.  French cuffs fold over themselves and require cuff links.  Convertible cuffs are like barrel cuffs but have eyelets where the buttons are, so that they can take cuff links.  I much prefer French cuffs because they keep the cuff links from rattling around, unlike the convertibles which are too thin.

    Given that they're subtle enough, I like stripes.  All of my suits are chalk-striped, where the lines are less solid and more widely spaced than pinstripes.  Since my days at university, people around me have come to recognise my wearing narow-striped shirts with trouble - that's when I'm out for blood.
