    I've always frowned upon the government's efforts to wipe out Singlish.

    Singlish is something which naturally evolved out of the efforts of the various races of our society to integrate and get to know each other. As a young nation with a relatively short history, we don't have much of an identity, Singlish is one of the few things that defines who we are. If anything, it is one of the few things that make us truly, uniquely, Singaporean.

    The government's efforts to discourage Singlish has helped to reinforce the perception in some people that the only way to speak 'good' English is by speaking it with a foreign accent. I remember reading parents who send their children to special English language courses demanding that their children be taught to speak with a foreign accent.

    Sheesh... have we dropped so low as to think that our own local accent is inferior? Not everything from the West is good, alright.

    Still, do not glibly assume that all locals who speak with a foreign accent are all faking it. Trust me, some of them are not. I should know. I'm mixed Indian and Malay. Somehow, after my voice broke at puberty, I developed a Brit-like accent. I know I'm not faking it, cos my brother has my voice, and both my sisters have slight accents in their voices as well.

    Quite a number of people shunned me back in Sec 3 just because of my voice... it took months before they finally woke up their bloody ideas.
