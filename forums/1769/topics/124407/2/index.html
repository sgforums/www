    rhon dearie, most cars usually have 4 cylinders. arranged in 1 straight line.

    basically, some engines layouts u can find are:

    inline - cylinders in a straight line
    flat - cylinders on each side. imagine cylinders in a V, but the angle of the V is 180 degrees
    V - 2 banks of cylinders in a V shape, know angles include a range from 62 degrees to 120 degrees
    W - basically 4 banks of cylinders forming a W shape, although strictly speaking, they form something that looks more like 2 Vs joined at the base

    having talked about the layout, i'll name some engines configs that have appeared b4, and name an example

    inline 3 - mitsubishi colt, smart for2 coupe
    inline 4 - honda civic, mitsubishi lancer
    inline 5 - volvo S40 T5
    flat 4 - subaru impreza
    V4 - ford Corsair
    inline 6 - BMW M3, nissan skyline
    V6 - mercedes SLK350, lexus RX300
    flat 6 - porsche 911, legacy spec.B
    V8 - ford mustang GT, mercedes mclaren SLR
    W8 - vw phaeton 4.2 w8
    V10 - porsche carrera GT, lamborghini gallardo
    V12 - mercedes SL65 AMG, lamborghini murcielago, pagani zonda, ferrari 575M
    W12 - vw phaeton 6.0 w12
