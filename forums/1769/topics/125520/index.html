    disclaimer: some of it might sound familiar. dun remember whether i read it before or i wrote it before. hahaa...

    ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

    Ever heard it being said... When God made human, he split the soul in two, putting half of it in Man and the other half in Woman. That is why we all go through life trying to find our other half. And only when we find him/her will we be truly complete.

    Going by this logic, we all have only ONE significant other. Surely this cannot be right.

    What if he is married (to someone else's S.O.!)?

    What if he met with an accident and died before we found each other?

    What if we passed each other by and never knew?

    What if he's gay???

    So, I don't believe in this. I believe we meet a lot of different people at different times in our lives. Right guys, wrong guys, right timing, wrong timing.

    The perfect one, undoubtedly, is the Right One at the Right Time.

    The question is: will it ever happen?

  • Rhonda
    6 Apr 05, 11:45
    I'd rather think that each of us is a complete circle and when we find that special someone, part of our circles will be meshed together, but there'll still be a portion of our circles that'll stand apart from each other.

    If we think that we're only one half until we find that significant other, then what happens if we remain single? Does that mean that we're forever not whole?

    Nope, I don't think so. One can remain happily single, depending on circumstances. People who are stuck in unhappy relationships might even think that being single would have been a much better option.

    And, how about those whose partner / spouse died, leaving them behind? Or what about folks who have gone through a divorce? Does that mean that since they have found their 'other half', there are no more 'halves' out there for them anymore and so they're destined to lead the rest of their lives alone?

    I don't think so either.

    Whilst it's truly enticing to believe in something so sweet and romantic, I'd much rather think that I'm a whole person with lots to offer and he, hopefully, would be whole too and when we choose to come together, it's a conscious choice to amalgamate our lives together, to stay together in spite of and despite of issues and problems that may arise or present challenges to us.

    However, I hope that I'll still be given the freedom to pursue my own interests and still be able to retain my own identity apart from the unified 'US'. I would jolly well love to see him retain his own interests and all that too. Otherwise, it would be too cloying and whilst it might be fun in the beginning to feel so much alike and be immersed in sweet togetherness, in the end, when the first flushes of love fades, you would feel an overwhelming urge to have your own space.

    I guess, I might be what you call an 'Idealistic Realist'?

    Originally posted by ilovemyrolex:
    Honestly, being in a relationship is over-rated.
    It IS over-rated in that a lot of folks talk about it as if being in a relationship will cure all problems, as if it's the be-all and end-all, which it definitely isn't.

    However, being in a relationship does offer its joys and bliss too. There are some things that can only be enjoyed by couples in an exclusive relationship, bound by love and trust and fidelity. It does feel awesome to know that whatever you do, whatever happens, there'll be someone standing by your side, giving you their full support. When you return from work frustrated or upset, they'll be there to lend a listening ear and a shoulder to lean on. When he is in a rough patch, you can hug him and pamper him and do nice things to him to cheer him up. You can spend time together in activities that one or both love or you can just spend time enjoying each other's company and not doing anything at all!

    There's happiness in being able to do whatever you want whenever you want and however you want when you're single. There's also happiness in doing things with someone, for someone and together with someone when you're in a relationship. I don't think one can replace the other, we'll just have to make the most of our current situation, whatever it is.
