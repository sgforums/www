    Driving a stickshift in winter also has certain advantages.

    1. When moving off from a complete stop on a snow or ice-coated road, you can reduce or eliminate wheelspin by starting from 2nd (or even 3rd) gear to limit the torque applied to the drive wheels. A vehicle equipped with an auto tranny without traction control will likely spin its drive wheels initially while the tranny kicks up through the gear ratios as the accelerator is pressed.

    2. If you're unfortunate enough to get stuck in snow, a manual tranny makes it easier to vigorously "rock" the vehicle back and forth by alternating between reverse and 1st gears, in an attempt get free. You can do this too with an auto tranny, but the delayed engagement time makes the rocking motion less effective.
