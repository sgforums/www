    I think basically, you think of her so much, you are seeking quick answers here. You forget though... being in our 30's in no way means that we suddenly become clairvoyant or gain some sort of psychic ability to foresee the future.

    I've known couples who knew each other since Junior College who got married and are parents now. I've also known this guy who was utterly devoted and lovelorn for this girl in my clique, who sadly doesn't reciprocate his love but he'll hang on, week after week, month after month, lapping whatever little attention she paid to him, misinterpreting whatever little thing she said, etc etc... You could see he was pretty obsessed with her cause everywhere we went with her, he tried to follow or just blatantly tagged along uninvited. Now? She's married... not to him though. He's also moved on.

    What we like, who we are, and who we love in our 20's might not remain constant into our 30's. People change, situations evolve, things metamorphosise... It's best to find a love that's mutual cause only then can you both achieve the same steadfastness, single-mindedness and devotion towards each other that would otherwise not be possible in this constantly-revolving and evolving world. If one party is willing, and the other party is lukewarm about the whole affair, you'll never achieve a balanced partnership.

    Think about it this way - do you doubt your self-worth so much that you don't think you deserve to be loved just the way you are? It's only when we've learnt to love ourselves can we then have the capacity to love someone else in a wholesome and unselfish manner. Otherwise, you'd find a lot of self-serving or self-crippling hidden intentions and desires in the relationship.

    So, never be unequally yoked... we all deserve better.

    Let go, move on, live in the present and look towards the future. Unshackle those old chains and live free...

    I remember a discussion with a close friend once and we both agreed that if a relationship is 'meant to be', it shouldn't have to take a lot a lot of effort all of the time to make it work. Granted, you have to put in effort to make any relationship work but it shouldn't have to be tedious all the time. If that's the case, then maybe, it just isn't meant to be and it's better off for all involved if you acknowledge that it just won't happen and live and let live.

    Easier said than done, but possible. Heartbreaking at times, but hey, we all survive!

    It's hard to walk away and initially, it will be hard. The head often makes up its mind way before the heart can catch up with the decision, ie. you'd experience heartbreak and heartache long after the mind has already been made up. But soon, the heart does heal and catch up and then you can move on for sure.

    I always seek to learn from each encounter because surely, there are lessons that we're meant to learn with each experience. If there are patterns that repeat themselves, learn how to recognise the warning signs and nip them in the bud.

    Learn to let go... I'd rather someone love me for who I am than stand pitifully in the wings, hoping to get crumbs that drop from the table of the one who's having a feast. In short, do not shortchange yourself in life - why insist on chasing after someone who obviously doesn't seem to have the hots for you the same way you do for her?

    Good luck... and hope you'll finally let go and move on.
