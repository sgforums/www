  • Rhonda
    7 Mar 06, 17:27
    Hmm... if given free choice in the matter, I will choose NOT to have kids if I'm remaining in Singapore.

    Key reasons being stress, cost and lifestyle.

    Stress... Not talking about my stress but rather, referring to the high stress levels kids face whilst growing up here. This is an extremely competitive society with parents wanting their kids to come up tops not just in their studies, but also in extracurricular activities.

    Kids are meant to grow up and develp at their pace and at a young age, I wouldn't like them to have to worry about writing essays, Ting1 Xie3, etc. Nosiree!! Not right! And after school these days, they're given tons of homework. Not to mention, quite a lot of kids have tuition, remedial classes and extra-curricular activities too, right after school.

    That doesn't seem to be a kid's life - it sounds more like a mini-adult's kinda life complete with the stress and pressure to excel!

    And, it's also stressful for parents, cause I remember my ex-colleagues grumbling about the homework being so difficult, when they return home, they have to tutor their kids. Or teachers being control freaks and they have to scout around for specific kinds of files in specific colours for their kids group work, or worse, they complain about going home and more or less 'doing' the kids' project work for them cause it's way too difficult for their kids to handle.

    I'm NOT going to do schoolwork after returning home from work, nosiree!! You can drag me kicking and screaming to the table... and I'd STILL be kicking and screaming!

    COST... it sure ain't cheap to raise a kid these days! Everything is rising in costs and everyone is experiencing a drop in real income.

    My generation was kinda dumb... Bata shoes were the norm and nobody ever thought about 'style' and 'fashion' because everyone was gwai gwai toot toot. Now, it's sneakers, and branded ones too, no less!

    Mom, I need a new schoolbag! *ka-ching!! Mom, I need to buy textbooks! *ka-ching!! Mom, my sneakers aren't nice anymore... I want what Bobby's wearing! *ka-ching!!!

    And oh, son, can you skip your next two meals cause mom bo lwee liao!



    Don't forget too, it isn't just ourselves and the kids we have to support - there's the matter of ageing parents, unemployed siblings, and what-not as well!

    Lifestyle... if you have a kid, your already stretched lifestyle will take a further dip.

    On another spectrum, kids expect more these days and they'll go around comparing with their mini Joneses and want to keep up with them.

    Entertainment is costly, well, everything is, tell me what's cheap these days?

    So anyways, if you're earning well, good. If not, your own lifestyle will also be affected.

    I just cannot imagine slaving away at work just to have a hand-to-mouth existence with the craziness starting every month with the never-ending heaps of bills to pay, then coming home and doing the housework and the kiddo's project work or tuitioning him, and then taking care of elderly parents and/or parents-in-laws needs, and then feeling like you're on the verge of a grand mal burn-out but you can't cause you cannot handle yet another hospital bill...

    Craziness! And it's a brand of craziness I'd opt to do without, thank you!
