    I've just read a book that was listed in the 2006 Books Alive Great Read Guide.
    http://www.booksalive.com.au/



    This has been written by one of my favourite authors & is her 10th book.

    Cathy Kelly never fails to intrigue me with her stories... I feel like reading on & on, never wanting to put the book down (dangerous on a weekend when I should be doing housework instead!!).

    She writes beautifully, and realistically - much of what the characters face, are what normal people would face too, stuff like breakups followed by low self-esteem, the joy of mother-daughter relationships & husband-wife relationships.

    There are lessons for each of the characters to learn, in trying to let go of the past & moving on to live a good life in the present/future.

    I can't wait for her next book! I suspect it will be out next year in March

    I'm not reading these books... yet, but just thought I'd recommend them for those who just might be interested!



    If you've spent time looking up at the sky and being fascinated by the shapes the clouds seem to take on, you'll love this quaint li'l book! In it, find facts about clouds, the different names of different clouds (we learnt them in Geography in Sec 2, anyone remembers? ) and interesting little anecdotes.

    Off-hand, I can only remember Cumulo-Nimbus, Stratus and Cirrus clouds. And since I'm a natural dreamer who loves to gaze at the sky, clouds preferred, thank you, I think I'll read this soon!



    This is another interesting book, again on the weather, but this time, focusing on freaky but interesting real-life incidents. Would make for an amusing read, methinks!
