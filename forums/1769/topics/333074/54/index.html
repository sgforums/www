    Due to Economic Crisis, Ministries to Restructure
    Posted on Wednesday, October 29, 2008
    Topic: Politics
    by Henthesun, edited by K.K.Cheow

    Due to the current global economic meltdown and to mark the 20th anniversary of the launching of the Group Representative Constituency (GRC), all the Ministries in the Gahmen will be restructured into Giant Regulating Ministries (GRMs).

    “Just as we modified our parliamentary representation system into GRCs to suit our… sorry, sorry, the country’s needs,” said Mr. Cheng Hu Kong, a spokesman for Deputy Speaker of Parliament Mr. Abdullah Maggimee. “And just like how private sector companies merge to save costs, so we’re going to combine single ministries into Giant Regulating Ministries (GRMs).

    Mr. Cheng said that the GRMs will bring new benefits to the country.

    “First, it provides racial harmony,” he explained. “Because each GRM will comprise a minimum of 3 to a maximum of 6. Meanwhile, some ministries will have to be split, and this means more ministers. Which means more diversity.”

    “Second, it promotes healthy competition,” he said. “MPs will compete and work harder to qualify as one of the new Ministers.”

    “Thirdly, it encourages meritocracy,” he smiled. “By merging ministries, we can ask C grade civil servants to leave, instead of D grade civil servants as is the case right now.”

    Following the GRM restructuring, the new Governmental structure will be as follows:

    1. The Prime Minister’s Orifice, the Ministry of Finance and the Ministry of Law will be merged into a Central Governance GRM. For diversity purposes, an Indian Minister will be required in order to argue in court and count money.

    2. The Ministries of Community Development, Youth and Sports, Home Affairs and Education will be merged into the Home GRM. For diversity purposes, a Malay Minister will be required to monitor police and football affairs.

    3. The Ministry of Inhibiting and Censoring the Arts (MICA) will be rebranded as the Entertainment GRM, and will include sub-Ministries to regulate crucial entertainment sectors like casinos, Speakers Corner, crazy mega-churches, stunts done at charity shows, and Jack Neo. When asked why no Ministries were being merged with MICA, Mr. Cheng replied, “Because we’re already folding the State’s Times and MediaCock into them.” A Chinese Minister is required here, because Chinese people have the most developed cultural history of 'wayang-ing'.

    4. The Ministry of Environment and Water Resources, the Ministry of Transport and the Ministry of Health will be merged into a single Fuel GRM. “We thought these Ministries were a good fit for each other because their areas of regulation cross-over to some extent,” Mr. Cheng explained. “To protect the environment, we have to regulate transport, and because of the way we regulate transport, we affect people’s health. Look at how much stress the ERP is causing.”

    5. The Ministries of National Development, Trade and Industry and Manpower will all be merged into a single Work GRM. “One of the crucial tasks of this GRM will be to examine future work sectors to be developed, especially during this economic crisis,” said Mr. Cheng. “We anticipate that the growth sectors during bad financial times will include the sale of backsides, the frying of cuttlefish and the hanging of pigs’ heads.”

    6. The Defence and Foreign Affairs Ministrys have been combined into the Armed Forces and Foreign Affairs GRM or AFFA. “AFFA will also include the Insecurity, sorry, sorry, Internal Security Department,” Mr. Cheng said. TalkingCock tried to speak to someone at the ISD for comment, but we were told that everyone had “gone toilet”.

    “The Gahmen truly believes that this restructuring will save significant costs,” Mr. Cheng concluded. “So that taxpayers’ money can be better deployed into crucial new areas. Like funding all the salaries for the new Ministers.”

    TalkingCock also polled responses from the public about the impending mergers.

    “Wah lan,” said Mr. Yew Nai Tet, an accountant. “Last time our constituencies got grouped, now our ministries. In 20 to 50 years’ time, will our countries or continents be grouped too? We might not have any more sovereignty, but then bleddy Malaysia might stop complaining about that stupid lighthouse.”

    “I think it’s cool,” said manga enthusiast Miss Annie Mae. “But it’d be cooler if all the Ministries could merge into a single giant robot.”

    “What’s the point?” sighed professional optimist Mr. See Nee Ker. “With that logic, might as well just merge every single Ministry under one person. Like now lai dat.”
