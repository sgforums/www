    10 Apr 05, 12:03
    The wedding ring is important for its symbolism of never-ending love and also to signal to the world that you've chosen to commit the rest of your life with a partner already.

    A wedding ring used to be a sort of 'hands off!' signal to singles who might otherwise try to hit on the ringed person but these days in our sad, perverse society, there are folks who view the ringed ones as 'a challenge'.

    I don't think we should place a minimum monetary value on the wedding ring as such. To be honest, I kinda feel repulsed when I see ladies showing off their wedding rings, talking about carats and how many K gold and all that because hey, what really matters is not the ring per se or how much it costs or how large and brilliant the diamond but rather, it's the fact that someone loves you and you love someone back and you're both going to build a life together.

    I guess if the couple is well-off and can afford a nice ring that sets them back by a few thousand, well and good. If the couple is struggling financially, a simple wedding band will suffice.

    If I had a few thousand, I'd rather spend it on a nice honeymoon. Memories are afterall, everlasting too!
