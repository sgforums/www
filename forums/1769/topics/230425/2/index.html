    strange how people look at animals.. here comes the science...

    rabbits are jittery.. for a reason.. they are herbivores and prey of almost anything that eats meat... for that reason they are always on the alert for their own lives.. you can use that to sense if there is danger as they are hardwired for that...

    foxes are intelligent and will take advantage of everything around their area and will not hesitate to move to a better area if the pickings are better... this is evidenced by their success in places like urban UK.. they are the most widespread animal in the UK and few people even see them...

    lions are hopeless because they are not really that fast.. they are very strong and built for nothing but fighting.. male lions are not very good hunters because of their bulk and muscle.. lionesses are dependent on their sisters to hunt bigger game... smaller game are snacks and they cannot survive long on snacks... a lion without a pride is usually dead quite quickly..

    wolves are like lions.. they are hard-pressed to survive alone without their pack.. or at least a mate in order to start a pack.. alone, they aren't very good but if you show your dominance, you can become the "alpha male" in his "pack" and you will be able to control the wolf..

    monkey.. dependent on what type.. a chimp may actually turn on you.. capuchin monkeys aren't much use.. spider monkeys are at home in the jungle as are gibbons and you will not see them again as they are canopy animals.. but as far as monkeys go, again, they are social animals, kind of helpless without their band...
