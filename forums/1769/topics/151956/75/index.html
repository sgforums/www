    Originally posted by stellazio:
    Yeah i was thinking somewhere along that line about physio the rapist..But seriously i heard physiotherapist are paid equally as high as nurses but much more slack..Rhonda can confirm?
    Physiotherapists are paid more than us.

    Which other 'profession' can be paid less than us?!

    And nope... they are not 'more slack'. They do a lot of hard work when they work with patients in the ward. Like us, they have to do a lot of lifting, positioning and supporting of patients, so they end up getting backache like us.

    They also run outpatient clinics where people who have been injured or who have suffered from some illness that caused them to lose mobility but have since gotten well, go to for specially designed exercises to help them restore and regain as much mobility as they could.

    To be a physiotherapist, you have to love people, and be very very patient. Some patients need a lot a lot of coaxing and sometimes, you'll end up standing with the patients for a long time, supporting them, as they take one small, tiny babystep.

    Physiotherapists also have to give 'Chest Physio' to patients. When patients have a lot of secretions in their lungs or airways, the physio steps in to pound the back of these patients, and perform suctioning to help remove these secretions to help them breathe better.

    If you're paranoid about cleanliness, then you might not be able to perform the Physio role well. Some of the patients look dirty, and they might stink, or they are incontinent, but the physios still have to work with them, touching them, having close contact with them.

    If you'd like to be a Physio, you need to have 'A' levels. Why not check out the Nanyang Poly School of Health Sciences website?
