    Originally posted by zozo:

The doc interviewed said dengue is more dangerous the 2nd time around and if the patient is old (immune system not as strong).
    If someone has caught dengue before, subsequent occurences of dengue in the same person may become more deadly because instead of dengue, the person may end up having Dengue Hemorrhagic Fever, which can be fatal.

    Just last year, I nursed a man who had 'flu-like symptoms' for the past week, then suddenly collapsed on the Sunday morn at about 9am, reached the hospital at about 9:30am, and was pronounced dead by 10:15am! It all happened so fast, his newly-widowed wife couldn't accept it.

    Basically, in Dengue Hemorrhagic Fever, the virus attacks our blood cells and our platelet count becomes critically low and essentially, we bleed internally to death. You'll start seeing little red spots on the limbs and torso of the patient. Death usually comes swiftly in such cases.
