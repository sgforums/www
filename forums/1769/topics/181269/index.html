    Blues Men

    Dirty, funky, electric, old-school, we'ver got it all! These are names that will send shivers down your spine - with pleasure! Paul Ponnudorai, Zul (Tania) and Trevor Jalla (ublues), will each take centrestage before ending with a massive three-way blues guitar jam. A must for all guitar enthusiasts.

    Date: March 12
    Time: 7:30pm, 8pm, 10pm, 10:45pm
    Venue: Nokia Music Station (Esplanade Outdoor Theater

    Lounge Around

    Aya Sekine, Maya Nova and coll.eff (collective effervescence) show what it is to chill out with their out-of-the-box jazz. Bulgarian jazz vocalist, Maya Nova, joins highly proficient and experimental pianist, Aya Sekine, is a riveting performance. Led by vocalist, Jo Dong, coll.eff offers an alternative experience to standard club fare as they explore nujazz, jazzy drum n' bass and bossa nova sounds.

    Date: March 13
    Time: 6:30pm, 9pm
    Venue: Concourse

    Soul Amazing

    Let two of Singapore's most charming vocalists let you in on rhythm and groove. Performing together as a duet for the first time are local babes, Vanessa Fernandez and Michaela Therese. Groove along with them as they do their own interpretations of old and new hits from Ella Fitzgerald to Oasis.

    Date: March 13
    Time: 7:30pm, 8:45pm, 10pm
    Venue: Nokia Music Station

    Jazz Fingers

    Consider this a showcase of Singapore's music titans. Jazz Fingers boasts the talent of Rick Smith (jazz guitar), Christy Smith (double bass), Paul Ponnudorai (guitar), Andrew Lim (guitar), Mei Sheum (keyboard) and Nial Djuliarso (piano)

    Date: March 14 & 15
    Time: 6:30pm, 9pm
    Venue: Concourse

    Jazzology

    Farid "Mr. Gambus" Ali is one of the finest jazz guitarists in the region. Back in Singapore specially for the Mosaic Music Festival, he kicks off the evening performing with his band, and closes the night in a highly pumped jam with his friends from Good Omens in a soul-shaking performance.

    Date: March 15
    Time: 7:30pm, 8:45pm, 10pm
    Venue: Nokia Music Station

    Easy Indie

    Catch this showcase of downscale indie music from non-mainstream indie artist such as Set for Glory and Vertical Rush. The former is a young band that is making quite a buzz in the Singapore music scene with their energetic stage performances and passion for music, while the letter is a five-member outfit whose music is described to be a mesh and tinge of bands like Goo Goo Dolls, U2 and Switchfoot.

    Date: March 16
    Time: 6:30pm, 9pm
    Venue: Concourse

    16 Mar 06
    Nokia Music Station
    7.30pm - 2am

    7.30 - 8.15pm Pulse
    8.45 - 9.30pm Awi Rafael from Rafe
    10 - 10.45pm Ronin
    11.15 - 2am Pop Tart

    Rock Steady

    17 Mar 06
    Nokia Music Station
    7 - 10pm

    Rivermaya - Philippines' number 1 band - will be rocking the house with heart-thumping rhythms and pure rock alchemy, that is energetic and enthralling.     7 - 7.30pm Rivermaya
    8 - 8.45pm & 9.15 - 10pm E.I.C
