    Hmm - it's getting a bit hard to keep track of brands these days, what with all the changes in the companies.

    JVC used to have a great reputation for quality control - in the old days, if a unit failed QC, it would be destryoed so that no employees could take it out of the factory and sell it. I suppose they can't afford to do that any more.

    Ten years back, Kenwood came out with some pretty damned good budget systems, but then they started going for those funny-looking Manga-type designs, and have not really done anything particularly impressive since.

    Aiwa, well, is still Aiwa - going for the market that wants a lot of features without much consideration for performance specifications.

    Sony does come out with some good units, but they're mixed into a catalogue with some Aiwa-style pieces as well, so you do need to choose carefully.

    Panasonic's okay - they ask a bit much for what they're offering, but they're okay.

    Pioneer can brag all they want about specialising in A/V, but they still don't offer anything more than Panosonic do. So there.

    Then I suppose there's the Korean companies like LG and Samsung. I'll admit to harbouring some QC doubts about the Korean manufacturers after a few initial bad experiences, so I won't say much about them.

    Like Sony, I think Yamaha use their name to sell a lot of less-than-expected-quality items. Onkyo, on the other hand, at least seem to maintain some standard. Then again, some could argue that the price point of these two brands puts them out of the budget market.
