  • honeymouse
    28 May 07, 18:14
    Apart from finding someone who can take good pictures, I find the following is also important:

    - Make sure the photographer is easy to work with (you'll get an idea what sort of person he/she is like when talking to him/her). You don't want someone to ruin your big day or spoil your mood when you get someone whom you can't work well with.

    - Find out what is included in the package (say your package is $3k, what exactly do you get out of it? how many photos, do you get the negative, etc. Nowadays most photographers use digital so probably have no negative. Sometimes it's better using the non-digital camera as photographers are more careful when taking shots. When using digital camera, they tend to just keep clicking away thinking they'll get at least one good one out of it.)

    - Find out the paper they are using for the prints. Good quality paper makes a big difference.

    Hope that helps!

    29 May 07, 11:01
    WPJA has a few SG photographers.
    http://www.wpja.com/wedding-photographers/international/asia/singapore/

    Most important thing before you go around shopping is to set a budget.

    Then visit a few photographers to look at their work. Bearing in mind that they will show you only their best works. Ask to view the recent works so that you can guage the quality.

    Ask to view the albums they are offering. It is usually cheaper if you opt for slip-in albums instead of customised "coffee-table" versions.

    Some prices quoted are only for services with no prints. So be sure to check.

    Some photographers limit the number of assignments they take a year. These usually are very good, so it's good to start looking for them now.
