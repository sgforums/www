    OK, when you slide food into hot oil to deep-fry, the best way to prevent being scalded is to pat the food dry first. The drier it is, the less moisture that'll interact with the hot oil, and cause those 'pops' that will hit you and scald you.

    I was a latch key kid, all alone at home, when I started experimenting with cooking in my mom's kitchen. I started by deep-frying everything and I'd hold the wok cover as a shield with my left hand and stand as far away as I could from the stove, then very quickly slide in the food with my right hand, then give a yelp and quickly raise the wok cover to protect myself from the hot, spattering oil, and step back a few paces. I screamed a lot when I cooked last time! I was only nine years old and home alone so, it was a pretty daunting task!

    I quickly learnt to cover the floor over a generous berth around the stove, and also to cover everything else near the stove, as long as it doesn't become a fire hazard, to prevent the oil spatters from making the whole kitchen greasy and a nightmare to clean.
