  • Rhonda

    I completed courses in BOTH a Junior College AND a Polytechnic, and here's what I have to say :

    - Junior Colleges focus more on the academics. I learnt a lot about how to present an essay better, how to formulate your arguments, etc, 'soft skills' that have proven to be very invaluable in critical thinking. This will be very important when one proceeds to higher education in future.

    - Polytechnics prepare one to be competent in a particular field. There's theory AND practical training involved. Thus, when a poly graduate enters the workforce, their contribution to the company can be almost immediate, because they have been rigorously trained to take on the challenges of their role.

    One focuses on pure academics; another focuses on competencies. Both have their use in our economy.

    Academics should never be belittled - afterall, it's the basis where Research and certain fields like Medicine is built upon. You cannot do without academics in any field.

    Nowadays, both poly grads and 'A' level grads can enter the Universities to obtain the same degree. So then, isn't it time to stop arguing which is more superior but instead, choose the path that one is most suited for?

    I've benefited from BOTH and I don't regret the experiences I've gained from both. The academics widened my perception whilst the competencies taught me valuable life-long skills. I wouldn't trade one for the other!
