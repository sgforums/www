    Is there justice in marriage?
    By Karen T

    I'VE just been through a crisis in my 13-year-old marriage.

    A month ago, my husband, the only man in my life for the past 15 years, the father of my four lovely children, dropped a bomb on me.

    He told me that he had a fling with his secretary and he would be leaving his well-paid job so as to end the episode.

    Have I been a submissive wife? I would say, "Yes".

    Perhaps, he might say, "No". My occasional tantrums might have put him off, but he has chosen to ignore the fact that I've been stressed by the hectic routine at home.

    I had wished for a husband who would be patient, caring and most importantly, faithful to me.

    Over the years I've gone through four pregnancies and given up my career. I've sacrificed sleep and leisure, meals and rest, to cope with the demanding schedules of my three schoolgoing children and an energetic toddler.

    We have never even had a decent holiday Â— every vacation was spent visiting his parents and relatives in Malaysia.

    My husband is a man of few words; he doesn't seem keen to engage in conversation with me.

    No matter how tough my day, he has never given me a pat on my back or whispered "sweet nothings" to comfort me.

    I'm not a superwoman. There were times when I broke down and cried for attention. Instead of silencing me with a reassuring hug, he rained punches on me and even strangled me!

    He does not want to hear anything that might upset him Â— if I had to cry, I was expected to cry alone because he would never understand why I should feel hurt.

    I've accepted him the way he is Â— even when my heart has been bleeding for over a decade.

    Where's the respect? Is there equality in marriage? If only one spouse is working on it, we can't take for granted that the "receiving" spouse will reciprocate the effort.

    In my case, my husband was so absorbed with his secretary, that he didn't see my devotion and sacrifices for the family.

    He looked distracted when he was with us, came home later after work, showed little enthusiasm for birthday celebrations. I was the one organising every activity to keep the family together.

    A man might think that he has done a great duty for the family by bringing home the bread and butter.

    When he comes home after work, he expects the wife to be more than just the children's mother. He wants the wife to also provide him with gentle loving care.

    My husband has obviously missed the fact that I'm already dead tired after a long day with the children, having driven eight to 10 trips in the car, feeding and changing the baby up to six times a day.

    Don't I also wish he could come back to offer me some warmth and passion?

    We could discuss ways to rekindle our romance, but it's usually tough. Either both of us are too tired and mentally stressed, or, there's always interference from one of our children.

    Even in bed, we're "separated" by at least one child sleeping between us.

    I had relied on our marriage vow that, even though the relationship might dull, we'd remain faithful to each other. And on the hope that tomorrow might get better.

    I consoled myself by trusting that the man I married would be happy that I was totally independent and efficient at running the household alone, so that he could devote his entire time, energy and concentration to his work.

    I never expected that he would abuse my trust and spend his time, energy and money on another woman instead.

    Worst of all, he thinks he has the right to decide how he wants to spend his time.

    He knows what he's doing is morally wrong, but he simply can't resist the temptation.

    What shall I do? I've had to battle bitterness in silence, while learning to forgive him. If I kick up a fuss, I might upset him so much that he would be consumed with anger.

    I have to be magnanimous, yes, very magnanimous Â— otherwise, the marriage will be over, and that doesn't mean it would be better for us and it definitely would not be for the kids.

    There is no justice, but should we talk about justice in a marriage?

    Can I leave him? I can't. For the sake of my four young children. We depend on him for a living.

    I have to focus on moving forward. To give my children a healthy family life, I have to forgive my unfaithful husband, whether he's worthy of the forgiveness or not; and I have to continue to work on improving our relationship, whether he's worth the effort or not.

    I've also been advised not to hate the other woman Â… so I had to remind myself to thank her. Why? She has given me a wake-up call, otherwise, I would still be living in my own dreams for the future.

    Now that I know what my husband needs, I have to work on my role as a wife. Hopefully, from now on, we can live happily ever after.

    Well, I can only say I admire her quiet strength and courage after all she has gone through and still try to find the silver lining in the dark clouds.

    Just three or four days ago, I received a PM from a 33-year old man, telling me that he's married with a child but misses the active social life he used to lead. He asked if I'd be interested in a purely physical relationship with him as he's a businessman who works mostly at night and so would be free in the daytime to meet me.

    Spooked me! I mean, he didn't even bother to hide the fact that he was married with a child! He just blatantly declared it as if to absolve himself of all responsibilities should an affair come to fruition when one of the ladies he PM'd were to take up his offer! That was his 'exit' out of responsibility because when his mistress starts raising hell for him, he'd declare matter-of-factly, 'Hey! You knew right from the start that I was married with a child! I never hid that fact from you!' I can just picture that scene in my mind like one of those melodramatic TV dramas on Channel 8!

    It used to be that if one had an extramarital affair, the person and his/her lover would be shamed and there would be a stigma attached to them because the people around them would gossip and frown upon them.

    These days, sadly, it's becoming so common that most of us no longer bat an eyelid when we realise that someone we know is getting a divorce because his / her spouse had cheated on them.

    There seems to be lesser guilt involved these days. An erosion of values? Judging from the ravines and gorges that you see in quite a few modern-day marriages, I'd say yes. There's definitely more of the 'I, me, myself' self-serving mentality and less of the 'I'll not do it because I love my wife and child and do not wish to hurt them' stance. Responsibility and commitment, sorry, let me have my fun with this exciting person first.

    Let's not talk about marriage... dating itself has a different code of ethics these days and call me conservative or old-fashioned but it scares me speechless at times. It used to be that if a guy liked you, you'd know because it would be darn obvious. These days, a guy may genuinely be attracted to you, or he might just treat you like a sister / buddy / companion in boring times / 'friend' only and you wouldn't really be able to tell. The lines have been blurred to a large extent, values that used to be upheld have been relaxed and even eradicated. You can now go out with a member of the opposite sex, be totally and unrestrainedly touchy-feely, flirt with no holds barred, 'act' like you're a couple and successfully deceive all who meet you both, but then, you're both just having your fun and enjoying each other's company and c'est tout (that's all).

    Sure, that's 'fun', we should all 'relax' and 'take things easy' and not be so 'uptight', 'don't be a prude', yada-yada... but I'm not so sure that this is a good direction that we're heading towards. We've become so permissive that we've made a lot of excuses for not upholding a lot of good ol' values that we take a lot of things lightly these days. And that's when things will start to slide. Some things should still be accorded the importance and gravity that they deserve.

    Suffice to say that I look back at the men I've dated and the two that I almost married, and I can't help but shudder. Sure, there were those good men that have 'gotten away' but they were the exceptions rather than the rule.

    Ask me again why I'm single! Like I patiently told grandma AGAIN just two weeks ago, 'Ah ma! In a changed world where men and women can lie, cheat, steal, commit adultery, etc without batting an eyelid, why would you want me to put my trust in just any man so hurriedly?' Ah ma could only sigh and mumble that 'Indeed, we live in a different world now. But may the gods 'por pi' and let my good granddaughter find a good man!' (And then, grandma HAD to shatter this almost golden moment by adding, 'Afterall, my nana may be plump lah, but still quite bwee-swee one! But she's really a nice person!' (OK, my ah ma doesn't know the b!tch that I am at times but ssssshhhhh!!! :lol I jestfully told grandma, 'Ah ma! Di gong way buay hiao di si teng! Diam diam gong wa bwee!' (translation : 'Ah ma! When you talk, you don't know when you should stop! Always saying that I'm fat!' )

    Ah well, her gods had better do an immaculate job of sieving the wheat from the chaff because I have zero tolerance when it comes to infidelity.
