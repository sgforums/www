    IT BOILS DOWN TO BOUNDARIES

    .
    People get married for various reasons and, for a first-world country where people are not fighting for the most basic needs, one would hope that communication, love, respect, sharing and joy fall into the realms of marriage.
    .
    Why do people start a family? One would hope that it would be for more than just procreation. It should be a continuation of a contented couple wanting offspring to enjoy the warmth and love within a family and radiated from a loving couple.
    .
    I see in Singapore, however, an interesting phenomenon. Not only is family life built around the children's schedules, but even grandparents are cast aside in the "busy-ness" of catering to their tuition and multiple extra-curricular activities.
    .
    Is it the failure of the education system Â— which most children can only keep up with by having extra tuition Â— or are parents going overboard to ensure that their kids are ahead of the game?
    .
    I have seen the mad driving around of children from one place to another. It is exhausting for the parents and boundaries must be set. Life is full of compromises: Would it not be better to accept that it may mean the kids achieve "B" grades instead of "A" grades?
    .
    The writer has accepted that she has no life and she is totally exhausted by all her child-rearing duties. Aren't children supposed to be a joy to add to a good marriage? When did they become a source of exhaustion?
    .
    In this mad rush around for the children, not only are the parents cast aside, but the grandparents are also relegated to secondary importance.
    .
    What are we teaching our children? Is it that when you grow old, you will be the next generation to be cast aside Â— for the sake of the children's children?
    .
    Our priorities appear to be misplaced.
    .
    What about making it a priority to keep the family intact? Has the writer ever made herself or her marriage a priority and set boundaries for the children?
    .
    Why is there always at least one child sleeping between them? This family has obviously lost some boundaries as individuals, as a couple and as a family.
    .
    It is wonderful to be self-sacrificing, but one can only love others if one learns to love oneself.
    .
    If you don't love yourself, how do you know how to love your husband and to love your children?
    .
    I agree with the writer that she does not have a marriage. What she has is a man on whom she depends for a living Â— for both herself and the children. He obviously sees it the same way.
