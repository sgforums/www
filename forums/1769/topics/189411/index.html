    Songs used to be so simple then.

    Almost every song relates to a personal experience, and regardless of the genre - was straightforward, with identifiable lyrics. There's just something about oldies that makes them live throughout the ages, and the recent revival of some songs which were hits way back then and become hits again re-released by modern artists, proves just that.

    Remember "Tell Laura (I love her)?" that's a song that always strikes a chord in me. It reminds me to always tell someone whom I care about how I feel as and when - for the next moment may be too late. And "Can't Take My Eyes (Off You)"... catchy tune, catchy lyrics, yet oh-so-simple.

    That's probably the beauty of oldies.

    It sees music as a form of entertainment - rather than a means to prove a point, or as a mode of 'release'. It's honest, and speak to the hearts of many. It's simple, and reminds people of their own life's experiences.

    The Swing and Big Band Era churned out classics by Count Basie, Glenn Miller and Benny Carter. Revived jazz hits by Harry Connick Jr. and Bryan Evans, to name just a couple - became hits again in this day and age. Their appeal, perhaps, will never wane.

    What are some of your favourite oldies? Or perhaps songs that are of significance to you? I'm sure all of us have a favourite or two.

    Music is, after all, a universal language.
