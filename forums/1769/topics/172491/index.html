    Have you seen the TV ad where a couple sit in front of the girl's parents and the guy says something like,

    ''Uncle, I have been going out with Lynn (sorry, can't remember the girl's name ) for a long time already, and we... ''

    Then the Uncle put out his hand to mean ''STOP!'', and then he whips out a cloth-covered manual - a Marriage Instruction Manual!

    Apparently, a few govt organisations are organising Marriage preparation courses for couples who are intending to get married.

    This isn't a new idea - for donkey years now, churches have been offering such courses to those in their congregation who are contemplating marriage. In the US, back in the late '90's, such courses were introduced in the South and a debate was going on on whether they should be made 'compulsory' as a preventive measure in the face of the rising divorce rates.

    Would you go through such a course when the time comes for you to consider it? Do you think such a course would help somewhat in stemming the rising divorce rates?

    If you're already married, what do you think of this? Most interestingly, share with us what you wish you'd known before marriage.

    For those who are interested, here's the short intro to the Marriage Preparation Courses on ROM's website :

    ''You are about to enter into a marriage and you desire that this impending union is life-long and fulfilling. Knowing that a good and sustaining marriage does not just happen, you will need to put in effort and get prepared.

    Participate in a Marriage Preparation Course to prepare yourself and your partner for the journey ahead. The duration of a course is usually between 8 and 16 hours. Both of you can expect to learn about the following interesting and enriching topics:

    marriage roles and responsibilities

    finance management

    sexuality/intimacy

    conflict management

    communication with in-laws and

    individual differences/family-of-origin issues

    You may also take an optional personality test such as Taylor-Johnson Temperament Analysis (T-JTA), Myer-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI), PREPARE Inventory.

    Visit Family & Community @ eCitizen for a detailed listing of Marriage Preparation Courses coordinated and supported by the Ministry of Community Development, Youth and Sports.

    Please contact the organisers directly if you have any enquiry, or want to obtain details about the rebates for the course(s) attended.

    The above was extracted from : http://app.rom.gov.sg/internet/reg_info/rom_info.asp

    There would be guys who are uncomfortable with such courses because they think it's unnecessary, or they might be very confident that they can manage whatever issues that might crop up in the marriage.     Generally they make you discuss real issues like money, kids, in-laws, sex etc so that you know what you're getting into. The courses usually offer some general guidelines on how to manage but the bulk of the discussion is left to the couple; everyone has different ways of handling things.

    And during the sessions, you might actually discover some things about your partner that you didn't know about, could be positive or negative - had a couple who couldn't agree with each other and walked out.

    The course's mantra is: A wedding is a day, a marriage last a lifetime.
