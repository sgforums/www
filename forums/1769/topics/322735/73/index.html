    Sometimes, I wonder if Melbourne can continue being a good place to live in and whether in the near future, there'll actually be an exodus of people out of here. 

    The thing is, housing is becoming more expensive, and you don't necessarily get quality abodes for the price you are paying. 

    On top of that, my huge concern is the govt's slow-to-respond attitude to a lot of issues that need prompt and timely attention.  For instance, infrastructure like public transport, a more efficient and cheaper broadband, more efficient services, etc. 

    A friend from Singapore moved here with her husband, and their home line took TWO MONTHS to install!!!    And they are living in South Yarra, a good, well-established suburb close to town!  

    And the water situation... the govt has been talking about possible solution for YEARS and NOTHING has been done so far.  We are STILL in Stage 3 Water Restriction here.  Now, with climate changes, and with Australia being the hottest and driest continent in the world, should water resources become scarce, the entire agricultural industry will topple, and the economy will definitely be shaken and Australia will find itself in the grip of massive inflation. 

    Although I do want to own my own home, there are issues of concern to me and basically, the govt here don't really get my vote of confidence.  Melbourne was once a quiet city before it thrived, and I'm just wondering if the tides will turn. 

    In the meantime, I'm living a cushy life here, better than what I can afford in Singapore.  However, I'm wondering how much longer this high income and good welfare can last because when there's an economic downturn, high wages and benefits etc will not aid the economy at all. 

    I guess I had better always have a Plan B!
