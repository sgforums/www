    OK, I digressed with my earlier post but now, I'm going to take the bull by the horns!

    I feel that love draws two people to journey through life hand-in-hand, not knowing what landscapes they will encounter, what storms they'll have to weather, and where the nearest oasis providing a respite from it all will be.

    Despite the uncertainties, out of mutual love, no one opts to leave the other, even when the going gets real tough. This is where the 'in sickness and in health, through good times and bad' part comes in.

    I guess the onus is to choose the right partner before we commit ourselves to love him/her. Some people have the depth and strength of character to walk through burning brimestone with you and you'll both come out forged in fire, stronger in your relationship. Others will whine and shuffle their feet and need to be dragged, and they'll come out at the other end disillusioned, bitter, and unwilling to continue the walk with you any further.

    It pays to be prudent in choosing who we love. I used to think that we don't have a choice, that when cupid strikes, that's it, even if your match is a bad guy, you just have to grit your teeth and bear it out cause that's your lot in life. (And that's how I ended up with a pai kia in my silly younger years! )

    However, I no longer think that way. I feel that whilst it may sound callous, we need to evaluate who we fall in love with, know the person's strengths and weaknesses well before we entrust our lives with him/her and embark on a journey together. Afterall, you can't put a lamb through a buffalo's paces and if you're taking the buffalo route, you'd jolly well not allow a lamb to tag along because both of you will be hindered and unhappy!

    We need to evaluate with our eyes wide open because once we make that commitment, we don't turn back. Ideally.

    Sometimes, love appears to be totally spontaneous and out-of-control, at other times, love appears only after careful thought and the slow and steady getting-to-know-you stage. I guess there's no one-size fits all kind of love.

    To the sentimental folks, love would be all that the poets espouse it to be - a flurry of heartbeats and emotions, being swept off your feet, totally ecstatic and never humdrum. To the more 'sedate', objective-driven folks, they would view love as a steady partnership with no nasty surprises and where the typical milestones in life are experienced together. Most of us are in-between folks and we may have our very unique idea of what love really is.

    Is love always enough? I feel with the right partner, yes, it can be enough.

Always give your newly-chosen partner a fair chance instead of confining him/her in a small room with walls built from your past experiences, baggages and prejudice.
