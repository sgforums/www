    there was also a merger of 2 organisations to become the one i am in now.. there is a clear difference in cultures... one just goes about doing the work, while the other does minimal work while trumpeting whatever the hell they do...

    one side does the work and then goes home, the other bums around during office hours and then clocks OT doing work after office hours, trying to show off to the (usually) workaholic boss..

    when the merger happened, people got merged into departments, the cultural differences were clear as a bell... some bosses started to ask what the hell was going on with so many people clocking so many OT hours while some people were going home on time, yet the productivity was equal..

    after much teething problems, seems like there's an uneasy truce now.. the 2 sides still look at each other with some suspicion... but the newer recruits are pointing the way... unsaddled by the baggage of the past, they learn to work hard, work right and then get a life outside the office

    i can't say if the culture of my organisation is good or bad.. coz it's still evolving after a sudden and painful merger... but i've seen good and bad of both sides.. unfortunately, it seems to be mixing up into one huge Mediocre Culture
