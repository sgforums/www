    As far as I can tell..... you shouldn't apply CPR if you're not properly trained in it.

    Improperly performed, CPR can lead to further complications. For one thing, performing CPR while the heart is actually pumping on its own can mess up its rhythm further. Once the heart restarts, you have to stop chest compressions immediately.An untrained person may not know that.

    Improper compressions can also lead to broken ribs and puncturing of the liver by the xyphoid process.

    It is possible for two (trained) people to perform CPR simultaneously, and is in fact recommended since CPR can be a really, really siong thing to do alone.
