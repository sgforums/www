    PSD yet to decide on year-end salary revision for civil servants
    By Imelda Saad, Channel NewsAsia | Posted: 23 October 2008 1831 hrs

     
     
    Photos 1 of 1

       
     

     

    SINGAPORE: The Public Service Division (PSD) says it will need some time to study the private sector data and salary trends before coming to a decision on whether the Civil Service will see a year-end salary revision for 2008.

    It said, "Civil service salaries are reviewed and revised regularly to keep in line with the private sector."

    However, bonuses are expected to be scaled down.

    The PSD says a significant part of a senior civil servant's annual pay is structured as a GDP Bonus, which is pegged to the GDP growth rate.

    If Singapore's GDP falls, the bonus will also fall. For example, ministers can get a three-month bonus if Singapore's GDP grows by 5 per cent. If the GDP growth is 2 per cent or less, they will not get a GDP Bonus.

    Minister-in-charge of Civil Service Teo Chee Hean had said in 2007 in Parliament that ministers will enjoy higher GDP bonuses only if the economy is doing well.

    "Twenty per cent of the ministers' salaries will be predicated on the GDP bonus. Another quarter is performance-dependent," Mr Teo said.

    Singapore's economy is expected to grow by three per cent in 2008, lower than the initial forecast of between four and five per cent.

    The third phase of salary adjustment for civil servants is scheduled for the end of 2008, following similar adjustments in January 2008 and April 2007. This is to bring public sector pay closer to private sector benchmarks.

    - CNA/yt
