    Today I rec'd a call from a friend who wants to recommend me to his company (I didn't even write in to apply for any job). He needed me to go see his GM today. But tomorrow I have a major event going on and today I was really busy. His company was really keen and he is trying to fix an interview for me on Monday. I have a feeling that this company might be able to pay me higher... $500 to $1000 more.

    But if I join the new company, I will lose all the exposure and stop learning new things. I will be going back to doing sales (I hate sales).

    On the other hand, I love money.

    What do you think I should do?

    Go for the stuff you have passion for. You only get paid at the end of the month when that's going to be your happiest moment. On the other days of the month, if you don't like your job, you'll be thinking that you're dragging yourself to work everyday. Not only that, while in your job, you'll think of why you accepted the job and is already thinking of the good old days, or might even job hop again soon.

    You can really help yourself by delegating your jobs to people. They are inexperienced. Everyone is inexperienced at first, but are they qualified to do it? If you need people to speak/write Chinese, then it's quite hard because that takes time to train them. But you can assign simpler stuff for them to do. Give them self confidence. Motivate them and train them. In time, they will be able to take on greater responsibilities. If they are permanent staff, then at least you have a peace of mind that they don't take the knowledge they got from you and run away, like interns do.

    Somehow if you choose the money route, I suspect you will be posting more complaints here in sgforums.

    If you love the job you're doing and it gives you a sense of fulfilment, achievement and recognition, then even if you have to work long hours in your job everyday, it'll be a breeze.

    But if you detest your job and it makes you feel insignificant and discontented, then working at your job for just an hour will feel like you've slaved away at three hours!

    Basically, I think it's dreadful to have to be in a job that you really cannot tolerate. Granted some folks have higher tolerance levels but in the long run, forcing yourself this way causes unhappiness and stresses the body too, y'know. Overall, not healthy to the body, spirit and soul.

    I think there's no harm in checking out your friend's company for a look-see and just to keep in touch with what's happening in the job market for your industry. Satisfy your curiosity, but then consider carefully if you're actually ready to leave your current job behind. You sound pretty busy, but yet quite fulfilled, in your current job and if so, you might not be prepared to make the move yet.

    In any case, working at such a wide job scope in your field will be a definite asset when you finally feel ready to make a career progression move some time in future. Think of all the valuable, priceless experience you're accummulating!

    I guess it boils down to you in the end. I'm just wondering, if you have to consider very hard in the first place, then maybe, you're not convinced the new job is better and / or you're not ready to quit this current job yet.
