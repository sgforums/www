    Check this out...the world's longest bony fish, the stuff that most likely gave us stories of sea serpents: The Oarfish.

    This is a rather small specimen....because

    The oarfish can supposedly grow up to 17m in length and are believed to live in deep waters. The only ones that come up to the surface are either sick or dying. But appearences of oarfish in shallow waters have also been linked to plate tectonic movements: their recent mass appearences have preceded major earthquakes in Chile and Japan and Haiti.

    Few have seen them alive. Its an enigma like the giant squid...but here's a footage of an oarfish estimated at 15-30 feet swimming in its natural habitat at about half a kilometre down.
