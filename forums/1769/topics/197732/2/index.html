    Originally posted by Kuali Baba:
    But surely going beyond the common boundaries of logic, perhaps for self-gain, is too much?
    It's quite simple, really.

    People who file litigation lawsuits for trivial matters beyond logic are simply out for a fast buck. Lawyers, knowing just how much they can exploit the trigger-happy system to win suits for their clients, are also lured by the money.

    In the end, it's all about the $$$$.

    But in the end, who truly suffers from all these litigation lawsuits?

    When we have McDonalds fearing people who scald themselves, they pass on the extra costs of manufacturing new cups to the consumers.

    When we have burglars sueing homeowners for putting toys on the floor, we have rising premiums for home insurance.

    When we have parents sueing schools for playtime injuries, the kids don't get to have fun anymore.
