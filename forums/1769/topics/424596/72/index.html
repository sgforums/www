    from the HDB website

    http://www.hdb.gov.sg/fi10/fi10329p.ns/w/eSERSBenefits?OpenDocument#Compensation

    http://www101.hdb.gov.sg/hdbvsf/eampu200.nsf/0/default.html

    note that the 50k is not the total compensation for the old flat, but what they'll have left over after the new flat payment. that is to say, they'll get 50k plus a new flat, which would not count towards their new flat eligibility, meaning if they sell this new unit at prevailing market rate say 5 years down the line, they'd still be eligible for new flats and the accompanying subsidies, just as if they've never lined up for a flat in the first place.

    that's why during my real estate classes back then, we were drilled, SERS announced, resale value immediately goes up.

    now whether it's right or wrong, good or bad, that one is subjective and it's really not the point here, but I think it's important for us as thinking citizens to find out all of the facts first before we start assigning blame.
