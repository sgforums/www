    So, I haven't exactly settled down enough to go explore beyond my familiar 'territories' like Victoria Gardens, Target, Myers, Borders, Readers' Feast, K-Mart, Coles, and the Victoria Street shops. I'm looking at the day tours available in Melbourne though and will go book myself on one or two of these soon!

    BUT, I will do so soon. In the meantime, I love the pho at Victoria Street, the Japanese food at Gigi Restaurant along Collins, the Singaporean food at Singapore Chom Chom (where I go to whenever I feel homesick and need a lard-laden boost ), the Guo Tie at the Shanghai Resturant in Victoria St, and the quasi-Jap/Korean food at Tokyo Sushi along Bridge Rd.

    I'm gathering a few similarly restless and curious colleagues to go watch movies on Tuesdays when it's much cheaper, and will be asking around to see who else is interested in the day tours I'm eyeing.

    BTW, I still like The Straits Times. I find that there's more of an international coverage there. The newspapers here are kinda more ... myopic in scope. They don't do world news coverage very well, sadly.

    It's frustrating... a lot of places I wanna go, I can't get to because I don't drive YET and don't own a blardy car and that's why I end up making countless trips to and from the shops lugging stuff back home.

    And oh, my colleagues just had a good laugh when I described to them how much difficulties I ran into just to construct my own furniture (cause it's cheaper than buying ready-made ones over here) and someone older (but I'm not sure is wiser) declared, "Hey, women aren't meant to construct furniture! I just buy ready-made!" Oh well, my workmanship ain't perfect... but at least, the pieces I've set up are still standing tall and sturdy!
