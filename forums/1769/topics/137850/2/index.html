    I know too well firsthand, the true benefits of exercise.

    Right after college, I slowly became a couch potato as I dived head-on into establishing my career. All that overtime, all that social eating, and all that additional weight!

    What first encouraged me to reverse the cycle was, let's see, more than 10 years out of college, in my last year in nursing school in Nanyang Poly. Damn SUAY!! My batch was the first batch that had to do the IPPT!!! I didn't know the range of my cursing vocabulary until then!

    I tried to weasel my way out of it. First, I asked the coach if there was an 'age limit' to IPPT. But DRATS!!! He said that there were different ranges for different age groups! Doubly SUAY man!!!

    Next, I conjured up imaginary ills and made excuses to the coach, hoping I could be excused from IPPT totally. Drats... have to produce valid certification from a GP to be excused.

    There goes all my hopes.

    I really had to drrrraaaaaaaaaaaaaaag my feet to the IPPT. Worse, half the nursing school population appeared to be there at the same time!! That means, if you do the Standing Broad Jump and landed SPLAT! like some malformed duck, EVERYONE would be talking about it!!! If you attempted Sit And Reach and aiyoh... cannot even touch your toes, people will giggle and laugh at you! If you did the Shuttle Run and skid and fall on your fat arse, oh glory, you will be the poster child of clumsiness and the new mascot for the unfit the next day! DREADFUL!!!

    BUT...

    Though I didn't train for it, I did pretty ok. Failed overall but there were much younger peeps who did much worse than me. Hey, I failed my 2.5km run by just a wee 3 mins! Not too bad for someone who had been a couch po-tah-to for donkey years! At least, I didn't give up 1/4 way through, like a few young 'uns did!

    That made me think... I'm not THAT unfit afterall!! My general fitness level is pretty good. I ought to take advantage of that and start working on getting fit again.

    So, there you go! There were times when I failed and wandered far far far away from my goal, but slow and steady wins the race!

    Now, I'm injured and all exercise plans have to be shelved until full recovery, and I'm really upset that I have to miss all my exercise classes.

    Exercise changed me from a sluggish, drowsy person to a livelier, more energetic, confident and a tad less moody than I used to be. (Yes, excess fat does predispose one to depression and moodiness.) I cannot imagine NOT exercising now... well, not for too long a period anyways! I love that 'I'm on Top of the World' feeling after every workout, that flush in the cheeks, that 'We are the Champions' kind of ooomph!

    The benefits of exercise? You bet!!

    Originally posted by dragg:
    but recently quite a few people died after or during exercise.

    That's when you do not work within your limits.

    Now, this is a double-edged sword... how would you know when you have truly reached your limits? People will always tell you to 'challenge yourself', to 'push yourself', 'you can do it!' but how would we know when is really enough?

    I've seen quite a few folks who ended up in hospital because they over-exerted themselves and ended up bursting an aneurysm and stroking out. And then there are those who over-estimate or over-push themselves and end up with various injuries that ends up taking lots of time to heal completely.

    Basically, if you have pre-existing medical conditions like hypertension, hypotension, irregular heartbeat, heart problems, etc. you might need to seek your doc's advice before embarking on an exercise regimen. If you do have a medical history, be aware of it and do not try to be a 'hero' and show off and push yourself because you might just push yourself into the Accident & Emergency Dept of the nearest hospital. Not worth it.

    Everything in moderation!

    For me, i started exercising about a year ago.. then i was about 10 kg heavier.. After about 3 months, I started to feel different, both mentally and physically. after 6mths, ppl ard me also started to notice a big difference.

Soon, I started running further and faster. Eventually, I grew more confident about my fitness, and completed the Navy Biathlon in Mar. Now I'm training for the Half marathon in Sept.. and my ultimate prize for the year: my 1st marathon in Dec.

    I believe the most important abt exercising is that you have to think of it as a change in lifestyle. start small, like doing 10 min brisk walking, but do it regularly. If you start with a large step, u may be demoralised if you dun succeed. Slowly increase it as you feel more confident. (In fact, this is also how i approach a lot of situations in life, when i have to do things that are out of my comfort zone.)
