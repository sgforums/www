    i always figure if you are in a country, go do what the locals do...

    especially if you are there for studies.. it can be an education in itself not to be confused with getting the 'education' of paper...

    when i was in melbourne for 3 years, there were people there who have never ventured out of the little suburb they were in, never ate anything other than the really lousy 'asian food' takeout place...

    when their lease on their house ended, they moved to the hostel where they met a few others.. for the first time in their 2 years there, they went out with a local-born (my roommates) and ate something other than McCrap's, Hungry Jacks' (burger king) or that stupid chinese takeout...

    we introduced them to Souvlaki, pasta, pizza, gelato, the works... australian rules football, rock concerts, having brews at the pub, sightseeing....

    i bet they were kicking themselves for missing out of a lot of things for 2 years

    so, try not to be too caught up with looking for 'rice, asian food' and whatever.. you'll do well doing what the locals do, eating what they eat, widening your horizons
