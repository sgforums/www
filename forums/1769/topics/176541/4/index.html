    I think to oversimplify porn as simply portrayal of sex act between two or more parties can be very dangerous considering the capacity of human beings to be creative within that limit.

    The standard argument for porn is that it often sets 'standard' expectations about what men and women should do during sex. And in standard generic porn, a woman is almost always portrayed in a certain way, to enjoy certain things. And if a woman DOESN'T enjoy it, while it seems she's a bit weird, or frigid or is 'ultra conservative'. Or even, if a woman doesn't behave in a certain way during sex, she's not enjoying it enough. Same thing with men, are they supposed to give pleasure only if they are THIS well endowed?

    Most of us would claim intellectually we know the difference between porn and reality - it's just fantasy! But the problem doesn't lie with you and me, who occasionally surf porn sites or watch a couple of adult videos to spice up the sex life. Sure, we get it even when it is not legalised~ but what does legalisation mean?

    But legalising porn means as a society you accept it as part of your life and culture. That every adult above a certain age have the right to have access to porn, whether or not they are socially, sexually or intellectually mature enough or not. What about adult industries? Would you accept that too? Since it is already semi-constitutionalised anyway - why are we still keeping it as a legal taboo?

    I am not against legalising of porn but I think there's a danger where people are calling for it stating WHY we should legalise it without thinking through the implications and difficulties of HOW to legalise it.
