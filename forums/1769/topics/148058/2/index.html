    A few of the posts in here are really amusing!

    My burning question, upon reading this thread, was : How is Carbon Monoxide formed?

    I know that car exhaust fumes contain carbon monoxide. But how exactly is it formed?

    A search on the EPA (Environmental Protection Agency, USA) explains that the sources of Carbon Monoxide are :

    Unvented kerosene and gas space heaters; leaking chimneys and furnaces; back-drafting from furnaces, gas water heaters, wood stoves, and fireplaces; gas stoves; generators and other gasoline powered equipment; automobile exhaust from attached garages; and tobacco smoke.


    Notice the common denomination amongst all these would be gas-powered appliances, or poor ventilation when burning certain substances like tobacco, or poor ventilation when having a burning fire in the chimney.

    Nope... no mention of our very humble aromatherapy candle or burner.

    Next, they listed ways of preventing Carbon Monoxide Poisoning :

    Keep gas appliances properly adjusted.
    Consider purchasing a vented space heater when replacing an unvented one.
    Use proper fuel in kerosene space heaters.
    Install and use an exhaust fan vented to outdoors over gas stoves.
    Open flues when fireplaces are in use.
    Choose properly sized wood stoves that are certified to meet EPA emission standards. Make certain that doors on all wood stoves fit tightly.
    Have a trained professional inspect, clean, and tune-up central heating system (furnaces, flues, and chimneys) annually. Repair any leaks promptly.
    Do not idle the car inside garage.


    And nope... still no mention at all, of our aromatherapy candle nor burner.

    So, whilst I still could not find out how carbon monoxide is formed, this site provides plenty of information, sufficient to allow us to conclude that it takes more than a burning candle or aromatherapy burner, to cause Carbon Monoxide Poisoning.

    So, let's close this X-file!
