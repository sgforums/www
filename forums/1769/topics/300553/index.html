    Over here, I turned down a party invitation because the last day of 2007 was incidentally the hottest day of the year in Melbourne as well! At a sizzling, furnace-hot 41.1 deg C, I wisely chose to stay home with my fans working overtime! I now am totally sympathetic to muffins and all manner of baked goods as they sit in the convection oven! There were strong winds too but believe you me, having a HOT wind blast in your face just feels like someone turned the hairdryer to high and is pointing it at you!

    So, I chose to stay home because from my bedroom, if I squint through the neighbour's trees, I can actually glimpse a bit of the fireworks as they go off in Melbourne City! I can hear them too so it's a really fantastic feeling! I can't see much of it because I'm being blocked, and too bad I don't drive otherwise, if I had driven over to my hospital and sneaked into one of the dayrooms in a particular block, the view would have been spectacular!

    And, to usher in the new year, I said a prayer that my endeavours will go smoothly. (And ok ok, I confess, for better prosperity too! ) Also quickly went through past events of the year, wanting to learn from them and not commit the same error again, and thanking God for the blessings.

    That's it! No drunken wild parties and all that! An interesting snippet - on the TV news today, the Victorian Police reported that there were "only 550 arrests last night across Victoria" and then proceeded to say that the crowd was "well-behaved"!

    Apparently, the ambulance service fielded hundreds of calls yesterday too because of the heat wave. I even had to "rescue" my succulents by shielding them from the direct sun with newspapers because last year, in Summer, I noticed that some bits of the plant became "chao ta" (burnt), making me feel like a bad mother!

    So tell us! Tell us!! What did you do? I already know TX was with his sweetie at the Waterfront watching the fireworks. What about the rest of you?
