    1. Do you think that you're living a healthy lifestyle? Why or why not? Have you taken any recent medical tests / check-up's?
    Yes, when I'm in Melbourne - it's a relatively healthy lifestyle. I walk a lot on a daily basis, I have to move about a lot and exert myself physically on the job, I eat healthily and my portions are controlled.

    Yes, my last full medical check-up was done just a year ago. Everything's in order and under control. Clean bill of health!

    2. What lifestyle habits do you think you will have to change in order to become healthier?
    Think I'll have to incorporate regular exercises more. I hate cardio exercises but think that for the health benefits, I just have to somehow force myself to do it.

    Also, I have to continue watching my daily dietary intake. If not careful, my tendency is to over-eat.

    Other than that, I don't smoke and have never bothered to even try, and I very rarely drink and when I do drink, I prefer CHOYA. No other bad lifestyle habits except having a tendency to over-eat and being too lazy to exercise much.

    3. How often do you exercise? What's your favourite activity? What new sports / activity would you love to try?
    Daily walks.

    Favourite exercise would be the BodyBalance, BodyPump, and Spinning classes in the gym. I love 'em cause the modules incorporate really great music to exercise to and I NEED that musical distraction!

    I'd love to try Rowing. I'd also love to learn how to ride a bicycle. And oh, instead of BodyBalance, enroll in a proper Yoga and/or Pilates class.

    4. Imagine that you've just been given an entire week off! What would be your preferred way of spending that time-off?
    Either spend that time lazing around at home, and making plans to go out for shopping / movies, or travel to some leisurely resort to veg out in scenic surroundings.

    5. What is your opinion on diets?
    Diets, esp fad diets, are stupid. They often do more harm than good and the effects are usually only temporary. Some diets might even endanger you in the long run.

    6. Would you rather be slim and forever hungry and craving for food, or would you rather satisfy your urges and live with being plump / fat? Elaborate.
    Depends, I go through phases. Right now, I'm torturing myself, trying to make sure I don't eat as much as I used to because one thing I've learnt is that we don't really need to eat that much to survive and that Singaporeans are forever over-eating.

    7. What sports / activities do you consider to be dangerous and which you'll never ever embark on?
    Hmm... bungee jumping can injure you really badly and give you an effect akin to whiplash and you could end up with serious spinal injuries. Not worth that risk.

    Surfing's dangerous... can get hit by the board and get concussed or a serious brain hemorrhage if you fall off; can get bitten by hungry sealions or sharks, as is the case in Australia; etc.

    Attempting daredevil acts is dangerous. Jumping off a cliff to dive is also dangerous. Hmm... can't think of anything else atm.

    8. Have you ever spent time worrying about your health? Do you fear falling ill or being diagnosed with chronic ailments? What worries you?
    Yes. I'm quite a worrier, as are most nurses and docs. We tend to over-diagnose ourselves each time we experience some symptoms!

    I worry about falling ill with no family nor close friends to rally around me. I worry about being ill and forgotten and poor...

    I also worry about getting chronic debilitating illnesses like cancer, kidney failure, leukemia...
