    That's so true. There are pros and cons to both sides.

    When you're living alone, there'll be times when you feel desolate and deserted and unloved and you'll feel like crying your heart out and yearn for companionship.

    Then, when you get married or have someone else sharing your home, you'll start to grumble about how they do this and that wrong, how their living habits irk you, how you wish you had some alone time, how their music is too loud and yucky, etc etc.

    Wanda made a good point when she said that we have to accept our situation and be content. A lot of times in life, we find ourselves in situations that are less than ideal and we start to fret, get all flustered and start feeling miserable. I guess what's important is to learn to be self-sufficient. Learn to love your own company. Even when you eventually get married, or even if you find a live-in companion, there will be moments when you're not alone anymore, but you feel lonely. Even soul mates need time away from each other and there will be times when the soul feels lonely and desolate.

    I do get hit bad at times by a desolate feeling, but I always tell myself that life swings like a pendulum, and that you just have to accept the good times with the bad. Granted sometimes, it feels as if the pendulum has swung over to the 'bad' side and has become frozen in time there, suspended there, prolonging the bad times but, it always swings right back to the good side.

    Just like that song in 'The Crow', 'It won't rain all the time'!

    Hang in there!
