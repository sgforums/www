    23 Mar 07, 17:08
    Originally posted by MooKu:
    what's systolic and diastolic? as in, what do they measure?
    Systolic: maximum blood pressure when the heart is in a compressed stage.

    Diastolic: minimum blood pressure when the heart is relaxed.

    A reading of 140/90 means that the systolic pressure is 140mmHg, while the diastolic pressure is 90mmHg.
