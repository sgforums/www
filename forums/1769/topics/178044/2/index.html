    why do fat people feel cold, rhonda asked for explanation so let me explain once again using the same heat transfer equation.

    From my previous reply, we ignored conduction and radiation, which forms part of the Eout portion. We did not take into account heat loss by convection. With reference to my previous reply, the following can be understooded

    Heat rate Qconv = hA(Ts-Ta)

    Ts = Body surface temp
    Ta = Ambiance temp
    h = coeff, which is the same since all humans
    A = surface area

    Since h, Ts, Ta remains the same for both fatso and skinny, we look at A. fatso's A is higher, hence heat loss by convection will be higher. Since Qconv is part of Eout, Eout will go up, causing Estorage to be lower. Hence, this explains why fatsos feel colder.

    the sensation of hot and cold felt by a person depends on many factors. among them, the 3 most significant ones are: 1) sensitivity of our nerves 2) metabolism 3) efficiency of digestion system

    1) hot and cold sensation does not necessary equate to more and less heat in our body respectively. our body is not an accurate thermometer and each of us are calibrated differently becos we have different number of nerves cells with each sensitivity. i do not know of any reports that state that fat people have different sensitivity or quantity of nerve cells than thin people.

    2) & 3) a thin person does not necessarily have high metabolism, and likewise, a fat person does not necessarily have low metabolism.
    case (a) a thin person can have low metabolism by having a poor digestive system. so this type of person will not feel hot all the time.
    case (b) a thin person that has high metabolism, he/she will feel hot and react faster to cold temperatures.
    case (c) a fat person can have high metabolism by having an efficient digestive system, provided the digestive system is more efficient than the high metabolic rate. he/she will also react well to cold temperature.
    case (d) a fat person who has low metabolism will not react well to cold temperature.

    these 4 cases illustrate that the physical size of an individual is not an accurate way to predict his/her reaction to cold temperature.

    also, a muscular person will also feel hot becos to maintain the muscles, it heats to burn lots of fuel (that is glucose), which generates heat. if you want to be more resistant to cold climates, builld up your muscles. this works no matter u are fat, thin, low/high metabolic rate, efficient or inefficient digestive system.
