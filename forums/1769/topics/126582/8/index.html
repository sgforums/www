    Originally posted by LazerLordz:
    How's life in Minnesota?
    My apologies for this late reply.

    Life in Minnesota is, by and large, enjoyable. The locals, who are mostly of Norwegian and Scandanavian descent, are friendly and accommodating, especially out in the suburbs and surrounding countryside. The people here might be a bit "colder" closer to the cities, but I think that's true of any built-up urban area. Take Singapore, for example!

    For a midwestern city, the cost of living is surprisingly high. Residential property prices have gone through the roof in recent years, and property taxes are among the highest in the nation. State income taxes are also very high.

    Weather-wise, Minnesota is about as varied as they come. Winters here are legendary for their variations in snowfall and temperature. Some winters can be surprisingly mild with little snowfall, while others make the news worldwide. I was here during the famous "Halloween Snowstorm" of October 30/31, 1991, when 40" of snow fell in a 48-hour period. I was living in a ground floor apartment at the time, and I couldn't open the sliding patio door because of a 6-foot high snowdrift on the other side.

    Some winters can also be brutally cold. We've had still-air temperatures as low as -25 degrees *Farenheit*, with wind chills that have plummeted to -40. Human skin doesn't react very well to those kinds of temperatures and will freeze if exposed for as little as a minute.

    Summers here are absolutely gorgeous, though, and make enduring the occasional harsh winter worthwhile. Minnesota is a very verdant state, with lots of parks and lakes. Minnesota's state motto is "The Land of 10,000 Lakes," so there's no shortage of opportunities for boating and fishing.

    MG
