    Just back from these places...

    Phuket is really.... like a giant tourist trap. Besides the white beach that is easily available in lots of places in Thailand, don't understand why anyone would want to go there at all.



    Every other shop caters to tourists (mostly european) - the seafood restaurants, bars, the souvenirs, the tailors. Many of the shops are opened by European tourists who have decided to stay on. The whole atmosphere was very un-thai - the architecture, the unsmiling vendors, the persistent calls of the touts. The beach is impersonal - deck chairs for sale, loads of services available, probably at least 200 m away from ur hotel room.



    Anyway, won't go back. I was curious to see the change the tsunami might have but... oh well.

    Krabi on the other hand is less of a touristy place only because it is less developed - and therefore less shops. But the shops were all the touristy kind anyway. We manage to find some non-pizza, non-seafood, non-screamingtraditionalthai restaurants, and found little eateries further away from the beach stretch. Nice yummy food with fresh ingredients for 1/4 of the price.



    Went kayaking amongst the limestone karsts. Damn nice. Anyway you gotta do it if you ever go Krabi still water, big fat jelly fishes...

Orchard Road is used very frequently by locals for shopping and ther activities as well. But not many of us would go Chinatown to do our daily shopping.
