    Something I picked up in some of the posts here... Ambition - Why is it wrong to lack ambition at work?

    I mean, I want to go to work, fulfill my responsibilties and do the best I can during my shift, then when I finish my day, I have other things to look forward to in my life. I do not want extra responsibilities, nor do I covet after any title nor status.

    I'm happy like that but back in SG, it almost seems 'wrong' to not be ambitious. I'm glad that here, there's way more flexibility plus there are tons of people in my workplace who are opting for less than 5-day weeks to accommodate their various interests in life.

    To me, there's more to life than work, work, work. If I have to work, work, work, then I also wanna play, play, play!
    Nothing wrong with not being ambitious. When I was interviewed for my current job, the GM asked me where do I want to be in the company in 5 years time.My reply was I do not know. I told him I am not ambitious corporately, I have my ambitions but just not in the corporate world. But I went on to say that it does not mean that having no ambitions about my work means I will be slacken. In fact, my own values will not allow me to slacken but instead excel in my job. If I get a promotion or raise, I am happy. If not, I am happy with what I have anyway. I was hired.

    I think the problem with people nowadays is that when they say they are not ambitious, they totally slacken off, they bo chup, they give mediocre work. Being not ambitious does not mean you cannot excel in your work. To me, some people use lack of ambitions to be mediocre.
