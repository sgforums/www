    Bus service 502 is my favourite example of shocking incompetence when it comes to planning. This express route connects the Jurong suburbs with the downtown areas of Orchard Road and the Marina district.

    The bus company doubles the capacity during "peak hours" by splitting the route into 502A and 502B, while keeping frequency about the same. The problem is that this "peak hours" splitting is roughly between 8-10 am and 5-7 pm

    What I have noticed is that between 5 and 7 pm, 502A and 502B mostly run at littel more than half capacity. Some days, there are even empty seats, let alone have standing passengers Â– and this on the homeward-bound leg.

    But between 7 pm and 11 pm, 502 is packed solid. Sometimes, the bus is so full, it doesn't stop to pick up passengers anymore, so those left stranded at the bus stops have to wait another 25 minutes for the next 502 (yes, that's the frequency of this service during "off-peak").

    No route planner seems to have realised that the surge is not at the theoretical "peak" of 5 to 7 pm, but much later.

    I really wonder whether route planners work short hours, going home at 5 pm sharp everyday, completely unaware that other Singaporeans work late."

    "Solving the taxi problem must involve relooking at the capabilities of bus and train. The latter must cater to a wider variety of passengers, not just the slim, agile and baggage-less. If the existing duopolistic companies aren't keen to do this, then we must have new competitors."
