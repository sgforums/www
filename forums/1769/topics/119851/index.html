Praises, Compliments, Appreciation...

  • Rhonda
    27 Feb 05, 19:31
    I often feel that most of us aren't expressive enough when it comes to dishing out praises, compliments and appreciation. Also, when someone else actually comes along and tells us something nice about ourselves, we often try to brush it off and become very 'uncomfortable'.

    Maybe I get to see sudden death or a sudden inability to speak or sudden unconsciousness so much that I feel life's too short to not live it up to the fullest so I often like to let my friends know that I like them by telling them how special they are. Basically, I don't want those I love to not know how fond I am of them. Often though, people start getting very uncomfortable.

    Example:

    "You know, you're one of the sweetest people I know. I'm so lucky to have you as a close friend!"

    "... ... ... oh... erm... aiyah... hehe! No mah... I never do anything special one!" *nervous giggles

    With caucasian friends, it's the reverse. They're often very expressive and whilst sometimes, you wonder if they're just being sincere or just being 'nice' and socially correct, it sure does feel good hearing positive things! (Especially if you are constantly in an environment where complaints and harsh words and rebukes are the order of the day. )

    Maybe we ought to go out and tell a friend today how fond you are of them, how much you appreciate them and how special they are to you. It'll definitely make their day!

    (Hopefully, you don't scare them off... some folks just don't take compliments and appreciation very well! hahaha! )
