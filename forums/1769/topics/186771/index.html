    People tend to believe that I'm this prudish strait laced nerdette, at least when it comes to the things I write. Just because I don't swear or use profanities liberally doesn't mean that I don't have such thoughts whilst I'm phrasing my sentences! It is not everyone's talent(?) to come up with short and sharp insults that are so subtle that most people don't get the sarcasm until they think back, or perhaps re-read / recollect the statement over. Then again, some never do.

    Of course, there's the standard, "drop dead," variations of "idiot," "jerk," and the like, but when somebody is really angry it doesn't always make for an effective comeback.

    It's more challenging if we insult / be sarcastic without the use of profanities... and I would think that the effect would be far greater. It makes people actually have to think, as opposed to familiar swear or curse words that would not actually deliver anything apart from the fact that the other party would know that we're pissed off.

    Put it this way... assuming if I were to say :

    "Oh... I don't think you're dumb, but what's my opinion as compared to thousands of others?"

    ...and saunter away nonchalantly, is definitely more cutting than if I just said :

    "You're such a c**k and a half!"

    Not only would he be momentarily flabbergasted whilst letting the double edged meaning sink in, he'd be racking his nearly non-existent brain for a wittier comeback!

    Even in politics throughout the ages, a "well-meaning" insult have let some politicians have their say and get away with it. For example, Adlai Stevenson said: "If they were to stop telling lies about the Democrats, we will stop telling the truth about them" - now... that's a classic!

    Just yesterday while I was having some drinks with my buddies, this guy came up to me (it would help if he was even remotely attractive!) and said :

    "Hi, I think I've seen you here before. Do you come here frequently?"

    If he was a tad more 'sincere' about it, I would've given him the benefit of the doubt, but asking such a clichÃ©d pick-up(?) line whilst ogling and leering takes away any well meaning intentions that he may have had; So I replied:

    "Not if you do..." and he goes on to say :"Really, ah?"

    Oh... by the way, I'm not usually this smart, I'm just making a special effort today.
