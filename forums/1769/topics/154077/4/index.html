    2 Feb 06, 09:39


    I would recommend this book to all who are serious about mastering Chinese cooking!

    It has details on how to choose your wok, how to prime your wok, and how to maintain your wok in optimal condition.

    'Breath of a Wok' is the literal translation from 'guo1 qi4' (Mandarin) or 'wok hei' (Cantonese). It is a concept that Chinese chefs uphold... sort of a form of respect for this important cooking utensil.

    There are many recipes inside, all of which are from famous chefs or very experienced cooks. It's more like a compilation of recipes from a whole lot of people who have a passion for Chinese cooking.

    Available in Singapore for more than $60. Seen in major bookstores.

    You will absolutely droooooooool over the photos inside this book! Full-colour photos of every single recipe!

    This is suitable for cooking for one or two. You can pack it neatly and bring it to work for lunch. Basically, all recipes in this book consist of a bowl of rice with one dish cooked on top of the rice. Very compact and convenient!

    There are the usual Japanese one-dish recipes like Chicken and Egg on Rice and Breaded Pork Cutlet on Rice. Then, there are other interesting one-dish meals like the Sukiyaki Rice Bowl, Korean Barbeque Rice Bowl, Tofu and Pork Rice Bowl, Oysters Simmered in Miso on Rice, etc etc.

    Another cookbook I'm bringing with me to Australia!
