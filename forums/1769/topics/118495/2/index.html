    Book day plans - taken from the Belfast Telegraph

    02 March 2005
    World Book Day, designated by UNESCO as a worldwide celebration of books and reading, was marked in over 30 countries around the globe last year.

    The origins of the day we now celebrate in the UK and Ireland come from Catalonia, where roses and books were given as gifts to loved ones on St George's Day - a tradition started 80 years ago.

    This year's celebration of all things book-related is taking place tomorrow.

    Schools and libraries will all host events, and to get in on the act the Verbal Arts Centre is teaming up with BBC Radio Foyle for a live broadcast from the centre.

    The Mark Patterson show will be transmitted from 3-5pm tomorrow afternoon, and will feature activities focusing on books, reading, and projects undertaken from the Verbal Arts Centre to promote the spoken and written word.

    Highlights include members of the children's book group talking about the visit of Children's Laureate, Michael Morpurgo to the centre on Wednesday, March 16, a competition to win a Â£50 book token, a poetry challenge, an opportunity to swap-a-book in the centre's cafe, and guests talking about their favourite books.
