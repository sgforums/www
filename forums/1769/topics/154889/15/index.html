    well ..... I'm seriously thinking of grad school now MG ... com sci of course .... can't stand econs sometimes ..... but i'm not sure if it's a good idea to immediately get a masters without getting some industry experience first ? .... what do you think ? ...
    Funny you should ask that.

    Yesterday afternoon, I was sitting in on an interview with a gent who was applying for a Functional Analyst position in our organization. He was an MBA grad from the U of MN with excellent grades. But he had no work experience, as he had gone on to do his post-grad work right after finishing his undergrad degree.

    Fact is, with all the layoffs that took place earlier this decade when the economy tanked, there is currently no shortage of recently-laid-off qualified candidates to fill positions in corporate America which are just now starting to become available. From an HR persepective, it would make more sense to hire a candidate with work experience than one who is a fresh grad, especially if the former is willing to start at an entry-level salary. This is the reality of hiring practices in this country (and I do not see this to be any different in Canada).

    If you intend to pursue your Master's, I would recommend you get at least a few years of working experience under your belt first. This way, you'd be more prepared for classes at the post-grad level, and you'd have work experience on your resumÃ© after finishing your post grad studies.

    MG
