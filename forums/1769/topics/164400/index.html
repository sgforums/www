    I REFER to the letter, 'Revive medical benefits for regular blood donors' (ST, Nov 15).

    I am a champion blood donor. I always consider blood donation as the greatest gift one can bestow on unknown others. I never donated blood expecting anything in return.

    For the record, I also donated my body for medical purposes as early as 1976, when I was young, at my creative best and in good physical health. This was reported on the front page of the venerable paper Streats on Jan 24, 2003.

    I always carry my body-donation card on my person wherever I go, even now when I am in Canberra on a project.

    As a Buddhist, I remember Lord Buddha exhorting:

    'This body of flesh and blood I bear

    'All for the world's good and welfare...'

    Our reason for donating blood should always be to make a gift of liquid love, expecting no returns whatsoever.

    Ananda Perera
