    (the Bear morphs into the Grammar-Nazi mode)

    To "send someone home" is to command or instruct a person to go home. That is, A sends B home by instructing B to go home. A does not move from the original location while B goes home.

    I would believe that the correct term to use is "fetch someone home", "accompany someone home", "escort someone home" or "bring someone to their home".

    The meaning of the former and the latter are very different.
