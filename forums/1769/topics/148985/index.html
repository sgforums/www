    I just had a recent transfer to a new work area at the start of this month.

    At first, I was apprehensive, because for the past three years, since I graduated, I've been in my old ward. I didn't like it there much but I had settled into a comfortable routine, and things were predictable. Well, except for the Sisters' moods, which were highly volatile! I guess I was in my 'comfort zone'.

    My sudden transfer stunned me. Inside, I could feel a sort of resistance against this change. However, at the same time, I thought that maybe, this change would do me good because I had stagnated at my previous workplace already. It was probably high time to go somewhere new, learn new things, broaden my experience and yes, make the good ol' resume look even better!

    So, I went with an open mind. And guess what? I now have more job satisfaction!! Here are the factors that makes my job satisfying :

    - Great Teamwork! Here, everyone, including the seniors and the management, roll up their sleeves to pitch in. It really boosts your morale when you know that if you're busy and stuck with a 'bad' case, your colleagues will willingly come over to help you without being asked even. Here, they adopt a macro view - they run the entire ICU and they don't just think that they only care for the patients they are in charge of. That really really helps! I was really impressed! Now, even when we're busy, I'm happy and I work with a spring in my step.

    - Enlightened Management! The sisters not only help us a lot, they stand up for us and fight for us when need be. They talk to us like we're equals, unlike my previous workplace, where the 'Wong Ah Mah' (Empress Dowager) or the military style was used. The sisters don't look at us with suspicion and they also don't talk to us with sarcasm and keep making accusations and hurtful comments at us. This makes the working environment really pleasant! I no longer dread interaction with the Sisters like I used to.

    I still work the same hours, and my pay remains the same, but my job satisfaction is much higher and I'm happier! Things aren't perfect, but they're better and it makes a hell lotta difference to staff morale! Now, I'm motivated!
