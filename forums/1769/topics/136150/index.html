    Some facts about the flying squirrel :

    - It doesn't really fly, it glides, sort of like an eagle except it never has to flap any wings. There are blanket-like membranes of skin between its wrists and hindlegs that give it the ability to glide long distances.

    - They have long, flattened tails that are used to guide their glides.

    - They have large eyes, or "bug eyes", I like to call them.

    - Flying squirrels are nocturnal rodents, which means they feed at night on fruits, nuts, buds, and insects.

    - They nest in hollow trees, deserted buildings, and birdhouses.

    - These gregarious mammals seldom descend to the ground.

    - This is a sociable little animal with strong maternal instincts.

    - They have been known to make affectionate and playful pets.

    - Its principal enemies are owls and cats.
