    MahaBodhi Temple - This was build next to the Bodhi Tree (this is the sapling(son) from the Bodhi Tree in Sri Lanka (father) whose original tree (grandfather) was located more than 2,500 years ago

    It is believed that 250 years after the Enlightenment of the Buddha, Emperor Asoka visited Bodh Gaya. He is considered to be the founder of the original Mahabodhi temple. It consisted of an elongated spire crowned by a miniature stupa and a chhatravali on a platform. A double flight of steps led up to the platform and the upper sanctum. The mouldings on the spire contained Buddha images in niches.

    Some historians believe that the temple was constructed or renovated in the 1st century during the Kushan period. With the decline of Buddhism in India, the temple was abandoned and forgotten, buried under layers of soil and sand.

    This temple was excavated by Sir Alexander Cunningham in 1883 based on the records by the traveller monk Hsuen Tsang from China.
