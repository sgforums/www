A holy pala in a heroic often has to really pull out the stops and break whatever key yr FoL is assigned to, esp when shit hits the fan.

    I don't like instances and raiding but for the sake of gear I did a lot of heroics on my holy pala, which means a lot of stress trying to keep everyone alive. Its enough to drive anyone mad. Finally pala is a very gear dependent class. Compared to other healing classes naked, holy palas are probably worse with base stats(last we did an indepth comparism at lvl 70, it was). The reason FoL is so mana efficient is down more to how the gear scales with it, rather than anything to do with palas being "imba", because yr static mana cost stays the same, and then yr amt bonus healing gained from gear is on par with other classes. To illustrate, lets pretend paladin's base flash of light heals 100hp for 50 mana cost. Lets also pretend shaman's base lesser healing wave heals 200hp for 80 mana cost. Shaman's heal is actually more mana efficient as well as a bigger heal, at the same casting speed. However because casting speed is the same, they both gain the same % from bonus heal. So lets assume both pala and shammy have gear that gives them 200 spellpower, and that they get 70% of this bonus, which is 140. So now the pala's heal is 240 for 50 mana, the shaman's is 340 for 80 mana. The pala's heal is now 4.8hp/mana while the shaman's is 4.25hp/mana. People always think pala's are op, actually they're not. Its how the game mechanics are designed for gear to scale. Until you can get the gear, healing will actually be a bit of a struggle.
