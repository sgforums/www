    ok this view might be disagreeable to some people but.. I feel that when we visit third world countries, we should not hand out goodies to the kids. especiall sweets who give children in villagers decaying teeth because they dont have much access to dentistry or oral care.

    why?
    it really teaches them that 'tourists' are people who gives things and teaches them to 'beg' from tourists whenever they come. I am sure we have all been to villages, where children follow you for a long long time, asking for food, drinks, pencils, pen, sweet, money. If you dont have toys, they ask for sweets, if you dont have sweets, they ask for money. Somewhere down the road, someone will give something, if you ask long enough or hard enough.

    what happens next? the cute kids of today grow up to be the tourist touts of tommorrow. do you still give to them? when they are no longer cute? they are still 'poor' you know.

    want to give stationery? give it to the school directly. Usually the teachers will know which children need it more.

    giving out little gifts directly to children make you feel good momentarily, and makes them happy very temporarily. but if you really want to do a good deed for them, then consider less easy options that can really improve the villagers lives' better in the long run
