    I've been to several derms in the past. Their treatments vary, but the one thing in common is that they all strongly encourage use of sunblock. It's almost compulsary, in fact.

    Formulations for sunblocks are unique and different. There are good and bad ones, and each react differently to different individual's skin (thus element of trial and error) . Variety is wide enough to suit almost all skin conditions - oily, dry, level of protection, waterproof , budget concerns, etcetc. I think greasiness & resulting breakouts is one of the bigger concerns.

    There is enough info online to short list the credible, popular sunblocks, and from that list select one that is suitable.

    IMO there will be one that is most suited for one's skin. The problem is finding the perfect product.

    What concerns me more is the risk of NOT using sunblock. Skin cancer, pigmentation, deteriorating texture, all tricky & expensive problems to deal with when skin matures.

    Am I the only one who thinks of migrating to Thailand? Think about it....

    - S$12 massage budget per week, and thats 2 solid hours per week. Yah ok, its not exactly a spa, but I usually close my eyes when being massaged anyway, so it makes no difference to me how the surroundings are.

    - Great people who are not too uptight, are polite and warm (well, most of them anyway). Great "Mai Pen Lai" laid-back, forgiving attitude. Great informal culture.

    - Great food at affordable prices. Tom Yum Soup S$3. Green Curry Chicken S$2~2.50. Banana Crepe S$0.50~0.80. Kai Tawt fried chicken thigh S$0.80. Kai Yang roast chicken thigh S$0.80. Packet of sticky rice S$0.20. Katsudon at an aircon restaurant S$3~4. Great buffets too! Sizzler salad buffet S$4-6 (over here its S$16).

    - Great beaches with great topless girls from the around the world (for those who prefer guys, great topless hunks from around the world).

    - Great cost of living (many average Thais live on S$100 per month, including food for the family, utility bills, etc). You'll never be a slave to your job again!

    - Great clothes at reasonable prices. T-shirt S$3. Jeans less than S$20. Sandals S$10. Underwear S$1.50. Socks S$1.

    - Great nightlife. Oooooh, pole dancing, balloon shooting, etc. While they had all that, we were still debating childishly here whether to legalise bar-top dancing or not. Great sandwiches too - for eating or otherwise.

    - Great, great sports (this was the main draw for me when I initially visited Thailand - its about 90 trips later now).

    - Good mix between the old-fashioned (countryside) and modern (downtown huge, shiny malls).

    - Can modify your car any way you want. Can even lengthen it, raise it, etc!

    - Traffic fines are affordable + no demerit point system. Tolls and parking are very reasonable. Vehicle insurance and road tax are so, so, so affordable.

    - Freedom of speech and expression! But laws are not that strict, so people tend to behave and not abuse their freedom too much (to avoid getting whacked).

    - Big domestic market. Good for technical salespeople like myself - can bluff your way through easier than here.

    - Airport tax much lower than Changi (greatly appreciated by frequent travellers like me).

    - Don't need to leave the country for holidays. Can drive all over Tahiland (cultures, lifetyles, food, languages/dialects, etc are different in different parts of the country). Petrol is about 60% of the petrol prices here. Great countryside too! Like the floating riverside restaurants at Kachanaburi.

    - A bottle of Thai whiskey (30-40%, 35 cl) can be less than S$4.

    - Cheap to fly back to Singapore on budget airlines, if/when you need to. Cheap to fly to KL once in a while for shopping too. Or you can go by land/boat to Vientienne for a break. Soon, there will be a highway from Bangkok to Vietnam....and I heard they're planning one to China too.

    - Great medical facilities/services (for retirement). Doctors are polite, don't mind answering questions, take their time to explain things to you whilst not making you feel like you're being a bother for asking.

    - No NS.

    - No CPF.

    - No ERP.

    - No COE.

    - Can download MP3 from the internet for free.

    - Even their great fruits are on "auto mode" and don't require picking in the real sense (ie. durians).

    Um.....to summarise it all, great for a cheapo food-loving burnt-out corporate cheekopek like me. Sigh....still waiting for my company to post me to Thailand. Was to be last year, but didn't happen....yet.
