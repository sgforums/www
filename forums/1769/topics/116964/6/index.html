    Eric, you just can't trivialise the difficulties couples DO face as they slowly merge and start life as a family unit.

    Even if you've known a person for donkey years and think you know him/her inside-out, there's still a lot of surprises in store and learning and compromising to do when you actually move in together. There are habits you never knew about each other, the different needs for privacy, different tolerance levels for various issues and circumstances, etc.

    Ask any married couple... the first few months of living together can be the hardest. It can be little things like ... he leaves his cups wherever he finishes his drink without bothering to clean up; she leaves her hair in the shower clogging up the drain without bothering to remove it; and the all-time favourite - squeezing the tube of toothpaste from the end or helter-skelter any which way you want, etc etc.

    Love can be the lubricant that smooths the path but it does not eradicate all obstacles and challenges. Love can bring two people together and bring on the commitment that 'No matter what happens, we're in this together and I will still love you' and that can actually help a couple surpass the trials they'll face. But, love does not mean you'll have a problem-free time.
