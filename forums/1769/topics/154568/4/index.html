    we were on a flight from Singapore to Australia
    and he asked the flight stewardess for a glass of ... (i couldn't understand either because his accent was very strong)
    she glared at him snootily and said "what did you say?!!!??"
    he repeated again.
    and he said in the same tone of voice "I don't understand you!!!"
    he repeated again.
    This time we both made out it was "slew liwer" or something like that
    and she exclaimed VERY LOUDLY
    "OH! YOu want a Screwdriver!!! Why don't you just SAY so properly then!?"

    (forgot to add) AND she told him to REPEAT after her. 'Say SCREWDRIVER" a few times.. which .. he did.. patiently and calmly.

    He smiled, thanked her profusely and took his drink.

    I was shocked and stunned. Being about 17 then I didn't know what to do about it. The blatant racism for so shocking. The air stewardness even 'tsk'ed and shoved the drink the the Japanese businessmen.

    After about 15 mins of angry simmering contemplation over the incident, I turned to him and said "I am sorry but that air stewardess was really rude. You've got to make a complain!"

    The following conversation we had was something I remembered for a long time. Especially when I was faced with racism, bad service, or simply just bad behaviour.

    "If you travel all over the world, you meet all sorts of people. Some who can control themselves, and some who can't. Often it isn't their fault, but their society and upbringing. Instead of complaining about them, SHOW them what is the right thing to do. When enough people show them this, one day they will learn that their environment have changed and they need to change too."
