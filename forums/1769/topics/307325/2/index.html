    So basically, you have an image. Black and white. No greyscales, nor tones.

    Example: Taken from one of my works. :P

     

    For commercial/mass printing, this image will be put on a plate. Whatever the size. It will be clear, because of the contrast. Notice, blacks have tones too, there are light blacks or even dark blacks. And a white, may not truly be white too. Cause of other colours.

    To use a bromide print, and to also include tones. Or partially coloured areas. Halftones are introduced. Basically patterns of dots, of different sizes, and spaces in between to emulate tonal values.
