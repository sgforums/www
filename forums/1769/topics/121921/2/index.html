    Shift work bites the big one. During my undergraduate years, I worked as a residential care worker, and did 48-hour shifts every second weekend, plus support shifts from 4-7 pm on weekdays when I was needed. The one thing that really annoyed me about it was that I had to keep changing my plans according to to my shifts - dinners would be cancelled, I couldn't set time aside to focus on my thesis, and so on.

    Not that 9-to-5 was much better - I'm not a morning person at all, so most people learned to stay out of my way until I've finished my third coffee of the morning. Fighting traffic to and from work every day was a killer too.

    Now that I'm running my own show, it's a lot better. Except for the occasional morning meeting with clients, I can do my work at night when I'm most comfortable, and I can take days off at my own disgression!
