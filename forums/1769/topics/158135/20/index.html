    Hmm...where to start...briefly, Dunhuang is famous for the caves with its Buddhist drawings dating thousands of years back, the most famous being Mo Gao Ku which is opened to the public, and there's the the Ming Sha Shan which is a dessert to play sand in, ride camels lah. Also found here is the town (forgot the name) with the Flaming Mountains (Huo yan shan) from the Journey to the West stories....so called coz the temp is really really hot, easily over 45+ deg C.

    Xinjiang is a big stretch of land + dessert (e.g. Takalamatan (sp)? dessert), including the city of Urumqi with its Heavenly lake at almost 2000m altitude, Kashgar in the west (near to Pakistan) which is inhabited mainly by the Uygher (pronounced Wee-ghur) tribes who are muslim but look like Europeans/Russians. They have lots of the hamid melon which has been making its way to local supermarts.

    Oh...i did the pakistan-karakoram highway to Kashgar in '97. I like pakistan, esp. the north from Gilgit to karimabad - that was before 9-11. I wd like to bring SO but the situation now may not be stable.

    Accom in China is easy to find, whichever class there is (always ask for lower than their published rate)...unless it happens to be the peak local vacation season (2 wks bef and aft their national day 1 Oct, i think).

    Transport is mainly by long dist. buses or take domestic flights if you can spend more, but in XJ, there no long dist. buses, so it's mainly by long-dist. train, and booking the sleeper is a really tricky thing, and we suspect they have a 'kelong' system whereby the sleeper tickets are all reserved for certain tour agents and of course John's Cafe which is found in most towns/cities in XJ - it's the only way to get soft sleepers - by paying abt 10% commission.

    The only thing to deal with and is the most culturally shocking thing is their toilets.
