    Originally posted by Rhonda:
    Don't you miss Singapore food?
    Well, yes and no. I am not a particularly good cook to begin with, and the ingredients necessary for authentic S'pore food are rather hard to come by in this part of the country.

    Last night, per a good friend's suggestion, we headed to downtown Minneapolis for dinner at a recently-opened Asian restaurant called The Peninsula. Surprisingly, they had a number of recognizably Malaysian/Singapore dishes on the menu, like ayam and beef rendang, and--get this--HAINANESE CHICKEN RICE!

    Needless to say, I ordered that, and was rather surprised at its authenticity. It even came with the ginger and chilli sauce, and real black sauce (hak yao). They even served it with the proper rice that was cooked in chicken fat/stock. All in all, not a bad find, even if the price was a bit steep (US$10 for a two-serving portion). I'll probably be heading back there again to try some of the other selections on their menu.
