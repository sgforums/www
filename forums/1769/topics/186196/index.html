Is Revenge really "A Dish Best Served Cold"?

  • BabyRS

    "Revenge is an act of passion; vengeance of justice. Injuries are revenged; crimes are avenged."
    ~ Samuel Johnson

    Why are some people so hell bent on getting revenge?

    I do admit... we are humans, and our sense and sensibility often clash in life, but there are some who will do just about anything to satisfy their need to see someone get back what is deemed deserving for a past action.

    Is it just a need to sate our moral intuitions? Will it make us happier to see the person suffer in return?

    In truth, if we do not express anger and moral outrage at evil, something is very wrong with us. To not feel anger at an injustice will make us saints by any means... and I know that most of us are incapable of reigning in our emotions. Most of the time, it takes all of my willpower to remind myself that "Two wrongs don't make a right"; but not carrying out the action does not diminish the fact that the thought IS there.

    How far will you go?

    There are those who will object: " Isn't retribution just a pretext for vengeance and our impulses?" For example... in politics - there are some countries who retaliate in the name of justice. "We shall not let them trample all over us - let's go to war! They shall get what they deserve!" Isn't the basis of that simply wanting to exact vengeance? Is it actually possible, in reality - to distinguish between vengeance and retribution?

    Seriously though... between our doubts and cynicisms - there are several ways that retribution can be deemed to be different from revenge / vengeance.

    1. Revenge often has a "face". It usually rears its ugly head when there is a tangible action done - when we are able to see and feel the consequences of another's actions. Retribution (or karma), however, usually addresses an "objective" wrong.

    2. Revenge has no limits. There is no telling how far a person will go to avenge himself. Retribution has both upper and lower limits. We do not punish equally... and who is to say that someone got what he deserved in deference to his actions?

    3. Vengeance is subject to our own satisfaction. It delights in seeing someone hurt or come to harm to answer for his past misdeeds. Retribution leans more toward "for the greater good of all". It takes no pleasure in punishing to sate an inner need - it does so because it needs to have a detterent effect on the wrongdoer.

    4. someone who partakes in revenge is doing so to "retaliate" against an injustice done, whereas retribution seeks impartial justice and demands fairness on all accounts. (depicted in a blindfolded Lady Justice)

    Revenge is in all accounts - a short lived pleasure; but it does come as a 'closure' to perhaps a deep seated, long term simmering displeasure.
