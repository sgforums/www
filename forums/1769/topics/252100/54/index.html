    You have to complete a certain number of credit hours to graduate. Each class you take in a given semester earns you credit hours. For example, if you take 5 classes during a semester, and each of those classes counts for 3 credit hours, you earn a total of 15 credit hours during that semester. So, for example, if your intended degree requires 120 credit hours of coursework, and you plan on taking 15 credit hours of classes each semester, then you're looking at 4 years of studies to get your degree (assuming 2 semesters per year and no summer classes).

    The graduation requirements will usually specify how the total number of credit hours required for the degree have to be distributed between elective and core courses.

    If your plan is to enroll in a community college and subsequently transfer to a 4-year college, you might want to check ahead with the 4-year college to find out which CCs they accept credits from.
