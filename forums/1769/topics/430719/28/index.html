    me and fatumnette are heading up to bangkok again in late august .... when are you heading ? ...

    ayuthaya is just basically ruins plus tourist traps to trap the tourists touring the ruins .... 

    river kwai is in kanchanaburi .... you can take the train to the place ... but be warned .... the train goes at the speed of a clopping horse .... the trip is seriously long .... faster to take the bus from bangkok .... takes only a few hours ... there are house boats to rent over there .... you can stay on the river if you don't mind roughing it a bit.

    if you like nature and trekking there are a few really nice national parks out of kanchanaburi town .... there's all death railway thingy like hellfire pass etc which they show to every singapore army boys that go there to train (our training base is in the outskirts of K-town ... so you may just run into NS boys on their nights out). You should go to erawan national park ... I really enjoyed hiking there .... there's a series of very nice water falls you can trek up and swim in .... with fishes that'll suck on your toes and eat the dead skin .... original fish spa .... there's also khao lam national park ..... another very national geographic like location ... the other big one, sri nakarin, is actually a giant dam ... like lake kenyir .... but there are house boats you can rent also .....

    why not go to the beaches east of bangkok ? ... like rayong and sattahip and the islands beyond ? ....

    it's like phuket without much of the trash and ang moh lager yobs ....

  • Fatum
    11 Jul 11, 01:18

    andaman sea monsoons (phuket, krabi etc), can get pretty bad ..... you can't even swim off the north side of phuket right now ... 

    on the east coast, the monsoons are pretty mild .... i've gone and dived there from september through to december, supposedly right smack in the middle of the monsoon on the east coast, with no probs .... seas a bit more choppy offshore, vis a bit worse than the dry season, but still good ...

    koh kut is famous, now, for it's six senses resort which'd cost you a kidney per night .... but note that koh kut has zero night life ... when I was still in thailand, the place was just beach shacks and backpackers ... totally unspoilt ...

    if you want a wee bit of partying, then it's koh samet, there are some thumping beach bars with an international crowd at sai keow beach .... you can snorkel off the rocky points or you can sign up for dive or snokelling trips offshore ... I remember I got a scare from a really big barracuda during one of the dives ...

    you can make your way from bangkok's airport to ekkemai or eastern bus terminal, and there are buses to pattaya, sattahip, rayong, trat and beyond .... I suggest you stage to pattaya first and then stage out from there ...there are some really cheap digs in pattaya, and it's not all sleeze really .... don't believe the bus timings, the buses do take a bloody long time cos they'll stop very often from chonburi onwards.....

    note also that beer and food is more expensive the further out you go ... in those islands everything has to be shipped in and a beer can cost 3 times (to around singapore prices) the price in pattaya. Though I remember a meal in trat, right by the cambodian border .... scallops, prawns, crab, lobster, seasnails, fish, horseshoe crab salad, plus beer, for only 700 baht ....
