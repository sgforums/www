    Just thought I'd share 2 rather inspiring observations and.. err the occasional thinking process they've invoked.. Humm.

    I.
    About 7 plus in the morning and I was on my usual half asleep way to work. I saw a middle aged man in his 40s pushing his trolley with his goods at the back of a row of shophouses. From behind the delivery van, another middle aged guy shot out, laughing with a plastic bottle in his hands. He started chasing his co-worker, spraying him with the water from the bottle as they ran laughing loudly.

    It was just so unlike the usual sombre mood of workers in the early morning that these two men, who obviously either started work much earlier than most of us, or have been working through the night has it in themselves to not be dour and sour, and still enjoy what they are doing. It.. was also.. infectiously cheerful to be greeted by such cheerfulness so early on my work day. A reminder to have fun, I guess, and not treat everything so seriously

    II.
    Err. A bit voyeuristic this one. Was borrowing books at the library and.. -guilty- I picked up the borrowing slip left behind on the machine. Before I realised what I was doing, I was already reading the titles of the books borrowed by the previous user. Sigh, I know it's kind of.. invading privacy but... I was drawn by the first title I saw 'How Not to be a Crochety Old Man' and.. then "Not Letting Age Get You Down".... and ... well.. Its like a little glimpse into someone's heart... It just.. shows.. u know.. Hmm.. so much courage and self-awareness and the desire to not let life dictate who you are... I mean, most of the time people are reading self help books on how to improve in ways that would benefit YOURSELF directly like.. How to make Friends or How to make MOney... but this guy... well..

    Hmm then again. Maybe its the guy's wife who borrowed the books for him
