    Four more hours to the end of the day for me! 

    My day started really eventfully.    Don't know why, my brain is like a sieve these days, full of holes and information just conveniently slides out of my brain!    Packed my bag early for my trip for my Immigration Health Check-Up, and somehow, my passport slid out of my envelope and onto my study desk!  So I left home, waited for the tram, boarded the tram, sat down, and out of the blue, thought, "I don't think I have my passport!" Dug around in my  handbag, true enough, no passport!!    Took another tram and walked briskly home, grabbed my passport, and called a cab on the way.   THANK GOD the cab fare turned out to be only A$13.10!!   

    Thankfully also, the check-up went smoothly.  I've got priority by virtue of my occupation!!    They stapled a special pink slip to my forms and in total, my waiting time was a tad less than the others!    Not bad!  The counter staff said for doctors and nurses, priority is always given and the visa applications are even processed in a different section from the rest.  Happy happy happy!! 

    Since I was in town, I went to explore another area of town I've never done before.  Went to the University's bookshop, hung around in the Korean grocery store, walked past the Korean restaurant they recommended, and ended up buying tons of snacks, and then crossed over to Melbourne Central to get Gelato in a waffle cone, and then crossed over to the mini park in front of the Library to sit on the bench and watch the seagulls as I slowly enjoyed my gelato!    You can choose 3 flavours in a waffle cone for just A$4.50.  Sadly, they didn't have the Ferraro Rocher flavour today, so I had the Mars flavour, the Blood Orange, and then the Strawberry.  It was sunny and warm - about 32 deg C, and it was lovely sitting on the stone bench, licking my ice cream, and watching the world go by. 

    Took the free City Circle Tram to Bourke Street (Chinatown) to the Laksa Mee stall to eat Malaysian Laksa Lemak, but it was already past 3pm and they were closed until dinnertime!!    They've been getting rave reviews so I die-die MUST try it on my next day off! 


    Made the fatal mistake of walking into JB Hi-Fi and ended up with another ton of DVD's.     And oh, almost forgot, before I reached JB Hi-Fi, I saw a lovely sign saying, "Old Speckled Hen"!!!    Yippeeeeeeeeee!!!    Gonna drag my Irish colleague there real soon!! 

    Finally, made my way home!  What a looong day out!    Melbourne is growing on me!
