    Baked Mozerella Spread

    what:
    mozerella
    cherry tomatoes
    basil leaves
    anchovies

    how:
    slice up a couple of big lemons (crosswise) into 3/4 inch slices
    remove the pulp to leave a ring of rind
    shove some mozerella into the ring (half the depth)
    put a slice of the cherry tomato into the mozerella
    place a bit of anchovy into the cheese
    place a bit of basil into the cheese
    shove cheese until level with rind
    use a couple of big basil leaves to cover up the whole thing (top and bottom, can also use greaseproof paper)
    bake in 200 degree oven for about 4 minutes..

    enjoy by taking the top off and slopping the melted cheese onto hot crusty bread

    As requested, here's a couple of c-o-c-ktail recipes:

    (1) Singapore Sling

    Ingredients:
    2 oz or 56ml gin
    juice of half lemon
    1 teaspoon powdered caster sugar
    soda water
    half oz or 14ml cherry brandy

    Method:
    Pour the gin, lemon and sugar into a glass (Pilsner) filled with ice. Stir. Add the soda water, leaving just enough room to float the cherry brandy on top. To garnish, place a lemon wheel inside the glass, and drop in a cherry if desired.

    (2) Pina Colada

    Ingredients:
    3 oz or 84ml light rum
    3 tablespoon coconut milk
    dash pineapple juice
    half handful of fresh pineapple (chopped)
    dash sugar syrup (optional)

    Method:
    Blend all the ingredients at high speed with a cup of crushed ice until smooth. Pour the mix into the glass (Large Wine Glass). Garnish with a pineapple wedge and 3 pineapple leaves.

    Cherry Ripe mud cake

    Ingredients:
    250g butter, chopped
    1 tablespoon instant coffee granules
    1 and two-third cups (400ml) coconut milk
    200g dark eating chocolate, chopped coarsely
    2 cups (440g) caster sugar
    three-quarter cup (110g) self raising flour
    1 cup (150g) plain flour
    one quarter (25g) cocoa powder
    2 eggs, beaten lightly
    1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    2 x 85g Cherry Ripe bars, chopped coarsely
    200g dark eating chocolate, chopped coarsely, extra
    125g butter, chopped, extra

    Chocolate Panels
    300g dark chocolate Melts
    1 teaspoon vegetable oil

    Method:
    (1) Preheat oven to slow (150 deg. C or 130 deg. C fan-forced). Grease deep 22cm round cake pan; line base and side with baking paper

    (2) Melt butter in large sauce pan; add coffee, coconut milk, chocolate and sugar. Stir over heat until chocolate melts and sugar dissolves; cool to room temperature.

    (3) Whisk in sifted dry ingredients, then egg and extract; stir in half of the Cherry Ripe. Pour mixture into pan, top with remaining Cherry Ripe; bake about 1.75 hours. Stand cake 10 min; turn, top-side up, onto wire rack to cool.

    (4) Combine extra chocolate and extra butter in small saucepan; stir over low heat until smooth. Refrigerate until mixture is spreadable.

    (5) Meanwhile, make chocolate panels*.

    (6) Spread chocolate mixture all over cake; place chocolate panels around side of cake.

    *Chocolate panels
    Combine chocolate and oil in medium heatproof bowl; stir over medium saucepan of simmering water until smooth. Cut two 6cm x 50cm strips of baking paper. Spread chocolate evenly over strips; lift strips to allow chocolate to drip off paper. Allow chocolate to set, then, using ruler as a guide, cut chocolate into 4cm panels with sharp knife. Carefully peel away baking paper.

    tip: You can also melt the chocolate for the chocolate panels in a microwave oven; melt on Medium (55%) about 1 min, stirring twice during cooking. Stir in the oil once the chocolate has melted.

    MM's IRISH STEW (reposted for easy ref)

    INGREDIENTS
    2 lb (900g) Gigot Mutton or Lamb chops
    2 lb (900g) Potatoes
    1 lb (450g) Onions
    1 large Turnip
    4 large Carrotts
    1 bay leaf
    1 Sprige of Parsley
    Pepper & Salt
    Pinch of thyme ,sage (or favorite mix of herbs)
    1 and a half pints (850ml) of Water

    INSTRUCTIONS
    1. Divide the meat up into even cutlets (ignor if using lamb chops)
    2. Cut off all excess fat from meat
    3. Peel and half Potatoes
    4. Peel and Slice the Onions
    5. Peel and Chop Turnip and Carrotts
    6. Place the Meat in a Large Pan, add a pinch of pepper,salt, bayleaf and seasoning on top
    7. Place the remainding Ingredients in the Pan in layers as following:
    Meat - Onions - Turnips - Carrots - Potatoes
    8. Add a pinch of seasoning between each layer
    9. Add Water, salt, pepper and seasoning to taste bring to the boil
    10. Reduce heat and place lid on Pan, simmer on a gentle heat for 2 hours
    11. Check regularly and stir if necessary to ensure no sticking is occuring
    12. Add additional water if required, remember the dish should be quite thick, so do not make it too watery.
    13. Chop the Parsley finely and lightly sprinkle on top of stew when serving

    Serving Suggestion: With Homemade Brown Bread and Irish Butter

    Simple easy pig's trotters with ginger and vinegar

    2 pig's trotters, approx 2kg
    1 kg old ginger, skinned & smashed lightly
    4 tablespoons sesame oil
    1 bottle black vinegar (you need 4 cups)
    9 cups of water
    600g brown sugar(can adjust to your taste)
    4 hard boiled eggs, shelled

    * clean and pluck hair off pig's trotters, cut into big serving pieces. (tell the supermarket people to cut it for you the best)

    *Heat sesame oil in a wok, when oil is hot, fry the old ginger till golden brown and gragrant.

    *put the fried ginger into a big claypot. Incude in the viengar, water and brown sugar. Bring to boil and leave to simmer for half hour till the ginger becomes soft. (If not claypot, the slow cooking pot or the very least...the teflon coated electic rice cooker.)

    *put in the pig trotters and continue to simmer till trotters are sofe (approx 2 hours)

    *add shelled hard boiled eggs half an hour before serving.

    Note: the best if you simmer the vinegar for a week in the slow cook crockpot. Top up with pig trooters & vinegar as and when you run out or run low. The finally last day of that vinegar is going to be gluey and taste great.

    For extras: throw in some 5 star spice.

    Lazy auntie's Braised Pork Hocks. (Instead of pork trotters, family prefers pork hocks, cos it got more meat.)

    4 slices of pork hocks.
    4-5 slices of ginger
    1 tablespoon sugar
    1 tablespoon salad oil (I use cooking oil)

    10 pieces dried black mushroom (I use fresh shitake mushroom)
    1 cup dark soy sauce
    1 cup light soy sauce
    Dried chilli (No chilli, so I used chilli powder; I threw in 1 teaspoon chilli powder)
    Peppercorns (no peppercorns, so I used black pepper; 1 teaspoon)
    Star Anise 2 pieces
    5 spice powder (1 teaspoon)
    1 cup rice wine
    some salt
    some water to help cover the ingredients in the final stewing pot.

    Here's how.

    Wash the pork hocks
    Put a pot of water to boil, put in pork hock to boil for 3-4 minutes

    In a wok or a bigger cast iron pot. Put in oil fry the ginger and the sugar till the sugar melted.
    Put in the mushrooms.
    Put in the pork hocks & pour in the dark soy sauce, light soy sauce, rice wine & some water to cover totally

    Sprinkle in the dried chilli or the chilli powder,
    Sprinke in the peppercorns or black pepper
    Sprinkle in the 5 star powder
    Throw in the star Anise
    some salt

    Put everything to a boil and use slow fire to slow cook after boiling.

    For extras,

    hard boil some eggs. cracked the eggs sheels and put into the braised pork soup for the extra braised eggs 1/2 hour before serving.

    Also save the soup and use them to boil noodles. So you have braised noodles too.

    To thicken the soup, you can also add in some corn starch.

    And for serving, some black vinegar at the side.

    For truly hawker style, some deep fried fish slices, onion stalk slices.

    Godiva Tiramisu

    GODIVA LIQUEUR, 5 ounces divided (3+2)
    ESPRESSO OR STRONG BLACK COFFEE, 1/2 cup
    LADYFINGERS, 16 to 20
    MASCARPONE, 1 pound
    EGGS, 2, separated
    CONFECTIONER'S SUGAR, 1/3 cup
    DARK CHOCOLATE, 3 to 4 ounces, grated
    Directions

    Mix 3 ounces of Godiva Liqueur with coffee, and set aside.
    For each serving, trim 4 ladyfingers to fit along the sides and bottom of a 1-cup souffle dish.
    Pour 1 ounce of the reserved coffee into each souffle dish and set aside.
    Beat together the mascarpone cheese, egg yolks, sugar and remaining 2 ounces of Godiva Liqueur until smooth
    Whip the egg whites until stiff, and fold into the cheese.
    Divide the filling evenly among the souffle dishes.
    Sprinkle with chocolate and refrigerate overnight.
