    PORK RIB COLA CHAR SIEW

    Prep : 1 hr (includes marinating time)
    Cook : 30 mins
    Serves : 4

    500g pork ribs
    2 heaped tbsps Lee Kum Kee Char Siew Sauce
    2 cans of Coca-cola
    Fresh coriander to garnish

    1. In a saucepan, rub char siew sauce over pork ribs evenly.

    2. Pour cola over ribs and allow to marinate in room temperature for 1 hr.

    3. Bring pan contents to the boil over a high fire. Stir occasionally to ensure sauce doesn't stick to pan. Cook for 20 mins until sauce thickens slightly.

    4. Turn fire to low and continue stirring for 5 - 10 mins or until pork ribs are soft. Serve hot, garnished with coriander.

    Milk chocolate mousse cake



    Serves 10

    4 eggs, at room temperature
    125g caster sugar
    2/3 cup (100g) plain flour
    30g Dutch cocoa (see below)
    25g butter, melted
    1/4 cup (55g) caster sugar, extra
    2 tsp brandy
    Chocolate shavings and cocoa, to serve

    Mousse
    400g milk chocolate, coarsely chopped
    200g dark chocolate, coarsely chopped
    41/4 cups (2.25 litres) thickened cream
    1 x 5g gelatine leaf

    Preheat oven to 180ÂºC. Grease and line a 24cm round springform tin. Using an electric mixer, beat eggs on high speed for 5 minutes or until light and fluffy. In a saucepan, bring sugar and 2 tbs water to the boil. Pour hot syrup into egg mixture and beat on low-medium speed for 6 minutes or until mixture cools and forms thin ribbons when beaters are lifted.
    Sift combined flour and cocoa over egg mixture. Gently fold in, then fold in butter. Pour into prepared tin. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until a skewer inserted into the centre comes out clean. Stand in tin for 5 minutes then turn on to a wire rack to cool.

    Place extra sugar and 11/2 tbs water in a small saucepan and boil over medium heat until sugar dissolves. Remove from heat. Allow to cool then stir in brandy.

    For mousse, place all chocolate in a heatproof bowl over a saucepan of barely simmering water. Stir until melted then cool to room temperature. Meanwhile, bring 1/4 cup cream to a simmer in a saucepan over medium heat. Remove from heat. Soak gelatine in cold water for 3-4 minutes or until soft. Squeeze out excess water then add to hot cream and stir until dissolved. Cool to room temperature. Using an electric mixer, whisk remaining cream to soft peaks. Working quickly, add chocolate and gelatine mixture to cream and whisk for 1-2 seconds or until chocolate is 1/2 incorporated. Using a spatula, gently finish folding in. (If chocolate is added too slowly or over-whisked mousse will be grainy.)

    To assemble cake, line base of a 24cm springform tin. Using a serrated knife, level top of sponge then place sponge in tin. Brush with brandy syrup then spoon over mousse. Refrigerate overnight or until set. Top with chocolate shavings and cocoa.

    Short Ribs



    Source: Ladies' Home Journal

    Makes 8 servings
    Prep: 40 minutes
    Start to Finish: 4 hours 40 minutes

    Ingredients
    4 pounds boneless beef short ribs
    1 tablespoon olive oil
    1 large onion, chopped
    6 garlic cloves, sliced
    3 large carrots, coarsely chopped
    2 celery ribs, chopped
    1/2 cup dry red wine
    1 can (28 oz.) crushed tomatoes in thick puree
    3/4 cup pitted sliced green olives
    3/4 cup low-sodium beef broth
    1/4 cup coarsely chopped flat-leaf parsley, plus additional for garnish
    1/2 teaspoon ground allspice
    1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
    1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
    2 thyme sprigs

    Directions
    1. Rinse short ribs and pat dry; season with salt. Heat oil in a large skillet over moderately high heat until hot but not smoking. Add ribs, in batches, and cook, turning, until browned on all sides, about 5 minutes; transfer, as cooked, to slow cooker. Pour off all but 1 tablespoon of fat from skillet. Add onion, garlic, carrots, and celery; cook over moderate heat, stirring and scraping up any brown bits from bottom of skillet, about 3 minutes. Add wine and simmer 2 minutes. Transfer to slow cooker with remaining ingredients and stir to combine. Cook, covered, on low heat until ribs are very tender, about 4 hours. Remove thyme sprigs. Serve ribs with their sauce over buttered noodles or mashed potatoes and garnish with parsley. Makes 8 servings.

    Nutrition facts per serving:
    calories: 845
    total fat: 71.5g
    saturated fat: 29g
    cholesterol: 152mg
    sodium: 617mg
    carbohydrate: 13g
    fiber: 3g
    protein: 37g

    Elindra's Easy-Peasy 'Oxtail Stew' for Lazy Gluttons

    Servings: 2 - 3 persons

    Ingredients
    800g - 1kg Fresh Oxtail
    1 large yellow / white onion (Cut into chunks)
    8 Cloves of Garlic (Smashed with skin removed)
    As many Potatoes and Carrots you want to eat (Cut to desired size)
    2 Cans Campbell's Oxtail Soup
    Lea & Perrins Worcestershire Sauce
    Red Wine

    Method
    Add water into a big pot (enough to cover oxtail) and bring to boil
    Add Oxtail into water and boil for 1 min and remove and empty water
    (you can skip this step if you want a stronger beef flavor)
    Dump the 2 cans of Campbell's Oxtail Soup and add 2 cans of water. Mix well
    Add in Oxtail, Onions, Potatoes, Carrots and Garlic, bring pot to boil
    Stir frequently to prevent stew from burning
    Simmer for at least 2 hours (I like to simmer it for at least 4 hrs)
    Add in about 2 tablespoons of worcestershire sauce or until desired taste
    Pour in as much red wine as you like and
    Simmer for additional 10 mins.

    We like to eat the stew with warm baguatte

    PS. I forgot to add that it taste better if you add in a pinch of dried Rosemary leaves

    Jellyfish Salad

    ingredients:
    jellyfishy thingy.. get from provision shop at the market.. looks like a translucent mouse pad
    vinegar
    sugar
    plum sauce
    sweet chilli (sin sin chilli sauce is good)
    chinese celery

    preparation...

    jellyfish thingy:

    soak in cold water.. change every 5 to 6 hours until it is clear (takes a couple of days)

    when the water is clear, taste the water a bit (don't chug and drink the stuff!) it must be tasteless..

    hot water from a flask (do NOT use boiling water, temp should be around, maybe 85degrees or something) pour onto the jellyfishy thingy, stir a bit (about 5 to 10 seconds), then run under tap water or cold water, until cool.. remove and towel dry with kitchen towels... place in container...

    when you want to eat, take out, towel dry (again coz it kinda "loses water" ) and slice it up or use scissors to cut into strips... (do that just before serving, try to dry it out as much as possible)

    sauce:

    about a jar of plum sauce, some chilli sauce, a splash of vinegar and sugar... stir stir stir.. until all dissolves and mixes.. put in fridge until you want to serve..

    serving:

    get the stems of chinese celery (kng chye) and use the stems.. cut them into about 2 to 3mm pieces, stir a whole lot into the sauce...

    get the jellyfishy thingy, dry out, cut into strips..
    put into plate..
    pour the prepared sauce onto the jellyfishy strips..
    mix.. serve

    the serving must be done just before eating.. else the jellyfishy things kinda disintegrates and "leaks" water..

    enjoy

    Stuffed Pickled Green Chillies

    Part Un - Stuffing
    dried shrimp
    bit of red chilli
    tumeric (fresh)
    unripe papaya

    Method
    grate the unripe papaya

    pound the crap out of the dried shrimp, red chilli and tumeric into a coarse pasty mess.. heat up a wok with a bit of oil and stirfry that bit for a while to bring out the fragrant oils locked in the stuff..

    after the stuff becomes fragrant (doesn't take long) throw in the papaya and stir around for a very short while just to mix up the stuff..

    remove and allow to cool

    Part Deux - Green Chillies
    get some BIG FAT (must be big and fat) green chillies (not bell peppers), slice them lengthwise and remove the insides... try not to break the chillies as it's the crunch which makes it yummy

    Part Trois - Putting The Thing Together
    get the cooled stuffing and shove into the prepared green chillies... be generous...

    Part Quatre - Pickling
    get a jar which will fit the chillies in.. put the stuffed chillies into the jar

    dissolve a bit of sugar into vinegar to take the edge off the vinegar... a little will do..

    pour the vinegar into the chillies and bung into the fridge

    ready for eating in about 1 to 2 days

    Steamed Spare Ribs

    Ingredients:
    1 kg of spare ribs (chopped into small segment)
    1 Tablespoon of salted soya bean
    1 x Preserved Plums
    2 x Fresh chillies
    2 - 3 tablespoons of Water
    3/4 teaspoon of Sugar
    Soya Sauce
    Corn Flour

    Method:
    Marinade the spare ribs with some corn flour and soy sauce for 30 min. Mash the salted soya bean, preserved plum and fresh chillies in a bowl (or mortal and pestel if you have one). Remove the stone of the plum from the "salted soya bean mixture". Add 2 - 3 tablespoons of water and 3/4 teaspoon of sugar and stir until sugar is dissolved.

    Pour the "mixture" on the marinated spare ribs until all the ribs are covered with the "mixture". You can add a few tablespoons of water to it if you want more sauce (optional). Steam it for about 20 - 25 min.

    Serve it with rice. Yummy!!

    Pickled Crab

    What:
    - Live Mud Crab
    - a LOT of dark soy sauce (superior grade but must be salty enough to preserve crabs)
    - garlic, smashed.. as much as you want for taste
    - chilli, cut up.. as much as you want for taste.

    How:
    - Clean the crabs.. until they are VERY clean.. this involves the little flap/tail bit where you actually have to squeeze the yuck out..

    - do NOT kill the crabs or allow the shell to crack..

    - wipe until totally dry.. this is very important.. allow crab exterior (shell) to dry totally...

    - place crab into a pot and start pouring the dark soy sauce into the pot until the crabs are covered... this starts the pickling immediately and also kills the crabs..

    - add the smashed garlic and chilli into the mess.. stir a bit..

    - after a couple of days, the crab is ready to be eaten.. serve with shell cracked and soaking in vinegar and chilli.. best with teochew porridge..

    (this same method can be used for the tree-climbing "vinegar crab" which lives in swampy mangrove areas.. teochew call it "buar-kee" )

    Easy Two-Egg Pavlova


    (The pavlova in the picture was my first attempt, before I had a proper tray and you can see I have overcooked it slightly and some "peaks" were burnt. )

    Ingredients:

    Shell:
    2 egg-whies
    1 1/2 (one and a half) cups castor sugar
    1/2 (half) teaspoon vanilla
    1 teaspoon vinegar
    1 teaspoon cornflour
    4 tablespoons boiling water

    Filling and topping:
    250ml of thickened cream
    Fruits (eg. strawberry, passionfruit, kiwi fruits)

    Method:
    Shell:
    Place all ingredients into small bowl of electric mixer, beat on high speed until mixture is very stiff (approximately 15 min). Spread on to prepared* tray. The mixture makes on 11 inch. pavlova.

    Baking in electric oven - Bake in moderate (180 degrees celcius) oven for 10 min, reduce heat to slow (150 degrees celcius), bake for further 45 min, cool in oven.

    Baking in Gas oven - Bake in moderate (180 degrees celcius) oven for 10 min, reduce heat to slow (150 degrees celcius), bake for further 1 to 1 1/2 hours, cool in oven.

    Filling:
    Beat the thickened cream with a electric mixer on high until cream is stiff.

    Use a knife to gently "knock" the top of the pavlova shell to make a hole in the middle for the filling.

    Fill the hole with the whipped cream and decorate it with fruits.

    * To avoid the pavlova to stick to the baking tray, cover the tray with some cornflour.

    Sweet Eggplant Stew (Semur Terong)

    Ingredients

    - 1 lb. eggplant
    - 4 tablespoon vegetable oil
    - 1 tablespoon shallot
    - 1 clove garlic, sliced
    - Half a cup of water
    - 1 tablespoon soy sauce
    - Half tablespoon pepper
    - Quarter tablespoon nutmeg
    - 1 tablespoon vinegar
    - Half tablespoon salt
    - 1 tablespoon sugar

    Directions

    Cut the unpeeled eggplant into half inch thick slices, and then cut the slices in half. Fry lightly in 2 tablespoon of oil for 2 minutes or until light brown or softened. Set aside.

    Stir fry the shallots and garlic in the remaining oil until light brown. Add the water, sweet soy sauce, pepper, nutmeg, vinegar, salt and sugar. Let these entire ingredients cook for approximately 3 minutes to prepare the sauce.

    Cook the eggplant slices in the sauce for additional 2 minutes to distribute the flavors. Shake the pan several times to mix but not mash the eggplant.

    Make four servings.
