    Had a little adventure in one back in the winter of '01 while riding it to work. It was an extremely cold morning with a very stiff wind blowing across the freeway. We were doing the speed limit (60 mph) and, as the artic emerged from under a bridge, two things happened simulatenously: (a) we rolled over a patch of 'black ice' on the road and (b) we were hit by a very strong gust of crosswind. The rear half of the of artic started to fishtail violently as the rear wheels alternately broke and regained traction. I was on a longitudinal bench seat in the fore section, and looked back to see the entire rear cabin pivoting from side to side. A couple of passengers were thrown into the aisle, but there were no injuries. The fishtailing lasted for several seconds, until the driver slowed the artic down enough for the rear tires to regain traction. It was an... interesting... ride, to say the least--for a while, it really felt as if we were going to go completely sideways.
