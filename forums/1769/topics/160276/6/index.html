    I don't play the blame game.

    I've seen both selfless and selfish people. You can't judge the condition of an entire population (Singapore or elsewhere) just by one incident.

    As such, it's the luck of the draw who you approach first. This girl was terribly unlucky to meet unhelpful people.

    I really can't make any qualified statement of "people WILL help" or "people WILL NOT help" . All I know is that I will give help if it is required. And if I need help , the outcome will be dependent on my crisis management skills , luck and the generosity of other poeple.

    If I were in the mother's shoes, depending on the child's condition (not specified in article) and circumstances, I may decide to take the taxi, because I've read that ambulances may take a long time to respond. I believe it's possible that we reach a hospital more quickly in a taxi than an ambulance can bring us there. This would depend on the circumstance

    My mom believes that when you have a child, get a car - specifically for the use during emergency when you can't afford to wait. But of course, not everyone can afford transportation.

    But in the same attitude of helping yourself/ hedging against risks/ increasing your odds of weathering a crisis , first aid knowledge is important, not just for yourself or family but also if you're approached for help by strangers, you can provide better assistance.
