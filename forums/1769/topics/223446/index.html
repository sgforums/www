    Getting there is much easier... (which isnt a good thing because if TOO many people go there, it loses its charm)

    Now there are direct coaches from Singapore (check out konsortium) though return trip you still got to stop over at KL

    Alternative, you can drive up to IPOH and use the new road which is a lot more straightforward. though a couple of hours further. But hey, you arrive not vomiting - so that's a good deal.

    What things to see and do there there...my favourites are

    - Nuah at one of those naan shops and have my breakfast, teh and thosai
    - Visit cactus valley and look at all the cute plants.. maybe buy some... pots too
    - Visit Kea Farm and look at all the nature produce.. uhh pots.. err more plants..
    - Go to Tea Plantation and NUAH there
    - Go to Tea plantation and walk around
    - Eat steamboat
    - Eat strawberries and cream
    - Eat Scones and Tea
    - Go to Pasar Malam and buy fantastically fresh vegetables for a salad
    - And the GIANT CORN that tastes great without cooking
    - AND all the crap junk pasar malam food like deep fried cempedak (ooh)
    - If you can, take a guided trek, visit orang asli village, and maybe a couple of smaller waterfalls
    - Just NUAH bring indoor games and play w ur friends

    Woooo so much to eat and nuah.. so little time....[/list]
