    Hi Littlestream,

    Sorry, I'm a little late.....

    I run the Asia-Pacific branch of a European company and I work from both the office (cum warehouse) and home. I'd very much work from home if possible as I have the discipline to control myself....eg. I first work for two hours before watching TV and sleeping the next two hours. And besides being able to work in just my underwear (which is real comfortable), I save on time and petrol money (I drive a guzzler).

    Sometimes I'm at my office/warehouse, sometimes at home, sometimes half-half, sometimes overseas (I make 1~2 trips per month on the average). Sometimes I work from swimming pool and gym downstairs too

    I know of some people who know that they don't have the discipline to work from home and would rather not - cos they know they'll be doing nothing and will lose their jobs. They need to dress up and work in an office under supervision. Stupid people!

    Was gonna tell you to examine your own character and hopefully find yourself suitable for it and try it out....but it looks like you've already made the decision. Congratulations on the excellent choice!

    The only disadvantage I can think of is that after getting used to working from home, you'll never be able to work in a big company with colleagues, fixed timings, etc, again. Its so sickening to follow timings, see all the office politics, etc. When you're used to working from home, commuting to the office is a stress in itself! I've been working independently for 8~9 years now and will have difficulties adjusting back to normal office life (if I ever have to).

    (Before this present company, I used to run a regional rep office for a US public listed company.....also working from both office and home).

    Another plus for me working from home is that I'm able to watch my sexy neighbour washing her car on some days, always around 10 am. Wah lau...when she stretches out to clean the windscreen.....
