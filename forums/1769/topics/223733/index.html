    Once the coach left the city and got on the expressway, we could see the bridge (darnit, forgot the name of the bridge! Heeelp!!) This is the highest bridge in Australia as it goes over the Yarra River near the Docklands where container ships travel. Therefore, it has to be built high enough for these large vessels to pass by underneath it.


    And then, we hit the farmlands! We were clearly in the suburbs now! I can almost hear the Dixie Chicks singing, "Wide Open Spaces"!


    There's currently a drought here and it's been touted to be the worst drought in 20 years. There's a total fire ban today as there are about 50 forest fires raging right now in the Northern parts of Victoria. There's also a Stage II Water Restriction in force. Look at how dry it can get! You can see the low water levels of the pond in the foreground.


    On the Great Ocean Road now!


    Look at this really interesting road that seems to appear from nowhere and head nowhere! I call this "The Road to Nowhere"!


    Taken during our first stop. It was sunny but with extremely strong winds and therefore, very cold! Brrrr!! And this... I call it, "Tidal Art"!


    Whilst admiring the pristine beach, I spotted these two surfer dudes carrying their surfboards in the distance. I stood there gazing at them until a group of very noisy teenage girls came over and got really excited over them. Nothing like a rowdy bunch of teenage girls with their incessant shrieks and all to ruin an almost magical moment.


    Peekaboo! Wouldn't you love to live in a little beach hut like this one? Your very own little niche by the beach!
