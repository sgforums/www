    Originally posted by choco B:
    HEY that tall built in shelf thing with the oven is a good idea for my kitchen!

    Hmm now where to put it

    How do I instruct the contractor to build something like that? Do I just buy the oven and he'll measure it out & build?

    Is the oven like tight-fitting and fits permanently in the hole, or is it just an empty shelf and you slot the oven in?

    Err what if you change ovens, are all ovens the same size?
    Yes, you do need to have all the appliances for the right fittings. All ovens should be of standard size. So if you know what model of oven you're going to buy, just get the manufacturer details from the shop and they will have something to tell you the minimum space required for the oven.

    Just make sure your contractor is doing the right thing by having one circuit breaker in the Distribution board just for the oven only. It means you can't simply use the normal wall power point for the oven as the electric oven requires more power and if you do so, you are likely to overload the power point and cause fire.

    Do you have any plan/measurement of your kitchen? The overhead cabinets are definitely a must-have for us as we are so short of storage space before the renovation.
