    Can we hope to eliminate racism? Maybe. Are we going to get rid of general prejudice and discrimination? I doubt it - the tendency to discriminate is something that is at the core of human existence.

    One explanation of this tendency is that humans base their identity on the groups that they belong to, be it race, social class, occupation or some other facet of their existence. The long and short of it is that they need to maintain their self-esteem, and therefore attempt to develop the best possible image of the groups that they belong to. If they can't make their group look good, they make other groups look bad and tell themselves, "Thank God I'm not one of them". Since the 1950's, when Henri Tajfel first put his theory forward, this tendency has been substantiated in a fairly large volume of research.

    So what's the bottom line? Those who are prejudiced have little, in anything, good to see about themselves, so they need to reinforce their fragile egos with stereotypes and prejudices. Losers.
