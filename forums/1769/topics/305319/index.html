    Changing the carpets in the hospital wards, but not testing beforehand to see if the selected carpet is suitable for hospital wards! They ended up purchasing carpets that made it really hard to push equipment and beds and trolleys around! Heck, even the IV pole with wheels is tough to push around now!

    As a result, Occupational Health and Safety had to issue a memo saying nurses are not to push beds around now but instead, they had to train existing porters to use specially purchased motorised equipment called Gzunders, to fit it under the beds, and then push them around. Each Gzunder costs about A$1K and now, Radiology's employees are unhappy because Radiology refuses to purchase any Gzunders and the porters are having a really tough time pushing the beds around. They are already openly talking about "going on strike" to protest this. Potentially, we're looking at lots of medical claims for backaches and related 'injuries' and strains from this.

    Recently, when the ICU team came to transport my patient to the ICU, they realised that they couldn't use the Gzunder anyways because if the Gzunder is put in place, the doctor cannot stand at the head of the bed anymore, and since the Air Viva was being used to bag oxygen to the patient, that can't do!

    Nurses are also really frustrated, because we might be barred from pushing beds, but pushing the patients around on wheelchairs and commode chairs just got more difficult!! The patients in one room commented that the management is so stupid and "ridiculous" when they saw us nurses huffing and puffing trying to push a commode chair and the chair barely moved. If you have a patient of generous proportions sitting on it, you'd better pray your brute strength gets you to the bathroom on time before they stain / soil the new carpets!

    I think the impedence is due to the cross-weave pattern. Apparently, the old carpets were weaved in one direction, so there's little resistance.

    You'd think that the building planning folks running the hospital would have known better!

    My workplace is notorious for being poorly-stocked in medical supplies, but today, we have hit an all-time low!   

    - Spirit Swabs - nowhere to be seen!!!  All of us were walking around the whole ward, opening all the counter drawers, in search of spirit swabs!!!  WHICH blardy hospital does not provide spirit swabs to the wards?!    In the end, I had to ration what few I had left in my little bag that I carry slung across my shoulder! 

    - 10 ml and 20 ml syringes dangerously low in supply!  Again, nurses had to go around to the different hubs to grab a few for use! 

    - 19 G non-bevelled needles - Haven't seen them for such a long time that I'm wondering if they are a figment of my imagination. 

    - 19 G needles - Awaiting supply. 

    - Duoderm wafers - Non-existent

    - Baxter Pump drip sets - fight for the remaining few!

    - 250 mls Normal Saline bags - order up your own from the Pharmacy, and then hide them from the other nurses.  :P

    - Medicine Cups - you're lucky if you can find one.

    - Tegaderm with Pad - Dream on!

    - Normal Saline for use in Dressings - haven't seen these for 1.5 weeks liao!  Had to improvise and use the N/S for I/V!
