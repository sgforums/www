    It's really odd.

    Previously, I wanted to work overseas because I couldn't tolerate the working conditions locally and I couldn't envision the idea of myself staying put in the same job with the same organisation for longer than necessary, as stipulated by my bond.

    However, lately, I think and feel differently about this issue.

    I now feel that I can actually see myself working in my current environment and somehow, adapting to it and carving a niche for myself. I've also been wondering if I might be leaving too soon because there's just so much for me to learn in my new workplace.

    Then again, something tells me that when your primary reasons for leaving aren't so much the 'Push-Pull' factors ('Push' factors = disadvantageous signs that 'push' one towards migration; 'Pull' factors = the attractive things about the target country that 'pulls' one towards it), therefore meaning that you aren't escaping anything here nor having unrealistic expectations and hopes of the destination, but rather, they are reasons like career development and planning, it means that you are more or less ready to leave, and with the 'right' mindset.

    Would appreciate advice, comments and if you're a Singaporean who chose to work overseas, I'd really love to hear from you. Thanks in advance!

    Originally posted by Rhonda:
    Would appreciate advice, comments and if you're a Singaporean who chose to work overseas, I'd really love to hear from you. Thanks in advance!
    Just some purely random thoughts:

    Could it be that you are experiencing some "pre-departure jitters" and are trying to rationalize not going through with your emigration plans?

    If that is the case, it would seem perfectly normal. After all, you are leaving everything you know--everything that is comfortable and familiar--behind. You are leaving the proverbial "comfort zone" to go live in an environment that is initially foreign to you. It is this fear of the unknown that might be causing you to have second thoughts.

    Keep in mind that you have already researched the potential for career development in your field, and have come to the conclusion that the prospects for advancement are greater overseas. Will this scenario be any different if you elect at the last minute to remain in Singapore? Will you then spend the rest of your life in Singapore regretting that you didn't go through with your plan to emigrate?

    Besides, you're going to Australia, which is essentially just a hop, skip and jump from Singapore, so you'll be getting the best of both worlds: a better life in a country with greater personal freedoms, and proximity to your homeland.
