    Ok according to the resident expert, the fugly pike I have:

     

    “They are exported out of Peru under the name manchada pike. A rough translation of manchada is stained or speckled

    As far as I know, the only place they have been collected is Peru and curiously I have seen manchada pikes that appear to be johanna type and a saxatalis type (possibly lucius). The source of that unique color pattern is somewhat controversial - many think that it is a parasitic organism that infects the fish slowly over time and will eventually kill it. Because they are rarely collected in the wild, no one has had enough specimens to study and to the best of my knowledge there are no scientific papers on them. But I am sure that there is a facinating story involved and that it will be revealed by someone much smarter than me.

    My personal experience with them-
    I bought a manchada (johanna type) from Jeff Rapps and had it for almost a year. It was large (about 10") and had a pinkish belly (probably a female) and patches of metallic green. It also looked like someone poured black rubber on parts of it - very cool looking IMHO. I am pretty sure that it's coloration was caused by another organism (virus, parasite?), but I can't be sure. I believe my specimen could have been a female to R1's apparent male. It is known as an "Army Pike" in Japan and Europe. Probably because of the camouflage pattern.”
