    Heres one that all my girl pals get from my when I make it....tried 5 different recipies at one go,did a taste testing and this was the best one hands down....

    Ondeh Ondeh

    8 pandan leaves
    300g glutinous rice flour
    1 heaped tablespoon tapioca flour or corn flour
    pinch of salt
    285g grated coconut, mixed with a pinch of salt
    150g gula melaka, finely grated and mixed with 1 tablespoon castor sugar or brown sugar.

    Pound pandan leaves till fine. Add enough water to bring it to 210ml. Add salt. Strain and set aside.
    Boil tapioca flour with 85ml of water over low heat, stirring till transparent. Pour tapioca mixture immediately into the glutinous rice flour in a large bowl . Stir well till absorbed and gradually add in the pandan water to form a paste.
    Place paste on a smooth surface anf rub well to form a smooth dough. if dough is too soft add a little flour. Divide dough into 4 parts. Roll each part into a longish roll and cut into 12 pieces. Keep uncut dough under a piece of cloth.
    Bring a big saucepan of water to boil. Take 1 piece of cut dough and roll with the palms of hands till well rounded. Fill with oneand half teaspoons of the grated sugar in the centre.Close u the dough to form a ball. Put into boiling water. Repeat process with all the dough and boil till each ball surfaces. Keep boiling for a further 2 mins to fully dissolve sugar inside.
    Remove each ball with a tea strainer, dap strainer onto dry cloth and roll it in grated coconut. Pop Ondeh Ondeh into mouth as you are making them....happiness!!!!!!

    Another hot favourite thats super easy to make...

    Easy Tiramisu...(no cooking involved)

    (for 6)

    3 egg yolks
    1/4 cup white sugar
    2 teaspoons vanilla extract
    1 1/8 cups (210g) marscapone cheese
    24 ladysfingers (biscuits you can get from Cold Stor)
    1 1/4 cups brewed coffee + kahlua, rum ,or baileys (your choice of alcohol)
    1 tablespoon unsweetened cocoa powder + bitter chocolate shavings.

    Beat yolks with sugar and vanilla until smooth and light yellow. Fold marscapone into yolk mixture. Set aside.
    Lay ladysfingers into tray. Sprinkle coffee liquer mixture over lightly. (less is more, sparingly is best).
    Spread 1/2 the marscapone mixture over the ladysfingers. Repeat process with remaining ladysfingers and marscapone. Cover and chill, best overnight. When ready to serve, take out of fridge, sprinkle with cocoa and shaved chocolate and serve.

    Easy Peasy Nutella Chocolate Cake

    3 tablespoons butter
    3 tablespoons cocoa
    1 cup self raising flour
    1 cup sugar
    1/2 cup milk or chocolate flavoured milk
    2 large eggs
    1/2 teaspoon vanilla essence

    Preheat oven to 160deg celcius
    Melt butter. Put other ingredients into a large bowland pour the melted butter on top. Beat on high for 3 mins. Pour the mix into prepared cake tinand bake. Start checking after 25minutes with a toothpick. If toothpick is clean after removing, it is ready to be taken out. Remove cake from tin when cooled down and "ice" it with Nutella.

    (For a fancy cake for birthdays I do two cake tins and then stack them one on top of the other with a layer of nutella inbetween. Usually use one whole bottle of the large nutella bottle for this....after "icing" decorate as you please and you have a scrumptious easy peasy affordable chocolate bithday cake for serious nutella fans)

    Stir-Fry Curry Beef (Something easy and delicious to prepare)

    Ingredients

    1. Top Blade Muscle - 300g
    2. Red Onion (sliced into cubes) - 200g
    3. Garlic (chopped) - 2 tbspn
    4. Cooking Oil - 60ml
    5. Lemon Grass (Cut into 6cm and lightly smash it) - 2 nos.
    6. Meat Curry Paste (Just buy yr fav) - 100g
    7. Coconut Cream - 100ml
    8. Salt - a pinch

    Method
    1. Mix half of the curry paste & top blade muscle together
    2. In a hot frying pan, heat up the cooking oil and saute lemon grass until flavor is out
    3. Add in onion and saute until soft
    4. Add in garlic and stir till flavor is out
    5. Pour in remaining curry paste and fry for 5 mins on low heat, keep stirring.
    6. Add in seasoned beef and fry
    7. Pour in coconut cream
    8. Give it a boil and simmer beef in cream for about 3 mins
    9. Season with salt and serve hot

    Fried Potato Balls Covered with Edamame (Makes about 4 balls)

    Ingredients

    Potato Balls
    - 3 potatoes
    - 100g minced chicken
    - 2 tbsp minced onion
    - 1 tspn Soy Sauce
    - 1/2 tbsp sugar
    - 600g edamame, unshelled
    - Salt to taste (if you buy the unsalted edamame)
    - 1 1/2 egg whites (approx 3 tbsp)
    - Oil for frying

    Nijiru (cooking liquid)
    1 tspn Katsuodashi bonito concentrate
    3 cups water
    1 tspn salt
    3 tbsp Mirin
    1 tspn Light soysauce

    Method

    1. Peel potatoes, cook in nijiru until tender, then drain. Return potatoes to pan and shake pan over medium heat until all liquid evaporates.

    2. Mash potatoes whil hot and mix in 1/2 egg white, then divide into 8 equal portions and set aside.

    3. Fry chicken and onion over high heat, add soy sauce and sugar. Cool and divide into 8 equal portions and set aside.

    4. Rinse soybeans in sieve and rub to remove the fluff. Boil for 7 mins (If unsalted, boil in salt water)

    5. Shell and remove membranes from the beans. Set aside.

    6. Taking the 8 portions of mashed potatoes, form 8 small patties. Place chicken and onion mix in the center of each patty, shape into balls with diameter approx 5cm

    7. Roll balls in remaining egg white and press soybeans firmly onto the surface of the balls.

    8. Pour oil into a deep fryer with enough oil to cover the balls completely. Heat oil to medium-low temp (160 - 170 degree C) and fry the balls, taking care not to color the surface (approx 3 mins) - Meaning please don't fry till the soybeans turn brown

    9. Raise oil temp to 180 degree C for approximately 1 min then remove balls from the oil and drain.

    Serve hot. - The balls should be crisp on the outside but not too oily.
