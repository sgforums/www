    Teriyaki Cod Fish / Salmon
    Servings: 1 - 2

    Ingredients:
    - 1 cod or salmon fillet
    - Olive Oil (Optional. Pls don't use Extra Virgin as it will only spoil the flavor; also can substitute with any mild flavored cooking oil)
    - Kikoman Thick Teriyaki Sauce

    Directions:
    1. Preheat oven at 200oC on grill
    2 Wash fillet and pat dry
    3 Rub a little olive oil on the fillet, coating it evenly
    4 Place fillet on baking tray. Line it with aluminium for easier cleaning.
    5 Grill for about 5-10 mins or when fish is cooked.
    6 Transfer to plate and pour Teriyaki Sauce over
    7 Serve and eat

    my speciality which i posted before and tested by care bear

    takes patience and a lot of work but is simple to make

    braised duck

    ingredients:

    1 duck washed throughly (make it a point to totally wash out the ear-holes and mouth)
    1 packet of 5-spice powder (buy from medical hall)
    salt - amount equal to 5-spice powder
    1 bottle medium-grade dark soy sauce
    handful of sugar

    equipment:

    1 large deep wok
    1 small rack to prevent duck from contact with bottom of wok
    1 large spatula/ladle
    1 large plastic bag and container/large plate
    1 fridge

    how to do it:

    preparation-

    wash duck
    mix salt and spices together
    coat duck inside and outside thoroughly with the mixed salt/spice
    put in plastic bag and tie up, put in container or on large plate
    bung in fridge for 3 to 4 days

    cooking-
    rinse duck (not too much, just to get most of the spice off)
    heat wok (high heat)
    throw in sugar and let it caramelise and almost turn into a black treacly mess
    pour in 3/4 to whole bottle of soy sauce
    bring to boil while stirring, ensure sugar dissolves
    put in rack
    place duck in and keep using ladle to baste exposed half of duck while the lower bit stews
    turn over duck every 10 to 15 minutes, continue basting
    when duck turns golden brown (the colour of braised duck), fill wok with water until it covers duck (usually about 30 to 45 minutes)
    continue to let simmer, turning over duck every 10 to 15 minutes until duck is cooked*
    remove duck from gravy
    use gravy to stew things like boiled eggs and taupok (eggs are best stewed and submerged for up to 3 days or longer)
    other things may be cooked like duck liver and gizzard when duck is almost done

    * check if duck is cooked by piercing the thickest part of duck (usually the duck Breast) with a fork - if only clear liquid comes out, it's done...

    Chris and my fav duck receipe

    Description:
    A savoury slightly sweet roast duck. It is not the conventional traditional chinese roast duck dish.

    Ingredients:
    - 1 whole duck (I prefer to buy the frozen duck imported from Australia from Cold Storage as the meat is sweeter and finer)
    - 1 piece preserved beancurd
    - 1 1/2 tablespoon brown miso paste
    - 1/2 teaspoon sugar
    - A good generous bath of chinese rice wine (approx 6 tablespoons)
    - Sprinkle of bbq salt
    - Generous sprinkle of Cinnamon powder
    - Generous sprinkle of Nutmeg powder
    - Dash of Lea & Perrins Worcestershire Sauce
    - 1 teaspoon of seaseme oil

    Directions:
    1. Marinade duck with above ingredients. Make sure you marinade the duck evenly and ingredients are well mixed.
    2. Let the duck marinade overnight or at least for 5 hours
    3. Put duck in aluminium foil lined baking tray and cover duck with aluminium foil.
    4. Roast duck till almost cooked (about an hour or slight more) - Oven set at 200 oC
    5. Remove aluminium foil and brown the duck nicelyand evenly
    - My preference is that the duck meat is still slightly pink. Taste better this way

    Cheesy Scrambled Eggs

    Ingredients:
    2 Eggs
    About a tablespoon of milk
    Pinch of Sugar
    Butter
    Slice of Cheddar Cheese

    Method:
    1. Whisk eggs, milk and sugar together
    2. Heat up pan on low heat and add butter
    3. Wait for butter to melt
    4. Oil pan evenly and pour in egg mixture
    5. Mix them like how u scramble eggs till 3/4 cooked (or well-cooked if you like but I like them abit runny)
    6. Transfer eggs to plate and top it with slice of cheese
    7. Wait for cheese to soften and melt
    8. Eat!

    BBQ Baked Beans

    This is a recipe from an American friend. It's very popular in the South there. Enjoy! I did... tremendously!

    You'll need :

    One can of BBQ Baked Beans
    Brown Sugar
    Bacon or Bacon Bits
    BBQ Sauce

    In a skillet, lightly fry the bacon / bacon bits. Remove and soak up the excess oil from the bacon with a kitchen towel. Set aside.

    Pour the BBQ baked beans in a pan. Heat it up. When it starts to simmer, sprinkle a few tablespoonfuls of brown sugar to taste. Let it simmer for a while to allow the brown sugar to caramelise. (Oooh... caramelised sugar! )Add in some BBQ sauce, again to taste.

    Lastly, add in the bacon bits. Remove from stove and serve!

    (If you want, you could fry some vidalia onions and garlic alongside the bacon and then add them all into the pot. )

    This recipe was seen on TV last week in the Discovery Travel and Living Channel. I think the chef was Kelly? She's a Chinese in Australia. Anyways, the Fried Rice looked really yummy and it was easy to cook so here it is, reproduced from memory! And oh, she claims this is the traditional Fried Rice, without the 'new' ingredients like corn and all that.

    Ingredients:
    Cooking oil
    Eggs, beaten lightly
    Diced Garlic, Onions and a small amount of finely chopped Ginger
    Soya Sauce
    Chinese Rice Wine (Hua Diao Jio)
    Rice (cooked and left overnight)
    Bacon
    Scallions

    Heat the cooking oil in the wok. Slowly pour in the lightly beaten eggs. They'll become fluffy in the hot oil and when ready, scoop them up and set aside.

    In the same oil, throw in your garlic, onions and ginger. Fry them till fragrant and slightly brown. Add in some salt, then Chinese Wine, and Soya Sauce. Throw in the sliced bite-sized bacon to fry.

    When ready, pour in the rice. Overnight rice is the best for Fried Rice. Stir-fry. Add more soya sauce, if necessary, to taste.

    Scoop the rice out of the wok, sprinkle with the scallions. Serve.

    (Don't know how 'traditional' this dish is, especially since they use bacon!)

    His Chicken Wings

    Here's a recipe from a good friend who cooked this for me once and I just HAD to ask him for the recipe!

    You'll Need :

    Chicken Wings
    Dark Soya Sauce (I recommend the Red Dates Soya Sauce (Thick) from Tong Foong Sauce Factory, available at NTUC outlets, good for Tau Yew Bak too!)
    Five Spice Powder

    Marinade the chicken wings in a generous pool of the dark soya sauce and five spice powder mix. (Not too much five spice powder, please! They tend to turn chao tar fast when you fry the wings!) Leave in the fridge for a few hours.

    Remove from fridge. Heat up a wok of oil. When the oil is hot and ready, slowly slide the chicken wings in. Don't slide them all in at once... three at a time, so that the temperature won't drop too drastically.

    Remove and place on kitchen paper towels to absorb the extra oil. Serve whilst still hot.
