    Hi Seancannot,

    If your profile is right, then you're in your early 30's and not that old. I know many older people without even S$30K of savings.

    Depending on your lifestyle, S$30K in modern Singapore is not very much money. (For my lifestyle, its not much because if I should need to change my car, the downpayment itself would take up more than half of the S$30K).

    If I were you, I'd just keep the money in the bank. Perhaps place a portion of it in savings (for liquidity) and the remainder in some short-term fixed deposit (for better returns). I say short-term because I suspect that interest rates might go up in the next 1-2 years, so you may wanna pull it out and re-invest in the near future (unless, of course, you can find a long-term fixed deposit with flexible rates).

    "Investing" in any kind of trading/business is only for those with EXTRA cash to spare (and to lose). I wouldn't consider your life savings EXTRA cash. Such trading type "investments" are not "investments" at all - they're in reality a gamble, a two-edge sword which can cut both ways (too risky in your case).

    30 K is all my saving / no other $$$ beside 30 K
    Then it may be better to first adopt some of my suggestions in my earlier post.

    i have life insurance 50 K coverage
    We would just focus on life insurance. Purpose of Life insurance is to transfer risk (usually loss of income from your death) from your dependants (aged parents, spouse, children) to the insurer. So if you have no dependants, u dun really need insurance.

    Wat is a adequate sum? It depends on your income and your age. if you earn 30k a year and can still work for 20years u may need 600k insurance coverage. As this would ensure that if u die now (touch woord) your dependants can still afford their current lifestyle for 20 years. theoritically speaking...

    but as you grow older...you may wish to reduce your coverage provided you have catered for savings/investments. a good way to do this is using a 'layered' term insurance

    say at age 30
    you buy 1 5yr, 10yr, 15yr, 20yr, 25yr, 30yr term policy of 100k each. then just dun renew any of the policies.

    doing this at age
    30 u are covered for 600k
    35 at 500k
    40 at 400k
    45 at 300k
    50 at 200k
    55 at 100k
    60 no more coverage

    at 60 if you have proper financal planning, you should do fine living on your returns from your investment portfoilo ;p for your dependants, if children they should be able to take care of themselves soon.
