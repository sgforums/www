    I have a private financial advisor who manages my finances. I pay about 1.5k per year for his services, plus a percentage of the profits I acquire each year. He is supported by a whole team of expert analysts, so the research is much better than what I can do myself.

    I have a rather conservative profile...so bonds feature quite prominently in my portfolio. But I have quite a diversified portfolio in more than 15 different investments, covering unit trusts, money markets and equities in energy, healthcare and other sectors. He also consolidated all my life insurance policies (I had more than 5 over the years) and replaced them with a million-dollar policy. I was surprised that I saved quite a bit on premiums with this change. He aslo gave me income tax advice on tax avoidance (not evasion, which is illegal)
