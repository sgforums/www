    Originally posted by ilovemyrolex:
    I think X-men has a made a very good point.

    I think exercise need not be deliberate, just something that you enjoy doing.

    Lots of folks seem to associate exercise with hard work.

    That was an issue I discussed with TX about just last week!

    He said that he loves the physical exertion and challenge that comes with exercising. He said if there's no hard work involved, no pushing yourself to reach your limits, then it's not real exercise.

    I have a different interpretation of things. See, I'm your regular lazybones and I'm like a cat nua-ing here and there. Exercise doesn't come naturally nor easily to me. To kick-start my exercise regimen and introduce exercise as a regular feature in my life, I had to make sure it was fun and enjoyable for me. That's why I attended all those gym classes in the beginning... the music they played in the classes were wonderful! It gets you in the mood!! I loved the classes because sometimes, it felt more like dancing and grooving than pure exercise. It's exercise disguised as something fun! hehehe!

    Once I started to have fun and enjoy the sessions, the rest came naturally. I started to push myself more and before you knew it, there had been a lifestyle change!

    Different strokes for different folks, I guess! Whatever impetus we need to get us moving, the important thing is that it makes us so happy exercising that regular exercise gets incorporated into our lifestyle!
