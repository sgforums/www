    How does Singapore stack up in being a global city?

    The latest data from UBS is out:
    http://www.ubs.com/1/ShowMedia/media_overview/media_global/mediareleases?contentId=34588&name=PL_2003_e_m.pdf

    (Note: This latest report is published once every 3 years with the data sampled in 2003).

    As a global city,
    Singapore is less expensive than: New York, London, LA, Paris, Dublin, Tokyo, Berlin, Amsterdam, Zurich
    Singapore is more expensive than: Sydney.

    Wages:
    Singaporeans working in Singapore earn less than their counterparts in:
    New York, London, LA, Paris, Dublin, Tokyo, Berlin, Amsterdam, Zurich, Sydney.

    Purchasing power of typical basket of goods:
    Singaporeans buy less things compared with people staying in:
    New York, London, LA, Paris, Dublin, Tokyo, Berlin, Amsterdam, Zurich, Sydney.

    To buy a Big Mac in Singapore, it takes 22 minutes of work.
    It takes less time in working hours to buy a Big Mac for people in:
    New York, London, LA, Paris, Dublin, Tokyo, Berlin, Amsterdam, Zurich, Sydney.

    Expenditure on goods+services:

    Singaporeans spend less compared with workers in:
    New York, London, LA, Paris, Dublin, Tokyo, Berlin, Amsterdam, Zurich

    With the exception of : Sydney

    Food prices:

    Singaporeans spend less for food compared with workers in:
    New York, LA, Paris, Dublin, Tokyo, Zurich

    With the exception of : Sydney, Berlin, London, Amsterdam

    Apartment rents:

    Singaporeans spend less in rent than workers in:
    New York, Tokyo, London

    But spend more than in:
    LA, Paris, Dublin, Berlin, Amsterdam, Zurich, Sydney.

    Public Transport:

    Singapore is cheaper than:
    New York, London, LA, Paris, Dublin, Tokyo, Berlin, Amsterdam, Zurich, Sydney.

    Private Car:

    It's cheaper to own a car in Singapore than in: nowhere else!
    Singapore is the MOST expensive city to own a car. period.

    Cost of services:

    Singapore is cheaper than New York, London, LA, Paris, Dublin, Tokyo, Berlin, Amsterdam, Zurich, Sydney.

    Gross and nett hourly pay:

    Singapore workers earn less pay than New York, London, LA, Paris, Dublin, Tokyo, Berlin, Amsterdam, Zurich, Sydney.

    Working hours

    Singapore workers work longer hours than those in:
    New York, London, LA, Paris, Dublin, Tokyo, Berlin, Amsterdam, Zurich, Sydney.

    Taxes and social security

    Singapore workers pay less than those in:
    New York, London, LA, Paris, Dublin, Tokyo, Berlin, Amsterdam, Zurich, Sydney.

    Income disparity

    The gap between the rich and the poor in Singapore is bigger than in:
    New York, London, LA, Dublin, Tokyo, Berlin, Amsterdam, Zurich, Sydney.

    Except in: Paris [/b][/url]
