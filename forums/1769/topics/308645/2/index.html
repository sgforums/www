    What we're discussing in this thread here, is the fact that luxury fashion items may be priced that way for maximum profits, not because of quality nor craftsmanship any longer.  If you would read the very first post with the article exposing the practices, you will then realise that that sets the entire premise for this discussion. 

    Whether one chooses to spend one's money on luxury fashion items or not, is not our business.   It is their money to spend as they wish.  The goal here, is to expose the unfair and unethical practices of some of these luxury fashion companies and thus, enable us to make informed decisions when we make purchases.

    And btw, please distinguish between luxury FASHION products, and luxury cars and houses... all are ostentatious goods, but they are not quite the same.  As stated in the article, if you'd read it, high-end fashion houses may no longer be giving you the quality you thought you paid for.  Instead, you are simply paying grotesquely inflated prices for products that do not really warrant that price.  However, for luxury cars, you are paying for quality finishes, for leather seats, and hopefully, for a more powerful engine, top-of-the-line safety features, etc etc etc.  And for luxury houses, you are paying for the price of the land, the quality finishes, the interior design, the architect's fees, etc etc etc.
