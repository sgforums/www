    oh.. simple interest is when interest is calculated on the principle only.. which means the initial amount... compound interest is calculated on accrued amount which is the principle and the interest accrued...

    for example...

    simple interest (yearly) of 10% in a fixed deposit

    principle = $100
    1 year later, $100 + 10% of $100 = $110
    2 years later, $110 + 10% of $100 = $120

    compound interest (yearly) of 10% in a fixed deposit

    principle = $100
    1 year later, $100 + 10% of $100 = $110
    2 years later, $110 + 10% of $110 = $121
