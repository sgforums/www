    Ok....pictures will follow later. This is an overdue post.

     

    On Sat, 5 of us (Me, LF, 7-11, Fatum & VK) went to JB to eat the "infamous" duck that induced a nosebleed from VK. It was an impromotu last min thing as 7-11 was busy and had a craving for it.

    And me despite my sprained back, decided to hobble along since I have nothing better to do at home anyway :(

    I shall leave out the shopping bit but we got lost trying to find the place. LOL It was a day where we spent lots of time driving around in circles but I have to say it's worth it

    The "restaurant" was this bungalow with a few tables. There is this shed with a giant pit on the floor covered with soil. I think the pit for cooking the duck is 5' x 8' in size

    The "Begger" Duck
    Silky soft flesh, moist and juicy from being slowly baked in the ground. The juice is solid but if you're too 'heaty' or unwell...I don't think you can eat it because all 5 of us got "high" from the herbs used! It's really THAT potent!!!

    Btw, all of us agreed that 1 duck is not enough!!!

    Stir-Fried Sweet Potato Leaves
    We all agreed that it would be better with belachan. The leaves were a tad old but it was very well stir-fried. There was a 'wok' flavour to the leaves.

    Fried Tung Hoong
    Nicely fried with good wok flavour. It's very tasty but then again I might be bias coz I love to eat Tung Hoong

    Melon Soup
    The kind I like where the melon is hollowed and steamed for the soup. It's really pure melon essence!!!

    And I forgot to take pics of the Melon Soup.....but here is a picture of the Achar we ate as a starter :P

    Btw I think the next time we go again we need to try the pork stomach version!!

    Damage: RM 90+ (They forgot to charge us for the beers too)

    This is not the end of it. Right after dinner we walked to the Malay eatery (kinda like a food court) to feast on ice-kachang and satay
