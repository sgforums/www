    If you have conducted your research on real estate purchasing and investment, there are various techniques and strategies to leverage resources to make your home purchase.     No I do not have any grand inheritance.

    I'll give you a plan to work on so as you can get into your first home.

    Nurse A makes around 75000 a year (+1 makes 75000 per year)
    Nurse B makes around 75000 a year (+1 makes 75000 per year)

    You need to do your full calulations on it. If you are unsure, drop me a message, I'll send you a spreadsheet.

    Nurse A & +1 buys a brand new house (investment property) for $450,000
    Nurse B & +1 buys a brand new house (investment property) for $450,000 next door to Nurse A & +1

    Interest rates is around 9%

    Nurse A & +1 rents Nurse B & +1 house for $1
    Nurse B & +1 rents Nurse A & +1 house for $1

    Nurse A & +1 installs Aircon and other stuff due to Nurse B's request.
    Nurse B & +1 installs Aircon and other stuff due to Nurse A's request.

    Basic tax laws.
    Because both your (Nurse A & B) houses are investment property, you are able to claim the following.
    1) Interest rates for the loan.
    2) property depreciation
    3) Cost of any installation. Aircon & stuff
    4) depreciation of the stuff in your property (curtains, aircon)
    5) Cost of all repairs

    You will be claiming a fair bit from the gov. This is known as negative gearing. This technique will work if both (Nurse A & +1) are earning good income.
