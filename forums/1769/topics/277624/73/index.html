    Samsung TVs and video products look good in the showroom and do deliver outstanding picture quality for a reasonable price, I'll admit.

    Where they utterly fail is in delivering long-term reliability. Back in the dark pre-LCD days, I purchased a Samsung CRT monitor for my PC on the basis of its image quality and competitive price. That monitor failed within a week (one of the three electron guns went bust), and I returned it to the store for an even-up exchange. The identical replacement unit worked fine for about a month until it started to emit a whining sound and eventually could not be switched on. That unit went back to the store, which was nice enough to replace it with a more expensive Viewsonic monitor at no cost to me.

    That was my first and last experiece with a Samsung consumer product.

    Samsung products are consistently rated at or near the bottom for reliability in surveys carried out by consumer publications like Consumer Reports.
