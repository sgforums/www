    shinta, if it's dust that has accummulated in the spaces between the keys, you can use either vacuum or get a can of compressed air.

    If it's yucky gunk like spilled coffee or dropped food (yucks!), remove the keys gently, and wipe with a damp cloth or use one of those wet tissues for household cleaning, or just a damp paper towel (although the lousier grades will disintegrate).

    If it's not THAT dirty but you wanna clean between the keys, I use cotton buds that I dip in spirit, or just water, and drag the cotton buds between the keys.

    Sometimes, I ka-pok a few satchets of spirit swabs from work and wipe over the tops of the keys if the PC's off.

    If I have to use a PC at work for public use and the keyboard looks grimy and greasy and resembles a playground and red-light district breeding area for germs and what-not, I'll put on disposable gloves, grab a Mediwipe and swipe quickly over the keyboard first.
