    When you start a Body Pump class, you have to be thick-skinned and go with 'baby weights' in the beginning. Don't feel embarrassed that the weights you're carrying are puny compared to those around you... they are 'lao jiao's and they started the same way you did too!

    When I started Body Pump, I told myself, 'Take it easy! Get the techniques right first or else, you'll sustain serious injuries!' True enough, if you do not practice the right posture, you WILL get knee / back / shoulder injuries. I did the squats and lunges the wrong way and kena major leg pain for days afterwards until a good instructor taught us how to get that done right and then, no more excruciating pain!

    I've seen folks who just carry the rod with NO WEIGHTS at all at the ends. That's wise of them.

    We're not in it for the competition so there's nothing to be embarrassed about. We measure ourselves against our own yardsticks and don't be too harsh on yourselves cause we all start somewhere.

    We may not be able to complete the entire class the first time round, or we may be carrying 'baby weights' for weeks, but at least, we've gotten ourselves off the couch and have started exercising!

    Be your own cheerleader! Hooray for every little milestone! I reward myself by purchasing yet another Reebok sports shirt to exercise in and look good at the same time!
