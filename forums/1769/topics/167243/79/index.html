    Originally posted by choco B:
    Do different colored cats have certain typical characteristics or personalities
    Yes, gingers tend to be males 90% of the time, tri-coloureds are 100% female...same for tortiseshell.

    If you see cats with very broad faces and jaw - these are mostly unsterilised and territorial males who roam around to mark their territory and mate with females (just like in the big/wild cats kingdom). These can be fierce so forget about calling them.

    Male cats tend to mark territory with their urine (dunno how they got so much ever-ready stock), even after they're sterilised.

    Cats with tipped ears (ie a small portion cut off) means they have been sterilised by volunteers cat carers.

    White cats with mixed colour eyes (one blue, one yellow) tends to go deaf in older years, same for totally white cats with blue eyes.
