The Shelf Life of Beauty Products

    Here's a very interesting article on how long you ought to keep your beauty products for!

    Ladies, pay heed!


    "From eye shadow you bought last year to the minty lotion you got two birthdays ago, your vanity is likely to be full of items you think are too nice to throw away but that you rarely use.

    While some cosmetics last up to three years, others - like that eye shadow or mascara - must be replaced every few months. "Preparations can break down, making them less effective and putting them at greater risk of contamination," says Irwin Palefsky, Snr Vice President of Cosmetech Labaoratories, a consulting firm that develops products for beauty companies.

    Even though most cosmetics contain protective preservatives, it's important to know how ingredients affect such products. Those high in water content provide a breeding ground for microorganisms. Preservative-free products are easily containated (as is eye makeup, which has limited preservatives). And items containing lots of oil can go rancid if exposed to air often.

    To keep track of beauty products, label each with the date of purchase or receipt. Then go through your supplies every couple of months, and do a look, feel and smell test. Does it look cloudy? Has the colour or texture changed? Is it bulging at the lid? Is it separating or difficult to spread? Does it have a strange smell? Let the answers, and the timeline and tips in the chart that follows, guide you.


    From : Martha Stewart's Living, 2002?
    Written by : Patricia J O'Conner

    SKIN CARE PRODUCTS

    MOISTURIZER : 6-12 months
    Keep jar lids tight to prevent from drying out; always clean hands before using. If product separates, becomes liquidy, or smells strange, discard it!

    TONER : 6-12 months
    Look for cloudiness or a foul odour.

    CLEANSER : 6-12 months
    Choose tubes or pumps, which help prevent contamination from microorganisms.

    EXFOLIANTS : 6-12 months
    Keep cap tightly closed to prevent water from entering in the shower. Oil-based scrubs (like many of the sea-salt ones) last longer than water-based forumulas.

    EYE CREAM : 6-12 months
    Never share eye products. Look for tubes instead of jars. Apply above eyelid, not on eyelid itself.

    LIP BALM : 1-2 years
    Don't leave in the car; it can go rancid if exposed to heat and air for prolonged periods.

    HAIR PRODUCTS

    SHAMPOO : 6-12 months
    Store above shower head so water doesn't get in. If it changes in smell or appearance, throw it out.

    CONDITIONER : 6-12 months
    As with shampoo, watch for changes in consistency and odour.

    STYLING PRODUCTS : 1 year or more
    High-alcohol (20% or more) gels and hairsprays can last many years. Close caps and lids tightly to prevent formula from drying out.

    NAIL POLISH

    1 year
    Keep caps on tightly; store at room temperature. Don't add remover to bottle to thin.

    MAKEUP

    FOUNDATION AND CONCEALER : 6 months
    Use sponges or brushes (not fingers) when applying so you don't contaminate with microorganisms from your skin.

    EYE AND LIP PENCILS : 3-6 months, 6-12 months for lips
    Sharpen pencil before each use to clean off surface. Discard if rancid odour develops.

    LIPSTICK : 2-3 years
    TO prevent rancidity, store at room temperature and not in the car or refrigerator.
    (Beading does not indicate rancidity; it's merely oils escaping from the formula.)

    MASCARA : 3-6 months
    Keep cap on tightly to prevent from drying out. Never put wand down on counter or tabletop. Don't add water if it gets thick.

    POWDERS (FACE, BLUSH or EYES) : 3-6 months for eyes, 6-12 months others
    Wash applicators monthly; use water (not saliva) if you use wet powder. Dry before storing.

    APPLICATORS (BRUSHES, SPONGES) : 3-6 months or longer
    Wash sponges used for liquid makeup with soap and water; dry. Replace those used with powders every few months. Cleaned regularly, brushes can last for years; use mild soap and a little water (do not immerse).

    FRAGRANCE

    3 years or longer
    Reduce headspace in bottle to preserve scent (that is, if you've used half, pour what remains into a smaller bottle); store in the refrigerator.




    SUN PRODUCTS

    SUNSCREEN : 2 years
    If it separates or doesn't spread well, replace it. At the beach, keep in a beach bag, out of direct sunlight.

    SELF-TANNER : 3-6 months
    Don't stock up. The self-tanning ingredient, dihydroxyacetone, oxidizes within a few months, turns brown, and can take on an unpleasant odour.
