    One thing I learned a long time ago: never, ever, send your car through a car wash on frigid winter days. Sure, the car wash facility and water are heated, but whatever water isn't blown off during the drying phase, freezes on your drive back. In other words, water in the keyholes, locks, and around the door frames freeze, meaning you can't open the doors until the water melts. I had this happen to me a long time ago, after I came back from washing the car on a particulary cold winter day. The doors were frozen shut and the windows wouldn't roll down, so I had to exit the vehicle by folding down the rear seatback, crawling into the trunk, and pushing the trunklid open (which, fortunately, wasn't frozen shut).
