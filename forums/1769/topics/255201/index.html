    the 80s were more than just UK synth-pop and the New Romantics.. in the US, there were rock bands.. none were bigger than Guns N' Roses then.. their Appetite for Destruction album had to be the greatest debut album of all time.. it spawned a slew of classics, which are still amazing 20 years later!

    Their arena rocker:

    Paradise City - Guns N' Roses

    Their anthem to down and dirty urban decay of LA:

    Welcome To The Jungle - Guns N' Roses

    They had their melodic side in their sweet melodic ballad:

    Sweet Child O' Mine - Guns N' Roses

    From their EP Lies, another tender melodic ballad:

    Patience - Guns N' Roses

    After these, came the excesses which caused them to fire their drummer, Steven Adler. He was replaced by Matt Sorum of The Cult.

    Shortly after Use Your Illusion was released, guitarist Izzy Stradlin left and was replaced by Gilby Clarke.

    The band disintegrated after releasing an album of their influences, The Spaghetti Incident.

    Now, the world awaits Axl Rose's incarnation of GN'R with Chinese Democracy which has been delayed for close to a decade. The other members have gone their own way, with Stradlin having limited success with his Juju Hounds. Slash played guitars for many others including Michael Jackson. He's also had limited success with his own band, Slash's Snakepit but is now with a band some termed "Stone Temple Roses" as Slash, Sorum and McKagan are with Stone Temple Pilots frontman Scott Weiland and guitarist Dave Krushner is now Velvet Revolver. Their album 'Contraband' is highly recommended.
