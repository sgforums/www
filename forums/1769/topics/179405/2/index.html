    Two scenes...

    1) Wandering around Bath in UK, it was 6 pm, I was alone, cold, and broke. Found a burger king and splurged on a hot chocolate to warm my freezing hands, but decided to walk and drink it at the same time. It was a quiet night, hardly anyone on the streets, those streets on stone tiled during Roman times. I was looking at the stone streets when suddenly I heard a bell toll and looked up. And I saw a chapel beautifully lit in a soft white light and the sky, a deep blue behind. And I remember being stunned by the entire moment, bells ringing, an almost empty street, a glowing white chapel in front of me and it was as if time stood still. It's a magic moment and until now, I will never forget it.

    2) Sunset at Sri Lanka, Negombo beach. In my entire life, I have never seen a sunset as glorious as this one. Was standing there next to my husband, the beach was crowded with people. There were no seats for them, all of them in groups, in pairs, alone just standing there looking at the sun set ahead. Have you ever seen a completely empty sea? No vessels, no islands, just a sea with roaring waves stretch out to the horizon and how it slowly turns colour as the sun turn bloody red and turned the entire sea and sky red. And the entire beach became silent, literally, the crowd stopped their conversations as if to respect the sacred moment. The children who were playing soccer sat down in a row to look at the sunset... it was like the beginning of the world, or the end, the moment the sun sinks below the horizon and the sky turns a deeep blue. and then ALL of a sudden, it is like you can breath again, and the noise returns.
