    Oh, and one really really strange thing I encountered... a few weeks back, I woke up one morning, put on my contact lenses, and realised that, lo and behold!... I couldn't see well!!

    So, I thought I had placed the L lenses in my R eye and vice versa, so I switched, but still, cannot see clearly!

    So, I re-switched again, thinking that maybe it's one of those flukes. I still had blurred vision!

    OK, deep breaths, opened up a new pack of disposable contact lenses cause maybe the old one's really old liao! Biangz, the world still looks blur to me! So I switched sides again thinking that maybe I got them wrong again although they were clearly labelled L and R. Still can't see!

    PANIC!!!!

    Up till today, a few weeks later, I'm still stumbling around with blurred vision. I have to squint a lot and sometimes, squinting doesn't help either.

    One of my friends suggested that maybe, an incident at work that happened recently just shocked me so much, the stress affected my eyesight! She reasoned that if one can be shocked till one's head of hair turns white overnight, why can't the eyesight get afflicted?

    Don't know if there's any medical truth to it or not but I'm going to ask the next friendly doctor I see. Too bad the docs here are all busy and most of them aren't exactly approachable.

    Weird, right?
