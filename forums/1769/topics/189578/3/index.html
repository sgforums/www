    Not just bipolar disorder... depression too.

    Bipolar Disorder just made international news in 2003, with the suicide of a world-famous 3-star Michelin Chef, Bernard Loiseau. In his autobiography written by Rudolph Chelminski, a famous food-writer and Bernard's friend.

    Upon hindsight, Louiseau's wife, staff, friends, and even guests of his hotel, La Cote d'Or, had noticed peculiarities in his behaviour and moods months before his suicide, however, no one really knew what he was suffering from, and the only person who suspected Bipolar Disorder, a Psychologist who stayed the weekend in his hotel, didn't pursue the matter with Loiseau.

    People afflicted with Bipolar Disorder bear a heavy burden. They literally wear themselves down with sheer exhaustion.

    Loiseau was initially put on medication for depression, but the medication made him 'slow' and blur, and he couldn't afford to be like that when he's working, and so he stopped taking his medication.

    In the end, overwhelmed by the tides of misery brought on by an aggravation of Bipolar Disorder, he ended his life at home.
