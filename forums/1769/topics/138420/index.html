    I've heard a few folks complain that they're experiencing a dry, irritated throat recently. When sbst275 asked for advice on what meds to take for his irritating cough, I thought it might be helpful to include some basic info on cough syrups in here.

    Please, the advice here does NOT replace a sensible visit to the doc! It's just for general knowledge!

    Cough syrups can generally be categorised in two broad categories : EXPECTORANTS or COUGH SUPPRESSANTS.

    EXPECTORANTS
    If you have a lot of mucous clogging up your breathing airway, expectorants will help to thin the mucous and make it easier to cough out. Basically, expectorants are given for coughs that sound very 'wet'. The main expectorant used is guaifenisin, available under many brand names.

    There are other ways to loosen and clear the respiratory secretions associated with colds. These include using a humidifier and drinking six to eight glasses of water a day.

    Side effects are not common, and may include vomiting, diarrhea, stomach upset, headache, skin rash, and hives.

    COUGH SUPPRESSANTS
    If you have a 'dry' and irritating cough that won't seem to stop, a cough suppressant will provide relief because it will help lessen the coughing episodes. It helps to suppress those cough episodes so you'll be able to sleep better at night. The active ingredient in most cough suppressants is dextromethorphan, available under many brand names.

    You may experience the side effects of drowsiness, nausea, vomiting, stomach upset, and dizziness.


    If you have a cough that lasts more than seven days, or are associated with fever, rash, sore throat, or lasting headache, you should pay a visit to the GP!

    Take care and good health to all!

    Cuz, in the olden days, the folks here didn't know that there were cough suppressants available! So instead of taking meds that would ease them and put a stop to the hacking cough, they just bore with it.

    Why '100 days'?

    A mixture of agak-agak-ology and exaggeration. Can you imagine a Hokkien conversation going like this...

    *Man is with his friend. Suddenly, man starts coughing like he's going to turn blue and die...

    "Wah! Di eh ga sow ah nee jia lat! Wu jia yo bo?" (Wah! Your cough is so serious! Have you taken your medication?)

    "Jia yo bo eng lah! Wah ga sow ah nee ku liao, simi yo poon bo eng! Wah ga di gong... wah ga sow zi bak jit liao!!" (Eat medicine no use! I have been coughing for so long, no medicine will be of use! I tell you... I've been coughing for ... 100 days already!!)

    "Wah! Ah ni lee hai! Wu lai xiong (littlestream's favourite word! hahaha!) bo?"
    (Wah! So long already! Have internal injury or not?)

    So, that was probably how the '100-day cough' thing came about.
