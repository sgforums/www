    Ever got a slight itching in your throat when you eat durians ? ..... a slight buring, tickling sensation at the back of your mouth ? ....

    That, according to an aunty who loves her durians so much she mounts early morning expeditions to bukit timah nature reserve for "organic" durians during the season, is caused by heavy pesticide use at the thai and malaysian durian plantations .....

    she says that they are now using so much agricultural pesticide around the plantations the squirrel population there has mostly disappeared ...

    apparently, one way to tell how much pesticide was sprayed on the durians, is to look at the number of flies buzzing around the durian stall .... durians have a very strong, pungent smell as we all know, and flies should rightly be attracted to them .... if you don't see any flies buzzing around the durians, forget it, it's not worth the health risk .... if even flies wouldn't want to have anything to do with the durians, you better not too ....

    The above test seems to hold true as well ... we got a crop of bukit timah "organic" durians recently, together with a few store bought ones .... left a shell outside side by side in the garden .... flies and bugs came only to the "organic" one .....

    and you don't get that slight, itching, burning sensation at the back of your throat when you slurp into a soft, yellow organic "golden pillow" at all .....

    watch out for the flies next time you buy durians ...
