    Originally posted by JLennon:
    what i understand is that lack of water will lead to water retention. Is that true? if that's the case excessive drinking should not lead to water retention?
    When the body senses a drop in fluid levels, it will try to retain water by eliminating less fluids (less urine).

    However, as water is needed constantly to replenish fluids lost via sweat and other metabolic and cellular processes, it's not enough to just try to keep whatever fluid there is, within the body, cause that will eventually be used up and depleted.

    And nope, this isn't what's meant by 'water retention' in the pure sense of the term. At least, not in the way the term is most commonly used.
