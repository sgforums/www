    World Harmony Run



    The World Harmony Run is a global relay, seeking to strengthen international friendship and understanding. As a symbol of harmony, runners carry a flaming torch, passing it from hand to hand amongst members of thousands of local communities, on a route spanning 70 nations.

    This is a true grassroots event. Each relay team is made up of runners from all walks of life, dedicating their time to serve a cause they believe in. The teams visit schools, community groups, and running clubs, as well as local and national government organizations. There they celebrate the goal of world harmony, and recognize individuals working for harmony within their own community.

    The World Harmony Run does not seek to raise money or highlight any political cause, but simply to create goodwill among peoples of all nations, while transcending political and cultural boundaries.

Sri Chinmoy is internationally renowned for his numerous initiatives which have united people from many cultures and walks of life to work for a more fulfilling world. An athlete, philosopher, artist and poet, Sri Chinmoy, now in his seventies, has dedicated his life to advancing the ideals of world harmony and oneness.

    WORLD HARMONY RUN IN SINGAPORE
    Changing The World Together

    June 18, 2005 7.30am MacRitchie Reservoir

    In the early morning of June 18 2005, the Singapore leg of the World Harmony will be held at the MacRitchie Reservoir. Participants from all walks of life will come together to celebrate the ideal of World Harmony in this torch relay. They will run a route of 4.8km around the forest of MacRitchie Reservoir before passing a flaming Harmony Torch to another core team of runners who will begin a road run covering a distance of 30 km. Along the route, the runners will visit various communities such as the Ceylon Sports Club, Civil Service Club, Joo Chiat Community Club and other religious organizations to share a moment of inspiration with them. During which, the flaming Harmony Torch will be passed amongst the members of these communities as each person who hold the torch make a wish for a more harmonious world.

    Towards the end of the Run, the runners will visit the historical Merlion Park with flaming torch to remember the ideal of a progressive Lion City through harmony.

    This event is special and significant because it strengthens our national ideals of International friendship, multi-racial and religious harmony.
