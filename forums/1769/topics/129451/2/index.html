    Warning : My thought of the day is kinda depressing. So if you dont wanna spoil your day, your night, your life, whatever, do not read on.

    I was just browsing through my friendster and came upon my best friend profile. Looking at it brings back a lot of nostalgic feelings. He wasnt just my best friend and only real friend, but an ex boyfriend whom I had much cherished in the older days.

    Looking at his photos with his friends and girlfriend reminds me of how much I have lost. Do not mistake me for I have long regarded him as a friend and no longer harboured any romantic notions of him, though that path had been long and hard.

    It was just that once he and I shared a world so close and bonded that when it was so suddenly severed I lost myself in the subsequent years. I may not have found myself back yet but I am trying hard. Everyday.

    That period of time I had lost a large number of friends. Whom I am trying to find the courage to apologise for taking off like that. I admit I have a problem with taking flight rather than confront problems with relationships and friendships. But lately, I realised running away aint going to help, especially when I realised I missed those people whom I had lost so much.

    I treasured friendships alot. Especially when I know true friendships are rare and hard to find. Yet I find myself daily battling with a cowardly response of running away. I have no idea why but talking about issues like these confuses me much. And in confusion I sought safety in distance. I knew that probably aint the right thing to do but I still persist in doing it. I do not know why.

    And looking at those photos reminds me once again that I have not sought these friends out and bridge the gap between us. I know that most of these people had moved on with their lives and I am reluctant to seek them out and cast a shadow across their bright lives with my presence and I do not know if I would ever run away again. I especially do not want to find them only to lose them yet again. Thus this constant inner turmoil about what to do.

    Sigh...I guess that was a long thought of the day...
