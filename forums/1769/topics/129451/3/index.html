    Ferret, reading your post reminded me of some situations in my life too.

    I've lost a few close friends through 'natural attrition' too. When my electronic phonebook conked out once, taking along with it to the grave all my friends' contact no.s, I was beside myself! I was mourning over the loss of all the contact no.s - how was I going to call up my friends?!

    And then I realised something... if they missed me and were thinking of me too, they would call me. Which a handful did, the rest never did.

    I guess friends are like those little paper boats with candles inside that the Thai's put out in the river during one of their festivals... some of the boats would float out into the ocean and never return, other paper boats would always float pretty close to shore and remain within your range of vision always.
    As we go on in life, inevitably, we keep some, we lose some. Not to forget, there will always be new paper boats that'll come and berth in our docks, be it for a short while only or for a long, long time.

    I've learnt to look back and smile at the happy memories. I no longer question why or think about the what-could-have-been's. Friends come, friends go, and there will always be that special few who'll stay on and make their home in our hearts.
