    For me, I like to reflect and be introspective when I'm travelling. It's even better now for me now because I don't take SBS Transit buses much now. It was TV MObile that took away much of my introspection period.

    Anyway my thought of the day:

    When we were young, our parents spent much effort on training us to sleep alone. Much coaxing, threatening, and rewarding were done to achieve this. When we reached the teenage age, we practically felt that we were invincible. After some time, when the need to find love hits us in the 20s, we start to crave for intimacy.     My thought of the day :

    Sometimes, one makes long-term plans and commits to making sacrifices for the next few years to achieve it and when the goal comes close to fruition, there is such a sense of calm, peace and contentment and pride in oneself.

    It's never easy to commit oneself to a goal that will take years to achieve. It calls for discipline, a lotta lotta sacrifice, painful lifestyle changes and more... and sometimes, you feel less-than-human because of your circumstances. You feel like you're trudging on through the endless days, when the hours just drag on by, slowly...

    That's when close friends make all the difference.

    Close friends can be buddies you've known for years, or pals you've just known recently but who are on the same wavelength as you. Real friends soften the pain of your struggles by just being there for you, offering a listening ear and advice when necessary.

    At the end of it, you'll look back and realise what was really precious to you, and who were real pals who stood by you through your trials and who were your cheerleaders in your little victories and milestones. You'll realise that a lot of times, you weren't really yourself as you were burnt-out, depressed, or you've momentarily lost sight of your self-worth and confidence, but your true friends never abandon you just because you became 'boring company' or weren't in an uppity mood. Precious gems.

    Victory might be long in coming... but it nevertheless, remains sweet, and sweeter still, if the toil was long-drawn and great.
