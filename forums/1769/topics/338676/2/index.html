    I first got certified in hantu .... in 1998 ....

    people claim there's nothing to see there, but back in the pre-land reclaimation days, and before hordes of irresponsible instructors let their rampaging students demolish the corals to rubble, there were a lot of things to see there .... huge heads of anemones with clownfishes, groupers underneath the ledges, patrolling batfishes, a bamboo shark, and the clowning glory, a sea turtle !!!!!! ......(the famous 3 legged one ....), seen it all at hantu before

    and I dove the hantu/sudong wreck quite a few times before, good place for wreck dive training, not haunted like the sister's island one ...

    I would say, if you can get certified at hantu, you can dive everywhere ... the vis is sometimes pretty bad, and the currents, a little out from the ledge can be disorientating, it's the best place to train fundamentals like navigation (VERY VERY VERY important, a sadly neglected skill nowadays)

    and when you finally go for a leisure dive at a place with great vis ... you'll appreciate things so much more ...
