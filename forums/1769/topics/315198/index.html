    Did you notice, like I did, that milk seems to be a tad more diluted these days?  My favourite brand of Skim Choc Milk does seem to become more and more watery, compared to its creamier texture when I first bought the milk about two years ago. (I'm lactose-intolerant, btw, but I seem to have slowly re-conditioned my body to accept skim chocolate milk and other dairy-based products without suffering for it later!  )

    Here's why our milk seems to be more diluted and watered down :

     

     

    The Secret Ingredient in Milk We Don't Know About

    As a child we were told to always drink lots of milk to make sure our bodies would grow tall and strong.

    But little did we know that much of the milk on our supermarket shelves have been modified with a secret ingredient the milk industry doesn't want us to know about.

    The mystery substance the major companies are adding is called permeate.

    The cheaper substance can make up as much as 12 percent of milk depending on the brand.

    So what exactly is permeate?

    "It's a by-product that's made during the manufacture of dairy products. They actually make skim milk out of it," farmer Paul Weir said.

    "But what the major companies are doing is adding this stuff back into full cream milk. The effect is to dilute the milk and make more of it. And you and I have no idea if we're drinking it."

    So which two companies admit to using permeate?

    National Foods- which make Pura Milk and Dairy Farmers.

    Both companies also make the generic brand milk for both supermarket chains.

    Milk from the farm costs processors about 50 cents a litre, while permeate costs only half that amount - just 18 cents a litre.
