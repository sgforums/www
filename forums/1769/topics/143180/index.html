    For discussion's sake: My hubby and I were going out, walking towards the bus-stop when a very thin woman approached us, asking for help. According to her, she lives in AMK, and was supposed to meet her friend there (at Bishan) to take money from her friend so that she can buy her asthma inhaler refill (or something like that, she was holder the inhaler thingy). But her friend didn't turn up, so she wants us to give her $10 to buy the inhaler. I asked her where her clinic is, thinking that her clinic is nearby, so that we can all go to the clinic and buy the thing for her, and I can pay $10 to the clinic directly. But she claimed that she visits a Dr Lim in Toa Payoh, and when I asked her which block at TP, she got angry and said in Mandarin: "you want to help, you help lah!" and walked away, before we could speak further.

    I wonder: should I have given her the benefit of the doubt and just spare her $10? Was it wrong for me to ask her those couple of questions, just to be safe she's not pulling a fast one? She does look like she's not well, and could not stand upright properly. What if she was indeed sick and needed help urgently?

    It's kind of like those old folks or visually-handicapped folks going around selling tissue paper. I sometimes find it difficult to refuse them, knowing that it's difficult for folks like them to find paid employment to survive. After all, $1 is nothing much to many of us, but the thing is there are so many of them, that I meet at least one of them selling tissue everyday!

    What would you do in both situations?
