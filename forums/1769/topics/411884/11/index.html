    He got it wrong with the ice.

    Ice is important in the early stages of swelling. That is when you really need to constrict the blood vessels to reduce the buildup of fluid that causes the swelling.

    Ice will only be detrimental in the long run, since long-run vasoconstriction will reduce the flow of nutrients and oxygen-rich blood to the injury site. Basically, you should stop using ice once the swelling has gone down.

    A few minutes of having fewer nutrients is far less detrimental than having the injured area swell to kingdom come.
