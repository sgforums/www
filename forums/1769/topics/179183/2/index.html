    Let's start with salmon done Japanese style!

    SALMON DENGAKU (SAKE NO DENGAKU)
    (Serves 4)

    Ingredients :
    4 serving size salmon steaks

    For Dengaku Miso Topping :
    60g shiro miso
    1 T mirin (yum! yum!! )
    1 T dashi stock (Boil 4 Cups of water with 30g Kelp and 1 Cup of Dried Bonito Flakes)
    1 egg yolk

    Instructions :
    1. Broil or bake salmon steaks. In a saucepan, mix miso topping ingredients except egg yolk and place over hot water.

    2. Add egg yolk and mix thoroughly until smooth and glossy.

    3. Baste broiled salmon steaks and broil until the topping turns light brown.

    Note : Feel free to substitute the salmon fillets with red snapper, scallops, or other fish fillet.

    salt, pepper, olive oil

    herbs, soysauce

    vodka, tomatoes

    Hey Lau,

    Something you might want to try.

    Salmon with a hint of lime juice, fresh chopped chillies, some honey, soy(or fish sauce) and some lime leaves. If you want to make it creamy, add some coconut. If you want it fresh and zingy, leave it without the coconut.

    About a table spoon of lime juice or more, depending on your pref for zingy.

    Depending on how hot you want it, one or two chopped chilli padi.

    2-3 tablespoons of honey

    2-3 tablespoons of soy sauce

    depending on your pref about the lime leaves.

    Depending on how many you are cooking for, adjust the quantities to suit. THis should suit about 2 fillets of salmon.

    You can do this dish a couple of ways:

    1. is to cook the sauce and keep it aside, then pan fry the salmon which you coat with a hint of salt and pepper. Then pour the sauce over.

    2. Marinade the salmon with the sauce and leave over night preferably, but at least 2 hours. Then grill it wrapped in some banana leaf and add a dash of coconut over later and the remains of the "sauce"

    Oh, try and get fillets with the skin still on.

    Happy cooking!
