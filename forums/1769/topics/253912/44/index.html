    May 23, 2007
    Where's the fizz in soft drink ad campaign?

    RAG-AND-BONE folks have taken the fizz out of a $200,000 soft drink advertising campaign just days after it was launched at 450 bus stops across the country.

    They were drawn to the 100 aluminium cans which framed every poster panel - ripping them off to make a quick buck.

    At some bus stops, the cans lining one side of the panels were completely removed.

    The ads for the soft drinks, 'Anything' & 'Whatever' went up on Friday and were supposed to run for a month.

    'Anything' is a carbonated drink while 'Whatever' is non-carbonated. Each comes in six flavours.

    But by Tuesday night, Out Of The Box, the soft drink company, was scrambling to take down the cans.

    Said managing director Johnson Tan: 'My friends told me they saw aunties who collect scrap aluminium ripping the cans off the panels.

    'We did consider that the cans may be damaged or removed, but we didn't expect 100 per cent damage.'
