    I tried the yuki yaki before.. in 2005... at cineleisure branch b4 it closed down

    Things may have changed since then..

    One thing to note.. the cineleisure branch that I went to have seats of up to 6 pax. I am not sure if the marina branch have bigger tables/seats..

    Below was my comments. Cost should be outdated????

    ------------

    Cost: Adults $19.90 nett. + $2 (Drink, which must be ordered) and +$2 if you want the ice-cream. Pay at the counter b4 proceeding into the restaurant. If you paid w/o ice-cream and then later decide to have ice-cream, it'll cost you an additional $7.

    Food:
    Meats: Variety of marinated chicken, Pork, Beef
    Seafood: Salmon, Prawn, Sotong, Dory fish, Lala
    Yong Tao Fu ingredients: Variety of fishballs, tofu, fish tofu, etc etc..
    Vege: only Cabbage and chinese lettuce
    Sushi conveyor belt: Only some variety of sushi.
    Drinks: Seasons Lemon Tea, F&N Zappel, Soda Water, Plain water, Diet Coke, Guave juice, Orange Juice, Lime Juice

    There is a teppanyaki pan and a steamboat on the table. Seats comfortably for 6 ppl. Food are taken from the fridge at the side of the restaurant.

    Personally, I think the food is quite fresh and the marination of the food is quite tasty. Not much msg (as I did not feel very thirsty after dinner).

    Ice-cream
    After you finish your meal, the waiters will come and clear away the teppanyaki pan. Switch of a button, water will come out and wash away the oil, residue. Waiters then wipe dry the plate using kitchen towels. They will turn the teppanayki plate temperature to cold. Take the ice-cream mixes from the fridge, pour in and wait for it to freeze. This takes only a couple of minutes.

    Flavours of ice-cream available
    Chocolate, Strawberry, Tiramisu, Green Tea, Honeydew, Green Apple, Rum n Raisin, Mango, Mango Sorbet, Orange sorbet, Almond, Coffee, Lime (and a couple more I think.. cannot really remember)
