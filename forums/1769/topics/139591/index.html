    defensive riding i know, driving... ehhhhh i learnt a little only.

    1) Basics, keep your safety distance.
    2) I know it doesn't sound really logical but prepare for the unexpected.
    3) If your wheel locks when you brake to hard, release and depress the brakes again.
    4) You're not a drifter, so when you jam your brakes and start sliding on wet ground you'll slide in that direction, nothing can stop you. Turning the wheel like Initial D isn't going to help much since its wet ground.

    Then again when I went through the defensive riding course, we were told to put our bikes into a slide and maintain our balance. The instructors there said to us, no slide no cert.
