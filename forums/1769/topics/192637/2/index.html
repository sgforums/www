    Here in the Minnesota, electricity consumption usually peaks at the height of summer when most air conditioners are in operation. Due to capacity shortfalls and equipment malfunctions in the power grid, the utility company occasionally has problems meeting the electricity demand; power outages and "brownouts" (when the delivered voltage falls well below 110 volts) are the result. In fact, we just encountered the first blackout of the season last night: our residential neighbourhood lost power for about 4 hours, beginning at around 10:15 p.m.

    In order to help "stabilize" the demand, the utility company offers a 10% rebate on your residential electricity bill if you agree to install a cutoff switch on your a/c unit. This switch is operated by the utility company, and they use it to switch off your a/c unit during periods of peak demand to help reduce the strain on the power grid. There is no charge to have the switch installed, and by law, the utility company can not deactivate the a/c unit for more than 10 minutes per hour.
