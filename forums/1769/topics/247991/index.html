    What you know is related by your colleague to you, you didn't hear the boss scolding her first hand?

    The tone of his voice matters. Also there is a chance for words to be twisted out of context.

    Is there someone in the hierarchy between you and your GM? If there is, it is more his place to speak to the GM.

    If there isn't, and you report straight to your GM, you can still do something.

    Find a time to talk to him in private, like in a "by-the-way, just to keep you updated" manner, tell him that your colleague has been feeling very down , depressed, lost appetite, etc. ever since he scolded her using such harsh words.     Don't expect an apology, don't push for any admission from him, but hopefully he will tone down in future. Do not be confrontational.

    If I were insulted, I'd immediately say, "I may have screwed up and I apologize for that, but there is no need to call me names and get personal".
