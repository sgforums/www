    No big surprise. Renault was always known to be weaker than McLaren on high-speed circuits like Silverstone - in fact, Alonso's pole position was a surprise to everyone, Alonso included. Not that it matters - with more than half the season gone, Alonso still leads Raikonnen by a healthy 26 points for the WDC. Juan-Lap Wonder is not a threat to the championship - his win just kept Alonso from extending his lead by another 2 points.

    From performances over the last two years, Alonso is easily the more deserving of the championship. While Raikonnen seems only able to lead from the front, Alonso guns it all the time to make sure he's in a position to take advantage of anything that happens ahead of him.

    As an engine supplier, Renault have won championships, but the WCC has always eluded Renault as a team. Hopefully they'll ratain their 15-point lead over McLaren and get their first WCC too. I've been a McLaren fan since 1988, but the way they've been screwing up lately (somebody should explain to Ron Dennis that it's two races per engine, not two engines per race), it'll be a travesty if they win.

    In any case, at least I get to see someone knock Michael Scummaker off his perch before he retires. [/i]
