  • Wanda
    1 Aug 07, 23:05
    If you don't want to spend on expensive down jackets, you can get / borrow a cheaper polyester one (make sure it's long enough to go over your hips) and wear at least 4 layers: - thermal wear, long sleeve t-shirt/knitwear, fleece sweater and the polyester winter jacket. These are must-haves in addition to the above: wool socks, leather / Thinsulate gloves, polyester/wool scarf over the neck/chin+mouth. These were the minimum that I wore when I went up to the swiss alps, but it also depend on your tolerance level (of cold).

    Don't buy those woven gloves with lobangs, as they don't work at all, get leather gloves (about $40) or Thinsulate gloves which are available from about $10-$18, depending on sales. If possible, spend a bit more on better quality material which will really do its work of keeping you warm. Hope this helps.

    ps: I forgot to add - a wool/polyester cap is also a must have, as we lose lots of heat from the head.

    The idea of keeping yourself warm is not to let outside wind/air blow inside your clothes, and not to let your body warmth flow out.

    So underwear in one important part, find those thermal underwear that when you wear it, it should be quite tight fit, and on bottom of the pants and at the end of sleeve, it should have elastic part closing.

    Outer part, get those jackets filled with feather, in China they call "Yu Rong Fu", i dont know how to call it in English.

    just remember on the sleeve, and bottom of pants, it should be "closed" by elastic part, so that wind cant blow in. Bottle neck is good too. And get a headwear and protect your head from the cold winds too. Hoods on jacket is no good, becos it will "collect" the wind from the front direction.

    If that place snows, i believe many place will be slippery(bcos those snow will melt and frozen again to become ice on floor), so make sure your shoes can walk on slippery surface.
