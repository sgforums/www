    I feel that it is a romantic, sentimental notion, a kind of self-delusional mechanism, in a way, to think that if we truly love someone, we'd be happy to see that person happy with someone else. It's a self-sacrificial concept popularised by a lot of love songs, esp Mandarin ones.

    I think we're deceiving ourselves if we don't mourn over our loss first, before proceeding to that stage. Before you reach that stage where you can be so generous with your love, there will be months upon months of heartache, pain, tears, wrenching heartache, moving around feeling as if there's no meaning in a lot of things you used to do together now that you're apart, etc etc. You don't reach that stage this early in a breakup.

    Eventually, we'll realise that yes, we might still love the person, but the person has chosen to move on and not to spend the rest of his/her life with us, so we have no choice, but to let our love go free too. And yes, sooner or later, we'll have this quiet acceptance whereby we'll be able to bless the one we loved with whoever he/she chooses to lead his/her life with.

    But again, that's not before months of gut-wrenching agony.

    Don't speed up the healing process... go with the flow, cry and wail if you must, but don't shut yourself out from your friends and the rest of the world.
    Because, for all you know, the one who's really meant for you might just be round the corner, and I'm sure you wouldn't want to shut him/her out!
