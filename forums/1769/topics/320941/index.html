    I just did this morning, and I thoroughly enjoyed it! 

    WARNING : Don't proceed if you haven't watched it yet!  Spoilers ahead!!

     

     

     

     

     

     

     

    I started crying buckets when Carrie was abandoned by Mr Big on their Big Day.  It was so heartfelt!  It was also very real... a groom, twice-married previously, who had massive cold feet because his bride turned into some kind of Bridezilla because of the wedding, and overwhelmed by fear, he drove away.  And then he saw her, and realised the massive implication of his action, and regretted being so silly, and decided to turn back but it was too late... 

    I felt that the entire scene was expertly handled.  It wasn't too sentimental, wasn't too draggy, and it broke my heart! 

     

     

    Overall, I really love how close the girls were.  One thing struck me though - they seem to never be in any sort of financial need!  How lucky! 

    I felt it was odd that my girlfriend's husband loved the movie until I realised how well they portrayed the wedding from the guy's point of view.  All Mr Big wanted was Carrie, but Carrie wanted 75 guests, which eventually turned into 200!!    This reminds me of an old thread we had in here about wedding dinners and such. 

    Did you like the movie too?
