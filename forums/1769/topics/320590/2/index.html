    No matter what I do, I STILL have dreams / nightmares every night.    The encouraging thing is, the nightmares are no longer vivid ones that scare the bejesus out of me. 

    I was feeling tired in the middle of a 12-hour shift when I overheard a few colleagues talking about their bedtimes and one of them, in her early 20's, said that she heads to bed at about 8-8:30pm every night and sleeps well before waking up for her 12-hour shift!   

    That made me re-think about my own sleep habits and made me realise that hell, I've been suffering from a sleep deficit for years now!  I keep sleeping less and less at all manner of odd hours, and I think over the years, it catches up on you. 

    Maybe, all the nightmares and dreams are nothing more than a disturbed mind due to perpetual bad-quality and not-enough sleep! 

    So, I'm going to adjust my bedtime now.  Factor in 1.5 hrs of pre-sleep reading or daydreaming in bed, then sleep for at least seven hours, ideally, eight.  Sometimes, due to work rostering, not quite possible, because if you work two 12-hour shifts in a row, all you can do is work, come home, shower, sleep, and then back to work again.
