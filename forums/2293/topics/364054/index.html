    Sale Sensation

    I-S turns the spotlight on our local fashion insiders to find what’s hot this Great Singapore Sale. By Zaki Jufri. Photography by Richard Lee and Tetuko Hanggoro Prasetyowibowo

    The Great Singapore Sale (GSS) is upon us again, and who better than our very own style icons to let us in on the best deals on the hottest threads this season. Here, five style icons show us their picks from the hottest boutiques and high street stores that are participating in this year’s GSS.

    The Shoo-In

The spunky looker is one of the people responsible for the highly touted Belvedere Beat series of parties that was held here last month. She’s also a favorite with magazine editors who occasionally feature her in their stories for her bold sense of style. “Beauty is in the details, and it’s worth spending to achieve that, especially on accessories like shoes and bags,” she tell us. “If you buy from high-street labels like River Island, make that outfit truly yours by accessorizing it so that you stand out from the crowd and not be afraid of bumping into someone wearing that same dress or top.”

    Shop Savvy
    Here are some tips to make your outings a success and probably save you a pretty penny.

    1) Do Your Research
    Doing your research on your favourite stores can help you save that bit while still keeping in line with the latest trends. Do familiarize yourself with the sales period for the different stores.
    —Being a VIP member for certain stores lets you be the first to know when sales start. Club 21 has regular fashion news and sales preview mailer, simply sign up to be the first to be in the know.
    —Keep your eye out for newspaper advertisements for sales from the bigger stores.
    —It is always wise to check for credit card privileges before going on a shopping spree.

    2) Set a Shopping Budget
    Before hitting town, create a realistic shopping budget. Know how much you can splurge after factoring out your bills. Decide how much you want to set aside and always stick to what you need rather than what you want.

    3) Go for Classics
    Always remember the rule of thumb: Fads fade, but class is eternal. Choose colours that would never go out of season. Creams and dark shades are classic choices, but for that extra edge, inject one or two pieces with bold prints or electric colours.

    4) Get the Right Fit
    Select cuts that would flatter you. Opt for pieces that highlight your silhouette. An embellished belt would help define your waistline while giving a kick to an otherwise drab tunic top.—Andrea Lin
