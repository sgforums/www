    Origins’ recycling initiative

    Nothing wasted, everything can be reused. The idea isn’t new, but Origins are now allying it with their own ambitions.

    The brand - part of the Estée Lauder group - will be launching a vast recycling project in the USA, starting with the empty cosmetics containers brought in by their clients.

    John Delfausse, vice president responsible for packaging across all the group’s American brands, explained, “the majority of recycling companies only collect the most obvious items, such as soda or water bottles . Cosmetic products and toiletries’ tubes, pots and bottles are often left out and rarely recycled.”

    With the “Return to Origins” recycling program the brand will be taking empty containers from all cosmetic products, whatever the brand.

    From March 29 the 450 Origins boutiques in American as well concessions in department stores, except Nordstrom, will be collecting empty containers to send to a recycling site to be sorted. What can be recycled will be reintroduced into the production cycle and the rest converted into energy. This is an ambitious project which could well spread to other countries.

    By Jonathan Fulwell (Source: Emilie-Alice Fabrizi)

    Environmentally friendly design ; it’s in the bag

    Remember, ecological packaging was, not so long ago, only a minor genre in the design world, or worse an uncreative area where only talking of recycling had currency. These prejudices should by and large be put to rest from now on after the exposition ‘Eco’Pack Attitude’ organised by the Designpack gallery in Paris: a playful voyage to the heart of eco-design attacking our prejudices head on.


    Exhibits from the exposition Eco'Pack attitude

    The life-cycle of materials is highlighted in their innovative use in hand-made items and works of art, the more “eco-sensible” can even be made at home. Eco’Pack Attitude is showcasing a wide-view of the issues. Metals, plastics, glass and other everyday materials are on the menu, from their origin to their recycling. This will be a huge building-site of creation for designers who are betting on ecodesign taking off.

    The exposition is has also been designed to apply the brakes to a large number of our supposed priorities; all the more important for a modern consumer society with near saturation of packaging. Discover the uncensored show from 9-24 May on the Rue de Richelieu in Paris.

    By Jonathan Fulwell (Source: Emilie-Alice Fabrizi)

    Can't decide which handbag to buy? Rent it first

    SINGAPORE (AFP) - A Louis Vuitton bag, diamond-inlaid guitar, Gucci shades and a dog. Total cost? Less than 1,000 Singapore dollars (654 US). The catch is, you're only renting. 

     

    Model from the new "Panama Flight Bag" collection by Louis Vuitton


    As consumers scrimp to survive the worst global economic crisis since the Great Depression of the 1930s, Singaporeans are increasingly turning to Internet-based rental outlets to at least maintain their sense of style, business operators say.  

    "Just like how local coffee shops can expect better business than restaurants in times of economic turmoil, I believe a guitar rental service would be much appreciated in these times," said Elaine Khoo, owner of Rent-A-Guitar.

    Her online company offers electric and classical guitars of various makes as well as amplifiers and guitar accessories for just one dollar per item a day -- with a deposit, of course.

    Although not yet able to provide figures, Khoo said she has been "pleasantly surprised" by the response to her service, and said the economic crisis partly inspired her to set up her business in February.

    "I see the economic gloom as an opportunity for me to promote a service in which customers pay a smaller amount to rent the guitar rather than buy," Khoo said.

    Another rental company that says it is doing well despite the gloomy economy is thatbagiwant.com, which allows trendy customers to rent luxury handbags and sunglasses at prices vastly lower than their retail cost.

    "Because of the recession, business has gotten better," said owner Tan Ho Ching, adding that fashion-conscious customers unsure about buying costly bags are now embracing his service to save money.

    The customer base for thatbagiwant.com more than doubled in the three months after Singapore, in October, became the first Asian economy to enter recession.

    "People who were sitting on the fence are now trying it out, preserving their capital," he said.

    Dog-sharing service Easy Dogz has also seen more customers in recent weeks, according to owner Herbert Lim.

    The service allows clients to take home breeds including golden retrievers and Jack Russells after paying rent of 30-50 dollars a day on top of a 100-dollar membership fee.

    The company has not been adversely affected by the economic downturn, said Lim, and have even received a boost after running ads in a local newspaper.

    More conventional rental services also say business continues to be brisk.

    DVD rental firm Homepal Private Limited said more people were signing up, with chief executive officer Eric Tham estimating a 10 percent rise in membership over the six months to February.

    Homepal allows customers to rent DVDs over the Internet and delivers the movies to their homes by post. Members pay a monthly subscription fee and are allowed to keep the DVDs for as long as they want.

    "There's more people staying at home. This is a form of entertainment that is affordable," said Tham.

    Games rental company Red Sands Gamers Club also said it membership was up by more than 50 percent since Christmas, and owner Jeffire Yang said this was due to customers' cost-cutting measures during the holiday period.

    "I spoke to my customers. They said that they bought (games) consoles instead of travelling overseas (to save money)" which led to increased membership for Red Sand's games rental services, Yang said.

    Membership allows members to enjoy discounts on goods and services offered at Red Sands.

    Even one of the major players in the local telecommunications market has rolled out a mobile phone plan that is similar to a rental scheme.

    MobileOne's Take3 programme allows customers to own new mobile phones at significantly lower prices after subscribing to any of its service plans.

    Customers have the option of swapping their phone for another after nine months for a fee, or do the swap at no cost after 20 months.

    Under the programme, an LG KF900 Prada II phone would cost 201 Singapore dollars per month, against a purchase price of 1,288 dollars.

    "They get to enjoy the use of the latest handsets without paying upfront cost. it is likely to be especially welcome in the current economic crisis," said Geraldine Lai, a spokeswoman for MobileOne.

    It's different for Singaporeans with such deep pockets they can still rent an entire island -- and keep it all to themselves.

    Indonesian island resort Pulau Pangkil Kecil, accessible by boat from Singapore, can be rented for varying rates depending on the season -- from 2,600 Singapore dollars per night for one to ten people on a normal weekday to 4,500 dollars on selected public holidays when bookings are tight.

    The cost covers all meals and use of the island's facilities, which include nine luxury accommodations, a bar, swimming pool, speedboats and more than 20 staff, according to the island's website.

    "It's the same as last year, which was one of our busiest years -- people still see us as very good value for money," said a company spokesman.by Philip Lim

    Avril Lavigne launches perfume

    After her clothing line the Canadian is getting ready to launch her first fragrance. The perfume, christened Black Star, is the result of a partnership with the marketing giant Procter & Gamble and comes complete with a low price tag to help win over Avril’s fans and the younger members of the public in general.


    “Young consumers love her, she has a very special relationship with the public,” explained Heike Hindenlang, director of global marketing for prestige products and womenswear at Procter & Gamble. He added, “adolescents like to try new things, they like to buy smaller bottles of perfume rather than large ones”.

    That is why the smallest version of Black Star will be sold in 15ml bottles at €16, but it will also be available in 30, 50 and 100ml at €24, €33 an €49 respectively.

    In pink and black packaging (the favourite colours of the singer) the “fresh, young and edgy” Black Star presents itself as an olfactory representation of Avril Lavigne. The perfume will be released in Europe in July and will be followed by body lotion, deodorant and shower gel.

    ¥15.6 million Hello Kitty figurine to celebrate 35 year anniversary

    2009-04-01

    AFP - The Japanese group Sanrio announced on Thursday March 26 the creation of a small figurine of its star figure “Hello Kitty”, covered in diamonds, topazes, sapphires and other precious gems, it’s a unique doll worth ¥15.6 million (€125,000). 
     


    Photo : Sebastien Bozon/AFP


    Presented on Thursday March 26 in Switzerland at the watch and jewellery exhibition “Baselworld 2009”, this exclusive item – designed to recognise 35 years for the world famous pink kitten – was made in collaboration with the Austrian frim Swarovski, a crystal and precious stone specialist.

    The statuette (10.5cm tall and weighing 569g), called “Super Hello Kitty Jewel Doll”, is adorned with 1 diamond, 1,939 white topazes, 403 pink sapphires and 1 citrine (a type of yellow quartz).

    How, where and when this curiosity will be sold has not yet been established, confirmed Sanrio in a press release.

    Amongst the innumerable fans of “Kitty-chan” some, rich or fascinated, won’t hesitate to pay a small fortune for this luxurious representation of the cute kitten, a symbol of sweetness and peace adored by many generations of young girls.

    A Japanese buyer has previously paid ¥10million (€80,000 at the time) for another “Hello Kitty” jeweled piece a few years ago.

    The blank-faced animal was born in 1974, and since then Kitty has become the cash-cow of Sanrio. Its image decorates thousands of different items (clothes, fashion accessories, electronic equipment, nick-nacks etc) and is sold in around 70 countries.

    Kitty is also, like Mickey for Disney, the figurehead of a larger group of acolytes and the star of two theme parks in Japan.

    She was officially named “Ambassador for the Japanese Ministry of Tourism” in 2008 to tour China in a bid to attract visitors to Japan.

    Director’s Cut

    April 3rd, 2009

    I-S explores film’s fashion moments. By Zaki Jufri

    From the day James Dean popularized the t-shirt in A Rebel Without a Cause to Diane Keaton’s androgynous Annie Hall, movies have always inspired fashion and style. How many times have you walked out of the cinema thinking: “I want to look like that!”

    While glossy fashion magazines have always been the source of choice for fashionistas, it is on the silver screen that identities are shaped and imitated. It is not just the clothes that people covet but the whole package itself. “Fashion and film have always had a symbiotic relationship with each other,” says fashion stylist Furqan Saini. “Designers (and people like us, for that matter) on the hunt for the new or old and rarefied have always turned to the celluloid closet for inspiration,” he adds.

    In the now famous scene from American Gigolo, for instance, the suave and debonair Julian Kay (played by the equally suave Richard Gere) throws one jacket, shirt and tie after another on to his bed as he mulls over the most stylish combo. Look a little closer and you will see Kay’s wardrobe full of creations by the Italian fashion maven Giorgio Armani, and it has become fashion history that Armani’s career is inextricably linked to this ‘80s film noir classic. Kay’s overt display of vanity together with Armani’s elegant threads sparked a new trend of masculinity that had never been experienced before—a precursor to today’s metrosexual.

    So what’s your favorite film for fashion inspiration? What film changed the way you dress? We suss out some classic looks, and how can you adopt them.

    Confessions of a Shopaholic

    Step aside Carrie Fisher, here comes Rebecca. This latest fashion romp has critics dubbing it the new Sex & The City. But of course! SATC’s costumier Patricia Field was roped in to style Confessions. The movie’s titular character Rebecca Bloomwood’s style, according to Field is a mix of “bold, colorful, energetic, voluminous and eclectic.” And this is very much exemplified by the use of bold print prints and loud colors with vintage-inspired pieces thrown in for variety.

    Polka dot top ($79) and pink belted skirt ($99) from River Island.

    Jacket ($890) by Juicy Couture.

    Bag ($730) and shoes ($560) from Kate Spade.

    Flashdance

    When Jennifer Beals put on her torn t-shirt and leg warmers in Flashdance (1983), she launched a fashion revolution. The title song, “What a Feeling,” remains one of the best-loved pop songs of all time spawning fans who secretly long to whip out their tights and slide across a shiny audition room floor. Flashdance helped bring dancewear into mainstream fashion, in particular leg-warmers, leggings and oversized tops, and was a precursor to the ‘80s revival. The movie’s biggest sole contribution to fashion will be the baggy off-shoulder top which has inspired brands like adidas Originals and Stella McCartney.

    Maroon top ($89), black halter neck top (price unavailable) and hi-top sneakers ($149), all from adidas Originals.

    Denim cut-offs ($90) and leggings ($39) from River Island. 

    Trainspotting

    This 1997 cult classic ushered in a new wave of pop culture hysteria in the UK. Renton, Spud, Sick Boy and the rest of the gang showed the world how to dress like a rock star in “heroin chic” as it was termed back then. And so, this became the IT look in the mid ‘90s, with fashion magazines like i-D and The Face running editorials featuring gaunt and skinny models in equally skinny clothes. Judging by all the gaunt indie rockers and kids out there, this look is still very much in vogue. And who could forget the runway shows of designer Hedi Slimane?

    Printed t-shirt by April 77 Records ($79) from Queen’s Couture.

    Leather jacket by Surface 2 Air ($1,980) from Asylum.

    Black jeans by Chronicles of Never ($399) from Asylum.

    Boots by Dr. Martens ($199) from Front Row.

    Pulp Fiction

    Quentin Tarantino’s 1994 seminal hit is a fine example of hip simplicity. John Travolta and Samuel L. Jackson’s dapper gangster look was instantly appropriated by the fashion set who liked its simplistic coolness and timelessness (think agnès B, Helmut Lang and Calvin Klein).

    White shirt ($425), jacket ($1290), pants ($635), tie ($195) and shoes ($465), all from agnès B Homme.

    Naomi Campbell hits out at racism in fashion world: report

    BERLIN, April 3, 2009 (AFP) - British supermodel Naomi Campbell lashed out Friday April 3 at what she sees as latent racism in the fashion industry, which she said gives preference to "blond, blue-eyed models" over black women.

     

    Naomi Campbell - Photo : AFP


    "You know, the American president may be black, but as a black woman, I am still an exception in this business. I always have to work harder to be treated equally," she said in a Glamour interview appearing Monday April 6 in Germany.

    She added: "In the past, there were more opportunities for black models but the trend towards blond women has again become extreme. In magazines, on the catwalk, I see blond, blue-eyed models everywhere."

    In 2007, the London-born Campbell, along with fellow models Liya Kebede and Tyson Beckford, launched a campaign against what they said was discrimination in the fashion world.

    SKorea recalls cosmetics contaminated with asbestos

    SEOUL, April 7, 2009 (AFP) - A South Korean safety watchdog said Tuesday 7 April it has banned a local cosmetics company from selling five products which used raw materials contaminated with cancer-causing asbestos.


    The products from L'Ocean cosmetics company including face powder and make-up base were being taken off the shelves, the Korea Food and Drug Administration said.

    The products were made using talcum powder imported from China, which was found to have been contaminated with asbestos, it said in a statement.

    The agency last Thursday announced that it had found asbestos in baby powder made by eight manufacturers. It banned their sale and ordered a recall.

    Asbestos can cause lung cancer when inhaled.

    Fresh Finds

    Whether you’re a confirmed label junkie or a vintage fan, you’ll have something to look forward to with the latest host of fashion labels making their debut here. By Baey Shi Chen

    ELOHIM

    The lowdown: Whoever said that local fashion is uninspiring has probably not heard of Sabrina Goh. A graduate of Singapore’s LASALLE-SIA College of the Arts and a finalist in the Singapore Young Designer Contest in 2006 and 2007, the Singapore-based Malaysian designer worked for local fashion brands and SINO London before striking out on her own with ELOHIM, which is—interestingly—Hebrew for “God of Creativity.” Goh describes her own style as “androgynous, architectural and artistic,” and designs for strong, confident individuals.

    What’s in store: Get nostalgic with its standout Spring/Summer ‘09 collection. Derived from the designer’s affinity with Lego creator Ole Kirk Christiansen’s philosophy of “I put together,” this collection consists of well-structured and minimal-inspired pieces that involve interlocking Lego blocks with different configurations of felt to form abstract graphics and embellishments on skirts and mini dresses. Cool graphic tops are also paired with leggings to create looks of edgy nonchalance.        

    Who it’s for: Cool fashion mavens who like a strong conceptual approach to their clothes. Available from Black Market and Hide and Seek.

     

    Juicy Couture

    The lowdown: Tais-tais swear by its candy colored velour and terry cloth tracksuits. This Californian creator of couture sportswear recently opened a store here and launched its Spring/Summer ‘09 collection. Known for their definitive take on relaxed Californian glamour, the design duo of Gela Nash-Taylor and Pamela Skaist-Levy has created a series of playful, feminine looks for the hot months ahead. Inspired by quintessential summer locales such as the Capri and Malibu, the collection is colorful and exuberant. Think postcard views and ice cream in picturesque cafés while you zip through cobble-stoned streets on a Vespa.

    What’s in store: Look out for embroidered peasant blouses and floral-print dresses paired with trenches in zesty brights for carefree elegance. Worn with gladiator flats, they add an edgy touch. There is also a hint of girly mischief in the Lolita-esque polka dots and cherry prints that are reminiscent of screen sirens Brigitte Bardot and Sophia Loren. And who could forget its signature tracksuit? All done up in the season’s hottest colors with a glamorous twist with crystal embellishments and tongue-in-cheek slogans like “Live For Sugar.”

    Who it’s for: Fashionistas looking to add some boho breeziness to their summer looks and arty types who want to stand out from the crowd with a hint of luxe. Available from Juicy Couture.

     

    Mystic Vintage

    The lowdown: With a shared love for the arts, cooking, indie music and vintage eyewear, the founders of homegrown label Mystic Vintage trawled the world to put together their one-of-a-kind collection of bold and stylish glasses for fellow fans. The brand’s cerebral-cool factor is evident in the trio’s quirky references—the Mystic Vintage photo shoot was inspired by Philip K. Dick’s sci-fi novel Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep? What’s more, with whimsical names such as “Gentle Giant,” “Slow Dance” and “Laura Femme,” you’ll add an extra dose of character to your look with these frames.

    What’s in store: Comprising specially handpicked pieces from fashion brands such as Christian Dior, Lanvin, Dunhill, YSL and also iconic optical brands like Rodenstock, Silhouette and Metzler, you can be assured of quality and great design.

    Who it’s for: Soulful nerds who aspire towards the studied cool of pop icons such as Andy Warhol, John Lennon and Jarvis Cocker, and glamour pusses looking for a dash of kooky charm. Available from Black Market and Blackjack.

     

    NOM*D

    The lowdown: We were surprised to find out that this exciting and thought-provoking fashion label hailed from the unlikeliest of places, New Zealand. Unlike its flashier neighbor Australia which has produced cult brands like Ksubi, Sass & Bide and Chronicles of Never, New Zealand is known more for mainstream fashion. The brainchild of Margarita Robertson, NOM*D’s Summer ‘09 collection is nothing short of irreverent. Aptly called “Insiders,” it is made up of unique materials and vintage finds reworked into new designs.

    What’s in store: Pieces that look original and fresh just as they make a tongue-in-cheek reference to history and established classics. Key looks include bold stripes and houndstooth linings paired with dresses that have been chopped, seamed, upended and zipped together from matronly nylon petticoats to create a slouchy and thoroughly modern silhouette. The signature print on dresses this season features a pattern derived from a vintage men’s scarf. Similarly, traditional gentlemen’s jackets are cropped and shorn, and waistcoats are doubled over and attached to shirts with zips on show. Who knew that old classics could be so versatile?

    Who it’s for: Savvy dressers with a wry sense of humour and a penchant for daring and edgy design won’t be disappointed.

    Available from Blackjack.

     

    Petar Petrov

    The lowdown: Since his days as a costumer for advertising campaigns and music videos before studying fashion at the University of Applied Arts in Vienna, Bulgarian designer Petar Petrov has been intrigued by cultural differences. He is also drawn to simplicity of form rather than ideological concepts. Focused on the play of contrasts, his latest collection is a reflection of this aesthetic with its striking hi-lo combination of understated elegance mixed with street influences.                                           

    What’s in store: Having launched his first womenswear collection in 2007 following the success of his menswear, Petrov is a relative newcomer on the womenswear scene. However, Petrov has managed to create a collection that exudes purity of form and stylish sophistication without appearing overly polished. Look out for highly wearable and modern styles created from a simple color palette, such as loose-fitting dresses with clean lines paired with skinny jeans for effortless cool, and also bolder looks such as an unexpectedly sexy playsuit.

    Who it’s for: Fashion fans who favor a minimalist look with a hint of the avant-garde. Available from Blackjack. -

    Keeping It in the Family

    The clothes that cool kids wear. By Zaki Jufri

    Today’s fashion-conscious moms and pops think of their kids as extensions of themselves and so want their kids to look cool like them. Like us adults, kids want (or so we think) something much cooler than the staple kiddie print tees and pink flowery dresses. We give you a lowdown on what’s hot for tots this spring.

    Du Pareil Au Même (DPAM)

    One of the more recent additions to the new wave of chic French wear for tots, Du Pareil Au Même has been making creative French designs for kids since 1986. Each season, DPAM produces a timeless and classic collection (usually 850 styles in all) with fabrics that are a mix of straight design, cute and humorous prints, lovely details and functional, comfortable cuts. DPAM recently launched its Spring/Summer 2009 Collection—look out for nature inspired prints, ethnic embroidery and rock-star inspired denim that will surely make your kid stand out from the crowd.

    Price point: From $34.90 for a t-shirt to $64.90 for a pair of jeans.

    Available at #03-22 Raffles City Shopping Centre, 252 North Bridge Rd., 6338-6510

    GapKids

    Like its adult counterpart, the spring collection for GapKids looks set to be a bright one. In classic Gap fashion, the casual “Americana” concept is translated into its children’s apparel but with a contemporary twist—swathes of colours in bright and fresh hues with hints of spring’s vibrant optimism. For the boys, it’s all sporty—wide and bright stripes with lots of rugby details. As far as the girl’s collection goes, it looks to us as if it’s going to be blooming with nonchalantly cool florals, kooky prints, flowing skirts and wide leg bottoms. Details are aplenty—floral embroidery on light tunics and A-line dresses; big bold rugby stripes on jerseys and polos—while bottoms are kept hemmed and cuffed to complete the look.

    Price point: From $39 for a polo T-shirt to $59 for a pair of jeans.

    Available at Gap, #01-18/23 Wisma Atria, 435 Orchard Rd., 6732-0438 

    Guess Kids

    Although it is known more for its oh-so-sexy jeans and flirtatious tops for ladies, its trendsetting Guess Kids line have made it a hot ticket item for soccer moms everywhere. For its current collection, Paul Marciano (Guess’ creative director) went back to the Summer of Love for inspiration. Bohemian-inspired patio dresses and loose fitting tunics in bright colors set the stage for a whimsical wardrobe, while dresses, skirts and jeans are patchwork and floral embroidered denim pieces. The boy’s collection has a clean palette with pops of bright yellow and orange as well as the incorporation of all shades of blue. T-shirts with vintage graphic prints and peace sign insignias are seen throughout the assortment of burnout tees. The denim collection features heavy stitching, high contrast blasting, leather trim and detailed embroidery.

    Price point: From $45.90 for a T-shirt to $65.90 for a dress.

    Available at Guess Kids, #01-22 Forum The Shopping Mall, 583 Orchard Rd., 6734-3957 

    Petit Bateau

    Say bonjour to the grand-daddy of très chic kiddie fashion. This century old cult children’s wear brand is known for its simple chic. Meaning “Little Boat” in French, the brand caters to kids, from babies to tweens, and has loungers for adults. This season, Petit Bateau reinvents the classical nautical stripes style—kitted, printed, straight like a brush stroke and even out of control—to form wild tartans in vivid colours like orange, cherry red, peony pink, apple green, mint and even bright purple. Mummies can deck out their girls in comfortable cotton jerseys and skirts, colourful polka-dot blouses, cute nautical style dresses, tops and tights, while the boys will look adorable in dungarees, fun printed tees and casual pants or denims.

    Price point: From $55 for a dress to $69 for a bodysuit set.

    Available at #02-31 Tanglin Mall, 163 Tanglin Rd., 6235-4006

Demonstrating items on mobile telephones needs, for example, the enlargement of the format of the photos”, explained Charles Henri Leroy, director of client relations who oversees marketing, services offered in stores and personal services.



    Hermès Sandals available on Hermes.com

    The epitome of French luxury and defendant of artisanal know-how, Hermès has nonetheless been pioneering, selling ties, handkerchiefs and perfumes on the Internet since 2002 in the USA. It was not until March 2008 that the fashion house opened its institution’s own site though. A “reverse path” compared to other luxury brands, observed Charles Henri Leroy, “adhering to the strategy of Jean-Louis Dumas and his son Pierre Alexis, who, from the start, saw the Net as a means of supplying a supplementary service to clients and a tool to recruit new ones who, without which, would not have access to the products, the brand not being present in their country”.

    As for the institutional site, Hermès has waited until technology permitted, through the quality of photos and videos, to show all the facets of the label and its history. After America, sales sites have been opened in the principal countries of Europe, Switzerland and since last year in Australia and Japan. The price is based on current market prices and available products that are sold has been gradually enlarged, with differences by country: the American site is the richest in leather products and offers watches, whereas the French sells jewellery.

    Coming soon, ready-to-wear men’s wear and, why not, ladies’ fashion. “We have no taboos. It’s like in our real boutiques, each director has his own selection”, though always with “multi-channel continuity” between what is available on the web and in stores.
