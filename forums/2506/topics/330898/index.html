    A skill that is used for History&SocialStudies.

    Question Type: Usefulness of Source

    Examples of Question:

    How useful is the source in helping you to understand...? Explain your answer.

    To what extent is the source useful in...? Explain your answer.

    ANSWER the question!

    • The Source is very useful...
    • The Source is not very useful...
    • The Source is useful to a large/certain/small extent...

    Explain content usefullness & limitations.

    • Describe information provided in the source
    • Explain the content/hidden message.
    • If it's a cartoon, describe & explain the characters, words, symbols etc.
    • Does it help us to make conclusions eg. Who is to be blamed?
    • Does the source provide you with a complete picture?
    • Explain the limitations of the source.

    NO SOURCE IS USELESS

    Cross-reference to check for reliability

    • Use another source to support your answer.
    • A source that is supported by another source is more reliable and hence, more useful.
    • A source that is contradicted by another source, is less reliable and hence, less useful.

    Use Provenance to detect biasness & propaganda/hidden motive

    • Examine the author/date/source origin and determine if the source is biased or not?
    • If source is not biased, examine if there is enchanced reliability.
    • Eg. Source that is balanced in view or is willing to criticised his/her own country or fellowman may be enhanced reliability.
    • Examine whether source contains propaganda/hidden motive to influence audience of a certain view.
    • A biased source that contains a propaganda/hidden motive is less reliable and hence, less useful.

    Conclusion

    • A reasonable conclusion

     

    Highest level is usually explanation of source reliability using Cross-reference and explanation of tone & purpose.
