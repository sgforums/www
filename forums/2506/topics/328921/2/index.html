    If your goal is to strengthen your NAPFA then you should do more of your weaker stations.

    If you suck at SBJ, take the initiative, use the school's mat and hop everyday on it. SBJ is not just about building the "explosive" force, it's also about having the "feel" just before you jump and your jumping technique. Train your mind to coordinate with your body.

    If you suck at pull-ups, do more pull-ups. Do your max each day before your meals, it works that way.

    If you suck at 2.4, run 2.4 everyday. 2.4 is more on your breathing techniques and the stride you're comfortable at.

    You get the idea.

    If your goal is to strengthen your body then obviously the equation changes. You should then vary your exercises to suit your needs.
