    this is juz my personal guide~~~~~~~~~~

     

    1. Cut Normal Trees when you begin. These aren't hard to find, they are scattered all over the land. Simply use a Bronze Axe to chop them down. In no time, you'll be gaining experience.
    2. Once you have achieved Level 15, congratulations. You can now move on to Oak Trees. A little difference between Normal Trees and Oak Trees is that Oak Trees can give you more then one log for each tree. This makes the entire process quicker. Okay, so you can go to Varrock and chop them there for the best experience.
    3. If you have achieved Level 30, congratulations again on a job well done. Now, my personal advice is for you to stay at Oak Trees until roughly level 40. This will make cutting Willow Logs, which become available at Level 30, a LOT quicker and easier to cut. Also, Willow Logs are the second FASTEST experience for this skill in the game. The fastest (which is harder to achieve access to, is teaks). Of course, it becomes more obvious when you're a higher level, but for now, you can stay cutting Willow Trees until you're a few more steps up the ladder.
    4. This isn't so much of a step as a reminder. Or, a helpful hint. Willow Trees are the fastest experience in all of Runescape. Other trees may give more experience for each log you cut, but Willow Trees give a fair bit, and you get a large amount of logs from them. Basically, what I'm saying here is, if you want to get Level 99 as fast as you can, you should stick to Willow Trees. Or for the go-getter player, you can try for teaks. Teaks are the fastest experience in the game. It's been proven that you get as much as 20-30k more experience per hour.
    5. If you're not anxious, and you want a bit of GP while your at it, you can cut Yew Trees ( located at Seers Village, and Varrock, the two best places, along with the southeast corner of gnome stronghold ) once you reach Level 60, or Magic Trees at level 75 (the most profit can be gained with these) which can be located at Seers Village as well, or in the Elf Village (only accessible if you've completed a quest which are very, very slow to cut and often very crowded.
    6. Also cut Maple trees that are acuired at lvl 45 they are fast and give 100 xp each. These can be found at camelot behind bank or near the wc expert.

     

    • Woodcutting may seem like the most boring skill, but it's actually one of the easiest ways to get money.
    • When cutting down high level logs, always sell them to other players - fletchers will buy these for crazy prices.
    • Try to keep up with your axes. Buy the next advanced axe when you achieve the level to use them and sell your current one. Just remember that you need the matching attack level to wield it, for example level 40 for the rune axe and level 60 for the dragon axe (members only).
    • Woodcutting skill capes cost 99k (as do all skill capes)and are MEMBERS ONLY!
    • If you are bored, do not move to another skill, invite a friend to cut trees with you, or join into conversations with other fellow cutters. Listen to music or watch TV while you're cutting, but beware of the "Warnings".
    • If you also have a house in Rimmington with a servant and you also have 60+ woodcutting, cut yews by the portal and use your servant as a banker.(optional)*
    • With autoers gone due to update keep going for your goal it may be boring, but it's faster than it used to be.
    • When you advance a level, reach 10k xp to go on yew logs, then switch to willow for the last 10k xp!!
    • Make sure you have enough patience for this or you will get bored easily.
    • Long-term plan: Achieve at least 100,000 exp. per day for about 3 months (I recommend Willows) and you will automatically earn the title Master of Woodcutting. =)
    • To reach level 99 in any skill, you would need to have obtained exactly 13,034,431 exp.
    • You may run into a random event battle, so you can either run away or have a weapon on hand to defend yourself.
    • Watch out for trees that move their branches and have red eyes. These are called Ents, and your axe will break if you attempt to chop them down. These are unpredictable random events, so keep your wits about you.
    • Be sure to glance at the computer at least every couple minutes or so because you could be under attack!
    • Don't be tempted to cheat to get ahead, you WILL get caught and banned, and it's just not worth it.
    • For various aspects of Woodcutting in Runescape, there are certain requirements before you do certain things. For example, Maple Trees and Magic Trees, which are two high level trees, are both found in areas that you can only access if you are a Member. Also, the Dragon Hatchet is an item you can only obtain with a Membership status.
    • You may want an iPod, MP3 player, or music on your computer because woodcutting can get boring at times but it can be fun if u chat to other players
    • Chop yews near rimmington and if you can teleport to falador because that is the closest bank. Rimmington has 4 trees and is very good to train your woodcutting.
