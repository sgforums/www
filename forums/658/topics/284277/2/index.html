    8 Nov 07, 13:02
    Overall I would give the album a 2.5/5.

    The only tracks worth listening are Niu Zai Hen Mang, Qing Hua Ci, and Yang Guang Zai Nan.

    Qing Hua Ci is really unique where the notes are like all over the place. Seems like lots of thought went into the song. I will place it just behind Dong Feng Po and Ju Hua Tai.

    And you know there's something wrong when the tracks duration at the back cover are all the same (3'25). The album was really rushed.

    As a fan I'm just so disappointed. Wu Shuang could have been a good song if not for the Linkin Park rip-off at the intro. In the ballads, he sounds like he's so bored he's about to die. Wo Bu Pei sounds like a sad version of Ai Zai Xi Yuan Qian, but I'll give him credit for singing with real sincerity and sadness, though the chorus just slumped into mediocrity with its generic tune. Che is a poor attempt. He also used tunes from the Secret OST, which shows that he doesn't really care for his listeners. Tian Tian De sounds like something he would write for S.H.E. He's already almost 30 years old but still sings like he is 19. He should really come to terms with the fact that he can't surpass Jian Dan Ai.

    It's now 3 poor albums in the row for Jay Chou. Though he can still have a knack for a good tune (like Mi Die Xiang), but sadly he seems really happy being the typical Taiwanese popstar. Perhaps he's jaded. Someone needs to slap him on the head.

    The only thing he did right with this album is the name. He is really busy, as reflected with those mediocre songs you would normally expect the Taiwan Industry to churn out week in week out.
