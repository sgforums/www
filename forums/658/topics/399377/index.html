    Haa. Glad that you made up your mind to pursue your interest. May I ask which school would you be attending for your beginner course? And learning the piano requires a lot of patience. I am for one, pretty much of a late bloomer, I only enjoyed after many many years of coaching from my teachers. 

    If you are going for long term, please get a piano, not those keyboard, if you take good care of it, it can last you for 10years. Choose the piano according to your own touch and feel. Different people have different preference, different piano, even if the same model is different too. 

    Classical gives you a broader perspective. See where your interest lies. If you know classical, chances are you are able to play pop out, if you know pop, there is a chance that you cannot play the classical pieces due to technical difficulties and other interpretations.
