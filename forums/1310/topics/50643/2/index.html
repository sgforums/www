    Khairul's suggestions are worth considering seeing that the current formation seems to be expecting too much from Russell and Ang.

    Apart from that, I have also made several other obeservations. Firstly, the AMCs are not holding the ball long enough for the formation to be effective. More often than not, when the pass was made, we do not have enough players up front.

    Secondly, our AMRs and AMLs are playing too wide. We must bear in mind that there is only one striker up front to receive the cross. The AMs should play a tighter game, switching positions if required.

    Thirdly, our finishing have been quite poor of late. We have won games because our opponents failed to finish off their numerous chances and the same goes for us too.

    Lastly, the formation requires us to be compact both defensively and offensively. The AMs need to come down during defensive periods and likewise, the defenders need to push up during offensive periods. Failure to do either will only leave huge gaps on the field.

    All things considered, the current formation may be too demanding for our skill level, experience and players rapport/understanding.

    However, I do also notice some good improvements in the team and players. Some of our players are getting fitter by the match. Off the ball running have also improved and we can see more players supporting each other.

    It takes time for a team like us to gel, especially when we only play together once a week.

    Despite our poor results recently, I still look forward to every sunday and the week will only be complete after the game.
