    23 Apr 09, 01:40

    Imo, if you really know how to appreciate japanese food. any decent traditional japanese restaurant r better than sushi belts' sushi.

    In a cuisine where "freshness" is highly prized as one of it's virtues, it's always better to have japanese food prepared upon your order than grabbing off sushi belts where only god knows how long that plate had been on that belt... not to mention the fact most of the sushi will have been on the belt way before the operation hours (chanced upon the belt around 80% full when I walked past one sakae sushi outlet at 930+am and yet the outlet is open at 11:30am *shudder*)

    but as I stressed, "acceptable quality for acceptable value".

    And btw, next time any of you go for a 3pm - 5pm sushi buffet. 1) pay attention to the color of the sushi (avoid "pale" colored meat) 2) the sushi rice (avoid sushi with the rice base "loose" or "broken") 3) the hot items (touch the base of the plates to check whether is it still hot) these are signs that the plate may have been on the belt for a long long time, thus the reason for the buffet timings. :P
