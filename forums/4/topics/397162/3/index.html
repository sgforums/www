    Everybody judges one another by their own set of rules. More often than not, ignorance is bliss. Go to a wetmarket one day and see how the fish monger kills fish. More often than not he takes a wooden stick and bashes the fish a few times on the head. This doesnt kill the fish, only stuns it. He then chops the fish up into pieces alive. Now some people may say "Oh thats so cruel!" Well, naturally if we all opted for humane measures to kill the fish, which would probably cost more than the price of a simple wooden stick, would you be willing to pay more for the fish to die more peacefully? People will always find ways to feel morally superior over others. The vegetarians will call meat eaters "Uncultured barbarians, with no sympathy for living creatures". Meat eaters call people who eat shark fins "Uncaring people who dont care about endangered species" and people who eat shark fins say "But our ancestors did it and its part of our traditions!" Now what about eating fish whilst they are still alive? Is it cruel? Then again would it be more cruel than bashing the fish a few times on the head with a wooden stick and disembowelling it whilst it is still alive? Or what about chopping the fins off a shark and throwing it back alive into the sea to die because it can no longer swim? At the end of the day, everybody has their own comfort zone of morality.
