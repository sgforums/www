Food caterers to time-stamp meal packets

    Mar 4, 2011 - Straits Times

    By Amresh Gunasingham

    FOOD caterers are going to have to display clear labels on meal packets or at buffet tables that tell people the food must be eaten by a specified time or it will go bad.

    The new rule will be introduced some time towards the end of this year, Minister of State for the Environment Amy Khor said on Friday when she announced the move to give added protection against people getting food poisoning.

    Her ministry is targetting these caterers because they typically serve food to a large number of people at any one time and any 'lapses...could potentially result in mass food poisoning,' she added.

    She also said that studies have shown that food in air-conditionedplaces can be safely consumed up to four hours after it had been cooked.

    Dr Khor announced the new rule in Parliament when replying to Mr Liang Eng Hwa (Holland-Bukit Timah GRC), who had asked for more severe penalties to be imposed on recaltriant food caterers.

    Dr Khor stressed that the new labelling measure is part of her ministry's overall effort to ensure the safety of cooked food, and any punishment will be in accordance with existing regulations regarding food poisoning.
