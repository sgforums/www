    Just now watch a show that talk about cooking fried rice...
    It is said that cooking fried rice shows one person's skill in culinery.

    before i forget the tips...i will post it here to share...

    1) Add more oil to the wok. Pour the beated egg in. This is to prevent the egg from sticking to the wok. Another purpose is so that you need no add much oil when u cook the rice.
    2)Cook the egg. Fire must not be too high, if not the egg will become chao tak. Throw in the spring onion next. Make sure the egg is not overcooked.
    3) Cooked rice should be left overnight. Actually, the wetness of the rice is the most important. Keep it as dry as possible with little bit moisture.
    4) After cooking the egg, throw in the rice. Toss it around so that the egg aroma and rice can come together.
    5) Add some seasoning.
    6) Add in the soy sauce. Important thing to remember is that soy sauce should be added around the wok, not onto the rice itself.
    7) Stir fry till aroma comes. Serve.

    Something simple yet amazing...

    ermmm flipping the wok is not 100% needed to achieve the wok hei when flrying rice. The big flame is needed for the wok hei no doubt, still the temp must be comtrol. Reasonable amount of wok hei can be achieve by conventional household stove.

    Beside looking damm cool, which is totally not the point. Flipping the wok is done to stir the rice and making it move constantly to prevent wok sticking when high heat is used. Still when small amount of rice is been fried u can use the stir method but need to stir very fast. My skill not there yet but this method produce a ricebowl of good wok hei fried rice as vouch by my chef fren. When flipping a wok is it important not to lift the wok or sauce pan too high away from the fire. That defeat the purpose of high power fire.

    Adding butter just before the fire rice is served gives it a good aroma.
    The eggs add pior to the rice also helps in preventing the rice from sticking to the wok besides giving it good aroma.
    Freshly cook rice can be fried too, need a lot of skill , practise makes perfect.
    Adding some wine be it chinese wine or sake prior to serving gives the rice a nice aroma.

    Best tasting fried rice i eaten is Kimuchi Chahan from Tamaya a jap resturant where i use to work. 2nd best tasting fried rice i eaten is fried by my fren at a canteen at CIAS cargo centre.
