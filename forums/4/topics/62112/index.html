    Eating Sago Worms!

    A pictorial presentation of the preparation of a perfect worm meal.


    First get a bunch of sago worms - they have to be alive and fresh (i.e. still crawling!). The ones you are looking at were collected at our farm.


    After washing the worms and marinating them with salt, fry them! Here you see Suze doing the honors.


    Fry the worms until they stop crawling and turn slightly brown and crispy. Then break each worm in half (if not they will pop!). Gives a whole new meaning to the phrase "fry until crisp".


    The worms are coming along well... keep stirring. The aroma is heavenly. Peace to the world!


    Here they are, all crisp and crunchy, ready for the mouth and stomach (of those who can stomach these delicious worms!).


    Time for some heavenly snack - they taste real good . . . like cream in a crispy skin (with a slight peanut taste). Wanna have some?

    (source: Pastor Peter de Run's site )
