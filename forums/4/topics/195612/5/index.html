    For tomato pasta I use:

    Meat: 150g beef
    Tomato: 1 Can Plum Tomatoes, 3 Scoops Tomato Puree
    Sofritto: 1 Clove of Garlic, Half an Onion, 1/4 Carrot, 1 Small stalk Celery,
    Oil: Olive Oil
    Herbs: Basil, Oregano, Bay leaf
    Seasonings: Sea Salt n Freshly Ground Black Pepper
    Toppings: Parmesan to serve (buy in a block and grate. don't buy powdered)

    Saute the sofritto in olive oil (for olive oil, more is better) for about 10 minutes. Don't burn anything.
    Stir in the beef, and cook. Integrate the sofritto into the beef to break it up.
    Then I add the canned tomatoes (chopped finely). I usually drain the liquid in the tomatoes into a cup, then drink it (don't waste! its alot of vitamins) because if it goes into the sauce its gonna get very soggy and you have to cook it longer to make it thicken. I don't wanna destroy the vitamins by cooking too long. Then I add the tomato puree to increase the tomato content of the sauce. (Traditional recipes boil down larger quantities tomatoes for an hour, so they can skip the puree. But what the heck, Im cooking for myself so save some fuel. The fresh tomatoes also help to liven up the sauce.)
    When the tomatoes and puree are in, I stir in about a tablespoon of basil and about a teaspoon of oregano(oregano is very strong, don't use too much) and the bay leaf.
    Then add salt n pepper to taste. Serve when you think its ready. Serves about 2.

    *For a more authentic recipe of bolognese ragu, don't use garlic or herbs, and use a bit of pancetta in the sofritto. Also, reduce the amt of tomato paste.*

    For cream cheese (alfredo) you just cook pasta, melt some butter, heat some heavy cream, and grate some parmesan.

    Put grated parmesan on cooked pasta, then pour the hot butter on it and stir. Then, pour heated cream on it to finish. Season with salt, black pepper and nutmeg.

    It goes well with sauteed mushrooms in butter, or chicken, spinach, peas.

    For carbonara (egg n bacon):

    Cook pasta in salt water.
    Cook bacon (or if you can find it, pancetta, which is an unsmoked italian bacon) in a frying pan in olive oil or butter.
    Crack an egg and add freshly ground black pepper to it. Beat mixture. One egg for about one large handful of pasta.
    Drain pasta and reserve some cooking water, just in case your end product is too dry. Do not rinse, if you do so sauce will not stick well to pasta. Do NOT wash the pan!!!!
    Return pasta to the pot in which u cooked it. Put egg into the pot and stir. Let the heat of the pot and the pasta cook the egg. If you're squeamish, you may turn stove to the lowest heat, but try not to get an omelette. You want a smooth texture, kinda like cream.
    Grate parmesan (parmigiano reggiano) and/or pecorino romano cheese over the pasta, the stir again. You may add salt/pepper to taste.
    The egg should look as if it disappeared into the noodles, not become a pool of water at the base of the pot. If the noodles are too sticky or dry, add the reserved cooking water. You may also dump the cooking oil into the mix for more flavor, unhealthy, but delicious.
    Wait 3 minutes, then serve. Pass the cheese and pepper!

    Dairy section of a good supermarket should have the cream. It looks like cups of yoghurt.

    Remember, LOTSA OF PARMESAN, because it is the source of all cheesiness(now wasn't that just cheesy)! About same amount of parmesan as cream, measured in a cup. Butter around 2/3 the amount of cream.

    Use flat noodles for cream sauces like fettucine, bavette or tagliatte.

    Grate-it-yourself parmesan is tastiest. On a budget, I recommend Perfect Italiano, cus the real thing from nothern Italy is abt $6 for 100g (the size of a kuay lapis slice). Grated/presliced parmesan is nearly flavorless, and whatever you do do not buy anything kraft calls cheese. Its solidified vegetable oil + chemicals sold as cheese!

    Edit : Mediterranean bay leaves and indian bay leaves are actually 2 unrelated species of plants altogether, Laurus nobilis and Cinnamomum tejpata respectively.

    Al Dente = to the tooth, meaning firm enough that you can feel something when u bite, and not be like mush. The equivilent word locally would be "Q"?

    Best way is to cook according to package instructions. Aka, 8 minutes means 8 minutes, and of course you begin in rapidly boiling water. Then drain it immediately. You may also cut a strand to test. If it feels like cardboard its not done yet. You may wanna blanch it in cold water to stop cooking, but I dont do so because it also drains away the starch in the pasta. The remaining heat is really insignificant to me, cus i try to dress it immediately. The starch helps the sauce to really cling onto the pasta.
