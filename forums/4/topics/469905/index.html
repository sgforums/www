    Yesterday is Deepavali (should be Vesak Day). Have not gone to Chinatown Red Star Dim Sum Restaurant for yum cha, so went yesterday. The queue is so long, no prior booking is allowed and it is first come first serve basis.

    The restaurant still has the traditional trolley ladies pushing the dim sum from table to table and the ladies will chop a small circle on the card on each dim sum taken. Very nostalogic leh.

    The dim sum is good and cheap and there are a lot of varieties of the dim sum.

    I don't know of your taste.    Those of us who have gone to Red Star for dim sum agree that the place is nostalgic and the dim sum is served from the push carts.   We find the quality of dim sum not very good and prices are not cheap.

    Other than the nostalgic ambience I think the dim sum's standard is not acceptable.    That was my first and last time there.

    If you want nice dim sum in a nostalgic place, go Swee Choon.     it has been existing for about 60 or 40 years in Singapore.      They are not open in the afternoon.

    We give a thumbs down for Red Star.
