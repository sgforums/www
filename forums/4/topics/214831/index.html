    Tiffin Club
    Tel: 6323-3189

    What This swish kitchen in Jiak Chuan Road opened by businessman Ajai Zecha and actress Michelle Saram serves hot, homecooked lunches on weekdays to businesses within a 'reasonable walking distance'. Most of its customers - call them 'members' - are creative types working in the nearby Chinatown shophouses. The food, mostly Western fare, is delivered by staff carrying brass tiffin carriers.

    Sample Hunter's chicken stew, mango salsa, broccoli, walnuts and garlic chips sauteed in olive oil with rock salt and black peppercorns and mashed potatoes.
    Price $15 for a small portion, $20 for a large one. Members have a credit account that they can top up regularly.

    Verdict The mango salsa - essentially a light mango salad - had bite to it, thanks to crunchy toppings like red pepper and celery. The chicken stew was tender, while the olive oil and garlic chips lent great aroma to the otherwise bland broccoli. The mashed potatoes were nothing to shout about.
    Prices may be a bit steep but the upside is that you get quality fare plus hearty servings.

    Rating ****
