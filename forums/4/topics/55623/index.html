    Long before all these stalls, there were afew that I know of, used to push their carts along the road in Chinatown area... I am used to those Chwee Kuay than the current types, like Tiong Bahru/Bedok stalls. They still do but not many now, only one I know and left is still selling in Chinatown hawker centre. However, there is a branch from of either Bedok or Tiong Bahru stall (not sure which one) has also set up a stall there. Last few times I was there in the morning, business wasn't that good as most people queue at the other stall that I like most given a choice.

    Its only open in the morning between 6.30am ~10am, or even earlier on Sat & Sun (close at 9am maybe, due to sold out). Its make and sell style, as they sell, they make and when all is done, they close shop. Its been there for years already. 8 for $1 (CHEAP). the kuay is soft and tender, unlike those common ones which I hardly eat, and the toppings are also self made, light brown in colour boiling in ceramic pot. Most topping will turn drak brown or black upon further boiling, but not this. that's my favourite, no. 1. Often only get to eat at the Bedok stalls as I often go there....
