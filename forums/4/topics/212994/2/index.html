    3 mins is of cos a general rule. But its NOT MORE than 3 minutes, for lipton, generally, 2 minutes is enough. Temperature of water is important too. If the water is not hot enough, 2 minutes would not be enough sometimes for the tea to release its tanin. If the water is too hot, then 2 minutes could be too long. For most tea, including black tea and chinese tea, using water that is just off the boil is best. For green tea, using water that is set aside for around 2-3 minutes off the boil.

    Black tea is meant to be a bit tanin and so called siap. of cos too siap is not nice also, maybe that tea is not for you lor hehehehe... but chinese tea leaves can stay longer in the teapot compared to black tea without becoming astringent.
