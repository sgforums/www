    Prata party, anyone?: Soak up chicken and mutton curries with Phoenix Garden Cafe's famous roti prata.

    The Phoenix Garden Cafe in Hotel Phoenix. It struck gold in the 1990s with its roti prata, which still remains one of the best you can get in an air-conditioned eatery.

    It was voted one of the top five favourite dishes in a public poll in 1996 held in conjunction with the Singapore Food Festival that year.

    The roti prata, served with chicken and mutton curries, is available as part of its daily international buffet.

    But over the years, the buffet has been beefed up with even more signature items. The chicken rice is another staple that appears in the spread every day.

    Then there are other items such as laksa and mee siam that alternate with each other. Laid out for self-service, you decide what toppings to add.

    For the laksa, for example, you can add pieces of poached chicken from the chicken-rice counter or prawns and salmon from the seafood section.

    And for dessert, what is destined to be another classic attraction is the durian mousse cake. Moist and rich with durian pulp, each mouthful is almost as good as eating the fruit itself.

    Adding variety to the buffet is an array of salads, soups and hot dishes which are changed daily. But it's the staples that have made the cafe the place to go for good local fare.

    PHOENIX GARDEN CAFE
    Hotel Phoenix Singapore,
    277 Orchard Road,
    Tel: 6737-8666

    Price: $23.80 for adults and $16.80 for children (weekday lunch), $25.80 for adults and $17.80 for children (weekend lunch and daily dinner)

    Just like the good old days: Savour home-styled dishes that come in tiffin carriers at Cafe 2000.

    For this month, as part of Singapore's 40th birthday celebrations, the eatery is featuring a tiffin promotion with home-styled dishes that remind you of Mum's cooking.

    Better known as tingkat, tiffin carriers were once de rigueur for workers who often packed their lunches to work.

    Comprising several tiers, these containers were ideal for carrying different dishes.

    What I find quaint about Cafe 2000's promotion is that the dishes are actually served in a four-tiered tiffin carrier.

    The rice is in one of the tiers while the other three contain dishes such as spicy deep-fried chicken, sambal prawn or ikan bilis petai.

    A soup and a dessert come in separate bowls.

    The whole set costs $16. But you can order two carriers, which are enough to be shared among three people, for $28.

    The meal also includes a free flow of teh tarik, which comes in a stainless steel container.

    While the nostalgia factor will strike a chord with babyboomers, what is really attractive is the great price and the homely cooking.

    And there is something to be said for celebrating the birthday of this nation of food lovers by bringing back some of its culinary memories.

    CAFE 2000
    M Hotel, 81 Anson Road,
    Tel: 6421-6222

    Price: $16 for one tiffin carrier, $28 for two. Available for lunch only from Mondays to Saturdays this month. The menu changes weekly.

    If you like your crabs small, the New Signboard eatery in Geylang is your kind of place.

    Unlike other seafood restaurants that serve mainly huge mud crabs of 1kg or more, it specialises in crabs that weigh about half a kilogram.

    You have a choice of about 20 style of cooking methods, including the common chilli and black pepper crabs as well as more unusual dishes like crab congee and crab curry.

    You can either choose to pay $18 for every kilogram of crabs you consume or $8 per crab. There is no charge for the cooking.

    For those who like their crabs meaty, the non-airconditioned eatery does stock some of the big varieties. Some people, however, say small crabs have smoother meat and are sweeter.

    New Signboard, 323 Geylang Road (Lorong 19),

    Tel: 9336-4774
