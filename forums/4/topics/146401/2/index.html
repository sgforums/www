    If you want a change from the usual champagne brunches on Sundays, the Four Seasons Hotel's new Weekend Oriental Brunch may be your cup of tea.

    Held at Jiang-Nan Chun on Saturdays (11.30am to 2.30pm) and Sundays (10.30am to 3pm), this is a typical Cantonese meal of dimsum items and dishes such as spare ribs flavoured with orange sauce and braised sea cucumber with seafood.

    There are 100 items on the menu. Priced at $48 for adults and $25 for children, it includes a free flow of Chinese tea and fruit juices.

    If you want a free flow of champagne, just add $40 per person.

    The price is not cheap, but you do not get inferior food at the Four Seasons. All the dishes are served the same way as a la carte orders, instead of being laid out on a buffet spread.

    Jiang-Nan Chun, Four Seasons Singapore,

    190 Orchard Boulevard, Tel: 6831-7220

    POT LUCK: Kuishin-Bo boasts over 100 items, including the popular Kaminabe (Japanese paper pot).

    Eat all you want for $30

    KUISHIN-BO

    Where-neh?
    3 Temasek Boulevard, Suntec City Tower 1, #03-002, Tel: 6238-7088
    Lunch buffet is at $23.80+ (Mon to Fri) and $25.80+ (Sat, Sun and public holidays) for adults, from 11.30am to 3pm. Dinner buffet is at $31.80+ (Mon to Thu and Sun) and $39.80+ (Fri, Sat and eve of public holidays and public holidays) for adults, from 5.30 to 10pm. Children below 1.4m are charged $16+ on all days.
    Visit www.kuishinbo.com.sg for details.

    The brainchild of the operators behind the Fiesta and Kuriya Japanese restaurants, Kuishin-Bo has been around for more than four years. Its buffet was introduced in April last year. It can seat 200 diners and reservation is strongly advised.

    Mr John Yek, the division manager, said: 'For restaurants, it is simple. It boils down to the food and that is why it is important for us to maintain the quality.'

    Under the supervision of a Japanese master chef, the buffet boasts over 100 items, including the popular kaminabe (Japanese paper pot) and expensive delicacies such as snow crab.

    Every 30 minutes, an announcement is made for limited special items, and diners rush to queue for it.

    The food is served buffet style, where diners pick their own food from the spread, which changes monthly.

    PIGGY'S BIRTHDAY MENU
    Iggy's one. And to celebrate, the restaurant owned by Ignatius Chan at The Regent Singapore has come up with a special 12-course menu that will be available from Thursday to Sept 15.

    The menu is compiled from the many original creations the restaurant has come up with over the past year. Iggy's food is often inspired by what Chan himself eats on his travels abroad.

    For the anniversary menu, dishes include interesting items like cold somen tossed in classic caviar condiments; steamed foie gras with tofu in a ponzu scented jus and truffle bun; and Sakura ebi cappellini with konbu and home-made scampi oil.

    Each dish offers an interesting mix of flavours and textures that you won't find anywhere else.

    The menu is priced at $120, which is lower than the $150 the restaurant normally charges for its smaller, 10-course menu.

    Each dish comes in bite-sized portions but there is definitely enough to fill you up.

    Be sure to block off at least a couple of hours to enjoy the meal. This is not something to hurry over.

    Iggy's, The Regent Singapore, Level 3, 1 Cuscaden Road, Tel: 6732-2234

    NEW AND SIMPLE
    A new eatery in Wheelock Place is simply called The Simple Life, which has a nice ring to it.

    Opened on July 1 by the people behind Fish & Co, it serves local hawker fare such as kueh paitee, tahu goreng, chicken rice and prawn noodle soup in modern, air-conditioned surroundings.

    Prices range from $5.90 to $6.90 for starters and $8.90 to $13.90 for main courses. Main dishes like chicken rice and nasi lemak come with a choice of local drinks such as bandung, calamansi and barley.

    The Simple Life, Wheelock Place 02-18, 501 Orchard Road, Tel: 6738-3212

    Also new at Wheelock Place is Sun With Moon Japanese Dining & Cafe, which opened on Aug 23.

    If the name sounds strange, it's a combination of Japanese Dining Sun, which has its first outlet in Chijmes, with a new concept called Japanese Cafe Moon.

    While the Sun half of the new outlet features items such as kamameshi (steamed seasoned rice pot) and half-broiled sushi, the Moon cafe focuses on modern Japanese desserts and drinks made with infused tea.

    A meal here would cost you slightly above $30 per person, with the kamameshi, which comes with toppings like beef and salmon, averaging $20 each.

    The cafe opens all day from 11.30am to 11pm from Sundays to Thursdays and till midnight on weekends.
