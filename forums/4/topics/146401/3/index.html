    Vansh, the trendy Indian restaurant at the Singapore Indoor Stadium, is turning the humble dosa on its head this week.

    Dosa is a South Indian pancake that is traditionally made with a batter of parboiled rice or lentils. But for Vansh's dosa promotion, on till Sunday, the chef has come up with unusual batters of whole grain wheat, semolina and even soya bean pulp.

    These are served with equally interesting fillings such as peppered prawn, cheese and nut, and Sichuan chilli.

    The dosas are available for dinner only from 6.30 to 10.30pm at prices from $8 to $22.
    Vansh, The Stadium Waterfront 01-04, Singapore Indoor Stadium, 2 Stadium Walk, Tel: 6345-4466

    GINSENG HOT POT



    Hot pots are hot in every sense of the word. They are all the rage here, but one often ends up sweating profusely over the steaming pot.

    The Buffet, which offers a hot pot or steamboat buffet in the evenings, is one of the coolest eateries in which to enjoy a meal.

    It is also very clean, with the tables fitted with ceramic hotplates that do away with the need for flames, as well as a clear glass pot in which to serve the stock. Besides the usual chicken and tom yam soups, a different special soup is also featured each month. This month, it's ginseng soup which is brewed with chicken and fresh ginseng.

    And, on top of the usual items on the buffet spread, which include seafood and yong tau foo, there is also black chicken to go with the soup.

    The steamboat is available from 6.30 to 11pm. It costs $38 for adults and $19 for children.
    The Buffet, Level 2, M Hotel, 81 Anson Road, Tel: 6421-6226

    NOODLES WITH A TWIST

    Noodles are the theme of the month at Beads Restaurant in the Grand Mercure Roxy hotel. On the menu is a selection of both traditional Thai noodles as well as pasta with a Thai twist.

    For example, you can find a Thai-style penne with roast duck and broccoli as well as macaroni with sliced chicken, ginger and fish sauce.

    The more familiar choices include beef noodle soup, kway teow soup and noodles with seafood Thai yong tau foo.

    Each serving, priced at $14, comes with a fresh Thai coconut.
    Beads Restaurant, Grand Mercure Roxy Singapore, 50 East Coast Road, Roxy Square, Tel: 6340-5678

    EGYPTIAN BUFFETS



    For the Ramadan month, two hotels are offering an Egyptian buffet for its break-fast dinner.

    The first is the Grand Hyatt, which is replacing its normal halal buffet at The Straits Kitchen with specialities from the Land of the Pyramids.

    Preparing the food are two chefs from the Hyatt Regency Sharm El-Sheikh in Egypt, who will present dishes such as Turkish kofta, goulash with cheese and mezze items (small appetisers and dips) like tahini and tabboule.

    These will complement its spread of local Malay dishes which include lemang, serunding and lontong. There will also be a barbecued whole lamb. The buffet dinner is on till Nov 3, and is priced at $48 for adults and $25 for children.

    Across the road at Royal Plaza on Scotts, a similar buffet is held at Cafe Vienna.

    Here, the food is prepared by the hotel's own Egyptian chef, Emad. He, too, has come up with a barbecued whole lamb as well as specialities such as kofta, tagine of lamb and beef cooked with eggplant. You get a good selection of mezze as well.

    From 7 to 9pm, musician Zubir Abdullah performs Malay pop songs on the traditional gambus and keyboard.

    The dinner ends on Nov 2. It is priced at $48 for adults and $24 for children.

    The Straits Kitchen, Grand Hyatt Singapore, 10 Scotts Road, Tel: 6735-1234

    GET KINKI

    Before you start getting any naughty thoughts, Kinki is a region in Japan that covers a few prefectures, including the cities of Kyoto and Osaka.

    The food from the area will be featured in a promotion at Inagiku until Sunday, prepared by guest chef Akikazu Fujiguchi from the Relaxin Japanese Restaurant in Osaka.

    His dishes include an excellent stone-grilled wagyu beef and a yummy braised lobster with white miso. And, if you like cold noodles, don't miss the cold soumen with snapper. There is also an unusual lightly grilled wild chicken that is practically raw. Only for those with a strong stomach.

    Inagiku, Level 3, Raffles The Plaza, 2 Stamford Road, Tel: 6431-6156
