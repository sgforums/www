    Teh is Tea = Tea + Condensed Milk + Sugar
    Teh O is Black teh (no milk) = Tea + Sugar
    Teh Kosong (no sugar) = Tea + Milk
    Tea O Kosong = Tea (no milk, no sugar)
    Tea C (commonly known as Hong Kong style)= Tea + Evaporated Milk + Sugar (10c more)

    Tea Kao = Thick Tea (more tea added than normal)
    Tea Po = Diluted tea (more water added than normal)
    Tea Siu Tai = Tea Less sweet (less milk)
    Tea Ka Tai = Tea more sweet (more milk)
    Tea Peng = Tea + Ice (usually 20c more)

    So try calling out these drinks and see what you get...
    1) Teh c peng Kao
    2) Kopi siu tai

    Siu Tai, Ka Tai po, or kao, not much diff actually, due to $$ issues.
    **Above is same for coffee

    additional:

    tak gui (or Kicking) = Milo
    tai ka ho (Everybody happy)= Horlick
    Lao Hor (Means tiger in hokkien) = Tiger Beer
    Tiao Hgee (Fishing) = Chinese Tea (in a bag) (60c)
    Dinosaur = Ice Milo + milo power topping
    Godzilla = Same as Dinosaur but extra extra Milo topping (pls confirm, limited places only)
