  • 105090
    2 Feb 06, 21:34
    Tea = Tea + Milk + Sugar
    Tea O = Tea + Sugar
    Tea Kosong = Tea + Milk
    Tea O Kosong = Tea (no milk, no sugar)
    Tea C = premium milk. (10c more)

    Tea Kao = Thick Tea (more tea added than normal)
    Tea Po = Diluted tea (more water added than normal)
    Tea Siu Tai = Tea Less sweet (less milk)
    Tea Ka Tai = Tea more sweet (more milk)
    Tea Peng = Tea + Ice (usually 20c more)

    Siu Tai, Ka Tai po, or kao, not much diff actually, due to $$ issues.
    **Above is same for coffee

    additional:

    tak gui: Milo
    tai ka ho: Horlick
    Lao Hor: Tiger Beer
    Tiao Hgee: Chinese Tea (in a bag) (60c)

    You're not Singaporean? Or you seldom patronise kopitiams?

    To add further / give u a better idea:
    Teh is tea + sweetened condensed milk;
    Teh c is tea + unsweetended evaporated milk + sugar

    So u can have teh c kosong, which has no sugar.
    Or teh c kao kosong / teh c pok kosong kae c (Wahaha...u may get a very confused kopi auntie or get scolded for the latter).

    Other interesting variants:
    - Tark Keeww (meaning kick ball) = milo
    - Clementi / kim boon tai = ice lemon tea

    Teh = Tea + Condensed Milk + A bit of evaporated milk
    Teh O = Tea + Sugar only
    Teh Kosong = Tea + Evaporated Milk only
    Teh O Kosong = Tea only
    Teh C = Tea + Evaporated milk + Sugar
    Teh Gow = More tea, less water + Condensed Milk + A bit of evaporated milk
    Teh Poh = Less tea, more water + Condensed Milk + A bit of evaporated milk
    Teh Siew Dai = Tea + lesser Condensed Milk + A bit of evaporated milk
    Teh Gow Siew Dai = More tea, less water + lesser Condensed Milk + A bit of evaporated milk
    Teh Kah Dai = Tea + more Condensed Milk + A bit of evaporated milk
