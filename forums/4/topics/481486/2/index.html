    Personally, I feel that a cafe is a place that sells food. People go there, buy their food. Place their food on a table, sit down on a chair, and relax.

    Well, I am not the type who goes around looking for which cafe to go to enjoy. However, I have a good friend who loves to visit cafes and has been writing blog articles about her visits to cafes.

    I am not telling you her blog, because I am afraid it may cause her unncessary harrassment. Nonetheless, here's a list of cafes that she has written about which has interested me:

    1. 1. Tom’s Palette
    2. 2. Saizeriya
    3. 3. The Muffinry

    Whereas for me, as I was saying, I am not the kind who goes around looking for cafes, so I only know a few, that are either near my school or those that I hangout often:

     

    1. 1. Good News Cafe (I like their cookies and their mayo sandwiches. Cheap and enriching.)
    2. 2. McDonalds (I particularly like the one at Jurong Point, where there are tables and chairs facing the bus interchange. Can eat while see buses drive pass me. The one at Pioneer Mall also quite relaxing. Haven't been to the one at Jurong Central Park, but I think it would be lovely eating in a park environment.)
    Well, I do not really visit cafes. So those two are about the only cafes I know.
