    the following issue will be a sensitive one, so beware.

    i believe many of us Singaporeans love Ban Mian. at least i know it definitely used to be very popular. but lately i am very upset that our local food might be going going gone.

    i had been to a Ban Mian stall at a Food Junction foodcourt. i don't know since when it starts to be operated by non other than, Ah Tiongs. the Ban Mian that i ordered as usual, had ikan bilis. but before the Ah Tiong added them to my noodles, i noticed the strong stench from the ikan bilis. they were not fried, but were rather moisted and reddish. after i had tasted the ikan bilis, i was extremely disappointed. the ikan bilis were obviously not fresh and were definitely about to spoil.

    i understand that normally the ikan bilis could be kept for many days (but still tasted fresh) for Ban Mian but for this case, i had no idea how the ah tiongs handled it.

    can we call the NEA for this case?

    that is 1 stall down for 1 of my favourite local food.

    just today, i had another bowl of Ban Mian at a stall in a Kopitiam Foodcourt. it was operated by ah tiongs once again. i tried not to be prejudiced. but i couldn't understand their attitude.

    usually they had ikan bilis on top of the noodles as a form of garnish. when i couldn't see them today, i asked the stall ah tiong if she had added. she said yes. after i sat down and sort of stirred my noodles, i found like only 3 ikan bilis? i went back to the stall to ask for more. she did give me more but like slightly more than 5 pieces? and with a rather unhappy look on her face.

    i seriously could not understand why. i thought i was their source of income.

    that is another stall that i will never patronize again.

    there was also a Fishball Noodle stall in Lau Pat Sat that was recently handed over to ah tiongs to operate. the noodles cooked by them tasted funny. 1 trait that was missing was the Q-ness of the noodles when the previous couple (i believed were locals) cooked. the ah tiong also almost added tomato sauce to my noodles when i asked for chilli only.

    previously i swear i had no opinions with ah tiongs. now i am worried about our local food being cooked by ah tiongs.

    does anyone have any similar experiences?
