    The two easiest buffet diet tips are these: vegetables and fruit.

    When you first approach an all you can eat buffet, scan it for produce. Then, cover at least seventy five percent of your plate with these items.

    Now you’ve got things off to a great start, and you’re ready to keep making healthy choices throughout your meal. Almost all buffets have salad choices and vegetable side dishes, and these items offer the most vitamins and nutrients for the least calories. That means feeling full, getting what your body needs, and still getting a full, colorful plate of food.

    Once you’ve put down your produce, look around for protein options that are grilled, baked, or broiled instead of fried or stir-fried. When you find one that looks good, add a bit to your healthful plate, but make sure produce remains the primary food on your plate.

    Main thing I feel is to pace yourself. Don't go "sprinting" away at the food at the beginning. Having said this, I feel its also not a good idea to starve yourself before a buffet as you might gouge yourself too quickly and end up feeling full too fast. Personally, I feel one should have very light snacks during proper meal times before the buffet so that at least the stomach will not feel too empty and you will not feel the urge to choing the food.

     

    Don't drink too much water during the buffet. It might give you the premature illusion of being full. Take sips in between. Try to not eat too much spicy or dry stuff (unless they are the relatively expensive stuff) as they will make you thirsty.

     

    Make sure you have enough time on your hands. A proper "attack" on a buffet to me should last at least 2 hours, at least for the kind where the food is being ordered. If you need to rush off somewhere or anything, try not to have a buffet meal on that day.

     

    What I've said is just my own opinion. Different people have different approaches which work for them as well.
