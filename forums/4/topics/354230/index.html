    AVA intensifies tests on pistachios as precaution after US recall

    SINGAPORE : The Agri-Food and Veterinary Authority (AVA) has said it is intensifying its checks and testing of pistachios available in Singapore.

    It stressed this is a precautionary measure after news that the United States' second-largest pistachio processor, Setton Farms in California, was conducting a voluntary recall of over 450 tonnes of the nuts due to possible Salmonella contamination.

    The US recall came after Kraft Foods in America detected salmonella contamination in pistachios found in its Back to Nature trail mix.

    The pistachios were traced back to Setton Farms.

    The California Department of Public Health also said another manufacturer, Kroger Company had also voluntarily recalled its Private Selection Shelled Pistachios, although it was unclear if its products were linked to Setton Farms.

    Singapore's AVA said that although 39 per cent of all pistachios imported into the country comes from the US, none of the affected products are sold here.

    Salmonella contamination can cause serious infections in people with weak immune systems, such as children and the elderly. - CNA/ms
