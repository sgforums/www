    What Does Halal Certification Typically Involve?

    • Typically, it involves ensuring that your premises, operations and ingredients comply with halal certification standards. Below are some examples:

      Operations
      Your operations – including production lines, kitchen utensils and equipment, crockery and storage areas – must be ritually cleansed by Muis-appointed personnel if they have been used to prepare pork or pork-related items.

      Operational Manpower
      You must appoint at least 2 permanent Muslim employees as Halal Liaison Officers to ensure that all halal terms and conditions are met and maintained at all times.

      Ingredients
      Alcohol and pork cannot be on your menu or used as an ingredient. All ingredients must be halal, that is, all meat and poultry must be halal-slaughtered and any other ingredients must be halal certified/licensed.
