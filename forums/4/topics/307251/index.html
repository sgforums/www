    Visited Shokudo with a friend. The place is really big, really comfy tables and chairs...got a nice "jap feel" to it. Its like marche where you get a card and go in and swipe and swipe and pay when you exit. 100 dollars penalty for losing the card of course.

     

    Good:

    Super huge range of food( teppanyaki, jap hot pot, meat skewers, udon, donburi, curry rice, fried rice, jap "yong fau foo", jap dumplings, dessert, soup, salad)...about 230 items in total according to the sunday life article ytd(the article caught my eye and made me go)

     

    price is reasonable or maybe some would consider a bit steep. For me the price is ok because I shared lots of dishes with my gf.

     

    ambience

     

    decor

     

    Bad:

    have to queue for about 30 minutes(new and popular mah)

     

    some of the popular choices were sold out and THEY DID NOT put "sold out" signs...the chefs left the counter without telling me who was waiting in front of him

     

    quality of food(personally I was satisfied with the quality of food but many of my friends who went after me claim that it was below-par, however I can safely tell you guys  not to order the vege paper hotpot cos its' tasteless)

     

     

     

    The waffle is really great...my GF asked for the butter and honey waffle but changed her mind half way so there was butter, honey and ice cream on our waffle in the end(haha beautiful mistake)...it was crazy omg. but i forgot whether the price is 5.80 or 8.50(ulps).
