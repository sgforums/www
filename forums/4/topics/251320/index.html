    A small excerpt "More on Trans fats" from my blog.

    However, have you ever wondered about the recent invasion of fried pop chicken or teriyaki chicken and a countless other forms of prepared chicken, pork ribs, pizza and more? All appeared utterly delicious to the eye, because we can never detect the presence of that whitish oil in the food.

    This is because the hydrogenated oil is fully absorbed into that piece of nugget or french fry and you cannot see it but taste that wonderfully new delicious flavour, that we or our mothers can never replicate at home. No wonder there is an increase in the number of people eating out and popping processed food in the toaster or microwave.

    But beware, not all nice to look at and eat is good for our health. The increase in heart diseases, type II diabetes and high cholesterol due to trans fat is on the raise. One example of the effects of trans fats vs saturated fats came from the "Walter Willett Nurses Study" (Professor of Medicine, Harvard Medical School). The study of 80,000 women concluded that a 2% increase in trans fats increased a woman's risk of heart disease by 93%, while the same study found that a 5% increase in saturated fats increased heart disease risk by 17%. . Some clinical studies suggest a possible association of trans fatty acids with obesity, metabolic syndrome and diabetes. Trans fat behaves like saturated fat by raising the level of low-density lipoprotein in the blood (LDL or "bad cholesterol") which increases the risk of coronary heart disease (CHD). It has the additional effect of decreasing levels of HDL, the "good" lipoprotein which helps remove cholesterol from arteries.
