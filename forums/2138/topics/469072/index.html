Most annoying office habits revealed

    Using so-called management speak, including ‘thinking outside the box, ‘let’s touch base’ and ‘going forward’ is almost certain to wind up colleagues. 

    But it’s arriving late for meetings that tops a list of bugbears that make up the ‘nightmare colleague’, according to the Institute of Leadership and Management (ILM).

    Tardy colleagues are closely followed in irritation-value by messy ones, who leave dirty bowls or plates on their desks.

    The office gossip also gets the thumbs down, along with those who discuss confidential work matters openly. 

    And while you might think that you’ve made a heroic effort to get into work despite your stinking cold, your colleagues are unlikely to agree. Those who come into the office when they should have stayed in bed actually exasperate fellow workers.   

    Unnecessary emails or ones that are sent when you sit directly opposite someone are also likely to make colleagues want to scream.  That’s alongside leaving a mobile phone on loud and taking regular cigarette breaks.

    Workers typically spend more time with colleagues than friends or family, so it’s not surprising that after a while habits started to grate. 

    Top traits of a nightmare colleague
    1. Arrives late for meetings
    2. Leaves dirty bowls and plates on their desk after lunch
    3. Gossips about other workers
    4. Discusses confidential work matters openly
    5. Sends you an email when they’re sitting directly opposite
    6. Leaves their mobile phone on loud
    7. Takes regular cigarette breaks
    8. Comes into work sick when they should have stayed in bed
    9. Brings their children into the office 



    "When office-based teams work in close proximity for long periods of time, we see that seemingly trivial issues can grow disproportionately, if left unchecked, and begin to cause upset and resentment," said Charles Elvin, chief executive of the ILM.

    The tea-break is a particular a source of tension between teams and their manager, as more than a quarter of bosses reveal they have never got a round in for their team.

    Office clothing also creates friction, as a similar number of managers have had to reprimand staff for inappropriate clothing, including attire that’s too revealing and flip flops. 

    However, the survey also revealed it doesn’t take much to brighten the work day. Birthday cake is ranked as the top pick-me-up, followed by free tea and coffee.

    Simple gestures also mean a lot. Receiving a 'thank you' when a job is well done means the most to UK workers, along with team mates who take the time to say hello and goodbye each day.
