    Singapore Flyer Cross 1 million vistors mark

    The Singapore Flyer has seen more than a million visitors since it started operations five months ago.

    Of the number of tickets sold, half were bought by tourists and the locals made up the rest.

    Chairman of the Flyer, Florian Bollen says they are on target to achieve the 2 point 5 million visitors mark for its first year of operation.

    "Not only are we on track but we also have a very nice trend at the moment because we are up 20 per cent from last month to this month, so things are really improving and we are very happy about the situation and developments."

    On concerns over the poor businesses at the retail outlets, Mr Bollen says they are working some retailers to address this problem.

    He added that only 10 per cent of the tenants are unhappy with the sales.

    "There are certain retailers who are doing extremely well and others who are still trying to find the right target group. We are working together with these retailers to make sure that all of them are very happy and we improve their business. At the moment I can only think of 3 retailers who are really struggling to find the right target group but 80 to 90 per cent of our tenants are quite happy with the situation."

    Mr Bollen was speaking to reporters after launching the new visitors centre for tourists at the Singapore Flyer.

    The centre, to be managed by the National Association of Travel Agents Singapore and the Association of Singapore Attractions, will provide visitors with information on the various tourists places in Singapore. --938Live
