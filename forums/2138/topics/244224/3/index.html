    It's a bit hard to explain....

    But suffice to say that if it is used for all the normal activities that a mosque does (regular mass prayer sessions, sermons, etc), and it is generally recognised by the community that it is a mosque, then it is a mosque. There is no prescribed architecture that a mosque must have.

    It just so happens that most of the mosques that you see with domes and minarets follow architecture common in the Middle East. Otherwise, anything goes... like the Hajjah Fatimah Mosque along Beach Road, which has French and Moorish influences in its architecture.

    Likewise, it just so happens that mosques would usually have verses of the Quran displayed on the walls. While my home does have verses of the Quran on the walls, I don't consider as a mosque since my home doesn't function as one.
