    i personally don't believe any of it...

    let's give you the background. a stroke is basically like a heart attack, only this one happens to the brain. the tissue is deprived of oxygen, and when that happens, it becomes damaged, and if it goes on long enough, it dies. that then leads to a loss of function controlled by that tissue.

    strokes can generally be classified into two basic groups, ischemic or hemorraghic. an ischemic stroke happens when a blood vessel leading to the brain becomes partially or totally blocked, cutting off blood supply to the brain. this is the more common form of stroke and makes up about 90% of all stroke cases.

    the end result of a hemorraghic stroke is basically the same as an ischemic. the affected brain tissue is deprived of blood, this time, due to the fact that the blood vessels leading up to the brain having burst instead of becoming blocked. this can happen for a variety of reasons. this is a rarer form of stroke though, and is the more deadly of the two, the mortality rate is about 75%.

    because of all this, i just can't see how pricking the ten fingers can help a stroke patient. also, the email got it wrong by bringing up the point of the patient's mouth being crooked. that happens only AFTER the stroke has occured, specifically about 12 to 24 hours after, and depending on severity of damage. also, capillaries don't rupture as easily as they say it does. besides, you need a lot of capillaries rupturing before you can get that kind of effect. the best thing to do if you think that someone is having a stroke or a heart attack is to get the person to a hospital IMMEDIATELY. the chances of surving such an attack deteriorates drastically once it's past the 1st hour since the onset of symptoms set in. so try your best to get the person into a hospital within the hour...

    hope that helps...
