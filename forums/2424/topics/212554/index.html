    Van der Sar set for Newcastle

    Edwin van der Sar insists United need to rediscover the art of beating the Premiership's less-fancied teams, starting on Sunday.

    The Reds' failure to match Chelsea's charge to the Premiership title last season came about largely through a failure to do just that.

    And, following last week's draw at promoted Reading, this weekend's clash with Newcastle has taken on extra significance as the Reds aim to stay in touch with the champions.

    "Reading was the kind of game we needed to win," said van der Sar.

    "It was matches like that which cost us last year. We believe we have matured, so the focus is now on Newcastle and ensuring the same thing does not happen again."

    Van der Sar appreciates the task will be aided by the return of Gabriel Heinze, who caught the eye against Benfica in only his second game back from injury.

    "Gaby is a quality player," he said. "He came back into the team against Reading last week and then did really well against Benfica.

    "He has been injured for a long time but we are all hoping he can stay fit now because he is such a huge asset to the team."
