    Rui Costa and Nuno Assis have been ruled out of Benfica's UEFA Champions League match with Manchester United on Tuesday.

    Fergie backing for key duo

    Sir Alex Ferguson has backed Wayne Rooney and Cristiano Ronaldo to star for Manchester United against Benfica.

    Rooney has been below his best so far this season and has not scored in Europe for nine games, but Ferguson is confident he will deliver in Tuesday's UEFA Champions League clash.

    Ferguson believes the England striker, who has had to serve a three-match ban for a pre-season red card, is still on the way back to fitness following his broken metatarsal.

    "I am not concerned about Wayne's form at all," he said. "All he needs is games.

    "He did well at Reading on Saturday. He showed a big improvement. There was far more sharpness from him and I am sure that progress will continue."

    Ronaldo, meanwhile, is expected to receive a similarly hostile reception to last season, when the Benfica boo boys eventually prompted him to lose his temper and respond with a 'one-fingered' salute.

    The Portuguese winger has ignored taunts from rival fans in The Premiership this season, and Ferguson feels he will continue to show his growing maturity.

    "I have not spoken to the boy," said Ferguson "I don't need to. Cristiano is intelligent and he understands the situation.

    "He has been fantastic for us so far this season, probably our best player. He is growing up terrifically and has a fantastic future ahead of him."

    Defender Rio Ferdinand, meanwhile, has insisted it is time for United to start making an impact in Europe.

    Ferdinand was a Champions League semi-finalist with Leeds, but has so far been unable to match that achievement at Old Trafford.

    "I joined Manchester United to win trophies, and part of that was winning European trophies," said the 28-year-old.

    "The Champions League is a great opportunity to showcase your talent and we have not managed to do that."

    United crashed out in the group stages last season, and Ferdinand is eager to avoid another shock exit.

    "I do not want to be in that situation again," he stressed.

    "It was a very disappointing experience, both individually and collectively. But what we have to try and do is turn the negatives into positives.

    "The last couple of years have been part of a transitional period for us.

    "We have been trying to build up the experience with new players coming in. Now it is time for us to go out and prove our worth as individuals and as a team."
