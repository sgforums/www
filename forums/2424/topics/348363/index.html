    EVANS BLOW LEAVES FERGIE SHORT

     

      

     

    Manchester United face a defensive injury crisis ahead of Tuesday's clash with Inter Milan after Jonny Evans limped off against Blackburn.

    The 2-1 win against Rovers on Saturday evening fired the Red Devils eight points clear at the top of the Premier League table but left manager Sir Alex Ferguson bemoaning a lengthening injury list ahead of Tuesday's Champions League showdown with Jose Mourinho's side.

    Nemanja Vidic is suspended and both Gary Neville and Wes Brown have already been ruled out. Now Ferguson seems certain to be without Evans, who limped off immediately after Cristiano Ronaldo's winner.

    It means Ferguson is now crossing his fingers that John O'Shea recovers from a sore heel in time to play against Jose Mourinho's side at the San Siro.

    "It is a nightmare," admitted the United chief.

    "Somewhere I have to find two centre-backs but it is looking very doubtful because Jonny is unlikely to play now."

    If O'Shea does not make it, Ferguson faces the uncomfortable prospect of calling up reserve team rookie James Chester or playing one of his more experienced players out of position.

    Javier Zanetti: Inter Are Not Scared Of Manchester United

    As the words and mind games continue to rebound from end to end, Zanetti has added his opinion of the United showdown...

     

    Inter captain Javier Zanetti has echoed Jose Mourinho's words by insisting his side are not scared of Manchester United, and they will play to beat them when the two giants tussle for Champions League supremacy on Tuesday.

    The Nerazzurri welcome Sir Alex Ferguson's men hoping to put one foot in the quarter-final stage and head to Old Trafford with a positive aggregate score. While all the talk has been of United's incredible form, Zanetti believes Inter have the qualities to match the Premier League side on every level.

    "We will be playing a great team and they are doing well in both the Champions League and their domestic campaign," Zanetti told Inter's official website.

    "United are competitive and it will be a match between two great teams who are trying to extend their runs in Europe.

    "Inter has all the right cards to win this challenge. We are always aiming high and the game tomorrow will be a chance for us to show the world that we can win."

    The papers in England have reported fear coming from Mourinho's camp, but Zanetti is not shaking as he eyes a result which will boost his side in every sense.

    "We are not scared of any team and we can do it. This game arrives at a good time for us, and I think we can get the win," concluded the captain.
