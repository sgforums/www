    Mourinho: Difficult Result For Inter And Manchester United

    Inter and Manchester United played out a scoreless draw at the San Siro in the first leg of their intriguing Champions League encounter, as Jose Mourinho and Sir Alex Ferguson faced off once again in their careers.

    "Manchester United is not Bologna and going to play there in the Champions [League] will not be like playing an away match in the championship," Nerazzurri coach Jose Mourinho said to the microphones of SKY, following the match.

    "The 0-0 result is difficult for everyone. We cannot lose, but they cannot have a scoring draw or they will be out."

    'The Special One' they went on to analyze the match for the reporters: "Manchester did better in the first half and us in the second. With [Ivan] Cordoba [who replaced Nelson Rivas at half-time], we played a higher line and the defense was better and more compact.

    "I am not sorry for the result, as it would have been ugly to have to go to Old Trafford and chase the match. Instead, we are going there to play."

    Mourinho concluded by praising 18-year-old Davide Santon, who did an admirable job defending Cristiano Ronaldo, especially in the second-half.

    "He gave a fantastic response in a Champions League match against the world champions," hailed the former Porto boss.
