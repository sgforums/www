    DIMITAR BERBATOV celebrates his 30th birthday tomorrow - by setting a target to finish the season with a goal for every year.

    Manchester United's record signing has already banged in 20 since August.

    Five of those came in the space of four days as he followed Saturday's hat-trick against Birmingham by netting twice in the midweek 3-2 win over Blackpool.

    And boss Alex Ferguson sees no reason why the £30.7million hitman cannot continue his current hot streak.

    Fergie said: "Dimitar is playing with great confidence at the moment.

    "When strikers are in that kind of form you hope it lasts a long time. He could easily get to the 30 mark."

    Berbatov's future has been the subject of speculation with 18 months on his current deal left. But Fergie dropped a strong hint the striker will get sorted out - and suggested he could play on at the top for a while yet.

    Berbatov has never had tons of pace so Ferguson is not concerned about him slowing down in the twilight of his career.

    Fergie said: "He's under contract at the moment. Is he going to stay? Yes, it's not an issue.

    "You don't necessarily need to be quick to be a top striker.

    "Teddy Sheringham had no pace, Ruud van Nistelrooy wasn't quick but he was a really good goalscorer. It's what happens in the penalty box."
