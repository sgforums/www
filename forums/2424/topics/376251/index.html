    Owen: I've proved I'm a Red

     
     

    Owen's injury-time winner gave Manchester United victory in a staggering seven-goal derby extravaganza against City

     

    MICHAEL OWEN hopes he has proven his dedication to Manchester United with his dramatic last-gasp winner against Manchester City.

    The England striker played eight years at arch-rivals Liverpool, before his career stalled at Real Madrid and Newcastle. But the 29-year-old, signed on a free transfer this summer by Alex Ferguson, scored his second goal for the club to seal a 4-3 derby win.

    And asked whether that would confirm hero status with the Old Trafford faithful, Owen said: "Yes, if there was any doubt before then maybe.

    "It was always going to create noise when I joined but even if the fans didn't take to me you're never going to pass up an opportunity to play for team like this."

    Despite needing his late intervention to seal all three points, Owen believes the win was richly deserved. He added: "We dominated and the lads played well for a lot of the game.

    "There were individual mistakes but over the years before I was here the defence has been fantastic." Two-goal Darren Fletcher added: "It was an unbelievable match, we showed great character to come back. "The goals were mistakes but we look at the positives — we showed character to the end."
