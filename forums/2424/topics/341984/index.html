    Tottenham Hotspur manager Harry Redknapp has identified Manchester United's Ben Foster as the back-up he needs for Heurelho Gomes.

    Tottenham Hotspur are preparing to make an offer for Manchester United goalkeeper Ben Foster in the January transfer window, according to a report in the British tabloid the Sun.

    Apparently, Spurs manager Harry Redknapp feels that he will provide healthy competition for current White Hart Lane number one, Heurelho Gomes.

    Gomes, who has been capped on 17 occassions by Brazil, endured a torrid start to his goalkeeping career in England following a series of blunders.

    Although the 27-year-old's form has improved significantly recently, it is thought that Redknapp remains keen to add some cover to the goalkeeping department.

    Edwin van der Sar is the current first choice at Old Trafford and with Foster having to compete with Tomasz Kuszczak for the back-up role, his chances of regular first-team football with the Red Devils are very limited.

    Furthermore, the England international is believed to be out of contract at the end of this season and while United are reported to have offered a new £15,000-a-week contract, Spurs are claimed to be offering £20,000.
