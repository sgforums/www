    Premier XI: Right back

    Since the Premier League's inception in 1992, only two United players have held down a regular spot at right back. Which one will you select?

    Paul Parker
    Apps: 76 (3)
    Goals: 1
    A speedy and tenacious defender, the diminutive Londoner was a crisp passer of the ball who possessed uncanny positional sense. Parker was solid and rarely flamboyant but was plagued by injuries in the latter half of his United career. Nevertheless, he made 79 Premier League appearances and was integral in the historic 1992/93 title victory.

    Gary Neville
    Apps: 349 (15)
    Goals: 5
    Red through and through, Gary Neville made the right back position his own when injuries stunted Paul Parker's career. Committed to a fault and tough in the tackle, Neville's defensive qualities are abundant. Following David Beckham's departure in 2002, he also began getting forward more frequently and often delivers telling balls from wide positions.
