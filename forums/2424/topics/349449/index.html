    Cristiano Ronaldo is bullish about Manchester United’s chances of overcoming Inter in the Champions League in a fortnight’s time.

    The two sides played out a goalless draw in a game of contrasting fortunes on Tuesday night.

    While United held sway in the first half, Inter were the dominant force after the break and the tie is evenly poised heading into the second leg.

    World and European Player of the Year Ronaldo’s one regret from the game is failing to grab an away goal, but he feels United have the quality to secure safe passage to the quarter finals.

    ”We played nice football and showed we are a great team,” Ronaldo told the Daily Telegraph. “If we play like that in Manchester I think we win.

    ”I'm very proud. The lads played brilliant, like a real team. The only bad thing is we didn't score, but that is football.

    ”We respect them but in my opinion we have a better chance at Old Trafford because we have a better team."
