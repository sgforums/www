Ferguson Expects A Maturer Nani

  • Dead_Man_Inc
    12 Aug 08, 00:12

    Manchester United's Portuguese signing of last summer, Nani, 21, has drawn likely comparisons to compatriot Cristiano Ronaldo.

    Both were schooled by Sporting CP; both endured similar journeys within their first season; and whilst the latter lit up the Premier League last season and drew plaudits from all pundits, the former has the potential to do so.

    Perhaps the part of his game that still needs tuning though is his mentality. His accusations of disrespect from Arsenal, together with his needless headbutt aimed at Lucas Neill and consequent sending off against West Ham United in the penultimate league match last season, are testament to that.

    Sir Alex Ferguson is calling on his number 17 to not only step up his game a level this coming season, but also to add a maturer outlook too. Quoted in the Manchester Evening News, the Red Devils boss said: "Nani has gone through the same process all the young players do, and you are looking for that jump up in performance level from the first season."

    Speaking about areas of improvement, Ferguson added: "His maturity should be better - which is important. He is still young, but when he develops he will be an outstanding talent. He has courage and speed, can play with both feet and does not melt in big atmospheres."

    Nani is suspended for the first two games of the season due to the aforementioned red card.
