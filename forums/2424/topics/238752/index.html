    Cristiano Ronaldo and Ryan Giggs have both been passed fit for Manchester UnitedÂ’s Barclays Premiership clash with Fulham at Craven Cottage.

    Ronaldo sustained a foot injury in the Champions League win over Lille on Tuesday, while match-winner Giggs suffered a minor knock.

    However, Sir Alex Ferguson expects both men to be part of a full-strength squad as United look to establish a nine-point lead over Chelsea.

    Fulham have lost Franck Queudrue for the lunchtime kick-off against the Red Devils.

    Queudrue suffered a badly bruised ankle in training and his place at left-back will be taken by Liam Rosenior, who was left out last week for the first time this season because manager Chris Coleman thought he needed a rest.

    Fulham - beaten 5-1 at Old Trafford on the opening day of the season - have captain Michael Brown available again after suspension, but will have to do without Vincenzo Montella after his sending-off against Tottenham Hotspur and Ian Pearce is still unavailable.
