    I'm pleased we stood firm

    ALEX FERGUSON was content with a point after seeing Manchester United survive a first-half battering at Sunderland.

    United battled their way to a 0-0 draw at the Stadium of Light with Edwin van der Sar making a vital save to deny Steed Malbranque.

    Bolo Zenden also struck a post as Steve Bruce's men went for the kill before the break.

     

    The United boss said: "Sunderland had the most chances in the game and I have to say we were fantastic defensively.

    "That's the area in which we have been leaking goals and throwing games away this season, so I am pleased with that.

    "It was a very determined Sunderland team. Their home record this season has been very, very good, so I am not pleased but I am satisfied."

    Sunderland largely controlled the first half and would have been ahead with 16 minutes gone had Van der Sar not pulled off a fine save to deny Malbranque.

    Ferguson, who had left Wayne Rooney at home, replaced Michael Owen with Dimitar Berbatov at half-time.

    And the change quickly helped United look more threatening.

    Fergie added: "We had no attacking purpose in the first 25 minutes of the match.

    "It wasn't until towards the end of the first half that we got any semblance of keeping possession of the ball.

    "In the second half we did a lot better, there was more threat by us, but they had chances from set-pieces."
