    Bottom plays top in a test for the belief that any team in the Premier League can beat any other. United, still unbeaten, have won 15 of their 24 matches, Wolves have lost 15 of theirs and even in a two-horse race, it would take a brave punter to back Mick McCarthy's selling platers. The league leaders are strengthened by the return of first-choice right-back, Rafael, after suspension and Darren Fletcher is fit to resume in midfield. More significantly, they have Wayne Rooney scoring goals again after those two against Stoke.
