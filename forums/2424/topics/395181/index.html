    Wayne Rooney may recover from injury in time for Bayern Munich tie

    • United forward was expected to be out for two to three weeks
    • Club's medical staff only rate his chances of playing as '40%'

    Wayne Rooney could play against Bayern Munich but Sir Alex Ferguson does not want to risk aggravating his ankle ligament injury. 

     

     

    Wayne Rooney has been tipped to make a shock return for Manchester United ahead of Wednesday's crucial Champions League match against Bayern Munich.

    The striker was expected to be out for at least two to three weeks after he injured ankle ligaments in last week's first leg in Germany, which United lost 2-1. However, according to reports last night, Rooney has responded well to treatment and could be in line for a surprise recall to Sir Alex Ferguson's squad if he comes through a training session today.

    Rooney has been wearing a protective boot on his injured leg since hobbling off at the end of last week's defeat in Munich. Ferguson will not risk the England forward if there is a chance of aggravating the injury further and last night United's medical staff attempted to play down the chances of a comeback by only rating his chances of playing as "40%".

    Rooney's return would be a boost for a United side that lost 2-1 to Chelsea in the Premier League without him on Saturday. They now trail Carlo Ancelotti's side by two points with five games left to play.

    Sir Alex Ferguson will not risk Wayne Rooney in Champions League quarter-final



     






    Sir Alex Ferguson says he is not prepared to risk Wayne Rooney in Manchester United's Champions League quarter-final second leg tie against Bayern Munich tomorrow night.

    There was speculation this morning that the England striker could make a shock return at Old Trafford despite having sprained his ankle in the first leg last week. However, Ferguson dismissed those reports by claiming Rooney had "no chance" of featuring, before admitting there was a slim chance the striker could be involved as a substitute.

    "He's got no chance," Ferguson said. "We've made some good progress with the lad, the medical team have done fantastic, but I'm not prepared to take a risk on a player who is not 100 per cent fit."

    Ferguson then added: "The boy is making good progress. The medical team have done a fantastic job but it is an ankle injury the same as any other ankle injury. You can't take a risk with a player who is not 100 per cent."

    Rooney was originally thought to be sidelined for two or three weeks but the striker's recovery has been quicker than anticipated, which led to rumours that he might be rushed back for the must-win match.

    One piece of good news for Ferguson is the likely availability of John O'Shea, who has not played since suffering complications in his recovery from a dead leg sustained in Ireland's World Cup play-off defeat byFrance in November.
