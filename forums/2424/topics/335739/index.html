    Cristiano set to be Prem's biggest earner

    CASHING IN - Cristiano Ronaldo

     

     

    MANCHESTER UNITED are ready to make Cristiano Ronaldo the highest-paid player in Premier League history to stop him joining Real Madrid. 

    The Portuguese winger still has over three years left on his current £120,000-a-week deal. However, a FIFA ruling means he can buy himself out of his contract at the end of next season. So United are ready to offer him a staggering £150,000-a-week deal next summer to keep him out of the Spanish champions’ clutches.

    Old Trafford boss Sir Alex Ferguson said: “Next summer will be a key element of his contract situation — he will have three years left then. “Nobody (at Manchester United) has ever bought himself out of his contract. I don’t think it will be an issue.” 

    FIFA’s Article 17 states a player can buy himself out of his contract by paying up his wages once he has served three years of his deal. This would allow Ronaldo to pay a £12m compensation fee and then hot- foot it to the Bernabeu. 

    But United chief executive David Gill plans to open talks with Ronaldo, 23, at the end of this season over a deal worth nearly £150,000 a week. And in a pop at Real president Ramon Calderon, Fergie added: “There is no question of Ronaldo leaving. 

    “Calderon promised everyone they would get the lad in the summer. There was a complete arrogance about it. So I was delighted Ronaldo stayed. “We are ready for more shots. We have enough ammunition ready.”
