    FOSTER STILL EXPECTING UNITED LOAN EXIT



    Man. Utd 1/6, Draw 9/2, Bolton 12/1

    Ben Foster is still expecting to spend some time on loan away from Manchester United despite making his debut for the Red Devils this weekend.

     

    With Edwin van der Sar injured and Tomasz Kuszczak suspended against Derby on Saturday, Foster was handed his United debut between the sticks.

    Despite impressing in the 1-0 victory over the lowly Rams, Foster has no thoughts of finishing the season as the club's first choice goalkeeper.

    The former Watford loanee does not think it would be right to be bumped ahead of either keeper in the Sir Alex Ferguson's pecking order, although he is still keen to play first-team football.

    "I've only been back from injury two weeks and I was delighted to get the game, but I'm not looking to overtake the other guys," said Foster.

    "If I was Edwin van der Sar or Tomasz Kuszczak and I was being kept out of the team I'd be very disappointed.

    "I don't expect to be in the team. I may have to go out on loan for the rest of the season to play some games and then maybe next season it will be a different story."

    JUSSI JAASKELAINEN could have played his last game for Bolton after he was sidelined for the rest of the season with a back injury.

    Losing the Finnish keeper, who is out of contract in the summer, is another major blow for the Trotters.

    They remain in the bottom three following yesterday’s dismal defeat to 10-man Wigan.

    Boss Gary Megson admitted: “Jussi’s out for the season now. He’s been told to take six weeks’ rest to see if his back problem can be resolved.

    “After that he may need an operation — but even if he doesn’t then that’s him done for the rest of this season.”

    Jaaskelainien, 32, was crocked in the warm-up before the UEFA Cup tie with Sporting Lisbon at the Reebok 11 days ago.

    Oman keeper Ali Al Habsi has filled in since, while former England international Ian Walker is also on the books.

    Jaaskelainen has not ruled out staying at the Reebok.

    But Celtic are among a number of admiring clubs.
