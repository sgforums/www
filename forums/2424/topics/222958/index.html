    Tottenham boss Martin Jol has ruled out letting Jermain Defoe leave in January as speculation mounts on United's movements in the mid-season transfer window.

    Sir Alex Ferguson last week dropped a strong hint that he will strengthen his squad when the transfer market opens next month.

    The Reds have been linked with a host of strikers, Defoe's name included, with Fernando Torres and Klass Jan Huntelaar also widely tipped to leave their respective clubs, Atletico Madrid and Ajax.

    But, former West Ham trainee Defoe, touted to follow Reds summer signing Michael Carrick to Old Trafford for Â£12million, is off limits according to Spurs boss Jol.

    "Jermain's going nowhere, he's very important to us," he told the London Evening Standard. "I'm not going to sell someone who is playing such a crucial part to our challenge in the Carling Cup, Uefa Cup, and Premiership."

    Rumours abound because of the perception that Defoe is not a regular at White Hart Lane, but Jol would disagree with such a statement.

    "There is talk of Jermain not playing regularly but he has started six of the last nine games," he added. "Robbie Keane has only started three games in the Premiership lately, but nobody is talking about him wanting to go."
