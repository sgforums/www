    Manchester United Ready To Cut Short Tom Cleverley Loan Spell At Wigan Athletic

    Sir Alex Ferguson believes youngster can challenge for first-team role

    Tom Cleverley - Manchester United (Getty Images)

     

     

     

    Manchester United boss Sir Alex Ferguson is considering bringing young midfielder Tom Cleverley back early from his current loan at Wigan Athletic.

    The 21-year-old midfielder is on loan with the Latics for the season, but United have an option to recall him in January — and the Scot is intent on using it.

    "We'll probably bring Tom back in January. Tom's going to be a great footballer, I really like him," Ferguson told Inside United.

    "I've heard the fans are starting to talk about Tom, and I think they're right to do that because he could very well be the next top player to come through the ranks."

    After impressing during United's pre-season tour of the United States, many had expected Cleverley to be included in the club's first-team squad from the start of the season. But Ferguson belives the decision to give him six months exposure to Premier League football at the DW Stadium was the right one.

    "I felt it could be hard to give him enough football," he said, when reviewing the decision to allow his temporary exit.

    "I think five or six months on loan could really help him. He'll benefit from first-team football in the Premier League."

    Cleverley, who previously enjoyed a successful long-term loan spell at Championship side Watford, has been able to make just one appearance for Wigan so far this team due to a knee injury.
