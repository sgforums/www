    Premier League statement re: G Heinze

    The Premier League has issued the following statement on the case of Gabriel Heinze v Manchester United FC.

    The Premier League Board appointed panel, having heard submissions and evidence from both parties, has ruled to dismiss Gabriel Heinze's (the Player) case.

    The Hearing concluded that nature and intention of the disputed 13 June 2007 letter (the Letter), especially when taken in context of verbal discussions and Manchester United FC's (the Club) transfer policy, was unambiguous in that it envisages only an international transfer.

    Furthermore the Hearing finds the Letter constitutes an 'agreement to agree', and did not create an obligation or binding agreement for the Club to transfer the Player to any particular club. In other words the Letter is evidence of an intention to negotiate, both between the parties and with potential buying clubs, and not evidence of any intention to create legal relations.

    Any appeal will be heard by the Premier League Appeals Committee, which is made up of an independent legally qualified Chair, a member of the Premier League Panel and a PFA appointee.
