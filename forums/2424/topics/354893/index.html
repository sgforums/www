    Federico Macheda profile: Who is the new Manchester United star?

     

    Federico Macheda arrived in the Premier League with a bang on Sunday as he scored the winner from the bench to get Manchester United’s title chase back on track.

    Federico Macheda mini-compilation

    But who is the teen wonder?

    1) Federico Macheda was born in Rome on 22 August 1991, making him just 17 on his Premier League debut.

    2) His team mates know him as Kiko

    3) He started out in Lazio’s youth system but joined Manchester United in 2007. He signed professional forms last August.

    4) Kiko started out in the U18s, scoring on his debut against Barnsley. He ended last season as top scorer with 12 goals, including this strike against Everton.

    5) Macheda was part of United’s 2008 Milk Cup-winning team, scoring in the final 

    6) After making his reserve team debut in February 2008, he has appeared more often this season. Stepping up to the reserves has suited him – 10 goals making him top scorer in the Barclays Premier Reserve League (North), including a hat trick against Newcastle on Monday which helped push him into Sir Alex Ferguson’s thinking for the Villa match.

    7) Perhaps we shouldn’t be surprised Macheda turned into a supersub – the reserves are managed by Ole Gunnar Solskjaer.

    8) Macheda was added to United’s 30 man Champions League squad at the start of February

    9) At the final whistle on Sunday he had 640 fans on Facebook.

    Lazio chief: We couldn't keep Macheda

    In the wake of Federico Macheda’s remarkable debut display for Manchester United on Sunday, Lazio president Claudio Lotito has called for Italian clubs to be afforded more protection regarding their young talents.

    Macheda inscribed his name into the national consciousness with a wonderful winning goal in injury time of the five-goal thriller against Aston Villa, taking just 30 minutes of professional football to open his account for United.

    While his crucial input into United’s title challenge has made unexpected headlines in England, the Lazio staff are well aware of the player’s potential having seen him lured away from their youth setup aged just 16 in September 2007.

    The manner of Macheda’s departure is hardly novel – United and their Premier League rivals have frequently exploited contractual laws across the continent to recruit the finest young talents available and indeed Sir Alex Ferguson signed another young Italian, David Petrucci, from Lazio’s great rivals Roma in June.

    Now Lotito is adamant that changes must be made to the current system that affords Premier League clubs the opportunity to sign players from abroad at the age of 16.

    "We tried in every way to stop Manchester United from taking away our player but the present Italian regulations don't give you any type of defence," said Lotito.

    "In reality we find ourselves in front of a proper cattle market.

    "I talked repeatedly with Macheda's parents, we established a dialogue, but it was not possible to do anything.

    “We could not compete with United's offer. That is not right, we have to be provided with more reliable and concrete rules than those which govern Italian clubs."

    Uefa president Michel Platini has declared his strong opposition to players moving abroad at such a young age and previously instructed the European Commission to address the matter.

    Macheda is "new van Basten"

    Federico Macheda's father reckons his son can become the new Marco van Basten after his stunning debut on Sunday.

    The Manchester United rookie was thrown on against Aston Villa as Sir Alex Ferguson gambled on the untried 17-year-old to add punch to his attack.

    Macheda impressed and came up trumps in stoppage time with an exquisite turn and curling shot beyond the despairing reach of Brad Friedel to spark wild scenes of celebration at Old Trafford.

    After running to hug his dad Pasquale, which earned him a yellow card, Macheda will have to be content with a place on the bench for Tuesday's Champions League clash with Porto.

    However, the praise is ringing in his ears after his crucial strike took United back to the top of the table.

    "Ever since he was a young boy, I have watched my boy and thought of Marco van Basten," Pasquale told The Sun.

    "In the past 18 months, he's developed enormously, physically and technically at United.
