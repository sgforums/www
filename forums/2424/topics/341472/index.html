    Stoke City v Man Utd

     

     

    Stoke keeper Thomas Sorensen returns after a scan revealed no serious damage to his injured fingers.

    City have a string of other injury concerns, however, with Dave Kitson and Andy Griffin (both knee), Salif Diao (calf) and Leon Cort (groin) doubtful.

    Manchester United defender Patrice Evra starts a four-match ban and is likely to be replaced by John O'Shea.

    Otherwise, Sir Alex Ferguson has no new fresh injury or suspension worries to contend with.

     

    Stoke (from): Sorensen, Shawcross, Sonko, Abdoulaye Faye, Higginbotham, Delap, Amdy Faye, Whelan, Cresswell, Pugh, Fuller, Pericard, Tonge, Soares, Simonsen, Olofinjana, Davies, Wilkinson, Griffin, Diao, Cort, Kitson.

    Man Utd (from): Van der Sar, Kuszczak, Amos, Neville, Ferdinand, Vidic, O'Shea, Evans, Rafael, Ronaldo, Anderson, Giggs, Park, Carrick, Nani, Scholes, Fletcher, Gibson, Berbatov, Rooney, Tevez, Welbeck.

     

     

    Stoke manager Tony Pulis:

    "We'll get 28,000 people there, the place will be rocking and I'm just delighted for everybody in the city - it's good times. "We'll expect a tough game - we know what they're like and what players they've got. Whichever team Sir Alex Ferguson picks, they're full of quality. "If we work hard enough and we have a good slice of luck on the day then you never know what might happen."

    Manchester United midfielder Michael Carrick believes Stoke could provide more of a threat than Liga de Quito, who they beat to become Club World Cup winners:
    "Quito was a test but Stoke will be tough. "They will make it a battle and try to ensure it is not easy for us. They will probably come at us a little bit more than Quito did. It will be hard."

     

    HEAD TO HEAD

    Last months reverse fixture was Stoke's second successive 5-0 drubbing at Old Trafford in top-flight football, spanning 23 years. But the Potters' last two home games against the Red Devils have both been 2-1 wins; first in the old First Division on Boxing Day 1984, then in the first leg of a second round League Cup tie on 22 September 1993.

    Stoke won the first ever league match against Manchester United 7-1 on 7 January 1893 (top-flight).

     

    at Stoke only
    League (inc PL): Stoke 15 wins, Man Utd 9, Draws 11
    Prem: Stoke 0 wins, Man Utd 0, Draws 0

    15 November 2008 - Ref: Peter Walton
    Man Utd scorers: Ronaldo 3, Carrick 45, Berbatov 49, Welbeck 84, Ronaldo 89

     

    REFEREE

    Chris Foy (St Helens, Merseyside)
