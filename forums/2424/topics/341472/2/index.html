    Sir Alex Ferguson Pleased With Resiliant And Energetic Performance

    The Manchester United boss gave his post match reactions to Sky Sports, and confirmed that Rio Ferdinand will be sidelined for the next Premier League game.

    For lengthy period's of Boxing Day's curtain raising Premier League game, it looked as though Stoke City would battle to a well-earned draw at the Britannia Stadium.

    Manchester United though refused to accept the draw and, perhaps buoyed by the sending off of Andy Wilkinson, managed to break a deadlock that lasted for over 80 minutes of play.

    Argentine international Carlos Tevez was the hero of the day as he took advantage of a Dimitar Berbatov pass that he duly poked home to seal the win, and close the gap at the top of the table to just four points.

    Speaking to Sky Sports after the game, United boss Sir Alex Ferguson reflected on the win:

    "At this stage of the season they are all big wins, we've just got back from Japan and were wondering how we would handle it but we coped very well, and the energy was good.

    "Sometimes you have to be patient in games like this, and we took the risk from playing Ryan at left back."

    When asked whether the trip to Japan will have caused his squad some unwanted tiredness, he said: "Not at all, they showed great energy.

    "I was thinking this could end up a draw... but Carlos scores important goals."

    Regarding the change of dynamic when Wilkinson was dismissed for two bookable offences, Sir Alex added: "The sending off didn't give us a lift, just a numerical advantage. It may have effected them; it's difficult to say."

    Prior to kick off Jonny Evans replaced Rio Ferdinand in the line up, who suffered a back complaint. "He'll be out against 'Boro. We've got to get to the bottom of this problem," Ferguson confirmed.

    "When you get to New Year's day, and if we are only a couple of points adrift, then we'll be happy with it [the club's standing in the league]. Then we can push on from there," he concluded.
