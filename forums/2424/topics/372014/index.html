    Evans knock a worry for United

    UNITED have a defensive headache ahead of their Premier League opener against Birmingham on Sunday.

    With Nemanja Vidic already ruled out, stand-in Jonny Evans returned from Northern Ireland duty with an ankle problem.

    And, with Reds boss Sir Alex Ferguson already denied the services of young full-back Rafael and Wes Brown and Gary Neville also strggling for fitness, the champions face a race to get him ready for action.

    Northern Ireland manager Nigel Worthington left the young centre-half out of the team that drew 1-1 with Israel at Windsor Park on Thursday night to allow him to return to United for treatment.

    “There was no way I was going to risk him from our point of view,” said Worthington.

    “With him being in and around the first team, I wasn’t going to risk him with Manchester United either. Jonny trained extremely well on Monday and Tuesday but woke up with a stiff ankle on the day of the game.”

    Goalkeeper Ben Foster is also battling to be fit after withdrawing from the England squad earlier this week with a minor injury. First choice Edwin Van der Sar is already ruled out for up to two months with a broken hand.

    PLEA ... Alex McLeish

     

    ALEX McLEISH has already begged one favour from mentor Alex Ferguson - tickets for tomorrow's curtain-raiser at Old Trafford.

    The Birmingham boss needed to find seats for 15 members of his family and close friends. One call to Manchester United boss Fergie and Big Eck was sorted.

    McLeish revealed: "I haven't spoken to Alex this week but I asked him for a few tickets last week and he obliged. "I've family and friends coming down from Scotland. A few came out of the woodwork when they saw the first fixture!

    "So that means I'll be in hock to Alex until he comes to St Andrew's. "My son Jon is using a friend's season ticket so I've told him to sit on his hands. My son Jamie and daughter Rebecca will be there too."

    McLeish played for Ferguson at Aberdeen and admits Fergie's aura has left a lasting impression on him. He added: "I'm my own man but when you play under a manager like that you can't help but pick up things.

    "He set standards pretty high and I have tried to keep to those standards. He called us in one Sunday when we conceded a corner! "He had this amazing will to win and players like me, Willie Miller, Gordon Strachan, Mark McGhee and Jim Leighton were enhanced by his guidance.

    "Nobody is as good at doing the hairdryer! I've tried it. If you feel it, let it go. But if you're making it up then it won't feel right. "He is a great psychologist. He would be quite expensive if he charged - but he is my pal."
