    NO WORRIES ... Ian Holloway

    IAN HOLLOWAY'S Blackpool are confident they will beat the big freeze for the visit of Manchester United on Saturday despite having no undersoil heating.

    The Bloomfield Road pitch has been covered all week even though the Fylde coast has not seen the worst of the wintry conditions.

    Even so, temperatures are unlikely to rise above freezing over the next three days.

    A club spokesman said: "We've not had the snow they've had in other parts of the country. We've just had a ground frost.

    "The pitch is covered and we've not got any worries at this moment."

    The way United were hammered 4-0 by West Ham in the snow on Tuesday will encourage boss Ollie to do everything possible to get the game on.

    Blackpool's Premier League clash with Manchester United on Saturday has been postponed after the Bloomfield Road pitch fell foul of adverse weather conditions sweeping the UK.

    Overnight frost and temperatures of below minus eight have left parts of the Blackpool pitch frozen solid and despite groundstaff working around the clock referee Peter Walton called off the match on Friday after consulting with club officials.

    Mr Walton expressed concern that the severely affected areas of the playing surface would not thaw sufficiently for the game to be completed.

    Blackpool have the pitch covered, with hot air blowers doing their best to thaw the turf, but continued freezing conditions are expected to make the problem worse.

    Manchester United defender Rio Ferdinand said on his Twitter page: "Blackpool game tomorrow called off due to the frozen pitch, so gutted tweeps. "
