    The tension may be at its peak well before Manchester United take the field. Their hopes rest with Manchester City, who lead the Premier League on goal difference, faltering at Newcastle United, who shocked Chelsea by winning 2-0 at Stamford Bridge on Wednesday night. Such an outcome would see Old Trafford brimming with euphoria before the match there has even begun. Swansea's season has been excellent, but their form has faded of late, with just a single victory for Brendan Rodgers' side in the last seven matches.
