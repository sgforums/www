    Wayne Rooney sought the advice of former West Ham youngsters Michael Carrick and Joe Cole to ensure Giuseppe Rossi is in good hands under Newcastle boss Glenn Roeder.

    Rooney rates his Reds team-mate highly, so much so he believes the tiny Italian to be the club's most clinical finisher. However, the 19-year-old needs regular Premiership football, so he will spend at least four months on loan at St James' Park.

    Roeder helped develop a clutch of current Premiership stars when he worked with the Upton Park club's academy, and was given glowing tributes from his former charges.

    "I have talked to the likes of Michael Carrick and Joe Cole about the way Glenn Roeder brought them through at West Ham and the feeling at Old Trafford is that Giuseppe is in good hands with Newcastle," said Rooney.

    Rossi, who was on Italian Under-21 international duty this week, should make his Magpies debut against Fulham this weekend. And Rooney is convinced he will be a hit at St James' Park.

    "Giuseppe is a great player and one to watch out for," he added. "His touch, feel of the ball and awareness are brilliant. He is perhaps the best finisher at United and will only get better."
