Blues don't worry Carrick

  • mcfadden
    6 Feb 07, 19:11
    Michael Carrick says Manchester United are not looking over their shoulders at the title threat posed by Chelsea.

    United currently hold a six-point lead over the reigning champions after both clubs picked up away wins at the weekend.

    Chelsea briefly reduced the advantage to three points by beating Charlton Athletic 1-0, but the leaders responded by thrashing Tottenham Hotspur 4-0 just 24 hours later.

    Carrick is delighted with his side's current form and insists the club's focus is not being distracted by events at Stamford Bridge.

    "Chelsea's challenge was always going to be strong and it could be stronger now they have Terry and Cech back in the side," said Carrick.

    "But it's not something we focus on.

    "We're in a good position. We've earned that and we don't want to let it slip. It's up to us to get a good result every week.

    "Chelsea have played well in big games and I think there will still be plenty of twists and turns.

    "We didn't feel any pressure after Chelsea had closed the gap on Saturday.

    "It's up to us what we do and we know if we win the right amount of games we will win the league.

    "We are not looking over our shoulder and worrying about Chelsea. I think we showed that we are good enough to go away from home when the pressure is on and get the result."
