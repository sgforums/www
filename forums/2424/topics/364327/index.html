    *Mystery Club Enter Battle For Wigan Winger Antonio Valencia* . The Latics have confirmed that a unnamed third party has entered the race to secure the signature of their star wide-man. . . *Wigan Athletic chairman Dave Whelan has stated that a third party is now involved in the Antonio Valencia saga, but the source has yet to be named.* It is expected that the Ecuador international will end up at either Old Trafford, or find himself within the lavish confines of the Bernabeu. Whatever happens, the Latics stand to make a huge profit on the 23-year-old, who had signed a four-year deal with the club in the winter of 2008.

    Bayern enter battle with Manchester United for £18m Wigan star Valencia

     

    Heading for the top: Antonio Valencia (right) has plenty of offers

     

    Bayern Munich have joined the chase for Wigan winger Antonio Valencia, in a further indication that they are bracing themselves for the loss of Franck Ribery.

    It comes after approaches for the France international from Real Madrid, Barcelona, Chelsea and Manchester United, as revealed in Sportsmail yesterday.

    However, United remain favourites to land Valencia for around £18million.

    Wigan chairman Dave Whelan is hoping to wrap up a quick deal with the Barclays Premier League champions.

    He said: 'I know Sir Alex Ferguson likes Antonio, and I don't blame him, because he's a brilliant player.
