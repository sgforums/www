    Celtic boss Strachan: United can win European Cup again

     

     

     

    Gordon Strachan last night tipped Manchester United to become the first club to retain the Champions League trophy after watching them beat his Celtic team 3-0 at Old Trafford.

    Two goals from United’s Bulgaria striker Dimitar Berbatov and one from Wayne Rooney put Sir Alex Ferguson’s side in pole position in Group E ahead of a return visit to Celtic in a fortnight.

    Both Berbatov’s goals appeared to come from offside positions but Strachan was
    in no mood to complain after seeing his team soundly beaten.

    Celtic manager Strachan, a former United player, said: ‘I would not bet against
    them winning this trophy again this season. They are better than anybody we have played at any time in this competition.

    'The first two goals were offside but I’m not going to sit here and scream about them. ‘We were blown away at times and it’s one of the best performances I have ever come across in European football.’

     

    Dimitar Berbatov scores his first goal in United's 3-0 win at Old Trafford

    Berbatov’s goals were his third and fourth in Europe for United while Rooney now has nine goals in his last seven games for club and country.

    Strachan added: ‘Rooney is unplayable when he is like this. He is a world class
    player who just happens to be supported by 10 other international class players.

    'It will take some team to stop United this season.’

    United manager Sir Alex Ferguson admitted his team had got a break in being awarded two goals from an offside position.

    But he added: ‘Celtic were always on the back foot.’

    Despite his goals, Berbatov admitted he must improve, saying: ‘You have to work
    so hard here and that’s why I must give 100 per cent all of the time.’
