Van Nistelrooy: Life Was S*** At United

  • Dead_Man_Inc
    5 Jun 08, 14:14

    The former United striker did not have the best preparation going into Germany 2006, it is fair to say.

    A training-ground spat with Cristiano Ronaldo and a heated dispute with Ferguson resulted in the Holland hitman being sold to Real Madrid prior to the commencement of the tournament.

    The former PSV Eindhoven endured a terrible time in Deutschland, relegated to the bench for the majority after falling out with Netherlands boss Marco van Basten.

    Van Nistelrooy embarked on a self-imposed international exile after 2006, insisting he could not play for the Oranje while van Basten was coach.

    But the 31-year-old has since relented, and is optimistic ahead of this month’s European Champions finals.

    “I’ve never said I didn’t want to play for the national team,” he insisted. “Right now I’m far calmer than two years ago and that was because at United I was going through a s**t period.

    “And at that time, with a big tournament, that was something I certainly didn’t need. I tried hard to reject everything but it only worked to a certain extent.

    “Then on top of that I had a conflict with Dutch coach Marco van Basten. Now I feel a lot better and I believe in our success.”
