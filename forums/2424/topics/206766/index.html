    ROY KEANE is set to make a sensational return to football as boss of struggling Sunderland.

    Chairman Niall Quinn expects to unveil his former Republic of Ireland team-mate as the new manager at the Stadium of Light early next week.

    Keane has been out of football since retiring as a Celtic player last season, but he is heading for his first managerial role as Sunderland boss with the task of shaking the club out of what he called its "losing culture".

    Quinn is still in negotiations with the former Manchester United skipper but believes an agreement can be reached in the next few days.

    Keane has always planned to go into management and his winning mentality and aggressive leadership qualities are just what the Wearsiders need.

    Keane has been lured with a multi-million transfer budget and top wages funded by Quinn's eight man consortium.

    After five games and five defeats combining his role of chairman with that of manager Quinn admitted he had to bring someone in to run the team.

    Roy Keane will be confirmed as Sunderland manager following Monday's match against West Brom after the club confirmed they have agreed terms with the Irishman.

    The former Manchester United captain was at the Stadium of Light on Sunday and has agreed to take over from manager-chairman Niall Quinn, who has occupied the role while a permanent choice was sought.

    A statement form the club said: 'Roy Keane visited Sunderland today where he met with players and officials from the club.

    'Keane and the club have agreed terms for him to be appointed as manager, and it is envisaged that he will sign a formal contract immediately after Sunderland's game against West Brom tomorrow.'

    The 35-year-old Irishman was at the club's Academy of Light training headquarters on Sunday and met the players after training.

    Keane's appointment would give the club a huge lift despite his lack of experience, although with the transfer window due to close next Thursday, he would have little opportunity to strengthen his squad.

    The Black Cats entertain West Brom having lost each of their first four Championship games and worse, having been dumped out of the Carling Cup by bottom-of-the-league Bury.
