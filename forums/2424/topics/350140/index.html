    Manchester United keeper Tomasz Kuszczak insists the battle with Ben Foster to be the club's long-term number one is not over.

    Foster was named Man of the Match in the Carling Cup final win over Tottenham and followed up an impressive 120 minutes with a superb penalty save from Jamie O'Hara as the champions emerged victorious in the shoot-out.

    The former Watford loanee has overcome injury problems to assert himself as the logical successor to veteran Edwin van der Sar and Sir Alex Ferguson has admitted he feels the 25-year-old is the future at United.

    However, the Polish media feel Foster is given unfair preferential treatment in the English press because he is viewed as a great hope for Fabio Capello's national team.

    Kuszczak rarely lets The Red Devils down when called upon but does not radiate confidence and is now likely to be considered third in the pecking order.

    "In Manchester, it is not journalists but Sir Alex who will determine who will come into the first team," declared Kuszczak to the Polish media.

    "Before the match with Tottenham, I was prepared [for the news] that I can not play.

    "I do not want to say that I have a bad press here but no, I am not an Englishman."
