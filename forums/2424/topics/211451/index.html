    United are now only ahead of champions Chelsea on goal difference, five points ahead of Liverpool and seven ahead of The Gunners, who still have a game in hand.

    After failing to play catch up for the last three seasons, the Â£18.6 million man is targeting a victory against top flight newcomers Reading to keep up their title ambitions.

    "It is vital we do not slip because then we have to start chasing other teams and it becomes difficult.

    United lacked a cutting edge against Arsene Wenger's men, as the England international made way for John O'Shea in the middle of midfield.

    With Wayne Rooney not yet firing on all cylinders and Nemanja Vidic returning to fitness to strengthen the backline, Carrick believes there is still much more to come from Sir Alex Ferguson's side.
