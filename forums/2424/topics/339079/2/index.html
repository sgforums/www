    CARLOS TEVEZ left Old Trafford with his future looking secure. But Rovers boss Paul Ince followed him down the tunnel after this Manchester United masterclass with his very much in doubt.

     

    Ince used to call himself The Guv’nor as he picked up titles and cups aplenty in his pomp as a player in the red of United. Soon he won’t even be The Gaffer. He has overseen the Lancashire club’s slide into the drop zone after failing to win in nine league games.

    Now this stuffing, with Rovers’ own fans chanting ‘You don’t know what your doing’ at Ince. The scoreline flattered Rovers, who were totally outplayed last night with two of their goals coming in the last six minutes when the game was already up.

     

    They were the fall guys as Tevez landed three devastating blows to claim his first hat-trick in English football. He forced an own goal to open the scoring, set up Nani for another and grabbed three more of his own. As messages to the boss go, this was as good as it gets for a first-team recall.

     

    He has started just six league games so far as the arrival of Dimitar Berbatov has nudged him off centre stage after his 19 goals in United’s double last term.

     

    Yet last night this terrier-like striker would not stop chomping at the bit. Some top players cannot raise themselves for these Carling Cup games. Tevez saw it as an opportunity — and took it.

     

    Now the team selection for Saturday’s visit of Sunderland will be very interesting. What a problem for United manager Alex Ferguson to have. While the side he sent out last night may not have been United’s strongest XI, it was certainly a line-up to be reckoned with.

     

    Arsene Wenger stuck to his teen terrors on Tuesday but came unstuck as Arsenal were knocked out by Burnley at Turf Moor. Fergie’s team had a mixture of youth and experience with big names like Anderson, Nani, Ryan Giggs, Gary Neville and Tevez all present and correct.

     

    The best pre-match news for United fans was Paul Scholes on the bench after more than two months out with a knee injury.

     

    STAR MAN - CARLOS TEVEZ (Man United).

    MAN UTD: Foster 6, R Da Silva 6, Neville 5, Evans 6, O’Shea 6 (Evra 5), Nani 7, Gibson 7, Possebon 6 (Scholes 5), Anderson 7, Giggs 8 (Manucho 5), Tevez 9. Subs not used: Kuszczak, Park, Vidic, Welbeck.
