    Huntelaar Would Have Considered Manchester United

    Klaas-Jan Huntelaar has admitted that Manchester United could have scuppered his move to Real Madrid had they shown interest in him...

    Dutch hitman Klaas-Jan Huntelaar will join Real Madrid from Ajax on January 2. He's needed to lead the line in the absence of crocked countryman Ruud van Nistelrooy.

    Los Blancos will unveil the 25-year-old on Thursday, in order to inject a timely morale boost into the club.

    Before the presentation, though, 'The Hunter' has admitted that things could have been very different had Manchester United followed up on their previous interest in him by challenging Madrid for his services.

    United shelled out €30 million to take Dimitar Berbatov from Tottenham Hotspur instead, while Carlos Tevez's future at Old Trafford has been unclear for some time. Thus, a bid from Sir Alex Ferguson was highly unlikely.

    Still, the Oranje star has admitted that it could have given him a headache.

    Manchester City are said to have had a bid rejected for his services during the summer - one much higher than the €27 million agreed between Madrid and Ajax.

    Expressing a distaste for the cash-rich Citizens, the striker said: "There was enough interest in the summer, but these weren’t the clubs we had thought about.

    "Manchester City didn’t meet the criteria we wanted. Manchester United? Maybe that would have been another story!"

    Huntelaar's words are not what Los Merengues want to hear, as they have a bitter relationship with the Red Devils as it is. He's also hinted that City may not be able to buy happiness in January.

    Greg Ptolomey, Goal.com
