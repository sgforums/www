    The fact that Manchester United assistant manager Carlos Queiroz was in Portugal last night to watch Sporting's Uefa Cup quarter-final clash against Rangers has sparked speculation about whom he might have been scouting.

    Rangers beat Sporting, who are not commenting about any possible scouts at their Jose Alvalade stadium on Thursday might.

    However, Queiroz's presence suggests he is continuing to keep tabs on the progress of Miguel Veloso and Joao Moutinho.

    Queiroz possesses in-depth knowledge of Portuguese football, while United have a special relationship with Sporting Clube de Portugal, from whom they acquired Cristiano Ronaldo and Nani.

    And United manager Sir Alex Ferguson is a known admirer of both Veloso and Moutinho.

    The two players are being strongly tipped to make summer moves out of Lisbon, and United appear to be serious potential suitors.

    That fact that Ronaldo and Nani both made successful moves to Old Trafford, and that compatriot Queiroz is also at Old Trafford, could be a significant attraction for one or both of Veloso and Moutinho.
