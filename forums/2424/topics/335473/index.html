    PATRICE EVRA says Manchester United are bracing themselves for a Hull backlash.

     

    The high-flying Tigers lost 3-0 to Chelsea in midweek - their first defeat since August. That incredible run saw them beat title challengers Arsenal at the Emirates. And United left-back Evra believes Phil Brown's men will go all out for victory when they travel to Old Trafford on Saturday.

     

    The France ace said: "When you beat the big teams, in particular, you feel more confident and it gives you more freedom to play good football. "That’s why I expect them to come for a win.

     

    "I don’t expect to see all of their players defending, I think they’ll have a go. "If that happens I think it could be a great game because that would open up space for us."

     

    Evra is looking forward to playing against pal Bernard Mendy, who joined Hull in the summer. He added: "We first met when we were playing in France and I also played with him for the French Under-21s - he's a good friend.

     

    "I’m happy to see him doing well at Hull but when I see him at Old Trafford I’ll be focused on helping United win the game."
