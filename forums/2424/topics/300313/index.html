    Big Match Facts

    In each of the last three years, Manchester United have finished the season in the position they entered the year; third in 2004-05, second in 2005-06 and first in 2006-07. They go into this game trailing Arsenal by two points and will be wanting a repeat of the 2002-03 campaign when they were five points behind the Gunners at the outset of the year and won by the title by five.

    Birmingham go to the Theatre of Dreams in search of their 50th all-time Premier League victory.

    The Brummies may feel the backlash of Saturday's defeat at West Ham, as United endeavour to claim their 10th successive league scalp at Old Trafford.

    The Red Devils have not suffered back-to-back defeats in 101 Premier League matches since April 2005.

    Manchester United have lost a New Years Day Premier League fixture only once; they went down 4-1 at Tottenham in 1996.

    Birmingham began 2007 with 2-0 home win over Wigan; they were the first Premier League points the Brummies had gained from the first fixture in a new calendar year.

    It's 30 years since Birmingham last won at Old Trafford; Kevin Dillon and Trevor Francis were their scorers in a 1-2 triumph on 2 January 1978.

    Birmingham have never beaten Manchester United in the Premier League, and have lost all four visits to Old Trafford, without scoring a single goal.

    Tevez concern for United

    Carlos Tevez is a major doubt for Manchester United's FA Cup clash with Aston Villa on Saturday.

    After scoring the only goal to beat Birmingham, the Argentine eventually had to be replaced after failing to shrug off a hefty first-half bang on the ankle.

    Tevez was carried into the tunnel by two members of the United's backroom staff after the final whistle, with manager Sir Alex Ferguson confirming it is unlikely he will be involved at Villa Park.

    "It was a bad tackle and the longer the game went on, the worse the injury got," said Ferguson.

    "In the end, we had to bring Carlos off. We will assess him tomorrow to see what the damage is but he is doubtful for Saturday now."

    Happily, Ferguson should have a ready-made replacement for Tevez in Wayne Rooney, who missed a second successive game with a virus but is showing signs of being fine for the weekend.

    The Scot explained: "It would have been a bit of a risk to play Wayne today but he should be fine for Saturday."
