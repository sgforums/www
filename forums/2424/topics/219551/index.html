Evra earns French recall

  • Dead_Man_Inc
    10 Nov 06, 10:52
    Patrice Evra has been given his first call-up to the France squad in two years by national coach Raymond Domenech.

    Evra, who earned the last of his five caps for Les Bleus in November 2004 against Poland, is in the squad to face Greece in a friendly next Wednesday along with United team-mate Louis Saha.

    The inclusion in Domenech's squad is reward for Evra's fine performances this season. United would normally have been reeling from the absence of Gabriel Heinze as he completed his recovery from a knee injury.

    But the 25-year-old French left-back has been outstanding and proved himself a valuable member of United's squad, so much so that Sir Alex Ferguson must now give serious consideration to which player he selects first.

    France squad: Coupet (Lyon), Landreau (PSG); Abidal (Lyon), Clerc (Lyon), Escude (Sevilla), Evra (United), Gallas (Arsenal), Mexes (Roma), Sagnol (Bayern Munich), Thuram (Barcelona); A. Diarra (Lyon), Govou (Lyon), Higuain (River Plate), Makelele (Chelsea), Malouda (Lyon), Mavuba (Bordeaux), Ribery (Marseille), Toulalan (Lyon), Vieira (Inter Milan), Wiltord (Lyon); Anelka (Bolton), Benzema (Lyon), Henry (Arsenal), Saha (United).
