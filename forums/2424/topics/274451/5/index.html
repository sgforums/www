    We Were Wasteful, Says Ferguson

    Manchester United boss Sir Alex Ferguson claimed his could not fault his sides peformance other than their wastefulness in front of goal, as their worst start to a Premiership season continued with a significant 1-0 defeat to rivals City.

    "We had enough chances and played fantastic football at times," claimed Ferguson.

    However, he added: "We were just wasteful, we had so many opportunities to win it ourselves.

    "I can't fault the performance - we completely dominated. But we should have sewn the game up."

    Manchester United were without the suspended Cristiano Ronaldo and injured Wayne Rooney and Louis Saha, but Ferguson wasn't letting that become an excuse.

    He said: "Of course you miss strikers but the number of chances we had and the amount of possession we should have won the game."

    Eriksson Admits City Were Lucky

    "It's fantastic, it was a very difficult game and we were lucky as well," said the former England manager after the 1-0 win against United, who dominated the match but could not find a breakthrough.
