    There are two reasons:

    REASON ONE

    Footballers prefer Real Madrid simply because it is offering highest salary.

    Google for the 50p tax rule imposed by the Labour Party (Gordon Brown) and you will understand why.

    During contract negotiations, Class A footballers demand wages that are post-tax. This means English clubs will find it alot tougher to match or exceed the wages that are offered by Spanish Clubs.

     

    REASON  TWO

    Footballers prefer Madrid because they believe Real Madrid will never go bust (like Leeds FC).

    The legal structure of Spain is that each province is fairly autonomous in its insolvency laws. For deep seated clubs like Barcelona (Catalonia) and Real Madrid (Madrid), the legal autonomy means that well connected Presidents are in a position to hold off insolvency by leveraging on the interests of creditors (being ironically also the same people as the investors in the club) - no matter how indebted the club becomes.

    There are arguments against the viability of the "high debt - high revenue" model of business model for football club (Google for these yourself), but for Madrid this appears to be working.

    Although Man Utd under the Glaziers is also operating on the "high debt - high revenue" model, Man Utd cannot take on as much debt as Real Madrid, because (i) the legal regime for insolvency in UK is less forgiving; and (ii) Man Utd is a publicly listed club bound by need to show profit to shareholders.
