    Fergie fumes at Rooney red card

    Manchester United's 2-0 defeat at Fulham on Saturday left Sir Alex Ferguson not surprisingly frustrated, with the Red Devils boss disappointed with his team's first-half performance and also the second-half dismissal of Wayne Rooney.

    The Premier League champions suffered a 2-0 defeat at Craven Cottage and ended the game with only nine men, as Paul Scholes was dismissed for deliberate handball in the incident that saw Danny Murphy convert a penalty and Rooney red-carded for dissent.

    Ferguson, who also played down fears that Dimitar Berbatov had suffered a serious injury, despite the Bulgarian leaving Craven Cottage on crutches, was angry with referee Phil Dowd for showing Rooney a second yellow card for an incident the Scot considered to be innocuous.

    Ferguson told MUTV: "Did he (Rooney) throw the ball at the referee? The ball was thrown direct to where the free-kick was being taken - did it hit the referee? No.

    "He threw it because he wanted to get the game going. What can you say about that?

    "There wasn't a bad tackle in the game but John Pantsil was booked in the first half for nothing - it was just a tackle. Tackling isn't always perfect - I thought it was totally unfair.

    "But there's no point talking about the referee - we didn't play well enough to win the game in the first half and that's why we lost it.

    "In the second half I thought we did really well - they kept going and credit to them.

    "But we're home to Aston Villa in our next game - and that will be crucial."

    After United's reverse to Liverpool last week, Saturday's defeat to Fulham consigned the team to back-to-back Premier League losses for the first time in 147 games.
