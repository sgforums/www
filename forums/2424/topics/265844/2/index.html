    Tevez: 'Excited' by move

    United set to sign Tevez

    After securing West Ham's Premiership survival by scoring against Manchester United on the final day of last season, Carlos Tevez is about to be given the chance to find the Old Trafford net on a regular basis by becoming Sir Alex Ferguson's fourth multimillion pound signing of the summer. The 23-year-old striker is currently on duty with Argentina at the Copa America in Venezuela but it is believed that his representatives are finalising the details of his departure from West Ham, with whom he's understood to have a contract until 2010. The fee is believed to be around Â£20m, though neither Manchester United nor West Ham would confirm that talks are in progress.

    Tevez arrived at West Ham amid much fanfare last summer but initially failed to live up to expectations. However, even when his efforts were not bearing fruit, he refused to become discouraged and his determination to succeed when many of his team-mates looked listless made him a favourite with Hammers fans. Eventually, he was rewarded with goals, bagging seven in the club's last 10 games.

    United have already bolstered their attacking power this summer by signing Portugal winger Nani and left-sided Brazilian raider Anderson. But with continued uncertainty over Louis Saha's fitness and the expected departure of Alan Smith, the club is eager to recruit another striker, someone who can be trusted to systematically convert the abundant chances the side is likely to create.

    Many observers feel Tevez, who has only scored four goals in 25 appearances for Argentina, does not fit that bill, and that he prefers to rampage all over the final third of the pitch in a style more akin to Wayne Rooney, meaning that if the two were to be paired together, the attack may still lack a focal point. However, Ferguson is of the opinion that, even if the Argentinian is not a 'fox-in-the-box', his pace, power, intelligence and instinct mean he will combine well with United's other attackers and plunder plenty of goals, even if many opportunities are spurned for want of a specialist poacher.

    Tevez was once fined when playing for Brazil's Corinthians for turning up to an official press conference wearing a Manchester United shirt.
