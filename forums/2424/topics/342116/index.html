Ferguson Plays Down Chances Of United Clean-Sweep

  • Dead_Man_Inc
    28 Dec 08, 20:45

    Manchester United manager Sir Alex Ferguson believes his side have to be realistic about their chances of silverware this season, with the chance of success still alive on four fronts.  

    Having secured the Club World Cup in recent weeks, the Red Devils are still in the hunt for their third Premier League title in as many years, they face Inter Milan in the last-16 of the Champions League, while they have also reached the semi-final of the Carling Cup.  

    Added to that, their FA Cup participation begins in the New Year, meaning that the domestic, European and world champions could wind up the season in possession of five major trophies.

    However, while Ferguson acknowledges how great an achievement that would be, he believes it would require as much luck as skill due to the increasing demands on top-flight squads.

    "To win one major trophy is unusual but nice all the time," Ferguson told the Daily Star.

    "But there is no chance of winning all four competitions we are left in this season. It is appetising and we will have that opportunity if we are still in them all come March and April, but it is not realistic.

    "I won't be making any ridiculous statements about winning the lot. We would have to be very lucky in terms of everyone still being fit and we have got a job to do in the league.”

    Ferguson went on to express his satisfaction with the Old Trafford side’s start in the Premier League, believing that it has provided firm foundations for a solid title defence.

    "Our start has been very difficult. Every game we have had after a European match has been away from home. And when we came back from Japan we were away again at Stoke on Boxing Day,” he continued.

    "We have also had away games against all the top sides. So our start has been satisfactory, but not perfect at the moment. We are in a very competitive league, but we still have a good chance of keeping our title."
