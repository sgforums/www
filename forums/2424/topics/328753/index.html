    Manchester United striker Manucho’s foot injury means he is still some way away from being considered foir selection, according to manager Sir Alex Ferguson.

    Manucho will not be making his Manchester United first-team debut imminently because he is recovering from a foot injury, Alex Ferguson has revealed.

    Angola international Manucho spent the second half of last season on loan to the Greek side Panathinaikos, with a work permit issue preventing him from playing in England.

    However, that problem has now been cleared up and the 25-year-old is free to play for United.

    But that won’t happen until his metatarsal injury heals fully. The injury forced him to miss United’s pre-season trip to South Africa and Nigeria.

    "He is still recovering from a metatarsal injury and has done bits of training," said Ferguson.

    "It is quite promising in terms of recovery from the injury. But he is still well short of a competitive match."
