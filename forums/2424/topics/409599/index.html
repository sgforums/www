    Even Wayne Rooney's England team-mates promise to wind him up when United play Everton on Saturday

     

     

     

    Wayne Rooney has been told to expect a hostile reception at Everton on Saturday - when even his England team mates will join in.

    Three Lions defender Phil Jagielka admits he intends to wind up Rooney after revelations that the Manchester United striker had an affair with a prostitute.

    When asked whether Rooney will get slaughtered by fans at Goodison, Jagielka said: ‘There’s a good chance, he normally gets quite a bit if stick anyway. I can’t see that changing from Saturday.

    ‘It should be amusing. Up on Merseyside he normally gets a little bit of stick. I’ll be giving him a little bit as well if he plays on Saturday.

    ‘All the England boys know who is playing who. It’s always interesting playing against people who you have just been away with in the week. No doubt I’ll give him a couple of bits of banter if he plays.’

    Jagielka will be charged with stopping Rooney in Saturday’s lunch-time kick off, having lined-up alongside the striker in England’s wins against Bulgaria and Switzerland in the past week.

    He said: ‘I’m sure Wayne can deal with it – he is a tough lad and he’ll let his football do the talking.

    “It’s not for me to comment on how he’s been, I’m not exactly best of friends with Wayne. I’ve not been hanging around with him but he seems his normal self.

    ‘I hope the abuse doesn’t cross the line but, at the end of the day, we are all human beings. Sometimes football takes over and you never know what is going to happen.’

    ‘I’m not going to say who’s going to come out on top or he’ll score a hat-trick. It’s always a tough battle, he’s obviously a good player and you have always got to concentrate.’

    MOYES ... cameo bid

    DAVID MOYES tried to get Wayne Rooney to turn out for Everton last week.

    The Manchester United ace returns to Goodison today and is sure to receive another hot reception.

    But if Rooney had got his wish, he would have played in Everton colours again during Jamie Carragher's testimonial.

    Boss Moyes contacted England to see if the ex-Toffee striker fancied a brief cameo against Liverpool at Anfield last Saturday.

    Moyes revealed: "It was interesting Wayne was keen to play for Everton.

    "If it had been possible he would have played but he wasn't able to as he was on international duty."
