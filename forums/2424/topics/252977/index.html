    The Malaysian leg of Manchester United's pre-season tour to Asia looks set to be abandoned. United are due to play games in Japan, South Korea and Macao this summer, followed by a friendly in Kuala Lumpur on 27th July against a Malaysian XI.

    However, the country's football authorities have now ruled that United cannot play there in July, as it would clash with the Asian Cup. Fifa president Sepp Blatter has urged the newly crowned Premiership champions to reschedule, while the Asian Football Confederation is also keen for the tour not to go ahead as planned.

    "Players, coaches, referees, administrators and leagues are going to benefit out of these visits no doubt," AFC chairman Mohammed bin Hammam said on Sky Sports News. "But what I will not accept is for these tours to take place during the off-season of the European leagues, especially during the organisation of the Asian Cup. "I will fight for these tours from European clubs not to happen during July 2007."

    The AFC and the Football Association of Malaysia signed an agreement last year banning the promotion of any other soccer event during the Asian Cup. And Tengku Abdullah Sultan Ahmad Shah, president of the Malaysian FA, admitted: "We have to abide by the ruling, I guess. "If they want to come at that time they can't. The Asian Cup will have to take precedent."

    A Manchester United spokesman has said the first three games of the tour will definitely go ahead.
