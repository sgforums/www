    Rio Ferdinand missing as Manchester United depart for Bursaspor

    • Central defender's absence appears to be precautionary
    • Evans, Macheda, Anderson, Rooney, Giggs and Owen also out

     

    Rio Ferdinand has been spared the four-hour flight to Turkey. Photograph: Neal Simpson/Empics Sport
    .
    .

    Rio Ferdinand was the major absentee from the 20-man Manchester United party which headed to Turkey this morning for their Champions League encounter with Bursaspor.

    No official explanation was given for Ferdinand's absence although he confirmed on Twitter that he is not injured and it seems likely that Sir Alex Ferguson's decision was precautionary due to the defender's long-term back problems. With Premier League games against Wolverhampton Wanderers, Manchester City and Aston Villa to follow in the next fortnight, Ferdinand has almost certainly been excused to give him the best possible chance of featuring in those fixtures.

    Jonny Evans, Federico Macheda, Anderson plus long-term victims Wayne Rooney, Ryan Giggs and Michael Owen were also missing, with Ferguson pledging to pick virtually his strongest possible side from the players that did travel. "I would rather take a strong squad and get through," the manager told the Manchester Evening News. "If we win the match we have 10 points and that's us really qualified. I have to look at it that way."

    Michael Carrick was on the plane and the midfielder has rejected the theory that the Champions League group stage is becoming less of a challenge. "The Champions League is not easier than it used to be," the England international said.

    "In terms of the stature and history of the clubs, it might not be as good, but the games are still tough, especially away from home. People might point to our away record, which is very good, but all those matches have been hard.

    "Travelling to Turkey, for instance, presents you with a long journey and a different type of atmosphere. You are encountering players that you don't see every week. It brings a different challenge to Barcelona, Inter and Real Madrid. It is something you have to face if you want to continue in the competition."
