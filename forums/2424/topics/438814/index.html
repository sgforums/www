    Double boost for Fergie as Rooney and Chicarito return to Man United squad

     

     

    Boost: Wayne Rooney is set to feature against Norwich on Saturday

     

       

    Wayne Rooney will confirm his availability for England's Euro 2012 qualifier in Montenegro next Friday night by returning to Manchester United's squad against Norwich at Old Trafford on Saturday.

    The striker has missed the last two games with a hamstring strain, but both Rooney and Javier Hernandez - who suffered a dead leg at Stoke last weekend - have been declared fit.

    Rooney might start on the bench with Sir Alex Ferguson giving Danny Welbeck another run-out. But Rooney's return is a major boost for club and country as England look to clinch qualification in Podgorica.

    'Wayne and Javier Hernandez will be fit. They trained yesterday very well and I was pleased with that,' Ferguson said on Friday.

    'Jonny Evans also comes back, hopefully that will help us in the defensive part as we're certainly changing the back four at the moment.'   

    After Rooney was injured in training before the match against Stoke City last weekend, Ferguson said the England striker could be out 'for a few weeks' but he has made a quicker than expected recovery as he bids to continue his fine start to the season which has brought nine goals so far.

    Ferguson believes Rooney's partnership with Hernandez could better the 52-goal total shared between Andy Cole and Dwight Yorke when United won the Treble in 1998-99.

    'I hope that but it's a tough league,' said the United manager. 'They haven't played a full season together but the goal threat is immense. I expect Hernandez to score 25 and Wayne has had a fantastic start.' 

     

     

    Dead leg: Javier Hernandez was injured against Stoke (above)

     

     

    Ferguson blamed a shaky defence rather than a weak in attack for United throwing away a two-goal lead to trail 3-2 before Ashley Young's last-gasp equaliser on Tuesday night.

    Ferguson said his team had been rather 'cavalier' in their approach and needed to tighten up.    

    'People say our performance was careless and we under-estimated our opponents and maybe there's an element of truth in that,' he said.

    'I certainly wasn't happy, even in the first half I could sense we were too relaxed and could have lost three goals in the first half.   

    'We could have scored goals as well but were too cavalier. We're attacking in the way we're playing at the moment and people can enjoy it but we have to do better defensively.'   

    Unbeaten United have scored 22 goals in six league games so far.
