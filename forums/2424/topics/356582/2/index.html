    PREMIER LEAGUE TITLE RACE

    United back on top but Fergie has the hump with casual attitude

     

    Ferguson: unhappy with his team casual display.

     

     

    MANCHESTER United put one hand on the Premier League trophy last night but got a blast from the boss.

    A 2-0 win over Portsmouth saw them go three points clear at the top with a game in hand. But Alex Ferguson was unhappy his team did not finish off Pompey before Michael Carrick’s 82nd-minute goal.

    Fergie said: “We were too casual so I’m not taking anything for granted. We have got to put our foot down. “There is no leeway unless you can keep performing properly. "This could have been embarrassing as we could have thrown the game away when we should have been five or six up. 

    “In the first half the football was some of the best you’ll see all season. But when you miss chances at 1-0 up you encourage your opponents.” 

    Wayne Rooney put United ahead after nine minutes before Carrick got his first goal since November late on. Carrick said: “We should really have finished the match off earlier and at 1-0 they had a couple of chances so the second goal was important. 

    “Hopefully we can keep this momentum going and put it to bed as quick as we can.” The game was Paul Scholes’ 600th for United while Ryan Giggs will make his 800th on Saturday against Spurs. 

    Carrick said: “They are the perfect example of what it is to play at this club.” The downside for United were foot injuries that could keep out Gary Neville and John O’Shea for the rest of the season. 

    Ferguson said: “They are going to be out for weeks. They both suffered bad tackles and the referee did nothing about it. “You normally get a firm display from Peter Walton but not tonight.”
