    Fletcher fit for Pompey battle

    Sir Alex Ferguson has confirmed Darren Fletcher will return for Manchester United's game against Portsmouth on Wednesday.

    Fletcher has missed United's last three matches with a minor injury and was one of several players left behind in Manchester for Sunday's FA Cup semi-final defeat by Everton.

    United welcome Pompey to Old Trafford in midweek in the Premier League and Scotland international Fletcher will be back in the squad.

    However, Ferguson revealed fellow midfielder Michael Carrick is struggling to shake off a minor complaint in order to take his place in the team.

    "We left quite a few players back in Manchester and they'll all be ready for Wednesday," Ferguson told MUTV.

    "Darren Fletcher will be fit, for example, but maybe Michael Carrick is the one we'll have to assess a bit more, he could be a close call."

    Wayne Rooney will return after Ferguson dismissed reports that the striker had suffered a metatarsal injury in last week's UEFA Champions League win at Porto.

    Cristiano Ronaldo, Edwin van der Sar, John O'Shea and Ryan Giggs could also come back into the United starting line-up against Pompey after not being involved at Wembley.

    Brown on comeback trail for Manchester United

     

    Wes Brown. Out since November but coming back.

    Wes Brown has given Manchester United another fitness boost by coming through an hour of his comeback game against Preston at Deepdale.
    Sidelined since November with a foot injury, the England international is keen to play some part in a hectic end to the season and manager Sir Alex Ferguson will be delighted to learn of his contribution to a 4-0 Lancashire Senior Cup win.

     

    While Wednesday's Premier League clash with Portsmouth is likely to come much too soon for Brown given the length of time he has been out of action, Ferguson will waste no time in bringing him back into the first-team fold.

    Meanwhile, Ferguson has confirmed Darren Fletcher will be fit when United resume their title challenge, having missed the last two games with a minor hamstring problem.
