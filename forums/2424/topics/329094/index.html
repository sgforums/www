Poyet Stays Mum Over Berba

  • Dead_Man_Inc
    1 Sep 08, 10:02

    The latest reports suggest that Manchester United are finally set to clinch the signing of Dimitar Berbatov after offering Fraizer Campbell plus money in exchange, but when a Sky Sports reporter pressed Poyet on whether the Bulgarian has played his last game for the club, he simply said: "You will know tomorrow."

    Poyet was also asked whether he believes that new signing Roman Pavluychenko and Darren Bent can provide sufficient firepower up-front during the length of the campaign, to which he responded: "Probably not. But there is a day and a half to go (until the transfer window closes). I know the chairman is working very hard, so let's see what happens."

    Before Sunday's match at Chelsea, which ended in what was a commendable 1-1 draw for Tottenham, the club's manager Juande Ramos admitted that the ongoing Berbatov situation has contributed to their false start to the season.

    "You get the feeling that the sooner the whole thing is finished the better," said the Spaniard.

    "The chapter finally closes with the end of the transfer market. I think there is going to be lots of reasonably furious action between now and then."

    He added: "Without a doubt, it has had an awful lot to do with the start to the season.

    "It would have been great to have been a lot calmer. But you have to grin and bear it and get on with things.

    "Certainly the ideal situation is that you get your squad settled early, you work with all the players in the pre-season period."
