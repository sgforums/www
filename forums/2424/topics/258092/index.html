    THE Â£31million double swoop for wonderkids Anderson and Nani is the most important deal of Sir Alex Ferguson's reign. After 20 years in charge at Manchester United, Fergie's era is almost over. With just a couple of seasons left, the great Scot is preparing for the future. And with the signings of this talented duo United have pulled off a real coup.

    Before the deals were announced on Wednesday evening, Anderson and Nani were linked with the biggest names in the European game. Real Madrid, Barcelona, Chelsea, Arsenal, Liverpool, Inter and AC Milan were all interested in the pair. By winning the race to their signatures United have fired a warning shot to their Champions League rivals. But Fergie's masterstroke is not just the capture of these talented players. In Nani and Anderson, United have ready-made replacements for ageing star duo Ryan Giggs and Paul Scholes.

    Nani is a natural winger who can also play as a striker. In this way he is very similar to Cristiano Ronaldo. Nani, like his Portuguese team-mate, has great pace and trickery but he is still a raw talent and will need nurturing if he is to reach Ronaldo's level. But the bonus for United is that he can play on the left side. When Giggs retires, the 20-year-old will have had time in the United squad and will naturally fill the void left by the Welshman's exit.

    Anderson can play two roles for United. Before his switch to Europe, Anderson played in an attacking left-sided role similar to that of Ronaldinho at Barcelona - hence the comparisons. But the Brazilian has prospered playing a central midfield role for Porto and it is this position where he could become a key player for United. Paul Scholes' forward thrust from midfield has been vital in United's success over the years. And once he retires it will be important that Anderson makes that position his own. The 19-year-old is quick-footed - like Nani and Ronaldo - but his strength on the ball is what sets him apart from other talents his age. This will be key in the English game against defensive midfield opponents such as Gilberto, Claude Makelele and Javier Mascherano.

    Both players should prosper under the stewardship of Fergie. But it will be the Scot's replacement who will really benefit from these high-profile transfers. We are still yet to find out who that lucky manager will be, but Wednesday's deals could be a good indicator. Fergie's Portuguese assistant Carlos Queiroz was present during negotiations for both players. He also played a major role in bringing Ronaldo to Old Trafford.

    That suggests United are already preparing for the future without Fergie, Giggs and Scholes - the men who have made them so great in the last 20 years. But United fans should not worry. If QueirozÂ’s first three acquisitions, Ronaldo, Anderson and Nani, are anything to go by the future is bright for the Red Devils.
