    Manchester United’s Federico Macheda Admits Desire To Return To Italy

    The 18-year-old was in a buoyant mood, after being called up to the under-21 national team.

    Federico Macheda burst on to the scene at Manchester United last season, with a last minute winner on debut, against Aston Villa at Old Trafford on April 5, 2009.

    Since then, he has gone on to make a name for himself in Italy, which has consequently seen him earn yet another call-up to Pierluigi Casiraghi’s Italian under-21 squad, for their European championship qualifiers against Wales and Luxemburg respectively.

    “What I have achieved by being called up to the under-21 national team is beautiful and an important goal for me,” Macheda was quoted as saying to Sky Sport24.

    “It’s a great feeling of joy and emotion, and I am looking forward to starting this adventure.”

    The 18-year-old went on to discuss his feelings about a potential return to Italy in the future.

    He added, “One day I’d love to return to Italy, as obviously it was where I was born and of course I always follow Italian football.

    “Lazio? I am a Biancocelesti fan, so maybe one day I will return there, but again, I am happy at Manchester for now.

    “To play in the Champions League with them is my dream this season.”
