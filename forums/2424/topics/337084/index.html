    Manchester United's attack-minded midfielder Paul Scholes could return by the end of the month to take on Manchester City, after injuring his knee six weeks ago.

    Since early October influential playmaking midfielder Paul Scholes has been sidelined with a knee ligament injury.

    Early reports though suggest the Salford born 34-year old could return in time for the heated and highly anticipated Manchester derby on November 30.

    He returned to the training ground on Wednesday and is believed to undergo full training in a week and a half meaning he would then have seven days to suitably prepare and build up his match fitness in time for the Manchester City showdown.

    A United insider reportedly told British tabloid the Sun: "Paul was spraying 40 yard passes around as if he had never been away.

    "To do that takes some power in the knee. He was also sprinting around — you would not believe he suffered an injury just six weeks ago.

    "He probably could play in the derby — it’s just a question of what the boss wants to do."
