    Legendary French defender and former United centre-back Laurent Blanc says he has been tracking Darren Fletcher's career after being tipped off by Sir Alex Ferguson that the Scottish midfielder would make the grade at Old Trafford.

    Blanc was with United between 2001 and 2003, leaving a few months after Fletcher made his debut against Basel in the Champions League. Then aged 19, Fletcher is now 22 years old and regularly involved in the first team at Old Trafford.

    He is also an integral part of Walter Smith's Scotland squad, who take on France in a Euro 2008 qualifier Park on Saturday. Blanc knows Fletcher will be a key player, and one for Ramond Domenech's side to look out for.

    "I knew Darren when I was at Manchester, although he was just a young kid," he said. "He was already training with the first team and going away with us to games.

    "One day, Sir Alex pulled me aside and said, 'See that young boy there? He's going to be a great player for us.' I have followed his career since and he has done very well to play for United every week. I remember he had great ability and I'm delighted he plays for Scotland."
