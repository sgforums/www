    What makes that team so special is not because of the star status(in fact, probably half the team at the time wouldn't even be considered the top player in the world in his position) of the players, but rather the telepathy, camaraderie and attitude. Of course, I don't mean the players were bad, but their star appeal just wasn't there(which, in a way, proved a blessing in disguise as the team was essentially far more cohesive and united).

    As Keane and Bruce recounted years later, that team could play if the opponents wanted to play and scrap if their opponents wanted to scrap and aboveall, the confidence throughout the side was just immense - and justifiably so - and I'm proud to say that's the only United side I've ever seen which every single member would pull his weight in every match they took part in.

    For me, at least, Hughes' equaliser in the FA Cup Semi Final in 93/94 just sums up what that team is about - United had a greater(with more strength in depth) squad in '99, but in terms of the quality of the first 11 alone, no other United side would come close to touching the '94 one...
