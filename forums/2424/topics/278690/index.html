    The 24-year-old Holland international smashed 36 goals in 51 games for the Amsterdam giants last term and Ajax's failure to reach the Champions League group stages this season could force them to offload their prize asset.

    After selling Ryan Babel to Liverpool and Wesley Sneijder to Real Madrid in the summer it was expected that Ajax boss Henk ten Cate would be able to keep hold of Huntelaar until at least the end of the season. However the 3-1 aggregate defeat to Slavia Prague in the Champions League qualifying tie has placed a huge financial burden on the club and a bid in the region of Â£18million would be hard to ignore in January.

    Sir Alex Ferguson sees Huntelaar as the ideal man to replace the goals of Ruud van Nistelrooy, who left for Real Madrid in the summer of 2006.
