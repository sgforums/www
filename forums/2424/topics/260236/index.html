  • omgukilledkenny

    Darren Fletcher isn't concerned over the arrivals of midfield trio Nani, Anderson and Owen Hargreaves at Manchester United.

    He said: "We've spent money to bring in three new midfielders and it's exciting.

    "Owen is a great player and has never quite received the praise he deserved. But he had a terrific World Cup last summer and is a main player for England. Anderson and Nani both have excellent reputations and I'm sure the signings will prove to be money well spent.

    "It will make it even more difficult to get into the team but it's a challenge I'm ready for. From the outside I'm looked upon as a squad player - that's a label you're given. It's not a tag I like and I try to fight against it.

    "But that's the way it is for a lot of players at a club this big. I played nearly 40 times last season, so it's not that bad being a squad player!

    "I'd love to play in every game but you have to be realistic and know that few achieve that. I believe I contributed to winning the title and deserved my place as the season got down to the business end.

    "When I was left out of the side I didn't sulk. I kept my head up, worked hard and was patient.

    "It paid off because it meant I was in the right mind when I was called upon by the gaffer.

    "There were games I thought I played well in and then the boss would drop me in the next game.

    "Obviously, I was disappointed when the manager left me out but I had to get over it quickly."

    What a good professional.
