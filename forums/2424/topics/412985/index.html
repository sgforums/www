    DR RICHARD STEADMAN has hinted Owen Hargeaves could return to first-team action on Saturday.

    Manchester United midfielder Hargreaves has not started a senior game for over two years due to ongoing knee problems.

    Two operations and an extensive period of rehabilitation seemed to have put the 29-year-old back on track towards the end of last season.

    But his hopes of starting the current campaign were dashed when he experienced discomfort on his return to training.

    United boss Alex Ferguson sent him to see Dr Steadman and the Colorado-based specialist is optimistic about his patient's chances of making an appearance against West Brom.

    The world renowned surgeon said: "Owen should play some part on Saturday.

    "Our physical therapist, Luke O'Brien, has been working very closely with United's coaches on his rehab.

    "It's been a long road back and there have been some setbacks along the way.

    "But I've got a lot of admiration for the way Owen has responded — he's worked very hard and has never given up. Things are looking good for him now."

    As Hargreaves has been omitted from United's 25-man Champions League squad, he is restricted to domestic games until January.

    If he does not make an appearance against the Baggies, a more lklely comeback date would be the Carling Cup clash with Wolves on October 26.
