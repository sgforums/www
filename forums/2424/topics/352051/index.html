    Sir Alex: use Sunday as a recovery day after their hectic recent schedule

     

     

    MANCHESTER UNITED’S punishment for their 4-1 mauling by Liverpool was a DAY OFF. 

    Boss Alex Ferguson decided against hauling in his shamed stars for extra training after Saturday’s Old Trafford embarrassment. Instead, he stuck to his original plan to use Sunday as a recovery day after their hectic recent schedule. 

    A United insider revealed: “It’s been three games a week almost since they got back from the World Club Cup in Japan, so with a free week ahead of Fulham it was decided a day’s rest would be better. 

    “The players don’t need telling again how much the boss hurts.” The FA have confirmed Nemanja Vidic will sit out United’s next two league games — including the home clash with Aston Villa — following his red card. It was the Serbian defender’s second dismissal of the season. 

    United have had some welcome news, with Owen Hargreaves taking the latest steps along the long road to recovery. The England ace has missed the whole campaign after a knee op to cure a long-standing tendinitis problem.
