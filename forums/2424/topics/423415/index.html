    Marseille Coach Didier Deschamps: We Can't Let Manchester United Score Tomorrow

    Deschamps believes that a goalless draw with Manchester United tomorrow wouldn't be a bad result...

     
    Didier Deschamps (Olympique de Marseille)
    Olympique de Marseille coach Didier Deschamps has underlined the importance of keeping a clean sheet against Manchester United at Stade Velodrome in the Champions League on Wednesday evening.

    AFP quotes the French tactician as replying when asked if a goalless draw would be a good result for him, "It wouldn't be a bad result in itself, let's say 50 percent of our work would have been done because we hadn't let in a goal.

    "To defend well is great, but we also have to be capable of creating problems too."

    He continued, "If Manchester are at 100 per cent it's going to be very complicated. Manchester are one of the favourites to win the Champions League.

    "It would be logical if they go through, so it'll be a real exploit to knock out this team."

    "I'm convinced the atmosphere won't be anything like it is for a league game," the French legend added. "People come from afar. That's a plus for our players and an added pressure for our rivals, even if United's players are used to this type of atmosphere.

    "People speak of the atmosphere at Old Trafford as an advantage, I don't see why we can't say the same thing about the Velodrome."

    Deschamps also hailed the winning mentality that Manchester United have cultivated over the years and stated that winning is now part of their DNA.

    "United have an important history, they've won an enormous number of titles," explained Deschamps. "Their manager Alex Ferguson symbolises that, he's in his 24th season, that's something exceptional in the world of football.

    "This club has the culture of winning, like all the great clubs. The culture of winning is like a lightning conductor in the big clubs, it's in their DNA.

    "Nani, [Wayne] Rooney or [Dimitar] Berbatov, to name just a few, can make the difference at any moment. I've heard it said many times this team doesn't display a great team style, but I can't help noticing they don't concede many goals - only one in the six group games - they score a lot, and above all they win a lot of matches!"

    UEFA launch probe into crowd trouble after flares and lasers at Manchester United's draw against Marseille

     

     

    Lighting up: Manchester United fans lit flares inside the Stade Velodrome on Wednesday

     

     

    UEFA will probe Manchester United and Marseille fans after crowd trouble in Wednesday's Champions League clash.

    The European governing body has has opened disciplinary cases against both clubs after the goalless draw at the Stade Velodrome.

    UEFA is studying reports that United fans broke competition rules by lighting flares during the match.

    They will also investigate Marseille after fans shone laser pens at various United players, including to distract Nani while he was attempting to take a corner.

     

    Beaming: Nani (left) and John O'Shea were victims of the lasers during the Champions League match

     

     

     

    Three years ago, UEFA fined Lyon over 'incidents of an unsporting nature' when fans aimed laser beams at United old boy Cristiano Ronaldo.

    UEFA's disciplinary panel will hear the cases on March 17.
