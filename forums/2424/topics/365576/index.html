    United fail with Douglas trial bid

     

    Manchester United have failed in a bid to take Douglas Costa on trial as his Brazilian club feel the young forward has already proved himself.

    Douglas has a price tag of around £18million on his head and his agent has already admitted he doubts whether United will come up with a big enough bid to convince Gremio to sell.

    United have been tracking the Brazil Under-20 forward for more than 12 months and are believed to be very keen on signing him but Sir Alex Ferguson wanted to see the 18-year-old in action at United's training HQ for 20 days first.

    However, Gremio say he is too good for such a proposal.

    Their president Duda Kroeff said: "We have said to them [Manchester United] that this will not happen.

    "Douglas is not at the stage where he would go through such a situation."

    Douglas has played less than 20 first-team games for Gremio, but he has shone for Brazil at youth level, which has seen him attract interest from almost every big club in Europe.
