    Van Der Sar: I Was Half The Keeper At Juve

    Speaking ahead of their pre-season clash with his former team at Old Trafford tonight, the veteran Dutch keeper suggested that the Bianconeri have learned a hard lesson as a result of the Calciopoli scandal.

    "I am glad they are back for the players," said Van Der Sar, who was at Juventus between 1999 and 2001 before making his first foray into England by signing for Fulham.

    "But of course the allegations and certain things that happened were not nice.

    "I just hope they learn from it and they will be part of the Italian elite again."

    The 37-year-old went on to insist that he does not regret not having signed for United in the first place when he left Ajax in 1999.

    "Maybe it did not end the way I wanted but it was a great experience," he added.

    "I never played to my full potential. It was my first time abroad and Juventus played quite differently to the way we played at Ajax.

    "I had to get used to a lot of different things and although I was able to speak the language in about three months, sometimes on the pitch I was half a second late shouting something and the moment had gone, which was unfortunate.

    "A lot of people say that I should have gone to United instead but I had already had two meetings with Juventus before United came in.

    "It was not like I was talking to Palermo or Fiorentina. They were two great clubs on the same level and you cannot make choices with the benefit of hindsight."

    Del Piero Thanks United Fans For Applause

    For Juventus it's time to draw up a first balance after their tour in England. A victory against Arsenal (1-0), a defeat against HSV (3-0), and a scoreless draw against English and European champions Manchester United: these were the results obtained by the Bianconeri, the first two matches being in the Emirates Cup organised by the Gunners.

    On his official website, Juve captain Alex Del Piero talked about the match against the Red Devils.

    "Our English away trip ended with a 0-0 draw against the reigning European champions. In these matches we've had a chance to play matches against international opponents and prepare for the Champions League preliminary match, which is crucial for our season," he stated.

    Despite it being only a friendly, the match against Manchester United will remain in Pinturicchio's heart for a long time, specifically because of the applause he received by the English fans.

    "Personally, I'm very happy about the way I'm playing right now. And on my exit from Old Trafford, the applause from the fans moved me and gave me a big emotion, for this I thank them," he concluded.
