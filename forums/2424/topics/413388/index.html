    SCHOLES TACKLE INJURED ROONEY - REPORTS

    .
    .
    Wayne Rooney's ankle was injured in training on Tuesday following a tackle by Paul Scholes, according to reports.

     

    As if Sir Alex Ferguson's extraordinary press briefing wasn't sufficient backpage fodder for one day, Rooney was stretchered off from training just hours before the Manchester United boss confirmed the striker wanted to leave Old Trafford.

     

    Other than ruling the player out of the tie with Bursaspor, United have given no indication as to the severity of Rooney's latest ankle problem.

     

    However, The Times reports that 'Rooney was later spotted walking on crutches' and claims the injury was a result of 'a tackle by Paul Scholes'.

    Yes, It's a win, but are we going to get used to winning by such a slim margin?

    Overall a decent effort but here are my takes from this match:

    Kuszczak: Was not called into action at all throughout the match, but my heart still skips a beat whenever a cross or corner comes in.

    Rafael: Solid Right Wing Back play from this young Brazilian. Attack and Defence, he's everywhere. This slot is his to keep for years to come.

    Vidic: Tried to be clever on a couple of occasions and slipped but luckily no Bursaspor player capitalise. Needs to work on his leadership skills on the pitch as well as United's attack died down the moment they scored the goal.

    Smalling: Decent outing but could improve his decision making.

    Evra: Simply tormented the left flank, making those clever runs behind the defence.

    Anderson: Shields the ball well, finally made a couple of excellent through balls but he was sloppy at times too with his weak and soft passes but i was surprised he was substituted instead of that rubbish Carrick.

    Fletcher: Sloppy performance, takes ages to pick out a pass.

    Carrick: Complete rubbish. Cant pass the ball properly and thinks he's Scholes. Cant shoot but chose to and completely air ball the shot.

    Nani: Showed flashes of brilliance but cant deny that he's the Man of the Match with the solo effort yet again.

    Kiko: A targetman who cant find the target. Seems to have lost his touch since his emergence a couple of years back.

    Park: Headless cockroach let loose. Only managed a couple of runs behind defence and tracking back to help in defence.

    Obertan: Came on as a subsitute and immediately impressed with a 20min cameo. Will we get to see him more often?

    Chicharito: Perhaps should have scored but definitely the one to look out for and will improve with Solksjaer's guidance.
