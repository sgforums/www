    The Manchester United winger's future has been the subject of intense speculation for some time, with the Real Madrid chairman claiming on multiple occasions that a deal had been agreed, only for the British outfit to deny it right after.

    Ronaldo has been coy when quizzed on where he wants to be since the end of Euro 2008, as he is aware Manchester United hold all the aces over his future.

    He has now been extremely vague once again when questioned about his future, having gone as far as to say that he could well continue at Old Trafford.

    "What I want is to be happy regardless of where I play. If I am motivated, any place is fine," Ronaldo told ESPN.

    When asked about his opinion on the fact that his rumoured exit from Manchester United has been one of this summer's biggest transfer sagas, he said: "The big players are always wanted by big clubs, not only me. This is something normal. I had already been wanted in previous years."

    He further added that he is not "anxious" about his future and does not know the next chapter of the Ronaldo saga: "Only God knows it."
