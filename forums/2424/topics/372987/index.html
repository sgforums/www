    Wenger: Bring on United

     

     

    ARSENE WENGER is ready to renew rivalries with Alex Ferguson after another Gunners romp.

    Arsenal host Celtic in midweek before travelling to Old Trafford for next Saturday's tea-time game. And boss Wenger is licking his lips at the prospect after his side followed up their 6-1 win over Everton with a 4-1 pummelling of Portsmouth.

    He said: "We have had a brilliant start. It should create confidence and momentum. "We now go into another week which will be another test against Celtic and then Manchester United. "It looks to be exciting and interesting."

    Abou Diaby netted twice in his 100th game for Arsenal. And Wenger drew comparisons with former Gunners skipper Patrick Vieira.

    The Frenchman said: "When he decides to play more defensively, he is exactly like Vieira. "He has quick transition from box to box. When we are on the counter-attack, he is fantastic. "This could be his season. He has all you dream to have to play in the Premier League."

    Arsenal captain Cesc Fabregas was replaced at half-time by Aaron Ramsey. And Wenger revealed the Spain midfielder could be out for a fortnight if his hamstring problem proves serious.

    He added: "If it is a little pull, he will be out for three weeks, if it is just tightness, maybe he can get away for Wednesday. "In 48 hours, we will have a better view."

    William Gallas and Ramsey also netted for Arsenal, while Younes Kaboul replied for Portsmouth.

    CESC FABREGAS could miss Arsenal's crunch visit to Manchester United next Saturday evening with a hamstring injury.

    There are fears that the Gunners skipper could be out for three weeks after being subbed at half-time in the 4-1 thrashing of Portsmouth.

    Boss Arsene Wenger said Fabregas - who is unlikely to make Wednesday's Champions League qualifier against Celtic - will have a fitness test today to assess the extent of the injury.

    He said: "Cesc has a hamstring problem. He will be short for Wednesday certainly.

    "If it's a little pull he'll be out for three weeks. If it's just a tightness maybe he can get away for the Celtic game but after 48 hours we'll have a better view."

    It will be a massive blow for the Gunners as the Spanish midfielder has been in top form this season.

    He was a doubt before the 2-0 win at Celtic at Parkhead with the same hamstring niggle but played the whole game.

    But the problem got worse in the Pompey clash and it seems the Spanish ace could now be out until September.

    COMEBACK ... United defender Wes Brown

     

     

    WES BROWN is pushing for a recall ahead of Manchester United's clash with Arsenal at Old Trafford on Saturday.

    The defender's pre-season preparations were hampered by a thigh injury.

    He featured as a substitute in United's opening-day win at Birmingham before struggling during the shock defeat to Burnley at Turf Moor four days later.

    Brown, 29, was left out completely by Alex Ferguson for Saturday's win at Wigan but came through an hour of last night's reserve-team triumph over Bolton.

    And his availability could be crucial considering Jonny Evans is struggling with an ankle injury.

    With Rio Ferdinand sidelined by a thigh problem and not likely to return until the Manchester derby on September 20, Fergie has limited options if Evans is ruled out.

    ARSENE WENGER has dubbed Arsenal's European tussle against Celtic tonight BIGGER than the game at Manchester United on Saturday.

    Gunners are 2-0 up in their Champions League play-off.

    But boss Wenger said: "This game is a more important one than Saturday for the club, by far. We have to give absolutely everything be- cause tomorrow you cannot come back.

    "Saturday, even if we drop points against Manchester United, we can always catch them up - you have 35 games still to go.

    "If we lose the game against Celtic, we have zero. It is a massive game."

    Arsenal could scoop around £25million by taking part in the Champions League group stages alone. But the Emirates chief insisted: "It's not the huge amount money that makes it such a huge fixture for us.

    "It's not the financial blow that matters, it's that we want to be in the Champions League and this is a much bigger game for us than Saturday."

    Arsenal are massive favourites to go through after their first-leg victory in Glasgow.

    But Wenger insisted: "It is not job done, it is job to do. That is how I approach it.

    "Celtic have no choice, they have to come out and have a go at us. We will be faithful to our tactical approach."
