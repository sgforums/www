    Man Utd Owners Reveal Ferguson's Decided To Sell Ronaldo To Madrid

    Manchester United manager Alex Ferguson was the man who made the ultimate decision to sell Portuguese icon Cristiano Ronaldo to Real Madrid, according to the club's American owners, the Glazer family, and also hinted that the Scotsman's hands will not be financially tied when it comes to dealing in this summer's transfer market.

    An £80 million offer was accepted by the Red Devils from the Spanish giants yesterday, and the player is expected to complete his move over the next couple of weeks or so.

    The American entrepreneurs' purchase of the club has led to a massive amount of debt at Old Trafford, but the Glazers were at pains to point out that the sale of the superstar was unrelated to that particular aspect of the club's structure.

    A spokesman from the Glazer family told Sky Sports News, "The idea Manchester United are motivated by a debt burden is just not true. It is not an issue.

    "The fact is Cristiano Ronaldo decided after six years it was time to move on and the manager said 'okay'.

    "Sir Alex Ferguson is in total control of his squad. He's empowered to make whatever decisions he thinks are in the best interests of Manchester United. That continues to be the case.

    "A substantial number of world-class players have been brought to the club in the last few years and that will still be the case."

    Names such as Franck Ribery and Karim Benzema have already been touted as potential transfer targets for the Red Devils, and it seems Ferguson has been given a green light to go ahead and try and secure the services of whichever players he thinks can take the club forward.
