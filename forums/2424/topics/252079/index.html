    PEARCE: RON IS OUTSTANDING

    Stuart Pearce is relieved he never had to deal with the threat posed by Manchester United star Cristiano Ronaldo. As the finest full-back of his generation and the owner of 78 England caps, Pearce came face-to-face with all the top wingers of his time, and usually came out on top.

    Yet the Manchester City manager accepts Ronaldo is a phenomenon the likes of which are rarely seen. And, while he admires the Portugal international, he is glad it will not be his responsibility to stop him when City tackle United in the Manchester derby at Eastlands on Saturday.

    "I am glad my time in the game finished before his started," smiled Pearce. "He is outstanding. Together with Didier Drogba he has been head and shoulders above anyone else in the league.

    "Once he gets into his stride he is very difficult to stop because of the speed of movement in his feet. "He has pace but he also has power and has adapted to the physical side of the game in England very quickly.

    "Age-wise, he is nowhere near his prime yet either. I am delighted he is playing his football in the Premiership and hopefully, he will be here for many more years to come.

    "One day he might even see the light and come and join me at Manchester City." Pearce's final comment was delivered with his tongue firmly in his cheek. The City boss knows his club could virtually sell their entire first-team squad and still not raise enough to buy Ronaldo. Instead, he will tell his team to try to prevent him getting the ball as the best way of stopping him wreaking havoc.
