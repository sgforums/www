    Man City v Man Utd preview

    Manchester City will be without Joey Barton for the derby, while Rio Ferdinand could be recalled by Manchester United. Barton has been suspended by City following his training ground attack on team-mate Ousmane Dabo earlier in the week. The midfielder will sit out the rest of the season, but City boss Stuart Pearce is confident the fracas will not unsettle his side ahead of Saturday's game.

    With Barton missing for City, rookie Michael Johnson is expected to come into the team, although Trevor Sinclair is also an option. Dabo was on the bench for last week's 2-0 home defeat to Aston Villa, but is unlikely to be available for selection as a result of his injuries from the Barton attack.

    Georgios Samaras and Emile Mpenza started up front against Villa, but drew a blank as City failed to break their home hoodoo. City have not scored at home in the Premiership since New Year's Day, so Pearce must decide whether to freshen up his attack.

    Ferdinand is expected to come back into the side after a groin injury, and he could take the place of Nemanja Vidic at centre-half. Patrice Evra is available after a European suspension, although he will hope for better memories than on his last visit to The City of Manchester Stadium.

    The French full-back made his United debut in the corresponding fixture last season, but was substituted at half-time in a 3-1 defeat. With Gary Neville unlikely to play in the league again this season, either John O'Shea or Wes Brown will be at right-back, depending on how Sir Alex Ferguson organises his back-line.

    Louis Saha is pressing for a starting role after a hamstring injury, while Alan Smith is also chasing a recall to the side. With Ferguson having bemoaned the timing of the game, he must decide if there are any tired legs, or minds, from Wednesday's match in Milan.

    Man Utd boss Ferguson to shake up derby XI

    Manchester United boss Sir Alex Ferguson admits he'll be freshening up his line-up for today's early kickoff against Manchester City.

    "We all know European games do take it out of you and, with the 12.45 kick-off, we will certainly have to consider that," said Ferguson on Sky Sports News.

    "There are some players who are available for that, in that respect - Rio Ferdinand, obviously, Louis Saha, Alan Smith, Patrice Evra, of course, is available after he was suspended on Wednesday.

    "We are just assessing what the players are like today. It's difficult to assess how players are after a European defeat, and also playing on Wednesday then a lunchtime kick-off.

    "The fact it's a derby game, it's difficult to assess what the right thing to do is as they all want to play in a derby game, so I will have to make good decisions on that.

    "At least for the first time in a few weeks there are some options to freshen it up if I want to."
