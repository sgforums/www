Two matches ban for Vidic

  • Dead_Man_Inc
    16 Mar 09, 19:41

    Nemanja Vidic will miss United's next two fixtures in the Barclays Premier League after being sent off against Liverpool on Saturday.

    The Football Association's official website confirms the Serbian defender is "suspended from all football until the Club have completed 2 first team matches for a Sending Off in a match played on 14/03/2009."

    Vidic misses one match for his offence against Liverpool - denying a goalscoring opportunity for the visitors' skipper Steven Gerrard - and another because he has now been sent off twice in domestic fixtures this season.

    His previous red card in English football was also against Liverpool, in the 2-1 league defeat at Anfield on 13 September. Vidic was suspended for one match then, United's 1-1 draw away at Stamford Bridge on the following Sunday.

    This time, Vidic will be ineligible to play away at Fulham this coming Saturday, 21 March and the home game against Aston Villa on Sunday 5 April. The latter fixture will be brought forward by 24 hours if the Reds are drawn to play on Tuesday 7 April in the Champions League but the Serb will still miss the Villans' visit, regardless of the date.

    Vidic's other dismissal, in the FIFA Club World Cup final, is irrelevant. That misdemeanour caused him to miss United's 0-0 first-leg draw with Inter in the Champions League.
