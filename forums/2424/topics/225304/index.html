    Alan Curbishley faces a baptism of fire in his first game in charge of West Ham United when Manchester United visit Upton Park on Sunday. Having sacked Alan Pardew on Monday, following last weekend's 4-0 defeat at Bolton Wanderers, The Hammers moved quickly to appoint a replacement.

    Curbishley stepped into the breach on Wednesday and will hope his debut at the helm will prompt a change in his fortunes against The Red Devils.

    During his time at Charlton Athletic, Curbishley never got one over Sir Alex Ferguson's mob in the Premiership, although he will draw only minimal comfort from The Hammers' record against United. West Ham have yet to beat Sunday's opponents at home since the Premiership started, but Curbishley will be guaranteed a full house and full commitment from his players.

    In selecting his first side, he will have to pick a team without the central defensive pairing of Danny Gabbidon and Anton Ferdinand. George McCartney and James Collins paired up at Bolton last week and Curbishley has few alternatives at the back.

    Christian Dailly was given a game in midfield and will hope to keep his place, with Javier Mascherano unlikely to be included. Up front, Marlon Harewood and Carlos Tevez started last week, but Bobby Zamora and Teddy Sheringham are pressing for recalls.

    The current Premiership leaders travel down to the capital having won all bar one of their away games in the league this season. Ferguson could stick with the team which won the Manchester derby 3-1 last week after picking up no new injuries.

    Patrice Evra missed that game with a minor injury and could again sit out with Gabriel Heinze starting at left-back. Ryan Giggs will move into sole possession of second spot in United's all-time list of appearances, if he plays at Upton Park. With United having another midweek off, it would be a surprise if Ferguson opts to utilise his squad players for Sunday's match.

    Player to watch: Ryan Giggs

    WEST HAM UNITED take on the League leaders Manchester United in Alan Curbishley's first match in charge. The new Hammers appointee will hope he can make a better start at Upton Park than he made on his departure from the Valley. His last game in charge of Charlton was a 4-0 defeat to United at Old Trafford on 7 May. Curbishley admits that he never fulfilled his potential as a player at West Ham, and hopes to do better as a manager. A first ever managerial victory over Manchester United would be a massive step in the right direction.

    Bobby ZAMORA is West Ham's leading scorer with five goals - all in the Premiership. ZAMORA needs one more to total 100 League goals.

    West Ham boss Alan Curbishley ahead of his first game in charge:
    "I do not think I will change things too much this weekend and I have not got any pre-conceived ideas.
    "However, the players all owe Alan Pardew a performance, because there has been a lot of criticism of them.

    "It would be great if that was with a win as well on Sunday."

    MANCHESTER UNITED are defending the only remaining unbeaten away record in the highest League. They have won seven of their eight matches on the road, including the last three, and need to score once to bring their total of Premiership goals scored away from Old Trafford all-time to 500.

    Sir Alex Ferguson's side are aiming to extend the longest current unbeaten sequence in the top tier to 13, with a 15th maximum of the League campaign. Victory would give United 47 points - the most they've ever had after the first 18 matches of a Premier League campaign.

    Louis SAHA is United's leading scorer with 12 League and Cup goals. Paul SCHOLES has scored seven Premiership goals against West Ham. SAHA and Wayne ROONEY are the club's joint top marksmen in the Premiership with eight each.

    Manchester United boss Alex Ferguson on Alan Curbishley being appointed West manager this week:
    "You always wonder what kind of effect it will have in the dressing room.
    "We're expecting it to be difficult. Players will have a desire to do well and impress the new manager.

    "It will be hard and we've got no illusions about that, but we have a good record at Upton Park."
