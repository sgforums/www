    Ferguson: Manchester United Must Keep Focus

    Manchester United boss Sir Alex Ferguson has been keen to taper expectations after his side's Carling Cup conquest kept them on course to win the quintuple.

    Manchester United already lifted the Club World Cup trophy earlier this campaign and are looking in good position to do the same with that of the Champions League, FA Cup and the Premier League especially.

    Ferguson knows how burdensome big expectations can be, so his most recent comments following their success over Tottenham on the weekend were reasoned rather than rambunctious.  

    "I'm not getting carried away with it, only the media will. We will keep our feet on the ground," he was quoted as saying by BBC.

    "We could go to Fulham in the FA Cup, the ball comes off someone's backside and we are out of that one," he warned.

    United are seven points clear in the Premier League, face Fulham in the quarter-final of the FA Cup, and their Champions League tie with Inter of Italy is deadlocked at 0-0 after the first leg in Milan last week.
