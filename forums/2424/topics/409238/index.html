    NICE ONE MATE ... but Fergie (right) can't wait to face Smith in the Champions League

    ALEX FERGUSON is relishing another Battle of Britain night against Walter Smith's Rangers.

    Manchester United are set to face the Scottish champions in their Group C opener at Old Trafford on September 14.

    Smith worked as Fergie's No 2 in 2004 and the United boss said: "Walter's already been on about tickets! We're both really looking forward to it."

    United faced Celtic in the Champions League in 2006 and 2008 and Rangers in 2003.

    Ibrox supporters were also in Manchester for the 2008 UEFA Cup final at Eastlands - when some fans caused trouble in the city.

    But Supporters Trust chairman Stephen Smith rubbished talk of a travel ban insisting: "There were 150,000 Rangers fans in Manchester for the final and only a tiny fraction were involved in trouble.

    United also face Spaniards Valencia and Bursaspor of Turkey.

    Ferguson added: "It's a good draw for us. The only trip of any great distance is the one to Turkey."
