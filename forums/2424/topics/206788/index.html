    GROUP F

    MAN UNITED
    Celtic
    Benfica
    FC Copenhagen

    United have been drawn against Celtic, Benfica and FC Copenhagen in the Champions League group stages.

    The Group F clashes between United and Celtic will be the first competitive matches between the pair.

    There have been numerous friendlies down the years both at Old Trafford and Parkhead, the most recent of which took place in Manchester in May for Roy Keane's testimonial match.

    The Reds will be looking to gain revenge on Benfica after they knocked United out of the competition last season.

    The Portuguese side's 2-1 win at The Stadium of Light last December also resulted in United finishing bottom of the group and in turn missing out on a UEFA Cup place. United overcame Benfica 2-1 in the match at Old Trafford thanks to a late goal from Ruud van Nistelrooy.

    FC Copenhagen will be making their Champions League debut after overcoming Ajax in the qualifying round. They are only the third Danish team to reach the group stages since it was introduced during the 92/93 season - Aalborg BK and Brondby IF are the others.

    United's last trip to Copenhagen came during the 98/99 Treble-winning season when they travelled to Denmark to face Brondby.

    The match itself took place at FC Copenhagen's Parken Stadium and ended in a 6-2 victory for United thanks to goals from Andy Cole, Roy Keane, Ole Gunnar Solskjaer, Dwight Yorke and two from Ryan Giggs.
