    TEVEZ BACK IN UNITED FRAY

     
     
     
    Manchester United striker Carlos Tevez is in contention to feature in Wednesday night's Carling Cup tie against Derby. Tevez has missed United's last two matches after he was allowed to return to his native Argentina for personal reasons.
     

    However, he was due to resume training with the Red Devils on Monday and is in line to figure in the first leg of the semi-final in midweek.

    "Carlos will be back in training on Monday and he'll be involved on Wednesday," United boss Sir Alex Ferguson told MUTV.

    Tevez has been instrumental in United's wins over QPR and Blackburn Rovers in the previous two rounds and is expected to start at Pride Park.

    Ferguson will largely select from the same squad which overcame Southampton 3-0 in the third round of the FA Cup on Sunday.

    He added: "It'll be virtually the same squad [that travelled to Southampton]. I might leave out one or two, but it'll be more or less the same group of players."

    Clough takes over as Derby boss

    Nigel Clough has left Burton to become the new manager of Derby.

    The 42-year-old former Nottingham Forest star has left Burton after 10 years to take up a new challenge with the Championship strugglers.

    Clough, whose father Brian steered Derby to the League title in 1972, leaves Burton with the club at the top of the Blue Square Premier.

    Derby, who are still in the Carling Cup, have been without a manager since Paul Jewell left on 28 December.

    Manchester United

     

     Nemanja Vidic insists Manchester United's lengthy Carling Cup campaign is a help rather than a hindrance. United head to Derby County on Wednesday for the first leg of a semi-final that means every January midweek is filled with competitive action. "This is not a distraction," Vidic said.
