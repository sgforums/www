    Boss relieved at slender loss

    The margin of defeat was one of the scarce positives for Sir Alex Ferguson following United's Carling Cup reverse at Derby County.

    The Reds boss believes his side could have been staring at a mountain rather than a molehill of a task for the second leg, after the Rams fluffed a few chances at Pride Park.

    "The positive is we've only lost 1-0," reflected Sir Alex. "I think we're lucky to only lose 1-0. You need a bit of luck in the cup and we've had a bit of luck tonight.

    "It's a fantastic result for us considering the performance. We just didn't play well.

    "I think we were overworried about their crossing and setpiece play. I think we looked a bit panicky at the back. We normally can deal with that very well but we didn't do that tonight.

    "We could have lost four goals so 1-0 is a great result for us and it gives us a fantastic chance now at Old Trafford.

    "In any semi-final you hope to give yourself a bit of breathing space by winning the first leg but if we take any team back to Old Trafford with a one-nil deficit, we expect to overcome them. I'm sure we will (overcome Derby)."

    Sir Alex will again back his young players to finish the job, after seeing them conquer Middlesbrough, QPR and Blackburn at home in previous rounds.

    "I think we'll persevere with our younger players," Sir Alex confirmed.

    "They deserve to play. Gibson was one of our better players tonight and I have sympathy for Welbeck - he never really had any service so it's unfair to judge him."
