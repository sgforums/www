    Sir Alex Ferguson has confirmed Rafael da Silva should be fit to face Chelsea on Tuesday.

    The Manchester United defender was stretchered off with a knee injury in the Reds’ Champions League first-leg victory at Stamford Bridge last Wednesday, immediately casting doubt on his involvement in the return leg at Old Trafford.

    The young Brazilian did manage to limp unassisted onto the United team bus that evening, but the Reds still sent the 20-year-old for a precautionary scan the following day.

    At his press conference on Friday morning, Sir Alex revealed there had been “no bruising or ligament damage to his knee” and was confident the right-back would be fit to line up against Chelsea on Tuesday.

    There was a similar diagnosis after the Reds’ 2-0 win against Fulham on Saturday.

    “Rafael should be okay for Tuesday,” he revealed, while also acknowledging the return of a number of other players.

    “Wes Brown’s back, Antonio Valencia’s back… John O’Shea’s been out with a hamstring injury and he’s come through [the Fulham game] well. Players are coming back and that gives us a heck of a boost.”

    The boss reserved special mention for winger Antonio Valencia, who again shone in Saturday’s 2-0 win.

    “The man’s a marvellous footballer,” Sir Alex said. “I think we chose the right time to take him off today, to keep him fresh for Chelsea. He’ll have an important role on Tuesday.”

    The Manchester United manager, Sir Alex Ferguson, has a major injury doubt over the Brazilian full-back Rafael ahead of their Champions League quarter-final second leg against Chelsea at Old Trafford.

    Chelsea arrive at Old Trafford for their Champions League quarter-final second leg as underdogs as they look to overturn a one-goal deficit - but there are plenty of reasons why they can do it.

     

     

     

     

    Away goals

    United fans may have left Stamford Bridge last Wednesday celebrating the grabbing of a 'precious' away goal, but they would have been better off winning 2-1 in the first leg than 1-0.

    That result means that it is not possible for United to prevail on away goals in this tie. If Chelsea manage to score more than once, United will have to match them over the 90 minutes to win the tie.

    United have not won back-to-back games against Chelsea since the 2002-03 season, when Diego Forlan grabbed the winner in League Cup and Premier League clashes at Old Trafford.

     

    One eye on Wembley

    While United's players will all have Saturday's FA Cup semi-final against local rivals Manchester City on their minds, Chelsea have a trip to West Brom this weekend, a comparatively cosier fixture.

    For evidence of just how the derby at Wembley could affect United, look no further than the 3-0 drubbing City had handed to them last night at Anfield.

    That result also means that Chelsea remained third in the table, easing the domestic pressure on their players greatly.

     

    A 'fair' referee?

    Ferguson is currently in the middle of a five-match touchline ban for suggesting referee Martin Atkinson, the official in charge when Chelsea beat United 2-1 last month, was not ‘a fair referee’.

    If he believes such things as fair and unfair officials exist at the top level of the game, then he may not find Olegario Manuel Bartolo Benquerenca's record good reading.

    The Portuguese official has taken charge of two Champions League matches at Old Trafford, against CSKA Moscow and Rangers, and United have won neither. Conversely, Benquerenca has taken charge of two European games involving Chelsea, blowing the final whistle on two wins for the Blues.

    And, just to add an extra layer of faux-significance, the same referee took charge of England's win over Wales last month, in which he awarded a penalty scored by Frank Lampard.

    Firing squad: Hernandez and Park send United through and leave Ancelotti's future in the balance

     

    Kneesy does it: Manchester United's dreams of a Treble are still very much alive

     

     

    Manchester United took another big step towards the Treble on Tuesday night as they reached the Champions League semi-finals for the fourth time in five years.

    Javier Hernandez and Park Ji-sung gave United a 3-1 aggregate win after substitute Didier Drogba equalised on the night for 10-man Chelsea, who had Ramires sent off in the second half.

    Defeat means Carlo Ancelotti's side are likely to finish the season empty-handed and leaves the Italian with his job on the line.

    Ancelotti later admitted he may have blundered by starting with £50million striker Fernando Torres, who was substituted at half-time after failing to score for the 11th game.

    Ferguson, who was taking charge of his 100th European game at Old Trafford, paid tribute to Ryan Giggs who set up both of United’s goals, saying: ‘He’s incredible — a unique person and player. His contribution is big because of the goal he set up in the first game, and the part he played in the goals tonight. To play at 37 requires tremendous sacrifice.’

    Drogba, a half-time replacement for Torres, cancelled out Hernandez’s goal after 76 minutes but United were ahead again through Park 21 seconds later.

     

    Easy Pea-sy: Javier Hernandez kept up his habit of scoring important goals at important times

     

     

    Ferguson added: ‘That was the break we got in the game, to be honest. It was a stupid goal for us to lose and we had to respond quickly. Park’s record in big games is fantastic.

    ‘We played very well against a very good team. Even with 10 men they had a go and credit to them, but I thought we were the better team on the night.’

    Questioned about winning the Treble as United prepare for Saturday’s FA Cup semi-final with Manchester City, Ferguson said: ‘I keep being asked about it but you need luck to do that. Saturday is a massive game.

    ‘Momentum is a great thing and everybody is desperate to play in every game. They’re desperate to play against City on Saturday.’

     

    In safe hands? Carlo Ancelotti seems unlikely to hold on to his job for much longer

     

     

    United are now set for a likely semi-final meeting with Schalke.

    Ancelotti insisted he does not fear for his future following Chelsea’s latest European failure. He said: ‘I’m not concerned. I have to work. I have to try to do my best. It’s not my decision whether I stay or not here. I haven’t spoken with him (Roman Abramovich).’

    Asked if it was a mistake to start with Torres rather than Drogba, he added: ‘Maybe. Could be. But I thought a long time to take this decision. I preferred to start with Fernando for this kind of game, with these kind of tactics. Didier played very well in the second half.’
