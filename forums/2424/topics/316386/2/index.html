    Chelsea v Bolton and Wigan v Man Utd referees chosen

     

    Steve Bennett has been appointed to take of the title decider at the JJB Stadium where table-topping Manchester United take on Steve Bruce's Wigan.

     

    The other match which sees second-placed Chelsea host Bolton will be refereed by Chris Foy.

    Two of the three red cards Cristiano Ronaldo has received during his time at Old Trafford have been handed out by Bennett and United manager Sir Alex Ferguson said Bennett would have taken "great delight" in sending off the Portuguese for headbutting Portsmouth's Richard Hughes in August 2007.

    Ferguson also accused Bennett of failing to protect Ronaldo after the player was sent off for a reckless lunge during the 3-1 defeat away to Manchester City in January 2006.

    WIGAN are looking forward to being the most hated team on the planet — by wrecking Manchester United’s title dream. Chairman Dave Whelan knows United’s 330million fans worldwide will probably never forgive his club if they deny Alex Ferguson’s side all three points at the JJB Stadium on Sunday.

     

    That would surely hand the title to Chelsea, who entertain Bolton in their final game. But even though Whelan is a great admirer of Fergie, he vowed the visitors would be getting no favours from Steve Bruce’s men.

     

    Whelan declared: “We would probably be the most disliked team in the world if we win. But we don’t mind that. We’re going to give it a go. “Talk of us rolling over and letting Sir Alex’s team win is rubbish. There is nothing we would like better than to roll Manchester United over.

     

    “If we can beat United, we’ll do it. Our lads are up for it big style. They are relaxed and confident after our fantastic win at Aston Villa last week, which guaranteed we would stay up. “If the players put on another performance like that, I’d say United have a hell of a fight on.

     

    “This match will be beamed worldwide and that is a fantastic opportunity. We are on centre stage of the Premier League. That’s huge.”
