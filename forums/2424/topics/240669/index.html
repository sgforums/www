    UEFA TURN DOWN LILLE'S GIGGS APPEAL

    UEFA have rejected Lille's attempts to invalidate Manchester United midfielder Ryan Giggs' winning goal in their Champions League match. The French side lost 1-0 to a late, quickly-taken free-kick in Lens last week but the club appealed to UEFA, claiming there had been a technical error by the referee in allowing the Wales winger to convert the set-piece without blowing his whistle.

    UEFA's control and disciplinary body previously vetoed the claim and that decision has been ratified by the UEFA's appeals body.

    "The appeals body supported the original decision that there was no proof of a technical error, and validated the result in Lens, a 1-0 victory to the English club," said a statement on UEFA's official website.

    "Lille will have the possibility of making a further appeal to the Court of Arbitration for Sport within three days of receiving notification of the UEFA appeals body's ruling."

    PREVIEW - Manchester United vs Lille

    Sir Alex Ferguson has warned his Manchester United players not to get sucked into a war with Lille at Old Trafford. The French side are still seething over Ryan Giggs' controversial late winner at the Stade Felix-Bollaert a fortnight ago.

    Having had two claims against the goal turned down by UEFA, Lille's sense of injustice has only increased, with their camp openly questioning Giggs' integrity over the quickly-taken free-kick the Welshman insists he would convert again if he had the chance.

    With the memories of Lille's last visit to Old Trafford - when Giggs sustained a fractured cheekbone following a dubious challenge from Efstathios Tavlaridis - still fresh in the mind, Ferguson has told his players not to let their minds get distracted from the task of protecting their narrow advantage and securing a quarter-final place.

    The United boss said: ``Our focus is firmly on qualifying, nothing else. `It is hard to understand why they are still going on about it but there is no reason for us to get involved in grievances or any emotional attachment to the game.

    `This is a big event for us. It is important we progress and do better than we have done in the previous few years.''

    Ferguson did allow himself a little chortle when it was suggested United should again try the quick free-kick routine which led to Lille almost storming off the field, a move likely to be punished by UEFA at an independent hearing on March 22, which will also encompass the security problems which saw riot police fire tear gas into the visiting section of the ground.

    However, the Scot grew much more serious when the specific allegations against Giggs were mentioned. Ferguson rebutted: ``I do not think we should try to defend Ryan Giggs given the career he has had. `His career does that for him. I won't get involved in that.''

    It is 11 years since Ajax became the only side in Champions League history to overturn a first-leg deficit inflicted on home soil to advance in a knockout tie - Panathinaikos the hapless victims - so United would not appear to have too much to disturb them.

    They may not have been at their best over the past couple of weeks but four successive away wins, culminating in Saturday's potentially pivotal Barclays Premiership victory at Liverpool, has emphasised the staying power of Ferguson's troops.

    Yet, in failing on two occasions to beat their French opponents last season, and by losing to both Copenhagen and Celtic already this term, the Red Devils know little is certain in European football these days.

    Ferguson continued: ``Europe is a test, it doesn't matter who you play.

    ``You cannot take anything for granted as it proved last year and against Celtic and Copenhagen, when we were very careless.

    ``But I trust the players because they are showing great expression in their game.

    ``We are approaching the game in a good mind and with a strong spirit, so we have a big chance.''

    With Louis Saha injured and Patrice Evra doubtful, Henrik Larsson and Gabriel Heinze seem certain to start, while Alan Smith is virtually guaranteed a place on the bench even though he has not made a first-team appearance since the Carling Cup defeat at Southend in November.
