    Owen Hargreaves has told Manchester United fans that they can expect much more from him this season.

    Although most Red Devils supporters will say they were happy with the 27-year-old's contribution to their domestic and European success last season considering that his campaign was hampered by injury, the Canada-born England international insists that he was actually a long way from his best.

    "There is a lot more to come from me," he was quoted as saying by Sky Sports News.

    "I don't think I played a game healthy last season. I was at about 50 or 60% at best.

    "When I had another scan on the knee this summer it had not improved, so I came back early to try to fix it at the beginning of pre-season.

    "Hopefully I will be fit pretty soon."

    Hargreaves, who arrived at Old Trafford from Bayern Munich last summer after spending much of the 2006-07 season on the sidelines with a broken leg, is still not quite match-fit, and his latest comments suggest that United's next match against Portsmouth still comes a little too early for him.

    "I am not going to set a timetable but I am close," he added.

    "I am working on my fitness every day and while I have not joined in with the lads yet, hopefully it will happen this week.

    "Whether I am fit for Portsmouth or the match after that, the most important thing is I am healthy for the rest of the season."

    "I know how much I better I can get being around the players and coaching staff here," he added of his move from Bayern.

    "I have not even come close to fulfilling my potential yet. And, providing I stay healthy, this could be a really big season for me."
