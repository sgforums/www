    Rafa Benitez's £229m spending spree: The 76 players signed during the Liverpool manager's reign

     

     

    Star signing: Rafa Benitez parades Spanish striker Fernando Torres

     

     

    Those leaping to the defence of under-fire Liverpool manager Rafa Benitez have pointed to his lack of muscle in transfer market. But the records show that the Spaniard has spent £228,600,000 since arriving at Anfield in 2004.

    Sportsmail brings you the full rundown of Benitez's extraordinary shopping basket. Let us know what you think of his purchases by leaving your comments below.

     

     

     

    2004

    Josemi

    Cost: £2m (Malaga, July 2004)

    Where is he now? Benitez's first signing at Liverpool made a decent start to his Anfield career, but suspension saw Steve Finnan establish himself as first-choice right-back. Signed for Villarreal in a straight-swap deal with Jan Kromkamp. Now playing for Mallorca.

    Xabi Alonso

    Cost: £10.5m (Real Sociedad, August 2004)

    Where is he now? Joined Real Madrid in the summer for £30m, after five season at the club winning three major honours at the club.

    Antonio Nunez

    Cost: £1.5m - part of the Michael Owen deal (Real Madrid, August 2004)

    Where is he now? Became the first player in Liverpool history to score his only goal in a major final in the Carling Cup defeat to Chelsea. After leaving Anfield, the Spanish midfielder spent some time in the La Liga before signing for current club Apollon Limassol in Greece.

    Luis Garcia

    Cost: £6m (Barcelona, August 2004)

    Where is he now? Had three season at Anfield before moving to Atletico Madrid in the deal that brought Torres to the club.

     

    Total 4 players

    Energy: Luis Garcia impressed the fans during his stint with Liverpool

     

     

    2005

    Fernando Morientes

    Cost: £6.3m (Real Madrid, January 2005)

    Where is he now? Failed to live up to his reputation as a goalscorer and is now at Marseille after a spell at Valencia.

    Scott Carson

    Cost: £1m (Leeds United, January 2005)

    Where is he now? Another in the long line of future England No 1s, Carson spent most of his Liverpool career on loan. Suffered a torrid 2007, which ended with him fumbling a shot from Niko Kranjcar leading to England's failure to qualify for Euro 2008. Currently enjoying life at West Bromwich Albion.

    Mauricio Pellegrino

    Cost: Free (Valencia, January 2005)

    Where is he now? The first Argentinian to play for the club, the defender followed Benitez to Anfield from Valencia on a six-month deal. At the end of the stint, Pellegrino signed for Alaves before retiring a year later.

    Peter Crouch

    Cost: £7m (Southampton, June 2005)

    Where is he now? After three seasons at the club, Crouch made a surprise move to Portsmouth, and is now at Tottenham with former boss Harry Redknapp.

    Pepe Reina

    Cost: £6m (Villarreal, June 2005)

    Where is he now? Reina has been a consistent performer for Liverpool and is one of the leading goalkeepers in Europe

    Mohamed Sissoko

    Cost: £5.6m (Auxerre, June 2005)

    Where is he now? A surprise departure to Juventus in 2008 after two-and-a-half year stint at Anfield.

    Antonio Barragan

    Cost: Free (Sevilla, July 2005)

    Where is he now? After just one first team appearance in his 12 months at Anfield, the Spanish full-back signed for Deportivo La Coruna in a £1m deal.

    Bolo Zenden

    Cost: Free (Middlesbrough, July 2005)

    Where is he now? The Dutch midfielder had an injury-plagued time at Anfield and left for Marseille after two years at the club. After claiming he wanted to end his career in England, Zenden was snapped up by Sunderland where he made his debut against his former club in the famous beach ball match.

    Godwin Antwi

    Cost: Free (Real Zaragoza, August 2005)

    Where is he now? After guiding Liverpool to the 2006 FA Youth Cup, the Ghanaian defender was shipped out on loan to Accrington, Hartlepool, Tranmere and Hereford before being released by Benitez.

    Besian Idrizaj

    Cost: Free (LASK Linz, August 2005)

    Where is he now? Journeyman has had loan spells at Luton, Crytal Palace and Wacker Innsbruck before signing for Eilenburg and then Blackburn Rovers. Currently at Championship side Swansea City.

    Jack Hobbs

    Cost: £750,000 (Lincoln City, August 2005)

    Where is he now? The young defender had a sticky start to life at Anfield before being loaned out to Leicester City, who have now signed him on a permanent deal.

    Miki Roque

    Cost: Free (Lleida, August 2005)

    Where is he now? After four years and no first-team appearances, the Spanish defender left for Real Betis.

    Paul Anderson

    Cost: Swap deal with John Welsh (Hull City, November 2005)

    Where is he now? The young striker failed to live up to his tag as the 'next Michael Owen' and signed for Nottingham Forest after spending a year on loan at the City Ground.

     

    Total 13 players

    Glove affair: Scott Carson failed to make the breakthough

     

     

    2006

    Robbie Fowler

    Cost: Free (Manchester City, January 2006)

    Where is he now? Often referred to as the Premier League's 'most natual goalscorer', Fowler returned to Anfield after five years away. But as they say, you should never go back, and Fowler was shipped out to Cardiff after just one season. A short stint at Blackburn followed and Fowler is now rediscovering his old form at North Queensland Fury.

    Jan Kromkamp

    Cost: Swap deal with Josemi (Villarreal, January 2006)

    Where is he now? Unable to displace Irish international Steve Finnan in the starting line-up, the Dutch defender moved to PSV Eindhoven after just eight months and 14 appearances.

    Ryan Crowther

    Cost: Free (Stockport County, January 2006)

    Where is he now? After coming through the youth ranks at Edgeley Park, the 21-year-old winger was snapped up by Benitez. Currently a free agent after being released in August.

    Daniel Agger

    Cost: £5.8m (Brondby, January 2006)

    Where is he now? Agger has been plagued by injuries of late and is battling it out with Martin Skrtel to partner Jamie Carragher in the heart of the defence.

    David Martin

    Cost: £250,000 (MK Dons, January 2006)

    Where is he now? Another one for the future, the Essex-born goalkeeper has spent his Anfield career out on loan with Accrington Stanley, Leicester and Tranmere Rovers.

    Craig Bellamy

    Cost: £6m (Blackburn, June 2006)

    Where is he now? Sold to West Ham after one season after an argument with a team-mate and lack of goals, Bellamy is now at Man City.

    Mark Gonzalez

    Cost: £1.5m (Albacete, July 2006)

    Where is he now? Scored just three minutes into his debut in a 2-1 Champions League win over Maccabi Haifa and enjoyed an impressive season before being Benitez announced the club had agreed a £5m fee with Spanish side Real Betis. After Betis' relegation, Gonzalez signed for CSKA Moscow in a £6m deal.

    Gabriel Palletta

    Cost: £2m (Banfield, July 2006)

    Where is he now? The Argentinian signed for Liverpool only to return to his home country to join Boca Juniors in a swap deal involving Insua.

    Fabio Aurelio

    Cost: Free (Valencia, July 2006)

    Where is he now? The first Brazilian to ever play for Liverpool, the left back has become a fan favourite at Anfield, despite persistant injury problems causing him to lose his place to Insua.

    Martin Hansen

    Cost: Free (Brondby, August 2006)

    Where is he now? The Danish goalkeeper is currently enjoying life in Liverpool's reserve side.

    Dirk Kuyt

    Cost: £9m (Feyenoord, August 2006)

    Where is he now? Selfless and energetic Kuyt is a fans' favourite at Anfield.

    Nabil El Zhar

    Cost: Free (Saint-Etienne, October 2006)

    Where is he now? The French-born Moroccan winger is highly regarded by Benitez, who has singled him out as one for the future.

    Astrit Ajdarevic

    Cost: Free (Falkensberg, December 2006)

    Where is he now? After failing to make the grade Ajdarevic moved to Leicester last January.

    Cost: £6.7m (Birmingham, June 2007)

    Ryan Babel

    Cost: £11.5m (Ajax, July 2007)

    Where is he now? Dubbed the new Thierry Henry by the Dutch media, Babel has failed to make an impression on the Premier League.
