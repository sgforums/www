    SCHOLES: MORE TWISTS IN TITLE RACE

    Paul Scholes is refusing to get carried away by Manchester United's stunning four-goal triumph at Tottenham on Sunday. The Premiership leaders began what was supposed to be a far stiffer set of away fixtures than major rivals Chelsea on the title run-in with the biggest win at White Hart Lane in their illustrious history.

    United's triumph maintained a six-point advantage over Jose Mourinho's men at the top of the table and with relegation-threatened Charlton due to visit Old Trafford this weekend, there appears no immediate prospect of Chelsea closing the gap.

    However, like his manager Sir Alex Ferguson, Scholes prefers not to look too far in the future. With a trip to Liverpool looming at the beginning of next month and Portsmouth, Chelsea, Everton and Manchester City also on the list of places still to visit, the Red Devils' star feels there are a few unexpected developments still to come.

    "There will be a few twists and turns yet," he said. "Both teams have got some difficult games left, especially when you look at our away games. There is a long way to go." Although Scholes has been widely touted as a potential player of the year, with Arsenal skipper Thierry Henry among those who have voted for the former England star, the player himself believes there is room for improvement, particularly in the goalscoring stakes.

    Scholes bundled home the third at Tottenham, but it was only his sixth of the season, a tally the Salford-born star is not entirely happy with. "I would like to have a few more goals," he said. "I have six at the moment. Hopefully, there are more to come."

    Ferguson is awaiting a full assessment on the broken nose Edwin van der Sar suffered at the weekend. A precise timescale on the giant Dutchman's likely absence had not been made public, although it would be a major surprise if Tomasz Kuszczak was not called on for the Charlton clash and the FA Cup tie against Reading the following week, when Wayne Rooney will be suspended.
