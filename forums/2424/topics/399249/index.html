    Fergie Interested in signing Borriello

    Manchester United are reportedly considering a shock move for AC Milan forward Marco Borriello. Sir Alex Ferguson is a big admirer of in form Italian international and is looking for an effective partner for Wayne Rooney and has earmarked the big powerful San Siro man as a potential target.

    The £18m rated Naples born striker has been a member of the Rossoneri since 2002 but has spent loan spells at a number of Serie A sides during that spell but a successful stint at Genoa, where he netted 19 goals, led to his return to the AC Milan fold and he hasn’t looked back since.

    This season Leonardo has put a lot of faith in the 27 year old and he has netted 14 goals this term, making him the club’s top marksman. His form this term has led to his inclusion in Marcello Lippi’s 30 man provisional World Cup squad.

    Ferguson may well feel that Borriello’s physical attributes would lend themselves to a role in the Premier League and could well make him a perfect foil for England goal machine Rooney.

    Fergie interested in signing David Villa

    Sir Alex Ferguson is ready to offer up to £45m to seal the signing of Valencia striker David Villa. The Manchester United boss has earmarked the Spanish international as his must have target this summer as he looks to improve his striking line up.

    With Dimitar Berbatov reportedly set for a potential exit and with Michael Owen once again sidelined with injury problems the Old Trafford side is looking for another goal scoring option to help carry the burden with Wayne Rooney.

    David Villa has had another consistent season at the Mestalla, netting 28 goals in all competitions. Valencia are still in staggering financial debt and may well have no choice but to sell the 28 year old this summer. The La Liga side may find it hard to turn down an offer of £45m and Villa himself would probably jump at the chance of a move to the Premier League.

    The Glazers have stated that funds will be available for Ferguson to invest in his squad but given the debt the owners have saddled the club in such funds may have to come from sales of some of the club’s squad players such as Ben Foster and indeed the faltering striker Berbatov.
