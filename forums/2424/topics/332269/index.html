    Paul Scholes ruled out of action for ten weeks after suffering medial ligament damage

     

     

    Paul Scholes, the Manchester United midfielder, will be out of action for 10 weeks after suffering medial ligament damage in last night's Champions League win over Aalborg.

    Scholes was injured just 16 minutes into the 3-0 victory and it immediately became obvious that he could not continue. Sir Alex Ferguson, the United manager, confirmed Scholes had suffered ligament damage to his right knee and a scan this morning has confirmed the extent of the damage.

    He will not be available again until the visit to Tottenham on December 13, which comes immediately before United head to Japan for the World Club Championship.

    Scholes is also expected to miss the remainder of the Champions League group stage, plus key Premier League away games at Everton, Arsenal and Manchester City. In total the 33-year-old could miss 16 matches.
