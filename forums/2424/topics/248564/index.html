    Rio Ferdinand looks set to miss Tuesday night's Premiership encounter with Sheffield United after limping out of Saturday's FA Cup semi final win over Watford.

    Ferdinand was originally a doubt for the game after picking up a groin strain in Tuesday night's Champions League clash. He started on Saturday at Villa Park but aggravated the injury and was replaced by Darren Fletcher, who moved to right back, in the 40th minute.

    Even before Ferdinand's exit, the Reds' defence had a real look of unfamiliarity. Patrice Evra and Gabriel Heinze started together for the first time this season, with Evra used on the right hand side as he was when he came on against Roma in midweek. With Fletcher's introduction, Evra switched to his favoured left back position, while Heinze moved into the centre to partner Wes Brown.

    Ferdinand now joins fellow United defenders Gary Neville (ankle), Mikael Silvestre (shoulder), John O'Shea (calf) and Nemanja Vidic (shoulder) on the treatment table, although O'Shea and Neville are expected to return soon.

    Sir Alex does have youngster Craig Cathcart available and could hand the 18-year-old his United debut when Neil Warnock's Sheffield United side arrive at Old Trafford on Tuesday
