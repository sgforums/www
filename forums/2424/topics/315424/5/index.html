    VAN DER SAR DARES TO DREAM

    Veteran goalkeeper Edwin van der Sar can scarcely believe he is on the verge of becoming a two-time Champions League winner.

    Manchester United's formidable keeper is daring to dream again after helping Ajax become kings of Europe in 1995.

    The Dutch side came within a whisker of regaining their crown the following year but van der Sar had to settle for a loser's medal as they lost to Juventus on penalties.

    He later moved to the Italian giants before joining Fulham and then United in June 2005.

    At 37 Van der Sar is savouring every moment as the team chase a glorious double of league and Champions League.

    United booked their place in the final in Moscow thanks to a 1-0 aggregate victory against Barcelona and the Dutchman said: "You remember 13 years ago you won it and 12 years ago you lost it.

    "It has been a long time in between and it is a great feeling to go to the final again and try to win it for a second time.

    OWEN HARGREAVES

    ONCE again adapted to a right-back role. A Champions League winner with Bayern Munich and used all his European experience in patrolling his flank. 7

     

    WES BROWN

    JUST signed a new deal and deserves one too for some of his displays this season. He invariably fits in well as Ferdinand’s partner when Vidic is out. 7

     

    RIO FERDINAND

    DREAM TEAM STAR MAN. Alex Ferguson said it was his destiny to become the next United captain — and he looked it last night. Commanding display. 9

     

    PATRICE EVRA

    CAUGHT up in controversy after the Stamford Bridge defeat but you would not know it last night. Strong in the tackle and excellent going forward. 7

     

    JI-SUNG PARK

    SHOCK inclusion at the expense of Giggs. Nobody can doubt the South Korean’s workrate as he charges all over the pitch and blocks clearances. 7

     

    PAUL SCHOLES

    IT was like turning back the clock to the Scholes of old. Broke from deep to latch on to the loose ball before firing home a sensational winner. 8

     

    MICHAEL CARRICK

    REALLY growing into his role at United. Used to look overshadowed next to Scholes but after a poor first half looked calm and assured. Booked. 7

     

    NANI

    HAD a very poor game at Stamford Bridge and many doubted if he would play last night. But the Portuguese star bounced back to perform last night. 7

     

    CRISTIANO RONALDO

    WAS never going to get the freedom of the park against a side like Barca. But struck fear into their hearts when he got the ball and raced forward. Booked. 7  

     

    CARLOS TEVEZ

    AT his terrier-like best scurrying around the pitch. While he may not have the guile of Rooney he certainly makes up for it in sheer effort. 7
