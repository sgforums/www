    CHELSEA V MAN UTD

    Ancelotti confirms Chelsea team, Drogba benched

    By Soccernet staff

     

    Didier Drogba: Still recovering from injury

    Carlo Ancelotti has confirmed his starting XI for Sunday's Community Shield against Manchester United and said it would contain only one change from the side that lost their final pre-season friendly at Hamburg. 

    Hilario will start in goal, with the same outfield players from Wednesday night's game taking to the field at Wembley.
     
    Blues boss Ancelotti revealed Didier Drogba would be on the bench, while No.1 Petr Cech, defender Alex (both calf) and full-back Jose Boswinga (knee) are all out.

     

     

    Starting XI: Henrique Hilario, Paulo Ferreira, Branislav Ivanovic, John Terry, Ashley Cole, Michael Essien, John Mikel Obi, Frank Lampard, Salomon Kalou, Nicolas Anelka, Florent Malouda.

    Subs: Ross Turnbull, Didier Drogba, Ricardo Carvalho, Yossi Benayoun, Yuri Zhirkov, Gael Kakuta, Daniel Sturridge, Jeffrey Bruma.

    Reds ready for Wembley trip

    Sir Alex Ferguson has a number of injuries to contend with ahead of Sunday’s Community Shield showdown with Chelsea. But otherwise his Reds squad is nearing full fitness for the start of the season, and the United boss will use the game to enhance his men's match sharpness.

    Rio Ferdinand, Anderson, Owen Hargreaves, Michael Carrick, Gabriel Obertan, Rafael and Fabio are all either out or doubts for Sunday’s Wembley curtain-raiser.

    “Rio is still well behind,” said Sir Alex at his first press conference of the new season at Carrington. “It’ll be a few weeks before he is back amongst us. Anderson is making good progress and he should be training in two or three weeks.”

    Hargreaves is still in America continuing his return from tendonitis in his knees, while Carrick and Obertan are recovering from ankle injuries. “Michael’s injury is nothing serious but he’ll be out for two weeks,” added the boss.

    Rafael and Fabio are doubtful for Sunday’s game against Chelsea as they have both been suffering from food poisoning.

    Two players definitely earmarked to play at the weekend are Wayne Rooney and Michael Owen. They both played 45 minutes against an Airtricity League XI in Dublin, and Sir Alex will give both men at least another half on Sunday.

    "We'll use Sunday's game as we have done over the last few years," the boss concluded, "as a mechanism to get players further down the line in terms of fitness."
