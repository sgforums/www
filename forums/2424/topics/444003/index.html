    Jones KO adds to Fergie injury list with star man facing six weeks on the sidelines

     

     

     

     

     

     

    Phil Jones could miss up to six weeks with a suspected fractured cheekbone.

    The Manchester United star will have an X-ray on Thursday after being knocked to the floor by a Clint Dempsey elbow after just two minutes of his side’s 5-0 win at Fulham on Wednesday night.

    United also lost winger Ashley Young to a suspected ankle injury after the substitute clearly hurt himself going into a challenge with Fulham midfielder Dickson Etuhu.

    Rio Ferdinand travelled to west London but did not play after picking up a foot injury, although the defender is expected to be fit in time for Blackburn Rovers’ visit to Old Trafford on New Years Eve.
    .
    United already have a long injury list which includes Nemanja Vidic, Anderson, Darren Fletcher, Tom Cleverley, Fabio and Michael Owen.
    .
    Sir Alex Ferguson said: ‘We’ve got a couple of injuries. Phil Jones got an elbow to the jaw and we’ll send him for an X-ray on Thursday. It doesn’t look good.

    concern and Ashley is out for two or three weeks.

    There's no stopping Welsh wizard Giggs as goal at Fulham extends remarkable record

     

    Three and easy: Giggs scores United's third goal at Craven Cottage

     

    Pure delight: Ryan Giggs celebrates after scoring at Craven Cottage

     

     

    Ryan Giggs' goal against Fulham on Wednesday night saw him extend his unique record of scoring in every Premier League season.

    But not everything went his way — when the 38-year-old winger scored United’s third goal at Craven Cottage, it turned out to be the 19,999th goal scored in the Premier League.

    Guess who scored the 9,999th? Former United midfielder Paul Scholes.

    Giggs’ first came way back in August 1992 in a 2-0 victory over Nottingham Forest.

    The Welshman has actually scored in 22 seasons, since making his debut for United in March, 1991.

    It was a comfortable night for United at Craven Cottage, with the visitors 3-0 up at half-time following goals from Danny Welbeck, Nani and Ryan Giggs.

    Wayne Rooney and Dimitar Berbatov scored superb goals late on to give United their heaviest win in three months.

    Sir Alex Ferguson's men go into Christmas still two points behind their neighbours Manchester City in the title race.
