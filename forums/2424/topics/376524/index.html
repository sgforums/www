    Keeper Foster up for the chop

    STRUGGLING ...Ben Foster

     

    BEN FOSTER is facing the axe for Manchester United's date at Stoke City on Saturday.

    Tomasz Kuszczak will play in goal tonight as United put out a reserve side against Wolves in the Carling Cup. But if he has a good game he will stay in for the Premier League match at Stoke after a series of blunders by Foster.

    First-choice Edwin Van der Sar, 38, is recovering from a broken thumb and will not be back for three more weeks.

    Foster, 26, had hoped to replace the Dutch veteran permanently but United are worried about him flapping at high balls of which there will be plenty from Stoke.

    Ben Foster will start for United against Stoke City, Sir Alex Ferguson has confirmed, while the Reds will be without Edwin van der Sar for three more weeks.

    Tomasz Kuszczak impressed between the sticks in the Carling Cup against Wolverhampton Wanderers on Wednesday, but Foster keeps his place for league duty against his former club. Van der Sar, who suffered a broken hand in pre-season, isn't expected back until mid-October.

    "Edwin is still three weeks away," Sir Alex confirmed at his Friday press conference. "It was a good opportunity for Tomasz to play on Wednesday, but Ben will start against Stoke. He’s not had a lot of game experience but he’ll be okay."

    The boss also confirmed that there were no fresh injury concerns after Wednesday night's 1-0 victory, and added that the players who played in the 4-3 derby victory over Manchester City are all in contention for selection this weekend. Sir Alex revealed his respect for the work Tony Pulis is doing at Stoke and says that the trip to the Britannia Stadium will not be easy.

    "Stoke have a good home record, and I'm not surprised how they've started the season at all," he said. "They have fantastic support. They say the Britannia is the noisiest ground in the country and I wouldn’t disagree with that.

    "Last season was about survival because it’s not easy for anyone coming out of the Championship into the Premier League. But last time I was there you could sense a marked change. They’re an ambitious club with a good managerial structure and a good chairman. They're a club on the up and they're going to be a force this season."
