    MANCHESTER UNITED are worried Wayne Rooney’s fitness could add to the injury crisis that is threatening their season.

    Manchester United's Ryan Giggs Claims West Brom Will Be No Whipping Boys

    They may be poles apart in England's top division, but bottom meets top on Tuesday evening as West Bromwich Albion host Manchester United.

    The Baggies are battling it out in one of the tightest relegation battles to ever grace the Premier League as it involves up to 11 clubs, whereas the Red Devils are the reigning domestic, European, and World champions.

    Veteran winger Ryan Giggs, 35, has warned his side against complacency as he believes that Tony Mowbray's men will provide as stern a test as any.

    He told the club's official website: "When you come towards the end of the season every team has something to play for.

    "Some are challenging for the title or a Champions League or UEFA Cup place, while others are doing everything to stay up. So every game is important whatever team you're up against.

    "We've just got to approach every match with the right attitude otherwise we'll get beaten."

    United have already beaten WBA 4-0 this season, however, all the goals in that encounter arrived in the second half after a hard fought opening period.

    "They outplayed us at times in the first half with their passing and movement and kept it at 0-0 until half-time," Giggs said.

    "They're on a good run at the moment, especially at home, and they're a team that like to play football. So it'll be a tough game.

    "We're just focused on getting the three points and staying top. Once you hit the front you want to stay there and that's definitely our aim."
