    Manchester United are set to sign youngster Charni Ekangamene from feeder club Royal Antwerp on his 16th birthday.

    The rookie attacker is still only 15 but is earmarked for a professional contract at Old Trafford after impressing during a trial spell in Manchester.

    Sir Alex Ferguson has often used the Belgian side to develop his own youngsters, including Danny Higginbotham, Luke Chadwick, John O'Shea and Darron Gibson, but is ready to reverse the trend.

    After former Antwerp defender Ritchie De Laet joined United from Stoke City in January, Belgian-Congolese kid Ekangamene looks on his way to the European champions later this year, despite rival  interest from Ajax.

    "He is a great talent," Antwerp's youth co-ordinator Alex Vervecken told HNB.

    "He is mentally strong and is never nervous."

    Vervecken and club secretary Paul Bistiaux accompanied the teenager on his initial trip to Manchester and a contract is expected to be signed when he becomes eligible as The Red Devils have been monitoring his progress carefully.
