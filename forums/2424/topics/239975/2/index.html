    Star-studded Euro squad to face Man United

    The European Commission today unveiled its all-star football squad to play Manchester United in a one-off charity match at Old Trafford. Tuesday night's televised game is a joint 50th anniversary celebration of the creation of the European Union and of United becoming the first English side to play in UEFA's European club competition.

    The Commission's first 'signing', David Beckham, has had to drop out through injury, but will be at the match to see his old club face up to an EU team boasting Liverpool players Steven Gerrard and Jamie Carragher, Barcelona captain Carles Puyol and many of the Italian World Cup-winning squad.

    Three Brazilian stars - Ronaldinho, Ronaldo and Juninho - will play as 'guests', because they are not strictly eligible for a 'Europe XI' team. An unusually popular European Commission press conference saw Manchester United legend Sir Bobby Charlton take the stage alongside the Europe XI coach, Italian World Cup winner Marcello Lippi.

    And UEFA President Michel Platini was on hand to present Commission President Jose Manuel Barroso with a Europe XI team shirt - number 27, to reflect the number of EU member states.

    Sir Bobby said he was proud of Manchester United and proud of the European Union, and the match was a 'very significant' occasion. He added: 'The history of Manchester United is tied up with that of Europe.

    'Some of the club's greatest moments have been played out on a European stage. 'I look forward to welcoming so many stars and friends to Old Trafford to celebrate these two significant anniversaries.'

    Beck in 1993.

    DAVID BECKHAM and Sir Alex Ferguson will publicly end their four-year feud at Old Trafford tonight in front of 76,000 fans and millions more watching on television. Beckham is a guest of honour at the exhibition match between Manchester United and an All-Star Europe XI to mark the club's 50-year involvement in the European Cup.

    And although Beckham and Fergie have hardly spoken since their highly-publicised fallout which led to the former England skipper's move to Real Madrid, the pair will put their differences behind them for tonight's charity game. "I wouldn't be the player and the person I am today without the manager," said Becks. "It's been well documented that we had our ups and downs, but I owe almost everything to him.

    "Obviously I had strong parents and a strong family behind me but, without him giving me the opportunity to get into the United team, I wouldn't be the player I am today and wouldn't have won and achieved a lot of the things I have."

    Although Beckham has been back to Old Trafford on several occasions with England, he never had the chance to say farewell to United fans and admitted he is looking forward to finally getting the chance tonight. Injury has robbed him of the chance to play in this match, but the former United midfielder admitted there was no way he was going to pass up the chance to say a final goodbye to the club he served for almost 14 years.

    "The one thing I've looked forward to for years since I left United is going back there and saying goodbye to the fans, because I never really had the chance to do that," said Beckham. "I always feel that Old Trafford is my home, my rightful home. I'm obviously from London but I spent so many years there and it's my club. "It's the club I've always supported and the club I will always support. It's the best stadium in the world for me. The Bernabeu is incredible, but United is the club where I feel I grew up."
