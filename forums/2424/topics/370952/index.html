    Manchester United have handed a trial to Swedish starlet Linus Sjoberg.

    The 15-year-old midfielder is currently with Vasteras but he has been spotted by United's Scandinavian scouting network.

    The Premier League champions have now made an offer and Sjoberg is expected to link up with United on trial later this summer.

    "You have to be cautious dealing with the social and footballing parts as the social environment is important when you are younger," agent Martin Klette told Fotbollskanalen.

    "There are both good and bad foreign clubs as there are good and bad Swedish clubs.

    "People have a tendency to focus on younger players that have had problems abroad and forget that there are very good clubs to grow in abroad."

    AC linked with United target

    Gifted youngster Sjoberg's agent claims Italian interest

    Linus Sjoberg's agent has claimed AC Milan have joined the race for the Swedish starlet's signature.

    The gifted 15-year-old midfielder, who is contracted to Vasteras, is currently enjoying a week-long trial at Manchester United.

    Sjoberg is considered a huge prospect in Sweden, and Jonsson has claimed Serie A giants Milan are among his suitors.

    "Milan have also declared their interest, that is correct," Sjoberg's agent Per Jonsson told Fotbolldirekt.

    "We chose to let him start at United but Milan is also an option. They think he is extremely interesting and are impressed most by his technique."

    Speaking of his time at United, Sjoberg told VLT: "It's wonderful. Really cool to practice with United, it's the biggest club in the world."
