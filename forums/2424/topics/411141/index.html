    RIO FERDINAND last night suffered another blow when he was told he will not be Manchester United's captain this season. 

    The centre-back already faces a battle with Steven Gerrard for the England armband after missing the World Cup through injury. 

    And Old Trafford manager Alex Ferguson insisted: "Nemanja Vidic has been given the captain's armband because he has been available all the time." 

    Ferdinand, 31, has never been United's official skipper but he has led the team out many times because of club captain Gary Neville's injury woes. 

    And many fans expected him to get the nod - particularly when he was given the honour against Rangers in midweek. 

    But now Ferguson has confirmed it will be fellow defender Vidic, 28, who carries out the duties on a more regular basis. 

    Fergie said: "We want it to be a player who's consistently available for us. 

    "Everyone will recognise that if Vidic is fit - and he has been all season - he'll always be there. 

    "You wouldn't leave him out of your team unless you were resting him. 

    "He has started the campaign as the captain and it will stay that way." 

    Fergie's decision suggests that Ferdinand has some way to go before convincing the boss he is completely clear of injuries. 

    But the Old Trafford chief is pleased to have him available for tomorrow's clash with Liverpool. 

    Fergie added: "It is good to have Rio back. If he plays, it is because he is an outstanding player.
