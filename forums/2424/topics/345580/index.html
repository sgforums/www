    Toffees lifted by Cahill news

    Everton can call off their search for a new makeshift striker after X-rays revealed that Tim Cahill has not suffered a broken foot.

    The Australia star, who has performed admirably in a forward role in recent weeks, was substituted after 85 minutes of Wednesday’s 1-1 draw with Arsenal after damaging his foot.

    The sight of Cahill hobbling off the turf, bootless, was no doubt a worrying one for his club as the midfielder has a history of metatarsal problems.

    But scans have delivered positive news for David Moyes, even if Cahill is still a doubt for Saturday’s game against Manchester United.

    “Tim Cahill has received a fitness boost after X-rays on his injured foot revealed the Australian international has not suffered a break,” read a club statement.

    “Cahill hobbled off five minutes from the end of Wednesday night's 1-1 draw with Arsenal after taking a knock to his right foot.

    “He underwent a precautionary X-ray on Thursday but the results have revealed no major damage, although severe bruising means the makeshift striker is now a major doubt for the trip to Old Trafford on Saturday evening.”

    Cahill boasts four goals in eight Premier League starts since being utilised as an emergency striker by The Toffees.
