    FA Cup: Big four draw?

    United could face one of their "big four" rivals in the last 16 of the FA Cup after Arsenal, Chelsea and Liverpool all went safely through their fourth round ties.

    "We'll probably get one of them," Sir Alex Ferguson told MUTV.

    "We don't have a lot of luck in draws. I think we've had Premier League teams 15 times in our last 18 FA Cup ties."

    Arsenal were the most comfortable winners in the quartet, beating Kevin Keegan's Newcastle 3-0 at the Emirates thanks to Emmanuel Adebayor's brace and Nicky Butt's own goal. Liverpool were less than convincing against Havant and Waterlooville, ranked 123 league places below them. The part-timers took the lead twice at Anfield before eventually losing 5-2.

    Chelsea were 2-1 winners at Wigan in the Saturday tea-time cup tie but were fortunate to avoid a replay when Marcus Bent hit the crossbar for the home side, moments after Antoine Sibierski had pulled a goal back with a tremendous shot past Petr Cech. Nicolas Anelka and Shaun Wright-Phillips were on target for the victors.
