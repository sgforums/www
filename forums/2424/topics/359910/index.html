    Rooney on revenge mission to shut up Arsenal fans and clinch title for Manchester United

     

    Aiming high: Rooney (top) is ready to celebrate in style against the Gunners

    Wayne Rooney is determined to ram the Arsenal fans' chants back down their throats at the weekend by winning the Premier League title in front of them.

    Manchester United just need a point against the north Londoners to be crowned champions after coming from behind to beat Wigan 2-1 last night.

    And England striker Rooney has extra motivation as he cringes every time the Gunners faithful sing 'we won the league in Manchester' referring to their victory at Old Trafford in 2002 - the year they clinched the Double.

    'One point will win the title for us. Hopefully that can come on Saturday. It would be brilliant to do it in front of our own fans and brilliant to win it against Arsenal, who are a great footballing team.

    'When they come to Old Trafford you always hear them singing about winning the title at Old Trafford, so it would be nice to win against them. That would be special.'

     

     

     

    Cheeky one: Carlos Tevez scores with a backheel to level against Wigan

     

    A draw or better against Arsenal would mean United have won the title three years in a row for only the second time in their history and put them level with Liverpool on 18 League crowns.

    Even if Wenger's side win, United have an away trip against Hull eight days later in which to clinch top spot.

    Rio out of Gunners clash

    Rio Ferdinand is out of Manchester United's clash with Arsenal on Saturday but is expected to return in plenty of time for the Champions League final against Barcelona.

    "I'm sure Rio will be available for the final," declared Ferguson at a press conference.

    "These calf niggles usually take ten days to two weeks which is what we have with him but I'm hopeful he'll be fit for next Sunday's game against Hull.

    "He should be really, in which case it's good news for us. At the moment, he's not available for tomorrow but that's the only concern we have.

    "Everyone else is fit.

    "Obviously, I think I'll freshen it up from the game on Wednesday. It was a very heavy pitch and a really gruelling game against Wigan, who played a big part in that match and we had to overcome that."

    Ferguson appreciates Arsenal will be keen to spoil the party and exact revenge for the manner of their Champions League exit to The Red Devils.

    "We have got to concentrate on winning the league tomorrow," he stated.

    "We'll be trying to achieve that and do it the best way, that's our way.

    "A point is always a dangerous game to play, thinking a draw is enough, we need to go for the win."
