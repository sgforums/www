    Celtic captain Neil Lennon claims that Gary Neville told him that Louis Saha would miss the late penalty in their Champions League clash.

    The Frenchman had his spot-kick saved when Celtic were leading 1-0 and an equaliser would have sent United through to the knock-out stages.

    Neville apparently predicted the miss but rather than - as captain - asking someone else to take the penalty, Neville watched Saha take a penalty that was at the perfect height for Artur Boruc to save.

    "Gary Neville actually came across to me and said about Saha, 'his head has gone, he's going to miss this," said Lennon.

    Saha's miss meant Celtic went through to the knock-out stages, leaving United needing a result against defeat to claim a place in the last 16.

    Goalkeeper Artur Boruc revealed how memory served him well as he saved Louis Saha's late penalty to book Celtic a place in the Champions League knock-out stages for the first time.

    Poland international Boruc took heed of the way Saha shot when he scored from the spot in United's 3-2 win over Celtic at Old Trafford in September - and went the correct way, diving to his right to palm away a firm strike.

    Boruc said: 'I don't know about it being a great save from the penalty. Every time a `keeper saves a penalty it's luck, that's all.

    'I was thinking about the last penalty he scored against me and I thought he might do the same thing, that's what was on my mind.
