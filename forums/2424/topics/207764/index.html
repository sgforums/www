    Newcastle have agreed terms with United to take Giuseppe Rossi on loan until 1 January 2007.

    The Italian striker will link up with the Magpies immediately and, according to Sir Alex Ferguson, will gain some invaluable first team experience.

    "It was a big decision for us especially as we don't have the choice of strikers," admitted the United boss.

    "But this is something that Giuseppe wanted as he needs regular first team football.

    "When he returns he will be a more experienced player who will be able to offer us much more."

    Rossi joined United in July 2004 from Serie A side Parma. After impressing for the Reserves in his first few months at Old Trafford, he earned a well deserved call-up to the senior team.

    His first team debut came in the Carling Cup victory over Crystal Palace in November 2004. He has since made a further 11 appearances, bagging four goals in the process.

    Newcastle boss Glenn Roeder has thanked Sir Alex Ferguson for allowing United's highly-rated Italian striker Giuseppe Rossi to move to St James' Park.

    The 19-year-old Italian Under21 is currently on international duty, but will join up with his team-mates ahead of Newcastle's next Premiership fixture against Fulham on 9 September.

    The Magpies completed the transfer of Rossi late on Wednesday night, and Roeder was delighted to have secured the capture of Rossi on a four-month loan deal ahead of other interested clubs, with Mark Hughes at Blackburn and Bryan Robson at West Brom also keen on taking Rossi.

    "A lot of other Premiership clubs wanted Giuseppe and I suppose some people will be surprised that we have got him," Roeder said. "But I must thank Sir Alex for his part in this because I really believe that he has done us a big favour."

    Sir Alex had initially stated that Rossi would spend this season at the club earning his stripes in the first-team. But the manager had a change of tact and decided that the former Parma youth player would benefit more from first-team experience elsewhere.
