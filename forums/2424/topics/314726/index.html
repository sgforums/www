ANDERSON feared injury had ended his dream of joining United

  • zocoss
    17 Apr 08, 12:35

     

    ANDERSON feared a nightmare injury had ended his dream of joining Manchester United. But today all the Brazilian thinks about is beating Barcelona in the “biggest game of his life”.

    Just a year ago, it was all so different after he picked up an horrific broken foot in Porto’s derby clash against Benfica. The injury left Anderson devastated and worrying his switch to Old Trafford might never materialise. Boss Alex Ferguson had wanted to nab the midfielder much earlier and blood him in United’s reserves — yet the setback put a stop to all that.

    Anderson eventually went back to his homeland to complete his recovery and, after a six-month lay-off, Fergie splashed £18million to land him. Anderson, 20 last week, said: “I would have joined United earlier as they were following me for a long time. But I broke my foot and I was out of action for six months. “I did my recuperation in Brazil and when I arrived in Portugal I was told United continued to be interested. At the time I thought it was such bad luck because I would not be at the level United expected of me.

    Anderson can't wait

    “But I did not worry about the scouts of Manchester. I just focused on making a full recuperation.” Anderson had goose-bumps when he finally made his debut for the Red Devils at the start of the season. And now he cannot wait to face Catalan giants Barca in the Champions League semi-final next week — the game he believes should have been the final.

     

    Anderson added: “When I made my United debut I felt something special. It was a great moment and I felt so happy. "I’ve played in great teams — like Gremio or Porto — but the day I played my first game at Old Trafford wearing the red shirt against Sunderland, that is a day I will never forget.

     

    “For me, the semi-final against Barca will be like playing the final. “Playing against Barca is the most important game of my life and I will prepare myself as if I was playing the final of the Champions League. “I think it should be the final because if you look at the stats we are the two best teams in the competition.

     

    “Ronaldinho will not play and Messi will not be at his best but the squad of Barcelona is excellent. “I like Andres Iniesta a lot, he’s my favourite player from Barcelona. Then there is Xavi, Deco, Eto’o, Henry. It’s a great team.” Like Ronaldinho, Anderson comes from Porto Alegre and, inevitably, comparisons are made with the two-time World Player of the Year.

    Yet Anderson insisted: “For me, Ronaldinho will always be the greatest. “He’s done it all and I’ve not won anything. I’m just starting out. But it gives me great pride that people compare me to him.” Anderson is just as proud of being part of the United set-up. He added:

     

    “Cristiano Ronaldo has helped me adapt. “You work a lot here. I’ve trained like never before. Training sessions are hard and you live for football 24 hours a day. “Alex Ferguson is special. For me, he is the best. "On occasions, he gives the impression he is distant but he’s always on top of everything and he likes working with the young players.

     

    “Look at the work he did with Giggs, Scholes and even Ronaldo. “It’s an honour to be able to work with a manager like him.”
