    Ryan: This is the one

    Ryan Giggs has been involved in 36 previous United-Liverpool battles but he says this one could be the most important.

    Victory on Saturday lunchtime would put Sir Alex's men ten points clear of Liverpool and Chelsea, while Rafael Benitez's side are desperate to keep their title hopes alive.

    This fixture already has a kick to it, but with the implications the result could have on the title race there is even more focus on the outcome.

    "There’s a lot riding on this match," Giggs says in the Liverpool edition of United Review. "If we win they’ll find it very difficult to catch us, but if they win then they’ll feel they’re right back in the race. It’s a massive game for both teams.

    "It's right up there with the other Liverpool games I've played in," adds Giggsy.

    "Liverpool have made great progress this season and have got a lot closer to us than they have been in recent years."

    United were used to playing second fiddle during Liverpool's domestic dominance, but those roles have been reversed in the Premier League era. This season, both teams have locked horns as genuine title contenders.

    "Over the years there have been more important games in terms of the title race – games against Chelsea and Arsenal, perhaps," says Ryan.

    "But I believe the biggest game of the season is always Liverpool. That’s the game you really know you have to perform in because if you don’t you won’t win. It’s that simple."
