    Ryan Giggs hopes Manchester United's draw with Middlesbrough will be viewed as a point gained at the end of the season.

     

    The weekend's action has seen United's advantage at the top cut to three points after the leaders responded to Chelsea beating Manchester City by drawing 2-2 at Boro.

    Wayne Rooney's goal secured a point for the reigning champions and skipper Giggs was content to settle for a draw.

    "I think overall we're pleased after going 2-1 down," Giggs told Sky Sports.

    "We want to win every game, but we hope that will be a point gained at the end of the season.

    "We started the game really well and obviously got the goal, but Boro are a good team and it's always a difficult place to come."

     

    Confidence

     

    Giggs admits the United squad are relishing the run-in, with the second leg of the UEFA Champions League quarter-final with Roma to come on Wednesday.

    The Welsh winger added: "It's just about keeping your good form now, both as an individual and a team. You want to go into every game with confidence and knowing you can win every game.

    "We know it's not going to be easy, but if we play to the best of our ability, then we'll have a chance.

    "It's an exciting time, it's big game after big game and every game is like a little final in itself. You want to be involved in games like this and all the lads are looking forward to it."

    United Fans Call For Giggs To Be Dropped

    Ryan Giggs made his 750th appearance for Manchester United at the weekend, but that hasn't stopped growing numbers of Red Devils fans calling for Sir Alex Ferguson to drop the Welshman

    Giggs has delivered a string of poor performances in recent times, with some United fans failing to see what the 34-year-old brings to the side nowadays.

    The career-long United servant is only nine games off equalling Bobby Charlton's all-time record for Manchester United appearances, but among sections of the fanbase at least it appears there is little room for sentiment.

    “Can you possibly tell me why Ryan Giggs keeps getting the sympathy vote? He was woeful today," said one caller to a phone-in show on United's own TV channel, MUTV.

    Another said: “I do not see what Ryan Giggs brings to the side at the moment. He has been a great servant and got some fantastic goals. But at the moment he’s not worth playing. He’s no threat.”

    Sir Alex Ferguson himself was unhappy with Giggs on Sunday as he failed to latch onto a great through-ball from Park Ji-Sung late in the game at Middlesbrough, which ended 2-2 and saw United's lead at the top cut to just three points.

    Indeed, Fergie's recent team selections suggest he is reluctant to play the winger in many big names now, hence his exclusion from the XI that won in Rome last week.
