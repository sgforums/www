    Tomasz Kuszczak has warned Ben Foster he will have to fight for his place in the Manchester United side next season.     But Kuszczak, 25, believes he can be No 1 after a year as reserve keeper. He said: "Ben Foster is a good keeper and we have Edwin van der Sar as first-choice now. "But I feel there is a big chance for me to play and I want to fight them for it - and win."

    Kuszczak on a mission

    Tomasz Kusczak has set his sights on becoming United's number one stopper despite the imminent return of Ben Foster.

    The Polish goalkeeper made 13 appearances this season - his first at Old Trafford - as deputy to first choice keeper Edwin van der Sar.

    The 25-year-old faces further competition next season from Foster who returns to United after two years on loan at Watford, but Kusczak insists he is determined to fight his way to the top of the pecking order.

    "Ben Foster is a good keeper and we have Edwin van der Sar as first-choice now," he says.

    "But I feel there is a big chance for me to play and I want to fight them for it - and win."

    Kuszczak kept four clean sheets during his spell between the posts this season.
