    Sneijder would consider Utd move

    Wesley Sneijder has not ruled out playing in the Premier League before the end of his career but Manchester United is the only English club he would join.

    Sneijder was heavily linked with a move to Old Trafford following the World Cup finals in South Africa but he opted to stay loyal to Inter Milan after helping the team to Champions League, Serie A title and Coppa Italia.

    However, his agent reported recently talks over a new contract had broken down leading to fresh speculation that the playmaker could be on his way out of the San Siro.

    Now the player himself has admitted that he would love the chance to play for United.

    "If somebody asks me if I will ever play for Manchester United, it's impossible for me to say no. How can you say that if the chance came you would not play for one of the biggest clubs in the world," he told the Daily Star Sunday.

    "I don't think there is any player in the world who could publically rule out ever playing for Manchester United, and if they did I would doubt they were telling the truth.

    "The Premier League, in terms of top clubs, is the strongest in the world, and it would be nice to finish my career saying I have played at the very top in Spain, Italy, and England.

    "There is not many who can say they have done that, and if that does happen for me I would only want it to be with Manchester United."
