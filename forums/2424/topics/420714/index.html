    A tale of 2 halves and 2 magnificent players for either team: Adams for Blackpool in the first half and Giggs for United in the second half.

    Summary of match:

    1. Playing 5 in midfield in away matches as predicted earlier backfired. Berbatov is not getting any support and hardly had any sniff at goal before the formation reverted back to 4-4-2 midway through the first half.

    2. Gibson is garbage. A 37 years old Giggs outplayed someone who's 13 years his junior. It's like Lucas for Liverpool last season, we have Gibson for United.

    3. Giggs must have drank some magic potion alongside Scholes, the performance they put in really puts the young ones (Fletcher, Gibson) to shame.

    4. Berbatov is a lethal weapon upfront this season, almost everything he touches resulted in a goal. Goal number 19 in the premier league for him, I'm rooting him to score past 25.

    5. That wonder boy Chicharito always, yes, always impress when he comes on. It's hard not to compare him with the great Ole, a miracle supersub. He could have easily grabbed a hat-trick had the keeper not been alert both in the one-on-one and the cross from Giggs.

    6. Concussion to Rafael - A sight to forget. He was doing well until the head collision, let's hope he's alright.

    7. United fighting spirit wins the match. I thought a draw will be good enough and United went further by taking the 3rd away win of the season. Kudos to Fergie and his half-time hairdryer team talk.
