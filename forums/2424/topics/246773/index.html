    Portsmouth v Manchester Utd

    Ch 24 - 12:15 am - (Live) (Sun Morning)

    Portsmouth's on-loan Chelsea defender Glen Johnson (knee) is struggling, but Noe Pamarot is fit again. Harry Redknapp may pick from Lomana LuaLua, Svetoslav Todorov or Benjani Mwaruwari to partner Kanu or the Nigerian could play alone up front.

    Nwankwo KANU is Portsmouth's leading scorer with 12 goals - 10 of which were in the Premiership. KANU is a hat trick shy of 50 career League goals in England.

    Patrice Evra is set to return to Manchester United's squad after missing six games with a knee injury. Other than that, the bulk of Sir Alex Ferguson's squad will be the same as the one which travelled to Rome.

    Cristiano RONALDO is United's top scorer with 18 goals. Of those 16 are in the Premiership, which leaves the Portugal World Cup star two behind Chelsea's Didier Drogba at the head of the race for the Barclays Golden Boot.

    Ryan GIGGS is a double short of 100 League goals.

    --------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Portsmouth : James, Lauren, Primus, Campbell, Stefanovic, O'Neil, Davis, Hughes, Kranjcar, Taylor, Kanu, Traore, LuaLua, Benjani, Pamarot, Todorov, Mvuemba, O'Brien, Ashdown.

    Man Utd : Van der Sar, Kuszczak, O'Shea, Ferdinand, Brown, Heinze, Evra, Ronaldo, Fletcher, Scholes, Carrick, Giggs, Richardson, Eagles, Rooney, Smith, Solskjaer, Saha, Dong.

    REFEREE
    Mark Clattenburg (Tyne & Wear)

    --------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Portsmouth boss Harry Redknapp:
    "This is probably the most complete Manchester United side we have faced in the Premiership. "We have had two good results against them in our first two seasons in the Premiership and Sir Alex Ferguson knows that he will face a tough match here. "We have a good record on our pitch having lost only three games at home this season."

    Manchester United winger Ryan Giggs on the title race:
    "Over the last couple of years nothing has really happened in the last four or five weeks of the season. "It's going to be difficult but it's going to be exciting. There will be highs and lows to the end of the season and it's whatever team handles it best really. "If we keep playing like we have done all season we'll have a great chance of winning some silverware. That's what we hope to do."
