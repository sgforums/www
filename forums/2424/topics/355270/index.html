    WAYNE ROONEY is a major injury doubt for Alex Ferguson as he bids to stop Manchester United’s season going down the pan. 

    Rooney was hit hard on the ribs by the ball at the end of the first half of the Red Devils’ 2-2 Champions League quarter-final first leg at home to Porto on Tuesday night. 

    He bravely played on but was clearly in some discomfort as he left the ground after the game. Striker Rooney, 23, will be checked today and the hope is he has only heavy bruising. But even that could keep him out of Saturday’s trip to Sunderland. 

    United have lost two of their last three Premier League games and only won Sunday’s home clash with Aston Villa 3-2 thanks to a last-gasp goal. 

    They gave up a 2-1 lead against Porto to draw 2-2. It is now likely they will have to become the only English club to win at Porto if they want to progress into the semis. 

    And with Dimi Berbatov still out with an ankle problem, it will be a massive blow to be without Rooney on Wearside. 

    Boss Ferguson is also sweating over Rio Ferdinand, who has a groin injury. Fellow defender Jonny Evans is likely to miss the game with a similar problem.

    Manchester United defender Rio Ferdinand has not recovered from his groin injury and misses out again.

    Jonny Evans is a doubt with the groin injury he picked up against Porto. If he is unfit then John O'Shea will deputise in the centre of defence.

    Wayne Rooney is in the squad despite suffering a rib injury against Porto.

     

    Man Utd (from): Van der Sar, Foster, Kuszczak, O'Shea, Neville, Vidic, Evans, Evra, Eckersley, Ronaldo, Nani, Fletcher, Gibson, Carrick, Scholes, Giggs, Park, Rooney, Tevez, Welbeck, Macheda.

     

     

    HEAD TO HEAD

    Sunderland's only victory over Manchester United in over a decade was 2-1 in the Worthington League Cup on 28 November 2000 at the Stadium of Light, when Kevin Phillips scored a penalty in extra time to put the Black Cats into the fifth round.

    Sunderland's only victory in 15 Premier League matches against Manchester United was 2-1 at Roker Park on 8 March 1997, when Michael Gray and John Mullin were on target.

    United are in line to complete the double over Sunderland for the fourth time in the Premier League and the second in successive seasons.
