    Sir Alex Ferguson was gracious but defiant after seeing his hopes of a fourth consecutive title crushed by Carlo Ancelotti's free-scoring Chelsea side.

    United's title rivals beat Wigan Athletic 8-0 home, to render academic the Reds' own emphatic victory, 4-0 over Stoke City on the Old Trafford turf.

    "I didn't know what the Chelsea score was [during the second half] to be honest. But at half-time I accepted the fact they were going to be champions," Sir Alex told MUTV.

    Gary Caldwell's red card and Frank Lampard's subsequent penalty conversion to put Chelsea 2-0 up after 32 minutes meant the rest of the afternoon's activity in West London and Manchester was largely a formality.

    "We felt it would be difficult for Wigan to get something at Stamford Bridge and when we heard they were down to ten men, all our hopes evaporated then," admitted Sir Alex.

    "We applaud Chelsea in winning the title. We know hard it is to win it - it's the hardest league in the world and we've won it for the last three. I congratulate Carlo Ancelotti on a wonderful achievement. He's a good manager and a good guy."

    Sir Alex will swiftly turn his thoughts to challenging Chelsea for the 2010/11 championship.

    "We won the last three titles before this one. That was fantastic and the players have been great. We'll come back next year, that's exactly what Manchester United do."

    Earlier, the

    manager went on the pitch for the traditional end-of-season speech to the Old Trafford crowd.

    "The players and the supporters have always been magnificent and you've been magnificent again," he said.

    "I think we can be proud that we've challenged hard for a fourth title in a row. Next season we'll go again and bring back the title to the best place in the world."
