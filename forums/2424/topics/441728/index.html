    Ashley Young and Tom Cleverley to return

    COMEBACK KIDS ... Tom 'n Ash are ready to return

     

     

     

     

    MANCHESTER UNITED'S pursuit of the noisy neighbours will be boosted by the return of Ashley Young and Tom Cleverley this weekend. 

    The pair were the hits of United's explosive opening to the new campaign before injury. But they will be back for Saturday's game at Swansea as Alex Ferguson's troops look to close the five-point gap on bitter rivals Manchester City. 

    Cleverley, 22, has not played a league game since suffering an ankle injury at Bolton on September 10. 

    The midfielder returned against Aldershot in the Carling Cup where he suffered another ankle problem and has missed the last two games. 

    New £16million buy Young picked up a toe injury in training before United's trip to Everton on October 29 — ruling him out of the two England internationals. 

    But the 26-year-old has been back in training this past week and will return to the wing where he has been both a supplier and goalscorer for United. 

    Striker Danny Welbeck is, however, a doubt for the trip to Wales to face Brendan Rodgers' side. 

    He picked up a muscle injury after coming on as a substitute for England against Spain last Saturday.
