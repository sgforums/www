United's Plan B

  • omgukilledkenny
    11 Jun 08, 20:09

    United are plotting a major spending spree should they lose superstar Cristiano Ronaldo.

    The European and Premier League champions are fighting tooth and nail to hold on to prize asset Ronaldo, who has the reputation of being the best player in the world.

    But they they do not want to get caught out if the 42-goal Footballer of the Year finally forces the issue and says he wants to quit Old Trafford before the start of the season.

    The club would not leave the player to rot in the reserves but instead would tell Real Madrid to shatter the £47m world transfer record, which Madrid paid to Juventus in 2001.

    United have already made an official formal complaint to FIFA over Real's conduct in the Ronaldo saga, but the Bernabeu giants insist they have done nothing wrong.

    But the Reds are intent on not being caught cold by a possible Ronaldo departure, and if a Karim Benzema deal proves unachievable, they have drawn up a list of other top targets.

    Profile

    Klaas-Jan Huntelaar has been constantly linked with a move away from Ajax and at 24 fits nicely into United's age profile.

    Tottenham's Dimitar Berbatov remains a crowd-pleasing option with a Premier League pedigree of 27 goals in 69 matches for Spurs.

    But the signing that would most certainly compensate for any potential loss of Ronaldo would be Barcelona's Lionel Messi.

    The Argentinian will be 21 in a fortnight, and wowed Old Trafford and United with his individual displays for Barca in the Champions League semi- final ties against the Reds.

    Getting Barcelona to agreeing to a Nou Camp departure would be tough but a £50m bite out of any fee received for Ronaldo might force the Catalan club's hand.
