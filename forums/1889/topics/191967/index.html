    23 May 06, 21:56
    There is a hadith which states that frogs are considered as haram.

    Truth be told, it's a matter of debate among Muslim jurists about whether or not the concept of 'two worlds' applies all types of animals which can live on both land and water. Some are of the opinion that crabs and lobsters fall under this category and are not allowed, while others say that they are permissible.

    The general consensus among Islamic scholars seems to be that amphibians (turtles, frogs, etc) are definitely not allowed, while other types of seafood such as crabs and lobsters are subject to debate.

    Maybe this article will shed light on the debate that Islamic scholars are having over this issue: http://www.ifanca.org/newsletter/2002_09.htm

  • fudgester
    25 May 06, 13:57
    Originally posted by Honeybunz:
    Jews also dun eat pork and lard.

    Can we assume that Kosher food = Halal?
    Not quite.

    While Judaism has similar (in some ways, even stricter) dietary laws than Islam, kosher is not necessarily equal to halal and vice versa. For example, in Islam, alcohol is forbidden, while in Judaism, it is allowed.

    Likewise, in Judaism, meat and dairy products can't be mixed (no cheeseburgers and tandoori chicken), while Islam has no such ruling.

    The consensus is that food certified kosher may be consumed in the absence of halal food. Of course, if halal food is available, then it has to be consumed first.
