    Tian Fu Teahouse, Park Royal Hotel on Beach Road.

    http://www.parkroyalhotels.com/hotels/singapore/beach_road/parkroyal/restaurants/index.html

    This is one of the new establishment featured recently in Sunday Times where people can go and sample tea. Feeling extremely bored today I popped in to have a feel of the place.

    The decor of the place is nice, quiet and tranquil, cina without too much of being in-your-face, and wonderfully spacious.

    I felt peckish, so I went for the high-tea set, which comes with 9 dimsum selection and 5 teas. Sadly, one cannot choose the teas: 

    2 greens - Longjing from Westlake, Zhejiang; Zhuyeqing from Sichuan

    1 floral - Chrysanthemum buds with Goji berries

    1 black - (they still consider it black! Beegeezus!) Pu'erh

    1 oolong - Dahongpao from Wuyi city, Fujian

    The dimsum selection is seriously Dian Xin in its presentation; not the 3 per bamboo basket sort, but simply 1 piece each, like a sampler, to touch the heart with a flutter of joy (not really) and not filling the stomach to bloating point. 2 pieces of dimsum each accompany 1 tea. The dimsum is not bad, though I find the hargao skin a tad too thick, and the wonton too spicy to go with the tea.

    The tea selection. The 2 greens and the chrysanthemum are surprisingly good, the Zhuyeqing is fresh and lively with a lingering sweet floral aftertaste, the Longjing is subtle and nutty, the chrysanthemum calming. The pu'erh and oolong are great disappointment...
