    Heard of Sir Charles Gairdner hospital - considered there but RMH offered up first. I did my degree in australia and got my registration in NSW which I transferred to Victoria.

    Melbourne is pretty good. Nurse patient ratios are best among other states : 1 nurs to 4 patients . U just need to find a partner to help u around. Yes you can get pr but never ever let the hospital bond u through the employer sponsorship - 3 years . You apply for it yourself through skilled migration onshore. I think it is better to apply now though if you have 1 more year of bond to go . I got mine in 1 year, my friend who applied recently got it in 7 months.

    Better to arrive as pr than not arrive as PR. Medicare card can save u alot. Medical costs are expensive here. Post registration bachelor degree course will also be cheaper for PRs - about 4.8k per anum because nursing is now a priority area. Alternatively work first then wait for PR then do a post reg bachelor. Before you go, make sure you confirm a job with someone first then go because rent is bloody expensive in australia.

    Visit Alfred hospital too . http://www.alfred.org.au - pretty good for a trauma level 1 center, they are the only hospital who do heart lung transplants as well and have a burns unit.
