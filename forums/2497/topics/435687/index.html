    Fabregas: I will pay to leave Arsenal! Captain offers to give up £4m to force Barca move

     

    Praying for a move: Fabregas is ready to give up £4 million in bonuses

     

     

     

    Cesc Fabregas is so desperate to leave Arsenal for Barcelona that he has offered to give up more than £4million worth of bonuses.

    Just a week before the start of the new Premier League season, the 24-year-old Spaniard has told Arsenal he will waive the payments - believed to relate to loyalty bonuses on the remaining four years of his contract - in a last-ditch bid to force through a move.

    Fabregas did not travel to Lisbon, where Arsenal were beaten 2-1 on Saturday night by Benfica. Arsenal manager Arsene Wenger said Fabregas had not been ‘ready to play’.

    Wenger added: 'He’s not injured. He’s training normally but hasn’t played since Bolton, which is two and a half months. The situation with Cesc is not sorted out but I don’t want to talk about it any more.'

    But the latest twist in the protracted saga of the Arsenal captain’s desire to return to the club he left at 16 amounts to an official transfer request.

    The Arsenal Supporters’ Trust drew attention to the £4m clause in Fabregas’s contract last week and the player himself has now offered to give up his right to the money to make the deal easier for Arsenal to accept.

    It could prove decisive, although Fabregas will still have to see if Barcelona raise their latest offer of £32m plus £5m in additional payments, rejected by Arsenal last week.

    Arsenal have insisted since the start of the summer that Barcelona will have to pay £40m for Fabregas and the Catalan club’s vice-president, Josep Maria Bartomeu, and director Raul Sanllehi left London on Friday still having failed to secure coach Pep Guardiola’s prime summer target.

    The impasse over Fabregas threatens to jeopardise manager Arsene Wenger's transfer plans.

    Arsenal cannot make a move for £20m Valencia target Juan Mata until they have secured a price for either Fabregas or Samir Nasri, who was also left out of the squad travelling to Portugal.

     

    What's the Mata?: Valencia's Juan Mata remains and Arsenal target

     

     

    Serious consideration has now been given to selling Nasri to Manchester City for £25m, which would provide the funds for a Mata bid if Barcelona do not come back with a better offer for Fabregas.

    According to reports in France, Wenger lost faith in Nasri after a poor performance against Boca Juniors in the Emirates Cup. But Wenger insisted: 'It’s nonsense. I don’t know where these stories come from.'

    More worrying for Arsenal is the realisation they are making little progress in their recruitment of a much-needed centre-half.

    Some close to the deal expect Everton and Arsenal to agree on a deal of around £15m for Phil Jagielka but the England player is not pushing for a move and Arsenal cannot be sure they will land their man.

    Barcelona to complete £35m deal for Fabregas with Nasri next to leave Arsenal

     

     

     

     

     

     

     

     

     

     

    Finally off? Ces Fabregas (right) looked unhappy in training this week

     

     

    Barcelona are certain they have finally succeeded in their long pursuit of Cesc Fabregas with a package worth around £31million.

    Arsenal were still waiting to receive any such bid but the clamour from reliable Catalan sources suggested a verbal agreement was very close.

    And to make matters worse for Arsene Wenger, he also looks set to lose Samir Nasri to Manchester City after a change of heart at the Emirates.

    The latest Barcelona bid for Fabregas is thought to be a £25.5m transfer fee plus £5.25m of add-ons, depending on the Arsenal captain’s success at the Nou Camp.

    Fabregas has agreed to forgo £4.4m he is owed by Arsenal as a loyalty payment, which had been a sticking point in this summer’s negotiations.

    Barcelona have included this sum in the transfer package, claiming it takes the total beyond the £35m asking price, but Arsenal see this as a payment saved, rather than part of the fee paid.

    The clubs are deep in talks about the details, such as how much is to be paid up front. The Gunners want the £25.5m now but Barca are offering £18m with the rest to follow next year.

     

    Another exit? Samir Nasri could now follow Fabregas out

     

     

    Arsenal appear to have accepted that Barcelona cannot greatly increase their bid by the end of the month, and that Fabregas is set on returning to the club he left eight years ago.

    The 24-year-old has not played for Arsenal since a defeat at Bolton in April. Wenger was ready to name him in the squad for Tuesday’s Champions League play-off against Udinese, but the midfielder is well short of match fitness.

    The latest move is set to bring an end to one of the longest-running transfer sagas and Fabregas could even be unveiled by the weekend.

    The midfielder has made it clear this summer that he wants to finally rejoin the club he left as a 15-year-old boy.

    Arsenal have also decided to accept bids of £22m for Nasri, which could leave them bereft ahead of their opener at Newcastle on Saturday.

    Juan Mata is expected to join from Valencia and Wenger is chasing at least one centre half.

    Arsenal fans, who have put up with six seasons without a trophy, have only seen Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain and Gervinho arrive in major deals this summer.

    Wenger, who turned the Gunners into a force in English football in the 90s, is under pressure to end their long wait for silverware.

    But with the threat of both Manchester clubs, Chelsea and a rejuvenated Liverpool, that task appears as tough as ever.
