    ROY KEANE will know which of his Sunderland stars have lost their focus — by spying on them from his office window. 

    Keano fears some of his cock-a-hoop Black Cats may take their eye off the ball after the historic win over Newcastle last weekend. The Mackems chief reckons it took him ‘50 seconds’ to get over winning massive games as a player. And he says anyone still living off their derby-day delight can forget about playing against Stoke.

    He explained: “I’ve seen it with other teams. They’re just so happy for winning a big derby match they take their eye off the ball for the next few weeks and lose games.

    “I will know if that has happened to any of our players by the way they walk out to training. “If they walk out on their heels, that’s not good. I think you should jog out to training, so I’ll be looking out of my window. “Players coming out with tracksuit bottoms on, that’s a sign too of how switched on players are.

    “Hat and gloves. Are they good? It depends how cold it is!” The Irish legend added: “I regret not enjoying results a bit more when I was a player. I tended to save it up to the end of the season and then have a mad six weeks!

    “I very rarely celebrated during the season — you can’t relax in this game.”
