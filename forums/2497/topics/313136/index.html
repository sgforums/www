    Rob Styles, who awarded the season's daftest penalty at Birmingham on Saturday, has been left off the Barclays Premier League list for this weekend's games.

     

    The Hampshire official baffled players on both sides by pointing to the spot when Manchester City's Sun Jihai went shoulder to shoulder with Gary McSheffrey.

    BBC Match Of The Day pundit Mark Lawrenson described the incident as 'the worst penalty decision I have seen in my life'.

    Sun said yesterday: 'I turned around and chased the ball with McSheffrey, and it was in front of me. 'He went for the ball and tried to push me, but I just used my shoulder for a 50-50. When I heard the whistle I thought he was diving maybe, but the referee pointed to the spot.

    'I could not believe it. Even somebody who knows a little bit about football wouldn't think that was a penalty. 'I couldn't do anything about it so I had to walk away, but that decision killed the game off for us.'

    McSheffrey beat Joe Hart with the spot-kick to clinch a 3-1 victory and give Birmingham a huge boost in their relegation battle.

    In the current climate of extra scrutiny of referees, Styles finds himself without a top game in either the Premier League or semi-finals of the FA Cup this weekend.

    It is not the first time this season Styles' decisions have been criticised. After he awarded a penalty to Chelsea at Anfield in August, referees' supremo Keith Hackett apologised to Liverpool manager Rafa Benitez.

    STYLES `RESTED` AFTER MISTAKES

    Referee Rob Styles has been 'rested' from Premier League action this weekend after a series of bizarre decisions.

     

    Styles appeared to 'even things up' in Birmingham's 3-1 win over Manchester City last weekend, by awarding Birmingham a highly contentious penalty after sending off defender Franck Queudrue earlier in the game.

     

    This was the third game in a row that Styles has pulled such a trick, giving penalties to Nottingham Forest and Watford after dismissing players in their games against Northampton and Stoke respectively.

     

    Styles, who came under immense pressure earlier in the season because of his poor handling of the Chelsea v Liverpool game at Anfield - in which he awarded an extremely soft spot-kick to Florent Malouda - has been left off both the Premier League and Football League lists for the weekend action.

     

    Uriah Rennie will also be putting his feet up after his decision to send off Steve Kabba in Watford's game against Hull was overturned this week.

     

    This suggests that referees chief Keith Hackett is continuing a policy of cracking down on poor performances from his officials. Mike Riley was 'rested' last weekend after his perceived mishandling of the Tottenham v Chelsea game last month, in which he failed to send Ashley Cole off for a late and high challenge on Alan Hutton.
