    With Charlton having lost and confirmed for relagation, the fight is now between Wigan, West Ham and Sheffield Utd.

    It so happens that Sheffield Utd will be hosting Wigan for their last Premiership match. West Ham is away to Man U. Both matches will be played concurrently, adding to the drama of it all.



    West Ham need at least a point at Old Trafford to be assured of safety.

    Secnario 1
    However, should West Ham lose the match against Man U, they will be relegated if Wigan beats Sheffield United. Wigan already have a superior goal difference to West Ham and with a West Ham Loss and a Wigan win will further increase Wigan's goal difference advantage. Unless Wigan beat Sheffield by a 3 goal margin then Sheffield will go down instead.

    Scenario 2
    Should West Ham get the min point against the new EPL champions, Sheffield United will be safe and Wigan will be relagated if Wigan does not win the match.

    Scenario 3
    A West Ham win and a Wigan away win will however result in Sheff Utd being relegated. Wigan will be level on points with Sheff Utd and in fact beat Sheff Utd on goal difference. A victory for Wigan would mean it's goal difference of '-23' would become '-22' at least and Sheff Utd's '-22' would become a '-23' instsead.

    The onus is on West Ham and Wigan to get something out of their matches with Wigan needing nothing less than a win. Wigan however still need to rely on Man U to beat West Ham. Sheff Utd might still be safe if they lose as long as West Ham lose.

    The other end of the table is very interesting indeed.
