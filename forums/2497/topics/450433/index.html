    Feel the fourth! Newcastle gatecrash the £30m battle for Euro spot

     

     

    Pounce: Papiss Cisse helped fire Newcastle one step closer to the Champions League

     

    Down: Tottenham have been in poor form of late

     

    Spirit: Newcastle are in tremendous form

     

     

     

    Newcastle were the big winners as the fight for fourth place in the Premier League and a £30million Champions League bounty took another twist.

    Chelsea are sixth after being held 1-1 by neighbours Fulham on Monday when a Frank Lampard penalty was cancelled out late on by Clint Dempsey.

    Newcastle had earlier gatecrashed the race with a 2-0 home win over Bolton thanks to another goal from their prolific January signing Papiss Cisse.  

    It put them level on 59 points with fourth placed Tottenham, who stumbled to a 2-1 defeat at home to Norwich.

    Spurs manager Harry Redknapp warned: ‘It’s going to be close - it will be the team who wants it most.’

    Chelsea boss Roberto Di Matteo added: ‘We’re closer to that fourth spot than we were. We were always playing catch-up, so the situation was difficult from the start.

    ‘We have to look at it positively. We are closer now than we were a month ago.

    ‘I think it’s going to go down to the last game. Again today, you saw some unexpected results.’

    Even in-form Arsenal, who go to  bottom club Wolves tomorrow on the back of eight wins in nine games, are not certain to qualify with just a two-point advantage keeping them third.

    Redknapp, favourite to be offered the England manager’s job, is under most pressure after Monday’s defeat left his side having taken just six points from their past eight games.

    .

    'Spurs were once 10 points clear of Arsenal, but have faltered since Fabio Capello quit in February and uncertainty began to surround Redknapp’s future.

    Elliott Bennett gave Norwich victory after Jermain Defoe had levelled Anthony Pilkington’s early goal.

    Redknapp admitted: ‘We had a bad day. It’s going to be close, but there are still five games to go. We don’t lose too many games at home. I don’t think we have to win all five. Chelsea play Newcastle; they can’t both win.

     

     

    Dumped: Tottenham have been brought back down to earth by recent results

     

    Destroyer: Clint Dempsey nodded home from a corner to foil Chelsea

     

     

    ‘It would be lovely to finish above Arsenal but we’d settle for finishing fourth. We’ve not been in bad form but today was a setback. All to play for though, still.’

    Newcastle have been the surprise package and yet another goal from £9.5million January recruit Cisse, following a wonder strike by Hatem Ben Arfa against Bolton, gave them their fifth win in a row.

    Manager Alan Pardew said: ‘If the FA Cup semis go our way, we could be in Europe by the weekend and if that works in our favour, then there is nothing we can do except go for the Champions League.

    ‘I have never ruled out the Champions League. To have this points total and not finish in Europe would be tough to take, so we’ve got to keep our eyes on the ball. And we’re not giving up on anything, apart from winning the title.’
