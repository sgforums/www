    CHELSEA'S OPPONENTS.

     

    MARSEILLE

    THEY'RE managed by ex-Blues midfielder Didier Deschamps and the Stade Velodrome will prove an intimidating venue.

    The French side, Ligue One champs, received £24million for Didier Drogba when Chelsea signed him in 2004.

    DANGER RATING: 2

    DANGERMAN: Brandao.

    FIXTURES: Sep 28 - Chelsea v Marseille; Dec 8 - Marseille v Chelsea.

     

     

     

    MSK ZILINA

    QUALIFIED by topping their Slovakian league.

    The club's best-ever European performance came in the 1961 Cup Winners' Cup when they reached the quarter-finals.

    Up until 1995 the club was known as SK Zilina.

    DANGER RATING: 1

    DANGERMAN: Emil Rilke.

    FIXTURES: Sep 15 - MSK Zilina v Chelsea; Nov 23 - Chelsea v MSK Zilina.

     

     

     

    SPARTAK MOSCOW

    FINISHED runners-up in Russian Premier League last season behind FC Rubin Kazan.

    Their home ground, the Luzhniki Stadium, has a FIFA approved plastic pitch - which England played on in 2007 - and holds over 78,000.

    DANGER RATING: 2

    DANGERMAN: Aiden McGeady.

    FIXTURES: Oct 19 - Spartak Moscow v Chelsea; Nov 3 - Chelsea v Spartak Moscow.

    ARSENAL'S OPPONENTS

     

    BRAGA

    BRAGA pipped Porto to second place in the Portuguese League to secure their place in the qualifiers.

    They dispatched Celtic 4-2 on aggregate and then edged out Spanish side Seville 5-3 over two legs in the play-off round.

    DANGER RATING: 2

    DANGERMAN: Albert Meyong.

    FIXTURES: Sept 15 - Arsenal v Braga, Nov 23 - Braga v Arsenal.

     

     

     

    PARTIZAN BELGRADE

    SERBIAN champions Partizan are making their first appearance in the competition for seven years.

    The 1966 runners-up made the group stage by beating Anderlecht on penalties after 2-2 draws in both legs of their play-off.

    DANGER RATING: 1

    DANGERMAN: v.

    FIXTURES: Sept 28 - Partizan v Arsenal, Dec 8 - Arsenal v Partizan.

     

     

     

    SHAKHTAR DONETSK

    A QUICK return to North London for Eduardo, the Croatian striker who left Arsenal for Shakhtar this summer.

    The Ukrainian champions - and 2009 UEFA Cup winners - qualified directly for the group stage without having to go through a play-off.

    DANGER RATING: 2

    DANGERMAN: Eduardo.

    FIXTURES: Oct 19 - Arsenal v Shakhtar, Nov 3 - Shakhtar v Arsenal.
