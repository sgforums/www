    CHAMPIONS LEAGUE LOWDOWN: The teams to avoid, plum picks and all you need to know about the group stage draw

     

     

     

     

    12:00am (Fri)

     

    It's the draw that will define the season. We tried to get excited about the Champions League qualifying rounds - but here comes the serious stuff: the draw for the group stage.

     

    At 5pm on Thursday in Monaco, the 32 teams left in the competition will learn who is standing between them and a place in next spring's all-important knockout stages.

     

    Chelsea, Manchester United, Arsenal and new boys Tottenham are flying the flag for England, while Rangers represent Scotland.

     

    As ever, the participants are seeded and put into four pots to keep the best and worst apart. But the vagaries of UEFA's coefficient rankings means that there are dangers lurking in each pot.

     

    Last season, for example, eventual winners Inter Milan were in Pot Two and were pulled out in the absolutely stinking Group F alongside Barcelona, Dynamo Kiev and rookie Russian champions Rubin Kazan.

     

    Rubin won at the Nou Camp a year ago and are once again the team to avoid from Pot Four. Tottenham's relatively poor record in recent European competition puts them alongside Rangers in Pot Three, but teams can't draw others from their own country, so those lying in wait for Spurs in the top pot are Inter Milan, Barcelona, Bayern Munich, AC Milan and Lyon.

     

    One team from each pot will be drawn to make up the eight groups of four teams.
