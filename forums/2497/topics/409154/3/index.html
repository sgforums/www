    TOTTENHAM'S OPPONENTS

    INTER MILAN

    UNDER former boss Jose Mourinho, Inter scooped the Serie A title, Champions League and Italian Cup last season.

    Ex-Liverpool chief Rafa Benitez is now in charge at the San Siro with Mourinho at Real - but the holders should qualify easily.

    DANGER RATING: 3

    DANGERMAN: Wesley Sneijder.

    FIXTURES: Oct 20 - Inter v Spurs, Nov 2 - Spurs v Inter.

     

     

     

    FC TWENTE

    LIKE Spurs, this is their first Champions League Group Stage.

    In 2008, Arsenal KO'd Steve McClaren's Twente in qualifying.

    The former-England boss, who is now at Wolfsburg, steered them to their first Dutch title last season.

    DANGER RATING: 1

    DANGERMAN: Theo Janssen.

    FIXTURES: Sept 29 - Spurs v FC Twente, Dec 7 - FC Twente v Spurs.

     

     

     

    WERDER BREMEN

    FINISHED third in the Bundesliga last season behind Bayern Munich and Schalke.

    Qualified for the Group Stage by beating Sampdoria 5-4 on aggregate in the play-off.

    However, the loss of classy playmaker Mesut Ozil to Real Madrid is huge.

    DANGER RATING: 2

    DANGERMAN: Marko Arnautovic.

    FIXTURES: Sept 14 - Bremen v Spurs, Nov 24 - Spurs v Bremen.

    MAN UNITED'S OPPONENTS

    BURSASPOR

    TOPPED the Turkish Super League last season to qualify for the Champions League and were also runners-up in the Turkish Super Cup.

    The club reached the quarter-finals of the Cup Winners' Cup way back in the 1974-75 season.

    DANGER RATING: 1

    DANGERMAN: Federico Insua.

    FIXTURES: Oct 20 - Man Utd v Bursaspor, Nov 2 - Bursaspor v Man Utd.

     

     

     

    RANGERS

    SCOTTISH Premier League champions and managed by former Everton boss Walter Smith.

    United chief Alex Ferguson played 57 times for the Glasgow giants between 1967 and 1969, scoring 25 goals for the club.

    DANGER RATING: 2

    DANGERMAN: James Beattie.

    FIXTURES: Sept 14 - Man Utd v Rangers, Nov 24 - Rangers v Man Utd.

     

     

     

    VALENCIA

    ROBERTO SOLDADO

    SPANISH side will be much less of a threat without star duo David Villa and David Silva.

    They were both sold over the summer to balance the books. Their first league game of the season on Saturday against Malaga will show a lot.

    DANGER RATING: 2

    DANGERMAN: Roberto Soldado.

    FIXTURES: Sept 29 - Valencia v Man Utd, Dec 7 - Man Utd v Valencia.
