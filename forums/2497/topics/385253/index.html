    Final 32 sorted in Europa League

    The final 32 of UEFA’s Europa League has been confirmed after the completion of the group stage on Thursday night.

    The top two teams from each of the groups plus the eight sides transferring from their third-place finishes in the Champions League have all qualified and the draw will take place in Nyon, Switzerland on Friday.

    The draw conditions on UEFA’s official website are as follows:

    - Clubs from the same association must not be drawn against each other
    - No team can be drawn against the other qualifiers from their group
    - The four UEFA Champions League qualifiers with the best record in their group (Unirea, Juventus, Wolfsburg, Marseille) are placed in a seeding pot with the UEFA Europa League group winners. The other four (Liverpool, Rubin, Standard, Atlético) are placed with the runners-up
    - The runners-up and the lowest four ranked UEFA Champions League sides will be at home in the first leg.

    ‘The draw for the round of 16 will follow once the draw for the round 32 has been completed. There will be no seeded teams. The 16 green balls containing slips of paper for 'Winners of match 1' to 'Winners of match 16' will be placed in the large central bowl and shuffled. The first team drawn will play its first match at home against the second team drawn and the same procedure will then be repeated for the other matches. There are no restrictions on the draw.’

    Liverpool Unseeded For Europa League Knockout Phase

    Reds could face a heavyweight in last 32


    Liverpool face the prospect of being drawn against some tricky opponents in Europe next year after UEFA today confirmed that the Reds will not be seeded for the knock-out phase of the Europa League.

    Poor goal difference in their Champions League group means that Rafa Benitez's side have missed out on being named as one of the 16 seeds in the new-look competition.

    Seeded teams have the additional advantage of playing the second leg at home.

    Everton and Fulham, England's other representatives, are also unseeded.

    "Liverpool will not be one of the seeded teams," a UEFA spokesman said, according to Press Association Sport.

    "The top seeds are the 12 group winners from the Europa League and the best four third-placed sides from the Champions League.

    "Among the clubs dropping down from the Champions League, Juventus and Unirea Urziceni won more points than Liverpool, while Marseille and Wolfsburg had a better goal difference."

    The round of 32 begins on February 16. The Europa League final will be held in Hamburg on May 12.

    LIVERPOOL will take on Dan Petrescu's Unirea Urziceni in the Europa League last 32.

    Rafa Benitez's side were dumped out of the Champions League after a nightmare group stage campaign earlier this season.

    Reds boss Benitez has already said his side will be concentrating on the Premier League this term.

    But he will have the chance to win back some doubters with a good run in the Europa League.

    That task will start in eastern Europe against former Chelsea star Petrescu's Romania champions.

    Liverpool legend Kenny Dalglish said: "I think they got eight points in their Champions League group which included Rangers, Sevilla and Stuttgart so that's a decent number of points.

    "Obviously there is plenty of coverage now, it's not as if you're going to get a team that won't have been on television so there's plenty of tape there for Rafa to have a look at.

    "And also there's a phone call up the road to Walter Smith at Rangers, I'm sure he'll be happy to disclose any information he's got on them.

    "It will be a difficult game. They go on a break until March now but whether the rest helps them or hinders them remains to be seen."

    Meanwhile, Liverpool's Merseyside rivals EVERTON will meet Portuguese side Sporting Lisbon.

    And FULHAM face Ukrainian side Shakhtar Donetsk — the last winners of the UEFA Cup.
