    Championship - Gera anxious for top flight football

    Championship - West Brom midfielder Zoltan Gera wants a move away from the Hawthorns following the clubs failure to gain promotion to the Premiership.

    Being consigned to another season in the Championship has inevitably led to speculation that the clubs top stars, such as Gera, Jason Koumas and Diomansy Kamara, will demand moves away this summer.

    Gera claimed: "I am not a major star and I don't know what the club are planning for me.

    "I don't want to think about my future just yet. I want to enjoy my break following the season and internationals.

    "It's no secret that I want to play in the Premier League but we did not get there which is a regret of mine. Perhaps I will get a chance to play in the Premiership again some time, I don't know.

    "I hope I will get the opportunity to go back there but right now no decision has been made over what will happen over my future and it's not really my choice at the moment."

    The Hungarian international has in the past attracted interest from Middlesbrough, Portsmouth and Wigan.

    But Albion chairman Jeremy Peace has insisted that the club are not desperate to sell, and will hold out for top prices if their players are to be sold.
