    World Cup 2010: Tom Huddlestone And Darren Bent Set To Start For England Against Japan

    Both players have final chance to impress Capello before final squad choice.

    England vs Japan

    • Starhub Football Channel 222
    • *TONIGHT*     8.10 PM
    England Training Session,Emile Heskey, Michael Dawson, Matthew Upson and Darren Bent, England (Getty Images)

    Tom Huddlestone and Darren Bent have both been confirmed as members of England's starting XI to face Japan on Sunday.

    The pair are widely seen as outsiders for places in Fabio Capello's final 23-man squad but now have a final chance to stake their claim to a place on the plane to South Africa 2010.

    Given the form of Peter Crouch and Capello's admiration for Emile Heskey's ability to hold the ball up and Jermain Defoe's pacy trickery the match is crucial for Bent.

    The Sunderland hitman was ruled-out of Monday's 3-1 victory over Mexico due to a hamstring injury and has yet to show the talent for England that saw him net 24 Premier League goals last season.

    Huddlestone came off the bench against Mexico and is seen by many as an alternative to Gareth Barry if the Manchester City man is unable to shrug of an ankle injury in time.

    And Capello insists he is a fan of the Spurs man in that all-important  holding midfield role.

    "I have seen Huddlestone in a lot of games," Capello told reporters.

    "This year he always played very well as a holding player and I am happy because he is here as he is one of the important English players."

    Other selections against Japan include Portsmouth's David James who will start his first international match since the qualifying victory over Ukraine in April 2009 and Spurs' Aaron Lennon.

    It is thought Lennon will also be handed a a starting berth on the left wing after hugging that touchline in training for much of this week.
