    Barcelona are ready to make a summer move for Arsenal skipper Cesc Fabregas, according to a report from El Mundo Deportivo.

    The Spanish newspaper claims that the Catalan midfielder has told his friends and family that he has agreed a four-year deal with Barca.

    The only thing that now apparently remains to be sorted out is a an agreement between the Gunners and the Spanish giants.

    However, Arsene Wenger is unlikley to stand in the player's way as he is well aware of his strong desire to move back to his boyhood club.

    El Mundo Deportivo     speculates that a fee of approximately £34 million (€40m) will be the fee in the deal, whilst Yaya Toure could move in the opposite direction.

    Real Madrid, Inter and Manchester City are also reportedly considering moves for the player.

    Barcelona midfielder Yaya Toure seems certain to leave the Catalan club this summer with Sport quoting his agent as saying there is a 90 per cent chance he will depart, with Arsenal the most likely destination.

    "The player has a 90 per cent chance of leaving Barcelona this summer," Toure's agent, Dimitri Seljuk, is quoted as saying.

    Seljuk also suggested that the reason the Ivorian midfielder has not featured as much this season is so that the club could avoid paying financial incentives stipulated in his contract.

    "Unfortunately, this season, Toure has not played what he would have liked. I think that this is due to a clause in his contract renewal. He has a major financial bonus if he plays more than 60 per cent of official matches of the season."

    The agent also claimed that Toure, who turns 27 today, is the best player in the world in his position and would command the interest of of most European clubs if he were made available.

     "Toure is the best player in the world in his position. He can play in defence or as an attacking midfielder, which he showed at Monaco, where he played in five different positions in five games," he noted before adding that a deal with Arsenal may be on the cards.

    "He is happy in Barcelona but I think most clubs in Europe are interested in the services of Yaya Toure. In any case, Arsenal are interested in him and I think we can do something with the club of Arsene Wenger."

    It is suggested that Barcelona would be willing to let Toure depart for the Emirates as a makeweight in a deal to bring Gunners captain Cesc Fabregas back to Catalunya

    BARCA FAN ... Cesc Fabregas

     

     

    CESC FABREGAS is ready to walk out on Arsenal next week after admitting: I'd love to join Barcelona.

    The Gunners skipper will decide his future this weekend after crunch talks with boss Arsene Wenger. But reports in Spain quoted Fabregas as saying: "If I ever leave Arsenal it will be to play for Barcelona. 

    "I would like to go, whether they want me or not is another matter. "I am 23 years old and I have a long career in front of me. I've never hidden the fact that playing for Barcelona is a dream that I would like to fulfil."

    Fabregas, recovering from a broken leg, wants the matter resolved quickly so he can concentrate on helping Spain in the World Cup.

    He added: "I would like to know my future before the World Cup because to play with other things in my head will not benefit anyone."

    Fabregas' words will come as a huge shock to Arsenal fans who were desperately hoping their best player would stay at the club.

    He has grown increasingly frustrated over Arsenal's inability to win trophies, while Barca continue to bring in silverware.

    According to Spanish sources, he is also concerned about Wenger's long-term intentions.

    The Arsenal boss only has a year left on his own contract - and has not yet agreed to carry on after that.

    There is also little sign of big-name players moving to the Emirates during the summer, casting further doubt on Arsenal's ability to challenge for the major trophies.
