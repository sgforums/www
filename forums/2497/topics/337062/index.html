    Real Madrid’s miserable week shows no signs of ending, as it has been confirmed that striker Ruud van Nistelrooy is set to miss the remainder of the season through injury.

    The Dutch striker underwent an exploratory arthroscopic operation in the United States and knee specialist Dr Richard Steadman has decided the forward will go under the knife.

    This operation means Los Merengues’ goal-machine will not play again this term, which is a huge blow for the Primera Liga champions.

    Real chairman Ramon Calderon and sporting director Predrag Mijatovic are likely to devote their time to pinpointing a replacement for their main source of goals.

    “After the arthroscopy on Ruud van Nistelrooy, a partial rupture of (...) his anterior knee ligament operated on in 2000 and a lesion of the cartilage have been found,” read a club statement.

    “The club’s medical team and Dr Steadman, with the player’s approval, have decided that surgery is required.

    “The estimate length of his unavailability is expected to last between six and nine months depending on his recovery.”

    Crespo On Real's Radar – Reports

    Following the news that Ruud Van Nistelorooy is set to miss the remainder of the season, Real Madrid are in search of a new striker to replace the Dutchman.

     

     

    It has been revealed that Real Madrid’s Ruud Van Nistelrooy will miss the remainder of the season as the Dutch striker requires a similar operation to that which he underwent in 2000 prior to joining Manchester United from PSV.

    It’s a crushing blow to Los Merengues and as a result, they are now in searach of a possible replacement.

    Many names have been linked with the club in recent weeks and the latest, accoring to Datasport, is Hernan Crespo of Inter Milan, who is all but certain to leave the Italian club in January following another falling out with boss Jose Mourinho.

    It has been reported that Madrid are keen on the striker as, amongst other things, he is not cup-tied in the Champions League, due to his omission from the competition's squad by Mourinho.

    The other two names that are on El Real's radar are Didier Drogba of Chelsea and Diego Milito of Genoa, who has had a tremendous season in the Serie A thus far since his summer move from Real Zaragoza.

    A move to the nine time European champions for Crespo sounds unlikely, however if he is who the club want, then the move could be a real possibility.

    It is also rumoured that the 31-time La Liga champions are also interested in Manchester United's Carlos Tévez, Jan Klaas Huntelaar of Ajax, Mario Gomez from Stuttgart, and CSKA Moscow man Vagner Love.
