    Tottenham willing to offer Keane, Bentley for Villa’s Carew


    Tottenham are making a swap offer for Aston Villa striker John Carew.

    The Daily Star says Tottenham are ready to offer club captain Robbie Keane as bait to land Carew.

    Spurs will approach Villa and make it clear they are prepared to let Keane join them as part of a deal to net Carew.

    Martin O’Neill will be told he can have Keane if he stumps up £5m plus Carew as Tottenham cut their losses on the Irish striker they signed for £12m from Liverpool a year ago.

    Spurs boss Harry Redknapp has targeted the Villa star as one of the men he wants to aid a Champions League charge.

    They want to recoup as much of the £12m they paid Liverpool last year to bring back Keane.

    Spurs are also willing to use want-away David Bentley as part of a deal to sign Carew, 30.

    O’Neill is a big fan of Keane and was linked with a move for the 29-year-old last summer.

    Tottenham Hotspur Boss Harry Redknapp: Robbie Keane Going No-Where

    Spurs keen on Keane stay...

    Robbie Keane

    Tottenham manager Harry Redknapp has played down reports of Robbie Keane leaving White Hart Lane. The Irish hitman has been linked with a number of clubs, with Celtic and Aston Villa believed to be leading the chase.

     

    However Redknapp is a big fan of the Republic of Ireland international and says only a massive offer would tempt him to sell.

    "Please God, he'll still be here in February," Redknapp told the Press Association. 

    "He is the captain, he's here, he's done a great job for us and is very important to us.

    "There's real competition between him, Roman Pavlyuchenko, Jermain Defoe and Peter Crouch but unless someone comes up with £50million or something for him I can't see any way he won't be here.

    "He's part of the plans and I haven't spoken to anyone about him leaving or wanting to leave.

    I never made any enquiries at Aston Villa, if I did I would have spoken to Martin O'Neill who is a very good friend."
