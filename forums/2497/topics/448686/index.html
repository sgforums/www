    Robbie cries Fowl as Blackpool offer Reds legend £100 a week

     

     

    No deal: Robbie Fowler trained with Blackpool but will not be turning out for the Championship outfit

     

     

     

     

    Blackpool failed to land Robbie Fowler after offering the striker just £100 a week.

    Fowler looked set for a spell with the Seasiders after training with the club but was shocked when chairman Karl Oyston put his offer on the table.

    The salary would have been topped up with £5,000 for every first-team appearance but Fowler, 36, is several weeks away from match fitness.

    Blackpool boss Ian Holloway, who has 38-year-old Kevin Phillips and Brett Ormerod, 35, up front, said: 'Unless I can get things done and a deal done in a slightly different way, then he won't be coming. If it doesn't happen, I'll be disappointed, but such is life.'

    Fowler established himself as one of the best finishers in the game during his Liverpool days and starred for Leeds and Manchester City before returning for a second spell at Anfield.

    He played for Cardiff and had a brief stay at Blackburn before heading abroad.

    Fowler turned out for North Queensland Fury and Perth Glory in Australia before signing for Thai outfit Muangthong United.

    Hull and Doncaster have been linked with Fowler while Fleetwood were also weighing up an ambitious bid for the former England international.
