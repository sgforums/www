    Xavi Hernández has stated that he wants to stay with Barcelona for the rest of his career as negotiations over a new contract at Camp Nou continue.

    The Catalan playmaker is confident that a deal will be struck to see him stay at the club for several more years because he has always been made to feel wanted.

    With his current contract set to expire in June 2010 there is no rush to put pen-to-paper, but coach Pep Guardiola wants the matter sorted to prevent any kind of problems arising.

    “I am sure that we will reach an agreement because we both want it to happen,” the club's official website quoted Xavi as saying.

    “My dream is to retire at Barça. I have always said that I want to stay here. I have always felt comfortable and highly valued by the club. At no time have they said that they want to sell me.”
