    BRAZIL NUTS ... Phil Scolari is furious with his Chelsea flops

     

     

    BIG PHIL SCOLARI has slammed his fading Chelsea stars in the wake of their 3-0 humiliation at Manchester United. And he accused some of playing at no more than 35 per cent of their potential. 

    Scolari’s job at Stamford Bridge will be on the line if he cannot turn the tide threatening to engulf the Blues, who have managed just one win in their last six games. 

    He accepts the criticism but says it is now time for his team to listen and do things his way — starting with tonight’s FA Cup third-round replay at Southend. Changes will be made in tactics and personnel as Chelsea switch back to Scolari’s preferred zonal marking system and big names like Ivory Coast hitman Didier Drogba are dumped. 

    If that does not work, Scolari is ready to take the blame because he knows the zonal tactic is unpopular with his defenders. The Brazilian gaffer admitted: “In the last five games we played at 50 per cent of our potential, no more than this. 

    “Some players have been at 75 to 80 per cent, while others have been at 35-40 per cent. “But the balance for our team as a whole in the last five games has been 50 per cent. 

    “If you ask me if this team are ready to win things, then ‘no’. They are NOT ready to win a trophy — and they know this. “They might not say things publicly but they know we are not ready. 

    “They need to understand that now and we need more work. Sometimes when we are wrong we need to pay more attention. We need a win — now.” 

    Chelsea are second in the Premier table, four points behind Liverpool and one point ahead of United, who have two games in hand. And Scolari added: “I understand people don’t have a very good opinion about me because we have just lost a game 3-0 against Manchester United at Old Trafford. 

    “And I know that I need to work more than before with my players. All the players have had their chance. I have given every one of them at least five or six games to impress. 

    “We will start with one team against Southend United and maybe I will start with the same team against Stoke City. “But I need answers from the players. I will give them their chance and I need answers. 

    “In the last three or four games — we need to talk about the fact that we have lost goals from set-pieces. “Against Southend, we must not make the same mistakes. “But what has happened is a lesson for us — in 25 games we had no mistakes and then we have in the last three or four. 

    “My team knows it’s not normal to have so many mistakes. “Eight times we have made mistakes and have conceded in five games. “When I arrived we marked zonally but then we changed to a man-to-man system. Now with the new system if things go wrong it will be my fault — not the players’ — because it is my system.”

    Scolari added: “In the last four games, we are wrong. “We need to admit to the people, to the critics and to the fans what has happened. “We need to explain it and there is nothing more than this to say. “The players have been trying hard, I know that. 

    “Do you think that any player will go on the pitch in front of 75,000 people like at Old Trafford and not want to play? Never. Sometimes they make mistakes because they are not in a good condition but not because they do not want to play. 

    “If this happened then they would be finished. “I am a good man but I am not a fool. Sometimes I’m a fool but not every time. “And if Chelsea lose a game it’s not just me — it’s the whole of the team. We are in the same boat.”
