    Chelsea skipper Terry: I'm fed-up with Man Utd success

    Chelsea captain John Terry says he's fed-up with Manchester United's three-year dominance of the Premiership.

    "I’m fed up with seeing Man United winning trophies. I want to push on as a player and win so many more things for this football club,” Terry told the Sunday Times.

    “I want to win the Champions League. I swear to God I’m desperate to win it.

    “But I’m really desperate to get the title back off Manchester United. It’s been three years and it’s killed me personally. Going three years without the Prem title isn’t where we want to be as a football club.”

    Chelsea hero Lampard: So sweet beating Man Utd

    Chelsea midfielder Frank Lampard was delighted with their "sweet" Community Shield success over Manchester United.

    After the game Lampard admitted it was a "sweet moment" to claim the Community Shield at the expense of the defending Premier League champions.

    "This is sweet. It feels nice to make a winning start to the season," Lampard told Sky Sports 2.

    "After that last goal we could have been on a downer but we didn't let out heads drop."

    The United players were furious that Lampard's goal in normal time was allowed to stand but the England midfielder believes it was within the rules of the game.

    "Anyone should look at the rules," he said. "It's up to the referee to stop it but Man United just stopped when their man was down.

    "It's in the rules, when the referee stops it he stops it. We carried on and I'm sure United would have down the same in that position."
