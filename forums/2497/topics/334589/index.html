Windass: Hull For Europe

  • omgukilledkenny
    23 Oct 08, 02:50

    Hull City forward Dean Windass has said there is no reason why his side cannot remain in the upper reaches of the table for the remainder of the current Premier League season.

    The Tigers have had a stunning start to the campaign having been promoted via the play-offs last May and currently sit sandwiched between the likes of Chelsea, Liverpool, Arsenal and Manchester United at the top of the table, having already defeated the Gunners at the Emirates Stadium.

    And Windass has spoken to the press about the high levels of belief in the Hull camp, stating his confidence that their good form can continue throughout the 38-game league season.

    The 39-year-old feels there is no reason why they should not aim high and says European qualification is a genuine possibility.

    "The lads were superb last Saturday in the 1-0 win against West Ham and our season continues to go from strength to strength," Windass stated.

    "The great thing about the campaign so far is, apart from one bad game against Wigan [a 5-0 home defeat], no-one has really bothered us, and as a result belief in the camp is huge at the moment,” he commented.

    "We believe we can be up there challenging for Europe at the end of the season, and if everyone keeps working hard then we will keep upsetting some of the bigger sides.

    "We've got games coming up against both Chelsea and Manchester United and there will be no pressure on us in those games. Everyone will expect us to lose but maybe we'll surprise people again."

    Bookmakers’ are not convinced Hull’s strong start will continue however, with Phil Brown’s men still fourth favorites to suffer relegation.
