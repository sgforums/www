    AC MILAN superstar Kaka has ruled out a reunion with Big Phil Scolari at Chelsea. The striker worked under the new Blues boss when Brazil won the World Cup in 2002. But the FIFA World Player of the Year insists he is happy at AC, despite their failure to qualify for next season's Champions League.

     

    Kaka said: ""I appreciate Chelsea's reported interest but I have not got the slightest desire to leave Milan. "The only way today to leave Milan would be if Milan want to sell me. "But, from what I've seen, it seems difficult."

     

    Milan pulled Kaka out of the Brazil squad for the Olympics this summer but he is adamant he does not have any problem with the Italian club.

     

    Kaka added: "Milan have the right to veto me. "I have spoken publicly about a desire to play in the Olympics. "For now, the decision is this. Now the Brazlian FA could also make a request to Milan."

    Luiz Felipe Scolari's dream of building a fantasy Chelsea team hit a THIRD massive set-back last night - when Brazilian star Kaka told him: "Thanks, but no thanks."

    Owner Roman Abramovich is backing boss Scolari with an unlimited transfer budget as the Blues bid to bounce back from last season's failures in the title race and the Champions League Final. But prime target Kaka has joined Fernando Torres and David Villa in insisting he does not want to join Chelsea.

    The AC Milan midfielder, 26, said: "I appreciate Chelsea's reported interest, but I have not got the slightest desire to leave Milan." Valencia's Villa, the hottest striker at Euro 2008, has rejected out of hand a switch, regardless of how much he might be paid.

    Villa declared: "Money means nothing. Of course, everybody works to earn money, but if you are not happy, material things mean nothing. "My only target in life is to get better at my job."

    Liverpool marksman Torres has also dismissed the Blues' overtures, with the Reds insisting the Spanish frontman is not for sale at any price. Meanwhile, Chelsea target Franck Ribery injured his knee as France crashed out of Euro 2008 and the Bayern Munich winger looks set for a long spell on the sidelines.

    The west Londoners are now monitoring Ajax striker Klaas-Jan Huntelaar, and are also expected to tie up the signing of Portugal's Barcelona reject Deco after the Euros.
