    Liverpool down and on their way out - but defiant Rafa Benitez remains hopeful of a miracle

    Benitez took off Fernando Torres - only to watch Lisandro grab a 90th-minute equaliser.

     

    AGONY ... Jamie Carragher can't believe it

     

     

     

     

    RAFA BENITEZ defiantly insists he does NOT fear for his job - but admits Liverpool need a Champions League miracle.

    The Kop kings are clinging to Euro survival by a thread after a cruel end to their trip into the Lyon's den last night.

    Lisandro's late strike means they will be OUT if Fiorentina beat the French in three weeks. But Benitez roared: "I don't think my job is on the line. I'm not thinking about that - just the next game.

    "Clearly it's difficult now but it's not impossible. "We have to win our next game against Debrecen and see what happens in the other match - then approach the last depending on the result. "But we have to keep going, that's all we can do. We have done miracles before... maybe we can do it again."

    With Anfield legend Kenny Dalglish, a club ambassador, watching from the stands, Liverpool took an 83rd-minute lead thanks to Ryan Babel's 30-yard scorcher.

    Benitez then took off Fernando Torres - only to watch Lisandro grab a 90th-minute equaliser. It mirrored the manager's move at Fulham on Saturday, when he replaced star striker Torres with the score 1-1, only for his side to lose 3-1.

    It was the SECOND time Lyon had stunned the Kop giants with a late strike after nicking the first game on Merseyside in the dying seconds.

    Benitez admitted: "Obviously we have to be really disappointed because this was a great opportunity - we were there. Again it's a late goal against Lyon and it makes a massive difference.

    "How I keep my own head up now is very simple - by watching the performance of the players and hearing the fans singing at the end."

    Liverpool, third in Group E on four points, are away to Debrecen on November 24, while Fiorentina host Lyon. Then Italy's Fiorentina visit Anfield in the last game on December 9.

    Benitez added: "We know football is like this. "Sometimes you need some luck and tonight we had none at the end.
