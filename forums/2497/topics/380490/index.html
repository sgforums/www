    JOHNSON: REDS FACE DISASTER

    .
    .

     

    Liverpool defender Glen Johnson has admitted an early exit from the Champions League would be a "disaster" for the Anfield outfit. Rafa Benitez's side are floundering in the Premier League title race after suffering their fifth defeat of the season away to Fulham on Saturday, a match which full-back Johnson missed through injury. 

    And their European ambitions are also hanging in the balance after back-to-back defeats at the hands of Fiorentina and Lyon ahead of Wednesday's crucial return fixture away to the French side. 

    England international Johnson knows the implications of the Merseyside giants failing to progress to the knockout stages and does not want to even consider the prospect of slipping into the Europa League. 

    "It would be a disaster for this club not qualifying for the knockout stages - but it's not something we are thinking about," he told the Daily Star Sunday. 

    "We have still got to believe we can do it. We don't even want to start thinking about the Europa League. "It will be no consolation at all. It's not the aim and it's not the plan because we want to be in the Champions. It is one of the main reasons why players want to play for a big side. 

    "The Champions League is the best club competition there is. I played in it a few times with Chelsea and playing in it again was something that I was looking forward to doing. 

    "And I would love to go on and win it with Liverpool. But really I don't want to look too far ahead because we have made things very difficult for ourselves in the last two games. 

    "On paper we should be winning this group. The Champions League is just as important to us as the Premier League. "They are both massive competitions and we like to do well in them both. The further that we get in the Champions League and the closer we get to the title, the happier the club will be. "But playing in Europe, as we have shown over the last few years, suits our style." 

    Former Chelsea and Portsmouth defender Johnson admits Benitez's men need to take maximum points from their remaining fixtures against Lyon, Fiorentina and Debrecen to be sure of progressing. 

    Lyon currently head Group E with a six-point cushion over the Reds with Fiorentina, who face point-less Hungarian side Debrecen in midweek, enjoying a three-point advantage. 

    Johnson added: "We are three points behind Fiorentina, so we may need to win all three remaining games. Lyon have got to play Fiorentina as well, so somebody will drop points. 

    "We would be happy with three wins but Lyon are a fantastic team who score a lot of goals at home. 

    "Any away game in the Champions League is hard. We must stick together and fight for each other. Hopefully we can keep a clean sheet and come back with three points."

    ARSENE WENGER: EDUARDO DIVE STORM MEANS ARSENAL ARE MARKED

     

     

    ARSENAL v AZ ALKMAAR

     

     

     

     

    Arsene Wenger reckons referees are reluctant to award Arsenal penalties following the Eduardo dive storm.

    The Gunners star was hit with a two-game Champions League ban after being found guilty of diving in the qualifying clash with Celtic, although the decision was overturned on appeal by UEFA.

    But while Eduardo and Arsenal escaped punishment over the affair, Wenger still believes there has been a heavy price to pay for his club.

    Twelve days ago, against AZ Alkmaar, they had two strong penalty claims rejected. 

    And as Wenger's side prepare to face AZ at the Emirates on Wednesday, he said: "In Alkmaar, there was a 100 per cent penalty call against Carlos Vela and another against Robin van Persie. 

    "I fear completely that some referees are going into the game with that mind-set. 

    "What happened with Vela was unbelievable. I hoped referees wouldn't think that way but after that I changed my mind."

    This is gonna be Rafa’s 3rd final

     

     

     

     

     

     

    RAFA BENITEZ steps into the Lyon's den tonight facing his third Champions League final as Liverpool chief. But there will be no trophy up for grabs, no lap of honour if they win. There is just the knowledge that defeat all but spells the end of Liverpool's European campaign before it has really left the blocks.

    Never mind the 2005 Istanbul epic, the most dramatic Euro final in history. Forget the 2007 Athens revenge of Milan. Tonight the prize is far, far more important - both in terms of the club and, very likely, the long-term future of the manager.

    Effectively, it's a one-off shoot-out with just about everything at stake. Beat Lyon and all bets are suddenly back on, with progress to the knockout stages heading the agenda again. Defeat and the embarrassing trapdoor to the Europa League swings open.

    When they reached those two finals in three years, even though one ended in defeat, the financial windfall from both was obvious - in terms of prize money, TV cash and the marketing spin-offs. This time only a dark abyss looms should they crash. And for a club whose money woes are well documented, the repercussions for all concerned do not bear thinking about.

    To cap it all, Benitez must pull a rabbit out of the hat with arguably the most rag-tag team of patched-up stars and rookies he has sent out for a game of such importance. Skipper Steven Gerrard's groin meant he didn't even make the trip, while fellow crocks Glen Johnson, Fabio Aurelio and Martin Skrtel failed to travel. Reserve full-back Martin Kelly is another absentee, while Daniel Agger's back problem remains such a concern he spent the entire flight standing up.

    Striker Fernando Torres will lead the line with his manager admitting he could need a hernia op, with the prospect of his long-standing groin problem flaring up at any moment.

    The lack of numbers is so severe that £20million summer signing Alberto Aquilani could well make his first start, despite lacking match fitness and just recovering from a virus.

    Benitez admitted: "We'll see how he trains then we'll decide. It's something we have to discuss, but it's possible he can play from the start, why not?"

    Little wonder, then, that Benitez was last night calling for another dose of the famous European spirit which has hauled Liverpool out of so many holes in the past.

    The defiant Kop boss, edging ever closer to the brink, roared: "We are very positive. We have some experience and we know it's difficult. "But we also have the belief we can do it because we have done it in the past. We respect Lyon but we have to do our job and win. All of the Liverpool players are ready to play, that's the main thing, whether it's Torres or anyone else.

    "The team spirit, work-rate and mentality are very good. We are still working very hard and because we have experience, we try to manage when we are under pressure. "It's easy for a manager when you are winning, but when you are under pressure you have to show character, you try to analyse things and be calm.

    "If you are nervous, that's when you make mistakes - but we have experience of the way we need to do things. "We can use that experience from the past to help us perform well this time too.

    "It's a massive game, a massive competition. We have to approach it thinking we can win. "The players are disappointed because we're not playing as badly as results show."

    After sneaking past Debrecen in the opener, the Reds crashed in Florence and were then mugged by Lyon's late recovery at Anfield. While defeat would not mathematically spell the end of the dream, the worrying prospect of Lyon and Fiorentina playing out a draw in three weeks, to ensure their progress and the Reds' exit, remains.

    Yet at least history is on the side of the Reds, whose defeat at Fulham on Saturday was their sixth in seven matches. They have twice before in this competition reached this stage with just three points - and both times they went through.

    They have NEVER lost three on the bounce in the Champions League. And two years ago the Reds recovered from having just one point at this stage to win the next three and go through.

    Benitez added: "We have to go and do the right things and just think about winning - we cannot think in any other way."

    RAFA QUOTES FROM ANTHEM

    .
    .

     

    Rafa Benitez quoted from You'll Never Walk Alone when asked about his future as Liverpool manager ahead of the crunch clash with Lyon. 

    Benitez goes into the massive match in danger of leading Liverpool to their seventh defeat in eight games and exit from the Champions League. 

    But Benitez said: "I am really pleased to be here and I want to be here for a long time. Always, if you walk through a storm you hold your head up high, so that is what we will do." 

    The Spaniard cleverly used the language of the fans to keep them on his side during the worst spell of his tenure at the club. 

    He said: "Our fans are very clever. They know what the position of the club was when we arrived and what it is now. They know sometimes you have bad moments and that we are working so hard to try and change this." 

    Liverpool face Lyon without Steven Gerrard, Albert Riera, Martin Skrtel, Martin Kelly, Andrea Dossena, Glen Johnson and Fábio Aurélio, while Fernando Torres, Daniel Agger, David Ngog, Alberto Aquilani and Diego Cavalieri all have problems. 

    Torres' injury has been a subject for debate after Benitez admitted that he could need surgery. But the Spaniard claims that an operation is avoidable, saying: "I was talking with the doctor and he said it's a sportsman's hernia. 

    "It means he has a problem and we have to manage the problem. The positive thing is he is improving. He is training, sometimes with pain and playing sometimes with pain, but it's less pain every time. 

    "Hopefully we can continue doing well with him, and maybe he will not need an operation, that is our idea."

    DEAR Rafa,

    You once commented that Liverpool fans are an intelligent bunch.

    Well, indeed we are and we also happen to be a very forgiving bunch.

    We recognise a spirited defeat and we are also able to identify a totally unforgivable defeat when we see one.

    For a start, we are always behind any player who pull on a red shirt but for once, myself and millions of Kopites around the world have had enough seeing players like Phillip Degen and Andriy Voronin putting on the shirt and do not understand the significance of it.

    Bill Shankly once said football isn't a matter of life and death, it's much more important than that.

    Which is why this defeat by Fulham last Saturday hurts badly - it is the knowledge that our season, no matter what anyone says now, is virtually over.

    I sincerely wish to see Lyon hammering the final nail into the coffin tomorrow and send us out of the Champions League.

    Dear Rafa, you said in your pre-match notes a few hours before the game that only Steven Gerrard, Albert Riera, Alberto Aquilani and Glen Johnson are out.

    Yet we were all shocked to see people like Nathan Eccleston, Jay Spearing, Damien Plessis and Daniel Ayala on the bench.

    Where were Fabio Aurelio, Daniel Agger, Martin Skrtel and company? This is a question only you can answer.

    If you are telling us you wish to protect your players, well let me remind you that this is the Premier League, this is a season where our arch-rivals Man United are tied with us on 18 titles.

    It is safe to say that Bob Paisley and Shankly would both rise from their graves in anger should they ever surpass our record this season.

    The final straw was not when you substituted both Fernando Torres and Yossi Benyoun - our most likely source of goals - but when you put in a young defender when there was something like 10 mintues to go and our team was chasing the game.

    It shows how much you wanted to win the game. Other teams would have put in a striker to try and go for the kill.

    Are you telling us you have no options on the bench?

    Well, who was the one who put all these kids on the bench and left out the other first-team players for no apparent reason?

    Who was the one who substituted Torres with just 64 minutes gone on the clock?

    I'm horrified, this is a complete travesty that can only be resolved by two methods.

    1) You resign

    2) You get sacked

     

    Yours sincerely
    Floyd Koh
    You Will Never Walk Alone
