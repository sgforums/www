    Arsene Wenger reportedly met FA chief executive Brian Barwick this week to offer his opinion on who should succeed Steve McLaren as England boss.

    Details of the meeting are sketchy, but the Emirates gaffer has since made it perfectly clear that foreign frontrunners Jose Mourinho and Fabio Cappello are unsuitable in his view.

    "You are very nationalistic; you want the English national team to do well. I believe that you have to choose an Englishman who represents the country," the 58-year-old told the Guardian.

    "I do not understand your logic because you want people to represent the national qualities. Why do you go for a foreign guy?

    "It is like you go to war and you say 'now we choose a general from Portugal or a general from Italy to lead our army'. Would that idea cross your mind? Never."

    Wenger and Mourinho enjoyed a healthy rivalry during the latterÂ’s spell in charge of London rivals Chelsea. Wenger insisted, though, that there is a wealth of homegrown managers who can lead England to the World Cup in 2010.

    "You have [Harry] Redknapp, [Alan] Curbishley, [Steve] Coppell, [Gareth] Southgate, [Stuart] Pearce, [Sam] Allardyce - you have plenty, but you do not rate them enough. Give them a chance.

    "The national team does not play with a Portuguese player or an Italian player so why should suddenly the leader just be foreign? The club is open to the world and the national team is representing the country."

    Wenger has been repeatedly criticised for snubbing English players in favour of expensive imports. Sir Alex Ferguson is the latest football figure to call for a foreign quota in the Premiership. Wenger has only two Englishmen Â– Theo Walcott and Justin Hoyte Â– in his first team squad.

    However, the wily Frenchman reiterated his belief that the decline of English football is not a result of the global nature of the Premier League.

    "I have said many times that from 1966 to 1996 we had no foreigners [in England] and you didn't win any more or any less,

    "You cannot want the best league in the world and only play English players. It is not my responsibility that the English national team does well. I am happy if they do well but it is not my first priority or responsibility. "
