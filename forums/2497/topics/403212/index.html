    Factory aims to produce 25,000 Vuvuzelas every day!

    A Chinese factory making vuvuzelas is having to increase production to keep up with huge demand during the World Cup.

    Chinese manufacturers say almost 90 percent of the vuvuzelas used in South Africa are made in China.

    The Ninghai Jiying plastics factory based in the coastal city of Ningbo is one such manufacturer raking in the profits from the vuvuzelas.

    Its general manager Wu Yijun told Reuters, "We had developed this vuvuzela trumpet in 2001, but we did not manage to sell it back then. During the 2006 World Cup in Germany, we did not manage to market it for the event, even when we sold other kinds of trumpets.

    "After South Africa was successful in its bid for the World Cup, we made our hardest push for the vuvuzela to be used for the tournament," he added.

    "With regards to the production volume, from January to April this year, we were producing 25,000 vuvuzelas every day to fill containers that are exported from Ningbo port," Wu said.

    "The production had stopped in April but there has been a gradual increase in our production in recent days.

    "We were making 10,000 vuvuzelas a day for the past few days and I estimate we will make 25,000 per day in the next few days.
    "The demand from the Chinese market alone is surpassing our production capability," he said.
