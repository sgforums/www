    Sol Campbell is back! Arsenal sign former England defender again after Notts County saga

     

    Gun and dusted: Sol Campbell in action for Arsenal's reserve team at West Ham on Tuesday night

     

    Sol Campbell will complete a shock £20,000-a-week return to Arsenal in the next few days. The 35-year-old defender has been training with Arsene Wenger’s side since walking out on Notts County in September after one game. He will sign for them on a free transfer until the end of the season.

    Campbell played the first half of the Gunners’ reserves game against West Ham at Upton Park on Tuesday night and has been pencilled in for the FA Cup fourth-round tie against Stoke City on January 24.

    After his side’s 4-2 win Campbell said. ‘I felt fine - 45 minutes was enough after four months out.'

    Earlier he had talked about his excitement at getting the opportunity of returning to play for Arsenal.

    ‘I cannot tell you how much I’ve missed playing after being out so long since leaving Notts County,’ he told ESPN. ‘There are still a few technicalities to be ironed out but I am signing until the end of the season, and it’s fantastic, great, marvellous.

     

    Respect: Campbell has paid tribute to the influence of Arsene Wenger

     

     

    ‘I’m ecstatic to be back at Arsenal and I have worked bloody hard to get there. Arsene Wenger has been an inspiration.

    ‘He has watched me closely after letting me train at Arsenal for nearly two months. He’s not stupid - he wouldn’t have taken me back if he didn’t like what he has seen.

    ‘I’ve missed it and I’ve a gut feeling this is the right move. It’s no longer a hunger to get back - it’s turned into a craving. I’m raring to go.’

    Wenger has been prompted to re-sign Campbell, who last played for Arsenal in May 2006, owing to concerns over the fitness of William Gallas.
