    Eriksson targets new goalkeeper
    Manchester City manager Sven-Goran Eriksson is set to add a goalkeeper to his list of summer acquisitions.

    The Swede, who has signed eight new players since arriving at Eastlands, is expected to make another move in the transfer market after learning first-choice Andreas Isaksson, pictured, is likely to miss the first month of the new season. The Sweden international has sustained a hairline fracture of the thumb.

    young goalkeepers

    With understudy Joe Hart also injured, Eriksson has revealed he is unwilling to begin the Barclays Premier League campaign with the inexperienced Kasper Schmeichel.

    "Unfortunately we had two goalkeepers get injured within two days," Eriksson told club website www.mcfc.co.uk. "It's not the best way to start the Premier League, starting with two very young goalkeepers. Unfortunately it's more or less a must that we bring another keeper in."

    City lost their latest pre-season friendly to Spanish side Valencia 1-0 on Saturday following a goal from David Silva.
