    England pay the price for World Cup failure with stars snubbed for prestigious Ballon d'Or prize

    England stars have been overlooked for the prestigious FIFA Ballon d'Or award with seven members of Spain's World Cup-winning squad shortlisted for the prize.

    Arsenal skipper Cesc Fabregas is one of three Barclays Premier League players on the 23-man list of nominees. The others are Chelsea's Ivory Coast striker Didier Drogba and Sunderland's Ghana frontman Asamoah Gyan.

    But England's disastrous World Cup campaign in South Africa sees the likes of Wayne Rooney, Steven Gerrard, Frank Lampard and John Terry all snubbed.

    Pain game: Wayne Rooney, Steven Gerrard and John Terry all missed out on the world player of the year shortlist

    Manchester United manager Sir Alex Ferguson will battle it out with Chelsea rival Carlo Ancelotti and Arsenal boss Arsenal Wenger for the world coach of the year.

    But Real Madrid coach Jose Mourinho, who celebrated a monumental treble in charge of Inter Milan last season, is the favourite for the award alongside World Cup winning Spain manager Vicente del Bosque.

    Andres Iniesta, who netted the winning goal in the Johannesburg final against Holland in July, is among the contenders along with his Barcelona team-mates Xavi, Carles Puyol and David Villa.

    Spain captain Iker Casillas and his Real Madrid colleague Xabi Alonso also make the shortlist.

    Barca's Argentina forward Lionel Messi and Brazil right-back Daniel Alves, Atletico Madrid forward Diego Forlan - who scored five goals at the World Cup and received the Golden Ball award - and Real Madrid pair Cristiano Ronaldo and Mesut Ozil make up 11 players nominated from La Liga.

    Eyes on the prize: Seven Spain World Cup winners are on the list including David Villa and Arsenal skipper Cesc Fabregas

    Inter Milan quartet Samuel Eto'o, Julio Cesar, Maicon and Wesley Sneijder are also included after winning an unprecedented Serie A, Coppa Italia and Champions League treble last term, with their former boss Jose Mourinho heading the candidates for the inaugural World Coach of the Year award.

    Messi is the reigning Ballon d'Or winner and FIFA World Player of the Year.
    The awards have now been merged after, after an agreement in July, so the best player in the world will now be recognised by a single accolade - the FIFA Ballon d'Or.

    The final decisions will be made by the captains and head coaches of the national teams as well as by international media representatives selected by France Football.
    The winners will be revealed in Zurich on January 10.

    World Player of the Year nominees

    Xabi Alonso (Spain), Daniel Alves (Brazil), Iker Casillas (Spain), Cristiano Ronaldo (Portugal), Didier Drogba (Ivory Coast), Samuel Eto'o (Cameroon), Cesc Fabregas (Spain), Diego Forlan (Uruguay), Asamoah Gyan (Ghana), Andres Iniesta (Spain), Julio Cesar (Brazil), Miroslav Klose (Germany), Philipp Lahm (Germany), Maicon (Brazil), Lionel Messi (Argentina), Thomas Muller (Germany), Mesut Ozil (Germany), Carles Puyol (Spain), Arjen Robben (Holland), Bastian Schweinsteiger (Germany), Wesley Sneijder (Holland), David Villa (Spain) and Xavi (Spain).

    Top of the pots: Real Madrid coach Jose Mourinho, who guided Inter Milan to the Champions league title last season, is nominated for boss of the year

    World Coach of the Year nominees

    Carlo Ancelotti (Chelsea), Vicente del Bosque (Spain), Sir Alex Ferguson (Manchester United), Pep Guardiola (FC Barcelona), Joachim Low (Germany), Jose Mourinho (Inter Milan/Real Madrid), Oscar Tabarez (Uruguay), Louis van Gaal (Bayern Munich), Bert van Marwijk (Holland) and Arsene Wenger (Arsenal).
