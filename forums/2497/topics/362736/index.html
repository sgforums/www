    Arsenal Striker Bendtner Hits Back At Ibrahimovic 'Junior Team' Claims

    The Dane thinks the Inter's striker's criticisms of the Gunners' transfer policy is way off the mark...

     

     

    Nicklas Bendtner has hit back at Inter forward Zlatan Ibrahimovic's claims to feel sympathy for Arsenal fans, because of the thrifty transfer policy of Gunners' manager Arsene Wenger, with the Frenchman preferring to keep the club on a sound financial footing by investing in youth rather than big-name stars.

    "Just look at Arsenal. They don’t buy the best players, which is sad, very sad for their supporters," the Swede told Aftonbladet. "They pay a lot of money for a season ticket just to see a junior team. That can't be fun."

    However, Bendtner has responded forcefully to those claims, asserting that his manager knows exactly what he is doing, and has only the best interests of the north London club at heart.

    “He is not correct. His words don’t make any sense at all," he affirmed to Danish newspaper B.T.: "We could have performed better this season but we did reach the semi-final in the FA Cup and the Champions League. Further than his Inter side.

    “We scored as many goals as Manchester United and that shows what we are capable of. "And in the Premier League there’s so much competition because it’s the best league in the world. It’s clearly better than Zlatan's league, Serie A."

    Arsenal's transfer targets, if any, for the summer are yet unclear, but they have been linked with players as diverse as Newcastle United striker Obafemi Martins and Bolton Wanderers' England defender Gary Cahill.
