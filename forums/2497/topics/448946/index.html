    Kenny Dalglish has called on the FA to examine Arsene Wenger’s comments after the Arsenal manager used Luis Suarez as an example in an outspoken attack on cheats.

    Wenger said Suarez ‘went overboard’ to earn Liverpool a penalty in a game at Anfield earlier this month after he collided with goalkeeper Wojciech Szczesny, even though television replays showed there was contact between the pair.

    Liverpool boss Dalglish was incredulous that Wenger claimed ‘nobody touched’ Suarez and queried why the Frenchman did not feel the need to raise the subject immediately after the match, which Arsenal won 2-1.

    Dalglish said: ‘He (Wenger) had a go at who? He never said that to me. He said it was a fantastic performance and they were very fortunate to win.
