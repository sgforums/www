    IAN HOLLOWAY cranked up the pressure on Fernando Torres - insisting he is struggling to deal with his goal drought.

    Liverpool striker Torres has failed to score in the last four matches. And Blackpool gaffer Ollie, whose team visit Anfield tomorrow, reckons his lack of goals is now playing on his mind.

    He said: "Torres is one of the world's top strikers. But when you get in one of these ruts it is hard for any player, especially one as talented as him, to cope with it.

    "He's been a sensation since he joined Liverpool and I'm in awe of him because as a centre-forward he has the lot. He has blinding pace, he can finish with his left or right foot and his head.

    "Like any cup of coffee, the cream will rise to the top - hopefully not on Sunday though. I hope his lack of form continues."

    Holloway accepts his team may look like lambs to the slaughter after being thrashed 6-0 at Arsenal and 4-0 at Chelsea already.

    But the chirpy Seasiders boss declared: "We look a winnable match - so we have to go and prove that isn't the case.

    "I want to come out of Anfield full of pride and have people say how difficult we made it for them."
