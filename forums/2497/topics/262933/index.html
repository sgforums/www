    Details Of Barcelona's Chivu Negotiations

    Roma have rejected Barcelona's first informal offer but the Catalans haven't give, and will land him one way or the other early next week.

    Barcelona's cash plus player offer did not meet with the immediate approval of the Roma bosses, sources in Italy are revealing.

    However, the differences are very minor, and both parties seem to be aware that an agreement is close, as has been apparent from comments coming from the Roma camp.

    "Barcelona are a very serious club," Prade had said on Friday afternoon. "We have talked about Chivu. Concrete offers have not been made yet. Next week we will meet again."

    The meeting will be for Barcelona to come in with an improved offer. By then, they will also have had time to understand whether players being offered would actually be willing to move to Rome.

    Roma would ideally like a straight cash deal worth 18-20 million euros, or 12 million euros along with Giuly and Belletti, while Barca are currently offering 13m along with just one of Belletti, Sylvinho and Giuly.

    The estimated difference is said to be worth less that 2 million euros, something Barcelona are in no mood to miss out over.
