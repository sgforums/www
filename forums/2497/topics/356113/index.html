    GIBBS JOINS GUNNERS` DOUBTS



    Chelsea 6/4, Arsenal 15/8, Draw 11/5

    Arsenal's defensive crisis has deepened ahead of the FA Cup semi-final against Chelsea on Saturday after Kieran Gibbs emerged as his latest injury concern. Right-back Bacary Sagna withdrew through illness on the eve of this week's Champions League clash against Villarreal, while Gibbs picked up a groin problem late in the victory.

    Gunners boss Arsene Wenger is already without Gael Clichy (back), William Gallas (knee) and Johan Djourou (knee) for Wembley. "We still have Sagna as a doubt for the weekend," said Wenger. "And after Villarreal we have Kieran Gibbs as a doubt too. He has a little groin problem. Clichy, Almunia and Djourou are still out."

    Goalkeeper Manuel Almunia will also miss the semi-final with his ankle injury, meaning Lukasz Fabianski will deputise, but who plays at left-back instead of Clichy and Gibbs is less straightforward.

    "There is the option to play Sagna in there if he has recovered," Wenger said on Arsenal TV Online. "The other is to play (Mikael) Silvestre there and put (Alex) Song at the back.

    "There are not many options but we'll deal with the situation no matter what happens. "I am happy to take 48 hours more so I can assess the [injury] situation. Also it means I can look back at how I rotated before and, as well, how we can keep the balance right considering the opponent.

    "We have to rotate a little bit because we play every three days now and only the squad can achieve what we want to achieve."

    Almunia and Djourou are a fortnight away from action, while Clichy needs two-and-a-half weeks of rest. It means they are all doubts for the first leg of the Champions League semi-final against Manchester United.

    The good news for Wenger is Andrey Arshavin and Eduardo are both available for Wembley.

    WOBBLY ... Petr Cech

    PETR CECH is an accident waiting to happen and could send Chelsea out of the FA Cup today. Arsenal legend Ray Parlour has told Gunners boss Arsene Wenger to target the wobbly keeper after watching Cech’s midweek meltdown. 

    Parlour, 36, was at Stamford Bridge on Tuesday as Cech dropped a series of clangers in the epic 4-4 draw with Liverpool. He then spilled the beans in former boss Wenger’s office 24 hours later after his old team cruised into the Champions League semi-finals. 

    Parlour said: “If I was Arsene I’d be telling Theo Walcott to charge down that wing and ping in as many crosses as he can. “Stick Emmanuel Adebayor in the box against Cech and something could give. 

    “On Tuesday, Cech wasn’t just wobbly for the first goal. Towards the end of the first half a cross came in, he flapped at it and the ball hit somebody on the back — it could have gone anywhere. 

    “Cech is nowhere near as commanding as he was last season. His confidence is shot to pieces. I saw mistakes creeping in against Bolton last weekend.” 

    Cech was caught wall-watching when Liverpool left-back Fabio Aurelio swung a free-kick into the net with the Blues keeper yards out of position on his line. 

    Parlour said: “Where he was standing was ridiculous. Even I wouldn’t have stood there and I’m not a keeper. 

    “Maybe the head injury Cech got a year or so ago is to blame. But he isn’t as commanding as he once was, he is not so brave. 

    “Perhaps the problem is lack of competition. Carlo Cudicini has gone and who challenges Cech now?”
