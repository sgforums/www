    Munich chief hits out at Arsenal boss Wenger for 'kidnapping' too many young players

     

    Under fire: Arsene Wenger has been criticised by Karl-Heinz Rummenigge

    He claims the signing of Cesc Fabregas when he was just 15 in 2003 as the perfect illustration of his point.

     

     

    Bayern Munich chairman Karl-Heinz Rummenigge has launched an unproved attack on Arsene Wenger and accused the Arsenal boss of signing too many young players which he describes as almost 'kidnapping'.

    Wenger has brought several youngsters to the North London and many of them have gone to become world class players. But Rummenigge believes managers should not be allowed to cherry pick the most promising players from around the world and has called for it to be stopped.

    He told Sport-Bild: 'One like Arsene Wenger signs hosts of players from France and elsewhere year-in year-out. 'We have to take care that this sort of child trafficking is stopped. This has taken on a different scale in the meantime; the word kidnapping is not too far off anymore.

    'Fabregas is really the best example for this. He signed from Barcelona at the age of 15 on a free transfer, just because he was about to sign a contract at the age of 16.'

    Wenger rejects Rummenigge criticism

    Arsene Wenger gave Karl-Heinz Rummenigge short shrift in response to criticism from him about his recruitment policy.

    Bayern Munich chairman Rummenigge hit out at the Arsenal manager in an interview with Sport Bild magazine for snapping up Cesc Fabregas from the Barcelona youth set-up when he was just 16.

    He said: "Wenger seems to take a host of players from France or wherever else each year and this has got to be prevented.

    "The best example has got to be Fabregas. He transferred to London from Barcelona as a 15-year-old for nothing because he could only be contractually bound at the age of 16."

    But Wenger fired back at Rummenigge, insisting they used the same tactics to sign Paraguay international Roque Santa Cruz, currently of Blackburn, who moved to Germany from South America when he was still 17.

    When asked about Rummenigge's comments after last night's 1-0 Champions League semi-final first-leg defeat to Manchester United, Wenger said: "It is an old speech. Look at Santa Cruz at Blackburn. Ask him what age he came to Bayern Munich. Then you have an answer for Karl-Heinz Rummenigge."
