    Players get list of what expected from Real Madrid

    Real Madrid players will soon receive instructions from management on what is expected from them this season.

    Marca says Real GM Jorge Valdano, on orders from president Florentino Perez, has drawn up a list of eight points for Real's players to follow:

    1. Be available to the fans of the club
    2. Respect the coach and managers
    3. Make great efforts and show dedication and sacrifice
    4. Always be on time
    5. Avoid risks to health
    6. Do not go out at night
    7. Present yourself properly, taking care of your appearance
    8. Collaborate with the club and the media.

    Karim Benzema’s Night Life Causing Real Madrid Concern - Report

    The new book of conduct will see a number of popular activities curtailed at the Bernabeu...

     

     

    Real Madrid are becoming increasingly concerned with Karim Benzema's enjoyment of the Spanish capital's night life and plan to hold talks with the player, according to a report.

    Only days after the club issued all players with a book on etiquette, revelations that the French forward has been seen out and about on a regular basis have left president Florentino Perez concerned.

    The manual includes eight points on how to behave and conduct yourself, and one of those is to not be seen out late at night on the town.

    Benzema joined Madrid from Olympique Lyonnais in the summer for around €40 million, and the club are keen to ensure that he does not go down the same path as Wesley Sneijder, according to El Mundo Deportivo.

    After moving to the Bernabeu last summer, Sneijder enjoyed the Madrid night life to such a degree that the club felt it affected his fitness and form, and was behind their desire to sell him after only a year.

    Sergio Ramos is another first-team regular to have been spoken to about his behaviour after featuring in various gossip magazines after being photographed at night.

    Royston Drenthe and Gonzalo Higuain have reined in their nights out after being told that it could affect the club adversely.
