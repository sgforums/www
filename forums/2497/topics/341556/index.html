    Sparky contradicts Diarra claims

    Manchester City manager Mark Hughes claims an offer was never made to Real Madrid new boy Lassana Diarra.

    Moneybags City were keen on the Portsmouth midfielder but decided not to compete for his signature when Real made their decisive move ahead of the transfer window.

    Hughes remains convinced that the club can still attract exciting signings despite their precarious league position and feels the Frenchman was wrong to state he opted to head for the Bernabeu in preference to The City of Manchester Stadium, even though there was more money on offer to stay in England.

    "That statement surprised me," said Hughes. "We were made aware of what level the deal was at and what it would take to bring the boy to the club.

    "In the end, we understood the figures and felt it wasn't right for us. We never made an offer for Lassana Diarra and we weren't given permission to speak to him so, for him to say we offered him more money, is completely wrong."

    Hughes is not concerned about City's current plight, as they find themselves in the bottom three despite their envious financial clout.

    "We've been linked with every player that's known to man I think," he added.

    "We have to accept that but, at the end of the day, we have a project here at the football club and at this time of its development, it's very exciting.

    "If players see an opportunity to come here, we can offer them something that will stimulate them and allow them to progress. I think, at this moment in time, people will be realistic about our development and look at where we want to go to from a footballing view.

    "That's exciting for anybody and the fact that this moment in time we're not in a position we'd like to be in, most people in football understand that's not where we anticipate being at the end of the year.

    "As a consequence, our ability to attract players won't be damaged."
