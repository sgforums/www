    Manchester United & Tottenham battle it out, for England's young potential goalkeeper, Ben Foster.

     

     

    Tottenham boss Harry Redknapp is seeking a permanent deal for Manchester United goalkeeper Ben Foster.

    The Sun says Redknapp is ready to offer Foster £20,000 a week to join his White Hart Lane revolution.

    The Tottenham manager desperately wants a keeper as back-up to unconvincing No1 Heurelho Gomes.

    And Manchester United’s Foster has been targeted as he is understood to be out of contract at Old Trafford this summer.

    United have tried to get Foster to sign a new deal by offering him around £15,000 a week but Spurs are prepared to top that.

    Foster signed for United for £1million from Stoke in 2005 but has spent most of that time out on loan.
