    Mourinho wants to go back to Chelsea and 'kill them' when he gets a new club

    Jose Mourinho has warned Chelsea he will be out to 'kill' them when he takes over the reins at a new club.

    Â 

    The former Blues boss reiterated his love for the club he managed for more than three years before his sensational departure in September. But he is desperate to meet them in Champions League combat next season and knock them out of Europe.

    "I hope to play them next season in the Champions League," said the Portuguese. "If I play them in the Champions League, I want to go there and kill them - that's my message."

    Mourinho was replaced by Avram Grant, who had originally been brought to Stamford Bridge as director of football. He refused to express an opinion on his successor, saying: "I feel nothing for him, I feel everything for the club, but not for him personally.

    "I still feel Chelsea is a part of me, I'll have Chelsea in my heart forever. I left and for five months you couldn't get a bad word from me in relation to the club and you cannot do it in the future too."
