    Capello wants Scholes for Euro 2012

     

    Capello clearly believes the return of Scholes will boost his cause.




     

     

    Fabio Capello will make a last-ditch bid this week to lure Paul Scholes out of England retirement. The England coach failed in his attempt to convince the 35-year-old Manchester United midfielder to end his exile before this summer’s World Cup finals.

    Scholes admits he regrets rejecting that approach. Now, as Capello prepares for England’s qualifying campaign for the Euro 2012 finals, the coach is ready to knock again on the Manchester United midfielder’s door.

    While Capello has deemed David Beckham to be yesterday’s man, he sees Scholes – who is six months older than Becks – as a man for the future.

    Scholes has been in ­impressive form for United, producing man-of-the-match performances against ­Newcastle last week and in the Community Shield victory over Chelsea at ­Wembley.

    England face ­crucial back-to-back ­European Championship qualifiers at home to ­Bulgaria on September 3 and away to Switzerland four days ­later.

    Indifferent results will almost certainly lead to ­increasing calls for Capello to stand down from his £6million-a-year position.

    As Sunday Mirror Sport revealed last week, the opening three fixtures will determine whether or not the Italian coach remains in the post or parts company with the FA.

    Capello clearly believes the return of Scholes will boost his cause.
