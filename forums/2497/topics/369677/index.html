    Arsenal's Clichy slams Adebayor over Man City money grab

    Gael Clichy has slammed former Arsenal teammate Emmanuel Adebayor over his move to Manchester City.

    The French fullback told The Sun: "I really believe if you are a player who thinks only about money then you could end up at Manchester City.

    "You have to think if you want to play for a big club and have your image or if you want to play for a good club and earn big money.

    "When you ask someone to move for something like £300,000 a week it is just crazy."

    Eastlands boss Mark Hughes has spent £80m this summer in a bid to gatecrash the Big Four.

    Clichy declared: "I've been here six years and this is not the first summer where a club have spent big money to get big players.

    "But it does not always mean a team will do well.

    "Sometimes when you have players coming in like this it can take six to seven months for them to adjust."

    Clichy denies Ade blast

    Gunners stopper unhappy with tabloid reporting

    Arsenal full-back Gael Clichy has denied saying former team-mate Emmanuel Adebayor joined Manchester City for money.

    The Gunners defender hinted over the weekend that the primary reason any player would sign for Mark Hughes' team is for a lucrative pay packet.

    Clichy is unhappy the way his interview has been reported, stating he is not the type of player to judge or single out a fellow professional.

    The 23-year-old says does he not know the reasons behind Adebayor's Eastlands switch and now wants to draw a line under the matter.

    Really bad

    "I felt really bad about this," he told the club's official website. "I am not a player who would talk like this; say a name and talk this way about a friend.

    "If you read the article you will see that I am not saying any names - Ade or anyone else. That is not the way to do it. If I need to say something I will go out and say it myself.

    "Saying big names in a big article like this when I did not say anything, I think it is wrong. I just wanted to clarify this.

    "Only Ade knows why he went and we have to live with this. He was a good player for us but now he is gone and I don't want to talk about him anymore."
