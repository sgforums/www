    Sunderland moved to the top of the Coca-Cola Championship for the first time this season with a come-from-behind 2-1 win at Southampton on Monday evening. After Marek Saganowski had put the hosts ahead at St Mary's on 67 minutes, man of the match Carlos Edwards soon levelled. However, Sunderland left it late to secure the points, substitiute Grant Leadbitter netted a spectacular winner with just three minutes to play.

    Derby had started the day at the top but dropped two points as they were held 1-1 by Coventry at Pride Park. It could have been worse for the Rams after Leon McKenzie struck for the visitors after 73 minutes but parity was restored just six minutes later. Matt Oakley was the scorer for County when he converted after good work from James McEveley and Steve Howard.

    Birmingham's promotion bid also suffered a setback as they were beaten 1-0 by strugglers Barnsley. Victory for the Oakwell side lifted them out of the relegation zone - at the expense of Leeds - and Daniel Nardiello is the man they have to thank after he grabbed the game's only goal in the 52nd minute. A stormy affair ended with Blues' Neil Danns and Barnsley's Bobby. Hassell sent off by referee Mike Russell for violent conduct after the final whistle.

    Leeds, meanwhile, continue to go through the whole spectrum of emotions, getting sucked back into the bottom three following a dramatic 2-1 loss to Colchester.

    COLCHESTER 2-1 LEEDS

    DENNIS WISE tore into his defenders after two late goals pushed Leeds back into the Championship drop zone.

    When Eddie Lewis fired them in front early in the second half, it seemed Leeds were on course to build on the win on Saturday that had lifted them out of the bottom three for the first time since November.

    But slack defending at Layer Road allowed first Chris Iwelumo and then Jamie Cureton to turn the game on its head on a day when results elsewhere went against the Yorkshire side.

    "We were looking very comfortable but then conceded two crap goals from set plays," fumed Elland Road boss Wise. "We had a mad 10 minutes when we didn't defend properly and it drives you potty.

    "You ask them to do something and they don't do it. It makes me realise why we lose games when they don't do these things. Some of them don't take it on board enough, unfortunately.

    You keep telling them about these small details that can be so important but sometimes they just think you're mad and don't do them.

    "So this is why we'll get back on the field and do them again. And I'll drive them potty until they get it right." This was Leeds' first visit to Colchester since their shock FA Cup defeat in 1971, when Colchester were in the old Fourth Division.

    The victory means Colchester must still be regarded as contenders to gatecrash the play-offs, with Leeds the 14th team to have been beaten at this tiny ground this season. Cureton said: "Yes, there's an outside chance we can make it and it would be a massive bonus if we did, but it's not something we talk about."
