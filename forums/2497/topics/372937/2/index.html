    Michael Owen strike caps return to form for United

    Wigan Athletic 0 Manchester United 5

     

     

     

     

     

     

    Michael Owen scores his first goal for Manchester United as they bounce back from their defeat at Burnley to thump Wigan with a flurry of late goals. 

    .

    .

    WITH one sweet swish of his left boot and delicate application of instep to orb, Michael Owen was back. He preened himself before his new club’s supporters and they sang the chorus they had been storing since Sir Alex Ferguson signed him: “Are You Watching Merseyside?” Manchester United were also back, finding true form for the first time this season.

    How inevitable it happened at Wigan. They have faced Ferguson’s team 10 times since reaching the top flight and lost each time with a cumulative goal difference of -26. If this was cricket, they would be known as United’s rabbit.

    In fairness, Wigan did not do their Flopsy Bobtail act until 11 minutes into the second half, when Wayne Rooney scored his 100th goal for United with a sharp and decisive header, to jolt his team out of their early-campaign lethargy and break his opponents’ resolve. Until then, Roberto Martinez’s men were competing, trading chances with the champions and pressing a United midfield and forward line which — except for Rooney and the industrious Darren Fletcher — seemed worryingly containable.

    Rooney’s goal woke Dimitar Berbatov, who scored a fine one of his own and helped Rooney notch a second before Owen struck. In stoppage time Nani exposed Chris Kirkland’s poor positioning with a crafty free kick and suddenly Wigan faced their record Premier League defeat.

    While it remained 0-0, doubts created by Wednesday’s defeat at Burnley still hung over United. “You always look for a response,” said Ferguson.

    “We’re not going to kill ourselves because of it but we’re going to take reminders from it. It shouldn’t have happened but it did happen and the players had to do something about it.” Owen’s strike pleased him particularly. The striker, introduced for Rooney, tailored his run expertly to the needs of Nani who required time to manoeuvre into a position from which he could deliver a telling ball, something the Portuguese, in his most mature United performance to date, did with aplomb. The pass found Owen in a pocket of space no bigger than a picnic-rug, with Kirkland fast advancing. His dinked finish was instant, sending the ball home via the far post.

    “Fantastic goal, great run, the movement was excellent,” Ferguson slavered. “Michael has been England’s best goalscorer over the last decade and he proved that today. It will do him the world of good.” Rooney — marvellously for England — is adopting his colleague’s predatory mien. Goal No 100 came via a cross by Antonio Valencia. Rooney’s next strike went in off Mario Melchiot’s shins after Berbatov found him in the box.

    Berbatov looked typically enervated before scoring and energised thereafter. In a give-and-go with Paul Scholes he reached Scholes’ chipped pass, flicked it over Kirkland and hooked home the bouncing ball. “Their first goal affected us too much psychologically,” said Martinez. “It was two different games.”
