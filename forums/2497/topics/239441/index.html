    PREVIEW-Soccer-Man Utd need Anfield win to open gap of 12 points

    V S

    LONDON, March 1 - Manchester United seek to intensify the pressure on Premier League title rivals Chelsea, who kickoff more than four hours later, by opening up a 12-point lead with victory at Liverpool on Saturday.

    United, face a tough task at the home of their bitter rivals. Third-placed Liverpool, have not conceded a goal at Anfield in nine league outings and are unbeaten at home this season.

    United, though, can draw encouragement from an outstanding record on the road, having won 10 and drawn two of their 14 away games in the league this season. It is likely to be a classic example of an immoveable object meeting an irresistible force.

    Liverpool suffered two heavy home defeats by Arsenal, conceding a total of nine goals, in early January but both came in cup fixtures where manager Rafa Benitez selected weakened teams.

    Benitez, who will try to defend a 2-1 lead at home to Barcelona in their Champions League first knockout round second leg on Tuesday, faces a delicate selection dilemma. By resting players against United he risks a repeat of the Arsenal defeats and the end of his team's proud home league record.

    European Champions League

    But Benitez also knows he will need a full-strength side to eliminate European champions Barcelona three days later. United, fresh from securing their place in the FA Cup quarter-finals with Tuesday's replay win at Reading, also have a Champions League clash to prepare for.

    Alex Ferguson's men hold a 1-0 lead going into their home game with French club Lille. Liverpool kick off against United at 8.45, four and a half hours before Chelsea visit Portsmouth.

    If Liverpool win at lunch time, a victory for Chelsea would cut United's advantage to six points, with the Londoners having a game in hand. Chelsea also entertain United at Stamford Bridge on April 15.

    Elsewhere, fourth-placed Arsenal host Reading while third-bottom Charlton Athletic travel to bottom club Watford and Aston Villa visit Fulham. Wigan Athletic also meet Manchester City, Newcastle United face Middlesbrough and Everton play Sheffield United.

    On Sunday, West Ham United entertain in-form Tottenham Hotspur and Blackburn Rovers travel to Bolton Wanderers.

    Kop in for a shock - Le Tissier

    This is always one of the biggest games of the season, but this is probably as big as it it gets. If United go to Anfield and get beat then it really does throw the title race open and it gives Chelsea the belief they can catch them. The belief that they can close the gap if they win the meeting between the two at Stamford Bridge. If United go to Anfield and win, then it a massive step towards regaining that Premiership crown.

    A lot of people have been saying the Fulham game last week, when they somehow scraped a 2-1 win, will be the moment we look back on and say 'that's where they won the title' - but I disagree. Most people, myself included, expected them to go to Craven Cottage and win, as I am sure Jose Mourinho did. But Liverpool at Anfield is a different matter. People will be expecting them to drop points here, so if they can go there and win, no-one will remember Fulham.

    They are in such good form right now I just can't see them slipping up, although if you offered him the draw right now I am sure Sir Alex Ferguson would snap your hand off! That is not only down to the situation at the top of the table, but the terrific form Liverpool are in at home. They haven't lost there for something like 18 months and you've got to say there haven't been many draws in there either. It's been a phenomenal run.

    That sort of form should be enough to be pushing United and Chelsea, but for my mind, Rafa Benitez is now counting the cost of all that early-season tinkering. Steven Gerrard has said they dropped too many 'silly points' and I said it at the time. Early on is when you need those points in the bag, because one thing you can't do in the Premier League is give the others a head start.

    Their home form was never really an issue and never really where Rafa made those strange selection decisions - although I just wonder if he will go for Peter Crouch here, if he's recovered from that kick in the face. I know Dirk Kuyt and Craig Bellamy seem to be the pair he favours but with the Barcelona game coming up in midweek, I wouldn't be surprised to see him rest one of those. Maybe Crouch can unsettle Rio Ferdinand and Nemanja Vidic.

    Because of those lost points, the Barca game is bigger than this for Liverpool - even though the fans might disagree. I can't see Rafa, particularly after what happened against Arsenal in the FA Cup, resting too many because if this one goes badly wrong the fans will not let him forget it. But he can't afford to lose anyone for Tuesday night.

    I know United have a slender lead against Lille, but I can't see that causing them any problems and I can't really see Fergie resting anyone here. Even if they pick a couple of injuries I can see them winning their second leg quite comfortable. That means Gary Neville, Michael Carrick, Paul Scholes, Cristiano Ronaldo, Ryan Giggs, Henrik Larsson and Wayne Rooney back at Anfield - and it means United taking another massive step towards the title.

    LE TISS PREDICTS
    AWAY WIN - This, along with the game at Chelsea, will be the ones United have been really looking at in their run-in and because of that I can't see them being beaten. Anfield will be heaving and Liverpool at home are pretty formidable right now, but United have so many experienced players - and fresh ones at that - that if any side in the Premiership can handle that, it is them. I also think they are decent enough to keep Liverpool at bay as well, so I fancy a 2-0 win.

    MATT'S MAIN MAN
    WAYNE ROONEY - He is due a big performance and I wouldn't be surprised if he produces it on his return to Merseyside. He might have been a little quiet of late, but he is still only a young lad and has every right to dip in form - and he will keep doing so until well into his mid-20's. But even when he's quiet he has still been showing those flashes that make him so special and I can see him hitting top form over the next few weeks.
