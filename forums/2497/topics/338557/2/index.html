    PHIL SCOLARI has demanded an apology from ref Mike Dean — after claiming the official cost his side victory against Arsenal. The Chelsea boss blamed Dean and his assistant Simon Beck for failing to call Robin Van Persie offside before Arsenal’s equaliser. Van Persie then scored a winner three minutes later to condemn Chelsea to their second home defeat of the season.

    Big Phil fumed: “Never before at Chelsea have I said anything about the referee. “But today is different. I don’t want the referee to do something different, only for him to look at the TV, see he was wrong and say sorry.

    “Tell the assessor to ask the referee to do both sides, not one side. “They scored a goal that was one metre ahead of our player, then the same linesman gave a decision against Kalou that was never offside.

    “They made a mistake. They’re not bad, I understand that. I don’t want three points, I just want ‘sorry’ because they made a mistake. “In the first half they make the same mistake again when Deco passed to Kalou who was onside and was called offside.

    “In the second half, another big mistake against us. It’s difficult to play and, after this, my team lost their concentration and didn’t play well. “I want to hear some words from the referee and for him to say that it was offside.

    “Offside killed my team. But, if it’s possible to understand, if we had won we would now be 13 points ahead of them and the title would be finished for them.”

    Arsenal’s shock victory means a win for Liverpool against West Ham at Anfield this evening will put Rafa Benitez’s side three points clear at the top of the Premier League.

    Gunners boss Arsene Wenger claimed the result keeps his side in the title hunt and praised former skipper William Gallas for the way he has responded to the problems of the last two weeks.

    Wenger said: “William was outstanding today, absolutely amazing. He came out of that period with a lot of credit because his commitment is fantastic.

    “I believe the win gives us the belief that we can come back.What kind of impact it can have on Chelsea, I do not know.”

     

    FURIOUS ... Phil Scolari
