    Michael Ballack is not concerned by the fact Chelsea trail Manchester United by six points with 11 games remaining in the Premiership.

    United have held sway for much of the season and the gap has remained at six points since the turn of the year.

    Chelsea's resolve has been tested by a crippling injury crisis in recent months, but they have stuck to their task to hold the deficit at six points.

    Inspirational captain John Terry handed Chelsea a boost recently when returning to action and with the likes of Arjen Robben nearing full fitness, Ballack feels Chelsea are ready to mount a strong push for the title.

    "We are six points behind Man U, but it is not a drama," the Germany midfielder said on Sky Sports News.

    "We had a little bit of a problem with injuries and so on, but if our players come back we will have a strong team and I feel we have good chances of winning again."
