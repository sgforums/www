    Benitez vows to knock United off their perch with Liverpool ready to end years in the title wilderness

     

     

     

    Rafa Benitez has made it his mission to end Manchester United's title domination after sealing his Liverpool future. The Anfield boss signed a new five-year contract last night to end months of speculation over whether he would quit.

    Liverpool have gone 19 years without winning the league and have watched arch rivals United rule during that period. But Benitez, whose new deal is worth £5million per season, is confident that they will eventually triumph.

    'Clearly we have to improve everything still if we want to be contenders every year,' said the Spaniard. 'The target is to improve the squad and work in different ways to make sure we stay together. The target is to improve every year and I think now it will become easier to win trophies.

    'I have a lot of confidence because I have signed a deal like this. I want to win the title and I think we can do it. The main thing now is to work harder and try to do the right things.
     
    'I am really excited at having the opportunity to improve and win things. When you have time it's easier. We have five years' experience so we can start improving things straight away.'

     

    Benitez has expressed his relief at finally signing the deal after his future was plunged into doubt during negotiations with co-owners George Gillett and Tom Hicks.

    'They are pleased with the job we're doing,' he added. 'Everyone always wants to win more, but clearly they can see the progression. They can see the improvement of the squad, so I think they are really pleased - hopefully they'll be pleased at the end of the deal.

    'We've been talking about things for a long time, so it's good that it's been finalised and we can finish with the talks. Now we can start working with everything sorted.

    'This is important because the players want to know who the manager will be. The fans do also. But decisions in terms of players - it's really important to know who the manager will be, then you can start working.'

    Benitez also offered an insight into how happy he is with his family on Merseyside.
    'My daughters and my wife are really settled down here and I think they, particularly the little one, are more English than Spanish,' he said.
