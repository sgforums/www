Tykes buzzing after cup win

  • turbo_drift
    9 Mar 08, 14:08

    Brian Howard and Kayode Odejayi have expressed their delight at helping Barnsley reach the FA Cup semi-finals.

     

    Striker Odejayi grabbed the only goal in the Tykes' shock 1-0 win over Chelsea in the quarter-finals, while Howard was the hero in the previous round with the winner in a 2-1 win away at Liverpool.

     

    They admit that their progression to the last four of the competition is nothing short of remarkable and that the whole squad feel that anything is now possible.

     

    Unbelievable

     

    "The lads have put in an unbelievable performance today (Saturday)," Howard told Sky Sports News.

     

    "Odejayi has been outstanding and (Istvan) Ferenczi up front against two of the best centre-halves in the world and all the lads have put in 100 per cent effort and we're in the semi-finals of the cup.

     

    "It probably won't sink in until Monday morning or Tuesday night when we're back to the bread and butter of the league against Ipswich, but we will savour the moment of this for as long as we can.

     

    "I don't think they'll be a better chance for a Championship side to go on and win it, and every side that is left in it has got to fancy themselves.

     

    "Obviously we've knocked out Liverpool at Anfield and Chelsea have come here, the holders, and we've knocked them out so there's no reason why we can't go on and win the thing."

     

    Deserved

     

    Odejayi also admitted that after knocking out two of the best sides in the country there is no limit to what the Tykes could achieve.

     

    "I'm still finding it hard to believed," he told Sky Sports News.

     

    "Everyone around the stadium is buzzing and we all deserve it for the shift we put in on the pitch.

     

    "It's what dreams are made of. I'm just so pleased for the management staff, the fans, the boys on the pitch and all the way down to the kit man.

     

    "Not many of us have been to Wembley, and this is the new Wembley, so it's a nice touch that we get to go there in the semi-finals.

     

    "With Chelsea, Liverpool and Manchester United not around it the latter stages it's there for anyone now."

    Grant - Barnsley deserved win

    Chelsea manager Avram Grant admitted his side were second best as they suffered a huge giant-killing at the hands of Barnsley in their FA Cup quarter-final at Oakwell.

     

    Kayode Odejayi scored the only goal of the game just after the hour to give Barnsley the most unlikeliest of wins - although Chelsea barely threatened at the other end.

     

    "We started the game well then we conceded the goal and we couldn't score," he told the website.

     

    "We didn't play well, especially in the first half, and all the credit to Barnsley. They won against Liverpool, they won against us. All credit to them and I congratulate them.

     

    "I am very, very disappointed and sad about this. It's an important competition. We had a chance to do something in this competition and we didn't do it so now we need to look forward for the league and Champions League."
