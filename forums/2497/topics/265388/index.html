    Mijatovic In Rome To Seal Chivu Deal

    Real Madrid sporting director Pedja Mijatovic has travelled to Rome with the clear objective of signing Christian Chivu, come what may.

    The Merengue bosses have already tabled a 16 million euro offer, and are ready to go up to 17m if need be. Roma have maintained that an 18 million bid will seal the deal.

    Barcelona and Inter both have offers totalling 18m, but the deal would include players as well. Madrid are ready to offer cash.

    AS are reporting that Madrid have already agreed a 4.5 million a year salary with Chivu's agent Gigi Becali, who, rather interestingly, had travelled to Barcelona on Monday.

    Marca insist that Mijatovic could even throw one of the three Cs - Cassano, Cicinho and Cannavaro - into the deal in order to land Chivu.

    Barcelona based Sport add that Fifa agent Ernesto Bronzetti - who often negotiates on behalf of Madrid in Italy, and who had earlier insisted that Chivu wants to move to Barcelona - also travelled with Mijatovic.

    The situation, as it stands, is that Chivu has now agreed terms with all the three clubs (all said to be around 4 million euros per season), and it is Roma who must choose the club they want to sell to.

    Barcelona's final offer is 14 million euros plus Ludovic Giuly, Inter are offering 15 million and half of Obinna's registration, while Madrid are at 17 million, but also prepared to throw players into the deal if the cash sum is lower.

    Stay tuned as this complicated saga concludes. A final decision is expected today!

    Roma Accept Real Madrid's Chivu Bid

    AS Roma have revealed through that they have decided to accept Real Madrid's offer for the Romanian international defender.

    The Giallorossi bosses have decided to go for an 18 million euro offer from Los Merengues, after Barcelona and Inter Milan refused to offer more than 15 million in cash.

    Read a statement: "AS Roma have accepted the offer presented by Real Madrid in order to acquire the registration of Christian Chivu.

    "Given Inter's decision to end their transfer negotiations for Chivu, the completion of his transfer to Real Madrid will now depend on the player's will."

    The announcement is not a surprise, as Fifa agent Ernesto Bronzetti had revealed as much yesterday, but it is the first in terms of official confirmation, and does bring this long running saga close to an end.

    As Goal.com had reported this morning, the player wants a 5 million annual salary, while Real Madrid are willing to offer 4.5. Talks are in progress at the time of writing this article.
