    BITING BACK ... City ace Robinho

     

     

    Saturday, 5 December 2009      

     
    Channel 27:  "Live"
     
     
    Manchester City - Chelsea   - 1:30 am
     

    ROBINHO has come up with a three-point plan to scupper Chelsea's charge at the top of the table at Eastlands tonight. Manchester City's fit-again £32.5million samba star says: "We must drown their midfield, open the scoring - and stop Didier Drogba."

    The Brazilian admits that Carlo Ancelotti's Blues are the top side in England at the moment - but they are not unstoppable.

    Robinho, 25, said: "Chelsea are the best team in the Premier League at the moment because Manchester United are not playing with the same rhythm as they did at the end of last season.

    "It is not an easy game for us but given the spirit in our squad this week, I believe the Blues will suffer in Manchester. "It's the basics for us. We must score goals - without them we don't win matches.

    "Drogba is one of the best strikers in the world and Nicolas Anelka is having some big moments too. We are working on a system to stop them - but our defence must avoid any costly errors.

    "From my point of view, John Terry and Ricardo Carvalho are the best central defenders in Europe but they do have their little weak points and I want to exploit them."

    Robinho, 25, has been a frustrated spectator while City's own title hopes have stuttered on a run of seven successive Premier League draws, leaving them a massive 14 points behind today's visitors.

    The Brazil ace - whose very first City strike came against Chelsea last season - is only just back after his enforced lay-off following a stress fracture in an ankle he suffered while playing for Brazil in early September.

    He is confident Mark Hughes' side can rip up the form book at Eastlands today and still challenge for a top-four finish at the end of the season to book a ticket to next season's Champions League.

    And Robinho, who made his comeback as a sub in last weekend's 1-1 draw with Hull, swept aside speculation linking him with a January move to European champions Barcelona by insisting he has unfinished business at Eastlands.

     

    He said: "City will play in Europe but the Champions League is the main challenge and it's still possible. "I still dream that City will see the very best of me, the way I played for Real Madrid. I don't want to fail in this adventure.

    "I came to England to triumph and to demonstrate my quality. I still think this is possible so I do not want to speak about my future. "I have a deal with City and now my mission is to work and help the club. "I have spoken with manager Mark Hughes and he has no problems with me - he's happy that I have returned after injury."

    City fans will be pleased to hear Robinho's assurance that he is committed to the Eastlands cause - especially after World Cup legend Franz Beckenbaeur's remarks that the Brazilian 'can play in a circus but he's not a team player'.

    They certainly need him firing on all cylinders again. Robinho has 14 goals in 33 starts for City since his shock arrival from Spanish giants Real 16 months ago.

    He has yet to get off the mark this season although Hughes' men are buzzing after Wednesday's 3-0 Carling Cup triumph over Arsenal earned the club's first appearance in a cup semi-final since 1981.

    That victory took some of the pressure off the Eastlands boss, although Hughes is under no illusions about the size of the task facing his side tonight.

    The Welshman said: "If Chelsea continue to play in the same vein absolutely they will win the title. "They've been the standout team up to this point - they are close to being the best team in Europe.

    "That's the size of our challenge. We're not only going up against the best teams in the Premier League, but some of them are the best in Europe."
