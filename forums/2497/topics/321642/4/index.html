    Guus: We did the perfect job

     

    GUUS HIDDINK saw Russia KO his countrymen and crowed: We outplayed them in every way. Dmitri Torbinsky and Andrei Arshavin struck late in extra time to give Dutchman Hiddink - a former Holland boss - a Euro 2008 semi-final spot.

     

    Ruud van Nistelrooy had barely disrupted the Russians' superiority when his 86th minute sidefoot cancelled out Roman Pavlyuchenko's opener. And Russia chief Hiddink, 61, said after the 3-1 win: "Usually the Dutch are a team who one cannot outdo tactically, technically or physically, but we did on all three counts.

     

    "Of course we realised we couldn't give away too many free-kicks to them but as you get tired you tend to commit more fouls. "However, my players responded really well and instead of relying on the counter-attack they pressed for the winning goal."

     

    Star man Arshavin said: "The coach said he expected the Dutch would play very aggressively against us. "But it turned out that they ran out of breath before we did. At the end the better Dutchman - our trainer - won.

    Team-mate Yuri Zhirkov also saluted his boss - the man dubbed a "coaching wizard" by the Russian media. Zhirkov said: "What else can you call him, given that he has taken so many teams to World Cups and European Championships."

    Hiddink, meanwhile, insisted that losing manager Marco Van Basten, 43, will turn into a successful Holland chief. He said: "When I was Dutch team manager, around 1995, it was felt in the federation it was a pity if top players disappeared from the game. "We felt we could take advantage of some world-class players like Marco.

     

    "I approached him at the end of his career, but he said he wasn't sure it was for him and didn't feel he'd make a good coach. "But that changed later on and he has done a very good job in a very short period of time."
