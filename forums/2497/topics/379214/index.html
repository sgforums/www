    England boss Capello threw steel tray after seeing player on phone

    England boss Fabio Capello lost his rag with one Premiership star last week.

    The News of the World says Capello reacted furiously when he spotted one player texting on his mobile phone during a meal while the team were in the Ukraine.

    The Italian was so angry at the breach of one of his strictest rules, he picked up a metal serving platter and launched it at a wall.

    He then turned on his squad and made it clear he would not tolerate any further breaches of discipline.

    An agent of one of the players said: "The players eat at two tables with the coaches on another. During dinner, Capello saw one of the players secretly texting with his phone beneath the table. Before anybody really knew what was happening, Capello stood up and threw one of the trays across the room and it hit the opposite wall.

    "He then shouted at the players that all he demanded was one hour of their time during meals without any distractions, especially people using mobile phones. It was a real shock for the players to see Capello like this. They'd seen him angry before but he'd never reacted quite as violently.

    "They spent the rest of the meal in absolute silence and everybody was scared to catch his eye. If he wanted to remind them who was in charge, it worked."

    REVEALED: Emile Heskey was England star slammed by Fabio Capello for texting at dinner.

     

    Handshake: But Fabio Capello wasn't so friendly with Emile Heskey when the striker was caught texting on his phone at the dinner table

     

     

    Emile Heskey was the player who incurred the wrath of Fabio Capello for a breach of the rules over mobile phones.

    When he became England manager, Capello banned his players from bringing their mobiles into team meals. And after spotting the Aston Villa striker secretly sending a text under the table during the recent trip to Ukraine, he exploded with rage.

    The Italian had already caught the attention of his players by accidently dropping his metal food tray - it is said to have made an almighty crash - but it was when he turned on Heskey that he reduced the entire room to a stunned silence.

    Capello reminded the players of their obligation not just to him but to each other and the need to follow the strict protocol that includes leaving their phones in their rooms for team meals.

    He said it was not too much to ask to give an hour of their time without interruptions from outside.

    Heskey still earned selection alongside Wayne Rooney against Ukraine but the incident served as a reminder to the players that Capello's stance has not softened - even after their qualification for next summer's World Cup in South Africa.

    He still demands that the players always leave the dining room as a squad, rather than individually, and also orders them to rest in their rooms at certain times.

    The diet is strictly controlled, too, although some players have been known to send for secret supplies of their favourite snacks.

    It is an approach that has won Capello many admirers, given the state of the squad he inherited after England's failure to qualify for Euro 2008.

    He also continues to command the respect of the players - even if there is the occasional breach of the rules.
