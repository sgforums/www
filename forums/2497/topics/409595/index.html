    Robin van Persie will be out for at least a few weeks, according to the Dutch FA. Photograph: Michael Mayhew/Sportsphoto

     

     

    The Arsenal striker Robin van Persie will be out of action for several weeks after hurting his left ankle in Saturday's Premier League match at Blackburn Rovers.

    The Dutch Football Association said the forward will miss Holland's European Championship qualifiers against San Marino and Finland because he is likely to be out of action for "at least a few weeks".

    Van Persie set up Arsenal's opening goal before being replaced in the 35th minute of the 2-1 win at Ewood Park. He missed five months of last season after injuring ankle ligaments in November while on international duty.

    Holland have recalled the Hamburg striker Ruud van Nistelrooy, 34, after a two-year absence.

    Walcott was taken to a Swiss hospital for scans on his right ankle, which he damaged in the build-up to Wayne Rooney's 10th-minute opener.

    But X-rays showed no breaks and just bad bruising.

    England boss Fabio Capello spoke to Walcott over the phone moments after the 3-1 win.
