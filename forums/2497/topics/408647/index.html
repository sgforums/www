[YOG] Singapore beats Montenegro 3-2 in a thrilling game

  • Y_Shun
    20 Aug 10, 13:17

    Singapore defeated 10-man Montenegro 3-2 in a thrilling game to qualify for the semi-final of the Youth Olympic Games (YOG) Boys’ under-15 football tournament.

    The Cubs surprised the 5,850 roaring fans barely three minutes into the game when striker Suhaimi Muhaimin scored after he slotted the ball past the onrushing Montenegro goalkeeper Marko Kordic.

    But Montenegro struck back six minutes later when their skipper Nebojsa Kosovic delivered a harmless looking free-kick into the Singapore penalty area which fooled Cubs custodian Fashah Rosedin and found its way into goal.

    Montenegro then took the lead in the 22nd minute from Kosovic’s penalty kick after defender Illyas Lee was adjudged to have raised his boot too high when challenging Aleksandar Boljevic for the ball.

    But Singapore bounced back to score the equaliser from skipper Jeffrey Lightfoot’s towering header minutes before half-time.



    The turning point came in the 63rd minute when Montenegro midfielder Nickola Jovanovic was sent off. Referee Wilmar Roldan showed him the red card after he punched Mazlan Ammirul while they were challenging for the ball.

    With a draw looking imminent, Cubs midfielder Brandon Koh brought the partisan crowd to its feet when he scored the winning goal in the 75th minute, courtesy of a careless mistake from Montenegro defender Danilo Sarkic.
