Nasri's Marseille Career Over?

    Marseille maestro Samir Nasri has made a strong suggestion that he will be leaving the club this summer, saying today's game against Racing Strasbourg may be his last for the club.

    Nasri scored the winning goal against minnows Racing Strasbourg today, on the final day of the season, as l'OM finished a comfortable third, qualifying for next season's Champions League.

    Dubbed by many as the new Zidane, Nasri has been linked with a move away from the Velodrome for a few years now, but recently signed a contract extension with the club and looked likely to stay.

    Indeed news of his contract extension broke just 10 days ago, with the deal set to see him stay at the club until 2012. However, with contracts becoming increasingly insignificant in modern football, it looks as if Nasri, who has been linked with the likes of Real Madrid and Juventus over the past couple of years, will be leaving the club.

    When asked about his goal in today's game, he said: “It is very important because it is perhaps my last match at the Stade Velodrome.”

    This came after L'Equipe reported that the playmaker is one of Arsene Wenger's transfer targets for the summer.
