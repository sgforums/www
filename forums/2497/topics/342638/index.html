    Rafa Benitez is about to add another new teenage talent to his bulging group of young prospects, with Icelander Victor Palsson set to move to Anfield.

    Meanwhile, Sebastien Leto could also be on his way back to Merseyside in the near future after he refused to commit his long-term future to Olympiacos.

    Palsson, who is a strong midfield player, originally had a trial at Liverpool’s Melwood training ground in October.

    He has recently been promoted to Danish side AGF’s first team, but now they are braced for his departure, subject to medical.

    Leto meanwhile, who has been carving out a reputation on loan at Olympiacos this season, appears keen to give it one more shot at Anfield once his loan spell ends.

    The 22-year-old winger signed a two-year arrangement with the Greek club, but after scoring the goal of the week in a recent encounter with Thrasyvoulous, Leto would only comment “we’ll wait until June to see what happens” when asked about his future.
