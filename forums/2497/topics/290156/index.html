    The extraordinary globalisation of the Premier League could make English football the first world sport to earn more money from their supporters abroad than at home. An estimated worldwide audience of one billion watched Manchester United play Arsenal on Saturday and, if growth rates continue, the revenue from overseas broadcast rights will eventually exceed those paid by companies for the domestic rights.

    The broadcaster for much of the Asian market is ESPN Star Sports, the media giant jointly owned by Rupert Murdoch's News Corporation and Walt Disney.     The Premier League are well aware of the interest abroad, but reject any suggestion that the game could get taken away from the traditional supporter.
