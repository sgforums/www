    Blue skies thinking: How Tottenham's planned new stadium should look.

     

    TOTTENHAM have revealed the first images of their proposed new 60,000-seater stadium.

    The ground, which is yet to be named, will be built on land close to White Hart Lane. And chairman Daniel Levy believes the new stadium will benefit the club's supporters.

    He said: "The brief I gave the architects was to design an iconic stadium that would be one of the best in Europe. "My over-riding priority is to re-create the terrific atmosphere we have at the current stadium, within a world class stadium that offers state-of-the-art facilities.

    With the shape of the stadium and the wavy roof, Spurs supporters could well be forgiven for thinking they are visiting the home of their hated rivals. But KSS chairman David Keirle believes the design is groundbreaking and are all about the Spurs 'brand'.

    'The visuals of the new stadium show that it’s really seen as a building which responds to the brand, which we see as representing style and flowing lines of football.

    Virtual Spurs fans walk to their new home.

    The roof has been designed to focus the noise back onto the crowd and onto the pitch. All seating is enclosed, there are no open corners, so everything is focused back onto the pitch and the acoustics will maximise the impact of the vocal support from fans.

    "This means designing a stadium which has supporters as close as possible to the field of play and not simply looking to chase headline capacity. "Our fans will find themselves closer to the pitch than at any other comparable stadium in the UK. This design puts fan experience first."
