    He's behind you: Robinson's job is under threat

    People won't show any sympathy and I'm hurting

    Paul Robinson departed this arena admitting that he fears for his place as England's first-choice goalkeeper after his error midway through the first half last night presented Kevin Kuranyi with the tap-in and the catalyst to Germany's victory.

    The Tottenham Hotspur goalkeeper failed to deal with Bernd Schneider's cross from the right flank, the ball veering towards the top corner with Robinson, off balance, palming it out rather than over the bar. The unmarked Kuranyi duly converted and, while the 27-year-old was not at fault for Christian Pander's winner, he was clearly shaken by his mistake with his jittery performance thereafter followed by a half-time substitution.

    Asked in the aftermath whether he feared for his place, Robinson replied: "Yes, definitely, of course. I'm disappointed with the way I played this evening. People won't show sympathy and you can understand why and I'm hurting this evening. We want to be one of the best teams in the world and want to test ourselves against the best. Performance-wise, we did reasonably well out there, creating a lot of chances without winning the game but I'm disappointed with the first goal.

    "I got myself into a reasonable position when the ball was to be crossed, but I was unlucky on two counts: firstly, the ball ended up going in towards the top left-hand corner from out on the touchline and, secondly, when I have palmed it out it's gone straight to their player and he's stuck it in. I've only managed to get it out straight to him."

    Steve McClaren, perhaps tellingly, opted against reaffirming Robinson's position as his No1 after the blunder, Yet, with Ben Foster still recovering from a knee injury and Scott Carson untried at this level, James may now be in a position to regain his place in the starting line-up against Israel. "David has played consistently well for Portsmouth this year and fully deserved his chance to come back into the squad again," added Robinson. "I'm disappointed with the way I've played this evening. I've started the season really well for Tottenham. My own form has been good and this was just a little set-back tonight."
