    Podolski denies China victory

    Germany were held to a draw by China in the first leg of their tour of Asia in Shanghai on Friday.


    Hao Junmin gave the hosts the lead in the fifth minute, but Lukas Podolski equalised for the beaten Euro 2008 finalists within two minutes.

    Although the two nations are 95 places apart in the FIFA World Rankings, China had the better chances but were denied by a combination of Germany goalkeeper Robert Enke and poor finishing.

    China Delighted With German Draw

    It is early days but there is a smile back on the face of Chinese football.

    It was the first match for recently appointed coach Gao Hongbo. 

    “It’s a result we never imagined. This is a whole new start,” Gao said after the game.

    “The most important thing, though, was to give the Chinese fans a reason to believe in us again.  

    "Germany are a strong side and we need to learn more from them.”  

    Goalscorer Hao was also smiling after the match.

    “We followed the coach’s tactics and he researched the German team very well,” he said.

    “Gao selected many young players for this game and we cherished this opportunity very much. As you know, Germany are very strong and we just planned to learn something from the match, we did not expect too much.

    “Scoring is not important for me, I really hope this goal could give our fans more confidence. 

    “Germany might have been tired and not in top form, but I think our performance was not bad at all,” defender Cheng Liang said.
