    Wenger says Henry should get the Ballon d'Or

    Arsene Wenger maintains it would "not be right" were Thierry Henry to once again be overlooked for the European Footballer of the Year award.

    Reports in Italy suggest World Cup-winning captain Fabio Cannavaro is set to scoop the prestigious Ballon dOr later this month.

    However, Arsenal boss Wenger maintains there are few who could compare with the remarkable season of Henry, who led Arsenal to within minutes of Champions League glory and played in his second World Cup final this summer.

    Wenger declared: "Congratulations to Cannavaro if that is the case - but for me there is only one candidate this year and that is Thierry Henry. It is unfortunate and it is not right that he does not have the recognition. Thierry just deserves it. He was the top goalscorer in England, the best player in England, the second-highest goalscorer in Europe behind Luca Toni. He played in the Champions League final and he played in the World Cup final. He lost one on penalties and he lost the other in the last few minutes with 10 men. Overall I do not think the fact that a team wins on penalties can make such a difference. When you look at what this guy has achieved in his career as a player I can tell you there are some players in the Ballon dOr who have a lot to answer for when you compare them to Thierry Henry."

    Wenger added: "The only one in contention with him this year was [Samuel] Etoo, who won the Champions League and the Spanish Championship. He is the only one who could fight with Thierry."

    Both Henry and defender William Gallas played a full 90 minutes for France in their midweek 1-0 win over Greece in Paris.

    The Arsenal captain chalked up his 39th international goal to move to within two of Michel Platinis record.

    Wenger, though, lamented the fact two of his key men were left to play out the whole match where others - like Manchester Uniteds Luis Saha - did not.

    The Arsenal boss said: "I feel overall the friendly was not best placed. What is difficult for us is we played Sunday against Liverpool, then Wednesday, Saturday, Tuesday - and at a period of the season when the players who played the World Cup suffer a little bit. But on the other hand I have enough players to deal with that."

    Wenger added: "What can I do about it now? They have played. I ignore it. The fact that I am upset with it or not does not change a lot. I just think this friendly is not in an ideal position - somewhere physically you cannot be 100% in every game in such a short period of time."

    Wenger played down reports of a spat with Les Bleus manager Raymond Domenech over the issue.

    "Everybody does his job like he thinks he has to do it," the Arsenal manager said.

    "I do mine like I think I have to do it and that is it. What is important for us is to win tomorrow - to beat Newcastle - and all the rest I cannot influence."

    Henry could well be deployed as the lone striker once again on Saturday, the skipper admitting it was a somewhat "harder" role he has grown into.

    Wenger observed: "He rightly feels a little bit more responsibility to stay central, even if you do not stop him from going out wide on the flanks. We play normally with four strikers because he has less direct support and more from deep. But I agree physically it is a bit harder for him."

    Wenger backs calls for video replays

    Arsenal coach Arsene Wenger has thrown his weight behind calls for referees to use video technology, saying that failing to use TV replays was like refusing to use electricity.

    The issue is back on the agenda after Blackburn Rovers manager Mark Hughes and Watfords Adrian Boothroyd called for technology to be used in the wake of controversies last weekend.

    Asked if he backed the use of replays by referees, Wenger told a news conference on Monday: "I agree. Its a position Ive defended for a long time but some people refuse progress. Its like saying we dont want to play with electricity - we want to play night games with torches."

    Wenger, whose team face Hamburg SV in the Champions League on Tuesday, said: "I feel that refusing progress is difficult to defend. You want the right decision to be taken. Now, every game, every decision of the referee is analysed on television and the only one in the whole game who has no access to the video, and the right picture, is the referee. I dont find it right. But what is even more surprising is that the referees dont want to use it. Its amazing."

    Wengers comments echoed those made by Hughes, whose team had a penalty appeal turned down in their stormy Premier League draw with Tottenham Hotspur on Sunday.

    "Ive never been an advocate of technology but sooner rather than later they should bring it in," Hughes said.

    "When huge decisions at the top level have an impact on teams, management and players then something has to be done."

    Boothroyd gave his backing to technology for referees after his sides defeat by Portsmouth on Saturday.

    We got what we deserved says Lehmann

    Goalkeeper Jens Lehmann believes Arsenal got what they deserved at the Emirates Stadium tonight after coming from behind to beat Hamburg.

    Arsenals Champions League future was anything but certain when they fell behind to Rafael van der Vaarts early stunner, but Robin van Persie levelled after the break.

    Emmanuel Eboues first European goal and second for the club put Arsenal ahead before Julio Baptista came off the bench to net his first Gunners goal to secure a 3-1 win.

    "It was very close," Lehmann said on ITV4.

    "Unfortunately it happens now to us that we always concede and have to fight against 10 men defending. We worked hard and weve got what we deserved because sometimes you need to have that little bit of luck and we had that today. At the end it was a vital win for us."

    One sour note was a yellow card for captain Thierry Henry, who will miss the final Group G game in Porto, in which Arsenal need to avoid defeat to guarantee qualification.

    "Its sad but you have to cope with everything," Lehmann added.

    Arsenal boss Arsene Wenger hailed his sides attitude after going behind. "They scored a great goal and this time we couldnt do a lot about it," he said.

    "After that I feel weve shown great character, great spirit. Very unfortunately, (we were) 1-0 down at half-time but we hit the bar, we made a few changes and it worked out. Overall it was a great team performance."

    Wenger acknowledged Henrys booking was a blow but believes his side will cope without their talismanic skipper when they travel to Portugal.

    "Thats unfortunate but we know that it will be a good game for us and Im confident we can do it," he added.

    "He was frustrated tonight because his shirt was held a lot. In the box a lot happened. Its difficult for the referee - he couldnt always see it. Because it was a lot of unfair play against him, he got a little bit frustrated."

    Hleb wins Belarus Player of the Year award for the fourth time

    Arsenal midfielder Alexander Hleb has been voted Belarus top player for the fourth time in the last five years.

    In a nationwide poll of the countrys soccer experts, conducted by the sports daily Pressbol, the 25-year-old playmaker was the overwhelming winner, ahead of Dnipro striker Sergei Kornilenko and Tom Tomsk midfielder Alexander Kulchiy.

    Hleb also won the honour in 2002, 2003 and 2005 and finished second behind Dynamo Moscow striker Maxim Romashchenko in 2004.
