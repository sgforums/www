    Avram Grant faces the embarrassment of sitting in the dug-out for next Wednesday's Champions League match in Valencia without being allowed to coach his players.

    This is because the Israel Coaches Association has pledged to block his application for a Pro Licence ahead of the game.

    Under UEFA Champions League guidelines, the new Chelsea chief needs the licence to coach from the touchline, but sources at the ICA have disclosed that Grant 'faces hostility' after making an application two weeks ago Â— a full week before Jose Mourinho's departure.

    Grant did not take the Pro Licence course when he was manager of Israel and they see no reason to grant special dispensation simply because he is the manager of Chelsea.

    Stamford Bridge chiefs claim they have applied to UEFA for special dispensation for Grant to be in the technical area for next week's clash with Valencia, but there are no guarantees that it will be accepted.

    UEFA's technical director Andy Roxburgh has told friends that Grant does not have the necessary qualifications to coach Chelsea and that the chances of him being given permission to instruct his players are slim.

    Grant cleared to coach in Europe

    Uefa has no plans to ban new Chelsea manager Avram Grant from coaching in the Champions League despite uncertainty over his qualifications.

    Grant has yet to obtain the Uefa Pro Licence which is normally required to coach in European competition.

    But a Uefa spokesman said: "We are awaiting the outcome of discussions between Chelsea, the Israeli Football Association and the Premier League.

    "But we have no intention of banning or taking any other disciplinary measure."

    Uefa would only be forced to consider Grant's case if the Premier League formally complained to the game's European governing body about his lack of qualifications.

    Former Israel coach Grant was named as Chelsea's manager following Jose Mourinho's shock departure last week.
