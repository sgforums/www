    New Manchester United signing Henrik Larsson admits the option of a move to Aston Villa was never an issue.

    Larsson has agreed to head to Old Trafford from Helsingborgs on loan when the transfer window reopens in January. Reports had claimed he also had the chance to sign for Villa, who are managed by his former Celtic boss Martin O'Neill.

    But the ex-Sweden international revealed he had not been in contact with O'Neill and only had thoughts for United. "Man U took contact with me and wanted to borrow me," Larsson told Sky Sports News.

    "I did not have contact with Aston Villa, with the gaffer there who was at Celtic when I was there. "We haven't spoken about a loan deal, so it was never really something that I had to think about."

    The 35-year-old is now waiting to learn what role Sir Alex Ferguson has lined up for him when he links up with the Premiership leaders. He added: "I'm very happy, obviously, as it's one of the biggest clubs in the world, if not the biggest.

    "I've been fortunate enough to play for big clubs in my career. I was at Celtic, Feyenoord, Barcelona and now a loan period for 10 weeks in Man U. "I know what I'm good at and the players they have there are world-class players, quality players.

    "I hope I can contribute with something, then wait and see what's going to be."
