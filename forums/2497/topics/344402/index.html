    RICARDO CARVALHO is the latest Chelsea star to have a bust-up with Big Phil Scolari.

    Carvalho disagreed with the decision to switch to zonal marking at set-pieces. Boss Scolari then dropped the defender for Wednesday’s FA Cup win at Southend.

    The row follows incidents with Nicolas Anelka and Didier Drogba in the last two weeks. Carvalho is believed to have spoken to Scolari in the days before the tie and said he thought a change to a zonal system was wrong. But Scolari insisted on the switch after seeing his side concede too many goals at set-pieces.

    This incident also comes on the back of John Terry and Frank Lampard questioning the intensity of Scolari’s training regime.

    Carvalho was one of Scolari’s most trusted stars for the Portugal national side for more than four years.

    Chelsea have played a man-for-man system for the majority of the last five years and other players share the Portuguese stopper’s concerns.
