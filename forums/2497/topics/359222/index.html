    You cry babies! Chelsea KO was justice for 'diving' says Dunne.

    Fall guy: Drogba has a tantrum when he fails to win a free-kick against Barca

     

     

    Manchester City captain Richard Dunne last night claimed Chelsea deserved to be knocked out of the Champions League by Barcelona because they are ‘divers’. As Chelsea continue to bemoan what they see as an injustice at the hands of Norwegian referee Tom Henning Ovrebo, Dunne indicated they have only themselves to blame because of some of their past behaviour.

    Talking before tomorrow’s derby against Manchester United, Dunne said: ‘The refs have been given rules and they have no option but to punish defenders sometimes. But there are a lot more divers as well. It has crept in more and more, but what goes around comes around. You dive around and teams score against you in injury time.’

    Dunne did not specifically refer to Chelsea’s defeat by Barcelona on Wednesday, when Andres Iniesta scored the decisive equaliser in added time, but the Republic of Ireland defender has long been a critic of Didier Drogba, whose behaviour after the semi-final shocked the football world.

     

    Take it as red: Dunne gets his marching orders against Hamburg in the UEFA Cup.

     

     

    Speaking about Drogba in March, Dunne said: ‘I am not an admirer of how Drogba plays the game. It is frustrating when you see your opponent testing the referee.’ Dunne, sent off seven times in his City career, will face another player with a reputation for theatrics at Old Trafford tomorrow, United’s Cristiano Ronaldo.

    The Portugal winger has been sent off twice against City in his United career and Dunne added: ‘He is the best player in the world and he plays the game to his advantage. At times, he can be running so fast that any nick could send him tumbling. He makes the most of the situation, I suppose. But diving or not diving, he’s impossible to mark when he’s on form.’

     

    Last time out: Ronaldo inexplicably handles a Wayne Rooney corner for his second yellow card during United's 1-0 win at Eastlands

     

    UEFA confirmed yesterday they have received referee Ovrebo’s report. They are set to issue charges next week against Drogba, who approached Ovrebo aggressively and screamed ‘F***ing disgrace’ at TV cameras in the immediate aftermath of the game, as well as Michael Ballack and Jose Bosingwa. All three are likely to be banned for at least three European matches next season.

    Ovrebo’s wife, Bente, said yesterday her husband, who holds a full-time job as a psychologist, was ‘extremely shaken up’. She added: ‘It is terrifying. Why are people reacting like this? It’s just football.’

     

    'It's just football': Michael Ballack remonstrates with Ovrebo

     

    FIFA president Sepp Blatter joined the debate by demanding that more countries make their referees fully professional. He said: ‘I’ve been saying for 10 years we must improve refereeing and that means it’s time to have professionals and use only them in high-level competitions.’

    English uniformed police will join 2,000 Italian officers on duty for the first time in Rome on the day of the Champions League final — May 27 — in a bid to protectUnited fans from local hooligans.
