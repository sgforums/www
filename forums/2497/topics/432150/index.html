    Wigan have given Aston Villa permission to speak to Roberto Martinez about the vacant managerial job at Villa Park

    The Spaniard, who kept the club in the Premier League thanks to a four-game unbeaten run at the end of the season, has spoken to Villa officials who are on the look-out for a successor to Gerard Houllier, who stood down earlier this month for health reasons.

    He told the Daily Telegraph: "Aston Villa have acted above board and in a totally professional manner.

    "They wrote to me asking for permission to speak to Roberto. He was on holiday at the time and I granted Villa permission.

    "He arrived back yesterday and I presume he has spoken to Villa. I have made it clear to him I will be extending his contract.

    "I have never let him down on anything and he has never let me down. I know I can never say never in football, but I would be very surprised if he is Aston Villa's new manager."

    IT'S straight back to the drawing board, Randy.

    Roberto Martinez's snub to Aston Villa has left owner Randy Lerner and his board desperately searching for options to be their new manager.

    And Villa now face the embarrassment of chasing a manager who was clearly not their first choice.

    Everton and Bolton are bracing themselves for approaches over David Moyes and Owen Coyle respectively but, understandably, neither of those will have taken kindly to not being Villa's No 1 pick.

    Having ruled out Steve McClaren, Mark Hughes and Rafa Benitez - plus a friendly 'no thanks' from Carlo Ancelotti - Martinez was sought out to replace the departed Gerard Houllier.

    Both Moyes and Coyle fit what Lerner is looking for - but both have secure jobs and Villa are clearly not the attraction they once were.

    Being rejected by a 37-year-old manager who just avoided relegation with Wigan last term is just another kick in the teeth for Villa.

    A Villa source told SunSport: "The club are now furiously moving on to their next target. They had made some plans in case this happened but there will not be an appointment as soon as they would have liked. It's a case of going back to the list now."

    The prospect of crawling back to Hughes or McClaren is getting increasingly likely.

    After Martinez decided to stay loyal to current chairman Dave Whelan, Villa claimed they had not discussed the managerial role with him.

    Although they may not have got down to the nitty-gritty of contracts, they had certainly made contact with the Martinez camp. It is a significant snub to Villa with Martinez choosing to stay and sign a new three-year deal at a club which has little or no money to spend.

    Bolton chairman Phil Gartside also knows he has a special manager.

    Any phone call from Lerner over the next few days would not be welcomed by the Trotters board.

    Gartside said: "They all make comparisons between him and Alex Ferguson. Owen's 44 and I'm not sure he will work until he's 70. But he's got that ability to be an all-round manager, he's not just a coach.

    "Man-management wise he's got all the abilities and coaching-wise he's got all the tactical knowledge."

    VILLA PARK LOOKS A NO-GO AREA

     

    Roberto Martinez had been high on Aston Villa’s wanted list since the departure of Houllier

     

    ASTON VILLA could be faced with two more Premier League managers turning them down after Roberto Martinez rejected their job yesterday.

     

    Everton’s David Moyes and Bolton manager Owen Coyle are set to say no if Aston Villa owner Randy Lerner comes calling.

    Lerner and his chief executive Paul Faulkner both refused to admit any embarrassment that Martinez, who only escaped relegation on the last day of the season with Wigan, turned them down. Neither would comment directly, hiding behind a club statement.

    Villa did not even get to talk to Martinez about their job, the Spaniard meeting his friend and Wigan owner Dave Whelan, inset, yesterday before announcing he did not want to speak to Lerner and was staying put. Martinez has signed a new three-year deal with Wigan worth £2.1 million, leaving Villa to go back to a short list that gets shorter every day. 

    Villa say they still hope to have a manager in place by the end of next week, but it is unlikely to be Coyle or Moyes.

    Coyle would have been interested but is not happy at being ranked behind Martinez in Villa’s pecking order and Moyes, who has seen winger

    Ashley Young being sold for £20m with the chance Stewart Downing might follow, is not interested.

    Carlo Ancelotti, Steve McClaren, Mark Hughes and Rafa Benitez have all been discounted by Lerner for various reasons, but Martinez turning Villa down is a huge blow, no matter how the club try to dress it up.

    It took Lerner and Faulkner six weeks to find Gerard Houllier after Martin O’Neill walked out on them and fans have been told that will not happen again. 

    “We remain focused and are working hard to secure the best candidate for Aston Villa and will have the new manager in place to prepare the players for the pre-season programme and next season’s campaign,” said a spokesman in a club statement.
