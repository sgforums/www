Bramble arrested on suspicion of sexual assault

    Sunderland defender Bramble arrested on suspicion of sexual assault and possession of
    class A drugs

     

    Arrest: Titus Bramble is a central defender for Sunderland

     

     

     

    Titus Bramble has been arrested by police over claims of sexual assault and possession of class A drugs.

    The 30-year-old centre-back, who played in Sunderland’s 2-1 defeat at Norwich on Monday night, is currently being questioned by police and is in custody in Stockton-on-Tees.

    He was arrested in the Yarm area in the early hours of Wednesday morning.

    A Cleveland Police spokeswoman said: `A 30-year-old man has been arrested on suspicion of sexual assault and possession of a class A drug.

    'He is currently being questioned by police and inquiries are on-going.’

     

     

    Sentence: Tesfaye Bramble was jailed last month

     

     

    A Sunderland spokesperson said: 'The club is looking into this matter but due to ongoing police investigations is unable to comment further at this stage.'

    A year ago, Bramble was arrested by Northumbria Police over an alleged rape in a city centre hotel but he was later released without charge.

    His brother, Tesfaye, was jailed for four-and-a-half years for raping a 19-year-old. He is appealing against his conviction.
