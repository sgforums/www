    Tony Adams to succeed Harry Redknapp as Portsmouth manager

    Tony Adams is set to be named as the new Portsmouth manager just over 48 hours after Harry Redknapp left the club to join Tottenham.

     

     

    Portsmouth are set to unveil Tony Adams as their new manager following Harry Redknapp's departure for Tottenham Hotspur.

    Adams, who has been Redknapp's assistant for the last two years, had been tipped to land the job as Portsmouth look for consistency. 

    The former Wycombe Wanderers boss will be paraded in front of the media at a 1pm press conference.

    Earlier today Redknapp backed Tony Adams and Joe Jordan to take charge of the club. "Tony and Joe are both terrific football men," said the 61-year-old. "Whoever takes the job, they've got a good group of players there.

    "It's a great job for one of the boys to take, I brought them to the club, they're great lads. I only wish Portsmouth every success."
