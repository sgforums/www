    LIVERPOOL COULD MEET CHELSEA AGAIN

    Liverpool were handed a plum draw against PSV Eindhoven in the quarter-finals of the Champions League. Manchester United also avoided the more difficult teams remaining, paired with Serie A side Roma. Both United and Liverpool will play the second leg of their tie at home.

    Chelsea were given the draw against Valencia, where they must travel to the Mestalla for the second leg. AC Milan will host Bayern Munich at the San Siro before a second leg in Bavaria.

    Providing they overcome PSV, who conquered Arsenal this week, Liverpool face the winner of Chelsea and Valencia but must play the second leg away from home.

    United or Roma take on the winner of Bayern and Milan's clash, playing at home first - meaning a possible trip to Old Trafford for England midfielder Owen Hargreaves. The quarter-final sees Manchester-born Simone Perrotta face United for Roma, while former Chelsea defender Asier del Horno, now with Valencia, could take on his former club.

    UNITED TARGET ROMAN CONQUEST

    Manchester United will meet Roma in the Champions League quarter-finals. And if the Reds prevail they'll meet AC Milan or Bayern Munich in the semis.

    Chief executive David Gill was delighted by the draw - but admitted his personal preference would be to win the domestic title. "It's a fantastic draw. Obviously any team at this stage is a difficult draw," he told Sky Sports News. "We haven't been in the quarter-finals for a few years and to be back is fantastic."

    Asked about avoiding the other English teams, Gill said: "For me, playing in Europe is about playing other European teams. Obviously Chelsea and Liverpool could meet in the semi-final and it could be an all-England final, who knows?

    "It is a personal thing. I think for me personally, to win the Premiership this year would be a fantastic achievement. If I had to choose which one, that would be it.

    "We have underperformed in Europe in the last few years. A club of our stature should be in the latter stages."
