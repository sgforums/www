    Italy honoured with world's largest lasagne at Euro 2012

    Italy could be stuffed when they take on England in the last eight at Euro 2012 on Sunday night, after a Polish restaurant created the world's largest lasagne in their honour.


    The diner created the gigantic meat feast for Cesare Prandelli and his squad who are staying at a nearby hotel in the vibrant city of Krakow, where England are also based.

    Weighing a colossal 4.8 tonnes, the dish was crowned the largest ever made by Guinness World Record officials on Wednesday.

    A team of chefs ten worked around the clock to create the giant lasagne which took over ten hours to bake before being sliced into 10,000 portions.

    Executive chef Giancarlo Russo admitted he hoped the Italian squad would sample his work saying: 'I don't know about the players or if they will try the lasagne because they are on a diet, but they can try just a little bit.'
