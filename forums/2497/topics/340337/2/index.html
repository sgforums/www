    Diarra tight-lipped on future

    Pompey ace says 'anything could happen'

    Portsmouth midfielder Lassana Diarra is remaining tight-lipped over where his future lies.

    The much-coveted France international looks set to end up at either Real Madrid or Manchester City next month.

    The Spanish giants had an 'exceptional' offer accepted by Pompey on Wednesday, while City boss Mark Hughes later said he was still interested in trying to reach a deal.

    Speaking to the Spanish media, Diarra, 23, said 'anything could happen' and he will not speak about the situation for a few days at least.

    But he did say that he hopes his future will be settled soon.

    He told AS: "I cannot talk. I don't want to. They are calling me from England and France, but I will talk inside a few days."

    Asked whether he would prefer Madrid to Manchester, Diarra replied: "I will not say anything. Football is complicated and anything could happen."

    He added in Marca: "I cannot say anything, really. You have to respect me. I will talk inside a few days, now I cannot say anything.

    "I cannot talk. I hope my future will be settled in the next few days, soon. This is the only thing I can say."
