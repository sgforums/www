    Thierry Henry warns ex-wife: 'You won't get your hands on my £10m'

     

     

    See you in court: Thierry Henry has hired top lawyers to fight ex-wife Claire Merry's claim on his £10million fortune.

     

    Thierry Henry is facing a messy divorce battle after he told his ex-wife to keep her hands off his cash. The formal Arsenal striker warned Claire Merry her reported demands for $10m pound were unrealistic and she should prepare for a tough time in court.

    Merry, 27, a former model, was granted a quickie divorce from Henry, 30, last September after she found raunchy texts on his phone and he went on dates with make-up artist Sadie Hewlett.

    Merry and Henry, married for four years, met in 2001 while filming a Renault Clio advert and have a twoyearold daughter, Tea.

    After the divorce was granted for his " unreasonable behaviour", Merry, from Croydon, vowed to take the FC Barcelona star  -  said to be worth £25million  -  to the cleaners. She is demanding a settlement based on his earnings. It includes money from his ad deals with Pringles and McDonald's and a cut of the £5.9million marital home in Hampstead, North London.

    In 2004, ex-wife of England and Arsenal star Ray Parlour won maintenance amounting to more than a third of his future income. In the initial settlement, Karen Parlour was given two mortgage-free houses worth more than £1million and a £250,000 lump sum.

    But she was told by the Court of Appeal her award of £250,000 a year personal maintenance would go up to £406,500 and be reviewed in four years.

    Henry has hired London lawyers Mishcon de Reya, who represented Princess Diana and Heather Mills in their divorce battles.
