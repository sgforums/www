    ROBERTO MANCINI refused to lay into Carlos Tevez after his shocking miss cost Manchester City their unbeaten record.

    The Argie ace incredibly blasted the ball over the bar from just eight yards in front of an open goal with 15 minutes gone as City crashed to their first defeat of the season.

    It was a contender for miss of the century - but boss Mancini insisted: "We had this chance with Carlos, but we also had three or four others.

    "We had a situation where we we should score - but this is football. If you don't score a goal when you have these situations you can lose.

    "Every game is difficult and you have to play to the last second."

    Delighted Sunderland boss Steve Bruce could not believe his luck as Darren Bent cashed in on Tevez's miss with an injury-time penalty.

    Bruce admitted: "We rode our luck with the Tevez miss, it's about time it went our way. I don't think he'll miss one like that ever again."
