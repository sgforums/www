    PRIDE ... Almunia would love to pull on Three Lions jersey

     

    MANUEL ALMUNIA insists he will be as proud as anyone if he is ever called up for England.

    Arsenal's Spanish keeper today becomes eligible for a British passport after chalking up five years in this country.

    He has been linked with a controversial call-up to Fabio Capello's squad - and now that could become a reality.

    The 32-year-old stopper insists he would be as chuffed as a player born and bred in Bradford, Barnsley or Bermondsey to wear the Three Lions on his chest.

    He said: "It is almost impossible for me to be called up by Spain.

    "But it would be an honour if the England coach counted on me."

    Almunia would face competition from David James, Rob Green, Ben Foster, Scott Carson and Paul Robinson for a place in Capello's squad for the World Cup finals.
