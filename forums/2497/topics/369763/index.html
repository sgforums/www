    IMPARTIAL OBSERVER - Jose Mourinho reckons the title is Man City's for the taking

    JOSE MOURINHO BACKS CITY FOR TITLE

    Carlos Tevez will clinch it, says Special One

     

    JOSE MOURINHO is backing moneybags Manchester City to win the Premier League next season. 

    The man who won two championships at Chelsea is convinced Mark Hughes' side can win the title for the first time since 1968. 

    Mourinho said: "Adebayor up front with Carlos Tevez - unbelievable! And they have Roque Santa Cruz and Robinho too. What a strike force! That's it, City are champions for sure."

     

    Spree

    The Special One, now in charge of Inter Milan, was quick to defend the Eastlands club over their astonishing spending spree that looks set to catapult them into contenders. 

    He added: "It's not buying the title. You buy the players you think can do the job but you still need to prove you are the best - the best coach, the best team and that's tough and no amount of money can buy that."

     

    Love

    Mourinho refused to talk about his old side Chelsea, even ahead of this week's friendly match with the Blues in Pasadena. 

    He kissed his Inter badge and said: "This is my club now - I love Inter." 

    As for the John Terry saga, he added: "It is not my club so it is not my problem. I don't care."
