    GOONER ... Jens Lehmann struggled against Wigan reserves

     

     

     

    JENS LEHMANN showed he is still king of the cock-ups on his Arsenal return last night.

    The 41-year-old veteran, brought out of retirement to ease the Gunners keeper crisis, made a string of blunders in 2-1 defeat at Wigan reserves.

    First the German stopper allowed a back pass by Sead Hajrovic to bobble over his foot.

    It was similar to Paul Robinson's howler when he totally missed his clearance following a Gary Neville back pass for England against Croatia in 2006.

    But this time Lehmann was more fortunate as the ball did not find the net.

    Lehmann was caught out again as striker Callum McManaman, 19, rounded him to fire Wigan into a 53rd-minute lead.

    Then the German was caught in possession only for Daniel Boateng to save his blushes by blocking Daniel Redmond's shot.

    But Lehmann's nightmare was complete in the 86th minute when sub Joe Holt rounded the ring-rusty keeper to make it 2-0.

    Rhys Murphy reduced the deficit from the penalty spot with virtually the last kick of the game.

    But Lehmann seemed unfazed by his horror show. He said: "It's great to be back - I was surprised to get the call from Arsene."

    McManaman, a distant relative of ex-Liverpool ace Steve, said: "It was great to score past Lehmann. He's still a great keeper."
