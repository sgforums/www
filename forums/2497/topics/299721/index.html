    Ashley Cole has appealed against the red card he received in Chelsea's Boxing Day draw with Aston Villa.

    An exhilarating game in the capital ended in a 4-4 stalemate that saw three players sent-off, with Villa's Zat Knight and now Cole having lodged appeals.

    The England defender was dismissed in stoppage time by referee Phil Dowd after it was adjudged he had handled Gabriel Agbonlahor's header on the line.

    Gareth Barry scored the resulting penalty but like Knight, Cole has appealed for leniency from the Football Association.

    An FA regulatory commission will consider both appeals on Friday.

    *Villa appeal Knight red-card*

    Aston Villa have decided to appeal against the red-card picked up by Zat Knight against Chelsea on Boxing Day.

    Knight was dismissed for a challenge on Michael Ballack just before the break with Villa leading 2-0.

    Andrei Shevchenko dispatched the resulting spot-kick and the game went on to finish 4-4, with Ricardo Carvalho and Ashley Cole also seeing red for Chelsea.

    Martin O'Neil was incensed by the decision after watching the incident back and has made the decision to lodge an appeal against the defender's resulting suspension.

    Knight would only have to serve a one-match ban for preventing a goal-scoring opportunity, but the Villa manager is determined to have the decision overturned.

    The case will be heard on 28th December.
