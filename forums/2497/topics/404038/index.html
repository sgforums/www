[Official] Liverpool appoint Roy Hodgson

  • Poignant
    1 Jul 10, 16:45

    Roy Hodgson has been appointed the new manager of Liverpool FC.


    The 62-year-old former Fulham, Inter Milan and Switzerland manager has agreed terms with the club on a three-year contract and becomes the 18th manager in our history.

    He will be unveiled to the world's media this afternoon after overseeing the first day of pre-season training at Melwood.

    Hodgson joins us from Fulham, where he guided the Cottagers to the Europa League final in May and was voted Manager of the Year by the League Managers' Association.

    He exclusively told Liverpoolfc.tv: "This is the biggest job in club football and I'm honoured to be taking on the role of manager of Britain's most successful football club. I look forward to meeting the players and the supporters and getting down to work at Melwood."

    DANNY MURPHY has backed new Liverpool boss Roy Hodgson to be a success at Anfield.

    The ex-Reds midfielder was one of Hodgson's key players at Fulham and skippered the side during their run to Europa League final last season.

    Murphy said: "I admire Roy Hodgson. He has a philosophy of playing good football which will fit well at Liverpool.

    "He gives players responsibility and a platform to work from.

    "Maybe it is time Liverpool had an English manager who knows about the club."

    Murphy flourished as Cottagers captain under former Inter Milan and Blackburn manager Hodgson.

    And he has nothing but praise for the 62-year-old Londoner.

    Murphy added: "Roy is a talented manager who succeeds because he works incredibly hard.

    "He instils confidence in his players and has a style the players can enjoy.

    "His success has been a mix of a lot of things but primarily hard work.

    "He has real tactical knowledge too - in a way it has come from Roy's Italian managerial roots, it is an Italian style."
