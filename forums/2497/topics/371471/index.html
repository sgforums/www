Steven Taylor: Newcastle Football Club is a joke!

  • limpper

    Defender Steven Taylor has hit out at Newcastle over their current predicament, labelling the club "a joke".

    The Magpies head into their first game outside the top flight for 16 years at West Brom on Saturday evening, with no full-time manager and uncertainty over the club's future ownership.

    Taylor expected Alan Shearer to take up the managerial hotseat following the club's relegation in May, but with no permanent appointment yet made it will be caretaker Chris Hughton in the dugout at The Hawthorns.

    The 23-year-old said: "The players are very frustrated because all we want to do is have a manager and a bit of stability. That's all we ask for.

    "The new manager should be given time to do it his way and if he wants something just give it to him. The chairman, if he loves this football club, would do that. I'm devastated Alan Shearer has not got the job. As players, we haven't a clue what is going on.

    "I've read every week that next week Alan is going to be named manager. Then Joe Kinnear is going to get the job, then Kevin Keegan and then Alan Shearer again. I don't get it. The club is a joke."

    Taylor continued: "Everyone thought he would be back in place. We thought it might take a couple of weeks but then Alan would be the manager.

    "And that's why I missed the (Under-21) European Championship. He wanted me right for the start of the season and of course I was happy to do it. I have so much respect for him and whatever he says, you do it.

    "He said he would be back and would do it his way. I guarantee if he was still manager now, all our big players would still be here. The chairman would have had a headache over the wage bill but whatever Alan Shearer wants, you give him. He commands that much respect and he would take us straight back up."

    - Football365

    Shearer remains hopeful of landing Newcastle job

    Alan Shearer remains hopeful of landing the Newcastle United job.

    Shearer, who ended last season’s relegation campaign as Toon’s caretaker-boss, was back on BBC TV duty at The Hawthorns yesterday.

    Shearer said: “I would love the chance to finish what I started last season.

    “I’m in limbo. I don’t know what’s going on. I want it sorted.

    “It could be someone comes in, buys the club and wants me as manager.

    “Would I like to be manager? Without a doubt.”

    Prospective Newcastle United owners The Profitable Group have stated that Alan Shearer will return as manager of the club if their bid to buy the north east side is successful.

    Steve McMahon, the former Liverpool player who is a representative for The Profitable Group, has said that a bid has been launched by the Singapore-based consortium.

    It was last week revealed that two bids had been received for the club, with both bids thought to be around £100 million, which is the new asking price set by owner Mike Ashley.

    The Profitable Group are now the first bidders to publicly declare their interest in buying the Magpies, and McMahon is hopeful a deal can be completed, with rumours that Shearer will be installed as the club's new manager if the bid is successful.

    Speaking to Singapore's Straights Times, McMahon said, "We've had a bid in and it's under serious consideration.

    "It's a strategic move and will be great for Newcastle. We can turn what is a disaster at the moment into a success.

    "By the end of the week, we'll know if we're successful or not."

    The club were relegated to the Championship last season, and Ashley is now desperate to end his footballing nightmare and sell the club as quickly as he can, predicting losses of around £280 million.
