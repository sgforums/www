    BALLACK BLAMES CHELSEA TACTICS

    MICHAEL BALLACK claims his disappointing start to life at Chelsea is down to Jose Mourinho's tactics.

    Ballack, 30, has failed to have an impact after joining on a free transfer from Bayern Munich in the summer. And the German international insists his failure to adapt to life in the Premiership is a direct result of the way the champions play.

    He said: "I would have hoped there would be more crosses in the air for me to score from. "At Munich the tactics were similar but there were far more crosses. "The game we play is slightly different from what I was used to. The full-backs do not press forward as they did at Bayern. "And if we sometimes change and play with forwards on the wings, they are the type of players that like to get the ball into the box and try to score rather than pass it on.

    "That situation makes it more difficult for me to play to my strengths, like heading the ball into the net." Ballack scored 57 goals in 153 appearances for Bayern but has netted just three in 24 games under Mourinho.

    The irony is, of course, that it is Mourinho's reshuffling to fit Ballack into the midfield that has forced the Portuguese to leave out his more natural wingers.
