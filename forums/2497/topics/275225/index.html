    Spurs to sack Jol 'later this week'

    Martin Jol's days at Tottenham appear to be numbered, with numerous newspaper reports tipping the Dutchman to be sacked later this week.

    Although Jol still retains the support of the majority of Spurs supporters following a difficult start to the Premier League season, the White Hart Lane hierarchy are believed to have lost faith in Jol's ability to secure Champions League football.

    Sevilla coach Juande Ramos is poised to be appointed as his successor.

    Tottenham officials met with Ramos in Spain on Friday and The Guardian believes that he is keen on taking the job. 'Ramos could even take charge for Sunday's fixture against Manchester United,' the newspaper suggests.

    Were Jol to be fired this week, it would be the fourth time in the past 10 years when the Tottenham manager was the first top-flight casualty of the season following the ousting of Gerry Francis in 1997, Christian Gross in 1998 and Glenn Hoddle in 2003.
