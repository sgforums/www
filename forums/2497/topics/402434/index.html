    Revealed: Arsenal's new yellow away kit for 2010

     

     

    Arsenal

     

     

    Sportsmail can today exclusively reveal the new away strip that will be worn by Arsenal's stars next season.

    The yellow shirt, featuring pencil-thin pinstripes, is the latest shirt to nod to the Gunners' proud history.

    The redcurrant stripes and cuff sleeves are a throwback to its Woolwich Arsenal days, while on the reverse of the badge is stitched the motto 'Victoria Concordia Crescit', which translates as ‘Victory Through Harmony’.

    The shirt is also made from recycled polyester, using up to eight plastic water bottles, as part of Nike’s ‘Considered Design’ programme.

    The shirt - priced £45/50 for adults and £35/40 for children - can be ordered online from today and is available in club shops from July 13. For more details, go to arsenal.com

    TUG-OF-WAR ... Arsenal midfielder Cesc Fabregas is a target for Barcelona

     

     

    ARSENAL have sent a message to Barcelona that they will fight to keep Cesc Fabregas. They have released images of him in the new Gunners away shirt. 

    The Spain midfielder is wanted by the La Liga giants but Arsenal are determined to keep their skipper.

    Fabregas, 23, wants to return to his hometown club after the World Cup but the Gunners have turned down a £25m offer.

    New Barca president Sandro Rosell, who takes over from Joan Laporta next month, said: "When we get together, Laporta will explain at what stage the Cesc situation is at.

    "If negotiations are advanced we will continue with the negotiations."
