    MARTIN O’NEILL will sacrifice Aston Villa’s UEFA Cup bid to maintain their Champions League charge.

     

    The Midlanders travel to Russia today for Thursday’s second-leg tie with CSKA Moscow after last week’s 1-1 draw.

    But Villa boss O’Neill is planning to leave a number of senior players at home as he prepares for Premier League clashes against Stoke on Sunday and Manchester City two days later.

    Skipper Gareth Barry admitted: “This is a difficult one for the manager.

    "If you are looking at Stoke as the priority it might be vital, after playing two games in four days last week, that a few players get some rest.”

    Despite losing 1-0 to Chelsea on Saturday, Villa remain fourth in the league, six points ahead of Arsenal.
