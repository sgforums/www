    Reds and United tracked Trez

    David Trezeguet claims Manchester United and Liverpool were interested in signing him before he agreed to extend his stay at Juventus.

    The World Cup winning striker finally ended speculation over his future by penning a contract extension with the Italian giants on Monday.

    The Frenchman had fallen out with the club's hierarchy following the departure of former boss Didier Deschamps but has now signed with the Turin side until 2011.

    "My father (Trezeguet's agent) told me I had other propositions from ambitious clubs like Manchester United, Liverpool and Barcelona," the striker told L'Equipe.

    "But as soon as Juventus showed their desire to keep me I accepted immediately.

    "I didn't want to leave Juve but I needed to get reassured by the officials.

    "The departure of Didier Deschamps did make things more complex but we finally all made efforts and came to an agreement."
