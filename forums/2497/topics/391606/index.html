    CHELSEA STEP UP BID FOR VILLA

    Anelka situation prompts fresh move for Spaniard

    VILLA: Spain sharpshooter

     

    DAVID VILLA is ready to change his mind and quit Valencia for Chelsea this summer.

    Chelsea have moved into pole position, after Real Madrid indicated they will persevere with French flop Karim Benzema.

    Valencia remain under financial pressure to sell and the Blues are waiting to take advantage.

    Villa's £30million price tag would instantly solve the La Liga outfit's financial problems, after their failure to sell their legendary Mestalla Stadium to property developers.

    At 28, the Spain striker is seen as the finished article and he has a strike record and reputation feared worldwide.

    Villa initially claimed he did not want to move abroad, as his wife had just given birth to their second child, and named Real as his preferred destination.

    Despite Real's continued interest, though, Bernabeu president Florentino Perez spent nearly £50m on Benzema last summer and Real are not prepared to give up on the former Lyon forward.

    Chelsea's interest in Villa has increased in recent weeks with their failure to reach agreement on a new contract with Nicolas Anelka.

    Anelka's deal runs out in 17 months time and chief executive Ron Gourlay has been told to reduce the club's extraordinary wage bill.

    The French forward is demanding £120,000 a week, £40,000 a week more than he currently earns.
