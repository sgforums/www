Cech: Every game a final

  • turbo_drift
    11 Apr 07, 09:50
    Petr Cech feels the next fortnight will be 'very important' for Chelsea's ambitions of winning four trophies.

    The Premiership champions reduced the gap to current leaders Manchester United to three points over the weekend after beating Tottenham, while their rivals lost at Portsmouth.

    Before next week's league game with West Ham United, The Blues face Valencia in the second leg of the UEFA Champions League quarter-final and Blackburn Rovers in the FA Cup semi-final.

    And in-form keeper Cech accepts Chelsea are approaching a critical juncture in the season as the club look to add to their Carling Cup success.

    "These two weeks will be very important ones for us, everything will be at stake," Cech told his personal website.

    "Namely the second leg of the UEFA Champions League quarter-final against Valencia, the FA Cup semi-final against Blackburn on Sunday, then West Ham in the Premiership on Wednesday and Newcastle on Sunday.

    "Everything is at stake in these games - if we win them, we will be near a successful end of the season, if we lose any of them, we are out of the competition.

    "This is valid for the Premier League as well, in which any mistake would mean Manchester win the title.

    "Simply, every game from now is kind of a final."
