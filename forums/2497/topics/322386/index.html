    Lampard In Milan To Complete Inter Switch

    Frank Lampard has arrived in Italy to complete his proposed 9 million euro transfer to Internazionale.

    Following reports from France Football on Friday night that the deal for Lampard had been agreed between Inter and sellers Chelsea, it is now understood the England midfielder is travelling to Milan this weekend to undergo a medical.

    According to Italian media sources, Lampard touched down in Positano last night and will be in Milan to sign the contract later today.

    The details of the deal, so far as is known at this stage, are that Lampard will earn some 7.5 million euros per season over the lifetime of a four-year contract. 

    That would represent an estimated 2.5 million increase, more or less, from what he is on at Chelsea, who would received a 9 million euro transfer fee.

    The relatively low size of the fee reflects the fact that Lampard only had one year left on his existing contract with the Blues.

    The player has reportedly been keen to link up again with new Inter manager Jose Mourinho, with whom he struck up a strong working relationship at Stamford Bridge.

    Speculation in Italy suggest that an agreement over Portuguese international Deco facilitated the Lampard transfer.

    Apparently Inter, who like Chelsea wanted to sign Barcelona's midfielder, agreed to back out of the race for Deco, leaving the coast clear for Luiz Felipe Scolari to recruit him for his new Chelsea squad.

    Lampard Refuses To Comment On Inter Reports

    Chelsea midfielder Frank Lampard has refused to confirm whether reports claiming that he has already signed for Inter are true - though the fact that he did not take the chance to deny the stories either could be seen as being telling.

    France Football claimed on Friday that Lampard had signed a four-year contract with Inter, worth €7.3m a year, and that the Nerazzurri would pay Chelsea a transfer fee of €9m.

    Lampard, who is currently holidaying in Italy, refused to be drawn out when pressed to comment on the rumours by local journalists.

    "Inter? Let's wait and see. For the moment I am here on holiday with my family," said the 30-year-old.

    "My future at Chelsea? I don't know anything yet," he added.

    "Italy is a beautiful country but I am not due to meet anyone here."
