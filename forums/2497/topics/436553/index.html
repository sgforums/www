    Exclusive: Moyes willing to sell stars to raise funds for desperate Everton

     

     

     

     

     

     

     

     

     

    Jack Rodwell

     

     

     

    Everton boss David Moyes is willing to sell his stars in a desperate bid to raise cash before the transfer ­window slams shut.

    Moyes is open to offers for Jack Rodwell, Jermaine Beckford, Diniyar Bilyaletdinov, Joseph Yobo and Yakubu as word goes round that quick money is ­required at Goodison Park.

    Rodwell was mentioned in talks with Tottenham about a potential swap involving Peter Crouch – as Hotline revealed – and the England Under-21 starlet could be sold if the price is right.

    Drastic

    Moyes is being forced to ­consider drastic measures ­because owner Bill Kenwright has confirmed the badly-kept secret the Toffees are stuck in a financial mess.

    Everton may have to raise up to £10million in the next 10 days, and Moyes does NOT want to lose Phil Jagielka to Arsenal to balance the books.

    The word is out that he would sooner part with lesser lights.

    Rodwell’s sale could cover that shortfall even if it will come as a shock, while Bilyaletdinov is also up for grabs.

    Beckford, a target for Leicester and Blackburn, may also be sacrificed.

    Moyes and Kenwright had the chance to sell Yobo to Fenerbahce earlier in the ­summer and may now be ­prepared to take a smaller sum to get him off the books.

    Yakubu is another who has been available, but previously overvalued.

    Neither he nor Yobo feature in Moyes’ long-term plans. The Scot has been looking for loans and cheap deals, but the bank overdraft is limiting him – and only a ­handful of exits will give him any breathing space.

    Doncaster keeper Gary Woods is on Everton’s radar.

    The 20-year-old stopper ­began his career at Manchester United and the Toffees are considering signing him as back- up for Tim Howard after m­issing out on Andy Lonergan to Leeds. Goalkeeping coach Chris Woods has checked out his namesake on ­several ­occasions and Moyes hopes to put together a package to bring him to Merseyside.
