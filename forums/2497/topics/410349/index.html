    By Ion Danker – September 8th, 2010

    The Football Association of Singapore (FAS) is launching an immediate investigation into a brawl which broke out in a Great Eastern-Yeo’s S-League match between the Young Lions and Beijing Guoan at the Jalan Besar Stadium on Tuesday evening.

    The fight erupted in the 89th minute of the game, after Young Lions’ Afiq Yunos levelled the score at 1-1, as two separate fights broke out involving players and officials from both teams.

    Two Young Lions players, Gabriel Quak and Abdul Hameed, were sent to hospital after they were injured in the brawl.

    In a statement issued last night, the FAS said: “We take a serious view of this gross misconduct by the players and are conducting an investigation into this matter and the perpetrator will be dealt with severely.”

    Football fans Yahoo! Singapore spoke to were shocked to hear about the incident.

    Said student Galvin Ng, 18, “I never imagined a serious fight like this can happen in our local football game, it’s really embarrassing.”

    Engineer Danny Lim, 32, agreed, but was not surprised the brawl involved Beijing Guoan. “I’ve heard about their players, and how they provoke opposing teams with their late tackles and I think it’s time for FAS to act on this guys,” he said.

    The brawl forced referee Zaid Hussein to abandon the game minutes before the final whistle with the score at 1-1.
