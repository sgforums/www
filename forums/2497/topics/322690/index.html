    City target Ronaldinho returns a bigger man

    Wide boy: Ronaldinho made his return in Venezuela

     

     

    To Manchester City's horror, Ronaldinho is proving to be a bigger target than Mark Hughes' club may have anticipated. The 28-year-old made his return to football after an absence of almost four months on Saturday, playing 56 minutes of a charity match in Venezuela.

    The Brazil playmaker and Barcelona team-mate Lionel Messi each captained a team of internationals and while the football on display was often dazzling, it was Ronaldinho's midriff that really caught the eye.

    Sporting a dark shirt that did little to hide his bulge, the watching press pack dubbed him Gordinho, or Little Fatty.

    Ronaldinho - who has been linked with a move to Manchester City as well as Chelsea and the two Milan clubs was making his return from a knee injury.

    Despite the added bulk, it took the Brazilian just 11 minutes of his return to score and the two-time Fifa World Player of the Year bagged another in the second half, while also setting up two others in a 7-7 draw.

    Ronaldinho refused to comment on his future after the game but has recently stated that he would like to play in the Premier League.

    "Every player would love to play in such a competitive championship like the English league," Ronaldinho said.

    Little Fat One Replaces The Fat One – Brazilian Press

    The Brazilian press have been poking fun at AC Milan’s prospected move for Ronaldinho, saying that the apparently overweight Barcelona man will be replacing another player who has struggled to stay in shape over recent year; Ronaldo.

    During his time in Spain with Real Madrid, Ronaldo assumed the unflattering nickname of ‘El Gordo’, which translated means ‘The Fat One’.

    Ronaldinho has also been criticised over the past year or so for not being as trim as he should be, however this has not stopped Milan from wanting to sign him, and the 28-year-old is expected to complete his move to San Siro in the coming weeks.

    At the weekend Ronaldinho made his long-awaited return to football in a charity match in Venezuela, however he was looking decidedly overweight.

    The Brazilian press have been having a field day over the Brazilian’s expanded waistline, saying that he will be bringing home the “Barriga” (the Portuguese word for bacon) when he moves to Milan.

    Ronaldinho has also been compared to current Milan striker Ronaldo, and has been labelled ‘Gordinho’, which means ‘little fat one’. Little Ronnie will be hoping that this negative publicity won't cost him his move to the Rossoneri.
