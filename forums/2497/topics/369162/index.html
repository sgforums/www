    Positive news for Nasri

    Gunners midfielder could be back in six weeks' time

    Arsenal boss Arsene Wenger is hopeful midfielder Samir Nasri could be available in six weeks' time.

    On Tuesday, the France international suffered a broken leg during the club's pre-season training camp in Austria.

    Initially, it was expected Nasri would be sidelined for up to three months, but Wenger has offered a more optimistic timescale.

    The French coach has also called on long-term injury victims Tomas Rosicky and Eduardo to help fill the void left by Nasri.

    "Unfortunately he fractured his fibula and he will be out for six weeks," Wenger told the club's official website. "It was the bad news of the day.

    "He accidentally nicked the ball away from somebody having a shot on goal. Unfortunately he got kicked and broke his fibula. It's not a complicated one, it's a simple one but it will take six weeks to heal.

    "But the options are Tomas Rosicky who is back. And in [those] two months Eduardo will be back as well. We do have offensive options but we don't want to lose important players before the season starts."
