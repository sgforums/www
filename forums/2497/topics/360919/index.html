    Mourinho hints Deco could follow Quaresma to San Siro

    Ricardo Quaresma will be taken back into the Inter fold by Jose Mourinho, but only if the winger changes his attitude.

    Quaresma unsurprisingly played little part in Chelsea’s season after arriving as a January loan, and he will promptly return to Italy during the summer.

    The Portugal international rarely shows the work ethic that is a hallmark of Jose Mourinho teams, and the former Chelsea boss admits Quaresma will need to show him something different if his Inter future is to be prolonged.

    "It is likely that he will return to us," Mourinho told A Bola.

    "He must then show a different mentality to that which we saw during his first few months at Inter."

    Mourinho then hinted he might end Deco’s stay at Stamford Bridge, admitting he is thinking about signing a Portuguese player who does not play in his home country.

    "The fact that I am not thinking about other Portuguese players at the moment is not because of what has happened with Quaresma, it's due to the market situation and I don't see anyone in Portugal that interests me," he added.

    "Maybe I will look for a Portuguese player that doesn't play in Portugal."

    Carvalho on Jose's radar

    Inter Milan manager Jose Mourinho is thinking about a possible summer bid for Chelsea defender Ricardo Carvalho.

    The Portuguese centre-back played under his compatriot at Porto before moving to Stamford Bridge in a £19million move in 2004.

    And Mourinho now confesses that he would like to join forces with Carvalho, who has the UEFA Champions League experience Inter crave, for a third time.

    The 31-year-old was linked with a move to Italy last summer but a transfer failed to materialise as Mourinho settled in at Giuseppe Meazza.

    However, Mourinho has now hinted that he could look to take advantage of Carvalho's injury-hit season and the managerial uncertainty in West London.

    "Carvalho is one of the best central defenders in the world," he said in La Gazzetta dello Sport.

    "A year ago it was impossible to think of him. Now I think it is."
