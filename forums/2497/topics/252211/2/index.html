    There have been some dismal failures to add success at home to European Cup final appearances. In 1975, Leeds United lost to Bayern Munich but finished ninth in the table; in 1982, Aston Villa beat Bayern in the final but came eleventh in the league.

    This is the third consecutive season when an English finalist has been hopelessly off the pace in the Premiership. Liverpool finished 37 points behind the champions in 2005, Arsenal 24 points behind last year and Liverpool are 18 points from the top now, having played one game more.

    Challenging for trophies at home and abroad is perhaps tough psychologically as well as physically and, in the case of the latter, it has become no easier as the workload in Europe has increased. Liverpool have played 14 matches in the Champions League this season to reach the final, whereas they needed only either six or eight games to reach each of their five finals of the 1970s and 1980s.

Until recent years, leading clubs in this country played far more than their counterparts abroad because of the 22-team top division, unlimited FA Cup replays and the presence of a second domestic knockout competition, the League Cup. Cutting the league to 20 clubs and scrapping FA Cup second replays and League Cup replays has largely removed that disadvantage but the English league is still considered by many to be more physically demanding than others in Europe.
