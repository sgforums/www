So who can replace Henry?

  • omgukilledkenny
    26 Jun 07, 00:02
    Thierry Henry's Arsenal exit has sparked a flurry of speculation about who could replace the club's record goalscorer.



    We look at the leading contenders:

    MICHAEL OWEN

    The England striker has emerged as the bookies' favourite, largely because of rumours he is unsettled at Newcastle and has a reported Â£9million release clause. He is certainly in a similar league to Henry in terms of finishing ability but lacks the Frenchman's knack of scoring out of nothing and laying on goals for others.

    NICOLAS ANELKA

    The man whose controversial departure paved the way for Henry's arrival at Arsenal is being heavily tipped to make a shock return. On his day, Anelka can be as unplayable as his more feted compatriot but has failed to demonstrate the same level of consistency.

    SAMUEL ETO'O

    Arguably the only striker who could truly replace Henry at Emirates Stadium, Eto'o is Spanish football's goalscorer supreme. Henry's arrival may well signal his exit, though Arsenal would surely have made his acquisition part of the deal were they interested.

    FERNANDO TORRES

    The most exciting young striker in Spain has long been linked with a move to the Premier League and possesses all the skills to be a success at Arsenal. However, his goalscoring record in Spain is rather modest compared to Henry's remarkable statistics, while his price tag may exceed the Â£16million Arsenal are set to receive from Barcelona.

    DAVID VILLA

    A completely different type of player to Henry, Villa is nonetheless one of Spanish football's hottest properties. Like Torres, however, he may cost in excess of Â£16million and it is far from certain he possesses the attributes to adapt quickly to the pace of the Premier League.

    CARLOS TEVEZ

    After taking time to settle in England, Tevez demonstrated why he was one of the most sought-after young strikers in the world by almost single-handedly rescuing West Ham from relegation. Boasting breathtaking skill and a superb eye for goal, he would certainly not be out of place at Emirates Stadium.

    RYAN BABEL

    If Arsene Wenger is keen to continue his policy of unearthing young talent and, if Arsenal are looking for a bargain, they could do worse than take a chance on Ajax and Holland prospect Babel. The 20-year-old was one of stars of his country's European Under-21 Championship success and would command a reasonable transfer fee.

    DAVID TREZEGUET

    The only one of the contenders with a goalscoring record comparable to that of Henry, Trezeguet is coming off the back of a season in Serie B following Juventus' demotion for match-fixing. A genuine target-man, he offers different qualities to his France team-mate but nonetheless has the experience to help nurture some of the club's younger players.

    DIEGO MILITO

    The Real Zaragoza striker is coming off the back of a superb season in the Primera Liga. Like Trezeguet, he is a powerful striker, and is extremely strong in the air. But he is more likely to be a fall-back option than first-choice for Arsene Wenger

    DIEGO FORLAN

    After emerging a something of a laughing stock at Manchester United, Forlan confounded his critics by finishing top scorer in his first season in Spain. The Villarreal forward has not quite hit those heights since but is still a more than reliable finisher. However, he would still represent a real gamble, due to his previous failure in English football.
