    Rafa - Johnson OG changed game

    But Reds boss felt Gerrard was denied first-half penalty

     

     

    Rafa Benitez conceded Glen Johnson's own-goal paved the way for Liverpool's demise to Arsenal on Sunday. It was the second time in a week that the Kop faithful had witnessed their side surrender a lead after the Gunners came back to pile more misery on the Reds, as they suffered their sixth league defeat of the season.

    Liverpool's dire week began in awful fashion as their UEFA Champions League campaign came to a sorry end when Fiorentina secured a 2-1 win on Wednesday. And Sunday's identical score-line was the first time since Robert Pires' winner for Arsenal in 2003 that the Reds relinquished a lead in the league on home soil.

    The Reds were 1-0 up through Dirk Kuyt's strike at and seemingly cruising going into the interval, but the Merseysiders crumbled in the second-half as Andrey Arshavin sealed the win with his fifth Anfield strike in two seasons after Johnson put the ball in his own net.

    Benitez's side now trail fourth-placed Aston Villa by five points and are a serious doubt to regain their Champions League status, which according to former boss Graeme Souness would see them go into financial meltdown.

    Positve

    But Benitez was trying to focus on the positives and insists his side still stand a good chance of a top-four finish, so long as they play like they did in the first-half and not the second. "I think the own goal changed everything," the Spanish tactician told

    Sky Sports

    "The confidence changed and then we started making mistakes - we conceded the second goal too early too - but the first half was really, really good and we were very pleased and we thought it could be even better. "But the second-half changed and then they were playing with more confidence and we were playing with less confidence."

    Benitez did lament Liverpool's failure to get a first-half penalty when Steven Gerrard was sent tumbling by a clumsy William Gallas challenge in the 12th minute. "I think it was a penalty," said the Reds boss, whose team have now won just three times in their last 15 games.

    "It doesn't matter if the ball goes away because it at the end of the day it doesn't matter if you foul someone in the box - it's a penalty."

     

    Must-win

    Wigan are the next team up for Liverpool on Wednesday and Benitez knows his side must bounce back quickly. He added: "We know we have another important game on Wednesday, we have some players coming back so we have to keep working hard and be ready for the next game.

    And the Reds tactician still maintains his side are on course to qualify for the Champions League next term despite Sunday's reverse. "I think again top-four is the same target, it's a long way (until the season ends), we have to keep going, keep winning the games we can."
