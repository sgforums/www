    WORLD CUP 2010: England suffer shattering blow with captain Rio Ferdinand ruled out of tournament

    England's World Cup plans have been thrown into chaos with Rio Ferdinand ruled out of the tournament with a knee injury.

    Ferdinand was taken to hospital in South Africa for a scan after sustaining an injury to his left knee during the final moments of Friday's training session in Rustenburg.

    He left hospital on crutches and has since returned to England's training headquarters.

    Sportsmail understands Ferdinand will be sidelined for at least three weeks, ending any hopes of playing in the finals.

    Tottenham defender Michael Dawson is on his way to South Africa having been called up as an emergency standby.

    Ruled out: Rio Ferdinand leaves hospital on crutches

    The huge blow comes just eight days before England's World Cup campaign kicks off against the United States.

    Ledley King and Jamie Carragher will both now fancy their chances of a place in England's defence for their World Cup opener on June 12.

    Put through his paces: Rio Ferdinand taking part in England's first training session in South Africa

    Capello earlier told reporters: 'The bad news is in the last moment of the training, Rio suffered a problem with the knee.

    'He has just gone to hospital for a scan. We hope it will not be so bad an injury but we have Michael Dawson on call.

    'It came from a minor tackle during the training. How bad is it? We will have to wait and see.

    Worry for England: Ferdinand is having a scan on his injured knee

     

    Vice-captain Steven Gerrard will now wear the captain's armband in South Africa.

    Ferdinand had been optimistic of enjoying an injury-free World Cup after a season dogged by problems at Manchester United.

    He made just 21 appearances for his club this season, starting only 13 Premier League games, in a season disrupted by a back injury.

     

    Reasons to be concerned: Ferdinand was dogged by injury problems last season with Manchester United

    However he had trained with no setbacks during England's two-week camp in Austria, and also came through the friendly internationals with Mexico and Japan unscathed.

    Goalkeeper David James also missed training on Friday but Capello admitted to being much less concerned with that situation, insisting the 39-year-old had 'only a slight knee problem'.

    Training: Rio Ferdinand and Gareth Barry with the rest of the England squad in Rustenburg on Friday

    England play their first World Cup match next weekend against the United States in Rustenburg on June 12.

    They then face Algeria and Slovenia in their next two group games, on June 18 and June23.

    WORLD CUP 2010: Didier Drogba set to miss finals after breaking arm in Ivory Coast's friendly with Japan

     

    Didier Drogba is set to miss out on the World Cup after breaking his arm in Ivory Coast's friendly match with Japan.

    Drogba collapsed under a heavy challenge with Marcus Tanaka, just minutes after the opposition defender had deflected the Chelsea striker's free-kick into his own net. 

    Team spokesman Eric Kacou confirmed that doctors had diagnosed the arm fracture after he was taken off 15 minutes into the Elephant's 2-0 friendly win.

    Kacou said: 'It is a fracture in his right arm. The doctor hasn't said yet whether he can play in the World Cup.'

    Agony: Drogba appeared to be in some pain straight after the tackle from Tanaka

    And manager Sven Goran Eriksson conceded that losing Drogba would be a huge blow to his side's chances in South Africa.

    He said: 'It's very worrying, he is our captain, one of the best strikers in the world. It [his challenge] wasn't bad or stupid. He didn't want to hurt anyone. Football is a game of contact '

    The injury is devastating news for the team as Drogba was in sparkling form for Chelsea last season.

    He scored 37 goals in 44 appearances as Carlo Ancelotti's men landed the Premier League and FA Cup double.

    Ouch: Striker Drogba feels the full force of Tanaka's challenge

    Early exit: Drogba is helped off after picking himself up off the floor

    His absence in South Africa would seriously hinder his side's chances of qualifying from Group G - the 'group of death' - which also contains Brazil, North Korea and Portugal.

    Ivory Coast won the game 2-0 after Manchester City defender Kolo Toure adding a late second for the Ivorians.

    Eriksson's side open their campaign against Portugal on Tuesday before facing Brazil and North Korea.

    Pirlo could miss World Cup

    Injury could force Italy midfielder to sit out tournament

    Italy midfielder Andrea Pirlo is in doubt for the World Cup after sustaining a calf injury on Wednesday.

    The AC Milan player is resting at home after damaging the calf muscle in his left leg during the Azzurri's 2-1 friendly loss to Mexico on Wednesday night.

    The Italian Football Federation (FIGC) have confirmed that Pirlo, who will sit out Saturday's friendly against Switzerland, will fly to South Africa with the rest of the squad and continue to undergo evaluation ahead of Italy's opener against Paraguay on 14th June.

    Italy's doctor Enrico Castellacci also said the recovery time for such injuries is between 15 to 20 days, meaning Pirlo could be fit for Italy's second group match against New Zealand on June 20.

    Coach Marcello Lippi is already uncertain over the fitness of winger Mauro Camoranesi, who has a knee injury, and the loss of Pirlo could prove a severe blow to the holders.

    Pirlo was named man of the match in the 2006 World Cup final after scoring a penalty in their shoot-out triumph over France.

    The midfielder also landed Italy's first goal of the tournament that year, in their 2-0 win against Ghana.

    Mikel latest Chelsea star to miss World Cup finals

    Nigeria suffered a blow just under a week away fromn the start of the World Cup as they announced that star midfielder Mikel John Obi had been left out of the squad because he did not want to aggravate a knee injury.

    The 23-year-old Chelsea star - who follows club team-mates Michael Ballack, Michael Essien and possibly Didier Drogba in missing out on the finals because of injury - is likely to be replaced by one of the seven players dropped from the squad at their training camp in England last Monday.

    "We have dropped Mikel from the World Cup squad after he told the team he did not want to put his career at risk as his knee injury has yet to fully heal after a recent surgery," disclosed top team official Emmanuel Attah on Saturday morning.

    Nigeria are drawn in Group B along with two-time champions Argentina, Euro 2004 winners Greece and 2002 World Cup semi-finalists South Korea and play their first match on June 12 against Argentina.

    World Cup 2010: Arjen Robben Injured In Netherlands Friendly Win; Will Not Travel With Squad To South Africa

    The Flying Dutchman had to abandon the game early...

    Arjen Robben injured (PROSHOTS)

    Netherlands winger Arjen Robben had to limp off with an injury during the 6-1 friendly win over Hungary.

    The Bayern Munich man came on as a second half substitute and scored just past the hour mark and again ten minutes from time, but failed to finish the game when he was taken off four minutes from the end.

    Robben attempted a backheel but crumbled to the ground in pain as he clutched his left leg in pain with what looked like a suspected hamstring pull. He was able to get back on to his feet to trudge off the pitch with the assistance of the medical team.

    There were worrying scenes as Robben went straight down the tunnel into the dressing room accompanied by Dirk Kuyt instead of just getting treatment on the bench.

    According to Dutch TV, Robben has since been taken to hospital for MRI scans on his hamstring and Oranje coach Bert van Marwijk has confirmed that the 26-year-old winger will not travel with the rest of the squad to South Africa tonight.

    Van Marwijk added in the post-match conference, "It doesn't look good with Robben."

    Extensive tests on Robben's condition will only be carried out by doctors tomorrow [Sunday].
