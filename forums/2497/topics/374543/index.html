    Man City owners keen on Barcelona striker Henry

    Manchester City's owners are keen on Barcelona striker Thierry Henry in a swap for Robinho.

    The News of the World says City's Robinho and Barca striker Henry will be the subject of further talks in the January transfer window after the Spanish champs' move for Robinho last month could not be completed before the deadline.

    City will hold out for around £30million for the player they brought from Real Madrid last year. But the prospect of a swap deal involving Henry would appeal to both parties.

    Henry, 32, is exactly the kind of global superstar City owner Sheikh Mansour bin Zayed likes to target.

    Attempts were made to get him last season but Henry blocked the move to stay with Barcelona and win the Champions League.

    Barcelona willing to do cash deal for Man City's Robinho

    Barcelona hope to do a cash deal for Manchester City star Robinho in January.

    The Daily Mail says Barca would be happy to bring Robinho into their squad mid-season, not least because he is still eligible for Champions League football.

    But the sticking point will inevitably be the fee. The Catalan club would offer around £15million, but City's owner, Sheik Mansour, is unlikely to sanction such a deal after paying £32m for him. However, the clubs maintain a close relationship after City played in Barca's pre-season tournament last month and a deal will be discussed.

    Over the coming months, City will find themselves in a similar position to the one they put Everton in over the Joleon Lescott affair: having to choose between keeping an unhappy player or allowing him the move he desires.
