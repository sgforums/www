    Emmanuel Adebayor 'to ask for Arsenal move'

    Arsenal face a battle to hang on to Emmanuel Adebayor as speculation increases that he has instructed his agent to facilitate a move to AC Milan. The Togo international has already begun talks with the Premier League club about a new contract that would improve the terms of his existing £2 million-a-year deal, which runs until 2012.

    Further discussions are planned in the next few weeks but reports in Italy suggest that Adebayor will use the next meeting to tell the club, via his French agent Stephane Courbis, that he would like to move to the San Siro.

    The Italian giants have been very public in their pursuit of the striker, with club sources suggesting that they are willing to pay £32 million for a player who scored 30 goals last season. "We wrote to Arsenal to ask about the availability of Emmanuel Adebayor, but we didn't get a reply," admitted Milan vice-president Adriano Galliani, who has Adebayor top of his shopping list along with Chelsea's Didier Drogba and Barcelona's Samuel Eto'o.

    While Adebayor has given mixed messages about his future, Arsene Wenger is adamant that the striker will not be sold. "They just have to call me and they will get the answer they deserve," the Arsenal manager said.

    Meanwhile, Arsenal's search for a chief executive has taken a twist with their demand for a skilled networker who can help kill off Fifa's controversial 'six-plus-five' principle.

    The club view the proposal of Fifa president Sepp Blatter, which would limit the number of foreign players in a team to five, as a potential "disaster", given the continental composition of Wenger's squad.

    Although the European Commission say Blatter's vision is discriminatory and illegal, it appeared to move a step closer following the overwhelming backing of football's world governing body in Sydney a fortnight ago.
