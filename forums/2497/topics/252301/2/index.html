    Barton arrested for alleged assault of Dabo

    Manchester City's bleak conclusion to the season took another turn for the worse last night when Joey Barton was arrested in connection with the alleged training-ground attack on his team-mate Ousmane Dabo. Barton was interviewed at Stretford police station after Dabo made a complaint that he was attacked by Barton at the start of the month.

    A police spokesman said: "A 24-year-old man has been arrested in connection with a Section 20 assault following an incident when a 30-year-old man was assaulted at Carrington." Barton has been bailed until July 11 while the police make further inquiries and it is thought they will now interview several of Barton's team-mates and possibly Stuart Pearce, who was sacked as manager on Monday. The club postponed a disciplinary hearing against Barton that was scheduled for yesterday.
