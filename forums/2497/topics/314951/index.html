    Arsene Wenger has admitted he's less than sure whether to stick with William Gallas as team captain next season.

    The Frenchman, who took over the skipper's role from the departed Thierry Henry, has received much criticism of late for the way he has led his side.

    Both Arsenal fans and football pundits have accused the defender of bowing down too quickly in the face of pressure and failing to adequately rally the troops when it mattered most in key matches.

    Asked whether Gallas would be his long-term skipper, Arsenal boss Arsene Wenger said he hadn't planned on reallocating the captain's armband before the end of this campaign, but that next season was a lot less clear.

    “I can’t answer that question," said the Emirates boss. "I want to analyse the season, after the season has finished. At the moment, he will be my captain until the end of the season. I believe William has learned a lot this year," Wenger continued. "I can only say his commitment and desire to do well was exceptional. I don’t want to go into any other individual assessment. I made the decision to make him captain and I have to stand up for that decision.”
