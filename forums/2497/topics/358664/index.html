    Wenger: Arsenal is my club

    Arsene Wenger has moved to end recent speculation over his future as Arsenal manager.

    The Frenchman has cut a more frustrated figure on the sidelines in recent seasons as he looks to close the gap on Manchester United and Liverpool at the top of the Premier League.

    The Gunners have now gone three seasons without winning a major trophy and Wenger is determined not to make it four.

    The 59-year-old has recently been tipped to take over at Real Madrid in the summer but Wenger insists that Arsenal are the only club for him and he remains as determined as ever to bring UEFA Champions League glory to the Emirates.

    "I believe Arsenal is my club because it is my club, it is the only club," he said

    "I believe in the potential of this club and want to give this club the opportunity to win the Champions League. That is why I work so hard."

    But Wenger, who has a contract until 2011, adds that success in Europe is not the only reason why he is set to prolong his stay with the Gunners.

    "My obsession is to play as well as possible and win as much as possible," he explained.

    "I don't think that winning the Champions League is the sole motivation to continue as a coach."
