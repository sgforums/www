    Internazionale manager Jose Mourinho has taken a swipe at his former employer Roman Abramovich, claiming the Chelsea owner is overly possessive of the Premier League club.

    The Special One left Stamford Bridge last September falling out with the Russian billionaire over transfer policies, ending a trophy-littered three-season stint in west London.

    The Portuguese has since succeeded Roberto Mancini – who is in the running to take over at Chelsea – as coach of Inter, and is reportedly keen on bringing Blues stars Frank Lampard and Didier Drogba to San Siro.

    But the former Porto boss shifted his sights to a different type of target yesterday, firing a brisk volley at Abramovich regarding his non-existent emotional ties to the Blues, while praising Inter president Massimo Moratti’s love for the Nerazzurri.

    “Moratti has been breathing football since he was a child,” said Mourinho.
     
    “But he never speaks about ‘his’ Inter. To him the team belongs to the fans.

    “Abramovich hasn’t got history or family history behind him.”
