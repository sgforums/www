    Lucas Leiva: Some of my Liverpool critics will never shut up

    Brazilian thanks Masch for helping him improve but accepts he'll always have his doubters...


    Liverpool midfielder Lucas Leiva believes that playing alongside Javier Mascherano has helped him improve as a player.


    Despite that though, he feels that however much he improves a certain section of his critics will never give him credit for his work on the pitch.

    "I'm trying to improve all the time but I'm feeling good on the pitch. I think I've improved a lot since last season and hopefully I'll keep going," he told the Reds' official website.
     
    "I think things might always be more difficult for me at Liverpool, to be honest, but I'm trying to prove I can do well for myself and my team-mates. The real supporters of Liverpool know this and support me a lot.
     
    "When I came to Liverpool I came with a challenge of winning titles and making history for the club. Hopefully I can do that.
     
    "I'm playing almost every game now. I have a good link with Javier Mascherano. I understand the way he plays and he helps me a lot. I think it's good for the team that we can keep the balance."

    And the Brazilian insists that Liverpool's players and fans must move on from their Champions League exit and focus on winning the Europa League.

    "We have to move on. We didn't do enough to get to the next stage and so now we have to concentrate on the Europa League and getting into a better position domestically," he added.
     
    "We'll try to win the Europa League because it's still an important competition. Some good teams play in it and we'll try to get a title for ourselves."
