Cristiano Ronaldo registers 'CR9' trademark

  • omgukilledkenny
    15 Jan 09, 22:38

    Cristiano Ronaldo, the Manchester United footballer, has registered CR9 as a trademark - fuelling rumours he is to leave the club for Real Madrid.

    Ronaldo, who plays with the squad No 7 for Manchester United, has reportedly been promised the No 9 shirt by the Spanish club.

    The digits "CR9" were copyrighted last month in his Portuguese homeland to be used on merchandise or as the name for a restaurant or bar.

    CR9 was registered by his agent Jorge Mendes at the National Institute of Industrial Property in Lisbon. Ronaldo has already registered CR7.

    The 23-year-old has long been linked with a move to Real Madrid. He has revealed that he is learning Spanish and also has a sponsorship deal with a Portuguese bank giving them exclusive marketing rights if he goes to Spain.

    Yesterday it was claimed that Ronaldo, the Fifa World Player of the Year, had signed a "secret" deal to join Madrid next year.

    Madrid president Ramon Calderon is said to have sanctioned a transfer fee of £77 million - rising to £105m with clauses - with Ronaldo picking up £11m per year, plus £7.25m for his agent.

    The huge £210,000 a week wages would dwarf the £120,000 he is paid at Old Trafford and Madrid are said to be encouraged that the weakening of the pound against the euro puts them in a stronger position than last summer.
