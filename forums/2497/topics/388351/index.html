    Joe Cole To Quit Chelsea And Join Manchester City - Report

    Joe Cole is considering quitting Chelsea and joining Manchester City on a free transfer following a disagreement in his contract talks, according to The Mirror.

    Joe Cole

     

     

    The 28-year-old has been in negotiations with the London club for some time now, but has reportedly been unable to see eye-to-eye with the Stamford Bridge outfit on a new deal.

    Cole is believed to have demanded a hike of £50,000, which would put him on the list of the club's second-tier earners such as Nicolas Anelka, Michael Ballack and Michael Essien, whose wages are believed to be in the region of £120,000-a-week.

    But as the Blues are said to be holding firm on their offer of £100,000-a-week, it is claimed that the England international will look toward greener pastures at the end of the season.

    It is therefore claimed that City will look to capitalise by offering Cole a £5 million signing-on fee and weekly pay of £150,000, which would put the midfielder among the Premier League's best-paid players.

    Joe Cole May Leave Chelsea Unless He's Given First-Team Assurance - Report

    The winger could leave Stamford Bridge in search of regular football...

    EPL: Joe Cole, Chelsea

     

    Chelsea attack-minded midfielder Joe Cole is fast approaching the conclusion of his current deal that expires in the summer and is reportedly hesitent in extending terms as he is seeking assurances from manager Carlo Ancelotti that he will become a first-team regular.

    Money was reportedly the initial sticking point as the Blues management were deemed to be offering £30,000 per week less than Cole's reported £130,000 per week demands, however, London's Evening Standard reports that the Englishman is eager for first-team football.

    Cole's playing career in west London has oft been beset by injury, and the 28-year-old has only just returned to action following a long-term sidelining due to sustaining a complex knee problem in September.

    Ancelotti's decision to bench Cole during a Champions League tussle with Atletico Madrid in the group stage is alleged to upset Cole, who was further saddened as he remained an unused substitute.

    Meanwhile, reports have emerged that AC Milan have joined Manchester City in the list of Cole's admirers, as both clubs moniter the developing situation with interest.
