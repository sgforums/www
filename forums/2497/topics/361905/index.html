    Chelsea midfielder Michael Ballack believes Blues are the strongest team in Europe

    Feeling blue: Ballack missed out on Chelsea's FA Cup winning celebrations in 2007 but the German hopes to join the party this time round at Wembley

     

     

    Barcelona's Champions League triumph will only serve to intensify Chelsea's nagging mood of missed opportunity, and Michael Ballack maintains there was nobody stronger than them in Europe this year.

    Guus Hiddink's team nullified the Barca threat for 180 minutes of their semi-final against the Spanish champions, only to be beaten on away goals after Andres Iniesta's equaliser in stoppage time of the second leg at Stamford Bridge.

    Ballack said before Saturday's FA Cup final against Everton: 'We've played a good season with a lot of good performances. In the Champions League especially, I think we were one of the strongest, if not the strongest, team. But we didn't get to that final, so this is our last chance to win something.'

    Ballack's career has become almost as notable for its near misses as its successes. He has lost his two Champions League finals, was a runner-up at Euro 2008 and missed Germany's defeat in the 2002 World Cup final through suspension.

    He was also injured two years ago when Chelsea beat Manchester United in the first FA Cup final at the new Wembley. The only trophy he has won in three years in London is the Carling Cup in 2007.
    'These things happen and you can't control them. It's just bad luck,' he said. 'While you are obviously happy for the team, there is also some disappointment.

    'But since the FA Cup final in 2007, those feelings have changed and now I know I helped contribute a lot on the way to the final, even if I didn't get to play at Wembley, so I definitely feel part of the victory.'

    Ballack is by no means certain to start at Wembley. Hiddink has favoured a midfield trio of John Mikel Obi, Michael Essien and Frank Lampard in the three games since Barcelona.

    Mikel will regard Chelsea’s season as a failure if his team do not lift the Cup.

    The Nigerian said: ‘It has to be if we don’t win anything. We have to win.’
