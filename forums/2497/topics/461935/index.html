    Rafa Benitez was booed as he took his place in the Chelsea dugout for the first time.

    Benitez has stepped in as interim manager after ruthless Russian owner Roman Abramovich sacked Roberto Di Matteo following the midweek defeat to Juventus.

    Fans do not want the former Liverpool boss at Stamford Bridge and made no secret of their feelings before kick-off as the Blues faced Manchester City.

    Home supporters in the Matthew Harding Stand sang 'we don't want you here' and paid tribute to their Champions League-winning former boss by chanting 'there's only one Di Matteo.'

    And in the 16th-minute, huge sections of the support united in applause to celebrate Di Matteo, who wore the No 16 in his playing days.

    Benitez got the better of the Blues during his time in charge of the Merseysiders, knocking them out of the Champions League twice.

    And at his official unveiling at Stamford Bridge, Benitez was grilled on the explosive comments he made against the London club during his time as Liverpool boss.

    He said: 'I want to be honest. If I said this or that, I have to analyse the context. We were playing against Chelsea in the semi-finals of the Champions League. If I am a fan I want to see the manager fighting for my club. I don't see this as a lack of respect for Chelsea's fans, just a manager defending his team.'
