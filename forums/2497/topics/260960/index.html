    Reports in Spain claim Real Madrid are aiming to agree a deal to sign Liverpool goalkeeper Jerzy Dudek.



    The Poland international is leaving Anfield when his contract expires at the end of the month following six seasons with The Reds.

    He lost his place in Rafa Benitez's first team to Jose Reina and has opted to move on to preserve his international position.

    Real Betis have been linked with the 34-year-old, but speculation in Spain has suggested Primera Liga leaders Madrid also want Dudek.

    Iker Casillas is the undisputed No.1 at the Bernabeu, but his understudy Diego Lopez is being tipped to join Villarreal over the summer.

    That would leave Real requiring cover for Casillas and Dudek has emerged as a live option for the club.

    According to AS, a deal has been agreed with Dudek's agent, although there is a split in the Real camp about the merits of signing the former Feyenoord keeper.

    Dudek considers Real Madrid move

    Liverpool goalkeeper Jerzy Dudek has confirmed that he is weighing up an offer to join European heavyweights Real Madrid.

    The Polish goalkeeper is out of contract and has been linked with a move to the likes of Benfica and Real Betis since Liverpool manager Rafael Benitez decided not to renew his contract at Anfield.

    Since his heroics in the penalty shoot-out that helped Liverpool win the Champions League in 2005, Dudek, 34, has only made 12 first team appearances.

    The arrival of Jose Reina pushed him to number two but he would play understudy to another Spaniard at the Bernabeu, where Iker Casillas dons the gloves.

    However, Dudek said: "After today's press speculation, I must admit that I have received an offer from one of the greatest clubs in the world, if not the greatest one.

    "I was able to keep it in the dark for a long time, because the first contact was established a dozen or so days ago.

    "The offer is an honour for me. I am seriously considering it now. As for now, I can say that a few details have already been agreed upon."
