    Carlo: My boys are still tops

     

     

     

    CARLO ANCELOTTI saw Chelsea slip up at Spurs but roared: This changes nothing. Ancelotti's men blew a chance to stretch their lead at the top by losing 2-1 at White Hart Lane.

    Manchester United are just a point behind after they beat deadly rivals City in the early kick-off. But the Stamford Bridge boss insisted: "The title is still in our hands.

    "We don't have to be afraid — we just have to look to the next game against Stoke. "I don't think the players were nervous, we knew how important this game was. "We didn't do so good but Tottenham played very well, at the same level as they did against Arsenal. "We were a little bit down, but this is football."

    Spurs boss Harry Redknapp meanwhile claimed his side were worthy winners — and exceeded their performance in midweek. He said: "We deserved, there's no doubt about that. I thought we were even better today than we were against Arsenal.

    "That first 30 minutes or so we completely dominated and then we had some great chances in the second half. "We missed three or four unbelievable opportunities to really kill them off completely."

    Despite opening up a two-point lead in fourth over fellow Champions League rivals Manchester City, Redknapp is refusing to get carried away.

    He added: "I still think it's going to be close. Villa and Liverpool have it all to do to catch us but certainly Man City have still got a great chance. We have still got to go to City."
