    Edin to Eastlands! Manchester City closing in on £30m January move for long-term target Dzeko

    EXCLUSIVE

     

    City's target man: Dzeko is expected to cost the club £30million

     

     

    Manchester City were last night in talks with Edin Dzeko’s agent over the fine details of a £30million transfer from German club Wolfsburg.

    Sportsmail understands that Wolfsburg have finally been persuaded to talk over a deal that would include a down payment of €35m (£30m) as well as some performance-related add-ons.

    The deal is not yet set in stone and cannot be completed until the transfer window opens next week across Europe.

    However, the fact that Dzeko’s agent — recently a guest of City at the Manchester derby — has travelled to England for talks is indicative of how confident City are of completing what is likely be the biggest transfer of January.

    City tried all last summer to sign the giant Bosnian striker but were thwarted by the huge fee placed on his head by the Bundesliga club that is owned by car giants Volkswagen.

    Indeed, as recently as last week, City were being told they would have to pay £38m for him.

    However, City coach Roberto Mancini has implored the board at Eastlands to do all they can to sign a player he feels could make the difference between winning the Barclays Premier League and finishing in the top four.

    What is helping Mancini’s cause is the fact that Dzeko himself has made it clear he wants to come to City. As Sportsmail revealed before Christmas, he took the extraordinary step of writing to City confirming his interest back in August.

    Edin Dzeko Could Be Decisive Factor In Manchester City Winning The Premier League - Roberto Mancini

    Italian boss believes Bosnian striker will be pivotal in title race

     

     
    Edin Dzeko

     

     

     

    Manchester City manager Roberto Mancini has admitted that Wolfsburg striker Edin Dzeko could be the missing piece of the jigsaw as City strive to win the Premier League, according to reports in Germany.

    Mancini has publicly stated his interest in signing the Bosnian, but City will have to pay £34 million to land the highly-rated striker, who scored against neighbours Manchester United in the Champions League last year.

    Dzeko has made no secret of his desire to leave the Bundesliga side, and on Tuesday Goal.com UK revealed that the clubs were close to agreeing a deal for the 24-year-old, despite interest from AC Milan who were his favoured club.

    The move could see a swift exit from Eastlands for players struggling to make an impact in the City first-team, such as Roque Santa Cruz and Emmanuel Adebayor -the ex-Arsenal striker who recently turned down a loan move to relegation-threatened West Ham United.

    With Manchester City currently joint top of the Premier League, Mancini believes that Dzeko could be the difference between winning the league title this season or falling short.

    "We have the chance to win the league this year and the decisive factor could be Edin Dzeko. This player can decide titles and that is why we want him,’’ the manager told Wolfsburger Allgemeine Zeitung.

    Wolfsburg are set to make a huge profit on him after signing him from FK Teplice for £3 million in 2007. A move to City for £34m would also break the Bundesliga transfer record, which was set when Mario Gomez moved from Stuttgart to Bayern Munich in May 2009 for a fee estimated to have been just under £30m.
