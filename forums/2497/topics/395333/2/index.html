    Mike Dean demoted from Premier League duty after handling Manchester United's controversial defeat to Chelsea

    Under fire: Referee Mike Dean


    Clanger: Linesman Simon Beck fails to flag Drogba offside


    Flashpoint: Didier Drogba scores his controversial winner at Old Trafford

     




    Controversial referee Mike Dean has been grounded from Barclays Premier League action this weekend.

    The Cheshire official has come under fire for his last two performances and has been demoted to Championship duty.

    Sir Alex Ferguson was critical of Dean's failure to award Manchester United a penalty in their 2-1 loss to Chelsea, calling it a 'poor, poor performance'.

    And the week before, Dean awarded Blackburn a questionable spot-kick at Burnley. Dean will officiate Bristol City's game against Swansea on Saturday.

    The assistant referee who blundered over Didier Drogba's goal at Old Trafford on Saturday has also been demoted.

    Simon Beck, who failed to flag Drogba offside, sits out the next set of Premier League fixtures and will join Dean running the line at Bristol City.
