    Arsenal boss Arsene Wenger is keen on prising away Alberto Aquilani from Roma and towards the Emirates, according to Tuttomercatoweb.com.

    The Italian midfielder has raised a number of eyebrows with Juventus, Real Madrid, Barcelona and Inter all showing great interest in the player.

    Aquilani has been having problems regarding the renewal of his contract at Roma as both player and club have failed to reach an agreement. This, as well as Roma's on-going crisis in Serie A has led to rife speculation that the Italian international wants out.

    Tuttomercatoweb.com claims Wenger is standing by and ready pounce at the first sign of an exit. The Giallorossi may be forced to sell up in order to curb mounting debts with Italian bank Unicredit and a January exit could be on the horizon.

    Arsenal still haven't replaced Mathieu Flamini who left for Milan in the summer. Aquilani will represent a good deal for the Gunners boss as he is young and one of Europe's hottest midfielders.

    Roma have played hardball so far but with the club and player at constant logger heads over a new deal as well as the abject form at Trigoria so far, Aquilani to Arsenal could take off sooner rather than later.
