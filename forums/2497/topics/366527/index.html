    I Want To Join Barcelona - David Villa

    David Villa has told Barcelona, in a face-to-face meeting, that he wants to join them - and now the Catalan club are set to make a push to secure his services.

    The Valencia striker maintained his silence over a move away from Mestalla throughout the Confederations Cup, but is now reported to have made his intentions clear.

    Blaugrana technical secretary Txiki Begiristain flew to Asturias on Monday to speak directly to Villa and was told in no uncertain terms that the player wanted a deal to be done.

    "I want to join Barcelona," Villa is quoted as telling the Barca chief by Sport.

    Valencia admitted that there have been talks, but the rumour mill has since gone into over-drive regarding the type of deal that could be put forward to try and convince them to sell.

    If Barcelona meet los Che's asking price then a move could be done, but the involvement of loan players is said to complicate matters and could delay any move.

    Villa has yet to return to Valencia after going back to Asturias to be with his family, and until he reports for pre-season training there will be no outcome.

    Lucas Brown, Goal.com

    VALENCIA DENY VILLA TALKS

    Manuel Llorente has dismissed suggestions that Valencia are in negotiations with Barcelona over selling David Villa.

     

    The striker looked set to join Real Madrid earlier this summer but has more recently been linked with European champions Barcelona.

     

    Reports in the Spanish media on Thursday morning quoted Villa as saying he would be interested in a switch to Camp Nou.

     

    Valencia president Llorente has now moved to reassure supporters, saying there have been no talks with Barca regarding Villa.

     

    "Valencia is not negotiating and has not negotiated with Barcelona, at all," he told Europa Press.

     

    Llorente does not yet know whether Barca have spoken with Villa and his agent, adding: "That I will find out when I meet people directly."

     

    David Silva has also attracted attention from some of the continent's finest clubs but Llorente is confident of keeping both the midfielder and Villa.

     

    He said: "They are players who are wanted in international football and it is not strange that important teams want them. We are used to it."

    Real have given up on signing Villa


    Real Madrid have confirmed they have abandoned their pursuit of highly-rated Valencia striker David Villa after clinching a deal to sign Olympique Lyonnais forward Karim Benzema.

    Benzema agreed a €35m (£29.7m) deal with the Spanish club on Wednesday and Madrid's director general Jorge Valdano said that his capture, along with that of Kaka and Cristiano Ronaldo, means they don't need another goalscorer.

    "Benzema fulfils the goal of having a striker, a goal scorer who brings a lot of presence and aggression to the forward line," Valdano said.

    When asked if the French international's arrival had put an end to their bid for Villa the Real chief said: "Benzema and Villa both have these [goal scoring] characteristics so the answer to your question is yes."

    Last month it appeared that Madrid were on the verge of signing Villa from cash-strapped Los Che but the club decided not to sell their star player at the last moment and Real later declared the deal to be "100% finished".
