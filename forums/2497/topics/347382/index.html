    Barcelona swoop for Torres - Spanish giants place Liverpool striker on summer short list

     

    Head boy: Fernando Torres (right) puts Liverpool 1-0 up against Chelsea as Blues defender Alex dives in

     

    Barcelona are lining up a summer swoop for Liverpool's star striker Fernando Torres. The Spanish giants have placed the Euro 2008 winner on their short list for next season as a potential replacement for Thierry Henry and Samuel Eto'o.  

    Henry will be 32 this summer while Barcelona have yet to open contract talks with Eto'o who current deal ends in 2010.  

    Eto'o almost left the Nou Camp last year and later this month Barca sporting director Txiki Begiristain and coach Pep Guardiola are scheduled to meet to discuss new additions for next season.  

    There is a growing feeling that either Henry or Eto'o will leave and Torres is greatly admired by the Barcelona management.  

    Joan Laporta, the Barcelona president, met Torres at the recent FIFA World Player gala dinner. It was an informal chat with Barcelona superstars Leo Messi and Xavi also present but it was seen as an important meeting as a basis for any future negotiations.  

    Inter Milan are keen on Eto'o and Barcelona are aware of Liverpool's precarious financial position.

    Torres planning long stay with Reds

    Fernando Torres has pledged his long-term future to Liverpool despite reports in Spain that Barcelona are weighing up a big-money move.

    Speaking ahead of Spain's friendly with England on Wednesday, Torres insists he is happy to stay at Anfield and cannot imagine himself playing for "any other team".

    He said: "I don't see myself playing for Real Madrid, or Barcelona or Chelsea or any other team.

    "I see myself playing for Liverpool. I have a long contract and that's that."
