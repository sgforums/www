    Luis Fabiano's agent has claimed AC Milan have made an official approach to Sevilla for the Brazil striker.

    Milan are in the market for a forward following Kaka's £56million departure to Real Madrid and had made Wolfsburg's Edin Dzeko their No.1 target.

    But with Wolfsburg adamant the Bosnian will remain in Germany, it seems Fabiano has emerged as Milan's top transfer priority.

    Fabiano's representative, Jose Fuentes, told Sky Italia: "Milan have made an official offer to Sevilla for Luis Fabiano.

    Desire

    "The player has already said he wants to play for Milan next season. I don't know how much the bid amounts to, but there is this important and official offer on the table.

    "Now it is up to the Rossoneri and Sevilla to reach an agreement, then my client and I will speak to Milan.

    "The buy-out clause on his contract is €30m (£25.7m), but Luis Fabiano has concluded a four-year cycle with Sevilla and now Milan represent a big opportunity for him.

    "I repeat, the player has publicly stated he wants to join Milan, now it is up to the two clubs to strike a deal.

    "I think it will take two to three weeks, but no more than that."

    A transfer to Milan for Fabiano would seemingly end any possibility that Emmanuel Adebayor could leave Arsenal for San Siro.

    Fabiano pleads for Milan move

    Luis Fabiano has reiterated that he hopes to seal a transfer from Sevilla to AC Milan this summer.

     
    Brazilian hopeful he will secure move to Italian giants

    Fabiano has pleaded with Sevilla to accept Milan's approach, despite reports suggesting the offer is below the Spanish club's asking price.

    "I know that Milan have made an offer and I would like for Sevilla to consider it," he told ABC.

    "Until now I've preferred to stay on the sidelines, because Milan had not made me any proposals, but now I understand this is an incredible opportunity for me.

    "I am happy at Sevilla, but I realise that at the age of 28 there will be few other chances like this for me. Milan are one of the best clubs in the world."

    The Italian giants are keen to bring in an attacker for the new season as they look to replace Kaka, who has signed for Real Madrid.

    Further speculation suggests Real attacker Klaas-Jan Huntelaar is also a target of Milan, with the Rossoneri eyeing a loan deal.
