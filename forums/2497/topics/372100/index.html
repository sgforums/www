    Arsenal boss Wenger: Wilshere proof we didn't need midfield signing

    Arsenal boss Arsene Wenger says the emergence of Jack Wilshere is proof he was right not to go out and sign a new midfielder this year.

    He told the Daily Mail: "When Cesc Fabregas was a teenager, I would play him with Patrick Vieira and it did not work. Gilberto Silva and Vieira worked, Fabregas and Silva worked, but I could not play Fabregas and Vieira. But Fabregas was 19 and if he did not play I knew he would want to go, so we risked destroying everything, all the work we had put into this player. That is why I sold Vieira.

    "Now we have that situation with Jack Wilshere. He is 17 and we cannot ask him to play every game to win the championship. But next year he will be ready, he will want to play all the time and if we have bought a player in his position he will want to go."

    Arsenal boss Wenger upbeat over team improvement

    Arsenal boss Arsene Wenger insists his young team are continuing to improve rapidly.

    "We are young and have developed. Why should we not be better in August than we were in March?" said the Arsenal manager, who will be without Walcott on Saturday, but hopes the England forward could be back for the Champions League qualifier against Celtic next week.

    "We lost two great players. However, we have bought Vermaelen to replace Kolo and we have Eduardo back, who for me is a great player.

    "We have players who develop very well and are young, like [Carlos] Vela, [Nicklas] Bendtner, Walcott, who are all strikers, so I do not feel we are short in these positions."

    Wenger insists his squad can deliver come May.

    "The ambition is to finish as high as possible. What is as high as possible is to finish first, it is as simple as that," he said.

    "The competition is of a high level and many aspire to that, so let's continue to play the game the way we want to play it and I believe we can show that we have made big steps forward."
