    BONUS ... Henry and pals hit jackpot

     

     

    THIERRY HENRY and Co have landed a Hand of Wad - thanks to his Hand of Frog.

    France's skipper negotiated a deal where players earned bonuses of up to £406,600 for reaching next year's World Cup.

    So Henry's double handball which set up William Gallas' deciding goal against the Irish last week has meant his pals have hit the jackpot.

    Henry will pocket £382,075 from the deal as each player was due £12,264 for every group stage and friendly win - with each win bonus doubled if they qualified.

    Karim Benzema and Alou Diarra were named in every squad during the campaign and each collected the maximum £406,603, which included other bonuses.

    Boss Raymond Domenech scooped £813,207 from his own deal.

    He said: "If I was coach of a club, I would be earning 10 times the amount I am now."

    Swedish ref Martin Hansson, who failed to spot Henry's handball, admitted: "I've wondered whether to give up this job. But I now realise it was not my fault."
