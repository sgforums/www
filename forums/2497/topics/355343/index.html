    REDS AWAIT GERRARD SCAN RESULTS

    S
    Liverpool are awaiting the results of a scan that could rule skipper Steven Gerrard out of the Champions League quarter-final second leg at Chelsea. Gerrard had the scan on his troublesome groin problem on Thursday afternoon, and the club may not have the results until Friday morning.
     

    The 29-year-old arrived on Thursday morning at the club's Melwood training complex for fitness tests which were reviewed by Liverpool's medical team before it was decided he needed a scan.

    The England midfielder struggled with the injury during Wednesday night's 3-1 first-leg defeat by Chelsea, when he was man-marked by Michael Essien.

    Gerrard was sidelined in February with a hamstring injury sustained in the FA Cup trilogy with Everton, and he has also suffered previous groin problems this term.

    Before the Chelsea defeat there had been fears Gerrard was still struggling from the ankle injury that forced his precautionary exit from Saturday's win at Fulham. But Gerrard trained with the squad on Monday and Tuesday, and was passed fit to play.

    But now Liverpool are bracing themselves for Gerrard missing Saturday's home Premier League game with Blackburn as well as the quarter-final second leg at Stamford Bridge next Tuesday.

    Liverpool manager Rafael Benitez was concerned about his captain's fitness after the Blues match. He said after the game: "Steven was not 100%, you could see that.     Down and out: Gerrard suffered a groin strain against Chelsea

     

     

    Rafa Benitez faces a critical decision on Steven Gerrard’s fitness today as the success of Liverpool’s season hangs in the balance.

    Tests revealed last night that the team’s talisman had suffered another groin strain during the 3-1 Champions League defeat by Chelsea.

    After sensationally blowing the Premier League title race open in recent weeks, the

    The scan confirmed Gerrard has suffered a strain in his adductor muscle, but he has made it clear he is ready to play through the pain.

    Benitez and his medical staff are wary of the possible repercussions and may reluctantly conclude he needs to rest.

    Victory is essential against Blackburn tomorrow and Arsenal a week on Tuesday to keep alive title hopes, while all Gerrard’s dynamic qualities will be needed at Stamford Bridge next Tuesday if Chelsea’s commanding quarter-final first-leg lead is to be overturned.
