    Van no Spurs-man

    Playmaker opts to stay with Hamburg

    Holland international midfielder Rafael van der Vaart has ruled out a move to Spurs.

    The Hamburg playmaker - believed to be a target for several sides - says Tottenham 'are not the club I want to go to'.

    While the international has not ruled out a move to a club which excites him, it seems more likely he will stay in the Bundesliga.

    He told Sportweek: "The interest has been there for some time already, but they (Tottenham) are not the club I want to go to. Tottenham are not a club I want to swap Hamburg for.

    "The chance that I will stay at HSV is really big. As long as no team comes by which makes me immediately excited, I see no reason to leave. And so far none of those clubs have called us."

    Capel happy at Sevilla

    Spurs target not looking to leave Rojiblancos

    Tottenham target Diego Capel has stated he is happy at Sevilla and not looking to leave the club.

    The 20-year-old was the subject of a bid from White Hart Lane earlier this month as manager Juande Ramos looks to strengthen his squad.

    Even though Spurs' offer exceeded a buy-out clause in his contract which allows him to speak to other Spanish teams, Sevilla shunned their approach.

    With Spurs possibly mulling over upping the ante, Capel has ruled out playing Premier League football next term by stating he is not looking to move on.

    Enjoy Sevilla

    "Now I'm a Sevilla player and what I want to do is enjoy that," he told Sevilla's official website.

    "I hope to be here for a long time, certainly, because I won't listen to offers from other sides.

    "The club has made some great signings and we hope things turn out well.

    "The objective this season is to qualify for the Champions League and we hope this will be a successful year."
