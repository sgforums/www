    Gerrard blow for Liverpool: Injured midfielder to miss Arsenal clash

    Steven Gerrard warms up at Stamford Bridge

     

    Liverpool's title hopes were dealt a crushing blow last night when Steven Gerrard was ruled out of their next two games with a recurrence of his groin injury.

    Gerrard missed Tuesday's Champions League quarter-final return with Chelsea due to suffering a reaction in training the previous evening. He has been ordered to rest for the next week and will be unavailable for Tuesday's home game with Arsenal and the following Saturday's trip to Hull.

    A club spokesman said: 'In the training session before the Chelsea game, Steven unfortunately aggravated a small strain he had previously suffered in the adductor muscle.'

    Meanwhile, Liverpool's Charles Itandje will be fined and offloaded at the earliest opportunity after 'inappropriate' conduct at this week's Hillsborough memorial service.

    Manager Rafa Benitez and other senior Anfield officials are furious at television footage showing the goalkeeper, 26, laughing and simulating dance moves.

    They will impose the maximum fine at their disposal, around £25,000. Sacking him may prove difficult but Benitez will do his utmost to remove him during the summer.

    Liverpool fans rage at keeper 'dancing and joking' during Hillsborough memorial service

    Controversy: Charles Itandje (top left under the 'Anfield' caption) appearing to sway and grin as the Hillsborough anthem 'Tragedy' was being sung

     

    Rafa Benitez is said to be disgusted by the antics of Liverpool reserve goalkeeper Charles Itandje during the Hillsborough memorial service.

    The club are reportedly looking into taking disciplinary action against the Frenchman and his future at Anfield could even be in jeopardy after TV footage appeared to show him fooling around during the 20th anniversary ceremony.

    Hundreds of outraged Liverpool supporters have complained about Itandje's behaviour on internet message boards, with most saying his behaviour was totally at odds with the solemn occasion.

    Fans claim the French keeper was seen laughing and joking, simulating dance moves and trying to make team-mate Damien Plessis laugh during the Tragedy song sung at the service.

    Plessis was clearly embarrassed, while the rest of the Liverpool squad conducted themselves impeccably, as did members of the youth and reserve teams who were also present.

    A senior Anfield figure who has viewed the footage from Wednesday's service told the Liverpool Echo that Itandje's behaviour on Wednesday was 'wholly unacceptable'.

    Manager Rafa Benitez is also said to be 'disgusted' having seen the images after being alerted to the issue. He has moved swiftly to seek action against the 26-year-old.

     

    Fans claim the keeper was seen laughing and joking, simulating dance moves and laugh during the Tragedy song sung at the service.

     

    Itandje joined Liverpool on a free transfer in the summer of 2007. The former Lens player has only featured in seven first-team games since then, with his last appearance coming in last season's shock FA Cup exit at the hands of Barnsley.

    Though he retains a squad number, Itandje has not been included in Benitez's first-team squad all season and unsuccessful attempts have been made to offload him in the last two transfer windows.
