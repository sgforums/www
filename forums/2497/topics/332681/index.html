    Luiz Felipe Plans Luis Fabiano Capture

    Chelsea boss Luiz Felipe Scolari is looking to strengthen his attacking artillery following the injury to key striker Didier Drogba in midweek.

     

     

    The Côte d’Ivoire hotshot suffered a knee injury during Wednesday’s 0-0 draw at Cluj in the Champions League and the club have confirmed that he will be out for about six weeks.

    While in theory he is expected to be back before the winter transfer market, there is a possibility that he could take longer to return to full match fitness and sharpness and even then, there is a likelihood of a relapse.

    With Nicolas Anelka in less than impressive form and not particularly too happy with his squad status, manager Scolari may be considering the option of bringing in another top class striker in the January trade period to boost his frontline.

    According to The Times, Sevilla’s Brazilian hitman Luis Fabiano is reportedly at the top of Big Phil’s Christmas shopping list and the Stamford Bridge bosses are expected to free up the necessary funds for the move, especially after missing out on Robinho in the summer.

    Luis Fabiano has a contract that runs until 2011 and his buy-out clause, as quoted by Spanish sports daily As, is set at a meagre €30 million (â‚¤24m) but the player himself reserves the right to 10% of the transfer fee. Money may not be an obstacle for Chelsea but they will have to negotiate with one of the most stubborn and inflexible club presidents when it comes to selling players, José María del Nido.

    The 27 year old striker joined Sevilla in 2005 after a dismal one-year spell at Porto and he has since netted 57 goals in 120 appearances in all competition for the Andalucian outfit.
