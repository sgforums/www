    Man Utd v Middlesbro

    Rio Ferdinand, John O'Shea and Patrice Evra are all fit for Manchester United after recovering from minor knocks. Park Ji-Sung is still out with a knee injury and Louis Saha has a hamstring problem, but Nemanja Vidic will return to training from injury on Monday.

    Cristiano RONALDO is United's top scorer with 21 goals. Of those 16 are in the Premiership, which leaves the Portugal World Cup star three behind Chelsea's Didier Drogba at the head of the race for the Barclays Golden Boot. RONALDO needs a double to total 50 goals for the Red Devils.

    Middlesbrough midfielder Julio Arca is short of fitness after a groin tear. A back injury means Abel Xavier is rated as doubtful, although Robert Huth is available and striker Mark Viduka has recovered from a stomach upset.

    Aiyegbeni YAKUBU is Middlesbrough's top scorer with 16 goals - 12 in the Premiership.

    Manchester United : Van der Sar, Kuszczak, O'Shea, Ferdinand, Brown, Heinze, Evra, Fletcher, Ronaldo, Carrick, Scholes, Giggs, Richardson, Rooney, Smith, Solskjaer, Cathcart.

    Middlesbrough : Schwarzer, Davies, Taylor, Woodgate, Pogatetz, Boateng, Rochemback, Cattermole, Downing, Viduka, Yakubu, Morrison, Jones, Parnaby, Riggott, Huth, Lee, Johnson, Christie.

    REFEREE
    Chris Foy (St Helens, Merseyside)

    --------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Manchester United boss Sir Alex Ferguson on Cristiano Ronaldo, who was labelled a "cheat" by Boro's Gareth Southgate earlier in the season:
    "I'm sure he won't be affected by what has been said. "He is prepared to hold the ball at any given moment and wants to attack. "Then it is up to the referee to decide if players kicking him are fouling him."

    Middlesbrough boss Gareth Southgate:
    "I feel we have improved as a team as the season has gone on. "We have had a disappointing couple of weeks in terms of results and performances, but I must not lose perspective of what we have done. "The majority of the players, I could not ask anymore of, really. Their attitude has been first-class throughout. "We have has a couple of blips, but it is important I do not lose sight of what they have given me this season already."

    Liverpool v Wigan

    Liverpool will have both Craig Bellamy and Xabi Alonso back in their squad. Bellamy has shaken off a knee injury which kept him out for two games, while Alonso missed the midweek win over Middlesbrough with a thigh strain.

    Peter CROUCH is Liverpool's top scorer with 18 goals. Dirk KUYT is the club's leading marksman in the Premiership with 10.

    Wigan winger Lee McCulloch has severe bruising on his foot and is a serious doubt and defender David Unsworth is struggling with a groin strain. Midfielder Antonio Valencia serves the second game of a three-match ban and Chris Kirkland and Fitz Hall are out.

    Emile HESKEY is Wigan's top scorer with nine goals - all in the Premiership.

    Liverpool : Reina, Finnan, Arbeloa, Carragher, Hyypia, Agger, Riise, Pennant, Gerrard, Alonso, Sissoko, Mascherano, Zenden, Bellamy, Crouch, Fowler, Kuyt, Dudek, Gonzalez.

    Wigan : Filan, Granqvist, Jackson, De Zeeuw, Camara, Kilbane, Heskey, McCulloch, Johansson, Landzaat, Aghahowa, Scharner, Skoko, Taylor, Folan, Cywka, Cotterill, Baines, Boyce, Unsworth, Pollitt.

    REFEREE
    Mike Riley (Leeds, Yorkshire)

    --------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Liverpool manager Rafael Benitez:
    "We need to beat Wigan to make sure of the Champions League next season. "The players have to keep going, keep winning and they know they are trying to keep their position in the team. "I will field a strong side against Wigan, it is important we do that and win before we go to Chelsea. "But Wigan work so hard, their manager was watching our win over Middlesbrough on Wednesday, so I expect them to be well prepared and to have a plan against us."

    Wigan manager Paul Jewell on Anfield:
    "It's one of those special theatres of football, one of the best places to play and to watch, just like Highbury. Anfield will always be a special place for me. It's where I watched matches as a kid. "Obviously, the most important thing is that it's not about me, it's about us trying to nick a result against Liverpool. "It's a very difficult place to go, but a great place to go and play football - I just wish I was playing."
