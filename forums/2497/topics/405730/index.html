    Joe Cole signs for Liverpool as former Chelsea ace snubs Arsenal and Tottenham for Merseyside

    Liverpool have signed former Chelsea midfielder Joe Cole on a free transfer.

    The 28-year-old England international left Stamford Bridge at the end of last season after failing to agree a new contract with the Barclays Premier League champions.

    Cole and has agreed terms on a four-year deal believed to be worth nearly £90,000 a week at Anfield and he will undergo a medical in the next 48 hours.

    Blue Monday: Cole has joined Liverpool after quitting Chelsea

    The England midfielder received offers from Arsenal and Tottenham, and also attracted interest from Manchester United only for his wage demands to scupper a move to Old Trafford.

    Teams in Italy were also linked, but after turning down the chance to play Champions League football, he has instead chosen to join new Reds boss Roy Hodgson at Anfield.

    The capture of Cole was seen as being a key factor in proving to the likes of Steven Gerrard and Fernando Torres that Liverpool can still compete for quality signings.

    Cole is the second summer arrival at Liverpool after Serbia forward Milan Jovanovic joined following the expiration of his contract with Standard Liege.

    Ordinary Joe: Cole struggled to make an impact with England at the World Cup

    Cole will be seen as a direct replacement for Yossi Benayoun, who moved to Chelsea for £5.5million earlier this month, and only time will tell who got the better of that exchange.

    Hodgson is still on the look-out for a new left-back with Emiliano Insua on the verge of completing his move to Fiorentina and Fabio Aurelio already released.

    After that the Liverpool boss will hope to secure commitments from Gerrard and Torres about their futures.

    Recruit: Jovanovic signs on at Liverpool

    Former Liverpool midfielder Jamie Redknapp, whose father Harry was keen on signing Cole for Spurs, said reports the player was holding out for the most lucrative contract were unfair.

    'From what I know, compared to other players in the Premier League now, he's not being greedy at all,' Redknapp told Sky Sports News.

    'He just wants a good deal and rightfully so - he's a free agent and he's worked that situation nicely - but I know he's not asking for anything astronomical, like what is being portrayed in the press.

    'He's just looking for the right club and he needs to play football now. He missed a lot of time at Chelsea last season with his knee injury and didn't quite get back to the level he showed previously.

    'He needs to go somewhere, be loved, and play football and get back to being the Joe Cole we all know.'

    JOY HODGSON ... boss Roy is delighted at capturing Joe Cole



     

     

    JOE COLE will pocket a whopping £19MILLION at Liverpool.The England midfielder has signed a four-year deal worth £90,000 a week.

    Cole, 28, gets the No 7 shirt - the same as Reds icon Kenny Dalglish - after leaving Chelsea as a free agent this summer.

    Anfield skipper Steven Gerrard is a massive fan. He said: "Joe's as good as Leo Messi or Cristiano Ronaldo on the ball and he'll give any player a run for their money when it comes to tricks."

    Kop legend Ian Rush added: "It's fantastic news for the club."Skill-wise, Joe is the best in England - there is no one better."

    Cole becomes new boss Roy Hodgson's first signing and is due to undergo a medical this morning in Switzerland.

    Hodgson has promised he will make signing more English players a priority as part of his Liverpool masterplan.

    Liverpool finished just seventh in the league last season but Hodgson lured Cole to Anfield with his vision for the future.

    Chelsea withdrew their offer of a new contract for Cole some months ago and he was officially released before flying out to the World Cup with England.

    Ironically, Liverpool's £5.5m sale of playmaker Yossi Benayoun to Chelsea cleared the way for his free-transfer move.
