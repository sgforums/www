    MOURINHO'S MIND GAMES WON'T WIN HIM TITLE SAYS MARTIN O'NEILL



    MARTIN O'NEILL has warned Jose Mourinho he risks looking stupid using mind games in his increasingly desperate attempts to catch Manchester United at the top of the Premiership.

    The Chelsea boss is feeling the pressure after his second-placed side drew their last two games against Reading and Fulham at home to lose precious ground on United.

    Chelsea trail United by six points, with Mourinho's efforts to unsettle Sir Alex Ferguson with a series of remarks - like his prediction Chelsea would be top by Christmas - having failed miserably.

    Now Mourinho, ordered by Chelsea owner Roman Abramovich to 'sort out' his team's recent shortcomings, has even turned on his own players and blamed the club's board for their injury crisis.

    The Portuguese chief's criticism of his underperforming stars has infuriated some of the players and threatened to alienate him in the dressing room. But Villa boss O'Neill, believes Mourinho will be wasting his time trying to rattle the experienced Fergie and thinks he would be wiser backing his own players instead.

    "I have a high regard for Jose Mourinho, but all this supposed psychology or mind games doesn't really wash with me," said O'Neill, whose team were beaten 3-0 at home by United last month. "As a manager, if you have the players and confidence in them and yourself, then you can go and achieve like Manchester United have done.

    "Manchester United are not only matching Chelsea but surpassing them and giving them a problem. "I don't think mind games really matter, though, and these mind games can actually look a bit mindless if they are full of contradictions, so I wouldn't worry too much about that aspect."

    "Chelsea over the last couple of seasons have been the best team in the country and they have earned their luck. "Where I think Chelsea have been lucky is that their major players - like Frank Lampard - have stayed fit. "Terry has missed very few games until now and when you have your great players playing every week you normally do well."

    Mourinho moans at crazy prices

    JOSE MOURINHO last night warned that the injury crisis threatening Chelsea's title bid will not force him into paying inflated transfer prices.

    Mourinho claimed to be pleased with his players after an uninspiring draw at Aston Villa saw them miss a chance to narrow the gap at the top of the table to four points.

Ricardo Carvalho is also suspended from the Carling Cup semi-final first leg with Wycombe next week, but Mourinho insists he would rather play his 55-year-old assistant Baltemar Brito in central defence than pay over the odds.

    Chelsea have been linked with Manchester City's Micah Richards but Mourinho said: "With the reality of the English market I prefer to play with Brito than pay the crazy money.
