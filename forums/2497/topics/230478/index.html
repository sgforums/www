    Following a break for the FA Cup third round, the Premiership resumes with Manchester United and Chelsea reconvene in their battle for the title, while clubs at the bottom end of the table aim to pick up vital points in fixtures which were staged in reverse three weeks ago.

    Watford v Liverpool - preview
    35 per cent of the goals Watford have conceded have been in the final 15 minutes of matches - the highest in the division. Liverpool have conceded more goals in their last two cup games (9), than they have in their previous 15 Premiership games 8.

    Bolton Wanderers v Manchester City - preview
    Bolton have attempted just 60 shots on target - fewer than any other side in the division. Only 16% of City's goals have been netted from set-pieces - the lowest percentage in the top flight.

    Charlton Athletic v Middlesbrough - preview
    Charlton have lost just one of their last seven home Premiership matches. Mark Viduka has scored seven goals in 10 league matches against Charlton.

    Chelsea v Wigan Athletic - preview
    Chelsea have not gone three home games without a win in the Premiership since September 2002. 22% of Wigan's goals have been scored in the first 15 minutes of matches - the highest percentage in the division.

    Manchester United v Aston Villa - preview
    Cristiano Ronaldo has fired in more shots than any other player in the Premiership - 89 and has also hit the woodwork a league high six times. Villa have won none of their last 10 Premiership matches.

    Sheffield United v Portsmouth - preview
    Sheffield United have attempted the most headed shots - 61. Portsmouth have not won at Bramall Lane since November 1955.

    West Ham United v Fulham - preview
    West Ham have scored with just 6.5% of their attempts at goal - the lowest conversion rate in the top flight. Fulham have won 87 corners this season - the fewest in the division.

    Blackburn Rovers v Arsenal - preview
    There have been 14 penalties awarded in Blackburn matches this season. Robin van Persie has scored six goals in four games against Blackburn.

    ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    On Sunday, there are two games live on espn with Rocky star Sylvester Stallone due to be at Goodison Park to see if Everton can deliver a knockout blow to Reading.

    The weekend's Premiership fare concludes at White Hart Lane as Tottenham Hotspur welcome Newcastle United to the capital.

    Everton v Reading - preview
    Everton have conceded just two headed goals - fewer than any other team in the top flight. 67% of Reading's goals have been scored in the first half - the highest proportion of any team in the Premiership.

    Tottenham Hotspur v Newcastle United - preview
    Spurs have conceded more first-half goals than any other side - 17. Newcastle have won zero points after conceding the first goal of the game.
