    Camoranesi Plays Hard Ball With Juve

    The Italian-Argentinean is even thinking of buying out his contract.

    The Italian-Argentinean midfielder is thinking about using a FIFA ruling to buy out his contract, which expires in 2009.

    This is allowed for players over 28 years of age who have been at their club for more than three years.

    The money would be entirely within reach of clubs. Many will be willing to reimburse his 5-7 million expenses in order to secure his services.

    Camoranesi would personally favor a move to Englandm it seems. However, he must appeal to the clause quickly, within 15 days of the final game of the season (which in Juve's case was on June 10 at home against Spezia).

    So, unless a solution is found to the current impasse by Monday, Camoranesi and his agent will be invoking the clause.
