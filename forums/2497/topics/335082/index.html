    David Ginola, who revealed his intention to return to Tottenham last week, has now announced he would be thrilled to be a director for Paris Saint Germain.

    Ginola understood Spurs manager Juande Ramos was under massive pressure last week, and expressed his intention to play his part with his old club's near future.

    But now that Ramos has been replaced by Harry Redknapp, the Frenchman has set his sights on other objectives with another of his previous clubs, PSG.

    “I know PSG very well and had the idea of working for them several times,” Ginola told RMC.

    “I would like to recreate a family atmosphere, see the fans smile again, because today I do not feel much passion around the club when I hear interviews.

    “I met the previous directors and told them: 'Put me as general manager, I can take the responsibilities, like I always did, and I can cope with the press.'

    “I can get my shirt dirty, not only collect a cheque at the end of the month.”
