    Arsenal seal Nasri deal


    ARSENAL have completed the signing of French whizkid Samir Nasri from Marseille. The Gunners have shelled out €20million (£15.8million) for the promising playmaker, 20, who has put pen to paper on a four-year deal at The Emirates.

    A statement on the player's personal website read: "We inform you that Samir won’t be an Olympique de Marseille player next season and that he has signed a four-year contract with London club Arsenal."

    Nasri has made 12 appearances for the France national team, and was the was the youngest member of Raymond Domenech's squad who failed to make it past the group stages of Euro 2008, making two substitute appearances..

    He has spent the last four seasons at the Stade Velodrome and came to prominence in France's Under-17 European Championship side in 2004. Nasri can play on either wing and won the French first division's Young Player of the Year award in 2007.

    He is seen as the ideal replacement for Alex Hleb, who looks set to leave the Gunners this summer, with Barcelona and Inter Milan all credited with an interest in the Belarusian.

    End in sight for Nasri saga

    Marseille chairman Pape Diouf has stated that Samir Nasri’s tortuous move to Arsenal is still in the pipeline, but a ‘few days’ are needed to finalise the transfer.

    Arsenal have been attempting to secure the France international for weeks now and it was thought that the conclusion of Les Bleus’ Euro 2008 campaign would pave the way for an official announcement confirming his move to North London.

    A statement on Nasri’s official website confirmed he was en route to Emirates Stadium on June 23 but this was swiftly withdrawn as Marseille denied a deal had been concluded.

    L’OM have already signed his replacement after seeing Hatem Ben Arfa join from Lyon on Wednesday, and Diouf now expects the drawn-out saga to come to an end soon.

    “The documents need to be fulfilled," Diouf told a press conference. "A few days are still needed.”

    Arsenal’s only confirmed signing of the summer has been Aaron Ramsey from Cardiff, with Jens Lehmann and Mathieu Flamini both leaving the club on free transfers.
