    JOSE MOURINHO has vowed to KILL Chelsea when he returns to management.

    Mourinho was sacked by the Blues earlier this season despite leading the club to back-to-back Premier League titles.

    The Portuguese coach, who is keen to take charge of an Italian or Spanish team, said: "I hope to play Chelsea next season in the Champions League.

    "If I play them in the Champions League, I want to go there and kill them â€” that's my message."

    Mourinho was replaced at Stamford Bridge by Avram Grant, a close friend of Blues owner Roman Abramovich.

    But the Special One insists he does not hold a grudge towards Chelsea's former director of football.

    He added: "I feel nothing for Grant. I feel everything for the club but not for him personally.

    "I still feel Chelsea are a part of me. I'll have Chelsea in my heart forever.

    "I left and for five months you couldn't get a bad word from me in relation to the club and you won't get one in the future."

    Mourinho's departure was shrouded in mystery, with the build-up dominated by rumours of a bust-up with Abramovich.

    But the ex-Porto boss claims he remains on good terms with the Russian billionaire.

    He said: "I think it was last week that I spoke with people from the club.

    "I was speaking with Mr Abramovich and [chief executive] Peter Kenyon because we still keep in touch.

    "I was telling them I wish them well, I wish them always to succeed, I wish them always to win."
