    Maldini: Inter Milan coach Mourinho desperate for attention

    Former Italy coach and AC Milan chief Cesare Maldini says Inter boss Jose Mourinho is just courting publicity over his blazing row with Marcello Lippi.

    “The old ‘who will win the title’ question is a classic fixture of pre-season interviews,” explained Maldini, who was in charge for the 1998 World Cup.

    “Lippi, even if he is the Coach of the Italy squad, has every right to freely express his opinion. The real issue here is that Mourinho just wants attention.

    “He always has to be at the centre of the spotlight’s glare and be the focus of the media, so he created this controversy out of nothing.”

    Mourinho infuriates Inter Milan fans with shutout

    Jose Mourinho has infuriated Inter Milan fans after locking out over 5,000 people from an open training session yesterday.

    The team was all set to train under the applause of its supporters, but when around fans turned up to the ground, they found the gates locked.

    In a change of plan, Mourinho had ordered the squad to warm up in a different area of the campus away from the prying eyes of the media and fans.
