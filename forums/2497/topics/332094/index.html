    Wenger: We're Good Enough, But Not Tall Enough!

    Arsenal manager Arsene Wenger has admitted that his side are lacking in height at the centre of their defence, but insists that the team have enough experience and ability to mount a challenge this season...

     

     

    It's been a well documented fact that Arsenal are potentially vulnerable to aerial threats from set-pieces this season as their defence is lacking in height. Brede Hangeland scored for Fulham against the Gunners from a free-kick delivered into the box, and Daniel Cousin rose highest to head Hull into a decisive 2-1 lead at the Emirates this weekend to hand Arsenal two league defeats.

    Manager Arsene Wenger is aware of the problem with the relatively diminutive William Gallas and Kolo Toure in the heart of the backline and has hinted that the 6'3 Johan Djourou could be drafted in for some vital inches at the back.

    However, the Frenchman maintains that his side are not lacking in ability, but need to improve their defending as a whole. "I believe that we have more a size problem than quality in our defending problem. Our centre backs are not very tall. I don't know what we can do. It's not only centre backs who defend. As a team we are a bit short. "I do not necessarily think it's inexperience. We gave them too much room on the first goal and we didn't clear the ball on the second one, and that's basically it.

    Arsenal's second defeat of the season, and they are yet to face a fellow top-four team, has left them in a dangerous position at this relatively early point. Wenger is eager that the side's defensive lapses are rectified and that they can bounce back in time for their next fixture.

    He continued: "I don't know how many games we can afford to lose in the league. What is important now is to recover and prepare for the next one. Can we afford to lose not one more? I don't know."
