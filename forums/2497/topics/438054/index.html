    I'm rich, great and handsome, and I deserve more protection from referees, fumes Ronaldo

     

     

     

     

    I'm Real-ly angry: Ronaldo was disappointed with the standard of refereeing

     

    Going over: The Real Madrid star was fouled on a number of occasions

     

     

    Cristiano Ronaldo faces a UEFA charge after accusing Norwegian referee Svein Oddvar Moen of jealousy. 

    The Real Madrid star claims that he was not give sufficient protection during his side's 1-0 Champions League win over Dinamo Zagreb.

    The UEFA statistics showed the Ronaldo was fouled four times and ended the game with a bloodstained ankle.

    'It is always good to get off to a winning start,' said Ronaldo, who was making his 500th senior career appearance.

     

     

     

     

     

     

    A bloody mess: Ronaldo was fuming with the treatment he received in Real's win

     

     

    'However, I hope we will not have a repeat of this refereeing performance, it was shameful. 

    'Some referees allow them to give me stick like (the Dinamo players) did today. I think it's because I'm rich, handsome and a great player. They envy me - there's no other explanation.

    'We are very happy with the three points we took, but I'm not so satisfied with the refereeing. I hope we never have this referee again.'

    Karim Benzema agreed with Ronaldo and said: '[Jerko] Leko deserved the red for all the fouls he committed throughout the game.'
