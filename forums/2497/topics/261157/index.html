    The Milan President confirmed the interest in the Blues striker, but admitted that the chances of signing him are slim since the English club is not interested in selling him.



    Didier Drogba will most likely remain a dream for the Rossoneri fans, following the revelation of President Silvio Berlusconi, who admitted that the Blues do not want to sell him.

    "I put the money on the plate, but they are not selling him," declared Berlusconi when asked about the possibility of Drogba's arrival to Milan.

    This means that another primary target for the Rossoneri will not be arriving after the failed transfers of Eto`o' and Ronaldinho, even though the Ivorian striker had declared earlier this week that he wants another big experience in a different league.

    If the Drogba bid fails, Milan will try to lure Thierry Henry away from Arsenal, but will need to beat the competition of Barcelona, unless Eto`o' or Ronaldinho change their minds about moving to Italy.
