    Man City boss Hughes tells Fergie to lighten up over Tevez billboard

    Manchester City boss Mark Hughes has told Sir Alex Ferguson to lighten up after complaints from the Manchester United manager over their Carlos Tevez billboard.

    Hughes told City's website: "Sir Alex Ferguson is a man I hold the utmost respect for and he is fully entitled to his opinion. I am very happy with the transfer business Manchester City have conducted this summer, and that the players we have recruited are as excited as I am about the season ahead.

    “The poster campaign in Manchester is a bit of fun between fans in a similar vein as the banner at Old Trafford which counts the timespan since City’s last trophy win. We’re glad it’s got so many people talking.”
