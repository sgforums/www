Bellamy unfazed by speculation

  • turbo_drift
    23 Feb 07, 20:10
    Liverpool hero Craig Bellamy has admitted he is not worried about speculation regarding his future at the club.

    Bellamy's long-term future at Anfield has been thrown into doubt following last week's reported bust-up with team-mate John Arne Riise on a training camp in Portugal.

    The Welshman put all the rumours behind him to inspire Liverpool to a memorable 2-1 win over Barcelona in midweek and he insists he is used to speculation over his future.

    "I am a player who has always been linked with other clubs each season," Bellamy told the Daily Star.

    "At Blackburn last year it was 'he's moving on' all that sort of stuff.

    "At Newcastle I was joining a different club in every transfer window. I live with speculation.

    "When you are at a club like Liverpool and are my sort of player there are always going to be stories about getting rid or bringing someone else in.

    "It doesn't bother me at all, it's part of football and life.

    "I am going to enjoy the moment, which I repeat is the best of my career.

    "This result after all that had been said about me is right up there."

    Meanwhile, Bellamy has revealed his golf-swing celebration at Camp Nou was pre-planned among the Liverpool players.

    Bellamy celebrated his equaliser against Barcelona by swinging an imaginary golf club in a gesture towards the media following his reported attack on Riise with a golf club.

    Bellamy said: "If any of us scored, that was the deal, I was supposed to do it straightaway, but there was a delay, because it was one of those was it in or wasn't it moments?

    "I don't know about any turmoil at the club. All I can say is we have had a decent week. We were all looking forward to this game and determined that nothing would be allowed to spoil it.

    "It probably sounds mad and people can write what they like, but we have a quiet group of lads here. In terms of having a laugh, the last week has been the best we've had since I joined the club.

    "It brought us all closer together and maybe because we knew Barcelona were coming up, we knew we had to work for each other. It has turned out perfectly."
