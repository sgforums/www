    On a day when Arsenal's chairman has mocked Chelsea's hopes of dominating World football we ask which club is the biggest brand across the globe?

    Chelsea chief executive Peter Kenyon has previously explained his goal of making the Blues the leading football brand by 2014, but their hopes seem to be falling apart as the club experience numerous PR disasters.

    Today Peter Hill-Wood explained: "I don't want to run Chelsea down, but one has to concede Manchester United and Liverpool are probably the biggest names in UK football and probably throughout the world. For Chelsea to think they are suddenly going to dominate United and Liverpool is fantasy. It's not going to happen."

    Arsenal may have more realistic hopes of competing with the two most successful clubs in English history, especially as they have emerged as Britain's richest club. However, outside of England there are also plenty of candidates to rival the quest to be the global brand leader.

    The obvious names are Real Madrid, Barcelona and from Italy AC Milan and Juventus, but how do they all compare with the likes of Manchester United and Liverpool?
