    Fab 4-4: Liverpool top but assassin Andrey says United are in pole position

     

    Death knell: Arshavin's four goal haul surely put paid to Liverpool's title hopes

     

     

    Andrey Arshavin lit up Anfield with a stunning four-goal display last night and then admitted: ‘We have played into Manchester United’s hands.’

    A clinical show of finishing from Arsenal’s £11million signing looked to have kept alive Arsenal’s slender title hopes until Yossi Benayoun forced home a Liverpool equaliser deep into injury time to leave the teams locked at 4-4.

    The point was enough to lift Liverpool above defending Barclays Premier League champions Manchester United and into top place on goal difference. 

    But, with United having two games in hand on Liverpool, one of the most thrilling spectacles of the season had succeeded only in strengthening United’s hold on the crown.

    Anfield boss Rafa Benitez admitted: ‘It’s a big chance gone for us.’ But, with Arsenal still to play United and Chelsea, Gunners boss Arsene Wenger vowed to revive Liverpool’s sagging title challenge.

     

     

    Benitez fumes at the basic errors that cost his side dear

     

    Wenger said: ‘Liverpool should not worry too much, because we will be heading to Old Trafford full of confidence. It is still very open because we will go into that game with every intention of winning it.

    ‘We are half-happy at scoring four goals but half-unhappy at conceding four. Arshavin is a great player, and great players can play anywhere. He has such a short backlift when he shoots.’

     

    Short backlift: Arshavin (left) strikes

     

    Benitez could hardly conceal his huge disappointment at his side’s defensive deficiencies. He said: ‘I can’t explain why we made so many errors at the back. Many of them were so basic, and they cost us goals. It was not like us, and I don’t know whether it was a case of nerves creeping in and affecting us.

    ‘It was frustrating to make so many mistakes but there were a lot of positives. We showed character and that helped us to come back so strongly at the end and salvage a point. That was enough to put us in top place, so that is another positive. United may be in the driving seat, but there was a lot about our performance that should encourage us.’

    Liverpool co-owners Tom Hicks and George Gillett were in the directors’ box after
    apparently holding meetings with financial institutions over loans that are still outstanding.

    Injured Steven Gerrard said: ‘There should always be a good togetherness at the club, it is one of our strengths. It was a good sign to see the two owners together. Hopefully, they will sort their differences out.’

    ARSHAVIN BLAST STUNS REDS

     

    Arshavin signals four to the crowd after his final goal.

     

     

    Post match interviews:

     

    Liverpool manager Rafael Benitez cursed the "massive" mistakes which cost his side victory and handed the initiative back to Manchester United in the Barclays Premier League title race.

    "It is really disappointing because we conceded four goals and normally we were not conceding these kind of goals," he said. "We made massive mistakes. It is difficult to explain because it was different players in different stages of the game.

    "You don't remember any save from Pepe (Jose Reina), it is just four chances and four goals and all of them have been our mistakes.

     

     

     

    Arsene Wenger had mixed emotions about the result. "It was a game at a frenetic pace of top quality technically, and you could see both teams created many chances," he said.

    "We are half-happy because we scored four goals and half-unhappy because we conceded four. "Also, 4-3 up with two minutes to go, the team is disappointed [to draw] but I give credit to my team for a great performance tonight."
