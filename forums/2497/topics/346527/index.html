    WHAT'S THE STORY? ... Andrey Arshavin

     

    THE Andrey Arshavin transfer mystery deepened last night as suspicions grew that rules had been broken. The Russian frontman officially joined Arsenal on Tuesday evening — nearly 24 hours after the transfer deadline closed. 

    Arshavin yesterday claimed in a Russian paper he actually signed for Arsenal TWO minutes before Monday’s deadline. He said: “I only believed the deal was done at the moment I put my signature on the contract. That was about 16.57 or 16.58 London time.” 

    However, the spokesman for Arshavin’s club Zenit St Petersburg Alex Petrov appeared on a Russian TV station at 17.03pm to declare the deal had broken down. Then at 17.14 news agency AP put out a statement saying Petrov told them the deal was off. 

    And a full 33 MINUTES, at 17.31, after Arshavin claims he signed the contract, Petrov was quoted by AP as saying: “The deal didn’t happen. Arshavin is coming back to Zenit.” 

    But the Premier League are adamant all forms were signed correctly and submitted to them “bang on five o’clock on Monday”. 

    A spokesman said: “The Premier League and Football Association are satisfied all the requisite documentation regarding Andrei Arshavin’s transfer to Arsenal was submitted to us before the deadline.” 

    But the assurance has not quelled suspicions rules were stretched. Aston Villa are today expected to demand explanations at a Premier League meeting aware Arshavin’s arrival could ruin their bid to qualify for the Champions League. 

    Crucially the PL insisted a deal between the clubs had to have been agreed BEFORE the 5pm Monday deadline for it to be allowed later to go through. 

    Boss Arsene Wenger kept in constant contact with Arshavin throughout the month-long saga. Arshavin, 27, said: “I received a lot of support from Arsene Wenger, who I spoke to on the phone several times. He’s a great man.”

    Villa call for Arshavin probe

     

     

     

    ASTON VILLA are expected to lead an Andrey Arshavin backlash at a Premier League meeting today.

    Villa chief executive Paul Faulkner will be there and “expects the topic of Arshavin to come up”.

    Villa boss Martin O’Neill declared: “I haven’t anything to say on the matter. “There is a Premier League meeting tomorrow where some points are being raised, not necessarily by us. It’s about clarification to the extension.”
