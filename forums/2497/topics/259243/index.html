    Wayne Routledge

    Wayne Routledge is ready to quit Tottenham in search of regular first-team footballl and a host of clubs are ready to make a move.

    The England Under-21 winger impressed last season during a season-long loan spell at Fulham.

    The Cottagers are keen to take him back to West London, but a number of Premiership clubs are ready to make offers.

    Tottenham already have a sizeable squad and although they would ideally prefer Routledge to stay - the former Crystal Palace man is unlikely to turn down the chance to play week-in-week-out.

    Routledge arrived at White Hart Lane in 2005 but injury curtailed his chances and he joined Portsmouth on loan, before his subsequent move to Fulham last term.

    Up to five top-flight clubs are understood to have registered an interest in the 22-year-old.
