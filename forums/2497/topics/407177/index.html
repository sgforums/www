    Report: Barcelona Striker Zlatan Ibrahimovic Offers Himself To Milan

    Secret talks with Milan are taking place, according to the Catalan press.

    Ibrahimovic & Guardiola - Barcelona-Inter - Champions League (Getty Images)

    New questions have been raised as to how far Zlatan Ibrahimovic's career at Barcelona will be preserved as Sport.es claims he has offered his services to Milan.

    Despite denials from Ibrahimovic, Pep Guardiola and Milan over a transfer, the Catalan press report his agent Mino Raiola has been in secret contact with Rossoneri president Silvio Berlusconi, who just two weeks ago said he would not be afraid to land a mega star.

    Ibrahimovic is said to be fearing the emergence of David Villa at Camp Nou and feels it would be hard to reconcile his relationship with a starting role, despite guarantees from his coach that he is not leaving.

    The lure of joining Milan, who are set to fight for the Scudetto and Champions League, and playing against former team Inter, appeals to the Sweden striker.

    Raiola has been addressing the questions at issue with Berlusconi and much of those relate to finances.

    Milan CEO Adriano Galliani last month said 'Ibra' was "too expensive" and "earned too much" after he learned the player commands a €10 million per annum wage deal.

    But the constant change of moods and behavior, as well as Ibrahimovic's long association with Serie A, could see the bureaucratic wheels turning.
