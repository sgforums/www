    Saturday

    Wigan v Manchester United, 7.45pm
    Wayne Rooney's goal drought is a cause of concern for some, but he will fancy his chances of putting it right on Saturday as he scored four goals in three meetings with Wigan last season across all competitions.
    Wigan have only kept one clean sheet in their last 14 Premiership matches, a stat which could buoy Rooney.

    Arsenal v Watford, 10pm
    Arsenal's win against Charlton ended a run of 15 games without a victory in Premiership matches where they had conceded the first goal of the game.
    Watford have won three of their last five visits to play Arsenal in all competitions.

    Aston Villa v Tottenham, 10pm
    There have only been 11 goals in the last seven league meetings between Villa and Spurs at Villa Park and no more than two in any one of those games.

    Liverpool v Blackburn, 10pm
    Blackburn have allowed their opponents more shots on target - 48 - than any other team, while Liverpool have allowed the fewest number of shots on target against them - 17.

    Manchester City v Sheffield United, 10pm
    The Blades have failed to score in their last six league visits to play City in Manchester, while Stuart Pearce's charges have yet to concede a goal at Eastlands this season.

    Middlesbrough v Everton, 10pm
    James Beattie has scored seven Premiership goals against Boro - no opponent has been on the receiving end more often.
    Boro have failed to score in the first half of nine of their last 11 league games.

    Portsmouth v West Ham, 10pm
    The Hammers are unbeaten in their last four visits to Fratton Park and have won three of those games by a 1-0 scoreline.
    Portsmouth have scored more headed goals - four - than any other team in The Premiership.

    Reading v Chelsea, 0.15pm
    Chelsea have scored the opening goal of the game in each of their seven Premiership games this season and are the only team to have been leading at some stage in each of their matches this campaign.

    Sunday

    Newcastle v Bolton, 11pm
    Only one of the 13 Premiership meetings between Newcastle and Bolton has ended in a draw. The Magpies have mustered only one clean sheet in their seven Premiership games so far.
