    BETRAYED ... Terry with former pal Bridge

     

     

    JOHN TERRY and Wayne Bridge finally have the chance to settle their beef this week.

    On Saturday, the pair will be enemies when Manchester City play Chelsea.

    But then they look set to line up as TEAM-MATES when England host Egypt.

    That Wembley game could end in fireworks — so we've gone through the archives to see how their day may end.


     

     

     

     

     

     

    10. Steve McManaman v Bruce Grobbelaar

    Wispy McManaman gave as good as he got when Grobbelaar came angling for a fight.

    The stopper came charging towards his team-mate after a weak clearance had put Liverpool in trouble.

    Grobbelaar grabbed McManaman by the throat — only for his victim to go for the face.

     

     

     

     

    9. Ricardo Fuller v Andy Griffin

    Fuller SLAPPED Stoke ace Griffin against West Ham last term.

    The Potters had just shipped a soft goal — and with the teams in place for the restart, Fuller marched towards his pal and let fly.

     

     

     

     

    8. Stan Collymore v Trevor Benjamin

    Collymore and Benjamin turned to fisticuffs in a reserve team match at Leicester.

    The warring duo reportedly exchanged punches in an almighty half-time bust-up.

    After the scrap, transfer-listed Collymore was hurried out of Filbert Street with a move to Bradford.

     

     

     

    7. Kieron Dyer v Lee Bowyer

    Newcastle was a powder keg in 2005 — with bad-boys Lee Bowyer and Kieron Dyer lining up alongside each other.

    The fuse blew against Aston Villa when the pair were sent off for trading punches in a 3-0 home defeat.

     

     

     

    6. Jamie Carragher v Alvaro Arbeloa

    Liverpool relegated West Brom with this 2-0 win last season.

    But it was the Reds who had that sinking feeling as Carragher did battle with Arbeloa.

    Carragher went toe-to-toe with the Spaniard after the Baggies nearly clawed one back.

    The former England defender explained: "We wanted to keep a clean sheet."

     

     

     

    . Nicklas Bendtner v Emmanuel Adebayor

    Adebayor turned on Bendtner at White Hart Lane — in an argument stemming from the Dane's trainers.

    Togo hitman Adebayor was livid that Bendtner had flouted a smart dress code at Arsenal.

    Their hatred reared its head in a Carling Cup tie against Spurs when Adebayor butted his strike partner in the face.

     

     

     

    4. Olof Melberg v Freddie Ljungberg

    Sweden's 2002 World Cup campaign was thrown into chaos when Ljungberg throttled Melberg.

    Former Aston Villa defender Melberg threw himself at his team-mate in a meaty training ground tackle.

    But Ljungberg stormed back at Melberg and desperate pals had to intervene.

     

     

     

    3. Graeme Le Saux v David Batty

    Le Saux brawled with Batty as Blackburn were beaten in Moscow.

    The full-back landed a punch on his pal after weeks of simmering tension.

    But Le Saux admitted: "I am not a fighter. I hadn't closed my fist properly.

    "I was in a lot of pain, which just made me feel more ridiculous."

     

     

     

    2. John Hartson v Eyal Berkovic

    Hartson was fined two weeks' wages for a sickening attack on West Ham team-mate Berkovic.

    The striker booted Berkovic in the FACE after a training ground dispute.

     

     

     

    1. Craig Bellamy v John Arne Riise

    Hot-head Bellamy went after Riise with a GOLF CLUB after a team night at the karaoke.

    Liverpool had been in the Algarve as they prepared for a Champions League clash with Barcelona.

    Incredibly, Bellamy and Riise both then scored at the Nou Camp — and the striker celebrated with a joyful air shot.
