    Barcelona see Villa winger Young as Ribery alternative

    Barcelona are eyeing Aston Villa winger Ashley Young.

    Sport says Barca will move for Young should they fail in their pursuit of Bayern Munich star Franck Ribery.

    Barca management are attracted to Young's versatility, with the midfielder comfortable on both wings.

    The European giants' interest has raised the prospect of Barca offering Alex Hleb in part exchange for Young, who Villa would demand in excess of £20 million for.

    Ashley Young at the centre of £22m transfer battle as Spanish superpower Barcelona joins Real Madrid in race for Aston Villa star

    Barcelona and Real Madrid are lining up summer bids for Aston Villa winger Ashley Young.

    The Spanish giants are battling to sign Franck Ribery from Bayern Munich, but have Young lined up as an alternative once the France star chooses his destination.

    Madrid's interest in England star Young stretches back to last summer, but Barca have now entered the race amid fears that Real advisor Zinedine Zidane will convince Ribery to sign on at the Bernabeu.

    Flying man: Ashley Young has been in irresistable form for Aston Villa since joining them from Watford, and has earned international recognition (below)



    Pep Guardiola admires Young's ability to play on either flank and Nou Camp insiders believe he would be available for about £22million.

    Barca would be prepared to offer a part-exchange deal with Alexander Hleb, the former Arsenal player who is keen on a return to England after conceding he has no future at his present club.

    Former Watford star Young, 24, has gained international attention after breaking into Fabio Capello's England side and he looks a shoo-in for the World Cup.

    Given the tensions surrounding Martin O'Neill's relationship with the Villa fans and club owner Randy Lerner, he is likely to be none too pleased about a move for one of his star men.

    In 43 appearances this season, he has scored nine and notched 13 assists.
