    ALMUNIA ... nerves

    ARSENE WENGER is ready to ditch Manuel Almunia — for turning to jelly before big games.

    The Gunners blunder keeper could be heading for the exit door after his horror show last season.

    A string of mistakes saw his place come under threat and Arsenal's title challenge derailed.

    And Wenger admits that Almunia's nerves are starting to cause a problem.

    He said: "Manuel Almunia is a talented goalkeeper.

    "In training, he is superb, the only problem is that he gets nervous on big matches, thereby making silly mistakes.

    "After the World Cup, we will be making an announcement on goalkeeping."

    Wenger yesterday wrapped up an £8.5m deal for Lorient defender Laurent Koscielny.

    And the Emirates boss insists it will not be the last time he puts his hand into his pocket.

    He added: "Koscielny can be an outstanding addition to our team, but it is not over, we are still open to buy.

    "We are still in the market for another defensive player because we have the need for one more."
