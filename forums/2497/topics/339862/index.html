    SINGAPORE'S FOCUS IS ON THE FINAL

     

     

    JAKARTA (Dec 8, 2008) – The outcome of tomorrow's match between Singapore and Indonesia will not really matter to the Lions. This is because as far as the reigning champions are concerned, their main objective is to make the final of the AFF Suzuki Cup final.

    The defending champions, who have won two matches and have already qualified for the semifinals, will battle Indonesia in the last group match to decide which team will top the group. "The advantage is with Indonesia who will have their supporters. But on the pitch its 11 vs11 and we play every game to win," said Avramovic today.

    "This is the most prestigious and biggest competition in South East Asia and it is important that both Singapore and Indonesia have qualified for the semi finals. For us, our main objective is to be in the final," declared the coach who also led the team to the previous title.

    Based on performances thus far, both Singapore and Indonesia have impressed. It will be a close and cautious match tomorrow with the team that makes the fewest mistakes likely to prevail.

    Singapore have an enormous arsenal with the likes of Agu Casmir, Mohd Noh Alam Shah, Indra Sahdan, Alexander Duric and Shia Jiayi all capable of scoring but Indonesia too have shown that they have some heavy artillery in Budi Sudarsono and Bambang Pamungkas.

    For the record, Budi and Agu have both netted four goals each. Budi scored once against Myanmar and followed it up with a sizzling hattrick against Cambodia yesterday. Agu helped himself to two goals each against the Cambodia and Myanmar.

    The fans, who are expected to pack the 80,000 capacity Gelora Bung Karno Stadium, will be in for a real treat.

    Meanwhile, Avramovic also echoed the sentiments of Myanmar coach Carlos Antonio Falopa that players should have more time to rest between matches. "Players should have more time to rest. The quality of football will improve as the players will be able to play better. I wish to have my best  11 for tomorrow's game and for all my games but sometimes that is not possible."

    MYANMAR KEEPER, COACH AND TEAM LEADER BANNED ONE MATCH

     

    JAKARTA (Dec 8, 2008) – The Group A disciplinary committee has handed out a one match suspension to Myanmar goalkeeper Aung Aung Oo and two other team officials for their actions during the match against Singapore yesterday.

    Myanmar, unhappy with the referee for allowing a goal from Singapore, protested the decision and held up play for some eight minutes. The incident happened in the second half when a quickly taken freekick by Singapore resulted in the third goal of the match.

    The Myanmar keeper had charged at the referee and pushed him and for that he was immediately given the red card. The other Myanmar players also surrounded the match official from the Philippines.

    Head Coach Carlos Antonio Falopa and Team Leader Thein Aung also went into the pitch.

    The disciplinary committee, which met last night, issued the relevant letters to the Myanmar team. With the suspensions, the goalkeeper, coach and team leader will have to watch the match from the stands of the Jalak Harupat Stadium in Bandung tomorrow evening.
