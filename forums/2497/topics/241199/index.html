Roma rumble limp Lyon

  • turbo_drift
    7 Mar 07, 21:30
    Roma inflicted a rare home defeat on Lyon to reach the quarter-finals of the UEFA Champions League after a 2-0 success at Stade Gerland.

    With the tie finely poised after a 0-0 draw in the Italian capital a fortnight ago, Francesco Totti headed home for his 21st goal of the season midway through the first half.

    A piece of individual brilliance from Alessandro Mancini brought the second goal just before half-time as Lyon lost at home in Europe for the first time since October 2002.

    Juninho Pernambucano, as per usual, carried Lyon's attacking threat and his early shot was spilled by Roma keeper Doni, with Philippe Mexes in sharply to clear from a lurking striker.

    Daniele De Rossi was left aghast on six minutes to have a close-range header disallowed after he had met Totti's knock-back, but the Roma captain had been harshly penalised for a nudge on Eric Abidal.

    Sidney Govou was on the end of the majority of Juninho's crosses and was off target with two flick-ons before Lyon fell behind on 22 minutes.

    Cristian Chivu's raking pass sent Max Tonetto scampering down the left and his clipped cross was ideal for Totti, who was positioned perfectly to head past Gregory Coupet.

    Lyon's best source of chances was via crosses from the flanks, with Govou failing to get a clean connection on Abidal's deep centre.

    A Juninho corner then caused panic in the Roma box and, after a couple of ricochets, Sebastian Squillaci prodded a shot straight at Doni from close range.

    Just before half-time a mesmerising piece of skill from Mancini put Roma 2-0 up. He collected a searching ball from the right, bamboozled Anthony Reveillere with a string of step-overs and then lashed a shot into the top corner.

    Roma could have enjoyed a dream start to the second half when Rodrigo Taddei sprung the offside trap, but he lacked composure in skewing his finish off target.

    Doni, who had endured a shaky first period, then came to the fore as he was out quickly to save substitute Sylvain Wiltord's shot with his legs.

    Moments later, he produced an even better save, sticking out his left hand to turn away Juninho's curling effort as the French champions sought a way back into the game.

    An unsavoury element then crept into the contest as Simone Perrotta dived in an attempt to earn a penalty, and at the opposite end, Fred was similarly loose limbed after collecting Juninho's pass.

    A Kim Kallstrom piledriver pressed Doni into another acrobatic one-handed stop before the Swedish schemer swept in a teasing cross which just eluded the outstretched boot of Florent Malouda.

    Substitute Karim Benzema flashed a shot across the face of goal before Fred was lucky not to be sent off after leaving Chivu with a bloodied nose after disgracefully smashing his arm into the Romanian's face.

    The contest petered out to a tetchy conclusion as the French champions failed to translate their domestic dominance to the knockout phase of Europe's elite club competition.
