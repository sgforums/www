Late Torres brace downs Chelsea

  • limpper
    2 Feb 09, 03:58

    The Liverpool star striker, who scored the 2 goals against Chelsea.

     

    Fernando Torres hit two late goals in Liverpool's 2-0 win over Chelsea at Anfield.

    With the Blues down to ten men following the second half sending off of Frank Lampard for a lunge on Xabi Alonso, the game seemed to be heading for a stalemate.

    Liverpool looked to be unable to capitalize on their territorial possession until Torres scored a minute from the end then deep into injury time.

    The Reds now trail leaders Manchester United by two points.

    Chelsea boss Luiz Felipe Scolari admitted to sky sports that Liverpool deserved their win.

    "I think the result it’s after what’s happened with Lampard. It’s normal because they have more possession, they have more quality, they arrive more in front of our goal, but until then no,” he said.

    The Blues’ boss added: "If it’s possible I want the referee to look at this situation on the television and maybe he will change the red card for Lampard because as I look on television it’s a foul for the other player (Alonso) not for Lampard."

    "After this they are the owner of the pitch and they scored two goals,” he said before adding, “I think Liverpool are better than us, all of the game."

    Scolari insisted his players would not give up on the title yet yet. "We need to look again, we need to try again, because we have many games. But we are behind now, two clubs Manchester and Liverpool, and it’s more difficult than before but we need to fight until the last game."

    Goalscorer Torres said, "We knew before the game we had to win and we have more confidence now.

    "They are my first two goals after the injury; to score at home this season for the first time and against Chelsea is fantastic.

    "With one player less Chelsea had to defend. A draw was good for them but we had to win. We worked very hard and with determination, but we know we have to improve."

    Torres added: "We know that Manchester United is favourite at top of the table, and we will fight until the end."
