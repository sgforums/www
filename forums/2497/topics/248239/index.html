    RIO DE JANEIRO, April 12 -- Romario's failure to score his 1,000th goal after four matches may represent the end of ambitious commercial effort.

    Bank BMG signed a sponsorship contract with Romario's team Vasco, valid up to the end of Rio de Janeiro State League, comprehending the use of Romario's image.

    Additionally, U.K. footwear manufacturer Reebok, which supplies Vasco with sports apparel, created a jersey especially for the moment when the striker hits the goal for the 1,000th time.

    Vasco played on Wednesday Botafogo for a place in the State League final.

    Reebok announced that sportswear stores all over the country had units of the jersey on sale for suggested price of 159 reals (78 U.S. dollars) on the next day.

    However, Romario did not get to score one single time in an eight-goal match, in which his team accounted for half of the goals.

    Vasco got eliminated from the League, as well as from the Brazil Cup, and such sponsorships are threatened not to live up to Vascos next game.

    The team will only play again one month from Wednesday, for the Brazilian League, which has not even started yet.

    Meanwhile, other teams are demonstrating interest in hiring the player. Club Flamengo stated on Thursday that a proposal will be made.

    If Flamengo accomplishes its aims, BMG and Reebok will have definitely wasted their money, and losses will be inevitable anyway.
