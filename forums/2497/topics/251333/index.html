    ALEX WANTS CHELSEA MOVE

    Dutch champions PSV Eindhoven look set to lose the services of defender Alex next season, with the Brazilian looking to play for Chelsea. The 24-year-old, who was loaned out by the Blues immediately after his purchase in 2004 due to potential work permit problems, believes the time has come for his long-awaited move to London.

    "The chance that I will leave PSV is quite big," Alex told De Telegraaf newspaper. "At this moment Chelsea are very busy with getting a work permit for me. "Earlier they did the same with Salomon Kalou and several other players, which worked out fine."

    Alex has been unable to play for Chelsea due to his lack of caps for Brazil. Rules in England stipulate that a non-EU player must have played in 75% of his country's matches in the last two years to be granted a work permit.

    However, Alex does not comply and is hoping for a concession from the FA. Even if he is allowed to play for Chelsea, he faces stiff competition from club captain John Terry and Ricardo Carvalho in the centre-back positions. He added: "Chelsea are a great club, but it is most important that I will play a lot of games there. "If I only sit on the bench, it will be difficult. But whether that will happen, you only know when you are there."
