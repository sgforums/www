    Fixture and match preview

    Saturday

    Fulham v Everton
    All 10 Premiership meetings between Fulham and Everton have ended as home wins. Everton have not scored a Premiership goal at Craven Cottage

    Bolton v Wigan
    Wigan have failed to win in their last six visits to Bolton. Bolton have conceded only four goals in their last six home games - all of them to Manchester United last weekend.

    Charlton v Man City
    Man City have scored 12 goals in their last four visits to the Valley. Bottom side Charlton have only conceded as many first half goals as leaders Manchester United - three.

    Liverpool v Reading
    Liverpool have never met Reading in a league match. Liverpool have only failed to score once in the last 22 Premiership games at Anfield.

    Man Utd v Portsmouth
    United have won all three Premiership home games against Portsmouth. United have conceded just two goals in their last 12 Premiership games at Old Trafford.

    Watford v Middlesbrough
    Watford have not won any of the last six meetings with Middlesbrough in all competitions. Boro have won three of their last five visits to Vicarage Road.

    Newcastle v Sheffield Utd
    Newcastle have conceded goals to players of 10 different nationalities this season - more than any other side. The Blades have never won an away game against another team called United in the Premiership.

    Sunday

    West Ham v Arsenal
    All of West Ham's goals have been scored by Englishmen this season, while the only English players to score for Arsenal have been own goals.

    Aston Villa v Blackburn
    Blackburn have lost five of the last six Premiership trips to the Midlands. Villa have only lost one of their last 11 Premiership home games.

    Tottenham v Chelsea
    Spurs have failed to score in six league games this season - only Sheffield United have a worse record. Chelsea have opened the scoring in every single league game they have played this season.
