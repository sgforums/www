    Troubled Toon slash price of new away strip - before it's been released

     

    Bargain hunt: Newcastle are offering a 20 per cent discount on their new away strip amid concerns the majority of fans will refuse to buy one - before it's release

     

    Its been dubbed one of the worst football kits ever - and embarrassed bosses at troubled Newcastle must agree after dropping the price before it has been launched.

    The 'awful' yellow and orange striped away kit was unveiled last month and long-suffering Toon supporters have been forced to endure universal taunts from rival fans.

    The garish shirts, dubbed 'lemon sherbets', don't even go on sale until next week but the club have taken the amazing step of reducing the price by 20 per cent.

    Indeed it is yet another blow for the crisis club, who are still desperately searching for a new owner to replace Mike Ashley before the Championship season begins in just three weeks time - and they remain without a permanent manager.

    With uncertainty surrounding the clubs future thousands of fans have neglected to  renew their season tickets in show of defiance to Ashley's embatted regime.

     

    Newcastle fans are queuing up to get their hands on the new second strip... well not quite.

    ''The whole situation at the club is terrible. The shirt is utterly horrendous. I wouldn't buy it even with the 20 per cent off,' said Frank Gilmore, chairman of Independent Newcastle United Supporters club.

    'All of the fans I have spoken to have refused to buy it and I've been getting the mick taken out of me by mates who are Sunderland fans.

    'They must know that no one is going to buy it so they are slashing the price already.

    'I'm worried that the sale is going to take so long that it will be too late  for us when it does come.'  

    The players have been wearing the kit, described on the club's own website as a  'stylish new change kit', during pre-season - but the majority of the Toon faithful remain unconvinced.

    'The club must surely now realise this kit is truly awful,' added another frustrated supporter, Andrew Burnham, from Gateshead, told North News.

     

     

    No laughing matter: Defender Steven Taylor may be smiling in the new strip but many Newcastle fans are tired of being a running joke for rival supporters.

     

    'No fan in there right mind would be seen dead in it - you would look like a deckchair.

    'They'll be lucky if they can give these shirts away. I wouldn't even use it to wash my car with.'
