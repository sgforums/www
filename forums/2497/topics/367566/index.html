    ODDS SLASHED ON RIBERY TO LIVERPOOL

    Odds on Franck Ribery moving to Liverpool have dropped to 8/13 from 66/1 after a flurry of bets on Thursday.

     The French winger appears to want to leave Bayern Munich, with several clubs reportedly interested, including Manchester United, Chelsea and Real Madrid.

     There have been confusing signals coming from Bayern, with some club officials vehemently claiming he will not be sold, and if so only for a world record fee, but Franz Beckenbauer has suggested that the player should be allowed to leave.

     It's doubtful that Liverpool will be able to find the money for such a move, but in what bookmakers described as an 'unprecedented hour of trading' he was backed down from 66/1 to 8/13.

     Sky Bet's Dale Tempest said: "Every price we have put up has been taken on Ribéry moving to Liverpool, which has left us stumped as in recent weeks he has been liked with every other club but the Reds!

     "After seeing money for him to join Man United and Real Madrid at the same time that Bayern Munich say he's going nowhere, we've now seen a flood of bets on him to join Liverpool.

     "8/13 may seem a very short price but after the losses we made last week on Michael Owen, who was gambled down from 20/1 to move to Manchester United, we are taking no chances."

     Owen dropped from 20/1 to evens in a single afternoon - the afternoon before Manchester United confirmed his signing.

    Knee injury sidelines Ribery from Bayern friendly

     

    Bayern Munich winger Franck Ribery will miss Friday night's first pre-season friendly against Salzburg with a knee injury.

    The 26-year-old France international, heavily linked with a move away from Bavaria this summer, sat out training on Thursday and is not expected to resume until early next week.

    Team-mate Philipp Lahm told L'Equipe: "I cannot say yet whether he will be with us this season.

    "But, as long as he is, he must integrate into the squad because the team is the most important thing. He has given his all in recent days and left a good impression."

    The match, Bayern's first under new coach Louis van Gaal, could see Germany striker Mario Gomez make his first appearance in the club's colours on his 24th birthday.
