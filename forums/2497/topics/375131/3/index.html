    s-league is now prop up only with singapore government's coffers with an outfit called the singapore pools. the rest of the corporate sponsors mainly sponsor in kind, so, there is little real money going around the league. remove s-pool and the s-league will be dead too...

    i was involved in the commercial aspect of the league, working with a couple of clubs; one of them actually for free becos they are financially strapped.

    from the start, it hard to mobilize the true interest amongst the local football supporter in the s-league. a very simple case in point, why is geylang based in bedok, etc.? the core of the fan base must reside within the area the team (name) is in. in bpl, there has never been a case where blackburn team is based in leeds. that's travesty! the same reason why swansea or tranmere rovers are still well supported even when they are languishing. that's becos the locals can still relate to the team. this is the very core of the existence of a sports unit. i have lamented this early on but of course the singapore sports bereaucrats know this better. even in the sports franchise business in the usa, when the lakers moved to los angeles, they did not retain the origin of the city, detroit. they have to call it LA Lakers becos they are based in LA and the the name will need to be changed to secure the fan base support. here in singapore, it's all over the place. if there is no emotional support for the team, the team is doomed.

    some argue that the market here is too small, but there's nothing wrong with 3-5000 crowd becos the stadiums are small anyway. it can still create a good atmosphere.

    one last point of many, the folks here who bankroll teams here don't do it for charity. they want to make money out of it. as. why do you think jokers like colin chee introduced african slavery into singapore by bringing in afrikaans here in the guise of getting african reps in the league. africa team playing in the singapore league? only happens when smart alecky singaporean thinks that they can make $ out of it. 

    the only reason the team pull out of the league is because these `owners' worked their sum and realized there is no money to be made. i am not saying that it is wrong becos no one or very few actually works for charity or for the love of the game.

    the s-league today is on a life support system. pull the plug and the whole system collapse. the only reason it's chugging along is becos the support industries it build up. the magnitude of local grassroots business suffering will be greater. the other hand, there's face. let's face it, the mighty PAP never fail right?
