    Disgrace! Fabregas accused in spitting row as Brown rages over 'offside' Gallas winner

     

    CESC FABREGAS was sensationally accused of spitting at Hull assistant manager Brian Horton. The Arsenal skipper allegedly stormed over to Horton after the Gunners’ controversial 2-1 FA Cup win and gobbed at him on The Emirates pitch. 

    Injured Fabregas watched from the bench as the Gunners came back from behind to win the quarter-final 2-1 before clashing with Horton in the tunnel. That came after a William Gallas winner which Hull boss Phil Brown called 'a disgrace' because of an offside claim.

    Hull boss Phil Brown raged: “I was there, I witnessed it. Fabregas spat at our assistant manager. That’s their club captain. Hopefully he’s proud of himself. He spat at his feet.”

    But last night a stunned Fabregas phoned The Sun to vehemently deny the claim. He insisted: “I absolutely and categorically deny I spat at anyone and I cannot understand why Hull are making these accusations against me. 

    “I have never spat at anyone in my life. It is a disgusting habit. Why would I do something like that after a match I was not even playing in? “I was on the pitch at the end of the game celebrating with my team-mates but I promise I did not do anything wrong.”

     

     

    Bust-up: Cesc Fabregas (second right) confronts Hull players and staff at the final whistle.

    There were further claims that Arsenal boss Arsene Wenger, Hull No 2 Horton, Barmby and Gunners stewards were involved in a tunnel bust-up at half-time. 

    Arsenal gaffer Wenger also refused to shake Brown’s hand at the final whistle. Brown felt he was cheated out of a replay by Gallas’ late winner, which was clearly offside.

    FA AWAIT INFO ON `SPITTING` ROW


    The Football Association are aware of the allegations of spitting by Arsenal captain Cesc Fabregas which overshadowed Tuesday night's FA Cup tie with Hull, but await the referee's report and any possible formal complaint from the Tigers' coaching staff.

    Hull boss Phil Brown said he witnessed Fabregas - who denies the allegations - spitting at assistant manager Brian Horton in the tunnel after his side were controversially beaten 2-1 in their quarter-final tie with a late offside goal from William Gallas.

    Horton was thought to be considering making an official complaint to the FA, who are also waiting to see whether referee Mike Riley has mentioned anything in his report.

    Even though Fabregas, who is on the verge of a comeback from three months out with a knee injury, was not in the Arsenal playing squad, the FA still maintain disciplinary jurisdiction.

    However, because the alleged incident was not an on-field matter, it would not fall under the 'fast-track' system and so any investigation would seek submissions from all parties involved, as well as any potential television evidence.

    Fabregas released a statement denying the allegations. "I categorically deny that I spat at anybody after the match," he said. "I have never done this in my whole career on the pitch, so why would I do it when I am not even playing?

    "I can understand the frustration of losing a game to a dubious goal, that has happened to me many times in my career as well. "But this is not the fault of me or any of the Arsenal players."

    Aside from the post-match row, Brown was upset about Gunners boss Arsene Wenger attempting to get Hull players booked for time-wasting. The visitors - who took the lead through Nick Barmby in the 13th minute before Robin van Persie levelled in the second half - were cruelly denied a replay when Gallas headed in an equaliser, despite looking offside.

    Brown felt Riley "succumbed to local pressure" in the second half after his players were jeered by the crowd. "Mr Wenger decides to join in and the result is my goalkeeper getting booked in the second half," he said.

    Wenger, though, was upset with Brown for talking about incidents in the tunnel. "It is very disappointing for him to do that. I will just stick to what I said now - I won't talk about this in public," he said.

    'Spitting' Fabregas off the hook ... but what WAS he doing on the pitch dressed like a hoodie?

    Fabregas what was he doing on the pitch dressed like a hoodie.

     

    Cesc Fabregas is set to escape punishment over the allegations of spitting which marred Arsenal's progress to the FA Cup semi-finals because the match officials failed to monitor the tunnel area.

    But the midfielder came under renewed attack from Hull City manager Phil Brown yesterday and his snarling post-match antics are bound to attract further scrutiny.

    Referee Mike Riley and his assistants abandoned normal after-match protocol on Tuesday night as they felt under siege from angry Hull City players disputing the late winner by William Gallas.

    Usually it is the job of the fourth official and the linesman on the touchline nearest the tunnel to move inside and keep an eye on players as they enter the dressing rooms, while the referee and the other linesman stay outside to make sure everyone leaves the pitch safely.

    But after an incident-packed tie at the Emirates Stadium, Riley and his team headed quickly to their room to avoid the protests. They did not witness any flashpoint involving Fabregas and Hull's assistant manager Brian Horton and there are no CCTV cameras in the tunnel area.

    Fabregas, who missed the game through injury and watched from seats behind the Arsenal bench, has been accused by Brown of spitting at Horton's feet after a verbal exchange which started on the pitch after Arsenal's 2-1 win.

    Fabregas denies the allegations but Brown reiterated his claims yesterday and the FA have written to Brown and Horton asking for an explanation. Asked in a BBC 5 Live interview whether others had seen the spitting incident, Brown snapped: 'Are you questioning my integrity? I witnessed it.'

    Asked whether he stood by his post-match remarks, Brown added: 'Absolutely, 100 per cent. For one, being on the pitch after the game; two, dressing in the manner he was dressed and three, the pictures emphasise the aggression he was showing to my players and staff.

    'Then I witnessed him going down the tunnel and turning round and spitting at my assistant manager's feet. Whatever way you look at it, it doesn't matter, he addressed that spitting incident at my assistant manager.

    'The referee and linesmen had gone down the tunnel and were probably well inside their room, the same as Mr Wenger and the majority of the Arsenal staff and players.'

    Riley's match report contains no mention of the incident and the lack of independent witnesses will make any investigation very difficult.

    Perversely, the one person who may end up facing an FA charge is Brown. The FA were last night studying his wide ranging post-match rant which included an attack on referee Riley, who he claimed had been influenced by the home crowd and Arsenal manager Arsene Wenger.

    Brown is due to appear before an FA disciplinary committee today to answer a charge of improper conduct arising from an FA Cup third-round replay at St James' Park in January, when he and Newcastle manager Joe Kinnear were both sent to the stands after a bust-up.

    The Hull City boss has developed a reputation as an abrasive character and his No 2 Horton is also renowned for standing his ground on the touchline.

    City will be fined £25,000 for accumulating six yellow cards during the Arsenal game but yesterday Brown was still fuming about what he considers to be the disrespect shown to his club by Fabregas.

    The Spaniard is thought to have repeatedly told Hull City's players and their coaching staff to 'f*** off back up north you w*****s'. Sportsmail can reveal that Hull City's players then had to be restrained from attacking the Arsenal midfielder and his teammate Emmanuel Adebayor in the tunnel.

    Despite missing the flashpoint, Riley is unlikely to be in trouble for making a quick dash for his dressing room. It is only a recommendation that the four officials try to police the tunnel area, brought into play when the PFA blocked a proposal for compulsory tunnel cameras. The placement of cameras is left to the discretion of individual clubs.

    The quarter-final at Arsenal had been a stormy affair, not helped by the pressure heaped on Riley from Wenger and his team to stop what they thought was time-wasting by Hull after they went 1-0 up in the 13th minute. That issue triggered the initial row between Horton and Wenger, which simmered for the rest of the game.

    Robin van Persie, captain for the night in the absence of Fabregas and Kolo Toure and scorer of Arsenal's first goal, said: 'It was very frustrating. I felt like they stole almost 10 minutes.

    'Sixty thousand people come to see a football match, not to just sit there waiting. I was quite gutted about it and I told the referee a few times because when you play football, especially on an occasion like this, you should at least try.'
