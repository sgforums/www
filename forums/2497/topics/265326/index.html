    Juventus Go For Deportivo's 10m Andrade

    The latest on Juve's agenda is Deportivo La Coruna centre-half Jorge Andrade.

    "Juventus have made an offer, but it is very far from what we are asking for," Zaragoza sporting director Miguel Pardeza had declared yesterday.

    The likes of Cris, Pepe and Luisao have all been linked, and Jorge Andrade is the latest in the mix, available for 10 million euros.

    Juve are unwilling to dish out more than 16 million euros for Milito, and under the circumstances, Andrade becomes a very viable option.

    The defender is back in form after a serious knee injury that ruined his World Cup hopes, as well as a full nine months of his career.

    The Portuguese international is certain to leave Deportivo this summer, but as with Gabriel Milito, Real Madrid and Barcelona are both in the fray, something that could once again drive up the price.

    Juventus supposedly want a quick move, before the Chivu and Milito sagas come to an end, because the Spanish giants will only go for the Portuguese if they fail to net at least one of those two.

    Jorge Mendes, Andrade's agent, has good relations with Juve, having just facilitated Tiago's move to Turin, and could be a factor. Deportivo are supposedly keen on a loan deal for Raffaele Palladino.
