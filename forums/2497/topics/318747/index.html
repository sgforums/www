    TERRY: Haunted by miss

     

    FABIO CAPELLO is considering offering John Terry a timely boost by making him captain for England’s friendly against the USA.

    Chelsea skipper Terry, who said yesterday that his penalty miss in the Champions League final will haunt him for the rest of his life, could be handed the armband back for Wednesday’s game at Wembley.

    Capello, auditioning for the role of permanent captain for next season, has so far tried out Steven Gerrard and Rio Ferdinand in the games against Switzerland and France respectively.

    Terry was skipper under  previous coach Steve McClaren, and Capello will finalise a decision in the next 24 hours over whether to offer Terry the opportunity to prove he can lead England forward.

    The move would provide a much-needed shot in the arm for Terry, who would have won the Champions League for Chelsea against Manchester United only to slip as he took his penalty and watch his shot skim a post. “I have relived that moment every minute since it happened. I have only slept a few hours and wake up every time hoping it has all been a bad dream,” said Terry.

    “I walked forward to take the penalty knowing the game was there to be won and it was all down to me. What happened next will haunt me for the rest of my life. I have watched it again on TV and you can see I slipped – I still can’t believe it.

    David Beckham, meanwhile, was due to join up with the England squad after scoring a 70-yard goal in LA Galaxy’s 3-1 victory over Kansas City Wizards on Saturday.
