    Report: Manchester City Set To Axe Brazilian Ace Robinho From Premier League Squad

    Manchester City striker Robinho faces an embarrassing axe from the club's Premier League squad if they fail to find a buyer before the close of the transfer window.

    Robinho, Manchester City (Getty Images)

    According to The Sunday Mirror, the £32.5 million record British signing has been told to report to Eastlands within 48 hours of his country's friendly against the USA on Tuesday.

    Robinho then will have to prove his fitness and mentality to boss Roberto Mancini, who will need to be convinced that the player is ready for the new season if he is to be considered for a place in the squad that travels to Tottenham for the the opening day fixture.

    City hope Robinho can find a club that will meet their £25 million price tag, with Benfica and Besiktas both showing an interest, but as yet being unable to stump up the required fee.

    Robinho has begged City to allow him to continue at Santos, where he has been on loan for the last seven months, a move partly designed to help him gain his place in the Brazilian World Cup squad.

    However, Robinho now wishes to extend his stay in his homeland after successfully helping Santos win the Copa do Brasil, but City are not keen on another temporary deal.

    If a buyer cannot be found by August 31, Robinho will then face the task of proving himself to City and showing that he is committed to the club.

    A City insider told The Sunday Mirror, "It's crazy to think that the most expensive player in British football might be left out of our Premier League squad, but Robinho will have to show that he is worthy of his place. He has been informed he won't be allowed to go out on loan again and we are hopeful a buyer will materialise.

    "While the club is willing to take a loss on what was paid to Real Madrid, there is a determination to recoup a significant amount of the fee."

    With the new Premier League rules being implemented, Robinho could end up banking his £160,000 a week wages while only featuring in cup ties.
