    He is encouraged by what he has heard and with the interest of other English clubs failing to crystallise, notably that of Manchester City, he is ready to commit to Harry Redknapp and Portsmouth. His advisers listened to City's proposals at the end of last week but the lines of communication to Eastlands have cooled and, with Arsenal reluctant to sell to their neighbours Tottenham Hotspur, whose manager, Juande Ramos, would love to sign Diarra, Portsmouth have made their move.

    The other Premier League club to show their hand for one of the hottest properties of the transfer window were Newcastle United but the turmoil over their managerial vacancy has meant that new signings have been pushed down the list of priorities.

    Diarra is desperate for first-team football because he believes he has a genuine chance of starting for France in the European Championship in the summer. He fears that if his status as a fringe Arsenal player persists he may miss the opportunity. The France manager, Raymond Domenech, has favoured Patrick Vieira and Claude Makelele as a central midfield partnership but Vieira has had a series of injuries and Makelele has struggled to command a regular place at Chelsea. Whenever Diarra has appeared for France he has impressed.
