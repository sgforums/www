    CHEL HELL ... Blues rejected chance to sign Spurs' main man Rafael van der Vaart

     

     

     

    CHELSEA turned down the chance to sign Dutch star Rafael van der Vaart in the summer.

    The Blues reckoned the brilliant midfielder - now with Spurs - was "not good enough."

    So they rejected a £10million deal and instead spent £24m on Yossi Benayoun and Ramires.

    A Chelsea insider revealed: "We had a call to say Jose Mourinho was ready to let Van der Vaart leave Real Madrid for around £10m and did we want him.

    "But it was decided he wasn't good enough so we passed on him. That's not looking the best of decisions is it?"

    Van der Vaart instead joined Tottenham for £8m on deadline day and has been an instant hit with 10 goals so far this season.

    But Ramires and Benayoun have both failed to make any significant impact. Israeli Benayoun, 30, has managed six games having been sidelined since September with a ruptured Achilles.

    Brazilian Ramires, 23, has struggled to come to terms with the pace and power of the Premier League.

    SunSport's Chelsea source added: "Van der Vaart is just the sort of player we need - someone with flair, skill and vision.

    "Our midfielders are too alike - they are strong and powerful, good athletes but not with a great deal of invention."
