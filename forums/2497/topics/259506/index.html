Chelsea anger over Real claims

  • Slipshade
    7 Jun 07, 22:41
    Chelsea chief executive Peter Kenyon says they intend to report Real Madrid to Fifa over what they deem as an illegal approach for Arjen Robben.

    Real supremo Ramon Calderon revealed on Thursday that he believed talks had taken place with Robben with a view to him moving to El Stantiago Bernabeu.

    However, Chelsea have reacted angrily to Calderon's claims and insist that there are no official talks talking place with Real over Robben.

    "We note that Mr Calderon confirms Real Madrid spoke to Arjen," said Kenyon.

    "As a result Chelsea will be writing to Fifa to ask them to investigate an illegal approach by Real Madrid.

    "There have been no meetings or conversations between the Real Madrid sporting director Predrag Mijatovic with any Chelsea officials, anyone mandated to act on Chelsea's behalf or owner Roman Abramovich about Arjen's future."
