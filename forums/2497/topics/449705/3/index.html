    I won’t quit

    Steve is Kean to stay

    STEVE KEAN insists he will not quit Blackburn even if they go down this season.

    And the defiant Rovers boss believes the club are in a much better financial position than when he was appointed manager 18 months ago.

    Blackburn must beat Wigan tomorrow to stand a realistic chance of survival.

    But Kean said: “I don’t expect to go down but if we do I definitely want to be here and I expect to be here.

    “The target for us is to make sure we’re in the division we want to be in which is the top division.

    “Financially, we’ve got a lot of income from transfers and I’d suggest the capital inside the squad is a lot healthier than a couple of years ago.

    “Think about the value of Martin Olsson, Grant Hanley, Jason Lowe and Marcus Olsson. We got them for nothing.

    “Young players have come in and played so well. If someone comes in for them we will be demanding very high fees.

    “We have an up-and-coming group, the youngest in the Premier League. They are exciting players and coming through this experience will help them.

    “I’m confident we can beat Wigan and take it to the final game at Chelsea.”

    Kean has been a target for abuse from unhappy Ewood Park fans all season and was criticised for playing too defensively in last Sunday’s 2-0 defeat at Tottenham.

    But he insisted: “We need to make sure we start the game brighter. Tottenham had chances particularly in the first few minutes and we got pegged back.

    “But we have to improve on that performance as we have another big game against Chelsea coming. We can’t perform the way we did against Tottenham as that’s unacceptable.”
