    Liverpool negotiating with at least five clubs over deadline striker deal

     



     

     

    Liverpool boss Roy Hodgson hasn’t given up hope of a dramatic late swoop to bring in a striker before the transfer deadline closes at 6pm.

    The Anfield manager was in talks today with at least five different clubs as he attempted to find back up for star centre forward Fernando Torres.

    The Reds are trying to bring German international striker Mario Gomez in on a year long loan, and even though his club Bayern Munich are resistant to any loan deal, Hodgson is continuing to negotiation.

    He has also made enquiries about West Ham striker Carlton Cole and Spurs forward Peter Crouch, and is prepared to offer winger Ryan Babel as a makeweight in any deal.

    Liverpool have also made discreet enquiries about the possibility of a loan deal for Manchester City striker Roque Santa Cruz, even as the Paraguay striker was in talks with Lazio over a similar move.

    Hodgson also hasn’t given up hope entirely about persuading PSV Eindhoven to sell their highly rated centre forward Ola Toivonen, who has already scored six goals this season.

    The Sweden international is valued at £12million by his Dutch club, but Liverpool want to pay almost half that figure, after a recommendation by Kenny Dalglish.

    The Merseyside club have also made enquiries about young Dutch forward Ricky Van Wolfswinkel, the 21 year old Holland international forward.

    He scored a hat trick for his club Utrecht against Celtic in the Europa League, and he is rated highly by his club.

    Hodgson is also reported to have made enquiries about his former Fulham striker Bobby Zamora, and he will continue to push until the 6pm deadline in an attempt to bring in the striker he desperately needs.

    The Reds boss has already tied up one deal, with Paul Konchesky arriving from Craven Cottage in a £3million deal which includes Anfield youngsters Lauri Dalla Valle and Alexander Kacaniklic going in the other direction, while defender Daniel Ayala has gone on a year long loan to Hull, with Stoke also trying to prise midfielder Lucas out of Anfield.

    West Ham reject Liverpool's £9m Cole bid

     

     

    Carlton Cole: Attracting transfer interest

     

    West Ham United have turned down a deadline day £9 million bid for England striker Carlton Cole.

     West Ham rejected the offer, which they had been expecting for some time, because it came too late and left them no chance of signing a replacement.
     
    Roy Hodgson has been desperately searching for a new striker, thwarted in several attempts to buy a player from abroad, and when he finally went for Cole it was unacceptable for the Hammers.
     

    Sunderland and Stoke also bid £9 million, plus £3 million in add-ons, for Cole, but the centre forward would only have quit Upton Park for a bigger club.

    West Ham were made aware of Liverpool's interest, initially reported exclusively by Soccernet, only tentatively through third parities.

    Ryan Babel had been tipped to sign for the Hammers in a swap deal, but he now seems set to head for Tottenham in a £10 million transfer.

    West Ham are also interested in signing a goalkeeper and Roma's Brazilian No. 2 Doni is top of their wishlist.

    The 30-year-old has been told he can leave Italy with Bogdan Lobonc and Julio Sergio favoured between the posts for the Serie A side and could arrive at Upton Park on loan. Doni has also been linked with Portuguese side Benfica and Manchester City as well.

    Two players who will not be leaving Upton Park are Scott Parker and Matthew Upson, who have committed their futures to the club, for the time being at least.

    Liverpool tried to loan Cole from Hammers

     

     

    Carlton Cole: Attracting transfer interest

     

    Liverpool tried to loan Carlton Cole until Christmas because they couldn't finance a permanent deal unless they sold Ryan Babel, according to Soccernet sources close to the attempted transaction.

    Unable to sell Babel, they offered West Ham the alternative of a loan fee of £1 million plus Babel on loan for the same period. However, Babel wasn't keen on a move to Upton Park, and the Hammers were not interested in the unusual swap loan idea.
     

    The approach to sign Cole took place at 3pm on the final day of the transfer window, just three hours before it closed, with Liverpool only prepared to offer the Hammers £9 million for Cole if they could sell Babel to Tottenham Hotspur for £10 million, but that deal eventually collapsed.

    West Ham were left waiting for Liverpool to try to offload Babel, and as the deadline drew closer, the Hammers ran out of time to find a replacement for Cole.
