    Kaka: Pirlo should come to Madrid

    Kaka has told his former Milan team-mate Andrea Pirlo to join him at Real Madrid.

    Milan owner Silvio Berlusconi has revealed that he is now willing to sell Pirlo having become aware of his salary, and Chelsea are reportedly ready to make a move.

    However, Kaka, who played alongside Pirlo for six years, has advised his new club to make a move.

    “Berlusconi wants to sell Pirlo? In that case, I’m sorry for Milan,” he said in Corriere dello Sport.

    “But Andrea is a great player.

    “At this point, I’d like to see him come to Real.”

    Reports suggest Milan may look to sign Madrid midfielder Fernando Gago as a replacement for Pirlo.

    Milan deny Blues Pirlo bid

    Chelsea have made no offer for Andrea Pirlo, according to Milan administrator Adriano Galliani, who has denied any bids have been received for the midfielder.

    New Blues boss Carlo Ancelotti has been strongly linked with a raid on his former club to prise the Italy international away from the San Siro.

    Reports in England claimed Chelsea were closing in on the midfielder, but Galliani brushed aside any such claims.

    "We have not received any offers for Pirlo, not from Chelsea or any other club," said Galliani.

    "Pirlo is a Milan player," he added.  "If Pirlo leaves then we will see."
