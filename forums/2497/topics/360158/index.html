    ANCELOTTI ... heading to Chelsea

     

     

    CARLO ANCELOTTI is expected to confirm tomorrow that he will take over at Chelsea in the summer. 

    Sources close to the Italian coach believe he will make the announcement if his AC Milan side clinch a Champions League place by beating Udinese. 

    Ancelotti, 49, was stunned and disappointed by the verbal attack launched on him by Milan owner Silvio Berlusconi this week. 

    Berlusconi blamed the coach and his tactics for costing AC the Serie A title which will be won by Jose Mourinho’s Inter. 

    That was the final straw for Ancelotti who is understood to have been in touch with Chelsea officials since to confirm he is taking the job at Stamford Bridge. 

    He will remain at the San Siro for the remainder of the season before joining the Blues next month. 

    Ancelotti has already been involved in identifying new players for Chelsea and SunSport revealed this week he has identified Aston Villa’s Ashley Young as a top target.

    Ancelotti laughs off latest Blues rumours

    Carlo Ancelotti has firmly denied that he will be confirmed as Chelsea’s new manager this weekend and has described as ‘fanciful’ suggestions that he is already dictating potential signings to the Premier League club.

    Ancelotti is already seen as the heir apparent to the Chelsea throne in the English press with various reports claiming he could even be unveiled as Guus Hiddink’s replacement imminently.

    “I do not think anything will happen after the game on Saturday evening,” Ancelotti told the Italian press.

    “Sunday I am free. I will be with my father as it is his birthday.

    “Seriously, nothing will happen, either Saturday or Sunday.

    “I do not expect phone calls from the president, he has made a clear refutation, there is no need for any comments.

    “The press tends to sensationalise situations. There has always been and there will always be a very good relationship between me and the club.

    “There is no story Chelsea-Milan.”

    Recent reports in England have claimed that Ancelotti is already shaping Chelsea’s transfer policy for the coming summer with Arsenal striker Emmanuel Adebayor and Milan stars Andrea Pirlo and Kaka amongst his reported targets.

    But Ancelotti has ridiculed suggestions that he has handed Roman Abramovich a wishlist, stating: “These are voices that are imaginative. It is more than fanciful.”

    Frank Rijkaard next on the list as AC Milan talk with Carlo Ancelotti

     

     

    Chelsea are approaching D-Day in their attempt to make Carlo Ancelotti their next manager. The AC Milan coach will hold discussions over his future with Silvio Berlusconi, the Milan owner, on Sunday, with Chelsea preparing to turn to Frank Rijkaard as a fall-back option.

    Ancelotti is known to be flattered by Chelsea’s interest, with Roman Abramovich having made clear that he is No 1 choice to take over when Guus Hiddink, the interim manager, departs at the end of the season.

    But with nobody certain if Ancelotti will leave Milan, the Chelsea owner has been forced to draw up a list of alternative targets, including Rijkaard, the former Barcelona coach who has received a glowing endorsement from Hiddink.

    The difficulty for Chelsea has been in ascertaining the extent of the damage to Ancelotti’s relationship with Berlusconi, the Italian Prime Minister, who this week seemed to blame the coach for Milan’s failure to beat Inter Milan to the Serie A title. On occasions in recent weeks, Ancelotti has appeared in danger of losing his job, but Berlusconi tried to distance himself yesterday from that earlier interview and is expected to endeavour on Sunday to persuade the coach to reject Chelsea’s interest.

    Adriano Galliani, the Milan vice president, was insistent that Ancelotti will remain at the San Siro for at least another 12 months. “Carlo has a contract with us until 2010 and he will be on the bench of the Rossoneri next season,” Galliani said. “I spoke earlier to president Berlusconi and he was absolutely astonished to read what had been written.”

    Ancelotti has been made aware of the financial terms he would be offered at Stamford Bridge, but if Sunday’s meeting ends with him declaring his wish to join Chelsea, Milan are certain to try to play hardball by demanding at least ¤5 million (about £4.5 million) in compensation.

    Yuri Zhirkov, the Russia winger, is firmly expected to move to Chelsea in a deal that might be facilitated by Abramovich’s links with his club, CSKA Moscow, while strong rumours persist of an offer to Arsenal to sign Emmanuel Adebayor. Deco, the Portugal midfield player, may be offered a move to Corinthians.

    Ancelotti eyes Tevez coup as he prepares to be unveiled as Chelsea's new boss

     

    Adios: Carlos Tevez says goodbye to fans after United's goalless draw with Arsenal at Old Trafford on Saturday

     

    Carlo Ancelotti is set to be confirmed as Chelsea manager.

    AC Milan have lined up Marco van Basten to take over at the San Siro, clearing Ancelotti to replace Guus Hiddink at Stamford Bridge. And high on the Italian's agenda will be whether to make an approach for Argentina striker Carlos Tevez.

    Ancelotti, who discussed terms on a three-year Chelsea contract back in April, is a long-term admirer of Tevez. The 25-year-old is set to leave Manchester United after a two-year loan deal and Blues owner Roman Abramovich is planning to back heavily his new coach in the transfer market.

     

     

     

    Come in, Carlo: Ancelotti is set to be unveiled as Chelsea boss

     

     

    Liverpool and Manchester City would provide competition for a deal worth at least £25million to sign him from a consortium led by businessman Kia Joorabchian.

    Sportsmail revealed in April that Chelsea had turned to Ancelotti after conceding that Hiddink could not be persuaded to stay on beyond the end of this season.

    And it is expected they will make an announcement soon after the FA Cup Final against Everton on May 30 and Milan's final Serie A match a day later.

    Interim boss Hiddink is keen not to impinge on his duties as Russia's head coach in the run-up to next year's World Cup, but he will continue to advise Abramovich in an informal role.

    Milan have put up a fight to keep Ancelotti but the relationship between him and owner Silvio Berlusconi has soured in recent weeks as it became clearer that the former Italy midfielder's future lay elsewhere.

    Milan's defeat by Udinese on Saturday confirmed rivals Inter as champions and Berlusconi has criticised the coach for results and not using Ronaldinho enough.

    Ancelotti will still not confirm his intentions publicly, but Berlusconi opened the way for his departure.

    The supremo said: 'At the end of the season we will sit around a table and find the best solution for him (Ancelotti) and Milan. But if he goes, you can bet on a younger man emerging. Now there are many with wide shoulders ready for drive a great team.'

    Former Milan playing legend Van Basten is believed to have held talks about the San Siro job after arriving in the city over the weekend. Ancelotti appears to already have his mind on next season's Barclays Premier League title quest, warning over the weekend that Manchester United would not have it so easy next term.

    ‘My compliments to Sir Alex Ferguson for his victory,' said the 49-year-old. 'They have had, and they are still having, an extraordinary season. I have great respect for Manchester United. ‘The Premier League is very competitive and there are other teams who can win next season.’
