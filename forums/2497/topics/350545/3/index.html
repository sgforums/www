    Middlesbrough Game Will Decide Newcastle United’s Fate - Alan Shearer

    After crashing to defeat at Anfield, the Toon boss is already looking forward to next weekend.

    Newcastle United manager Alan Shearer admitted his side’s next game against Middlesbrough will be crucial if the Magpies are to avoid the drop this season.

    The Tynesiders slumped to a 3-0 defeat at Liverpool this afternoon and speaking to Setanta after the game, Shearer confessed his team had been well-beaten.  

    “I thought the first goal was offside but nevertheless that wouldn’t have changed the outcome of the game,” he said.

    “We were well-beaten today by a better side and we move on to better things hopefully.”

    Goals by Yossi Benayoun and Dirk Kuyt gave the Reds a 2-0 lead at half-time before Lucas Levia added the third late on, yet Shearer insisted Liverpool might have scored even more goals.

    “I thought if we could get one early second half then it might change one or two things. But we were always up against it because they are an excellent side, even without Torres,” he said.  

    “Three-nil it could have been more, perhaps should of, they’ve hit the bar a few times, but a disappointing day.”

    Newcastle finished the game with 10-men after Joey Barton was sent off for a foul on Xabi Alonso and Shearer revealed the midfielder had apologised to him for the red card.

    “He apologised, it’s been done,” said the Newcastle boss.

    “I’m sure people will look at it and he’s suspended now for the rest of the season.”

    The result leaves the Magpies three points from safety with only three games left to play.

    Next up for the St James’ Park side is a home game against fellow strugglers Middlesbrough which Shearer insisted was absolutely crucial.

    “Time is running out but we’ve still got two very, very important home games which will decide our fate, there’s no doubt about that,” he admitted.

    “The game against Middlesbrough, whoever loses that, will, I think, find it very difficult to come back from that. We know the stakes and we’ll get ready for it.

    “I believe we will get something from the next two home games, I really do. It’s easy to say that, we’ve got to go out and do that. We’ll regroup, we’ll get ready and we’ll work hard and we’ll be ready for next weekend.”
