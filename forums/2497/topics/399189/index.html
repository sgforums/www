    John Terry's World Cup dream in serious doubt after England and Chelsea defender suffers suspected broken metatarsal

     

     

     

     

    John Terry's World Cup dream is in serious doubt after the Chelsea captain was rushed to hospital having suffered a suspected broken metatarsal in training today.

    The defender is now set to miss the FA Cup final against Portsmouth following the accidental collision this morning at the Blues' Cobham training headquarters.

    Terry, 29, is wearing a protective boot on his right foot and must wait until Thursday to discover his scan results which will determine his availability for South Africa.

    A Chelsea statement read: 'John Terry has today taken a knock in training and has gone to hospital for a precautionary scan on his right foot. He left Cobham walking freely in a protective boot, and we await the results which are expected on Thursday.'

    If Terry's worst fears are realised, it would compound a horrendous year for the defender.

    The furore over off-field revelations surrounding his private life, after his affair with former team-mate Wayne Bridge's ex-girlfriend Vanessa Perroncel, saw him stripped of his England captaincy after a 12-minute showdown with Fabio Capello in February.

    It is the latest metatarsal horror to hit the national side in the build-up to a major tournament.

    Manchester United striker Wayne Rooney was rushed into a recovery for the finals in Germany after he broke his fourth metatarsal four years ago, two years after suffering the same anguish six weeks before the European Championships in 2000.

    David Beckham infamously sustained the injury after Deportivo's Aldo Duscher left the midfielder sweating over his England place two months before the finals in 2002.

     

    Pain game: 2010 has largely been a year to forget for Terry, who was stripped of his England captaincy in February

     

     

    The news comes as a further blow to England manager Capello, with serious injury doubts over midfielder Gareth Barry and skipper Rio Ferdinand.

    The Italian, who named his 30-man provisional World Cup squad on Tuesday, must now decide whether to draft in another defender.

    Ironically, should Terry miss out, that man could be Manchester City full-back Wayne Bridge, who earlier this year declared he would not play for England alongside Terry.
