Reading go online to cure their Little problem

  • omgukilledkenny
    18 Sep 07, 22:56


    Reading's medical team are surfing the Internet in a desperate bid to find a cure for Glen Little's Achilles problem.

    The Royals winger has been sidelined with the problem since April and boss Steve Coppell is becoming increasingly frustrated with his failure to make a recovery.

    They are now seeking specialist advice from around the world in an attempt to get him back in action.

    Coppell said: "He gets so far with his recovery, but no further. It is a real mystery.

    "We are scouring the internet and our own resources to find the leading man in the world on Achilles injuries. He is making progress, but it is so painfully slow it is almost unnoticeable.

    "It is a big concern for us."

    Long lay-off for unlucky Little

    Reading's injury woes mounted after it was confirmed Glen Little is to undergo Achilles surgery which will rule him out for up to six months.

    Little, 31, has been plagued with injury throughout his career. They have limited his impact since his move from Burnley in 2004 and he has not appeared at all for the Premier League side this season.

    Royals physio Jon Fearn told the club's official website: "Glen and I recently saw a specialist in Achilles management problems.

    "He thinks that what we may have is a small tear in the tendon that has not responded to the treatment we have given him."
