    Inter Milan Form Guide

    A two-legged blockbuster tie against the best team on the planet was always going to stretch Inter Milan to their vigilant best.

    Nevertheless, buoyed by a resolute 2-0 win over Juventus in the Derby D’Italia which put their Serie A title challenge firmly back on track after an almighty wobble in recent weeks, the Italian champions will feel now is the time to end their 45-year wait for a third European crown.

    Recollections of previous encounters with Barcelona however do not bode well. Outplayed in the group stages at the San Siro before being arraigned 2-0 at the Camp Nou, it is not difficult to see why the Nerazzurri start the clash as underdogs.

    Coach Jose Mourinho will say he cares not, but his lugubrious expression suggests that it is on his mind.

    Barcelona Form Guide

    After a well-deserved 2-0 win over eternal rivals Real Madrid in El Clasico, Barcelona were brought back down a peg or two after being held 0-0 by city neighbours Espanyol.

    "We will go all out to win the game, as always," midfielder Sergio Busquets told Spanish daily AS. "This is a decisive stretch of the season, we are in a semi-final of the Champions [League] and we will go for it," he added.

    Past Encounters Between the two Sides

    In football’s very own version of a twist in the tail, the tie will be suffused with past connections, as well as former team-mates in Samuel Eto’o, Maxwell and Zlatan Ibrahimovic returning to their old stomping grounds.

    The reappearance of Los Cules’ most detested man after Luis Figo and Florentino Perez – Jose Mourinho, whose previous battles with the Spanish champions whilst manager of Chelsea has not been forgotten in a hurry, will be interesting for the neutral.

    Send the kids to bed early, lock the doors, grab a cold beer and tune in with zeal for a potential European classic!

    Team News:

    Inter Milan manager Jose Mourinho is set to start with attacking trio of Wesley Sneijder, Samuel Eto'o and Diego Milito as the Nerazzurri attempt to create a buffer zone between themselves and their hosts in the first-leg.

    Only backup goalkeeper Francesco Toldo and full-back Davide Santon are unavailable for the Italian champions.

    Despite making a six-hour round trip to Milan by coach, apart from defender Gerard Pique, full-back Eric Abidal and midfielder Andres Iniesta, Barcelona have no injury worries to contend with. Captain Carles Puyol returns to the first-eleven after being suspended for the second leg against Arsenal.

    Likely Line-ups:

    Inter Milan (4-2-1-3): Julio Cesar, Maicon, Samuel, Chivu, Zanetti; Cambiasso, Stankovic; Sneijder; Pandev, Milito, Eto'o.

    Barcelona (4-3-3): Valdes, Dani Alves, Milito, Puyol, Maxwell, Busquets, Keita, Xavi, Messi, Ibrahimovic, Henry.

    Prediction:

    Inter Milan 2-1 Barcelona
