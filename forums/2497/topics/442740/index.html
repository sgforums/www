    Euro 2012: England avoid giants as Republic land tough draw

     

    EUR THE MAN ... Fabio Capello will take charge of England at next summer's Euro 2012 finals

    Euro 2012 draw

     

    Group A: Poland, Greece, Russia, Czech Republic 

    Group B: Holland, Denmark, Germany, Portugal 

    Group C: Spain, Italy, Republic of Ireland, Croatia 

    Group D: Ukraine, Sweden, France, England

     

     

     

     

    FABIO CAPELLO breathed a huge sigh of relief as England avoided a Euro 2012 Group of Death.

    The Three Lions, who have been cut from odds of 12-1 to 10-1 to win next summer's finals, were yesterday drawn in Group D along with France, Sweden and co-hosts Ukraine.

    But the England boss admitted he was panicking at the prospect of being placed in Group B which ended up with Holland, Denmark, Germany and Portugal.

    Capello said: "There was a terrible moment while I waited because Group B was really strong.

    "After we got past that, groups C and D were difficult, but Group B was strongest.

    "I'm happy because I know all the teams. They're all very good and play well and they'll be difficult games.

    "The most difficult will be the first one against France on June 11 because the first game is always hardest with the pressure.

    "The result of that game will be really important. But the most important thing will be the players who will stay with me here in Poland and Ukraine and that they'll be at the top of their form.

    "The first game will be difficult and the last one against the hosts is tough.

    "But all the games in this tournament will be difficult because the levels of the teams will be very high."

    The big blow is England will play all their group games in Ukraine — despite being based in the Polish city of Krakow.

    England's opener will be in Donetsk. They then play Sweden in Kiev on Friday, June 15 and return to Donetsk for their match against Ukraine on Tuesday, June 19. The draw was not kind to the Republic of Ireland, though.

    Their odds of winning the competition went right out to 100-1 after they were matched with Spain, Italy and Croatia.

    Group A was dubbed the 'Group of Life' with any of Poland, Greece, Russia and the Czech Republic capable of making it through.
