    POOL LOSE AGAIN

    Has Pool's Title Charge Been Ended By Stray Beach Ball?


     

     

     

    Liverpool's title challenge looks in serious trouble after they lost their fourth game of the season, going down 1-0 to Sunderland in bizarre circumstances.

    Darren Bent was credited with the only goal at the Stadium of Light, but the ball took a wicked deflection off a beach ball - which appeared to have been thrown onto the pitch by a Liverpool fan - and deceived Pepe Reina.

    As the football struck the beach ball and veered to Reina's left, the Spanish keeper appeared to react to and follow the beach ball, who dived the wrong way as the ball flew into the net.

     

     

     

    Liverpool were furious and protested to referee Mike Jones and his assistant but any claims fell on deaf ears. The Reds were furious, and it could have got worse as Lorik Cana met Reid's corner with a firm header but he missed the target.

    Rafa Benitez - without both Steven Gerrard and Fernando Torres - experimented with a 3-5-2 formation, but the visitors put in a disjointed and rather disorganised performance.

    The match will also be remembered for a touchline flare-up between Benitez and Sunderland boss Steve Bruce.

    Bruce and Benitez became involved in a furious row on the sidelines as the injured Jones was treated on the pitch. But the fourth official eventually separated the pair as tensions grew.

     

    Benitez made a number of switches as the match went on but his side could not muster the equaliser they desperately wanted.

     

     

     

    Sunderland: Gordon, Bardsley, Turner, Ferdinand, McCartney (Henderson 46), Malbranque, Cana, Cattermole (Zenden 58), Reid, Bent, Jones (Campbell 66). Subs Not Used: Fulop, Nosworthy, Da Silva, Healy. Booked: Cana, Reid. Goals: Bent 5.

     

    Liverpool: Reina, Johnson, Agger, Skrtel (Voronin 72), Carragher, Babel (Ngog 81), Lucas, Aurelio, Spearing (Mascherano 72), Benayoun, Kuyt. Subs Not Used: Cavalieri, Riera, Insua, Kelly. Booked: Kuyt.

    Att: 47,327

    Ref: Mike Jones (Cheshire).

    RAFA REFUSES TO BLAME FLUKE



    Liverpool Rafa Benitez refused to blame the fluke goal which gave Sunderland their 1-0 victory.

     

    The only goal at the Stadium of Light arrived after five minutes when Darren Bent's shot defelected off a beach ball and past the helpless Jose Reina.

     

    But Benitez admits the goal should have stood and he could have no complaints about the result after a poor performance.

     

     

     

     

    STEVE BRUCE says only football's biggest nerd would have disallowed Sunderland's mad winner. And the Black Cats boss shrugged off a spat with Rafa Benitez as "handbags".

    Darren Bent's bizarre strike deflected off a Liverpool club balloon and past gobsmacked Pepe Reina. Ref Mike Jones allowed the goal — even though there's a law in the footy rulebook for dealing with objects on the pitch.

    And Bruce said: "Listen, I thought it was a deflection off a player. I have to say when I have just seen it there, if anybody knew that rule — that it is supposed to be a drop ball — then you are a saddo.

    "They have got it on telly with the guy who threw it on and it's got Liverpool crests all over it. What a shame."

    Benitez refused to blame the freak goal for Liverpool's loss. He said: "It's a very technical question. It could be a goal, it's difficult to say. In this case, it has to be a goal.

    "These things can happen in a lot of games. It was a bad situation for us - it was in the middle [of the goal] and it was very influential. "But we didn't play well, that is the main thing for me."

    Bruce blew his top at Benitez after the Spaniard complained at injured Kenwyne Jones' lengthy on-pitch treatment. But the Sunderland chief brushed aside the clash after the game. He added: "It was handbags. It's all over, it's done.

    "You do things in the heat of the moment which, when you look back at it, you think, 'Maybe I shouldn't have done that', but there you go."
