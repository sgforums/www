    There could yet be a shock twist to the English Premier League title race.

    Manchester United face a probe, which could lead to a points deduction, into Tim Howard's 3 million pounds(s $9million) transfer to Everton.

    The goalkeeper joined Everton on a year-long loan deal last summer, and it was made permanent in February.

    Under the loan deal, he could not play for Everton against United. But that restriction no longer applied when the deal was made permanent.

    Still, United asked for Howard not to play against them on April 28 as one of the conditions of making the transfer permanent.

    So Everton played Iain Turner. They were up 2-0 when the second-choice goalkeeper made a bad mistake to allow John O'Shea to score.

    The Red Devils eventually won 4-2 at Goodison Park.

    Both clubs have declined to comment.

    The League's spokesman Dan Johnson told the News of the World : " As Howard is a permanent transfer, there is no reason why he could not have played against Manchester United.''

    "Had such a clause been included in the transfer, we would not have allowed it. It would have been a clear case of third-party interference.''

    The issue of third-party agreements in player transfers has been under the spotlight. West Ham were fined a record 5.5million pounds on April 27 for breaking the rules over the transfers of Argentinians Carlos Tevez and Javier Mascherano.

    Last season, Brimingham were refused permission to insert a clause into transfer deal forbidding Robbie Savage playing against them for his new club, Blackburn
