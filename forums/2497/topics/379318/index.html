    Liverpool beach balls selling fast as rival fans flock to Anfield to snap up £10 bargain set

    Double vision: An errant red beach ball cost Liverpool three points at Sunderland

    'One gentleman came in this morning and asked for three sets,' a club shop assistant told Sportsmail Online.

     

     

    Rival football fans hoping to rub salt into the wounds of Rafa Benitez following the freak goal at Sunderland are flocking to the Anfield megastore to snap-up £10 'beach sets', containing a replica of the now in-famous red ball, a club source revealed.

    Pepe Reina was fatefully deceived by Darren Bent's freakish winner at the Stadium of Light when the keeper was cruelly sent the wrong way after the striker's shot deflected off of a red beach ball thrown onto a pitch by a young Liverpool supporter.

    Although the set was mysteriously unavailable on Liverpool's official online megastore on Monday morning, the increasingly popular product is available to buy at the club's stadium store where punters are queuing to get their hands on the bizarre souvenir.

    The Reds' website was bombarded by rival supporters looking to snap up the reduced price beach sets (down from £20), complete with a bag, towel and, of course, now in-famous beach ball.

    Despite the online shop reporting that the new must-have for any visiting supporter to Anfield is 'no longer available', the stadium store confirmed that they have plenty in reserve and expect to sell many more in the coming weeks.

    The club source added: 'Lots of people have come in to ask about them. 'They are becoming really popular after Saturday so we have enough sets left to sell.'

     

     

     

    Sale of the century: Rival fans flocked to the Liverpool website to snap up £10 'beach sets' which are now no longer available to buy from their online store

     

    While Benitez and Liverpool followers will struggle to see the funny side of the story, several postings were reported online on Sunday, reviewing their 'bargain' buy.

    Natalie from Manchester, told Yahoo.com: 'I bought this set, but seem to have lost the ball in Sunderland. Is it possible to order a replacement?'

    Another supporter from London joked: 'This is one of the most flexible products I have ever purchased. Great for not only the beach, but can also play up front.'

    In what could prove to be this year's ultimate Christmas stocking-filler item for football fans, surely it can only be a matter of time before fellow Barclays Premier League clubs start to cash in on replica red balls...

    Liverpool to search United fans for beach balls after club shop sells out

    • United supporters planning beach ball tribute

     

      

    Liverpool have sold out of the £10 "Beach Set" that diverted the team's title prospects at Sunderland and will search Manchester United supporters for any offending items, including beach balls, before Sunday's Premier League clash at Anfield.

    The club's online store has experienced a rush on the "Beach Set" package following Darren Bent's winning goal at the Stadium of Light on Saturday, when his shot struck a Liverpool-crested beach ball and ricocheted beyond goalkeeper José Reina.

    United supporters are suspected of being behind the increased demand for Liverpool beach products, with a "tribute" to Bent's goal planned but risk having them confiscated at the turnstiles on Sunday. "It will be the normal search policy," said a Liverpool spokesperson.

    Rafael Benítez, who refused to use Bent's goal as an excuse for Liverpool's fourth league defeat of the season, has no intention of asking for the Sunderland match to be replayed. Precedent exists for replaying matches when a referee has erred over a goal but the Liverpool manager said: "A lot of people are saying this but we will not be asking for a replay."

    Benítez, meanwhile, has denied Liverpool's season has reached crisis point ahead of tonight's Champions League group tie with Lyon and believes the visit of the French club, plus United on Sunday, can transform the club's disappointing form. Liverpool will welcome back Steven Gerrard tonight, after the captain missed Saturday's defeat with an adductor strain, but Fernando Torres remains sidelined with a similar problem.
