    CHIMBONDA SEEKS ARSENAL REPEAT

      

    Sunday, 8 February 2009     

     
    Channel 27: "Live" 
     
    Tottenham v Arsenal   -  9:30 pm
      
       
       
     

    Tottenham full-back Pascal Chimbonda wants to turn back the clock this weekend and dish out another defeat to rivals Arsenal. The 29-year-old full-back was in the team that thrashed Arsenal 5-1 in the Carling Cup last season and after a short spell at Sunderland, his second game back after rejoining Spurs is against Arsene Wenger's men.

    Spurs had not beaten Arsenal in nine years before their famous win, making the victory all the more sweet. "Yes, a great moment, a fantastic night," Chimbonda said. "I know if we play well we can beat them, we have so many good players at the club."

    Chimbonda has faced Arsenal already this season and his Sunderland team were set for three Barclays Premier League points until Wenger's men equalised at the death.

    "This will be different because we won't look to defend, we will want to show our football and if we play well we can win," Chimbonda said. "If we can it will be so important for our league position and, of course, the fans. We know how much it means to them."

    Jamie O'Hara could be in contention for a place after recovering from a hip injury, while Robbie Keane is set to make his first appearance since rejoining Spurs from Liverpool.

    Spurs boss Harry Redknapp could partner Keane with Roman Pavlyuchenko or Darren Bent, who scored twice last week in the defeat at Bolton. "I had a dip of form before the Bolton game but I'm back now," Bent said. "It came from nowhere and that can happen."

    He added: "At times you feel like everything you hit will go in and it was the same in training before that, everything was going in. "But in the last few weeks it changed and it felt like I was missing chance after chance.

    "Sometimes it happens but I'm a confident guy and I'll stay positive. I know if I get into the positions I'll score the goals."

    Knocking back Arsenal to join Spurs was the best move I ever made, says Zokora

     

    No regrets: Zokora (right) is loving life at the Lane

     

     

    Didier Zokora has no regrets over snubbing Arsenal to join Tottenham.

    Zokora signed for Spurs in 2006 from French club St Etienne and has already made over 100 appearances for the north Londoners.

    Speaking ahead of Sunday's derby clash against the Gunners, the 28-year-old Ivory Coast star told the Telegraph: 'When I signed for Spurs, Arsenal also wanted me. I spoke with (Arsenal boss) Arsene Wenger after the World Cup but I preferred to come to Spurs because it gave me a good option for my life. I am very happy.'

    Zokora, who is under contract until 2012, insisted he has no plans to join Real Madrid despite being linked with a move to the Spanish giants.

    He added: 'Everybody knows Real Madrid wanted me but I signed a new contract for this club and if they want me, I stay. The club needs me, my life is here.

    'Real are one of the best clubs in the world so that is good for me, no? That means I am not a bad player!'

    Sorry lads! Red-carded Eboue  apologises to Gunners team-mates after north London stalemate

     

    Emmanuel Eboue has apologised to his Arsenal team mates after his red card in the north London derby

     

     

    Emmanuel Eboue was forced to apologise to his Arsenal team-mates on Sunday over his red card. He was sent off eight minutes before the break when, having already been booked for dissent, he earned a second for kicking out at Spurs midfielder Luka Modric.

    ‘I am sorry,’ said Eboue.

    This game was very important for us. We must always win when we play Spurs and give our best for the fans. 'I’m so disappointed to get the red card.’ 

    To cap a miserable afternoon, Eboue saw his14th-minute effort ruled out by referee Mike Dean.

    Insisting his first yellow card was harsh and that his goal should have stood, Eboue said: ‘For the first yellow I did not say anything bad. I was in front of the ball and talking with Manuel Almunia (following a foul).

    'After that I said to the referee you have to whistle and he said OK. 'I came back to take my place then he called me and gave me a yellow.

    'I don’t understand why. For the second, Modric kicked me when I tried to go forward and I put my leg up. 'The second card is normal, I accept it.

    ‘Also, it was a goal. I don’t know why he did not give it. 'The referee said I touched Woodgate but I didn’t.’

    Manager Arsene Wenger added: ‘I do not want to develop paranoia. 'I cannot say I’m against the referee, but he got a big decision wrong. 'I’ve seen it three times on TV and it’s a goal.’
