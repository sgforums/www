    Carra: 'This Is Our Year'...
    Even With Stewart Downing?

     

     

    It's June so that means Scouse optimism. This time it's Carragher, who says: " I am confident we can produce something this season." Maybe he wouldn't be so confident if he knew that Rafa has his eye on a £10-12m swoop for Stewart Downing rather than David Silva. So Liverpool fans, how confident are you?

     

     

     

    Liverpool defender Jamie Carragher admits it is time for the side to deliver next season. The Reds go into next term aiming to end their 19-year wait for the Premier League title and they have now gone two seasons without a trophy.

    "At a club like Liverpool every season is a big season and we will be going for every trophy we possibly can," Carragher told Sky Sports News. "It is a big competition not just in the Premier League but around Europe, but I am confident we can produce something this season."

    When asked if the Anfield outfit needed new players, Carragher said: "Of course we do. We didn't win anything last season so we want to look to improve and that is something we have already done.

    "We have brought in two or three players already and I am sure the manager is looking to bring some more in. "When we get ready for the first game at Sunderland in the Premiership, hopefully we will get everyone in by then."

    Carragher also welcomed the news that work has finally got under way on the club's new ground away from Anfield. The New LFC Stadium is expected to be completed for the beginning of the 2011/12 season and Carragher is excited at the prospect of playing at Liverpool's new home. "That would be brilliant if we get the new stadium. It is something to look forward to in years to come," added Carragher.
