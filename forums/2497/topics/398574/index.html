    Deco close to Fluminense move as Portuguese midfielder bids to end Chelsea nightmare

    End of the road: Deco has been forced to play a bit-part role at Chelsea

    Fluminense hope to agree a deal to sign Deco from Chelsea early next week after dispatching officials to London.

    The 32-year-old still has another season left on his Stamford Bridge contract but is pleading to be released so that he can be closer to his sons in Rio de Janeiro.

    Flamengo and Deco's first club Corinthians have also shown an interest, but Fluminense will be the first to offer a concrete proposal when officials arrive in England over the weekend. 

    A meeting is being planned for after Chelsea's final Barclays Premier League match of the season at home against Wigan Athletic on Sunday, when the Blues should clinch the title.

    Deco is due to join up with Portugal's World Cup party almost straight after the FA Cup final against Portsmouth on Saturday week and Fluminense want a deal agreed before he leaves England.

    The club hope to show they mean business by announcing other big signings in a bid to challenge Corinthians and Santos in the Brazilian Serie A, which kicks off this weekend.
