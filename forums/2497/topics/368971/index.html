    Man Utd stunned as Tevez leaves rented mansion trashed

    Manchester United have slashed Carlos Tevez's final pay-packet after his home was trashed, it has been revealed.

    The Sun says the club had paid the rent on the £1.5million home which needed up to £30,000 of repairs after the ace left.

    Insiders said posh Italian furniture was damaged and the Argentine's dogs allegedly ruined other items.

    Details of the damage at the house in Prestbury, Cheshire, emerged after Tevez, 24, was unveiled last week as a Manchester City player following a £26.5million move.

    A United source said: "The house was a tip and we made Carlos pay. We don't know who was responsible.

    "But the matter has now been resolved."

    Tevez: I don't know if I'll shake Ferguson's hand

    New Manchester City signing Carlos Tevez admits he may not shake hands with his former Manchester United boss Sir Alex Ferguson when the teams meet next season.

    He told the Sunday Mirror: "There is no mystery why I left United and Alex gave me all the reasons to leave.

    "I did not play an important role in any of the important games. In the Champions League I didn't play, I didn't play in the derby. I tried several times to speak to him, to express my feelings, but the only thing he'd say was 'Don't worry, don't worry, this is going to change'.

    "He never gave any justification or explanation - I don't know if I will shake his hand this season.

    "My team-mates tried several times to convince me to stay and that things would change.

    "I'm always very thankful to them and we had a very good relationship because we won a lot of things in two years.

    "I want to give the best to help the team develop and win trophies."
