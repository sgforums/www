    Steve Nicol: Beck move makes it easier for MLS clubs



    Beckham's move will make it easier for MLS clubs to recruit players, according to Liverpool great Steve Nicol.

    The former Anfield and Scotland star, who has guided the New England Revolution to runners-up in MLS for the past two years, claimed he had always struggled to bring in quality players from Europe because of the "stigma'' attached to the sport in America.

    But with Beckham heading to the US now, he said: "This is a great move for the league, every which way you want to look at it. "I think the one thing it's done has got rid of the stigma. Ever since Pele and Beckenbauer and all those other guys came over here, it put a stigma on our league. Not only among journalists but among players.

    "Quite frankly that stigma should have been gone a long time ago but Beckham coming while he's still at the top of his game - it's only a few months ago that he was captain of England at a World Cup - will help.
    "What it will do is encourage other players to come here, and not at 34 or 36 when they're past it.''

    Beckham's move though, has seen Nicol being transformed from a struggling recruiter into a coach in demand from across the Atlantic.

    "In the past, players have said to me: 'I'll come and play for you but in a couple of years when I'm done at my club'. But now, even since Beckham's announcement, I've had players on the phone, I've had agents on the phone wanting to come and play in MLS.

    "Before Beckham signed his contract they'd never have dreamed of coming."
