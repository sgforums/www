Wenger expects Henry return

    Arsene Wenger has hinted that Thierry Henry will return to Arsenal in some capacity in the future but it appears unlikely that the man voted the club’s greatest ever player will return to don his boots.

    Henry left Arsenal to join Barcelona in the summer of 2007 and The Gunners have missed his drive, experience and all-round excellence with Wenger even admitting his side may have won the league last season had they held onto their captain.

    But the Gunners boss expects the striker – who scored a record 226 goals in 380 games in Arsenal colours – to return to the club, perhaps in a coaching capacity.

    "Is there ever a chance Thierry will come back? Of course,” said Wenger.

    “This club always wants players of his quality to come back. It will be difficult for him to come back as a player because in 2010 he will be 33.

    "But he has a very strong link with the club. He had a massive influence here - and we had a massive influence on his career."

    Reflecting on Henry’s departure to Barcelona two years ago, Wenger lamented the impact that the captain’s departure had on a side that so nearly took the Premier League title before Henry’s replacement, Eduardo, suffered a horrific injury.

    “It was his decision,” Wenger added. “I tried to convince him to stay.

    “He said: ‘The way you are going you will have a great team in two or three years. But I can’t wait that long. I am 30 years old.’

    “Last year we had 83 points and with him we might have got the extra points that would have won us the championship.”
