    EPL,Wayne Rooney,Manchester United vs Bolton Wanderers(Getty Images)

    Wayne Rooney is thought to have told Manchester United that he does not wish to sign a new contract at Old Trafford.

    The forward has had a difficult relationship with manager Sir Alex Ferguson in recent times, as he openly rubbished the 68-year-old's comments concerning an ankle injury.

    With less than two years left to run on Rooney's contract, there has been a fair amount of speculation that he could be sold for a fee believed to be in the region of £50 million.

    Spanish giants Real Madrid and Barcelona have both been reported to be interested, whereas there has also been some speculation concerning a shock move to rivals Manchester City.

    Wayne Rooney warned Real Madrid won't rescue the wantaway Manchester United striker

    Real Madrid have warned they won't rescue Wayne Rooney from his Manchester United misery.

    The Spanish giants have been mooted as the ideal destination for the England striker, who has intimated that he won't sign a new contract at Old Trafford after falling out with boss Sir Alex Ferguson.

    But Madrid's prime dealmaker - director general Jorge Valdano - insists his side don't need another striker and has ruled out any moves in January.

    On the move: Rooney leaves Manchester United's Carrington training ground on Monday

    Unwanted: United striker Rooney

    Asked specifically about Rooney, Valdano said: 'Madrid have two great strikers: (Gonzalo) Higuain and Cristiano (Ronaldo), who already showed last season that they are capable of scoring 60 goals between them.

    'As well as that there are (Angel) di Maria and (Mesut) Ozil, who are going to help these two goalscorers. The question is, who would we move aside if we signed another striker?

    'In January there will be neither ins nor outs. June is a long way away.'

    Jose Mourinho said very much the same thing last week, insisting his current squad would remain until the end of the season. However, it is also understood that the former Chelsea boss does feel short up front and is not convinced by France striker Karim Benzema. 

    A move this winter might not be an option, but Mourinho recently told Madrid-based newspaper AS that he would love to work with Rooney in the future and former Old Trafford darling Ronaldo would also love to work with the striker again.

    Rare joy: Rooney scores against West Ham earlier this season

    Madrid may have to re-think their strategy if others move in January, but Barcelona fans seem lukewarm to welcoming a player who's star has fallen so dramatically since being talked about in the same breath as Lionel Messi earlier this year.

    A poll of nearly 30,000 visitors to the website of Catalan newspaper El Mundo Deportivo finds only a small majority in favour of snapping up the 24-year-old.

    Benzema could be used to tempt United into a deal. Ferguson has long admired the Frenchman and was disappointed to miss out when Madrid signed him from Lyon before the start of last season.

    Back on the radar? Benzema (second left) has attracted United in the past

    The 22-year-old has his critics in Spain and is already being linked with a January move to Juventus after a meagre return of 10 goals in 40 appearances.

    The most sensational prospect for Rooney - and the one United will want to most avoid - is for Manchester City to make an offer their rivals can't refuse in January.

    No deal: Madrid chief Valdano

    For Madrid, Rooney is not even their only option. Mourinho has been linked with a number of more physically imposing strikers and recently talked up City's Emmanuel Adebayor and his old Chelsea man Didier Drogba.

    Athletic Bilbao's 6ft 5in striker Fernando Llorente is the most recent to appear on the radar, especially after scoring three for Spain this month when standing in for Fernando Torres.

    And Valdano admitted: 'I like him (Llorente), but from that to finding him a place in Madrid, that won't be easy. Every season there will be a new player, but it doesn't always have to be a centre-forward.

    'Llorente is improving a lot and he is ready for new experiences, but often the media are discussing things that are not happening in the club.

    'It's not a debate in Madrid (whether we need a new striker). Mourinho had to come out and stop these types of rumours. And as well as that, Benzema has not had the last word.'

    Shocked Sir Alex Ferguson admits Wayne Rooney wants to quit Manchester United

    • Striker won't sign new contract, admits dumbfounded boss

    Sir Alex Ferguson has confirmed that Wayne Rooney wants to leave Manchester United and won't enter into contract talks.

    The Old Trafford boss insisted he had never fallen out with the striker and is 'dumbfounded' by the his change of heart.

    And in an extraordinarily frank interview before Wednesday's Champions League game against Turkish champions Bursaspor, Ferguson questioned the striker's respect for the club.

    Stunned: Manchester United boss Sir Alex Ferguson comes clean about his relationship with Wayne Rooney

    'I was in the office on August 14 when (United chief executive David Gill) told me he’d had a call from (Rooney's) agent saying that Wayne wasn’t going to sign his contract,' said the Scot.

    'So then David came across to see me. He said he couldn’t believe it and neither could I. I was dumbfounded, I couldn’t understand it at all because only months before he’d said he was at the biggest club in the world and he wanted to stay for life.

    Care free: Wayne Rooney trains with United ahead of the Bursapor match - but finished the session on a stretcher

    'We just don’t know what’s changed the boy’s mind. David was shocked, I was shocked.

    'I had a meeting with the boy and he reiterated what his agent had said. He wanted to go. I said to him, "Just remember one thing: respect this club. I don’t want any nonsense from you, respect your club".

    'And I don't know if he's done that. I've got doubts on that because you read all this about "he's fallen out with me" and all that nonsense.

    'What we’re seeing now in the media is disappointing because we’ve done everything we can for Wayne Rooney, since the minute he’s come to the club.

    'We’ve always been there as a harbour for him. Any time he’s been in trouble, the advice we’ve given him – I’ve even been prepared to give him financial advice, many times.

    'But you do that for your players, not just Wayne Rooney. That’s Manchester United. This is a club which bases all its history and its tradition on the loyalty and trust between managers and players and the club.

    'That goes back to the days of Sir Matt. That’s what it’s founded on. Wayne’s been a beneficiary of this help, just as Ryan Giggs, Paul Scholes and all the players have been. That’s what we’re there for.'

    Ferguson insisted he had never had an argument with his star striker and insisted that Rooney had suffered an ankle injury against Rangers in September - despite contradictory comments by the 24-year-old.

    The 68-year-old kept the door open for contract talks to resume but conceded: 'There’s no offer on the table for Wayne because they’re not prepared to listen to an offer. But there’s always an offer there for Manchester United to negotiate with a player. That’s still there.'

    Carefully scripted: Sir Alex Ferguson reads from notes to deliver his explosive Rooney revelations

    Clear signs that all was not well came in explosive comments from Rooney last week when he claimed he had not been injured all season - despite Ferguson earlier citing ankle problems as the reason for the player missing games this season.

    Rooney's comments, after playing for England against Macedonia, not only contradicted his club boss - but also his own statements shortly after hobbling out of the Champions League game against Rangers last month.

    'He was injured,' said Ferguson. 'We sent him for a scan and while he was able to train, he was still carrying traces of his injury.

    'He got an ankle injury in the Rangers game. He was seen clearly hobbling at half-time. He confirmed it himself in an interview with Sky.

    'Why he was saying he wasn’t injured, you can only guess yourself. It’s disappointing. When it came to the Sunderland game, I felt I would leave him out and give him a complete break so he could be fit for England.

    'I thought if he went and played for England, at Wembley, we would get his form back. That was my intention, that was my idea, to galvanise him. Take a break from the games, get the training done, get his fitness back. Get rid of the traces of the ankle injury away and play for England.

    'So why he’s come out and said that, I’ve no idea.'

    'We’ve never had any argument, not a bit. I think you have to understand the mechanics of these situations when people want to leave the club.'

    And it seems the injury has not gone away. Rooney looked relaxed in training today - laughing and joking with team-mates - but he ended the session on a stretcher and will not play against Bursaspor because of an ankle injury.

    Rooney will seek a monster pay day if he moves elsewhere, but Ferguson has rejected the idea that United can't compete financially with the likes of Real Madrid or Manchester City.

    'I had a meeting with him, and he intimated to me, in his own way, that he wanted to leave,' explained Rooney. 'I did say to him, as David Gill intimated, that there was an offer there if he was prepared to accept it. It was an offer that never got to any financial discussions.

    'I know that David was prepared to offer him a contract that would be difficult to better elsewhere.

    'That is what you do with top players. There is no big problem with that. We have been doing this kind of thing for quite a while now.

    'Contract issues, over the last 10 years, have accelerated to a degree where they are quite phenomenal.'
