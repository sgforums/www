    Wenger rejects Madrid rumours

    Arsenal boss Arsene Wenger has rejected reports suggesting he and Cesc Fabregas could be leaving the club.

    Reports emanating from Spain have suggested Fabregas could move to Real Madrid this summer and that Wenger could also make the switch to El Santiago Bernabeu.

    Wenger made a visit to Spain fuelling speculation that he was in the country to meet Madrid and discuss his own and Fabregas' future.

    However, the Frenchman insists he was in Spain to meet with Atletico Madrid regarding Jose Antonio Reyes and that both himself and Fabregas will be staying at Emirates Stadium.

    "I met with the directors of Atletico Madrid, nothing else," Wenger told Marca

    "Cesc is going to spend the next year with Arsenal."

    Wenger also moved to rubbish suggestions that he offered himself to Madrid following the recent departure of David Dein at Arsenal.

    "I have not offered myself to Real Madrid. I am going to continue at Arsenal."
