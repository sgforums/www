    At last! Here's playboy Alberto Aquilani - just what Liverpool and Rafa Benitez have been waiting for since Xabi Alonso left

     

    Hometown boy: Given his debut by Fabio Capello, Aquilani went on to make 102 appearances for the Giallorossi

     

     

    Arsenal  vs  Liverpool

    Wednesday, 28 October 2009

    Time: 3:45 am (Thur Morning)

    Carling Cup

     

     

    In one corner of Anfield’s Kop, they have had a banner waiting for Alberto Aquilani for some time. ‘Il Principino — A Hero has Arrived’, it reads.

    Whether Aquilani will go on to fit such weighty billing remains to be seen. But tonight in the Carling Cup at Arsenal it seems likely that the ‘Little Prince’ — as he was dubbed in Italy — will finally arrive in a Liverpool first-team shirt.

    Signed from Roma in the summer for £20million as a replacement for the much-missed midfielder Xabi Alonso, Aquilani has slowly been recovering from an ankle injury and is expected to be only on the bench at the Emirates Stadium tonight.

    Nevertheless, he cannot assert himself on Rafa Benitez’s team soon enough. For all the excitement of Sunday’s victory against Manchester United, this is a Liverpool team screaming for more genuine artistry.

    ‘We can see in every training session that he has quality, game intelligence and good movements,’ said Benitez last night. ‘He is a different kind of player for us. We talk about using Steven Gerrard deeper sometimes, but maybe in Aquilani we have a player who can play there and pass the ball really well in the final third.’

    In Italy, they will tell you that Aquilani has star quality to burn. One of three home-grown players in last season’s Roma team, the 25-year-old was never as vocal or flamboyantly Roman as the other two, Francesco Totti and Daniele de Rossi.

    Nevertheless, his importance to that team, when fit, was all-consuming. In Rome they stress he is not a like-for-like replacement for Alonso, rather a more forward-moving player in the mould of Arsenal’s Cesc Fabregas.

    Arsenal and Chelsea both tried to sign him when he was 16. So did Roma’s neighbours Lazio. Juventus, meanwhile, have tried to snare him twice.

    Former Liverpool defender John Arne Riise, who played with Aquilani at Roma last season, said: ‘He is the perfect replacement for Alonso. He is a fantastic passer of the ball and very technical.

    ‘He is definitely different to Xabi but he is the type of player who can break teams down. He will find space everywhere and will score goals. I expect him to do well in England.’

    Born and raised in the upper-middle class suburb of Monte Sacro, Aquilani had spent his entire career at Roma until he was sold in the summer to ease the club’s financial problems.

    Known as a good passer and prodigious worker, he has nevertheless suffered regularly with injuries. His father Claudio was in charge of the ambulance fleet at the Olympic Stadium on matchdays and has certainly given his boy a lift home on more than one occasion.

    Aquilani has blamed misdiagnosis by the Roma medical team for the ankle problem that has sidelined him this season but previously there have been problems with hamstrings and his hip.

    Coincidentally, his last appearance for Roma came at Arsenal in the Champions League back in March. Once again, he picked up an injury.

    ‘It has been difficult,’ Aquilani said yesterday. ‘Now I just want to build up my confidence again and bring the best out of my game. ‘I will be very happy when I make my debut. That is what I am here to do. I really want to play but I know I have to be patient.’

    Aquilani was present at Anfield on Sunday as his team-mates rolled over their great rivals. He sat close to captain Gerrard and his wife Alex in the directors’ box, and the Italian has a glamorous girlfriend of his own.

     

    Glamour girl: Aquilani's girlfriend Michela Quattrociocche

     

    Michela Quattrociocche is a 20-year-old Italian actress and her relationship with Aquilani came on the back of his previous dalliances with Miss World contestant Pamella Camassa and former Miss Italy Francesca Chillemi. Known in Italy as a ladies’ man with a quick wit, he has not always steered clear of controversy.

    At the Roma training ground two years ago, he was heard to suggest to team-mate Christian Panucci that he should get himself a tattoo ‘that shows you sitting on the bench’. He took a punch on the jaw for his troubles.

    Equally peculiar is the rumour that he kept a marble sculpture of Italy’s former Fascist dictator Benito Mussolini at his home in Rome.

    Asked about it in an interview in Italian sports daily Gazzetta dello Sport last year, he replied: ‘My uncle is very keen on him and he gave me something to do with Mussolini. But, as for me, I don’t know anything about politics.’

     

    The happy couple: Aquilani and his beau

     

    Living now in a city that has only ever tolerated right-wingers on the football field, Aquilani may do well to steer clear of such complicated issues as class and politics over the coming weeks. But if he can succeed on the field then nobody will care much.

    In Rome they already lament his departure. Though they were never close friends, his fellow Roman De Rossi said recently: ‘We needed the money so nobody seems to mind. But in the months to come I can promise you we will all be sorry for this sale.’

    It is known that Aquilani has already started English lessons and is progressing quickly, though one respected judge believes it is not the language that will prove critical.

    England coach Fabio Capello gave Aquilani his Roma debut in 2003 and he said: ‘He is a very interesting player and this will prove a good moment for him to move. He will have to understand English football, though.'

    Duo set to start for Gunners

     

     

    Midfielder Samir Nasri and goalkeeper Lukasz Fabianski could make their first appearances of the season following injury in Arsenal's Carling Cup fourth-round tie against Liverpool.

    Manager Arsene Wenger is expected to again send out a mixture of experience and youth, with Philippe Senderos and veteran Mikael Silvestre likely to start in defence.

    Mexican forward Carlos Vela, Sanchez Watt, who scored in the last round against West Brom, Fran Merida, Wales midfielder Aaron Ramsey, along with England Under-21 duo Kieran Gibbs and Jack Wilshere could all feature.

    Meanwhile, Wenger believes Ramsey can rise to the challenge and stake his claim for a regular first-team place by leading the young Gunners to victory.

    Ramsey will turn 19 on Boxing Day, and Wenger believes the former Cardiff trainee has come a long way since his £5million move during the summer of 2008, scoring in the 4-1 Barclays Premier League win over Portsmouth as well as playing in Europe.

    "If you consider he is only 18, Ramsey has developed exceptionally well,'' said the Arsenal manager.

    "He has made a big improvement compared to last year, when he still looked a little bit like a youth player, but now he looks like a [first-team] player.

    "He is really pushing hard - and the other midfielders have a lot of work to keep him out of the side because he is getting very close.

    "The target I set him at the start of the season was to get between 15 and 20 games this year, and I think he will get that.''

    Liverpool skipper Steven Gerrard is out of the fourth-round tie, but should have recovered from his groin problem to play in Saturday's Barclays Premier League game at Fulham.

    The Reds will be without suspended midfielder Javier Mascherano, while £20million Italy international Alberto Aquilani is named in a first-team squad for the first time since his summer move, having now recovered fully from an ankle operation.

    Martin Kelly (ankle) and Albert Riera (hamstring) are out, while defenders Andrea Dossena and Sotirios Kyrgiakos could both figure having recovered from injuries, the latter defying an initial prognosis he could face six months out with a knee injury.

    Boss Rafael Benitez has made it clear Aquilani - the man he bought to replace Xabi Alonso - will begin his senior career with the Anfield club at the Emirates Stadium.

    Benitez said: "He will be with us. He is improving his fitness, so he's in the squad.

    "He cannot start at this moment but he will be there and maybe we can give him some minutes. If it's possible, we will try to use him.''

    Benitez believes the Italy international will add a new dimension to his midfield.

    He said: "We can see in every training session that he has quality, game intelligence and good movements. He is a different kind of player for us.

    "We talk about using Steven Gerrard deeper sometimes, but maybe in Aquilani we have a player who can play there and pass the ball really well in the final third.''

    RAFA BENITEZ has challenged his Liverpool side to prove the statistics wrong at Arsenal tonight. The two teams last met in the Carling Cup at the quarter-final stage in January 2007.

    Julio Baptista netted four times as the Gunners claimed a 6-3 victory in front of a stunned Kop.

    The Brazilian striker, now at Roma, became the first visiting player in 40 years to score four times in a game at Anfield — and also had a penalty saved by Jerzy Dudek.

    Andrey Arshavin repeated the feat the last time the two met in Merseyside in the Premier League in April's 4-4 draw.

    Arsenal were also victorious the last time the sides did battle in the League Cup in North London as they claimed a 1-0 win in October 1989.

    In fact, Liverpool have not won in their last 11 visits to the Gunners since recording a 1-0 win at Highbury in February 2000, when Titi Camara scored the only goal of the game.

    But Reds boss Benitez said: "This is a special competition in which you try to give opportunities to young players or players who are not playing. These things together make it difficult.

    "The Carling Cup for the young players of Arsenal is always an extra motivation. To play away for us will be even more difficult but we have to be ready to do a job.

    "We have to change the statistic and we will be really pleased if we win there for the first time."

    Arsenal youngsters overcome Liverpool in Carling Cup

    A young Arsenal side have overcome Liverpool 2-1 in the Carling Cup fourth round to progress to the quarter-finals.

    Arsene Wenger continued with fielding a young side for the competition despite Liverpool’s Rafael Benitez going with a fairly strong eleven for the beginning of the contest.

    The first goal went to Arsenal coming from Spanish U20 Fran Merida who blasted a left-footed shot past Cavalieri Diego to send the 60,000-strong Emirates Stadium crowd into raptures.

    But seven minutes later, the Reds were back on even terms thanks to a piece of brilliance from first team regular Emiliano Insua who looped a 25-yard shot over Gunners goalkeeper Lukasz Fabianski to open his account in English football.

    Arsenal were displaying a better style of game, knocking around passes at will but Liverpool were managing to stem the flow and even create some chances themselves.

    In the 50th minute, Gunners striker Nicklas Bendtner produced a powerful finish to again put Arsenal ahead and that’s the way the match ended.

    Liverpool’s Alberto Aquilani finally made his debut for his new club, coming on in the 76th minute but could not inspire his team to overcome Wenger’s young Gunners.

    Liverpool’s Benitez: Aquilani has quality, now needs fitness

    Liverpool boss Rafael Benitez was happy with the debut of summer signing Alberto Aquilani in Wednesday night’s 2-1 Carling Cup loss to Arsenal.

    The former AS Roma midfielder came on in the 76th minute of the contest after missing the beginning of the season with an ankle injury and Benitez was pleased with his efforts.

    “There were a lot of players showing passion, character and quality on the pitch, and Aquilani was one of these player," said Benitez.

    "He has the quality, but he needs to improve his match-fitness.

    "You can see he can pass the ball in the final third, he can make a big difference.

    "He gets closer every day, but still has to improve his match-fitness. It's a question of time.”

    Benitez added that he expects 25-year old Aquilani to become a key player for Liverpool.

    "I think that he will be an important player for us,” he added.

    "We want to push him, but we cannot push too hard as we know that will be a risk.

    "So we will keep going, keep working and add more minutes every game."

    Rafa Benitez saw the pressure return as Arsenal dumped Liverpool out of the Carling Cup.

     

     

     

    ALBERTO AQUILANI was left raging last night, after being denied a dream Liverpool debut. Aquilani, making his first-team bow almost three MONTHS after his £20million move from Roma, went loopy at referee Alan Wiley when he was denied an injury-time penalty.

    The Italian, fit after ankle problems, saw his spectacular overhead kick handled by Philippe Senderos.

    Boss Rafa Benitez declared after his Carling Cup KO: "Aquilani said it was a penalty. The worst thing was the defeat and the result. "But there were positives - and Aquilani was one of those.

    "It would have been a perfect start to win the penalty and, when you start doing things well, the fans support you more than if you just play.

    "Hopefully, he will be more lucky in the future. He needs to improve his fitness but he can pass in the final third and make a difference to us.

    "He could be an important player. But we can't push too hard with him. He will not start on Saturday. It is too soon."

    Nicklas Bendtner hit Arsenal's winner after Emiliano Insua's 25-yard volley had cancelled out a Fran Merida scorcher.

    Arsenal boss Arsene Wenger, in the quarter-finals for a seventh year in a row, said: "It was a game of top quality.

    "They were up for it, the passing was very good, the ball was played at high pace. We gave people what they paid for.

    "It was more a European Cup night than a Carling Cup night. I think all who played here will make it at the top level."

    The Carling Cup quarter-final draw will be live at noon on Sky's Gillette Soccer Saturday.
