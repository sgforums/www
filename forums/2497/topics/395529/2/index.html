    Rafa Benitez stuns fans by subbing Fernando Torres: Is this the moment Liverpool boss gave up on finishing fourth?

    Not happy: Fernando Torres is substituted at St Andrew's

     

     


    Rafa Benitez had Liverpool fans asking whether winning the Europa League was more important than finishing fourth in the Barclays Premier League as he substituted Fernando Torres 25 minutes from the end of the 1-1 draw at Birmingham City.

    Liverpool were level at the time and the draw leaves them four points behind fourth-placed Manchester City, having played a game more.

    Benitez decided to take off 20-goal leading scorer Fernando Torres midway through the second half, even though the player looked disappointed with the decision to replace him with David N'Gog.

    Torres played up front on his own in Liverpool's 2-1 Europa League quarter-final first leg defeat by Benfica 72 hours earlier, and manager Benitez said: 'He was exhausted. He had some ice just to protect his knee, but he was really tired, like the other players who played on Thursday against Benfica.

    'He knew we needed to win, but he was really tired, like all the players, and N'Gog had fresh legs, as did Ryan Babel, and that made a big difference in the last few minutes.'

    Benitez admitted his side now have "no margin for error" if they are to have any hope of qualifying for the Champions League next season. 'It is not a good point, despite Birmingham playing so well here this season.  

    'We deserved to win. We had a very good chance in the first half and a lot in the second half.   

     

    Dynamic duo: Gerrard celebrates with Torres after putting Liverpool ahead

     

    'We didn't have any margin for error beforehand and now we have less margin in terms of coming fourth.  

    'But it depends on the other teams and how they do as well. We have to believe we can win all our remaining games and have confidence the other sides will lose.  

    'We just have to keep going because there are still a lot of points to play for.  

    'But we said beforehand that we had to win the final six matches, or possibly draw one and win five. Now we have to win all remaining five matches.'
