Arsene Wenger warns Man City can buy into Champions League

  • Trump_Card
    15 Sep 08, 17:14

    Arsenal manager Arsene Wenger believes that Manchester City's new-found wealth could catapult the club into the Champions League next season.

    City manager Mark Hughes admitted after Saturday's 3-1 defeat to Chelsea that he expects many more players to arrive in January to accompany £32.5 million Robinho, who arrivedf on deadline day after the extraordinary £210 million Abu Dhabi United Group takeover at Eastlands.

    And Wenger is cautious that City's firm proposals to shake the foundations of the Premier League's big four when the transfer window reopens could have a large impact on the remainder of their season with the potential promise of an unprecedented qualification for Europe's elite competition.

    "Manchester City have an advantage because they do pay a lot of money," he said in a report this morning.

    "This year other surprise teams could come into it like Manchester City or Tottenham or Portsmouth. It is certainly very interesting and very tight.

    "Teams can get that little bit more balance by buying players in the transfer window."

    The Frenchman has come under scrutiny for not taking a City or Chelsea-like approach to bringing in reinforcements with his policy to not pay over the odds for players.

    After the departures of midfield trio Mathieu Flamini, Gilberto Silva and Alexander Hleb failed to bring in his preferred replacement signings and striker Robin Van Persie revealed that Wenger's principles prevented Arsenal from adding one new face in the summer.

    He said: "He [Wenger] has a principle and if he believes a player is worth £10m then he'll only pay that amount.

    "He wanted a player but the selling club were asking far too much in his valuation. So he told them: 'That's the price or forget it'."

    Wenger had previously warned that the Champions League could not simply be bought but he is certainly wary about the future threat City's riches warrant.
