    Real Madrid have agreed a fee with Chelsea for Dutch winger Arjen Robben.

    The Spanish champions have spent much of the summer chasing Robben and have now struck a deal with Chelsea.

    Robben has been a long-term target of Real president Ramon Calderon, who promised to sign the wide man upon his election.

    Calderon now appears to be close to finally fulfilling his election pledge after Chelsea confirmed an offer had been accepted.

    The former PSV Eindhoven ace is now due to travel to Spain to undergo a medical after agreeing a five-year contract.

    He has not played for The Blues this season and is being allowed to move on after three years at Stamford Bridge.

    If Robben settles on terms with Real, he will become the third Dutchman to join the club this summer, following the signings of Wesley Sneijder and Royston Drenthe.

    Earlier on Wednesday, Real also revealed an agreement was in place to sign Gabriel Heinze from Manchester United.
