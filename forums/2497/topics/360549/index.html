    Real Madrid Killed My Passion For Football - Julien Faubert

    A dream move to Real Madrid quickly turned sour and now Julien Faubert is hoping to find himself a new club.

    Julien Faubert believes that he became a scapegoat at Real Madrid when things went wrong and made it easy for people to blame him for not fitting in.

    The French international moved to the Bernabeu on loan from West Ham United in the winter transfer window and was keen to take advantage of the dream transfer.

    But instead he was left out by coach Juande Ramos and was reported to be overweight and was then seen appearing to be asleep on the bench during a game.

    Now he hopes to end his nightmare in the Spanish capital and start playing at a club where he hopes he will be appreciated rather than blamed for everything.

    "I do not want to waste any more time and hope to recover my desire for playing football because for a while I had lost it," he told France Football.

    "It has been complicated and I never received an explanation as to why I did not play.

    "I was prepared to remain patient, but that made it easy for them to say that the Frenchman would not play.

    "I think I was seen as a scapegoat for all this.

    "When I understood that I would not play much I focused on myself and now hope to find something else."
