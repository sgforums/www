    Buffon: I Almost Quit Juventus, But Not For Manchester City

    The Italian international went through a tough moment when his future was put in doubt.

    Juventus goalkeeper Gianluigi Buffon has revealed he almost quit the club back in October, when much speculation surrounded his future.

    The Italian international was constantly linked with mega-rich Premier League side Manchester City. Buffon claims he was never interested in going to Eastlands, but he considered leaving the Old Lady.

    "I kept on getting hammered around October with all these offers from abroad. Strange rumours too. Nobody denied any claims, and I stopped to think what was happening," Buffon told La Repubblica.

    "I thought. 'Is there something true in all these rumours?' Sometimes footballers are the last to know things, and I thought if that's the case then I need to find out, and so I met with the club in order to see if they still counted on me.

    "I wanted to be certain of the project here and whether we were playing to win, because if you only play for a while then your performances decrease."

    Buffon pondered Manchester City's interest, but he was never going to join them, believing that some things in life are more important than money.

    "Manchester City were interested, but with all respect, it was not for me," added Buffon.

    "I have a family and things to think about, I would never have gone, even if they doubled my wages. With all respect for money, some things in life are priceless."
