    Arsenal boss Arsene Wenger admits he will need to strengthen squad after crashing out of Champions League

    Arsenal manager Arsene Wenger has admitted his side will need strengthening in the summer after they were humbled by Barcelona in the Champions League quarter-finals.

    Wenger has been criticised for failing to invest in his squad despite not winning a trophy since 2005.

    Frustration: Arsenal boss Arsene Wenger endured a miserable night in Barcelona

    And he said: 'We have to add something, for sure, but we have some time to think about that," he admitted. "We are at a stage where we're developing players.

    '[Theo] Walcott is 22; [Nicklas] Bendtner is 22; [Samir] Nasri, [Abou] Diaby and Denilson are 22 or 23. They've shown some great qualities and they'll get stronger every year.

    'We were very young and what we did was very positive. We had six or seven players under 23 in the team and we had plenty of opportunities. We showed we lacked maturity in the weight of the final ball.'

    Stunning: Lionel Messi scored four goals for Barcelona as Arsenal crashed out of Europe

    Arsenal will look to bounce back from the Champions League disappointment as they chase the Premier League title.

    The Gunners take on Tottenham in the north London derby at White Hart Lane next Wednesday.
