    GLEE ... Rafa will get massive war chest

     

    RAFA BENITEZ has been given £30million to spend as a reward for signing his new contract. 

    The Liverpool boss penned a £20million five-year deal on Wednesday. And he has hit the jackpot again with American owners Tom Hicks and George Gillett promising to throw cash at Rafa’s Kop revolution. 

    As well as pledging £20m of fresh investment, Benitez has been told he can keep ALL the money he raises from sales — starting with the £12m they pocketed from selling Robbie Keane back to Tottenham in January. 

    That will spark a fresh attempt to land Aston Villa skipper Gareth Barry in the summer. And Spurs winger Aaron Lennon is also high on Benitez’s shopping list. 

    Hicks last night ruled out selling Liverpool and insists he will stay as long as Benitez does. And the American co-owner even reckons Gillett will abandon any thoughts of selling his stake to oil-rich Sheikhs. 

    Hicks said: “I plan to be here for the next five years and while I cannot speak for George, I would think he would want to be here too.” Benitez demanded total control over transfer policy and put pen to paper after chief executive Rick Parry announced that he will quit the club at the end of the season. 

    Hicks added: “I never thought for a minute we would lose Rafa because he has such a passion for Liverpool and the club.” The owner also took a swipe at Parry, saying: “Rick served the club for 12 years and people will know this was his hardest year. He is resigning and we appreciate his help. 

    “The new chief executive will communicate more effectively with Rafa, the board members and senior members of staff. “I feel very good about the direction the club is headed in now.” 

    Hicks insisted he did not have to make Benitez wild promises about new transfer funds. He added: “Rafa knows how much we want to win. “Over the last two years we have followed his recommendations and signed Torres, Skrtel, Benayoun, Babel, and Dossena.”

    Lennon and Barry top wish-list as Benitez is told he can spend £30m

     

    Target man: Lennon is top of Liverpool's shopping list

     

    Rafa Benitez will launch a £30million spending spree this summer and commit even more to securing the long-term futures of Steven Gerrard and Fernando Torres.

    Liverpool's Spanish manager will waste no time exercising his new power over transfer dealings and is already lining up potential signings after being set a budget that includes money recouped from Robbie Keane's return to Tottenham in January.

    Eager to strengthen in midfield and up front, he remains keen on Aston Villa anchorman Gareth Barry and Tottenham winger Aaron Lennon and is also pursuing targets in Spain.

    After ending uncertainty over his own position by signing a five-year deal on Wednesday, he will swiftly turn his attention to negotiating new contracts for a lengthy list of players, headed by Gerrard and Torres.

     

     

    Not giving up: Liverpool will be after Barry for a second summer in a row

     

    With just two years left on his current agreement, top earner Gerrard can expect a modest increase in his £100,000-a-week salary to keep him at Anfield into his thirties, while Torres will land a revised package after alerting Europe's top clubs with a stunning performance against Real Madrid.

    The £21m former Atletico Madrid striker, who arrived on a six-year deal worth £70,000 a week in 2007, is on course for a 12-month extension and weekly pay rise to £90,000. Jamie Carragher, Dirk Kuyt, Daniel Agger and Alvaro Arbeloa are also in line for contract renewals.

    Then Benitez will address areas that need improving at the end of the season. With his own position resolved, he feels in a stronger position to go for the calibre of player needed to break down packed defences at Anfield.

    There were even assurances about the club's ownership, with Tom Hicks insisting he would still be in charge when Benitez's new contract ends.

    Hicks and co-owner George Gillett have to refinance more than £300m worth of loans by July, but the Texan was in typically defiant mood as he vowed to meet the deadline and raise the funds needed for a planned new stadium at Stanley Park.

    'Rafa is going to be here for the next five years, and I know I am, too,' he said. 'I think George will be here for the next five years, although I cannot speak for him. We want to be here when the new stadium opens, because we want to build that sucker.'

    Hicks revealed head-hunting firms were being consulted over a replacement for chief executive Rick Parry.
