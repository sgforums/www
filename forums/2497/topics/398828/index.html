    Arsenal boss Arsene Wenger wants Sunderland's Craig Gordon to solve his problem position of goalkeeper, according to Sunday paper reports.

    The Gunners chief has insisted in recent weeks that he wasn't looking for a new No.1, but the alarming form of Lucasz Fabianski has forced him into a re-think.

    Fabianski has a made a number of high-prolife errors in recent weeks as stand-in for Manuel Almunia, another man who has had a less than convincing season, and many pundits have insisted that Wenger must buy a new keeper if the north Londoners are to challenge for the big prizes.

    And now it seems as though the Frenchman has come around to that way of thinking as he plots a raid for the Wearsiders' Scottish international.

    Sunderland would want to recoup the £9million they paid to Hearts to land Gordon in 2007 but, with money to spend, that fee would not be a problem for the Gunners.

    If Wenger fails in a move for Gordon, he will turn his attention to Blackburn's former Tottenham stopper, Paul Robinson.
