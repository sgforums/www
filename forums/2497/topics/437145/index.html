    Transfer frenzy! Premier League spending spree hots up on eve of deadline

     

     

     

     

     

     

     

    The transfer window exploded into a frenzy of activity on Tuesday.

    Manchester City made a shock move for Owen Hargreaves, Tottenham completed the £5million signing of Scott Parker and made a fresh bid, believed to be in the region of £9m, for Gary Cahill - while Sunderland launched a £10m approach for Peter Crouch.

    In a breathless day of multi-million-pound trading, Arsenal kicked into overdrive ahead of Wednesday's 11pm deadline.

    Arsene Wenger splashed some of the £80m at his disposal, completing a hat-trick of signings - Per Mertesacker, Park Chu-young and Andre Santos. He is now expected to follow up his interest in Everton's Mikel Arteta and Lille's Eden Hazard.

     

     

     

    Next up? Arsenal are in the hunt for Lille's Eden Hazard

     

     

    Newcastle also joined the spending spree, securing Inter Milan defender Davide Santon and looked set for a second bid for Freiburg striker Papiss Demba Cisse after their £10m offer was rejected.

    City were on Tuesday night ready to sign former England and Manchester United midfielder Hargreaves, 30, on a one-year deal after he came through a three-hour medical.

     

     

     

    Newcastle target: Papiss Demba Cisse (right)

     

     

     

    City manager Roberto Mancini was told Hargreaves was 'in great nick' and all that was left to conclude were the details of a contract that will, given his injury record, be heavily incentivised.

    Hargreaves is a free agent, and City have until Friday's deadline for submitting squads to the Barclays Premier League to finalise the deal.

    Late bid: Tottenham have made an offer for Gary Cahill (left)

     

     

    Bolton defender Cahill, 25, who has a £17m release clause written into his contract, was the subject of a firm bid from Tottenham and in another twist Joe Cole, 29, held talks with Lille over a loan move from Liverpool .

    Fulham also agreed a £10.6m fee with FC Twente for winger Bryan Ruiz, 26, but the deal has not been completed.

     

     

     

    Big-money move: Bryan Ruiz (left) is joining Fulham

     

     

    Arsenal plugged a hole in the centre of their defence with 6ft 6in German Mertesacker, 26, who joined in an £8m move from Werder Bremen. He arrived in the capital on Tuesday to undergo a medical before heading back to Germany for Friday's clash against Austria.

    Wenger completed the £3m signing of Monaco's South Korea forward Park, 26, and Fenerbahce's Brazil left back Santos, 28, signed for £6.2m.

     

     

     

    Plugging the hole: Per Mertesacker

     

     

    On Tuesday night it was unclear if Bolton had accepted Tottenham's offer for Cahill - but it is understood a bid of nearer to £12m would be enough.

    Centre back Sebastian Bassong, 25, has been mooted as a possible makeweight - as confirmed by sources close to the Cameroon defender.

     

     

     

    Swap deal: Sebastien Bassong (left) could be joining Bolton

     

     

    Sunderland boss Steve Bruce is still short of Tottenham's asking price of £12m for Crouch, 30, but a swap deal involving Asamoah Gyan, 25, who cost £13m, cannot be ruled out.

    Parker's £5m move to White Hart Lane came after the England midfielder, 30, handed in a written transfer request to West Ham.


    Pastures new: Scott Parker is joining Tottenham

     

     

    Chelsea 's Yossi Benayoun, 31, was reportedly given permission to leave Israel's training camp on Tuesday to return to England to undergo a medical at Spurs.

    Chelsea were said to be considering a swoop for 17-year-old Everton sensation Ross Barkley, while the Stamford Bridge club also still hope to land Luka Modric, 25.

     


    Surprise move: Chelsea want Ross Barkley

     

    Wilson Palacios, 27, will be confirmed as a Stoke player, while Crouch and Jermaine Jenas could leave.

    Heurelho Gomes, 30, is in talks with CSKA Moscow over a loan and Aston Villa have bid £4m for Middlesbrough defender Rhys Williams, 23.
