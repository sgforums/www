    Martin Jol has become a target for Dutch giants Ajax following more speculation that his days at Tottenham are numbered.

    Jol has been under pressure following Spurs' poor start to the new season and it was increased by Fulham's late fightback at Craven Cottage on Saturday.

    Spurs were cruising to a 3-1 victory against the Cottagers after dominating the game for large chunks, but two goals in the last 13 minutes denied Jol's men all three points.

    The draw unsurprisingly kicked off fresh reports that Jol could be ousted and PSV Edinhoven boss Ronald Koeman is the latest name to be linked with the club after Juande Ramos opted to stay with Sevilla.

    Jol is likely to return to his homeland if Spurs axe him. He is widely expected to become a frontrunner for the Holland national job next summer but could be tempted into taking a job with Ajax if he leaves the Lane.
