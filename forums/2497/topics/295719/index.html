    Maldini denies retiring after Club World Cup

    AC Milan captain and defender Paolo Maldini has squashed rumours that he would retire after the Club World Cup opening in Japan Friday.

    "I still have a contract with Milan until June of 2008 and I am prepared to strictly follow it," he said Friday, the day after the European Champions League and Super Cup winners arrived here for the tournament of continental champions.

    "I am in such good condition and I don't intend to give up the contract before it expires," added the 39-year-old Maldini.

    Italian media reported on Thursday that Maldini was considering bringing forward his retirement, expected for the end of the season, due to problems in both knees.

    Maldini, who has won seven Serie-A titles, five Champions League crowns and two Intercontinental Cups since joining Milan in 1985, has struggled with his knees in recent years. He returned to play last month after surgery.
