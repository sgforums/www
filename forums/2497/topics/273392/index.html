    The brother and agent of the Brazilian star has rebuffed the claims that he met with Galliani this week to discuss the transfer of the Barcelona player to the Rossoneri.

    Roberto Assis was interviewed by the Spanish website 'Globoesporte.com', where he categorically denied that he met with Adriano Galliani of Milan to discuss his client's future.

    "There was no meeting with the Milan directors," Ronaldinho's brother declared.

    "I was in Europe, but not in Italy and anyway Ronaldinho is happy at Barcelona. If one day he leaves the club it would be with the consensus of the Azulgrana directors.

    "However," he continued, "It's true that Milan offered 60 million Euros to Barcelona, but the offer was not accepted."

    Milan are not yet ready to give up on Ronaldinho, and if the opportunity arises, they will increase the bid to sign the Brazilian ace and bring him to Italy.
