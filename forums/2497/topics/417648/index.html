    Newcastle 3 Liverpool 1: Alan Pardew off to a flyer thanks to Kevin Nolan, Joey Barton and Andy Carroll

     

     

     

     

    TWO GOOD ... boss Pardew enjoys Toon's twin strikes late on to clinch victory

     

     

     

    ALAN PARDEW had a dream start to life at Newcastle after his side walloped Liverpool.

    The decision to appoint Pardew this week has been ridiculed but the new Toon boss had the last laugh as his side cruised past the Reds.

    Skipper Kevin Nolan led from the front but his goal was cancelled out by Dirk Kuyt. Scouser Barton was on hand to restore the lead and Andy Carroll thumped a late third to make it a perfect first day for Pards.

    Sol Campbell was sweating early on when he allowed Glen Johnson's long ball to slip under his foot. Fernando Torres could not cash in, however, and Tim Krul rushed out to the rescue.

    Maxi Rodriguez then had a crack but Newcastle weathered the early storm and started to settle in. But they could have been behind with 10 minutes gone after Jose Enrique gifted Liverpool a soft free-kick.

    The Toon star fouled Glen Johnson on the edge of the box, Raul Meireles crossed and the ball eventually broke to Sotirios Kyrgiakos. But Kyrgiakos could not take advantage and Krul parried his drive.

    Newcastle had their first sniff moments later but Carroll was denied by Pepe Reina. It did not matter though as the hosts soon broke the deadlock.

    Barton sent in a free-kick, Carroll nodded down and Nolan blasted past Reina. The Spaniard was called into action soon after when Carroll played a one-two with Shola Ameobi before pulling the trigger.

     

    Wide-eyed wonder: Newcastle owner Mike Ashley faced calls to resign before and during the game

     

     

    At the other end, Liverpool came close to a leveller 12 minutes before half-time. Meireles drilled a shot through the box that came off David Ngog and had to be cleared off the line by Enrique.

    Krygiakos then nodded wide as the Reds finished the half strongly.

    Newcastle came storming out of the blocks after the break and Reina had to be alert to grab Barton's cross. But within five minutes of the restart Liverpool were level.

    Kuyt picked up possession on the edge and, as Sol Campbell tumbled to the floor, the Dutchman's shot crept past Krul off the boot of Steven Taylor.

    Toon looked to hit back and Carroll thundered a header over the bar from the penalty spot.

    Pardew then threw Nile Ranger into the mix and the rookie could have had a first top-flight goal seconds later when he blazed wide.

    Newcastle did not have to wait too long to find the net, however, with Barton the hero. The former Manchester City ace tucked past Reina on 80 minutes after Carroll had flicked on a Krul punt forward.

    It was party time St James' Park but there was more to come and Carroll hit a glorious third in the final minute.

     

     

    Newcastle: Krul, Simpson, Steven Taylor, Campbell, Jose Enrique, Barton (Routledge 90), Nolan (Smith 87), Tiote, Gutierrez, Carroll, Ameobi (Ranger 63). Subs Not Used: Harper, Guthrie, Lovenkrands, Perch. Booked: Gutierrez, Tiote.

    Goals: Nolan 15, Barton 80, Carroll 90.

     

    Liverpool: Reina, Johnson, Kyrgiakos, Skrtel, Konchesky, Kuyt, Meireles, Lucas, Maxi (Jovanovic 85), Torres, Ngog (Babel 72). Subs Not Used: Jones, Aurelio, Cole, Poulsen, Kelly. Booked: Johnson.

    Goals: Kuyt 49.

    Att: 50,137

    Ref: Lee Mason (Lancashire).
