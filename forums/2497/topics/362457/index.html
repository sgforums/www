    Pato not interested in Blues link

    Reported Chelsea target Alexandre Pato has dismissed speculation linking him with a big-money move to the Premier League outfit, insisting he wants to stay with Milan and “win everything” on offer.

    Following manager Carlo Ancelotti’s departure from San Siro for Stamford Bridge, 19-year-old Brazilian Pato has been tipped to follow in the Italian’s footsteps, with an incredible £45 million price tag having been mooted.

    Having scored 15 goals in Serie A in only his second campaign with the Rossoneri, Pato has had praise lavished on him, but wants to remain in Milan to continue his pursuit for success with the club.

    He told Gazzetta dello Sport: "I have read the reports linking me with Chelsea, but my agent and the club deal with this type of thing. I want to win everything with Milan."

    Aside from the issue of his own future, Pato commented on his fellow countryman Kaka’s reported move to Real Madrid.

    "I just hope that everybody will be happy in the end: Kaka, Milan and Real. Or, perhaps, even Chelsea, who are also interested in 'Ricky'," he added.

    "I'm sure that Kaka will make the right choice for him and for Milan."

    Chelsea make improved offer for AC Milan striker Pato

    Chelsea have made a new offer for AC Milan striker Alexandre Pato.

    The Daily Express says Chelsea tabled an improved £38million offer for Pato last night as they conceded defeat in their pursuit of his AC Milan team-mate Kaka.

    Blues owner Roman Abramovich immediately acted to line up his fall-back option and Chelsea upped their initial offer of £31m for Pato to £38m, and offered the 19-year-old Brazilian, known as ‘The Duck’ a contract worth £5m a year.

    Pato’s agent, Gilmar Veloz, said last night that the teenager is keen to be reunited with new Chelsea manager Carlo Ancelotti.

    Veloz said: “We are pleased about Chelsea’s interest in Pato, but Milan have the final decision. The player has a contract with them, but if they came to agreement with Chelsea we would be open to start negotiations.

    “At the moment I cannot give guarantees to the Milan fans that Pato is staying.”
