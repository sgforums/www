    Yossi Benayoun set to join Chelsea! Blues close in on £6.5m deal to sign Liverpool midfielder

     

    Blues bound: Liverpool midfielder Yossi Benayoun is set to sign for Chelsea for £6.5m

     

     

    Chelsea are closing on a £6.5million deal to sign Liverpool midfielder Yossi Benayoun.

    Sportsmail revealed Chelsea's interest last month but Anfield sources said the fee had yet to be finalised.

    Former Liverpool manager Rafa Benitez was reluctant to sell Benayoun to Chelsea for anything less than £10m and had a £7.5m offer on the table from CSKA Moscow.

    However, the Israeli international was reluctant to go to Russia and had set his heart on Stamford Bridge. He has now provisionally agreed to join the Premier League champions on a four year contract with an option for a further year.

    Benayoun is on holiday in Israel but wants to fly to London later this week to complete the formalities and undergo a medical.

     

    Silva lining: Chelsea want to beat rivals Manchester City to the signing of Valencia ace David Silva

     

    The 30-year-old, who signed a two year contract extension at Liverpool last year, is expected to be offered around £70,000 a week.

    Chelsea are also keen on Valencia's David Silva - a target for Manchester City.

    Liverpool still to receive Chelsea bid for Benayoun

    Liverpool say no offer has yet arrived from Chelsea for midfielder Yossi Benayoun.

    Reports suggesting the Reds have agreed a £6million deal with Chelsea are premature but the Merseysiders are resigned to the fact the 30-year-old will leave and accept it is only a matter of time before an offer comes in, reports PA Sport.

    Benayoun signed an improved contract only last year but even before the departure of manager Rafael Benitez earlier this month he had already dropped several hints he wanted to leave.

    Back on May 2, after the 2-0 defeat by Chelsea at Anfield, the Israeli said "he couldn't say for sure" whether he would be at Anfield on the opening day of the new campaign.

    Chelsea are believed to see the former West Ham star as a direct replacement for Joe Cole, whose departure from Stamford Bridge was confirmed when the two parties failed to agree terms on a new contract after his current deal expired.
