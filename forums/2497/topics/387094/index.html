    Report: Bolton Wanderers And Burnley Agree Compensation For Owen Coyle

    Owen Coyle, Burnley (Getty Images)

    Lancashire rivals Bolton Wanderers and Burnley have agreed a compensatory figure that will pave the way for Owen Coyle to move from Turf Moor to the Reebok Stadium.

    Burnley are loathe to lose the Scottish-born boss, and are believed to have held out for more than £3 million in compensation from Wanderers, according to Sky Sports News.

    Coyle has been Bolton's top choice to replace the sacked Gary Megson, and the deal is set to be announced this evening despite a desperate last minute plea from Burnley chairman Barry Kilby.

    Financial clout is said to be the main lure for Coyle, who recently remarked that Bolton had a budget three times that of Burnley's.

    Coyle agrees to take Bolton job

    Owen Coyle has agreed to take the Bolton Wanderers job.

    The Guardian says Coyle is poised to be confirmed as the new manager of Bolton Wanderers in the next 48 hours. The Scot agreed to move from Burnley to the Reebok Stadium tonight after he spent the evening discussing terms with Barry Kilby, the chairman at Turf Moor. Barring a dramatic about-face on Coyle's part, the switch should be finalised shortly.

    Coyle had met Phil Gartside, Bolton's chairman, on Sunday in Scotland – where he was belatedly celebrating the festive period with his mother – to discuss the mooted transfer after receiving reluctant permission to open talks from Kilby.

    Burnley said after tonight's discussions that Coyle and Kilby had agreed "to take a further 24 hours to digest the information shared before making any further announcement". It is likely this reflects an ongoing wrangle over compensation.
