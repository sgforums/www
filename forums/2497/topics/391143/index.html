    Luis Fabiano puts Manchester City and Chelsea on alert for £15m summer transfer scramble

    Sevilla striker Luis Fabiano is set to spark a summer bidding war between mega rich rivals Manchester City and Chelsea after the Brazil star sounded out a potential big-money move to the Premier League.

    Fabiano, who missed a penalty in the 1-0 exhibition against England last November in Qatar, has a year to run on his current contract with the La Liga club.

    The 29-year-old hitman has struck eight goals in 12 games for the Rojiblancos having formed a fruitful partnership with ex-West Ham and Spurs frontman Fredi Kanoute.

    Changing man: Luis Fabiano insists he is happy at Sevilla put hinted things 'could change' with one year left to run on his contract

    And although the Brazilian admits he is content with life in Spain, he has alerted a host of prospective suitors with his admission that 'anything can happen.'

    'I have a year left on my contract and am happy at Sevilla. But things can change very quickly,' he claimed in The Sun.

    'I do not want to speak about my future at the moment because we are just thinking about the Champions League and the Spanish league where we still have a lot to play for.'

    On the move; Fabiano has not ruled out the possibility of one day playing in England

    The Brazilian would command a fee upwards of £10million which Sevilla would find difficult to refuse he the striker opts to stall over a fresh deal.

    With Robinho keen to prolong his short-term loan stay at Santos permanently, Manchester City could swoop for his services with Chelsea also seriously interested.
