    Real Madrid cool their interest in Young as Villa demand £30m for England star

     

    Villans hero: Ashley Young has been linked with a move to Real Madrid in January.

     

    Real Madrid have conceded defeat in their bid to sign Aston Villa star Ashley Young - for now. Sources in Spain say the Spanish champions will not make a move in the January transfer window after being told he would cost £30million.

    Juande Ramos made Young his No1 transfer target after taking over at the Bernabeu, but Real will wait until the end of the season to make their move after already shelling out about £20m to take midfielder Lassana Diarra from Portsmouth and a similar figure to sign striker Klaas-Jan Huntelaar from Ajax.

    Real president Ramon Calderon, sporting director Predrag Mijatovic and coach Ramos will discussed their strategy for signing the 23-year-old over the last few days.

    One option was to offer Rafael van der Vaart in part of a player-plus-cash deal. The Holland forward has struggled with injuries and form since signing from Hamburg in the summer.

    But Real are also looking a cheaper alternatives to Young and are still interested in signing Jermain Pennant from Liverpool.

    Benfica's Argentina winger Angel di Maria is another option, though the Portuguese team are reluctant to sell in January and would only settle for a deal at around £15m-£20m.
