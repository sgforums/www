    Who should Arsene Wenger have bought?

     

    Jonathan Woodgate
    Before signing for Tottenham in January, Woodgate checked with Arsenal to see if Arsene Wenger wanted to follow up his long-standing admiration with a firm bid. The cultured central defender clearly fancied a move to the Emirates.

    The only trouble was, Wenger had other ideas. Either the Frenchman didn't really rate Woodgate or he believed his squad contained enough quality centre-halves already. That didn't look the case at Anfield on Tuesday, Phillippe Senderos proving once again that he doesn't quite measure up at the top level.

     

     

    Igor Akineev
    It would be unfair to point the finger at Arsenal's goalkeeper when trying to work out where things have gone wrong. Almunia, after all, has proved fairly steady since taking the gloves off Jens Lehmann. That's not to say that he inspires and reassures his defenders or that he will win you 10 points over the course of a season to turn challengers into champions. Akineev's displays for CSKA Moscow were good enough to attract Wenger's attention last year and have secured his status, at 22, as Russia's number one.

     

     

    Franck Ribery
    It didn't need a man-of-the-match performance against England the other week to tell us that Ribery, once of Marseille and at Bayern Munich since last summer, is a bit special.

    Hindsight is a wonderful thing and but for Tomas Rosicky's injury problems we might not be talking about Arsenal's need for an intelligent wide man with pace and a goalscoring threat. Ribery gives you that and he would have offered so much more class and composure than, say, the temperamental outpourings of Emmanuel Eboue.
