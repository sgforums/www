Ronaldo: I Don't Intend To Return To Europe

    Brazilian superstar Ronaldo, affirmed that he won't use Corinthians as a stepping ground to return to Europe, because his intention is to end his career in Brazil.

    On Tuesday Ronaldo agreed to a controversial move to Brazilian giants Corinthians, despite the fact that he had been training with his beloved Flamengo for the last few months.

    But although there has been much speculation that he could return to Europe in the near future, Ronaldo has affirmed that his intention is to remain in Brazil.

    "My plan is not to return to Europe, I don't intend to use Corinthians as a trampoline. I already had an incredible life in Europe. But right now I have signed a contract with a great club, and if things go well, the doors will surely always be open," Ronaldo told TV Globo.

    Other Brazilians, such as Adriano, returned to Brazilian football to rediscover their top form before heading back to Europe. But Ronaldo insists that he is looking forward to his latest challenge, and is hoping to make it permanent.

    According to reports, Corinthians will organize a high-profile friendly match with a top European club, most probably Real Madrid, where Ronaldo will likely make his official Corinthians debut. The match is expected to take place on the 17th of January, in Sao Paulo.

    Finally, Ronaldo stated that he wants to "return to his all-time best" and that he intends to be the top goal-scorer of the Paulista State Championship next year.
