    CHELSEA will be reported to FIFA if they follow up their interest in Chilean wonderkid Nicolas Millan.

    The 15-year-old winger has been dubbed South America's answer to Cristiano Ronaldo.

    Chilean champions Colo Colo are furious after it emerged their star is set to go on trial with the Blues.

    Colo Colo boss Claudio Borghi has threatened to take action if Millan, the youngest-ever player to appear in the Chilean league, travels to London.

    Borghi said: "I do not know who authorised him to travel. If he goes, we will make a formal complaint to Chelsea and FIFA.

    "If he travels without a contract being signed, it can be dangerous, therefore they can steal him at any time.

    Â“It would be possible for him to go since several others have done it."

    Borghi wants include Millan - who is also wanted by Inter Milan - in his squad for tomorrow's clash with Cobresal.

    But the youngster will not be available if he makes the trip to West London and that will force Colo Colo to complain to FIFA.

    Millan said: "No date has been set for a trial but an invitation exists. It would be a dream to be with players like Drogba. Chelsea is like Colo Colo, but there is more money there."

    Chelsea have refused to comment on the reports.
