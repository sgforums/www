    ALEX FERGUSON emerged the big winner as FA Cup draws left two rivals facing a fixture nightmare. Fergie’s Manchester United coasted past Spurs on Saturday and into a fifth-round trip to Derby or Nottingham Forest. 

    But Arsenal and Liverpool were both held to draws yesterday, leaving them to fit a replay into their already chaotic schedules that include vital Premier League and Champions League clashes. 

    After seeing his side held 0-0 by Championship Cardiff, Gunners boss Wenger admitted: “We could do without it, 100 per cent. But let’s not complain because we play football. It’s one more game to play. 

    "If that’s the price we have to pay to stay in the hat, we’re happy to do it. “We are very short at the moment and cannot rotate our players much. “I could rest a few players today but not as many as I’d wanted. “I’d planned to take Robin van Persie off but it didn’t work out that way. At 0-0 anything can happen. But I wasn’t too worried in the second half. They created more chances in the first half.” 

    Liverpool chief Rafa Benitez saw his side held 1-1 by Merseyside rivals Everton for the second time in a week. He said: “We have a lot of games coming up but we have a big squad and we can manage it. 

    “We have good players who can come in and when you play so many games you need that. “I made four changes today and it was a strong team because we want to progress. “It would be better if these games were all finished on he day, with no replays, but that’s not how it is done.” 

    While Wenger was groaning, Cardiff boss Dave Jones was delighted his side earned a money-spinning second crack at the Gunners. Jones said: “We gave it a go. With a little bit more composure or luck, it might have gone for us. “There were one or two boos in the last minute that we were keeping the ball in the corner, but we’re playing Arsenal. 

    “Give us a break. What we showed today is that we can match the best. “When we were getting booed in the last couple of minutes for keeping the ball, the only one cheering on our side was the chairman. 

    “It will be a massive pay day for us down there next week.”

    LAMPARD will play against Lampard in the fifth round of the FA Cup. 

    Yesterday’s draw paired Frank Lampard’s Chelsea with Watford — where his dad Frank snr is a consultant to boss Brendan Rodgers, the Blues’ former reserve boss. 

    Hornets defender Jay DeMerit said: “It’s a dream draw for everyone at the club. We know it’s going to be tough as they are a world-class side. “But everyone here is looking forward to it. 

    “As a club we’ve never been short of confidence and you want to test yourself.”

    Swansea’s reward for dumping out holders Pompey is a home tie with Fulham. All the big clubs avoided each other in the draw. 

    Manchester United must travel to Derby or Nottingham Forest. Liverpool will play Aston Villa if they win their respective replays against Everton and Doncaster. 

    If Arsenal beat Cardiff in next week’s replay they host West Brom or Burnley.
