    RAFA BENITEZ has made an astonishing pledge to all Liverpool fans: "I guarantee we will finish in the top four."

    The stumbling Kop kings are seventh in the Premier League and 13 points off the top - and also out of the Champions League.

    But Benitez, whose side lost 2-1 to Arsenal on Sunday, said: "You can't be negative, you have to think of the positives and I am.

    "We are working so hard and I can guarantee you we will finish in the top four. "If you want to be a good manager you have to find a way to change things. If you think confidence is low, you have to find a way to lift it.

    "You have to take positives and our first half against Arsenal was so good. We know everything changed after that but you have to look at how we played in the first and stick together."

    Tonight, Anfield pays tribute to the greatest Reds boss of all, when the must-win clash against Wigan marks a celebration of the 50th anniversary of Bill Shankly's first game in charge.

    Legends from his two great sides of the 1960s and 70s will take the salute of the Kop, as well as 11 members of Shanks' family.

    Those glory days are in stark contrast to the side's current form. Benitez added: "Everyone wants to win trophies here and our idea is to bring silverware to the club every year if we can. "We have lost some opportunities but still have the FA Cup and the Europa League.

    "In the summer we will analyse the situation. I spoke to Steven Gerrard and Fernando Torres about the future and both were very supportive. They know the priority is to perform now. We know we have to improve."

    Benitez's promise of a fourth-place finish has certainly put him in the firing line if he fails.

    RAFA STICKS WITH TOP-FOUR PLEDGE






    Rafa Benitez believes he was right to pledge that Liverpool would finish in the top four in the Premier League.

    The Spaniard declared earlier this month that he could 'guarantee' his side's qualification for next season's Champions League.

    The pledge came despite a disappointing start to the current campaign which has left Liverpool seventh at the halfway point.

    And Benitez's choice of phrase has been questioned by critics who think he should not make promises he is not certain to keep.

    He, however, has defended himself by insisting he has no option but to be positive.

    Benitez feels Liverpool are strong enough to climb the table over the next few months and is not backing down over his previous statement.

    "People keep asking me why I say categorically that we will be in the top four - but what do you expect from me?" said Benitez.

    "I have to say that. We have confidence and we have to do it because we are a top side."
