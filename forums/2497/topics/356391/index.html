    Deco warns Chelsea stopping Lionel Messi is Mission Impossible

    Deco has warned his Chelsea team-mates they face mission impossible trying to stop old Barcelona pal Leo Messi.

     

    Deco spent four seasons at Barca before moving to London - and he has watched Messi's rise from the Catalan giants' reserves to the summit of world football. Guus Hiddink's Blues must now overcome Barca to reach the Champions League Final.

    And Portuguese playmaker Deco said: "Leo Messi is incredible. He is one of my best friends and at the moment, almost impossible to stop on his day. He is an incredible player and, if he is on form, we can only wait and see who has the ability to stop him."

    Chelsea are without Ashley Cole for the first leg of the tie, but Deco remains confident that last season's finalists can stop the free-scoring Spaniards.

    He added: "Barca are one of the rivals we managed to avoid in the first knock-out rounds, but at this stage you cannot choose your opponents. "If we want to go all the way to the final in Rome we must beat them, which will not be easy. But we can do this.

    "I still have a great deal of affection for Barca. I was very happy there and I cannot help but think fondly about that stage of my life."

    Hiddink last night revealed Chelsea hired him as a panic measure because they feared they were "slipping" out of the Champions League.

    The Dutchman took charge at Chelsea two months ago after Luiz Felipe Scolari was sacked after a poor run which saw them in danger of losing their top-four status.

    Mancienne: I can do a job on Messi

    Chelsea defender Michael Mancienne insists he is ready for the task of marking Lionel Messi in the Champions League semi-final with Barcelona.

    Blues boss Guus Hiddink is considering playing Mancienne at left-back against Barca in the first leg at Camp Nou next week, with first-choice Ashley Cole missing through suspension.

    Mancienne played at left-back in Monday night's reserve team draw against West Brom as Hiddink wanted to try out the England Under-21 international in that role.

    Now the player himself says he is ready to face arguably the best player on the planet in the shape of Messi.

    "It was a new one on me, I've never really played there before. I would love the challenge of facing Messi, " Mancienne told Chelsea TV.

    "The manager came to watch and I was asked to have a go. The manager is keeping his options open.

    "They told me a couple of days ago I would have a go at left-back and I did ask Ashley a few questions about it. It felt alright."

    Monday night's game also saw the return of Juliano Belletti after injury. The Brazilian scored to put himself in contention for a first-team recall.
