    Dentinho destined for Premier League


    Brazilian starlet Dentinho could be set for a switch to English shores this summer, with both Arsenal and Manchester United reportedly interested in securing the youngster's services.

    The 20-year-old Corinthians talent has netted 30 league goals in 96 appearances since breaking onto the first-team scene in 2007, and has recently been partnered up-front by legendary forward Ronaldo.

    And with United - seeking a replacement for Real Madrid-bound FIFA World Player of the Year Cristiano Ronaldo - appearing set to miss out on fellow samba prodigy Douglas Costa, Dentinho may be next on the Red Devils' hit-list.

    Arsenal are all also believed to be keen on the 5ft 9in front-man. However, Corinthians' high valuation of the player could prove a stumbling block in any potential deal, according to the player's agent.

    “They [Corinthians] want too much money for Dentinho, so the club who are willing to pay that kind of money will land the player," Ferhat Ciftci told Sport.co.uk.

    "He is a great talent. Another great Brazilian talent. A very good technical player who is good on the ball.

    "The fans will love him in England, the public will worship him. A little bit like Cristiano Ronaldo.

    “He wants to play in the Premier League. It doesn't make a difference if it is Arsenal or Manchester United.”
