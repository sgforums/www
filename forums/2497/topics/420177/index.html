    Chelsea Refuse To Offer More Than €20 Million For Benfica Defender David Luiz - Report

    Chelsea are reluctant to increase their €20 million offer for Benfica defender David Luiz.

     
    David Luiz, Benfica  
     
     
    It appears unlikely that highly rated defender David Luiz will leave Portuguese champions Benfica for Premier League giants Chelsea in the January transfer window as the Blues have made it clear that they're not offering more than €20 million for the centre back, according to a report in A Bola.

    Chelsea recently made a €20m offer for the Brazil international, but Benfica were not impressed and rejected the bid, holding out for at least €25m.

    Nevertheless, Chelsea owner Roman Abramovich is reluctant to splash the cash on David Luiz and has decided to stall negotiations for now.

    Chelsea's main reason not to increase their offer for David Luiz is the fact that the defender won't be eligible to play in the Champions League following his involvement with Benfica.

    The Champions League hopefuls are eager to add a new centre-back to their squad in the winter transfer window and manager Carlo Ancelotti has made the 23-year-old David Luiz his primary target on the transfer market.

    Benfica expect Chelsea to return for David Luiz toward the end of the January transfer window if they fail to land another centre-back before.

    Benfica Want €30 Million Plus Chelsea Players For David Luiz - Report

    Benfica have raised demands for Chelsea target David Luiz, according to reports in Portugal...


     
    David Luiz, Benfica

     

     

     

    Benfica have hiked up the price for David Luiz with Chelsea having to offer €30 million plus a number of players if they want him.

    The latest news from Record.pt comes less than 24 hours after the Portuguese club released a statement denying that they were in talks with Chelsea over a transfer.

    23-year-old Luiz has been tipped to join the Blues during the January transfer window, but official statements coupled with evolving reports are sending out mixed messages.

    The official word from Benfica is there were "no negotiations with Chelsea or any other club" in relation to reports claiming that the Brazilian was for sale.

    But Record insists that what was initially reported as a €25m offer plus the services of Paulo Ferreira has now turned into demands of €30m and a handful of players.
