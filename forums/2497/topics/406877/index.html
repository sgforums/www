    UNSETTLED IRELAND HITS OUT AT MANCINI

    Irishman wants to be kept informed of any interest

    IRELAND: Yet to hear of any interest

     

    STEPHEN IRELAND has hit back at Roberto Mancini after the Italian told him last week that he has no future at Manchester City.

    The former Republic of Ireland international did not reach the same high level last season that he did the previous campaign, when he was voted Player of the Year.

    Mancini is desperate to reduce the size of the squad as he needs to comply with the Premier League's new rules on squad sizes not exceeding 25 players.

    But Ireland believes he was not given a fair crack of the whip under Mancini as the team lost out to Spurs in the race for fourth place.

    "I came off playing 67 games two years ago to playing just 16 times, including substitute appearances last season.

    "I can't really live up to what I did before that if I am not getting the opportunities," he said.

    "For me, I found that very difficult but if the manager had had a team big enough and strong enough to get through the games and bully teams and get the points we would have done really well.

    "I am very ambitious but things are out of my hands.

    "I only know what I read in the papers. I haven't heard one bit of interest.

    "I have seen Fulham, Spurs and Sunderland mentioned but I haven't been told anything. I'd hope the club would contact me if there was some interest."
