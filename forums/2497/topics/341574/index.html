    The Roman legend will not play against Arsenal after further tests have ruled him out of action for at least 60 days.

    It has been confirmed Roma captain Francesco Totti will be out of action for up to 60 days following further tests at the Villa Stuart Clinic this morning.

    Scans have revealed the Giallorossi captain has suffered a second-grade muscular tear in his right leg, the new results mean the one month lay-off prediction has gone out of the window.

    Totti will now need between six and eight weeks to make a full recovery.

    It's bad news for Roma coach Luciano Spalletti as his main man will miss several vital games including the Serie A match against Milan, as well the first leg of the Champions League last 16 clash with Arsenal.

    The talisman has been ordered to rest for the next 10 days and he will undergo further tests at the beginning of the new year.

    Er Pupone sustained the injury during the 3-2 defeat to Catania after attempting a back-heel pass.

    The Giallorossi are currently in eleventh place in Serie A, nine points off the Champions League places.
