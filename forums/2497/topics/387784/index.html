    Real Madrid Striker Karim Benzema Disappointed With Bench Role

    French star not enjoying life on the bench but happy to be learning.

    Karim Benzema, Real Madrid (Getty Images)

    Real Madrid striker Karim Benzema has admitted he is “disappointed” to constantly find his name among the substitutes, but assured fans that he is happy in Madrid and sees the experience as a learning curve.

    The 22-year-old Frenchman has recently found himself playing second fiddle to Argentine forward Gonzalo Higuain.

    “Yes, you can say I'm disappointed because I am. But it is something that is part of the job. Things are well, I'm at a great club and learning," he told L'Equipe.

    The €35 million forward, who came on to replace Brazilian star Kaka with just over 20 minutes to go against Mallorca at the Bernabeu last night, said he was not going to let his time out of the team become “an issue".

    The former Lyon man went on to say that his job now was to “convince the coaching staff and earn their trust,” adding, “I will give everything to get myself a place [in the team].”
