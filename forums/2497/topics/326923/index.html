    Modric: I'll ensure Spurs break into Champions League cartel

     

    Modric: Looking to fire Tottenham into the top four.

     

     

    Tottenham's Croatian star Luka Modric says he will repay the club's faith him by bringing Champions League football to White Hart Lane. The £16.5million summer siging from Dinamo Zagreb is convinced that Juande Ramos' men can break into the top four, with the 22-year-old excited by the wealth of talent accrued at N17.

    The arrivals of Modric, Heurelho Gomes, David Bentley and Giovani dos Santos have cushioned the blow of the departure of Robbie Keane to Liverpool.

    Despite the uncertain future of mercurial striker Dimitar Berbatov, the Croatian schemer is convinced the club is headed for big things.

    Modric said: 'I will justify the money Tottenham invested in me. The first target has to be to threaten the big four. 'We’re capable of achieving some really good results,' he told The Sun.

    Modric's comments echo those of Brazilian keeper Gomes, who remarked: 'I am not thinking of just making it into the first four.' 'I am thinking of winning, being first or second, in that range. Some people might say I am aiming very high, but that has always been my thinking. 'I think as a champion and I want to win,' Gomes added.
