    Chelsea skipper John Terry today called a crisis meeting as he attempted to stop growing unrest among his team-mates.

    The unhappy squad met after training at Cobham this lunchtime to discuss the implications of Jose Mourinho's departure and the appointment of Avram Grant as his successor.

    Star players remain unconvinced by the appointment of Grant and some suspect he played a part in Mourinho's downfall because of his friendship with Roman Abramovich.

    Skipper Terry, with plenty of input from other senior players, talked about the future and stressed the importance of presenting a unified front as they prepare for Sunday's visit to Manchester United.

    The new Israeli boss who has a close relationship with Abramovich, clearly has a tough job proving to his players that he is the man to lead the club forward.

    It seems certain, for now at least, that Grant will lean heavily upon his assistant, the long-serving club stalwart Steve Clarke.

    In the long-term though, Grant will need to persuade the players, some more than others, that he has no agenda other than to move the club on.

    Mourinho To Fans And Players: Please Don't Revolt

    Jose Mourinho insisted he was happy after having parted ways with Chelsea, and also spoke about his future. Most importantly, he urged the fans and the player not to revolt over his departure.

    "I'm fine, in professional terms and in personal terms I'm fine," said Mourinho during an interview on Portuguese television.

    "I'm happy to have left and I'm happy with what I achieved at Chelsea not only with the results but with the mark I made."

    In a rather selfless gesture, he also urged the fans not to make life difficult for his replacement, Avram Grant.

    He also asked the players - some of whom have already threatened to leave - to remain faithful to the club despite his departure.

    "I don't want the supporters to chant my name if they get a bad result in the future," said the Portugeuse.

    "I don't want players to threaten to leave because I have left. I don't want demonstrations of any sort."

    Moving on to his future, he ruled out a job in Portugal at the moment, and admitted he would want to coach again some time in the future.

    "I don't want to coach in Portugal nor do I want to be the coach of the Portugal national team for the next few years," he said. "I think sometime I will return to coach in England."

    Kenyon is adamant Chelsea need not worry about the likes of John Terry and Frank Lampard leaving in the wake of Mourinho's departure.

    "I am not concerned about there being a mass exodus in the summer," Kenyon added. "I have spoken to them as well as other members of the team.
