    Cristiano Ronaldo's temperament is under fresh scrutiny after he was involved in a half-time tunnel row with Emanuel Pogatetz during Manchester United's 1-0 win over Middlesbrough.

    Berbatov struck in the 69th minute for only his third league goal of the season to record a 1-0 win that sank a stubborn Middlesbrough team who had subdued Old Trafford with their dogged display.

    So dogged that Pogatetz even grabbed Ronaldo around the neck late in the first half. Ronaldo was so incensed at not getting a penalty that he chased Pogatetz as the teams left the pitch and went eyeball to eyeball with the Austrian. The feud continued in the tunnel.

    Ronaldo argues with Emanuel Pogatetz as the teams go off at half-time

    Cristiano Ronaldo and Emmanuel Pogatetz lock horns

    This is the latest in a series of incidents involving Ronaldo, who seems increasingly likely to react when fouled.

    Ronaldo has clashed with Celtic's Scott Brown, Tottenham's Michael Dawson and Andy Wilkinson, of Stoke City, in recent weeks. He also earned a red card for two bookable offences against Manchester City.

    Pogatetz too has history with United. He was sent-off in the Carling Cup tie between the sides earlier this season after a knee-high lunge on Rodrigo Possebon.
