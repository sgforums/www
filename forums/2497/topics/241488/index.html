    Chelsea striker Andriy Shevchenko has revealed his deep dissatisfaction with manager Jose Mourinho and says he has had "better coaches."

    "He is professional, no more and no less. He is a tactical fox and has strict ideas, but I've had better coaches," Shevchenko told the website of German TV channel ARD this week.

    "It is the first time in my career that a coach has said publicly that I don't fit in with his concept.

    "That is not exactly great for motivation. If that is what he really thinks, then why am I here?

    "We had a meeting and he said his opinion had been twisted. But deep inside it still bothers me. If the owner asks me what I think of the manager, then I will speak my mind."

    The Ukrainian who has been written off as a 30-million-pound flop by the British media after his move from AC Milan last year claimed he had become a pawn in the deteriorating relationship between Mourinho and Chelsea's billionaire Russian owner Roman Abramovich.

    "It is no secret that I was more on the wish list of Mr Abramovich than the coach's," Shevchenko said.

    "But Mourinho was very positive and happy with me in the transfer talks. Then a few months ago I suddenly became a target for him because he was having a spat with the chairman."

    He also admitted he was struggling to adapt to life in London.

    "I am here in body, but not in spirit. In January I wouldn't have been opposed to being sent out on loan, but that is not an issue any more."

    He said he felt he was less comfortable in the Chelsea tactical setup than at Milan, where he was the undisputed target man.

    "At Chelsea everyone is responsible for joining in the attacking moves, which is a plus point, but for me personally it is very hard for me to make an impression," he said.

    Shevchenko helped to steer Chelsea to a place in the quarter-finals of the Champions League on Tuesday, setting up the crucial goal for Michael Ballack in the 2-1 win over FC Porto which gave the Londoners a 3-2 victory on aggregate.
