    Manchester United Concerned By Ji-Sung Park's Condition After International Games

    Alex Ferguson bemused by Korean winger's knee problems.

     

    Park Ji-Sung, Manchester United (Goal.com)

    Manchester United manager Sir Alex Ferguson has expressed concern about the amount of knee injuries sustained by Ji-Sung Park following his trips to represent South Korea.

    Ferguson and his staff are looking closely at how the player travels to and from international duty in Asia as they look to minimize the injury problems the player has endured over the past year or so.

    "The problem for Ji this season has been the succession of injuries which have de-railed him in terms of appearances," Ferguson explained, according to The Daily Telegraph.

    "But all of the problems have come after South Korea international games. We have discussed the problem with their staff, as to why he comes back with knee swellings.

    "That's why we are looking towards changing the way he comes back from international games in the future.

    "But he is a very important figure at Manchester United and he will be involved now that he is fit."

    Park may feature in central midfield or even at right-back for United at VfL Wolfsburg in the Champions League this evening, as Ferguson looks to shuffle a squad severely stretched by injuries to his back four.
