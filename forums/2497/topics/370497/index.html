    Arsenal midfielder Tomas Rosicky has suffered a minor injury setback after returning from 18 months on the sidelines. 

    After playing half the recent pre-season game against Barnet, Rosicky was substituted as a precaution by boss Arsene Wenger. 

    The club have confirmed that he sustained a "minor problem" in the friendly on July 18, with the 28-year-old subsequently left out of the Gunners' last three pre-season matches. 

    Rosicky himself, though, insists he is raring to go and hopes to be playing regular football in the near future. 

    "I have a feeling that I am finally getting back to my usual standard of football," he said. "During the injury struggle I began preparing many times but it took a long time to get this far. "I am fit, which is the most important thing now." 

    Rosicky backed Wenger's judgement in leaving him out of pre-season training despite his desire to play. "I thought that I would spend more time on the pitch (against Barnet) and then the two matches that followed I had to watch from the stands," he said. 

    "I wasn't angry with the decision - just glad I could walk again."
