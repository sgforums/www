    JOHN TERRY has warned Chelsea's rivals to be afraid, very afraid.

    He says the arrival of £50million Fernando Torres gives the Blues one of the best attacks on the planet.

    Torres looks set to link up with Didier Drogba and Nicolas Anelka in a new-look frontline. And skipper Terry roared: "They will be among the most feared strikeforce in Europe now.

    "Any teams who say they don't fear us, do so at their peril."

    Torres hit seven goals in just eight games for Liverpool against Chelsea.

    Terry said: "Physically, he is one of the strongest out there. His pace is frightening, his heading is unbelievable and his finishing is top drawer, as we have seen to our cost. "So I am delighted we have got him now.

    "I know Fernando has said he is pleased to be on the same side as me. "Believe me, I am just as delighted - although I still have to face him in training!"

    Salomon Kalou is the man most likely to make way for Torres against Liverpool on Sunday.

    Terry added: "There is a buzz around the camp. To sign players like Torres and David Luiz was a huge statement for the club."
