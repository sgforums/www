    TEAM NEWS

    Arsenal

    The Gunners' injury woes show few signs of easing, although Alex Song could return if he passes a late fitness test on a cartilage problem in his knee which has kept him out of the last three games - all of which Arsenal have lost. If the holding midfielder is fit, he could find himself deployed in central defense alongside Sol Campbell, as both Thomas Vermaelen and William Gallas are out for the rest of the season with calf injuries.

    Manuel Almunia is unlikely to have recovered from a wrist injury, which means Lukasz Fabianski could have another chance to prove that he's not an even bigger liability between the sticks than the Spaniard; goalkeeper is certainly one position Arsenal fans will be expecting Arsene Wenger to address in the summer transfer market.

    Wenger could also give Robin van Persie his first start since November, which might go some way towards rectifying the baffling decision to keep the outstanding Dutch striker on the bench until it was much too late against Wigan last week.

    Andrey Arshavin has spoken optimistically of recovering from a calf problem in time to face City, but the Russian must be considered doubtful. Meanwhile captain Cesc Fabregas, Aaron Ramsey, Denilson, Johan Djourou and Kieran Gibbs are all ruled out.

    Last starting XI (v Wigan):     Fabianski, Sagna, Campbell, Silvestre, Clichy, Walcott, Eastmond , Diaby, Nasri, Rosicky, Bendtner.

    Manchester City

    Mancini should have Micah Richards available again after a knee injury, the England defender having missed City's last four Premier League games, although Nedum Onuoha is likely to retain his place at right-back.

    There could be three ex-Gunners in City's starting XI: revered former captain Patrick Vieira, who can expect a warm welcome if he plays against Arsenal for the first time since leaving for Italy in 2005; central defender and City captain Kolo Toure, who should also get a friendly reception; and striker Emmanuel Adebayor, who is no doubt braced for hostility from the home fans.

    Vieira could start instead of Gareth Barry or Nigel de Jong if Mancini seeks to rotate his central midfielders.

    Another former Arsenal player, Brazilian left-back Sylvinho, is doubtful with a calf problem but would probably miss out anyway following the impressive return of Wayne Bridge after injury.

    Michael Johnson, Martin Petrov (both knee) and Joleon Lescott (hamstring) are all ruled out.

    Last starting XI (v Man Utd):     Given, Onuoha, Kompany, Toure, Bridge, Adam Johnson, De Jong, Barry, Bellamy, Tevez, Adebayor.

    PLAYERS TO WATCH

    Arsenal

    Arsene Wenger has rapidly been running out of players at a crucial phase of the season, and it has cost him and the team dearly. The one bright spot has been the return to fitness of Robin van Persie, whose Arsenal career has been blighted by injury but who, when fit, is a class above the other strikers at the club - and most others in the Premier League.

    The Dutchman - whose five-month lay-off with ruptured ankle ligaments sustained in a friendly international for Holland against Italy - demonstrated in a 22-minute cameo from the bench against Spurs recently just what a difference he might have made to Arsenal's season if he'd remained injury-free.

    Van Persie, who joined the Gunners from Feyenoord in 2004, has scored 71 goals for the club in 137 first-team appearances (plus 57 as a substitute) and is the leading marksman among the players currently at the Emirates. Last season he scored a career-best 20 goals and provided a league-leading 11 assists, and in the current, injury-interrupted campaign, he has scored eight and contributed eight assists.

    Manchester City

    Emmanuel Adebayor stole the headlines in the reverse fixture earlier this season when, taunted by the traveling Arsenal fans who perceived him as a mercenary individual in the wake of his acrimonious departure from the Emirates, the former Togo international celebrated scoring against his ex-employers by running the length of the pitch to slide on his knees in triumph in front of the incensed Gooners.

    Adebayor also landed in hot water for kicking Robin van Persie in the face as the Dutchman lay on the ground, the Arsenal striker claiming it was deliberate. The African incurred a three-match suspension for that action. Kolo Toure and Mancini have both urged him to show greater restraint on Saturday and focus on helping City claim the three points.

    Adebayor scored his last league goals for Arsenal in this fixture last season, and in fact has scored five goals in the last four Premier League meetings between the two sides - four for Arsenal and one for City. He has returned to form lately, scoring four goals in his last three games, and is City's second top scorer with 13 goals (all in the league), behind Carlos Tevez who has struck 28 (22 league).

    PREDICTION

    Arsenal have won their last five League matches at the Emirates, and it is a little over three years since they last lost three consecutive Premier League games. Meanwhile Manchester City have not lost two consecutive Premier League games all season, so something may have to give.

    City are also unbeaten in their last five away games and have won the last two, thrashing Burnley 6-1 at Turf Moor in the most recent. There is a great deal riding on the outcome on Saturday, from points to pride to position to prospects in Europe next season. Expect the high stakes to be matched by high tension - and a few goals.
