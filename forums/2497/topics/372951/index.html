    Wesley Sneijder Omitted From Real Madrid's Squad For Rosenborg Match

    The Dutchman has been left out of the squad's plans for the Santiago Bernabeu trophy.

    Wesley Sneijder has been constantly linked with a move away from Real Madrid lately, with Inter Milan reportedly keen on his services.

    While the two clubs have reportedly agreed a €15m fee for Sneijder, the 25-year-old wants to stay and prove himself at the Spanish capital club, according to his agent.

    The latest reports in Spain, however, claim Real Madrid are prepared to exclude Sneijder from all first-team duties, should he refuse to leave the club and he has been left out of the squad that will take on Rosenborg for the Santiago Bernabeu trophy, which will he played on Monday.

    Sneijder, Rafael van der Vaart and Real Madrid's third 'keeper Adan have all been left out for the match, while Ruud van Nistelrooy and Lassana Diarra have been included in the squad.

    Miguel Torres, Christoph Metzelder and Sergio Ramos are all ineligible because of injury.
