    Liverpool star reveals January loan option

    Dutch international Ryan Babel has admitted that playing more minutes for Liverpool is a necessity if he is to secure a place in Holland’s squad for the 2010 World Cup and made clear that he is currently unhappy with his limited outings so far this term having only started against Tottenham Hotspur.

    The 22-year-old, who moved to Anfield in 2007 having seemed destined to join Arsenal, also revealed that he wouldn’t turn down a chance to return to former club Ajax if they offered him a loan move in January.

    "There have been a lot of things promised, but they haven't been followed up," Babel told De Telegraaf.

    "I would get to play for more minutes and have more chances, but after the first defeat, I only lost my place.

    "I think half a season at Ajax with a view to the World Cup wouldn't be a bad idea. I can add something to the Ajax squad.

    "Loaning me out was not an option (in the summer). Now we have agreed to review the situation in January.

    "I see the current situation as a signal that I really have to get playing."

    RYAN BABEL has accused Rafa Benitez of breaking promises which could wreck his World Cup dream.

    Liverpool's Dutch winger now wants to join Ajax on loan after claiming he was made a scapegoat for last month's 2-1 defeat at Spurs.

    Axed Babel, 22, said: "There were all sorts of assurances and promises from the club but they have not kept them. I should get more playing time but after the first defeat of the season I was the only one who was singled out - and I lost my place." 

    Babel, bought by Benitez for £11.5million from Ajax two years ago, has played just 14 minutes as a sub since that defeat.

    He lost his place in the Holland squad for Saturday's friendly against Japan and the midweek World Cup qualifier in Scotland - until Ibrahim Afellay's injury saw him swiftly recalled. 

    Babel added: "Being left out was a real shock and very worrying. I didn't expect not to be named, I'd already booked my ticket! We will have to review the situation again in January. 

    "But I think half a season back at Ajax, with a World Cup coming up next summer, wouldn't be a bad option at all. 

    "I see the current situation at Liverpool as a signal I have to be playing more."
