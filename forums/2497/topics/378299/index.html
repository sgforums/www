    JUVENTUS are preparing to deepen Liverpool's misery with a January raid for Javier Mascherano.

    Rafa Benitez's side are already six points behind Chelsea following their third defeat of the season at Stamford Bridge on Sunday.

    Now Italians Juve are planning a fresh bid for Argentine midfielder Mascherano, 24, who they tried to sign from West Ham before he moved to Anfield in January 2007.

    The Turin giants are ready to offer £23million as they seek to grab the Serie A title away from Inter Milan. And club officials plan to open talks with their Anfield opposite numbers in the coming weeks.

    Mascherano is already a target for Barcelona, with Liverpool rejecting an ]approach for him from the European champions during the summer.

    Mascherano, who possesses an Italian passport, is tempted to move away from Merseyside even though he is under contract until 2012.

    He is believed to be unhappy at the break-up of his partnership with Xabi Alonso, who joined Real Madrid in the summer.

    And he was dismayed at criticism for his performance in the 2-0 defeat at Chelsea.

    The Kop have got their own back on Chelsea by stealing Benfica wonderkid Toni Brito Sa from under their noses.

    Benfica have accepted Liverpool's offer of £290,000 for one of the most highly-rated youngsters in Portugal.

    Guinea-born Brito, 16, had a trial at Chelsea when Phil Scolari was boss and was ready to sign a six-year deal.

    Report: Javier Mascherano Wants Improved Liverpool Contract

    Javier Mascherano is on a collision course with Liverpool boss Rafael Benitez over stalled contract talks, according to The Daily Mirror.

     

    Javier Mascherano

     

    The Argentina midfielder is reportedly still unhappy over Benitez’s refusal to sell him to Barcelona in the summer. The situation has been further exacerbated by the club's subsequent refusal to hand him a pay rise that he felt was promised to him to ease his disappointment at missing out on a dream move to the Camp Nou.

    However, Mascherano's performances have not hit the heights that they did in seasons prior, and the Merseyside club are reluctant to offer the midfield enforcer an improved contract until he returns to the level of consistency that originally endeared him to the Kop.

    There was never any prospect of Benitez selling the 25-year-old once Xabi Alonso was allowed to join Real Madrid in a £32 million deal.  With the Spanish star gone, and new-boy Alberto Aquilani still yet to make his debut due to injury, Benitez has been extra-reliant on Mascherano to deliver the goods.

    The former River Plate, Corinthians and West Ham United player is currently earning around £60,000-a-week.
