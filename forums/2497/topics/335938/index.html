    Chelsea hit-man Nicolas Anelka would be absolutely delighted to partner Didier Drogba up front as part of a 4-4-2 system.

    France international Anelka has been at the top of his game this season, scoring decisive goals for his club and country, while Drogba remains sidelined through injury.

    The number 39 understands striker Drogba will be back in action in the near future, after damaging his knee in Cluj, and he would jump at the chance of playing alongside him.

    "Didier and I can play together but we have not done that since I have been here," said Anelka.

    "We have never started together up front. If we have started together then I was either on the right or left.

    "So it's a shame, because I wanted to play with him when I first signed with Chelsea, but I hope it will happen. I think it could work with the two of us. But it's the choice of the manager."
