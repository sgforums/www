    Fernando Torres insists Chelsea old boys are not up to speed

     

    Stepping up the pace: Fernando Torres believes Chelsea need to increase the speed of their playing style

     

     

     

    Fernando Torres has risked the wrath of his Chelsea team-mates after blaming his barren goalscoring run on the club's 'very slow' and 'older' players.

    The Spain striker has scored just one goal in 21 games since his £50million move from Liverpool in January. Manager Andre Villas-Boas insists he is willing to axe Torres from his starting line-up should the striker continue to misfire.

    Chelsea, who are at Sunderland on Saturday, have been labelled an ageing side in recent seasons, with key quintet Didier Drogba, John Terry, Florent Malouda, Ashley Cole and Frank Lampard all over 30.

    Torres has indicated the squad's lack of pace and the fact a number of players are approaching the twilight of their careers as a key reason behind his lack of goals, but has indicated Villas-Boas is trying to freshen things up.

    'When I changed club, I knew it was going to be a slow process, although I didn't expect it to be so long,' said the World Cup winner.

     

     

     

     

    Old boys: Didier Drogba, Ashley Cole and Frank Lampard are all over 30

     

     

    'Chelsea is, between the English teams, maybe the least English. That's because of the kind of player Chelsea has, an older player, who plays very slow, who has a lot of possession and that's what the club is trying to change.'

    Torres is confident the arrival of Juan Mata from Valencia and the return of the suspended Daniel Sturridge can help inject more urgency.

    He added: 'When the team start to get the ideas of the new coach, about paced and vertical football, it's going to be good.'
