Drogba happy to swap

  • turbo_drift
    3 Mar 07, 22:58
    Chelsea striker Didier Drogba would trade in his golden boot for Premiership silverware.

    The 28-goal striker is the leading candidate to be top goalscorer come May, but his personal hopes are in stark contrast to his club's chances of overhauling Manchester United at the league's summit.

    Jose Mourinho's side already trail United by nine points and it could be 12 if United emerge victorious at Liverpool on Saturday lunchtime.

    However Drogba has not given up all hope of catching United and admits he would be only too happy to trade in personal acclaim.

    "I may be the leading scorer but my greatest joy will come if we win the league again," said Drogba.

    "Our performances in the Champions League and cup competitions show our strength.

    "But in the Premiership there is still much to be desired.

    "We do not see the Premiership as being concluded because things could still go wrong.

    "I don't think Chelsea are outsiders as far as winning the league is concerned. The season is not yet over and changes can still take place as far as the leadership is involved.

    "I think we still have a pretty good chance - more than other contenders.

    "Everybody sings Manchester United's victories up at the moment but it is not over until it is really over."
