    Levy denies Jol exit talk
    Recent speculation dismissed as 'wholly inaccurate'

    Tottenham chairman Daniel Levy has dismissed reports that the club have agreed a compensation package to part company with under-pressure manager Martin Jol.

    Jol's position at White Hart Lane has been the subject of constant speculation after the club's disappointing start to the season following a further summer of investment in new players.

    It had been suggested that an emergency board meeting was held on Monday where a deal was struck for the Dutchman to leave his post.

    Undermined
    But Levy told the club's official website: "There has been no board meeting, let alone any emergency board meeting, and reports that we have agreed a compensation package with Martin are wholly inaccurate - we have not even discussed the subject and there is no reason to do so.

    "We will not allow ourselves to be side-tracked or undermined by external agendas or media hype. Our focus is on winning games.

    "For the record Damien (Comolli) was not in Barcelona this weekend, he was watching Southampton v Barnsley."

    Disregarded
    And a statement on the club's official website read: "Normally we only have to remind fans to ignore speculation during transfer windows, and we do that in the best interests of our playing staff and our supporters.

    "Given the level of speculation about Martin and his position at the club we feel it is necessary to do the same thing now.

    "Much of the reporting that has been carried since mid-August has been speculative and unfounded and accurately provided information has been disregarded."
