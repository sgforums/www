    SWITZERLAND set the WC alight with stunning victory over favourites Spain.

     

     

     

    SWITZERLAND set the World Cup alight with a stunning victory over favourites Spain.  

    After a lacklustre start to the tournament, striker Gelson Fernandes' scrambled winner yesterday produced one of the biggest shocks in finals history. 

    Spain boss Vicente Del Bosque said: "I am very upset, very upset. We tried not to lose our way of playing and win in the most orthodox way possible.

    "In the end we resorted to trying for a heroic comeback but couldn't do it.

    "They tried to stop our game and benefit from set-pieces and counter-attacks and they got their reward.

    "We tried to win but we couldn't. There's nothing else to say."

    Spain had 22 shots on target - more than any other team so far in the finals but could not find a way through.

    Torres came on as a late sub but looked sluggish and blew a number of openings.

    Xabi Alonso hit the bar after 70 minutes and Switzerland also struck a post through Eren Derdiyok.

    Swiss hero Fernandes, now at St Etienne after an unsuccessful spell at Manchester City, said: "I'm not used to scoring so I was a bit surprised.

    "It was a bit lucky. I don't think I realise right now what's happened. It's a very special moment for the country. Historic.

    "I'm just very happy and we just need to concentrate for the next game as it's going to be tough against Chile.

    "I think Spain are a very good team and they had most of the possession.

    "We ran a lot - it was very hard for us because technically and tactically they're a very strong side. But to win against Spain you need a bit of luck."

    SPAIN vowed to claw their way back into World Cup contention - even though history is against them.

     

    No team has ever won the trophy after losing their opening game.

     

    Following the Swiss shocker Spain boss Vicente del Bosque admitted: "It's not a good omen and it's not a good sign we have given to the rest of the teams and the football world.

    "But we have to face the next games and try to win. That should be our spirit and that's our future.

    Spain face Honduras in a must-win Group H game in Johannesburg on Monday and then meet Chile in another crunch clash four days later.

    The European champs are not used to losing - this was only their second defeat in 49 games.

    But Del Bosque added: "You can always lose in football, naturally, but I have a certain determination to turn it around. "We can't lower our heads and think we had bad luck. We have the chance to avenge ourselves. That is the way we have to be thinking.

    "We tried to find solutions from the wing and put in Fernando Torres as a second striker. We tried to get forward and create chances.

    "They defended well and blocked all our passes and crosses from the wings. They were very efficient."

    Switzerland have now kept five successive clean sheets in finals games - including Germany 06 - to equal Italy's all-time record set in 1990.

    And the Swiss hailed keeper Diego Benaglio for denying the Spanish.

    Boss Ottmar Hitzfeld, whose men play Chile on Monday, said: "Our match-winner was Benaglio, he had to be excellent if we were to win against Spain.

    "From the first minute he was focused, he was sensational in his positional play, he's world class in one-on-ones.

    "These were three unexpected points.

    "Chile will be just as difficult to play as Spain but my team will go into the match with a great deal of confidence."

    It is the first time the Swiss have won a World Cup opener since 1954.
