    Jose Mourinho is not good enough to manage Manchester United, says Bryan Robson

     

    SPECIAL ONE ... Jose Mourinho

     

     

    MANCHESTER UNITED legend Bryan Robson has poured cold water in the idea of Jose Mourinho taking over from Alex Ferguson.

    The Special One, currently in charge at Inter Milan, has enjoyed a friendly rivalry with Ferguson from his time at Porto and Chelsea.

    But should the 67-year-old decide to call time on his illustrious spell at Old Trafford, Robson believes Mourinho would not be an ideal replacement.

    He said: "I just don't agree when I hear people say that Jose is the perfect boss to take over from Sir Alex.

    "Jose is a great coach and the things he achieved at Porto and Chelsea mean he commands huge respect.

    "But Manchester United aren't just a club who only want to win things. The fans demand that success is achieved by playing the Manchester United way.

    "Attacking football is a vital part of the club's heritage. For me, Jose Mourinho is too cautious in his approach to the game to be manager of Manchester United.

    "The important thing for any manager who follows in Sir Alex Ferguson's footsteps is to recognise the traditions of Manchester United and maintain them."
