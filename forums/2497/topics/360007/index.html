    Chelsea owner Abramovich ready for Man City cash battle over Adebayor

    Chelsea owner Roman Abramovich is ready to outbid Manchester City for Arsenal striker Emmanuel Adebayor.

    Gunners boss Arsene Wenger opened the door to selling Adebayor at a fans Q&A last night.

    “I don’t want to go into any individual assessment of who we want to keep and who not, at the moment, and normally Adebayor is part of our plans for the future,” he said.

    “And, if I feel that a player should go, or could go, why not sell him to one of the other top four?

    “It’s unusual but we sold Ashley Cole to Chelsea and we bought William Gallas, so you can never say I never do it, because I’ve already done it.”

    Wenger also admitted a deal to sell Adebayor for £30m to Milan almost happened last summer. Instead, he stayed and signed a new, £80,000-a-week contract.

    “I believe he has been disturbed at the start by a little bit of bad feeling from the fans because he was close to leaving the club, and it unsettled him a little bit,” he said.

    Wenger also hinted the whole story about what happened with Adebayor a year ago had yet to come out.

    “I cannot tell you all the truth about what happened behind the scenes,” he said.

    A source close to Abramovich admitted last night to the Daily Star: “If Wenger is ready to sell, Roman will go for him.

    “But if Arsenal ask too much, he will not pay it.”
