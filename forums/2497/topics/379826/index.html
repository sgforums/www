    MASCHERANO NOT INTERESTED IN NEW DEAL

    More question marks over Argentinian's future

     

     

    JAVIER MASCHERANO has told Liverpool he's not interested in a new contract. 

    In a move which raises further doubts over the midfielder's long-term future at the club, he dismissed claims money was the root of his wish to join Barcelona last summer. 

    Liverpool boss Rafa Benitez is desperate to thrash out fresh terms with the midfielder to prevent a renewed bid from the Spanish giants. 

    He rejected their £30m swoop after Mascherano stunned The Kop and asked to leave in June. 

    Anfield officials were convinced he was angling for a £90k-a-week deal, but Mascherano hit back. He said: "This is not true. 

    "I have never asked for a new contract. I already have a contract with Liverpool. I don't play for the money. 

    "I play for me and to win things. Money is not important to me. 

    "Obviously I will not play for free but why should I?"
