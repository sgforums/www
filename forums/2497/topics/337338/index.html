    Arsenal manager Arsene Wenger has revealed that defender Bacary Sagna will be sidelined until December due to an ankle injury.

    The France international was stretchered off in the 2-0 home defeat to Aston Villa on Saturday and Wenger has confirmed he is set for a spell on the sidelines.

    "Yes, it is serious," said Wenger on the club's official website. "He will be out for a few weeks, he has an injured ankle."

    Sagna will miss France's friendly against Uruguay in Paris on Wednesday and should also be absent for Arsenal's Premier League trips to Manchester City and Chelsea.

    The 25-year-old could also miss the Carling Cup quarter-final against Burnley on 2nd December.
