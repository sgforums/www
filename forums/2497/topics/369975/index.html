    Liverpool ace Gerrard: I still have dreams

    Liverpool skipper Steven Gerrard says there's still plenty he wants to accomplish in the game.

    He told the Daily Star Sunday: “I still have many more dreams.

    “That means winning the Premier League because that is the only club medal that is missing from my own personal collection.

    “And it would be nice to go to a tournament with England and be successful so that means the World Cup in South Africa next summer for me.

    “I want to go one better at least than a quarter-final which is the furthest so far that I have gone in a tournament with England.

    “It’s the last four and the final that I am now aiming for.”

    Gerrard added: “I want another success in the Champions League too.

    “People could see my reaction when I lifted that trophy in 2005 – how much it meant to me, my team-mates and the supporters.

    “That was the best night of my career and I want to match it and experience that success again.”

    Liverpool skipper Gerrard sees coaching future

    Liverpool skipper Steven Gerrard has hinted he sees a future in club management.

    Gerrard said: “Now I am thinking about what I would do at the end of my career – I’m not getting any younger.

    “It would be nice to stay involved in the game. I’m not sure at what level, either as a manager, or a coach or in the media.

    “But I want to stay involved because football is so important to me.”
