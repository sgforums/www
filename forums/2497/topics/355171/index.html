    AC Milan star Kaka is ready to join Florentino Perez's Real Madrid revolution.

    AS says Kaka is willing to join Perez's presidential ticket after being offered terms worth a clear £2.5 million-a-year more than what he is currently on at Milan.

    Kaka will earn £8 million-a-year - after tax - at Real, with his father and agent Bosco Leite having negotiated the deal with Perez.

    For now, Kaka will remain silent on the matter, though a fracture in his relationship with the Rossoneri became clear after criticising the club's medical staff over his injury problems last week in Brazil.

    Kaka would prefer Premier League switch

    Kaka has suggested he would prefer to move to The Premier League than join Real Madrid if he leaves Milan.

    The Brazil star has been strongly linked with a move to the Bernabeu in recent days, with the Spanish press claiming that Madrid presidential candidate Florentino Perez has agreed a deal to take the player to Spain.

    However, Milan vice-president Adriano Galliani insisted this week that the player is not for sale and rubbished the reports he had already agreed a deal.

    He told La Gazzetta dello Sport: “Kaka cannot sign with Perez because he is not Real’s president, as far as I am aware.”

    Kaka, though, has never hidden his interest in The Premier League and, although he rejected a big-money move to Manchester City in January, it appears he is still open to arriving in England.

    "I like The Premier League a lot, so maybe that would be my choice if I leave Milan,” he said in The Daily Star.

    "Whenever I can, I try to swap experiences with my friends playing in other countries and leagues.

    "All the players that I've talked to that play in The Premier League, or used to play there, say it's a tough league - tough, very fast and competitive."

    Chelsea have been strongly linked with a move for Milan coach Carlo Ancelotti this week, leading to suggestions they could reignite their interest in Kaka.
