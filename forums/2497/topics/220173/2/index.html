    Pique wants Zaragoza stay

    Zaragoza centre half Gerard Pique would consider staying at the club beyond his one-year loan deal.

    Pique, 19, contracted to Manchester United, has now cemented his place at the heart of Zaragozas defence and helped them climb to third in the Liga table.

    "I have sometimes thought that I would like to stay here for another year," he said.

    "I am very happy in both my personal life and sporting one. However, there is no hurry in making a decision. There is plenty of time to speak about my future from January, and I have not ruled anything out."

    Pique revealed that United manager Sir Alex Ferguson has been in contact to ask how he is getting on in Spain.

    "[Ferguson] called me after the match with Villarreal, in which I started and scored a goal. I told him I was happy here and thanked him for giving me the opportunity to play in Zaragoza."

    For now, the player - who has scored two goals this season - is happy to help the club in its endeavours.

    "I am happy with my work. The most important thing is that the team continue to win and that we climb the table."

    Dhorasoo thinking over Bolton option

    Vikash Dhorasoo is pondering his next move ahead of Januarys transfer window with Bolton Wanderers still an option open to the former Paris Saint Germain man.

    The experienced midfielder was sacked by PSG after a very public fall-out with the management.

    Agent Alain Migliaccio has told France Football that Bolton are one of a number of clubs pursuing his client, but obviously nothing can happen until the New Year.

    "Bolton are still attracted, as are two other English sides," Migliaccio stated.

    "But I know the English clubs well. Even if they show an interest, that doesnt mean they will make an offer to the player. We have contacts with several clubs. The only trouble is he cant get a contract with English, Spanish or Italian clubs before January. So we will take stock at the beginning of the winter window. But Vikash will continue playing, thats for sure."
