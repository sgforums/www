    CHELSEA TO APPEAL DROGBA DECISION

     

    DISGUST: Drogba given six-match ban

    FURIOUS Chelsea are poised to launch an appeal with UEFA, after Didier Drogba was hit by a draconian six-match ban for his antics after Chelsea’s Champions League semi-final defeat by Barcelona.

     

    Although the final two games of the ban are suspended, Drogba is set to miss most of Chelsea’s group games next season and will not be available for new coach Carlo Ancelotti until November.

    With Drogba also set to be absent for at least a month early next year on African Nations Cup duty, it means Ancelotti could effectively miss one of his main strikers for three months of the key competitions.

    Drogba ran on to the pitch at the end of Chelsea’s defeat by Barcelona at Stamford Bridge, haranguing Norwegian referee Tom Henning Ovrebo, swearing into television cameras, then carrying on the confrontation with the official in the tunnel.

    Chelsea, themselves hit by an £85,000 fine for failing to control their players- with full-back Jose Bosingwa handed a four-game UEFA ban, one suspended, for calling Ovrebo a “thief” – were left stunned by the punishments and look likely to appeal today.

     

    DROGBA: Set to miss most of Chelsea's group games next season 

     

    Chelsea had hoped the punishments, handed out by Uefa’s Control and Disciplinary body after a hearing in Nyon, would be tempered by the fact both players apologised immediately and also admitted

    their culpability. But last night it appeared those actions had carried little weight with Uefa.

    A statement by the European governing body said: “Drogba and Bosingwa were facing charges of being in breach of the principles of sportsmanship by insulting the referee by making offensive comments.”

    Chelsea were fined not only for failing to control their players - midfielder Michael Ballack also confronted Ovrebo on the field but escaped individual punishment - but for the throwing of missiles by their enraged fans at the final whistle.

    The club and players have three days to appeal, after receiving written explanations for the verdicts.

    A Chelsea spokesman said: ‘We’ve received the UEFA verdict and we’ll now take time to assess it in full and consider our response.”

    Chelsea were fuming at Ovrebo’s performance in the second leg of the semi-final, as he turned down four potential penalties, and Barcelona went through courtesy of Andres Iniesta’s injury-time goal.

    Drogba’s fine and his absence from so many games next season is not expected to affect his status at the club. He is expected to sign a new three-year contract after talks in the coming weeks.

    TERRY HITS OUT AT EURO BANS

    .
    .


    Chelsea captain John Terry has criticised the severity of the punishments handed out to Didier Drogba and Jose Bosingwa for their Champions League semi-final tirades.

    The pair were banned by UEFA for, respectively, four and three European matches - with extra games suspended - this week for their part in the controversy which followed the Londoners' second-leg draw with Barcelona at Stamford Bridge.

    A 1-1 scoreline sent the Blues crashing out to the eventual champions, but the Catalans' injury-time equaliser came after referee Tom Henning Ovrebo had turned down several strong penalty appeals from the English side.

    Drogba, in particular, caused controversy with his post-match reaction, when he swore while branding the Norwegian official a "disgrace" on live television.

    He apologised a day later, however, and Terry believes he deserved softer treatment from European football's governing body.

    "What happened with Didier and the comments made after the game took the hype away from the referee a little bit," the England skipper told talkSPORT.

    "It was a very bad performance from him [Ovrebo], we all realised that. There were three or four clear penalty decisions that we should have had that night and that's something as a fan and as a player you will never forget.

    "It was disappointing.

    I would like the people in charge to take these things in to perspective and realise that after the game Didier came out and apologised for his actions.

    "I think he realises that kids were watching and he deserves a ban. But to that extent? I really don't think so. Maybe a two-match ban."

    Bosingwa was punished for calling Ovrebo "a thief" in a post-match interview, and again Terry does not believe the right-back's misdemeanour warranted such a tough penalty.

    "Jose Bosingwa's ban, I don't know where that's come from," said Terry. "Obviously every player was feeling what Bosingwa felt and thankfully nobody else said what he said. But he said what every fan and every player was thinking.

    "Hopefully Chelsea can appeal and we can get that [the ban] down a little bit because we want those two players for the campaign next year."
