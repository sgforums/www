    Gunners caught with their trousers down... on and off the pitch

    Brief show: Arsenal striker Nicklas Bendtner in disarray hours after his side's European exit.

     

    As his shell-shocked players threw their socks and shorts towards the kit skip, Arsene Wenger told them to keep their dignity for the last three games of the season.

    The Golden Generation, as Wenger had labelled his young team before they beat Roma in February, had shown their immaturity in both legs of the Champions League semi-final, having their pants pulled down at the Emirates by the best team on the planet.

    Hours later one of their players, Nicklas Bendtner, was falling out of a London nightclub, trousers round his ankles, with only a pair of black jockey briefs keeping his dignity intact.

    Bendtner's behaviour at Boujis is embarrassing for Arsenal, especially at a time when the supporters are beginning to question whether this group are capable of fulfilling their potential.

    Some players are even beginning to doubt their quality, with Emmanuel Adebayor suggesting that the team who finished four points behind Manchester United in the Barclays Premier League last season have gone backwards.

    He claimed that United are unstoppable and almost, almost, criticised Wenger over the sale of Alexander Hleb to Barcelona and the enforced departure of Mathieu Flamini.

    Backwards step: Emmanuel Adebayor believes Arsenal must invest this summer to move forward

     

    'We have taken a step backwards - not only me, but the team,' admitted Adebayor. 'We need some players. Hleb went, Flamini went to Milan and it has been quite difficult to play.'

    There is no temptation inside the manager's office at London Colney to start again, little appetite to be ruthless and rip up the template of teenagers that he believes will eventually dominate the Premier League, the Champions League and beyond.

    It is incredible to think that Arsenal, three times Premier League winners under Wenger, are slipping so far behind United they cannot give them a game over 180 minutes of football at the very highest level.

    They beat United 2-1 at the Emirates in November, but the world champions come to life towards the end of the season. As the world's best player crowed, without challenge: 'This time of the season is Cristiano time.'

    Even the captain Cesc Fabregas was caught up in the maelstrom, getting just 79 touches when he averages 122. 'United have many players who can make a difference, Chelsea are the same,' said Fabregas.

    'United were much better over 180 minutes and deserve to be in the final. We haven't been good enough.'

    Bendtner, meanwhile, apologised last night for his post-match behaviour, saying: 'I may be young but my actions were a poor error of judgment. I love this club and was so disappointed to lose against United.'
