    Alan Shearer insists Newcastle United will go for the win against Chelsea this weekend.

    "I am not having the nonsense that we are forgiven for getting nothing against Chelsea, because it is Chelsea, and then we move on to our next match at Stoke City," Shearer said.

    "We are two points adrift so if we don't win on Saturday and other teams do it could be five, so we have to get something.

    "I understand why the fans are outside and I understand why the press are here, but we have to get away from this Alan Shearer thing. It is not about me and I will be trying as hard as I can to emphasise that.

    "Yes, I will go out and see the fans and sign autographs and speak to them, but we cannot forget we have a job to do. The bonus for me is keeping this club in the Premier League, nothing more."

    Drogba blow for Shearer showdown

    Chelsea boss Guus Hiddink has revealed Didier Drogba is injured and will not face Alan Shearer’s Newcastle this weekend.

    The Ivorian damaged an ankle in bizarre circumstances during training but this blow has been offset by the news that fellow striker Nicolas Anelka will play following a bruised toe which sidelined the hit-man for France's most recent World Cup qualifiers.

    “Drogba did an exercise yesterday [Thursday] where he got injured and hopefully he will be available for next Wednesday [Champions League],” Hiddink said.

    “We did some shooting practice and he then hit the ground with his toe and this hurt his ankle.

    “Anelka has been practicing the last few days and he has been getting better and better so he is in.”

    The decision to appoint Shearer as temporary manager at Newcastle has been welcomed by Hiddink with the Dutchman believing the former England striker is in a no-lose situation.

    “You have to respect the decision by whoever is in charge. There is always the reaction when something is happening in the club, it was the same a little bit here,” added Hiddink.

    “You make some decisions and you get a reaction within the club and the team.

    “Everyone knows their situation, so of course there will be a reaction in the team and from the crowd. So we can expect a tight game and a fighting spirit.

    “I respect him [Shearer] very much because I know him as a player - he is quite a personality. I cannot judge if he will stay to the end of the season or longer.

    "It is always good to have big players making the transfer to managing."

    GUUS HIDDINK has told Alan Shearer: Your fame means nothing. 

    Newcastle legend Shearer faces Chelsea today in his first game as a boss following a glittering playing career. But Blues manager Hiddink warned: “Top, top players think because of their talent everything is going to happen automatically.

    “It’s not always that when you are a top, top player you are guaranteed to be a top manager as well. “Most of the time the players who have not reached the top, top level can sometimes imagine a little bit more what the difficulties are.” 

    Hiddink is expecting St James’ Park to be pumped up with the Toon’s new ‘Messiah’ in the dugout and battling to keep the club in the top flight. But Hiddink believes Newcastle may need more than an adrenalin rush to spark their survival bid. 

    He said: “It depends on your religion whether or not you believe in Messiahs. “At kick-off he will not play and I will not play. We can prepare as we have done but we both have to sit down. It’s not a game between managers. 

    “The instant input and change in the dressing room gives the players a lot of energy and that’s an extra aspect of the game we have to deal with. "But besides this game, there are other things for managers to get interested in...

    “How to prepare a team, how to make training sessions with aims and objectives. “Training is not just for passing the time or having a nice workout. 

    “Every day we aim for a strategic, physical or tactical aspect of the game. “That’s the part where new managers have to broaden their knowledge of the game. “I have the benefit of experience.” 

    Hiddink added: “I think Alan Shearer’s team will reflect his personality.”
