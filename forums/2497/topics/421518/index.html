    ARSENE WENGER is not in favour of managers receiving any percentages of transfer deals.

    Earlier this week, SunSport revealed Blackpool boss Ian Holloway will trouser a slice of the profit should the Seasiders cash in on star midfielder Charlie Adam.

    In his time, Emirates chief Wenger has boosted the club's coffers with huge profits on players like Nicolas Anelka, Marc Overmars and Patrick Vieira.

    But he said: "It is down to the chairman and to the board. Personally I am not in favour of it but if the chairman wants that and if the club agrees... I always tried to keep my contracts not involved in these kind of clauses as it can be misinterpreted - and I never wanted that."

    Aston Villa boss Gerard Houllier was 'surprised' to hear Holloway stands to cash in on any Adam deal.

    He has had a war of words with Ollie over a failed bid for the playmaker.

    And he said: "I don't know if it's legal or illegal.

    "It is surprising but it is their business, not mine."
