    Man Utd to spend Benzema cash on Aguero

    Manchester United boss Sir Alex Ferguson is ready to get serious about a deal for Atletico Madrid ace Sergio Aguero.

    The Daily Star Sunday says Argentinian Aguero is seen as the ideal replacement for Carlos Tevez having formed a potent partnership with former United striker Diego Forlan in La Liga.

    The son-in-law of Argentina manager Diego Maradona, Aguero has admirers throughout Europe and so far Atletico Madrid have been fending off all inquiries by quoting a £50million asking price.

    United, and other clubs like AC Milan and Barcelona, believe that is too high but Fergie is weighing up an offer of around £35m – the amount they were ready to pay for Lyon’s Karim Benzema before Real Madrid raised the stakes.

    Valencia wing ace Silva now Man Utd's marquee target

    Valencia winger David Silva is looming as the big-name deal Manchester United boss Sir Alex Ferguson wants to close before the summer market shuts.

    The Manchester Evening News says the United boss tried and failed to sign the Valencia star last summer, but hopes to make him his marquee capture following the departures of Cristiano Ronaldo and Carlos Tevez.

    Silva, however, is seen as a more than useful recruit with United still in the hunt for a world class left-sided midfielder.

    Ferguson has yet to effectively replace Ryan Giggs since the Welshman was converted from a left winger to a central midfielder.

    Nani has failed to make the position his own, while Ji-Sung Park is not a natural winger.

    Zoran Tosic is still seen as too fragile to cope with the rigours of a full Premier League season and new signing Gabriel Obertan is also one for the future.

    Ferguson is a long time admirer of Silva, having been knocked back in his attempt to bring the 23-year-old to Old Trafford last year.
