    Kop legend: His days have got to be numbered at Liverpool...

     

     

     

    RONNIE WHELAN last night became the first Liverpool legend to stick the knife into Kop boss Rafa Benitez and warned: "His days are numbered."

    Whelan savaged Benitez for taking off Fernando Torres in Saturday's 3-1 defeat at Fulham to protect him for Wednesday's crunch Champions League tie at Lyon.

    Former Republic of Ireland midfielder Whelan, 48, who won 12 major trophies in a glory-filled career, said: "It's all gone wrong with the manager and he's shown exactly where his priorities lie.

    "He wants to win the European Cup so he can get a job in Europe. For me, his days have got to be numbered at Liverpool.

    "Why after such a great result against Manchester United, do you want to take a huge step backwards after losing to Arsenal in the Carling Cup as well? I don't see where he's coming from."

    Whelan, speaking on Ireland's RTE Television, added: "He's taken players off who are the only players who are going to give you a chance of winning the game. "And he drags them all off because he's got a game on Wednesday.

    "When I saw the team I thought he's not bothered about this game. He's putting all his eggs in one basket and I can't see why he's done it." Benitez took Torres off after an hour with the teams level at 1-1.

    Liverpool went on to lose disastrously and finished the game with nine men after Philipp Degen and Jamie Carragher saw red.

    It was Liverpool's eighth loss this season and their sixth in seven games. They are nine points behind leaders Chelsea and, with their Premier League hopes all but dead, defeat at Lyon on Wednesday could spell the end for Spaniard Benitez.

    Lyon will go into Wednesday's clash in high spirits after a late Bafetimbi Gomis goal gave them a 1-0 derby win against St Etienne.

    LYON's DEN.. Rafa faces crucial clash..

     

    RAFA BENITEZ summed up his future in one sentence.

    When I asked how long before he reconsidered his future, the Liverpool boss replied: "My future is against Lyon."

    After the 3-1 defeat at Fulham, their eighth loss this season, the Wednesday clash with Lyon is make-or-break for Benitez who has major doubts over Fernando Torres and Steven Gerrard.

    Lose in France in the Champions League and Benitez will be left without any vestige of excuse to masquerade a disastrous campaign that has seen his lousy Liverpool lose half their matches.

    Rarely has so much rested on a game so early in a campaign. So the significance of the Lyon result is impossible to underestimate, particularly as rivals Fiorentina face Group E whipping boys Debreceni.

    Liverpool already trail leaders Lyon by six points and Fiorentina by three. Another defeat means they cannot catch Lyon, while the Italians would be all but out of sight.

    Benitez said: "The most important thing for me is to know how many players we have available and start preparing a team."

    Stand-in skipper Jamie Carragher said: "These are tough times and it hurts a lot.

    "This is un-Liverpool like. But it is not just the fans who are disappointed, it is the players, the staff and the manager.

    "We have got to get back on track with Lyon, which is another massive game.

    "We made up for a few things last week by beating Manchester United. Then we have had two defeats since.

    "There is only us on the pitch that can turn it around and there will be a lot of talk about this result again.

    "We have got to sort this out with the management and staff. We can't afford to be too down-hearted for too long because we've got to get ready for the next game."

    The bookmakers have already given their verdict - Liverpool are 9-1 favourites to win the EUROPA LEAGUE. Ladbrokes offer 7-2 that Benitez will not even last the month.Inspirational skipper Gerrard is still a doubt for Lyon after missing the Fulham defeat with a groin problem.

    Torres was subbed on the hour at Craven Cottage despite not suffering any ill-effects.

    But Benitez still insisted the Spanish star needed four days to recover after playing 80 minutes against United and was a doubt.

    He added: "Maybe David Ngog will be available. Gerrard could be. We'll have to see about Martin Skrtel and Daniel Agger.

    "We don't know about Torres. The last time it was four days to be ready again.

    "But that's the problem we have now with injuries, you cannot play without Gerrard, without Torres, without Johnson, without Albert Riera, without a lot of players in too many games."

    Benitez defended his decision to include Torres in the starting line-up and then haul him off to introduce Ryan Babel after 63 minutes even though the game was at 1-1.

    He said: "We needed to do something because always you think about protecting the player. In this case we were more or less in control of the game.

    "But we had some chances in attack with Andriy Voronin, Dirk Kuyt, Yossi Benayoun and Ryan Babel so to keep the player on the pitch could be a problem - maybe you lose the player for one month.

    "We were analysing before the game what to do. So we decided to play him. That was the most difficult decision. And after you have to manage during the game.

    "The question is, if you don't play Torres you'll be talking about why he is not playing so it's a difficult decision.

    "This league will be difficult but you can see that all the teams can lose and lose points."

    Having already lost to both Fiorentina and Lyon, the Reds are faced with disaster should they capitulate again.

    Benitez has in the past pulled Liverpool back from the brink of Euro elimination, famously against Olympiakos in 2004 when Liverpool scored three second-half goals and went on to win the trophy in Istanbul.

    The Kop boss added: "Hopefully we have experience and confidence and can do it."

    He also refused to write off his side's title hopes. He added: "We have to win against Birmingham. That is the priority."
