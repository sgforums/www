Inzaghi Extends With Milan

    Milan striker Filippo Inzaghi has extended his current deal for another year. He will now play on until 2010, the club have announced.

    Inzaghi's current deal was set to expire at the end of the season where he would have been free to find another club in January.

    However, the Rossoneri have wasted no time in securing the player's services for another year and they have confirmed Inzaghi has put pen to paper.

    A statement on the club's official website reads:

    "AC Milan would like to announce the contract extension of Filippo Inzaghi until June 2010."

    The deal means Inzaghi will have played at the club for nine years by the time his contract ends.

    Superpippo signed for Milan in 2001 from Juventus and he has enjoyed great success at San Siro winning the Champions League twice as well as the European Supercup once, a World Club Cup, a Scudetto in 2004 and the Coppa Italia in 2003.

    The prolific hit-man is likely to be involved in action against Sporting Braga tomorrow night in the UEFA Cup and he will be hoping to celebrate his latest deal with a goal.
