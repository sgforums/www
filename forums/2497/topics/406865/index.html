    SAY YOU'LL STAY, FABREGAS

    Wenger plea to skipper

    WENGER: Wants Fabregas to say he's staying

    ARSENE WENGER has told Cesc Fabregas he must publicly commit his future to Arsenal this week. 

    Fabregas will return to training on Thursday after a summer of speculation about a move to Barcelona and the Arsenal boss says it is now down to his captain to kill the story once and for all. 

    Wenger, who also warned Barcelona skipper Carles Puyol to keep his mouth shut, said: "The story in the last six months has made our lives difficult and I believe what is important is that when Cesc comes back, he comes out and speaks. 

    "Only Fabregas can clear the air when he comes back from holiday. The noises come back a lot from Spain and are translated here, and I believe it is important for the Premier League that all the clubs keep their best players. 

    "Puyol has to keep to his own affairs and leave the rest to Cesc Fabregas. He has five years left on his contract and that's it." 

    Fabregas broke off from his holiday in Mykonos to fly back for talks with Wenger in London on Friday. And his Arsenal team-mates are hoping their skipper and talisman will say he is staying at the Emirates - at least for another year.

    Bacary Sagna said: "I know he is on holiday but he will be back soon.

    "I do not know what he is going to do but I hope he will stay because he is a great player and we need him."

    New signing Laurent Koscielny said: "He's a big player so of course we want him to stay."

    Johan Djourou added: "Cesc is very important. So is Robin van Persie. These players are vital to us. They have proved themselves over the year.

    "Cesc is a great player and the captain, so it would be good if we could keep him.

    "I hope he stays. It is not our business, it is the business of the football club, but obviously we want him to stay."
