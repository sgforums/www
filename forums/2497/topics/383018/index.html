    FC Twente keen on Manchester United winger Zoran Tosic - report


    According to a report in Dutch football magazine Voetbal International, Eredivisie table-toppers FC Twente are considering a loan move for Manchester United youngster Zoran Tosic.

    The Serbian international joined the Red Devils from Partizan Belgrade in January 2009, but has found first team chances limited.

    The 22-year-old could be tempted to leave the English champions on a temporary basis in order to get regular first team action ahead of the 2010 World Cup.

    Twente coach Steve McClaren is reportedly eager to add the promising winger to his ranks in January as he wants to bolster his side in preparation for the race for the Eredivisie title.

    The former England gaffer reportedly wants to drop Kenneth Perez from his starting XI in order to make way for Bryan Ruiz and Tosic would be the replacement for the latter on the right wing.

    Tosic agent insists on Man Utd stay

    The agent of Zoran Tosic insists there's no plans to leave Manchester United in January.

    Tosic has been linked with a loan move to Steve McClaren's FC Twente.

    But Zoran Pavlovic told skysports.com: "Zoran is well in Manchester and he is staying at Manchester United.

    "I have had no contact with FC Twente and Zoran is very happy at Manchester.

    "He has three years left on his contract and he wants to fight for his place.

    "His first option is to stay in Manchester."
