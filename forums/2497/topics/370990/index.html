    AL OF A MOVE ... Aquilani could replace Alonso

     

     

    LIVERPOOL will splash out £20million to land Roma midfielder Alberto Aquilani - but only when Xabi Alonso finally joins Real Madrid. 

    The Reds have agreed a deal in principle for the Italian, 25, who has won 11 full caps and turned down Arsenal and Chelsea when he was 16. 

    Liverpool are demanding £30m for Alonso plus a series of add-ons worth up to £5m more - yet Real are adamant they will not go that high. 

    Anfield managing director Christian Purslow met Real official Jose Angel Sanchez in Barcelona before Sunday's friendly against Espanyol. 

    Purslow admitted: "There has been no agreement. Talks have been very friendly but we need to continue talking." 

    Alonso, 27, is finally expected to move to Spain by the end of the week. 

    But big-spending Real have hinted they will walk away if the price does not come down, with Standard Liege star Steven Defour the next target on their shopping list.

    Liverpool Close In On Juventus Midfielder Christian Poulsen - Report

    With Xabi Alonso set to leave Anfield, Rafa Benitez seems to have turned his gaze to Italy in search of a replacement...

     

    Liverpool today officially confirmed that they had reached agreement with Real Madrid to sell Xabi Alonso to the Spanish giants, and their hunt for a replacement for the Basque midfielder appears to be gathering pace this afternoon, as Sky Sports News reports that the Reds have contacted Juventus regarding a possible move for Danish international Christian Poulsen.

    Earlier reports in Italy had suggested that a deal had already been agreed between Liverpool and Roma for Alberto Aquilani, but the rumours concerning Poulsen have now begun to gather momentum.

    Poulsen moved from Spanish outfit Sevilla to Juve last year for £9 million, but was recently told by the Italian side that he can leave.

    Fulham and Everton have also been identified in media reports as admirers of the 29-year-old, but it now looks, according to Sky, that Rafa Benitez has become the favourite to land the Dane.
