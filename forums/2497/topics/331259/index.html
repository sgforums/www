Rafa Fumes Over Gerrard's 'Offside' Strike

  • omgukilledkenny
    21 Sep 08, 01:50

    After seeing Steven Gerrard denied his 100th goal for Liverpool, his manager Rafa Benitez was left confused over the decision to disallow the goal.

    Referee Andre Marriner judged Dirk Kuyt to be offside when Gerrard swung in a second minute free-kick in the clash against Stoke City at Anfield.

    The attempted cross found the far corner without a touch, but despite replays proving otherwise, Marriner ruled the Dutchman as offside.

    It would have given Gerrard his century of goals for the club, after hitting 98 and 99 in midweek against Marseille.

    Benitez was unhappy with the decision and let his feelings be known in the aftermatch of the 0-0 draw.

    He said: "The disallowed goal was a key decision. If that had been allowed it would have been a much more open game.

    "I have no idea why the goal was disallowed. The referee told Jamie Carragher that it had been his decision, not the linesman's.

    "But it was a massive mistake. And nobody knows why the effort was ruled out."

    Stoke battled hard and were well worth their point but Benitez was disappointed that his side's possession on the ball cold not have been turned into goals:

    He continued: "The players worked very hard. They tried everything, but maybe we needed a little more ability in the last third to make use of all that possession and all those shots."
