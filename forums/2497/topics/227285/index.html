    Terry facing FA rap



    John Terry will become the first England captain to be found guilty of improper conduct by the Football Association.

    Terry was keen to defend himself when he appeared before an FA disciplinary commission today after being charged in the wake of comments he made about referee Graham Poll after being send off against Tottenham in November. He was urged to admit to the charge in the hope of avoiding a ban. He will now not attend the personal hearing, having instead made a written submission that will be examined by the disciplinary commission today.

    Terry was accused of questioning the integrity of Poll in an interview with Chelsea's own television channel. In a statement that was subsequently issued, the FA said: "Terry claimed in an interview, which was widely reported, that Poll had given him conflicting reasons for his second yellow card, saying: 'On the pitch, Graham Poll said to me that it was for the barge on Hossam Ghaly where I just kept running'.

    "'Then, after the game, he then said to me it was for the fall when me and Ledley King fell so, you know, he's obviously had a look at it, or got people to look at it and decided that's probably the best option for him and it covers every angle for him.'"

    In his written submission Terry has said it was never his intention to question Poll's integrity. But he does concede that his words could have been chosen more wisely and he will now wait to see what the disciplinary commission decides.

    The FA will certainly be relieved that they do not have to arbitrate between the England captain and one of English football's most senior referees when Terry has essentially been accused of lying.
