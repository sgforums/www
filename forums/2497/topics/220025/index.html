    McClaren disappointed not to beat Holland

    A frustrated Steve McClaren said his England side deserved to beat the Netherlands after they conceded a late equaliser in a 1-1 friendly draw on Wednesday.

    McClarens men were heading for a much-needed victory after taking a 37th-minute lead through striker Wayne Rooney, only for midfielder Rafael van der Vaart to level in the 86th minute.

    "Were all disappointed that we didnt win the game. We deserved to, there were half a dozen good chances apart from the goal," McClaren told a news conference.

    "But I asked for a performance, everyone wanted to see that, and thats what we got... on another day wed have scored more and won the game comfortably."

    A decent showing was considered a minimum requirement for England after a 2-0 defeat in Croatia and a 0-0 draw with Macedonia has left them third in Euro 2008 qualifying Group E.

    Pleased with the way his men handled the novel 4-3-3 formation designed to echo the Dutch line-up, McClaren said: "The system nullified them.

    "It worked, the players did well to have the organisation and discipline to carry it out."

    The one blot on Englands copy book was to concede to a set-piece. Englands defence failed to intercept Arjen Robbens long throw-in and substitute Jan Vennegoor of Hesselink was able to head the ball back for Van der Vaart to rifle home.

    "I wouldnt have thought that we would have conceded from a long throw and thats the one disappointment from the night," McClaren said.

    "But its not something thats cost us points and its something we will look at. Obviously, we dont like conceding goals and we have to find out reasons."

    Dutch coach Marco van Basten, who was missing some key players at the Amsterdam Arena and whose full-strength side are top of qualifying Group G, had no real complaints.

    "Im satisfied with a fair result against an England side at full strength, while we missed players like Robin van Persie, Edwin van der Sar and Wesley Sneijder, who all three bring something extra," he said.

    His team lacked their usual attacking threat without striker Van Persie, with Liverpool forward Dirk Kuyt struggling against his Premier League rivals at the back for England.

    "Kuyt played an unfortunate match against a very experienced pair like (Rio) Ferdinand and (John) Terry," Van Basten said. "You have to realise you will not get past them easily."
