    Mourinho could be in trouble again

     

     

     

    Inter Milan coach Jose Mourinho could be in hot water again after he was spotted mouthing instructions to his assistant.

    The Special One, who has earned a repuatation as a trouble maker, is already serving a touchline ban for an offensive gesture aimed at the referee during Inter Milan's match against Sampdoria in February.

    The controversial coach crossed his wrists in an apparent handcuff gesture which brought the wrath of the Italian referees down upon him. He was handed a three match touchline ban and fined €40,000.

    However, Mourinho was spotted mouthing tactical instructions to assistant coach Giuseppe Baresi during Inter's match against Genoa - something that is not allowed while serving a ban.

    According to reports in Italian sports publication Tuttosport - judge Gianpaolo Tosel could give Mourinho yet another touchline ban.

    Trouble is nothing new for Mourinho who has been involved in numerous collisions with the football authorities - most notoriously when he accused the referee of conspiring with opposition coach Frank Rijkaard during Barcelona's 2005 champions league clash with his then team Chelsea.
