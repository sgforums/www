    Sir Alex Ferguson: Dimitar Berbatov Will Be With Manchester United Next Year

    Bulgarian has failed to impress in his first two seasons at United.

    Dimitar Berbatov, Manchester United (Getty Images)

    Manchester United manager Sir Alex Ferguson refuted reports that he is prepared to sell striker Dimitar Berbatov after two frustrating seasons at Old Trafford.

    The Bulgarian international has failed to live up to expectations since he moved to United from Tottenham Hotspur in September 2008 for £30.5million.

    Berbatov netted only 12 times in the Premier League this season and, despite a lackluster year, Ferguson insists that the striker will not depart United this summer.

    "No, he will not - definitely not. There's a problem with Manchester United in terms of expectation and media attention,” Ferguson told The Mirror.

    "There's speculation every year and we've got to live with that hype. We know Dimitar is a good player and he will be with us next year.

    Despite losing the race for a fourth consecutive Premier League title to Chelsea by just one point and being from the Champions League in the quarter-finals by Bayern Munich, Ferguson believes that his side do not have a lot they need to get better at.

    "We don't have a great deal to improve on. The one area of concern last season was that we had 16 players missing through injury.

    "That impacts on the stability of the team as the season develops. But we always felt that when our back four was in place there was a sense of purpose and strength."
