    Kaladze dismisses Chelsea deal

    Premiership - Milan defender Kakha Kaladze has pledged his future to the Rossoneri, quashing speculation he is set to join Chelsea.

    The Georgian has long been a target of Jose Mourinho, who reportedly wanted him to be part of any deal taking Andriy Shevchenko back to the San Siro.

    "The club and I cleared everything up," Kaladze told Milan Channel.

    "I am happy and I want to tell all the fans, all of those that love me and that love Milan, that I will be a Milan player until 2011.

    "I have a contract and I respect all of the rules of this side. In regards to my statements after the final, I certainly did not want to express any desire to leave."

    Kaladze came off the substitutes' bench during last week's Champions League final, which saw the Italian side become champions of Europe for the seventh time.
