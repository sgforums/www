    Philippe Senderos quits Arsenal for AC Milan

    AC Milan have completed the signing of Arsenal defender Philippe Senderos on a one-year loan deal, according to Gazzetta dello Sport.

     

     

    Milan have been in the hunt for a centre-back for some time now due to persistent injuries picked up by Alessandro Nesta and Paolo Maldini. The Rossoneri have already sold Dario Simic and Digao, while their proposed move for Chelsea defender Branislav Ivanovic fell through at the last minute.

    That left coach Carlo Ancelotti with a defensive headache, and the loan signing of 23-year-old Senderos ensures Milan have cover at the back with the new Serie A season starting this Saturday.

    Milan also have the option to make the move permanent for a set fee next summer, and a transfer to the San Siro would seem likely as the Swiss international has fallen down the pecking order at Arsenal.

    Manager Arsene Wenger signed Mickael Silvestre from Manchester United last week - a firm indication that he does not see Senderos' future at the Emirates.

    Senderos made 74 Premier League appearances, scoring four goals, after his move to London from Servette in Dec 2002.
