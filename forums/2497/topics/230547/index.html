    He has managed just five goals and again drew a blank against League Two minnows Macclesfield in the FA Cup at the weekend.

    Shevchenko's performance against Macclesfield raised a number of eyebrows, but he has been backed to find his true form by England star Lampard.

    "I do feel sympathy for him but it is no problem," Lampard told The Sun.

    "Saturday was not a day where because he cost Â£30million and is a world-class striker he had to score.

    "He has to work hard and try to lead the line, which he did. It doesn't always happen for you."

    Reflecting on the win over Macclesfield on Saturday, Lampard added: "I hope people don't make a big deal about the fact he didn't score against Macclesfield because football is not that simple.

    "He doesn't seem to be affected by not scoring. He is working hard in training and is a great professional.

    "Of course he wants to score more goals, that is what he has done his whole career. He is keeping his head down and grafting. If you watch Saturday's game he didn't score but you could see he was trying to.

    "You could see the frustration, that is normal, but as long as he keeps trying the goals will come.

    "We can all try too hard - that is not really a big fault. That is just showing what you want to do. Eventually it will turn for him."
