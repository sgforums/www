    Being crowned kings of England will be better than conquering Europe, says Liverpool skipper Steven Gerrard

     

    Silver lining: Gerrard would love to add the domestic title to his trophy haul after lifting Europe's top prize three years ago.

    Steven Gerrard  believes winning the Premier League with Liverpool would be bigger and better than their dramatic Champions League triumph three years ago.  

    The Reds came from 3-0 down to beat AC Milan in a penalty shootout in the Istanbul final but the Liverpool captain thinks being champions of England would rank as a more significant achievement.  

    He said: 'We call the Premier League the bread and butter around here and we need to win it. 'I'm desperate for the League. I can't show you how much we want to win the Premier League. 'If we do win it I think it will be bigger than Istanbul and I would want to go on and win it again. We have had a good season so far but it's easy for me to say we're a great team and the squad's great. 

    'The hardest thing is to actually go out there and prove it on the pitch. 'It has been a long time since we have been able to say Liverpool are top of the league in December but we are happy where we are.'
