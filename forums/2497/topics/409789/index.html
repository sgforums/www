    Rafael Benitez Angry & Empty Handed Following Inter's Lack Of Spending - Report

    Inter coach Rafael Benitez is not impressed after hopes of signing players fizzled out.

    Rafa Benitez is anger at Inter

     

     

     

    Rafael Benitez's anger is bubbling away and about to boil over after becoming the first Inter coach to have failed to sign a player under the Massimo Moratti era.

    Tuttosport     claims the Spanish coach is frustrated with the lack of spending at the club. He was keen on Dirk Kuyt and Javier Mascherano, but the club ended their interest after Liverpool made their price, with the latter joining Barcelona.

    Moves for Gokhan Inler and Giampaolo Pazzini also failed over transfer fees and reluctance to push.

    On Monday Benitez mumbled that he hoped "to sign someone" before the end of the transfer window, but those hopes were dashed on Tuesday when the clock struck 19:00CET and there were still no players.

    Despite missing out on his targets, Inter did make some adjustments. Luca Castellazzi arrived on a free deal from Sampdoria as back-up to Julio Cesar. Philippe Coutinho arrived in Italy after he was signed two years ago. Jonathan Biabiany also came back from Parma after a loan spell.ï»¿
