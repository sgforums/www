    Defoe joy at £7m Pompey move

    Defoe.. teaming up with Redknapp again

    JERMAIN DEFOE completed a deadline-busting £7million move to Portsmouth last night — to end a nightmare day on a high note.

    The striker was gutted to be left out of Fabio Capello’s England squad then learned Spurs had agreed to sell him.

    Defoe, who failed to hold down a first-team place at Spurs, said: “I can’t wait to play for Portsmouth. I just want to get my boots on.

    “I fully understand why I was left out by England. You can’t expect to get picked if you’re not playing regularly.

    “Hopefully I can play football here. I just want to score goals.”

    Redknapp, who paid Charlton £1.6m to sign Defoe for West Ham in 1999, said: “He’s a boy I know well and he will be a great signing for us.

    “He wants to play and he needs to play.”

    The deal was only confirmed at 12.55am.

    Benjani's £9m move to Manchester City was not completed but that deal may be ressurrected today as the Premier League investigates paperwork.

    City boss Sven Goran Eriksson did at least seal a £5m deal with Swiss side Basel for Ecuador striker Felipe Caicedo, 19.

    His agent Saif Rubie explained: “He’s agreed a 4½-year deal.

    "He’s over the moon and can’t wait to get over here.

    "It’s taken the best part of two months to come together.”

    And on a busy day at Eastlands Eriksson completed a loan deal for Inter Milan starlet Filippo Mancini, 17, the son of the Italian giants’ coach Roberto Mancini.

    City nab Benjani for 1/2-price

    Benjani.. signed for just £4.8m

    MANCHESTER CITY have landed Benjani for nearly half the £8million fee they agreed last week.

    They bagged him for just £4.8million three days after the transfer window shut.

    City officials told Pompey top brass of their new offer last night — insisting the South Coast club could take it or leave it.

    Pompey had to agree as they have a financial commitment to buy Jermain Defoe from Tottenham for £8m.

    Eastlands bigwigs were allowed to complete the deal yesterday as the move had been put in motion ahead of last Thursday’s midnight transfer deadline.

    They have also given the player a 2½-year deal, rather than the original four-year offer.

    City halted the deal after the 29-year-old striker had arrived in Manchester just an hour before the window closed.

    Eastlands chiefs were unhappy with some of the paperwork and the fact the medical had been rushed.

    Yesterday they gave the striker, who has 12 league goals this season, a full day of tests — which he passed.

    They also solved the paperwork problems.

    Pompey could not begin talks over Benjani with any other club as the deadline was gone.
