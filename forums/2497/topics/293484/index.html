    Bangkok University claim third place after narrow win over Woodlands

    Bangkok University claimed third place in their first appearance in the RHB Singapore Cup after edging Woodlands Wellington 1-0 in the playoff match on Saturday evening at Jalan Besar Stadium.

    Suriya Domtaisong, omitted from the Thai side's starting lineup for this match, came off the bench to score the only goal of the game on 74 minutes, striking from 35 yards out to beat Yazid Yasin.

    Woodlands WellingtonÂ’s ambition of wrapping up their season with a win and third place medals in the Cup was dashed by a thoroughly articulate Bangkok University team.

    The Thais won 1-0 with a brilliant individual goal from Domtaisong after the Rams had failed to put away their chances all through the first half and then in much of the second.

    In addition, Woodlands had no joy with referee T Aravinthan, with a strong penalty claim turned down on the hour mark after striker Laakkad Abdelhadi came down under a challenge from University defender Kriangkrai Chasang, when trying to get on the end of a left-sided cross from Zahid Ahmad.

    The ball ran beyond the far post and nothing eventually came of what appeared to be a very promising moment for the Rams.
