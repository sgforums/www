    Brazilian club Flamengo have started negotiations to take AC Milan striker Ronaldo back to South America.

    The three-time world player of the year has struggled to impress Milan coach Carlo Ancelotti and has also suffered a series of persistent injuries.

    The 31-year-old is in Brazil receiving treatment for a shin injury, but Flamengo vice president Kleber Leite said it would not hinder the transfer.

    Ronaldo signed for AC Milan in January 2007 from Spanish giants Real Madrid.

    "We have made contact with the player and we are certain he can launch his career again here," Leite told Italian paper Gazzetta dello Sport.

    Ronaldo's contract finishes in June 2008, but Milan will be hoping to get back some of the Â£5m they paid Real for the striker's services.

    Flamengo are keen to upstage their city rivals Sao Paulo, who secured a six-month loan for Inter Milan's Brazilian international Adriano last week.

    Ronaldo's Father: My Son Wants To Play For Flamengo

    Ronaldo's father, Nelio, has commented on the recent speculation linking his son with a move to Flamengo.

    It was recently reported that Brazilian outfit Flamengo had begun negotiations with AC Milan's Ronaldo with a view towards a transfer for the player back to his homeland.

    Indeed, Flamengo's president was quoted on a Brazilian website as saying that talks were well underway. However, AC Milan moved quickly to deny this, and subsequently all was quiet.

    However, Ronaldo's father himself now claims that his son, who is currently in convalescence in Brazil, has his heart set on a switch to the Rio outfit.

    Speaking to the Gazzetta dello Sport, Nelio remarked, "He's always talking of his desire to move to Flamengo, who he supports, and sooner or later he will do so."

    For his part, Flamengo president Kleber Leite has once again fanned the flames of speculation by claiming that he wishes to offer the troubled forward a deal through to 2010.

    His current AC Milan contract expires in June 2008.
