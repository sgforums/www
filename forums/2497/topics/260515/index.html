    Drogba in Blues warning

    Didier Drogba has warned Chelsea he is not certain to stay at Stamford Bridge.

    Last season's top goalscorer has admitted he would be keen to try his luck in another country before the end of his career.

    Drogba enjoyed his best season with the Blues last season, netting 21 Premiership goals and scoring the winner in the FA Cup final with Manchester United.

    The Ivory Coast international says he would consider moving on if Chelsea's ambition did no longer match his own.

    "If I think the club lacks ambition, then it is sure I will not stay too long," Drogba told The Sun.

    "I'm 29 and I don't have much time to lose.

    "I'm at the last big turning point of my career.

    "It's time for one final big choice - should I stay or should I go? I am tempted to discover another world and to see what footballing life is like outside England.

    "It would be a big disappointment if I never manage to play for teams that I dream of playing for. That would be terribly frustrating.

    "Spain and Italy attract me a lot and, although I am a long way from saying farewell to Chelsea, you never know what can happen."
