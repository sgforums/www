I HAD TO WALK AWAY OR ROT AWAY Ex-Gunner Bentley

  • zocoss
    28 Jan 07, 13:26


    DAVID BENTLEY freely admits leaving his "comfortable" life at Arsenal was a gamble.

    But Blackburn striker Bentley now believes it was the best career decision he has ever made and he says more young English players should follow his brave example. Bentley, 22, is now a candidate for Steve McClaren's England squad for next month's friendly with Spain after fulfilling his huge early potential with a rich vein of form at Blackburn.

    "It became very comfortable for me at Arsenal and that's the case for a few young English players at some of the big clubs," said Bentley. "You get brought up with a silver spoon but if you don't push yourself then you can end up rotting badly rather than progressing. I could have hung around at Arsenal until I was 23 or 24 or gone on and played regular games for another club.

    "I had a great education at Arsenal and got to play and train day-in and day-out with the likes of Dennis Bergkamp, Thierry Henry and Patrick Vieira. "But I felt I was becoming a bit stifled at Arsenal and even though it was a risk I decided to go to Blackburn. I've really felt myself progressing, getting better and improving through playing games."

    McClaren has been to see him on several occasions and there is growing pressure on the England coach to give some new faces like Bentley a try, "I look at the England squad and I think I'm more than capable of stepping up to that level," said Bentley. "When I left Arsenal my aim was to establish myself as a Premiership player and my next goal is to become an England player.

    "I've played left, right, central in midfield and up front this year so maybe that versatility will help me. But the biggest single thing for me this season has been playing games and if I keep improving then hopefully I will get in. If you're happy to just sit there at a big club, pick up the odd trophy and be at the back of a few team pictures then you are never going to get into the England team."
