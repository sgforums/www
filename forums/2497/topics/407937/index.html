    Report: Barcelona Agree Fee For Werder Bremen's Mesut Oezil, But Chelsea Are Not Giving Up Just Yet

    The Blaugrana look to pip Manchester United to the finish line for the German international, but Chelsea are still in the race as well.

    WC 2010 - Messi and Oezil(Getty Images)

    Barcelona have surged past Manchester United in the chase for Werder Bremen's Mesut Oezil, according to a report in Spain. The Bundesliga club have reportedly accepted a bid of between €12million-€15m for the 21-year old, say Sport.

    Barcelona are waiting for the approval of coach Pep Guardiola before discussing personal terms with Oezil, whose Werder deal expires next summer. If the trainer gives his blessing, the move should be a formality.

    Oezil previously commented on a switch to the Blaugrana, stating that to play alongside Lionel Messi would be enough to persuade him to join.

    However, Chelsea are becoming increasingly serious suitors for Oezil, according to a report from The Times. The Blues are believed to be pondering a €16m deal for the youngster.

    Chelsea's interest has apparently been lead by Serbian agent Vlado Lemic, a man with strong German connections who enjoys a firm working relationship with Bremen general director Klaus Allofs.

    The Bundesliga outfit are believed to be keen to sell Oezil this month in order to rule out the chance of him leaving for free when his contract expires next summer.

    At just 21 years of age, the German also possesses the added asset of having potential re-sale value, something which has been unusual amongst many of the Blues' expensive signings in recent years.
