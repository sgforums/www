    Chelsea's injury woes deepened on Wednesday night when they lost Didier Drogba with damaged knee ligaments. The Ivory Coast international striker is likely to be out of action for six weeks.

    Luiz Felipe Scolari, the Chelsea manager, has already lost midfielder Michael Essien for the majority of the season with a knee injury and Ricardo Carvalho (knee) and Deco (thigh) are both still out having been injured in the 1-1 Premier League draw with Manchester United last month.

    Not that the treatment room at Old Trafford has been any less busy. Michael Carrick could be out for another 5 weeks, Owen Hargreaves is currently still suffering from irritation of the patella tendon. Paul Scholes is out for ten weeks with knee ligament damage and United will learn today how serious is the ankle injury suffered by Wayne Rooney against Danish side Aalborg in the Champions League on Tuesday.

    Who do you think will be missed most of the players currently sidelined and which squad do you think is better prepared to withstand a catalogue of injuries to key players?

    With Liverpool boasting a near-clean bill of health at present, could this week’s casualty list among the Big Two open the door for someone else to mount a credible Premier League title challenge this season?
