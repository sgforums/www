    AC MILAN could launch a summer swoop for Chelsea misfit Andriy Shevchenko.

    The Blues striker has barely featured this season despite Jose Mourinho's departure from Stamford Bridge.

    Chelsea would be unable to recoup anywhere near the £30million they paid Milan two years ago.

    But the Italians could launch a cut-price raid for the Ukraine star, who spent seven years at the San Siro.

    Milan president Silvio Berlusconi said: “It would be good for Shevchenko to return to us.”
