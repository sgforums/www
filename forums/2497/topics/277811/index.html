    Everton boss David Moyes has insisted he needs to spend more money if the club are to be successful despite a good start to the season.

    Moyes says cash must continue to be spent when the chance comes up despite breaking the club's transfer record last week when landing Ayegbeni Yakubu from Middlesbrough for Â£11.25m.

    The boss says that is the sort of market he needs to be working in if they are to compete with the biggest clubs in England and that means increasing the club's revenue stream, possibly by moving grounds.

    He told icliverpool: "I think if Everton want to move on we will need to compete. Everybody knows there's limited resources to what we can make through Goodison and television and so on, so I think for there to be regular signings like Yakubu, we need to look to the bigger picture.

    "Whether it was a redevelopment of Goodison or whether it was a move to Kirkby, the fact of the matter is if we want to compete we're going to have to find a bigger revenue stream."
