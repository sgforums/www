    Scolari:  seeking help from the all mighty...

     

    PHIL SCOLARI believes Chelsea have been helped in their injury crisis — by God. The Stamford Bridge boss began praying in Romania on Wednesday night, after seeing Didier Drogba suffer another knee injury during the 0-0 Champions League draw with Cluj.

    Now Drogba, who has rejoined crocked stars Deco, Ricardo Carvalho and Michael Essien on the sidelines, should be back in a month rather than needing a lengthier lay-off for surgery.

    Scolari said: “I am religious because I prayed for this. God help me. And sometimes God helps me. “I prayed for Didier, as Didier is part of my family. All of these players are my family.

    "When I saw the injury, I asked my God ‘Look out for this player. He has tried to play for three months and he has worked hard’. "I think God listened and he gave to Didier one more chance to play next month.”
