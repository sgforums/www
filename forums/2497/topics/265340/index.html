    Brazilian sensation Alexandre Pato has insisted he does not mind which club he joins.

    Chelsea have been linked with the 17-year-old Internacional player and were reportedly closing in on a deal last week.

    However, Internazionale and Milan are also understood to be monitoring Pato's progress and he admits he is equally fond of both English and Italian football

    Although Pato is adamant he has not set his heart on one particular club, he does accept that joining Milan's growing band of Brazilians would be very appealing.

    "I must say the truth is I have no preference," Pato told Gazzetta dello Sport.

    "For me it is important to make the jump towards a great European club.

    "England and Italy are the same. Of the rest I cannot judge.

    "If I went to Milan I would find a beautiful group of Brazilians and it would be beautiful to play with people such as Kaka and Ronaldo."

    Ronaldo is a particular hero of Pato's but the teenager is modest about comparisons that have been made between him and the talismanic striker.

    Pato explained: "Ronaldo has been my idol since I was a child. At 17 he was an absolute star, whereas I must still learn a lot.

    "I watch Ronaldo and Kaka on television and use them on the PlayStation.

    "I often choose Milan but also Chelsea, Barcelona and Inter."
