    Ronaldo camp rubbish recent reports

    Cristiano Ronaldo’s management team have denied the credibility of a recent interview given by their client in which the Portugal winger allegedly admitted deciding to leave Manchester United last summer.

    Sunday newspaper The News of the World claimed to have an exclusive with Ronaldo in the wake of his pending move to Real Madrid.

    In it the World Player of the Year appeared to confirm one of the worst kept secrets around, namely that he felt he had reached the end of the road at Old Trafford after last season’s Champions League final win over Chelsea.

    Management company Gestifute have now moved to deny that Ronaldo has given any such statements since news of his record-breaking transfer emerged on June 11.

    A statement read: "Given the strident way that statements allegedly attributed to Cristiano Ronaldo have been printed, particularly in the English media, Gestifute reiterates what it recently stated: Cristiano Ronaldo has been silent since he went on holiday, and all statements (in which he is supposedly quoted) are totally false and abusive.

    "The best player in the world - who is spending the off-season with his family - has not, to date, given any interviews, nor will he in the next few days.

    "As a result, it can be restated here that any statements that are still being attributed to him are devoid of truth and authenticity and must not be taken seriously."
