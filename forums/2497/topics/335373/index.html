Rafa: Babel ready for striking role

  • omgukilledkenny
    29 Oct 08, 23:59

    Rafa Benitez says he expects Ryan Babel to play more and more as a striker for Liverpool – but admits he has little intention of including him in the first-team.

    The 21-year-old Holland international has spent most of his time on the wing since moving to Anfield last summer, but he can also play up front and showed his potential as a forward when he came off the bench in the 1-0 win over Chelsea on Sunday.

    And, after Spain winger Albert Riera joined the club in September, Benitez has revealed that he wants to play Babel as a striker on a regular basis.

    “When we signed Albert Riera, we thought of Babel more as a striker,” he told the club’s official website.

    “Ryan is still a young player.

    “Some Dutch players come to England for only one or two years – in this case, this is his second year and he was injured at the start coming back from the Olympic Games, but he is giving us something more.”

    Benitez believes Babel can make the most impact as a substitute for the time being, though, due to his pace, and believes his performance against Chelsea was a perfect example.

    “Clearly, there is a massive difference when you are talking about a player with pace – normally their stamina levels are not the same.

    “To do the same thing 20 times at maximum intensity, it’s not easy, so they need time to rest.

    “For these kind of players to play 90 minutes at this pace and high tempo is impossible. That is the reason why he can make a massive difference in 20 to 30 minutes.”

    He added: “I was really pleased with him the other day at Chelsea. I think that was the perfect game for him.

    “At that time, we needed pace and power and ability so we could cope with the two centre backs – he could beat them.

    “It was perfect and really good for him.”
