    Chelsea are reportedly set to beat Liverpool to the signing of highly-rated Lyon midfielder Florent Malouda.



    The Frenchman has repeatedly stated his desire to move to the Premier League and Liverpool had been thought to be favourites for his signature.

    However, it now appears Blues boss Jose Mourinho will make his fourth signing of the summer with Lassana Diarra moving in the opposite direction, possibly on loan, according to French radio station RMC.

    Malouda said recently: "Liverpool and Chelsea have shown their interest. I don't know how it's going to end but in my head I'm going to leave (Lyon)."

    He added: "At Chelsea there is Didier Drogba, as well as everybody else, but then at Liverpool, they have such a good coach and the team has played in two Champions League finals in three years.

    "The most important thing for me will be the desire shown by the club. To go and play abroad, you really need to be wanted."

    Lyon have moved to reject suggestions of a deal with Chelsea that will see Florent Malouda head to Stamford Bridge this summer.

    Jose Mourinho is an admirer of the France international winger, while fellow Premier League club Liverpool are also understood to be keen.

    Malouda has stated his desire to leave Stade Gerland in the summer in search of a new challenge abroad and The Blues were reportedly close to striking a deal.

    However, that is not the case, according to Lyon head of communications Olivier Blanc, who also laughed off talk of a swap involving Lassana Diarra.

    Blanc insists any agreement is still some way off and that the French champions have no interest in the swap deal offered by Chelsea.

    "Contrary to what I've heard, there is no agreement with Chelsea about Malouda, and absolutely nothing about a swap deal with Lassana Diarra we turned down a fortnight ago," he told French press.

    Meanwhile, Blanc has revealed midfielder Tiago is set to depart Lyon, with Juventus his likely destination.
