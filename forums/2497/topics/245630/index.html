  • turbo_drift

    Chelsea star Arjen Robben looks set to miss the rest of the season as he needs to undergo knee surgery.

    Robben returned from international duty this week with the injury and the club have decided to send him under the knife.

    Blues boss Jose Mourinho expects the Dutchman to be sidelined for a lengthy period and he admitted it was a major blow to lose the winger.

    "Robben will be out for a long time," Mourinho told it's TV.

    '"For me it is a bit difficult to accept because he played two matches for his country, 90 minutes last Wednesday, and the next day he came here and he is injured.

    "He needs surgery and we lose an important player maybe for the rest of the season."

    Better news on the injury front for Chelsea is the imminent return of Joe Cole.

    Cole has not played since breaking his foot against Manchester United in November and he was forced to go under the knife.

    The England international is making good progress with his fitness and Mourinho expects to have Cole back in full training next week

    "Joe Cole is coming back and maybe next week we have him,' added Mourinho.

    "But again we can never have our best team with every player available. But if I can lose one and gain one, it is not so bad. "

    Robben out for four weeks after knee surgery

    Chelsea winger Arjen Robben will be out of action for at least four weeks after undergoing knee surgery, the club said on Saturday.

    Robben returned with a swollen knee after playing in the Netherlands' 0-0 draw with Romania and 1-0 win over Slovenia in Euro 2008 qualifiers over the last week.

    'Arjen underwent arthroscopic surgery in London... It will be at least four weeks before he returns to full fitness,' English said.

    Attacking midfielder Joe Cole, could be back next week and his England team mate Frank Lampard has been cleared to play despite missing his country's win over Andorra on Wednesday with a damaged wrist.
