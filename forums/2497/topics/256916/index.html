Pennant reveals England pain

  • turbo_drift
    28 May 07, 18:12
    Jermaine Pennant has revealed his disappointment after being overlooked for England once again.

    The Liverpool wide-man was one of a few Reds players to come out of their UEFA Champions Legaue final defeat with some credit after he put in a stirring displays in Athens against Milan.

    Many predicted that Pennant would be rewarded for his form with a call-up to Steve McClaren's squad for the forthcoming matches with Brazil and Estonia.

    However, Pennant was denied a call-up with former captain David Beckham receiving a recall instead.

    "I'd have hoped I was knocking on the England door because if you are not knocking on the door after playing in a Champions League final for Liverpool then there must be something wrong," Pennant told the People.

    "The Champions League has been good for me as a player and I wasn't at all fazed to have played in the final.

    "It would have been a great pick up to be selected by England so I'm desperately disappointed.

    "After a few problems, the last season was a real turnaround and to have played in the Champions League final and finish third in the Premiership I couldn't have expected anymore.

    "Being picked for the England games would have been a good way to end the season."
