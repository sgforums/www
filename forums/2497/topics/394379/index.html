    Gianfranco Zola in despair: West Ham crowd taunt boss after Wolves mauling as police step in as Carlton Cole clashes with fans

    Shell-shocked: Gianfranco Zola looks devastated as his team slump to defeat

    West Ham’s Carlton Cole had to be pulled away by police after an altercation with a fan in the tunnel after his side’s humiliating 3-1 defeat by Wolves.

    A fifth consecutive defeat leaves Gianfranco Zola’s Hammers just three points above the relegation zone and piles the pressure on the Italian manager.

    ‘You’re getting sacked in the morning,’ shouted West Ham fans after Matthew Jarvis scored Wolves’ third goal.

    ‘I’ve got a long night in front of me to think about it,’ admitted Zola, who looked shattered after the final whistle.

    ‘The owners (David Sullivan and David Gold) can do whatever they feel is the right thing to do. I try to my work as best as I can every single day. If that’s not considered good enough, there you go.

    ‘I didn’t see the incident but Carlton Cole represents West Ham and, although he might not be playing his best at the moment, he’s trying.

    ‘The players are working for West Ham and are committed to West Ham. That’s what the fans have to realise. I’ve no problem if the fans have a go at me. The lucky thing is I don’t understand any words.’

    Police intervention: Cole (left) clashed with a fan in the tunnel

    But for Wolves boss Mick McCarthy, the win topped a magnificent 10 days that has seen his side take seven points from three away matches and move ever closer to safety.

    McCarthy said: ‘We’ve not shored anything up, yet, but tonight’s gone a long way to achieving that. The score didn’t flatter us.’

    West Ham boss Zola tells fans: Blame me, not players

    West Ham United boss Gianfranco Zola was disappointed to hear the fans turn on his players during last night's defeat by Wolves.

    The West Ham supporters chanted "you're not fit to wear the shirt" at the team - but Zola urged them to direct any anger and frustration towards him.

    "I think that chant is a little unfair because the players they tried everything," said Zola.

    "The players did their best but in a disjointed way and it made it very difficult for us. They played as individuals.

    "That is very, very painful for me because it is my duty to correct it. The team is not playing very well and I take responsibility for that.

    "If (the fans) want to pick up on somebody they must pick up on me because I am responsible.

    "If they want to chant something they must chant about me. They have to leave the players because they belong to West Ham, they are working for West Ham and they are committed to West Ham.

    "If they want to pick up on somebody they must pick up on me.

    "Last year when we were playing well, I was getting compliments. I took the compliments, I can take the criticism as well. But they should leave the players.

    "My concern is the team and I have to make sure they react in the right way. I have a long night in front of me to think about that."
