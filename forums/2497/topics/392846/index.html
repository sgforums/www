    He parked his car outside the hotel as the beauty paid for her room with a credit card.

    She then walked back out into the car park before re-emerging through the revolving hotel doors with the millionaire footballer following behind in a hoodie-style top.

    Twelve seconds later church-goer Evra, wed to wife Sandra and dad to four-year-old son Lenny, was seen at the lifts with the girl, with whom he had laughed and joked.

    As they waited by the elevator, the blonde leaned in and planted a kiss on grinning Evra's cheek - unaware she was being filmed by CCTV.

    Back inside ... girl walks through foyer with Evra pictured behind

    Yesterday France international Evra admitted it was him in the pictures - but declined to say why he was visiting the hotel 15 miles from his mansion in Alderley Edge, Cheshire, in the early hours.

    Wearing a purple hoodie-style top, he spoke through the window of his 4x4 as he set off for training ahead of United's game at Wolves today.

    Waiting's over ... pair watch as the lift arrives and doors open

    Looking perplexed, Evra studied the CCTV pictures and said: "Yes, that's me, I can talk to this woman. We were just at the same hotel."

    Asked why the blonde had kissed him, the 28-year-old replied: "I have never met her before" - then he wound up his window and sped off.

    Husband ... Evra with wife Sandra

    But the soccer star's mysterious middle-of-the-night visit baffled hotel guests who recognised him.

    One said last night: "We couldn't believe it. There was hardly anyone about at that time of the night and we looked up when we saw this pretty girl checking in.

    "Then this bloke followed her and there was no mistaking who he was - even with the hoodie.

    "I was going to ask him for an autograph, but he was straight over to the lifts.

    "It's not the flashest of hotels by any stretch of the imagination, so it was weird to see a Man United player bowl in at that time of the night.

    "We couldn't work out what he would be doing there in the early hours of a Monday - especially as United had a match two days later. It didn't seem the best way to prepare for a game. We saw the girl. She was a real babe - she dressed well and looked like a model."

    With phone ... star yesterday

    Evra, snapped checking his mobile phone before travelling to Wolves yesterday, visited the hotel on February 8. It is a 25-minute drive from the home he shares with 28-year-old Sandra, his high school sweetheart and partner for more than 15 years.

    The star has always insisted he is devoted to his wife and said in a recent interview: "She's my first girlfriend, and I hope my last!" Discussing his responsibilities as a role model, he said: "As a footballer, I need to be mindful of the example I am setting.

    "Sometimes I react badly on the pitch and the first thing I think of is what impact my behaviour might have on youngsters watching."

    Evra has also spoken at length about his religious ties and revealed he has more time to attend church playing in England than abroad.

    Evra ... with the Carling Cup

    He said: "In France training is in the afternoon. I like to sleep in the morning and I didn't go. Here I've more time to go to church.

    "I just say thanks to God that I don't have a lot of injury, just to keep my feet on the ground and don't try to be somebody I'm not."

    Evra also dotes on his son and told a club fanzine three years ago: "I have always loved children and find their innocence irresistible.

    "I can't describe the joy my son has brought me. Even when I get home a bit uptight, all it takes is one look from him and everything is forgotten." Evra's hotel visit could land him in hot water with his superiors at Old Trafford - as just two days later he turned out for United in their 1-1 draw with Aston Villa.

    His manager Sir Alex Ferguson is a huge advocate of seeing his players settled and married.

    Last September he spoke about Wayne Rooney's growing maturity, saying: "Marriage helps footballers. It helps them settle down - you know where are they are too! It's good for the stability of a footballer."
