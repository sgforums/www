    BARCELONA'S PEP GUARDIOLA TARGETS HIS AWAY CURSE

     

    Pep Guardiola has admitted he is desperate to break his Champions League knockout duck

     

     

     

     

     

     

     

     

     

      

    Arsenal vs Barcelona 

    Wednesday, 16 February 2011

    Time: 3:45 am  -  (Live) (Thur) 

    Mio Channel 115 

    Champions League 2nd Rd (1st Leg)   

     

     

     

    PEP GUARDIOLA has admitted he is desperate to break his Champions League knockout duck with Barcelona against Arsenal tonight.

     

    Barca are yet to win a single away game in the knockout stages under boss Guardiola and have drawn five games and lost twice on their travels.

    And Guardiola has promised Barca will go for the jugular at the Emirates Stadium in the first leg of their last-16 tie against the Gunners, even if it means they risk defeat.

    “This is the best moment to break this record,” said Guardiola. “There have been moments when we have lost and drawn, but teams in Europe are very strong.

    “The other reality is in the last two years we have reached the final and semi-final, so the percentage is good for us. Sometimes we have played badly and qualified and sometimes we have played well and been knocked out.

    “We know the tie will not be decided tonight, it will be decided in Barcelona, but we want to win the game.

    “We are Barcelona and we will go out to attack. We might lose, but we want to attack, try to score a goal and go for the win. That’s what we’ll try to do.

    “I have a lot of respect for Arsenal and Arsene Wenger and we know they will also try to attack.

    “They have very good players in Theo Walcott, Robin van Persie, Cesc Fabregas and Samir Nasri.”

    Barcelona midfielder Andres Iniesta said: “We know our results away from home in Europe have not been great, but when we have lost we have then won at home. Nothing will be decided in the first leg.”

    OPENER ... David Villa

     

     

     

    DAVID VILLA claims Arsenal did not deserve to beat Barcelona.

    The Spanish striker scored the opener at the Emirates in the Champions League last-16 first-leg clash last night.

    But goals from Robin van Persie and Andrey Arshavin stunned the visitors.

    Villa said: "They are a very good team. They've caught us on two counter-attacks and caused us a lot of damage.

    "We were better but they hurt us on the counter. We didn't deserve this result.

    "The scoreline, when we were 1-0 up, should have been bigger looking at the chances we had.

    "But Arsenal are a great team and took advantage of their opportunity.

    "We have a team to get through. We will be ready for the day."

    Keeper Victor Valdes is equally is confident Barca will progress.

    He added: "We had the game under control but they took advantage of their chances well.

    "There's still the second leg and we have faith that things will turn out in our favour.

    "It's possible in our stadium. We are confident that it will all work out for us."
