Madrid To Offer 80m For Kaka

  • omgukilledkenny

    Spanish daily AS continue to insist that Real Madrid will launch a massive offensive to land Kaka once Fabio Capello is sacked on Monday. The transfer strategy for the summer is already in place...



    Ramon Calderon is unwilling to give up on landing Kaka, and believes that persistent pressure on Milan will tell.

    AS report that Real Madrid have a 110 million euro transfer budget for this summer, and are ready to spend 80 of those on the Brazilian play-maker, making him the most expensive signing in football history.

    Jose Angel Sanchez, a board member with contacts in Milan, and sporting director Pedja Mijatovic have been handed the license to step up their efforts.

    The offensive will be launched on Monday, when it is expected that Fabio Capello will be sacked.

    Bernd Schuster, the man supposed to replace him, has already given his endorsement saying: "Kaka is more important that Cristiano Ronaldo."

    Diogo Kotscho, an associate of the player has admitted that transfer records would be torched if the move goes through: "If Madrid managed to sign Kaka, he would be the most expensive signing in football ever.

    "Real Madrid's offer is really good, and until the 31st of August, it is impossible to rule out the fact that Kaka will be at the Bernabeu next season."

    The report suggests that should this move go through, Real Madrid will use the rest of the budget will be used to land Heinze, Saviola, Dudek and Makelele.

    However, Real Madrid also have a plan B in place, should the attempt fail: Cesc, Robben and Daniel Alves will enter into the fray instead.

    Kaka Will Not Join Real Madrid - Ronaldo

    Ronaldo believes that his compatriot and club teammate will not be joining Real Madrid this summer.

    "Too much is being said about Kaka's possible exit," he said. "I believe he is going absolutely nowhere.

    "He is absolutely delighted at Milan."

    The former Real Madrid also revealed that he had congratulated some of his friends at the Bernabeu for their La Liga win.

    "I did send some messages to congratulate my friends," he said.

    Almost inevitably, he was asked about Capello's future, and the possibility of Bernd Schuster taking over at the Bernabeu.

    "It does not interest me; it is not my problem," he quipped. "I have my coach, who is Carlo Ancelotti, and Real Madrid is history for me."

    Speaking of history, he did commend Roberto Carlos for his fabulous 11 years at the Madrid.

    "His history with Real Madrid is incredible. He has given the best of himself to the club."

    Calderon: 80m A Fair Price For Kaka

    Ramon Calderon believes that 80 million euros is not too much to pay for Kaka.

    AS have linked Real Madrid with an 80 million euro approach for Kaka, after they set a side a mammoth 110 million transfer budget.

    The report seems to have basis, though as Ramon Calderon has himself admitted that he would consider it to be a good piece of business.

    "We hope to sign him for a reasonable amount, but I do not think 80 million is inflated," he said.

    "We paid 75 million euros for Zidane when he was 29, so how is it wrong when Kaka is just 23 (actually 25)."

    He was aware, though, that it would not be easy: "We need to be alert, because we know Milan will not let him go easily."

    The pressure certainly will have increased following Barcelona's capture of Thierry Henry.
