    Barcelona's Thierry Henry To Request Free Transfer

    Barca's Thierry Henry will reportedly ask the club for economic freedom as he prepares for the final stage of his playing career...

    Champions League : Thierry Henry (Barcelona)

    Knowing that his future lies away from the Camp Nou, Barcelona forward Thierry Henry will ask the club for a free transfer to his next club, according to AS. His next move is strongly rumoured to be to either the New York Red Bulls of the MLS or even the Premier League's West Ham.

    Henry, currently training for the World Cup with the French national team, has not officially given word on his plans to depart the Camp Nou, but the news is a near certainty according to the Spanish press.

    Henry has had a tumultuous three years at Barca. Many viewed him as woefully disappointing in his first year after moving to La Liga from Arsenal, but 'Titi' quickly atoned by being a crucial player in Pep Guardiola's treble-winning side.

    During the past season the Frenchman's age seems to have caught up with him as injuries hampered Henry for much of the year and he lost his starting position in the team to the younger and more sprightly Pedro Rodriguez.
