    Robinho set to miss Olympics

    Forward has joined team-mates at club's training camp

    Brazilian ace Robinho is set to miss the Beijing Olympics after suffering a groin injury.

    The Real Madrid forward has left for the club's training camp in Austria after pre-season medical tests revealed the injury.

    The 24-year-old player, who has been linked with a Chelsea move this summer, admitted he was disappointed that he would miss the Games.

    He said: "Unfortunately I won't be able to go to the Olympics with Brazil, but that is Real Madrid's decision and I respect it."

    The Spanish champions confirmed on their website: "Real have sent a fax to the CBF (Brazilian Football Federation) explaining Robinho's injury and asking that he be left out of the Olympic squad.

    "On the advice of the medical staff, the club has decided that the best thing for the player would be to go to the pre-season training camp with the rest of the squad where he can follow a rehabilitation programme under the supervision of the club doctors."

    Raddy announces squad for match against Brazil Olympic Team

     

    Singapore National Coach Radojko Avramovic has announced his Singapore Selection squad of 21 players for next Monday’s friendly against the Brazil Olympic Team at the National Stadium.

    The squad features 18 players who were in the Lions’ squad for the World Cup Qualifiers in June. Young Lions central defender Afiq Yunos is the only player uncapped at senior international level.

    Lionel Lewis and Qiu Li make a return while defenders Precious Emeujeraye and Noh Rahman have been ruled out of the game through injury.

    Avramovic has also called up three foreign players - defenders Kenji Arai of SAFFC and Valery Hiak of Home United as well as Geylang United’s Slovakian attacker Miroslav Latiak.

    Says Avramovic, “This match will be a great experience for our players. They will be coming up against some of the best players in the world of today and the future.”

    The squad will report for centralised training from Friday onwards in preparation for the match. 
     
       
    Singapore Selection vs Brazil Olympic Team
    Monday 28 July 2008
    8.00pm
    (Gates open at     5.30pm    )
    National Stadium
