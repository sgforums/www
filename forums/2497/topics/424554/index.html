    Chelsea are set to cash in on Jose Bosingwa and Salomon Kalou in the summer as Carlo Ancelotti continues to re-shape his side after spending £71 million in the January transfer window.

    Bosingwa, an £18m signing from Porto, is reportedly attracting interest from Juventus, while Kalou is a target for Turkish giants Galatasaray.

    Bosingwa has struggled to hold down a first team place this season with Branislav Ivanovic often preferred at right-back, and Ancelotti has no shortage of cover in that position with Paulo Ferreira also at the club.

    Chelsea have already targeted Ajax right back Gregory van der Wiel as a potential replacement for Bosingwa.

    Ivory Coast international Salomom Kalou has failed to win a regular starting berth at the club since arriving in 2006 from Feyenoord.

    Despite scoring nine goals this season the forward has started just two league games all season.

    Meanwhile, January signing David Luiz has made an impressive start to life at Chelsea, though Ancelotti says he can still improve. “David has to know English football – and he has to be calmer on the pitch,” the Chelsea boss told reporters

    “Obviously he showed fantastic ability and quality. He will improve because he will have more experience and knowledge of English football. He can change his behaviour on the pitch.”
