Inter Banish Adriano From Europe

  • omgukilledkenny

    Inter Milan have officially left Adriano out of the 25-man squad submitted to UEFA late on Saturday night.

    This sees the relationship between the player and the club sink to a new low, even though Roberto Mancini played it cool.

    "For me it has been a great disappointment to leave out any player," he said after the game against Empoli.

    "My relationship with Adriano? I have spoken with him a lot in these past years and there has always been great confidence on my part.

    "As I said in yesterday's press conference, I'm convinced he will return to being the great champion he was. He has to do his bit, and we will also do more to help him. Together, we can do a lot."

    Earlier in the week, Adriano refused to move to the likes of Parma or Lazio, or to England, on loan, and the general opinion is that this is Inter's response.

    However, that the player will not be Cup-tied, and thus be a lucrative winter target for top clubs, might just have played a part too.

    Adriano accepts Inter's Euro snub

    Adriano insists he is content with life at Inter Milan despite his exclusion from the club's UEFA Champions League squad list.

    "If Roberto Mancini (Inter coach) didn't want me he would have definitely put me out," Adriano told Gazzetta Dello Sport.

    "Mancini explained to me it was a hypothesis he was considering. I told him to do what he wanted.

    "It is for this reason I haven't been surprised by the exclusion. I only think about training and I'm tranquil."
