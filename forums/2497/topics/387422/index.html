    Guardiola refuses to pen Barcelona deal until Arsenal’s Cesc secured

    Pep Guardiola's delays in signing a new deal with Barcelona is over his demands the club signs Arsenal captain Cesc Fabregas.

    The Daily Star says Guardiola is adamant the Gunners skipper should be signed in the summer. Guardiola has so far signed a year-on-year deal, but is holding out at the moment because he wants to make sure the club are committed to landing Fabregas.

    The matter is complicated by the fact their will be presidential elections this summer. But Guardiola is believed to want all prospective candidates to make Fabregas the central player in their transfer strategy.

    A source close to the 38-year-old manager, who Fabregas has admitted was his idol, said: “There is no way that Guardiola will not sign a new contract and he can almost write out his own terms for what he has already achieved.

    “But he wants to make sure that he gets his staff the contracts he wants and that there is a commitment to Fabregas.

    “It will be an important signing for them this summer and any new president will want to keep Guardiola happy.”

    TARGET ... Cesc Fabregas

     

     

    REAL MADRID president Florentino Perez's bid to prise Cesc Fabregas away from Arsenal has been dubbed 'Operation Cesc' in Spain. 

    Perez and his director general Jorge Valdano have held summit talks over a £43million summer bid for the midfield ace - and how they can knock deadly rivals Barcelona out of the running. 

    Arsenal are desperate to hang on to their captain but might be willing to talk if Real's £25m-rated striker Gonzalo Higuain is thrown into the bargain. 

    Against that, Barca hope to weigh in with a straight £40m bid. If that fails, an alternative is to offer unsettled Ivory Coast midfielder Yaya Toure, brother of former Gunners defender Kolo, plus £30m. 

    Barca president Joan Laporta has already promised "to fight for the kid" who played for the club as a teenager. 

    A Real insider revealed: "The price demanded is £43m and that is what we are basing our calculations on. 

    "This is 'Operation Cesc', which we hope will prove a blow to Barcelona."

    Arsenal Boss Arsene Wenger Warns Barcelona And Real Madrid Over Players

    Hands off warning from Gunners boss...

    Arsene Wenger

     

     

    Arsenal boss Arsene Wenger has issued a hands off warning to Spanish giants Real Madrid and Barcelona for the January transfer window.

    Wenger went so far as to write an official letter to both clubs to personally tell both clubs that none of his players were for sale.

    In recent weeks both clubs have publicly declared their interest in signing Cesc Fabregas. Wenger has condemed the methods undertaken by the Spanish clubs of declaring their interest through media outlets.

    "We wrote them a letter. We do not say that Messi and Kaka will sign next week here. If one day we want to sell a player and they are interested they can call us. They don't need to go through a newspaper," Wenger revealed to Sky Sports.
    "[I wrote the letter] a long time ago to say we are not ready to sell any of our players. I don't remember when but they did not write back.

    "In Spain you have to understand it is a bit different. The chairmen are elected by the fans so you have to make a campaign: if you elect me, I come with him, him and him.

    "But they have never spoken to the guys, not to the clubs, most of them, and it's just part of the speculation."
