    The Premier League has appointed Stuart Attwell as its youngest ever referee.

    The 25-year-old from Nuneaton has been promoted to the list of top flight officials after just one season in the Football League.

    Premier League referees' chief Keith Hackett told BBC Sport: "Last season week-in week-out he produced consistent, high quality performances.

    "Under pressure he remained calm and showed a great deal of maturity when managing players."

    Attwell has been fast-tracked through the refereeing system, having started in 1998, and was promoted to the National List of Referees in 2007.

    Hackett said he has no doubts that Attwell will be able to handle the extra pressure of stepping up to the Premier League, where the money at stake means decisions are scrutinised even more closely.

    He added: "I can confirm that Stuart is the youngest ever referee to be promoted to the Premier League.

    "He has earned the respect of players, managers and spectators by demonstrating excellent fitness and a high standard of performance in his decision-making.

    "My task as his boss is to ensure that he transfers those skill sets to the Premier League.
