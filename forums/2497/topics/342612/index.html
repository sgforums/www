Wenger declares Toure will stay

  • omgukilledkenny

    Arsenal chairman Peter Hill-Wood has revealed he has turned down a transfer request from wantaway Gunners defender Kolo Toure.

    The centre-back has failed to secure a regular spot in the first-team plans of manager Arsene Wenger this season and Manchester City are thought to be considering a bid.

    Hill-Wood is aware that the £10million-rated Ivory Coast international has indicated that he is keen to leave Emirates Stadium, but he insists the Gunners are not looking to sell.

    "I know about it (the transfer request)," Hill-Wood said in the Daily Star. "But we are not going to accept it.

    "We are not looking to let any of our players go and he is under contract - and we expect him to honour it."

    Toure joined Arsenal seven years ago and is the last member of 'The Invincibles' squad, which remained undefeated for the duration of 2003/04, still at the club.

    Wenger declares Toure will stay

    Arsene Wenger has dismissed suggestions that Kolo Toure could be poised to leave Arsenal following the defender’s decision to hand in a transfer request at Emirates Stadium.

    Chairman Peter Hill-Wood confirmed on New Year’s Day that Toure had asked to leave the club, with reports claiming that he was disillusioned with a lack of playing time and that he had fallen out with fellow defender William Gallas.

    Manchester City has been mentioned as a possible destination for Arsenal’s longest-serving player but Wenger has firmly ruled out a January exit for the veteran of the 2003-2004 ‘Invincibles’ season.

    "The situation is that Kolo is a player who has two-and-a-half-year contract and will remain with us," said Wenger.

    "I am happy with players who are committed and Kolo is a big example.

    "There was a minor incident that you can multiply by 20. It happens every day in every club which is sorted out. Kolo is happy here.

    "We have a very young and compact squad and I don't expect anybody to leave."
