    Last Updated : Saturday, October 27, 2007 12:18:50 PM
    Latest update on Singapore - Palestine WCQ

    As of 1200hrs on Saturday 27 October, the Palestine National Team has yet to arrive in Singapore for the FIFA World Cup 2010 Asian Zone qualifying match against the hosts on Sunday 28 October, 5pm at the National Stadium.

    The Team Managers' Meeting took place this morning with the Match Commissioner Shahir Ahmed of the Maldives explaining that he has not received any written confirmation from the Palestine FA on their travel or non-travel plans.

    Said Shahir, "I've also yet to receive any official documentation from FIFA or AFC on the status of this match. As such, it cannot be officially called off and preparations for it to go ahead as scheduled must still continue."

    The FAS will continue to get in touch with the Palestine FA and is hopeful that the match will still go ahead in the event the team arrives by tomorrow.

    Pending advice from the Match Commissioner, the FAS will be issuing a media statement on the status of the match on Sunday 28 October.

    The public is advised to check with the media as well as the FAS website (www.fas.org.sg) for the latest on the status of the match.

    In the event that the match is called off, fans who have purchased match tickets can obtain a full refund at the FAS office at Jalan Besar Stadium (Admin & Finance dept) during office hours (9am Â– 6pm) from Tuesday 30 October.
