Bergkamp Voted Into Hall Of Fame

  • omgukilledkenny
    4 Sep 07, 12:43


    Arsenal legend Dennis Bergkamp has been voted into the National Football Museum Hall of Fame.

    The Dutchman, who has left the Gunners are retired from football, was selected by viewers of BBC's Football Focus as supporters were given the opportunity to select the player they felt most worthy of joining the elite list.

    Bergkamp was selected ahead of Sir Trevor Brooking, Ray Clemence, Paul Scholes, Len Shackleton and Ray Wilson to become the first Dutch player to be included in the hall of fame.

    He becomes the fifth former Arsenal player to be included following on from Alex James, Tony Adams, Liam Brady and Ian Wright.

    Bergkamp played in 423 matches for Arsenal during his ten illustrious years with the club and scored 120 goals during that time.
