    BRIDGE TOO FAR ... John Terry could be up for a move to Manchester City

     

     

    JOHN TERRY has been offered a record-busting £300,000-A-WEEK to join Manchester City. The future of the Chelsea and England skipper is now on a knife-edge. 

    Terry, 28, has been blown away by City's offer, which is worth a mind-blowing £15.6million-a-year. But it is a measure of how boss Mark Hughes views the centre-back as crucial to City's development. 

    Terry would become easily the world's highest-paid player. His salary would dwarf even that of the footballer closest to him - Cristiano Ronaldo, on £255,000-a- week at Real Madrid. 

    Hughes reckons Terry is the key to City bursting into the Premier League's top four and becoming one of Europe's leading clubs. The Welshman is convinced he can get a good five years out of the centre-back, despite some of the injury problems he has suffered in the last couple of seasons, and has long had him on his radar. 

    City failed with a move for Terry last season and, though the latest £30m offer has been firmly rejected by Chelsea, SunSport understands the pursuit is far from over. 

    In fact, the Londoners are bracing themselves for another bid in the region of £40m. Sources close to Terry say the player is beginning to wonder whether Chelsea really want him as much as they claim and admit he has been impressed by the faith Hughes has in him.

    SunSport can reveal Terry has had no contact with incoming manager Carlo Ancelotti since the Italian got the job and is worried that his influence at Stamford Bridge could be on the wane. 

    Terry has become more than just the skipper at Chelsea. He was almost on the board and was the link-man between the players and the hierarchy. Ancelotti may have different ideas about how the club should operate. Yet with Hughes, Terry knows he will be the main man whichever stars arrive. 

    Terry currently earns around £150,000-a-week with the Blues, so would be doubling his wages. City are also prepared to pay Samuel Eto'o £250,000-a-week to secure his services from Barcelona. Clearly money is no object to City's Arab owners. 

    Chelsea suspect Terry has been tapped up and City sources admit the club would not have made a bid without doing its homework. In other words, they have already found out whether he would be interested in the move before making their official approach. 

    SunSport was told: "This one could run for a while. John is really interested in what City have to say. It is not impossible he could leave."

    Chelsea Boss Ancelotti Seeking John Terry Meeting - Report

    As the Italian prepares for his first day at work at Stamford Bridge, his main priority is to sort out the skipper's future...

     

     

    Carlo Ancelotti is seeking a one-on-one meeting with John Terry to find out if he wants to stay at Chelsea.

    Ancelotti starts work at Stamford Bridge on Monday, and according to The Guardian, one of his first orders of business will be to meet Terry to discuss his future.

    Recent speculation linking Terry with a move to Manchester City continues to hog the headlines, and the London club are concerned by the fact that the England defender has not yet publicly announced his intention to stay, particularly after Chelsea themselves released a statement to that effect on their official website.

    City are reportedly ready to make Terry the highest-paid player in world football, by offering him in the region of £270,000 per week. They are ready to offer £40 million for his services, with the option of including Robinho, who came very close to signing for Chelsea before moving to Eastlands, still a possibility.

    While the Pensioners initially refused to consider any offers for Terry, his general silence on the matter has forced a re-think. They are adamant that they will not be held to ransom over a new contract offer because of these rumours, while some at the club are reportedly concerned by the amount of power Terry holds at the Bridge.

    Terry, along with the rest of his international colleagues, does not return to training until Thursday, but he has arrived back in London after his holidays, and Ancelotti is keen to meet him as soon as possible, to resolve the matter.

    Chelsea have twice rejected bids by City for Terry, their captain

     

     

    Manchester City will make an increased bid of £40 million for John Terry, although any transfer is likely to be dependent on the England captain’s willingness to force the issue.

    Chelsea suspect that Terry may be using City’s interest as leverage in an effort to secure an improved contract, but if he is serious about leaving Stamford Bridge, the club will insist that the defender submits a transfer request as they are determined not to be painted as the villains in any prospective sale.

    City would not have pursued Terry so rigorously had they not been given grounds for encouragement and are refusing to concede defeat despite having a £30 million bid rejected last week.

    Terry would stand to earn about £200,000 a week at City — compared with his existing wages of £135,000 a week at Chelsea — and is said to be impressed by the scale of City’s ambitions, although the club must be aware that they could be being used as a bargaining tool.

    Chelsea would consider opening talks with Terry, whose present contract has three years left to run, about a new deal.

    The tone of the statement Chelsea released last week, confirming that they had “completely rejected” City’s offer, reflected their frustration, but despite encouraging Terry to reiterate his commitment to the club, the defender has yet to do so.
