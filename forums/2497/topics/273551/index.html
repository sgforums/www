    Geovanni celebrates with fans after scoring City's second

    West Ham 0-2 Man City

    Rolando Bianchi and Geovanni scored on their debuts as Sven-Goran Eriksson's new-look Manchester City side got off to a winning start at West Ham. The Hammers toiled unconvincingly early on and City were well on top by the time Bianchi slid in to score at the far post after Elano's impressive run.

    West Ham came back strongly but the closest they came to an equaliser was when Dean Ashton's volley flashed wide. Geovanni's low shot sealed the win late on after good work by Nedum Onuoha. It was a display that was sure to have pleased watching City owner Thaksin Shinawatra, who has bankrolled Eriksson's spending spree.

    The Swede gave debuts to five of his foreign summer signings - Vedran Corluka, Javier Garrido, Elano, Martin Petrov and Bianchi - with three other recent arrivals on the bench. But there was plenty of home-grown talent on display too with Kasper Schmeichel, making his first start in goal, bringing the number of academy graduates in City's side to four. And any fears that Eriksson's hastily-assembled team would take time to settle were dispelled by a confident start by the visitors.
