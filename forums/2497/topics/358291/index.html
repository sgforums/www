    Have you seen the size of Micah Richards? Arsenal ace Arshavin left stunned by Premier League foes

     

     

    Huge and Powerful : Robert Huth & Micah Richards

     

    Andrey Arshavin admits he has been left stunned by the sheer size of his Barclays Premier League opponents.

    The Russian star has settled in well at Arsenal after making his £15million switch from Zenit St Petersburg in January.

    But Arshavin says he has struggled to come to terms with the physical battles in this country - highlighting Manchester City's Micah Richards and Middlesbrough's Robert Huth as fearsome foes.

     

    Land of the giants: Andrey Arshavin (centre) has overcome big challenges.

     

    ‘I didn’t expect it to be easy to adapt to English football,' he said. 'In Russia, I never had to face such physical battles.

    'If I jump for the ball and a defender pushes me in Russia, the whistle would blow every time. Here it never does.

    'But it’s up to me to change what I do on the pitch — better movement, quicker decision-making.

    'I’m not afraid to be in a battle but if it’s a Micah Richards or a Robert Huth I’m up against? I’m only half their size!

    'When the defenders are that big, you can’t fight them. You have to use your brain against them.'
