    BLUE AIN'T A BIG CLUB



    ARSENE WENGER has launched a bitter attack on Premiership champions Chelsea by insisting: You're not a big club.

    The Gunners boss taunted Jose Mourinho and:

    ACCUSED Chelsea of playing boring football.

    LIKENED Blues to Spanish minnows Alaves.

    RIDICULED chief-executive Peter Kenyon's boast that the Blues will become the biggest club in the world.

    BLASTED their small fan base.
    And Wenger insisted: "You could easily underestimate the size of Arsenal.

    "When we built our new stadium we had to question whether to make it big enough to house 50,000, 55,000 or 60,000 fans.

    "Today you see that, with a good team, even 60,000 is too small."

    In stark contrast, Chelsea struggle to fill Stamford Bridge, which has a capacity of 42,240.

    And Wenger scoffed: "The fact we play more attractive football is one of the answers.

    "You also have the natural size of the club through history.

    "Real Madrid are bigger than Alaves, even if Alaves has a better team one year.

    "To say Chelsea can be the biggest club in the world looks premature.

    "Building a fan base takes a very long time. The dad brings the son, who becomes a fan."

    Arsenal are 10 points behind Chelsea and have not beaten them in nearly three years.

    But Wenger added: "It doesn't bother me. I am confident that we can make something happen."

    CHARLTON HITS BACK AT KENYON

    Sir Bobby Charlton has told Chelsea it takes more than winning a title or two to become a big club.

    Kenyon recently forecast that the Blues would be the world's biggest club by the year 2014.

    But Manchester United great Charlton says despite the money of Roman Abramovich it will take more than brief success to deliver long-term stature in the game.

    He said: "I am not going to criticise Peter Kenyon. He can say what he likes. But being a big club is about more than just winning a league title or two."

    On the day Manchester United's official Opus was launched, Charlton claimed one of the most famous chapters in the Red Devils illustrious history might be about to be written.

    The unique 850-page, 37kg Opus chronicles United's past, right from their earliest days as Newton Heath until the present day.

    Yet, as Charlton acknowledged, the Opus could be missing a major story by next May if United can suppress the threat of Chelsea to claim their 16th league championship.

    After a weekend of drama at Old Trafford and Stamford Bridge, Sir Alex Ferguson's men now stand eight points clear of the back-to-back title winners.

    And, even though Chelsea still have a game in hand, for the first time since Jose Mourinho arrived in England, United are favourites to finish the campaign as top dogs.

    "I wouldn't say winning the title this season would be the greatest achievement in Manchester United history but it would be the most pleasant," he said.

    "When you come up against someone like Chelsea, who appear to have the finance to be able to buy anyone who is available, it is going to be difficult to finish above them.

    "I wouldn't say we were an underdog but they just seem to collect players. We don't have the type of unlimited finance to be able to match that and we cannot put an excessive burden on our supporters in terms of ticket prices, so to win the league this season would rate very highly indeed.

    "I really do believe there is a strong feeling for us to do it this season and part of that is because of the way we play and the exciting players we have in our team."
