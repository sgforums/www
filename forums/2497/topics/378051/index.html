    Drogba has no problems if Vieira arrives at Chelsea

    Chelsea striker Didier Drogba is relaxed over reports that Arsenal legend Patrick Vieira could move to Stamford Bridge.

    However, Chelsea are currently banned from signing players until 2011 following their pursuit of Gael Kakuta, but that embargo is being appealed by the Londoners.

    "Patrick Vieira will be a good addition to our squad, if we can recruit," he told RTL.

    "We know that we will loose two midfielders in January. John Obi Mikel and Michael Essien will be in Angola for African Cup of Nations.

    "Vieira? We'd take him anytime. We played against him in pre-season in America. He was ready, in good form and match fit."

    BLUES BACKING ... Didier Drogba wants to see Patrick Vieira at Stamford Bridge

     

    DIDIER DROGBA has told Chelsea boss Carlo Ancelotti to snap up Patrick Vieira in January.

    The Blues have appealed against their 18-month transfer ban.

    And Drogba wants them to swoop for Inter Milan veteran Vieira if the appeal is upheld. He said: "It would be a good move for Chelsea to bring Patrick in. Don't forget we are going to miss several players in January, particularly in midfield, because of the African Nations Cup.

    "We played against Vieira last summer and he was very strong."

    SunSport revealed yesterday Ancelotti sees Vieira as the perfect replacement for Michael Essien and John Obi Mikel when they are away on international duty.

    Former Arsenal skipper Vieira, 33, was at Stamford Bridge to watch Chelsea's 2-0 win over Liverpool on Sunday.
