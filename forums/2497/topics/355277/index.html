    Err... Its more than just changing the name leh... Not that simple...

    Before in the old Division 1 time, they came under the control of the Football League... When they change to the Premier League... they formed a limited company to oversee their own affairs... Meaning the 20 clubs now run their own affairs. They are a breakaway league from the old Division 1 to 4 which came under the Football League's control...

    The Football League First Division, was well behind leagues such as Italy's Serie A and Spain's La Liga in attendances and revenues before the Premier League was introduced...

    The Premier League is operated as a corporation and is owned by the 20 member clubs. Each club is a shareholder, with one vote each on issues such as rule changes and contracts. Before this was not the case...  Now the clubs elect their own chairman, chief executive, and board of directors to oversee the daily operations of the league.

    They have commercial independence from the Football Association and the Football League, giving the Premier League license to negotiate its own broadcast and sponsorship agreements. You could say they broke away to take advantage of the lucrative television rights deal. The argument given at the time was that the extra income would allow English clubs to compete with teams across Europe.

    Since the change to the Premier League, clubs need to take better care of their own financial affairs... Running a football club was no long just something they had to do on the field only... Off it is just as important too... We have seen some doing very well in their marketing of their club brand and some very badly even going bankrupt... While some have remain stagnant...

    Since the introduction of the Premier League, clubs are more able to afford stadiums improvements and match attendance and revenues has also risen...

    So you see... Do not be confuse, It not just a name change that simple...
