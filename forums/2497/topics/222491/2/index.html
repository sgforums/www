    Brief Premier League match reports on Saturday:

    ARSENAL 3 TOTTENHAM HOTSPUR 0
    Arsenal swept to a resounding win, albeit helped by a controversial penalty, that should settle their nerves after consecutive defeats at Bolton and Fulham.
    Togo striker Emmanuel Adebayor opened the scoring in the 20th minute, evading Tottenham's offside trap to fire past Paul Robinson. Gilberto then converted a controversial 42nd minute penalty, harshly awarded for Pascal Chimbonda's tackle on Tomas Rosicky, and then scored another in the 72nd minute after Robin van Persie was tripped from behind by Jermaine Jenas.

    BLACKBURN ROVERS 2 FULHAM 0
    Days after beating Arsenal, Fulham slumped to a sobering defeat by Mark Hughes's men at Ewood Park.
    Democratic Republic of Congo forward Shabani Nonda lobbed Fulham keeper Jans Lastuvka in the sixth minute and his South African strike partner Benni McCarthy struck the second in the 24th minute with a deflected shot.

    PORTSMOUTH 2 ASTON VILLA 2
    Two goals from Portsmouth's Matthew Taylor looked to have turned this match around after Villa's Gareth Barry converted and then conceded a penalty at Fratton Park.
    Villa went in front in the 37th minute when Pompey keeper David James clipped wingback Gabriel Agbonlahor in the area and Barry converted the spot kick. Portsmouth levelled in the 52nd minute through Taylor after good work by striker Benjani Mwaruwari.
    Taylor then put Pompey in front from the spot in the 80th minute after a hand-ball by Barry, but Villa clawed back a point when Colombian striker Juan Pablo Angel pounced on a poor clearance by Sol Campbell. A frustrated Portsmouth finished with 10 men after a late red card for Pedro Mendes.

    READING 1 BOLTON WANDERERS 0
    Reading ground out another victory that moves them up to sixth in the table after absorbing plenty of second half pressure from Sam Allardyce's side.
    Ireland striker Kevin Doyle got the winner in the 33rd minute, heading in his eighth goal of the season, from a cross by John Oster. Steve Coppell's men were put to the sword in the closing stages but held out for a fourth league win on the trot that puts the promoted club in the race for European football next season.

    SHEFFIELD UNITED 2 CHARLTON 1
    United had to come from behind for a dramatic late victory in a relegation dogfight that lifted Neil Warnock's side out of the drop zone.
    Andy Reid gave Charlton a 17th minute lead, following up from Darren Ambrose's parried effort, but United levelled with a Chris Morgan header from a corner. They snatched victory with a Keith Gillespie piledriver from outside the area in the 88th minute, to the delight of the Bramall Lane faithful.

    WIGAN ATHLETIC 0 LIVERPOOL 4
    A day after manager Liverpool Rafael Benitez said he hoped to see the best of Craig Bellamy, having been cleared of assault charges by a Cardiff court, the Welsh forward scored two and made another in a rout at the JJB Stadium.
    Bellamy finished off a long deflected ball from John Arne Riise and repeated the feat with a Steven Gerrard through ball inside 26 minutes. He unselfishly laid the ball on for Dutch forward Dirk Kuyt to make it 3-0 and there was still time before the break for Wigan's Lee McCulloch to score an own goal.
    Liverpool cruised through the second half to register their first away league win of the season.
