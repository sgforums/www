    Arsenal line up £20m Russian striker Arshavin

     

     

    Arsenal are preparing a club record £20million bid for Russia forward Andrei Arshavin in a dramatic move to bolster their squad this January.

    Gunners manager Arsene Wenger is breaking his habit of holding back transfer cash and Arshavin, the Zenit St Petersburg front man who was at the club's London Colney training ground last week, is expected to join early next month.

    Tottenham were close to landing the Euro 2008 star last summer and still hold out hope of securing him.

    But the involvement of their north London rivals means they are unlikely to get their man, and that will come as a bitter blow to Spurs chairman Daniel Levy, who had the opportunity to sign Arshavin in August.

    Wenger looks likely to spend money after years of being frugal in the transfer market and emphasising the importance of Arsenal developing their own young talent.

    Danny Fiszman, the largest shareholder in Arsenal and the driving force of the club, has cleared Wenger to spend the money on the basis of the £37m profit the club made last year.

    Wenger coy on Arshavin talk

    Arsene Wenger would not be drawn to comment on reported interest in Andrei Arshavin, but did suggest Arsenal could raid the market to compensate for the loss of Cesc Fabregas.

    Arsenal were dealt a crushing blow on Tuesday with confirmation captain Fabregas will likely be sidelined for four months with a knee injury.

    Fabregas’ absence leaves Wenger with a huge void to fill, as he looks to claim a place in the top four let alone sustain a title challenge.

    Arshavin has been strongly linked in recent days and the Russian schemer is desperate to secure a move away from Zenit St Petersburg.

    Wenger would not be drawn to comment on the Russian, but suggested there could be arrivals in January.

    Responding to a direct question about Arshavin, Wenger said: “At the moment nobody is going out and nobody is coming in.”

    Wenger did concede that he could be persuaded to open his chequebook.

    He said: “More likely yes, but we have internal solutions. We are not desperate because of that (Fabregas’ injury).

    ”In a way it is more important to focus on the games before January.”
