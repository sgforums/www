    Italy rocked by Cannavaro injury

     

    Italy captain Fabio Cannavaro will miss Euro 2008 after tearing ligaments in his left ankle during training.

    The Real Madrid defender collided with Giorgio Chiellini in a practice match at Italy's base in Austria on Monday. After the incident, Cannavaro laid on the pitch for about 20 minutes before being stretchered off.

    Fiorentina's Alessandro Gamberini has been called up as his replacement, with goalkeeper Gianluigi Buffon likely to take over the captaincy.

    Italy had been in Austria for just six hours when Cannavaro suffered his injury. A statement issued by the Italian Football Federation said: "The news we feared was confirmed in Baden late on Monday evening.

    "Fabio Cannavaro will say goodbye to our European adventure, before it has even begun. "The captain has suffered torn ligaments, which happened the moment his foot made contact with the ground. His ankle twisted awkwardly."

    Cannavaro, 34, capped 116 times, skippered Italy to 2006 World Cup glory in Germany, winning the World Player of the Year award.

    His absence is a serious setback for Italy, who are in Euro 2008's 'Group of Death' with France, the Netherlands and dark horses Romania.

    Persie

    ROBIN VAN PERSIE has warned Holland’s Group C rivals that Romania could be the tournament’s surprise package.

     

    The Dutch dropped five points against Romania and finished below them in a tough qualifying section.

     

    Arsenal striker Van Persie, 24, said: “Everyone talks about a fantastic group with the best teams like France and Italy, but Romania have a strong side, trust me.

     

    “We got one point against them in qualifying so we know what they are capable of. They are like a machine.”

     

    He added: “I thought it was a joke when I heard the news of the group.

     

    “It’s a great challenge. No one is secure that they are going to qualify.”

     

    BLATTER

    SEPP BLATTER reckons England are not at Euro 2008 because Premier League sides help foreign players reach their peak.

     

    The FIFA president insists the downside of having one of the top leagues in Europe is the England team suffers.

     

    Blatter said: “In the strong Premier League teams the best players are not always English.

     

    “These teams are preparing national players for England’s opponents.

     

    “The Premier League has benefited the other countries taking part in the European Championships but not England.”

     

    Blatter is trying to convince EU lawmakers to approve a controversial rule that will mean each team must contain six home-grown players.
