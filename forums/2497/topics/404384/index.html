    Teenage boy murdered in suspected 'vuvuzela rage'

    A teenage boy was shot dead in South Africa in a suspected case of ‘vuvuzela rage’. 

    Asanda Cele, 14, was allegedly killed by a neighbour irritated by the sound of his horn after the World Cup hosts beat France in their World Cup first-round match, police have now revealed. 

    The Manchester United fan was shot twice in the head and once in the arm, and died of his injuries at a hospital in Durban.

    A neighbour has now been arrested and charged with murder, though released on bail until next month.

    Asanda’s mother Nontobeko, a nurse at the hospital where the boy died, said: ‘My son loved soccer. He would always be blowing his vuvuzela, whether his team won or lost. My neighbour didn’t like vuvuzelas and the noise they made, and I think that’s the reason he killed my son.’

    The instrument was found next to Asanda’s body.

    Police spokesman Captain Thulani Zwane confirmed the neighbour’s arrest and charge, adding: ‘The suspect told us when he saw the victim he thought he was a robber and subsequently fired at him.’
