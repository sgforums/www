    The Premier League is well represented as 15 players currently plying their trade in England are included, while Barcelona's Thierry Henry is also in contention largely for his performances for Arsenal and France.

    Manchester United's famed attack is celebrated as Wayne Rooney, Ryan Giggs, Cristiano Ronaldo, Carlos Tevez and Paul Scholes are all in, while Liverpool are represented by Steven Gerrard and Fernando Torres.

    Arsenal too are not shy of nominees as Kolo Toure, Cesc Fabregas and Robin van Persie get the nod.

    Didier Drogba's future at Chelsea may have been cast into doubt of late but what cannot be disputed is that the Ivorian has enjoyed a fantastic year and he is joined in the long short-list by team-mates Florent Malouda, Michael Essien and Petr Cech.

    The sublimely talented Dimitar Berbatov is another nominee, proving Tottenham can break into the top four in at least one category.

    Ronaldo takes on Kaka and Messi for top award



    Cristiano Ronaldo has made the three-man shortlist for FIFA's world player of the year award. The Manchester United winger is up against AC Milan's Brazilian Kaka, who is the favourite, and Barcelona's Lionel Messi.

    The award will be made at FIFA's world player gala in Zurich on December 17. Each national team manager and player is given a vote for their player of the year.

    None of the trio have won the award or even been shortlisted before and it has never been won by a Premier League player.

    Italian defender Fabio Cannavaro is the current holder of the award. French midfielder Zinedine Zidane and Brazilian striker Ronaldo both won it three times, while Ronaldinho has won the award twice.
