    Ryan Babel may be a Liverpool flop but he's a 'sick rapper', according to hip hop pal Sway.

    http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jNTmWdfj-LA&feature=player_embedded

     

    Not since John Barnes memorably 'busted' lyrics in England's 1990 official World Cup anthem World in Motion has an Anfield star conquered the charts. 

    And while Ryan Babel may have struggled to find the Liverpool groove ever since his arrival from Ajax in 2007, he sensationally been hailed as a 'sick rapper' after linking up with artist Sway on the UK hip hop star's forthcoming album, The Signature 2.  

    The 22-year-old Holland international is one of a host of stars to collaborate for the new album set to hit stores in 2010 and Sway is backing his close football pal to be the record's success story thanks to his 'amazing' cameo on the unannounced track.

    He said: 'Ryan Babel's a good mate of mine. He's a nice guy, he's a sick rapper.

    'The way he flips his flow is marvellous man, so I wanted him to do a bit - half Dutch, half British, and he really pulled it off man.'

    'We linked up in New York as well. He's a nice guy and I have a lot of fun with him.

    'We linked up with Neutrino and I think it's a track that people are gonna like.'

    It's not the first time Babel has featured on a hip hop track, mastering his MC'in skills with compatriot Daryl on the recent release Eeyeeyo, featuring Ali B and Soumia.
