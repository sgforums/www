    I WAS appalled by the poor seating arrangement at the Concert Hall of the Esplanade - Theatres on the Bay. Having spent so much money in coming up with a beautiful building, why didn't anyone look into the fundamental issue of seats for the audience?

    Even by Asian standards the seats were cramped. I wonder how bigger Caucasians can sit through a three-hour performance without suffering. I had to sit sideways in order to shift position because I just couldn't stretch my legs. My knees were literally knocking on the back rest of the seat in front.

    Then when someone in mid row wanted to move out for a toilet break, everyone of us on that row had to stand up in order to allow him through.

    The ambience and sound system of the Concert Hall were simply fabulous but the economy-class seats were simply not in congruence with the opulence of the venue.

    MUNIR SHAH

    More generous seats? Higher ticket prices

    WE REFER to the letter by Mr Munir Shah, 'First-class hall, economy-class seats' (ST, March 5), and Ms Teo Ching Hwa's letter published in the This & That column (ST, March 7).

    The layout of Esplanade's performing venues was designed by Theatre Projects Consultants (TPC) from Britain.

    This also included the seating arrangements. TPC are theatre design consultants with vast experience and an impressive portfolio.

    Accompanying our reply is a response from TPC Design Director Mark Stroomer which, we hope, will shed some light on the design rationale for Esplanade's venues.

    The objective of the design and construction of the Theatre and Concert Hall was to achieve the best possible acoustics, a sense of intimacy and an optimum seating capacity.

    The very positive response from many of the artistes who have performed at the Esplanade and the feedback from our audiences affirm that, finally, Singapore has a performing-arts centre of international distinction that we can all be proud of.

    We do recognise that some aspects of our seating plan may not fully meet the very high standards that Singaporeans expect.

    However, apart from the impact on acoustics, a more generous seating arrangement would have resulted in fewer seats. This in turn would translate into higher ticket prices for performances, as presenters would have fewer seats to sell to recover their costs.

    The design decision that was made swayed towards having enough seats to ensure that ticket prices are kept reasonable. Affordability of the shows was of overriding importance to us and the compromise in seating has not come at great sacrifice because, in comparison to many other internationally renowned venues, Esplanade's seating arrangements are actually considered to be very good, if not generous.

    We thank both Mr Shah and Ms Teo for writing to express their views. We hope that our patrons, including Mr Shah and Ms Teo, would be willing to put up with a little inconvenience to appreciate the other very positive elements that contribute towards a memorable concert or theatre outing and that the wide range of ticket prices, made possible by the seating capacity of the venues, allows for more Singa-poreans and visitors to enjoy performances at Esplanade - Theatres on the Bay.

    CAROLYN TAY (MS)
    Director, Marketing Communications
    The Esplanade Co Ltd

    --------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    THE seating arrangements in both the Theatre and Concert Hall at the Esplanade are based on traditional proven designs for drama and orchestral venues.

    The spacing and length of rows are comparable to and improves upon many internationally renowned spaces.

    Modern European opera houses such as Gothenburg and Helsinki, both completed in recent years, have a shorter distance between seats and longer rows than the Esplanade lyric theatre.

    Similarly, classic venues such as Royal Opera House, London; Metropolitan Opera House, New York; and the Garnier Opera House, Paris, have tighter and smaller seating layouts.

    The same can be said of modern concert halls in the United States and Europe having similar or less legroom than in Singapore. Many have rows of up to 50 seats or more.

    There are performance venues that give extra space but these do not in general succeed in creating the excellent acoustics and intimacy that the Esplanade enjoys.

    Going to the theatre is an interactive experience that requires the sharing of reactions and emotions. People need to be sensitive to the proximity of others to create the collective experience that a live performance demands.

    Esplanade - Theatres on the Bay has been designed to international expectations of acoustics and theatrical intimacy.

    The venues bring a great many people as close to the performer as possible in a room with a fine acoustic.

    MARK STROOMER
    Design Director
    Theatre Projects Consultants, London
