    Depends on your soundcard and software.

    Cheaper soundcards use a very primitive kind of Midi, which is basically some artificial tones mixed together to sound like "instruments".

    Better soundcards use "wavetable", which uses actual samples of real instruments to playback. It sounds more real, but is still far from perfect. Some software uses wavefonts, something similar wavetable, and more customisable.

    But, no electronic instrument will ever sound like the real thing. For example Woodwinds, there are basically three ranges, lower, mid and upper (altosimmo and all that), all have different characteristics.

    This, and many other factors seperate electronic music from the real thing.
