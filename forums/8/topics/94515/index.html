    I agree with Parka that if these Foreigners are exceptional talents or are willing to fill up positions where there is a shortage of supply (eg nursing) we should welcome them with open arms, However, out of the PRs and Foreigners here holding white collar jobs, how many of them are doing jobs that Singaporeans can't do??     To have extraordinary talented non Singaporeans working in our country is definitely a plus point that we can built on and develope. These people are usually at the pinnacle of their careers and would add tremendous value to the growth of the companies by holding key positions in the companies.

    I also strongly support the notion of hiring foreigners to take up positions in blue collar jobs that Singaporeans think too lowly of.

    However, what really concerns me is the high percentage of non locals hired in the middle management positions in many of the companies here. Speaking from my experience in the Finance Industry, these would include positions such as analysts, accountants and auditors. These are jobs that Singaporeans are hungry for and would find no reasons to turn down. In addition, as mentioned in my earlier posting, there is no wage discrimination in these positions between the Singaporeans and Non Singaporeans in these position. Hence, this is not justifiable to argue on the grounds that non Singaporeans are cheaper here.

    More importantly, these positions are importantly training ground for Singaporeans where they can be trained and groomed to their maximum potential. By allowing the foreigners to compete with Singaporeans in these positions, aren't we shooting ourselves in the foot by shutting out young Singaporeans the opportunities to gain valuable learning experience? Hence, this is not a case where we did not make use of the opportunites, but are we even given the opportunities to ponder over in the very first place? These Singaporeans whom we are shutting out are the ones we are going to rely on in times of national crisis and are those who would or have given all for this little island that they call their country and home.

    To give an analogy in terms of soccer, we have seen spanish teams like real Madrid and Barcelona winning countless international championships with the help of foreign talents but when was the last time Spain as a national team win any major international trophy??
