    Hmm...if you like fighting but do not have a martial arts background, 2 things will probably happen during your 'training':

    1. You'll only be doing moves which even primary kids know, the usual punches and kicks. And regarding kicks, most people who aren't the atheletic type can't stretch their legs far enough or straight enough to deliver a full force kick to the abdomen and above.

    2. You will have a tendancy to use force and go out of control, resulting in injuries to yourself and sparring partner. In addition, your moves will most likely be ineffective against someone with martial arts background. The striking force of punches and kicks is only one of many components necessary. You'll still need speed, agility, fluid and sucessive movements (one move after another) and of course, the two most important components: Intuition and Self-Control/Discipline.

    If you're serious on learning some self-defence martial arts, you'll have to find a legitimate instructor or sensei.
