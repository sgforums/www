    JW extension has a surplus of 3200 units of flats built more than 4 yreas ago and still vacant. I think they clear about 1000 units during the last walk-in excercise and are trying desparately to get rid of the remaining 2000 over units.

    There was even a proposal for private developers to take over these surplus units and turn it into private residential or premium hostel for NTU's student's and staff but no developer in their right frame of mind took up the offer.

    I lived in JW Ext (St.91) for 5 yrs but have since moved out a yr ago.

    I took a big hit getting rid of my 5 room flat but I am glad I got out of that place. Briefly, here are the main downside:

    1. Noise pollution. Damn bloody jet planes are practicing as early as 7 am during the mth preceding to National Day and end as late as 10 pm sometimes. The roar is deafening!!!

    2. A hassle to get to city area. By public transport, u need feeder bus and all that. By private, your petrol will burn a hole in your pocket.

    3. If you have kids, you will have a hard time finding a good school. If you hang around Jurong Point and observe the behaviour of the students from the neighbourhood schools there, you will know what I mean. A lot of "mud rocks"...

    4. As for air pollution, the cocoa smell happens once every 2 or 3 days but you will get use to it.

    5. Huge population of foreign workers in that vacinity because there are a few huge foreign workers quarters there. Come weekends, they will swamp Jurong Point lke ants. Also, you will find plenty of them drinking into the wee hours at the 24 hrs coffeeshop.

    In a nutshell, stay away from that estate.
