    Well for this fee hikes, let's not be myopic. Don't get me wrong, i am not a PAP fanatic. Let's look at the market of public transport in Singapore.

    Public transport is no longer government owned (with the exception of MRT which is partially owned by the government, but the government has the intention to fully privatise it "when the time is ripe"). The public transport companies involved in this hike are :

    TIBS Holdings Limited
    Singapore Bus Services

    Since they are privately owned, their main interest is to make money from the services they provide. We can't expect a company to sell you something at a price lower than the manufacturing price right ? So how are these companies performing in terms of revenue ? Let's see.

    The last fee hike is in 1997.

    The sales growth of TIBS Holdings (1400 workers, 184.7 million Singapore dollars market capitalisation) are :

    1997 = 13.7%
    1998 = 8.9%
    1999 = 3.6%

    Do you see a decrease in sales growth ? At this rate of decrement, if you do a little bit of Maths, you'll discover that the sales growth will be about -1.5% in year 2000. Negative ! That means the company is not growing anymore, in fact, growing negatively !

    SBS is owned by the private corporate Delgro Corporation Limited (8500 employees, 618.4 million Singapore dollars market capitalisation). It operates about 200 services with more than 2,400 buses daily. SBS carries 2.5 million passenger trips a day and relies entirely on fare revenue to finance its operations

    here's the sales growth of the company :

    1997 = 28.7%
    1998 = -11.8%
    1999 = -5.2%

    as you can see, the sales has already become negative.

    Public transport is very different from other industries in any country because the revenue of the company relies fully on the transport fees and it can't "expand its market" cos the market is limited by the population of the country which is more or less constant over a few years. So with the other prices (bus servicing, repair and maintanence, employees' salaries etc) increasing, how can you expect the revenue of the company to remain stagnant ?

    In market analysis, it is definitely reasonable to increase the transport fees annually since our inflation rate is not zero. But because the fee hikes involve the interest of the public hence a government body (Public Transport Council) is set up to monitor it and any increase in fees can only be implemented if the body gives the green light.

    As for the case of the MRT, it is NEVER earning money. This is also part of the reason why it is "not the time" to privatise it fully since who will want to operate an organisation which is not making money ?

    Hope this information gives you people a better understanding of the fee hikes.
