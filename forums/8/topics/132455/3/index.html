    The Hotel Equatorial one was about 8 years back. It was my ex-boss trying to get me back to work under him. I didn't take it up due to personal differences between him and me. We were there at the pub in the evening just after work, so there was no band playing yet.

    The Hyatt Hotel one was about 8 years back. It was in the afternoon. This US expat had ended his term and was going back home....and was looking for a local to take over his place (the company wanted to save cost). I took up this job. I've known this guy for some time, so when he called, he said "Hey, you wanna take over an expat position? Then you gotta attend the interview expat style....at the pub".

    The Eaton Hotel (HK) one was just 4 years back. It was an interview by a European company. One of their senior ranks called me while attending an exhibition in Japan. He said he would be enroute to HK in the next few days before returning to Italy....and asked if by any chance I could meet him in HK. I checked my frequent flyer mileage, checked with my travel agent who gave me a special rate.....and I said yes. Total price of trip to HK = S$300. This was the longest interview I had - 11 hours straight. But it was more like a friendship chat. I took it up and I'm still in this position today.
