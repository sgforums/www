    A new keychain gadget that lets people turn off most TVs - anywhere - has become an instant best-seller.

    Inventor Mitch Altman's TV-B-Gone sells for just Â£9 and he says orders are pouring in so fast his website keeps crashing.

    "I thought there would just be a trickle, but we are swamped. I didn't know there were so many people who were into turning TV off," he said.

    Hundreds of orders came in after the tiny remote control was mentioned in Wired magazine and other online media outlets.

    The keychain fob works like a universal remote control but one that only turns TVs on or off.

    With a zap of a button, the gizmo goes through a string of about 200 infrared codes that controls the power of about 1,000 television models.
