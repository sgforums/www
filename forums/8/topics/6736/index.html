Slide in the American military too...will it happen to us?

  • Altrex
    24 Mar 00, 23:11
    West Point Military Academy. One of the world's foremost military schools and the place which produces the mighty American armed forces' officers.

    The Straits Times (page 6) had a report today highlighting the new trend in American military doctrine. From "minimise casualties while completing the mission", which we are likeswise following in S'pore, to "minimising casualties IS the mission". This is supposedly being taught to cadets now in West Point.

    The approach is now to minimise American casualties at all costs even at the risk of compromising the mission. Why is this so? Cos the American public can't bear to see American blood spilled unless there is a very good reason (other Pearl Harbour?). Reasons for this shift in thinking could be linked to Vietnam where GIs "died like flies" in the Viet jungles. This is well illustrated in many movies, especially in Hamburger Hill.

    I wonder if one day, or even now, the S'pore public will have the same opinion. Minimise casualties at all costs, even to the extent of compromising the mission. Cos S'poreans are too scared to see blood spill.

    Well, my stand is that while minimising casualty is VERY important, there should NEVER be a day when it becomes THE overriding concern. Men do die in wars. Soldiers or civilians, it doesn't matter. WW2 statistics show that the civilian death toll and the soldier death toll is similar. We must be prepared to take risks when we fight and if we are too scared to put our lives on the lines when it matters, what's the point in the first place? If you enter a battle thinking of protecting your own backside all the time, you will end up letting your mates, who depend on you, down. The battle would be lost from the start because you think that you don't dare to fight and take the risk in the first place.

    This, I feel is especially important in the army. Infantrymen die very easily. This much I concede. However, as a good soldier, you are supposed to put your life on the line for your country in the first place.

    The American armed forces might be mighty. But if this attitude sinks in, there will be a slide in the operational capability. Still, I'm not doubting that they can thrash almost any other armed forces in the world today due to superior arms and technology.

    But S'pore is not like American. If one day, S'poreans have the same attitude (or is this attitude already prevalent), I feel that it would spell the end of the SAF.

    Heaven forbid.
