    I just graduated from Lasalle -sia just a month ago.....

    I would suggest going to Lasalle before they get absorbed by NUS...once NUS takes over..the courses will be very regimented and based on rote learning...trust that it is going to be like this...

    Lasalle SIA offeres diploma and Degree in design that are both recognised locally and internationally...the other schools are not really recognised as international qualifications, although you probably can get away with passing it off as one.

    NAFA is known for being able to produce people who can illustrate and present their drawings well. I have seen their work and it looks inpressive. but the problem is that it might be a bit traditional in terms of teaching method and course layout.

    Lasalle sia is known for being the school of slacker...although the physical drawing ability of the students don't quite match those of nafa, Lasalle students are generally more well rounded and are taught to value ideas and consepts above all else, which I might add is what most design houses look for above ability. Lasalle also makes sure that whatever course you major in, you have a very strong base that includes your introduction into fine arts and other medias. in short you will learn painting, sculpture, drawing, computers(multimedia) and a few other things also depending on the scheduling that they set for the semester. But you will get a very good base before you major in design or whatever else.

    like I said earlier, lasalle will stress more on what your ideas are. In the past, it was quite easy to get away with substandard work but have really good ideas, but now the standard is really high already. They expect you to have a standard to keep and still have some ideas behind what you are talking about. Also, it is very well known that the verbal presentation ability of lasalle students are usually better than thiose of the other institutions....and lastly it is considered a private tertiary institution...if that matters to u.

    The diploma course is pretty stressful for interior design(if ur brother is going to tak eit).

    Here is a list of course difficulty from easiest to hardest.

    1.fine arts
    2.graphic design/multimedia
    3.interior design

    the degree course for design tho is damnz tough like hell...They teach you based on curriculum from Australia...beleieve that they care very much about where your ideas come from...how you got those ideas and using wjhat method you got those ideas from...very very stressfull....I almost quit because it was so tuff...and I'm a person who usually sees everything as being easy to a certain extent....I have never had to work so hard for anything in my life before this degree course...so a warning...it is not for everybody as ur english has to be very good to be able to even understand what is going on in the reading and instructions....harg!!!!....I can't even think about it without cringing...(cringe)...

    all- in all any school will do, but I just know that lasalle sia is very well known outside of singapore as well as inside and I dare to say that the "paper" has more weight than the other schools....but you pay for that weight in hefty sums...

    Graphics should cost you: diploma $3000+++ dollars per semester....

    graphics degree (BA graphic design) $ 5000+++

    interior diploma: $3000+++
    interior degree(ba): $7000

    Anyway the amounts are per semester, but the government is now supporting the school but the prices are the same for some reason...so it is recognised by the gov already.

    and keep in mind that the amount here doesn't include materials which can amount to some additional $2000 per semester if you aren't careful...and depending on the course...

    ok, enuff already...I hope it helped..
