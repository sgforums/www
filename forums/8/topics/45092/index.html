    >dig out essay on chinese dialects<

    ahem ahem!
    teochew, hokkien all these is actually a big umbrella.......
    called northern dialects (bei fang fang yan)
    there are about 7 main (read...MAIN) norther dialects....top 3 is hokkien, teochew and cantonese......

    some are the original native language (tu yu).....some have been "contaminated" with other places' dialects when the ppl are displaced (last time is usually due to things like war, land no longer fertile for farming, govt planning)
    sounds agah agah the same, except for some things like accent, stressing differently, usage of terms

    the chinese we speak now is the dialect tt is most pervasive in the Beijing province.......
    beijing is most densely populated and alotta administrative agencies were concentrated there....so the beijing dialect is chosen as the universal Chinese we speak now.....

    still got alot more
    lazy to summarise, translate and type out
    but conclusion is.....dialects can be branched into sub-dialects depending on origin and locale and even situational

    by the way........li bai and all those poets of that era are "hokkien"
    try reading their poems in hokkien
