    I've called up MOE and enquire about pte/in-hse dips and the truth is that MOE neither accredit/recognise nor disregard/deny any pte dips in SG. The governance of the course contents is still up to the provider itself, but they are required to be registered with MOE and info to be provided like the module synopsis and some other info. However, MOE does not review the cse contents/syllabus/what cse notes they use/textbooks etc. Like some pri schools which uses books from publisher A and some from publisher B. In the case of PEO, the diplomas are usually offered in-hse like SMa, Kaplan, PSB etc. The real values of the dip lies in which prestigious foreign unis the schools has tie-up with do gain advanced standing/module exemptions. Much like a polytechnic's diploma's value lies in it can gain module exemptions in local uni admissions or overseas universities, where they deem the dip has met a certain requirement to be able to go into the 2nd/3rd/final year of studies. Another scenario is that the pte diplomas are taken from the 1st year of that certain prestigious uni, and they were allowed to term it as a diploma on their own. Much like the NAFA concept where you stop at year 2, you get a dip, you finish year 3, you get a degree, which is what doodlegal explained.

    In Ms Sim's case, SMa's dip is offered in-hse which i believe they dun send it to ECU for grading/marking as it is offered in hse, like what i've mentioned above. If you actually go through the ECu's webby, you would find that none of the modules in the dip are part of the 16 degree modules if you take up the degree with ECU, which means that the dip is definitely an in-hse one, so there shldn't be any ECU logo. Perhaps you've mistaken the degree and the diploma? Local poly dips also have tie-ups with foreign unis to gain advance standing/direct entry to 2nd/3rd/final year, but you don't see any foreign uni's logo on local poly cert. It simply means that the dip has met the criteria to embark on ECU's degree program. Also, if you see that on top of the 16 core modules in ECU's list, there are 8 more minor/elective modules required before they consider you to have adhered to their degree awarding standards, thus this dip could have served as a minor/elective purpose.
    IMHO, your degree is not going to be affected as it's coming from ECU as the dip is most prolly just the minor/elective portion, and it's listed on APAC's website. However, if you still feel unsafe, do look up APAC website for other accredited certs to take up or even go to JCU/NUS where these are confirm recognised in the psychology industry. Mature candidates(over 21) with relavent working experiences will also be considered for local polytechnic admisions tho, do check with them.
