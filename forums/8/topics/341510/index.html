    Okay, first thing, it appears that you don't have a clear idea of what you've gotten yourself into.  The APS does not and has never approved universities - they only approve courses, and only degree (not diploma) courses at that.  You will have no recourse with the APS since they can't pull accreditation on a course they never accredited in the first place.

    You can approach your university student ombudsman who will investigate the case and maintain your confidentiality.  On the other hand, before you lodge any reports, you'd jolly well make sure that you have evidence to prove that these students are getting the marks without attending the exams.  If you want to send the ombudsman on a wild goosechase based upon nothing but unsubstantiated allegations, do so at your own peril.

    23 Dec 08, 19:39

    Hang on.  Are you doing a degree, or are you doing a diploma?

    If you're not doing a degree, forget the APS - they have nothing to do with it.

    In any case, you said you want a refund.  The APS cannot help you there.  In fact, the ombudsman probably won't be able to either.

    If it's a refund you're after, CASE seems to be the most useful body you can approach.  Frankly, I don't think your chances are good.  If you paid for a full year, I'd expect that you'd get one semester's fees back at most.

    One thing you might want to try is approaching the university with this issue.  It may be that they are not aware that the school in Singapore is doing this, and if so, they may back your demand for a refund to protect their reputation, and possibly terminate their affiliation with the school.

    I don't think it's fair to make a general conclusion about Australian universities on the basis of what's happened.  In my eight years at Australian universities I've never had anything like this happen to me.
