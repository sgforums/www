    SINGAPORE: Singapore has banned the import of pet birds from all the countries which have had a bird flu outbreak.
    The bird business has been badly hit and shops selling ornamental birds, however, have not fared as well.

    Despite all the bad news about bird flu, Singaporeans aren't chickening out of buying ornamental birds, say sellers.

    Yet Bernard Lim said his business has dropped by as much as 40%.

    That's simply because he can't sell some of the traditionally more popular birds from Thailand and Indonesia.

    "We used to sell about 1,000 birds each month for people to set free as a compassionate act. Now we still have buyers but we can't sell the birds," he said.

    But these restrictions aren't knee-jerk reactions.

    As early as October, bird importer and exporter Chua Thiam Chuan was ordered to stop bringing in birds from Indonesia.

    And then before the Lunar New Year, it was birds from Thailand.

    And on Tuesday, Mr Chua received yet another letter, this time banning birds from Pakistan.

    "AVA reacted very fast. Once the news of bird flu was out, we received a fax the next day telling us the birds were banned," he said.

    Before the birds are brought to the shops, they're quarantined twice.

    Once in their exporting countries and another time here in Singapore.

    Their stools are also checked by the AVA before they're declared safe for sale.

    "The birds are checked at the airport and if they're sick, AVA will tell us within a week that we can't sell them," said Mr Chua.

    So far there are 7 countries whose ornamental birds have been banned.

    So Mr Chua has turned to importing other more exotic pet birds from South Africa and Holland. - CNA
