    Oh no, my cow ate my diamonds

    Indian merchant recovers hundreds of tiny gems from cow's dung

    HIS efforts at cajoling the animal must have left him pooped.

    For three days, a diamond merchant in India made it his business to make his cow do its business.

    He fed the cow with fibre-rich food and laxatives to make it pass dung - for glittering within its tummy are many precious stones.

    On Wednesday, the cow ate a small bag full of tiny diamonds that had been left in a haystack in Limbudi, Gujarat - a state on the west coast of India, reported The Hindustan Times.

    Diamond merchant Dilubhai Rajput said the bag contained 1,722 small diamonds worth 40,000 rupees ($1,490).

    Mr Rajput said: 'Killing the cow would have angered animal lovers, so I tried a different way.

    'My employees and I fed the cow lots of grass, grain and fruit. With the help of a veterinary doctor, we gave it lots of laxatives,' he said.

    Three days of perseverance finally bore fruit when the cow dropped the precious dung.

    Mr Rajput and his men then groped the dung with their bare hands and managed to recover 310 gems from the fertile heap.

    The poo remains precious as it's still studded with over a thousand gems. But Mr Rajput is confident.

    He said: 'I am sure, within a week, I will retrieve all my diamonds from the cow.'
