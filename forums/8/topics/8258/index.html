    Well, Hata-San,

    When I am younger, I used to believe in the Christian God, then when I get older, I realize that I lack faith in God, so I become an atheist which is totally on the other side. But I suppose that for me now, I still adhere to the Christian virtues, which are also embraced in other faiths or moral philosphies.

    I don't know where there is really a proper distinction between good and evil in modern society. I felt that the moral code we have in these days is decided by the society of this time. An example will go like, people in China of the past condemns adultery and would punish the adulterous pair by throwing them into the river, but look at today, the matter is settled by divorce. In short, there seemed to be a moral degradation of values but I don't think so, because morals are so relative.

    So how does the discussion of morals come into the picture of retribution and karma? Well, believe it or not, the older I get, I realise that there is retribution and karma, based on the backlashes made by my own actions in the past. Perhaps, the question is that once a person suffer the retribution and acknowledges the matter in an repentant manner, he or she should move on. However, when I look back, some of the actions were not considered "bad" because there are people who would say that they would have done that as well.. So I guess, retribution and karma are more of the consequences based on a certain moral action which one has done in the past. The world is not really black or white but formed by shades of grey.

    After all the backlashes and of course, I have paid enough for the "bad" deeds which I have done, I suppose I just live my life happily in another way... I once asked myself recently suddenly while watching people passing the cafe by the window pane, whether I am happy, and then I looked again and smile,"I may not be eternally happy because I have not found that someone, but at least now I appreciate what I have and be contented with I have." In that sense, I would prefer to be nice to people and help them when needed and not expecting anything back... Well, I always greeted the waitress in Starbucks cafe in my town, and one day, they actually gave me free coffee.. That I thought was good karma at work...

    I do believe that one should not expect any rewards when doing good, hence there does not exist good karma, but retribution due to bad karma, yes, I do believe that.... Life is suffering, and that is how it keeps people going....

    Merely opinions of mine... Just two pennies worth..
