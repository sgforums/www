    i found a lor mai kai recipe

    Recipe

    Serves 6

    - 300g glutinous rice, soaked in lightly salter water. About 30 minutes will do or the rice will be too soft and too sticky. Wash it then drain
    - 250g of chicken thigh fillets minus the skin to reduce fat intake, Cut into bite sized strips. Season the chicken with 1 tbsp of dark soy sauce, 1 tsp of five spice powder and 1 tsp of sugar
    - 2 or 3 cloves of garlic peeled and minced
    - 1 medium sized onion, peeled and minced
    - 750 ml of stock. I personally go for the low-fat and low-sodium canned type
    - 50g of dried prawns, saoked- 2 tbsp of oil
    - 8 to 10 dried Chinese mushrooms soaked and stems removed. Cut into equal halves
    - 3 to 4 tbsps of dark soy sauce (not enough add later)
    - 1tbsp light soy sauce
    - 2 tsps of sugar
    - 2 tsps of sesame oil
    - salt and pepper to taste

    Methods
    - Place rice into preferably a round container with a flat base for even steaming. Then use high heat to steam it
    - Meanwhile, with 2 tbsp of oil, stir fry the chicken pieces with the mushrooms, garlic and onion till fragrant
    - Add in the stock and boil for a few minutes. Tip : try not to use up all the stock. Bring to boil for a few minutes and do prevent the stock from drying up by covering your cook-ware well
    - Add in the dried prawns (this is also the time to throw in sliced chinese sausage if you like but I omit this because sausages are basically not good for health)
    - Throw in the remaining ingredients to cook for a while. Also remember not to let the stock dry up so do close your cookware well & most importantly, the chicken should be cooked but not over cooked
    - Pour chicken mixture over the rice and mix them well. Add more stock if the mixture is too dry after mixing. - Finally steam rice until it is thoroughly cooked. Remember the earlier tip ?
    - To garnish, sprinkle chopped fresh chives or fried shallots but this is optional
