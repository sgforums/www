    Haha, I just wrote this huge post, but I didnt sign in. So I lost all of it.
    Basically, what do you guys think of the Singapore Education System?
    What do you think of GEP?
    Of streaming?
    Of PSLE?

    Personally, I think that streaming really shatters a person's confidence. Or (sometimes) it makes people think too well of themselves. I know that it's supposed to allow people to study at their own pace, but things such as intellect shouldnt be decided at such a young age. By streaming, you may sometimes be sending the message that someone isn't good enough. That very someone may lose confidence in him/herself, and may never release their full potential. Instead of living out who they could be, they live by how they've been labelled -- ex/ a failure, a loser, a "slow-learner". Everyone is gifted in their own way. They may be good at comforting people, at cooking, at singing, at art -- but these things usually arent taken into account when it comes to 'educating' people. I have nothing against people who go to prestigious schools, but thinking about it, those who enter are usually accepted due to their high PSLE scores -- basically, those who are good at math, science and english. What about those people who have talent in other areas? And is one test good enough to determine someone's intellect? I have this friend. She's really bubbly and kind and everything -- and she was once really good at math. Actually, I'm sure that she's still good at math -- but she's lost faith in herself. Why? 'Cause she didnt do too well in her PSLE. Her teacher was counting on her to get an A* and to get into some really good school. In the end, she only got 1 A* for her exam -- Science. She counted on math, but due to stress, she messed up on the examination. According to her, she forgot how to do all the really easy questions. In the end, she went to CHIJ -- St. Theresa (i think that's how you spell it), but she was really disappointed in herself. Now, she gets Bs and Cs. Sometimes, I wonder, if her PSLE score was good and she didnt lose faith, would she still be getting A*s? Also, I think that the PSLE creates a lot of division among students. I know that some students from different schools get along just fine, but some dont. Maybe it has something to do with how some people got better scores then others, thus leading to them getting into "better" schools? People slowly form this twisted impression that those in "less good schools" are gangsters (ah lians/ ah bengs), while those in "prestigious" schools are nerds with "elite uncaring faces". Though it may be true in some cases, it usually only generalises the minority. Don't you think all this division may be getting too far?
    Also, what is the purpose of the GEP? Isnt the GEP simply about allowing people with greater intellect to learn at a faster pace? If there is such a need, why cant the student simply skip a grade instead of being labelled with such a title? People may be slightly spiteful that they are younger, but at least they probably wont gain as many glares or weird looks as (I hear) some GEPs receive.
    I dont know how to end this, so .. yeahh.
    What's your opinion? xD

    Actually I think streaming to a certain extend is an advantage. Streaming allows student to know where they stand, and giving them the opportunities to set an even higher and better, of course realistic target/goals in their academic life. Having targets and goals set create an invisible motivational force which slowly push the students toward their goals. And when a student reaches its goal, (s)he will set an even more goal in the future which is positive.

    Singapore Education System works in a way in which you-get-what-subject-based-on-your-grades. After having PSLE, most of the students in Singapore are promoted to Secondary School. Apart from Gifted programmes, they were either stream into the Express or the Normal Academic/Technical. When comes Secondary Two, students were given a choice to what subjects combination they wanted. In the Express stream better student will have the Pure Sciences, whereas in the NA Science were given combined which required less detail in the syllabus. NT student will have basic science.

    I am a NA student with strong passion for science. Often many will regard me as the "Top Science Student" in NA. After all the glourious title etc, in the end I am only having combined science. Partial, I blame Singapore Education System for wasting my passion, effort, time and talent for Science. But nevertheless, I still blame myself for not getting into an Express Stream due to my average PSLE results. You see, I am a live example of the Education system in Singapore.

    The negative side of streaming not only entitle to my example. Streaming also cause student to loss confident in what they had, results. Academic declined students might develope negative thought when the results in the report book doesn't great. They will have thought like "I am so stupid", " I am dumbass" or "Why am I born retard" such thoughts. This is extremely unhealth and Sinagapore MOE are trying their best to solve this by recruiting more caring teachers/educators.

    Streaming is a must but definitely should be re-reviewed.
