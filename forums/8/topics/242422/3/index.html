    yup. one can use debit or credit or atm card overseas. It's the system you using.

    Credit cards uses eiither visa or mastercard system. In sg it doesn't matter because almost store that accepts visa accepts mastercard and vice vesa. Overseas is a different ballgame altogether. Some areas only accept visa OR mastercard.

    Debit uses the same set of systems.

    Our local ATM cards uses NETS system. So if you find a NETS overseas most properly you can use it too. But take care, some may be the same system but because some overseas bank uses a 5 digit pin instead of a 6 digit one, hence although the system is compatible but the hardware is unable to read from your card.

    Other systems overseas also include Cirrus, Mastro.

    My take, depending on which country you're going, it's always good to have 3 cards from 3 different banks for emergency uses and big ticket purchases. The best is still to use cash that you change over at the local money changers as the exchange rates are better than visa or mastercard's. (Yes, when you swipe overseas it's NOT at the banks' rate but Visa or master's)
