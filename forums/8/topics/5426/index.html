    Well....several things to deal with here.

    Firstly, about the girl. I dont know what she's like but perhaps she's just been maxxed out or tired lately. Too coincidental? Probably.

    Ok well on to the next possible reason. Youknow sometimes, dealing with a person who has a crush onyou may be just as hard as dealing with the feelings you expereince when you have a crush on someone, especially if you don't feel anything for the person. You still want to maintain the friendship, yet don't know how to stop him/her from liking you. While your not a bitch/bastard, you just want to settle everythin ASAP. Unfortunately, some people figure that the best way to do this is to make the person dislike you,then when everything's cool, try to fix the broken pieces of the damaged friendship. Not a very good method to use, but it's probably one of the most obvious.

    Some people handle openess and frankness well. Others react violently to surprises or when they're caught off guard. Maybe you told her too fast or at a wrong time (life's a bitch ain't it).Whatever the case, it's best to remedythe situation ASAP. Send her another email explaining yourself. Or call her. Coz right now, she prob thinks you're just another John after her. Shewon't have much of an impression till you tellher your side.

    Personally, I rank friendship above anything else. I had a crush on this girl who's like my best friend and I so messed everything up for a good 1.5 weeks coz I put her in a fix. So I sent a couple of super-long emails explaining everything and eventually everything was settled and it's back to normal,or perhaps better.

    What I'm saying is that if it's a crush, it'll come and go. If she doesn't like you now, don't jeopardise your friendship with her. Clear it up, and move on. You'll never know. Maybe fate will deal you a better hand in the future, but while r/s may not last, a solid friendship will.

    Plus, by building a friendship, you'll at least know her better.
