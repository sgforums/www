    6 Oct 07, 16:33
    The death penalty is meant as a strong deterrence.

    Put it this way. When we are taught criminal law, the most simplistic explanation for the purpose of criminal law is this: The purpose of criminal law is to preserve and protect law-abiding citizens and as a tool of social control. Penalties give a practical incentive to obey the law, and censure those who still choose to break the law.

    We do lock people up in hopes of rehabilitation. But realistically, its more for retribution. The other two reasons would be expiation and incapacitation. We are not as noble as we hope to be. And really, you can't really rehabilitate criminals by locking them up together. You'll more likely breed smarter criminals when they share tips on how to commit crimes.

    As for the death penalty, the only thing I feel is unfair in Singapore is that such cases are heard by only one judge. I personally believe that the jury system is best for such cases. It's been found that juries are typically much better at deciding questions fo fact, leaving the judge free to contend with questions of law. Also, think about it. 12 heads are better than 1. There will be more experience and juries allow community input into what we believe to be right or wrong.

    However, in Singapore, that's not going to happen. The next best thing we can have is to have a panel of 3 judges presiding with a majority verdict needed for conviction. While people may complain about the demands on the court system, I think it is only fair given the severity of punishment. Also, once the sentence is carried out, there is lesser by way of compensation if it were a wrongful conviction. I personally believe it is rather frightening that just one person will decide your guilt.
