    Singapore's first women's Everest team raises funds for climb
    By Valarie Tan, Channel NewsAsia | Posted: 12 July 2008 2354 hrs

    SINGAPORE : Singapore's first all women's Everest team has so far raised only 20 per cent of their S$1 million budget to scale the world's highest peak.

    The six-women team was to have climbed Mount Everest in March this year, but a shortage of funds delayed their trip.

    As part of their fundraising efforts, the team is now conducting team-building workshops for women and youths. Their latest workshop was for the cabin crew of SilkAir, which is sponsoring the Everest team's plane tickets.

    The group is now planning to attempt the climb in March next year, but fundraising continues to be an uphill task.

    Jane Lee, team leader of the Singapore Women's Everest Team, said: "Few people believe that a group of women can stick together for so long to climb a mountain, and I think fewer people believe that a group of women are capable of climbing a mountain together.

    "But I feel that our string of successful expeditions have proven that as a team, we are performing and we are capable. And as the first women Everest team, we will make it next year when we go up Mount Everest."

    More information on the team's efforts is available at this     website. - CNA /ls
