    RIP to them all

    In regards to the life-jacket issue, I found the article in todays ST in which their Dragonboat coach talked about the incident quite thought-provoking.
    He reckons that if the whole team had worn life-jackets, then there would have been more casualties.

    This is because he says that the only reason that some of the rowers stuck under the pontoon were able to escape is because they managed to sink/swim down to the deeper waters where underlying currents propelled them out and away from the pontoon. With lifejackets, it is likely that they would have been sucked under the pontoon, but trapped at the surface where they would surely suffocate.
    I think his theory may indeed be correct, and while life jackets would definitely be helpful in instances such as if a rower fell out of the boat in the middle of the river, they might have indeed caused more casualties if the rowers had still been sucked under the pontoon. Just my $0.02
