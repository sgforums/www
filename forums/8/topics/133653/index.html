    Background... As Singapore used to be part of the british colony, most of the professional and legal framework is based on English format etc. So in Singapore there are a few professional bodies that are offering courses in finance.

    Diploma

    LCCI (London Chamber of Commerce and Industries). The basic of the accounting... simple book keeping. Can get exemption from further studies.

    ACCA, CAT (Association of Chartered Certified Accountants, Certified Accounting Technician). Dip program by ACCA.

    Degree and degree equilvalent

    ACCA. The largest Accounting body in UK.

    CIMA (Chartered Institute of Management Accountants). Finance qualification with a focus on Management.

    ***************

    The biggest private school offering the lessons for the courses above are:

    SAA (Singapore Accounting Academy - the training branch of ICPAS{Institute of Cerified Public Accountants of Singapore})

    FCT - Private school, with good lecturers (last time i heard).

    You have to find out for yourself their schedules and what are the respective schools strenghts in terms of subject teach.

    ***************

    Depends on your current qualifications, u may not be able to go directly to a degree / ACCA / CIMA or others related programs... so u must start off with a diploma and work then work your way there. This will take at least 2 yrs for the diploma... longer if you repeat the subject.

    If u r looking for a good qualifications with minimum investments, ACCA, CIMA are good choices as they do not required a lot of investment in monetary terms. However, if you want certainty in completion... an overseas degree is a better bet.

    ACCA and CIMA has the reputation of failing ppl that is not up to the mark. I have known people that are in the programs for more than 7 yrs.

    Dip --> Aussie degree (major accounting) --> Aussie CPA --> S'pore CPA

    ACCA (all levels must clear) --> convert to S'pore CPA

    LCCI is all below Dip (local) level.
