    Gedanken is German for thought, and a gedanken experiment is one that is conducted in principle (rather than in practice) in order to clarify an idea.

    Arguably the most famous gedanken experiment was proposed by physicist Erwin Schroedinger in 1935. To cut a long story short, he described a box with a cat in it. The lid of the box is rigged with a vial of prussic acid, which would break if the lid was to be lifted. To find out if the cat was alive or dead, one would have to lift the lid, killing the cat. The moral of the story is that the very act of observation can determine the result.
