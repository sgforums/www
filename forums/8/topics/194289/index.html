    Driver attacked in $1.5m phone robbery dies
    Stephanie Yap
    6 June 2006
    Straits Times
    English
    (c) 2006 Singapore Press Holdings Limited
    THE lorry driver who was bludgeoned for his $1.5 million

    cellphone cargo died in hospital yesterday morning .

    Mr Wan Cheon Kem's injuries were so severe that an eight-hour

    operation to remove shards of his shattered skull could not save

    him, said family members.

    Meanwhile, the last two of the 10 suspects in the case

    surrendered to the police yesterday.

    Mr Wan, 46, was attacked by robbers on May 30 while he was

    driving along Changi Coast Road. He was found slumped on the

    dashboard of his parked lorry at Pasir Ris Park.

    His mother and siblings, who kept vigil by his side in the past

    week at Changi General Hospital, were still in shock when The

    Straits Times spoke to them at his wake at Sin Ming Drive last

    night.

    His brother Richard, 48, said: 'He never smoked, never drank. He

    has been working as a driver for more than 10 years with no

    problem. Do you think being a driver sounds dangerous?'

    Mr Wan, who was single, lived with his mother and a younger

    sister in a flat in Pasir Ris. He was described as his mother's

    favourite and her constant companion.

    Mr Richard Wan said of their mother: 'When she first heard what

    happened, she really couldn't take it. She cried a lot.'

    At the wake last night, the widow in her 70s broke down at her

    son's coffin, and appeared to be whispering her last goodbyes to

    him as her four other children gathered protectively around her.

    Police say that two men - Nakamuthu Balakrishnan, 48, and Arsan

    Krishnasamy Govindarajoo, 38, who allegedly bashed Mr Wan on the

    head - may now face murder charges. They were initially charged

    with armed robbery before Mr Wan died.

    Eight others have been arrested in connection with the robbery.

    Of these eight, two - both aged 23 - may also face murder

    charges. The last to be arrested, the pair had evaded the law

    for almost a week before they turned themselves in when cornered

    by police raids yesterday morning.

    Of the other half dozen men, five could also face similar

    charges pending investigations, while one has been arrested but

    not yet charged.

    One of the 10 men was Mr Wan's colleague from the freight

    forwarding company where he worked.

    Ragu Ramajayam, 36, was charged on Friday with conspiracy to

    rob. He is also accused of tipping off the robbers earlier that

    morning.

    Another of Mr Wan's colleagues, a 36-year-old manager with the

    company who wanted to be known only as Mr Lim, spoke fondly of

    him as a cheerful, hardworking and helpful man, and said that he

    had appeared to be on good terms with Ramajayam.

    However the case plays out, the dead man's family hopes that the

    punishments meted out will have a strong deterrent effect.

    Said Mr Richard Wan: 'We want justice, and hope it is a severe

    punishment. This will set a precedent.'
