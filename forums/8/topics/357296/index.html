    Serial molester slapped with 32-year jail term By Claire Huang, 938LIVE | Posted: 24 April 2009 1922 hrs SINGAPORE: A man has been slapped with a 32-year jail term for outraging the modesty of two minors, aged nine and 14. Huang Shiyou, who was a National Serviceman at the time of the offence, pleaded guilty to five charges of terrorising the two girls. The 22-year-old will also receive the maximum 24 strokes of the cane for his offences. Another nine charges have been taken into consideration for sentencing purposes. All in, Huang is accused of preying on nine girls aged nine to 14, over a period of 16 months. Huang's father, who was seated in the public gallery, was clearly devastated as he bowed his head and tried hard to stifle his emotions. Meanwhile, Huang, who was in the dock, stared blankly into space. The harshness of his sentence hasn't sunken in as yet, it seems. Perhaps Huang never thought he would end up behind bars. The Temasek Polytechnic graduate was a brilliant student who topped his class several times. According to his lawyer Mr Chia Kok Seng, Huang had won several scholarships when he was studying and people who knew him described him as a "responsible" and "well-brought up boy". When he was enlisted into the army, Huang's leadership qualities and sheer diligence saw him rising through the ranks. He became the second in command in his section as a regimental policeman. But behind the rising star was a tortured soul. The court heard that Huang's troubles started at the age of 17. His parents had disapproved of his then girlfriend and gave him the cold shoulder. Later on, Huang found out that his girlfriend was unfaithful and was dumped by her. In his mitigation, Mr Chia said his client got so depressed that he attempted suicide by slashing his wrists. A psychiatric report stated that the depressed Huang was suffering from chronic adjustment disorder. But the prosecution pointed out that everyone gets depressed at some time and this didn't warrant a crime spree. The prosecution added that the mental report stated that Huang knew what he was doing at the time of the offences. In urging for a severe sentence, the prosecution told the court that the accused had planned his offences and preyed on multiple young victims. What made it worse was the fact that Huang had used a pen knife to pressure five of his nine victims into submission. In asking the judge to show some mercy, Mr Chia said that his client is remorseful and understands the gravity of his crimes. He explained that the trauma of Huang's failed relationship coupled with the stress of his stormy family ties led to his downfall. But this argument clearly didn't cut any ice with Justice Chan Seng Onn, who noted that Huang had systematically and deliberately targeted young victims. He added that the sentence given has to deter Huang from re-offending and also reflect the gravity of his acts. When asked by the media if they would appeal, Mr Chia said it depends on Huang's wishes. - 938LIVE.
