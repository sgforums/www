  • loti39
    18 Oct 06, 13:57
    Actually one of the reasons why it seems hard to find a room in sg is because we don't have a culture of moving out

    Most of us stay with our parents until we get married.. and for those people getting married.. they work out the numbers and its always cheaper to buy a place than rent one! Little demand.. so the residential rental market not very developed.. and asians like their privacy.. also alot of them got free rooms also dun want to rent to strangers..

    Having said that its not a dead market. I am renting my place too. Some tips is to look for ads placed on supermarket (eg cold storage) classified boards.. there is always at least 1 notice on rooms for rent!!

    the place I am staying at now is quite nice.. all minimal furnishings available and landlord not staying here so no restriction on visitors etc.. but too bad all 3 rooms are occupied

  • loti39
    18 Oct 06, 14:53
    Housing agents will definitely be helpful... but fact is alot of people who have rooms to rent do not go to housing agents! Cause usually the commission is paid by the landlord.. and most of the time they are not in a big hurry to lease out their room plus there are so many "free" advertising methods out there.

    And some housing agents are also not interested to do rentals, especially if only a room as the commission is very little and they may have to bring the client around to alot of places before a suitable one is found. so not worth it.

    Try the ad boards in the shopping centres in town or expat areas such as Holland V.. with your price range you will definitely be able to find something...
