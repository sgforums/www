    As usual, these are tough questions, and I only have viewpoints to some of them.

    Planning for stuff is usually good. But you can't plan for everything, and shit happens. In addition to planning, which is of course important, you need to be flexible to tackle whatever crap comes up along the way.

    As for children going astray, if there were clear answers, there probably won't be any trouble today. But the fact is, there are so many factors in young people going astray, and lack of parental support is only one of the predisposing factors. I say predisposing factors because not everyone who doesn't have someone there when they go home turns into young monsters.

    Some time ago Mind posted the topic "Upbring of Children," but its a pity that the response is not good. Probably because not many people have had actual experience in that, so no point talking about it.

    Still, I think women and men will never play the same roles in life. I feel that they can have same responsibilities i.e. bread winner, but as far as the roles are concerned, they are never meant to be the same. For example, you can't expect a female to instill male qualities in a child, because she isn't a male.
