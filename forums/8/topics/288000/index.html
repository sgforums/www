    Ex-TV host Benedict Goh jailed 3 weeks for drink driving
    By Claire Huang

    Goh was given a custodial sentence of 3 weeks' jail, disqualified from driving all classes of vehicles for 4 years and fined $800. -- ST PHOTO: LAU FOOK KONG

    FORMER TV host Benedict Goh, 37, has been jailed 3 weeks after pleading guilty to drink driving and colliding into a stationary car.
    In court on Friday, he was also disqualified from driving all classes of vehicles for 4 years.

    For colliding into a broken down vehicle, Goh was fined $800.

    And his lawyer Mr Lee Teck Leng said Goh is remorseful.

    'Benedict deeply regrets the offences and acknowledges that drink driving is very irresponsible, and he hopes others will learn from his mistakes and not commit drink driving.'

    Mr Lee added they have filed an appeal in court 'because there's another case with similar facts that is actually on appeal right now'.

    He said that they felt it 'appropriate' to 'safeguard Benedict's interests by actually filing a notice of appeal', so as 'to see how that other appeal turns out' and take it into consideration.

    District Judge Terence Chua when delivering judgement said that drink driving is a serious offence, and 'the number of such incidents has been rising steadily'. He also noted that 'drink driving cases constitute a third of the traffic cases mentioned in court'.

    Concluding that this is not acceptable, he said that the court must approach sentencing holistically.

    A drink driving conviction calls for a mandatory jail term and fine.

    The judge added he took into consideration other mitigation factors - there were no injuries, Goh gave his full cooperation and did not flee the scene. It was also clearly an accident in which Goh was taken by surprise.

    However, the fact that Goh was not able to avoid hitting a stationary vehicle even though he was driving a safe distance away, the judge said, meant he was impaired by alcohol.
