    This is the email i sended to STforum, Today, TNP and CASE. Thank you all for the supports and advices.

    I am sending in this letter to share this very bad experience on
    Valentine's Day with this following florist that I engaged for a delivery
    service.

    On 30th Jan 2005, I went to Xpressflower.com @ Nature's House -
    Century Square, located at the ground floor of Century Square, to
    place a delivery order. I ordered 6 stalks of sunflower. Due to the
    fact that my delivery date was on 14th Feb, which is a peak season,
    all the charges were increased. The whole package cost $91.50. I paid
    on the spot by NETS. Also, I requested that I would pass a card to
    them 1 week later to be delivered together. 1 week later, I went back
    with the chosen card in red envelop and my receipt with Order No on
    it, and I passed it to the staff.

    On 14th Feb night itself, I called up the lady who was supposed to
    receive the bouquet of sunflower to check whether she liked it. And I
    found out she had not received it. I reminded her to inform me if she
    received it the next morning.

    On 15th Feb early afternoon, I received an sms from this lady saying
    that nothing was delivered. I called up the given service number to
    find out what happened, at 12pm. A female staff answered. I gave her
    my Order number and mobile number to verify. I was told by her that 1
    of their trucks broke down yesterday so they were not able to deliver
    on time. I was angry and disappointed at that time because they did
    not even bother to give me a call to inform me about this, and if I
    never checked with that lady, then they might as well ignore my
    delivery!

    This is very irresponsible. This female staff asked me if they could
    deliver it on that day. I was frustrated and could not decide at that
    time, so I requested her to call me back in 30 minutes time. However,
    no one called me after that. So I called again at 1pm, and talked to
    that female staff again. I requested them to deliver on that day
    before during working hour, which she quoted to be before 5pm. At the
    same time I requested to be given discount because the order was not
    delivered on time and I was paying a peak season charge. I was
    rejected, the reason given is, the following day was also considered
    peak season to them. She could only offer me an extra box of
    chocolate. As I was too frustrated to argue, I accepted the offer.
    Then, I mentioned my card in red envelope, and asked them to make sure
    it is delivered together. She replied that she would look for it.

    At 5.20pm, I received a missed call from this florist. When I called
    back, I was informed by this female staff that she was just able to
    find my card and hence the bouquet was not delivered yet! She asked if
    they could deliver to the residential location. To avoid any
    inconvenience, I gave her the recipient's contact number (the one on
    the invoice was wrong), so that she can arrange with the lady
    directly.

    At around 7.50pm, I called up to check how the status. A guy answered.
    And I found that it seemed that female staff never contacted the
    recipient and nothing was arranged! Once again, this guy, with better
    telephone etiquette, requested the recipient's contact and promised to
    settle it ASAP, and he will call me back.

    And the fact is, after 1 whole day, I am still waiting for the return call now.

    Just when the Singapore Tourism Board is touting Singapore as a
    service- oriented industry, the service rendered by the aforementioned
    florist not only was horrible, but wholly unacceptable.

    No call to apologize when they were not able to deliver their service
    to a fully paid customer. No sincerity to offer any compensation,
    apologies for any damage caused.

    The follow-up was terrible. No coordination between staff taking over
    duty, because obviously the guy knew nothing about my case.

    I hope my letter can raise the awareness of such rampant and
    unprofessional services.
