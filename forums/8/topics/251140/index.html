    you're talking about 3D pictures or stereo pictures. this one's not one of them, coz it doesn't contain any 'hidden' picture. for it to work, the same pattern must appear at least twice horizontally. if you look carefully at this picture, the pattern doesn't really repeat.
    there are a few types of 3D pictures. one of them is generated by computers, another type are 3D photos taken by stereo cameras.

    the computer generated type looks like repeated pattern, and works by incorporating two pictures into one. they are the same picture with a very slight variation in perspective, so that one of them is meant to be picked up by each of your eyes. these are easier to see, becoz the two pictures are just closely laid over each other. the method to see these is to focus slightly beyond the surface of the picture. they only work with very simple outlines, and don't show complicated textures or colors, but there are programs that convert any simple graphics into 3D pictures.

    3D photos on the other hand, are two photos lined side by side. they are the same shot taken with a slight angular difference. there are two methods to see these. you can either force your pupils together (which strains your eyes alot) and superimpose them in your vision, or to use a special lens that helps to seperate your left eye's vision from the right.
