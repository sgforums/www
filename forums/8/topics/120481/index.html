    Love and Education are two things. If they can separate school and relationship, there's nothing wrong with it.

    A person who educates for a living must remember that his responsibility is to educate students and thus show no favoritism in his work. He must remember that he sets an example for the rest to follow as the students take after him. If he's found exploiting his position for personal gains and harbours favoritism, he has failed in his work and should not be granted stay.

    A student who attends lesson must see lessons as lessons, not a place to enjoy her personal relationship with her mentor. Failing in that, the student has failed to respect the mentor, and should not be granted stay.
