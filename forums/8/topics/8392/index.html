Which era you want to live in? An Inquiry into Values

  • prodigal
    19 Jul 00, 08:12
    Dear all,

    Originally, I wanted to post this in Faroutnews, because the response there might be more interesting. But nevertheless, I thought what I am writing would not be something good. So I decided to flood the gates of Chit Chat with my long essay again. If there is someone whom I should dedicate this piece of essay to, it would be MayaJade. It was out of a recent totally different conversation which gave me the inspiration to write such an essay. If u people think that it is good enough, you can always crosspost it to FON for me.

    I start this essay about the era which we lived in. The kind of uncertainties, threatened with things which could wipe out the entire human race. Well, if you think that nuclear holocaust is not the worse case scenairo, there will be always exist a deadly virus Ebola, and we have global warming, a huge asteroid roaming around us, and even war can break out anytime, if one idiot would press the button. I was thinking of the recent Camp David talks on the affairs of Israel and Palestine, and somehow it lead me to make a relation of our values system to the era which we lived in.

    So, let me use a more simple way of explaining of our era. We lived in a time where people are very mobile and can move around, thanks to globalisation. Because there exists new modes of communication ranging from telephone to even ICQ or IRC, so people are trangressing the boundaries and we have more new ways of living our lives. The problem with advancement in civilization is that it progresses with good things as well as brings in problems. As George B. Shaw once put it,"Science never solves a problem without creating ten more." Similarly, technology has the kind of double-edged weapon which can go both ways.

    So, coming back to the question where I begin from. Which era would I want to live in? I would prefer to live in the old days of Victorian age or medival times (the days of Camelot), or in the Tang Dynasty where literature and enlightenment are reached. Because a hand written letter is always more sincere than an electronic mail. Why did I pick this kind of time? Maybe I find the values in that time appealing and perhaps, the world is not complicated as before. I have done both good and bad in my life, and when I look back, I still find, finding my own principles and integrity means I have some value or worth to live. I could degenerate after doing bad, but I learned to understand life consists of a series of trials which mould me, and then it could mean to struggle against adversity.

    One could always argued against me saying that the moral code is a set of values which are set and conditioned by the society. I agree with that, but what I am trying to argue here, is that society is degrading because our world is being exposed to more and more possible dangers induced by the way we deal with the environment and people, and we begin to condone more and more sins. Chivalry, or perhaps, Junzi (the Confucian code of gentleman) is dead. I don't abide by that kind of code, but I already struggling with my own values against the tide of the world. To survive is to adapt, and sometimes, I have to yield when I make political decisions. So can we still have honor in the way we see the world? I still believe in honor, even I lost it before in a time. We lived in an age where anti-heroes are worshipped and you see poor kids in a high school in Colorado got shot to death, because of some nice hollywood movie, where u see a static model of Keanu Reeves holding a gun and shooting around to kill. It reflects the way we view society. So, I might be old fashioned or too principled in making such a claim.

    So, at the end of the day, I appeal in this essay to inquire into our values. Can we really condone adultery or some clown who blabbers about beding his wife's sister? Can we condone people who do bad deeds but glorified by gangster movies?

    Well, I hope to hear opinions, perhaps, if very least, tell me which era you would want to live in.....

    Have a good day.

    yours sincerely,
    The Prodigal
