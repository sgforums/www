    I noticed many students are obsessed with studies and grades. Most of our academic system have been geared towards narrowly judging and streaming students. Very often, we forget that the most important purpose of education is to bring out the best in students by enabling them to discover whatever latent talent they have, be it academic or otherwise, and developing those talents to the fullest.

    So for those of you who are stressed out in school, I hope you will go all out to try out new things, make friends and discover whatever talents you have. Learn to like the subjects you're studying. Forget the teacher if he sucks. Forget your grades if they suck. Find out the things you really like to do. Find out the things you are really good at. Put your hearts and mind into doing those things and may you find your bearings in life. Life is not a sprint event, it's a fucking long marathon. Just keep running in the right direction and you'll get to wherever you're headed. Eventually.

    Originally posted by Ito_^:
    i dun think u stand a chance at being a lawyer with a bad grade.. do ya?
    If you're talking about A levels results. Sure, if you didn't make the minimum, you wouldn't even go through to the interview.

    But let's start with the beginning.

    Impressions and Perceptions
    ---------------------------
    Why do you want to be a lawyer ? Do you like the professsion because of the glamourous image projected by the media ? Do want to be one because you've heard about top-notch lawyers charging $1000 an hour ? Do want to be one because you have a sense of justice and would like to help the underprivileged, the unjustly persecuted ? Do you think lawyers have it easy making tonnes of money from helpless clients ?

    Reality
    ------
    How many lawyers do you know personally ? Have you talked to them ?
    Do you know that a high proportion of lawyers in Singapore quit the profession within the first 5 years ? Do you know how hard young lawyers work ? Do you know some of them work through to the early morning hours, even over weekends ?

    Perceptions and realities of any specific professions aside, I mentioned you need to find out what you are good at, and what you really love doing. Hopefully the two will be the same, but it's not always the case. When it isn't, you have to decide which to choose and continue trying (either the one you love or the one you are good at) or to move on to finding something else. That's the finding your bearings part. That's the hard part
