    Singapore banks 'worried about talent exodus'

    SINGAPORE - Singapore banks are worried about a talent exodus this year, says a survey of senior executives in the finance industry by recruitment firm Robert Half and the Institute of Certified Public Accountants of Singapore.

    The findings show that nearly eight in 10 top decision-makers in the banking and financial sector are fearful that their best people will be poached by a competitor in the next 12 months.

    The concern is most acute among large firms that employ more than 1,000 staff, with about 90 per cent of them worried about their best employees leaving, the banking and financial services salary guide found.

    The survey also found that finding suitable replacement for a finance and accounting position takes longer here compared with other major markets in Asia. A management position takes 6.5 weeks to fill in Singapore, compared with 5.9 weeks in Shanghai, 6.4 weeks in Hong Kong and 4.9 weeks in Japan.

    Survey findings also showed that more than one-third of banks and financial services companies plan to hire in the first half of this year, compared with one-quarter of companies in other industries.

    About half of those surveyed expect salaries to rise in the next 12 months. Those in the financial services and banking sector expect salaries to increase by an average of 5.7 per cent, while those in the finance and accounting industry expect salaries to rise by 4.7 per cent on average.

    The findings were based on surveys with 300 C-level executives in Singapore.
