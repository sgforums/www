    CHARLES WINS IN LOVE BUT WILL HE LOSE THE CROWN?

    Britons okay with Charles marrying Camilla but want William to be king

    LONDON - THE heir to the British throne may have won public support for his engagement to lover Camilla Parker Bowles but he faces an uphill struggle to convince the nation he should one day be king, according to a poll.

    Two-thirds of Britons accept Prince Charles' plans to marry Camilla, his mistress during his turbulent marriage to the late Princess Diana, according to a YouGov survey in the Daily Telegraph.

    But a narrow majority wants the monarchy to skip a generation, with Queen Elizabeth handing the crown to her grandson Prince William, 22, on her death or abdication.

    Royal watchers say Charles, 56, would never consider such a move, which would overturn the centuries-old convention of the monarch's eldest son assuming the throne.

    Aware of public misgivings over his lover, he has ruled out Camilla's becoming queen once he becomes king.

    The Telegraph's survey was conducted within hours of Thursday's announcement that Charles is to marry mother-of-two Camilla, 57, on April 8.

    It found 41 per cent would prefer William to be the next monarch, with 37 per cent favouring Charles. A similar poll in November 2002 gave Charles 48 per cent and William 28 per cent.

    Still, questions of the royal succession have been overshadowed by newspaper debate about Camilla's new title, her wedding dress and the engagement ring.

    'Love has conquered all,' the top-selling Sun tabloid said. 'Charles is entitled to happiness in his life with a woman he has loved for so long.' British papers carried pictures of the diamond and platinum ring which a beaming Camilla showed off with Charles at Windsor Castle, where they will marry in April.

    Charles was divorced in 1996 from Diana, who once said 'there were three of us in this marriage', referring to her husband's affair with Camilla, whom he met in 1970.

    Meanwhile, The Australian Republican Movement - which seeks to sever ties with the British monarchy - said it had been overwhelmed with new membership applications since news of the wedding plans broke.
