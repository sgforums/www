    2006-04-13
    (6 out of 8 people found this review helpful.)
    about what a lot of the reviewers are saying: chaiyya chaiyya (that hindi song that everyone's talking about) is indeed on the soundtrack, it is listed as the last song. Also, this is the remix for the Inside Man film and not the original song from Dil Se (the original hindi movie that the song is from). Either way, watching Dil Se is pretty cool, the cast actually dance on the top of a moving train (no harnesses or anything) for the music video to this song.

    Otherwise, this is a great album (and music) to go with a mediocre film. I was surprised to find so many songs that just stuck out like a sore thumb in a movie (in a good way), just the whole way chaiyya chaiyya was used for the opening and closing credits was a light surprise. It just...fit so well...

    More information about "Chaiyya Chaiyya"
    2006-04-07
    (21 out of 23 people found this review helpful.)
    For those of you who liked "Chaiyya chaiyya"... I have been a fan of AR Rahman since he entered the Indian music scene in 1992 with the soundtrack for Roja. He revolutionized Indian film music and has composed soundtracks for many Tamil and Hindi films (as well as the West End/Broadway musical "Bombay Dreams" and the recently openned stage version of LOTR in Toronto). If you liked "Chaiyya chaiyya", I suggest you try listening to some of the following Hindi soundtracks: Dil Se.. (the rest of the songs besides "chaiyya" are also amazing), Lagaan, Roja, Bombay, Swades, and Taal to begin with... There are many other great albums but these are some of the best.
