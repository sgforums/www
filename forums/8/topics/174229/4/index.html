    Originally posted by Fatum:
    how's the maintenance ah ? ... and the initial setup ? ...

    I'm keeping only fresh water fishes at the moment ...
    Well the amount of equipment and maintenance really depends on the type of lifestock you intend to keep in your tank.

    If you're keeping only fishes, you can use the same setup as a freshwater tank. Liverocks should be added to the tank as they provide a large surface area for beneficial bacteria to host. Note that liverocks must be added to a tank filled with saltwater one month before the first fish is added.. otherwise you'll experience the 'New Tank Syndrome', in which the ammonia levels in your water rocket sky high and kill the fishes inside.
    The major cost in keeping a saltwater tank is the water. Cos you've got to get the saltmix to create your saltwater, both for the initial setup and for the fortnightly water changes, and saltmixes are expensive. Some people get around this by getting their saltwater from the sea, doing their own filtration before adding the water into the tank.

    If you're planning to keep corals/anemones, then its a different story altogether. In order for corals to survive, you need:
    1) Adequate lighting (ordinary fluorescent lamps are wholly inadequte!)
    2) Tip top water quality (You'll need this expensive device called a protein skimmer, to constantly remove nitrogenous waste products from the water.)
    3) Chiller (this is to keep the water temperature at a constant 26-29 degrees). This is necessary as the high-output lamps used tend to heat up the water by quite a bit.
    Such a setup can cost upwards of $1000.

    One thing about keeping a saltwater tank... Rust. Anything that can rust (including the tank's stand) will rust, 1000x faster than for a freshwater tank.
