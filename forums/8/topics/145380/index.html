  • About A GaL
    1 Aug 05, 07:49
    After I immigrated to Canada in 1991 I immediately renounced my Sickgapore "bandage" ...citizenship I was obviously not a citizen of Canada yet. At the time Sillypore allowed us to renounce the bondage without having to prove that we are citizen of another country.

    I took out all my CPF to buy my bangulow in Edmonton...there was no admin charges ..or may be a few $ of fee to pay...I can't remember.

    Later when my mother suddenly died of stroke in Sickgapore, I was indeed "stateless" and it took me almost 10 days to get a "certificate of identity" from Canada to allow me to travel under a special pass only to Singapore...not even allowed to transit via USA.

    But let me tell you.: "It was worth the risk!" The freedom from bondage was and is sweater than the inconvience of being a stateless.

    Last week I just returned from Singapore. After an absence of almost 10 years from Singapore this recent visit opened my eyes and convinced me that I made the right choice to immigrate to Canada.

    All the taxi drivers (about 10) who drove us complained that life is tough in Leegapore. Even the toilet attendent we met in a building in Orchard Rd. complained. Ten yrs ago my group of S'porean friends asked me "why I need to immigrate?" Now the same group I met again, told me that I made the right decision to leave Sillypore. Many of them after being laid off started their engineering cos. They have to go to Malaysia,Vitnam to search for business. there is none in Sinkgapore. They told me they try to remain small to be out of the Temasick radar screen....thus need not have to compete in tendering with the Mdm Ho's group.

    The heat alone in Sickgapore is enough to drive me and my wife mad. We lost all our appitite to eat and did not even want to move around. It just suck our energy out. We visited Jurong Bird Park and the Hap Par villa..it melted us. My daughter age 17 refused to walk ...too hot for her. But funny enough my Canadian-born son, age 9 seemed to enjoy the heat. He was running like a rabbit.

    In Canada...city of Edmonton our temp is from 25 (day) to 16 degrees (night)...just in Genting Highland....we lost it.
