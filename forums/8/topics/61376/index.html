But if an actual T.rex is capable of running above 40kph, no matter how rational or easy to believe our theories may be. They are wrong.

    In the end, science... is just a tool. We use this tool to do a job, and that is discovery. We do not get hung up on the tool and start defining our entire lives by it.

    Ultimately there are several things I can observe about this universe and to a limited extent, observe.

    Our universe is finite, it is limited physically and governed by laws.

    Everything in our universe has to have a cause to exist, that is, something must cause them to exist. Dust forming planets, stars, the like.

    We are by nature, highly limited and finite things, hence forth our view of truth and the universe is greatly finite as well.

    Hence, ultimately a finite universe but be caused to exist... it cannot simply exist and the laws simply cannot define ourselves.

Unfortunately, this first cause is obviously not subject to our laws of physics or universe, and hence cannot be comprehensively known or discovered through systems that are using these natural laws. Science is one of them.

    Therefore, we cannot find out about this first cause using science as it would obviously be unscientific and the results gathered would not suffice.

    What to make of it? In the end, one needs to realize that we are not just here for here's sake. Something caused all this entire reality into being, and no matter how far we backtrack, we realize that in the end something ultimate, that does not require to be caused to exist put all this finite system into motion.
