Add Some Colour To Your Life

  • Hydro
    26 Sep 03, 03:26
    Here are some great reasons to add colour to your life.

    The next time you're feeling low, try some colour therapy instead of popping an aspirin. Colour can do wonders to improve your mood and make you feel great about yourself.

    Colours can help you de-stress which is great for your overall health, and applied or worn in the right way it can add verve and joy to any aspect of your life - from your home, to your make-up, and even your wardrobe.

    Here's how colour therapy works:

    Colour Your Home

    Colours in your home are the ones you have to live with for a long time, so you want to make sure that they're the right colours for your moods.

    Work up an appetite in your kitchen with yellow. This colour of sunshine, is warm, welcoming, and the perfect way to start your day.

    Colour Your Wardobe

    If you have a big project that requires your creative juices to be flowing, opt to wear blue. This cool, relaxing colour, not only has the ability to calm those with frayed nerves, but it's also known to up your creativity.

    For a hot date, turn the heat on with red. This excitable and energetic colour ignites passion, and promotes sexual desire.

    Colour Your Food

    It's no surprise that the brightest coloured foods are the ones that are best for you. Bright green vegetables like brocolli, spinach and kale are known to reduce the risk of certain cancers, and contain folic acid which keeps cells healthy.

    Red and orange fruits and vegetables like tomatoes, carrots, and mangoes, contain lycopene which is also an anti-oxidant. Carrots contain beta-carotene which is great for vision and gives your skin a healthy glow.

    Colour Your Office

    You may not have control over the colour of the walls in your office, but that doesn't mean you can't inject some colour into a drab and un-inspirational work environment.

    Green - the colour of luck, compassion, and harmony is the easiest colour to throw into your workspace. A green plant can go a long way to uplifting your mood and calming your senses during a busy workweek.

    It's also great for eye strain. If you've been staring at a computer, or reading a document for too long, gaze upon your green plant for awhile, and relax those corneas.

    Colour Your Relationships

    If your love life is going through a bit of a rut, perk it up with some pink. Red may increase your sex drive, but pink will get the romance going.

    Pink flowers placed strategically as a centrepiece at your dinner table is bound to create some warm-feelings, and romantic sparks.

    If you've had a fight with a loved one, go for purple as a peacekeeping gift. Purples and violets are known to have a balancing effect, and is known to inspire contemplation.
