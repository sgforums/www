    Originally posted by vito_corleone:
    graduating from one of their universities as a skilled professional....thats my plan at least
    That's the route I took. After NS, I came Stateside to pursue a postgrad degree, with every intention of returning to Singapore upon graduation.

    However, several years in a foreign country changes one's perspective of Singapore. And not necessarily for the better.

    Just prior to graduating in '91, I was offered a job by an IT firm that was willing to sponsor an H1-B work visa application on my behalf; I've been here since then, and am currently a PR with a pending citizenship application.

    As for marketable job skills, IT used to be the hot ticket here back in the nineties, until the bubble burst around 2001. Since then, corporate downsizing and outsourcing have put a significant dent in that job market, although it is slowly recovering.

    The nursing profession, however, remains in overdrive due to the continuing shortage of qualified nurses in this country. A foreign student graduating with a nursing degree is almost guaranteed a job here.

    So if you don't mind being a nurse...
