    Coca-Cola files lawsuit over a recall in Japan

    By SCOTT LEITH
    The Atlanta Journal-Constitution

    Coca-Cola has sued a food-additive maker for allegedly supplying an unapproved flavor that led to a costly product recall in Japan.

    In a suit filed Friday in Fulton County Superior Court, Coca-Cola and its Japanese unit accused Quest International of providing Coke bottlers with a lemon flavor that hadn't been approved for consumption in Japan.

    The suit claims Quest repeatedly told Coke that the flavoring was acceptable under Japanese laws.

    It was not. In May, Japanese officials discovered that Coke had been using the flavor in products sold under two names: boco and Winnie the Pooh Honey Lemon.

    Coke complied with a request to recall the products. In its lawsuit, Coke said it withdrew 54 million cans and bottles.

    The lawsuit claims the Japanese government also ordered Quest to suspend its operations and recall the flavor.

    The suit does not ask for a specific amount of damages. Last month, however, Coke said its sales in Japan dropped by 3 percent in the second quarter, largely because of the product recalls.

    Quest is based in the Netherlands but has an office in Georgia. The company could not be reached for comment late Friday.

    Coke said boco and Winnie the Pooh Honey Lemon were "highly successful" before the recall. Company spokesman Ben Deutsch said Coke has retooled the formulas for the two products since the recall and is selling them again in Japan -- among Coke's biggest and most profitable markets in the world.
