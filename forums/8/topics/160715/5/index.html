    Explanation:

    For everyone to raise their hands, the only combinations possible are either 2 red hats & 1 green hat OR 3 red hats.

    Basically, since these are the only 2 possible combinations, anyone who sees a green hat can walk out knowing that his is a red hat.

    If, however, they saw 2 red hats, it is always possible for theirs to be a green hat.

    Since neither A & B saw a green hat, they did not walk out, as it was always possible that theirs could be green.

    C sees them wondering, and he knows that his has to be red hat, for if his was a green hat, neither A or B would hesitate to move out.

    P.S: Chances are, by this logic, if C walks out, A & B are likely to think that theirs is red, as they might believe that C walked out because C saw a green hat. Since A & B can see that each others hats are red, they'd think that theirs is green.
