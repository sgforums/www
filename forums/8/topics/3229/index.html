    I have to admit that I view fortune telling with a degree of scepticism.

    I feel that the degree of truth and accuracy in the readings is proportional to the amount of faith you have in it. Believing in a prediction that fortells you will become rich will give you confidence when engaging in business. This would go a long way in fufilling that prophecy. Believing in a prediction that says otherwise would have the reverse effect.

    I mean this is how I feel fortune telling works. It plays on the mind and sub consciounsly translates to reality. If I were to predict that you would become rich and all you do is sit on your stool in front of your house and wait for that to happen, then chances are the event will not materialize at all, except maby by fluke chances (say, a bank checque of $10m addressed to nobody happens to flutter with the wind to your stool)! Ultimately your action is the mechanism that drives the event, not the prediction.

    That is why you may notice fortune telling is often conveyed in a vague and general form. The reason being there is no means for it to be specific. But I may be wrong here, since my only knowledge is ristricted to my casual study of the Tarot Cards. Perhaps better informed people can enlighten me further.

    The future is not for any of us to know, we shape it from our actions today. I believe certain things like life and death are pre-destined, but for other aspects of life, I'm sure that our destiny is pretty much in our own hands.
