    3 Interested buyers can submit their applications to HDB during the initial one-week application period. A computer ballot will then determine the order of selection and all applicants will be given an appointment to book a flat, subject to availability when their turn is due. Applicants who apply after the initial one-week period will be given appointments after the last balloted position. After all scheduled appointments have been processed, remaining flats will be available for booking on a walk-in basis. Unsold flats will be withdrawn at the end of the 5th month, so that they can be prepared for re-launch one month later. The step-by-step sales procedure is at Annex B for reference.

    4 Flat buyers can look forward to the following benefits under the improved system:

    (a) greater certainty when planning their flat purchase as the launch dates and location sectors follow a fixed pattern;
    (b) no need to queue as all applicants who submit their applications in the first week of the launch will be given equal chances to select choice units through balloting;
    (c) more time to study the flat list available on the first day of launch, before submitting their applications within the first week; and
    (d) improved convenience with the option of submitting applications via the internet.

    5 To facilitate the transition to the combined balloting/walk-in system, flats currently available for sale under WIS will be withdrawn by 15 Mar 2007.

    Investigation into Feb 2007 WIS

    9 HDB offered for sale 556 flats in established estates under the WIS exercise on 2 Feb 2007. The formation of an unofficial queue outside HDB's Sales Office prior to HDBÂ’s announcement on 2 Feb led some members of the public to believe there had been an information leak on the launch date of the exercise.

    10 HDB has completed its investigations into the matter. We interviewed 40 applicants who were at the head of the queue, as well as HDB staff and contractors involved in the WIS. We have found no evidence to suggest that information on the launch was leaked.

    11 Investigations revealed that most of the applicants had decided to queue up based on speculation on internet forums that there would be a WIS exercise for flats in established estates in Jan or Feb 07. This arose from HDBÂ’s earlier announcement in Nov 06 when it suspended the sale of flats in established estates that the re-launch would be in 1Q07. Some participants on the internet forums even organised themselves into groups to take turns to keep watch at HDB Hub for tell-tale signs of a sales launch, such as the placement of queue posts. When they saw that a queue had formed for the regular monthly WIS exercise for 3-room flats on 1 Feb 07, some decided to join that queue in anticipation that it was for the flats in established estates.

    12 Many of the applicants were unaware that the flats offered were balance units from earlier sales exercises and units given up by previous buyers. Out of the 4,600 appointments for interested buyers to book their flats, only 355 units have been booked, reflecting an overall acceptance rate of less than 10%. To date, there are still about 200 units available for interested buyers to select.

    13 The incident showed that the existing WIS sales process could be enhanced to ensure greater transparency and convenience to the public. The improved system was therefore designed with these objectives in mind.
