    the pin at the top of a 3-pin plug is 'earth' n conducts the electricity safely away from the metal body of the electrical appliance should there be a short circuit in the electrical wiring...so the 3-pin plug is only for electrical applicances with a metal body...

    for electrical appliances which are covered with a non-conducting material such as plastics...the 2-pin plug should suffice since there is no danger of electrical shock arising from a short circuit....
