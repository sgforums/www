    To Sparkle :

    Basically, I have nothing against cohabitation because I think I will prefer that to marriage. It is unfortunate that in the modern days, marriage is no longer considered to be a sacred union of 2 people. In truth, and if I specialize my example to modern day Singapore, you will find marriage has reduced to nothing but a piece of social contract where the marriage has other connotations, such as the flat, the wedding dinner, income (the 5Cs) and other things. I have friends who get married young and surprisingly and unfortunately, one-two couples has ended in a divorce, and their reason is basically that they are not compatible, after like 7 years of r/s , transiting from courtship to marriage. People of a younger age, in my opinion are not very responsible as compared to the past.

    The whole appealing thing about cohabitation is that people can do away with their responsibility because at any time, they can decide not to live together and go seperate ways. There are no strings attached because there is no legal binding. In my opinion, cohabitation is pretty unfair for a gal, because she suffers more social prejuidice in our culture compare to a guy. The worst consequence is that when she gets pregnant. I was told by a lawyer once that there is a law in the Singapore constitution that states that a child with unmarried parents is not entitled to free education. That is why the Community Chest takes care of these children, by looking sponsors to their education. I think that a lot of cohabiting couples will go to marriage when they have a kid.

    During a coffee break, I was asking a British friend about their views on cohabitation. She told me that in British law, there is a status called common-law husband and wife. In this special status, the couple is a cohabiting and share certain legal rights as a married couple. This is very common in Britain these days. Unfortunately I cannot go into specifics for I am no lawyer. It seems to me a good alternative to marriage.

    The basis of a marriage for a person is to ask the question whether he/she can live a lifetime with another person. Although I am against feudalistic chinese values that we have arranged marriages, yet they last longer than what we have today. I recalled my grandfather once told me that he only loved my grandmother after going through the horrors of World War II, and it is difficult for him to accept an arranged marriage in his time.

    Actually, I prefer cohabitation even though I am one of those people who still believed in the sacred nature of a marriage. I have said my peace.. So, what do the others think?
