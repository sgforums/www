    I refer to http://www.smrt.com.sg/mar05-announcementfinal.pdf

    The Profit and loss statement:
    Strictly speaking although on paper, SMRT made an increase of 41% of net profit after taxation in the Financial Year 2005, compared to 2004, most of the 41% doesn't come from normal operation. It came as the tax provision from the preious years were written back to the P&L this year. Why did SMRT write back the provision, I don't know. Maybe its some statutory requirement. I would have expected SMRT not to write back so as to have a poorer net profit to justify for a fare increase. Maybe it is the adoption of a new accounting standard that forced SMRT to write back the provision.

    The adoption of a new acc standard did forced SMRT to write off more on the Amortisation and impairment losses of goodwill part. This distorted the eventual net profit figure. Another point to note is "should this amortisation figure" be listed under operation expenses when it is a significant change in interpretation as depicted by an early adoption of a new acc standard?

    To make the P&L fairer for interpretation and more transparent, SMRT should adjust the 2004FY figures to reflect how it would have looked like if the new acc standard was adopted back then.
