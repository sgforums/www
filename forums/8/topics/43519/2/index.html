    Possum's question on the swords - there is/might be a logical explanation for this.

    In today's boxing matches, they always state the guys reach and the power of his punch. Lets the opponent know what he's facing. For example, if his reach is 3 ft and his opponent's is 3 ft 1 in, then he better duck faster than he can punch.

    Regarding the sword, the length and weight lets the opponent know the reach, and also an indication of how fast it moves, and how heavy a blow it can deal (at a certain speed). The name of the sword gives an indication of who made it and when/where it was made - lets you know the hardness, sharpness, balance, centre of gravity, etc of the sword. The history of the sword gives an indication of who held it - very much like classifying a Stradivarius as a Guanari (prob wrong spelling for both, but what the heck).

    So, declaring your sword is quite a serious matter in those times. Except, of course, that those times never existed, save in Gu Long and Liu Jia Chang's novels.
