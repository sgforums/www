    I think churchgoers needn't cover themselves up completely in order to dress modestly.

    I agree that modesty in dressing is something about which it is fairly difficult to be obejctive; but think that it is safe to say that any form of dress that exposes or accentuates parts of the body that are likely to provoke arousal if they are not concealed, should be deemed immodest.

    It follows therefore that by the definition I have just adopted, there will be many forms of dress which could still be judged modest and which needn't entail that a person who is clad in them, should be covered up completely.

    I'd like to respond to one or two other points that some others (the Christians) have raised:

    1) It is true that Christians sometimes sin. However, when a Christian sins by dressing immodestly and when, after realising that she or he has indeed dressed immodestly, doesn't feel in the least bit inclined to repent of his or her sin in this regard, then I don't think that, if you're a Christian yourself, you should be making light of such a state of affairs. I believe that any sin of which you should be unwilling to repent, is likely to portend that you are not truly a Christian even if you are nonetheless someone who believes himself already to have been made a Christian.

    2) I don't think that anybody should be making the claim that because his so-called 'church' features a pastor who in his opinion is able to preach 'good sermons', then his so-called 'church' must therefore be deemed automatically superior to a more manifestly morally objectionable so-called 'church.

    3) Many so-called 'conservative' churches preach an ideology that is clearly designed to cater to people who have it all, and who, after having it all, want now to be assured that they can continue having it all after they die. Such 'churches' are unwelcoming to marginalised folk, the poor, socially marginalised and so forth, and they have their own peculiar set of problems such as the various problems posed by the horrendous pride of their members.

    4) I am personally disgusted by the people who look down on the bar-top dancers and yet attempte to 'come up in life' in what they construe to be ways that have found favour with God, and who accordingly hanker after the kind of 'conservative' sermons that act as a sop to their inclinations in this regard. Such people are as much to be reprobated as any morally loose bar-top dancer, if not even more so.

    Sorry, but I am quite sickened.
