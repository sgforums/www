    was it a typo error?

    if you were refering to 'MX' then of course 'FX' is a better card. FX was a newer version that was to replace MX. However, if it was not a typo error on your side, then i guess you were refering to the series nunbering system for MSI graphics cards. According to MSI, their series numbering order for the PCI express series of graphics cards goes like tis..."MSI NXxxxx" (e.g MSI NX6800).

    if so, of course the MSI PCI express card from nVidia supersedes their FX brothers in handling newer graphics intensive games and functions. another difference is the graphics card slot of course. FX and MX cards should fit into older AGP/ PCI slots while your NX (MSI) should fit into the newer PCI express slot.

  • 'm back

    no you can't. older mainboards up to PGA 478 (intel) and socket 754 (AMD) comes with the AGP (up to 8x) graphics card slot and the PCI 2.3 for PCI graphics card. newer mainboards based on the LGA 775 socket (intel 900 series etc) and AMD mainboard based on the socket 939 (AMD Atthlon 64/64FX etc) uses the newer PCI-E (express) slot which has a very different physical dimension, keying orientation and the number of gold finger contacts. this alone is enough to create an incompatiblility between newer cards and older boards, let alone the connectivity and circuit routing internally between board and card.
