    AMERICAN
    Dan Ryan's Chicago Grill
    There is a warning on the menu at Dan Ryan's Chicago Grill that reads: "We serve American Portions". If you're up for the challenge, head down to this no-nonsense, American-style eatery. As Dan himself says: "Our philosophy is to offer authentic American food in a lively, upscale atmosphereÂ… to provide a consistently high standard of American food and service at reasonable prices."The restaurant's strapline is Steaks, Seafood, Ribs - and you'll get all of these and moreÂ… and moreÂ…. and more. Our recommendation? Go for the kids' burgers, and take advantage of the colouring pencils they provide at the same time. ($$; Tanglin Place, #B1-01, 91 Tanglin Road, Tel: 6738-2800.)

    FRENCH
    Au Jardin
    Somewhat controversial was the glassing-in of the 1920s black-and-white residence in the Botanic Gardens where Au Jardin is located, but it has to be said that fine dining and sweltering heat just don't go together. Perhaps it does make sense to sit at a table Ã  deux on the second-floor glass-panelled balcony and take in the tropical vista lovingly planted over 100 years ago. Open evenings only and for Sunday Brunch, Resident Chef Galvin Lim's short menu concentrates on Provencal-style French cuisine. "We hope to bring an accent and feel of Provence, with the scents of rosemary, lavender and thyme, in our little tropical paradise in the Botanic Gardens," says partner Ignatius Chan. ($$$$; Singapore Botanic Gardens, EJH Corner House, Tel: 6466-8812.)

    Au Petit Salut
    Great French food with rustic flair. The focus is on fresh ingredients, including the bread, and regulars will find it difficult to get bored as the menu changes monthly. Try the set lunch menu for good value, or treat yourself to the stewed white beans with duck leg confit, gizzard and streaky bacon if you dine here at night. An extensive wine list includes wines from the Old and New World, and if you just can't get enough, Au Petit Salut also offers a home catering service. ($$$; Chip Bee Gardens, 44 Jalan Merah Saga, #01-54, Holland Village, Tel: 6475-1976.)

    Les Amis Restaurant
    Many believe the recently revamped Les Amis is already one of the best French restaurants in the world. The world! Offering the crÃ¨me de la crÃ¨me of both traditional and innovative French cooking and an internationally acclaimed wine list with over 1,000 mainly French labels, spot-on service and utterly contemporary dÃ©cor, it's a no-brainer for many. Felicitations to head honcho, Justin Quek. ($$$; Shaw Centre, #02-16, 1 Scotts Road, Tel: 6733-2225.)

    Les Saisons
    Ex-Intermezzo French chef Nicolas Joanny and Singaporean Mackie Chee have come together to form Les Saisons, an affordable, minimalist French restaurant in the former Saint Pierre premises. Meaning 'The Seasons', the cosy, curtained interior seats a maximum of 30 guests. The emphasis on food is also intimate: the restaurant prides itself on home-made everything, from the bread right down to the ice cream. Offering seasonal set and Ã  la carte menus for both lunch and dinner, try the rosemary crusted rack of lamb, Atlantic sea lobster roasted in salt butter flavoured with coriander, or braised ox tongue with Morel mushroom sauce. ($$; Central Mall, 7 Magazine Road, #01-03, Tel: 6557-0080.)

    Vis-Ã -Vis
    Any cravings for Gallic fare can be sated with a good feed at Vis-Ã -vis, along Upper Bukit Timah Road. Further pacify your taste buds with a bottle or two from the extensive wine list. House specialities include sautÃ©ed lobster with mushrooms and a roast rack of veal marinated in onion juice and pimiento oil. For a classic dessert, don't miss the Normandy apple pie drizzled with honey and accompanied by Calvados apple brandy ice cream. ($$$; 12 Chun Tin Road, Tel: 6468-7433.)

    FUSION
    Buko Nero
    Italian food with an Asian twist. Local produce is put to good use, adding a bit of zing to the flavours and complementing Italian favourites. All of this is served up amongst chic and soothing dÃ©cor. The Ã  la carte menu changes every few months, but regulars include codfish and beef tenderloin. The house speciality, which has been on the menu since the restaurant opened, is the tau kwa, served with sautÃ©ed vegetables. ($$; 126 Tanjong Pagar Road, Tel: 6324-6225.)

    Cameleon
    The sleek-looking Cameleon (sic) restaurant places a strong emphasis on classic Japanese food - sushi rolls, anyone? - but also gives more than a nod to the best cuisine from other Asian countries. The Thai-inspired udon comes in tom yam or green curry flavours, from Korea comes kim chee, and from good old Singapore there's laksa. For the perfect fusion of East and West, there's even a chicken teriyaki pizza. If you can't find something tempting here, you're way too fussy. ($; Robertson Walk, #01-17, 11 Unity Street, Tel: 6734-1101.)

    Doc Cheng's
    Doc Cheng's offers an eclectic array of dishes, delivering on its promise of fusion food. There's less on the menu than at first appears due to the long, vaguely amusing dish descriptions which can become tiresome by the time you get to dessert. For starters try the Thai crab cakes with Malay turmeric sauce. Then move on to the spit-roasted beef tenderloin drizzled with vindaloo jus. Finish up with macadamia crunch bars - caramelised chocolate crunch bars with Hawaiian poha berries. Choices are limited for vegetarians. ($$$; Raffles Hotel Arcade, #02-20, 1 Beach Road, Tel: 6337-1886.)

    Restaurant 360
    Located in the One Fullerton building, Restaurant 360 offers awesome views out over the Singapore waterfront. The view inside is just as pleasing with the interior lending a light and open ambience. Japanese and Western influences are put to work in the dishes here. Try the chilled gazpacho with crab meat and avocado cube, the roasted veal rack with sweetbread and split jus, or the roasted spring chicken in natural jus served with mushroom duxelle. Freshly shucked oysters, sushi and sashimi are also available. ($$$; One Fullerton, Level 2, 1 Fullerton Road, Tel: 6220-0055.)

    Saint Pierre
    Modern French-Japanese combo Saint Pierre always comes up with the goods. Run by Chef Emmanuel Stroobant and his wife Edina Hong, award-winning Saint Pierre is an innovative, experimental eatery. Stroobant feels that some Old World techniques and foods are too heavy for the tropics, so tends to dip into his Japanese store cupboard for a lighter touch. Try the braised black cod with white miso and warm Japanese eggplant salad, or the roasted milk-fed lamb saddle en croute with baby carrots, zucchini, and nori consommÃ© on the side. ($$$; Central Mall, 3 Magazine Road, #01-01, Tel: 6438-0887.)

    Salt
    Owner/chef Jimmy Chok takes the dishes of his traditional Chinese grandmother and gives them a twist with French ingredients like foie gras and duck breast at this clean shophouse venue. Expect fusion fare - local products with European cooking techniques or vice versa - and a bit of panache stirred in for good measure. But to prevent it all getting too messy, the overall emphasis is on keeping it simple and letting the ingredients speak for themselves. Innovative food is the end result. ($$; 94 Amoy Street, Tel: 6223-1266.)
