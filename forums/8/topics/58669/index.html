    though many here may disagree with what i say, i have a thing or two abt romance over the net.

    First, i think there's a lot of trust involved, especially in IRC chat rooms and lobbies where ppl with common interest hang out. You strike out a conversion with someone and then "love interest" blossoms.

    The next stage is the exchange of emails or contact numbers (if it gets that far) and further intimate chatting.

    Finally, this reaches a climax when you actually meet this person which you held some interest in.

    Frankly, i enjoy posting threads on forum pages. I enjoy arguing points in the political section with serious ppl like atobe, gun, centurion etc.

    But i feel that the main reason why ppl do so is to be able to express their concerns and feelings on the basis of annoyomity. It is due to this blanket cover that gives ppl true freedom of expression...even if it is sometimes misled or simply wrong. If we were all required to post our names and contact info, we would either place pen-names or leave it blank.

    That is perhaps the reason why i fail to see how romance on the net can be anything other than a fascade; you can merely generate a ficticious image of the person you speak to or share info with. Even pictures sent can be fake. You literally stretch trust beyond the limits for something like this.

    I for one prefer to conjure up romance the old-fashioned way - getting to know some friends, looking for common interests and similar characteristics and allowing it to take me wherever it goes...

    just my 5 cents worth...
