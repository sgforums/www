    It seems the accepted norms of how romantic relationships are built is broadly divided into 2 categories; the narrow conformist method and the open anything goes method.

    The narrow conformist method seems to be within our generations of teenagers, and even persists into adulthood. It involves constructing friendship between among people who are together in contact within a common faction; i.e. class, peer group, outing, etc etc. And these relationships last for a while, with parties within the circle only wanting to form romantic attachments within that existing circle.

    The open anything goes method, seems to be within the later middle aged westernised group of people, mostly such people are the ones who engage in personal ads, clubbing on their own to pick up people and "arranging" dates with people (outside of their narrow conformist circle mentioned above).

    My age group relationships work mostly in the narrow conformist group, while adults tend to be more open-ended.

    The narrow conformist method is hell for those who cannot fit into existing normal cliquish frameworks, resulting in lack of potential relationships with narrow people who LIMIT their relationship possibilities to those only within their own respective frameworks.
