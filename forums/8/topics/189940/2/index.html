    ... we are the 'dotcom' generation ....



    But seriously, I often use those dots to magnify or emphasise a train of thought, rather than make the sentence 'sound' and appear 'staccato-like'. People DO get 'breathless' reading long sentences with just the token punctuation marks such as the commas and semi-colons to mark a 'lull' in the sentence. People also get 'breathless' when reading a paragraph and having to stop every few words or so.

    More than just for the writer per se, I think the "..." (Rule of thumb: No More than 3!!) serves as a breather for the reader itself. However, this usually makes sense only for long sentences that's a continuous explanation of a point - rather than as a substitute for a co-ordinating conjunction such as "and", "morever", "but" or "therefore" to connect two seperate sentences.

    Of course, it is grammatically incorrect, for there are no (not that I know of, at least) punctuation mark name for the "...", and in proper written English of higher levels, such blatant and lazy usage of dots to string a sentence together is frowned upon.

    But until my forum posts are graded - I'll stick to my dotty conjunctions.

    its an informal speec/conversation orientated style of writing. Its meant to convey the pause within a statement, or perhaps to denote the way we end a statement.

    eg.

    "Gee.......... I can't remember." <- conveys the pause made by the person making the statement.

    "I don't know........" <- conveys the way the sentence trails off as spoken by the person making the statement.

    ".........' <- denotes speechlessness, usually due to surprise, being flabbergasted or being nonplussed.
