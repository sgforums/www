    Automatic transmission cars may be allowed to be used in driving tests

    By Dominique Loh

    The popularity of automatic transmisson cars may change the way driving tests are conducted.

    Student drivers could have a choice of either taking their driving test with a manual or automatic car.

    But do not expect to take driving lessons on automatic cars anytime soon.

    The Traffic Police said of all new cars imported into Singapore, 60 percent are automatic transmission cars.

    But even before a motorist get to drive one on the road, they will have to start on a circuit with a manual car.

    Changing to automatic cars overnight will be a logistical challenge for driving centres.

    Mr Tan Tiaw Beng, Senior Manager of Singapore Safety Driving Centre, said: "In fact, we intend to purchase 10 automatic transmission cars for this purpose. We have our yearly depreciation plan, we will depreciate our older cars with newer auto cars. We will do it gradually because we still do not know the market response."

    Reactions, however, were mixed among learner drivers.

    "Automatically, automatic."

    "I think I would choose the auto one, it seems easier."

    "I think I prefer the manual first."

    "For a starter, I would rather know manual then I change to auto, for me I have never driven before, so I cannot guage actually."

    But the Traffic Police is still looking into the proposed change.

    There are licensing issues - would a person end up holding one licence for a specific type of car?

    And it may take up to a year before some driving centres are adequately equipped with the cars.

    There is not much difficulty for someone who was tested on a manual car to switch to driving an automatic car.

    But the same cannot be said for those tested on automatic cars to switch to manual.

    The techniques for manual driving is simply more complicated.

    At the Singapore Safety Driving Centre, students who pass their driving test can also opt for free lessons on automatic cars.
