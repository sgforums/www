    What are the issues n considerations in the growth stage of a product life cycle?

    yah.. like what ben said, this is a very general question.

    broadly speaking, once a NEW product has penetrated the market succesfully, the next stage is to plan how to continue maintaining and better still, capture larger market share.

    shld the product penetrate based on being a niche product, the company wld have to ensure that its product or services are not easily imitated/copied. Esp when a product tends to command a higher price should it be a niche product, the company needs to adds value to the core product. For instance, to provide superior after sales service/technical support, making it tougher for competitors to imitate. With such efforts, the customer will be more willing to pay the high price (should there by no close competition) even though a product is in the growth stage.

    else, shld the product be a more general commodity where competition is rampant, the company may have to adopt a different pricing strategy in order to retain market share. for instance, throw in promotions to entice customers to purchase in bigger quantities or extended service contracts. another way to retain interest during the growth stage is to launch complementary products/services so that consumers will be "locked in". for eg, Sony products are rather proprietary as they are designed such that the different equipment can be easily communicated with one another to faciliate consumer's use. so a product in a growth stage has extended life cycle as it can be used with its newer products from the same "family".

    this is a very interesting topic: the rship of brand & marketing.

    i believe most traditional or academic papers wld tell u that branding activity is part of the marketing. or in other words, marketing drives branding.

    if i understand correctly, brand equity refers to the intangible/intrinsic value of the company or product (with the brand) in the market. for instance, a similar product may be worth or priced $xx, but due to higher brand equity, it can command ($xx + $yy) or in other words, it can fetch a premium price.

    as such, brand equity has a lot of influence or relevance in marketing. The marketing plan's price, promotion or place (distribution) strategy relies heavily on the brand equity of the product. For instance, a product with lower brand equity (not so well known in the market) may require more elaborate planning &/or spending on A&P activities (pull strategy) to raise the brand awareness among its target customers.

    However, in recent years, a lot of bigger corporations are changing their mindset when it comes to branding or increasing brand equity. it has become such that branding drives marketing (not the other way round).

    these corporations are spending a lot more effort and resources to strategically plan & execute their branding & marketing activities to reach their brand objective. the brand carries more than just the intangible value of the product. it also represents an identity/values of what the company behind the brand stands for.

    Take for instance, VIRGIN. VIRGIN's brand equity is high and they have been able to diversify their business rather boldly (w/o prior experience) and some even rather succesfully. No matter what they do, their working style revolves around being consistant with their brand value, ie, being "fun, maybe totally irrelevant & thinking out of the box" style. As such, their target customers around the world gets a very clear message of what VIRGIN stands for and even look forward to receiving new products/services from VIRGIN (partly coz they are curious as to what VIRGIN will come out with next).

    not sure if the above helps. just my personal thoughts on branding & marketing!
