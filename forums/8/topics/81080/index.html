    but most important thing is, i just ignore them not because they are giving me attitude but because i know they have the right to be angry and temperamental at times, and i just tell myself that: Fine, i'll just leave him/her alone for today, cos i know they weren't usually like that in my other classes.

    it's my way of being respectful to my students, knowing that they have mood-swings of their own, and i never think otherwise that these students have gone crazy of some other matter.

    and for my case, when i get angry, i'll try to control myslef but i hope students will know that just like when they are angry, it's sometimes hard to contain yourself. yes, i do feel touchy when i'm angry in class, but i'll try my best not to reprimand students for the wrong reasons. and even if i do, i'll try to contain myself but it is not easy.

    so students, do understand that, yes, teachers do get angry. but it really make a difference between a teacher who gets angry and vent thier anger on students, and those teachers who do not. how to see the difference?

    well, a good teacher will reprimand students and focus on the fault made by the student, and NOT on the character on the students..

    and please, don't assume it's always bf/husband problems that causes a teacher to have a bad mood on a day. it can be some other things, and let's just stop guessing what is it that makes a teacher to have a bad day, it's really childish.

    my final advice is, if u see that a teacher is feeling moody, be nice to him/her, ignore him/her nicely and behave, just like how i treat my students when the students themselves are feeling moody. don't just focus on the day when a teacher is angry, but try to recall the other days when a good teacher tries his/her very best to entertain you all during the lessons.
