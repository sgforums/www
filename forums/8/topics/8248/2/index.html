    People always say that SILENCE IS GOLDEN but it really depends. It's NOT a matter of whether people can stand silence anot, it's really WHEN to be SILENT and WHEN to CONVERSE. Anyway I doubt anyone can really stand silence, especially for a LONG period of time. It will be too AWKWARD! However, this is dependent on the occasion and also venue you are in. One can't possibly converse much in a library and silence is definitely better. After all, those library patrons are trying to get some reading or research done so silence will be appreciated! Also, in a solemn situation such as a funeral, silence is certainly valued.

    And of course during gatherings and outings or dates, conversation is encouraged. It is through conversation that people can communicate and get to know each other better. For those of you who claim that you prefre silence but for how LONG? With awkwardness hanging in the air, how to continue with the date? Hmm, some people are listeners (or so they say) but doesn't these listeners want to contribute any ideas to conversations? Or to let others get to know them better? If you just keep mum, how do you expect others to get to know you? Will appear rather snobbish right? Can these so-called listeners stand silence themsevles? PROLONGED SILENCE?

    Well, I still feel that one should do what the occasion deems fit. Be it silence or noise, there is an appropriate time for such but too much of the latter can be irritating too. Hmm, maybe we should just talk when necessary and NOT just to make small talks and also DON'T make the situation awkward by keeping quiet all the time.
