    More reports ....

    The decision to call off the search operation, which had been pursued amid poor weather and rough monsoon seas, was a difficult one made over the last 48 hours.

    It finally hinged on advice from two forensic experts from the Health Sciences Authority, including the director of its Centre for Forensic Medicine, Dr Paul Chui.

    Dr Chui said a body decomposes rapidly in water and marine creatures as well as other vessels could also contribute to the process, given that the accident happened in a busy waterway.

    'The longer the lapse between the time of incident and the time of locating the body, the slimmer the chances of recovery,' he said.

    Operations to lift the wreckage from the seabed are continuing, said the Chief of Defence Force, Lieutenant-General Lim Chuan Poh.

    The search had involved the Navy, Air Force, Police Coast Guard and the Singapore Civil Defence Force, whose personnel combed the Singapore Straits, the northern coasts of Bintan and Batam and the south-eastern coast of Johor.

    'Both Indonesia and Malaysia also assisted us with naval, air and ground search resources and I would like to thank the Indonesian and Malaysian armed forces,' Lt-Gen Lim said.
