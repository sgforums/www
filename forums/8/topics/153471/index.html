    A high-level carnivore would need to eat and get his energy with a long chain.

    He needs the energy, yet the energy has travelled and lost significantly along the chain.

    As school thought me, when a creature eats, not everything is spent for growth. Some energy is spent for heat and other needs. So when a high-level carnivore eats, actually there are lots of wasted energy along the way.

    OK let's say we are a high-level carnivore. Or whatever. Omnivore. Peak of the food chain.

    When u want to eat a chicken, u have to raise a chicken and feed it with grains. U need to open up a farm.

    Research says, that same farm u open can supply more energy to u if u eat all the grains instead of giving some of the grains to feed some chickens and then eat the chickens.

    OK. So there is waste here.

    Let's call this 'inefficiency #1'.

    Now comes 'inefficiency #2' :

    After we eat, we need to poop.

    Let's think about this for a moment.

    Doesn't that mean the energy we are getting comes from inefficient source? If not, we don't need to poop, do we? Has anyone ever thought of why we are pooping? Poop is waste. That means the actual physical food we eat is inefficient, is it not?

    It's like, ok I eat this chicken to get energy, and I eat it, and then I poop the chicken out.

    That means the chicken is only a vessel carrying the energy. Is it not?

    Same like the chicken who poops after eating grains. He eat the grain, get the energy, and poop the grain out.

    Even if we are vegetarians and we eat the grains directly, we save some lost energy from the chain, but not from the plant heat. And we poop still.

    So, think about it, even if we eat the grains directly, there is still waste.
