    Study and work at new ITE College East
    Vendors at its eight retail and food outlets will be asked to employ students for at least half of their job openings

    By Maria Almenoar

    WHEN school starts at the new ITE College East in January, accounting student Ong Hui Jia, 18, will not have to look far to find a part-time job.

    It will be available on campus at Simei Avenue.

    The college expects to have enough work for between 5 per cent and 10 per cent of its enrolment, which will be 3,000 initially and increase to 7,200.

    Said its principal, Mr Tan Seng Hua: 'Vendors at our eight retail and food and beverage outlets on campus will be asked to fill at least half of their job openings with our students.'

    The move is to expose the youngsters to work, and to do this in a familiar environment.

    MEGA-CAMPUS OPENS IN JANUARY

    The $240-million 10.7ha campus at Simei Avenue has three curved buildings, five to six storeys high, laid out in the shape of a triangle. There is one each for administration, technology and business, and they are connected by bridges to a central platform on the third level.

    There are also specialized centres - for multimedia learning (top), music and arts performances and for working students to set up 'office'. The college enrolment will be 3,000 initially and increase to 7,200. The college also has an Olympic-size swimming pool (above), an indoor basketball courts which seats 1,200 spectators, two street soccer courts, four tennis courts and three basketball courts. -- CHEW SENG KIM

    ITE College East is one of the three mega-campuses to be set up by consolidating the existing 10 institutes. This is being done, Education Minister Tharman Shanmugaratnam explained on Tuesday, so students can be given more choice of courses, and to encourage diversification in the schools and competition among them.

    The other two colleges, ITE College West in Choa Chu Kang and ITE College Central in Ang Mo Kio, are expected to open in the next 10 years.

    Yesterday, the media was taken on a tour of College East's 10.7ha campus. Work on this $240 million project started about 19 months ago and the finishing touches are on now.

    The campus has three curved buildings, five to six storeys high, laid out in the shape of a triangle. There is one each for administration, technology and business, and they are connected by bridges to a central platform on the third level.

    The campus has an Olympic-size swimming pool, an indoor basketball court with seats for 1,200 spectators, two street soccer courts, four tennis courts and three basketball courts.

    There are also centres for music and arts performances, multimedia learning and one where working students can set up 'office'.

    Students from other ITE campuses will be allowed to use the facilities.

    The college will offer five courses exclusively - biochemical technology, nursing, business studies in sports management, integrated logistics management and infocomm technology.

    It hopes to build a name for itself in student development programmes, focusing on leadership training and professional etiquette. So nursing students, for example, will be taught how to behave in a hospital setting.

    In a bid to work more closely with its industry partners, the college has set up six centres for the businesses to set up laboratories and run training classes for students and teachers.

    A certificate may be offered at the end of a course.
