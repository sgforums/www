    This post is to clarify what Ariana (Page 1 of this topic last article) had mentioned in her article which I find intruiging.

    The definition of Ah Beng is rather controversial and not everyone agrees on this, but even the Straits Times had an article to describe the "typical" Ah Beng which I may now proceed to elaborate on.

    Physical traits would include bad dressing, lousy colour co-ordination, super baggy pants, T-shirts with vulgar words or funny messages on them, chains, earrings (multiple), necklaces (large gaudy ones), super-gelled hair etc....

    Also in the list would be branded goods like branded wallets sticking half out of their pockets, dyed hair which is a little too long sometimes, a fancy looking comb which they whip out to comb their hair, and of course the ever-present pager/handphone.

    Stereotyping is a crime I know.....but then that's how trends and fads get born isn't it ? We don't call the 80's msuic retro movement and electronica just a stereotype when it encompasses part of our culture.

    In terms of attitudes, they like to swagger (yeah that's the word !) around shopping malls, constantly comb their hair, talk VERY loudly, shout to each other, parade around, talk on handphones while glancing around...and the list goes on !

    These are just personal observations and are not intended to offend anyone who has actually done any of the above. I am not saying that if you do 80 % of the things I describe above automatically makes you an Ah Beng ! These are just a subset of what makes up an Ah Beng.

    But I agree with Ariana that Ah Bengs may have hearts of gold to their gf and their buddies. This is through interaction in the Army where a guy gets to meet all sorts of people from all sorts of backgrounds. For guys like Fries, learn what you can about people and life while you're in the Army, because that is a place to obtain rich experiences ! For Firewire, who may or may not have gone through compulsory conscription (correct me here please...), this brings to light certain issues...

    Friends of mine in the Army who are "Beng" in terms of character and dressing (and who even smoke and flirt etc...) actually turn out to be NICE guys and SINCERE friends ! I used to be biased against them too initially but I found out that the truth is some more educated guys from well-established JCs are actually more unsrupulous and back-stabbing than these supposed "Bengs". Thus, you might say that I actually prefer these Bengs to those artificial "smarter guys".

    Since this post is getting a little too lengthy, I will continue my analysis in a subsequent posting...
