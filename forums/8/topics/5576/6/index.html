    To Rebellyon:

    Your analysis is valid but let me offer you a different angle. Bengism is not even a subculture. It is a counterculture; a kneejerk reaction of the lower socio-economic classes to the mainstream. The lower socio-economic classes percieve that they have been marginalised by society and strive to find an identity thus reinforcing the ingroup outgroup bias. This identity they adopt depends on the cultural history of their society. In the Singaporean context, ingroup outgroup bias is very clear between, and even within the cultures. The chinese are historically clannish in behavior. This causes the formation or "gangs" or secret societies.

    The Bengs and Lians yearn for upward social mobility yet openly reject it by adopting the percieved opposites of mainstream culture. Just look at their dressing and it doesn't take a very smart person to deduce that they want to be percieved as wealthy and stylish.

    The link between repetitive hypnotic music and the counterculture still eludes me. Some people think that it is just a case of bad taste, but I think there are deeper correlations. I have nothing against Techno/Eurodance, I myself listen to Judge Jules, Brian Transeau and Einsterzunde Neubauten.

    The things about Bengs that turn my stomach is that they think they can get away with everything and muscle their way through. Good for you if you're a Beng, but think about the person on the other end. It really sucks.

    As for Sparks, I've never been there myself, but I imagine thats the place where Bengs and Lians would be happiest judging by the drunken hordes outside Taka after 3am.

    Can Bengs abide by social norms? By definition they cannot because they are the counterculture. No one should make excuses for their ill behavior. Their reasons and intentions for their actions have been debunked to death and written in a thousand Sociology papers, and the police don't buy it either.
