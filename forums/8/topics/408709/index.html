For Andrea, who sparkled me up in USS

  • Maxwell@Andrea
    21 Aug 10, 01:38

    It was my third day in Singapore. It would be the best day because I met Andrea. The days and nights will be doomed afterwards, coz' I'd never see the glistening you again.

    Andrea,

    It's great if you could see it. At least you know here's someone from Hong Kong think of you always.

    I met you in the Universal Studios in Sentosa. On Thursday, I was puzzled about the underlying movie of the Ancient Egypt section. I asked a staff-lady in the entrance of the souvenir showroom which was also the exit of the thrill game. Maybe my spoken English was quite broken, you were misled and told me that inside was a roller coaster! But then you were really smart to aware and spoke Mandarin to me. Ahha, the movie was Mummy. I felt stupid asking it. However, your smile eased my embarrassment.

    You're like an angel. Yes, you are.

    The query was over, I had to go. But I came back several times just to see you. "Thank you, Andrea!" was what I told you the second time we met (or, frankly, I came to you). Read from your nametag, hoped to impress you a bit more. And you smiled again.

    And then I really played the roller coaster! I knew after the game I would exit through the showroom, where you used to served at! So the thrill of the game was easily overcome by the longing to see you again. "Impressive!", I said. It was your recommendation. Actually, you're the most impressing one, and I was just trying hard to impress you! Stupid me...

    Although I deliberately pass by the entrance of the showroom several times, I couldn't see you anymore. Expecting to see you in the farewell group of staffs when I was leaving, disappointed to see no familiar face.

    Andrea, I miss you so much. How amazing it was to run into you in a short trip, and the moments were like lasting for eternity.

    If I could make dreams like Inception the movie, I'd come to see you as my projection. Though it's unreal, to see you smile again is truely wonderful for me to stay in the dream forever.

     

    Best Wishes,

    Maxwell
