    So I'm sitting here on a Friday night, thinking about how I turned 28 a few days ago. It's occurred to me that in 2 years I'll pretty much have lived half of my natural life (I don't really have high expectations). It's also occurred to me that while I think I've had some truly awesome experiences compared to those who live back home in the States, I still haven't done a few things that I've long wanted to do. They are, in no particular order:

    1. Quit smoking. It's so hard to do, but the times I've tried and lasted for a month plus, I've felt great. I'd like to kick the habit for good someday soon.
    2. Get into a UFC-style fight. I think back at all the times I got my ass kicked as a kid, and would really just like the chance to get into a ring and get over the fear of getting my ass kicked again and just let loose. I'm pretty sure I'd still get my ass kicked, but at least this time I'd try to hold my own.
    3. Be a monk. I'm married and have a job, so obviously I can't dedicate my life to monk-hood, but I'd like to try living in a monastery and experiencing the life of a monk for a month or so.
    4. Start a business. I consider myself an "idea man," unfortunately with a full time job, I lack the willpower and motivation to move my ass and set something up.
    5. Find some sort of faith. Being from the US, it's customary that many people are raised on some sort of Christian-based religion. I sort of was, very loosely, but I still don't have any set beliefs. At the same time, I envy those people who have some sort of faith because their life has some sort of purpose.
    So, what are 5 things you'd like to do before you die and why?
