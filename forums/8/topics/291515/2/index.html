    Looks like we need some science in this thread.

    Firstly, cellphones do not operate directly from the mains. This is true for most if not all. This is how electricity travels in your phone

    Mains ---> Charges Battery ---> Phone

    The mains electricity does not ever directly power the phone, which means there is no way you can get electricity to "flow unrestrained" from the phone itself. As for whether you can get a shock from the power in the battery, it's possible, but the power output is very low and one will not be "thrown to the ground with a heavy thud".

    Only way the incident could happen is for the user, with wet hands, to come into contact with the charging port directly.
