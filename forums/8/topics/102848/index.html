    Actually there are a few singaporeans in sydney who I know

    I can also give u tips about sydney since I lived there before

    For singaporean food - take a train to paramatta - Temasek Restaurant is near the train station - their hainanese chicken rice is almost like back home....so are alot of their dishes

    Buses - they never run on time .If they do run on time - u will still get to your destination late (look at the roads)

    Chinatown - Chingmys at Dixon place have very nice crispy chicken rice if you stay near the CBD. Haymarket is not as great as QV market here but it is adequate

    Never use a chinese owned tour company at haymarket- the last time we did - the driver insisted on a 5 dollar tip despite bad service , we refused and he refused to drive back.

    Emergency departments - if you are sick , go see a doctor asap. If on weekend good luck waiting in the ED of Sydney....the wait can take up to 6-8 hrs. If you have a gunshot wound - at least 4 hrs ( I heard this from my sydney priest - he was shot but waited in ED for 4 hrs because as they assumed that he wasn't in pain). If you want to get seen faster - do what the ladies do - scream shout cry in pretend agony ( if u have no pain)

    Roads - yes the roads to kensington/coogi beach/marouba have lovely views......and traffic jams

    Newtown - worth having a look at ....they have discount clothing sometimes. And lotsa eating places - thai /japanese etc.
    Glebe too - I used to go to the bade mansion cafe - lovely vegan food.

    Always be kind to beggars and the homeless - u never know when they will help you. I used to buy food for the homeless local beggar ...and one day I was nearly mugged by a bunch of teenagers - he came up with his usual smell and smile at them - they ran.

    When you see a bunch of fellas walking towards you at night- quickly go to a crowded place/flag a taxi

    At night - never go out once the sun set

    There u go - tips to sydney

    I'm living in Sydney, and going to uni here. Well technically I'm not Singporean cos I'm an Australian citizen, but I lived in S'pore for 10 years and completed high school there. Plus, I'm half Singaporean by race.

    Public transport here is a nightmare, especially on weekends.

    For the best Chinese food in Sydney - take the train to Ashfield or perhaps a bus that goes along Parramatta Rd. Ashfield is known as 'Little Shanghai' to the locals, great Shanghainese food.

    Sydney has fantastic Vietnamese Beef Noodles, known as Pho. Try Pho Pasteur along George Sreet in the city.

    Do NOT ever EVER try 'Malaysian' or 'Singporean' food in the city. It's is never the same as back home, and full expensive too. Yup, the best eating places are at Newtown (great Thai restaurants) and Glebe.

    And it's not true that you can't go out after sunset. If you do, just keep your wits about you and if possible, always walk with another person. People here get mugged often, YES a lot of times by teenagers!
