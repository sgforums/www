  • Thonglet
    29 May 11, 22:33

    Very few, if any of you, got it as bad as I did (way back when). 

    I did my O-levels back in 1986 in the oldest Christian Brothers' Schools here. We had 2-3 teachers who would routinely slap, kick, drag us around by our collars and throw us against the walls, lift us off our feel and slam us into the cupboards. The 2E10 classroom had a HOLE IN THE BLACKBOARD from where one unfortunate student had his head bashed into it. 

    And yet, we got into as much mischief as any class nowadays. More, in fact. It was just that there was never any disrespect INSIDE the class. We knew where to draw the line. Whether it was fear of the rod that kept us in line, or a more strict and internalised code of conduct, I do not know.

    I am a teacher now.

    Because of my background in policing and prisons work, my schools had automatically put me to teaching the NAs and NTs.

    Do I think that schools need to institute corporal punishment more? No.

    I believe that consistency and a belief (on the students' part) that they WILL be caught and punished is more important. As long as the punishment informs, educates, is relevant and is slightly more painful than the act of disobedience was pleasurable; it will work.

    I've never laid a hand on my students. I've never sent one of them out of class. I've yelled at them a grand total of 5 times in the last 3 years.  And yet, I would willingly bet that I can get them motivated better than most teachers out there. 

    It's all a matter of taking time and showing respect. 

    Yes, this sounds overly-simplistic. It's actually harder than it sounds. But it works. For me, at least.
