  • DreamCatchers
    2 May 09, 21:58

    I agree that punishment have to vary according to age of students as well as the severity of the offence. There is no ultimate punishment to fit all students of all ages and offences. So punishment is ultimately up to the teacher.

    I suggest that as a teacher, you should do this after punishing the student:

    1. Find a quiet spot and look at the situation from an objective angle. Replay the scene again, but this time imagine yourself being a student.

    2. Ask yourself if the punishment is too severe (did you let your emotions control you?)

    3. Ask yourself if the punishment is able to deter the student from making the same mistake (if it does not, then the punishment has lost its purpose)

     

    Ultimately, I feel that every student does things for a reason, whether its right or wrong, and as a teacher, it is up to us to find out this reason from the student and address it, so the student can understand and learn.

    As students, how many of us prefer a teacher who punishes us harshly after an offence over a teacher who has a strong rapport with us and takes the trouble to find out our trouble and addresses it?
