    Summer 2006 will bring us a much-awaited sequel to the super-blockbuster, "Munnabhai MBBS," titled "Munnabhai MMG"

    MMG? Apparently an abbreviation for 'Meets Mahatma Gandhi,' the filmmakers have issued the following warning for the movie:

    Caution, this movie may contain non-violence.

    Technically not a sequel, the second Munnabhai movie will bring back favorite characters, but in a completely different situation, rather than continuing series. Everyone's favorite professional poser is back in trouble, having been arrested. Munna decides to defend himself in his court trial and sets off for law school. Gracy Singh returns as Dr. Suman Asthana, aka Chinki. Also reprising his role as Munna's sidekick, Circuit, is the very comedically gifted Arshad Warsi. Shah Rukh Khan will also appear, in an undisclosed special appearance, and Munna's love interest will be played by "Parineeta" favorite, Vidya Balan.

    The rights to produce the English version of "Munnabhai MBBS" were purchased by Fox, and are presently negotiating with director Mira Nair and actor Chris Tucker to star, as reported by EGO Bollywood Pulse.

    Vinod Chopra Productions is contemplating taking legal action against McDowell's over a recent advertisement. Vinod Chopra Productions' rib-tickler 'Munnabhai MBBS' is being used to promote alcohol by McDowell's, without the knowledge and approval of the makers.

    A radio spot of ‘Bagpiper’, whose theme revolves around popular characters from the films - ‘Munnabhai’, (Circuit and Dr. Asthana), is currently on air. Such surrogate advertising alleges the production company is in violation of copyright, as the content has been directly picked up from the film.

    Insisting that it is damaging to the reputation of the company, which has never associated its films with any alcohol or tobacco brands, Vinod Chopra Productions is debating whether to carry out legal action against McDowell's.

    Munnabhai M.B.B.S.,' a feel-good comedy about a Mumbai gangster pretending to be a doctor during one of his father's impromptu visits, is now being made into a Hollywood film by 20th Century Fox, sources close to the deal said.

Although the possibility of 'Munnabhai' becoming a Hollywood film has been talked about for sometime now, the film's writer and producer Vidhu Vinod Chopra was reported to have clinched deal with Fox. Chopra himself was noncommittal saying talks were going on.
