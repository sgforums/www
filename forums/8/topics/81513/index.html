    The temperature won't be so cooling soon, my dear. Already we have some cases of 33 degrees. Last year's summer was crazy - it was 39 to 40 degrees at some point. And since Shanghai is near the coast, expect it to be pretty humid as well (never experienced it, but some of the Singaporeans here claim it is more humid that Singapore in August).

    The biggest problem with China is not corruption actually. It is negligence (though both are probably related). I have never had to pay my way around here...but demanding things get done properly really gnaws at your nerves.

    Here in China, life is cheap. People die of negligence in hospitals, in the roads, in their homes, etc, all the time. One of my colleagues dare not eat at their local family-fun restaurants (which has nice and cheap food if you were to ask me).

    And don't bother to act 'like the locals'. The moment you speak your putonghua, they can tell you are not local (though they have a hard time trying to guess who you really are...I've guesses ranging from Koreans to HKers to Taiwanese). Be friendly with them, chat them up a bit in putonghua or Shanghainese, give a 'please-don't-fleece-me' look as you laugh with them, and you will be surprised what kind of discount they will give you.

    Heh...and while it is true that the toilets at the malls are pretty good nowadays in Shanghai (not necessary for other parts of China!), you still come across the disgusting ones...like those at the MRT stations...
