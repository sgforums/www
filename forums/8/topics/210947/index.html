    When buying waterproof jacket you need to consider the following..

    1) waterproofness
    2) breatheability
    3) lightness..

    so far Gore-tex have been able to meet these requirements... that's why SAF chose this lining as their jacket but SAF's one is 2-ply gore-tex which is first generation.

    the current one is 3 ply with XCR.

    Why gore-tex is good is that its waterproofness is PERMANENT. unlike other which is just a thin lining of nylon or other aqua-phobic material. which wears out over time (think SAF field pack or Outdoor/Eastpak bags).

    It is breatheable so that when you sweat or perspire, it does not get retained inside. therefore you do not feel stuffy when wearing the jacket.

    lightness... self-explanatory.

    Brands like Timberland are catered more for the consumer market who sells using "catchy" hi-tech terms...

    you should go for outdoor brands and see what "technology" they implement within the jackets... hint: North Face, Columbia, Mammut, etc etc..

    even of these brands.. i feel North Face and Columbia is quite commericialised already...
