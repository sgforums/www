Not a very 'safe' threshold.

Even a job with the civil service is no longer a sure 'guarantee'. If you live in an HDB flat, and you end up unemployed, at least, you can approach the govt for some sort of assistance so you can keep the roof over your head. You don't have such a safety net if you live in condos.

  • Gazelle
    23 Jul 05, 10:38
    Other things to consider is how much your colleague and his wife have in their CPF OA, and what is the value of your existing property. The maximum CPF contribution right now is $4.5k and that ceiling will eventually come down to $4K I think, therefore the monthly contribution to your OA account will only be around $1K per month.

    Please also note that there has been some adjustment on the CPF medisave account recently. Any surplus in your MS will now flow into the SA instead of OA.

    Alternatively, your colleague can also consider buying an EC, which usually cost around 450K for a decent size 3 bed rooms unit.

    On top of servicing your loan, I think it will be advisable to put aside 30% of that $6K as saving for children education, and your own retirement funds etc.

    If your colleague is consider upgrading to a condo because of the recent 90% loan and 5% cash payment, I think he better think twice.
