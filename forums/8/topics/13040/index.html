    Some parents argue that Primary 6 pupils should be allowed to pick their secondary schools only after their Primary School Leaving Examination (PSLE) results are out. This allows them to make a more realistic choice, they say.

    But the Education Ministry has ruled this out. Doing so would mean a shorter curriculum time for the students, as it entails either bringing forward the PSLE or delaying the start of Secondary 1.

    In any case, the spokesman pointed out, there is no guarantee that the students would get into the school they prefer - no matter when they make their choice.
