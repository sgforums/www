    aiya A level quite simple one lah. just employ the right strategy. so long as you do 10 yr series, there are enough easy questions in the papers to score A. will have hard ones sometimes but its no big deal. they can't kill. thats how i got thru the A levels at least. i had some natural ability for maths and physics, but my chem and bio were in the dumps. as for mcqs, about 3/4 or above are repeated questions or concepts so if you've done tys, mcq should get at least 85 percent. i must say, in my whole life, i have yet to pass a single biology test. my one and only A in bio came in the A levels, simply cos i was familiar with the tys, mcqs and had err, luck. i'm talking from an average person's point of view, not the star student pattern who can do everything. as for S papers, you got to be quite good to score distinction. again, there are enough not-that-hard-if-you-think-a-bit questions to let you score a decent merit. to get ungraded for S paper simply is a lack of technique in exam skill, cos most of those who are allowed to take S paper have the ability to pass them, they simply panicked or had poor time management. some students can handle stress in exams very well, some cannot. some don't need to because they can always answer the questions! in the S paper, it becomes quite interesting when some of those who normally don't need to handle stress face something out of the ordinary. so you see people with 4As and 2 ungraded S papers sometimes and you start wondering! sort of a sieving process for scholarships as well, they want people who can handle some stress first. and of course there are the nuts who get distinction for S paper and get a B or C for the same subject. finally, time management is very important. no point writing 20 pages for a 20 mark question and 2 pages for the next 20 mark question. you'll get more marks if its 10-10.
    these little things in the exam matter a lot more than just studying.
