    Originally posted by zhjjhz888:
    some people say NTU boring but actually isn't NUS the same?
    It really depends on what you want.

    NUS has a broad spectrum of courses. Take for example their business course. It offers a general bus admin degree that covers most of a combined and well-mixed core syllabus.

    NTU has the NBS (Nanyang Business School). After a common course in the first year, they specialise students into a specific course such as applied econs or marketing.

    So while NUS business grads are a jack of all trades, they are a master of none. NTU is vice versa.

    For engineering, NTU offers a very hands-on approach with their Lab projects and assignments. They do have sadistic lecturers who set feverishly difficult exam papers designed to demoralise students however. Their courses range from direct specialisation such as:

    1. Computer engineering
    2. Materials engineering

    and indirect specialisation which includes a common engineering course which branches out into:

    1. Electrical and electronics
    2. Mechanical and aerospace
    3. Civil and environmental

    NUS has a similar focus except they emphasize on concept rather than hands-on.

    With NTU expanding their scope to include School of biological science and mechanical and aerospace, they are offering niche degrees that cater to the emerging industries. The aim is also to join NUS as a full-fledged university by 2010. (earlier if possible)

    It's really up to you to decide which varsity you really want.

    As a note, NUS halls such as Temasek and Kent Ridge required hard core sporting activities or JCRC participation. NTU with a larger residence intake, can retain a larger pool of residents should they choose to stay each year; fighting for points is not as tough as NUS.
