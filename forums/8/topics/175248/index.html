    Letter from Anne Xavier

    I was retrenched in December and while searching for a new job, I wanted to take up some short courses on IT skills.

    As an NTUC union member, I was looking forward to taking advantage of the courses conducted by the NTUC Learning Hub which gives discounts for older workers like me.

    On calling its hotline, I was told that union members who are unemployed are not eligible.

    I was disheartened and, wanting to know the logic behind this policy, I contacted the NTUC customer services hotline.

    I wanted to know what was the objective in offering courses to the employed member, and am of the view that it is more important and more urgent for the unemployed to upgrade their skills in order to be employable.

    The officer I asked for suggestions suggested I find a job first, then apply for the course. She added that I should contact their Career Link office.

    I found this suggestion to be absurd.

    The NTUC website talks about rewarding union members "who take personal responsibility to upgrade their skills".

    My intention was to take the IT course and personally search for a job, and not to seek NTUC's help to find a job, as I am confident my work experience and skills make me employable except for the fact that I lack one particular IT skill.

    Can someone change this policy so that even the unemployed can benefit from being a union member?
    Letter from Anne Xavier
