    My temp holiday job.

    I can be considered as working as a quality controller. Basically, the more senior people among us will do the checking of 300 screws randomly from each lot using the microscope or pin gauge. If there is any problem, like out of the 300, got one screw has defect(s), the whole lot is then given to us to do 100% sorting, meaning we have to check every of the screw using the equipment (microscope, pin gauge or magnifying glass) as required.

    This job is extremely harmful to your eyes. Because we may be using the scope from starting work to going back, the eyes are often subjected to strong light, causing us to be unable to see things clearly by the time we went home, especially if the lighting outside is dim. Even now, 2 weeks since I quitted the job, I still cannot see clearly when the surrounding is dark.
