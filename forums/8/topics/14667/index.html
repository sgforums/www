    Hey no offence but haven't you considered that if you didn't smoke at all, that this raise in the price of ciggarettes wouldn't affect you at all?

    You said the government wants to take away your right to smoke, but have you considered the rights of all the non-smokers in Singapore? I guess if its ok if you only smoke when there's no one around but there're a lot of times when I'm waiting for bus at a bus stop or somewhere else, and its really annoying when someone nearby lights up a ciggarette to smoke. Yea, he gets all the smoke filtered through his ciggarette, but all the bystanders get the unfiltered smoke. Is that fair at all to all these passive smokers?

    Perhaps, instead of seeing that the government is trying to revoke your right to smoke, why not look at it in a way that the government is trying to uphold the rights of people who don't want to smoke? By smoking in the prescence of people, you don't give them much of a choice, do you?

    And at the risk of pissing people off, I want to say I really don't see the point of smoking at all. You're just paying through your nose for an earlier death, and affecting those around you. If the habit of smoking wasn't so widespread I'm sure it would have been banned long ago. To me its no different from smoking opium or taking drugs.

    And lastly, if ciggarettes really played such a big role in the economy, why do you think the government is taking steps to cut down on the number of smokers by spending money on anti-smoking campaigns? Why would they offset the profit they make through imposing higher taxes on ciggarettes by spending the money on discouraging the habit? And not to mention the prohibition of smoking in air-conditioned public places. If it was really played such a big part in the economy, why would they discourage people from smoking and make it so restricted? Alease think before you make such accusations. As much as we like to make fun of our government and with good reason, they're not always out to get us. As for the issue of money, its just that it has been proven as the most effective disincentive, and is why Singapore is called the "Fine City". The effectiveness of campaigns are usually less than stellar and persuasion doesn't usually work at all. Personally as a non-smoker, I'm glad the government is taking such steps to encourage smokers to kick the habit.

    Before I end off, I really don't mean to piss of anyone....just please try not to be so selfish.
