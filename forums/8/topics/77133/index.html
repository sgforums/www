    Quoted from The Straits Times

    China's competent ones
    Comes with a study mama at the primary school level.
    Recruited from China at Secondary 3.
    Aces mathematics and science.
    Struggles with English.
    Surprisingly, has to put effort into Higher Chinese, because more memorisation of idioms is required here than in China.
    At university, tends to hang out in his own large clique in the canteen near the 'economy rice' stall, study rooms, and in computer classes.

    India's intelligentsia
    Scholarship holders come in various waves during secondary school, junior college and university.
    Others come as early as primary school, if one or both parents work here.
    Makes friends easily because he speaks English.
    Aces exams because standards in India are higher.
    Often spotted in debating clubs, sports tournaments and computing classes.
    Struggles with dietary concerns; vegetarians cannot find vegetarian food easily.

    Asean's achievers
    Comes either on the bond-free Asean scholarship or their parents' fortunes.
    Lives in hostels or with appointed guardians.
    Struggles with English if he did not come from an English-medium school.
    Achievers are often present among school prefects and student councillors.
    Brats tote the latest cellphones, branded clothes, zip around in cabs and coast until they go to a 'soft' university in Australia or the United States.
    Often blends in with Singaporean students completely by the time he goes to university.
    Sometimes absent for a few extra days after Chinese New Year break because of celebrations at home.
