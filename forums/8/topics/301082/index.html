    Originally posted by Stanley Ho:
    Been there done that but they don't even know how to explain diff between pte, sole Propiertorship..... they say I need to go somewhere else. They only do the paperwork of setting up only
    A sole proprietor is someone who owns an unincorporated business by himself or herself. The company and the owner are the same legal entity. Which means the owner is completely held liability of the company. It is the simplest legal structure.

    While a private limited company is a legal entity separated from its officers, with its own profits, losses, assets and liabilities. Ownership of a private limited company is established through the division of shares. The more shares you got, the more profits you gain.

    Unlike a public limited company, a private limited company is restricted from selling shares to the public. All private limited companies protect the associated officers from financial liability should the company encounter problems.

    Hope this helps. Btw i don't think this is important in a biz plan writing.. usually.
