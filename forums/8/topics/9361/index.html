    On love...(Oh yeah...that 4 letter word no one can escape from...L-O-V-E)

    As young girls, most of us grew up listening to fairytales starring 'Prince Charming' or some knight in shining armor, or as we now call him 'The [imaginary]Mr. Right'. In a sense we have learned to be idealistic and somewhat fantasy-driven in the practical or theoretical search for this person. We often set out 'qualifications' that this person must meet which ends up looking like a greedy and indecisive child's christmas list.
    "All I want is an attractive, intelligent, financially secure, ambitious, athletic, humourous, articulate, romantic, confident, and communicative guy - is that too much to ask!?" is what we often hear over drinks amongst girlfriends and often playing in repeat mode in our own minds. We say this, when perhaps the reality of the situation is that all we want is someone that will understand us and make us feel loved unconditionally - then maybe we'll be able to settle for less of the above (maybe). As human beings, the one thing that we all have in common is love. Yet, just as unique as our DNA, so is our concept of this phenomenon and the way in which we approach it. Everyone believes in some theory about what happens to someone after they die, but no-one ever knows for sure. This is a somewhat morbid, yet illustrative comparison to the notion of love - one of the great mysteries of life. To define love, however, is not impossible. We create our idea of love from experience, inexperience, others' experience, or perhaps even movies or song lyrics. Whatever the case, it is specific to the individual. My own definition of love would be that it has to be a two-way street. To be 'in love' with someone requires that the other person feel exactly the same way in heart and in mind. For example, I cannot say that I'm in love with Ekin Cheng for obvious reasons (though I wish that I could!).

    Confused and somewhat hopeless about the whole issue at hand, my two closest girlfriends and I pondered about this concept this weekend on San Francisco's beautiful wharf. We sat on our blanket, admiring the glitter effect the sun had on the water, sipping Starbucks and reading one another real life love stories from a compilation book. It made me realize that every single person "owns" a collection of stories of love that makes her the person she is today - one that is unique to only ourselves, and cannot be compared, duplicated, or judged in any way. Also, that love, although felt by all, is understood by few, if any. We all have our own ideas about what constitutes love - like receiving poetry, having our passions unlocked, or being complemented by your partner when waking up with no make-up and your hair being in a mess! (oh the horror!). All in all, it is the little things in love that count. Love is something that could make the most articulate person at a lost for words, or make the most stubborn heart become mellow. It is something that we all set rules upon, yet break it when we do happen to find our that certain somebody. It is something that we all ask for advice about, but only end up following our own hearts.
