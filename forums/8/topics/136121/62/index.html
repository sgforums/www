    Aug 14, 2005
    HK man infected with flesh-eating disease

    HONG KONG - A Hong Kong man is critically ill after he was infected by flesh-eating bacteria, the fourth such case reported since May.

    It said the victim suffers from necrotising fasciitis, more commonly known as a flesh-eating disease because the bacterial infection rapidly destroys soft tissue.

    It can cause bloodstream infections and death within the same day, especially in people with liver conditions.

    Hong Kong's Department of Health said investigations are underway.

    Earlier, two women aged 55 and 62 and a man aged 78 were infected with the disease.

    Two died from the disease.

    None had recent travel history.

    The health department said the bacterial cause of the infection, Vibrio vulnificus, is naturally present in warm seawater and may cause infection through open wounds.

    Hong Kong authorities have warned people to avoid exposing open wounds to sea water. -- AP
