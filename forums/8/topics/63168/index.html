Building a new empire in a company?

  • Honeybunz
    2 Dec 03, 21:57
    Not sure if this has been a trend in most companies...

    When a new guy takes over a high post in a company, he will bring in his own people from his ex company to the new one. He will do so either by creating new job positions or removing the existing ones.

    I see this happening in one company that I know of. Last year there was a change in CEO and the CEO brought in his own guy to fill up the marketing director post and attempted to fill up some manager posts, again, with his own people.

    This year there was an announcement:
    In the next month, this same company will have a change of executive chairman. This soon-to-be chairman (who is currently only a boardmember) is already bringing his own guy to fill up the marketing director post after getting rid of the existing marketing director. All the junior staff and executives in this company are speculating whether the new marketing person will replace the existing staff with his own people when he comes in.

    Is this culture happening everywhere?
    Does it mean whenever a new boss come in, he will bring his own people and sack the old ones?
    Who do you think of this culture?

    I guess a company will only change a CEO in 2 situations: Firstly the current CEO decides to retire or going for greener pastures and challenges. Secondly, he/she is sacked because results are not there.

    I would not understand the need to change all the staff in the first instance but if the reason for a change of CEO is the 2nd instance, then such actions are needed. A CEO who comes into a company to do firefighting needs results FAST thus he has no time to build rapport, build team spirit with people he is not familiar with, he has to assemble a team he is comfortable with in a short time and whom can give him results fast. So i guess thats how i see it.
