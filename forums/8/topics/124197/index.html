    Originally posted by skyfoo:
    picture a guy in a moving vehicle(bus). the spot he is standing on the bus is A and B is the spot on the ground which he is over. so when the guy jumps, will he land on A or B?

    a similar qn, i once saw a bee flying about in a bus, so does it mean that the bee is travelling at the speed of the bus in additional to its own speed?
    1st question:

    In this case u are talking about velocity in two directions. If you were to study physics, u will learn that there is such a thing called "resultant velocity" i.e. the final velocity with a specified direction between two velocities of different speeds and direction...

    In this case, u moving forward at speed of the bus, taking that as north direction. You jump off, assuming at 90 degrees to the path of motio, let say east direction.

    Neglecting wind resistance, you will land on a spot on the ground but further ahead than spot B since you are moving in a North- East movement. In other words, the answer should be A or rather a spot parallel to location A.

    2nd question

    Yes, in addition to the speed of the bus, the bee is also moving at its own speed. This is provided that the bee is actually moving forward in the bus in the SAME direction. If the bee is moving against the bus direction, its velocity in the DIRECTION of the bus is less than velocity of the bus...
