    DUBAI : A US hostage has been killed "at the hands" of Al-Qaeda's alleged chief in Iraq, Abu Mussab al-Zarqawi, an Arab television station reported, showing extracts from a video.

    The man said that his name was Benjamin Ford, from the city of San Fransisco in California, said the Al-Arabiya channel, adding that the video had appeared on a website.

    The hostage called for the "immediate" departure of US forces from Iraq, warning that the foreign troops would otherwise face death, it said.

    The video showed a young man wearing a white t-shirt, sitting on a white plastic chair with his hands tied behind his back.

    The man was shown speaking to the camera, although the television aired the video without sound.

    The television did not show parts of the video of the man being killed.

    Still pictures of dead Iraqis -- allegedly "victims" of the foreign forces in Iraq -- were shown in the video, said the television presenter.

    There is no record of anyone by the name of Benjamin Ford among the list of hostages, including several Americans, being held by various groups in embattled Iraq.

    The United States, which heads the force battling insurgents in Iraq, blames Zarqawi for much of the violence and has put a 25 million dollar bounty on his head.

    Zarqawi's group has claimed responsibility for several bloody attacks in Iraq, and the killing of some foreigners it had taken hostage.

    IRAQ EXECUTION A FAKE

    A video which purportedly showed an American being decapitated by his captors in Iraq was a hoax.

    The "victim", Benjamin Vanderford, has now admitted that he videotaped the staged beheading at his friend's house using fake blood.

    American Vanderford, 22, said he began distributing the footage on the internet several months ago as part of "a stunt".

    One reason was to gain attention but the other was to "just make a statement on these type of videos and how easily they can be faked".

    He had originally made it as part of an unsuccessful campaign for local elections in San Francisco.

    Video footage of the hoax was posted on an Arabic website on Saturday and screened by Arabic TV news stations.

    In it, Vanderford said: "My name is Benjamin Ford. I am from San Francisco, California.

    "We need to leave this country right now. If we don't, everyone is gonna be killed in this way."

    The video then showed him being beheaded.

    The stunt was exposed as the Iraqi government ordered Arabic news station al Jazeera to close its Baghdad offices for one month.

    Interior Minister Falah al-Naqib said this week that Arabic satellite channels were encouraging kidnappings by showing images of hostages threatened with executions.

    No formal reason for the ban has been given yet.
