    Another idea: To keep yourself healthy without spending a whole truckload of cash on gym memberships or swimming pools, try getting your own bicycle, and try reaching destinations that are physically and mentally trying. (For 6 hours we rode from CCK to West Coast then to VivoCity)

     

    A good bike can be had for around $300 (with decent gear systems and brakes).
    $300 is just worth 1 year of gym and swimming pool entries (once per week) whereas if you get a bike, it's worth a lifetime of freestyle exercising.

    Step 1: Plan your route
    Be mighty ambitious. Try aiming for somewhere far. For starters, a 40KM distance back and forth should suffice.

    Step 2: Get some friends
    Friends are important, for you will most likely land yourself in surprising situations. So their help will always be crucial. Imagine losing your direction in the remote Lim Chu Kang farm area!

    Step 3: Plan budget
    For my case, we always try to aim for $0 (In the end we still end up spending on a macdonald's meal everytime)
    For those who dont want to spend any cash, bring your own food e.g. biscuits.

    Step 4: Plan starting time and end time
    My advice: Start early, return late. At around 7am, the cool air gets you motivated and relaxed.

    Step 5: Cycle like hell
    Don't stop for anything, the only thing you should think about is reaching the end point in the least time possible.  

     

    End result: Most often you will realize a substantial increase in muscle mass in your thighs. For distances of >60KM, you will shed at least 1.5KG.  

     

     

    Notice: I cannot stress this enough, but ALWAYS abide by road safety rules. No point in losing your life while in the process of living cheap.
