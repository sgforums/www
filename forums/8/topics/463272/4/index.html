    Sorry for the late reply Atake. This is my advise to you Atake. If you want to go for a semi-retirement and later for full retirement, first of all you must make sure that you are debt free. Second, your children are fully independent financially and they are not depending from you or your wife for financial support and likewise, you and your wife must be financially independent too from your children. So you and your wife do not financially burden your children...well...unless they voluntarily wish to support you and your wife financially during your semi-retirement or later when you go for full retirement...by all means take it. Thirdly, if you have bought a property in Malaysia, in JB or Penang, and have fully paid on the property and have no financial debt, it will be good for you to try out the semi-retirement process and later for the full retirement. When you go for a semi-retirement process, your work will be on contract basis or on freelance basis....you can work on a flexible working hours. About your preference of going into semi-retirement or later full retirement by the seaside, well, in JB those seaside properties like in Danga Bay and Setulang Laut, they are located right on the beach front facing Singapore northern coast line. However, the beaches are not as great like those beaches on the eastern coastline of Malaysia. I think, if you want a nice seaside beach house, Desaru area will be a very nice place for you. Remember this Atake, if you are debt free, its easier for you to go for semi-retirement because you just need to work to past time and your financially commitment is only your daily expenses. And if you are single, debt free and financially your are strong, then by all means go for that semi-retirement....but then again....at what age you wish to go for semi-retirement and later full retirement, its your choice and planning. Good luck to you.

    Hi Atake. Its the other way round actually. Semi-retirement you are still working and you still have many friends. Its when you go into full retirement then you probably feel lonely if you are not active. When you do part time or assignment basis work during your semi-retirement, you are no longer ambitious or career hunger to climb the corporate ladder anymore rather you just want to past time and earn some money. You can do jobs like relief taxi driver, security, part time office work etc. The other way you can do is to work for few months full time and then stop work and relax. Then work again if you feel like working not because you need the money but you want to pass time. When you go for full retirement then probably you will face the emotional phase problems like feeling unwanted and unimportant anymore because you are longer working and earning income. If you are not active you will feel lonely and depression will set in. I'm 50 now and I'm into my semi retirement now for 2 years now. I keep myself active by taking long walks and I exercise regularly. I mix around with my neighbours in JB regardless of their age and gender. I find hobbies to keep myself occupied....like cooking, gardening, reading etcs. The most important thing when you want to go for semi-retirement and full retirement later.......YOU MUST HAVE MONEY IN YOUR SAVING ACCOUNT. And keep yourself healthy and remain active as a human being with your family, friends and the community.
