    S'pore's most important commodity is manpower. We sell this valued-added property to gain prominence and be the best in asia and the world...the question is are we in the right direction? Over the years, we have been guided by the market demand and supply forces on wat occuaptions are most needed.

    But i feel that the right way forward is not to curb our studies and aspirations on only wat we have available. We should not limit our new generation (even our current batch for that matter) just becoz of our jobs and pay cheques.

    Our universities boast of being world class but all we're doing is raising the annual fees and filling up our existing courses with more lawyer/doctors/engineers wannabes. Throw in accountancy and business studies and we have a pretty "exotic" number of courses available.

    Many ppl who consider photography, marine biology, archieology, palentology, conservational geology have to take their studies overseas...worst they can definitely find no niche market in s'pore for their subsequent talents; which means they have to permanetly work overseas or drop their "wild" dreams.

    How can we claim to be world class when we stifle our youths with a limited set of options? How can we even smugly look at ourselves and say this is truly wat we wanted to do? Go ahead..ask any would be engineer or accountant in s'pore (locals of course)if they're studying wat they really wanna do...many if not most will offer negative responses...

    The fact remains that we have a long way to go before we dress ourselves in smart suits and call ourselves "world class".
