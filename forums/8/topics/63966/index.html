    I ORDed long time ago. Being a specialist is fuck up. Neither here not there. You are either a man or an officer. Never a specialist. You will understand the reason when you do your reservist next time.

    Anyways, SISPEC training need not necessarily be bad. Especially, if your company has one or two white horses in them. But during my time, I had it bad, real bad. My company had the most sadistic instructors who were hell-bent to "torture" us both mentally and physically. The company opposite mine, Charlie, had it good as they had three white horses in the company.

    My company, Delta, always book-out late, book-in early, kena tekan the most, most confinements, as well as the toughest trainings. Only one or two instructors in my company were more welfare, and they were treated with disdain by the rest of the instructors.

    My company not surprisingly, got the best IPPT record, and was chosen to lead the entire unit up Elephant Hill for our graduation march. Hehe, I can still remember the rest of the companies struggling to keep up to us.

    At the end of the day, it really depends on your company and the officers in charge, it does not matter from which unit you are in. Well, unless you are joining some elite units like the Commandos or SOF. But be it SISPEC or OCS or the rest of the other units, the training standards and welfare differs from each company even in the same unit. Just pray that you get into a welfare company. I still remembered my instructors shaking their heads at us on our first day at SISPEC when they realised that we are going to join Delta.
