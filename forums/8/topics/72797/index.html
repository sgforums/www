    If your projects does NOT requires you to print in High-resolution(300 DPI) artwork, I suggest you can make do with a default 72DPI resolution since it will be viewed on screen only.

    On the other hand, you might wanna do a High-res one so you could sent for print for your portfolio. BUT, working in a hi-res canvas will slows down your program if your machine does not have enuff RAM. hmmm... too technical for u?

    Anyway, you dun have to sketch your artwork in colours. Draw them with black pencils(or black coloured pen) and scan it in as a RGB image. Why RGB? Cos for ONE: by working in a RGB mode in Photoshop, it allows you to use additional functions than in CMYK mode. TWO: your artwork will be view on screen, so RGB is closest/accurate to the colour you would see it in other monitor. Unless you are sending it for print, use CMYK.
