    Hi Tired,

    The motion speaks that the "Teens of To-Day Have Lost Their Idealism". So, first of all, you have to see what kind of definition which the proposition would take. First, confine your definition of teens, which you would take the age group from 13 to 21 and take the today as of last decade. Then what do you mean by losing your idealism? Idealism would be thought of the set of values which may or may not be achievable but nevertheless we could set ourselves a goal to the future.

    The proposition is likely to use the case where the teenagers in US has digressed themselves into the violence, and the best example is the Colorado shooting case. You can argue on this based on two points :

    1)The Pop Culture :

    Pop culture or popular culture would be one of the good indicators to looking into the teens of today, since the teenagers contributed a significant proportion to what is pop culture : for example, drinking coke, wearing swatch watches, the kind of music they listen, the food they eat and up to other things which you name it they have it. Be more specific go to the songs, demostrate the one would see that in the early 80s, the songs in that era are pretty pessimistic and it reflected the mood of that time, but now it featured on songs which are more optimistic, which indicate teenagers have preferred to seek happiness. This argument is used in a debate on idealism, and it turned out to be a good point to shoot down the proposition.

    2)Values of teenagers today

    This point is more subtle. You have to quote examples of teens doing social work, community services and getting involved with adventure groups and stuff like that. You may quote examples of teenagers (go to the nearest newspaper) who have used their time well to do all sort of things.

    This is what I can offer.. It seems to be quite a good motion to play. If you are debating in the British kind of system which I am presently doing, you can always twist this motion into something else to debate, or confine the teens of today to just British teenagers, so that you can confine the scope fo the debate. Not to mention the best part, where your opponent can stand up in between your speech between the 1st and 4th minute and raise a point of infammation, and you can choose to answer it or not...
