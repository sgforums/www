    regarding your topic on enbloc sales, you have to agree that there can only be two sides of the argument: firstly, some residents would want to stay put for sentimental reasons, whether be it for fostered friendships or age-old reminiscences. the other side would be that, the windfall that the owner reaps in the event of an enbloc sale. we all know that if a property is older than 10 years, 80% of the residents have to agree before the sale can take place, as compared to 100% (currently) if the property tendered is less than 10 years old. now: 80% of the residents have decided to reap the monetary rewards from a good decision to buy the property years ago. the other 20%, seem to want to do all that want to stop this 80% from making their millions? isn't that a tad selfish? now, one might say that it could take years or friendships and sentimental values aside could well disappear along with the property, but i ask you this: if your friendships are that strong, can't the money, (often at least twice the market value) buy a new residence close to your friends, or enough to bring what sentimental value along with you? not many singaporeans get the chance to make a million dollars or more overnight, and it is a lot of hard work - the people who are against the enbloc sale merely heighten their difficulties and show others how selfish they are by planting their posteriors firmly on a pot of gold.
