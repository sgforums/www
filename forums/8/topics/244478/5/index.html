    10 Jun 07, 16:06
    I think like most developed economies, consumption based tax (such as GST) will increase. Hence I have no objections against the GST increase. But what should also happen at the same time is that there is a decrease in non-consumption based taxes, such as income tax. In other words, as GST increases, income tax should fall. It is that second part that we are not seeing.

    Another example is road tax [I got no car ]. With increases of ERP rates (consumption), there should be a corresponding decrease in road tax (non-consumption based). We are not seeing that second part.

    GST offset packages are one-off goodies that make no econmic sense. They are really only to gain political capital and citizen goodwill.

    Nope, not expecting them to reduce road tax. Just 2 points:

    (i) increases in certain taxes should only be at the expense of decreasing others. The idea that we need to keep accumulating foreign reserves because someday we may need it, is correct in principle, but needs to be balanced against the need to provide a decent cost of living to the citizens. Any government in the world can use the fear tactic and say we need to increase taxes, just in case we need the money down the road. A good and mature government is one that satisfies that need, but balances it against other concerns.

    (ii) increases in taxes require a check and balance approach. Until today, I have not heard a single convincing argument why gst needs to be raised. Help the poor and lower middle class? Using a small fraction of the profits made by Temasek (and maybe Singapore Pools also) each year will be sufficient for such expenses.
