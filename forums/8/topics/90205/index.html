    from the ST ...

    Crowds are gone from this chicken rice stall

    IT WAS anything but a normal Thursday at the Sin Kee Famous Chicken Rice Stall in Margaret Drive. The crowds were missing, for one.

    For another, helpers who would otherwise be brusquely serving the dish to workers on their lunch break were doing the unthinkable - trying to entice passers-by to take a table.

    But the biggest giveaway was that stall manager Steven Goh, 45, was actually sitting down with nothing to do.

    He said: 'Usually, you won't even get this table to sit and talk. It would be packed during lunchtime.

    'If the situation continues, we may take a break and see how it goes. Workers may need to clear their annual leave,' he said, obviously worried.

    An instant later, however, he brightened.

    'Bird flu affects only a farm in Kelantan. Other farms should still be fine... The situation may just be temporary,' he said.

    It had better be, he added. The last time there was a bird flu outbreak in this part of the world, his sales dropped by 30 to 40 per cent.

    It could be much worse this time around, said Mr Goh.

    'The situation is different, because it now affects our main supplier of fresh chickens.'

    There's no question of using frozen chickens either. 'The taste of frozen chicken is different. It won't be as tasty as fresh ones,' he said.
