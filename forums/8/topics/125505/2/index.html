    my own version for life in poly is similar to rock's

    year1, duper blur sotong. guai guai attend all the tutorials and watever sh-t
    first few weeks of poly, the whole class will go for lunch at the same foodcourt and sit together.

    better go for orientation and make friends with ppl from diff classes and stage, useful when u didnt do reports etc.

    2nd sem,,,slack liao.

    year2, things start to get harder for u, the modules really get abit traumatising

    year3, do FYP liao. i've gone thru this so i have some clear cut warnings.

    1. look at the list of proj and supervisors. most will be teaching u now or before. choose those u really like, respect, love. dont choose those that u have moderate r/s with. they might become damn nasty and sarcastic to u.

    2.grp mates, choose those equally smart, stupid, hardworking or slack ones. ppl who are generally willing to do work. bright teammates may not be the best choice.i have bright teammates but 1 has done stupid things like super heating alcohol in a pressurised chamber.

    3. dont think a project shld be easy, clear cut etc. u will be terribly wrong. painfully wrong.

    4. prepare to sacrifice loads of sleep and time for FYP. and hair too.

    and in year3, u will be extremely pressed for time.

    i love to put it as such

    year 1, walk in the park
    year 2, walk on hot stones
    year 3, walk in hell

    1 thing to note. yr O lvl scores are totally irrelevant in poly

    u can be good in english ( at o lvls) yet can get D in language modules
    A in chem phy, can get A to D in chem modules. and the chem modules are a far higher lvl and way diff context in poly. things u nvr knew or asked abt will be thought.

    A in maths(possible exception) possible to get ave grades.
