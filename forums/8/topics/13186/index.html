    KUALA LUMPUR, Malaysia -- Malaysia's main opposition Islamic party is demanding the government withdraw a TV spot it says portrays party leaders as terrorists and implies links with Osama bin Laden's al Qaeda network.

    Nasharuddin Mat Isa, Secretary General of the Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party (PAS) said the film -- aired nightly during the evening news on Malaysia's two state-owned networks -- was designed to discredit the party ahead of a state by-election Saturday.

    "We have no link whatsoever with al Qaeda and no link to any other terrorist group, local or internationally," he said.

    The TV spot splices together images of PAS leaders with footage of an Afghan woman being executed, shots of U.S. warplanes bombing Afghanistan, and slow-motion scenes of the Malaysian Islamic extremists recently convicted of plotting to overthrow the government.

    PAS itself is not named in the clip although the commentary refers to its policies in the two Malaysian East Coast states that it controls, including its restrictions on Muslim women.
