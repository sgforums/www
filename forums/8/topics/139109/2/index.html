    HDB flats are all 99 yrs leasehold, even Exec Condos. So after 99 yrs up, you are to return to HDB your flat and get your butt to live elsewhere.
    In reality, HDB flats get en bloc about 25 to 30 yrs and the lease would start over and you have to pay for the upgrading. This is the govt's way to ensure revenue, apart from the 'rent' collection monthly.

    For private property that are 99 yrs leasehold, you can throw money to the govt and top up the lease. Govt is pretty ok with that for now.

    For freehold, although you need not throw money to govt to own the property but after 25 to 30 years, your property becomes so run-down, you need upgrading as well. You would have to spend money for upgrading as well. No difference.

    In any case if govt needs the land where your freehold property is, they will tell you to get lost by paying you off. So no such thing as owning a property in Singapore.

    The humid weather in Sg ensures gov would would not be outta money as flats needs upgrading. It's all carefully calculated by the government already to ensure constant flow of revenue for the govt from property.

    IMO, renting a property long term would be the best way to go and you don't pay maintainance fees and property tax as a tenant. This Asian mentally to 'own' a place needs to change. Nobody 'owns' any property in Singapore.

    Since it's renting from the govt for 99 yrs, why not rent it from others instead of govt. You save a hell of a lot more!
