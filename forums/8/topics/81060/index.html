    hmm...
    Go to an Optometrist first. Check if there is any signs of cornea scratches. If there is, it will take about 1 day to 1 month for your cornea to heal depending on the layers involved. Most importantly, check the cornea for signs of ulcers, or giant pupillary conjunctivitis.
    If there is ulcers, you will need to see an ophthalmologist/eye doctor straightaway!
    If there is GPC, you will need to clean your lens thoroughly.
    Check your lens if there is any tear. The tear in lens may irritate your eyes as cornea may be scratched.
    Do protein remover procedure on your conventional lens. If you experience irritation after this procedure, that may mean that you will need to change a pair of lenses as there is already protein and toxin buildups in your lenses.
    Have you changed to a new brand of contact lens solution recently? If thats the case, it may be a cause for your irritation too. That will mean that your eye is sensitive to the solution used. If thats that case, you will need to change to another brand of contact lens solution again.
    Cheers.
