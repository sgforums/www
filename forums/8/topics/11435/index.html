    My dear fellow Singaporeans,

    The Stanford University in the US is conducting a distributed computing project called Genome@Home that utilizes idle times of internet connected personal computers from all over the world to design genes for
    proteins. One of the purposes is to find better pharmaceuticals to cure diseases.

    Thousands of individuals have joined the project and each individual can select a team to associate him/herself with. A team called SINGAPORE has been created since March 15, 2001. It will be soon ranked
    as the top 100 teams. Currently, a team from Italy is at the top of the rank.

    If you would like to volunteer in this project, either for fun or for a good cause, please visit Genome@Home Team Singapore website at http://www.geocities.com/genohome. We need more members to help us bring Team Singapore to a higher position in the world ladder.

    More people, more fun, more science!

    Genome@Home - Team Singapore
