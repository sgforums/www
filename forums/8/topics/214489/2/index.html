    Specific Heat Capacity- change in temperature
    -Q=M x c2 x delta tita (w/e), where

    q=energy/heat transferred(joules) , M= mass (kilogrammes), c2 = specific heat capacity (jou;es per kg per degree celcius

    specific heat capacity is the amount of heat used to increase temperature of UNIT MASS of Y by a degree celcius, (depends on substance)

    -C=m x c2=q/delta tita

    C=Y's heat capacity, c2= Y's specific heat capacity, q=energy involved etc etc

    Heat capacity = amount of heat needed to raise temp of BODY by a degree celcius
