    Employees in China have the shortest working hours in Asia. This was according to the Hudson Report for Asia. The report revealed that among the 2500 employers surveyed in Asia, 41% of respondents in China worked more than 50 hours a week and 7% worked over 60 hours. Media, public relations and advertising companies reported the longest hours with 63% of staff working over 50 hours a week. China also witnessed the largest increase in working hours with 58% of employees working longer hours than they did two years ago, the report said.

    In comparison, Hong Kong had long working hours with the highest proportion of employees putting in over 50 hours a week, accounting 70% of respondents. Twenty per cent of those interviewed reported that they worked over 50 hours a week. In addition, 38% of employees had seen their hours increase over the last two years, the report stated.

    Though long hours had traditionally been part of the work culture in Japan, it was noted to be changing. In general, the survey found that employees were no longer expected to work long hours but 63% respondents worked more than 50 hours a week. On an average, 37% respondents work more hours than two years ago.

    Singapore also had a high percentage of employees working long hours with 64% of respondents across all sectors working 50 hours week, and 19% of respondents putting in more than 60 hours. Also, 37% of Singaporean respondents reported an increase in working hours over the last two years.
