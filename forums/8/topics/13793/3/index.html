    HArlow, do your homework. They DO receive a pay this high.

    FYI, On 29 June 2000, DPM LHL announced massive pay rises for cabinet ministers. PM Goh's annual salary jumped 14% to $2.25 million, or $187,000 a month, five times that of the US president (in 20 Jan 2001, U.S. presidential annual pay was increased to usd$400k, still approximately 4x lower, with VICE president's salary increased to only usd$171,500).
    The salary for the most junior minister was increased by 12% to S$968,000, putting Singapore's ministers well ahead of their counterparts in the U.S., the United Kingdom and many other countries.

    What gives?
    1. Cost of living in Singapore is much less than that of U.S. and UK.
    2. Remember how small Singapore is, and how big U.S. and UK is.
    3. Compare the amount responsibilities that Singapore ministers have, to that of the U.S. and UK counterparts.

    So what if the economy WAS going well, when other bigger countries were doing as well, if not better, with their ministers paid less. In fact, Malaysia is doing better than Singapore during this recession, and Singapore was the only country that registered negative growth, while Taiwan registered positive growth for the fiscal year.

    A meagre paycut is nothing compared to the retrenchment rate. $150 (10%) paycut to someone earning $1500 a month means a lot, as he needs the money to feed his family. But what is $8,000 to someone earning $80k a month? Golf club membership?

    The argument "pay increase = low corruption" is the joke of the entire world, and it questions the integrity of those serving the nation.
