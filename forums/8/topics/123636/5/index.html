    you from operation side? you know the different between ESP and ETS? seem like you not really sure how the ESP, ETS actually work also the ECB in the train. maybe you want go reconfirm again before you mislead the public with your limited knowledge.
    ESP (Emergency Stop Plunger) are located on SMRT's EWL and NSL platforms while ETS (Emergency Train Stop) are located on SBS Transit's NEL platforms. ECB (Emergency Communication Button) are inside the trains only.

    ESP: Push the ESP at the platform to stop a train. When pressed, approaching train will stop. If the train is already at the platform, it will not start.

    ETS: To activate ETS, lift the seal at the front of the ETS and press the red button to activate it. You can speak to staff through intercom once the call is acknowledged (push-to-talk).

    ECB: Press the button to speak to the train officer/Operations Control Centre person. Help will be rendered, if necessary. If the button is pressed before the train starts off from the platform, the train will not start. If it is pressed while the train is moving, the train will stop at the next station but will not move until the button is reset. Use the ECB only in emergency so as to avoid unnecessary train delay.
