    hmm...think this problem is settled liao..

    but anyway...for those in the yr 1 engineering in univ or poly...

    easy way is :

    1). Use equilibrium of static forces, i.e. sum of all forces in the y-direction = 0.
    2). Use "sum of moments about point A = 0".
    3). Use "sum of moments about point B=0".

    So you got 3 unknowns, Fa, Fb and X(dist. from point b, when the human load will tip the beam", and 3 equations, therefore you can solve for all.

    Additional boundary condition is that, just when the beam "about" to tip over, Fa is zero, i.e. the reaction force at pivot A is zero, that is there is no load.

    Simple.
