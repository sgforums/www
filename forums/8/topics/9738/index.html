    IN A block of 100 flats, a painter is kept employed by painting one flat each month from January to November. The flats are painted in the same order and he takes a holiday every December. If my flat is painted in August 2000, it will next be painted in: a. July 2008; b. September 2009; c. September 2001; d. October 2011; e. November 2012

    A hardware store sells numerals for house numbers. It has large quantities of the numerals 3, 5 and 8, but no others. How many different house numbers with no more than three digits can be made from these numerals? a. 33; b. 21; c. 27; d. 36; e. 39

    ANSWER:

    Answer: b -- September 2009. The painter paints 11 flats each year, so since 99 = 9 x 11, after exactly nine years all other flats will have been painted. This occurs in August 2009, so my flat will again be painted in September 2009.

    Answer: e -- 39. The single-digit numbers possible are 3, 5 and 8, three in all. For two-digit numbers, we have three possibilities in the first place and three in the second place, giving 3 x 3 = 9 such numbers. For the three-digit numbers, we have three possibilities in the first, second and third places, giving 3 x 3 x 3 = 27. House numbers possible: 3 + 9 + 27 = 39.
