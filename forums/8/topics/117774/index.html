    kusu island lots of big garfish, can be quite fun but need to know how to tie double hook line and skill to bring up. no need lah, for newbie fisherman who just want some quick action and learn, dun want to spend a bomb ... tsk.. my advice.

    Equipment:

    fishing rod: Get the telescope type which can fold in and quite small to put in the bag. Damage abt $12-15 bucks get @ beach rd, geylang or changi

    reel: simple one with 10-15pound line will do, sometime they bundle the reel and rod together mebbe all in $20-$25 or less, best place to hunt for bargains is beach rd.

    lead stone or fishing weignts: pls dun go buy monster size stone hor small small will do, best is get a few used spark plug from garage or HDB carpark sometimes can find le.

    swivel: some small swivel can le, 1 packet 80c - $1.2

    Hooks: get those already tied hook jig lines for catching small fish, 1 packet abt $1.2, get abt 3 packets enuf le. Buy those with the smallest hooks

    Location: Favorite place for 70%-80% chance of catching fish East Coast Bedok Jetty. Go daytime preferbly just after a heavy rain for best results and very fast action the taban will be most hungry by then. The bad thing abt this place is getting there are no toilets ard, only bushes nearby but have to quar jwee as there are women blader and joggers ard. Bring own food and water.

    Location: Other place is clifford pier, but patience waiting game this place you need totally different equipment, hit chance low 20-30% but there are monsters, meter long size kim bak lor have been fished out before. Good thing abt clifford pier is easy transport since it's smack in town area, there is also a public toilet, a mama shop to buy titbits, a small pau/timsum shop if you hungry and if you bored can walk over to look at KTV girl singing at nearby open air lounge. Or if you thirsty just hop on to a floating pub moored closeby. But as I say this place is patience game.

    Location: Tanjung Berlayer(aka Labrador Park)
    This place is quite good, tamban and other fish hit chance abt 40%-60%, toilet also nt very far, transport a bit hassle unless you drive but still within walking distance from main rd. But the biggest downside is the hot sun, the whole jetty has been refurbished but there is only 1 or 2 shelters on the jetty and most of the time occupied by regulars so have to share. Have to bring own food and drinks though there is an ah pek occasionally selling ice-cream on his bike, by 6pm you shud leave because it gets dark & ppl say hantu(though been there so many x nvr seen any)

    For newbie fishermen best is to head out to bedok jetty and get yourself trained & hooked to the hobby before venturing elsewhere enjoy har.
