Singapore students nowadays too soft, individualistic: Tharm

  • wuming78
    16 May 04, 21:44
    SINGAPORE : Acting Education Minister Tharman Shanmugaratnam says Singapore students nowadays are individualistic, soft and not hungry enough, unlike the country's pioneers 30 to 40 years ago.

    Speaking during his Ministerial Community visit to Nee Soon South, he said that to him, this, not any economic issue, is the biggest challenge.

    Back in the days when Nee Soon was plantation land, people had the "gotong royong" or community spirit.

    But these days, Mr Tharman says youngsters are more concerned about themselves.

    And many are not game, choosing indoor CCAs like IT clubs over sports and rugged activities.

    Gone are the farming days; youngsters now do not have to struggle as hard and so are not hungry enough.

    "This is very natural because of success, improved living conditions, quality of housing estates, the number of places we are offering in our polys and ITEs -- people don't have to struggle as hard as they had to in the past," Mr Tharman said.

    Educationists see this as a worrying trend and that's why Mr Tharman says these intangibles have to be the focus in education.

    He cited the example of how when students were given the opportunity for overseas attachments and internships, Singapore students would tend to choose the "cushier" destinations like America and Australia instead of China and Vietnam, while foreign students in our system would grab that chance to go anywhere.

    "We must have people who are always adventurous, always hungry, willing to take on difficulty and turn it into advantage. Our youngsters must be willing to venture abroad, and when they graduate, work abroad for a while, get that experience and create opportunities of their own," said Mr Tharman.

    At the dialogue with residents, education-related questions dominated, including setting up more international schools overseas and discipline in schools.

    Mr Tharman also opened the first Emergency Preparedness Centre, where residents can learn what to do in an emergency.

    He said these exercises should be taken seriously as emergencies can happen anytime. - CNA
