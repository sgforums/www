    How's it going, y'all?

    Seems like this debate over Singlish will always be an issue. How about this as a suggestion? Personally, as a migrant out of Singapore, there is nothing wrong with Singlish. Someone did say that western countries have their own style of speaking English. Asian countries have their own way of speaking too. But don't forget that these new forms of English are mere spawns of the primary language like Cantonese, Hokkien and Khek were out of Mandarin. Therefore, what is the essence of Singlish? It's all fine and square that Singaporeans use it amongst themselves, but I think the problem arises when people think Singlish is the primary language, perfecting it in their everyday use but not proper English first.

    And I reckon there's more to this. Let's take Mandarin as an example. If you spoke to me in Hokkien and I spoke back to you in cantonese, we'd have a hard time. But if you wrote your message down and I did the same, we'd understand each other almost perfectly. Therefore, Singlish is merely a verbal spawn. And therein lies the rub (as said by a character in 'The Da Vinci Code' by Dan Brown), when we start to write how we think (and assuming we believed that Singlish is the primary language), then we literally corrupt proper English as we know it. So it becomes a problem when people start thinking and writing in Singlish thinking it's proper English.

    Personally, before learning Singlish, learn proper English first, verbally and in writing. Which one helps you to survive better in the world even if you do plan to stay in Singapore forever? Don't care what other countries do or think. If you justify your own actions by citing others, isn't that 'monkey see, monkey do'?

    Bring it on!!
