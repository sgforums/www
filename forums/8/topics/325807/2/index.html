    I think if you want an environment of less pressure, NTU would be better. Hear stories from some friends in NUS that in some courses, lecture halls would be so full that may have to even resort to sitting on the steps! You'll never find that in NTU.

    Also, exam timetables in NTU are generally better spread out than NUS. It's like lets say you take the same number of modules for a similar course in both universities, NTU's exams will tend to spread over 2-3 weeks (unless you screw up by repeating too many subjects) but in NUS, chances are it'll be done and dusted in 1 week. So more stress loh. There's a saying that goes, in NUS, people ask one another, you get A or B for your subjects. In NTU, it is you got "pao" (repeat) your subject anot?

    NUS have many big lecture halls and theatres and they always ensure that there is enough seats for everyone.

    Only exception is maybe for big class like economics which have about 800 students taking at semester and three lecture classes of size 330. Problem is students dont want to go for their own lecture groups which they bid for and they squeeze into the afternoon best time slot or whichever suits them.

    Exams timetable in NUS is more packed because NUS have many available rooms for exams. NUS can afford to squeeze all the exams for law, medicine, engineering, music, fass, science, computing, business into 2-3 weeks. Amazing right? Yeah NUS are profit-driven and probably they can save more by squeezing all the exams into 2-3 weeks.

     But yes, NUS is probably more stressful than NTU. But with the gpa system introduced in NTU now, probably the stress level is getting closer. Now both unis are easy to pass, not easy to score.
