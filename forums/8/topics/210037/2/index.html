    Firstly, what's the name of company?

    Quite a number of company function that way. You will probably need the photos then compiled into com cards before they start sending. But the trust is entirely on the company. And even if the com cards are sent to clients, you are not guarantee on the jobs and very likely, the agent would probably re-use his/her favourite batch of models, leaving little opportunity for the new comers (unless you are good).

    Ok la, to salvage the situation, I suggest that you keep whatever shots you have taken. I used to help out in my ex-company and our max shots (can't remember how many shots liao) cost only 329. Not too sure if you have been rip off in the cost, but still, keep that shots. And if you really want to work in this line, I suggest you do freelance unless you can get yourself to reputable companies. But then again, you will be competing with so many other models for jobs. Furthermore, your company will keep a portion of the commission - before giving you your comission.

    This 'rest' is up to the integrity of your agent because chances are, you will not be in the scene where the negotiation take place (unless he/she is very open about the deal and show you 'proof' about the deal). It is still very much the 'trust' with your agent/company and should they cut a whole chunk of commission for themselves - naturally you will get less. This is also where people can be a fucker because many things are confidential and not known to the model.

    For me, yunhaier see no need for modelling companies (although big clients usually find reputable modelling company, but this isn't always neccessary). If you can eliminate the middle man, in fact, you will be earning more.

    Widen contacts and establish network. And of course, learn more and not follow blindly at what people are saying. This industry quite complicated as well.
