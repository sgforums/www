    Medical corp is actually a very overlooked career field for people signing on as specialists most of the time. The officers who go in are usually doctors who start at the rank of captain after their Medical Officer Cadet Course. This means a CPT will earn up to a $1000 more than a similarly ranked CPT in normal vocations cos of the medical aspect. I think a LTC DR can touch up to $12,000 per month.

    Spec regulars will go through paramedic level 1, then level 2 and level 3 usually. I think you get $500 per month bonus if you attain level 3. Level 4 is for real good hardcore people which is done in Canada, in the Justice Institute of Columbia.

    I was the PA of the course commander who runs the Level 2 and 3 courses so I know all this crap... it's not a bad vocation to be. I believe when you get your level 3 you can convert to be ambulance crew...civil defense...and the certs are valid in all countries.
