  • Sandwichedclass

    I always endorsed the government's view and vision that Singaporeans need to constantly themselves to stay competitive in this global business environment.

    With globalization, we are not only competing against fellow Singaporeans but other foreigners as well.

     With that in mind, I have decided to enroll for a FICS course in SMU which is suitable for people in operations or control functions.

    It'll provide funding to private candidate who are Singaporeans or Singapore PR.

     Since I have been in a control functions role for 2 years, I thought such a course would help upgrade my current knowledge.

    In order to do the course, I need to forgo precious time with my 2 young kids for 8 Saturdays.

    But I think it might still be worthwhile, as I can emerge more knowledge and be able to compete against Foreign Talents employed in the financial industry.

     However, to my disappointment, I wasn't even accepted by SMU for the course and I didn't even manage to get a proper answer why they have rejected me.

     If someone like me, a Singaporean with right tertiary background and experience and keenness to upgrade is not given a chance to, I wonder who would SMU select for the course?

    If the whole environment does not support Singaporeans who wish to upgrade and make themselves competitive, it is no wonder Singapore always need foreign talents.
