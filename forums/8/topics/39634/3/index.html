    STILL IN SCHOOL
    Send us home too, say tertiary students
    By Lee Hui Chieh

    THOUSANDS of students from polytechnics, Institutes of Education (ITEs) and universities are upset at being excluded from a 10-day shut-down of schools to prevent the spread of Sars.

    Although the polytechnics said they did not notice any significant increase in absences yesterday, at least 7,600 signed an online petition started by poly students calling on the Education Ministry to close their institutions as well till April 6.

    Advertisement

    Many of them, like Nanyang Technological University undergraduate Kelvin Goh, 23, were unhappy over what they saw as a case of double standards.

    He said: 'Does it mean that our tertiary students have stronger immune systems? Tertiary institutions are also considered crowded places.'

    Explaining why the decision to close schools involved all levels up to the junior colleges, the Government has said that institutes of higher learning were exempted because they 'have a different system of operation from our national schools'.

    'Furthermore, the students in our post-secondary institutions are older and are better able to understand the situation and take the necessary precautions.'

    But many parents and students had problems accepting this reasoning.

    Said ITE student H.N. Lin, 20: 'It's a little strange because ITE students are also mostly around the same age as those in JCs. This is like saying we are virus-proof.'

    On top of that, many felt that tertiary students faced a higher risk of exposure and infection because there were more students in these institutions. There are also lectures, which are attended by between 300 and 500 people.

    The ITEs, polys and varsities have more than 100,000 students in all.

    Particularly worried were those from Nanyang Polytechnic and Bishan ITE, where nursing students are attached to hospitals including Tan Tock Seng and the Communicable Disease Centre, both of which treat Sars patients.

    A spokesman for the polytechnic said that all 150 students sent to TTSH and CDC for training since mid-February were told last week to stay at home for two weeks, and should not have returned to school for any classes.

    All other trainee nurses also had their attachments stopped yesterday. An ITE spokesman said 61 trainees at TTSH have been told to stay at home.
