    "High fees, more students, yet SIM stops refreshments. Why?"

    STOMPer Miss Anonymous says she wonders why the Singapore Management of Institutue (SIM) has stopped providing free refreshments even though its high fees and an increase in student enrolment suggest more income.

    She said SIM although SIM charges expensive school fees, they had always provided reasonably quality refreshments.

    According to her, the refreshments refer to food, such as tarts, bread, cakes that were previously offered by SIM, as well as tea and coffee during in between breaks offered to both students and lecturers of any lecture hall.

    She said, "It has been, for many years, this way, providing refreshments so that students need not have to rush to the canteen (which during lunch time, is fully packed) to get their food, for just 15mins."

    However, she said the school announced last month that the provision of refreshments would be stopped as "more students are coming to the institution and (the school) are unable to cope with the increase of students and providing them refreshments".

    The STOMPer said:

    "In this case, students assumed that more students means more cash inflows and meeting the increasing cash outflows, SIM should continue to provide refreshments.

    "The FAQ that SIM gave was found in the student portal, but kept emphasizing the alternatives. alternatives were "providing reasonably priced food at the pushcarts.

    "No adjustments were made and SIM management emphasized that it was provided free and they cannot cope.

    "More students, more fees, more income.

    "Students do not see the reason why SIM should stop providing refreshments.

    "However, they felt more that SIM wanted to use the money that they saved on, for a new campus."

    STOMP is contacting SIM for comment.
