    K, let me share what I learnt around.

    I used to think PDFs are great. It creates an aura of professionalism for your resume... and also to show you put in lotsa efforts into its preparation.

    However, feedback from some friends in HR shows otherwise. To them PDFs resumes give them lotsa inconveniences.

    U see... you are not the only one submitting your resume. They will have to assess each submission by making comments and evaluations. For the word document, they can insert their comments against each entry in the resume. However for PDF resumes, they will have to print it out first, then write their comments manually. The comments on the printed PDF resume will thus not be capture electronically and the HR has to open a file to store your resume, instead of storing electronically.

    So... dun always think of beauty... Practicality matters most. If you are so worried about the professional look.... then here's my advice.... send a PDF copy and a word copy.

    cheers
