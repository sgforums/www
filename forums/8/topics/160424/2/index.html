    I'm guessing that u are probably a gal.....well ur knee probably hurts due to these reasons:

    1. Pre-exercise warm-ups
    2. Type of shoes worn
    3. Place where u run

    1 - As most people said, without proper warm-up, u get these but it shldnt occur often. Also there are some minorities like someone I know who dun need much warm-up yet can be more active, agile & flexible than others who warm-up.
    Anyway, I doubt this is your case.

    2 - The type of shoes & paddings in it affects alot. FYI, not all shoes provided by the army is good. In fact if its the blue-yellow new balance shoes, u can discard that. It's the worst running shoes ever cos many complained its causes more ankle twists & strains than others despite its thick paddings.
    It is chosen because its one of the few cheapest lots available.
    For good running shoes, look for those that are labelled 'Running Shoes' or 'Cross-training' type.
    I forgot the details in looking out for the difference in these but I recall its gotta do with the shoe tip & the traction studs below.
    Cross-training shoes lasts longer & is the best if u wear your shoes for all purposes including for your runs.
    No matter what, padding is always impt & get extra if your shoes does not have ample paddings, esp at the heel area.

    3 - Place where u run also affects if the terrain is not the regular running tracks.
    Best would be under MRT tracks or running in the park.
    Of course get a partner to run with u if u are a gal.

    Last of all, if u feel any sore/pain after an exercise, massage the area & take note if it still hurts 2 days after that. Do not aggreviate it by exercising further if its a sports injury. Also consult a doctor if it gets severe.
    Hope this helps...^^
