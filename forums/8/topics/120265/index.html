    -------------------------------------------------
    March 2, 2005
    It's an emergency but settle bill first

    MY BROTHER-IN-LAW was visiting a relative at Mount Elizabeth Hospital on Friday evening when he had a stroke. He was rushed to the Accident and Emergency Department.

    However, as his medical records are with Tan Tock Seng Hospital, an ambulance was called to take him there instead.

    Here is the alarming part: We were told by the Mount Elizabeth staff to settle the payment for the ambulance before it would leave for Tan Tock Seng Hospital.

    At that worrying moment, we settled the bill as we were told.

    When we reached Tan Tock Seng Hospital, my brother-in-law was rushed to the Intensive Care Unit. The doctor told us that if he had arrived slightly later, his life would be in great danger.

    What puzzles me is why Mount Elizabeth Hospital could not send us the bill later. Isn't saving lives more important?

    When it is a matter of life and death, hospitals should be more compassionate.
