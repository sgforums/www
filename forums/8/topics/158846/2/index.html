    Web threads

    A weekly roundup of the most interesting Web discussions by Singaporeans.

    Rude callers

    AKAJUNE thinks it is about time Singapore has a campaign to promote phone etiquette.

    InnocentBlood recounts the years when she worked in a call centre.

    She had to deal with 'corporate customers - educated professionals - who somehow felt that it was totally OK to be rude over the phone since you can't see them face to face anyway'.

    Several agree with her observation that people 'tend to be pretty mean when they are hidden behind a phone'.

    ikileo suggests making small talk to defuse rudeness on the phone. She cannot understand why 'people can't just be nice and civil to each other'.

    forums.hardwarezone.com

    Defensive walking

    WHAT do you do to avoid being hassled by the armies of salesmen and survey takers along Orchard Road?

    Netizens suggest different approaches, including adopting various 'looks'. jbjjbjjbj, for instance, thinks it helps if you wear a sullen face, or an angry expression.

    He also recommends the NBA shield trick. 'Basically you see the direction from which they approach, ensure there's a buffer in between both of you, usually someone walking in the same direction as you.

    'Essentially you walk at the same pace as the buffer, so he would approach and stop the buffer first.'
