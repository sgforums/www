    Originally posted by striderz_o405:
    SAP schools... but why dont you want to put autonomous+independent schools instead?
    Because unlike other schools, SAP schools have the flexibility to allow pupils who do not meet the criteria of either A* in Chinese or Merit in Higher Chinese, to offer Higher Chinese. This is if they are assessed to have exceptional ability in Chinese and are able to do Higher Chinese without affecting their performance in other subjects.

    These schools were well-established Chinese-medium schools in the past. They offer the Special Course to the top 10% PSLE pupils. This is to enable the pupils to be effectively bilingual in both English and Chinese and also inculcate in them traditional values in a Chinese school environment.

    St Nicks has the distinction of being the only IJ among them.

    PSLE is perhaps what determines you next 6~8 years of education and to some extent your uni days and your future life.

    Get a good PSLE score and get into a good school which offers IP and you do not need to fret for O lvls. Last time there was O lvls for those who did not really score well for PSLE to have a 2nd chance. Now with IP the places in the top JCs have reduced by quite a lot.

    I am not going to pass judgement on IP cos I am one of the lucky ones that are in the first batch of IP students. PSLE is still important IMO. The hard work that you put in now will give you sweet rewards later. I personally did not regret studying for 3 months prior to my PSLE. Looking back, I am amazed at myself and still wonder if I can repeat the feat.

    Maybe the best I can say is that PSLE is not for anybody but yourself. It does not matter whether your secondary school is one of the elite few top ones or those that are just slightly below them. What matters is that at the end of the day, you can get a job that you like and lead a hopefully more comfortable life. A good PSLE grade will just give you that nice start you need to fulfill your dreams.

    It is a realistic world outside.
