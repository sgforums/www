Complaint on a bloody cab driver ...

  • meander
    28 Sep 04, 18:07
    It was a Sunday night and I shared a cab with a friend (still serving in the army then) from Jurong point. I was going back to NTU, and my friend Sungei Gedong camp. So naturally we told him NTU and after that SG camp.

    For those who don't know the place well, NTU is just a short distance from Jurong Point, cab fare is usually about $4. And NTU is just along the way towards Sungei Gedong camp, it's just a small detour. Also, to reach SG camp, it's just $6+ from Jurong point.

    BUT THE BUGGER HAD THE GALL TO EXIT VIA THE EXPRESSWAY 2 JUNCTIONS BEFORE NTU.

    So in the end he went to all the way inside Sungei Gedong camp where my friend dropped off, then made a big round back to NTU. Basically he more than doubled the fair.

    His flimsy excuse was that we should have told him clearly that SG camp was near . But my instructions were clear, NTU then SG camp. Even if he didn't know where SG camp was, he should still have gone on to NTU first?

    I told him that he took the wrong route and why should I pay for HIS mistake but he refused to back down and insisted on the meter fare of $16 (should have been around $7). After quarrelling for some time, I was prepared to pay the ridiculous full fare, then complain the following day to bust his a$$.

    Then the lao kan of a cab driver did something that REALLY pissed me off. He fiddled with the meter and *accidentally* pressed the "new fare" button, effectively erasing the previous fare, then told me he couldn't print the damn receipt. Yeah right what a damn coincidence . A taxi driver can't operate his own meter and screws up a simple thing like printing a receipt.

    His condescending attitude while haggling with me wasn't in the slightest bit apologetic. In fact he even told me I was wasting his time, time in which he could go pick up more passengers so i should just pay up quickly.

    I blew up and told him $8. Take it or leave it. If not you can drive me to the nearest NPP and we settle it there. The bugger accepted the $8 with a disgruntled face, and sped off only a moment after I unloaded my stuff (I had quite a bit of luggage in the back seat).

    Argh, never got to see his plate number because he sped off. Or else I'll make sure there's 1 less cab driver on the roads today.
