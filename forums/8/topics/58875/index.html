    Frustration: Her 'command and conquer' ways

    JOE got four miserable dollars a day allowance.
    No, Joe wasn't a kid in primary school. He was 27, a salaried man, and married. And the sum was what his wife of two years gave him for lunch every day after confiscating his monthly pay of $1,200. Breakfast and dinner were provided by her.

    To be fair, she also gave him three cigarettes a day. Not that she wanted to help him quit - she's a heavy smoker herself. She just felt any more would be a waste on him.

    And she didn't mind handing out some more of his own money to him so that he could buy petrol for his motorcycle, as long as he submitted a strict mileage and destination record every time he asked for it.

    Heaven forbid if he should claim more than necessary. He would have to give a blow-by-blow account of his whereabouts.

    Joe, now 28, is big-sized and looks like he can hold his own. After all, he rides a Harley and has multiple tattoos.

    He's just, well, terribly nice.

    Nice enough to end up marrying Rita even though his intention was to dump her.

    "I wanted to break up with her after being together for two years because I felt there was no future for us. She was really possessive and bossy. But then she tried to take her own life after I brought it up.

    "While she was lying in hospital, she asked me to marry her. How can you say no in this type of situation, right?"

    Er, right. So the couple got married in 1998, and moved into their flat. Somehow, Joe tolerated what he called his "command and conquer" wife and her nasty rules, even after she proved to be a nightmare.

    "We had a spare bedroom in our flat so I suggested that my mum could come and stay over whenever she wanted. Rita said she wanted her mum to have the room. Her mum lives one street away! But she just told me my mum was my problem and that was that."

    It wasn't always so bad, though. When the couple first met while working at the same cafe, Rita was an adventurous girl. Joe loved to ride his motorbike - he had a scrambler then - and Rita would ride pillion as he took dangerous jumps. The couple would also spend hours at coffee shops just chatting.

    But the witch in Rita started to emerge after some time. Her temper flared easily, and her possessive and controlling nature reared its ugly head. When Joe wanted out, Rita swallowed 35 paracetamol pills and landed herself in hospital.

    So Joe found himself with a wife that wouldn't allow him to indulge in his hobbies, biking and sailing. He wasn't allowed to meet his friends, and his pay went into a joint account that he couldn't access.

    "She said she wanted to keep my pay because I could not control my spending, but I don't waste money. We bought most of the furniture and appliances for the house with my savings!"

    Although Joe hinted at separation and divorce on several occasions but never demanded it, it was Rita who eventually wanted a divorce, much to his relief.

    She took everything that she had paid for, down to the chopsticks and bed sheets.

    He doesn't mind though. When she left, Joe took his bike for a long ride to celebrate.

    "That woman was a real b**** from hell, and I've learnt my lesson. Good luck to the next guy she hooks up with, man."

    Understand women? Don't even try

    THE harsh reality is this: Most men simply don't know what women want.

    And, really, who can blame us?

    Unlike men, whose desires can be categorised into beer, women and football, the female sex is one of incredible complexity.

    When I say complexity, I'm trying to be nice here. I actually mean to say contradiction, but I really don't want my wife to lock me out of the bedroom tonight.

    Seriously, though, just think about it.

    They want equality, yet they still want doors opened and chairs pulled for them.

    They demand that we do housework, since they also have careers these days, but when we clean, they tell us not to touch things around the house because we mess them up.

    They get insanely jealous if any other girls look at their partners, but they themselves crave attention from other men.

    They call us sexist when we admire a woman's well-proportioned chest, but it's okay if a bunch of girlfriends get together to check out a cute guy's cute butt.

    They can't believe we can spend two hours to play computer games, yet see nothing wrong in spending four hours choose a pair of shoes.

    You know, sometimes, I wonder if God created women just to have fun with us.

    I mean, how is it possible that a being created out of our rib is so completely confusing to us?

    This entire article might sound strange, especially coming from a married man.

    But, you see, I can say all these things with authority because my lack of understanding of femaledom didn't hamper the development of my relationship with my wife.

    When it comes to understanding women, there are really only two things you can do, but alcholism is generally frowned upon by society, not to mention that the hangover's a pain.

    So, really, there is only option two.

    Which is, don't even try.

    Just chalk women down as one of those great mysteries in life, like why no women care that Keanu Reeves is a bad actor.

    You see, I think we're just not meant to understand what's going on inside their heads, simply because it then makes the journey of discovery all that more engaging.

    I've known my wife for some 16 years now, and I'm still peeling the layers off her every day to understand her better.

    And not just her. Some of my best female friends, I'm also relishing knowing more about them as the years go by.

    The journey's hit some rocky bumps, for sure, but you can never say that it's been uninteresting.

    If you're Christian, then you will know that women are a gift to men, although He might have been sniggering as He pulled out our rib.

    I think that they are, indeed, for the world would be terrible without them. But to paraphrase a line from the movie Spider-Man (who also can't understand women, by the way) they are our gift, they are our curse.
