    This country...the country of my heart, the country that witnessed my upbringing, the birth of what I claim as among the most formidable of minds. It was within me, following upon the "straight and narrow path" of those walking the spiritual pursuit, and it was only possible because of the state of the country then. The necessary ingredients, together with what I call the "initial configuration" of my setting, within this place of organic culture, that I took root and progressed throughout my formative years to become enlightened. Enlightened...enlightened in the sense of the Buddha.

    Is it to be asked if this is possible? What exactly could I mean? Only but that, in the suggestions put forth by the Buddha in the claim of His own awakening, that there lies a process, biological, that when a person awakens into  "Enlightenment", will naturally find signs and presence of the "power" manifesting, within the human bodily vehicle. And so it was with me, and I will call it what it is: the "Kundalini awakening".

    In that state of grace, I became powerful beyond degree, and better, better than that power, was the gift of bliss. I had attained the fruit of the saints, the true goal of life, as so often lauded, so often sought, within me, in great and strong possession. This happiness is only so, because of a few things, notwithstanding the meaning of life itself. Not to procreate endlessly, as a means of continuing one's existence through the generations of offspring...for a life like that has ultimately no meaning. But then, to attain the goal of life, as so often spoke of...what that entails is this, that the potential within, the height and limits of my "powers", or to be better expressed, whatever latent abilities already within, for my case being my command of the English language, flower with full and deep form. And the satisfaction, from a point after being that the goal beyond "the goal", where we decide once and for all, in finding our true selves which is the expressed meaning of "Enlightenment", we now write the rules to live by, the goals and meaning made by us, in the holding of ultimate responsiblity.

    That satisfaction, for me, a man first among equals, it has always been for the higher matters, the greater pursuits, of which I put foremost my ability to achieve, to explore and fulfill to the extents of my potential, for I gain a pleasure, in my many numerous gifts and abilities, for which I spent the most part of my formative years in grooming, in learning, in acquiring, there is no greater pleasure then, for me...and that I am to be recognised in worth for my very substance...truly, that is how a philosopher will define "Man".

    Now though, man no longer has that meaning, for he is led by lesser desires, seduced by the physical and the superficial. I can only agree on one thing, that Singapore, with a lack of a real culture, in the manner of those in other developed countries, allows such an environment, where the very best of humanity can be distilled. That at least, is my belief. But it seems the Singapore I knew, in my past, ten years ago, has significantly changed. They have become conquered by evils, of greed, of lust, of ignorance and the great stupidity that comes with selfishness. Will there not be one to equal the capability of one now resting and decorated as our "founding father"? It is only a pity that he was never to witness, at least alive, of the rising of one of the most formidable of minds in this era, coming right from his country, one who conquered his own Enlightenment, and reached a stage where most can barely reach. It was not easy, and ultimately, I have to reveal that I failed in reaching the full and complete height of my Enlightenment, and the awakening has been sealed to some degree...but still, I can only say it is about a question of "time", that if I had that...in due course, something to be greatly admired, can be conceived, within the bodywork of one who pushes forth the culmination of Humanity's progress, into its finest. For any who dares calls himself a man, that very well should be your judging criteria, that let not one so fallen and yet still so eloquent (me), far surpass you. I rest my case on what it means to be happy, as being the pursuit, and the experience from that, of Spiritual Enlightenment. I can only pray that my words are welcomed on listening ears, abiding in an understanding that might be elusive.
