    Freshmen wear convocation gowns at NTU welcoming ceremony

    Some may think wearing a convocation gown before an actual graduation is inappropriate, but Nanyang Technological University on Monday gave its 4,400 new students that experience at a welcoming ceremony to mark the start of the new academic year.

    This is the first time that NTU has welcomed its first-year students with gifts like ties and scarfs.

    They were also the first to don convocation gowns at a freshman welcoming ceremony, although their own graduation is three to four years away.

    Chiew Jingyi, an engineering student, was among the thousands who were surprised by this year's ceremony.

    "I thought it's just some talks and speeches. But the light-up was something refreshing after having been in army for two years. I feel like we're being accepted into the university family," he said.

    But there were mixed feelings about wearing the graduation gown now.

    Andrial Evan Savero, an engineering student, said: "It feels great to put on the gown."

    Vivien Choo, an engineering student, said: "Now it's like no feeling. We haven't graduated. We haven't gone through the whole process. When we graduate, we will have more satisfaction."

    The freshmen seem eager to start their new journey into university education.

    But they also ponder their future as more graduates are facing difficulty in securing a job in this present economic situation.

    And so putting on that graduation gown is seen as one way to encourage the undergraduates in their studies.

    The tough job market could also be one reason why relatively new courses in Life Sciences, a growing sector in Singapore, saw a 72 percent jump in intake this year.

    Professor Su Guaning, President of NTU, said: "The spirit should be pervasive, in a sense that you should look for your own opportunity when adversity comes."
