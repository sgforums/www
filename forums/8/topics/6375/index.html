    split the 12 coins into groups of 4. Weight Grp1 against Grp2.
    Grp1 balances Grp2 ===> odd coin is in Grp3.
    1) Weigh 3 coins from Grp3 against any 3 coins from Grp1/2.
    If they balance ===> last coin in Grp3 is odd coin, use 3rd weighing to determine if it is heavier or lighter.
    If they do not balance ===> U know if the odd coin is heavier/lighter if the 3 coins from Grp3 go down/up. To find the odd coin, weigh any 2 coins from the 3 Grp3 coins.

    Grp1 does not balance Grp2===> odd coin is amongst the 8 coins.
    1) Take 3 coins out of Grp1 and place aside
    2) Transfer 3 coins from Grp2 to the pan with Grp1 coin.
    3) Replace the 3 transferred Grp2 coins with 3 coins from Grp3.
    Balance is tilted the other way ===> Odd coin is one of the 3 transferred Grp 2 coins and it is lighter/heavier depending on whether the pan is up/down.
    Balance remains tilted in same fashion===> Odd coin is either the Grp1 coin left in the original pan or the Grp2 coin left in the original pan. Use last weighing to see which one it is.
    Balance is level===>Odd coin is amongst the 3 Grp1 coins taken out. Depending on previous position of pans, you know if it is heavier or lighter. Weigh any 2 out of these 3 coins to determine which is the odd one out.
