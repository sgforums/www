    mane trouble
    Long hair days are here again.

    If only Lucy Liu had not swung those locks
    By TEO PAULIN

    One day more than two years ago, my hairdresser dabbed on some gel, brushed clean my shoulders, whisked out a hand mirror, and showed me the new me.

    My hair, cut short for the very first time in my life, was sheared to a sharp, funky Gwyneth Paltrow-in-Sliding-Doors do. I was transformed.

    Freed from the weight of more than 20 years' worth of nondescript, long-haired frumpiness, my posture straightened. I was lighter on my feet. I walked out the hair salon a new woman.

    When I crossed the street, I got wolf-whistles of greater variety than just those sucking sounds that came from the back of army trucks.

    Blithely, I used less shampoo. I had no use for the comb. I concluded, with a sniff and a snort, that long hair is for insecure women who are still hanging on to their locks like childhood blankets. Wimps.

    Short hair, I pronounced, showed that a woman is confident of who she really is. She didn't need no curtain to hide no multitude of hang-ups, from zits on the forehead to Mickey-Mouse ears to double chins.

    But suddenly, all this puffed-up bravado ground to a halt last week when I saw Charlie's Angels.

    That wretched Lucy Liu - I swear from now on I will slime her freckles at every journalistic opportunity - proved that long hair has one very good use.

    According to her, you swing it loose a few times in slow-mo, and it gets you your man. Whether it's swinging it after taking off a helmet, swinging it after removing your sun-glasses, or simply swinging it for the heck of it, any man will be hooked.

    I tried swinging my Gwyneth do, with only my floppy fringe at swayable length, only to look like someone on the onset of an epileptic fit. Short, it seems, is no longer hot.

    No wonder my ex-boyfriend found a new love in that ditz with the Jennifer-Aniston layered mane (Gwyneth, you are my soul sister!).
    No wonder when I bump into any young, strapping NS man these days, the only response I get is "Sorry, auntie.''

    Now, I have to wait another three years before I can get back to my former Farrah Fawcett glory days. All thanks to the lousy Liu theory.

    Well done, Angels.
