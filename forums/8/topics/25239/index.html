    it depends on wat kind of sales are talking about.. i'm more involved in b to b sales (business to business) rather than b to c (consumer) so i'll comment on wat i know better... b2b

    most of the time the person u are talking to doesn't have the final say in buying ur product or service. in other words they don't sign the damn cheque. a good sales person would do well having the mindset that his explanations and sales pitch would in turn help the person listening to convince his boss.

    another thing to remember is that the person that you are selling to is buying something from u NOT from his own money. if he likes you, he will try to convince his boss to buy from you, it's the boss that will rather listen to his trusted employee than to u. the best is of course u can convince the boss as well as his staff.

    also never take a rejection personally. it's all business.. but do well to maintain a relationship. the employee may move companies and remember u not becos of ur products but becos of ur service, and u too may move on to another company selling different products.

    always remember to create an impression on the person u are pitching to, becos if they don't buy anything now, they will remember u and not the rest of the world who's pitched to them as well. and when they do, it's becos they need to buy something. and u'll get ur sale!
