    A weekly roundup of the most interesting Web discussions by Singaporeans.

    Road to maturity

    stardusz thinks living on one's own and away from one's parents will make a person more mature and responsible.

    Such a person, she says, will learn to manage his money better and become more resourceful.

    eviltrap agrees, saying that before she moved out, she depended on her parents for everything. Being on her own has taught her that money is not easy to come by.

    alvinaloy, however, writes: 'Living on your own is just one of the ways to maturity.' He feels that if a person is not mature, living on his own may not necessarily make him so.

    forums.hardwarezone.com

    The age of civility

    alfseeto says that, contrary to popular belief, young Singaporeans are not rude.

    While eating at a Tiong Bahru hawker centre, he observed that many young Singaporeans acknowledged an old tissue seller politely or bought packets from him. He said older Singaporeans at the hawker centre, however, were very cold towards the tissue seller.

    chryzh35, however, writes: 'I don't think age is a factor in a person's attitude towards fellow beings. Our society in general is rude and selfish, always chasing the dollar and most times at all costs.'

    He adds: 'This competitiveness has got to do with the fact that we are a young and small nation and we need to be aggressive in all areas of economics.'
