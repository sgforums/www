    I was talking with a friend about this the other day, and read an article on the corpun website.  It seems that the Singapore government likes to cane people, by bodybuilders trained in martial arts, leaving scars for life, and excruciating pain.  Now, you might argue that these people deserve it because they commit horrible crimes like rape, but I have a lot of questions:

    (1) First, is it true that you can not only get the cane for serious offences, but also relatively minor offences such as graffiti, shoplifting, and overstaying your visa?  I read that there are over 3000 people caned for overstaying every year, which would add up to about half of the entire caned population.

    (2) While several blue-collar offences such as drug dealing and minor crimes like graffiti are targeted by caning, why is it that Singapore doesn't cane offenders such as spammers?  Or white collar fraudsters that rip off the poor?  It seems the caning unfairly discriminates against the underclasses.

    (3) With regard to the caners themselves, what is their typical age and background?  How do they get selected?  How young (or old) do they have to be?  I heard that persons as young as 16 can be caned, is it true that persons this young can become caners also?

    (4) Singapore prisons are pretty horrible.  So for example, if someone is caught dealing drugs, wouldn't 10 years in a Singapore prison be enough punishment, rather than making them undergo this additional agony?


    Just curious and asking some questions, I hope you can respect them.

    Cheers.
