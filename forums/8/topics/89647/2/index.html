    NO!!! I dont think this is the correct way. Separate the mother, then where will the milk come from???!!!

    as Bear suggest, try not to look at the hamsters too much to avoid stressing the mother out. Mother hamster will eat their young when threatened. Also, never touch the babies! The mother can smell the difference in scent.

    Drape a cloth over the cage to minimise disturbance and drafts but don't cover it entirely (so they dun suffocate)

    Only open the cage when you are replenishing the food and water. Try to do that quietly though.

    Only when the babies have grown fur then it'll be safe to touch them.

    I'm speaking from experience but you may wan to search internet websites for more guidance.
