    Hmm.

    Well,

    Im a student currently studying in Sydney and half my family lives in melb, so i travel to and fro quite frequently, and i know both the victorian and NSW education schools and uni systems quite well.

    Ive also gone through this whole tedious process of hunting for a good school. So, let me share some stuff...

    alright.. Trinity college is good,not too bad, its a foundation course which does NOT in anyway give you confirmation into your desired uni course. It only allows you a lower ENTER/ATAR/UAI score into that course. And do note: upon completing your studies at Trinity College, you can ONLY apply for Melb uni. Nothing else, as the qualifications are only recognised by Melb Uni.

    Yes 1 more thing. You are actually eligible to take up year 12 ( which is JC2 equivilant) upon completing the O levels, if you have (depending on campus) 6 distinctions , and sometimes, you'd be required to sit for an entry test.

    Well. 1 more. impt. You MUST be an international student to study at Trinity College. If you're a PR= they dont accept. 101%. If you halfway get PR, your whole study is also gone.

     

    Well, saying that, you could also consider other foundation colleges like the one from UNSW(but thats in Sydney), or Taylors College( which is a foundation for Monash Uni).

    But--- i wouldnt really advise on Taylors college. Its either Trinity or UNSW foundation.

    The only con abt UNSW's foundation is that you cant enter medicine course in Uni if you take up their foundation studies. All other courses are fine and you will be eligible for them.

    Yes, also to answer your question.

    The top Australian Unis , in terms of ranking , is not better than those top unis in the US and UK. (this is only if you're comparing top universities).

    also, it depends in the course which you wish to pursue.

    eg. Harvard is well know for its research in medicine, whereas Washington Uni is well know for clinical medicine.

    Yep. All the best!
