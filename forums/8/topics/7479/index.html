    First we name the doors as "left" and "right".
    The question should be:"Does the left door lead to Heaven according to the other guard?" If he says yes, you should go to the right door, if no, you should go to the left.

    Why?
    Case 1:
    You pose the question to the truth-telling guard, and the left door leads to Heaven.
    The truth-telling guard knows that the other guard(the lying one) would say that the left door does not lead to Heaven, and so he answers "No".

    Case 2:
    You pose the question to the truth-telling guard, and the right door leads to heaven.
    The truth-telling guard knows that the other guard(the lying one) would say that the left door leads to heaven, and so he answers "Yes".

    Case 3:
    You pose the question to the lying guard, and the left door leads to heaven.
    The lying guard knows that his truth-telling counterpart would say that the left door leads to heaven, and so he lies "No".

    Case 4:
    You pose the question to the lying guard, and the right door leads to heaven.
    The lying guard knows that the other truth-telling guard would say that the left door does not lead to heaven, and so he lies "Yes".

    So whoever you ask the question to, you will still know which door leads to heaven.
