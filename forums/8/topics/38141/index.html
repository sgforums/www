    I am an avid hamster lover. I do own hamsters at home. However, I noticed something which pet shops in Singapore are doing and would like to bring to everyoneÂ’s notice, especially animal lovers.

    Recently, I have been to Pets Station Holdings, Jurong Point branch. They sell dwarf pudding mandarins hamsters at $38 each. Pudding mandarins are hybrids and therefore are not considered as pure breeds. I noticed something about their pudding mandarins. Most of them are in bad condition. Parts of some of the hamsters are bitten and injured. There were also traces of injury on their fur. Should injured hamsters be sold at a whopping price of $38?

    Also, I have sold Winter White Sapphires at $1 each to them before. I checked with them a few days later about how much they sold my hamsters for. They quoted a price of $20 each. I was very disappointed at the price they quoted. Why? The hamsters they sold are not sterilized, but they sold it at a sterilized price. For a $20 price tag, I believe others can get better quality of hamsters and sterilized ones. However, buyers do not know this, because buyers often buy from more popular places such as Pets Stations. Places like Bugis Village sell better quality hamsters, but not many know. The implication of buying injured and un-sterilized hamsters is that, they are more prone sickness, and die easily. When hamsters are sick, the hamsters end up being discarded. IsnÂ’t this cruelty to animals too? Hamsters are smaller in size, thus are much easier to discard, unlike dogs or cats.

    I have questions for the AVA. Do they have any authority on controlling prices and quality of hamsters being sold in licensed pet shops? If the authorities are not going to implement any actions, I believe pet shops will be quoting higher prices to make a quick buck out of animals. This only results in discarded animals. Also, should all animals including small animals be sterilized in Singapore? Would diseases be spread easily if they arenÂ’t sterilized? I suggest the authorities do something about this.
