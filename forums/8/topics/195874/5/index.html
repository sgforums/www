    Originally posted by MrFugue:
    Rolex itself is for the mid-end range of luxury watches. Rolex Cellini's range has Rolex President itself.
    Nope. Uh uh. No no no no no.

    First, there is no watch called the Rolex President. There is a bracelet style called President, which was named such because it was fitted on a Day-Date that was presented to Dwight Eisenhower to celebrate his reelection. The Day-Date is part of the Oyster Perpetual line, being set in an Oyster case with a self-winding movement.

    The Cellini line is entirely different - the cases are flatter, not hermetically sealed like an Oyster, and contain hand-wound movements.
