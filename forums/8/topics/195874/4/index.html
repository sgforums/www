    POOI! Slap that liar in the face!

    Swatch owns Omega, and Omega and TAG tend to use Eterna ebauches. An ebauch is a generic movement (or mechanism) that is bought by another manufacturer and customised.

    Rolex, on the other hand, make all of their own movements. The last non-Rolex movement in a Rolex was the Zenith El Primero in the Daytona, before Rolex finally introduced their in-house 4130 movement in 2000. The 3130/3135/3185/3186 movements that power the rest of their Oyster line are designed and built by Rolex, and the 31XX line can trace its heritage back to the cal. A2XX movements, also designed and produced by Rolex and installed in the Explorer, Submariner and Turn-O-Graph models of 1953.
