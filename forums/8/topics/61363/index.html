    GEAR up for a clash of the funnies, as a host of foreign comedy acts returns to tickle your sense of humour.

    The laughfest kicks off tonight at Chijmes Hall with American-Filipino stand-up comedian Rex Navarrete, who last performed here in March.

    Comedy club 1 NiteStand, which operates out of Clarke Quay, has just completed its October shows here and will be bringing in six more stand-up comedians from Britain, Canada and Australia this month and next.

    And to top it all off, The Punchline Comedy Club's Whose Line Is It Anyway? - an improvisational comedy show which encourages audience participation - makes its long-awaited return at the end of this month, after its sell-out performance here last year.

    Show organisers say they brought the acts back because of previous overwhelming response.

    Says Mr John Moorhead, organiser of Whose Line Is It Anyway?: 'The show sold out so fast last time that many missed out. Also, because the response was excellent, the cast wanted to come back too.'

    Tickets for the show, which ran for four nights at Bar None, sold out a week before it opened.

    Similarly 1 NiteStand, which has been showcasing three foreign comedy acts at the end of every month, sells out regularly, while Navarrete's previous show was 90 per cent sold out, say its event producers and promoters, X-terra.

    Evidently, despite hard times, mirth-seekers are more than willing to fork out good money - prices range between $50 and $66 - for a good chuckle.
