    The phrase "I hereby confer your degrees with all the appropriate honors, rights, privileges, and responsibilities pertaining thereto.", in some way, shape or form is used whenever degrees are conferred.

    The honors, rights and privileges that come with the degree simply pertain to acknowledgement of the holder's skills and knowledge, and the holder has a responsibility to employ those skills and that knowledge in an appropriate manner.

    Beyond the field of the subject matter relevant to the degree, none of these honors, rights, privileges, or responsibilities apply. To suggest that a degree holder needs to be more humble or tolerant is a sentiment that does not apply, well-meaning though it may be. By equal measure, beyond the field of the degree, there is absolutely no obligation to treat degree holders any differently from anyone else.

    If a degree holder acts like an arrogant twat, there is no reflection upon the degree itself. The only conclusion that can be reached is that this individual is an arrogant twat.
