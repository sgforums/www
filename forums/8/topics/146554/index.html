    1) Did i say i say we believe in it intellectually and thats all?

    Response:

    You didn't say this but you yet said that we (unlike you) didn't believe that faith in Christ was sufficient to save our souls; that we believed that there were other requirements which we needed to be meeting.

    Since we believe that faith in Christ by itself does not save (in the sense of intellectually accepting propositions and as evidenced by the testimony of the Book of James to this effect) and since you were contrasting what you took to be true Christianity with our position on this score, I naturally assumed that it was your position that a person only needed to have faith in Christ in order for him to be able to be saved (and that this had meant his believing a certain set of truths about Christ).

    2)Did i mention we can just believe and sin all we want?

    Response:

    Luther appeared to believe in this; at any rate, he once said 'sin boldly'.

    In any case, if you don't believe that we 'can just believe and sin all we want', surely it would have to be your position that being saved (or being made regenerate or whatever else you may wish to call it) must be more than just a matter of faith in the sense of mere belief, and that it would have to include NOT 'sinning all you want'.

    This being the case, why did you have the audacity to contrast our position with yours, for the purpose of suggesting that our position wasn't Christian?

    3) 'please do not make this kind of assumtions(spell). and that whats it with this need to saint someone? we all know we just have our Do our duty to God. not really need to matters on what respect or whatsoever you gain on earth. '

    Response:

    Jesus Himself said to 'let your light shine before men, that they may see your good deeds and praise your Father in heaven.'

    I fully agree that what makes a person a saint isn't the favour that he has found with men; but I also deny that if a person is a saint then the good deeds which he does could fail to be made manifest to those who are in a position to observe them.

    If we really did our duty to God, then it will not matter to us if we gained the respect of our fellowmen; but more often than not doing our duty to God entails helping the least of Christ's brethren in a world where there are so many opportunities to help those who are less fortunate than ourselves on so many levels; in a world like ours, our good deeds in this regard will generally therefore not fail to go unnoticed by most people.

    That was my point - stop distorting it.

    Finally, ask any informed Catholic whether he will not be offended by your statement that he is not a Christian and you will then be told by him how much he thinks of your lying claim that you respect Catholics.

    You are deceived.
