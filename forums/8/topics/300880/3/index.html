    Hey RETARDED_MORON( I feel weird typing that),

    I went for a medicine interview at Monash university, melbourne last July. (Applicants for Australian medicine entry will have to take an aptitude test first, something like an IQ test, the top cohort will be selected for an interview). Just a little background about myself, I went to Australia for tertiary studies after my 2 years stint in NS last year. I was fortunate to pass it but the offer given to me was conditional depending on my grades( which I studied in Australia)

    Just a few tips for the interview:

    1. Its very important to be yourself. Do not try to put on a different personality just to impress the judges. They can see through you.

    2. Be consistent with your answers. You should not add in experiences or stories which are untrue, because all the questions posed might indirectly lead back to the very first question. Hence, if your answers seems inconsistent with what what you said earlier, then you are in hot soup.

    3. Always put on a simile( this I'm not sure with NUS, but it worked for me) throughout the interview. It makes you look more friendly and warm. Fortunately for me, my interviewers were very nice people.

    4. Since at least 1 of the interviewers is a medical doctor, it always helps to read up on medical news. Remember, what they are looking for in a potential doctor is to be able to keep updated on new developments in the medical field.

    5. Lastly, motivation, leadership, maturity and flexibility are characteristics of a doctor they are looking for. You should try to emphasize these points and demostrate that you possess such qualities in your secondary, JC or high school.

    Hope this helps with your interview. My aim of writing this is to allow you to understand what the interviewers are looking out for. I hope that this knowledge would calm you down a little prior to your interview and I wish in all the best in your interview.
