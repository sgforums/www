    Hi TS. I suppose you're around 18/19 of age and have just completed your JC life? I'm not from NUS med, but I was from the trip-science fac of a certain JC that recently moved to Bishan, so I have lots of friends in med. from what I hear:

    1) So the rumour goes. They split the candidates into 4 levels according to grades, or whatever other indicators. The top-tier candidates are accepted; the second tier ones are NOT accepted so that other industries and the scholarship boards have some pple to choose from; the third tier candidates are accepted; and the bottom they keep for appeal or reject. This is why we had lots of rather good students who failed to get into NUS med - they were the second tier ones. And mind you, we're easily talking about 4As for the 3rd tier candidates even, so there're actually quite a few things out of your control here.

    2) Since you're a foreign student, you will not need to do NS. Write to all the hospitals in Singapore and intern there. Get a good recommendation letter etc etc. This few months will give u the edge over the other candidates.

    3) For essays, interviews, etc. Just do your best. I've got quite a few friends who don't seem to be that great at interviews either but they still got through.

    4) Most important point. At age 18 or 19, you do not have any idea of what you want to do in the future. Believe me. None of us believed other pple when they told us that when when we were in your shoes. Not getting into Med does not mean the end of everything. I've seen lots of cases like this. Pple move on and end up in meaningful courses and careers even when they didn't get their 1st choice.

    Yup all the best!
