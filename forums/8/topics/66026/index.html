    Experimentation on animals is termed "vivisection".

    I've done a research on this before... and some of the reasons behind banning, other than ethical reasons, include things like cheaper alternatives and the reliability of such experiments.

    Cheaper alternatives would include computer simulation and tests on human cells and tissue cultures. And also with the discovery of the human DNA, this knowledge enables scientists to conduct research and test what effects the researched medicine has on the human body with higher accuracy.

    Humans and animals have bodies that work similarly but only on a superficial level. The cellular make-up of human beings and non-human beings are, after all, different. We are also different physiologically, metabolically, anatomically, genetically and psychologically. Even the blood circulation of human beings are different from four-legged animals, simply because we walk on two legs while they walk on four. Although sometimes animals do react to some drugs in a way similar to how humans response, it is hard to tell which are the reliable responses.

    Also, because of the unreliability of experimental results, animal research actually hinders medical progress rather than contribute to it. They have led to delays in discoveries of significant medical treatments. For example, back in 1928 when Alexander Fleming discovered penicillin, it did not act on rabbits when they were being tested on. Hence, Fleming was misled about the effectiveness of penicillin until a decade later.

    Whereas some drugs do not have an effect on animals when it is the opposite for humans, several drugs have actually been taken off shelves after users reported serious side effects, even leading to death.
