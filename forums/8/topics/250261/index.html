    shatec is more recognised and established here and overseas than sunrice definitely. classified has ads specifying diploma from shatec, but never sunrice.
    shatec has a dip in culinary skills course which trains you to be chefs not cooks, and theres is also a dip in pastry and baking course. imo culinary skills is better, you learn briefly to cook a big variety of cuisines and also pastry classes. course fees is ~$6k/year, course is about 2 years, including 6 months UNPAID attachment with shatec restaurants, then 1 year paid attachments of you finding your own hotels.

    after you completed your course and attachments you can find your own jobs with any hotel or restaurant, likely becoming chefs within a year. you will know basically how to own your own place too.

    imo it is not good to work full time in the food industry, you have to work split shifts, go in the morning to prepare ingredients for lunch, then cook lunch, after lunch you clean up kitchen then waste hours before having to come back to prepare ingredients for dinner, then cook dinner, then wash up entire kitchen then go home late. the mediocre pay is not worth it. unless you are confident of becoming a top chef or own your own cafe (similar aim of 10000s not to mention there are 1000 too many restaurants already and shop space rent is up to $40000 a month)

    hopefully you are talented and creative in cooking.

    fun? depends what fun is to you. the pace in the kitchen is really fast. you can't really afford a screw up. if you love shouting, swearing, drinking, sweating, having the most stressful 4 hours during dinner service then yes, the kitchen is the best job you can find out there.

    people often say chefs are like rockers.. it's true ! we sing, we drink, we swear, we create, girls (here in canada at least) dig us, ppl thank us (in canada again), we eat for free and by the end of the night, we come out all sweaty and dirty

    if you can accept that then yeah, it's fun! life as a pastry chef might be different in some places. you start your shift way before anyone else does like 7 or 8 in the morning. if you work in a hotel, you bake the breads, cakes, pies & desserts for every kitchen / restaurant in your establishment.

    in a restaurant, same thing goes but most of the time u r the only pastry chef working there. so yeah, u got shit load of baking to do in a short time span before lunch service starts.

    you said you have a kid which needs your attention now? you might want to consider coming into this line then. you have no time, no life and not much of a pay when you first start off.. starting pay sheme in most hotels in sg will be $985/mth and restaurants start at $1150/mth. think about it because it might not suit u now...
