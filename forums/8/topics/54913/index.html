    Take for example- Golden Shoe Hawker Centre, you see people
    placing tissue paper and walk away.

    Okay, the law says that you can sit anywhere and hawkers cannot
    reserve the seats for their stall's patronage customers.
    How about customers?

    Once I was looking for a seat, I had a fish soup and somehow,
    was in a foul mood beco'z the soup scaled me a little due to my
    clumsiness. So I just push away a tissue paper and took the seat.

    At about 5 mins later, unfortunately a beautiful lady tap my
    shoulder looking frustrated told me that she had reserved the seat
    I look at her blur and said " Arh " she said she placed her tissue
    paper there.
    So I bitched with her and said, " What is your name? " as far as I know, there is no name written on your seat and if you have reserved,
    I have to question the building management why they did not block
    up this area or have a reserve tag like restaurants do have...

    She raised her voice saying " I placed the tissue paper there!!"
    So I responded even louder, " So the tissue paper represent you,
    so CHEAP har...
    here is 50 cents go get yourself another tissue paper and call
    the police, tell them I violated your property. "

    The guy around me actually burst into laughter and she was in tears,
    quickly hurried away.

    This is the ugly Singaporean side, simply no class!
    I have worked in US, UK and Australia and even in Indonesia,
    never encounter this tissue paper effect before.
