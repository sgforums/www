Driver tampered with fuel gauge prior going to JB

  • Silenthunter
    12 Dec 02, 16:22
    12Dec Straits Times:

    A 47-YEAR-OLD motorist was arrested at the Woodlands Checkpoint on Tuesday night for tampering with the fuel gauge in his car.

    He had fitted his Honda Accord LX with a switch so it would look as if it held more petrol than it actually did, so that he could evade the three-quarter-tank requirement.

    This rule requires any driver of a Singapore-registered car to have his fuel tank at least three-quarters full before it enters Malaysia.

    Customs officers usually just check the fuel gauge, and may use a dipstick to check the petrol level if they get suspicious.

    In Tuesday's case, a tip-off led to the customs officer finding the switch hidden under the dashboard. The driver admitted rigging the fuel gauge.

    For breaking the three-quarter-tank rule, the penalty is a fine of up to $500.
