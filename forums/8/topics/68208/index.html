    There is a great misconception that tailored pants are expensive. In fact, they can even be cheaper than off the rack!

    There are many, many tailors in Singapore who make three pairs for $100. You should be able to find these chaps renting half a store (or less!) in estates like Toa Payoh and AMK. Some of these guys cut the material and make it themselves.

    Unless you enjoy the feel of 100% polyester, you would do well to upgrade to those that are at least $40 a pair! You will come to realise that you get what you pay for, and my suggestion is you should always avoid high polyester content.

    I've made several pairs at Benson Fashion Trading.
    The main branch is at 631 Bedok Reservoir Road #01-952 and they have outlets at Jurong East St 13, Parkway Parade, Hougang St 21, Upper Cross St and Toa Payoh Centre.
    These outlets have a guy who will just measure you and show you fabrics. They make the goods at some other location.

    They do a good job and gave me a large discount when I was interested in the "$200 for three pairs" material. Some of these are used for jackets/suits too - much better stuff than the cheapest types!

    If you are afraid of hole-in-the-wall tailors with just one table, you can try Dickson Menswear at Suntec City. Tel 6836-9078.
    He's at the basement of Tower 4, just next to the escalator down from Carrefour. He also sends the stuff to a workshop elsewhere to be made.
    I've made several pairs from here too - not bad, although in my opinion I got a better deal from Benson.

    You can choose whether you want pleats or cuffs, how high you wear your pants, how long you want them to be, what kind of pockets and much more. Talk to the guy taking your measurements and get his opinion too!

    Can anyone recommend a tailor for shirts? I've not done it yet in Singapore and would like opinions on good shirt tailors.

    It is hard to get good recommendations for tailored shirts in Singapore because most men don't tailor their shirts.
    Those who do usually fall into two categories:
    1) "cheap = good" types who don't care much about shirting material and settle for the very low end fabrics
    OR
    2) the oned who use top-of-the-range types like CYC, Oxford, Justmen, Fashion Tailors Menswear.

    As with pants, you can obviously buy shirts off the rack for $30-40 BUT that's if you like high polyester content or low threadcount cotton, button-down collars, few standard colours and barrel cuffs. To those who like these, good for you.

    For those looking for spread collars, pure cotton yet wrinkle resistant, convertible cuffs or French cuffs and other features, you'll not often find these at the $30 range at the stores.

    There are other off-the-rack shirts that cost $45 upwards. These are the 100% cotton, 80-threadcount, 2-ply types and in a different league. e.g. the Robinsons house brand shirts, Hardy Amies.

    My opinion is to go to a tailor, make one shirt, wear it for a few months and see how you like it, then go back for more if you do.
