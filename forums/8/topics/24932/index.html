    I don think it's about the money only lor. Give them $200 also not enough. Seriously, the pilots need to get proper rest. Even though it's not like they have a whole lot to do when they are flying long-distance; the auto-pilot does _most_ of the flying for them, but you still need rest! SIA shouldn't go around thinking that they can boss people around just because they are the employers and go changing contracts as and when they see fit. If a pilot breaks his contract, they take action against him. So now SIA is the one in breach of the contract, so I say industrial action is only fair.

    And I fully support the two pilots that got sacked! Yes, I'm not saying that they were not at fault at all. But conditions then were so atrocious that it was a total setup. Airport lighting conditions were dogsh**. Weather was dogsh**. Granted, they should have double checked and triple-checked, but which pilot would have imagined themselves to be in a situation where they lined up on the WRONG BLOODY RUNWAY?? And then there is the concept of Open Reporting in the aviation business. Basically, if you f*** it up, 'fess up, share your experience, and don't f*** it up again. You DO NOT get crucified for 'fessing up. If you do, people will be unwilling to admit their mistakes, and unsafe practices or incidents will go unnoticed. Pilots will want to "get away" with whatever mistakes they make, and this will degrade aviation safety. Caveat: If the f*** up is due to outright negligence/laziness/incompetence, then yes, the individual should be punished. But in this case, I really think that the two pilots are not so.
