    Aug 26, 2007

    CNET Asia
    Sober IT truths from the island-state
    by Nicholas Aaron Khoo, Singapore

    In the latest cyclone of anti-piracy nicknamed 'ODEXed' to hit Singapore on anime download which is quite well covered in 'unker' Tan's blog, the focus has begun to shift from ODex to the neo-villian SingNet.

    Heard from the grapevine (blogs, forums, and coffeeshops)

    "SingNet failed to send a lawyer to contest ODex. Gasp!"

    "WTF!!"

    -.-"

    "o.O"

    "I'm changing ISP" (heard quite a few times)

    What a PR disaster! And the best part of it in, SingNet is still quiet about it while the entire blogosphere is going crazy over it.

    A quick scan around the net and you will find mounting displeasure over SingNet's attitude towards this whole issue.

    It's so bad it's overshadowing the 'arrogant Stephen Sing'.

    And it's no longer about whether ODEX is right to sue the kids for piracy in the first place, it's about SingNet not even lifting a finger to protect their customers' privacy.

    SingNet should formulate a PR campaign to salvage their reputation, fast.

    And in the process change the lawyers advising them on IP issues.

    For those of you who do not know, SingNet is the ISP arm of Singtel, one of the largest telecommunications giant in the region with business reaching as far as Australia and way beyond.

    For a long long time, Singapore has been making waves worldwide through good local companies like Singapore Airlines.

    Singtel is supposed to be another such iconic company - with their aggressive global expansion strategy.

    And for a long time the market has believed that Singtel is a professionally run company.

    All the more which makes this "SingNet failed to send a lawyer to contest ODex" unacceptable.

    Even more so, their silence on this PR disaster.

    For the 1,000 SingNet subscribers who have been issued letters by ODEX and paid their settlement, I can imagine their anger towards SingNet will not be small; noting that Stephen Sing was so scared he had to go to the police after receiving death threats.

    Although I do not wish for any of that strong anger to reach here, do sign up for a CNET Asia account and share with me your thoughts in the comments box.

    Whilst this privacy issue is already such a big thing for their customers, if SingNet still does not understand customer privacy and get it's act together, it will reflect poorly of Singtel as an iconic company for Singapore.

    Odex owned you; time to own Odex back

    Hi guys,

    While most anime downloaders, especially PacNet users, are rejoicing at the news, there may be news that might make you even happier.

    The latest Pacnet hearing has certainly flipped the tables on Odex; both in it's result and the judicial reasoning employed to attain that result. According to the Straits Times report, District Judge Ernest Lau arrived at his judgment by two reasonings;

    The draconian and drastic consequences of such an order upon the receiving party; ie, thus, an extremely strong standard of proof would be required for such an order in a civil case.
    A proper action for copyright infringement should be brought directly by the Japanese anime studios or the EXCLUSIVE LICENSEE. Odex is a sub-licensee (don't ask me about the difference in definition but hey, it's straight from the horse's mouth...) (Pay attention to this one ) but Odex is NEITHER the copyright owner nor EXCLUSIVE LICENSEE of the anime titles.
    Interesting. So the point of my post?

    According to a little used s.200 of our Copyright Act, , any person threatened with legal action for copyright infringement may bring an action to declare such threats unjustifiable, obtain an injunction against further such threats and...

    Recover damages he has sustained.

    Yup, you heard right.

    You can sue Odex for any damages they may have caused you as a result of the now baseless legal threat they have made against you. Especially if District Judge Ernest Lau's judicial reasoning is upheld through appeal and thereby strengthened.

    Furthermore, you may choose to launch this as a class action suit. Anyone who was issued with the dread Odex letter can choose to join the class action suit both concentrating your forces and of course, spreading out the legal fees.

    Dozo.

    P.S There is considerable legal doubt whether the settlement contract signed with Odex will stand. You can claim duress which will render the contract void (I believe there are plenty of substantial reasons which I won't go into here, to argue duress) and any contract clauses that require you to give up your legal cause of action against Odex smacks of illegality.

    The Unfair Contract Terms Act was pretty unhelpful here but I believe there are common law doctrines to be argued against those clauses.
