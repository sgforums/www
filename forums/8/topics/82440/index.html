    For beginners or people interested to pick up violin, i think those at music plaza should do fine. Gain some experience from some not so good violins and then get a better one at a later date.

    The sound of a violin depends mainly on the wood used to make the violin. Cheap ones usually means not so good wood while those that cost a bomb, uses nicer sounding wood.

    Handmade violins would also cost more, plus the kind of cut for the tone hole(?), might affect the price a little too.

    If your friend is already experienced, maybe it is more worth it to get a semi-professional model. Maybe can enquire at Music Plaza.
