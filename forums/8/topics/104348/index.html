    I was from MI when it was still two seperate Centralised Institutes.I got 17 points for my L1R4 and subsequently entered Jurong Institute, for the sole reason that I wanted to do my 'A'-Levels and not a Diploma.Mind you, it's not bed of roses to do a 3 year Pre U course.You still have to put in your best effort to make it to University.Some of the teaching may not be up to par to JCs but at the end of the day, if you depend too much on spoonfeeding, you will end up suffering anyway.Think independantly and do your own research.There will be people who are not motivated to do well there and you gotta stay focused on your goal.It's no joking matter if you spend 3 years and you have not got a 'A'-Level certificate which can get you into any university.

    If you want a place where it's not such a pressure cooker environment,by all means, do go there.Whether you make it is up to you.The place can be quite close-knitted as students feel this general us-versus-them attitude and that usually pushes them to show that they can outshine their JC counterparts.But please make your 3 years count.I did my best and am going to NUS next year with 2 distinctions.Well, that's what I can say.There are other JI alumni people in SGForums hanging around.I'm pretty sure they can give you a clear picture of the place too.

    The 'A' Level subjects offered are..

    1. English
    2. Geography
    3. History
    4. Physics
    5. Chemistry
    6. Economics
    7. Mathematics C
    8. Management of Business
    9. Accountancy
    10. Art

    Geography is only offered at the Toh Tuck campus.
