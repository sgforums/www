    APPEAL ALLOWED
    Expat escapes jail sentence for punching cop

    A JAPANESE expatriate who pushed and punched a police officer while drunk, was given a reprieve yesterday when his appeal against a short jail sentence was allowed.

    Chief Justice Yong Pung How set aside Miyakoshi Tadao's two-week jail sentence and fined him the maximum of $500 instead.

    The 45-year-old managing director of Niigata Power Systems was sentenced in May after admitting to pushing Sergeant Ho Kwong Soon, 23, against a parked vehicle on Oct 8 last year.

    He had also punched him on the right cheek in a carpark at Block 90, Zion Road at about 12.20am.

    A second charge of kicking another police officer on the left shin was considered during the sentencing.

    The two policemen, acting on a message, found Miyakoshi lying near a van in the carpark.

    The Japanese woke up and immediately walked away, leaving his bag behind.

    When Sgt Ho tried to hand over the bag to him, a drunken Miyakoshi turned and pushed it away.

    He then went on to push the officer's chest against a parked vehicle and punched him on the cheek.

    The court heard that Miyakoshi had been out drinking that night with three colleagues. He lost his way after his colleague dropped him off in a cab in Zion Road, near his Kim Seng Walk apartment.

    Yesterday, his lawyer, Ms Julia Yeo, said her client had an unblemished record and was of good character.

    He had turned the Singapore branch of his company around, taking it from a $1.75 million loss-making venture in 1999 to a $1 million profit-making one last year.

    She said Miyakoshi would lose his job and would have to return home in disgrace if he was jailed.

    Chief Justice Yong said two weeks was a 'very low' sentence, but mused if Miyakoshi knew what he was doing.

    'He was dead drunk,' he said, to which the deputy public prosecutor said that that could not be an excuse.

    Chief Justice Yong then added: 'He didn't know what he was doing.'

    He allowed the appeal and imposed the maximum fine.[/url]
