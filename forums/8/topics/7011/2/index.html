    Me:

    I cried very often when I was in my primary school days. I hardly got the attention from anyone, so crying is one effective or rather, pathetic way to do so

    As I go on to secondary school days (hardworking period), I places great emphasis on my school work (the only thing that makes me cry when my scores are beyond my expectations.. etc)

    During poly days, I hardly cry. School work is manageable and basically my classmates and I laughed through the three years.

    There are a few movies that I can recall crying over.

    1) Liang Zhu (It was a terrible feeling as I cannot cry out loud - My father is around and he is bound to say something or laugh at me if he were to see my cry) Hmph!

    2) You've got mail (Nothing touching about this movie actually, until I relate it to my experience. I really cried hard but softly - (in the cinema, no choice) but I am glad I have someone beside me to share this with.

    3) American Beauty (the part when the man dies and the things that flashes across his mind) http://www.sgforums.com/ubb/Forum78/HTML/000009.html

    I watched Titanic two times too, but I didn't cry at all throughout the whole show. All my female friends admitted they cry because they find the scenes touching. Even two of my male classmates went *sniff sniff* and *sob sob*

    Songs don't usually make me cry - unless they reminds me of my own experiences.

    I like the song 'Ku' by Ah niu too. It is by itself very meaningful but it relates to me even more. I was once in a dilemma during the process of making a decision in a relationship. Finally, I do not want to drag any further and made a decision. The next day, I cried. It was then that I have made a wrong decision. The answer was just right in front of me after I cried. (Remember the "The eyes that tear, see the world more clearly."?)

    Fortunately, I was given the chance to 'take back' my former decision and I am grateful till this day. Is it safe to say that those who are deeply involved in relationships (not necessary BGR) are more vunerable to crying?

    Girls who don't cry:
    I have a female friend who hardly cries. I find crying an absolutely healthy thing to do, rather than suppressing it and getting 'internal injuiries' (which is why, I cry often too, especially when I am angry) At the same time, I admire her for being to hold back her tears even in really bad times. There are very few people who are as strong as her, at least on the outside.

    Guys who cry:
    I like guys who open up and cry. They are human who needs to express their feelings too. Of coz, not to the extent of crying out loud in the public. Even girls try to do it discreetly. I feel that 'Da zhang fu liu xue bu liu lei' generation is over and I hold my respect to those guys who cry and generously admits it.
