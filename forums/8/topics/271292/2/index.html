    July 31, 2007

    It's official: Zoe Tay's contact has not been terminated. In a MediaCorp Studios statement issued last night, senior vice-president of Production Resources Andrew Cheng said that there is no termination of artistes who are pregnant.

    It's official: Zoe Tay's contact has not been terminated.

    In an interview she gave to a magazine, the 39-year-old star who is seven months pregnant with her second child said that "pregnant artistes will have their contracts suspended temporarily and reinstated after they give birth. That is why for this period, I will have no income." The artiste also said that there was no such policy back then in 2004 when she was pregnant with her first child.

    This quote was later picked by and reported in a Chinese daily yesterday.

    In a MediaCorp Studios statement issued last night, senior vice-president of Production Resources Andrew Cheng rebutted the news saying: "There is no termination of artistes who are pregnant."

    He said: "The present service contract provides flexibility to artistes who are unable to discharge their duties fully due to their personal interest, to be paid according to the jobs they can perform. This also applies to artistes who are pregnant.

    "Mediacorp Studios has always adopted a pro-family practice. Although the nature of the service contract is "contract of services only", we still pay all pregnant artistes the three-month full pay ( similar to permanent staff on maternity leave) with no obligation or extra commitment by the artistes."
