  • About A GaL

    http://www.channelnewsasia.com/stories/singaporelocalnews/view/162175/1/.html

    SINGAPORE : Labour chief Lim Boon Heng has said ignorance in Singapore's multi-racial and multi-religious society has led to some Malays and Indians feeling discriminated against when looking for jobs.

    Mr Lim was speaking at a National Day observance ceremony at the National Trades Union Congress on Monday.

    He said non-Chinese job seekers say they have been asked if they can speak Mandarin during their job interviews.

    The few who could were even asked if they could write Chinese.

    Mr Lim says such employers should stop these tactics.

    The minister says if any minority group feels there is discrimination, there will be tension and this could break the harmony Singaporeans have painstakingly built.

    Mr Lim urged all employers to play their part and recruit on the basis of merit, not by race or religion.

    He says it is also in everyone's collective interest to observe the established tripartite Code of Employment Practice. - CNA /ct
