    Dun play play eh, the danger is very real, it's just that you're lucky. In a thunderstorm, lightning can be conducted through water pipes and right into your heater which couducts the power through the water, which conducts it through you and into the floor at over 24,000 volts of potential difference.

    Resulting electrical passage and heat generated via resistance causes 3 degree burns, sudden powerful muscle contractions or worse, stop the heart. Then you die.

    The reason why you usually get away with it is because of the farday cage effect of your house which tends to conduct the electricity around when struck, rather then through you via paths of less resistance.

    However don't count of that, be there be a break in the safety cage effect, the power from the strike will be conducted through you, and that can be quite bad.

    You can be struck inside a house, and yes, surges can travel through a phone line as well and wreck your modem, cpu, or even you when you are using a phone. My modem got fried by a strike.
