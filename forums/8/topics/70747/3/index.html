    My nick comes from the gedanken experiment commonly known as Schoedinger's Cat.

    In writing about quantum physics, Erwin Schroedinger noted that we use models rather than actual objects to understand nature. These models, of course, are subject to interpretation and manipulation. To illustrate his point, Schroedinger described a box containing a cat, with its lid rigged up to a vial of prussic acid. To find out if the cat is alive, the box must be opened, but if the box is opened, the vial breaks and the prussic acid fumes kill the cat. Therefore, the very act of observation determines the outcome.

    Similarly, in psychology, asking a person a question can influence how they think about something, since the question can raise awareness of issues hitherto not considered. Again, the act of observation influences the outcome. My nick's just a bit of a professional reminder to myself.
