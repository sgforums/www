  • caramon
    22 Feb 05, 01:02
    Singapore was portray one of modern apartment blocks with all sorts of amenities, aircon HDB flats...HDB carparks filled with many luxury cars...clean streets and smiling faces..dozens of golf courses filled with blissfull golfers...aplenty country clubs and town clubs.. BUT beneath all that lies the unconceal-able truth..

    What it is about is the unseen, unheard, poor in Singapore whose
    utilities have been cut off, whose children can hardly afford text books.. The number of such people have in fact increase as unemployment which was unheard off in the 80s became common in the recent years..

    Go around any kopi-tiam, you will see many old folks selling packets of tissue and collecting empty soft-drink cans as their main source of income.. Go to Jalan Besar, China town and you will see the Living wretched old man lives by scrapping and sleeving off old electrical wires of appliances to sell the copper as scrap,cutting out old carton boxes to sell etc...

    Most of these peasant are the " living dead" they go through life in motion and most don't complain ...they have accepted their fate in life.. It is people like us who have social conscience that are demanding that the govt do more to help the needy.. I agree that you don't give them a fish but teach them how to fish.. But its becoming hard for them to even afford that fish rod that could eventually help them save their lifes...
