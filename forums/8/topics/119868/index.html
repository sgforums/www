    I dare not open her letter..

    Standing on the MRT platform, red Outdoor duffel bag at my feet, leaning on a ceramic tiled pillar, the wind create a soft breeze towards my face.. For the umpteenth time I used my free hand to straighten my white shirt, cursing silently when my hand brushed roughly across the edges of my badge, pinned limply on a corner of the shirt pocket..

    With my other hand, I clutched tightly at the white envelope..

    It was not the first time I had faced such a dilemma.. A dilemma of both wanting and not wanting to open the envelope, afraid of the contents; fearful of the consequences; aware of the potential heartbreak..

    A flicker of movement caught my peripheral eye, and I turned to find yellow letters on the previously empty message board. " Marina South S01".. The train was arriving soon..

    I looked down at the envelope as the train pulled into the station, seemingly unstoppable until it came to a muffled, controlled halt.. I was transfixed on the envelope I held, not caring for my hair, coaxed to untidiness by the wake of the incoming train now.. all I could think is the letter, and what turn in my life it would bring.. The envelope was bare, but I knew whatever it contained was addressed straight to my heart..

    I'll read the letter on the train, I thought, stepping away from the afternoon heat into the chilly confines of the MRT train.. I put down the bag and leaned against a pole.. As my fear grew stronger, heartbeat grew faster, as trepidation bit into my self-composed confidence, only one thought flashed mindlessly in my head..

    We had a big quarrel last week.. I cursed myself for that but what's done, I hope to get it undone..

    I had bought her this bouquet of flowers, like what I used to give her in the past.. Later bringing her to her favourite Japanese restaurant.. She likes that restaurant very much.. Buy her a teddy bear later from Isetan Lido.. Try to tell her how I feel.. That should patch things up..

    I hope...

    Finally I muster enough courage to open up that envelope..

    The luxury of hope was gone.. It was as certain as the departing train; as surely as the neatly written words I had read over and over again in the past half an hour..

FireIce was even less formal but utterly irresistible in creased shorts and a loose white singlet.. my eyes was full of adoration as I looked at FireIce..

    We had read our vows as we held hands and laughed at the sheer joy of being young, in love and staying in a five star chalet resort.. We had plan the years blissfully stretching ahead of us, together forever..
