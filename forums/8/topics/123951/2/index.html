    "Feb 19, 2005 Straits Times
    TODAY'S SIGHTS
    Matilda set for makeover


    [size=9]NEW LEASE OF LIFE: Built in 1902, Matilda House is to be restored to its former glory. -- ASHLEIGH SIM[/size]

    URA working to restore Punggol's last remaining historical bungalow
    By Sheryl Loke

    THIS large early-style tropical bungalow stands alone on a small hill in Punggol.

    Residents in the neighbourhood do not know much about it, but checks with the Urban Redevelopment Authority (URA) of Singapore reveal that it is a building worthy of conservation.

    Matilda House, as the bungalow is known, is the only remaining historical bungalow in Punggol. It was built in 1902 for a Mr Joseph Cashin, whose family history in Singapore can be traced to the early 1840s.

    Though deserted now, it is not difficult to picture the building in its former days of glory - its open verandas, raised floors and louvres speak of space and luxury.

    According to lawyer Howard Cashin, the house was named for his paternal grandmother, and it now stands as a reminder of the colonial days.

    The Cashin family, originally from Ireland, is one of the oldest to have settled in Singapore.

    Mr Joseph Cashin was once a lawyer's clerk and invested in opium farms, legal in the 1880s, putting the fortune he made into real estate. He even has a street here named after him - Cashin Street off North Bridge Road.

    The URA has been working with other agencies to explore suitable uses for Matilda House so that the building may be restored to its former splendour and adapted for new uses. No firm plans are available yet, but the URA has realised the house's potential for community use or as a clubhouse once the surrounding area is developed. Meanwhile, more adventurous children still hang around the bungalow, hoping to catch a glimpse of the legendary spooks."
