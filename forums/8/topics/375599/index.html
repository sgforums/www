    Now I want to talk a bit about insulting ads.

    The first one is the one with Good Mental Health promotion.....

    You know, one giraffe, one elephant, and one monkey.....

    The monkey said....."got peanut still better than no peanuts...."

    This is highly insulting.

    The first time I watched it, I was already uncomfortable....

    Second, third, and subsequent times I got more annoyed and irritated....

    It seems that the ads conceptor just want to equate people with monkeys and animals, and we should just better be thankful if we have peanuts to eat, meaning, we should lower ourselves to the standards of animals. As long as you have some basic neccessities met, you should not starve and so on, you should be happy.

    I find it very insulting, looking down on people, deliberate cheapening of human lives, crude, low class, no IQ, and stupid ads.

    Also, I can understand if some messages targetted towards children use cartoons and animals, that I can understand. But for a message targetted towards adults, struggling with economic crisis, it is highly patronising and insulting. Not only do they use kiddie method of using cartoon and animals, but they use a very crude and rude inference or message or principle.

    And then there is another one.

    In many public places, you can see Graciousness Campaign...

    They use Pua Chu Kang and Rosie in posters...and sometimes in songs and in videos..

    This is very insulting.

    I dont know what the conceptor of this ads was thinking when he was dreaming one night. Did he think it's such a good idea? No.

    PCK and Rosie are basically caricatures and stereotypes and examples of classism.

    I also notice Good Graciousness using PCK and Rosie are mostly aired in public places such as MRT, Bus stations, in other words, to the working masses, middle class and lower-middle-class.

    So this ads conceptor one night must be thinking, "Haha those poor people, I teach you good manners with your cousins, PCK and Rosie, who look exactly like you all"

    It's insulting, patronising, crude, rude, insensitive, no class, low-IQ, ads.

    The PCK and Rosie are depicted in typical low class manners according to their charicatured stereotypes typified by them, and the song also use the typical made-up not really Singlish but worse, "Gurmitlish" fictional accent, and so on.

    I think those two ads should be banned, completely.

    Please let me know what you think, and also are there any other ads you have found to be offensive?
