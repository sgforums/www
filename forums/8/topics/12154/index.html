The 4th University: Is it that good?

  • OrionNebula
    1 Aug 01, 11:13
    This is quoted from straits times 28 July 2001:

    Panel says yes to fourth university
    -------------------------------------
    A high-level committee has said it should have a strong technical bent, work closely with industry and provide flexible learning opportunities
    -------------------------------------

    With the declaration of the formation of the 4th University in Singapore, I personally feel quite skeptical regarding the standard and the kind of recommendations make by the high-level committee.

    Firstly, the general idea that this university will be solely for polytechnic graduates is off. It was stated by the committee: "A major consideration, the report says, would be making it easy for National Technical Certificate, A-level and diploma holders, mature students, mid-career workers and foreigners to study full-time or part-time."

    What does this imply? Even a person with NTC certificate also are enrolled into the university!? Does that make people with diploma certificates from the 4 polytechnic in singapore worthless or even degrading? Then what is the worth of the degree cert?

    Secondly, as compare to NUS and NTU, wouldn't this 4th University be taken as a 2nd or 3rd grade university and be considered as a dumping for people who did not manage to get into NUS and NTU?
    Even the recently formed SMU, is not so recognised at all. Maybe in singapore but not in overseas.

    Recently, I have a friend who graduated with a master course from University of Essex, and he even told me that there are students down there who don't even know what is NUS and NTU (becos they don't know where is singapore). Don't even talk about SMU and worst till, the 4th university.

    Thirdly, by allowing mid-career workers or foreigners to take part-time degree, isn't it something like a open-U? Or can I say that it is comparable to SIM?!

    Personally, I am a polytechnic graduate from NP and I did not manage to get enrol into NUS or NTU for twice, due to one of my subject having a P (supplementary paper). This has put me into a dilemma of going to overseas to get a degree, but I have to work a few years so as to save sufficient amount to be able to afford the expenses.

    Now, with the committee delcaring the approval for the formation of the 4th University, I have to think twice again to go overseas.
    But it seems to be that the pros are more than the cons for the 4th U in singapore, due to the above reasons.
    This 4th university may not be that recognised as compare to NUS and NTU and it even allows NTC cert people to get in. I feel a bit pathetic about this issue.

    Can anyone comment on this issue?
