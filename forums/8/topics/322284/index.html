    If the landlord doesnt pay u back the deposit, keep everything in writing (the TA, the utility bills and other misc fees).     It all depends on the crafting of the rental agreement.   Industry practice is that you need to stay a minimum of 3 months before giving the 1 month notice, i.e. you stay a total of 4 months. 

     

    As I said, it all depends on how the actual agreement was crafted.  You, being the tenant are at the losing end because the owner is holding your deposit.  How do you think you are going to get back the deposit if you act tough with the owner... or the agent (but the agent doesn't seem very experienced).
