    Hi all...

    The 2 1/2 years of NS taught me many things, like living together with other guys and tolerating all their habits, be they good or bad. I sort of agree with raymone, in that there are certain things that cannot be learnt in our everyday smooth-flowing lives. I guess a person's true character shows when the whole bunch of guys are being stressed till breaking point, and sometimes even further. That time, you will see maybe the popular guy trying to wriggle his way out of doing things, and the most hated guy in the platoon encouraging the rest to go on.

    I think I was considered one of the siao on guys when in BMT at 40 SAR, back in 1995. Of all the luck, I had to be posted to Bravo Coy, the most siong one. Really trained hard, even wanted to sign on. But luckily, I didn't get into OCS, so I didn't. After becoming a sergeant, went to School of Armour, became an instructor and got first contact with office politics. Imagine, my head of department can tell OC everything can do, but once return to office begin to say what f-ed up ideas OC had. That was what eventually disillusioned me about the army.

    Well, this is what I have to say about my experience. Hope it prepares you for the road ahead, Jack...

    Cheers.

    I'm still serving NS, and i've had the priveledge of being on BOTH the combat AND service sides for half of my NS each.

    I'd like to say that even now, I think that i've learnt more from NS than I ever did all my 12 years for formal study. Not academically, of course, but i've grown the most as a person during these almost 2 years and i'm eternally thankful for NS.

    The combat side is physically tough but extremely rewarding. There's true friendship, camaraderie, bonding, shared experiences. On the service side, you sit around and grow a belly, maybe even go home everyday, but your friendships are never REALLY tested, your "ally" will stab you in the back without blinking, everyone looks out for themselves only, you're frustrated and just wanna go back to the combat side, even as a rifleman.

    NS isn't about whether we think our homeland can be defended when some superpower bears down on us with a salvo of nukes - it's about what i've seen of the combat side - loyalty, comradeship, true friendship that is willing to support and sacrifice for what we believe in - the Sovereignity of our Republic. It's not that we have a 2% chance of winning a war or not, it's that we have 100% commitment to win it. THEN we WILL win. When Vietnam broke out, who do you think people placed their bets on? But it was their indomitable will and unity that held the country through (ideals aside).

    All you shiteating bastards who think like Himura Kenshin had better listen up - get loyal or get the hell out of my homeland, since you don't seem to care a hoot about it.
