    Hi... 1st posting to this forum...

    NS? Call me a "seow nang" if you want, but I sort of miss my NS days compared to my current NTU life...

    Back in NS you get to switch off your brains for 2 1/2 yrs after JC/poly... even if you were an officer or specialist, the level of thinking required just does not beat that of a student's or professional's.

    Before any1 mistaken me for a "kengster" or a downgrader in NS, let me assure you that I'm not. I was a scout by vocation, and IMHO that's the 3rd most siong vocation you can kena in the army, after the commandos and the guardsmen. Land, air and sea, I've done everything thinkable less airborne and heli-repelling.

    Those were the days, but then again, the grass is always greener on the other side. Back in my NS days, I thought that JC life was better... during my JC days, secondary skool seems like a breeze... during seconday skool days, primary skool blah blah blah... u get my point...

    Even within your 2 1/2 yrs, you'll experience the same thing. BMT seems the most siong thing you've ever gone thru, but after I entered SISPEC, I missed my good 'ol BMT days... and in my unit, SISPEC seemed like heaven.

    Furthermore, now SAF is macham WELFARE army... from the commercialized cookhouse to the lifts in BMTC (Tekong), sponge padding SBOs to the new SAR 21s... army daze now was only a shadow of its past (And we're talking about only1 to 2 yrs ago when your veggies had sand and rice tasted like meatballs).

    Therefore, wherever you are doing now be it student, NS etc, treasure it although it may seem siong or boring, coz once you cross the fence to the greener side, the grass there will die.
