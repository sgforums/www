    im thinking, the reason the effective radius of the shell seems diminished might be because it was likely to be facing up, rather than down.

    Normally when a 40mm shell detonates, it would be due to it striking a solid object head on. This means the resultant expanding pressure is vented sideways and backwards. As the shell in question was likely facing up, if it's used as a paperweight, then most of the explosive pressure from the detonation would have been vented upwards in a cone, instead of in a hemisphere towards the side and back as it normally would. This would explain why it did not cause as much injury as it was expected to.

    Also, we have to bear in mind the probability that this was a defective round, or that it might have been lying there long enough that its explosive compounds have decayed and is less effective.
