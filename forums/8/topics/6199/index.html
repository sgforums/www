    Heya Moog-ie,

    Well the long and short of the answer to your question is simply the magic phrase, BPM - or beats per minute. How the dance categories are divided depends very much on the BPM which in turns affect the style of the mixing...

    For example, at 120BPM, it's probably hardcore trance or it could also be a souped up breakbeat/bigbeat but it would never be just ordinary house (which will go something like 90BPM). Another frequently misused term, breakbeat, is actually a descriptor for a type of sampling rather than the common usage as a genre (as popularised by Chem Bros and/or Norman Cook)

    There are certain genres which are etymologically linked. The examples I'll use are selected on their popularity rather than quality as you might find identifying them easier.

    a) Trance and Ambient (repetitous beats varied by the degree of weird sound effects or ordered to highlight a certain short sequence of notes) eg. Mark Snow's X-Files theme. ATB, John Digweed mixes, Eiffel, Sash! etc...

    b)House/Garage/Handbag [easy-going sequences and medium beats interspersed by piercing vocals (usually female) and is the most popular genre] eg. LOADS! Any Pete Tong mixes, Paul Oakenfold (Perfecto), even M-People is considered House.

    c) Big Beat [charactered by consistent use of breakbeats and discordant sampling, easily the most dance friendly] eg. Chem Bros, Norman Cook, Asian Dub Foundation.

    d) Drum&Bass/Jungle [based on the jazz chords that has been spruced up by hard beats and sampling. In the case of jungle, the jazz rythmns are almost totally replaced. Heavy emphasis on synths/drum machine. NOT acid jazz] eg. Goldie, Bjork, Red Snapper, Propellerheads.

    e) Acid Jazz [essentially spruced up jazz tunes, employing drum machines and synth tones sometimes. Seldom uses sampling. Enjoyable nonetheless] eg. Jamiroquai, The Heavytones, The Brand New Heavies...

    note : I personally dun think there is such a thing as "techno". It's a personal view alright. :p What is meant by "techno" in Singapore is "Eurodance". Basically cheesy house music popular in Continental Europe where no one understands English :p. This has been associated with the beng-culture in SG. To say, "I like to listen to techno" makes no sense. It just reflects the person's "poseur status". Recently, dance music has been repackaged as "Electronica".

    Synth sounds/sampling/drum machines are nothing new... even goddamn Jefferson Airplane used them to some degree. Not to mention the New Romantics in the early 80s. Flock of Seagulls, Culture Club, Duran Duran are guilty as charged. I remember OMD having a B-side which is just dance beat as early as 1985 and they are anything but avant garde. Joy Div/New Order were experimenting starting from the late 70s.

    Good enough for ya?!
