    When people's mind is all clouded with money, this is what you get.
    Sad but true. It is not MLM that is bad, its the people mindset. I have been emphasising to my friends that true MLM is a long term career, not a money game. Many successful MLM company emphasise all round development for its distributors.

    Yet, these companies numbered a few. Experts found thousands of newly established companies in US, yet only 40 were able to break thru the first five formulative years and become successful companies. The rest are either lousy ones or iilegal scams.

    Good companies I met emphasise this point to me, and set realistic goals and give good advices on how to proceed, while other just emphasise on cheques and cars. I told my friends till throat hoarse, dont look at money, cars and houses, analyse whether you can be successful in this company or not.

    This is the reason why I am perplexed now, coz I see my friends anyhow join, just hantam. Especially youngsters who are not streetwise. Sometimes, I feel like blaming Robert Kiyosaki, coz many people love to read his success stories, but do not know the application. Worse, his method of success cannot be wholly applied on MLM, coz its a different business altogether from what he does. MLM is between investment as purported by RK, and traditional business. Once you try to apply his philosophy on biz onto MLM, voila, you get pyramids and scams.

    I am praying for my friends now......
