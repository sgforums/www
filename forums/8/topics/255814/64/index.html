    COURTING couples will get a chance to take their love to new heights on Valentine's Day next year - but they had better hurry and book a place.
    The operators of the 42-storey- high observation wheel said yesterday that it will operate its first public rides on Feb 14 next year.

    However, 100,000 tickets have already been booked. The wheel can take about 27,000 people a day, with a basic 37-minute ride costing $29.50 an adult.

    Each of the wheel's 28 capsules, which can take up to 30 people, can be booked for $1,000.

    Ms Patsy Ong, managing director of Adval Brand Group, the attraction's main ticketing and sales agent, said she expects more than 500,000 visitors within the first three months.

    She added that the greatest interest so far is from corporations and tour agents. However, she said that 20 per cent of the tickets will be set aside for walk-in visitors daily.

    Singapore Flyer managing director Peter Purcell said yesterday: 'Interest in it has been much more than we ever imagined.'

    He was speaking at a press conference to announce the appointment of Mr David Beever as the general manager.

    Between now and the opening, Mr Beever is looking to to beef up staff strength from the current four to 150.

    Mr Purcell yesterday also showed off the capsules which, at the size of a single-deck bus, are 20 per cent bigger than those of the London Eye.

    Safety features include a device to prevent the doors from opening during the flight and closed circuit cameras in each cabin.

    Mr Purcell said that test rides should begin by November.

    IT ONLY opens next year, but the Singapore Flyer, an iconic giant observation wheel which stands 165 metres high over Raffles Avenue, is already stirring up a lot of buzz.

    In the last two months, around 100,000 people have already pre-booked their rides on the Flyer. With such a positive response, it seems that the Flyer might be fully booked for its first three months when it opens in February next year.

    Out of these 100,000, half are corporate bookings, while the other half were reserved by travel agencies.

    The Singapore Flyer, which cost a whopping $240 million to build, will comprise 28 fully air-conditioned glass capsules.

    With each capsule able to accomodate 30 passengers, everyone inside can enjoy a bird's eye-view of Singapore's city and beyond while the 'capsule' rotates around the wheel.

    Each ride costs $29.50 per person, and lasts for 37 minutes.

    For those willing to fork out $1,000 or more, they can even book an entire private capsule for themselves.

    This means they can also customise the capsule to meet their needs - from adding dining tables and chairs for a glitzy dinner, to having a themed wedding bash on board.

    For normal passengers, canapes and pastries will be served on board, and you can also select from a variety of wines and champagnes. An in-flight host will also be available on request.

    The Singapore Flyer is slated to open by Valentine's Day next year.

    You can book tickets online at www.singaporeflyer.com.sg .
