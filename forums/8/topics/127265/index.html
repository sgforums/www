    Hi Faye,

    Sorry, this is not a direct answer to your question on FitnessFirst.....but since you now say that you're interested in joining a fitness club, maybe I can throw in some views.

    I was with Planet Fitness Suntec for a year (and still have access now to the Suntec branch on one-day passes). I was never with Fitness First.

    Basically, there are three well-known players here - Fitness First, Planet Fitness and California Fitness. All are basically the same in terms of atmosphere (yuppy, semi-friendly), except that California is more crowded and with more pushy sales staff too. Never heard anything bad about the other two.

    On a not-so-upscale level, there are also community centre gyms, private gyms, etc....most of which have some sort of aerobics too.

    The "Body" type aerobics (Body Combat, Body Pump, Body Attack, etc.) can be found everywhere....its 'invented' by a New Zealand based company called LesMills (www.lesmills.com) and they licence instructors to teach them worldwide. In Singapore, these instructors are mainly free-lancers who teach at every gym (so you MAY see the same faces whichever gym you join). Most gyms have these "Body" type aerobics....and I believe they are on a fixed schedule, so you'll go through the same steps at any gym anywhere in the world at any one time (they change their steps every few weeks and label it Body Attack. For example, the whole world will do Body Combat 1 from Jan-Feb 2001, Body Combat 2 from Feb-Mar 2001, and so on and so forth). Its all activated by LesMills in NZ...they say change, the whole world changes.

    Being an ardent gym-goer for the past....er....very many years, I have seen so many times that one person likes a certain gym, but another can hate it. Feelings and impressions are subjective and hard to describe in words. Best is to see/feel it for yourself. Most gyms will give you a free trial period (eg. 7-day trial period) if you just ask them. I would recommend taking advantage of this to get a first-hand feel of each for yourself (after all, its a reasonably big investment for the average person like me).

    Good Luck!

    PS: You can see Planet Fitness aerobics schedule here in pdf format http://www.planetfitness.com.sg/downloads/scheduleAprJuly05.pdf

    PPS: Fitness First schedule are here in html format, although I suspect you've already viewed it http://marketing.fitnessfirst.com.sg/Timetables/Timetable.html
