Have fun at duck race on Nov 12

  • no heart
    1 Nov 00, 12:07
    Come Nov 12, someone who has adopted a rubber duck could be $1 million the richer.

    But the day also has a wealth of entertainment in store for all who gather by the Singapore River.

    Way before the 100,000 rubber duckies take to the water for The Straits Times Million Dollar Duck Race, youngsters from 12 to 16 years old will have a chance to be budding journalists.

    With a little help from The Straits Times professionals, they can have an interesting time reporting on the event -- and win cash prizes to boot.

    There will be 50 places for the rookie reporters, who will be divided into teams of five, each team guided by an ST reporter.

    After a brief talk, they will be sent into the crowds to find a story.

    And each beat will write their joint report on laptops provided on-site.

    The best story will be published in the ST the next day and the winning team members will each receive $100.

    The runner-up team will get $60 each, and those in third place will receive $20 each.

    There will also be plenty going on to keep the adults happy, especially when it comes to shopping and eating.

    Stalls selling knick-knacks, handicrafts and food will be open for business at the Raffles Landing Site.

    A fashion bazaar with clothes contributed by local designers will provide an opportunity for guilt-free shopping.

    All the proceeds from the bazaar will go to non-profit charity organisation Touch Community Services, which offers a range of community services to the needy and disadvantaged.

    And for those who like a giggle, five of ST's cartoonists will be on hand with charcoal pencils and paper to sketch a caricature of anyone -- for a mere $5 fee. All the money will go to Touch.

    The duck race itself starts at 3 pm at Read Bridge near Clarke Quay.

    Of the 100,000 racing duckies, 99,000 are yellow and 1,000 are red.

    If one of the yellow ducks is the first to cross the finishing line near Raffles Landing Site, the adoptive owner will be $10,000 richer.

    If a red duck wins, it will make its owner an instant millionaire.
