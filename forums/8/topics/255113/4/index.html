    Tie Guan Ying is one of my favorite teas. The tea drinking provinces are down south, in Fujian area. Northern china seldom drink tea, or should i say, less tea drinkers. Maybe they do, but not the Kung Fu tea, (Gong fu cha) like Tie Guan Ying.

    The best teas can cost quite a lot, the top range ones could be around 5000rmb for 1 jin. Thats 1k sing for around 500grams.

    Beijing is a nice place to visit. I've been there twice and enjoyed it thoroughly.

    Places of interest are the Forbidden city, great wall, and wang fu jing.

    Food: You can't miss the famous Beijing duck. Gotta try it!

    Always on the lookout when you're out touring around, you'll look like a tourist right away and the minute you speak, they will know you're from overseas. The tour guides and taxi drivers, they are all out to make quick money, so they may introduce you to different places etc and sometimes its just made up stories, so beware.

    (Even chinese from other provinces also get cheated!l)

    I recommend visiting their night clubs. Prepare to spend a minimum of 1500rmb. They all have minimum spending for the rooms.

    The top KTVs in Beijing will put all singaporean KTVs to shame.
