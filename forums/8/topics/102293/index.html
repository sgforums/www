    slang
    noun

    1. A kind of language occurring chiefly in casual and playful speech, made up typically of short-lived coinages and figures of speech that are deliberately used in place of standard terms for added raciness, humor, irreverence, or other effect.
    2. Language peculiar to a group; argot or jargon

    accent
    noun

    # The relative prominence of a particular syllable of a word by greater intensity or by variation or modulation of pitch or tone.
    # Vocal prominence or emphasis given to a particular syllable, word, or phrase.
    # A characteristic pronunciation, especially:
    1. One determined by the regional or social background of the speaker.
    2. One determined by the phonetic habits of the speaker's native language carried over to his or her use of another language.

    slang is a word used to describe something, usually perspersly made up to make fun of something, or the colloquial way to call something

    accent is a way of delivering the language, by the differences in tone, pitch, stresses, etc....
    same language.....but "sounds" different...
