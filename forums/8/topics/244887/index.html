    My batch, by intermediate, only 1/3 are left... and those who eventually reach JLPT 2 are even lesser...

    I think many people in the batch do not put in effort to memorize the vocabulary and think they can get by without putting effort to study.

    Its just hard work just like going to Kindergarten to learn ABCs and grammar rules again.

    But learning Japanese can be very enjoyable... I learnt at Ikoma many years back.. totally enjoyable and i really looked forward to Japanese classes...Lots of funny games and we created wacky sentences with the Japanese we learnt.

    At intermediate level there is a breaking point, which decides whether you can master the grammar.. its the Verb-conjugation part - that is to change to past tense etc... make sure extra attention is paid to that because its the foundation for many other part of the grammar...Those who missed that lesson cannot grasp the future chapters..

    Once you clear that Hurdle...

    It would be acquiring vast amount of Vocab in Kanji which would enable reading and comprehension of text.
