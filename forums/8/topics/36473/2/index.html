    OFFICIAL PRESS RELEASE

    What? -- Probably the largest Japanese-themed (or culturally specific) Dance event in WA history.

    Music: J-Techno, J-Trance, J-Pop, J-Rock, Eurobeat, Eurodance, EBM (Electronic Body Music).

    Features: Fashion Show (Redstripe / Wildwardrobe /Lily Clothing & Accessories), Piercing show (Flesh Mechanix), Sushi Catering (Yoko's Gourmet Cafe)

    Specialty Drinks: Japanese Slipper, Oyster Shooter

    After Party @ The Amplifier (Globe Entertainment Complex) Â– Trade in stamped ticket for free entry and a free drink! Doors open till 6am!

    Where? -- Gilkisons Dance Studio. CNR Murray and Irwin Street, Northbridge - Non-smoking Venue, Smoking area provided (*Warning: Guests smoking in inappropriate areas, such as the dance floor, will be asked to leave) *Gilkisons is an Australian National Heritage building; Very rustic and opulent. Many ballroom dancing competitions have been held there along with Club events such as Dominion and Radium Labs. We have chosen this venue because it is suitable to house a much larger crowd than a conventional club. For the sake of comparison, Gilkisons is nearly twice the size of Zouk in Singapore.

    When? -- 29th March, Saturday. Doors open @ 10pm till late.

    Who? -- Renegade (The Haven and Outbreed's Resident) + Guests: DJs Martin & Brett (Dominion/Nevermore), Toby (Amplifier/The Globe), DJ Evac (Radium Labs) and Jason (Amplifier)

    Why? -- Outbreed was organized with the intent of bringing people of different cultures together. It is also a opportunity to examine the richness of Japanese culture and to dispel harmful stereotypes that the Japanese (As well as all other asians) experience in foreign countries. Outbreed was created with a alternative sub-theme to add some spice to the mixture. As Japanese nightlife is often (wrongly) associated with kitsch and subversive practices, we have decided to parody this with an alternative overtone (prominent in the piercing show and music)

    On a purely clubbing perspective, Japanese dance music was never given enough exposure abroad although CD sales have boomed in Singapore, Malaysia and even amongst sporadic cult fan clubs that have sprouted throughout Australia in the wake of the popularisation of Anime and Manga. Japanese dance music is energetic and flamboyant, but the genre has hardly received any dance floor outlet outside its country of origin. Experiencing J-techno/trance/pop in a proper environment gives a truly escapist feel; those who do not understand the language still find it an exciting, adventurous affair- the vocals reportedly take on the role of another instrument, adding a new dimension to its 'feel'. The explosive combination of music at Outbreed will thrill club-virgins and hardcore fans alike.

    How much? -- $15 presale ($13 special for TSL,MARs,POGS members) / $18 @ the door
