    Hole in heart can be divided into two spectrum:-

    The one that presents with bluish discoloration (Cyanosis) early and the other late

    The early ones are the most dangerous and require a rapid surgical intervention...

    They can be remembered as the five Ts

    Tricuspid Atresia
    Total Anomalous Pulmonary Venous Connection
    Transposition of Great Arteries
    Truncus Arteriosus
    Tetralogy of Fallot

    The later ones, can be remembered as below, they are safer and present with bluish discoloration of the skin and mucous membranes later in life

    Atrial Septal Defects (very good prognosis)
    Ventricular Septal Defects (depends on the hole size)
    Patent Ductus Arteriosus (produces a machinery murmur, spot diagnosis)
    Mixed Atrioventricular Septal Defects (requires early repair)

    Yeah... the hole provides a lower pressure for the blood flow as compared to the normal passageway where blood entering the right heart will go up the pulmonary arteries (which has a higher pressure). Depending on the type of hole in the heart, the blood will travel from right to left or left to right.

    With continuous left to right shunting of the blood, sooner or later, the blood flow will change its course from right to left because of a raised pressure in the pulmonary artery leading to a normal physiological constriction to protect the fragile pulmonary capillaries.

    Such conversion is known as the Eisenmenger Syndrome...

    Okay, here's the thing... you need to get your heart screened up by an Echocardiography (ECHO) and have an ECG (Electrocardiogram) done. Also, get a Chest X Ray (CXR) done as well. These are the basic most investigations to see how severe your heart condition is.

    You seem to be normal, hence you are normal. But considering pregnancy in the future, prior to entering the course of it, you need to know the risk you have to take. The body undergoes enormous physiological changes during pregnancy, your blood volume goes up, your hemoglobin levels come down, your kidney has to work extra hard, your heart has to pump all the excess blood volume around the body to supply sufficient substances (oxygen, toxins and nutrients) to and away from you and your baby.

    The heart is under severe stress during pregnancy and this is where all the symptoms you have not been having, will start to appear one by one causing a lot of distress. From shortness of breath, to awakening at night because of lack of air, to gross leg swelling, racing heart beat, chest tightness and cyanosis, all of these will accentuate itself as the heart is burdened. This is even worse during labor where the uterus has to contract at a great force to expel the baby out and when the placenta is retrieved, the bleeding will be often more severe as there is an autonomous regulation of blood flow back to your heart after constriction of all the blood vessels in the placenta (the placenta is an organ that has a lot of such blood vessels to feed your baby while in the womb). Such enormous blood flow channeled back to your heart will strain up your heart and can cause a severe collapse in the labor room.

    Having a heart disease in pregnancy is a high risk pregnancy. You need to however get your heart checked up to find out the type of heart disease you are suffering from before attempting to get pregnant. Complete your family early, best depends on the condition of your heart.

    It needs to be borne in mind that with each pregnancy, your heart gets worse, so having only a child or two would suffice in certain cases. But with our current advent of such great technology and ample follow ups with prenatal, antenatal and postnatal care, such great demands and risk are often reduced though problems may still arise.

    Get your heart checked up FIRST!
