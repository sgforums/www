    Again, as I said in your other thread, what makes you think other countries want you? Go see if you've got enough points before you even consider whether to move.

    Just a breakdown for those of you wondering ..

    Day-day cost of living in Australia is much higher than in Singapore; although the actual cost of major expenses are much lower. Decent accomodation would run you anywhere from $300k to $800k depending on location/age etc. Of course, cars are much cheaper; current petrol prices this morning stands at $1.34 - 4cents (with $30 coles rebate).

    Tax is much higher here. It is based on your income. >$60k, you'd be looking at a massive chunk gone; go see the Aus. Tax Office's rates on their website.

    An average meal outside will cost you $10 ppax and this aint at some fancy restaurant either; a normal eatery. Even eating at home isn't exactly cheap.

    And just since the TS is thinking in cents, and not dollars ..

    1) Haircut = $20; Asian stylists charge $30 for men, and much higher for ladies depending on length.

    2) Cinema tickets = $9 I think for students, or $11 I forgot ... much higher; and there's no sweet popcorn here, and that salty crap costs alot.

    3) No such thing as ZINC or Newbie here; clothes will run you anywhere from $50-$110 from a nice boutique e.g. Tarocash. Of course you could buy your clothes from KMart or target.

    4) New release games here cost $100 - by comparison in Singapore, it's half that.

    5) Clubbing here for men - door entry $10-20, unless you have friends here, then often it's waived, drinks will cost $10 for a good bottle of beer unless out of the tap, but I don't drink crap.

    Anymore questions shoot.

    Cost of living in Melbourne is much higher, but nobody I know has ever told me they prefer life in SG; I know alot of people who say they miss SG, but that's on a totally different level.

    The above prices are all in AUD, and the rate stands at about 1AUD=1.25SGD (give an take a few cents for fluctuation).
