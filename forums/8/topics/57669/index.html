    SEPT 22, 2003
    Network for social service volunteers

    VOLUNTEERS who work with social service agencies can now join a network to make friends and share resources.

    In the past, they were were simply recruited and despatched to welfare organisations, with hardly any opportunity to meet up except on an ad hoc basis.

    The network, called Volunteer Circle, is administered by the National Council of Social Service (NCSS). Its programmes include camps and tea sessions for contact-building and training sessions.

    Volunteers may also be asked to help out at other welfare organisations' events.

    Said Volunteer Circle vice-chairman Soh Chee Keong: 'They need a platform to talk about their experiences and to encourage each other. Sometimes, they get disheartened and need encouragement.'

    He added that the group will also be head-hunting those who are natural leaders and who can conduct training sessions, or who are capable of recruiting volunteers for welfare organisations which have not had volunteers helping out before.

    Unlike the National Volunteer and Philanthropy Centre, the umbrella organisation for all volunteers here, including those in areas like the arts and sports, the new group is meant only for those in social services.

    Currently, it has about 200 members, most of whom are regular volunteers. They paid a one-time fee of $5 to join.

    According to the NCSS, there are more than 200,000 people here volunteering with welfare organisations. This number includes one-time helpers.

    The Volunteer Circle membership card may give them discounts of up to 40 per cent for admission to places like the Jurong BirdPark and Singapore Zoo. Specifics are still being worked out.

    Said Mr Soh: 'When the member volunteers organise group outings for, say, the disabled or elderly, the whole group will get the discount.'
