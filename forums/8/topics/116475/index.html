    Originally posted by icebaby0:
    What are the "MUST" places to visit?
    Hi,

    Its always easier to answer if you state your interests, etc. Generally, the areas are:

    Shibuya - for shopping.
    Jimbocho - for books.
    Harajuku - for young weird fashion and such (best on Sundays).
    Ginza - for very high end and expensive stuff.
    Ikebukoro - for drinking at night.
    Akihabara - for the newest electronic products.
    Roppongi - for partying with foreigners.
    Shinjuku/Kabuki-cho - for red-light district and lots of cheap food.
    Asakusa - for "cheap" shoes, big temples, etc (actually, not very cheap).
    Chiyoda/Asakasa - if you're interested in government offices and palaces (?).
    etc
    etc

    You will probably have to stop by Shinjuku and Shibuya, no matter what...becuase these are the two major train interchanges in Tokyo. So might as well get off, walk around and see something there.

    If its your first time in Japan....NO MATTER WHAT, DO NOT TAKE A TAXI. Or risk your whole trip budget blown on a single taxi ride.

    If you don't have airport transfer, to/from Narita to/from Tokyo itself, take the train or TCAT (Tokyo City Air Terminal) bus.

    Lastly, Tokyo may not always be a friendly place for foreigners (language and arrogance problems)....so you may have to learn ways to entertain yourself.
