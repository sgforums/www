    Temasek petition removed
    Host insists author provides full valid name. By Seah Chiang Nee
    Jul 24, 2005

    The petition demanding Temasek Holdings to open up its books was removed because "the author did not provide a full valid name", according to a notice.

    The host has given the author three days to do so before permanently removing it. (http://www.petitiononline.com/sg090865/petition.html)

    When readers log into it, they no longer could see the results of the petitions. Instead, it carries a notice, which explains that "anonymous petitions are not permitted."

    Before its removal, the petition had been running for as few days and had drawn several hundred names, mostly anonymous, nowhere near the 40,000 petitions received calling for NKF CEO Mr. T.T. Durai's resignation.

    The notice adds: "If you are the author of this petition, reply to the email we sent you with your full name.

    "If you are the author and do not have the email we sent you, send an email to support@petitiononline.com asking us to reactivate your petition. Be sure to include your full name and petition ID in the email."

    The removed petition was under the headling: "Demand for Independent Audit of Accounts of GIC, Temasek Holdings, EDB & Finance Ministry" and was addressed to the Prime Minister of Singapore.

    It called for "audit by an independent commission that must include members of the Opposition Parties."
