    I believe you did not cycle your tank well. What is the water's parameters? Normally for a new tank, you need to have water in it for at least three weeks. Set the filters and air pumps running. Put in some hardy fishes (parrot fishes). These fishes will defecate and set the biological environment running. After about three weeks or a month, if the fishes are well and the water parameters are good, you can release your goldfishes in the tank.

    I guarantee you your fishes will be very happy as by then the beneficial bio is already in the tank.

    I am not too sure about the size of your fishes. There might also be a possibility of overcrowding. A 2ft tank is not the biggest tank around.

    Do not worry about your fishes not eating. It is very normal for new fishes not to eat for a few days even to a week.

    What you can do in future is to age your water. Like what someone suggested. Leave the water in a bucket overnight.

    Change the water in your tank once a week. And never change more than 1/2 of your tank's water at any time.

    And pls note that goldfishes are big eaters and poopers. Therefore, you need to remove their wastes quite regularly.
