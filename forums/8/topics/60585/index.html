    Hey, it's Mr Condom: Thai activist uses business and humour to push family planning

    Condoms, not chocolates or cookies. That's what you get with your after-dinner coffee at his restaurants and on your pillow at his resorts.

    He is Dr Mechai Viravaidya, the man whose concerted and creative efforts to educate Thais about birth control and safe sex dramatically cut his country's population growth rate - from 3.2 per cent in 1974 to 0.8 per cent last year - and slashed HIV/Aids infection rates by 75 per cent between 1990 and last year.

    In Thailand they call the 62-year old physician and former civil servant "Mr Condom". Dr Mechai and the Population and Community Development Association (PDA), the non-governmental organisation he set up in 1974, have worked hard to make the condom as ordinary and everyday a thing as the cabbage.

    They have, for example, organised condom-blowing competitions in factories and villages and had pretty girls distribute condoms to the Bangkok police in a "Cops and Rubbers" blitz.
