    Alright, time to clear the air up on the difference between "activation" and "registration". Please be sure to use the correct word depending on what you are referring to.

    There is a BIG difference between windows xp registration and windows xp activation.

    Registration can be done either over phone or over internet. Same as Activation. The similarities stop here.

    Registration is OPTIONAL. Activation is COMPULSORY, unless your copy of Win XP is provided on a so-called system restore CD from your computer manufacturer which you got free when you bought your comp, in which case it has already been activated, or unless your copy of Windows is bought under "volume licensing" agreement with microsoft (see the last paragraph).

    Registration process will ask you for personal info. Activation does not, you will only need your activation code which is somewhere with your Win XP packaging.

    If you don't register Win XP, your comp WILL NOT stop working after any period. If you don't activate, your comp WILL stop working after 30 days until you activate XP.

    Activation makes sure that one copy of windows XP will be permanently installed on only one computer in the world. The only people who do not need to activate Win XP are companies who have signed volume licensing agreements with microsoft. They get to install XP on multiple computers using a volume license product key that tells windows to skip product activation.
