Is productivity too overrated?

  • digimonfan2008
    27 Jun 14, 12:26

    Saw this slogan on the exterior of a bus:
    “We have found a way to cook our [products] 10 times faster, that’s productivity!”

    I saw this ad, I sighed and added that company to my list of banned food. Turns out, the so-call productive way is microwave. Those who don’t know, please google “microwave food causes cancer”. Those who thinks microwaved food is still healthy, please continue eating microwaved food, it’s your health, not mine.

    Faster isn’t always better. Let’s take poultry for example…

    Chickens have been fed with hormones and steroids so that they grow and mature quickly in 6 weeks. They have to be harvested (ie; slaughtered) at 6 weeks because if they grow to 8 weeks, they will die of heart attacks as the heart does not grow at the same speed as their bodies. No wonder, I’ve been seeing pink chicken flesh when I bite into fried chicken at K**!

    Same goes for cows and sheeps, so that they grow faster and produce more meat and milk. All in the name of PRODUCTIVITY! What happened to natural farming?

    Something closer to us, our workplace. I’ve worked in companies where 1 person was hired to take on 2 persons’ workload/tasks in the name of productivity. Such jobs are often labelled “fast-paced, dynamic environments” in job ads. Employer gained cost-savings but still lose money due to high employee turnover. Nobody will stay long in such jobs. Some ppl will take on such jobs due to long periods of unemployment, ran out of money or unaware, but nobody will stay long.

    Some productivity is good but when overdo it, it yearns adverse effects. Do you agree?
