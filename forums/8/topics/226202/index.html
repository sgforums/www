    No.1 and 2
    Basic Medical Degree in Singapore - 6 years
    Internship for full qualification - 1 year
    Specialist training - post 2 years after u qualify, add 5 years to train in specialty together with a Master of Medicine or a Doctor of Medicine.
    And you must attain fellowship of one of their medical societies in Australia/UK or USA . Oh ya , you need to keep publishing and publishing articles if you want to go far. Average 2 per year. One doctor I know publishes 6 articles in peer reviewed journals per year.

    No. 5
    Best University for Medicine in the World - John Hopkins University - you need a basic degree to study medicine in USA because they come under graduate scheme. It will also cost you more than quarter of a million dollars for tuition fees and textbooks. If you graduate from JHU - that is prestige . Easy to recoup the investment in JHU education

    I think for JHU - 4 years graduate scheme. Take the USMA or something which is their board examination and then do an internship for a year to gain full qualifications as an MD.

    Second Best is Harvard and Oxford. Third is Imperial College London.

    Sociology and Mass Communications - ehh .....not many universities rank around for that. Any university will do - like the ivy leagues or oxbridge universities.

    Australian system - I can tell you .
    If you have A levels -> Interview -> Accepted ->undergraduate medical school -> Graduate -> 2 years work ( 1 year for internship to qualify) -> choose specialty i.e. neurology -> 4-6 years training depending.

    If you don't have the required A levels or you went through polytechnic -> Basic Degree -> Take GAMSAT/MCAT ( MCAT only for international students) -> Score in 75th percentile -> Interview -> Accepted ->Graduate Medical School -> Graduate -> 2 years work (1 year for internship to qualify) -> choose specialty i.e. neurology -> 4-6 years training depending.

    You need to take exams further before you can become full fledged specialist. You also must become a fellow of the Royal Australasian College of (insert specialist field) which means you have to publish a lot of journal articles.

    Originally posted by littlefishtan!:
    So when i am like 25, i can graduate lah. oh so whats the starting pay and stuff?
    depends where you work. Starting Pay in Australia on average is around 52-56k basic without shift penalties. In Singapore , I don't know.

    Medicine is not all about the money. You have poor doctors , you have rich doctors. Just depends . I won't recommend that you do medicine just because you think it is a faster route to being a millionaire. You will burn out very fast or become very cold in the profession.

    Your insurance will eat up 1-2 months of your pay for professional indemnity (to protect against negligence claims) . When you hit specialist level , depending on how high risk your job is , some surgeons actually pay out an equivalent of 3 months worth of their salary for professional indemnity PER YEAR , plastic surgeons even more. Should you be sued , your indemnity premium increases.

    IF you are looking to earn lots of money - you might as well do finance and accountancy or actuarial science . Actuarial goes for 50k starting and will go up to 100-200k per year . Plus you just need to maintain your actuarial fellowship and you just make sure you don't f**k up the insurance premium calculations. Your maths have to be damn solid. Finance and Accountancy - I know a few who started with banks and moved up to 70k in their third year but very high risk job.
