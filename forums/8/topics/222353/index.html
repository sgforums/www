    when was the last time you visited Haw par villa, also know as, Tiger Balm gardens? it's free entry now and most of the statues are still ard...except for the slides that was introduced somewhere in from late 80's or early 90's...

    i remember going there once and tickets cost something like $16...there used to be a boat ride which takes you ard the 10 courts of hell...though the rides are no longer there, you can still visit the 10 courts of hell...the walk through haw par villa may be tiring due to the steep slopes, but it's very enlightening...you can find chinese folk classics like the Journey to the West, Bai se zuan, and more...

    also, there is Hua Song, a museum where you can view antique artefacts and learn more about chinese history...a chinese restaurant serving tim sum and chinese cuisine...but the price is relatively high since it's a restaurant...if you're looking for something that's cheaper, there's also a cafeteria selling hot dogs, toasts, and even zhi char...should try the hor fun, really very nice! the fried rice is also very tasty...at $5 per plate, it may cost more than your neighbourhood kopi tiam, but since it's a place of interest, i find it acceptable...
