    ST, April 14, 2006

    KUALA LUMPUR - MALAYSIA'S largest telecommunications company blamed a debt collection agency yesterday for a misplaced decimal point that resulted in a RM800 trillion (S$350 trillion) phone bill that was sent to a dead man.

    Government-linked Telekom Malaysia said the agency mistakenly sent out the notice demanding RM806,400,000,000,000.01 instead of the actual RM80.640000000000001. It threatened court action if the bill was not paid.

    'It is a mistake,' Telekom spokesman Mariam Bevi Batcha said. 'It's clearly not Telekom Malaysia's fault. We have given a stern warning to the debt collection agency.' She declined to name the agency.

    The dead man's son, Mr Yahaya Wahab, said earlier this week that he had disconnected his father's phone line in January and settled an RM84 bill. He nearly fainted when he saw the new bill, he said.

    Ms Mariam said the company was waiting for a full report from the agency. -- ASSOCIATED PRESS
