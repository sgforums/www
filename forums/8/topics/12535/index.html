    The offer of 6 months of zero brokerage to the customers of any remisiers who are trying to transfer to another stockbrokering house by UOB Kay Hian is unscrupulous for the following reasons:

    1. This is a bully tactic and a direct way to threaten any remisiers who wishes to transfer out of UOB Kay Hian to another stockbrokering house for any reasons. I firmly believe that the remisiers are not slaves, and they are not under any obligations to stay in any stockbrokering house, and as such, this tactic that is employed by UOB Kay Hian is not right.

    2. If the remisier were to lose most of his/her customers, then effectively, his/her career is destroyed. The agreement between the remisier and UOB Kay Hian is merely an agency agreement, with the remiser as the agent to the customers. The customers does not, in any legal fashion, belong to UOB Kay Hian. On the contrary, to my knowledge, the customers of the remisiers I know are mostly brought into UOB Kay Hian from another stockbrokering house, and they are mostly signed up by the remisers themselves. This fact puts more weight on the argument that the customers actually belong to the remisier. Furthermore, this offer of 6 months of zero brokerage as an effective deterrence to remisiers who are leaving a stockbrokering house is unheard of in the Singapore Stock exchange until the 24th October 2001.
