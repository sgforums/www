    As much as I have to admit that there are some of my teachers whom I actually dislike, I have to say that this pay hike for teachers is the most justifiable in the entire civil service. (compared to ministers and other sector pay hike)

    Teachers nowadays definitely face tougher challenges than what was faced ten years ago, this is from all aspects. Firstly are the students. Many students today are simply rude and disrespectful towards teachers. They give teachers an emotional test. Secondly is the demand of the work itself. Today, teachers have to do work planning and reflection, stay back for remedials even for lower primary students and sacrifice their personal time.

    From my own experience, my teachers when I was in Upper Secondary, their sacrifice for us is simply extraordinary. My Math and Physics teacher used to stay back until 6.30PM just to help us in case we have questions. My Language teachers gave us their phone numbers and did not hesitate to offer us help should we need one even during the weekends. They were always trying to help us while balancing their time with meetings, plannings and for most of them, their family. Though it is a 5 day work week, many are willing to come down on Saturdays just to help. Officialy, their work hours are 7.30-2.30, many are at school by 6.30 and leave at the very least, at 5.00. This is true even for Primary school teachers.

    A teacher's job isn't as simple as it was before. Parents today are "more difficult". The school management demands more from them. They make a lot of personal sacrifices. And don't forget about the CCAs. I say, if there is indeed any group of people who deserves a pay rise, they are the ones.

    I'm not an MOE teacher by the way.
