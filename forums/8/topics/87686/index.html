    Originally posted by the Bear:
    dude... it is random...

    which means the "consecutive winning numbers" are also random... the chances of it happening are the same as any number you choose..

    I slightly disagree. It does improve the chance of winning.

    We all know that a Toto combination is a finite series of numbers. From a mathematical viewpoint, that means that there is a finite set of possible combinations.

    Let's not talk about Toto and use a simpler example. If the proposed rule of non-repeating digit is used, given 10 digits, 0 to 9, in the first round, what is the chance of picking a drawn digit? The probability is 1/10.

    In the second round, since the digit selected in the 1st round is excluded bcos of the non-repeating rule, then there are only 9 valid choices. Hence the probability is increased to 1/9.

    In the third round, the same thing applies again and the probability is now 1/8.

    So the probability is (1/10 * 1/9 * 1/ = 1/720

    If repeating is allowed, then it'll always be (1/10 * 1/10 * 1/10) = 1/1000

    1/720 is higher than 1/1000 => Chance of striking is higher.
