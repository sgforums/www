    Desert Fox & Linda L, i did some 'research' on this B4, the Bear's answer of Greenland is 100% correct.

    beside the normal definition of 'surrounded by water/sea' for island - which applies to every land mass (be it a Continent or a island), the real difference is by size and definition,

    Therefore, if a land mass, be it Continents like Eurasia (Europe and Asia is physically 1 big Giant Continent [/b]with only imaginary border at the Ural mountains in Russia) or Australia or Africa, so long it is BIG enough to be defined as Continent, it is no longer officially considered as a real island

    so while some non-scientist/layman/common people call Australia 'island-Continent', However, since it is huge enough to be defined as one of the seven Continent, it isn't scientifically and officially defined as 'island' and defined as a 'Continent'.

    Hence Australia cannot displace Greenland's position as largest island even though it is about 3 times larger than Greenland. (Linda L, your niece is correct - Greenland is 1/3 the size of Australia!)
