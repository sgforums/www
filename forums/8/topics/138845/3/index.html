    Not far off the mark, one-niner, but just a couple of things:

    - I do believe that Omega's movements are not exclusively from Lemania - for example, the Seamaster's cal. 1120 movement is a reworked ETA 2892/A2
    - ETA movements are not the only ones with a 28.8 kbph beat - Rolex's in-house 313x movement, which powers most of their range, also runs at the same speed.

    I've just had a quick glance through, and while I've seen mention of PP and JLC, I'm quite surprised that nobody's mentioned Vacheron Constantin or A.Lange & Sohne yet.

    The dress watches that these houses tend to produce are a bit fragile for my liking, since I tend to be somewhat careless. For the moment, my Rolex Sea Dweller does the job just fine - it goes with anything from suits to cargo pants, and the 3135 movement, not to mention the case, is easily one of the toughest around. I've got a Datejust as well, but the gold parts scratch very easily, so I usually reserve it for occasions like weddings.

    TAGs and Omegas are admittedly nice watches, but within that cheaper-than-Rolex-but-more-expensive-than-Seiko range, IMHO brands such as Zenith and Oris represent better value for money. As a rule of thumb, COSC ratings are good to have, and sapphire crystals are an absolute must (unless you're going for a vintage piece).

    Back to the original point of this thread. Sure, you can browse watch sites such as TimeZone to check out watches, but nothing, I mean nothing, can substitute for actually trying them on. Case in point: watches like Panerais, Breitlings and some Omegas (e.g. Planet Ocean) all look great in photos, but it's not until you strap one of those bad boys on your wrist that you'll see if it's too big for your wrist, or if it's too bulky to wear with your long-sleeved shirts. Do your initial research on the net, but at some point head down to the shop and try it on.

    Anyway, here's a shot of my daily wearer. It's no Patek, but hell, it's tough and goes with absolutely anything I choose to wear.
