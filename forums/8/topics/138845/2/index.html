    I own a Tag Classic 2000 series,a simple yet excellent watch.Imo a steal at it's retail price of $1100.I purchased it around 4 years ago,so I'm not too sure whether it's still available.

    Those interested in getting one,get an export set.It's an original imported by a local retailer from a 3rd world country(Vietnam etc) where the watch makers price their watches lower as these countries have lower purchasing power.Such parallel imports are prohibited by the watch makers and penalties are imposed on local agents in these poor countries if they are caught.

    So they discreetly export it out but each watch has it's own serial number.My Tag originated from Russia with a Russsian dealer's stamp on my int'l warranty.I bought it in S'pore at 30% off retail price.

    The shop I purchased it from displays local sets but will sell u export sets if you go thru a recommendation by a person who knows them.Local agents actually do go around conducting "undercover" checks and report these shops to the pricipal in Switz or wherever to take action.

    So when I sent in my Tag for servicing,they asked me where had purchased it from and I said it was a gift from a friend who visited Russia.They cant do anything and have to servie it as it's an int'l warranty.

    For those interested in getting a luxury watch,ask around if anyone you know has access to export sets.

    For starters, the Tourbillion was invented 200 odd yrs back by A.L Breguet. The main problems with mechnical watches was the effects of gravity affecting the accuracy of the movement in the vertical and horizontal planes meaning when the watch is not always lying flat on the table but being worn in a position ever changing.

    So in an effort to maintain accuracy along these planes, Bregeut invented the tourbillion,where the escapement movement actually sits in this little cage called the tourbillion cage and its tuned to rotate once every 3 mins ( not very sure of the time diff) to cancel out the effects.the invention was patented and not very well used in watches due to the craftsmanship invovled in shaping the small cage and placing the entire escapment on the cage. therefore its almost the peak of a master watch maker to be able to create a tourbillion movement.

    Back in 2003, Frank Muller stunned the world by releasing the Revolution 2 during the 2003 Basel Fair in Swiss.It was the 21st century tourbillion with 2 rotating axis both on the vertical and horizontal axis.

    But since then ..almost every watch manfacturer claims a tourbillion watch as their crown jewels. Well for many of these companies, it certainly is a new age in horology, but many ppl in the industries are pretty sore at the piece of the tourbillion pie fasico.
