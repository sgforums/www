    Parka, in response to ur question, incorporated companies do save.

    Typically under normal circumstances, an audited company should have at least 2 reserve accounts.

    The first one is the Capital Reserve. This one is meant solely for capital expenditures only. Cannot anyhow take out one.

    The second one is known as the Revenue Reserve. This one is ususually the 'purse' of any organization. Money can be taken out for varieties of purpose, so long as the required consensus is there.

    Alternatatively of course, companies would also typically have other reserves in place. But in common practice, we typically see the above two only.

    In the context of SIA, althought I am not too sure about how they organize their funds, but from their tactical policy of purchasing new planes regulary after every few years, I would reckon that a massive amount of funds would probably be laid trapped under their Capital Reserve.

    The restriction in the wothdrawal of such would probably hence contribute as another factor that is suffocating on their normal operations under the current extraordinary stressful business environment.
