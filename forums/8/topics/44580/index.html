    I think for fresh Poly Grads with no experience, ask for an reasonable amount, probably 1.5k -1.6k. Some companies do not like prospective interviewee to lower down their salaries, as they know they you will quit the job if found a job that pays what a poly should get. Another reason is that show no or low confidence of yourself.

    Most importantly is to write a good cover letter and resume to "sell" yourself, according to the job requirements stated in the advertisement. If you done a good job in this, next battle will be interview. Dress appropriately according to the job that you are going to be interviewed. Do some research on the company, products and what probably your job will be. Don't dress shabbily for a sales position ot dress like a "doll" for a technical position. Display confidence in your interview and ask or reply approriate questions and answers. Thanks the interviewer in the end for giving you the opportunity. You may also want to ask how long will you get a reply. Send a thank you letter to the employer irregardless you get the job, probably after the interview...
    The rest will depends on yout luck and heaven....

    Just my personal opinion.
