    Here goes the solutions...
    using 1st law of thermodynamics, since assuming no heat lost this implies internal energy can only change via work done.
    Many of u think along this line....I really lurve u ppl for being so smart.
    Now as heat supplied is equal, expansion is the same since the the tension and the reaction force by the floor are equal(use newton 3rd law).
    Now the tricky part is here.....as the floor sphere expand,it does work against gravity as its centre of mass rises.
    For the string's sphere, it does do equal work against the string but its centre of mass DROP as it expands, hence the loss of gravitational potential energy COMPENSATE the work done against the string. Hence, the one on string loses no energy but the one on floor loses energy as it work against gravity to expand which causes a corresponding in its centre of mass...

    Darkness is right to relate gravity with internal energy....
