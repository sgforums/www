    Originally posted by coffeeortea:
    haha.. good luck then.. the cost of living there is higher than in Singapore =D
    Ok i haven't worked in the UK before because i am currently in Univeristy here.
    And yes, the cost of living is higher.But, imo, not that much higher, and i think for the lifestyle i am after, savings potential is greater in the UK.

    Rent is about S$600/month outside of london.
    Groceries will set you back about S$60/wk, though my fren seems to get by on 45/wk. Tv Licence is about S$360/yr. Gas + Electricity +water will set you back about S$50/month.

    You also have to pay income tax and National Insurance. I am not exactly sure how much is due for National Insurance, but Income Tax isn't really that high. First S$15000 is tax free. Next S$90000 is taxed at about 22%. Any amt thereafter will be taxed 40%. I have been told that at an annual salary of S$84000/yr, your take home pay will be about S$5400/month.

    Graduates on average earn about S$54000/yr though you can expect more if you work in London.

    Note though, that it is difficult to get a job in the UK because of immigration controls. Some, like nurses obviously have an easier time than say Lawyers.
