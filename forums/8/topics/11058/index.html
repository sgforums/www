    Moose - ah... a fighter of words eh.

    Knowing the disgruntled manner in which many NSFs perform their NS, I suggest you should use the 'contribution' line with caution. At any rate, we all know what happens during NS. NSFs (even reg) 'keng' and 'siam' whenever they can. Paying them a few hundred dollars more won't change that. Agree?

    In addition, under what framework do you rate your military contributions? How does that translate to a justification for a raise of a few hundred dollars? Don't you people like to call SAF "S'pore Acting Force"? If that is the case, isn't your contributions a bit on the deficient side?

    You mentioned a increase of a few hundred dollars would be insignificant since the SAF already pays so much on your upkeep. Lets just do some simple mathematics: $200 increment/NSFmonth X 12 months X 75,000 NSF = $180 million/year. With this kind of quantum, I hope you will change your mind on the 'insignificant'ness of the increment of a few hundred dollars. Furthermore, are you happy with just $200 more?

    You mentioned recruits getting $240/month. Did you know that during my time it was below $200? I past through that period with lots to spare and without taking a single cent from my parents. And now, I believe upon POP, you get $280/month. Talking about subsidies for tertiary students, I'm sure you'd agree that you actually have more living expenses to settle then. You still pay adult fare, food and clothings don't come any cheaper, etc. So, where does the stretching of money come in?

    When you are in NS, you are given a special status of NSF. You are considered gainfully employed but not in the sense of doing a regular day job. In that case, you are not getting paid. Your allowance is not CPF payable. It is an allowance, so please do not compare it so! And for the NSFs who choose to book out everyday - its their choice! They didn't opt to stay in. If they did, I'm sure they will be given the same food and lodging.

    If you really want to calculate effective pay, lets do it:

    Allowance: $300 - $500
    lodging: $300 (consider renting a HDB room)
    food: $360 ($4 X 3 X 30)
    Total: $960 - $1160

    As you can see it is roughly about what you can get with an 'A' level or Poly cert.

    Coming to the food, I must say that the outside hawkers do not have to contend with the logistical nightmare of feeding a few thousand people three times a day everyday. As NSFs, the amount of food required is also incredibly high (we all know how much we can eat). So your $5 includes overheads and the extra portions of food you will not get outside. It is regrettable that the food does not compare with outside hawker food. My basis of comparison with China farmers is that we Singaporeans are way too pampered with good food! Not that we should all go eat like those poor people, but at least when we get slightly lower-quality food here, we are able to accept it with grace. Whatever happened to the soldier's ability to endure hardships?

    Houses - why do we pay through our noses? Most probably because we do not match the house with our earning capabilities. So many people really want to 'live it up' in big houses and own cars. With those kind of expectations, I won't be surprised that people are paying through their noses. With that, how can we blame anyone else but our ownselves? We are not walking with 2 feet firmly planted on the ground.

    You said you wanted the govt to take care of you - So does that mean you won't mind paying 30-40% more income tax *NOW*? Bear in mind that those extra taxes won't translate into cheaper houses, cars and life-style expenses for you *NOW*.

    As to whats wrong with taking care of the elderly? Idealistically - Nothing. But if you go to the extent of paying full pensions, medical fees and whatnots, you are sure to get a lot of people who abuse the system. That is why we will never go into this kind of system. Public welfare drags the country down, and eventually everyone with it. You learn about this kind of things in public finance. The western countries are moving away from this system now because they now know its shortcoming; S'pore has no reason to go the other way!

    Finally, your argument on how societies advance baffles me. Your argument seemed to bank on the fact that societies advance because people get paid more. That is absurd! People get paid more because they have progressed significantly with their skills and knowhow! And if you read through your posting, you have failed to justify why NSFs should get a few hundred dollars more. Your agruments just seemed to say:"hey, I'm getting too little, pay me more." In life, there is no free lunch; unless you are worth that much, you get only paid this much.

    Thank you.
