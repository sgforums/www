No doubt $180 million is a small % of our GDP, but to spend such amounts just on one economically unproductive field warrants serious consideration. Supposed you do get the increment, the total sum spent on NSF's allowances alone will hit $600-$700 million. Doesn't it occur to you that such sums are astronomical? Ok, so Singapore is very rich now, but that does not warrant spending its surpluses in ways that benefit a small portion of the population in the short-term.

    Hardships do not apply to food. Really? Then what does? If our soldiers lose morale so easily over SFI food, then I say our soldiers have gone too soft.

    About the houses - it is a fact that our public housing compares very favourably to other major cities. But at any rate, I don't see how a middle-class family can be struggling to buy a 4 or 5-room flat unless they are financing a whole slew of other expenses such as club memberships, cars and luxury items.     And as to what is wrong with asking the govt to take care of the elderly - In the perfect world, nothing is wrong. In the real world, we know the system will breed overdependence on the part of the people. It will result in abuse of the systems of which no checks have ever being effective enough. I urge you to go study the welfare systems in the Western country to find out what exactly is wrong. I myself did a research project on this area, tying the Western experience to the local context.
