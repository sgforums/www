    I think we can't do much for those who is already caught in this current situation.

    What I find worrying is that there are a lot of Singaporeans who are headed in that direction. Is there anyway we can prevent this vicious cycle is the question.

    I have seen quite a number of people around me who are just making ends meet. No savings, no insurance etc and they are paying for their bills and children. They are still young now but what happens when they are older and fall sick? Where is the money coming from? As well, due to daily necessities alot of items are bought via monthly installments. They won't feel it now, but once they hit their 40s.....we see another group of people affected.

    I guess the key now is to stay relevant but it is really a chicken and egg problem - You need money to stay relevant. Another way is to change the mindset that a lot of Singaporeans have - Ask what you can do for the company and not what the company can do for you. If one can do that, the rewards will come naturally.

    To me, we are all in our comfort zone and having FT here is taking away that feeling because alot of the FT who come in, come in hungry and will work for a lower pay and pick up more duties. I'm not talking about highly paid caucasians here but skilled professionals from India, China, Malaysia etc. As locals we will have to further prove to our companies that we are worth the higher pay they are paying us for our work, and not paid higher because we are locals. The world is getting smaller and competition fiercier. We really don't have that luxury anymore.

    From what I see, things will only get tougher, not easier. Guess we all have to brace up for the rough ride
