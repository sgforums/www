    Anyone care to share your experience of observing the total lunar eclipse on last Sunday July 16th?

    This is one of the longest duration of total Lunar Eclipse since 1859. To be actual, 1 hour and 47 minutes of totality. This eclipse is especially noteworthy because the Moon passes almost dead center through the Earth's shadow as shown in the images below.



    What I saw was a reddish moon during totality with bright constast between the "seas" and the surface of the moon.
    When the reddish moon beginning to emerge from the shadow of the earth, a cresent waxing moon slowing appears like a rusty diamond giving off it's first spark of light. Wow cool man! The view through the telescope and bino was breath-taking!

    Anyone care to share your experience???
    Anyone gone down to Science Centre Observatory?
