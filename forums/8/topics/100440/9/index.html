    Nov 2, 2004
    Huang Na's stepdad arrives at last
    By Tanya Fong

    HUANG Na's stepfather, Mr Zheng Wen Hai, 28, finally arrived in Singapore on Monday night after a long wait for his visa.

    He was greeted at the airport by his wife, Madam Huang Shuying, 27, at about 8pm. The couple broke down when they met. It was the first time they had seen each other since Madam Huang rushed back from Fujian, China to look for her missing daughter.

    Mr Zheng told reporters he hopes to stay in Singapore until the trial of the man charged with her murder, Took Leng How, 22, is over.

    'I still can't understand why anyone would do such a thing to her. She's an innocent child. I hope I can stay here until the trial is over. We need to know why this happened,' he said.

    Took, who disappeared shortly after Huang Na went missing, surrendered to police on Saturday and was charged in court on Monday.

    He is a former colleague and flatmate of Madam Huang.

    Madam Huang said on Monday she will not have a wake for her daughter. Her remains will be cremated and taken to China.

    Huang Na went missing on Oct 10. Decomposed remains believed to be hers were found in a box at the bottom of a hill in Telok Blangah Hill Park on Sunday.

    For full report, see Wednesday's edition of The Straits Times.
