    SINGAPORE: 22-year-old Took Leng How, wanted by Singapore Police in connection with the case of missing China girl Huang Na, has surrendered to the Malaysian Police.

    Took, a Malaysian, gave himself up in Penang at about 1.30pm Saturday, four days after Singapore Police issued a warrant for his arrest for abduction.

    Singapore Police say Took will be brought back to Singapore on Saturday night but did not give further details.

    Took fled Singapore after being called in by Singapore Police for questioning in relation to the case.

    Huang Na has been missing for nearly three weeks.

    She was last seen on 10 October in the company of Took at the Pasir Panjang Wholesale Centre in Singapore.

    Took worked as a vegetable packer at the Pasir Panjang Wholesale Centre and was also a former co-worker of Huang Na's mother, Huang Shuying.

    Huang Na's disappearance sparked an islandwide search as volunteers hit the streets hoping to find the 8-year-old girl.

    In fact, the search went beyond Singapore's shores to Malaysia.

    Took was last questioned by Singapore Police on 19 October.

    The Singapore Police have kept his passport.

    He was supposed to report for questioning again but failed to turn up.

    That's when reports surfaced that he had fled to Malaysia and finally back to Penang where his family lived. - CNA
