    SINGAPORE : It is possible that the search for missing eight-year-old girl Huang Na has come to an end.

    Police say they have combed Telok Blangah Hill Park on Sunday morning and found a sealed box.

    The brown paper box has been brought to the morgue.

    But police say they still cannot confirm if it is actually the body of the missing girl.

    They added they have also established that there is physical evidence that the Chinese student was attacked in a storeroom at Pasir Panjang Wholesale Centre.

    A police spokesperson also said the arrest of 22-year-old Malaysian Took Leng How on Saturday night helped to narrow down the search to Telok Blangah Hill Park.

    Took turned himself in to Malaysian police on Saturday and was brought back to Singapore for questioning.

    Took, who was last seen with the girl at the Pasir Panjang Wholesale Centre, worked as a vegetable packer at the Centre, and was a former co-worker of Huang Na's mother, Huang Shuying.

    The girl has been missing since October 10.

    Took is a prime suspect in the case. If found guilty of murder, Took could be charged in court on Monday. - CNA
