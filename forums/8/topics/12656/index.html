    It's pretty common. It's a condition where the valve that guards the junction between the left atrium and ventricle of the heart becomes faulty. Normally, when the left ventricle contracts, blood flows out through the aorta to other parts of the body. During this time, the mitral valve closes and prevents backflow of blood from the left ventricle into the left atrium. In mitral valve prolapse, backflow is able to occur, so that output of blood from the heart may be compromised. In addition, turbulent flow of the blood as a result results in heart murmurs.

    Mitral valve prolapse at a young age is usually inherited. But not to worry, an artificial valve can be used to replace the faulty valve in the heart through an operation, and results are fantastic.
