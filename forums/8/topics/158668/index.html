    Originally posted by sinicker:
    i am 18yrs old. when i study, say for 1.5hrs, my back hurts.. i've done standing up and stretching.. putting a cushion on the back. what shud i do? A lvls in 18 days.
    Is there any pain on bending down with your knees straight? If there is, it is most likely a slipped disc (this often happens in between 20-30 years old). This is self limiting and 90% of people suffering from this illness will be fine after 1-2 weeks of rest and analgesics.

    If the pain persist, it may be a postural problem. Change your posture according to your working environment. Sit up straight on a chair and don't hunch forwards or lift up your legs and put it on the table. Stretching and standing up for a few minutes will help. Don't put a cushion, as it is soft and allows you to give up the straight position you will maintain hence deviating your attention to a relaxing position.

    If it goes away with activity and comes with rest, this condition is probably inflammatory and you would like to access for symptoms of blurring of vision, shortness of breath, pain over the heels and pain over the buttocks.
