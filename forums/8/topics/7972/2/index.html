    Well, alphabetically, chicken comes before egg.

    Firstly, the question is flawed. You must first understand that there must be at least 2 chickens of different genders or 2 eggs which will eventually turn to chickens of different genders. So now the problem becomes more complicated....

    Let C be chicken and E be egg.

    Now, say we start with 2 for illustration. We can have 2Cs, 2Es, or 1E & 1C.

    If we have 3, then we can have 3Cs, 3Es, 2Cs & 1E, or 1C & 2Es.

    If we have 4, then we can have 4Cs, 4Es, 3Cs & 1E, 2Cs & 2Es, or 1C & 3Es.

    As you can see, the problem goes a long way. But one thing is certain, we have to have at least 2.

    Now, the most probable answer would be they appear both at the same time. The reason is that, by taking the average, you get xCs & yEs where x and y are between 2 and infinity. This is assuming that the probability that each combinations occuring is the same.

    So, to answer that question, I would say they both exist at the same time.
