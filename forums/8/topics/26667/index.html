    First things first, I prefer the term 'companian animals' as opposed to pets, which I find rather demeaning. Reason being that studies have shown that the company of animals actually reduces stress, which in turn reduces the chance of heart attacks, strokes, high blood pressure and what not, whilst the animals gain no real benefit as we were the ones who domesticated them in the first place for our own needs, and all of their physiology is geared towards survival in the 'wild' (of which sadly little is left).

    In fact, they tend to suffer under the process of 'selective breeding', which enhances certain handicaps, such as the pushed-in (brachycephalic) face of the bulldog and Persian cat, that adversely affects their health, thus making them unsuitable to survive with the lack of human ownership.

    As such, I advocate the keeping of 'mongrel' cats and dogs, who display more hybrid vigour and can be readily obtained from SPCA (who would otherwise euthanize perfectly healthy animals simply due to space/time/money constraints). If you love animals enough, you wouldn't support industries that exploit them.
