    Dear cyclists.

    TriFam is organizing the annual bike event on 1st January 2007 (Public Holiday) in the name of Sylvester Ang who passed away on Christmas Eve 2004. This is a mark of respect and remembrance to our dear member whose love for sports and especially cycling, knew no boundaries. It is also to create awareness for drivers to be patient and keep the road safe for cyclists on the road.

    Sylvester was a passionate and committed individual. Those who knew him personally would tell you that he never once missed his riding sessions with any of his different groups of sports enthusiasts and friends. With the same drive, he fought hard for his life and inspired many. His fighting spirit serves as a reminder that we should never give up on what needs to be done for the greater good of the cycling community i.e. to continue to ride safely and increase awareness on road safety.

    If you would like to honour the memory of someone who shared the same passion, join us for a special ride to remember our friend, Sylvester Ang. Your support for this event will help in many ways. The bigger the group the greater the awareness.

    Thank you and we look forward to see you there.

    Team TriFam
    www.triathlonfamily.com

    Date/time: 01 Jan 2007, 0700hrs

    Guest of Honour: Mr Ong Kian Min

    Meeting place: Dempsey Road carpark

    Route:
    Dempsey Road -> Napier Road ->(straight) Grange Road (turn right) Irwell Bank Road -> keep left on River Valley Road > Left on Hill Street -> Left > on Stamford Road -> Orchard Rd -> Penang Rd-> Somerset Rd -> Right on Grange Rd -> Napier Rd -> Dempsey Rd.

    IMPORTANT:
    1. Helmets are COMPULSORY.
    2. There will be 2 CISCO outriders leading the ride and controlling the traffic, riders are not allowed to overtake them. THIS IS A CONTROLLED PACE RIDE.
    3. Those with aerobars, will have to stay at the back of the pack.
    4. The minimum age for the riders must be at least 18 years and above.
    5. Riders will be given a bib to wear to help spread the message of safe cycling. Riders may bring their own race belts to put it on or pin it on the jersey.

    Supported by: Safe Cycling Task Force, Team FOSA/Geylang, SACA, Triathlon Family Singapore
