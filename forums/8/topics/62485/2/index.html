    Once got this winner Clayton Alderfer came up with the ERG model, where ERG stands for Existence, Relatedness and Growth in this order. This represents the order of what people are looking for in a job, from least important to most important. Existence refers to the basic things that keeps a person in existence, eg food, water, shelter, etc. Relatedness refers to how a person relates to the people around him/her. Growth refers to the person's own personal growth, either mental, character, etc.

    Then got another champion called Abraham Maslow also made his own model, and called it the Maslow's Needs Heirarchy. People first need to fulfil their basic physiological needs, meaning they need money to eat, live, etc. Second, they must have a sense of personal safety. Thirldy they want a sense of belonging. Fourthly esteem of others, what others think of you. Fifth, own self-exteem, and lastly self-actualisation.

    Psychologist David McClelland then identified the 3 most impt secondary needs. Need for Achievement, Need for Affiliation, and Need for Power. Need for affliliation refers to a desire to seek approval from others, conform to their wishes and expectations and avoid conflict and confrontation.
