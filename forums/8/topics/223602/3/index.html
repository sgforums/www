    Second, even if people think you are funny, they might not appreciate your sense of humour. Lots of people laugh at my jokes - without truly understanding WHY I am saying it sometimes. They just think I am eccentric. -shrug-

    It isn't always easy to find someone who have the same sense of humour as you. When you do, its almost like singing a jazz duet, the improvisations on both parts driving the melody to create something so original that the laughter is secondary - and you always depart from that person feeling a sense of gratification. Contrary to what you said, some of my most memorable moments of laughter have been with the wittiest funniest girl friends. And yeah, they are Singaporeans.

    And yet.. while some may enjoy the oscar wilde type of sarcarstic witticism, cutting irony - I don't think I can live with someone who is constantly working on the quality of humour - but rather, someone who has the sense of humour to find the funny, cherished little things in the mundane life both have to lead together - I guess that's what is important about it all.
