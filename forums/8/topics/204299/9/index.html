    I'm still not sure what the big hooha is about, and I find the arguments of double standards quite weak and unlikely to stand in a court of law.

    Is proposing in uniform unheard of? Most militaries have a tradition of that, in fact they often go out of their way for such events. Is a kiss an acceptable part of a proposal? Certainly.

    Is kissing in uniform unheard of? Not unless we want to chase down all those soldiers who kissed their loved ones after coming back from Iraq or East Timor. No, the restriction given out was a more general restriction on being publicly amorous which ought to be applied with common sense. In the same way it's unacceptable to play with the officer sword but acceptable to use it in parade. Both actions involve holding the sword but it would be illogical to deem all acts illegial by virtue of holding the sword. There are kisses, but different kisses in different situtations mean different things.

    Those who think that all kissing is unacceptable in uniform probably didn't sit down to think a little bit about the law. It's about as ridiculus as a thread I read somewhere back saying that you cannot defend yourself from attack because you would be breaking the law by causing hurt to somebody... which had anybody read the law at all would know it's really another thing.

    If it was a Cpl and not a Cpt who proposed would he be fried? I do not think so but then again it depends on his unit. Which brings us back to my first point... if the unit is willing it can, and ought to happen. Certainly being a Cpt gives you more pulling power, and that is the way of things. But injustice? That's pulling it a little.

    And thirdly, if I myself could have done it... I would have gone for it. Frankly I don't think the Cpt bothered if 1, or 55,000 people saw it, and people who took a saner view of him might realize that. The guy is in love, and that's a greater motivation as opposed to attention-seeking (and that is very unlikely).

    And no I don't think it's shameless... it's only shameful if you do something wrong. I don't think it was wrong.
