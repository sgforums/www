    Increase in I = Increase in A
    Those bright regions wil be even brighter as the resultant A increase, implies that intensity also increases.
    But at dark region, resultant of A will be the same which is 0. Its intensity will have no change.
    And, A does not have any effect on fringer seperation since it is independent on A.
    Oh yes, this is under the assumption that the distance between the slit and the screen is still the same.
    If the distance is different. Then it would be a different case all over.
    Explanation is that since the distance between the slit and the distance is further, the waves that are traveling will lose energy to the surrounding. Amplitude decrease, implying that resultant amplitude decreases. Thus intensity would decrease due to the distance of the screen and slit being further away.
