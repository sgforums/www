    SINGAPORE : More than a thousand people complained last year to the Consumers Association (CASE) about receiving unsolicited sales calls from companies.

    Telemarketers used hard sell tactics to promote their products and irate customers do not understand how these companies obtained their personal particulars.

    CASE says it received 1,032 complaints last year about nuisance calls from telemarketers.

    From January to April this year, it has received around a hundred.

    The usual tactic: consumers say they are told they have won a prize, and then the hard sell starts as callers push their products.

    Many do not understand how callers get their details.

    "Now there are a lot of listings being circulated, with names and mobile number. I supposed they must have bought it over the Internet or other sources and then made the calls," one person speculated.

    "I've got friends who actually work in there kind of places. They say you can actually buy (the details) from events or something," another said.

    One person who received a call from a telemarketer said, "They told me I could get a prize but when I went down, it wasn't the case. So I won't trust them again."

    Channel NewsAsia's news team contacted one such company, which claimed it obtained the information from other companies that held lucky draws; consumers had put in their contact details on coupons.

    Most consumers think such practices should be stopped.

    "This is a business ethics issue. You shouldn't pass on or sell such information to others," said one consumer.

    "Unless a law is passed to prevent such breaches in privacy, otherwise it would be difficult to fix the problem; it's so common," said a second person.

    CASE legal executive Tan Jeh Yaw explained, "There is no specific law at this present moment which restricts such personal information from being given to another unauthorised person."

    So all CASE can do is to give a verbal warning to such companies, and advise consumers not to put too many personal details on lucky draw coupons. - CNA
