    Oxford Dictionary of Psychology

    Cognitive Behavioural Modifications:

    A form of therapy based on cognitive therapy and behavioural modifications, in which the client or patient learns to replace dysfunctional self speech (such as I knew I'd never be able to cope with this job) with adaptive alternatives. (The job's not going well, but I am capable of working out a plan to overcome the problems). It's application include anger control, stress management, coping with anxiety, and developing social skills. Also called cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT).
