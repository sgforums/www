    Acne is caused by the hormonal changes in your body throughout adolescence but sometimes it goes on much for a bit longer and some even have acne until they are 25!

    I have suffered from it for the longest time. I think I was 10 when I first got acne, and I still do (although it is not as bad now).

    Some advice:
    1. Wash your face regularly with a gentle cleanser. Do not get a really strong soap and wash it once a day. It is even worse to get a strong soap and wash your face all the time. It will dry your face up and cause skin irritation (redness). Note that after a while you will have to change soaps as they will become ineffective after a while. It is not that you are beyond help when a soap ceases to work, but rather you face has gotten used to it. I recommend either Neutrogena, proactiv or something along that line.

    2. Do not touch your face!!! I know it is hard but everytime you do, you are spreading bacteria and you will see new pimples coming up where you touched very soon.

    3. Use blotters when you can. I know it is a bit sissy but who cares. Go to the toilet and blot lor. It is just to keep excessive oil off your face. It does wonders.

    4. Try to cut down on oily food. This is subjective. Try it out and see if it helps. It does for me...

    5. Wash your face after exercising, ASAP!!! Do not let bacteria build up...

    If you are desperate and have a lot of $$$, try Roaccutane. I tried it about 7 years ago and it was about $500 for a treatment period of 4 months. It closes your oil glands and actually limits the amount of oil secreted on your face. Prepare to have chapped and bleeding lips and dry skin.
    When I took it, it worked like a charm but after I finished the treatment, all the pimples came back again. I was then told that I would have to take another 4 month course of treatment. IT MAY WORK FOR YOU THOUGH...

    Taking antibiotics and applying cream is the most effective for me and it is not very expensive. I am sure that you would rather spend a bit of money to save your face than look like a crater-face in the future, right? Dun say vain lah, but if it is really very bad, do something about it.

    Take care man...
