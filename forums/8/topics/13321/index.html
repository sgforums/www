Life of a pupa

  • Illegal Operation
    26 Jan 02, 21:31
    For the first time today, I travelled to one of the newest HDB estate in Singapore. Through the years, efforts were made to emulate the looks of a condominium. At its best, this is a leap towards higher standard of living, or even at its least, it satisfy the yearning of commoners, that is, one of the 5Cs.

    So life is pretty clear cut isn't it? Born, study, strive to squeeze into one of Singapore's university, (your interest notwithstanding, since its the prestige of your course that matters the most), then work(once again, interest notwithstanding. If the industry needs an engineer then that is what you become).

    Then marry. If you do well enough, buy a condo. Or else, there's always a condo-lookalike at Punggol Estate. Next you have kids. And your kids will go through the same cycle again. And so will his kids.

    18 year old poet Teng Qian Xi describe Singaporeans as one who possess the migrant mentality. That is when security and ownership overrules all. But then what fun is it to live a life if the excitement of not knowing what you are going to get is taken away and a life story pre-programmed takes its place?

    Put it this way. Will you play a match with passion if you were playing against kids half your size and you knew you will win? Or will you prefer to pit against your equal? The latter obviously because half the fun from playing the game comes from not knowing its result! Then why let the cars and the cash and the material wealth bog you down when life is suppose to be passionate?

    Security and ownership were only half the things on the migrants' minds when they settled in Singapore. The other half was the yearning to lead a better life both for themselves and their decendents. It is a shame if the decendents now chose to live the life of a pupa.
