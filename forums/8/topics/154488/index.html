    Squatter-campers have to leave Changi Beach Park by tomorrow

    By Melissa Sim
    FOR odd-job worker Danny, home is three tents pitched on Changi Beach.

    The yellow one is his living room, the blue his bedroom, and the red functions as a kitchen-cum-storeroom.

    Over the last few months, he has bought a gas stove and added a plastic garden set to his sea-side retreat. He tries to catch fish when he has no money for food.

    The 39-year-old, who spent seven years in jail for fighting and was released last February, is one of about a dozen people who have turned part of Changi Beach Park into a shanty town.

    Danny broke into tears when asked about his plans. He said: 'I like living here. It's peaceful.'
