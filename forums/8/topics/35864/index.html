    Sadly, it seems that Singapore is being run more and more like some kind of corporation where the citizens are just employees.

    Those who are not considered "productive" can be retrenched? What happened to the sacred duty of a government to look after the very people who elected them to care and run the country for the good of the whole?

    Even the blasted unions don't seem to be on the side of the workers... as of now, the PSA workers don't know what's going on and are given the "Mushroom treatment" (kept in the dark and fed sh!t). Is there any union around on this planet that has the cheek to put this to unionised workers?? "SPWU said union leaders should explain that it is not wise to wait until companies are in the red before they lay off excess workers, and that now, even other firms with profits do it."

    Strange thing is that while private corporations are fighting and taking losses to prevent job cuts and retrenchments, it would seem the GLCs are leading the way in mass slaughter of "excess fat".

    But looking at our government, it is not surprising that this is happening.

    I remember not too long ago, a Singaporean child's life hung in the balance. The Minister of Health was lamenting that $200,000 was "spent" for the child's medical needs. What was even more galling was that he said that he did not want to set a precedence where private hospitals dump patients who could not pay onto the public hospitals.

    It was actually the private hospital which did not have the facilities to treat the child which came up with their own money so the KK Hospital would admit the child. Before that, KK refused to admit the child even when they were the only hospital in Singapore with the facilities to treat the child!!

    We have to face the fact that we. Singaporeans, are no longer citizens in the true sense anymore to the powers that run this country. We are just chattel to them.

    When I was young and naive, I thought my country would love me as much as I loved it.

    When I grew older, I just wanted to work for my little place in the sun.

    Now, older and perhaps a little wiser, but much more cynical, I just hope and pray that the powers do not run my remains through the sugar-cane press when I am useless to them.

    Looking at all this, can you blame me for being a quitter who is still in Singapore?
