    *takes out his law textbook*

    Trade mark....

    from the trademarks act(TMA....things cannot be registered as a trademark if:

    1.it does not satisfy the definition of a trademark as stated in section 2(1) of the TMA....means the mark must be capable of distinguishing......like words invented from scratch....like KODAK...EXXON....or words which bear no meaning to the product or service itself like LEGO...

    2. Mark is devoid of any distinctive character
    eg. registering the letter 'a' simply without any graphics or special design added to it would be a mark devoid of any distinctive character....

    3.The mark is descriptive of the kind, quality, quantity, intended purpose, value, geographical origin, the time of production of goods or of rendering of services, or other characteristics of the goods or services....

    to put simply...marks that describe goods or services in a way that refers to the quality, intended purpose etc,will not be registrable...

    4. The mark is a generic word...
    like the word software.....cannot be registered......or Xerox which was once a brand name...now commonly used as a word to mean photocopy...

    How to register a trademark?

    1.Trademark may be registered if it doesnt come under any of the above categories...

    2.Application made at IPOS...sample drawings of logo of the mark must be sent with standard application forms...

    3.the rest up to the registrar liao.....once registered....trade mark receives protection for 10 years.....renewable for further periods of 10 years subject to payment of prescribed fee...

    plus...singapore being a member of the madrid protocol....anything registered here would be recognised in countries that are in the madrid protocol....think 70++ countries are in it...
