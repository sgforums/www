    I was just thinking... what are the qualities that actually attract a person. The true type of qualities.. those that are not measured by normal material and physically acheivable terms.. not those extrinisc one...

    Intrinsic values that cannot be bought or taught... but that which is caught and brought up through a person's lifetime.

    Things like Degrees... or type of job... or even how buffed or well endowed a person is..are extrinsic... can be measured by worldy terms... cars.... these things can be arranged...

    There was someone who's signature line was the person that we love in the end will be a contradiction to what we have set for our criteria... or something like that..

    Why do I say that...
    Looking at the course of the threads that are posted here regarding what is attractive to the other person... what we can do to change or buy just to have the attention of the one we like.. or love....

    We all talk about personality and character.. but what is it in that person that is intrinsic that cannot be faked that attracts you? Personality? that can be arranged.. People have got different personalities for different occasions..so that one is questionable...
    Character... now that one is up for debate... I don't have an answer for that... but the character is also formed from the habits that are cultivated throughout a person's lifetime...so what habits that you have now... is something that has been built over the years that you have lived... most of it cannot be changed.. but beliefs can be analysed and refined... then the character can be polished to be "better"(depending one who's opinion of what better is)

    Things like honesty and humility...
    submissive and God-fearing
    Disciplined
    Health as a lifestyle... not as a discipline
    clear-headed
    childlike, not childish
    Patience... not tolerance...
    to be quiet and listen... not just quiet....(or obstinate)
    leadership qualities...
    forgiving...
    filal
    to be UN-paranoid
    To correct with compassion.. and to be corrected with humility...

    This is just an example of what I feel are intrinisic qualities.. somethings that cannot be faked... for long...

    How we would love a person of high designation in a company.. or in the eyes of the public.. but all that can be bought.. or earned...

    The cars.. and cards.. given time and desire... these can be gotten...

    even education.. degrees.. and Phd's.

    Looks... hmmmm... can be bought nowadays...or bluffed.. but for how long?

    In the end... what are you really looking for? What will make you really happy? That you can see your unborn children in her eyes... and your love in her heart.

    To look into his eyes and see a responsible man who will give his life for you..... that will mean so much that he will live for only you... to know that he is human and that he will accept correction....

    So all these talks about qualities and characters and material possesions...

    weigh them... are they intrinsic or extrinsic...
    and last but not least.. look inside yourself.. and see if that which you portray to others... is it intrinsic... or extrinsic....

    Real people will know...

    We all have a dream: that happy-ever-after state of things after marriage... after an intense scrutinization of the other party, as if he or she were a pig up for sale... (no offence...)

    I think it's very much a matter of give and take... there are some devestating situations such as:

    1. even if the other person were to fit the entire list that you have created for your future spouse, and yet there is a hint of doubt that you'd want to be with him or her for an entire lifetime... it could be disappointing for the prospective one as he or she has been misled that THAT relationship is THE ONE... don't be a heart-breaker.

    or...

    2. "Hmm... you qualify for almost everything, except for such and such a quality (which I would most likely cultivate in you- if you are worth the time)... so it's SAYONARA!" You lose that person simply becoz he or she ain't good enough, because there might be that perfect one/ or someone better out there.

    Perhaps I have painted a rather bleak picture, however, I have come to understand that it is very much a give-&-take situation. For every single quality that one dares to put on pen & paper, that person must be honest and humble to admit that the quality is either present or lacking in the author.

    One thing I take comfort in: that we do not have to perform up to a certain standard to be approved by the Lord. If not, no one would ever qualify to be His bride.
