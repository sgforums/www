    SINGAPORE : A man has been hit by a train at Bukit Batok MRT station.

    This follows an incident about two weeks earlier, where a man was killed in a train accident at Bishan MRT station on July 28.

    At about 1.50pm on Saturday, a northbound train hit the man along the tracks after it pulled into the station.

    Civil Defence and police officers found the 47-year-old man trapped in a 40-cm gap, between the train undercarriage and rail tracks.

    It took SCDF officers 15 minutes to extricate the man.

    SCDF said the man had cuts on his head and back, and both his legs were injured.

    Meanwhile, the police say they are still investigating.

    The accident disrupted the travel plans of some 4,000 passengers on the northbound service.

    SMRT despatched 12 buses to ferry affected passengers between Choa Chu Kang and Jurong East stations.

    The injured man was semi-conscious and was sent to National University Hospital for treatment.

    SMRT said full service resumed at the station 45 minutes after the accident, at 2.40pm. - CNA
