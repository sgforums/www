    I cut and paste from my another post:-

    In my year enrolment 2003/2004 (last year), my QET got 2 parts, Vocab section and Essay Section.

    Vocab got 2 or 3 passage, basically, you fill in the blanks with the words you think is correct. They give super chim subjects, like what Oil spills with enviromental blah, blah...

    Then Essay got a few topics to choose from also. I wrote on something on SARS, impact on Economic, blah, blah...

    Basically, write something professional, this is Uni, so they expect some degree of professional stuff.

    Last I heard, they took out the Vocab section for this year, don't know true or not.

    Lucky, I passed. But my friend who failed his QET, has to take up another English module. According to him is they train you how to structure your compo, must got head, body ending, spelling, vocab,grammer etc, etc. So basing on this, I think this is what NTU looks out for in your QET.

    So Good Luck
