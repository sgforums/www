    Haha I know this one.

     

    1) Firstly, split 6 balls into two parts (3 each).

    2) Weigh the 6 balls, 3 on each side.

     

    Assumption 1:

    If one side is actually heavier then the other, that means that the heavy ball is in one of those 3 balls on the heavier side. Now, take out all the balls and weigh 2 out of 3 of those balls. If you get one side heavier than the other, good for you cuz you got the answer. If both side is equal, the remaining ball out of the 3 that was not weighed is the heavy one.

     

    Assumption 2:

    If you weigh the 6 balls and both sides are equal in weight, that means that the heavy ball is in the remaining 2 not weighed. Now all you have to do is to weigh the 2 and find out which one is heavier.

     

    A simpler way of explaining is to imagine that you are given 3 apples (1 heavier than the other 2) and you are supposed to find out which one is heavier (for this, 1 try on the weighing machine is already enough). You only got to weigh 2 of them. If both sides are equal that means the remaining one not weighed is the heavy one.
