    harrows.

    Are you sure refinancing for HDB is advisable?

    Yes, of course the instant savings is very tempting. But over the long term:

    1) Rates are not fixed, and can shoot up once the economy restores. I believe housing loan cost about 8% at one time. Refinancing at that time would be useless as most banks will be charging the same rate. Who ever heard of refinancing back in early 1990s?

    2) As mentioned, floating rates left much to be desired because of its unpredictable nature. It makes financial planning difficult because instead of 3.75% constant, you have a variable. What if rates choose to go up when your child arrives?

    3) Banks will have first charge over your property and will foreclose on it if you fail to pay up. In my knowledge, HDB has never ever done something like that. What are the implications?

    i) Many people in the past who went bankrupt still have a roof over their heads(e.g Kenny Goh of Ken-Air), because their HDB flat and CPF is protected by an Act(donno which one). But now, if one goes bankrupt, creditors can go after his HDB flat, and make him homeless.

    ii) Once you refinanced, your CPF is also left unprotected. Should your HDB fall drastically in value, the bank will claim most of the money in event of sale, then you can spend your retirement golden years in a some public park.

    My advise is: Stick to your HDB loan. You may be paying slightly higher now. Treat it as a premium for having a peaceful mind and the right to smile smugly to yourself when your neighbours are mulling over escalating interest rates. The savings are really much anyway, just a couple of hundreds per year on the average. Don't miss the forest for the trees.

    Open for discussion.
