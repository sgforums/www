    7 Sep 09, 01:21

    Could also be TS's room isnt that conductive to sleeping.

    Maybe its the temperature, the air, the bed condition, the lights conditions and the timing situation.

    Temperature is best to be kept cool.  A hot shower before sleep will help the body to wind down to sleeping temperature.

    Air: Windows open, dusts in the room makes your room itchy?  Bad smells or not sunning your pillow?

    Bed: It accumulated bad vibes from long time use, maybe a spring is broken, or you are too used to the shape of it which makes your now bigger body uncomfortable.

    Lights condition refers to your having your handphone, laptop or tv on standby mode, having a kind of blinking lights or that someone may call you and your body naturally keep alert for blinking lights of your handphone.  Same with your laptop/pc lights,  Too much blue light before you sleep (screen light) projecting into your brain before you sleep makes you too alert to sleep.  So do consider shutting pc down 1 hour before you sleep and keep your handphone away, and turn off tv totally instead of leaving its standby lights on.

    Lastly is timing........Your body can keep a schedule.  But you arent using the body clock properly with regular sleep time.
