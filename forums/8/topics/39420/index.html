    SARS: Second death confirmed, all schools up to JC level to close till 6 April

    By Walter Fernandez / Julia Ng / Hasnita A Majid

    Singapore's Health Ministry has confirmed the country's second Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome-related death.

    It is understood the man, one of the first SARS victims, is a 39-year-old pastor of the Faith Assembly of God Church at Kim Keat Road.

    This was confirmed by several members from his church.

    The second fatality comes a day after the SARS claimed its first victim in Singapore.

    This patient, a man in his 40s to 50s, died of lung failure under intensive care at Tan Tock Seng Hospital on Tuesday night.

    He has already been cremated.

    The director of medical services says anyone who dies from SARS will undergo an autopsy and be cremated immediately.

    At a media conference, Health Minister Lim Hng Kiang declined to confirm if the first victim was the father of one of the index cases, nor would he give details of the death.

    He added that the ministry wanted to respect the family's wishes.

    Meanwhile, there are 5 new SARS cases in Singapore, bringing the total number infected to 74.

    All the five are hospital staff and the latest update is that 10 of the 74 patients are in serious condition.

    To allay the growing fears among many parents and in answer to repeated calls for the Government to do more, the Education and Health ministries have decided to close all schools up to junior college level from Thursday till 6th April.

    This will cover all primary and secondary schools, junior colleges and centralised institutes, as well as child care and pre-school centres, People's Association tuition classes and madrasahs.

    These closures will affect 600,000 students islandwide.

    But the current school term will be extended into the June holidays to make up for the lost curriculum time.

    However, Singapore's universities, polytechnics and Institutes of Technical Education will stay open.

    The Government said these students are older and better able to understand the situation and take precautions.

    Health Minister Lim Hng Kiang said: "There's no need for us to over-react, and be completely panicky. At the same time we should not be overly complacent.

    "Say for example tomorrow (Thursday) when we close the schools, I hope parents won't take it lightly and send the kids to the shopping centres and they congregate there. Then it defeats the whole purpose.

    "I think the whole idea of closing the school is to minimise big groups of students having close contacts, and that's the reason why sports and competitions are all closed.

    "So I think Singaporeans must be educated on the nature of SARS and take the appropriate measures, working with us to deal with it," Mr Lim added.

    Education Minister Teo Chee Hean meanwhile said: "The precautions that we've already taken in our schools are quite adequate. This is really an extra precautionary measure, to reassure parents and to give them some peace of mind.

    "We also urge employers to exercise flexibility and understanding, in allowing employees affected by the closing of schools to take urgent leave to make alternative childcare arangements."

    The education and health ministries have said that they will review the SARS situation before the end of next week.

    Parents whom Channel NewsAsia spoke to were generally relieved that most schools are going to be closed, although a few expressed some concerns about their children's school work.

    One parent welcomed the move, saying: "I think it's a good thing the Ministry has closed the schools so that the disease won't be spread."

    Another said: "We're quite taken by surprise but I guess I'll be teaching my kid at home so that she won't miss too many lessons. But I guess health is more important at this moment than education."
