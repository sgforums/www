    The Traffic Police is cracking down on motorists and passengers who don't buckle up while on the roads.

    From May 7, drivers who don't put on their seat belt will be fined $120, and given 3 demerit points.

    It's the same penalty, if their passengers, including those in the back seat, don't buckle up, or if the drivers don't use child restraints for children in the vehicle.

    These rules were introduced in 1993. Just last week, the Traffic Police warned close to 600 motorists and passengers who didn't buckle up. Some 40 drivers were also warned for not using child restraint seats.

    To drive home the message, the Traffic Police and Automobile Association are conducting a month-long campaign about using rear seat belts and child seats.
