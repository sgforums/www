    yeah....i agree....a cultured person is s'posed to be like an all-rounder i think. much like the greek (not geek) nobles or the confucian jun zi (or gentleman). i think when u say someone is cultured, u expect both good comportment and great learning. kinda like with both brains and the ability to carry it off. and to carry yourself well too. too often nowadays being cultured is associated only with the outward manifestation of it without the use of too much grey matter or the ends to which it is put. if u look back at the old days, only the elite received any education. and this was typically not just a training of the mind but also a kind of "social training" if u like, how to act and behave towards ur fellow man (and woman too of course!). so etiquette and self-reflection and learning went hand in hand.

    nowadays, however, education is pretty different. it has become so focussed and specialised it has lost some of its original purpose and intent....to create a gentleman of sorts. we still fall into the trap of equating better educated ppl wif more refined and cultured ppl. but nothing could be further from the truth. many highly educated ppl are not only less than refined in social mannerisms, some have no sense of morals at all! so nowadays the family takes over the responsibility of bringing up the perfect lady or gentleman (which is not to say this wasn't also the case in days of old. just that nowadays formal education doesn't really cater to this kinda thing.)

    still ppl expect that if ur more highly educated, it means it naturally follows ur more likely to be refined or cultured or whatever. so it's a whole package deal. and it just goes to show how the notion of education has changed so dramatically over the years. i think education is the capacity to learn. ur as educated as u are capable of learning. and from what i know, life itself is the greatest teacher. after that, it's all up to you. whether or not u pass ur finals, is up to you.
