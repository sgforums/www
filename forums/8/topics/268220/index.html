    Depends I guess.

    I have a wisdom tooth in the back of my mouth, it used to cause me hurt and pain but after I visited the dentist, the problem was solved without having to undergo an expensive operation to remove the tooth.

    The dentist did an x-ray and she realize that it was either laying on top of my nerve or infront of it ( the tooth is bent inside the gum ). Since X-ray could't tell whether it was on the nerve or not, she suggested that she would help me drill off parts of the tooth so it would not cause any pain. If I had choosen to remove it and if it really lies on top of my nerve, my lower jaw could go numb for an indefinite amount of time.

    So if the tooth cause you pain, go see a dentist and ask for a solution, if not, you can choose to leave it alone.
