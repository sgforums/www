    I beg to defer. Here's my mode of operation for shopping:

    1) I like, I can afford, I ask myself do I really need it

    2) If yes, I would then ask myself if I am willing to let the store earn the kind of money (i.e. the price) from me. If no, I don't buy.

    3) If yes, I would look at my wallet to see got enough cash. If no, I would look elsewhere to compare prices and I won't buy.

    4) If wallet has enough cash, I would ask myself I can tolerate the thought that my wallet would be outta cash after buying (Using credit card or net is bad, I hate the thought of diminishing figures in my bank account)

    5) If yes, I would ask myself if I should reserve the money for other things instead. If no, I don't buy.

    6) If yes, I don't buy. If no, I also don't buy, I would have lost interest in the item after this long thinking process and won't buy it.

    7) Repeat step 1 for every other item that catches my fancy.

    This is what happened to me at all times, esp sales. In the end, no matter how tempting a thing can be, I'm worn out by my own thoughts to buy anything.
