    That depends on what one is regretting. And the circumstances that lead up to the feeling of regret.

    There is nothing more painful than not trying despite having all the odds stacked against you and only to regret having given up after the opportunity slips you by.

    Regret not having said or done something which could have meant so much is just as bad.

    With regret comes pain, with pain we learn, we grow and become stronger. It's better to have failed and learnt from that failure than to be successful all our life. For then, we would have learned nothing, and would have left this world no richer than when we entered it.

    Yes, regret is painful, okay. maybe not as painful as a broken limb, physically, but then I digress.

    The only thing more painful than regret is to regret not having done something only to realise that it's too late to salvage the situation. Which merely seeks to separate the pain of regret into two different layers.
