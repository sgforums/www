    A little history on Contiki :-

    Did you know ContikiÂ’s history started in 1961 with a young New Zealander named John Anderson?

    In 1961, John Anderson arrived in London to tour Europe. He didn't want to go alone and he didn't have much money, so he put a deposit on a minibus and gathered a group of travelers who spent twelve weeks exploring Europe together. At the end of the trip, John tried to sell the minibus but no one wanted it. So in the Spring of 1962 he decided to advertise his Europe trip again. This time, he was able to fit two trips into the Summer season, he doubled his business and Contiki Holidays was "born". The first tours were all booked by 19 - 29 year olds so right from the start Contiki was all about youth travel.

    Contiki offers three ways to travel called Camping, Concept, and Time Out.

    The basic difference is in the type of accommodation.

    Participation Camping tours offer twinshare tents with two single air mattresses for your campsite experience.

    Concept tours are mostly twin-share and include stays at Contiki properties, campsites and hotel/pensions.

    Time Out tours offer tourist class hotels and often include a night at a resort or five-star property. On Time Out tours you can choose single or twin-share or triple-share.
