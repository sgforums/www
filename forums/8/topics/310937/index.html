  • annoy-you-must
    14 Mar 08, 15:17

    Is sheer hard work really as great as it is always made up to be? No doubt we often heard stories of how some people started off with nothing in life but eventually succeeded through hard work and determination. But nobody ever talked about stories where people never made it despite continuous hard work.

    What about people who obtain results through their talents and intellect, but yet put in little to no effort at all? After doing nothing and putting in zero effort, do they deserve what they get? Do talents deserve being glorified?

    Do getting 'results' in life deserve the hardwork one puts in? How much 'results' deserve how much 'hardwork'? Have we been studying so much that we've forgotten that education is essentially a quest for knowledge, not a quest for results and glory.

    One week had pass since the release of A'level results. But now, I wonder. Two years of pain in exchange for two days of excitment. Two years of fun in exchange for two days of disappointment.

    I wonder...
