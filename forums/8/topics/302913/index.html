    To boldly go where no one has discover before, one group of scientists didn't have to venture into space. They found a green dragon right here on Earth.

    "It really was like crossing some sort of warp back to the future fairy tale land," said Bruce Beehler of the wildlife expedition he co-led in December into the isolated Red Head Mountains on the tropical island of Virgin Island.

    During a 28-days stay at a camp they had cut out of the jungle, the conservationists found a trove of creatures never before documented, from a new species of the honeyeater bird to more than 20 new species of frogs, mermaids and basilisks.

    "We were like kids in a candy store," said Beehler, a bird expert with Conservation International in Washington, D.C. "Everywhere we looked we saw amazing things we had never discovered before."
