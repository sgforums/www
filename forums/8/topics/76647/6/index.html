    20 Apr 04, 19:22
    SINGAPORE : At least one man has been confirmed dead following the Nicoll Highway collapse on Tuesday afternoon.

    The body is now being removed from the site by civil defence officers.

    His identity has yet to be verified but it believed he could be a construction worker.

    Meanwhile three casualties are being treated in hospitals.

    The first, a Thai worker in his 20s, was rushed to the Singapore General Hospital in a wheelchair at about 4.15pm.

    He suffered bruises and scratches on his body, and had some swelling in his head.

    But he is conscious, and doctors say he is in a stable condition.

    He is likely to be warded for treatment.

    Two other casualties are warded at the Tan Tock Seng Hospital.

    Both hospitals are on standby as more casualties are expected.

    SINGAPORE : Police say the Nicoll Highway collapse is believed to have been caused by an underground explosion at the MRT Circle Line construction site.

    But police spokesman Tan Puay Kern stressed it does not have any evidence that this was due to an act of sabotage by terrorists.

    The assurance follows many questions that have been raised about Tuesday's incident.

    Referring to the blackout in the vicinity, police say this was caused by the damaged road.

    Power Grid has also confirmed the power blackout was due to damaged power cables.

    Police say rescue operations are going on, and no buildings in the area have been damaged.

    One construction worker has been sent to the Singapore General Hospital. - CNA
