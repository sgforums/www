    rechargeable batteries more economical in a long run if u r using mp3 players, digital cameras, mobile music playback equipment, high powered torch light or lightinig equipment etc. with full discharge and recharge cycles of 500-1000 times, they are more efficient to be used soon after a full charge and its life prolonged after a full/ near full discharge cycle to drain out all the existing power in it to prevent 'memory' effect. dun take your bet on NiMh rechargeable batt being memory effect proof. the numberings on the battery physical is the capacity of the power of that battery when fully charge. the higher the number..the more power it can store and run an equipment 92300mAH >850mAH. it is true that if you charge them fully and leave it unused for a while it will drain out eventually. for new batteries undergoing a few charging and discgharging cycles....the rate of loss is lower. as the battery grows older after more cycles...u will notice a shorter life span when powering your equipment and it drains out faster when left untouched.

    dun ask me how i know because i believe in getting the batteries, using them and making a judgement myself. rechargeable batteries are also more environmental frendly as it cuts down wastes whenever an alkaline battery is being used and not properly recycled or disposed of causing pollution to the environment.
