    1) Go to "control panel"
    2) Click on "Add or Remove programs"
    3) Look for something that looks obviously like an SP2 thingie and click on the change/remove tab on the right.
    4) If the above does not exist, look for lines that go "Windows XP Hotfix (SP2) Qxxxxxx"
    5) Continue from 3) or 4) delete all the individual lines to reverse the SP2 installation process.
    6) Follow instructions to reboot.

    Do not worry, you are not the first to encounter such terrifying moments using SP2. Though I haven't encounter problems using SP2, the above are just my recommended way to fix your encountered problem.

    I haven't come across a non-buggy version of the SP2 combined pack. For me, I just used the windows update method in the programs folder and every SP2 component needed was done just fine, no problems, maybe you want to try that after you fixed the above.
