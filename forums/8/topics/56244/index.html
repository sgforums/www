    Plasma TV sets more practical at police HQ
    I REFER to Mr Leow Yeow Seng's letter, 'Why splurge on plasma TV?' (ST, Aug 29).

    The police are ever mindful of the need to exercise fiscal prudence in procurement.

    We are also equally mindful of the need to serve the public better and to provide a conducive environment to those who do come to the police to seek help, as many of them are generally in a state of anxiety.

    The average waiting time to be served at a large regional police station, such as the Jurong Division Police Headquarters (JDHQ), is about 15 minutes. National and crime-prevention messages are screened for members of the public waiting to be served.

    With the large reception area at JDHQ, it was felt that having two large plasma sets was more practical than having several smaller TV sets placed around the reception area.

    PHILLIP MAH
    Assistant Director(Media Relations)
    Singapore Police Force

    SEPT 5, 2003
    Reply by police on plasma TV sets baffles me

    I REFER to the reply by Mr Phillip Mah of the Singapore Police Force, 'Plasma TV sets more practical at police HQ' (ST, Sept 2).

    His justification for the plasma TV sets was that:

    TV sets screening national and crime-prevention messages serve the members of the public who are required to wait for an average of 15 minutes.

    It is more 'practical' to have two large plasma TV sets than several small ones.

    Even private corporations such as banks are cutting cost and not providing TV sets and chairs in their lobbies for crowds standing in queues for more than 15 minutes. Why does a non-profit organisation such as the police need to be different?

    I do not understand what is so 'practical' about the large plasma TV sets. If members of the public want to watch the screenings, they can sit closer to the smaller TV sets. However, I am sure most will not care about the TV programmes when they are in the police station.

    I had hoped that the police would admit that it was a mistake and promise to review all future spending.

    On the other hand, I was not surprised to see the justifications by Mr Mah - a typical public-service response to criticism.

    TAN CHEE YONG
