    Hmmm, well you have effectively 2 weeks before christmas, so technically based on healthy weight loss levels you should not lose more than 4kgs of fat.

    One of the most effective things is to do cardio excercises first thing in the morning before any food. All you should have is 2 glasses of water to hydrate your body and wait for about half an hour to prevent stiches from the water before embarking on excercise.

    The main reason you need to do excercise before any food, is due to the fact that your body is in a fasted state and is already low on blood glucose levels. The induced cardio activity will then force your body to depend on its glycogen levels and then start to use up the fat stores the body has. Scientific studies have shown that this form of excercise can be up to 300% more effective as compared with excercising at other times of the day.

    With this in mind, you should aim to wake up as early as possible and start cardio activity and get it out of your adgenda before going to work/school. This prevents guilt of not working out and frees you for the rest of the day from excercise if you have a busy schedule.

    Get plenty of rest, at least 8hrs of sleep, this is to allow your body to repair the tissue damaged in excercise. This is important as your body's muscles are its natural fat burners, metabolically speaking it takes more energy to sustain muscle than fat so as you progress in your excercise routine and build muscles your body's basal metabolic rate increase allowing your to burn fat faster.

    You should also eat frequently. However you need to plan your meals way ahead and preferably home cook them. This way you know what goes into the food you are eating, fried and oily foods are definately a no no.

    The 3 meals that we have a day are great for time schedules and office work, but the human body was never developed to adapt to that. Our bodies are engineered in the way of the hunter gatherers, eating small portions sporadically throughout the day. But civilisation has created an oversurplus of food easily accessible to us and we tend to overeat.

    Ideally you should eat 6 smaller meals a day each consiting of a portion protein, a portion of carbohydrates and a portion of vegetables. A portion can be roughly equated to the size of your clenched fist.

    It's a common misconception that by eating less one will lose weight. This is technically true in the initial phases and will produce the results relatively fast, but the danger of directly reducing your food consumption is that you will indirectly reduce your metabolic rate without knowing it. Food reduction triggers the starvation mechanism built into our bodies and as our bodies adapt you will reliase the next time you try to lose the same amount of weight, it might not be that easy.

    One thing that people might not know is that eating technically increases your metabolism. Thats why you need to spread your food over multiple meals but remember in smaller portions. It would serve no purpose if you ate 6 big meals instead of 6 small ones.
