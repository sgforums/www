Ezlink...

  • greyskymorning
    23 Oct 03, 22:18
    They are still selling like hot cakes and EZ-Link is now rethinking its sales strategy to try and contain the problem.

    This year, a daily total of more than 3,000 adult ez-link stored-value cards have been sold. Since the ez-link system was launched in April of last year, nearly 3.5 million cards have been sold.

    EZ-Link is baffled by the high sales and worried that subsidising the $6 manufacturing cost will lead to higher operating costs and higher card prices.

    Mr Nicholas Lee, EZ-Link's senior vice-president of business and technology, told reporters yesterday that the number of unused cards is also increasing. These could be held by tourists, day-trippers from Malaysia, or even people who keep four or five cards for "visiting relatives".

    "Card sales can continue as long as they are being used. The question is whether we should continue subsidising these buyers at the expense of other commuters," he said.

    Some cards are returned in such a damaged state they cannot be used again.

    "Because we have been in operation for less than two years, we want to avoid ugly situations at the sales counters. So we still give refunds for them," said Mr Lee.

    At the moment, refunds are given within 12 years of purchase for the stored-value $2 deposit and anything left of the $3 travel deposit for every card.

    EZ-Link is looking at options such as charging a higher deposit, having personalised cards, making people pay for the manufacturing cost or linking all cards to Giro.
