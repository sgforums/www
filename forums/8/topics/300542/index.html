    If the wedding band is the one that gets most worn, isn't it better to make THAT one more nicer than the engagement ring ?
    And since the wedding band is the one that is more significant since it is the one that symbolize forever union, isn't it more sensible to spend more money on the band than the engagement ring ?

    If you ask any girl which would they prefer to wear everyday, they'd choose the nicer ring.
    I don't know about New York, but where I live, they wear the Engagement ring AND the Wedding band together. Everyday.

    In Singapore, we are partly western and partly Asian.
    The traditions and practices has evolved over time to suit the needs of modern Singaporean.
    During my mother's time, there's only ONE ring.
    The proposal/betrothal is done with offers of gifts to her family.

    There are 4 pieces of gold and wedding dowry involve. The tradition is very different from Westerners.

    My Asian friend had a hard time getting her American husband to accept the idea of a dowry as it seems like the family was selling their daughter to him. But in fact, it was an offer to thank the parents for the effort in raising their daughter.
    If you want to understand more, you'd need to do some research.

    Here's a link for your reference and for TS too.

    http://www.singaporebrides.com/features/custom1.htm

    The practice will vary depending on their dialect group.

    Ofcors, newer generations have forgone most of the more elaborate procedures and opted for the simpler westernized wedding.

    1. I have 2 rings. 1 which he use to propose, the other which I wear as a wedding band. I differ from jojobeach in the sense that, i think the proposal ring shd be the one with the diamond, while your wedding band which you tend to wear day in day out, is a simpler one.
    Tip: BRING YOUR GIRLFRIEND TO BUY THE RINGS unless you really know her taste damn well.

    2. I have no diamonds on either of my rings because generally I'm not a jewellery person and don't see the point of spending $ on a diamond ring which i'm not going to ever wear. As far as i am concerned, might as well tattoo a wedding band but my hubby doesn't like tattoos but...anyway So, there is no must - as long as your girlfriend AGREES with it. She might have different thoughts -heh heh-

    3. Most people ROM one year, then have their customary the next - thanks to HDB balloting and whatever needing an ROM cert but cannot move in together immediately. But really there is no hard and fast rules to anything.

    4. Which dialect group are you and how traditional are both sides families? Some things which you would need to take note:
    - groom's family usually give a 'dowry' of gifts. Ensure that this is discussed first between your gf and her parents, then formally either through those 'aunties' OR what I did was just to have both families dinner together and finalise what items to buy and etc.
    - before the customary, I had an event called the 'guo da li' which is basically groom's family delivers the gifts to my place which include all the cakes which you need when u give invites to your relatives.
    - depending on HOW traditional and what expectations both sides have, gifts will include:
    - some dried plant thing to be exchanged to pomegranate leaves
    - suckling pig OR pig trotter's cans
    - liquor to be exchanged with some sweet drinks
    - oranges
    - 4 dian jin: 4 piece of gold usually earrings, necklace, pendant
    - angpows: which the groom gives, and the bride's family will take a portion and return the rest
    - another angpow specifically for bride's mom

    a lot lah.. u tok to those chinatown aunties they tell u everything.

    4. For ROM.. dont go ROM to ROM la... cos ... queue up and all. Can invite a JP to go anywhere to officiate one.. i got mine to go to changi beach free location summore.

    Waaa I say so much... cos nostalgic lah.

    But.. serious.. How to get married? With sincerity and honesty - that will make the marriage far more than just the wedding. And its the marriage that matters - not the rings, the weddings, the parties.
