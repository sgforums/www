    Samsung Electronics Market Value Tops $100 Bln

    Tuesday Jan 03 2006 21:55:00 EST

    (RTTNews) - The market value of shares in Samsung Electronics Co. Ltd., Asia's most valuable company, exceeded 100 trillion won or $100.1 billion for the first time ever Wednesday on expectations of higher fourth-quarter earnings. Samsung Electronics' stock was trading at 685,000 won per share on the Seoul bourse, up 3.01% or 20,000 won from the previous day. Based on the latest price, the value of Samsung Electronics' common stock was estimated at 101.4 trillion won.

    Samsung joins Vodafone Group Plc as the only technology stock outside the US to achieve this landmark. Vodafone's market value is estimated at $137 billion. In the process, Samsung overtook Nokia Oyj of Finland, Motorola Inc. of the US and Sony Corp. of Japan in market capitalization. Nokia is now valued at $83 billion, slipping from a near $300 billion market value in 2000. Motorola's market capitalization is now estimated at $58 billion. Sony, which was once enjoying a market capitalization of $107 billion in 2000, has fallen to less than half of Samsung's current value.
