    Thanks
    depending on what you like to do, there are all kinds of things there.

    You can visit wangfujing like an alley full of knick knacks and souvenirs and all kinds of snacks(scorpions and grasshoppers on satay sticks), shopping centers are everywhere some of the largest I've seen has escalators up to 10 stories and all shopping.

    The thing abt shopping is bargaining even with seemingly fixed price items in department stores it is possible

    Then you have the sights like forbidden palace, great wall, and imperial tombs.

    Everything in China these days cost money nothing is for free, don't believe day tour packages advertising for 100RMB they are not inclusive of entrance tickets. Arrange day tours only from the hotel recept never take day tours from touts.

    Beware of anything that is too good to be true.
    Beware of pirate private taxis(no joke, it may be the last time anybody sees you alive)
    NEVER!!! EVER!!! SHARE TAXIS WITH STRANGERS
    Beware of pretty girls who approach you to bring them to nearby bar/disco for a drink(I know someone conned into paying S$600 for 3 beers)
    Beware of changing money with road strangers(a lot of fake money around)
    Bring USD(very few banks will agree to change singapore dollar)

    Remember to try peking duck and the tsingtao beer(china version) and if you more daring try the xiang rou pau.

    Places to see
    -----------------
    Yu Yuan - there the imperal summer resident & City Yellow temple - the old shanghai - showcase the old shanghai , good for paintings ......
    The BUND - basically the harbour side view.

    Resturant to try
    ----------------------
    Mei Long Zhen Resturant - (sichuan dish) Every world leader who visited shanghai eat in this resturant, with picture of all amercian president who been there and even LKY .

    Don't eat anything that is not cook ! The shanghai folks kill gems with vingear and wine, which you don't take enough to do the same. I alway stay away from sishu in china.

    Just note, only major resturants accept credit cards ! One note of advice, trust no one that you don't know.

    If staying longer , join a tour to Huang Zhou or siu Zhou which are just on top of shanghai, very nice scenery places.

    Enjoy your stay.

    i guess the most popular place for pubbing/clubbing in Shanghai would be Xin Tian Di. if i'm not wrong it's started by Hongkongers and u can find lotsa foreigners there.

    food-wise u gotta try the xiao long bao. u shd b able to find pretty tasty ones in any regular restaurants.

    for shopping, my fav haunt is Xiang Yang Shi Chang. it's more well-known as an imitation gds haven but u'll also be able to buy clothes/shoes and find some shops selling fairly cute or unusual items. be careful not to be fleeced by them as they'll inflate the prices knowing u're not a local. a general rule of the thumb is to offer abt 1/3 of their asking price, then negotiate to a price u're willing to pay for tt item. note tho tt u'll b pressurised to buy anything which u've haggled over, or risk being cursed or spat at... haha...
