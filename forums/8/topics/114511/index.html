    For yr benefits, PLS READ THIS, though it may be a bit outdated. You may be the next victim. Take
    note
    when you travel to JB, especially those of you who drive in to JB regularly, plse take note and extra care.

    Just to let you friends know I too had a bad x-perience
    on Sunday 26th Sept in JB. When my spouse and I passed thru'
    the JB
    CIQ check point at about 1205 pm. and headed for a golf game at Pendorosa, thinking of a bit early to the tee-off time and with intention to eat lunch, we turned into a road just diagonally opposite

    Pelangi Plaza where a 24hrs Standard Chartered Bank is located, didnt we realize that we were being followed by a thug on motorbike.
    Since we couldnt find any food stores along the way, I made a right turn from Jln Tebrau into a street outside a row of specialist clinics with the intention to get back into the main road. As it is a one way street and slowed down by parking car, I began to wonder why the motorbike raider was following so close that my rear car sensor keep peeping on left and right. I guess this is the time he strike.
    Less than 30 seconds, I felt the rear tyre punctured suddenly. I
    slowed down and stopped the car at about 20 metres away at road side in the midst of single storey terrace house.
    When I look around , I mmediately noticed a malay guy approached
    about 10 metres away us.(Maybe he was about to strike) We quickly went
    into the car, locked and then moved slowly about 100m until reaching
    the Poton show room at the junction of Crown Royal Hotel.. To assess
    the surrounding, when I looked into the rear mirror, I noticed the malay guy got into a white Porton Wira which had been waited at road side when we passed. I turned into the car park and parked our car at outside the show room. We noticed being followed by the same man, and also the white Proton was just at the T-junction adjacent to the car park hesitating whether to turn into the same car park..
    It was only when we went into the show room that the driver(also
    a Malay) started to drive off. Luckly we can get the tyre repaired at the same Proton workshop.
    We found that the cause of the puncture is similar to modus
    operandi used in the circulated email. Pls see details photo attached. One
    a 4mm long sharp hollow needle made from black metal strip of the umbrella upper stick skeleton which hollow needle will cause air
    to leak out faster from the tyre as compare to normal nails when
    hit..

    If not because of my wife reminded me of the below circulated email of such ploy, we almost being a victim to a
    robbery.

    So, please, "when you have a puncture tyre suddenly, do not risk stopping your vehicle in the middle of the roadside. Try to roll
    to the nearest petrol kiosk or tyre shop where there are more human traffic thus limiting the chance for them to strike / rob u."
    Also it is not advisible to change money at JB. You may be easily
    targeted.
    We were lucky and thank my spouse for being alert AND the circulated email.

    My parents actually kenah before. While moving off from a traffic light just outside Holiday Plaza, their tyre suddenly burst. They stopped at the roadside and while my father was changing the tyres, my mother went into Holiday Plaza to change money. She just took her purse along, leaving the handbag on the passenger seat (she thought it was safe since my father was there).

    Soon, this guy on a motorbike came long and offered my father help to change the tyres, to which my father refused, not wanting to risk facing any scams. However, while they were talking, another guy came from the passenger's side, took my mother's handbag and ran, hopped onto a motorbike and rode away.

    My father couldn't do anything because he couldn't just dump the car on the roadside. Besides, its never a good idea not knowing what you're chasing, especially in a foreign land. How can anyone run after a motorbike anyway?

    I personally know people who have gotten into trouble there.....windows smashed while cars are parked, robberies, extortion, etc.....even just being scolded or challenged to fights just for the sport of it. Yes, I've gotten my fair share of traffic fines too.

    I've been lucky though. I'm a very frequent traveller to JB and KL and have never really gotten into any serious trouble, except for a case a few years ago, I was going through Malaysian immigration going into JB. I was alone and the immigration counter was on the left side of the car. So I stepped out of the car, closed the door and walked across to the left for immigration procedures. The door locked by itself and the engine was still running. hahahahaha. You should've seen how pissed off the long line of drivers behind me was - they all had to somehow change to other lines. As for me, I had to take a cab back to my house to get the spare car key. Took 3 hours before I went back to claim my car. The immigration officers were very cool and polite.
