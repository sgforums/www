    There are many reasons why one might have bad breath. The first step to take is to figure out exactly what is making your breath foul.

    If it is due to decayed teeth, it is very easy to deal with. Regular trips to the dentist and good personal hygine can eliminate bad breath in this category. Problem is that breath problems usually do not stem from your teeth. I have heard stories where people replace all their teeth just to find out that their breath problem still persists.

    You may have this condition called post-nasal drip due to sinusitis. In this case, the mucus from your nose flows to the back of your throat and gets built up there. When the mucus decomposes from the back of your throat, it will give off a rather foul smell. So if you have sinus problems, treat them now. Pending full treatment of sinusitis, try rinsing your nasal cavities regularly with home made saline solutions. This will help curb post-nasal drip.

    In any case, brush the back of your tongue regularly as this is usually the site where foul smell originates.
