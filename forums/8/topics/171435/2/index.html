    That second hand piano that you have recently purchased contains a lot more than the hammers, strings, and other pieces of wood and metal. The keys rest in what amounts to a storage space. This storage space has probably been unseen for anywhere from 50 to a hundred years. The question is, what lies hidden in this storage space. This isn't urban myth, nor is it anecdotal observation. The dirt and debris that settles in a piano over a space of a hundred years is to be seen to be believed. If you do shop for a piano from a dealer, ask that he remove the keys for you to have a look underneath. Look at the interior underneath the keys. Minimally, this should be clean, free of rubbish. Maximally, the interior felts, paper washers, and everything else that contributes to smooth operation of the key should be in good condition.
