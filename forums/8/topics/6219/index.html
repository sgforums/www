    Yummers...

    Let me offer a slightly different take on this thread. I guess I am being presumptious but please bear with me. Friendship is ephemeral at best. Truly. Even those who you think will stay frens with you forever and ever (when u r in your teens).

    All of a sudden, it's the late twenties and people marry. Their family/spouse becomes more (much more) impt to them than frens. Yah, you can still maintain that contact, but the friendship is altered at a fundamental value because their spouses' views has direct bearings on your relationship. When u r there, u'll know.

    When u start working, frenships change too. They become "networking". Nodes leading to other nodes. At a surface level, they are still your frens. New people you meet are "frens" but in reality (whether you see it as such is another matter), you become part of the networking chain. SDS has changed its function as a match making society to an unabashedly forum for young professionals to network.

    Just don't expect too much. Rely on yourself to get you through life.
