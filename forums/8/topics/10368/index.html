    BY ANDRE YEO
    Nov 17, 2000

    THEIR daughter's death is a mystery.

    The 10-year-old girl died in April apparently after falling from a block of flats in Hougang.

    Her parents have been waiting for answers since. Yesterday, they thought they were going to get them at the coroner's court.

    But when the case was adjourned for police to complete their investigations, the girl's Taiwanese mother turned hostile and aggressive, waving her arms in the air and shouting.

    "Arrest me! Go ahead! Arrest me!" she screamed in Mandarin outside the courtroom, thrusting both arms forward.

    The ruckus drew as many as eight police officers to the scene. They advised her to calm down and explained to her husband that they could arrest his wife for unruly behaviour. But the enraged woman didn't care.

    The 40-year-old housewife, and her jobless Singaporean husband, 46, wanted to be known only as Mr and Mrs Yeo. Their daughter, Yeo Tuan Wei, 10, was a pupil at CHIJ (Ponggol).

    They arrived at the court yesterday with two male friends, around 2.30 pm.

    The case was to have been heard before state coroner Chay Yuen Fatt yesterday.

    But Mr and Mrs Yeo were told that it had to be adjourned because the police had not completed their investigations.

    "Seven months already," Mrs Yeo was heard saying, her eyes red. "How long must I wait?" Mr Yeo added in English: "They can't tell us to come here, then say they have not completed their investigations!"

    They went back to the courtroom and vented their frustrations in front of six police officers and some 15 people, who were stunned.

    Said Mr Yeo: "We can wait till midnight, even 3 am!"

    Because of the commotion, other cases were postponed. People left, but the couple stayed on.

    With a piece of tissue in her hand, Mrs Yeo screamed: "I want them to hear the case now! I'll wait! Even if it takes all night!"

    Staff members stood by, amazed and embarrassed.

    Then, at 4.30 pm, a court clerk told the couple that Mr Chay would see them. But Mrs Yeo would have to remain calm.

    Before the clerk could finish her sentence, Mrs Yeo shot back: "What is she saying? Tell her to speak in Mandarin!"

    Mr Yeo tried to pacify his furious wife but she insisted: "I didn't shout. That's how I talk."

    They then entered Mr Chay's chambers. At 5 pm, Mrs Yeo emerged and continued to complain loudly.

    "I don't care anymore! I want to go back to Taiwan."

    But moments later, she said: "Seven months already, and they still can't decide. I will stay here."

    She still refused to leave the courtroom. Five police officers stood nearby, advising the couple to leave, as the courts had closed for the day. After a 15-minute stand-off, Mrs Yeo finally got up and left. But not before shouting: "We are Taiwanese! We shall do it the Taiwanese way!"

    Speaking to The New Paper outside the court yesterday, Mr Yeo explained why they were so upset.

    He said: "We still don't know what our daughter was doing at that block of flats and how she fell. We don't even know how many floors she fell.

    "We have been waiting for answers for seven months, and nothing has been solved yet. We are suffering."

    The case has been adjourned to Nov 23.
