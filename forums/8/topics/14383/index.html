    Hey queenie, love is something that needs both parties to invest in to work... If you've been the one doing everything, then the relationship isn't going to work out because it takes two hands to clap. The best you can do in situations where you're the one losing out, is just to learn to let go and walk away, cos if you cling on you're the one that's gonna get hurt.

    But then again, you also have to ask yourself if the other party has been investing in the relationship in ways that you can't or didn't see, right? Like maybe the time he lent you 10 bucks for your cab fare and had to walk home, or something like that... Also remember to try and see ways that you may have dissappointed him, the same way he dissappointed you.

    A relationship also needs understanding and trust. You need understanding and trust, to see what your boyfriend may have done for you, and he also needs to see what you have done for him. It also never hurts to talk to him. Get everything out in the open and clarify your doubts. Its much better that way, than to let things bottle up and misunderstand each other. Just make sure the confrontation doesn't get ugly. Keep it mature and measured

    Good luck with it~
