    ISABEL HITS US EAST COAST

    Gusts of up to 100mph have hit the US East Coast as Hurricane Isabel begins wreaking havoc.

    Thousands of people have been evacuated throughout the US mid-Atlantic region as flights were cancelled and power supplies cut.

    The incoming hurricane triggered the shut down of federal government offices in Washington.

    More than 300,000 people were left without power on the North Carolina and Virginia coast.

    Further power disruption was expected as Isabel moved inland.

    Airlines cancelled almost 700 flights, mostly affecting airports in North Carolina, Virginia and Washington's three major terminals.

    States of emergency were declared in North Carolina, Virginia, Washington DC, Maryland, West Virginia, Delaware and Pennsylvania.

    Emergency shelters were opened and schools closed across the region.

    More than 200,000 people were ordered to evacuate from low-lying areas of North Carolina and Virginia or risk being trapped by up to 11-feet of water.

    President Bush moved to his Camp David retreat in Maryland where he was said to be monitoring the situation.

    British Airways, bmi and Virgin cancelled flights across the Atlantic ahead of Isabel hitting land.

    Hundreds of passengers were stranded in Britain as BA cancelled its three services to and from Washington on Thursday and a daily flight to Baltimore.

    Bmi cancelled its two flights to and from Washington from Manchester but was still operating a service to Chicago.

    And Virgin Atlantic said it had scrapped its one flight to Washington from Heathrow.

    Blowing a gale at Virginia Beach

    Thousands of people along the coast prepared by boarding-up windows, hauling boats inland and stocking up on generators, batteries and torches.

    Hardware stores in the region have run out of lamp oil, tape, camping lamps, can openers, plastic sheeting and candles.

    The Foreign Office has issued a travel warning and told people to keep a careful watch on events.

    Isabel weakened from a category five monster with 160mph winds to a strong category two early on Wednesday, with sustained winds of near 110mph.

    Isabel is the first major hurricane to threaten the mid-Atlantic since Floyd wreaked havoc on the East Coast in September 1999.
