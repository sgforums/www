    Yesterday, while i was driving along ave 3 ang mo kio, beside serangoon community club, or the place where sell many birds, at midnight 3pm.

     

    Suddenly, through my windscreen, I saw a big car from right side of my view in front of me 5 to 8 metres ahead flying across and crashed onto the ground upside down.

     

    Then, the car alarm went off. the car airbag was torn by the smashing glasses. I looked at the scene, feeling absolutely gutted in disbelief in my van. My mind was in auto pilot, and it seems what those people has described as a surreal experience. My mind just cannot believe that it is seeing and experiencing this thing.

     

    I moved my van backed a little abit in case I scared her car explode, but then i decide, what the heck. ANyway, by now, i can see her crawling out of her car painfully and in shock. Lucky she was not trapped and crushed by her vehicle. I keep thinking after this incident how good her car must have been in safety features. Really a good buy.

     

    As she stood up, I was the nearest towards her, almost no one still at the moment except me another indian person standing at the roadside. She walked towards me as i gesture her to move away from her vehicle. Still in trauma, she requested me for my phone to call her parents and a close friend. I then proceed to check if she has broken any bones or not. I discovered she has just a few cuts that are probably caused by smashed glasses. By now, the indian guy has helped me to call the ambulance. Within 2 minutes after she crawled out, there were already a group of motorcyclists stopping and helping out, some going to her car to retrieve some of her stuff.

     

    Within 5 mintues, more people came and well, as usual, kapo abit. There was one guy like very professional with accident scene. He helped to stop the car alarm although the car was turn turtle and smashed in front. He also helped to cleared the debris to allow other cars to move. There were 3 lane and her car was smacked in the middle.

     

    Soon, ambulance (civil defence) and her parents came... With a worried look on her mother face, I really emphatised with her mother how she feel at the time. I also pity that the girl has to go through this trauma. Actually, i was stunned and shaking when her car landed in front of my van and also when she borrowed my phone.

     

    I hoped she is fine and will recover well.
