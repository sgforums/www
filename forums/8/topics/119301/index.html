    Premier Taxis chairman fined $1,000 for road rage act

    SINGAPORE : The Chairman of Premier Taxis was fined $1,000 in the Subordinate Courts on Monday, escaping a jail term for his road rage act.

    It was all smiles as Timothy Chua, 37, came out of the Subordinate Courts accompanied by family members and lawyers.

    In her judgment, Magistrate Ngoh Siew Yen said while there usually was a need for a custodial sentence for road hooliganism, Chua's case was different.

    She said his act was not premeditated and he did not use a weapon.

    She added that he had also shown genuine remorse over his actions.

    Chua attacked a motorist after a traffic collision along Bukit Timah Road in May 2003.
    The judgement came as a surprise to even Chua and his family.

    Before that, Chua was preparing himself for a possible jail term and when the magistrate passed the judgement, Chua's sister broke down in court and wept tears of joy while Chua himself heaved a sigh of relief.

    Subhas Anandan, Chua's lawyer, said: "I am quite happy, very happy, with the sentence and told my client to forget about the whole thing and start anew and I believe that's what he's going to do. He's going to take a holiday and forget the whole thing.....I am very happy for his mother. She was very worried and she's old and not well."

    The Attorney-General's Chambers had earlier called for a deterrent sentence.

    It is not known if they would appeal against the judgement. - CNA
