Compared to Singapore its much much cheaper. For Example, a nice trolley-full of grocery shopping would come around 100 Drihams (S$45).

Waited 10 hrs at Brunei int'l airport for transit. Took an afternoon nap there before leaving for Dubai in another 7hrs flight. Plane fare cost S$600 two way flight.

After 6pm onwards, the weather is terrific for outing.If you can withstand Singapore weather its no problem down here. Lucky its not humid like in Singapore.Hehehe.     The city is developing. Its a shopping and food paradise over here. Plenty of ultra-modern huge shopping malls with huge cineplexes. Some of them has about 6-7 cinemas in each. The big ones comparable to Suntec city. There are 5 of them. There are 5 big carrefours here. Said to be Carrefours' third biggest around the world.
    Yesterday we went to see the nightlife in Dubai. The most happening place in the Gulf with plenty and plenty of pubs,bars,clubs,discos. This is where Singapore lose hands down. The entertainment scene is said to be changing everything. They got almost everything -pop,R&B. funk, rock, jazz, trip-hop, etc etc. 2 nites ago we even went to the famous cyclone club. There are 200 hookers from all over the world at that big nightclub. Extremely pretty Arab hookers there with round bosom (felt so soft when touched) and voluptous body with porcelian looking skin. Even PRCs,Malaysian and Singaporeans hookers (only 3) are there. Too bad wasnt allow to take photos inside. Shit.Damn.
    Yesterday morning we went to the Gold Souk (Markets). Its huge. Took some pictures there too.
    In the evening while going along Sheik Zayed Road to Dubai biggest shopping mall called Ibin Battuta, saw many projects still under development. Saw two mega projects which will provide ski and snow related activities. The first one is the Mall of the Emirates (Ski Dubai) which is due to operate in September 2005 and the second is the Dubai Sunny Mountain Ski Dome, due in 2006. Both these Malls will include huge shopping malls. Ski Dubai is a great engineering feat, a 25-storey structure and the Middle East's only indoor ski resort and the world's third largest.
    The mountain-themed resort is being built within the billion-dollar Mall of the Emirates, this will be the third largest shopping centre in the world after the Dubai Mall( which 5 times the size of Suntec) and the Mall of Arabia which will be at Dubailand (One gigantic Mall with theme parks the size of Orchard Road + Raffles place + City Hall + Bugis) both of which are still under construction. So the world number 1,2 and 3 shopping mall will be in Dubai.

    Also passed by the Dubai Marina which a city with the city and also the Dubai Waterfront which is a conglomeration of canals and islands studded with luxury hotels and homes. Larger than the island of Manhattan in New York. It is expected to house 400,000 people and transform the last stretch of Dubai's undeveloped Gulf seashore. You will see plenty of Skyscrapers still under construction. Expected to be finished next year or the year 2007. The development and construction is very fast over here in Dubai.

    Also pasted through the Dubai Media City as well as the Dubai Internet City which houses IT companies such as Microsoft, IBM, Oracle, etc etc etc.

    Finally arrived at Ibn Battuta Mall, the Middle East region's largest themed shopping experience and probably Dubai's biggest currently. Not to sure which is bigger Dubai Deira City Centre or this. But one thing is for sure. Its as bigger as Suntec City or even bigger.5-6 Malls combined into one (Chinese Mall,India Mall,Egyptian Mall,Persian Mall etc.)
    The mall houses a 21 screen Megaplex and the region's first IMAX experience all built to international standards with cutting edge entertainment technology, including automated cinema, wall-to-wall screens, stadium seats, full digital surround sound and an ultra stylish lobby. Took 4 hrs to visit walking the whole mall. Hehehe.
