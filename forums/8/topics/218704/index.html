    And then, after you get used to the assault on your senses, you realise that things don't get any better. Try browsing movie showtimes and you are asked to register. Why would any webmaster want you to register to browse movie showtimes? Again, that is not a rhetorical qn because I really want to know. Coz, you know, I thought websites were supposed to help a company draw customers as opposed to driving them away. Perhaps there is a method to this madness that my puny mind has not yet been able to grasp. So webmaster, get in touch!

    Anyway...So, you survive the assault on your sense, you've calmed down enough to register (and you're too stupid to go to yahoo movies instead) and you try to book a ticket using the website. That is when your computer will start emitting odd noises and your browser becomes non-responsive. Yes. They are using Java. Again, WTF??!!

    So let's recap: The web design hasn't changed in years, it looks like it has been made by a 12 years old, it pisses you off and it well it pisses you off somemore. The qn every sane man would be asking himself is "why dun use i www.gv.com.sg instead of this sorry excuse of a webpage?"

    My belief that the world is full of retards is, yet again, affirmed. Good bloody morning everyone. I think this is going to be a beautiful day.

    PS: Webmaster, if case you can't be arsed to read the rant above, the conclusion is this: www.shaw.com.sg is about as useful as a chocolate teapot.

    Me understand your problem...

    Eng Wah Online system is using a decentralised booking readback system, which means the booking isn't centralised at the server but at their various cinema locations...

    As the system is active inline with their over the counter sales, the application is very resource-extensive as it will fight the processing power and strains on the processor in the server...

    When the server is "generating and processing" your request, other factors like over the counter sales, website browsing and simultaneous online booking up to 10 users allowed, will create a bottleneck in the dataflow, thus sometimes creating scenarios of double booking, non-registration of payment transaction through the payment gateway, non-issuance of booking even with payments...

    Different Eng Wah cinema locations use different bandwidth for the ADSL service, including the main server host... Thus there's another bottleneck created from the data distribution at the main server...

    Hope this information helps..
