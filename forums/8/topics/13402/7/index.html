    Is Christianity the spiritual fountainhead of western civilisation? A faith that is apparently based on transcendental ethics? Of course it is, but then, there is no greater error than to consider western civilisation as an outcome of Christianity. The real intellectual foundations of the modern west are to be found in the old Roman conception of life as a purely utilitarian structure without any transcendental considerations. Indeed, it can be expressed as such: "Since we do not know anything definite-that is, provable by means of scientific experiments and calculations, about the origin of human life and its desitiny after death, it is better to concentrate all our energies on the development of our material and intellectual possibilities without allowing ourselves to be hampered by religious ethics and moral issues based on assumptions which defy scientific proof."

    Throughout long centuries, the spirit of Europe was oppressed by a religious system that embodied the contempt of human nature. To ascribe, therefore, the practical achievements of modern western civilisation to Christian teachings is not accurate. Christianity has contributed very little to the powerful scientific and material development in which the present western civilisation of the west excels all others. Indeed, these achievements rose out of Europe's age-long intellectual fight against the Church and its historical hostility towards science. The history of the Middle Ages is full of the bitter struggle between the genuis of Europe and the Church.

    Galileo was forced, on pain of life imprisonment and later, exile, to recant his views that the Earth orbited around the sun. Church authorities at that time believed otherwise because of a verse in the Bible:

    "So the sun stood still, and the moon stopped, Until the nation avenged themselves of their enemies. Is it not written in the book of Jashar? And the sun stopped in the middle of the sky and did not hasten to go down for about a whole day." (RSV Bible, Joshua 10:13)

    The ancient Library of Alexandria, amongst whose ruins were found the practical designs for the building of space ships, was burnt down by a mob very much motivated by priests who condemned the scientific blasphemy that was promoted by the library.

    In America, parents have consistently lobbied to block all teaching about natural selection and the theory of evolution in schools, while almost all scientists in that great country hold the theory to be absolute truths.

    I admire the faith and dedication of Christians, and I have the greatest of respect for my Christian brothers and sisters.

    "...nearest among them in love to the believers wilt thou find those who say, 'We are Christians': because amongst these are men devoted to learning and men who have renounced the world, and they are not arrogant." (The Quran, 5:82)

    but objectivity should always be the rule of the day.
