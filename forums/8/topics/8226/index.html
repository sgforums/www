    weekends and weeknights, my days were spent exploring the kampongside, catching spiders, playing marbles, climbing trees, flying kites in the kampong, tagging after Ah Leh, carrying paper lanterns on Mid Autum Festivals, lantern fighting, free rambutans when in season, Sunday school from outside the window ...

    oh yeah, what i missed most about my childhood is the wide open spaces of the camp grounds and kampong.

    Angeldark, said I would support u, so here is my contribution.

    Well, most of my childhood was also spent in a kampong, my mum's hometown in Negri Sembilan. And yes, chicken, ducks, and the animal that lingers most in my mind, the dogs. I was so scared of them when I was young. Those days were carefree. When I woke up early in the morning, I was let to go free to just do my what my heart desires, those were the days of absolute freedom. Was part of this gang in the village, and we used to terrorise the girls there, hahaha, and also in our freetime, we would just cycle around, with no destinations in our minds.

    Occasionally, we would go play among the grass patches, which although we were warned about snakes and things like that but we just defied authority anyhow, a habit which I brought along with me even uptil this date, hahaha. I still remember all the cool stuff which we used to be able to catch, the various species of grasshoppers, (actually i don't know if we called it correctly, but used to call it things like butterfly hoppers, lion hoppers, but we could be wrong, and honestly, no one was there to correct us and we honestly didn't care either).

    Well, I was told by my mum that a few days I was brought back home after I was born, she left me lying on the floor near a door. It happened that the wind blew and the door slammed against my head and I was bleeding profusely (*ouch* that must have hurt you might say, well, I have no recollections of that though), so from the time I knew how to speak, the recital of Tang poems was my bedtime duty. My mum will read to me the poems and I would be able to recite every single line without fail to my mum and so proud was I. Even my sis couldn't boast of such memory, hehehe...... By the time I was 5 or 6, I could recite any of the 300 Tang poems with a click of my fingers, that was how good I was. Unfortunately, as the guy grew older, so did his memory. The only poems I remember now is the one about the moon in front of the bed, and of course the one which so nicely describes my relationship with my mum.

    I guess when I was in Primary school (here in Singapore of course), my reputation wasn't exactly that great. I used to do things like throwing people's bags into the drain, hanging out with those cool guys from Primary 7 and 8, loitering around the ex-Goldhill Square. Sighz, those were the days. It was fun, and if I had a chance to relive my childhood, I wouldn't change a single thing.

We were asked to move out when I was 8 but I still went back with Grandma to take a look occasionally after everyone had moved. By then, the once lively kampong was hanging with silence. Gone were the children's laughter.....our neighbours' constant bicker.... the barber who lived next-door.....the swing that was our parents' saviour during dinner time (the kids will only take their meals when on the swing!) and LOTSA more..... I think our kampong was one of the LATEST that was evacuated and pulled down and I am glad that I had spent my childhood there. Nowadays, as I take a ride down to Mandai zoo or Seletar Reservoir, I only see a patch of wilderness where the kampong once was. It saddens me but well, life goes on and all I can do is to cherish those memories... clinging on..... :p
