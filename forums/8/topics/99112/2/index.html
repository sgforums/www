    What could have happened to Huang Na?
    By Dawn Chia
    dawnchia@sph.com.sg

    LITTLE Huang Na was seen in a blue denim jacket and bermudas at the Pasir Panjang Wholesale Centre 11 days ago. She was smiling and seemed happy.

    Then, the 9-year-old girl, who came to Singapore from China with her mother about 18 months ago, vanished.

    The police are intensifying their search for the girl, and the Criminal Investigation Department has joined the Clementi Police Division to work on the case.

    Her mother, Madam Huang Shu Ying, has been frantically combing various areas - Sentosa, HDB estates, Bukit Timah hill, Mount Faber - to find the Primary 2 girl.

    FAMILY PROBLEMS?

    Private investigator (PI) Harmon Singh said interviews have to be done with the girl's relatives (even those in China), classmates, teachers, neighbours and even the school cleaners because 'you never know who'll point you in the right direction'.

    Mr Lionel de Souza, who was a police officer for about 26 years before starting his own PI firm, said it's crucial to find out what the relationship between the parents is like.

    'We have to establish whether the parents are estranged, and if the child is trapped between them,' he said.

    Madam Huang declined to give us her husband's contact number in China, as she does not want to 'further worry him'.

    But she said there are no problems with her husband or relatives.

    The police have taken away the girl's passport as part of their investigations.

    A Chinese Embassy official told The New Paper that they have asked Xiamen officials to process a visa for Huang Na's father.

    Mr Wang Yong Li said: 'We met Madam Huang on Monday, and she expressed her wish to have her husband here with her, so we're helping to expedite the visa application. We're also keeping in touch with her every day, to assist in any way.'

    HELD BY A CRIMINAL?

    Mr Singh, who was also with the police force for 21 years, doesn't rule out the possibility that Huang Na may be held by a sex offender or a mentally ill person.

    He said: 'Checks have to be done on people who have been in the girl's company - whether they have a mental record or have been convicted sex offenders.'

    Huang Na has been described by those who know her as 'cute, childish and clever'.

    She was a familiar figure in the Pasir Panjang Wholesale Centre as she would wander around alone while her mother helped out at one of the vegetable stalls.

    Private investigators say it's unlikely that she has been abducted for ransom, as there have been no demands from any kidnappers.

    $10,000 REWARD

    Retired businessman Yeo Aik Seng, 60, is offering a $10,000 reward to anyone with information which can help find the girl.

    He told The New Paper in Mandarin: 'I'm not related to them in any way, but my wife and I... feel very sorry for the mother.'

    Anyone with information can contact senior investigation officer Loong Yew Jeong on 6774-0000 or 6872-7902.

    Saturday October 23, 10:17 PM
    Over 120 volunteers join in islandwide search for missing girl Huang Na
    The search for 8-year-old Huang Na has mobilised many Singaporeans.
    Over 120 volunteers handed out 7,000 flyers at coffeeshops, supermarkets and malls in the Central and Western parts of the island.

    It has almost become a movement as volunteers have expanded the search to include 5 other missing people.

    One volunteer Johann Yao and his team are hard at work from early in the morning.

    They are trekking an 11-kilometre route in MacRitchie Reservoir distributing flyers and literally leaving no stone unturned.

    The China-born girl has been missing for nearly 2 weeks and he empathises with her mother's anguish.

    "I actually feel how she feels as my daughter disappears in shopping centres and I do get frantic looking for her so that's why I called up and organised this search party for Huang Na and the 5 missing persons," said Johann.

    Taxi driver Loy is also distributing flyers to help the mother.

    He said: "I think we should help her to cut down her finding time."

    Other groups took to HDB estates and malls in Toa Payoh, Clementi, Hougang, Sengkang, and Bukit Batok.

    "So bus stops, petrol kiosks or any of the shops that you come across just go inside and distribute it to them," said one volunteer.

    "I think that these 6 persons missing are quite pitiful so I and my wife came from Jurong West to distribute flyers and let more people know about these missing persons and hope to find all of them," said another.

    The man behind the search effort believes that ordinary citizens have the power to help.

    He claims 2 people have called him to say they saw Ah Hao - or Took Leng How - in Johore.

    The police are searching for Took in connection with Huang Na's disappearance.

    Joseph Tan, Head, Volunteer Effort, said: "Though we're not professionals we are plain simple ordinary citizens we believe that our efforts and our hearts are not wasted though the police has to depend on information received from the man on the ground. We need volunteers we need their legs, their hands, their time, their efforts and their hearts to actually reach out to more shops and designated areas to create more awareness for these missing people."

    His efforts have not gone unnoticed - he received a call today from a mother who said her 14-year-old daughter Sophia Sim has been missing for 8 days.

    About 100 concerned members of the public registered to be part of future search efforts and with this new pool of volunteers, organisers are hoping to conduct yet another search effort for these missing persons next weekend.

    Those interested in joining the search effort can phone the organisers at 6293 5250. - CNA
