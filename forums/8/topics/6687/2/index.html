    millennium, you asked for figures. Have you looked them up yourself? Anyway, here they are.

    Average crew complement for each ship type :
    Nimitz-class CVN - 5000
    Ticonderoga-class CG - 380-387
    Arleigh Burke-class DDG - 320-380
    Spruance-class DD - 320-350

    Yes, we can man one or perhaps even a few of these smaller ships like I've mentioned before, at a very high price, of course. There'd be no lack of volunteers for such plum assignments, but it makes no practical sense to do so. These ships perform functions we do not need, now or in the near future.

    I don't believe I need to elaborate on the profound lack of need for an aircraft carrier. The CG and DDG basically serve as guardians of the fleet, usually centered around a carrier, which we don't have. Their weapons systems are specifically designed to counter MASSED air and missile attacks, both of which our neighbours aren't capable of. They are also platforms for Tomahawk missile launchers, which we don't need, to say the least. Finally, the Harpoon and anti-submarine roles can be filled by our small fleet, not to the same extent that these ships can, but nonetheless more than adequate for our needs. Anything else I missed?

    As mentioned earlier both by myself and Mr Hata-san, costs are astronomical. Maintenance on a squadron of these ships, weapons systems upgrades, R&D, plus technical staff expertise all cost a bomb. We are talking tens of billions at the outset, with plenty more to follow in successive years. At such costs, there's got to be a compelling justification for the purchase. I can find none.

    If we're just going to have a huge cruiser bristling with weapons patrolling the Straits of Singapore, then it looks plain stupid. Until the day when Singapore decides to spend a significant chunk of its reserves to flex its naval muscles in the world, we'd do just fine with our existing fleet.

    Why the need for submarines? Perhaps we are moving towards blue-water fleet capability as Mr Hata-san has suggested. Also, consider what submarines can do that surface ships can't...
