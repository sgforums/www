    ok guys, listen up, time for some lectures on the defence capability of Singapore and her forward planning strategy.

    When Singapore achieved independence in august 1965, she was a small and vulnerable state with no natural resources and military capability. All matters of defence was under the control of Britain, Singapore's ex-colonial master. Initially, matters of defence can be depended on Britain for help. However, with the speech by Britain in the late 1960s that they are withdrawing troops south of suez, Singapore's government was in a lost...how is this small country defend herself with just 2 infantry regiments and nothing else. Clearly, drastic steps need to be taken.
    National Service was the option the PAP government decides on. It was clear that as a small and economically weak country (back in the early days of post-independence),Singapore can't afford a army consisting of regular and professional troops.The scheme was meet with hostile reaction from the Chinese population as they believe that "good man do not become soliders". However, due to govt. propaganda and tactics, the bill was passed without protest and the first batch of civilains were inducted into the 1st Singapore Infantry Regiment

    From there on, there was no looking back. With professional and war veterans advisors from Israel, the NS programm got off to a good start. Within a few years later, the Republic of Singapore Navy and Air Force begans to take form and the small republic was on her way to having a military capability capable of holding their own against her closest neighbour of Malaysia and Indonesia.

    From 1965 to 1990, the Singapore Armed Forces have grow from strength to strength and was believed to have numbered 25 000 personnels. From a purely consripted army, the SAF became more professionalised and the reservist troops were maintained at operational readiness throught regular in-camp training and mobolization excerises. In terms of manpower and quality, the SAF proved herself capable of taking on the best in the world as evidenced by the numerous excerise she had with the United States Marines, the Australian Army and fellow ASEAN countries.

    In terms of technical hardware, the SAF proved itself a leap above her neighbours with the acqusition of superior and modern weapons. For the Army, the standard rifle of every infantryman of M-16 is upgraded to the SAR-16, a rather revolutionary weapon that suits the climate and the average Singaporean males. We also see the birth of the Bionix, a locally produced armoured carrier that boosted better firepower and mobility than the her precessor. More and more, we are seeing the birth of the 21st century soldier in which information technology is brought to the battleground.

    We see more hardware upgrading in the Air Force and Navy. As mentioned by the writers in the forum before me, the RSAF and the RSN has brought and upgraded their planes and ships respectively. For more information regarding their recent purchases, please refer to the previous thread.

    In terms of defence doctrines, the SAF has shifted from a "poisonous shrimp" mentality of fighting (in which we will lose but we will make u pay dearly before we capitulate) to a "swift and decisive victory" dogma.The SAF has grown more confident of his ability to tackle outside aggression and believe in taking the war to his enemy. A few good examples of this would be the planting of rubber and palm trees in Pulau Tekong and the various armoured excercies conducted in the plains of Shoal Bay.

    To further defend Singapore's soverignity and territorial integrity, the SAF must continue to be a notch above her neighbours and acquire the latest in technology...for manpower is a premium in Singapore. With the most modern weapon and the most motivated and trained troops in the SAF, Singapore can be assured of a "swift and decisive" victory if war ever comes.
