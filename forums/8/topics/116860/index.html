    whats your definiation of gd music ? its more of the CD recording label and the artiste themselves, most pop artistes nowadays are just pretty boring stuff.i think your refering towards the recordings, and how do the artistes actually produce their particular albums right ?

    it really depends on your personnal preferences though, much of the run of the mill pop CDs lilke those along HMV level 1 and 2 have medicore recordings.

    If your looking for voice recordings, you could try the Jazz selection in HMV, people like ella filtzgerald, neil armstrong are gd starters. For the pure jazzy feel, Miles Davis, Wynton Marsaillis along with Quincy Jones is gd stuff.

    Jumping to the classical section,if you ever like classical, go for CD labels like Deutsche Grammophon , Decca or the Philips Label. Their recording for the music are fabulous. For listening to the strains and the depth of your system, try Herbert von Karajan's Beethoven's 9 Symphonies from 1963. Deutsche Grammophon remastered in CD format and it was remasterd from the original vinly recordings hence the authencity and crackling of the vinyl play.

    To listen if your system is all set up with the tune, u opt for some new age CDs from Jean Michel Jarre's Oxygen Series, thats gd stuff. The greek composer Vangelis is also a gd selection, especially with his earlier work and recordings other than his already over played Chariots of Fire

    Lastly to listen to the overall system try piano recordings, pianos have one of the highest frequenices in the instrument range. Ryuichi Sakamoto and George Winston are gd selection as well from these genre of music.

    But if your still miss your gd old strings and drums, Dire Straits offers a blend of rock and soulful music , coupled by mark knopfler's rather slow and abstract strings of his guitar.

    All the best in starting in your foray into Hi Fi systems. Don't try to buy 1 componet, only to upgrade to a better one when u have the money, most of the modern systems work the same. Just eye ball the component you want to use and buy it.

    Its the same with cameras, choose a good system, learn its features and maiximise on its capabilities, its not the equipment, but the setup
