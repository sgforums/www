    Hi Obigood.

    I offer my advice.

    Inheritance tax or estate duty is tax on a deceased person's estate. Estate is assets left behind to their family when a person dies. Estate taxes is a distribution of wealth tax to 'rob the rich and aid the poor'.

    Estate including your CPF balance which is worth less than $600,000 will be totally exempted from estate duty.

    If your CPF balance exceeds $600,000, estate in excess of $600,000 will be exempted.

    A special exemption is your residential dwellings. Exemption is given to residential dwellings valued at $12 million or less. Even if you have a sprawling bungalow worth $11 million, you don't have to pay a single cent of tax.

    Estate duty is 5% on the first $12 million and 10% on the taxable balance.

    It is important to do estate planning when a person dies asset rich and cash poor. This is because the family do not have access to the estate until they pay up the estate duty.

    A person dying without a will be deemed as dying intestate and his assets will be distributed according to the written law. In your situation, i believe, your nok is only your parents, so all your assets will be shared equally between your parents.

    If you have wife and children, your wife and children will each get 50% each. Your parents will get nothing.

    If you have no children, your parents and wife will get 50% each.

    The advantages of writing a will are:

    1. To ensure distribution of wealth according to your wishes.

    2. Quicker administration.

    3. Avoid dispute by appointment of an independent executor to administer your estate.

    As a result to changes to an act, the will is no longer deemed to be a legal document and a lawyer is no longer required to draft a will.
