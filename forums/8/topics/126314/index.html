    Many people don't have problems with the Japanese culture per se. The Japanese work ethics is admirable, their dedication to quality is laudable.

    The bone of contention is not with the culture, however. The motive behind the Chinese and Korean boycott is a protest against the adamant refusal of the Japanese to acknowledge the atrocities committed by their expansionist-minded forefathers during World War II. It is indeed time to forgive and many would want to forgive, but forgiveness is meaningless and redundant when the perpetrators do not acknowlege the wrongs committed.

    Chinese: "We forgive you..."
    Japanese: "Forgive us for what? We deserve gratitude, not forgiveness."

    It is debatable whether the bombs dropped at Hiroshima and Nagasaki were acts of retribution. Many historians, especially Americans, consider the bombs as a desperate measure to bring the proud, rather-die-than-surrender Japanese to their knees, to stop the war from prolonging any further. It is possible that without the two bombs in 1945, Singaporeans would be studying their textbooks in Japanese today.
