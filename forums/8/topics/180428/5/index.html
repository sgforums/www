    The principal is wrong to say that he/she doesn't want to hear any parent complaints. Students are enrolled in schools, whereby teachers and ultimately the principal are answerable to the parents for the student's education, discipline, and not to mention welfare and well-being.

    That being said, parents have the right to know what has happened to their child in school, and report to the relevant authorities, should they find that there has been misdeeds by the school.

    Often students/children might not know what is regarded as wrong-doings (or "mismanagements") by the school, and cases like theses may never see the light of day. Hence I advise you to talk to your parents and let them decide on the appropriate action.

    In such a situation, the head of the school is involved, hence, complaints may have to be directed towards a higher level. However, it would be good for your parents to talk to your principal to see what are his/her reasons for such harsh physcal punishment.
