    HE was downing beer at his neighbourhood coffeeshop when word spread that police officers had surrounded a flat to arrest a burglar.

    It didn't dawn on him it was his own flat being raided and the 'burglar' had actually been let in by his maid.

    On Jan 26, at about 11pm, more than 10 policemen surrounded the ground-floor flat at Block 115, Lorong 1 Toa Payoh.

    The owner, Mr Eng Lai Tee, 50, was drinking with friends at a nearby coffeeshop when word spread that a suspected burglar had entered the flat.

    The police had received a call saying a man had climbed into the flat through the back window.

    Mr Eng, who is single and spoke in Mandarin, said: 'I was quite shocked. At the coffeeshop, some people were talking about the police, but I didn't expect it to happen at my flat.'

    His next-door-neighbour, Mr Chan Cho, 73, was stunned by the presence of the police and said: 'The maid refused to open the door. She said she didn't have the keys. I immediately called the owner of the flat on his handphone.'

    When Mr Eng got the message, he dashed home.

    After he unlocked the door, the police caught and handcuffed a Bangladeshi man.

    But here's the twist.

    They later released the man because he was not a thief but 'a friend' of the maid.

    When The New Paper spoke to the maid, Ms Mina, 26, said in Bahasa Indonesia the man was a cleaner working in the area.

    The maid, who has worked for Mr Eng for about 10 months, claimed: 'I don't know why he came.'

    She said she has a husband and child in Indonesia, and added she did not have many friends as she was not given any days off.

    Until that incident, Mr Eng said he was not very strict with his maid because he felt she was good at her household duties and at looking after his mother, who had suffered a stroke. He pays her $230 every month.

    Mr Eng said the maid had since apologised to him.

    He added: 'I can't take another chance since she dared to ask people to come to my house. I'm applying for another maid.'

    A police spokesman told The New Paper they have looked into the matter and are not taking further action.
