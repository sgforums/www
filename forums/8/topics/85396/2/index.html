    The New Paper - 20 Jul 2004

    Ah Jie pulls out, but gets 40,000 calls

    By Yeoh Wee Teck
    weeteck@sph.com.sg



    SHE was supposed to be the cherry on the top of a human ladder pyramid - the grand finale of last night's charity show in aid of the NKF Children's Medical Fund.

    But just two hours before the show, MediaCorp Ah Jie Zoe Tay (left, with Sharon Au) dropped out.

    She had already rehearsed for the item but, last Monday, was advised by doctors not to go on.

    Apparently, Zoe was suffering from a persistent back ailment.

    At the last minute, gung-ho Sharon Au was called upon to stand in for her.

    Though visibly nervous, Sharon executed the stunt to perfection (see picture, right), eliciting heaps of praise from a grateful Zoe.

    But stunt or no stunt, the Queen of Caldecott still managed to rake in the money.

    When she made her appeal on television, 40,000 calls were made.

    Zoe, 36, looked bewildered when told of the figure.

    'Is it?' she asked.

    She said she had worked hard during rehearsals.

    'My body was aching all over. I had to use heat pads all over my thighs to relax my muscles.

    'Maybe next year then.'

    When we suggested she host next year's show, Zoe declined.

    'I don't think I'll be suitable. I'm very emotional and if I start crying, I don't want people to think I'm playing with their emotions too.'

    The plight of the sick children tore at many hearts last night, taking donations to $1.03 million - $10,000 more than last week's show.

    But the show was not without its funny moments - like the one where two of MediCorp's 'biggest' stars took to the air.

    The Bouncing Chef and his Lady had the audience in stitches - and in awe. Would the fragile-looking harness hold all 120kg of the rotund Moses Lim?

    It didn't help that the actor had had just one rehearsal - on Friday - before taking the stage for real.

    The audience held their breath as Moses, 54, hurtled towards the studio ceiling.

    Then it was Chen Liping's turn - on a bungee cord.

    Asked if he was going to do it again, Moses joked: 'Let's not make it a habit!'

    Liping - who said she weighed less than Moses but refused to reveal her exact weight - said she couldn't remember when was the last time she was so wired.

    The 36-year-old star said: 'It was not as easy as it looked - I had to stretch a lot and my legs and arms are aching.'

    But she said she is game to go for it again next year, to which Moses retorted: 'I didn't say anything!'

    Funny man Mark Lee raked in nearly 260,000 calls in four acts - the most by one artiste that night.

    Said Mark, who was having a late dinner when we called him just past midnight: 'For the first time in two weeks, I'm having a good meal!'

    But in his typical style, he joked about his efforts.

    'I'm 36, but still can tahan lah!'

    --------------------------------------------------------------------------------
    YUMMY COAL DISH FOR JACK

    THANKFULLY, the night went by with no major mishaps.

    Jack Neo, however, burnt the inside of his left cheek from swallowing 10 sticks of fire and chewing on hot coals.

    'I was rinsing my mouth, when I realized it was smarting. It was then I knew I was burnt,' he said. 'My tongue is numb now.'

    Jack, 44, said he also singed his lips and burnt his chin during a rehearsal two weeks ago. And, according to him, there really is no skill involved in the act.

    'There is no technique to this, just getting the courage to put it into your mouth.

    'I was only worried that the kerosene might drip into my eyes, but luckily I was wearing my glasses,' he said.

    Kids, don't try this at home!
