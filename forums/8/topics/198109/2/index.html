  • oxford mushroom
    2 Jul 06, 19:51
    Sounds like a vascular anomaly that ruptured...cerebral aneurysms and arteriovenous malformations in the brain are rare abnormalities of blood vessels in the brain. When they rupture, the mortality is very high. Thankfully, they are rare but occasionally seen associated with conditions like polycystic kidney disease and Marfan's syndrome. Quite a number of victims tend to be very young though. The typical story is a young person who wakes up with a sudden terrible headache and lose consciousness soon after. The prognosis is very poor once the vessel has ruptured.

    The difficulty with diagnosis is because these conditions tend to be silent until they rupture. The only way to diagnose them is a brain scan and angiography may be required in some cases in addition.

    Some cases give a 'warning bleed' before full rupture and the only symptom is headache, especially associated with neck stiffness. However, everyone gets headache from time to time and it is not logical to scan everyone with a headache. Not only is the cost high but there are risks with the procedure as well. Rarely, some patients die from an allergic reaction to the dye used for the scan. So doctors must not bow to pressure from the ignorant public and simply scan everyone with a headache. You will kill more people that way than save the very few with this rare condition.

    The management is correct in this case. Give painkillers and see if the headache goes away. If the pain is persistent as it appears to be in this case, make an appointment to see a neurologist. There is nothing in the story that indicates a dangerous vascular malformation, which is, after all, rare. Unfortunately the young man happens to be one of the very few with this condition. Had he presented with an epileptic fit, he would have had the scan immediately but no, he only had the common headache.

    A non-urgent appointment to see a neurologist for persistent headache in two weeks is very reasonable. In the UK, you would be lucky to see one in 3 months' time.
