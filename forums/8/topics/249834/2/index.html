    I would like to say that everything usually begins from monopoly. And then it proceeds on to other forms of markets. Like oligopolys, competitive markets and so on.
    The telecom market began with monopoly (Singtel). The television too was monopoly (mediacorp). Bus services was monopoly. MRT was monopoly. English Newspaper was monopoly. Do correct me if I am wrong =) But as u can see, they eventually progressed into competitive markets. And now we have affordable internet, hp, fixed line services.
    Monopoly is indeed unhealthy for customer but when he isn't left with a choice he can't do much then DEMAND for better prices and more companies offering the same services.
    Do you think Starhub is being very advanced in having its smart tv consoles and flashy interface? Trust me, its about time they provided in services. In India, there are services which are far more advanced then that of Starhub's and they cost a fraction of the cost. In the end customers benefit and the market is also making money.
    Customers are MEANT to be picky and demand things. Thats the customer's job. You don't expect a customer to go "Oh?! Only $100 for this? Too cheap la! I really appreciate it! Can I pay $110 instead?" Cheap or not, customer WILL demand the same product for $90 as long as he can say money.
    And by the way, comparing Vietnam's library system (or any other system) to that of Singapore's is totally ridiculous. Singapore is a microcosm in itself. You have keep in mind about how small Singapore is and thus how easy it is to maintain. Lets say you have 50 libraries in Singapore. Going by the size, Veitnam should probably have 5000.
