    Recommended Salt Consumption for an Adult

    The recommended daily allowance is no more than 2000mg of sodium a day, which is equivalent to a teaspoon of salt or 5-6 teaspoons of soya sauce.

    Average Salt Consumption of Singaporeans

    Based on the National Nutrition Survey 1998, the average sodium intake of Singaporean adults (aged 18- 69 years) is 3527mg each day. The Chinese have a higher sodium intake than the Malays and Indians and those aged 18 to 39 years have a higher sodium diet than their older counterparts. The higher sodium intake among young adults and among the Chinese could be due to these individuals being more likely to add salt or sauces to their food at the table.

    The 1998 survey also showed that more Singaporean adults (59%) are adding salt/sauces at the table as compared to 31% in 1993. There was also an increase in the hospital admission rates for stroke between these two years.
