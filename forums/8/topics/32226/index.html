    I work from 6am to 8pm everyday other than monday selling laksa at Bukit Batok Central. Between 12 to 2pm I am busier because the workers from the nearby factories will come out for lunch and my wife wouldn't be around to help because she has to send our 8 year old child to school.

    What's life like as a hawker? The same as everyone else. I work to make ends meet. Pay off the bank loan for my flat, monthly instalments for the van and make sure I sell enough to cover the store's rent. At the end of the month, we usually have around $400 to $500 that will go into boy boy's education fund.

    At the end of each year my wife and I check the balance off our account. With the surplus, we bring boy boy to a country nearby for holidays.

    I knew I was going to live this way long before I started selling laksa. My dad lived like that. So do most my friends. If you are studying now and wonder where you will be in 10 years, then this is the answer. You may be selling laksa, operating a crane, writing softwares or even managing a team of salesperson. Your flat might be bigger than mine. You might drive a Nissan Sunny rather than my crummy old van, and you might go to Paris instead of Bali for your holiday but it wouldn't differ a lot....
