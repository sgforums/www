    Because he haven't stepped down. If this letter is written after he has stepped down, everything would be in past tense.

    For point 4, note this:

    Any sentence that has these words (to, could, will, would, can, can't, wouldn't, couldn't, may, might), the verb will always in present tense.

    For example, I want to buy a can of Milo.
    I would like to request for a transfer.

    If the word "be" is added like to be (verb), it would be in past participle.

    For example, XYZ company will be bought over by ABC company.

    For point 5, Singapore's judicical system is still here. It's not as if we have merged with another country. If we have merged and our old system is being studied by other countries, it will be "was studied".

    many ppl are confused over the usage of have and had, eg. you have done/you had done. if the thing remains done till present, 'have' is used. on the other hand 'had' is used to show something was done but not anymore in the present.

    for instance you just go out of the house, you will say, "i have locked the door." (since the door remains locked)

    when you return home you will say, "i had locked the door (when i went out)."

    i had been a bad boy last time. (not a bad boy now)
    i have been a bad boy since young. (still a bad boy)
