    I assume you are talking about getting a degree in Engineering Vs a degree in business management.

    I would say you have a better chance of making more money with business management than engineering. If you are thinking of working for a firm just look at the starting pay as well as career advancement of engineers and business grads. Business grads earn tons of money at a much younger age, attain key positions faster, compared to engineers who start with a low pay and have to work for many years to reach the same level. In many cases engineer's pay just cant match those of business grads, even after tons of experience and attaining key positions.

    And forget about the old saying "upgrade your skills". That’s just shitty nonsense that the local media keeps spouting out as if it’s the cure for all problems. Whatever skill you upgrade there will be someone from India or china with even better skills willing to work for a lower salary than yours. In the end you just end up paying for upgrading your skill that won’t serve you at all.

    Even if you have plans to start your own firm, a business degree, coupled with all the business contacts and networking that you would have developed during studies or during work experience, would put you in a better position to be successful than an engineer.
