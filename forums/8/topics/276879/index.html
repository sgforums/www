    Generally the criminal law is used to respond to social issues.

    The reasons for criminalizing certain conducts can be split into two broad categories; malum in se and malum prohibita, they mean acts wrong in themselves and acts that are wrong because they are prohibited.

    The use of safety belts are quite clearly the latter. It is a response to the social wellbeing of the people. the response might have come from the people, with post-accident groups saying they would have worn a seat belt if they were told to, thus reducing the severity of their injuries. In Singapore, it is more likely that the law was introduced to bring it in line with the rest of the deveoped world.

    As for the argument that the invasion of private rights are for the benefit of the State, it is most clearly seen in the criminal offence of assualt. It used to be that if both parties consented to the fight, neither would be criminally liable. However, the State stepped in with the reasoning that maiming a person would deprive the King of a capable person in defence of the Crown.

    Also, the criminalzing of certain behaviour, such as sexual acts and euthanasia is seen to be a way of protecting the moral fabric of society. It serves to protect the young and easily influenced from acts that are deemed to be in conflict with commonly held moral values.
