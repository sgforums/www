    the old scheme rental i believe is only a few hundred dollars for hawker stalls.

    now it is in thousands, not easy to break even.

    assuming you lease a whole coffee shop for business. the rental, depending on the location will be in tens of thousands. you get to sub-lease the stalls to other hawkers. the success of the coffee shop actually depends very much on the general crowd and also the quality of the hawkers you sub-lease to.

    on top of that you have to pay a few months downpayment. assuming the rental is 30k per month, you might have to fork out 30k x 3 mths = 90k for downpayment.

    can you afford?

    running a coffee shop is a big-time business.

    try selling porridge (ba chok, not teochew muair) to get a feel of what hawker life is about.
