    Lovelorn Japanese men fall for virtual girlfriends (The Sunday Times, 26th Nov 2000)

    More than 30,000 men choose one of several women to date over e-mail. The women write back, revealing their intimate secrets and desires

    TOKYO - Japanese men are falling for virtual girlfriends on Internet-capable mobile phones.

    More than 30,000 lovelorn men have chosen one of several women, including a school teacher, a flight attendant and a bar hostess, to date over e-mail.

    The women - who are actually a programme on a computer server - write back, coquettishly revealing their most intimate secrets and desires on credit card-size mobile phone screens.

    And the relationships are so realistic that men who talk about football or sports during the exchanges get cut off.

    The service, Love by Mail, is made by Bandai, the same company who produced the Tamagotchi, the electronic pet that took Japan by storm in 1997.

    Bandai says Love by Mail subscribers demand extremely realistic scenarios.

    Talking sports is a no-no, and pledging your love too early could also earn you abrupt rejection in the next message, according to ananova.com, a British online news service.
    The goal is to get your girlfriend to reveal her darkest secrets and offer her undying love.

    And true to life - love and passion eventually fades - most men give up after three months, because that is about how long it takes the mystery to fade, says Ms Yukiko Takahashi of Bandai Networks, the toymaker's Internet arm.

    Researchers say it has hit a cultural nerve.
    'Japanese people have always been good at discovering what makes technology fun to use,' said Mr Yuichi Washida, research director at the Hakuhodo Institute of Life and Living.

    'They don't think it is strange to communicate with virtual people.'
    Most of the subscribers are men in their 30s - the same population that snapped up Sony's 1,700 (S$4,200) computerised dog, Aibo, when it went on sale in June last year.

    A relationship with a virtual girlfriend goes for much less, at 300 yen (S$5) per month for subscribers to NTT DoCoMo's i-mode phone service.
