    Merlion is spouting water again, STB still studying possible lightning protection

    The Merlion is back at work.

    It started spouting water again today around noon.

    The 36-year-old sculpture had to undergo repairs to its re-plaster its head after it was struck by lightning late last month.

    A piece of its head had fallen off, hitting the wave form at the base.

    The Singapore Tourism Board says repair work will continue on the wave form until the end of this month.

    Meanwhile, all scaffolding has been removed from the sculpture.

    The STB says it's still studying possible lightning protection measures for the Merlion Park.

    The Merlion currently does not come with a lightning conductor as it was designed and built as a sculpture.
