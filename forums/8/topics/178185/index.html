    this cut is the early 1990s used by traditional barbers, but in fact has little information about it. this term appears to be used to describe different styles over time.

    layered cut mean different for hairdresser and traditional barber. traditional barber according to him is it means cutting the hair to "even" out and portruding, curly or uneven hair so that your hair gets a neat makeover giving a "naturally thin but dark " appearance and so it is haircut to reduce the thickness of hair according to the old fashioned barber.
    if u want to go on further u can reduce the length of hair as well if u wish to.Kinda different form tapered or short hair cuts where the barber uses the machine cutter straight on the head to achieve a haircut which show a "light colouredness or the scalp skin" after the cut. Unlike layered, this form of hair cutting will change and bring out the natural shape of your head which some people dont like to. ....

    after cutting layered , it also exposes the "growing hair" underneath , this "growing hair" will grow the same way it is cut but it normally will grow back untidy again after a long time till your next visit to the barber.

    Short and simple - if u saw the barber pull out his customer hair with a comb, holds it and cut it with the machine or usually scissors, he's doing a "layered" cut where the hair remains dark in colour but just gets thinner or slightly shorter
