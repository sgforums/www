    Hi Leungchunghong,

    There are 4 general chords for every Major Keys. There are 3 Major chords and 1 Minor Chord, namely I, IV, V and VI, with VI being the minor chord.

    Example 1:

    For C major, the 3 Major chords will be C, F, G and the 1 Minor chord will be A minor. The C chord will consist of the notes CEG, F chord consists of FAC and G chord consists of GBD. The A minor chord will consist of ACE.

    Example 2:

    For G major, the 3 Major chords will be G, C, D and the 1 Minor chord will be E minor. The G chord will consist of the notes GBD, C chord consists of CEG and D chord consists of DF#A. The E minor chord will consist of EGB.

    Note that in G major, there's 1 sharp (#) in the key, namely F#. Subsequent Major Keys with sharps are:

    1) D Major, 2 sharps F# and C#
    2) A Major, 3 sharps F#, C# and G#
    3) E Major, 4 sharps F#, C#, G# and D#

    so and so forth. There are also flats (b) like F major where there's a Bb.

    Although theoretically you playing the piano and playing on the guitar is the same thing, the chords employed for the guitar may not be the same order as in the piano.

    Hence, you will need to understand the theory of music before you can actually play on the piano. There's a reason why there are no black notes between B and C and also between F and G. You need proper music lessons instead of teaching yourself blindly without understanding the theory behind it. Good luck!
