  • MooKu
    17 Nov 03, 08:31
    In JCs we don't have to fulfil 80 hours of Community Involvement Programme [CIP]. The minimum requirement is (I can't quite remember but I think) 27 hours for one point. This one point will contribute towards the PEARLS points, which will in turn contribute to your entry to university.

    I think that by asking students to do volunteer work through the CIP is not such a bad idea. They get a chance to see how volunteering is like, and in turn they get a chance to see whether they like it or not, and some might even continue to volunteer after that.

    It's sad, but it's the truth that sometimes we really do need students to help raise funds for the needy.

    If students weren't asked to do CIP work, less students will be exposed and I guess less volunteers would be a result of that.

    Just that, perhaps the school should educate the students more on what they are doing, such as which foundation they are raising funds for, what that foundation does etc. And of course not just raise funds lar, JC students (I'm not sure if ALL, but my school has this requirement) have to do at least one home visit in a year in order for the CIP points to be valid.

    i'm in uni now... on top of the 80 hours of community service that we got to fulfill, we need to volunteer our services to non-profit organizations as our projects in certain courses

    for example, some groups will set up a library or computer corner in homes, bring some old folks out, organize trips to the SPCA, conduct art classes, promote family bonding... all sorts of activities lah

    even on top of these, there are certain CCAs that are devoted to volunteerism work as well, like the LEO club and BP-mentoring scheme... for the leo club they organize activities such as home visits, flag days and donation drives... and for the BP mentoring scheme student mentors go to a school every week to coach the students there and provide counselling services.. in other words, be a friend to those who need them

    it's really interesting in a way.. and sometimes we all need a push in volunteering ourselves.. and i agree with wat mooku said.. it's an exposure
