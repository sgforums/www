    One bold step taken was the introduction (in a joint operation with the British Airways) of the supersonic Concorde on the London-Bahrain-Singapore route in December 1977. The service was suspended after three flights when the Malaysian authorities denied the Concorde use of controlled airspace "for environmental reasons". It had a brief reprieve in 1979 before it was totally terminated in Oct 1980. This service was however, a marketing triumph for SIA as SIA became known as one of the four airlines in the world to have used the supersonic airliner. It captured headlines and the admiration of the air transport industry.
