    more about the case

    Close to the tragedy, Malaysia stepped up intrusions around Pedra Branca, which has been part of Singapore since the 1840s.

    Deputy Prime Minister Tony Tan told Parliament there were five violations by Malaysian navy and police boats last week alone.

    This is even though both sides agreed in 1994 that what happens at the island has little bearing on the court's final judgement.

    But more distastefully, Malaysia also tried to exploit the RSS Courageous tragedy to advance its 1979 claim on the island.

    Just a day after the search for the body of missing Singapore Navy servicewoman 2nd Sergeant Chua Bee Lin was called off, the Malaysian government sent a third party diplomatic note to Singapore.

    They protested that since Malaysia claims Pedra Branca, the RSS Courageous had in fact been in Malaysian waters when the accident happened.

    Malaysia also said its Marine Department had begun investigations into the incident, and it has even sent over some forms for the commander of the RSS Courageous, Major Tan Yang Yong, to fill up and submit.

    Dr Tan said: "It is most unfortunate that the Malaysian government has chosen to exploit this tragic accident. It can only serve to stir up emotions and make it more difficult to find an amicable settlement of the issue.

    "Pedra Branca is Singapore's territory and the RSN will continue to safeguard Singapore' sovereignty and integrity. The RSN will continue to patrol the territorial waters off Pedra Branca."

    Source: Channelnewsasia.com
