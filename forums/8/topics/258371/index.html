  • rmyfrm

    Check out http://www.skateline.com.sg - I think that's the correct site.

    Don't know if you're a newbie ... but just in case you are ... note the many differences in skate models and specs. Don't buy based on looks - that's how many skate co.s get you. Do your research before you buy.

    I bought my current pair while on promotion at skateline, they were almost 50% off ~ ILQ9, which is similar to ABEC7ish. I think it's a rollerblade frame .. don't remember .. I skate until the frame's abit bent and scratched until beyond recognition. Still works though.

    Some of my friends always buy cheap friends and build it up .. but it's kinda pointless imo.

  • rmyfrm
    2 Jun 07, 14:16
    Also note in what direction you want to progress for skating. Don't want to buy a pair of aggro blades just cause it's "cool" and then find that you cannot skate fast enough to catch up with the handsome/beautiful man/woman at ECP.

    I have a pair for aggro, a pair for freestyle (slalom), and 2 pairs for recreational/fitness - 1 is a speed skate but el cheapo one .. don't even know hwy I bought, pointless to speedskate anywhere in Singapore.

    I personally am an urban skater, I most frequently use my recreational skates most of the time (3-4times a week). They are more then sufficient for jumps under 3 feet, and work well with the harsh conditions of road blading.
