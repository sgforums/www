  • the Bear
    24 Jun 03, 17:47
    it's real...

    it's called "blading"... wrestlers take a few aspirins on the day of the match to thin their blood...

    during the match, either the ref slips them the hidden blade or they have it taped somewhere on them... they'll run it across their forehead, making a shallow cut... since their blood is thinned by aspirin, the bleeding is very dramatic...

    when the bleeding is stopped backstage, it usually stops for good and the wrestler has a scab there, and total healing happens after a week or so...
