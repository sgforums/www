    I'm with ERA for about 1 year and I'm loving it.

    The $380 course fee at ERA is so much cheaper! I had to pay $800 when I join but you get it back when you meet the sales in 6 months (Well, I got mine back after 6 months!)

    As with any job, there' good and bad side. The above post said is very true. It's a matter of how you manage and adapt to the requirements of the job.

    I see flyers, advertisements and all the expenses as investments. No capital outlay, no gain (since when you know of anything in this world where you don't have to invest and you get money in return). Definitely there will be returns, it's a matter of how much.

    Currently, I can't recruit as yet as I'm not a manager yet but then I've friends who join ERA because of me.

    However, if you are lookiing to join real estate industry because of the money, I sugg you go look elsewhere. Passion for the job is the key thing. Money will come along with it. But if you place money before passion, be prepared to leave the industry in 6 months and be one of the casualty statistics that many have succumbed to!

    My 2 cents worth!
