    An email to Malaysian High Com. Fellow hardwarezonner any chance that the malaysian side will take action?

    To Whom It may concern:

    On 17th September 2005, my friend and I, 2 Singaporeans, were travelling in Johore Bahru.

    On our way back to the checkpoint around 8pm, along the stretch from Caltex petrol station, we were halted by a Malaysian male, Chinese, around 30+. He told us that couple of days ago there was a fight around the area near the checkpoint and his 'brother' was stabbed, now warded in hospital.

    He claimed that his 'brothers' around the area claim that we looked identical to the assailants during the scene of the fight and happened to recognise us today. He also claimed that initially when we walked pass he wanted to slash us as revenge but just wanted to double confirm if he really did slash the correct people as he keep claiming that he is not a crook. Shortly, another male subject came along.

    Both of them threatened and demanded that we follow them to a nearby coffeeshop all the stretch down from the checkpoint for verification with their 'boss' if not they are not going to let us go and if we do try to run, they have people along the whole stretch of road and opposite standing by. Fearing that we might be harmed as we are foreigners in Johore Bahru, we reluctantly agreed to their demand as safety was our main priority and our conscience was clear.

    My friend and I were brought to a coffeeshop operated by a group of Indians. After some talking, they seeked 'financial assistance'. They asked if we had any cash but we do not have much. They extorted to check our wallets and their jestures frightened me and my friend. I was forced to surrender my remaining SGD $40 and a Motorola E398 mobile phone w/o local service provider SIM card. My friend also was forced to surrender SGD $10, RM $10 and a Sony Ericsson K700i mobile phone w/o local service provider SIM card. Initially, they tried to ask for our credit card details and even wanted us to give them our passports as they said Singapore passports can fetch a high price however we refused to give in.

    The 1st male subject that I mentioned earlier handed us a piece of paper stating his name as Ah Keng (verbally he told us he is called Ah Keong) with a mobile no. 016-757-0089. He wanted us to go back to Singapore to bring back another RM $3-4k in order to redeem back our handphones. After which he let both of us leave the coffeeshop unharmed.

    When we went back to the checkpoint, we asked the customs officers on duty if there is any police station in the vicinity. They only told us downstairs and refuse to render more assistance to us. Traumatised by the earlier incident and we do not know the way around, we decided not to stay in Johore Bahru any longer and left for Singapore.

    We headed to Police Cantonment Complex as soon as we cleared Singapore immigrations to lodge a report and sadly, we were told by the officer on duty that Singapore police force is unable to intefere in Malaysia's domestic affairs. We tried calling the number given earlier by the subject and was dismayed to know that the line was invalid. We even tried searching for Johore Bahru's police headquarter's telephone number on the Internet however the line gave an engaged tone or a voice automated system. We do not understand the Royal Malaysia Police Force and the automated voice system as it is all conversed in Bahasa Melayu.

    My friend and I hope that the local authorities in Johore Bahru can follow up on this unfortunate incident based on the report we made with Singapore police force and take appropriate actions. If required, we can fax over the police report for your kind perusal.

    Thank you and may you understand our plight.
