      In his book, Deepak Chopra shares with readers this insight: You are not in the world; the world is in you.

      He begins the exposition with this set of beliefs that is binding and allows no room for soul-searching, or even for the soul itself:

      - There is a material world.

      - The material world is full of things, events and people.

      - I am one of those people, and my status is no higher than that of anyone else.

      - To find out who I am, I must explore the material world.

      Deepak then highlights an embarrassing problem - that there is no way to prove the existence of an outside world, which therefore undermines the entire basis of materialism.

      The outside world will never produce any spiritual answers until you take on a new role as the manufacturer of reality. When that occurs, this new set of beliefs will fall into place:

      1. Everything I am experiencing reflects myself: Therefore, I don't have to try and escape. There is nowhere to escape to, and as long as I see myself as the creator of my reality, I wouldn't want to escape even if I could.

      2. My life is part of every other life: My connection to all living things makes it impossible that I have enemies. I feel no need to oppose, resist, conquer, or destroy.

      3. I have no need to control anyone or anything: I can affect change by transforming the only thing I ever had control of in the first place, which is myself.
