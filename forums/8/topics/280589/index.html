    Anyway, for getting a degree overseas, whether or not the universities are trash are subjective. Going to and Ivy League Uni or to Oxbridge doesn't guarantee you success. Likewise, graduating from a crap uni doesn't necessarily hinder you. It's up to the individual.

    One thing you have to realise when you study overseas is that you have to fend for yourself a lot more. You need to be careful regarding the friends you make and you must be clear and firm on what you want. It's easy to lose sight of the fact that you're there to study, not play. Also, you will feel lonely from time to time, you must learn how to cook, do the laundry, clean and do grocery shopping pretty much on your own, not to mention budgeting. It might sound daunting, but if you think about it, you have to do that sometime, so might as well learn it soon.

    If you're planning to study overseas, it's best to choose one where you have friends already ther to advise you. Else, choose a place that's familiar. Also, try to have the language on instruction to be one that you're very familiar with. Psychology requires tons of reading, don't expect yourself to get through it all if you can't understand most of it.
