    Ginger, tea, herbs can prevent cancer

    The humble ginger is finally getting the credit it deserves in the western world of medicine.

    Experimental studies have shown that root, which is more commonly known for its cold-treating properties, could help lower a person's risk of developing cancer.

    Scientists infected mice with human colon cancer cells for the study. They found that as they fed them 6-gingerol - the spicy substance from ginger, fewer mice developed tumours.

    After 15 days, tumours were found in 13 mice given normal diets. Only four tumours appeared in those fed the ginger extract before and after being injected with the cancer cells.
