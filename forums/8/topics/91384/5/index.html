    right... lemme try to help u there...

    1) normally an open water course would cost roughly say 350 to 400. It varies with market forces and the market players. The course outline would be as follows:

    a) 2 theory lessons
    b) 2 pool sessions ( confined waters to practice the drills that you'll be executing in the open sea)
    c) A weekend trip away to Malaysia for your Open Water certification

    2) Dive trips for the course... i would say it varies but a safe bet would be 5 dives in total. 3 on saturday and 2 on sunday.

    3) You are not required to get the stuffs as there are equipment avaliable for rent. However if you do decide to get stuffs... a few places i could recommend would be Richmond, Oceanic and Waikiki Dive centre all located at The Concourse at Beach Road. Well a most basic set would comprise of a mask, snorkel, fin, wetsuit and booties ( if you're getting an open heel one). This would probably set you back say ard 300 dollars for average but good equipment. For regulator and BCD, we'll talk abt that later

    Hope that cleared some of your doubts with regards to the Open Water course.
