    i'm using PI 512k unlimited adsl now.....its quite decent.....the transfer rate is usually quite stable, average about 30-40k per second, so usually i can have about 2 downloads each at around 30k per second to fill up the 62k download cap

    when i play online games, depending on the server, the ping is usually about 50ms, which is also quite good.

    depending on where you live, cable might or might not be better. if you live in a HDB flat where there are a lot of users, your download rate can slow down to a 56k modem speed. if you live in landed property areas, the download speed can be a few hundred k, consistantly.

    one thing about cable, though, is that the upload is capped from what my friends who use cable tell me....you can only upload about 10k, whereas adsl is 62k download, and 32k upload. and if the subscription cost really rises, then i think you'd be better off getting adsl.

    Originally posted by nick34:
    but u using 256 and i am using 512 ...... will there be a difference ???
    bandwidth and ping time are two separate things....

    256k and 512k refers to the bandwidth, meaning how much you can download in a given time.

    ping time, is how fast your connection can send info to the other side.

    for online games, ping time is more important than bandwidth, since most games only use a few k per second.

    basically, 256k is like a sports car travelling at 200kmph, and 512k is like a sports car with a boot that's twice as big, but also travelling at 200kmph.

    i hope this clears things up
