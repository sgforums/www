    2)  There can be an enaggement ceremony after which you call each other finacee but not married yet, by law, you are still not legal.  Agreeing to it does not mean anything.  Likewise, you can registered your marriage and yet not get any wedding, but she is still your wife legally by law.

     

    3) ROM and church wedding, what do you really want to do?  ROM can be different from church wedding.  You see, you can registered with ROM for marriage and not have a church wedding till later date, or not at all but you are still legally married. 

     

    I suggest you check with your church about your wedding date and ask them whether they do the arrangement for you to have your registeration at church instead of at ROM office.  Many do provide this service.  If your church as a registrar that can ordain your wedding or you can get someone who can do it for you at your church wedding, no problem then.  Do check with ROM or your church.

     

    4)  Wedding buffet and dinner on the same day?  If you can afford both by all means, but that will cost you a bomb and not forgetting your friends and relatives who will be giving anyg pow and gifts.  Do you really want to burden them?  Besides, what time will your buffet be? and then follow by dinner, can they really eat?

     

    I only had church wedding and buffet at home for relatives and some close friends.  Even church decoration was shared with another couple who hadppened to had theirs 2 hours after mine.  They didn't mind and also due to time constraint.  Dinner was at restaurant in the evening.

     

    5)  IF you are choosing special dates especially for chinese parents, some must be "Good" auspicous day, then you better book way in advances, check with the restaurant.  Sometimes, the queue can be as far back as >1 yr, that's what my brother did, what's more, restaurant impose restriction due to that very day was very important to chinese.  For me no problem, 1 yr in advance no problem booking

    Under the eye of Singapore law:

    1. You just have to pay that $20 odd dollars (more info at ROM);

    2. Schedule your wedding date with ROM (more info at ROM);

    3. Book your JP or get assigned (prepare angbao for JP from $50 - more info at ROM);

    4. Get 2 witnesses to witness your ROM (usually best friends or parents).

     

    The rest of it, money and creativity are the limits. All the posts are just template of regular Singaporean ROM PROCEDURES. Follow at your own risk. Once ROM-ed, you 2 are legally married. Technically, she's your wife.
