    NO......most of you all are wrong.

    that is a typical "blue screen". I got it many many times, but its ok.

    this occurs when there is a device in conflict with your computer....most probably a "new" modem or graphics card or something.....

    this causes the conflict and sets the computer into a safety function known as the memory dump..... (NOTE: DO NOT switch off the computer while the program is DUMPING!!!!, else you may corrupt the computer data or cause it to crash, just let it run finish).

    The way to remedy this is to check through the device manager or download new drivers, if its hardware, then too bad, the particular modem or graphics card or something is not compatible with your computer set up.
