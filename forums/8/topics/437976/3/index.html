    Poor justification for costly decision

    I READ with disappointment about the Night Safari's abrupt cancellation of the Halloween Horrors event slated to be held from Sept 30 ('Halloween Horrors event axed'; last Friday).

    The last-minute cancellation reflects an unfathomable lack of strategic planning and business logic. If the Halloween event was not in line with the park's core purpose, the Wildlife Reserves Singapore's (WRS) board of directors should have had the strategic clarity to review the annual event at the start of the financial year itself and decide on whether to continue with it.

    To call it off after 90 per cent completion, when the costs for holding this large-scale event had already been incurred, seems illogical as it would yield zero returns on investments and negative publicity for WRS. It is shocking that WRS has made such a costly decision without consulting all the stakeholders.

    One group of stakeholders clearly neglected in the process was the 17 Singapore Polytechnic students who had conceptualised the theme, developed characters and designed costumes as part of their project. As an educator myself, I can only imagine the disappointment of these students who spent months on it.

    As an organisation that claims to be big on corporate social responsibility, what kind of message is WRS sending to the youth? Is it telling them that a powerful organisation can make any decision based on its whim and fancy, with little regard for other people who may be affected?

    To date, the reasons given by WRS for wanting to focus on family-bonding activities and negative feedback from shareholders do not justify its action.

    With WRS helming three parks - the Singapore Zoo, Night Safari and Jurong Bird Park - there is no lack of family-friendly events and wholesome activities over a period of 12 months. The Halloween Horrors at the Night Safari was just one of the attempts (a commendable one, indeed) to diversify the nature of events and audience base. In fact, it had proven to be highly engaging for young people and families with older children.

    The more acceptable action would have been to allow this year's Halloween Horrors to proceed and then re-evaluate its plans for next year, so as to minimise the impact on all stakeholders.

    Bay Wee Wen (Ms)
