    Sometimes I think that the emotional attachment in pet ownerships can be very strong. And as usual, emotion is always that irritating thing that clouds the issue and makes decisions (a simple Yes or No) a real trial.

    Presently I am also reaching that stage of my dog's life. If my calculations are correct, my dog is already 13-14 years old; blind in both eyes. Even so, she is still able to move around, eat and chase away the occasional cat But when it comes to the day when she is unable to move on her own free will; I think putting her to sleep will be the most humane thing we can do.

    If you feel bad about it, I don't think you should. After all, if you have given your dog a very good life and treated it very well, I think putting it to sleep will be more of a deliverance to your pet than a barbaric act.

    Of course, talk is cheap and its a whole different story having to act on that decision. To be honest I don't know if I would have the courage myself to make that call when the time comes, even though logically I know that decision is the correct one.

    As for your friend you mentioned, I believe that she already knows what to do; its just that she does not dare to do it.
