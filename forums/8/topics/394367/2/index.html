  • Veggie Bao
    24 Mar 10, 12:39

    Praying may not really has an "effect" on the surroundings, but it has an effect on the prayer / the person.

    Praying will make a person more contemplative, reflective, and understand the general directions and situations of life.

    Praying is similar to a form of deep meditation.

    As I said, logically speaking there might not be any real benefits or practical outcome especially on specific requests, at least not on a direct causal relationship, but the effect is the mental conditioning of the person, which may bring the improvement indirectly.

    For instance, I never pray I get Toto or 4D.

    But when I was faced with school examinations, I did pray that I would do well.

    Praying that I do well in exams, makes me more calm and determined to study. As a result, it may boost and improve my exam results.

    Praying for the welfare and happiness of my family and loved ones, may not have a direct effect on them, but makes me more aware and conscious of them and makes me cherish them more. As a result, I may give them more attention and help them or support them in some ways. That can give them some improvements indirectly.

    I hope you understand now.
