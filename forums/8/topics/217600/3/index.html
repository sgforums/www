    30 Oct 06, 10:56

    This seems to be the entry-level salary range of non-managerial staff (basic) .. they're based on feedback from friends, family and my own experience so it may not be entirely accurate :

    O levels : $800-$1.3k
    A levels/Dip : $1.2k-1.8k
    Degree : $1.5k-2.6k

    - Call centre jobs tend to pay more (basic). Jobs that require overnight shift / 3rd language abilities tend to pay 20% more (basic).

    - Average salary range is subjective to industry / company

    - Job advancement/pay increment is HIGHLY dependent on your paper qualifications, even if you've exhibited a personal capacity / performance that is on par with, or even surpasses, degree holders. So much for meritocracy!!

    - Managers (not incl supervisors) tend to earn 2-10 times more than their underlings depending on their position. There appears to be quite an imbalance of salary here.. Our salaries today aren't much better from 10 years ago. Therefore it would be stretching the truth to say that Singaporeans today are wealthier.

    So yeah, like some forumers have said, the "average salary" says almost nothing. If we could get a real breakdown of the figures on the respective positions / industries, we'd get a much clearer picture.. because "Average" is very subjective...
