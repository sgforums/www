    The correct question to ask is: What I want my salary to be, and what I must do to achieve it.

    A common trait among Singaporeans is that they think they want to work only as much as they are paid. So, say, given a salary of 1.5K, they work as much as what they perceived to be worth 1.5K of their effort.

    What makes you think you are worth that 1.5K, or that 10K then? Has your presense in the company added value to the company? Or are you secretly hoping that you may skive and escape from responsibilities hoping that nobody knows and still get your huge paycheck at the end of the month?

    Prove that you are indispensible in your firm and your salary will rise naturally. It entails an effort on your part, to take on responsibilities that others will shun, to self study for a skill no one else in your firm has, to put in more hours than your collegues will. But will you do that? Until you do, do not ask how much you think you need to have to be satisfied.

    Having a huge paycheck is a by-product. It is to deserve it because you have worked for it, not the other way round.

    The analysts at investment banks starts their career on an average of 5K salary. Most comes from ivy league collegues in the states. They work an average of 14 to 16 hours a day. Many will go on to become consultants with a 10K paycheck and drives a BMW before they hit 30 years old.

    The auditors at top accounting firms starts their career at 2.5K or less. Many work 16 hours a day during peak periods. Those who holds out long enough eventually becomes managers and partners drawing 10K a month or more.

    The software engineers at technolog firms starts on contract, drawing 1.8K to 2.5K with no job stability because sometimes their contracts are not extended. They too work more than 12 hours a day with weekends thrown in. Those who proved their worth eventually become permanent staffs, goes on to become managers and system analysts. The most capable of them will draw more than 10K during their mid thirties.

    Entrepreneurs slogs their life away for what they believe in. They work 18 hours a day, and 7 days a week. Many become bankrupts after burning their life saving in their ventures. Many starts again from where they failed and learnt. They become multi millionaires making many times more than 10K a month.

    Can you identify yourself among one of these groups?
