    Hmm..

    Seriously, I would attribute this notion to inmaturity amongst the youths. An important part of our own existance lies in accepting ourselves for who we are. Be at peace with yourself; and you will be at peace with the world. I'm a Chinese and proud to say that I am one.

    I have to admit that when I was younger, too had this idea that the caucasian society was much superior to us and that as non-caucasians, we were inferior to them in all aspects.

    As a result, from young, I was more familiar with the Aurthrian Legends than our Chinese folklore; I was familiar with all the Greek Gods and knew nothing about the traditional Chinese Gods; I was enthralled by Shakespere and ignored Chinese classics; I marvelled at the Parthenon, but hardly gave a second glance at the Forbidden City; worst, I even shunned my own mother tounge, devoting most of my time and efforts to English.

    But as one grows older and is exposed to the hard facts of reality, such illusions eventually wear off. If you look at it, the Western culture is no Utopia. Sure it has its good points but ultimately, there's nothing like our own culture.

    Now I am trying to make up for lost time, making conciencious efforts to understand my culture more. Perhaps one of my greatest regret in life (up till now) is not being able to have a strong command over my Mandarin. But I'll keep trying...

    I no longer view ourselves inferior to any race on the planet, neither do I view ourselves as superior to any others. All humans are equal.

    Please, love and accept yourself for who you are.
