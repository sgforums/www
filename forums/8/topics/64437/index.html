Rocket Scientists to Look into EZ-Link Redevelopment

  • kopiosatu
    16 Dec 03, 10:57
    by Laopokcar

    Following the furore created by the fixed $5 deposit of the EZ-link card, the Gahmen has decided to tap into the expertise of Russian and Chinese rocket scientists to re-develop the use and functions of the EZ-link card.

    EZ-link company head Mr Tan Boh Lui revealed this at a press conference recently. He was quoted to have said: " It is not a U-turn but more of a re-think. The ignorant public are complaining about the 5 bucks, without knowing that the mere 5 dollars they pay go a long way in hiring these foreign talent to fine tune the EZ link system."

    The new EZ-link card is said to be a much improved one and will be re-named EZ-win-win. It will have a GPRS tracking system and a voice chip. The GPRS will be able to track the movements of the user and monitors the route he takes. In cases where the route taken doesnÂ’t generate a substantial profit for the company, a gentle reminder will be emailed, snail-mailed and SMSed to the user, informing him of the 'correct' route to take next time.

    Beaming , Mr Tan said: "See we take care of our people so well. The Gahmen tells them what to do and now even how to walk, take MRT and take bus. It's a win-win situation mah, we earn profits and so we won't raise the fixed deposit in the long run to hire more brains to fine tune the system again. I win, you win, everyone wins!"

    The voice chip is designed so as to act as a deterrent to users who 'just cannot follow simple instructions' according to Mr Tan. For users who refuse to heed these gentle reminders, vulgarities of various dialects and languages will be sounded off from the voice chip, in a bid to shame the stubborn fool. The idea stemmed from Singapore being a multi-racial society and was also a brainchild of the various head managers who were formerly kopi tiam bengs.

    Mr Tan summed it up by saying that the project took some 3000 manhours and careful planning by 26 head managers and 55 scientists. "We spent countless nights out in the KTVs and geylang lorongs to study and evaluate how the common man on the street is reacting to the current EZ-link system. So now hopefully Singaporeans will understand and be thankful that the 5 dollars they forked out was put to good use."
