    SINGAPORE : Education Minister Tharman Shanmugaratnam has said when it comes to history in schools, students need to know not just the facts but more importantly, its significance today.

    He was responding to recent media reports about a group of teenage students who went camping and named their group Hitler while professing admiration for the man.

    Mr Tharman was speaking to reporters at an Investiture Ceremony for some 1,770 teachers on Tuesday.

    He said the way forward was not to have a kneejerk reaction to this, but to get the students to find out the facts behind what Hitler did, which is what the school is doing.

    The Ministry will then use this as a learning experience for all schools.

    Mr Tharman said: "They were clearly unaware of certain basic facts of history, particularly the fact that Hitler committed major crimes against humanity in his own society and against humanity at large and this was a crime of race, a racial crime.

    "So it should be something that our students should be very sensitive to, particularly given how much emphasis we give to racial harmony and understanding how at any point in time in any part of the world, you can get wars of race within a society, we have got to be very sensitive.

    "What is important is that we turn this into a leaning experience, this is what schools are for." - CNA/de
