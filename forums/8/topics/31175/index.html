    if wan play games, choose amd, if not 4 data ...intel?
    That was true. About 2 years ago. Today, the best Intel chips are actually better than their AMD counterparts at games, scoring higher marks on 3D-tests, et cetera.

    In fact, AMDs are now doing better than Intel chips at business applications because they are (always have been) using a more sophisticated mathematical algorithum in their core for processing calculations. In layman's terms, an AMD does more calculations per second then an Intel does at the same speed. That is why Intel concentrates on boosting their speed. It is simply different focuses for each company.

    About the AMDs being hotter, than is very, very mildly true. As mentioned earlier by someone else, temperature is only a concern if you intend to overclock your CPU. If you overclock an Intel CPU, the temperature would be a concern too.

    Finally, on which is better...

    AMD have and always will be popular for one thing: bang for the buck. The performance you get for each dollar you spend is unbeatable. And it's not shabby performance either.

    Intel has the definite edge over AMD with it's newest chip that has the Hyperthreading technology. Other than those chips, the rest are normal and the performance advantage over the AMD is measured in tests conducted at labs... not something you can feel playing games or writing a Word document.

    Overall, if you want outright performance (and have truckloads of $money$), go for Intel.
    If you (like normal people) have a budget, there is only one choice: Advanced Micro Devices.

    Do note that if you get an Intel chip, not only is the chip itself more expensive, but also the other components of the computer such as the motherboard and memory. It all adds up.
