    Example: Mr A has been a couch potato for 3 years, weighing 115 kg at 1.8 meters tall. His BMI is way into danger zone and he is physically weak.

    A suggested workout for him was this: Gym, cardio, Gym cardio every alternate days.

    For his gym workout, he has to focus on his major muscle groups, like deltoids, biceps triceps, abdominal, hamstring, chest, inner out thighs etc... His objective will be doing heavy weights at more reps. YES! More reps.

    The next day after his gym work out, he should be concertrating on cardio excercises like cycling, jogging, swimming etc. Cardio as in constant heart rate for a certain period, then hit it to between 70% - 85% of maximum heart rate, then slow it down
    then raise it again. This will increase weight lose and metabolism rate for the starting month, until body hit a conditional period, where doing all the same is just maintenance.

    For skinny people, it's not just eating lots of rice to be big. Muscle groups require lots of protein, and you need to build/work on them. It's actually easier for skinny to gain lots of muscle mass then for fat-fukers to lose the fats around the muscles.
