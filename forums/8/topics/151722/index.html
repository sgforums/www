    RElated News

    Shareholders of Raffles Holdings have voted overwhelmingly in favour of the company's plans to sell its hotel business to Colony Capital.

    The S$1.45 billion deal was first announced in July.

    It involves selling 41 hotels worldwide to Colony, including the historic Raffles Hotel.

    The group will record a divestment gain of S$605 million from the sale which according to some estimates will take the hotel operator 12 years to make.

    Shareholders flocked into Raffles City Convention Centre during tea-break to pass a resolution to approve the sale of Raffles' hotel business to Colony Capital.

    Almost everyone voted in favour of the deal.

    Jennie Chua, President and CEO, Raffles Holdings, said: "We are very happy to announce that it was an overwhelming "yes" for the divestment. I remember the figures correctly - 99.97 percent for and 0.03 percent against."

    The sale is expected to be completed in the next few months.

    Shareholders will receive a special dividend of 40 cents a share before the end of this year.

    With the deal, a majority of the hotel operator's 9,000 staff will join Colony Capital.

    About 25 to 30 people will remain with Raffles Holdings to run its remaining operations.

    CEO Jennie Chua said she was still in discussions with the buyer.

    She said: "Right now, I would be able to confirm that I am with Raffles Holdings. Of course, Colony Capital and I are talking on some opportunities like I have also said I will remain as chairman of Raffles Hotel. If there are similar opportunities we'll be talking those."

    Raffles has formed a special board committee to look into its future business options.

    An announcement is expected within the next few months.

    Raffles said it was looking to enrich shareholder value and generate higher yields than those from its existing hotel business.

    There has been speculation that it will participate in the integrated resorts project.

    Ms Chua said: "It is too premature as the RFPs have not been finalised and discussed and decided. So for us to say that is top of the list, that is a bit strange to consider as top. Certainly, people like to think that we are somehow involved in the integrated resorts but I think right now it rather very premature."

    Raffles will continue to hold a 45 percent stake in Tincel Properties, which owns the Raffles City Complex.

    It will also continue to own Swissotel the Stamford and Raffles the Plaza.
