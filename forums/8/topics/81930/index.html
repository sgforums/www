S'pore NDP04 launches world's first national mobile blogging

  • wuming78
    9 Jun 04, 23:41
    SINGAPORE: Some Singaporeans are making history in the run-up to this year's National Day Parade, by putting their diaries online and the rest of the nation can, for the first time, interact with them using mobile phones.

    This interaction is called moblogging or "Mobile Web Logging" and Singapore will be the first and only nation in the world to adopt moblogging at a national event.

    Those who put their diaries online are called Web diarists or bloggers.

    Tan Eck Kheng, one of the bloggers, goes on the streets to gather materials for his entries, usually a reflection of Singaporean life.

    "You see, it's a blistering hot day and there's no one using umbrellas around. Maybe Singaporeans think umbrellas are only for rain or maybe they don't want to be like an Ah Soh," he noted.

    "I'll keep blogging until I realise that people are not reading what you are writing. No point writing and blogging when no one is really interested in what you are saying," said Eck Kheng, a publisher.

    Another blogger, Philip Wu, has been uploading shots of his loves and life onto the National Day Parade website.

    Singapore actually has the largest per capita of bloggers in the world.

    And the NDP committee thought this would be a great way to get Singaporeans here and overseas interacting.

    Philip Wu said: "I figured that I should write for the overseas people who might be reading it. So, I created this thing called Letter from Singapore so that I can explain the week's happenings, my impressions of Singapore in developments during the week."

    You can read the blogs or online diaries at NDP website - CNA
