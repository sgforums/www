A complaint letter ~~~~~ very long

  • daniuge
    23 Apr 03, 10:34

    As an advance birthday celebration, I invited my
    friends to Sakae Sushi (Marina Square) on the 15th
    March 2003. I must say that I am absolutely impressed
    with the staff of this branch for presenting me with
    such an unforgettable experience.

    My friends and I were in the queue at about 6.45pm and
    there were only 2 groups, of less than 5 each, before
    us. Assistant Manager (trainee) Hong Jie was recording
    down the number of people of each group so that he
    could make the seating arrangements. When we told him
    that we need a table for 10, he replied that there
    weren't seats for us at that moment but we would get
    our seats by 7.20pm. Fine, we thought, understanding
    that it isn't easy to get seats when there's so many
    of us. We don't mind waiting, since we (except for 2)
    don't have any programs after that. We stood by the
    side, chatting as we wait.

    7.20pm - Groups had been leaving and others entering,
    but no staff approached us to let us know if we could
    go in. A few of us approached Assistant Manager
    (trainee) Hong Jie again. He pointed to the second
    table near the entrance, mentioning that the guests
    there would be leaving soon and we would be sesated
    there. As the table seemed a bit small, we enquired if
    it could accommodate the 10 of us. He argued that the
    table is big enough and could take on 11 people as per
    a past experience. In the midst of our enquiry, he
    left to bring other guests to their seats.

    10 minutes passed. The guests, whom he said would
    leave soon, were still in their seats. We approached
    him again (not that we wanted to, but he's the only
    one we could grabbed hold of). We once again expressed
    our concern that we don't think that particular table
    could accommodate the 10 of us, and as all of us were
    very hungry (which we weren't initially), we hoped to
    get a table ASAP. We also suggested that he could get
    us two smaller tables that are next to each other. He
    grumbled that he had specially arranged that table for
    us and only now that we told him we don't want to be
    seated there. I felt accused. We did not state that we
    did not want that table but that we had waited long,
    and we seriously do not feel that we could all fit
    into that table. And for goodness sake, anyone who had
    brains could easily think of alternatives without the
    customers making suggestions, and not insisting that
    we should sit at that particular table he arranged.
    Moreover, all the staff kept hustling around and no
    one paid attention to our call for them. Nevertheless,
    he said no problem confidently and went back into the
    restaurant (seemingly making the necessary
    arrangements).

    Some time later, one group of customers left to pay
    their bills, and another group at the table next to
    theirs seemed to be preparing to leave. Just when we
    thought that we would be getting those two tables, Mr
    Hong led a couple at the queue to one of those tables
    (when there were other seats available by the conveyor
    belt!). Soon, he approached us and told us we would be
    getting two tables, diagonal to each other. We
    rejected the offer straight away, once again insisting
    that we do not want to be separated that far. He
    looked frustrated, but we couldn't care less. We had
    indicated our preferences right from the beginning and
    he should have planned better. What's the point when
    my friends are celebrating my birthday and we wanted
    very much to sit together so that we could chat? After
    all, we hadn't met up for quite some time. He went
    off.

    Then, the guests at the table (which Hong Jie had
    painstakingly reserved for us initially) left.
    Finally, we thought, we could be seated. BUT, Mr Hong
    brought a group of three customers into the restaurant
    and gave the seat to them!!! After much effort, we
    managed to get hold of him. His explanation was that
    the table COULD NOT accommodate all of us due to the
    lack of space! He also told us we would be getting
    those tables at the back whereby it's bigger (when
    they were of the same size!!).

    Two of my friends left. They had an appointment at
    8pm. They had tried to stay as long as they could.
    They weren't given any choices then, they were already
    late and HAD to take a CAB down to Tampines without
    any food.

    We managed to get hold of another manager in the store
    and told her that we only need a table for 8 now, and
    please get it ASAP for us. She's more sympathetic and
    polite to us. She tried to help. Nevertheless, it was
    only at around 8.45pm that we managed to get a table,
    one of those at the back that Mr Hong said was
    'bigger', but we need to pull extra chairs before all
    the eight of us could be seated.

    Our nightmare had not ended.

    We wanted food that weren't on the belt. It took us a
    great deal of effort before we could get someone to
    serve us. All the staff seemed to be just walking
    around. We were being asked to 'WAIT' a couple of
    times by the busy waiters/waitresses (inclusive of Mr
    Hong), but they never came back. Finally, one came and
    took our orders, three plates of fried tofu and six
    chawamushi, to be specific.

    I guess the kitchen were waiting for the beans to
    ferment (to get tofu) and begging the hens to lay eggs
    (to make the chawamushi). A really tough process I
    guess, for it took donkey years before our orders
    arrived. I also strongly believe that Sakae must be
    very successful in their innovation of using cold oil
    to fry the food, for the FRIED TOFU was COLD. Economy
    is bad; perhaps, this innovation could help Sakae save
    on its gas and thereby cut cost?

    Another painstaking effort was made before we could
    get a waiter to place our order for some ice water. 6
    glasses of ice water and 1 can of diet coke. The
    waiter hurried off and returned soon. He apologized
    politely, telling us that they had ran out of glasses
    and asked if we could wait. ASAP please, was our
    reply. One point to note, the restaurant was not fully
    occupied then.

    A few minutes later, the can of diet coke arrived,
    with a glass containing ice. Our iced-water never
    arrived till the time we left. We did not bother to
    remind the waiter. I have to assume that Sakae had
    used all its funds for its expansion plans and left
    none for buying glasses? I also assumed that Sakae had
    this policy that different types of glasses are
    strictly meant to contain the specific type of drink?
    No other glasses can be substituted to contain
    iced-water?

    Sometime later, one of my friends ordered fried maki
    stick (fried crabstick), hoping that those ordered
    would be hot as compared to those taken from the belt
    (despite our previous experience). Fried maki stick
    arrive... cold... ICED-COLD! (Somewhere must have gone
    wrong with our orders, we wanted iced-water and fried
    maki stick, but the waiter gave us a combination of
    both! Iced-fried maki stick... another new recipe?)

    We requested for the plate with one and a half maki
    stick left to be heated up. The waitress reluctantly
    took it away, and as usual, it took ages before the
    maki stick returned. Hot this time, but left with one.
    Come on, this is the kind of QUALITY food that I
    should be getting for paying $1.90 for a plate of 2
    tempura maki sticks when I could have gotten a pack of
    12 maki sticks, costing only $1, and fried myself at
    home! Furthermore, even if the maki sticks are not
    replaced with freshly fried ones, the plate should
    also be returned with one and a half sticks!
    (Seriously speaking, I would never order such a dish
    if not for my friend who is crazy about it.)

    Two concerns I have to raise here.
    Â·Is Sakae really COMMITTED to serving QUALITY food to
    its customers as mentioned by the founder when he won
    the Entrepreneurship award?
    Â·Is this the kind of service that customers should get
    for paying a PREMIUM PRICE for such common
    MACHINE-MADE sushi that could be easily bought in
    supermarkets and enjoyed in the comfort and better
    ambience of one's home?

    That evening was definitely one of my most
    disappointing dining experiences ever. I had enjoyed
    some of my past dining experience at Sakae Sushi and
    have no qualms about recommending friends and overseas
    visitors to the restaurant. But, with the episode on
    the 15th of March, I do have second thoughts about it.
