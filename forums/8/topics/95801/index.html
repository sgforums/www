    Woman cabby brutally murdered in taxi

    A WOMAN cabby was found murdered and her body left lying on the floor of the back seat of her vehicle in a deserted road in Tuas early on Wednesday.

    At about 4.40am, Madam Lim Choon Fang had triggered the distress device in her cab to alert the cab company's call centre, but she did not respond when the centre contacted her.

    Police who were alerted traced her vehicle some 90 minutes later to Pioneer Sector Walk, where they found Madam Lim lying face up in a pool of blood in the taxi.

    She had injuries to her forehead, face and legs, said a police spokesman.

    The engine of her taxi was turned off and the doors were shut.

    CID officers at the scene recovered some S$118 in cash as well as a cash card worth S$18 from the vehicle.

    Madam Lim had spoken to her husband Ang Kim Weng, who drives the taxi in the day, at about 11 pm on the phone.

    She did the night shift as a relief driver.
