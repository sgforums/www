    The film can be criticized for omitting a large portion of Moore's book. The film depicts only the first half of the Battle of Ia Drang, which took place at Landing Zone X-Ray, and shows Moore's successful defeat of a North Vietnamese attack. It does not depict the second half of the battle: after Moore's troops had left the Landing Zone, the men from Company C, who had arrived to support Moore's troops, were left to march to Landing Zone Albany. Colonel McDade, the leader of these troops, made a tactical mistake on the trail, calling forward all of his commanders to interrogate two unarmed North Vietnamese soldiers. A sudden ambush cut off platoons from their officers, and the American Troops suffered many casualties. By omitting this part of the battle, the movie suggests that it was an unambiguous victory, when in fact Moore himself considers it to have been a draw.

    Source: Wikipedia
