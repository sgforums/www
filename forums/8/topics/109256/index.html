    Yes, unfortunately it can happen. In your own country, there are two things they can do to your passport:
    (1) Do a check.
    (2) Just look at the passport and do not do a check.

    This is in different from entering another country where they must ALWAYS do a check with foreign passports.

    So if they didn't do a check on your son's passport at Woodlands (just looked at it and gave it back to you), then its possible that the expiry went undetected.

    But its a blessing in disguise for you that it was detected on the Malaysian side...because:

    (1) Its an offence to try and leave a country without a valid passport, so you may have gotten into trouble if the guys at Woodlands spotted it.

    (2) Its not really an offence to try and enter a country without a valid passport - because they just won't let you in (so in actuality, you haven't entered their country yet....thus its not an offence).

    Just curious...how did you get back into Singapore?
