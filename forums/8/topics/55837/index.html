    Do the right thing: Don't SMS while driving

    I REFER to the letter, 'Hold that SMS? Not this driver' (ST, Aug 26), by Madam Wendy Lim Hsui Giok.

    Sending an SMS message with one hand while driving a vehicle with the other constitutes an offence of Handphone Driving which can lead to a fine of up to $1,000 or to imprisonment for a term up to six months, or both. One may also be disqualified from driving all classes of vehicles for up to six months.

    Using a mobile phone while driving distracts drivers and reduces their ability to control their vehicles and to react to changes in road situations. It is irresponsible and puts the lives of road users at risk.

    Motorists who wish to use their phones are advised to have their passenger make or receive the call. Motorists who are driving alone can receive urgent calls through a hands-free device but should keep their conversation short. They can return the calls or continue with the conversation at a more appropriate time.

    Passengers who notice their drivers using a phone while driving can play their part by advising the driver to refrain from doing so.

    We request Madam Lim to contact Deputy Superintendent of Police Richard Lim Cher Khin on 6547-6104 to provide further information on the violation she noted so that we can look into the matter.
