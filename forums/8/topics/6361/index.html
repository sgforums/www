    The confidence in our military forces brimming forth from Singaporeans is heartening, but I fear that it is based purely on a scenario of outright war with no complications. I hate to dampen anyone's arduor, but allow me to present a hypothetical situation for your consideration.

    Imagine this. An economic crisis several times the severity of the '98 recession hits SE Asia. Relations between Singapore and Malaysia deteriorate to unbelievably low levels. One fine day the water pipeline running from Johor to Singapore gets blown up, claimed by Malaysian authorities to be the work of "extremists". Malaysia assures Singapore they will do everything to restore the water supply, and even send elite army units to guard the pipeline. Yet, in the meantime, the pipeline remains broken. The lofty promises aren't very reassuring.

    At this point Singapore has no choice but to annex Johor and hold the water supply ourselves, by military force. Let us then make the assumption that the attack is a success. International condemnation of the aggressor (that means us) notwithstanding, a holding operation in foreign territory against a hostile population is terribly expensive both in terms of money and manpower. Remember, many of our troops are reservists who hold regular jobs in peacetime. How long can we conceivably sustain a state of war before the domestic economy is crippled? And then the counterblow is delivered : Malaysia notifies us that some of the water has been poisoned.

    The point I make is that in this particular scenario, all your fancy military gadgets will matter only in the first few days. After the initial victory they are utterly useless. Consider that.
