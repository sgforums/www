  • bismarck

    This has got to be the most embarrasing moment of my life.
    As of 11pm, 1 of my goldfish died.
    I found it motionless at the bottom of the water bucket to isolate it to give it some med. A short while before it died, I noticed it was already breathing rather slowly and weak. It took me a few seconds to realise it was dead because normally it would be close to the bottom and the breathing would be rather slow and weak.But it would still move if I touched the body.

    But now it didn't move when I touched it.

    When it died, there was a mixture of feelings, sad because a life had died in my care. At the same time, I was angry because it just died within 48hrs of buying it. Right from the start, the fish was already hiding in corners where the force of the water coming out of the pump couldn't reach it.

    Angrily, I emptied the water and dumped the fish into a plastic bag, while constantly spewing profanities at the god-forsaken fish.

    My sis would have been happy since she was hoping my fishes would die so she can lobby for a dog.

    Oh well, maybe keeping a fish wasn't that simple as I thought.
    Now, I will go tie up the plastic bag of the dead fish properly.
