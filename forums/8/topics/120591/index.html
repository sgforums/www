    Originally posted by DriftingGuy:
    This is very true.. all my seniors all went on to become the above mentioned.. no one wants to come back to become a process engineer and breathe in toxic fumes...

    Btw..off topic: Gentlerock.. you got any advice for chemical engineering or common engineering who wants to go into finance/sales/marketing?
    Its not difficult.....start off by applying for sales jobs which ask for engineering diploma/degree (usually these are sales positions in engineering companies - as big as Exxon, as small as Tan Ah Beng engineering pte ltd). Once you choose the line, you'll probably remain there forever (eg. water treatment, oil/gas/petrochem, machinery, valves, pumps, etc etc).

    Then the rest is up to you....how you perform, your luck, etc. Then slowly move step to step up the ladder...and if the right opportunities come, jump to another company and continue your climb there. The most important thing is that you have to keep learning.....learn some accounts and management and get involved in them at work too, if possible.

    Then one day, when you see an advert for sales managers/directors....apply lor. Tell them all your experiences, knowledge (eg. accounts, planning, management, etc)...and try till you get employed.

    Then hor, when you have enough experience and confidence....this is the trickiest step.....approach foreign companies which want to start branches in Asia. Ask them to employ you and you run the show for them in Asia. Then you can be your own boss without risking your own money. You also can be other people's boss .

    (Worked for me.... ).
