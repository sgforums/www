    The job market is more of an effect than a cause. Most engineers don't stay in their technical positions for very long. People study engineering to build up a solid technical foundation and to train up analytical thinking skills, but eventually most engineers would want to progress to management or other non-engineering-related positions. Hence the constant demand for new engineers in the market.

    Point is: It doesn't really matter what field you specialise in, if your final career objective is not an engineering or technical position. Industry trends are dynamic, and nobody knows for sure which industry is going to boom or stay in Singapore for long. Instead of going with the flow, why not just choose a path which you are comfortable with, or which you think you'd excel in? The grade/class of your honours degree would help immensely in landing that first job.
