    no this is not a suicidal thing, i'd just like to challenge the theory of knowledge

    what is life all about?
    everyone works so hard in pri. sch to get into a good secondary school, why do they want to get into a good secondary school? so that they can get into a good Poly or JC, why do they want to get into a good poly or JC? to get into a good uni. why a good uni? so that they can get good jobs. why need good jobs? so can earn alot of money and be successful. why the need to be so successful? to own a big house and a big car and a bunch of maids. whats the reason behind all this? to be happy! is this what life is all about? to be happy?

    i don't believe in the chinese saying "xian ku hou tian" which is to work hard first and enjoy the benefits later. what for? you work so hard most of your life just to be happy and to lead a comfortable life? well i'm leading quite a comfortable life now as a JC student, why work so hard? i can just slacken a bit and enjoy my youth now and be happy too? why work so hard and wait for so long while i can enjoy life now? what are we persuing in life?

    what is our goal in life? is it all about realising one's material dreams? why did that man jump onto the tracks? because he was poor? is that all there is in life? wealth and materials? besides wealth and everything, there is still love, friendships and others, is that not a good enough reason to live? is our world revolving around money, houses, cars, properties?

    Are we guaranteed a successful life if we enter the best university in the world get good grades and get a good job? how do u define a successful life? a big fat pay? a 7 or 8 figure savings account?

    so with the whole relationship web of getting into good schools and good unis u get a good job with good pay and u are happy, is it indirectly saying that by studying u will get a good job achieve happiness? well, does study make u feel happy? another question, if i play more now, does it mean i won't have a future?

    Your views? (and congratulations for reading all the way till here..)
