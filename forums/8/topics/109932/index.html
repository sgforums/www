    Dec 30, 2004
    New year celebrations called off

    KOTA KUALA MUDA (Malaysia): Malaysia and Thailand have decided to forgo New Year's celebrations in memory of the victims of the devastating tsunamis that hit both countries.
    The Malaysian government yesterday cancelled New Year celebrations nationwide, urging people to pray for the victims of the waves that killed at least 65 Malaysians.
    Prime Minister Abdullah Badawi said the government hopes to stage prayer gatherings instead of fireworks and parties this weekend.
    Datuk Seri Abdullah, who cut short his vacation in Europe to return home, was speaking yesterday after visiting the worst-hit villages in this north-western town in Kedah.
    Residents with damaged houses would get RM2,000 (S$860) and those whose houses were destroyed would get RM5,000. Fishermen who lost their boats would get up to RM3,000.
    In Thailand, New Year celebrations by both public and private sectors will turn into national days of remembrance for the victims of Sunday's tsunamis.
    Thai Prime Minister Thaksin Shinawatra has cancelled plans to attend New Year celebrations in the northern city of Chiang Mai with tennis superstars Maria Sharapova and Venus Williams.
    He has also ordered three days of national mourning. Everyone should wear black from Tuesday to Thursday, when all faiths should hold religious services for the victims, he said.
    The Thai Cabinet ordered all flags to fly at half-mast for the three days. It also urged temples and shrines across the country to hold rites for the dead.
    More than 1,600 were confirmed dead in six south-western Thai provinces after Sunday's disaster. -- AGENCE FRANCE-PRESSE, ASSOCIATED PRESS, THE NATION/ASIA NEWS NETWORK

    MediaCorp cancels 'live' telecast of The Nation's Countdown on Channel 5

    SINGAPORE : MediaCorp has announced it will cancel the Channel 5 'live' telecast of The Nation's Countdown on 31 December, as a mark of respect for the victims of the Asian tsunami tragedy.

    It says as a national broadcaster, MediaCorp is mindful of the sentiments of Singaporeans as well as those of our neighbours, whose countries were directly affected by the tsunamis.

    The cancellation is in line with its initiative to use all its media platforms to drive the Red Cross' aid efforts.

    At the same time, MediaCorp says it recognises that there are Singaporeans who still wish to usher in the New Year at The Nation's Countdown.

    Many tickets have already been sold.

    As such, the event, which is co-organised by MediaCorp TV and Sentosa Development Corporation, will proceed as planned.

    A one-minute silence will be observed at the event to mourn the victims of the disaster.

    MediaCorp encourages those attending to donate generously to the humanitarian funds. - CNA
