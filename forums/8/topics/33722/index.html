    Canadian singing sensation Avril Lavigne continued her unstoppable rise in the pop music world by winning three categories at the MTV Asia Awards here.

    The 18-year-old Lavigne was voted favourite female artist and favourite breakthrough artist by viewers of the US-based music cable channel, which has a large Asian following.

    Robbie Williams was voted favourite male artist, edging out Eminem, Moby, Ronan Keating and Enrique Iglesias.

    Blue won the favourite pop act award, beating A1, Destiny's Child, No Doubt and Westlife.

    Linkin Park won favourite rock act and favourite video.

    The 18-year-old Lavigne, whose hit song "Complicated" has become a teen anthem, beat Jennifer Lopez, Kylie Minogue, Pink and Shakira for the favourite female artist prize.

    Her rivals for the favourite breakthrough artist were Michelle Branch, Shakira, Blue and Norah Jones.

    Lavigne was also given the Style award by MTV Asia.

    Earlier this month, she was nominated for Grammy awards for best new artist, song of the year for "Complicated", best female pop vocal performance and best pop vocal album.

    In the Asian categories, Malaysia's Siti Nurhaliza, Singapore's Stefanie Sun, Hong Kong's Sammi Cheng, and the Philippines' Regine Velasquez won their respective countries' favourite artist awards for the second year running.

    Other local MTV Asia favourite artists were D2B (Thailand), Cokelat (Indonesia), Jay Chou (Taiwan), A.R. Rahman (India), j.t.L (Korea) and Yu Quan (China).

    The inaugural Asian Film Award was presented to Sanjay Leela Bhansali of India for the "Devdas," and the Inspiration Award was awarded to Taiwanese boy band F4.

    International superstars Coco Lee and Shaggy hosted the awards at the Singapore Indoor Stadium
