    it does happen...

    people who are without nationality are considered "stateless"

    there is a protocol signed by many countries to try and prevent statelessness, however, there are still quite a few.. however, they are mostly permanent residents of a country..

    permanent residents have no right of return to their resident country unlike citizens, stateless PRs can apply for travel documents like Certificates of Identity for travel... however, their requirements to enter other countries may be more stringent and some countries may not recognise the travel document and thus will not allow them to enter...

    malaysia has a lot of stateless persons as they provided amnesty for indonesian illegal aliens some time ago.. they were given a choice of returning to indonesia, or renouncing their indonesian nationality and be given Malaysian PR.. some chose the latter...

    and there are those in singapore and malaysia who missed the registration when the countries went independent.. they are basically considered "stateless"...
