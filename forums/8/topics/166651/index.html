    turns nightmare into sweet dream. Team Singapore did it

    The historic Women Artistic Gymnastics team's gold medal marks not the success of an individual but that of Team Singapore's.

    Gymnastics gold medal turns nightmare into sweet dream. Team Singapore did it

    This new chapter in Singapore's sports history comes not from foreign talents, not from training overseas, not from the funded core sports or merit sports. It comes not from a group with theoretically-correct systems.

    It comes from an entirely local team which trained in Bishan Sports Hall. It was considered an 'other' sports classified by the Singapore Sports Council.

    It comes from a sport which was delisted by the Singapore National Olympic Council and the Singapore Sports Council in 2001. So what is behind its success? Support from Team Singapore.

    The supporters came from the Ministry of Education, which has gymnastics as a sport with all-year-round combined school training since 1999. From the schools for releasing their students to train.

    From the Major Games Preparation Committee formed by the Singapore Sports Council in 2001 and 2003. From the hard-working coaches who were not affected by the never-ending paperwork and changes.

    From the loyal family members who fetched the gymnasts for training, gave them a shoulder to cry on when they get injured.

    From the media which cheered and supported the team all year. And from the passionate volunteers who always suggest ways to improve performance.

    All this proves one thing: Team Singapore can do it!

    Just like Singapore in the Sars crisis, gymnastics risked being wiped out. But the association did not sit around and have its pity-party. Instead it tried its best even if things were not perfect.

    All the stakeholders stuck together for the sake of the country. The worst nightmare for gymnastics in 2001 has been transformed into the sweetest dream it ever had.

    Elim Chan Ming Yam (Miss)
