  • Gedanken
    8 Nov 07, 21:37
    Fair comment, all of it. Quartz watches are certainly more accurate than mechanical watches; chronometer standards for automatic movements are -4 to +6 seconds every 24 hours, and cheap quartz watches can easily exceed those standards. However, as you've noted, automatic watches do have their allure, and high-end watch collectors tend to go for those on account of the higher manufacturing standards required.

    As for the alignment issue, it's a matter of manufacturing quality, and TAG, unfortunately, is not known for that. I'm not too impressed with their servicing standards either. One of my fellow watch collectors took a chanceon a TAG, and when he sent it for service, they poliched the case till it was completely out of shape - he raised the roof and they finally changed the case on the watch two months later.

    That's one of the key areas in which you'll find a difference between lightweights like TAG and serious players like Rolex. When I send my Rolexes in for service, what I get back is a virtually brand-new watch with a one-year warranty.
