  • dracky
    14 Feb 07, 09:27
    Average household income is not a very useful statistic.

    First, the average is skewed by the high income of the top 20% earners. A median income, which means 50% of household earn more/less than, would be a better indicator.

    Second, household income is temporarily distorted by young working adults that live with their parents. Thus, for a few years until these young adults get married and move out, some middle-class household can report income of above $15K.

    Although household income has grown, the growth for most of us is nowhere near the 6-8% achieved by GDP growth. More of the gains from our economic growth have gone to the owners of capital and global talents. There is, however, not much that the government can do, because we are an open economy and are subject to global competitive forces.

    We should stopping assuming that every Singaporean should be or is enjoying a high standard of living because Singapore is a developed city. Even in Japan, where I'm working now, there are homeless people in the parks.

    I just hope that the tax burden on the rich be increased in tomorrow's budget. But by all indications, we'll probably be hit with GST and the rich gets a corporate tax reduction.
