    Yes, globalisation may well be the reason why ex-managers are forced to settle for jobs as taxi drivers or clerks, but have we conveniently forgotten or ignored the fact that these ex-managers were drawing salaries similar to that of managers in other developed countries, while taxi drivers/clerks' salaries are pegged to developing countries' market rates? Isn't it the least disturbing that our govt is so quick to brand them whining sore losers without first trying to comprehend the ground situation from their perspective in order to come up with the solutions we pay them millions - highest in the world - for?

How much quality of life must a regular Singaporean family sacrifice in order to attain, and maintain, this "6k a month" average?
