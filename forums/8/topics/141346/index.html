    I took my 'A' levels chinese oral last year. Based on experience, unless you are those Jia Kan Dang type... or you can't speak a single word of mandarin...

    Passing would be easy, but if you're talking about achieving merit or distinction, that's entirely different thing...

    THINGS TO LOOK OUT FOR

    - Fluency (Try not to startle, even if you encounter unknown words)
    - Confidence (Speak like you're reporting news, try not to be nervous)
    - Pronunciation (Don't Pronounce EVENING as "pang2 wan3, it's bang4 wan3)
    - Loudness Of Speech (Don't speak like a mouse)
    - Knowledge Of Words (There will be 1 or 2 sheng1 ci2, so be prepared)
    - Impromptu Responses (Hua4 Ti2 must respond promptly and maturely)

    Just my two cents worth... hope it helps...
