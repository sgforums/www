  • dragg

    Recently, the "no maids allowed in condominium pools" policy has come under fire again.

    On The Straits Times forum last week, Ms Sabrina Chang posted her question on why our condominiums openly discriminate others because of their job and nationalities. She also stated that her children are less than five years-old and cannot play at the pool without supervision of her maid when her husband and her are not around.

    But Mr Francis Zhan, the chief executive of the Association of Management Corporations in Singapore (AMCIS), begs to differ. He says that the rule has been around for about 20 years, and the rationale is that "maids are here to work and not to enjoy themselves", and "if we allow al lthe maids to swim in the pools at the various condos, it will be swarmped and over-utilised."

    A poll of 100 condo residents was done by The New Paper and it revealed that 63% of the residents felt that that policy was unfair.

    What are your thoughts?
