    Literature is compulsory for all lower secondary students.

    You can't drop humanities, it's one of the required subjects to gain entry into a JC, CI and Poly.

    Physics will not be difficult if you like it. Same goes for Chemistry and Biology. But I've to say Pure Bio maybe too hard for some students, as it can be quite detailed and a lot of big big terms to remember. You might like F&N instead of Bio.

    If you took combined science for your O Levels, it will be harder for you in JC. You have to study doubly hard because a lot of things are covered in pure science and not in combined science. It's recommended if you wish to go JC, do some reading up before school starts. This will lessen your stress.

    Social Studies is not offered in lower secondary, it's a compulsory subject from upper secondary onwards, so you will only touch Social Studies once you are in Sec 3. First few chapters of Social Studies should be easy if you remember your Sec 2 history facts well.

    As for Geography, I only can say that Geography Elective is easy because it's mostly common sense. It's even better if you read the newspapers a lot, especially those about manufacturing and farming. I'm not too sure for Pure Geography, I never took it.
