    if a foreign worker is a 'skilled' worker, he undergoes training at the Construction Industry Training Institute to learn a particular trade, for eg. bricklaying...he undergoes lessons and tests, until he is ceritfied 'skilled'...that takes a few months i thk, depends on the intelligence of the worker..

    however, there are workers that have already picked up construction skills back in their hometown, esp. the Chinese Nationals..they are very specialised in architectural trades like plastering, painting, etc..

    anyway, a foreign worker is either employed by a general contractor, or a sub-contractor..

    the subcontractor can be one that deals only with labour (i.e. he provides any contractor with workers), or he could be sub-contractors for piling, or electrical, or doors and windows..etc.

    genearally,a normal residential project takes abt 2 yrs to complete...commercial projects maybe slightly longer...the workers of a general contractor may have to work on that particular construction project for the entire construction process..if the foreign worker is only contracted to do part of the project, eg..he is employed by the sub-contractor in charge of piling, then worker will be on site until piling is done.

    so, it depends on what trade the worker is employed for, and by whom he is employed..

    hope tt answers ur qn...btw, why r u interested in how long it takes to build a building? thinking of building one urself?
