    interview of steph song

    IF audiences are going to get stirred up over the breakout star of 2003, it might as well be actress Steph Song.

    Not only is the Australian-Chinese ingenue brainy, sexy and eloquent, she exudes more charisma over a single phonecall than many of our homegrown Ah Jies combined.

    Steph was the seductive neurosurgeon in First Touch and, in Blue Print's final episode on Friday, she plays a violinist torn between Edmund Chen and Tay Ping Hui. Look out for her also as a kaypoh crime reporter in Heartlanders, and a spoiled bitch in the new Channel 5 teen drama Chemistry.

    The Kuching-born 25-year-old has lived in four continents and attended 15 different schools - a result of following her father, who has a PhD in plant genetics, around the world.

    Steph spent her childhood see-sawing between Colombia in South America and Canada, in the redneck province of Saskatoon, Saskatchewan.

    At 14, she relocated to Australia, and later graduated with a double degree in science and journalism from the University of Queensland.

    Steph then migrated to Los Angeles on her own and enrolled in acting school, earning her keep doing Coke commercials and music videos for Oasis and Fiona Apple.

    How do you find Singapore so far?

    I love it here! I've always been in areas devoid of Asian culture, let alone a well-established Chinatown.

    I missed out on growing up with people of my own race, on Chinese culture and history. When I came here, I realised I was very white and I didn't like it.

    Also, Hokkien was like my own secret language because I never spoke it to anyone else, so it was a shock to me that millions speak it here!

    What are your Chinese New Year plans?

    I'll be back in Brisbane to see my parents. There'll be steamboat, and mum's going to make her bee hoon - or what she calls longevity noodles.

    I'm looking forward to seeing what the year of the goat has in store for snakes.

    Among your co-stars Edmund Chen, Tay Ping Hui and Vincent Ng, which hunk would you date in real life?

    Vincent is lunchtime material. It's got to be daytime so that I can have a good look at him, he's just so yummy! He has an amazing body! Trying to pinch some fat off his waist is like pinching fat off your wrist - it's impossible!

    I'd have dinner with Ping Hui because he's a very engaging conversationalist. He's very professional, his mind is always on the job, and I'd love to talk to him more about work.

    And Edmund for drinks, because I've never laughed so much in my life. He's a very cheeky fellow and his energy is contagious.

    What are you hopeless at?

    When I moved to Sydney, I wanted to learn to surf. My Australian boyfriend's a surfer, so he bought me the wet suit, the surfboard, the lessons. But I was overcome by the waves and, after 15 minutes, I'd throw in the towel because it got too cold.

    Reality TV shows are all the rage. Are you a fan?

    I feel that it's pulling audiences away from shows with good acting, but when I tune in, it's very difficult not to get hooked!

    I watched about 15 minutes of the Bachelor and thought it was complete rubbish.

    But I would do Fear Factor. Once, I watched four angmohs complaining about eating a century egg! It's delicious. I'd definitely win that one.
