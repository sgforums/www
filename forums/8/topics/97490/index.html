    HOW IS BONE MARROW COLLECTED FROM A DONOR?

    Upon identification as a compatible match, the donor will be counselled before undergoing a thorough medical examination to ascertain his or her fitness level to undergo the procedure. Currently, there are two standard methods to harvest the bone marrow.

    Method 1: Bone Marrow Harvest

    Marrow is removed from the back of the pelvic bone using a special needle and syringe. The entire process generally takes 45 to 60 minutes whilst the donor is under general anesthesia. Though there is no surgery involved, an overnight rest in the hospital is recommended and the donor can resume his or her normal activities.

    The amount of marrow harvested is less than 5% of the bodyÂ’s marrow and this is naturally regenerated within a few weeks.

    Method 2: Stem Cell Harvest

    Stem cells are really the blood cells found in the marrow. The donor will be given G-CSF (Granulocyte-Colony Stimulating Factor) as n injection to mobilize the stem cells into the blood stream. A few days later, the stem cells will be collected as part of a blood donation procedure. Donations can be done in an outpatient setting as the procedure takes approximately three hours.
