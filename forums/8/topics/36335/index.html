    Farts - also called flatulence (say: flah-choo-lents) or intestinal (say: in-tes-tih-null) gas - are made of, well, gas!

    When you eat, you don't swallow just your food. You also swallow air, which contains gases like nitrogen (say: nye-tro-jen) and oxygen (say: ahks-ih-jen). Small amounts of these gases travel through your digestive system as you digest your food. Other gases like hydrogen (say: high-dro-jen), carbon dioxide (say: kar-bon dy-ahk-side, the gas that makes soda fizzy), and methane (say: meth-ane) are made by the breaking down of undigested food in the large intestine. All these gases in the digestive system have to escape somehow, so they come out as farts!

    Gases are also what can make farts smell bad. Tiny amounts of gases (hydrogen, carbon dioxide, and methane) combine with hydrogen sulfide (say: suhl-fyde) and ammonia (say: uh-mow-nyah) in the large intestine to give gas its smell. Phew!

    All people fart sometimes, whether they live in France, the Fiji islands, or Fresno, California! If you have a dog, you may have even been unlucky enough to have heard (or smelled) Fido farting. Intestinal gas is totally normal, and it's very rare for farting to be the sign that something is wrong in the body.

    If you're known as everyone's farty friend "Boomer" and you want to get rid of some gas, try cutting back on foods like beans, onions, and fried foods. If you have a lot of gas after you eat ice cream, yogurt, or milk, talk to your parents about it - your body may have a difficult time digesting the natural sugar found in dairy foods. And don't forget that farting can sometimes be your body's sign that it's time to take a trip to the bathroom.
