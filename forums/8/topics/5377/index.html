    Then also, the conversation between Susan and Sarah Now that's even faker. Seems to me there and then that Sarah knew what was going on and just feigned dumb. And as for Susan, she went on to say something that was pretty redundant. I mean, we'd all been watching the show and there was really NO NEED for Susan to regurtitate the BBS slander -- like as if the writer had to clarify with the audience what's going on. And when Sarah "heard" it, her reaction was like a big question mark straightaway without even hesitating or contemplating over Sarah's invective accusation. Like I said, the actresses knew their lines and what was going to be said so well that it all becomes so fake when they say them all out. So the scenes were all loosely put together (poor editing job there) and well, it becomes more like a stage play with acts and scenes, with some scenes supposed to last less than 10 seconds and so everything is rushed. The director has really got to inject some sense of continuity and let the pace go naturally, rather than forcing down constraints and eventually giving us a summarised version of the episode that lacks substance.

    Also of course, I asked the SPIN webmaster why didn't they feature local music. The reply, cos TCS is stingy. Now you heard that, and you know why TCS productions always end up flops. They'd rather make a cheap production and get snowballs of ridicule and criticism than to make a more costly one and receive commendations and positive reviews in return. They'd rather play Britney Spears' Baby One More Time than something from our local talents, which we certainly do have in abundance (But kindly leave out that shit cheena song "Celebrate This Moment" which is worse than any Jessica J track in existence. I have no idea why Eric Khoo would ever attempt to make such a classy music video for a song so cheesy it can pasteurise your eardrums on repeated exposure).

TCS just doesn't bother. They think we'd be so impressed with those foreign songs they spun (more than 12 different track snippets get played in that single 20 min episode, all by Western artists).
