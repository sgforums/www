    If you wanna do that, you've gotta build some sort of reputation first. You know like when this site started out, Jason Ling aka Chief aka MrBall did lotsa promotional stuff -- got his friends, organised outings and more importantly he cashed in on his other great -- the now defunct JCGirls and SGGirls that was in the limelight for the later half of last year -- and he got this ever-ready mailing list of people who checked out his site. I think you need some sort of a blowover, some mega media publicity to actually get your site into the limelight and when that happens and the visitors start coming, not once, twice but on a regular basis, you'll know you got it made.

    I have a website called The Singapore Madonna Link and it was made really famous in 1998 when I actually recorded a sample of the song Frozen -- then unreleased in the US but was played first on Singapore radio -- and uploaded it onto my site. And I got into some sort of a fiasco with Warner Bros. Records in the US -- they threatened to sue me big time and all -- and it was all a very hyped-up event. This incident received coverage all over the world -- it was even reported on MTV News USA. And both print and electronic media carried the story... And wow my visitor rate shot up by almost 10 times instantly -- from 500 hits a day to 5000...

    So you see -- controversy pays ... Of course if your site doesn't have that sort of potential to spark heated debates, then you'd really got to start from scratch -- which means ruthless promotion.. and it can be expensive too.. You have to give sufficient credibility to people, so that they can be convinced to visit your site instead of the millions already established out there
