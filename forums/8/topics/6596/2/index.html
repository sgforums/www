    To All:

    The matter of the existence of God/how many gods are there/which one is the true religion has always been, still is and I think will always be a problem that will remain unresolved until we eventually die.

    Here's how Merriam-Webster defined religion:


    1 a : the state of a religious <a nun in her 20th year of religion> b (1) : the service and worship of God or the supernatural (2) : commitment or devotion to religious faith or observance
    2 : a personal set or institutionalized system of religious attitudes, beliefs, and practices
    3 archaic : scrupulous conformity : CONSCIENTIOUSNESS
    4 : a cause, principle, or system of beliefs held to with ardor and faith
    I believe we are all agreed that the basis of most, if not all, religions is not merely the worship and service of God/gods and the supernatural, in fact, it is a set of fundamental principles aimed to bring about good in this world, starting from the individual. Sometimes, it is even a personal set of beliefs (which might just put atheism in the category of religions).

    So in the end, any religion is just another human institution, created by their respective founders with the purpose of bettering the human race. We do not really care whether or not they (the founders) are who/what they claim to be, or whether there is only one god or many gods; as long as what the religion you believe in teaches does not go against your own conscience, then it doesn't really matter.
