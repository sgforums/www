    Halloween coming to Universal Studios Singapore

    SINGAPORE: Singapore's biggest theme park is gearing up to send more than shiver up visitors' spines for this year's Halloween.

    Resorts World Sentosa (RWS) said half of allocated tickets to Universal Studios Singapore's first Halloween event have been sold a week before the seven-night "spooktacular" starts on October 21.

    The Integrated Resort said it is also considering collaborations with Singapore Polytechnic for future editions of the scare-fest, which is intended to be a yearly affair.

    The school's partnership with Wildlife Reserves Singapore to stage an annual Halloween event fell through recently.

    There will be six scare zones for the Universal Studios Singapore's "Halloween Horror Nights" and a fright night awaits for those who turn up, warns Andrea Teo, who is the vice-president of entertainment and events at RWS.

    "This will be extremely scary. In fact, I think we've probably raised the normal scare-quotient to about two above normal. This is not suitable for children. So I expect there will be a reaction. It will be interesting to watch what the reaction will be. All I can say is that you guys have been warned," said Ms Teo.

    In an old Peranakan home, the spirit of a murdered matriarch plots revenge and it looks like she got her wish.

    Slithering through the zones are 280 "scare-actors" who've put in lots of sweat and fake blood - 25 litres a night to be exact.

    "I'm a "Carn-Evil Barker" in Hollywood land where we're having the "Carn-Evil" - evil carnival. What I do is that basically I draw people to the various attractions that we have so I shall keep it a secret. I won't tell you," said Jody Doo, one of the scare-actors.

    But the scariest bit could be piling on the pounds as a special menu has been specially catered for the occasion.

    - CNA/fa
