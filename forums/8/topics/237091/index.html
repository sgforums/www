  • viciouskitty74
    13 Feb 07, 16:13
    Libra, I lived there for a month last year. December.

    FYI, phuket is purely a tourist island. Its existed purely to chop tourist.

    The islands are beautiful. But unless you pay, you wont be getting into a boat to get to them. Or you can just be at the beach and gaze and relax at those lovely sands.

    If you stay at the mainland, you can try those eco tours or the Simon Caberet Shows. its expensive but worth it. Or you can sit at the various bars at Patong Beach and see how the guys goes to sian the Thai Girls at the bar.

    And there is a large shopping centre there called "Central Mall".

    But to buy really cheap stuffs, you need to be able to speak Thai fluently and find your way to the locals night markets.

    FYI, the night markets is more then a pasar malam. Its really a market where folks sells meats, veggies & clothings. The heels you see at Charles & keith are sold for about 99baht to the locals.

    Try S$1/- equals to 22 baht. So 100 baht is roughly S$4.50/-

    And always keep 500 baht hidden away. You need to give that as Customer Service Charge at the Airport before you depart.

    In other Words: DIE DIE RESERVE 500baht per person to pay at the counter (besides the departure gate) at the airport!

    FYI, the spas there are a subculture of the spas from Bali.

    They exist to reap monies off folks that cant speak thai. Their services are like what our commercial spas are charging back in singapore.

    For the beaches, there is roaming massage girls, where you sit at the reclining beach chairs and they will come over and give you a foot massage while you relax.
  • viciouskitty74
    13 Feb 07, 16:26
    Phuket isnt unspoilt.

    Its a tourist island.

    And hotels are plenty.

    Would you need a 5 star hotel if you are going to be roaming the islands all day? Your luggage need the 5 star hotel?

    For shopping, go Bangkok.

    For Spas, go Bali.

    For Scuba, go only if you got the Open Water Padi Licence & go with yoru dive group.

    For eco tours, ridng elephants on mainland will be good enough.

    For exploring the islands. Unless you really can canoe. Your arms has to be strong.

    For the food, unless you got a local there, with at least a vehicle to bring you to the eat all you can local buffet for 100 baht. And you can eat all you can the seafoods, thai style & drink those cheaper then Singapore Whisky & Beers.

    Or you are daring enough and rent a small kup kia and ride around to the temples and explore the island. its roughly the size of Singapore island, without the Woodlands part. And the beaches is beautiful...if you manage to find them.

    Otherwise, stick near to your hotel and brings a good book and a sunscreen to laze at the beach nearby.
