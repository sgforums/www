Adult EZ-Link cards to cost more

Next
  • Xcert
    4 Nov 03, 00:38
    Quoted from channelnewsasia

    SINGAPORE : Adult EZ-Link cards are going to cost more from Tuesday.

    Commuters will now have to pay a $5 non-refundable fee for each card they buy.

    The company that makes the cards, hopes this will stop people from buying cards and not using them.

    The company has warned commuters.

    There are just too many people buying extra EZ-link cards but not using them.

    There are as many as 200,000 unused cards, and this adds unnecessarily to the company's operating costs.

    Yet EZ-Link is still selling about 3,700 new cards everyday, but it doesn't keep a tab on who buys them and how many.

    One woman said: "For women, normally, they need more than one card, some they put in their handbags, the other in their wallets."

    This habit is costing EZ-LInk $6.6 million a year, as each card costs $5 to produce.

    Nicholas Lee, Senior Vice President of EZ-Link, said: "If this continue, the higher cost has to be translated and passed back to somebody - it may be the public transport operator or the commuters."

    So the company has decided to make commuters foot the bill.

    You still pay $15 for a new card, but instead of $10 of stored value, now there will be only $7 to use.

    Instead of a $2 refundable card fee, you will have to part with $5.

    EZ-Link says although it's thought long and hard about the change, it expects some public backlash.

    "That's too fast for people to realise or think about it."

    "If tourists come to Singapore and see the $5 deposit they might be put off, why is the ticket so expensive."

    "It's not fair if it's non-refundable."

    "I don't see other countries have this charge of $5."

    "I think people still don't care about how much it cost, but for convenience purpose. They'll still keep an extra card for themselves."

    But there's good news for senior citizens.

    The company is waiving the $2 refundable card fee so they pay $16 instead of $18 for a new card.

    Ez-LInk is giving out 50,000 cards for free to needy Singaporeans. But the company has yet to decide who qualifies for the scheme. It says it still needs to discuss this with community organisations.

    Ez-Link is also thinking of waiving the $5 non-refundable fee for those who have never bought a card before.

    Some might say these are ideas that should have been sorted out before any announcement. - CNA
