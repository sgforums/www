    hi all,

    my advise to all is not confuse between REAL turtles, terrappins and Tortoise.
    (despite we all call them turtles by large)

    TURTLES - sea (can survive and swim deep water without drowning) and need deep water level

    Terrappines - BOTH land & water based, need water to play, cool and waddle in BUT like the many $3 very cute red ear sliders (2 red/orange stripes near ear area) - NO CONSTANT WATER OVER THE HEAD (esp nose) over the long term or it WILL DROWN due to fatigue cos cannot swim constantly to breath air.

    TORTOISE - land based, no water except for drinking, heard will die if constant contact (not drown) in water (tortoise 'expert'/ breeders please add info)

    Actually it depends lah, there are many species of soft-shelled turtles and most if not all are endangered or vulnerable. Since many of you guys are already keeping them, please do take good care of them, and please don't buy anymore.

    Basic differences between tortoises and turtles (general):

    Tortoise - high-domed shell, stocky legs, lives entirely on land and cannot swim, herbivorous

    Turtles and terrapins - flat shells, usually have flipper-like or webbed feet with claws, turtles can live entirely in water, terrapins need access to dry land, both are carnivorous

    Special note to ppl with star tortoises: pls keep the terrarium as dry as possible as star tortoises come from arid areas with low humidity and Singapore is too humid.

    I am not condoning the keeping of exotic reptiles by providing such info. I'm trying to help you guys give your pets a better life.
