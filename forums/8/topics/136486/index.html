    There are basically two types of broadband connections in Singapore.

    1) Asymmetric Digital Subscriber Line (ADSL)
    2) Cable

    1) ADSL works through phoneline and dialling on the network. You need to make sessions with the service provider by dialling up and logging in. Information is transfer to and fro thru phone lines
    ADSL providers includes: Singnet, Pacific Net

    2) Cable works on the basis on similar to LAN (Local Area Network) or rather a Wide Area Network. In this type of connections, the internet is always on meaning that you dont have to make session with the service provider. Once you on your computer, you are able to surf the net without any form of dialling or logging in. Information is transfer to and fro cable.
    Cable provider: Starhub Maxonline

    LAN is usually used in office and internal to a organisation itself and not for broadband / public.
