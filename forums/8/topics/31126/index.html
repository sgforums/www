    Custody of the child is USUALLY granted to the mother in most instances... UNLESS the father has STRONG CASE to show that the mum is UNFIT for the child... In this case, quite unlikely... Despite the fact that the young mum's family has a bad temper, have they in any way caused any harm to the child...? If they haven't, the family's temper shouldn't be much of a concern in this case.....

    The FACT that the child has been with the mum for around 2 years (and properly taken care of..) would most likely render the mum FIT for the upbringing of the child, DESPITE the fact of whether she has evidence against her husband not visiting the child for 2 years... That's just ADDED BONUS if allowed for...

    If the husband finds that the wife is too poor to support the child, she can always apply for MAINTENANCE in favour of the spoken child....

    The MAIN CONCERN here IF permited, would be the husband's family background... As in whether they has 'ways' to get across the 'red tape'....?

    For more information and advices, you can always suggest for her to visit the Legal Aid Burea for advice... Should cost her only a $1 for that....

    Regards....
