    Most of us are born with good eyesight. Only a very small percentage are born with eye problem.

    In Singapore, we have the lazy eye syndrome, and lots of unqualified opticians who capitalise on it.

    Walk around, how challenging is the scenery.

    The buildings, the pavement, the roads, the trees, the shrubs. They are all near, and when you walk about you need not see far. You need not focus on any particular point, you can just walk with your eyes open. And thats what most Singaporeans do. The landscape is unchallenging to the eyes.

    Then there is the stress of school text books, the PC Screen, and poor posture in reading for prolong periods x a number of years.

    Eye sight fluctuates, as health affects and enviroment affects it temporarly. This can be cured by rest, and eye exercise. Also country side scenery helps exercise the eyes, as you focus on the birds and the fruits and look out where you step, and also the distant clouds and hills. You look far and near. This keeps your eyes well. But sometimes it get weak due to illness or stress, and you can't see as clearly. This is usually temporary.

    Going to the optician and getting a pair of spectacles, and you are done for, if you become dependent on it. Spectacles prevent the eyes from recovering, if used for extended periods, it becomes a clutch.

    Spectacles are supposed to let you see well, so as to reduce stress on the eyes, and help them in recovering.

    In Singapore's case spectacles ruin many  eyes, and they have become clutchs.

    There is no food that is good for the eyes.  But Vitamin A is reported to be good for the eyes, and is found in red vegetables like carrots.

    Take care of your eyes, and your childrens, at an early age.
