    1. Define your topic premises so you can structure your essay around it.

    2. Go in depth. Don't just say "Raising bus fares are bad because economy in recession, and they already raised it 3 times in last 5 years". List examples, Statistics, historical evidence, compare situations.
    Which means - research.<<-- this one hor.. no shortcut.

    3. Quote famous people.

    4. Plan your essays. Got good points, no plan also no use. What's your premise? How far do your points support your premise? What points goes against your premise?

    5. Round it up

    Basically writing GP is like writing out a debate strategy. It isn't just a matter of illustrating points. Imagine you are on a debating team and given that topic. Every point you give may be debunked by your opponent (who incidently is also you), how would your opponent give the other side of the story? How can you convince your audience that your conclusion is the one that makes the most sense?
