    Interviews... if you find urself being asked a lot of questions in a short span of time, its probably not going to well.

    As an interviewee, u should attempt to dominate the interview by providing leading responses to subjects you are prepared to answer.   An impressive interview is one where the interviewee is able to speak most of the time, and allows the interviewer to find out what he needs to know about the applicant.

    As an interviewer, I look for eye contact and body language. I like people who look at me, and a certain degree of animation.  I look for a person's ability to react to unexpected questions, and notes his first response to it.  Does he freeze, frown, smile or look away? The response should be rational and not like a "Miss South Carolina."

    Points are given for Dressing and Handshake, but depending on the position, may not be strongly criticised.  Sales and Guest Relations positions, expect to be penalized.

     

    Hope this helps.
