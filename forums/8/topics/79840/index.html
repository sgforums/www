    AT A'FAMOSA GOLF RESORT, MALACCA

    HE was looking for his golf ball when he came across what he thought was a log. Thinking that his ball could be under it, Mr Hong Kee Siong grabbed the piece of wood and lifted it.

    Bad mistake.

    The 'piece of wood' turned out to be a crocodile, and Mr Hong was actually grabbing its tail!

    The maneater turned around and snapped twice at his leg.

    PUNCHED CROC

    The Malaysian company director, 42, said that the crocodile then tried to drag him into the water.

    That was when he tried one of Crocodile Dundee's stunts. He punched the croc's head. The knock did the trick and the reptile released him from its deadly jaws.

    Mr Hong is now suing A'Famosa Golf Resort Berhad in Malacca for negligence.

    Yesterday, he named the resort as defendant in a suit at the high court in Kuala Lumpur.

    Mr Hong's golfing misadventure took place on Jan 22, Chinese New Year eve, this year.

    The attack took place near the appropriately named Crocodile Nine, Hole 7, reported Malaysian newspapers.

    Mr Hong claimed that the resort was negligent in not detecting the presence of a crocodile in a golf course which was a place for recreation and sports and open to the public.

    He said that it was the first time that he was visiting the resort and only saw the notice board with the 'Beware of Crocodile' sign after the incident.

    He said he was also not told by the staff about the presence of crocodiles in the resort.

    He told reporters outside the court how he was attacked.

    He was trying to get up a slope when he came across 'a piece of wood'.

    'It turned out to be a crocodile. It bit me,' he said.

    He said the crocodile was about 4.5 to 5.5 metres long. After he managed to escape from its jaws, he said he was taken to the Tampin Hospital in Negri Sembilan.

    SHOCK

    Mr Hong claimed that he was seriously injured on the left leg, which required 30 stitches, and suffered mental shock and anguish.

    Traumatised by the attack, he said he had not hit a golf ball since.

    His lawyer wrote to the resort on Apr 26 informing it of the incident but to date had not received a reply, he added.

    He is seeking general damages including aggravated and exemplary damages, RM286 ($130) in special damages, interest, costs and other reliefs deemed fit by the court.

    Source: Newpaper
