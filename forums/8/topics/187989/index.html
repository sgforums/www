    While the essence of morality is the limitation of self-interest, Confucius is clear that this does mean complete denial of self .We had already seen a hint of this with one started with the character "self" and ends with the characters for "others". If what you don't want for yourself, you shouldn't to do others, then you would like others to do for you what you would indeed like for yourself. We see a similar word structure, and stronger implication, "If you desire to establish yourself, also establish others"..Allowed me to quote "This sounds more like Mohism called "mutual profitableness", but it is clearly essential to Confucius. The idea is distilled in a modern Japanese saying, jiri rita, "self profit, profit other" or "self-interest altruism". This can, of course, also be read in Chinese, contains different characters for "self" and "other" than Confucius uses, but these could easily be substitued. Helping oneself and others at the same time is characteristic ofwhat we might even call the "worldiness" of Confucianism and Chinese civilization.
