    A little info about the day...

    Eid al-Adha is a religious festival celebrated by Muslims worldwide as a commemoration of Ibrahim's (Abraham's) willingness to sacrifice his son Ismael for Allah. It is one of two Eid festivals that Muslims celebrate (Muslims in Iran celebrate a third, non-denominational eid ). Like Eid ul-Fitr, Eid ul-Adha begins with a short prayer followed by a sermon (khuba). It is also called the bigger Eid because it lasts a day longer than Eid ul-Fitr[citation needed].

    Eid al-Adha is four days long and starts on the 10th day of the month of Dhul Hijja of the lunar Islamic calendar. This is the day after the pilgrims in Hajj, the annual pilgrimage to Mecca in Saudi Arabia by Muslims worldwide, descend from Mount Arafat. It happens to be approximately 70 days after the end of the month of Ramadan.

    Sheep distributed to mosques ahead of Hari Raya Haji sacrificial ritual

    SINGAPORE: At least 5,500 sheep have been safely delivered to 43 mosques and three organisations.

    The animals are meant for the kurban, a sacrificial ritual carried out as Muslims mark Hari Raya Haji or Aidil Adha on Thursday.

    The sheep were brought in by a company called Mini Environment Service (MES).

    MES had won a tender set up by a joint committee of mosques, which oversees the sacrificial ritual.

    This is the first time that the task of supplying the sheep had been given to a private company.

    In previous years, this was handled by the joint committee of mosques.

    Each sheep costs about $226, which is the lowest price quoted among the three companies that submitted their bids for the tender.

    After factoring in other costs and GST, each sheep costs $293. This is higher than the $270 set for each sheep for the past three years.

    The sheep were distributed from 10pm on Tuesday to 5am the next day.
