    Originally posted by Parka:
    This is more of a review for the board game.

    I was dragged to this board game cafe by my friend just now to play a game called "Cashflow".

    Has anyone played this game before?

    Basically, it's a financial planning game that tries to simulate the real world. The objective of the game is to learn how to invest and earn a passive income.

    (Passive income = Income you get by not working. E.g. Buying a house and renting it out).

    For most of us, I think our financial equation is "income from salary - expenses=savings". Because I studied Business in NTU, I've already learnt the basics of financial planning.

    The new term for me today was passive income.

    The goal is to use the income from salary and invest in things that will generate passive incomes, like stocks, bonds, property......When your passive income exceeds your expenses, it means you don't have to work to support yourself anymore.

    Great game for people who are not that good at financial planning.

    But don't play too much. Play once and spend your time in the real world mastering the art.
    The place is called Settler's Cafe.

    Cashflow? Used to play, but I hate the hassle of writing and erasing my balance sheet and income statement. And of course, if you wanna make it more realistic, you have to add in house rules.

    The game cost about S$399, if you import and all that stuff.

    P.S: I go there rather often to chill out.
