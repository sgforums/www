The Queen Mother dies, aged 101

  • dreamer
    31 Mar 02, 12:52
    Queen Elizabeth, the Queen Mother, died peacefully in her sleep this afternoon at the age of 101, Buckingham Palace announced today. The Queen was at her mother's bedside when she passed away at Royal Lodge, her Windsor residence.

    Weakened by a recent bad cough and chest infection, the Queen Mother's condition deteriorated this morning and her doctors were called. She died at 3.15pm.

    Buckingham Palace said: "The Queen, with the greatest sadness, has asked for the following announcement to be made immediately: her beloved mother, Queen Elizabeth, died peacefully in her sleep this afternoon at Royal Lodge, Windsor."

    The Queen Mother's coffin is expected to be taken to the Royal Chapel of All Saints in Windsor Great Park tomorrow morning. No decision has yet been taken on whether the Queen and the rest of the Royal Family will attend Easter service as planned at St George's Chapel at Windsor tomorrow.

    Members of the Royal Family were informed of the Queen Mother's death before the news was released to the Press Association at 5.45pm.
    The Prince of Wales and his sons Prince William and Prince Harry were informed in Klosters, Switzerland, where they are on a skiing holiday. They will fly back to England tomorrow.

    The Duke of York, who was also abroad, was told the news in Barbados where he was on holiday with his ex-wife, the Duchess of York, and their children Princess Beatrice and Princess Eugenie.

    The Duke is understood to be making arrangements to return home. Other members of the Royal Family were already arriving at Windsor for their traditional Easter gathering.

    Those at Windsor included Viscount Linley and his sister Lady Sarah Chatto, whose mother Princess Margaret died on February 9. The death of Margaret added to the strain on the Queen Mother, who had watched as her younger daughter suffered a series of strokes.

    The Queen Mother attended Margaret's Windsor funeral six weeks ago in a wheelchair. The seemingly indomitable Royal matriarch was determined to be at the funeral but looked pale and drawn behind a black veil.

    Senior royal advisers are expected to meet tomorrow morning to finalise details of the Queen Mother's funeral service. Other details, including opening condolence books for members of the public and possible charity donations, will also be decided.

    The Government is in discussions with the Speaker and other political parties about a date for the recall of Parliament so respects can be paid to the Queen Mother, Downing Street said tonight.

    The Union Flag was tonight flying at half mast at Buckingham Palace. A notice informing the public of the Queen Mother's death was placed on the gate outside.
