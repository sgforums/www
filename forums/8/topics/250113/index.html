    Ok since A level, I'll be less specific bah...No notes, only the former A level text which I USED to have, but moving on...

    First, Archimedes Principle. It states that the net fluid force on a body submerged (or floating) in a stationary fluid is an upward force equal to the weight of the fluid displaced by the body Therefore, buoyant force is an upward force which the fluid exerts on the object due to the weight of fluid displaced by the body.

    In a static fluid, weight of the fluid causes an increase in pressure with depth. At the water surface, it is atmospheric pressure, below it, as h increases, pressure increases:

    P = Po + pgh (p is density, g is gravitational accel, h is ht below the water surface)
