Ahmad Latiff to play for Crewe Alexandra for 2 years

    By Tan Kim Han

    AHMAD Latiff Khamarudin (right) will get another chance to parade his skills in Europe.

    The Woodlands Wellington midfielder, 25, who has been in erratic form this year, will be in England for a two-year playing attachment starting on Nov 14.

    Matt Brown, the new coach at Woodlands, has arranged for Latiff to be attached to English club Crewe Alexandra.

    Crewe are now lying 16th in the 24-team English Championship League, formerly known as the English First Division.

    Among their better-known players are Danny Murphy, now of Charlton Athletic, and Leeds United's former England U-21 midfielder Seth Johnson.

    Latiff underwent trials in Holland in 2000, but was unsuccessful.

    Said Brown: "Let me reiterate, it's a playing attachment and not a trial.

    "The agreement between me and Latiff is that he will play for Crewe for two season at least even if he has any offers from overseas."

    Brown is also talking to three London sides for an attachment for Latiff, including League One (former Second Division) club Brentford and a Premiership club (Arsenal).

    Latiff was not available for comment yesterday.

    Brown, former match analyst at Liverpool, hopes that the experience will benefit the talented but temperamental star.

    He said: "Latiff has played under many coaches. None have been able to change him. I'm just going to improve him."

    Latiff has not played for the national team since the 1-2 loss to Japan in a World Cup qualifier in March.

    He was sacked by national coach Raddy Avramovic in June for skipping training sessions before the qualifier against Oman.

    It is the second time that he has been axed by the national selectors for disciplinary reasons.

    When told of Latiff's attachment to Crewe, Avramovic declined to comment.

    But the Serb has always maintained that the door is not shut on Latiff if he proves himself worthy of selection.

    That he is talented is undoubted. But a question mark hangs over his temperament.

    In 2000, he left for trials in Holland in the midst of the national team's preparation for the World Cup qualifiers.

    Subsequently, he left for Indonesia to represent Club Persikabo.

    The Crewe stint is not the first for local players in recent months.

    Last year, Home United sent strikers Indra Sahdan and Sutee Suksomkit to Chelsea, thanks to sponsor Emirates Airlines.

    Earlier this month, Young Lions Baihakki Khaizan and Ridhuan Muhamad spent two weeks with Serbian club Red Star Belgrade's first team.
