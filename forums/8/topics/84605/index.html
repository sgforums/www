    I was molested at sales job interview
    SHE was desperate for a job and waited two hours to be interviewed.

    But instead of finding work, Ms Fazlina, 21, was allegedly molested by the interviewer at his shop on Thursday. Ms Fazlina (not her real name) is unemployed.

    She had called a chatline earlier in the week hoping to get a $1,000 cash prize.

    Rather than just leave her name and a short message, she recklessly left her handphone number in her introduction message.

    Over the next few days, five men called her.

    One was Joe, who called her on Wednesday.

    'We were chatting, then I mentioned that I was looking for a job. Joe told me he had a shop selling jewellery that had a vacancy, so we arranged to meet on Thursday at 8am,' said Ms Fazlina.

    The next day, she went to Golden Landmark shopping centre at 8am, but had to wait two hours outside the shop.

    'I felt quite suspicious when I saw the shop, because the glass panels were all covered with newspaper, and there was no-one else there, so it looked very spooky.

    'Plus I had to wait for so long - but I really wanted to get a job,' said Ms Fazlina.

    Despite the telltale signs, she took no precautions. She said that when Joe arrived, he took her to the office inside the shop, shut the door, and sat across the desk from her.

    'At first he asked normal questions, like what kind of job I was looking for and if I had any experience in sales,' she said.

    'Then suddenly, he asked me to massage him. I got a shock and said no. He kept asking and I got scared, so I stood up to leave.'

    But then, she claimed, Joe stood up, hugged her and pulled her towards him.

    'He was trying to kiss me, and I pushed him away, but he kept trying to grab me again and to touch my chest. Then I kicked him and he landed on the chair,' said Ms Fazlina, getting agitated.

    At that moment, her handphone rang. She blurted out that it was her mother calling, and ran out of the office and the shop.

    The caller was Mr Hafiz, 21, an NSman whom she had met on the same chatline.

    'I had called her to ask if she had got the job, but she kept crying, ' said Mr Hafiz.

    When Ms Fazlina told him what happened, he was furious, and helped her to call the police.

    A police spokesman confirmed that a man in his late 30s was arrested that morning at the shopping centre.
