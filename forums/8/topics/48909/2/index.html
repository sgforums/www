    Your missing the entire point. This is NOT about religion. Its definitely A OK in having a relaxed attitude towards life. Its A OK too not to be absorbed in materialistic welfare.

    BUT, this will only work fine if you live in the countryside depending on subsistence farming to feed yourself and your family.

    BUT we are in Singapore, a developing or developed country, where the pace of life is quick, where every minute is money, where every citizen counts. How much each of us put in is indirectly responsible for how well Singapore does on the world stage AND how our decesendents will live their lifes. Our forefathers have worked hard to build our foundations and bring us where we are today. If not for them, we will probably be still at the same stage with most of our Southeast Asian countries and not be known as one of the world's most thriving economy.

    If everyone was to adopt a relax deposition, will we have today, the world's best airport? The world's busiest ports? The top four economic powerhouse in Asia? Will we have such an extensive transport system? Will we have a higher standard of living compared to our neighbours? Or will we be bathing from rivers, living in slums, and having the kids work in some factory sewing footballs to keep the family afloat?

    As I said earlier, its ok to be relaxed if your living in some countryside depending on farming. Hell, who does not want to have a relax life? But in Singapore, we just cannot adopt this mentality. We have come so far and work so hard, its just not possible to put everything down and relax. We have too much to lose. As it is, Malaysia is already pitting themselves against us in many aspects.

    Life here is a rat race and everyone is working hard not only for themselves but for their children and ultimately, the welfare of the country as a whole.

    But if only a section of the people in a country is rowing the boat and another section are passengers and just admiring the view, what will become of the country? Do you think it is fair for a certain portion of the country's population to be rowing the boat? This is prevalent in Malaysia right now, that is why Mahathir is lambasting the Malays for the attitude towards life and wanting them to pull up their socks and match the Chinese. He has went as far as to set up various welfare schemes for the Malays, the Bumiputra... But still we see little improvement. Luckily, the situation is not as bad in Singapore.

    In ending, as I said earlier, some people here are against the Malays for a few reasons and adopting a relax atitutude towards life is just one of the few. "Houston, we have a problem" is definitely an understatement.

    Think deep, think hard, learn not to see things from the surface.
