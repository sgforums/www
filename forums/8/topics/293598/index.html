In the Artic, significant melting of sea ice and permafrost will occur and forests will advance north inside the Arctic Circle. As ice melts and seawater warms and expands, global sea level will rise from 1 to 3 feet (30cm to 1m). Some regions on Earth will become hotter and drier, others will become cooler and wetter. Agricultural productivity will increase in some areas and decrease in others. For example, recurrent, severe summer droughts are expected in the grain-growing middle of the United States.

    I have no doubt that as our global population continues to grow, there will be more competition for food and resources. If a famine hits somewhere or a certain resource is depleted (oil), the global population will decline substantially.

    Oil is used in the transportation of food and finished goods. Singapore is extremely reliant on import of food products, if there is a sudden depletion of oil in the world, the existing transport system will not be able to adjust fully.

    The global community should promote less reliance on oil and find other forms of energy. It's always good to develop another energy resource, should an oil shortage hit, with reliance on another form of energy, if food resources are allocated efficiently, the effects of global starvation would not be too drastic.
