    i dunno how to play baseball/softball, so cannot help with qn1. this is for qn2. actually this method is quite crappy, but now tired cannot think of the more "elegant" methods...

    a number from 4000-7000 means the 1st digit can only 4, 5 or 6, meaning 2 even and 1 odd.
    consider cases for each digit (i find considering cases crappy), where E stands for even and O for odd,
    case 1
    1st 2nd 3rd 4th
    E E E E : 1st digit has 2 choices, namely 4 or 6, 2nd dig 4 choices out of a pool of 5 even numbers from 0-9, 3rd dig 3 choices, 4th 2 choice = 2x4x3x2 = 48
    case2
    1st 2nd 3rd 4th
    E E O E : 1st dig has 2 choices, 2nd dig 4 choices, 3rd dig 5 out of 5 odd numbers, last dig 3 choice = 2x4x5x3 = 120. but the odd number can be placed as either 2nd or 3rd digit and is still considered as a different number, we take 120x2(for 2 possible slots to put it)=240.
    case3
    1st 2nd 3rd 4th
    E O O E : 1st dig has 2 choices, 2nd dig 5(being odd), 3rd 4(being odd also), last 4(since 1 even digit has already been "consumed" by the 1st digit) = 2x5x4x4 = 160
    case4
    1st 2nd 3rd 4th
    O E E E : 1st dig has 1 choice (have to be 5!), 2nd dig 5 choices, 3rd dig 4, last dig 3 = 1x5x4x3 = 60
    case5
    1st 2nd 3rd 4th
    O E O E : 1st dig has 1 choice (equivalent to "no choice" in everyday terms), 2nd dig 5, 3rd 4, 4th 4 = 80, BUT (refer to case2) since one of the "O" can either be in 2nd or 3rd (2 places to chuck them), we multiply by 2 ie 80x2 = 160
    case6 (last!)
    1st 2nd 3rd 4th
    5 O O E : 2nd dig has 4 choices, 3rd 3, last 5 = 4x3x5 = 60

    summing up all the cases, 48+240+160+60+160+60=728 ans!

    Let me have a try... the number question:

    if the first number and last number is even
    then the middle two numbers will have a pool of 8 numbers (10 - 2even numbers) will have permutations of:

    8! / (8-2)! ==> 8!/6! = 56
    since the 4th digit has only 4 numbers to choose from then when the 1st digit is even, the number of permutation is 56*4 = 224

    there are 2 occurrences of the 1st digit being even numbers so that's 448.

    as for the case of the first digit being odd.
    then number of permutation for the 2nd and 3rd digit is same (ie 56) but this time you multiply it by 5, obtaining 280.

    add them you get 448+280=728. hope it helps.
