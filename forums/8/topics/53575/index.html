    The last three posts are right on the money. Be around to give your friend an opportunity to talk about it, because talking is part of the process by which she will sort out how she thinks and feels about her father's death.

    You do not need to provide too much input. Occasionally agreeing with what she says or a simple "uh huh" every now and then to let her know she has your attention should be all that you need to do. At all costs, though, avoid saying, "I know how you feel".If she hits a sticking point, summarise the last few things that she said.

    People tend to go through five stages of grieving: denial, anger, bargaining, depression and finally acceptance. In the first four stages, the person will do or say things that may not entirely make sense. They may try to deny that the event has taken place, get angry at the deceased person for leaving or attempt to think that if they do something, things will be all right again. All of these are entirely normal. Make sure that they do not go doing something stupid, but at the same time let these stages run their course.
