    Most educational agents in Singapore will be able to handle enquiries on Australian Universities. They will be able to provide free service and arrangement (e.g. application, hostel arrangement, airport reception, and etc). Please note that this services are supposedly to be free. If the agent you have approached charge you for such service, just look for a new one.

    Australian University will also send their International department representative to Singapore for presentation or open house twice or more per year. Depend on the university and agent, you will be able to get your application fees waive if you submit them to the representative on the same day. (Save your money too.) Application will range from A$50 - A$100, depending on the university.

    IDP will also be able to arrange such procedure but they tend to leave the applicant to make their own decision in hostel and generally not too customer-friendly.

    Eight university in Australian formed the Group of Eight University. These universities are able to obtain more research fund from the government and generally you will not go wrong by joining them.

    Do however note that some university are good in some areas of studies but do not make it into the group of eight as the G8 focus on research.

    Good Luck

    A little info I should post here after I received a couple of PMs regarding the options for Singapore students who have completed O Levels and wish to study in Queensland, Australia.

    Unfortunately, there is no JC in Australia. There are two routes you can take, very much like Singapore:

    1) Australian High School

    The transition from their equivalent of "O level standard" is seamless. After year 10 (their equivalent of sec 4), students wishing to go to uni or other tertiary study can study for 2 more years. Complete year 11 and year 12, at the end of which you will sit for an exam and then obtain an OP score. This OP score then determines which uni and which course you can go. The lower, the better. Entrance to competitive courses generally needs very low OP (e.g. medicine must have OP 1, law OP 2)

    High school for international students costs roughly AU$8,000 a year. The more reputable ones in Brisbane where I live are Brisbane Grammar, Brisbane Girls. Generally hard to get in. Quite a few state schools take in international students, like Indooroopilly State High School. Please refer to the Queensland Government website for this. The URL is http://www.qld.gov.au/services_for_queenslanders/education_and_learning/international_students.html

    2) TAFE (Technical and Further Education)

    The second route is to go for a TAFE diploma course, like polytechnic. After a TAFE diploma is obtained (duration differs among courses, but usually 2 - 3 years), students with good results can apply for direct entry into 2nd year of uni in a relevant course. This is good if you already know what you want to do and want to start working as soon as possible. Again, more info at http://www.qld.gov.au/services_for_queenslanders/education_and_learning/international_students.html . As the TAFE programs are controlled by the State government, there isn't any TAFE college that is "better" per se than another. However this is subject to personal opinion.

    All universities in Australia are recognised in Singapore. This is because it is under the Association of Commonwealth University. However, some ministry or public sector are known to do theirs own universities screening when interviewing potential employees. It seem that there is no way to know their guideline unless you have friends or relative working in that HR department.

    G8 Universities in Australia are well known in Singapore and Australia. There should not be any problem if you want to look for a job with qualification from this universities. But the arguement is that this university are given the G8 status based on their research achievement and hence, totally irrelevant to universities whose are strong in non-research based subject.

    Macquarie University are strong in Finance and Deakin University are strong in Accounting subject. However, these two universities are no-where in the ranking. Do check with the Professional Bodies in Australia and Singapore if the specific course are recognised. (E.g. for accounting course, you can check with ICPAS in Singapore and CPA Australia) Do sufficient research before you decide which university to attend.

    Also note that the different states in Australia will also determine the amount of living cost you spend. Area like Melbourne and Sydney are cities area and generally more expensive than others.
