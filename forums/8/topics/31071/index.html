    The Great Orchard Rd Crush
    By Arlina Arshad

    THEY came in droves from opposite sides. They all wanted to move on, but no one wanted to give way. So they pushed and shouted.

    Meanwhile, babies bawled, balloons popped, grandmas shrieked and a claustrophobic woman swooned.

    This was the Great Orchard Road crush yesterday, in the underground passage connecting Wisma Atria to Ngee Ann City.

    The human traffic jam began building up when it started raining at around 2 pm.

    Hundreds of shoppers, including parents with babies and bags, pregnant mothers and grandparents, streamed in to use the retail link - with seven specialty shops on one row and Isetan on the other - at Wisma Atrium Basement 1.

    It never occurred to anyone that the sensible thing to do would be to keep to one side, letting by people coming the other way.

    Instead, most people walked five abreast and ended up bumping into one another in the middle.

    In the crush and the heat, 37-year-old Mary Tan, who was carrying her two-year-old son, fainted after reaching the 'finish line' at Ngee Ann City.

    She was taken to one side, and when she came to a few minutes later, she started crying. Passers-by passed her tissues and medicated oil.

    Dabbing her eyes with a tissue, Madam Tan said she had wanted to take her son to Paragon 'to see Santa Claus'.

    She added: 'I got stuck in there, couldn't breathe properly and felt very stressed.

    'By the time I reached the end, I felt like I'd lost all strength, and blacked out.'

    She called her friend, and said they would continue shopping.

    By 3 pm, the mall security officers were alerted, and four of them stationed themselves near the passage to direct those heading towards Wisma Atria to use the covered walkway on level one instead.

    This made some people angry. One young man barked at the security officer: 'You don't have the legal right to stop me,' before pulling his girlfriend and then barging their way through.

    Sales manager Teo Peng Huang, 35, who was with his wife and daughter for a family outing, was disappointed with how the people behaved.

    'Singaporeans are just inconsiderate and they won't give way. They want to rush, but they don't realise this contributes to the jam.'

    Before walking away, his wife said: 'Very scary, very scary. Really regret coming out today.'
