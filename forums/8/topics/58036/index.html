    It's eyewash! Kahn blasts critics

    Andy Hooper

    Bayern Munich and Germany goalkeeper Oliver Kahn has lambasted his critics and says he'll be the judge of when it is time for him to quit.

    The Bayern skipper has been troubled by a mysterious eye condition in recent months and has looked unusually fallible as the Bundesliga champions shipped 10 goals in six league games.

    But Kahn, hero of his country's march to the World Cup Final last year, has hit back at press scrutiny of his early-season performances, claiming it has been coloured by lurid tabloid tales of his private life.

    'I accept one per cent of the criticism but I will not take the remaining 99 per cent because it comes from people who have no idea what goalkeeping is about,' he told Kicker magazine.

    'I will play only as long as I can put up top performances,' he added. 'If that was no longer the case, I would quit straight away.'

    Bayern are fifth in the Bundesliga standings, three points behind leaders VfB Stuttgart.

    'Since certain things about my private life became public, the judgement of my performances is no longer objective,' Kahn told Kicker.

    Kahn made headlines for weeks in the German media earlier this year after admitting he had an affair with a barmaid while his wife was pregnant.

    The 33-year-old last week admitted that the eye condition had troubled him, but said that the blurred vision from which he had been suffering was no longer causing him difficulty.
