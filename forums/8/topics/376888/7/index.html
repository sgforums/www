    I did a little research on WKC just now and also spoke to a friend this evening regarding that brand. As I said before, I'm not too familiar with it...umm...doesn't seem to be that well marketed as compared to other brands. However, my friend and I agreed that the quality of the swords seem quite good (their average quality could beat CAS's average range). However, according to what I read on their site, they are using modern methods to make the blades. So don't expect something traditional.

    The blades are supposedly made in Solingen, Germany. According to my friend, it is a well known as the city for knives. It is a well known fact that a lot of knife craftsmen there made bayonets and knives for the Third Reich. However, how good they are at katanas is anyone's guess.

    Judging from some blade geometries, I'd say their line of katanas will excel in soft-med hardness target cutting. Hope that helps.

    Originally posted by Joeypoh2212:

    Of the 3 WKC blades on sale, the blue Hand Forged Folded Steel Katana seems to be the best made in terms of geometry. I can't tell much from the small photos cause I usually need really up-close pictures to judge properly but the fittings seem to be relatively good quality and the hamon (seems to be of kikusui design) has a nice hada showing.

    Again, these WKC blades don't seem to be built for hard target cutting. You may cut hard targets if you wish, however, you will notice that the edge blunts very fast because the edge geometry is not made for that purpose. All in all, a fine blade worth collecting. I wouldn't consider the WKC Tamahagane katana as the geometry isn't to my liking, I see some sections which just look kinda weird. Which is a pity cause the blade itself shows fine hada, though I can't say how pronounced it is by judging from small pics.

    Umm...sorry I can't disclose how sharp swords are brought in. Basically, sharp swords are restricted items, the police will inspect any incoming swords be they sharp or not. Sharp swords will be blunted if the police forsee that one is incapable of handling these weapons or properly storing them, especially if you have little knowledge on these items.

    For me, I'm a practioner. So I can easily attain a licence to bring in swords for my own use.

    To find out more, I suggest you refer to the police arms and weapons division website.
