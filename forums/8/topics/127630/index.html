    Hi again firered,

    Seriously I don't know what your worry is because there are loads of people above 21 taking martial arts.....21 is actually quite a young (and good) age to get serious on martial arts. And not everyone in martial arts is as fit as you think.....I've seen so many times people talking like heroes, but start panting like hell just after 10 lousy kicks.

    BJJ is Brazilian Jiu Jitsu, a modification of traditional Japanese Jiu Jitsu (a.ka. traditional Judo) by a Brazilian called Gracie (he's over 70 now and still active in the art). Its mainly ground fighting - very technical sort of wrestling. BJJ doesn't depend on grabbing of the Japanese gi, so its more effective for streetfighting situations (although scraping your back on the ground will hurt - ouch!).

    Since you have karate fetish , then karate is a fine art to start with (and cheap too - for someone in NS like yourself). You can change later, after a year or so, to other arts. Very often its the person which matters, not the art.

    Try joining classes with adults....(many classes are filled with kids only).

    Marine Parade CC has a seeminly good karate class, although I can't find their website anymore. Trying checking with the CC for details.

    If you're willing to travel a bit, the Shitoryu Karate Association at McNair Road (opposite Ceylon Club) is another very good place to learn and train. Lots of adult members, locals and foreigners. Link is here: http://ska.org.sg/

    Lastly, you may want to know that everything in this posting was written by someone who has never practiced karate before.
