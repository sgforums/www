  • Gedanken
    9 Apr 08, 18:08

    Pixel count is overrated - if all you want to do is print your standard-sized photos, 2 megapixels is enough resolution.  On the other hand, high resolution can make up for a lack of zoom.

    For starters, I'd recommend something with manual functions alongside full-auto - if you do decide to get more into photography, you can learn to manually adjust shutter speed and aperture.  Image stabilisation is fairly common these days, and it's handy.  Something with a fair amount of optical zoom (at least 6X) will also be handy - forget digital zoom because it only creates crap pictures.  If it takes AA batteries, all the better - you don't want to run out of juice halfway with nothing to replace some gollywog rechargeable.

    Have a look at Canon's Powershot A-series (e.g. A650, A720) - they're good beginners' cameras.
