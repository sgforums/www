    An important message for all Singaporeans:

    Article taken from ChannelNewsAsia.com

    ========

    Instead of allowing religious diversity to divide people, we should use religion to bring people of different faiths together, says the Minister of State for Community Development and Sports Yaacob Ibrahim.

    Associate Professor Yaacob was speaking at the Inter-Religious Organisation Day celebrations on Sunday.

    The celebrations saw many faiths coming together for one common prayer - for peace and harmony in Singapore and the world.

    Recent events have raised the question of religion's role in nation-building.

    Although fringe groups may seek to undermine harmony from time to time, Singapore leaders say the country's religious groups have worked well together.

    "Religious harmony remains strong. Because the majority of people are very steadfast in their faith, are very steadfast in their respect for others, they're very steadfast in their sensitivity towards others. So that's the more important thing," said President S R Nathan.

    For religion to remain a constructive force, Associate Professor Yaacob said all communities must work at promoting religious harmony.

    Besides taking a moderate approach and respecting the sensitivities in each religious community, Singaporeans must continue to expand the common space.

    And the Inter-Racial Confidence Circles (IRCCs) can only succeed if religious and community leaders get their members to actively participate in them.

    "There is no one model for an IRCC for all of us to follow. When I was studying in the United States, my fellow Muslim students and I would have our Friday prayers at the basement of a church. We learn to accommodate each other's needs. We got along well under the supervision of the priest of the church," said Associate
    Professor Yaacob.

    Such shared use of religious grounds is just one way of promoting interaction and understanding.

    For a start, there's now the latest Inter-Religious Organisation edition and its website was launched for better understanding of different religions in Singapore.

    Source: http://www.channelnewsasia.com/stories/singaporelocalnews/view/2685/1/.html
