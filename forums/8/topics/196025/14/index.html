    Circa 1960s..

    Bus companies - STC (S'pore Traction Co), Hock Lee Bus, Tay Koh Yat..

    bus fare only 5 cts..10 cts can get you a long way. There was a bus service from oustside the Maxwell Market to the other end of the route opposite the condo at the foot of Mt Faber. Total fare was only 10 cts.

    I remembered taking an electric STC tram bus. Kind of weird because it was so silent. During those times, it was everyone for himself/herself as far as seats are concerned..no proper queues etc. Bloody bus conductors always yelling.."Ow way chia..!!.."

    A steaming bowl of kuay teow fish ball soup cost only 20 cts and 30 cts got you mee-pok tah, solid one and damn nice. Chow Kuay teow only cost 30 cts a plate, can also bring yr own egg.

    There was no big dept stores around except a single one called Robinsons. Prior to the big fire which burnt it down, it was THE place to go to do yr Christmas shopping...I remembered especially the huge Marklin toy train set on an area about 15 tables size. They have a few trains running around which never failed to fascinate all the kids..

    The 2 stone lions at the Merdeka Bridge...
