    Well, the RSAF family isn't all that small. Not only do they include the likes of the pilots, technicians and aircrew, u cant forget the air defence guys and the air ops guys, in short, the C3 gang, hehehe..... For an A-Level, you might consider going for one of the study awards (maybe the ATA local or overseas??), or maybe u might choose to work for a while and go on a no pay leave to study (this is a tougher option), or study while u work (the toughest option).

    Anyway, in terms of prospects, after the pilots (which seriously the selection process is ridiculously tough), the next best vocations would be that of the ADA and Air Ops already. Especially ADA, whereby you can further fulfil the dreams of being a pilot, how..... dunno whether it is classified or not.......

    Many talk about red tape, beaurocracy, politiks and stuff, although I agree they are present in RSAF, but u find them everywhere, but only especially so in RSAF, so, well, your choice. This time, u r signing with the government, so u cant really wriggle around much. The maximum period of time I remember they can hold u back is, I forgot, 6 months to a year, whichever is the case. Anyway, you can also sign for the Saver's Scheme (at least that is what I remember)..... but remember, if u wanna join the force, join as an officer, the prospects are better that way.

    Just remember Bangster that your signature will carry you through the best yrs of your life. And you are a JC student too. If you were from poly, the incentive will be higher since u need some work experience before going to the Uni and going to the forces would be a good tradeoff for NS. But a JC student, that would be a little tricky. Would rather you go on to get your degree first. No such thing as "unconditional".
