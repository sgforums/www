    It's because most Singaporeans are not natural English speakers.

    English is not the first and main language for most Singaporeans.

    This is because of the bilingual policy. If you see the data the government release, most people read English papers compared to Chinese (because Chinese is harder to read and write), but most people watch Chinese TV and listen to Chinese radioes compared to English ones.

    Because of this bilingual policy and its effects on the linguistic ability of its population, this is a fundamental factor.

    An expert once predicted 200 years for English in Singapore to become the first and main language, and completely integrated in the national pscyhe.

    Bilingualism means biculturalism. And currently Singapore has not shifted so much to the English. I think as long as bilingualism exist, people will prefer their mother-tounge culture. Major sections of the population like to watch Chinese TV as a result.

    I think better funding and better management of English TV is fine. But it's superficial. The fundamental underlying will be harder to change.
