    in forming opinions of a person (esp. on irc/forums), we usually ignore his overall behavior and consider only whther or not he treats us well. This is unfair since the person's relationship with us might constitute only a minor percentage of his life activity (READ cyberworld ). What we often do, however, is magnify the fault that affects us and minimize or ignore the virtues that affect numerous other ppl. While it is wrong to do this with respect to others, it is a good antidote against pride for us to do it in forming great opinions of ourselves (heh heh heh... ). After all, we cheat ourselves with pretenses and lies, and ironically fail to do good ourselves while criticizing others. Were others to do these things, we would look down upon them, but not when we are the subjects in question.

    We would usually debase/discredit a person easily. Even a person who until now has been highly commended, will become demeaned in our eyes with the revalation of only one of his flaws or infirmities. when the tendency to accentuate a person's "unpleasant" behavior (according to one's social ethics) is combined with our opposite tendency to give greater weight to the good in our selves while ignoring our own imperfections, we have a wonderful formula for conceit.
