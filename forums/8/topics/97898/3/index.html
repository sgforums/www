    Jerry hopes to continue singing despite Singapore Idol exit

    SINGAPORE: Jerry Ong, who made his exit from the Singapore Idol contest on Friday, says he wants to remain in the music industry.
    The 28-year-old was gracious till the end.

    Even after his exit was announced, he was still all smiles and sang his farewell song together with his fellow contestants.

    He told Channel NewsAsia he's already busy making his post-Idol career plans.

    "This was like my biggest chance of cutting an album. Even though I am out, I still believe there's something about my singing, my voice which can capture people. I'll either be back in the underpass or singing at some other places."

    There were also words of consolation from the judges.

    "Jerry is a nice guy and an honest performer. He can do something somewhere in this industry and I think he should pursue it," said Dick Lee, one of the Singapore Idol judges.

    But Jerry's die-hard fans were inconsolable.

    "A lot of people have been saying bad things about him. I think he's very poor thing and I support him," said a fan.

    "He's so cute, we love Jerry!" said another.

    "I am quite surprised. I thought vocally Christopher was not up to what he should have been. If you compare them vocally - Christopher should have been out, but that's how Singapore voted," said a third.

    Some fans thought otherwise.

    "I wasn't really surprised because Jerry was not popular among all the people I know. I'm surprised Sylvester was one of the bottom three," said one.

    "I expected Jerry to get out. Jessie and Beverly were much better singers than Jerry. I'm not surprised at all," said another.

    With Jerry's exit, the title of Singapore Idol will be contested among the seven remaining finalists. - CNA
