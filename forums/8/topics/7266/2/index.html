    I didn't say that, you did.

    How can you possibly compare dogs to humans? Humans are able to communicate in ways that other humans understand, likewise dogs communicate in ways that dogs understand. The twist comes when humans starts making decisions for dogs. Humans are able to do that because we claim to be the most intelligent among other living things.

    This is my take on breeding.

    When it comes to getting pregnant, I'm sure that your mum made a decision that she wanted to bring you into the world, to love you and nurture you. She made the decision. She also made the decision on how many children to have and when to stop.

    With female dogs, when they come into heat, their instinct directs them to seek out males and to mate. Their instinct does not direct them to stop.

    There's nothing un-natural about them wanting to mate. However, when there's a human behind it all, it becomes very un-natural.

    I'm sure you know all about puppymills and backyard breeders. There are very ethical breeders who breed to improve the overall bloodline of a breed. There are also others who breed to make money.

    Ethical breeders know the risks involved and they take very good care of their females before allowing her to be bred. This is to ensure the health of their litter and their mother. No doubt they are making money, but at least the female is bred with ultimate care. Very often, the people who buy the pups are required to sign a contract stating that they will spay/neuter the pup by a certain age.

    I have seen females been forced into mating. It's not a pretty sight. Of coz I cannot expect soft candle-light and a romantic dinner, but surely the females deserve more than to be shaven down to just skin and being left in a small cage with a male.

    I believe if someone wans a pedigree puppy, get one from a reputable breeder. The SKC has the names and contacts. If someone just wans a companion dog, the SPCA, K9LOVE and ASD has plenty to offer.

    These are my views.
