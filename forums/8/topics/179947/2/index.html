    As has been pointed out, what exactly constitutes Australian culture? Peter Costello's got his heart in the right place, but by bringing in the issue of Australian culture and then not addressing it, he's messed up badly. On the other hand, opponents of Costello's stand have proposed that Australian values do not differ from common human values, and equally failed to substantiate what these values are.

    There was an interesting public debate about the Cronulla riots a while back. A number of Cronulla residents argued that the riots were a long time coming because the cops didn't do anything about the trouble that was obviously brewing. Some White Australians said that the Lebanese tended to hang around in large groups and sexually harrass and abuse women on the beach, while some Lebanese people said that they started hanging around in groups in the first place because they didn't feel safe. It's six of one and half a dozen of the other as to who's wrong and who's right and where it all started, but one thing's for sure - this is a vicious cycle that needs to be broken.

    Fact is that in broad strokes, Costello's right. Except for refugees, people who take up residency in Australia do so by choice, and if they don't like it, they can jolly well leave. If we're talking universal rules of conduct here, everybody knows that you don't enter another guy's house and proceed to sh!t on his living room carpet, so what's the beef? Costello's just stating the obvious, and that's a good point at which to start breaking the vicious cycle. I for one am glad that someone's finally got the balls to stand up and say something useful instead of hiding behind politically correct and practically useless piffle.
