    Ok, in time for my revision for exams!

    Kidney failure got 2 types.

    Chronic renal failure and Acute Renal failure.

    Chronic renal failure is gradual, the patient need not require dialysis immediately. We can't stop the illness from progressing to end stage renal failure( the kidney fails completely and dialysis is required) but can only slow down the progression through medication and diet.

    Acute renal failure is when you get it suddenly, from infections or accidents and your kidney lose function. The patient will require dialysis immediately. However, acute renal failure is reversible, unlike chronic renal failure.

    Some common causes are inflammation/ infections, blockage (due to cysts), injuries, tumours, damage from agents, insecticides, poisons, and other dieases like uncontrolled hypertension( high blood pressure) and the main cause is Diabetes.

    Just to add, your kidneys can lose 75% of their function before someone realise they have kidney failure.
