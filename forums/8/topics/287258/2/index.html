    Oct 23, 2007
    Copperfield's KL show cancelled

    KUALA LUMPUR - David Copperfield's performance in Malaysia this month has been cancelled after the FBI launched an investigation into the world-famous American magician and illusionist, a report said yesterday.

    The organisers of his two-day Kuala Lumpur stint, to be held next Tuesday and Wednesday, said the decision was made after FBI agents raided his warehouse in Los Angeles last week.

    'He needs to help in the investigations. Copperfield is not prevented from leaving the United States but he needs time to get to the bottom of things,' Arianna Event Management director Arianna Teoh said in The Star newspaper.

    FBI agents reportedly took away a computer hard drive and a digital camera memory chip.

    They also visited the MGM-Grand Hotel and Casino on the Vegas Strip, where Copperfield often performs.

    Mr David Chesnoff, lawyer for the 51-year-old magician, said: 'We understand there is an investigation, we are in touch with the investigators and are respecting the confidentiality of the investigation.'

    Teoh said Copperfield's performance could still take place later.

    At press time, Singapore event promoter Unusual Entertainments is still 'in discussion and pending further instructions from David Copperfield's management' to decide whether his shows on Nov 2 and 3 at the Singapore Indoor Stadium will go on.

    AFP
