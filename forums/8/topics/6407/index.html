    Hi there,

    Since we have a thread on fairy tales, we can start one long thread on a broad category of books which I think most people would read in everyday life.

    Well, my favourite science-fiction book is "Timescape" by Gregory Benford, and I liked "Eon", "Forge of God" and "Moving Mars" by Greg Bear, "Earth" by David Brin (who wrote the Uplift wars). I am in the process of trying Roger Zelanzy's "Lord of Light" which has been tipped by many as one of the groundbreaking books in science fiction, depicting a group of beings with higher technology but appear in the forms of Indian gods and Buddha. Also, for those Babylon 5 fans out there, do you know where the concept of telepaths come from? It came from the first Hugo winner written by Alfred Bester (recall Bester in B5) entitled the Demolished Man. Recently someone recommended "The Reality Dysfunctional" by Peter Hamilton to me, and I am planning to read it once I finished my books at home.

    In the realm of fantasy, there is J.R.R Tolkein with his Lord of the Rings series, Robert Jordan's "Wheel of Time" series, Janny Wurts' "The cycle of fire" and also Raymond E-Feist's "Magician" series. Hmm.. there are so many books to read in this two realms...

    How about you?
