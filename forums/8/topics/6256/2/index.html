    Hmm...due to the amount of "serious" discussions already on this thread, I'd thought I'd avoid hopping on the bandwagon of being too philosophical (as most of what I say would probably be very repetative and redundent anyway) and instead share some experience and observations on it.

    The point I want to make is that different groups of people will always see you differently. My JC classmates see me as an inactive and genrally reserved person; My NS campmates see me as a responsible, fun-loving person; My classmates in the uni see me as a workaholic. And I'm sure the forumites whom I have met have a different impression of me altogether. But no matter how each group sees me, I'm still me. Its just that I have to act differently in different environments and hence, earning a "mask" from each one. But when you take that down, what lies beneath is still you. I still remember once when I cracked a rather lewed joke in JC, many friends were rather surprised as most thought I was supposed to be the "guai guai" type. So there, my "mask" which had a halo above it suddenly had the halo removed; meaning people now see me differently; closer to my "true self" if you would.

    As for the mask we put on personally, I have to agree its done more as a form of protection (think its been mentioned many times). After all, how can I trust people whom I met not too long with my inner thoughts, inner desires? But this form of protection will still break down eventually anyway, when others know you better.

    That is why I really pity people who take pains to put on a mask to hide many things. Time will be its undoing; in accordance with my belief that truth will come to light one day. And often, this is the case.

    As to finding yourself, I'm afraid I do not have any satisfactory answers here. What I can say is always be true to your ideals and beliefs, because that is the essence of every individual behind the mask.
