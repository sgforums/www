    hope i can make myself useful here...

    CASE 1 :The hovering fly will move if the train is in variable velocity. (aka acceleration)

    CASE 2 : The hovering fly will NOT be seen moving if the train is in uniform velocity. (stationary or constant speed)

    Explained...
    Let's use a macro example: The earth is in constant rotation and if an airplane or bird
    is to fly in opppsite direction. What will happen? Have u ever seen a bird that's flapping it's wings(except humming bird!) and seems motionless ? No right? Cos the air surrounding the bird/plane is also moving at the same speed as the Earth rotates.

    Let's put this situation back to the MRT train. Assume the cabin is air-tight. Air inside it will move at the same speed because there is no outlet. The air is forced to move together with the train. As the fly hovers, it is carried along by the air. So it can be seen by a person inside the cabin that the fly in indeed hovering. But while u are looking from the platform outside, the fly is not as it's carried by the pocket of air inside.

    Another case is when the train is moving off,
    do u experience your body tends to move in the opposite direction? If the train is not in constant velocity, then although the fly tries to hover, it will move backwards.

    Ha! I think MRT too clean to see fly inside already.
    So I think a good illustration of my point is to look at those helium filled balloon children carried into the train. Look at how the balloon moves as the train accelerate, turns, constant speed and braking.
