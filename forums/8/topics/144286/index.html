    Think i found a way to do it after some researching. U have to rip the audio file from a CD or convert a exisitng Wav file on your HDD to 16 bits 96KHZ Wav (usually the norm will be 16 bits 44KHZ wav). The key here is the KHZ. Then convert it to MP3 16 bits 96Khz to save space. Now this method is ONLY FOR THOSE HAVING 16 BIT SOUND CARDS.



    U have to find a program which has the option of creating 96KHZ wavs and mp3s. Because 96 KHZ and above is DVD standard.....

    The desired will be 16 Bits 192KBS 96KHZ mp3 = DVD quality mp3!
