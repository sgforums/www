  • SingaporeTyrannosaur
    28 Aug 10, 01:36

    A war between Singapore and Malaysia or any of the ASEAN countries is highly unlikely, there may be saber (or keris) rattling at times but most of the rational head-of-states know that all this is just big talk. A real war would not be started unless there was a very compelling pushing factor to do so, as of yet it does not exist.

    The reason why Singapore invests so much in maintaining a credible armed force is that quite simply, it's economic survival is highly dependent on it having the means to secure if necessary, its interests by force given its geopolitical nature of lacking strategic depth and natural resources.

    If Singapore had not had a credible armed force, then of course other nations in the region would not hesitate in jerking us around when it comes to international policy. What? We changed the price of water? What are you going to do about it?

    That aside, you must also realize that our region is politically volatile in many ways. Malaysia, Thailand, Indonesia, Philippines have been dealing with long rooted ethnic insurgencies (although in the case of Indonesia it might be really a legacy of their past invading and attempting to "unify" the archipelago). A lack of a credible defense force will certainly lead to a spillover onto our nation. We can't shut our eyes and ears to the reality of our current situation. We didn't become a secure nation because our region was nice and peaceful... your grandparents and parents who were through the years of the Communist Insurgency can tell you that.

    Also a lot of people seem to assume that a war in Singapore means the Singapore military defending the island from a foreign invader, ala. in WW2. This is quite far from the defensive posture of the SAF. Singapore lacks the strategic depth and resources to fight a static battle of attrition, this means that when it comes to war our main option is to be able to take the fight to the enemy and end the conflict quickly and decisively. To this end the SAF has established extensively in technological force multipliers and force projection. I do not think the SAF intends to fight a war on our own soil, if our military planners know what they are doing.

    On the flip side, the big question remains. We have the means to protect ourselves but do we have the WILL to do so? This is not so much about having no practical combat experience. You must realize that in WW2, most of the soldiers involved, especially on the American side were little more then draftees given a rifle, basic training, and pushed into combat. Conscripts is less of an issue then the WILL to fight.

    And this is where the problem is, our government (ala. PAP) has done a good job of making Singapore many ways that it is, but they have been far less successful in instilling a sense of nationhood in Singaporeans. Most nations arise from a grassroots movement, a people with a shared identity and common vision, but Singapore was an accidental nation, created due to political issues. This does not mean that there is no Singaporean identity. The problem is that our government has issues realizing that Singapore is bigger then the PAP, and that Singaporean's ideas of Singapore may not always be theirs. Many aspects of "nation" building have been prescriptive efforts from the ministry instead of really knowing what the ground really feels and reflects. The Singapore of the PAP vision and Government line might be actually quite different from the Singapore that true-blue Singaporean know and love. 

    This is a serious issue, because if Singaporeans are always told that Singapore is something that it is not, then a disconnect takes place. People fight and die for nations because they are WORTH fighting and dying for. But currently after so long of being put up with an image of Singapore that we know is pretty much a facade, and then being told to defend it... that's a bit hard for some of us to take.

    Is is not surprise that some of us are unmotivated and dubious when it comes to NS? I know we can defend and kick butt if we need to... but WHAT exactly are we fighting and dying for? Somebody's else's idea of a nation that is not the same as in our hearts?

    Many of the current policies do not help.
