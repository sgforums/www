    FEB 27, 2004

    SilkAir offers 'budget' fare for S'pore-Phuket trips
    SILKAIR is taking a budget approach to trips. The Singapore Airlines subsidiary yesterday announced a one-way fare to Phuket, Thailand, starting from $60.

    This is about the same starting price as the Singapore-Bangkok fare on Malaysian budget carrier AirAsia.

    However, SilkAir passengers would have to pay $100 for the one-way trip back, making the round trip $160. The offer is valid for travel between March 26 and June 30.

    The airline is also offering cheaper fares of $150 each for those travelling in pairs and $130 each for a group of four.

    Recently-appointed SilkAir chief Mike Barclay insisted that the offer was not a reaction to competition from low-cost airlines.

    He said: 'This is not the first time we're offering low fares. The only difference is that before, we never used to shout about our promotions.

    'But more and more, Singaporeans expect value-for-money deals, so from now on, when we have such promotions, we'll make more noise.'

    The regular price for a round trip to Phuket is $310. The airline is also selling return tickets of $130 to Langkawi and $180 to Medan.

    By year-end, there will probably be three low-cost airlines set up here. All - Valu- air, Tiger Airways and the Singapore arm of AirAsia - are likely to fly to destinations up to five hours from Changi.

    This could pit them against SilkAir, which serves mainly tourist destinations in the region, and some experts fear the worst for the airline.

    But Mr Barclay is optimistic about the airline's future, as he believes budget airlines are more likely to be interested in landing in major cities, rather than offbeat destinations like Manado and Lombok, which SilkAir flies to.

    So far, the airlines are proving him right. Valuair, for instance, has said it plans to fly to Bangkok, Hong Kong and Jakarta when it starts operating in May.
