  • badboy
    30 Jan 00, 21:16
    The chances of a patient accepting part of a liver from a stranger ( eg an unrelated friend ) is something like one in thousands. A liver consists of 2 lobes. It is possible, and it has been done before, to remove one lobe from the donor and give it to the recipient. If everything goes well, in both the donor and the recipient, they'll develop the corresponding other half of the liver in as short as one month.
    As for the requirements, blood tests are a must of course. But there're many other things to test for, to assess the chances of a tissue rejection. Tests have to be carried out to confirm this. One thing to note though : A significant risk is involved in the donor as well during transplantation. This is because many of the blood vessels have to be resewn, and complications can occur. But there's still hope. As long as there's hope, there's a cure.

  • badboy
    30 Jan 00, 21:23
    rainboy :

    That'll depend on what stage the cancer is at. If it is in a very initial stage, where only a small part of the liver is affected, partial resection can be done to remove part of the liver, and the liver will regenerate in a matter of months. A full recovery is possible.
    However, if the cancer is detected too late, where metastasis has taken place, ie the cancer has spread, especially to nearby organs like the pancreas, then chances of recovery become very very slim, even with a transplant. The pancreas and liver are very very important. So it pays to take good care of them.

    p53 is a tumor suppressor gene which I doubt is being used as a therapy for cancer. In normal people like us, we have the p53 protein in us as a result of expression of the p53 gene. In essence, this protein prevents the abnormal, uncontrolled division of our body cells ( ie cancer ). It is when this p53 gene is suppressed that cancer becomes likely to form. However, there're many other tumour-suppressor gene other than the p53 gene. Keep your fingers crossed, breakthroughs in gene therapy are likely in the VERY near future.
