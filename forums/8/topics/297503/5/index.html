    i trust that you have read the reply from the cops about how their actions are calibrated according to the criminal procedure code, which decides which offences are seizable, and which are not. who came up with the cpc? the lawmakers lah. the police do not have the ability to deem an offence to be seizable or otherwise at will; it is only in accordance with the nature of the offence.

    if you'd really like it, then here are some numbers that you might want:

    court [general enquiries]: (65) 6336 0644

    Duty Registrar No: 65) 6332 4351 or 6332 4352

    and to quote your question, "what in the ****ing h**l do we need to make police report since the cops will come and go and delegate the job to some court?"

    well, in order to lodge a magistrate's complaint, a police report must be produced first. the magistrate will then decide whether or not police intervention is necessary, whether mediation between the two parties is sufficient, or if there are other legal ways to settle the issue.

    and you don't have to retrench cops to 5%. too many of them have resigned anyway. prolly to work at "the airport or do some admin/filing work", to put it in your words.

    hope the abovementioned answers your queries.

    most importantly, remember: just because an offence is non-seizable and the perpetrator is not arrested on the spot, it doesn't mean that he is being let off. at the day's end, a penal offence has already been disclosed.

    it just means that it might take a slight touch longer to nab him due to a need to calibrate the steps of prosecution in accordance with the law.
