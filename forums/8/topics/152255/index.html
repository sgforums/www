    Pros and Cons of different technologies

    Each implementation of the magnetic levitation principle for train-type travel involves advantages and disadvantages. Time will tell as to which principle, and whose implementation, wins out commercially.
    Technology Pros Cons
    EMS (Electromagnetic) continuously suspended when power is provided; proven, commercially available technology narrow gap between track and vehicle; not inherently fail-safe
    Superconducting EDS (Electrodynamic) systems large gap between track and vehicle currently (2005) reliant upon low-temperature superconductors that must be refrigerated with liquid helium; vehicle must be wheeled for travel at low speeds
    'Inductrack' system failsafe; good suspension distance; no power required to be provided to the magnets new technology still under development (2005)
