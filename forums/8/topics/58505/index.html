    Chihuahua, the mythical northern state of Mexico on the border with the
    United States, is fast
    becoming a catchphrase. In Chihuahua, the title
    of the new Coca-Cola ad, the guitar and voices come in tune thanks to the cola soft drink. Coca-Cola's traditional spirit - an optimistic viewpoint on life, where Coke is found in everyday situations that become special - is served up with a twist.

    Buenos Aires was the city with a Metro system chosen for filming. All of the characters are Argentines. The film was made in June, during four nights in which work took place from midnight to 5 a.m., with 300 extras.

    The ad was made in a real subway car, since the
    agency and ad director Andy Fogwill's goal was to create a sensation of reality. In fact, the train car did not stop moving from one station to another. One starring scene in the ad is the drinking shot, which was created using a travelling circular that went in and out of the car, a surprising effect in which the camera surrounded the main character, who is sitting in a seat. The cinematographic complexity ended in that scene. A large part of this last production's success is undoubtedly the catchy Mexican refrain, based on an old mambo that was re-composed for the campaign.
