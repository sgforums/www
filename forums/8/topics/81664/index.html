    It's not a scam... or rather.. not entirely a scam.

    Basically you pay $60(or $65 considering currency fluctuation) for an overpriced CD full of ringtones and games and whatever.

    You then get to join a queue for your phone. For the Nokia 7610 , there is a queue of 35 people. Once 35 people select the 7610 as a free gift after buying the CD , the 1st guy at the top will get the phone free.

    The second guy then has to wait for another 35 people to buy the overpriced CD and select the 7610 as their gift for him to get his phone and so on..

    Or you can get 35 referrals to get your phone faster , jumping queue in a sense. Means you just sucker 35 people to put you as their referral and you get your phone! These 35 people must also buy the CD though... This is useful cause normally you'd be in the 5th or 6th position.

    Anyway , there are quite a lot of these websites around , offering XboX , SonyEricsson Z1010 , Nokia 7700 etc. etc.

    I didn't pay for the CD though , it might mean me buying a useless CD for nothing. And at 60 bucks too!
