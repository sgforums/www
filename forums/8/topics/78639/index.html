    well..if you take it that one drop is as small as one molecule, as thats the smallest size the drop can be.

    for this example, we shall assume that the liquid is water. 1ml of water would have a mass of 1g. the relative atomic mass of a water molecule would be 18 (1x2 + 16, H2O wad). Using the mole concept, 1g/18 would give you 0.0556mol (3.s.f). to get the amount of molecules in 1g of water, multiply the amt of moles by avogrado's constant, 6.02 x 10^23, and this is = to 3.34712 x 10^22 number of molecules in 1g of water.

    Therefore, if you take the drop size as one molecule of water, there would be 3.34712 x 10^22 drops in 1 ml of water
