    22 Feb 02, 12:56
    Originally posted by potato82:
    what is a good uni in UK or US for Computer Studies???
    If you are into research positions, of course its MIT, Cal-Tech etc. But let's be more realistic here and omit schools like Carnegie Mellon (best for Computer Science).

    If your aim is Microsoft, students from the following are given priority for internships:
    1) Seattle Univ.
    2) Univ. of Washington

    If you aim for the Silicon Valley, the following has strong links in the industry:
    1) UC - Berkeley
    2) Santa Clara University (follows Stanford Univ's Curriculum)
    3) San Jose State Univ.

    If you are more into Info Tech:
    1) UC - Irvine

    If you are into gov. positions:
    1) Univ of Illinois - Urbana Champaign
    (the NCSA, National Center for Supercomputing Applications, is just there)
    Again, this is more of a research position.

    If you are into computer security, you will be better off majoring in Mathematics, or do a CS + Math double. The NSA (National Security Agency) mostly recruits mathematicians to deal with encryption/decryption etc.
    Mathematicians make the best programmers.

    Unlike sg, private schools in U.S. are generally better than state schools.

    Potato: read my posts above for a list of some US colleges. Costs vary from country to country, state to state, and even city to city. Private schools are generally more expensive due to better facilities and smaller class sizes. I think AU will be a better option for you, as you have to put living expenses into consideration, on top of tuition fees. Check the school's website for more details on the estimated annual costs, and maybe add around $5000 for living expenses.

    monkeying: In my opinion, RMIT is over-rated, partly due to the name of the school (typical Singaporean mentality). Its tuition fees are extremely high compared to other schs, but its accomplishments pale in comparison to its peers. To put it bluntly, they are there to suck your $$$. I think other reputable schs shld have sufficient facilities for their students, but you'll probably get your own computer instead of working and sleeping in computer labs. Most private colleges generally have better facilities anyway. From what i know, Curtin receives pretty generous fundings for research purposes.

    Monash is not bad, and a very popular choice among Singaporeans. But its more of a business school, rather than a technical one. If you want to do some networking, that's a rather good place to be. Most ppl I know go there for business and communications.
