    $1.7K seems to be a bit thin. Let's see.

    $1.7 - $800 = $900

    So u would have $900 to spend per month.

    Petrol costs are going up. If u are driving everyday, u might be paying a monthly petrol consumption cost of about $200-$300 a month. This would depend how big is ur car and the distance travelled of course. But in this case, I will be a bit more conservative at $300. So,

    $900 - $300 = $600.

    Road tax goes with the size of ur car, so the smallest car would be 1L, and that would be:

    $400 + 0.25 * (EC - 600)
    = $400 + 0.25 * (1000 - 600)
    = $400 + 0.25 * (400)
    = $400 + 100
    = $500 per yr.

    So u would need to save $500/12 = $41.67 a month.

    $600 - $42 (rounding up) = $558

    How about the insurance and ur monthly expenditure? It's kind of thin.
