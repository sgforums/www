    the best method is still strict diet and constant exercise.... sure fire way

    strict diet means counting calories not necessarily cutting down food. daily calories must be higher than 1500 kcal (2000 kcal for 75kg and above) or else body will go into starvation mode then the body will take in whatever food and store it up as fats. reduce fat intake (reduce oily stuff). drink more water. hot food can help burn some fats. calcium can increase metabolic rate (but milk and cheese are a bit tricky cause they have fats too).

    constant exercise helps to increase metabolic rate to burn fats.

    3 types of metabolic rate. resting metabolic rate, exercising metabolic rate and feeding metabolic rate.

    resting metabolic rate burns abt 60% of daily fat burn. It is increased through more exercise per week. exercising metabolic rate burns abt 25% of daily fat burn. this depends of the type, length and intensity (calculated by ur heart rate) of your exercise. feeding metabolic rate burns abt 15% of daily fat burn. it can be increased by taking 5-6 half meals instead of 3 full meals (total calories counts should still be the same).

    for exercise try more aerobic exercise like cycling, jogging and swimming. try all these 3 instead of jogging only because the knees and the ankles take a lot of pounding when jogging. if only jogging is the only aerobic exercise, do not increase the distance more than 20 % at a time.
