    Originally posted by NeonTetra:
    care to explain?
    If you paid attention to NDP, there are three Colours based on the Singapore Flag between the RSN and RSAF Guard-of-honour. The three may resemble the State Flag, but they have the emblems of the services charged on it. That three "flags" are the State Colours. They have been consecrated by various religious leaders, and their protocols dictate them as "Colours".

    The Singapore Flag that is flown at flagpoles and homes are not individually consecrated, and their purpose does not qualify it to be termed a "Colour".

    Another way for the layman to differentiate them are by the physical details. A flag is usually not fringed and corded.

    For more information on local flags/colours, you can refer to this page... http://www.geocities.com/inescutcheon/FlagsMilitary.HTML
