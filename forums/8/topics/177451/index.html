    in malaysia, 012 belongs to maxis. 01xxxxxxx belongs to mobile lines, 02 to 09 belongs to state land lines. and even mobile lines have locality differences.

    010 - Celcom
    011 - Atur 450
    012 - Maxis (Hotlink Prepaid)
    013 - Celcom
    015 - Jaring
    016 - Digi
    017 - Maxis
    018 - Telekom Wireless In Local Loop
    019 - Celcom
    Note that 015 and 018 arenÂ’t mobile phone networks, but are VoIP broadband lines.

    02 - Singapore (yes it works, trunk call)
    03 - Selangor/Wilayah
    04 - Penang/Kedah/Perlis
    05 - Perak
    06 - Negeri Sembilan
    07 - Johor
    087/088/089 - Sabah
    082/083/084/085/086 - Sarawak
    09 - Pahang/Terengganu/Kelantan

    a maxis mobile phone registered in KL (wilayah) will carry the prefix 0123xxxxxx. simarily, +60124930031 is indeed a maxis number registered locally in penang. this prefix is useful in calculating the cost of calling ur fren in other states - the actual difference is determined by how far you are (location) and the location where your fren's mobile/fixed line is registered.
