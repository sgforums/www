    It depends on what type of position you're applying.

    If the jobscope involves production, you will have to follow the 12 hr shift patterns as production runs 24/7 in semicon industries.

    Some shift patterns are 2 days 2 rest, (same as police)
    Some are 3days, 2 rest, 2 days, 3 rest.
    Some are 4 days, 2 rest(work for 1 month Day shift, next change to night shift).

    If you count the number of working 12 hrs days, it is true that you wokr for about 15-20 days per month only.

    But if spread out say normal 5ays work week, each day work 8 to 9 nrs, it still equals to 15-20 days per mth, 12hrs per day.

    It all depends whether you can tahan or not.

    Some cant tahan, but some like cos they can rest more days.

    Realistically, if you're a dip holder or a avg deg holder, working 12hrs shift will not get you far in terms of career prospects, cos you are stuck with production and you have little chance to interact with management.

    Of cors, if you happen to catch the eye of management, and they think you have prospect, they will prefer to ask you work normal shift.

    Some ppl prefer to stay away from management, prefering to piah for OTs and higher pay by working in shifts.

    Feel free to PM me if you have any queries and I'll do my best to answer.

    Cheers & Best of Luck!
