    I now understand how people got their systems below the 1k mark , the graphics card and motherboard were the main reasons.

    IMO , if you want a gaming system , you got to pay more for a better graphics card , simply put , the card you are getting now will be obsolete in around 6mths. Mine is starting to show its age in games like Doom 3(playable at high resolutions but not good enough to justify the price)

    And with the coming releases such as Need For Speed Underground 2 , which some people are complaining already with their cards being the nVidia 5900 XT and 9600 PRO , your card(Radeon 9250) , i can safely say , won't last long. I also dare to say , you will have trouble with HL2 should u decide on playing it.

    I think , for a better bet , in the long run , get a higher end config. My setup lasted me almost 10 months and now it's only starting to show its age in terms of graphics and HDD space , for the amount i paid , i think it was a good bet.

    I have friends buying computers worth 1k and below , complaining after just 2 months , that they have problems running CS Source or Unreal 2. Doom 3 is just crawling.

    To me , price , although it is the main deciding factor , should be put aside for sound opinions and suggestions.

    All this talk about getting the cheapest will only result in you getting what works Today , and possibly not Tomorrow.

    MSI 865PE Neo2-PLS $245
    Intel pentium 4 3.0C 512kb 800mhz $235
    PC3200 400mhz kingston CL2.5 256MB x 2 $174 ($87)
    Maxtor 80GB 7200rpm + 8mb $115
    ATI radeon 9250 128MB $93

    I suggest Hitachi 7K250 120GB with 8mb buffer for $149. Because frankly , i feel scared after reading in HWZ about people's Maxtor dying. I have 2 myself but 1 is dead already , now only 1 is left and i am just praying it will hold.

    Motherboard , i suggest Asustek P4S800D , this supports 8X AGP , Dual Channel DDR , HT technology , SATA and USB 2.0. $154.

    Add in a little more and get a Power Color 9600 Pro 256mb memory for $229 or 9600 Pro 128mb memory for $203. I suggest 128mb memory for $203 cause games don't fully utilise 256mb memory anyway , and even if they do , this card won't be able to handle it well in the first place.

    I also changed the ram for your to TwinMOS , you won't be overclocking so i doubt 2.5CL Kingston ram is needed. To me , memory is all the same unless you're into overclocking.

    I also suggest buying in a BUNDLE. Meaning you should really try and buy the Motherboard + CPU in 1 shop. There is usually a bundle price and that will shave $30-50 bucks off easy. And if the 3.0C isn't available , get the 2.8C , 200MHz isn't that big a difference to everyday usage. So this might help shave off more.
