    Originally posted by Manager433:
    Anyone knows if a 'Fluid' Harddisk is better or IDE is better?
    Fliud motor is just having a very thin layer (in microns) of lubricating fluid (not industrial type) between the bearings and the contact area of the rotating spindle. seems like Seagate (e.g barracuda) has drives with a FDB motors. so when you shut down the system and power down your drive, usually you will not hear the motor spinning down as compared to other brands of drive (e.g maxtor drives).

    many comments on the type of motor but most HDD manufacturer promises 3-5 years of HDD life despite the type of spindle drive they are using on their products . IDE is just the term on the interface with the mainboard and should not be taken as comparision with FDB motors. you should compare IDE (the interface) with SATA (serial ATA), ultra 320 or Fibre channel. the 3 menntioned has transfer rate (rate at which data is channel to and from the HDD) which can be compared and weighted for pros and cons.

    Prescott is the latest "range" of Pentium 4 processors. Think the pin count is still the same "478" but check with the counter staff to see if the board and the processor u thinking of getting would work together anot.

    3.0E/3.0A/3.0C indicates which fsb(front side bus speed) the processor works with or the amount of on board cache it has. 3.0E is the extreme edition processor, 2mb on board cache(i think) extremely expensive. 3.0A i think is a slightly older processor that runs on 533mhz fsb and has 1mb(i think again) of on board cache. 3.0C is the processor that runs at 800mhz fsb with 512k of on board cache.     more info. prescott is set to replace the aging northwood line. it is coded as 'E'. all the 'C's, 'A's are northwood. people complained prescott isn't up to mark. it runs hotter(bad for overclock), and doesn't have much performance boost over northwood 'C'. fyi, most techies prefers northwood over prescott. that is, just get 'C'.

    for ram, pc2100 = 266mhz, pc2700 = 333mhz, pc3200 = 400mhz. most newer systems support 400mhz, so get that. if your board uses dual channel ram, get twin pack(2 sticks of 256mb), the rams have to be identical in order to work. fyi, dual channel helps boost performance greatly.

    also, at least get a 9600pro($200). i recommend chaintech 5900xt($300). better still, sapphire 9800pro($400).

    like the rest, if gaming is what you has in mind, AMD is the only choice.
