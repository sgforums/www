    (taken from CNA)

    S'pore to open fifth polytechnic to cope with Dragon Year baby boom

    Singapore is opening a fifth polytechnic next year, partly to cope with the 1988 Dragon Year baby boom.

    The new Republic Polytechnic is likely to be built on a plot of land next to Woodlands MRT station.

    It will admit its first batch of 800 students next year, but its intake is expected to grow to 1,800 by 2005.

    It will focus on engineering, information communications and applied sciences, especially bio-medical sciences.

    Education Minister Teo Chee Hean told MPs on Monday that Republic Polytechnic will also adopt a new teaching approach.

    Business models will be integrated into its courses to cater to the industry's need for multi-disciplinary students.

    Explaining the rationale for another polytechnic, Rear-Admiral Teo reminded MPs of the 1988 Dragon Year baby boom.

    He said, "The first batch of this larger Primary 1 cohort came into primary schools in 1995 and are expected to reach poly level in 2005. The 4 polys are already operating at full capacity, and to cater to the increase in student cohort size eligible for poly education, we need 4,000 additional places for pre-employment training every year."

    More students are also going to polytechnics each year, some 40 percent of a bigger Primary 1 cohort, compared to 28 percent a decade ago.

    The new polytechnic will also cater to the growing demand for continuing education and practical training among adults in Singapore.

    The Education Minister said Republic Polytechnic will also be a chance to inject new ideas and catalyse improvements in the education system.
