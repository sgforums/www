    A half-fuc*ed essay but at least there won't be so many of them for you to waste time on anymore.

    Why I will not write anymore.

    Words, words, treacherous written words! Writing traps my thought and forces me to commit while my thoughts never stay the same. I can deal with them changing but one day when someone reads what I have written he will not know that they are no longer true.

    Writing also simplifies the thought into an expressible form. It depends solely on descriptions and/or illustrations and forces me to label things so that they can be identified by the readers. Oh, what do I care about the readers!

    There was a time when I loved pouring my feelings into stories, poems, letters and journal entries. It started off as a way to deal with the havoc my emotions create inside me. However, after a while it became a way to communicate those feelings to the readers, a circus exhibition or a published autobiography. Why did I want to share them, why did I let others scrutinize what could have been mine alone? Why did I need to be understood and why did I let myself be dependent on others, the oh-so-fickle others?

    I wish I could write these paragraphs in purple. They look so pretty on my foolscap paper.

    mayaJ - unlike terence, i shall have to reply without stroking your hair or gently touching your cheek. i hope you can find it in your heart to forgive me.

    "Won't it be superb if we can get rid of this dependency on people whom at the end of the day don't care about us all that much."

    maya, my dear child....from which secret corner of your beleaguered soul comes this ever-growing need to renounce the world? what desire does it satisfy? at what goal does it aim?

    methinks your writing is a means to self-discovery. towards finding yourself. but let me elaborate on that by replying to your question:

    "why do we have to surrender to humanity, anyway?"

    the surrender is not to humanity. if there were to be a prisoner, it would be the solitary soul. humanity frees us and is the enemy of captivity. only within dialogue can the seeds of emancipation bear fruit. the Other is our salvation, don't you see? but how?

    firstly, understand that the Other defines your Self. it bestows identity. it teaches independence. only in relation to someone else (the Other) do you understand yourself for who you are. meaning arises from the play of differences. your "need" to write reveals u as a part of humanity, which always relates to the world in a discourse of addressivity, as it strives to define itself. to be human (to be who you are) is to relate, to address. you are being only human, maya my dear.

    so how has your need bound you in chains? the only chains that hold u are the chains of signification. and these chains give autonomy even as they keep you in bondage. there is no creature more mythical than absolute freedom. the identity (the Self) you gain from the Other sets you free as much as it keeps you within the walls of your sense of being. identity enables as much as it disables. but is there really the possibility of a different paradigm? u feel trapped within the demands of humanity only because (i suspect) you have yet to find yourself. and finding yourself (if that is to be your goal) cannot be done in solitude. for what u find in solitude, u cannot give back to the world.

    "as for misleading words, so what's the point of writing it down when you know tomorrow it may not be true and the readers too know that it's something iffy?"

    you have said it yourself. writing, said maya, "forces me to label things so that they can be identified by the readers." in a world of constant change, we have a psychological need for identity and stability. the fixity of writing (assuming a fixity of interpretation) is a means of creating a self-identity. a paradox of the human need for the absolute in a heraclitean world of flux.

    "say, what i've written, what's the point of you reading it if you know that I may think it's rubbish by the time you log into the forum?"

    it matters not. you would then have a new understanding of yourself. a new mayaJ. all that matters is self-creation. and that you have done (and will always do) with each new word that you write and with each new utterance you make. self-understanding presupposes the Other. it presupposes you address the Other. if only so you can better find yourself.
