    The way i see it everyone in society has two options when choosing to map out their life.

    1st would be the most obvious....push yourself in academia.....reach for as high as you can go...and by this i mean, in terms of individual lifestyles and when a person feels that enough is enough and he needs to begin his career...or a popular deviation to this would be multi tasking the two....study and work...and improve yourself by targeting the market that is catered to the neediness of paper qualifications.

    the 2nd option is to pick a trade...and to develop skills by practicing that trade..many think this to be a highly risky and tedious path...but i've known alot of people who succeed in this path....they are normally people who have found their calling or passion in life and paved a way for themselves through hard work and determination focused in that direction.

    Example: A friend of mine is totally hopeless at academia....he hasn't the patience or time...but since young he has always liked to do this particular trade....so he enrolled in a course....practiced on his own....and pushed his way to be next in line for a creative director promotion....and he's only 23 this year...he started 3 years ago.

    Another example would be myself....I could never stick to one interest so i couldn't pick a particular trade to immerse myself in....so i'm paving my path through studies....hoping to get by on paper qualifications.
