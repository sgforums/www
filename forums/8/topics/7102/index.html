    For a start, I hope that there will be no flames here. Intellectual discussions on the topic. Hate-posts are quite pointless.

    Let's face it. Man U can't have 5 strikers. Even ignoring the question of the wage bill, how on earth are u going to keep them happy and sharp? Strikers need matches to stay sharp, to keep the goal-scoring instinct.

    Let's look at the current 4 strikers.

    Andy Cole. Fast. Good first touch. Quick on the turn. Can't hold the ball up. Misses too many chances. Can shoot outside the box. Out and out striker.

    Dwight Yorke. Fast. Holds up the ball well. Very good at scoring once in the box. Can't score long range. Weak in the air. Can play a holding position behind the front men.

    Ole Gunnar Solsjkaer. Powerful shot. Very accurate shooter. Good at off the ball running. The striker in the squad which misses the least chances. Out and out striker.

    Teddy Sheringham. Very good at holding up the ball and passing to other players in good positions. Best at playing just behind the front men. Not fast. Old. More of a provider than a scorer.

    So, let's look at Ruud van Nistelrooy.
    23. Powerful shot. Strong in the air. Quite fast. Ability to put chances away. Good with both feet.

    I would probably sell Andy Cole. He's contributed greatly to Man U in the past 2 years. I feel sorry to let him go. He'll be a threat in other teams. However, Cole has a big problem. He needs pple to get the ball to him. Once the ball doesn't get to him, he's useless. Moreover his defensive qualities are ZERO. Yorke can defend. Solsjkaer can win the ball in midfield. Sheringham as well. Cole is just a scorer, if he puts the chance away that is. True, he has class and ability. Still, letting him go would be the safest as I feel that out of the 4 of them, he's the one who can most easily be cut off. So he won't pose too much of a problem when he faces Man U in the future.

    I would sell Cole to Tottenham to get them to sell Sol Campbell over. Ronny Johnsen is back. Wes Brown will be soon. Next season, the defence will be more stable.

    Man U's attack's fine. The midfield can't be better. Once the defence is shored up, things will be fine.

    Of course, assuming Ruud passes his medical tests the second time round....

    Sometimes, you just need a key person behind the front men. This is Teddy's biggest strength. He was never an out and out striker. Even in Euro '96. He was the provider to Shearer.
