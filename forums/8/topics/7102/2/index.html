    UNITED ADMIT RUUD DEAL IS OFF
    by Adam Marshall

    MANCHESTER UNITED have sensationally pulled out of their record bid to sign PSV
    Eindhoven striker Ruud van Nistelrooy.
    As reported first on planetfootball.com, it appears as though a major problem has arisen
    over the exact nature of the player's medical examination.

    United's doctors wanted to perform arthroscopic surgery on the knee to assess the full
    damage, but van Nistelrooy, and his advisers, fear an operation would scupper his hopes
    of being involved in Euro 2000.

    A statement from the Premiership champions read: "Manchester United is very
    disappointed that the transfer of Ruud van Nistelrooy from PSV Eindhoven cannot
    proceed at the present time.

    "As reported on Tuesday 25 April 2000, further tests were necessary for Manchester
    United to proceed with the transfer. The discussions that have been held between all
    parties failed to reach an agreement as to the extent of these tests and, as a result of
    these differences in opinion, we cannot proceed at the present time.

    "Manchester United wish Ruud every success with his rehabilitation programme and will
    continue to monitor the situation very closely in order to achieve our desire to make him
    a Manchester United player."

    PSV chairman Harry van Raaij has already revealed that the deal is is unlikely to go
    through before the start of next season, and that it is indeed disagreements over the
    fitness tests that has caused the move to collapse.

    Speculative Italian newspaper reports have linked Milan with a counter-bid for the
    Dutchman, although Roma have already officially denied that they are looking to make an
    offer for the player.

    "We weren't interested in van Nistelrooy before United bought him, and we're not
    interested in him now they haven't," stated Roma president Franco Sensi. "He's not a
    player you can buy as a reserve and, anyway, if United don't want him, there must be
    something wrong with his knee."

    The next step is unknown - will United be able to assess their potential signing's
    performance in the European Championships, before deciding whether to revive the
    transfer, or could van Nistelrooy be destined for another season in Holland?

    What is for certain is that the whole affair has been a huge embarrassment to United.
    The declaration of intent will have unsettled the likes of Andy Cole, and, to a lesser
    extent, Ole Gunnar Solskjaer and Teddy Sheringham, and it has seemingly widened the
    rift between Martin Edwards and Sir Alex Ferguson.

    Van Nistelrooy out for eight months
    AMSTERDAM, April 28 (Reuters) - PSV Eindhoven striker Ruud van Nistelrooy suffered further ligament damage to his injured right knee during training on Friday and is expected to be out of action for at least eight months, the club said.

    Van Nistelrooy was taken to hospital where an examination on Friday afternoon revealed he had torn suffered further damage to his medial ligament and also torn the cruciate ligament.

    The injury means the PSV striker's slim hopes of making the Dutch squad for this summer's Euro 2000 have now vanished.

    The renewed knee trouble comes only a day after Van Nistelrooy's Â£18.5 million move to Manchester United was called off, at least temporarily.

    Van Nistelrooy has not played for PSV for more than a month because of the medial ligament injury and was undergoing light training on Friday morning after returning from Manchester.

    Television footage showed him jumping to head a ball and collapsing in a heap and yelling after an awkward fall.

    'He was in pain initially, but walked back to the changing rooms. He has decided to go to hospital to make sure,' PSV spokesman Pedro Salazar told Reuters.

    The proposed transfer to Manchester United fell through this week after the English champions insisted the 23-year-old striker undergo further tests, including possible keyhole surgery, after a two-day medical failed to satisfy them.

    The PSV striker had said he did not want to jeopardise his rehabilitation and his chance of making the Dutch Euro 2000 squad.

    The new injury raises further questions about the proposed move to Manchester United but the English champions had not ruled it out for good after hearing the initial news of the injury.

    'Our thoughts are with Ruud at this time,' said United financial director David Gill. 'This isn't good news for the player or for either club.
