    Thought I'd share what I knew about BMTC School II Nee Soon Camp -- which shouldn't be a problem now to the new recruits since Tekong's gonna be their home from now on

    My PC told me this story about Leopard Company, particularly Platoon 3 which was facing the SOC field. During lunchtime on his first day as a recruit he forgot to fall in with his mug and so was reprimanded by the sergeant who subsequently told him to get the mug from his bunk. He sprinted up the stairs to the third storey and as he was walking towards his bunk on the far end, suddenly the door of one of the bunks along the same corridor swung open violently. When he peeked into the window he saw nothing. It couldn't be the wind since the back door was closed shut and there wasn't even a gale at that time. His sergeant was already ejecting his lung power from downstairs so he just scampered back to his bunk without investigating further. But as he left and locked his bunk, he saw that the front doors to the other bunk that was opened less than a minute ago was tightly shut with a lock placed on it. And as he sprinted down the corridor, his eyes couldn't resist peering into the bunk again, and he saw it -- a figure of a girl in red sitting on one of the bedposts.

    The White House is another haunted spot. There's this story about these two guys doing their patrolling duty around the area in the wee hours of the morning. After reaching the summit of the hill with torchlights turned on they found the checkpoint. One of them recorded the time of their arrival while the other went over to the vending machine to get both of them a drink. To do so, he had to pass by a Female toilet. As he was about to leave the machine, he saw something coming out of the toilet. It must have been something horrible for he died of shock on the very spot. His other friend went berserk.

    Tekong surely has a kaleidoscope of ghost stories and I guess most of them have already been documented. But there's one story which many may not have heard of -- there's supposed to be this place in Tekong which has a black stone column -- can't exactly remember what it's like and exactly where but I'm sure it's near some lake which in turn is near to the rubber plantation. Groups of officers supposedly lost their way while "topoing" the ground around the stone, even with updated maps and compasses. They'd end up moving in circles around it. There's a similar story involving the inhospitable jungle around Lakaun SAF Camp in Brunei where supposedly a group of soldiers went missing for weeks when they thought they were lost for only a couple of hours.

    Anyway it seems like with BMTC and Sispec crammed in Tekong, that island would be too populated to incur any more ghost stories
