    Hi,

    H2 Chemistry is the most important. For medicine, either Bio or Phy works fine in terms of getting into uni (for NUS). But in my personal opinion, and based on what we are being taught, Bio will definitely be more useful. Overseas Unis may require Bio or Phy specifically (mostly Bio).

    Bio at H2 level is definitely content-heavy. You will do well if you have a passion for it. If not, it will be alot of memory work. Physics requires alot of understanding, application and practise, just like maths.

    With regards to readjusting to a new class, definitely it will be something to face. But consider that the subject you choose will live with you for 2 years, while this readjustment is just a one-off.

    Talk to your seniors, especially if anyone is taking both, though highly unlikely.

    Reconsider H3 Pharm Chem. The whole syllabus is extremely tough and lectures are nonsensical, probably worse than the physics you are getting now. But I guess it may be better in your year, since my year was the first and no one was really clear what was going in. If you can, though, NUS Bio is a more viable and enjoyable option, plus you can get it over and done with in 4-5 months, rather than 1.5 years for the MOE syllabus, and that the exam does not come with the A levels, leaving you free to concentrate on your H2 subjects.
