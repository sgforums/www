Singaporeans - All talk no actions?

  • Bravehart
    21 Nov 99, 09:58
    Hello,

    I'm just posting this in part due to what I read on Firewire's emotional "What tha Fuck!!!" post. In it he brought up a very interesting aspect of S'poreans: all talk and no action (well...not pharased this way..but the meaning is close enough).

    I would think this is more true of the so called more "educated" ppl that make up our society. What I am about to say is based largly on my experiences during my NS stint.

    But, allow me to give some background information before I proceed further. I was an A level student. Upon completion of my A Levels, I began my NS life at BMTC2. Unlike many of my contemporaries in NS, I did not make the cut for OCS or SISPEC, and was posted to the hellhole know as 30SCE as a Pioneer.

    As you all probably know, operational ready units like 30SCE have mono-intakes that are made up of largly people who stop their education at the O Levels (some even drop out before). Natuarally there existed some gap between the more "educated" ones who were posted along with me, and the mono-intakes. But being in the same platoon meant that we had go though any shit with them, whether we liked it or not. As such, I was able to work with them not as a superior (as a result of my supposedly higher education qualification) but rather as an equal.

    One thing I noticed is that for the mono-intakes, action is done in a blink of an eye and on the conclusion of a statement. There is no room for talk. Either you join them in doing what they are going to do; or you just stay out of it - simple as that.

    This is quite in contrast to "educated" people like us who tend to talk over it, evaluate, discuss, blah blah, jabber jabber, often leading nowhere until someone finally gets pissed off and DOES somehing.

    I supposed being "educated" means that we are supposed to be able to analyze issues and tasks objectively and such, but I noticed that often the discussion is more about impressing and protecting one's own interest. Just like the clowns who participate the UN, WTO or other global/regional forums, its all about self interest. Ok, perhaps this is a bad example because of the social and political implications involved as a result of these forums, but the point is that discussion/tok among individuals with self interest never gets anywhere.

    You would probably notice that self interest is given greater emphasis on people with higher education where the stakes are supposedly greater. No doubt the stake is greater (in terms of absolute value) but the comparative value of the what's at stake is pretty much the same. Put simply, the pain experienced by a rich man losing $100000 dollars would probably be the same as that of a poor man losing $100, even though the values vary by a geat amount. Hence I think it is unfair to say that lesser educated people have any less to lose.

    Which brings me back to my earlier point. Most of the mono-intakes I worked with were very helpful individuals. Helpful in the sense that they would be willing to step out of their job scope to lend a helping hand in order to finish the collective task earlier. Even those who were appointed I/Cs (on a rotational basis) chipped in their effort on the ground level.

    This was in stark contrast to some OCS cadet trainees (all who were either A level or Diploma holders) with whom I had the "honour" of working under during the construction of the Grandstands for NDP 99. The Cadet I/Cs were essentially supervisors, doing the usual "all tok, no action". The other cadets meticulously did what they were instructed to do, but few willing to overstep the scope of their task. Perhaps this was expected of them as part of their training as future officers *shurg*.

    Meanwhile AETC, the other unit who was building the grandstands concurrently on the other half of the field finished the same job in a timing quicker than us by a day. It might be interesting to note that AETC was comprised mainly of mono-intakes. No doubt experince did play a part, perhaps the main difference was that the ICs there were willing to to help out rather than just order from the sidelines.

    This is true with the mentality of the "less educated" people -- action speaks louder than words. Respect and support is given to those who do the most action, and not the chap who speaks most charismatically, which sadly, is what most of us excel exceeding well to various degrees.

    On an ending note, please note that I am not agianst the quality of the SAF NSF officers (but if you want to I can easily open another topic on this) or any other group of individuals, I'm just relating them as living examples of what I have observed.

    The bottom line (in case you fell asleep reading halfway through) is that "all talk no action" seems more predominant in the higher educated people than lower educated people. I understand my scope of observation is limited to the army. But by the time we enter this phase in our lives, most of our habits, mannerisms, outlook, preferences and such are already fixed to such a degree that we are unlikely to change radicaly for the remainder of our lives. Thus I suppose it would be quite an accurate reflection of S'poreans even after they have left the service to pursue their civilian lives.

    Just thinking out loud only

    Oh! I also sincerely apologise for the length of the post (in addition to any breakdown and inconsistiencies in my arguments...I haven't written that much for a long long time...).

Thus the difference from a school dropout O-Level student, A-Level Student, Degree/Diploma holder is the discipline and analytical skills. The higher your education, you are more aware of aspects in your field.

    When we first start out learning chemistry in secondary school for example, we are taught only the basic properties which mainly involves memorising. As we proceed on to higher levels of education, say JC, we are taught the reasons behind their properties. Who knows what they teach in the polys or unis. But the bottom line is education is the systematic building up of knowledge. it is a learning process that continually expands our horizon. It opens up avenues of thoughts that are otherwise unavailble to "lesser" educated persons.

    What we are saying that this additional analytical ability is does give you the ability to find and exploit loopholes to one's own advantage. Knowledge is power in today's context, an asset most are proud to hold. Our status in the social ladder is often based on our education system.
