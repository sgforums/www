    Chua successful and highly educated

    NOT much is known about the bachelor-businessman who was the last to see Ms Li Hong Yan alive in March.

    Attempts to contact Mr Adrian Chua Boon Chye, the 39-year-old former owner of the plush Sentosa Cove bungalow where the Chinese national was found dead, drew a blank.

    Those who knew him described him as successful, happy-go-lucky and friendly.

    Mr Heng Lee Seng, for example, who provides corporate services to Mr Chua's real estate investment management firm Roundhill Capital, said the businessman always shows up at Mr Heng's HLS Corporate Services in Tanjong Pagar cheerful.

    Mr Chua founded Roundhill Capital in 2001 and has been its chief executive since. The company has more than US$5 billion (S$6.5 billion) in investments in Asia.

    He was also a director of food and beverage company Foodbex Global between 2001 and 2006.

    From 1995 to 2001, he was a vice-president in the real estate arm of the Government of Singapore Investment Corporation.

    As a student, he attended Raffles Institution and the former Raffles Junior College, before he enrolled to study real estate at the University of California, Berkeley, where he co-founded and became president of the Credit Union for Berkeley Students in 1995.

    He also has a master's in finance from the London Business School.

    Mr Chua told the court yesterday that he now lives at Jalan Lim Tai See in Bukit Timah.

    When The Straits Times checked out the four-storey, semi-detached house with a rooftop pool yesterday, its curtains were drawn and all seemed quiet.

    The house belongs to his parents, Mr Andy Chua and Madam Sally Lim, who own a similar house next door.

    Mr Chua's maid Aye Aye Tun, 26, was seen getting out of a black BMW and entering the house yesterday.

    The Myanmar national also took the stand in court yesterday. She was the one who discovered Ms Li's body in the pool at the Sentosa Cove bungalow, which has since been sold.

    Neighbours at Jalan Lim Tai See said they have hardly seen Mr Chua around. One of them said the businessman was often overseas and was last spotted at home last year.
