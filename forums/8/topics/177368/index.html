    Zinc reacts with hydochloric acid according to the equation.

    Zn + 2HCl --> ZnCl2 + H2

    If 0.05 mole of zinc react with 0.075 mole of hydrochloric acid,

    (a) identify the limiting reactant

    (b) calculate the amount (in mole) of the excess reactant which remain unreacted.

    My ans for (a) is HCl. But my teacher says is Zn. Pls enlighten me.

  • chanff8
    7 Feb 06, 17:32
    (a) is HCl wad... Cos 2 parts of HCl reacts with one part of Zn. 0.05 moles of Zn can react with 0.1 mole of HCl, but HCl not enough, only 0.075 moles.

    (b)
    Since HCl is the limiting reactant, the number of moles of Zn it can react with is:
    0.075/2=0.0375 moles

    Remaining number of moles of Zn=0.05-0.0375=0.0125 moles
