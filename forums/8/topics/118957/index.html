Most Taiwan LCD makers to raise 17-inch monitor panel prices

    Digitimes -- The majority of Taiwan-based TFT-LCD makers, including AU Optronics (AUO), Chi Mei Optoelectronics (CMO), Chunghwa Picture Tubes (CPT) and HannStar Display, may raise prices for 17-inch LCD-monitor panels, according to sources.

    Prices may increase as soon as the market has seen increased demand for 17-inch LCD-monitor panels, and inventory is running short, indicated Hsiung Hui, executive vice president of AUO. However, AUO would only increase prices in the range of US$5, and CMO and CPT would not raise their prices dramatically, the sources indicated.

    AUOÂ’s inventory levels decreased to 44 days in the fourth quarter of 2004, down from 56 in the third quarter, according to the company. CMO saw its inventory levels fall from 53 days to 51 days, while HannStar went from 51 days to 40 days.

    HannStar plans to raise the prices of its lower-end 17-inch LCD-monitor panels by US$5-10, and the company is negotiating the price increase with its clients, according to company president David Joe.

    However, panel makers have only secured orders through April, and there is still much uncertainty in the market as to whether long-term demand is strong enough to support price increases, said CC Liu, sales and marketing vice president of CPT.

    Since last September, prices for 17-inch LCD-monitor panels have slumped by around 25%, falling from US$200-210 to US$152, according to WitsView Technology.
