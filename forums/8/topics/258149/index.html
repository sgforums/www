    There are certain things you don't buy from China, but usually these lean more towards industrial equipment such as machine centers, coordinate measuring machines etc for engineering work. China-made models may have reversed-engineered their european and japanese counterparts to some extent, but they have not yet reproduced the quality and reliability of their more illustrious competitors.

    When it comes to handphones, headsets, harddrives etc, most of them are made in China. I still prefer Taiwan-made for motherboards but the china market is increasingly competitive. I won't be surprised that Taiwan, Malaysia and other mainstays succumb to the dragon in another 10 years time.

    For now, it's really what product you're buying and whether paying that extra dollar for quality is really worth it, especially with the rapid advancement of new technology in the electronic market.
