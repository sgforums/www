    That's why some couples choose to stay together, long after romance is dead, in a state of matrimony that can best be described as the "nondivorce."

    Though the situation varies, a couple in a nondivorce usually remain living together under one roof, more like passionless roommates than spouses. There are no hard statistics, but some divorce experts say they're seeing more of this unromantic phenomenon, driven by three big financial factors:
    the high cost of legal fees in divorces,
    the expense of setting up two households and
    the difficulty in obtaining adequate health insurance, especially if one spouse has a pre-existing condition.

    That's a shift from decades past, when couples stayed together because divorce carried a social stigma or was forbidden by religious teachings. But along with finances, there's another driving factor in today's not-quite-a-divorce arrangement: Many couples, turned off by their friends' bitter, rancorous breakups, would rather stay together than endure the messiness or inflict it upon their children. by Colleen DeBaise.
