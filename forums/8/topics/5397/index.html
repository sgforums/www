    nah....dun think he/she is from carnegie mellon....maybe from one of the uni of london colleges?

    anyways, i dunno of any survey company who does surveys from a forum! hope ur sample size is more than 4 man!

    my comments: i see ur assuming s'poreans really do prefer american univs. i think this trend has become more marked only in recent years. there are still many (very good) jc students here with a hang-up over oxford and cambridge. they only think of those and the ivy league univs for their undergraduate education. but more and more students are preferring the states. i think it's partly due to better marketing, lower entry requirements and cheaper fees. of course the liberal arts thingey is a big draw. plus u can do a double degree in the same amt of time but not in the UK. u can even finish ur undergraduate plus master's in the same amt of time it takes to do just the former in the UK. but that's at the undergraduate level.

    as for the graduate level (e.g. in the liberal arts, of which i am most familiar), u gotta blame both the academics and the graduate students. if u speak to ppl from australia to new zealand, from s'pore to timbucktoo, the academics and graduate students will encourage you to go to the states for ur graduate studies (and not just the ivy league ones. many state univs are really good too) cos the faculty there is top notch. think they bought over some of the very best british academics too! research facilities there too are very good. students want to work under first class reseachers and professors.

    the graduate programmes in the states, tho very open and liberal, also require you to pass some core requirements. ppl prob see this as more thorough training. this is not always the case in britain (london colleges inclusive).

    prob the most impt factor for a graduate student is financial aid. US univs give good support in terms of fellowships, teaching/research assistantships and so on. and you don't have to be a first class student to get one. what are impt are your GREs, writing samples and recommendations. the focus is on potential. not always so for UK univs. they don't seem to want international students and this you can tell from their (un)willingness to give out financial aid. quite honestly, the financial support is appalling. whatever happened to Blair saying that this issue would be redressed?

    in terms of marketing to international students, just take a look at the web-sites of british vs american univs and you'll know the difference. for many british univs, u have to hunt high and low and all around for an international student's page that will give u info on course fees and financial support. the american univs put that info up front.

    what makes a univ top notch is not just the faculty and their publications or research facilities but the quality of the graduate students. and US univs have been more open (and serious) abt taking in international students than UK univs, in my opinion.

    in terms of reputation amongst academics, i think collectively, university of london is first class. and so are the IVY league univs. but ppl here still tend to think oxford and cambridge in the same breath as the latter. and seldom think too much of london colleges, until ppl who are in the know or who have studied there tell them they're really quite good.

    the thing tho is this. at the graduate level at least, what is impt is the quality of the programme you are considering. NOT the overall reputation of the univ. some ivy league univs are comparatively weak in some of their programmes, and would not be first choice programmes for graduate study. in such cases, knowing that u received your training in a reputable department (even in a not-so-famous univ) and under top-quality faculty will get you further in the eyes of fellow academics and ppl in the industry. so the comparison at graduate level has to be made between programmes and not between ivy league and london colleges as a whole.

    hope this helps.
