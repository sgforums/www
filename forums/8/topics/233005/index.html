    Admissions Criteria

    Admission is based on academic merit as well as open competition among all eligible applicants. The minimum requirements for Singapore 'A'-Level students are

    (a) Pass in at least 2 'A' Level subjects and General Paper in one sitting,

    (b) Mathematics requirement: pass in 'AO' Level (lowest level within the 'A' Level) Mathematics (need not be from the same sitting as for (a) above) or 'O' Level Additional Mathematics, and

    (c) "Mother Tongue" (non-English language) requirement.
    You can see that the emphasis is on Maths rather than econs or any arts subjects.

In my case I have already made up my mind to study law in spore...after which I hope to go on and read sociology and ethnic studies in some liberal arts college in the US.     But anyway, studying the humanities unlocks a whole plethora of opportunities. Job prospects are aplenty. If you are game enough you can venture into politics, law, business, finance, advertising, social science research, human resource (hello more than just teaching). Well its true to an extent you wld have just shut urself out from jobs in engineering or biomed industry but ask yourself, how many ppl you know actually prowl these areas after their a levels? Many of my seniors who took science go on to study courses which you would have an edge in if you study humans. At the end of it all, universities and companies juxtapose a set of students with similar academic results but the humanities student will be equipped with flexibility in thought to deal with complex ideas, and have an emotional make up capable of coping with elements of uncertainty. It may be frustrating to realise that in ur subjects, answers are not necessarily right or wrong but that they require careful and cogent debate. An ARTs student will need high level of dedication and motivation to cope with such rigour, but ultimately you will emerge more mature and hardened in thought.
