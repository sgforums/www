    Have someone who is able to setup a bbq fire and are willing to get their hands dirty to prep (that's where the water for washing of hands is useful)

    There are so many times where I know of people who says that "oh i know how to setup a fire" - most of the time, they are rubbish at it - so much smoke take so long. No fire.

    Here's a tip:

    Take 3 pieces of charcoal. arrange it to form a triangle, then do like so "/\ " then take another 3 pieces of charcoal and stack it on top of the /\ but digonally. When you feel it's high enough place a fire starter in the middle of the triangle

    Repeat this for the rest of the pit.. and u will get your fire in 5~15 minutes

    Also ready some paper plates to be used as a fan for the fire.

    If it's at a beach, bring along a football/volleyball some cards.

    I'm quite used to organising such events - night cycling .. chalets.. bbqs.. but i do get pre event jitters (esp for night cycling as i have to consider many aspects such as safety, body conditioning, pace .. in a convoy of about 10-15 bikes)

     Don't worry everything will turn out fine. :)

    The key to making them feel comfortable is flexibility.. they are here to enjoy and chill.. some event organisers are just too anal. They just stick to their time plan and ruin the whole atmosphere really.

    For example your time plan states 8pm - ice breaking games. If at 8pm your friends are happily eating/ doing something fun just scrap off/postpone the ice breaking and let them enjoy..

    The trick to a good charcoal fire is to fan it in a figure of 8 type of direction and not just flap flap flap flapping works though but you will have flappy and ITCHY armpits cum quicker aching arms 

    I always say, or rather what the all good UG guys would say, a good foundation is good. Once the starter is lighted up and in that foundation, wait for a while for it to heat up the charcoal. 
    Then fan. Fan flames will come out, if you fan it right.

    Not having a fire doesn't mean that it isn't hot enough, that's a misconception... once the charcoal is hot.. aka glowing or white, it can last for a good minimum 1 hour or more before topping up is needed
