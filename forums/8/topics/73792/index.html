The pedestrian's guide to the green man

    Tired of waiting in your car at a red light when no pedestrians are crossing?

    With pedestrian-activated push buttons installed at most traffic junctions that is practically a thing of the past.

    Green time for vehicles is adjusted by the GLIDE system according to traffic flow at different junctions. Although vehicles can be automatically detected by detector loops, pedestrians can only be detected if the push button is pressed. These large push buttons are found on traffic signal poles at the corners of a traffic junction.

    Push buttons are useful as the "green man" is only activated when a pedestrian is waiting to cross the road. When there are no pedestrians waiting to cross, green time can be redistributed according to traffic demand. Efficiency improves and other traffic will not be delayed.

    This means that pedestrians must press the push button if they want to cross the road. Once they do, the "green man" will be activated for them to cross the road safely.

    To remind pedestrians to press the push buttons, stickers and "Walk with care" signs have been installed above each push button. Happy pushing!

    Did you know?

    There are about 1,250 signalised junctions and 580 signalised crossings in Singapore.
    An audible alarm is installed at certain pedestrian crossings to aid the visually handicapped. This helps them to cross the road safely without the help of a sighted person.

    Taken from Land Transport Authority website, 22 March 2004.
