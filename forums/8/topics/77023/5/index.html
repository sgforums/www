    According to source, Nicoll Highway MRT station has 2 by 2 stacked tunnels, like those in City Hall and Raffles Place MRT interchanges. This means that Nicoll Highway station has 4 platforms on 2 levels and may be an interchange with the future Bukit Timah MRT line or Eastern Regional MRT line.

    The main reason why Nicoll Highway station tunnels are deep is that the tunnels between Nicoll Highway station and the next station Boulevard station (next to the National Stadium) has to pass under a section of Republic Ave which is a vehicular underpass, and the Kallang Basin.

    This part of the tunnels is build using the cut-and-cover method. Also, as many has known, this part of the tunnels is on formerly reclaimed land.

    In fact, Nicoll Highway station is the most tough project on the entire Circle line, considering the depth of tunnels, the tunneling method used, the soil stability and other concerns.

    The 5.4 km Marina Line, which will form the first stage of the Circle Line, will be a fully automated, wholly underground system. It will be the first medium capacity system in the Rapid Transit System (RTS) network in Singapore. Construction for the Marina Line will begin at the end of this year, and is target to complete in 2006.

    Nishimatsu Construction Co. Ltd., a company incorporated in Japan, has long been recognised as one of Japan's leading general contractors. Lum Chang Building Contractors Pte Ltd, a locally incorporated company, is registered under G8 for general building and civil engineering with the Building and Construction Authority.
