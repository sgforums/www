    26 Jan 08, 00:25
    Totally and utterly disagree.

    Firstly, no one will tell you that Poly is a breeze compared to Sec school. Whoever who tells you that is an idiot. A Polytechnic is an institution of HIGHER learning. It means you need to take a pro-active approach to your work. No one is gonna chase you to make your submissions. No one is gonna make you stay back and finish your work "After School." You've gotta motivate yourself to do that.

    Secondly, You meet liars, backstabbers and unhelpful people everywhere. Love them or hate them, up to you. All in all, you learn a real world lesson. Know how are your enemies and who are your friends. Invaluable.

    Thirdly, Poly life can be fun. I know I said you have to proactive and hardworking in Poly. But there is always room for play. Find a CCA that you like to join, and you'll meet even more friends with a common interest. Polytechnic facilities are also top of the line. Swimming pools, tracks, soccer fields, badminton courts, tennis courts, Gaming Centers, lounges, and the list goes on. You learn how to balance work and play. Another important life skill if you ask me.

    My poly experience was pretty good. I spent 60% of my time studying, and 40% playing in year 1. 40% studying and 60% playing in Year 2, and 30% studying and 70% playing or hanging loose. Curriculum time is not specified so I could play as much as I want as long as I can maintain my A's & B's. Work Smart, Play Hard. You won't get this kinda "freedom" to manage your studies in JC. But it sure prepares you for uni.
