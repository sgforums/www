    after paying all my bills including giving my ma, I throw the almost rest of my salary into stocks.

    every month buy 1-2 lots, I find that buying securities is better than putting money into the bank:

    1) Value appreciate
    2) Get dividends
    3) Harder to get the money back to spend immediately(unlike an ATM you need to plan in adv)

    Of course when I first started out it was not so easy as I didn't earn much, but I dutifully saved 25% of my pay every month, then once I had enuf I bought shares.

    If I made money from selling my securities I would plough all the money back into buying more shares. very rare I would go on shopping sprees on spend on myself.

    Overtime(few years) from this activity of selling shares and ploughing back and buying more shares with your salary it actually snowballs quite substantially.

    And you know what? Your lifestyle slowly melds in to this habit you will find that you can still survive and not really worse off if you didn't have X amt of spending money waiting in the bank.

    17 Dec 04, 01:42
    Originally posted by titoo:
    Hey, thanks for teaching me how to start building on shares...I am also wanting to start investing but with minimum amount, how do you think I could start?
    mmm...I suggest you don't have a ballpark figure in your mind. You shouldn't take a chunk of your money and dump into stocks, thats dangerous. Instead you should prorgessively buy. So that periodically you can check if you made a good choice on your investment or not.

    Important is when mistakes happen don't lose heart but treat it as tution fees, thats also another reason why you shouldn't dump a whole lot of your money into stocks at 1 go.

    Depending on your earning capability buy whatever you can afford to lose. Buy 1 lot every month or save for few months then buy. Never overleverage, contra, short, margin or borrow to play stocks.

    what to buy
    Your first Goal is to grow and widen your base, go for stocks which consistently have a record of paying good dividends, keep buying them proressively for a few years. A good way to ensure your dividends are ploughed back and not squandered upon receiving the cheque is to use your CPF to buy so that everything goes back to CPF and earns interest.

    How long to keep and when to sell
    You should allow time for your investment to work, a rule of thumb that I use is 3years or 50% profit, whichever comes first I will sell.

    maxim: You are an investor here to make money and not want to become the guy who sold it at the highest price.
