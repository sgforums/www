    Oh no.. I also faced exactly the same problem. And mine is a D-Link router but an Acer laptop. So I guess its a problem with the Acer laptops then. I do hibernations too most of the time. But mine is not always.. It only happens sometimes. Eventually I changed to lan cable becoz wireless was giving me a big headache during gaming.

    Not sure whether u have tried this though, works sometimes:

    Once u On the laptop from hibernation if u can't get the net,
    Then open Start>Run> type "cmd"
    Once you get the command prompt type "ipconfig /release", press enter.
    Then type "ipconfig /renew", press enter.

    It will take like probably 30 seconds or something. After it is renewed again you need to wait a minute b4 it can detect the wireless net.

    If that doesn't work you can try this:

    Instead of rebooting your computer just remove the power cable of Belkin (or switch it off in other words) then power it up after like 5 seconds. Then see if you laptop detects..

    Must be in this order
    Switch off Belkin
    Switch off Wireless button on ur Acer
    Wait 5 seconds
    Switch on your Acer Wireless
    Switch on Belkin..

    Yeah.. Good luck. Hope that helps...
