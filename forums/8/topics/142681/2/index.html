    I dunnoe what fellow forumites think, but my thoughts when someone said "I am regretful" actually meant "I am sorry to myself"; in other words, it is more of the guilty comforting himself. The best is the guilty feels guilty. The apologethic feeling doesn't extend to a second person.

    But when someone says "I am sorry to what i have done", it goes further than being regretful. Now the state of being regretful involves a second person. There is a more positive air of being truely sorry.

    Think of it this way:
    I am regretful = Shouting "I am going out now"
    I am sorry - Shouting to Mum, "I am sitting on a chair now"

    Of course different people have different definition of regret and sorry. You can comment on what you think.

    As for me, I still want a plain out-right apology that kopitiam uncles and aunties can easily understand and not a twist of words or indirect apology. It can be something like this: "Dear fellow Singaporeans, I am sorry for my peanuts remark on 12th July 2005. I hereby convey my deepest apology to all of you and retract my peanuts remark. I will not make such insensitve remarks again."
