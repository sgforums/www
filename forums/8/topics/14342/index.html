    Never did I expect my usual night out at Zouk to turn out so exciting. Greeted by more than 19 talented regional and local artistes at the entrance, I discovered that they were all there for a good cause amid the gloom and doom of war and the economic downturn.

    In collaboration with artiste and event management company, Music & Movement, these artistes had voices that rang out loud and clear in their promotion of goodwill and idealism. They were all gathered at Zouk on 1st December 2001(World AIDs Day) to launch the CD 'Love Is The Answer', an album aimed at raising funds to help kids battle AIDs/HIV. The chosen beneficiary for this charitable project was the "KK Outreach to Kids Fund-HIV Prevention & Treatment Programme" which offers financial assistance to families who cannot pay for their children's medical treatment of chronic conditions such as Thalassaemia, cancer and HIV infection.

    Local artistes such as Tanya Chua, Steven Lim and Anna Belle Francis made appearances at Zouk-themed as Give love a chance- to perform and showcase their talents, all for the sake of raising funds. Many had their fortunes told by these local celebrities, including myself. Popcorn and candy- floss were also on sale at the main dance floor of Zouk. All proceeds from the sale went to the KK Outreach to Kids Fund. There was a lucky draw at the end of it and I was one of the two winners to walk away with a Zouk Complimentary Membership card. BUT, I've still yet to get it.

    Love Is The Answer is a fantastic CD containing 15 easy-listening songs of love and hope such as 'True Colours' by homegrown artiste Tanya Chua, 'Friend Of Mine' by Lea Salonga and 'Get Here' by Sandy Lam. One of the tracks, both sung and composed by singer-composer, Dick Lee, titled 'It All Begins With Love' asks everyone to strip away the prejudices and remember that at the core of everything is love. My personal favourite track is titled 'Gaining Through Losing'. It is an English version of the popular Chinese hit by F4, 'Liu Xing Yu'. This all-star track was intelligently composed by Ken Hirai and later translated into English by both Hirai himself and the 'Happy Bunch' from Music and Movement. The true inspiration comes from the last line, "Let me tell you, LIFE is GOOD my friends". Its positive tone is most encouraging--these are not the best times and if we just accept our lot in life, cherish it and share the happiness with others around you, this world will be a better place. Indeed, music works in strange ways even without wordsÂ… it can be inspiring. Not surprisingly, all the other songs from the CD are compassionate expressions of love. Says Mr. Lim Sek, Managing Director of Music & Movement, "... at any time of the year, these kids need the money for their treatmentÂ… the album is relevant in all aspects and at all times. The money will go to the kids and also raises awareness of AIDS." So, do yourself and the unfortunate kids a favour: support the cause for AIDS and help to raise funds for the unfortunate kids by getting the CD today!

    Love Is The Answer is available in stores from $18.

    Stacy Swee
    Arts 2 Student
