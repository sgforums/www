    Hi...

    I'm doing a PhD in engineering (overseas). So whats stated below might be useful if you are considering an engineering PhD...

    I think its feasible to have a PhDs in Singapore since its moving into a research based economy.

    As for the salary range, it depends on what kind of area you are working in...but generally it may be ard the range of say 3500-5000 starting? I'm not sure about MSc though...could be a couple of hundred dollars more than a first class honours degree....

    In general, DO NOT go into a PhD programme just for the sake of money. You'll find that the time spent in doing a PhD might not be reflected in your pay. I have seen some of my colleagues suffering here. Most important is that you love doing research and love creating knowledge. If you dislike doing research, most probably you might have a real hard time in your 3-5yrs PhD programme.

    To qualify for a PhD in Sg, its preferable to have a 2nd upper degree (I'm not sure if they do take in 2nd lower) and a supervisor to take you in. Of course work experience do count though I'm not sure if some supervisors prefer fresh grads or industry-experienced postgrads.

    You could write your own research proposal or look at each prospective supervisor's website. Emailing them to discuss about your aims and objectives might also be a good idea.

    A PhD programme work both ways: you need a good supervisor who is willing to support your work while your supervisor needs good student.

    If you are unsure, you might consider doing a full-time MSc first which I personally think its worth it. MSc program will give you a good foundation which you may use in your PhD work. Generally, MSc graduates have an easier life in their PhD programme.

    I hope that helps a wee bit...
