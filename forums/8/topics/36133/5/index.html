    On attachment at the hospital today, I got to watch a labour... A new life, a new beginning... It really made me realise how precious a life is... and wonders how a mother could go through so much pain, and bring a child into this world of ours...

    Halfway through my breakfast, my classmate got a call from the labour ward, stating that a woman is about to give birth and asked if we would like to observe... We went down immediately, and was just in time for the baby...

    We saw the baby's head at the vagina opening, and the baby was just about to come out... Suddenly, the obstetrician did an episotomy, cut the perineal skin of the mother, and then... niagra falls...
    The blood gushed out red, fast and strong, and my colleague could not help but gasp... I was totally stunned, I mean, the vagina opening was more than big enough for the baby to go through, sure the cut would save time in delivering, but wouldn't it be so much more painful, with all the stitches and everything?

    The doctor delivered the baby, he was underweight... around 2kg... so they sent him to the special care unit...

    Then instead of waiting for the plancenta to be delivered, the doctor forced (pulled) at the cord... then the cord snapped, and the placenta was left inside the woman, and the doctor... she calmly slid her whole palm inside the woman's vagina and retrived the whole, red, and very bloody plancenta...

    The mother was tired, but looks really satisfied, and seemed to be a rather fair amount of pain... After all that cutting and stitching, who could blame her...

    I really respect the sacrifices a mother have to make, just for delivering a baby into this world... A new beginning for a new life, I hope that the boy would grow up to be a fillial son, after all that the woman have gone through for him, he better!

    I know for today, I am going to be a better daughter... Afterall, less than 2 decades ago, my mother went through roughly the same thing for me...

    ~Introspective~
