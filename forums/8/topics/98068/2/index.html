    Martin, I don't work there. However, when you go and visit your mom, talk to her and tell her everything you want her to know. If you and your family need some privacy, ask the nurses if you can draw the curtains. We may have made a lot of medical advances but we still cannot know for sure whether comatose patients can or can't hear from their loved ones. I'm biased towards the opinion that they can hear their loved ones but it's just that they are not able to respond. I say that because from my observations, a lot of these patients do exhibit elevated blood pressure / heart beat or some other symptoms that show that their loved ones' voices and presence is a stimuli.

    Sometimes, stroke patients experience a 'locked in' syndrome where they can hear and feel everything happening around them but they are unable to open their eyes, unable to talk nor gesture. You see these patients putting up a tremendous struggle as they try to 'wake up'. Sometimes, you see tears flowing out of their eyes. I always talk to these patients whenever I'm doing something for them and tell them how filial their children are, how much love the family has for them and how they can go in peace and without regrets because they have lived a wonderful and fulfilling life. When medicine cannot prolong someone's life any further, it's best to let them go in a dignified, peaceful manner. And always always tell them how much you love them before you never have that chance again.
