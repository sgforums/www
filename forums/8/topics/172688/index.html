    Originally posted by alwaysdisturbed:
    wats the difference between alternative rock, emo, indie rock, progressive rock, soft rock?
    Alternatives varies, They are usually Rock, mixed with other genres like blues or folks and even Rapping. Linkin park, Staind or Gomez are usually classified as Alternative. Smashing Pumpkins are during one time, classified as Alternative too. Emo rock are usually playing with Alternative bands on gigs for they are known to blend.

    Emo is classified as a type of harder punk. The Used, Funeral for a Friend, Taking back sunday or Matchbook romance are usually classified under Emo rock.

    Indie rock stands for Independent Rock, which actually deprive from Independent Bands. Their music style are usually rejected from the mainstream in the past. Bands like Nirvana (Grunge) Franz Ferdinand or The Pixies. Indie bands in the past are pop/rock bands transformation.

    Progressive rock are usually called Progressive Metal, for they are known for their heavy but unique sound. A distinct way of telling Progressive Rock from other genres are the slow start of the music changing to real heavy. Bands like Sonata Arctica or Stratovarius. Progressive are usually not popular in the U.S.A or U.K. Most of Progressive Rock bands comes from Finland, Germany or countries as such.

    Soft Rock, as the name suggest are softer rock. Usually compared to Alternative Rock, they uses Rock 'N Roll style much more. Soft Rock usually have piano as a main instrument. Bands like Coldplay (usually debated as Alternative) or Keane are grouped as Soft Rock. The thing about Soft Rock is that, pop singers usually sing Soft Rock as part of their music too, so finding pure Soft Rock bands ain't easy.

    Some info are from bands websites.
