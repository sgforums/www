    There are only 6 other rules. As for what Oakley was talking about, I have absolutely no idea.

    The SAF has mainly 8 different pledges, declarations, oaths and creeds, namely, the SAF Declaration (the mission of the SAF, etc...), the SAF Pledge (the very first one you take in BMT), the Oath of Allegiance (which you take at the same time as the SAF Pledge), the 7 Core Values, the Specialist's Creed, the Officer's Creed, the Oath of Command and the one in question, the SAF Code of Conduct.

    The SAF Code of Conduct is the one with the 6 rules governing the daily conduct and behaviour of a serviceman. After such a long time, I can't remember the exact wording of the 6 rules, but I think there's something regarding care for weapons or something like that.

    What is popularly known as "Rule no.7" is the famous add-on to these 6 rules.

    I really can't think of any SAF manifesto with 12 rules...(12!!!!), perhaps Oakley would like to enlighten us?
