    Chinese Triads or Criminal Gangs

    Chinese triads are secret societies that can trace their history back to the 17th century. They were formed by monks and officials displaced by the Qing or Manchu dynasty. These societies played an important role supporting the Taiping Rebellion, which lasted from 1851-64, and resulted in more than 20 million deaths and the eventual distruction of the Qing dynasty.

    Today these secret societies are reputed to control Chinese organized crime from their base in Hong Kong.

    The groups get the name "triads" from the triangle that they use as their symbol. The triangle represents the union of heaven, earth, and man.
