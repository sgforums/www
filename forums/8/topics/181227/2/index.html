    Sometimes it works both ways.

    You may want to put a low salary so as to get a job but the potential problem with that is that your next employer will be adjusting your salary based on your last drawn salary.

    If it is low, your next employer will likely not give you an adjustment to say market rates next time unless you are a damn solid star performer.

    I believe HR managers have guidelines that they cannot adjust salaries at their own whims. So if you switch jobs and your salary is 1.2K, your next employer may give you a 10% increment from your last drawn. There will also be a tendency to push down wages since they may think you are a lousy worker that can be exploited (since you have such a low salary expectation).

    I would like to tell you everything is fine and you will get your deserved salary after working for 5 years or so, but trust me, things don't work this way now.

    Increments will be less than before and any additional money you make will be through your variable bonus.

    So your basic salary can actually remain stagnant for 2 to 3 years until u get promoted. If you can live with that, then by all means do so.

    But think carefully before u take that low paying job.
