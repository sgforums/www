    The formula for number average is Sum of number of moles multiply by Molecular weight divided by Sum of number of moles

    The formula for weight average is Sum of number of moles multiply by (Molecular weight squared ) divided by Sum of number of moles multiply by Molecular weight

    To the best of my knowledge, number average is NOT always larger then weight average, the opposite is true for most polymers unless the polymer is monodisperse meaning that all its chains are of the same length.

    The ration of weigth average over number average is always more then one and this ratio itself is a use as a form of parameter.
