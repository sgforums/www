Woman killed in robbery at betting outlet

  • SiMpLeLiFe
    12 Aug 02, 12:58
    A BETTING-OUTLET employee was slain brutally by robbers yesterday when they struck at a popular Singapore Pools outlet at Toa Payoh Central in a brazen morning hit.
    The assailants ambushed the 36-year-old woman, who worked at the outlet at Block 177, when she came in alone some time after 7 am.
    She was preparing to open the shop for business at 8 am.
    Once in the shop, the assailants attacked and slit the woman's throat, before ransacking the shop and carting away a safe containing an undisclosed amount of cash.
    The outlet, Chye Kwang Trading, is a popular betting outlet which attracts punters from as far away as Bukit Panjang as it has seen three Toto jackpot prizes since 1997.
    It is a common practice for 4D outlets to keep a substantial amount of cash on Sunday so that they can pay up to $5,000 for each winning ticket for Saturday's bets.
    Yesterday's murder was discovered only at about 9.50 am when the shopowner dropped in to check why no one was answering the telephone.
    When he opened the roller shutters which was down but not locked, he was shocked to find his employee's lifeless body behind the counter.
    The police have withheld the woman's name pending further investigation.
    At about 11 am yesterday, when news of the murder reached the victim's family, her husband and son rushed to the scene.
    When they saw a glimpse of the woman's body behind a white and blue police cordon tape, her son, a police national serviceman, became distraught and shouted at scores of passers-by who had gathered to watch the commotion.
    'Who killed my mother?' he cried repeatedly.
    He later hugged his father, who sobbed loudly: 'Daddy's a failure. I couldn't protect your mother.'
    Both father and son declined to talk to reporters and left the scene after undertakers came to take the body away at 2.30 pm.
    According to Madam Chan Ah Yoong, 56, who owns a shop nearby, the dead woman, whom she knew as 'Chris', had been working at the betting outlet for about three years.
    'When I saw the police cars, I thought someone had fainted.
    'I realised it was murder only when I saw another employee crying inside the police car,' she said.
    Cases of murder during robbery are rare here.
    The last such case happened in October last year when Miss Soh San, 28, a senior business analyst with M1, was found dead in a lift at Block 172, Bukit Batok West Avenue 8.
    She was believed to have been stabbed after struggling with a robber who is still at large.
