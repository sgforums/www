    The poly batch eh?Being the most recent intake,I guess i'll relate to you some experiences and wisdom of mine.Like many an enthusiastic enlistee,i vyed for selection into the unit.Perhaps i was momentarily inspired by the potrayal of cdos in what i read in bks and saw in shows.Here's my honest advice:Weigh your options wisely and do not be temporarily blinded by glory and the high allowances that cdos command,the magic wears off in one week.(1/2 of the batch wanted out after a few weeks).No it isn't really the physical torment that wears you dry,but the 90% mental strain the eventually takes many soldiers down here.I can't tell you how many are fighting to be downgraded in my coy.
    But i will tell you first hand that out of each intake of 200 only an estimated 50 will wear the beret.The rest will be chopped in the various phases of training.You efforts do not necessarily secure your goals.
    i can say with conviction that there is a lot of pride in being part of the battalion because we feed on it during the intense training to sustain our sanity.Teamwork is heavily played in a unit like ours and the satisfaction attained from training is none like any other,a class of it's own.
    Do consider,do some "give and take" anaylsis
    and ask yourself that,in a unit fraught with the most risks,whether a permanant organ or body defect is worth a tradeoff with a red beret.("It will not happen to me" is a common delusion,when in fact mishap strikes without selection)
    I must admit though there is a slight euphoria when my friends in other units revere at my exploits
    but at the tail end i'd rather not have such publicity and forego the "shit" i've waded in.
