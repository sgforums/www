Pressure selling of Time-sharing Schemes

  • SaYa
    15 Sep 02, 17:53
    I believe time-sharing does make a bit of sense. It is one way to pool resources to achieve higher mass, higher bargaining power, for the common good. Well-established examples include group insurance, unit trusts, etc.

    However, whereas the latter few (above) are well-regulated, time sharing still lacks a framework to ensure the quality of the resorts, the reliability of the agents. So, there are many many uncertainties and risks when participating in such schemes.

    In fact, there are even dishonest time-sharing agents out there. These are only interested in getting business from you. They just want cash only. They don't care about your interests. They don't even mind misleading/cheating you to make you go into time-sharing.

    So, if you find yourself being pressured by such people to participate in such schemes, be assertive - heck care their stress tactics. Make them spell out all the terms that they have promised you in a legal contract. If need be, verify the contract validity with CASE, before you sign it.

    Well, you are paying quite a big sum for the thing. So, don't be hasty. If they say that the "offer period" is going to be over soon, you still don't have to rush, just let it be. There'll be other "offers" coming up.

    For dealing with such sorts of people, you must always remember that - you are the customer. You are doing him a favour by doing business with them. It isn't "they are doing you a favour". Always remember that.
