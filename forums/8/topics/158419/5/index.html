    While TRUE that users are often the weakest link in a system's security, & since this is the norm, I say the best software in keeping spyware and adware at bay, is still also the user.

    By this, I meant that users, should read up more about the variable identities of spyware and adware (their many forms of seemingly legit names), and it's symptomatic interactions with your browser. If your Firewall prompts or warns you of something you can't understand, it is usually best to click on the Firewall's 'recommended' action, as in the case of Norton Internet Security 2005 (Spyware Edition). And it is prudent to read up on what the message means. Self-education, not blind faith.

    Knowing how spyware and adware behaves, does go a long ways in keeping your system, trouble free.

    Browser-jacking, or Browser-hijacking is no longer an issue with NIS2005 (Spyware Edition). The moment NIS2005 detects that your Homepage has been re-directed or changed in any way, it will notify you by re-diverting the attack to Symantec's website, informing you that your Homepage was changed, and that you can set your Homepage back again by going to Internet Options.

    I go anywhere I very well please on the Internet, and have never been utterly confounded by any attacks, because I know what all those popups and prompts are; - fakes, enticers, and multi-links that leads to spyware, adware, and trojans. Some links when clicked, changes your Internet Explorer's homepage settings, and if you're using a dial-up connection, your dial-up settings can also be changed to dial-up a porno server.

    READING, is important. Reckless downloading and surfing, only brings trouble.
