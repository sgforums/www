    SINGAPORE : Singapore's total fertility rate (TFR) last year was 1.37 - the same as that for 2002, which was a historic low for the country.

    The total fertility rate is the average number of children born to each woman aged 15 to 44.

    Advertisement

    Speaking at an Edusave scholarship and bursary presentation on Saturday, Home Affairs Minister Wong Kan Seng said Singapore had one of the lowest fertility rates in the world.

    And a TFR of 1.37 is a far cry from the 2.1 needed to replace the population.

    But it's no longer just single graduate women that the Goverment's worried about.

    Mr Wong said the percentage of unmarried men aged 35 to 39 was growing faster than unmarried women.

    "We know that families also feel quite stressed bringing up children, but at the same time, we have to recognise that it is important that our population continues to grow, for the future of our country." - CNA
