    Trial of Took Leng How, accused of murdering Huang Na, opens

    SINGAPORE : The trial of Took Leng How, charged with the murder of 8-year-old Huang Na, opened at the Supreme Court on Monday.

    After months of investigations, details are now emerging about how the girl was murdered.

    The 22-year-old accused faces the death penalty if he is convicted.

    The prosecutors will attempt to paint Took Leng How as a cold-blooded killer who took no chances.

    In their opening statement, the prosecutors said Took lured Huang Na to the Pasir Panjang Wholesale Centre on 10 October 2004, sexually assaulted her and then smothered her to death with his bare hands.

    The prosecutors added that Took stamped and kicked the victim to make sure she was dead.

    They will also introduce evidence that Took had tied up Huang Na's body, sealed it in nine layers of plastic bags and dumped the body down a slope at Telok Blangah Hill Park.

    Huang Na's disappearance sparked a nationwide search.

    It wasn't until three weeks later that Took himself led police investigators to the body.

    Took had assisted police on numerous occasions with their investigations, even agreeing to undergo a lie-detector test. But he was never treated as a suspect or arrested because of the lack of evidence.

    On 21 October 2004, Took gave Police officers the slip hours before his polygraph test. He had escaped to Malaysia through the Woodlands Checkpoint.

    Took was arrested by the Royal Malaysian Police and brought back to Singapore on 30 October 2004.

    Took was relaxed and calm when he was brought into the packed courtroom.

    He was seen smiling and waving to his father who was present at the public viewing gallery.

    The case has not only generated immense media attention but from the public as well, some of whom showed up at the Supreme Court hours before the trial began.

    The prosecution has started introducing forensic evidence, linking Took and the victim to the crime scene.

    76 witnesses are expected to testify in this trial, including the victim's mother Huang Shuying.

    - CNA /ls
