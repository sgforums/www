    SCS GREAT SINGAPORE DUCK RACE 2007

    SINGAPORE, 29 May 2007: The Great Singapore Duck Race is BACK with a very important MISSION!

    After 3 years of intense training overseas, the Rubber Duckie Heroes are ready to take the plunge down Singapore River to raise more funds for charity and bring on some serious fun for the community .

    Presented by the Singapore Computer Society to celebrate their 40th anniversary, the proceeds of this charity event will go to support 6 beneficiaries representing a cross section of needs:

    .Concern and Care Society

    .Handicape Welfare Association

    .Metta Welfare Association

    .Tampines Family Service Centre

    .Tan Tock Seng Hospital Community Charity Fund

    .TOUCH Community Services

    The Adopt-A-Duck season will kick off on 20 June 2007. Each adoption ticket costs S$10.

    The Race Day is set on 9 Sep 2007 at the Singapore River.

    You donÂ’t want to miss this historic journey as it might well be the last, before the river turns into a reservoir.

    The Great Singapore Duck Race is organized by Festival Square Circle. Diakonos Productions is the licensee which hold the rights to organize the Duck Race in Asia. The first ever duck race was brought to Asia and held at the Singapore River in 1998. The Great Singapore Duck Race has won multiple international and local awards and also set the World Guinness Book of Records for largest number of rubber ducks ever races out of over 200 events round the world. Over the years, Diakonos has brought the Duck Races to Malaysia, Hong Kong and next stop will be China.
