    Just watched "Charlie and the Chocolate Factory", a book i read as a kid but honestly can't quite remember.

    I expected the movie to be fantastical Tim Burton sensation and visually and stylistically I guess it is, but it just doesn't quite work as a story on screen.

    I think the problem lies with the character Willy Wonka - Johnny Depp is always good at what he does and I think he was playing the role as it was intended but I feel it kind of misses the audience. We aren't supposed to relate to him of course as he is a ridiculous character, but also he is not a hero in the story, is generally quirky but not funny or effecting to drive the adventure.

    To draw parallels with other characters Depp has played, Willy Wonka is not as funny as he should be, and not funny like Jack Sparrow, and he doesn't exactly touch the viewers heart, in a way that Edward Scissorshand did.
