    Here's a list of decent bars to try out!

    Alley Bar
    Wedged between esteemed company on Emerald Hill is the aptly titled Alley Bar, residing as it does in the alley alongside Peranakan Place. From the extremely long terrazzo counter to the stunning gilt-edged mirrors, Alley Bar remains a class act 18 months after opening. It is probably Singapore's thinnest bar Â— the main section often resembles a crowded train carriage Â— but given that most of its clientele is fashionable and good looking this is probably not such a bad thing. A side-room offers a chill-out alternative to the crush and the music is just the way we like it: trendy without dominating. (2 Emerald Hill, Tel: 6235-9108.)

    Balaclava
    Part art deco hideout, part le Corbusier style haven and part jazz music venue, Balaclava is a clandestine delight for the discerning drinker. A decent and modern bar where the interior is as important as the drinks, Balaclava has a one-for-one happy hour from 3pm-9pm, live music and top-notch service. (Suntec City, #01-01B, 1 Raffles Boulevard, Tel: 6339-1600.)

    Insomnia@Chijmes
    Sister-bar to the one in Hong Kong's trendy Lan Kwai Fong is Insomnia@Chijmes. The imported stone makes for a cute Gothic feel whilst avoiding the dingy drear. Offering a wide array of good bottled beers, saucily titled shooters, and house-pours for only $5 during the 5pm-9pm happy hour, the snack menu is mightily tempting and hats off to the house band which can certainly get the joint jumping. (Chijmes, #01-21-23, 30 Victoria Street, Tel: 6338-6883.)

    Living Room
    High stools at the impressive bar counter make way for leather sofas, generous armchairs and mahogany panelling within. Although decked out like a Victorian drawing room, the place doesn't take itself too seriously - expect good deals on bottles of liquor Monday through Thursday, and good live music nightly. (Singapore Marriott Hotel, B1, 320 Orchard Road, Tel: 6831-4506.)

    Mezza9 Martini Bar
    Thanks to the overly efficient air-con, it's not just music that's chilled at Mezza9. The multitude of martinis is mixed to perfection, and the wines are indeed fine. A very cool start, or end, to any evening, and of course this being the Grand Hyatt you can expect a seriously class act. (Grand Hyatt Singapore, 10 Scotts Road, Tel: 6416-7189.)

    Milk Bar
    As the nightlife scene seems to have abandoned the cheesier end of Boat Quay, so the trendy set is gravitating up-river toward that bastion of cool, Zouk. One of the new additions to this burgeoning bar enclave is Milk Bar. Offering a smallish techno and house club upstairs, downstairs is (fittingly) more down-tempo, offering swish cocktails and a less frenetic atmosphere. (The Gallery Evason Hotel, #01-09, 76 Robertson Quay, Tel: 6836-4431.)

    Post Bar
    Still the preferred early-evening hangout of trendy young things is The Fullerton Hotel's Post Bar. Located discreetly off the Lobby, flattering lighting, smart drinks and tempting seafood platters make this a popular spot for business or pleasure - air-kissing required. (Fullerton Hotel, 1 Fullerton Square, Tel: 6877-8135.)

    Provignage - The Wine Cave
    Don't worry, it's not really a cave (in this context we're sure it's French for cellar). Situated just up-river from Clarke Quay, wine prices are affordable, the list interesting - and it has the privilege of Heineken's top honour - Star Bar status. (Riverside View Serviced Apartments, #01-12/13, 30 Robertson Quay, Tel: 6834 1490.)

    TangoÂ’s
    Tempting on at least two counts, if the attractive happy hour offers from 3pm-9pm and upmarket nibbles aren't enough for you, the setting is quite classy and Holland Village always buzzing. (35 Lorong Mambong, Holland Village, Tel: 6463-7365.)

    Union Square
    Billed as Pub-Wine Bar-Pool (the ad agency obviously slaved all night over that one), Union Square also has proper dancing and some good drinks offers. The black and red interior is pretty original too. (The Amara, #02-05, 165 Tanjong Pagar Road, Tel: 6224-6116.)
