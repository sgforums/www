    Silver cans worth $260,000 in cash and prizes can be found throughout the island in month-long treasure hunt starting tomorrow (29th nov)

    A GREAT treasure hunt begins in Singapore tomorrow.

    Everyone is welcome to join an islandwide search for 259 small silver cans which will be hidden in bushes and stuck beneath tables and chairs at hawker centres.

    Some will also be tied to lamp posts, traffic lights and trees.

    They will contain cash and prizes worth a total of $260,000.

    Called The Big Search, the month-long treasure hunt has been organised by SP E-Services, a subsidiary of Singapore Power.

    It will end on Jan 1 or earlier - if the cans are found by then.

    The clues for the location of the cans can be found on the website www.can.com.sg.

    They can also be accessed using Wireless Application Protocol, or WAP-enabled, phones.

    Certificates tucked inside the cans are redeemable for $100, $1,000 or prizes such as mobile phones, airconditioners and food vouchers.

    The most attractive prize will be a $95,000 Citroen Xsara car, which will be parked somewhere in Singapore.

    The treasure hunt will specially draw 11,000 volunteers from the Boys' Brigade in Singapore and Singapore Red Cross Society.

    But the prize money and goodies they collect will go to their respective organisations to benefit the elderly, poor, disadvantaged and disabled.

    Members of the public can, however, keep the prize they find.

    The SP E-Services' managing director, Mrs Jeanne Cheng, said:

    'The main objective of the hunt is to introduce more people to the world of the Web in a fun and interactive way.

    'At the same time, since this is the traditional season of giving, we wanted to do our bit for society.'
