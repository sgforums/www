    2 S'poreans on trial over bid to withdraw $157b

    Sun, Nov 18, 2007
    The Straits Times

    HONG KONG - TWO Singaporeans are on trial in Hong Kong for using fake cheques and certificates of deposit to try to withdraw more than US$108 billion (S$157 billion) from a DBS branch in the territory.
    Also in the dock with Eddie Woon Kwok Jin, 53, and Lim Ban Hoong, 62, on the opening day of the trial on Friday was 32-year-old Ma Ping, a mainland Chinese woman, the Hong Kong media reported yesterday.

    The Singaporeans had approached the bank in Hong Kong's Wanchai district in April and presented photocopies of certificates of deposit and cheques allegedly issued by Overseas Trust Bank (OTB) in 1948.

    The bank originally belonged to OTB but was acquired by DBS in 2003.

    Staff of the bank alerted the police after finding that OTB had not been established yet in 1948. The bank lost no money in the incident, reported the South China Morning Post.
    The three pleaded not guilty to the charges.

    Woon and Lim claimed that they were entrusted by Ma to withdraw the money from the account of one Sa Haiian, who was said to be Ma's aunt.

    The prosecution said the false papers predated the opening of OTB in 1955, and the bank had no record of such a customer.

    Also, contrary to banking practice, the cheques were found to bear two branch names - Wanchai and Shanghai - and the authorising signatures were not in the proper spot, reported the Ming Pao Daily News.

    Mr Wong Yiu Chung, a DBS branch manager who spoke with Woon and Lim during their first meeting, testified that the two claimed to have 30 cheques issued in 1948, each with a face value of US$3.6 billion.

    But they refused to produce the original, saying that they would do so only when they met the bank's senior management.

    Mr Wong then requested that Ma appear personally at the next appointment.

    The three were arrested by the police when they turned up at the bank in June.

    The trial continues tomorrow.
