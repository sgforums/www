    Yup, I totally agree...the best thing about blogshops are the prices. Without the need to pay rental, staff and bills, they can then transfer their savings over to their products so that consumers like us can enjoy them.

    On second thoughts, more often than not, blogshops don't really offer the range unlike physical magic shops. We are pretty much limited to buying only products that they import based on their popularity.

    Of course, they do offer us an option to bring in products that they don't offer but only when a customer orders and will eventually have to wait slightly more than 2 weeks to a month to receive them.

    Blogshops offer us more by giving us alternatives to where to get the best things at the best prices.

    Besides that, true service can only be experienced at a physical magic shop.

    I patronise both physical and virtual magic shops. Good physical magic shops have fantastic service and knowledgeable personnel to advice me in what is most suitable to buy and areas to work on in order to present an entertaining performance. Also, going to a magic shop is a magical experience by itself. You are well surrounded by toys that us grown ups may have long forgotten to love. Not knowing what new tricks you're going to see, the sense of awe and of course, the exchange of thoughts and ideas with other people who share your passion! Blogshops can't offer that.

    But of course, due to the physical attributes that surrounds a physical shop, prices of products can't be lowered to an extent similar to those of a blogshop.

    Currently there is non in Singapore, but if someone can offer the perks of a physical shop as well the product prices of a blogshop, I would consider that the ultimate success.
