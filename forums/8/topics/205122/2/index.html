    Guys.. Some of you are being quite rude towards the depiction of Hindu deities.. May I request that you do not assume things (e.g calling the serpents Medusa) if you aren't sure about the facts.

    The Lion Deity picture: His name is Narasimha, the fourth incarnation of major Hindu deity, Vishnu. Although a very benign Deity, Vishnu assumed this fierce form, as the demon (whose name is Hiranyakasibu) he is killing had obtained a very complex boon from another God.

    According to the boon, the demon could not be killed indoors or outdoors, at dawn or at dusk, by man or by animal, by any man-made weapons, on the ground or in the air.

    Thus to kill him while honoring the boons, Vishnu took on a form that was neither man or animal, He dragged the demon to the castles entrance (neither indoors or outdoors), He placed the demon on His lap (neither on ground or in the air), He waited for midnight (neither day nor night) and killed him by ripping his torso using His claws (not a man-made weapon)

    The serpent (whose name is Sesha) at the background serves Vishnu as His bed. And as someone posted earlier, Narasimha is worshipped in the hindu temple at Waterloo Street.

    The boy (not girl) in the picture, is the demon's son, Prahalada. Unlike his father, Prahalada was a devotee to Vishnu, and was the one who calmed Him down after He flew into a rage while killing the demon.

    Hope that helped to clear your queries on the picture
