    I've just found out that Roadies (people who ride Road bikes ) are more affected by distractions than MTB-ers (Mountain bike riders ).

    Why do I say so?

    One day I was with my friends at Sentosa. We were onboard the 2nd level of an open-air double decker bus, going downslope towards the beach.

    Halfway down the slope, we spotted 2 Roadies chionging down by the side of the road, side-by-side.

    Deciding to encourage them (they look like they "pia" very hard ) , my friend yelled out "TOGOPARTS!!!!!!!!!! "

    In response, one of the Roadies raised both arms and yelled "WOOOOOOOOOOO! "

    Sadly, this action caused him to lose control of his bike and he buanged into his friend, causing a messy heap by the roadside!

    The Rest of us:

    The Roadie who kana buang (to his friend ) : x@#$%!

    The guy who yelled Togoparts:

    On the other hand, MTB-ers, BMXers and Street Riders (people who ride FR bikes in urban areas ) are very much unaffected by distractions and can multitask while riding (eg: Talk on phone on 1 hand, eat and drink using both hands and riding "handless " , taking items out of bag (therefore riding handless for a few secs while the bag is brought to the front , etc..etc..
    )

    My guess is because Roadies are focused only on pia-ing as hard as possible that they sometimes become unaware of their surroundings and a little distraction might prove dangerous!
