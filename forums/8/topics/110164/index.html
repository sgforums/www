    To all my fellow divers,
    Fins Magazine of Singapore had posted this thread online:

    I have been told that there may be a need for experienced divers who are strong in search & recovery skills to help with recovering bodies in low visibility waters.

    Likely places include Sri Lanka and Thailand.

    These divers must also be able to handle the trauma of fishing decomposing bodies out of the waters.

    I have also to say that, the mental trauma of doing a thing like this will stay with you for many nights to come.

    I have no details at this point - but if you think you can handle this, and only if you can handle this, drop me an email at edmund@finsonline.com, and I'll let you know when thigns are firm.

    "Thai officials are now calling for volunteers from anyone who is able to help - from professional divers to help find the bodies, and grief counsellors for those who need them."

    (Posted by one of the people who is organising a team of divers for Search & Recovery work - Fei-wei
    PADI OWSI 466067)

    http://www.channelnewsasia.com/cgi-bin/search/search_7days.pl?status=&search=diver&id=124700

    I'm asking for:
    1. Volunteers for recovery work, especially ex-Navy divers who have done this sort of stuff. Although I need all the help I can get, can we please have only DMs and above and/or techies (unless you are ex-Navy which in this context is more relevant). This is for safety reasons - no point going there only to have us rescue you.

    I'm just 1 of several hundred OWSIs here, but if the response to cleanup days shows the spirit of volunteerism in us, then we should have the 10 volunteers that I'm looking for.

    2. 2 co-ordinators / logistics persons, if you are a experienced diver so much the better.

    What volunteers can expect:
    1. This will be an entirely own-cost effort - be ready to pay your own airfare and expenses etc, although if this effort reaches semi-official status the airlines may look upon us kindly. I'm thinking it should cost $600 onwards, depending on how long you want to stay.

    2. Dirty, stinky and tiring work - the bodies have been soaking 3 days already so by the time we hit the water it will be 5 days, which would make the corpses just about a pulpy mess.

    If you would like to help, please e-mail me, or call me at 91476031. (If you have another number you have been using, please call the above number all the same - it's for this effort). If you can't help, please forward this appeal to as many divers you know. If you want to give money, please let me know too.

    I hope to be able to leave with all volunteers for Phuket Friday morning or Saturday morning.

    (Warning from Greg Doyle [Technical Diver])

    Be careful about recruiting people for body-recovery. The diving would be in contaminated black water. It requires a full biohazard setup including dry-suits and helmets with bacteria/ameobe proof double exhausts, surface supplied gasses / umbilicals, decontamination equipment and safety divers.

    It is well beyond technical diving as commonly understood on FiNS.

    Coordination of this is best left to professionals. I would guess that only the SCDF and some commerical diving operations have the expertise.
