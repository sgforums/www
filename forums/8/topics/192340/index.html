    Amount to pay depends on these factors:

    1) Depends on degree, the higher your degree, the more expensive it will be.

    2) Depends on the kind of lenses you are getting. If your degree is high, yet you want your lenses to be super thin, be prepare to pay a bomb. Add in scratch-resistant pay more. If you use specialized lenses that help to keep your degree in check, pay even more. Use those specially for computers pay even more.

    3) Depends on brand of lenses you want. Seiko lenses tend to be more expensive than the other ones available, but they last if you pay a little more for scratch-resistant lenses.

    4) Depends on frame. Some fashionable frames aren't as expensive, just depends on what you get. Get a titanium which is ultra light and durable, you can prepare to pay a bomb. But this have one disadvantage, wind blows and it flies away. Can't remember the price for metal and plastic frames, should be about the same. One more thing, request for those very odd colours will require you to pay more because the optician has to specially order it from don't know where.

    Anything more, ask your optician.
