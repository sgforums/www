    Going with somewhat the same vein as Revelations said, I've a quote here from the notes of Moby's latest albumn Play. I didn't know he is a christian albeit one with rather liberal left wing views.

    "Fundamentalism (of any kind) troubles me. The world is too big & too intricate to conform to our ideas of what it should be like. In my experience I've found that most fundamentalists aren't so much attached to their professed ideologies as they are to the way in which these ideologies try to make sense of a confusing world. But the world is confusing, and just because we invent myths and theories to explain away the chaos we're still going to live in a world that's older and more complicated than we'll ever understand. So many religious & political & scientific & social systems fail in that they try to impose a rigid structure onto what is an inherently ambiguous world. I m not suggesting that we stop trying to understand things. Trying to understand the worls can be fun and at times helpful. But if we base our belief systems on the humble assumption that the complexities of the world are ontologically beyond our understanding, then maybe our belief systems will make more sense & end up causing less suffering."
