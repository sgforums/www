    If you are able to identify the person who gave this offer to your cousin, you can report immediately to CASE and get them to investigate.

    If you can't, it will be difficult to convince them that the shop has not extended its due service. Very seldom do small privateers offer a wear and exchange if does not fit policy. Only medium-sized and large organisations encourage it.

    Again, the manner in which the manager spoke to you (if you quoted him accurately) is unacceptable. However, as with most complaints to CASE, without a witness it's your word against his. The most CASE will do is log your complaint and KIV. If other shoppers encounter similar problems with the same shop or shopowner, then they will investigate and blacklist the shop.

    If you think CASE will take immediate action, dun get your hopes up. This is the situation nowadays. Many customers are rudely served and then challenged to make a complaint. In this case, there is no point in speaking with a lawyer as the cost of the shoes is just below $200/-. Consulation fees can run way beyond that and if the lawyer's letters are exchanged, then legal costs will increase accordingly.

    For now, i recommend that you retain your receipts and put in an official writing to CASE to log-in the matter. Then see how it goes.

    In future, remember to get the name of the person who made this verbal agreement. And try to purchase shoes from a reputable distributor. You may be looking for the best bargains but sometimes, it's worth that little extra to avoid hassles like this.
