Day in the life of see hum

  • Illegal Operation
    18 Jul 02, 09:56
    I learnt today that I might live longer for the next couple of months at least. The recent eat-hum-get-hep-A scare slowed harvesting down.

    Why allow me to live.

    I haven't got an eye, so I can't see. No legs, so I can't walk. No mouth so I can't eat. When plaktons sticks on to my body, I melt them. Does plaktons taste salty? Slimy? Sweet? I will never know. When fishes bite the worm and ends up deep fried and served on the table, they deserved it. When the see hum gets ripped out of its shell and toss into gravy, they haven't got a choice. No fighting chance. No means to escape the crutches of the harvester.

    Mom said we are the products of god's creation gone wrong. He first made the see hum. He learnt that see hum got no legs and thus cannot walk. So he dumped us into the sea. Then god made tortise. Too heavy. He dumps them into the sea too. The rest, as they said, is history.

    I am the see hum. I knows nothing of what the world looks like. When I am hungry, I simly stretches my muscles. It pushes my shell open to let water enter me. Then the plaktons comes in. Even the plaktons could swim.

    When I opens up and finds no water, I have probably landed in a basket.

    When I opens up and finds hot water, I am probably on my way to be eaten half raw. Then I will scream. I learnt that I should not scream because that will only make it easier for them to pry me open and eat me up. Damn these people to hep-A for eternity.

    Then when I open up to find no more shell, I am on my way to reincarnation. And please god, please. Make me something else my next life. Tortise also can...
