    Hi,

    Other than the dryness and wearing of the wrong side that may be accountable for the pain u're experiencing, u may wanna pay attention to other areas like whether the pain is due to infection of the cornea. Sometimes the surface of the eye (the area where it is in contact with the lenses) maybe scratched, causing bacteria to thrive in the infected area when u do not wash the lenses clean. So i advise you if the pain still persist (normally accompanied with tearing and redness), you have to seek attention from eye specialist for only they have the specialised equipments to observe the surface of the eyeballs. Not trying to scare you, personally i have came across such worrying problems when i wore lenses last time. It actually got very bad that the eye developed cornea ulcers. The pain was excruciating and i couldn't wear lenses anymore... Please do not hesitate to see an eye specialist if the pain persist, let him/her do a thorough check on ur eyes and see if anything unusual is happening before it is too late cos in severe cases of cornea ulcers, it may lead to scarring of the cornea surface (in this case, one could not wear lenses anymore due to the pain encountered everytime u wear it) or even worse, it may lead to blindness. Do not worry, if there really is ulcers or inflamed scratches on the surface of the eyes, the specialist will normally prescribed you with antiseptic eye lotion to apply every few hours. Your eyes will need plenty of rest and no lenses to be worn for quite sometime til the wound has completely recovered.

    I hope in your case it's not due to anything bad, it may simply be dryness or incompatibility of the lenses u're wearing. Juz stay cautious and hygiene is really impt in the cleanliness of the contact lenses.

    Take care.
