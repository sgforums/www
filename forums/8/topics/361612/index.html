    Yes, I heard of ppl barely touch the 8k ceiling buy boon keng city view flat for 600K. After selecting the flat then do the math then realise they everyday have to eat grass. So they forfeit their selection but had to fork out the deposit. Ouch.

    Interest rate for HDB loan is 2.6%, depending on your age, can take up to 30 years. I think the max is 35, not so sure.

    CPF contribution can take max safe level of 35% combined household income.

    So lets say you work out the monthly installment to be $1300, but your CPF max out at $1000, you have to fork out the 300 bucks by cash every month.

    So if you really want to buy flat, you honestly have to think long term. If you think you rather not fork out extra cash in hand every month, it would be wise to get a flat that your CPF contribution alone can completely cover. Thats why ppl prefer BTO, its usually cheaper. Look at the sengkang and punggol flats for instance, 4/5 room don't exceed 350K.
