    Without a doubt, one of the most entertaining wrestlers ever. Even when he played the role of the bad guy, ya many of you will remember the custody saga involving himself and Ray Mysterio over Dominic, he still exuades that charm and charisma which endears him to all the fans out there. He perfectly fitted into the role of the lying, cheating, stealing latino sterotype, riding out in a pimp'd mobile as his theme played overhead (which many in the audience asusme he stole it off some guy). He swung matches his way through witty deception. (I will always remember the match he had with Batista, where he took the title into the ring while the refree was taken out, then tosed it to one side, lay flat on the ring, and making it look as if Batista had cheated by knocking him out with the title.) Many question what's so great abt wrestling? Guys clad in tights beating the hell out of each other in well rehearsed syncronized motions, enacting lines off shabbily written scripts with sharp predictable plot changes? Yes, that is wrestling. But there is a part of wrestling that many of us will fail to understand or even appreciate-the passion in entertainment. It is guys like Eddie, who night after night, battle their bodies to bring out whatever entertainment value is left in those cheap-written plots, it is his personality and his passion that transcends the bounds of his role, bringing to life a character that breaks off from the chains of rigidity and predictability that scorns our everyday existence. It is painful, it is brutal, it is black comedy, but we love it and we love them all who give it. For all that, we salute you Eddie and all the wrestlers out there. May you rest in peace.
