    Do not forget people that this is not a debate. Each path has its own strengths and weaknesses. It is up to the person to choose the path he wishes, and to proceed from there.

    The Poly Path was originally intended to cater for students who could not qualify for JC. The objective was to teach them a skill, so that they could join the workforce once they graduate.

    The JC Path has always been the same. It is to prepare students to go to the university to obtain at least a degree. Have I got all these right? Please update me if I am wrong.

    However, the government did not forsee that there would poly graduates who did not want to join the workforce, but who instead wished to continue their studies. This number of poly graduates who wish to further their studies has been growing and is still growing.

    Now, there is a problem. NTU and NUS were there to cater only to the JC students at first. Now Poly graduates want to have a chance to further their studies at these places as well. But NUS and NTU cannot allocate enough places to satisy them, because they were there to cater to JC students, not Poly Graduates.

    So the Poly graduates have no choice but to either go to work or further their studies overseas. Not everyone can afford the cost of studying overseas, so those who cannot want to study at the local universities.

    But like I said earlier, the universities were there originally to cater only to JC students. And due to the limited number of places, only a small fraction of Poly graduates could manage to get a place. Imagine two universities catering to graduates from 14 JCs and 4 Polys, not to mention foreign students as well. There is simply not enough space for everyone. I am not counting the places of SMU in this case; the number of places they have are not significant yet.

    So some people started to complain about this, and the whole thing became JC vs Poly, when there is actually no conflict at all. It is not a faceoff. And here I see people arguing about this, trying to prove that each is better than the other.

    Well, I shall state my opinions objectively. Choose the JC path if you are certain that you will be able to make it to a university, because frankly an 'A' level certificate is not a job certicate. Be prepared for fierce competition for limited places. There are 14 JCs and only two universities.

    Choose the Poly path if you wish to obtain a job certificate and have no plans to further your studies yet. After all, a diploma does command higher pay and rank compared with an 'A' level certificate, but of course, the best is still a degree at least.

    There are factors that may influence or affect your decision directly or indirectly, and you may have no choice at all. For me, I got a E8 for my mandarin paper, so there is no way I am going to qualify for JC. Yes, I am not afraid to say that my mandarin is like shit, and I do not give a damn about Chinese culture sorry.

    So I had to go to a polytechnic. Is that the end of the world? Now, in just six months, I will be graduating from Temasek Polytechnic. I have plans to further my studies in NTU, which is why I have chosen an elective that will enable me to do so.

    Bottom line: It's your choice, your life. You do want you want.
