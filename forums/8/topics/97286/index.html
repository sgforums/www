    Viewers express unhappiness over losing good Singapore Idol singers

    SINGAPORE : Some viewers say it is not fair to see the good singers being bundled out of the Singapore Idol contest......yet it is this element that is getting them glued to the programme, week after week.
    On Friday, another crowd favourite, David Yeo, met the same fate as his previous idol-mates, Jeassea Thyidor and Beverly Morata.

    Many viewers felt that David, affectionately known as the Ah Beng idol, is not the weakest in Thursday's show.

    And they were definitely dismayed when he was voted out, after receiving the least number of votes from the public.

    "We are really dissapointed that he got eliminated this week because he is a really good performer and a good singer as well."

    "He can sing and he should not be voted out tonight and it is a real pity."

    "I was really surprised because I thought David sang quite well and the song was really nice as well."

    However, the judges were more objective.

    Mr Ken Lim, Judge of Singapore Idol, said: "David has always been very technical. The public sort of saw that in him in the choice of song yesterday and in that sense he does not seem very versatile."

    Ms Florence Lian, Judge of Singapore Idol, said: "I do not think he was the worse singer in yesterday's competition, again I am a little bit dissapointed but I am not surprised."

    Mr Douglas Oliveiro, Judge of Singapore Idol, said: "Well, if you ask me I will kick Jerry out...but David did not come across to me as the guy who have had the longevity in the competition."

    Many say it is not just a talent contest but also how one connects with the crowd for them to continue voting for the idol week after week.

    And although David may have been ernest and committed in his performance, it is the public's vote that matter most.

    Well, for now at least, he can extend that commitment to his other dream of becoming a successful lawyer.

    And Idol-fever gripped hundreds of fans at Orchard Cineleisure on Saturday evening.

    It was the first public appearance for the remaining eight Idol finalists as they received a grand welcome at the 8th birthday bash of MediaCorp Publishing's LIME magazine.

    Fans got the chance to check out the Singapore Idol finalists.

    The crowd pleasers took turns singing acapella - to the delight of their fans!

    The finalists also took time out for an autograph signing session. - CNA
