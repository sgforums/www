    The Smoking Cessation Support Group provides voluntary counselling to assist in-patients who want to quit their smoking habit during their admission in Changi General Hospital. This service is also available to the public.

    In addition to counselling, the support group screens and refers patients to the pharmacist and/or the doctor for Nicotine Replacement Therapy or other smoking cessation aids if they are unable to cope with the withdrawal symptoms.

    Smoking is the single most important preventable lifestyle habit that increases the risk of cardiovascular and respiratory diseases, including lung cancer.

    Although physicians dutifully advise smoker patients to reduce, if not quit, smoking, patients often do not know how to stop nor do they know how to cope with the withdrawal effects.

    Many smokers are not aware of the availability of smoking cessation aids, such as nicotine patch, nicotine inhaler and oral medication, to assist them to pull through or cope with the withdrawal period.

    There is a team of nurse-counselors as well as a doctor to assist the patients. A Pharmacy team is also available to provide advice to the public Mondays to Fridays, from 9.00 pm to 4.00 pm.

    You can contact Ms Ang Sai Yim at Tel: 68501889 or Mr Shashu at Tel: 68501492 for more information. Or Email: sai_yim_ang@cgh.com.sg
