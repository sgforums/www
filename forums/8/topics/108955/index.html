    maybe i should highlight that most furniture items from ikea are made in china. i am not saying that china suppliers are not good... actually they have improved so much that the furniture they manufactured are sturdy these days.

    but it is usually ikea's policy to always cut the costs. china suppliers want the big business of ikea and are willing to cut the cost because ikea buys in bulk.. however.. the cost cuts also meant that the suppliers has to source for cheaper raw materials which are much more inferior in quality that what the suppliers normally use..

    though also the furniture samples are sent for lab testing before they are sold on the market.. the lab test standards for ikea are not as strict. nevertheless the furniture samples still pass the test. but it is not known if the final products on retail are exactly of the same specifications as those that are sent for testing.
