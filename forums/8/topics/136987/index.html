    14 Jun 05, 19:51
    1) Yes, even if there is no written contract between A and B, a verbal representation will still constitute a contract in this case.

    The consideration that you are wondering about is already implied

    In other words, the $20,000 in this agreement include the consideration for A to part with his Vertu as well as the consideration to allow B some time to think about whether he want to buy. So Im thinking there is consideration

    2)Not too sure....Im thinking maybe the postal rule cannot apply here.

    The reason for this postage rule is to protect the offeree in those olden days when there is bound to be a time lag between the date the offeree made up his mind and the date his acceptance reaches the offeror cos the fastest way to convey a written acceptance is through postal mailing.

    In this case, B cannot seek protection through the postal rule since he eventually chose to fax.

    But I think B can still sue since A has breached his contract by selling his goods early while B had taken all the available steps to communicate his acceptance (including making a phone call to communicate his acceptance).
