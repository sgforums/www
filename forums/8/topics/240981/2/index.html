    Originally posted by hiphop2009:
    what if the comments in STOMP are defaming? can we use dat as a base for legal cases?

    *juz asking for curiosity sake*
    There are legal criteria as to what constitutes defamation and what constitutes acceptable defences. If you want to learn how to defame someone, just watch CSJ.

    If someone makes comments about you that are clearly false (and you have evidence to that effect) and those comments are designed to denigrate your character or standing in the eyes of those who have seen or heard those comments, then you have a case. Remember, if you take someone to court for defamation, you have the burden of proof. You've got to show evidence that the other party is in the wrong.

    Posting a photo of someone jumping queue, smoking in a non-smoking area, or eating in the MRT train...that's all perfectly legal. If you add a comment, then you are making an interpretation of the photo...be careful that what you say is true, something CSJ has never learned.
