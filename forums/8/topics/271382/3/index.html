    Honors is only more useful at the start of one's career, not that much once you go further down your career path.

    Don't expect to be paid more just because u have honors for your 1st degree. Civil service will definitely do that, some big MNCs may do, but don't expect it.

    Having studied already and you are in your 1st job. Make good with it. But do know that engineers suffer a natural flaw. They become more expensive and value add less as they become older n more experienced. More prone to lay off. Imagine if ur work experience is 10yrs worth n ur pay is 5 times that of a fresh grad, is ur value add necessarily 5 times more that of a fresh grad? Unlikely if u are in pure engineering role. From mgmt's perspective, instead of having u, they'll just hire more engineers, keep less senior engineers like u n still be able to do the job.

    Now job market booming. It's great, but everything moves in cycles, when economy goes down, be prepared, be nimble. Now is the time to start preparing urself. I wud recommend trying to move into a role which has some management job function, it increases ur value and allows u even to change industries shd the need arise.

    For further education, if u really want, then go ahead n do a MBA but only after considerable years of experience. Our local masters are still acceptable but go only for those which have tie-ups with reputable overseas graduate schools. At MBA level, it's not so much of theoretical knowledge. But networking and simply the reputation of the school which u got into.

    U'll find that the pple that go the furthest in their career are those who know how to connect well with pple, outgoing, make use of pple well. In short they are pple kind of pple. If u want to go far, focus on developing this aspect of urself.

    The true fact is no idiot aims low in their career. Everyone wants to be the best, just like no idiot wants to be poor but there are still poor pple out there. Every industry career structure is a pyramid, there are less places for the pple at the top. So u gotta differentiate urself and standout from the crowd.

    How u do that? U have to find that out urself better.
