    Roti Paratha {Flaky Whole Meal Bread}
    Makes 10-12

    Ingredients
    1 1/2 cup whole meal flour, fine ground
    1 1/2 cup plain flour
    1 1/2 teaspoon salt
    6-8 tablespoon melted butter
    1 cup water
    Canola oil for frying

    Method
    1. Sieve whole meal flour, white flour and salt into a mixing bowl and rub it 1 tablespoon of butter. Add water all at once and mix to form a firm but not stiff dough. Knead dough for 10 minutes (the more it is kneaded, the lighter the bread will be). Form dough into a ball, cover with clear plastic wrap and stand for 1 hour.
    2. Divide dough into 12-14 equal portions and roll each into a smooth ball. Roll each ball of dough on a lightly floured board into a very thin circular shape. Brush melted butter into thin dough. With a knife, make a cut from the center of each circle to the outer edge. Starting at the cut edge, roll the dough closely into a cone shape. Pick it up, press the apex of the cone and the base towards each other and flatten slightly.
    3. You will now have a small, roughly circular lump of dough again. Lightly flour the board again, and roll out the dough very gently, taking care not to press too hard and let the air out at the edges. The parathas should be a round as possible, but not as thinly rolled as the first time.
    4. Cook on a hot griddle liberally greased with oil, turning parathas and spreading more oil, until they are golden brown. Serve hot with curries.

    note: The whole meal and plain or roti flour can be replaced by 3 cups of plain white flour.
