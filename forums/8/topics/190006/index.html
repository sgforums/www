    I met a new person from Singapore today. I didn't know her, nor she me. I was just showing my apartment for her to rent.

    Well, I know a few people who are versatile enough to be able to switch back and forth from speaking Singapore accented English to a more American like inflection. My sister constantly amazes me when I observe her on the phone changing her speaking style depending on who she is talking to. Some might called it two-faced, I call it practical.

    But going back to an issue that has been raised, I know its easy to say that everyone should be proud of Singlish, but lets have a look at reality. Even the government is against the mutilation of British English. I know they concentrate more on the fact that Singlish mangles grammar, but pronounication is also important in that everlasting "first impression."

    Take a look at the forum threads. Not all, but a significant percentage of posts reveal a very abbreviated form of English. Singlish, mixed with slang, internet abbreviations, and Singapore-centric terms such as NPNT. A foreigner coming upon sgForums would probably not understand, and worse, have a low opinion of our English skills.

    This is absolutely not a problem and infact a plus for the the site as SGForums is built for Singaporeans and frequented by Singaporeans. However, compare the language to that of an American forum site like Craigslist's Rants and Raves, and you'll see the difference. In the case of Singaporeans living overseas and working overseas, we have to adapt to their culture. After all, we are guests in their country, so if we want climb their social ladder and advance ourselves in their system, we have to play by their rules. As a result, it is frustrating not to be able to adjust speaking style when others do it so effortlessly.

    Again, I would like to stress, there is nothing wrong with Singlish, but that only applies if you restrict yourself to a small corner of the world.
