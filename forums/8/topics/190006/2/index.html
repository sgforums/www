    Korean mothers are typically near obsessive about their children's education, even more so than Kiasu Singaporean mothers. Trust me, I speak from personal experience.

    Actually the best ENglish is to speak it WITHOUT an accent. If you notice, many newscasters actually speak that way, which is why regardless of whether you are in American, Australia, Britain or even Singapore (though over here, some have weird accents) - the news is actually easier to understand. Of course it is inavoidable that certain cultural pronunciations are different but by and large, it is the clarity of speech that should be the focus.

    Having said that, a lot of Singaporeans speak with a very 'clipped' enunciation - THAT may be the reason why Singlish is not so easily understood because we tend to shorten a lot of our vowels, and harden a lot of the consonants. You can definitely improve on that without needing to fake any accent at all.
