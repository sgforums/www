    taken from STI

    Hold-up fails at betting outlet

    A MAN with dark paint smeared all over his face and armed with what resembled a silver pistol tried to rob a Singapore Turf Club betting outlet at Balestier Hill Shopping Centre yesterday morning.

    But he failed, and no one was hurt.

    The man apparently fled when he saw two of the shop's employees hiding under the counter.

    Last month, robbers struck at a Singapore Pools outlet in Block 177, Toa Payoh Central just before it was due to open and murdered one of its employees, Madam Song Beng Hua, 36. The robbers also carted a safe with an undisclosed amount of cash away. Police are still investigating.

    In the Balestier case, the attempted hold-up happened at 8.40 am, just over an hour after the ground-floor outlet opened for business at 7.30 am.

    When The Sunday Times visited the shop yesterday, the manager and its employees declined to comment. But the owner of a neighbouring paint shop said that the shopping centre is usually quiet in the mornings.

    He added that a customer from the betting outlet came into his shop, saying there was a robbery and seconds after, he spotted a man dashing across his shop carrying a sling bag, but he could not see the face.

    According to a police statement, the man fled when two female employees at the outlet ducked under the counter, and three customers there fled when they saw the man enter the outlet.

    The police are looking for the 1.8m-tall man, who is slim. He was last seen wearing a long-sleeved, blue checked shirt, dark trousers, a pair of white gloves, a dark baseball cap and industrial boots.

    Anyone with information on the case can call the police on 1-800-255-0000.
