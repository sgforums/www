    With a Diploma in sales and marketing...my seniors have found jobs as either salesmen, no not door to door, but sales executives in companies..and some have gone on as marketers not sure where though lol.. Most of them went on to the Curtin program. Once you graduate from the DSM program, you can choose either to go to Curtin Australia or Newcastle Business school in Northrumbia England.

    If you want to take the Curtin Australia route it can be done between 1 year and 4 months to 18 months. This will give you a Bachelor of Commerce degree.

    If you opt for the Northrumbia route you'll take a 6 month advanced Diploma course, then a 1 year program to earn a Bachelor of Arts degree.

    Or if you don't want to go to a degree course but more of a professional certificate qualification first.. you can go and take the Chartered Institute of Marketing, CIM (UK) paper that gives you the highest professional qualification available in marketing.

    Generally those who take the CIM (UK) paper take 2 subjects I think...because you get exemptions from your DSM course. But the failure rate is high, about 70%-80% failures per batch and you just pay your exam fee, they give you a case study a month before the exam and you go in and write rubbish.. But I have to warn you CIM is usually only taken by those who have working experience, without it my lecturer said that it's quite difficult to pass, unless you have really good marketing ideas. I heard from him the markers mark your paper based on a "Would I hire this guy to run the marketing program for this case study's company?" That'll earn you either a pass or fail. Note that this CIM (UK) tie up is no longer existent in MIS, so you'll have to do your independent agency who can sign you up for it.

    Hope all this stuff helps
