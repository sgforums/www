    when your computers are connected to a router (not a switch or a hub), the router will be the "server" (so to speak). most routers nowadays (for home solutions) will come in-built with a firewall, NAT, etc. to the "outside" world, only your router will be visible. they will not be able to access your computers unless settings are done in the router, or they manage to hack into the firewall

    routers allocate each node (computer) that is connected to it, the same bandwidth. but, the amount it can allocate is limited to the amount of bandwidth the broadband connection can offer. you will notice a lag if one of the other computers are using almost all of the bandwidth, ie. downloading files
