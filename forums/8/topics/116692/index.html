    recent box office hits: -


    Ayutha Ezhuthu
    Cast: Surya, Madhavan, Sidharth, Esha Deol, Meera Jasmine & Trisha
    Based on a true story of three young men with varied backgrounds, aspirations and attitudes. 3 different youths with one similar motive- To take on the world the way they see it. Their paths cross over the Napier Bridge on a Thursday morning and their lives change irreversibly. Inba, the hired assassin, Michael, his intended victim, and Sidharth, the innocent by-stander caught in the cross-fire between the two. A horrific showdown results in their lives going in total disarray, changing their destinies.Directed by internationally acclaimed indian director Maniratnam, this movie is an innovative portrayal of the little explored genre of sociopolitical conflict, as to why the world is the way it is now..
    Great stunts by Vikram Dharma, excellent music by AR Rahman and breaktaking cinematography by Ravi Chandran makes this one of the biggest blockbusters of the year.... stylish stuff -Highly recommended


    Autograph
    Cast:Cheran,Gopika and Sneha
    The world is full of people whose notion of a satisfactory future is, in fact, a return to the idealized past.This low budgeted but excellently crafted movie acted by mostly unknown actors is about the autobiography of a young man about to marry, who decides to take a trip down the memory lane back to the 70s and 80s, not only to rediscover his past but to recreate the nostalgic moments in a young man?s life which live in his memories for ever. Along with his journey, he recalls at different points his past loves during his child hood, his college days and adult life.And the incidents in the film are very real and everybody would identify and relate them to their real life memories.The main hightlight of the movie is the touching moments and extreme realism pertaining to people,places and emotions which seem to co-incide with real life contributed to its roaring success.Intense depression and melancholy through the visuals and camerawork is prevalent thoughout the movie.One of the most innovative movies of the year. -Likely to leave u with a heavy heart


    Munna Bhai MBBS
    Cast:Sanjay Dutt, Sunil Dutt, Gracy Singh, Jimmy Shergil

    Munnabhai MBBS is a hilarious and heart warming story of a local goon's journey into the medical world - a place he neither belongs to nor believes in. It is a classic story of a right man in a wrong place. A world very different in the one he lives in.The misfit muscles his way into a medical college to prove a point- He might just cure you. And he does. Without resorting to tired-and-tested gimmicks. But not medically dissect any further, many moments in this picture make it worth a prescription for laughter therapy
    -Well worth the trip to the local doctor


    Ennakku 20 Unakku 18
    Cast : Trisha, Arun and Shreeya
    Two strangers meet during their journey from Mumbai to Chennai. They longingly look at each other eyes for more than 24 hours but do not exchange a word. On reaching the Chennai central, they part ways only to realise that their hearts are no longer with them. Both of them, students in different colleges, start searching for each other without knowing name or address. They live next doors but never meet each other.Music is catchy and melodious and is a plus point to the film. Camera work is worth mention especially in the songs shot at Venice and Rome. A film aimed at teens.


    Ji
    Cast: Ajit and Trisha

    Run of the mill action entertainer where a student opposes political leaders
