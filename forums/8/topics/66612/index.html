    After working at the airport for 2 months, I'm finally being brought back to reality after taking the first lunch in school, in NTU.

    It's pretty amazing when you compare the prices and the quality of food.

    There's simply no competition.

    And the reason for that can come from the lack of quality control.

    If you don't know, food sold in Changi Airport are checked and scrutinized occasionally. Hence it's hard to find food that's lousy there.

    There is also myraid stalls in the staff canteens there. It's almost hard to find food not in your favour. If you can't find them in Terminal 2, you can in Terminal 1.

    In case you haven't tried, the Cha Kway Tiao and Mixed Rice in Terminal 1 are great.

    There are 2 prices there, staff and non-staff prices. Food that are sold to staffs cost $0.40 cheaper. So for rice with, fried chicken cutlet, kang kong and scrambled eggs, it is only going for $2.20 there if you're buying as a staff.

    Things are pretty much different in school.

    Let's take a look at what I ate just now, in school. Rice with curry chicken, tuo fu and xiao ba chai. I had to pay $2.60 for that. Now that's not the worst part. The worst part is it doesn't even taste half as good as those sold in hawker centres or quarter as good as Changi Airport if you want to compare.

    Unrepentant, they are

    I can't imagine how many years they have been tending that stall.
