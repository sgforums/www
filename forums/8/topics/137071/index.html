    How big is singapore? some say 40km x 40km of usable landspace.

    How many singaporeans need work? about 2 million by my estimation.

    How many are jobless? About 80,000 based on last known figure.

    I was given the impression that singapore is so attractive to MNCs that all prefer singapore as place to setup plants, companies, branches, HQs over the other SE.Asian countries.

    Malaysia has 10 times more people than singapore requiring work. Why are they able to provide millions and millions more jobs than singapore?
    If Malaysia Boleh, Singapore?
    USA also has 100 times more people requiring work. They are able to provide 10's of millions more jobs. Why not singapore?

    Singapore population needs to double at least in the next few decades. If for the last 5 years, unemployment hover at around just under 100k, would there be job for my child? Would there be job left to offer me till I am 65?

    Singapore leaders are all excellent. But I cannot understand why unemployment exist at such a comparatively high number when we are only asking for 80k jobs. Why can this not be met? If so, how to solve it? I have been waiting for an answer for 5 years.

    Assuming singapore will double its population. How is it going to generate 2 million more jobs when 80k jobs are missing for 5 straight years?
    I am amazed at the confidence of our leaders.

    Given all the geographical, political, social, educational advantage that singapore has, why are we still not able to generate sufficient jobs employable of singaporeans? Was there a 10 year plan to forecast job demands and prepare the youths for it? If yes, did it work? If it did, what happen to last 5 years? There can be a plan for everything. But what matters is whether it is effective. I can have a 1 million dollar security system that does not work effectively. And then what?

    From now on, we should have an early warning system to prevent what happened the last 5 years. If only we forecasted the problems and taken proactive and preventative actions? Would that have helped?

    As more jobs are created, more FTs come in, unrestricted. Then what?
    The more important issue is the 80k jobless, if we consider them important at all. Are we so sure our FT policies and especially its handling
    is flawless? Can we provide FTs all the jobs they want while at the same time provide jobs for all singaporeans which are only a small number?

    If singapore, a tiny city, could not generate enough job for a small population, can we consider singapore as a better country than say Malaysia which in terms of job creation is 10 times better than singapore?

    A large country is harder to manage. Yet, they can provide more jobs. So what is missing?

    Can singaporeans depend on singapore to provide job opportunities or they have to adopt other countries to feed themselves?

    I myself has been jobless for the last 3 years. I have no choice now but to look abroad for greener pastures. I did not try before as I did not think I have the character suitable for it. But now, its either I stay broke here or I suffer a little and at least get to earn some money abroad.

    I feel like I am the bangla who works here. I can emphatise with them now. Before we look down on a thai, china, india construction worker, think how tough they are to be able to work abroad. They have the courage, the determination to earn a living abroad.

    toomanyjobs?

    In the construction industry, laborers are indeed cheaper than locals who have lots more to pay as living expenses. That is the difference. In terms of nett savings or income, I think not diff by much.

    In IT industry, FTs are not cheap anymore, probably since 3 years ago.
    To get an indian IT person is so easy that it takes merely a few days to grant permits. Given that the entire world is supplying IT labor to singapore, its created a problem for locals certified but fresh. Basically, there is no longer a need to hire fresh when price of fresh can be used to pay price of 2 years experienced IT FT from afar.

    So, what you say is over-generalising the state of employment in singapore.
