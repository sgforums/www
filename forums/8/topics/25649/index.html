The fishball noodle seller

  • Illegal Operation
    1 Sep 02, 03:06
    Just had a couple of drinks with a friend at a coffee shop nearby. When we left, the fish ball noodle store was still open. We asked when it will close, according to the lady, presumably from China (from her accent), it opens 24 hours a day, 2 shift, 4 workers.

    This lady and her partner, we had been counting between beers, sells around 5 bowls of noodles an hour, that's a average of 2.50 a bowl. Lets say between 11 and 7 am, she sells 8x10 bowls an hour, that's around a gross profit of 200 dollars. After raw materials, rent, profits? Yes. Handsome profits? probably not.

    Then I wondered how many Singaporeans would be willing to do be in her shoes, and do her job. Little I suppose... it suddenly occurs to me that foreign workers, those who have presumably stolen work from the heartlanders...deserves their keep, and for sheer hardwork. Would Singaporeans (2 in fact) work the hours between 11pm to 7am merely to sell that couple bowls of noodles? I doubt. When bus loads of Malaysian workers arrives from Johor, were they 'stealing' jobs from Singaporeans, or were there merely filling the gaps of employments where local Singaporeans refuses to take?
