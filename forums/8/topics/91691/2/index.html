    from CNA

    SINGAPORE: Two women died in separate accidents at MRT stations in Bukit Batok and Redhill on Tuesday.

    In the first accident, a Chinese woman in her 50s died after she was hit by a train at the Bukit Batok MRT station.

    She has been identified as Madam Loh Suan Choo.

    SMRT said the incident happened at about 1.50pm.

    It is not clear how she ended up on the train tracks.

    Train services between Jurong East and Choa Chu Kang stations were disrupted, affecting some 2,500 passengers.

    Services resumed at about 2.40pm and SMRT deployed some 19 buses to ferry the affected passengers.

    At Redhill MRT station later on Tuesday, a woman in her 40s died later from injuries sustained on the rail tracks.

    The SCDF told NewsRadio that the victim was found underneath a train carriage.

    She was unconscious when pulled out. She suffered head injuries and was sent to the Singapore General Hospital where she died.

    It is also not clear how she ended up on the tracks.

    Train service between Redhill and Tiong Bahru was disrupted for about an hour, affecting some 4,600 commuters.

    Police have ruled out foul play in both cases.

    Tuesday's incidents happened just slightly more than two weeks after another MRT train accident.

    On 14 August, 47-year-old Richard Chua fell onto the MRT tracks at the Bukit Batok station and was run over by a north-bound train but miraculously survived.

    Chua was found trapped between the train under-carriage and rail tracks and was semi-conscious.

    Civil defence and police officers took 15 minutes to extricate Chua, who suffered injuries to his leg as well as cuts to his back and head. - CNA
