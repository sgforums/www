    I am for the death sentence for drug traffickers.

    Firstly, in nguyen's case, it is impossible to get the courts to pardon him at this point in time. He committed a crime in Singapore and he must be punished according to the law of Singapore. That he is an Australian or even if the Queen intercedes on his behalf makes no difference. If foreign nations were to see Singapore make an exception for nguyen, it could be potentially disastrous for Singapore. Singapore could be seen as a weak nation ready to buckle under pressure. In the global political arena, foreign nations would use this weakness to push Singapore around to obtain benefit for themselves.

    I understand the Australians' reaction towards Nguyen. It is natural for one to stand up for one's own countrymen, especially since Australia does not have the death sentence. However, they too have to understand that since Nguyen broke a law in Singapore, he cannot be pardoned because he is one of them.

    Originally posted by HENG@:
    What IF U WERE HIM?
    HENG@ is right to point out that we have to view the matter from the perspective of the offender. However, there is also the perspective of society.

    From the perspective of the offender:

      To Nguyen, death as a punishment is probably too harsh. But then again no one will accept death as a punishment. He might claim to feel remorse. But in reality, he probably just regrets trying to smuggle drugs into Singapore.

      Nguyen might wonder why is he being punished for trying to help his brother and for being 'forced' into this by the drug bosses. But what of the lives he might ruin? He could, with his 400g of drugs, ruin the life of yet another (and maybe more). The drugs, if not found, would ruin the life of more youths like him. They might even enter a life of crime to support their expensive addiction.


    From the perspective of the Society:

      Drug peddlers should be kept as far away as possible. Every one of them has the potential to contribute to the deaths of many more. I think that this is undisputable.

      If the death of a few traffickers, who have knowingly committed an act that will potentially destroy members of society, can send a message to others not to try, the death sentence is justified. Since the traffickers have been warned, and since they continue to traffic drugs, they have chosen death themselves.

      It is not fair to allow a potentially destructive element of soceity back where he can wreak more havoc just because he does not want to adhere to the rules set by the soceity.


    The death penalty is a good deterent against drug trafficking. It may not be the case in other nations, but it is in Singapore. There are statistics to prove it.

    A drug trafficker who is released could very well find himself in the same circumstances that forced him to traffic drugs in the first place. He might be an addict once and might become an addict again. he could still have the same financial difficulties that forced him to turn to a life of crime. Being known to the drug lords means that they could still find him and force him into being a drug mule again.

    I believe we should not put our society at risk for a person who has violated our laws in full knowledge of the consequences of being caught and the destruction he could bring to society.
