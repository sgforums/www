    Because the judge was not at the crime scene, there are certain cases where it will be difficult for him to say for sure who is the mastermind and who is the runner. It will be even more difficult for him to judge that are the actual motivation behind the crimes. Looking into motivations is not as simple as it may seem to you and me. In fact, judging anything that has to do with human emotions is never easy. A judge, therefore, has to use hard evidence as a guage for every case he handles.

    I am not saying that the legal system in Singapore does not factor in human considerations. In other crimes, human motivations do play a role in deciding the severity of the punishment to be awarded to the criminal. Criminals have the option of using examplary behaviour in the past, the fact that they are first timers etc to appeal for a lesser sentence.

    However, drug trafficking is a crime that once done, warrants the death penalty regardless of the original motivation. The trafficker may be doing it to help one sibling, but the result of his actions is the destruction of the lives of many others.
