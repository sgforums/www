    Mother begs Queen to help save her son

    By Mark Russell
    October 30, 2005

    THE mother of condemned Melbourne man Nguyen Tuong Van has written a letter to the Queen in a desperate last-ditch bid to save her son from being executed in Singapore.

    The two-page letter, written in Vietnamese and addressed simply to Queen Elizabeth, was sent to Nguyen's Melbourne legal team with instructions to forward it on to Buckingham Palace.

    The Sunday Age believes Nguyen's mother, Kim, pleads with the Queen in the letter to personally intervene in the case and ask the Singapore Government to spare her son's life.

    The letter was translated yesterday but Mrs Nguyen's lawyers did not want to release it publicly until they had had time to discuss it thoroughly with her.

    An emotional Mrs Nguyen is not coping well with the situation.

    "She's found it all incredibly hard," a family friend told The Sunday Age.

    "She has suffered a lot. .. She suffers intensely.

    "She's an intensely loving mother. She looks like her heart is breaking."

    Nguyen's lawyers believe the execution, if it goes ahead, will not take place for several weeks. Executions are usually carried out at 6am on Fridays.

    In other developments:

    Melbourne lawyer Lex Lasry, QC, confirmed that he would meet the Singapore high commissioner, Joseph Koh, in Canberra at 3.30pm tomorrow to plead Nguyen's case. Mr Koh said last week Singapore would not back down on plans to execute Nguyen. Mr Lasry also hopes to meet Prime Minister John Howard and Foreign Affairs Minister Alexander Downer to discuss the case.

    Students from secondary schools, community groups and religious leaders will light 3000 candles today for Nguyen and his family in Brisbane.

    Nguyen, 25, a sales executive from Glen Waverley, has been on death row since March last year when given a mandatory death sentence after being caught carrying 396.2g of heroin.

    Nguyen, who had no previous criminal convictions, had been acting as a drug mule to help pay off his twin brother Khoa's legal debts after being pressured by loan sharks.

    Khoa, a convicted heroin trafficker, is living in Western Australia and has not been to visit his brother since his arrest in Singapore.

    A prayer service will be held at St Patrick's Cathedral at 2.30pm next Sunday to pray that Nguyen's life be spared.
