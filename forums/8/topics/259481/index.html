    paintball is like running around with water-like pipes shooting mini balloons at each other. the range of a paintball marker(notice the word "gun" is rarely used in paintball jargons, as you well see later) is very much limited and thus restricts the size of the field playable.

    the gears painballers don are generally colorful and tournamentish looking, which in the eyes of mil-sim fans such as airsofters, an attempt to break away from the misconception of war-loving violent atmosphere(very untrue) which mil-sim players tend to potray to the unfortunate opinion of the general public . to some extend, some newbie paintballers think the same as the former.

    believe it or not, paintball first started out as a tool for marking trees in plantation(can't remember as to why but you can read that up if you're interested). it later became popular and thus heavily marketed as an "extreme sport", which explains the flashy gears and markers so as to mould it into a more accepting sport.

    it's a sad thing airsoft is still illegal in singapore(apart from some clubs which are springing up but still, it's only IPSC at best and you must store your guns in an armoury, which of course they will charge u, not the mention the already high cost of the gun itself). it's a hugely potential niche market and if it ever becomes an open market, paintball might very well face extinction albeit some hardcore fans will remain loyal to their beloved sport. this is what is happening in Hong Kong.
