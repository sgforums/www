    Which Cat high u talking abt? I did lower primary at the Bright Hill building ( where Ai Tong Primary is present day) between 1990-1992 while the campus at Bishan St 22 was under construction. I remember, rather vividly in fact, that there were two moulds erected outside the open field seperated from the soccer field by a fence and a row of trees where primary school kids gathered around every reccess to pick worms from (till today I still can't finger out why we did that?) Besides the worm picking, the other attraction was of course, the two moulds. Rumor has it that inside the two moulds were coffins left behind by the grave diggers when they cleared the land (Bishan was a graveyard before it became what it is today) To spice it all up, it is said that some scouts (it's always the scouts for some reason) saw light illuminating from the mould in the wee hours of the morning. There are many rhetorical versions of this, some claim that student saw 'flying coffins' there one night. Anyway, all this rumors and myths just brought more students to the area for their daily pilgrimage. I remember it got so bad that the teachers actually posted prefects round the area to stop students going round there during reccess.
