    IMO you definitely have a case against the fund management practices of your school and its cost cutting measures. It's wrong for the school to pass down essential learning costs to its staff and students, like printing of worksheets, while building extravagant decorative structures.

    Bad fund management is a possible risk because some schools are more autonomous and independent than others and the board is given more control. Policies are then decided by the school board and principal.

    I don't see much repurcussion if you spoke up about this because your immediate teachers are feeling the brunt of bad policies too.

    However there are other groups you can approach to represent your case. Your school student council, if they are daring enough. Or your alumni, if they are concerned enough. Or your school's parent support group. They should be well-aware of the additional costs they are paying for their child's education. Or you could just get a parent to bring up this issue to the school or to the papers.
