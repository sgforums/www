    Hey guys, I wanna know which school (Secondary level) besides mine are currently conserving utilities and saving money. I know there are plenty of schools which do that but I just want to compare it against the situation in my school.

    Just some background info, our school has started conserving paper since last year and students have to pay for their worksheets. This caused some teachers teaching sec 4 classes last year to think twice before printing any notes for their students, who are sitting for their O levels within a few months time.

    The beginning of this year, my form teacher told us that the prinicpal decides that paper is too cheap, they shall conserve INK instead.

    Of course, both students and teachers are rather irritated by this news. Teachers have been buying their own papers for printing of worksheets and study notes since last year, and now they school is monitoring the use of ink as well. Teachers have to login to their account before they are able to use the printer. This also means students have to pay for both the paper and the ink.

    However, the weird thing is that my school is always talking about reducing costs. Right now, there are 3 ponds in my school compound, supposedly for decoration purposes, one of them for religious purposes. The 4th one is being built right now. Isn't this a waste of money?

    Second evidence is the large number of rocks along the school's driveway and carpark. Guess what, the rocks have our school song's lyrics printed on it. And decorations of the carpark using huge colourful balls looked ridiculous.

    I feel that this is going too much. The school kept encouraging us to donate to the school but I find money being spent on useless objects. Its true we need a nice environment to study, but the school is beautiful enough without those rocks and the 4th pond (located outside chemistry labs, exploding fishes anyone? ).

    Many students in my school object to the excess spending of money on decorations as well. Anyone facing similar situations in school? Care to discuss?
