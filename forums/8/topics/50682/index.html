Retrenching and Hiring

  • Duke Law
    2 Jul 03, 10:05
    I notice that quite a few companies who have been retrenching are also discreetly hiring younger replacements with less experience as a cost cutting measure.

    I think that all companies running into financial difficulties should look into that as an alternative to retrenchment per se. I mean it's not a bad idea to hire younger and more vibrant staff to replace the older ones, who have reach a point of saturation in their work. No doubt they are experience but most of them can no longer put in the hours or have the stamina the younger ones can, due to their age and family commitments.

    It also help them put their career into perspective and make them more motivated to learn and pick up new skills again.
