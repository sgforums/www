    Don't worry about that part Sinicker. Caging a dog is actually good for the dog in certain occasions. For example, when you are frightened and introduced to a situation you may not like, you will generally want to feel comfort in the form of physical security. A dog's cage can act like that.

    It's called a crate, to be more politically correct. My dogs' crates are generally for them to relax in when people who are afraid of them visit me. When I punish my dogs, I never punish them while they are in the crate because that place is where I want them to recognize as a place of security, comfort and their own 'pad' if you want to call it. It is like a bedroom for them where I will not enter with anything but good or essential intentions.

    It's not unfair, it's actually humane when you think about it. Imagine if a dog greets a person over enthusiastically and you punish it by throwing your dog into his/her crate; that's being unfair. By using a crate correctly, you make your dog feel secure and safe in unfamiliar situations.

    Think about it, when animals sleep in the wild, where do they sleep? In the wide open space where predators can jump on them? Or a cozy and safe place to bed down for the night?
