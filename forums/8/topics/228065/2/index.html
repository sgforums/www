    Some Chinese dialects have more speakers than other European or Asian languages. Take Cantonese for example. There are around 70 million Cantonese speakers worldwide. That's more than the number of Polish speakers, or Turkish speakers, or Italian speakers! Even Hokkien, with around 45 million speakers, has more speakers than Thai, Indonesian or Malay globally... (Information extracted from http://www.davidpbrown.co.uk/help/top-100-languages-by-population.html)

    I was born in HK, so Cantonese is my mother tongue. I'm proud to know Cantonese because it's a fascinating language. (The word "ketchup" comes from the Cantonese word "ke chup".) However, it is not easy to master Cantonese, as it has 9 tones compared to Mandarin's 4 tones, and it has some words and expressions that are not found in other dialects. Nevertheless, it's a pleasant surprise to hear Cantonese when I go to the Chinatowns of other cities (e.g. London, Vancouver, San Francisco, etc). In fact, Cantonese is so common in Vancouver that people are calling the city "Hong-kouver"... Haha...
