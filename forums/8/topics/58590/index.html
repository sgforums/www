S'pore allows all night bars in designated zones

  • Hydro
    3 Oct 03, 22:01
    SINGAPORE - Singapore, trying to shake off its straight-laced image, will soon let bars and nightclubs in designated areas stay open round the clock, police said on Friday.

    Licensed nightspots in three nonresidential areas will be allowed to stay open all night starting Monday, police said in a statement.

    Most nightspots in this tightly regulated country currently close at 3 am.

    'As we strive to remake Singapore into a more vibrant city, police and (other officials) remain open to considering extended licensing hours of entertainment for other areas,' the police statement said.

    The three newly designated areas are the tourist island of Sentosa, Marina Centre in Singapore's fledgling theatre district and the waterfront bars at Collyer Quay.

    The zones were chosen because of their distance from residential areas, the statement said.

    Singapore is trying hard to shake off its dull image and market itself as a media and arts centre, despite its long history of strict law enforcement and tight censorship.

    Officials in recent years have taken small steps to spice up the nightlife, such as lifting restrictions on where people can dance and allowing some explicit language in plays.
