    Originally posted by snop_coolz:
    hummmm...how to check the semester fee one??
    You want to enrol there, don't know how to check. Very cute.

    Webby for you: http://www.rp.edu.sg/admissions/admissions_info/jaefaqs/fees.asp

    It is the same throughout all polytechnics.

    Now for tuition fees.

    http://www.np.edu.sg/ss/financial_assist.htm

    Local students can apply for these few financial schemes:

    Tuition Grant - this is provided by the government. You just have to fill in the form provided in the enrollment package. Fill in that and submit. No repayment needed.

    CPF Education Scheme - use your family/relatives' CPF to pay school fees. Deducted automatically. Pay back one year after you graduate from polytechnic or one year after you leave the polytechnic. Again, there is a form in the enrollment, just fill in that and submit. Payback interest is charged at the current CPF rate, and at least $100 per month for a maximum of 12 years. To be paid in cash. If you don't, you get warnings, then court letter.

    Tuition Fee Loan - only if you are a needy student. Covers 75% of your tuition fees. The rest pay yourself. Payback depends on which bank you apply to. Interest depends.

    Government Loan - only if you have applied for Tuition Fee Loan. It will cover the rest of the 25%, if not lesser. If you are really needy, an extra $1000 will be given out as well.

    Mendaki - for Malay local students. Up to 70% fees paid or 100% fees paid, depending on how needy you are.

    Edusave - if you have enough money

    Any other schemes, call up your school. These are the schemes available to local students.
