    Originally posted by wonderamazement:

    but the impact must have been really massive for the girl to be flung out... was she?


    Assuming an impact at 60km/h, lets do the math.

    60km/h is 16.67 m/s

    assuming deceleration is over 1 second, the deceleration = (16.67-0)/1

    =16.67 m/s^2

    We all know earth's gravitational pull is about 9.81m/s^2

    so a deceleration experienced in a crash at 60km/h would be 16.67/9.81 = approx 1.7 times more than earth's gravity. Does it hurt when you fall down? If you have a pane of glass, and you fell onto it from a meter above, what are the odds that you WILL break and pass thru the glass at 1 standard gravity? Pretty high right? Imagine falling onto the glass at almost TWO times standard gravity... and thats only at a low speed of 60km/h!

    So no, impact doesn't have to be massive for people to be flung out of cars. 50km/h impacts are actually deadlier than you think, it's just that cars have gotten so safe nowadays and seat belt laws are so strict that people survive 50km/h crashes thinking "thats a small impact". The truth is, no, its not.
