    She is the latest victim of a series of recent horrific gang rapes

    KUALA LUMPUR - A series of horrific gang rapes, including one in which the victim was eight months pregnant, has caused concern in Malaysia, according to media reports.

    In the latest attack, the 25-year-old pregnant woman was waiting for her husband to pick her up from work in northern Penang state early on Wednesday when four men, believed to be Bangladeshis, dragged her to a deserted spot and raped her.

    The traumatised woman was found by her husband in a semi-conscious state and has been hospitalised, The Star newspaper reported yesterday. Penang state police have detained two foreigners over the incident.

    In south-west Malacca state, a 14-year-old girl was allegedly gang-raped by her boyfriend and seven of his friends on March 1.

    The girl was repeatedly raped for more than 10 hours before she was finally sent home.

    Her ordeal began when she accepted her boyfriend's offer for an outing on his motorcycle along the beach.

    But the 18-year-old boyfriend, a factory worker whom she had befriended for less than a month, took her instead to an isolated spot where she was gang-raped.

    In Kuala Lumpur, an 18-year-old girl who befriended several youths at a shopping mall and a nightclub was taken on a terror ride and gang-raped at a cemetery several days ago.

    She was also robbed of cash and valuables.

    The girl later struggled to a main road and was taken to a police station by a passer-by.

    The teenager was the third to have been gang-raped in the Malaysian capital in recent weeks, said the newspaper.

    The other cases involved two 14-year-olds, one who was raped in a carpark and the other under a tree in a secluded spot in the city centre.

    Kuala Lumpur police chief Dell Akhbar Khan said the three cases were a rare occurrence and not the work of the same group.

    'We do not have a gang of serial rapists running loose in the city,' he was quoted as saying, adding that police would step up patrols in isolated spots. --AFP
