    There are several known causes for drooling during sleep. Some medications may increase saliva production, faster than a person can swallow while sleeping. So when the excess saliva can't go in, it has to come out.

    Sleep posture could also be another cause. When lying on the back, saliva accumulates at the back of the throat and often causes a swallowing reflex that prevents drooling. When lying on the side, however, saliva can pool in the cheek and overflow on to the pillow, or the partner's face or whatever the person's head is resting on.

    Also if a person breathes through the mouth instead of the nose while sleeping, there is a higher tendency to drool and occasionally foam (ie. blow bubbles). That usually happens to people with colds or sinus problems.
