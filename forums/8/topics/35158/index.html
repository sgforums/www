    One fan's bad experience

    WHEN 15-year-old Claire Leong went home after an autograph session by Avril Lavigne (right), she was close to tears.

    The Canadian teen pop sensation, who received a stunning five Grammy nominations Line is overdrawn this year, including one for Best New Artiste, held her first-ever autograph signing session at The Heeren last month.

    Claire claimed that after waiting for nine hours, all she got was a shove and a scribble.

    She said: 'The organisers didn't say we have to bring along an album and I was told only when it got to my turn. A woman pushed me and yelled 'Give others a chance' even though it was my first time in the queue!

    'Luckily, my friend had extra CDs, so I managed to meet Avril.'

    But that was when things got worse.

    'I barely had a minute with her, she wouldn't even look up at me. She took the ring I bought for her and threw it into a black garbage bag.

    'And all she wrote was an 'A' on the cover - that was her autograph! I was devastated.'

    Her mother, Ms Angelia Koh, 51, a housewife, said she was 'so angry' when her daughter returned home.

    Ms Kelly Chew, managing director of BMG Singapore, Lavigne's record company here, explained: 'We had only an hour and Avril signed nearly 800 autographs in that time.'

    There were, by her estimates, about 3,000 people crowding around to catch a glimpse of Lavigne.

    Ms Chew added that it was stated in the ads that you would need a CD to get an autograph.

    She said Lavigne, 18, was 'frightened' by the huge turnout.

    As for throwing the ring into a black garbage bag, Ms Chew said: 'We needed to store all the gifts in a plastic bag, because how else are we going to lug all the presents back?'

    As for the pushing, she said about 20 security guards and 10 BMG staff members were on hand to maintain order. But the crowd ignored their repeated requests for orderly behaviour.
