    In Singapore, one of the problems lies in the mindset of "I want to learn something that will bring about economic gains". English is the universal medium and working language in most countries and is therfore deemed to be more impt. How many companies require you to be proficient in Chinese vs how many requires you to have a good command in English? The answer is clear.
    Another reason is that some parents themselves do not have a good grasp of the Chinese language so how to expect the next generation to be interested when the parents themselves do not use it?

Chinese is widely used now but its status is eroding with emphasis being on English. Furthermore the teaching language is English for most subjects. Maybe when China emerges as an economic superpower, it will spark off greater interest in Chinese.
