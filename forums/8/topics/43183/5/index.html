    Common measures taken by companies to minimise the impact of Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome (SARS) on business operations

    These are some of the common measures taken by companies to prevent the spread of SARS and to minimize disruption to business operations. The actual details of implementation may differ from company to company due to the nature of their operations. Please click on the names of the companies below to find out more about their guidelines on tackling SARS at the workplace.

    Precautionary Measures At Workplace

    Educate employee on the symptoms of SARS.
    Encourage employee to practice good personal hygiene.
    Company to arrange for more frequent cleaning of common areas.
    Encourage employee to measure his temperature twice a day (ie before going to work and after meal break).
    Employee who has a fever above 37.5 C or other symptoms of SARS should not report for work. He should see a doctor. Employee to keep company informed of outcome of medical consultation.
    Supervisors to look out for employees showing signs of illness.
    Employee who develops SARS symptoms while at work

    Employee who develops SARS symptoms while at work should wear a mask and inform his supervisor and/or colleague immediately. He should be isolated from the rest of the workers and arrangement should be made to transport him to Tan Tock Seng Hospital (TTSH) for further assessment. Employee to keep company informed of outcome medical consultation.
    A list of the employee's contacts at work should also be compiled to enable contact tracing by Ministry of Health (MOH), if necessary.
    If necessary, company to disinfect area of work and to brief employees on situation.
    Employee issued with Home Quarantine Order (HQO) by MOH

    Employee who has been served with a HQO shall keep company informed and be granted paid hospitalization leave.
    Company to monitor colleagues of suspected SARS employee for symptoms of SARS.

    Visitors to the Company

    Visitors and other non-employees (eg suppliers, contract workers etc) in the company are required to sign a health declaration and screened for signs of fever.
    Overseas Travel

    Overseas travel (business or personal) to SARS affected areas should be minimized and alternative means of communication to be explored (eg. telephone, e-mail, tele-conferencing etc).
    Upon return from SARS affected countries while on business travel, employee may continue to work from home for 10 days if possible. If not, unrecorded leave will be granted unrecorded leave for the purpose of 10 days' self-quarantine.
    Employee who returns from travel to SARS affected countries on a personal basis shall be required to consume their annual leave for the purpose of 10 days' self-quarantine.
    Business Continuity Plan

    Where possible, company splits staff of a department or section into 2 or more teams to work separately on different days/shifts or different locations.
    Company may introduce flexible work arrangement like work from home.
