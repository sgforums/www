    Long time back i was reading about this movie on the net...it was released in 1997 right?...Now i recall that

    Cube is a 1997 Canadian sci-fi movie directed by Vincenzo Natali. Seven people, who are complete strangers to each other, awaken inside a maze of cubes, having no memory of how they got there. They are forced to work together to escape and, along the way, have various (sometimes frightening) personality clashes.

    Despite its low budget, the film achieved moderate commercial success and has acquired something of a cult status as a niche science-fiction title. Much of the film's appeal lies in its surreal, almost Kafkaesque setting - no extensive attempt is made to explain what the cube is or why it is created, or why the "inmates" were specifically selected to be imprisoned inside. Although the world "outside" is referred to, it is presented in an extremely abstract fashion as either a dark void or a bright white light.

    The 2002 sequel Cube 2: Hypercube featured a more conventional plot in which the cube was the product of a conspiracy by the military-industrial complex, which in itself alienated fans of the first film. Although Hypercube had a bigger budget, and therefore better presentation in some aspects, it featured a completely different cast and crew that were evidently less concerned with creating a plausible cube universe and instead were apt to regard the concept as something of a novelty. Examples of this were the ridiculously glib misunderstandings of quantum mechanics to create rooms where time was greatly accelerated or even reversed. As a consequence much of the franchise's cult appeal was tarnished.
