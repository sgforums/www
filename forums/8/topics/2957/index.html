    IMHO, JC is the fastest and easier route to a uni education ( 2yrs compared to 3 yrs for a poly education). However "A" level grads will have a disadvantage compared to their poly peers if they are ineligible for uni because "A" level certs count for nothing in the job market as they are not trained in any technical field. In a sense, poly students have something to fall back on even if they can't enter uni. Actually, going to a poly first may be a better option even though it takes 3 years because poly grads are exempted for a number of modules offered in certain uni faculties such as computer science and as a result they can even graduate within 2.5 years. Hence they only lag behind their counterparts by 0.5 years in entering the job market. However based on my knowledge 5 years ago, one has to be the top 10% of ur poly cohort in order to be eligible for uni. Quite tough though.
