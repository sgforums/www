    Looks like interest in this topic has dried off. I shall just post 1 of the incident relayed by a friend to close off.

    My friend and a colleague of his went back to campus during a holiday because they had some work to finish. After some discussion and splitting of work load, he went back to his office and left his colleague in another room to work.

    As soon as he stepped in, he heard the phone ringing. So he picked it up, and its his colleague. She sounded flustered.

    "There are banging noises coming from the ceiling! ... and footsteps outside but I can see no one!"

    She was very scared and wanted my friend to quickly come back to her room. So my friend set the phone down and quickly made his way back. He couldn't hear or see anything as he approached the room. When he stepped inside, there were no disturbances as well...

    So he proceeded to calm her down, and tell her it's just in her imagination, etc. Whatever it was has stopped anyway. After some time, she calmed down and my friend felt safe enough to go back to his office.

    Now, it so happens that this room the girl was in, has a dead tree outside the window. There were a row of trees but only the one closest to this room was dead.

    So my friend went back to his office. After a while, his colleague dashed into his office. She was frightened and shocked. My friend calmed her again, and she told him the noises got worse and louder after he left. She was sure it's not just her imagination.

    My friend later revealed that they soon got used to such events. And they only seem to happen to someone when they are alone...

    This is incident was experienced by a lab tech. His lab is in one end of a U-shaped building. So one evening, he was going off duty to meet his colleagues for dinner. He closed the lab and locked the door as usual and made his way to the other end of the building to meet his colleagues.

    By the time he reached the other side of the U, he looked back at the lab and he saw the lights were on! So he took a closer look and he saw, to his surprise, there were students in the lab using the facilities. This made him mad, so he huffed back to the lab and burst in.

    He scolded the students "Can't you see the lab is closed? How did you unlock the door?"

    The students were puzzled.

    "But the lab was not locked, we thought it was open so we came in to use."

    Apparently the unlocking of doors became a very serious problem at one time. Because security officers would carry out checks at night and find that all the doors were not locked. They even went so far as to issue out a warning notice to the faculties, telling them about negligent behaviour, protecting campus property, etc. etc.

    My friends faculty replied that they would claim responsibility if a few doors were not locked. But if all the doors were unlocked?? The case was dropped after that.
