    Example: Jiang Shi (Chinese Vampire)
    It's just a story to scare grave robbers. Basically, in Ancient China when people died, sometimes their family members want to bury their bodies in their birthplace (Jia Xiang). So they employ some people who actually carry the bodies of these corpses from a place to another. One of these groups actually managed to make a technique to string the corpses together and drag them... without using much strength... and rumours flew...

    Vampire:
    The medieval vampire was actually a myth created by europeans. There were many cases of rapes in those days and in order to prevent young noblewomen from getting into trouble, a story was weaved. "The vampire is a handsome blood sucking monster who hunts women at night." Well, later, somehow they start to add female vampires to the stories.

    Werewolf:
    The earlier werewolves were just lunatics, (and lunatics don't shave.. dont cut their hair and nails either) and they attack people by biting and clawing. Later, the common people believed these people were possessed... and start killing them in their sleep... (Ahem.. cruelty to nuts)

    Monsters:
    Many monsters and beasts were actually stories made to frighten children to keep the children at home when night falls as parents would not be able to find their children and protect them from danger.
