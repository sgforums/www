    The grass is always greener on the other side. Especially when you have gone to study in another country while hearing the horror stories of how tough working life is in Singapore. Long hours, stressful environments etc.... I'm sure those conditions can be found anywhere, but I'm not sure. What I'm trying to say is that somehow the working environment is less threatening compared in Singapore. Just a guess. I've never worked seriously before.

    The cost of living is high in Singapore. Lots of things that people desire, like a house or car, are so expensive. No, of course I'm not saying that they are what you have to use to show your achievements, but they play a large part in assuring people that they have earned something.

    Singapore is small. I hear friends lamenting about having few places to go. Compared to what they think they can do in a larger country, Singapore will appear boring and lifeless after some time.

    There seems to be a lack of oppotunity to argue with the authorities, even when you have the right arguments. Somehow people get swept up in the various policy changes and have to take it sitting down. Good luck to those who try to voice dissent, even when the decisions are idiotic. Again, I'll admit that this is my own impression.

    As for myself, my current wish is to sustain a comfortable (though not cushy) existence without having to slog day and night with little time to do the things that I would like to do. Or, to put it bluntly, I want a life outside of my career. To me, at the moment anyway, that seems hard to achieve in Singapore.
