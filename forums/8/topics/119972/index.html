    Right now, there are very little quality theme parks in Singapore("escape" is ok, except that the rides there are carnival rides, and the park doesn't really have a theme). With reference to 27/2/05 Sunday Times's article, "theme park 101"(found in the "think" section), i agree that Singapore lacks a park that takes us to vistas that are not regularly seen in everyday Singapore life. As a themepark fanatic, it would be great if themeparks giants like universal studios or Disney could have an south east asian branch here. Marina East, piece of land at the bay area has more than enough space to house a Disneyland style themepark, hotels and parking( I have calculated). Disney have in fact considered Singapore to be a viable location to build it's themeparks.
    The problem now is whether Singaporeans are willing to spend at least $50(standard themepark admission price for universal and Disney parks)each a day for each visit. I am willing.
    So, what do you think of this proposal?
