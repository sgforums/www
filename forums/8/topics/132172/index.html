    Most popular email programs like Microsoft Outlook, Outlook Express and Lotus Notes come with the read/return receipt function. The function could be activated so that the emails would be sent out with the tracking request tag.

    However, the read/return receipt function is not entirely foolproof:
    1. Recipients of the email could choose not to respond to the read/return receipt request, and no such receipt would be sent back to sender. This option has become increasingly popular especially for unsolicited emails (eg. "spam" mail) because recipients do not want the sender to know the email account is active by sending back the read/return receipt.

    2. Different email programs use different request formats. An email sent from Outlook that is read with Notes may not necessarily generate the read/return receipt back to the sender.

    3. The read/return receipt is only sent back after the email has been opened. The recipient could have opened and deleted the email without actually reading it. The receipt may also not be generated if the email was only read through the preview pane and not actually opened.
