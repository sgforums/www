    BANGKOK - A criminal court yesterday sentenced a former Buddhist abbot
    to 160 years in prison for raping underage girls, but he will have to
    serve only 50 years under Thai legal limits.

    Chamlong Polseu, 65, who was once a popular spiritual leader to many
    politicians, was convicted of raping and molesting more than 10
    hill-tribe girls under the age of 15, a court official said on condition
    of anonymity.

    The monk's conviction is the latest embarrassment for Thailand's
    Buddhist clergy, plagued by scandals ranging from sex and alcohol abuse
    to murder.

    Chamlong, also known by his clerical name Pawana Phuttho, committed his
    crimes in the early 1990s when he was the abbot of Wat Sam Pran temple
    in Nakhon Pathom province, 50km west of Bangkok.

    The meditation guru was arrested in 1995 after hill-tribe girls housed
    in a dormitory he had established in the temple came forward with the
    accusations. He was defrocked soon after.

    Six of his followers, all women, were also jailed for three to 10 years
    for conspiring with Chamlong, according to the website of Thai newspaper
    Krungthep Turakij.

    Meanwhile, the head monk of the Samiennaree temple agreed to remove
    corporate sponsorship logos from his famous Bangkok temple yesterday
    after the brash advertisements caused an uproar in the deeply religious
    kingdom.

    Monk Prakru Uthaithamarat said the advertisements were merely a sign of
    gratitude after 1 million baht (S$42,000) of corporate money was pumped
    into a new pavilion for the temple. \-- AP, AFP
