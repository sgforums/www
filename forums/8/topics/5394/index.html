    Those who fervently support Singlish are idiots too.

    Pure Singlish, in effect, should befine. Afterall, it's just a mixture of english and thevariouslocal languages, all packaged to give it a uniquely Singaporean identity.

    What's really bad, and is perhaps the core of the issue, is how the words are pronounced, and GRAMMAR. Singlish is often accompanied by poor pronunciation and grammar, and as such, sounds really bad. Hence, it comes across as being unpolished and hard to swallow.

    Throw in your "lahs" and "siahs" and "lors". That's fine. But make sure your grammar is correct or Singlish becomes really hard to comprehend and an insult to the english language it's based upon.

    It's not about who we're trying to copy, but it's about presentation and respect for a language we base Singlish upon.

    While accents vary and perhaps pronunciation too, get the grammar in order. Leaving out an "s" when u want to speak in plural or adding one when you want to speak in singular is ridiculous. Sounds ridiculous too.

    As unfortunate as it may be, Singlish is often synonymous with bad english.

    With the older generation growing old and the younger generation taking over, the government aims to replace dialects with the 4 standard languages, probably with English as the de facto leader. Now while it's excusable for the older generation to make mistakes in speaking english which is in effect a 2nd language to them, english is taught as a first language in schools now so to actually promote Singlish would be totally contrary to what the education system here champions, ie. good english.

    Once you master standard english, Singlish adds life and flavour in the local context. But singlish should never, and can never, replace good standard english.
