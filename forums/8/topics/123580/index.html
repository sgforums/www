    SINGAPORE PRESS: Govt May Be In Talks With Casino Bidders
    SINGAPORE (Dow Jones)--The Singapore government may be unofficially contacting some bidders for the casino resort to inform them that they may be invited for the next round of talks should the casino proposal be approved, the Business Times reports.

    The newspaper quoted unnamed sources as saying some companies have already met the Singapore Tourism Board's request for concept requirements.

    The companies include CapitaLand Ltd. (C31.SG) in partnership with MGM Mirage (MGG), Hotel Properties Ltd. (H15.SG), Kerzner International (KZL), Eighth Wonder, Harrah's Entertainment (HET) with Keppel Land (K17.SG), and Australia's Tabcorp.

    STB has already scheduled meetings with these parties for the purpose of "further discussions."

    The paper says everything hinges on whether the government gives the go-ahead for a casino to be announced on April 18.

    "STB has called up several of the companies that have submitted their concepts, so this means it is as clear as anything that they have been shortlisted,' the newspaper quoted one one source as saying.

    The meetings with these players are also aimed at clarifying issues connected with their proposals.

    The Ministry of Trade and Industry said yesterday the government is currently reviewing the concept proposals received for the casino.

    The paper also said Wynn and Las Vegas Sands Corp. (LVS) are strong contenders for the project.
