    SINGAPORE : Singaporeans have been reminded that "it's not unreasonable" for commuters to bear part of the cost of stepped-up security at train stations and bus interchanges, if necessary.

    After all, Minister of State for Transport Balaji Sadasivan said, security is a "shared responsibility".

    Deploying uniformed guards at MRT stations and bus interchanges costs less than 1 cent per journey.

    And train and bus operators know it is in their commercial interests.

    Dr Balaji said transport operators had so far absorbed the cost, and there will be no fare hike this year.

    But since the precautions are ultimately for the safety of commuters, he said it was not unreasonable for commuters to also share the cost - if this becomes necessary.

    Dr Wang Kai Yuen, MP for Bukit Timah GRC, said: "What is the difference between providing security in the airport versus providing security in the train station? We have not heard requests from commuters or users of the airport to bear the security cost?"

    Dr Balaji said: "Users still pay for the security at the airport. Passengers pay a fee of $21 for using the airport of which $6 is the passenger security charge. So if you use the principle of shared costs, then using the principle from the airport, then you can see that for transport on the rail and roads, the operator and government and the commuter may have to pay for it."

    But Dr Balaji assured Singaporeans that any extra security precautions will be in line with the threat level and not excessive, and any fare hike will be closely scrutinised by the Public Transport Council before it is approved. - CNA
