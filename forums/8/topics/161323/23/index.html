    Firstly, on your example on sea cucumber, evolution is not about changes in the characteristic of a single organism. Sea cucumber CANNOT JUST evolved a defense mechanism that allowed it to turn itself inside out for predators to eat some of its insides. Evolution is about the accumulated changes of HERITABLE traits found in the population. The traits which you have described is a naturally occuring trait that is found in small numbers in the entire population. Possible causes of this trait is due to genetic variation within the population. It can be due to random mutation, independent assortment during meiosis one, or meiosis two, random fertilisation etc.

    The trait become common in sea cucumber due to directional selection. Predation acts as a selection pressure and those sea cucumbers with the ability you have mentioned are at a selective advantage. They are more likely to survive and pass on their genetic traits to the next generation. This leads to increase in allele frequency of the ability you mention.
