    Well, in response to rckong. It is clear from the gospel of John that God's face remains hidden from the eyes of men. While Islam (my faith), does not share certain points with Christianity, Muslims believe too that God's face is hidden. In the words of Karen Armstrong (a Catholic nun who left the ministry and became a religious commentator on the three faiths of Judaism, Christianity and Islam): "God is simply too awesome an experience!"

    This can be easily discerned from the stories of the Prophets in the Biblical Old Testament. In one scene, God told the prophet Isaiah to hide behind a rock because he wanted to pass a vision to him. Isaiah petulently asked why he had to do that, and God replied, "You will go mad if your eyes even saw a small part of me"

    In the book of Deutoronomy, when God appears to Moses on Mount Sinai to pass down the Ten Commandements, Moses experiences God as a "blinding light"...in some apocryphal (meaning works that are not considered official or "inspired") writings, Moses was said to have been blind when he descended from Mount Sinai.

    Anyway, about the idea of Jesus being a temporary face for God. Think about this, the Hindus share an almost similar belief. Like the Christians, they too have a Trinity consisting of Bram'ha (the Creator), Vishnu (the Sustainer) and Shiva (the Destroyer).

    In Hinduism, Vishnu is said to have sent down an incarnation, or an avatar, to earth. This incarnation's name is Rama.

    In hindu temples, you'll find the faces of these entities reflected on the idols. Similarly, you'll find plenty of statues of Christ in Catholic churches too.

    The idea of a "temporary face" is somewhat moot, don't you think?
