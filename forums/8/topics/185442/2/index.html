    Originally posted by Lim Wei Ming:

    Partially true.

    You can mix PC100 and PC133 RAM together, but the motherboard will only use the slowest available.

    You can't mix PC133 and PC2100 RAM together. Firstly, PC133 RAM has lesser number of pins than PC2100 RAM. Secondly, they are totally 2 different standards, despite being RAMs.

    For some motherboards, you can't even mix DDR and DDR2 RAM together. To know this, go to your motherboard's website and find out.

    You can mix single and double sided RAM together, and again, the PC will automatically choose the slowest available.
