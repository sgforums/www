    Bladez87,

    Wings is lats (the triangle pieces below your armpits, which stretch to your waist). If you've seen Bruce Lee movies where he did his pre-fight stretching, you'll know what wings are. Wing training has to be done carefully, safest done with machines. Any movement which pulls in (opposite of chest press) will use wings. Chin-ups also use wings.

    Pects is neh-neh (chest). Heavy bench/chest presses for mass (different angles if you're going for competition, but not necessary for normal people), flyes for definition and 'carving/fine-tuning'.

    Professionals usually recommend 3-4 sets for building. 1st two sets, lighter weights, 12 reps. Last two sets, heavier weights, 8 reps. Correct resistance/weight is when you have difficulties the last two reps of each set.

    I feel that push-ups is not really useful for muscle building leh, but only for endurance. For neh-neh, the only way to grow is heavy bench presses.

    Um....178 cm, 60 kg. Seems a bit lean leh. You have to eat more lah. Just stuff yourself with proteins.
