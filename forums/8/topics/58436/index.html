    CASE is pleased to announce the launch of the Specific Absorption Rate (SAR) awareness programme on 1 October 2003. This programme aims to raise awareness about the SAR value of mobile phones, and hence give consumers the opportunity to make informed choices before making their purchases.
    CASE wants consumers to have full information to make informed choices. Consumers have the right to know that radiation is emitted and absorbed into our tissue when handphones are used. They must also be informed that such radiation exposure is controlled by threshold limit on handphones sold in Singapore. The range of radiation within the threshold limit for each type of handphones should also be made easily available. More importantly consumers need to be educated on the meaning of specific absorption rate, a measure of the amount of radiation absorbed. This launch is intended to tell consumers all the above and CASE is doing this in collaboration with the relevant parties.

    Specific Absorption Rate

    This Specific Absorption Rate (SAR) is the unit of measurement for the amount of radiofrequency or Â‘RFÂ’ energy absorbed by the body when using a mobile or cellular phone. A mobile phone converts sound into radio waves, which it transmits. It then receives radio signals, which it converts back into sound waves. Such conversion is done through communication with a base station.

    All telecommunication equipment in Singapore is subject to the Info-Communications Development Authority of Singapore (IDA)Â’s stringent regulations. Specific Absorption Rate values form part of the criteria for mobile phones. If the SAR value exceeds 2 W/kg, IDA will not approve the import/use of the mobile phone.
