    My parents have been divorced for more than 5 yrs already. I dun think that the divorce influences my growing up process in a negative way. Life was hard with me and my mum alone. It does not help that I'm an only child and my mum is not highly educated and holding 2 jobs to bring in more cash. We do not receive any alimony as well.
    However, today even though I still studying, both of us are able to cope pretty well. I need to work to get $$ and support myself and ease the burden off my mum but it taught me to treasure things that I have more cos I know the difficulty of attaining them. I may not have much material comforts and money is constantly an issue on the mind but the whole thing taught me to be more disciplined and independant. It helped me to mature a lot in my thinking especially where affairs on the heart is concerned.

    I never have any objections should my mum want to remarry. In fact I think it would be good for her to have someone to care for her if she finds him suitable. Of cos I care for her but the kind of care I can give is different from what a husband can give. I do not have a cynical view of marriage and in fact it makes me more determined to try my best to ensure my own marriage in future will work out fine. As for being scared of not being a good parent or husband in future, I think everyone wants to be a good husband/wife/father/mother. I'm worried that I may not do a good job but it is not the result of being in a divorced family. Rather I want to excel at whatever roles I undertake be it a husband, a employee or anything else.

    Jasonnex, I do not have many close friends as well but think quality is more impt than quantity. Battles may be tiring and discouraging when fought alone but I think success taste sweeter when you obtain it through yr own efforts and strengths.
