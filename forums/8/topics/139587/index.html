    I'm not sure if GPS receivers are practical in Singapore. On one of my recent visits, I brought along my ancient-but-trusty Magellan GPS 315 handheld GPS receiver and found that satellite reception was severely hampered by the plethora of tall buildings on the island. The GPS receivers installed in automobiles might benefit from larger and more sensitive antennae, so reception might not be an issue with those units.

    However, I am compelled to ask: why would anyone require a GPS to get around in Singapore?
