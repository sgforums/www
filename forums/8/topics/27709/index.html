Nicholas Tse Escapes Jail Time

    HONGKONG - Pop idol Nicholas Tse avoided prison yesterday and was instead sentenced to 240 hours of community service for trying to cover up a car crash - in a tumultuous court session delayed by a bomb scare

    Fans screamed with joy after Magistrate Allan Wyeth announced the sentence - two weeks after convicting him.

    The 22-year-old singer, who had spent the past two weeks in jail awaiting his punishment, appeared relieved on hearing the sentence, but kept silent.

    'I'm very sure the 14 days you've served serve as a loud and clear wake-up call to you,' the magistrate told Tse, a bad boy of Hongkong's entertainment world.

    The singer and movie actor had faced a maximum of two years behind bars and a fine of HK$100,000 (S$23,000).

    After the sentencing, Tse was driven quickly away in a car with his parents, movie star Patrick Tse Yin and veteran actress Deborah Li, followed by a police motorcycle escort.

    Mr Wyeth accepted defence arguments that Tse had played a largely passive role after he crashed his Ferrari on March 23 and agreed to let a chauffeur claim to have been behind the wheel.

    The fact that no one was hurt also was important, the magistrate said.

    A police constable, Lau Chi Wai, who agreed to let chauffeur Shing Kwok Ting take the blame for the crash, was sentenced to prison for six months.

    Shing pleaded guilty, served four months, and then testified against Tse and Lau.

    The early-morning court proceedings were disrupted by a bomb scare.

    As Tse was brought into the courtroom for the sentencing on charges of conspiracy to pervert the course of justice, a bomb threat was phoned in, prompting officials to whisk him away and order some people to leave the building.

    A court security officer also urged a throng of reporters to refrain from using their mobile phones for fear that the signals could trigger a bomb. Chaos ensued as his advice was ignored.

    Sentencing went ahead after about 40 minutes of security checks turned up nothing.
