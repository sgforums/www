    a. Calculate the position of the centre of mass of the seat and plank. [3m]

    b. If a victim of mass 75 kg is strapped onto the seat, calculate the additional force required to be applied on the counter-balance stone to balance the instrument. [2m]
    a) clockwise moment = anticlockwise moment
    1000(10) x 2.0 = (4.0 - x) x 1200(10)
    x = 2.33 m

    b) Let M be the mass to balance
    M x 2.0 = 75 x 4.0 + 1200 x 1.667
    M = 1150 kg

    Additional mass = 1000-1150 = 150 kg = 1500N of additional force

    ok remember..
    Force = mass x acceleration ( farther = mother)
    moment = force x perpendicular distance, in other words, force = moment /perpendicular distance
    gravitational force = 9.81 or 10( both constant) x mass

    as u could see, gravintational force is somehow like F=MA, just that the acceration is replaced by gravitational pull(9.81m/s^2 or 10m/s^2 depending on your syllabus)

    so there are only 2 formulas you need to remember. the third one can be derive by replacing 9.81 or 10.

    so.. when the question ask u about force topic, write these 2 formulas on the paper first (f=ma and moment = force* dist)

    when u see mass given of the object in the qn, note the mass
    if u see
    acclereation, or falling object(falling object is at 9.81m/s^2 or 10m/s^2 unless resistance of air), note it .

    so mass given, accleration given... u see the formula.. which one contains mass and acceleration? F=MA right?
