    There is a lengthy on going discussion on rings, where to purchase, qualities to look for in hardwarezone's forums. If I recall, it's in the Housing forums, as a sub-forum called Weddings.

    I think it's best to purchase a proposal ring, and then 2 plain wedding bands; wedding bands are generally cheap because they aren't fancy. As far as I am aware, girls usually prefer to wear the ring with the bigger rock; ultimately girls will compare with other girls (truth of the matter - noting to do with the sanctity of the marriage, or what it stands for etc.) - and the proposal ring often has more meaning as well.

    A marriage band is nothing more than an upgrade on the existing couple rings, used to symbolize the day you got officially married, but a proposal ring, I think would mean much more because that is the day you ask your significant other to spend the rest of your lives together.

    Good luck!

    Wedding bands, are quite simply, plain bands of metal. They are only bought after your S.O. has accepted your proposal and in the lead up to the wedding.

    They are quite plain, but like couple rings, alot of couples write mushy mushy private stuff inner side. So plain on the outside, meaningful on the inside.

    Many of the ladies I've worked with tend to wear their proposal ring; esp the young ones, when they just get married, all the others will comeby, start observing, comparing, asking questions - so naturally of course wear the one with the big rock.

    Many of the men I've worked with don't even wear their wedding bands; some do, but to others, it's just so .. darn mafan to get up in the morning and remember to wear it, or remember to take it off when they get back ... not to mention as guys, we have a tendency to lose stuff and that is not one thing that you're going to enjoy losing or explaining to your wife.

    EDIT: As far as I'm aware, I think it's very rare that a guy proposes with a pair of wedding rings .. it's an option, but it's not common.
