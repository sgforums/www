    Lemme explain in proper

    Yes it is possible to hit 3 megabits per second but the likelihood of its occurance is limited.

    1. Only if the sender can send at 3 megabits per second to you. (I've encountered it before even if the sender is living in a foreign country.) Most of the times, the senders are not capable to send at such high speed, so u need to maximise to that limit by downloading from different sources.

    2. Only if the upload speed is not overtaxed at the same time. Due to the settings, when you maxed out your upload speed, it is not possible to go even more than 100 kilobytes per second, roughly 800 kilobits per second.

    3. Only if your neighbourhood is not overtaxed by too many people surfing at that time.

    Other things u have to note
    The download speed is up to 3 megabits per second, roughly 375 kilobytes per second.

    The upload speed is up to 128 kilobits per second, roughly 15 kilobytes per second. (insufficient for gaming experience)

    My past experience with Korean sources:
    A stable 300 kilobytes per second with no frustration download speed.

    Local sources
    A stable 180 kilobytes per second with slight frustration download speed

    American sources and other sources
    Lots of problem. speed between 15k (handshake problem) to 250 kilobytes per second.

    Gaming problems:
    Low latency (compared to Singnet and PI), thus, affecting reaction time in real time games
    Limited upload, thus, jamming entire response for 3D games like lineage 2.

    Upload problems:
    As mentioned before, limitations on upload and uploads affecting download rates + handshake problems. Inability to stream music and perform video conference properly due to limitations on upload.
