    Originally posted by winnie^_^:
    i mean r u all hongkong pple? not hikers. haha.
    any nice xiao chi to intro? other than smelly beancurd and curry fishballs?
    Just go to a stall selling xiao chi... (There're plenty of them in Causeway Bay and Mongkok) Then point your fingers at anything that look interesting to you... If you've the appetite and money, you can try everything.... Haha...

    You can also go to a bakery... Recommended items include Po Lo Pau (Pineapple Bun - actually there's no pineapple. It's just the name. It has a crispy yellow crust on top), Gai Mei Pau (cocktail bun, usually filled with shredded coconut), egg tarts (HK-style or Portuguese-style), Lo Po Bang (wife cakes), Dan Wong Lin Yong So (Egg-yolk lotus paste pastry), Pei Dan So (Century egg pastry), etc etc etc.

    For wife cakes, the most famous bakeries are Hang Heung and Wing Wah. Both of them originates from Yuen Long, but you don't need to go all the way to Yuen Long lah... There are many branches in HK Island & Kowloon. Wing Wah even has a branch in HK airport. While Singaporeans like to buy bak kua (barbeque pork) as gifts, HK people like to buy wife cakes.... Haha.

    How to say Lychee in other dialects?
    Yah... The English word lychee comes from the Cantonese word lai chi.

    Other English words that originates from Cantonese include Longan, Ketchup, Kung Fu, Dim Sum, Wok, Mahjong, Sampan, Kowtow, etc.

    On the other hand, many Cantonese words originates from English.
    Ba Si - bus
    Dig Si - taxi
    Bou Ding - pudding
    Ce Lei Zi - cherry
    Fei Lum - film
    Ka Lei - curry
    Si Dor - store
    Si Dor Be Lei - strawberry
    Song Na - sauna
    Tib Si - tips
    etc etc etc

    By the way, learning Cantonese is more difficult than Mandarin, because Mandarin has 4 tones but Cantonese has 9!!!
