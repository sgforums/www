    Computer cannot locate OS?

    The BIOS is working perfectly. A way to see if the hard-disk is damaged can be to check its presence in the BIOS setup utility if the problem occurs again.

    If NOT present in the IDE drives list, then it means that the hard-disk connection is broken, or there is no power provided to the hard-disk or the IDE Controller is damaged (part of mobo damaged, possibly Northbridge chip)

    If the hard disk works in another location, it means that your motherboard is damaged; if it doesn't replace the hard-disk.

    ----- ----- -----

    If it is present, it means that your Windows installation is most likely corrupt, OR you have a virus. Some real-harm viruses disallow access to Windows unless they are loaded, so that the viruses will be loaded everytime Windows loads (it will load even before you can select Safe Mode)

    In this case you can plug in the hard-disk to another computer. Scan and remove the virus, backup your files, and re-install (recommended REFORMAT) Windows.

    ----- ----- -----

    Sometimes temperatures also affect the stability. Letting the drive cool down might help. If you laptop fan stops, it will damage more components like the motherboard and CPU.
