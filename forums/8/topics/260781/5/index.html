    Regarding GP, let me put it this way, to get into NUS FASS they see your A level sub grades, but to see which course you go into, they see your GP and (if have) interview. First year in nus fass you take common subjects with everybody else in NUS. 2nd year on then you specialise, so even if you dont do too well at A levels, but do fantastic in 1st year, you may still get in psychology.

    Regarding the degrees,

    Bachelor Degree is the most basic degree you take in uni. For psych, it's 3 years. If you get a Bachelor Degree with HONOURS, you can study an extra year to get your Masters. For other subjects like sciences, etc, if you dont get honours you can still do Masters but only after 2 years working experience. However, for psych, you HAVE to get honours in order to do your Masters. They don't accept people without honours.

    To be a practicing psychologist, you need Masters. So, if you want to practice, you have to get honours. However, if you plan to use the psych degree to go into another occupation, like doing business, or human resource, etc, then of course don't need larh.

    Choir is okay.. CCA grades and "impressiveness" depends on what competitions you took part in, etc. Have you taken part in many? To get into competitive degrees like Psych, Medicine, Law, etc.. you have to be all-rounded.
