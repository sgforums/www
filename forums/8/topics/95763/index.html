Wife cuts his allowance, so he cuts her throat

  • The Hobbit
    29 Sep 04, 21:20
    Source: NP

    HK man confesses this to police, but pleads not guilty to murder in court

    MADDENED by his wife's proposal to cut his monthly allowance by 50 per cent, a 70-year-old Hong Kong man stabbed her to death over breakfast.

    Tam Shu-kin, his wife and three daughters were living on public assistance of nearly HK$12,000 ($2,600) when the incident took place, reported Hong Kong's Apple Daily.

    Although the money was drawn under Tam's name and deposited into his bank account, his wife, Madam Chan Mui-chai, 46, managed the money.

    Tam and his family were first put on government welfare in May 1998. At first, Madam Chan would give him HK$1,600 each month.

    Then, last year, she cut that to HK$1,000.

    When she told him she was cutting his allowance again on Sep 16 last year, Tam got so mad he went to the kitchen and got a knife.

    Madam Chan was having breakfast in their flat in Wah Oi House in Wah Kwai Estate, and had her back to her enraged husband.

    Tam hid the knife in his pocket, then crept up behind her and stabbed her in the neck.

    The force was so great, he completely severed the woman's windpipe.

    Yesterday, Tam pleaded not guilty to murder in the Court of First Instance, saying he was provoked into the attack.

    After the killing, Tam telephoned his sister-in-law and told her what he had done. He then telephoned the police.

    When the police arrived, Tam, who was still drenched in blood, was agitated. He said that he had killed his wife by mistake.

    In a video-recorded interview with the police, Tam admitted to killing his wife by 'poking' her neck once 'on an impulse' with a fruit knife, reported The South China Morning Post.

    In the interview, which was played in court yesterday, he said he stabbed his wife from behind through the neck in a fit of anger.

    'I flared up, on an impulse, (I) took a knife, poked her, she fell down,' Tam said, his voice trembling.

    Prosecutor Stephen Pallaras QC told the court the force of the attack was sufficient to sever Chan's trachea, leaving a 7cm deep laceration on the left side of her neck.

    The prosecution said that by the time Madam Chan arrived at Queen Mary Hospital, she was dead.

    Mr Pallaras told the jury that Tam claimed he was provoked into killing his wife, but this argument was not accepted by the prosecution.

    The trial continues.
