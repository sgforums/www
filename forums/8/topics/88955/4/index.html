    National Day motivators get parade crowd up on their feet

    SINGAPORE : There are daredevil stunts, acrobatic displays, and stunning fireworks, but National Day isn't complete without the cheering crowd of 60,000.

    It took a special group of performers, the motivators, to rouse them into action before the parade proper began.

    The crowd are the ones who make the Stadium roar, but before that they, too, needed a little the pre-parade motivation.

    From here on it was over four hours of singing, cheering and dancing.

    The 450 motivators, from Touch Community Services, Temasek Polytechnic and the 10 ITEs, have been rehearsing since June for the big day.

    "We meet a lot of people and it's once in a blue moon that you motivate these people and make a lot of friends also," said motivator Gary Leong of ITE West Clementi.

    "The kids are really fun to motivate because they jump around but the adults are harder. Some of them just sit down and act cool but when the fireworks start, all of them behave like children," said another motivator, Nur Fadhilah Bte Rahim of ITE Bishan.

    "I love to be a motivator because we can motivate people and stay here throughout and watch the whole performance. It's cool," added Angel Yeung of Temasek Polytechnic.

    But when the Army's paratroopers started their 1,500 metre sky dive, even the adults could not hide their excitement.

    And there was more action with vehicle displays from the army, police and civil defence.

    Soon after, the Commandos took centre stage with their rapelling act, while the Civil Defence's elite DART team glided over the heads of spectators.

    It has been one very exciting pre-parade, and despite all the screaming and shouting, these motivators and the crowd still had not lost their voices. - CNA
