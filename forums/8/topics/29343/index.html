    learn the basics well...remember the fundamentals of how you should hold your cue, stablise your hand, making sure it doesn't swing left/right while cueing, haiving the correct posture and practice more...when i first started out about 12 yrs back, i can easily spend more than 10 hrs everytime i play, that was just after my 'o' levels...you can only learn so much from others, the rest is up to your own judgement..and don't be too anxious, learn step by step...
    after getting your basics right, you should learn how to place your cue ball...techniques like 'siding' 'follow ball' and 'screw tarek' is essential if you want to place your cue ball correctly, of course, how much power you put in every shot counts too...
