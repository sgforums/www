    chokky:

    bus lanes are so designated, in order to help buses be as unaffected as possible by traffic jams, in order to stay on schedule, so people would be encouraged to use public transport that is punctual and reliable.

    about the car tax and maintenance you pay, those could be avoided if you used public transport, right?

    basically you can't just look at the issue of having bus lanes as a monetary thing (you pay tax and fines). the bus lanes are paid for by civilians who use public transport (a lot of them btw), and they did so because part of the bargain was that the government provide them with a reliable and "world-class" public transport system.

    note that although tibs and sbs are "companies", they have to answer to the government which provides them with the authorization to run a public transport service. this is why the public in general holds the government responsible for the screw-ups of the bus companies. they can't really be looked at as profit-generating private commercial ventures, and hence i think they and the people who use public transport deserve the bus lane. you don't see private bus-hire companies being granted the privilege to use the bus lanes, do you?

    by the way, if you were as law abiding as you claimed, why all the fines you mentioned? (this is not a personal attack, so don't take it as such) as for writing into the straits time forums, perhaps other people writing in response to you might be able to give you more insight into the rationale behind setting aside bus lanes, since what i have said may or may not be correct.
