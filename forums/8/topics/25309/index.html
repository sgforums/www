    ok about time we addressed the topic..

    I hold an Aussie PR... but am in sg now, still a singaporean.

    My parents migrated to australia together with my brother and i for 2 main reasons that i know of:

    1. Education
    My brother and i wouldn't have passed our O level chinese to save our lives. Even if we did get thru our A levels we probably couldn't get into a local U (i guess my parents wanted their kids to have degrees like themselves). So even if we stayed in singapore i'd probably end up as an overseas student in australia.

    2. More "wholesome" lifestyle
    When we were kids i seldom saw my parents. My mum worked shift, and my dad regularly came home quite late from work. I was looked after by my aunty, and not a maid becos my parents refused to let a stranger look after the kids. By and by, i got to know my aunty as a "mother" better than my real mother. Well i guess that's better than referring to a maid as a mother, but the problems were there. This and many other reasons caused my parents to decide to move to australia where (ironically) they met in the first place (while they themselves were studying).

    i'm not saying that everyone should migrate to another country but there are serious pros and cons for me, personally to stay in au or sg.

    while i'm still "young" i feel that sg has more opportunities and the fast pace lifestyle is a good way to train oneself to take pressure and handle problems efficiently. however u can't do this all ur life. i do foresee myself using the above two reasons that my dad did when i'm his age (40++) to move back to australia.

    can anyone blame me? or my father for that matter? let's hear some serious opinions..
