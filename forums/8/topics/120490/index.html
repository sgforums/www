    First pirate attack since tsunamis hit
    A MALAYSIAN-FLAGGED tug boat was attacked on Monday evening by gun-toting sea pirates who fled the scene after capturing the boat's captain and chief officer.

    This incident - the first reported attack by pirates in the Malacca Strait since tidal waves struck the region - ends a two-month period in the aftermath of the Dec 26 tsunamis when there were no reported attacks in the 520-nautical-mile-long straits.

    According to piracy watchers from the London-based International Maritime Bureau (IMB), the gang of armed pirates fired on the tug when it was sailing south-west of Penang.

    The tug had nine crewmen aboard. The vessel was towing a barge filled with coal and was headed for Lumut, a port on the west coast of Malaysia, where the coal was destined for use in cement plants in the area.

    Mr Noel Choong, head of the IMB's Piracy Reporting Centre, said gunfire was sprayed at the vessel indiscriminately and the tug's chief engineer was hit in the leg. He bled heavily from his leg wound but survived. 'The pirates used the same method of attack seen in previous incidents in the area,' he said. 'We advise ship owners to maintain a strict anti-piracy watch and report any suspicious vessels to the IMB so we can inform authorities of such vessels.'

    Last year, a total of 37 pirate attacks took place in the Malacca Strait - the world's most dangerous shipping lane - up from 28 attacks in 2003.
