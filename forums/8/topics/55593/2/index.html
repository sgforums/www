    As of this moment of time, I don't think the ISPs here in SG are obliged to track down music swappers unless the law forces them to do so. The situation now in SG is that d/ling n distributing music online is a civil offence, not a criminal offence, meaning only RIAS and the record companies have the right to sue music swappers in a civil law suit for copyright infringement. Which is y I'm gonna rubbish the claims made by the thread starter becos:

    1) the government cannot publicly prosecute n hand sentences to music swappers YET cos its only a civil offence, altho chances are a bill is gonna be passed in parliament to make music swapping n d/ling a criminal offence.
