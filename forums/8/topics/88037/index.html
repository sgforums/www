    The New Paper - 02 Aug 2004

    And now, words from the veterans

    SOME women like their men to have it on the chest while others prefer guys with a goatee.

    There are also those who love the shaggy look on legs and arms.

    But, one thing's for certain - almost all agree that it has to be kept neat and tidy.

    'I like men with hairy chests, said Ms Jane Cheong, 23, a student.

    'It's so sexy when you catch a glimpse of it through a shirt that's unbuttoned at the top.'

    Ms Cheong added: 'I think men who take care of themselves are very sexy, so if he grooms his hair to look more polished or if he gets a trim because his hair growth is excessive, I am perfectly fine.'

    Ms Cheong feels that at the end of the day, men still have to be a bit of a caveman.

    ILLUSION OF MANLINESS

    Another chest-hair-loving woman, 24-year-old student Gayathri Mohanakrishnan, agreed: 'I like male hair... its presence gives a pleasant illusion of manliness.

    'If hair is in the right places, it can be really sexy and can provide a warm and comfortable sensation.'

    Indeed, hair on men has to be on appropriate areas, it seems.

    'I think men with moustaches are so ah-pek (Hokkien for uncle), ' said Ms Cheong.

    'Yuck! And don't even get me started on nose hair.

    'It's positively stomach-churning to see curly wisps of hair popping out of the nostrils.'

    But not every woman likes men with hairy chests.

    Ms Rachel Yeo, a 27-year-old leasing administrator, hates chest hair, but is fine with facial and leg hair.

    'Chest hair and armpit hair are a huge turn-off!' she said.

    Whatever it is, having hair is not as bad as not having any.

    'If he is completely hairless, that's a little frightening!' said Ms Cheong.
