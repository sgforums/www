    Singaporean unicycles in under 4 hours from Pasir Ris to Boon Lay MRT stations

    06 July 2006 2026 hrs (SST) 1226 hrs (GMT)

    A Singaporean is trying to go on an expedition from Pole to Pole later this year.

    But first, Siddharth Rajan has gone on a unicycle to bolster his chances.

    A trip from Pasir Ris to the Boon Lay MRT stations on a unicycle is a total distance of 27 stations, and almost 40 kilometres.

    Siddharth, 22, has gone on this route and broken his own nine-hour record achieved last year.

    Undeterred by the rain and the wet roads, he crossed the finish line in under four hours.

    The expedition from the South Pole to the North Pole is undertaken by the Pole to Pole Leadership Institute.

    24 people will be chosen to embark on the expedition in November this year and it will be the first-ever human-powered journey.

    Participants will ski, cycle, sail and walk the whole distance from the South Pole to the North Pole.

    Along the way, expedition members will also undertake various humanitarian and environmental projects.

    Siddharth said: "Dare to dream. It was only a couple of years ago that I really decided to go for adventure as my profession. It's just been two years and I'm already prepared for an expedition and I could go to the Poles in November. If you dream and try hard enough, you could succeed."

    The Pole to Pole Leadership Institute is an international organization that inspires, empowers, and trains youth to take initiative and become leaders addressing the critical issues of our time.

    For more information, visit www.poletopoleleadership.com and www.supportsid.com. - CNA/ir
