    NOV 26, 2002
    Temptation island
    Will even more visitors be lured to Sentosa now that a resort spa will open on the island from Monday?
    By Elisabeth Gwee
    FROM next Monday, stressed-out Singaporeans will not have to jet off to Thailand or Indonesia for some resort-style spa pampering.
    All they have to do is hop into their cars and drive to Sentosa.
    Singapore's first resort spa opens at The Beaufort Hotel on Dec 2.
    Sprawled over 6,000 sq m of landscaped garden, which is about the size of a football field, Spa Botanica features six outdoor treatment pavilions, mud baths, labyrinths and a 22-m lap pool. A colonial pre-war building houses 14 indoor treatment rooms.
    Mr Anthony Ross, Beaufort's general manager, says: 'Previously, if people wanted that kind of spa experience, they'd have to spend time and money travelling to their holiday destination.
    'Now, they have it at their doorstep.'

    PLANS FOR SENTOSA
    THE spa is just one of the ways that the Sentosa Development Corporation (SDC) is hoping to turn the island into a complete holiday getaway.
    Other plans include an eco-type hotel set in the tree canopies of one of the island's nature trails.
    Mr Gurjit Singh, SDC's property director, says that it will be inspired by the five-star Andaman resort in Langkawi. The SDC is currently looking for investors for the project.
    Two to three informal beach-front restaurants will be added to Siloso Beach by the end of next year. These will be deve loped and operated by the SDC's newly set-up food and beverage team.
    A theme park also looks set to open along the northern waterfront where the Asian Village used to stand.
    The SDC is in talks with several foreign theme-park operators to build one that combines indoor

    and outdoor land and water rides.
    The site of the former Fantasy Island theme park will be turned into an underground car park for about 1,000 cars in 2005.
    As part of the SDC's 10-year masterplan to make Sentosa more accessible to the public, car entry rates were reduced from $6 to $2 two weeks ago.
    Mr Singh says: 'The 'old' Sentosa was seen as an expensive attraction. People felt it wasn't up to the mark, so it wasn't worth spending $6 to come in.'
    Since the price reduction, visitor figures have increased by 25 per cent over the same period last year.
    This has also increased the demand for accommodation on the island.
    A spokesman for the NTUC Sentosa Beach Resort says although his hotel is fully booked until December, enquiries from walk-in customers have 'definitely gone up'.
    A spokesman for Shangri-La's Rasa Sentosa Resort says that hotel guests have never had to pay for entrance fees. But the reduced entrance fees might increase business at its restaurants.
    'PREMIUM' PRICES
    BUT while it is now cheaper to get into Sentosa, its newest attraction has prices that might not exactly draw the crowds.
    Treatments at Spa Botanica cost 10 to 20 per cent more than treatments at city spas.
    For example, massages range from $110 to $190, while those at Amrita Spa at Raffles City - the largest spa here - range from $100 to $140.
    Beaufort's spa packages with hotel stays, which range from one night to a week, are also slightly higher than others in the region.
    A three-night stay at a deluxe room includes a massage, a body scrub, a body wrap, a reflexology session, health consultation and full use of the spa and its facilities. That costs $2,428 for two people.
    A similar three-night package at the Banyan Tree Bintan's Ocean Front jacuzzi villa costs $2,269 for two people, excluding the $45 return fare for the ferry ride to Bintan.
    Mr Ross says that treatments are 'priced at a premium' because the spa is targeting an upmarket clientele.
    He is confident that the spa will open up a new leisure market for the island, and for the hotel.
