    Just a brief guide:
    Any form of weight exercise when you are older serves to only increase the density of your bones, and very very little on the thickness. Thickness is either due to frequent exercising when younger or due to genes.

    If you mean bigger muscles, then yes, weightlifting do help. However, please bear in mind that weightlifting only trains one aspect of your body; fitness and health are not just lifting weights alone. It is a combination of many other factors, one of which includes cardio.
