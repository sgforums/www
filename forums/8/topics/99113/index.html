    HK bars face smoking ban

    Hong Kong

    HONG Kong is to ban smoking in bars and restaurants, joining cities such as New York and countries such as Ireland in leading the charge against tobacco.

    Legislation, expected to be in place within a year, will also cover factories and offices.

    The territory's legislature voted overwhelmingly in favour of the ban in a late-night debate on Wednesday and the measure was backed health chief York Chow, officials said yesterday.

    A government statement said the extension of the statutory smoking ban to more premises would protect the public from the health hazards of passive smoking.

    Hong Kong currently allows smoking in restaurants, although establishments seating more than 200 people must have non-smoking sections.

    'We are delighted,' said Ms Annelise Connell, vice-chairman of Clear The Air, one of the anti-tobacco groups that have long campaigned for a smoking ban in Hong Kong.

    Hospitality industry figures had opposed the ban, saying it would deter customers. In particular, they fear it will scare off visitors from mainland China, the world's heaviest-smoking country, upon whom the local tourism industry depends.

    The government statement said the new law would also include more anti-smoking campaigns among young people in a bid to reduce the 16 deaths a day caused by smoking-related illnesses in Hong Kong. -- AGENCE FRANCE-PRESSE
