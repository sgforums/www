    my 2 cents worth here.

    Are u really keen on working in the Accounting sector in the long term? I would advise u to find some full-time or part-time work in relation to accounting as to confirm where your interests and ability lies. Cos if u decide to take ACCA, all u have is an certificate which is recognised by finance or accounting sectors.

    And if u wish to make a career change few years down the road due to unforseen circumstances, your ACCA cert may not be relevant to other business sectors like Logistics or Manufacturing. Thus, you will apply for the new job as a Diploma or A Levels holder i assume.

    If i'm not wrong, if u can pass all the ACCA course without repeating any modules, the total cost in Singapore dollar should be below S$10,000 inclusive of membership fees (correct me if i;m wrong).

    However, if u want to play safe in this current depressed economy, you can try to expand your budget abit and consider taking SIM-RMIT Bachelor of Business (Accountancy) for around S$15,000 (Advanced-Standing Level). At least this is a Degree programme and you can apply for CPA after you get your degree. It's a kind of safeguard as even if you want to make a career switch later on in your career path, your opportunities would be wider as you would be looking for graduate positions and not just diploma or A Levels positions.

    Basically, its down to whether u want to be a Diploma / A Levels holder with ACCA qualifications or Degree Holder with Accounting Background?
