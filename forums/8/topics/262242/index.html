    Originally posted by Deino:

    If your short father has a recessive tall gene and your short mother also has recessive tall gene, they can produce either a short/short childx2, OR a short/tall child OR a tall/tall child.

    Dominant gene/Recessive gene

    S/T Father + S/T Mother
    1st child S/S S/S S/T T/T any one of these
    2nd child S/S S/S S/T T/T any one of these

    and so on...

    So since there are more probability of S/S genes, the chances are shorter children. Actually 3 short is to 1 tall. But the S/T and T/T children can reproduce tall offsprings but the S/S offsprings have limited chances of reproducing tall offsprings unless she can find a mate with S/T, T/S, T/T genes.

    Glossary : Dominant gene determines how you look.
    Recessive gene is a hidden gene within you that can be
    passed down to yr next generation.
