    Churra..not twist ball... unless you are a 'helicopter' player. It got to do with the lifting of the finger.that's why they call it 'fingertip' bowler. The condition of the lane also play a part in the delivery..that's why we usually do some 'warm up' before the actual game. For your ppls info..it takes 21 and half spins of the ball from the 'arrow' to the 'pocket' of the 1 - 2 pin or 1 -3 pin..depending on which hand you are bowling (left handed bowler are called southpaw). Anything more or less would not give you a solid 'strike' unless it is a fluke.
