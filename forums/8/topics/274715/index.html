    Singapore should bid for 2010 Youth Olympics, say industry players
    SINGAPORE: Singapore should bid for the inaugural Youth Olympic Games in 2010, as this would give the Republic a better chance in 2014, should the first bid be unsuccessful, say industry players and experts.
    The event would also give young athletes a boost to excel in their sports and perform well at international level, say sports officials.

    The 2005 International Olympic Committee (IOC) session here was the closest Singapore came to staging an Olympic event. But the experience gained and the impression left on delegates would help Singapore in securing the next big event.

    The Youth Olympic Games is a new event mooted by the IOC to get the world's youth to be more active.

    And while Singapore authorities are still reviewing the possibility of hosting it, some industry players think Singapore should grab the chance.
