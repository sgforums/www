    To describe how is it like being a lone ranger, must first see the..hmm..personality of person. Sociable people find lone ranger sucky at times. People who are dependent on others will find being a lone ranger a great handicap. On the general note, being a lone ranger sucks ass (_,_). Singapore is so small, there's bound to be interaction amongst people. But being a lone ranger kicks ass when you need to take time-out, think things through, reflect, etc.

    I've spent most of my time a lone ranger (not by choice), so I'm kinda used to it. To me, it means unconditional freedom to do what you want and to grow at your own pace. But it also comes with a price that some find too high to pay...

    About having unwanted people hovering over you, well, you can't have the world, so why not settle for the moon? In other words, we don't always get what we want, but we must always be grateful for what we have.
