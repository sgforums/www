    Two wires is preferred. To explain in brief, this is how it works:

    1) One wire, best if it's quite hard yet thin, to slot in at the bottom of the keyhole and apply force clockwise (or counter clockwise depending on the lock in which way it's opened).

    2) Then another wire to 'scratch' the top part of the keyhole the bump all the cylinders, usually about 4 or 5. When all cylinders are locked (because of the clock or counterclockwise pressure) you can start turning it. But needs skill to know whether are all cylinders bumped.
