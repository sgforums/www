    Satellite images show sprawling cities built in remote parts of China that were left completely abandoned, years after their construction.

    Elaborate public buildings are completely unused, except for a few government vehicles near communist authority offices.

    Some estimates put the number of empty homes at 64 million, with up to 20 new cities being built every year in the country.

    Regional governments in China built massive real estate projects, including Kangbashi in Inner Mongolia and Zhengzhou New District, which have remained empty.

    Kangbashi, built in just five years, was meant to be the urban centre for Ordos City - home to 1.5million people.

    It was filled with office towers, administrative centres, museums, theatres and sports facilities as well as thousands of homes, but is virtually deserted.

    Famous short-seller Jim Chanos issued a dire warning, said he expected China's economy to implode in a real estate bust.

    He said the country was 'on an economic treadmill to hell' and the country's bubble was 'Dubai times 1,000'.
