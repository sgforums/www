    Teachers have the power to refuse a student proper education from their class if the student fails to oblige to the rules and regulations set by the School (MOE).

    Students have the rights to proper education regardless of race, language or religion as long as they obey the rules and regulations set down by the school.

    Thus, the teachers has minimal influence outside school as long as the pupil does not wear the uniform of the school, the badge of the school or any symbolic items related to the school.

    However, the teachers have the moral obligations to inform the parents of the pupil's wrongdoings, poor conduct and problem in learning.

    The power to expel a student from an institute of education solely lies on the head, usually the principal, after receiving complains. The principal, however, must inform the parents of the decision and the reasons behind it.

    The students are expected to show proper conduct even outside school as long as they wear the uniform, the badge of the school or any symbolic items related to the school.

    The teachers have no power to subject the student to corporal punishment.

    The student has the right to protect themselves from any other disciplinary actions as long as the student follow the rules and regulations set by the school.

    These rules and regulations are different in different schools but not exclusive of the rules set down by the Ministry of Education.
