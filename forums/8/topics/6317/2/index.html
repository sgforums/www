    yes, values are certainly not something we (or perhaps anyone else can) create in a vacuum. in fact it seems to be a universal law that nothing can arise without antecedent conditions of possibility (unless of course we are speaking of god, which we are not). seen in this way, it may be only a matter of emphasis when we speak of favouring one value over another. all that is needed here however is only to qualify the sense in which value creation takes place - that is, it cannot take place ex nihilo.

    at the risk of turning this into a nietzsche forum, i am interested in how you would separate the creation of new morals from the particular values held. is the former merely a set of codes of conduct (to be) accepted by society? if so, you attribute to the ubermensch the notion of social or political reform - in itself contentious becos some say the ubermensch is a concept pertaining only to self-development (in an admittedly open society). how does the latter come about? how does the ubermensch come about the particular values he holds and wishes to promote? if we are not to rule out socio-historical forces that shape the ubermensch's values, then existing morals do play a role. but if morals do play a role, then even "new" morals cannot be all that new (the way new values are not all that new). further, what sharp (and meaningful) dividing line is there now between morals on the one hand, and (particular) values on the other? they seem to collapse into one another.

    turning to snagism proper, you seem to imply that there is no worth to being a snag. and u seem (or at least seemed at the start) to do this by equating the origins of snaggism with incompetence in some men. let me link this to the slave morality. nietzsche sometimes concealed an admiration for the inversion of values undertaken by the slaves. but he saw this as being based on ressentiment. can we then say that slave morality is bad becos it issued from a failure to compete? we are now far removed from the socio-historical conditions which gave rise to the slave revolt. are we battling the same battle? what is crucial for us now is the worth of (the slave) morality for us - i.e. whether values like pity or meekness or whatever have any value now. the question is not HOW these values came about. which is why i brought up the genetic fallacy. because one would commit a genetic fallacy if one discounts the worth of a value (for instance) on the basis of its origins. but nietzsche does not make this mistake. he grounds it on physiology and tells us that slave morality (when we look at our make up) is not life-affirming - it does not give full play to the active forces which lie within us, but only affirms the reactive.

    nietzsche, however, realises that to convince is not the same as to give sound arguments. that is, for the vast majority of ppl, to convince them of something is only to sway their emotions in some way. which is what i presume you are trying to do by equating snagism wif their motive for adopting snag characteristics - it may be a convincing argument against snagism, without it being at the same time a sound argument against snagism. and the latter is what matters in evaluations of formal logic. to turn your argument into one that is not a genetic fallacy, i believe you have to adopt the same tactic nietzsche did. that is, you have to show that there is something inherently unmanly about being a snag ("inherently" being the key word). you have to show that being "a man" is not merely a social construction and that apart from that construction there is still a residue left behind which is non-snag in nature.

    but you have brought up a point which is interesting and i will briefly elaborate on that. let us grant that snagism has some value (it may be a wrong impression, but i gather you think snaggism has no value becos there is some essentialist way in which a man should be a man). what do we say about those who adopt snag qualities because of (non-snag) motives? is appearance (of snaggy-ness) all that matters? is it okay to be a snag just to get women? is it okay to be a snag cos it seems "in"? we are talking of a complex notion of value formation and what constitutes moral worth. just food for thought.
