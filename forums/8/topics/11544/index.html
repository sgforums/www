    27 Apr 01, 17:54
    Hi dante,

    Yeah, arranging a time to play can be a headache. That's why I will try carry on with the game as long as most of the players are available.

    As for the difference between adnd3e and adnd, basically, in my opinion, the game have been greatly improved. For example, characters now have feats, or special abilities, that make even fighters interesting to play. No more consulting tables to hit or calculating THACO, attacks are simply 1d20+modifier roll higher than AC.

    As for specialized classes, you must be refering to Prestige Classes, which are classes that your character can take once they meet certain requirements. Example: Arcane Archer, Blackguard, etc.

    If you want to read more about dnd3e, I would probably recommend the following website:
    http://www.rpgplanet.com/dnd3e/3erulebooks.htm
