    1) Education is not the solution to this problem. We are not talking about ghetto kids plying their trade due to lack of formal education. We are talking about affluent kids who comes from middle and upper social strata of Singapore and more often than not, highly educated. Which brings to the point of education. Are we teaching the right values to the kids of today and the adults of tommorow? I am afraid not.

    2) Tougher laws seems to be a singularly Singaporean experience. The concept of killing one monkey to warn the whole bunch of them will work fine in a country full of uneducated peasants but in this modern and highly wired society? We can't just be bolting the stable whenever the horse had ran away and beside, this is not a cure. It is just a temporary measure to "frighten" the youths of today. Be smart, the way to tackle them is not to fighten them but to educate them with the proper moral values and keep their life focused and fulfilled.

    3) I agree with this concept of deterrence. Mst kids are latchkey and part of their problems could be due to loneliness and a need to scream for attention. As i mentioned earlier, keeping their life focus will really go a long way in helping them. If part of their skills can be channelled into doing something useful, we will perhaps see less juvenile problems and more scholars, sportsmen and dot.com entrepreuners. Of course, i am dreaming here but hey, it's a small step.
