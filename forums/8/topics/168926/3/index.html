    You will learn an entirely new pidgin call 'Singlish', if you stay long enough in Singapore. Essentially, it's an efficient way of speaking English where you say only the crucial words e.g. noun, verb, place/time are used. Conjungations are dropped and grammar doesn't matter anymore. Your mind will use words with the shortest syllabus from a variety of languages (English, Mandarin, Hokkein, Cantonese, Teochew, Malay and sometimes Indian) to form your sentence.

    Almost everyone in Singapore speaks this.

    Major road names in Singapore are all 3 letters. Tune in to the radio in the morning or evening and you get DJs splurting out 3-letter word 'AYE, PIE, BKE, CTE, TPE etc to caution drivers and you get a good laugh that locals do comprehend the entire mish-mash of letters.

    Anyways, so what about stuff I should bring from America? Is anything here more expensive over in singapore ?

    In general everything in Singapore is cheaper. But don't expect quality. E.g. you can get cheap clothes here but you wear them out quickly.

    Comparing clothes that you buy in US and Singapore, spending the same amount of money, you get slightly better quality clothes in US than in Singapore.

    Note: US brands (anything) is always more expensive to get in Singapore than in US.

    Of course, you have to take into consideration that Singapore offers Asian sizes of clothes and shoes. So you better be prepared to the limited choices due to size limitation.

    Anyone have any tips for gettin used to the humidity? Does everyone in general stay inside, or is everyone just used to it. (For example in Las Vegas, even the locals arent used to the very dry hot weather, so they run from Ac to Ac)

    The most uninviting feeling is when you get out of Changi Airport and the warm air sticks to yr face instantly and you cannot get rid of it. Thereafter, you start perspiring. It gets scary to see water droplets forming on your arms and dripping off your face but you are not even doing anything strenous.

    You will get used to the humidity in 1 week. Just 1 week of profuse sweating and then you will be fine! I've been through that!

    If you stay in NTU, you practically do the Las Vegas way of running from LT to LT or to libraries to get cool air. Downtown Sg is better, you are always enclosed in Ac environment. At least you don't need a moisturiser all the time coz it's so humid here. But the sun will be scorching hot, esp in summer (May - July)
