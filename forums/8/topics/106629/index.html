    pls teach
    83A indicates durometer, ie hardness of the urethane used for the wheel. Theoretically, harder wheels give u faster rides but less grip, especially on wet surfaces. But then again this is also determined by the formula & quality of the urethane compound. Cheap crappy hard wheels may say 83A but won't give u the grip of a quality wheel with the same durometer.

    For recreational skates, maybe 78A or 81A is max u need tho .... nothing too fancy since u hardly skate heh, no sense wasting good $ on sth u don't do often!

    TX
