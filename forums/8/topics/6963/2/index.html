    April Showers -- The Lyrids

    --------------------------------------------------------------------------------
    The Lyrid meteor shower typically displays its maximum rates from any given location in the early morning hours of April 22nd. The shower is detectable from April 16-25.
    Usually at the bottom end of activity for the major showers, the Lyrids have produced four recorded outbursts of very high activity: 1803, 1922, 1945 and 1982. The 1982 outburst produced zenith hourly rates of 250 for a very brief time, just a little more than half an hour (Jenniskens 1995).
