    joho and kop21 have said it all.

    it is quite hard for us to give assurance that you will be feel totally at home as it also vastly depends on your initiative to make friends with strangers.

    to share my own experience, my first outing wasnt a great one, considering meeting a group of strangers at a disco pub where conversations were barely audible.

    i even had difficulties feeling comfortable with some of the guys whom i crapped online so often prior to the that meetup.

    nevertheless, the barriers gradually disappears as i attended more outings.

    i believe the keys here are:
      - tell yourself you are there purely to socialise, not holding too much expectation in finding a gf, wife, bosom friends and etc.. be yourself and be natural.
      - its not the last outing to be organised and it never will. so do away with the "do-or-die" mentality. if you cant get along very well at the bbq, it doesnt mean u will be outcasted forever. after the bbq, attend a few more outings and u'll naturally fit in.
