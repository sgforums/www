    Hairdresser said die, die must dye

    The New Paper
    January 24, 2006

    On 19 Jan, I went to a hair salon in a Jurong shopping centre and experienced the poor service of a hairdresser who was intent in earning more from me that I had intended to spend.

    I told her I wanted a trim only and that the length of my hair should remain about the same.

    She kept asking me if I wanted to colour my hair or highlight it. I politely said no.

    Throughout the time with her, she repeatedly said that my hair would look better if it was dyed.

    After the haircut was done, she had cut my fringe shorter than I had instructed her to do.

    Then again she said that with the fringe shorter, my hair would look better if it was dyed brown!

    This woman never took no for an answer and it got me quite annoyed. I won't go there again.

    - Tan Seng Wei
