    Prader-Willi syndrome (PWS) is characterized by:

    1. Severe hypotonia and feeding difficulties in early infancy.
    2. Excessive eating and gradual development of morbid obesity in later infancy or early childhood, unless externally controlled.
    3. Mental retardation and distinctive behavioral problems in all patients.
    4. Hypogonadism is present in both males and females.
    5. Short stature is common.
