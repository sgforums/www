    What you've heard is the good side. If you know how to enjoy life, of course it will be slacked and fun. The reverse is true.

    There are a lot of activities in poly to de-stress. Even if you have no wish to join, go out with your friends, have a good game of soccer, LAN, etc to de-stress. After that, then continue with your work.

    To lessen your stress, try reading up before your lectures and tutorials. If you can understand certain parts well, it will save you time of asking your lecturers. Spend this extra time on your projects or asking about the parts which you don't understand.

    As for working in groups, this is part of life. You've got to adapt. When you stepped into the working world, you will work with even more people with even more varied personalities. To handle them, ask yourself this question: Are you a leader or a follower or somewhere in between? If you are leader, try not to lead for most of the projects, to lessen your burden. If you are a follower, don't keep quiet all the time. Voice out your displeasure if you really can't cope. Compensate for that once you have more or less finished your stuffs.
