    10 March 2006, The New Paper,

    SORRY, guys. Joey Swee does not quite fancy you.

    In her own words, she finds Singaporean men too shallow, too domineering and too boastful.

    'When the timing is right, they'll say, 'Let's go apply for a flat together!',' claimed the 29-year-old actress-compere.

    She speaks from experience, of course.

    Joey has had two Singaporean boyfriends - a three-year relationship in her teens and a four-year relationship that ended when she was 23 years old.

    She also has many guy buddies.

    And while she has had no lack of Singapore suitors, she has become more sceptical of male attention after joining Star Search in 1997.

    'It's harder to make new friends because you wonder if people are trying to make use of you,' she said.

    It didn't help that Joey wanted a 'hong hong lie lie ' (Mandarin for grand and spectacular) romance.

    So she settled for a foreigner - Indonesian-Chinese Gani Bustan, 41.

    'Some people are nice to you or give you treats because you are who you are,' she reasoned. 'But with a foreigner, all the 'privileges' I had are gone, because he doesn't have a clue who I am.'

    The couple were introduced in 2003 by Joey's girlfriend, who had married Mr Gani's best pal.

    Initially, they kept in touch via e-mail and instant messages.

    Things got serious when Mr Gani, who runs his own finance company, started flying to Singapore for business regularly.

    He proposed last December aboard a plane. They were returning from a nine-day China tour.

    They had their wedding ceremony in Jakarta last month and will register their marriage here next Monday. Then they will fly to Paris in April for their honeymoon.

    She said: 'He's a very genuine person and he's very caring and dotes on me.

    'We're from two different countries, so we treasure what little time we have together. It's more hong hong lie lie because we're always flying to see each other.'

    The jet-setting will continue during their married life, as Joey intends to continue - with her hosting and acting.

    When asked how she managed to keep her 'trophy hubby' under wraps, she replied: 'Of course you cannot talk about your final catch, or else someone will come snatch him away!'
