    Interesting.

    True, that teachers do not understand how students feel but yet students also do not know how teachers feel either.

    Everything have 2 sides, in addition it includes Karma too.

    Students, are growing teenagers, therefore difference in behavoir becomes more distinct and clearer due to influences from many factors. Some rowdy, some goody 2 shoes while others, your typical back stabber.

    Teachers, faces pressure from principal, HOD and parents, not only that, but also students, especially the "Few" and several other factors. These people also have a fixed mentality, for they have fully matured and depending on the age, their mentality differs too. In addition, the amount of pressure they can handle also constitute a significant point.

    Put these 2 groups of people together, results can be on either extremes. Ever wondered why you like some teachers while others may be dirt to you? Mainly due to communications but remember the 1st time you look a stranger straight into the eye? Compared to your attitude towards your best friend, it is very different.

    If you expect a teacher, who is total stranger in the first place to be friendly towards you, it will be tough, remember they have a fixed mentality ( with exceptions to certain young teachers ). Most important thing is to get to know the teacher, bond with him/her, understand why he/she treat you like this.

    Being, a matured adult, a teacher can definetly understand why you don't like him/her and gradually change for beneficial effects.

    Bonding is important but always remember the teacher is a figure of authority, treat them with due respect and they will treat you as the same, if not, more. Play hard and Work equally hard.

    Down to the point, an eye for an eye, treat the teacher like dirt, he/she give you dirt.

    Students faces problems but remember, don't vent your anger upon the innocent. Teachers are human beings too, they too need TLC, understand them, their woes may not necessarily be smaller than yours, if not, larger.

    I, too once favoured certain teachers while condemning others, major reasons being they are nice or retard. Without any in depth understanding of the teacher, I condemned those who irks me or who I think is not being nice. Therefore, these comments get spread out and the teacher, of course do not like it too and bores down hard.

    In my current school, I had such an experience. A Gp tutor, male, on the 1st day of school handed out composition test and expect us to complete within a stipulated time, according to 'A' Levels. Naturally, all of us was't very happy about it, so things went downhill from there. Until things get serious, he wanted to talk to me face to face because of my bad attitude, he was stern but I gave nuts to his talk. Right in his face, I looked straight in his eye and called him arrogant, doesn't deserve any respect from the students, told him that I never liked him ever since I sat my eyes upon him, naturally he was flaring but the face to face talk got settled eventually. Later on things got smoother, class began to joke with him, he became more friendly ( less stern ) and even asked me to joined the inter-class debate competition.
    I was skeptical but as things progress, I thought he was alright, this thought toally set in during the last round which will decide whether we will be champions or 1st runner up. We were up against his own class, we thought he would help his class instead of us but he fufilled his duty to us as a GP tutor by putting his best to help us, though we lost and got 1st runner up but the experience we had were in a rank of its own.
    GP became fun with jokes and laughter throwing around but all things must come to an end. He was due to leave the school and left a while ago, the feeling was relatively moody, the class didn't want him to go but before he left, he gave us a set of 'assignment' to do which is kinda weird for a parting gift. When we open it, we realized it is actually his thoughts and feelings for the time he is with us, nice comments he left us and a thanks coupled with a miss. He's a nice teacher.

    Anyway, this story clearly illustrate that students and teacher must understand each other for miracles to happen, both parties must give and take, understanding and communications is important to foster good relationships.

    Just my 2 cents worth. Thanks

    Lexanez~

    My dear teacher, why are you so old fashioned? We all know that teachers are humans too, then why are you acting as a good role model for us? Why can't you be more open?

    We, as a student, has our own problems and stress too. Who says a young child cannot have problems and stress? We are humans too. So please understand us too. Stress and problems do make us grow, but in an unhappy way.

    We always hope that you are in good mood, so that we would be happy for you too. We, be it a student, a child, a teenager or even your friends, care lots about face. We easily feel awkward, so we do not show our feelings and emotion easily on our face.

    Lastly, please note that naughty students or active students, have feelings too. Me, myself, being a naughty student, cry easily and got hurt in our heart easily. We are humans, like you too.

    I wish the teachers out there see this, and know how we feel. We totally understand the stress and hardships you are under. And, most importantly, humour and friendliness is what we are finding inside you.
