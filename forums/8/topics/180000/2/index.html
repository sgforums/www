    A two-year-old girl has been missing from her home at Block 62 Circuit Road since 7.30pm on Wednesday night.

    Nurasyura Mohamed Fauzi was last seen by her grandfather, sleeping in the room at their ground floor flat.

    Nurasyura Mohamed Fauzi, affectionately known as "Noi Noi," has a favourite hangout.

    The playground, just outside her home is where she usually plays, either by herself or with the neighbours' children.

    Mastura Kamsir, Noi's mother, said, "I hope that right now, we can get her as soon as possible because I am very worried."

    Noi was last seen dressed in her favourite colour - a pink top, pink three-quarter pants and pink slippers.

    Police believe Noi could have left the house through the back door which was left unlocked.

    Johari Mohd Yus, Noi's grandfather, said, "We all split up to look for her...over more than an hour. At 10pm, her parents returned home from work."

    Fatimah Yusoff, Noi's grandmother, said, "If the mother is not back, I'm the one who puts her to bed. I will not let her go out of my sight. I will take her everywhere I go but she happened to be sleeping then."

    Police have been combing the area.

    Noi's biological father, Mohamed Fauzi, who visits them about once a month, dropped in on the family to help out.

    A witness said she had seen Noi alone at the playground at about 7.30pm.

    Jumakyah Sudin, a neighbour, said, "...I was sitting over at the benches. I saw that she was sitting at the playground alone. When I called her, she walked away.

    "Sometimes her auntie will accompany her but yesterday I thought her auntie went in for a while so I just ignore(d) (her)..."

    The missing girl's parents and relatives have put up about 50 posters in the area - including neighbouring blocks and shops.

    For now, the family is keeping their hopes up that someone will come forward and shed some light to where Noi might be.

    Noi is described as a petite Malay girl with straight, shoulder-length hair.

    Anyone with information can call the Police hotline at 1800-255 0000.
