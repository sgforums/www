    I remember reading somewhere that a man on a bicycle is one of the most efficient heat engines of all - the efficiency can be something like 75%. In other words, 75% of your effort gets translated into the motion of the bike.

    In contrast, jogging is extremely inefficient - I should think that only 10% of your effort goes into motion. Much of the rest goes into speeding up the heart and warming up your body.

    So for the same distance covered, you burn less energy through cycling.
