SMRT 3rd quarter FY2005 net profit grew 25.2%

  • Manager433
    28 Jan 05, 19:58
    Net profit in 3Q FY2005 rose 25.2 per cent to S$23.9 million due to higher revenue and lower operating expenses and income tax expenses.

    Revenue grew 1.0 per cent or S$1.7 million to S$170.2 million as a result of SMRT TaxisÂ’ larger hired-out fleet, which offset lower fare revenue.

    Operating expenses declined 1.0 per cent or S$1.4 million caused by lower depreciation (net of amortisation of asset-related grant) charges and other operating expenses, which offset higher diesel costs.

    Income tax expenses were 26.9 per cent lower as the asset-related grant is no longer subject to tax and corporate tax rate was lower.

    In 4Q FY2005, while fare revenue is expected to remain stable, non-fare revenue will rise due to higher contribution from a larger taxi fleet as well as from upgraded and increased shop spaces at MRT stations. Operating expenses will increase mainly due to higher repairs and maintenance costs.

    On the redevelopment of shop spaces, the incremental rental revenue target of S$3.0 million for FY2005 will not be realised due to the delay in receiving approvals. However, SMRT remains confident of achieving the annualised projected rental revenue of S$10.0 million from the redevelopment.
