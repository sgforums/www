    Reasons to bring this up is to clear up a lot of doubts & too many are making use of laws to hurt the husband's or wife's. Be fair. I know this will raise a lot of issues.

    Many do not know how to survive the maintenance issue. Know the rules better for you.

    Each child monthly maintenance lowest is about $300. This amount is provided by both parents. 50% each. It's fair. For the women side, actually not a big amount to be expected. If she work during the family, expect about $100 or less. Reason being she can support herself & not being a burden of the husband in the future. If she did not work, then it's a case by case issue. Usually court will order her to go work. So women cannot just seat back & suck from her ex-husband's salary. This is fair also.

    I will throw some points here. I myself have sucessfully reduced maintenance 2 times from $600 per month to $250 per month for wife & 2 kids. How I manage to do that.

    First : Good record. I take my steps to do it. For the first year I pay faithfully $500 per month for everything. On date, not a single cent less or a single day delay.

    Second: Keep all receipt spend on kids, also the bank's record when transfer your maintenance.

    Third: Write down whatever your expense per month. Most forget to add in is to mainten your own parents, your insurance policy, your food, Hair cut, Clothes, phone bill, petrol or transport fee, PUB bills, medical bill is any, Dental, TV & radio licence fee, Town Council fee, Course fee if you are attending, Loans you are paying, Maintenance for current wife/girlfriend, savings for your own wedding (second marriage), add in a small amount for other misc items .... But everything must be logically sensible amount & best if supported with actual receipt (No Photostated)

    Fourth: if driving, downsize you car or state reasons why you cannot sell. Example market value too low would be selling with debt. State out how much. Best if some car re-sale company can offer a written letter to estimate your vehicale. Or it's a company needed car. Or best still ride a bike. Myself ride a small bike so there is no argument at all.

    Fifth: consolidate all informations on a written letter, (Hand written). Including your pay slip, CPF statment, Bank account book (Updated), whatever tax or expenses you paid, medical statement. State that your case is sincere & have been faithfully paying maintenance on time for the pass "X" months. You still loves you kids & will support them faithfully. Then go for the review. You must be reasonable in your case. Remember time is on your side. Don't go for review often, once per year it's ok. As long as you pay your maintenance faithfully your words weights more.

    What they may ask?
    Can you change a higher paid job? Can you reduce your expenses? What is the amount you can afford to pay? Prepare for all these questions. Think for your kids when answering these.

    Basically the maths is : Income - total logical expense = If greater then $150 per child you pay this amount, if smaller then you pay whatever you can.

    I have been ask weather I can go to work as a constructions worker. I told them I have medical reasons, I can go work but I request that my ex-wife be responsible for me if I am injure. Medical reason was confirmed my ex-wife.

    Hope I have raise a BIG issues.
