    I believe it is scientifically impossible to go back to the past. Not in terms of the technology but, logically, it cannot happen. The present is made through chain events of cause and effect. If something was changed, example, you going back into the past, by just stepping out of a time machine, you are already manipulating the cause (in the past) and thus the effect (the present) cannot happen. But to you, it already did (the present is also the past) and thus a whole dilemma is created.

    I remember my first insights into time travel was a Ray Bradbury story. Can't remember the title of the story but it involved a company who brings its customers back into the past to hunt extinct animals. One particular customer wanted to hunt a tyranosaurus rex (sp?). Anyway, he did something that he wasn't supposed to and when they returned to the present, the whole present had changed. And just because the guy accidentally killed a butterfly which he shouldn't have.

    My state is that time travel is NOT possible. That is unless the theory about many worlds (read His Dark Materials by Phillip Pullman), i.e. the existence of multiple dimensions, is true...
