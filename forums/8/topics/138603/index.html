    ever wonder why japan can provide very tip-top service?

    it is because of their mindset. to them even colleagues are their customers. so they make it an obligation to do their job well, as a 'service' rendered to their colleagues.

    since they give tip-top 'service' (work) they expect as much in return. so everywhere they go in japan they expect and receives good service.

    we, singaporeans, have a problem. we always equate salesman, customer service, front line people as 'service job'. we only criticise, hardly or never praise 'service' people. so much so nobody wants the job. and worst still, service jobs pay pittance and have odd working hours.

    ask yourself how often you see a singaporean appreciating good service? look at how japanese customers behave. they appear as polite as the person serving, sometimes it is difficult to tell who is the customer. it is exactly because of such appreciation that 'service' people continue to serve well or even better.
