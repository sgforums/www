    There are many different ways of crossing a cheque. The benefit of this is that it enables you, through your bank, to trace the path of the cheque through the banking system and to identify the person who finally presented it for payment. However, this procedure is often not possible with uncrossed cheque (cash cheque), which is why it's not advisable to write them.

    A cheque crossed with two parallel lines means that when the cheque is banked, it must be paid into the payee's account. The crossing however still allows the cheque to be negotiated and to be cashed at other branches of your bank, other banks or businesses.
