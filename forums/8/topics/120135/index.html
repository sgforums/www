    Frequently Asked Questions

    1. Is the embryo really human life?

    All doctors and scientists agree that humanbegins at conception, so even though the fertilized egg is still very tiny, a single cell in fact, it has all the genetic information it needs to grow into a baby and it is already growing and developing immediately after conception.

    2. Isn't abortion a religious issue?

    It is not merely a religious issue but a moral issue affecting all people. Just like stealing and killing is always morally wrong regardless of religion, so too is abortion as it kills a human life.

    3. But isn't abortion is legal in many countries?

    Yes it is legal but morally it remains wrong. Immoral actions that are legalized remains immoral.

    4. Why bring an unwanted baby into the world?

    The baby's new life was brought into the world when there was conception. Killing it remains immoral even though the baby is unwanted. We want you to consider other options.

    5. I am really not ready to be a mother, what can I do?

    We cater to the needs of expectant mothers so please call us (63399770 ) and let us try to help you.

    6. How can anyone really help me, besides my parents will kill me if they find out I am pregnant!

    No parent in the world has ever killed their daughter because of her unplanned pregnancy. We will discuss with you in upmost confidence the most helpful course of action that is the best solution in the circumstances. We understand how you feel.

    7 I am not sure if I am pregnant, but if I am, what are my options?

    Call us and we will arrange a confidential pregnancy test, and also discuss your options in confidence. There is an alternative to abortion and we can help you.

    8. What are the effects of abortion?

    Not merely wanting to scare you but it is found that besides the Post Abortion Distress Syndrome , there is the increased risk of Breast Cancer and Subsequent Premature Delivery, and also other medical risks. (See Article)

    Campaign aims to aid teens with unplanned pregnancies

    SEVEN community agencies have come together to launch a nationwide campaign to help troubled teenagers facing unplanned pregnancies.
    .
    Called Babes, it aims to offer shelter, guidance and counselling, and also to provide medical services as well as post-natal follow-up. These teens can also SMS to seek help.
    .
    The seven agencies are Beyond Social Services, Andrew and Grace Home, Rose Villa, KK Women's and Children's Hospital, Tanjong Pagar Family Service Centre, Family Life Society and aLife.
    .
    Said NMP Eunice Olsen, Babes' spokesman: "I think for a lot of girls who realise that they're pregnant, the first thing that will come to mind is fear and guilt. I have come into contact with them in my work. More often than not, they would resort to drastic measures.
    .
    "Babes is coming in to tell them: Look, this has already happened, the mistake is done. But this is the help that is available to you. So, I think what Babes is trying to do is to help them through that process."
    .
    Every year, an average of five babies are abandoned in Singapore.
    .
    According to the Ministry of Community Development, Youth and Sports, a quarter of callers to the National Pregnancy Helpline between February and December last year were teens.
    .
    Teens also make up 66 per cent of single mother cases referred to the medical social work department of KK Women's and Children's Hospital in the first eight months of last year. The Health Ministry's records also show that between 1998 and 2004, an average of 1,574 teens had abortions every year.
    .
    Posters carrying the helpline number and the SMS service will go up at 45 MRT stations from Thursday.
    .
    Those who need assistance can call the National Pregnancy helpline at 1800-6868 623 or text a message to 8111 3535. Â— NewsRadio 93.8, Channel NewsAsia
