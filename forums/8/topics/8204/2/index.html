    Hmm...Quality Post (QP)~~

    The contents of a Quality Post should, first and foremost, bear relevance to the initial topic of the thread. Yes yes...while I realise that it is easy to sidetrack when addressing a certain forumite (esp when both are of the opposite sex) during the course of the exchange of ideas, please note that everything written can be read by others who are probably not interested at all (ie...spare a thought for them). For personal messages may I direct such individuals to ICQ, IRC or even the time-tested email programs.

    Secondly, the post should be written in a clear, unambigious and concise manner. Perfect grammer, spelling and sentence structure is not necessary (I mean we have school already, no need to torture ourselves outside of school hrs) Such features in a post are certainly plus points (not to mention its intimidation and "whoa" factors), but most of us can get by without it. So long the idea gets through, that's all that matters. This may sound a little contradictory, but in practice we do it more often than we realise.

    Thirdly, a QP should have an OPINION. Yes, meaning "yes, I agree", or "no, I disagree", plus supporting facts and figures. You see, a discussion cannot really flow with everybody sitting on the fence. Alot of us are rather shy to voice out our opinions, probably for fear of sounding silly or thinking that such opinions are unoriginal. As long as it was done in good taste and it is constructive, I do not think that anybody would mind it at the end of the day.

    Next, we should note that a QP addresses the opinions and not the forumite as an individual. The line between the forumite and that of his/her opinion is seperated by a rather thin line, and it is very easy to cross that boundary, which may give rise to the dreaded (and unfortunate) phenomonon called "Flamming". While I admit that such threads can be quite entertaining, I do feel that the forums would be a much better place without it.

    Now we move onto the minor things that give rise to a QP, starting with the length. There may be many different schools of thoughts on this one, but I think that the best length is one that fits onto the computer screen nicely. There are several reasons for this; If the post is shorter than this length, chances are it may not score well in terms of content and depth, while any length longer than that would discourge all but the most hardcore forumite to read through the whole thing. That would mean all of your effort in typing out that marvelous opinion of yours may just go to a waste, now you would not want that to happen right? Of course this is just a general rule of the thumb, feel free to disregard in times of emotional duress or when one has achieved a certain level of mastery in the English language.

    Humour and wit, while not important ingredients of a QP, do add much needed flavour and colour to a post. Of course I have to stress, only attempt humour if you are good at it, else you would certainly fall flat on your face trying (which in a way, gives rise to another form of humour that has its roots in the realm of cheese..duh).

    Just thinking out loud

    okok...if you have managed to read to this point, give yourself a pat on your back for preserverance and now would be a good time to think of doing something better with your life hehe
