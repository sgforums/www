    Further tests being conducted on Singaporean man tested positive for SARS - By Walter Fernandez
    http://www.channelnewsasia.com/stories/singaporelocalnews/view/48585/1/.html


    A Singaporean man at Singapore General Hospital has tested positive for SARS. The patient has been moved to Tan Tock Seng Hospital - the frontline hospital in the SARS battle.

    But several more tests are being done on Monday to confirm this preliminary diagnosis. In the past, an initial positive result has sometimes proven to be a false alarm. The Health Ministry said the patient had been isolated at Tan Tock Seng Hospital, and contact tracing has begun.

    The Ministry believes this is an isolated case and said it was caught quickly because of Singapore's high level of vigilance.

    A media conference with the Health Minister has tentatively been scheduled for Tuesday. Singapore's hospitals are also stepping up their precautions. The Ministry said affected wards at Singapore General Hospital have been closed to visitors.

    If this suspect case is confirmed, it will be Singapore's first SARS case since early May. Experts have been warning that SARS might return towards the end of the year - in the winter months. But it is understood the Singapore man has not been to China any time recently.
