    Basically, there are a few things about handphones.

    Size, weight, battery lifespan, reception and functions

    Let's assume first that you just want a phone. That's it. To make phone calls and such.....

    Nokia phones generally have the best battery lifespan, since the days of the Nokia 8110, our old fave "banana" phone. Which other brand gives u 5 hours of talk-time? Nokia phones are also the most user-friendly and have the most functions and got the most nice sounding melodies.

    However, Nokia phones suffer from a rather well-known flaw. They're the real fragile phones. The LCD display of a Nokia 6110 is well-known to screw up if u drop it and the phone is not protected by a cover. Likewise for the Nokia 3210.

    Ericsson phones has the advantage of being melody-programmable. U can program just about any song in it. Besides the infamous lid problem of the 768 and the 788, the 377,388,628 and the 688 are quite hardy, with the VERY old 377 being the best of the lot. Though apparantly Ericsson solved the lid problem with the new T18 and T28.

    Let's move on to my phone, the Motorola StarTac X. The StarTac X is the joint 3rd lightest handphone in the market, after the v3688 and the Ericsson T28s, tied with the Philips Genie at 95g. Even the Nokia 8810 weighs 120g.

    Contrary to popular belief and stated by some earlier posts, the StarTac X does NOT suffer from any fragile problem. the StarTac 70, 80 and 90 does, but not the X. The X is DEFINITELY more "drop-proof" than any Nokia handphone. In fact, my X has actually flew and whacked a car (accidentally of course) and nothing happened. My X was bought in November 1998 in its early days and it's still in great condition.

    Though I must admit that all Motorola phones have terrible limited functions and are not user-friendly. They also suffer from the common problem of not being able to tell you the date and time of your missed calls. However, they do (from the X onwards) have the great benefit of having a built-in vibrator. The L-series' vibrator is a bit weak compared to the X's and the v3688.

    Reception wise, the most powerful phone in the market is the Philips Genie. Without a doubt. I'm from the Navy and we "field-tested" just about every phone in the market out at sea, taking into account the different networks of course.

    The Genie wins hands-down, followed by the Nokia 6110. The StarTac X surprisingly comes quite close after them. The Nokia 8810, 3210 on the other hand is terrible. A disgrace to the great 6110. The 5110 is terrible too. Likewise for all the Samsung phones.

    How did we test? Simple. Out at sea, there is no network. We put all the phones together (same network) and approach land. The phone with the strongest reception will be able to receive the network first. So we look at the time discrepancy between the phones receiving the same network.

    This little experiment also taught us one thing. M1 is great in Singapore, but it SUCKS big time the moment u go to the coastal regions. Of course, everyone knows that M1 doesn't work in Tekong. Actually M1 doesn't work anywhere away from the mainland, except Sentosa.

    So what's the moral of the story?
    If you value weight and compactness, is willing to charge your battery everyday, get the StarTac X. If you want long battery lifespan, and are sure u won't drop your phone, get a 6110. The Motorola L-series is also worth a try. If you want style, get the 3210. The T28 and the v3688 are just not worth the money.
