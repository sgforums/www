A tale of a hawker and a kopi maker

  • Illegal Operation
    28 Jun 01, 15:18
    I checked my bank account last night. It turned out I had enough money to buy over Ah Beng's kopi-store...

    You see, for 25 years my laksa business grew with Ah Beng's kopi-store which I had faithfully stayed with. I would say that my business grew with his, and we never could do without each other. As I sold my bowls of laksa, so did Ah Beng, who sold more kopi.

    During the last few years Ah Beng's business took a turn for the worst because Starbeans opened opposite. Many preferred to ta bao laksa. The landlord wished to take Ah Beng's stall back. I make good otaks too, so it was suggested that I buy over his space.

    And so I checked my accounts, and I do have enough money to take over the stall...Ah beng is 85 this year. He bears strong sentiments to everything in the coffee shop. Before I left yesterday night, I spotted Ah Beng polishing this teah-tarick flask. It was probably my imagination but I saw his eyes water. It was a losing battle for ah beng. He's fighting against a higher authority, one that will never be in his control.
