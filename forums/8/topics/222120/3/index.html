    Most likely either a berry aneurysm at the base of brain or an arteriovenous malformation (AVM), which can occur anywhere on thye surface of the brain. The former is due to a weakness in the wall of the blood vessel whilst the latter is an abnormal formation of arteries and veins.

    Rupture can lead to rapid death and treatment has to be quick and not always effective. Treatment before it ruptures is much more successful but it is often silent, the only common symptom being epileptic fits in some (not all) patients. Yes, it does occur in young people and is one of the causes of sudden death in them. A berry aneurysm can be part of a syndrome and may be associated with hypertensiona and renal disease in patients with polycystic kidney disease. AVM is not associated with high blood pressure.
