    I don't know about bank loans but I know what your tuition fees will be approx 4 times of an American. You cannot work as a student especially from a rich country like Singapore, but if you are from poorer countries like vietnam, Indonesia or the like you can apply for a hardship work permit. You'll need to be pre qualified and verified you are eligible though.. You can work on campus as a student, but the max pay I've seen is like $8 an hour. That's not a lot.

    You cannot be found working off campus or you'll be deported and fined I think. You can do odd jobs like neighbour babysitting, lawn mowing, contract murders and heroin sales but I don't recommend the last two.

    One good thing you want to take note of especially if you're on a tight budget, watch the transportation service in the destination city. America wasn't built for buses but for cars. If you plan to own a car, be prepared to pay like $120 minimum per month for insurance, and that's for the most basic policy. That was one consideration we failed to think of before I came here and it's costing me $480 for 6 months.

    Getting cell phones, anything that usually comes with a bill is not easy as in Singapore. You need a history of credit, to get credit you apply for a credit card, which you also need to have credit to apply for. Telephone line, apartment deposits all usually include a check of your history by their financial people so be prepared for higher interest rates or total denial of service.

    Good luck amigo.

    24 Jul 01, 21:43
    If you need to borrow money to cover your entire expenses in the US, I'll suggest you look for alternatives like SMU, distance learning courses or Australian Us. All these cost a fraction of what a US education will cost. Trust me, you will not want to be heavily in debt right after you graduate from college.

    The estimated cost of about $25k (May I know which college?) will not remain stagnant. Tuition and housing rents are guaranteed to increase on an annual basis. The extent of increment will depend on which city you are talking about.

    With an F-1 visa (Student visa), it is illegal for the holder to work off-campus. On campus work is permitted. But bear in mind that on-campus jobs are very very competitive.

    As for the off-campus work after 9 mths of residency as mentioned by delerium, correct me if I'm wrong, you only have a total of 12 months to work. You will want to use this for summer internships when you are in Junior/senior years; and after graduation before your employer starts the process for a H-1 visa (working visa) or green card.

    Having said all these, there's one most important point. You have to ask yourself if you can bear the stress of studies, worrying about financial problems, working part time on-campus and being away from family. If you think you can, by all means, come to the US. Where there's a will, there's a way.
