    1 Savour seafood Singapore-style, including the spicy Chilli Crab, Black Pepper Crayfish and Butter Prawns for an unforgettable, lip-smacking meal. (Then head straight for the public toilet nearby to commences the Bombing of Hiroshima.)

    2 Check out the unique hotels in Singapore - from the hip Gallery Hotel with its distinctive postmodern decor to Hotel 1929, a boutique hotel in Chinatown known for its funky interiors and vintage chairs. (Then check into the most talk about Hotel 81 in Geylang.)

    3 Pamper yourself at the one of Asia's biggest outdoor spas, Spa Botanica and indulge yourself to a massage in a lush garden setting. (Or pamper yourself at one of the AsiaÂ’s smallest indoor spas and indulge yourself to an Exotic Massage in romantic setting.)

    4 Try your hand at porcelain painting and create your own Chinese masterpieces at Ming Village. (Or try your luck at Toa Payoh Town Central for some Friendly Version of Computer Software and Video CD.)

    5 Tee off at night, under the flood-lit fairways at Jurong Country Club or Orchid Country Club. (Or Face Off your friends during the with a friendly war game of Paint Balls at Orchid Country Club.)

    6 Hitch a wild ride on the amphibious half-boat, half-truck craft on a Ducktour and splash into the harbour after a city tour. (Then rescue the Taxi Driver who jump off from the Shears Bridge.)

    7 Snap a picture with one of Singapore's most photographed personalities, the doorman at Raffles Hotel. (Then pull the ear of the person beside you and ask him/her to guess the color of the Turban.)

    8 Restore your ying and yang, or brave some of more exotic delicacies at the Imperial Herbal Kitchen, including deep fried scorpions and ants. (Deer penis is available too upon special request.)

    9 Party till the wee hours of the morning at Singapore's premier clubbing zone, Mohamed Sultan Road. (Walk along the road to see local clubbers imitating their National Symbol of The Merlion.)

    10 Get face to face with cheetahs in their enclosure on a special zoo tour. Or board a buggy for a personally-guided tour of what goes on behind the scenes at the Singapore Zoological Gardens! (Then face to face with Singaporean in their Mass Rapid Transit during peak hours.)

    11 Swim with the pink dolphins at Sentosa and experience the thrill of being a dolphin trainer for a day. (Then join the protest against their ill treatment.)

    12 Dive with the Sharks at the Underwater World and meet a world of sea creatures including the rare dugong, or sea cow. Even non-certified divers can take part on this fun-filled activity! (Then enjoy the famous shark fin soup at any Chinese Restaurants nearby.)

    13 Discover life in Singapore during the turbulent World War II years. Visit Changi Chapel and Museum, Johore Battery, Reflections at Bukit Chandu and Fort Siloso on Sentosa. (Discover unique personality most widely known as Half Carrot at Changi Village during weekends nights.)

    14 Learn how to be a falconer and handle birds of prey at the Jurong BirdPark. Or have a hearty breakfast beside a flock of pink flamingoes! (Then buy some Bird Nests from any Chinese Medical Shop nearby.)

    15 Catch a glimpse of the elusive Malayan Water Monitor lizard, which can grow up to 2 meters, at the Sungei Buloh Wetlands Reserve. (Then catch a glimpse of the aggressive crocodiles which can grow up to 4 meters at the Singapore River.)

    16 Enjoy the highest high tea in Singapore at the Equinox on the 69th storey of Swissotel The Stamford and be rewarded with breathtaking views as you tuck in. (Then enjoy the breathtaking views again at the lowest mountains in Asia known as Mount Faber.)

    17 Sample the fiery fish-head curry served with rice on a banana leaf and have a feast the traditional way - with your hands! (Consult Medical Advice at any Poly Clinic nearby for Blistering Fingers cause by the hot food.)

    18 Indulge in retail therapy on Orchard Road, Singapore's favorite shopping street, and pick up everything from premium apparel brands and Southeast Asian antiques to the latest electronics and kitschy souvenirs! (Keep a look out for the blacklisted shop in that area too.)

    19 Visit the world's largest fountain at Suntec and discover how you can benefit from feng shui, the ancient art of geomancy, which is integrated into its design. (Then visit the smallest village at Holland Village which became a very popular TV Series in Singapore.)

    20 Go shopping at 4am at Singapore's 24-hour mall, the popular Mustafa Centre and choose from over 120,000 products! (You will also get to smell over 120,000 type of spices and perfumes too.)
