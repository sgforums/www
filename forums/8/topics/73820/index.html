    Originally posted by thoreldan:
    Got a new job, starting in late april. Reservist coming late May. Wish to defer because just start new work and got probation period. I know i need some letter from my employer. How do i defer? report for first day then go and see my S1?
    Get a letter from your employer stating your name and company name and that you have started work on XXX date and will be on probation on XXX month.

    After that you need to write/type a cover letter.

    a. Address the letter to the S1 of your unit.

    b. Clearly state your rank/name and NRIC

    c. Also state the ICT date which you are trying to defer for eg 1st Apr to 21st Apr.

    d. State that you are seeking deferment and it might help if you say that you really need the job.

    e. Sign the letter and fax the letter and your employer's letter to the S1 office

    f. It might help if you immediately follow up with a phone call to make sure you receive the fax. Get the Rank and Name of the person who acknowledged receipt of the fax. Sometimes SAF offices can be quite chaotic and you don't want your letter to be lost.

    g. And then follow up with another call two or three working days down the road to make sure they are looking at your case

    h. If it is "approved", they will send you a letter stating that your ICT is defered. Please keep the letter and do not depend on their word only to avoid getting charge for AWOL.

    i. State in your letter that you had "attached" the employer's letter to your cover letter.

    Typically for NSmen starting on a new job they will defer and starting new business, new born, recently married, compasionate reason or urgent/important work assignment.

    I think you have a good case. Please do the above and good luck!

    From:

    ex-NSF clerk
