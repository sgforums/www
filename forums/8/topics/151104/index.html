    IRAS collects S$17.9b in taxes for FY 2004/05
    By Loh Kim Chin, Channel NewsAsia

    SINGAPORE : Contributions from property tax grew nearly 28% for the financial year 2004/05, compared to a year ago.

    According to the latest annual report of the Inland Revenue Authority of Singapore (IRAS), property tax amounted to S$1.7 billion.

    It cited the lapsing of property tax rebates for commercial and industrial properties from January last year for the increase.

    The brisk property market also resulted in a near 10% rise in Stamp Duty collection to S$815 million.

    Due to an increased Goods and Services Tax (GST) rate, GST contributions grew 17% to S$3.5 billion.

    Contributions from Income Tax and Goods and Services Tax amounted to S$13.5 billion or 75% of total tax revenue collection.

    Estate Duty collection nearly doubled to S$357 million.

    Meantime, collection from income tax came in at almost S$10.1 billion, up 3% on year.

    As a whole, IRAS collected taxes amounting to S$17.9 billion, an 8.6% increase.

    This represents 65% of the total government operating revenue and 10% of the Gross Domestic Product.
