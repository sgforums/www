    Guys,

    Jamie Han never called Lee Kuan Yew a despot.

    I thought somebody will eventually bring this up, so I waited and keep quiet. But I see so many people keep getting the impression he called LKY a despot and nobody seem to correct this.

    Please read the Straits Times articles carefully. Jamie Han was suggesting that no matter how enlightened a despot is, without checks and balances he will be a tyrant.

    Jamie Han was talking about despots in general, he was not saying that LKY is a despot.

    Read the ST article on the Wed, 2 Feb. Jamie Han stated that he didn't direct his comments personally at LKY, at no time.

    Read the ST article on the Tue, 1 Feb. Please point to me where exactly Jamie Han called LKY a despot?

    MM LKY himself at that time, he returned to the point about 'despots' only later, in reply to another question from another person. LKY said he is not a despot.

    So ladies and gentlemen, Jamie Han didnt say LKY is a despot. He was talking about despots in general. LKY, in response to another question then mentioned about despots and said he is not one.

    Whether Jamie Han is irreverent is up to you all to decide. What Im concerned about, is why some people here seem to get the idea that Jamie Han called LKY a despot.

    If u all say Jamie Han is irreverent, it's ok to me. But if some people get the idea that he is irreverent because he called LKY a despot, then they misunderstood, and I want to clarify.

    Jamie Han never called LKY a despot.

    Directly, Jamie Han didnt say it. He didnt say "LKY you are a despot." or anything like that. So if anyone of u think he directly said it, you misunderstood.

    How about indirectly? Was Jamie Han indirectly implying that LKY is a despot?

    Did Jamie Han's action of bringing up the issue of despots and the need for checks and balances, in front of LKY, mean that he is saying LKY is a despot?

    Please remember, Jamie Han was discussing and participating in an intellectual exchange in a University in a public setting/ forum. He mentioned about despots in general and the need for checks and balances. This was a discussion, a suggestion, an expression, an idea. This was all in the setting of an intellectual exchange. He didn't mean to imply that LKY is a despot himself and personally attack him. This is where many people here get it wrong and get the wrong idea.

    Please read ST article dated Wed, 2 Feb. He mentioned clearly himself, at no time was he directing his comments personally at LKY. Please also read the Tue, 1 Feb ST article about the exchange.

    After reading those articles, u can see clearly he was not directly or indirectly calling LKY a despot. I couldn't understand why some people think otherwise. This is a misunderstanding and is baseless.

    If u all want to know more, go to ST website and read, or watch CNA this Sat. (if he is shown)
