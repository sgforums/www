    Kallang National Stadium to be torn down, rebuilt as new integrated sports centre

    SINGAPORE : Monday was the last time the National Stadium was used for the National Day Parade.

    The 31-year-old stadium will be torn down and rebuilt as an integrated sports centre.

    The National Stadium has been home to many important events over the past few decades.

    Hundreds of thousands of Singaporeans have celebrated spectacular National Days there.

    And various regional sports meets and soccer matches have been played on its grounds.

    For sportsman Yeo Kian Chye, it is a treasure trove of memories.

    He represented Singapore in the 100-metre sprint during the Southeast Asian Peninsula Games in 1973 at the National Stadium.

    "This is first place I ran in front of capacity crowd in 1973. Although I lost, did not perform very well, very disappointed but I come back. I feel very sad that this place is going to be torn down," he said.

    This year's National Day Parade will be a swansong for the stadium as it is due to be demolished and rebuilt as a state-of-the-art sports centre.

    Construction of the stadium began in 1965. It was completed eight years later.

    The first National Day parade held there was in 1976, when 60,000 people turned up to celebrate Singapore's 11th birthday.

    The reconstruction of the new stadium will be carried out by private developers and it is estimated to take four years. - CNA
