    Another man jailed for sham marriage
    A 38-YEAR-OLD dispatch rider became the second man this week to earn a jail term for accepting money to marry a Vietnamese woman so that she could continue to stay on in Singapore.

    Chee Tuck Phak is at least the sixth man to be taken to court for entering into a marriage of convenience with a Vietnamese woman.

    He was sentenced to six months' jail and ordered to pay a penalty of $2,300 yesterday after admitting that he was rewarded with $1,050 for marrying Nguyen Bich Thuy.

    He received the money from a person called Low Soo Kai at a coffee shop in Hougang Avenue 3 on July 2 last year.

    Three other corruption charges involving $1,250 were considered during sentencing.

    Chee, who has a child by a common-law wife in Batam, was given until next Thursday to sort out his affairs before he starts his jail term.

    On Tuesday, Lee Kim Leow, 45, unemployed, was jailed for six months and ordered to pay a penalty of $3,500 after he admitted twice receiving $500 from Nguyen Anh Ngoc to marry her in name only and sponsor her application to extend her social visit pass in December 2001 and January 2002.

    He got the bribes from her through an unknown Vietnamese woman. Five other charges were considered.

    In yesterday's case, Deputy Public Prosecutor Muhamad Imaduddien told the court that Thuy was one of seven Vietnamese women arrested in Guillemard View by anti-vice officers on Aug 4 last year.

    Investigations showed that Low, who was also arrested, had arranged for four Vietnamese women to enter into sham marriages with Singapore men.

    Low told Chee in a Geylang pub in May last year that he could earn some money if he married a Vietnamese woman.

    Chee agreed, knowing he would not be allowed to have sex with the woman.

    He was paid after he registered the marriage with Thuy at the Registry of Marriages on July 2 last year and sponsored her application to extend her visit pass.
