    depends lar if u a modder and hard core gamer then a gaming machine is never about cost saving...but if u know how to bargain or get 2 know the store runner then maybe can get cheap cheap. those who got experience in using comp parts will know which manufacturer parts and boards go well with one another...if not go get a bundled PC off the stores OEM be it Dell, Acer or blah blah blah. ask me i say the standard gaming rig should have 512 - 1024 MB DDR/ DDR2 ram, a mainboard recognised for gaming (e.g asus, gigabyte, MSI etc), minimum SATA HDD/ western digital, ATI Radeon or nVidia geForce graphics card, a stable PSU of minimum 400 watts and up, Intel or AMD processor of over 2 gig CPU speed, though most advance users will go for AMD processors.

    my gaming rig was assembled last November and it has been quite a while since i followed up on comp hardware. know that intel and AMD have released new boards and processors with DDR2 RAMs but ain't not inerested coz heard that performance wise still not much of a leap. even Longhorn has been delayed with only the release of service pack 2.

    but anyway if u r really in to gaming i guess the graphics card will be ur major investments coz newer games are tested and take the GPU as the platform to better gaming experiences. while if you are gaming online, on top of the hardware spec/s u will need a good internet connection to achieve low pings(maybe 512 BB unlimited), location and maybe an ethernet router instead of USB modem.

    ...and before i forget...most CRT monitors should be okay for gaming
    but if you are getting a LCD, look out for the response time coz a high response time may cause your PC to hang in a graphics demanding game.

    as for what are the parts needed, you can ask the shop for advise. i recommend you go to hardwarezone forum to look for ideas. personally, if you're looking for a average costing pc that performs well, these are my recommendation:

    cpu and motherboard: amd64 3000+ and abit kv8pro $460
    graphic card: sapphire 9800pro $390
    ram: kingston 512mb $160. go for 1gig if u have $$

    these are the three most important parts. as for the rest like harddisk, sound card, casing, roms etc, its up to your own budget. on average should be around $1200-$1500 for this power gaming machine.
