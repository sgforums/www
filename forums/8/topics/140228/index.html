    placing is most important i guess.. thats something my dad taught me whenever i play with him.. hes a pool shark. trust me, i have a pool table at home and i play until sian, dont get me wrong either.. im not top notch at pool but im sure i'll put up a challenge.

    IMO.. i would seperate pool into 4 components, mainly,

    1.stroke
    2.placing
    3.accuracy
    4.sequence

    1. stroke is very important, it helps make your shots accurate, so that you wont miscue etc.

    2. placing is mainly where your white ball will go after making a shot, therefore, to have good placing, you will be able to run the game in your favour even though you have more balls to clear on the table.

    3. accuracy as it speaks for itself is whether you can get the balls into the pocket. as for me, i use feeling and a technique called "ghost ball". Ghost ball is a technique where it will be 100% correct as long as you hit the exact spot, it will help people in making shots i guess.. and etc.. i find that a problem that stops me from getting balls in all the time is not hitting the right spot althought i know where the right spot is, i know it is not the stroke and i guess you need to have very good judgement.

    4. sequence IMO is which ball to hit first, how you should try to clear the game and win.
