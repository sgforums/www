    I visited Singtel website and got this message.

    Dial-up Internet users please note: Incidents of modem jacking
    Please check that you are connected to your local Internet Service Provider each time you dial into or are being re-connected to the Internet.

    There have been worldwide reports of fraudulent IDD calls made via an Internet connection. This happens when a dial-up customer is prompted to download a file or has a programme downloaded automatically when visiting certain websites. The programme could create a dialer in your PC which may be used to make unauthorised IDD calls when you are online, without your knowledge.

    Some preventive steps you can take:

    If you hear your modem re-dialling to the Internet, check that the connection is made to your local ISP. If you are not sure, terminate the connection and re-dial if necessary.
    Exercise caution when prompted to download and install files from any website.
    Subscribe to a Phone Lock* service, which will allow IDD calls to be made only through a PIN code.
    Broadband customers who use a modem to access the Internet should also exercise the same precaution.

    For more information, please visit www.singnet.com/tou/misc/fraudidd.asp.

    * SingTel is offering its customers a month's free usage of its Phone Lock service upon application. Promo ends 31 December 2004.
