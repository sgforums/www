When I turned around, I realised it was the cover of a lamp that had fallen down... and it actually hit the head of an old man... Why is this happening if there is regular maintenance?

    4) Irregular bus arrival timings - leading to extremely long waits and empty buses trailing one another...

    5)..........

    Regarding Point (1), it's actually a case of fraud... Read the following analogy:

    --------------------------------------------------------------------------
    Signboard: Coffee - 60 cents per cup

    You: Auntie, coffee one cup.
    Auntie: Okay... 60 cents please...

    You hand over 60 cents to the auntie.
    After a while, the auntie returns with a cup...

    Auntie: Here your coffee.

    You take a look at what is in the cup...

    You: Auntie! I want coffee. Why you give me tea?!
    Auntie: No more coffee lah. Take it or leave it. You paid for it already.
    You:......

    You are speechless. No refund for getting the wrong order. No other coffee stalls in sight. You either drink the tea or walk far away to another place to buy coffee and then pay for it again.
    -------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Is this a case of fraud? Pay for coffee which is more expensive and then get forced to accept tea... This is exactly the case for aircon buses with spoilt aircon! You pay the fares for an aircon ride but you don't get the aircon! Same scenario right?! In the end, you either finish the ride and suffer in silence or get down the bus and wait for another one, which means you have to waste time and money again....
