    SINGAPORE : Singapore turns 39 on Monday with a big party at the National Stadium.

    For those without tickets, there are six locations where they can catch the parade on big screens.

    They can head down to Marina Promenade, Stadium Waterfront, Fort Canning, Creative Technology HQ, Singapore Discovery Centre or the huge field next to Simei MRT.

    Also going out 'live' on MediaCorp, the parade can even be enjoyed via the Internet.

    Unlike previous years, the parade on webcast gives viewers an additional angle this year for a panoramic experience.

    Viewers can see up close what is happening on the field and the stage.

    The webcast starts from 5pm, giving viewers a glimpse of the pre-parade festivities.

    For the first time, the webcast will be available right up till November.

    The team behind this is finetuning the streaming so viewers can catch a smooth show.

    They hope to double the 24,000 PCs which caught the webcast last year.

    Major Ng Hock Sing, Chairman of Website and Feedback, said: "For this year, we are using two data centres instead of just one - Web Vision and One Band - so for local audiences there will be better bandwidth and they will be able to see a smoother video."

    "We have linked up with service providers in various other countries, like US, UK, Australia, China, Taiwan, so that overseas surfers if they log in to see our videos from these countries, they should be able to get a much smoother video with less interference."

    This year's SMSs and MMSs have also made their way into the parade and onto the big screen.

    During the preview parade, some 1,000 SMSs and 400 MMSs were received. - CNA
