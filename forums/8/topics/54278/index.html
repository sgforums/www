    If I remember correctly, the explanation goes this way: Man has three bodies; the astral body, the physical body and the etheric double. The astral body (yes... the center of one's consciousness and the one that is capable of astral travel) is connected to the physical body with a silver cord. Death ensues when the silver cord that connects the astral and the physical body is severed. The etheric double is a layer of energy that surrounds the physical body from the time the physical body forms. In an average adult, it is a roughly 2 inch layer of energy surrounds the physical body and is an exact duplicate of it. Upon physical death (severance of the silver cord), the astral body is recycled into a new consciousness (reincarnation) and the physical body, being merely an organic shell, returns into its simpler chemical elements (decay and decomposition). The etheric double, however, exists as energy and has an existence completely separate from either the physical or astral body. It exists as energy and eventually dissipates itself. In the meantime though, while the etheric double has not dissipated, it will continue to perform the activities that the physical body used to do. The etheric double, while it has no intelligence, has habits -- due to its "close association" with the physical body. Therefore, if the physical body used to go to the same office for the past twenty years, the etheric double will continue the "habit" of going to that same office every day -- just as the physical body used to. That is, until it dissipates itself. Existing as energy, certain parts of the physical body, in the process of dissipating itself, transforms into other forms of energy -- thermal, photochemical, electromagnetic, and mechanical. This partly explains the occurrences of hot and cold spots (thermal) in haunted houses, pictures of ghosts and apparitions (photochemical), poltergeists (electromagnetic and mechanical) or any combination of these forms of energy interfering with those naturally occurring within our environment.
