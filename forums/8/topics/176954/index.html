    I think one common misconception that many people have is that to help someone in need of blood, you need to find people with the same/compatible blood type to donate blood.

    This is not the case.

    Anyone with any blood type can go donate, and specify that you'd like to help this particular patient.

    What the blood bank will do is to release a pack of compatiable blood (in this case, B or O) to the patient in need, once it receives a replacement pack from a donor, and it doesn't matter what blood type the donor donates.
