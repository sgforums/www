    Want to post Reach? Register first

     

    NEW REALITY:  Mr Lee at the Lianhe Zaobao event yesterday to celebrate the Chinese daily's 90th anniversary. PHOTO: LIANHE ZAOBAO

     

    Citizens who wish to post on the website of the Government's feedback arm, Reach, must log in and register from next month, Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong said yesterday.

    This comes in the context of the Government's "New Media rules" which include requiring news websites that exceed a certain readership to be licensed and requiring news and current affairs sites not to receive foreign funding.

       Mr Lee was addressing some 300 guests at an event at Singapore Press Holdings to celebrate Lianhe Zaobao's 90th anniversary.

       Rules and norms have yet to catch up with the new reality of the media, he said. To take full advantage of the new media environment, Singapore needs to build a secure and responsible online space to encourage a constructive exchange of views.

       Mr Lee listed three problems that have arisen as a result of the rise of social media - hacking, cyber-bullying and trolling.

       The new rules will protect responsible users from those who abuse cyberspace, he added.

       "In Singapore, we see much constructive engagement online, but also no lack of trolling," he said. "We must fight back against such trolling, and provide a safe and responsible online space, which promotes constructive participation."


    News, The New Paper, Saturday, November 23 2013, Pg 4

    Hacking a serious offence: DPM Teo

     

    The recent spate of Singapore websites getting hacked should not be taken lightly, Deputy Prime Minister Teo Chee Hean (right) said yesterday.

    Hacking "cause inconvenience to others and... sometimes serious disruptions to services which are important to many people in the country", he said.

       He was speaking to the media on the sidelines of a parenthood event at the Pasir Ris Elias Community Club.

       On Wednesday, the websites of 13 schools, including Raffles Girls School (secondary), St Andrew's Junior College and Henderson Secondary School, were hacked by a person or group who signed off as "Jack Riderr".

       The Singapore Art Museum also said on the same day that the names, e-mail addresses and, in some instances, nationalities of 4,000 individuals on its online mailing list had been illegally published on a New Zealand-based website on Nov 5.

       The authorities said they were still investigating if the museum's website had been hacked.

       Mr Teo's who is also Minister for Home Affairs and Coordinating Minister for National Security, vowed to "take action against (the perpetrators) if we can find them", The Straits Times reported.
