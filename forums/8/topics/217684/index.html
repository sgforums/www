    I think to enjoy learning, you need to cast the net wide and make a paradigm shift. Essentially we tend to compartmentalise knowledge. BY not compartmentalising what you NEED to know, you open your mind to peripheral areas which CAN lead you back into what you need to know. And this time, when you look at it again, you look at it with a different perspective.

    i.e. Maths is boring. However, YOu like literature or history or nature. So, you look at some life stories of mathematicians (and be amazed that it can be so interesting), you read about how certain mathematics theorem can be applied to nature, i.e. symmetry ratio in flowers and plants etc.. how maths are used in code that are used in wars...read abt the impact of maths on people.

    OR you might love MATHS and find literature and history boring... then you draw relationships, compare, analyse, why does this action equal to this reaction? What equation happened, that resulted in this answer?

    Essentially, all knowledge is linked. The greatest scientist is an artist who creates new things, and in the art of a great artist is a mind who can perceive detail, translate the intangible into tangible.
