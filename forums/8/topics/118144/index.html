    IMO, it is true that having selfish attitudes will get you to somewhere but won't let you get far in terms of relationships with people.

    You may be successful, but in the end, you may lose your friends prior to your selfish attitudes. As a result, you become a lonely successful person. Will you be happy? Will you die happy?

    It is also true that one must be more concerned with his own needs and benefits. Nevertheless, one must not be afraid to share. Only the narrow-minded ones are afraid to share their success, their talents and some other things. They are afraid to lose. They are afraid to be overwhelmed. That's what makes them small people.

    Big people are the ones who are generous in their giving. They give with a smile in their hearts, and with that, they gain respect from people in the society. With respect, and a good character, it will accelerate oneself from glory to glory.

    On the whole, there're only two choices.
    - Would you want to gain respect by being generous and big-hearted?
    - Or would you want to be painfully selfish and lose everything in the end?

    Every man for himself. That's true. BUT! The reason why we are made differently is because we need each other. Humans are made to be interdependent. You may not have the talent that I have, and vice versa, because we are different. And because of this, the more we should share, our talents etc... while at the same time, minding our own business.
