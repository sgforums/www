    Speaker of Parliament and Jurong GRC MP Halimah Yacob has urged Singaporeans to stay home over the long National Day weekend from August 7-10.

    She hopes Singaporeans would take the time to celebrate together.

    Madam Halimah said, “My appeal to Singaporeans is to please stay in Singapore during the long weekend. I understand that Singaporeans take the opportunity during periods of long weekends to chill out with their families overseas.”

    “On other occasions, that’s all right but on this occasion I hope that they will stay in Singapore and really celebrate together as a nation. It will be very sad if a quarter of Singapore goes overseas to celebrate,” she added.

    She also hopes that the travel industry will support her call.

    “I know the travel business will be affected but I hope they will understand and see the reason why we are making this call. In Singapore, the government does not declare public holidays willingly, it is really something that they thought about carefully,” she said.

    “It is important that everybody come together and celebrate in their own way together with family, friends and neighbours and then really we have that real meaning of being part of a nation.”

    On Saturday (14 Mar), President Tony Tan declared that Singapore will have an additional holiday on 7 Aug (Friday), as part of SG50 “birthday celebrations”.

    With Monday already a holiday since National Day (9 Aug) falls on Sunday this year, Singapore will have a 4-day “Jubilee Weekend” from 7 to 10 August (Fri to Mon).

    President Tan also hopes that Singaporeans will take part in the many activities in Singapore, lined up by the government during the long “Jubilee Weekend”.

    In a Facebook posting, Education Minister Heng Swee Kiat likewise, tried to persuade Singaporeans to participate in the long-weekend activities in Singapore. He said that public attractions and facilities will have “special concessions” during the period.
