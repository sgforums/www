    WHAT THE JEHOVAH'S WITNESS BELIEVE:

    The Jehovah's Witness movement was started by Charles Tazze Russell. He formed a class for Bible study, which continued from 1870 until 1875. This Bible class of six members, which included his father, became the first nucleus group of Jehovah's Witness, and after a while it became one of the largest cults in the world.

    Jehovah's Witnesses are one of the most aggressive and fastest growing of all anti-Christian cults today. Much of the success of Jehovah's Witnesses is a result of their program of visiting homes and distributing literature. Their standard publications are the THE WATCHTOWER and AWAKE.

    It is important that we understand that the Jehovah's Witness organization is not another Christian denomination. It is an anti-Christian cult.
