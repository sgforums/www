    Government blocks web-site
    Days after news of the terrorists' arrest in Singapore, a suspicious web-site whose
    existence was exclusively revealed in Littlespeck, was blocked by government. It was
    recruiting able-bodied Malays as life-time "field operators" in Indonesia, Malaysia and

    Updated on Jan 14, 2002

    Original report "Intrigu'ing or Dangerous?"

    Six days after news of the terrorist arrests in Singapore, www.harimau.org (Malay for
    "tiger"), a suspicious recruiter of Malay "field operators" in Singapore, Malaysia and
    Indonesia, had its access blocked by the government.

    A check into the site showed the last update on its database took place on Jan 6, 2002.

    It announced on its main page that "the organisation had difficulty in accessing and
    updating Harimau's own website due to the tight security cordon being implemented."

    Carrying a postal address in Johor, the anonymous operators did not say which authorities
    had blocked its access - whether Singapore or Malaysia.

    However, readers can still read its previous contents. The only difference is that it cannot
    post new materials into it or remove old ones.

    The site still carries this headline on top: "Overthrow the PAP. Down with PAP's
    marginalisation and discrimination practices against all Malays."

    The new announcement continued: "Please keep visiting our web site as a show of
    support. The counter will record all web site hits. The counter is an indicator that Harimau
    still have your support.

    "Meanwhile may Harimau respectfully suggest that surfers visit www.fateha.com for news
    and comments on Malay/Muslim issues.

    "Harimau will resume maintaining its web page once Harimau has resolved certain issues
    that Harimau had with the Authorities that be.

    "Harimau condemns the terrorist actions carried out by United States Of America and its
    Allies on Afghanistan."

    In the context of modern day Asean cooperation, a visit into it makes intrigu'ing reading.

    Among its anti-Singapore articles, it openly appealed for able-bodied men (aged 19-35) to
    join a nine-month training at a base camp before being assigned to Harimau-linked Groups
    located in various countries.

    Applicants with military background will be highly considered, it added.

    It added: "It is almost certainly not recruitment to join a triad, but it has one clandestine
    characteristic of the Mafia when it declares that acceptance and membership are for life.
    'The organisation is your family'"

    Fateha.com

    We logged onto www.fateha.com that Harimau is diverting its readers to visit. It proclaims
    itself as a website for Singaporean Muslim news.

    One article posted on Jan 12 - the day access to harimau.com was blocked - condemned
    the detention of the 13 terrorist suspects under the Internal Security Act (ISA) a week ago.

    Most Singaporeans, it said, "have jumped at the revelation" (Not clear what is meant). The
    article went on:-

    "The 'revelations' are merely government claims... what is missing thus far, is the
    accused's defence.

    "It is worrying when the government is not made to defend its accusation. A system where
    an accusation is sufficient opens the way for abuse.

    "If the government is confident of its evidence, then it should allow for it to be challenged in
    court. If we agree that the government does not have to make a case, then why bother
    have the courts at all?"
