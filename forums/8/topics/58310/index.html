    This is my personal style...

    I think it's not really necessary for you to keep things too clean, just prevent it from getting dirty and most importantly CONTAMINATION. For the benefit of some, contamination actually means to the transfer of particle (in this case, dirty ones) from one source to another. So...

    1. Wash your hands before you touch your contacts. And after you've WASHED your hands, please don't anyhow touch any unneccesary and /or dirty surface.

    2. Don't leave your lens exposed to open area for too long especially under a dirty or dusty environment.

    3. Try to be gentle with your eyes. Though they've told you that putting contacts on won't cause much pain to your eyes, but that doesn't mean it won't cause damage. Having hurt your eyes just makes it more vulnerable to infection.

    4. Avoid using soap which might be too strong to wash your hands as such chemicals can be too strong and damaging to your eyes. Hence increase the chance of infection.

    5. Wash away the SOAP or whatever detergent solution you use for hands PROPERLY before you touch your contacts and eyes!

    6. Oil and grease is a BIG no no for the eyes. Plus, they can be really DIFFICULT to wash off properly. When your hands are contaminated with these, make SURE that they're PROPERLY washed off. And the solvent or whatever you've used to wash them off properly clear off too. Do NOT take chances with oil and grease as they can REALLY damage your eyes.

    Personally, I use a monthly disposable which I throw after a month. Washing of contacts is to rid of mainly protein & other debris contaminants which might be present.

    7. Avoid rubbing the lens too hard using your fingers as it would only risk damaging it. Not only that, it would also risk contamination of anything being transfer from your fingers. What I usually do is just to spray the washing solution over them. I only rubs them a bit if I find protein contaminants not being washed off it. ie. you can tell especially when your contacts are blue. If the contacts appear more misty or white in color, extra attention shall be needed.

    8. Do NOT use your nails. Try to use the tips of your fingers instead.

    9. Try to keep your nails short and CLEAN. If you have to keep your nails, make SURE that they're CLEAN during the wearing and washing process for contacts.

    10. Especially for females. If you have to apply anything to your nails, make sure that they ain't anything which may chip off easily. When you've applied your nail polish and you have long nails. Take the effort to check if your nails are clean as the nail polish make the dirt beneath the nails non-visible to the eyes.

    11. Make SURE your nails are CLEAN even if you don't use the nails touch your contacts. Solution can easily pass the contaminants over.

    12. Just before you put on your contacts. Take a while to examine it to make sure that it hasn't been chipped or has anything which is not suppose to be there to go along into your eyes. You might also wanna check the area around your finger tips.

    Last but not least, I usually don't change or wash my container until it is really dirty on the outside... But of course I'm not encouraging that! >
