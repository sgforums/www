    For Engieering subjects, the main difference would be, in poly, students are being taught on how to do something by examples, using formulas directly, learning stuff that is distributed, learning maths but no idea how it is applied on Engineering. In exams, just vomit what you have learned.

    In uni, you are taught the theory, the reasons behind each and every formula, you used. Each modules add up, meaning, you will used what you have learned in year 1, in year 2,3 and final year. In exams, the examiners will test on your understanding of the topic rather than testing on how well you can vomit the answers.

    I quote you my example, I gradutaed from poly in 1999. In my 3 years in poly, we were taught maths: Vectors, laplace transforms, etc but never really know what these maths are supposed to do.

    By the time, I came to Uni, ho ho, totally different story, you will learn that maths is an intergral part of engineering not like a subject on its own. You learned laplace transform so that it can aid you to solve electic circuit problems, etc.

    Originally posted by HanHao:
    can anyone give good details on how school life on these 2 schools differ?
    School life is not too different in terms of the freedom you are given, but a university syllabus (coupled with pre-university education) would naturally be more demanding, and would require more self-discipline when it comes to study.

    If you're not tight on finances and have grades good enough to enter university, then it is probably a safer ('kiasu') choice to take the university route. You'd spend more time and effort, but generally you could have a better headstart in your career with a degree than with a diploma.
