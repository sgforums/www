    There are reasons why no retailer can do this

    First of all, most electronic products, like your laptop, is protected under trademark law, thus any unauthorised access to the internal hardwares of a laptop will void the laptop. Also, before repairing a laptop, you have to bypass some password protection, like BIOS boot, which Courts has no way to access.

    Also, even if Courts suggest a partner-ship repair service with lets say HP. HP will never agree. No companies will agree. They will never authorise anyone to gain access to their computer's built legally.

    As for the mp3 your company sells, it is easy for you to send for repair because if a mp3 is broken, then it is borken. For laptops, problems can be caused by lots of different reasons, and thus the repair fee is at different price, even if under a warranty. Also, if the fee HP offered is too high, the customer might choose to repair his computer else where, and thus you cannot just mail a laptop for service. You have to bring it over since lots of decisions are to be made.
