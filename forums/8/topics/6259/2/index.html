    Well, there're both physical and mental aspects to falling asleep. For the physical part, that'll depend on your built-in biological clock ( comes with free batteries ). But you've gotta set it right gradually. For a start, don't force yourself to sleep if you're not tired. There's a limit to how far your body can go. At the first hint that your body is weary, head for the bed. Another thing you can try doing is, while brushing your teeth, give your tongue a brush too. That'll kinda remove food debris stuck on your tongue which can give you a dry-mouth unpleasant feeling that can hanper your sleep.

    As for the mental part, you've gotta find something that suits yourself. Most importantly, don't think of any undone tasks or worry about anything at all. Just lie comfortable, imagine yourself at the top of a mountain, lying down, where you can see nothing but just vastness of green hills all around, and hearing nothing but the howling of the cool winds. Get yourself into the drowsy state and you'll soon fall asleep. Try consciously not to feel conscious, if you know what I mean. Usually, once you get past the first hurdle, do it a few more times for the next few nights, and your biological clock will start functioning properly again. Good luck! =)
