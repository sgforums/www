    Diabetes' Causes
    Here's what is known about the cause of diabetes.

    * To develop diabetes, you need to have inherited the ability to get the disease.
    * Inheriting a single faulty gene alone isn't enough to cause the disease. Something in your environment must trigger it.
    * The triggers in the environment are different for the different types of diabetes.

    Researchers don't know exactly why some people develop diabetes while others don't. They do know that both genetics and environment play a role. To develop diabetes, you must have inherited the ability to get the disease. Genes don't make you get diabetes. They just make you more likely to develop the disease than someone who doesn't have them. In addition, something in your environment must trigger the disease.
