    This happened along Beach Road yesterday.

    I was from Concouse towards lavendar MRT station when I was approached by an indian man dressing shabbly. Before he could even speak to me, I held out my hand to indicate that I am not interested and quickly walked away.

    End of story. Then along the way I began to wonder why hadn't I stopped to listen. I certainly would if he was dressed in shirt and tie. It was what he wore and probably who he was. I must have concluded sub consciously that this guy, like many others I have encountered on the streets, stops passerbys to spin stories of getting lost, needing cash and stuffs.

    Was it then? I don't know. But before I could walk furthur, I stole a glimpse. This indian man was with a friend beside. They could have been lost and just asking their way... but like many others, I have turned them down even before they could ask.

    I have stereotyped them and that was really uncalled for.
