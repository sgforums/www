    i was looking at my "fish" tank. my father put it there 4 years ago when we moved into this house, he put those plastic stones, put the ceramic bridge, put fake seaweed, put the bubble thingy, put the night light.

    the 1 thing he didn't put, was fish. no fish. i dunno why but he always likes to have the bubble on, it's damn irritating, it creates this buzzing sound all day. and at nite he turns the night light on. who needs a night light? even fishes, if there were any, need to sleep at night.

    ya so anyway i was looking at the tank. i used to wonder if our tank was a mosquito or watever insect-that-lays-eggs-water would breed in there. but it always looked clean enough, i couldn't spot any larvae or stuff except for the occasional cluster of dirt.

    but then dunno why today so special. while admiring all those brown dirt there (which i just realised is probably the skin of the mosquito pupae) i saw this short 1 cm thingy swimming across the water. i dunno wat it was but it fascinated me. so i just kept looking and looking and looking. then i tried looking for more of them. i managed to find 1 more.

    so i decided to spray some baygon into the water.

    i was hoping to see them crawl and squeel in pain. but they were no where to be seen

    so i unplugged all the wires and brought the entire tank into the bathroom and poured away as much water as possible without the stones falling off. then i placed it on the sink and just watched.

    i saw 1 larvae swim to the surface and twist in pain. after a few minutes, i spot another one. it almost seemed like synchornised water ballet, it was a beautiful thing to watch.

    then i spotted another 2 more.

    that was a fun 20 minutes.
