    Singapore invites casino proposals

    SINGAPORE - Singapore on Wednesday invited investors to submit initial proposals for an integrated resort with gaming facilities, moving a step closer to building a casino in the city-state despite fierce opposition from religious groups.

    Singapore invites casino proposals

    In a briefing chaired by Dr Vivian Balakrishnan, Senior Minister of State for Trade and Industry, the government said investors will have until the end of February next year to submit their project outlines.

    Singapore will proceed with the venture and call for a 'formal' request for proposals only if the first-round proposals are 'compelling', the government said.

    Authorities expect the facility - if it is built at all - to have a mix of non-gaming attractions, with casino gaming revenues making a minority contribution to the resort's total revenue.

    Singapore currently collects S$1.3 billion in gaming duties a year from other legal gaming activities, which include betting pools and wagers on international soccer matches.

    Public debate on whether Singapore should have a casino has been raging for months now.

    Although the majority of Singaporeans say they have no reservations about gaming, a vocal minority has staunchly opposed allowing a casino in Singapore, fearing that it will spark a surge in vice, money-laundering and problem gambling. -- AP
