    by Lau Cheow

    Temasek Holdings recently announced that it will venture into the fashion and clothing business with a new line of clothes designed by its Executive Chairman, Mdm Ho Ching.

    Singapore Technologies has teamed up with The Gap to create a new clothing brand called The Pap, to cater exclusively for fashion conscious South-East Asians. The new line of clothes, inspired by Mdm Ho Ching's father-in-law, will be called The Classic as well as the Emperor's New Collection.

    The Classic line comprises all-white uni-sex shirts and pants, made from tough, abrasive white denim that can withstand high temperatures and rain. Windcheaters made out of a more friable material are specially designed for this range, and they come in many colours, most of them blue.

    It is the New Collection, however, that is drawing interest from fashion aficionados. Special material created by the Singapore Institute of Nanotechnology will be used to create a translucent, almost transparent fabric that is the basis for this new line of
    clothes.

    "The clothes are so delicate they virtually weigh nothing - and you could almost see through them, but of course you can't - it just gives the impression that there is nothing there," exulted Ms Boh Cheng Kor, a Singapore-based fashion expert.

    "Still," she added, "you are getting a bargain, just like with our politicians, you are getting nothing for a lot of money."

    The move towards transparency and openness is also in keeping with Temasek's corporate policy, as it strives to move into new untapped business lines.

    But will the clothes sell?

    Marketing experts are divided on the issue. Uncovering too much of one's hidden assets may be a problem, concurred political fashion consultant Ms. Tsao Keng. "Look what happened to NMP Steve Chia. This was a sad case of opening up too much and being exposed for it."

    Still, there are rumoured plans of the entire Cabinet turning out in the Emperor's New clothes during this year's National Day. "Now that would indeed be a flash of inspiration," said Ms. Boh.
