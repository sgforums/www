    Depends what the fines are "levied" for.

    Most of the time, when a new law comes out, there will be provisions on fines.
    At the same time, they will discuss where the fines will go to.

    Eg, if someone will be fined for speeding....
    it may be "pre-allocated" that part of the fine goes to funding anti-speeding campaigns, part goes to recovery of cost that lead to the person's speeding offence.

    Sometimes, fines may just go to "General Revenue", simply, extra money.

    If i am not wrong, Singaporea fines has always been payable to the department that issues the fine. Because some countries, your fines are payable to Ministry of Finance where the ministry will distribute it again to the department.
