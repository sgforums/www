    The attractions that stand out in SG include:

    1. SENTOSA
    2. ZOO
    3. BIRD PARK
    4. SHOPPING
    5. FOOD
    6. DISCOVERY CENTRE
    7. SCIENCE CENTRE
    8. HAW PAR VILLA (Closed down?)

    We also have a theme park in downtown east, historical attractions in Thiam Hock Thian and Old Chinatown, a touch of the arts in Esplanade and occasional badminton championships that attract World class players.

    Out of all these, perhaps the bulk of the revenue comes from food and shopping. And these are hardly enough to satisfy the govt, whose lack of of risk-taking and creativity in the past prevented SG from taking advantage of potential sponsers.

    In F1 racing, the country could have generated significant revenue from ticket sales and constructor's sponsership. Instead, it ended up in sempang, Malaysia.

    Now they choose to conservatively gloss over the socially controversial word 'casino' by calling it 'integrated resorts' in the hopes that it can kick-start a stagnent tourism industry.

    They should focus more efforts in enhancing our image as a food and shopping paradise instead of just depending on the 'great SG sale'. By lowering rents and offering more 'pasa marlam' style (with higher standard then those red-white tentages of course) and invite celebrated chefs to show case their qualities. Throw in a section to cater to shoppers and you may have a tourism spree unparalleled in the region. This will not only attract foreigners but locals as well and build on our reputation of a food/shopping attraction internationally.

    We've heard of theme parks. Why not have a similar concept with food and shopping?
