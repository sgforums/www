    Dong Zhi (Winter Solstice)

    The Chinese words Dong Zhi means 'arrival of winter.' Dong Zhi is the celebration of the good year one has had. In olden China, many poor people were not able to protect themselves from the bitter cold winter. Families would thus come together to eat bowls of warming tang yuan (glutinous rice balls). The Chinese word tang (meaning 'soup'), sounds like tuan which means reunion, while yuan means round and complete. Eating tang yuan on Dong Zhi therefore represents family reunion and harmony.
