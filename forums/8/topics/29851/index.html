    tact ( P ) Pronunciation Key (tkt)
    n.
    Acute sensitivity to what is proper and appropriate in dealing with others, including the ability to speak or act without offending.
    Archaic. The sense of touch.

    [French, from Old French, sense of touch, from Latin tctus, from past participle of tangere, to touch. See tag- in Indo-European Roots.]
    Synonyms: tact, address, diplomacy, savoir-faire
