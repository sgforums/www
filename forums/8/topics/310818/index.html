    Me: Then how do you earn your commission? Because we came across an ad that says "Job offer: Recruitment consultant - $1,500 + commission + attractive bonus".

    RC: No no no... The company pays the agency in a lump sum. Then we calculate. We count, we do your timesheet, we contact here and there.. Very troublesome..

     

    Note** -> In the end, I still feel that the pay that we get is just what they see fit? Because the figures are there for them to decide. So they can be like telling you $6 per hour because they choose to earn more instead of letting the people that work earn more? You get what I mean?

    RC: You only diploma. Everyone here is a degree holder or at the very least, doing part time degree. The reason being that the client (companies looking for employees who approach them) will feel better talking to a degree holder. And also, your namecard looks better!

     

    At this point of time, we really feel like punching her because of her attiude and her ugly face. :P   Anyways, her job is so simple that I think a O lvl student can do it with a bit of training involved. Its just calling people and asking them whether they want a job or not? And calculation wise, O lvl maths will be sufficient????? Duh..

     

    SO my point is that, from my conversation with her, I really feel very stressed about looking for a job through agencies because they can eat you if they want to. But the problem is that, most of the advertisement in for example, JobsDB dot com is filled with recruitment agencies!  Its so stupid. Seriously.

    The plus point about companies outsourcing for recruitment agencies is that its fast. And they get to sack you as and when they want to because there is no strings attached. And if you get injured or whatever, its the agency that is in charge of your medical fees or whatever.

     

    Soooo...... Any thoughts people? Or anyone working for them can clarify my doubts about how the system works? I also heard from some other agencies saying some agencies giving low rates to job-seekers.
