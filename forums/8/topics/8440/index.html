  • Enigma
    24 Jul 00, 00:46
    It's near impossible to walk down the streets of Singapore without hearing phrases such as "Where got like that one", "You got enough money or not?" or "Why you always wear until so nice?"

    Singlish....

    Some would argue "Why be so serious? relak abit lah. Talk to friends only mah." But then again, why the need to resort to using incomplete sentences peppered with grammatical errors when a simple phrase like "Surely there's no need to be so serious when talking to friends." would do just as well.

    I'm not implying that I converse in impeccable English, and I know I don't, but at least I attempt to.

    It is rather sad to see how the standard of English, both spoken and writen, by most Singaproeans leaves much to be desired.

    Is there really a need to use Singlish? A national identity? Certainly we could use much more desirable traits?

    Any comments?

    singlish only benefits those who already have a solid grasp of the english language. it's an illusion that one can switch with ease between the two forms of spoken language if they don't even have a good foundation in standard english. by persisting or continuing to speak in singlish because they're comfortable with it, they're just harming themselves in the process.

    i've been told countless times that i don't speak like a local, that my english is too proper, too uptight and so to accommodate friends and to make them feel comfortable, i do speak singlish, i don't deny that it's really fun and at the risk of sounding really arrogant, i can switch between the two with ease as my foundation in standard english is pretty okay. most caucasians whom i've come into contact with have no problems understanding me when i speak that "uptight" english and vice versa. however, my favourite choice of spoken language is mandarin.

    just an additional opinion, speaking singlish all the time or most of the time does corrupt one's standard of proper english over time, so it's best to use singlish sparsely if one wishes to retain one's standard in the english language.
