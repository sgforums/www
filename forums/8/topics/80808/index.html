7 die in oil tanker fire at Singapore's Keppel Shipyard

  • wuming78
    29 May 04, 22:03
    SINGAPORE: Seven people were killed in a fire on a Portugal-registered oil tanker undergoing repair at Singapore's Keppel Shipyard at Benoi Road.

    The dead are five male Indian nationals and two male Malaysian Chinese.

    Police say the seven were carrying out welding works in a compartment of the tanker at about 1pm Saturday when a fire broke out.

    The fire was put out by Keppel Shipyard personnel shortly after it started.

    SCDF spokesman Lieutenant Colonel N Subhas said the Singapore Civil Defence Force was activated by Keppel Shipyard at about 1.25pm.

    The SCDF responded with three ambulances, two fire engines and four support vehicles.

    60 personnel, including 18 members from the DART team, conducted search and rescue operations.

    Colonel Subhas said the ship hole where the accident took place was extremely hot and the floor was oily and sticky.

    The tank measured 50m by 10m by 24m and extreme heat resulted in one DART officer also being injured during the search.

    He was sent to the National University Hospital, but his condition is not serious.

    The tank was also in pitch darkness, adding to rescue difficulties.

    After the last body was recovered at 6.55pm, search and rescue operations were called off.

    It is understood that the vessel had docked in Singapore on 22 May for repairs and was due to leave in early June.

    The name of the oil tanker is Almudiana.

    The fire is believed to have started at the Number 3 starboard of the cargo oil tanker.

    The dead are subcontract workers from Wah Soon Marine Pte Ltd.

    Keppel Shipyard's executive director Nelson Yeo says the yard is saddened by the incident and loss of lives.

    And, it will do its best to work with Wah Soon Marine to help the families during this period.

    The Police, SCDF and Manpower Ministry officials are investigating the cause of the industrial accident. - CNA
