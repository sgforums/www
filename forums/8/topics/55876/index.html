    Positioned as the compact version of the current GD87/88, PanasonicÂ’s X70 model comes in a neat little package which aims to further satisfy our needs with a string of improvements and new features.

    What made the GD87/88 different from the other GSM clamshells out there in the market was its unique sizing.     On the inside, a 65,535-colour TFT LCD and integrated digital camera are just two of the features retained from the GD87/88 on the new X70. Java application compatibility is also another new feature of the X70 which allows the running of third-party applications directly from the handset.

    CommunicAsia may have been postponed, but the show goes on for Nokia.

    The Finnish market leader yesterday unveiled its two latest handsets in a regional event held in Singapore. The 6600 and 3100, although not mind-blowing, steadily extend Nokia's implementation of color displays.

    The 3100 is, at its core, similar to the Nokia 3530, which was released earlier this year. Like the 3530, the new 3100 comes with a 4,096 color screen and supports a variety of housings.

    But the new Nokia will attempt to succeed where the previous one failed. The 3530 delivered color at a mass market price but couldn't quite make an imprint, possibly due to its hefty frame. Here, Nokia has whittled down the bulk with the 3100, making it a more attractive proposition. Weighing just 85g, the handset already looks set to become a hit with youths.

    Less conventional is the other new Nokia--the 6600 with its oval outline and sizeable girth. This handset weighs some 122g and measures 58.2mm across. The major technical improvement in the 6600 is its long-overdue 65,000-color active-matrix display.

    When we tried out a pre-production unit, we found the screen an excellent electronic viewfinder. Unlike previous Nokia camera-phones, the response time of the 6600 is fast enough to keep up with sharp movements, even under low-light conditions. To top it off, the video-capable 6600 even comes with a 2x digital zoom, plus a new technology called Synchronized Multimedia Integration Language (SMIL). This last innovation allows users to compose messages with text, sound and images in sequence.

    Other notable features include the handset's 6MB internal memory, Bluetooth support, as well as streaming video and audio capabilities. Like the 7650 and 3650, the 6600 uses Series 60, Nokia's implementation of the Symbian OS.

    At the launch, the Finnish manufacturer also unveiled some new mobile accessories, including the funky Fun Camera add-on as well as an FM radio-cum-headset.

    The 3100 and 6600 will be released in Q3 and Q4, respectively. Pricing details are currently not available.
