    Firstly the idea is more a dream than a possibility. Let me, a layman, analyse certain facts with you.

    Firstly, the setup cost.

    The necessary equipment:
    Chairs, Tables, Utensils, Cloth, POS system (includes cash register, NETs, Credit Card etc), Cups, Glasses, Plates, Menu, Coffeemaking Equipment. Refridgerator, Air-conditioning. Music-Jukebox/Hi-Fi/Whatever Sound System, Containers to keep things (Stationary etc), Cupboards, Washing Detergent and many more. Initial Set-up cost.

    The necessary staff:
    At least 2. Let me say, each earns $900 a month (+ CPF blah blah deposits). Your annual salary given to staff will be around $2,3400 (including 13th month)

    Designing and Renovation, (Calculate yourself)

    Monthly rental (unless you're going to buy the place which is really questionable)

    Earnings per cup X number of cups sold

    Number of cups sold will be depending on number of customers and number of cups per customer.

    Number of customers and staying time. Average staying time for a student is 2 hrs without purchasing any drink (or free glass of plain water, unless you want to collect money for that.) Average staying time for people down for tea break 30 mins. Average staying time of people who decide to stay for extreme hours during exam periods - 4 hrs. Number of chairs will indirectly affect number of customers. An overly congested place (whether too much chairs or too many stoners) will make people have a bad experience and refuse to visit again.

    Even if coffee bean decides to allow you to sell their franchise coffee, THE necessary skills to make the coffee....

    To break even, you must deduct from earnings after paying monthly wages, monthly maintenance. And that may take more than one year.

    Does that look like a good deal to you?

    Not to me.
