    I saw some of the messages posted by those people talking about the 2001 Leonid shower in these chit chat forum. There are many mix comments though.

    I just come back from Ayers Rock, Outback pioneer hotel in Australia after staying for about one week. The sky down there was superb and the leonid shower was fascinating. At least, a few thousand meteors plus many fireballs!

    I did not stay in singapore to see as the chances will be very slim due to bad weather condition during this period of
    time plus the terrible light pollution and city glow from the horizon. Even if you are in a very dark area, the above factors will render your chances of seeing the meteor shower to zero.

    If you are interested, you can check out the following URL: http://spaceweather.com/meteors/gallery_18nov01.html to see the various photos of leonid meteors taken from people around the world.

    Final Words: singapore is a very bad place for observing the night sky with very few days of clear skies. Don't expect to see the milky way!
    And don't ever bet on what the radio and newspaper in singapore tell you, they are misleading. Get out of singapore and locate a very dark place with clear skies to observe such astronomical events and your efforts will be rewarding.
    It will indeed be fulfilling and enjoyable!
