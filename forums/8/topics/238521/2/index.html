    8 Mar 07, 01:16
    For your information, there seems to be more part-time students than full time students in MDIS, advance diploma in IT course. If the course education is useless, why will companies encourage or even sponsor their employees to go for the course? If money is a problem to you, you can choose to study part-time and work full time, else study full time and work part-time. For me, I am currently studying full-time in MDIS.

    Study wise, IT is really a difficult field that is not suitable for just anyone. You must have a logical and analytical mind to actually understand how computer systems really work. Modern computer systems are very very complex. An operating system alone can contain BILLIONs of lines of code, more code = more bugs = more security vulnerabilities. If you think programming is too much for you to learn, IT is really not for you.

    Work wise, IT management or development professions are usually very stressful because of the high responsibility that they have to bear. You worry about information system that does not work perfectly, you worry about hardware and power failures, you worry about DoS attacks, you worry about unhappy employees corrupting your database, you worry about employees stealing data and selling it to competitors, you worry about not doing enough backups and hope that nothing will ever go wrong with your information system, and if you are into system developments, you may have to cope with projects... deadline... projects... deadline... And if you develop a system that does not meet requirements, you may get sacked or even get sue by your customers. Believe me, these are the jobs/work that can ensure that you wont be sleeping well at night. If you cannot handle stress or bear heavy responsibilities, think twice before you apply for a high level IT position.
