    From Strait Times Webby:-

    SEREMBAN - A plucky 22-year-old salesgirl used her size to her advantage when she went head to head with a thief, who finally succumbed after she sat on him until police arrived.
    Bruised but uncowed, Ms Wong said, "I just wanted to teach these snatch thieves a lesson". -- THE STAR

    It all started when a youth entered a cellphone outlet in Jalan Tun Dr Ismail here at noon Thursday where Wong Pei Fan was manning the counter.

    Shortly thereafter he fled with a brand new phone with the salesgirl in hot pursuit.

    She caught up with him 20m from the shop. Book salesman M. Jagesdwaran, 44, who was at the scene, said he saw Ms Wong grab the thief's T-shirt before shoving him to the ground.

    The thief fought back but Wong gave him a kick in the groin.

    While he was grimacing in pain, she sat on him and shouted for help.

    According to Mr Jagesdwaran, the gutsy girl gave the thief another blow in the face when he tried to resist.

    At one stage, the thief tried to crawl away with Ms Wong riding 'piggyback' but he quickly gave up when he found that she could not be unseated.

    By this time, a crowd had gathered and Mr Jagesdwaran said he called the police, who arrived soon after to arrest the suspect.
    The suspect in custody after Ms Wong kicked, punched and pinned him down. -- THE STAR

    Ms Wong, who sustained minor injuries on her elbow and knee, later told reporters she was angry when she ran after the thief and did not think about her own safety.

    'I really wanted to teach these snatch thieves a good lesson. That was why I used all my strength to stop him from getting away,' she said.

    This was the second time that Ms Wong had encountered a snatch thief.

    She said she had had a chain snatched while walking along a back lane here a few months earlier.

    Police commended the salesgirl for her bravery and said they needed the cooperation of such people to curb snatch thefts. -- The Star/Asia News Network
