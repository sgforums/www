    Things you SHOULD eat:

    1) Foods that are in healthy proportions. Serving sizes in restaurants and certain foods at food courts are just as supersized as your macdonalds meals.
    2) Carbohydrates in the low glycemic index. Whole grains, brown rice and certain potatoes.
    3) Lean meats as your protein sources. Chicken without skin, lean cuts of pork and beef, etc.
    4) Lots and lots of vegetables and fruits with FIBRE in them. Fibre fills your stomach without giving you any excess energy source at all. For your case, 1/4-1/3 of your food should consist of vegetables.

    Things to NOT eat:

    1) Sweets, foods containing alot of simple sugar like cakes, chocolate bars, soft drinks, starbucks, coffeebean, bubble tea. If you do happen to take, know that you have exactly 20 minutes to expend 200 kcal of immediate energy, if not, 9 kcal is converted to 1 gram of fat in your body.
    2) Staple that is high on the glycemic index, white rice, white bread, sweet potatoes. All these foods have 100 glycemic index, the same as sugar. Eating them is as good as taking sugar by the tablespoonfuls.
    3) Foods high in fat, KFC, Bak Kut Teh.
    4) Foods that are prepared with alot of oils and fats, roti prata, goreng pisang.
    5) Foods that have hidden fats, nasi lemak, nasi goreng, buttered rice.

    Things you MUST do:

    1) Include an exercise program in your weight loss program.
    2) Weights training is MANDATORY.
    3) WRITE down everything you eat and look at it every night before you sleep and every morning before you start eating.
    4) Influence the support of those close to you, parents, siblings, etc.
    5) Consult a Fitness Trainer or a Nutritionist.
    6) Certain supplements and food can help suppress your appetite.

    Things you MUST NOT do:

    1) Cut down caloric intake drastically without exercising. (Fygi has explained why)
    2) Buy Extrim, Slim10 and all that SHIT that gives you eventually weight loss PLUS kidney failure, heart stoppage.
    3) Go for a slim wrap at beauty centre for $2,000.
    4) Look up the internet for ways of slimming down. Sure, 10% of it is true. Now which 10%!?

    There are generally two types of exercises

    Aerobic / Isotonic - A high energy consumption exercise, running, jogging, swimming, football, basketball, badminton, tennis, threadmills, double strollers... you get what I mean.... (Running for 30 minutes churns out the most calories in all the different types of exercise, about 1000+ kcal)

    Anaerobic / Isometric - A type of exercise to tone up muscles or build up muscles such as weightlifting (there are various types of machines nowadays, I don't really remember the names of it), the small dumb-bell lifting...

    So if you wanna lose weight in gym, look out more for aerobic exercises, if you wanna tone muscles (and hence gain weight - and mind you muscle weighs more than fat) go for anaerobic...

    anyway, what I would like to add on is that you can work out indoors, depending on your weight, height and body fat percentage, there can be a few workout regimes.

    1st) CARDIO: This is the most important if you're looking to burn fat, if you're obese, do not even start on weights, keep running. run for 20mins, at around 65% of your heart rate, most gyms have treadmills that can help you take your heart rate. Running is the best because it will give you a total body work out. However, i would strongly advise you take glucosamine if you're like me, running everyday can take a huge toll on all your joints and cartilage. Bike riding and cross trainers don't work you out half as effectively as running does. Do not run fast for 10mins, you use up your immediate energy supply, what you want is for the body to dig into its fat reserves, so 20mins, at a good speed. an indication of this is that you can talk while running, but not sing a song.

    2) WEIGHTS: If you're marginally overweight, it would be better for you to increase your metabolism, and muscle mass first WITH CARDIO - there is no way you can skip your cardio and burn fat. Doing an array of resistance training can help you in your goals. Afterall, if you lose weight and become bones you wouldn't look half as good as you do being all toned and lean. Besides the superFACIAL aspect of it, if you were to start on weights first then cardio, you can be guaranteed to be burning alot more of your fat reserves if you follow my advice in (1) because with weights, you would have used up your immediate energy reserve first.

    3) TIP: There is no better time to do cardio then in the morning.
    4) TIP: To get that same morning effect, do weights first, unless you're obese than follow (3)
    5) TIP: You have to stick with your regime, don't be self conscious like everyone in the gym is looking at you, it's alright to run slow for 20-30mins, the gym is a very supportive place, ppl are all here for a common reason.
    6) TIP: For the body to burn fat, you have to lower your calorie intake, and up your calorie burning. which means, eat less work more, no pain no gain.
