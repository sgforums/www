    If you have a month, I'd say spend 30mins in the morning doing some form of cardio on an empty stomach, gradually increase intensity. this can be jogging, or even aerobics. In the evening, spend 30mins-45mins on different resistance exercises with weights, e.g. squats, ab crunches .. target a different body section each day. 1 month isn't alot of time, but you should notice yourself being alot leaner and toned.

    If you have access to a gym, even better, you'll be able to get a fitness coach who can monitor your progress and structure a detailed plan for you.

    I personally only have 1hr+ after uni each day to hit the gym, so I do 45mins of weights .. i do a whole body workout because i'm not looking to buff up but maintain lean .. i do not target a particular section .. and followed by that I do a 4km (20min) moderate run .. that's to burn fat ..
