    The Spider Who Wanted To Be a Fly

    There was a naive spider who lived in her web somewhere on the lower branches of a pine tree in the cold countryside. Everyday she sat on her gleaming silken web to admire the flies which moved around with a buzz and great dexterity. She longed to fly like they did instead of just sitting around her old stationary web. So she watched. And waited.

    One day a young fly in his hurry and recklessness was caught in her web. The fly knew this could spell his death but being a young punk that he was he decided to die with a blast. However the spider did not want to eat the fly. She wanted to ask him to teach her how to fly. She approached him while he shivered inwardly. Her small head was hovering above his unmoveable body and he struggled to be free. Strangely the spider merely used one of her legs to poke at the fly and released one of his wings. He was amazed and he became still to see what the spider would do next. She poked once again and the other wing was freed as well. Then she jumped up and down her web like it was a trampoline. The fly thought she was insane but played along. He pumped himself up and down while the spider attempted to smile at her newfound playmate. The momentum of the fly carried him up and he was freed from the web. He flew away without a good bye.

    So she tried to befriend few more flies but it was always the same story. She didn't realize that she couldn't be a fly simply because she was destined to eat flies and the flies knew it. She did eat lesser flying insects like mosquitoes and the flies could smell the latter's blood in the web. She didn't know. Days went by and her wanting and disappointment turned her heart black.

    A fly and his sweetheart were caught in the web one day. Our black-hearted spider as usual came to see the newcomers but this time she rolled them into two cocoons of white waxy substance. She poked her fangs and legs at both of them -not to eat them because she retained her old habit- to hurt and make them afraid. The spider had seen how the two of them flew with cheerful buzz and somehow it didn't seem that this fly would leave his sweetheart, why had they left her then? When the female fly died, the spider released the male from his prison and tossed both of them out of her web into the emptiness outside. His weakened wings could not flap anymore so he just drifted gently with the wind, slowly towards the ground. The spider watched fascinated.

    After a few more experiments she decided to move to a much higher position. She traveled to the peak of the mountain and found the tallest pine tree there. It stood precariously at the edge of a valley and the chilling mountain breeze drowned the peak with restlessness. The spider who wanted to fly stood on a quivering leaf. She lowered herself on a strand of strong silk and the wind swung her around, it almost felt like flying! But it wasnt's enough. Above the valley she cut her string. The wind carried her light body but she invariably went downwards. The valley was deep though and along her flight down she met some birds (which wanted to eat her but failed somehow) and some flying insects (she waved at them). Once the breeze took her to the wall of the valley and she was suspended long enough to tell her story to a spider sitting on his web. Then with a smile she drifted down again, probably to die at the end of her descent, probably not because of her light body and soft belly. But she was happy then, she had flewn once.
