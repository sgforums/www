    'She was an ordinary girl with an extraordinary face,' said Ms Lam, who believes that the next wave of stars will come from the Internet.

    'She has that girl-next-door quality that the Taiwanese will like. Although she's still a bit of an introvert, the blog is a way to see another side of her that's not that sweet!'

    But Ms Lam added that 'a lot of honing and polishing' is needed.

    Dawn will get intensive singing, dancing, Mandarin and even Japanese lessons till she becomes 'idol drama material' and a 'well-rounded artiste'.

    Ms Lam said: 'We can only support her with the resources to groom her for the industry. Whether or not she will make it to the top will depend on her own hard work and, of course, the right timing.'

    Dawn herself is a mixture of nerves, excitement and eagerness as she attempts to break into the entertainment industry.

    She said: 'Every kid has dreamt of becoming a singer or actor and it was a fantasy I never thought of pursuing. But since the opportunity has presented itself, I might as well give it my best.'

    BOYFRIEND

    Dawn admitted to having a Singaporean boyfriend - until Ms Lam jumped in and advised her to keep a 'single status' for the sake of her career.

    She also seems well-prepared for the criticism that comes with the territory.

    Her good looks have inspired much jealousy.

    'In school, someone started weird rumours about how I got into a catfight with another girl. People also accuse me of having plastic surgery, but what can I do?' she shrugged helplessly.

    Pictures of her clubbing with celeb pals such as Fiona Xie and Alex Polstra on her blog have led to Dawn being labelled a 'laidback airhead' who leads a 'dazzling decadent lifestyle'.

    But Dawn was a straight-A student who attended Singapore Chinese Girls' School and Raffles Junior College.

    She spent a year at the Singapore Management University (SMU) but left for the US on a scholarship to study business and communications at the University Of Southern California.

    She has also been called a 'rich spoilt brat' because she writes about driving an SLK Mercedes - but in the US, it is 'really cheap at US$15,000'.

    Dawn revealed that she is 'well-to-do but not loaded', and that her family - comprising her retiree dad, HR manager mum and 16-year-old younger sis - lives in a semi-detached house in Bukit Timah.

    She said in her calm, soft voice: 'I guess when you get more known, you will always get haters.'

    Which is why Dawn avoids turning her blog into a 'social-political commentary' or shooting her mouth off on controversial issues - although she feels the handicap toilet debacle surrounding Wendy has been 'blown out of proportion'.

    She said: 'If I need to express my opinions, I usually post my comments on other people's blogs.

    'My blog is for my own personal enjoyment, for my friends and family. I never blogged for an audience.'

    That will change next month when Dawn's supporters can look forward to her online journal, which will chronicle her journey to becoming a star.

    Blogger Benjamin frowns on such a 'passe' publicity gimmick as it has been 'done to death with so many reality TV programmes'.

    But Sandra Ng aka Sandralicious has voiced her support of Dawn, adding: 'It's a cool idea. People will be fascinated by its novelty. It'll feel a bit more personal, bring her closer to the public.'

    Izzy aka Sarong Party Girl also feels that the diary will succeed provided it is well-written and easy to identify with.

    'Everyone loves a Cinderella story. And even the 'zero to zero' plot works in modern culture, especially if she can turn a failed career into a touching story of burnt hope and how it is so difficult to be an artiste in Singapore. I'm sure people would buy that.'
