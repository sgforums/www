    Usually the doctorwill perscribe tetracyclene or doxycyclene depending on the severity of the acne.

    Personally, I'd recommend against the use of these antibiotic pills chiefly because although they kill the bad bacteria, they also kill the good bacteria too. And yes, one can have clear skin after 3 - 6 months of consuming these pills, however the effect is not lasting. After some time the acne will come back plus its not good to take the pills on a long term basis. Side effects include nausea, increased sensitivity to sunlight and yeast infection.

    But then again, its my own personal opinion, different people have different reaction to medication. So what might not be suitable for me, could be suitable for you and vice versa

    What you pay for consultation depends on whether you're a private (GP-referred) or a subsidized (polyclinic-referred) patient.

    If you're a subsidized patient then no matter which doctor you see its around $25 (if i remember correctly).

    However if you're a private patient then consultation charges depend on the seniority of the doc you're seeing. Think it ranges from around $45 (medical officer) to $80+ (senior consultant, incl the professors).

    Then of course these are only the consultation charges, gotta add to it medication charges, so its gonna be quite a bundle.

    btw, i've heard of a doctor in chinatown who specialises in seeing skin cases, supposed to be v good and charge only $40+.

He / she would try to diagnose for you because referring you to the appropriate doctor. I think the main reason why that's the practice is because they wanna cut down time on handling 1st timer who would usually have more 'history recording' to do...
