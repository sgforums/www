    You can get a HDB loan as well. But the rules just changed. Read today's Straits Times.

    I think the new formula for the maximum loan year goes something like this:

    65 - (Average age of all co-owners)

    So if you're adding your grandma's name inside together with yours, for example, say your age is 32 and your grandma is 64. Then the average age of both of you is 48. So the HDB loan you take must be repaid in (65 - 48 = 17) years.

    Previously, they allowed you to take a max 30 years loan (65 - 32 = 33 > 30) But they changed the rules supposely to want owners to be more prudent in their finances.

    Of course, I could be wrong. I'm using the ST example and did some calculations on my own. It's advisable to seek a housing agent properly.
