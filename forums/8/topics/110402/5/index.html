    Dear all,

    Many thanks for your valuable comments.

    I been to one of CHC services during the weekend and here is what I observed:

    1. Most of the attendees are pretty normal average people. About 80% are below 25 years old. There are a few old folks (50 and above) there. Most are in simple casual clothes (jeans, T-shirts, polo), non too conservative nor beng.

    2. The not-so-normal thing is during the song singing time. With the jumping up and down, clapping of hands, flashing of lights, its like a rock concert! People got really hyped up, the environment is quite like the Anthony Robbin's Live with Passion seminar that I been to some years back.

    At this point, I have a question in my mind. Is it the jumping up and down and the rock music that provide CHC goers the "Yes I am with God feeling"? The reason I linked the jumps and rock music with this feeling is because, after the LWP seminar with Tony Robbins, I am super motivated... It seemed like I can do anything.

    3. There are slow songs and during the slow songs, most of the audience pray in tongue. People started praying in tongue together and stopped together as if on que. First time I experience this senario. Not sure if this is common in other church. I do not know how to speak in tongue.

    4. The total time spent singing was about 45 minutes. Total service time was 2.5 hours. They like to sing some verses of the songs over and over and over again -> perhaps this contributed to the "Boring" comment by one earlier poster.

    4. As for pastor Kong's preaching, I didn't feel touched by his presence. Perhaps he was tired by organising the various relief-trips to Aceh -> I cannot testify if the trips are real or fake. He spent about half an hour explaining what CHC did to help Aceh and Sri Lanka. According to him, CHC supports an orphanage in Sri Lanka, Jaffra.

    5. The crux of the service should be the goal setting part where pastor Kong explains how to make plans for the year with support by certain straight-forward verses in the bible.

    6. Tith was collected twice, once for the church and another for the Tsunami victims. By the way, CHC donated $50,000 to the disaster. I feel that CHC has an extremely systematic way of collecting tith. Instructions on how to pay your tith are very clear. There are multiple ways of paying it - nets, credit cards, cash, and cheque. There are even automated payment kisoks in the canteen!

    ----
    Car and house:
    I think its ok for the pastors to drive cars because sometimes they might need to visit church members or run some errands for the church. However, if they are driving mercs or lexus, that will put me off. To be fair, we don't know if the Nissan and Audi are registered under the pastor names or CHC - same to the condo.

    Over all an interesting eye opener. I most prob will not join the church as I am not a tongue speaker. It feels odd when I don't speak tongue when all are doing so. Jumping up and down at the songs is also not for me as I don't chiong. I also don't like the hillsongs used by CHC (personal feeling. no offense). I feel that the hillsongs are rather simple and not as meaningful as the hyms. Also I feel that the hillsongs sanged that night mostly depict a "the world is a chaotic place, you are a lost person, come to the church to feel motivated again" feeling.

    Personal observations and feelings only. Comments are welcome.

    Blessed night.
