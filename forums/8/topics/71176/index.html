SAT and Mother Tongue no longer required for uni admission

  • wuming78
    28 Feb 04, 22:04
    SINGAPORE : The Scholastic Aptitude Test and the Mother Tongue are both no longer required for university admission starting from this July.

    This is the recommendation of a university admissions review panel.

    Second year Junior College student Cheryl Foong is relieved as she does not have to resit her SAT for a better score.

    She said: "I've spent in the effort studying it. I've put in the money to do it. So maybe they could include it but they should have like said it earlier."

    A university requirement implemented only last year, the SAT is now scrapped.

    Professor Chong Chi Tat, Chairman, University Admissions Review, said: "If we continue to impose SAT as a requirement for admission then we are really double-testing students and already we know that students are highly pressurised."

    With the SAT out, A Level results will now make up 95 per cent of total entry score, 5 per cent coming from taking part in Co-Curricular Activities.

    Another change is that Mother Tongue is now an optional entry requirement.

    Tharman Shanmugaratnam, Acting Education Minister, said: "The Ministry continues to encourage all our students to go as high as possible, as far as possible in their Mother Tongue as they are capable of. But once you get to junior college, once you get to university, it's important especially for the universities that they are able to select students based on those who are most able to take advantage of a university education."

    Tay Hui Ying, Student, said: "I don't understand why they are making so many changes."

    "People will just be contented with passing the subject and nobody will put in effort to do well in the subject."

    Jeremy Gan, Student, said "They just need to get a D7 and get into university and doesn't add much stress to them and on the other hand they also get their basic Chinese skills."

    But there's another group cheering the changes - polytechnic students.

    No more SATs.

    Only about 70 polytechnic students made it into the local universities based on their SAT score last year.

    And they won't need to have a relevant diploma, say a business diploma to apply for a business course.

    "With the changes I might be considering going straight to the university first before going out to work," said one student.

    Under the new system, apart from grades, faculties can have their own criteria like aptitude tests and interviews which will come under a faculty score.

    While the recommendations take effect this year, the two universities, NUS and NTU, say they won't be implementing all of it.

    For one thing applications for university admissions is just round the corner.

    For instance, faculty score will not be a tool of assessment for students who are coming in in the July intake.

    The universities will also have greater autonomy in admitting those with exceptional abilities like sportsmen who may not have the required grades.

    Professor Su Guaning, NTU, Committee Member, said "It's not intended to rescue people because they are not so good at certain things but more to pick out those with a very composite all-round kind of excellence."

    Up to 10 per cent of each intake will be set aside for such individuals. - CNA
