    Gentleman(1993)

    "Not every gentleman is what he seems to be on the exterior"


    A temple priest by day Kicha (Arjun) is a respected person in his local community and
    runs a small catering business with a pool of workers under him
    But Kicha, a seemingly simple man, unknown to anyone has 2 faces , is a devoted priest by day notorious high tech robber by night .
    He has stolen millions from the country's numerous government facilities, banks and auction events to help the poor and needy
    The home minister and police is adamant to catch this mystery robber yet are always unsuccessful at every event of his robbery
    But there is a story behind Kicha robbing from the greedy
    businessmen and government,
    one that started with the tragedy of his own life..
    A trendsetter movie for debut indian director Shankar which with lots of action and romance... and one of the first ever music heard from reowned composer
    AR RAHMAN...all songs romantically beautifully

    Indian( 1996)

    "Every man born in his soil has the duty of keeping his country safe from threats , both foreign
    and domestic"

    An old retired freedom fighter, after fought for independence in WW2 shows partiotism and ideology for his country a second time ,takes up his old arms
    against corrupt elements ruining the country's social and economic welfare.
    This time he goes up against enemies not outside the country but within,
    in every government sector , building and city where corruption runs rampmant in the modern 21st century
    bribes and corruption by government officials for "not boycotting their duty, but to do their duty itself" .
    Calling himself "Indian", he hunts down those responsible for the suppresion of the poor who cant pay bribes
    to get jobs and education and even basic necessities. As result succesive serial killing , he gets himself up against the police force and
    modern times ....Good story, direction and cinematography by Shankar
    Excellent fast fusion music by Ar rahman, the best work to date..

    Nayak(1999)

    "Our country has hundreds of cities.
    Thousands of streets. Lacs of homes. A billion people. A zillion problems.
    And so many problems. But there is one man who stands up and raises a voice against the system.
    One who challenges.
    who dares.
    Who makes the difference. "


    (Anil Kapoor) is a common man with an uncommon persona.
    A fearless television news reporter, his passion for his job
    and pursuit of the truth makes him incur the wrath of insatiable power hunters.
    Like infamous Chief Minister Balraj Chauhan , for instance.

    Balraj Chauhan is one of those due to whom the word 'politician' has earned its very own unflattering connotation.
    One fateful day, the reporter in a live telecast show broadcast on TV assumes the
    disillusioned voice of the people and publicly interrogates under the pretext of routine tv segments interviews the Chief Minister
    on the pathetic state of affairs in the country.Balraj Chauhan's answer to divert the reporter's riveting questions makes mocking invitation to the reporter himself to become the Chief Minister for one day and see what he can do. To his dismay, our reporter hero accepts.
    Little does Balraj Chauhan know that with this offer, he has dug his grave.
    Little does he realise that one ordinary common man, one day, one chance is all that is required to change the political structure of a nation
    What follows is a dynamic battle of wits and fists when a ordinay common place reporter assumes power to bring about major changes to both his fellow citizens and to his enemies in the political arena

    -tense moments and lots of action....a commendable interview scene...one of the best works if director Shankar ..Songs were a let down..except for one commendable scene is of the shooting music video of the ex Miss World commercial with some funky input and rhythms by ace musician AR Rahman

    Citizen (2001)
    "Every living citizen has his own rights regardless of class race or religion"


    An hard working car mechanic, helps the locals in need
    but he keeps his real identity a secret so that
    his task can be completed without hesitation.
    With numerous disguises, and a letter with the word
    `Citizen` contained within it,
    he kidnaps three high profile
    government officials and keeps
    mentioning the name of an unknown local village not on any map
    But investigations revealed both the village and its 600 odd inhabitants seemed to have been wiped away from the face of the earth.
    The diggings tell a story of a mass massacre
    that must have taken place about a couple of decades back.
    'Citizen' is finally apprehended.
    He comes out with his story of the gory happenings and how he, as a little boy, had been the
    sole survivor of the nightmare that gripped
    the village that day, twenty years back in the hands of evil government servants

    - poorly executed script and acting, not too great music though..offers nothing new
