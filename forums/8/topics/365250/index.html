    I read a biography on Bruce Lee many years ago.  The biography was approved by his wife.

    When he was young and growing up in Hong Kong, he did like to challenge the ang moh sailors to fights.  And some of these fights did get him into trouble with the law.  It was due to this that his father decided to send him overseas to study.

    He went to the States, and majored in Philosophy. And it was here that his own philosophy in his martial art began to take form.  And it is here that he boasted that he can take down any Chinese Kung Fu masters who opened up martial art schools in the USA, in less than a minute.  This, of course, did not go down well with the Kung Fu masters.  One challenged him to a fight.  And in front of other martial art practitioners and kung fu masters, he knocked out the master who challenged him in less than 30 seconds.

    Unlike the chinese kung fu practitioners at that time, he believed in immobilising his opponent in as few moves as possible.  His brand of kung fu, which he termed "Jeet Kune Do",  is a style derived from Wing Chun, but he incorporated many other fighting styles, and cutting away all the unnecessary and limiting moves he saw in the various styles.  What he created is a style which is very economical in its moves, thus improving speed and giving opponents very little chance of anticipating what his next attack is going to be. He honed his body to such an extend, it was said that he could break a thick wooden plank with his fist just three inches from it.

    What is seen in his old movies was not at his full speed and power; it was said that he had to slow down the way he fight in the movie because the filming was unable to capture his moves as he was too fast for the camera.

    There was an incident when he was at the set of the hollywood TV series, "The Green Hornet", which he was a co-star.  Two big angmoh stuntmen, teased him and challenged him to show off his martial art.  One of the stuntmen, held a punching bag, and asked him to kick it to test his power.  Luckily the over 200Ib ang moh was standing with a swimming pool behind him.  Bruce Lee stood in front of the angmoh with the bag and kicked from where he stood, sending the bag and angmoh into the pool.
