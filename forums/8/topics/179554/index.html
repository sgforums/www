    i disagree. here is why

    lower body temperature does not = lower body metabolism, in fact, your body has to burn more energy, and work harder, to bring your temperature back to normal

    the same goes for high temperature, when your body is too hot, energy is released to try and bring your body temperature back to normal.

    based on this logic, the time when your metabolism is lowest is when you are feeling normal.

    also, more food = higher metabolism.

    there have been many arguments between doctors/scientists and etc. one example is

    when the body temperature is cold, it needs to work harder to bring the body temperature back to normal, however, one way of maintaining heat is not to lose body fat, so.. it might be a win lose situation, with the increase of metabolism but decrease of fat burning.

    it is kinda hard to explain, but just know that to achieve the optimum peak of metabolism without gaining a disadvantage of something else is pretty hard to do, which is why it is best if you experiment yourself. dont take it to the extremes though =D
