    When my ex gf dumped me and went with another guy I was full of remourse and hatred. I despised the guy that "stole" her away from me to an extent that I thought was immoral. I could've sworn that I would never "back door" another guy.

    While i didn't poke a million blunt serated knives into the guy, i could be forgiven for using a voodoo doll instead. I admit to being very hateful and bitter, and thankfully none of those emotions surfaced to actions. That i'm proud of.

    Over the months, I've been hearing points of view from some frens, close or otherwise, in the course of reminiscing the relationship, that in such situations, it is no one's fault. further, their underlying point was "may the best man win"

    I have to admit, at first, i was pretty obstinate and refused to even think about what they said, let alone agree. Given time, i started to put myself in the "bastard's" shoes and thought to myself if i met a gal and she was attached, but she was all i ever looked for in a girl, and she's not married (well for argument's sake so wat if she was?), why shouldn't i go for her? That's probably wat the guy was thinking, and i sure hope they're meant for each other. I digress.

    I've always told myself, and to many a close friend: "If you don't fight for your happiness, who will?" and this could be one of the hang-ups about life. Your happiness could be at the expense of someone else, even if it was someone you don't know.

    So after all the long cock and bull story, wat's ur take?

    Are you a person that believes in making choices and sticking with it? If your partner is unsuitable for watsoever reason, and you break it off is fine. I'm referring to finding someone better, DEFINITELY better, but you would still stick with your partner if no problems exist? we all know too well that while many relationships are "strong" but the real feeling of love and passion were thrown out the window way way back...

    Or are u a person that heads for greener pastures? "May the best man win" is definitely part of your moral dictionary? In other words, in the case with my ex, if you were me you'd bid them good luck and congratulate the "bastard" for seemingly being able to provide better for her. and you wouldn't think twice about going after a gal that u are sure would make u extremely happee and vice versa even if she was attached/married.
