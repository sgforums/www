INTERVIEW WITH AN MRT DRIVER

    Our Cherry N. George interviews a mass transit train driver who is responsible for transporting hundreds of thousands of talkcockers every day. Find out the candid responses from this humble, but very important person!!!

    Cherry N: Hello Talkcockers! This is your famous journalist Cherry N. George! I have with me Mr. Relac bin Satukorner, a train driver who will relate his daily adventures. Welcome Mr. Relac bin Satukorner to talkingcock.com. And I must be proud to mention that you are the first Mat to be interviewed! Welcome!

    Relac bin Satukorner: Eh!! Brudder!!! Relac ah, joe! I'm glad to be interviewed by the famous Cherry N. George... we need more people like you in Singapore, you're my favourite journalist. Just fire your questions away, brudder!

    Cherry N: Well, let me start by asking this question, why is it that there is a yellow line that we must stand behind at about 1 metre away from the edge of the platform? Why yellow of all colors?

    Relac bin Satukorner: I suspect this is part of the cheena conspiracy, man! At first we say we want a white line, but the cheena babi management ask why we always want to worship orang putih? Since Singapore is 77% Cheena, we should have a yellow line.

    Cherry N: So the color chosen for the line of safety was racially motivated... hmmm. But moving on, as a train driver you have to be on the track for very long hours. Don't you get tired easily? Don't you need breaks?

    Relac bin Satukorner: Alamak brudder, we always take breaks lah! Every time we want to take a coffee break, we just stop the train and announce "Passengers, due to a train fault, this train will be slightly delayed, solly for the inconveniens corrs". Then we take out our Nasi Lemak and kopi and relac one corner, maybe merokok some marlboro, that kind of thing lah!

    Cherry N: No wonder we've all heard this standard response for delay at one time or another! Moving on, isn't driving a train for 8-12 hours a day very boring? How do you keep going at it day after day?

    Relac bin Satukorner: Hey, Cherry, we train drivers also have our kicks when we are driving the trains, OK. I'll tell you a secret. Whenever we get bored, and stop at the station to pick up passengers, we will purposely sound off the door closing signal early and say over the intercom "Passengers, train doors closing. TRAIN DOORS CLOSING NOW!!!!!" And we see the passengers struggle to squeeze into the train car like sardines. This scene is very funny, even funnier than the good old days of Phua Chu Kang!!

    Cherry N: So you train drivers also have your thrills. Well, we've just about run out of time. I'm Cherry N. George, for Talkingcock.com signing off. And remember, never be economical with the truth!
