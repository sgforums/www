    was in genting and KL about 2 weeks ago...if U know a place called "See Cheong Kai"(Cantonese) in KL...dun bother going...it's under renovation...construction work going on...food stalls not many...imitation goods still can find...beware of pickpockets.

    Sungei Wang and Mid Valley mall are shopping centres which U can try...though personally I prefer Mid Valley...
    Times Square(or is it Times Centre?) is touted to be the largest shopping centre in KL but my understanding is that it is not fully opened yet...something like 200++ shops out of 600++ shops are operational...not exactly a small number so if U have the time...U can try shopping there...located close to Sungei Wang...

    Enjoy...

    Don't have impression things in KL are much cheaper than Singapore. Genting is international tourist attraction, so don't need to say. I don't find KL a place good for shopping, Thailand is better and much much cheaper.

    Food ... I dunno what's famous in KL. The food not really that nice lah. It is not Penang or Ipoh anyway (hint the food).

    But it's good to walk around the place. Sungei Wang the previous poster mentioned, is like Far East of Singapore. Times Square is very very big, but most shops in upper levels not open. There's an indoor theme park inside.

    The traffic there in KL quite bad. Far cry from Singapore. I see their traffic light, the green man quite cute, like the Lode Runner running animation, but the countdown not yet zero the light is red liao .. duh. I dun see a single zebra crossing there ... haha

    Genting is cool (pardon the pun) Now they stop coaches going up to the peak. To go up, your coach/bus stops at Genting Skyway, the new cable car station with a bus terminal below. Then you take a ride in what they touted (you can see the banner when you queue) the 'longest and fastest cable car in South-East Asia'. Beware, the queue is damn long, since everyone (first time visitors, pple staying not at the peak etc) will take the cable car. I queued for around 45 minutes ... hahaha

    The cable car ride up quite scary, 'cos you actually go up, down, up, down the valley .... haha ... Sentosa version no kick after you sit this. I think it is 6 or 8 person per car. You sit back to back, 3 or 4 per row, so you can look out the window.
