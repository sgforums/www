    Wait a minute. There is really no concrete basis to suggest that the doctor involved did not know his medicine. Contrary to what would apparently be a widely held Singaporean belief, medicine is not black and white; the 'shaken baby syndrome' cases is just one example where medical opinion is split. Doctors have to make judgements to the best of their ability, based on the information available to them at the time, and those judgement calls may not always be correct. It is said that hindsight is always 20/20. Unfortunately, Doctors do not have the ability to predict the future. To punish them for honest mistakes would be unjust, unfair and detrimental to healthcare in Singapore.
