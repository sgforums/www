    dear xinyi,

     

    i think it depends on his personality and ur r/s with him as a child. i believe he's aware of his own condition and ur mother, the doctor and most adults around him are constant reminders of his own illness.

    imo, no one likes to be reminded they are sick. and no one likes the sympathy vote for being sick. the sudden attention and niceness they receive may even backfire.

    ur father was once healthier, stronger, happier and much more lighthearted. so perhaps u can try relating to him not as a sick man because he has pride, but as a companion and child.

    bring him out for meals, watch tv with him, tell him jokes, ask him stories about his first job, his first date with ur mother, his most daring travels, buy him a cd of his favourite artist, get him an mp3, download all his fav music for him, the list goes on.

    hopefully by helping to normalise his lifestyle, spending time together or creating some laughter, u can reduce the stress he undergoes post operation and bring the sparkle back in his eyes again.
