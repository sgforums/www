  • CoolMyth
    8 Nov 05, 08:14
    x = distance, v = speed, t = time.

    x = vt

    Differentiate w.r.t to t, then:

    dx/dt = dv/dt + v

    But since dv/dt = a = acceleration = zero at constant speed, hence

    dx/dt = v

    Hence, the rate of change of distance is simply the difference between the speed of both person, that is,

    dx/dt = 5 - (- 4) = 9m/s (since the lady is walking in the opposite direction as the man, I'm taking the man's direction as positive)
