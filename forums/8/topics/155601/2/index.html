    If one day, only money will bring people forward to "donate" their blood, it's no longer considered a donation. It will be a sale.

    Thank God that most people in Singapore aren't having that thought because I've been through the times when I need 2 packet of blood. So far already needed 19 packets of blood for my health condition else my organs will fail on me.

    We pay for the laboratory test for cross-matching, compatability test, medical equipments such as blood fliter. The cost for the stay covers the plugs,sodium drip, daily test, etc. If you find it ridiculous, then join those oversea where they receive free medical care but pay taxes like a king.

    You won't understand the needs of a patient when he's in critical condition. We do pay for the bills, but it does differ when you stay in subsidised ward and private ward. Government subsidised for B2+ wards and below whereas B1 classes are not being subsidised unless it's a warded case. If you know you can't afford to stay in B1 class due to the bills, go and find a B2 class. Not much differences but some people do preferred / need a quiet environment to recover faster.

    You may ask why nurses in B1/A wards respond to nurse call almost immediately. That is because B1/A wards are only 4 patients and 1 patients respectively. That will make lesser patients in the whole ward taken care by the nurses and they can respond faster. That also differs if you have ICU patients in your ward because the manpower become reduced.

    If you want to know how much blood is needed each day, visit any hospital wards that cares for "blood condition patients" and ask them. We can cough out money to pay the bills cause money can be earned but health is gone once gone.

    For people out there, you won't understand unless you go through it. Setting a needle inside your vein isn't easy. Your vein "runs" if it's smaller and also dependent on the temperature. In cold condition, your vein will hide. Some people have deeper veins and that means you need to endure more pain to set the plug be it you are a patient or a blood donor. The needle is almost 1mm.
