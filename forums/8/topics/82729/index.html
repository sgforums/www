    Among the three clamshell phones, the 6260 and 6170 models are positioned as mid-range, function-oriented products while the low-end 2650 model is more entertainment-oriented.

    The 6260 model is a GSM triband/GPRS handset that features a 180-degree twistable display, VGA camera, Bluetooth connectivity and video recording. The 6260 will be available worldwide next quarter, Nokia said.

    The 6170 model, encased in a polished steel cover, is also a GSM triband/GPRS handset. Other features of the phone include a 65,536 colors 128x160-pixel main display, a 4,096 colors 96x65-pixel external display, and a VGA camera. The 6170Â’s launch is set for the fourth quarter.

    The remaining two models are the 2600 and the 3220 for entry-level users.

    Nokia also launched other products, including a Bluetooth keyboard that is compatible with its 7610, 6630 and 6260 phones and a video stand featuring a video camera with 2x digital zoom for face-to-face interaction.
