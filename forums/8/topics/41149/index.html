    Priest accused of siphoning $4m in church funds

    A CATHOLIC priest has been accused of embezzling more than S$4.3 million of Church funds.

    Joachim Kang Hock Chai is alleged to have diverted the funds from the accounts of the Church of St Teresa in Bukit Purmei Road into his personal bank account, between July 1998 and February last year.

    In all, he is accused of making 13 transfers to his account, involving amounts ranging from S$20,000 to S$1 million.

    He is also accused of misappropriating S$300,000 meant for St Teresa's church-building fund.

    Kang had been at St Teresa's, which has a congregation of 2,000, for 12 years, before he was moved to the larger Church of the Holy Trinity in Tampines last year.

    The move was part of a massive reshuffle ordered by Archbishop Nicholas Chia when he took over as the Catholic Church's leader in Singapore.

    The 54-year-old, who is represented by lawyers Peter Cuthbert Low and George Lim, has been remanded for a week for further investigation, and to allow the authorities to look into the church's accounts between 1989, when he joined St Teresa's, and 1998. He will appear in court again on Wednesday.

    If he is found guilty, he can be jailed for up to 10 years and fined.
