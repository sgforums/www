    like what a forummer mentioned its more important to have a healthy debt equity ratio.

    it does not matter how much u earn or how much u spend. a healthy ratio is usually 60% expense and 40% savings. savings could be in the form of insurance, investments, fd or plain old deposits.

    looking at the thread starter friend...with a 4k income and a car and house loan coming up, heres what his breakdown would most likely be like:-

    Salary: 3200 (after cpf)
    Car Loan: 800
    Car Exp: 250
    Bills: 150 (HP, Tel, Internet)
    House Loan: CPF
    Entertainment: 300
    Food: 300 (based on $5 per meal over 30 days)
    Left:- $1550

    He should have slightly more then $1200 per month unless he has more expenses. Which is not too bad, unless he spends money unnecessarily.
