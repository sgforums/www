    Jackie Chan hints at sequel for "The Myth".

    International superstar Jackie Chan indicated the possibility of a sequel to his latest blockbuster "The Myth" while promoting the film in Mumbai recently. "The Myth" opened across Asia on September 23.

    "The Myth" makes Chan's fourth collaboration with Tong, following blockbusters "Super Cop", "Rumble in the Bronx" and "First Strike".

    In the movie, Chan plays an archaeologist who stumbles upon an undiscovered mausoleum, which transports him back to his dark past as a Qin dynasty general who is in love with a Korean princess (played by Korean actress Kim Hee-seon). She, in turn, is betrothed to the Qin emperor.

    Sherawat, who features as Chan's other love interest feels that it is great to work with Chan and Tong.

    Fans who are used to the action star's style of straight-talking, trademark one-liners and slick Kung fu moves on screen are in for a surprise as the period film is written to display not just his muscles but also his melancholic side.
