    "During my visit to Singapore last September, they expressed their readiness to discuss questions affecting our common sea boundaries from Batam island (in Indonesia) up to Johor (on the Malaysian peninsula)," the minister said.

    The two countries were currently assessing the readiness of their respective technical teams to begin talks, Hassan said.

    Speaking to reporters after attending a ministerial meeting on the maintenance of maritime security, the minister said Indonesia's technical team was actually ready to start the talks but its Singaporean counterpart was not as it was still busy handling matters arising from border disputes with Malaysia.

    "They (Singaporeans) seem to be still preoccupied with business connected with their dispute with Malaysia over Batu Putih island which has been referred to the International Court of Justice in The Hague, and over a land reclamation project on Singapore's northern sea border," he said.

    Asked about Indonesia's stand on the need for the UN to play a role in Iraq, the minister said the UN Secretary General had already sent a team to Iraq to assess the extent to which the world body needed to operate there.

    He said a process was now going on to give Iraq a mandate to handle its own political and security affairs.

    Asked whether Indonesia was or would be involved in the process, Hassan said this would depend on further developments in Iraq.

    "We now just wait for further developments in Iraq," Hassan said.
