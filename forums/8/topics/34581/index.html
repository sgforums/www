    Traffic Police patrols to book jaywalkers
    I REFER to Mr Floyd Ang's letter, 'Stop jaywalkers before someone gets killed' (ST, Jan 30).

    Jaywalkers pose a danger to themselves and fellow road-users. Traffic Police take a serious view of jaywalking. Offenders can be handed a $20 on-the-spot fine, which can go up to a maximum of $100.

    We will continue to patrol roads regularly to deter such inconsiderate behaviour. It is, however, not possible for Traffic Police to be omnipresent on the roads.

    We urge all pedestrians not to take unnecessary risks and endanger themselves and the lives of others by jaywalking. They should always cross roads at signalised pedestrian crossings, overhead bridges, underpasses or zebra crossings. At traffic-light junctions, pedestrians should never cross when the 'red man' appears or when the 'green man' is flashing.

    Motorists are also reminded to be alert to sudden occurrences on the road, such as people darting out, and slow down or prepare to brake when necessary.

    Accelerating, revving the engine or sounding the horn to startle pedestrians are inconsiderate and dangerous acts that should be avoided.

    PHILLIP MAH
    Assistant Director
    (Media Relations)
    Singapore Police Force
