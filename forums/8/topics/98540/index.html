    From what I know/feel.....

    (1) Golf is supposed to be THE status symbol game. Shows that you can afford an expensive club membership, which can also be an investment.

    (2) The game itself is addictive, probably because you get to hit ever so un-often.....and you just can't wait to take the next shot, hoping to do better. Its like you're only allowed to put one potato chip in your mouth every 30 minutes....you just long for more and more before the next one.

    (3) Its a kind of gambling. I know of someone who has won a Mercedes (hole-in-one).

    (4) They want to network with potential customers, employers, etc. I actually know of some people who were employed in high positions because of relationships formed on the green.

    (5) Its the perfect excuse to get out of the house on weekends, for both women and men. When mother-in-law's birthday comes, "oh...I have to play golf with a very important client, or else I will lose my job, my company will collapse, etc". Not everyone ends up the golf course. Some end up at their lovers' houses, etc.

    (6) What better excuse for guys to visit JB or Batam/Bintan for some fun? "Honey, I'm going across the boder to go at the nine holes again"....but what they didn't say was that there was a tenth hole somewhere there too.

    (7) Once a year, its also a good excuse to give one's spouse to visit Pattaya for a week.

    We've covered all basic attractions of life already (status, money, excitement, career, extramarital affairs, etc). What other reasons do you need? Maslow would be proud of our golfers....

    As for me, I took it up for a few weeks and never moved out of the driving range. Bloody boring. I'd rather watch Teletubbies on TV.
