    28 Apr 00, 23:20
    On paper, our Armed Forces really are little more than a minor inconvenience. Someone like Saddam might have a bit of a harder time with Singapore than he did with Kuwait, but we would still fall eventually.

    In fact, I wouldn't even classify SAF as a "defence force". Japan has a defence force. Thailand has a defence force. At best, SAF is a deterrent force. It is the job of the SAF to make taking over Singapore so costly that no one would consider it. SAF isn't about winning wars, it's about preventing Singapore from becoming another Kuwait. Thankfully, we don't have vast fields of oil or mineral-rich mines to make us as tempting a target as Kuwait was. Dropping a nuke on Singapore would like-wise be foolish, as you would end up with a pile of rock in the middle of the sea. Not worth much more than the bomb itself.
