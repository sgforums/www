    Fares may be higher for North-East MRT line
    This was hinted at by Transport Minister Yeo Cheow Tong, who said the line's cost would be a factor when considering fares

    COMMUTERS may have to pay more when riding on the new North-East MRT line, compared to the existing two lines.

    Transport Minister Yeo Cheow Tong hinted at this yesterday when he told Parliament that the $5 billion underground line is a costly project, and that the Public Transport Council (PTC) will take its cost into account when approving the fares.

    Advertisement

    Single-trip fares cost between $0.80 and $2.60 on the current North-South and East-West lines, which together cost close to $5 billion to build.

    But on balance, Mr Yeo said, both SBS Transit's justifications for its fare proposals and the impact of these fares on commuters will be borne in mind by the PTC when it sits down to decide on the fares for the North-East line.

    To Hougang MP Low Thia Khiang, who asked if the money saved on manpower on the driverless trains would contribute to lower fares, Mr Yeo replied that the PTC would take all these factors into account.

    But he cautioned that while there will be ''some savings on the drivers, the system will also be a lot more complicated''.

    ''This is because being driverless, the control systems and everything else will be more complicated, and the maintenance cost in effect will counter the savings on the drivers,'' he explained.

    The chairman of the Public Transport Council, Mr Eric Gwee, could not be reached for comment. SBS Transit, which took over the operation of the 20 km-long line last month, is expected to submit a set of proposed fares soon.
