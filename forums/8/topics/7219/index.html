    Here is a complaint letter from internet about DBS recent movement.

    Dear DBS,

    You may not admit it, but when you took over POSB in 1998, many POSB account holders were very unhappy. But we had no choice in the matter.

    We were told that we will benefit from better services, and we could only hope that this will materialise. It's now about one and half year since. Let us look at what has been dished out to POSB account holders.

    Our housing loan rates have been increased. Monthly account fees and penalties for not meeting the minimum daily balance have been introduced.

    We now have to pay for cheques. Whereas POSB passcard statements showed useful information like where the NETS transaction was made, you gave me statements with meaningless 3 character general codes. It was not until this year that you were able to give me the type of information POSB could give me years ago. The conversion from POSB internet banking to DBS internet banking was anything but smooth. You discontinued access to the POSB internet banking website without any warning at all. Unauthorised NETS deductions were made from many POSB accounts. For such a serious mistake, you contributed a miserable $1 to me, but only to cover lost interest.

    Sure, DBS has made available to us new investment and other financial services. But such things are of use only to people who need them. And to most POSB account holders, they only need a savings and a current account.

    But POSB already had them. Most will need an ATM card. But POSB had this. Some may need phone or internet banking. But POSB also had them. In fact, I believe POSB was the first to introduce phone and internet banking in Singapore, and if I may add, self-updating passbook printing service and current account auto-GIRO facility, which are all very practical and useful services. I can safely say that the additional services introduced by DBS are pale in comparison to what POSB already offered its account holders.

    Sure, our savings now earn a better rate of interest. But then again, it will soon be offset by the new charges and tax payable. In any event, I dare say that for most POSB account holders, they really did not mind the lower interest on their savings deposits. If they had minded, they would have long withdrawn their money and deposited it elsewhere. This is where sometimes, the people in authority are mistaken. They think that money is everything to everyone.

    Does DBS not realise that for many POSB account holders, POSB is more than just a bank? When I was in a primary school in a rural part of Singapore, POSB used an old silly van to bring the bank to my school. I was taught to lick stamps to paste on some cards. If I am not mistaken, I was also given a POSB piggy bank. I am sure POSB lost money doing these things. But by doing so, POSB achieved two very important things:

    (1) it taught all of us the importance of savings, a habit which Singaporeans can be proud of
    (2) it secured me as a long term customer. I am working today and still use POSB as my primary banking account. I am sure many Singaporeans do the same.

    POSB allows young children to open cute "My First Account"s with their birth certificates. They even contribute the first $1 to open the account. You don't even need to put in any money. And if the account reaches $1,000, they set up a trust fund for your children.

    As far as I can remember, POSB was the first to encourage account holders to save more by issuing lucky draw tickets for every $500 or $1,000. And they always have this silly but quite entertaining annual POSB show, which by the way seems to have been terminated for no good reason.

    What DBS has is this : penalise account holders if they fail to keep at least $500 in their accounts. Add a $2 monthly fee. This is savings by fear. It may work, but at a price to DBS.

    I am sure that there are far more POSB account holders than DBS account holders. Why is it then that for ATMs, the buttons for POSB account holders are always placed at the inconvenient left side of the screen while those for DBS are conveniently located just next to the command buttons? And those who use phone banking will know how long one has to wait before one can get to the POSB accounts. I know I am a bit pesky but surely the fact that there are more POSB ATM cards and more POSB ATM machines must count for something.

    I am sure if DBS does not want to take our frustrations into consideration, we cannot do much. I am not sure what my maid is going to do with her account. She will probably have to close the account, and I may have to pay her in cash. Maybe we might as well open an account somewhere else.

    As for my own accounts, I will probably close some of them. But I will probably make a request for a new cheque book (which is free for the first request) just for fun before closing my account. It is a nasty thing to do, I admit, but it is just a small protest on my part.

    DBS may have the use of our funds temporarily, but they have lost us POSB account holders' goodwill, forever.
