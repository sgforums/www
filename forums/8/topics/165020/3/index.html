    Step 1: Plug in the Starhub modem (USB) into your PC, which you intend to be the "server" of you home network.

    CAUTION! If this "server" is not turned on, the other PC cannot access the Internet!

    CAUTION! Do not use the LAN cable connecting to your Starhub Modem!

    Step 2: Connect your 2 PCs together using a LAN cable (it is something like a phone jack, but bigger. You can use the cable provided with the modem)

    Step 3: Turn both PCs on, if not on.

    Step 4: On the server, go to Control Panel and Network Connections

    Step 5: Right-click the modem connection, and select Properties

    Step 6: Click the "Advanced" tab at the top

    Step 7: There should be a frame called "Internet Connection Sharing". Select your LAN CABLE CONNECTION from the box.

    Step 8: Click OK. There should be a message saying that if you continue, the server's LAN port will be manually configured -- which means that you can no longer access Internet via the LAN CABLE unless you change the config (it is easy)

    Step 9: Your other PC and server PC should both have IP addresses. ENJOY!
