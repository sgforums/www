    We are not allowed to divulge too much. I know quite some secrets of our army but if I tell you, I have to kill you...

    Bascially, we go through basic military training for about three to five months, depending upon one's physical fitness. After which, we will be posted either to the respective combat arms to be trained as an operationally combat soldier.

    Other than that, some are posted trained to be Sergeants or Officers. They are then posted to respective combat or support arms to undertake different roles.

    The first months of basic training teaches us basic soldering skills and trains our dispicline and fitness. After posting out to the various combat and support arms, we then undergo skills / combat training specific to our vocation.

    All in all, training using takes up to about 6 months to a year depending on one's vocation. After that, we are "certified" as combat ready soldiers.

    After two or two and a half years active service, we will then return to civilian life. But we are still required to sit and pass our yearly fitness tests, as well as to return for re-training and reservist (NS men) training. Reservist duties will take up about 13 years, officers will serve 10 years more. But i heard it is abit shorter now.

    That's about all in a nutshell.
