    Your life's in your handwriting, expert believes

    SINGAPORE : Can the way you cross your 't's and dot your 'i's change your life?
    One international expert on handwriting believes it can.

    Handwriting expert Bart Baggett said: "They teach people to cross their 't's about half way up the stem and that's moderate self-esteem. And my research shows that most self-made millionaires not only sign their names large but have 't' bars crossed higher at the stem."

    Mr Baggett said he is his own success story - having changed his handwriting at 14, and creating a successful business from handwriting analysis.

    Some businesses have also jumped onto the bandwagon, using handwriting analysis before hiring top workers.

    Mr Baggett also has tips for what you should look out for in a partner.

    "I can spot boring (people) in handwriting but people who are boring are usually more stable. So people who aren't boring may also have a motorcycle, a tattoo and a criminal record," he said laughingly.

    Analysing the signature of Prime Minister Goh Chok Tong, Mr Baggett said: "This guy's brilliant. He's very intelligent, he's a smooth talker, this guy could sell ice to Eskimos."

    Looking at the signature of former Malaysian prime minister Mahathir Mohamad, Mr Baggett said: "This one's a little more tricky. He is intelligent and bright and has a lot to prove." - CNA

    Handwriting analysis is interesting....

    What is the basis of handwriting analysis:
    It is believed that thoughts and mood affects your handwriting, the opposite is also true. Thus graphologists believe that by changing the way you write...you can change the way you think and feel.

    Some examples of what you can find out via handwriting analysis

    Character traits
    Capacity for concentration
    - Look at the size of letters

    Confidence in ability to make decisions / ability to succeed
    - Look at the baseline

    ~ rigid and straight
    (someone who's a gd decision maker)

    ~ sections of lin / entire words drops below the line
    (the more often the words drop below the line, the more depressed the writer is)

    Intellecual abilities
    - Speed of handwriting

    Introvert or extrovert
    - Size of letters

    For signatures (Some of your traits are)

    If the signature is VERY MUCH bigger than the rest of your writings
    - Attention seeking
    -Thoughtless
    - Bossy
    - Exaggerated ego

    Slightly bigger than normal handwriting
    - Ambitious
    - Self-confident

    Slightly smaller than normal handwriting
    -Insecure
    - Introvert

    Much smaller
    -Inferiority complex
    Lack self worth

    Same size
    - You are unpretentious, how you behave in public is the same as what you are in private

    Underlining of your signature (ONCE)
    - Confident and firm

    Underlining of your signature (More than once)
    - YOu wants to be noticed

    Overscoring your signature
    -Defensive
    - Insecure with the image you portray in public

    Signature slanting to the right
    - Friendly
    -Positive self image
    - Optimistic and outgoing

    Sloping to left
    - Depressed
    -Negative self image
    - Pessimistic
    - Shy
    - Reserved
