    Hazy conditions, thundery showers forecast for Wednesday: NEA

    SINGAPORE: Hazy conditions and reduced visibility can be expected on Wednesday (Oct 28), with prevailing winds forecast to be weak and blowing from the east, said the National Environment Agency (NEA). Thundery showers are also expected in the afternoon. 

     

    In its advisory on Tuesday, NEA said that Singapore had been experiencing hazy conditions with reduced visibility on Wednesday, as haze in the surrounding region continued to be blown in by the weak prevailing winds. As of 6pm, the 24-hour PSI was 128-153, which is within the Unhealthy range. 

     

    NEA also said that the air quality for the next 24 hours may be in the mid to high sections of the Unhealthy range.

    Air quality to stay in Moderate range through Friday: NEA

    SINGAPORE: After weeks of haze and air quality rooted in the Unhealthy range, Singapore saw clear blue skies on Thursday (Oct 29). 

     

    The National Environment Agency (NEA) said in an advisory that there was a significant reduction in the extent of haze in Indonesia's Sumatra and Kalimantan due to showers in the region, but haze was still observed in parts of Sumatra island. A total of 66 hotspots were detected there on Thursday.  

     

    Air quality for the next 24 hours is expected to be in the Moderate range. 

     

    NEA added that the prevailing winds are forecast to be weak, and are likely to blow from the northeast or east on Friday. Thunderstorms are also expected between midnight and the pre-dawn hours. 

     

    Several people took to social media to express glee over the clear skies with the hashtag #hazefree. Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong also posted a photo of blue skies on Facebook, noting that it is a good day to take a walk or bring children to the playground. "We are now in the inter-monsoon period and the winds are changing to north easterly. Hopefully the blue skies will stay!" he wrote.


    -- CNAonline
