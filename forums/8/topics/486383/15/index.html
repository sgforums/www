    Hazy conditions, thundery showers expected on Tuesday: NEA

    SINGAPORE: Air quality on Monday (Oct 26) stayed in the mid-section of the Unhealthy range as haze from Indonesia's Kalimantan continued to be blown in Singapore's surrounding regions, said the National Environment Agency (NEA).

     

    A total of 11 and 84 hotspots were detected in Sumatra and Kalimantan respectively on Monday, NEA added. It said the low hotspot count was due to partial satellite pass and cloud cover.

     

    Thundery showers in the afternoon on Tuesday could bring temporary respite, but hazy conditions and reduced visibility are still expected, NEA stated in its advisory.

     

    NEA also said that the air quality for the next 24 hours is expected to be in the mid to high sections of the Unhealthy range. 

     

    Separately, Defence Minister Ng Eng Hen shared video of burning forests in Palembang, the capital city of South Sumatra island. He said the Republic of Singapore Air Force troopers filmed the video from their Chinook which had been deployed to help Indonesia to put out haze-causing forest fires. "On some days, the visibility was so poor they could not fly," Dr Ng wrote on Facebook. The Singapore troops returned from their two-week mission on Saturday.


    -- CNAonline
