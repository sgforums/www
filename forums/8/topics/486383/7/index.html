    Air quality could enter mid section of Unhealthy range on Thursday: NEA

    SINGAPORE: Hazy conditions continued to affect Singapore on Wednesday (Sep 23) with some deterioration in the late morning and afternoon, said the National Environment Agency (NEA). This was due to haze from Sumatra being blown in by the prevailing winds from the south and southwest. 

     

    As of 6pm, the 3-hour PSI was 139, while the 24-hour PSI was 86-115, in the Moderate to Unhealthy range. This was an increase from the corresponding readings of 97 and 84-116 at 11am the same day. 

     

    NEA added that these conditions are expected to persist for the rest of the day, and that hazy conditions are still expected on Thursday, as the prevailing winds are forecast to continue blowing from the south or southwest. 

     

    The agency advised that given the air quality forecast for the next 24 hours, healthy people should reduce prolonged or strenuous outdoor physical exertion. The elderly, pregnant women and children should minimise outdoor activity, while those with chronic lung or heart disease should avoid it altogether. 

     

    - CNA/hs
