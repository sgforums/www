    Hazy conditions expected on Saturday: NEA



    SINGAPORE: Slightly hazy conditions are still expected in Singapore on Saturday (Sep 26) though the air quality has improved, said the National Environment Agency (NEA).

    In an advisory on Saturday, NEA said the 24-hour Pollutant Standards Index (PSI) for the next 12 hours is expected to be in the “high end of the Moderate range and the low end of the Unhealthy range”. The 3-hour PSI dipped below 100 on Saturday morning, while the 24-hour PSI was between the Moderate and Unhealthy ranges.

    The air quality was an improvement from Friday, when the 24-hour PSI entered Hazardous levels. NEA attributed the change to a shift in prevailing winds, adding that “moderate to dense” haze is still present in Sumatra.

    The 3-hour PSI hit this year’s high of 341 at 5am on Friday before falling below 100 for a few hours, as all primary and secondary schools suspended classes. The Ministry of Education has said all schools will reopen on Monday, unless the air quality reaches Hazardous levels.

    Hazy conditions to persist through Sunday: NEA

    SINGAPORE: Slightly hazy conditions are still expected in Singapore on Saturday (Sep 26) though the air quality has improved, said the National Environment Agency (NEA).

     

    In an advisory, NEA said the 24-hour Pollutant Standards Index (PSI) for the next 24 hours is expected to be in the high end of the Moderate range and the low end of the Unhealthy range, and may enter into the mid-section of the Unhealthy range if denser haze is blown in.

     

    The 3-hour PSI at 6pm on Saturday was at 68, while the 24-hour PSI was 81-97, in the high-end of the Moderate range.

     

    NEA said widespread moderate to dense haze continues to be observed in central and southern Sumatra, and that some haze was also observed to have spread into the sea areas south of Singapore.

     

    It added that hazy conditions are still expected on Sunday, and there could be some deterioration in the air quality if denser haze is blown in by the unfavourable winds.

     

    The air quality was an improvement from Friday, when the 24-hour PSI entered Hazardous levels. NEA attributed the change to a shift in prevailing winds, adding that “moderate to dense” haze is still present in Sumatra.

     

    The 3-hour PSI hit this year’s high of 341 at 5am on Friday before falling below 100 for a few hours, as all primary and secondary schools suspended classes. The Ministry of Education has said all schools will reopen on Monday, unless the air quality reaches Hazardous levels.
