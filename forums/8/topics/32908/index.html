    Infernal affairs was the first in a long line of chinese movies to break the taboo of predictable plots: revenge, jealousy, money, love...

    Before that, most chinese movies spanned similar plots, or adapted storylines with the same old plot....just look at China Strike Force - the ultimate love, revenge, money show with the asian interpretation of a babe with boobs in Norika Fujiwara. Not to mention the pathetic attempt at using a mix of english and accented mandarin among the cast.

    If there's a reason to keep watching such shows, it would have to be the massive fan-base for artistes like Andy Lau and Tony Leung or Kelly Chen, Shu Qi, Vicki Zhao etc etc...

    If there's a reason to quit watching them...then you'd have to be the "sane-est" person on this planet. While the concept of "watch for the stars" still exists, an increasing viewership demands "shows with ellaborate plots".

    English shows have various mixes of sci-fi, investigative, horrer and imagination that make shows such as 6th sense and panic room worth the $6.50. While upcoming shows such as and come across as cheap flicks out to make a quick buck the same way as did (anyone remember the not-so-innocent Britney Spears in her hollywood debut?)

    I enjoyed infernal affairs for the multiple twists in the show. While i anticipate the upcoming blockbuster as a show with great cinematography, i doubt the plot will be anything other than love and greed.
