    most ATX board and micro-ATX boards can be mounted in a standard ATX case. But the smaller micro-ATX case cannot house an ATX board. There is usually no limitation in the PSU size althhough some are longer in length (or depth). The general cross section of any PC psu is the same, about 7" by 4" so there should be no problem fitting any one in a micro-ATX or standard ATX case. Power rating is not a concern in the case size too but rather the parts and application of your PC. However, you need to check your motherboard connector type if it supports 20 pin ATX or 24pin ATX2.0 but generally there is no standard or concern if an Intel cpu- supported board or AMD cpu-supported board will work with anytype/ brands od psu (but the rating, again is a concer though).
