    power consumption can be calculated as follows:-

    when power (watts) = v (voltage) x I (current)
    or P = V/ R2 (R, resistence)
    or P=I2 x R

    assumig you have a CD writer rated at 12v 5 amps, hdd rated at 12v 5 amps, motherboard (with CPU) installed rated at 24volts 10 amps, the ttotal may be so:-

    mainboard P=24 x 10 = 240 watts
    cd writer 12 x 5 = 60 watts
    hdd 12 x 5 = 60 watts

    total power drawn with this set up =240 + 60 + 60 = 360 watts. when using a 500 watts psu you are save to say that the power demand has not exceeded the rated psu limited. of course usually the mainboard will draw more power since it shall power up all the ram modules, CPU fan, graphics cars etc.....and if u intend to add more other stuff and tap power from the same psu...more wattage shall be demanded and you are nearer to an overloading
