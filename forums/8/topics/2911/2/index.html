    ForeVer:

    Hmm...what I am trying to put forward is that God needs us as much as we need him. But first let me put forward the notion that I am using the word <God> to refer to a higher power, an entity whose actions are incomprehensible to us. Please note that it is not specific to any particular religion.

    Humans by nature are very insecured beings who need assurances in life. In life, we find assurances in the form of our friends and family, people whom can support us through times of difficulties. But certain events are beyond us, and moral support can only bring us that far.

    Have you ever been confounded by a situation that is way beyond your control? Where the only thing you can do is to wait helplessly and watch? It is times like this where the best thing you can do is just say a prayer and hope for the best. Even as a non-follower of any religion, I do find myself doing that when faced with such a situation. It is a form of assurance knowing that my prayer may be heard by a higher power and that I have done my part to alleviate the situation, even though in actual fact I have done nothing. The prayer may mean noting in practical sense, but it does make me feel a lot better.

    Thus a follower of a religion would be able to find strength to tide over a crisis, as he/she will feel the <God> of their religion will look after them as they have devoted their faith to Him.

    Perhaps you can say <God> is a manisfestation of our race to counter our inner insecurities. Thus whether He exists to us or not depends on how much we believe in Him. That's why you always hear people say that <God> is present within each individual. It is up to us whether we want to accept Him.

    Just a thought about religion from a psychological point of view, hope nobody was offended by it.
