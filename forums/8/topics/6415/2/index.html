    A bible recommendation here:

    For those new to bible reading:
    THE STUDENT BIBLE (NIV version by Zondervan Publishing) is quite good with lots of interesting articles that explains the culture & customs of biblical times.

    THE QUEST STUDY BIBLE (NIV version by Zondervan), again lots of explanatory terms & notes & articles.

    For more in-depth understanding of the bible:
    LIFE APPLICATION BIBLE (NKJV version by Tyndale, NKJV is quite difficult to find, usually available in Living Translation or NIV), the notes are great - sometimes even longer than the bible verses itself. Tend to skip those really controversial verses but good enough.

    Oh, just bear with me a little while more, I would like to recommend a Christian author - Frederick Buechner, I have 3 of his books which I find very interesting & very useful to understanding the bible. These are "Peculiar Treasures - a biblical who's who", "Whistling in the Dark - a doubter's dictionary" & "Wishful thinking - a seeker's abc". For these three books, Buechner is observant, very humourous & he's not beyond sarcasm either. Borders has a good selection of Christian books and no I don't work there, wish I did though, then I can get staff discounts.
