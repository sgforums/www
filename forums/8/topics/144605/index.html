    GP fined, issued with warning after misdiagnosis led to death

    Channel NewsAsia
    25 July 2005

    A locum general practitioner (GP) has been fined $10,000 and censured by the Singapore Medical Council for professional misconduct.

    A disciplinary committee convicted him on two charges - for wilful neglect of his duties and grossly mismanaging the treatment of a patient.

    A nine-year-old boy died after Dr Kong Kok Leong failed to diagnose he was suffering from appendicitis.

    The boy was suffering from acute abdominal pain.

    His father brought him to see Dr Kong in January 2002 because his condition hadn't improved despite treatment from another GP two days earlier.

    Dr Kong examined the patient and diagnosed he was suffering from gastroenteritis.

    But the boy's condition got worse.

    He collapsed at home and was rushed to hospital where doctors operated on him for a perforated appendix and peritonitis - inflammation of the membranes lining the abdominal wall.

    The boy remained critically ill with severe sepsis and multi-organ dysfunction and died nearly three weeks later.
