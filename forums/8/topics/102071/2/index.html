    I got 255 cm (Sec 3) this year and that topped my class (pathetic, I know). Well, the technique is pretty simple. Like somebody mentioned earlier, aim further than you can reach; that would force your body to outdo your expectations. Also, using arms to your advantage is very important here. Your arms can really give you a lot of momentum to carry you forward. Next, you would also want to bend forward above the mat before your feet leaves the starting line. That would mean you have a head start. Now that you are mid-air, stay tucked all the time. Your knees should be as close as possible to your chest and you would want to keep your eyes focused on said target for as long as possible. Your arms should try to reach forward. All these combined would give you the maximum horizontal distance.

    Now, for the landing, you should NEVER untuck your knees from your chest. Doing that may give you a solid landing, but you would lose a hell lot of distance if you would to do so. Remained tucked throughout your jump.

    One last tip: Remove your shoes. They are heavy; they never help you with the leap. NEVER. Not to mention that most people actually wear one size larger, which means in actual fact, your toes are more or less one inch away from the starting line (but it appears that your feet is directly on the starting line, since what matters here is your shoes, not your feet inside). Way too much distance to give away. Hope you get a good idea on how to maximise your jump. Good luck.

    This is a bad tip. Getting a good pair of running shoes(I want to say tennis shoes but they are heavier as they usually come in high cut) will help you alot. If fact, if you really desperate, you should get shoes that super light, have very high insoles and are low cut.

    Training
    1) Do standing knee jumps. Learn to tuck your knee to your chest while in mid air. Aim to go higher each time. 10 reps of 3 sets. For advanced training, buy weight trainers, both chest weight and ankle weights, go to a sporting goods store they will know what i'm talking abt. Repeat 10 reps of 3 sets, trust me this time its alot harder with weights on!!!

    2) Afraid of Jumping far? Always jumping short? This is new form of training but guaranteed to work. Go to the deep end of the swimming pool and practice your standing board jumps into the pool, each time look forward.... 50 reps

    3) Now that you have no fear of jumping out to the open space, its time to do it on the ground with a bench place about 200cm from you and try jumping over that bench.
