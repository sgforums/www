    this is the difference wif sexual infidelity. here, just once would constitute betrayal. u dun need anything sustained like in the case of emotional betrayal. of cuz sex isn't always just sex. sometimes there are real emotions involved too. so here we would have betrayal at both levels.

    roughly, i have sketched the difference between the 2 kinds of infidelity. but as to which would hurt more, it would really depend on the person being hurt. it is not just the betrayal that hurts but what the betrayal actually means to you. if u value and treasure your emotional closeness to ur bf, then u will wonder what is it abt you that "drove" him to the other person. u will think there is something u lack that is made up for by the other party. and so u will be insecure abt your ability to connect (emotionally) wif ur bf. "what is it that i can't fulfill?", you'll ask yourself. "haven't i listened enuf?", "haven't i tried to understand you?". and more and more you may feel it's something abt you that is wrong or perhaps abt the two of you that cannot be bridged. the more u feel u have been doing fine and that u have been trying hard, the more hurt u will be if there is a betrayal.

    same thing goes for sexual betrayal. how have i not been able to satisfy him? was she better than me? the focus of insecurity is usu on what "i" haven't done or been able to do. but for me, i think that sexual betrayal is more hurtful. the reason why being that emotional infidelity is often reserved to your thots and feelings. but to actually go a step further and carry out those feelings in a sexual act is to go a step too far. the matter now becomes not just betrayal but how u could take that extra step. what kind of person would u be to take that extra step. one who has little control over your actions? one who takes sex lightly? i think a whole lot of questions are thrown up abt how a person is like if he takes that extra step. and so the hurt is multiplied by the fact that the person is other than who u expected him to be. u won't even know who that person is anymore. he would have done something totally out of character. notice the stress here is that he would have "done" sth out of character. not that he "thot" or "felt" sth out of character. and that makes all the difference. i can think of killing someone, but it takes another kind of person to carry it out, even under the most testing conditions. it becomes harder to trust someone like that. what must have driven him to actually carry out his desires?

    so while both kinds of infidelity would hurt (and it would hurt more the more worth u place on emotional or sexual fidelity, and the more u feel u have "invested" in that area of ur life), i think sexual betrayal would cast further doubt (and so potentially more hurt) abt the person u thot you knew and trusted.
