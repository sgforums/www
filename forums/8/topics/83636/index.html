    what are stem cells?

    all cells in our body have the same DNA(exactly the same). why is that yr skin cells look so diff from brain cells and why do they look diff from yr stomach cells ?

    simple. these cells are what is known as differentiated cells. meaning to say they are like general workers who are trained to do specific things. just like in uni or poly, before u choose a course, u are just another O level grad. after choosing(differentiation) u are an engineering student while yr friend is a IT student and yr classmate is an business student.

    the O lvl student is like a stem cell, undifferentiated. it has the same DNA as with all cells within the organism it was taken from.

    why are scientists so interested in stem cells?

    stem cells have the ability to become any cell in the body. u brain cells u got it, u want muscle cells, u got it. it's just like choosing a 'course in poly'
    needless to say, u need to be in a poly to choose yr course, so yr stem cells must be placed in yr brain for it to become a brain cell( this term is hopelessly misused )

    it has the potential to be used for treating brain illnesses like alzheimer's, parkinsons',etc. and it also can be used for treating many diseases. (this 1 u gotta check it out, i am not sure)

    stem cells used for research are taken from human embryos(yes u heard me, HUMAN, growing,dividing,living embryos). some say this is a direct breach of human rights(yah..tell that to US).
