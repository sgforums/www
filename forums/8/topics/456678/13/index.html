    I want to be able to live life

     

    There are four things I wish for when I am 34. Firstly, to live in a Singapore where success is not just academically-centred.

    In schools today, we are told that outstanding qualifications equate to a bright future. And, so, many of us strive to attain that A* - only to be told afterwards that we "still have areas to work on". There is never a point of satisfaction. So we try to achieve what is humanly impossible by sacrificing our rest, quality family time and social life.

    I remember when I cried while revising for my continual assessments, just because I couldn't seem to stuff everything into the minute brain of mine. I remember when I topped the class in a few subjects but could not feel even a hint of joy. I remember when my friend said: "Study and work so much then, in the end, we will die. What's the point?"

    In 20 years' time, I want my children to be able to balance school and play. I want them to have a childhood worth remembering. I want them to learn from school values that will help them tackle obstacles in life. I do not want yet another generation of living zombies.

    I wish for life, overall, to become less fast-paced. At 6.30am on the MRT, I see people looking glum and stressed, instead of excited over a brand new day. For many, life in Singapore seems to have become more like a fight for survival. Even the elderly are doing physically-demanding jobs like cleaning.

    In 20 years' time when I am into my career, I want a job that allows me to spend quality time with my family. I want to be free from overwhelming financial worries.

    I want to work in an environment where everyone is beaming with fulfilment. I want to be able to sit back and enjoy the beauty of life. I don't just want to exist; I want to live.

     

    Writer is 14 years old!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!

    by Charis Sim
