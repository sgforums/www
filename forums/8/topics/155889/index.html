Right move to proceed with Bali retreat

  • dragg
    5 Oct 05, 06:29
    i extracted this article from the straits times forum.

    I REFER to the article, 'PM Lee to meet Indonesian President in Bali today' (ST, Oct 3).

    My heart goes out to the victims of the recent Bali bomb blasts and their families. I agree with Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong that we should be strong and not 'concede' to the terrorists.

    As a citizen, I support our leaders' decision to proceed with the Bali retreat with President Susilo Bambang Yudhoyono and the Indonesian ministers this week. I respect their courage.

    We should indeed continue to support our Indonesian counterparts in their efforts to recover and battle against our common foe, the terrorists.

    As a Singaporean, I am also concerned about the safety of our leaders as PM Lee, along with our key Cabinet ministers - Foreign Affairs Minister George Yeo, Industry Minister Lim Hng Kiang and Defence Minister Teo Chee Hean will be present together in Bali, the two-time target of terrorists.

    I share the hope that this retreat will enable our countries to further strengthen our ties of friendship and boost our efforts in jointly countering the threats of terrorism.

    In Singapore, we should continue to build our defences and preparedness against the eventual terrorist attack. Vigilance and resilience are essential in this long-term battle against terrorism.

    We citizens can do our part in being alert to threats and preparing for emergencies.

    We have fire drills regularly. We should also have regular defence drills so that our people will be better prepared against terrorist attacks and the aftermath.

    Edmund Lim Wee Kiat
