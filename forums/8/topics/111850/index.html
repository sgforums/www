    Well internet transactions are not as safe as we all know and Internet Fraud involving credit cards are not as easily disputable as physical fraud or loss. In internet transactions, It requires quite a tedious process to really pin point whether it is a genuine fraud or the card holder is trying to pull a fast one.

    In cases of counterfeit, most banks will absorb most losses except in special cases. Outright cases are you are in Singapore holding on to the card but transactions are going on in another country. In this case, most card holders are absolved from any blame because there is nothing they can do really. Things to watch out for when using your credit card in foreign countries:

    1) Do not let the card be taken from your sight.
    2) If possible, use cash especially for small amounts.
    3) You may be surprised, in HOT countries, hotels and popular shops can be the place where your card are counterfeited. In fact, anywhere is a possibility.

    The advice is that, every end of the day after you finished using your card overseas, make it a point to check with the bank the next day a summary of your expenditure. Most counterfeits only need a few hours to issue a counterfeit card once they collect your information. Thereafter, a few hours later, you will most probably see trial transactions involving negligent amounts like $20++ or $1 at places like petrol stations and once those transactions go through, within hours, yuor card would most likely be maxed at places selling jewelleries, electronics and expensive and resellable products.

    In cases of LOSS, if the fraudulent transactions takes place after you report loss, you are safe. If it is before, it is hard because again, some card holders may be trying to pull a fast one.

    So whatever it is, take care while using cards overseas. In fact, if you can, dun use it at all unless necessary.
