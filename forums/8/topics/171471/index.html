    Marina Bay New Year countdown promises visual extravaganza

    SINGAPORE : As the year draws to a close, a new tradition begins at the Marina Bay.

    A New Year countdown spectacle awaits families and loved ones on December 31.

    Some 2,500 lighted spheres will dot the Marina Bay waterfront, setting the bay aglow come New Year's Eve.

    These spheres, with New Year wishes written on them by Singaporeans from all walks of life, have been released into the waters of Marina Bay at the Esplanade .

    Together with a state-of-the-art lighting and music display, the countdown event promises to be an extravagant visual arts presentation -- the first of its kind in Singapore.

    Said Benoit Milord, executive producer of Marina Bay Countdown, "The lighting is one element of the Marina Bay countdown. What we are doing is to symbolise the wish. The wish is symbolised with white spheres on the water and the lighting will symbolise the soul of the water itself."

    This is just part of the programme for the Marina Bay countdown, which will culminate in a nine-minute long firework extravaganza at the Esplanade Waterfront.

    Said Mr Milord, "At midnight, the fireworks come and take the lead, because fireworks are light; they take the lead and take the wishes to bring the wish into the sky."

    Throughout the evening, there will be performances and roving artists complete with video dedications from the public.

    Said Pearl Lee, director of corporate communications, Esplanade Theatres on the Bay, "I think in tone and character, it's going to be very different; it's very special. This isn't like the sort of head-banging parties that you traditionally associate with New Year's Eve. This is a very meaningful, inspirational event."

    And with a turnout of about 100,000 expected, the event organisers at the Esplanade hope to start the New Year with a big bang. - CNA /ct
