    Dude, I know Huskies are handsome, and damn cool. I love them too. But before you make your final decision, I'll list you some details you might want to know more first.

    1. They have 2 layers of coat, which is absoutely not suitable for a climate like we are in. Imagine at winter countries, they can live happily under like minus 30 degrees? In fact, they snuggle under the snow to keep warmth. So, if you insist in keeping one, you have to trim its fur frequently, and it will turn out that he won't be as handsome as what you think he would be.

    2. Huskies are the direct evolution of their wolves ancestors. They have very dominant characteristics. True, all dogs are loyal, but Huskies might get a little tricky. Extra caution has to be observed when dealing with small kids.

    3. Large breeds, need a lot of exercise. Plenty. If not they will get plump, and heavy. Large dogs are prone to an illness known as Hip Dysplasia. It is something like arthritis of the hips, in dogs. It will cause extreme pain as the ball part of his bone and socket joint will wear, tear, and rub against each other. It will heavily affect their lifestyle, they will get depressed, and they can't even walk.

    There are only 3 ways to get this settled.

    - Go for an operation, which is extremely costly. If I'm not mistaken, they don't do ops like this locally. You might have to send him oversea. This info may be a little outdated though. You might want to check it out.

    - Put him/her to sleep. Note, Hip Dysplasia can be found in breeds as early as 5 to 6 months old. It can be hereditary.

    - Let it be, keep him by your side selfishly, cause you definitely can't bear to let him go. I've seen a Golden Retriever suffering from Dysplasia. The owner walked him, that dog was limping, or dragging his hind legs rather. He was obviously in pain. I felt him, too.

    3. As simple as it is, Huskies, like wolves, howl. You live in HDB, which is illegal to keep a Husky. So if it howls, how would your neighbours react? Don't you even dream of removing its vocal, like what they do in China.

    I'm not scaring you, I'm just desuading you. In the first place, Huskies shouldn't even be allowed in Singapore, unless you can afford to keep him cool 24/7.

    Like you, I've seriously thought of getting one. I didn't.
