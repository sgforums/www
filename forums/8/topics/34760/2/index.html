    I'm so sorry to hear about your friend Curad. So sad...

    Eventhough so many people say to cheer up and move on, that's the WORSE thing to do right now!

    It's OK to feel sad, to break down and cry or feel depressed. We have to go through the hurtin' if we want to feel the healin'!

    When humans face great loss, they go through 5 stages:

    1) Denial - They don't want to believe that the other person or thing is gone. They pretend like that person is still around and refuse to acknowledge the loss. This is a defense mechanism. At this stage, it is important to gently, but clearly remind oneself of reality.

    2) Sadness - Slowly, no matter how much they deny, they soon begin to realise that that other person is gone, and start to feel great sadness. This sadness is natural, and everybody must go through it. Here is where the emotional and practical support of friends and family play an important part.

    3) Same as Sadness, but worse. As one go on day by day, the sadness becomes depression. Nothing seems to matter anymore. You may stop eating, loose interest in things and people that were once important to you, become irritable and have mood swings...if the depression is severe, you become a 'totally different' person. At this time, it is important to ACKNOWLEDGE that you are not a superhero and need the help of close friends and family who UNDERSTAND what's happening to you.

    It is a very good idea to seek professional help if the depression becomes so severe that it affects and changes your life -loose job, loose friends, start doing irrational things etc. If not, you may end up killing someone or even yourself. Often, deprerssion and anger go hand-in-hand.

    4) Anger - "How can god do this to me?" "What have I done wrong?" "If only I had seen him or her sooner? " .....When reality takes a frim grip, anger is another reaction which often surfaces as we can not or do not want to believe that something like this should or could happen. Also, sometimes, that anger is directed at ourselves - blaming ourselves for that person's death.

    5) Acceptance & Reconciliation or Recovery
    With UNDERSTANDING that no matter what the circumstances and what we want in life, death is inevitable, and once a person has gone through all the above stages, can a person BEGIN to recover and move on in life.

    While many people recover from the loss, RECONCILIATION is a different matter as it is coming to full terms with life and death and the hurt and loss you feel. Not many people fully go through and UNDERSTAND (or want to understand) what happens to them in life (they still focus on the pain or anger).

    It is important to note that all the above stages (except perhaps the last) do not have to happen in sequence, and often, signs of more than 1 or 2 different stages can happen at the same time Curad.

    So, if you feel sad, don't allow ANYONE to tell you not to, or to surpress it. It's God gift of release and part of the Healing Process. Just know that if you need help, there is no shame in asking for it.

    Take your OWN time and just know that life is beautiful...that's all.
