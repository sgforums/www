    For cases like these, the Police will require the victim's help to lodge a Magistrate's Complaint. Thereafter, the Magistrate will decide from three possible courses of action:

     

    1) mediation: usually in cases where the person is known, the Magistrate will summon the both of you to appear before him / her in court. He / she will then act as a mediator for you both. This is usually the first step, and the most common course of action.

    2) Police  intervention: if necessary, the Magistrate will direct the complaint to the Police, for the investigator to commence further, more detailed investigations.

    3) The Magistrate will, upon his / her discretion and analysis of the case on its own merits, direct both parties to seek recourse via their own legal means [i.e. hire own lawyers] as he / she deems it a trivial matter and a waste of Police /  Court resources to intervene in such a case.
