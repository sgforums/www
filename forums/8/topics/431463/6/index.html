    A dispute at chicken eatery Nando's over a request for hot water had popular actress Joanne Peh walking out in a huff to applause from wait staff. And she has taken her unhappiness online.

    On Monday and Tuesday, the Mediacorp artist, 28, tweeted about her bad experience at the mid-range restaurant, which serves grilled chicken and has three branches in Singapore.

    Tweets such as 'Somebody please save this restaurant. They have no idea what service and initiative means' and 'The collective applause fr ur staff is outright disrespectful. Fix the attitude.' went out to more than 20,000 followers of Peh's account, @JoannePeh.

    In response, Nando's tweeted an apology the following day: 'We saw ur tweets abt ur experience and would like to sincerely apologise for this incident.'

    Representatives of the company also called the actress to apologise, according to Peh.

    But the actress was still fuming when she related the incident to Life! in a telephone interview on Tuesday.

    She had gone to the Tanglin Mall branch of Nando's on Monday for dinner with her deejay boyfriend Bobby Tonelli, 36.

    After placing their orders, Peh asked for hot water but was told by wait staff that this was not possible. She then asked to speak to the manager, who again said that Nando's does not serve hot water.

    'The conversation started out cordial, but after a while, I started raising my voice in bewilderment. The manager actually told us he once got into trouble for serving hot water, which I have never heard of before,' says Peh.

    The actress says the couple were eventually told that hot water would cost $3.90, which is the price of a cup of tea at Nando's.

    After Tonelli pointed out that a bottle of water costs only $1.80, the manager agreed to charge them that price.

    This agreement was apparently not conveyed to his staff, who were still 'dumbfounded' when the couple asked for hot water again. The couple then decided to leave, about half an hour after they walked in.

    Peh claims that when they walked off, the wait staff, including the manager, clapped. The actress stresses that her unhappiness was not over having to pay for the water, but the 'poor communication and lack of initiative'. She also finds the applause 'disrespectful', calling the entire incident a 'bizarre experience'.

    When asked, Peh says she was aware of Nando's policy not to serve water and that there was no verbal abuse or personal attacks from either side during the entire exchange.

    In response to queries from Life!, Nando's regional marketing manager Fanny Chai says: 'As beverage sales is a key source of business revenue, it is our corporate decision not to serve water on the house.'

    She adds that staff members usually offer bottled water or a hot beverage such as tea as an alternative, when customers request warm water.

    This was not clearly explained to Peh at the time, resulting in a 'misunderstanding', says Ms Chai.

    She declines to comment or confirm if wait staff had clapped when Peh walked out, saying that internal investigations of the incident are ongoing.

    Charging for tap or hot water is not an unusual practice. A check with 15 restaurants here revealed that eight of them charge for tap water or hot water. Costs and practices vary. Bali Thai, which serves Thai and Indonesian cuisine, serve complimentary tap water but charges 50 cents for hot water. Barcelos Flame Grilled Chicken charges $2.45 for a 600ml bottle of mineral water and does not usually serve hot water.

    When asked if Nando's will now consider serving free water, Ms Chai says: 'We are still assessing the situation at this point and will take this into consideration.'
