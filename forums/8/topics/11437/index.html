    I think I'm in a very relevant topic!
    I was lucky to comission from OCS in Mar 1997, and was later posted to BMTC as a platoon commander for a while. I know the OCS entrance criterias well cos it was then my turn to choose ppl to go OCS. Heehee...
    Well, what some of the replies above are true. Especially the fact that you must be at least a diploma or A levels holder.
    Okie... now for some tips to getting that much desired epaulette and the Sword of Command:
    Performance in BMT DEFINITELY plays a big part. The PCs will choose potential cadets based on
    1) Sit test. You have to be damn fuking on to be noticed by the sit test umpire. The answer you provide for your quest does not have to be the model answer. But more impt, you shd stay calm, composed and organised. Make sure you look into aspects like giving good commanding instructions, the safety of your team, the encouragement you give to your team to carry on, etc.... And remember: during sit test, volunteer, volunteer, volunteer!
    2) Daily attitude during BMT. Make sure you win your platoonmates' trust and popularity by helping out the weaker ones, volunteering yourself for extra duties and fatiques, etc.... Bcos at the end of your BMT saga, peer appraisal will be a determining factor.
    3) When your PC ask you if you want to go OCS. Say "Yes". It's not the time to be humble here.
    4) Your training performance. Things like SOC, IPPT, field-training, marksmanship, should be taken seriously. No one wants an officer who can't shoot, run, or conceal, right? But don despair if you don do well, or even fail any of these. It's your training attitude that we platoon commanders look for the most. I had got OCS mates who went into OCS failing their IPPT or other stuff. But of course lar, they had a fuking hard time in OCS later on....
    5) Field camp. One of the biggest event in BMT is the field camp. I've only got to say: Do fuking well. It's very important bcos the commanders will be on the lookout for those who can survive under outfield conditions, and under pressure.
    6) Don get despaired by poor results. Like I said, it's your training attitude that commanders look for the most. Results are important, but secondary.
    7) Don be a show-off. I had got really showy recruits who come in with NCC cadet-lt rank, or airborne badge, or whatever fuking knowledge prior to their enlistment to BMT. We don give a fuking damn. I am very against show-offs. I gave these show-offs a really hard time, and made sure they never get to go OCS. I mean, it's important that you display your knowledge if you have them. But do it a humble way. Trust me, we know who are the ones with 'stuff', and who not.
    Don break the rules (well, at least don break it too often). Needs no explanation right?
    9) To me, this is the most important. DON BE A CCB HYPOCRITE!!!!!!!!! We know it when you act wayang, or when you boot-lick, or when you show different attitudes in front, and at the back of the commaders.

    Hmmm.... that's it for now. Will post more suggestions if I can think of any. Oh yar, don despair if you don't get any leadership appointment during BMT. I never got any oso.
    But trust me, OCS is going to be one hell of hard time (well, at least during my time it was). Here's wishing you all the best!

    True that there are differences in chances if you are from different batches. As far as I remember, the January BMT batch (i.e. enter OCS around April) will be huge bcos OCS will be taking in cadets for all three arms (i.e. Army, Airforce, Navy). What's more, they will be places for various vocations from all three arms, e.g. artillery, infantry, signallers, armor, commandos, ADO, ADAO, Pilot, etc etc. But the Mar/Apr batch is smaller in comparison bcos they mainly take in infantry officer cadets, plus a handful from Airforce and Navy. Thus it is natural that there will be less places then. The President will usually be at the January (BMT) commissioning parade bcos it is the largest and grandest of the yr.
    As to your question regarding the sit test, it is actually called "situational test". It is a 3-day outfield camp, whereby recuits will face many quests and challenges thruout the 3 days. Each recruit will wear a number tag (for identification and grading purposes) and will be asked to complete some quests (such as "how to get the ammo box across the river with 2 poles?" kind of questions) as a team of 6 to 10. The leader of the team will be rotated at every station. Like I said in my earlier posts, sometimes you will not complete the mission. But the umpire will grade each individual based on their calmness, attitude, thinking, ideas, leadership ability, charisma, and overall performance. Also, at each station, the umpire doesn't only grade the 'leader', but each and every individual in the team.
