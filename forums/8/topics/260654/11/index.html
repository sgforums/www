    Yes you may be outnumbered, but the vital advantage is that you must be willing to die to protect yourself, while the average robber isn't usually willing to die to rob or get injured robbing somebody. If you communicate quite clearly that despite being outnumbered, you can take out or even kill a few of them before you finally go down it is a very big discouragement. Many just want a simple meal.

    But if it really comes down to it.

    And if it really comes to the fight and you have no choice, go straight for the leader if you can. Taking out the leader usually takes a lot of the fight out of the group.

    Also, if you can, try to grab one of your opponents in a lock and use him as a shield, keep redirecting your opponents into each other to tie them up and use positioning so that you never have to face more then one at a time (not to mention they have to get through their friends to get to you). Poisitioning is key to surviving.

    It is usually beyond your skill level to fight all of them using hard martial arts, and grappling arts like BJJ (though very effective 1 v 1) are not very useful. So one has to apply a lot of soft martial arts to survive in a multiple opponent battle. The idea is not to floor them all, floor those you have to floor and redirect the rest away, make an opening and make a run for it.

    Also, it is important not to let them get a grip on you, so learning grip breaking is important.

    It is not impossible, just very difficult and dangerous. But there are people who have sucessfully defended themselves against multiple opponents before, but they are an exception.
