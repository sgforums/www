    The first thing you learn in self defence is how to avoid getting into trouble in the first place.

    The second thing you need to learn is how to win without fighting, and that is either by defusing the situtation or simply running away, especially if the robber does not have a ranged weapon or leaves you with an escape route (not surrounded).

    Despite what we learn, things like weapon (gun/knife) disarms are to be used only as a last resort (like the guy really trying to kill you) and to give you the knowledge of what to do if you really have no choice.

    I have a bowie knife and know that a good cut by it is usually to the bone, and most people don't stab or slash at you like in your drills and there is just too little margin for error. Factor in the possibility of multiple opponents and you see just how dangerous it can be. Run if you can, and fight only as a last resort.

    If faced with a knife, the best thing to do is simply to run or get a ranged weapon or shield (like a chair) to defend yourself. Carrying a knife yourself might get you in trouble with the law and chances are the robber would have deployed his piece long before you do and you need at least 12-20 feet of space to draw your own safely assuming he lunges at you the moment you try to get your own piece. Never fight a knife unarmed unless you have no choice, the knife defence skills you learn are good, but they are not to be abused in their application that might get you killed.

    Running works if the person does not have a firearm to do his robbery. Never go into a space you are not sure you can't escape in quick order. And yes, travel in groups if you can.

    In the couple's case, they simply should have kept running after they reached the dead end where a car cannot go... it is unlikely the robbers would follow them on foot if they have gotten a good headstart. But of course the girl might not be up to the run.
