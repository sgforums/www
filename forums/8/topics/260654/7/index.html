    The first thing you learn in self defence is how to avoid getting into trouble in the first place.

    The second thing you need to learn is how to win without fighting, and that is either by defusing the situtation or simply running away, especially if the robber does not have a ranged weapon or leaves you with an escape route (not surrounded).

    Despite what we learn, things like weapon (gun/knife) disarms are to be used only as a last resort (like the guy really trying to kill you) and to give you the knowledge of what to do if you really have no choice.

    I have a bowie knife and know that a good cut by it is usually to the bone, and most people don't stab or slash at you like in your drills and there is just too little margin for error. Factor in the possibility of multiple opponents and you see just how dangerous it can be. Run if you can, and fight only as a last resort.

    If faced with a knife, the best thing to do is simply to run or get a ranged weapon or shield (like a chair) to defend yourself. Carrying a knife yourself might get you in trouble with the law and chances are the robber would have deployed his piece long before you do and you need at least 12-20 feet of space to draw your own safely assuming he lunges at you the moment you try to get your own piece. Never fight a knife unarmed unless you have no choice, the knife defence skills you learn are good, but they are not to be abused in their application that might get you killed.

    Running works if the person does not have a firearm to do his robbery. Never go into a space you are not sure you can't escape in quick order. And yes, travel in groups if you can.

    In the couple's case, they simply should have kept running after they reached the dead end where a car cannot go... it is unlikely the robbers would follow them on foot if they have gotten a good headstart. But of course the girl might not be up to the run.

    Alternatively, the best way to react to a dude trying to ram you off the road or chasing your car is to engage him directly and aggressively (assuming his car is not an overmatch for yours).

    And yes, don't drive into roads you have no idea off... as long as your car is moving, even if it's very slowly you are quite safe and the robbers can't get you (even if they keep ramming you).

    Alternatively a weapon that is legal to carry around in your car would be the humble stick, which can come in many forms like the japanese bo staff or chinese gun, or even kali sticks.

    A bat or crowbar is nice as a club, but it is inflexible and cannot be effectively used to parry or block. While it can be used by newbies, it's not really the ultimate in legal self-defence stick weapons. While you are on more even odds carrying a bat or crowbar with a robber with a knife, your chance of getting wounded seriously or even killed is still quite high if you are not trained.

    Takes a bit of skill the learn, but in the hands of a skilled user a staff fighter can easily hold off or even defeat multiple people using shorter knifes or parangs. Generally the range advantage can be used to disarm or discourage your opponents with shorter ranged weapons by hitting their weapon hands or trapping their weapons and the strikes carried by the staff can approach over 2000 lbs of force at the end. This is nearly twice the power required to knock somebody out cold.

    And of course, attach a legal-to-carry pocketknife to the end of the staff with duct tape if you got time and you got an improvised spear, use with trusting attacks and the person at the recieving end of it will be discouraged very fast.

    If staffs are too long for you then you could try dual stick fighting aka. kali sticks style. Against an untrained opponent with a knife it can be extremely useful if you keep your distance and aim for his knife hand with punishing strikes or alternatively using one stick to tie up and parry his knife and the other to land punishing strikes. Kali sticks can land blows with forces of up to 1000 lbs and the strikes can be chained up more rapidly then with a staff combo. Note that in history kail stick users were quite sucessful in fending off bayonets when they were used in wars. Not to mention they are easy to carry concealed.

    Though all this takes time to learn and be good at, but well worth it when push comes to shove.
