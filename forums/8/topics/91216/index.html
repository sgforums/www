    If your trip is abt sightseeing, I think 1k should be fine cause it will set you back abt $4500HKD.

    A decent meal will set you back abt $30~$40HKD, not in restaurant; they will cost more. Let's say a normal day out three meal and traveling will be at least $200HKD. Note prices will be different in HK island, Kowloon and N.T.

    Word of advise, purchase a store value card, like the one back in SG Ezlink; here they call it Octopus Card. The Card Deposit is $50HKD which can be refunded when your trip end at any MTR station. The Card allows you to travel on public transports like Ferry, MTR, Buses and Mini Bus, also you can use it to purchase food or drink in most store like 7eleven, watson..etc. Vending Machine also accept the use of the card. Taxi service is very expensive in HK, unless needed; advise use public transport; HK MTR service takes you to most of HK area.

    If you are going to Ocean Park, a day out will set you back around $200~$350HKD(include food & drinks). Admission on side is $185HKD, all rides & show are free; If you are taking the CITYBUS Tour is $206HKD, tickets include admission and round trip transport from Admiralty MTR station or Star Ferry Pier. Try out the free ride, cable car is free, the shark & coral aqurium..etc. IS a full day event go early in the morning.

    The Peak, same as usual take the MTR and alight at Admiralty station look for the bus terminal exit same as Ocean Park exit take CityBus 125 $2.50HKD. Alight at the Peak Tram Terminus, dun worry every bus in HK have the next stop display on the LED screen infront of the bus. If you choose to walk exit alight at Central MTR station exit J2, look for Lippo tower walk towards it; takes abt 10mins. The peak tram will cost $30HKD, tru & flow. The last trian off the peak will be at 12midnight dun miss it, it will cost abt $300HKD to take a taxi down the peak to the nearest MTR station. Advise take your meal before you head up, best timming will be at around 7~9pm.

    Lantau island, catch a ferry from the central pier take abt 6mins. Enjoy the view & breeze sit on the upper deck. The only sight is the 26-metre-high Giant Buddha, the 19century fort.

    When in HK head down to the night market in Mong Kong & Yau Ma Tei, close around midnight, like Sg parsa malam but bigger scale & lots more stuff to view around.Flower market, GoldFish Market, Ladies Market, Temple St, Jade market...start early around 8pm after dinner and start your walk. Try some of the roadside food delight, is quiet nice & value for money, no worries is clean & you wont get food poisoning.

    Guess is answer most of your question, should require addition info. let me know.

    Enjoy ur trip to HK!
