    its a legal and insurance requirement that you wear eye protection [using real firearms-ballistic rated polycarbonate protection, though paintball masks are also used, though they tend to be restrictive when aiming] when you play with these.

    there have already been ballistic tests with airsoft gun carried out in various countries.
    thats why 1J [328fps] is considered the safe limit for a full auto capable weapon.
    at close range it can leave a mark, and though not obviously tested, its said that though the plastic ball might bruise the eyeball, it would not pierce it.
    at anything other than point blank, airsoft plastic balls are considered considerably safer than paintballs at those velocities.

    the sniper rifle at 500fps is subject to site rules [in some cases, military sites only], insurance and minimum engagement distances [over 20-25m only], so there would be no chance of injury, and to date there never has been.
