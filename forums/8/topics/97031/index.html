    Girl, 4, thrown off 6th floor

    Woman dashes towards road after the incident, chased by girl's mother who catches her and hands her to the police

    By Ben Nadarajan

    A FOUR-YEAR-OLD girl was flung off the sixth-storey corridor of a Telok Blangah block early yesterday, minutes after neighbours were woken up by a heated quarrel in her home.

    Sindee's fall was broken by this shelter. The woman in custody is under arrest for attempted murder. -- LAU FOOK KONG
    Sindee Neo is now fighting for her life in the intensive care unit at KK Women's and Children's Hospital (KKH).

    Moments after the incident, the girl's Thai mother chased down a woman and took her to a nearby police station.

    The 36-year-old Singaporean woman was detained and questioned for about 12 hours before being arrested for attempted murder.

    The suspect, who is unemployed, is a friend of Sindee's father, a Singaporean in his early 30s. The exact nature of their relationship is unknown.

    After being flung off, the girl crashed through an aluminium shelter near the lift lobby of Block 1, Telok Blangah Crescent and fell face-first onto the concrete floor.

    Sindee Neo fell face-first onto the concrete floor.
    Although the metal structure helped break her fall, the impact left her with a fractured jaw, and she bled profusely from head injuries.

    She was taken to the Singapore General Hospital, where she underwent surgery.

    Sources said she had bled so badly that she needed about 10 packets of blood. Each packet contains about 400ml of blood.

    For a brief period yesterday morning, Sindee seemed to be on the mend. Her condition stabilised and she was transferred to KKH, which has paediatric specialists.

    But a hospital spokesman said last night that the child was still in critical condition.

    The metal roof of this shelter was damaged when Sindee fell from her block. Her condition is critical. -- LAU FOOK KONG
    Her parents, who were at the hospital, did not want to be interviewed.

    Their neighbours said that 'loud shouting and vulgar words' from the Neo family's second-floor unit woke them early in the morning. It is not known how the girl went from the second floor to the sixth, where she was flung off.

    When they heard a loud thud, they rushed out and saw a woman in a floral dress dashing towards the main road. Mrs Neo, who was barefoot, chased after her.

    She caught up with the woman in the middle of Henderson Road then wrestled her into a taxi and handed her to officers at the Central Police Division headquarters in Cantonment Road.

    Meanwhile, Mr Neo, an odd-job labourer, tended to his daughter and called for an ambulance.

    Attempted murder carries a maximum punishment of life imprisonment.
