    as kohanson said, it's maths

    take two digits, eg 6 and 4
    add them together and you get 6+4 =10
    put them together and you get 64
    subtract one from the other and you get 64-10=54

    now let's use algebra :
    take two digits, eg a and b
    add them together and you get a + b
    when you put them together, you are actually getting 10a + b
    subtract one from the other, you get (10a + b) - (a + b) = 9a

    in other words, no matter what digits you started off with, you will always end up with a multiple of nine. Look at the symbols on the chart - all the multiples of 9 (up to 81) have the same symbol - that's the answer they always give.
