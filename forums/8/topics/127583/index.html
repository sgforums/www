    S'pore gives green light to casino project

    SINGAPORE - Singapore announced on Monday that it will allow construction
    of two casinos, gambling that the decision can boost its economy and juice
    up its image.

    Critics fear residents of one of Asia's safest cities will succumb to vice
    with casinos on their shores for the first time.

    Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong revealed the government's decision in
    Parliament after months of mulling the economic pros and social cons.
    Singapore has received nearly 20 proposals from international gaming giants
    angling to build a casino in Southeast Asia's most prosperous country.

    "The Cabinet has decided to proceed with the project," Mr Lee said.

    Singapore's leaders had encouraged citizens to speak up on the casino plan,
    triggering lively exchanges in the media, on Internet chat sites, in
    offices and homes. For a while Singaporeans appeared to be split evenly in
    the debate - unusual in that Singaporeans so rarely challenge their
    government's plans.
