    there are many forms of non-verbal communications

    chronemics - study of meanings, usage and communications of time
    proxemics - communication of interpersonal space and distance (comfort zone)
    kinesics - facial expressions, body movements, gestures, conversational regulators
    haptics - patterns of tactile communication, the amt of touch, location, type and public or private manifestation
    physical appearance - dressing
    oculesics - the study of messages sent by the eyes, including eye contact, blinks, eye movement and even pupil dilation
    vocalics - paralanguage.....tone, pitch, pauses, volume
    olfactics - the study of smell

    all the above are highly dependent on the individual's culture and background and perhaps education and values....
