    Your blood type is determined by the presence or absence of two antigens – A and B – on the surface of red blood cells. A third antigen, called Rh factor, will either be present or absent (positive or negative). Antigens are substances that may trigger an immune response, causing your body to launch an attack if it believes they are foreign.

    Taken together, these factors determine the right type of blood for your body, should you need a transfusion. Receiving the wrong type can be catastrophic, even resulting in death.
