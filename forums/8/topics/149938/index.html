    This symbol goes back to the days of ancient Rome. In the early modern period, in the Fraktur script, which was widepread though Europe in the Middle Ages, one slash (/) represented a comma, while two slashes (//) represented a Dash. The two slashes eventually evolved into a sign similar to the Equals sign (=), then being further simplified to a single dash (-).
