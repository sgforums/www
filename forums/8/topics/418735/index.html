    What's the reason for this sudden cross?

    From my understanding, you are unable to change due to 3 main reasons.

     

    • depending on the type of unit it is - If it's an Sea or Air unit, there will be lots of fundermentals being taught from Sec 1 onwards. Take for example Sea units have undergone  1 star kayaking and  2 star kayaking during Sec 1 and Sec 2 respectively. As for land units, thought they might not have any specific out of the norm activities, Cadets since Secondary one would have learnt plentiful, such as individual field craft, rifle drills (Nearly the same syllabus as NCC, but there are their differences), foot drills, orienteering.
    • Cadets since Sec 1 have undergone rifle stripping and assembly, individual marksmanship training (a simulator with a modified gun), and they will do their live firing during Sec 3. 
    • Cadets would also have gone through 2 NCC HQ camps - Camp steel and Camp feast, which are essential to developing and shaping the cadet as an individual in accordance to the 5 Core values. 
    I hope this help you to understand why you are not able to cross into NCC at such a stage. During Secondary 3, there will be live firing (safety aspect, if you do not have any background/foundation in handling the weapon) and cadets will take up leadership positions AFTER going through a HQ certification/course - specialist course. Thus the foundations since secondary one are very important.

    Hello there John,

    I'm sorry but unfortunately, Kenneth is right. For uniformed groups, you are only allowed to switch in the beginning of secondary 2. For land cadets, you would have completed the basic syllabus of Individual Field Craft/Drills/Trainfire/Orienteering by the end of secondary 2. Your life as a secondary 3 cadet, or part 'C' will be a very hectic one as you have alot of leadership courses such as Specialist Course and Senior Specialist course which requires you to be proficient in your secondary 1 and 2 fundamentals. Thus, I suggest you stick with your current CCA to avoid disappointment if you manage to switch CCAs.
