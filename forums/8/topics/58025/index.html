    $15,000 fine for sex chat on radio station
    Regulator fines owner of WKRZ 91.3FM after woman agrees to simulate sex act on air and talks about her sex life; DJ has been sacked

    By Loh Keng Fatt

    A FINE of $15,000 has been handed down to UnionWorks for raunchy content on its English radio station WKRZ 91.3FM.

    The Media Development Authority of Singapore (MDA), the industry regulator, announced the fine yesterday.

    In a statement released on its website, it said The Morning Show programme, broadcast on July 27, had featured explicit discussions on sex.

    The Straits Times' Life! section then ran a story that DJ Pete Leung had requested a 21-year-old female listener to simulate a sex act for him on air. She agreed and also openly discussed her sexual preferences with him, including what she and her boyfriend did.

    The MDA told Life! that it was aware of what had happened on the show and was looking into the matter with the two-year-old station.

    The statement yesterday said that it had received complaints from the public that the content had been distasteful and offensive.

    The MDA said it had reviewed the programme and 'found WKRZ's broadcast of the sexually explicit conversations to be obscene and in bad taste'.

    It added that 'WKRZ has breached the MDA's Radio Programme Standards & Censorship Code' pertaining to obscene and offensive sexual content.

    'Having considered UnionWorks' explanation of the matter, and taking into account the severity of the breach, the MDA has decided to fine UnionWorks $15,000,' the statement said.

    When contacted, Mr Bernard Law, chief operating officer for UnionWorks, said: 'We have paid the fine. We accept that what happened was distasteful.'

    He added that the station had assisted the MDA in its investigation and had tightened its internal processes.

    'In fact, we sacked the DJ the day after the show,' he said.

    UnionWorks, a joint venture by SPH MediaWorks and NTUC Media, also runs Mandarin radio station UFM 100.3.
