    Originally posted by Diehard_89:
    this is one method

    is the area under graph another method?
    Area under graph does give u the answer. I'm not sure whether it is allowed, although I think it should be unless the question specifically states that differentiation and integration must be used.

    If you use the graph method:
    Just take note that the intercept of the x-axis (time) is the time for the turning point of the particle. It is the point at which the velocity is zero. The area after this point will be a negative if you do normal integration because the particle is now travelling backwards. However, we are concerned with distance here, and hence u will need to take the positive value, and add it to the area above the x-axis before this intercept.
