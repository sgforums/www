    Quoted from channelnewsasia

    For two days next month, some RSAF jets will not be landing at Tengah Airbase, but on a stretch of public road instead.

    Part of Lim Chu Kang Road and Old Lim Chu Kang Road will be closed on November 9 and 10 for the Emergency Runway Exercise.

    Lim Chu Kang Road will also be closed on November 7 to prepare for the exercise.

    Jets like the F-16, usually land on a three-kilometre long runway.

    But come November, they will land and take off from a two-kilometre long public road, and that's also just half as wide as a normal runway.

    Six months of work went into preparing Lim Chu Kang Road from Jalan Murai to Chua Chu Kang Road.

    And two days before the exercise, all lamp-posts and bus stops will be removed temporarily.

    Colonel Richard Lim, Commander of Tengah Air Base, said: "The emergency runway exercise is a significant, very important capability for the RSAF. It provides us the flexibility to launch even if the main runway is unserviceable for whatever reasons.

    "We are doing it on an absolutely necessary basis, we have taken great pains to go out to the neighbouring residential area to explain to people and we are sensitive to the inconveniences caused."

    Traffic will be diverted to alternate routes around the area.

    Four types of aircrafts - the A4-Skyhawk, F-5, F-16 and E2C-HawkEye - will be involved.

    For the first time in this series of exercises, the E2C-HawkEye will be taking part as well.

    And for the pilots flying this type of aircraft, they said it's back-to-basics flying, even though the wingspan of the Hawkeye is twice that of the F-16.

    The last time Lim Chu Kang Road was used as a runway was five years ago.

    Since 1991, the RSAF has been hosting this bilateral exercise several times each year. In this exercise, pilots from the two air forces conduct various types of air combat training. For the coming exercise, the USAF will participate with about 200 personnel and six F-15 Eagles. The RSAF contingent includes 200 personnel, eight F-16C/Ds, eight F-5S/Ts and four A4-SU Super Skyhawks.

    Besides professional benefits, the exercise also provides opportunities for interaction and professional exchange among the officers and men from the two Air Forces. In addition, the exercise also promotes mutual understanding and fosters closer relations between the RSAF and USAF.
