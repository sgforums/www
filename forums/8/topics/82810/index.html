    MOM should help stop bias against part-time qualifications
    THE Manpower Ministry has encouraged workers to upgrade their skills through full- or part-time courses.
    .
    But here's a cautionary note for would-be students: Part-time diplomas, degrees and certificates are often regarded as second-class qualifications by employers, despite the fact that similar teaching modules have been conducted and students are imparted the same knowledge as in a full-time course.
    .
    After making sacrifices for better qualifications, a student's hope for a better salary, promotion or employability is often dashed. Friends and relatives of mine have been victims of this discrimination.
    .
    Their employers have reasoned that courses by private institutions are inadequate and a part-time student is like an ill-trained doctor whom patients are less likely to approach.
    .
    I hold a local degree recognised by employers, but even so, I feel that on-the-job training is the best training one can receive, as it relates directly to the job.
    .
    As full-time students, we are taught many formulae for dealing with abstract problems that we cannot appreciate the actual application of. That is not the case for the working student.
    .
    It is time the ministry helps stop this discrimination.
    .
    Goh Wee Choon

    Source: Today
