    When PM Goh said that the per capita income was $40K, what is meant is that the average income of a Singaporean is $40,000. It does not necessarily mean that all Singaporeans earn $40K. If you looked at it objectively, it is quite possible since for every employee in Singapore, there is a mandatory CPF contribution made by the company of 12% which even though you do no see, is calculated into the per capita earning field. Also, it includes bonusus which are paid out at the end of every year (13th month bonus).

    Management level staff are usually paid between $6K-10K per month (depending on type of management position held). Even executives are paid in excess of $2K so calculating out the average is not that difficult. If for example my gross income is $5K, the company contributes a further $600 (CPF) which makes my gross income $5600 per month.

    If I earn $2.5K per month, my actual pay is $2.8K (with Co. CPF contribution) and to top it up, I would get the mandatory AWS (Annual Wage Supplement) of 1 month's pay which will then amount to $36,400 in which case, I might get further performance bonuses which will eventually hit the average per capita income of $40,000. In Singapore, we do earn a decent living but because there is the forced savings in the CPF, we do not see much of it until you retire or when you decide to buy a house etc.
