    From Straits Times

    Bikini-clad man jailed, fined for insulting women's modesty
    A YOUNG man who exposed himself by wearing a pink bikini was jailed a day and fined a total of $11,000 for insulting modesty and other offences.

    Tan Wen Zhong, 21, admitted to five charges, ranging from theft to outrage of modesty, between 2005 and this year.

    The court heard that a 20-year-old woman was taking a lift home at about 3.15am on April 13 when Tan entered the lift on the fifth floor, clad in a woman's bikini.

    The victim's mother later reported to the police. On May 4, police went to Tan's flat at Bukit Batok and found 46 items of assorted women's wear including panties, skirts, shorts and bras.

    He could not explain how he came to have those items. Police also found five video CDs containing four obscene films.

    Earlier on April 20 last year, Tan grabbed the buttocks of a 23-year-old woman as she was walking into a lift at a block of flats at Bukit Batok. She screamed and he fled.

    He also stole a pair of sunglasses worth about $16 at a shop in Boon Lay MRT on Dec 21, 2005.

    Two other charges of insulting the modesty of women were taken into consideration.

    His lawyer, Mr Teo Chek Siew, said his client was badly affected by his parents' divorce during his formative years. The psychiatrist felt that as a result, he could not cope and deal with life's problems.

    He said the former ITE student who completed his national service hoped to pursue a higher certificate in electronics after the case.

    Word or gesture intended to insult the modesty of a woman.

Whoever, intending to insult the modesty of any woman, utters any word, makes any sound or gesture, or exhibits any object, intending that such word or sound shall be heard, or that such gesture or object shall be seen by such woman, or intrudes upon the privacy of such woman, shall be punished with imprisonment for a term which may extend to one year, or with fine, or with both.
