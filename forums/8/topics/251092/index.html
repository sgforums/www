    most ppl see stock market as a good way of earning money. yea it is, its relatively low risk in fact, but only if u dont play in the stock market with BORROWED money. That is an incredibly low percentage move especially if you dont know what you're doing. let me create a scenario.

    1: you utilise the generous credit line the bank offers u with whatever collateral you put up to speculate in the stock market.
    2a: you earn big bucks, you get greedy, u borrow more money, buy more shares.
    3a: you mortgage your star shares to buy more shares
    2b: you are incredibly bad lucked and ur stocks dont perform up to expectations
    ( either 2a or 2b happens, it doesnt matter )
    3: your shares divebomb in market value
    4: the bank suddenly turns nasty and demands for assurance that you'll be able to pay back the loan. ( which u obviously cant since most of ur assets were already tied up. THATS why you borrow money to speculate in the stock market)
    5: the bank doesnt believe your assurance that the shares would recover soon and that you'll be able to pay off the loan. Bank forces you to sell off your shares at the current market value ( which sucks since u paid alot more for them in the first place ), then extend the deadline for u to pay off the balance of monies unrecovered
    6: You are forced to sell off your OTHER assets in a hurry, lest the bank file for your bankruptcy. (note that when time is not on your side u always get ripped off when trying to sell off stuff.)
    7: meanwhile, the interest rate turns the might-have-been-small balance into a staggering sum, or turn a sizable balance into a monsterous sum.
    8: you are unable to raise enough money and eventually u file for bankruptcy
    9: work your ass off for a decade or 2 before u either pay off all your owed monies ( interest rate becomes negotiable and more humane AFTER u become a bankrupt ) or fail to pay off your money after being enslaved for a sizable chunk of your life. Bank writes off your debt and u become a semi-discharged bankrupt. not a bankrupt but still have some of the restrictions imposed on a undischarged bankrupt.

    Note that scenario 9 is in worse case scenarios, when sum owed is in the 6 digit range. People owing $500, 000 and above generally do not get discharged (unless hes an amazing entreprenur who worked his way into paying it off completely )

    ON the other hand..
    Speculating in shares with ur savings. Well.. the maximum possible gains is of cause alot lesser then if u speculate with borrowed money.. but well.. maximum possible loss is very very attractively low.

    1: You speculate in the stock market
    2a: Your shares perform and u earn money.
    3a: you keep some of the earnings in the bank and reinvest the rest of the profits
    4a: even if some shares underperform u can afford to keep it. Most of the shares which drop in value are generally able to recover most or even all of their original value after a period of time.
    2b: you're a horribly unlucky fellow and all your investments go bust on you.
    3b: So your savings go wiped out, but you still got a job, creditors arent chasing you, you've got a chance to save up enough for your retirement and swear off speculating.

    note that the main reason why borrowing money to speculate in the stock market has a high bankruptcy possibility is because the person in question is unable to hold a share that has divebombed in value until it recovers all or part of its original value. The financial institute which loaned him the money would aim to recover the money as soon as possible ( when the stated loan period expires ) and generally WOULD not give a grace period. The shares could take a long time or even decades to recover its original value which pain sucks for him. So after being forced to sell his shares, the recovered sum of money would be significantly smaller than the original loan amount and the person will be in deep trouble. So dont speculate with borrowed money if you don't know the stock market
