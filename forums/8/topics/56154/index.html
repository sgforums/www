    Plasma TV sets more practical at police HQ

    I REFER to Mr Leow Yeow Seng's letter, 'Why splurge on plasma TV?' (ST, Aug 29).

    The police are ever mindful of the need to exercise fiscal prudence in procurement.

    We are also equally mindful of the need to serve the public better and to provide a conducive environment to those who do come to the police to seek help, as many of them are generally in a state of anxiety.

    The average waiting time to be served at a large regional police station, such as the Jurong Division Police Headquarters (JDHQ), is about 15 minutes. National and crime-prevention messages are screened for members of the public waiting to be served.

    With the large reception area at JDHQ, it was felt that having two large plasma sets was more practical than having several smaller TV sets placed around the reception area.

    PHILLIP MAH
    Assistant Director(Media Relations)
    Singapore Police Force
