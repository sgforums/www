Duck Race

    STILL a month away from the big race day, and The Straits Times Million Dollar Duck Race 2000 has already set a new world record.

    To date, 77,000 ducks have been adopted, breaking the previous world record of 75,000 ducks raced in the 12th annual Bank of America Fiesta Bowl Duck Race in Phoenix, Arizona, in October last year.

    They have been adopted within 26 days since its launch on Sept 23.

    Based on the figures, this also means that a new national record has been set.

    The previous record was set last year by the Radio Corporation of Singapore, which raced 62,000 ducks.

    A pleased Straits Times editor Leslie Fong said: ""We are on course for a record-breaking celebration along the Singapore River for the biggest Duck Race ever.

    And everyone's invited to join in the fun for charity's sake. Just to make things different, Straits Times journalists and photographers will be there to give the public some insights into their jobs -- how news is gathered and how the better news picture is taken.

    This will be the third duck race to be held here. A total of 100,000 ducks is expected to compete at the Singapore River on Nov 12 at 3 pm. There will also be performances by buskers, or street performers, and food festivals along the river side.

    The first duck to cross the finishing line will win $10,000 for its adoptive parent. If a red duck crosses the line first, the person who adopted that duck will win the top prize of $1 million.

    All proceeds go to Touch Community Services, which has 3,000 beneficiaries. They include children, youth, families, the elderly, the handicapped and people with special needs.

    Indeed, duck races have been bringing in about $20 million for charities every year since 1988. In total, more than $121 million have been raised for non-profit organisations worldwide.

    The first duck race here, held on Nov 22 in 1990, raised $500,000 for the Singapore Red Cross Society with 50,000 ducks in the race. The second, held last year, raised $620,000 with 62,000 ducks.

    Co-sponsors of The Straits Times Million Dollar Duck Race 2000 are Citibank, eWorld of Sports.com, MobileOne and Tricon Restaurants, operator of Pizza Hut, Kentucky Fried Chicken and Taco Bell fast-food outlets.
