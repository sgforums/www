    SINGAPORE (AP) -- Singaporean officials on Saturday blasted local Internet sites that call Osama bin Laden a "good Muslim" and criticize the Singapore government for arresting suspected al Qaeda militants in the city-state.

    A Web site in Singapore and one based in neighboring Malaysia have condemned the Singapore government for its close links with the U.S. military and for being insensitive to the concerns of Muslims.

    "If the Singapore government is truly concerned about religious harmony in Singapore, it should take appropriate actions to remove itself from the American and Israeli attacks on Muslims," Zulfikar Mohamad Shariff said Saturday in a news release on his Web site Fatheha.com.

    Government officials have said Shariff has a right to publish his Web site as long as he does not incite violence. But several officials on Saturday said Fateha, which calls itself "the voice of the Singapore Muslim community" and claims to have 360 members, only represents the fringe of society.

    "I think he has to think very carefully about the impact these kinds of statements have on Singaporeans," Education Minister Rear Adm. Teo Chee Hean said on NewsRadio 93.8. Teo is also the second minister for defense.

    Singapore arrested 13 people in December for allegedly plotting to blow up numerous targets in the Southeast Asian city-state, including the U.S. and Israeli embassies and U.S. military personnel. Eight of the 13 have trained at al Qaeda camps in Afghanistan, the government has said.

    Shariff, 30, told The Associated Press Saturday that the suspects were probably motivated by the U.S. military presence in Singapore and Singapore's foreign policy.

    "The Singapore government's position in aligning with the United States in its attack of Afghanistan, its support of Israel, even with the daily murder of Palestinians, strikes a negative reaction from the Muslim community," he said in the news release.
