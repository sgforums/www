    that's why ppl is willing to dump money to build professional RC car. loosening/tighting the suspensions will prevent spinning out.
    the simple idea is
    having the front wheels with much stronger grip than rear wheels will cause oversteer(very easy to spin out, the front wheel grip the land while the rear slides),
    having the rear wheels with much stronger grip than front wheels will cause understeer(cannot turn sharp conrners,the rear push forward and the front slides).
    suspension springs tightness, tire material, camber angles, weight balance between Front/rear, etc affects how your car move and turns.
    those $30 RC car are meant to be play without moding, cos moding only one portion will affect the balance of how the car's handling and these toy RC car have some portions that normally cant be modded.
    If u figure out a way to adjust the grip/weight balance, u can run the car normally with your Tamiya plasma dash motor on.

    The thing TwinTurbo_Supra did was to made all the tyres no grip at all, so that all four wheel slides. i did that before also, fun to see the car sliding around.

    When comes to professional RC cars, it's not really modding, cos they are meant to be customized in many ways, parts are supposed to be swapped.

    For those cheap toy cars, it require more idea and more work to be done to mod it. Cutting, painting, soldering, connect wires, change circuit board components ,etc.
