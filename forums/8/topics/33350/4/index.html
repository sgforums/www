    Originally posted by trlnlty:
    can explain more? like what do u mean by adjusting.. my camera has a portrait option but the blurring of the back ground is not that much
    i don't know about the functions of your camera, but i was using my fren's olympus something (c70?) camera some time last year, so maybe they've got some similar stuffs.

    adjust means setting the functions, if u look at the options u'll see stuffs like ISO speed etc etc etc where you can set your shutter speed.

    high shutter speed, like say 1/2000th of a second is where you take pics moving at really high pics. usually for freeze frame pics, like in some athletic races at the end, they use freeze frame shots to see who passed the line 1st.

    low shutter speed , look at kopio's pics. those wif the psychedelic lights. if you leave the camera really still, anything moving will be smudged, but anything not moving will turn out extremely clear.

    at the bottom of the LCD screen, there's this f/x.x thingy where x is some no. which is your apperture, either that or focal point, can't remember.

    try this experiment.

    put 3 chairs about 5m apart from each other. place your camera on a stationary object (maybe another chair) and start taking pics, dun adjust any settings except for your apperture, adjust them from low to high. you'll see the difference in your pics. at mid (default app, usually 8 ), all 3 chairs will look really clear. at 32, the 1st chair will be extremely blured, but the last chair will be in perfect focus. vice versa for apperture 3.

    i may be mixed up about the numbers, it could be app 3 for far shots and app 32 for near shots. haven't touched a camera for awhile. but 8 or 5 is usually reservered for general focusing.

    do remember that when you adjust your apperture, you change the amount of light going in, hence you'll have to tweak the shutter speeds as well or your pics will turn out over or under exposed.
