    Looks like you (or your dad) have already made the choice.

    Anyway, whenever ppl ask me on which digi cam to buy, the first thing I ask is 'what you want?' If you want travel friendly cameras which not too bad features, go for the Canon IXUS series. If you want powerful zoom there's only 1 choice, Olympus c730uz. It's the smallest digi with the biggest zoom. If you want to print your picture big big then go for high mega pixel cameras.

    I bought the Olympus c730uz a few months back and like all digital cams, it got its good and bad points.

    Bad points.
    1. Not able to see what you take in the dark, there's no assist light like the canons that can help the camera to focus so if most of your pictures will be taken at night then can forget about this camera.
    2. Not travel friendly. It's got the lens sticking out so kind of hard to keep.
    3. As it's using AA batteries, it might not last as long as the other cameras but so far I have no complains.
    4. Too many manual features. To some this might be good but to those who just want to aim and shoot then this is a bit troublesome. Even the intensity of the flash can be adjusted can you imagine that?
    5. The camera settings goes back to default setting once the batt is changed.

    Good points.
    1. Zoom... no need to say more.
    2. Works almost like a video camera. It can zoom while recording and it comes with a mic so sound recording is fine. And the length of the video clip is not fixed like the IXUS (30sec) but depends on how much space you have on your memory card.
    3. Compatible with 2 types of memory card. One is the XD card which I heard is coming up with one that has the size of 8GB.
    4. Can playback your video clip on the camera with the sound.
    5. To me (think someone mentioned) using AA batteries is an advantage cos you need not worry about forgetting your charger.

    2 week back when I went down to Sim Lim, a shop is selling this camera at $999 and gives a 128MB XD card free on top of the 16MB XD card that comes with it.

    For ppl who wants to bring this camera underwater, Olympus do have a marine housing for this camera for the price of about $400. I'll be bringing mine underwater this May. Just keep my fingers cross that it will survive the trip.
