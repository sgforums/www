  • Forbiddensinner
    28 Jul 09, 11:13
    Originally posted by marcteng:

    allied educators means what?

    They are teachers' helpers in short.

    There are mainly 3 types of AE - (Counselling), (Learning and Behavioural Support) and (Teaching and Learning).

    (Counselling) - Counsellor in short. They also counsel teachers and other school personnels too. The difference from the other 2 is that you will require a degree for this position.

    (Learning and Behavioural Support) - They help out in teaching children with special needs, such as autism and dyslexia. Some might take up Dip ( Special Ed ) to teach in schools catered to students with special needs after they finish their bond and is recommended by the principal.

    (Teaching and Learning) - Assistant to teacher. If they perform well, they can be recommended by principals for teaching courses.
