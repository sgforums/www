    Let me trey to explain using economics.

    Most of you have already identified one of the reasons for taxing cigarettes at such a high rate - Negative externalities. Smoking causes health problems to the smoker and to others around him. The smoker pays the tax to make him consider the ill effects of his habits on others and himself, so as to smoke the 'socially optimal amount' -- which is to smoke less. ~

    The second reason for taxing cigarettes is because cigarettes are addictive, and therefore has low elasticity of demand. This means that government can tax it highly without causing too great a drop in demand for cigarettes. Therefore, this excise tax is a good tax because it collects a high tax revenue and yet minimizes distortions on social surplus, which is measured by the quantity of goods demanded at the efficient level.

    Hope you all can benefit from this explanation! B)
