    One of the main grouses of presidential hopeful George W. Bush is that he has the global knowledge of a high school dropout and that others would make a much better president than he will. However, that is missing the point entirely. Domestic politics are clearly tied in with the foreign exploits of the United States and whatever limitation Bush Junior might have, he is a pretty shrewd politician. And the mark of a successful president is to glue the nation together and lead it. After FD Roosevelt, none of the presidents can exactly be called experts in foreign affairs. Nixon depended largely on Kissinger for his containment policy via Red China and Eisenhower had his Dulles. Thus, you do not need to be an expert in international relations, but rather, the ability to attract men of talents to you. And that is the essence of modern government, a highly bureaucratised organisation ran by a manager.
