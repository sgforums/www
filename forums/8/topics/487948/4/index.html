  • RIPLKY
    29 Mar 15, 07:10

    I know for a fact that my late grandparents and parents were very grateful to you. Myself & my children, we are all very grateful to you and I am sure my grandchildren would be too. I regret that I never got to shake your hand or talk to you and the closest I ever got to you, was yesterday when you were lying there in Parliament house.

     

    I asked myself - Would Singapore be as good as what it is today if not for you? My answer: "Maybe, but probably not".

     

    If you had not escaped death during the Japanese occupation, your death would be quite insignificant and mourned by only a few. Today, millions of us are mourning for you as we send you off on your final journey because you survived and by doing what you did for Singapore, you have made a great difference to our lives and that of our future generations.

     

    I thank you, We ALL thank you. You will forever remain in our hearts.

     

    29 March 2015
