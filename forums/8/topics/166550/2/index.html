    Easy lah.

    Just "break" the number down into prime numbers.

    For example, 100.

    100 = 2 X 2 X 5 X 5
    = 2^2 X 5^2

    sqrt of 100 = 2 X 5
    = 10

    Much more tedious, but as long as you understand prime numbers and what a square root will do, you can easily square root a number (square number) without a calculator.

    Other numbers that are not square I don't know how to do. There is a complex method, which I don't understand.

    The same goes for cube root and whatever root you are given.
