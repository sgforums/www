    About game development and design.

    Gaming design and development is very hard and tedious work.

    To do fairly-well, you need to be well-versed in computer science, mathematics, physics and art, among other subjects.

    A game developer needs technical knowhow and business savvy to succeed.

    Passion alone is not enough. This is not the same as sales/marketing where passion alone can bring you far.

    If you ask me to develop an online booking/accounting system, I will say it can be done. But if you ask me to develop a good computer game for adults to play, I will definitely give up before its halfway done.

    The art part of game development is just too much for me to handle.

    Art will include,
    Composing storyline, in-game character design, map design etc.
    Composing music to suit gameplay, create sound effects, voice acting etc.
    Create computer graphics, animations, dynamic 3D enviromental design etc.

    The key point I want to say is, you NEED and MUST have a team of artists and computer experts to do well in game development. If you are the type that prefers to do project alone than with a team, then game development is a big no no for you.

    as a guy who taught himself C++ / VC++ at 15 yrs old, i strongly advise against going for this course.
    i advise you to choose a general computing diploma at this time and adopt a wait-and-see approach for this "Game Design and D...." course.

    if you have a poly diploma in general computing (software engineering, especially), in future, it'll be very easy to pick-up on games programming.

    Furthermore, at the moment there is no option to further your studies in this in Singapore, and I'd have to go overseas in order to do so. The problem is that my parents arn't exacly rich, so I'm really wondering if it's feasible for me to take up this course. I'm really really interested in it.
    if you die-die must take this course, then u must be prepared to find work in the US or other western countries and apply for US green card to work there.

    if you're talking about the PC / PS2 / xBox games industry, it doesn't even exist.
    if you're talking about those small-time, Java-based little games for mobile phones, we do have a small but slowly-growing market here.
    the industry for mainstream gaming (PC / console) won't be established in the next couple of decades.

    in fact, don't talk about games even....singapore's technological culture is more based on Business-to-Business software systems, like engineering software or those neuro-surgery systems to help doctors etc.

    can you seriously find a software company in SG that sells PC applications for consumers? like Microsoft Office equivalent? or Photoshop equivalent?
    there's a lot of small-timers in the West who do these things cos' there's a market. in SG, there's none.

    also, "Game design and development" is too general.
    is the course training you to become a games programmer? or a game artist? or a game storyline writer?

    if it's the combination of all three that the course is teaching you, then u better stay away more, because games development is a very huge discipline of it's own, unlike other programming genres (database, graphics, security etc.), and a course that promises to teach you all of these is definitely an introductory crap course. you'll be wasting your parents' hard-earned money.

    FYI, if you're looking to become a games programmer, you don't need to learn graphics design.
    u can suck at art but it won't matter, because in a real-world setting, the programmer won't be the one creating the 2D/3D artwork, only the artists.

    the project manager will plan the thing, the writers will plot the game's storyline, the programmer will be doing all the coding and integrate the graphics with the artists department, the marketing people will decide how to market the game and the sales people will push the product out.

    also, speaking from experience, i can GUARANTEE you that u won't find a good or proper job with his kind of "Game design and d..." diploma, because it's really too general liao, and add the singapore climate to it,....well, doom.

    worse still, if you're thinking of going overseas (USA, UK etc.) to find jobs, you'll find yourself competing with the Eastern Europeans or Indians, you'll be screwed also because this kinda diploma will become dust when compared to their expertise, and their expected salaries is rather low too.

    i understand this is your interest, but strongly advise you to pick up a general computing diploma and then advance from there.
    just to let you know, if your computer programming foundation is very strong, u can always pick up games development via the online forums or take up an eLearning / distance learning course.

    by taking up general computing, you're giving yourself a whole lot more options.
    besides, the computing industry overall isn't doing very well in SG since several years and now also.
    the ST classifieds only has a handful of IT jobs everyday compared to other sections and for one, my cousin, a recent NUS graduate with Bachelor in Computer Science (with Honours somemore) had to search 3 months before finding a job which underpays him + sick over-time hours.

    lastly, a common misconception is that games development is easy and fun, like what we see in WarCraft, Halo......fancy graphics, Hollywood-styled storylines, state of the art sound effects....

    the truth is that among the different genres of software development, gaming is among the most difficult.
    your science (especially physics), needs to be extremely good or you'll be struggling to even understand the basic AI theories.
    also, not sure if you've looked at the programming code for even those 1980s 2D simple games like Paintball....they will make even those university mathematics class look like ABC.
