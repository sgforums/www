  • sillyme
    21 May 07, 00:10
    You do not need to be super fit. But you need to do some preparatory conditioning. Mount K, although high, is fairly easy to climb. The terrain is fairly easy.

    But you have to be careful not to rush the climb if this is your first climb above 2000m. Some people may be prone to altitude sickness.

    It is a two day climb. The first day, you climb to Laban Rata which is a rest point. Most people should reach by late noon. Try to be early so that you can get more rest. More importantly, there is no heater for shower. Once the sun sets, you'll get freezing water. Have dinner and rest.

    Then at 3/4 am, you will start the second part of the ascent. Some will struggle to climb in the dark. Good to have head lamps so your hands are free to climb the ladders.

    Near the peak, take small and steady steps as the air is thin. It will take more effort to walk a few metres. Don't try to go up fast even if you see that the peak is near. Most people will be breathless. However, if possible, try to be at the peak to watch the sunrise. It is spectacular! Furthermore, there is no vegetation at the top. In the late morning, it is unbearably scorching. That is I advised to do some training before you go. You don't want to be too slow climbing up.

    The wind is strong at the peak, so bring a windbreaker. Bring sunblock and lip gloss if you need. Bring sleeping bag also. Bring a small bag for your ascent to the peak. This is so that you can leave the heavy stuff at Laban Rata and collect it on your way down.

    The downward journey is quite tiring. The steps that made the climb up easy, puts a lot of stress on the knees.

    That's all I can remember. Note that, I went to Mount K. 10 years back. I am not sure if the facilities have changed.

    For a sum, you can hire porters to carry your bag. That is how I saw an old lady climb up the mountain. I think she was more than 60 years old.
