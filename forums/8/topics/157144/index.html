    All my fault, fine me more, says jam-causing Mercedes driver
    Wednesday Â• October 12, 2005

    Vinita Ramani
    vinita@newstoday.com.sg

    THREE days after he caused a traffic jam at Empress Place by parking a Mercedes Benz SLK along a bend in the narrow road Â— outraging some readers who wrote in to this newspaper Â— Mr Ralf Lee has just this to say: The readers are right. And he is sorry.

    "I didn't sleep properly on Sunday night, Monday was a bit better, but I am still a bit stunned by it. I didn't understand what people had to go through, the time they wasted because of my short-sightedness.

    "I can never make amends for what they went through," said Mr Lee.

    He even agreed with those who said that the $70 fine he paid simply wasn't enough.

    "I can't afford a car and had to drive my girlfriend's car on the weekend," he said.

    "I certainly wouldn't mind a heavier punishment because I know that no amount of punishment can be used to make up for all the inconvenience I caused and repay others for the time I've wasted."

    And if he was dragged to court over the incident, the 28-year-old researcher said he would accept it.

    Mr Lee, who had initially refused to divulge his name to this newspaper, got in touch after reading the discussion he sparked in the yesterday's Voices section ("Parking storm rages on", Oct 11).

    Now he can't stop apologising.

    He said when one of the cars parked along that stretch of double yellow line exited, he slotted his girlfriend's vehicle in its place so that they could catch the final hours of the Vatican City exhibition at the Asian Civilisations Museum.

    Said Mr Lee: "Never did I imagine that a big bus, which usually requires about two lanes to make a turn, would pass by. In that hour or so, I had no hint at all of the trouble I'd caused by parking the car there. There was also no announcement over the public announcement system."

    Even after he left he museum, he remained unaware of the chaos he had caused, in which scores of vehicles were lined up behind the tourist bus that his car had obstructed.

    It was only when the traffic policeman asked him to pull over and the angry crowds descended on him that he realised what had happened.

    "I just didn't expect it, honestly. I didn't think something like that would ever happen to me. You know, I hate inconveniencing people, I hate causing others trouble. In fact, I dislike people who do that," said Mr Lee, whose voice still sounded shaky on the phone when he spoke to Today.

    "Yet I ended up being the one causing trouble. I feel so lost, so I wanted to respond and apologise to everyone."

    He said that his girlfriend, who was with him when the incident happened, was equally shaken.

    Said Mr Lee: "I do hope that the authorities can consider drawing a zig-zag yellow line or make some special markings at that particular spot to prevent people like me from unknowingly causing so much trouble."
