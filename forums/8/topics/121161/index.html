    8 Mar 05, 09:20
    From the fact that are you are going to NUS after A level, that means you are a girl la?

    Ok life in NUS is like this:

    1) No more spoon feeding, you have to possess a level of self-discipline in order to get what you want.... if you skip lectures and tutorials and tests, NO ONE CARES but you will when you realise that your CAP (Cumulative Average Point) falls below 2.

    2) Assuming you have discipline, join an eca or club. You can make a lot of friends that way. Also, join freshman orientation camp, normal orientation to make more friends... I didnt join any eca because i find it too hectic but now i regret.. luckily my faculty is a close knitted familly: Year 1 - year 4 all same class..

    3) Why is (2) important ? Because MOST faculties DO NOT practise the class system.. Most students wont meet the same people in the class more than 2 times...

    4) Don't do last minute mugging for exams, you can still get away doing that in JC but most likely you will die if do that in NUS..

    5) Mid-term tests are VERY important. They hold low % but they WILL decide your overall grade since most likely everyone will score the same in exam.

    6) There are a lot of chio bus and yandaos on campus... Just don't forget that you are here to study

    7) Register for hostel only if you are the following:

    a) I am a foreigner la! So Bo Pian!

    b) I am in the ARTs, i have SOOO Much time i dont know what to spend on.. maybe the hostel activities?

    c) I have mastered the secret of time management! I can play very hard(Hostel activities) and work very hard too.

    d) I am a scholar...

    Some one said that difficulty wise: Engine>Science>Arts.. that is true to a certain extent only....

    Most importantly, help others if you can and enjoy your life in NUS!!!

    The above applies to NUS and to a certain extent NTU.. Not sure about SMU etc....
