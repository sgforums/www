    Err, Bear, that's technically incorrect. Regardless of the test used, the formula for IQ is (Mental age / Chronological age) * 100. If your IQ is 110, you have the ability level expected of someone 110% your age.

    The implication of this is of course that if your ability level remainsd constant, your IQ drops as you get older.

    Regardless of the test used, a score of 100 is always the average and the standard deviation is always 15. A rule-of-thumb interpretation of a standard deviation is that it results in a difference that can be observed without the need for using a test. In other words, a person with an IQ of 115 and above would be easily recognisable as having higher-than-average intellectual abilities.

    As for hoganrock's question, I typically score between 137 and 142 - in my profession, I often play guinea pig for colleagues. I wouldn't say that I have difficulty interacting with people who are one standard deviation on either side of my score, although the MENSA crowd can occasionally be a pain in the arse. That said, if someone does not offer an intellectual challenge, I usually lose interest quickly and walk away.
