    Yesterday at Raffles Place mrt ctrl station..

    it was the peak period.. everybody knock off from work and going home..

    I went thru the transition, then i noticed two men standing at a spot, motionless. Then I saw one of them holding a stick. Then I continued walking. The other man was much taller, I din lift up my head and look at him.

    Then suddenly one man walked towards them, tap the blind man's shoulder and asked "do you need help? where are you going?" Then I took a look again at them, both of them were blind..

    gees... and I din even realize that! I tot the other man was ok de.. They were standing there, juz very near the transition lor.. and knowing singaporeans like to push and bump around, especially when it was so crowded at that time.. they might be pushed around and fall lor.

    Warmed my heart a little.. People working at Raffles Place are rather snobbish de.. and also very fast paced.. i ever done a survey over there and nobody actually stopped for me. But I saw that man extending his helpful hand to the two blind men.. makes me feel that there are still people who actually care for others, even strangers.. people who are not so unfeeling.
