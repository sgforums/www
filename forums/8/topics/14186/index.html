    The education system is flawed, like as if a contagious disease, and this disease is spreading like an epidemic. Singaporeans, perhaps consists of the most people with poor knowledge that brags about their capabilities and probably contains the most thick-skinned shameless fools who will go public for the sake of money.

    Have you watched 'Who wants to be a millionaire?' on TCS channel 8 on the 27th of March 2002? It is humilating to have one Singaporean not knowing about the fairy tale 'Snow White and the Seven Dwarves' but it is even more shameful to see our fellow Singaporeans go public shamelessly without knowing that a python is a non-venomous snake. This is sad to know that we are indicating that majority of the Singaporeans are poor, in their general knowledge.

    Fellow Singaporeans heed my call, do not make us fools and clowns by going public when some Taiwanese can watch our TV programmes with their cable. DO YOUR HOMEWORK.

    Our nation is facing a huge disaster. The current education system is not helping the nation anymore. Look at the number of 'O' level passes and 'A' level passes and judge yourself. Is this the end? Look into the mirror and ask yourselves: are you knowledgable?

    Mind you, we do not have natural resources and Chinese from China are starting to compete with our locals in the service sector. Our nation will starve if nothing is done to improve it.

    Take a look at the way students are answering questions. They are answering questions because they have the answers. If you ask them something they dont know, I'll wager that they cant be bothered to even find out. They are fed like babies, mouthful by mouthful. They are dragged about by masters like a dog, educated only how to answer certain questions. ONLY those that will affect their results.

    The concentration on aggregates only reflected that Singaporeans will never be improving creatively, it only ensured that these people will become employees and not employers for they only know how to answer questions with answers already provided and not do their HOMEWORK. In this case, in this society, men must acquire knowledge from different sources, however, what I perceive in out nation are people who happens to only learn what that will appear in their exam papers.

    Do you see many 6 'O' level passes in UK? I bet you won't 'cause their government won't put too much concentration on aggregates. Singaporeans may have good assessment skills, but they failed in their knowledge and practical work. Look at how the scores are weighed in the favour of assessment in Physics, Chemistry and Biology. Practical exams seemed to become an assessment in local institutions of education.

    Singaporeans only tread behind footsteps of others, they never create their own path. Entrepreneurs? Probably 10 out the million in Singapore will become, and most likely the number will not increase. Look at the country, where most offices belong to the Government, how is Singapore going to progress through these difficult times? As if the competition from our south-east asian neighbours isn't enough, we got China that is rising fast.

    The Government must slice the current education requirements badly or it'll face grave difficulties in allowing free learning space for its citizens. If the texts goes thinner, there might be hope for the bag weighs light and the students won't be that tired, resorting to 10 year series. They won't learn why they are answering, they just answer.
