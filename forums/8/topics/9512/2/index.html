  • Arbiter
    12 Oct 00, 17:04
    I disagree, Moose. 2D can implement LOS quite well, but some elements are lost. With the addition of a 3rd dimension, alot of details can be added.

    For example, if individual trees are modelled in 3D, you can shoot between them, or even between their branches. If a hill is modelled in 3D, I can hide my units behind it, but if the hill has a gradual slope, maybe only the bottom half of my unit is hidden. The exact height of the terrain feature plays a very important role in cases like these, and it's hard to implement accurately in 2D. If you add flying units, LOS in 2D becomes even more problematic, especially flying units that can operate at different altitudes.

    With the new T&L engines in GPUs, even dynamic light and shadow can be modelled, and to do so accurately you MUST have 3D.
