    Oftentimes.....we wish that someone could see how we really feel, know us for what we truly are.

    Accept us as the person we are. Sometimes we spend a lifetime looking for this person....sometimes we don't find the person at all.

    A lot of times, thats easier said than done. Since there is no way to influence the thinking of others....why don't we start by understanding ourselves first? Know who we truly are inside....what are our fantasies, dreams and gut feelings. Peel off the masks you've been wearing....dare to face the world in your own true self.

    Only then can we try to understand others, to steal a peep into their lives, thoughts and feelings. By doing so, let the person gain a glimpse into your mind too, let the person understand you.

    Truly, it is give and take.

    Now, what have you learned about yourself lately?

    While it's true that Starcraft has alot of tactical considerations, faction variety and many combined arms approaches, RTS games using the "harvest -> build -> conquer" model generally rely more on the first 2 parts. If you can out-harvest and out-build your opponent, you are practically guaranteed a win. Small unit tactics loose out compared to overall strategic planning.

    The latest batch of 3D games focus more on combat tactics, and place less emphasis on resource harvesting.

    Besides, 3D isn't just eye-candy y'know.
    Terrain features, height differences, LOS all come into play once the world is 3D.
