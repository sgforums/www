  • BillyBong
    8 Jun 04, 20:53
    Back packing there is fun. But i recommend you get a copy of Let's Go or Lonely Planet to guide you to the best places.

    There's no need to get a HI (hostel international) card as there are plenty of youth hostels there to choose.

    Transport will be a small problem as the locations between scenic places are far and long.

    If i were you, i would visit fiordland national park and take a cruise to mitre peak in milford sound, fiord of fiords. Pray for rain and you will see the largest concentration of waterfalls in one place in the whole world.

    After which i would take a trip up to hermitage (did i mention far and long?) and visit Mount cook national park. I believe this location was used for LOTR filming at some point.

    The rest...well read it in Let's Go. The book will tell you everything you need to go, even a short history lesson and useful maps. Lonely planet is another worth flipping through...

    gd luck and enjoy!

    NZ....went there like in 2000, but i still remember it so well.......but at that time no LOTR, so cant help u there.

    well anyway just a few tips:

    Start from Christchurch, and end in christchurch, if u r doing only the south island.

    South island is mainly nature, scenery and animals, so expact lots of that!

    Travel in a circle

    Remembered I went Christchurch - Greymouth - Franz Josef Glacier - Queenstown - Milford Sound - Te Anau - Dunedin - Christchurch. I missed Mount Cook, it was sort of out of the way.

    Highlights:
    Christchurch - charming town, but cant remember much, except the tap water for the whole town is unfiltered spring water!

    greymouth - Pancake Rocks and Shantytown

    Franz Josef Glacier (but there is another one along the way, called the Fox Glacier if I am not wrong) - went climbing up a glacier for 1/2 a day, i remember 1 day climb also available.

    Queenstown - lots to do! - stay at the youth hostel - if u r lucky, ur room window will actually be a postcard of the Remarkables!

    Went water rafting and horseriding there - the horseriding is to die for! Scenery is almost like........switzerland!

    Milford Sound - remembered it was a 2+ hour boat ride - it is quite a touristy thing, but quite worth the price. The journey from Queenstown is interesting as well, u pass through a not so long Homer tunnel, but after that, the scenery dramatically changes from green to white!

    Te Anau - Glowworm Caves - less touristy than the one at Waitomo in the North Island.

    Dunedin, a Scottish town - animal paradise - saw the rare yellow eyed penguns, and got REAL close to a giant Walrus that was sleeping.......but dare not touch it .....and almost kidnapped a cute little young calf from one of the farms.....if not fot the mother cow and the father bull ........your main highlights will be at the Otago peninsular.

    I really would recommend staying at the YHA hostels, though there are plenty of other hostels to choose from.....at least at the YHA, everything like the tours could be arranged nicely for you.....and u can prebook your next hostels with them as well.

    Driving is best! but the last time I went, got no driving license, so had to take buses all the way......

    best thing is I remember the exchange rate was so good: 0.75 SGD = 1 NZD! Now i think it is really expensive!

    Btw I went in August 2000, so it was really winter!

    Have fun!
