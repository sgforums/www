    I never understand why pregnancy is always equated to NS.

    Firstly:

    a)Pregnancy in Singapore is not compulsory, and women have pretty much the choice to opt if they wanted to get pregnant or not.

    b) While pregnancy is no mean feat, and can be biologically demanding. Can it be said to be as dangerous or physically demanding as some of the combat-orientated vocations in the army?

    c) Also, who has more freedom in the time and direction of life? An NSF or a pregnant lady? A lady is pregnant for 10 months (20 assuming she has two kids). But for a large proportion of this period she can still continue with her jobs, or choose to leave itÂ… or in fact do pretty much anything she wants with her life that her condition allows. An NSF on the other hand, is limited to his allowance, and is (if he's stay in) confined to a very limited location with very little prospects for at least 24 months. Now I'm not saying that NS is a prison, but HIGHLY doubt pregnancy is a more restrictive position.

    d) Pregnancy is not easy, but the lady typically has the constant support of friends, family and spouse, and has the freedom to seek them out as and when she wants. These options are far more limited for an NSF, especially if he's stay in.

    And most tellingly of all, why do some ladies here see pregnancy as NS? Particularly so when they are the very persons I suspect have no intentions of having kids or have the great freedom to choose if they want to or not. I have yet to see a law being passed making pregnancy mandatory or the government setting up some ministry to process and impregnate all suitable females above 16 years of age as "National Service" with no consideration to their dreams and ambitions.
