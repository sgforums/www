    from tripod

    Q: What is bandwidth?
    Bandwidth (also known as "data transfer") is the amount of data that is used to transfer a member's Web page (including HTML, graphics, text, sound files, etc.) from the host server to the person viewing the page. For example, if the total contents of the page equals 1 MB, and the page is visited 100 times in one hour, the total bandwidth for that time period would be 100 MB.

    Q: Why is there a limit on bandwidth usage?

    Bandwidth is the largest expense associated with hosting millions of member sites. For that reason, there is a monthly bandwidth limit on all sites.

    Q: How is bandwidth monitored?

    Each member has a monthly bandwidth limit. We calculate a member's bandwidth use by totaling all the bandwidth consumed over the past 30 days and comparing it to this limit. If a member is over the limit, the web site is suspended. We also monitor usage on an hourly basis. A member's monthly bandwidth limit is divided into hourly amounts, which are then compared against the site's hourly bandwidth use. If a site exceeds the hourly limit, it is suspended for a short while. Hourly limits are set high enough to allow traffic levels to spike or peak during the day, while still keeping sites from quickly exceeding their monthy limits.
