    Originally posted by controversay:
    As an ex JC student, i can tell u there are benefits to going whichever one u wish. However the final choice is really up to u and u alone.

    Polytecnic has long been deemed by many as the place NOT to go if u have gd grades. That was past stereotyping. This is the present, past beliefs dun and shouldn't apply here.

    Going to poly does not prevent u from ending up in the Uni...it only makes u generally 1 year older than the existing batch of students in the same course and year as u.

    Similarly, the culture at poly is more relaxed and hands-on, even for commerce related courses. Poly students come out wiser for it. No uniforms are required, this of course enhances the lack of monotony in the campus grounds...

    3-year courses culminate in a diploma which enables u to go out and work if u have financial difficulty. A level certs are nothing but a passport for the uni...

    On the other hand, JC cultivates a enriched tertiary education life. Ppl from similar walks of life come together with similar goals and ideals. And everyone there is generally the same age so interaction is not a problem.

    The system is similar to previous education standards, with the usual mid-term exams and final term exams and the occasional annoying class tests. Hence adaptation is not a problem.

    2 years and u're ticket bound to the uni or NS (whichever sex u belong to). Basically, A levels is an express train to the uni.

    My recommendation is go to poly...u trade one year for added cirriculum that cannot be gained from JC...

    If u decide on JC, a simple advice would be - enjoy ur life there, dun be too focused on grades (though that's important too). It's also better to go for a JC that does not have it's priorities on statistics rather than ur well being, in short go for a JC other than the top five. Less stressful and more exciting.

    Remember, the final decision is of course your own. Cheers...
