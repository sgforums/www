    In a free market, prices are determined by forces of demand and supply. When dd increase or supply decrease, prices rise, and v.v. (the invisible hand thing..) Guess anyone who have had any basic contact with Econs will know this. Well, in such a market, there is always the pros and the cons. Pro coz the consumer is the king. Those who have the demand and can afford to pay for it will get what they want. However, this makes it difficult for those who cannot afford the product unable to consume what they would like to. If say it's only a luxury good, then doesnt really matter...can do without. But what about necessities such as education?? Suppose the government does not intervene and provide cheap education to the public and let the private sector set the equilbrium price for it, most probably we'll end up seeing that many pple would not be able to afford education. Which then leads to illiteracy, poor standard of living, poverty, no economic growth...and it goes on.

    Another example would be "free" things like our beautiful parks...in such a case, nobody will be willing to pay a price for it, coz they know that if they dun pay, they still get to use it...so in the end when consumers are not willing to pay, there will be no suppliers.....there will be no parks..so, such a case is also when the govt needs to intervene to provide a better standard of living for its people......

    all in all, we cannot let the free market forces determine everything. In certain cases, the government will have to intervene.
    What if we consider the case if the government intervenes in everything. It may be possible that in such a case, there will be equitable distribution of wealth, however, if u were part of such a system, would u be willing to work hard?? everybody will just sit and "kiao kar" (shake legs) since no matter how hard u work, u get to enjoy only that much.     so conclusion is both systems will have to work hand in hand.......
