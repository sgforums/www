    I am not married yet but I can say that I may not marry the person I love most. Love in marriage is optional to me. As long as I think you are suitable for me, can live with me through thick and think, you can be my wife.

    Let me give real-life example:
    X = A man
    Y = A woman

    X finds Y attractive and open-minded.
    Y finds X charming and witty.
    They started dating each other.
    X and Y fall in love with each other, thus decided to get married.
    A few years after marriage, they start to find faults with each other.
    X accused Y of being unfaithful, flirt wife.
    Y accused X of being irresponsible, gambler husband.
    Thus, they filed for separation and then a divorce.
    And that is the end of their relationship.

    However, one important point to note is that X already knows Y is a flirt and Y already knows X is a gambler BEFORE marriage. Both X and Y did not change much, yet the same person that they once love, they despite one another in the end. Somewhat ironic isn't it.

    Summary:

    A decision to marry a person you love but cannot live with = Big mistake (or more like a disaster)

    A decision to marry a person you do not love but can live with = Minor mistake

    A decision to marry a person you both love and can live with = Ideal case
