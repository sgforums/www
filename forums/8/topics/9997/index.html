    Well, just to add on some juicy details...

    The distraught vitim's brother barged into the press conference room and was followed closely by the airport's police, who tried to drag him out. Rick Clements intervened and the man got to stay...

    This was followed by the speech made by Mr. Tan (the victim's brother). He told the press that all those relatives and next-of-kins who were huddled away in the airport did not get any first-hand news as promised... The buddies just told them that they knew nothing... well, the relatives knew that their loved ones were obviously dead, and Mr. Tan said that he just wanted the true news...

    Mr. Tan was very upset, on the verge of a breakdown, and as I watch from my home TV, I thot he was either going to get a heart attack, stoke, or just faint from all that worries.

    The airport staff urshered Mr. Tan's daughter (who was with him) out of the conference room, and Mr. Tan, on seeing that his daughter was not with him, demanded the immediate presence of his daughter.

    Mr. Tan told all present that he did not get any news, neither did his wife, who was in Taipei. He told his wife to barge in to the press conference there, and was told that the buddies there did not try to encourage nor help his wife on this aspect... Well, we all knew his wife managed to interrupt that 2am press conference in Taipei...

    Not in the article was the part where Mr. Tan said that when he tried to get news by watching CNN on TV, the airport switched off the TV. When he tried to get the press release into the barricade area for family members, the security there tried to prevent him from doing it.

    Mr. Tan paused some time in the midst of his speech and drank whatever remained from a mineral water bottle, showing his stress and tiredness...

    Mr. Tan said that he could understand if the buddies and people around them did not reveal the information 'cos they are under the employment of SIA, and he doesn't blame them... but he said that what they (relatives, next-of-kins,etc...) wanted was just news...

    Mr. Tan also condemned one of the Chinese radio station (he can't remember if it was 933 or 958, but I did listen to 933 and I believe they were not the culprit) for spending a lot of airtime discussing the effect of this accident on SIA's reputation rather than reporting the latest of the accident. He felt that it was very wrong for the radio station to do this when all the relatives, who were isolated, wanted true news.

    He didn't mention much after that and Rick Clements went forward to console Mr. Tan, and Mr. Tan started crying after this...

    Well, Mr. Tan left the room and most of the reporters followed like hounds... and that pretty much ended the interesting press conference...well, so far...

    Well, something more to add... at the 11.30am news conference, the reporters asked why there were barricades around the "family area" , and Rick Clements intially said that it was not his decision, but towards the end, contradicted himself by saying that he requested this to be a media-only news conference...

    Okay...that's all...alll the juicy gritty details...
