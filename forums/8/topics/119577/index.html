    ok lets rock

    The first thing to do upon reaching Bangkok is to get a map, you can get it at hotel, ask the recept for places to go or the best is to locate the many english speaking travel information booths located along most shopping areas for the latest free maps and information on events.

    If you looking for most cost efficient stay within close proximity to transport the best place I think would be "Asia Hotel" which is connected to the sky train(MRT) but I heard Asia is almost always fully booked so you have to try yur luck.

    The sky train really cheap fare, connects to almost every shopping place including chatuchak park where you'd find asia's biggest pasar malam, 6000 over shops in 1 place selling all sorts of bargainable goodies from footwear to clothes to bed linen to curtains to handicrafts, ornamental plants even pets and aquarium fish. The place is jammed packed starting from friday night till late sundays. As I mentioned bring a huge shopping bag, water bottle. For food you can snack there.

    Previously when I was in Bangkok there I didn't stay at Asia I stayed at "First hotel" not really a very exciting place but it's within walking distance to Ratchathewi station(Asia Hotel) and abt 3-4 blocks on the map to pratunam market where you can get really cheap wholesale stuff. Some of the stuff that you'd find at the pasar malam in singapore comes from pratinum market really really cheap and bargainable.

    Looking at my notes there are several place for shopping experience.

    If you take the sky train and stop at siam(mrt station) There is the shopping belt there but the prices are comparable to singapore not really bargain if you ask me but good experience to walk around.

    Other shopping areas

    Soi 1-6 located at siam square(walking distance from siam station) can find all the mei mei stuff here.

    Pratinum market (clothes)
    Suan Luang Market
    Nai lert Market( I think this one closed down already)
    Suan Lum Night Bazaar(knick knacks)
    Bo Be Market
    Sam Yan Market

    Patpong unofficial bazaar(cannot find on tourists maps) as one forumer mention mostly cheong stuff, you have to bargain.

    Things to beware of
    *Con men posing as singaporeans who show you their passports and I/C asking for help to get home.
    * Con men trying to be helpful to show you the best and cheapest places to shop.
    * Taxi waiting outside hotels: Never never take. Always flag down passing taxi & ask if they run on the meter.

    * Pickpockets at bazaars, always secure your belongings.

    * toot toot: 3 wheelers, not dangerous to take but they always try to rip you by charging sky high fares.
