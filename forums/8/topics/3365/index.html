    I am not sure what this thread actually purports to prove/disprove or even to critique? It's probably trying to link the beng-ism to japanese influences, although the originator presupposes too much on the ability of readers to construct their own thesis. It also offers the originator a safety zone to distance himself away from any resulting flames by saying, "That's not what I said."

    Isaac, being evasive aside, (and other posts too) show an alarming ignorance of historical continuity. Perhaps this is symptomatic of the tyranny of the present.

    Remember the Far East kids? Checkers? Extremely baggy pants and multiple hair dyes? Well, J-pop has always been part of Singaporean teen life simple because it is a big part of HK and Taiwanese life. Alan Tam's greatest hit "Peng You" was a Japanese song ... and his contempories, Leslie Cheung had numerous hits which were originally Japanese songs. The biggest tearjerker on SBC 8 in the 1982-3 was O-shin. Beng-ism is profoundly influenced by HK and Taiwanese culture and the Japanese influence is through that avenue.

    The point I'm making is that for people like Isaac, only the present is accountable for itself. The lack of self-reflexivity for history continuity continues to puzzle me ... but this could be tied in with a general world trend of condensation of the simulated present. As Eco writes, "we are living in the Middle Ages" ; the present continuous and the flatness of time.
