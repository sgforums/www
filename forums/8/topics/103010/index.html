    Copyrite changes not targetted at home users

    The Copyright Amendment Bill is not drafted to catch the home user who in good faith innocently downloads music, movies or computer programs.

    Speaking at the Second Reading of the Bill in Parliament, Deputy Prime Minister and Minister for Law S Jayakumar says the Act aims to target those who obtain a commercial advantage from such downloading.

    Because if you look at the guidelines this end-user criminalization provision applies to where a person infringes a copyright work and the infringement is willful and either the extent of the infringement is significant or and the person does it to obtain a commercial advantage.

    Professor Jayakumar said the court will have a set of guidelines to help determine if the amount downloaded is significant or not

    This, he explains, will include the volume and value of the articles downloaded.

    The court will also examine whether the infringement will have a substantial prejudicial impact on the copyright owner.
