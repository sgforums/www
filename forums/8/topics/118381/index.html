Fruit juice or fool's juice?

  • Light n Shadow
    19 Feb 05, 09:41
    This has been on my mind for some time and I feel I must get it out.

    I've been unhappy with the ridiculous prices charged for a small cup of fruit juice at food courts.

    It's one of the few healthy liquids around to buy when water is not available. As such I find the charges incomprehensible.

    Most of the time, the cup is half filled with ice cubes. That leaves at least a pint or less of fruit juice (one type. example : watermelon) to be poured in. Paying $2 to $2.50 or more for that pint or less of fruit juice is unjustifiable. When I look at the prices of fruits in the supermarket (like watermelon which goes for $2 or so) I couldn't help but wonder how does the stalls calculate the charges.

    The food court I always go to now has increased the price by 10 cents. I'm giving up on drinking anything there now.

    I don't understand how is it that the fruit stall at Shaw Tower could charge only $1.50 for a much larger cup and they went to the trouble of crushing the ice and sealing it very nicely for their customers and the ones out-of-town could charge exorbitant prices with their small size cups with chunky ice cubes.
