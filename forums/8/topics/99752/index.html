    Ad body tightens rules on hair loss, bust ads

    THE screws will tighten on beauty salons and hair-loss centres that claim in advertisements that their products can boost bust size and hair growth.

    Starting Jan 1 next year, these advertisements will not be allowed to use testimonials to support their claims unless they have been first approved by the Advertising Standards Authority of Singapore (ASAS).

    There are another two rules:

    One is that advertisements that claim to alter the shape and appearance of the breasts or bustline must carry a disclaimer to say that there is no scientific proof that non-surgical treatment can enlarge breasts.

    The other is that advertisements for products that claim to trigger hair growth must also carry disclaimers that, other than certain registered medicinal products, no product has been scientifically proven to retard hair loss or promote hair growth.

    These three rules were announced by the Advertising Standards Authority of Singapore (Asas) on Tuesday.

    The guidelines come in response to complaints by consumers who say they have been misled by such advertisements, said Asas.

    Between January and August this year, the Consumers Association of Singapore (Case) received 326 such complaints from dissatisfied consumers.

    The guidelines are effective immediately, but advertisers have been given till Jan 1, to allow for enough time for them to re-do their advertisements. Advertisers who do not comply will be penalised with adverse publicity or have their advertisements withheld, Asas said.
