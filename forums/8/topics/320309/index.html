    Alcohols are aliphatic compounds containing the hydroxy group (-O-H). They are not acidic because the proton does not readily dissociate, in turn because the negative formal charge on the conjugate base is not stabilized by either resonance or induction, and is in fact destabilized by the electron donating R groups (unless substitution of H by electron withdrawing groups, eg halogens, has occurred to a significant extent).

     

    The only alcohol which is non-toxic, or to be precise, least toxic to human beings, is ethanol (CH3CH2OH). Ethanol is commercially produced by anaerobic respiration in yeast (hence known as alcoholic fermentation or breakdown of carbohydrate to produce alcohol). Hydrating ethene (ie. adding H2O to H2C=CH2) is an alternative mechanism for production of ethanol.

     

    Ethanol, being a primary alcohol, can be oxidized by common oxidizing agents (eg. KMnO4, K2Cr2O7, etc) or by enzymatic action of bacteria in the presence of (oxygen in) air, to ethanoic acid CH3COOH. Ethanoic acid, aka vinegar, is what makes your sushi rice sour. I usually ask for normal warm rice (with sashimi served separately to keep it cool) in lieu of sushi rice. I suggest you try asking for this setup the next time you makan sushi.
