    Warned 3 times
    ST breaking news 11.39am 4 Mar

    NANYANG Technological University student David Hartanto Widjaja was warned not once, but three times, that he had to buck up or lose his scholarship.

    In the past year, the 21-year-old NTU engineering student received three warning letters, copies of which were sent to his parents in Jakarta, The New Paper reported on Wednesday. But Widjaja's grades did not improve.

    Two weeks ago, his scholarship was terminated. On Monday, Widjaja, and Indonesian, stabbed Associate Professor Chan Kap Luk, 45, in the back in his office during a discussion on the student's final year project , then slit his own wrists before fleeing and falling four floors to his death.

    An NTU spokesman told TNP: 'Widjaja's Asean scholarship was terminated at the beginning of this year as his grades had fallen below a Grade Point Average (GPA) of 3.5. The school had asked him to seek help for his studies when we noticed that his grades were dropping, but he didn't.'

    The spokesman added: 'NTU had also advised him on the various loans offered to him should he have any financial difficulties in his school fees.'

    The scholarship's criteria is apparently not at all stringent, and there is no penalty for termination of the study award, said TNP.

    Four of NTU's Asean scholars said the scholarship terms were reasonable and they didn't face major difficulties in achieving the required grade - a GPA of at least 3.5 for each semester, an equivalent of a B grade.

    Was it the stress of the final year project that led to Widjaja's tragic death then?

    Many final-year students didn't think it should have caused such a dramatic reaction.

    A group of Electrical and Electronic Engineering students told TNP that the stress of possibly wasting three years of hard work because of a poorly done FYP was very real.

    Said Wayne Lim, 26: 'Your FYP grades will eventually affect your overall results. IT's easy for your grades to slip, but harder for them to go up again.'

    NTU prof discharged

    The professor who was stabbed by a student at the Nanyang Technological University on Monday was discharged from hospital at around 9.45 am today.

    Associate Professor Chan Kap Luk sustained injuries to his back and hand and had been warded at the National University Hospital.

    Prof Chan was attacked in his office by his student, 21-year-old David Hartanto Widjaja.

    The undergraduate was said to have slit his wrist thereafter and leapt off a building in the engineering faculty.

    He died from his injuries.

    His body was cremated yesterday.

    Meanwhile his parents and elder brother who had arrived in Singapore on Monday night to help identify the body, are expected to head back to Jarkarta today.
