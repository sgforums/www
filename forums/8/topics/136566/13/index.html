    In stating that he believed in God's existence, he might have been affirming only that the concept of God is meaningful for him; in other words, he might have been saying only that he believed in God in the sense of his believing that the concept of God can for him be assigned a meaningful content, and so that it would be possible for God to be named in the course of a discussion centering on whether or not God exists.

    Thus for instance, if I say that I believe in unicorns I may only be saying that I believe that the concept of 'unicorn' is intelligible; and that it would therefore be susceptible of being assigned to something that can be ascertained to exist in actuality, even if it does not exist in actuality.

    It follows therefore that I could in this sense believe in unicorns whilst consistently denying that they exist.

    Of course, I readily admit that he himself obscured the issue by saying in the first place that he 'believes in God's existence'; when it might have been better for him to have said instead that he believes that the question of God's existence can be meaningfully discussed.
