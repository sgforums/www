    There is a three-part structure. Students are required to pass 14 papers in total. In parts 1 and 2, students can sit up to four papers of their choice at any one sitting, subject to certain progression rules. In Part 3, students can choose two subjects from a list of four options to match their work experience or chosen fields of employment. All students must sit for the three core papers and these must initially be taken together

    Course Contents

    Part 1: Consists of three papers. (3-hour each)
    1.1 Preparing Financial Statements (Computer-based examination for International & UK streams only)

    1.2 Financial Information for Management (Computer-based examination)

    1.3 Managing People

    Part 2: Consists of six papers (3-hour each)

    2.1 Information Systems

    2.2 Corporate and Business Law

    2.3 Business Taxation

    2.4 Financial Management and Control

    2.5 Financial Reporting
    2.6 Audit and Internal Review
    Part 3: Consists of seven papers from which students must pass five papers.

    Options (any two from four) (3-hour each)
    3.1 Audit and Assurance Services

    3.2 Advanced Taxation

    3.3 Performance Management

    3.4 Business Information Management

    Core Papers
    3.5 Strategic Business Planning and Development

    3.6 Advanced Corporate Reporting

    3.7 Strategic Financial Managementt
