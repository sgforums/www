    "The Beat of Passion" Multi layering of harmony has fascinated me since birth. The first time I reacted was when my aunt clapped and it was music to my ears. Ever since then, I've grown with music. Music bearing any notes - Hindustani, Karnataki, Western, Chinese elevates me, even from my darkest moments. The added dimension to the music is the voice. The voice of the singer who sings 'the joys and sorrows of the human soul.' Had these words been in prose of drama, there would be no thrill. But, the moment it is embodied with music and Taal, it becomes a song that reaches the heart, head and body. My moments with A.R.Rahman at his music studio are embedded in my memory. The voices of Ashaji, Kavita, Alka and Sukhwinder echoing the poetry of Anand Bakshi, had me visualising my charachters Mansi, Manav and Vikrant going through the emotional highs and lows of life even before the actual picturisation. That's the charisma of 'Taal' music.

    'Taal' will alwaays be dear to my heart, and to me it is definitely my most favourite work too date. The credit goes to A.R.Rahman and Anand Bakshi without whom 'Taal' would not have happened. I am sure you will share my sentiments whilst listening to the music of 'Taal' as the music delights your senses. A Beat of Passion I have lived for years.
