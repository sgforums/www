    System.out.print(number%10);

    number % 10 gives you the remainder of number when it's divided by.
    Also, "number /= 10;" is short form for "number = number / 10".
    Why lidat? Because programmers are lazy

    Eg number = 1234

    Iteration 1 :
    1234 % 10 = 4
    number = 1234 / 10 = 123

    Iteration 2 :
    123 % 10 = 3
    number = 123 / 10 = 12

    Iteration 3:
    12 % 10 = 2
    number = 12 / 10 = 1

    Iteration 4 :
    1 % 10 = 1
    number = 1 / 10 = 0

    no 5th iteration. Number was probably declared as a interger, and integer division does not return the decimal places. So 1234/10 = 123 and NOT 123.4

    Hence when number = 1234, 4321 is the o/p.
