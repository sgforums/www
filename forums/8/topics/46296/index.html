    Well, it seems that to totally understand this issue, we gotta bury deep first to find out what's really happening. Some of the following is gotten from information privy amongst the union members at the moment.

    First, majority of pilots accepts pay cut, only the union management is rejecting the move.

    Second, there's a big issue about not firing foreign pilots. Alpha-S (Alpa-S? Alfa... whatever) says that SIA should retrench (aka fire) foreign pilots first then any pay cut consideration with local pilots can be considered.

    SIA's position is that they should not retrench foreign pilots as this is a sign of "betrayal" to these pilots. Singapore is depandant on expat pilots and retrenching them when times are bad and recruiting them with bonuses equal to emperor's ransom during boom time gives SIA that bad image.

    Anyway, this is the picture I was given... SIA is asking local pilots to take a pay cut and go on no-pay leaves every 2 months. And at the moment, flights are cut like 30-40% meaning not as many pilots are needed.

    But for the foreign pilots, they are not ask to take this approach, they are still paid as per normal, and with reduced flights taking place, they are basically idling on the job. Sitting around not flying and getting paid.

    I guess it is so unfair that local pilots have to bear the brunt whereas foreign pilots still enjoy SIA perks during SIA's gloomy times.

    You have to "Bid" for your flights. First come first serve rules.

    If your late, you fly sectors on holidays. not very nice.

    depending on the pilot's aircraft type, for example 747-400 on a trip to LA, they will be doing 2 sectors with a stop over at HK

    So to say, 4 hrs to HK, 1 hr stop over (1 sector completed)
    after turnover personnels are done with the cabin mock ups, the flight crew depart HK on 16 hr Flight to LA.

    On this flight depending on bidding and pilot schedules, this would either be a 3-4 man crew or there will be an exchange of fresh pilots to continue from HK Bound LA.

    Should the same crew from SIN be flying all the way to LA, CAAS dictates no pilot are allowed to fly more than 10 hrs on long hauls and no longer than 8 hrs on short hauls, therefore spare crew, usually a first Officer or Capt and a Second Officer, takes over in monitoring flight progress.

    To conclude, all 4 crew members get paid for flying only 10 hrs each on a 20 hrs based flight. which also mean they get paid even when resting the rest of the 10 hrs.
