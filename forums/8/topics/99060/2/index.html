    2005 is the 3rd yr that ERS is given out, each time is either $200 or $400, depending on the property that u live in. Most HDB-dwellers should receive $400 per lot.

    As for the returns, ERS will earn tax-free dividends each year in the form of bonus shares, payable every year on 1 March from 2004 - 2008. The rate is a minimum guaranteed 3% + real GDP growth rate of the previous year.

    The above means that even if the economy if not growing, we can expect 3% return on ERS. This implies that when we have $1,200 ERS from the 3 lots, 1 yr's interest from 2005-2006 is at least $36. If the economy grows at real return rate of 5%, an 8% return would be reaped --> $96 interest.

    In the market, we can hardly find such guaranteed return, backed by a AAA-rated govt. Patience in withdrawal seems worth it. Who doesn't like "free" money, even if it's a few hundred dollars?

    For more info, refer to www.ers.org.sg. (An extract: "The Economic Restructuring Shares (ERS) are part of the offset package meant to help Singaporeans adapt to the structural changes in the economy, especially the increase in the GST rate. The ERS will be given out in three lots, with one lot each year starting from 2003. The shares will earn annual dividends, in the form of bonus shares, over 5 years. The bonus shares will be calculated at a rate of 3% plus the real GDP growth rate of the preceding calendar year, with a guarantee of at least 3%.")
