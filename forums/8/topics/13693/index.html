    Buddhism is "safe" from the ravages of science because it has never pretended to be anything else. It does not commit itself to making statements about creation and astronomy, whilst Christianity does. Look at the Bible, and you'll find tons of statements that defy science. Christians like to see science as an abstract, almost like a religion where theories are accepted purely on the basis of faith. They ignore the fact that the very foundation of science is empirical evidence.

    Science has never pretended to be infallible. A scientific theory can easily be disproved by an innovative experiment. Such simple things like the fact that the earth revolves around the sun is starkly contradicted by verses in the Old Testament, especially in the book of Jeremiah. Other things like the age of the earth can be established not by one method, but by a whole series of other methods. And yet, Christians still believe that the earth was created only a few thousand years ago.

    From a layman's point of view, if God, the creator of all life, really did have a hand in the Bible, wouldn't his statements be the least consistent with reality?
