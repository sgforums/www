    Originally posted by mahawarrior:
    I'm confused...what exactly constitutes illegal downloading? Is getting files from Limewire illegal? What about sending files over MSN?

    And why should downloading stuff be illegal? What kind of stupid law is this?
    As long as it breaks copyright laws, it is illegal downloading, whether or not you pay.

    Things under copyright laws include softwares, music and videos.

    Getting files from Limewire or any other P2P may or may not be illegal. If something distributed over P2P is under GNU General Public License aka GPL, it is legal. If you have a group of artists friends, who distribute THEIR OWN art work, music, etc, it is legal, unless they specify that it shouldn't go around the world.

    Read the full GPL license here

    Anything that states EULA or End User License Agreement means that the software is protected by copyright laws. If you download it via P2P, from anywhere where you can get it free, you are violating copyright laws and it will be illegal.

    Sharing files over MSN is the same. So long as that piece of work is not under GPL, you break the laws.

    Even if you pay for something, but that company is not authorized to distribute it, it is illegal as well. However, you are not to be blamed for it. The company who unauthorizely distributes such work is liable.
