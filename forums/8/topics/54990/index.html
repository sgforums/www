    Fergie hails Ronaldo's 'marvellous debut'

    Sir Alex Ferguson has admitted Manchester United's army of fans have a new hero to worship in Portuguese teenage sensation Cristiano Ronaldo.

    'It looks like the fans have a new hero,' admitted Ferguson.

    'It was a marvellous debut, almost unbelievable. I felt his penetration could make a difference for us.

    'We have to be careful with the boy. You must remember he is only 18. We are going to have to gauge when we use him.'

    Bolton boss Sam Allardyce was forced to admit Ronaldo's arrival was the pivotal moment in the game.

    'His introduction was crucial,' said the Trotters chief.

    'You only have to look at the contribution he made. When they provided him with the ball, he was different class.

    'Sir Alex will use him when he is ready but that was 20 or so exceptional minutes.'

    Even Giggs could not hide his delight at Ronaldo's introduction, which reminded the Welshman of a great United number seven he used to play with.

    'When we first saw him play for Sporting Lisbon, he impressed us the same way Eric Cantona did when we played Leeds,' said the Welshman.

    'He is a great prospect and he is going to put on more great performances like he did today. He lifted the crowd and the players and there are not many people who can do that.'

    'Overall the scoreline flattered us a little bit. We expected Bolton to offer us stern resistance and they were pretty dangerous at times.

    'But our passing improved after the interval and it is a good win for the first day of the season.'

    The performance capped a big week for Ronaldo, who followed up his midweek United move by being name in the Portuguese squad for the first time for Wednesday's friendly with Kazakhstan.

    Notoriously loathe to release players for international duty, especially those as young as Ronaldo, this time Ferguson will allow his new young star to leave with his unqualified blessing.

    'It is a good thing he is going with Portugal,' said the United chief. 'He has the chance to talk with Luis Figo and Rui Costa in his own country and that experience will be invaluable when he comes back to us.'

    After his earlier failure, there was venom in the van Nistelrooy strike which wrapped up the scoring three minutes from time, hitting the target for a ninth time on the trot in the Premier League and 11 games in succession for United, both new record marks.
