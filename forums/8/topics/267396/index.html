    Similarities in Huang Na and Ying Ying murders

    Both girls killed while one parent away in China.

    THE deaths of 3-year old Shearwey Ooi Ying Ying and 8-year old Huang Na hold a few chiling similarities.

    Both girls were killed while one parent was away in China.

    Huang Na was killed at Pasir Panjang while her mother, Chinese national Huang Shuying, was away in China. Ying Ying was killed while her father, Mr Ooi Ng Chew, was away in Yunnan, China.

    Both men lived in the same home as dead girls. Huang Na's killer, Malaysian vegetable packer Tong Leng How, 23, stayed in the same house as Huang Na and her mother, while Ying Ying's suspected kiler, Ong Chee Leong, 29, shared an apartment with her and her mother.

    Both girls were also alone with their alleged killers - both hailing from Penang - at the time of their deaths.

    The disappearances of both children also sparked huge volunteer search efforts.

    Ying Ying's disappearance sparked a state-wide search involving more than 1000 members of the Penang Malaysian Chinese Association, while Huang Na's sparked a search effort that extended from Singapore across the Causeway.

    Most uncanny is that Ying Ying and Huang Na share the same surname in Chinese characters. The Hokkien surname "Ooi" is equivalent to "Huang" in Mandarin.
