  • IR
    28 Aug 08, 21:56

    Zara has nice clothes. Most of my working shirts are from there, and the cutting is quite fitting but comfy. It makes you look sharp, unlike most "higher-range" G2000 shirts that are either too baggy or thick. I like their jeans too, but they're quite pricey.

    Having more than 20 ties at home, my favorite brand is Paul Smith. Goldlion makes nice ties as well, for more conservative occasions.

    Pants are best to be talior-made. You can never buy a pants that fits your perfectly off the shelf, even after altering it due to folds behind the legs.

    I don't really like most working shoes in Singapore. I have a pair from HK and another from TW, and they look sharp. Both pairs have a glossy finish on the leather, which doesn't require much maintainence (polishing). I'm kinda picky on dress shoes, they must look nice and also be comfortable.

     

    A general tip...when buying clothes, most will pick colours that are matching or easy to match. Don't forget the depth of colours too...e.g. a "warm" coloured tie matched with "cool" coloured shirt will clash.
