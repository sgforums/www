    Wanton Mee initially did not come with Char Siew. That is why if you order this in Hong Kong, or any chinese restaurant like Crystal Jade, they only give you wanton with the mee and maybe a bit veggie (HK one dun have veggie).

    But in the 1950s, a street side hawker came up with this because a lot of his customers asked to add char siew in the wanton mee. So he decided to add char siew in every bowl of noodles unless the customer say dun wan. Slowly all the other hawkers also do the same.

    That is why only in Singapore, you see wanton mee comes with char siew one.
