    Earth does travel around the Sun, but the path is not a perfect circle. It's an ellipse, slightly lopsided. One end is a bit closer to the Sun than the other.

    All planets in our solar system travel around the Sun in elliptical orbits. It's Kepler's 1st Law. The eccentricity of Earth's orbit is 1.7%. In January when we're closest to the Sun (perihelion), the distance is 147.5 million km.

    Earth reaches aphelion from the Sun. Curiously, our planet is warmest when we are farthest from the Sun. Thus, boy A is wrong about the fire theory.

    So, is the sun closest to earth in the morning or in the afternoon? To answer the question, please do a calculation of the earth's orbit to the sun in the morning and afternoon. You will find the answer in the tenth decimal place.
