Singapore to Represent China at Next SEA Games

  • kopiosatu
    16 Dec 03, 10:42
    by Lau Cheow

    After the impressive showing of ex-Chinese sportsmen and sportswomen for Singapore at the recently concluded SEA Games in Hanoi, China has officially asked Singapore to accept more Chinese athletes, in effect, providing China with an official presence in South-East Asian sports.

    As nearly two-thirds of the athletes winning gold medals were originally from China, this move makes perfect sense, and is seen to be a strong signal that China is taking the SEA Games seriously as it gears up for its own Olympic Games in Beijing in 2008.

    The Chairman of the Chinese Olympic Federation, Mr. Yao Yun Dong said that it worked to China's favour in several ways. The SEA Games can be used as practice venues for her own athletes who can train in Singapore. Sporting exchanges have always been an important feature of Chinese diplomacy, added Mr. Yao, pointing out that it was ping-pong diplomacy that led to the easing of ties between the US and China in the 1970s.

    "However, more importantly, " said Mr Yao, "this move allows us to send our second and third-rate athletes to gain experience in sporting events and win some medals, otherwise they'd be demoralised if they were to stay in China and not get selected for the national team."

    Speaking for the Singapore National Olympic Council, newly elected adviser, Mr. Chong Guo Ren said that Singapore would be delighted to accommodate China's wishes, as it opens the door to more sporting exchanges in future.

    "We see Singapore and the SEA Games as stepping stones to greater things, like the Olympic Games," declared Mr Chong, "I'm sure my comrades, uh, fellow Singaporeans, feel the same."
