    H2 Maths is mostly on C Maths content plus some F Maths stuffs (like Linear Regression, and De Movire's Theorem) ... Pure Maths 60% and Stats 40% ... questions set by Cambridge are all compulsory.

    H1 Maths focuses more on the Statistics part of H2 Maths. Pure Maths in the H1 level here is very A-Math like and very condensed, and it accounts for only 40% of the grade. Statistics follows closely with H2 Maths (minus Poission Distribution) and it accounts for 60% ... and if you're wondering if H1 Maths = AO Standard. You are hell wrong.
    H1 Maths also contains Linear Regression, a once F Maths topic

    Anyway, I also studied H3 Maths in NUS once ... haven't received my grades yet but it seems to be like F Maths as well (Vector Spaces, Linear Combinations and such)... just that it's a 4-month course .
