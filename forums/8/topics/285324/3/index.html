    Training on hard surface (road) do not prepare you for running on soft surface (grass)

    On hard surface you have the push, or spring in your stride.
    On soft surfaces like grass, gravel, your energy is dissapated into the cushion, and there is a slight drag that will exhaust you very quickly.

    Be more specific in you training.

    You can try some mental adjustment while ALSO training regularly by running as often as you can, with a fair bit of commitment.
    - jog with a happy, high attitude (you must like what you do)
    -no need to set siong target, just enjoy the exercise of your body
    -keep you attention on a distant object, do not think about your fatigue
    -jog at a constant, not hurried or irregular pace.
    - Appreciate your body's ability to maintain that speed at the beginning, and stick to that speed.
    -be aware of the constant pace and breathe/move in synchro
    -in fact your body will adjust to that speed (usually after a certain distance0, sometimes much further than 2km even) after that it becomes autopilot and becomes easier. That phase will be like gliding. You will feel light and like you can run forever at that speed.

    even when not running, try to breathe slowly and deeply to develop your lungs. When your breathing method corrects, your health and mind will be stronger.

    After reading this, go and jog
