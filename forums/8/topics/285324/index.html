    if you can run within the time limit during practice runs yet you are not able to perform during school runs, it could be due to a mental problem.

    the thing is, for borderline passers like us, mental strength is crucial in 2.4km run. it can be demoralising when u realise you are slowing down exponentially suring the run or like "how come i only ran 2 rounds and i feel so tired already? i still got 2/3 of the run to complete. how?"

    yea it sucks, but try to adopt a different attitude. first, start slow. doesnt matter if u cant catch up with those fast runners at the start. the thing is to pace yourself properly. instead of chionging 1 min 45 for the first 2 rounds and die like 2min 30 for the next 4 rounds. keep it at a steady time of around 2 mins per round and you will be laughing ur way to a relaxed, slow and easy pass.

    next, instead of thinking how many runs you completed, psyche yourself on how many you just have to complete, "like i ONLY have 4 rounds to go" rather than "sianz i ONLY completed 2 rounds". keep pushing yourself. with 2 laps to go, try increasing your pace.

    it's all in the mind yea. the pain and fatigue, get used to it.

    just my $0.02 from experience.
