    This rather interesting link came up on the forum sidebar with this thread: http://programcritique.com/subcategories/InvestmentOpp/RobertKiyosaki.html?Name=Robert%20Kiyosaki

    I agree with the author that going for Kiyosaki's seminars is overkill - I expect that all Kiyosaki has to say can be found in his books.

    Taken in the wrong spirit, Kiyosaki's books can of course be a big disappointment. If you're looking for a game plan of how to get rich quick, don't waste your time reading his books.

    However, he does have a knack for laying out some home truths about money, and those are useful for those who have never conceived of money as anything other than what you get in return for a day's work. Rich Dad, Poor Dad serves as a useful reader for those who are at the stage of asking, "How, exactly, does money work?".

    Take the basics that he lays out, try to apply them to situations that you experience, and those books can be useful.
