    Job Suggestions for Zoo Animals
    Posted on Sunday, March 14, 2004
    Topic: Local News

    by asingaporean

    Following the latest retrenchment at the Singapore Zoo, our reporters were on location to discover what new jobs the animals can excel in if they, too, get the chop.

    Ah Meng - she will undergo retraining under the Skills Redevelopment Fund as the next Chief Justice of Singapore. From one zoo to another so it is a low learning curve. She is expected to sail through the course as good breakfasts is after all as integral a factor in getting the job done in both old and new positions.

    Lions - what better redeployment of the national symbol than to have them chained next to the goal posts of the Singaporean football team? Any player who slacks on the field gets eaten up immediately. There is now extra incentive to not embarrass the country at
    the international matches. Sending the ball skywards one metre from the goal posts? Makan. Drop the ball when it is already in the goalie's hand only to have it sent into the net by the other team. Extra fast makan. Goal 2010? Hell, yeah!

    Tigers - reassigned to Asia Pacific Brewery as Auditor General and Auditor General at large. Any punk who tries to embezzle money again will be, yep you guessed it, eaten alive. This service can be outsourced to any company who suspects their accountants are slyly cooking the books. Give the man a tiger takes on a whole new meaning.

    Hippopotamus - Minister for Manpower. Why? Easy. Sits there and opens his big mouth all day long. Why look for one in a hospital when there was a natural talent in the zoo escapes us.

    Giraffes - Presidents of Singapore so that they can oversee the national reserves. We hope we got this one right. That's all there is to the job, right? Overseeing only and nothing else, right?

    Snakes and Pythons - outsourced to Temasek Holdings. These guys can really swallow. And the pythons will have our money in a death grip.

    Pandas - the solution to the world's most expensive parliament lies within this group. Surprised you , didn't we? No, we don't think monkeys cut it. They may be paid peanuts but they are too noisy. Docile and non-confrontational, pandas are the perfect Singaporean parliamentarians. Sure winners during elections, too. Who can say no to a panda? Problem is there isn't any in Singapore and not enough in the world to form a GRC. We have standards, you know.

    Polar bears - sign on with SAF as Rangers. They have proven abilities to blend in with the vegetation. Missions to Malaysia are bound to succeed. Besides, they can survive any water torture SAF instructors throw at them.

    Monkeys - GLCs directors. Can't see no difference. And on top of that, you can pay them peanuts.

    Elephants - NTUC Union Chiefs and sagely Senior Ministers. Because they have really long noses.
