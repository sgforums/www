    What bear said is very technical, but if you can undersand and follow it, it's actually very good. Only a few points to add :
    1. rythm - stand at the starting line and swing your arms back and forth, bending your knees at the same time. On the last swing, swing your arms forward, etc, etc, etc (following that Bear said).
    2. in your mind, aim to land some 3m away from the starting point. I remember that when I forget to do this, I always land far short of my usual jumping distance.
    3. practice often - helps in your technique as well as increase your tigh strength.
    4. do weights training for your legs. Carrying a bar-bell on your shoulders, do the squat. Or use the leg press machine (I think that's what it's called), if your gym has it.
    5. Give yourself a minimum of 2 mths if you want to train your legs properly, instead of going on a destructive intensive crash programme at the last minute.

    Hope this helps.
