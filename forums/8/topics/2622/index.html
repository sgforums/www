    I think before I give non buddhists a very
    wrong impression or starter buddhists an
    even wrong impression. I better reiterate
    buddhist philosophy.

    If you read deeply into buddhist philosophy,
    you will understand the final stage to
    mental enlightenment is to recognize that
    all the buddhist ideas that are taught to
    him are devoid of any inherent existence.

    In higher philosophic levels of buddhism,
    multiple negations are being made.

    Therefore the idea,

    Buddhism tells you that you have a problem
    (1st noble truth), then it tells you a
    solution to the problem, then finally it
    tells you that seeing yourself as having a
    problem and trying to solve the problem is
    the problem itself (thus negating what has
    been taught to you initially).

    This level of philosophic discussion will
    require the people discussing it to be very
    learned in buddhist philosophy. I shall not
    try to explain it here, as few people have
    even shown this level of understanding.
    Discussing it here will only confuse people.
