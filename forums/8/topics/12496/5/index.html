This applied both to team play and FFA. In FFA, most pple will go defensive and anyone who tried to rush are running a risk cos they can only get rid of one player even if he succeed and the possibility is very low due to the long distance of travelling unless the opponent is near. Furthermore, by rushing, he slow down his building progress.     Originally posted by Zeraph:
    in lets say an allied 2 on 2
    how to stop double zealot rush?
    Double zealot rush in the first 6 minutes is almost unstoppable, but you always have zealot killers - the ultimate Lurkers!!

    Protoss observers dont produce fast enough for their first zealot rush and so the zealots are sitting ducks for Lurkers.
    A good way is to have the Lurkers burrowed below the larvaes - your opponent got to kill the larvaes first even if they manage to detect the lurkers. The larvaes are so hard to kill that it take some time even for zealots.
    Build your buildings to surround the sunken colonies so that the zealots can't directly attack them. They've got to destroy the building first before they can attack the colonies. This would save sometime for you to get rid of some zealots first and salvage the situation.

    Meanwhile while the zealots attack, send one drone to your friends base, build a hatchery there to take cover just in case you got wiped out.

    double zealot rush is the usual strategy i use when i play 2on2 and both of us are protoss. its very difficult to stop the rush if your ally sits around and does nothing, whatever race you are. the most important thing is to scout and once you find that they are going for a zealot buildup, you have to consider your options. if you are terran, bunkering is the most obvious solution, unless you can lock the zealots out with supply depots while having enough marines shooting from behind. without any of these 2, terran is gone case. but careful and constant repairing of the bunkers will keep you alive. meanwhile, your ally should attack one of the protoss. try to go for the probes, since quite frequently attacking players don't watch their bases. if you are zerg, sunkens have to be made, preferably with zergling support. place the sunkens with buildings around to shield them. how many you make depends on the number of zealots coming for you. the big problem defending is when you are protoss. making zealots-you have only half their number. sometimes, lucky players get off with making a shield battery to support their zealots. a good try that works if there is an opening to block(eg.bgh). what is done is that 2 gateways are made nicely such that the only way for zealots to pass thru is the slit between the gateways. then, place 1-2 cannons behind the gateways. in the slit, place 1-3 zealots and HOLD them. this can hold off easily 3 times the number of zealots, more if there is a shield battery to recharge the zealots in the slit, simply because when the opposing zealots are hit first by the cannon, they try to walk thru the slit, and get hit by the zealot in the slit supported by 1-2 cannons.

    still whatever defense is made, the winner often is the team that coordinates best.
