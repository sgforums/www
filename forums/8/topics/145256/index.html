    This time old debate has been around since LKY first opened NJC in 1969.

    JC became THE place to go after LHL and LHY both schooled there together with LWL.

    Just as LHL and LHY went to chinese schools instead of RI (and since then, many still believe in the SAP concept) , LKY hoped to set the trend of younger generations being seeded towards the JC-university route so that he could develop a pool of 'graduates' to replace his aging old guard.

    As a result, many among us bear the misconception that polys are for "losers' while JC are where the brightest go.

    Imagine where parents would 'nudge' their children to....

    This bias view, however misguided, was intrinsically nailed down on our generations; as a result, the poly intakes were simply 'second-best' and we continued to believe (naviety) that only the best went to JCs with the 2nd-rate students heading for diplomas.

    Hopefully, the mindset of our next generations will adjust to a more open view towards polys. Such broad-based understanding is needed to allow our youngsters the right frame of mind to determine just which path is best for their own educational needs.
