    ok, me wanna correct one mistake, it is called either IN the money or OUT the money, no AT the money!!!!
    Call Option: right but not the obligation to buy..exercise this if strike price is below market value (IN money), don't exercise in opp direction (OUT money) but lose out on option premium paid
    Put Option: right but not the obligation to sell..exercise when strike price is higher than market value (IN money), don't exercise in opp direction (OUT money) but gain on option premium received...
    warrant are usually attached with issue of bonds to make the bond look more attractive to investors... u can detached the warrant and sell it to the market or hold it..usually warrant got an expiry date and give u an option to buy the company shares at a certain price...of course u will exercise the option if the market value of the company share is higher than what u can buy from the warrant contract...if this happen, then IN the money cos u buy at lower price and sell to the market at higher price...
    Right issue usually give to existing shareholders at a discount...so u can sell it to the market and earn the profit, so is called IN the money

    After a brief email discussion with a postgrad in my college, this is one of the conclusions.

    Rights are a form of cash call (i.e. the company is asking shareholders for additional cash) - In order to avoid
    undersubscription (i.e. the coy not getting the full amoung of cash it is asking for from shareholders), the company has to issue it in the money.
    Warrants are not normally used for cash calls. Rather, they are issued as a sweetener to a new bond issue (borrowing from bondholders). Therefore, the company does not need to issue it in the money as
    full subscription is guaranteed by the attractiveness of the bond+warrant
    issue and not the exercise price of the warrant.

    A reply to this though:
    You are right to say that warrants are just another type of option, but Rights issues are NOT call warrants and can only be issued to existing shareholders.

    It does not give you the right to buy or sell shares in the future. This is the main reason why Rights issue can be issued In the money. Why rights issues are not options: It must be issued only to the existing sharholders (all have equal rights or entitlement of this rights)Options or warrants can be issued to anyone
    (including non-shareholders)Rights issue will dilute the value per share immediately, but not the Warrants.

    Why warrants can be issued to everyone?
    Why warrants donot dilute the mother shares?

    Because warrants are issued At the money or Out of the money. Therefore, it can be issued to everyone who is willing to bet for the gearing effect of the warrants insteads of holding the mother shares.

    Hope this helps.
