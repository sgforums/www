    4D System Roll - Easier for some people to buy their 10 numbers in a roll.

    iBet - I liked this idea. You are actually proportionating your bets this time, instead of straight bets like $24, $12 etc....of course, the winnings are proportionally reduced. But straight calculations showed some facts:

    Let's use the number 1123(not my number hor)

    i)If you use System Bet instead of iBet, you need to spend $12, assuming you are betting all 12 permutations for $1 Big. If you strike first prize, with System Bet, your winnings is $2,000. But, with iBet, assuming you also played $12 for some reason or another, your winnings is only $1,992 ($166 x 12). Singapore Pools has earned $8 from your winnings using iBet.

    ii)But of course, to address this discrepancy, they have also increased, abit slightly to the other prizes. Using the same number and betting amount, if you strike the consolation, you would have won $60. But with iBet, with a $12 bet, you would have struck $72(12 x $6). A "better" return of $12.

    Of course, this "better" return is only restricted to the consolation prize. For all other prizes, System Bet > iBet.
