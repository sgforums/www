    Leukemia is a major killer of children within the age group of 0-15 and a bone marrow transplant offers the only hope of a cure for many leukemia patients. However, the odds of matching a patient with his or her siblings are 1 in 4 and for those without a sibling donor, the chances of finding an unrelated match are 1 in 20,000.

    Since its setup in 1993, the BMDP has some 40,000 donors in the Register, facilitated 8,000 searches and found 125 successful matches for patients both locally and abroad, to date.

    Tissue typing is the first step towards maintaining the Registry. The process of tissue typing is very complex and expensive. Currently, the cost of testing each new donor is S$150 (compared to previous cost of S$300).

    As the only bone-marrow donor Register in Singapore, the BMDP is committed to improving the chances of survival for patients with leukemia and other blood-related diseases. Towards this end, BMDP is targeting to expand the Registry further to 50,000 donors by 2008 so as to enhance the probability of finding a compatible match for leukemia patients.

    At 5,000 donors a year, the BMDP needs S$750,000 to fund the costs of testing alone or approximately S$2.5 million to achieve our target of 15,000 new donors by 2008.

    Q: ARE THERE ANY RISKS OR SIDE EFFECTS?

    A: Other than the remote chance of a reaction to anaesthesia or an infection, the risks to bone marrow harvest are minimal. Donors generally feel no pain during the bone marrow harvest as they are under general anaesthesia. After the procedure, there may be slight discomfort in the lower back lasting a few days, which is much like a muscle ache or similar to pain felt on the tailbone after a fall. With the stem cell harvest, donors might experience some flu-like symptoms, slight bone pain, or a feeling of heaviness during the injections. These discomforts usually disappear soon after the collection is completed.

    Read more FAQs on the Bone Marrow Donor Programme website.

    A short summary...

    All medical expenses will be borne by the bone marrow receiver.
    You can choose not to go ahead with the donation process if a match is found. Of course, this is highly discouraged. Don't give false hopes.
    All you need to do to be put on the donor register is to go for a blood test (small prick nia).
