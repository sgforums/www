    Mastercard electronic is a debit card, while Mastercard is a credit card.

    Both can be used on internet transactions and outlets with the mastercard sticker on the door. When used at outlets, the shop assistant will print out the charge slip and verify your signature.

    Debit card can only be used when you have enough money in the bank. Eg, you have $1000 in your bank account and you decide to buy a $200 bag. When you use your debit card, the machine will process and verify with the system to see whether you have the money, then the transaction is approved. The $200 will eventually be deducted from your bank account in a couple of days' time. This means that you will not need to worry about paying bills.

    Credit card has a pre-approved amount that you can spend on the card. It is based on your monthly salary. Eg if you are paid $2500 a month, the approved amount will be max $5000 (usually max is double the monthly salary). The credit card centre will not care whether you have money in the bank or not. So when you buy a $200 bag, it will be charged on the card. This means that you will now owe the bank $200, which will be billed to you within a month. You can either pay in full or by installment.
