    THE Consumer Protection (Fair Trading) Act lists 20 unfair practices which retailers have been known to engage in. Here are some examples:

    HARD SELLING
    A consumer wants to buy an exclusive watch and the retailer pressures him into buying it by saying there is another very interested buyer when there is actually none.

    OVERCHARGING
    A consumer calls a plumber to repair a leak in the toilet and payment is agreed over the phone at $50. After the repairs are done, the plumber insists on $200 for the total bill.

    MISLEADING
    A consumer buys a digital camera and is assured he need not pay for servicing if the camera requires repair within the warranty period of three years. At the repair centre, he finds out the warranty covers servicing but not replacement parts.

    FALSE ADVERTISING
    A brochure states that a tour package to China costs $888 but in small print at the bottom is a clause saying the package price applies to the fourth person only.

    WHAT CONSUMERS CAN DO
    Ask for a refund:

    - Consumers have up to one year from the time they discover the unfair practice to take action against the retailer.

    - For timeshare and direct sales companies, consumers can seek a full refund if they change their mind within three days of buying something.

    Turn to Case:

    - Take note that the consumers'association (Case) can intervene only when the complainant is a member. Case membership costs $25 a year or $400 for life.

    - Also, there is a case-by-case administrative fee of up to $50.

    - Case will write to the retailer and ask for its side of the story. If the matter cannot be resolved, Case will arrange for both parties to meet professional mediators.

    - If the matter still cannot be settled, consumers can turn to the Small Claims Tribunal.

    Tourism board:

    - Tourists who fall prey to unfair business practices can complain to the Singapore Tourism Board (STB) by phone, post, fax or e-mail. For details, visit the STB website at www.stb.com.sg
