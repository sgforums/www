    It is an interesting idea you have there, but I highly doubt most singaporeans
    can move over there and take tokyo as their home.

    Japan is too different from Singapore in many ways, not only in terms of language and culture, but also the attitude of the people.

    Let me list some of the common and basic differences:

    1) Japanese people teach their children to be independent from young, hence their children are very independent, to the point where they can take the train by themselves for 15km+ to buy grocery at as young as 4 years old. Ask a singaporean child to do that, and you will be considered lucky if your child somehow manage to find their way home by themselves.

    In short, we are too spoon-fed to be able to adapt to the much tougher society over there where the people take care of themselves more than the government led them by the noses.

    2) Language problem. Japanese as a language is already not really that easy to learn, somemore one will have to learn how to differentiate between the different ways to write a single word, it will take a lot of time and patience for one to be able to truly communicate in propere japanese.

    3) Japanese people are very polite as compared to singaporeans as you have noticed yourself, to them, it is as bad as a sin not to be polite towards those around them, no matter how much they dislike them. Of course, the younger generation is slowly growing out of this tradition, but I will say their kids are still much polite than the Singaporean ones. If a Singaporean goes over there and does not pick up their habit fast, he/she will easily offend a lot of people.

    4) Singaporeans are very use to complaining about everything left and right, in Japan, they don't do this. Instead, they find innovative ways to solve whatever problems that they might have. Hence, their people often come up with a lot of interesting items and solutions to problems, whereas Singaporeans just keep up their whining about this and that without doing anything to make their own lives better.

     

    As you have said so yourself, such a list will be nearly non-exhaustive, so I will just list these few points here. If you truly immigrate to Japan after your NS, I will say that you are truly an admirable and brave person, for you are willing to try and change your own life.
