Govt relaxes rules over use of National Symbols

  • wuming78
    3 Jan 04, 22:16
    SINGAPORE -- Companies and individuals can use a design, trademark or logo incorporating the lion head symbol, provided it's done in good taste with changes to Singapore Arms and Flag and National Anthem Act, The Ministry of Information, Communications and the Arts (Mita) said in a press release on Saturday.

    Mita said it wants more Singaporeans to use these symbols because they are the country's 'most visible symbols of sovereignty, pride and honour'.

    Homes and non-governmental buildings can also now display the national flag throughout the year - not just during National Day. But it has to hang from a flagpole and be illuminated when flown at night.

    Public and private organisations can also sing or play the national anthem on any appropriate occasion, and Singaporeans are encouraged to sing the anthem during national celebrations or events of national significance.

    [/quote]
