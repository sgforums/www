    fish traps like bubu traps can be very bulky, though I have seen some malays at labardor park lay one.

    During low tide they came with snokling gear and wetsuit (no air tank). They went into the water and came out with this huge cage and took out abt 4 nice table size groupas.

    bubu can be very effective traps but unfortunately you cannot watch it 24/7 if someone knows the location they will come and steal the fish.

    I used to have a smaller collapsable version few years ago. I'd buy a few of these and some long rope and bring to the jetty when I go fishing.

    quite fruitful.

    I guess the old way that they do to attract fish is to use coconut palm leaves. they take a few large palm leaves anchored to a small boulder and take a boat out to shallow coastal area, then they place it at under the water and thats where they lay their bubu traps.

    The palm leaves under water will attract small fish which attract larger prey.
