    Took Leng How's appeal dismissed, death term upheld for Huang Na's murder

    Took Leng How, the man who was sentenced to death for the murder of eight-year-old Huang Na two years ago, has had his appeal rejected.
    The 22-year-old vegetable seller was found guilty of killing the girl in October 2004, and given the death sentence after a 13-day trial in August 2005.

    He appealed against the sentence.

    The Court of Appeal met a few months later, on 21 November 2005.

    On Wednesday, it handed out the verdict.

    But it was not a unanimous decision.

    Two of the three judges - Chief Justice Yong Pung How and Judge of Appeal Chao Hick Tin - upheld the decision of trial judge Lai Kew Chai that the accused was not suffering from an abnormality of mind at the time of the offence.

    Therefore, Took's defence of diminished responsibility was not granted.

    The third judge, Kan Ting Chu, however felt there was a reasonable doubt on Took's conviction for murder.

    Justice Kan questioned whether Huang Na's death was caused by Took smothering her mouth and nose or whether she died as a result of a fit.

    Took's family members and relatives, who were present in court on Wednesday, have asked the defence lawyers to appeal to the President for clemency. - CNA/ir

    Source : Today's yahoo sg news
