    ABOUT 100 people queued for two hours before opening time yesterday for free chicken rice in Toa Payoh.

    Watch Channel i news clip here

    And within two hours of the first plate being dished out at 10.30am, all the food was gone.

    However, a few just watched as some returned to Jia Mei Chicken Rice stall, in Toa Payoh Lorong 8, for a second helping.

    Among them was Madam Lim Seok Gwee, a 44-year-old housewife, who said: 'I won't eat it even if you pay me to. I will not risk my life.'

    Mr Ng was coy about how much it cost him but from estimates on how many dishes he would have sold with the 150 chickens he served, the takings forgone were about $3,000.

    But it was worth it, he said. 'At least I go home tonight with more hope that tomorrow, there would be more customers.'


    Business was slow because of the bird flu scare, but when a chicken rice stallholder decided to give his food away free, the crowds came. -- LIANHE WANBAO
    Hawker Ng Choon Huat, 44, made the offer on Wednesday on MediaWorks' Chinese Channel U, while expressing his frustration at how his business had halved following the bird flu scare.
