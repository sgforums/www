    I think comparing usb2 and firewire will be missing the point. Both have different architectures and although usb2 may theoretically have a higher bandwidth, its internal structure limits its capabilities. Direct comparison by bandwidth is too simplistic.

    IEEE1394(firewire) is a TRUE peer-to-peer bus. In fact, its uses are not restricted to external devices alone. You set up a mini-LAN w/o using NICs (typically 10/100Mbs) , and use its 400Mbs bandwidth instead. However, it is considerably more expensive than USB due to its more complex protocol and signaling rate.

    USB2 is a network in tree topology, with a dedicated host. In order to attach further devices you need hubs (or do daisy-chaining, which is not an elegant solution). Only the host can initiate data transfers, and the host is polling all devices constantly, eating up bandwidth and increasing latencies. The USB architecture is inferior and inflexible. The overhead on the bus offsets its theoretical speed.
    On the other hand, firewire has much lower CPU utilization compared to USB.

    Nonetheless, both serve their own purposes, and very often, higher end video editing devices tend to favor firewire due to its robustness and stability in architecture, wheareas other devices favor usb2 due to lower costs in implementation.

    Think of usb as a hub, and firewire as a switch.

    Bluetooth? Another technology designed to serve a entirely different purpose.
