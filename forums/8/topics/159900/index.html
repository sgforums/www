    ''Faye Wong has always been an artist that reigned in her own class.

    Regardless of what the press writes about her, one fact remains .... well, as a fact, the girl can sing. Although never vocally empowering as Divas such as Mariah Carey or Whitney Houston, she definitely doesn't have a paper thin voice.

    Faye's voice is crystal clear and projected with such poise and the pitch is controlled with perfection. Although her artistic growth is ever evolving as displayed through the five albums she has released during the period, Faye Wong was never an artist that succumbed to releasing albums merely to blend in with commercial radio.''
  • Mr Fugue
    29 Oct 05, 07:30
    ''Lastly, Faye's singing is ace. Her voice is incredible. Her singing technique is flawless.

    Many people today think that contantly forcing the voice to push out notes is "good singing", but the truth is that any half-decent (jay, cindy and the like) singer can do that.

    What is truly amazing is the control of the voice. You will never hear Faye force out high notes like Coco Lee because Faye can hit those notes smoothly and with ease. Also, you will never hear Faye wail her notes out like Sammi Cheng because Faye can project her voice with force and power by singing from her diaphram - the proper way. Lastly, in all of Faye's albums not once has she had a flat note. However, singers like Elva Xiao, etc have countless flat notes.''
