    An example of the questions asked on Science Centre's website:

    Question No. 20225 :
    What lies beyond the kuiper belt?

    Answer:
    There are nine planets in our solar system (not including the newly discovered object, sedna, that has been referred to as the tenth planet, but some scientists would not classify it as a planet because of its small size). The Kuiper Belt is a disk-shaped region beyond the orbit of Neptune, the eighth planet, extending roughly from 30 to 50 AU from our Sun. (1AU is the distance between the Sun and the Earth). Pluto, the ninth planet and also very small, is seen by some as part of the Kuiper Belt, rather than a planet. The Kuiper Belt contains many small icy bodies and acts as a source of the short-period comets.

    If we were on a space ship and traveling beyond the Kuiper Belt, we would find the Oort Cloud, a vast shell containing billions of small icy bodies. Some of these icy bodies become long-period comets when they plunge towards our solar system and get close to the sun. The Oort cloud is located between 50,000 and 100,000 AU from the sun, about a quarter the distance from our sun to the nearest star, proxima centauri.

    Traveling further, past the Oort cloud, we would reach proxima centauri at a distance of 4.2 light years away. This may seem a very long distance, but compared to the size of our Milky Way galaxy, which is 100,000 light years across, it is insignificant. There are a hundred billion stars in our Milky Way galaxy. Our sun, together with its nine planets, is only one of them, and quite an insignificant one at that.

    Beyond our galaxy, there are again billions of other galaxies. Most galaxies are grouped in clusters, and those clusters are again grouped into super clusters of galaxies. So, we can see that there is a lot that lies beyond the Kuiper Belt. In fact, the vastness of the universe is quite unimaginable.
    And the question was asked by a primary school student

    This one is also quite interesting.

    Question No. 20172 :
    Why is the lunar cycle longer than the orbital period of the moon round the Earth?

    The Lunar Sidereal Period is the time it takes the Moon to complete one orbit around the Earth with respect to the stars. The Lunar Sidereal period is about 27.3 days.

    The Lunar Synodic Period is the time between successive New Moons and is about 29.5 days. This is the basis of the lunar calendar.

    The Lunar Synodic Period is longer than the Lunar Sidereal Period because while the Moon orbits the Earth, the Earth also orbits around the Sun. It takes the Moon 27.3 days to complete an orbit around Earth with respect to the stars, but during this period, the Earth has also moved through 27.3 days of its orbit around the sun. For a New Moon to happen, the Sun, Moon and Earth must be aligned. But on completion of the Sidereal Period, they will not be aligned yet. Thus the moon must move on for an extra 2.2 days to catch up and re-align itself with the Earth and the Sun.

    Question No. 20178 :
    I never understand this,"If something travels faster than light, time runs backwards." Nevertheless, I figured that if let say, I, can travel faster than light, I would see black or, simply no light, because I have beaten light, thus seeing no light. I am confused. Can you help?

    The ultimate speed limit in the universe is the speed of light in vacuum (about 300,000km/s). Einstein's theory of relativity states that nothing in the universe could break that speed limit. Let us do a though experiment of racing with light in the context of current physics. Let's say you are racing side by side with a beam of light and you are traveling at a speed of 99% the speed of light. Common sense tells you that the light beam racing side by side with you will overtake you by 1% of the speed of light. But this turns out to be wrong - the light beam will still overtake you at the speed of light! Amazing but true... (see explanation below)

    You may then argue that since the light beam is zapping past you at light speed and I am already traveling at 99% the speed of light, it must be traveling at higher than light speed with respect to the ground. However, this is again not valid.
