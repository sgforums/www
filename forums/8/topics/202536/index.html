    Gown under fire

    MISS Singapore Carol Cheong's choice of evening gown during the Miss Universe pageant in Los Angeles last week has been flamed for being outrageously distasteful.

    Anonymous Coward felt that her outfit looked like cheap lingerie.

    Destini thinks that Miss Singapore should have donned a traditional outfit befitting Singapore's rich heritage. She writes: "I think the gown was a mistake and not representative of us Singapore girls at all."

    But JulianW disagrees: "I'm sure it made her stand out. It has ethnic details too. Controversy is one sure way to succeed. Way to go!"

    http://tomorrow.sg

    Army rules OK?

    SOME national servicemen and army regulars are hoping several rules can be done away with. These include not being able to use an umbrella in camp, having to book out dressed in a collared shirt and trousers, and not being able to sleep on the MRT and buses while in uniform.

    Bukitimah recounts being booked for walking into camp while holding an umbrella, instead of using the army-issued raincoat. He writes: "Punishment for holding that stupid umbrella? 7 days' extra guard duties."

    Seequincy01, however, says: "The army is about discipline. Some of the rules have their purposes even though they seem ridiculous."
