    Glass
    -----

    A long time ago, there was a Kingdom called
    Asheron. Its people are like our people,
    they carry on their daily lives just like
    you and me. There was competition for food
    and shelter, war with neighbouring states,
    tax on income and even worship of many gods
    and its related festivities.

    One important festival is the King's annual
    bear hunting. It began a long time ago when
    the Kingdom's founder, killed a bear in retaliation
    for it having scared one of his servants.

    The people of Asheron celebrated whenever
    that day begins, and every year, the King always
    return with the carcass of a dead bear because
    he was skilled with his bow and arrow.

    On one year, the ruling King fell ill
    and at his servants' advice, he issued a decree to
    award anyone who hunts and kills bears. This ruling
    was greeted by most people and some hunted bears purely
    for the sake of prestige. Immediately the King complained
    of this and told his subjects

    "Let this be known again that I hunted for the sake
    of my people's safety"

    As time passed, his illness got worst,one night
    he made a secret journey to a neighbouring Kingdom
    and he decided never to return.

    His servants knew about his disappearance only on the
    next morning. They got worried and couldnt explain
    his disappearance. Some thought it was an enemy kidnap
    plot, some thought it was the bear-ghost that hunted
    him. His servants searched for him through out the
    whole kingdom, but he was never found. His people knew
    about it all and many griefed. In their grief,
    they dedicated a new god solely to him, his personality
    grew into a cult, the bear-spirit. Over the years,
    a new King replaced the missing one, and every year
    the King and his subjects worship the bear-spirit and
    bear hunting slowly ceased.

    One night, the new King rest, having finished participating
    in the rituals of bear-spirit worship. He slept
    in his palace bed and dreamt about an old man

    "What are you?" asked the King in his dream

    The old man produced a transparent plate
    in his hands and said "Give me this"

    The King woke up, sweat on his brows, and he called
    out loud for his servants. The next day
    he called for his royal astrologist, asking
    him to explain his dream.

    "Who is the strange man?" asked the King

    "He is the Bear-Spirit!" said the astrologer

    The King was not surprised at his answer.

    "What is that shiny thing that I saw?" asked the King

    "That is what your highness must give him!"

    The King was not pleased at his astrologer's reply.

    Many of the courts' subjects were asked, and no-one
    know of the answer, the King felt agitated and ordered
    every shiny thing in the Kingdom to be brought to him.

    Crystals, diamonds, bronze, gold, silver, none of them resemble
    the object that the King dreamt of.

    One evening, a servant interrupted the King's nap
    and told him of an important message.

    "Your highness, we have what you wanted!"

    The King immediately asked for a court session
    and a young maiden was presented to him
    along with a shiny object.

    "Yes! that is exactly what it appears in my dream!
    Tell me what do you call it?"

    "Glass" she said.

    The King learnt that glass is being made
    in other kingdoms, and he immediately fell in love with
    it. After months of discussion with his subjects,
    he issued a decree to build workshops that made
    glass especially for him.

    Beautiful glass wares were made and were presented
    to the King. As the years went by, they became part
    of the items used in the annual bear-spirit worship
    rituals. One day, he accidentally broke a layer of glass
    made to decorate over a table. He felt a state
    of shock ever since. As the weeks passed, he felt
    ill.

    "Your highness, our traders are worried about
    the civil war that is occuring in the neighbouring Kingdom
    of Juggernaut" said one servant

    The King sat on his bed and drank his medicine.

    "Your highness, you must issue a decree to mobilize
    our soldiers at once!" said another servant.

    "Your highness, the bear-spirit will protect us!"
    said yet another servant.

    The King exhaled a depth of breadth and said

    "Our children are not ready."
    "They have improper values"

    "What are the right values?" asked his servant.

    A dying King wrote down a decree
    which was going to be his last.

    "Our school children must be taught the proper values"

    One servant held up the decree and read it aloud

    "On the 1st year of study, he must write and draw
    on paper and keep them"

    "On the 2nd year of study, he must rear a pet"

    "On the 3rd year of study, he must pick red
    seeds and keep them in glass jars"

    "On the 4th year of study, he must release the pet"

    The servants that surrounded the King looked at each
    other. Some had the look of bewilderment. Some made
    nods on their heads.

    After the King died, a group of royal servants
    designed a new administration to govern the entire Kingdom.
    Invasions were made into neighbouring countries.
    There were victories and defeats. Slowly, the Kingdom
    of Asheron became the Republic of Asheron.
    It grew into a powerful nation-state.

    And the King's decrees were never put in place.

    ---
    End
