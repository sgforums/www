    I thought long and hard about this question about confidence...

    I think we need to differentiate between confidence and whether one is introverted/extroverted. One of my sons is less extroverted that the other one, yet his confidence level is always very very high. Simply put, he is not a very chatty person like his brother, but he knows who he is and what he can do. While my extroverted son is always attempting to play catch-up with his brother.

    Perhaps children need to be pointed out to the little victories that they may have during the day. Simple things like being made the group leader for class decoration, or just coming in top 10 in a test is a good affirmation that something right was done. I use the half empty/half full glass sometimes to demonstrate the perception of success. For each little victories that happened, there will always be an encouraging word and I continue to invite them to do more. I tell them the reward is the feeling of accomplishment and the satisfaction that something is done right.

    Confidence is not built overnight and it does not need mighty deeds. It need little things that are done right and the appreciation one gets for it. Gradually, a child will learn to be assured of themselves as to what they are capable of doing by being a change agent. Once they know that inspite of their age (and size) they can choose to participate in steering their own lifes (or their classmates), confidence will grow.

    Some kids also need to understand their own psyche, ie, what they are and what can/cannot do. In household, there will never be sportsmen, and my boys are ok with it. They will never participate actively in the schools' sports day 'cos they are not really built for competitive sports, but both of them are quite good with mind games, like chess and nowadays, Scrabble. My younger boy is getting so good that he beats the entire family sometimes.
