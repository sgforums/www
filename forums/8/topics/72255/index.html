Kiss in public could mean jail in Jakarta

  • www
    9 Mar 04, 10:46
    By Devi Asmarani

    JAKARTA - Couples caught kissing in public could end up in prison for five years.

    Under an anti-pornography Bill being drafted, hip-swivelling dance performances would also be banned.

    The Bill, being drafted by a parliamentary legislative team following pressure from hardline Islamic groups, is scheduled to be discussed in Parliament after the general election next month.

    According to the draft, a couple caught kissing on the mouth in public could be sentenced up to five years' jail or fined 250 million rupiah (S$50,400).

    The Bill also proposes jail terms for public displays of nudity, lewd gestures and erotic dances. Watching erotic performances could also lead to a jail sentence.

    Legislator Aisyah Hamid Baidlowi, the head of the legislative team, told The Straits Times that tougher measures were needed to arrest a decay in moral values among the young and to stem the increasing flood of sexually explicit shows and publications.

    The country's criminal laws are inadequate in addressing these problems, she added.

    Mrs Aisyah explained that passionate kissing in public should be outlawed because 'this is a predominantly Muslim country' and such behaviour was contrary to local values and culture.

    The same goes for musical performances on national television deemed to be indecent.

    Mrs Aisyah said dangdut singer Inul Daratista, who provoked much unhappiness among Muslim conservatives with her hip-gyrating movements on stage, would likely be banned from performing if the Bill was passed.

    The singer, who shot to fame with her controversial stage acts, was partly the trigger for the movement against 'porno-aksi' or acts of pornography last year.

    Ms Inul has so far brushed off the criticisms and has even inspired a host of other aspiring dangdut singers to come up with their own racy dance moves.

    But calls for tougher laws have also gained ground among conservative Muslim groups and Islamic parties.

    Two years ago, the country's highest Islamic body, the Indonesian Council of Ulemas, called for a ban on several television shows, including American series Baywatch and a live broadcast of a local ballroom contest.

    Muslim groups have also been upset by the flourishing of raunchy publications.
