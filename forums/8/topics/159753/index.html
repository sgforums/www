    28 Oct 05, 12:38

    As reported in the ST today, going by the British paper, The Times Higher Education Supplement (THES) ranking of the world's best 200 universities places NUS tops in Singapore (rank 22 falling from 1. NTU ranks 48 (up from 50 in 2004). No figures for the other local universities are published.

    The rankings are largely based on measures like the no. of times research is cited by other academics, staff-to-student ratios and the no. of students and staff recruited overseas. Both NUS and NTU faltered in this category of ranking.

    In other areas, NUS was ranked 9th-best university in the tech disciplines (which includes engineering, comp science), 15th in biomedical sciences and 13th in social sciences. None was mentioned on NTU.

    In the employer review (in which international companies prefer to recruit from), both fared the same (12 out of 100 points).

    However, for NTU, strong scores for international faculty (95 points) and international students (53 points), gave it the edge over others ranked close by.

    As for UNSW, SMU, etc, no comments were published.

    So which is best in 5 years time? Who knows?
