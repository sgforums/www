    My sister visited New Zealand last yr and bought back a souvenir cap which printed 'Queenstown ... Down Under',

    Remember the 80s song 'Down Under' by the Aussie group 'Men at work' which was used as the theme song of 'Finding Nemo'?

    see, even in song lyrics, 'Men at work' don't wish to selfishly make 'Down Under' to mean only Australia.

    Oceania is a 'continental region' with Australia as its Continent and also PLUS surrounding island, just like Asia Continent strictly speaking don't include Japan, Indonesia but Asia 'continental region' (usually just called Asia or Asia continent) does include the islands. It is the world's only 'Continental region' that don't follow the main continent's name - most likely because Australia Continent is already used as a country's name and hence quite confusing if also means the entire region as well.

    secondly Australia does not equal Australasia i.e. Australasia means 'bigger things' than Australia. Australasia is a imprecise terms that normally refers to Australia, NZ and nearby Pacific islands only, however, it generally don't refer the entire Oceania region and it does not refer to Asia, esp Asia Continent (i.e. mainland).
