    George Lam in intensive care after falling during concert
    HONG KONG -- Veteran Hong Kong singer George Lam Tse-cheung was in intensive care but in stable condition after falling into a hole on stage during a live performance, local media reported on Saturday.

    George Lam - seen here with his wife, singer Sally Yeh, during their concert last Saturday at the Suntec City Convention Centre - lost consciousness after falling into a 2.5 metre-deep hole while performing on stage.
    The 55-year-old Canto-pop singer fell down a 2.5m hole that appeared after a movable platform on stage was lowered on Friday night, video footage on local network TVB showed.

    Hong Kong's Commercial Radio reported that Lam was in intensive care at St Teresa's Hospital, while TVB said he was in stable condition.

    Police spokesman Margaret Ho said a man named Lam had been admitted to St Teresa's with injuries to the back of his head and his ear, but she declined to give his full name.

    Staff at the private hospital said they had no admission record for the singer and so could not provide an update on his condition. It is not uncommon for celebrities to check into medical facilities and hotels under false names.

    The semi-retired Lam was making a guest appearance at a concert by veteran TV actress-singer Lisa Wang at Hong Kong's Coliseum.

    Pictures printed on the front page of the Oriental Daily News showed Lam was still singing into his microphone as his right foot stepped into the hole.

    Another picture showed the singer vanishing into the stage, with his left arm and leg still protruding from the hole.

    Lam, who shot to stardom more than two decades ago, has appeared in several movies and is a noted music composer. In recent years, he has mostly stayed out of the limelight. -- AP
