  • Honeybunz
    5 Jan 05, 14:46
    Why all job interviews ask many tough questions like a stress test?
    Just went for an interview. Spent the whole morning there. Was there immediately after my breakfast. Very early. By the time I came out, it's already lunchtime and I was mentally exhausted, having to answer so many questions. A lot of my interviews in the past and this one are about two to three hours

    After that, I talked to an engineer and poured out my frustrations over having to be asked so many different questions at every interview. To my surprise, his interviews are much simpler. Interviewers only asked very straightforward questions like "What are your skills? What are your papers?" He commented that his area of work requires more hard skills rather than soft, while mine is the other way round. This is so da mn unfair. This guy is paid three times more than my last job.

    As much as I can recall, during the first couple of years, my interviews were very easy. Interviewers did ask questions, but very simple ones. But during the last few years, I have come across a number of difficult interviewers trying to see whether I could handle stress and difficult situations. One of them even asked me questions like "You sure you can work under me???" and waited for my response. Another difficult one asked me "Why should I hire you?" and again he was looking out for my reaction to that.

    Though I can safely say that I have handled all these well, I don't understand why I have to go through all these while there are other interviewees out there who don't have to. While I really look forward to a new work environment, going to an interview has been a drag for me because of all the experiences that I have had with the nerve-racking interviewers.

    Anybody has any awful interview experience to share?

    Its an employer market now.

    I interview people but I do not know if other employers are doing all this for the right reasons. But not to say I wanna make things difficult, its jut that job fit and the personality fit that I would be looking for. And questions on behaviours help clarify if the person is as what is in the paper qualifications. Additionally, i want to ensure the person i employ is fully aware of the workload and to find out the kind of expectations so that we can manage it properly.
    Don't take it personal, for my, it a part of my job and I wanted to ensure the best fit in terms of attitude and other qualities. Thus answer to the best of your abilities. I am the engineer in these interviews so my questions are not that difficult and I don't talk alot while I observe and simply throw in some off the mark questions. I will also profile the person based on the answers and can roughly tell some things about the person. It becomes easier as you go through many interviews and you conduct many interviews.
