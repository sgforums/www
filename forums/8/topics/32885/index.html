    Online shoppers jump at offer of printer at bargain price of S$66

    By Wong Siew Ying

    Some online shoppers thought they had hit the jackpot when they found a Hewlett Packard colour laser jet printer, which usually costs S$3,800, going on sale on Monday for just S$66.

    One of those who jumped at the offer at the website run by Digiland Mall is Mr Tan, a student.

    Advertisement

    Mr Tan knows a good deal when he sees one, and on Monday morning, he took up the offer after hearing about it from a friend.

    And the university student is not the only one, many others have ordered the printer that's usually priced at S$3,800.

    Some have even been billed the S$66 advertised as the printer's price.

    But just a few hours after receiving confirmation for his order, Mr Tan discovered the webpage had been taken down.

    He, and many others who ordered the printer, hope the local online company will honour the deal, even if it was a mistake.

    Mr Tan said: "They'll have to be so-called responsible for their actions, because with regards to e-commerce, it's just like setting up another shop and making price tags."

    But when contacted the Consumers Association said that although it is good practice for companies to honour such deals, online transactions like these are not binding even if a customer's credit card is billed, if the company doesn't accept payment.

    In such cases, consumers just have to accept that the company made a mistake and the deal was really too good to be true.

    And that is exactly what's happened.

    In a statement released on Monday, Digiland says it received 4,000 orders for the printer at the wrong price but adds that it was a genuine mistake and the company does not intend to process any of them.
