Targeting Loansharks not solution

  • xtreyier
    22 Nov 09, 13:30

    The hideous crimes committed by loansharks and their runners must not go unpunished, and every effort must be exerted to wipe them off the face of Singapore. The misery they cause with their usurious rates of loan interests drove many to the loss of careers, families and even their lives.

    However, wiping them off is not the solution nor solving the root of the problem.

    The problem lies in money, espacially the lack of it.

    Many attributed such loans as being taken up largely by gamblers. But how true is that statement? Even if it is true, why did people had to gamble in the first place?

    More often, majority of such people comes from low income base and had thought to supplement their income for a better life thru gambling, eventually becomming a habit that led them deeper and deeper into ruin with each victory or defeat at the roll of the dice.

    Some dont even gamble but find it a task to make ends meet. No legal banking entities would loan them for they have no worthy collateral as security against loan.

    Education is one means to escape from poverty, but not many can aspire to scholarships, for places are limited, not to mention, each person has her/his talent in different aspects of life that complement societies.

    CDCs and welfare organisations had put in enormous efforts and had done well to help all it could, but there are limits, not only on such organisations, but on the recipients themselves, for who wants to depend on charity?

    Jobs and living within one means type of education are crucial to prevent abject poverty from creeping in. But when that period of time when even a dollar is crucial, such people have no where to turn to. Who better than a loanshark, inspite of the harsh terms?

    I dont presume to have a solution, does anyone have any bright ideas for bridging loans set up by our society to the less well off and less priviledged, so that they may not fall prey to gambling as a means out of poverty or turn to  loansharks?

    I am no bright spark, but just to start the ball rolling, if any chance at all, i offer up a suggestion.

    Most of the loans taken up from loansharks are between a few hundred to probably $2000. Higher amounts would only mean the wealthy are doing it to tide over their cash flow, and they need not our society's help.

    Those who borrow low are usually the lower income group to tide over their own cash flow during some more demanding financial periods.

    My suggestion is this:-

    1.Town councils to offer loans from $100 up to $2000 to these lower income citizens at market bank rates for a period of 3 months. Collateral will be their passports so that they dont run away.

    2. Less well off citizens will have 3 months to pay back in full + interest.

    3. Should they fail, they will have to offset such payments in lieu - by working part-time to clean up the estates at night after work, at the wage rate of a full time worker.

    4. Town councils will then organise and control the work flow and hiring of workers so as not to affect their budgets. 

     

    It may be shameful, but I do think its better than to have one's IC and hp no. pasted on lifts and corridors, harassed at nights, lives threatened. Furthermore, there still is a chance he can pay up in full, as the amounts are not huge.
