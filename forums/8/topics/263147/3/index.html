    I am a user of a film Minolta Dynax 5 SLR. Still deciding to continue with the Sony systems or to jump to the Nikon system. Read the Sony may be launching the alpha200 and alpha300 soon on clubsnap forums.
    One thing I like about Minolta DSLRs and Sony had inherited over is that the "Anti Shake" mechanism is built into the body of the camera. This is different from Canon and Nikon where the Anti shake system is built into the lens.
    For the Anti shake system in the body, what ever lenses I have can use the function. For Anti shake built into the lenes, I will be buying the same anti shake function each time I buy a lens with the function. And those lenses without the function will not have anti shake.

    But the advantage of Nikon or Canon systems is that they are much more commonly used and I can borrow lenses or get 3rd party or 2nd hand lenses more easily.

    Remember, buying a DSLR/SLR is not just buying a camera, it is buying into a system. See which systems fit you better.
