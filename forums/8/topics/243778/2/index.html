    In case u guys did not know hokkien,
    during China's golden age, which is the Tang Dynasty, the language spoken there sounded more like a variant of hokkien..

    this is evident because linguist found out that Tang Peoms rhymes and sounds better when recited in hokkien instead of mandarin.

    In fact Japanese language is more related to Hokkien instead of mandarin too, due to Tang's influence to Japan during that era, in the later years, during the Ming Dynasty, much of Japanese pirates raided the Hokkien province, thus influencing japanese speech and literature even more.

    The proper term of Hokkien would be "Min" language.

    and cantonese is "Yue", due to its association with "Yue nan" (vietnam) in history.

    Hokkien, isn't an "uneducated" language, is just that people in Singapore "downgraded" the language. and soon, due to political changes, Hokkien was downgraded further to a "dialect"

    Go on, get a Tang poem and recite in Hokkien...sounds more melodious than mandarin.
