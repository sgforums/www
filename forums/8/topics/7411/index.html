    the thing is, most people seem to equate education with maturity, which doesnt necessarily apply in all situations. an educated person is usually matured (but the probability is decreasing every year) but a supposedly "uneducated" person, might not be the direct opposite. qualifications are only a reflection of certain traits and characteristics of a person, not a factor itself. a degree doesnt automatically give you a sense a worldliness, maturity, or sophiscation. not all U grads stay up to date on current affairs. and likewise, a "lack" of education doesnt condemn you to a lifetime of ignorance either. it's the person him/herself, and how they react to the situation, and different environments.

    in the end, the qn lies within you, whether you'll allow it to affect you or not. the problems supposedly brought on by a difference in education, is more a question of different environments. if two people grew up experiencing utterly opposite lifestyles, they dont require a disparity in qualifications to have a communication breakdown. similiarly, putting 2 U grads together won't guaranteed a happy union either.
