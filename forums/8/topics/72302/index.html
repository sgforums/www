    NEW YORK - Who needs American Idol? William Hung doesn't - he already has a recording contract.

    STILL HUNG UP!: He might not sing very well, but William Hung is still the talk of the town as the UC Berkeley student will release his album, The True Idol, in April.
    The Hong Kong-born singer, who became a fan favourite - but not a judges' favourite - during open auditions for the hit singing contest with his spirited version of Ricky Martin's She Bangs, has signed a deal with Fuse Music Network and Koch Records.

    His debut album, tentatively titled The True Idol, is scheduled for release April 6, it was announced on Monday. It will contain She Bangs, along with another Martin hit, Shake Your Bon Bon, and a cover of Elton John's Rocket Man, which Hung sang recently on The Ellen DeGeneres Show.

    And for those of you hankering for more Hung, the disc will include a 40-minute DVD with behind-the-scenes footage from his studio recording sessions and fan questions and answers.

    'He may not be the next American Idol, but he is definitely a star and inspiration to everyone who sings in the shower,' said Mr Marc Juris, president of Fuse, which will exclusively air his She Bangs video.

    Koch Records general manager Alan Grunblatt was even more effusive: 'He is the new Elvis!'

    Hung, a 21-year-old civil engineering student at the University of California, Berkeley, has inspired fan clubs and websites that sing his praises. -- AP
