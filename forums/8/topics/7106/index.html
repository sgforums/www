    hi e10,
    it seems most countries that have conscripted women into their armed forces have done so in times of national crisis. since the men are constantly needed as infantry to replace those killed in battle, women were usually needed to fill in the less combat intensive roles that the men have vacated. once the war is over the women are usually demobilised and the armed forces again are an all male occupation. Two examples I can think of are present day Israel surrounded by hostile neighbours & the Soviet Union in World War 2.

    Does anyone know how Israel Defense Force utilise women in combat roles ?

    I read a couple months back In WW2 the Soviets enlisted women as signallers, medics, MPs and in all women anti-aircraft gun units. There were a couple of all female fighter wings too. which were usually better than most of their male counterparts. but since the war is infantry intensive & you can't put women in there, the majority of Soviet women were conscripted into building weopons for the war effort.

    I can't think of any nation that has complusory enlistment for women during peacetime. It just isn't practical considering women can't be in infantry, arty & armour and they don't have a need for so many medics, nurses, signallers, technicians, MPs etc.

    The countries that have low birth rates & complusory NS like Sweden, Italy, Switzerland seems to do fine without enlisting women. I guese its a matter of reorganising the structure of the unit perhaps & getting more high tech weopons.
