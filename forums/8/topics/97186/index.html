    Some like it hot... and why not?
    Boiling Coca-Cola and cooking chicken in it? Not harmful, says an expert

    THERE is a Chinese recipe that calls for boiling Coca-Cola and cooking chicken in it. I would like to know if boiling Coke is harmful - would there be chemical reactions in the process of heating the soft drink?

    BOILING foods in Coke is unlikely to cause any adverse health problems.

    It is not, of course, a traditional Chinese method of preparing foods as Coke has not been around that long.

    Most carbonated drinks are mixtures of water, sugar, acids, colours, flavours and carbon dioxide dissolved in the water to give them the fizz.

    But once the drink is boiled, the carbon dioxide will be lost.

    There is also some caffeine in Coke but the amount is small in comparison with a cup of coffee - around 30mg a can compared with 130mg from a cup of coffee.

    So this will not cause a problem for most people.

    Cooking the chicken in Coke may help to tenderise the meat as the acids present will help to break down the protein, and the presence of the colours and flavours may give the chicken some additional attributes that contribute to its eating enjoyment.

    Those people who are deficient in calcium should not have a high caffeine intake as this may interfere with calcium uptake. So if you fall into that group, then perhaps the 'Chicken-in-Coke' dish is not the best for you.

    Not too much of any particular food is recommended, so if you have cooked your chicken in Coke, it would not seem a good idea to drink Coke or indeed any other high caffeine drink with the meal - stick with water.

    Associate Professor Philip John Barlow
    Director
