    There are a few things you could do when dealing with spam:

    1. Block the address, or create a filter rule to delete any email from the particular sender or domain.

    2. Write an email to the postmaster of the sender's ISP (eg. postmaster@terra.com.mx) and report the spam abuse.

    3. If the sender's email address is spoofed (or fake -- most likely as Cote D' Ivoire domain names do not end with .mx), do a whois lookup at various websites like samspade.org with the source IP address from your email headers, identify the actual ISP and send an abuse report email to the postmaster of that ISP.

    One last thing, do not reply to spam email as it only identifies your email address as active and valid. Hope this helps.
