    mr threadstarter:

    If your intention is to enter NUS/NTU/SMU, go to JC for your pre-university education. Chanes are.. a lousy JC student has better chances of being admitted into a local varsity than a lousy polytechnic student. Why? Our education system has been historically biased towards -ahem- -ahem-

    There will be slackers, industrious students and geniuses regardless what pre-university institution (JC or Poly or CI) you enrol. It's just the proportion of such people differs. As long you are brilliant student (at JC/Poly/CI), you will definitely make it into university.

    if you aren't confident that you can perform well at Polytechnic, go for JC. It is a good safety-net, to make sure you get into local varsity. If you intend to go overseas to furthur your education, I think the polytechnic route is good because it saves you one year living expenses abroad and the school fee for 1 academic year if you were to enrol at an overseas university. The polytechnic route prepares you for direct entry into the 2nd year of your degree.
