    StarHub to raise cable TV subscription charges in July

    SINGAPORE : StarHub will increase its subscription charges for cable TV by between $4 and $10 a month.

    From July 11, subscription for the Basic Group and Value Pack will go up by $4.

    That means their cheapest package of three basic groups will cost $24.

    And in October, the price for the Sports Group will be up $10 to $25. In 2004, the price for this group almost doubled - from $8 to $15.

    So sports fans will be the ones who pay most.

    "It's outrageous! They've got enough money and they don't show enough international sports anyway. Why are they charging more money?" questioned Michael Reid, a StarHub pay-TV subscriber.

    "I'm unhappy that the increase is so great. But it's a monopoly, there's nothing we can do about it," said another StarHub pay-TV subscriber.

    Some are already thinking of alternatives.

    "At the moment, Internet streaming of sports telecast is quite popular. So maybe some people will turn to that," said James Kuwe, also a StarHub pay-TV subscriber.

    But not all seem to mind the higher costs.

    "I feel that it's kind of okay because I'm into sports. It's my passion. So I don't mind paying a little bit more," said StarHub pay-TV subscriber Azmi Bin Affandi.

    StarHub's Thomas Ee said the hike is only natural.

    He added that it has not increased prices like other pay-TV operators around the world, despite a steady rise in costs.

    He also said that it's impossible to continue absorbing the cost indefinitely so it's left with no choice.

    The price hike will also affect commercial enterprises.

    StarBub increases for such establishments will be rolled out around October.

    "I think what we'll do is adopt a wait-and-see attitude and look at the level of price increase for what it is now. If it's not too high, we'll not pass on the increase in prices to consumers," said Mark Teo, marketing and sales director of Peranakan Place.

    But not everything will cost more. StarHub will roll out two new channels at no extra charge. The History Channel and the Crime and Investigation Network Channel will be available to cable subscribers in mid June. - CNA /ls

    Approval not needed, MDA says of StarHub's subscription fee hike

    SINGAPORE : Starhub Cable Vision (SCV) was "not required" to seek the approval of the Media Development Authority (MDA) on its decision to raise subscription fees, the regulatory body said on Thursday in response to Today's queries.

    Nevertheless, MDA added: "StarHub is required to inform MDA of any changes to its published subscription rates and it did so."

    Under the existing media competition code, MDA does not regulate the pricing of media services.

    Asked to comment on the fact that the monthly subscription fee for SCV's sports package had more than tripled in the last three years, and whether consumer interests were adequately protected, MDA noted that content prices, "particularly those of popular sports like the English Premier League have been increasing worldwide".

    In the United Kingdom, MDA pointed out, rival pay-TV operators BSkyB and Setanta recently paid a total of Â£1.7 billion ($5.20 billion) for broadcast rights - a 66-per-cent increase over what BSkyB dished out the last time round, when it had enjoyed exclusive broadcasting rights.

    Meanwhile, MDA is revising its media competition code. Among other changes, it is considering price controls - a proposal that has met with strong resistance from incumbent media players. A second round of consultation would be held and the process completed by year's end.

    Mr Seah Seng Choon, the executive director of the Consumers' Association of Singapore (Case), said it would support price regulation in markets "where there is a single dominant player". "At least then there would be somebody watching over the manner in which the price adjustments are made," he said.

    Case felt "very strongly" about SCV's stance on not allowing recent subscribers to terminate their contracts without incurring penalties. It has written to SCV and plans to meet with its representatives.

    SCV told Today it would reply to Case's letter "by providing more information about our rate adjustments". -

    CASE leaves investigation of cable TV hikes to Competition Commission

    SINGAPORE : The Consumers Association of Singapore (CASE) said it had already raised the issue of StarHub Cable TV's price hike to the Competition Commission, and would leave it to them to investigate the matter.

    CASE President Yeo Guat Kwang was speaking to reporters during the Committee Against GST Profiteering walkabout in Hougang on Sunday.

    He said CASE was not against price increases, but companies must justify such increases.

    Mr Yeo said: "Lately we see organisations like SCV making adjustments not in terms of percentage, but in terms of multiplying factor. This is something we are quite concerned about, because it is important for the service provider to come up with clear explanations.

    "We are not against periodic adjustment per se. As businesses, we know and understand that when costs go up, businesses must make adjustments. The question is transparency, and how positive businesses are actually doing it in terms of giving consumers clear information.

    "If the price adjustment is not in gradual terms or small percentages but in multiplying factor like two or three times, I think businesses should respect the choice of consumers."

    He called for such businesses to give consumers a choice to opt out of contracts even if the businesses have done nothing illegal.

    Mr Yeo said: "Although legally the companies and service providers have the right to do so, I hope they will consider the sentiments of consumers. They should give consumers the right to opt out." - CNA/de
