    15 Jan 00, 09:26
    This is my first time where I decide to start a thread. This question have been brought up in some threads and I am trying to find my own answers. I am looking forward to the answers of many others.

    How many of you have ever gotten over your ex-bf/gf? Can you actually get over someone? Is it true that one could go into another r/s with the memory of someone whom you share with? Suppose if you are married, and your spouse pass away, will you marry again? The basis of these questions is : Can we actually let go, whether for ourselves or the other party?

    I will attempt to put my own answers and you may choose to disagree. IMHO, I don't think that it is ever possible to get over someone whom you love deeply. Every now and then, even years later, something reminds you of the other person - a certain smell, a few notes of a song, a certain place or some words which trigger some memory - and suddenly you feel just as bad as the day the other person told you he/she never wanted to see you again. If you want to know the honest truth, you never completely get over someone you really cared about... Someone mentioned earlier in one post that love is when you take away everything and you find that you are still concerned over that person. And perhaps, it is this reason that some people could not let go.

    In the case of when you lose your loved one to Death, I think getting over is much difficult. I am sure that I will get opposition to my stand and I can accomodate a difference in opinion here. Their opinion is echoed thru a quote in a comic series entitled Sandman, where Dream, the protagonist of the story told his son Orpheus (who could not get over the death of his wife, Eurydice), and he said,

    "You are mortal: it is the mortal way. You attend the funeral, you bid the dead farewell. You grieve. Then you continue with your life. And at times the fact of her absence will hit you like a blow to the chest, and you will weep. But this will happen less and less as time goes on.
    She is dead. You are alive. So live."

    Perhaps, the only comfort one seek from emotional hurt is by the simple notion of Time. Time heals all wounds, yet I think it is not whether the healing is the question, as we can never wash away the scars.

    Well, after listening to all my blabber, what do you think?
