    A man being forced into a car, and later putting up a struggle in the vehicle.
    Two members of the public, who witnessed these acts separately, called the police who later uncovered the murder of a 56-year-old Chinese man.

    Police found a red car abandoned on level 4B of a 5-storey carpark in Boon Keng Road, and a body in the boot.

    They were first tipped off at about noon by a woman who was at Jalan Wangi, off MacPherson Road.

    She saw a man struggling with two other men, and being pushed into a car.

    Twenty minutes later, another call - this time from a driver near Bendemeer Road.

    The driver reported that 3 men were struggling in the back seat of a red Mercedes Benz.

    One man was bleeding from head injuries and there were bloodstains in the car.

    Police searched the Boon Keng area, and at 12.35pm, found the car in the carpark at Block 6A.

    Channel NewsAsia was not allowed to get close to the car in which the crime took place.

    What police saw was the stereo speaker was removed, and in the hole where the speaker was supposed to be, a human hand was sticking out.

    Police immediately broke into the front door of the car, opened up the boot, and found the body of the man.

    "The deceased was a Chinese man in his 50s. What he was wearing was a pair of dark brown trousers and a green checked short-sleeved shirt. He had injuries to his face, pretty swollen in the face region," said police spokesperson ASP Philip Mah.

    "What we're doing now is to carry investigations at the scene, as well as to identify what the motive of the murder was.

    "What I'd like to say now is to register our deepest appreciation to the two members of the public who were able to note down the particulars, the details of the vehicle and give us some location as to where the vehicle was.

    "Now this is very crucial, very important in our investigations," ASP Mah added.
