  • fetch

    The very definition of a country is not static. It changes with the times. Altho i've been a singaporean all my life, and i hope that this little island will exist hopefully 100years from now, looking at the way things i'm going, i'm not very hopeful. I don't think we'd survive for another 100 years as long as the pappies are in power.

    People say absolute power corrupts absolutely... if you do read machiavelli, he proposes all the amoral stuff for leaders: be inhumane if you must, be cruel if you must, for a leader's greatest aim is for glory and power. that is what i think pappies are doing right now, especially the old man. bring down the opposition at all cost. but leave one or two of them, just to satisfy the people. give them the illusion that they hold the voting power. it's a delusion in my case.

    cities and states rarely last beyond their founding fathers, unless the virtue of the founding father is trasmitted to the masses instead. but in our case, has it been done? the iron grip is still ever present. the OB markers are there. once our great leader passes, we may still have the young dragon, but after? the lineage will fail, cos it's destined to fail. things good and fair are not a blessing, nor eternal. then what? the iron grip is still there... even if the masses want to take over and save our dear old island, too bad.

    but again, looking at the creative minds of singaporeans on the net, with people at talkingcock.com and sintercom and the roundtable, we may have some hope yet. we have capable people for sure, but the more capable people are, the more they are turned off by the iron hand.

    i love this place, cos i grew up here. i want it to succeed, but not at the expense of the poor and disadvantaged. we have forgotten this segment of society, while we all chase for more $ and bigger pay packets. i want it to be home, but what i see is a nation who is like a harlot, who tries to be everything and anything profitable. an education hub, a biomedical hub, electronics hub, transport hub, tourist haven, financial hub...

    do you think our lines are spread too thin? do you think we really have that many good people to support all these lofty aims?

    i'm sorry i have to gripe at my country, but let's just hope her future inhabitants will care a bit more. tall order. are you hubbing yet?
