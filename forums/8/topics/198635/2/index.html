    Triple fireworks treat planned for NDP 2006

    SINGAPORE : For the first time, a triple treat is in store for fans of fireworks at this year's National Day Parade.

    Besides the traditional fireworks finale, you can also expect fireworks to go off midway and at the start of the show.

    The show will start with a bang, with fireworks bursting into the sky as high as 80 metres from the rim of the stadium for about a minute.

    The stadium will be transformed into a giant birthday cake with 41 candles, signifying the nation's 41st birthday.

    As a safety precaution, the last few rows round the stadium will be cordoned off; Mountbatten Road and Nicoll Highway are also closed to traffic.

    Brackets have been specially tailor made for this year's show; each of the 50 crates can take a load of up to 50 kilogrammes.

    It takes some eight men to launch just one crate and each of them is unique, packed with individualised fireworks.

    Said Major Jason Lee, Chairman Fireworks, NDP 2006, "We have many, many layers of safeguards. All the fireworks cues are actually coded into a digital code and it is actually overlaid onto the music that is being played during the show. In the event that our digital system fails, we have a manual voice back-up to ensure that we can still control the fireworks."

    In the middle of the show, a minute of multi-coloured fireworks will burst out from the heart of the stadium during the Provost display.

    About 20 props and mobile stages will be rigged with low-level fireworks, hitting as high as 20 metres.

    Said Adam Tan, pyro-technician, NDP 2006, "We have quite a lot of mobile platforms, mobile staging. We needed to introduce a wireless remote firing to control the synchronised fireworks remotely. Therefore we have imported a German system, and this system works well in an environment of close proximity with a wireless device to about 500 metres."

    The climax of the fireworks show is the three-minute grand finale.

    The fireworks will be launched from the practice track, some 200 metres from the stadium.

    They are expected to scale some 250 metres into the sky, providing a spectacular view even for those watching the fireworks away from the stadium. - CNA /ct

    Team Italy kicks off Singapore Fireworks Festival

    SINGAPORE : If you don't have tickets to the National Day Parade, there is no reason for you to miss out on the fun.

    Organisers of this year's parade have lined up four fireworks displays as part of the Fireworks Festival to complement the celebrations at the National Stadium.

    Team Italy dazzled Singaporeans in the first of the series on Saturday night, by making the fireworks dance on water.

    To create that illusion, main designer Francesco Ambrico, who is also a magician in Italy, selected two spots where the barrels of fireworks pointed directly at the water.

    Only the latest and most sophisticated types of fireworks were chosen, and care was taken to ensure there were enough fireworks to create a 12-minute display.

    Said team member Dominique Brezac, "We're displaying with a computerised firing system, so we have a back-up on this in case one is broken during the show. Also, we have the possibility to stop the fireworks at anytime according to what can happen during the fireworks."

    His team of 12 men worked round the clock for the past week to put up the spectacular show. - CNA /ct
