    Another S'porean killed by falling tree in Mt Ophir, Johor
    Posted: 20 May 2007 1816 hrs

    SINGAPORE: Another Singaporean has died after being hit by a falling tree in Mount Ophir, Johor.

    48-year-old Abdul Razak Abdul Hamid, a security officer at the Changi Airport, was killed at about 10pm on Saturday when a tree fell on him following a storm.

    Muar police said a post-mortem showed the cause of death was massive abdominal and chest injuries.

    Mr Abdul Razak leaves behind a wife and three children.

    According to the Malaysian Star newspaper, he had gone to the mountain with 11 other Singaporeans.

    He was killed not far from where a Singaporean climber died on May 15 after being hit by a falling tree.

    27-year-old trainee teacher Mohammad Rohaizam Tumadi was asleep in a tent when the incident happened at 5.15am.

    He suffered severe head injuries and was pronounced dead on arrival at the Tangkak Hospital. - CNA/ir
