    there's a difference between being contented and blindly accepting things in life as being undeniable facts. singaporeans may be kiasu, and we may set what some will term "unrealistic" goals and aims, but isn't that what got sg so far? our ancestors that came over to start a new life were not simply satisfied when they had a slightly better standard of living than what they "suffered" back home, but instead decided to strive further and test their limits. if they did not have that spirit of "shang jin xin", sg would definitely have not gone as far as it is today.

    being contented and ambitious isn't mutually exclusive. one can be happy with what he enjoys now, and yet, still continue striving to go that extra mile, to go that step further, for his own reasons. and therein lies the difference.

    there are those who will complain non-stop, expecting life to be perfect, and all things to be handed to them on a silver platter. these are the truly greedy ones... or rather, lazy. all they know how to do is pick and criticise, expecting others to do the dirty work for them. they think the world owes them. majorly. these people will never be contented, because in order of be contented, you first have to realise what you want. such people equate happiness with material gains, or status symbols. if i get a degree, i'll be happy. if i get a high-paying job, i'll be happy. if i fall in love, i'll be happy. if i have a sports car and a condo, i'll be happy. if i have a kid, i'll be happy. but regardless of how many of such "aims" he achieves, he'll never truly be happy. because he cannot appreciate his "wealth". he doesnt even know the meaning of happiness.

    such a person is the total opp of someone who is motivated. if one is ambitious, and likes to test his limits, you cannot condemn him as being greedy and not knowing the true meaning of contentment. he is happy, and does realise the value of his life now, but would like to go that bit further, to achieve a little more. it's that extra for him. you cannot blame others for wanting more than you do. most of us would like to enjoy a little material stuff for awhile. we want to sit in a good car, watch a decent movie, have a delicious meal. most of us, at one pt of time or another, would like to live the good life for awhile. however, unfortunately, in order to do so in sg, you have to work. it doesn't mean that all office workers are mindless participants of the whole rat race... how can you be sure of the motivation behind their hard work? different people have different standards and achievements for themselves, what matters is not the dream, but the action. what they do (or dont do) to fulfill that dream is the discriminating factor.

    sgreans have to learn to appreciate what they have, and not simply demand for more because it's the social "norm". contentment shouldnt be at the expense of ambition.
