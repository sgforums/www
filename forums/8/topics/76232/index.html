    -from CNA

    Bank customers with S$10,000 deposit can now apply for credit cards

    Bank customers earning less than S$30,000 a year can now apply for credit cards provided they have at least S$10,000 deposited with the bank.

    The credit chalked up on the credit card will then be secured against the deposit.

    This change, announced by the Monetary Authority on Thursday, takes effect immediately.

    In the past, applicants for credit cards need to have an annual salary of S$30,000 to qualify for a credit card.

    The change is good news for retirees and well-heeled individuals who have no income but plenty of money in their banks.

    The Monetary Authority stresses that the change does not compromise the fundamental objective of discouraging Singaporeans from spending beyond their means.
