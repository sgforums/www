    The wealth of a nation can only really be created by productive labour. As the banking system becomes more and more deregulated, opportunistic financiers will gravitate towards manipulating financial instruments for profit. Interest rates will rise to the point where income generated by labour cannot keep up. This will make more people play the numbers game instead of engaging in productive labour until one day the system collapses because there is not enough resources for the majority, who will presumably then violently revolt. To prevent this financiers must create a climate of fear and insecurity that will cow the majority or send them far away to fight distant wars of plunder. In this way, they kill 2 birds with one stone.

    The solution to end all this suffering is to eliminate interest on loans and replace it with profit sharing arrangements. Then financiers can only earn from productive labour and the vicious cycle of rising interest rates and its consequences is broken. Governments cannot be trusted to effectively regulate interest rates because moneyed financiers will eventually coopt government and start a new cycle of rising interest rates. However, the restriciton of interest on loans can be enforced because it will have the support of the majority national stakeholders.
