    Hi Jubilee,

    "The Little Prince" is a fairy tale children book written recently, and it deals with various issues, and you should read the book and see the pictures drawn on the book. Some stuff may seem trivial at the first glance, but you see the depth later. It is difficult to explain the story to you. You have to read it to see for yourself.

    Yes, you mentioned "Alice in Wonderland", which is one of my all-time favourites and I have read the original version of "Alice in Wonderland" and "Thru the Looking glass". For some of you who might not know, Lewis Carroll is a mathematician who specialized in geometry, and he encoded a lot of wierd geometry in his book which is still worth of study and think about. For example, the chess board is using a concept in pure maths called Banach spaces. You can read that in the story, in a subtle fashion.

    I recommend another children book entitled "Flatland" written by Abbott, and it is about how a 2-dimensional being encounters a 3-dimensional being.. It is quite funny, and for those who are interested in geometry...
