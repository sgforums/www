    Here's how it works:

    Direct from MSN

    "when it comes to almost the end of winter
    which is almost the start of summer
    a designated day would be chosen
    on that day, at 2am in the morning you move the clock one hour ahead, i.e 3am
    you lose on hour

    when autumn starts in march the next year
    you choose a designated day again, and move back the clock at 2am
    so u gain back one hour

    in summer till spring, ie. nov till march
    we have long days, sun sets at say 8, 9?
    in winter we have short days
    sun sets at say 4,5"

    This is based on Australia but the concept is there

    31 Mar 07, 22:09
    In Australia, daylight saving starts on the last Sunday morning of October (Spring) and finishes on the last Sunday morning of March (Autumn).

    During Summer time, daylight is longer and having daylight saving (to move the time forward from 2am to 3am) provides a few benefits to the people. We can enjoy longer daylight after work, can do more outdoor activities in the nice warm weather and can also save electricity. In Summer, sun doesn't set until 8 or 9pm.

    When Summer finishes, weather is getting cooler and daylight is getting shorter so we adjust the time backward from 2am to 1am. We just did that last Sunday which made us 2 hours ahead of Singapore now instead of 3 hours.
