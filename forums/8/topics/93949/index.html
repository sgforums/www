    Taipei-- A-do and JJ Lin were barred from performing in China on Tuesday.

    They were to sing at a show in Xiangshan in a Zhejiang province with Malaysian act Ah Niu and HK singers Aaron Kwok and Priscilia Chan, reported Taiwan's United Daily News.

    But the Chinese culture bureau did not grant the Singaporean performers permits.

    It did not give a reason for rejecting their application .

    The Bureau reportedly just said in a document : "All allowed to perform except Singaporean singers A-do and JJ Lin"

    Hsu wei chi, the Taiwanese manager of the two singers, told the paper that they were to take part in five other activities in China in the next two months.

    But the events were also cancelled, she said.

    Singaporean Stefanie Sun gave concerts in China in the year , but she has been bisy recording an album in Taiwan recently.
