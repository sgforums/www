    Does anyone here know that there is a difference between love and romance?
    its a slight difference that can easily over-looked.

    A common fallacy about love is that it has to be wildly romantic. Otherwise it doesn't qualify as the real think. This perception has found its way into our world through the books we read, as well as the TV programmes and movies we view. We go away thinking that we now what love is all about. Then we throw ourselves passionately and mindlessly into romance, conveniently forgetting that romance is short-lived.

    True love takes time to build. A touch of romance will make it so much sweeter, but love does not have to be full of romantic and dramatic moments all the time. More importantly, it rests upon a solid foundation of shared thinking and values,mutual appreciation, and admiration. Just as you would get crappy grades if you do not study consistently over the year, you would end up with a shaky relationship if you skip the basics and plunge straight into heady romance. YOu really have to like and understand the person, not just the way he or she looks, or whether he or she does romantic things for you.

    Lok back at your past relationships, think about it, are both of you in love? or rather, you were two dreamy people trapped in a romantic "haze" that blured your vision and clouded your thinking? Most of us wouldn't stop by to think if we would be suitable for each other. We didn't bother to question the foundation on which our relationship was built on. IN our haste to live out our romantic dreams, we had skipped the "friendship" stage. Had we taken time to get to know each other, we would have discovered marked differences in our thinking and values, and would have become friends rather than a couple.

    More than we care to admit, we all desire romance. It's the stuff that makes us feel we are walking on air in a hypnotic state.Nevertheless, romance is just a small part of true love. A relationship based solely on romance will end the minute the romantic feelings fizzle out.

    Most important of all, take time to really understand and appreciate each other, and most of all, by understanding yourself well and reminding yourself what you really want in life.
