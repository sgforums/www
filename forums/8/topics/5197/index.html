    Real/eternal love is often misinterpreted as love between a couple that will last for at least a life time.
    That's wrong. In real love, the other's well-being is more important than yours, and as this world isn't prefect, you often find yourself stuck in a situation that you loved someone but that someone don't love you. Most would give up the other to save themselves from hurt, a few will get more hurt from regret that they gave up, the rest will realised that actually they didn't like the other enough to call it love.
    There have been many cases where people settle for second best,(because of family pressure, peer pressure, age, financial problems, gratitude) and sometimes you wonder if that marriage is a transaction itself. And can you fault it? (especially when it's a case of survival?)

    But if the love is so strong that the well-being of the other is all that matters, and you'll find yourself lending a helping hand to him/her regardless of whether he/she appreciates it or not.
    If he/she also loves you in the same magnitude, it's a bonus.

    People is often searching for true love, not knowing the true love is actually to be given to others by themselves.
