    Results alone is not a good indicator of students' abilities. However, I think it is the easiest method to implement so far. I know people who are smart and yet they don't do well in exams.
    I don't agree with streaming in primary schools but I think streaming in secondary schools is ok.
    Mixing weaker students with better ones is a good idea only to a certain extent. I've been giving tuition for about one year now and I've seen students who are really weak in their studies. I don't think it will be a good idea to put them in the same class with better students.
    What kind of pace should the teacher follow in this kind of class? If the teacher slows down to accomodate the weaker students, the rest of the class will be bored with the lesson. However, if the teacher goes at a fast pace, the weaker students will be left trailing behind.
    Getting the better students to help with the weaker one should be done with supervision because concepts and ideas may get mixed up and distorted along the way. I agree that it is beneficial for the students as well as the teachers.
    I have one thing to say regarding this. Sometimes the problems do not lie in the ability of the students to grasp new things but rather it is because of language barrier. Students who mainly speak in their mother tongues at home generally have more difficulties in understanding their lessons at school. What do you think about this? Is it true?
