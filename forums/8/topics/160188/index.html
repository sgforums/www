    Philosophers, being philosophers, have long debated on this topic as well. In this essay I would like to cover two branches of philosophical thought that talk about the meaning of life, and they are existentialism and nihilism.

    Existentialism is a philosophical thought that views the individual, the self, the individual's experience, and the uniqueness within a person as the basis for understanding the nature of human existence, and it believes the person himself defines the meaning of life. The French philosopher, Jean-Paul Sartre, talked about the belief of the freedom of the individual, in which we have no choice but to create our own values. By doing so, we are hence laying down the basis of our life, the reason of our existence. He also said that existence precedes essence, and the essence of who we are is determined by our actions and thoughts, rather than by our own existence. Hence the power to choose is at the forefront of most existentialistsÂ’ beliefs, and most do not believe in fate or destiny.

    One of the most prominent concepts in Existentialism is individual freedom. The main idea of existentialism depends on individual freedom, and this concept forms the foundation of existentialism. Only when we act on our own completely free will can we be held responsible for our existence.

    Nihilism is a philosophical view that the world, together with and especially human existence, is without any meaning, purpose, comprehensible truth, or essential value. Nihilism is most often associated with Friedrich Nietzsche, due to the fact that he mentioned that Â“God is DeadÂ”. Nietzsche defined nihilism as any philosophy that rejects the real world and our physical existence and this would result in an apathy toward life. He describes it as "the will to nothingness", the desire to destroy meaning, knowledge, and value.

    Nietzsche proposed a remedy to counter the destructive effects of nihilism, and this is the ubermensch. To be an ubermensch, one must give value to their existence by behaving as if one's very existence were a work of art. Nietzsche believed that being ubermensch would be a necessity for human survival in the post-religious era, which he claimed we are living in right now, when he said that Â“God is DeadÂ”. Another way Nietzsche proposed to remedy nihilism is that we go through a revaluation of our morals.

    We as humans were created mostly alike, with slight differences in build, intelligence, et cetera, we were mostly created equal. However, people would be brought up in different environments, and this would result in vast differences in thinking, and hence, their perceptions of their meaning of life. The first group that Camus talked about, are people who find no meaning in life, and are similar to nihilists. These people can be loosely classified into two groups, people who once had an earthly possession or dream that they lost, hence they lose their meaning of life, and the other group is the true nihilists, and people who have come to a conclusion, logically, that life has no meaning.

    On the other side of the Â‘meaning of lifeÂ’ spectrum, lies the successful existentialist. This group consists of idealists, rebels with a cause, and people who live life to the fullest. In a way, these people let their meaning of life dictate the way they use their lives.

I do have my dreams and my ambitions, and that, together with God, gives me a reason to strive for certain things, gives my life meaning and a lot to hope for, both in the near future, and in the realm of the eternal.
