    Here's my take on this issue...

    Academic performance (the paper) is a gauge of your ability to handle situations just as well as any work experience. If you think about it, what's the difference between a resume full of good references from past employers and one that is full of As and Bs? almost none other than the former is more subjective while the lattr is more objective. But even that is debatable. One has to go through so many years of schooling to prove that he is ideal for a certain job e.g. engineering. You need formal education for that, I'm afraid. Medicine and dentistry as well.

    Academic qualifications combined with experiences (not neccessarily work experience) form your education. You're learning everyday, right? Sure, there has to be a balance, but where do you start? Think about it in its simplest form. Would you rather read 20 pages of a person's resume that is filled with past employer's comments, which can be very subjective? Or would you rather look at a 2 page result sheet? That's only for 1 person by the way. And we all know that a person in HR may have to browse through over a thousand applicants for just one position.
