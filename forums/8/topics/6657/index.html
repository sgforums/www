    I've heard from a friend that the 3210's built-in antenna is "directional". I sort of agree with him. He said that there was once his 3210's signal strength is 4 bars, and when he turned the phone it dropped to 1 bar. Guess that's is the trade-off of having built-in antenna. Me used Ericsson 628, 688, Motorola StarTac X before, then switched to Nokia 6110, 8210, 8850. I think Nokia's software is simply great! Just like the way the menus work, the configurable profiles etc etc..

    The 8850's backlid is the coolest I've seen... plus the chrome buttons. The glass screen also adds a classy touch. But the reception sucks! The signal strength fluctuates a lot, and in "ulu" areas it can't decide whether to use GSM or PCM. I've sold my 8850 after barely a week after having a dropped call. So it's back to using the hip and light 8210. Same functions as 8850, lighter, and best of all... Cheaper!! As for the yellow backlid display... too bad! Just gotta live with it I guess...

    As for the T28, the blue backlid is nice, but I don't like the antenna! It's even thicker than the phone itself! (with ultra-slim batt)

    FnU: So far only the 3210 has ringtone composer. 8210 and 8850 doesn't have. So it's wise to keep a 3210 to make your own ringtones. Or you can get a infra-red device to send the ringtone to your 8210/8850.

    I strongly recommand the 8210. Many latest functions, like voice dial, dual-band, built-in vibrator, picture messages, changable front cover, and great value for money. So far it's the lightest in the market. Me got mine before it was officially launched, paid $1.1k+!
