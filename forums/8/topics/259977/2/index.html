    TS, my grandma scenario almost the same as yours. She move around my uncles and aunties home until they finally decide to put her in the nursing home 7 years ago. By then she had dementia, and was bed-ridden

    Yet, I don't blame my aunties, uncles or even my dad for doing so. Look at it this way. They have their own lives to live. The adults would have 2 work in the morning, the children have 2 go to school. No one is at home most of the time. And usually, during this long time from morning to evening, there's always a big possibility that something may happen to her, and no one is at home. Putting her in a old folks home/nursing home is the best possible way out.

    Anyway, back to my grandma. 8 months after my grandma was put in the nursing home, we receive a call that we better rush down to the nursing home as it seems my grandma could possibly pass away that night. That evening, we went to visit her. I remember how her face lit up when she saw us(as she doted on me and my sis a lot when we were young).

    The next morning at around 8plus, the nursing home called to say that my grandma had pass away. It seems as if she wanted to see her children and grandchildren before she leaves.

    I just hope she's happy wherever she is now.
