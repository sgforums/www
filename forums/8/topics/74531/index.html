    Body found in floating fridge
    TOKYO - Japanese police said yesterday they had found the body of a woman inside a refrigerator that was floating in the water at a Japanese port.

    'We initially received a report of illegal dumping of a refrigerator, which was found floating in the port,' said a spokesman for Niigata prefectural police.

    Advertisement

    'Port workers picked up the fridge and landed it on a wharf. They noticed something like human hair coming out from the door of the fridge. When they opened the door with police officers, they found a woman's body inside,' he said.

    The body was found at the Kashiwazaki port in Niigata prefecture, 230km north of Tokyo.

    'We have set up an investigation team with 100 officers and investigators,' the police spokesman said.

    An autopsy was being performed yesterday on the body, which was found early on Tuesday, the spokesman said, without giving further details.

    News reports said police suspect that the woman had been strangled and that the fridge door was bound by a metal wire.

    The woman was aged between her late teens and early 30s and was wearing a dark parka and a pair of blue jeans, reports said.

    There were no signs of major external injuries and the body was wrapped inside a blanket, the online edition of the Yomiuri Shimbun said. -- AFP
