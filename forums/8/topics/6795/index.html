    Excerpt from SPPA (Singapore Planned Parenthood Association) Annual Report 1999, Teen Survey on Sexuality and Relationship 1999, Page 9.

    -----

    Examinations to pass. Kinks with a boyfriend. Other pressing commitments. Computers attacked by a virus. Despite these difficulties, SPPA Youth Committee and youth groups gave time and effort in coming up with the Teen Survey on Sexuality and Relationship in Singapore. The youths helped in the design of questionnaires and in the encoding of data. The aim of the research was to understand the extent of sexual activity of youth participating in adolescent sexuality programmes of SPPA

    Highlights of the survey:
    1. Some 2,369 youth respondents participated in the survey, mostly from the secondary schools;

    2. Of the 2,369 respondents, only 38.5% reported to have dated;

    3. Majority of respondents did not approve of pre-marital sex;

    4. Only 3.4% of the 2,369 respondents reported to have sexual intercourse (85 respondents);

    5. The data revealed that much of the pre-marital sex among the 3.4% of the respondents who had sex was unprotected.

    -----

    (Note: Survey is sponsored by NYC - National Youth Council)

    If the survey is to be taken at face value, pre-marital sex, or at least up to the age of 18 years old is not common at all.
