    And if you're confident you can beat them, you still have to use any weapon that you can get hold of, be it a chair, or anything that can hurt the person. Strength is the most important, and Reaction is equally so.

    Well, we really did the "Zha Ma" all the time, and I tell you, its really tiring, and your leg can tremble very badly after 10 mins. In fact I already feel quite stressful doing this for 5 mins.

    Then there is this punching thing while doing Zha Ma... and tell you what, sometimes the master will just stand on his whole weight on our thigh when we do Zha Ma, and we will be considered not good enuf if we fall from his standing. He always try to catch us unprepared also...

    And after 1.5 years of training, I was still doing the basics and also 1 standard set of punching moves before I stopped. Quite lazy during that time. But guidance of a master is really important, becos even in ZhaMa, you can get it wrong also...

    ShaoLin Boy, kungfu is something too deep for modern people to understand and endure. You need a lot of guidance and practice before you can be good.
