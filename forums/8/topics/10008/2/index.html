  • Arbiter
    4 Nov 00, 22:08
    Pointing fingers for the sake of pointing fingers is counter-productive, there is no argument there.

    But finding fault does have it's uses. One cannot learn from mistakes until one finds out who, what, how, and why. Unfortunately, pointing fingers is part of this process.

    Maybe, pilots around the world will be less over-confident, and double check to make sure they are on the right runway from now on.

    Maybe, next time pilots will learn to voice out if they THINK something is not quite right with the plane ahead of them.

    Maybe, new procedures/checks will be implemented to more effectively catch this kind of error before it's too late.

    Maybe, airports will learn not to leave miscellaneous material just lying around on their runways, whether in-use or not.

    Maybe, ground radar will be made mandatory at all major air transport hubs, or at least at the ones that experience regular low-visibility conditions.

    If just one of these maybes materialises, and in so doing saves another plane-load of passengers from going up in a searing fireball, wouldn't it be worthwhile?
