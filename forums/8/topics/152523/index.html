    During the course of group meetings at work, i always encounter 2 different kinds of species:

    1. Possumbottoms

    The restless qusimodos who cannot remain in their seats for long, always figeting, cracking their knuckles, drumming their fingers, spinning their pens or changing their seating positions etc.

    The probable reason for this behaviour is likely:

    a. They can't stand long meetings because they either:

    i. a waste of money
    ii. unproductive

    b. They consider themselves hands-on personnel and simply cannot comprehend why these bookkeepers enjoy coffee, tea and small talk.

    2. Long Winders

    The straight one liners who enjoy long (very long) meetings. They exhibit traits like:

    a. love to summarise what another person has just said

    b. express themselves in very long sentences

    c. tend to reiterate what the chairman (usually the company president) has just mentioned.

    They are likely to:

    a. move less at the table,

    b. Have their handphones on silent mode

    c. enjoy free drinks and refreshments because long meetings gives them the chance to escape other responsibilities, which are usually more pressing at that point of time.

    I unreservedly fall under the possumbottom catagory.
