    Singaporeans are not overly idealistic. Wanting the 5Cs do not equate to being idealistic.

    Firstly, let's understand what being idealistic means. A person is idealistic when he/she tries to live according to high standards which are often impractical.

    Since it's not impossible to obtain the 5Cs and is not the least impractical, it's neither right nor justified to consider that idealistic. Examples of being idealistic...
    "the world will live in peace", "there will be no hunger", "everyone will be cared for".

    Everyone has their own ideals and wish, but I believe Singapore to be too practical a country to be considered overly idealistic. We may dream of greater things in life, but as long as we are not blind to the truth or show no understanding of what the world is like, we are safe. People like Hitler, Julius Cesar, stalin, karl marx are idealists, but in singapore? hmm.. don't think they are any.....
