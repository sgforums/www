    Facilities still lacking in Punggol New Town after two years

    I bought a 5-room flat in Punggol after queueing for almost five years. Because of the publicity on Punggol 21 as an attractive waterfront town, I endured the long waiting time to get hold of my dream flat.

    It's been two years since I moved into this new town and the basic facilities are still lacking in this beautiful place.

    All the Punggolians have is a small Punngol Plaza to cater to their daily necessities. There are no restaurants such as McDonalds in Punggol where we can bring our kids for breakfast.

    For those who drive, there is no petrol kiosk.

    I understand that the economic slump in early 2000 had greatly reduced the demand for flats in new towns like Punggol and a lot of public facilities were put on hold.

    However, with the recovery of the Singapore economy, I think it's time for the authorities to revive 'Vision Punggol 21', which convinced people like me to grow my roots in Punngol.

    You won't understand the frustrations of living in Punggol unless you live there yourself.

    I have seen neighbours who simply refused to move to their new Punggol flats because of the great inconvenience.

    I look at it as a vicious cycle where the authorities wait for a more sizeable population in Punggol before they carry out their public projects, while on the other hand, Punggol residents wait for more facilities before they decide to move in.

    I sincerely hope the authorities have not forgotten the promised 'Vision Punggol 21'.

    I love this town and I still strongly believe the vision of a waterfront town would become a reality in the years to come.

    Kenneth Ho Kan Ping
