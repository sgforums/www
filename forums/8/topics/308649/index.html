    A prenuptial agreement, or premarital agreement, is a contract entered into by two people prior to marriage or civil union.

    The content of a prenuptial agreement can vary widely, but commonly includes provisions for the division of property should the couple divorce and any rights to spousal support during or after the dissolution of marriage.

    Prenuptial agreements will never be legally binding in Singapore because the Government is pro-marriage/pro-birth.

    Think about it, pre-nuptial agreements actually encourage a culture of divorce since couples will find that they have little to lose. And there is a chance of the father reneging on the alimony given for child/wife support should the wife really need it.

    I believe that the Women's Charter, though some might argue can easily be abused to favor the protection of women and children over the men, does lead to increased marriage stability and birth rates. That is inline with what the government wants also
