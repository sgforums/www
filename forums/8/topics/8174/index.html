    2 Jul 00, 04:06
    That's just it; Macs are more "surface" level kinda systems. They're for what is termed the "naive user". Open software, use it, close software. I bet all you Mac users can't use the console and 90% don't even know it exists. In that sense, a Mac is one with the OS and I guess that's where the stability comes from.

    PCs can be more raw from the "techie" kinda perspective. It's a lot easier to go around poking in the RAM and to make the system do exactly what you want it to do, plus there're heaps of resources out there to aid you vs. Mac. I guess I compare the two to maybe an auto gearbox vs. manual stick shift.

    On one hand you have a simple to use "pretty pretty" system that has less chance of failure and also less flexibility and control. On the other hand, you have more readily accessible power for a cheaper price.

    In terms of software, PCs dominate hands down; any major title can usually be found on both platforms anyway but I don't know many ppl who play CS on Macs. You always hear of the latest PC-emulator for Mac but almost no one can be bothered emulating Mac.
