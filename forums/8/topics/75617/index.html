    A Parable For Our Times

    Once upon a time, a foreigner landed on the shores of an island and found a giant coconut red and white in colour. He presented this artefact to the island's tribal elders, who thought it a wondrous object and mounted it on a huge pedestal. Many tiny birds flocked around the coconut, pecking at it with their pathetic beaks, only to fall to the ground exhausted.

    The writers of the island, who mainly dwelled in the southern half, became obsessed with the coconut, declaring it to be the best coconut ever found on the island, undoubtedly without equal in all the world.

    Only one man, the wisest and oldest of the tribal elders, a certain S'ralex from the north, remained unconvinced and went away to fashion tools to break this coconut. Then, one Sunday afternoon, took a little drill to the mighty coconut and drilled into the hard outer shell.

    The following Saturday he continued his drilling, aided by a small ginger-headed monster who he named Sekholes. And lo, it came to pass that the drill broke through and S'ralex and his many supporters drank in the milk that lay within.

    S'ralex then took a hammer to the coconut and smashed it into many parts, and the coconut was revealed for what it really was, an object with a hard outer shell but a soft milky inside. S'ralex then cast the many parts of the coconut onto the ground.All of a sudden,many birds blue in colour began to peck at the flesh of the once impregnable coconut.

    The writers of the island then became disinterested in the object, and returned to their favourite subject, a golden god named B'ekam who had been previously been declared false by S'ralex and cast from the shores of the island.

    Here endeth the lesson.
