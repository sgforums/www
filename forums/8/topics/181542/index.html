  • photo_seeker
    8 Mar 06, 20:37
    They will definately only use Local A because General A needs you to be warded for observation, a.k.a post symptoms of General A.

    Your Toe will be numb before they do anything to it, but you can still feel the pulling action, though without any pain in the process. However, my advise is for you to request to let the doctor do it only when you know your toe is numb.

    Cause I have a friend who had a minor surgery carried out to remove part of his thumb due to an incident which he sliced his thumb and the Local A wasn't effective when the doctor started slicing the flesh and pulling out of the nail which eventually cause quite some pain. But don't worry, you have the rights to tell the doctor to wait or even inject more Local A during the process. Just complain that you still can feel and they will pump in more Local A.

    The dressings will be kept as it is after it is wrapped up by the doctor/nurses after the operation. However, my friend being a qualified First Aider, he open the dressing daily and do the cleaning. Basically because he is worried of infection due to discharge, which is common after any surgery.

    Remember to keep the dressing dry, BY ALL MEANS ! Else, you will risk a slower recovery of your wound.

    Last of all, do not worry about the indemity form you're made to sign because it's part of the documentation. I've just underwent a Major Surgery 3 weeks back and here I'm alive typing away already.

    You should be able to walk relatively well, but some slight pain which is able to be covered by the pain-killer. Any wound will cause pain, isn't it ? Take care, rest well and you'll recover faster. Do remember to avoid certain food though.
