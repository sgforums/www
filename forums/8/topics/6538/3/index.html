    Trapattoni: Please save me, United by John Greechan and Ken Lawrence

    EXCLUSIVE: Embattled Fiorentina coach Giovanni Trapattoni last night put his
    faith in Manchester United to save his job by refusing to be part of a Champions
    League stitch-up.

    United are already through to the quarter-finals but a comfortable draw in Valencia tonight would see both teams qualify.

    That would leave the English champions top of their group, the potentially dangerous Fiorentina out of Europe and Trapattoni out of a job.

    Sir Alex Ferguson has left out man of-the-moment Ryan Giggs because of a tight hamstring, yet insists the Welsh winger will be fit to face Bradford on Saturday,
    giving ammunition to the cynics who suggest United, already without suspended
    David Beckham, won't be giving their all in the final group match.

    But Trapattoni is confident that professional pride will not allow United simply to
    cruise.

    'I don't think they would ever sink to such a level,' he said. 'In all these years I have
    learned something about Sir Alex Ferguson. I know he would never allow his
    players not to give 110 per cent. He wouldn't stand for that in any game.

    'There is no doubt in my mind that Manchester United will go there to win and that
    they will play the best team available. They will defend their reputation. It is a team
    with 11 internationals, one of the greatest clubs in the world.'

    The Fiorentina coach would have gained heart from hearing Sir Alex talk about
    United's quest for two more pieces of soccer history in Valencia.

    They are already the first club ever to qualify for the quarter-final of the Champions League four times in a row. Tonight, in the Mestalla Stadium, they have the chance to beat a Spanish club on Spanish soil for the first time and also score their 300th goal in European competition.

    United could still lose top spot in Group B if they lose by three goals to Hector
    Cuper's side and the Old Trafford manager said: 'Tactically, you should never plan
    just to contain. We will be very positive and we want to win. I would be less than
    happy if we lost even 1-0, although that would not take us off the top of the group.'

    United trained for only a short time on a pitch so saturated by two days of rain that
    Slovakian referee Lubos Michel decided to hold a precautionary inspection at
    breakfast time.

    Ferguson added: 'If the rain continues, we will see a very different type of game
    than normal in Spain. A wet, greasy surface could make it an extremely exciting
    match.'

    With Giggs and Beckham out, Ferguson is expected to bring in Quinton Fortune
    with Ole Gunnar Solskjaer likely to partner Teddy Sheringham in attack.
