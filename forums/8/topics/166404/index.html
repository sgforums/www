    You can try Transtar if you don't mind the cost, S$56 (To) & $39 (back), stops at Pasaraykat bus terminal, just behind Bukit Bintang. If you know the way, you can walk, its about 15-20 minutes (for my pace, about 10min) to Marriot/KL plaza (opposite Regent hotel), Sungei Wang plaza & Lot 10 is nearby. Coach with attendance and serve 1 meal + drinks. Individual console, more like business class airline seats, you select your own movies, cartoon, KTV video, massage chair, 16 seater coach, 2nd link way, etc..... Was there last Nov. with this coach, not bad except for the price. no SURCHARGE during peak (www.transtar.com.sg)

    Some one mentioned about the Copthorne hotel bus service, it had already shifted to Copthorne Waterfront hotel in town. you can also check out the website. www.aeroline.com.my (or sg).

    5 Star also offers such services (~S$90 return), but the time is fixed (enquired during NATAS fair), this is a new service, likely, I believe are other such luxury service which I saw at NATAS fair but wasn't that interested due schedule of departure.

    I took this coach instead from Global holidays at Novena Square, just trying out and also save me some $200 in coach cost. they charge $30 single and $50 return to KL stopping at Bangsar LRT. No surcharge for peak or school holiday & eve of holidays, travel by 2nd link way. There is also a seating lounge for travellers both in SG & KL office (some light makan + drinks). The only catch is, you need to find your way to KL hotel, which I thought initially kind of hassle, but after the trip, realise not that difficult to get taxi either. Average fare to KL Bukit Bintang where I stayed is ~RM10. I pay extra cause I had my mum-in-law, wife & 3 kids with me. You are expected to pay extra for luggages, etc.. just like old time SG in the olden days, always ask for meter charging.

    For the adventurous one travelling during this holiday season, I would suggest, taking single trip on any coach service, but travel by Transtar on the return (S$39) because its more worth it than the 26 seaters, which charges at $26 (S) & $52 for return but comes with surcharge $10/trip. I hope my info still not out of date.

    Kontsorium charges $45 return to KL only to Pudu station but with $3 fuel surcharge, not sure its for both way or per trip, trip stopping at hotels and else are priced differently

    You can go from JB if you like and the best place is from LARKIN (not Karlin) and 44 seater buses cost RM21 upwards. I prefer the 25 seater "VIP" buses at least cos they are more comfortable and they cost RM30 upwards.

    If you are going from Singapore you are spoilt for choice. In my opinion, Beach Road is the best departure point (I live in the east) mainly because you can have your pick of bus companies from Sri Maju, Transtar, Five Star, Grassland, Konsortium and many more... Just go to Golden Mile Complex, (you know the place which is like little Thailand) and ask. Prices start from S$27 up for 25 seater bus.

    Also note that prices depend sometimes on demand and supply. Do not be surprised to be quoted a higher price during peak periods such as now which is school holidays. Also buy your return tickets early ... sunday afternoon tickets back to Singapore are always quickly sold out. AND DO NOT BUY FROM road side peddlers promoting their bus tickets ... You risk being stranded at JB or Woodlands instead of Beach Road and they never leave on time. Sometimes, they wait till enough passengers before they leave... If you are desperate, give it a shot. Better to take a schedule bus to JB if tickets to Singapore are sold out. Very easy to get to Singapore from JB right?

    I prefer to skip the hassle of going to JB (Larkin) The savings is not worth the time wasted. Also, I prefer to depart around midnight cos I sleep in the bus. I find travelling in the day a waste of time and the trip is longer cos of heavier traffic. Expect a 5 hr trip (give or take) depending on traffic and causeway situation.

    I go up to KL at least every other week and my preference is Sri Maju and Konsortium cos they leave exactly on time and I find them cheap, comfortable and reliable. Of course there are the "Premier" buses as well like Nice and Transtar .. "nice" but not necessary for me.

    Staying in Bukit Bintang area is your best bet ... lots of choices Eg: Federal Hotel, Radius International, Allson Genesis, Regent, Hotel Istana, and even budget hotels like Katari & Pudu Hoste near Pudu Bus Station where most of the buses bring you. Bt Bintang is also the most happening place for shopping, makan and pubbing etc but I wouldn't know much about this cos I go to KL as part of my job.

    Any search on the net will give you lots of info ... there are even backpacker guest houses which are really great at Tengkat Tong Shin Area behind Bt Bintang Eg Eight Guest House, Green Hut, Red Palm etc Prices start from RM 20 for a dorm room bed and RM 60 for a double room (2 persons) Cheap and clean if you just want a place to sleep.

    Hope it helps ... PM me if you want to know more

    Take a train to KL.. Can take the night train which departs Tanjong Pagar Railway station at 1030pm, reaching KL the next morning, around 530am. Remember to ask for the sleeping berths (beds), can sleep through the night... when you wake up, reach already. Minimal time wasted, minimal boredom (compared to the bus) and very comfortable too.

    Hotelwise, it depends alot on the class of hotel and your preferred location. I suggest the Crown Princess... 5 stars, and quite close to KLCC (their version of suntec), walking distance. But if you prefer to stay in the Bukit Bintang district (the KL equivalent of our Orchard road), i suggest the Parkroyal Hotel.
