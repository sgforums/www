    First of all, this is how you differentiate a cold from a flu, though most pple thought they are the same. Generally if you are suffering from sore throat, runny nose, nasal congestion, a low-grade fever with muscle aches and a general sick feeling, you got a COLD. Influenza or flu, as commonly known, is catagorised by a high fever, a dry cough, much worse aching all over, and feeling terrible. So in another words, FLU is much more worse than COLD, and need immediate medical attention.

Hard to believe? Believe it. The medicine that doctors prescribe are mainly antibiotics or stuffs that helps you to relieve. On top of that, our body immune system will work hard to make you feel better, eventually get rid of the symptoms.

    1) If you are a smoker, smoke less as they will irritate your respiratory tract.

    2) Gargle with warm salt water or suck lozenges to relieve sore throat.

    3) Inhale steam, but don't scald your face. Good for blocked or runny nose.

    4) Drink warm or hot fluids, never drink icy cold water.

    5) Take plenty of Vitamin C. Oh, they don't cure colds or flus as well, but a healthy dosage can relieve your symptoms.

    6) Hot ginger tea should also help too.

    7) Eat raw garlic if you don't mind the after breath. They are anti-viral and can boost your immune system.

    8 Consume more fruits for the essential vitamins as well as the juice. You need plenty of fluids beside drinking warm water.

    9) No air-conditioning room. It traps lots of airborne virus and would irritate you even further.

    I guess heaty foods are those who can make your body temperature rises, and cooling foods can balance out the temperature to the norm. Our body is designed to function well in the norm body temperature, thus any degree change in temperature and we can find ourselves falling sick and getting fever.
