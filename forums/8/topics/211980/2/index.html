    I see an undeserved respect for aussie degrees in this thread. In the first place, aussie degrees are absurdly easy to obtain. my dad regularly interviews grads for jobs and he finds that even people clearly lacking in intellectual capacity manage to obtain first classes. Also note that many of the universities in australia are in fact polys; they merely changed their names some years back but they remain sucky. there are a few unis that are recognised in sg, i think you can find the list on the ministry of manpower website. if you fail to get into any of these universities, don't bother going degree or no degree. you would simply be wasting you time and money.

    many employers view an aussie education as a soft option (i'm not giving any real evidence here but you can go ask around for yourself) and it will do you little good to have a degree from australia. furthermore, any work experience you obtain there will be worthless in singapore. it is well known how laid back they are and having worked there certainly doesnt count as an asset.

    i haven't offered any alternatives because i cant think of one. perhaps the best you can do for yourself is to overcome your current disadvantage with good performance later. a degree only gets you in the door most times.
