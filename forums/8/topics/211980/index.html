    Having studied in Australia for 3 years, I think pursuing an overseas degree may be a good option for you for the following reasons :

    a) Your english won't be an issue. There are some local Aussies whose standard of english can't even match yours, and many other International Students whose english is much, much worse than yours, but they can still pass because they can at least grasp the concepts and can be understood. Even if u're officially an english-as-1st-lang student, u CAN opt to take eng-as-2nd-lang classes for free coz it's all incl in the fees.

    b) Your result shouldn't really be an issue as long as it's a reasonable pass. Be confident, let them know u're serious abt it, get the proper referrals if required, and there's a high chance they'll let u in. Singapore's standard of education is highly regarded there coz our pre-university curriculum is much more intensive than theirs.

    c) The more laid-back pace can mean 2 things.

    i) Unlike in Singapore, you will have more opportunities and time to study comfortably, explore your options, evolve as a person, and open your eyes. In other words, you get to be yourself.

    ii) You may have problems adjusting to this newfound freedom .. I've come across many Singaporeans who are too used to being told wad to do, not used to having time of their own, not much time for hobbies, not motivated enough .. Aussie shops also close early.. there are alot of distractions like gambling and drugs and MANY S'porean students fall prey to these due to lack of self control..

    d) Last but not least, it would give you a great opportunity to try and get overseas work experience.. so that when you come back home, u'll have a great set of skills most companies would be glad to put to use, for (hopefully) decent pay =)

    All the best 2u, whatever decision u make ^^
