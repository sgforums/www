The ingrain philosophy of most Asian Chinese is still very much confusianism, leaning towards filial piety and the unity of the family unit. Here is not to say that parents who bash up their kids for no darn reason is correct.     So to all those who still think their parents were hard on them, perhaps they might want to look inward and ask yourself if you had played ball.

    Originally posted by shade343:
    So that means your sons did not turn out to be rebellious? Dont mind me If I ask, when you cane your children, did you rush at them and cane them repeatedly on the buttocks or hand? Or was it just one stroke on the palm as a lesson?
    Good question. This is exactly what I meant when I previously wrote than when meting out punishment, it should NEVER be done in rage. Punishment should always done in a calm manner. It is pointless to rant and rage and relentlessly hit out at a defenseless child.

    Caning is done in my house with the charges read out clearly FIRST, ie, the child need to know why he is caned, that chances had been given and ignored. Also the child is also made to understand what consequences would take place if such mischieve is left uncorrected. Method of punishment is then spelled out - one stroke on the palm, two stroke on the palm or a removal of privileges etc.
    There is always a limit, either in terms of number of strokes, or in cases of privileges, a time frame. Once I banned my younger son from xbox for three weeks. His brother can play it anytime he wants but he can only watch.

    I learned NOT to develop rage when punishing the kids because of my mother. The only punishment she knew was to cane, and she'd developed anger in that process.

    While I respect and love my parents, I am aware that not everything they do is right. As a responsible adult, one must adapt and improve the good and throw out the bad when emulating one's elders. Caning was good for me, being angry when caning was bad for me as it does not serve to do anything but to channel all that negative energy to me.

    I was once in a taxi where the driver and I were talking about child upbringing. He said that he always bring his son to the beach when the child messes up. There, he would bash the child up as he is angry. I asked him what was accomplished by that action. I question why he needed to get angry and why channel the anger to the child. He suddenly realised that he was wrong in that. Hopefully the next time the child messes up and is punished, the punishment is something more constructive.

    And no, my sons are not rebellious. But like every child, they are curious. As a parent, it is my responsibility to equip them to cope with life, so no questions are left unanswered. I educated them on sex education even way before the schools do them. They are aware of all the f words that come across in the latest hip hop songs. They know that such words are not to be used. They are also taught to respect others. Each of us have our personal space, and there should not be any violation of such space. To give you an example, permission is seeked when opening personal drawers. Even I need to ask them that I need to open their drawers.

    Allow me to repeat my stand: punishment is necessary, caning is only ONE of the means. It has to be controlled and purposeful. It has to be explained. Once the child knows you mean business and play ball and become responsible, caning is not longer necessary. The child, now can be seen as an equal where negotiations can take place. If the child can earn my respect because he is seen and proven to be responsible, we can all throw away our canes.

    My boys, while will forever be my offspring, are also my equal in many decisions in my home. It is only when there is something critical, that my father hat comes on.

    The trick, really is, when the adult is angry, is he mature enough to handle himself?
