    To seek (or rather pine for) contentment by asking for material benefits like success in work and more tangible equivalents such as the 5 Cs... would be a fallacy. Look at it this way, have you seen rich men and women parading themselves around in their WRX's and Prada-outfits? Here's a question: do they look happy to you? There are individuals out there that have the fortune of being bestowed the 'blessings' you described. But perhaps they too are looking for something that might give them release from whatever pains they are experiencing in their lives or an unknowable pleasure that has eluded their industrious efforts to maintain and improve a luxurious lifestyle.

    Likewise are those social butterflies that attract many admirerers and often wind up becoming celebrities. The sheer bulk of attention numbs them to the world and the amount of pursuers make them spoilt for choice.

    We humans are so like that. We build ourselves up by studying hard and working our butts off to get to a state of security where most purchasable convieniences and joys are within reach-- But always look towards the things that are not.
