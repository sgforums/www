    Originally posted by Qitai:

    I apologise for my poor explanation. Perhaps this should suffice:

    Taken from HDB Website:

    If you are a Singles Grant recipient, you may request to top-up the Singles Grant to a Family Grant when you get married. You and your spouse must meet the following eligibility conditions:

    Date of Marriage and Application
    You and your spouse must be married on or after 1 August 2004. You must submit your application within six months from the date you register your marriage.

    Citizenship
    Your spouse must be a Singapore Citizen (SC) or Singapore Permanent Resident (SPR).

    Household Status
    You and your spouse must not have:
    - bought or sold an HDB flat purchased direct from HDB; or
    - bought or sold a resale flat purchased under the CPF Housing Grant Scheme for Family; or
    - enjoyed other form of housing subsidy (for example, purchased an Executive Condominium from the Developer, enjoyed benefits under the Selective En bloc Redevelopment Scheme, privatisation of HUDC estate, Sale of Flat to Sitting Tenant Scheme, etc).

    However, your spouse can be a SC who is also a Singles Grant recipient.

    Income Ceiling
    Your gross monthly household income must not exceed $8,000.

    Ownership Of Private Residential Property
    You, your spouse and other persons listed in the application must not own any private residential property (including HUDC flat and/or Executive Condominium), house, building, or land or have disposed of such a property within 30 months before the application, or both.

    Top-Up Grant for Buying Another Resale Flat
    The Top-Up Grant can be used to pay the downpayment or to reduce the mortgage loan. Hence, if you do not have enough CPF savings, the grant can be used to pay the downpayment. The balance, if any, must be used to reduce the mortgage loan. However, the Top-Up Grant cannot be used to offset the cash payment where the declared resale price exceeds the market valuation.

    If you are buying the resale flat with a bank loan, the Top-Up Grant will be treated as part of your CPF fund. It can be used to pay for the CPF portion of the downpayment (i.e. not the cash portion), or to reduce the mortgage loan. It will be included in the computation of the CPF withdrawal limit.

    The treatment of downpayment in CPF fund and cash for buying a resale flat with bank loan is summarised in the table below:

    Period 20% Down Payment
    Cash Portion CPF (including Housing Grant) Total
    1 Jan 2004 to 31 Dec 2004 2% 18% 20%
    1 Jan 2005 to 31 Dec 2005 4% 16% 20%
    1 Jan 2006 to 31 Dec 2006 6% 14% 20%
    1 Jan 2007 to 31 Dec 2007 8% 12% 20%
    From 1 Jan 2008 10% 10% 20%

    Notes:
    (a) The cash downpayment is computed based on the lower of the market valuation or resale price.

    (b) The cash downpayment is in addition to the cash-over-valuation ('cov') for cases where the transacted resale price is above market valuation. Resale flat buyers must pay the 'cov' in cash.
