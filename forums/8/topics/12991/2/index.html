    blurblop is right.

    Pyramid or MLM or Network marketing or what ever you call it you name it, is just a name. It is not the shape of an triangle that make it illegal, it is the things people do that make it illegal.

    Stop guessing but visit the site of MTI - Singapore Ministry Of Trade And Industrial, search for multi level marketing. Mr Benjamin Tan, Chairman, Direct Selling Association of Singapore pointed out an example of illegal pyramid schemes like S888.com. Participants had to pay S$27,000 to S888 to join the scheme, and once they join, they receive a fixed commission of S$3000 for every addition member they recruited. Point to note, there is no Goods or services involve. When recruitment become harder, the bubble burst and the people below the triangle lost all their life saving.

    To sustain long in this business, I choose a company that their products I like and even though without recruitment, which is not easy, I still earn through selling the products. If you have the mentality of joining MLM to recruit as many people in short time in order to earn a lot of money (or the primitive pyramid mentality), you chance of surviving in this business is 0%.

    Read RichDadPoorDad, Robert Kiyosaki emphasize the benefit of MLM in the sence of character building which involve public specking, selling and human relationship etc, and not just making money where money will come along the way. Joining MLM don't put you in the I quadrant.

    To be frank, I have just started not long and not very successful in this business at the moment, so I wish myself luck as well as to all of you who are in this business and who plan to join in.
