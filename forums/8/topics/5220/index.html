    Honestly I have no idea why. Let us assume that gambling of any form is bad. To argue and be consistent with first principles, one should ban all forms of gambling in their various guises, including ToTo, 4D etc. But as you know now we even have legal football betting. To argue that gambling is bad, why we should even ban the stockmarket. Don't lots of people buy into the stock market hoping to make big bucks quickly?
    [of course stock market can be classifed as buying financial assets rather than pure gambling, whereas when you buy 4D or bet on horses you aren't buying any assets whatsoever, but in the case of all these instruments punters are still betting on an uncertain outcome.]

    Govt's answer is simple. Since people like gambling so much, if govt bans all gambling, then gambling will just go underground. So it's better to have legalized gambling and at least some of the money goes to charity (rather than some gambling syndicate) and you don't get that many loan-sharks running around chasing people's debts.

    So let's have some gambling. So we have 4D, Toto, and now a world class race course. Why no casino? Perhaps it's just the societal view that race courses and horse racing are somehow more dignified than casino. As you said, it's quite proper to have dignitaries at race events. Even the Queen of England attends some race events. But you don't see Kings and Queens and Presidents attending some Casino event for example. So maybe that's one perhaps superficial reason but still a reason.

(Man U, Liverpool etc). I'm pretty sure that there is a lot of betting going on in Singapore, perhaps not in gambling syndicates, but among supporters of different football clubs let's say.
