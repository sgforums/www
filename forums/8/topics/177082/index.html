    No paper forms liao, all online.

    JAE will start one day after O Levels results are released. However, you can log in to check your points.

    There's no need to fill up all 12 choices. However, if you want a greater chance of entering a poly or JC that you want, better fill up all. By limiting yourself, you might be disappointed if things don't go your way.

    If you have problems filling the 12 choices, one easy way out is to select a particular field, say IT. Then choose all the IT related courses from alll polys and fill them in. How you rank them is up to you. Or if you want science courses from no particular JC, just fill up this way also. One advantage is that you will not be disappointed. The disadvantage is that you are just limiting yourself to a certain field only.

    If the course you want has high COP, one way to overcome it is to study something related. For example, biomedical sciences is notorious for the high COP. However, biomedical engineering has lower COP. Just put biomedical engineering as your choice.

    Hi!

    If you have no other better choices to fill out your last few slot, put at least 1 "dumping ground" course. Those with COP 26+.

    In fact, i only have 1 poly course, offered by 4polys in mind last year. However, do be realistic, do some extensive research first, and infer from previous year's COP as well.

    If your L1R4 is 13, and you're interested in Biomed Sci, you can omit SP.
    Save your slot to fight for NP,TP's Biomed Sci.
    In the event that there is someone competing with you for the same course and with the same L1R4 aggregate as well, the system will admit the one who ranked the course higher.

    Don't waste your slot.
    And for the last few slot, like the 11th or 12th, put one course that has COP of 26+

    Just to be safe, however, do find out the course structure and everything before putting them as "insurance". Because sometimes shit really happens, and you wouldn't want your insurance to be your nightmare for 3years.
