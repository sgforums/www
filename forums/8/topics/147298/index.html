    i extracted this from the straits times forum.

    We refer to the ST Online Forum letter by Henry Lim Li Shun on Sept 30 (SCV's set-top box should be free for subscribers) and thank him for his feedback.

    Rental fee for set-up box covers maintenance costs

    We would like to assure him that StarHub imposed the subscription charge for our analogue set-top box only after careful consideration.

    We would like to clarify that the $2 rental fee for analogue set-top boxes is to recover the increased maintenance and repair costs associated with the continued delivery of our analogue cable TV service.

    StarHub is one of the few remaining operators in the world to continue to operate both an analogue and digital service, as we want to give our customers the benefit of choice.

    To maintain both our existing analogue and digital platforms, a rate adjustment was needed in order to ensure that the quality of our services on both these platforms are not compromised.

    In addition, most manufacturers have stopped producing analogue set-top boxes and their spare parts.

    In order to maintain the analogue service, we have to source for these components via alternative suppliers which are invariably more expensive.

    Even though StarHub is the sole pay TV provider in Singapore, there are many alternative sources of news and entertainment in Singapore, including seven free-to-air channels.

    StarHub has never considered itself a monopoly in the entertainment business and has always endeavoured to weigh the value of what it offers.

    It wants to provide an attractive package of programmes at a price that will appeal to sufficient numbers of customers in order to earn a fair return on its investments.

    It is a common practice for cable TV operators around the world to charge subscribers a small fee for the rental and usage of the set-top boxes, as there are costs involved in maintaining these units.

    In the region, Malaysian pay-TV subscribers who subscribe to the services of the largest pay-TV operator in Malaysia pay upfront to purchase a decoder to view the programmes on the operator's Direct-To-Home satellite platform.

    In Hong Kong, customers of the two major pay-TV operators pay a deposit and monthly rental fee for the set-top box, which is loaned to the customers.

    This same practice is also adopted in Thailand. It is noteworthy that these costs exclude the monthly subscription charges that customers pay to receive the pay-TV services.

    StarHub Digital Cable aims to provide customers with affordable access to its cable TV services. Hence, customers of StarHub Digital Cable are not required to purchase the analogue or digital set-top boxes or pay a deposit fee to use them.

    Until September 1999, our customers were billed a monthly rental fee of $3 for the analogue set-top boxes.

    This fee was subsequently waived, as we had then achieved some cost-savings in the provision of our cable TV services and we wanted to pass on the cost-savings to all our customers to provide them with greater value.

    However, due to the increased costs of maintaining the analogue and digital operating systems, we are now unable to continue to waive the monthly rental charge.

    Please be assured that the rental fee for our analogue set-top box was reinstated only after careful deliberation.

    Additionally, the rental fee of $2 for an analogue set-top box is now a third less than what was charged previously.

    We seek Mr Lim's kind understanding on this matter and hope to receive his continued support.

    SINGAPORE : StarHub has posted sterling results for its third quarter.

    It reported a net profit of S$65.1 million for the three months to September, reversing the year ago loss of S$1.4 million.

    The results beat market expectations.

    Revenue grew 17 percent to S$398 million.

    The turnaround came as StarHub added new mobile phone subscribers and recorded good sales growth.

    Going forward, StarHub expects its full-year earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation and ammortisation to come in at around 31 percent of total service revenue.

    The telco has also raised its guidance for the full year.

    It now expects full-year operating revenue to grow at a percentage rate in the mid-teens, above the previous forecast of low-teens.

    StarHub has declared a third-quarter dividend of 2.5 Singapore cents a share, which will be paid on November 25.

    Going forward, the telco says a new service launch, higher promotion expenses during the holiday season, and costs incurred from recent industry wide policy changes will hurt profit margins.

    But it expects to see continued strong earnings before interest, taxes and amortisation.

    Said its chief executive Terry Clontz, "The strength of our EBITA margin is very good if you look at the third quarter. Our EBITA margin in the fourth quarter is likely to come in between 30 and 31 percent. So for the full year, that will give us about 31 percent EBITA margin.

    "But keep in mind that that additional promotional activity for i-mode and for our normal festive offers is investing in the future, so we make smart investments to acquire customers and to grow revenue best." - CNA /ct
