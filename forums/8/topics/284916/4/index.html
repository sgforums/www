    Originally posted by venny:
    so how exactly does illusion works? jus becos we r left or right brained?

    how can we all be looking at the exact same thing but see different things?
    because essentially it is just a FLAT 2D SINGLE COLOUR IMAGE. there is no clock or anticlock wise movement.

    the brain uses what it knows to try to interpret the movement into something logical and thus is made to arrive at a conclusion that the image is either moving clockwise or anticlockwise.

    in reality, the 2 legs are side by side on the screen. one leg stays in the centre, the other one moves to the left then to the right and always passes the leg in the centre.

    when the leg "passes", you think that it either goes behind or infront of the other leg because that's LOGIC.

    but like i said it is nothing more than a flat piece of blackness on ur screen, to the computer, when the legs are "behind each other", it's ONE patch of black(leg), but to the human mind, its 2 legs in front of each other.

    to link up all the movement, we therefore have to conclude either clock or anti clockwise for the picture to make sense to us.
