    heard of some horror stories, seen them often previously where i worked, its pretty mixed i guess, most of the ones who had failed operations usually share the same stories.

    most of the time its not so much as the operation went wrong but rather the doctors who own the clinics super on to do lasik for anyone who comes in just to earn an extra buck. so you get all sorts of people who were not suitable for lasik getting the operation done ending up with worse vision then before or dry eyes. others i've seen had sucessful lasik but they never took care and the degree came back sometime later sometimes back to the same level or higher.

    this issues aside i've also seen cases that were successful. and they really are much happier. especially the ones who were blind with out their glasses. some happened to have dry eyes too but they consider it to be quite worth it compared to previous situation. some still end up with a little bit of degree but what they spent on lasik they save on their glasses. no need to spend 200-400 bucks for their lenses each time so after a few glasses replacements the money come back liao.

     

    but if you're planning to do lasik just go for a check up first to see if you're suitable(knew at least a couple of people who ignored their docs when told  they can't go for it yet go private clinic to try). hear what the doc has to say then take sometime to consider. after operation also need to do follow up also.

    Have you considered Lasek?

    It's always good to do a bit of research and compare it for yourself.

    I think Lasek is better, cos it removes and burns off the surface of your cornea using laser. Then it allowed your cornea to grow back as new...eliminating the risks of getting a possible infection.

    But you will need to take a longer time to recover...like about 7 days for your new cornea to grow back again.

    Lasik will probably take a shorter time to recover, but I'll go for Lasek if I'm in your position.

    Hello, I would like to share my experience also. I just did my lasik not long ago, sometime before CNY. I did it at the lasik surgery clinic at Paragon and it was a LOUSY experience. I sincerely suggest you go for other places, check around and choose one that you feel most comfortable. You probably only got to do this once in your life, so choose wisely, and you only have a pair of eyes. I understand Lasik is more of a commondity right now as you can see it everywhere (at least in Singapore), but that doesn't mean the clinic can be so "unprofessional". They have a few options available, each comes with different prices, while you were seeking their advise which one suits you better, they said "IT DOESN'T MATTER"! I felt what they really care is about pushing you to do the surgery, like doing a hard sell on insurance product. Who cares about your eyes.... what the he**- how does it not matter? It is my eyes, not food nor clothing...
    Somemore, I had a hard time scheduling my 2nd and 3rd review, they told me the doctor is busy that i would have to wait until June (supposedly the 3rd review should be in April). I was quite upset about it, they simply don't care about you once you are done with the surgery. Making $ is their utmost priority.

    Anyways, I really don't like the way they treat the patients. Highly suggest you not to go there!
