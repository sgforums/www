    Four Singaporeans among those hurt in Jakarta blast
    SINGAPORE -- Four Singaporeans were believed to be among those wounded when a bomb exploded outside the Marriott Hotel in downtown Jakarta, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs said.

    'The Singapore Embassy in Jakarta is monitoring the situation closely,' a statement from the ministry said. 'We have received a report that four Singaporeans who were at the Marriott Hotel may be injured.'

    The explosion which killed at least 10 people, including at least one foreigner, and wounded 103 others was caused by a car bomb, Indonesian police said.

    Indonesian Defence Minister Matori Abdul Jalil condemned the blast as a 'terrorist attack', but said it was too early to point the finger at Jemaah Islamiah.

    No group immediately claimed responsibility for the bombing. But since last year's bombings in Bali, which killed 202 people, authorities have warned that more attacks were likely in Indonesia -- possibly by Jemaah Islamiah.

    A reporter from Jakarta's El Shinta radio station said: 'I saw four badly burned bodies on the street,' adding that two cars were on fire in the hotel's vicinity.

    Ms Mellanie Solagratia, a spokesman for the hotel, said most of the damage appeared to have occurred in the basement and on the second floor. She said occupancy in the 333-room hotel as of Monday was 77 per cent.

    Black smoke could be seen rising from the front of the hotel, located in a district where many foreign embassies are housed, an Associated Press reporter on the scene said.

    The adjacent Rajawli building houses the embassies of Norway, Sweden, Finland and Denmark. None of the staff were injured, officials said. -- AP

    AUSTRALIANS URGED TO AVOID JAKARTA

    CANBERRA -- Australia warned its citizens to steer clear of central Jakarta after the explosion at the Marriott hotel on Tuesday.

    Australia already had travel warnings in place for Indonesia following last year's Bali bombings that killed 202, including 88 Australians.

    A spokesman for Foreign Minister Alexander Downer said the government was seeking more details about the blast outside the Marriott Hotel.

    'In the meantime we are warning Australians to stay away from central Jakarta,' he said, on condition of anonymity. -- AP
