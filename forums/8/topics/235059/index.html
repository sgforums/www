Contradictory forces behind entrepreneurship

  • Cystaire
    1 Feb 07, 16:12
    Here's a curious topic to chit-chat about.

    People start companies for a variety of reasons. Whatever those are, a business must at least make enough money to sustain itself.

    That is the bottom-line.

    So it is expected that entrepreneurs should like money. In fact that should be a strong motivating factor. They must be quite ambitious, driven, visionary and greedy.

    But if they are too greedy, they may destroy the start-up long before it reaches sustainability. Even powerful businesses often start slow and lose money for years before they become profitable.

    So entrepreneurs should also be patient, and not be affected by money-losses too much.

    How do they balance these powerful, contradictory forces?
