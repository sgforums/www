They may be rich (some only act rich), but many are ngiow and kiam-siap until words also cannot describe. For many of them, if they see a 10-cent coin in the middle of the highway, they would probably run across and try to pick it up.

    I know of this tai-tai who drives a Jaguar, but goes to this certain cafe in town at 8:45 pm to wait.....wait wait wait.....till 9:00 pm, when the cakes are cleared at half-price.

    For tai-tais, the best is to get something not overly expensive yet exclusive (ie. something different, not easily available to the public), or something which money cannot buy.....for example:

    - Give tickets to a show/musical (eg. PCK The Musical) and try to arrange private tea with the actors and maybe some autograph signing (at the interval or after the show). This is of course not easy, but not impossible either.

    - Tickets to best seats SSO concert (they cannot reject because they're supposed to be high-class and claim to listen to classical music, etc....although they know nothing more than I do). Once again, try to organise a private tea with the orchestra members, etc.....and watch the tai-tais try to pretend to talk about classical music.

    - Lunch for her and her family served in a suite room of a downtown hotel. You can use the same hotel suite for tea and dinner for other tai-tais who have won prizes. I'm sure this can be easily arranged with the hotel. If you do this, please let me use the suite at night.
