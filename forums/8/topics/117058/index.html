    China, Japan row over lighthouse

    JAPAN said yesterday it had placed under state control and protection a lighthouse built by nationalists on an island disputed by China and Taiwan, touching off the latest row with Beijing.

    The 5.6-metre lighthouse was erected in 1988 by Japanese right-wing activists to mark a claim on Uotsuri-jima, the largest of the Senkaku Islands, known as Diaoyu in China, which lie between Taiwan and Japan.

    'Those who built the lighthouse said they no longer can run it,' said Vice-Foreign Minister Shuzen Tanigawa. 'So the state has taken it over.'

    China immediately complained about the Japanese action over the oil-rich but uninhabited island. It's Chinese territory, a Chinese foreign ministry spokesman claimed.
