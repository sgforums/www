    MY HUSBAND and I had an unpleasant experience more than a week ago at a Geylang durian stall.
    On April 21, my husband with a group of friends decided to have some durian after dinner in Geylang.

    We headed for this big durian stall opposite Geylang Lorong 11 which had tables and chairs for customers to eat the fruit at the premises.

    We got ourselves a table and my husband went to choose the durians. Once he stepped inside the stall, he was approached by a durian seller who, without asking, started to open a durian and invited my husband to try the fruit.

    My husband asked him how much the durian cost but his reply was: 'Never mind, just try first'. So my husband tried and commented that the durian was 'not bad'.

    With this comment, the durian seller swiftly picked up five durians, put them into a basket, weighed them and brought them to our tables. My husband kept asking about the price but none was given.

    At our table, he opened up another durian and asked me and another girlfriend to try it out and so we did. Again he asked whether the durian was nice and we replied that it was not bad.

    He immediately started opening up another durian and said: 'See? Confirm good one, this one'.

    We asked him again about the price and then came the howler. He told us the durians cost $20 a kilogram. We asked him how heavy the basket of durians was and the reply - 10kg.

    We rejected the durians but the durian seller insisted that he could not take them back as the sale of this basket of five durians had already been 'keyed in' (registered).

    After threatening to leave immediately, he relented and said he would return the unopened durians, but the rest (three which had already been opened) would have to be paid for.

    We ended up with three durians which cost us $140.

    Tey Siew Yee (Ms) Doris
