    Last year, on Novemeber, sources in HongKong has confirmed that the pirates have managed to break the technology code of GD-ROMs and they are able to pirate DC games in the near future. Now its confirmed....

    DREAMCAST PIRATED CDS HAVE ARRIVED. (aka backup copies)

    Early last month, the hacker group 'Utopia' and 'Kalisto' have managed to break the security by SEGA to prevent piracy. One need not modify his Dreamcast to play the backup copies. Only a boot disc (currently ver 1.1) is required. Also, this bootdisc doubles up as an alternative for gamers to play import games regardless of its terriority. As of today, several shops around Singapore have been spotted selling these backup copies. The price of each disc ranges from $10 - $20.

    Perhaps you are wondering....some Dreamcast games requires more space then a normal CD can handle as Dreamcast games makes use of a special format which they call it GD-Rom. A GD-ROM is a gigabyte worth of space whilst a normal CD-ROM just contains 750megs of space. So how can the games fit into a CD? Maybe movies or some sound effects are ommitted in the backup copies?

    Well, reports have said that Sony is going to release a new format of CD-ROM soon. (perhaps its already released) This CD-ROM boasts a space of 1.3GB. If this is true, you will soon have a complete backup of the orginal game.

    How is this going to affect the Dreamcast industy in Singapore?

    The main distributor in SIngapore, Seow Choon, has said that orginal game sales would definitely be hit by this and perhaps in the near future, orginal games will be low in quantity and thus the price could be higher. Furthermore, you may even forget about a server for Dreamcast online games here.

    I strongly urge all Dreamcast gamers in Singapore not to support the piracy scene for Dreamcast. I know this is impossible, but at least buy the orginals for those great games (eg: Soul Caliber, RE:CV).

    And erm, please do more raids on Sim Lim Sq and other shops selling pirated CDs. The pirate CDs are causing great harm to the gaming industy.
