    Hahahaha, Colette, I am sure u are braver than that. Have you ever had any experiences with the supernatural??? What are they supposed to look like???

    Naive, sure. The 7th month is simply a month of the lunar calender whereby it is believed that all hell breaks lose. Commonly known as the Month of the Hungry Ghosts, the gate of hell opens up on the first day of the 7th lunar month and the spirits are allowed a month of feasting and revelry in the world of the living. To ensure that the ghosts enjoy a pleasant "vacation", lavish feasts are set out, paper hell money are burnt for their use, chinese wayang shows are performed for the dead. The climax of the Hungry Ghost month is the 15th, which is also known as the Chung Yuan Jie, when great sacrificial feasts are set in the temple and priests pray for the dead. Whether or not one believe in the existence of the supernatural, you will never fail to feel the festivities, especially in the Chinatown area. It is supposed to be bigger where you are now.

    Anyway, there was this one foggy night whereby I walked back home all alone along this quite street. With the burning hellpaper money flying all around, it really makes the ambience appropriate for a scary setting.
