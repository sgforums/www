    There was this girl at the Bugis area who approached me, a few months back. It was at the car park, not bus-stop. She was Chinese, in her early 20's, quite shabbily dressed. I can't remember if she had facial pigmentation.

    She claimed that she was from JB, here on work permit....with a mother and bother living with her...and that she's lost her job. I knew she was lying because she spoke with a typical Singaporean Mandarin accent....and work permit holders aren't allowed to bring their families here. Besides, once you lose your job, you have to leave the country.

    Anyway, she asked for S$8 to buy dinner for her family. out of pity (and since I took up so much of her time listening)....I offer her some coins from my pocket, which she took. I told her that I wasn't willing to give anymore than that. Then she said "wah, so little, how to buy dinner?". I snapped back "not enough then give me back lor". She quickly said "thank you" and walked away.

    Throughout the whole incident, she was very friendly and polite. Or maybe that was in her beginning stages of this 'job' when she was still a little frightened.
