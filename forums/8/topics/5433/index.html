    "Intellectual" sacarstic? I hope not heh. Anywya....

    LDR=Long Distance Relationship? If so then propbly it's demanding if you zealously want to document his/her social life overseas. But if you just want to know who her friends are, then alright. But I guess that's just a broad way of defining everything.

    HOW much you want to know is another thing. If you want to know all her friends overnight and as much about that as possible, that'd irritate the hell out of me.

    Basically, the only thing holding you 2 together in a LDR is love and trust. Actually more of the latter. The physical aspect of a r/s isn't around for most of the year, and while it can be the strongest bond, it can be the weak link in a chain, coz it's always damm easy to get physically attracted to someone else.

    However, if you trust your gf, then that shouldn't be a problem. If you do, let her live her life overseas, and you live yours here. Ask her how she is, what was her week like and stuff,but demanding details would be too excessive.

    Doubting your gf/bf would be the worst in a LDR.

    On a brighter side,my classmate has been with her bf who's in the US for like almost 3 years. He left when they just got together and in her words "she hasn't physically been together with him in the same country for more than a month at a stretch". Pretty taxing Iwould imagine, but they're still going. Ofcourse,email and ICQ and Netmeeting has helped a lot.

    They also have a rather unique agreement allowing each party to see or date other ppl. I'm not sure if every r/s can take that but well when you're young, you'll always like to keep your options open. While neither side has actually seriously used that agreement,the fact that such an agreement can actually exist examplifies the importance of trust in a r/s, what strength it brings to a r/s.
