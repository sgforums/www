    hmm a review from someone who compared 7250 and 7250i..

    General Comments:
    I have had both Nokia 7250 and Nokia 7250i mobile phones. The reviews for the 7250 can be seen here already, so I will concentrate on the differences between the 2.
    1. Night mode camera. This is a MUST if you take quite a lot of pictures, as the quality on standard photos taken by this phone is very poor when lighting begins to fade slightly. This mode rectifies that problem, and the picture quality has been greatly improved because of it.
    2. Once a picture is in the gallery, there is a zoom option, and you can scroll around the picture with the arrow keys. There is only 1 level of zoom however, but this does show the finer details of the picture on screen.
    3. "View in sequence" allows you to view each picture in turn without pressing any more keys.
    4. A picture's contrast can be altered.
    5. A picture editor is supplied allowing you to insert text, other frames and clip-art.
    6. XHTML browser (apparently). Frankly browsing appears to be no different as on the 7250.
    7. Visual caller ID. Pictures on the phone can now be assigned to contacts so it appears when they call you.
    8. LESS MEMORY (3.6MB free on first startup) I think the 7250 had around 4.3MB.
    9. The clock screen saver now also shows icons in the banner, such as message recieved, silent mode, keypad lock etc.
    10. Grid menu option. Instead of the usual Nokia 1-option per screen, icons can also be shown in a 3x3 grid allowing faster navigation.

    That's about all i have noticed up to now.

    WARNING. If you use the Nokia PC Suite for downloading games/pictures etc. YOU WILL HAVE TO UPGRADE TO VERSION 5.16 as the 7250i is not recognised correctly by previous versions (at least in the version I had previously, 5.1)

    As for what I think of the phone, I think it's excellent. It looks great, and overall use is quick and natural (as is the straight 7250). The battery lasts very well, and signal strength always seem to be on or very near to the maximum. (the 7250 has 7 bars for signal and battery as opposed to most other Nokia's 4). It still does not have call voice activation, but personally I do not see that as a problem. If you're thinking about getting a 7250, I would seriously try to hold out for a 7250i, as the extra features are worth having.
