    Fateha's Zulfikar seeks refuge in Australia

    By S. Ramesh

    Zulfikar Mohamed Shariff, the controversial editor of Fateha.com, has left Singapore for Australia.

    He was being investigated by the police for criminal defamation against Senior Minister Lee Kuan Yew, Muslim Affairs Minister Yaacob Ibrahim, and Temasek executive director Ho Ching.

    But he said he planned to continue his political activities from Melbourne.

    Zulfikar is no stranger to controversy - he's the prime-mover behind the tudung debate.

    He was also fined for trespass at Tanglin Police Station on Labour Day.

    And ealier this month, the police seized computer equipment from his office and home.

    This was because of three articles on Fateha.com, criticising Muslim Affairs Minister Yaacob, the appointment of Ho Ching as the Executive Director of Temasek Holdings and the tudung issue.

    Police are investigating Zulfikar for criminal defamation, but he has decided not to wait for the outcome.

    Zulfikar said: "I have to leave because I do not want to be just another victim of the PAP politics.

    "After discussing with family and friends, their response is - if I go to jail for two years, the cause we've been trying to save will die because the platform, fateha.com - for two years I would not be able to edit that. So according to them, it's better to be out of Singapore and yet be able to raise issues through Fateha."

    Zulfikar says as long as he's got access to the Internet, he can remain editor of the controversial website and lobby his cause.

    His wife and four children have also left Singapore for Malaysia. They plan to join Zulfikar in Melbourne sometime next week. DuH
