    Frankly speaking, I do see a change in attitudes nowadays, and a change in the ways we see things. You can call me old fashioned or whatever, but I do believe there are compelling reasons not to engage in sex before marriage.

    Sex is not just some physical act to get done and over with. Especially, as someone has mentioned, you do it with somebody you love. There is a bond and attachment within that act that implies love and trust.

    Now, within marriage, this love and trust thing has been declared with something more tangible than mere words (albeit the tangible thing is a piece of paper and the law). Without marriage, the words 'I love you' do not stand out as strongly.

    Granted you are very much in love - what then if there is a breakup? We cannot even say there will be no divorce, what more a breakup - without a paper, and without the law backing up? I believe the strong attachment in sex will hamper your future relationships. Ask anyone who had premarital sex - like it or not, what happened in the past will haunt.

    It is our own decision to make, whether to have sex with that person we love before marriage. But ultimately, we must live with the decision we make. And I say it is best to make a wiser decision.
