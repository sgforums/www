    I did my schooling in India right upto A levels.

    Yes, you are right, almost every principle/theory/concept is taught bottoms-up. The education philosophy is highly different from the one in Singapore. I can sum up the education philosophy of Singapore as "Jack of all trades and master of none"!!. Here we seem to get taught many concepts/methodologies, (some very latest ones too), but nothing gets taught in depth. I have studied polytechnic as well as degree here and its the same story. Teachers and lectures seem to be more interested in imparting application skills rather than conceptual foundation. Meaning, you might be able to use a particular concept/theory/methodology to design something without even understanding fully what that concept/theory or methodology means. The result is .......... you lack strong foundation!!!You might get to know how to "do" a lot of things but you seldom will be a master of any one of them.

    However the Singapore system has advantages too, just as there are disadvantages to the system in India. Here in Singapore you will get to work on /study some of the very latest developments in the industry, the technological exposure is up to date. However in india you might not be exposed to the latest developments, you might be "stuck" with things that might be obsolete in the real world. Also the infrastructure in India does not come anywhere near to the ones in Singapore. The instruments, Labs, workstations, sports facilities (and almost everything else) is antiquated. There wont be any glamor or glitz associated with Indian universities.

    And living in India...............thats a different ball game all together!!!

    So if you are looking for rigorous, hard core, conceptual education, India might be the place for you. There are umpteen lecturers at NUS and NTU who are from India. I am sure you can get in touch with any one of them for more info.
    Btw, dont worry about cost of education or of living...its dirt cheap compared to our "first world" Singapore.
