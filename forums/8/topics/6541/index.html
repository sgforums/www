    OK everyone...I have not posted since God knows when..I think nearly 3 months ago already...but something I read in the papers had sparked this off...so here goes !

    In Sunday Plus, Sumiko Yan (the ever-enterprising Japanese Lady) has written an article about "women wasting their eggs". A sort of auto-biographical and cynical way to comment on Singapore's women I guess.

    The question is: What role does marriage have to play in many girls' lives ? Do they actually wish to marry and have kids soon, or would rather adopt the attitude mentioned in the article (which is career first, or maybe no maternal instincts etc...)

    My friends in University always complain their bf are so career-minded, and never seem to give a dam* about settling down, so don't even THINK about children.. But I feel that eventually when they start working, they might be saying the same thing too.

    As for guys, please comment on your idea of marriage and having kids...seems one reason why Sumiko is undable to get hitched and married is becos she meets "men who have a phobia of marriage".....is this JUSTIFIED ?

    Personally, let's just say that I am the kind of guy who looks for true love with a woman, and to settle down with her and start a family as soon as possible. But the dream always remains a dream for me because I am not even attached yet. Still, I see couples all over the place "wasting" their chances to enjoy a good, happy family life because they have postponed it for work-related reasons.

    So how do u think I feel about this ? It's the classic case of "I wish I could be in his shoes"...because some men (and women) don't appreciate being attached and don't feel any commitment to getting married or having children.

    It may sound sad everyone....but to me, those who truly believe in love and marriage and having kids seldom find their right partners; whereas those "bastard" guys who neglect their gf and are too "career-minded" get the girl's undying love and afection.

    It's happened to me in real life and I've seen it, so don't say it's not true. It's just a sore spot for any unattached person, whether girl or guy.

    I know a friend (girl) who is worried to be a spinster for the rest of her life....yet she is a nice girl, passionate and not bad looking. Who can explain why she was never approached by men ?

    Please feel free to air your comments....I would really like some frank opinions on this.

    Hopefully Singapore is not as "condemned" as I assumed it always was...when it comes to settling down and having children at least..

    Cheers all !
