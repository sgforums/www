    4 May 04, 12:15
    Soft lenses are much more comfortable to wear than RGP lenses.
    Normally, a person will get used to wearing soft lenses within 3 days. It is easier to wear, but the only problem is that it builds up deposits easily. And if not clean properly, eye infections may arise from there. Thus, daily disposables are quite popular nowadays.

    RGP lenses are much uncomfortable to wear than soft lenses.
    It takes about 1 week to 2 weeks to adapt to RGP lenses. Normally, on the 1st day, you can only wear about 2 hours, the purpose of that is to let the eye get used to the feeling of the lenses as it is quite hard as compared to soft lenses. The next day, increased the wearing time to 4 hrs and so on, until a maxi of 14 hrs a day.
    It is easier to care for RGP lenses, coz of the material that is used to make it. You can rinse the RGP lenses with tap water in Singapore! (Even though Saline is a better choice)
    Risk of infection in rare in RGP lenses. RGP lenses risk the chances of dislodging.

    Orthokeratology lenses are not RGP lenses. It uses the same concept but, you can't put on the RGP lenses for night use (wearing to sleep).
    OK lenses have 3 to 4 tiny holes in it, the purpose of it, is to allow the cornea to take in oxygen, so that the next day, the cornea won't swell up so much.
    The theory behind the OK lenses is basically to flatten the cornea during the sleeping time. However, you have to wear the OK lenses for a period of time to flatten the cornea before you can cease wear in the morning. Thus, its more of a cosmetic reason to wear OK lenses and not to control myopia.
