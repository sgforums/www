  • wuming78
    17 Apr 05, 20:36
    wah.. not bad. so many of you responded asking about volunteer work.

    for me, my interest is in working with youths. so i am currently volunteering with mcys (probation services branch), the boys' home (though i have stopped for awhile cos of work), gracehaven (providing tuition weekly) and student care service. as you can see, my "targets" are youths-at-risk, and youths who have committed some form of crime.

    other than these, there are alot of other areas which one can volunteer in, with various levels of commitments required, e.g. working with children, with children having terminal illnesses, with old folks, providing tuition, etc.... basically, if one has an interest in volunteering, there is really no fear of not being able to find an area to volunteer in! and one does not need to worry that volunteering means taking up large portions of your time - although this is welcome, one can certainly choose volunteer opportunities that either require less physical commitment, or are adhoc.

    i think the nvpc website is a good place to start if you are interested.

    and do remember that volunteering also benefits oneself too - it widens one's social circle, enables one to pick up certain life and people skills, and of course, gives one a sense of satisfaction.
