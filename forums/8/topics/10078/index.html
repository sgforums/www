Biggest swoop on pasar malams

  • no heart
    7 Nov 00, 16:29
    Police seized about 100000 pirated CD-ROMs and music CDs, as well as arrested 47 people in the single largest swoop on pasar malams here.

    The 30-minute crackdown on Sunday evening targeted two syndicates active in housing estates at Sembawang, Choa Chu Kang and Hillview Way and followed a surveillance that began last month.

    Fifty officers from the Criminal Investigation Department (CID) simultaneously hit 15 retail stalls at three pasar malams at around 6.30 pm.

    The stalls were located at an open field along Sembawang Avenue beside Sun Plaza, near Block 529, Choa Chu Kang Street 51, and near Block 2, Hillview Way.

    The officers seized about 80,000 pirated CD-ROMs and CDs and found another 20,000 pieces in four vans parked at Choa Chu Kang and Hillview.

    Their estimated market value was $1.2 million, as each disc cost an average of $12, up from last year's low of $5, due to increased police enforcement.

    Those arrested included two syndicate supervisors and four drivers.

    Five of them were women, aged between 17 and 34, and the rest men, aged between 16 and 53.

    About two-thirds of the seized products were music CDs. The rest were CD-ROMs, including computer software, educational software and games.

    This is part of police efforts to drive home a stern message to the Intellectual Property Rights (IPR) pirates that we will not tolerate any IPR infringements.

    This is the third raid on pasar malams and the sixth on pirated goods this year, netting a total of $10 million worth of pirated goods.

    Sellers of pirated CD-ROMs and music CDs will face charges of selling or distributing articles that infringe copyrights.

    They can be fined up to $100,000 and/or jailed for up to five years.
