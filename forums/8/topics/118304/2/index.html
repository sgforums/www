    what kind of animation are you looking to do?

    3d? 2d? traditional?
    game?
    film?
    cartoon?

    for games and 3d. NYP.

    for 2d and traditional, NAFA would be a better choice, because of the anatomy and life drawing classes in the 1st year. their 3d is quite good too, the lecturer (yes only 1) is very skilled and talented, though sometimes short tempered.

    TP is more towards design like graphics and advertising. SP and NP, don't really hear much about their students or works. for some reason, laselle students (and the school itself) has a reputation for being either stuck up, style without substance, or really really good, depending who you hear it from.

    alot of people tend to miss NAFA, because the campus looks so old, and simply because its name says it's a fine arts academy.

    either way, you won't go wrong picking either NYP or NAFA. but remember that the school only teaches you the very basics, you'll have to work very very hard on your own if you want to achieve any good results.

    many people tend to be disappointed when they realize the school 'only teaches them so much'. but how much can actually be taught in just 2 years?
