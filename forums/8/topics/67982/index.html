    Mauritania girls forced to be fat
    NOUAKCHOTT (Mauritania) - To be fat is to be revered.

    At least in Mauritania, where many of its adult women were sent to 'fat farms' when they were children, not to lose weight, but to gain it.

    Advertisement

    Indeed, the white Moor Arabs in this African country were once so obsessed with obesity that over a third of its girls were force fed to a 'life-threatening' degree, the BBC reported.

    'I make them eat and eat and eat. And then drink lots and lots of water,' said Ms Fatematou, an enormous woman who runs such a farm.

    She said the girls grow to between 60 and 100kg, 'with lots of layers of fat'.

    Fat was seen as a sign of wealth - if a girl was thin she was considered poor, and would not be respected.

    But the practice is dying out. An official study has found that now, only 11 per cent of young girls are force fed.

    But Ms Fatematou said that she was soon expecting some seven-year-old girls for the treatment. If they refuse, she said: 'We grab them and we force them to eat. Of course they cry.'

    But she added: 'Once they are fat and beautiful, they can serve their men well. Once they are fat, they can be married.'
