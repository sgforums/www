  • SingaporeTyrannosaur
    6 Feb 03, 23:10
    I noticed all these after viewing the movie a few times... yes, I had ang pow money and time to burn...

    Notice the imperial guards in the first part? They were killed by the boxers right? Notice later they come back in the movie as regular boxers, the dudes at the bridge, the dock, the wax musuem and finally on the barge!

    Notice the rig Chon was fighting over to save Roy from being dunked into the river? The rope that is connected to make the entire jog work is slack and does not move when the thing is rotated, yet the entire jig works.

    The Queen's 50th Jubilee and the Jack the Ripper Whitechapel murders are out of sync. The Queen's Golden 50th Jubilee takes place in 1887, the Whitechapel Ripper muders in 1888. But there's an easter egg in Jack the Ripper's apperance in the flim. Apparently the producers assumed he was Montague John Druitt, one of the few people suspected to be the serial killer. Why? The sudden stop in the savage murders coincided with finding his body in the Thames river. What did Lin do to the Jack the Ripper? Yes, she kicked him off a bridge into a river.

    In the wax musuem scene showing the Royal family, if you look closely in the background, you will notice a wax figure of Lord Rathbone standing beside Queen Victoria! It appears to be him with his classic gelled up hair! They do show a few quick shots of him. An easter egg? You decide.

    There are many historial refrences in the movie, some of them are apparent to us ("the 'automobile' idea, yeah like that'll work"), while some others make refrences to future american culture, if you actually bother to check them out.

    You guys spot any more?
