    GPRS is the lying transmission protocol. GPRS is method to connect to the service provider. Another way is to connect using CSD [Circuit Switch]. If you connect using Circuit Switch, you will be charged according how long you are connected. Just like how long you are making a call. GPRS charges according how much data is sent/received, so you can be connected all day doing nothing without being charged. Ideal for Instant Messaging kind of application [icq, yahoo, msn] in which you want to connect all the time but only send/receive msg as and when.

    WAP is a higher level procotol that define how you access and view information from the www. WAP browser is like web browser. You can use your web browser to surf the net via underlying dial up connection, broadband, etc, the effect is the same but the experience may be different due to the speed.

    SingTel mobile settings allow you to do WAP over GPRS [charged by ho w much data transmitted] and WAP over CSD [charged by how long you are connected]. Of course you should use WAP over GPRS. To enable your GPRS and thus WAP settings, you have to refer to your telco. Is quite easy.

    If you are sending an english sms, each character you type is roughly 1 byte, so if you type 166 characters [not words], then roughly 166 bytes of data will be sent. 1kbyte is roughly 1000 bytes. Thus 1kbyte [0.52 cents]is roughly equivalent to 6 english sms.
