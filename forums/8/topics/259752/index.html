    Eh paiseh I retract my previous answer after some checking up on the properties of chords in a circle.

    Basically, we make use of the property that the product of the segments of 2 intersecting chords are equal.

    See http://www.algebralab.org/Word/Word.aspx?file=Geometry_CirclesAnglesChords.xml question (i) for a worked example.

    So for this question, if we draw another blue line to one of the corner of the square, the length of the blue line will be 24cm. See my new image.



    Therefore using the above-mentioned property, (1) x (2) = (3) x (4).

    Let the diagonal of the sqaure be 2y, 24 x 24 = 16 x (16+2y).

    Manipulating, 16 + 2y = 24 x 24 / 16 = 36

    y = 10 cm

    Since the radius of the circle = red line + y, radius = 16 +10 = 26cm.
