    For those of you who're confused about the latest processors on the market, check this out. This is taken from a local website.

    Processor: Intel Pentium-M aka Centrinos Designed from the ground up, this latest processor from Intel offers great performance specifically to meet the demands of mobile computing. Stamina of battery life and performance per clock cycle has been brought to a higher level with the release of this processor

    Found in: Standard version of this processor powers notebooks from mid-range to desktop replacement notebooks
    Ultra-low voltage version powers some of the ultra-portable notebooks and Tablet-PCs on the market today

    Processor: Intel Pentium 4 Ported over from the desktop segment, this processor offers great performance with Hyper-Threading technology. Due to its architecture, the processor gets very hot after a while in operation and requires elaborate cooling and not battery-life friendly

    Found in: Being a battery sapping silicon and coupled with the need for elaborate cooling, this processor is normally used in desktop replacement notebooks and has very fast computing speeds

    Processor: Intel Pentium 4-M Basically just a modified version of Pentium 4 with the addition of IntelÂ�fs Speedstep technology to offer better battery life for notebooks

    Found in: This processor can be found powering desktop replacement notebooks and some ultra-portable notebooks. Battery life is average but slightly higher then the non-M Pentium 4s

    Processor: Intel Pentium 3-M 3rd generation of Pentium processors and slapped with enhanced Intel SpeedStep technology

    Found in: Can be found in ultra-portable notebooks and Tablet-PCs due to its Micro FCPGA2 and FCBGA2 Packaging. Low battery consumption provides for longer battery life but provides modest computing power. Notebooks based on this processor are a rarity these days

    Processor: Mobile Intel Celeron Just like the desktop version of Celerons, this processor is essentially a stripped down version of the Pentium 4, as a result, price and performance of this processor are just a fraction of the Pentium 4s

    Found in: Powering budget to near mid-range notebooks, performance and battery life provided by this processor is adequate and acceptable

    Processor: VIA processors These processors consumes tiny amount of voltages and as a result heat emission is very low. Fan-less cooling can be achieved with this processor. However, performance level of this cool running processor isnÂ�ft something to boast about

    Found in: Powering ultra-portable notebooks and machines that needs to be quiet during operation. Very good battery life with reasonable performance

    Processor: Transmeta Crusoe Not a true x86 architecture processor. This extremely low power consuming processor operates while emitting very little heat allowing for definite fan-less cooling for extremely silent mobile devices

    Found in: Powering a couple of ultra-portable notebooks, devices based on this processor have very long battery life and can be built to extremely small dimensions. However, performance of this processor is sluggish
