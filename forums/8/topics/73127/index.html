    THEY were alerted by the sound of barking. When they looked up to see what the commotion was, they said they saw something shocking: A woman letting go of a dog over the edge of the corridor.

    The dog fell six or seven storeys to its death.

    One eye-witness, a 17-year-old student who declined to be named, said she was under Block 473, Ang Mo Kio Avenue 10, chatting with friends at about 3pm yesterday.

    'My friends and I heard two dogs barking. People started gathering opposite (under a covered walkway) and looking up. We suspected that something was happening and joined the crowd. When we went there, we saw the woman release the dog.

    'My friend shouted at her, but she just touched the plants outside the flat and disappeared.'

    They were standing just opposite where the dog landed, near the middle of the block.

    Another witness, Mr Martin Chan, who was walking his own dog, was stunned when the dog landed nearby.

    He called the police immediately.

    Mr Chan, 23, a financial adviser, said: 'I heard another dog barking upstairs. Some people were looking up. The next thing I knew, a dog landed about three metres away from me.

    'It was quite scary, especially because I'm a dog owner myself. I was shocked that someone would do this.

    'I think the dog died instantly. It didn't move or anything.'

    WHAT MIGHT HAVE HAPPENED

    When The New Paper arrived on the scene at 3.30pm, the police confirmed that a dog carcass was found in the void deck, but did not comment on the cause of death.

    The New Paper questioned residents of Block 473, to try to piece together what might have happened.

    One resident living on the seventh storey, who asked not to be named, said there were no dogs on his level.

    He said that the dog, a 3- or 4-year-old shitzu, lived just below him on the sixth storey.

    'I always hear this dog barking and jumping against the corridor,' he said.

    Nobody responded to our attempts to contact the person living below his flat.

    But the neighbours confirmed that a woman lived there, and she owned a dog.

    But they added that they rarely saw either of them.

    Most of the other residents learnt of the incident only when the police arrived.

    Mr Madhavan Kannan, head of the Agri-Food and Veterinary Authority (AVA) Centre for Animal Welfare and Control, said: 'If the dog was thrown down from the apartment, (the perpetrator) can be charged for cruelty to animals.

    'The penalty for this is a $10,000 fine, one year's jail or both.'

    DID YOU SEE IT HAPPEN?

    THE dog appeared to be someone's pet.

    It was certainly well cared for.

    Ms Deidre Moss, a spokesman for the Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals, said: 'Apart from the bleeding, the dog was in generally good condition. It was well taken care of, and must have been somebody's pet.'

    She added: 'On veterinary examination, there didn't appear to be any fracture. The dog was bleeding from the mouth and had a broken left lower jaw.

    'It is assumed that the dog died from head injuries. This may be because it fell from a height.

    'We do not know the cause of death, but the dog died under questionable circumstances.'

    Ms Moss does not think the dog jumped to its death.

    'Because of the size of the breed, it is highly unlikely that the dog could have found its way over the balcony.'

    She appealed to witnesses to contact the police.
