    2 June 2005

    SMRT WITHHOLDS INCREASE IN TRAIN AND BUS CONCESSION FARES
    FOR SENIOR CITIZEN EZ-LINK CARDS

    1. The Public Transport Council (PTC) approved on Monday, 30 May 2005, the adjustment of MRT, LRT and bus fares with effect from 1 July 2005.
    2. SMRT Corporation Ltd (SMRT) has reviewed the concession fare structure for senior citizens, and will withhold, for one year, the 1-cent increase for Senior Citizens ez-link concession fares as approved by PTC. SMRT will maintain the train concession fare for senior citizens ez-link cards at $0.64 and bus concession fare at $0.63.
    3. In addition, SMRT will also extend the concession travel period for senior citizens after reviewing the peak hour travel patterns and in keeping with the five-day work week. In line with our principle of encouraging senior citizens to travel during off-peak hours, concession fares will now apply throughout Saturdays, Sundays and Public Holidays. And on weekdays, it will start at 9.00 am instead of 10.00 am, and will extend to 4.30 pm instead of 4.00 pm. This will save senior citizens an average of $0.58 per trip when they travel on SMRT trains and $0.14 per trip for SMRT buses during the new concession hours (9.00 am to 4.30 pm and after 7.00 pm from Mondays to Fridays, and on Saturdays, Sundays and Public Holidays). This would mean SMRT's train subsidy per trip is four times that of buses, which is significant given SMRT's large base of train commuters.
    4. SMRT's decision to not increase the Senior Citizen ez-link concession fares and to extend concession periods will benefit some 90,000 senior citizens trips made on SMRT trains and buses each day.
    5. The revised fares (with effect from 1 July 2005) for SMRT trains, SMRT buses and Bukit Panjang LRT (BPLRT) are:
    MRT Fares

    There is no change in off-peak concession fares for Senior Citizen ez-link Card.

    There is no change to existing fares for passengers using the Child/Student ez-link Card and Monthly Concession Passes.

    Across the board, there will be a 1 to 3 cents increase for Adult ez-link Card
    Across the board, there will be a 10-cent increase for Single Trip Ticket.
    The existing and revised MRT fares are shown in Annex A.

    BPLRT Fares

    There is no change in off-peak concession fares for Senior Citizen ez-link Card.
    There is no change to existing fares for passengers using the Child/Student ez-link Card and Monthly Concession Passes.
    Across the board, there will be a 1 to 2 cents increase for Adult ez-link Card.
    Across the board, there will be a 10-cent increase for Single Trip Ticket.
    The existing and revised LRT fares are in Annex B.

    Bus Fares

    There is no change in off-peak concession fares for Senior Citizen ez-link Card.
    There is no change to existing fares for passengers using the Child/Student ez-link Card and Monthly Concession Passes.
    Across the board, there will be 1 to 2 cents increase for Adult ez-link Card.
    Across the board, there will be a 10-cent increase for cash fares.
    The existing and revised bus fares are in Annex C.

    6. Conclusion

    SMRT will keep commuters informed of the new fares through various channels such as posters, leaflets and its website
