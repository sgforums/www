    Extreme sports race on Oct 24
    SINGAPORE'S first extreme sports race, The Action Asia Challenge, will be flagged off on Oct 24 at the Merlion and end at the Esplanade.

    The race will feature nail-biting activities like the flying fox, demonstrated yesterday at the Esplanade. There will also be abseiling and mountain-biking.

    The start and finish points were revealed yesterday by Action Asia Events, the company bringing the race here.

    One aspect of the race that sets it apart from others is that athletes cannot scout out the course beforehand - the 50km race route will be revealed only on the day.

    The 300 who have signed up to take part - in pairs - will be required to swim in the sea, abseil from bridges, mountain-bike and run along uneven terrain, among other things.

    Many of the activities in the race, which is being co-organised by the Singapore Tourism Board, will be held at Singapore landmarks and tourist attractions.

    Said Mr Michael Maddess, the race director: 'The overwhelming response is unprecedented since the event started in 1998. It broke the record for the fastest time to fill slots. And there's a waiting list of 40.'

    The event was previously held in Hong Kong, Macau, Vietnam, Taiwan, the Philippines and Malaysia.
