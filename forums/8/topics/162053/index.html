  • shade343

    FROM next year, St Michael's School may be renamed St Joseph's Institution (SJI) Junior (Essex Road Campus), if the Ministry of Education approves.

    The 51-year-old primary school and the 153-year-old SJI have long been in the same extended family, under the Lasallian religious order. But plans such as the name change are afoot to thicken the blood ties.

    SJI wants to tap a ready stream of talented younger pupils in the Josephian Talent scheme, which will be available to pupils selected to join SJI Junior. And it is not just about talent in academic subjects, but also in sports, the arts and more.

    With the changing education landscape and top schools pulling ahead, the move is an important one for SJI.
    .
    "SJI Junior will give us an added dimension because we can track the boys over the years. It won't just be exam-based admissions," Mr Noel Hon, chairman of the SJI Board of Governors, told Today.
    .
    "The PSLE will still be the main criterion but we'll also be able to understand the boys' different strengths."
    .
    SJI also aims to increase the number of boys from affiliated schools or children of alumni. De La Salle, St Anthony's Primary and St Stephen's are the other Lasallian primary schools.

    And while the successful practices of the new scheme will be shared with the trio, St Michael's is the chosen one due to its close history with SJI Â— it merged with the old SJI Junior.
