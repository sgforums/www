    For the more cynical perspective.

    Love is nothing but an arbitrary value notion placed on a relationship between two people. The idealized notion of romantic love is a medieval concept brainwashed into us by the relentless drivel the media, pioneered by the twin forces of Disney and Hallmark, which deludes us into thinking that the "right, perfect one" exists, only to be inevitably disappointed when the reality fails to live up to that sterling expectation(Ally McBeal does a pretty good job discussing this issue despite its other flaws as a show). Love is also tainted by the neurotic whims and fancies of social pretension such as our inhibitions against sexual activity outside the institution of marriage, as well as the psychological bitterness tha overwhelms an individual once the initial rush of courtship is over. Sure, some people manage to overcome these and stay together out of social stigmata, or because of fear of loneliness, or simply because they're lucky enough to achieve a level of physical and mental compatibility to build a lasting relationship on. But those are few, compared to the many miserable relationships that either fall apart and die, leaving both parties bitter and resigned, or, even worse, those who stick together grimly despite the lack of feeling because they can't imagine life alone.

    What is love? Simply a point of view, a rabid delusion, a figment of the imagination inspired by too many bad TCS serials and Valentine's Day cards. A notional feeling that can be purchased with the careful application of free meals, movies, flowers and other baubles.
