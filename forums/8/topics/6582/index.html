    the subject is plain and simple, laws that are either hardly justifiable, or sometimes downright unfair.
    in the Northern Territory, Australia, there is a law dubbed "mandatory sentencing", a bill specifically designed for criminals of Aboriginal descent. what this basically means is that any Aboriginal who commits an offence, no matter how small or how petty it might be, MUST serve a jail term. last month, an Aboriginal man was sentenced to 4 years prison after convicted of stealing a packet of biscuits. the police said the man had a previous record of misdemeanor. it is a known fact that "black" deaths in custody is very high in Australia, and the man has no hope of bail or parole. the UN has condemned the so-called bill and secretary general Kofi Annan expressed the UN's concern over the legality of the concept.
    but at the other end of the stick, in New South Wales, a man who commits first degree murder can appeal his sentence, and even have his conviction downgraded to manslaughter and he can expect a good behavior bond parole within 2 years.
    justice IS well and truly blind.
