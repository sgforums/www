    By Leong Chan Teik

    YOU could say Mrs Gretna Yen, nee Goh, has it all.

    She is a beauty queen, the reigning Classic Mrs Singapore World.

    She's been happily married for 28 years to Peter, 50, a regional director of an engineering firm.

    She has five children aged between 22 and 12. The three youngest are triplets, all girls.

    At work, Mrs Yen is a project director with Keppel Land, and oversees the construction of One Raffles Quay. The landmark office development in Marina South at the New Downtown consists of two towers: one 29 storeys high, the other 50 storeys high.

    But life hasn't always been as rosy, reveals Mrs Yen, 48, in an interview at her Bugis Junction office.

    Q Are you a saver or spender?

    A I'm more of a spender. I spend a lot on the home and my children. I'm a strong believer in keeping the house well-organised and beautiful, so every family member can feel the love and comfort of home.

    For example, in 2000, we made substantial renovations to our terrace house in Upper Serangoon, adding another storey to the existing two-and-a-half.We now have eight bedrooms, three store rooms, a large living-cum-dining room and a roof garden.

    Q What do you indulge in personally?

    A I have a weakness for rings with gemstones, and branded handbags. I consider them reward for my hard work.

    One afternoon, I walked into Chanel and Louis Vuitton outlets, and bought a complete set each of their handbags with matching briefcases and wallets. They cost well over $12,000 in all.
