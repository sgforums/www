    the amygdala is an almond-sized gland located in the so-called mid-brain of the cerebellum. it serves as the human body's main first-line defence against possible threats. information received from our senses will go through the thalamus (also located in the mid-brain) and some inputs get transmitted to the amygdala. the rest gets sent off to the other parts of the brain, primarily the prefrontal cortex which is our main processing centre. should the amygdala identify the sensory information received as a possible threat, it activates the 'fight or flight' response. our autonomic nervous system is thrown into 'stand-by', and our entire body becomes alert so as to prepare for an emergency response.

    the amygdala is located in the mid-brain, traditionally called the brain's emotion centre or the reptilian brain. obviously, it was added on when reptiles evolve from other earlier types of lifeform. it primarily serves as an emotional centre for the organism and is selected for by evolution as an asset to thrive in the pre-human earth. we humans have an additional layer called the grey matter or the cerebral cortex which serves as the locale for conscious thought.
