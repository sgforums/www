    Plagued by unsolicited SMS service

    I RECENTLY received an SMS from FunSMS saying that I had registered for a Web SMS service, which charges $1 per message.

    The content was as follows: 'Thank you for registering at webSMS. Now your friends can contact you via webSMS. Please reply to Q to 91020029 if you want to quit. $1 SMS sent/received. Sender 78801.'

    I called my service provider for advice and was told that it had no control over such service providers as there are many such small companies in the market. I was told to call 6728-1371 but my call was routed to a mailbox that was full.

    I called the Infocomm Development Authority for advice. I was told that there are no regulations in this area.

    Consumers are being put on the service without their consent and they have to take the trouble to unsubscribe. During this time, should any SMS come in, they are expected to pay for it.

    Besides the authorities, what will the telcos do about this? After all, billings go through them.

    'Plague' SMS was solicited, actually

    I REFER to the letter 'Plagued by unsolicited SMS service' (ST, Oct 1).

    I am the project manager of FunSMS Mobile Services and am writing to reply to that letter.

    First of all, the SMS sent to Mr Foh Meng Sing was a solicited message since we sent only to customers who are either registered on our website or have used our services in the past one year.

    The customer may have thought the SMS was unsolicited for the following reasons:

    Normally, customers register for a certain service and tend to forget after a long period.

    Customers may have got a recycled mobile number recently, and the previous owner may have registered for various services.

    Every six months, we send SMS to all our registered users to reconfirm their registration.

    This time, we sent out the following SMS:

    'Thank you for registering at WebSMS. Now your friends can contact via WebSMS. Please reply 'To Quit' to 91020029 if you want to quit. $1 SMS sent/received.'

    This SMS, which was sent to customers, is free. They will not be charged for it. The charges stated are made when customers used WebSMS services.

    Customers can reply to 91020029 to unsubscribe upon receiving the SMS and they will be unsubscribed automatically.

    My hotline is 67281371 and we operate Monday to Friday from 10am to 4pm.

    Our voicemail may be full in the morning due to calls overnight and customer may have called before our voicemail is cleared.

    Mr Foh can call our operations director on 90188434 or e-mail her at customer@funsms.com.sg to get the registration details of the particular mobile number.

    SAM GUNA
