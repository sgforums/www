    25 Aug 04, 10:21
    The MRT is the number 1 public transport here, bus services act as a feeder to the MRT. That's why you see LTA building more MRT lines and more bus services are withdrawn over the years.

    MRT lines coming up: Circle line, Bukit Timah line (linking Bt Panjang with the city) and East Regional line (linking Tampines, Bedok, Marine Parade, MacPherson and Kaki Bukit). Extension of existing East-West line to Jurong industrial estate.

    Proposed MRT line: North Coast line, linking North and East via North-East.

    LRT line coming up: Jurong Regional line (linking Boon Lay with NTU).

    Current MRT elevated stations' platforms are too small to handle the crowds in the peak hours, esp at Yishun, Bishan and Boon Lay.

    Also, accident prone are elevated stations and ground level station (Bishan), due to the small platform sizes and lack of platform doors.

    To me, there is no competition by train lines. SMRT operates the East-West and North-South lines while SBS Transit operates the North-East line. Totally different lines. The only competition between the 2 compinies is that one company's bus service competes with another company's MRT line or bus service.

    For example, SBS Transit service 2 competes with SMRT East-West line between Bugis station and Tanah Merah station, SBS Transit services 105 and 147 compete with SMRT East-West line between Clementi station and C'wealth station. As for SMRT, SMRTB service 857 competes with SBS Transit North-East line between Little India station and Boon Keng station.

    As for bus service, one example is SBS Transit service 25 competes with SMRTB service 854 between Hougang and Bedok.
