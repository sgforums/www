The True Rationale Behind The Price Hikes

  • D3athwish
    17 Sep 02, 02:07
    From what i observed during the past few months, the government is raising the price of virtually everything, from bus fare to health care, and raise in GST coming soon.
    This sets me wondering, what is the purpose of all these?
    As we know, globalization has been accelerating its pace and Singapore has not been spared. The recession is stepping into the 6th year and there are no signs of recovery yet. There is no significant advantage in productivity of the Singapore workforce as compared to our neighbouring countries or even China. As a result, the need to reduce labour cost is conceivable.
    However, after a few years of pay freeze and even pay cuts, the competitiveness of Singapore has not been improved. Therefore the government tries their version of pay cut: reducing the value of the Singapore dollar. By increasing the prices of commodities, the true purchasing power is actually reduced and results in artificially induced inflation. The value of Singapore dollar will drop in terms of exchange rate.
    At the end, the government achieves reduction in labour cost without actually implementing pay cuts.

    Singapore must be one of the only few countries who will accept her wage cuts willingly.. in other countries, there would already have been riots from trade unions.

    Well, they raised GST because, indeed the govt needs her revenue from somewhere, as she has deducted the coporate taxes. As you said, we are facing a recession. It means that Singapore must generate jobs. Where do you get that? Obviously, there must be an increase in investment. In singapore, investment is very important. By cutting our coporate taxes, it would make investing in Singapore more attractive.. and in the long run, increase our productive capacity, and our economy can revive in the long run. Furthermore, the govt has been giving out subsidies to the lower-income group, as this GST will affect them most as it is regressive. Where does all these money come from? Of course from increase in indirect tax such as GST. It's only an increase on 2% to singaporeans, how much can it hurt? Moreover, a lot of companies are absorbing the GST. In my opinion, it's justifiable. The govt has to get her revenue from somewhere, and having GST seems to bring about the least adverse effect on economy.

    Furthermore, when the 3% GST was implemented.. there wasn't much increase in aggregate demand. Thus, I doubt it would really bring about inflation in Singapore. Afterall, advancement in technology has increased our productive capacity.. hence, I doubt inflation will be much of a problem.

    Just my 2 cents worth of opinion.
