    As far as the lyrics go, I feel like Vincent has definitely outdone himself again. He isn't focused so much on proper nouns and historical references anymore but has manages to convey a mood that is his own and thicker than just alluding to any historical period can allow.

    The subject matter of the song is very similiar, I find, to lots of the Gothic material that Visual-Kei has pumped out over in Japan. There is mention of death, graveyards, vaguely gruesome reference to insects, disillusionment, etc. Altogether creepy and dismal. But in Chinese and with such a different musical style it is definitely something very fresh and edgy, even. I feel like the song that is most closely related to Nocturn is In the Name of the Father (of YHM) with it's owls and fog and whatnot. Even with that being said, this song is a great leap from it. As a side note: YFZM was the first song released from YHM also as Nocturne is the first song to be previewed from 11CP.

    The literal subject of this song seems to be the speaker/singer mourning for his deceased lover. Whether or not that is metaphorical is up for debate although the tragic atmosphere definitely supports it. But the main focus is the speaker/singer's response to the loss. He appears very numb and jaded (as the words emotionless, no sympathy, expressionless, etc convery). He is stuck in the past until the very last two lines where he attempts to hope for love in the future. Definitely dark but lovely.

    Some great lyrics that I want to highlight in here:

    A sound just like a wind in the night
    The heart breaks with such a beautiful sound
    -
    Just, breathlessly beautiful

    You are at the location at which you were buried
    -
    This one sentence shows how much he longs for the deceased lover. It's like, "You aren't here anymore, but in place of you, you are now that location. And I will stand by that place forever." Almost existential (What's left of us after we die? A location).

    And for you I've become anonymous
    Playing the piano under the moonlight
    -
    These two lines are even deeper than they sound. The idiom 隐姓埋名 literally means to hide and bury one's name. By 'becoming anonymous' he is saying that he has lost his identity. The individual is gone. He has Become the piano under the moonlight, and the sound that come from it. And just the Imagery! A solitary phantom, if you will, creating haunting music in the moonlight. It a scene to get lost in.
