    Probably the 9782872384th thread on NZ.

    Recommended tips:

    1. Do NOT take a guided tour.

    You'll have little or no time to really enjoy each location you stop at.

    2. If your trip is 10 or less days, focus on South Island. More things to see.

    Now the fun stuff:

    1. Rent a campervan and drive around the island. There are plenty of nice spots and campervan parks dotted on both islands so make use of them. That will help reduce your costs.

    2. Plenty of places to visit. NZ is a nature's paradise. If you enjoy tranquility and splendour, NZ is the place to go. Fiordland National Park is a MUST GO. Hike the fjord routes for a senic view of the valleys and for paranoramic camera shots.

    Mitre Peak:



    Milford Sound:



    (downside: reports of recent tourism indicate a massive influx of Chinese tourists; dirty filthy buggers who litter and defile the landscape so the place may not be as attractive as it used to be)

    3. Extreme sports such as whitewater rafting, shotover jets and tandem-skydiving are available, include cave-diving where you rappel down into the canvernous interiors.

    4. NZealanders are generally very friendly and courteous. Even the supermarket cashier greets you! Be sure to extend the same courtesy while visiting their country.

    If you consider the North Island, 90-mile beach and Rotorua National park are worth visiting for the coastline and mudpools respectively.
