  • elindra
    4 Jun 05, 13:39
    1. Understand your financial situation

    2. Break down your finances on what goes to pay for what

    3. List down what is a need and what is a want. You can scrap the wants if you do not have enough money.

    4. Understand the expectations of both families. The more traditional ones will insist on a traditional sit-down dinner which I personally find a total waste of time, and yes, like some kind of freak show event like what foxwalk said.

    5. Also, both of you need to understand each other's expectation when living together. If you think you are going to argue over housework, be fair and work out a time-table or something.

    Anyway for my hubby and me, we had a pretty lavish ROM to shut-up the old folks because I did not want to have a wedding dinner. (A pain in the neck especially for the seating arrangements) As well, I didn't think it is fun parading like some kind of exhibit and having to starve for the most of the night.

    Therefore, we had it buffet style and it was free sitting. We had it in a hotel so it was not exactly what you will call cheap, but it was still within our budget.

    Also, don't forget that besides the wedding and the flat, you will need cash for renovations and furnishing.
