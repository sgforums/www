  • Chin Eng
    7 Sep 04, 13:50
    It's kinda of hard to gauge the definition of "heavy user"....
    I would consider myself in the Prosumer range of digital camera users and still would not print too many photos.
    My point really is, you need to decide for yourself how many photos you'd be printing per week/month. Factor in the cost of the special coated paper needed plus the cost of the ink cartridges.
    If your printer is for a small business eg small design house, than it is probably justifiable for you to pump in some money for a good printer. But if your printer is for, say, little baby's photos, than I'd say, maybe not.

    Also you might want to consider if you needed the photos immediately, or, like me, a hobbyist photographer who is willing to wait a while to accumulate a bit then go to E-pic to print.

    Nowadays, with the self-service booth, printing photos is so easy. No need to upfront pay up a couple of hundred bucks of good money and then a few months later printer become redundant.
