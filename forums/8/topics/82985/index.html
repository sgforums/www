    It happened sometimes when you are using dial-up, try restarting your computer again and reconnect to your isp again. I also don't know exactly what is wrong, but I suspect it could be one of your device drivers not properly loaded into your OS. Hmm...I think I remember already, your modem com port...the IRQ port might be shared by another peripheral. How to find that out? I think if you are using win2k and below, you need to go to your program files, directx, double-click on the directx...I think is dxdiag file...will pop up a directx menu. Go to the last tab/folder and you will see a few buttons...got one is to tell you about your device setup, conflicts and stuff....click on that and another window will open. That window will have a conflict/sharing folder...click on that and you can see which device's irq is conflicting/sharing. I suspect that your modem irq port is shared with another peripheral and that's why sometimes you cannot connect to your isp correctly.

    I'm using winxp now...so they don't have this directx menu here. Or maybe I just haven't found it yet.
