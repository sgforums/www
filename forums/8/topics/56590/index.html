    To my dimay, classical music's future loods bleak than ever. Not only there has been a sharp decline in the number of classical listeners, those who claim themselves classical musicians are not doing adequate work to rescue the industry from falling into oblivion. Meanwhile, stiff competition from the thriving pop music industry is threatening to swallow the art that has existed over several centuries, and has weathered countless upheavals and revolutions.
    Why can't classical music find a world of its own in the new century?Composing technique evolves, and music instrument revolutionalised, but the spirit that drives the musicians to accomplish great achievements shall not perish. The same spirit that provided boundless inspiration for Mozart to become immortal is always vital. Composers and performers are entrusted with the responsiblity to start a revival in classical music, and they must be willing to.
    One reason for the declining classical music industry may be its lack of popular appeal. Some poeple scoff at classical music's incomprehensible complexity and difficulty. However, genuine masterpieces like "canon in d" and Bach's "Brandenburg concertos" are suitable for both beginners and loyal listeners. While many new composers try to creat new forms and styles or become obsessed with dissonant chords, you can't find a Mozart tho can compose music of universal appeal. The failure of classical music lies in this.
    If in the near future, there is someone who is both a mastreo in composing techniques and a genius melody creator, then classical music may have a better chance of finding new young listeners. The world will be waiting for that historical moment to come, with great anticipation! Written by j.s.bach
