    It is the *nature* of us to procastinate.

    Newton's 1st Law :

    "anything at rest tends to stay at rest, anything in motion tends to stay in motion"

    So we are usually happy with our current state and refuse move on.

    Newton's 2nd Law :

    "in order to accelerate, you need a force"

    if we want to move on, this "force" must come within ourselves (ideally) and that is the difficult part ! Without this force we will remain where we are (according to Newton's 1st law) and that is what we call "procastination", we will procastinate until we find that "force" to move us. Note that this force may be external too (from parents, friends, supervisors etc).

    Newton's 3rd Law :

    "For every action there is an equal and opposite reaction"

    For every of our action, there will always be some resistance. From frens, parents, public, peers etc, it is never a smooth ride. And that is why it is sometimes difficult to move on. We will be able to move on only when the "force" within us is great enough (Newton's 2nd Law) to overcome the resistance.

    So what is the moral of the story ?

    Build that force in you.

    It is all about self-motivation and self-discipline.

    There are 2 kinds of procrastinators, according to an article I read in The Economist, 1999.

    The first type is called the 'sophisticate', people who are procrastinators by nature, but recognises their flaw, and therefore overcompensates for it by doing everything at the earliest possible moment.

    The second type is called the 'naif' (pronounced 'naive'). These people are your good old procrastinators who leave everything to the last moment.

    Neither type of procrastinator is superior to the other, but rather, depends on the situation. Certain cases, it is better to be sophisticate, in others, it is the naif who wins.

    So, what kind of a procrastinator are you?
