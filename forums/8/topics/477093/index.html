    A man decked in a samurai outfit and wielding a samurai sword had jumped the fare gate at Paya Lebar MRT at 12.30pm Monday, SMRT said in a statement on its Facebook page on Monday.

    The public transport operator said it has been alerted of the incident, and that its staff informed the police immediately. According to eyewitness reports, the man then alighted the train at City Hall MRT, and boarded the northbound train towards Jurong East. He got off at Dhoby Ghaut station.

    The man was eventually apprehended at 12.45pm at Jalan Pisang, near Bugis MRT station after a foot chase, eyewitnesses said. Police investigations are ongoing.

    A SMRT spokesman said: "Passenger safety is our priority and we do not tolerate abhorrent behaviour nor allow offensive weapons to be brought on our trains. The spokesman urged passengers who witness such acts to activate the Emergency Communication Button to communicate with the train captain, who will report the situation to the control centre. Staff at the nearest station will render immediate assistance and alert the police and security staff, she added.
