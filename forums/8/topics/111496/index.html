    Hi,

    Sorry to learn of your plight. I've had the experience of living alone. And I always like to give the bad news first 'cos it gives a happier ending, so here goes....

    CONS

    - Lots of hidden costs from the time you move in (eg. eating utensils, cooking gas, utilities, SCV, property tax, TV/radio licence, etc etc. Thousands!). Curtains are a killer, cost-wise! There are costs you've never heard of, like fire insurance, etc.

    - Lots of stupid things to take care of (eg. water pipe leaking, aircon leaking, fridge goes kapoot, heavy rain with strong winds blows rain water in through the windows even when closed, toilet flush stops working, light bulbs start blowing, etc).

    - Forget about having lots of free weekend time. You have to clean house, wash/iron clothes, etc etc.

    - You become a slave to your job because of the mortgage. You'll never say "no" to any customer or boss again!

    - Registered letter come also no one collect. Wanna get someone to come and repair damaged things, or wanna move in new TV or furniture also must take leave because no one else is around to help.

    - Sometimes a bit lonely.

    - Sooner or later, you'll feel that the place would be more lively if there was someone to share with.

    PROS

    - A sense of belonging, something major to call your very own.

    - No one complains when you don't do the dishes. Just leave them in the sink and wash them when you want to use them next!

    - Can save on clothes because can walk around naked. But if you are deep frying food, watch out for those hot flying oil droplets! Believe me, they hurt like hell if you kenah on the wrong place.

    - Can make as much noise as you want (unless you have kaypoh neighbours, which can be hell).

    - Almost absolute freedom!! Which also almost corrupts absolutely....

    - Can lock yourself in the toilet, sit on the toilet bowl (or lie in the tub), take hours and read a whole novel and no one will complain or knock on your door.

    - If you are sharing your apartment with another, you can chase him/her out when you're not happy with the person. You are the owner after all.

    There's six of each for you, fair enough....
