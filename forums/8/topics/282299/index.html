    I agree with warming up first before hitting the weights. But I don't consider a 5-10 minutes on a cardio machine as a full cardio workout, that is just warming up; to me, at least 30 minutes of cardio to be considered a proper cardio workout.

    There are many school of thoughts out there, some say cardio (not warming up exercise) comes after weight training, and some say it does not matter. I know there is a school of thought which believe that cardio should come after weight training, to avoid burning up muscles.

    But it make sense that if you are planning to do weight training, you don't do too much cardio before that; you will be tired out before hitting the weights. Usually my PT will ask me to do 5 to 10 minutes warming up before working out with the weights.

    Anyway, there are lots of literature out there in the internet on exercise. Just do a search and you find one you think make sense.
