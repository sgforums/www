    Not EVERYTHING can be made a copyright...

    You can do a homepage for yourself and if a corporation has a website with layout similar to yours, you can't sue the corporation for copyright infringement.

    However, intellectual property such as articles, artwork, photographs are copyright.

    Please also do not confuse copyright with infringment of privacy. If I were to post a picture of you on the net, I'm not violating any copyright law, only violating your rights. (also not to be confused with photos of models on fashion sites etc.. which are copyright)

    In anycase, this whole copyright issue is very subjective, just be careful what you download and what you put up on the web. Usually, if it's for non-commercial purposes and does not violate the rights of any person, it should be okay.

    Sometimes I feel pity for people like Enigma.

    They never understand things even when
    people explain to them.

    If you are posting a picture taken by me, I
    _CAN_ sue you for copyright infringement.
    Photographs taking IS CONSIDERED as
    intellectual property.

    And the copyright of the photographs BELONGS
    (usually) NOT to the person inside the
    photograph, it belongs to the person TAKING
    the photographs.

    And you cannot sue someone for infringement
    of privacy if the photograph is taken in a
    public place. You can however sue the
    photographer for defamation if he takes your
    photograph in promoting a merchandise or
    relate you to something which _you_ think is
    demeaning to YOUR reputation.

    That is all. I am speaking from a legal
    viewpoint and for what I do for a living,
    learning basic law is compulsory. I will not
    be surprised you are just guessing all these
    things which you have typed here.

    And of course you cannot sue someone for
    copyright infringement for coming out with
    similar looking web interfaces. That is a
    no brainer statement which small children
    know how to make
