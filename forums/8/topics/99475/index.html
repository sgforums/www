    Singapore Discovery Centre to close for $25m revamp



    SINGAPORE : The Singapore Discovery Centre wants to make National Education fun and exciting by offering interactive multimedia displays.

    But that will be about a year from now as the centre is closing at the end of October for a $25 million high-tech makeover.

    Students like Esmeralda and Kalaivani, who say they have been seeing the same exhibits since the centre was opened eight years ago, are looking forward to the changes.

    Esmeralda said: "There should be more interactive stuff, it will be more fun."

    Kalaivani said: "Actually I would like more games around here, so that everyone can actually at least try some games."

    School groups make up nearly half of all visitors and so it pays to listen to the young.

    Desmond Ler said: "New inventions and new robots and new discoveries like movies."

    Mohd Yunos said: "I want to see the place bigger because cramped a lot of schools come, cramped already."

    The current interior will be overhauled and in its place, there will be more multimedia shows and more hands-on activities.

    It will also have less of the current Singapore Armed Forces highlights which takes up about half of the exhibits.

    Mr Jordan Woo, General Manager of Singapore Discovery Centre, said: "Many people have the opinion that National Education is something that's very serious and that it is boring.

    "It is not going to be your typical National Education centre. I was down at the National Constitution Centre in Philadelphia and at first that was also my perception. But no, national education can be interesting, it can be fun, it can be engaging."

    The new centre is designed by New York-based consultant Ralph Appelbaum Associates, who is behind the Smithsonian Museum.

    The crown jewel in the new centre is a globe-like Future Wonders theatre.

    It will run a sight and sound show on the exterior and inside, visitors will get a chance to use cutting-edge technology to create their vision for Singapore.

    A lake has seen quite a fair bit of action from fishing competitions to paddle boats.

    And as part of the revamp, they are considering building barbeque pits and creating picnic spots around the lake to create more buzz in this area. - CNA
