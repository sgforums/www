    An exchange of a sofa chair purchase has brought more hassle than I had bargained for. Under the terms and conditions (T&Cs) of the store, a buyer can exchange for any goods purchased prior to 7 days notice before the delivery date provided the total value of the exchange goods are of the same or of higher value. I was told I could exchange my sofa chair for other items in the store but the items had to be at least of the price of the chair and the additional $50 installation and transportation charges.

    I have picked out 4 items to fulfill the required amount of which 3 were items that I do not really require. However, much to my surprise, the salesperson had added on another $30 installation and transportation charges to the bill, in addition to the original amount of the chair and $50 installation/transportation charges that I had to make up to.

    I left the store after having paid about 34% of the total amount as deposit and I had a feeling that I was misled into paying double for installation and transportation even though I had not utilized their install/transport services as yet. Hence, I tried to call up the store for clarification.

    After 5 hours of waiting and umpteen calls and messages left to speak to the salesperson who served me, I finally spoke with him on the following day. However, what left me shocked were the following points he made:

    1) The salesperson insists that the initial $50 install/transport charges were part of the sales and that it cannot be taken apart from the goods sales amount. However, their T&Cs indicated that the purchase price does not include delivery charges. (these are the exact wordings in their clause). He kept insisting that my interpretation of the charges was wrong and no one else would share my interpretation and repeated trying to indoctrinate me that the install/transport charges had to come together with the purchase price.

    2) I offered to pick up the items I had purchased with my own transportation and to have both the $50 and the $30 install/transport charges taken off from the bill. The salesperson said that he could only waive the $30 even if I were to pick up the items myself and that they will be absolve from all responsibility if I fail to install the furniture myself if I choose that option. The $50 transport cost would still have to be charged accordingly even though I did not use or require their installation and transport at all as their sales revenue would decrease if they waive it.

    3) After I have instructed him to hold on to the goods while I seek second opinions on this case (In their T&Cs, it is stated clearly that I have up to 1 month for delivery), the salesperson change his tactics to say that the goods may not be available if I were to delay the delivery.

    When I told him that I was not in a rush for the good to be delivered, he exerted pressure on me to say that I would have to give at least 2 weeks notice before delivery of goods which would leave me with only 2 weeks to inform them of my desired delivery date.
