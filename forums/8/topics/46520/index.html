    hehehe advertising,my fav topic. It has been proven in research that whether an ad is being liked by the masses has nothing or little to do with whether the product sales increase. In fact there have been cases where advertisements were well received but yet sales decreased and vice versa.

    Let me just put it simply. You all like the IKEA ad rite? I like it too. But do you see urself rushing to IKEA to buy their stuff after seeing that ad? Not many of us i believe.

    I once challenge my advertising lecturer on the "ADAM kING" Ad. I proposed that it is an ad done with half hearted effort. Neither does it brand Guiness, neither does it pushes sales, the values that Guiness is trying to associate with its brand are just not being brought out well by the campaign. My lecturer then said "Do u think Guiness is trying to reach out to you, a 25 year old young yuppie?"
    I said "I dun think so, their target customers are more likely more matured adults in their late 30s and above." My lecturer then reply "then why should Guiness care that you dun like the ads?"

    Advertisements are NOT meant for masses. If you ever advertise your product to the masses and try to be all things to all men, you will fail miserably. The secret of big brands and why they survive, like Mercedes, BMW, Gucci etc is becos they target and only advertise in places where their customers will see them. Will Gucci care if I poor kid who earns less than $2000 like their ads? NO, they are more concerned about whether those affluent people who are likely to buy their products like their ads.

    Advertisements are very interesting. The next time u see an ad which u dislike and dun understand and u know that it is not trying to target you, then it could be one hell of a good ad.

    Well TV is of cos a mass media, just like Radio (Singapore is one of the few countries where radio is considered a mass media simply becos of our size.). But dun be surprised if i tell you that advertising on a mass media does NOT imply that I am trying to reach the masses.

    Ok example. Say I am selling a imported soccer magazine. And i did a research and find that my target customers who are most likely to be males, between18 - 40 (primary market), who is interested in soccer news etc and i also found out that their media habits include watching TV at Prime Time slots. They dun listen to radio and doesnt respond well to pop ups in internet. So how do i reach them with my message. Through the media that they tune in to of cos! logical? Now, since i only want to sell to this group of people, i would tailor my ad in a way they will understand and like my message and eventually buy the magazine. Of cos, anyone else who sees this message and does not fall into the target group, we would welcome them to buy our magazine, but they are hardly our focus of our advertising because they are our 'secondary target', which means bonus customers to the advertiser. Thus, even in a mass media, an advertisement is very targetted, becos in 30 seconds, you cannot try to tell 10 different messages to 10 different groups of people. Buying habits differ from group to group.
    As for how the primary target is chosen, that is another issue by itself but once it is chosen, all advertising and marketing efforts need to focus on them. If you notice, Mercedes advertise on TV too. Do they expect masses to buy? No, it is likely they know that people whom they want to buy the cars would be watching TV at that time. This is target advertising.
