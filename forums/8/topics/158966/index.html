    an "elementary" reproduction of feelings that i had when listening to the album....

    "blue light" - ethereal as the name suggests... same chorus towards the end as that of "horses"
    "buddhas remains" -- it makes you feel a great awakening inside you.. fills you with a feeling like the soul being stirred... maybe fill with buddha's spirit!
    "horses" - awesome chorus, i just kept on listening to it on a forever loop
    "lai chi theme" gives a huge power surge deep inside..
    "monk" - close ur eyes... and u would really see monks sitting atop rocky ledges and meditating in the thin mountain air.. reminds me of a program about Monastries of Wudang Mountain....you will fully agree how well the song picturises monks meditating upon various truths of life.
    "escape" -- makes you imagine people really running away, hiding from prying eyes, just managing things in the nick of time.. & now.. one of these was the theme song? ..

    i kinda really appreciate that jolin tsai's song. considering the above, this pop song was the best .... and so was that "lords..." song.

    Add on more about ARR

    I have listened to Yanni and recently ARR.Since both Yanni and Rahman are heavily into ambient, new age music.

    There was something different about ARR....improves his instruments , sound effects , SYNTH usage, recording and sound engineering and ... instruments of AR Rahman ..he uses heavy sound effects...space age effects.s that of the digital age....His compositions are an intriguing cocktail of musical pieces that literally blow your mind. His music is unique in its offbeat instrumental interludes, unconventional harmonies, and use of distinct perfect voices and thumping rhythms

    It (sound engineering) is certainly a science. ARR is a good technician behind it who determines the right variables for a particular effect. (I say this not becos I am a sound engineer, but I attended some recording sessions in my friends studio). If it were some science with fixed formulae, u and I can learn them and use it all the time,ARR's strength lies not only in his perfect sense of melody and rhythm but also in his immaculate sound engineering...Very intelligent, smart and creative...

    I think he is an all rounder, I mean his grounding is very very solid. He knows Indian classical music, he is in touch with Indian folk music, he knows about western music and he has really studied western classical also. He knows about Middle Eastern music. And electonic types of pop and techno So there is no wonder you see different colours in his songs.... His range of imagination is expansive.

    I fact i chanced upoun Arr's music by accident in 1992 at the time of my O'levels....Found a new casette lying on a hot weekday afternoon around and it had an interesting modern cover design and lettering.
    Initially i was not expecting anything special but when i played it on my walkman, first struck me as slow moving love songs ( for the info , this was before arr became a pop composer and gone commercial)..Now it didn seem like any average tamil songs ... It was a new expereince, high tech, interesting sounds flowing flawlessly..the song stanzas and raaga structures used were utterly unique, in the sense it slowly grows on yr ear after some listenings..I was learning classical Indian music in '92 and my mentor ( well known teacher and many years experience) commented on arr on that day
    " Hmm.interesting i never came across this level of complexity, he has not totally deviated from traditional basics, he had brought it to a different level, so who is this person?"

    "I told him i did not know who this stranger is but he did something interesting with this stuff" ...
    His reply was " whoever this person may be, he's in a deep emotional state and his music is coming straight from his heart"

    then we went on to discuss to see how the overall texture of the songs was enhanced by clever use of sound vs mood

    The cassette seems like a lot of tech been used...a new concept in its heyday in the Indian music movie world....As expected, it took everyone by surprise that Indian music had jumped light years forward to this new trend..Interestingly it was a huge leap everyone agrees...Then overnight, arr became a household name.a few months later, others were released and at the same time i still havent noted that the music was indeed composed by Arr
    one year passed, and then a half when the pop types were coming in it was during this time i was probably and more aware of this new breed of music that i checked that casette
    tapes for the credits.It wrote "AR Rahman" and as i traced back to releases before, i realised that it was this composer all the while been responsible for
    all the great melodies . In fact arr real name was Dileep kumar b4 he converted from hindu to islam....It was credited as Dileep kumar in his first release.....Below attached is the pic of arr's first release in its original version casette cover art
