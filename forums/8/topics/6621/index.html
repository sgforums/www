  • MayaJade

    Racism is giving different treatment to people based on their race. It can be very mild like thinking that africans have beautiful voice to more extreme treatments like genocyde.

    While mass extermination of a race should be prevented by any means necessary, one really cannot help but committing other acts of racism. For example, every time my room mate decides not to stay in the hall anymore, I spend the next few days dreading that the next one who comes along may be an Indian. I cannot handle the smell of the incense that always seems to waft out from them. Maybe not all Indians carry this smell, I'm sure to them this smell is good, but those that I've met make me dizzy after 1/2 an hour.

    Isn't that being racist? Yes, I have started to make assumptions on a person based on his race. It is true that people of the same race share similar traits and practice similar customs. Because Man are forever finding patterns, we can't help but associating certain behaviour with certain race. If we do not like that behaviour, it is inevitable that we feel certain antipathy towards the race, knowing that there might be anomalies but unwilling to take the risk.

    hmm, my two cents (popular phraseology isn't it?)

    race is a concept. an abstraction. it is not a real thing, perceptable to the sense. it is an idea used to distinguish and classify human beings. other, similar ideas, like gender, are also used to classify human beings. so what is unique about race as taxonomy? race is a concept which has unique relationship to other concepts and objects which is then linked in political discourse. deconstructing the race concept begins with the identification of its field of objects and concepts presented in discourse. race is a means of describing the world, and like any other means of description, it is a means for organising the world. for better or worse.
