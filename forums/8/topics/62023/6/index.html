    To answer your follow up on question on current days application, it is a personal conviction that it is not a LITERAL command to cover a woman's head.

    If we study into Paul's teachings which were taught to Timothy, you would see a teaching about intrinsic focus. Like in

    1 Timothy 2:9-10, "Likewise the women are to adorn themselves in respectable apparel with modesty and sensibleness, not in braids and gold or pearls or expensive clothing, but, as is fitting for women who profess godliness, through good works."

    Timothy is not condemning the use of nice expensive clothes,gold and braids. He is condemning the misuse of these things.The point is not to warn against something intrinsically evil, but to warn against its misuse as an expression of self-exaltation or worldly-mindedness.

    Thus in the teaching about the covering of head for women, the culture then was that there are women who took off their coverings (which were a social norm THEN) as an act of rebellion towards authority. Thus, in the same way, Paul is not teaching that taking off coverings or not covering their heads are bad but that the purpose of the actions. I would say that in present day, PAUL would say "Do not do anything or show acts of rebellion in open public to challenge the authorites in the church."
