    Throwers given 48-hr deadline
    Change of heart as SAAA offers the Chinese pair a one-year contract again
    By Peh Shing Huei

    THE two China-born throwers dumped by the Singapore Amateur Athletic Association last month have been given 48 hours to accept a one-year contract.

    The offer was faxed to the lawyer of Luan Wei, 22, and E Xiaoxu, 20, at 4.55pm yesterday. They must revert by 5pm tomorrow.

    The SAAA said it is doing so 'purely out of magnanimity and goodwill', though president Tang Weng Fei said he believes the pair have potential 'provided they work very hard'.

    The throwers were dropped from the association's foreign talent scheme after failing to meet targets set by the SAAA in March.

    Luan had to reach 17.50 metres in the shot put, and E 52m in the hammer throw within six months. Luan's best effort is the 17.17m set last year, while E's is 48.64m, achieved in July.

    They refused to go and, after their parents flew here for talks, the SAAA relented by giving them a one-year extension to meet the same targets.

    The pair did not take up the offer when the deadline lapsed last Friday, arguing that there were no written contracts.

    The SAAA had said that the offers 'will not be revived' but, evidently, there has been a change of heart.

    The terms are the same as those offered a fortnight ago, with conditions such as meeting the targets in a year, training under the SAAA's coaches and no increase in allowances.

    'I wish they can accept it,' said Tang in a press conference at the SAAA office yesterday.

    The oil trader added that he had offered E a job at his Nanjing office for 2,000 yuan (S$400), but she did not take it up.

    He also rebutted the throwers' accusations that they have not been treated well, and that the SAAA reneged on the promises made when they arrived in 1999.

    'We pay the rent for their HDB apartments,' he said, 'and we hired a chef to cook for them because they said they are from northern China, and have different palates from us.

    'After the chef left, we arranged for them to have free dinners at a seafood restaurant every day. We even paid for their taxi fares to the restaurant.
    'But, after a while, they got tired of the food too.'

    Restaurant owner Teh Chor Joo verified Tang's claims at the press conference. The association pays Luan $800 a month and E $600.

    Said SAAA senior adviser Loh Lin Kok: 'They don't have to pay a cent for anything else. Maybe they just need the money to buy some sweets.'

    THE two China-born throwers embroiled in a row with the Singapore Amateur Athletic Association are willing to back down from some of their demands.

    But they also want some concessions from their former employer before signing the new one-year contracts.

    The SAAA had offered E Xiaoxu, 20, and Luan Wei, 22, new deals on Thursday, giving them 48 hours to accept the offers, after dumping them last month for failing to meet the SAAA's targets.

    Yesterday, the pair's lawyer, Edmond Pereira, replied to the association, faxing over a letter and seeking some clarifications.

    He said: 'They want more details on their studies. They don't want to just learn English.

    'Who would send their kids overseas just to learn English?'

    The throwers also do not want to train under national throws coach Ma Yongfeng, though they backed down from their earlier demand of having former coach Guo Huaiyun back. They found it hard to relate to Ma's methods.

    'Anyone else will do,' said Pereira.

    The throwers, who are permanent residents, and their parents had also demanded earlier this week that the SAAA guarantee Singapore citizenship for the pair.

    But, according to Pereira, they and their parents have since accepted the fact that the SAAA cannot guarantee Singapore citizenship even if they meet the targets set by the association.

    The targets are the bronze-medal distances at the South-east Asia Games - 17.50 metres for Luan in the shot put, and 52m for E in the hammer throw.

    The Foreign Sports Talents scheme is meant for athletes who are significantly better than Singaporeans, and it was set up with the aim of making Singapore an elite sporting nation in Asia.

    The SAAA said it would respond to the lawyer's letter accordingly. Today's 5pm deadline for the pair's decisions still stands. -- PEH SHING HUEI
