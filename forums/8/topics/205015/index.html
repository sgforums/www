Japanese PM visits war shrine

  • Beckhamagic
    15 Aug 06, 08:01
    TOKYO - Japanese Prime Minister Junichiro Koizumi on Tuesday visited the controversial Yasukuni war shrine on the anniversary of Japan's World War II surrender despite warnings from Asian neighbours.

    Under light rain, Koizumi, wearing a tuxedo jacket with coattails, walked out of his limousine and entered the main hall of the sprawling shrine in central Tokyo escorted by a priest in a white robe.

    Yasukuni supporters waved Japanese flags as Koizumi visited, while hundreds of police kept watch.

    The Yasukuni war shrine honours 2.5 million war dead including 14 top war criminals from World War II.

    Koizumi has visited the shrine once a year but never on August 15. On his last visit in October 2005, he did not enter the main hall.

    China and South Korea view the shrine, which was a spiritual centre for Japanese nationalists during World War II, as a symbol of Tokyo's past imperialism.
