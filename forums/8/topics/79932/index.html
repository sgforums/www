    anyway this apply to all rechargable batt... just that different type of rechargable batt, charge style, and manufacturer's technology contribute to the depth and strenght of effects.

    The batt would jus go dead immediately. There is two major effects the user will observed.
    1. Memory effect
    Occurs when user charge a partial discharged batt. lets say the user decide to charge the batt with 70% remaining power to 100%. The top 30 % will be fresh and discharge as per normal when used. As it reaches 70%, the user will experience a sudden drop of power( less current output). The chemical in the batt for the 70% portion will be reluctunt to give up their stored energy.

    2.Lazy effect
    Occurs when user do a partial charge on the batt. The uncharged portion will develope a resistence to store the energy. Keeping a batt in a partial discharge state will also leed to Lazy effect.
