    The seeds were eaten in Melanesia and Polynesia and the people there called it the "food tree". The seeds were roasted before eating. Elsewhere they are boiled. In Java, they are roasted, shelled, then eaten with rice. They are said to taste like soy bean. The raw seeds are toxic and may cause intoxication. Studies show the cooked seed to be rich in oil and proteins and easily digested by both humans and livestock.

    The seeds are curiously similar in weight. Four seeds make up about one gramme.The ground seeds can produce an oil which was used as an industrial lubricant.

    In Ancient Indian medicine, the ground seeds are used to treat boils and inflammations.
