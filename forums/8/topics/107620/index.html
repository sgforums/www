    Hmm, I just finished my business law module for last sem, and it was an open book exam.

    Well, what i can say its depends on subjects. Modules like law are every open ended, and subject to debates and arguments.

    When assessing those taking such modules, assessor should be posting questions that do not look for 'right answer', but rather dwell into how well students can debate and argue to show his understanding, than teaching him how to search for answers.

    In anyway, B S Tabalujan's books sucks. It barely scratches the surface. If the assessor is like the one i mentioned above, a student will get poor results if he just rely on that book for the whole semester, not just exams.

    Well, in anyway me and my friends had fun debating with one another over the legal technicalities in the book, and suggest everyone has a legal dictionary as well as sourcing other books for readings, especially for modules such as this.
