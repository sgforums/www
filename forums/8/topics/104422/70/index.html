    Punggol LRT completed, to start operations next year

    SINGAPORE: Singapore's third Light Rapid Transit system - the Punggol LRT (PGLRT) - has been completed and will start ferrying passengers in the first quarter of next year.

    This means a direct link for Punggol residents to the North East MRT line at Punggol MRT station.

    The system, built at a cost of $354m, runs for 10.3 kilometres across 15 stations, and will serve residents in Punggol 21 New Town.

    The Land Transport Authority handed over the system to operator SBS Transit on Wednesday after testing it.

    SBS Transit will conduct its own trial runs and train staff for the new system before the driverless trains start running next year.

    The system was built by a consortium comprising SembCorp Industries, Mitsubishi Heavy Industries and Mitsubishi Corporation and construction began in June 2000.

    Singapore has two other LRT systems - the Bukit Panjang LRT and Sengkang LRT. -CNA
