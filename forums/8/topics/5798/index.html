    God rules in Heaven but the Devil rules on Earth. This simple credo inspires both the traditionally religious and those of a New Age persuasion, as the world aproaches the end of the Piscean Age. According to one's point of view, the world will eitherbe saved by the Second Coming and enjoy a thousand years of peace, or humanity itself will achieve a higher wisdom and eradicate wars, inequality, poverty, and famine.

    This view of the end of the twentieth century reflects the belief that Satan has been given this century to lead the followers of the Church away from the path of righteousness. This notion can be traced to Pope Leo XIII (1873-1903), who had a vision of a discussion between Satan and God, in which the former surmised that, given enough time and power, he could overthrow the Church. In response, God gave him the twentieth century as his chance to test the faithful. However, the arrangement was that, at the dawning of the new millenium, this license would be withdrawn.

    Fundamentalist Christians expect the Beast, also known as the Anti-Christ, to rule the worl just before the advent of the new millenium and many candidates have been put forward for this title, including Saddam Hussein, the late Ayatollah Khomeni, Colonel Gaddafi, and even Bill Clinton. Admittedly, the final two years of this millenium are rich with a dread symbolism: ninteen hundred and ninety-eight is three times the number of the Beast- 666. Nineteen hundred and ninety-eight is even more omnious, since it can be constructed as an inversion of the figures 666, together with a figure one, which is the universal symbol of perfection, unity, predominance and beginning.
