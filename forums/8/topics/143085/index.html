DPM Tan says S'poreans have much to learn from London blasts

    SINGAPORE : Deputy Prime Minister Tony Tan warned Singapore could very well be the next target for terrorists.

    He said the people of London had beaten the terrorists by getting back to their normal lives quickly.

    So too, must Singapore if it ever comes under attack, he added. The stark message is that Singaporeans have to live with terror, and take security measures as a necessary fact of life.

    Dr Tan, who is also Coordinating Minister for Security and Defence, said this in a ministerial statement in Parliament in response to the recent London explosions.

    Singapore's public transport system comprises a comprehensive bus and train network - both underground and above ground.

    And security agencies have been progressively increasing its security, to prevent an attack, and to deal with the consequences of one.

    Immediately after the July 7h London bomb blasts, police patrols at Singapore's MRT stations were stepped up and train operators increased checks done on commuters.

    Singapore's security agencies are now planning to deploy explosive detection dog teams at MRT stations to carry out surprise checks.

    And there are lessons for Singapore from the latest London incidents.

    Dr Tan said: "Even a city as hardened and prepared as London became victim to terrorist attacks.The next target could well be Singapore. One of the lessons we can learn from Britain is practice does work.

    "In Britain they have a Civil Contingency Secretariat, which practices these scenarios again and again. When it happened, all their systems went into place. There is no panic, people were evacuated, rescued, life went back to normal within a day and that is what we have to do in Singapore."

    But the fight against terrorism is not just the government's responsibility.

    Dr Tan mentioned a Channel NewsAsia news item which showed that Singaporeans were just not vigilant enough.

    He said he watched the television programme where an unattended bag was deliberately left at Raffles Place and it was spotted by two American women.

    Dr Tan said the two women had been used to such an attack that they decided to raise the alarm.

    He reminded Singaporeans that they are "the eyes and ears, and the extended reach of the security agencies".

    Dr Tan said it was just as important that the country's Muslims are well integrated into the community to prevent any backlash if there is a terror attack.

    He said any backlash against the Muslim community could split the country and lay to waste the decades of hard work we have put in into nurturing racial and religious harmony, and in building up social resilience. - CNA/de
