    Suppose there is a frog living in a well with few other frogs, many of them feel that they may not leave the well ever or even if they do, they may live...in yet another well. Therefore, they do not care to know about the seas, the oceans and the mountains. They talk about well things and read the well newpaper which is full of well happenings such as one promising well frog who has passed away (and ignoring greater happenings outside such as frogs in some mountains getting massacred by hundreds)

    Is there something wrong with this way of thinking? After all, if there is no desire to ever get out of wells, it is only too practical to learn about well and forget about everything else beyond wells? So what if frogkind doesn't grow intellectually? It will be the problem of future generation frogs and well, so far, no frogs have been hurt by the lack of general knowledge (except when trying to pass pretentious froggy exams where most frogs have learned to pretend that they care and they argue about mountains as if they know what mountains are all about).

    Then, finally, that's frogs, who care about frogs, we are human. And as the song goes, "we are singapore.....", is it healthy for Singaporeans to pay attention mostly to Singapore happenings if as far as we can imagine, the African racial clashes have nothing to do whatsoever with our wellbeing?
