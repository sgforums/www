Civil service to cut starting pay

  • lemonie22
    9 Nov 03, 09:57
    STARTING pay in the civil service will be cut by up to 20 per cent from January next year to stay in line with the private sector.

    The PSD said it is also reviewing the salaries of officers currently in service and will reduce them if they are found to be out of line with private sector pay.

    The PSD also announced yesterday that civil servants will receive an annual variable bonus of half a month's salary this year - the lowest in 15 years.
    Since the Government started paying this bonus in 1988, it has always paid a minimum of three-quarters of a month's salary.

    The cuts in starting salaries and bonus payments will reduce the Government wage bill by $63 million a year.
