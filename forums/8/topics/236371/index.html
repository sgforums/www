  • LazerLordz
    8 Feb 07, 23:13
    (From what I remember...)

    Caesar's @ Fremantle has great pasta but ask the boss about steak and the chips, he might just start talking about the American sailors.

    I'd recommend the Penne Carbonara and the Caesar Salad, as the chicken slices are roasted before it's put into the salad.

    Westfield Galleria and Carousel near Vic Park are a few of the suburban malls with late night shopping. They vary from Thurs or Fri, and shops will usually stay open till 9.

    If you visit Booragoon, there is a fairly nice fish bee hoon diner.

    Visit Scarborough Beach for the view and seafood, while make sure that you drive up north to the Pinnacles and explore limestone formations. Dandaragnan Bay is a rustic lil town near the Pinnacles if you just want a quick rest stop with a nice area to park and have a picnic beside the beach. Mind you, it's just another estate though there's a war memorial at the beach.

    Kings Park is a must-go place too. There is a skywalk where you can walk at the treetop level etc..

    (1) Have a look at the website below and hopefully you get an idea what you want to do and see when you are in Perth:

    http://www.westernaustralia.com/en/

    (2) Like fymk said, it might be tricky during the Easter long weekends... can you come the week before or after Easter? Also, the school holiday starts from April 5th so it'll be peak season when you're here and it can be busy and accommodation/certain activities can be more expensive.

    (3) Sometimes you might get a good deal by booking your tours when you arrive at the destinations but there's a risk that the tour might get booked out. I think it's best to look at the website above to decide what you want to do first.

    If you are planning to hire a car to drive, just be very careful on the road.

    Happy planning and hope you have a great and safe holiday in Perth!
