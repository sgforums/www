    First thing: many times, people stay in a relationship for infinite number of reasons and the thing about people is that they chose not to abandon this relationship. Why? You might say, ask or even wondered.

    I) There is still attraction between the both of them.
    II) They (or either) is working things out in this relation or giving chance for this relation to go on - therefore, giving in, etc will take place.

    Do you know that 98% people in this world don't marry the perfect one? Instead - they build a perfect relation from their inperfect attribute contributed by one another. Marriage, in today's context is only nothing but a certificate. In reality - your husband/wife is someone that is able to spend the rest of your life with and growing together as the both of you *TEACHES* each other the definition of love and the definition of your own marriage and relationship.

    Whats's the use of a relation where there is absolutely nothing to learn from one another? You will give that relation up in no time - trust me on this. Because it sinks into routine mode relation that only serve to waste your time and learning nothing.

    She may like like (or love) that other guy - but what makes you people think that by being together as a couple, she and him will attain happiness together? What's makes you people think that he will be better than her present bf? The list of questions goes on...

There is a lesson to be learn from this post - so, if you have a gf - then always treasure and appreciate her and *not* have characteristic she mentioned above. Because if you are a *GOOD* decent bf who cares - regardless of what extraordinary situations that may happen next time - at *LEAST* you did what a bf should do as a bf - your girl will only serve to lose out if she were to give you up.

    Btw curad, to tell him on the surface may not be enough. Its good for good occasion - like anniversary, vday, etc, to speak heart to heart with him on unconscious actions that may indirectly and unseemingly ruin this relationship.
