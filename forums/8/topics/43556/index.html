    Henry relying on Rooney for title
    LONDON (Reuters) - Arsenal's French striker Thierry Henry is hoping that Everton's 17-year-old Wayne Rooney can help the London club retain their premier league title.
    The newly-crowned PFA player of the year believes the gifted England forward can help Everton beat Manchester United at Goodison Park on the final day of the season to give the Gunners a chance of overhauling the league leaders.

    Arsenal trail Manchester United by five points and even if they win all three of their remaining fixtures, there is nothing they can do if Alex Ferguson's side beat Charlton at home on Saturday and Everton on May 11.

    However, Henry believes that Rooney can play his part in Arsenal's championship bid.

    "He has a wonderful arrogance about him and the situation or the opposition doesn't appear to worry him," Henry was quoted as saying in the media on Tuesday.

    "When he plays against great defenders like Marcel Desailly, it's like he is playing in the street. It's not a lack of respect, he just wants to go out and play.

    "I like that in a player and hope that the premier league race is not over yet."

    Henry also admitted that although Arsenal are now second favourites for the title the club was not ready to concede defeat.

    "We still have games and I'm not a guy who likes to give up. I'm also at a club that doesn't give up," he said.

    "But we have put ourselves in a position where we have to look at other results to help us."
