    Hope you don't mind me saying that it really depends on your risk tolerance and investing goals. Darn, now I sound like one of those "financial planner" personalities. Nah, I'm not one of them, not to worry.

    But may I ask why you're looking into stocks? Trying to "invest"? To what goal? Beating inflation? Saving for a rainy day? Hoping to make a quick buck? (haha, but you'd be surprised, many forumers elsewhere have this attitude).

    If you are really, really conservative and cannot take the shock of your stock price falling 5%, 10%, (or, gasp, more!!!) in one day, then you might want to re-think your strategy.

    What about putting that money into really more stable instruments? Like money markets, bonds, or bond funds.

    Even blue-chips like SIA carry with them multiple types of risk. Hate to sound like a financial planner again, but here I go : you have to cope with company risk, which is specific to this particular company. And what about the risk to its particular industry? A 747-400 plane (touch wood) crashes somewhere totally unrelated and someone notices that SIA flies the same type of plane. Oops. And then there is the systemic risk, which happens when people generally panic and sell every stock they have, damn the consequences, and your particular stock suffers for no other reason than pure emotion - fear!
