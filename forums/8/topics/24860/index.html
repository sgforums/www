    now i think no one sells those kinda processors anymore, at least not new....

    now the most bang-for-buck processor is the athlonxp 1800+

    kan tang:
    i advise you to get the processor i mentioned above, and just get an ECS mainboard if you don't plan to overclock, cos ECS boards are very cheap, and very reliable. the board that i've been using for almost a year now is an ECS K7S5A.....very stable and no problems at all

    the ram, just get kingston or crucial, depending on the price. get a DVD drive, a Liteon CDRW drive, sound card up to you. i'm using my mainboard's onboard AC97 sound its good enough for me. (i personally advise you to not buy a sound card, and spend the money on a good set of speakers, not the altec lansing or creative "multimedia computer speakers". the sound of those speakers is really lousy. if you have a good hifi set, you can also just connect it to your comp via the aux input)

    the keyboard and mouse and peripherals you can decide yourself for the power supply unit, make sure you get at least 300w or better....350 or higher to be save

    for the monitor, if you're going to use your comp to play games or watch movies, i suggest getting a good conventional CRT monitor, like a sony (i'm using a sony 17" flatscreen). the LCD monitors still have problems, like fixed set of screen resolutions, laggy response times, and unless you get a very good (and expensive) one, the colour isn't going to very good, as compared to a sony CRT. the size of the monitor, up to you......depending on how much you want to spend. a sony 17" flatscreen should cost about 400 i think.

    for the graphics card, just get a nVidia geforce 4 ti4200....its the most worthit card now. don't get a geforce3 or geforce 4mx....not worthit.
    you can get a abit geforce 4 ti4200 64mb ddr for about $295 now i think.......the msi one costs $275.

    the hard drive, get brands like maxtor, western digital, but don't get brands like ibm or seagate.

    for the casing, up to you......see which one you like. and try to choose a full size atx casing, so you don't have problems upgrading or changing parts next time

    any more questions can just ask me
