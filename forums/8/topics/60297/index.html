    Vending machines in Japan get 'smart'

    DoCoMo set to put chips into cellphones so users can make coinless purchases

    By Kwan Weng Kin

    TOKYO - In Japan, canned drinks and chips are not the only items you can get out of a vending machine. Eggs and veggies can also be bought from your neigbourhood dispensers.

    INSTEAD OF USING COINS to buy items at vending machines, 45 million Japanese DoCoMo subscribers will be able to use their cellphones by the middle of next year. -- KWAN WENG KIN

    From these machines, you can also buy frozen foods, lunch boxes, magazines and fish bait, rent videos or have your passport photo taken.

    In some 'underground' locations, you can even get used underwear. Explore the sleazy backwaters of areas like Tokyo's Ikebukuro district, for example, and you might just come face to face with a machine that has such titillating items in stock.
