    Just to share my experience:-

    I did use my SA some yrs' back to buy a guarantted fund which earned minimum 5% returns, ie, very safe and sure-win. It has since matured and happily returned to my SA

    BUT, the funds are now stuck in SA; meaning my SA balance is now way beyond the minimum required n will remain there for good.

    Sorry to digress but some other "interesting" lessons I've learned abt my CPF fund are:-

    * Even though your company paid your CPF by the 14th of each month, it will be credited to your a/c only about 5 days later. For my case, the 19th. So on top of the 5 days' lost interest, you may have to ensure some lost investment opportunities during that period.

    * Interest on your CPF a/cs is based on the LOWEST balance of the month. Say if u happened to use all your OA balances on the last day of a month, the interest for that month will be ZERO! So plan your usage properly.
