    Some people are wondering why even govt sector or govt related are retrenching staff. To make things worse, retrenchment fees are not even paid in full. In the past, renched staff are supposed to be paid for the number of years served multiplied by the monthly salary. Eg. a worker who works in the company for 10 years and his current salary is $2000. His retrenchment package should be 10 x $2000. But lately, some govt sectors aren't practising that. Instead, they only multiply the number of years by half month salary only. This means that instead of 10 x $2000 in the above example, the worker is only paid 10 x $1000.

    Some of these organisation do not use the word "retrenchment". They try to make it sound nice by calling it "early retirement" for those 40 years old and above, and add a clause that implies "continuation of employment for this group of people is based on the work performance".
