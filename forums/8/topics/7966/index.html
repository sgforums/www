    HONEY APPLE CRISP

    6 cups apples (2 pounds)
    1 tablespoon lemon juice
    1/2 cup honey
    1/3 cup flour
    2/3 cup rolled oats
    1/2 cup brown sugar
    1/4 teaspoon salt
    1/3 cup butter

    {First peel and slice apples. Arrange in a greased baking dish. Sprinkle with lemon juice. Spread honey over top.

    {Next mix the flour, rolled oats, brown sugar and salt together. Cut in butter until mixture looks like coarse breadcrumbs. Sprinkle evenly over top of apples. )

    {At last bake at 375 degrees F. for 45 to 50 minutes, until the apples are cooked and the crust are nice and brown. Serve warm.

    { Enjoy... )

    Pears Roasted with Vanilla and Honey

    4 large ripe pears
    2 lemons, juiced
    1 vanilla bean
    1 cup pure honey
    1 cup dries white wine (or pear or apple juice)
    1/4 cup vanilla extract
    1/2 cup whipping cream (optional)
    2 teaspoons freshly ground coarse black pepper
    4 mint sprigs

    Pears: Preheat oven to 375 degrees. Peel pears, leaving stem intact. Rub pear generously with lemon juice to prevent browning. Cut open vanilla bean and scrape out seeds, reserving seeds. Cut bean into slivers. Make small cuts in the pears and insert the vanilla slivers halfway, creating pears bristling with vanilla quills. Place pears in baking dish; set aside. Add the honey, wine and vanilla extract to vanilla seeds and mix well. Pour over the pears. Bake on bottom rack of oven, basting pears every 10 minutes with honey mixture. Cook pears about 1 hour, until tender. Remove from oven; transfer pears to a platter, reserving roasting juices. Allow pears to cool.

    Sauce: Place roasting juices in a medium saucepan and brings to a boil. Boiling 3 to 5 minutes to thicken. If desired, slowly add whipping cream and cook until thick enough to coat the back of a spoon, about 6 minutes. Set aside. Heat pepper in a skillet, shaking pan constantly until pepper begins to smoke. Cool. Serve: Put each pear on serving plate. Place a sprig of mint next to the pear stem so it looks like leaves. Spoon warm sauce over pear. Sprinkle a little toasted pepper over pear and serve. Serves 4.

    {GARLIC SHRIMP AND STRINGRAY)

    1 ts Olive oil
    2 tb Slivered garlic
    1/2 ts Crushed red pepper flakes
    1/2 lb Lg shrimp, peeled, deveined
    1/2 lb Bay Stringray
    1/2 ts Paprika
    4 tb Chicken broth
    1 ts Fresh lime juice
    1/2 c Finely chopped fresh-italian parsley
    Salt and pepper

    In a large heavy skillet, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the garlic and saute until it begins to brown. Remove the garlic with a slotted spoon and set aside. Add the pepper flakes to the skillet and increase the heat to medium high. Add the garlic, shrimp, Stringray, and paprika. Saute for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly. Add the chicken broth and cook for 1 minute. Remove the shrimp and Stringray
    with a slotted spoon, place on a platter; set aside and keep warm. Add the lime juice, parsley, and salt and pepper to taste to the pan and just heat through. Pour the sauce over the shrimp and Stringray and serve immediately.
