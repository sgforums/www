    Seafood contains iodine. Iodine, when dissolved in extracellular fluid, will form iodide, which acts as a reducing agent which reduces Calcium ions to form Calcium metal. The decrease in concentration of calcium ions, which are needed as co-factors of thrombokinase which converts prothrombin to thrombin, will cause blood clotting to slow down due to the decrease in rate of formation of fibrin from fibrinogen.

    Originally posted by iaintmad:
    Does rubbing salt on wound help to heal it faster?
    Well, a salty environment makes it difficult for many microorganisms to survive as water is drawn out of their cells by osmosis. It may also reduce swelling by drawing out fluids from the affected area, however there's probably where the antiseptic properties is helpful.

    Salt will also cause the surrounding damaged tissues to turn hypertonic as the salt dissolves, causing pain and stings. This is due to one of the neuron receptor types that reacts to electrolyte concentration changes. On top of that, damaged tissues have more sensitive pain receptors which means it will not only be more painful, it may further damage the tissues and render them even more sensitive to pain.
