    Some facing indefinite wait for fibre links

    No fixed date given for home installation; OpenNet says only small number affected
    Published on Jan 16, 2012

    First, customers had to wait up to six weeks to get connected to Singapore's new ultra-fast fibre broadband network. Now, the wait has stretched even longer for some who have been given 'open dates'.

    Having an open date essentially means that no appointment has been fixed and the wait for one can be indefinite. The problem, previously confined to business users, is now affecting home subscribers.

    M1 customer Derrick Ho recently wrote about it on the telco's Facebook page. His initial appointment was on Nov 29 last year, but M1 told him his appointment had been rescheduled. As of last Friday, he was still waiting for a new date.

    M1 attributed the delay to OpenNet, the consortium building the fibre broadband network. 'The connection period for service is mainly governed by the availability of installation dates provided by OpenNet,' said an M1 spokesman. He declined to reveal how many of its customers have been given open dates.
