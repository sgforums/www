    If the girls that are in this world could be considered as grains of flour and a man had a sieve to trap only the big lumps while the finer grains fall thru then these big lumps would be the girls that he could choose from as a partner.

    The problem doesn't lie with the girls, or in this case, the lumps. No matter what, there will always be big lumps and small lumps. The problem lies with the size of the holes in the sieve.

    As a teenager going through many a crush and infatuation, it would seem that the holes of the young man's sieve are indeed small. Hence many a lump would remain behind on that sieve, leaving many choices for that young man. So he'll choose one (or more) of those lumps to be his partner.

    After a few relationships, he finds that the holes of his sieve get bigger. There just doesn't seem to be any lumps left behind for him to choose from. How can this be? Baffled, he begins to sieve through bags and bags of flour but rarely will a lump be big enough to stay in that sieve.

    When he finds that lump, you'll be sure he'll treasure it.

    It seems, the holes get bigger with relationship experience. He knows what he likes, wants and desires in a woman. Not that he's being critical, but that he knows better, compared to his teenage relationships, which woman will be a suitable life partner, which woman he will be able to be happy with and to also make her happy.

    How big is your sieve?
