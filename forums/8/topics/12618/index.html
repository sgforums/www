    an person dreams an average of 6 times a night..but ur conscious mind may not register or "acknowledge" all the dreams...

    according to Freud..dreams have both a manifest content (an apparent story line) and a latent content (the true meaning)...he suggested that the latent content provides a guide to a dreamer's unconscious..revealing unfulfilled wishes or desires...
    some psychologists opposed to this idea and suggested that the manifest content represents the true import of the dream...

    there is another theory called the "reverse-learning theory" which works on the fact tt dreams represent a process which unnecessary info is "unlearned" and removed from memory..according to this theory..dreams are meaningless...

    a theory which suggest the otherwise is known as the "dreams-as-survival theory", which suggests tt info relevant to daily survival is reconsidered and reprocessed in dreams...

    finally...the "activation-synthesis theory" proposes that dreams are a result of random electrical energy...this electrical energy randomly stimulates different memories..which are then woven into a coherent story line...
