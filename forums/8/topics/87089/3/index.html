    Originally posted by cooleo:
    This is how recuitment works> They throw away all resumes of non-grads.

    Do you or does your company do that?

    Well, it depends on the employers. I was told to hire an assistant. Even though I have a degree, I didn't look at qualifications at all when shortlisting the candidates. There were quite a number of dip holders even though we advertised for degree holders.

    My first round of shortlisting is based on whether there is photo or not. I think putting in the photo is just as important as the resume itself. It tells about who you are. But of course, I only chose those with proper photo (suitable for resume) and not those who pose in glamour shots for taking part in Star Search or something. Then I look at the expected salary. If the company is willing to pay only around $2000, no point looking at those who ask for $2800 or $3000. Even if they get the job, they won't last. Then the final screening is the job description. No point I get a high flyer MBA candidate who has not done corporate sales before or does not have any contacts.

    Eventually, the one that I selected (after interviewing a few degree holders and non degree holders) is a diploma holder. Her diploma is a non related field (I think it's nursing or something else totally not related to the job she was interviewed for). I chose her because of her achievement in her work and projects which she was able to present to me and my boss at the interview. She managed to make a few hundred thousand out of zero expenditure for her company. That's why I chose her. She is what I want.

    However, my boss selected another girl -- a degree holder who dressed well at the interview. Due to the nature of our works (having to meet lotsa corporate clients), my boss did emphasise a lot on appearances. He also didn't care if the candidate has a degree or not.

    I had no choice, but to tell the dip holder girl that she is not selected. I also told her that I would consider her first if there is a 2nd opening. But the girl selected by my boss whom we hired eventually didn't last long. She couldn't do what we were doing.

    I used to think qualification is important. But it only helped in getting the first job. I used to attach my certs, papers etc with my resume. But after getting the first job, my ex boss who hired me told me that he didn't pay attention to what qualification I was holding.

    After a few years of working, employers tend to ask what you do in your last job and what you can achieve. They seem to be only concerned about that. And I realised that I did the same when it was my turn to interview and select people.

    But of course, there are still a handful of employers who only look at the qualifications & certs in the first round of shortlisting. Like what I mentioned, it depends on the employers.
