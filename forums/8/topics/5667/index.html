    I think we have to see the tradition from which both philosophies come from. Western philosophy is responsible for a taxonomy of the sciences via Aristotle. As such the programme of critical questioning and emphasis on empiricism has a long a history as western philosophy itself.

    Then comes along Derrida in the 20th cent with his deconstructionism. now it seems meanings are unstable and language is unreliable, all of western philosophy is built unpon a very arbitrary logocentrism. The implications are horrific. Unstable meanings mean no real truths, only truisms. Creative readings of any text will reveal how some ideas signified by any binary system are 'privileged' over others. If it is correct to saying meaning is generated by the difference, then some differences will be given priority over others, whose meanings are 'deferred'. Meanings are inherently unstable and so will inevitably 'slip' when exchanged. There can be no 'presence' of stable meaning when communication takes place. So Derrida subverts any claims a philosopher might make about permanent truths somehow lying outside or beyond language.

    Hence, how do we know knowledge?
