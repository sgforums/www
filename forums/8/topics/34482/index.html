    http://photography.about.com/library/weekly/aa080502e.htm

    Get off centre

    Most of the time it makes a better picture to put the main subject to one side of the centre, rather than bang in the middle. If you are using a simple autofocus camera, this can sometimes cause a problem. Some cameras focus on the first pressure on the shutter release and allow you to move the camera to put the subject to one side before fully pressing the shutter. Some more expensive models have more complex focus systems that can focus off-center.

    Photographers sometimes talk about putting the subject on the 'thirds' - one third of the way across the frame from either side or from top of bottom. Putting the subject on the thirds both vertically and horizontally is sometimes referred to as using the 'intersection of thirds'. The idea of thirds comes from a slightly more complex idea in art, the 'golden section'.

    When photographing buildings, you can occasionally make use of symmetry, taking them from exactly in front of the centre of the building, but this tends to become boring. Often you will get better images working well to one side and thus showing the building as a three-dimensional object rather than just a facade.
