    I was still a kid during WWII. We lived downtown and after a frightening Japanese air raid many move to the kampongs. We move to somewhere near Siang Lim Temple at the end of Kim Keat Road (probably Lor 8 area). It was a rubber plantation and the family huddled together as we heard gun shots and bombs exploding.

    I was too young to understand danger. I ran to play one morning, pick up the dead angmo soldier's water bottles back to the kampong shed. My parents were so angry and I got whacked terok terok, the worst I ever been punished.
