Doggone babe magnets

    What sure way to attract girls than to have babies or doggies in tow, right? Well, only if you prefer poop to darling nosey aunties

    Marc Lim

    MOST bad ideas originate when you are either drunk or desperate. In my case, I was both during my cousin's wedding last year.

    Amid the free booze and endless questions from relatives about why 'Marc, the earliest to start dating' is still single, I decided enough was enough.

    No more dealing with nosey aunties. And no more offers of matchmaking me with 'this nice girl' they met the other day.

    I made it known, at least to the people at my table, that I was going to make a conscious effort to find a partner.

    Suddenly, everyone was an expert, especially from the 'we're so happily in love' couples.

    'No point trying to meet girls at clubs, they're probably not the marrying kind,' offered a friend's wife.

    'Also, don't fall for the myth that women you meet at the frozen food section are good catches,' said her husband. 'It only means they eat a lot.'

    In the end, they came up with the foolproof method, or so they claimed - babe magnets.

    Definition: an object which will help draw a woman's attention. And the table voted unanimously that babies and puppies were sure bets.

    Babies because they show that the guy is sensitive, caring and tender. And puppies because a guy with a dog implies that he is not afraid of commitment, and thus, won't shy away from a relationship.

    I took up the challenge.

    I mean, how hard could it be? Besides, I had two sure bets.

    As it turned out, babies would give me my first chance.

    My cousin from the United States had just flown into town with his American wife and their three-year-old son and nine-month-old daughter.

    His kids fit the bill of the perfect 'magnets' - Christopher is a talkative American-accented toddler and cute rosy-cheeked Trina has a smile to light up any would-be partner's face.

    I offered to babysit them for the day which I had all planned out. Lunch at McDonald's at East Coast Parkway, followed by a walk along the beach.

    I picked them up, popped Barney into the car stereo and we were set.

    As luck would have it, we rolled into a parking lot just as two 20-something women were getting out of their car. I made my move.

    Christopher opened the door and got out, but not before I offered a very audible 'say excuse me and be careful not to hit the lady'.

    'Excuse me,' said Christopher in the cutest three-year-old voice possible. It worked like a charm and it got their attention.

    'Oh what a cute boy!' squealed the woman who emerged from the passenger seat. 'How old is he?'

    'Three,' I answered as I eased Trina into the stroller. 'And this is his sister Trina.'

    'How precious! Can we touch her?' asked the other.

    'Sure,' I replied as I rolled the stroller towards them.

    Just as they were about to make contact, the stroller snapped shut, with Trina in it. How was I to know that you need to lock these darn things open before they are ready to go?

    Trina screamed her head off as I struggled to retrieve her and, needless to say, I blew my chances with the two women.

    There was no way they were going to hang around a wailing baby, a shocked three-year-old and a 27-year-old moron.

    French fries and ice cream came to my aid and it was not long before the children were normal again.

    But I did not account for poop. Either I must have freaked out poor Trina or she is lactose-intolerant because, boy, did she go.

    I thought I was prepared, armed with extra diapers and baby wipes, but no amount of army training could have prepared me for what I saw.

    I re-introduced myself to Barney, and sent them home.

    So much for babies.

    But as it turned out, dogs did not offer too much reprieve either.

    As I did not even make it to the beach my last time out, I thought I would head back to East Coast Park.

    I wanted to portray a macho image so I arranged to pick up Chuck, my friend's yellow labrador, at the beach and promised to send him home.

    Chuck proved a much better option than babies and within an hour of jogging with him - and picking up his poop - I had met three fellow 'dog lovers' and even obtained the telephone number of a cocker spaniel's cute owner.

    Things were going great until, during the drive back, Chuck decided that he had hidden a bone in my car's back seat.

    He started digging at the leather seats, leaving deep scratches with each rapid paw movement.

    I turned to scream at him, neglected to watch the road and bumped into the car in front of me. It was not a big accident, but one nonetheless.

    The woman with the cocker spaniel did call, but she suggested a foursome date - us and the two dogs.

    At that point, I was not exactly looking forward to taking Chuck out again, so I passed.

    I suppose the wedding table guests were right. Babies and dogs are indeed babe magnets, but I don't think I have the patience to put up with either one for the time being, even if it is just to meet women.

    So here I am, still single, but at least I can eat at McDonald's in peace and have an intact back seat.

    As for finding a partner, I have left it to the age-old method that is even better than babe magnets.

    Nosey aunties.
