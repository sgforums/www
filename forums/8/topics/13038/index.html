    Poly vs. JC.

    Poly is considered as tertiary education while JC is not. A primary objective of the poly path is to prepare one for a career, not further studies. That is why where jobs are concerned, poly students have an edge.
    But that does not mean poly students are superior academically.
    There is no way to compare A levels results and poly results. An 'A' levels diploma is internationally recognized while a poly diploma is not, although a poly diploma has an advantage in the industry.
    On the other hand, the strength of the poly path is also its weakness. Study materials are often cut and trimmed for the poly student's specific specialization. This is not good for a student with further education as a primary objective. In contrast, an A level student may be much stronger in foundation.
    As an example, try comparing the standard of math between a poly engineering student and a jc student who took F maths, or maybe even just C. Again, compare a typical poly student majoring in business with a JC student who took C Maths.
