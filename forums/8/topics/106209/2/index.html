    SMRT Corp lays off 21 employees

    Transport group SMRT Corp has laid off 21 employees from various deaprtment, it said yesterday. SMRT's executive vice-president for corporate services, Mr Yeo Meng Hin, said in response to queries that the retrenchment exercise on Tuesday came after a review of SMRT's business in a bid to be "more efficent and drive greater value".

    He added: "Letting go of staff was a last - and painful - decision we took after a much careful delibration."

    The transport giant declined to reveal which departments the workers were from or details of their severance pay, but said that affected workers had been given a retrenchment package "in line with market practice".

    They also received additional medical, dental and transport benefits and had been matched with employment agencies to help them find new jobs.

    SMRT has also arranged for those interested to join the company as taxi drivers.

    The news comes more than a month after SMRT announced net earnings of $81.3 million for the six months ending Sept 30. - Straits Times
