    I think I've mentioned something like this before, that I truly believe that having your dad and mom living under the same roof doesn't necessarily make an ideal, happy family. What matters most in any family is love, whether it's that from a single parent or both parents. And if you don't have that, just remember to love yourself as an individual and not waste away just because you think no one cares.

    Having a child is never meant to be a fun-filled, trivial affair and all that is clearly emphasised in the labour of childbirth. Before you actually have a baby, you'll have to ask, if you're financially stable, if you're ready for it, and is your partner ready for it, mentally and spiritually, whether or not you're engaged or married. You'll have to clearly weigh the pros and the cons, to understand your circumstances and be wary of the consequences, even though you may be the compassionate softie who believes that ridding of the growing foetus equals killing off a part of you.

    Then there is the question of social ethics. But would you rather have a child out of wedlock, or to deprive an innocent life a chance to live out in this world? Which is the lesser of two evils? Would your rather bear the brunt of gossip or suffer the backbreaking guilt of killing your own flesh and blood? Of course, the ultimate ethical advice is not to get yourself pregnant until you're married. But that's certainly not the kind of preaching you'd like to hear if you happen already to have that little life in you. I'd say, if you love your child, if you're financially stable, if you're ready for it, just go for it. And it's good if you have a boyfriend who still cares for you. If he's the one rushing through the marriage plans, consider yourself blessed. And if you get ditched, go on, shed your tears. But life still goes on and that little life in you needs to go on. If it's anything you should be wiser now.

    You really can't stop tongues from wagging. And if a couple gets married just so they can avoid verbal lashings is going to be grievously disappointed. People are still going to talk about you. I mean you could've done some other things, bad things, and you'd still get people talking about you. Why bother? Is reputation all that matters? Certainly not. It's happiness. And if you decide you're going to be sad just because you're on the tip of everyone's tongue, you're going to have a hard time pleasing the whole world, 'cos you can never please everyone all the time.

    Of course this may seem like bullshit to you, like you heard it a million times and that the bitter, blatant and prevailing truth is that everyone cares about their reputation you know... but try to get an alternative viewpoint, reach out beyond the superficial. If you don't care, that's when you realise there are people who don't care either and they live much happier lives. They got lambasted, were terribly misconstrued but they knew better not to build their hopes and dreams on what others think of them, and when they got dissed and flamed, it's only a matter of getting used to. They did not see themselves as mutants, as fools as a disgrace, just because of what people say or think about them. Different yes, but never warped, inferior or undeserving of a life. Almost like a curse, there's always this temptation to really be full of ourselves, to prove ourselves and conform to other people's expectations. But you'll never know -- your most critical opponents may just conform to your expectations just because you believe strongly in who you are -- that just 'cos if you're an outcast doesn't mean you don't belong to society. That wields power and respect, more than you can ever wish for if you were mindlessly copying from others the way to handle unplanned pregnancies, et al. If the Manchus, considered barbaric by the Han Chinese, can make half a billion of their southern neighbours wear pigtails, you can jolly well do something out of the blue, get condemned, and eventually get accepted by the same people who damned you to their last breath, at the first place.
