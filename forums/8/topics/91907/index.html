    How do I tell if a piece of jade is fake?

    And how do I differentiate between a good piece of jade and a mediocre one?
    I buy jade pieces occasionally and after some research and asking around, I gathered a few experiences :

    1) Buy from a reputable Jade shop. The one in Singapore that I go is Li Hong.
    2) Never buy a new jade piece with a crack. The first piece I bought in China has some minor cracks. The salesgirl actually said that jadeite is a natural product thus cracklines are inevitable ...to some extent yes, but a good master in carving jade knows how to make use of cracks and avoid making it visible. So if can see cracks, its either an antique piece or the sculptor sucks. Otherwise if you feel that it's fated to be with you, then negotiate until they sell it to you at a cheaper price.
    3) Usually fake jade is commonly the green ones, cos they fetch better price. I wont say fake jade is not jade, just that the unscrupulous pple use lower price jade, break down the strength of the jade and dip into coloring and make it look like expensive jade. Such are called B or C grade, depending on the methods used. The real naturally good ones are call A grade.
    4) If you are really interested in getting a good one, DON'T ever go to common jewellery shops. They usually sell B grade to get better price and such jade pieces might fade in color in time. If they can give u certificate for those really expensive pieces then it's ok. (should be true in Singapore, China no garantee)
    5) about recognising a good one, the color and transparency is important. Size does not matter at all. One factor is also your instincts, must be pleasing to your eyes....beauty is in the eyes of the beholder...(does not apply to white burmese jade with absolute transparency, you might as well buy diamond or crystal)

    Have fun learning,
