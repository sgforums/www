    SINGAPORE : A giant lantern is being built in Singapore's Chinatown as part of this year's Mid-Autumn Festival celebrations.

    The S$80,000 lantern, at about 14 metres tall and 10 metres in diameter, is all set to break the world record for the biggest lantern, which was built in India six years ago.

    Chinatown is getting all decked out for the Mid-Autumn Festival.

    But besides the dragon decorations, mooncakes and pomelos, a giant lantern will also be adding to the festivities.

    The lantern will be two metres taller and three metres wider than the world's biggest lantern built in India in 1998.

    Due to its size, it is being built in five parts and assembled in Chinatown.

    But before the four-tonne lantern can snatch the world record away from India, it has to be hoisted off the ground by a crane.

    The lantern will be on display in the open field between South Bridge Road and Banda Street from September 18 for one month. - CNA
