    I hate to say it because I enjoyed the tension and the emotion of the flick, but the more I think about it, the more it falls apart for me.

    Leaving questions unanswered is fine by me in some instances. And I certainly can make certain logical conclusions when given evidence. But we shouldn't let directors/writers off the hook so easy by allowing them the cop-out of "use your imagination." It's lazy story-telling.

    If you watch the DVD and watch the alternate ending where Jim dies and the women grab the guns, that's a good example of where you don't have all your questions answered, and have to draw your own conclusions. But the soldiers just assuming that the zombies will eventually starve, without ever explaining why they would make this assumption, or why that's exactly what ultimately happened makes little sense to me.
    Ok, I went and saw 28 Days Later for the first time tonight, and it had the alternate ending after the credits. So for those who are curious about the alternate ending, but don't feel like going to the theatre again, I will attempt to explain it to you now. Ok, in the orginal ending, Jim gets shot and Selena (is that the right name?) and Hannah drive off, and then you see flashbacks to some hospital, then Jim wakes up and he is in a bed in the countryside. Well, the alternate ending starts up right after Jim gets shot, then you have the dramatic driving away scene, then we actually see the hospital scene, Hannah frantically pushes Jim down the hall in a stretcher, while Selena tries to find some medical supplies to help him. The girls try to resescatate Jim, as Selena screas something very close to "Live damn you! Live!" But alas, Jim does not come to and dies on the hospital table. Hannah asks Selena "What are we going to do now?" and Selena answers "Keep moving" And the two walk out of the hospital together....fade to black. So, basically they worked in a happy ending and a sad ending. Take your pick.
