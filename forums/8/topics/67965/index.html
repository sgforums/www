    High alert on new WORM virus. Please do not open emails with suspicious Subject and Attachments.

    There is a new virus, Novarg (or Mydoom), that is spreading very quickly. The means of spreading is similar to Beagle virus.

    How does it spread ?
    It masquerades and sends out infected emails using any email address found in your computer.

    From: random email address

    Subject: (Varies, such as)

    -Error
    -Status
    -Server Report
    -Mail Transaction Failed
    -Mail Delivery System
    -hello
    -hi

    Body: (Varies, such as)

    -The message cannot be represented in 7-bit ASCII encoding and has been sent as a binary attachment.
    -The message contains Unicode characters and has been sent as a binary attachment.
    -Mail transaction failed. Partial message is available.

    Attachment: usuall a zip file (22,528 bytes)

    Attachment file names: (Varies, such as)
    -body
    -message
    -test
    -data
    -file
    -text
    -doc
    -readme
    -document

    Because of this masquerading nature, you may receive an infected mail seemingly to come from a colleague or friend or be questioned why you send the infected mail.

    What you should do ?
    Delete such mail.
    Do not open any suspicious attachments.
    Seek help from your Helpdesk if necessary.

    What should I do if I have already open the attachment
    Shut down your PC.
    Inform Helpdesk immediately for assistance.
