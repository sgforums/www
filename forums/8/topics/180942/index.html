    Of course there will be jobs for fresh grads. How else will fresh grads break into the job market. Problem now these past few years is, how do you keep your job

    I've been in business for a while now and will only hire people with the right attitude. Whosoever thinks that having the qualifications for the job will help them keep their job is seriously in need of a brain scan

    The key to survival is constantly be on your toes, not be lazy, learn as much as you can and don't rely too much on what you studied(unless you are going to be a researcher or academic). Key is to be street smarts.

    Always ask yourself, what do I have to do to make myself different from the rest and what can I offer that most others cannot. It can be a particular skill, a gift for closing deals, or contacts in an industry. SO throw away your books after you graduate.

    As I always ask an interviewee...What do you have that I want and not simply because you have a cert.

    Remember, Singapore may produce hundreds of grads every year but China and India combined produce tens of thousands of grads every year and so if they are going to cost cheaper to hire and have more hunger, I will hire them and not some local grad who will even debate with me over benefits and entitlements...

    Be Afraid!!! Be very afraid of China & India
