    I dont see the light in not becoming a specialist.

    A specialist is an important bridge between the officer and his men. And a specialist is usually given important roles. However, the initial training may be harsh, but essential to strengthen the force. It should not be taken lightly, though most of the time it was misunderstood.

    If you think you're a role model for fellow friends and extremely helpful, going for sispec might be a good option as you'll learn to exercise these abilities.

    Moreover, it enhances your chance of application for a local University.

    Note: NUS and NTU takes into account of your NS liabilities, and your CCAs. If you had not had any CCAs, you must work hard to at least obtain a specialist or officer position. Being a men of corporal rank gives u 0 advantage when you apply for your university. And if you ord with only lance corporal or lower rank, it is a disadvantage to your application.
