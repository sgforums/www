    The process of charging an officer and a specialist are both different. The process of charging a specialist and a man is also different.

    To have an officer (2LT/LTA) to stand trial for an offence, an investigation led by the CO and it requires the highest commanding officer's approval within a Base/Camp. (CPT and above require even more severe investigation, usually MSD)

    To have a specialist to stand trial for an offence, an investigation lead by OC and it requires the CO's approval

    To have a man to stand trial for an offence, an investigation led by a PC and it requires the CO's approval.

    To strip an officer of his/her rank, the officer must first be guilty of a chargable offence, (therefore had already stand trial and found guilty by court martial)

    To strip a sergeant of his/her rank, the specialist must first be found guilty of several offences and approval by S1 and CO (may be minor/petty offences - which can still be chargable offences if submitted to court martial)

    To strip a man of his/her rank, the man must first found guilty of a petty offence and approval by S1 and CO.

    Note: Petty offences are chargable offences that may be punished with extras, SOL.
