    I'm quite surprised that there are people still using CD players in Singapore, Year 2008, though you may be forgiven if you belong to one of the older generations.

    The number of songs that can be stored in a MP3* device depends on mainly these two factors:

    1. The quality of the music files (which is proportional to the filesize)
    2. The storage capacity of the device

    *MP3, in general context, refers to a music data file; if you really want to know what does it mean, Google and Wikipedia is always there for you

    The short answer to your second question, I would say 100 near CD-quality MP3s per GB of storage available on the device.

    What sort of connection are you referring to? An internet connection?

    I'm not a iFan, so I'm not sure with the specifics of purchasing a tune from iTunes store and stuff like that, but if you don't have PayPal funds, you can always trade them with SGD via bank transfers with those funds sellers.
