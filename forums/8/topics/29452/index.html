    NOV 14, 2002
    Get shirty
    Two homegrown shirtmakers have opened new outlets. The market is believed to be worth at least $50 million, and still growing
    By Lionel Seah
    DON'T scoff at the business shirt even if it is worn by only men from nine to five on a normal work week. The starched apparel does a good, brisk trade here.
    In just under a week, two homegrown business shirt labels have opened new outlets.
    Singapore shirt specialist CYC, which was established in 1935, opened its second custom-made boutique over the weekend at Republic Plaza 2.
    Meanwhile, newcomer Raoul, a month-old new label from high-fashion retailer FJ Benjamin, will also open its second outlet at Hitachi Towers tomorrow.
    In addition, French label Alain Figaret is on the lookout for a location for its third outlet along the Orchard Road belt.
    Ms Fong Loo Fern, the managing director of CYC, says: 'Men here don't wear suits. So shirts are one of the most important items in a man's wardrobe in terms of appearance. And men do invest in well-made ones.'
    At CYC, which shifted its focus from selling mass-produced shirts to custom-made ones in 1994, more than 1,000 pieces of men's tailored shirts are sold a month.
    Each shirt, which can take up to a week to produce, is priced from $75 onwards.
    Customers choose from more than 1,000 kinds of fabrics, 60 different collars and 10 different cuffs at the Raffles Hotel Arcade and Republic Plaza 2 branches.
    Ms Fong declined to comment on annual turnover, and would only say it is 'in the millions' and that it has grown at least 10 per cent since 2000.
    She is confident the market, believed by insiders to be worth at least $50 million a year, will expand.
    Ms Nancy Goh, the retail manager for Alain Figaret, agrees, adding that the brand has been experiencing double digit growth in annual turnover since its launch here in 1996.
    Figaret shirts are priced between $169 and $189.
    Its two boutiques at Millenia Walk and City Link Mall get about 70 male customers a week each, and each buys, on average, three shirts at one go.
    Male customers are also getting much younger.
    Where they used to be at least 25 years and above in 1996, 18- to 25-year-olds now form about five per cent of the regular male clientele for CYC and Figaret.
    Mr Douglas Benjamin, CEO of FJ Benjamin and the man behind the Raoul label launched last month at Millenia Walk, adds: 'There is a gap in the market for well-made, fashionable business shirts at affordable prices.
    'And this is what men want - especially the well-read and fashionable ones who are in their first jobs.'
    Raoul, which aims to appeal to top executives and first-timers alike, seems to have identified that niche in the business shirt market.
    Its 800-sq ft Millenia Walk boutique gets about 210 customers a week who buy between one and four shirts. These are priced between $59 and $109.
    After opening Raoul at Hitachi Towers, FJ Benjamin intends to open two more within the next eight to nine months.
    Guess there's no business like shirt business.
