    ***** EXTRACTED FROM THE STRAITS TIME, 30 MARCH 2002 *****
    THE border between Singapore and Malaysia was once an imaginary line in the waters of the Johor Straits.

    That was until the neighbours put it right in 1995.

    The move took 15 years and seven rounds of negotiations, but on Aug 7, 1995, Foreign Minister S. Jayakumar (left) and his Malaysian counterpart then, Datuk Abdullah Badawi (right), signed the agreement to fix the boundary at precise geographical coordinates.

    Originally, the 1927 Straits Settlements and Johor Territorial Waters Agreement had the line 'following the centre of the deep-water channel in Johor Strait'.

    But in the 1970s, the deep-water channel - essential for ships' safe passage - moved, and there was land reclamation on both sides, complicating the matter of the boundary.

    Starting in 1982, there were joint studies to determine the precise longitude and latitude coordinates for the new border. Several rounds of negotiations finally resulted in the permanent line.

    The border was also deemed final, and it would not be affected by any future development, including land reclamation.
