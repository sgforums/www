Science has proven that this in fact, isn't the case. The double-slit experiment seems to defy what we consider reality. When an atom is given the option of more than one path it acts as a wave. It seems impossible, even after faced with the test results but it's one way to show that the reality is far different from the human perception. But it gets odder than this. When we put up a detector to try to catch the atom acting as a wave it acts as a localized particle, it's almost as if nature doesn't want us to catch her in the act. When the detector is turned off it again acts as a wave with interference patterns. Okay, by now you may be asking what does this have to do with the question asked but it has a lot to do with it. I could list numerous examples of scientific data that doesn't seem right, that doesn't seem to add up with common sense but in all truth, human common sense isn't always correct. So if reality defies common sense, then maybe the answer to this defies common sense.
