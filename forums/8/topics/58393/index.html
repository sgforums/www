800 km run

  • silverboy
    1 Oct 03, 10:29
    FIFTEEN years ago in Australia, he had to fight hard to get his daughter into a mainstream school because she had a mild form of cerebral palsy.
    .
    Today, 20-year-old Neeka is studying art at an Australian university. Her father, former S-League coach David O'Connor, now wants to raise awareness in Singapore about the brain disorder that affects muscle control and coordination.
    .
    Next February, he will run 800km, from Singapore to Kuala Lumpur and back, to raise $1 million for the Spastic Children's Association of Singapore (Scas).
    .
    Awareness of cerebral palsy in Singa-pore is so poor that it took Mr O'Connor, a football coach for school children here, a year to locate the association.
    .
    It was this newspaper's report about the Spastic Children's Association School that gave Mr O'Connor, 53, his contact. The money raised will help make the school's 225 children self-reliant.
    .
    "You go to a supermarket in Australia and you'll see people with cerebral palsy riding around in motorised wheelchairs taking stuff off the shelves. They're part of society and that is what we want to achieve here," he said.
    .
    Mr Edmund Wan, executive director of the Scas, which oversees the school and a sheltered workshop, said the cost of motorised wheelchairs ranges between $3,000 and $6,000, some 10 times more than standard wheelchairs. The school only has two motorised wheelchairs for training and one used by a student.
    .
    Mr Wan added that while new buildings in Singapore may have wheelchair-friendly features, the Singapore environment generally does not allow the disabled to move around easily.
    .
    Mr O'Connor plans to complete his run in two weeks. A six-person support team will trail him in two vehicles.
    .
    "This will be a hard run," he said. "Once I start it, I will finish it. I want it to be hard because I want people to think I'm a little crazy Â… People will then say: 'If you're that crazy, we'll support you.'"
    .
    He invites runners to join him in a one-day charity run at Bukit Gombak stadium a week before his solo outing. For more details, e-mail johntaylor@pacific.net.sg.
    .
    Donations can be made to David O'Connor's Charity Solo Run, Bukit Batok Central Post Office, PO Box 340, Singapore 916512.
