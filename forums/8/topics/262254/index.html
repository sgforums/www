    Originally posted by sneezy:
    So Chiba is consider part of Tokyo, right? I m quite confuse where is belong to Tokyo.
    Chiba is to the west of Tokyo. Tokyo is a perfecture and a city of its own, on par as a perfecture like Chiba.

    However, you're right in that the Greater Tokyo Area refers to the metropolitan area of Tokyo as well as neighbouring suburban areas, where many people commute to Tokyo for work daily. To the east of Tokyo is Chiba, west is Saitama, to the south is Kanagawa.

    The airport is located within Chiba, that's how far it is away from Tokyo itself. After Narita Airport and Narita station, N'EX will stop at Chiba station before running express down to Tokyo proper. From Chiba station you can visit the Chiba Urban Monorail, the world's longest suspended monorail system. It's scary! Beyond that it is essentially a suburban area.

    On Saitama side, you can visit Tama town (home of Hello Kitty), Studio Ghibili etc. But it is also essentially an suburban area.

    Kanagawa has Yokohama, which was the base of foreign trade in the 19th century. It has more colonial buildings than other parts of Tokyo, with some recent modern development (Minato Mirai 21), and also the Yokohama Chinatown.

    All these places are at least half an hour's journey by train from the Yamanote Line.

    Originally posted by sneezy:
    got.. one more question. What card can be use to withdraw money on their atm machine? Does they consider the PLUS sign also?
    I don't recommend you withdraw cash there at all, unless you're on Citibank, which has branches there. PLUS won't work there. Japan is still a strongly cash-based society and even if you have a card, ATM's do not run 24-hours, usually only when the post office is open.

    In fact, the only ways I use a card overseas is to swipe for purchases and hotel stays. It's the most fool proof way to leave your cash for daily usage.

    And don't worry, everyone there is used to carrying a few ten thousands out, but you should worry about your bringing cash out unless you plan to step into red light districts like Kabukicho. Otherwise Japan is a very safe country, much like Singapore, just have the normal precautions and some vigilance.
