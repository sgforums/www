    Note that when they say can play CDR/RW. That does not mean all CDR/RW. I tried a few different CDR. Not all are good enough. If you get the cheaper one, chances are you cannot play. But there is always exception. I got one at 50cent and it works as good as the $1 one. Also can play CDR does not mean you just burn the audio just like that. The software you use is also important. Use the more popular one like Adaptec or Nero. They are more reliable. Also can play CDR does not mean you can burn Mp3 or wav format audio into the disk to play. The player cannot do it unless they say so. You need to convert the MP3 and wav format to the audio track form so that you can play in your player. That's all. Hope it is of help to you.
