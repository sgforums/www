    Home > News > Story Sep 23, 2007
    122 hit by lottery scams this year

    Victims lost a total of $1.5 million to fraudsters; police advise public to be more wary of such ruses
    By Selina Lum

    MORE than 100 people have been conned of a total of $1.5 million in lottery or lucky draw scams so far this year.
    The smallest amount was $6 and the highest $353,000, said the police, which released a statement warning the public about such scams.

    The 122 victims were duped into handing over money after receiving phone calls or SMSes claiming that they had won prizes in overseas lucky draws.

    Since January, the police have received nearly 300 reports of such scams.

    As such ruses take different forms and the tricksters change tactics regularly, the public should be careful, said the police.

    The bottom line: If anything sounds too good to be true, it probably is.

    This is how a scam typically works: A call would be made to the victim's mobile phone.

    The caller, claiming to be a staff member of an overseas company, would pretend to be conducting a survey or to invite the victim to attend an event abroad. A few days later, the victim would get a phone call from the company again - this time informing him that he had won a lucky draw entered on his behalf.

    Besides phone calls, another tactic used by the tricksters is to send SMSes to the victims' mobile phones, notifying them to call an overseas number to claim their 'prizes'.

    But to claim their winnings, the victims are asked to make upfront payments of 'administrative' or 'processing' fees via remittance companies to China.

    In some cases, local accounts were also used.

    To make their claims look credible, the swindlers may even provide the victims with login IDs and website addresses of supposedly reputable overseas firms.

    Inevitably, the victims who fall for the ruse would later find that there are no such lottery winnings.

    After pocketing their money, the fraudsters would either make up more stories seeking further payment or just abscond with what they have collected.

    The police said they take a stern view of such cases and have been working with overseas agencies to nab the culprits.

    Since 2005, at least six people involved in get-rich-quick scams have been caught and jailed for 14 months to three years.

    Anyone with information on these scams can call the Commercial Affairs Department on 1800-325-0000, or go to any police station.
