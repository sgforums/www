    Some figures are my own calculations. Full and unedited figures at http://www.nkfs.org/finance%20data.htm

    2003 Figures ($ in millions, unless otherwise stated; corrected to the nearest decimal point)

    Donations: 67.4 m
    Fund raising costs: (16.6m)
    Effective donation: 50.8 m

    Other income(assumed to be dialysis fees paid by patients since nowhere else states the equivalent) = 25.3m
    Direct charitable expenses: (36.2m)
    Net charitable expense: (10.9m)

    Investment gain: 7.6m

    Operating/Admin costs: (8.1m)
    PR: (0.6m)
    Capital Expenses(the cost of gold tap/toilet bowl/other extravagance may come under this): (4.7m)
    Total costs unrelated to fund raising: (13.4m)

    Net expense unrelated to fundraising and donations: (16.7m)

    Surplus/(Deficit): 34.2m

    ----

    Assets:

    Land and Building: 21.5m
    Tangible Assets (ie dialysis machines): 12m
    Total Capital Assets: 33.5m

    Investments: 172.8m
    Inventories: 1.2m
    Accounts receivables: 9.5m
    Cash and Deposits: 14.9m
    Total Non-capital Assets (can be converted into cash without affecting current operations): 198.4m

    Total Assets: 232m

    Total non-charitable liabilities: 11.5m

    Net non-capital assets: 186.8m

    Reserves for charitable causes: 220.5m

    ---

    Key facts:

    Estimated reserve for 2004 (assuming 40m in surplus, taking account into previous yearÂ’s increase): 260m

    Years it can last without donation cum fund-raising activities: 260/16.7 = 15.6 years.

    Assuming NKF cuts all fund-raising activities, while donations drop to 1 /4, NKF still breaks even. (1/4 of 67.4m donation = 16.8m; Net expense minus fund-raising activities = 16.7m)

    Income from investments could feed 3 /4 of dialysis cost. (investment income = 7.6m; Net expense to patients = 10.9m)

    Bulk of NKF expenses actually come from fund-raising activities. Minus that, percentage of beneficiaries could easily be above 75%. (67.4m donations. 13.4m in running expenses.

    Running expenses(include salaries, perks, bonuses, mercedez, chauffeur, gold taps, etc) is actually more than amount spent on beneficiaries. (13.4m on cost of running; 10.9m on patients)
