    Yes, that could work too. I do that all the time at the corridor outside our flat if my wife and I are craving for a small barbie. But we prop the tray up with bricks, else the tray would be too low and we'd end up with a back ache hunching over the 'pit' the whole evening or we'd be kicking dirt onto our food.

     

    Also to note - get the Reynolds ones, not some Chop Nam Huat brand from the market. They cost more, but the bottom are less likely to give way from the heat of the red-hot charcoal.
