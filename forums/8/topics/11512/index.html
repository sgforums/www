  • Silenthunter
    19 Apr 01, 23:55
    controversay is actually right about the squatting thing. Another thing that will make you a PES B is flat feet. Any medical history that you have (eg, injured limbs, asthma, operations) but are fully recovered will also get you to PES B.

    PES C is for those who are permenantly injured or are currently having medical problems (asthma attacks, epilepsy, heart problems,etc)

    PES A is for all fit men with minor or no medical history. But even if you wear specs...you can still be a PES A. (like me....until now still puzzled):/

    Aiyah....in army who care about PES status. It's the Attend status that counts:
    Attend B - Light dutys, no need to attend any physical training or duties.
    Attend C - Same as MC, go home or Sickbay
    Excused - Normal duties but exempted or specific activities.
    examples of common excuse:
    1)excused running/marching/jumping/squatting/lower limbs(leg injury?)
    2)excused boots/footwear (foot rot or fractures?)
    3)excused No4 (skin rashes or infections?)
    4)excused pushup/chinup/upper limbs (arm injury?)

    Unless you are the Super Duper Sissy Spoilt Cao Keng type(stereotyping but it usually true) who scared to be dirtied in combat vocation, want to be LOBO personnel or trying to be exempted from army then you can tried to keng for Pes C, E or F.
