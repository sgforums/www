    "You use most of your fingertips to pluck, the nails are there to protect your fingers when you strum."

    Actually, the string is supposed to sort of "slide" across the nail, to produce a clearer, and IMO better, sound than simply using the flesh of your fingertips. They're not really there for "protection." I never heard of anyone getting injured from plucking or strumming with the flesh of his fingertips.

    "And having long nails on your left hand is not a good thing as it would be hard to press down on the frets."

    um...I don't know of ANY guitarist who has long nails on his LEFT hand (or opposite, if he's left-handed and using a left-handed guitar). I thought it was pretty obvious that we guitarists were referring to the nails on the right hand...

    Obviously, if it's too long it'll get in the way (hitting wrong strings, etc.). Too short and it's hard for your nail to "connect" with the strings. The "optimum" length depends on individual, lah.
