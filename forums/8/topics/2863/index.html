    Just some suggestions:

    1. A permanent solution for mp3 files and such would be aquiring a CD-RW drive. The price ranges from $250 to $400+. It may be expensive but its a good long term investment.Furthermore, an external USB port based CD-RW would be easier to setup compared to a slave HD. No mess and you can store all your mp3 files into CDs.

    2. Get a zip drive. It costs $140+ for an Parallel/USB Iomega zip drive. You can use the zip drive to store your irreplaceable files. However most will not recommend a zip disk as a permanent storage medium.

    3. Add a new HD to your existing hardware. Transfer all the files to your new slave HD. However its tedious to install... You would have to open up your CPU casing screw everything in and set the jumpers. A dirty job.

    4. You should backup your files with the above methods before you replace the HD under waranty because if the company replaces your HD... You would lose all the information in it?

    5. Check for virus in your applications. eg. Maybe you icq program is infact a trojan virus? Unlikely though. You can get a free copy of anti-virus program at http://www.avp.ch to test your files.

    6. If any of your files lies on a bad sector and cannot be duplicated, you can use Norton Utilities to move the data away from the badsector through a HD scan. This will make your data readable again. You can get Norton Utilies from Sim Lim Sq. There maybe other applications that does the samething, however I haven't had the need to use such a program and hence I apologise for not being aware.

    7. Lastly, just some hypothesis about your HD problems. If your HD is placed near your CD-ROM, the frequent jerk of your CD-ROM may in the long term damage your HD. Is your computer placed in an air-conditioned room? Over heating of hardware in your CPU can result in damage. Is your room dusty? Microscopic dust particles can choke up your HD. Ok, I don't think I can think of anymore reasons at the moment.

    I love my 2 gig Seagate HD of 4 yrs. It doesn't have any badsectors at all and its quieter and more reliable then my Qauntum 10 gig. Maybe this has something to do with Fat16 and Fat32 handling of drive space. Seagate is alright as a brand.

    As for the OS, there is no documentation of Win98 hurting any HD that I am aware of. I think Win98 is a reliable enough OS. After all its the last of dos based Windows operating system. I won't recommend WinNT/Win2000 to anyone just yet as it is not as well supported at the moment by application developers. Furthermore I think Win95/98 hardware drivers are different from that of WinNT/2000. Its a hassle a normal user don't really need. For those that matters, the ability to run dos based programs is also a plus for the nostalgic quality. I tend to have the urge to run old games to have a run.
