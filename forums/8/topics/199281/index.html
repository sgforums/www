  • Oh Maria

    Thinking more slowly; inability to stay focused on a task at hand; occasionally realizing you don't remember how you got from point A to point B; sitting somewhere (maybe outside gazing at a tree) discovering two hours have passed, feeling as though only a few minutes have passed; finding yourself with a loss of words in the middle of a conversation.
    This can appear 'funny' at times but other times depending on your work or activities can lead to feeling in the dumps, listening, speed, memory & accuracy are also affected.

    If u are suffering form the above. U may be having Brain Fog.

    Nobody knows what causes brain fog but excess nicotine, alcoholism, drug use have been stongly linked to Brain Fog. Psychological factors such as depression and lack of sleep have also been suspected to cause it. Usuallyl Brain fog is linked to other conditions such as anemia and nutrinional defieciencies

    Brain fog is very common. It affects thousands of people, children as well as adults. It contributes to school and work problems, low self-esteem, accidents, unhappy relationships and even crime and delinquency.

    Although it is common, it is not a recognized diagnosis, either in medicine or psychology. Some people have been this way for most of their life and think this is normal. Brain fog can have many causes - physical, biochemical, emotional and even spiritual
