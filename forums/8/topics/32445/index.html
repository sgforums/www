    Windows 2000 and Windows XP are bootable disks, hence you can just put them into your CDROM and start your computer (remember to change your first boot device as your CDROM in the BIOS setup). After your system is booted, you will be greeted by the installation screen and you can partition your HDD and install the OS into one of the partition. You will be given the choice of FAT or NTFS file format for your primary partition. I am not too sure about the 2 but I uses NTFS anyway. Then just sit back and relax while the OS installs. Choose default values when the installation screen prompts you if you are not sure of what it really means.

    But please remember to get a license key before hand and also back up drivers if they are not available in disks or CDs (this is especially true for devices that are quite old).

    However, if you are just talking about formatting a secondary HDD, just right click them in Windows and it will give you the option of formatting it.

    Hope I helped.
