    I don't think it was a new thing ten years ago. Got it in 1991 and the staff were pretty well-versed in dealing with it.

    Personally, I had more trouble with tetanus toxoid. With dengue, my temperature went from 37.5 to 42 in three hours, I got checked into the hospital and slept for 12 hours. Apart from feeling like I had a bad case of the flu, the only other side effect was that when I got out of bed to go to the loo the next morning, my legs gave way.

    Apart from that, it was the usual stuff - blood tests, put on a drip for a week until I was discharged. No big dramas. The medics were a fun lot and I had a rollicking good time while I was warded.
