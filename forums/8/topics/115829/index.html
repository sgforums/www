    In most cases, the proper emergency response to a Generalized Tonic/Clonic epileptic seizure is simply to prevent the patient from self-injury by moving him or her away from sharp edges, placing something soft beneath the head, and carefully moving the person onto his or her side to avoid asphyxiation. If the seizure lasts longer than 3-4 minutes, Emergency Medical Services should be contacted immediately, as this may indicate the presence of Status Epilepticus, a potentially fatal condition. Objects should never be placed in a person's mouth during a seizure as this could result in injury to the victim's mouth. Despite common folklore, it is not possible for a person to swallow the tongue during a seizure.
    more info at http://www.epilepsy.org.uk/info/firstaid.html
