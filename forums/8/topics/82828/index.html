    Data is read off the imprints or dyes encapsulated by the plastic. If scratches are just minor that are caused by normal handling, it should still be readable by the laser. But if its badly scratched such as taking a blade and drawing a line across, I can't say the same.

    The plastic is more to protect the thin layer of data in between, so if the laser can't reflect off it properly, you can't read the disc without getting faults.

    The products which are sold commercially and claim to remove CD scratches are more for cosmetic purposes, thats why they come with disclaimers that will more likely fault you for not reading the fine print than anything else.
