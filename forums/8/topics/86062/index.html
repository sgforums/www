    Four crocs sighted in Klang River

    FOUR crocodiles, measuring between 2m and 4m long, have been spotted in Sungai Klang, next to a stretch of the Kuala Lumpur Federal Highway - one of the busiest roads in the country.

    At about 8.45am yesterday, three City Hall workers found a 2.5m-long crocodile basking in the sun on a sandbank by the river (left), reported Malaysian newspapers.

    One of the workers, Mr Abu Bakar Hassan, 55, said: 'First, we thought it was an iguana. When we stopped the lorry and went to the sandbank, we found it was a crocodile.

    'We then reported the matter to the Fire and Rescue Team and City Hall, but the reptile had disappeared into the river before the team came.'

    Mr Abu Bakar said it was the first time he had seen a crocodile in the 31 years of patrolling the river.
