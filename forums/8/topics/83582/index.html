    I feel there's no 'perfect' timing for marriage. Some take 2 mths after seeing each other, some take years. I mean look at those 2-3 generations before us, most don't date before marriage.

    I feel that the decision to marry your other half should come when you have considered all aspects of life for e.g
    money issues: whether you accept your partner's spending habits. Whether you are financially stable if you are buying a house....else staying with each other's family.

    You also need to look back at how you resolve differences when you were courting....did you resolve your differences after discussion or through some violent ways?

    Are there any expectations on you from him and vice versa after marriage?

    Marriage is also more than you and your partner. Because every decision you and your spouse make will in some way influence yours or/and his family....Can you interact with his/her family? If you can't, how are you going to resolve this issue when situations arises?

    Of course there are lots of factors that you need to consider....these are just some classic factors.
