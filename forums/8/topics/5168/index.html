    I don't know if u guys call this scary but it happened to me several times. I can swear by it!

    I am based at Seletar Camp. Though there aren't any stories to go around by my ex-colleagues. I do had an experience that I'd never forget.

    It was just after the Hungry Ghost Festival (if I remember correctly) and it was August on the Lunar Calender. I was on Guard Duty and as all the officers had gone home, leaving two of my friends and me in the Guardroom, we carried on our own activities until it was time for bed.

    I had just turned the lights off and laid in bed (I'm an extremely light sleeper) but somehow couldn't get to sleep. Then I heard noises outside the Guardroom. Initially I thought it was just one of my other team's colleague who had decided not to go home but return to Guardroom to sleep (he was on duty the next day). But something WAS very wrong.

    1) I heard shuffling footsteps outside the Guardroom.
    2)Someone turned on the television rather loud and the radio too.
    3)More and more footsteps were heard.
    4)The other bedroom door opened and someone shuffled the foot in and closed the door silently.

    I sat up in bed immediately and the footsteps seemed to shuffle less. Immediately, I ran out and saw... NOBODY! It couldn't have been my imagination! Anyway, I quickly shrugged it off and returned to bed. Just as my butt touched the bed and was about to lie down when I heard distincitively someone calling my name outside the gate...

    The terrifying thing is that, anybody with extremely powerful lungs shouting from the gate will NOT be heard when we're in our room. Even the motorcycle horn would sound muffled. UNLESS it's a car horn.

    Hit the pillow quickly, covered my ears and didn't sleep until it was time to get up.

    My last account...

    Another time I had the camp to myself again and was watching 'Legend of the 8 Immortals' on TCS8. My colleagues had went out for dinner and threw me alone there. Since the extension was cut off already, I had no other choice but to stare at that silly show.

    My camp dog started barking furiously and I sprang to my feet. Rushing to the spot where the dog was, I saw a small cobra about 25cm long. Rushing to get hot water and sulphur I returned only to find that the snake has escaped. Oh well, adventure's gone. Back to the silly show.

    I heard the dog barking at the gate. Adventure Time again! I grabbed the baton and rushed out, only to see... nothing again. I swore at the dog:

    'Why bother me with your stupid barking? Stupid Dog!'

    A gust of bone-biting wind started blowing at me. All my hair started standing on ends and the wind got stronger the longer I stood there.

    I started praying around, "Sorry 'brothers'. Sorry for my shouting..."

    The wind died down instantly and stopped blowing at once, once the dog and I moved away from the gate.

    I never swore at the camp dog again and treated him better from that day onwards. (No beating, no swearing. Nothing. Just talk and order the dog, he understands human language)

    I almost never encountered any experience with the supernatural, but my very first night at Dieppe Barracks (1 Guards, opp. Khatib Camp) wasn't exactly pleasant.

    Before we retired for the evening, my bunk mates and I padlocked our lockers, keeping the individual keys safely in our pockets. And I remebered distinctly in the middle of the night that I actually woke up. I instinctively felt for the key in my pocket -- it was still there. I looked around the room, dimly lit by the corridor lights. Nothing aroused my suspicion and so I went back to sleep.

    The next morning, all the lockers in our bunk were mysteriously unlocked -- the doors were left ajar, the padlocks hanging onto the ear of the doors with the keys attached to the locks themselves.

    And the funny thing is, nothing was missing...
