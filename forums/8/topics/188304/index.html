    Hi, the online proxy that I mentioned is used from a web browser. That means it only supports http and https connections on port 80. The proxies have their own policies in place as to what they allow and what they disallow when using their proxies. Online proxies are usually not "anonmynous" because they will surrender all logs to the relevant authorities when proper ordered to.

    These online proxies allow you to "evade" ip logging from the websites you are visiting for example runescape. Runescape disallows one IP from logging more than one account. To "fool" the system into thinking that its a different IP, you might want to use the proxy so that the runescape system sees the proxy's IP instead of yours and thinks its another person.

    Another use of the online proxiese is to visit websites that have been blocked by your administrator. Example is NIE administrator blocks sgforums.com. You would be unable to connect to sgforums.com website from NIE. Instead you are allowed to visit the proxy website and the proxy can be used to visit sgforums.com. This fools the NIE filters into thinking that you are visiting the proxy's website but in actual fact you are visiting sgforums.com

    For BT, if your ISP or proxy caps or blocks your BT downloads, using an anonmynous or online proxy would only serve to slow your connection and frustrate you, because in the end, your traffic will still need to pass through the proxy or ISP which will still block your BT traffic.
