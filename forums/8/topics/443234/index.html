  • Country Boy

    I subscribe to Starhub Cable TV in August 2010 on a 12 month contract. I was offered a 6 month discount from the time I signed up. I took up the compulsory 3 Basic Tier groups and the additional Sports group.

    After the discounted period, my bill was about $32.50 per month. A few months later, I was informed that subscription rates will be up $2 per group and that my new bill was effectively up to $36.50 per month. That is fine to me!

    I had been paying this amount for months even after the contract period expires. However, I received a 'shock' of my life last month when I was billed $44.50! I did not even add in additional channels! That is $9 increase without prior notice! Bear in mind that I have no overdue amount in my bill balance.

    I thought to myself: "Just pay it first and see if the following bill still costs as much!" I settled the $44.50 bill and still received the $44.50 bill this current month! A $9 increase without notice! This is absurd! 

    I now got so pissed that I will not be paying this current bill until Starhub send me a warning letter and then I will finally settle the bill and cancel my subscription!

    What on earth is Starhub doing? From what I understand, the tv rights to the other soccer leagues apart from the English leagues cost significantly lesser and that is why online betting companies are willing to offer free streaming of those non-English games to their customers. Maybe the reason is because Starhub came up with a show called "FC Daily" where they need to pay expert pundits to feature on the show together with the resources involved and Starhub wants to make up the costs by charging their customers more? We sports subscribers can live without this 'crap' show and pay less!!

    Starhub is not getting more customers and from what they are currently doing, they will lose more customers instead. Call that 'money-making'? What a joke!
