    Just in case I had confused some young forumites here regarding Tibet, here's some facts I gathered while i was doing Chinese Literature years back and when I spent 4 months in China just last year :

    Barclay, for your info too, enjoy !

    What the Western Countries say :

    Tibet was invaded by the Chinese PLA in 1949. An uprise by the Tibetans took place in 1959. Since then Dalai Lama had seek political asylum elsewhere ... The issue was brought up to the UN but the Chinese PLA never leaves Tibet.

    What the history of China says :

    The Tibetan Kingdom started as far back as the 7th Century as the Tubo Dynasty. During that time, China was ruled by the Tang Dynasty. Relationships between the 2 dynasties had been excellent. In 641, Princess Wencheng (member of Tang Taizong's family) was betrothed to Tibet. In 710, Princess Jincheng was also betrothed to Tibetan King Tsugtsen (correct spelling ???). Both dynasties were VERY closely related. 3 "Tang-Tubo Alliance Stelae" were even erected. One still stands at Lhasa (go see for yourself !)

    After the Tang Dynasty came the Song Dynasty. Similarly, both Song and Tubo Dynasties had enjoys years of good relations.

    After that came the Genghis Khan who unified China. It was then when Tibet was conquered and ruled under the law of Kublai Khan (121?-1294), Yuan Dynasty. Since then Tibet has been under the central government at Dabu (the present Beijing)

    After that came the Ming and Qing Dynasties and Tibet remained as part of China and was under the control of the central government. But they enjoy certain level of autonomy, like religion and customs.

    In 1911, the Qing Dynasty was overthrown in the Xin(1) Hai(1) Ge(2) Ming(4) (Xinhai Revolution). In 1949, People's Republic of China was founded by Mao Zedong of course.

    In 1951, representatives of the central government and local tibetan government signed the 17-Article Agreement. I shall not bore you with the details of the Article. But basically it says :

    a. PLA will march into Tibet to drive out the imperialists and the local tibetan army will help PLA with that.
    b. Tibetan customs will be protected and religious freedom granted. The old Tibetan system will also be unaltered and Dalai Lama will remain as the religious and political leader of Tibet.

    In 1959, an armed rebellion took place. It aimed to abolish the feudal serf system and wanted democratic reform.

    In that same year, Zhou Enlai, dissolved the Tibet local government and abolished the feudal serf system and implemented democratic reform as requested by the Tibetans.

    The new Tibet was formally formed in 1965. But before much can be done, the Cultural Revolution took place and it lasted 10 years from 1966 to 1976.

    1976, Mao Zedong died, and Gang of Four arrested. Then it came Sun Zhong San's Gong Chan Dang and Jiang Jie Shi's KMT .. blah blah blah .. not important liao.

    And so ... we have the Tibet today !

    So who do you believe ? China or the western countries ? Your choice. The truth is out there, not here. hehe
