    eCr1 is to let you indicate what unit you see you will be taking on the assumption that you pass all units of the exam. I think this eCr1 is give the planning guys an estimate the number of student taking each unit so as to facilitate the planning of time table.

    eCr2 happens after when you have gotten your results. If results are to your expectation, then you will have to stick the units you choose in eCr1. Indecisiveness in eCr1 and a request to change to another unit in eCr2 different from eCr1 will cost you an additional $52.50.

    As for term 1 and term 2, it is applicable only for the part time programme.
    Quote: "Students transferring to the part-time programme should note that part-time programme has two terms. Term 1 is from 4 July - 3 Sept 2005 while Term 2 is from 12 Sept to 21 Feb 2006."
