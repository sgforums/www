    Been Tokyo & Harajuku a few times (most recent last yr) but not Hokkaido.
    I've got a relative there so everything all free.
    Most go Disneyland & Disneysea (Disneyland is better), try hot spring bath- the traditional type- very fun. Go Odaiba (lots of big shopping mall), Mt Fuji (I saw a volcano), visit their temples. Harujuku is a must for teens. Shinjuku also very good.

    It is quite easy to go around, though their MRT very confusing (many lines criss-crossing). The Japanese are very helpful & most can understand simple Eng.
    Food- lots of small eateries, u can get very fresh sashimi, cakes, green tea ice-cream from their supermarkets. The range of products very wide.

    Originally posted by gerrykoh:
    Been Tokyo & Harajuku a few times (most recent last yr) but not Hokkaido.
    I've got a relative there so everything all free.
    Most go Disneyland & Disneysea (Disneyland is better), try hot spring bath- the traditional type- very fun. Go Odaiba (lots of big shopping mall), Mt Fuji (I saw a volcano), visit their temples. Harujuku is a must for teens. Shinjuku also very good.

    It is quite easy to go around, though their MRT very confusing (many lines criss-crossing). The Japanese are very helpful & most can understand simple Eng.
    Food- lots of small eateries, u can get very fresh sashimi, cakes, green tea ice-cream from their supermarkets. The range of products very wide.
    The japanese railway isn't that confusing lar. The 2 main ones are toei and JR; chances are that you are going to be on Toei most of the time. They are color coded, so it's quite simple to understand after a while.

    Anyway Odaiba is more of an architectural exposition. Plenty of nice buildings but the shopping cannot be compared against that of Harajuku and Shibuya. That's the teen capital, not so much of shinjuku htough it's really just farther down the road. Shinjuku is generally considered the main commercial district, somewhat similar to our orchard road. then you have the seriously high end and expensive ginza, where you can find pretty much any brands which costs and arm and a leg.

    Still if apple has the chance it might be quite worth it to take the monorail across rainbow bridge into odaiba. just don't get off. once you hit the expo centre, the monorail will turn back, and then you can get off at one station before and pay a lot less fare.

    If apple intends to go to hokkaido be sure to include the train fare there; i'm not sure how much it costs but it's easily sgd$100? coz' that's what the *bus* fare to kyoto costs. considering that hokkaio is much farther away, the train fare would probably be around sgd$200...

    Anyway who on earth wants to go to hokkaido? beo cows? nothing there...
