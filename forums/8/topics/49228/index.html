    Unique to the NEL, there are in-train CCTVs to monitor the passenger activities on board. During emergencies, in addition to automated train announcements, Operations Control Centre (OCC) staff can also make live announcements depending on the situation. Passengers can press the emergency call button in the trains to speak to our staff. The OCC is manned by highly trained staff who work round the clock.

    What to do during evacuationÂ…

    If a problem develops while a train is between two stations, the train will be brought to the nearest station so that passengers can get off the train. There is no need to be afraid of being trapped in a NEL train as passengers can alight by using the door unlock handle to open the door from inside the train.

    However, if an evacuation is necessary while the train is between two stations, the Customer Service Officer (CSO) on board, or staff from the station will be despatched to the scene, to render assistance. They can also direct the passengers through the intercom system to walk to either end of the train to use the detrainment door for emergency exit. Lighted directional signs along the tunnel will lead the passengers to the nearest station. The tunnels along the NEL are also lighted at all times. As the power cables are overhead, it is safe to walk along the tunnel without the fear of getting electrocuted.

    Passengers need not worry about power failures as the emergency light and ventilation fans on the trains will remain on long enough for evacuation to take place.

    NEL safety featuresÂ…

    The NEL has in place a Train Protection System that will ensure that trains keep a safe distance from each other. Being a fully underground system, the NEL also has a comprehensive fire protection system in place. These systems include highly sensitive heat and smoke sensors in the trains and in the tunnels. Every train is also equipped with fire extinguishers. Tunnels are fitted with large tunnel ventilation fans which will blow smoke from the tunnels in the event of fire.
