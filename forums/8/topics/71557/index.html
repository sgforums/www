    MARCH 3, 2004
    Cupid needs 1,800 men

    IT IS not easy playing Cupid, especially if you have more than 2,400 singles on your hands.

    But wait, it gets worse: About 2,100 of them are women.

    Still, Ms Lau Say Wei, 39, can count at least three success stories of couples who now 'send flowers to each other' since she took on the role of social development officer (SDO) at the Singapore General Hospital (SGH) last July.

    SDOs were introduced when the Social Development Unit was set up in 1984. As an SDO, Ms Lau organises activities such as disco nights to help her single colleagues mingle. To find the men, she also holds joint events with SDOs in other outfits, such as the police force.

    But the most popular event is the annual Mr and Ms SGH pageant.

    One couple who met through this contest is clerical officer Muhammad Hasnor Hashim and staff nurse Jia Jia alias Nurhaniifah Zhu (in background), both 24. They tied the knot last year.

    Ms Lau, a mother of two who is also the assistant manager of work-life at SGH, says: 'Many of them are looking for love but they don't want others to know it. It doesn't help that our staff is predominantly female, so it's even harder to get them to make the first move.'
