    Volcano in Indonesia Spews Ash, Dust
    Fri Mar 28, 4:07 AM ET

    JAKARTA, Indonesia - An Indonesian volcano with a history of violent explosions has erupted again, spewing dust and ash 5,000 feet into the air, geologists said Friday.

    There are no plans to evacuate the 15,000 people living close to Mount Lokon in north Sulawesi province, said Jemmy Runtuwene, a staffer at a monitoring station close to the 5,741-foot high peak.

    He said several explosions were heard for about four minutes, after which the volcano erupted on Thursday, firing ash and dust into the air.

    In February, Lokon, which is one of 129 active volcanoes in Indonesia, rumbled for two days.

    It's last major eruption was in 1991, when a Swiss hiker was killed and thousands of villagers were forced to flee their homes.

    Cyclone in New Caledonia Leaves Two Dead
    Fri Mar 14, 4:09 PM ET

    PARIS - A cyclone swept the South Pacific island of New Caledonia, killing two people and injuring seven seriously, French authorities said Friday. About 70 others had lighter injuries.

    More than 3,000 men, three airplanes, three helicopters and two boats were aiding in rescue and cleanup efforts, according to the French Overseas Ministry. New Caledonia is a territory of France.

    In a statement, President Jacques Chirac's office said he was keeping informed of the situation and offered his "solidarity and deep sympathy to families of the victims."

    Rescuers were still searching for a boat off Koumac, a city on the island's north coast, local officials said. Several fishermen were believed aboard.

    The island's north and west ends were worst hit by Cyclone Erica, which swept in Thursday.

    Overseas Minister Brigitte Girardin told residents she was "at their side for the ordeal" and planned to fly to the island Sunday. She set aside $322,000 in initial aid money.

    TOKYO - The Mount Asama volcano puffed up a column of gray smoke near the central Japanese city of Nagano on Sunday, but the government said there was no immediate danger of a major eruption.

    The 8,470-foot mountain near Karuizawa, about 90 miles west of Tokyo, last rumbled to life in 1990. In 1947, 11 mountaineers were killed after being struck by molten rocks on the mountain.
