    Whoa, whoa, relax, man. Not all students are like this; remember, it's always the minority who give the largest negative image.

    Yes, I'm a student in a JC, and yes, I admit I have a comfortable life. Many students have comfortable lives. However, that is not a good reason to state that ALL students are over pampered.

    The reason for the MOE adopting more liberal practices is that they want to breed a new kind of student: one who is willing to speak up and give ideas and comments. The more authoritarian style of learning and teaching doesn't help in this case, because the student is not considered; it becomes a very one-sided affair. If the teacher is a terrible one (in part with your unhappiness with grading teachers), then it would be worse, wouldn't it? For creativity to be nurtured, you have to remove certain restrictions.
    An analogy would be that of an eagle. Chain it to its perch and it'll never soar. Let it fly freely and it might attack you or do business on your clothes. The key to it is balance between the two; to train the raptor to be listen, but allow it to fly when it wants to. This new plan of the MOE is obviously in the development phase, therefore, it's not exactly working out the way we want it to.

    Of course, respect still has to be kept. I do my part by greeting any teachers I see, and paying attention in class. I also remind my friends to try and be respectful. It doesn't harm anyone in the process anyway.
