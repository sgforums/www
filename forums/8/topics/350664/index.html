Nightmare with Standard Chartered Bank

  • NewAgeSensitiveBoy
    7 Mar 09, 11:46

    Sometime early last month prior to my director's overseas trip, I was tasked to change the bank's signatory for the company; a routine procedure for the change of director within the company. What should have been a simple 5 working days process, as we were informed, turned into anightmare for our company.
     
    It was puzzling after 2 weeks when we did not recieve any response from the consultant Mr Edward Choy serving us, I decided to give him a call to find out the status. I was then told to forward him an email where he will get back to me. A week passed and nothing from him, I called up the bank and was told that he is no longer with the bank. I then called Mr Edward's extension of which a Mr Darren answered and told me that Mr Edward is on leave and that he will follow up on the case. Another week passed and nothing from him also. I then called up the hotline of which a Ms Rajes served me and told me she would get back to me and she never did. I asked to speak to the manager each time and the manager was always busy or having lunch and a "don't worry Mr Cheng we will get back to you as soon as we can."
     
    Finally I recieved a call in a rather hostile tone from Mr Darren who told me that this procedure would have already been cleared within 7 days (I later found that the status is STILL pending) and that he would direct my case to his colleague Ms Vicky.
     
    Ms Vicky though trying her best to help couldn't do much for our company except to persistently send emails to the "backend" people (whom they have no access to) to chase them and that the reply would be at their descretion. This is Day 3 already and the "backend" people have still not responded. How difficult it is to change the signatory and to respond to a SIMPLE email, I wonder.
     
    As a result of such poor services, our company is having problems making payments that are overdue as nobody is available to sign the cheques & remittance applications and I have to travel round the island to make cash payments to local suppliers. Our reputation is tarnished and we cannot place any orders for materials as a result of this delay. Thank you Standard Chartered for making our lives more challenging during this recession.
