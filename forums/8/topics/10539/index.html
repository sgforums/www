    Dear James,

    Well, there are a few thoughts which came to my mind while reading this thread. The whole context of this thread can split up into 3 simple questions :

    1)What do women want?
    2)What do men want?
    3)Age vs Wealth factor.

    Let me tackle this point by point.

    On point (1), I will assume the basis that there is an increasing equality between both sexes. Some critics might not agree, but it does not matter whether feminism is an issue. What matters, is that now, women have more say in what they want, as compare to many decades when they have to listen to their parents. They become more educated and decide to have increasing demands. However, these demands will change as time goes.

    For a 17-21 year old gal, she does not really know what she want. For guys, it is always a risk to have a r/s with women of this age, because the gals are changing at this age. They would not want to bound themselves to anyone, and they would want to have fun, and on that level, they will prefer the so-called "jerks" than the "nice guys". The point is that someday this gal will mature into a 24 year old, and she stops having fun, and been introduced to the rites of life, she would want the security and the wealth, and hence it is not strange to see the kind of reply given by Tet, which is based on pragmatism. I was asking a few American Caucasian gals (all are postgraduates between 22-25) in the bar, why they don't go to club these days. They told me that it is a trend in US that after college school days, they stopped their days of being crazy and really settling down. Of course, they also conceded that some people still go to clubs even if they are 30s, but that significantly decreases.

    On point (2), the situation becomes reversed for guys. The guys know what they want at a younger age. However, they have to juggle between career and women. When they have the women, and they have no career, the r/s will not last, because reality steps in. When they have career, but no woman, eventually, the r/s will last if they found one, because crudely in my own humble opinion, the wealth factor has dominated the r/s. Gone are those days with passionate love letters and romantic whims like kissing on the lift in Effiel Tower at Paris.

    Finally on point (3), I recall about a point which I once trying to make in Tet's earlier thread about women being materialistic and pursuing wealth. The irony of the day is that when the man in her story become the rich guy, his values had changed, because he had grown bitter due to the circumstances. Perhaps, I am still a romantic on some level, I can sympathize with that, because I find myself growing towards that more and more. So, in the end, is it really money and power which will determine the gal you get? I urge you to think about this, guys. Well, my personal opinion is no. I will conjure a hypothetical scenario and ask myself what I want to do if I have that kind of power and money. So what if you can get her body and make her succumb to your will, there will always exist the day where u fall, and she ran away with another man. Romantic love will fade as age increases.

    In an article entitled "Have you found your IT yet?" by Sumiko Tan even though I don't agree with her at times. She mentioned that there exist 4 kinds of people when it comes to a r/s. The first finds a soulmate early in life. She marries him, they set up a home, have kids, and their life's pretty settled from then on. I always envy this kind of r/s and found a lot in some of my ex-classmates. The second type moves from one relationship onto another, and the relationships are more often than not fraught with woes. Life is one roller-coaster ride as she soars with joy and plunges into misery. The third sort doesn't care for relationships, for she can be perfectly happy without a beloved by her side. Then, there is the fourth type, who dearly wishes to be involved with someone, but can't find the right partner. I belong to the fourth type...

    Just my opinions which are of 2 pennies worth.

    Have a good day.

    yours sincerely,
    Prodigal
