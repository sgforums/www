    Love in its purest form, is certainly without lust.
    It's about giving wholeheartedly and not seeking anything in return. If it's reciprocated, it's a blessing.
    However, the "love" that existed in our society these days is "conditional love", that is there's always conditions attached to loving someone. "I love you, and hence you must love me back." "I love you, but you don't love me anymore, and hence I won't love you too. If necessary, I'll hate you."
    I used to be amazed that how can someone love another so deeply before, and yet lose all his/her feelings so quickly, and turned to hate his/her former lover.
    Perhaps, they don't really love the other, they love themselves too much, and hence when they are hurt(by the rejection), unkind words/feelings are directed at the other party. It's sad, but I've seen too many cases.
    "I love you." People have been abusing these words to achieve their personal aims, whatever it might be. To me, I think there isn't much value left in them, if you really love someone, show it with actions. Saying it and making empty promises makes you a hypocrite.
    But then, seems that people r really gullible, they like to fall for jerks instead of those who really care for others but are just too shy and/or too inarticulate to express their feelings. I feel sorry for all the nice guys/gals out there who are left in the cold. Perhaps that's life.
    I wonder why so many people are concerned about the exterior shell, what's the point if the internal is rotting? Looks don't last.
    I often wonder how many couples really understand each other? How many can communicate by just looking into each other's eyes? And how many failed to understand simple hand gestures from each other?
    Many seemed to be seeking prefection in their partners, but what actually matters is that they are prefect for each other. People are often disappointed when they discover inprefection, many failed to understand that to love means to accept each other's flaws.
    I also noted that many people start a relationship before they really understand each other. It seems that it's like a trial test, if it works, that's great. If it isn't, then the experience is bitter.

    This brings about another question, do platonic friendship ever exist?

    Things to ponder about...

    Love can't be defined? I disagree. Why people can't define it is probably because the word's been so warped and used and abused and thrown around that nobody can recognise it when it pops up and mistakes everything else for it.

    Love is NOT eros. You can love your girlfriend/boyfriend, you can love your mother you can love your best buddy, you can love your next-door neighbor, you can love the stranger on the MRT. Heck, if you're a good enough person, you could even love the guy who stabbed you in the back at work the day before.

    Love is everything that is good. It's not a feeling or an euphoric rush, it's a labour and a service. Good doesn't mean "good for you" it means good for everyone else. Loving because it will ultimately benefit you ISN'T love, it's selfishness. Patience, kindness, temperance, humility, goodness, forgiveness, not seeking repayment... these are some of the characteristics of love and anything that isn't these isn't love.

    It's about sacrifice, and in today's selfish, "it's all about me" world, the real meaning of love has been replaced by a selfish, relationship-based eros and the true meaning and practise of it has been lost to the new generation(s). Imagine if everyone could really selflessly do the things mentioned above! It would turn the world upside down, for the better. So WHY DON'T WE???

    I guess it all comes down to us not being humble enough, not being brave enough, not being wise enough, not being selfless enough to show some love. HRC kinda got some of it right - "Love all, serve all." To love is to be the lowest servant indeed. DARE WE?
