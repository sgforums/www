    Slash: 'Chinese Democracy' drops March

    The controversially long-awaited new Guns N' Roses album Chinese Democracy is due in March, according to ex-guitarist Slash.

    Slash, now part of Velvet Revolver, claimed that Axl Rose's long-delayed album will finally see the light of day this year.

    "Axl's got a record coming out, Guns, I guess you call it, I think in March," Slash said. "Which is sorta cool, you know it's gonna be interesting to hear it. After all this time and after all this talk what's going on with him."

    "That's what...I've been told a lot of things over the years, but it definitely sounds like it's coming out in March," he added.

    The last Guns N' Roses album was 1993's The Spaghetti Incident.

    (source: eCentral.com)
