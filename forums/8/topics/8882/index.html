    I think Macs are fine if you've never used a Windows based PC before. But if you have you will find that the system is much more restricted as to what you can do and what you are supposed to do with it. Personally I prefer Macintoshes for children as I am a Macintosh Technician. But PCs are reputed to be better in programming and other technical issues.

    I have used a UNIX system before and I can safely say it is not prone to crashes like Macintosh and Windows PCs are. However UNIX systems are notorious for being user-unfriendly and I would suggest having a handbook of all commands on the desk next to you at all times if you want to do a quick efficient job. The UNIX system also doesn't utilize the mouse that much. However the child of UNIX, LINUX, has come up with the incentive of providing a Macintosh or Windows based interface for users acclimated to the other OS characteristics.

    Windows is most famous for its blue screen of death. The bad thing about them is they always say Fatal Error/Exception at a ridiculously long number that no one can figure out what it means unless your name is Yale Tubster and have graduated from MIT.

    The thing about Windows is that their error messages keep reiterating without any plausible solution unless you have a vague idea of what you have been doing on it and what you think might be wrong.

    One advantage about UNIX and LINUX is that they allow this superuser and multiple users system to be implemented. If there are many members in your family who need access to a computer system and most of them are well versed in electronic related matters, then I would recommend UNIX.
