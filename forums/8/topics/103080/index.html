    here u go:

    freezing and thawing several times will disrupt the cell membranes of the tissue (meat/vege) and bacteria that grows on the surface under cold temp (Psychrophiles) will be able to enter into the food. Thawing will allow Mesophiles (microbes that thrive in normal room temperature) to grow. Freezing does not kill any bacteria...it simply inhibit/inactivate microbial activity as mesophiles do not have enzymes that can withstand low temp. And thawing will promote their growth when given right temp.....repeititions of these will result in food spoilage.

    Sometimes, signs of food spoilgae (eg. meat) can be observe using our eyes. Purplish spots on meat will tells us that the meat is not suitable for us and be discarded. But major cause of food intoxication comes from bacterial that shows no color when they grow.
