    A memorial service for Asia's tsunami victims will be held on Sunday at 5pm at the Singapore Expo Hall 2.

    Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong will address the gathering and there will be inter-religious prayers.

    MediaCorp's television and radio stations will carry the event "live".

    Singapore's flags will also be flown at half-mast on that day as a mark of respect.

    Still reeling from its most destructive natural disaster ever, Asia is starting to pick up the pieces and rebuild its shattered coastal communities.

    Singapore has been playing a crucial role in the most affected areas like the ravaged province of Meulaboh in Indonesia.

    But relief efforts will have to continue for a long time more.

    Dr Vivian Balakrishnan, Minister for Community Development, Youth and Sports, said: "There is going to be many, many more months if not years of work and our voluntary welfare organisations, our government and our people, many ordinary people will have many opportunities to contribute in the future to the rebuilding of lives, to the rebuilding of communities and all of us are now examining how we can restructure our youth programmes, our assistance programmes so that Singapore plays its role, as a small nation, but a small nation with a big heart and an active united people.

    "We cannot solve all problems, but we can provide some assistance. We can therefore get ourselves organised in our usual Singaporean fashion, united, systematic, determined and make a difference."

    To express solidarity with its neighbours, Singapore will be holding an open memorial service this Sunday.

    Many non-Singaporeans have friends and family in the affected areas, and they are encouraged to attend the event.

    Dr Vivian added: "This will be an occasion for us to remember the victims. Not only in Singapore but indeed across the region. It will also be an occasion for us to pray for the safe return and the recovery of those who have been injured, and also an occasion for Singapore to stand in solidarity with our neighbours who have been so grievously affected by this disaster."

    Some Singaporeans have loved ones or friends who have been lost in the tsunami tragedy, but even those who did not, have been moved by the scenes of devastation and suffering in the neighbouring countries.

    Many are expected to attend the memorial service to express their sympathies, grieve for the dead and pray for those still missing.

    Members of the public attending the service are advised to arrive before 4.00pm.

    The Ministry of Community Development, Youth and Sports is leading an inter-agency committee, comprising the MFA, MICA, MOH, Singapore Police Force, Singapore Armed Forces and the People's Association, to organise the Memorial Service.
