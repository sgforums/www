    If you are travelling around, I recommend you get one with Intel Centrino Mobile Technology.

    Simply putting, it will be Intel Pentium M xGHz. x is a decimal, like in your question, 1.8GHz. Depending on what games you want to play, you may not want a Intel processor. A AMD Sempron processor works as well.

    To answer question 1, you can add RAM. If you are unsure, you can ask the sales technicians to help out. Or simply take the upgrade offers (like Dell, offer you upgrade this, upgrade that).

    For question 2, all laptops have at least one USB port. Just buy a mouse that uses USB port and connect can already. Nowadays laptops don't come with PS/2 Serial Port, so make sure you don't buy the wrong one, or you have to use the black space (it is called touchpad).

    Question 3: I won't recommend brands, but laptops that are using Intel processors have longer battery life.

    Question 4: If the hotel has wireless infrastructure, just register with the hotel to use their wireless network. If it doesn't have, it usually have some ports for you to connect to the Internet. Check with the hotel, you can email the hotel or ask around, like your friends who have been to the hotel.

    Yahoo is a good site to find old and in good conditions laptop.

    If you want to play games like RPG, I recommend you to get a desktop replacement. Their processors are usually Intel with HT Technology or Intel Celeron or Intel Celeron D.

    hm... going overseas, wan useable for at least a few years, wireless internet, a little gaming. Can state wat games you usually play?

    What I suggest is, 1.73GHz Centrino Mobile Processor.
    Try not to get one with AMD, uses more battery
    at least 512 DDR2 ram running at 533MHz.
    If you want mobility, get a 14" laptop.
    If you dun mind carrying a heavier one with better graphics for gaming get a 15".
    Super DVD drive is recommended since DVD is getting common nowadays. Dun be mistaken between CD-RW/DVD Combo drive and Super DVD drive. always check clearly to see which type of compact disc it burns and which type it reads.
    at least 60GB.
    At least 64mb of video ram since you play simple games.
    If hardcore games then at least 256mb (this should usually used with a 15" monitor)
    Get one with at least 2 USB. 1 is definitely not enough
    VGA connector for presentations. all laptops have this nowadays
    Bluetooth connection can come in useful.
    Memory card slots. Decide for yrself which memory cards you use the most. MMC, SD is the most common.
    Take note of warranty too. Get at least 3 years.

    Laptop's rams are more expensive, and its best to upgrade the ram when you are buying it since different laptop models may use different type of rams.

    USB is a must-have for any computer now. If you have a USB mouse just plug it in and you can use it. A few mouse brands may require you to install drivers but if you are using Windows XP most of the time its not necessary.

    Brand is not a problem when it comes to tahan-ing long hours of use. Its the user's responsibility to make sure you use it for long hours in a well-ventilated area or air-conditioned room.

    Wireless is intergrated inside all Centrino machines so dun worry.
