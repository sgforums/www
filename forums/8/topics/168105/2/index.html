    Go to

    Laptop Buying Guide (Home users)

    Laptop Buying Guide (Business users)

    Both are very useful.

    If you have some time to spare, order online and wait for delivery.

    No time, just go down to Sim Lim Square, Harvey Norman, Courts or Safe Super Store and buy one.

    Some advice:

    1) Get a laptop which offers international warranty as you are travelling overseas. You have to pay more for this. How much I am not sure.

    2) As you want to play games, I suggest you get a laptop with a minimum memory of 512MB.

    3) Buy an insurance for your laptop. Singapore is so safe that I can see people leaving their laptops around and 1 hour later the laptop is still there. This is not the case overseas. 1 minute later you come back and that's it. It will be gone. If possible, buy a lock. The lock is usually a Kensington lock.

    4) Don't sleep while travelling. I know you are tired, but there are people who will steal things when you are falling asleep. It doesn't happen in Singapore, but it happens elsewhere.

    5) Ask for extra batteries and adaptors from the salesman in case you lose it while travelling.

    6) Get a thumb drive or external HDD to back up your data. You never know when the laptop will decide to die on you.
