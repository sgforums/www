    SINGAPORE - It was a case that saw the Court of Appeal express "surprise" that a custody fight over cross-terrier Sasha had reached the highest court in the land.

    In the hearing yesterday, which only lasted 15 minutes, the three-judge court dismissed United States-based surgeon Tan Kok Chye's appeal and upheld the High Court decision that the dog will stay with his ex-girlfriend Connie Tan, a senior specialist in professional development.

    In delivering the ruling, Justice Chao Hick Tin said the court did not see any basis in the appeal. "(The case) should not reach the courts at all. Parties should have applied good sense in resolving it," he said.

    "He (Dr Tan) has unnecessarily taken time of this court off the other cases," he added.

    The two-year-old dog has been the centre of a lawsuit between Ms Tan, 30, and Dr Tan, 37, since March last year.

    The couple had adopted the dog in August 2008 when they were living in Alabama. Ms Tan returned to Singapore with Sasha in November that year when the couple broke up.

    In March last year, Sasha disappeared and was traced to Dr Tan's mother's apartment in Cavenagh Road. Ms Tan then sued her ex-boyfriend and his mother. She was granted a temporary court order to keep Sasha and, in March this year, the court decided that Sasha would remain under her care.

    This did not sit well with Dr Tan, who lodged an appeal.

    It was heard in court yesterday that Ms Tan had tried to resolve the matter with Dr Tan but a settlement could not be reached.

    Most of the talking in the appeal hearing was dominated by the judges. Before Dr Tan's lawyer Leslie Netto could present his argument that Dr Tan is entitled to keep the dog as he is named as the owner in the adoption agreement in Alabama, the judges fired questions at him.

    Justice Rajah commented that the appeal is neither about Sasha nor the adoption contract: "This is a contest of will and Dr Tan wants to demonstrate to Ms Tan that he will pursue this matter as far as he can."

    Mr Netto told the court that he had "discouraged" Dr Tan from the appeal but Dr Tan was "strong about it".

    Ms Tan, who was in court with two of her sisters, shed tears of joy after hearing the verdict and gave them a hug. She told reporters that she was "very thankful" for the court's decision.

    She said: "It's been difficult but I think what spurred me on is I know she (Sasha) likes my care and I know that if I didn't fight for her, nobody would."
