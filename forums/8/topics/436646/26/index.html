    What Makes Soy So Good For Me?
    Did you know that soy is a natural hormone balancer? Soy acts like a kinder and gentler natural source of estrogen in women and increases a person’s HDL cholesterol ( the so-called “good cholesterol”). The higher the HDL cholesterol the lower the risk of heart attacks.

    A growing body of evidence shows that the humble legume’s beneficial component is the isoflavones. Experts say 30 to 50 milligrams of isoflavones a day is a sensible goal, so make sure the form of soy you are eating contains isoflavones. The alcohol extraction of soy protein concentrates and isolates used in some products can remove as much as 90% of the isoflavones

     

    1 cup (240 ml) milk, (soy or skim)
    2 cups (250 g) flour (may be all or part whole wheat)
    2 egg whites
    2 tablespoons vegetable oil
    2 teaspoons baking powder
    1/4 cup (50 g) sugar
    1/3 cup (73 g) brown sugar
    1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
    1 cup (145 g) blueberries, blackberries, raspberries, frozen
     

    1. Preheat oven to 350F (180C). Spray muffin pans with non-stick spray or line with paper baking cups.
    2. Whisk together milk, eggs and soy oil.
    3. Place flours, baking powder, brown sugar, cinnamon and whisked combination of milk, egg whites, and oil into the Vitamix container and secure lid.
    4. Select Variable 1.
    5. Turn machine on and slowly increase speed to Variable 4 or 5.
    6. Blend for 10 seconds. Stop machine and gently stir the blueberries into the batter just until mixed. (If using frozen berries, do not thaw first.)
    7. Fill cups to within 1/3 inch of top. Bake for 15-18 minutes or until you can touch lightly on top and an imprint does not remain.
     
    Research Proves the Benefits of Soy
