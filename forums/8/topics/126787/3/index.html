    Hi guys, I would just like to share my 2 cents worth of experience.

    For weight training, there are two variations. You either train for strength, which is the maximum force your muscles can exert, or you train for endurance, which is the ability of your muscles to endure constant stress.

    Here's a quick list of the equipment you should use at the gym to tone and bulk up.

    Upper back - Lats pulldown, pull-ups
    Shoulders (Deltoids) - Shoulder press, shoulder lifts using dumb bells
    Triceps - Triceps extension
    Biceps - Bicep curls
    Chest - Chest press, bench press
    Abdominals (Obliques) - Crunches, sit-ups

    As for the legs, I train them by running long distances and through interval training.

    Always go for free weights over machines unless they aren't available, E.G. Too many people using, area's too crowded, etc. Machines restrict your range of movement to a particular one, and do not reflect the kind of day to day activities that your muscles will use.

    If you're signing up for a gym, the number one issue that you should look out for is convenience. Simple reason being that if you find it a hassle to go down to the gym on a regular basis, you will sooner or later lose the discipline to work out. Usually, your neighbourhood gym is good enough.

    As for diet, I can't give much help cause I don't really watch what I eat. However, I do stay away from foods high in the prime evils, E.G. Salt, sugar, saturated fat, etc. Of course, it doesn't hurt indulging yourself once in a while.

    All things said, the two most important things to remember are consistency and discipline. You will need to be consistent to see results, E.G. Run/Jog 3 times a week, and be disciplined enough to push procrastination away, E.G. "I will go for my regular run instead of lazing my arse in front of the television."

    Initially, you might feel discouraged, but persevere on! Before you know it, you'll notice the small bodily changes. Many small changes over time equates to a big change.

    Just for the record, I started running on a serious basis 16 months ago. There were numerous ups and downs, but I'm happy with my performance, considering that I run nearly 5 miles for my weekly training and a mile or two below that. I consider myself kinda slow in building up the momentum, but that's just my opinion.

    Hope this helps.
