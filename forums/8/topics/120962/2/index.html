    girl power
    At some point during fetal development, if I remember correctly, its the activation of the gene SRY (Sex determining Region Y) on the Y chromosome by certain proteins in cells within the gonads. [For the technically inclined, this involves certain proteins and enzymes loosening up the strand of DNA at the cistron wrapping around the histones in the chromosome such that it allows for transcription and translation to occur] The result is proteins which in turn turn on other genes, thereby resulting in the gonads becoming testes which start to produce testosterone. The testosterone then causes the development of male genitals. For a female baby, the 2 X chromosomes turn on other genes and cause the development of female genitals. Thus the physical sex of the boy or girl is determined by the genes. Intersex infants are a different story though, as is Klinefelter's syndrome and other similar conditions...
