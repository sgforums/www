    The problem could be a software or hardware.

    For software, try to stop some programs in your task tray, example, if you have an MP3 icon, ICQ, etc. which you are not using at that momement disable them. (Right-click on them and disable) See if the situation improved.

    If you know your PC well. you can do Ctrl-Alt-Del to bring up the detail background processes and try to End Task one by one, you can know which program is causing the fault. (However, Note Of Caution: Not For Novice Users)

    Also I agree with what the others have said, Win98 and ME are "well-known" for their BSOD (Blue-Screen-Of-Death). In the past, I do have a habit of restart the my Win98 after prolong usuage.

    And also, Win98 or ME have problems of managing memory which also can cause this problem. I suggest that you could install 3rd party free software such as Win RAM turbo to manage the RAM ususage

    For hardware, try opening up your PC casing and look for identifiable abnormal signs like
    a) CPU fan not moving
    b) Power supply fan not moving
    c) Wierd sounds coming from the Harddisk, motherboard, Fans,etc
    d) Funny smell when I open up casing, like rubber burned-smell,etc.

    OR as I always like to say, is your PC under Warranty?
    If yes, make it someone elses problem.
    If no, pay someone to make it someone elses problem.
