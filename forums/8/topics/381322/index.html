    Singapore before Raffles was part of the Johor Sultanate according to historical records.

    The Johor Empire is decentralized. It is made of four main fiefs and the Sultan's territory. The fiefs are Muar and its territories under the Raja Temenggung of Muar; Pahang under the stewardship of the Bendehara; Riau under the control of Yam Tuan Muda and mainland Johor and Singapore under the Temenggung. The rest of the Empire belongs to the Sultan.

    Originally posted by Kerubinist:

    Thought I'd share my blog entry here.

    Basically I went to the exhibition at National Library Level 10, called Vignettes of Time. It lasts till end of December so if you're free or passing by do take a look!! I not advertising or anything, I just think it's a really good exhibition worth taking a look.

    After the exhibition I went on a research frenzy on the internet about accounts of Singapore before 1819, and frankly there is very limited knowledge and evidence of the civilisation that existed on our land. The textbook will always tell you that Singapore is a fishing village just because Raffles says so.

    But Chow Yun Fatt tell me that Singapore is a pirate heaven in the 14th century, and it might be from an excerpt from a movie but there's actually solid accounts to back that up. The Malay Annals and Wang Dayuan's journals point towards the fact that Singapore was a thriving port with many pirates in the 14th century, and this really piques my interest!!!!

    Singapore was perhaps just a mere fishing village when Raffles arrived because the Portugese destroyed our port  with fire in the 17th century, to prevent the Dutch from taking over this strategic land. So before that, our country actually had a civilisation!!!

    I dunno bout you, but to me I'm very interested in discovering Singapore's old history. And it will be so awesome if the government actually bothers to conduct archaelogical excavations, so much will be uncovered. But it's a pity they don't care =(
