    Generally, the court does not have the power to give out compensation as and when it likes. In Hua Liang's case, if his family wants compensation, they have to bring a civil action agaisnt the driver.

    I realize you are upset about the perceived injustice of the cases, but without detailed information as to what really happened, we should not be so quick to condemn our judicial system.

    To illustrate my point, let's say that the driver in this case is heavily insured. Would it then not make sense to award such huge damages against her? The amount is easily borne by the insurance company, and the immediate welfare of the jogger's family is secured. The driver is not ruined financially;all she has to bear is perhaps the burden of increased insurance premiums in the future. This way, wealth is spread in the most efficient manner possible.

    I'm not saying that this is in fact what happened; what i am trying to illustrate is that certain facts and circumstances can turn what might be seen as an unjust decision into one that is logical and sensible.

    Re the way damages is calculated at 45%, the Judges probably have guidelines to follow. For eg. the guidelines might state that in a car accident, a passenger who is injured due to the driver's recklessness should have his/her compensation levels reduced by 30% if the passenger was not wearing a seatbelt at the time of the accident .
    The judges then have then unenviable task of trying to apply these guidelines to all the combination of facts before the court.
