    Here's a quick and dirty guide:

    The four *main* characteristics of diamonds
    1. Cut - Supposedly the most important factor, determines if the diamond will be able to reflect light properly when it comes from the top or if it'll just pass through the bottom. The two popular ones are the round/solitaire and rectangular/princess cut.

    2. Clarity - How many impurities (both visible and non visible ones) are in the diamond. Ranges (from best to worst) are FI(Flawless), IF, VVS1/VVS2(Very very slight), VS1/VS2(Very slight), SI1/SI2(Slightly), I1/I2/I3(Visible).

    Basically anything above SI are difficult to spot even under a microscope

    3. Colour - Ranges from
    D-F(Colourless)
    G-H(Near Colourless, visible when compared to better coloured ones)
    I-J(Near Colourless, visible via the naked eye)
    Above K - Visibly yellow

    4. Carat - Weight of the diamond, - the factor that has the greatest impact on price, usually exponentially

    There're actually more factors but its better if you look it up yourself. Here's a good place to start:
