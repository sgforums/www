    Just so that you get the correct picture...I will tell you what actually happens.

    The normal pregnancy is divided into three thirds or trimesters. In most cases, termination occurs when the pregnancy is in the first trimester, the fetus is small and there is no need for the graphic 'crushing of the head' story you hear. It is done under general anaesthesia. Everything is done through the vagina. The cervix is gradually dilated with instruments and a vacuum suction device is inserted to suck out the products of conception (which includes the small, developing embryo/fetus, placenta and amniotic sacs). Then another instrument is used to scrape out the lining of the uterus to remove whatever is left behind. The entire operative procedure takes about 30-45 minutes and iis usually done as a day procedure, so the woman can go home on the same day or the next day, depending on when the operation is done.

    For mid-trimester abortions, it is necessary to 'ripen' the cervix with hormones. Then an injection is given to induce premature labour and the fetus is delivered through the vagina like a normal birth. There will be contraction pains but of a lesser degree than normal labour. The fetus is extremely premature and either is dead on delivery or does soon after delivery. Depending on how long the labour process takes, the woman usually may have to stay a night in hospital.

    There is a potential legal problem with mid-trimester abortions. As the fetus may show signs of life after delivery, the doctors have a dilemma as to whether they should resuscitate the extremely premature fetus. This is particularly problematic if the woman is unsure of dates and the fetus turns out more mature than expected, though still within the period for legal abortions. The doctors have a legal obligation to render care to a baby, however premature, as long as it shows signs of life after complete separation from the mother. This is known as 'separate existence' and the fetus becomes a legal entity. (Before that, fetuses do not have legal identities, in other words, they don't 'exist'.) But if the doctors successfully resuscitate the fetus they may be sued for breach of contract since the woman came for an abortion, not a baby. To overcome this problem, some doctors in the West kill the fetus before delivery using high salt solutions (dangerous and now defunct) or crushing the head etc..To my knowledge, that is not a problem in Singapore and premature fetus after abortions in Singapore are simply allowed to die.

    I will not comment on the ethical or moral question of abortion. This is just to answer your question as to what it involves.

    Dilatation and curettage (D&C) has to be performed in all cases to make sure there is no residual products of conception, whether it is with a vacuum suction technique in the first trimester or with oxytocins in mid-trimester cases. They are all performed under GA in a sterile operating theatre.

    In Singapore, abortions are legal and only the consent of the woman is required. It can be performed in KK Hospital or other public and private hospitals. Before 2001, the cost of the procedure in government hospitals is fixed by statute at $5 but they have deleted that clause, so I suppose the charges will be higher now.

    Gynaecologists in private practice usually have access to an operating theatre either in their clinic or in a private hospital to perform the procedure.
