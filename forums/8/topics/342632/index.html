    I think for custom officers in North America (U.S & Canada), the rule of thumb for travelers is to fill up your declaration forms and have present with you the necessary documentation. Don't give them any reason to find fault with you because these officers are either plain nice or plain nasty. I've entered Canada quite a number of times, and with a Singapore passport it's always been easy. Most of my friends who experience any excessive questioning usually are the ones who failed to fill up their declaration forms properly or have problems with their documentation. As for the States, I think it is much easier now given the Visa-Waiver program, in where we are classified with other first world nations. Some people might find the photographing part a little disorientating, but when I entered the States thru the U.S-Canada land border a few weeks ago, they are now using a webcam to snap pictures of travelers. So it makes the process seem a lot less intrusive. I guess as with everywhere you go, there will be the segment of the population who will take exception to the fact that you are of a race different from them. I got shout down by a bunch of drunken native people in Canada once for being Chinese, but I've also encountered really sweet native people who are deeply curious about Singapore and our culture. So instead of seeing it as a matter of race, in the end, it is just a matter of individuals who are willing to accept and embrace other cultures, and those who just blatantly refusing to for one reason or another.
