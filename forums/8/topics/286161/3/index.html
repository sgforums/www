    Actually, if you want to study accounting, finance, business administration/management e.t.c. Assuming you did well for your 'O' levels and you want to get a degree in one of the above fields..
    Its better not do go Polytechnic AND JC.

    Path1:
    Private Institute
    (+1 year to get diploma)
    (+1 year to get advance diploma)
    (+2 years to get degree)
    Total = 4 years

    Path2:
    JC (+2 years to get 'A' level)
    Uni (+3 years to get degree)
    Total = 5 years

    Path3:
    Polytechnic (+3 years to get a diploma)
    Uni (+3 years to get degree)
    Total = 6 years

    You can get a degree in a private education institute in just 4 years after your 'O' levels. By the time people taking path 2 and 3 graduated, you already have 1 year or more working experience ahead of them.

    However, if you choose path 1, you really must study hard because you can't really afford to fail any modules. For each module you fail, you will have to pay extra $$$ to retake the module. Whereas, if you fail in path 2 or 3, you wouldn't feel the pain as most of your school fees are subsidized by the government.
