    I've lived on and off in Bangkok before and language was definitely a problem . But after a while, you speak enough basic words to curse others and to bargain. I was in Bangkok for business and was dating someone there....and also for sports training. Liked it there and may migrate there if I have the chance. People are friendly and easy-going....but for business, there is totally no sense of urgency at all....and very little initiative (but what the heck, life is not about work only). I felt much more relaxed there than I will ever feel here.

    I've also stayed in the US before for two months (business), but I don't think that can be considered as having lived there. Didn't quite like it there...always felt more comfortable in Asia. While most people are nice and polite (and food came in great portions), the few racists I've met spoiled it all. Some cursed me loudly, others challenged me to fights, etc.

    Currently in melbourne, in the quiet town of Frankston, along the bays of Mornington Peninsula, its a very quiet place.Gd for mug for my exams and work on my assignments with no distractions..No to mention the beach, wkends are spent at the beach, suntanning at the beach ..soaking in the sun and water ..before heading to the cafes along the bay for a nice warm meal of fish and chips. And i swear the fish and chips are gd stuff. Not those Long John Silver kinda of meal,a full fillet of hake or blue grenadier in great batter, washed down with cool lemon tea.

    Initally when i first arrivied, i had to get a little used to the weather, it was cooling and warm during the day, with temperatures reaching 1 degree during winter and it rained in winter, so it was pretty interesting when u could leave your food on the stove and don't bother putting it in the fridge as the weather was cold and dry. I remeber wanting to cook fried rice and you had to have over nite rice to fry.All i did was cook my rice, leave the pot open near the window and the rice was cold in 1 hr ..hehe..

    And the the ppl are so friendly here. Walking to school, it really brightens your day up when ppl just say "how ya going " "G'Day mate !!" you wil walk with a bounce in your stride for the day.

    Down side. u need a car to travel. And i frankly recommending ppl coming down to Australia to think abt buying one. You need to buy your groceries, as the residential areas ain't near the town and the buses are run on 1hr intervals.

    I still like to return to Singapore. I still miss the food though..
