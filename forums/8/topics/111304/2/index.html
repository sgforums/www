    On Tenterhooks,

    What I meant is to ask deeper questions - for example:

    Why do you make me do X sets of Y reps each?
    How do you choose what the appropriate resistance/weight for me?
    Why do some people become muscular and some don't?
    Ideally, how frequently should I visit the gym?
    Why do some people do their weights slowly/quickly?
    What are the differences between using free weights and machines?
    Should I run? How fast/long...for what purpose?
    What foods should I choose/avoid? And when to eat them?
    What other exercise/sports can I do for benefits other than using gym machines?
    How do you keep yourself interested or disciplined enough to workout constantly?
    etc
    etc

    Basically, target the questions at the following objectives:

    (1) Obtaining the knowledge of why exercises are done they way they are.

    (2) Being independent after your training sessions end, so that you won't be lost or stuck at the same level after they end.

    What I'm saying is to make the training come "alive" with lots of interaction, sharing, learning from each other, etc. Not just "tak see chek" (study dead books) like in school.
