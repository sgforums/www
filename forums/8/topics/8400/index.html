    Well, Revey,

    I don't know whether that history do existed. In the Hegelian sense, there are two kinds of history, the objective history where the exact events are narrated and subjective history where the events are romanticised or even biased towards a certain nation.

    In the spirit of Oscar Wilde, I would quote him on his view about history, "Anyone can make history. Only a great man can write it.... The one duty we owe to history is to rewrite it." In many instances, we glorified many conquerors who are actually murderers who started the bloodshed. The rise and fall of civilizations has given us the narrative history of what happened, but the justifications of what really happened, that should be read in a pinch of salt.

    A historian once told me in the college, that the first day of his undergraduate lecture, his History professor started the first lecture by saying,"There is no such thing as history." Simple but profound in some sense.

    yours,
    Prodigal
