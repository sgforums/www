    I used to go to Reds Bugis. $31.50 for normal guy's haircut. They have good service - They serve you tea and biscuit while you're waiting for your stylist. They wash your hair, then cut, then rinse again. (They will massage your head during the washing process.) Finally, they will help you style your hair. The above services may vary according to different stylists and also depending on the time of the day (peak or non-peak).

    I went for my haircut at Chapter2 Bugis today. Decided not to go to Reds anymore because the last time I went, Kelvin ignored my instructions and went ahead to cut the style tht he recommended. I hated the end result. Chapter2 charges a lower price for normal guy's haircut. Only about $26. Got to pay more if you request for a senior stylist to cut for you (Refer to www.chapter-2.com). However, they have slightly inferior service standards. They wash your hair directly at the basin, not at the seat. No head massage. Did not offer drinks or snacks. No final rinse after cutting.

    The above are my comments on the price and service standards of the 2 salons. However, if you want to ask which salon will give u a better haircut, I cant really tell you. It doesnt depend on the name of the salon. It depends on who cuts your hair and whether tht person can give u something tht u want, so just try out a few times and once u find a stylist tht u like, stick to him/her.
