    Let's not despise the uneducated or the low educated ( whatever that means - non PHD scholars are low educated as well?).     Education began with the greeks and it was philosophy based - even mathematics. It's purpose was to elevate the lives and minds of the masses.

     

    To draw even further back, it was religion - another form of philosophy, that taught mankind the concept of words, law and orders, right up to Jesuit priests evangelizing on pagan lands in latter centuries.

     

    The ultimate purpose of education was a philosophy to educate, to meet the increasing specific needs of blossoming civilisations, and to uplift lives of the common people.

    The lower educated cannot be blamed for being attracted to get rich fast schemes, for they know not philosophy or possess analytical skills of the educated, and only demand what they deemed is right being living in an equal society with the highly several times over-paid educated.

When all else disappears, only your character remains, as Ponzai Madoff and his clients found out.

    Character is something that can only be build up natuarally, cultivated through continual learning of diverse subjects, or put through the fire under adverse situations, and not obtained through any specific university degree. This is more important than education or get rich schemes. Without good characteristic traits, civilisation will end and revert back to barbarism - the law of the jungle.

     

    Myhumble plea to both sides is to end this debate on amicable terms. We need each other and must never allow a gulf to separate us all. We can listen to both sides and need not denigrate anyone.

     

    We are only flawed human beings after all, and no educated man alive dare claims he know its all and is superior to others and deem them stupid, nor can anyone untutored claim education had regressed mankind.

     

    Each and every single human individual has his/her special gift or talent that can contribute towards a better egalitarian society that is made up of you and me, our families and friends. from the artistes, musicians and even a hardworking roadsweeper can brighten up our lives.
