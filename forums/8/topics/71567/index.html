    Source :http://www.channelnewsasia.com/stories/corporatenews/view/73818/1/.html
    HELSINKI : Finnish Internet security company F-Secure said on Wednesday that due to human error at its London office thousands of its customers in Britain had received emails from the company infected by a virus.

    The incident, which happened on February 26, was a huge embarrassment to the group, considered to be among the world's leading anti-virus firms, officials admitted.

    "It was a major blunder, and obviously something that should never happen, especially from an anti-virus company like ours," Mikko Hyppoenen, in charge of anti-virus research at F-Secure, told AFP.

    He said an employee inadvertently approved the sending of an infected email generated by the Netsky.B Internet worm, that had obtained a copy of the firm's British mailing list by contaminating an outside computer receiving mails from F-Secure.

    Email recipients can also post emails on the mailing list, but the emails have to be approved by F-Secure before being shipped out, which the employee did by mistake, he noted.

    F-Secure had a stroke of luck in the episode however, since the virus was already old by the time the customers received it, and most of the recipients on the list had already updated their anti-virus software and thereby immunized themselves against the bug, Hyppoenen pointed out. - AFP
