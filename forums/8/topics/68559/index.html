    FIRED with determination, these National University of Singapore (NUS) students went on mission impossible.

    They have designed and built a car all by themselves.

    If you think that's not too difficult, how about just using motorcycle parts and engines?

    The students, five mechanical engineering and two civil engineering undergraduates, have been working on their final year project code-named Singapore Racer Zero One.

    They are raring to go for the Formula-Society of Automotive Engineers (FSAE) inter-university race in Pontiac, USA.

    Said team leader Michael Leong, 25: 'We practically worked until 2 to 3am every night. Our school holidays felt like no holidays and I seldom saw my parents during that time.

    'But it's a labour of love, which was worth every effort when we drove the car for the first time on New Year's Eve.'

    About 140 cars will be competing in the race in May. Most of the cars are powered by a 600cc motorcycle engine.

    Singapore's entry is the first from South-east Asia.

    In Pontiac, all the cars will be put through rigorous testing in terms of design, engine reliability and driveability.

    Usually out of 140 cars, only 30 would survive the competition.

    The rest would be eliminated.

    While the Singapore team consists of only seven students, some teams have as many as 38 students working on their cars, sponsored by automotive giants like Ford and Chrysler.

    The Singaporeans are unfazed, though.

    Said Mr Leong, who designed the chassis:'We've already built and tested the car. It moves and handles well. Half the battle is won. The other half of the battle is finishing the race in one piece and not be disqualified for some silly reason.'

    Thanks to the sponsorship of Singapore Petroleum, Yokohama YHI, Wai Seng Hydraulics, Dynaglass, Igus, Man Exhaust and NUS, the team is better equipped this year.
