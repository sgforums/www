    Did you have a strained (inflamed) or torn ligament?

    If the ligament is inflamed, it would require a period of total rest to heal fast. The reason why some inflamed ligaments, especially of the legs and feet, may take longer periods to heal is because people with the inflamed ligament do not stop the aggravating activity completely or get enough rest to let the ligament heal. For example, if you have an inflamed ligament at the sole of the foot, you should not walk too much and stretch the ligament unneccessarily.

    If the ligament is torn, it cannot fully heal itself. Surgery is required to repair the torn ligament.

    Take care.

    Originally posted by the.raven:
    under what circumstances will a ligament be torn? and how do u tell?
    A ligament could become strained or torn through sudden stress or movement of the muscles. Common causes include improper stretching before strenuous sports activities.

    It is hard to tell whether the ligament is torn or inflamed as both conditions may cause pain and swelling at the affected location. There may be a way to distinguish the severity of the injury, but a medical practitioner would be in the best position to diagnose the injury and determine if the ligament is torn or just inflamed.

  • Othello_Red
    10 Jun 05, 20:48
    There are different degrees of ligament tears. Not all require surgery to repair, but most do. Also, x-rays or CAT scans are mainly to check if you've broken anything. MRIs give you a better picture of the injury because they produce more high-definition images. Most times, the doctor will request that you go for at least a standard MRI to confirm the extent of the injury. If it requires surgery, normally it will be a minimally invasive procedure called arthroscopic surgery. Total reconsturction of the torn ligament through open surgery is pretty rare these days. With arthroscopic surgery and rehabilitative physiotherapy, you normally can be back to normal withtin 6-8 months.
