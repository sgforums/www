    Not an expert but will try to explain to the best of my knowledge.

    Bonds = Government/Cooperate issued Fixed Deposit essentially.

    You buy it at $1, they promised 4 Cents payout per annum for 10 years (as an example). After they sell the bond to public, it is traded on the stock exchange like everything else.

    Since this may compare more-favourable or less favourable to $$ in the bank or other other investments, the traded price maybe slight above $1 or below $1. In the extreme case, it is possible then that this may be traded at say $0.80. Factoring in the annum interest payout over the bond's remaining years, it may give a better or worse yield than other types of investment. When government/cooperate sees that it is traded at a very low price and they have extra cash to do so, they may choose to buy back those bonds to write it off.

    Gahmen sell bonds (Pulic Sector Borrowing Requirement) also to increase GDP, especially in recession. Can also.

    In recession, remember the non discretionary fiscal policy and automatic stabilisers?

    where G remain constant or high, T or tax drop due to low taxes collected. Income decrease, T decrease.

    So how to increase G to expand economy without increasing T, where increasing T will have contractionary impact?

    Simply, borrow from the public.

    Sell bonds, decrease money supply.

    Use the money to increase G, so dont have to increase T to fund G.

    But bad side - decrease money supply, increase interest rate, decrease C and I

    especially I, because pte investments will go down due to high interest in borrowings.

    This is called the crowding out effect.
