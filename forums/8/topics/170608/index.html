    SINGAPORE, Dec 22 (Reuters)

    THE STRAITS TIMES

    - Thousands of workers in Singapore's computer disk-drive factories face a new round of job cuts after the world's biggest hard-disk maker, Seagate Technology, said it was taking over beleaguered Maxtor Corp. The move comes just nine months after Maxtor said that it is axing two-thirds of its 8,000-strong Singapore workforce as it shuts down a plant in Singapore by March next year.

    Seagate, which is buying Maxtor in a US$1.9 billion (S$3.2 billion) share-swap deal, already employs 8,500 workers in Singapore, so it is likely to close Maxtor's last-remaining factory.
