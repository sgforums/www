    It was this perception by my folks that made them pressure me to go into a JC.

    I had the very pragmatic perception then that the polytechnic actually taught us REAL SKILLS that we needed to go into the job market. And after that, if we wanted, we could still further our studies in the University with 1 year of exemptions!

    But the difference would be with just A-levels, one would not be as well-prepared for a job as one with a Diploma.

    In the end, I ended up in a JC. Kind of wondered sometimes if I had actually stuck to my guns and went to poly.

    Polytechnic education is in no way inferior to a junior college education. In the first place, the two cannot be compared at all because of the different type of education they offer.

    The society used to perceive that a polytechnic education was inferior to education in a junior college mainly because one did not need a good O level score to enter a polytechnic, unlike JC.

    Personally, I do not find it a waste of time if some students actually head for a polytechnic after not making it anywhere useful with their A level results. Some people simply find it longer to find their right paths! And I'm sure they learnt something in JC. With an A level cert, students may be exempted from certain modules in a polytechnic, so the A level certificate may not be useless after all.

    Polytechnic education is more of practicality whereas a junior college education is more theoretical. In my honest opinion, besides being an easier route to a university, a junior college education is useless in the sense that a student learns something so much in depth and yet most are unable to apply what they have learnt, let alone put it to use.
