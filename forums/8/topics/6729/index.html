    Originally posted by Illegal Operation:
    Online retailing will have no place in cyberspace...
    Not true, online retailing is a totally new method of retailling whose potential is waiting to be unleashed. Please allow me to explain.

    Reach is about access connection. It means how many customers a business can connect with and how many products it can offer to those customers. Reach is the most visible difference between electronic and physical businesses and it has been the primary competitive differentiator for e-commerce so far.

    Richness is the depth and detail of information that the business can give the customer. Richness holds enormous potential for building relationship with customers in a future dominated by e-commerce.

    Traditional businesses (physical businesses) have always had to make a trade-off between richness and reach. Doing both - getting highly detailed, customised information to and from a massive audience - was prohibitively expensive. E-commerce businesses can exploit the dramatic displacement of the trade-off permitted by electronic connectivity and information standards.

    With little money, the e-commerce can provide a wide base of customer (reach) and access to a wide range of products (also reach) and detailed information about each product (richness) It can also collect huge amounts of info about each customer (richness again) and use it to sell more products and services.

    In e-commerce, or electronic retailing, we are no longer selling products or services, we are selling *information* This is something which the physical business can't accomplish at a low cost.

    E-retailors with navigational functions are shifting their affliation towards customers. When i say "affliation" i mean whose interests the business represents. It's a natural progression for pure navigators to affliate with customers. And traditional manufacturers and retailors must find ways to fight or imitate their e-commerce competitors' affliation strategies.

    So I say, e-commerce is here to stay and so is e-retailing. And their potential is not to be underestimated.
