    Okay. Just to share some valuable knowledge with you guys.

    The HR department has lots of resumes coming in everyday responding to the advertisments posted on the papers.

    Anyway, here's what's really going on behind the scenes when choosing resumes.

    Here are some short pointers.

    `` I've noticed that you don't have to have good qualification to earn $3000++ a month. In fact, there were quite a lot of people from this category.

    `` People applying for position more often than not ask for a lower salary than their previous job

    `` Never send in handwritten resume.

    `` Don't use too informal resume writing style

    `` Insert a professional looking photo and not some photo you took in the market

    ``Please use A4 size paper for your resume

    `` Previous working experience counts.

    `` Education counts.

    `` Activities you participate in school counts, when you're applying for managerial positions. A plus if you're a debating team caption, sports captain or hold any managerial position

    I temped before assisting HR Manager collate applications coming in through email, post and fax.

    As far as I noticed, those whose email came in later got to viewed first, while those who applied/sent in first were last to be seen or may not even get seen. Emails usually sorted latest on top. Moral of the story: don't send in too early, haha

    By fax, no privacy, most offices use common fax unless heng heng HR department has their own. I see my colleagues gathering around fax viewing the resumes sent in and making all sorts of comments.

    Post. What is an eyesore is applications with more than even 3 folds. My God, when you unfold, they cannot be straightened. So curled up and difficult to file also. My advice is 2 to 3 folds, if not, don't stinge and just get an A4 envelope and post it all straight.
