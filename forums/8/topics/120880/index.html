    Regarding about the psychology course, it doesn't matter which uni you study from, as long as at the end of the day, it is recognized by the Singapore Psychology Society. But, getting a basic degree won't enable you to earn the title of Psychologist. You definitely have to study for an honours, or at least a Master's degree( in Australia) or at least a PhD( in States).
    There are also education centres that offered Distance learning psychology degrees. But, before you applied for those psychology courses, make sure that they are recognized as a "Distance-learning" program in Psychology with their country's psychology society. Or else, all your time, money and effort will go down into the drain. You are able to study part-time with those distance-education program. However, they only offer the basic degree. If you want to further into Honours or Master, you will have to do it overseas. But, the good point is that, once you gain your honours or Master (in Australia), you can apply for the psychologist title in Australia with the Australian Psychology Society. Once you fulfilled their requirements, you can register with them as a psychologist. At the same time, you can come back to Singapore and register yourself as a Psychologist in Singapore. Singapore Psychologist Society will accept your application and automatic transfer if you are already registered with the psychology society overseas. In a way, you are actually recognized as a psychologist in both Singapore and overseas.
    Hope that helps!
