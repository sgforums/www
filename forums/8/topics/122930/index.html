    a couple of years ago land into a new job company making I/O cards so i got intereted a little in computer although my job more on storage hardware casing, cahssis and parts. realised that building a PC DIY so simple and the knowledge you gain can last throughout if you intend to DIY forever. the rest of the info i got it online..

    If you have a PC at home OEM and the warranty has expired, try removing the chassis/ casing covers and look into it after you have powered down the unit, unplugged all the power cords, dried and discharge/ grounded your hands and wrist. look into it and you will know how a PC works. Ask me i can only tell you a few simple tips.

    A PC need a big platform to transfer signals within...the biggest board is the platform and the mother of all, the mainboard. The mainboard needs a brain and the CPU is the brain...it determines the speed of the system and is only a small chip but the biggest sized IC you can see in the market with the special name "cetral processor unit". the brain works fast and need a HSF (heat sink fan) and that bulk of aluminum/ copper with a big black fan on it is the CPU/ HSF assy. signals are handed in and out and some need to take a queueing number or wait somewhere until they are ready for retreival..the RAM (random access memory) take cae of that. the ram slots are usually the colored slots nearest to the CPU. at the middle of the mainboar dis one unique slot for the graphics called the AGP slot. modern PC applications are graphics intensive and this card handles all that need to be displayed over your monitor. the big rectangular cube box with the most colored cables running in and out in bundles is the PSU )power supply unit) and it feeds wattage of energy into the mainboard and to the hardisk/s up to as high as 500 watts and more. the white identical connections below the AGGP slot are the PCI slots...for other rubbish and miscellaneous card installation (e.g TV tuner card, SCSI ctrl card, sound card...) nearest to the RAM slots are the IDE/ SATA connections for your hard disks and CD-rom. CD-romm, RW/ DVD-rom are basi interface hardwares that reads compact dics and DVDs via a high temp light source, encoding and decoding meanwhile to extract datd from the discs to the sytem and to your display. The harddisk is the the system's stomack and holds all the data inicluding the operating system that controls all the programmes in your computer and wha you can do with it. during startup, the bios (basic input/ output signal) is flashed and from here you can install OS or trouble shoot problems, choos how you want to boot or where you want to boot from...after that, only will the OS load and you can proceed to do what you want wif ur PC....to many things to day but such are basic stuff that do not require any class lessons or triaingins at all.....surf the net , explore and learn.....

    bascially during diy u need the following:

    1) CPU
    2) Motherboard
    3) Memory(RAM)
    4) Harddisk(HDD)
    5) Sound Card(unless u using onboard) + Speakers
    6) Video Card(unless u using onboard)
    7) P.S.U (Power supply unit) <--this most important as it can fry your whole system(get good ones like Antec or Verudium
    8 ) Casing (important also cos will affect your sys and cpu temps~) get good ones like Lian Li or Centurion (where the front bottom got 2 intake and rear top got 2 outake fans)
    oh ya some casings come with a P.S.U packaged, always say u dont want that P.S.U and get a better P.S.U like Antec or Verudium
    9) Optical storage(DVD-ROM,DVD-RW) for dvd-rom get the MSI dvd-rom cos true region free
    10) keyboard and mouse if u want new ones
