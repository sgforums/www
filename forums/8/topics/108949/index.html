    Missing girl found
    By Ben Nadarajan and Lee Hui Chieh

    A NINE-YEAR-OLD Filipino girl who went missing from her home in Potong Pasir on Wednesday morning was found alive and well yesterday, after an extraordinary 24-hour escapade.

    Hundreds of police and volunteers involved in the search - memories of the tragic hunt for murdered eight-year-old Huang Na fresh in their minds - breathed a collective sigh of relief as news broke that Kaye Charish Mae Patino had been found.

    But as the little girl told her story, relief quickly became amazement.

    Kaye left her home at about 9am to buy sweets at a shop downstairs. But instead of going back upstairs, where she had left the television on, she decided to go on an adventure.

    With no money or ez-link card, Kaye sneaked onto the MRT and spent several hours riding the North East Line, from Potong Pasir to Little India, back to Boon Keng and finally all the way down to Harbourfront, where she met her best friend.

    After playing with her friend, she then slipped onto a crowded bus headed for Orchard Road, where she hooked up with tissue seller Leo Keow Ngo outside Lucky Plaza.

    For an hour she helped sell tissues, before the old woman took Kaye back to her flat in French Road near Jalan Besar, where she spent the night sleeping on old newspapers in the corridor, unaware of the frantic search taking place.

    The breakthrough her desperate parents had hoped for came at 9.45am yesterday, when a contractor having breakfast in a French Road coffee shop saw Kaye with Madam Leo and called the police.

    On being reunited with her mother, Mrs Conchita Patino, Kaye's first words were: 'I love you, ma.'

    Though there is as yet no clear explanation of why Kaye went walkabout, Mrs Patino is just happy to have her back.

    'I have received the best Christmas present of my life today. I thought my girl was lost, but now she's found.'
