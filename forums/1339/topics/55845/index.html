    Abandoned Punggol Pig Farm

    Upon reaching the place, we were actually greeted by a large group (abt 8 ~ 10?) bikers standing outside the entrance to the farm. Upon questioning, we found out that they were actually contemplating whether to enter the place to explore or not. SUE quickly persuaded them to join us and they agreed.

    Everyone had to climb over a barrier that was blocking vehicles from entering the farm. Once everyone was in, SUE headed for the sties where the pigs were reared in.



    SUE checking out the place~!



    As expected the place was run down and dirty. There was also a faint pungent smell hanging in the air. Reminiscent of the pigs, perhaps?

    Queer Buddha Statue

    Standing at the entrance of the pig sties was a queer Golden Buddha statue. Upon further inspection, we found out that the rear part of the BUddha statue was broken....smashed...





    The statue could have been broken by falling debris from the roof, judging from the dilapilatd state of the ceiling. But then again, there was no debris anywhere near the statue. So what else could have broke it? Other explorers? The previous owners while moving it? Or something else~?



    The place where the statue was standing..

    Big hole in the ground~?

    At the end of the sty was a big hole in the ground. As it was raining earlier in the day, the ditch was filled with water. Covering the ditch was a makeshift structure with a zinc roof(see pic below). Funny that someone might wanna dig a hole and build a structure over it. Whatever was inside the hole was, sadly, submerged in the water by now.



    As SUE was checking out the hole, there was a quant little orb near the ceiling looking down and observing us...

    Next destination ~ War bunker @ Malta Cresent

    Our next destination was the War Bunker @ Malta Cresent. A war relic like this shouldn't be missed right~!



    The entrance to the bunker~....

    When we reached the entrance of the bunker, we found out that the ladder leading down was held by a slab of concrete. After "testing" out whether the concrete could hold the weight of a person, we found out that the concrete was quite unsafe if not held by a person while another climbed down. Our nobel Angeldark volunteered to hold down the concrete slab while the others climbed down into the depths of the bunker...





    Once inside, we realised that the air was quite thin and that it would be quite stuffy for us to stay inside for an extended period of time. Which makes me wonder, how uncomfortable it would be for the British when they had to hide in her during the air raids of WW2.



    The walls of the bunker were still quite new. Tree and grass roots were growing through some cracks in the wall and ceiling though.





    This opening in the middle of the bunker could have been an air vent or a gun turret.





    Grafitti on the bunker walls...

    Well, hope these photos give you a rough idea of how these two places look like. Another successful outing for SUE~!
