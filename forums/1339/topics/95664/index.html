    I checked out the place on can.com.sg, and it was listed as Cathay Apartments, which is linked to the old Cathay Building. The hill that it stands on is Mount Sophia.

    Did some research on it and here's what I got:

    From a blog of a MGS alumni
    "The other day I was cutting my way from the old Cathay cinema towards Plaza Singapura when I stopped and saw something that brought back many fond memories of my mispent youth. tucked between what used to be Picturehouse (those of you of a certain vintage (sic) will remember this place) and Cathay Apartments is a nondescript flight of steps known fondly to students of MGS back when it was til at Mt Sophia as "The hundred steps'. it wasn't exactly one hundred, more like 97 or 98, but somewhere along the way, it was just stuck with the name."

    "Memories of trudging up this flight of steps every morning came flooding back. It was a good short cut then, despite the early morning excercise. the other alternative would have been to walk all the way pass Paradiz centre and walk up Adis Road. sometimes, when we were feeling particularly lucky, we'd use the lift at Cathay Apartments. Just take the lift to the 7th floor, door open and we're right at the top. of course, the security there didn't take too kindly to the infringement and once in a while, Mrs Tham would announce over the PA during morning assembly that under no circumstances were we to use the elevators at Cathay Apartments to get to school. the girls will obey for about all of 2 weeks and after that, we'll be back again..."

    "Picturehouse and Cathay cinema is now no longer. in its place, there's some construction going on. but it was really nice to see that the hundred steps has not succumbed to development in the ever changing landscape of singapore."

    Here's more photos of that place:







    "Cathay Building & Cinema closed in December 1998. The 17th-storey building was the tallest building in South-east Asia when it was completed in October 1939.

    Built by Frank Brewer, it was designed as a hotel. A subsequent glut of hotel space led to its transformation into an office building, both by filling in all the balconies, and making bay windows."

    "Cathay building and cinema is now undergoing a S$100-million redevelopment which will see the construction of a new 15-storey complex, comprising offices, apartments, cinemas, and retail space. The historic building's brown-tiled facade will be preserved. The new complex will be ready by the end of 2005."
