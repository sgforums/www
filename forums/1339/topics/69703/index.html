    The 16-year-old native of Saransk, a town 400 miles from capital Moscow in Western Russia, claims that she can see inside human bodies, thanks to her X-ray vision.

    It all began when Natasha was 10. One day, she told her stunned mum Tatiana that she could see "two beans," "a tomato" and a "vacuum cleaner" inside her.

    Natasha was actually referring to Tatiana's kidneys, heart and intestines but didn't know the organs names.

    Initially, doctors dismissed her claims, but after exhaustive tests, they were left baffled.

    "I recognise this girl has unusual abilities," The Sun quoted Dr Irina Kachan, who conducted tests on Natasha in her hometown, as saying.

    Added another doctor" "She is like a human X-ray machine."

    "We were deeply sceptical at first, fearing this girl was seeking to make idiots of us."

    "But we cannot explain by normal means what she is seeing."

    In one instance, Natasha, who now plans to study medicine, drew a picture of a medic's stomach and highlighted a dark spot precisely where he had an ulcer.

    And when shown a girl suffering from multiple ailments, Natasha identified them all.

    She can even detect tiny details missed by ultrasound tests.

    "When I told my mother in detail what her inner organs looked

    like, she was scared," said the so-called "wonder girl."

    "Now, I am worried that they might be hiding something from me about why I can see through objects."

    "I don't know how it works - but I can see through things."

    "There was once a lady who had been diagnosed with cancer. I looked at her and did not notice anything like it, just a small cyst."

    Natasha, who ironically can't see inside herself, has become so famous in her country that she gets up to 20 phone calls daily and "patients" actually queue up the stairs to her family's flat.

    "I can't turn them down," she says.

    "I don't accept money either. I am often exhausted by the end of the day." (ANI)
