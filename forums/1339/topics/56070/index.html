Coincidence and Identical Twins

    On 19 August 1939 twin boys were born in Piqua, Ohio, USA. One was adopted by the Springer family in Dayton, Ohio, who thought the brother had died. The named their baby James. The second child was adopted by the Lewis family of Lima, Ohio,and they too believed that the twin was dead. By coincidence Baby Lewis was also called James.

    Six year later Jess and Lucille Lewis accidentally found out that their adopted son's brother was alive. Then in January 1979 their son James Lewis began looking for his twin, and after only a few weeks the two brother were reunited -- at the age of 39.

    The Jims were monozygotic, or identical, twins -- created from the same single fertilised ovum that split in two, and sharing the same placenta in the uterus. It is hardly surprising that genetic make-up should produce similarities beyond physical appearance. But the coincidences in the Jims' lives were astonishing.

    The twins had first wives called Linda, then they divorced and both married women called Betty. Lewis named his first son James Alan; Springer's was James Allan. Both had dogs answering to Toy. The two men had worked as gas station attendants and for the same hamburger chain. The twins drove the same type of car, and smoked the same cigarette and drank the same beer brands. They had regularly taken their annual vacation at the same resort in Florida. The both disliked baseball but enjoyed stock-car racing and woodwork. Both gained and lost our kilograms at the same age, bit their fingermails compulsively and had experienced a minor heart attack. And the Jims had bothe suffered haemorrhoids and migraines.

    Thomas Bouchard, professor of psychology at the University of Minnesota, recorded the similarities of the Jim twins and studied the lives of other identical twins who had been separated at birth. An English pair, Bridget Harrison and Dorothy Lowe, born in 1943, met for the first time in 1979. The lives of the twins matched in various ways. Bridget's son, for instance was named Andrew Richard, and Dorothy's son Richard Andrew. Then in 1962 they had both kept diaries for only one year in books of the same make and colour. The days they filled in and those they left blank corresponded exactly.

    Professor Bouchard explored the role of genetic inheritance as opposed to environmental factors. But his findings and other examples of coincidence, such as the synchronicity of pain at a great distance or, in rare cases, death, sugges there are psychic connections between idential twins.
