    Preview

    Blackburn boss Mark Hughes believes his super-fit squad are finally prepared to prise themselves out of the Barclays Premiership drop zone.
    Hughes admitted fitness levels were a concern when he took over at Ewood Park two months ago.

    But having successfully established a stronger training regime Hughes believes it is only a matter of time before the hard work begins to pay off.

    Rovers meet Birmingham in a cruciual test at Ewood Park on Sunday without a win in six Premiership games.

    Hughes said: ``I felt that given the demands of the Premiership fitness was an area in which we could do better.

    ``If you don't think you can get through the 90 minutes physically, then concentration levels go.

    ``It was something we needed to address and the players have shown a lot of application in their work.

    ``I think they feel faster and fitter now and soon they will begin to reap the rewards.''

    Hughes' Premiership bow has been far from easy with Rovers facing the possible scenario of kicking off against Steve Bruce's Blues in bottom place.

    He knows the five games between now and Christmas will play a major part in determining the success or otherwise of his first job in Premiership management.

    ``Improving our home form is a must for us and if we do that we will be okay,'' said Hughes, who insists on accentuating the positives of a largely dismal season so far.

    ``It's a home game and we need to get three points and hopefully we can capitalise on a bit of nervousness from Birmingham.

    ``It's important my players keep their belief in what they are doing. They are getting some reward for their efforts but not as much as they deserve.''

    ``We've had three draws in a row and we are being consistent which is the key. ``But too often we are giving ourselves a mountain to climb by going behind. When we start getting our noses in front we will be okay.''

    Hughes could face a striking headache with Jonathan Stead requiring a late test on the ankle he injured in last week's draw with Manchester City.

    Stead's natural replacement Jay Bothroyd is still suspended so Hughes finds himself short of options to partner Paul Dickov up front.

    Craig Short also needs a test on the hamstring injury which sidelined him last week, while David Thompson is in line for his first start of the year.

    ]BIG-MATCH FACTS

    BLACKBURN ROVERS     engage Birmingham City in a Sunday afternoon bottom six clash, and first meeting since the last day of last season. By kick off time Rovers, who've drawn their last three Premiership matches, could be back on the foot of the table.

    The club from the Red Rose county have only won one of 14 League and Cup games this season - that being Mark Hughes' first in charge. They prepare to face the Brummies after two 1-1 draws at Norwich and Manchester City. Their goal from the game at the City of Manchester Stadium was a spot kick from Paul Dickov - their first penalty award in a Premiership game since the 2002/03 season.

    These clubs met each other three times last season and there was little to separate them overall. Rovers won the League fixture at St Andrews 0-4, and then Birmingham returned the favour in a home FA Cup tie. This corresponding fixture ended 1-1 in May when a last minute header from substitute Stern John cancelled out Andy Cole's opener.

    BIRMINGHAM CITY are only three points better off than Blackburn, and have completed just one victory in ten top tier engagements, as they embark on their 2,500th bona fide post war League game.

    Steve Bruce's Blues go to Blackburn looking for back to back victories away from home, having picked up their first this season two weeks ago in a 0-1 triumph over Liverpool at Anfield when Darren Anderton netted the winner. Birmingham last managed successive victories on their top division travels on 30 August and 20 September last year when they came away from Newcastle and Leeds with maximum points.

    The Brummies are yet to record their first victory over Blackburn in the Premiership (two draws, two defeats so far). Their only maximum in 23 League visits to Ewood Park over 50 years was 1-2 on 20 January 1668 in the original Second Division (36 years ago). Their last top flight maximum there was also 1-2 on 28 September 1935.
