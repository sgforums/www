    Top tie on the cards on Sunday

    Bernardo Romeo: Starting line-up?

    HSV entertain the current Bundesliga table-toppers VfL Wolfsburg in the AOL Arena on Sunday, as both sides meet for a Bundesliga round 14 tie. The clash promises to be one of the more entertaining of the round, featuring two sides who like to play attacking football. Wolfsburg has lost its last three matches away from home, and will be hoping to turn things around in Hamburg on Sunday. Coach Erik Gerets and Co. has surprised many football pundits by more or less dominating the Bundesliga over the last couple of months. The 3-0 home win over high-flying Stuttgart in round 13 however, proved that the Wolves are top on merit.

    HSV tails are up

    Things have taken a turn for the better since Thomas Doll took charge from Klaus ToppmÃ¶ller, who was sacked five weeks ago. In round 13 last Sunday, HSV thrashed Hansa Rostock 6-0 away from home, so the Wolves' defensive line will know that they have a tough task on Sunday. A win for Thomas Doll and his side could move them up to ninth should other results go HSV's way. Wolfsburg has won only once in Hamburg. They beat HSV 2-1 in the 1997/8 season. On that day, the Wolves skipper Stefan Schnoor scored the goal for HSV.

    Reinhardt out, Weiser doubtful

    Defender Bastian Reinhardt has failed to recover in time from his thigh injury, his place in the squad will go to Christian Rahn who impressed in training this week. Belgian striker Emile Mpenza will not start the match; the goal-getter has been plagued by an allergic reaction all week. The Wolves could be without Patrick Weiser for the first time this season. The veteran defender is having trouble shaking off a neck muscle injury, sustained during his side's home win over Stuttgart.

    Naughty naughty...

    There are several players just one yellow card away from a one-match ban, ahead of the clash in the AOL Arena, for which around 45,000 tickets have been sold so far. HSV's Barbarez, Beinlich, Wicky, Jarolim and Boulahrouz are all on four yellow cards, Schnoor and Karhan are the Wolfsburg players who will have to be on their best behaviour. On the good news side for Wolfsburg, the Bulgarian midfielder Marian Hristov will be in the squad for the visit to Hamburg. Playmaker Andres D'Alessandro is suspended after being sent off recently.
