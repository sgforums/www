    Showtime for S-League aspirants Tampines Rovers SC

    They have made their intentions clear of joining the S-League. What better way for Tampines Rovers SC to prove themselves on Saturday night when they face a weakened Woodlands Wellington side.

    With Woodlands playing their second match in four days and having four S-League players out, it has paved the way for amateur outfit Tampines to claim the scalp of an S-League side.

    Although there may be tired legs amongst the ranks of the Rams, coach Karim Bencherifa cannot afford to rest any of his players due to injuries to several others.

    While coach Karim may not have the depth in squad to rotate his squad, they have the undoubted quality.

    With the likes of Daniel Bennett, Goh Tat Chuan and Ahmad Latiff Khamarudin, Tampines, the NFL Division One side, will have their work cut out.

    While Woodlands come into this match after a heavy 3-0 defeat in the S-League, Tampines earned themselves a morale-boosting 4-1 win over Police SA last week.

    Not surprisingly, Bencherifa was at the game to gather intelligence for this Singapore Cup clash.

    Although the Moroccan coach does not underestimate the NFL outfit, he wants his team to put up a solid performance and earn a good result.
