    BIG-MATCH FACTS

    ARSENAL     attempt to get their bid for successive Premiership titles back on track, against a Birmingham side, who are yet to win or even score against them in the Premier League. The Gunners need a confidence boosting result here ahead of Tuesday's decisive Champions League final group game, at home to Rosenborg.

    The Gunners lost 2-1 at Liverpool last Sunday, but have not suffered back-to-back defeats in 80 Premiership games since consecutive 1-2 losses away to Everton and home to Blackburn on 19 and 26 October 2002. They must now play catch-up, as they aim to stretch their record unbeaten home run in the Premiership to 28 matches. They surrendered the leadership to Chelsea a month ago, and now find themselves five points adrift having dropped 12 points from the last 18. Indeed in November, their record was inferior to that of lowly Norwich. But Arsene Wenger's side have scored more than three times as many League goals as Birmingham, and it could be argued that this match pits the Premiership's most attractive and the least entertaining sides. The 15 top flight matches involving the Gunners have produced 59 goals, where Birmingham's 15 have yielded just 27.

    Arsenal suffered a hangover from their title celebrations when frustrated by Birmingham in last season's corresponding fixture in May, which ended goalless. The Gunners have picked up 10 points out of the 12 available in Premiership contests with the Brummies.

    BIRMINGHAM CITY have registered just one League win since August, and are on their lowest points return ever to this identical stage in the Premiership.

    Steve Bruce's Blues built the longest sequence of draws in the top division this season in September and October when five successive games ended all square, and they prepare to face the champions on the back of two more stalemates. This match provides the chance to compare the sharpest and bluntest attacks in the top division. While Birmingham have only managed 12 goals this term (0.8 goals per game), the Londoners have piled in 39 (2.6 goals per game).

    Last season's goalless draw was Birmingham's second in three League visits to Highbury and third in five. The Midlanders are seeking a first win in 22 visits to the north London venue. Of the last 21, seven have been drawn and 14 lost. City's only win in 40 League visits over 78 years was 1-3 in the top flight on 19 October 1957.

    Arsenal vs B'ham preview

    It seems almost unthinkable that Arsenal could bow to a third defeat in seven days when they host Birmingham on Saturday - but only six weeks ago it was fairly hard to believe they would lose at all.

    A breathtaking injury-time strike by Neil Mellor condemned Arsenal to a second league defeat of the season last Sunday, and The Gunners' youthful reserves were expelled from the Carling Cup by a similarly under-strength Manchester United team in midweek.

    Manager Arsene Wenger has confessed that the ill form worries him, but will be hopeful that Birmingham's own patchy form, combined with a still formidable record at home, will buoy The Gunners to a much-needed win.

    Jermaine Pennant and Robin van Persie are the latest players to join a lengthy injury list that also includes Dennis Bergkamp, Edu and Gilberto Silva.

    Pascal Cygan has been ruled out but England international Sol Campbell will add much-needed steel to the defence.

    Birmingham manager Steve Bruce is another that has been frustrated with recent form.

    Blues have thrown away a half time lead in heir last two Premiership matches, and memory of last month's victory over Liverpool is already fading.

    The loss of the impressive Mario Melchiot at half time severely hampered Blues against Norwich last week, and the former Chelsea man is a doubt for Saturday's game.

    Muzzy Izzet will face a late check on his knee problem, whilst Stan Lazaridis is also doubtful with his hamstring problem.

    Julian Gray missed the Norwich game, but has shaken off his illness and has been declared fit.
