    Originally posted by dumbdumb!:
    but the argument here, is whether mary's role gives her authority as a divine being, to be worshipped like one. or even that she has the authority to intervene in any situation, just like God.
    No Christian church considers Mary divine as far as I am aware. The Roman Catholic Church does not consider her divine either, although she is highly venerated.

    As a Protestant, I do not agree with the RCC's stand on a few doctrines, such as the Immaculate Conception of Mary (1854) and the Assumption of Mary (1950), for example. I do not find the Scriptural basis convincing at all.

    As for her role in intervention, Catholics believe she can pray for us in the way you can ask your friend or pastor to pray for you. Catholics do not see death as a final event that prevents you from asking someone to intercede for you. Thus they believe they can ask long departed saints to pray for them.

    There is a relatively recent movement to consider Mary as playing a crucial role in redemption along with Jesus. That move to make Mary a co-Redemptrix is supported by various people including Mother Teresa but the previous Pope had resisted that move. If the RCC make Mary Co-Redemptrix that will indeed be a major doctrinal departure from the rest of Christiandom and will raise questions of her divinity.

    But at the moment, no....Mary is not divine, even as seen through Catholic eyes.
