    Salmon Fish Oil-More Than Just Brain Food

     

    When it comes to health benefits, the ingredients contained in salmon oil make it one of the most potent dietary supplements on the market. Salmon fish oil can improve brain function, transform the appearance and provides the highest level of the omega-3 essential fatty acids that are necessary for good health.

     

    How Is Salmon Fish Oil Created?

    The best quality salmon oil is created by using a cold-press method of extracting oil from the head of the salmon fish. During normal processing, the heads are removed and the processing plant uses a flesh press to squeeze the oil out. Some companies add other natural supplements to the oil and some make pure, virgin oil with no other added ingredients.

    The Health Benefits Of Salmon Fish Oil

    Salmon is called brain food. In order to receive the most nutritional value from salmon, a person would have to eat 3 or 4 servings a week. Most people do not eat this much salmon weekly. Taking a supplement can be an important step to increasing the levels of essential fatty acids that the body needs for health.

    Salmon fish oil contains DHA which is what the human brain tissue is made from. The human brain requires DHA for proper cognitive function. Nature made fish oil like salmon can improve memory. Oil derived from the salmon also contains the highest levels of omega-3 fatty acids that are considered a “super supplement” according to health research. Correcting the levesl of Omega-3 in the body has been shown to reduce blood pressure, lower cholesterol, control migraines and ease depression.

    The oil of the salmon fish has other health benefits as well:

    1. Promotes a strong immune system
    2. Protects the heart
    3. Provides lubrication to stiff joints
    4. Protects vision

    Using Nature Made Fish Oil Can Improve Your Appearance

    The benefits of salmon fish oil include improving the appearance of the skin. Nature made fish oil like salmon is known as the “internal moisturizer”. People who begin taking salmon oil supplements notice that their hair and nails become healthier looking. It also has the added benefit of easing skin conditions like inflammation and dry, itchy skin. The ingredients contained in salmon oil can moisturize the skin and organs from within making dry, wrinkly skin appear much smoother.

    Not only does this supplement provide excellent anti-aging properties from within, it can make a person look younger and more attractive on the outside. Today’s modern dietary habits make getting enough essential fatty acids in the daily diet almost impossible. The benefits of salmon fish oil make it the top choice when it comes to getting the most out of a fish oil product for health. Oil derived from the salmon fish is indeed a super supplement.

    How Drinking Tea Can Make You More Beautiful

     
    We show you the most effective teas to drink for a more beautiful you.

    We know that tea has countless health and beauty benefits, from slimming to slowing down the ageing process. But it’s not a case of one size fitting all – each tea is better for one use over another. So if you’re looking to drink your way to beauty and to drop a dress size, get the best cuppa for the job.

     
    The skinny on pu’er
    Some people may baulk at the strong, earthy taste of pu’er tea, but don’t be put off so easily – this tea from the large leaf species of the tea family is a certified flab fighter.
    Various studies have shown that pu’er is the best tea to accompany heavy, greasy meals due to its ability to break down oily food and aid in digestion. Drinking it regularly over a one-month period can significantly lower cholesterol levels in the blood stream. In fact, it is proven to be as effective as some cholesterol-lowering drugs, but without any of the side effects.
     
     
    My fair lady
    Among the latest ingredients to excite the skincare industry is white tea. Much of its goodness comes from the fact that it undergoes minimal processing. The leaves are air-dried instead of fried like other teas, as this process keeps them closer to their original state and seals in their natural properties. Not only does it contain less caffeine than black and green tea, it also packs a higher level of antioxidants. Some studies suggest that this ups its efficacy in combating signs of ageing and promoting overall body wellness. Supplies of white tea are fairly limited as soil and weather requirements make it hard to be cultivated outside its source in Fujian, China.
