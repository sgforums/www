    Age buster : retinoids

    FORMS
    Retinol, Retinaldehyde. Retinyl Ester. They are derivatives of Vitamin A.

    WHAT THEY DO
    Improve the complexion by refining skin texture and smoothing out lines. Says Dr Ang, "The gold standard of the retinols is retinoic acid or tretinoin (Retin A, Retacnyl, Stieva A) which has been scientifically proven to be effective.

    "Retinol exfoliates dead skin cells, spreads out uneven pigmentation. The idea is that this will stimulate collagen production and help improve the appearance of wrinkles and pores. Having said that, retinoids do not lighten complexion as much as bleaching agents do."

    Skin renewer: hydroxyl acids

    FORMS
    AHA(Alpha Hydorxy Acid),

    BHA (Beta Hydroxy Acid),

    PHA (Poly Hydroxy Acid)

    WHAT THEY DO
    Exfoliate and accelerate skin turnover, helps collagen formation. PHA, with its large molecules, penetrates skin more slowly with less irritation. Fat-soluble BHA is good for oily, acne-prone skin. "However, they can't reduce redness completely." Adds Dr Ang.Evans AHA 10 Revitalizing Concentrate, $19.90, from Guardian Pharmacy.

    FORMS

    Damage insurance: antioxidants

    FORMS
    Grape seed extract, green tea, soy pycnogenol, (pine bark extract), pomegranate extract, lycopene

    WHAT THEY DO
    Neutralise free radicals and protect skin from environmental damage, thus delaying the ageing process. "Antioxidants do have anti-inflammatory properties that can help to calm the skin. But they can't do much for deep wrinkles," says Dr Ang.
