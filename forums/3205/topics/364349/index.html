    All up, not bad for starters.

    I assume you're shooting in either Program or Full Auto mode, which is fine for starters although aperture-priority, shutter-priority or full manual mode will be a lot more fun once you get accustomed to the camera.

    It looks like all of the garden shots were taken on cloudy days - if you'd shot in RAW you might have been able to give the photos a bit more oomph by setting your white balance to "cloudy" or playing around with temperature settings.  You might still be able to get a bit more out of it by playing with hue and saturation in DPP.

    The exposure in the last photo was set off by the bright foreground - evaluative metering will do that.  Try setting your metering mode to spot metering before taking the shot so that the flower doesn't come out so dark.
