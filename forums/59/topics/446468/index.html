    There are many types of CCA in schools. Some are competitive CCA (eg. sports) while others are just classified as Interest Group like dance. CCA is mean Co Curricular Activities, it's a statement to show you have attended meaningful activities while you're in your student's life.

    I may be very wrong to say this... students who are from competitive CCA group who have great sportsmanship and won awards are slightly more valued than those who hold regular CCA.

    The best way is actually to be part of the school team and help the school win awards, if not at least help participate in events.  Many of us aren't sports people, but just hobbist. You can find an Interest Group which have your hobby and work from there. The key word is 'participation'. If you can win awards, that's bonus for you. Leadership role adds nothing more but a *shine*.

    You heard of Community work and Non profit organization? I know at least one close friend who wanted a place in university so much that she did community work regularly apart from school work and CCA.
