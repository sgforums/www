    We love blankety blanks. We leave them trailing at the end of our speech, screaming inside pleading the other party to hear, but often they end up as blank as before, a monumental reminder for us not to trust anyone else with our feelings again.

    Examples of blankety blanks:

    note: P and T after a separation between them

    P: Is it okay for me to call you like this? or would you rather I never dial your phone number again?
    T: My manager is not around, so I am alone here. It's fine.
    P: No, I mean me calling you for you, I wasn't asking about your manager. I don't care about your manager!
    T: Oh, it's okay. I'll tell you when I can't take it anymore. Please stop

    What a charming life!
