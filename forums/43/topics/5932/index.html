    Originally posted by [ish]:
    I have the Dreamweaver program too. However, i don't really find it very user friendly. Well, perhaps it's prolly because i didn't go through the tutorial sections thoroughly. Any advice from you people about the tricks of using the program?
    Dreamweaver not friendly? Manz.. you better not let the people at macromedia hear this...

    It really depends on what you mean by user-friendly. Dreamweaver doesn't provide as many custom coded scripts for users, as compared to Frontpage. HOWEVER, Dreamweaver allows the designer more freeplay.

    The rendering of layers, timeline, behaviour, histroy, HTML cleanup, preview, testing in browsers etc... are all pretty useful.

    You'll need greater independence and knowledge of HTML and web development to bring out the best in Dreamweaver. Frontpage is good only for standard websites, or personal pages which are simple.

    Get to know the software better, and take some time to read the dreamweaver tutorial and help files. You'll love the software once you're familiar with all it's features. You may even find it more user-friendly than frontpage.
