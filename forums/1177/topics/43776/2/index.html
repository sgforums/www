    Tonsillectomy is the surgical removal of tonsils, most often due to chronic infection. The patient is under a general anesthetic during the procedure.

    The surgeon uses special instruments, such as the Harmonic ScalpelÂ® or an electrocautery device, to cut the tissue between the tonsils and the throat. When the tissue has been dissected, the tonsils are removed. The surrounding blood vessels are then cauterized (sealed) to control bleeding.

    Tonsillectomy is a common pediatric procedure, with more than 400,000 operations performed each year in the U.S. Seventy percent of the patients are under 18, and the surgery is usually done on an outpatient basis (does not require an overnight stay).
