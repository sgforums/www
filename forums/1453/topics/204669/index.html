    Chelsea boss Jose Mourinho insists he doesn't have a problem with Rafael Benitez - but he's not so sure if the Liverpool boss has the same attitude towards him.

    Speaking ahead of Sunday's clash between the two clubs, Mourinho also made a u-turn and said that he thought Liverpool were possible Premier League candidates after initially writing us off.

    Mourinho said: "Maybe he has a problem with me - but I do not have a problem, not between me and Rafael Benitez. It is not vice versa.
    "I know my face is not always a very nice face. I am aggressive and so on - but do not make always me the bad boy.

    "I think Liverpool are also candidates for the Premiership - but more important than that I think they are candidates to win every game.

    "You can love or hate or prefer this style to that style, but you have to look at them as a difficult team to beat and a team that goes for every game with the intention of not losing or trying to win.

    "They are a very difficult team to beat - that is why in 12 matches we won six, we draw two or three and we lost two or three.

    "It is difficult to play against them."
