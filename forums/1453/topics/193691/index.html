    Benitez signs new Liverpool deal

    Rafael Benitez will now focus on turning Liverpool into Premiership title contenders having secured his own Anfield future.

    The Spaniard has signed a new four-year deal to keep him on Merseyside until 2010 having won the Champions League and FA Cup in his first two seasons.

    Benitez was linked with the Real Madrid managerial role last season with other big European clubs apparently interested

    He announced his intention to remain with the club towards the end of last season and he has now officially put pen to paper on his new contract.

    He told the club's official website: 'I'm delighted to have now signed the contract.

    'There was never going to be a problem once we reached an agreement last season, and now all the documents have been signed and sent to the club.'

    The deal represents a one-year extension to his original contract and ensures he remains on Merseyside until 2010.

    Benitez continued: 'I'm very happy at Liverpool and hope we can enjoy more success in the seasons to come.'

    The 46-year-old, who took charge almost two years ago, enjoyed a memorable first season in charge of the Anfield club.

    Although Liverpool finished fifth in the Premiership, they finished 2004-05 as kings of Europe after coming from 3-0 down to beat AC Milan on penalties in the Champions League final in Istanbul.

    Last month he led the team to more silverware - courtesy of another penalty shoot-out. Liverpool won the FA Cup, beating West Ham 3-1 on penalties after the match finished 3-3 following extra time at the Millennium Stadium.

    A third-placed finish in the Premiership means Liverpool have another chance of glory in next season's Champions League.

    Former Extremadura and Valencia boss Benitez is currently on holiday in Greece but is expected to make further changes to his personnel in his quest to bring the domestic title back to Anfield for the first time since 1990.

    Liverpool finished nine points behind Premiership champions Chelsea last season - although they did beat the Blues in the FA Cup semi-finals at Old Trafford.

    The Spaniard has already offloaded compatriot Fernando Morientes, while French international striker Djibril Cisse is also expected to leave the club.
