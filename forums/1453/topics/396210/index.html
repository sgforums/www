    I'll try to explain this as simple as I can.

    Throughout the previous seasons, Rafa has always been given +/- 20mil transfer funds per season plus any amount he generated from player sales.

    Hence:

    Feb 2009 - Sold Keane for 16mil (expecting 36mil available at end of season: 16mil from sales + the usual 20mil every year)

    June 2009 - Bought Johnson for ~6.5mil (plus 11mil for crouch, expecting 29.5 mil transfer funds left)

    July 2009 - Sold Leto for 3mil (expecting 32.5mil)

    5 Aug 2009 - Sold Alonso + Arbeloa for 33.5mil (expecting 66mil)

    7 Aug 2009 - Bought Aquilani for ~17mil to replace Alonso (expecting ~49mil left)

    (between 7 Aug to 21 Aug) - American owners refinance loans and decides not to give Rafa any transfer funds this season. Also, the profits from Alonso's sale used to repay owners' loans (you'll be pissed if you understand what's going on)

    21 Aug 2009 - Somehow coughs up ~2mil to purchase Soto as replacement for Hyypia.

     

     

    Imagine the owners stick to their word and gave Rafa the money as usual - who do you think he would have signed? It's no coincidence we were linked with some of the more expensive strikers during that same time period.

    My dates may not be 100% accurate but the facts are - Rafa was promised funds but then denied and hence messing up his plans - if you want I can look up some of the interviews during that time period where he let his frustrations show.

    I'm not a 100% rafa supporter. I booed when he took torres off and facepalm when he play lucas again and again earlier in the season. But I'm surprised at some of the posts made here suggesting how he done totally nothing for the club or how he should be sacked etc.

    Personally this season liverpool had been horrible, partly due to the injuries and insufficient squad depth (read: MONEY NOT ENOUGH). However, I think he done a great job overall considering the two clowns we have as owners and an inept ex-CEO.
