    German referee for Champions League final

    Germany's Herbert Fandel will referee the Champions League final between Liverpool and AC Milan in Athens on Wednesday, European football's governing body UEFA said Monday.

    Fandel, an experienced referee at international level since 1998, will be assisted by Carsten Kadach and Volker Wezel, UEFA said in a statement.

    The 43 year-old has proved to be something of a bogeyman for Liverpool and a lucky charm for Milan in the past.

    Fandel has officiated matches involving AC Milan five times since 2002, all of them victories for the Italian side in which they only conceded one goal.

    Meanwhile, Liverpool have never scored in three appearances with Fandel, including their 0-1 Champions League defeat at home to Barcelona in March, another defeat and a draw.

    Fandel's track record also includes an Under-21 game between Italy and England in 2000, when AC Milan's current star midfielders Gennaro Gattuso and Andrea Pirlo were on the winning side (2-0) against Liverpool's Jamie Carragher.

    Rafa admits doubts over duo

    Liverpool have fitness doubts over midfield duo Mohamed Sissoko and Boudewijn Zenden for Wednesday's UEFA Champions League final.

    Zenden suffered a sprained ankle during Liverpool's training camp in Spain last week and a similar problem has also laid low Sissoko.

    Manager Rafa Benitez continues to monitor the pair and will likely make a final decision on their chances of facing Milan during Tuesday evening's training session.

    Benitez told Sky Sports News: "We have another training session and we have to check Sissoko and Zenden - they have had some problems, we need to check today (Tuesday)."

    Benitez also revealed that Harry Kewell is in contention to start on the left should Zenden fail to recover in time.

    "Is Harry Kewell ready? Yes, I think so," added Benitez.

    Benitez denied his team will set their stall out to contain Milan and have, as such, made no special provisions to combat the threat of Kaka.

    "We always try to score goals and if it is necessary too we will defend. We know Kaka is a very good player but our system is to mark zonally and we will try to do the same."
