    Barcelona coach Frank Rijkaard has hailed Liverpool as a 'great team', as the two sides prepare to do battle in the last sixteen of the Champions League tomorrow evening.

    'I think Liverpool is a great team, they were and they still are. We are really looking forward to playing against them. It's going to be an exciting game between two strong teams,' he said.

    'Historically they are one of the greatest teams to play against. I have memories from my childhood of watching them on television - they were always a joy to watch.

    'I also like how they play now, very much. They have a great coach and great players.'

    Rijkaard also dismissed Spanish press claims that he is making special plans to counter the possible threat posed by Liverpool striker Peter Crouch.

    'It is actually not true that we have been making special preparations for Peter Crouch,' added Rijkaard.

    'He is a very important player but there are also many other important players at Liverpool.'

    Unlike Barcelona, Liverpool have had a ten day rest prior to the tie but Rijkaard refused to be drawn on whether that will prove disadvantageous to his team.

    'We'll just see tomorrow whether it is or not,' he added. 'Defeat against Valencia may act as a good motivation for us to do well against Liverpool.

    'We have a big respect for them and tomorrow the best team will win the game.'
