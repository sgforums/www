    MADRID, July 3 (Reuters) - Atletico Madrid have reached agreements to sign their former winger Luis Garcia from Liverpool and Brazilian midfielder Cleber Santana from Santos, the Primera Liga side announced on Tuesday.

    Atletico said that both players had agreed to sign three-year deals with the club who finished in seventh place in the table last season to qualify for the Intertoto Cup.

    They did not reveal the cost of either transfer but Spanish media said the club would pay around Â€4million for the 29-year-old Garcia.

    The former Liverpool winger will be presented to the media later on Tuesday but Atletico did not specify when the 26-year-old Cleber Santana would arrive in Madrid.

    While Garcia is returning to the Calderon from Anfield, Atletico's leading striker Fernando Torres is expected to move in the opposite direction.

    The Spain international is reported to have travelled to Liverpool to undergo a medical prior to a 36-million-euro move to the Merseysiders.

    Garcia played for Atletico in the 2002-03 season, scoring nine goals in 30 league games before moving to Barcelona.

    He spent just a season with the Catalans before Liverpool bought him out of his contract for nine million euros in August 2004.

    The versatile winger made an impressive start to his career at Anfield finishing his first season as joint top scorer with 13 goals and scoring some key goals in Liverpool's victorious Champions League campaign.

    He scored another 11 goals in his second campaign on Merseyside but missed the last half of this season after tearing the cruciate ligament in his right knee in a 6-3 League Cup defeat against Arsenal in January.

    Garcia, who has won 18 caps for Spain, scored 30 goals in 121 first team games for Liverpool.

    Atletico have signed six players since the end of the season including Uruguay striker Diego Forlan from Villarreal and midfielder Raul Garcia from Osasuna.

    RAFA: ALL THE BEST LUIS

    Rafa Benitez has paid tribute to Luis Garcia for his contribution to Liverpool Football Club over the past three years.
    Garcia signed for Atletico Madrid earlier this week, leaving Reds fans with a string of glorious memories.

    He scored several crucial goals during the 2005 Champions League campaign, most notably the winner against Chelsea in the semis.

    Benitez said: "I want to say 'all the best' because he was a fantastic player, a fantastic person and a fantastic professional.

    "He has been a very good player for us. He has talent and the skills to change a game. When you are at home the opponents play deep and there is not much space, but he can change this.

    "We missed him in the last six months. In some games I thought: maybe if we had Luis today it would be different."

    On why the time had come for Garcia to move on, Benitez added: "He was happy here but Atletico is a good club for him.

    "It was the right time, because after three years maybe the situation was different. He wanted to play every game and that's not easy when we have other players coming here. This was a very good solution for him and also for us."
