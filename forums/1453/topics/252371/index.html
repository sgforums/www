    Heighway brands Benitez 'crazy'

    Former Liverpool Academy coach Steve Heighway has branded Rafa Benitez 'crazy' for the Spaniard's decision to assume charge of the club's youth policy.

    Heighway left his job at Anfield last week after 19 years of nurturing now world-famous names such as Steven Gerrard and Michael Owen.

    While admitting he has the utmost respect for Benitez's managerial acumen, Heighway is adamant that his departure will prove detrimental to the club's long-term interests.

    Heighway, whose last responsibility at Anfield was to oversee Liverpool's FA Youth Cup win over Manchester United, told the Times: "Rafa is a terrific manager, but I think I'm the best coach of 17 and 18-year-old players in this club.

    "But I no longer get the chance to do that. It's crazy, mad and it's to the detriment of the young players here.

    "If they are not working with the best coach of young players, then what is this football club doing?

    "It's not an ego thing, but one thing I am absolutely sure about is that the best thing for the players is to be with me.

    "My influence is being taken away too soon over the best players and I'm not convinced that what they are going to do is better for them.

    "I would urge any football club to make sure the Academy is not one of the areas the manager controls."
