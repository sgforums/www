    Liverpool go into what is already a make or break showdown at Manchester United - even in October - with an injury crisis at just the wrong time.

    Skipper Steven Gerrard missed the midweek Champions League game in Bordeaux with a hamstring injury sustained during last weekend's home draw with Blackburn.

    Liverpool are hoping intensive treatment while his colleagues were away in south west France will leave Gerrard fit to lead out his side at Old Trafford, where the Reds go into the game eight points behind the Premier League leaders and knowing that a defeat and an 11-point margin will be virtually impossible to claw back.

    But boss Rafael Benitez also has Robbie Fowler (back) and Daniel Agger (broken hand) out of action and also unlikely to face Sir Alex Ferguson's men.

    Striker Craig Bellamy believes the squad have just got to get on with things, while insisting that Liverpool have a big enough squad to cope.

    Following the encouraging if not hugely entertainingt Champions League victory away to Bordeaux on Wednesday, the Welshman said: 'We have taken some criticism this season, even with the Champions League away victory we still have a poor away record despite being unbeaten at home, and we know what is at stake at United.

    'But you have to get on with it and show character. There's enough players with a lot of character at this club to handle the situation.

    'After the Champions League we now have Manchester United next in the league, and we know that's tough. It's a big week for us. We have some hard games and from now to Christmas I don't think we have a week where there's not two games for us.

    'But we have a lot of players and the manager will rotate us. Obviously that's nothing to do with me. He picks the best team he thinks fit for each game.

    'We are a big enough club and squad to get on with it and try to do as well as you can.'

    Benitez also accepts the importance of Sunday's clash, saying: 'We know we have Manchester United after the Champions League, and there is a gap now, so we know how important this game is. But every game against your main rivals is important, this is no different. You must win, or at least get a good result.

    'Now we will turn our attention to Manchester United. We know we are eight points behind, but we must keep going and think about our own game, and I believe that after the international breaks our team will improve.

    'You never want to lose against anyone, and especially against United, and considering the situation this is a game that is really important to win.

    'We know we have not been playing well at times, but now we have more time with the players with the international breaks over, we can spend more time working with them. And that can improve things. That will help us to start doing things really well on the pitch.'
