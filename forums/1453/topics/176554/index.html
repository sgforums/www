    The Story behind Defoe's deal - KopTalk Insider's

    Following on from last night's report on KOPTALK news that Liverpool had failed with an attempt to bring Jermaine Defoe to Anfield, we can now add further information about what appears to have taken place.

    The 23-year-old wants out of White Hart Lane after becoming frustrated with Martin Jol's tactics. Jol believes that Defoe and Robbie Keane cannot play together because they both lack "muscle". As a result Jol insists on playing Keane and Mido together with Defoe often rooted to the bench.

    KOPTALK understands that Liverpool were informed that Defoe would be up for a move to Anfield if a bid was accepted by Spurs. Manchester United became aware of our interest and made a late offer yesterday, that was rebuffed.

    Defoe was prepared to reject Manchester United to secure a move to Anfield but Liverpool's proposal of a straight swap with Djibril Cisse going in the other direction was turned down by Spurs chairman Daniel Levy.

    Had Liverpool offered hard cash, Spurs would certainly have sold. The Reds tried to value Djibril Cisse is at Â£12million but that didn't wash with Levy.

    It is extremely likely that Liverpool chief executive Rick Parry and manager Rafael Benitez will play down their attempts to sign Defoe to try and prevent Cisse from getting the hump who wouldn't have been told of the bid unless it was accepted. Even then Cisse could have rejected the move as he's desperate to remain at Anfield. Liverpool also don't want to make it look like they are keen to sell the Frenchman as that will have an effect on his future valuation should other clubs make an approach for him.
