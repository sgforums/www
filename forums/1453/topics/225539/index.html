    A word from Rafa:

    RAFA: WE HAVE NOTHING TO LOSE

    Liverpool manager Rafael Benitez is already licking his lips in anticipation of facing Barcelona in the last sixteen of the Champions League early next year.
    Benitez admits today's draw in Nyon could have been kinder to his side, but he is confident his players will be fully prepared to face the defending champions when the games come around.

    He told Liverpoolfc.tv: "We know they are a good team. At the moment they are playing in Japan in the World Club Championships and everybody is saying really good things about them.

    "They won the Champions League last season and so it is a difficult draw for us but we have two months to prepare for these games and we have nothing to lose.

    "Everybody says Barcelona are one of the best sides in the world so we have to upset the situation. We have to be mentally ready to play against very good players.

    "It is too soon to start talking about individual battles. We are looking forward to the games and we will play them with confidence.

    "I have experience of playing at the Nou Camp with Valencia and we did quite well there. Hopefully we'll do well again."

    Liverpool will travel to Spain for the first leg before welcoming Barcelona to Merseyside two weeks later and Benitez believes playing the second leg at a packed Anfield could work to his side's advantage.

    "The main thing for us is we know we'll play the second game at Anfield. This could be an important factor for us," he added.

    "It's an amazing atmosphere in our stadium when we play in Europe, and we know how good our fans are at putting pressure on other teams.

    "But I know it will be the first game which can make things possible for us. Last season we paid because we lost a late goal in Benfica and that cost us in the second leg."

    Benitez convinced Liverpool have edge on Barcelona
    tribalfooball.com - December 15, 2006

    Liverpool boss Rafa Benitez is full of confidence after drawing Barcelona in their Champions League tie yesterday.

    Benitez insists his 2005 heroes have nothing to fear when they walk out in the Nou Camp for the first leg.

    Benitez was Valencia chief when they smashed the Barca-Real Madrid dominance. They lifted two titles and won twice in Barcelona in three games.

    Benitez warned: "Over the last six or eight years Valencia have a good record against them, especially in the Nou Camp.

    "The scores have usually gone in Valencia's favour and they were something of a bogey team for them. They have certainly caused Barcelona a lot of problems.

    "We know how good their players are but we certainly won't go there with any fear. As a manager I love playing these kind of games."

    Liverpool are the ONLY English side to win at the Nou Camp when John Toshack hit the only goal in 1976.

    Benitez is pleased the second leg is at Anfield. He added: "I think that could be a real advantage."

    Eto'o two weeks away from Barcelona return?

    YAOUNDE, Jan 5 (Reuters) - Cameroon striker Samuel Eto'o, sidelined since September with a knee injury, pledged he would return to his Spanish club Barcelona at the end of next week and was determined to be the leading scorer in Spain this season.

    'What I want, I'm looking forward to, is this famous Sunday of January 14 when I will put on the Barca jersey again,' he told a media conference in Cameroon's capital Yaounde late on Thursday.

    'I had wanted to resume duty with the Indomitable Lions (Cameroon's national team) but I can't afford to wait until March,' he said. Cameroon are scheduled to play their next African Nations Cup qualifying game against Liberia in March.

    Eto'o injured his right knee during Barcelona's Champions League match against Werder Bremen last September.

    A Barcelona doctor initially estimated the injury would keep him out for two to three months but after an operation he extended the estimate to five months.

    Eto'o said: 'I am determined to be the leading scorer this season in Spain. The gap between me and others is not much. With hard work, personal sacrifice, I can catch up. I will catch up.'

    He has four league goals, 10 behind leader Frederic Kanoute of Sevilla.

    Eto'o was voted African Footballer of the Year three times in a row and is in line for an unprecedented fourth successive award after being named as a finalist for 2006.
