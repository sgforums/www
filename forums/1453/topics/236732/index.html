Benitez plans how to spend

  • turbo_drift
    10 Feb 07, 13:52
    Rafa Benitez admits that he may now be able to bring in a different type of player to Anfield following the club's takeover.

    A historic week in Liverpool's history has seen the Merseyside giants taken over by new co-chairmen George Gillett and Tom Hicks and the American tycoons are ready to show their considerable hand in the transfer market.

    In the past Benitez has had to be relatively frugal in comparison to some of the Premiership's biggest hitters but this could be about to change in what is sure to be an intriguing period for everyone involved at Liverpool.

    While Benitez insists he has no intention of squandering cash needlessly, he does accept that the spending power at his disposal could make things easier in a competitive market.

    "It has been a very important week and something that will change everything," conceded Benitez.

    "We talked about the future of the club, not money or this player or that player. We talked about who we had in the squad and ideas for the future.

    "They were nice people and good businessmen. That is a good mix, they can then do the right things for the club in terms of money and how we grow.

    "It could be a problem of inflated fees now, but I am happy with my scouting department and we are doing a good job now without any big money.

    "We have enough money to do business now and if we have more in the future it could be easier. If we have two players we like and one is more expensive, maybe now we can consider signing him.

    "We are doing the right things now and maybe we will not need to spend big money in the summer."
