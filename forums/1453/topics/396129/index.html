    Liverpool owners set to step aside as Benitez issues warning to Spurs and City

    Warning: Liverpool Manager Rafael Benitez

    Rafael Benitez has warned Manchester City and Tottenham that the pressure is on them to stop Liverpool from winning the race for fourth.

    Benitez's side host Fulham tomorrow in what may prove to be a dress rehearsal for the Europa League Final.

    Although they will have played more games, victory would put them level with Tottenham and one point behind City, who kick off an hour later at home to Birmingham.

    Liverpool remain outsiders to claim the fourth place Benitez guaranteed earlier in the season, but he hopes the pressure will tell on his rivals if they can win their remaining five games.

    City boss Roberto Mancini was at Anfield on Thursday night to see Liverpool beat Benfica 4-1 and Benitez said: 'He will see our team is strong and he knows that they have to win their games.

    'You never know what they think, but they are in a good position and, if they lose, it will be more through their fault so they will be more under pressure - especially if they make a mistake in their next one or two games.'

    Meanwhile, it is believed that owners Tom Hicks and George Gillett may stand aside and appoint an independent chairman in a bid to attract investment. The duo's refusal to listen to a £110m offer from the Rhone Group for a 40 per cent stake sparked an outspoken response from Benitez this week.

    It came as Hicks and Gillett held talks with their advisors in London ahead of a summer deadline to reduce the club's £237m debts.

    The unpopular owners could stand aside even if they do not decide to sell a stake, with British Airways chairman Martin Broughton one of the business heads being considered.
