    Liverpool to be without Gerrard for Toulouse tie
    Liverpool will be without injured captain Steven Gerrard and defender Jamie Carragher for their Champions League clash with Toulouse on Tuesday at Anfield.

    Midfielder Gerrard has a broken toe and Carragher has a broken rib. Liverpool hold a 1-0 lead after the first leg of the third qualifying round tie.

    "Both players will not be available for this game, It's a shame because they are both important players for us," manager Rafael Benitez told the club's Web site.

    Gerrard, who missed England's midweek defeat by Germany and the Premier League win over Sunderland, could be back to face Derby on Saturday but Benitez did not put a timescale on Carragher's return.

    "Carra is improving which is good news. I need to talk to the doctor to find out more and to see how long he will be out for," Benitez added.

    "It's clear that with Steven we need to take care of him. He could be available for the game at the weekend but we'll have to see.

    "Again I will need to talk to the doctor first because I don't want to run the risk of losing him for a long time."

    Benitez said Liverpool should still be good enough to hold on to their advantage against the French side.

    "I am always a positive person and I am confident we will get through, but the players know there is still a game to play and we have to get the right result."

    Liverpool are without Steven Gerrard and Jamie Carragher for their Champions League qualifer with Toulouse.

    Skipper Gerrard has not recovered from a broken toe while Carragher suffered a collapsed lung and a broken rib in Saturday's win at Sunderland.

    Fellow defender Sami Hyypia broke his nose in the same game but is available after training as normal on Sunday.

    Toulouse are without Dominique Arribage (knee), Daniel Congre (shoulder) and Albin Ebondo (thigh).

    Toulouse trail 1-0 after the first leg and prepared for the match at Anfield with a 1-1 draw at Nice on Saturday, Andre-Pierre Gignac scoring the equaliser for Les Violets in the 86th minute.

    Liverpool defeated Sunderland 2-0 on Saturday, but 29-year-old Carragher was substituted after 75 minutes.

    The defender had a scan on Sunday and will be monitored by club doctors, though there has been no indication from Liverpool as to how long Carragher will be sidelined.

    With Hyppia declared fit to play he is expected to partner Daniel Agger in the centre of Liverpool's defence.

    Liverpool boss Rafael Benitez opted for Fernando Torres and Andriy Voronin against Sunderland but Peter Crouch and Dirk Kuyt are expected to start against Toulouse.
