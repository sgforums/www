    Pool snag lanky Crouch
    July 21, 2005

    CHAMPIONS League titleholders Liverpool have won the battle to sign highly-rated England striker Peter Crouch from relegated Southampton.

    The 24-year-old - scorer of 16 Premiership goals last season in a vain attempt to stop Saints from being relegated - will seal a 7 million ($20.5m) move should he pass a medical later today.

    Liverpool manager Rafael Benitez has been keen on signing him but had to see off rival bids from Middlesbrough, Charlton and West Ham.

    However, the lanky Crouch - capped once for England against Colombia in May - is reported to have favoured a move to Liverpool and got his wish after Liverpool outbid their rivals.

    Saints manager Harry Redknapp had wanted to keep Crouch as he bids to restore the club's Premiership status but he has eventually had to admit defeat.

    'A fee of 7 million is good money and obviously it was too good to turn down,' said Redknapp.

    'I would love to have kept him.

    'He was a key player ... I am sure he will be a big success there.'

    SIXTH SIGNING

    Crouch will become Liverpool's sixth signing with Jose Reina, Boudewijn Zenden, Momo Sissoko, Mark Gonzalez and Antonio Barragan also joining the European champions.

    Liverpool were also boosted by Steven Gerrard's decision to extend his contract until 2009.

    But Crouch's arrival should accelerate the departure of Czech Republic international striker Milan Baros, who has fallen out of favour with Liverpool manager Rafael Benitez.

    Benitez said: 'Peter is very good in the air and can hold up the ball, which should suit us.

    'Especially in away games where we had our troubles doing that last season.

    'We lost 11 games away from home last season and so we needed to change things.

    'Peter will definitely give us more possibilities.'

    Benitez is a big admirer of the tall striker since watching him score against Liverpool at St Mary's in January.

    'He is a player I like,' added Benitez.

    'I hope to see him tomorrow, or maybe in two days in Switzerland.

    'He is another kind of player for us.' - PA Sport.
