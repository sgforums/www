    LIVERPOOL 1 - 0 EVERTON
    Sunday 30 March 2008 16:00 , Barclays Premier League

    LFC 1-0 EFC: THE VERDICT

    A goal from derby debutant Fernando Torres made Liverpool pride of Merseyside after a 1-0 win over Everton. Here's the best of the post-match reaction.

    The local paper
    Rarely can a Merseyside derby have been so routine and the outcome so predictable. No real drama, no controversy, no acrimony and no real excitement, just a realisation that the gulf that exists between Liverpool and Everton is unlikely to be bridged this season. such was Liverpool's dominance of this one that it became, at times, a mundane affair in which class ï¿½ not to mention the benefits of a much stronger and fresher squad ï¿½ told.
    The Echo
     
    The former player
    The Merseyside derby for me is the game I look forward to most and it's always extra special to beat Everton. I thought we started brilliantly and took the game to them. Fernando Torres is fantastic and he took his goal really well. We should have won by more in the end.
    David Fairclough
     
    The player
    Last week against Man United things did not go well for us but even when we were 3-0 down the fans were still with us and I could hear them singing my name. As a player that makes you feel really good, to know that you have their support, and for that reason my goal in the derby is for them. They were with me when things were going badly so it is good to be able to score a goal that will make them happy. To score the winner in my first derby is fantastic. It is very important to me that the fans are happy because if they are happy, I am happy.
    Fernando Torres
     
    The boss
    We are really pleased for our supporters that we won and have done the double over Everton. It was really important for the team and for the club. We had a lot of chances in the first half, but we didn't get the second goal and we know that while it is 1-0 it could only take a corner or a free kick and you can concede. I think the team showed a lot of character after the disappointment last week and, particularly in the first half, we showed a lot of quality.
    Rafa Benitez
     
    The fan
    What more can you ask for? 100 goals in all competitions, and what a way to achieve it! 3,000 points in the league at Anfield, and what a win to achieve it! It's always sweet to beat Everton. Martin Skrtel and Sami Hyypia were both excellent keeping Everton at bay, and with Torres in attack defenders just can't cope with him.
    Fire Bird, LFC.TV Message Boards
