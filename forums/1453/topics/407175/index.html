Palermo to return with better offer for Lucas

  • Poignant
    4 Aug 10, 23:24

    Liverpool braced for fresh Lucas Leiva bid from Palermo after snubbing Italians opening offer

     

    Palermo are ready to make a renewed bid for Liverpool midfielder Lucas Leiva.

    The Italians had a £3million bid rejected last month when new Anfield boss Roy Hodgson insisted that Lucas was part of his plans.

    Open to offers: Palermo believe they can talk Liverpool into selling Brazilian midfielder Lucas Leiva

    The Reds are poised to lose Javier Mascherano to Inter Milan if the Italians offer about £25million for the wantaway Argentina captain and Hodgson would be reluctant to lose another midfielder.

    Head strong: New Liverpool boss gave Lucas Leiva the captain's armband in their recent Europa League qualifier

    However, Palermo sporting director Walter Sabatini insists the door has not been closed on a deal and hopes to temp Liverpool with an improved £5m bid.

    'There is a dialogue with Liverpool,' said Sabatini. 'I believe there is room to negotiate.'
