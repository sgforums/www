    From Football365:

    UEFA "RELIEF" AT FINAL LINE-UP

    There was relief at UEFA after AC Milan's victory over Manchester United avoided an all-English Champions League final - and president Michel Platini is convinced the climax of the European season will pass off in Athens on May 23 without any crowd trouble.

    The fact Liverpool and Milan met in the Champions League final only two seasons ago will help security preparations, while a potential flashpoint between rival sets of English fans has been avoided.

    UEFA are also pleased Milan's victory means they are not faced with overwhelming dominance of Premier League sides in Europe's top club competition.

    One senior UEFA executive said: "I think many of us at UEFA are relieved it is not a Liverpool v Manchester United final - not just from a security point of view but because this is meant to be a pan-European competition, and it is much better if it is not two clubs from the same country in the final.

    "It should also make preparations that much easier - the logistics would be much more complicated with two clubs from the same country, and in security terms we are experienced in dealing with a Liverpool v AC Milan final."

    Platini, who was elected UEFA president earlier this year, praised the players and fans of all four semi-finalists.

    He told UEFA's official website: "I would like to take this opportunity to thank and praise the fans who have attended the semi-finals for creating such a wonderful atmosphere at the matches.

    "They have contributed in a positive manner to making the semi-finals such an exciting spectacle.

    "I am convinced that both Liverpool and Milan fans will contribute to making the Athens final a landmark in festive behaviour."

    Platini said the prospect of a rematch between the 2005 finalists was mouth-watering.

    He added: "They are two clubs who have won the trophy 11 times between them will meet for the second time in two years.

    "On the last occasion in Istanbul in 2005, Liverpool and Milan produced a match that occupies a special place in European football history.

    "I am sure that the two teams will be striving to make this year's final an equally memorable occasion at the splendid Athens Olympic Stadium."

    UEFA chiefs admit they will not be able to satisfy fans' demand for tickets for - and that even a stadium seating a million people would sell out.

    Liverpool and Milan will each receive more than 20,000 tickets for the final.

    UEFA communications director William Gaillard said there will be a big fan park in the Greek capital which would cater for supporters without tickets.

    He said: "We know that however many tickets we provide it would never be enough - we could probably sell out a stadium with a million seats!

    "Our advice to fans is always not to travel without a ticket because we do not want them to be exploited by ticket touts, but of course people are free to travel if they wish.

    "If they just want to savour the Athens experience then they can use the fan-park in downtown Athens."

    He said UEFA were not expecting problems between the two sets of fans.

    He added: "There is no history of hostility between the fans and it gives us an advantage that we have the experience of the two sets of fans in a final in similar circumstances two years ago."

    Fowler targets final hurrah

    Robbie Fowler is hopeful of having one final chance to shine in Liverpool colours.

    The forward will end his association with the club when his contract expires in the summer, but his final appearance at Anfield did not go according to script.

    Fowler endured a disappointing day in front of goal and was substituted with minutes remaining to allow the Anfield faithful to hail their departing hero.

    The move backfired, though, as Liverpool were awarded a penalty shortly after which was converted by Harry Kewell.

    Reds boss Rafa Benitez has admitted he was wrong to remove Fowler, but the striker is hoping for one last hurrah in the UEFA Champions League final.

    "I hope the Charlton game may not be my last game for the club," said Fowler. "Like everyone here, I want to be involved in the Champions League final.

    "We all want to claim a shirt, I am no different so I hope this will not be the end and I can contribute something in Athens."

    Fowler felt being substituted minutes before the penalty award summed up his season.

    He added: "Being substituted a minute before we got a penalty was just typical of the way things have gone for me this season, but it has been a good day.

    "I would have liked to have got a goal, but the most important thing is that we did not lose, with it being my last game at Anfield.

    "But it was one of those things, nobody could have known we would have won a penalty so soon after me going off.

    "I've had a very good career here at Liverpool, it was an emotional time as I walked around the pitch at the end and I cannot thank [enough] the people here at Liverpool who gave me the chance to come back.

    "It gave me the chance to say goodbye properly, and I didn't get to do that at the end of my first spell here."

    Benitez added: "I was just too quick. If I had waited a minute more, Robbie could have scored from the spot in front of the Kop, that would have been a perfect way to end for him.

    "I had wanted to give him the chance of a personal ovation, but it was just one of those things in the end. It was just bad luck for him, but at least I made sure he got the ovation he deserved."

    Maldini ready for final fling

    Milan captain Paolo Maldini has declared himself fit for the UEFA Champions League final against Liverpool.

    Maldini made his first appearance in four games in the 1-1 draw with Catania on Sunday.

    The former Italy international had been struggling with a knee problem, but after coming through 65 minutes unscathed against Catania he says he is ready to tackle Liverpool in Athens in nine days' time.

    "I feel good and the knee didn't give me troubles," Maldini told the Italian press.

    "The one with Catania has been a good test because the ground was hard. I have not had troubles.

    "I'm optimistic and I think in Athens I will play. I'm really keen on that match."

    Maldini was replaced by Kakha Kaladze on Sunday, but Maldini insists the substitution was decided before the match as Milan coach Carlo Ancelotti did not want to take any risks.

    "It has been an already defined replacement. I could play until the end but it wasn't the time to risk," concluded Maldini.

    Ancelotti slams Liverpool: You've lost English DNA

    AC Milan coach Carlo Ancelotti has turned up the heat ahead of next week's Champions League final by branding Liverpool the worst English team in the competition.
    Ancelotti said: "On a technical level, they are the worst of the three English teams to reach this season's semi-finals.

    "But that does not mean I would have chosen to be playing them in next week's final. I would have preferred Manchester United."

    And he warned supporters not to expect a feast of flowing football in Athens.

    Ancelotti is convinced his team will take revenge for Istanbul two years ago when Milan surrendered a 3-0 lead before losing to Rafa Benitez's men on penalties.

    He explained: "One of the reasons is obviously because we all saw how the semi-final went for Milan, both at Old Trafford and in the San Siro.

    "But I also believe it would have made a better final because United are a technical team who play good football and also let the opposition play.

    "I don't believe the same thing will happen with Liverpool."

    Ancelotti also taunted Liverpool by declaring that the Merseysiders have lost touch with their roots under Benitez.

    He said: "Milan will always have a strong Italian quality because, even with foreigners like Kaka and Seedorf, we remain true to our influences, philosophy and beliefs.

    "But Liverpool don't have an English DNA any more. They are more like a Mediterranean team and that comes from their coach."
