    Arsenal's Edu won't rule out Man Utd
    tribalfootball.com - December 18, 2004

    Arsenal midfielder Edu says he would consider a move to Manchester United next season.

    United boss Sir Alex Ferguson is keen on the Brazil international, who can move to Old Trafford on a Bosman in the summer.

    Edu told the Daily Mail: "It doesn't make me worried to play in England for another club.

    "If I see that it will be important for my future as a footballer, then I'm going.

    "Barcelona is a fantastic club. If you ask me about Madrid and Valencia and Manchester they are all fantastic clubs.

    "When the moment comes I have to be careful to make the right decision.

    "Arsenal have made me think a lot. If they offer another contract to me now they have to convince me that they really want me to stay.

    "If they wanted me to stay they should have signed me before, why have they left it until January?

    "It's no longer simple like it was. I thought it was going to be easy but now it will be more difficult."

    He added: "I've been very honest in the negotiations.

    "I said, 'I don't want the biggest money here. But I don't want the money I've been earning, I just want to be in the middle'.

    "It's up to them to pay me or not.

    "I have a contract until May, if Arsenal play me or not is up to Arsene Wenger, not me.

    "I can tell you 100 percent that I will be training, I will be doing everything to push myself. After that is not my problem.

    "Of course I'd like to play but if he says no, what can I do?

    "It's the same as if they want to sell me to another club. If I say no, they cannot sell me to them."
