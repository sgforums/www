    Liverpool hero Gerrard on THAT goal: I didn't mean it!
    tribalfootball.com - May 15, 2006

    Liverpool captain Steven Gerrard admits his wonder goal on Saturday was a fluke!
    Gerrard was struggling with cramp and his side were trailing 3-2 to West Ham as the game went into injury time.

    It was then he came up with his second goal of the match - an amazing 35-yard piledriver that forced the FA Cup final into extra-time.

    He admitted: "I did not hear the stadium announcer say how much extra was being added but I knew there wasn't long left and it was really hard - I was all over the place.

    "Just before that goal I had taken a free-kick and it nearly went out of the stadium, so I was delighted to hit my next shot like that.

    "I didn't mean to put it exactly there but knew if I got good contact it would hit the target and I caught it really sweetly."

    Gerrard then had to undergo the agony of 30 minutes of extra-time and a penalty shootout - when he netted again.

    He said: "In extra-time, I just didn't want the ball. I just kept looking at the clock and wanting it to run out. I had absolutely nothing left.

    "We basically finished the game with about five men! People were going down with cramp all over the place because we'd put so much effort in.

    "For my first goal it just sat up really nice to volley it. For my second I had a bit of luck in there as well.

    "I don't tend to get two goals in a game very often but I remember the knockdown for the first one was perfect."
