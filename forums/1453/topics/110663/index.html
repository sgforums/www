    i think having video refs could be an option ... but it's difficult to implement ... sometimes it may even bring up more problems ... it may sound funny, but having video refs does not eliminate all mistakes ...

    other sports like rugby league, rugby union, and cricket already use video refs ... when the refs onfield are unsure about some incidents, usually goal scoring situations, they will refer it to the video refs ... they also have video reviews where players can be charged for violent conduct or striking etc

    i guess the first question would be: at what level do we implement the video refs? ... it would be a massive exercise for fifa to implement it across all levels ... it means writing up new guidelines for the video refs and re-training them ... the cost of installing the technology is also huge ... video refs in a rugby league match may get 4 or 5 angles on the goal line ... the major leagues probably have similar number of cameras but what about games like the tiger cup?

    another difficult part is where does the video ref's responsibility ends? do they only look at goal scoring situations, particular incidents, or do they cover the whole game? ... because if they're involved in the entire game then there may be a lot of interruptions ...

    the video ref option is defintely worth exploring ... but it can only minimise the mistakes, it cannot eliminate all mistakes ... that's why an argument against it is that the refs' wrong decisions are part of the game ...
