    Terry: Lamps deserved award over Gerrard

    Chelsea captain John Terry believes team-mate Frank Lampard deserved to win the Football Writers’ Player of the Year award ahead of Steven Gerrard.

    Gerrard scooped the prize, voted for by football journalists, with PFA Player of the Year Ryan Giggs coming second and his Manchester United team-mate Wayne Rooney third.

    The Liverpool captain has enjoyed a hugely influential season, netting 15 goals in 29 Premier League games to help The Reds sustain a title challenge.

    But Terry feels Lampard would have been a more deserving recipient and was astonished his club team-mate did not even make the top three. 

    “I can't believe Lamps hasn't won it if I'm honest,” Terry told the official club website. “Nemanja Vidic has done well, but, even with him, one bad game against Liverpool and everyone ruled him out of it.

    “Six years now, I've been playing consistently with 'Lamps' and he scores 20 goals a year, for a midfielder that is unbelievable.

    “Jamie Carragher might tell you Stevie G deserves to win it, but, for me, seeing 'Lamps' day in and day out what he brings to this club is incredible. As a group of players we feel 'Lamps' should be as a minimum in the top three.”
