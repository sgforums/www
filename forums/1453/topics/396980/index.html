    Liverpool's Rafael Benitez: EPL Title Expectations Were Unrealistic

    Spaniard cites injuries as main cause of poor season.

    Rafa Benitez - Liverpool (Getty Images)
    Liverpool manager Rafael Benitez believes that winning the Premier League was far too much to expect of his team.

    After enjoyed a terrific Premier League season last year, losing only twice in the league on their way to an impressive second place finish.

    It has been a vastly different campaign for Liverpool this season as the team has lost ten Premier League games, and currently sit in seventh place with four games remaining.

    Benitez thinks that injuries have been a huge reason why the team has endured such a disappointing season, but the Spaniard also believes that the team’s expectations were far too lofty.

     “At the beginning of the season expectations were too high,” Benitez told The Mirror.

    “That is normal after such a good season.

    “But I always say the same – the Premier League is the Premier League and the difference in money is so big that if you want to stay at this level for a while, you have to do almost everything perfectly.

     “During pre-season we also had players coming back late and then we had injuries. We’ve had seven, nine or 11 players out at the same time in different months, players who would normally be starters.

    “We can talk about mistakes, but when you have so many players injured, especially key players, it is not easy to sustain a level of performance every week.”

    The most important injury casualty has been Fernando Torres, who had already missed significant time before recent surgery has confirmed that the Spanish international star’s season is over.
