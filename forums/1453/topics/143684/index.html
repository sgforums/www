    Italian full-back Pistone, like Stubbs, rejected a 12-month contract and had seemed likely to return to his homeland with Lecce.

    But that deal broke down and Everton, who have been struggling to add to their squad this summer, compromised by offering Pistone a two-year contract.

    And Pistone said: "It is definitely good for me because it is a big challenge in the season coming with the Champions League.

    "I think it is a very good thing for me in terms of my career so I am happy about it. There was a point when I thought that maybe I would not be coming back.

    "But I have always been in contact with the club and always been in negotiations during the period. I am happy that at the end we have reached an agreement because this is what I wanted from the beginning."
