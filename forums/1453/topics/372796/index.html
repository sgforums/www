    Daniel Agger is to undergo an operation on his troublesome back that will rule the Liverpool defender out for another two months. Having failed to appear during pre-season, Agger has missed both of Liverpool's opening league games due to the injury. According to The Daily Mail, Agger has now been booked in for surgery and will have an operation in France on Tuesday. 'The Liverpool centre-back has pencilled in Denmark's World Cup qualifiers with Sweden and Hungary in the middle of October for his comeback,' the newspaper reports. While the news represents a setback for Rafa Benitez, the Liverpool boss is expected to add defensive cover to his squad over the weekend in the form of AEK Athens defender Sotirios Kyrgiakos. It is also thought likely that Martin Skrtel, absent for the midweek victory over Stoke with a hairline fracture of the jaw, could be available for Monday's clash with Aston Villa
