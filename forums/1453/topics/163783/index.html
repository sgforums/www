    Liverpool, Newcastle rival Spurs for Cesar
    tribalfootball.com - November 20, 2005

    Liverpool and Newcastle United have joined Tottenham in their interest in Deportivo La Coruna defender Cesar.

    Spurs have been consistently linked with the defender and Depor president Augusto Lendoiro has now invited interested parties to get in touch with a view to a securing a move during January's transfer window.

    Cesar's contract runs out at the end of the season and the Spain international, who has made just two substitute appearances this term, is desperate to rekindle his career elsewhere.

    "If there is a club that is interested in Cesar and they make their intention known then we will try to come to an agreement," Lendoiro said.

    "Cesar wants to play, and we want him to play, so if he receives an offer then we will try to look for a solution that suits all parties."

    Spurs may face stiff competition from Liverpool and Newcastle, who were both reported to be watching developments, but coach Martin Jol may also need to persuade the player that his future lies in England.
