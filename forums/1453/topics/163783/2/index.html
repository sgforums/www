    Mark Gonzalez claims he will finally join Liverpool in two weeks.

    The Chilean winger has seen his move delayed by a number of months after he saw his original transfer from Albacete scuppered when he failed to secure a work-permit.

    However, Chile have since risen in Fifa rankings which has smoothed over the deal and Liverpool confirmed the deal was back on in October.

    Gonzalez - currently recovering from a bad knee injury - claims he will be on Merseyside before the end of the year.

    "I'll join Liverpool in two weeks and I'd be able to play a friendly in a month," Gonzalez said.

    "I'm anxious to fly and be with my new team-mates. It's important to get used to my new team quickly.

    "They know I'm a left-footed midfielder and like to attack.

    "If the coach wants me to play in defence, I'll do it, because I want to get used to it as soon as possible."

    Marseille coach Jean Fernandez is keeping his fingers crossed that the club can prise France striker Djibril Cisse away from Liverpool during the winter break.

    Marseille president Papa Diouf has made little secret of the fact that Cisse is one of their main targets and is aiming to land the former Auxerre player on an initial six-month loan deal when the transfer window opens.

    Marseille have struggled for success up front and with the African Nations Cup approaching - Senegal striker Mamadou Niang is their second top league scorer with four goals - Fernandez is hoping Cisse can be brought on board.

    "I am not in the managers' confidence but I know that we really need a striker and I would be very, very happy to have Cisse, who is a great player with fantastic potential," Fernandez told television channel Canal Plus after his side's 1-0 win over second-placed Auxerre on Saturday evening.

    "It would be an honour to have him with us."

    Cisse, who has scored nine goals in 27 appearances for France, has struggled to tie down a first-team berth at Liverpool this season, finding himself behind Fernando Morientes and Peter Crouch in the pecking order.

    The 24-year-old was again on the bench for Saturday's 2-0 victory over Middlesbrough.
