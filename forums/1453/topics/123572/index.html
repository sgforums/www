    Liverpool midfielder Igor Biscan has admitted he is likely to leave Anfield in the summer.
    Biscan came close to quitting Liverpool during the January transfer window only for a spate of injuries to end his hopes of moving on.
    The Croatian international has been informed that he does not figure in Rafa Benitez's long-term plans and that he can leave.
    Biscan, who is set for an extended run in the side following an injury to Dietmar Hamann, is putting all talk on his future on the back burner until the end of the season as he tries to help Liverpool end the campaign on a high note.
    "The way things are looking now I think I'll be leaving at the end of the season," Biscan told the club's official website.
    "I want to do well for myself over the rest of the season and in doing so I'll do well for the team.
    "If I'm going to leave the club then it's important I play games to show the other teams what I am capable of.
    "I am happy with the way this season has gone for me. I had a chat with the manager before Christmas and he told me I wasn't in his plans. In that situation you need to think about your future but I never thought about leaving.
    "Even though I wasn't guaranteed first team chances, I didn't want to go. I wanted to stay until the end of the season to do my best if games did come along for me."
    German side Hamburg are believed to be monitoring Biscan's situation at Anfield and could make a move for him in the summer

    Igor Biscan, despite his amusing appearance of cluelessness, is a terrific player. Even many loyal Liverpool fans forget that when he was signed in December 2000, he came in as a defensive midfielder and the Captain and most popular player of his club Croatia Zagreb. He had, at the age of 22, become a regular in the Croatian team (which at the time was excellent) and had played in both the UEFA cup and the Champion's League. He certainly had what it takes to make it at Anfield.

    He impressed for the rest of 00/01 season (including wins over Arsenal and Man Utd - outclassing Vieira and Keane on both occasions and being hailed in the press) until Houillier moved the towering lad into odd positions, on the flanks, and where he was most criticised, at centreback. Biscan could not cope with his increased defensive responsibilities and his form suffered until he was dropped from the side.

    I hoped that Rafa would see Igor's qualities and retain him as useful backup to Gerrard or Xabi, seeing as how Hamann is on the verge of retirement (or at least, moving on to a sunset club). Igor has been class on the Continent, with superlative displays in the Group Stages and First Knockout Round of the Champion's League. His vision and dribbling are top-notch at times, but he struggles to find consistency without being able to get regular football.

    He's worth keeping, and certainly capable of justifying the 5.5 million pounds the club paid for his services.
