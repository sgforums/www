    Liverpool's Manager Rafa Benitez has challenged his players to prove they are capable of taking Liverpool to the next level.

    The Spanish tactician has reassured the Merseyside outfit that his future lies in The Premiership but, ominously for some of his squad, he has also intimated that major changes to personnel will be made in the summer.

    Frustrated at a goal-shy strike-force Benitez is ready to raid the club's coffers in the summer to land a top quality striker, with the futures of Fernando Morientes and Djibril Cisse seemingly in real doubt.

    Ahead of their game with Newcastle on Sunday, Benitez has urged his side to press for second spot behind Chelsea, with the threat of an Anfield exit hovering over several of his team.

    "For me to see the team progressing this season is enough, next season will be completely different,'' said Benitez.

    "If we finish second it will be good for confidence, but then the season will end and it will be different next time, certainly financially.

    "At the moment it is enough to see my team playing against the top sides with the chance of winning, but if you cannot beat them you need to know why.

    "You must find what the difference is if you want to progress, and that means you must sign the players you need to improve in different positions.

    "And of course we must work harder in terms of our tactical work to achieve our aims."
