    Pre-requisites for SIA (Singapore Airlines) cadet pilot program are:-

    Singapore Citizens, Singapore Permanent Residents or Malaysian Citizens.
    Minimum 5 credits in the General Certificate in Education (GCE) Ordinary
    Level Examination or its equivalent. These must include credits in English,
    Mathematics and a Pure Science subject, preferably Physics, taken at one
    sitting. In addition, applicants must possess one of the following:-

    (a) minimum 2 'A's and 2 'O's, including General Paper, in the GCE 'A' Level
    Examination (or its equivalent); or
    (b) minimum 2 Principal Passes (excluding the languages; Mathematics and
    Science subjects preferred) and 2 Subsidiary Passes in the STPM (or its
    equivalent); or
    (c) a Polytechnic Diploma; or a Degree
    (d) You must be medically fit and have visual acuity of at least
    6/60 without optical aids, correctable to 6/6.
    *25 year old and above for Singaporean

    Echoing what Megatron has said, although there's high success rate for LASIK but it may still leave many with some slight refractive errors. There are known side effects like:
    - glares (halos, stardusts)
    - lose of visual acuity
    - reduced contrast sensitivty
    - corneal flap complications

    To my understanding, the aircraft operation is visually demanding and the cockpit environment can be unfriendly. So you won't know what might happen if complication strikes. You don't wanna risk lives when you're 10000m up in the air, do you?

    Maybe in time to come local regulation may change, till then study hard!
