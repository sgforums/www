    Fear to commit.

    Nowadays getting married does not mean you live happily ever after. Men and Women are not so steadfast in their allegiance to their spouse in the current era. A pair of couple nearing their 50th marriage anniversary can get divorced.

    Cost of living high. Long term speculated expenditure after marriage can make you shiver. Wedding costs, cost for delivering a baby, saving for the child's further studies, your own retirement saving, house loans, car loans, insurance bills, and many many more miscellaneous spending.

    The following are the estimated self saving (concocted from professional studies and evaluation)* necessary per spouse/single from age 40 till 65 on the respective category, excluding their CPF funds:

    If you are single, you must save at least S$300 per month between 40 yrs old till your retirement age of 65 in order to have a well secured retirement fund to survive.

    Likewise, for a couple whose wife prefer to "goyan kaki" and stay home to take care of kids (so called), the husband must save at least S$650 in order to secure himself and his wife after he stopped working.

    For a couple who are both working, from the age of 40 onwards, the spouses must pool in at least S$1,250 per month in their fixed account to safeguard their lives together after age 65.

    So, from the above empirical account for retirement savings, choose whether you want to get married or not.

    Rgds/Lawrence
