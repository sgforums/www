    Mashed Taro With Ginkgo

    Materials
    Taro 600 g
    Ginkgoes 80 g
    Sugar 220 g
    Spring Onion 4 sections
    Lard 3 tbsp
    Mashed Taro With Ginkgo
    bai guo yu ni (Mandarin)

    Steps
    1) Boil ginkgoes with shells in water for 10 minutes, remove shell and membrane
    2) Halve and remove central stalks, parboil in hot water
    3) Drain out water and seasoned with 1 tbsp sugar
    4) Peel and wash taro, Cut into thick pieces
    5) Steam until fully cooked, still cool, cut into dices
    6) Add 1 cup of water, mash it into smooth gruel
    7) Heat wok and add lard, fry spring onion, then remove it
    Pour into ginkgoes and cook in low heat for a while
    9) Add mashed taro and sugar, stir fry in low heat for 10 minutes, bowl up and serve

    Braised Duck

    ingredients

    1 duck
    1 packet of 5 spice powder
    salt equal to volume of 5 spice powder
    1 bottle of semi-premium grade dark soy sauce (Tai Hua Brand)
    1 handful of sugar

    method

    marinating

    wash duck thoroughly making sure bits of the head is cleaned (if you want to keep head)
    mix spice powder and salt together
    smear mixture evenly inside and outside duck
    put duck in a plastic bag and tie up
    bung into fridge (not freezer) and leave for 3 to 4 days

    cooking

    rinse duck (not wash.. just rinse most of the marinate off) not too thorough
    melt sugar in wok until it starts to turn into a black treacly mess
    pour about 3/4 of the soy sauce and dissolve the sugar
    heat until sauce starts to simmer
    put duck in and keeps scooping the black sauce over the duck
    turn over every 15 minutes and keep basting the duck
    when the duck turns golden brown, fill wok with water until most of duck is covered
    keep turning over duck every 15 minutes, taking care not to burn it (best put a small steel rack to elevate the duck from contact with bottom of the wok)
    keep simmering until cooked (about 2 hours)

    voila! braised duck!

    EGG CUSTARD TARTS
    6 servings

    Tart Pastry:
    1/4 cup Butter
    1/4 cup Lard
    1 Egg
    6 tbl. Sugar
    2 cups Sifted all purpose flour.

    Egg Custard Filling:
    2 Whole extra large eggs
    3 Extra large egg yolks
    1 cup Whole milk
    1/2 cup Half and half
    1 cup Sugar
    grated nutmeg

    Cream the butter with lard. Add egg and sugar. Beat well. Add flour, 1 cup at a time. The dough will be mealy. Work quickly with your hands to gather dough into a ball.
    Knead lightly . Leave aside for chill it at this point while making the filling.

    Egg Custard Filling: Be sure all the ingredients are at room temperature.
    Beat whole eggs at low speed with egg yolks well.
    Do not over beat, add sugar, then milk, then half and half.
    Let mixture rest 10 - 15 minutes. Skim foam from mixture.

    Separate dough into 24 balls. Press each into 2 1/2 inch tart shell .

    Fill shell with filling almost to the top. Sprinkle with grated nutmeg.

    Preheat oven to 300.

    Place tarts on cookie sheet and bake for 45 minutes.
    Cool for 10 - 15 minutes.
    Loosen slightly by inserting a toothpick along the sides. Tart shell should unmold easily.

    Seafood Marinara 1 servings

    1 TB olive oil
    1/2 onion -- finely chopped
    2 cloves garlic -- finely : chopped
    1/2 cup white wine
    3 cup good quality tomato sauce

    10 lg shrimp -- peeled and : deveined

    my version of chilli crabs.. but i can only cooked, usually need help on 'silent' the crabs..

    Chili Crab Recipe

    Ingredients :
    2 medium - Fresh crabs approximately 500 g each

    1/2 cup - Plain flour

    Cooking oil

    1 medium - Finely chopped onion

    5cm of more - Fresh ginger finely grated

    4 or 5 pc - Finely chopped garlic

    5 - Red chilies, finely chopped

    1 Bottled - tomato pasta sauce (Maggi)

    1 cup - Water

    2 tablespoons - Soy sauce

    2 tablespoons - Sweet chili sauce

    1 tablespoons - Rice vinegar

    2 tablespoons - sugar

    Method :

    Lightly and carefully coat the shells with a little flour (after wash).

    Heat about 2 tablespoons cooking oil in a large wok and cook 1 1/2 crab at a time, carefully turning and holding the crab in the hot oil until the shell just turns red.

    Add the remaining oil to the wok and cook the onion, ginger, garlic and chili for 5 minutes over medium heat and stirring regularly.

    Add the tomato sauce, water, soy sauce, chili sauce, vinegar and sugar. Bring to boil and cook for 15 minutes.

    Return the crab to the wok and simmer, turning carefully in the sauce for 10 minutes or until the crab meat turns white.

    anyway, here's a fool-proof recipe for radish soup

    ingredients

    1 dried cuttlefish
    some pork ribs (about 1 medium tray from the supermarket)
    2 or 3 radish, diced to 1 1/2 inch pieces
    salt to taste

    method

    wash cuttlefish
    wash pork ribs
    put the ribs in a pot of tap water and bring to boil

    put the ribs and cuttlefish in a slow cooker full of water and add the radish
    cook slowly for about 24 hours
    add salt to taste
