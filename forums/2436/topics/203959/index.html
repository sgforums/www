    The Hebrews have never believed in a trinity the way the Western world does. They have always believed that there is only one God with a triune nature, and man was created in that image. The Hebrews have recited the prayer of "sh'ma" (pronounced shma) every day for centuries now, and it comes in part from Deut. 6:4, "Hear O Israel: The LORD our God is one LORD." (KJV)

    This is the Hebrew text, It reads like this if we substitute our alphabet for the Hebrew symbols, "sh'ma yisrael YHVH elohÃ©nu YHVH ekhad."

    When the Bible translates the word, (elohÃ©nu), it simply gives us "God", "God of us", or "our God". What some people don't know is that all Hebrew words have numeric and gender specific values. This word is both masculine and feminine, but more importantly, its numeric value is three or more. This would seem contradictory to the word, ekhad, which is translated as "one" unless you know that ekhad means one by the joining of many, like a rope. So the meaning of elohÃ©nu is, "Our God who is above all gods, three or more in number, both masculine and feminine, superior in power and might, by the joining of many becoming greater in strength in unity as one."

    If you take a single strand of rope between your hands and yank really hard, you can easily brake it. However, if you take three or more strands and wind them together very tightly, you probably won't be able to break the rope. The idea of one by the joining of many is that no part or portion is complete without the other. God has no body outside of the Son, The Son has no Spirit outside of the Holy Ghost, and the Holy Ghost has no soul outside the Father. They are not separated in any way. They are not three, separate or distinct entities. They are three, separate and distinct facets of one entity.

    In Gen. 2:23-24, Adam says two things that are important for us to understand. First, the text says, "because she was taken out of Man." (KJV) Here is the Hebrew, Transliterated into our alphabet it looks like this, "meish luqakhahÂ—zot." The first word, meish, means "from man or from husband." The words, luqakhahÂ—zot, mean "this was taken out." In other words, everything that makes a woman female was inside of the man in the first place. God took those things out of the man, who was created perfect, complete, and whole in the image of God, then He created a woman. Adam then says, "and they shall be one flesh." (KJV) They shall be "ekhad" flesh. This word has absolutely nothing to do with sex in any way. The idea of the man and woman becoming ekhad is that they are incomplete one without the other. Simply put, a man is only half a human, and a woman is only half a human. A man and a woman combining their thoughts, emotions, passions, wants, needs and desires creates one whole Adam which means human.

    In John 10:30, the Pharisees (Hebrews) asked the Lord to tell them plainly if He was the Messiah. The Lord didn't answer their question, instead He said, "ani v'haav ekhad." Translated literally into English that simply says, "I and the Father are one." To the Hebrew mind, this was an outrageous and blasphemous thing to say, because to the Hebrews what the Lord said was, "I am God." "I am one of the strands that is God. That makes me God.(the flesh)" We know without a doubt that this is how the Hebrews took the statement, because in the very next verse, they picked up stones to kill Him. The punishment for blasphemy was death by stoning.

    There is only one God, and He is made up of many strands like a rope. To split God into three, distinct parts is actually a simplification of who He is. God is Righteousness, Love, Joy, Peace, Power, Might, Flesh, Spirit, Soul, Body, etc., all bound together like the chords of a rope making Him one God, who is Savior and Lord of all. God does not exist without righteousness anymore than He exists without His Spirit.
