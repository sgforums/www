    History

    The period of history that Daniel covers is more concerned with events than in the years when they took place. Different calendars were in force across the ancient world and they are different from those in force today, so dating can be accurate in parts and approximate in others depending on the dates available that can be matched to the events. It is generally reckoned that Israel went into exile in 586 BC. Daniel was among the captives. He had a prominent role in the Babylonian Kingdoms of Nebuchadnezzar and Belteshazzar and in the Kingdoms of the Medes and Persians under successive rulers. He was a young man at the beginning of his exile and an old man at the end. Daniel lived through the time when some of his prophecies came true and he foresaw the rising and falling of Empires until the end time. He saw the beginning of the return of his people to the land of Judah when Cyrus gave permission for the rebuilding of the Temple. This would be around 539 BC, the time when he prayed the very special prayer recorded in Daniel Chapter 9. The completion of the Temple in 516 BC would have been the end of the 70 years of exile and the beginning of the final building up of Jerusalem and its wall for the return of the exiles. This rebuilding of the wall is covered in more detail in the books of Ezra and Nehemiah, while the rebuilding of the houses of Jerusalem go beyond the explicit details given in Scripture, after the wall was completed and the Temple had been restored for worship in Jerusalem. The returning exiles did their rebuilding in various stages, often under great pressure from surrounding people, so that the project was often interrupted, specific mention being made of the way Darius 1st confirmed the decree of Cyrus relating to the rebuilding of the Temple (around 520 BC) and the major edict at the time of Arterxerxes in his twentieth year (around 444 BC), when the edict of Cyrus was confirmed and strengthened.

They come layer upon layer, with different aspects to the same picture adding to the detail to the revelation as the life of Daniel proceeded. The revelations were given at a time of desperate need in the life of the Kingdom of Judah and contained a message of hope for Daniel and His people. They remain as a message of hope for the times of trouble for Israel and Judah through other periods of history, including the time of the end, of which we see the initial stirring in our day.
