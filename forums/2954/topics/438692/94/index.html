    Be mindful of what consist of our body..

    Hair, skin, nails, teeth, flesh, blood vessels, skeleton, marrow, kidneys, heart, gall, gall bladder, lungs, intestines, stomach, faces, urine, brain, mucus, pus, blood, sweat, fat, tears, oils, saliva.

    All those are smelly and subject to decay, and body is same as them, it can be mine or others, there is nothing to cling..
    (Part of Vidharshana meditation)
    -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Meditation of sensory organs (Ayathana – Meditation).

    Consider your eyes and mindful of the reality of them like,

    Eyes are result of a cause and effect,
    Subject to decay and get sick, as a result of impermanence,
    Eyes are not me or my soul, and those are not mine, as I cannot have control of their existence,


    All the pictures seen by eyes are result of a cause and effect,
    Subject to decay and get sick, as a result of impermanence,
    Pictures are not me or my soul, and those are not mine, as I cannot have control of their existence,

    The mind exists in the eyes is result of a cause and effect,
    Subject to decay and get sick, as a result of impermanence,
    Mind in the eyes is not me or my soul, and those are not mine, as I cannot have control of its existence,

    The senses appear with the connection of eyes, mind and pictures, are result of a cause and effect,
    Subject to vanish, as a result of impermanence,
    Senses are not me or my soul, and those are not mine, as I cannot have control of their existence,

    The identifications appear with the connection of eyes, mind and pictures, are result of a cause and effect,
    Subject to vanish, as a result of impermanence,
    Identifications are not me or my soul, and those are not mine, as I cannot have control of their existence,

    The thoughts appear with the connection of eyes, mind and pictures, are result of a cause and effect,
    Subject to vanish, as a result of impermanence,
    Thoughts are not me or my soul, and those are not mine, as I cannot have control of their existence,
    Apply this to all other sensory organs and be mindful of their reality

    (part of Vidharshana Meditation)
