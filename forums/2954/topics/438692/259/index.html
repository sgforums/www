    A guest who visited Fo Guang Shan asked Venerable Master Hsing Yun:

    Guest: "Venerable Master, I want to ask you something though it's an impolite question."

    Ven. Master Hsing Yun: "What is it that you want to ask?"

    Guest: "Master, you are a vegetarian in public, but what if you are alone in your room, do you eat meat?"

    Venerable Master Hsing Yun did not answer the guest's question first, instead he asked the guest: "When you drive the car, do you fasten the seat belt for your own safety or just for the police to not come penalize you? If it is for your own safety then either there is or isn't a police, you will do so."

    Guest: "Now I understand!"

    From Records of Questions and Answers with Ven. Master Hsing Yun (originally in Chinese)

    If a person who upholds the name of Guan Shi Yin Bodhisattva enters a great fire, the fire will not burn him, all because of this Bodhisattva's awesome spiritual power.

    Further, if a person who is about to be harmed calls out the name of Guan Shi Yin Bodhisattva, the knives and staves of the attackers will break into pieces and he will be saved.
    If bandits enough to fill the three thousand great thousand world system infest a dangerous road on which a merchant chief in charge of costly jewels is leading a group of merchants, but among the merchants there is even a single person who says, 'Good men, do not be afraid! You should all single-mindedly recite the name of Guan Shi Yin Bodhisattva. This Bodhisattva bestows fearlessness upon living beings. If you recite her name, you shall surely be saved from these robbers,' and if upon hearing that, the merchants all cry out together, 'Namo Guan Shi Yin Pusa,' then they will immediately be saved because they recited her name.


    ---Lotus Sutra Chapter 25 (Universal Door of Guan Yin Bodhisattva)

    There is no single moment of success or failure in life. Because nothing is absolute, elation can turn to misery, and a misfortune can work out to be a blessing.

    --------------------------------------------
    A drinking session is joyful without forcing. A chess game is successful without competition. A fact should be told without wild talk. A human character grows without desires.

    There is no absolute winner or loser in this ever changing world. The only winner is “impermanence.” Hence, winners may not always win and losers may not always lose.
