    if there is no distinction between right and wrong, good and evil....reasoning would lapse into extreme point of neutrality or balance or emptiness where right/ wrong, good/ evil becomes meaningless as they are created out of judgemental elements of the mind. If i were to violate the so called objective truth to save a person's life, should i not?

    Originally posted by Ihatemen:

    I have a qns. A friend of mine owe me money. She keeps playing with me that she will return but I doubt so. Should I write off as bad debt? If this is it, please let me know.

    If it's a small sum and she really did not pay you back, you may treat her as your karmic debtor. Meaning you might have owed her money in your past life, you are paying what you had owed her. However, you also must have the wisdom not to lend money to her again unless it's life and death matter.

    I'm always mentally prepared not to get back the money when I lend money to someone who's poor and borrow money for 3 meals. I treat it as donations and I'm sure I can afford to do without that sum of money. However, I'm careful not to lend money to people who borrow money for things which I think they can do without.
