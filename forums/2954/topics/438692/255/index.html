    Fate can only be transformed by changing your views with Dharma

    -------------------------------------
    Wooden fish is probably the most common and most obvious Dharma instrument in Buddhism. In a chanting ceremony, the assembly will follow the rhythmic beat of the wooden fish to create a sound of resonance and order.
    A lot people ask. Why is it shaped like a fish? It is because the eyes of a fish never close; they are always open, swimming or resting, day or night. Precisely, it is this characteristic of the fish that is used to remind the monastics to always be diligent and heedful in their cultivation.
