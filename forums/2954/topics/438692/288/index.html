    Venerable Master Hsing Yun

    My experience with Amitabha Buddha (Amituofo) is –
    A recitation of Amituofo is a boat that can carry karmic obstacles and prevent us from falling into the samsara (rebirth) sea of suffering.
    A recitation of Amituofo contains the content of the Twelve Sections of Tripitaka.
    A recitation of Amituofo embraces all the mundane and super-mundane merits. 
    A recitation of Amituofo has the energy of the mutual combustion of firewood and the power of the accumulated water from streams.

    Reciting the Buddha’s name is not seeking for death but for life. Reciting the Buddha to pursue a better place of rebirth is to identify the continuation of infinite life, not just ending in one life.

    WHAT IS THE MIND?

    by Venerable Master Hsing Yun (1927 - , Fo Guang Shan)

    English translation: Zhi Yue
    The mind is like a monkey, for it is hard to tame;
    are you able to control your mind?
    The mind is like a flash of lightning, in that it is as fast as a split
    second; are you able to grasp your mind’s intentions?
    The mind is like a wild deer, in that it chases the sense objects;
    are you able to see through the illusions?
    The mind is like a thief,
    such that it steals your merits; are you able
    to subdue the Mara in your mind?
    The mind is like an adversary,
    for it causes suffering to the body;
    are you able to stop the maltreatment?
    The mind is like a servant,
    for it is ordered about by afflictions;
    are you able to be remain amiable and carefree?
    The mind is like a king,
    in that it has supreme power over the body;
    are you able to inspire virtue in people?
    The mind is like an ever-flowing spring,
    in that it is inexhaustible;
    are you able to give to all sentient beings?
    The mind is like an artist, as it paints everything;
    are you able to make the world a more beautiful place?
    The mind is like space,
    in that it is boundless; are you able to embrace the entire world?
