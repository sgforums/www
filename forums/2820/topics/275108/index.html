    Thanks folks.

    So I gathered that what we have in mind now is that university or schools can only provide the students with the basic business and technical skills and knowledge they need to know to do business or embark on technopreneurship. It's like students who have to learn engineering subjects to become competent in their engineering field to embark on a specialised engineering or engineering related career after their studies. Or even medical students, who learned and become competent doctors after schools. These professions have clear definition of problems in real life to solve. However, for students who wish to be technopreneurs, it would be a much more challenging thing as their real life business problems are more complex and undefined. Schools can only provide them the basic technical knowledge and soft skills in what they need to know, and probably they can do projects and gain experiences from business case studies. Whether they can really be technopreneur in real life, it's a question, and a question that they can only answer, and they will only know when they are in real business life.

    I'd give an analogy of technopreneur training to army training. Without going into war, we won't know whether the soldiers can really fight ?! But when they are at war, with the skill and knowledge that they have learned from army schools, then they will know how to fight it right if they want to survive. Agree?

    So my next question is, probably current school environment is not ideal to train technopreneurs?
    i) We may need more than just current school teachings, e.g business simulations like army training, real field business battle practise, etc ?
    ii) Or perhaps we are too restrictive to our thinking and understanding of current education model in Singapore in the provision of entrepreneurship training ? If not wrong, I was told that universities do have incubators, and students learn in school and do real business there. Are these effective?
