    Janethong - since S&B has stated that they are not time-share, then go file your claim at the SCT. They are opened during lunch or you can download a form - fill in the details and then fax it in.  Think you can send your $10 by cheque.  Check the SCT website.

    Your case - if I am not wrong will be misrepresentation.  Most of us got conned into signing for timeshare or timeshare-esque contracts.  Usually that we would enjoy certain discounts which do not pan out when you actually try to book.  State down what you were told before you signed up and what you are offered after you signed up and if the description do not match - then you have them on misrepresentation.

    SCT procedure requires you to attend consultation and then if both parties cannot settle - the case will be heard before a referee.  If they dont turn up at the consultation, you might get a default order which means you can go do your seizure against them after they fail to meet the payment date.

    Good luck!
