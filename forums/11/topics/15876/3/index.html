    SINGER: CHRISTINE FAN (FAN WEIQI)

    WHO IS SHE: THIS GIRL WHO GAVE US YIN WEI, HAD TO OVERCOME HER FEAR OF PERFORMING IN FRONT OF THE CAMERA

    TITLE OF ALBUM: TAI YANG (SUN)

    RELEASED BY: Decca

    Fan Fan's 2nd album is not available in most record stores...bought it from Music Junction as an import label...costs me $25.99...so if u like her...u either have to fork out that amout (worth it) or wait until some local company brings it in...might take a while...but even then, limited stocks available.

    Once again, Fan Fan proves that she isn't just a pretty face...her voice isn't the most superb, but under producers Jim Lee and Paula Ma Yufen, manage to showcase her talents very well...no surprise she had been nominated in the Golden Melody Awards and Singapore Hit Awards...but she is up against other singing talents like Freya, Yanzi, Jay...etc...but she is in her class of her own.

    Dun really like the image on the album...with the hair tied up...and all that. Title track Tai Yang is written by Pam Oei and Lim Kay Siu, both Singaporeans...with Fan Fan doing the lyrics. It's a sweet romantic song abt the love one being a sun...a fresh breeze amongst sad angsty love songs.

    In fact, there aren't sad angsty love songs...okie there are a couple of sad songs like TA MEI YOU CUO...this is a wonderful from the composer and lyricist of Yin Wei...could hear the words Ta Mei You Cuo echoing in the ears...it's a slight lament...done with such pathos (mixture of feelings)...of sadness, forgiveness, giving a very wu nai feeling. Really a good song.

    Fan Fan sounds very girlish in the fast tracks like Zai Lian Luo (written Zuo An An) and Shou Pa Jiao...(good lyrics by Lin Xi)...can give these 2 a miss.

    Recommend Mei Tian De Mei Tian...a song abt life...refreshing...Nong Qing Mi Yi written by Sandee Chan...is very Sandee Chan...I like Tian Shi De Chi Bang...it's a very sentimental with Fan Fan composing the tune...sentimental and touches me.

    Ru Guo Ai composed and written Shen Bai Se...is standard fare...arrangement done mostly on keyboard...this is a great song...reminds me of Hai Pa...and Jue Mi which sounds a little like Jewel's Foolish Games...this is one of the best written lyrics.

    OVERALL RATING: B+

    BEST LYRICS: JUE MI

    TITLE OF ALBUM: YOU DIAN YE (A LITTLE WILD)

    RELEASED BY: Alfa/BMG

    Landy's first album wasn't much of a bit success...it was back in 1999...end 1999...most people remember her for her duet with her mentor Jacky Wu...and it is said that her image then made her look overly matured.

    1 year plus later, and an image overhaul, and with a great looking bod, wun say she is tat pretty, this 21 or 22 year old lass now looks her age...and with a totally different direction...she chooses R&B now, quite a breakthrough, and a wise one...her album sales are relatively modest and good in taiwan. She is touted as Taiwan's answer to Jennifer Lopez and Japan's Ayumi Hamasaki...

    I wun say she is very good/perfect is singing r&b yet...give her sometime and i think she will shine. Jay contributed 3 pieces to this album, and the additional song is a duet with Landy in the song WU DING. I did not hear the original version, but this version is sure one of the nicest duets I hear these days. This song is found in her latest edition of the album which comes with a VCD.

    Besides Jay, Wen Lan got Mai Ke Lin...wonder who he is...he even duets with her in the Jay Chou composed Dong Xin...maybe producers not confident of her pulling off a song herself...but nevertheless Landy shines in both duets.

    Mai Ke Lin also contributed quite a few tunes...like Can You Feel It...some parts of the song remind me of J Lo's Love Don't Cost a Thing...there are a number of fast numbers in the album like L.A.N.D.Y...which wanna make u go dancing.

    Everyone heard Bei Dou Xing...no need further intro on that...the lyrics are a bit sad, and Wen Lan did fill some of the lyrics in, as well as some other songs...she sounds like Jay. The other song Jay composed is a ballad titled YAN LEI ZHI DAO...a bit like his HEI SE YOU MO...but i like this song very much...kinda touching. The other slow number is 1+1=0.

    If you like R&B and dun like screaming queens, this one is worth buying. Besides she would be here in Singapore soon.

    OVERALL RATING: B+

    BEST LYRICS: BEI DOU XING

    WANG LEE HOM'S WEI YI (B)

    Actually I am kinda disappointed with this album...does not measure up to Lee Hom's previous albums...in fact he seems to add mroe of the band factor into his album...however...it's really a highly listenable effort...i prefer AI DE JIU SHI NI over WEI YI...BAI HU LI and AN QUAN GAN also not bad...Lee Hom would be in Singapore soon.

    JAY CHOU (FANTASY) (A-)

    Jay Chou + Fang Wen Shan + Vivian Hsu...well...a highly anticipated album of the year...and it truly lives up to its expectations...at least it's not an album full of love songs...touching on topics like family violence...ninjas...etc...however some tunes seem familiar...oh...but i guess we can forgive him right? Actually...all the songs are really nice and listenable...the only thing is that the album is rather too short...less than 40 mins...

    COCO LEE (PROMISE)(A-)

    This is another highly anticipated album too...for once Coco Lee drops her vocal pyrotechnics...i am not exaggerating...at least u dun have her playing with her voice...acting like divas (think mariah)...she trims all these down to the bare minimal...u r rewarded with songs like SO CRAZY, DAO MA DAN, BU BIAN DE NUO YAN amongst many others...in fact all the songs are very easy on the ears and they dun fall below standards...many sentimental numbers in this album...go grab it.

    DANIEL CHAN (WO YAO DE ZHI SHI AI)(B+)

    More than just a pretty face...that is what Daniel is trying to prove...his taste for music is improving...kudos...to Xiaotung...of course...ballads are still important...and still remain in this album...assembling zhang ya dong and other producers...he manages to give us a fresh daniel chan sans ballads...but in the process i think he is losing his fans...But Daniel is maturing in his music.

    CINDY CHEN YU XIN (TONG MING)(B+)

    Although 15 years of age...she has assembled many great producers for her maiden album...and she is much better than a certain Genie...she does not scream as though her vocal chords would break any moment...in fact i could feel the sense of little Elva in her...her voice is definitely one of the best in the newcomers and is competent. 6 fast tracks 4 slow tracks...lyrics wise...very good...well written...and she does well in both fast and slow songs...support this newcomer.

    FAYE WONG (FAYE WONG)(A-)

    Been waiting for her since her last outing with Fables...probably scared a lot of people with her previous album...Forsaking her usual crew of Zhang Yadong...assembling a new group with Ren Shan Ren Hai, Nic Tse, Wu Bai, Tanya, Faye hopes to reach another level in her music...Well...result is...the whole album is not as schizophrenic as Fable and not as erm...alternative...but still this album is still a little not extremely mainstream...in fact I dun think Liu Nian could be the best zhu da song...it is not as good as XIAO WANG SHU even...still...this album is up to standards even though there are no new surprises.

    ZHANG HUIMEI (ZHEN SHI)(A-)

    This is Ah Mei's brand new album with Warner Music...what could i say...a brand new Ah Mei is here to stay...do not compare her with her previous efforts from Forward or her first few albums...would be totally unfair. This album consists of 11 tracks including Pai Shan Dao Hai...with 5 fast numbers and 5 slow numbers...with Ah Mei under the pen name of Katsu...penned the lyrics for Gan Ying...the mtv was filmed in spain with ah mei doing the flamenco..dance...hot man! The song is also hot...but uurgh...it's a cover song...but still a great song...recommened fast songs includes A Ji Yu Le...and the R&B Nu Ren Shuo...a look into a woman's thoughs...delivered well by Ah Mei...Back to the slow numbers...no more ermm...very SU (SONG) numbers like WO KE YI BAO NI MA, CHEN ZAO tat kinda line...in place are heart wrenching and touching pieces that remind us of the old ah mei days...like JI DE, ZHEN SHI, WO HEN WO AI NI...etc...ballads which are really endearing...once again...this is a new ah mei...and do support her.

    The curse of Ling Wu has never been lifted from Winnie Tsin.     RATING: B

    JULIA PENG'S QING GE SHOU

    Assembling 3 Chens (ex bf Chen Guohua, Chen Zihong and Chen Shanni), this album manages to bring Julia away from all those very very sad and depressing songs...at least this album has fewer of those...except for BIE WEN WO AI SHUI (which ties in with a tv serial)...most of the songs are really good...i dun really like the fast numbers...but i would recommend XUN XI and HAO YU ZHAO. There are 7 slow numbers include 1 English song...and the songs are quite touching...I recommend LU REN, TAI DU, JING GUO...Can't Stop Christmas...One of my favs is QING GE SHOU written by Chen Shanni...(alwiz like her compositions)...it starts of a little like XIANG SHUI...anyway Julia has a good voice...do support this album.

    RATING: B+

    SANDY LAM (YUAN LAI...Lin Yilian)

    Actually, I was a little disappointed when I first listened to the album...the melodies and tunes are really simple...and not so flamboyant as compared to her previous 2 albums with Virgin. However, the good part is that she stopped singing a lot of covers...this time you could find only 2 instead of the usual 4 or 5.

    Lin Xi contributed 1 lyric to this album, with Yao Qian doing 2...this album does not boasts big time composers or whatever...and it is almost half done by Singaporeans...with Eric Ng/Huang Yunren working with Sandy on the melodies...and Xiaohan (a female lyricist) penning lyrics for as many as 6 songs. Sandy has doubled as producer and singer and composer for this album.

    This album requires patience...to soak urself in the simplicity...like ZHI FEI JI...which needs no more introduction...Juz have patience to listen to all the songs...they r all good.

    Run down on tracks
    1) ZHAN ZAI DI YI PAI (Sandy+Eric+Xiaohan)...this is a catchy no...good opening for an album

    2) MO DU SHANG BEI

    3) WO DE XIN ZAI TIAO WU
    This song is a cover...with Lin Xi helming the lyrics...the song starts of slow before becoming fast at the end...it has i think the zither or some chi music instrument...in the music...

    4) ZAI YI GE YE WAN 7) SHI ER XIAO SHI BU XIA YU

    These 2 songs are done by Eric+Sandy+Xiaohan...those very simple melodies.

    5) I'M NOT STRONG ENOUGH

    One of my favourite tracks in the album...who says R&B ballads is all about screaming...this ballad overthrows that...one of the great songs in the album composed by Wang Peirong.

    IT WASN'T MEANT TO BE

    It's very karaoke friendly...and i love this song...gets to me.

    9) SHE BU DE SHUO ZAI JIAN

    Another karaoke friendly song...by eric and xiaohan...dun miss this wonderful song.

    10) HUI DAO YUAN LAI

    An upbeat number...sandy laps this up with ease...tis is also a cover song.

    11) MING MING

    The theme song of a lesbian theme movie...peony pavilion...this song is one of the strongest in the album...composed by wo else but CHEN SHANNI...yes...it's her again...the arragement is very unique and does not lose the modern feel of this song.

    Overall, welcome to the new Sandy Lam.
