    wow.. i started this thread quite some time ago when i was doing my project... quite surprised to see it revived....
    well... since then, i've had had some new perspectives on this issue... i used to think that talent was all it took... like if a band or a singer had shi li then they should and would make it big.. but i realise now the fame game is more than just about talent, it's about timing, about luck, about opportunites and publicity... an analogy is this: warner music recently had this recruitment drive thingy right? and there was this stefanie sun look-a-like who didn't make it right? and i was just thinking, no matter how talented this person is, the record company wouldn't want her because what would we want to do with another stefanie sun? similarly, it doesn't matter at all if you're an F4 look-a-like who can (god forbid!) sing better or something because for one thing you're not F4 right? so it's all about having the perfect timing, isn't it?

    and look at local band dreamz fm, they are the perfect example of how pure talent is not enough... it is really really a pity that they are not recognised enough, be it in taiwan or at home... but on a brighter side, the local mandarin-pop industry seems to have more and more male singers coming up now: with ah du, yong bang, and new all-male groups chou pi jiang and pink punk! -- filling up the void that has been left vacant by the sole jimmy ye all those years ago...
