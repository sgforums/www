    Actually the new version is by Cai Li Lian and Robin Goh (Wu Luo Bin, a ex-MediaCorp artiste), not Hong Shao Xuan. I think HSX did sing it in live performances before, but not recorded version. The Eric Moo version is with Huang Hui Zhen, who was also a XinYao pioneer now practising Chinese medicine/ acupuncture.

    Hong Shao Xuan has many nice songs in the past. If interested, can find those Liang Wen Fu's / Xinyao's compilation which has a lot of HSX songs. Ni Shi Bu Shi Hai Zai is the title track for "Gui Dao", a movie by Robin Goh and Cai Li Lian which also had that Xie Hou version. His other nice songs include "Sui Yue De Ning Yi Zhang Lian", "Dan Yi Zhi Liang Liang De Ge", "Ni De Dao Ying" (with Yan Li Ming), etc. He was my fave Xinyao singer, together with Xu Nan Sheng and Zheng Zhan Lun (ZZL not really in the Xinyao pack, but around that time...).

    Now Singaporean compositions have already lost a lot of the Singapore flavour and becoming rather globalised. Even Liang Wen Fu's compositions are going along the same route. (eg. Kit Chan "Ni Ku") Good or bad, I guess it's up to individual.
