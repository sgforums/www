    I watched "Vaginalogue" before some years ago when it was first staged in the Substation. It's a rather thought-provoking piece on the most private part of a female through use of dramatisation to portray how different people perceive the part. There is quite a liberal use of "raw words" (aka vulgarities - note the beginning, if there is no change to the play) but all in the right context so people should not feel offended. It made women have a new perspective to the part which is closest to them but perhaps the least understood. It also offered men a interesting viewpoint of the part besides the erroroneous use of it as part of vulgar language and criminal behaviour.

    However, given the often liberalised plays put up by Drama Box, which I suspect played a part in its almost non-existent funding by NAC, the small organisation will cease to operate after it fulfils its obligations to agreed plays (of which Vaginalogue is one). I do hope it will make a comeback in future.

    As for BaoBao, I hope she returns to air on that station which gives her the most amount of flexibility for her hosting style which is seen as too straightforward to the straight-laced. We really miss her!
