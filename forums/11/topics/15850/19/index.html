    Her style is rather mellow, sometimes a bit cheeky. It will remind one of Rene during her "da le yi ba yao shi gei ni" days - strong, silent but genteel at the same time. Very Rene book. A very commendable effort from a non-professional writer.

    I went to browse through my books over the weekend. The book about dreams of cat and strange things is titled "21 dreams of Liang Wen Fu". I am now re-reading his "Mei Pi Qing", a collection of his works in Zao Bao.

    Anyway, the bookfair was quite a disappointment. I was eagerly anticipating it, after the long wait since its postponement because of SARS. There were noticeably less exhibitors and many were companies selling children learning materials, like encyclopaedias, CD-ROMs etc. Popular Bookstore was prominently absent, unless I was really blind. Union and Commercial Press were the only stalls which I was really interested in. Even my favourite Cite Publishing (of Taiwan books) were not there.

    I also bought Huang Da Wei's and Hu Yan Bin's albums. IMHO, HDW's "TIME" was not as good as the previous "Qiu Tian 1944", perhaps the music genre was so diverse that the flow of the album seemed a bit too jarring. Hu Yan Bin's album was quite easy on the ear. Music style is similar to Jay Chou, but of course his singing is much clearer. His music is also more safe and less experimental. The spoiler is his duet with Rain Lee which seemed so out of place. Even his recent duet with Elva was much better.
