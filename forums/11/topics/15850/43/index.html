    16 Dec 03, 09:28
    Leon Lai won Best Actor last year for "3 jin". Tony Leung won for IA1 which fell within GH qualifying period for this year. IA2 and 3 should be for next year.

    Many of these Taiwanese films were not shown outside Taiwan so the foreign judges will have a vague impression of it. Of course they are supposed to be unbiased, but as long as the human factor is involved, there is bound to be some subjectivity. The thing is GH needs the more popular HK movies to be nominated so that people will pay attention to it. In the 90s, many awards were won by Taiwanese films which had good reviews but poor takings, and it was not able to create a "gong4 ming2" with the audiences or viewers and affected its ratings and popularity. In fact, this year's awards was almost dropped because of lack of funds, leaving the filmmakers who view it as the most coveted award in Asia shocked, so they started some fund raising campaigns with the locals to get it going.

    Another worry is that as the China movie market is starting to open up, the general view is that the already fierce foreign competition (esp from HK) will further subject the Taiwanese movies into obscurity. Then the funding of the mostly independent movies may just dry up and GH, a Taiwanese award ceremony, will end up having no local nominations eventually.
