    For starters, I'll like to recapture a P hua4 lian2 pian1 episode that I like...

    We all know Pavarotti and his powerful voice. But we know little about his encounter with a crying baby. The story goes that once, a baby in a neighbouring room was crying in the middle of the night and irritating everybody within earshot. Despite crying for a long time, the baby's voice nor did the volume of the crying changed a bit. This made Pavarotti curious about how a baby can cry for so long. Then, Pavarotti realised that whenever the baby is almost out of breath or the voice is giving way, it will instinctively stop crying, catch its breath, take a deep breath before blasting away again. This taught Pavarotti an important lesson. And guess what, Pavarotti will go down in history as one of the best opera singers who has ever lived.

    So... the moral of the story is... sometimes when unpleasant things befall us, if we could keep our cool and try to learn from the experience, we might just learn an important lesson in life.

    Rather than complain or feel angry at the baby, Pavarotti sought to learn from the baby. That made the diffence.

    During a lecture, the instructor held aloft a glass of water and asked the class to guess the weight of the water. Many answers were offered. If one has held it for one hour, it becomes pretty heavy. If one has held it for a day, an ambulance might be necessary.

    So... if we were to liken the glass of water to stress, we can conclude that regardless of the magnitude of the stress, it is the duration of shouldering the stress that gets to us. The moral of the story is to put down the glass of water (stress) when we feel tired (stressed) and get some rest. After some rest, you can pick up the glass of water (stress) and carry on. In this way, one will be able to shoulder stress for a longer period of time.
