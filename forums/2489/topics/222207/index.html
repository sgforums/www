    BIG-MATCH FACTS

    BOLTON WANDERERS play the second of back-to-back home fixtures against London's top two clubs. Reigning champions Chelsea form the opposition, just four days after Nicolas Anelka got his Bolton Premiership scoring record off the mark with a brace against his former club Arsenal, in the 3-1 victory at the Reebok.

    This match-up brings together not only the third and second placed clubs in the Premiership, but the third and second most winning outfits in the highest League. Trotters manager Sam Allardyce is eager his charges add to their 71 Premiership wins, rather than to the 71 defeats in his stewardship.

    The Trotters have beaten Chelsea only once at the Reebok. Dean Holdsworth scored when Bolton prevailed 1-0 on 26 October 1997.

    CHELSEA got a deserved point from Sunday's summit meeting with Manchester United, when they came from behind to draw 1-1 at Old Trafford. Now they run out just 19 miles down the road at the Reebok anxious to close the three point gap on the Red Devils at the top, or at least ensure it is not widened, as United are simultaneously hosting Everton.

    Three points from this encounter would provide Chelsea with their 1,000th post war League win.

    The Blues are unbeaten in five Premiership meetings with Bolton, and have won their last three visits to the Reebok (all by a 0-2 margin). There has never been a goalless draw between these clubs in a League encounter on Bolton turf.

    FULHAM host Arsenal in their fourth derby of the season against clubs inside the M25, anxious to avoid a third Premiership defeat on the spin. Indeed the Cottagers have managed just one win in six League outings.

Their last defeat to Fulham was 1-0 in the top flight on the banks of the Thames on New Year's Day 1966. Last season, Arsenal scored eight goals against Fulham - four at home, four away.

    BIG-MATCH FACTS

    MANCHESTER UNITED have a quick opportunity to return to winning ways, after Chelsea ended their seven-game victorious streak by coming from behind to hold them to a 1-1 draw in Sunday's top of the table showdown. It was United's 1,000th League draw in their history.

    Sir Alex Ferguson's side have still won more top League games than any other club this season, and own the top flight's sharpest attack and tightest defence. They're on a nine-match unbeaten sequence and need a victory to maintain their three point cushion at the summit at the very least.

    United have been beaten just once at home by Everton in the Premiership, but have only managed draws in their last two against them at the Theatre of Dreams.

    EVERTON may be seven places and 14 points inferior to their opponents, with six fewer victories, but they are still well placed to challenge for a Champions League place. They go to Old Trafford without a victory in five away League matches.

    This is the 15th season of Premiership football, and this fixture pits the highest scoring club in the Premier League era against the one to have conceded the most. United have netted 1,087 goals, Everton have shipped 751 goals.

    Peter Beardsley, Robert Warzycha and Mo Johnston scored without reply in Everton's very first Premiership visit to Old Trafford on 19 August 1992. It was their most recent League victory there.

    BIG-MATCH FACTS

    LIVERPOOL are aiming to make Portsmouth their fourth successive Premiership victims at Anfield, while Pompey travel aiming to end a four-match losing League run away from home.

    Rafa's Reds have been almost totally dependent on their Anfield performances for their Premiership points so far this season. They have procured 17 more points in front of the home supporters, than on the road - the widest such dichotomy in the highest League. They're also hoping to increase the Premiership's second longest unbeaten home sequence to 24 games.

    The Reds are unbeaten in five Premiership clashes with Portsmouth, winning the last three.

    PORTSMOUTH travel to the north west five places superior to their opponents, but knowing that defeat would see Liverpool leapfrogging them.

    It's vital which side nets the opening goal. Portsmouth have opened the scoring in six Premiership matches this season and have gone on to win all six. Liverpool have done the same on five occasions.

    Pompey's only Premier League victory over Liverpool was 1-0 at Fratton Park on 18 October 2003, when Patrik Berger's early goal separated the sides. It's 55 years since they last took away maximum points from Anfield, winning 0-2 in the top flight on 18 August 1951.
