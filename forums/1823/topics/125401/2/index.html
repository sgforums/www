    This article is from today's copy of Today, dated 11 April 2005.

    Does a mum-to-be deserve a seat on the MRT? Views differ

    Letter from CHERLYN ONG

    I BELIEVE that no one owes anyone anything. I was stunned to read that Ms De Silva's husband actually asked people on the train to give up their seats for her because she was pregnant.

    I would regard that as rude and thoughtless because everyone has their right to their seats since we all pay the same fare to travel.

    I am a mother of two and not once during both my pregnancies did I - or my husband - force someone out of their seat on the MRT for my comfort. If I really felt tired that day, I would take a cab instead.

    Now, after a hard day's of work, I too need a seat on the train. After all, I still have dinner to prepare, two active kids to look after, clean up my house, iron, wash, etc when I get home.

    So, who is to say I don't deserve to have my rest on the train and who is to say a pregnant lady needs the seat more than I do?

    I do give up my seat when I feel the person (be it an old lady, a disabled person or a heavily pregnant lady) is in danger of falling and injuring themselves if they have to stand on a moving train.
