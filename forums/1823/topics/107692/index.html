  • aRcTiC_sTaR

    While I've got nothing against Motorola, I don't think I'd buy another of its phones. Here are the reasons why:

    1.) The price of their phones depreciates very fast.

    2.) SMS interface is rather laggy, even without the dictionary on.

    3.) Not as user-friendly as compared to other phones, you need to read the manual to learn how to use it.

    4.) Older models have the answer call button on the right, and the hang up button on the left, which is different from most of the other phones.

    5.) Batteries' life span is rather short, and they take up to 3 hours to be fully charged.

    6.) Housing for flip-phone models costs around $100 for replacement.

    7.) Non-Symbian OS phones which are Java-enabled can't play common Java games which most other Java-enabled phones can.

    8.) Workers who handle the phones sometimes knock the phones on the table to remove batteries that are hard to 'pluck' out. Imagine using a 3G phone, say E1000 like a tool to smash crab shell when you're eating it =P

    *Please note that this post is not intended to defame Motorola or whatsoever. This is merely for general review and comparison purposes.

    Any Motorola phone owners who would like to add on to this list? =)
