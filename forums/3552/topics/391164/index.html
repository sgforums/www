    HK is a very safe place, but having said that, common sense applies everywhere, dun show your valuables in public, take care when you are in crowded places (which happens alot in HK).

    It has a very comprehensive transport system. Buy the Octupus Card from the airport which will allow you travel on the MTR and Buses etc. Taxis are relatively expensive.

    The temple street markets or ladies markets, most of them are in the Kowloon side of Hongkong. While there are upmarket shopping malls in both the HK Island side and Kowloon side.

    The peak would be a great place to visit at night with your BF. If you want you can buy the pkg to the peak plus the Madame Tussad's museum at the Peak ticket counter.

    If you are adventurous, do venture out to the islands like Lantau Island and Lamma Island. Great places to visit with Lamma ISland good seafood.

    TUng Chung, at then end of the MTR line, has a great factory outlet mall.

    HK Disneyland is not worth going, especially if you have been to Tokyo Disneyland or those in the USA. Its very small. The car park is bigger than the park itself. Need to walk about 20 mins to the entrance. But only take 30 mins to finish circling round the park..

    I suggest:

    You buy an octopus card at the Airport. This card is like our ezlink card. Can be used at 7 Eleven and many other places, apart from buses, trains and trams.

    You can try taking CityFlyer to your hotel (A21 for Nathan Road), instead of the Airport Express (train service)... The express bus will end you up on the streets (near most hotels), whereas for the Airport Express, you have to carry your luggage and transfer to the MTR at Hong Kong Station (the linkage is about 15 mins walk) and then up the streets as their MTR stations are underground and need to walk up the stairs .... as most stations do not have escalators and lifts. 

    Star Ferry
    KMB bus 1,1A,2, 6 brings you from Star Ferry to Nathan Road..... covers Tsim Sha Tsui / Jordan / Mong Kok area.....

    Ocean Park
    Take Citybus 629 from Admiralty MTR.
    You can see panda bears, aquatic animals and have a fun day riding all the amusement park rides.... Closes at about 6pm - 7pm... can visit the peak after ocean park. 

    The Peak
    Take NWFB 15C from Star Ferry (HK Island). It will bring you to the lower peak terminus where you can take the red tram up The Peak.... Visit Madame Tussauds Wax Museum up at the peak. Buy a ticket to view the upper gallery when you can see the scenary of Hong Kong. Nice time to visit will be around sunset. See the night view and day view.

    There are 2 queues for the peak tram at the lower terminus. One is for group tours (mainly chinese prcs) and one is for free n easy. Free n easy do not need to wait.

    Ngong Ping 360
    Take MTR to Tung Chung Station.

    Take the crystal cabin. Its a return 1 hour cable car ride to visit the great buddha at the peak. It'll cost you SIN$10 more but you get priority queue. No need to squeeze with the PRCs and budget conscious tourists visiting Hong Kong. You can get a crystal cabin for yourself, instead of squeezing a 10 person cable car up Ngong Ping Village.

    Monkey Tales Theatre is crap. Dun waste your money.

    Ladies Market
    Walk about and experience a feel of Hong Kong.

    Tsim Sha Tsui / Mongkok / Jordan
    Walk through the streets.
