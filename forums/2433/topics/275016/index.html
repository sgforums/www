    CALTEX NATIONAL KARTING CHALLENGE 2007

    Date : 5 October Â– 7 October
    Venue : East Coast Park Car Park E1 (Beside Ski360)
    Time : 0900-1700hrs

    Form up a team of 4 according to the official categories:

    (Based on the age of the oldest team member)
    Category A: 15-18 yrs old (Registration fee: S$15 per person)
    Category B: 19-25 yrs old (Registration fee: S$20 per person)
    Category C: 26-35 yrs old (Registration fee: S$25 per person)
    Category D: 36 yrs old and above (Registration fee: S$30 per person)

    Challenge Schedule

    Qualifying rounds will be held on 5 and 6 October 2007 from 0900 Â– 1700hrs.

    The Finals will take place on 7 October 2007 from 0900 Â– 1700hrs.

    Challenge Format

    1. Each team will consist of 4 participants karting in relay-style.
    2. Each team member will be required to complete a distance of 2.4km (takes approximately 6mins to complete) on the karting track.
    3. 1 representative from 6 teams on the track at every slot.
    4. Change-over of drivers to take place only at the Pit Stop Area.

    Challenge Timing

    1. Maximum time allowed for the entire team is 25 minutes.
    2. Timing commences when first driver is flagged off and stops when fourth driver returns to start/finishing line within 25 mins.
    3. The timing is inclusive of the change-over between drivers at the Pit Stop Area.
    4. Timing taken for the individual team will stop once the maximum time limit is reached and any running karts on the track are to return to the Pit Stop Area.

    Judging

    1. All teams will be ranked accordingly based on the total race distance covered by all team members.
    2. Teams will also be ranked according to the total time taken for the team to complete the required race distance.
    3. Top 6 teams from each category with the maximum distance covered in the shortest timing will qualify and proceed to compete in the Finals.
    4. The team with the fastest overall timing will emerge as Winner of that respective category.
    5. Should there be a tie between two teams in the same category with identical total race timing taken or identical race distance covered, the team with the fastest individual lap time will emerge as the Winner of that respective category.

    Do strategize amongst yourselves in order to clock in the fastest timing and pit your skills against other teams!

    Kart safely, it is a friendly challenge!

    I'm not doubting your driving ability... just that I've seen many people try to drive karts like their own cars, and end up taking the wrong lines or skidding out of control... thats y its good that you know how the kart will probably react...

    Actually I'm more concerned about brake and accelerator modulation... Since the karts have a fixed rear axle, if you brake hard enough, it pretty much works like an E-brake... hard braking will lock the wheels and pretty much throw you into a spin... which is what you'll want to avoid... especially during corners... Same goes for when you suddenly lift off the accelerator, it might cause you to lose control... Basically gradual acceleration is the key..
