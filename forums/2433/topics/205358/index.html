    As i've worked in as a 2nd hand car-dealer b4,
    i would have driven numerous car-types.
    2 of e car types (01' Sunny & 03' Peoguet206) i've rented & tested.
    Here're my views of few of em:

    91' Starlet XL 1litre:
    One of my fav mini-hatchback. Cheap, simple with good ride quality. Although bland interior, it's a petrol saver. After 100kmh, car becomes less stable due to lightweight. A very practical budget car.

    92' Suzuki Swift SF413GL 1.3litre:
    One of e best hatchback cars i've driven. Good pickup & speed for a small budget car like this. 1/2 tank can last 1 week if driven properly. Short gear-range from 1st-3rd gear. Low resale/ scrap value.

    92' Suzuki Swift GTi 1.3litre:
    Considered one of e most powerful sports-hatch of its time. Very powerful for a 1.3litre with 3cylinders. Magnificient pickup, but hard to come by one in good condition. Lower resale/ scrap value, considering its status as a performance hatch.

    93' Toyota Corolla XLI 1.3litre:
    Quite roomy but bland interior, but fuel consumption is slightly more than other 1.3litres. Stiff handling. One of e more common budget COE cars around.

    04-05' Hyundai Getz 5dr 1.3litre:
    Fuel consumption relatively ok, but noisy engine.Stiff and scary on tight cornering 80kmh & above. Cant get over 120 without a jerky ride. Overall good budget PARF car.

    03' Peoguet 206 1.4litre:
    Rented this car. Went rounding with it, & was a petrol-saver.$20 95-octane for 1 whole night, & plenty remaining e next morning. Relatively good pickup & stable cornering, probably due to its short gear-ratio. However, car becomes unstable over 100kmh, & around 130 seems to be e limit of this lightweight car. 120 on uneven ground already e car lifted off-ground. Any faster would send tis car flying. Tested its speed in an unintentional race with a 92' Colt on e TPE months back, which i lost.

    03-04' Honda Jazz 1.4litre:
    Considered e most popular mini-hatch, & best fuel consumption of its category - over 20km/l. Performs as a Honda should. Responsive handling. Relative high resale/ export value. Great value for money.

    03' Mitsubishi Colt 1.5litre:
    Comparable to Honda Jazz in terms of ride quality. Stylish interior. Petrol consumption not bad. One of e most common min-MPVs around next to e Jazz. Relatively high resale/ export value. A very practical daily car tat delivers performance.

    04-05' Hyundai Accent 1.5litre:
    Relatively budget car, but cheap interior & noisy engine. Good small-size sedan for short-term ride. Fuel consumption ok. Engine problems might start to kick-in after 2yrs. Low resale/ export value.

    91' Honda Civic GL 1.6litre:
    Old & "squarish" family car. Can't run fast. Didn't work for me. Water-tank overheats easily. Petrol drinker with no performance. An "uncle" car.

    91-93' Nissan Presea 1.6litre:
    Very powerful & responsive engine with low-ride. Good pickup, but fuel sucker. Stiff handling. I like its exterior styling. Potential to modd into R32 lookalike.

    01' Nissan Sunny EX 1.6litre:
    Rented this car. Good pickup & stability over 100kmh. Engine becomes slightly noisy at higher speeds, & is quite a petrol sucker. Did abit of "carpark rally" on my own, & performed not too bad despite its FF layout. "Drifted" quite well. Went rounding with a friend in Orchard, & beat a 360Modena right to its ass in tight cornerings.
    However, felt its turning radius not as wide as expected. Very pratical for daily use.

    96' Honda Civic SIR 1.6litre:
    Smooth ride & reliable engine. Fuel consumption not bad. Looks better with bodykits. Stable at relatively high speeds above 100kmh despite its lightweight.

    93' Honda Civic ESI 1.6litre:
    Alike the SIR, reliable engine & good consumption. Looks nicer in stock.

    93' Honda Civic VTi 1.6litre:
    Great & responsive VTEC engine. Spacious interior & one of e best Jap sedans of its time. One of e better performance COE sedans around. Responsive handling.

    92' Mitsubishi Colt GLXi 1.6litre:
    Relatively powerful engine with good soundtrack. Great potential for mods of engine & bodykit. Good-looking 3dr hatchback.Might consider gettin this as my 1st car. Starlet GT & Swift GTi's old rival.

    04-05' Hyundai Getz 3dr 1.6litre:
    Alike e 1.3 version, noisy engine, petrol sucker too. However, one of e better 3dr hatchbacks in e supermini section with responsive handling. Max speed electronically-limited to around 140kmh, but chasis can take punishment till only around 130.

    03' Subaru Imprezza 1.6litre:
    One of e most stable rides i've got on a 1.6 sedan. Only AWD performance for its sedan pricetag. Very strong accl & able to rev. Very responsive ride & stable during tight cornering at high speed 60kmh & above. Money for value. Great potential for modd to STi look-alike. Fuel consumption not too bad.

    02-03' Honda Stream 1.7litre:
    Great MPV with 1.7 Vtec. Efficient & reliable engine. One of e better MPVs for sub-2litre range. Fuel consumption comparable to other 2.0litre models.

    03' Nissan Praire 2.0litre:
    Quite a petrol drinker with stiff handling. Stylish sliding side-doors. Roomy interior. One of e more common Nissan MPVs around.

    03' Mazda Premacy 2.0litre:
    Powerful engine & very comfortable ride. Preferred to other 2.0 MPVs at its pricetag. Not very common on road nowadays, as most hav been exported overseas & replaced by newer models. Fuel consumption abit high, though.

    02-03' Mitsubishi Galant GDI 2.0litre:
    A powerful sedan with MIVEC engine. Spacious & comfy interior, with well-balanced performance on-road. Great exterior styling. High resale/export value. Fuel consumption not a problem.

    03' Hyundai Tuscani FX 2.0litre:
    Low ride coupe at relatively cheap price. Slower pickup compared to other 2litre performance coupes & sedans. Petrol sucker, but responsive handling. Ferrari look-alike in Red, so who cares when u're driving an Italian poser at a Korean pricetag? Driven hard, & chasis begin destablise at around 180kmh, which seemingly is also its electronically-limited speed.

    04' Nissan Cefiro 230JDM 2.3litre:
    Powerful & quiet V6 engine. Innovative keyless entry. Performs as a JDM luxury sedan would, but fuel consumption slightly higher compared to its 2.3 sector. Superior interior built-quality compared to its 2.4 Accord counterpart. High resale/ export value.

    04' Toyota Camry 2.4litre:
    Powerful & quiet engine. Keyless entry. Ride feels e same as Cefiro, but interior more lusious than e 2.3 luxury sedan. High resale/ export value. BMW 3-series performance at JDM price. Quite worth e bite.

    04-05' Honda Odyssey 2.4litre:
    The best Jap MPV for its money-for-value. Although a 2.4litre at its pricetag, it has great market resale value/ export value & is a low fuel consumer for its engine displacement. Very quiet engine & comfortable with cruise-control. Very responsive handling. Very high resale/ export value.

    04' Mitsubishi Grandis 2.4litre:
    One of e longest MPVs i've driven, next to e Odyssey. Powerful and loud 2.4 MIVEC engine. Can be heard from afar- noisest MPV i've driven, but reliable engine & cosy interior. Petrol drinker, compared to other 2.4 MPVs

    .......Still many others, but no time to type.
    All are my personal notes & opinions on e diff models & their performances.
    Next time will share more

    Yep, since we're on e topic of Test-driving,
    i would like to share wif u guys some general tips on test-driving a car
    on my experience working as a preowned car dealer.

    Pre-owned/ 2nd hand cars:

    - Check e mileage of e car. A 1-2 yrs old car mileage should be around 5k to 30k. 3-5 yrs should be around 40k to not exceeding 80k. Too much mileage for a new car means an overworked but non-seasoned engine, which may cause future problems like premature wear & tear of engine pistons & parts, etc.

    - Check engine bay interior & boot interior perimeter. A crack/ dent around boot or engine-bay means a possibility of previous accident. Signs of wielding will also means atttempted cover-up of impacted area. So do check thoroughly.

    - Check engine bay for portions of oily/greasy parts. Any sign of grease around engine compartment hints at leakage of either engine oil or transmission oil. Could be potentially dangerous to e engine if not fixed properly.

    - Check for signs of over-abrasion on tyres, & cracks on rims.

    - Insist on an inspection report by either VICOM or STA. Check for e paint-scan. Ensure e index indicates only 1 layer of paint. More than 1 layer on any area could means a cover-up of an accident-impacted area.

    - On road, at speed of around 50kmh, slowly release BOTH hands from e steering, & ensure car is still cruising straight. This is checking of wheels-alignment. If car swerves slightly to either left/right, it means e alignments are off & must be verified.

    - At around 50kmh, jam e pedal-brake to around 20kmh to test e sensitivity of brakes & ABS. Make sure NO car is following behind.

    - During testdrive, listen for abnormal sounds from e engine at different speeds, eg. below 30kmh & above 80kmh. Tyre noise, timing-belt, engine vibration, etc.

    - Make sure all necessary equiptments, eg. spare-tyre, triangle, spanner, tyre-jacks are present beneath e boot.
