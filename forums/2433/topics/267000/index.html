    not bad, being able to use the jet plane as a background balances off with the car.

    improve on the lighting that's all. in taking studio shots of subject, there should be 3 lights

    Key Light (placed in front of subject 6 o clock): is the main or predominant light on a subject. It is the light that reveals the shape, form, texture and definition of the the subject.

    Fill Light (placed at side about 4 o clock): any light that fills in the harshness of the shadows created by the key light. In other words, Fill Light helps with taking out unwanted shadow areas.

    Back Light (placed directly behind the object, cannot be seen by camera): any light that generally comes from above and behind, hitting the back of the the subject's head. It is a light that helps to set the subject apart from the background.

    and if u want and got more lights to spare

    Background Light (placed anywhere, just point at the background): any light used for lighting up a background.

    for me, i dun have so many lights at home, so i only use these 4 lights when in my sch studio. hope the tips are useful
