    I graduated in 2002.Not sure if this helps, there are currently two campuses, Toh Tuck and Bartley, both made up of the former Jurong and Outram Institutes.MI will be moving to their new mega campus in Bukit Batok next year, which will combine both former schools into one.

    The facilities there will be similar to any Junior College.I was from the Arts Faculty so I can tell you that some of the teachers are quite motivated, and others feel it's a dead end.I thought it was the end of the road when I ended up in JI then, because of the poor identity and the lack of motivation in many students.

    The main barrier here is motivation, because the ppl I've studied with are no less talented and were from top schools too, but we just happened to screw up our 'O's and yeah ..you get the point.

    I've been taught by very good Literature and Geography teachers and I think I'm quite satisfied by it.But if you can enter a JC, do so, unless you don't mind taking one more year to study and work towards a good 'A' level pass.

    I managed to get two distinctions and will begin my NUS term in August at FAS, possibly under the University Scholars Programme.So, it's a place where if you can learn and are willing to do independant study outside curriculum, you can score because of that one extra year.
