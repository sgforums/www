    [CNA] LTA embarks on trial to reduce waiting time for taxis

    Technology such as heat sensors and cameras will be deployed at seven taxi stands to aid in better matching taxi supply with demand, according to the Land Transport Authority.

    SINGAPORE: The Land Transport Authority on Wednesday (Nov 5) announced it will embark on a year-long trial for its Taxi Information System (TIS) from today, aimed at reducing waiting time for passengers.

    Seven taxi stands were identified for the trial: HarbourFront Centre, Hitachi Towers, Keck Seng Tower, Lucky Plaza, Ngee Ann City, OG People’s Park and VivoCity. They will be fitted out with heat sensors to detect commuters waiting in the taxi queue in real time, and supported by intelligent camera technology that displays footage for taxi drivers and commuters to view the queue status, said LTA.

    The average passenger waiting time at the taxi stands will be calculated based on the information collected, and transmitted to the taxi companies' call centres. These call centres can, in turn, broadcast the information to their drivers to encourage unhired taxis to call at taxi stands with high demand, it added.

    "With the TIS, taxi drivers can reduce empty cruising while commuters can also look forward to shorter taxi waiting times," LTA stated.

    In the initial stage of the trial, the system is configured to alert the taxi companies' call centres automatically when there are 15 or more people in the queue, and when the average waiting time for a taxi is more than 15 minutes. The agency said the algorithm will be calibrated where necessary during the trial.

    Commuters can make use of the www.mytransport.sg or MyTransport.SG mobile app to access real-time footage of the taxi passenger queue, which would help them decide if they want to join the queue. The app is only available for Android users currently, and the iOS version will be "available later", according to the press release.

    The information on average waiting time at the taxi stands will be available at the end of this year, the LTA added.
