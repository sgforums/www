    SINGAPORE - A 53-year-old man was killed after he lay down in the middle of a road and got hit by a taxi at Jalan Sultan. The accident took place early Monday at around 3am. According to Lianhe Wanbao, the victim, Mr Zainudin was arguing with an unknown woman before the accident took place. Following the argument, he walked to the third lane of the road and proceeded to lie down. A taxi that was driving on the same road then hit him and the body was dragged for 20m before the taxi came to a halt. Blood could be seen flowing out of the body. The cabby, known only as 60-year-old Mr Goh, was driving at around 60km/h and could not brake in time after seeing Mr Zainudin. A passenger who was in the taxi did not know that the taxi had hit someone and asked why the taxi had to be stopped. Mr Goh called the police after seeing the Mr Zainudin's injuries. The cabby appeared to be calm when he was interviewed. He told reporters that he had been driving a taxi for 21 years and this was the first time he was involved in such a major accident. He said he felt that his taxi licence will be revoked following the accident. Mr Goh added that he felt surprised that Mr Zainudin's companion left the scene of the accident even before the police arrived. When the cabby got down the taxi, he saw the woman crying beside the body. However she was nowhere to be found after Mr Goh went back into his taxi to call the police. A witness who only wanted to be known as Ms Liu mentioned that Mr Zainudin and the woman had been arguing for 10 minutes before the accident. The commotion can be heard by people at nearby shops. Another witness who wanted to be known as Mr Liang mentioned that the couple reeked of alcohol when the accident took place. The accident is currently under police investigation.
