[ST] 'Grey taxis' the answer to cab woes

  • Ct2220
    7 Oct 14, 15:21

    An Email from Gareth O'Brien to The Straits Time 

    RECENT discussions on Singapore's taxi woes seem to overcomplicate the issue.

    The problem is obvious: There are not enough taxis during peak periods (good for cabbies, bad for customers), and far too many outside these periods (bad for cabbies, good for customers).

    The solution is clear: Recruit part-time taxi drivers to cater to customers during peak periods, without hurting full-time cabbies outside these periods. Let one of the taxi operators run "grey taxis", whose drivers must be retirees over the age of 65 and who are willing to work only part-time.

    To start with, park 2,000 of these cabs at HDB carparks islandwide, and offer them for rental to these retirees at much lower rates compared with those for regular taxis.

    The driver can book a grey taxi online, collect it and drive it only from 6.30am to 10am, and 5.30pm to 9pm.

    The service would offer part-time work for retirees, avoid competing with full-time cabbies during non-peak hours, and benefit passengers.

    Gareth O'Brien
