3-vehicle collision on PIE after cabby 'can't feel brakes' -

  • bowah
    6 Sep 14, 13:31
    SINGAPORE - It was a horrifying moment for a passenger in a ComfortDelGro taxi when the cabby exclaimed that she could not feel the brakes. What followed was a three-vehicle collision along the Pan-Island Expressway (PIE) at about 3.10pm on Friday. The passenger, known only as Christopher, told citizen journalism Stomp: "The driver suddenly said, 'Oh no, I can't feel my brakes,' and then we hit the car in front." The car subsequently collided into another vehicle in front of it as well. Christopher, who was sitting in the back, hit his eye on the seatback in front upon impact. This resulted in an inflammation of the eye and a hospital bill of about $150. Coincidentally, a mechanic was passing by and stopped to help, while the taxi driver phoned Comfort DelGro for assistance. A tow truck and a replacement cab for Christopher arrived at the scene after 45 minutes. Comfort DelGro also sent another cab afterwards to bring him to the hospital for a checkup. Christopher commended the taxi operator's response to the accident. He told Stomp: "I feel that Comfort DelGro did their best to respond, although the replacement taxi took 45 minutes to arrive. They also said they would pay for my bill. "I have just gone through the checkup and will hope for full recovery soon. "I hope the taxi driver doesn't get into trouble, it really wasn't her fault, it should be some mechanical issue."
