    hi axel, when i was a newbie, i started out as a comfort hirer going omo. extremely bad move!!! first month was especially bad. i didn't have any problems with the driving part cos i have my own set of wheels for many years. but the learning curve for destinations & routes is really very steep and you gotta learn fast. in my first week there were days when i took home $20 after rental of $104.86 and diesel of about $40. also, i didn't know how to regulate my working hours, so there were days when i finished at 11pm and then couldn't wake up the next morning. i couldn't drive nights cos my night vision is poor, so out of necessity i have to drive in the day. it was only from my 2nd month when i managed to find a night relief that i settled into a work routine. that was back in end 2007 when the economy was still doing fine. it was all very demoralising and i was asking myself is it really so difficult to drive a taxi. it was also a kind of awakening for me when i realised that some people really do treat taxi drivers as the lowest form of life in sgp, maybe even lower than maids.

    so what we are trying to say in this forum is that : newbie hirer = bad idea, newbie omo hirer = extremely bad idea. tiredness = high chance of accidents.

    listen to poolie, "sit on the cow while looking for a horse". start as a relief first, build up your knowledge base and then consider your next move. don't be impulsive. good luck in your new career!
