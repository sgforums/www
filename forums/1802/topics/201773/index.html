    I WISH to relate a nasty experience suffered by my 73-year-old mother in Yishun on July 9 at almost noon.
    That day, it rained heavily. My mum and her maid were waiting to hail a taxi to get them home after they visited the wet market in Chong Pang. After a long wait, they managed to stop Trans-Cab SHB 7546 R. My mum told the driver her destination as well as the route to take (she takes that route regularly by taxi). Almost immediately, the driver scolded her, saying in Hokkien: 'Who's the taxi driver? You or me? You people always think you're so smart.'

    After the scolding, instead of taking the route given, he deliberately took a different and longer route, I suppose to bully the elderly woman into paying a higher fare.

    But the worst was to come. A moment later, still in Yishun, he took a booking and conveniently dumped my mum and her maid at the nearest bus stop. He even collected $3 and told my mum to take another taxi! I have confirmed with Trans-Cab booking staff that the driver did take that Yishun booking.

    The experience traumatised my mum so much, she refused to take a taxi again. Besides being elderly, she had knee replacement surgery just a few months ago and has a serious heart condition.

    Kelvin Tay Ghee Seng

    (Straits Times, 24 July)
