    There are 2 ways to say something,

    I am not talking about Yes or No.

    I am refering to.. say in nice, or say it bold.

     

    In this case, TS do not want to turn on Radio,

    as mentioned by some of the forumers here

    he can give a reason, "not working", or "no license", etc.

    and end it with, "i am sorry for your inconvenience".

    That's GOOD customer service.

     

    OR, he can say boldly,

    "This Radio not by Taxi Company", or

    "I got headache if radio on", etc.

     

    The difference is that, in the first case,

    you want to but unable to fulfil the customer request.

    Whereas in the second case, you can but refuse to do so.
