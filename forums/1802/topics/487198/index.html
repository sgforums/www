    Two pedestrians sent to hospital after accident with taxi at AMK junction
    Posted on 08 December 2014  |  2,447 views  |  6 comments
          

    An accident involving a taxi and two pedestrians occurred at the junction of Ang Mo Kio Avenue 2 and Avenue 5 yesterday (Dec 7).

    Stomper Kanaga was at home when she a heard a loud crashing sound outside her flat. 

    She immediately rushed to see what had happened and later informed Stomp that an accident had occurred.

    Kanaga said: 

    "A taxi was in the middle of the road and there were also two people lying there.

    "But passers-by quickly rushed to the injured pedestrians aid and helped move them to the side of the road."

    According to her, the taxi driver made attempts to drive away but he stayed at the scene after being yelled at by the passers-by.

    She added that the police and ambulance arrived on the scene within 15 minutes of the accident.

    Stomp contacted the police to find out more information about the accident. 

    A police spokesperson said: 

    "Police received a call today (Dec 7) at about 6.58pm informing us of an accident at the junction of Ang Mo Kio Avenue 2 and Ang Mo Kio Avenue 5.

    "Upon police's arrival, it was established that an accident involving a taxi and two pedestrians had occurred at the said location.

    "The two pedestrians were conveyed conscious to Khoo Teck Phuat Hospital.

    "Police investigations are ongoing."
