    To each his own. I am speaking from my perspective.... taxi driving is really last resort for people like me. It is of course better than a cleaner or kopi kia or whatever but why must compare with these especially if you are educated?

    I could have been a tutor but I don't like teaching. Was in the broking industry and seen many many of my colleagues became millionaires, driving big cars, staying in big houses. But I didn't have the luck and kena bad debts.

    Later did some small business and was raking in tens of $1000s profits monthly until it went downhill. Of course by then cannot find any job liao so taxi driving is a last resort, stop gap, temporary measure to support family and property and living expenses.

    But I learn a lot of how TDs struggle to make ends meet. Yes, it is a tough life but there is the freedom to move about, the chance to talk to friendly people, eavesdrop on juicy gossips, go places in SG you never knew existed before, learn to handle nasty pax, learn to drive safe, discover so many new roads and routes, discover just how big this llittle red dot is actually.

    For me this is a temporary occupation so it is ok. But I feel for those who gave up on life and are resigned to this type of life, especially the OMO TDs.

    Never give up on your dream even if you are now driving a cab. Spare some time to reflect and work on something that will give you a better life. Yes, you can get used to this life, the mind is a funny thing. You can get used to poverty and maybe even love this life, just like you will get used to collecting cardboards when you are old....
