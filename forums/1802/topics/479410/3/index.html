    They do register, but they are not that strict in term of discipline, due lack of audit as listed company requirement, locals taxi companies like TC and premier runs on their own managment policies that can tweat and change easily, and that created double or triple standards, favouritism and so called bo standard system. However, having said that, companies like TC and others that do not hv to answer the public shareholders, they are more nimble and swift in their management, meaning that they can adjust better to the changes in taxi industrials, unlike cdg, they need to hold meetings, AGM and executives committee to approve certain changes, and therefore they are less flexible, more strict on their system and that is why, end up more stringent on their drivers. So, there are pro and cons in how you look at the taxi companies, if you are a law abidding person, straight and no hanky panky type, cdg is good for you, but if you those not bothered by rules and laws, able to curry flavour inorder to work your way thru and dun not mind sub standard or double standard decision of management, then , TC or premier etc suit you.
