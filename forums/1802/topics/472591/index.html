    Start cabs' CBD surcharge earlier

    THE Land Transport Authority (LTA) says the city area surcharge encourages cabs to enter the Central Business District ("Surcharge spurs cabs to enter CBD, says LTA"; last Wednesday).

    Changing the start time of the CBD surcharge from 5pm to 2pm would yield positive results.

    Taxis not major cause of jams

    THE Land Transport Authority says taxis are subject to Electronic Road Pricing (ERP) because they, like all other vehicles, occupy road space and contribute to congestion ("Surcharge spurs cabs to enter CBD, says LTA"; last Wednesday).

    Taxis make up only a small fraction of the total vehicle population. Furthermore, not all taxis ply the road and the same locations at the same time.

    The LTA also says the city area surcharge was implemented as an incentive for cabbies to pick up passengers in the Central Business District. This money does not go to the drivers if it is used to offset the ERP charges.

    Also, residents who live in blocks next to bus lanes have difficulty hailing a taxi during bus lane operation hours as cabbies are afraid of being booked if they stop.

    Can taxis be allowed to share the bus lane, as is the case in some countries?

    Poh Soon Leong
