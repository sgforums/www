    If you drive OMO, start at 4am better, still under 50% fares, no MRT, no bus, many going to AP and open market stall takes cab, some workers/staff also take because they can claim. Once inside the taxis, go all the way for 4hrs to 6 hrs non stop, only for toilet break, there after, you already breakeven the rental and fuel cost and also make some earning. Go for your lunch and hv a nice nap, at home or at carparks. Then later, about 2 to 4pm, chiong again, this time, less taxis on the road, cos first shift one all go lim kopi, top up fuel and wash car liao Newbie first training is street picking and Q for a week, later when more familar with routes, then take call. Must know where is the CBD boundaries, the surcharge timming, the taxi stands and building + Hotel lobbies inside and outside CBD. Go slow first, talk less, focus, learnt, and like my whahahahaha brother getcarter said, every cabby learnt from his experiences on the road, on where to Q, where to pick, slowly he will hv his unique pattern, even morning come out carpark turn left, will alway turn left, unless got pax on the right. Net of $200, no problem as OMO, but not now, maybe after a month or 2, you will be like professional driver liao... but OMO must rest a day or 2, cannot chiong all the way, if so, please give me yr contact nos, I standby for you to sent you to lim chu kang. And finally, for whatever you do, try as much as possible to avoid accident even if you are on the right way, the motto is go out in yr cab, come back in your cab, dun come back taking another cab.
