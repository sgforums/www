    For me, only the M-B taxis are seriously worth riding on !
    Next would be the Opels & Volvo that's about it.
    London Cab maybe the newer version only & the rest, quite simply a pile of junk which i don't find worth my fare !

    For utimate running smoothness, i select the E 300 where the inline-6 cylinder 24-valve diesel sounds not too different from a petrol engine of the same cylinder count & cc when accelerating or crusing !

    The E 250, with its inline-5 cylinder 20-valve or 10-valve (depending on series) both come next in 'refinement' levels.

    The latest E 220 although less 'refined' with only 4-cylinders 16-valves but overall is well compensated with its very extensive technological improvements over the earlier models above such that overall, still a finer class of engine & car than the other taxis here.

    Having taken the Proto Saga & Wira taxis in Malaysia, i find either of those more appealing to take than ANY of the Japanese taxis in Singapore !!
    Their engine is so much quieter & performance as good if not better while suspension ride comfort about same.

In noise, vibration & harshness levels of course you can expect lower levels in these areas compared to most (but not all) diesel taxis around but problem i'll say is the relative fuel inefficiency of these engines compared to diesels. Gas engines driving range is much lesser & more frequent refueling required &/or you need much bigger tanks so eat into lugguage or even passenger seating space & hence comfort & utility level of the taxi is compromised !! Also performance not automatically superior compared to the latest & best diesel cars around.
