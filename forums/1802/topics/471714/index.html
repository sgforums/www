    TITLE: Taxi firm Smart reaches end of the road


    POSTED: 11 Jul 2013 12:16 PM


    Taxi firm Smart will not have its operator licence renewed when it expires on 30 September this year says the Land Transport Authority

    SINGAPORE: Taxi firm Smart will not have its operator licence renewed when it expires on 30 September this year.

    The Land Transport Authority (LTA) in announcing the decision cited several reasons, including the fact that the taxi operator had multiple failures in its taxi Quality of Service standards.

    This includes failing to meet the call booking cater rate standard, accident rate, and first inspection passing rate.

    LTA said its immediate priority is to minimise impact on commuters and to work with Smart to help it meet its obligations to its taxi drivers.

    The taxi firm has assured LTA that it will fulfill its responsibility towards its taxis drivers. Smart added it will assist and secure placements for them with other taxi companies.

    Smart's 268 taxis account for less than one per cent of the total taxi fleet of 27,781 in Singapore.

    LTA said Smart was not able to achieve a minimum fleet size of 800 taxis.

    LTA said it met Smart on July 10 to inform the company of its decision to not renew the licence.

    Smart has assured LTA that it will fulfil its responsibility towards its taxis drivers and to assist and secure placement for them with other taxi companies.

    LTA will work with Smart to provide necessary assistance to facilitate an orderly exit with minimum impact on commuters and drivers.
