SMRT revamps training programme for taxi drivers

  • FireIce
    11 Dec 14, 20:00

    SINGAPORE — SMRT will be revamping its training programme for its taxi drivers, bringing “renewed focus” to three key areas, namely, customer service, safe driving, and proficiency in the use of new technology to maximise opportunities, the transport operator said today (Dec 11).

    The enhanced training will feature a more robust syllabus with practical training sessions. The two-day programme, which will be launched early next year, will be organised along two main sessions — Operational Excellence and Safe Driving. Currently, SMRT taxi drivers are required to attend a one-day customer service and road safety course.

    As part of the operational excellence session, taxi drivers will learn about taxi operations such as the use of taxi equipment and how they can tap onto new technologies including web portals and third party applications. This will be complemented by a customer service module.

    The safe driving session will focus on road safety theory and practical lessons, as well as practical, safe and defensive driving skills.

    “A newer, more comprehensive training package brings greater value to our partners, as they will be better equipped to address the demands of an extremely dynamic industry,” said Mr Benny Lim, managing director of SMRT Roads.

    SMRT also said it is working closely with partners and the authorities to ensure efforts like the two-hirer and relief-driver schemes will help enhance operational standards.
