    want a life in Taxi Driving

    1) Be a relief or get a relief

    2) work on 12 hrs shift

    3) Give yourself a proper rest during this 12 hrs shift, 45mins to an hours of meal break, 15-30 mins of coffee break every 3-4 hours of driving.

    5) Spend, budget your expenses base on the income on 12 hours shift

    Dear TDs Bro’s and Sis’s,

    Being a TD is a tough job like any profession. Do not degrade yourself or feel dis-heartened that this is an inferior profession. We are providing a personalized transport service to commute our client from point A to point B at the best shortest possible route to reach the client’s intended destination.

    It’s a decent job and making an honest living.

    Don’t think that a CEO of a big organization or a high-earner don’t have their problems. They, too, face the same problem.

    There’ll always be black sheep in any profession you’re in. It’s all about balancing our assets and liabilities, ensuring our working capitals are always in the black. It’s also about balancing our time.

    Whether you’re OMO, a Relief or having a relief… as long as you’re comfortable with this arrangement, just be happy with what you’re doing.

     

    Important thing is whether: are you happy with what you’re doing? If no, then what are you doing about it? It’s a personal decision. Once decided, you have to be responsible for this decision.

     

    However, never, never compromise your dignity. Protect and preserve it at all cost. Cheers.
