    Here is the full statement from the Singapore Police Force:
     


    Police have arrested a 40-year-old man for armed robbery in Lim Chu Kang.

    On 18 June 2013 at about 1.45 am, the suspect boarded the victim's taxi along Marina Boulevard, and directed the victim to take him Lim Chu Kang Muslim Cemetery. Upon reaching the stated destination, the suspect pointed a knife at the victim and robbed him of his cash and mobile phone. The victim was then directed to drive to Orchard Rd where the suspect alighted. As soon as it was safe to do so, the victim called the police with the assistance of a fellow taxi driver.

    Police officers attending to the case managed to locate and detained the suspect who was leaving the scene along Orchard Road at about 3.30am, where he was subsequently placed under arrest. The cash and mobile phone belonging to the victim was also recovered from the suspect's possession. .

    The suspect will be produced in Court on 19 June 2013 for an offence of Armed Robbery under Section 392 read with Section 397 of the Penal Code, Chapter 224 which carries an imprisonment for a term which may extend to 14 years.

    Commander of Jurong Police Division, Deputy Assistant Commissioner of Police Wilson Lim reiterated that Police will make all efforts to track down the perpetrators of such crimes and bring them to justice. At the same time, he would like to caution taxi drivers to remain vigilant and wary if passengers asked to be taken to isolated destinations and to alert their call centre.
