    Higher fares but no gain for cabbies

    AS A TransCab driver, I want to clarify that the higher taxi fares do not give drivers extra gains ("Higher flagdown rates for new taxis"; last Tuesday).

    This is because from Nov 1, daily rentals will go up from $114 to $120. With the extra 20 cents per flagdown, we will have to pick up at least 30 trips a day to cover the rental increase. My average for a 12-hour shift is about just 25 trips. This means I will have to cough up more for rental.

    In my 12 years as a taxi driver, I have not encountered an increase in daily rentals for a 1 1/2-year-old car, which has a lower certificate of entitlement premium. Is this fair business practice?

    Orlando H. Scully
