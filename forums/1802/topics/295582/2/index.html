A taxi fare is charged by the distance , not speed or time , you can take a cab from Changi Airport to Tuas , one driver can travel at 160km/h and another can travel at 60/70 km/h , in the end when you reach your destination , your price is going to be the same , taxi fares are charged by its distance , not speeed , if speed that means every taxi driver will drive at 40km/h to earn more money!

    When the driver travels slowly , he will earn less as he is wasting his time , for example , a passenger is now waiting for cab at the destination where you're alighting , then there are 2 taxis , A and B , taxi B travels faster then Taxi A , obviously taxi B will overtake Taxi A and got the passenger. This is why if a taxi driver is travelling slowly , not only he is wasting his time , but he is earning less , in the above example , if Taxi A will to travel faster than B , he would have got that passenger and definitely he is going to earn more.

So most of the time itg is the distance that matters , although traffic jam may make the passenger pay more , as when there is traffic jam , the vehicle is stopped & every 25s of waiting time cost $0.10. So surely traffic jam you pay more , other then that , speed doesn't matters.
