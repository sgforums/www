    Uber drops 'U' from its app icon as it unveils new look and logo

     

     

    Popular ride-hailing app Uber has dropped the "U" from its app icon as it unveiled a new look and logo early on Wednesday (Feb 3).

    The new designs were inspired by "the story of bits and atoms", respectively the building blocks of technology and the world, Uber's chief executive and co-founder Travis Kalanick wrote in a press release on Uber's website.

    Mr Kalanick was personally involved in much of the rebranding, Wired reported. Forcing himself to learn about design concepts, Mr Kalanick told Wired: "I didn't know any of this stuff. I just knew it was important, and so I wanted it to be good."

    At "the heart" of the new logos for the rider and partner apps is the "bit"- essentially a square - with shapes and patterns surrounding it. The old logo was "somewhat distant and cold", Mr Kalanick said.

     

    The new Uber look will also include colours and patterns tailored after the different cities and countries the service operates in.

    "Every city has its own character and our long term goal is to have unique designs for cities as well as countries. This will mean adding hundreds more colour palettes and patterns over time," Mr Kalanick wrote in his press release.

    Uber currently operates in 68 countries, and transports food and packages in certain countries, as well as people.

    1. Ditch the cash

    This should be the first feature on Grab you should be using.

    Cashless payments make travelling so much easier — not only can you just step out of the car pretending to have your own personal driver like a real boss, you never have to worry about having enough cash or fiddling with NETS machines again.

    Just key in your credit card details into the payment settings, and you’re all set.

    2. Get a ride in a Flash

    If you’re not picky about your kind of ride, casting a wider net will help get you a ride faster.

    With Grab’s Flash button, you’ll be able to reach out to any of the 200,000 drivers across the region to get the one closest to you. Just key in your destination, and tap the blue lightning bolt above the “Next” button.

    3. Save your frequent routes

    Saving your frequent routes in Grab will make booking your trips in the future much faster.

    Go into the side tab and into your history of trips, and save your favourites by tapping on the star next to it.

    This will be saved, along with the payment option and service type used then, into your favourites menu on the left, and you can book that ride the next time with a single tap. 

    4. Customise your own location

    If you’re in some awkward remote spot in Singapore that Grab just doesn’t have a named location for, take matters into your own hands and customise your own location.

    Tap on either location bars, and tap the map icon on the bottom right-hand corner to pull the green pin to the exact spot on the map that you want. Grab will auto-suggest the name of the location for you and get transport to you, wherever you are.  


    5. Separate work and play

    Anyone who files expenses for cab rides with their company knows that awkward moment where you visit the Finance department with crumpled slips of paper at the end of the month.

    Instead of dealing with individual receipts, start separating your personal rides from your business trips. You can do so by labelling rides under “Business”, either while waiting for the ride to arrive or in your history tab.

    All business rides will be consolidated in the Grab Passenger Hub, where you can key in your company info and particulars, and even download a neat little spreadsheet that Finance will go crazy for. 


    6. Be a Social Rider

    Want to keep your friends or colleagues updated on your trip and your ETA? 

    Send them your ride directly so they’ll stop asking you what time you’re going to arrive. You can do so through Facebook or Whatsapp, and they’ll be able to track their ride for you at every step. 


    7. Referral Discounts

    If you have a friend who has never used the app, use your app to send them an invitation — if they start using the app, both of you will get $8 off your next ride, making it a real sweet deal for everyone.


    8. Make a booking in advance

    If you need a cab to drive you to the airport or to a special event that you cannot afford to be late to, book it in advance.

    While in the process of booking your ride, select Pick Up Later and set a time — your app will send you a notification once a cab has chosen the job. Just don’t be late, or the taxi uncle may give you a real earful.

     

    9. Keep your eye on the deals

    Sure, they may spam you with updates and news, but don’t avoid Grab’s notifications inbox completely or you may miss the deals they’ll send from time to time.

    Just check into the notifications on the left-hand side menu, and you may find deals like this sweet one for Grabcar Premium. Stay true to your deal-loving Singapore roots.
