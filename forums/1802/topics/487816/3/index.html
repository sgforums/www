    Man screams, hops into taxi and leaves door open when cabby tries to drive away after dispute in Chinatown

     

    A passenger was spotted shouting and standing on the door of a taxi after a dispute with a cabby at Chinatown. He even hopped into the cab and left door open when the cabby tried to drive away.

    The incident happened at around 8pm yesterday (Jun 3).

    According to Stomper A, the passenger clad in a striped shirt refused to allow the taxi driver to continue with his journey and stood on the vehicles' door to deter him from leaving.

    In a phone interview with Stomp, A said:

    "He was very aggressive and kept shouting.

    "I suspect that they were fighting over one of the two things.

    "The first being the cab fare and the second, they might have been arguing about the drop-off location."

    He added that he was not sure of the reason for the dispute but said that he heard the man questioning "Is this what Singaporeans are..."

    A mentioned that he did not catch the later part of what the angry passenger had said.

    But he told Stomp that the passenger kept screaming even while he was talking on the phone.

    "The poor taxi driver came our of the car multiple times and tried to talk to his friend (the man in the white shirt), but nothing happened.

    "The driver then went back to his vehicle."

    The commotion only ended when the angry passenger was engrossed with his phone and moved away from the cab for a moment.

    The Stomper said:

    "The taxi driver saw that he was distracted and went into the cab to drive away."
