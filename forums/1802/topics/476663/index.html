    TAXI companies do not address the needs of customers. Instead they are focused on meeting the demands of cabbies, who do not place emphasis on customer service.

    In the past, when cabbies owned their vehicles instead of renting them from taxi operators, they worked hard to pay off their bills.

    Due to high car prices now, some taxi divers use their vehicles for private purposes after meeting the minimum service requirements.

    Also, there is a need to raise the professionalism of cabbies, promote driving a taxi as a worthwhile vocation, and open up the job market to allow more competition.

    If the Land Transport Authority (LTA) is serious about limiting the growth of the car population, then it needs to work out an alternative transport system for the upper middle-class group, who would otherwise continue to buy cars and push up prices further.

    The LTA will need to regulate fares and raise service standards if it considers taxis a form of public transport.

    The taxi operating system requires a total overhaul. The objective is to make the service more customer-centric and part of the public transport system.

    Self-employment incentives for drivers could be considered, instead of the present system of having cabbies rent their vehicles. One way is to allow privately own taxis.
