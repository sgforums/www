    I think you are wrong.  Let's review the learned judge's considerations in sentencing the accused:

    1. I did not believe the Accused’s account of Leow’s conduct in the taxi. It is public knowledge that service quality is an essential aspect of public transport providers’ duties. As Leow said, taxi companies investigate every complaint made against taxi drivers. It is difficult to believe that Leow would respond to the Accused’s very polite request in such a shockingly rude and crass manner and drive as dangerously as described by the Accused. Also, it did not make sense that Leow would work (driving taxi) from late at the night to the next morning, drive the Accused almost to his destination, then tell the Accused to get off his taxi and forego his fare.

    2. Leow was a taxi driver and his taxi was parked at the road junction, it would be sheer foolishness for him to rob the Accused when Leow could be easily traced. And this incident took place at the brightly lit road junction with Leow screaming loudly. It was unimaginable that Leow would commit a crime at a brightly lit road junction and at the same time, screamed to attract attention.

    3. When a person takes a ride in a taxi and gets off without paying his fare, and the taxi driver chases after him and stops him from walking away, that person must know or at the very least, sense, that the taxi driver wants his fare.

    4. The Accused did not intend to pay the taxi fare and in the process of trying to get away, assaulted Leow.

    5.  Leow was a public transport worker. As he said, he was merely trying to earn a living, he did not deserve to be beaten. I fully agree with this. Violence against public transport workers cannot be condoned. They should be able to work in a safe environment and be protected from devious and violent passengers.

    6. The need to take a serious view of assaults on public transport providers was highlighted in Wong Hoi Len v PP [2009] 1 SLR(R) 115 where Justice VK Rajah set the sentencing benchmark of around 4 weeks’ jail for a simple assault against a public transport worker where the offender had no antecedents and pleaded guilty.

    7. I had no doubt at all in deciding that a custodial sentence was warranted.

    In fact, it had everything to do with TD, being a public service/transport provider and must be protected from unprovoked assaults under the law.
