    SMART automobile has recently included 5 brand new SKODAs into its modest fleet of 500 vehicles, bucking an industry of Toyota Crowns, Nissan Cedrics, and Mercedes.

    the 5 new vehicles, Skoda da Superbs, took delivery yesterday from the local agent for the Volkswagen-owned brand, which is Czech Auto.

    Said Mr Johnny Harjantho, managing director of SMART Automobiles:
    "The Superbs will be marketed as a premium service, such as Airport Limousine.

    The Superbs, which boosts lots of legroom can also be flagged down at a rate of $3.00 (a premium of 60 cents over the normal cabs).

    We will start with 5 (Superbs), and if they prove to be reliabvle and popular, we will buy more."

    Cabbies who drives the Skodas will pay a daily rental fee of $98 (10% more than their toyota counterparts).
    The Skodas are equipped with Euro-IV emmission standards, which qualifies from a sizeable tax break. The 5 Superbs has a 2.5 Litre turbo diesel V6 engine and costs more than $70,000 (marginally more than the 3L toyota crown). Mercedes Benz cabs costs around $110,000.

    SMART will also start to try out a Mercedes-Benz bi-fuel. This merc is capable of using petrol and CNG..

    the 5 new Skoda da Superbs reg. no. range:
    SHB9002E, SHB9003C, SHB9004A, SHB9005K, SHB9006U. all uses the electronic front display (found on Comfort cabs as well).
    The 5 Skoda Superbs are registered on 04/08/2005 and will retire on 03/08/2013



    (05 August 2005 The Straits Times HOME page H6)
