    I mean.....hand back to the company directly (STTA)?
    STTA, as in the Association, is something like a union. STTA drivers keep all their fares themselves. They pay for their vehicle, maintainence etc. No replacement cabs if its sent for repairs etc.

    STTA merely help new drivers get new vehicles, settle administrative matters. The existance of an office is almost zero, and those 2 telephone numbers are some clues to their presence.

    STTA, you probably wont find their information in books or websites. The only clue you get is "Singapore has 7 taxi companies, and a small number of private owners...."

    SINGAPORE TAXI TRANSPORT ASSOCIATION (STTA)
    STTA is a private cab company. The drivers are the owners of their respective vehicles.
    Customer Service Hotline: 6337 5117, 6293 5545
    (Fares: $2.40 at flagdown. 10cents for every 200-225m. 50% midnight surcharge from 12am to 6am. All other common surcharges by other cab companies such as Public Holiday, CBD, ERP, Peak, Airport applies. No bookings available.)
