    Same, cos it is also one turn, However, big wheel with a larger perimeter will in one turn move further than smaller wheel, but time and power are also affecting the rotation, becos if given the same power, it takes longer time for big wheel to rotate one round than smaller, but if you put more power on big wheel than smaller so that both rotate at the same time, bigger wheel will consume more fuel. Eg a tipper truck with 45inch big wheel and a taxi with 17 inch wheel, given the same power on the pedal, the taxi would had completed one round in X timming, truck would still be turning as it takes longer time to turn resulted in X+x time. After both completed one turn, truck will be further from taxi, as bigger wheel perimeter is longer So, for the truck to cope up with the same timming as taxi, the truck will then have to depress the fuel pedal more to create more power As for the taxi, to compensate for the distance lost to big wheel truck, the taxi must rotate more than one round, therefore we can said that Distance= perimeter of wheel/power x time
