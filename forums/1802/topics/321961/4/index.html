    I use a GPS unit when i drive, as i am not totally confident esp when it comes to the newer housing estates. However i do not depend entirely on it. I will still ask passenger for preferred route, and use the GPS as a supplementary guide. Before a major turn, I will ask passenger to confirm if its correct. Even if the pax is wrong and the GPS actually shows a better route, i do not question the pax; i will just follow the instructions.

    I do not need to sit still and key in destination once the pax comes onboard. Proceed in the general direction first, then find an opportunity to do keying in when stopped at traffic lights etc. Its impt to know ur GPS's functions well so you wont fumble. Takes me less than 10secs usually.

    If kenna "Seraya something" situation, just key in Seraya and all the various possibilities will appear, with the region (eg Jurong, Thomson, Katong) and you can then confirm with the pax which one is the intended destination.

    It also comes into good use when the pax has no idea where the place is, but only have the address and expects you to take him there. And its an obscure road that you have not heard of. GPS is priceless in that kinda situation.

    Of cos its impt to have a general feel of Spore roads, and have a good directional sense. If not, no GPS can help, sure kenna LRC from pax. Also impt to use a reliable GPS unit with a good and updated map. For me its a Holux with Mapking's SLA maps.
