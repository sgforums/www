    Originally posted by nick34:

    Must keep 12months free from dp then get back ... so answer is yes ... suspended 


    Correct.  The licence will be suspended.

    A lot of ppl mistakenly believe that as long as they have not hit 24 demerit points 2 years after their first offence, their total points will automatically be erased and reset to zero.

    So, in the test question example, some may think that after 5 Jan 2012 which is 24 months after the first offence, the total 22 points accumulated would be reset to zero.  Wrong.  Because from 10 Dec 2010 until 5 Mar 2012, which is less than 24 months, the offender already accumulated 24 points.

    But you rightly pointed out that there is an incentive.  If you do not get any demerit points within any consecutive 12 month period, all your previous points will be erased and you start from zero again.

    So, no need to wait 24 months.  As long as for the next 12 months, no demerit points, then all previous points are erased.

    By Christopher Tan Senior Transport Correspondent

     

    Motorists who install video-recording devices in their vehicles could get discounts on their motor insurance premiums.

    This possible incentive is being studied by a working group formed by the Traffic Police and the General Insurance Association (GIA).

    Both see the move as a way to encourage better behaviour on the road, Traffic Police Commander Cheang Keng Keong told The Straits Times this week.

    Improving driver behaviour, he said, was key to lowering accident rates.

     

    Background story

    Moves to make S'pore roads safer  

    Under consideration:

    • Insurance premium discounts for installing in-vehicle camera
    • Shortening demerit-free period for motorists to get insurance rebates

    Measures the Traffic Police plan to implement:

    • Make heavy vehicles found speeding undergo inspections more often
    • Beef up enforcement - more summonses issued, more officers to be put on patrol, more digital cameras to be installed
