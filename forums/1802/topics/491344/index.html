    The current situation is such that taxi drivers are Comfort's customers.   Taxi customers are taxi drivers customers.   But call-book customers are also Comfort's customers.

    From a money politics stand-point, a call-book customer only provides comfort with a max of $0.80 per day wheras we pay Comfort at least $100 per day for rental (and additional at least $7 to government for GST).  If you look at Comfort's annual reports, you would find that the most lucrative division of Comfort is the taxi business and the bulk of it comes from taxi renting to self-employed taxi drivers (and not from the call-booking division unlike that of Grab organization).  It works out because most of the business-minded taxi drivers work super-long hours every week (a lot more than the 44 hours per week that employees work) and all the taxi-drivers continue to pay rents even on Sunday, public holidays and other days like sick days and holidays (even when we go on holidays, we have to reduce the rents to find super-reliefs and effectively making a loss while Comfort continues to make in full amount).

    As per agreement, we only have to take 2-3 call-booking customers per day to support the call-booking division.  Many night-shift drivers do not even take a single call-booking as there are hardly any calls at night.  So when some taxi drivers do not take the calls, other taxi drivers benefit more.  So at the end of the year, the 30 million objective is met so no one in the call center gets retrenched, the company also makes from call-booking business and therefore offers levy-free call-books to taxi drivers.
