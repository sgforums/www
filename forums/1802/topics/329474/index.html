Cabbie collapses at wheel

  • QX179R
    4 Sep 08, 12:26

    A TAXI driver with a newly-arrived tourist in his back seat collapsed at the wheel on Tuesday morning and crashed into a median at Changi Airport.

    Cabbie Kwan Lok Pang was rushed to hospital but was pronounced dead on arrival.

    Police said his passenger, a Chinese national who had just jumped in at Terminal 1, was unscathed.

    It is not known what caused the 54-year-old to collapse, and his family members on Wednesday declined to say whether he had heart problems.

    Mr Kwan had been working for Premier Taxis for three years, said the company's managing director, Lim Chong Boo.

    Mr Lim said the cabbie 'looked healthy' and, like all taxi drivers between 50 and 65, would have undergone a medical check-up once every two years.

    Collapses like Mr Kwan's are uncommon, according to taxi companies. Premier said this its first, while SMRT Taxis said it has never had such a case. ComfortDelgro, the country's biggest cab company, declined to provide statistics.

    Police said Mr Kwan was approaching Terminal 2 on Tuesday morning when passed out and slumped over the wheel. His car veered towards the right before smashing into the centre divider.

    Mr Kwan suffered scrapes on his forehead and was unconscious when paramedics arrived. It is unclear if he was killed by the impact of the crash.

    On Wednesday, family and friends gathered for his wake in Jalan Teck Whye. His wife and adult children said they were too distraught to be interviewed.

    His younger sister, however, said Mr Kwan had always worked in the transport industry. Prior driving a taxi, he owned a transport company that went under.

    Premier Taxi's Mr Lim said his death was unfortunate.

    'We will do our utmost to help Mr Kwan's family,' he said.
