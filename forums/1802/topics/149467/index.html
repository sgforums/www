    There were fewer accidents involving taxis in the third quarter of last year compared with the quarter before.

    The Land Transport Authority, which rates the cabs according to set standards, said the rate fell from 2.3 in the second quarter to 1.7 accidents per 10 million kilometres between July and September last year.

    In spite of this, three taxi companies - SMRT Taxis, Trans-Cab Services and Premier Taxis - still failed to meet the standard.

    Comfort and CityCab taxi companies continue to have the lowest accident rate.

    The taxis are also rated in terms of service quality.

    While most companies were up to the mark, Trans-Cab, Smart Automobile and Premier Taxis could not meet the standard for Call Answer Rate.

    Financial penalties are imposed on taxi companies that fail to meet the standard in consecutive months, and these three companies will be fined between $2,500 and $7,800.

    The standards were introduced in June 2003 to improve taxi services for commuters.
