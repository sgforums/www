    Some disturbing trends on the road

    Â 
    Published on Oct 09, 2013

    I HAVE observed some disturbing trends over the past few months.

    First, some taxi drivers either ignore passengers trying to flag down their cabs, or refuse to ply certain routes. This has become more frequent and it takes at least three or four attempts before I can find a cabby willing to take me to where I need to go.

    It is not as if I travel to far-flung corners of the island. I live in the Meyer Road area and, on most days, travel either to the city or to Marine Parade.

    Second, some taxi drivers wait for on-call jobs in the side lanes around Meyer Road and refuse to accept flag-down requests. The moment one walks towards these cabs, the drivers switch their signs to "busy".

    I am sure this practice is not uncommon.

    Third, a growing number of drivers do not stop at pedestrian crossings when they see people waiting to cross the road.

    I have observed this at the junction of Mountbatten Road and Fort Road when I walk my child to school every morning.

    This is an alarming trend. In most cases, the culprits are drivers of private cars. And even when they stop, their vehicles keep inching forward in a threatening manner while pedestrians are crossing the road. This has got to stop.

    Ramesh Narayanaswami
