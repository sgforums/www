    Woman refuses to let taxi move off after cabby picks up passenger who cut queue

    A woman stood rooted in front of a cab at the taxi stand near Bishan MRT station for 20 minutes on Friday (May 31) in a bid to force out the passenger inside who had cut the queue she was in, reported STOMPer Karanjit.

    According to the STOMPer, the woman was in front of him in a long queue waiting for taxis, and was infuriated by the actions of the queue-cutter.

    In a video sent in by the STOMPer, the two women can be heard engaging in a screaming match, with the former shifting herself to block the taxi when the cabby tried to reverse and move off.

    In his report, STOMPer Karanjit wrote:

    "I was waiting for a cab at the taxi stand near Bishan MRT station at around 6.30pm yesterday (May 31).

    "There was a long queue of people waiting for a taxi when suddenly, this woman just cut the queue and boarded a cab that came in.

    "A lady in front of me in the queue got furious at the sight of that and went to stand in front of the taxi to prevent the cab from moving off.

    "The woman who cut the taxi queue then stuck her head of the vehicle to explain that she needed to go off as her son was sick.

    "However, the lady in front of me was not buying it and told her she's not going anywhere and that she should get out of the cab.

    "The cabby tried to inch away from the lady but she stood her ground, and told him that she will report him to his company if he moved off.

    "This went on for about 20 minutes at least when I got on a cab that came and left the area."
