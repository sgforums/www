    A complaint in today's ST:

    "With taxi fares about to rise, I thought it's appropriate I share my daily experience with taxis.
    I leave my office at around 4.45pm. At this hour, it's almost impossible to get a cab. There are many empty taxis, but none is willing to stop for fares.

    Most of the cabbies can be found waiting in the car parks. Some wait at the roadside till about 5.05pm before picking up fares.

    Taxi drivers admit that they wait for the peak hour surcharge to earn the extra dollar. Some admit that they would cruise around to wait for phone bookings because the $4 booking fee, on top of the peak hour and CBD surcharges, would cover their trip back to town.

    A few weeks back, I had an unpleasant experience. It rained. Many empty taxis passed me by. They filtered to the outer lane when I waved for them to stop.

    I tried to board an empty taxi when the traffic lights turned red but the driver locked the door and showed me the "On call" sign. I was sure he meant that I should book a cab instead. He wasn't on call because the cab's sign didn't show he was "Busy" or "On call".

    Taxi drivers claim that their earnings are insufficient but from the way they choose their passengers, it doesn't seem like it. I am sure a cabby who is not earning enough wants to waste time cruising around to wait for phone bookings.

    I hope the Land Transport Authority and taxi companies look into this matter as I'm sure my experience is familiar to others as well."

    Keith Gerard Tan

    As more complaints build up, pressure will be placed on taxi companies to clean up their act.
