    There is a significantly large number of TDs over 50 years old and therefore the general perception that it is a job for older people.  Many of these TDs are also less educated thereby enforcing the perception that it is the only job that they can do besides being security guards and cleaners.

    However, there is an increasing trend of younger people, those in their 30s, signing up as TDs. They are generally better educated.  Therefore, is taxi driving a career or just a job for these people?  It depends on how you look at it. But, you must be prepared to work hard.

    If I am 30 years old and a taxi driver, how can I improve my income? Well, besides having a taxi driver licence, I will also get my Tourist Guide Licence and work on a freelance basis.  These two jobs can go hand in hand.

    I pick up tourists and offer to take them sightseeing and a tour of Singapore.  They can book me for half a day or a whole day, depending on their needs.  I use my taxi to ferry them around and take them to places of interests or see the real Singapore where standard tours will not take them.  It is a very personalised service.  Some people just do not like to take group tours, where you have fixed timings, fixed places and they almost always take you to souvenir shops to earn their commission.

    If I am ambitious enough, I will also be a Property Agent.  In between lull periods or on weekends, I can bring my clients in my taxi to view houses.

    You are therefore a TD, a tour guide and possibly a property agent, all in one. Your taxi enables you to be mobile and do jobs that require such mobility.  Then, you can earn $7,000 a month.
