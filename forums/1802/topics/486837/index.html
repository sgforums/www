    Comfort waives call levy for cabbies

     
    By Christopher Tan Senior Transport Correspondent
     

    TAXI giant ComfortDelGro Corp is waiving call-booking levies for its cabbies for a week this month.

    This means Comfort and CityCab cabbies will earn 30 cents more for current bookings, and $1 extra for advance bookings during the waiver period from Nov 11 to 17.

    The move is expected to cost the listed company around $350,000 in revenue.

    Company spokesman Tammy Tan said the waiver is "a gesture of appreciation to all our drivers for helping us achieve over nine million successful booking jobs in the last quarter". The figure is a record for Singapore's biggest taxi operator, which controls 16,800 out of 28,000 cabs plying the roads here.

    But industry players said the move is also in response to competition posed by taxi apps that are elbowing their way into the lucrative taxi booking market.

    Comfort cabby Tony Pang, 64, described the waiver as "good and encouraging".

    "But maybe it is also to discourage drivers from using Uber and GrabTaxi," he added, referring to two of four taxi apps present here.

    These apps have been chipping away at booking revenue of taxi operators in the last two years or so.

    GrabTaxi, for instance, has seen its booking volume grow a hundredfold since it was launched a year ago, to an estimated 40,000 bookings a month. And now, Uber is giving away mobile phones to cabbies who sign up with it.

    Meanwhile, commuters are wondering why ComfortDelGro's goodwill gesture is not extended to them as well.

    Regular cab commuter Lynn Chua, 42, said: "I book cabs all the time. It would have been nice for Comfort to give loyal customers a booking discount."

    The financial consultant added: "But I suppose that would jack up booking volume, and it might not be able to cope with that."

    Ms Tan of ComfortDelGro said the levy waiver for drivers will benefit commuters indirectly as "drivers are incentivised to take on more booking jobs".
