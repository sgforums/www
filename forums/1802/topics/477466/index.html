Mr Tan told MyPaper he introduced the reward feature because he realised that "passengers are required to pay a booking fee when booking a taxi using other means, taking the decision to pay this amount out of their hands". "What we want is to allow a passenger to dictate when he wants to pay more for a cab...or save on his booking fee if there's a willing driver who wants to take the job." Public-relations manager Vernia Lim, 27, welcomed the idea of a reward system. She often has trouble getting a cab in the Central Business District, especially during peak hours and on rainy days.
