    ComfortDelGro taxis to install in-vehicle cameras

     

    The J002B cameras are equipped with a 140 focus lens and a 2.7” LCD screen. (Photo: ComfortDelGro)

    SINGAPORE: ComfortDelGro is fitting its fleet of 16,600 taxis with an in-vehicle camera, starting from Monday.

     

    The exercise is expected to be completed by the first quarter of 2014. The cost of installing the cameras and its maintenance is to be borne by the company.

     

    In a statement on Friday, the company said the in-vehicle camera is being installed as an added safety and security measure.

     

    Placed behind the rear view mirror, the cameras are programmed to start recording once the engine is turned on and will do so continuously for up to four hours.

     

    Its sensor is also able to activate one-minute video recordings the moment it detects sudden shocks to the taxi.

     

    ComfortDelGro said it ran a trial with the camera in June this year and it has received positive feedback from its drivers.
