    SINGAPORE: The Land Transport Authority (LTA) on Tuesday (Mar 8) announced it will extend the operating hours of Full-Day Bus Lanes by three hours. 

    From Mar 21, the lanes will operate from 7.30am to 11pm, the LTA said in its press release. Previously, the operating hours ended at 8pm.

    The LTA said this will help shorten travel times for commuters during late evening periods.

    There are 23 kilometres of such lanes which operate on weekdays and Saturdays, excluding public holidays, and they are mainly located in the city centre. 


    Separately, the agency said it will implement the Bus Priority Box, also known as the Mandatory Give Way to Buses scheme, at two bus stops along Orchard Road. This is part of a six-month trial to evaluate its usefulness at bus stops without bus bays, it said. 

    The bus stops are opposite Mandarin Orchard and Midpoint Orchard. There are currently 332 bus stops with Bus Priority Boxes since it was first introduced in 2008, LTA said.

    It added that it will use the findings of this six-month trial to determine if the scheme should be applied for similar bus stops in other locations.

    SINGAPORE - The operating hours of full-day bus lanes - those marked in red - will be extended to 11pm from March 21, up from 8pm now.

    The Land Transport Authority (LTA) said on Tuesday (March 8) that there are 23km of full-day bus lanes which operate from 7.30am to 8pm on weekdays and Saturdays. They are located mainly in the city centre.

    Separately, two bus stops along Orchard Road will be included in the Mandatory Give Way to Buses scheme from March 21. They are the first set of bus stops without bus bays in the city that will have this priority scheme, in which motorists have to give way to buses pulling out of bus stops.

    Illustration of Bus Priority Box at a bus stop without a bus bay. SOURCE: LTA

     

    One is opposite Mandarin Orchard (bus stop number 09037), and the other is at Midpoint Orchard (bus stop number 09038).

    The LTA will determine within six months of implementation the usefulness of this scheme at bus stops without bus bays and whether it should be applied for similar bus stops in other locations.

    Since 2008, the Mandatory Give Way to Buses scheme has been rolled out to 332 bus stops.
