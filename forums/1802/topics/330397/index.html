    Our MDT don't just serve us on providing bookings . More than that we gained from the MDT . First very important gain , we have a GPS system to locate us in case we are robbed , murder or kena any kind of mishaps. Security ! As a kind of S.O.S Second , we need the stuff for NETS and the rest of the cashless payment . Thirdly , it consolidated our daily earnings , itemised by trips , timings and distance . In case we received complaints against us , we have our daily datas to provide as evidence . Fourth , everytime we pick a customer from anywhere , our system capture our pick up point and alighting locations , so there is no dispute after those complaints . Fifth , it's the fastest way to interact lost and found between drivers and customers. Sixth , of course bookings , both current and advance .

    My MDT has been reliable so far. Only a couple of times when I needed to reset cos no GPS coverage.

    My main gripe is the very slow scrolling on maps. It'd be nice to have a feature like the one in Nokia Maps that suggests routes from place to place. Since our cab's position is already known, a computed shortest route to a destination which we key in should be possible. My handphone does not have GPS, so I need to specify start point and end point before the phone searches online for a possible route, with an alternative route option.

    I don't need a real-time GPS navigator, just the route. REal useful for newbies like me. And would definitely help reduce customer complaints on cabbies taking a longer-than-necessary route.

    A bigger touchscreen for the MDT would be nice too, big enough to see details without taking your eyes off the road too long. Perhaps 50% bigger than the current size should suffice. With some touch feedback when we key in alpha-numero keys.

    My LCD is difficult to read in bright sunlight. I'm sure there're better LCD's out there that don't have a problem with this.

    And would it hurt to install a simple little game for a cabbie waiting in a long queue?
