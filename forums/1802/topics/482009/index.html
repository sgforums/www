    SINGAPORE: The National Taxi Association is working with cab operators to enhance taxi co-sharing -- where more than one driver uses a cab, and take turns in plying the roads.

    Both will look into formalising hiring arrangements, where two drivers will be assigned to a new vehicle.

    Some 250 taxi drivers took part in a Share-a-Taxi workshop on Saturday, meant to help them network and deal with the challenges of finding relief drivers.

    The need for relief drivers has become more pressing as taxis meet higher availability standards to ensure more cabs are on the road serving commuters.

    The association is also in talks with cab operators to bring the co-sharing concept further, through a formal agreement between the hirer and relief driver, to clarify and protect each other's roles and responsibilities.

    Mr Ang Hin Kee, advisor to the National Taxi Association, said: "The concept is to make every taxi work harder, not every taxi driver. As it is today, the taxi driver drives about 12 hours a day over the six to seven days a week. So having more than one driver manage a taxi - it's easier and safer, and a smarter way." 

    - CNA/de
