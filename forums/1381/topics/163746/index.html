    Your question is with regards to the doctrine of the church in the area of marrying non-Christian.

    1. Some Christians subscribe to the belief that this means that you and your gf is "unequally yoked"
    2. Most churches will NOT conduct the wedding if the gf is NOT willing to convert to Christianity.
    3. You need to ask yourself your personal stand with regards to point 1.
    4. If you feel strongly that being "unequally yoked" is a critical teaching, then it will lead to point 2 because you will want a church wedding. If....
    5 You don't feel that being "unequally yoked" is important than you may not need the church wedding - therefore points 1 and 2, above, does not matter.

    Up to this point, it's easy - everything hinges on how YOU feel as a Christian. I will not give you my opinion on the "unequally yoked" issue. It's your personal conviction.

    After that, things may not be so clear cut. Meaning, what do you do if you want to worship God on sabbath (note that worshipping God is not an option, really). Will your wife follow you, will it cause a certain level of dissonance in the family, will it eventually lead you AWAY from God? This are just some of the more obvious questions that needed answering.

    On the flip side, I have seen non-Christian spouses coming to Christ because of the faith of the Christian spouse.
