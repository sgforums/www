    The general premise of the book is based on a certain amount of "fact": Opus Dei does exist, Priory of Sion is rumoured to exist etc. Dan Brown attempts to weave his interpretation of such facts into a conspiracy theory. He did make quite a few errors in his book though. Apparently the number of tiles in the Lourve is not 666 as he claimed and neither is the ratio of the queen bee to worker bees equals to the number of Pi (or something like that). The description of the security system of the Lourve is also rather presumptous at best.

    This book should be read as what it's classification in the bookshop says it is:
    Fiction. Nothing more nothing less. Don't loose sleep over it. Dan Brown's other books (Angels and Demons, Deception Point and Digital Fortress) are actually more ludicrous and rather disappointing.

    Wait for the movie supposedly starring Russell Crowe and Kate Beckinsale.
