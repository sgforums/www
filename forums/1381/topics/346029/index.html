    MDA flags Chick Publications to book importers

    Todayonline.com Weekend • January 31, 2009

    THE Media Development Authority (MDA) has notified book importers to consult the authority on material by “Chick Publications” before putting them up for sale or distribution.

    The American firm was in the spotlight recently, when it was revealed in a court hearing that objectionable comic books were sold at a popular Christian bookstore, Tecman, at Bras BasahComplex.

    In response to queries from Today, MDA said it had not received complaints about such material being sold at Tecman or other bookshops prior to the court hearing. But a raid on Wednesday of Tecman found the comics on the store’s shelves. MDA is investigating.

    Besides industry self-regulation, MDA is encouraging input from members of the public. “Such feedback is important and useful as it helps us to monitor the situation and take appropriate action,” added MDA.

    Close to 2 million publications are imported into Singapore each year. To facilitate clearance, MDA said it has a Registered Importers Scheme where publication importers are expected to self-regulate based on a set of content guidelines provided by the authority.

    “Importers are periodically briefed to familiarise themselves with the guidelines and can refer publications to MDA when in doubt,” it added.

    Meanwhile, Ong Kian Cheong — who is accused of distributing comic books critical of Islam — testified at his trial that the objectionable comic tracts were sold for many years in bookstores here.

    He and his wife, Dorothy Chan Hien Leng, have been charged with distributing seditious publications, objectionable publications and possessing seditious publications. The hearing continues. Leong Wee Keat
