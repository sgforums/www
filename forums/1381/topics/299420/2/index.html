    Read carefully at what God instructed him, what he could do and what he could not do.

    Symbol of the tree
    The only 'out of bounds' marker is the tree of knowledge of good and evil. God instructed Adam not to eat of it, the tree is a symbol of a freewill ability to choose between obedience/disobedience.

    The tree is also a symbol of the tithe, that everything was given to man, except for a small part to honour and remember God, failing which, will be trespassing on the sovereignity of God.

    'Death'
    'Death' here meant different deaths. Adam and Eve experienced this instantly, as said in the bible, their minds were darkened.

    Consider this, before eating of the tree, they were given authority and had great power over the earth, they were afraid of nothing. It was their minds that enabled them to do that, and instantly a part of their minds died.

    Now they have to live in fear, and needed clothing and protection, which they previously did not need.

    Then they also experienced spiritual death, their relationship with God and the ability so see and talk to a Holy God was broken - spiritual death.

    Eventually, they also experience physical death. They were made immortal, but now they are suddenly prone to sickness, disease, pain and will eventually die.

    Question
    So was it unfair to Adam? I don't think so. He was already warned and told exactly what would happen, God didn't deceive him into sinning, he already knew his out-of-bounds place.

    The half-truth
    The devil told a half true, that many people even today read and take it as truth.

    What the devil did, was to take what God had spoken, then insert a doubt "did God really say you would die? No you wouldn't...."

    Here the devil lied that they wouldn't die, which was not true, they will die - spiritually, mentally, physically. So its a lie.

    Will they become like God? No. This is a lie from the devil again. This knowledge of good and evil was the corruption in their mind, such that the knowledge of how to sin would enter them.

    Prior to this, Adam and Even were innocent, and they did not know how to sin and what sin was.

    So another argument was since they did not know how to sin, then they didn't sin when they eat of the fruit. But you fail to remember that the only thing God had given them a choice before that was obedience vs. disobedience. Therefore before eating of the fruit, they knew only one sin not to commit , which is not to disobey.

    Therefore Adam still sinned, and disobeyed, because he broke the only ONE law that he was supposed to keep.
