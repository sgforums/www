    There's a theory that the idea of god punishing descendants is actually due to biblical writers trying to explain natural disasters and conquests by other nations.

    One of the 'innovations' that allowed the Yahweh cult to survive was the idea of that God would punish his own people for their sins and the idea of delayed punishment.

    A religion would typically die out of a people was conquered by another. The defeated people would generally adopt the religion of the conquerors. However, the Yahweh cult made defeat by another nation a punishment from god, and part of god's plan, thereby making defeat not a defeat at all.

    Now that defeat becomes the result of god's wrath, the biblical writers had to explain why 'good' kings for example experienced defeats and natural disasters while some 'bad' kings had long, trouble free reigns. So they explained it by saying, "oh well, THIS king is good but look at the last king, he was terrible. So now god is punishing us. It's not this king's fault, it's that last terrible king."
