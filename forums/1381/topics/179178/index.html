    Matthew Henry's commentaries on
    Revelations 20:11-15 After the events just foretold, the end will speedily come; and there is no mention of any thing else, before the appearing of Christ to judge the world. This will be the great day: the Judge, the Lord Jesus Christ, will then put on majesty and terror. The persons to be judged are the dead, small and great; young and old, low and high, poor and rich. None are so mean, but they have some talents to account for; and none so great, as to avoid having to account for them. Not only those alive at the coming of Christ, but all the dead. There is a book of remembrance both for good and bad: and the book of the sinner's conscience, though formerly secret, will then be opened. Every man will recollect all his past actions, though he had long forgotten many of them. Another book shall be opened, the book of the Scriptures, the rule of life; it represents the Lord's knowledge of his people, and his declaring their repentance, faith, and good works; showing the blessings of the new covenant. By their works men shall be justified or condemned; he will try their principles by their practices. Those justified and acquitted by the gospel, shall be justified and acquitted by the Judge, and shall enter into eternal life, having nothing more to fear from death, or hell, or wicked men; for these are all destroyed together. This is the second death; it is the final separation of sinners from God. Let it be our great concern to see whether our Bibles justify or condemn us now; for Christ will judge the secrets of all men according to the gospel. Who shall dwell with devouring flames?

    CE's layman's explanation: apparently the persons appearing in this scenario does not seem to have salvation, otherwise, they'd be in heaven already. these people seem to have died, or perished, or buried. Yet their bodies are resurrected to face the judge to account for their lifes. I'd say babies and those who have not heard the gospel will fall into this category. Whether in this modern times of high technology that it is possible for people NOT to have heard the gospel is another issue.

    nw it is indeed a very comprehensive debate regarding the issue on babies going heaven if aborted or die soon after birth...there r 2 major stands...one offers a very direct answer...the other leaves the issue hanging in the air...it depends on ur church stand on salvation...wad i mean? well, here goes...

    1) the issue of calvinism n armenianism
    sounds chim??? nw calvinsim is a stand tat is taken by many traditional churches...they believe tat way b4 anyone was created, God had alredi predestined(chosen) the ppl who wil be saved...some of them go further to even say tat since God predestined some to be saved...hence He muz have also predestined some for hell...the 2nd stand is nt common cos it makes God a condemning God to most christians...(read Rom9:18-23) ppl who take the calvinstic stand wil tell u tat the issue on babies is simple...those God had alredi pre-destined 4 heaven will go heaven...cos calvinism preaches tat whoever God has predestined, tat person wil nt be able to resist the inward calling of the Holy Spirit when it comes...in other words, God's grace n calling wil be so great tat the person cun resist/wun wanna resist....its diff. from saying tat God is forcing u...He juz brings forth His grace n love so strongly tat no man can resist(see also Eph1:4-6)

    2) armenianism on the other hand preaches sth diff. it preaches tat God did nt predestined ppl to heaven...He left it up to man...so man has a free-will to choose whether to accept God anot...tis stand is also criticised cos it makes God one who isnt all-sovereign. some modern churches take tis stand...so for the issue on babies...they either tell u tat they dunno or tat the babies wil go heaven cos they know no sin(even though the original sin of adam is in them)

    3) tis stand is an in between of the previous 2...it preaches tat God did do sth to man's salvation...however, He did nt predestined...He predetermined...wads the diff? to predetermine means tat God determined in advance tat all men wil be saved ie He wants n have chosen all for salvation...but its diff. from predestination cos it gives man a choice to reject wad God has determined...again the dispute is tat since man can reject God, then tat makes God nt all-sovereign...the counter-argument is tat if God does everything 100% n nt give man a choice at all, then wad kind of relationship wif God is tat? a relationship involves 2 parties...so God n man shld work hand-in -hand...for such a stand, again theres no confirm answer whether babies wil definitely go heaven...

    my personal stand is the 3rd one...i feel tat God predetermined but He didnt predestined...true the word 'predetermined' is nt in the bible...but lets put it tis way, the word 'trinity' is nt in the bible too...yet christians all believe in it...as for babies in such cases, i believe God's judgment is fair n He wil judge wad is rite according to wad His Word actually says...
