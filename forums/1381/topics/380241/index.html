Christian Conversion: Exploiting the Depressed Mind

  • NEWater
    29 Oct 09, 15:52

    In my years of being a Christian, one thing I observed with absolute and unerring frequency, was how mass conversions could come about.

    Most of the time, these are held in what are typically called, 'Church Camps', where its participants are mostly already Christians who come on the presupposition of enjoying themselves, and some non-Christians who come like sheep for the slaughter.

    Over the next few days, innocent things are done, such as song-singing, games, religious discussions and the like. These sermons are held every few hours throughout those few days and most commonly enough, the sermons will always touch on some kind of "seed-sowing" agenda that will build up to the last, or second-last day, when the speaker will start speaking of depressing things, preparing the crowd to cry and it will culminate in the speaker and/or pastor to ask people to accept Christ.

    You will see many believers raise their hands to affirm their faith once more, and the non-believers, because of the surrounding psychological conditions over the past few days, and peer-pressure during the emotional moment, will most likely raise their hands to "join the family".

    This is not exclusively done in these 'Church Camps', but also more commonly so in hospices, when people are lying in their beds, waiting to die, and these Christians troll about looking for 'more souls to save', exploiting and taking advantage of the dying's emotional vulnerability.

    Christians will agree with me that you do not see people laughing with joy when they come to the moment of accepting Christ. Despite the sort of prize that Christians make it out to be when people accept Christ, one can observe that the motivation behind conversions, is due to the belief that for every person a Christian converts, he or she will earn a crown in heaven. Which is sadly mercenary of them.

    It is only when people's minds have been tremendously weakened by a series of clever marketing tricks by Christians, or because they have been so depressed that they feel that they need a supernatural presence to help them in their lives, that people will see the need to accept Christ.
