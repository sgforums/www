    Strict, but not unreasonable.
    One of the biggest problem in bring up children nowadays is the issue of overindulgence, ie, allowing children to do whatever they want, whenever they want. It inculcates the "me-first" mentality. Children must learn that the world does not revolve around them only. Whatever course of action they choose to take, there are consequences, some of which are irreversible. They need to understand this.
    Are we happy with children running up and down the aisle of the MRT?
    Screaming at the top of their voice if they do not get what they want at the toy shop?
    Disrespectful to the elders within the family?
    Disciplining children is not done solely for the purpose of control, or so that they do not make a scene. It is a method of equipping them with the necessary social skiils that when these children grow up they can cope with life better.
    While disciplining may have involved the cane at some point in time, it does not have to be that way all the time. Counselling and communication and lots of love are equally important too.
