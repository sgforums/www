    1) ARe you trusting in Christ alone for salvation?

    2) Are there changes no matter how feeble and small in you?

    3) Is there repentance?

    KNow also this:

    There are three classes of Christians John with respect to assurance of salvation.

    1) There is a person who doesn't know that he is saved but is saved. This is due to a faith so feeble and so small. This particular Christian is often beset with fears and doubts regarding salvation. May God comfort and grant this type of Christian full assurance.

    2) There is a Christian who is tottering from being sure of being saved to doubting his salvation. One time he is sure he is saved, another he is cringing in fear because of doubts and fears. I belong to this category.

    3) There is the fully assured Christian. I envy this Christian. He is usually up there in the ladder of holiness, for full assurance of salvation results in increased holiness. He is the indefatigable worker in the field of evangelism. He is the Christian who does great exploits for our Lord.

    REad the biographies of the giants in the faith, they are usually the ones fully assured of their salvation.

    Originally posted by shade343:
    So what is going to happen when Christ comes?
    That's a good question there.

    Actually, there are two answers you will get with this question.

    1) The Christian who embraces covenant theology will say that when Christ comes He will judge the living and the dead [Judgement Day] and eternity will ensue

    2) The Christian who embraces dispensational, premillinial theology says that there are several things will take place when the Lord Jesus Christ comes;

    a) Judgement of the nations b) He will reign for a thousand years after this e) the Judgement of all the unsaved from all of time f) eternity

    I believe that this one here the dispensational view is biblical to the core. Some people believe that the Lord Christ second coming is just a single isolated event that will transpire within an hour or so.

    But actually no. His Second Coming is a period of time , just like His first coming here two thousand years ago. When He first came He did not just stay here for about two to three hours, no He, stayed here for about 33 years. And this will be so in His Second Coming also, He will come judge the nations and reign for a thousand years on earth.

    He was rejected here on earth as king and Messiah when He first came, and when He comes again the Second time He will reign in victory and glory here on earth where He was rejected as king.
