    In short...from the bible...

    The Word of God is forever valid. So is the Law. The law is the basic foundation of morality, which every civil law in every country is based upon, such as no murdering, cheating, adultery, a rest day, etc. all of which are observed throughout the world as the fundamental truth, even if they don't acknowledge God as Creator.

    So anyone who tell you the law is invalid, is a liar.

    Yet, the law cannot save, nobody can keep the laws. The bible says, even if you break one, you break all of them. Therefore the law was not sent by God as a 'requirement' to be saved, but is to tell us what is right and wrong, that we've done wrong.

    Yes, the Law cannot save, but it has its purpose.

    So by law, we can never be justified, never be saved. Therefore salvation is by Grace alone, not by works. No amount of works, ceremonies can save us. It is by grace, which we cannot earn and do not deserve.

    However, from the beginning of time, prophesied in the Old Testament, God promised He will send His Son to die for our sins, so that we will be forgiven and redeemed. Therefore, with Christ, we can claim salvation and redemption, forgiveness of sin by grace.

    Law and Grace

    Law and Grace works together. The law is like a mirror, showing us what is wrong with us, but while the mirror cannot clean your face, Grace through the blood of Jesus can forgive and wash away our sins.

    Because the law is valid, that's why we need Grace. And Grace is there because the law can't save us. It is as simple as that. If the law can save, we need not have Grace.

    This is the same concept of salvation in both the Old and New Testament. In the Old Testament, before Christ came, God gave the Israelites the tabernacle and festivals and ceremonies, signifying law and grace. After Christ came, the ceremonies are no longer necessary because it has been fulfilled by Christ, as the Lamb that was slained.

    The Wrong Grace

    The problem with 'extreme grace' preachers is that they distort the meaning of grace to say that, because of grace, you will ALWAYS be forgiven, regardless of what you do and we need not repent anymore. Which is not true, as the bible also says if we take Christ for granted, one day we will find no place for repentance. In Ephesians, Paul also preached and warned against this doctrine.

    In theology, we call this doctrine, antinomianism - or the teaching of lawlessness. It is an age-old heresy that appears every few hundred years in the Church world. It comes and goes.

    Yes, works can't save us, it is purely by Grace. Yet, if we take Grace for granted, God will retract Grace from us. That's why in Revelations, you will still read of churches being warned and luke warm believers being spit out and rejected by God.

     

    Torah, Judaism, Christians

    The problem with people who claim that only the 'original' Torah and Judaism is a true religion is that, they missed the point that God said He will send His Son to atone for our sins.

    And Jesus did came, He proved Himself, totally sinless, blameless, died willingly on the cross. Moved in compassion, preached and revealed the Word of God to us.

    The New Testament and the Old Testament is a complete book that does not conflict each other. It is from the same God that the Jews worship, who has also sent Christ to reveal His love and salvation to men.

    Therefore, the Torah and the Old Testament for that matter, is basically half-the-story of God, the New Testament completes it.

    If you read carefully. The Old and New Testament cross references and proves each other's authenticity as the true Word of God, from the same God, with the same character and the same purpose and mission and plan for men.

    I hope you will see the light and find Christ :)

    But the right teaching of tongues is that - Yes, it can be spoken publicly with or without interpretation. If you are speaking to God, nothing wrong.
