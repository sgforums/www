    Well, it all depends on who you believe.
    Don Henley stated in write-up in their "The Complete Greatest Hits" CD: "....We were enamored with hotels. Hotels were a big part of our lives. The Beverly Hills Hotel had become something of a focal point....I though there were a certain mystery and romance about it....There are plays like Neil Simon's Plaza Suite and California Suite, which Glenn and I went to see while writing the song.... bla bla bla"
    Obviously this quotation does not prove either way whether the Eagles sang Satanic songs.

    While, hopefully, trying not to sound too pro-Eagles, bear in mind that there are always nay-sayers, and witch-hunters.
    One issue about backmasking that had bothered me is: If a song is sung by another singer, ie cover-version, does the backmasking still exist?
    Or to put it simply,
    (A) is backmasking merely a reverse sounding sentence, ie by saying a sentence, instead of singing the song, would the words have come across as backmasked....
    (B) alternatively, is backmasking a technical insertion, ie, phrases inserted in a recording attempting to reach the subliminal level of consciousness.

    If the answer is (A) - then we CANNOT even recite the phrases in Hotel California or for that matter work that combination of words in our conversation as it might cause someone to stumble.
    If the answer is (B) - then go ahead and buy the cover version, backmasked words are not in it.

    Or maybe it is (C) - the meaning of the words convey an ideology that is contrary to one's chosen lifestyle. Then there are plenty of this around, whether it's Eagles or Carlos Santana

    At the end of it all, it is cause and effect! Does the song (or for that matter, any genre of entertainment) affect you? Never seen Hotel California affecting aunties and uncles.
