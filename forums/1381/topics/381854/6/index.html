    Originally posted by Larryteo:

    After all, how can I believe in a religion that claims life is an illusion?

    No offence.

    In Buddhism, life is not an illusion but like an illusion, vivid yet ungraspable. There is a big difference between 'life is an illusion' and 'life is LIKE an illusion'. Because if it is truly an illusion or a dream, life will end and some other reality will appear when we wake up or attain enlightenment, but in Buddhism, the world rightly seen is enlightenment.

    We do not deny life, we reject a mistaken view of life, as something static, fixed, separate, graspable. Life is something dynamic, ungraspable, ever changing, without center or circumference. There is no reference point you can point to called 'me'. Life IS everything. When there is no grasping on a false separate 'me', no dividing into a 'me in here' vs 'universe out there', there is no suffering. There is no longer a holding onto a dead image of what 'me' or what 'life' is, no boundaries, no bondage, no suffering.

    Everything is vivid, but ungraspable like a mirage on the horizon. If we investigate, we cannot find a permanent entity called 'me' that truly exists inherently, there is just an everchanging stream of sensate reality that is ungraspable, and nevertheless vivid and clear.
