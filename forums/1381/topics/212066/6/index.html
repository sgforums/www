    Nope, it cannot. However, it is very useful in explaining why evolution happen. I hate to explain everything from the start but here goes:

    It was first noticed that all populations of animals grow exponentially, even with slow reproducing elephants. A single pair of elephants can give rise to a few thousand elephants in ten generations.

    However, we don't see that in everyday life. Why? Cos' due to competition, the less able elephants die off, leaving the fitter ones to carry on reproducing, thus more representation of their "better genes" in the gene pool. This survival of the fittest allows for evolution, so that each generation of elephants will be able to survive better in their harsh environment.

    We also see this type of breeding in pure breed dogs or cattle (although man-induced, in contrast to environment-induced). Understand so far?

    Here comes the role of adaptation. Evolution can only adapt to existing structures and modify them, instead of creating everything from scratch. Take for example giraffes, fossils of giraffes were recovered and found to have shorter necks (but still quite long) than the comtemporary ones. Why? In the natural evolution history of giraffes, those with longer necks get more food from higher branches and thus more capable of producing more offspring. Over time, their necks grew longer but the number of bones in their neck remained the same (7, same as any mammals). This is adaptation.

    The same adaptation rules can be applied to explain why humans have tailbones (when they evolved from ape-like prehumans), whales have rudimentary hind limbs (when they evolved from land-walking mammals) and bats have finger bones and membranes for wings.

    If creationism exist, why would adaptations be so evident everywhere? Imagine you're an engineer, wouldn't it make more sense to build a bird like an F-16 so that they can fly faster? Wouldn't it make more sense to have wheels instead of feet so that animals can run faster? Wouldn't it be more sensible to build fishes with turbines, so that they can swim faster?
