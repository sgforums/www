    Christ dominates the big screen (Straits Times, 28 Feb 2004)
    Three Christian films will be shown here soon but movie distributors say the trend is just a coincidence

    By Foong Woei Wan

    A CHRISTIAN revival is sweeping the big screen.

    Besides Mel Gibson's controversial The Passion Of The Christ, two other Christian films will be shown here soon.

    On March 18, distributor United International Pictures will release The Gospel Of John, which is the tale of Christ as told by his apostle John.

    On July 8, distributor Shaw Organisation will release Luther, a biopic of Martin Luther, the 16th-century German leader of the Protestant Reformation.

    John is a 2003 Canadian-British co-production narrated by Christopher Plummer and stars relative unknown Henry Ian Cusick as Christ.

    It is a word-for-word adaptation from the American Bible Society's Good News Bible.

    Luther is a 2003 European production based on the play by Briton John Osborne and stars Joseph Fiennes in the lead role.

    A spokesman for Shaw says it will be on a limited release.

    Gibson's dramatisation of the last hours of Christ's life is likely to be released here on April 1, says Warner-20th Century Fox.

    UIP and Shaw say the trend is just a coincidence.

    Other distributors, including Cathay-Keris Films and Golden Village Pictures, say they have no plans to release Christian movies.

    'We were on the lookout for a Christ movie before Passion generated such a controversy,' said Mr Nicholas Yong, UIP's managing director for Singapore and Malaysia.

    'The time was ripe. The Gospel Of John was released in the United States recently,' he said.

    Gibson's film has come under criticism for allegedly showing the Jews in a bad light, and also for its violent scenes.

    Still, it made US$23.6 million (S$40 million) in the US and Canada on its opening day on Wednesday.

    Passion may become the most-watched religious-themed movie since The Ten Commandments and Ben Hur.

    Warner-20th Century Fox has sent a print of the film to the Media Development Authority. No rating has been announced for it.

    UIP's Mr Yong expects a PG rating for John.

    'It's not as violent as Passion, it's more family-friendly,' he said.

    A spokesman for Shaw said of Luther, which has not been rated: 'All along, we've been planning to release it in July.'

    On top of these three films, a Christian play, The Gospel According To Mark, is in its second week, and is now at The Substation Garden. It stars stage and TV actor Rodney Oliveiro as Christ.

    World-In-Theatre's playwright, Sonny Lim, says it is playing to houses that are 'more than 50 per cent' full.

    But he doubts that the crowds have been drawn by the controversy over The Passion Of The Christ.

    It just happens that his Christian play is 'the first in years', he says.
