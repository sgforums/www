    If one should not believe in the Christian God then very well and then, let us cast Him down as a piece of evil dogma that only seeks to hinder all joys.

    But that leaves the bigger question of where all these joys and pleasures come from, let there be no joy and fear and let us be free to seek this happiness... it seems that the initial attraction of athiesm suddenly turns sour when one realizes the cold brutality of the creed.

    Having done away with any form of rules, it seems the only rule we have is that of power. The strongest and fittest shall rule in the end, and the strongest and fittest do not care a whilt for the suffering. But then there is no God, no nothing to resist them, nothing to say they ought to do otherwise. They are the gods and nothing is there to stop them.

    So what exactly do we want? We want to be free to make our choices without anybody cutting our fun. But being free to make choices without restrictions means being free to do things that might hurt and sadden others. And if there are no rules against these evils what hope do we have?

    It seems to me to have happiness you have to follow a certain set of absolute rules and apply to all men irregardless of how high or how low they are. And this set of rules cannot be given by man, but by something higher and beyond him. Trace this far back enough we would have arrived at God yet again.

    Toss away God from the picture, and you will not have to fear the threat of hell, because there will be hell on earth. The fear would have become the reality.

    So why is there the fear? We ought to be thinking why we are blessed enough to be in the state to fear hell, rather then be in the (as we so deserved) very place itself. The fear is an act of mercy and not cruelty, the moment one thinks of it.

    I do not for a moment think the fear is the main motivation for those who realize that those rules are out there for our own good so that we might have as much happiness, even more then we could ever have now.

    It seems to me that God does not want us to just enjoy and see the pleasures we have around us, He wants to give us the real and proper things of eden, but reaching there involves sacrifice (because we have the choice) of the unreal things we make the choice to hold onto now.

    So in the end the choice is still yours.

    What say you?

    Speaking of another kind of fear (not the threatening kind but the kind where we find God scary and awesome), I don't see anything wrong about it... we ought to be scared of God.

    God loves us, that's why he cannot just let us do whatever we want, because so often what we think we want turns out to be the very thing we hate. It seems that all His bothersome efforts to mess with us are an effort to give us the thing we really wanted all the while. If only we could trust Him.
