    Avoiding the question by stating that all preconceptions should be cast down is more jargon then fact, for it in itself relies on a preconception that preconceptions are bad.

    I do not for a moment think that faith and first principles were ever seperate, to have faith you will have to have a first principle. Without which no case (logical one at least) can be ever built. It may seem valiant to say a lot of things about this most excellent faith, but at the end of the day it seems to slit it's own throat by commiting the very same mortal sin it accuses other systems of idol-faith of doing, and that is to hold some preconception itself of God, and that is a preconception of the nullity of preknowledge.
