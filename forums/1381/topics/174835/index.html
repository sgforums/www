    Sanctification is being set apart. Once a sinner trusts in Christ alone for salvation, he is set apart to God from the world.

    Sanctification [positional] is simultaneous with justification. Justification is being declared righteous by God on the basis of Christ's saving work on the cross whcih you trusted alone for salvation.

    Justification speaks of our standing with God [we are righteous in His eyes even though we are still sinners in this world].

    While sanctification has two aspects: positional and progressive.

    Sanctification [positional] speaks of our position with God. WE are forever set apart unto HIm. Even though the Corinthians were an imperfect bunch, they were still called SAINTS [meaning set apart].

    Sanctification [progressive] speaks of our being conformed by and by to the image of the Lord Jesus Christ. The Corinthians maybe an imperfect bunch but my!!!!! how they grew into the image of Christ over time.

    The basis of sanctification is the cross.

    Personally, it works when you yield to the Holy Spirit and allow yourself to be taught in His Word the Bible.

    Providentially, all things work for our sanctificaion. ALL THINGS [even our failures]. I don;t know how God does this but we will see it in eternity.
