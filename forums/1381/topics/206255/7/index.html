    Objection 2. Further, Christ is called God in respect of His Divine Nature. But the Divine Nature did not first originate from the Virgin. Therefore the Blessed Virgin should not be called the Mother of God.

    Reply to Objection 2. This was an argument of Nestorius. But Cyril, in a letter against Nestorius [Cf. Acta Conc.     It is good for you, despondent, to ask for clarification. Okie, let me put this in Catholic's point of view.

    Purgatory
    1 Cor 3:12-15

    We believe that, on the hour of our death, our past works will be tested. If they passes the test, then we are rewarded, but if they fail, we will be passed through fire in order to be saved. This fire must not be Fires of Hell if we are promised to be saved. It cannot be the Eternal Fire of God in Heaven if our works fail the test, since everything in Heaven is pure and holy. So, there must have existed another kind of Fire which will purge our infirmities. The doctrine of Purgatory is built on the scriptural implication of "passing through fire".

    Note that Paul prayed for Onesiphorous after he died, "may the Lord grant that he will find mercy from the lord on that day" (2nd Timothy 1:16-18 ). If he is either in Heaven or Hell, the prayer would not make sense, because the souls in Heaven don't need prayers anymore, and souls in Hell no longer have the ability to receive prayer. So where is the soul whom Paul thinks still in need of prayer?
