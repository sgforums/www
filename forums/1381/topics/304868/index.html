    This is probably one of the better ones I've seen. Better in the sense that it makes sense (sort of) to me. I disagree with the statement that it's a matter of faith for all of us.

    "Why do I believe? Feelings, intuitions, the human history of looking beyond and sensing/yearning for something bigger, especially in times of crisis. Our race also has some nearly-universal sense of basic ethics (kindness vs. cruelty, generosity vs. selfishness) that often seems to me to go beyond the hard-wired self-preservation instincts of other animals. I wonder if that's where the divine comes in.

    But that's still not really it. For me I believe the Christian life is the best way for me to live. In this world. Even if I'm wrong about the existence of God, I won't have any regrets.

    And I surely admit the lack of proof, and don't look down on people who won't throw aside that fact and believe anyway. It's a matter of faith for all of us, atheists included. We all do the best we can with what we have/know/feel."
