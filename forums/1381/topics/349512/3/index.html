    I fully agree with you that there are lots of inequality in this world (in terms of material/mental well being, the ability to live in a dignified manner etc). Which is why in my original post, I had explained (in answer to your comments) that in my view, equality before God "does not mean that we will all be equally prosperous etc in this temporal life". It means that God does not discriminate based on our earthly statuses (president or janitor etc) and when we face God after we die, "he will judge us according to our faith and how we lived our lives, ie: our good works and our unrepented sins. It does not matter whether we are rich or poor, strong or weak, Singaporean or Malaysian, Chinese or Eurasian."

    You might also be interested to read Matt 25:31-46. In this story about the separation of sheep from goats, Jesus taught us the importance of charity, to right these inequalities whenever we can in our own little ways, because it affects our eternal salvation (ability to get to heaven).
