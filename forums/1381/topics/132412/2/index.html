    As I have explained above, in view of our sinful nature, we will find it impossible to achieve justification through the law. That leaves us with only one alternative means of justification. The central doctrine of Christianity is about the substitutionary justification provided by Christ.

    Because Christ is fully God and fully Man; unlike Man, he is holy and yet he is also the perfect representation of Man. The punishment of sin, which is the failure to obey the law, is death. By the death of Christ, he who is without sin takes on the punishment of sin on Man's behalf. So whilst we have all inherited the sinful nature through Adam, every man since Adam may become justified through the death of Christ, who never sinned. That's what it means when the Bible says,

    "Consequently, just as the result of one trespass was condemnation for all men, so also the result of one act of righteousness was justification that brings life for all men. For just as through the disobedience of the one man the many were made sinners, so also through the obedience of the one man the many will be made righteous." [Rom5:18-19]

    You must have heard the analogy of this idea of substitutionary redemption. A criminal is sentenced to a fine for a crime he committed. The judge who did not crime had to pass the sentence out of justice but out of mercy, he offered to pay the fine for the criminal. By simply accepting the judge's offer, the criminal becomes justified. His debt is paid and he goes free.

    For this to work, the person making the substitution must be a representative of the one who sinned. So Jesus has to be fully Man in order to take the punishment due to Man and yet has to be fully God so that he is not himself under the penalty of sin. Thus it says in the bible:

    "For this reason he had to be made like his brothers in every way, in order that he might become a merciful and faithful high priest in service to God, and that he might make atonement for the sins of the people." [Heb 2:17]

    Those who had the laws of moses before the birth of Christ were told to bring a sin sacrifice at regular intervals:

    "Once a year Aaron shall make atonement on its horns. This annual atonement must be made with the blood of the atoning sin offering for the generations to come. It is most holy to the LORD." [Ex30:10]

    That sacrifice of lamb is not by itself efficacious as atonement for sin. It is accepted as a foretaste of Jesus, who is called the lamb of God:

    "The next day John saw Jesus coming toward him and said, "Look, the Lamb of God, who takes away the sin of the world!" [Jn1:29]

    By his death, Jesus became the sacrifical lamb that replaced the sacrificial system set up by the Mosaic Law. The people before Christ had the law, they recognized their sins under the law and as provided by the law, they offered animal sacrifices. This was accepted as atonement because God, who is not limited by time, has provided the perfect sacrifice in the form of Christ, coming centuries after Moses. So this is what the bible means when it says,

    "God presented him as a sacrifice of atonement, through faith in his blood.
