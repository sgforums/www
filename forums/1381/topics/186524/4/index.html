    If logic can lead one to believe that humans can never fly or even touch the moon, how reliable can logic be? Be flexible.
    Ah but then again this all depends on if logic is generated by human minds or if a perfect law of logic already exists, and it is minds that try to comprehend this logic. Does the mind create logic or does it 'see' logic?

    I believe it's the latter, saying that logic can be anything or subject to any sort of change is as good as saying that logic is nothing at all. It is apparent that this Logic (Perfect tense), is also not some things, as much as it is some things. All men accept this principle, even those who claim that there is no absolute truth, in which they assert that the lack of absolute truth is an absolute that has to hold for their talkings to make sense. They then will have to construct their reasonsings why their absolutes make sense... I have yet to see a convincing reply though, beyond appealing to a lot of emotions.

    It is entirely true that certain things that once seemed illogical may now become logical. But that was not because they were illogical to begin with but because they were logical all this while, just that we have yet to perceive it. When finally understood, it would have been found that they have never contradicted anything to begin with.

    First and foremost, when we distil logic down to it's components we have first principles. That is basic pieces of reasoning that cannot be reasoned themselves, they have to be self-apparent and self-supportive. A good example would be the law of non-contradiction, where if A is equal to B, then B cannot be equal to A. This law cannot be denied in any way without changing all other forms of thought. The only way to get around this rule is to find some form of harmonization which removes the contradiction, but the law itself cannot be destroyed.

    Your binary and telephone ideas are good examples of how certain 'contradictory' concepts can become congruent with logic. But then they hardly mean that things which are really and truly contradictory can ever be reconciled. No matter of working in the universe can undo that, because they have been set in stone by absolute logic.

    Is it possible that Jesus might be what the Gnostics say He is as well as what traditional Christianity says, if some form of harmonization can be found. But as of yet if this harmonization is a reasonable one or even one that can preserve the truth of both without compromising them in either way is highly dubious, or at least a very long shot based on mostly speculation. Can I say they are absolutely irreconcilable? Certainly not because being absolutely sure is an impossibility.

    But I know that in the end we have to make a choice at the end of all possible reasoning. This matter either is, nor is not. Either what the Gnostics imply is true or is not, as is the same for Traditional Christianity. There are many arguments but I throw my wager with Traditional Christianity, not so much because the mass of people is there, but because my own personal rational reasoning point me towards there. I'm sure the Gnostics have their own compelling reasons as well, but it turns out that often only one of us can be correct.

    This then is a matter of debate. But I am dubious the Gnostics and Christianity can be absolutely reconciled. I suspect it could be a lot of wishful thinking.

    I suspect the view that anything can be harmonized... if the apporiate logic is found, to be one suspect of logic itself. If you change one thing in logic you change everything else that is implied by that very logic. Also if logic itself is always subject to change on a whim ala. no absolute logic style... then on what grounds do we believe this logic to be sound? By what standard do we measure it? What makes us think this logic is more or better then the other? Which logic then is true? Do we have a whole many sets of logics, feminist logic, stoic logic, atheistic logic, materialistic logic, humanistic logic, and all the other logics without ever cosidering if these logics are true? I don't know but it's a compelling question.

    And often I wonder why we want to change it at all. Is it because we have compelling reasons to change the way we see things... or is it because it is the way we'd wished to see things?

    Let's say we have compelling evidence that whales do exist, however you do come across a person who for certain reasons, believes that it is impossible for such an animal to exist. No matter what. No matter how convincingly you might show him, or even get him to touch and see the actual animal he can claim that all this is a delusion or a trickery of the senses, or appeal to some alternative reason as to why the very evidence he is seeing is not proof of a whale. It boils down to if the person's particular point of view is open to the possibility of the existence of a whale or not. Evidence can help to strengthen one's logic, but it can never create the logic itself.

    It is the evidence that appeals to the logic, not the logic that is birthed by the evidence. If the logic was created by the evidence then there would be no reason whatsoever to consider why this or that logic should be generated from that particular evidence.
