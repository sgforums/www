Some parishes have a system of Neighbourhood Groups, where parishioners are happy to come together once a week to have fellowship.     Missal, in the Roman Catholic Church, is a liturgical book containing all instructions and texts necessary for the celebration of Masses throughout the year. The Roman Missal (Missale Romanum) published by Pope Pius V in 1570, eventually replaced the widespread use of different missals by each episcopal see. Missals are now published first in Latin and from this definitive text translations are made into native languages. The first complete translation of the Missal into English took place between 1969 and 1970 when the Order of Mass was reformed by Pope Paul VI (1963-7. The word 'Missal', like Mass, is taken from the Latin missa, meaning 'sent'.     What else can I talk about...Ah yes, Cathecism classes.

    Cathecism classes are usually for the kids and the teenagers. The kids in primary school prepare for their first reconcilliation (confession) and their first holy communion. The teenagers prepare for the Sacrament of Confirmation. Depending on the parish, they may be confirmed in sec 2,3 or sometimes even 4.

    Unconfirmed adult Catholics will go for adult confirmation classes. It is prerequisite for all Catholics to be confirmed before getting married.

    Those interesting in joining the faith can take part in the RCIA, Rite of Christian Initiation for Adults. These classes are conducted by laymen and priests. They will be given instruction on the faith. Along their journey, they will have a sponsor, a practising Catholic who will guide them along the way.
