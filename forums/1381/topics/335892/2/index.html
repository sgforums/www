    Very true Omnia. But Nobunaga Oda's post does have a good point.

    What I have garnered from Nobunaga Oda's post - the jewel amidst the pile of distorted usage of scripture - is that we should develop self-control as a response towards God's love for us; He bought our bodies with his blood, and we were bought at a price; therefore we should honour God with our body (I Cor 6:20). This point is valid and edifying.

    But what is distorted about his view is that he completely ignored the 'love' aspect of God. God is love (I John 4:16). Yes, God is holy, and righteous, but He is love. His judgments are with grace. And no, He does not delight in sending gluttons, sinners, or even distorters of truth to hell. He died for them. He loved them. He hates the sins but He loves the sinner.

    So if you're a person who eats without self-control; just to satisfy the cravings of your belly (I can remember succumbing to this temptation quite awhile back this week), just repent. Stop eating like a glutton. Exercise self-control. And most importantly do this because you love God.

    And if you fail, don't condemn yourself - God doesn't condemn you. Come back under His cleansing blood and loving arms, and treat every irrational hunger pang (e.g. you just had a full dinner half an hour ago and you suddenly have a craving for two large slices of cake) as an opportunity to excercise self-control.

    Be filled with His love!
