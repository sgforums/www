    When one talks about proving whether an event in history happened one is talking about legal-historical evidence, not empirical evidence. There is no way to prove that the resurrection happened. Even if they find an empty tomb believed to be that of Jesus, you can still say it does not prove the resurrection happened. Empty tombs are not proofs of resurrections. So I think one way is to ask ourselves, given what we know about the testimonies and what happened to the apostles, and the beginnings of the early church, which best explains all these? That the resurrection happened or that it did not happen? To then say that the resurrection did not happen, the critics must then offer an explanation that even better explains all that.

    See also http://www.toughquestionsanswered.org/2010/06/19/what-historical-evidence-exists-for-the-resurrection/
