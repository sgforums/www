    Deut 18:9 When you enter the land the LORD your God is giving you, do not learn to imitate the detestable ways of the nations there. 10 Let no one be found among you who sacrifices his son or daughter in the fire, who practices divination or sorcery, interprets omens, engages in witchcraft, 11 or casts spells, or who is a medium or spiritist or who consults the dead. 12 Anyone who does these things is detestable to the LORD, and because of these detestable practices the LORD your God will drive out those nations before you. 13 You must be blameless before the LORD your God.

    The context of this verse is that it is addressed to the Israelites entering into foreign lands so that the Israelites do not embrace the religious culture found in the newly occupied territory. This is not to say that the warning is applicable to only to the Israelites at that time. It also serves as a warning to modern day believers who may not be aware of the danger of what the occult bring.

    However, in understanding the warning, we also need to define the dangers more specifically. To pronounce a blanket denounciation over anything remotely occultic (or even perceived to be occultic) is dangerous and irresponsible.

    When we look at the genres, something that is being defined as occultic may not really be occultic after all, similarly, just because an individual feels that a particular title or genre is occultic does not represent that this title is truly occultic.

    A simple definition for occult is, "of or pertaining to magic, astrology, and other alleged sciences claiming use or knowledge of secret, mysterious, or supernatural agencies"
    http://www.biblequestions.org/Archives/BQAR402.htm
