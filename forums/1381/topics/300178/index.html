    It is like this...

    God's gift and anointing, once given is irrevokable, because He is a generous and loving God, He never takes back what He has given.

    But if we live in rebellion and sin, the sin and rebellion will corrupt and eat it up whatever good that is produced and end up destroying our lives.

    For example, corrupt preachers and evangelists...even bible characters.

    Samson had a gift of strength but he had lust, the gift continued to work, but his sin destroyed him in the end.

    Solomon had wisdom, wisest man, same thing, destroyed by lust, hundreds of wives and foreign wives which was sin to God. God did not take away his wisdom, but it corrupted and destroyed him and his kingdom...

    and many others.

    So the evidence of the gift of God working and the anointing does not equate to holiness and purity. Just because a man is anointed and can do miracles, doesn't mean a thing, except that God has given him that gift.

    If we do not walk in obedience, sin will destroy us in the end.
