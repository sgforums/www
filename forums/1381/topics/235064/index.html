    I'll speak for the Methodist church...

    The Methodist Church in Singapore has a stringent structure of employment, with various levels of promotion. Obviously the higher one is promoted, the higher is the salary. Not too long ago, the executive council right at the top make a mistake on the Bishop's pay. They did not cover it up but put the issue on the table and explain what had gone wrong.

    Each church has it's own exco and oversees everything from the running of the church to the employment of it's workers. Granted that not every church is professionally run, and there will be some that require a higher level of management than others.

    I don't see anything wrong with a pastor making a reasonable amount of salary. Unfortunately there are always some who feel that a pastor should be paid next to nothing and has to live in extreme frugality.

    Each pastor also has to support his family and his extended family, and the salary structure may be pegged to some secular parallel.

    Having said all that, I am not aware of any Methodist pastor that is seen to be extremely well paid and drive around in high end BMWs or Mercedes.

    Again, as with some of the other threads.... I ask: what is the purpose of this thread?

    I don't believe sillyme and myself speak for all congregations, but I'd assume to be true that most churches observe a practical level of frugality. There are, of course, some churches (a minority I'd say) that may be able to afford their leaders a lifestype of luxury. At the end of the day, they are accountable to God like everybody else, and what God would do to these churches and their leaders is between God and them.

    2 Feb 07, 10:41
    1) Who is allowed to attend the management meeting?

    Each church may have more than one congregation. At church level, there may be administrative departments like building management, finance and human resource. Other departments may include Christian education, missionary and outreach. At congregational level, such departments are further broken down either at functional level like Sunday School, Ushers, Welcome committee, etc.

    Members who are deemed suitable to serve in such committees are either elected or appointed. The criteria of selection depends on a broad spectrum series of factors which may include professional qualification ie, architects and engineers who may run the building management dept, professional accountants and auditors who may run the finance departments and human resource heads to run the human resource depts and etc. The one point that needed noting is that this are all done at a volunteer basis, ie no salary or financial gains is involved.

    At the higher level, obviously not everyone can attend the meetings. This is no different from any organisation. Any committee can have the right to co-opt an ordinary member into any meeting if the need requires. And believe it or not, most committees will welcome ordinary members into such meetings if such ordinary members have been in the church long enough and show interest in knowing more.

    You obviously don't know that in any given church, looking for people to serve in such committees are NEVER easy.

    2) What information are shared during the meeting?
    Depending at which level of these meetings, many issues can be discussed, including the remuneration of church staff. Confidentiality issue is no different from any secular organisation as sensitive issues may be discussed and debated (sometimes in rather heated environment) upon.
