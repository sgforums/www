  • Chin Eng
    29 Oct 05, 21:21
    I do not believe the school of thoughts that gifts have ceased, however neither am I among those who believe that gifts are plentiful and will be given to any who ask.

    To me, gifts are for a purpose and that is solely for the furtherance of the Kingdom of God.

    On the issue of "vision", I think it means different things to different people. If anyone is to come up to me and said that he has a dream and God spoke to him, I would not attempt to question or doubt that. However, if this vision has an impact on something more than the individual, I'd say that God will also speak to the other stakeholders of that issue.

    In my experience in looking at such people, many of these people are also usually puffed up with self-importance. One cannot really take such people seriously. At the end of the time, it's the fruit that counts. I'd seen too many "visions" changing the course of many ministries only to also see these "vision" looking totally misguided and falling flat in its face.

    Many of such "visions" is a direct cause of ego. The ego of thinking that one is really close to God. In some instances, when the "vision" fails to bear fruit, the blame then falls on those who question the "vision" in the first place. You'd think if God wanted to do something, someone can stop it?

    On the question on whether does God still speak to the individual, why not! But does it have to be "visions"? Can't it be simple common sense? Can't it be merely a heart the cares that prompt the individual move?

    I'd not seen a single vision in my entire life, but that does not stop me from pushing my ministry of choice to a new level each year.
