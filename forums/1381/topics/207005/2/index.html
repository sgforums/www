    CHAPTER 5

    4 For, as supposed, an angel went down at a certain time into the pool, and stirred the water: then after the stirring of the water the first one who stepped in became cured of whatsoever disease he had.

    John 5:4 This was a common belief, but without any sound theological basis.

    8 Jesus says to him, Rise, take up your bed, and walk.

    John 5:8 Jesus did not go through a long ceremony of shouting, but just told the man to get up, carry his bed and walk. Bed here means a light and thin piece of straw bed, used by the poor.

    14 After these things Jesus finds him in the temple, and he said to him, Behold, you have become whole: sin no more, so that nothing worse will happen to you.

    John 5:14 Most likely, this particular sickness was due to a particular sin commited by the man.

    18 Therefore, because of this, the Jews sought more to kill him, because he not only broke the sabbath, but called God His own Father, making himself equal with God

    John 5:18 The Jews understood that Jesus claimed to have a unique
    relationship with God the Father indicated by the Greek word idion , thus making himself equal in being with the Father. This kind of relationship is not for any other human being. This is exclusive of the Lord Jesus Christ.

    19 Therefore Jesus answered and said to them, Truly, truly, I say to you, The Son will not do anything from himself, except what he sees the Father is doing: for whatever He does, the Son likewise does the same things.

    John 5:19 This verse does not teach that Jesus has no power to do anything. What Jesus meant was that he will not do anything apart from what the Father does. This teaches the complete unity of the Father and the Son.

    22 For the Father judges no one, but had given all judgment to the Son:

    John 5:22 The Son, who is the Lord Jesus Christ, is and will be the supreme judge of the eternal destiny of all human beings. The reason is he is the Son of Man, the Messiah Â– v. 27.

    23 That all may honor the Son, even as they honor the Father. He who does not honor the Son does not honor the Father who has sent him.

    John 5:23 The honor given to the Father should also be the same honor given to the Son. What is this honor? Belief in the deity of the Son. If one does not believe that Jesus is God, equal in being with God the Father, then he consequently does not honor the Father.

    12 And as they were filled, he said to his disciples, Gather up the fragments that remain, that nothing be lost.

    John 6:12 Some scholars doubt the historical reliability of the feeding of the five thousand. They call this a myth which was invented by Christians many years later. Other critics say that there was no real miracle.

    Challenged by the generosity of the boy, the rest of the people took out their own bread and fish and gave to others who had no food. But how would one explain the fragments which remained after the feeding? The only credible explanation is that a miracle indeed happened.

    The purpose of the miracle was to validate the prophetic authority of the Lord. Read v. 14.

    The texts

    39 And this is the will of the Father who sent me, that out of all whom he has given me I should not lose any , but should raise him up at the last day.

    40 And this is the will of him who sent me, that every one who sees the Son, and who believes in him, should have everlasting life: and I will raise him up at the last day.

    The Notes

    John 6:39,40. Those who have received eternal life are promised with all certainty that they will be raised to a glorified eternal existence at the last day, referring to the time of the rapture of the believers.

    The Texts

    50 This is the bread who came down from heaven, that anyone may eat of it, and not die.

    51 I am the living bread who came down from heaven: if anyone eat of this bread, he shall live for ever: and the bread which I will give is my flesh, which I will give on behalf of the life of the world.

    52 The Jews therefore argued among themselves, saying, How can this one give us the flesh to eat?

    53 Then Jesus said to them, Truly, truly, I say to you, If you do not eat the flesh of the Son of man, and drink his blood, you have no life in yourselves.

    54 He who eats my flesh, and drinks my blood, has eternal life; and I will raise him up at the last day.

    55 For my flesh is truly food , and my blood is truly drink.

    56 He who eats my flesh, and drinks my blood, abides in me, and I in him.

    57 Even as the living Father has sent me, and I live through the Father: so he who eats me, even that one shall live through me.

    58 This is that bread who came down from heaven: not as your fathers who ate the manna, and they died: so he who eats this bread will live for ever.

    The Notes

    John 6:50-58. This passage has been used as the basis for the practice of Â“eating the body and drinking of the blood of the LordÂ” during the eucharist. But if properly interpreted, this passage simply teaches that inorder to receive eternal life, one must appropriate the Lord Jesus by faith for he gave his body and shed his blood in behalf of sinful humanity.

    The teaching that the priest can turn the wafer into the actual flesh of Jesus Christ and the wine into the actual blood of Christ during the eucharist is not taught in the Scripture. For, according to this teaching, during the eucharist, Jesus goes through another act of sacrifice. But the Bible very clearly teaches that Jesus Christ suffered only once and therefore does not need to die again- see Hebrews 7:27. And he can no longer die because He has now a glorified body.

    Thus the celebration of the communion in many other churches is a commemoration, a remembrance of the cross-event, not a re-enactment.

    CHAPTER 8

    11 She said, none, Lord. And Jesus said unto her, Neither do I condemn you: go, and sin no more.

    John 8:11 The story of this adulterous woman is a beautiful illustration of divine forgiveness. While some critics doubt the authenticity of the account, yet this is an eloquent background to the statement of the Lord in John 8:12 that He is the light of the world.

    The Lord did not approve of her adultery. But because the accusers left, so the Lord said that neither will he condemn her to be stoned to death. However the Lord told her to go back to her house and commanded her to stop sinning. Those who received forgiveness of their sins should know that they are also commanded to stop practicing sin.
