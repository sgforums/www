    Allow me to differentiate between Eternal Atonement and Temporal Atonement. Eternal Atonement has been achieved by Jesus Christ and no further Eternal Atonement is possible (The gates of heaven is already open, how to open again?).

    However, we must make Temporal Atonement. Looking back at the Old Testament, God forgave David for his sin concerning Uriah, but he still left David the temporal punishment of having his infant son die and having the sword pass through his house (2 Sam. 12:13ff). Similarly, when Moses struck the rock a second time God forgave him (for Moses was obviously one of the saved, as his appearance on the Mount of Transfiguration illustrates), though he still suffered the temporal penalty of not being allowed to go into the promised land (Num. 20:12). They had to pay in part for their sins, so why not us?

    Is Jesus' sacrifice sufficient? Yes. We don't have to pay the eternal price if we repent. God forgives, but he wants us to learn from our mistakes via penance (temporal price). Penance is a form of discipline. Like all parents, God disciplines his children. Kindly refer to Heb. 12:5-13 which illustrates this.
    Penance does not deny Jesus' sacrifice. We must make the effort to walk towards those pearly gates.
