    I think that the article posted by laoda at the start is worth a good read.

    While I agree that it is not helpful to pinpoint any church or pastor in Singapore, it is a timely reminder of what service to God is and what isn't.

    It must realised, however, that many of such churches do not really have a higher authority to answer to (unless that higher authority is God). In many of such churches governances, the authority falls back to the pastor-in-charge (PIC) and his board of governors (or leaders).

    As all may know, I come from a church with a higher human authority than the local pastor - we have a district superintendent and beyond that, a bishop. We have pay scale and promotional grades. In other words, we have outside checks and balances. Even recently, my bishop's pay scale was open to scrutiny.

    As a responsible believer, we should always be watchful of such a leadership structure, because at the end of the day, we must ask: where is the external audit? Beyond ensuring that the P & L statement measures up, what checks and balances are there in such churches to ensure that the spirituality (which at the end, will determine the running of the church) is up to par?
