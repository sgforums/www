Jesus was trying to change the Jewish religion from within - he probably never intended to create a new Church.

    After Jesus's death, the Jewish authorities persecuted his disciples and followers. Amongst them, one of the persecutors was a man name Saul. After the persecution, stoning and killing of Stephen (Acts 7-8.1), he was enroute to persecute others when he had a religious experience and guess what, he converted and became one of Jesus's best followers. He was to become St Paul.

    Well, the world didn't end and St Paul and the rest of Jesus's followers decided that they needed more structure in their organisation. That's when the physical body of the Church organisation began to be formed. One of the issues that the new Church faced was that because they were recruiting Gentiles as well, the Jews in the Church felt, among other thigns, that the Gentiles should be circumcised as well. It is here that the Church was seperated from Jewism. St Paul decided among other things that Christians did not need to be circumcised and stripped the Church of its Jewish flavour.

    Another thing to note is that the Bible was only written down 100plus years after Jesus's death. Much of the gospel was actually verbally transmitted and only written down much later. In fact, there were many gospels (10 or 11?) with different accounts. The Church decided which 4 fit the best and put it in the Bible. The rest can are kept in the vaults of the Vatican.
