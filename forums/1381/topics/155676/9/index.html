    91. Matthew and Mark say that the last words of Christ were, in Hebrew, "Eloi, Eloi, lama sabachthani?" This has traditionally been translated as, "My God, My God, why hast thou forsaken me?" However, a more accurate translation would be, "My El, My El, why has thou forsaken me?" El is the name of a specific pagan god. Why would Jesus call out to a pagan god at the moment of his death?

    El is one of the names Hebrews used for God. Because YHWH (the "I AM", often transliterated into Yahweh or Jehovah; it was what God called Himself at the burning bush to Moses; most English Bibles translate it "the LORD") was such a formal name for God, they would use El in conjunction with other terms, leading to words such as Elohim and El-Shaddai. They would also use the term Adonai (literally, "Lord").

    Here is how important the name YHWH was to the Jews: when they would write that name down in copies of the Scripture, they would

    1) use a quill pen that would only be used for that word,
    2) take a bath before writing it,
    3) and after writing it, destroy the pen.

    If this name was so important to them, does it make sense that they would use in common speech?
