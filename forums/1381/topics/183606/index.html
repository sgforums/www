    Strictly speaking, pentecostalism is not a denomination. It is a movement based on a conviction.

    Personally speaking, i adhere to the Plymouth Brethren's convictions of non-denominationalism. First century Christianity never had denominations. We are all Brethren gathered around one head, Christ.

    We may have minor differences on non-essential points but we are

    one body, with one Spirit, one hope, one Lord, one faith, one baptism, one God and Father of all, who is over all, and through all, and in us all.

    Many churches influenced by denominationalism followed suit. Some drop the name Baptist in their churches so as not to cause division among Christians.

    Many Christian churches now are non-denominational. If you have repented of your sins and trusted in Christ alone for salvation, we are Brethren.

    Ask many a Christian now about which denomination they belong they would say: "i am non- denominational." And I am too.
