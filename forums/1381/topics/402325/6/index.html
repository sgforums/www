    Latest update

    The Straits Times reports that the Internal Security Department (ISD) is probing Pastor Mark Ng, 48, for a sermon he gave in 2008 in which he mocked Taoist beliefs. In a 10-minute audio clip posted on YouTube, Ng is heard joking with the congregation about Chinese rituals and in one instance, compared praying to Taoist deities to “seeking protection from secret society gangsters”.

    This is not the first time a pastor has mocked practices of other religions.

    In February this year, founder of Lighthouse Evangelism Rony Tan, 64,  was also hauled up and censured by the ISD for making insensitive comments about Buddhist and Taoist beliefs such as re-incarnation and nirvana. The senior Pastor later apologised for his remarks, but not before the issue sparked massive debate in multi-faith, multi-religious Singapore.

    Freelance designer Mohd. Mokhtar, 45, told Yahoo! Singapore that he now hopes the government will intervene and put a stop to religious leaders criticising or mocking other faiths.

    “It’s already the second time and I won’t be surprised it will happen again so it’s time for them to step in and give them a final warning,” he said.

    Aircraft technician Danny Lim, 30, agreed.

    “Such comments can harm religious harmony and it’s not right to be criticising other religions.”

    Engineer K. Dinesh, 29, added that religious leaders must realise they are constantly under the spotlight and as such, must refrain from talking about other faiths they have little knowledge of.

    “I think they should just speak on their own religion and there will not be any issues. It will cause a lot of unrest if they start criticising others.”

    Pastor Ng, who heads New Creation’s Chinese ministry, has so far remained silent over the latest issue but a statement posted on the church’s website said that Ng did not intend for his comments to be disrespectful to other races or religions.

    It also admitted that the particular sermon has been available for sale at the church bookstore since 2008 until early this year, presumably when Rony Tan was censured.

    “We would like to express our deepest apologies to any person who may have been offended by the remarks made by Pastor Mark Ng during a sermon delivered in August 2008,” said the church on its website.

    “Pastor Mark offers his unreserved and unmitigated apology to the public for his insensitive comments… He humbly appeals to those whom he has offended to forgive him of this serious indiscretion.”

     

    The Ministry of Home Affairs has also waded into the issue and said that New Creation had, on its own accord, contacted YouTube to remove the clip. It has also apologised and the ISD are looking into the matter.

    The original clip was uploaded by an anonymous user who said that he hoped to raise public awareness of different preaching styles.

    Mega-churches New Creation and City Harvest have both come under the public spotlight in recent weeks after it was revealed that City Harvest founder Kong Hee and 16 other church members are being investigated for misuse of church funds.

    SINGING THE PRAISE OF AN AMAZING TAOIST-BUDDHIST

     

    I don't know whether he was a Taoist or a Buddhist, so I better use a hyphenated religion to describe his faith. Generally in Singapore, a Taoist would say he's a Buddhist ("Bai Poot") when in fact he is a Taoist, & I intend to refresh our collective memory of a very inspiring Taoist-Buddhist Singaporean who died some five years ago.

     

    On that fateful afternoon 20/4/04, tunnelling work for the Circle LRT Line led to a massive cave-in at the Nicoll Highway Station. Instead of running away to ensure the safety for his own life first, as many of us would reflexively do in similar situations, a Taoist-Buddhist site foreman rushed into it to alert his workers toiling away underground, oblivious of the mortal danger to himself, & yelling at the top of his voice for all of his workers to evacuate quickly. Going deeper to continue his yelling to make sure, alas! he ended up being the only casualty of that disastrous collapse & was interred in-situ forever, as frantic efforts to locate & retrieve his body were unsuccessful.

     

    Who was he? Well, he was the late Mr Heng Yeow Peow, the 40-year-old site foreman then. As a Christian, I was so amazed with such a marvelous & ultimate act of heroism of this Taoist-Buddhist Singaporean. Many were, and I joined in the fund-raising campaign started by his caring and quick-reacting MP Ms Irene Ng (Tampines GRC), as Mr Heng was the sole breadwinner of his family.

     

    In my 28/4/04 letter of encouragement to his grief-stricken wife Mrs Sally Heng, sent through their MP (who is a Christian), I enclosed a POSB cheque for a small donation of $50. I also wrote: "He will always be remembered for his amazing selflessness. And I will suggest to our Prime Minister [Goh Chok Tong then] to set up a scheme to award and remember heroes like [her] husband." I was so pleased Mr Wong Kan Seng then came up with the posthumous "Award for Valour" for the late Mr Heng. Evidently, the kind Ms Irene Ng liked my suggestion, and had transmitted it higher up.

     

    However, I am not sure whether I was the only one who wrote to suggest to the authorities for the memorialising of Mr Heng Yeow Peow's amazing and tragic gallantry.

    I would be very sad if I was indeed the only one who thought and wrote to suggest it.

     

    Had Mr Heng been born in ancient Athen 2,400 years ago, odes would have been composed for him too. The brainy columnist of the Straits Times Mr Andy Ho wrote that "In laying down his life [for others], Yeow Peow exemplified an ideal that the ancient Greco-Roman philosophers had extolled for such noble deaths as the ultimate act of friendship." Sadly, no ode had been written by any of our Singapura bards for Yeow Peow. "How can we excuse ourselves for not memorialising his sacrifice as the highest form of citizenship?", Mr Andy Ho chided. (ST 3/9/09, pg A23).

     

    I fully agree. Hence I would suggest that Taoist, Buddhists and others should support my intention to write to Minister of Transport Mr Raymond Lim for a plaque to be properly designed and displayed in Nicoll MRT Station for the story of this amazing MR Heng Yeow Peow to be memorialised, as his self-sacrificing value is one that we greatly cherish, or should cherish in Singapore.

    This is also what my Lord Jesus has said: "Greater love has no man than this, that a man should lay down his life for his friends." John 15:13.

    But we don't all need to die for others in a tragic death, like what Mr Heng went through. Singaporeans just need to be imbued with the great spirit of altruism---always wanting to do good to others and to help improve our society. This will be our way to help PM Lee HL build for us a loving, caring and a great and enlightened Singaporean society. Then one day we can proudly proclaim our beloved Singapore to others as "An Island of Miracles!" (re MP Baey Yam Keng).

    Even if we fail to achieve this noble goal, what we would have achieved would be by itself most rewarding and amazing too.

    So "Inspire me, Lord, to be thankful to Thee for Thy favours to me and my loved ones to do good deeds which will please Thee. Admit me, through Thy mercy, to be among Thy righteous. " An-Naml 27:19. "For the noblest in God's sight is he who is the most righteous." Al-Hujurut 49:13.

    Ameen!
