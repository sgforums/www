    We all have our share of doubts my friend at some point in time. The apostles had their doubts....John the Baptist for one........the precursor of the Lord Jesus Christ even had his share of doubts:

    Lu 7:20 "And coming to Him, the men said, John the Baptist has sent us to You, saying, Are You He who should come, or do we look for another?"


    John the Baptist here was in prison already. This incident happend some time after he baptized the Lord Jesus and said of Him as :"Behold the Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world!!!!"

    It may seem a surprise to us that one as strong as John the Baptist should doubt corcerning the Lord Jesus Christ being THE CHRIST. However, we must remember that the best of men suffer lapses of faith too.

    REmember Abraham? Abraham was promised blessings in the land of promise.
    But when severe famine entered the picture. What did he do? Did he stay in the land of promise and chose to trust God? No. He fled for Egypt instead of remaining in the place of God's choosing.

    And there are so many ensamples of spiritual giants faltering in their faith.
    It will be too volumious if we have to talk about Gideon, Jacob, Sarah and etc.

    yes, my friend we experience doubts. However, you must know that doubt is not the opposite of faith. The opposite of faith is unbelief. You can have doubts and still have faith:

    Mt 14:31 And immediately Jesus stretched out His hand and caught him, and said to him, "O you of little faith, why did you doubt?"

    You can even have some degree of unbelief and still have faith:

    Mr 9:24 Immediately the father of the child cried out and said with tears, "Lord, I believe; help my unbelief

    The man obviously had faith. He brought his son to the Lord Jesus Christ [in the first place].

    but he was also assailed by unbelief.

    This is so my friend because we are not completely ridden of our sinful nature. The moment you trusted in Christ alone for salvation, a new nature was implanted to you. But the sinful nature ,although nailed to the cross when Christ died for us, is still there and still rears it's ugly head.

    My advice to you my friend is:

    Keep on reading the Bible. Faith comes by hearing and so faith must grow also by continual hearing of the Word of God. Our spiritual food.

    As you grow in grace and in the knowledge of the Lord, your spiritual muscles get stronger.

    P.S. please do not hesitate to ask us questions here......it is our duty to help a fellow believer.
