    Originally posted by Icemoon:
    Correct me if I'm wrong .. in Skibi's example Jesus gave up his powers to become man, then become God again.
    1) Jesus gave up his powers and his divinity to become fully Man.

    Theoritically this is possible as God being omnipotent can do anything, even to give up his nature and become fully man. However an important point to take note is that once a substance changes its form physically and as an entity, it ceases to to exist in its new form. Hence Skibi and I do agree that once Jesus gives up his divinity, he ceases to be God but instead becomes Man.

    2) Man Jesus becomes God after he completes his duty.

    This is naturally impossible. While I do concede its possible for the new form to undergo a transformation to return back to its oringal state, in this context it is impossible. It is impossible for a MAN no matter how divine his origins might be or how sinless he was, to ever become God. It is naturally impossible for Man of finite matter t ever take up infinity. The reverse of God becoming Man (in his doctrine) is possible but not the part of Man Jesus becoming God.

    God declared: "Â…that ye may know and believe me, and understand that I am he: before me there was no God formed, neither shall there be after me" (Isa. 43: 10). Before God, there was no God. After God there shall be no God formed. Hence, man (even Man) Jesus cannot so develop and graduate as to become God himself.

    As a resuly Skibi doctrine falls and the Chalcedonian Trinatiran view of Christ stands.
