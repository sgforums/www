    I started this forum without a theme verse and I left it very much without. Recently, while attending service, my pastor spoke of this verse
    "As the deer pants for the water brooks, So my soul pants You, O God. My soul thirsts for God, for the living God; When shall I come and appear before God? My tears have been my food day and night, While {they} say to me all day long, "Where is your God?" These things I remember and I pour out my soul within me. For I used to go along with the throng {and} lead them in procession to the house of God, With the voice of joy and thanksgiving, a multitude keeping festival. Why are you in despair, O my soul? And {why} have you become disturbed within me? Hope in God, for I shall again praise Him {For} the help of His presence." -- Psalm 42:1-5
    This verse speaks to me a lot as it is the very same song that first touched me when I was a young Christian. Let me share with your the song.

    As the deer panteth for the water
    So my soul longeth after Thee
    You alone are my heart's desire
    And I long to worship Thee
    (Chorus)
    You alone are my strength, my shield
    To You alone may my spirit yield
    You alone are my heart's desire
    And I long to worship Thee
    You're my friend and You are my brother
    Even though You are a king
    I love You more than any other
    So much more than anything
    Chorus
    I want You more than gold or silver
    Only You can satisfy
    You alone are the real joy giver
    And the apple of my eye
    Chorus

    I hope that no matter whatever situations you are in and however down you are, continue to put your hope in God. Just as the psalmist remembers God's faithfulness in His life and praises God, we too should rejoice in God for the wonderful things He has done for us.
