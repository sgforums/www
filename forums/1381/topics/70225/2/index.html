    The end of Apostleship after Revelation teaches an immediate ceasation of Prophecy and tongue speaking.

    The key is to read all Scripture with the context, and not taking verses and explaining them out-of-context.

    This study of tongue speaking is one of the fundamental doctrine that differentiate churches. (We can hold a full 5 day camp just to focus on this doctrine). Nonetheless, i like to post this question to all for some thoughts since someone mentioned about the similarity of the tongue speaking and chanting of mediums.

    I will be happy to give my opinions as long as it is understood that they ARE OPINIONS and does not represent any church in general.

    I fear that "new-age" Christians leaders have been extremely liberal in extracting verses in support of their causes and fads.

    Proponents of such beliefs will usually quote "new revelations" or such similar terminologies to enforce new methodology.

    Similar, over the past few years, there was the emphasis of cell-groups, children's church etc. While there is really nothing wrong for one to join a cell group or to emphasize that children, too, should learn to worship. It becomes questionable, when a church which embarks in such activities theologise or spiritualise the activity.

    A supporter of a cell group church will quote that in Acts, Christians meet in house churches, hence the validity.

    A children's church proponent will quote a verse from the Gospel stating that Christ admonished the disciples who were pushing the children away.

    A practitioner of the children's worship will quote Miriam's song and tambourine routine (in Deuteronomy) to support the use of such a musical instrument plus banners and streamers.

    To take verses out of context is like to insist that we should all wear sandals 'cos Jesus commanded the disciples to shake the dust of the sandals when the leave the towns that do not take in the message.

    Footnote: I am not against tongues, CGs, children's church, singing etc. I am just against the "theologizing" of programmes.
