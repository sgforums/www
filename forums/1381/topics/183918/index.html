    Ther 39 articles of religion found in the Book of Common Prayer of the Anglican Church:

    http://bible.crosswalk.com/History/AD/CreedsandConfessions/Confessions/TheEpiscopalConfession.html

    Article 28 will tell you the Anglican perception and teachings about the Lord's Supper.

    The Anglican church DO NOT suscribe to the doctrine of Transubstantiation which is of Roman Catholic origin. Rather, they see the elements of bread as wine as the Body and Blood of Christ broken and shed for us and this in symbolic and spiritual sense. The wafer remains as wafer, the wine remains as wine but when the communicant receives the elements he/she receives and reminded of the Body and Blood of Christ. Nothing mystical and nothing magical about the elements.

    Back to the Holy Communion Heresy issue: NCC's way of treating the Eucharist is something not to be tolerated, gearing towards Christian superstitions and completely missed the point of celebrating Communion.
