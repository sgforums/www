    My friend, there are two kinds of forgiveness in the Bible:

    a) salvation forgiveness and b) parental forgiveness.

    The former happens once for all time. It is not repeated. It is positional. Positionally the repentant sinner who has trusted in Christ alone for salvation HAS BEEN FORGIVEN OF ALL SINS THROUGH THE DEATH AND RESURRECTION OF THE LORD JESUS [who died for his sins --> PAST, PRESENT AND FUTURE].

    The latter is the forgivness of the saved sinner. One who through faith in the Lord Jesus alone has become a member of the family of God. This type of forgiveness is repeated all throughout our walk here on earth. We confess our sins and God through the DEPOSIT THAT THE LORD JESUS HAS MADE ON THE CROSS [WHO ANTICIPATED ALL OUR SINS FROM BIRTH TO DEATH] WILL FORGIVE US.

    judgement?

    the saved sinner has passed from that. All who have trusted in the Lord Jesus therefore have recieved salvation forgivenss and had their sins judged already for them when the Lord Jesus died on the cross. Judgement is past them. They have already been judged in the person of their substitute, the Lord Jesus Christ since they accepted Him as their substitute by placing their faith on Him.

    Joh 5:24 "Most assuredly, I say to you, he who hears My word and believes in Him who sent Me has everlasting life, and shall not come into judgment, but has passed from death into life.

    those who have not trusted in the Lord Jesus Christ, their judgement is yet future. They will have no substitute since they rejected the Lord Jesus. They will have to suffer for their sins.

    Think of salvation forgiveness as the forgivness of a criminal. Think of parental forgiveness as the forgiveness of a father to an erring child. The father will surely punish him but certainly not as a criminal. His punishment [or more accurately his DISCIPLINE] is not penal but parental.

    To obtain salvation forgiveness, you must trust [or believe] in the Lord Jesus Christ alone for salvation. To obtain parental forgiveness, one must confess his sins to His Father in heaven.

    Salvation forgivenss:

    Ac 16:30 And he brought them out and said, "Sirs, what must I do to be saved?"
    31 So they said, "Believe on the Lord Jesus Christ, and you will be saved,

    parental forgiveness:

    1Jo 1:9 If we confess our sins, He is faithful and just to forgive us our sins and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness.
