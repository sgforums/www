    Originally posted by novelltie:

    Roman Emperor Constantine the Great converted to Christianity following his victory at the Battle of Milvian Bridge in 312AD and made Christianity the main religion of Rome 

    Actually not. It is a common misconception and belief that Constantine I was the Roman emperor who made Christianity as the official religion of the empire. However the truth was Constantine did not made it the official religion, he merely ended the laws against it. He also never converted to Christianity before or after the Battle of Milvian bridge, having continued worshipping the pagan sun god and was only baptised when he was nearing the end of his life.

    It was Emperor Theodosius the Great, 10 years after Constantine I's death, who made Christianity the official religion of the Roman Empire.
