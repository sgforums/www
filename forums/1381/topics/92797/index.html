    I will attempt to answer your question with the understanding that yours is a genuine query, not an attempt to draw anyone into a theological debate.

    I came to know Christ through a gospel rally at Orchard Rd Presbyterian Church.
    I was not in an emotional crisis,
    I was not in pain,
    I was not in financial difficult,
    I was not in need of a miracle.

    Since then,
    I did not speak in tongues,
    Healed anyone or was healed of anything
    Did not become extremely wealthy but lead a comfortable life with my loved ones.

    Is Jesus real to me? Yes. But I cannot make Jesus real to you. Jesus can only be real to you if you let Him.

    I cannot prove to anyone that God exists to them. I am only convinced that God exists for me.

    In the most basic sense, one can either choose to believe that God exist or God does not exist. If at the end of it all, when I die, and that there is no God, it does not matter.
    However, if God exists and I choose not to acknowledge Him when I am alive, I am in deep shit.
    Of course, this explanation is really too simple to explain the concept of faith.

    If there is no God, the argument ends here. No issue, when we are alive, we may be living a lie, but who cares, so what!

    If there is a God, how then we know which religion points to the right God. I really have no answer for this. How then can we please God if God exists?
    By works? What then is good work, and what is inadequate good work? Good work by itself does not acknowledge one's acceptance of the authority of God, it merely points that one is able to be morally correct. So to me, good works is really a poor measure of one's religious affiliation.

    Hence, the Christian faith comes in: the criteria that one must consciously accept the existing of God and the redemptive work of Christ to erase one's sins.

    Then the next sticky question is the one in the other thread: what happens to people who HAS NOT heard the gospel. Theologians have many view to this and while I don't pretend to be a theologian, my opinion is that because an individual DID NOT HAVE THE CHANCE to hear the gospel, God will judge them on a separate yardstick.

    I probably am not able to give you all the answers, because each answer will lead to more questions.

    But personally, as long as I am convinced that my Lord Jesus is real to me is sufficient. His Grace Is Sufficient For Me.
