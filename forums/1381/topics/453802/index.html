    Hello,

    Paul wrote in Romans 12:1-2, "And so, dear brothers and sisters, I plead with you to give your bodies to God because of all he has done for you. Let them be a living and holy sacrifice—the kind he will find acceptable. This is truly the way to worship him. Don’t copy the behavior and customs of this world, but let God transform you into a new person by changing the way you think. Then you will learn to know God’s will for you, which is good and pleasing and perfect."

    All too often much church resources is fixated and invested in that time of worship so that people have a "great time of worship". But if you consider and meditate on the above passage you would realise that worship is not about singing songs. While worship includes prayer and singing of songs to God, it is certainly much more than that, though unfortunately many would associate it with that 20-30 min sequence of prayer-song-prayer that takes place on Sundays and cell meetings.

    Consider again in the NT, when the believers came together, there was no designated worship/song leader, but Paul wrote in 1 Cor 14:26, "Well, my brothers and sisters, let’s summarize. When you meet together, one will sing, another will teach, another will tell some special revelation God has given, one will speak in tongues, and another will interpret what is said. But everything that is done must strengthen all of you." 

    See also http://www.emmausnz.com/openworship.htm

    My suggestion? Share with your group about what worship really is, then sing the songs you have prepared. Whether you open in prayer or close in prayer is not really the point. You can't be faulted for doing it or not for doing it. Worship is not a routine set of things to run by anyway. Then encourage your group to each freely share a song (or two) or a teaching or a testimony when you meet again the next time.
