    As for the lost tribes, there are constantly groups of people who claim to be descendants of the 10 lost tribes. It is difficult to verify if the claims are true. The Assyrians did not keep the 10 northern tribes together but scattered them all over the empire, with the intention of destroying their cultural heritage. In contrast, The babylonians (present day Iraqis) treated the captives from the kingdom of Judah much better...some of them like Daniel got a first-rate education and were allowed to practise their religion to some extent. When the persian king Cyrus allowed them to return, they re-constituted the nation of Israel.

    Did any members of the 10 tribes taken away to Assyria manage to return? I dunno...there were suggestions that some of them did. For example, the Samaritans claim to have descended from Ephraim and Manasseh and they stayed around Samaria, which used to be the capital of the northern kingdom of Israel. They have some practice in common with the Jews but differ in other respects. As you know from the gospels, the Jews hated them although they claim to worship the same God and read from the same Torah.

    I had a discussion with a Jewish friend who is reading history in Oxford University. He is vehement in his belief that the 10 tribes are truly lost and that present day Jews have all descended from the kingdom of Judah. In fact, there are some who are insistent that the term Jews refer only to Judahites, although it has a wider connotation in lay usage.
