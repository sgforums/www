    Couple of points:
    1. The allegation that JK Rowlings is a white witch probably originated from a satirical article from a website called The Onion. This allegation was supported by a email-circulated article that falsed stated that Rowlings made some very derogatory statements regarding Christianity.
    2. As Harry Potter was never intended to be read as a Christian book, the fact that "God" was never mentioned should not even be brought in the discussion.
    3. Whether the practices described in the books are really paganistic or sorcery remains to be seen. Wiccans have made it clear that none of the practices in the books are similar to what they are doing.
    4. The practices described has very little relevance to Chinese occult.

    Firstly let me say that I am not here looking for a fight with anyone. I like and respect this forum and in the spirit of exchanging different ideas I ask for at least a cordial spirit in writing.

    While what I have written above seems that I am a red-blooded HP fan. Let me state that I neither support or is against Rowlings and her writings. I see Rowlings as another novelist out to make some money. She is no different from Robert Ludlum or Tom Clancy.

    Whether any individual likes Harry Potter or not remains as an individual choice. It does not mean that because I have read all her 5 books, I am now a supporter of witchcraft.

    Many anti-HP fans had stated that the books were loaded with various imagery of witchcraft. This I cannot deny. However with just a cursory look at any Brothers Grimm novels, you will that this is nothing new. Each culture will have our own bogeyman and fairies.

    There greater issue is whether such writing will influence the readers or not. If the influence factor exist, then the argument should be: is this factor Satanic or is it a fad?

    My children has seen the movies and read the books but I have not observe any negative behaviour emerging from them. As a matter of fact, my children also like Pokemon (another major bugbear among the churches and Christian school). Why are my children not affected? It is because they are taught from a very young age to discern good and bad, and real and fantasy. They know that their hands will not have the ability to create hooks to climb walls like Spider-man, and that Power Rangers are five guys in rubber suits. They place great priority in church life, prays regularly, learning hard to tithe and generally learning to be an example of a good Christian in school evening garnering various commendations from their teachers.

    Are children obsessed with Hi-Five or Manchester United in less of a danger than other children who read HP? I think not. As long as a child (for that matter, an adult) who is obsessed with one thing other than God, this obsession is just as bad.

    To label HP as evil only is insufficient. This will cause the child not to be able to discern for himself good and evil. As adults we have the responsibility to impart skills, especially discernment, not enforce our own preconceived opinions.

    As a note of interest, there are also Christians who think that Tolkien and LOTR is satanic.... hmm.... there is no pleasing all....
