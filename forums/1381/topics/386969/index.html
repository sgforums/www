    Just a short history tidbit on how we got our Bibles. It had taken nearly 4 centuries before our modern-day Bible were accepted by Christians.

    Btw I am Christian :) Just sharing more about Christian history.


    New Testament Period (c.35-90)

    In this period there is little formal sense of a Canon of Scripture

    Apostolic Fathers (90-160)

    Heretic Macion from Rome introduces a NT Canon to back up his Gnostic beliefs - 2/3 of Luke and all of the Pauline Epistles except for Hebrews.

    Polycarp first uses all four Gospels now in Scripture. Acts scarcely known or quoted. Pauline Corpus generally accepted by 130AD, yet quotations are rarely introduced as scriptural.

    Books not considered canonical: Philippians, 1 Timothy, 2 Timothy, Titus, Philemon, Hebrews, Clement of Rome, James, Peter, Jude, Revelations

    Irenaeus to Origen (160-250)

    Canon: 4 Gospels, Acts, Certain Pauline Epistles, Jude

    Books not considered canonical: 2 Timothy, Philemon, Hebrews, Revelations

    Muratorian Canon 190AD

    Excludes Hebrews, James, 1 Peter, 2 Peter

    Includes The Apocalypse of Peter, Wisdom of Solomon

    Origen to Nicaea (250-325)

    Gospels, Acts, Pauline Corpus Accepted

    Hebrews accepted in the East

    Disputed: Revelations, 2 & 3 John, Jude, 2 Peter, James

    The rest were widely accepted though lack comfirmation.

    Council of Nicaea (325)

    Questions canonicity of James, 2 Peter, 2 John, 3 John, and Jude

    From 325 to the Council of Carthage (397)

    Athanasius first lists our present 27 New Testament books as such in 367. Disputes still persist concerning several books, almost right up until 397, when the Canon is authoritatively closed by the Catholic Church.

    It was also determined that besides the Canonical Scriptures nothing be read in the Church under the title of divine Scriptures. The Canonical Scriptures are these: Genesis, Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers, Deuteronomy, Joshua the son of Nun, Judges, Ruth, four books of Kings, 3 two books of Paraleipomena, 4 Job, the Psalter, five books of Solomon, 5 the books of the twelve prophets, Isaiah, Jeremiah, Ezechiel, Daniel, Tobit, Judith, Esther, two books of Esdras, 6 two books of the Maccabees. Of the New Testament: four books of the Gospels, one book of the Acts of the Apostles, thirteen Epistles of the Apostle Paul, one epistle of the same [writer] to the Hebrews, two Epistles of the Apostle Peter, three of John, one of James, one of Jude, one book of the Apocalypse of John. Let this be made known also to our brother and fellow-priest Boniface, or to other bishops of those parts, for the purpose of confirming that Canon. because we have received from our fathers that those books must be read in the Church. Let it also be allowed that the Passions of Martyrs be read when their festivals are kept.
