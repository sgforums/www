    For a Christian, hell is a state where a person is excluded forever from communion with God through his/her own free choice (eg: by sinning gravely against God and not repenting). As the Catechism of the Catholic Church (CCC 1035) puts it, "The chief punishment of hell is eternal separation from God". Hell might or might not involve fire but regardless, it will be most unpleasant and painful because it is only in God that man can "possess the life and happiness for which he was created and for which he longs." (CCC 1035).

    Your murderer and your japanese general (as with everyone) will have to face God when they die. If they are unrepentent, then they will be eternally separated from God in hell. However, if they have truly repented from their wrong doings, God will welcome them into heaven for there is no sin that is unforgivable. Before entering heaven however, they (and a lot of us as well) are likely to first require 'purification' in a place/condition which the Catholic Church calls 'purgatory' (which comes from a latin word meaning 'cleansing' or 'purifying').
