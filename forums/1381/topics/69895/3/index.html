    You ask, "Why not pray directly to Jesus?" Of course you should pray to Jesus! But that does not mean it is not also a good thing to ask others to pray for one as well. You also ask why should we ask any Christian, in heaven or on earth, to pray for us when we can ask Jesus directly? If we can go straight to Jesus proves that we should ask no Christian in heaven to pray for us then it also proves that we should ask no Christian on earth to pray for us.

    Praying for each other is simply part of what Christians do. As we see, in 1 Timothy 2:1Â–4, Paul strongly encouraged Christians to intercede for many different things, and that passage is by no means unique in his writings. Elsewhere Paul directly asks others to pray for him (Rom. 15:30Â–32, Eph. 6:18Â–20, Col. 4:3, 1 Thess. 5:25, 2 Thess. 3:1), and he assured them that he was praying for them as well (2 Thess. 1:11). Most fundamentally, Jesus himself required us to pray for others, and not only for those who asked us to do so (Matt. 5:44).

    Since the practice of asking others to pray for us is so highly recommended in Scripture, it cannot be regarded as superfluous on the grounds that one can go directly to Jesus. The New Testament would not recommend it if there were not benefits coming from it. One such benefit is that the faith and devotion of the saints can support our own weaknesses and supply what is lacking in our own faith and devotion.
