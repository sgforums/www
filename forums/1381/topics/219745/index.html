    Both [Holy and sanctified] means to set apart in either OT or NT. It is a neutral word actually. Depending on its usage it could have shades of meaning.

    Let me quote the verse:

    1Co 7:14 For the unbelieving husband is HAGIAZO- SANCTIFIED by the wife, and the unbelieving wife is HAGIAZO-SANCTIFIED by the husband; otherwise your children would be unclean, but now they are HAGIOS-holy.

    HAGIAZO IS A VERB. HAGIOS IS AN ADJECTIVE.

    A piece of ground could be made holy.

    Ex 3:5 Then He said, "Do not draw near this place. Take your sandals off your feet, for the place where you stand is holy ground

    Is the Lord then saying here that the ground is saved [from sin]? Certainly not.

    A day could be holy

    Ex 16:23 Then he said to them, "This is what the LORD has said: 'Tomorrow is a Sabbath rest, a holy Sabbath to the LORD. Bake what you will bake today, and boil what you will boil; and lay up for yourselves all that remains, to be kept until morning.'"

    was the Sabbath here saved [from sin]? Certainly not.

    Garments could be made holy

    Ex 28:2 "And you shall make holy garments for Aaron your brother, for glory and for beauty.

    Were the garments here then saved [from sin]? Certainly not

    Things could be holy

    Ex 28:38 "So it shall be on Aaron's forehead, that Aaron may bear the iniquity of the holy things which the children of Israel hallow in all their holy gifts; and it shall always be on his forehead, that they may be accepted before the LORD.

    Were the things here then saved [from sin]? Certainly not

    The third person of the Blessed Trinity is called Holy.

    Does that mean then the the Holy Spirit was saved [from sin]? Certainly not

    You see the word "holy" could be used in a special sense as in being redeemed from sin or it could mean "to be separate from" without special reference to salvation from sin holiness.

    Garments, things , a particular day could be separated unto God without being separated from sin. [These things have no sin in the first place. These things are amoral]

    A particular person could be positioned in a special place of privilege that others may not be in. To be in contact with someone who can share the Word of God [the Gospel] to you is indeed a special place of privilege which others may not have the privilege of. If you are in a family of Christians, God has placed you my friend in a special privileged separated position that others may not be in.

    Grab the oppurtunity!!! Trust in Christ that you may be separated from SIN ACTUALLY. FROM IT'S PENALTY. FROM IT'S POWER AND EVENTUALLY FROM ITS PRESENCE.

    note: notice verse 14 an unbelieving spouse could be made HAGIAZO because of the influence of a believing spouse. But it does not mean that the husband or wife is saved. For Paul wrote in verse 16:

    1Co 7:16 For how do you know, O wife, whether you will save your husband? Or how do you know, O husband, whether you will save your wife?

    I thought they were made Hagiazo already in verse 14? Yes, the Hagiazo there does not mean being saved from sin. It means being placed in a special separated privilege or position. And that goes for the hagios children too.

    The nation of Israel was called a HOLY NATION. BUT NOT ALL OF THEM WERE SAVED. Only those who trusted in the coming Messiah were saved and therefore made holy from sin.
