    your friend has a serious misunderstanding and misconception about the doctrine of the Holy Trinity. True, mainline Christians believe in the doctrine of Trinity- ONE GOD -Father, Son and Holy Spirit.

    I shall quote from Article One of the '39 Articles of Religion' :

    "There is but one living and true God, everlasting, without body, parts, or passions; of infinite power, wisdom, and goodness; the Maker, and Preserver of all things both visible and invisible. And in unity of this Godhead there be three Persons, of one substance, power, and eternity; the Father, the Son, and the Holy Ghost."

    If he truly and fully comprehends the above and have a good foundation of the written Word of God, he will know that Jesus is God. And the concept of Holy Trinity is NOT three slices of Pizzas making up one whole piece of Pizza.

    To summarise:

    1. God is One

    2. Father, Son and Holy Spirit are personally distinct

    3. Father, Son and Holy Spirit are spoken in terms of equal deity and dignity.

    4. When the personhood of each member of the Godhead is denied, the result is modalism. (ie. God reveals Himself in different forms and modes at different times) THIS IS WRONG TEACHING.

    5. When the unity of Godhead is denied, the result is tritheism (3 gods??)

    6. Mentally separating the Father from the Son (or any other combinations) distorts sound biblical teachings about the Father as well as about the Son. All 3 Persons of the Trinity are equally merciful, loving, forgiving, holy, blessed, almighty and etc etc....
    The Divine attributes and reverence to be given are all equal. Not a single of the member is inferior to the other two.

    Lastly, the doctrine of Trinity is the profound mystery of God Himself and any attempt to fully comprehend, dissect and analyse using human mind is to seek the IMPOSSIBLE. There is a limit we can go about our exploration and wat we do know is based on the written word of God as He chose to reveal to us that much.

    Various analogies have been proposed in an attempt to make the Trinity easier to understand, but none is truly adequate.


    Most children who have attended Sunday school have probably heard analogies for Trinity. The Trinity is like a piece of clover: one plant but three leaves.



    Or, the Trinity is like water:it is one thing, but can exist as solid, liquid, or steam. Despite their popularity, such analogies should be AVOIDED. They are misleading. The clover analogy, for instance, implies that the divine nature is divided into three parts. The water analogy teaches modalism.

    (Quoted from 'Doctrine of God' by Dr.Timothy Wiarda, Singapore Bible College)
  • NeonTetra
    5 Mar 04, 00:47
    Another danger is that of mentally separating the Son from the Father. Occasionally, Christians may think of Jesus as tender and forgiving, while picturing the Father in heaven as a stern judge. Such thinking distorts both the truth about the Father(whose love is revealed in Christ) and the truth about the Son(who judges sin every bit as much as the Father).

    A third danger is mentally separating the Spirit from the Son. This kind of thinking appears in various forms, particularly in context where debates and uncertainties about the works of the Holy Spirit. Some Christians are well taught about Christ, and confident in their relationship with Him, may feel that they do not really need the Holy Spirit. Others may feel that they have Christ, but now need something additional, the Holy Spirit. Some seek the Spirit's blessings without a strong sense that these are received from Christ, or speak of remarkable spiritual experiences that seems to have little connection with Christ. All such ways of thinking run counter to biblical teaching, in which the work of the Spirit and the work of Christ are always inseparably bound together.

    (Quoted from 'Doctrine of God' by Dr. Timothy Wiarda, Singapore Bible College)
