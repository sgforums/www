    Since we have a topic on false prophets, let us established what is the office of Apostles and Prophets

    one thing we know, these are offices in the Body of Christ (ie. Church ) given to perform specific tasks, to edify, build, and guide the Church (ie. believers), and these 'office bearers' are appointed and annointed by God, not men.

    In the Early church, they are very active as they goes about their tasks.

    In the modern church context, is there still a place for these office?

    This is my personal opinion, In the past,
    Prophets (mainly mentioned in OT) were needed to proclaim the word of God to His people in regard to the things God wants them to do or to follow or the things that will be coming.

    Apostles (mainly in NT) on the other hand went about teaching, guilding and prononcing the good news of the gospel, and about the things that Christ had tell and/or show them, being the witness to His resurrection.

    One thing they do have in common is that God confirms their appointment through signs and wonders.

    In the current church context, is there still a need for them? does God still appoints such office bearers?
    There are many differing views on this, lets have a meaningful discussion on this topic (and if anyone feels needed, can also discuss on the type of things they do in the past as well)

    God bless

  • SingaporeMacross
    29 Apr 06, 23:24
    Originally posted by nightzip:
    so what's the job scope of the Bishop, the Deacon and the Priest? Any differences?
    The Deacon has the basic powers of Holy Orders, and is allowed preach and confer blessings. Deacons aid the Priests and Bishops in any way that is needed. Deacons also serve the people, especially the poor and the needy. The Deacons also distributes Communion. They are like a bridge between the clergy and the laity.

    The Priest has more powers. He is allowed to say Mass, hear confessions and absolve sins in the name of Christ. The Priest is always on standby for sick call, bringing Christ's peace to the those in their last moments.

    The Bishop exercises the fullness of Holy Orders. The Bishop is the head of a group of parishes in a certain geographical area. He has the authority to ordain Deacons and Priests. All clergy and laity are subordinate to their Bishop. St Ignatius says, "Where the Bishops are, there is the Catholic Church."

    In any case, one thing is certain: All clergy must be ready to die for their flocks, if need be. We must pray for our clergy always.
