    For those who are interested, the following article discusses the Galileo controversy (whether the earth was at the center of the solar system [geocentricity] or whether the sun was at the center [heliocentricity]:

    http://www.catholic.com/library/galileo_controversy.asp

    Interesting points from the article (for those too lazy to read the article ) :

    • During Galileo's time, the geocentric view was widely held to be true by the scientific community at large and not just the Church. 
    • Contrary to popular belief, Galileo did not manage to prove heliocentricity beyond reasonable doubt because he could not answer the strongest argument against heliocentricity which was made nearly two thousand years earlier by Aristotle. The measuring equipment of Galileo's day did not allow him to do so.
    • It was a good thing the Church did not rush to embrace Galileo’s views, because it turned out that his ideas were not entirely correct either. Galileo believed that the sun was not just the fixed center of the solar system but the fixed center of the universe. We now know that the sun is not the center of the universe and that it does move — it simply orbits the center of the galaxy rather than the earth.
    • In Galileo's time, the Church had just gone through the Reformation experience and was wary of controversy. Theologians were not prepared to entertain a layman re-interpreting scripture based on the heliocentric theory when he had not proved the new theory beyond reasonable doubt. The Catholic Church has since acknowledged that Galileo's condemnation was wrong (and has even issued two stamps of Galileo  ).
    • We have to remember that the Church's mission is to guide people to heaven; and not to make mathematicians, scientists or astronomers out of people. The Church can be wrong when it comes to these matters but not in matters of faith and morals.

    Separately, you don't have to worry about choosing between believing in the teachings of Christ and in the official teachings of the Church* . They are one and the same. Jesus established the Church on earth and granted it authority to teach faith and morals in his name as is made clear in the bible, eg: Luke 10:16 - "whoever hears you, hears me. Whoever rejects you, rejects me."; 1 Tim. 3:15 - the Church being "the pillar and foundation of truth"; Mathew 18-17 - the Church being the final arbiter on disputes in faith and morals and being given the authority to bind and to loose.  

    * not those of a single priest, another Catholic or a non-Catholic making claims on what the Catholic Church teaches; which might or might not be accurate.
