    I will try, on the assumption that this is not a flame-bait.

    Originally posted by technoboy:
    1) How do you guys know whether the things written in the bible is correct? Especially when you all claim that it is written more then 2000 years ago?
    There are many ways to look at this. The most common one for Christians and the one least acceptable to non-Christians, I think, is by faith. Therefore, I will not use this as a reason. When we look at the culture written about those periods, there have been cities, such as Ur of the Chaldees (where Abraham was said to have come from) that was not presumed to have existed. Archaelogical evidence have discovered that there is sucha place. So if all other issues are unacceptable to non-Christians, at least the historical part has been proven. Note, too, that most documentaries produced by non-Christians do not actually doubt the "HISTORY" of the Biblical stories, only the "DIVINITY" of the stories.

    Originally posted by technoboy:
    2) Where did the world come from? God created it? So who created god? And who created the one who created god?
    Again, the question is directed to the Christian world view: yes, God created the world. There have been some theories that perhaps, Earth is NOT the only world the God has created (but such theories do not affect the theology that is of this world). Who created God? The Bible said that God was never created. God existed from the beginning..... but this kind of question goes round and round, and unless one accept that God existed from the beginning, no one has the answer.

    Originally posted by technoboy:
    3) Why is it that people who doesn't believe in Christianity will go to hell? Is there even such a place? So those infants who died will go to hell immediately, since they are not even able to think and choose what religions to believe in?
    Again another question to the Christian worldview.... What is hell? Is it the opposite of heaven? Or is hell a place that bad people go. Note also that the
    "hell concept" is not exclusive to the Christian faith. Most other faith has such a place. It might be a place, or it might be a vivid description of the destruction of the soul, eternally.

    Originally posted by technoboy:
    4) I think that Christianity is just one of the many religions in the world, so why do Christians have to say that the other religions are not real whereas theirs are? Isn't that an insult to the other religions?
    Realistically, there are only two ways to this:
    1. Worship whoever you want, because all religion leads to the same God.
    2. Pick "MY" religion, because it's the only one that will bring you to the truth.
    Each religion will fall into one of the above categories.
    Yup, I have heard even pastors preaching that other religions are not real etc etc. From the theological stand point, it's in accordance with Biblical teachings. However, as an individual, would I SAY it out: probably not, because, as you had said, it is quite insulting (but this is a personal conviction), no different from, say, I think you are ugly, but I won't say it to you in the face.
