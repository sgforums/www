    This is an EXELLENT question. I can declare right here right now that tithing IS sanctified by God and when you tithe, you will surely reap.

    Lets look at Malachi, the last chapter of the O.T.

    "Will a man rob God? Yet you have robbed me.You say,how have we robbed you? In tithes and offerings. You have said: 'it is useless to keep God's ordinance' , yet you say ' how have we robbed you?'"

    God's ordinance here was in reference to the concept of tithes and offerings. Now, why would God say 'you have robbed me" if it was an act out of our "respect" for God?

    In the above chapter still in Malachi, God says :" Bring all the tithes into the storehouse, that there may be food in my house."

    The keyword here is "Bring". Normally, when someone says "Give me a car", it means that you own the car, and whether you give or not its up to you.  But when he says "Bring me the car", please dont say to another friend afterwards :" I gave him that car".    He'll say : Eh, come here come here... What you mean you gave me that car? You brought back my car!" 

    Am i understandable so far?

    "but what good does it do to follow this ordinance?"    God says, "first bring the tithe in, and TEST ME NOW IN THIS! See if I will not throw open the windows of blessing upon you."

    This is the only one time God says "Test me". How many believe this verse?

    "Jesus didnt preach tithing leh? Doesnt that mean its outdated?"

    Wrong. First I must make it clear that only believers can tithe. When Jesus was still alive, He could not ask people to tithte because He had not yet died for their sins. IF they tried to tithe, it would be regarded as a "debt payment" and not a tithe.  But at the cross, AFTER He paid for all our sins, once and for all, we are free to tithe!

    but there were cases of people tithing when He was still on earth. I refer to Zacchaeus, the rich tax collecter whom Jesus cried out to" I must come and eat at your house". Jesus showed him what pure undeserved favor was like and the result of that revelation is that Zacchaeus went out, and returned all that he cheated 4 times the amount, and gave half his possesions to the poor.  Only grace can lead a man to tithe. 

    For proof, the previous chapter, there was a rich young ruler. He asked Jesus " Teacher, what must I do to get eternal life? "  He regarded Jesus not as Saviour, but merely as Teacher. So Jesus gave him the "standard" answer. Actually this guy not bad..  of all the commandments, he already keep all, short of 1. Just one leh..  "Give all you have to the poor and follow me."  what happens when Jesus showed him the Law? The poor boy could not even give one coin. But under grace, Zachaeus opened up his coffers.

     

    So therefore, tithing and offerings cannot fully function by the law. Where do i get my proof? Lets go all the way back to Genesis. The story now is that there were some kings who were invading the land. The leader of those kings was Kerdorlaomer, King of Elam, which is present day Iran.  This king of Iran conqured their enemies and looted the cities, and guess who was in one of them? Abraham's nephew, Lot! So they carried Lot and all that he had away too.

    When Abraham heard of this, he took up all 318 fighting men in his house and hunted the kings down. Abraham took back Lot and his things, plus the defeated kings and their possession(including women and children) PLUS the invading army's plunder. Wow..  Thats ALOT of gold and silver and slaves...

    Then King of Sodom( an evil king for sure,just look at the name), one of the defeated kings, came to Abraham and said " Keep the gold, just give me the people."  Abraham said, "I will not take anything from you, not even a shoelace, lest you say I made abraham rich. Just give my men their share." 

    Then, another King appeared. This was King Melchizedek( his name means King Of Righteousness). In his hand was the Holy Communion elements. Abraham tithed unto Melchizedek. Eh? This was the VERY first direct mention of the tithe. But this happened more than 400 years BEFORE the law. So tithing and the law are two seperate things!
