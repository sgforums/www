    Originally posted by Eric Cartman:
    Not even that of God, right? What about pictures? What about that image of God in your mind?
    You need to quote your source for "No engraven idols".

    In the first place, there is no such word as "engraven" in the English language. There is "graven" and there is "engrave", both words meaning the act of carving out of a solid material.

    It is likely that your source is Deuteronomy 5:8 or Exodus 20:4 which should go something like:
    You shall not carve idols for yourselves in the shape of anything in the sky above or on the earth below or in the waters beneath the earth;

    The commandment is specifically on the making of a physical representation of God through the carving of stone or wood (these materials are common in those periods).

    The question that you are asking is specifically on the development of an image of God in your mind (ie, the mental stage). There is no specific command in the Bible about developing a mental image of God..... but because we don't know how God looks like, we CANNOT develop this image.

    Because your question is specifically targetted to Christians, you can safely assume that no Christian worth his salt will develop this mental image. This is not to say that a follower of another philosophy or faith does not do this act of developing this image.
