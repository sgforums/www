    Well for prayer many people think prayer should be beautiful verbally...that's why so many people do not feel confident leading a pray.

    A prayer first and foremost should be an intimate experience.

    Meaning that they should be done mostly in private: away from other people and in silent. Prayers are heart talk to God, that's why they are sacred and ideally should be kept in private. Let no one see you when you pray or try to pray in privacy. Let no one knows the consolation that you get when praying. Let no one know what you're praying about unless it is necessary like in a group praying. Or even one should continue praying even when he doesn't get any consolation in praying. Faith doesn't expect mere sensations.

    Of course there's that thing called group praying where a man may lead a pray of a group of people with a loud voice but the essence or the fundamental of praying imo is that it is an intimate personal relation with God and should ideally be kept private. A whisper of the heart is all that it takes. Prayer should come from the bottom of the heart not from mere mouth.

    Anyway it's just my personal opinion on praying. I can't lead a pray well too. Too shy for most of the time. But I know it's wrong to be concern with that aspect of praying that it should be beautiful verbally as that's trying as if to impress men instead of communing with God.
