    They're adaptations to various environments with afforded those who had them the advantage in survival to pass on their genes... over time the local related population will then start to take on these traits because these genes are sucessful in spreading themselves.

    To suggest race scientifically is to suggest a different species, this is not the case given people from different "races' can obviously breed and have fertile offspring.

    As for why Singapore and China Chinese look the same, we're barely three generations in, lol, hardly enough time for local change to take place. Additionally modern technology, travel, as well as general mixing will mean that such selective pressures from nature would be less pronounced.

    However take away technology, reduce us back to a level that puts us more at the whim of nature, take away easy travel and have isloated population that have to breed among themselves, you'll soon see the emergence of a new "race". But this is still very human.
