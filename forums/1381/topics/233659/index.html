    One school of thought is that the Genesis 1 account of creation is different from Genesis 2 account.

    In Genesis 1:27-28
    So God created man in his own image, in the image of God he created him; male and female he created them.
    God blessed them and said to them, "Be fruitful and increase in number; fill the earth and subdue it. Rule over the fish of the sea and the birds of the air and over every living creature that moves on the ground."
    This group of human just went out to the earth and populate it.

    In Genesis 2:7-8
    the LORD God formed the man from the dust of the ground and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life, and the man became a living being. Now the LORD God had planted a garden in the east, in Eden; and there he put the man he had formed.
    This man is Adam and the account that follows is about Eve, Cain, Abel and Seth.

    It is quite obvious that in Genesis 4:14 that there are other humans in the vicinity.
    Today you are driving me from the land, and I will be hidden from your presence; I will be a restless wanderer on the earth, and whoever finds me will kill me."
    It is unlikely, from the implication of this verse, that these people mentioned by Cain are other offsprings of Adam and Eve.

    There are many opinions as to HOW Genesis is to be read:
    Literal?
    Figurative?

    My personal opinion is that there are two groups of people, one the general population of earth, and the other, a special chosen group tasked to look after the gardenof eden who did not do a good job. It is also my opinion that this special group is written figuratively, ie, representing the people of God who has chosen to disobeyed Him.

    ...but as someone else has said, go talk to your pastor (assuming you attend church), and I don't believe any pastor worth his salt will turn you away.
