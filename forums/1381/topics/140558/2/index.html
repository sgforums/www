    Christ died of crucifixion, a method of execution invented by the Persians. It was adopted by the Roman army. The cross of crucifixion exists in several shapes. One in the form of the low tau (commonly known as the latin cross), the high tau. Sometimes a natural tree is used.

    Originally posted by Icemoon:
    Hey why in Acts they say Jesus died on a tree?

    Acts 5:30, 10:39, 13:29 and 1 Peter 2:24

    Well, for starters the Greek word for "Tree" is xulon which apparently can take to mean also clubs (for whacking), stocks (for locking up) and wood. Herein, it is must be said that a good understanding (or at least the desire for a good research) is necessary when reading the English Bible, which we all know was translated from Hebrew, Aramic and Greek.

    While the crucifixion is a cruel and unusual punishment in the Roman world, death by hanging on a tree is considered a taboo in the Jewish world. It is very likely that the reason for the word "tree" being used five times (one more in Galatians) is either a translation issue, or even possibly that Paul and Peter had wanted to elaborate the psychological state of Jesus' death in its humiliation and embarrasement to the Jewish culture.

    Of course we can choose to continue to read only in English and nitpick at all issues we don't understand and refusing to take in contextual, translational and cultural factors.
