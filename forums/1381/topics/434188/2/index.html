    I see.  I read about this form of 'logic' somewhere.  Even if we don't bring in the creator-God - without a doubt, we all can agree that evil is NOT good, not the absence of good.

    The trick question is: if one is to answer that evil is the absence of good, then the creator-God will be exonerated.  If one is to say that evil is the opposite of good, then one must begin with 'good', otherwise how to have opposite?  And vice versa.

    There is also a flaw in the statement 'evil is the absence of good'.  If good is absent, there is 'no' good to begin with.  So how do you fathom evil?

    You see what logically trick can do to those who lacked intellectual capacity to reason.  Someone who wants to justify that evil is not created will say evil is the 'absence of good' and these people will be sold!

    The point I want to make is this: anyone who believes there is a First Cause cannot find excuse that there is another Cause.  The Christian argument is this: God created 'good', it is Satan who created evil.  This mean there are two Causes!
