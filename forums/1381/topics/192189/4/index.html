    So tell me, what is the purpose of this PURGATORY?
    Purgatory is from the Latin word meaning 'purification'.

    This doctrine does not deny that salvation is completely the word of God and based on His Mercy through Christ.

    Take a look at yourself and the many sincere, devout Christians that you know. How many of us can honestly say that we have already attain the same nature as God? After accepting Christ, do we not still have our faults and shortcomings? Do we not fall into sin from time to time?

    Catholics believe that salvation means eternal life in communion with the Holy Trinity. Since the Trinity is perfection, it is not possible that there be communion with that which is imperfect.

    If one believes that many people who are saved are still imperfect when they die, then this doctrine makes sense as it means that there is a process of purification to build up the soul's capacity to handle eternal life. To use an analogy, if a person has been locked up in a completely dark room for years, exposing him to light suddenly make harm his eyes. Similarly, the soul that has not already been perfected in this life will not have the capacity to handle the communion with the Holy Trinity.

    Whether purgatory makes sense or not depends on your belief in what salvation consists. Some accept Luther's 'snow-covered dung hill' position of inputed righteousness, that Christ's grace covers up our sins without actually changing the state of our souls. In this schema, there is indeed no need for purgatory.

    The Catholic position is that the grace of Christ is an actual infusion of supernatural life into a soul that has accepted His mercy. And since the soul continues to live in this world, it can diminish or even use the grace through its own sins, thus requiring constant forgiveness and repentence. In this schema, purgatory makes sense.
