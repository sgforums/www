    When the OT (eg. Ex 20:5, Ex 34:14) describes God as a jealous God, it uses the hebrew word Qana. The particular form of the word was used only to describe God, it was never used to describe human's emotion. This is the first clue that God's jealousy is different from what we understand of human's jealousy.

    Elsewhere, another form of the word Qana, was translated as zealous. So when the word jealous is used to describe God, it conveys the idea of "protective love to guard one's beloved". Think "jealous for" not "jealous of"

    When God is described as jealous, it is in the context of warning the Israelites against worship of foreign gods (idols). God is being protective of His chosen people.
