    The brit mila (meaning ritual circumcision) is always held during the eighth day after the child is born. For example, if the baby is born on 1 Aug (monday), his bris will be held on 9 Aug (tues).

    The Jews view circumcision as a religious ceremony, similar to Christian's infant baptism. Thus, it is recommended that a Mohel perform the circumcision. A Mohel is a Jew who has been trained in the physical procedures of circumcision and understands the religious signficance of the ritual. If a mohel is not available, then a Jewish physician can perform the circumcision. It is customary to invite a rabbi to conduct the service.

    After the circumcision, the baby will be given his name (definitely a hebrew name) in the prayer.
