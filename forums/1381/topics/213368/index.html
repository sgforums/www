    Let me elaborate.

    In the beginning there was one Church. It was founded by Jesus Christ with St Peter as the first Pope, the Vicar of Christ. The phrase 'Catholic Church' was coined by St Ignatius of Antioch in 107, who wrote: "Where the bishop appears, there let the people be, just as where Jesus Christ is, there is the Catholic Church."

    In 1054 AD, the Great Schism took place. The Church split into half, East and West. The Eastern Church called themselves the Orthodox. There were differences over culture, language and certain doctrines. The Orthodox did not accept the authority of the Pope over all Christians. Despite this, both Catholicism and Orthodoxy recognise the Sacraments, Veneration of the Saints and have much in common. Some Orthodox Churches finally conceded and were reunited with Rome. They are called Eastern Rite Catholics.

    In the 16th Century, the Protestant Reformation took place. The Church was relatively corrupt during that time of history. It first sparked off in Germany by a former Augustinian monk Martin Luther. He tried to reform the Church, but was fed up, and so he left to set up his own church. It soon spread to other parts of Europe, and these Christians were now known as Protestants.

    As time went by, these Protestants disagreed even amongst themselves, and as a result split into hundreds of different churches. Orthodoxy was also subject to many divisions. The Catholic Church remains as Church which has stood the test of time for Jesus Christ promised that the gates of hell would not prevail against Her.
