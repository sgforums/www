    Today (25 Feb) is Ash Wednesday. It marks the beginning of the Lentern season up to Good Friday. Lent is the name given to the 40 days plus 6 Sundays before
    Easter.

    A time of repentance and reflection on the Passion of Christ, of abstinence and spiritual renewal.

    Lent is a season of soul-searching and repentance. It is a season for reflection and taking stock. Lent originated in the very earliest days of the Church as a preparatory time for Easter, when the faithful rededicated themselves and when converts were instructed in the faith and prepared for baptism. By observing the forty days of Lent, the individual Christian imitates JesusÂ’ withdrawal into the wilderness for forty days. Lent has been observed in the church since apostolic times.

    Freedom For Me

    "The Spirit of the Lord is on me, because...He has sent me to proclaim freedom for the prisoners and recovery of sight for the blind, to release the oppressed" (Luke 4:18 ).

    Most of us don't like to think about our own death. Before He was born, the Savior knew that His final hours would be spent on the Cross, dying to free us from sin. It helps me understand Jesus' sacrifice when I consider the story of another man who sacrificed his life, years ago, in Ragenbach, Germany. A crowd of people had assembled at an inn with the village blacksmith sitting near the door. When a mad dog rushed in, the smith grabbed the animal and held him down. Although the dog violently attacked him, he held on while his neighbors escaped. By the time the dog was shot, the man had already suffered mortal wounds. He consoled his friends saying, "Don't weep for me. I've only done my duty. Pray that God will not let me suffer long." Then the smith chained his body to an anvil and said, "Now you are safe. I cannot hurt you when I go mad. Bring me food while I am well, and keep out of my reach when I am mad. The rest I leave with God." His friends mourned him when he died nine days later.

    There are similarities between the blacksmith's sacrifice and Christ's, but there are more differences. The Ragenbach smithy died for his friends, while Jesus offered Himself for His enemies. The blacksmith's offering was admired by those around him, while Jesus was, and still is, unappreciated by millions of those for whom He died. The blacksmith reacted without thinking. If he had foreseen the events of that day, he would have protected his friends in some easier way. But Jesus was always a voluntary participant in the Father's plan of redemption. For our Lord, there was no alternate avenue, no possible modification of God's blueprint. This Ash Wednesday, I give thanks for a Lord who was anointed to die for the poor, the imprisoned, the blind, the oppressed andÂ…me.

    Prayer:

    Dear Jesus, May I ever be mindful that You willingly lived and died to free me. Thank You. Amen.

    A Doctor For My Sin

    "On hearing this, Jesus said to them, 'It is not the healthy who need a doctor, but the sick. I have not come to call the righteous, but sinners'" (Mark 2:17)

    When it comes to our health, all of us want the best medicine and doctors that money can buy. Most of us would say, "expense is no object." Thus, you might be surprised by the attitude of one well-respected physician. He found, as he became older and weaker, that his time and energy became more valuable. When a potential patient called for an appointment, he had an initial consultation. At that visit, he asked, "Are you sure that you have money to pay for this procedure?" Most didn't hesitate to say, "Absolutely." To those he suggested, "Since you have the resources, please go to another doctor. I am too weak to take care of a patient who has money." If the patient had no money, the doctor saw them at once.

    Jesus was also a physician who only had time to help those who had nowhere else to go. He had little time or patience for those who thought they could buy God's forgiveness with their own good deeds. Our Lord entered this world to heal our sin-sick souls. During the three years of His ministry, Jesus was constantly surrounded by social outcasts. They entrusted their lives into His heavenly hands. Today, Jesus stands before me and asks, "Do you think you are able to pay for your salvation?" If I said yes, He would challenge me to face my weakness, my inability to measure up to God's righteousness. But I know I am a sinner, helpless and hopeless without Him. So Jesus says, "Come on in. There is no waiting room." Then, looking at every sin I have committed, examining the worst of my spiritual maladies, He prescribes a medicine which creates heavenly health. The Savior says to me, "Be washed in My blood and you will be saved."

    Prayer:

    Dear Jesus, forgive me for those times when I have trusted home remedies to cure me of my sin. Allow me to trust You, my personal Physician, Who takes away my sin. In Your Name. Amen.

    A Full Life For Me

    "The thief comes only to steal and kill and destroy; I have come that they may have life, and have it to the full" (John 10:10)

    Jesus said He had come to die so that I might have a full life. That is a wonderful gift! In World War I, a young man named Charlie was dying on the battlefield. Another soldier stopped to provide what comfort he could. In appreciation, Charlie gave him a piece of paper saying, "My father is a man of wealth in Detroit. Take him this note and he will repay you for this kindness." The note said, "Dear father, the bearer of this letter made my last moments easier and helped me to die. Help him for Charlie's sake." After the war, the battlefield Samaritan, finding no available work, went to the father. Because of his hobo-like appearance, he was denied admission to the house and simply handed over the little soiled piece of paper. When the father saw his son's handwriting, everything changed. He put at the veteran's disposal everything money could buy. Charlie's name gave the soldier a good life.

    There are, of course, noticeable contrasts between the actions of an earthly father and that of our Heavenly Father. For example, no earthly father would send his son into a battle if he knew that boy would certainly die. No earthly father would allow his son to be killed so that someone else might have an abundant life. Yet, that is exactly what God did for me. Into a world that had repeatedly rejected His messengers, God sent His Son. Unlike Charlie's friend, we often don't even appreciate the sacrifice God made in Jesus. The Lamb of God carried the punishment that we deserved. He has given us everything His blood could buy. The Savior says to me, "I have come so you might have life and have it to the full."

    Prayer:

    Dear Jesus, You have given me a full life even though I don't deserve it, and often don't appreciate it. Fill my heart with gratitude for Your gift. In Your Name. Amen.

    What Would They Say About Me?

    "When she poured this perfume on my body, she did it to prepare me for burial. I tell you the truth, wherever this gospel is preached throughout the world, what she has done will also be told, in memory of her" (Matthew 26:12-13)

    Jesus said to me that it's right for His followers to do something to glorify Him. We certainly don't want to be like the husband who refused to open the car door for his wife. "It is sort of a sissy thing to do. Besides," he said, "My wife has two perfectly good arms." That unappreciative attitude spilled over to many areas of his marriage, and common courtesies became quite uncommon. That's the way things were until the wife died, and the heartbroken husband realized how much he loved her. On the day of her funeral, the pallbearers carried her casket to the car. In a minor mix-up, the husband and his children reached the hearse first. The funeral director whispered to the husband, "John, open the car door for her, won't you?" John reached for the door handle and then he stopped. He sobbed as he realized that this would be the first, and last, time he would perform this small gesture for the lady he loved.

    That story reminds me of all the times I could have done something to praise my Savior and chose not to. Unlike the woman who came to Jesus with perfume, tears, and a repentant heart, I come expecting Him to be good to me, to save me. I take His precious sacrifice for granted-half-heartedly worshipping, inattentively praying, serving without sacrifice. That should change. Not because I want to be remembered, but because the Savior who fulfilled the law for me, suffered for me, died and rose for me, deserves it. It should change because the Savior should be remembered. "Glorify the Lord," the Savior says to me.

    Prayer:

    Dear Lord Jesus, forgive me for giving You the leftovers of my life and loyalty. Allow me to see and appreciate Your sacrifice and give it the honor it deserves. In Your Name. Amen.

Years before the Savior was born in Bethlehem, a prince came to Athens seeking a position of service under the successful Athenian admiral, Cimon. So that he might be assured of a favorable audience, he brought a container filled with gold, and another overflowing with silver. He placed both at Cimon's feet. Cimon smiled and asked, "Sir, what would you prefer? To have me as your paid mercenary, or your friend?"

    Would I Keep Quiet?

    "'I tell you,' He replied, 'if they keep quiet, the stones will cry out'"
    (Luke 19:40)

    Jesus said that if I do not praise Him, the stones would shout their own hallelujahs. He was right. I've seen it, or something like it, happen. In the nine years I knew Margaret, there wasn't a single winter that she wasn't hospitalized for pneumonia or some other major ailment. But we could count on her to be in church the Sunday immediately following her release. Margaret had to praise the Lord! When her foot was amputated, she did not become a shut-in. The Sunday after she left the hospital, her husband wheeled her into church. She wore the kind of smile that told everybody that Jesus was alive and well-and at our church. The Sunday we had the worst Minnesota snowstorm of the season, church attendance was halved. But Margaret was there, wearing her new prosthetic foot and that priceless smile. She simply had to praise her Savior.

    I've known other people like Margaret. People whose life becomes a song of praise to the Lord. But most of us are not like that. We're the kind of people who yell at the TV during a football game and will give a standing ovation at an exceptional concert, but at church we prefer not draw attention to ourselves. We want the choir to sing their praises while we sit back and rate the music according to the degree of difficulty. We're not against praising God, but we're not exactly for it either. Jesus would not have been content with silence that day He rode into Jerusalem, and He is probably not happy with it now. The change our living Lord makes in our lives is worthy of shouting from the housetops, not whispering in the closets. Jesus says to me, "If you keep quiet, the stones will cry out."

    Prayer:

    Dear Lord, forgive me for being a silent, spectating Christian. Make me a voice to witness to others what You have done. In Your Name. Amen.

    Could Jesus Say This To Me?

    "May you never bear fruit again!" (Matthew 21:19)

    Jesus said to me that I should bear fruit, witnessing to His life in me. In the Civil War, General Lee invaded the North and was approaching Gettysburg, Pennsylvania. Hearing of the Confederate's movement, a Dutch lady in Gettysburg, Hannah, grabbed the fireplace poker and started down the road to confront the invaders. She did not get to fight, but after the war, the ladies of Gettysburg teased her, "Hannah, what in the world did you expect to do with that old poker against the entire Southern army?" Hannah replied, in her Pennsylvania Dutch accent, "Vell, I dint expect to do nodings, but I left em know what side I vas on, din't I!" ("Well, I didn't expect to do anything, but I let them know what side I was on, didn't I!" )

    So strongly did the Lord feel about producing fruit, that He showed little patience with those slackers who tried to minimally serve two masters, or no masters. He cursed neutrality. He was sad when the rich ruler left without a commitment (Matthew 19:16-22). Jesus wants dedication from those who meet Him. He expects us to produce fruit, to show what side we're on. Jesus doesn't have much use for people who treat the wonderful things He has done in their lives as if they were of no consequence. He knows indifference is the devil's ally.

    If Jesus had been in that Gettysburg parlor, He would have been pleased with Hannah. Jesus always liked the idea of people showing whose side they were on. That's why He praised the thief on the cross who made a confession of belief (Luke 23:42-43) and the woman who washed His feet with her hair (Luke 7:36-50). They showed their appreciation of His mission and His sacrifice. The work He has done to save our souls should never leave anyone fruitless. Jesus should never have to say to me, "May you never bear fruit again!"

    Prayer:

    Dear Lord, too often, like the fig tree, I have looked good on the outside, but produced no results that will nourish others. Change my shortcomings. In Your Name. Amen.

    Drinking From The Cup

    "'You don't know what you are asking,' Jesus said to them. 'Can you drink the cup I am going to drink?'" (Matthew 20:22)

    Jesus said to me, "Can you drink the cup I am going to drink?"

    "I've been a good Christian for quite a few years," I replied.

    "You didn't answer My question. Can you drink My cup?" That's when I thought about a time, years ago, when the doomed ship Empress of Ireland set sail. Among the passengers were 130 Salvation Army officers. The ship sank, and while it was going down, the crew discovered that there weren't enough life jackets for everyone. Rescuers found 109 of the 130 Salvation Army officers dead because each had strapped their lifejacket onto someone else. Could I drink the cup of those Salvation Army officers? How about the cup of the men and women who died as martyrs in ancient Rome? Can I drink the cup of the congregation in Bangladesh where 10 believers were killed in a bomb blast? If I doubt my ability to drink from the cup of fellow humans who have paid dearly for their faith, how can I even consider drinking from my Savior's cup?

    It would be impossible. Only Jesus could do it. He kept the laws, when I regularly break them. He gave freely, while I am selfish. He was silent in the face of false accusations, when I would have loudly defended myself. Jesus died for those who neither loved nor liked Him. I have difficulty contributing to the welfare of my own brothers and sisters. No, I cannot drink the cup of unjust accusations, unfair trials, and painful death except through the power of Jesus Christ. I give thanks for a suffering Savior Who drank pain here below, so I might have joy up above. When Jesus says, "Can you drink My cup?" I say, "Dear Lord, only by Your power."

    Prayer:

    Dear Jesus, forgive me when I think that I am virtuous and good. I am the most miserable of sinners and rely on Your mercy. Empower me to live more like You. In Your Name. Amen.

    Jesus asked me, "Do you recognize God's claim upon you, even as you do that of your country?" I pray I do.
