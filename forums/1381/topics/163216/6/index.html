    what's systematic theology?
    systematic theology is an organized, wholistic and systematic study of what the whole Bible teaches about a particular topic.

    Major topics in systematic theology usually consists of eight:

    1) Doctrine of God [what the whole Bible teaches about God]
    2) Doctrine of Jesus Christ
    3) Doctrine of the Holy Spirit
    4) Doctrine of Man
    5) Doctrine of Sin
    6) Doctrine of Salvation
    7) Doctrine of the Church
    8] Doctrine of Last Things [Future end time events]

    A word of warning: There are systematic 'theologies' so-called that are not really systematic 'theologies'. There are liberals who profess to be Christians who wrote heretical books.

    Who are liberals? They are so called 'scholars' who deny every major doctrine in the Bible.

    1) They deny that Christ really died for our sins [denial of his atonement, of his being Savior]

    2) They deny Christ's resurrection. Saying it's just a myth.

    3) They deny miracles in the Bible.

    4) They deny the inerrancy of the Bible and etc.

    REcommended systematic theology books:

    1) Henry Theissen's Systematic Theology
    2) Charles Ryrie ST
    3) Lois Berkhoff ST
    4) George Ladd ST
    5) A. H. Strong ST
    6) Lewis Sperry Chafer ST
    7) Oliver Buswell ST
