    Did someone ask for a professional view of this matter?

    The classification of homosexuality as a disorder was changed in DSM-III because of the definition of "disorder". A defining aspect of a disorder is that it generates distress in the individual. Since homosexuality in itself does not create distress, it is not a disorder. What does create distress is either:

    1) a disparity between one's self-image and one's sexual orientation
    2) getting fed up with self-righteous assholes who give gays a whole lot of grief

    As such, homosexuality per se no longer fit the definition of a disorder and its classification was changed.

    This is not to say that homosexuality is entirely excluded from the diagnostic process. DSM-IV still accommodates for distress that results as a side effect of a person's sexual orientation.

    Of course, if you wanted to extrapolate all the way, the same section of DSM-IV would apply to a straight person being distressed by living in a predominantly gay community.

    I'm sure the religiously oriented will have plenty to say about the classification process, and I expect that many members of the APA woul have contested the reclassification back in the 1970's, but that the end of the day, practicioners deal with what is, not what should be.
