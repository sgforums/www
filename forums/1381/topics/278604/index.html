    It depends on the congregation and the pastoral direction.

    Some pastors feel that cell groups are extremely important and is doctrinal and WILL quote the Book of Acts for the justification for cell groups.

    Other pastors feel that cell groups are good and participation is encouraged.

    More and more churches will embrace the CG philosophy as it is a good tool to oversee their congregation as population increases and pastoral team size is reduced.

    You need to decide for yourself if the CG movement is doctrinal or not (ie whether there is any biblical backing)

    Personally I don't think that the CG movement is a biblical teaching. This is not to say that the CG movement is NOT USEFUL. It is an extremely useful tool to have in a church, but the church need to be careful to label it correctly: whether it is a biblical teaching or as a programme.

    I use to be a CG leader but I no longer attend any CG and will not, in the foreseable future be attending any.

    In the context of Acts, the young church do not really have a focussed meeting point. In this portion of Acts that are used to justify CGs is really the church in it's infancy, where believers met at each other's homes.
    As the years go by, churches are formed ie, the various churches that Paul travelled to + the churches named by John in Revelation.

    When we read (and intepret scripture) we need to draw the line as to whether when something is said, is it a doctrinal teaching or a cultural comment. So when scripture noted that the believers wore a certain kind of clothing, do we take it to mean that we also need to dress likewise?

    My take is: if you are comfortable in that CG, by all means go ahead, but if you are not, there is no reason why you should continue doing that.
