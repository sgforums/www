    When I talk about miracles, I mean "a striking interposition of divine intervention by a god in the universe by which the ordinary course and operation of Nature is overruled, suspended, or modified."

    So I do not consider anything that could be a lucky coincidence, or something that is unlikely to occur to be a miracle.

    I don't consider an unexpected promotion a miracle. Nor winning the lottery. Nor having someone coming up to me to tell me about Christ just when I happen to be contemplating suicide.

    To me a miracle is something extraordinary with no natural explanation. And not something that we don't understand how it could have happened. Something that we understand could not have happened which serves as evidence that some supernatural being intervened.

    The flesh on a shriveled leg can grow. Cancer can go into spontaneous remission. Some people with HIV never develop full blown AIDS.

    If a leg is amputated at a joint, it's impossible for the leg to regrow. There's nothing there. The leg is gone. There are no cells. Nothing. If a leg were to be restored, that would be a true miracle.

    Another example of a true miracles would probably have been the three hours of global darkness upon the death of Christ.

    "It was about the sixth hour,
    and there was darkness over all the earth
    until the ninth hour. And the sun was darkened."
    (Luke 24:44,45)

    For darkness to cover all the earth, that would probably have been a miracle. As far as we know, there's no event that could have caused that. Having said that though, even if we found evidence for a global period of darkness, we would need to look for some natural explanations first. In any case, there's no evidence that this occurred.

    Finally, personal testimony is extremely unreliable as evidence. I fully agree and accept that what you experienced was very real to you. I also agree and accept that you and the other person you spoke of have no reason to lie. But people in every religion have their religious experiences that validate their beliefs.

    You asked for a sign and you received it. And you received a sign that was exactly what you expected. That in itself should make you wonder.
