    SCOTLAND: CHAMPION RANGERS STRUGGLE IN DUNDEE

    October 18, 2005

    THE disappointing run of form of Rangers continued as they struggled to a 0-0 draw away to Dundee United in the Scottish Premier League.

    It was a result which leaves Alex McLeish's men nine points adrift of leaders Hearts and six behind Old Firm rivals Celtic.

    McLeish admitted that with Nacho Novo and Thomas Buffel both on the sidelines, he needs Francis Jeffers and Federico Nieto to start to make an impression and take the pressure off Croatian striker Dado Prso.

    'We are looking for somebody to be a hero just now and take the pressure off Prso a little bit,' said McLeish.

    'Prso is a free spirit kind of player. He makes runs on the wings and it's just getting a poacher in the box to finish for us.

    'Nacho and Thomas are missing and they would be likely first-choice partners for Dado.

    Rangers had defender Sotirios Kyrgiakos sent off just before the final whistle for a second bookable offence.
    - Reuters.
