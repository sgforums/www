    Soccer: Wenger threatens legal action over Mourinho voyeur jibe

    LONDON : Arsene Wenger has threatened to take legal action against Jose Mourinho after the Chelsea manager branded his Arsenal counterpart a "voyeur."

    Wenger angered Mourinho last week by suggesting Chelsea may have lost a little bit of self belief following a 1-1 draw with Everton, which ended their winning start to the season in the league, and a League Cup exit at the hands of Charlton.

    That prompted Mourinho to claim Wenger was rattled by Chelsea's success. "I think he is one of these people who is a voyeur," the Portuguese coach claimed, accusing the Arsenal boss of being obsessed with Chelsea.

    Wenger responded on Tuesday by describing Mourinho's comments as "out of order, disconnected from reality and disrespectful."

    He added: "I will see if I take any action on that."

    Asked if that could mean legal action, Wenger replied: "I don't know but I leave that door open."

    Wenger went on to insist that he had simply been attempting to answer questions about Chelsea that he receives at every press conference, rather than deliberately setting out to make comments aimed at unsettling the champions. - AFP /dt
