    Soccer: Hammers complete Bellion deal as take-over looms

    LONDON : West Ham completed the signing of French striker David Bellion from Premiership rivals Manchester United on a one year loan deal as the London club found itself at the centre of take-over talks.

    Manager Alan Pardew was eager to strengthen his strikeforce before the transfer window closes after missing out on Liverpool's Milan Baros, Emmanuel Adeybayor at Monaco and former Hammer Fredi Kanoute at Tottenham.

    "David Bellion was wanted by a number of Premier League clubs and I feel it is a great compliment to this Club and its supporters that he wanted to come to West Ham," said Pardew.

    "David is 22 and will reinvigorate the right hand side of our attack and give me a whole new set of options.

    "He is the sort of player that can break a deadlock with his pace as he is without question one of the quickest players in the Premiership."

    Bellion has however been troubled by injuries and has not played first-team football since January and is still short of peak fitness.

    The former Sunderland player will be looking for more first-team opportunities at Upton Park than he was given at Old Trafford.

    He joined United in 2003 for two million pounds but he made just 40 appearances in all competitions, scoring nine goals.

    The former France Under-21s international began his English career in 2002 when Sunderland brought him to the Stadium of Light from Cannes.

    West Ham began life back in the Premiership with a convincing 3-1 defeat of Blackburn on Saturday .

    The club's hopes of a brighter future were boosted on Wednesday by the comments of Kia Joorabchian, the Iranian-born, English-educated head of London-based consortium Media Sports Investment, who gave notice of a bid for the Hammers earlier this month.

    Joorabchian told the London Evening Standard he has not made a bid yet - but it is only a matter of time.

    "My West Ham project has to be worth around 200million pounds," he said.

    "That's 45million on buying the club, 30million on its debts, another 30million on keeping the cash-flow going and around 100million on new players.

    "There is no point in buying West Ham unless you are going to turn them into one of the top teams in the country. There is huge potential there and at one point we are going to make a bid, although at the moment we have not done anything.

    "The fact that I want to buy a European club is obvious and there is still a possibly it could be another Premiership club other than West Ham." - AFP /dt
