    Jamie Carragher has written off Liverpool's title chances for next season - despite manager Rafael Benitez's search for perfection.

    Liverpool finished 37 points behind Barclays Premiership champions Chelsea last season, a gap Carragher feels cannot be bridged, so instead Champions League football is the target.

    The 27-year-old defender, who recently committed his future to the club by signing a new four-year contract, feels the Reds will not be contenders for the crown for at least another couple of years.

    'I don't want to sound defeatist, but it's going to be very difficult to break into the top three this season,' said Carragher.

    'It is still only Rafa Benitez's second season here and we have not got the resources of Chelsea or Manchester United.

    'Obviously, we want to win the league but it would mean a big jump to go from 37 points behind Chelsea to above them.

    'We've got to look for around the 70-point mark, which would definitely get us fourth.

    'Maybe the year after, we can add another 10 or 12 points and keep pushing so in the next couple of years we can go for the title.'

    Carragher concedes, though, Benitez 'isn't the type to rest on his laurels' following the club's remarkable Champions League triumph over AC Milan in May.

    The England international knows Benitez will push his players on, adding: 'He never takes things for granted, even after we've had good games or good results.

    'He is always on to you. Even when we've played really well, he will look at the negatives.

    'He is always looking for perfection, which probably is not possible, but that's what he wants.'

    While Carragher concedes Chelsea have the best team, he feels Liverpool have managed to hang onto the best player in close friend Steven Gerrard.

    Gerrard also opted to sign up until 2009 after being linked with a move to Chelsea, and Carragher believes the midfielder made the right move.

    'I don't think there is anyone in the world who can touch Steven Gerrard,' insisted Carragher.

    'Frank Lampard has been on fire for the last couple of years, but he's probably playing with better players than Steve is.

    'Even though all the talk was of him leaving, I felt it would always be difficult for Steve to do that, being a local lad.

    'People were telling me he had gone, but you are not gone until you sign that contract with another club. I'm sure he made the right decision.'
