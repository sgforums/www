    ALLARDYCE: SUCCESS IS A HAZARD FOR BOLTON
    August 30, 2005

    SAM Allardyce is worried Bolton's success last season could prove to be their undoing this term.

    Bolton claimed a Uefa Cup spot after finishing sixth in the Premiership last season and yesterday's 2-1 win at West Ham - their second in four days - took them to third in the table after four matches.

    Bolton also have a Uefa Cup tie against Lokomotiv Plovdiv to look forward to on 29 Sept, but Allardyce is concerned the number of fixtures may weaken his team.

    He said: 'The players gave a magnificent effort for the team yesterday - but it was a concern seeing how tired they were after the game.

    'They were tired because of the energy they had used in the 2-0 win over Newcastle last Wednesday.

    'We're going to have to face that situation week in and week out if we get into the group stages of the Uefa Cup.

    'I'm worried that we'll come across a better side than West Ham on a Sunday after we've played on a Thursday and lose the game because of fatigue.'

    Goals from Kevin Nolan and substitute Ivan Campo gave Bolton the win against a West Ham team that created plenty in attack but were let down by wasteful finishing.

    Teddy Sheringham scored a late penalty for Alan Pardew's West Ham team, but it was not enough to prevent the east Londoners slipping to their first defeat of the campaign.

    Allardyce is confident though, that West Ham can avoid relegation in their first season back in the Premiership after a two-year absence.

    He said: 'I see enough in West Ham to stay up this year, and to be comfortably clear of the bottom three. Even though they will be disappointed with yesterday's result, a win and a draw from their first two games is a great beginning.'

    Pardew, meanwhile, remains keen to improve his squad. His main summer signings - goalkeeper Roy Carroll, defenders Danny Gabbidon and Paul Konchesky and winger Yossi Benayoun - all impressed in patches.

    But the lack of a top-class striker was painfully evident.

    Pardew said: 'It would be nice to add another player to this squad - even though we look competitive now.' - PA Sport.
