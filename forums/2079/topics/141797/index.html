    Manchester United striker Alan Smith is confident the Old Trafford club will re-establish themselves as the best in the country next season.

    Smith would sacrifice a place at next season's World Cup finals to help United topple Premiership champions Chelsea.

    "We know we are good enough," Smith told The Sun. "In 12 months I will be saying 'I told you so'."

    He added: "People like Giggsy and Scholesy, who have done it all, are still so enthusiastic and driven to win it this time. It's quite incredible to see.

    "It inspires the rest of us and it is up to people like me, Rio Ferdinand and Wayne Rooney to take up the mantle. I have never won anything, so it's about time I did - and United is the place to do it."

    Smith is not worrying about winning a place in Sven-Goran Eriksson's squad for next summer's World Cup finals in Germany should England qualify.

    The United forward said: "This season is not about whether Alan Smith gets to the World Cup, it is about United and how we perform as a team.

    "If I play every game for United, we win the big trophies and I don't get in the England World Cup squad, it won't bother me one bit.

    "If England come along - and you would think it would if I had a good season - then that's great. But United is all I have on my mind."

    Soccer: Keane injury blow to Man United's Asian tour

    LONDON : Roy Keane and Wes Brown have been ruled out of Manchester United's Asian tour through injury while Gabriel Heinze is rested.

    But United manager Alex Ferguson was hopeful of having both Keane and Brown available for the crucial Champions League qualifier on August 10 and the opening game of the new Premiership season at Everton three days later.

    Argentina defender Heinze, who scooped both fans and players' player of the year awards in his first full season at Old Trafford, has been given extended leave after playing for his country in he Confederations Cup in Germany last month.

    With Keane missing, it will be interesting to see who Ferguson nominates as replacement skipper.

    Last season it appeared Rio Ferdinand was earmarked as Keane's likely successor when he was handed the captain's armband in preference to other senior figures in the United squad.

    However, with the England defender still in dispute with the Red Devils over a new contract, a stance that is causing Ferguson increasing irritation, Ryan Giggs could be handed the honour instead.

    As expected, new signings Edwin van der Sar and Park Ji-Sung have been named in the squad, along with Chinese import Dong Fangzhou, who has yet to make his first senior appearance for the club.

    United have friendlies at Clyde and Peterborough before they head to Hong Kong next Thursday, the start of a four-match programme in eight days.

    As well as the Hong Kong national XI, Ferguson's side will also tackle Beijing Hyundai, as well as J-League duo Kashima Antlers and Urawa Red Diamonds. - AFP/de

    MANCHESTER United are set for a Tokyo summit with Bayern Munich in 10 days' time to discuss a potential move for German star Michael Ballack.

    Sir Alex Ferguson has declared that only an experienced performer can improve his squad for the coming season.

    And 28-year-old Ballack, who has indicated he'd like a switch to the Premiership after appearing to lose faith at Bayern, is entering the peak years of his career.

    He could just be the player Ferguson is looking for.

    United fly into Tokyo on 27 July for the third match of their pre-season tour of the Far East.

    They play Kashima Antlers in Tokyo the same day that Bayern and Ballack face FC Tokyo as part of the their final warm-up for the start of the German Bundesliga on 5 Aug.

    Both Old Trafford officials and top brass from the German champions will be in the Japanese capital for four days with United then playing Urawa Red Diamonds in Saitama on 30 July and Bayern ending their build up against Yubilo Iwata.

    If Ferguson has targeted Ballack as his big name import for the close season, then the two sets of officials would use the convenient crossing of their paths to meet up.

    Ballack, estimated to be worth
    12 million ($35m), still has a year left on his contract in Munich but he has expressed a wish to move on.

    He played in the recent Confederation Cup and has been missing from Bayern's second training camp in Bonn.

    With the new season looming, Ferguson is in a hurry.

    He knows he has to make an impression at the business end of the table or his team could end up having to play second fiddle to Chelsea for a number of years to come.

    With his midfield engine of Roy Keane and Paul Scholes starting to splutter, Ferguson will have to move quickly to land a top player and Ballack fits the bill perfectly.
    - PA Sport.
