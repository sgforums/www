    Sep 24, 2011 - ST Forum

    DR LEE Siew Peng's sentiments on choice of marriage partner ("Mum's the word on smarter children"; Wednesday) made for excellent food for thought.

    It is not that men feel inferior linked in matrimony to a more successful or more intelligent spouse. Rather, such women are rarely attracted to men they regard as mere mortals.

    While it is generally true that intelligence can be perpetuated through offspring, the supremely intelligent and the geniuses hardly ever produce children whose ability exceeds theirs. Reversion to the mean is far more common.

    Often enough, children born with lesser genes catch up given the proper stimulation and challenges. Prodigies are born with their neuronal connections in place. These connections also develop in normal people, albeit later.

    Where the child turns out excellent, we pat ourselves on the back thinking that his success was because of us, when in fact, it is in spite of us. Where children fail, there is always society to blame.

    To contemplate eugenics is to go down a long and slippery slope. Just ask the Nazis.

    Dr Yik Keng Yeong
