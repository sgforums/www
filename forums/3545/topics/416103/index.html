    Nov 24, 2010 - Straits Times

    By Kimberly Spykerman

    A 12-YEAR-OLD girl found dead at the foot of Block 573 in Hougang Street 51 on Wednesday morning, is believed to have been murdered, said police.

    Investigations are ongoing into the case, which police have classified as murder.

    The girl, said to have weak arms and legs, was believed to have fallen from the fourth floor early on Wednesday morning.

    Police said they were alerted to the motionless body at the foot of the block at about 6.05am. The girl was pronounced dead by paramedics at about 6.20am.

    Neighbours said she suffered from weak arms and legs and have to be supported by a maid when moving around. She also attended a special school.

    The block comprises mainly of four-room Housing Board flats. Anyone with information on the case can call the police hotline at 1800-255-0000.

    DISABLED GIRL'S DEATH FALL
    Maid faces murder charge


    A 12-year-old girl fell to her death yesterday in Hougang. Her body was discovered at the foot of the block she lived in. -- ST PHOTO: TED CHEN

    AN INDONESIAN domestic helper has been arrested and will be charged in court on Friday with the murder of a 12-year-old girl, said police on Thursday.

    The maid was taken away for questioning on Wednesday, after Linda Lee Yee Lin, a physically and mentally disabled girl, was found at the foot of her block of flats in Hougang Street 51, at about 6am. She was wearing a pair of pink and white pyjamas.

    If convicted of murder, the Indonesian maid faces the death penalty. She started working for the family only last month.

    Neighbours said the maid appeared to be in her 20s and was 'small-sized' and 'skinny.'

    Linda's mother only found out about her death hours later, when she woke up and discovered that her only child was missing from the family's fourth-floor Housing Board flat.

    Linda attended a special school and could speak only simple words. Neighbours remembered her as a cheerful girl who smiled a lot.
