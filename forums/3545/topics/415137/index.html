    Nov 11, 2010 - ST Forum

    SMALL-TIME gamblers such as foreign workers cannot afford to lose ('It's off to the gambling tables on their days off'; last Saturday).

    They have come to our shores to earn a living so as to escape the poverty trap at home. Instead, they are now headed for a gambling trap which may spell their doom.

    Gambling is addictive. The Government must do more to discourage this vice. To prevent both Singaporeans and foreign workers from falling prey to the gambling trap, the entrance fee to both casinos could be raised to $500 per day and $10,000 per year.

    Besides the option of self-exclusion which might not work, foreign workers could also be banned from casinos.

    Patrick Low
