    Singaporeans donate to help foreign workers attacked in Kallang
    By Evelyn Choo and Leong Wee Keat | Posted: 08 June 2010 2053 hrs

    SINGAPORE : Singaporeans have again shown a readiness to help strangers in need.

    This time, they opened their hearts - and wallets - to three foreign workers who were attacked in Kallang about two weeks ago.

    One of them died from his injuries.

    Two of the victims - Egar Karuppaiah and Sandeep Singh - are recovering in Tan Tock Seng Hospital. They will be discharged in a few days but have nowhere to go.

    "I'm just a construction worker. I'm not rich. I have no money. My life is simple," said Sandeep, victim of the Kallang attack.

    One donor donated $500 to each of them and wants to send money to the family of the worker who was killed.

    Members of two voluntary welfare organisations - the Humanitarian Organisation for Migration Economics and Transient Workers Count Too - have the workers' contact details in India to remit all donations to them.

    "These workers will have problems with their livelihood in the future. They have families to support. That's why we feel it's important for us to assist them financially," said Jolovan Wham, a social working from Humanitarian Organisation for Migration Economics.

    The VWOs have been approached by about 10 people, asking how they can contribute and how the funds will be used.

    "Interested members of the public can donate to us directly, and we will disburse the funds to the workers or their families. Moving forward, there is a possibility for us on wanting to set up a fund for workers like these who fall through the cracks of the system," said Wham.

    So far, more than $5,000 has been donated.

    Those who want to know more can send an email to migrants.home@gmail.com.

    - CNA /ls
