PM Lee urges leadership renewal in Malay Muslim community

  • Clivebenss
    21 Nov 10, 14:39

    By Imelda Saad | Posted: 21 November 2010 1235 hrs

    SINGAPORE: Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong has called for leadership renewal within the Malay Muslim community.

    Speaking at the annual community leaders' forum on Sunday, Mr Lee said leadership renewal is vital at all levels, including the labour movement and politics.

    Leadership renewal cannot happen overnight but must be planned systematically, he added.

    Mr Lee noted that young people are keen to get involved. Malay Muslim organisations should find ways to tap this "resource", he said.

    Young people, Mr Lee said, can tackle problems and challenges with fresh enthusiasm and imagination.

    Mr Lee said that new issues will call for creative responses.

    To set the wheels in motion, this year's forum has for the first time involved the participation of young Malay Muslim Singaporeans.

    A youth-centric report was released, charting a 10-year forward-planning exercise for the community.

    The aim is to get young Malay Muslims here to take ownership of issues facing their community and to identify future leaders.
