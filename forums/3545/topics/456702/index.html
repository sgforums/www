    You are at the Central station HK , the queue forms in neat but crowded rows, 25 individuals in a row and you see a sea of rows after rows. The platform crowd controllers clap their paddles telling you to line up within the boundarylines drawn on the platform, so that passengers can alight first. The trains come in within a minute of each other-one leaving the next one arrives! Efficient super clock work!- that is how the MTR functions in Hongkong. We pack in body to body stacked together, catching our breaths from air pockets of space here and there as the train rushes us through to reach our destination.
