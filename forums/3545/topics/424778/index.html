    Mar 10, 2011 - Straits Times

    By Shivali Nayak, Multimedia Journalist, RazorTV

    THE behaviour of Singaporean men has been discussed a lot recently on citizen journalism website STOMP, as STOMPers debate whether or not it is alright for men to wear floral shirts, carry a woman's handbag for her, or reserve a parking spot for a lady driver.

    STOMPer Delilah was tickled after spotting a man in a pink floral tee-shirt, but other netizens like STOMPer debonair did not find anything wrong with that: 'I don't find anything funny about someone expressing their individuality without conforming to societal norms.'

    However, most STOMPers agreed that it was unnecessary for a man to carry a woman's handbag or reserve a parking lot for her.

    Also discussed on STOMP was the question of social graciousness in Singapore when it was posted that no one came to the aid of an elderly couple who tripped while going up the escalators at Hougang Green Mall.

    STOMPer Easygoingjoe wondered aloud: 'Something bad happens to someone and no one steps in to help - is this showing the true face of Singaporeans?'
