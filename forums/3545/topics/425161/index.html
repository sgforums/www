Enforce no-smoking rule in parks

  • Clivebenss
    15 Mar 11, 06:18

    Mar 15, 2011 - ST Forum

    I AM a frequent user of Jurong West Park Connector and the connecting neighbourhood park in Jurong West Street 62.

    However, there are some smokers who treat the park as a smoking corner. The street soccer court, which is situated close to the running tracks, is of particular concern as teenagers would often smoke there while watching their friends play. Residents from the neighbouring blocks would also light up while cutting through the park to the bus stop.

    I understand that the National Environment Agency has demarcated playgrounds and exercise areas as smoke-free zones, but such bans have not been strictly enforced.

    The parks are great places for the elderly to take their grandchildren to play or stroll, and the young are particularly vulnerable to the ill effects of passive smoking. It seems counterproductive to develop these parks to enhance healthy lifestyles, yet expose users to harmful passive smoking.

    I hope the relevant agencies would do more to ensure that parks remain smoke-free.

    Yeo Zheng Hang
