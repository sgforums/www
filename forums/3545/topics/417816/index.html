Keep CPF contribution rates simple and the same for workers

  • Clivebenss
    14 Dec 10, 07:41

    Dec 14, 2010 - ST Forum

    MADAM MARY WONG: 'I add my appeal among others to the Government to review the automatic cut in an employer's Central Provident Fund (CPF) contribution rates for workers aged above 50 ('No automatic pay cut at 60 for 34,400 workers'; Nov 25). The cut effectively amounts to a cut in remuneration, as CPF has long been seen as part of an employee's pay. Many employees peak in their careers from age 50, while workload and responsibilities remain the same, if not more. Although it is not mandatory for employers to cut an employee's CPF rate, as they are encouraged to make voluntary contributions, not many bosses will act on the latter. Voluntary contributions give employers an extra headache as they must make adjustments to their computerised payroll system. I appeal to the Government not to let ageism creep into CPF rates. Let remuneration be negotiable among employers, employees and unions, and keep our statutory CPF contribution rates simple and the same for workers of all ages in the spirit of fair employment practices. While there may have been good reasons to cut CPF rates previously, I do not think those reasons apply now.'
