S'poreans will receive news of Budget in good time: PM Lee

  • Clivebenss
    30 Jan 11, 16:27

    By Tan Qiuyi | Posted: 30 January 2011 1535 hrs

    SINGAPORE: During his visit to the Yio Chu Kang division on Sunday morning, Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong was asked about possible goodies in next month's Budget to be delivered in Parliament on the February 18.

    He preferred to leave the details to Finance Minister, Tharman Shanmugaratnam.

    But Mr Lee said that Singaporeans will receive the news in good time.

    He added that 2010 was a good year in which the Singapore economy grew by 14.7 per cent. More details of the economic performance are expected to be released just before Budget Day by the Trade and Industry Ministry.

    When asked at a separate event, Mr Tharman said the Budget will be for the good of Singapore in the long term and cover more than just one group of Singaporeans.

    He also noted that people here are feeling the impact of rising inflation.

    "It'll be something good for Singapore, but we don't shape budgets just for the short term. The budgets are very important about strengthening Singapore for the long term.Inflation has picked up, it's all across the region, look at the news China, India, Indonesia, that's something we have to live with. Fortunately in Singapore, it's been a lower rate of inflation than elsewhere because we have a relatively strong currency.

    But it's uncomfortable for many people and we know that and we make sure we help families where we can...The budget is something that we plan not just for the short term but for the good of Singapore in the long term. We try to cover more than one group, it's not just focused on one group of Singaporeans," said Mr Shanmugaratnam.

    Mr Shanmugaratnam was speaking to reporters at the launch of newly completed cycling tracks in Taman Jurong.

    -CNA/ac
