    May 27, 2010 - The Straits Times

    Sex predator conned 8 women

    He offered them up to $20,000 for sexual services with 'clients'

    By Elena Chong, Courts Correspondent

    POSING as a woman running a social escort business, he trawled the Internet for young women, enticing them with money in return for sexual services for 'clients'.

    Lim Jun Chieh's offer, of as much as $20,000 for the works, including two rounds of sex, proved too tempting for at least eight women who fell for his scam. In all, the 30-year-old cheated two women and bedded six others, including a 16-year-old girl who was promised payment of $5,000.

    Most of them ended up having intercourse with him without realising that he was behind the advertisements for social escorts on the Internet.

    Yesterday, Lim was jailed a total of 30 months by District Judge Ng Peng Hong after pleading guilty to two counts of cheating, and one charge each of procuring a woman to prostitute herself and having commercial sex with a minor under 18.

    He committed the offences over a three-year period, starting from July 2007, less than a year after his release from prison for similar offences.

    His modus operandi involved an elaborate act in which he posed as multiple women - Ling, Pink, Lingling and Madeline - to win the trust of his victims. He used the social networking Internet site Friendster as the base of his operations.
