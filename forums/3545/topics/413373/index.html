    Mosquito larvae found breeding in Sheng Siong wet market at Elias Mall
    By Ng Lian Cheong | Posted: 18 October 2010 2015 hrs

    SINGAPORE: An inspection by the National Environment Agency earlier this month found mosquito larvae breeding at a Sheng Siong-owned wet market at Elias Mall.

    The wet market has been closed for more than half a year as the Housing & Development Board (HDB) has not approved Sheng Siong's proposals as they do not meet HDB's requirements for wet markets.

    NEA said it found mosquitoes breeding in seven spots in the 12,000 square-feet wet market when it inspected the area on 7 October.

    It added that it found some 1,500 larvae, about half of which are believed to be from dengue-causing mosquitoes.

    As a result, NEA said the supermarket chain will be taken to court where if convicted, the company faces a S$10,000 fine.

    Pest experts say empty areas are conducive breeding grounds for mosquitoes once there is stagnant water.

    NEA returned to the premises for inspections again on Monday.

    According to David Teo, Property Manager with Sheng Siong, the NEA was "satisfied" with the measures taken.

    "After the inspection, NEA is satisfied with our preventive measure on mosquito breeding. NEA request us to remove all the debris on site for their next inspection. We have also informed our pest control operator to carry out weekly inspection," said Mr Teo.

    -CNA/ac
