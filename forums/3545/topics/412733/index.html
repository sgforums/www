    SINGAPORE: Two men were slightly injured when a van crashed into a shop at Block 104, Hougang Avenue 1 on Saturday.

    Police said the van crashed into the ground-floor shop that was selling items used for prayer offerings.

    The two men who were injured were in the shop when the incident took place.

    But as their injuries were minor, they were not sent to hospital.

    Incense shop gets surprise

    A WHITE van crashed into an incense shop in Hougang Avenue 1 on Saturday evening.

    The shop's owner, Mr David Koh, 44, rushed to the scene after receiving calls from his younger brother and mother, who were in the shop with his father.

    The police said two men in the shop suffered minor injuries caused by the wreckage.

    The driver, a 52-year-old woman, was unhurt. She is assisting the police in their investigations.
