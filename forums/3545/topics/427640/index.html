Man charged with acting as bookmaker

  • Clivebenss
    12 Apr 11, 12:59

    By Alvina Soh | Posted: 12 April 2011 1222 hrs

    SINGAPORE: A 50-year-old Singaporean has been charged with acting as a bookmaker.

    The unemployed man, Ng Ah Chye, allegedly did so on three occasions between July 11 and August 17 last year at Resorts World Sentosa (RWS).

    He is believed to have approached patrons at the RWS casino to offer them private bets at the baccarat tables. The offer of private bets between two or more patrons is an offence under the Betting Act.

    Anyone found guilty of the offence can be fined between S$20,000 and S$200,000 and jailed for up to five years.

    Ng will next appear in court on April 19.
