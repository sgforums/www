Caught in red tape after switch to age-proof trade

  • Clivebenss
    31 Oct 11, 15:49

    Oct 31, 2011 - ST Forum

    PRIME Minister Lee Hsien Loong's wrap-up speech in Parliament was indeed heartening ('From void deck to Nobel Prize lab'; Oct 21). His warning about an ageing Singapore society is timely.

    At 50, I have already prepared myself to explore new grounds and have entered an industry where old age is not a handicap.

    From a qualified engineer, I switched to become a taxidermist and obtained training in New Zealand. But when I tried to ply this trade here, I had to surmount many bureaucratic hurdles.

    The Agri-Food and Veterinary Authority (AVA) does not seem to be supportive of taxidermy exports from Singapore. Wild animals, birds and fish that died naturally or were killed in road accidents cannot be taxidermied, much less exhibited. Essential chemicals needed for this trade are not allowed to be imported, so substitutes that are less effective have to be used instead.

    Despite these obstacles, I was able to compete in the recently concluded New Zealand Taxidermy Association Championship with a pair of mantis shrimp procured from the local supermarket. I won third place.

    Every year, a world taxidermy championship attracts taxidermists from many countries. I am sure Singapore can benefit from hosting such an event.

    Casinos were deemed unsuitable and eschewed for a long time in Singapore. Yet, we now have two operating here. Should not taxidermy be relooked in a new light with stringent and outdated rules replaced with more pragmatic ones?

    Ken Mar
