    Jun 28, 2010 - Straits Times

    Teen gang slashes 2 students

    By Khushwant Singh

    A MONTH before he was to enrol in Republic Polytechnic, Mr Lee Dao Hong had a part of his finger slashed off. His friend, private school student Abel Lim Kay Hern, also 17, was slashed on the head in the attack on March 5.

    One of the seven attackers pleaded guilty on Monday to rioting with a deadly weapon with the common intention of hurting the two victims.

    Community Court Judge Soh Tze Bian ordered a pre-sentence and probation report on Muhammad Rozaidi Abdullah, a student of the MacPherson Institute of Technical Education (ITE). His accomplices have been charged and will be dealt with separately.

    The victims were walking to church for prayer services at 8pm with four friends, aged between 15 and 23. Along Pasir Ris Drive 3, they were accused of staring at the other group, who started shouting vulgarities.

    When someone in Mr Lee's group made a rude hand gesture, a fight broke out. One of Muhammad Rozaidi's friends was armed with a knife and starting slashing while the rest punched and kicked the two victims. The friends managed to escape.

    The amputation of Mr Lee's right ring finger measured 5cm and the court heard that the severed part was not recovered. Mr Lim suffered a 5cm-long slash on his scalp.
