    Feb 27, 2011 - Straits Times

    Teo Chee Hean says system must ensure good, tested candidates come in to take country forward

    By Kor Kian Beng

    THE ruling party cannot say for sure if any one among its line-up of new faces will become Singapore's next prime minister, Deputy Prime Minister Teo Chee Hean made it clear on Saturday.

    'We can never tell,' he said when asked by reporters if PM Lee Hsien Loong's successor is among the new batch of PAP candidates.

    'All we can do is to make sure that we have a system that continues to bring in good people,' he said at a ceremony for new citizens in his Pasir Ris-Punggol GRC.

    'We don't want a random process where somebody has no experience and is an unknown quantity. It may be nice for a change but he comes in and either runs your GRC and you don't know whether he's going to deliver, or more dramatically, if he comes in and runs your country and you don't know what he is and whether he is going to deliver,' he added.

    Mr Teo expressed confidence that Singaporeans want to make sure 'we have good, tried and tested people who can take the constituency and country forward'.

    In his first comments since the publication of the new electoral map last Thursday, he also highlighted one shortcoming of four-member GRCs.
