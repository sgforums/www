Drug trafficker loses bid against death sentence

    Posted: 04 April 2011 1920 hrs

    SINGAPORE : The Court of Appeal on Monday dismissed the appeal of convicted drug trafficker Yong Vui Kong, who is facing the death penalty.

    Yong, a 23-year-old Malaysian, was sentenced to the gallows in January 2009 for smuggling 47 grammes of heroin into Singapore.

    He had launched a petition calling for a review of the clemency rules concerning the death penalty.

    It was turned down last year and he appealed against that decision.

    In delivering the verdict on behalf of a three-judge panel on Monday, Chief Justice Chan Sek Keong said Yong's appeal was "devoid of merit".

    Yong's lawyer M Ravi said he plans to appeal again to the Singapore cabinet to seek for clemency.
