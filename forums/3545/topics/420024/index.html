Women can ease public sector understaffing

    Jan 11, 2011 - ST Forum

    I REFER to the letters by Mr Edmond Lo ("Wrong to equate gender inequality with national service") and Madam Koh Chin Chin ("Women's 'national service'"; both Jan 5).

    Without national service, our security may be threatened due to the likely critical and chronic understaffing of the military.

    In the same way, there is a chronic understaffing of a number of government agencies, leading to regular complaints of slow or inadequate service by members of the public.

    Therefore, there are grounds to explore the idea of "drafting" women to serve in government services full time for a number of years, and later calling upon them regularly to serve for a short duration of time.

    This would greatly bolster Singapore's economic defence.

    Jeremy Chen
