    Jan 27, 2012 - ST Forum

    IN MY view, instances of bosses unreasonably demanding instant responses to e-mail at all hours of the day (or night) are relatively few ('Addicted to work e-mail? Take a break'; Monday).

    Apart from employees who have an obsessive inability to let go of work and relax, and who incessantly check their e-mail, the people who pressure you for instant replies or want something done the day before are likelier to be co-workers and clients, rather than bosses.

    A colleague, issued with a company BlackBerry, refers to it as a leash. Another's response is that with the BlackBerry, he need never go to the office again, and can be at the beach and yet be working.

    A third sees the BlackBerry as a reference indicator of what work activities are happening, but it takes great discipline to keep it as just that.

    The problem here is that - while I am not suggesting that one be less responsible or diligent - the more promptly we respond or seem available to boss, client or peer, especially after office hours, the more we end up setting the wrong expectations, and the more they will come back for more.

    Lim Hock Chuan
