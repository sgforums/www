Chicken prices hit 10-year high

    By Ng Lian Cheong | Posted: 17 March 2011 2032 hrs

    SINGAPORE: The price of live chickens from Malaysia reached a 10-year high on Thursday, at more than six ringgit per kilogramme, beating the 1999 record of more than five ringgit per kilogramme.

    With this, poultry sellers said the wholesale price of fresh chicken in Singapore rose 20 to 30 cents per kilo.

    Stall owners at a Chinatown wet market have increased prices by about 50 cents a kilo to offset costs.

    Prices at major supermarkets remain unchanged for now, but are expected to go up in a week.

    The Poultry Merchants' Association said the increase is due to rising cost of grains and feed affecting farmers in Malaysia.

    Poultry Merchants' Association chairman Chew Ghim Bok said: "When the cost of production goes up, the farmer tends to be more cautious and rear less. And if you rear less, the supply will be short".

    -CNA
