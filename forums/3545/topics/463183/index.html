    Poly students with an eye for business
    The New Paper | Thu Dec 20 2012

    SINGAPORE - Budding entrepreneurs can pick up nuggets of wisdom from local success story Kenny Yap, 47, the executive chairman and group chief executive of Qian Hu Corporation Limited.

    Mr Yap started his business at the age of 25 and today, Qian Hu is the only ornamental fish service provider which is a public-listed company on the Singapore Exchange.

    Here are Mr Yap's tips for school-leavers and young entrepreneurs:

    Experience

    A tertiary education can give you a foundation, but you need to build up your experience by learning on the job.

    Wisdom

    Experience comes from making mistakes, but the ultimate aim is to become wiser. Without wisdom, you will continue to make the same mistakes.

    Passion

    Do the things you really like, which can create value or have a positive impact on others. Automatically, you will have work-life balance because you enjoy working.

    Motivation

    Money should never be the goal. There are jobs which will pay more than what you may earn as an entrepreneur.

    But true entrepreneurs want the freedom of creating their own rules.

    You must want to be an entrepreneur so badly that you feel you will die with regrets if you don't.

    Responsibility

    You must be able to create something out of nothing from limited means.

    You must enjoy being responsible for the lives of your employees who depend on you.

    Right attitude and perspective

    Understand that failure is necessary. You must dare to take risks and bounce back from setbacks.

    Despite failure, you must try again. Be sincere and earn the trust of clients by your actions.

    Forget your ego

    Confidence comes from within.

    People who care too much about losing face will be afraid of failure.
