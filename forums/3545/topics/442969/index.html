    Two men charged with murder of 20-year-old

    By Yahoo! Newsroom | Yahoo! Newsroom – 6 hours ago

    Two men were charged in court on Monday with the murder of full-time national serviceman Chen Zi Quan, police said.

    Lee Boon Leong, 22, and Desmond Neo Han Wen, 24, were reported to be part of a group of five men that allegedly attacked Chen in the carpark of Central Square around 6am last Saturday.

    Chen and three friends were heading home when they were attacked, reported The Straits Times. It was unclear whether weapons had been used, and Chen together with his friends left the premise after the fight, it said.

    Chen was admitted to hospital and died from head injuries on Sunday, according to the newspaper.

    Meanwhile, District Judge Lim Tse Haw has granted permission for the police to take the Lee and Neo out to assist in investigations and they will be remanded at the Central police division, ST reported.

    The duo are expected back in court next Monday, and if found guilty, they face the death sentence.

    When contacted, a police spokesman told Yahoo! Singapore that a 27-year-old man has also been arrested in relation to the case, but was unable to reveal whether the man was among those who allegedly attacked Chen, apart from Lee and Neo.

    This case comes on the heels of another attack involving a 17-year-old Raffles Institution student Anand Ravindran who was stabbed twice in the back at Potong Pasir Avenue 1 last Thursday. 

    Anand and his companions were accused by three other youths of staring at them. Five teenagers have since been arrested in connection with that attack.
