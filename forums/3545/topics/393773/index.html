    Youth Challenge to close

    It feels it is no longer relevant in engaging young people

    By Ang Yiying

    YOUTH Challenge, a once high-profile charity known for sending students overseas to do humanitarian work, is closing.

    The 25-year-old organisation is no longer relevant as there are many charities now that engage young people, said its seven-member board, some of whom had stepped up to the plate to lead the charity in 2006 when a Commissioner of Charities probe into it began.

    The proposal to close was upheld by 28 of its 39 registered members at an extraordinary general meeting on Saturday, said its president Looi Teck Kheong, a 44-year-old lawyer who has been on the board since 2006.
