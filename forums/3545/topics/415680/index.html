    By Imelda Saad | Posted: 19 November 2010 2218 hrs

    SINGAPORE: Minister Mentor Lee Kuan Yew has described it as "pipe dream" for Singapore to develop its own companies and expect them to compete with successful ones in other parts of the world.

    Speaking at Hewlett-Packard's 40th anniversary in Singapore, he said that is one reason why the early strategy to develop Singapore's economy was to attract multi-national companies.

    And as the years went by, the quality of investments Singapore attracts must change to keep pace with the expectations of workers.

    He said MNCs based here should offer higher value-added services and manufacturing to ensure Singapore can stay ahead of regional competition.

    Another key strategy, said Mr Lee, is to use the English language to train workers at all levels.

    "Had we taken Chinese as our working language, there would not be a Singapore today as it is. How would we make a living? How would we connect with the world?

    "But we speak English and of course we got Chinese as a second language, so we connect with them, not speak with them at the same level as they do, but we get by!"
