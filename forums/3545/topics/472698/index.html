    Just recently, we've witnessed our government's haphazard response to the haze: they fumbled, were slow to react, and obviously hadn't thought through contingency plans. When questioned by the people, they were slow to respond; when they finally took action, it was too little too late.

    Now here comes the scary part: this was just for a haze, a haze which we have experienced every year over the last decade- and we've all seen for ourselves how ineffective they were in dealing with it, and how their first instinct wasn't to protect the people.

    It makes me wonder then- how prepared are we actually against more fatal threats such as new strains of viruses or even biological terrorism? What assurance do we have that our government knows how to react if there was a serious outbreak? Will they reach out and protect the people? Can they do so with the required knowledge and effectiveness? Will they spend what's necessary to develop the right vaccination? Or will they simply stand aside and let the problem get out of hand, just because they want to 'calibrate' their actions and scrimp on spending for public health?

    As a parent of three young children,  I am very concerned. the government took one week to respond to the haze, and if they took a week to respond to a fatal viral outbreak, many people would have died by then.


    How can we be certain that we are sleeping safe?
