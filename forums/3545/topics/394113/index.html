    SAKURAS are blooming in Japan this month and so are Singapore's version of the sakura tree, the pink mempat.

    Indigenous to this region, the pink mempat bears flowers with small pink petals and a yellow centre that resemble the Japanese sakura, or cherry blossom, hence its nickname, 'Singapore Sakura'.

    Like the Japanese sakura, the branches are almost bare when the tree flowers, so the crown of pink buds bursting into bloom is especially eye-catching.

    Already, flowering pink mempat trees along Alexandra Road, Choa Chu Kang Drive and Joo Chiat Road, have stopped pedestrians and motorists in their tracks.

    Retired teacher Angie Ng, 68, said: 'I stopped my car to take a picture of these trees because they are so beautiful.'

    The blooms are expected to last for between one and two weeks.
