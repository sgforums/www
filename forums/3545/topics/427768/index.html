If not foreigners, then who?

  • Clivebenss
    14 Apr 11, 10:49

    Apr 14, 2011 - ST Forum

    PUBLIC unease about the influx of immigrants and foreign workers has led to policies checking their numbers here. However, without foreign workers, who is going to construct our houses, clear our rubbish and trim the grass in our housing estates, while we preoccupy ourselves with wealth building?

    There are plenty of jobs available to Singaporeans at fair wages, if they wish to take them up.

    I am from the service sector. For every job advertisement that we put up, only one or two out of 10 respondents will be a Singaporean. We will be lucky if any actually turn up for the interview. I remember a local respondent calling up to say that she was desperate for a job. Yet when I told her that we were located in the Bukit Timah area, she replied that it was too far and hung up. There are also those who do not wish to do shifts or work on weekends. Someone has to take over the jobs that we do not wish to do.

    Immigrants inject fresh ideas and offer new skill sets. They also possess the drive and motivation to succeed. With increased globalisation, we need to embrace cultural diversity.

    Unless we choose to accept slower growth and a lower standard of living, let's welcome the foreigners. Future generations will benefit from this policy.

    Kwok Shao Jong
