    SINGAPORE: Minister Mentor Lee Kuan Yew says he stands corrected on a comment he made about Muslims and integration in his book titled "Hard Truths To Keep Singapore Going".

    In a statement to the media on Monday evening, Mr Lee said the book was based on 32 interviews over a period of two years.

    He said the comment on Muslims integrating with other communities was made probably two or three years ago.

    Since then, he said Ministers and MPs - both Malay and non-Malay - have told him that Singapore Malays have indeed made special efforts to integrate with the other communities, especially since 9/11, and that his call was out of date.

    Mr Lee said he hopes this trend will continue in the future.

    - CNA
