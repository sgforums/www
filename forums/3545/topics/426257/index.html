    Mar 26, 2011 - Straits Times

    Stallholders in Aljunied GRC do this voluntarily till Dec 31, to support retail price watchdog

    By Ng Huiwen

     

    FOR the past 20 years, Mr Ng Boo Leng, 48, owner of Dragon Prawn Noodle, has been selling his signature prawn noodles at $2 a bowl.

    Despite rising inflation, he has no plans to increase his prices.

    On Saturday morning, he received a personal 'thank you' from Aljunied GRC MP Cynthia Phua, who went around to his stall and 45 others at the Hainanese Village Centre, a market and food centre on Hougang Avenue 1. She was there to thank them for their voluntary pledge to keep their food prices constant till Dec 31 this year.

    Said Mr Ng in Mandarin: 'I've been selling prawn noodles for so long, so I have many regular customers. I had to reduce the number of prawns in my noodles, but my customers know that I still promise quality.'

    Hawkers have blamed the rising cost of ingredients and fuel for them increasing their prices, while patrons have complained about having to cope with inflation too.

    Said Madam Phua: 'The stallholders really don't need to, but for them to make this commitment, I'm really very happy, because the residents will benefit. I believe this is where, in the community itself, it shows that everyone plays a part.'
