    Mar 25, 2011 - Straits Times

    by Elena Chong

    A WOMAN who has been placed on probation twice before for shoplifting because of her mental disorder, is back in court again for shop theft.

    But Goh Lee Yin's case was adjourned to May as her psychiatric report was not ready.

    The 30-year-old engineer's lawyer, Mr Choo Si Sen, applied for the adjournment on Friday as he was still waiting for the report from the Institute of Mental Health.

    Counsel and Goh asked the court to order the media not to publish her photograph but Community Court Judge Ng Peng Hong rejected it.

    Goh, who was previously diagnosed with kleptomania, has been accused of stealing a Gucci dress worth $3,199 from Gucci at Paragon on Dec 16 2010.

    She also allegedly stole a Chanel classic bag worth $4,790 from the Chanel boutique at Ngee Ann City on Feb 24, and a $610 Emporio Armani sweater at Meritus Mandarin Gallery in December 2010.

    The maximum penalty for theft in dwelling is a jail term of seven years and a fine.
