4 chinamen at a hawker stall

  • Rock^Star
    15 Dec 11, 21:31

    Just only, I went to a coffeeshop to order hokkien mee. I was surprised to see all 4 workers (all chinamen) laboriously toiling away. One taking orders and sending, another one cooking, another washing and the last one packing the food. And the one packing even shouted across in English to a customer seated outside: "Eh boss, you want chilli ah?" And then he started beckoning to his colleague in heavily accented mandarin (me thinks maybe it's the accent of Sichuan).

    As I sat down to watch the 7pm kungfu drama, I thought to myself that these chinamen are really hungry to make it in life. They forced themselves to learn and cook our local style of food, they learned to speak English, they are quick and responsive to customers.....and my gut feel tells me they are no university graduates but some poor blokes from the villages in China.

    And I saw images of our ancestors in them. I'm referring to our forefathers who have once upon a time come down south to SEA. Did our forefathers work like them? Learn the Malay language? Learn each other's dialect? And that's how they all made it.

    I shall stop short of saying foreigners, be they educated or uneducated......are going to take over Singapore one day. Oh btw, their hokkien mee was not salty enough but nevertheless a decent attempt.
