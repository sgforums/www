    Woman quizzed in Chinese flag case


    A Chinese flag hanging next to a Singapore flag at an HDB block in Hougang Avenue 3. Police said such displays are "generally disallowed". PHOTO: STOMP

     

    POLICE are investigating a 54--year-old Singaporean woman in connection with an offence under the National Emblems (Control of Display) Act.

      The Straits Times understands that the case is linked to an incident last week, where a Chinese national flag was flown in public next to a Singapore flag, at a Housing Board flat in Hougang.

      The woman, however, was not arrested.

      A photograph of the incident first appeared on citizen journalism website Stomp last Saturday.

      The police said in a Facebook post yesterday that the public display of state flags of any nation other than Singapore "is generally disallowed" unless an exception is catered for under the law.

      Those who do so can be jailed for up to six months, fined up to $500 or both.

      This is the second incident involving a national flag in recent weeks. Last Tuesday, a 13-year-old girl was arrested after she allegedly set fire to the Singapore flag in Ang Mo Kio.

      The flag that was set alight was one of 10 hanging in a public pavilion. Its remains were taken away by the police as evidence.

      The Ministry of Information, Communications and Arts issued a reminder earlier this months urging residents and business to start flying the Singapore flag.

      Yesterday, the ploce also reminded the public that the display of the Singapore state flag is governed under the Singapore Arms and Flag and National Anthem Act.

      The Act allows people to fly the Singapore flag at Housing Board blocks during the National Day period. This came after rules were amended in 2007 so flags could be flown over a three-month period from July to September, instead of just one month in August.

      Under the law, it is an offence to treat the national flag with disrespect. Offenders can be fined $1000.

     

    WALTER SIM
