    Haze could return in July

    By Amresh Gunasingham

    The haze could make an unwanted comeback in July if vigilance to curb forest fires in Indonesia is not stepped up, meeting of Asean environment ministers warned on Friday.

    The number of hotspots detected this month was an increase from March, despite this being traditionally a rainy season and weather experts predicting a weakening of the El Nino weather pattern, which brings hotter and drier weather.

    At a meeting of the sub-regional steering committee on transboundary haze pollution held in Balikpapan, East Kalimantan on Friday, Indonesia, which has been the epicentre of much of the acrid pall that has blown this way for much of the last decade, also pledged to cut the number of hotspots by 20 per cent every year, starting from this year.

    In a statement, the ministers said countries will be on alert and were committed to mitigating the fires.
