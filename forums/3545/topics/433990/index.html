    Jul 9, 2011 - ST Forum

    MR MARCUS Yip's Forum Online letter on Monday ('Preserving a slice of history') took me back

    to memories of an older Singapore.

    In our haste to join the First World nations, we have unwittingly erased the old Singapore and what our forefathers had built in the past century.

    I always associate Penang in Malaysia with the hawker stalls along its hawkers' row in Gurney Drive and Malacca with the nonya food along Jonker Street.

    Which visitor to Thailand could forget the famous Victory Monument at the Ratchathewi district in downtown Bangkok? The Albert Memorial in Kensington Gardens, England, still stands today since it was first opened in 1872. Every country has some famous landmark that is worth preserving for posterity.

    In my courting days, I used to patronise old Bidadari cemetery and the old cemetery at the present KK Women's and Children's Hospital. The dead did not disturb the living and we could coexist peacefully even in the suburbs of old Singapore. The famous chendol at the old Rex cinema and the delicious Indian rojak of Waterloo Street are now history.

    I have always wondered why the Esplanade Theatres on the Bay sleeps throughout the day and comes to life only after 7pm. Its concert hall, which can seat 1,800 music lovers, is seldom full.

    We should cultivate what is home-grown, and such culture and traditions will be here to stay.

    Tourists are not impressed by our Helix bridge and the Singapore Flyer but the Singapore Zoo and Jurong BirdPark seem to pull in the crowds every day of the week.

    Heng Cho Choon
