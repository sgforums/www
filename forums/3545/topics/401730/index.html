    Litterbugs, be warned

    By Low Yi Qian, Multimedia Journalist

    LITTERBUGS, consider yourselves warned. The National Environment Agency and NParks are tired of telling people to pick up their own rubbish, so their new anti-littering campaign this year will make sure offenders are duly punished if found guilty of littering.

    Last year, more than 8,000 summons were issued, with 40 per cent of those coming from the East Coast Park alone.

    This despite the 1,000 odd bins dotted all over the park which sees up to 7 million visitors annually.

    'We will follow NEA's stipulation in their anti littering campaign. NParks, where suitable, will consider implementing Community Work Order (CWO) in NParks,' said NParks spokesperson Bernard Lim

    That means if you litter, you may end up cleaning the trash.
