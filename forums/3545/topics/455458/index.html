    Posted: 10 July 2012 1825 hrs

    SINGAPORE: Staff from Gardens by the Bay have visited the 22-year-old woman who was bitten by a stray dog at Bay East Gardens on Monday night.

    According to an eye-witness, three stray dogs attacked the woman while she was strolling through the gardens.

    One of the dogs had bitten the woman's legs and the bloodstains could be clearly seen.

    The woman was sent to Tan Tock Seng Hospital, and has since been discharged.

    A spokesman from the park says staff will continue to render her assistance.

    The spokesman adds that the park has been working with the Agri-Food and Veterinary Authority to set up traps at Bay East Gardens.

    An AVA spokesperson said that they have been conducting control operations in the vicinity of the Marina Grove and Marina mall since this January, in view of public feedback on stray dog sightings at construction sites in the area.

    To date, seven dogs were rounded up, of which two were re-homed and five were euthanised humanely.

    The park has advised those who encounter stray dogs to stay calm, slow down and walk in a different direction from the stray dogs.
