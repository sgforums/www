    Outstanding Contribution Awards specially for new Singaporean citizens and PRs

     April 25th, 2012 |  Author: Editorial

    Photo CNA

    The Singapore Hua Yuan Association on Tuesday opened up nominations for its second “New Immigrant Outstanding Contribution Awards” to immigrants from beyond the Chinese community.

    The move aims to give recognition to new Singaporean citizens or permanent residents who have contributed to Singapore.

    The association hopes to select candidates who have contributed in areas including the arts, science and technology, commerce and education.

    It is not known why new immigrants have to be specially singled out for receiving such awards for their contributions to Singapore. Shouldn’t new Singaporean citizens be treated the same as any other Singaporean citizens?

    It is also not known why permanent residents, who are essentially foreigners not prepared to sink permanent roots in Singapore by becoming citizens, are specially singled out for receiving the “New Immigrant Outstanding Contribution Awards” too. The strong S$ which they are earning in Singapore should be sufficient “awards” already for their “contributions” to Singapore.
