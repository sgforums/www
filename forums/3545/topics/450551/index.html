    Apr 12, 2012 - ST Forum

    PRIME Minister Lee Hsien Loong said one of his main concerns about Singapore is the divide between Singaporeans and new arrivals ('PM Lee flags two worries'; last Thursday).

    This divide, if allowed to widen, may become Singapore's Achilles' heel if it is not managed properly.

    It is only natural that Singaporeans have such anxieties as the men serve two years of national service and are liable for annual reservist training, while foreigners enjoy the fruits of stability without having to make such sacrifices.

    For Singaporeans to complete national service, and to then see foreign scholarship recipients enjoying free education and competing for housing and jobs, adds to their anxiety.

    While the Government has heeded calls to prioritise Singaporeans after last May's General Election, more can be done to address these anxieties.

    These measures need not be drastic but should be wide-reaching in our daily lives, so that Singaporeans can feel special about their citizenship.

    Some suggestions include differential pricing for citizens, those who have done national service, permanent residents and foreigners, on daily necessities such as transport, utilities and town council service and conservancy charges.

    As a country, we have reached a point where foreigners must be part of the talent pool for Singapore to succeed.

    While we acknowledge and accept this, let us not create a generation of disenfranchised Singaporeans who no longer feel for Singapore.

    Otherwise, whatever economic success we have achieved will be a pyrrhic victory.

    Elgar Lee
