    S’porean on the run in Thailand after alleged murder

    Yahoo! Newsroom – Mon, Nov 28, 2011

    • A Singaporean man is on the run in Thailand after allegedly stabbing a Thai man to death in Bangkok. (AFP file photo)

    A Singaporean man is on the run in Thailand after allegedly killing his girlfriend’s Thai lover.

    A closed-circuit television video showed what seemed to be 38-year-old Hsu Wei Hua stabbing Thai drifter Ittiphan “Wai” Kuwakorn, 26, outside the sixth-floor apartment of the Regent Home condominium in Bangkok, reported The New Paper (TNP).

    According to The Straits Times’ (ST) report, media reports in Thailand said that Hsu had been dating Thai national Siriwan Yaemsi, 25, for three years, while TNP wrote that they were married.

    Singapore police, when contacted, also could not comment on the case or confirm Hsu’s identity as the matter is being investigated by Thai police.

    In the video footage that TNP described, Hsu knocked on his girlfriend’s apartment door at around 1pm on Thursday. When nobody answered, he appeared to have eavesdropped for about 30 minutes before kicking the door in.

    Hsu then caught Siriwan and Ittiphan naked in the apartment together. After confronting the Thai man, whom he had supposedly hired as Siriwan’s chauffeur, Hsu took a knife and stabbed the unclothed Ittiphan multiple times along the corridor outside the apartment.

    The drifter died in the unit’s bedroom after staggering back in, said TNP.

    It added that the Singaporean fled the scene in a black BMW 325i that he had given his wife. However, he did not take his passport and Singapore driving licence.

    Hsu’s identity is proving difficult to confirm, with people living at his listed address in Yishun denying any knowledge of the man, reported ST.

    The Bangkok police are reportedly still on the look-out for Hsu.
