A tap is all seniors need for more crossing time

  • Clivebenss
    19 Feb 11, 11:27

    Feb 19, 2011 - ST Forum

    THE Land Transport Authority (LTA) generally allots a longer time for the signal allowing pedestrians to cross the road at crossings where there are more vulnerable pedestrians, such as the elderly and physically challenged (''Green man' too quick for the old and grey' by Mr M. Lukshumayeh; Forum Online, Feb12).

    We have also implemented the Green Man Plus scheme since October 2009 at five pilot locations near health facilities and transport nodes. This scheme allows the elderly up to five seconds more green man time when activated.

    Seniors can activate this function simply by tapping their senior citizen concession pass on the card readers. We are currently studying the expansion of this scheme to other locations.

    At very busy major road junctions, the timings of our traffic lights must strike a balance between traffic efficiency and consideration for pedestrians.

    This means that the timings allocated have to be effective in ensuring that vehicular traffic flow is not too severely impeded by the extra time we give to pedestrians to cross safely.

    We thank Mr Lukshumayeh for his letter.

    Helen Lim (Ms)
    Deputy Director, Media Relations
    Land Transport Authority
