    Apr 12, 2010 - The Straits Times

    Are English standards slipping?

    By Shawn Lee Miller, Multimedia Journalist

    WHAT DO ex-beauty queen Ris Low and video game character Bolo Santosi have in common? They have both put Singlish in the spotlight in recent times.

    That led us to question how far the English language has evolved in Singapore. With the Speak Good English Movement into its tenth year, has our standard of English taken a step back?

    Dr KK Seet, Senior Lecturer at the Department of English Language and Literature at the National University of Singapore, offered his theory.

    'The standard of English here has been deteriorating in the twenty years that I've been teaching. In the early years, the students spoke better. I attribute it to the fact that it's an MTV generation, people have shorter attention spans and they don't read enough,' he surmised.

    Are Singaporeans getting so accustomed to speaking in Singlish that proper English has taken a backseat?
