    Oct 15, 2010 - Straits Times

    by Teh Joo Lin

    WITHIN three weeks, police have caught a 20-year-old suspect in connection with the murder of a woman in Woodlands Drive on Sept 22, following the release of a revised photofit of the suspect earlier this week.

    Officers from the Criminal Investigation Department's special section tracked down the male suspect and arrested him on Thursday.

    Police said on Friday that preliminary investigations revealed that the suspect is also believed to be involved in the attack of a woman jogger at Khatib Bongsu Park Connector on Feb 2.

    The suspect will be charged in court on Saturday for murder, and faces the death penalty if he is convicted.

    CID Director, Senior Assistant Commissioner Ng Boon Gay commended the officers who worked round-the-clock to solve the case.

    In the Woodlands case, the body of Ms Hoe Hong Lin, 32, a clerk, was found with at least one stab wound, next to a basketball court beside Block 895C, Woodlands Drive 50, after celebrating the Mid-Autumn Festival with her husband, son and nephew in a playground about 200m away from their block.

    She was believed to have been attacked on her way back after walking her nephew to her sister's flat at nearby Woodlands Drive 14.

    In the other case, a health-care worker was attacked on Feb 2 at the Khatib Bongsu park connector in Yishun Avenue 6. During the struggle, she grabbed the knife, causing it to break at the handle. The blade was later recovered at the scene.

    The victim suffered multiple wounds on her face and upper body, and a C-shaped open wound on her right cheek She was slashed just four days after another woman was knifed nearby, also in Yishun.

    Following the park attack, police released a photofit put together by the 23-year-old jogger, which showed a man with longer hair and smaller eyes. In a new clearer photo released on Tuesday, the suspect has a short crop and strong features.

    SINGAPORE : A 20-year-old full-time national serviceman has been arrested in connection with the murder of a 32-year-old woman at Woodlands Drive 50 that took place on September 22.

    He is also believed to be involved in another stabbing attack that happened in February.

    Earlier this week, police released a photofit of the assailant believed to be involved in the stabbing attack on a female jogger along the Khatib Bongsu Park connector on February 2. He is also said to be connected to the murder of Hoe Hong Lin at Woodlands Drive 50 on September 22.

    The suspect, a full-time national serviceman with the Republic of Singapore Navy, was handed over to the police on Thursday evening.

    The Defence Ministry said he was in custody of the Singapore Armed Forces Military Police Command, awaiting trial for the offence of Absent Without Official Leave.

    He is believed to have attacked Ms Hoe at a park while she was on the way home at night. A 13-centimetre knife was recovered at the scene.

    Police said the suspect has no criminal records, and that the motive behind the murder has not been determined, pending police investigations. But it is understood that the possibility that the suspect could be suffering from a mental illness has not be ruled out.

    Police said it was not an easy task to narrow down the suspect. The effort required the help of external agencies like the Health Sciences Authority, and forensic evidence played a key role in this case.

    Foo Jit Choon, assistant superintendent in the Special Investigation Section of CID, said: "With regards to the Woodlands murder, the unknown can be attributed to two factors - the victim and the suspect do not know each other, and secondly, at the time of the incident, it was very dark. Even though we had witnesses, they were unable to pinpoint to us exactly who was the suspect."

    Police are also investigating if the suspect is also involved in two other similar attacks in January and May this year.

    In all the four cases, the victims are Chinese women in their 20s and 30s, slim built and have shoulder length hair.

    They were alone at the time of the attack and nothing was stolen from them.

    It also appears that they were targeted at random.

    Ms Hoe leaves behind her husband and two children.

    Her eldest brother Nicholas Hoe said the family was informed of the arrest on Thursday night.

    He said the family feels relieved and hopes justice would be served.

    Mr Hoe said: "Let's say if this guy has no motive and it is just a random attack, I believe it is really unfair to my sister, she is really innocent. It is very hard, especially for my parents, but we have been trying to call them, trying to make sure that they are fine.

    "My brother also tries to bring them out, so that they would not just stay at home and keep thinking about my sister. Last night, after we received the information from police that they have detained the suspect, I informed my family and they also feel relieved."

    The suspect will be charged in court on Saturday with murder.

    If convicted, he faces the death penalty. - CNA/ir/ms
