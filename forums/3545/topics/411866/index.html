Keep rentals low to raise quality of hawker fare

    Sep 27, 2010 - ST Forum

     

    I READ with great interest the commentaries ('Singapore's amnesia of the taste buds'; Sept 22 and 'Celebrate our hawkers with Culinary Medallion'; Sept 14) which suggested a few ways to address the decline in the quality of Singapore's hawker food.

    While creative, I do not think those suggestions alone will nip this problem in the bud.

    One look at last Tuesday's report ('Sengkang market turns into ghost town') and it becomes clear that we may have overlooked an important factor that is more economic in nature: rising rentals of hawker stalls.

    If we want good hawker food to flourish in Singapore, we must stop squeezing out our hawkers.

    While upgrading hawker centres is a good idea, raising rentals simply makes the hawkers' life harder.

    In addition, turning over the running of hawker centres to private operators is a bad idea as their main motive will always be to maximise profits. They will bid high to get the right to run the centre and then squeeze the hawkers to recoup their investments.

    Hawkers cannot mark up the prices of their food too much. This means that for them to earn more, they must sell more. However, there is a limit to how many bowls or plates they can sell each day.

    Increasing rentals raises the cost of doing business significantly for them and they will have no choice but to resort to cost-cutting measures like changing the quality of ingredients used and how they are procured. Is it any wonder that the quality of hawker fare has gone down across the board?

    If we do not want to lose our hawker food heritage, we must build an environment that makes it financially viable for existing hawkers and even their successors to do well.

    Our hawker food culture is something worth keeping and the Government should continue to nurture that which is uniquely Singaporean.

     

    Joseph Khoo
