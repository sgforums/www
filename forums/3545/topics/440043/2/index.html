    The casino have no legal rights to refuse payouts, even if it was indeed a malfunction.

    In Singapore, there had been cases of Singapore Pools printing out scratch-it cards and lottery slips with mistakes, resulting in the gambler winning. Even though it was their mistake, the bet had been placed, a transaction has been made, and thus the operator is legally responsible to deliver what they have sold.

    Same too for the casino. The woman had inserted her token. the machine(the casino), has accepted the chip, and that completes the transaction. The casino is legally responsible to ensure that their equipment and machines are functioning properly. If it shows that she won $400k, even when she might have actually only won $50k, they are still responsible for delivering their promise of a $400k payout.

    If going by their logic, since the machine has malfunctioned, and therefore making the woman's claims invalid, then it would also have made all the bets they collected from all the gaming machines invalid, as malfunction = unfairness, and that means the previous gamblers could have been cheated off their money by a malfunctioning machine.

    And since all the machines are connected to a system, malfunctioning of a single machine will also mean that there's something wrong with all the other machines connected on the system. And hence, if they refuse to pay the woman, all the previous gamblers who have played on the machines have the rights to refunds from MBS.
