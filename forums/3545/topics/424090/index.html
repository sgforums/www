  • Clivebenss

    Mar 3, 2011 - ST Forum

    I REFER to Tuesday's reports ('PAP MPs question who WP is trying to help'; and 'Rebates, subsidies ease hardship only in short-term, says Low').

    Worker's Party (WP) chief and MP for Hougang Low Thia Khiang and People's Action Party (PAP) MP for Holland-Bukit Timah GRC Christopher de Souza were both right in disagreeing about lowering the goods and services tax (GST).

    While Mr de Souza is right that higher consumption by high-income earners leads to greater tax collection which is redistributed more among the lower-income group, Mr Low is also correct in saying that lowering the GST will directly help the lower-income group.

    The immediate benefit will be felt more than the indirect benefits in the form of tax rebates, which is exactly what this group of citizens need.

    The Government should review lowering the GST on daily essentials like food items which will help the needy, while the GST on non-essentials or luxury items should be maintained as they affect mainly the high-income group.

    Ong Chee Keong
