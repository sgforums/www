    MY WIFE and I are in our mid-30s. We married in 2009 and balloted for a flat that year but failed. We tried again last year but to no avail. Thus, we approached our MP Sam Tan (Tanjong Pagar GRC) for help.

    My wife had previously bought a Housing Board flat with her widowed mother. She sold it so that we could set up a home and start a family. I have not bought a flat or owned any properties before. However, the Housing Board said we are considered second-timers.

    We knew the criteria and restrictions that disqualified us from balloting as a first-timer for a Housing Board flat. That was why we approached our MP and appealed to the Housing Board to consider our case, as my wife's previous purchase of a flat was to provide for her widowed mother, who is now living with her other children.

    My wife has passed the optimal childbearing age. She is 35 this year.

    Since getting married, we have been living in my parents' four-room flat, which has six occupants. Without a place to call our own, my wife has almost given up the thought of starting a family. This very thought frightens me.

    We recently approached our MP for help again. However, we again received what looked like an automated reply from the Housing Board, explaining why we don't qualify as first-timers.

    Doesn't our situation warrant an assessment on a case-by-case basis?

    Ghee Soo Kee
