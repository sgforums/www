    Jan 20, 2011 - ST Forum

    REDRESSING FALLING BIRTH RATES

    CALLS for even more pro-baby handouts to persuade couples to have children, by arguing that other First World countries with a similar problem offer them, should be taken with a huge pinch of salt ('Fertility figures hit all-time low'; Tuesday).

    Singapore should not slide into welfarism and the state should not take over parental responsibilities.

    Ramping up pro-baby handouts to reverse a falling birth rate may result in throwing good money after bad.

    Falling birth rates are a global trend and not exclusive to Singapore.

    Despite resentment from some quarters, we should continue to open our doors to immigrants, selecting those who are not here to milk the system but ones who are self-reliant and ready and willing to contribute to Singapore's growth.

    The present incentives for parents are sufficient, have cost taxpayers billions of dollars and should not be seen as an unending stream. There are much better ways to spend public revenue.

    Arthur Lim
