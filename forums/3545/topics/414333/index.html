    Oct 30, 2010 - Straits Times

    By Ng Kai Ling

    THE Singapore Federation of Chinese Clan Association (SFCCA) will be making changes to its constitution to allow for new members and more council members.

    The new constitution, expected to be passed by the middle of next year, will open doors to immigrant groups and associations not formed on the basis of geography and kinship to be associate members.

    These will include groups that are registered as businesses and not societies, said SFCCA's chairman Chua Thian Poh at a swearing-in ceremony of the 13th council held on Saturday at Suntec City.

    'We are still working out the details on the rights of this new category of associate members,' he said.

    Another amendment will allow the federation to increase the number of council members from the current 15 to a number that will be decided.

    'If we want to do more and plan more activities, we need to bring in more council members so there is a stronger sense of commitment,' said Mr Chua, who is also the chairman and chief executive of property developer, Ho Bee Group.
