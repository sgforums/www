    Oct 4, 2010 - Straits Times

    PHONE scams involving the kidnap ruse and impersonation of government officials are resurging, police warned on Monday.

    In the 'kidnap scam', the scammers would trick their victims into transferring money into a bank account by claiming that their next-of-kin had been kidnapped and would be harmed if money was not transferred to the caller's bank account.

    In the 'impersonation scam', the scammers impersonate police officers or staff of the Supreme Court and ask the victims to transfer money into bank accounts to exonerate themselves from criminal cases or court hearings

    Police have noted a new method used by these scammers, who were able to display the genuine telephone numbers of a police department or that of the Supreme Court on the caller ID of the victim's phones, although no such calls were made. Examples of official numbers used by the scammers include: 63381034 (Supreme Court Enquiry Hotline), 64350000 (CID Hotline) and 999 (Police).

    Police said while it is normal during the course of their work for investigators to inform members of the public over the phone on court dates and appointments at the police station, they will never ask for money to be deposited into a bank account.

    'The public is advised to be wary when receiving such phone calls,' said a police statement. 'Cheating scams may take different forms but these scams are all designed to cheat victims into parting with their money.'
