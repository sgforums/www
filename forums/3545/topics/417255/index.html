Let's not under-promote ourselves

  • Clivebenss
    8 Dec 10, 10:55

    Dec 8, 2010 - ST Forum

    WITH reference to Dr Thomas Lee's letter ("Self-testimonials: False modesty is a liability"; Nov 27), I believe we under-promote ourselves too much.

    I keep seeing jobs going to foreigners in my office simply because they stand out in their ability to market themselves, even in corporate reviews. Westerners are also very good at viewing negative traits as challenges, not liabilities.

    One concern about students writing their own testimonials has to do with how their teachers would vet them. If the teachers are predisposed by cultural conditioning to be critical, they may vet "down" a student's write-up.

    Teachers play a critical role here. We should promote a more nurturing mindset among teachers in schools. They have to learn how to bring out the best in our students beyond academic achievements.

    They have to see students' non-academic abilities as "pluses" and not "minuses", even if these abilities do not contribute directly to grades.

    As a people, we are too conditioned to tell ourselves we are just not good enough. Our parents and their parents were also not trained to encourage their children. Instead, they preferred to discipline through pointing out mistakes. We are culturally conditioned to think negatively of ourselves.

    Low Hui Lin (Ms)
