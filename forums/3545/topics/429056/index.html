    Apr 28, 2011 - ST Forum

    QUEENSTOWN is one of the oldest housing estates built by the Housing Board. Now, the central area - around Queenstown MRT station, Margaret Drive, Commonwealth Avenue and Stirling Road - is deserted. It is like a 'ghost town', especially after 6pm.

    During a 'Let's Chat' session five years ago with our MP, we were shown the redevelopment plan for this area. There will be five 40-storey blocks of flats and other facilities in front of the Queenstown Library, stretching up to the MRT station. What is the progress of this redevelopment?

    Blocks 172 to 175 in Stirling Road were demolished a few years ago and the land is still vacant. There are also many plots of empty land along both sides of Alexandra Road, from Delta Road up to Tanglin Road. It would be ideal if the Housing Board can build more blocks of flats there.

    John Pereira
