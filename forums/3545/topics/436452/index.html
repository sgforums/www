    Burnt National flag hung by PAP found at Marine Parade   

     August 19th, 2011 |  Author: Contributions
    Temasek ReviewI

    In a sign that Singaporeans are getting sick and tired of the repressive PAP regime, a burnt flag hung by PAP grassroots members (or Bangla workers) was found outside a flat in Marine Terrace on Monday under Marine Parade GRC.

    It had already burnt itself out by the time the Singapore Civil Defence Force arrived on the scene a little after 7pm. The police are looking into the matter.

    According to a residen who lived on the 24th story of the 25-storey block, flags were hung at every floor for National Day. The burnt flag may have come from the banister of the stairwell.

    “I went out at 1pm and came back around 6pm. When I came out of the lift, I noticed burnt material on the floor. Upon closer inspection, I saw that it had been burnt in many parts, but it looked like the national flag, so I called the town council,” she told the state media.

    Despite a massive propaganda campaign by the state media and PAP grassroots organizations to implore Singaporeans to hang the National flag to ‘celebrate’ National Day, few flags were hung outside the windows of units in HDB flats.

    As such, the ‘face-conscious’ PAP regime has no choice but to activate its grassroots members and in some cases even employed Bangladeshi workers to hang the National flag along the common corridor and staircases to generate ‘fake’ support for the unpopular regime.

    While we condemned this act of burning our National flag, we urged PAP leaders to reflect on why such an incident had happened when it was unheard of in the past.
