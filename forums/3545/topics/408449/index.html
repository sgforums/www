    Aug 18, 2010 - Straits Times

    Fewer seek help from CDCs

    by Amanda Lee

    FEWER Singaporean residents are seeking help from grassroots organisations.

    All five Community Development Councils (CDCs), which function as local administrations of various districts in Singapore, offer community and social assistance services for residents.

    With improving economic conditions and more job opportunities, the number of residents seeking help at CDCs has dropped sharply.

    Only half as many - or 5,600 - registered for job help for the quarter ended June, from a year ago, said a joint statement from the five CDCs on Wednesday.

    During that period, the number fell from 2,000 in April to 1,800 in June.

    The drop has also led to fewer job seekers being placed - from 5,000 in the same quarter last year to 4,000 this year.

    Mr Matthias Yao, Mayor of South East CDC attributed the decline to the economic recovery. '"As the economy improves, more companies are hiring. Unemployment remains low, and job seekers find it easier to get jobs on their own,' he said.

    Mayors of the five CDCs are now focusing on skills upgrading for job seekers.

    "Upskilling and employment is the best social safety net for them (residents)" said Dr Teo Ho Pin, Mayor of North West CDC.

    Mr Zainudin Nordin, Mayor of Central Singapore CDC, encouraged resident to continue to upgrade themselves to enhance their employability.

    CDCs have started various initiatives. North West CDC has launched a Workfare Training Support scheme, the South West CDC has launched an 'Upskill the Unskilled' initiative.

    All the CDCs also work closely with ComCare Local Network, a series of schemes to provide social assistance for needy residents.
