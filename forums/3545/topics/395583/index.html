    Protect Merlion from snap-happy tourists

    OVER-ENTHUSIASTIC tourists are damaging the tiles at the base of the Merlion and putting themselves in danger - just to get that postcard-perfect photo.

    They trample on the 'waves' at the base of the Merlion, causing damage to the tiles. Imagine someone getting a deep cut because of the broken tiles, or worse, a child slipping and falling into the Singapore River.

    The Singapore Tourism Board or the authorities concerned should do something before someone gets hurt or more damage is done to the Merlion.

    Those cheap plastic chains surrounding the Merlion obviously do nothing to deter people from climbing over. Perhaps the next time the Merlion has a facelift - I hope before the Youth Olympic Games in August - a planter bed of bougainvillea (prickly but pretty) could be placed around the base to protect the landmark from the mob of tourists thronging the park every day.

    Seow Choon Kee
