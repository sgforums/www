    Sep 24, 2011 - Straits Times

    Waiter left accomplice to rob fast-food outlet alone

    A FILIPINO waiter planned to rob a fast-food restaurant but got cold feet at the last moment and left his accomplice to carry out the crime alone.

    Porte Gerald Christian Magsalubong, 30, who was originally charged with attempted armed robbery, pleaded guilty on Friday to an amended charge of having a dangerous weapon without lawful authority. He was jailed for five months.

    Porte was armed with a bread knife on June 8, when he and a compatriot planned to rob the Burger King outlet at International Plaza, Anson Road.

    His co-accused, Charlie Cabo Perez, 32, is a former Burger King assistant manager who tried to rob the manager of the outlet of $19,221, the contents of the safe.
