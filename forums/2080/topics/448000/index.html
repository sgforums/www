  • fudgester
    27 Feb 12, 14:25

    Circle Line works both ways.

    It allows some people to bypass the two main lines. I, for one, used to take the MRT from Buona Vista to the east in order to go home from NUS. Now I take the Circle Line all the way to Paya Lebar and bypass the city center in the process.

    On the other hand, it also directs more people towards the main lines. After all, the Circle Line is a 'feeder' line of sorts for the main lines.

    Let's say someone wants to go from Buona Vista to Ang Mo Kio. In the past, he would go down to the city, change at Raffles Place/City Hall, and take the northbound train. Now he would take the CCL to Bishan and change trains there. So you could say that the crowds have shifted from Raffles Place/City Hall to Bishan.

    End result? I ain't gonna complain about the utility of the Circle Line. I only wish they had actually built larger stations and used six-car trains instead of three.
