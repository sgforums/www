    2 Boon Lay stations open <!--<span class="timestamp">10 min</span>-->

    <!-- headline one : end --><!-- Author -->   <!-- show image if available -->

    THE first new MRT stations since the Government began its plan to make over the public transport system were officially opened this morning.

    Transport Minister launched the two new stations, Pioneer and Joo Koon, that will form the Boon Lay extension on the East-West line.

    The two stations will open to the public from Saturday . They are expected to help cut travelling times by up to 15 minutes.

    Pioneer station, situated along Jurong West Street 63, will serve residents in the vicinity, who currently have to take a feeder bus to Boon Lay MRT.

    Joo Koon station, located in an industrial area, will provide a direct rail link for workers there.

    Calling the opening of the stations a significant milestone, Mr Lim said the new extension will 'help to improve the attractiveness of public transport as a choice mode of travel.'

    The Boon Lay extension is among $40 billion of rail projects that the Government is building to double the rail network here.

    The new extension cost $436 million to build and was completed in 3 1/2 years.
