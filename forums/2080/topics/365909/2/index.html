    Replying to the topic: Fully Automated NSL and EWL Definitely no to that as it will increase the amount of time the train stops at a station. The main problem lies in that NSL and EWL has stations above ground, which means that if there's a rain, the computer cannot accurately calculate the amount of brake needed to stop as friction is greatly reduced. If this full auto is implemented, the following will happen; -Rain sensors will be placed on all part of the above ground tracks. -Signalling system will communicate with train to apply dry mode or rain mode -Train check to implement dry or rain mode at intervals on above ground section -If dry mode, stop at station as per norm -If raining mode, stop implemented in steps of 6 (40km/h/138m, 30km/h/70m, 20km/h/40m, 10km/h/20m, 5/10m, stop/0m) and enter station at 40km/h. (Gonna take hell of a time to stop compared to CM mode, maybe like 40~60 seconds?) So is it worth the money and effort? If yes, please enlighten me.
