    SMRT launches Silver Tribute Fund to help lonely and needy elders

    SINGAPORE: Public transport company SMRT is going the extra mile to help the elderly in Singapore.

    Some 500 SMRT senior management, staff and elders from Geylang East Home for the Aged and Bright Hill Evergreen Home took to the streets to collect donations for the needy elderly.

    And it was a sea of red outside the Orchard MRT Station.

    Led by SMRT President and CEO Saw Phaik Hwa, the donation march marks the launch of the SMRT Silver Tribute Fund.

    The Fund aims to raise money for the abandoned, lonely seniors in need of care.

    Money raised will be channelled to eight beneficiaries to fund programmes that help the elderly lead more meaningful and dignified lives. - CNA/so
