    $1.13b DTL contracts awarded

    7 Nov 08

    THE Land Transport Authority has awarded six contracts worth $1.13 billion for the Downtown Line.

    The contracts, which will cover all three stages of the project, will include the procurement of trains and other electrical and mechanical systems.

    Among the contracts is one awarded to the consortium of Bombardier Transportation GmbH / Bombardier (Singapore) for the procurement of 73 three-car electric trains at a cost of $570.7 million.

    These trains will be fully automated and driverless. Such trains are being used for the North-East Line and will also be used for the upcoming Circle Line.

    Another $287.5 million contract was awarded to Westinghouse Brake and Signal Holdings to provide the signalling system and platform screen doors at the stations, said LTA in a statement on Friday.

    Other contracts awarded include that for the control and communications systems and also the building of lifts and escalators.

    Canadian railway company awarded LTA contract for Downtown Line

    A :$571-million contract for trains to be used in the planned Downtown Line has been awarded to Canadian railway engineer Bombardier by the Land Transport Authority (LTA).
    .
    Deliveries are due to begin in the last quarter of 2012 and will be completed in 2016, Bombardier announced on Friday.
    .
    The order is the latest effort to promote public transport and ease road congestion during rush hours.
    .
    Singapore wants to triple the number of daily journeys on its rail network to 4.6 million by 2020, and in January additional spending of $20 billion was announced, to double the rapid-transit network to 278 kilometres by 2020.
    .
    Construction on the Downtown Line, which will connect the Central Business District and Marina Bay area with the northwestern and eastern ends of the island, began on Feb 12, the LTA said.
    .
    The first stage of the project will be completed in 2013, with the second and third stages due in 2015 and 2016.
    .
    The 40km-long line is designed to transport almost half a million passengers every day.
    .
    Bombardier’s order, its third in the country, is for driver-less Movia metro vehicles, consisting of three-car train sets, the statement said.
    .
    The vehicles are able to operate under a fully-automated mode. They have high capacity aluminium car bodies developed from a standardised platform, ensuring a high degree of reliability, safety and low life-cycle cost, the company said.
    .
    The trains are designed for a maximum operational speed of 80kmh. They are sold as “environmentally friendly” through the use of the latest propulsion technology with low energy consumption. Their structure is up to 90 per cent recyclable.
    .
    Last year, Bombardier won a US$590 million ($881 million) contract to supply 340 Movia rail cars from Delhi Metro Rail Corp. The company has won orders for 3,330 Movia cars from cities such as London and Shanghai, Bombardier said.
    .
    Separately, Singapore Technologies Engineering said its electronics unit won $160 million worth of contracts from the LTA for the supply of communications system for projects including the Downtown Line. - BLOOMBERG
    .

    Article from Weekend TODAY Business Section
