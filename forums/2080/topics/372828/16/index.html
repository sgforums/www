  • TIB1224Y
    22 Apr 10, 18:14

    During my train survey on Monday I was lucky (ears were not) to hear the sound from 047/048 in particular to 1047. The sound was like some of the early JNR (Japan National Railways) Series 101/103 motors during constant heavy rush hour loads.

    Being in mind the rush hour in the 70s in Japan is about 250 to 300 percent load factor. I also noted that the Bukit Batok southbound hit 250 percent load factor for several trains; nonetheless, take a look at the data I have at that post and you will understand.

    Back to the motor, some of the Series 101/103 had this problem of overworked motors (they grow hot when working at standard acceleration figures, 20% overload of the motors necessary to maintain the acceleration rate) and according to some rather exaggerated accounts, the motor shredded itself from the intense heat generated by the high load that the train is carrying and injured passengers!

    The overloading is true, however injuring passengers cannot be affirmed.

    The motor sound from 1047 matches closely to a motor overload (constant sharp pitch magnetic sound upon start -off). If continued over a long time it would lead to potentially dangerous issues.

    Of course what I state here is just from sounds, which do not account to exact working situation. Shall leave this to the depot crews to rectify.
