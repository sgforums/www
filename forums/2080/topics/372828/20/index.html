    Originally posted by ObsessionStaris:

    What effect will it have if the trains have flat wheels?

    Since there are so many people asking technical questions yet no one is (can? :S) answer them...

    Flat wheels (or so termed) are formed when prolonged braking is exerted on one area of the wheel or prolonged skidding on that area. Although skidding seems seldom in our system, but during rain this happens and not only during braking, but also acceleration as our trains have high acceleration power (P4, 3.6 km/h/s).

    Cast wheels are circular in shape and have a set shape, meaning it cannot adjust itself like an inflated tyre. So when the surface becomes flat as the wheel is being worn, the flat surface will knock against the rail every revolution the wheel goes. As the wheel turns slowly, the sound may be periodical and bass like, but as the wheel speeds up the vibration and sound will increase.

    Flat wheels not only cause noise pollution but affects passenger comfort and damages motor/gear transfer components.

    The solution is to replace the wheel, send the older wheels for tuning (to make it smoothly circular again), but reconditioning can only be done limited times, once the wheel is smaller than its rated size (it can be only millimeters difference from new to old) it has to be discarded.
