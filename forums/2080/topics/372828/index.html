    If the sounds are coming from the motors, then no matter which line the train is travelling on, there will be such sounds.

    For the video on the second post it would most likely be caused by worn bearings in the motor or along the connecting rods.

    Faulty motor sound wise, many things can go wrong in the motor. Shall not elaborate too much, but basically it has to do with windings and bearings inside the motor. After some time of wear-and-tear, strange sounds will start to emit and its time to send the motors for a health check.

    Other than the motor, voltage controllers/regulators (GTO and IGBT controllers, most commonly used) may also emit irregular sounds if they are of a unhealthy condition, but normally these non-moving parts, when emitting noise, will deem a bigger problem than motors.

    Last but not least you have flat-wheels...
