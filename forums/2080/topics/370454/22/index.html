    Also, when the first train comes into Bishan (To Marymount, Was it Set 06? where I know I got see a 0 and a 6), due to too many people are alighting, and none passenger from Bishan managed to board the train -- furthermore, 2 passengers side by side was kiapped by the train and a few more passengers failed to alight at Bishan (Their destination is Bishan by the way, whether transfer or not is OT). So a few more minutes later, Set 25 comes in (To Marymount again, note: No trains to Dhoby Ghaut yet) and I managed to board in. (I suspect by then I come in, the faulty trains was sent back to depot)

    What happens is, when it turn around to Bishan, it kiapped people again (And I heard a child cried lah, and an unhappy passenger complained to a CSO citing the train door is closing way too fast and that is no good along with the enquiry of the staff's ability to open the train door becoz of waiting for about 17minutes of train and only 2 passengers [on a door] managed to board the train {you can imagine how the ****ing pax amount is}, to which the staff replied as these trains are automatic and they cant do anything to open it.)

     

    Such incidents happen again in Serangoon Interchange as well. None of these kiapping passenger happened in the non-Interchange stations. Futhermore, I was at the 1st PSD (HW for both directions)

    Company: SMRT
    Line: CCL
    Trainset: 30 (In fact most carriages were affected)
    Train Type: C830
    Station(s): All/Most
    Direction: Marymount Bound
    Date: 29 Apr 2010, Thur
    Time: Evening
    Reason: Signalling Problems

    Details:

    1. During the evening rush hour, a signalling problem appeared to have developed on the CCL. There was little or no information about next train timings for Marymount bound trains on the PIS.
    2. Set 30, the one which I took, stopped longer than per usual at two stations: CC6 Stadium (2 mins) and CC7 Mountbatten (3 mins). SMRT staff were seen walking up and down with walkie talkies communicating with staff about the problem.
    3. At Stadium station, another DBG bound train which pulled in just after set 30 entered was similarly delayed.
    4. Set 30 had to be operated in manual mode. At Mountbatten station, the signal kept showing red prompting the train not to move off from the station; it was only after a "positive" was received was the train given the go-ahead to continue.
    5. I questioned the staff working on set 30 and was told that there was a signalling problem.

    Company: SMRT
    Line: EWL
    Trainset: 025/026
    Train Type: C151
    Station(s): All/Most
    Direction: Eastbound (Terminating at EW1 Pasir Ris)
    Date: 29 Apr 2010, Thur
    Time: Evening
    Reason: Faulty Announcement/STARIS

    Details:

    1. 025/026 appeared to have problems with its announcement and STARIS systems when I found that no announcements were made. Even the door closing warning was not present, but the warning lights flashed as per normal.
