    SMRT has begun retrofitting first generation trains. 001 had the honour of doing its test run today on the NSL (002's 3 cabs have not been touched yet) with Train Run Number (TRN) "001".

    Seats on every cab will now have the three colours, red, green and blue. The outer two seats will be the original colours, followed by their darker variants for the middle 3 seats.

    The interior flooring has been re-done, but more significantly, the exterior of the train (002 included) is now as-per 3rd generation trains i.e. black colour with thicker red lining.

    A reliable source stated that all 66 KHI C151 trains will be refurbished. The trains are now in their midlife age after more than 15 years in service since 1987. The major refurbishment aims to replace major items of equipment that have degraded with aging, wear and tear. Coinciding with this would be the opportunity to incorporate operational, performance and safety enhancements for the trains. These will include larger stand-back space at the doorways for better accessibility into saloon and spaces for physically impaired at selected cars. Additional line flow fans to ventilate and enhance interior comfort for commuters.
    Better in-train announcements regulated through ambient sound level monitoring control and increase in speakers. The refurbishment work is done progressively because SMRT can't afford to put too many units off service.

    The refurbishment work is carried out in the Rolling Stock Workshops at Bishan Depot. The first refurbished train was expected to roll out in May 06 and the last train to complete in April 2008. Having said so, the first few units are already undergoing tests at Ang Mo Kio / Yishun / Bishan after revenue hours.
