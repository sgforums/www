    MRT graffiti case put off

    By Elena Chong

     

     

    A SWISS national accused of vandalising two carriages of an MRT train, cutting the fence of the Changi depot and entering a protected place is expected to plead guilty when his case comes up on Thursday.

    Oliver Fricker, a 32-year-old business consultant, showed up in court yesterday - his second appearance since June 5 - and the three charges were laid before him.

    In open court yesterday, his lawyer Derek Kang said he would send another letter of representation to the Attorney-General's Chambers in a bid to get his client's charges reduced.

    Following this, Fricker, his lawyer and Deputy Public Prosecutor Sharon Lim went into a pre-trial conference, during which the consultant's next court appearance was tentatively fixed for Thursday.

    Meanwhile, the Swiss national is out on bail of $100,000, but his passport has been impounded.

    He was taken to court earlier this month for allegedly damaging public property and entering a protected place together with Dane Alexander Lloyd between May 16 and 17. Lloyd, a Briton, has since left the country, and a warrant has been issued for his arrest.

    5 months, 3 strokes for Swiss

    By Elena Chong, Courts Correspondent

     

    SWISS IT consultant Oliver Fricker, 32, was sentenced to five months jail and three strokes of the cane on Friday for breaking into a protected place and vandalising an SMRT train by spray-painting two of its carriages

    He had earlier in the day pleaded guilty to committing the offences at MRT Changi depot between May 16 and 17 with his friend, Dane Alexander Lloyd, who has skipped town.

    Deputy Public Prosecutor Sharon Lim said the pair came to know each other in Australia about three years ago, and had been keeping in touch occasionally through e-mail. Earlier this year, the 29-year-old Briton told Fricker he would be coming here for a visit.

    At the end of March, Lloyd e-mailed Mr Arman Alluwie, who sells spray paint in Singapore, to enquire if Ironlak spray paint was available here. He later ordered 11 cans from Mr Arman and requested that they be delivered on May 16.

    That evening, the pair proceeded to SMRT Changi depot to reconnoiter the area and left shortly after because of the rain. Late that evening, both men, each carrying a small bag holding a few cans of spray paint, entered the depot after cutting a hole in the fence.

    Fricker sprayed the word McKoy on the left of the train carriage. The other word Banos was spray-painted on the right. Lloyd took some photographs of their work before they left through the gap.

    Fricker was arrested on May 25. The court heard that SMRT's estimated losses totalled about $11,000.
