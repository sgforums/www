    Originally posted by Yongjunzer:

    why got train 201, i thought is 201/202 same?


    TRN stands for Train Run Number, different from trainset number. It's located at the top right corner of the train ends. TRN is constant depending on the day. For example, Monday and Tuesday are working days. At 6.45am, the train arriving Tampines heading WB has TRN029. The next day, the TRN will be the same at the same time slot.

    However, if it is a Saturday, the 6.45am train arriving Tampines heading WB may not be TRN029, as Saturday is not a working day for most ppl, so frequency stands at 4-6mins.

    P.S. Different trains may run the same TRN on different days. For e.g. On Monday, 001/002 has TRN029, on Tuesday, it could be any other train or it could even be 001/002 again with TRN029.
