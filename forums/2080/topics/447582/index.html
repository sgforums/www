    Rail clip drops from viaduct near MRT station

    SINGAPORE: A rail clip has dropped from the viaduct near Clementi MRT Station in a second incident in four days.

    Thursday's incident followed a similar happening on Monday when a rail clip dropped from the viaduct near TradeHub 21.

    "We are concerned about the latest incidents. As a precaution, we will be installing safety nettings along these areas immediately after passenger service tonight," said SMRT Train's executive vice-president Mr Khoo Hean Siang in a statement on Friday.

    "We are also working with the authorities to install wire mesh along stretches of the viaduct where there is human activity. This will be done progressively in the next few weeks. We will also do a thorough check through the entire system and replace any clips that show signs of wear and tear."

    "Similar to the clip found on 20 Feb, the clip found yesterday will be sent for a laboratory test, to examine the reason for failure."

    "We would like to assure the public that we take a serious view of these recent incidents of broken rail clips, and are putting all available maintenance resources to look into the matter, to ensure that public safety is not compromised and normal train operations can continue smoothly."

    Train tracks maintenance not rigorous enough: commuters

    SINGAPORE: Some train commuters said maintenance of Singapore's train tracks have not been thorough enough.

    This comes after two metal rail clips fell off tracks near Clementi and Jurong stations within four days.

    The temporary safety nettings which have been installed at stretches between the Dover and Jurong East train stations on the East-West Line, will be replaced by wire mesh along viaduct railings.

    Operator SMRT said train speeds may have to be adjusted along tracks where clips fell off to minimise the recurrence of more parts falling off.

    Commuters felt more should be done.

    One commuter said: "If parts are actually falling off, I think there may be problems with auditing. If there's proper policy and procedure in place, this shouldn't happen at all."

    "They have to fix the parts soon. The parts are starting to break off already. They're old," said another.

    One female commuter also shared her concerns over the temporary safety nettings.

    She said: "Even if you put the net, the net may not be strong enough to protect the thing (metal rail clips) from falling down."

    Incidents of broken clips falling off train tracks are quite rare. Three other such incidents have reportedly occurred in the last four years.

    - CNA/fa
