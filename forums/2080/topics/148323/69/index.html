  • FSJZ
    20 Jun 10, 21:50
    Originally posted by linwei20:

    Why is it that the train always slow down/ brake whenever it exiting the tunnel towards Red Hill Station? But why not when exiting from Braddell to Bishan Station, the TO did not slow down or brake? Only towards Red Hill Station, i observe alot of times already.

    Maybe it is because there is a slope between Braddell and BSH, and gravity acts as a natural braking force, too small to be felt. It is important to slow down before exiting a tunnel because the changes in air pressure can push and pull on the door (if you have observed, whenever the train goes in a tunnel, the door gets pushed in by the pressure and when the train goes out, the door gets "sucked" out). Sometimes if speeds are high enough, it may cause a "bang" when the train exits the tunnel and this explains for why the tunnel exits in high speed rail lines are curved outwards to slowly dissipate the pressure.
