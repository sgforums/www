  • ^tamago^
    7 Oct 07, 10:14
    Originally posted by y33bas:
    how come that when north bound trains enter sembawang station at a much slower speed than usual as compared to the speed they enter yishun station. As a case by case basis, MOST trains enter slower while only a few odd cases that they do enter at the same speed..?
    Southbound also very slow. The reason for the slow speed is that it takes 3'30" on schedule to travel from Yishun to Sembawang and that the train is travelling uphill towards SBW unless the train is behind time then it will be normal.

    A short period of acceleration near the football field is normal for NB. For SB, this happens after Yishun Sapphire before the crossings.

    The same thing happens for Sembawang southbound, where it is travelling uphill. A train that needs to be faster will cheong up the hill, while a train that does not will slowly glide up.

    Interestingly, trains enter SBW at 60kph at NB and 50kph at SB. Not a lot of difference. YIS NB is about 60~65kph, SB is 70kph.
