    You can't really blame the train operators for increased waiting times... sometimes it is due to mechanical fault that caused the waiting time to increase, others is due to overcrowding. During off-peak, train timings are inaccurate, therefore the plasma screens that show the amount of waiting time left to next train is actually not true. For example, a 8-minute show on the plasma screen may actually be 6 mins, and a 5 minute-show on the plasma screen could be 9 mins. They get the information on next train timings based on timetables, and tele-communications. However during peak hours, there will be a certain delay of X minutes in between Train A and Train B, so therefore the station manager will show the timings on the plasma screen based on the delays, timetables and telecommunication. Now you know why peak hour timings are more accurate. Also, during the early mornings, the first 7-8 trains have very inaccurate timings. This is due to the time that they were sent out of depot for service.
