    It may still be a cheaper alternative, in the long run, to erect protection screens on MRT station platforms.

    There are only two conclusions we can draw from the seemingly regular suicide jumps onto MRT tracks in front of an oncoming train Â— either our MRT stations are designed to be so unsafe that it is easy to fall onto the tracks, or there is something not right in our society that people would choose to end their lives in this highly visible method so as to cause the maximum amount of chaos for others.

    Either reason is a blight on Singapore's reputation. Suppressing such news may prevent copycat suicides, but the root causes of such desperate suicidal behaviour must be tackled vigorously and remedied.
