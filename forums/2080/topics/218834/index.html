    MRT feasibility studies underway for Downtown Line

    SINGAPORE: A new 40-kilometre MRT line could be in the works to connect the Bukit Timah and eastern parts of the island to the New Downtown.

    Feasibility studies for this are currently underway.

    This and other projects are in the pipeline as the Transport Ministry takes stock of the public transport system and develops a new road map for the next 10 to 15 years.

    Last year, the Land Transport Authority announced plans for a Downtown Extension Line to serve the Marina Bay area.

    Now, it is carrying out studies to check the feasibility of extending this to the Bukit Timah and Eastern Region Lines.

    Together, all three could form a 40-kilometre Downtown Line that will connect people from the northwest and east to the planned Business and Financial Centre and integrated resort at Marina Bay.

    Previous reports have said that the Eastern Region Line will be a rectangular loop around the Jalan Besar area and the East Coast while the Bukit Timah Line will link Upper Bukit Timah to the city.

    Meanwhile, buses will continue to be a key part of the transport system - with the focus on making it more commuter-friendly such as reducing waiting times and considering options like full-day bus lanes.

    More rail lines and better bus services are only part of the solution. The review will also look at how the entire transport system is set up.

    One issue is the public transport operator setup, which new GPC Chairman for Transport Cedric Foo, feels can be improved.

    Mr Foo said: "The two are separately listed in the Exchange, they compete with each other. So, obviously they will not try to help one another and that creates problems for commuters.

    "For example, when SBS issued monthly seasonal passes, the same seasonal monthly pass cannot be used on SMRT."

    Over at the ports, work has started to make Pasir Panjang Terminal a mega-port that will be able to handle the next generation of ultra-large container ships.

    Whilst tax incentives were introduced earlier this year to maintain Singapore as a preferred base for shipping. Focus is also on growing maritime services to be a second engine of growth, creating more jobs and business.

    And Changi Airport's Terminal 3 is on schedule to open in 20-08, by which time Changi Airport will be able to handle up to 67 million passengers annually.

    Air travel will also become easier and cheaper as Singapore pursues more liberal air services agreements with key areas like China, India and ASEAN. - CNA/so

    MINISTRY OF TRANSPORT

    Network review for a more seamless journey
    Move to improve access, connectivity of bus, train services

    By T. Rajan

    THE Ministry of Transport (MOT) has begun a review of the land transport network in its drive to improve access and connectivity.

    One key thrust of the review is to develop the train and bus networks over the next few years.

    In the ministry's addenda to the President's Address at the opening of Parliament on Thursday, Transport Minister Raymond Lim also outlined his plans to shorten public transport travel times.

    'In a city-state like ours, the vast majority of Singaporeans will have to rely on public transport as their principal mode of travel,' he said.

    Mr Lim added that building a high-quality and affordable urban transport system is crucial. As the population grows, future travel needs will also increase.

    To help achieve these goals, the MOT will continue to invest in the public transport infrastructure, he said.

    Mr Lim noted that moves to expand the network are already under way. He cited the Circle Line as one example of works in progress.

    When it is completed, from 2010 onwards, travel to the city from the suburban areas like Pasir Panjang will be faster, bypassing busy stations like City Hall.

    Also, the development of Marina Bay has made better rail access to the area more important.

    The Downtown Line, approved last year, will allow commuters to get across the island faster to places like Marina Centre, he said.

    Also on the cards are two new lines to link the northwestern and eastern areas of the island to the 60km Downtown Line.

    Mr Lim said the Land Transport Authority (LTA) is conducting feasibility studies to see how it can link the Bukit Timah Line and the Eastern Regional Line to the Downtown Line.

    Mr Lim will also be training his focus on improving bus service quality and concerns over issues like waiting times and reliability.

    The Public Transport Council's new set of Quality of Service Standards, introduced in July, will help ensure this, he added.

    Mr Lim also said that improving access for the disabled to the public transport system will continue, with initiatives like buses with wheelchair access, launched four months ago.

    He said: 'The LTA will upgrade commuter and road facilities... to help not only less mobile Singaporeans move around more easily, but also the average commuter to have a more seamless journey from door to door.'
