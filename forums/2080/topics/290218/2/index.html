    Firstly, Yishun has a side track, which allows trains to change direction while main services are still going on. Note that when they built Yishun stations and it's tracks, they had already guessed that due to the large number of housing estates like TPY, AMK and YIS, passenger volumes would be very high at this section after CBD area. Thus they built the side track even before they had announced that trains would begin turning over at Yishun during peak hours. If you ask me, that's a genius way of thinking.
