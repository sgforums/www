    STOMPer Edmund is unhappy with the new, revised female voice-over announcements heard on MRT trains recently. He sent STOMP this email with audio clip this morning (Jan 26):

    "Firstly, what was wrong with the previous British-accented ones?

    "Secondly, how did SMRT arrive at the conclusion that they should change the announcements? There are no new additions apart from wrong phonics, enunciations and being unable to speak Malay fluently!

    "Thirdly, in the face of rising inflationary costs, and transport costs being passed down to consumers, why fix something that was not broken?

    "My colleagues and I found the new Singaporean-accented female announcements incorrectly pronouncing words and stations names. Have a listen to the audio clip to see what I mean.

    "For example: Buonavista = Bone-navista! And Clementi becomes Clare- menti!

    The STOMper feels that visitors to Singapore's MRT system will have a good laugh at our expense.

    "To enlighten those who have never lived overseas, we may be discriminated against as poor
    English speakers if we can't pronounce words accurately or clearly with proper phonics.

    "Worse still, we may be classified as just being unable to grasp the English language.

    "This change is detrimental to the image of Singaporeans, and show that we do not carry the qualities of a native English Speaker. I am sure the 'Speak good English' committee would be more than happy to coach the voice-over talent.

    "The previous announcements were significantly better, superior in many ways, clear and professional.

    "I am shocked that SMRT has pulled a stunt like this! I am sure the voice talent that was hired for the task would fail any advertising voice-over auditions for leading brand commercials from BBDO for instance.

    "Even CAAS would not approve her for their announcements at Changi Airport no matter how local she sounds.

    "What a terrible shame and mistake this brings."
