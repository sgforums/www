    Oh, i see. Thanks. Anyway, I went to search for information just now. Found this article and just decided to post it.

    First MRT accident

    A front-to-back collision between two trains at the Clementi Station on 5 August 1993 was the first major accident on the Mass Rapid Transit (MRT) system since it started operation in 1987. This first MRT accident resulted in 156 injured commuters.

    Description

    At 7:50 am on 5 August 1993, an East-bound train from Jurong stopped at the Clementi Station for two minutes longer than scheduled due to a technical fault. It was then hit by another train. Operations at the three affected stations, namely, Clementi, Buona Vista and Commonwealth resumed within a day after intensive checks were carried out by MRTC engineers to ensure that the tracks, signals, power and other installations were functioning properly.

    The train, comprising six carriages, had a full capacity of 1,800 passengers. The peak-hour collision injured 156 commuters. Several passengers were flung against panels and steel railings of the train or piled on top of one another, suffering multiple injuries.

    Evaluation

    It was not known at that time why the automatic train protection system malfunctioned. The system would trigger off an automatic braking system when a train got too close to another. The accident may have been due to the inefficiency of the brakes. Braking effectiveness, observers said, depends on the friction between the track and the wheels. It was reported that spilled oil may have caused the accident. The oil was spilled on the tracks from a works train which carried out maintenance functions hours before the collision.

    A four-member independent panel of inquiry was set up by the Mass Rapid Transit Corporation (MRTC), chaired by Mr Chua Koon Hoe, Deputy Director-General of the Public Works Department. The panel investigated the causes of the accident at Clementi station and reviewed the operating procedures and safety measures of the MRT system.
