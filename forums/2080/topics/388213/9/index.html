    SINGAPORE: The Land Transport Authority (LTA) and Singapore Mass Rapid Transit (SMRT) are intensifying their publicity outreach in preparation for the train service disruption this weekend between Jurong East and Bukit Batok stations.

    Grassroots leaders will join LTA officers once again on Friday, September 17, to distribute flyers at Jurong East and Bukit Batok Stations.

    This followed feedback that some commuters were not aware of the service disruption between Jurong East and Clementi stations two weekends ago despite the publicity.

    This time round, LTA and SMRT said more prominent directional signs will be put up at strategic locations of the affected stations.

    Staff will also be deployed onboard the trains as well to provide assistance to commuters and attend to their queries.

    The queue formation for bus bridging services at Jurong East Station will be adjusted for smoother passenger flow and to facilitate faster boarding.

    There will also be priority boarding for the elderly and the young.

    Originally posted by Dragonaxe:

    Ok, so the limit for C-ATS is 5~10m, what is the warning distance if a train travelling at aprox 65m is in the "DANGER" zone?

    I am sorry but I don't really get what you meant.

    If you meant "What is the warning distance if a train is 65m from the train in front?", here's the answer;

    The train enters a YY block which has a speed limit of 25km/h when it is about 120m~180m from the train in front. Failure to adhere to this speed limit will result in EB being applied to bring the train to a complete stop. A warning is given at about 20~40m before the block. Failure to heed the warning will result in EB being applied to bring the train to a complete stop.

    At about 50m from the train in front, there is a point which has a speed limit of 15km/h. Failure to adhere to this speed limit will result in EB being applied to bring the train to a complete stop. A warning is given at about 20m before this point. Failure to heed the warning will result in EB being applied to bring the train to a complete stop.

    At about 20m from the train in front, there is a point which has a speed limit of 7.5km/h which will gradually decreased to 0km/h. Failure to adhere to this speed limit will result in EB being applied to bring the train to a complete stop. A warning is given at about 10m before this point. Failure to heed the warning will result in EB being applied to bring the train to a complete stop.

    The end of the block is about 5~10m from the train in front. If the train has proceed beyond the end of the block, EB will be applied to bring the train to a complete stop. The train will normally come into a complete stop within 4m.

    To "answer" the warning, the driver has to apply brake accordingly to keep the train's speed below the speed limit imposed before it reaches any of the point/block. A visual marking/signal can be seen to denote the position of each points/block.

    (Malaysia) Govt to form panel to discuss Rapid Transit System with Singapore

    The Government will form a committee comprising personnel from the Economic Planning Unit, Treasury and Johor government to discuss the Rapid Transit System (RTS) with Singapore.

    Transport Minister Datuk Seri Kong Cho Ha said Malaysia and Singapore had reached a bilateral agreement to jointly develop the light rail system during a meeting between Prime Minister Datuk Seri Najib Tun Razak and his Singapore counterpart Lee Hsien Loong.

    He said the committee members would first hold intensive discussions on the development of the RTS among themselves before meeting with the Singapore side.

    “The discussions will include the type of RTS that we are going to use; whether to build a bridge across the Straits of Johor or build a causeway, or an expensive underground tunnel that has minimum effect on the environment.

    “We have some of the options, we still have to sit down to discuss and come out with an agreement on how to jointly develop it,” he said at a press conference after attending a ministry Hari Raya open house yesterday.

    Kong said no budget had been worked out so far, adding that both countries would have to work together on the cost considerations.

    He said the 1.5km rail system connecting Johor Baru and Woodlands was expected to be completed by 2018 as part of efforts to “increase connectivity” between the two countries.

    He said Malaysia had identified a plot of land in Tanjung Puteri to build the rail station.

    On the relocation of the 78-year-old Tanjong Pagar railway station operated by KTMB, the minister said the station would be moving to its new site in Woodlands by July 2011, while the current station would be turned into a heritage site.
