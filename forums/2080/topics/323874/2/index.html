    Two rail projects to be brought forward

    The transport ministry will buy more trains and speed up two rail projects to further improve rail capacity.

    22 new trains will be added to the fleet , to be delivered progressively from 2011.

    On infrastructure developments, the government will bring forward improvement works to the Jurong East station as well the construction of the new North-South line extension.

    The rail extension, which will see the train network being extended further into the Marina Bay area, will be brought forward by a year to be ready by 2014.

    The Jurong East Modification Project, or JEMP which aims to reduce the infrastructure constraints of the station, is now expected to be ready by 2011 instead of 2012.

    Transport Minister Raymond Lim announced this in Parliament today.

    " With the JEMP, the headway during peak periods can drop to about 2 minutes along the busiest stretches of the North-South-East-West line . This represents an increase in capacity of about 15 per cent so commuters. This improvement will cost us about $800 million and enable the North-South-East-West line to meet projected demand all the way till 2020. "
