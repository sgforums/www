  • E5
    28 Jun 09, 20:46
    IMHO, EWL and NSL train only come to a sudden halt for 3 reasons; -ATP is in effect, another train is in front. -TO exceed the current speed limit allowed on a specific section of the track, causing ATS(Automatic Train Stop) to come into effect, meaning E-brake kicks in and put train into complete halt. It always happened when train is in CM if the TO is not cautious/newbie. -TO apply E-brake in CM to avoid overrun There's a few place in NSL and EWL which has tendancy for ATS to activate (I rarely took EWL so won't be able to provide you the location for it), they are namely; -Between Yio Chu Kang and Khatib NB, downslope from above ground to ground level. -Between Sembawang and Yishun SB downslope -Between Ang Mo Kio and Bishan SB downslope and Bishan crossover NB. -Downslope immediately exiting Marsiling Station NB -Slope between Choa Chu Kang and Bukit Gombak NB and SB. The pneumatic sound you heard is most likely the sound of compressed air gushing out when E-brake is engaged.
