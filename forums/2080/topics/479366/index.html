The new signals will indicate green, yellow or red, similar to current traffic lights. Green will mean that the platform is not very crowded and passengers should be able to board a train straight away. Yellow means there is a small crowd on the platform and commuters can expect to wait 2 or 3 trains before they will have room to board. Red will mean that the platform is extremely crowded and commuters should expect delays on their journey and may wish to take alternate transport options. The signals will allow people to make alternate travel arrangements before having to go to the platform which may help to reduce congestion on the platforms themselves. They are also very simple to understand and can also be used to signal that there are train faults, breakdowns or long delays as the signal will flash.
