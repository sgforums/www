    Train services disrupted after injured man found on MRT track

    Train services between the Woodlands and Jurong East MRT stations were disrupted for about 10 minutes on Monday at about 7.30am following an incident that involved an injured man.

    The man fainted and fell on the platform at Woodlands MRT station, injuring his head.

    SCDF officers were called in. They later sent the man to Tan Tock Seng Hospital.

    Callers to the MediaCorp News Hotline said the man, believed to be in his 30s, appeared to be on his way to work when the incident happened.

    The disruptions affected about 1,300 MRT passengers.

    Injured man found on MRT track, disrupting journey of commuters

    About 1,300 MRT passengers had their journey disrupted on Monday morning when a man was found injured on the rail tracks.

    The disruptions between the Woodlands and Jurong East stations happened at about 7:30am and lasted around 10 minutes.

    Callers to the MediaCorp News Hotline said the man, in his 30s, was on his way to work when the incident happened.

    Civil defence officers were called. They found the man with visible head injuries and later sent him to Tan Tock Seng Hospital.

    It is not known as yet how the man was injured.
