    The Bukit Timah Railway Station was once and formely a place where trains stopped to pick and drop passengers. After the Malaysian immigration decided to move to Tanjung Pagar station, the Bukit Timah station now serves as an interchange for trains that are meant to cross with each other, and also serves the activity of token passing.

    Normally, the station master only pass the token, which is a form of a key, giving the locomotive driver the right of way to Woodlands CIQ.

    There are three lines at the station. The first, nearest to the station platform is the mainline. The middle line is called the loop line, when there is a need to cross a train, crossing means two trains crosses with each other at the station. One of the train will proceed into the loop line and stopped till the train occupying the main line is cleared.

    It is quite eerie at night, but if you are a regular to the station, it will be peanuts to you. I've stayed at the station from 3pm to 11pm/12mn till the station is closed. The station is quite a good place to relax, peaceful. You can bring your books to sit there and study or read or to just relax your mind.

    You have to ask the permission from the station master first before using their premises.
