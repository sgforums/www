    SMRT counters Red Bull's Night Race allegations!

    In motor racing,

    SMRT has responded to allegations that its trains were the reason behind Red Bull F1 team driver, Mark Webber, retiring from the Singapore Grand Prix.

    Last week, Red Bull Team boss Christian Horner told Britain's Autosport magazine that it appeared a sudden electrical surge from a passing train at Turn 13 interfered with Webber's gear-box.

    In its response to a query from 938LIVE, an SMRT spokesperson yesterday said, there's no MRT track beneath Turn 13 which was at the junction of Anderson Bridge and Esplanade Drive.

    She said the nearest MRT tunnel is about 200 metres away, with a depth of about 10 metres.

    In addition, the spokesperson says train wheels and running rails are made of metal and therefore do not generate static electricity charges during train operations.
