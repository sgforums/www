    Incidentally, after reading and browsing some websites regarding the proposed JB Monorail, I discovered that...

    - Penang, Kuching and Kota Kinabalu will also have their own Monorail systems soon
    - the Monorail systems planned for the abovementioned places will be developed under the Rancangan Malaysia ke-9 (9th Malaysian Plan/RMK-9)
    - the JB Monorail system will cost an estimated RM 1 billion, with 2 local firms with foreign partnerships, including South Korea, reportedly have submitted proposals to the Johor Government
    - the JB Monorail is just one of a few rail networks to be developed under the Johor Bahru Masterplan 2020, which will include a Transport Hub similar to KL Sentral (JB's side will be called JB Sentral)
    - the JB local rail network will be complemented by a Bullet Train system linking JB and KL, part of the 9MP Southern Corridor Development
    - JB Sentral Transport Hub will be operational by year's end and will be Johor Bahru's main transit terminal
    - Johor Bahru KTM Station will be turned into a Railway Museum once KTM operations have shifted to JB Sentral
    - as recent as last week, 2 companies have submitted their bids, costing around RM 1.6 billion to construct Penang monorail network, known as LMT (Light Monorail Transit); one of the companies also announced that it is ready to invest RM 150 million to construct a hi-speed monorail system across the Penang Straits linking Penang and Seberang Prai
    - Penang rail network will come under the 9MP Northern Corridor Development
