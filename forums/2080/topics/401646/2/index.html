    Originally posted by Call4ljw:

    All T... I suppose this would be a linear (or gravitational?) concept? There must be at least a motor car on a train.

    All M, I also thought of this once. Not until I saw materials about All M trains, other than the Shinkansen/high performance trains.

    Local (slow to moderate speed) trains tend to carry lots of passengers. With a All M configuration on a heavily loaded train (200 percent load factor), the power draw would be huge. So much so that voltage drops and performance of the train/other trains will be compromised.

    Hence All M configurations have largely been avoided. However for companies with large infrastructure, good substations and power lines/supply, this poses little/no problem.
