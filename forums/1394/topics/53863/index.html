    2004 Mazda RX 8

    Mazda has announced that the rotary powered sports coupe first seen as the concept model RX-Evolv two years ago and shown as the RX-8 at the Detroit Motor Show in January this year will now go into production to bring a rotary engine car back into the Mazda line up and will carry the name RX-8.

    The stylish design and innovative packaging offers a sleek sports coupe that is a true four seater, with easy access to the rear passenger compartment through the freestyle four-door configuration that has removed the need for a central B pillar.

    Powered by an ingenious normally aspirated 250 bhp 1.3-litre rotary engine, coupled to a five speed manual transmission with a short throw gear change and 50:50 weight distribution, the RX-8 will meet the expectations of drivers who want the excitement and performance of a true sports car and those whose life style demands a four seater, but want the spirited driving experience of a performance car.

    The striking styling gives an athletic and stable impression with a dynamic feeling of motion. The classic rear-wheel-drive layout places visual emphasis on the rear wheels, the eye is drawn from the aggressive front air intake along the curvaceous body to the beautifully formed rear. Short front and rear overhangs underline the vehicle's look of stability, the low front end can only be made possible by the compact rotary engine and the front wheel arches stand high and clear of the bonnet to allow a clear view of the front corners.

    There are a number of innovative design touches that point to the car's power source; the rotor shape is used on the power bulge on the bonnet, the rear fog light, the gear stick and in the front seat headrests.

    The insightful interior is exciting yet functional, all instruments are placed ergonomically close to the driver for ease of use, and the driver and front seat passenger are held firmly in place by lightweight bucket seats. The gear stick is placed so the hand falls naturally to it and is placed on an aluminium central transmission tunnel that runs the full length of the cockpit.

    The rear passengers enter through the innovative freestyle door system; the rear doors are hinged at the rear and the B pillar has been removed; this allows easy access to the rear and sleek coupe styling, something that cannot be achieved with traditional coupes or conventional four door design. As a safety measure, the rear doors can only be opened when the front doors are open. When the front doors are closed the rear doors are locked safely into the bodywork by top and bottom latches and cannot be opened accidentally, thus making the Mazda RX-8 a true coupe design when driving but an easy access four door car when loading and unloading.

    The boot of the RX-8 is also highly functional with enough space for luggage or two sets of golf clubs.

    The Mazda RX-8 boasts front-midship layout: with the next-generation compact RENESIS rotary engine mounted 60mm further back and 40mm lower than in the RX7, the engine centre of gravity is behind the front axle giving the car a perfect 50:50 front/rear weight distribution. With a 2,700mm wheelbase the RX-8 is tremendously nimble and controllable with excellent high-speed handling.

    The RENESIS rotary engine, thanks to a redesign of the side intake and side exhaust ports, has high maximum outputs or 250 bhp at 8,500 rpm and torque of 152 lb-ft at 7,500 rpm.

    The transmission tunnel that runs the length of the cockpit also houses a backbone that runs from the front to the rear bulkhead enhancing rigidity and allowing a reduction in body panel thickness and weight; this also contributes to safety in the event of a collision. Other weight saving designs include a carbon fibre propeller shaft and lightweight closed section power plant frame which locks the entire powertrain into a single rigid unit enhancing direct and liner acceleration and crisp shift operation.

    "While the RX-8 is a genuine sports car, it also delivers the functionality of a 4 door, 4 seater. Embodying unprecedented value, it really is a 'sports car like no other.' Expect great things at next year's launch," said Masazumi Wakayama, Executive Officer, General Manager, Domestic Marketing Div.

    Lancer Evo VIII

    The Lancer Evolution series has evolved over the years as a result of competing in the World Rally Championship and other leading motorsport events worldwide and has attracted a wide following of enthusiast drivers. The production model is currently in its 7th edition in Japan and has been shipped in limited numbers to other world markets by Ralliart (MMC's motorsport affiliate) as a base model for use in competition.

    Lancer Evolution VIII marks the introduction of the series in the U.S. market where there has been considerable interest in the series for several years. MMC plans to phase Lancer Evolution into its model lineup in other world markets in the near future.

    Having commenced shipments of Lancer Evolution VIII to the United States, MMC decided to give the model its world debut at the Los Angeles Show ahead of its unveiling on the Japanese domestic market, scheduled for early 2003.

    The U.S. market specification Lancer Evolution VIII derives from the Japanese market Lancer Evolution VII GSR. While sharing major parts and components with the upcoming Japanese market Evolution VIII, the U.S. model has been developed exclusively for the U.S. to meet crashworthiness and emissions requirements, user needs and preferences, and road and traffic conditions in that market. With a price tag of under US$30,000, Lancer Evolution VIII offers unmatched driving pleasure.

    Exterior Changes

    Detail improvements for Evolution VIII over Evolution VII include: reduced air resistance; reduced, and optimized balance between, lift characteristics front and rear; improved engine cooling.

    The front bumper with integral grille marks the new MMC design identity with a raised triangular base in the center of the grille that is embellished with a chrome silver 3-diamond logo. The bumper also improves intercooler performance with a larger air intake opening on its underside. While conforming to and meeting 2.5 mph impact and other U.S. market regulations and requirements, Evolution VIII is just 80mm longer than VII.

    The rear spoiler uses carbon fiber reinforced plastic for both horizontal and vertical wings, a world-first on a production 4-door sedan, to reduce weight. The wing section design has been optimized to generate greater downforce without increasing drag.

    Interior Changes

    Evolution VIII uses an off-black color scheme and the strategic placement of dark titanium-finish panels to create a functional and sporty interior space. The sports instrument panel locates the tachometer in the center and features a full-scale speedometer that reads up to 170mph.

    Leather trim with black-stitching are used on the 3-spoke MOMOR steering wheel, shift knob, shift lever boot and parking brake grip. Evolution VIII is fitted with RecaroR front bucket seats developed exclusively for the U.S. market.

    Powertrain

    Evolution VIII is powered by Mitsubishi's 4G63-type 2.0-liter in-line 4-cylinder DOHC intercooler-turbocharged engine which conforms to Californian LEV1-LEV emission regulations and has been specially tuned for gasoline octane numbers used in the U.S.

    Mated to the powerplant, MMC's proven 5-speed manual transmission features detail improvements to realize slicker and smoother shifting.

    To meet the higher driving speeds common in the United States, the engine uses high performance synthetic oil instead of petroleum oil.

    Body, suspension, tires

    To improve handling characteristics and ride quality, body and suspension stiffness have been uprated through the strategic placement of reinforcements that minimize weight increase. Reinforcements have also been added to conform with U.S. crash regulations.

    Suspension is by McPherson strut at the front and a multi-link arrangement at the rear. Optimized damping characteristics combine synergistically with the stiffer body to improve handling stability at the limit, while enhancing the driving experience and the ride quality at all speeds.

    2002 Mercedes-Benz SLK 32

    Mercedes-AMG GmbH has introduced a new dimension into the compact roadster sector: the new SLK 32 AMG is not only an exciting car with high-tech equipment, but with its 260 kW/354 hp AMG V6 supercharged engine, it will occupy a leading position among the open two-seaters in this class and will set drivers' hearts racing. No other compact roadster sparkles with quite such supreme performance characteristics. The Mercedes-Benz SLK 32 AMG accelerates from standstill to 100 km/h in just 5.2 seconds and achieves an electronically limited maximum speed of 250 km/h.

    The new high-power engine developed by Mercedes-AMG from the V6 of the SLK 320 is responsible for this outstanding performance. By using a compact supercharger that has an exceptional air flow rate, the engineers from Affalterbach near Stuttgart were able to boost the output of the series-production powerplant by 100 kW/136 hp and the maximum torque by 140 Newton metres. They attached particular importance to dynamic torque generation. Thus the exciting roadster provides all the power it needs and more at very low revolutions, but also glides along effortlessly when cruising in a high gear; 400 of the 450 Newton metres is on tap from an engine speed of only 2300 rpm.

    The engineers at Mercedes-AMG created the conditions for these brilliant figures by using a belt-driven helical supercharger. This innovative technology produces roughly 30 percent more charge pressure than conventional engine-driven charging systems. The charger is controlled on the basis of the pre-stored characteristic engine data as a function of engine speed and load, and activated by an electro-magnetic coupling. This has also enabled the Mercedes-AMG engineers to give the high-performance roadster exemplary environmental credentials. Despite the high performance of its supercharged power unit, the SLK 32 AMG achieves an NEDC combined fuel consumption of a mere 11.2 litres per 100 kilometres using premium unleaded fuel (95 RON). The exhaust emissions of this muscular six-cylinder even meet the strict EU-4 emission standard, which comes into force in 2005.

    Rear axle lift reduced by 50%

    The body styling of the SLK 32 AMG also makes its own exclusive mark. As is the case with all high-performance vehicles in the Mercedes AMG range, top priority was given to a unique character and aerodynamic efficiency. The front and rear aprons, side skirts and an elegantly integrated airflow breakaway edge on the boot lid, which reduces the lift on the rear axle by more than 50 per cent, give the SLK 32 AMG its own dynamic appearance.

    The striking AMG styling elements are continued in the interior. Sports seats with integrated head restraints, designed especially for the high-performance roadster, ensure superb lateral support as well as high levels of comfort on long journeys. Both seats and door panels are trimmed with specially seelected two-tone leather as standard, the same fine material that is to be found on the AMG steering wheel and gear knob. Similarly attractive is the trim on the centre console in high-quality, dark birds-eye maple. The individually designed instrument faces, including a speedometer that shows speeds of up to 300 km/h - in the case of the SLK 32 AMG in silver - are also typical of Mercedes AMG. For the first time the speed is shown in steps of 30 km/h.

    The 2003 Acura NSX: The Legacy Continues To Grow

    The NSX supercar exemplifies the precision crafted performance that lies at the heart of every Acura vehicle. Developed out of years of racing experience, the NSX sets the benchmark for Acura technology, combining world-class performance and styling with exceptional drivability and refinement.

    "The NSX continues to excite the imagination of driving enthusiasts," said Dick Colliver, executive vice president, auto sales. "The NSX is a true icon in the supercar class."

    NSX engine is equipped with Acura's patented VTECTM (Variable Valve Timing and Lift Electronic Control), a Variable Volume Induction System, direct ignition and a computerized Programmed Fuel Injection (PGM-FI) system pioneered in Formula One racing. The NSX engine also features titanium connecting rods, which are stronger and lighter than comparable steel connecting rods.

    To complement these innovative powertrains, the NSX chassis features an all-aluminum unit body construction that is 40 percent lighter, yet has the rigidity of a steel structure. The 4-wheel independent double-wishbone suspension features aluminum-alloy control arms and hub carriers that minimize unsprung weight and provide optimum handling. The suspension also features aluminum subframes for the front and rear suspension to further reduce weight. Forged-aluminum alloy wheels reduce unsprung weight while super high performance tires - 215/40 R17 in front and 255/40 R17 in the rear - provide maximum traction and handling.

    The NSX's braking system features large-diameter, ventilated four-wheel discs and an advanced 4-channel Anti-lock Braking System (ABS) for confident braking. A sophisticated Traction Control System (TCS) helps limit wheel-spin and enhances control.

    The 2003 NSX features a removable roof panel for open-air driving. A sculpted, wedge-shaped body and forward canopy design clearly communicate the car's high performance personality. Additional features include a body integrated rear spoiler and airfoil, power operated sideview mirrors and Xenon High-Intensity Discharge projector-beam headlights. The NSX comes in seven exterior colors, which can be ordered with a matching or complementary interior color.

    Inside, the hand-stitched, leather-trimmed interior of the NSX is designed to evoke the image of a jet fighter cockpit, providing excellent visibility, easy-to-read instrumentation and supportive, power operated seats. As with all Acura models, the NSX comes with a long list of standard equipment and comfort and convenience features including an Automatic Climate Control System, an Acura/Bose" Music System, power windows and door locks, cruise control and a theft deterrent system with electronic immobilizer.

    Advanced safety features include a supplemental airbag restraint system (SRS) and 3-point seat belts with automatic pretensioners. Substantial front and rear crumple zones are designed to distribute impact energy around the passenger compartment in the event of a collision, while large side-sill extensions and sturdy support beams in each door help protect the cabin from side impact forces.
