    Yes what your pastor has said and the illustration he has used is correct. It should be followed or emulated, however it appears the good pastor has forgotten to mention further (from your post).

    The verse clearly states in it's literal meaning, where ever you place something important to you (can be the literal treasure - property or cash, or the figurative, if you son or daughter is your treasure), your heart will be in it all the way. If you offer that treasure to God, you will be in union with Him, as your pastor said.

    But it also must be taken in another more pertinent context and retain its' meaning. If you make Jesus, doing His Will or the Gospel your treasure, and that's where your heart will be also. In fact in Matthew and Luke, the verses continues to advise people not to build too much treasure on earth, which isn't permanent, at the expense of doing His Will. Instead, it advises to build treasures in heaven more, which is permanent. And by making that your treasure, that's where your heart will be.

    There's nothing in either Matthew or Luke to suggest that you should 'seed', build your treasure and thereafter offer it to God, or in offering your treasure to God, you can expect to be blessed with 'greater returns' on your treasure.

    In fact both Matthew and Luke advises to not to worry about all these, go and sell your possessions, give to the poor etc. God also provides for the ravens and birds, and will provide for you. He understands your needs, but build your treasure in His kingdom first, and these things will be provided for you. That's is where your heart should be in regards to your treasure. God first that is the treasure that your heart should follow.

    My message is more for others who read this, and want instead to focus more on the Word, than what is said or interpreted by 'messengers'. My advise to you is, fair enough listen to what the pastor says, but analyse it, if it's good and correct follow it. But if you think maybe, he got this one slight wrong or not perhaps 100%, then find the missing % and get the correct message in its entirety.
