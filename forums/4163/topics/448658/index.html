    CULTS WILL USE ONE OF THE FOLLOWING MANIPULATIVE TACTICS:

    Exclusivism. Cults will create the impression and seed the belief that only they have the truth and the correct path to salvation. No good Christian church claims that they are better than others. Leaving the cult is equated with leaving God, and members really believe that leaving the cult means they can never be saved.

    Guilt and Intimidation. Disagreeing with the leadership (or even just questioning it) is akin to disagreeing with God. It becomes a one way communication. Bible study schools and cell group meetings are used to smutter resistance, where members have their motives and character attacked until they fall into line again.

    Information Control. Members are discouraged, or simply have no time left to, read or watch any material not published by the cult. This includes newspapers, TV, radio, and even the Bible outside of the cult's own Bible studies. All criticism is viewed as an attack to the group. 

    Loaded Language. The cult uses its own language and terms, with common words having quite different meanings to the members. (For example, the Holy Spirit to a CHC'er is a force like electricity.) Attempts by non-members to reason with cult members is reinterpreted since cult members are taught to expect opposition, such as their group being called a cult, or claims that it is harmful, etc. (Closely related to thought stopping - see below).

    Love Bombing and Conditional Love. New members are almost smothered in love, acceptance, etc - a process known as love bombing. The new member has instant friends and all emotional needs are met. This normally lasts a few months - a honeymoon period - and then love and acceptance is turned on and off to control.

    Personality Replacement. Close friends or family notice the member's personality changes from what they were like before joining the group. The member is then indoctrinated with the cult's new doctrine. The old life becomes "evil". CHC uses "Bible" studies, cellgroup meetings, services and events to do this, resulting in cult members appearing to know their Bible better than many Christians - unfortunately with the cult's theology underlying their knowledge. It can take years to eliminate all this false doctrine. While this indoctrination is happening the member is adopting the accepted cult personality, which may be markedly different to the personality they had beforehand.

    Relationship Control. Reduces the time spent on relationships with people outside the cult or those who would tend to draw the member away from the cult. Members are often employed in cult-owned or affiliated businesses. Members are motivated to date and marry within CHC. This means that after being in a cult for a few years, members have no friends or family outside the cult - it can make leaving a very bleak prospect.

    Reporting Structure. Any indiscretions, however minor, are reported back to the leadership, with members being encouraged to report "struggling brothers." In CHC, this can result in "blindly trust thy leaders" sermons, where the leader preaches a sermon directly aimed at what a particular members are currently struggling with - and has talked about in confidence with someone else. When members hears this they think that God is speaking directly through the preacher to them.

    Shunning (a part of relationship control). Members are discouraged from talking to or associating with former members. In other words, former members are "shunned" (completely ignored) by present members. The fear of being shunned helps keep present members within the group.

    Thought Stopping. Step by step, members are indoctrinated (normally over years) to have thoughts stopped or reasoning processes interrupted when they hear a certain phrase or even a word CHC's favorite: "Do not let them hijack your faith"). For example, when a CHC member is talking with a non-member about Jesus wanting us to be rich, and the latter mentions that such doctrine is not sound. The CHC member thinks "Aha! We were warned about this!" and immediately starts to reinterpret everything he/she hears according to the cult teachings (see loaded language above). Reasoning processes are instantly affected and strong resistance to rational thought is triggered in the member. CHC members are not aware yet they do this constantly.

    Time Control. Keeps the member so busy with cult activities they have no time to think deeply about their involvement or pursue activities that might lead them away from the cult. The member might have cult meetings or Bible studies several nights a week.
