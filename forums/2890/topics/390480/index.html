    I believe this philosophy is known as the gambler's fallacy. Just because u throw a coin and it lands on heads 9 times in row, it doesnt mean that the 10th time has a higher chance of getting tails.

    Similarly in soccer, the chances of getting a draw in the last match (out of 10 matches) is NOT higher just because the previous 9 matches were no-draws, even though normal statistics do show that 25% of matches end in draw. this is because all 10 matches are Independent events. The outcome of one match is totally not related to the others. 

    I'll use the heads tails one to demonstrate. 0.5chance heads, 0.5chance tails

    Chances of getting 9heads and finally 1 tail = (0.5^ 9) X 0.5

    Chances of getting 9 heads and finally 1 head = (0.5^9) X 0.5

    They are equal!

    Based on your stats, I Agree that there's a 95% chance that there will be at least ONE draw out of 10 games. If you bet $5 each at one shot on 10 games, there's a 95% chance that you will earn from at least one game

    BUT the flaw with your system arises when you increase the bet amount every time there is a no-draw. It just doesnt work that way. The probability for EACH INDIVIDUAL match resulting in a draw is Simply Simply 25%.
