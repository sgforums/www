    Australian James Anthony Woodburn walked out of the district courts a free man on Monday after he was allowed to compound a molestation case, brought against him by a 26-year-old woman. -- ST PHOTO: CAROLINE CHIA
    By Khushwant Singh

    An Australian walked out of the district courts a free man on Monday after he was allowed to compound a molestation case, brought against him by a 26-year-old woman.

    James Anthony Woodburn settled the matter by apologising to her in court and paying $10,000 to her. He allegedly grabbed her breast in the shopping area at Marina Bay Sands, at 12.20am, on Oct 15. The 44-year-old regional manager of a marketing firm had also been charged with insulting her modesty by showing her his middle finger at about the same time.

    In his apology, Mr Woodburn - a Singapore permanent resident - said that he was genuinely and truly remorseful of his conduct and deeply regretted his actions.

    The slim, 1.7m-tall woman, with shoulder-length hair, later told reporters that she agreed to compound the matter as she wanted to put the incident behind her
