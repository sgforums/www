Heavy traffic at land checkpoints due to Hari Raya Haji

    Heavy traffic expected at land checkpoints during Hari Raya Haji weekend

    SINGAPORE: Heavy traffic is expected at both Woodlands and Tuas checkpoints from Friday (Sep 9), ahead of Hari Raya Haji next Monday, said the Immigration and Checkpoints Authority (ICA).

    Heavy departure traffic is expected to build up from Friday while arrival traffic is expected to be particularly heavy on Monday, ICA said in a press release on Wednesday. Travellers using both land checkpoints during this period can expect delays.

    ICA added that traffic build-up is inevitable with security checks, especially when a large number of travellers use the checkpoints at the same time.

    It has advised travellers to adjust their travel plans if possible. Motorists should check on the traffic situation at both land checkpoints before embarking on their journey, ICA said.

    Travellers are encouraged to car-pool where possible, maintain lane discipline at all times and ensure that they do not bring unlawful or prohibited goods into or out of Singapore, ICA added.

    For more information, the public can call 6863 0117, tune in to the radio for the latest traffic news, or visit http://onemotoring.com.sg or http://www.mytransport.sg to check the latest traffic conditions.
