    New Rain Shelters for Motorcyclists along Expressways

    The Land Transport Authority is building 31 new motorcycle rain shelters under vehicular flyovers along expressways island-wide, doubling the total number from the current 29 to 60.

    The rain shelter facility will benefit motorcyclists by providing refuge for them during inclement weather. The space in the 60 shelters can accommodate some 1000 motorcycles at any one time.

    "Motorcyclists are a group of road users who are especially vulnerable on the roads, accounting for more than half the number of road fatalities for the past few years. We are committed to improving road safety for everyone and conduct ongoing studies to review existing safety measures and add new measures when needed. Rain shelters are one improvement to our land transport infrastructure to increase motorcyclist safety, as we continue education and enforcement efforts." said Mr Yam Ah Mee, Chief Executive of LTA

    Gim Tian Engineering Pte Ltd will undertake the work at a cost of $2.58million, to be completed by end-2009. The 31 new rain shelters will be located along five expressways:

    1) Ayer Rajah Expressway (AYE)
    2) Bukit Timah Expressway (BKE)
    3) Pan Island Expressway (PIE)
    4) Seletar Expressway (SLE)
    5) Tampines Expressway (TPE)

    Signs will also be posted before each shelter to alert motocyclists of the shelter location.
