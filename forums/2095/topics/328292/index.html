New Lornie viaduct opens to traffic

  • QX179R
    23 Aug 08, 20:50

    New Lornie viaduct opens to traffic from Sunday

    Motorists travelling along Lornie, Braddell and Thomson roads in Central Singapore will have a smoother journey when the new Lornie viaduct opens to traffic from Sunday at 9am.

    The new roads are part of an ongoing development of the Outer Road Ring system. This is a network of major roads that literally form a ring along the outer areas of the city.

    Extensively linked to expressways and other arterial roads, the system provides motorists with routes to travel between the eastern and western parts of Singapore without the need to go through the city, thereby reducing congestion in the city centre.

    One viaduct connects Braddell Road to the widened south-bound carriageway of the MacRitchie viaduct.

    The other connects Lornie Road to Braddell Road.

    Senior Minister of State for Transport Lim Hwee Hua and some 500 community leaders took a tour of the newly completed viaduct on Saturday.

    Ms Lim said: "This much-awaited direct connection between Lornie and Braddell roads will allow motorists to bypass two traffic junctions, thereby reducing travel time by an estimated four minutes. It also means improved traffic flow between Braddell and Thomson roads."
