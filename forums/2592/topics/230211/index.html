    watashi is the compressed form of watakushi which is not commonly used. watashi is a former way of saying I.

    atashi (I)is for gals only.

    boku (I) is for guys only.

    omae (you) i think is not commonly used.

    kimi (you) is use among people of the same level.

    unless your command of the language is very strong it is advisable to stick to watashi at all times.

    i think ore is old use for I.

    *why??*
    Almost every japanese says this before eating, in almost situation. The closest English equivalent is *grace*, but it has lost its religious meaning. Dont hesitate to say this before a good meal. *WHY*? because you MAY EVEN BE COMPLIMENT FOR YOUR GOOD MANNERS. its very regonize around japan where people say itadakimas before any meal =)

    Irrashimase is when you enter to a japanese restaurant, the working staff there will greet you irrashimase = ( welcome in ) a warm polite gesture. hope you understand.

    katakana : are mostly used for foreign words.
    when using katakana, life gets a bit tricky when you hit the limitations of the japanese language. it has only one final consonant (n), so when the japanese pronounce English words with other consonant ending, they have to tack on a vowel, usually a (u)[b] "Game" becomes *ge mu* or "bed" becomes *beddo*
    with no final "r" sound, they usually use a long "a" --- for example "locker"
    becomes *rokka*. And since there is no " i " at all, " r " is used instead..
