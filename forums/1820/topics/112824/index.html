    Monte Carlo is the oldest tally on the calendar, first organised in 1911. This year sees its 75th running.

    Competitors start and finish in Monaco while most of the action takes places in the mountains of France.

    The conditions are mixed - snow, black ice, rain and dry asphalt can make appearances in the same event.

    Tommi Makinen won the rally 4 times consecutively - 1999, 2000, 2001 and 2002 - the only driver to have done so in history.

    Walter Rhorl and Sandro Munari also recorded 4 victories each.

    This event is Peugeot's jinx in recent times - it never gets a good result here.

    The drivers moved south for the second stage of the race, Saint-Antonin / Toudon. While the course is mostly uncomplicated, the drivers must keep an eye for the sharp rocks on the side of the road, which can easily puncture the tyres.

    This time a driver was able to beat the mighty Xsaras: Marcus Gronholm! The Peugeot driver sets the pace with a time of 14'05"7, only three tenths better than Francois Duval in second place and 1"1 faster than Sebastien Loeb in third.

    Behind the leading trio is Petter Solberg. The Norwegian's rhythm is also very close to the leader: he loses only 2"5 seconds over Gronholm in this stage.

    Markko Martin takes fifth place in his Peugeot 307. The Estonian is just barely faster than Toni Gardemeister in sixth, which is an encouraging performance by the Ford driver.

    Gilles Panizzi (Mitsubishi), Didier Auriol (Peugeot), Stephane Sarrazin (Subaru), and Roman Kresta (Ford) complete the top ten in that respective order.

    Loeb is still the leader in the overall results, followed by his teammate Francois Duval in second position and Marcus Gronholm in third place.

    Armin Schwarz continued to lose time in this stage because of the differential problem affecting his Fabia.
