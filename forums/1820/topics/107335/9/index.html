    Minardi could be banned from the Australian Grand Prix in a row over their new car.

    The minnows had hoped to start the new season with last year's PS04B chassis because their new car, the PS05, will not be ready until April.

    In order to do so, Minardi need to secure the agreement of all other nine teams. Only then will the FIA grant the team the special dispensation needed.

    Team boss Paul Stoddart has received the all-clear from eight teams, but world champions Ferrari have so far refused to give their assent, meaning there is a good chance Minardi will be disqualified from Melbourne.

    FIA president Max Mosley said, "If there was no prior agreement and he presented the 2004 car to us, that would be illegal under the current regulations. It would not be permitted to go out of the pit lane."

    Ferrari also plan to start the new season with their 2004 car, but have adapted it to fit Formula 1's new regulations.
