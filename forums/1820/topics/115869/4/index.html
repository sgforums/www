    Forte dei Marmi - A firefighting plane from Italy's civil defence agency crashed into an empty house near the Tuscan coast on Friday, killing the two pilots and sparking a small fire, an agency spokesman said.

    The plane slammed into the home of an elderly couple who were not there at the time because the building was being renovated, Falcone said. The plane burst into flames, causing its fuel tanks to explode. Five families who lived nearby were evacuated as a precaution, and the fire was eventually extinguished, he said.

    The Canadair MI-DPCK had left Rome at about 4.30 p.m. (15:30 GMT) to help fight a forest fire on the hills near the beach resort of Forte dei Marmi, about 20km north of Pisa, and had twice already fetched sea water to dump on the fire when it struck electric power lines, Falcone said.

    A helicopter that also was helping fight the fire warned the pilots "that they had hit the power lines and to aim for the sea," Falcone said.

    Witnesses told the ANSA news agency that the plane appeared to be experiencing some difficulties and caught fire before crashing into the building in the tiny village of Ripa, ANSA said.

    Falcone identified the two pilots as Stefano Bandini and Claudio Rosseti. He said the two had recently returned from Sri Lanka, where they had been sent to assist with aid efforts there following the deadly tsunami.
