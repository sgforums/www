    BAR RECEIVES TWO-RACE BAN
    Last Updated: Thursday, 05, May, 2005, 16:51

    The BAR-Honda Formula 1 team has been given a two-race ban following a hearing at the FIA Court of Appeal in Paris on Wednesday.

    This means that Jenson Button and Takuma Sato must now sit out this weekend's Spanish Grand Prix and the Monaco GP at the end of the month.

    The FIA has concluded that the team broke regulations by using fuel as ballast, which could have a given it a performance advantage.

    Button's car was found to be underweight when drained of fuel after the San Marino Grand Prix at Imola after finishing third, and although the FIA-appointed stewards upheld the result after lengthy debate, the governing body later appealed against their decision.

    BAR-Honda has had the 10 points that it scored in the San Marino Grand Prix taken away.

    It has also been given a six month suspended ban, which will remain in place for a year.

    BMW in talks to supply Sauber in 2006
    Unhappy with 'midfield position' with Williams

    BMW confirm link to supplying Sauber with its
    V8 powerplant in 2006

    BMW, engine-suppliers to the Williams Formula One team, are in talks to equip Sauber too from next season, the German manufacturer's managing director revealed here at Circuit de Catalunya on Saturday.

    "We are in constructive negotiations with the Sauber team concerning the supply of engines," said Helmut Panke. "Frank Williams is aware of that. The overall strategy is that all engine manufacturers will be asked to supply at least two teams... for the 2006 season."

    Ferrari, who announced last month that they would be supplying Red Bull Racing with engines, currently equip Sauber under the Petronas badge. BMW are envious and frustrated at the success rival manufacturers like Ferrari and Renault enjoy in the lucrative world of Grand Prix racing.

    "The performance of the BMW-Williams team up to now has not been such a high performance that we will expect easily to become champions this season. Formula One is the premium league in motor sports. This is why BMW will continue to be in there. This is why it will have to find a way to be successful and achieve a way to become champion in Formula One. I clearly say that with the current 'midfield position' of BMW, we are not happy."

    As to BMW going it alone Panke said: "The question of having our own team has come up over and over, because whenever, for example, Renault does very well, people say, 'look, Ferrari and Renault, they are the ones who show us what should be done.'"

    Williams are equal fourth with Ferrari on 18 points in the race for the constructor's title ahead of Sunday's Spanish Grand Prix in Barcelona.

    Schumacher talks down Alonso title hopes
    McLaren Mercedes the ones to watch out for

    Tough times for Schumacher
    and the Italian Champions

    Seven-time world champion Michael Schumacher talked down the title hopes of Fernando Alonso here Saturday as the Spanish driver continued his chase for victory on home soil in the Spanish Grand Prix. Alonso has been tipped as heir apparent to Schumacher after winning the last three races but the German, whose Ferrari team are struggling this year, insists Alonso is just one in a queue of drivers lining up to take his crown.

    "I know this comparison (between Schumacher and Alonso) is fashionable at the moment but I have to say I have a bit of a problem with it," said Schumacher. "There are a lot of other guys who are very fast. Clearly it would be an advantage for us if we scored more points than him but again there are also other drivers that I see fighting for the championship. I still believe McLaren Mercedes will have a big chance."

    Alonso set himself up for another strong challenge in Sunday's race at the Circuit de Catalunya when he finished second to provisional pole position winner Jarno Trulli in first qualifying here Saturday. Schumacher, who pushed Alonso hard for victory at the last race in San Marino, was a disappointing seventh fastest of the 18 drivers but he insisted his pace in the race will allow him to close on his rivals.

    "For one lap we are not strong enough, but I am certainly much happier on race pace, we are much more competitive then," said Schumacher, before admitting his hopes of victory are still slim. "We are strong in long runs but not that much compared to the others and it will be extremely difficult to win, but in racing nothing is certain."

    Schumacher, who started 13th at the last race after a mistake in second qualifying, faces the first task of securing seventh place on the grid or improving his position in final qualifying Sunday morning. But he admitted: "We have already proven that we have struggled on the one-lap qualifying and you have to say whatever happens in final qualifying, this is not the ideal position on the grid."

    Team boss Jean Todt remained philosophical after the opening qualifying run, insisting that making race predictions was futile and suggesting that he is hoping to capitalize on reliability problems of their rivals. "We are not really surprised by qualifying," said Todt. "We know what are our strong and our weak points and it is clear that in tomorrow's qualifying we will not be able to do much to move up the order. But if Michael can stay pretty much where he is at the moment, then we have a fighting chance, given that reliability and consistency of performance of the various car-engine-tyre packages will play a key role."

    "So far this season, our performance levels in the races have come from both ends of the spectrum, so it is hard to make predictions about what might happen in the race."
