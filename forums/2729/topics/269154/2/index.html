    Originally posted by jlzk:
    Zun bo?Got liddat one meh?Then does it means the system is bias or something?
    People exploit this kind of new system. Just do a good presentation at the end of the day and the grades are yours, at least this is what they say. To learn or not to learn is your business.

    There are two type of assessment in RP:

    1) Daily grades

    2) Understanding Test(UT).

    The way to calculate module grade is best 14 out of 16 daily grades and 3 out of 4 best UT.

    It's the most easiest to get high GPA in RP out of the other 4 polytechnics. From what I see, most of the students are able to get GPA 3.0 and above. You can hardly see someone with less than 3.0. That's the point.

    In contrast to other polytechnics, students study extremely hard in order to get good grades. There is a considerable learning there. And the exam system is there, so you can really see who's studying and who's not. However, this is very different case in RP.

    The 'facilitators' assess student based on their participation, contribution, and powerpoint presentation on a daily basis. Which means to say, you might need to be a good actor in order to get good grade. One must know how to act like he/she is participating in class.

    Also moderation for the Understanding Tests are unbelievable lax when in Year 1. A student can have a raw mark of 5 out of 30, and yet still score 'A' for that particular module test. I am utterly disgusted by what I've seen.

    In my opinion, what you learn in RP using the Problem-Based Learning system does not reflect deep learning and memorizing. The learning is there for a short period of time, and it's gone after that. Though the saying that induced learning by asking question helps learning, but I don't see the benefits of this over long terms. Students simply abuse this to get good grades in RP.

    As students advance from Year 1 to Year 2 and 3, it's harder to abuse the system as compared to when in Year 1. However the exploiting still takes place even in their Year 3.

    At the end of the day, I still feel that examinations is the best way to grade students.
