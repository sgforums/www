    hey there everyone.

    i wonder why nobody asked about ITE because i believe ITE is equally better than JCs and Polys. everyone have their different experiences of life inside ITE, depending of the kinda teachers you meet, the kinda schoolmates you meet, the kinda classmates you meet, the kinda co-curricular activities you join.

    in ITE, our classes are by modular basis. we learn different things every semester. we do not start our day with flag-raising ceremonies. and we have classes starting and ending at different times of the day, just like normal polytechnics.

    it's true that ITE's hands-on minds-on hearts-on because:

    HANDS-ON: we really do hands-on stuff. yeah apart from students who specialise in the technical, mechanical, electronical fields.

    for business and hospitality courses, we also learn how to create and manage our own events and use basic office computer applications. for students specialising in retail and tourism, they get to handle the point-of-sale system and also the system that's used commonly throughout the tourism industry for booking of air tickets, etc.

    for it courses, loads of hands-on either on the hardware or the software side depending on the specialisation you've chosen. the one taught in clementi campus is business-information technology in which you're taught on basics of business and also mixed with information technology. for dover and tampines campus if i'm not wrong, theirs is information technology which either allows to opt for the hardware field or the multimedia software field.

    nursing is also another option you may want to consider because of the high demands in the healthcare industry in the recent years because of the ageing population in singapore.

    MINDS-ON: simply means the determination that we ite students have to be better than others, irregardless of jcs poly universities. we have that focused-mind to aim ourselves towards polytechnics.

    HEARTS-ON: our student nurses are the best examples out there la. but seriously, ite lecturers do care for us even though they have to set us discipline and attendance standards. they do listen to your problems and help you with their heart and passion. some of the lecturers inside actually put aside their high-salaried careers just to teach us.

    --

    CO-CURRICULAR ACTIVITIES

    each campuses have different kinda CCAs. some have taekwondo, aikido. others have entrepreneurship, student council (i'm in it myself =P), green club, business studies club, etc =) there's even gaming CCA in ite as well.. in clementi campus, we have cyberCT. =)

    --

    anything question about ite, just post here. and also i welcome JCs and Polys too =DD

    hello all, im taking my A lvls this year and im frm cj.

    let me share my experience.

    in jc
    if u join a sport cca, which i did, with 3 or 4 times a week training, ur life is friggin hectic.

    let me be honest with you, if ure not smart, don't go jc, because i have classmates who are damn hardworking, but they just cant absorb at the fast pace, who are flunking. really go poly, u stand a much better chance.

    also, even for smart people, if hardworking, jc will be stressful for you.

    if ur smart but last minute time, ( me) yes its true, jc life is worse than hell, seriously, its super stressful, last time when i was taking my Os and my teacher said, u all shld consider going poly, coz A lvl is gonna be the worst exam u'll ever take, i thought to myself" aiya how stressful also cannot be so stressful"

    IT IS REALLY STRESSFUL. esp when ur prelims are near, u know nuts about certain things, or u haven't grasp concepts, coz everything is really going damn fast, esp if u are the last minute worker, u'll be in deep shit, coz everything is linked. it takes some time a grasp a certain concept, but after that u still have to link it to other concepts, and stuff.

    its really not as simple a OS.

    let me give u a good and practical example.

    in O lvl, i only need to learn 5 organic reactions.

    in A lvl i need to learn over a 100 and link them all up.

    don't forget, organic chem is just one of the small components of the chem paper. there's still alot of shit to learn.

    i hope my insight has better informed you into making the right choices.
