    Sex can boost the immune system 


    Definitely good news is how sex is good for you, at least in moderation. Psychologists showed that the immune systems of people who have sex once or twice a week boosts. Scientists evaluate how robust our immune systems are by measuring levels of immunoglobulin A. this is an antigen found in saliva and mucosal linings. This substance called IgA is the first line of defense against colds and flu. Important role for IgA is that it binds to pathogens at all the points of entry to the body and then calls on the immune system to destroy them. In study made to measure amount of IgA with people who had sex and who abstinence, results showed that participants who had sex less than once a week had a small increase in IgA over those who abstained completely. Those who had one or two sexual encounters each week had a 30 per cent rise. Unfortunately, people who had very frequent sex that means three times a week or more had lower IgA levels even than the abstainers had. The high levels of IgA in volunteers who had moderately frequent sex are easy to understand. Sexually active people may be exposed to many more infectious agents than sexually non-active people may. The immune system would respond to these foreign antigens by producing and releasing more IgA.  In the same time, why there was no IgA rise in the most sexually active group is less clear.
