  • ndmmxiaomayi
    10 Dec 07, 22:16
    Limited access probably means your network connections has broken.

    Most of the time it's caused by a timeout error to connect to your network. Right clicking on the network icon (near the clock) and select Repair should resolve the issue.

    If you receives this problem intermittently, you will need to check your router settings. If you have changed some values there, reset it back to the default.

    If you are using Vista... please take note that Vista takes a freaking long time to connect to the wireless network. In the meantime, it shows a limited access. It takes about one minute or so for it to recover before you will have full net access. It's just a Vista problem, waiting for Microsoft to figure out their problems.

    ... limited connectivity usually refers to "LAN OK" but "No Internet" (thus "limited", limited to LAN)

    That is to say, your computer can talk to the router but there is no access to the internet...

    The modem cannot connect to the ISP, Starhub.

    Since Stargub says there is no problem on their side... then the suspect would be your modem...

    ... junk you modem and get one that connects persistently to the internet. The modem connects persistently, not your computer. That is to say, if the signal drops, the Modem tries to reconnect by itself...

    ... buy a Modem with built in router and wireless... and persistent connectivity...
