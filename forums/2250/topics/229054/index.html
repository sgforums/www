    The answer to 2nd question.

    ok this is a little bit tedious.

    you need to download some partition program: partion magic etc...
    make sure u have boot disk made from the prog( cos around 5% chance will have system failure while doing the task)

    ok third, u need to know which partion is beside which partition. for example, i have C: D: and E: disk drive partitioned.... u must know the position of them inside the disk. and assume that the position is in this order..C: on the left, D: in the middle and E: on the right.

    if you need to shift space from C: to E: , you have to first shift disk space from C: to D:, then from D: to E:
    you must do a defragment( partition magic can do a dregrament for you i think) on the disk drive u want to withdraw the space from. this is to ensure that the free disk space is arranged nearer to E: drive.

    Note :all these above steps are only for IDE disk drives. SATA drive can just shift space in a easier way

    --------------------------------------------------------------------------------
    for your case, what you have to do is... (if u are using IDE disk drive)
    lets assume your D: drive needs more space. so.. u need to shift space from C:

    1. download the 3rd party partition program,
    2. Make a bootdisk from it.
    3. check your partition structure. is it C: first then D: or D: first than C:.
    4(a). if is C: first then D:, defrag your C: drive.
    4(b). if its D: first then C:, defrag your D: drive.
    5. next, just use partition magic and shift space. read its manual.. they got teach u how to shift space. and u are done.

    ALl the above : Do at your own risk

    The answer to first question.

    Those 2 drives need to be physically 1 drive. means that they are partitioned from a disk drive.

    you will also need to know which one has system file.. preferable to delete the partion which has NO SYSTEM FILES. or in other words, no Operating system(OS in short form).

    lets say i want C: and D: merge. C: has OS and D: doesnt have. and D: has no important files

    1. use a third partion prog like... partition magic.
    2. [VERY IMPT] do a boot disk using partition magic.( incase got boot failure)
    3. use partition magic to delete D: drive... then the D: drive space will become raw space, then add space to C: from the raw patition. then, execute the actions. this will require you to restart com.

    done.the method above will still keep your C: files

    This is however, only virtually looks like its merged... but actually, in the new C: drive, you can see a small 8mb parted in between the space drive. in other words.. physically its not merged..

    if you want to really full merge(w/o wasting the 8mb in between the c, you have to reformat the whole com, delete the partitions.. and make as one. ALL data will be loss. unless... you backup an image of C: it( preferablly using norton ghost)then it load back to the reformatted drive
