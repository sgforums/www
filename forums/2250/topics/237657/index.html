    All you list are important, but you missed out one point. Hard disk.

    Hard disk may seem unimportant, but they aren't. Hard disks have a spinning rate. The higher the rpm, the faster you can access data.

    Since a game is most likely to be installed on a hard disk, a hard disk that spins at 7200rpm would definitely be faster than one that spins at 3600rpm.

    Another issue is about FSB or Front Side Bus. FSB is a system bus that carries all electronic signal information between the CPU and other devices within the system such as RAM.

    With a greater value, they could carry more data, and therefore a PC could be faster.

    One thing about FSB is that they connect to the Northbridge of the motherboard (which means nearer to the RAM and video card slots). Being nearer to these buses means shorter travelling distance for the electrons and there would result in faster accessing of data. With fast data access, a game won't lag so much.

    This post is about PCI Express for graphics.

    Why PCI Express Architecture for Graphics.
    http://www.intel.com/technology/pciexpress/devnet/docs/pciexpressgraphics1.pdf

    If you are confuse about whether to install one or two PCI-Express GPU for your new system. Just choose to install ONE.

    For single GPU system, it does not matter if the mainboard supports ATI Crossfire or Nvidia SLI technology or not. It will work as long as it supports/has a PCI-E (X16) interface. Only when you are planning to install two GPUs, then you will need to check whether the mainboard support those multiple GPU technologies.

    If you are going for Dual GPUs system, make sure your mainboard AND GPUs supports either ATI Crossfire or Nvidia SLI. Common sense will tell you that for ATI CrossFire, buy ATI GPUs and for Nvidia SLI, buy Nvidia GPUs. However, keep in mind that GPUs consume significant electrical power and two GPUs means it will consume double the power and generate double the heat. Thus more headache especially if your PSU cannot supply enough power.

    Why choose Dual GPUs?
    It combines the processing power of two GPUs to double the graphics performance. You can buy a pair lower price GPUs, combine their performance and still out-perform a single expensive high-end GPU.
