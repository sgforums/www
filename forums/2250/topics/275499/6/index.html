    Wireless is a specialization of its own. Knowledge of networking is required to know wireless... because wireless networking branched off from wired networking. The concepts of wired networking applies to wireless as well. You will learn about wireless technologies in addition to networking technologies that you already know.

    Networking is also a specialization of its own. DNS, VPN (you will learn it in wireless as well), routing, subnetting, etc... all fall under networking. If you haven't finished your A+ book, I don't suggest you go further.

    A+ books cover a little on networking as well, get those concepts before proceeding to networking. And I have to stress one thing - know your binary, hex and decimal system well. They are needed for networking. While you have tools for that... remember that if you are setting a brand new network, there's no such thing as tools. Everything is manual.

    You are expected to know how to fix PCs (that means removing and putting it back, troubleshooting them, etc), so to answer your last question, yes. But you don't need to fiddle with the engineering part of it. Although it's good to know.
