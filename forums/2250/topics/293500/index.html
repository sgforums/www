    6 Dec 07, 00:55
    You need to figure out where the DC is caused by your router or your ISP's connection.

    To do that, plug your laptop or computer directly to your modem, and see if you get disconnected.

    If you do, you should give your ISP a ring and ask them what gives.

    If it doesn't disconnect you, its probably due to your wireless router. Wireless disconnect issues are difficult to troubleshoot. Try adjusting your antennae orientation to see if you are able to improve the signal bars.

    If you are getting full signal but experience disconnects, it could be due to interference. Try shifting locations, or setting your wireless config to auto channel selection.

    Other possibilities is that you have a faulty device, or a faulty configuration.
