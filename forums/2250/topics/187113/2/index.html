    Your task manager shows that your CPU usage is 100%, which means either some programs are eating a lot of resources or spyware is present. Get a scan and remove them.

    1) explorer.exe MAY be an indication that got a virus. A few viruses use this to hide themselves to prevent detection. w32.Codered, BKDR_ZAPCHAST.BB and the w32.mydoom.b@mm used explorer.exe as a hideout. So I suggest you get an antivirus scan. If the scan revealed that your PC is not affected, explorer.exe is a legitimate program.

    2) iexplore.exe is Microsoft Internet Explorer. It typically is all right unless you got a virus. Trojan.KillAV.B, W32.Mogi virus and W32.Comdor.K@mm uses iexplore.exe as a hideout as well, so you get your antivirus to scan as well.

    3) svchost.exe is a legitimate program. Also heck that your LSSAS.EXE's username is SYSTEM. If it isn't, means you have spyware on your computer.

    4) CCAPP.EXE belongs to Norton Antivirus. This program should not be stopped, unless you have a lot of advertising popups.
