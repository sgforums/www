    UPS = Uninterruptible Power Supply
    It is a battery device that serves as a temporary power backup solution to a electronic system in case of power failure. The lower-end UPS should be able to last an hour before it run out of power without recharging.
    In addition, UPS usually comes with electrical circuits that can guard against sudden power surges or outages, thus protecting your Power Supply Unit from damage.

    I am using Antec Neo HE 500W.

    It is stable and reliable. Why?
    It has industrial grade protection circuitry prevents damage resulting from short circuits (SCP), power overloads (OPP), excessive current (OCP), excessive voltages (OVP) and under voltage (UVP).

    It uses ACTIVE Power Factor Correction (PFC)
    http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Power_factor_correction

    Most cheap power supply units uses PASSIVE PFC and may not have sufficient protection circuitry. And if your PSU get "cooked", your system might get "cooked" as well.

    The best part of these Antec Neo HE series PSU is that it is highly efficient. Which means it generates less heat, thus saving electrical power.

    My computer system usually operates at 30 degrees to 34 degrees, which is even lower than an average human body temperature.
