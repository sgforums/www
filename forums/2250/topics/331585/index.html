    there is a way to beat them: welcome to the world of freeware

    U can still watch HD 5, 8 ect. its totally legal and nothing to do with starhub. Here is how to do it. its called HTPC setup and upconversion. Many digital tv stations do this as well

    You need

    1) Dual core Laptop or PC,

    2) Tv tuner card, or USB version ,

    3) digital tv reception (note: its normal Sd digital reception, not HDTV. The only difference is the resolution, digital 5, 8 can be received through antenna and its free to air)

    4) mainstream LCD HDTV (optional)

    download ffdshow and a tv viewing program like progdvb

     

    FFDSHOW:sourceforge.net/projects/ffdshow

    progdvb: www.progdvb.com 

     

    set up ffdshow and make sure during install u choose the option "do not limit". Install progdvb. when u start progdvb, ffdshow starts automatically

    FFdshow's resize features works with progdvb  to upconvert the resolution to HD on your PC or Laptop screen. All computer monitors are progressive digital displays. And HD resolutions are the same on the PC or laptop monitor eg. 1280x720 on PC  = 720p on HDTV ,1920×1080 on PC = 1080p on HDTV

    if u want to go further, u can connect your PC or laptop directly  to mainstream HDTV  using VGA or HDMI. make sure you have a LCD HDTV which can natively support 1280x720 = 720p ,1920×1080 = 1080p at the correct aspect ratios for the best picture quality possible. Even if the HDTV and resolution output from the PC or laptop  are mismatched, they still do work (but with black borders ect) These resolutions can be accessed from your windows desktop display properties and when connected to a HDTV , they will output accordingly

     

     

     

    Note to Vista users: U have to turn on microsoft redistributable package in vista"s "windows ON and Off features"  in control panel to make use of Progdvb's enhanced video renderer. Enhanced video renderer outputs picture without the annoying black borders

    Note to all users: FFDshow does not work in Microsoft media centre edition.Users with Intel GMA graphics cards on their laptop or PC  may possible expereince problems with resolution setting when connected to external HDTV or monitor
