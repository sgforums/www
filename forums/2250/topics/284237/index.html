    DMI is Desktop Management Interface.

    It's a layer between the hardware and software that manages them (the software is your operating system like Windows, Mac or Linux).

    If it hangs, it means that data couldn't pass successfully to the OS.

    One reason this happens is a bad hardware or new hardware.

    Most likely a hard disk error. Find out who's your hard disk manufacturer, go to their website and do a diagnostic test for your hard disk.

    If it's a new hardware, make sure that the hardware settings are configured correctly. Also check the CMOS settings to make sure that it's loaded correctly.

    If it doesn't work, reset your CMOS. Refer to your motherboard for a guide.
