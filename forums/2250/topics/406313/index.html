  • SBS7484P
    27 Jul 10, 14:03

    It all depends on your use..

    Don't go out and pick parts that you have absolutely no use for. That's just wasting your money.

    As for liquid cooling, there are basically two kinds.

    The basic type like Corsair H50 and upcoming H70, built by Asetek. These are highly similar to the 'watercooling' used in HP Voodoo, Acer Predator, Dell Alienware etc.

     

    The other kind would be a full custom loop in which you willl have to buy radiators, waterblocks, pumps, reservoirs, tubing, anti-kink coils, fans and other things like coolant with anti-algae (high silver content) and antifreeze.

     

    If you do wish to go into custom watercooling be warned that it is not cheap in the least way.

     

    Much effort and money has to be spent building the loop and to get the effects you desire.

     

    Also, don't say air cooling is lao tu. Radiators from a water cooling loop will still have to make use of fans to cool the liquid passing thru them, so fans are still an important part of the system.
