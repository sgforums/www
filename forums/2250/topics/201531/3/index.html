    24 Jul 06, 13:39
    Try not to deploy your router at one end of your house and use your wireless computers at the other end of the house. There would be naturally a lot of walls between your computer and your router.

    Try to deploy your router somewhere in the center (try, most probably it won't be) so you can maximise your omnidirectional signal instead of allowing half your signal to be wasted outside your place.

    Also, I read that you should elevate your router, keep it a small distance away from walls instead of against a wall with your antenna pointing up (for omnidirectional antenna).

    Do not let your router get blocked by a large metal body such as a metal wardrobe or a metal sheet as that would disrupt the signal.

    Choose a channel that is relatively clear of attenuation (for linksys that would be channel 11). There maybe too much radio waves sharing the same channel that would cause your signal to appear weak.

    These methods can add a little bit of signal and range. For most of us living in HDB flats, our place is not big enough to warrent the use of a repeater or booster, not even if you are living in a 5 room flat or an EC.
