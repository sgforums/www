    Yep.. You are right though, the 3rd is a nitrogen-cooled CPU ventilator, it incorporates the same cooling technology of an aircon, the copper pipes you see are the condensing pipes that circulates the nitrogen gas and the front unit is the mini circulator control pump.

    Me had a same encounter as you, my P3 CPU fan die on me and the overheating of the processor would hang the PC while running cos the floating point of the processing core in the processor is very sensitive to high temperature, and when running multiple applications increase the temperature as the processor core works even harder. After me changed the fan, it was more stable and more or less back to normal operations.

    As the CMOS battery, in an ideal and normal usage, it should last about 5 years, but there are some hardware theories saying we should change the battery every 3 years.
