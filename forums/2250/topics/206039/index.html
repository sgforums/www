    the 3 processor numbers come from different generations/ release rather. CD2 6700 being newer than the #945 and it being newer than the #651. with every improved version of the Intel processors, usually (but not always) the bus speed will increase. Anything above the 800mHz is preferred for a higher/ faster ram type as DDR2 instead of DDR.

    Intel used to make processors with speedstep technology so that the processor in this make will be intelligent enough to decide and cut down on power consumption when the application drain is low and pump up power intake when the usage is high. It is a preferred concept in mobile computers (e.g notebook) for better heat management/ dissipation without comprimising computing power.

    When Hyperthreading was introduced to have 2 virtual cores in one processor, the speedstep naming was 'dropped' and most processor there after were branded with the 'hyperthreading' tag. However speedstep technology is still incorporated in some/ most processors after the 'hyperthreading' ones.

    Data are processed from the very fast level 1(L1) down to level(L2) before approaching the memory controller then to the memory modules (ram). the highest L2 cache i know are still from Intel's webbie and its 2MB on one physical core. When you have 2x2MB, its the usual availability of 2 physical cores with one L2 cache of 2MB each OR 2 possible virtual cores with 2 separate L2 cache ready for disposal when hyperthreading is activated.

    Everything is in the intel webbie and its very informative.
