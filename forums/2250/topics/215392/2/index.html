    What pipi has said should be noted. But do not reject online computer shops too fast - especially not a major manufacturer like Dell:

    1) If the notebook arrives faulty (highly unlikely as the notebooks are tested before shipping) - Dell will ship a new one out to you in exchange or you can seek a refund - even mere dissatisfaction qualifies.

    2) Regarding dead pixels - see (1) regarding dissatisfaction

    3) Indeed roadshows give a number of freebies - Dell has none - they have no middleman. But Dell shapes their global supply and demand by fast changes in pricing - i.e. if they decide to run out Core Duos, they'll have a massive price reduction + cash off + free delivery + free memory upgrade + free hdd upgrade etc etc - if you miss out on this, too bad - these happen very quickly and you need to be monitoring the website - pricing changes usually occur every three days

    4) Not every time will you get free upgrades of $650/$1000, but it has happened before, and it will happen again. Depends on your luck and timing. It really depends on whether Dell is hitting their projected sales figures - if they're not, they'll have massive reductions + free upgrades just to bump it up and meet their quarterly financial targets.

    The last major run-out was when Core 2 Duos came out - Dell had massive sale - for the same price on the website now, you would have received a faster processer, more memory, larger hard drive, free anti-virus, free shipping + delivery (usually not free) + misc. freebies. That's how I got my laptop cheap. and Note: Core 2 Duo and Core Duos do not have major performance differences.

    I expect the next run-out will be when the new Core 2 Duo platforms come out which will be next year.

    5) Regarding notebook batteries, Dell has since switched suppliers - matter of fact, Sony was never Dell's only supplier. It really depends on your luck, your batch. In any case, Dell is not the only manufacturer affected by the recall, but as one of the largest selling manufacturers, they were definitely one of the worst hit.

    6) Just because you buy online, does not mean you won't get warranty. There's an interesting difference. When my HP crapped out, I had to bring it to a service centre. When my Dell dvd drive crapped out, 2 technicians came to my house and resolved the issue.

    But I must fairly point out, as I have earlier, Dell has HORRID CS. They are polite but absolutely incompetent. You will spend hours on the phone with them just trying to get them to understand your problem.
