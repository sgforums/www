    Hi bluejuice, maybe I can shed some light.

    Firstly, before you begin considering notebooks, you should first decide what you want your notebook to do.

    If its going to be for day to day office applications, surfing the web, and misc. non-intensive programs, then note that any core duo processor will be just fine for your needs. People tend to overestimate and give too much credit to processor speeds. Even if you're going to be gaming, processor impact between those few hundred mhz wouldn't be massive.

    If you're going to be gaming, then you should be looking more towards what graphics solution the laptop comes with - the Dell 6400 that you're looking at for example can be configured for either an onboard Intel graphics accelerator, or a dedicated graphics solution such as the X1300, or X1400.

    There are heaps of considerations that you're gonna have to factor in and account for. Instead of listing every one of them manually (because I'm lazy; but feel free to PM me if you need specific direction), why don't you tell us what you expect your laptop to do.

    Regarding the 6400, it's of excellent build quality for that price. Dell makes great notebooks for the price bracket because there is no middle man. Infact, its the only notebook company I know of who has a rather translucent operations process. Dell customer service is pretty incompetent, but always polite and great to deal with. Besides, within the first year of use, any manufacturing defects will come to light, and will/can be dealt with using the standard one year warranty. Anything beyond that one year is anyone's guess. Go with the Dell, and save yourself a couple of hundred quid~

    Let me explain the Dell build process briefly:
    1) You place your order - order is sent to the factory
    2) Parts are picked out from their warehouses and sent to the factory.
    3) The machine is built, tested and gets software installed.
    4) Shipped to your door

    During the build process, you can track your order at any point in time through their online order status check.

    FYI: If your Dell laptop does turn out to be a lemon (and only very rarely does that happen), you can definitely return it and get a full refund, OR get an exchange. I know that Dell will exchange even for the most minor of things, e.g. light leakage
