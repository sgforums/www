    I guess you got to change the way you connect your all your cables... getting a new router may help, though I don't know who helpful it is.

    Hyper-threading is a technology by Intel. I wouldn't explain it, too much to write. You will read this article instead: http://www.pcstats.com/articleview.cfm?articleID=1302

    There are problems with hyper-threading, which I think your processor is causing. You have to turn off hyper-threading and try your game again to see if it crashes.

    It can be done via BIOS (usually pressing the Del button after your computer beeps).

    Navigate to Power Options > Advanced > Power On Options > Hyper Threading. Press the + or - sign to toggle it on and off. Change it to Off.
