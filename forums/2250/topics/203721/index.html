    its is norm for the buyers to quote a much lower price since they wan to make profits out of it too. that's why i hope u can let it go with buyers who intend to use the hardware on their own. even if you buy a unit and use it for a day, the unit will still be considered as second hand used to another buyer. you get to sell what that can be bought brand new and get back some cash...while the buyers pay lower at the risk of the unit being faulty/ not functioning at tip top condition. u can't convince them the truth and the buyers have to take the chances of the unit working under 99% efficiency.

    if u wan to use it as a media PC why not try it out running for hours first. using a notebook for media function is unlike a desktop and u have to keep it real cool. furthermore you may need more ram, a decent sound output (speaker), and a USB TV tuner/ PCMIA TV reception card. its good if u can add another 512MB but decent ram modules can be had from eBay. a brand new USB TV tuner will at least set u back sgd100 ++ while 2.1 speakers can be had for much less than sgd100. if you intend to record TV programs your hdd MAY be slow and 40gig is not enough unless your notebooks USB ports are high speed and you can transfer them to an external hdd or use your CD-writer to burn them.

    Celeron consumes less power and in this case are preferred in smaller size notebooks.

    my best suggestion is still for you to use the notebook as it is. since you have to even consult your parents when u sell your own stuff i bet you are a student. why not wait up after you start working before getting a new notebook?
