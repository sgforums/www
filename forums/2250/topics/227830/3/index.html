    In Windows Task Manager, the networking tab will show Local Area Connection, this is referring to the LAN port which looks similar to a telephone connector ( at the back of the CPU) but slightly larger in size, almost all computers today should have this.

    It will show either 10 Mbps or 100 Mbps or 1000 Mbps depending on your Network Interface Connection (NIC) Card's type & speed settings. You can change your NIC card's speed setting in Device Manager.

    If you connect via USB, the computer will show another Local Area Connection, maybe Local Area Connection 2. USB Connections will also show different speeds, depending on the version of USB being used. USB version and speed is hardware dependent.

    The Local Area Connection/Local Area Connection 2 displays the connector's speed, which is the speed of the NIC Card or USB. It will not display the actual speed of your internet connection, it will only display the speed of the connection between the modem and your computer.

    Network Utilization rate depends on the software programs you are using and how much bandwidth they use.
