    Thanks for uploading it, I have checked and realised that majority is 0x0000009C: MACHINE_CHECK_EXCEPTION. Which put it in a simple form is more or less an hardware error.

    So recently have you update any of your hardwares like graphics card, sound card etc... Did you just bought your computer and if you bought it for quite awhile and this problem only happens now? Also have you tried viewing the same video in fullscreen in other browsers beside Safari.

    Another thing is to do a memtest, which is to check your RAM for errors. To install memtest go here & than prepare a blank CD and a burning software (e.g. Nero). Once you have downloaded the above file open and extract the content inside which you will see "memtest86+-2.11.iso". Open your burning software and simply points it to the iso and let it burn. Restart your computer and put the disc inside when it first start (BIOS screen) than press any key to boot the disk. Than you will see a blue colour screen than just let it do the test for at least 10 tries. Watch the error column to see if it has any number other than 0. If there is, it is most probably your RAM has problem but before you disposed off, it is wiser to go to your manufaturer and let them test it out.

    To burn from Nero: Nero>Disk Image or Saved Project>[select the memtest iso file]Ok>Next
