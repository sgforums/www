    i use the dv2713tx.

    normal use sees the cpu at 50-53C, hard disk at up to 50C and GPU at 60C.

    but when playing games, the cpu can heat up to 66C, hard disk at 56C and GPU at 82C (highest record).

    problem lies with the lack of ventilation and probably all the heat emitting parts in close proximity within each other.

    and the fact that the fan does not stay on MAX at all the time.. it's a variable speed fan which is controlled by the computer itself, without a manual override. and the berth for the fan speed is very loose, so it takes quite a lot of heat before the fan actually starts to rev up.

    therefore thats what makes the laptop too hot to be considered for "light" use like word processing, etc.

    It seems that your graphics card is an 8400M GS, and if so, what games are you playing? I know some games can push the GPU very hot, especially if your GPU is a weaker one. The GPU needs to work harder to let you play your game. I would suggest also lowering the resolution and details of the games you play. It will help. As for things like word processing, try and make new power plans for each task, like Gaming, Browsing/Word processing and such. I set mine to High Perf only when gaming.
