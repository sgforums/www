    For HTML, this link is good.

    http://www.w3schools.com/

    Books: Beginning HTML, published by Wrox
    Beginning XHTML, published by Wrox

    As you go along, HTML will not be enough. CSS, Flash, JavaScript will be used. Once you are competent in creating websites, look for this book in the library:

    Professional Web Design: Techniques and templates. It's a rather thick book, but it teaches you to create websites which are attractive.

    W3 Schools website teach you all these as well, you can slowly learn. To learn Flash, there's a very good site.

    http://www.kirupa.com/

    All your Flash basics.

    I don't have any links for networking, but have some books to recommend.

    1) Networking Foundations (black in colour)
    2) CCNAB (Cisco Certified Network Associate Basics). There are 2 books for this, networking basics are inside these 2 books. Published by Cisco.
    3) IP Addressing and Subnetting including IPv6, published by Syngress.
    4) IP Addressing Fundamentals, published by Cisco.

    For PC Repairs, try getting books from Thomson about A+ Certification. Other books about A+ Certification are good too, just don't get a bible or passport kind of books. There's another one titled Bigelow PC Hardware, a very complete book to PC repair. Everything about hardware.

    If you want to pick up those skills, you can enroll in ITE. Dover ITE has the course. Enroll in the Higher Nitec (Information Technology).
