    First question: No.

    Second question: Try and access Windows Update. If it prompts that your software is not genuine, you need to contact Microsoft. They will give you another validation code to validate. Please remember to activate within 30 days otherwise you may find yourself locked out of Windows.

    I believe Microsoft only allows the change of ownership of software one time only. If you have upgraded quite a few times this year, most likely they won't let you change ownership.

    You might want to read their EULA. It's for Vista, but it's the same.
