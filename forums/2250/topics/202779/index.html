    If it is your office computer, ask your administrator if East Asian Languages are installed. If it is not, ask the administrator to do it. It will save costs buying new software. If East Asian Languages are already installed, here's how you enable it.

    Go to Start > Control Panel > Regional and Language Options.

    Select the Languages tab and click Details. A new window would pop up.

    Click on Add and find Chinese (PRC). Click OK.

    Next, Under Preferences, click Language Bar. Tick this box, "Show the language bar on the desktop."

    Click OK. Apply all settings. If it prompts you to restart, just do so.

    As for translation software, there's one that's used commonly by China students studying in Singapore. It's called Jin Shan Ci Ba or Jin Sha Ci Ba, can't remember it. Just go around asking these students and they will be able to give you a link for it.
