    That budget is quite low, but I think is enough if you know how to look at specs (but be prepared to get a poor quality laptop).

    Unlike laptop for other purposes like study, a gaming laptop requires very good hardware specs.

    • CPU at least Intel i5/3.0Ghz
    • RAM at least 4GB
    • Needs to have a dedicated graphic card (like GeForce, Radeon)
    When buying, ask the salesperson if the laptop has the above specs if you don't know how to look yourself.

    I don't think it's a good idea for us to give you the exact laptop because you'd have to find it/order online. So now you know the minimum specs, go to store and ask for laptops that meet the above requirements.

    Normally, laptops that meet the above requirements are budget brands like Acer and Lenovo. Quality sometimes matter because it may spoil easily even though it has very high specs.

    Real good gaming laptops priced at ~$2000.
