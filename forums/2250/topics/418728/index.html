  • ndmmxiaomayi
    26 Dec 10, 19:46

    I suspect that your HDD may need extra drivers to run, and autorun.inf triggers the installation of the drivers. When Avira blocks it, the drivers can't install and your PC BSOD.

    Try this:

    1. Disable Avira

    2. Plug in your HDD

    3. Install whatever software it prompts

    4. Unplug your HDD

    5. Restart the PC

    6. Plug in the HDD (with Avira enabled)

    Does it work?
