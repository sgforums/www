    Contrary to some reports that the new iPad launch is being delayed to Mar 19, it is still on course to be released on Mar 16.

    Apple Australia - which oversees the Singapore market - confirmed that the new iPad will be launched in Singapore on Mar 16. Ms Fiona Martin, corporate communications director of Apple Australia, said it was an incorrect wire story that caused the confusion globally. They are currently working with the reporter to correct the story.

    Singapore will be one of the first 12 countries in the world to receive the new iPad. Other countries include Hong Kong, Germany, Australia, the United States (US), France, Canada, Japan, Switzerland, Puerto Rico, United Kingdom and the US Virgin Islands.

    The new iPad will sport a high resolution display, new A5X chip with quad-core graphics and a 5-megapixel camera capable of 1080p video recording. Prices start from $658 for a 16GB Wi-Fi only version to $1,088 for a 64GB Wi-Fi+4G edition. However, the new iPad will not be able to support the local 4G network.
