    The Generic Host Process for Win32 process that crash will also cause you to lose your sound as well, because this particular process handles a lot of things in Windows, from logging in to Windows, to your Internet connections and your sounds.

    This process, by the way is svchost.exe.

    So, this explains why you can't hear any sound after it crashes. There's no point starting it again, because it's started by Windows automatically, and only Windows knows how to start it, we don't. Restarting your computer solves the problem, but the moment it crashes again, there goes your sounds again, and the cycle repeats.
