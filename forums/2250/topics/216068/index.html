    Okay if your new 320GB SATA II hard disk has no jumpers fine. The makers probably had an auto configure or auto detect system in the hard disk itself to back down the speed to 1.5Gbs to work with your motherboard. However some hard disk makers have jumpers built on their SATA II drives. An example is the Samsung 250GB model SP2504C.

    jumpers here
    http://www.samsung.com/sg/support/productsupport/download/Model_Select2.aspx?type=&typecode=406815&subtype=&model=SP2504C&fileType=UM&LSSI=/sg/module/ssi/left/lmenu_hddodd_harddiskdrive.sec&RSSI=/sg/module/ssi/right/rmenu_hddodd.sec
    download the user manual version 4.0 (English) to see the illustrations of the jumpers layout.

    as a precaution it is advisable to set the jumpers on the compatible settings in case there may be any interface protocol issues.

    As for your mobo its an 865PE chipset..ancient to some. But at the time of print on the user manual, SATA II hard disk were not introduced to the market yet. There should not be any options in the bios to tweak the interface speed. Unless a BIOS update has been released otherwise it is better to post a screen shot of your BIOS screen so that others who may be in a similar situation as yourself be kept informed and aware of the situation.
