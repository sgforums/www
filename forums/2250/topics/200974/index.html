    i will not claim myself a wise man and all the knowledge of computers i have are through trial and error...and the occasionally help from tech guys over at internet forums (includin sgforums of course hehe).

    you made a big mistake before you decided to format. you should disconnect (at least the power supply cable) your "D" drive hdd prior to formatting your "C" drive hdd. after that you should be able to boot into your C and access your D.

    however we would like to know more about your D. is it a ned unformatted hdd? or was it a back up drive for your datas?

    please take note that unlike a firewire or USB external hdd which has been configured in a case at the factory to be readable by a computer system as a separate drive, when you try to achieve this concept with an internal connection vis SATA (or IDE) on the computer motherboard, you may have to do otherwise. and the whole concept is unlike RAID (which i wouldn't wanna discuss here and i assume that your 2 disks are not in RAID).

    are your backup datas in your "D" hdd? other forumers may post alternate solutions but if i were you and land myself in this situation i will get a external hdd case mod, connect and try to interface with the computer via external USB. see if you can access the files (only) in such a set up.

    if your "D" is a new hdd unit, the safer way is to disconnect your "C" hdd, format and install your OS again on your "D" hdd nevertheless before transfering datas and files you wanna backup from "C" to "D". dun worry once you format "D" you can also run it independantly and access your backup files and datas.

    unless the disks are RAID (be it 0,1,0+1 etc), you should try to avoid initiating a format hard disk with 2 drives connected and powered on at the same time.

    format /u c: /fs:ntfs

    This is to perform a surface format and at the same time convert your harddrive to ntfs.

    Other wise you could:
    convert c: /fs:ntfs
    format c:
