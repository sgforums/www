    Looks like your system is in pretty bad shape. I assume you have your XP disk. If you don't, just borrow from a friend...

    Try this:

    Start > Run and type in regedit

    Locate these key and delete the bolded ones:

    HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\System\CurrentControlSet\Services\Winsock
    HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\System\CurrentControlSet\Services\Winsock2

    Restart the PC. This step is a must, otherwise Windows can't repair itself.

    After the PC boots up, go to Start > Control Panel > Network Connections

    Right click on your connection and select Properties.

    Click on the Install button.

    Click Protocol, then click on Add.

    Click Have disk.

    Copy and paste in C:\Windows\inf

    You will see a list of protocols. Select Internet Protocol (TCP/IP), then click OK.
