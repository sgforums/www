    After this, click Set. Apply the settings.

    Take a look at your task manager. You can use either of these methods.

    1st method:

    Right click on your task bar, select Task Manager

    2nd method:

    Press Ctrl + Alt + Del simultaneously. Select Task Manager.

    Look at the CPU column. Is any processes using near 100% of the resources? If yes, tell us which process it is. Don't kill it unless you know what to kill. If it's your Internet Explorer (iexplore.exe) or Firefox (firefox.exe) or any other browsers, just right click on the process, and click End Process. Restart it.

    Next, how much disk space is left? If you have 80GB and less than 5GB is available, it will cause frequent computer crashes. Try not to use the hibernate option too often, as it uses up disk space.

    Try clearing all your temporary files.
