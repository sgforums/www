    Many causes.

    Problem 1:
    Could it be that your computer is low on memory? Hence, it might take some time to load your browser. Meanwhile, you are stuck with seeing warped text until it finished loading..

    Solution 1:
    Try increasing your virtual memory, if you have tons of unused memory in your hard disk(s).

    Problem 2:
    Could it be that your browser encoding is wrong or non-existent? If you are trying to view a Chinese website, then you might need Chinese Simplified (GB2312) instead of Western European (ISO).

    Solution 2:
    In your browser, go to View, Encoding and tick the Auto-Select. You might want to open up [More] to see if you have the language available.

  • Conner MacDermott
    12 Apr 07, 23:39
    Right click My Computer, select Properties.
    Under System Properties, click Advance tab.
    Under Performance, click Settings.
    Under Performance Options, click Advance tab.
    Under Virtual Memory, click Change.

    Select Custom Size.
    Take note of the Recommended value.
    Specify the rounded values as a start.
    Keep increasing until you are comfortable with your speed.

    Rule of thumb, don't exceed more than 10& of the total disk space.
