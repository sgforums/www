    While it's true that Lenovo has bought the PC/laptop department of IBM, there are still differences between them.

    Lenovo laptops with the IBM logo specs are poor when compared with other brands. Prices wise, it's a tad higher. Warranty is also longer. Typically 3 years, but could be extended to 5 years.

    And they last, although somewhat heavy and ugly.

    It's also heavier and as for now, is still selling XP machines.

    The series also start with T, X or R.

    Those without the IBM logo, has specs that are comparable with the other brands out there, warranty is 1 year (could be extended to 3 years if I'm not wrong), produced by Lenovo. The prices are also lower ($1000 odd for the average ones to low end ones, a few thousand for the higher end ones). They are usually Vista Home Premium or Vista Home Basic.

    They also look nicer and lighter.

    If you've been to the last PC Show, you will wonder why the prices have dropped drastically. That's because the IBM logo and promise for quality is no longer there.

    The series start with Y or N (can't really recall).
