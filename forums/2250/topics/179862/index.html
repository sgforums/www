    I think you should find out first what kind of RAM your desktop is using. You know, SDRAM, DDR SDRAM, etc....

    Anything below 266MHz are SDRAM. Usually for computers bought before 2002. Anything above 266MHz will be DDR RAM. Usually for computers bought after 2002.

    For SDRAM, they are backward compatible. However, one disadvantage, the CPU will use the lowest number available, which will be PC100 RAM instead of PC133 RAM if you mixed both. That is why your computer can be crawling even though you have 1 piece of PC100 RAM and another piece of PC133 RAM. The PC will use the slowest one.

    For DDR RAM, you can still mix them, but not all are able to support that. Check your motherboard configurations before doing that. If you didn't do so, you can damage the motherboard.
