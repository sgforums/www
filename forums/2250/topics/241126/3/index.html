    Originally posted by MoonIce:
    mayi

    tell some details like pro n con for each brands le
    Acer and Dell - top specs, cheap prices, spoil fast. Those who take care of it well, usually will spoil after the warranty period.

    For Dell, they bundle a lot of rubbish software as well. Acer still not that bad.

    LG - top specs, can last long if you know how, but prices also very nice. Plus design is nice also.

    Lenovo - no good specs, decent enough for use. Prices wise, quite high. But one thing from them, they are long lasting. Design is not nice. Meant for practical uses. Just black in colour.

    Asus - their prices are a mess. I see some high-end laptops cheaper than the lower-end laptops. But last long and nice design as well.

    HP - Have everything of the above, prices not as competitive as Acer and Dell. Have some nice designs, but laptop is practically useless.

    Sony - They manufacture a lot of things, so you will sometimes get the latest tech toys, like Blu-ray drives. Design is nice, laptops are light. But prices ain't exactly nice. They have among the highest prices, similar to Lenovo. Specs wise, competitive with Acer and Dell. A lot of rubbish software bundled in as well.

    Fujitsu - Nice laptops as well. Specs wise, good. But prices not as good. Another one of high prices laptops.

    Panasonic - New in this area. But have some creative laptops. How they fair, not sure. Prices definitely not as competitive as Acer or Dell.

    Toshiba - everything is good, but have a lot of problems with controlling heat.

    Macbooks - never try yet. Perhaps those who have can tell better.
