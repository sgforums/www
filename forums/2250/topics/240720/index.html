    5 Mar 07, 14:07
    Low in consumption - without ink leakages, the consumption is pretty low. Excluding the printing of pictures, whereby the consumption is high.

    New refiller/cartridge - generally, not all printer brands offer refillers. One reason for doing so is because of profits, as refillers are cheaper than buying a new cartridge.

    Different companies, different policies. For me, I'm using HP. They allow you to trade in your old cartridges for a new one at a cheaper price, usually bought from a wholesaler or distributor.

    Slow printer - I see your definition of personal printer is quite high. Most personal printers don't print fast, but at a good enough rate not to be slow. If you are looking for something faster than this, you have to get a lazer printer.

    Budget - What exactly is your budget? Different people have different definitions of cheap. Who knows your definition of low is $1000? +/- $100 = $900/$1100. Good enough for a lazer printer.
