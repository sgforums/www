    In fact you will have to look at every model and design of the notebook in question. Unlike PC modding with hardware and cards readily available in retails for upgrade and replacements, the notebook assembly process is very different. Older models may have the graphics integrated on the motherboard so replacement of graphics means replacement of the whole system. If you are getting the newer models with the nVidia GPU and mobile graphics card as shown with my link most probably you can upgrade. Otherwise you may just have to check with the notebook manufacturers on the availabliltiy of any graphics card for replacement. Other than the graphics and motherboard, hard disk, random access memory, USB expansion and other add-ons upgrades should not be a problem for most notebooks.
