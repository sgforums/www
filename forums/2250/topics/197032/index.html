    1) when you start your comp, you'll see the first screen with all the counting numbers, which scans your ram and hard disk.

    2) at that screen look right below for this sentance:
    "Press "DEL"(or any other key) to enter BIOS"

    3) after you enter BIOS, browse through all the menus and look for an option for you to change the "Boot Sequence" to "CD-ROM first" instead of C:

    4) then after that, load your winxp cd in and "Save Changes and Exit"

    5) when your comp reboots again after the "first screen", you should see something like "Press and key to enter windows setup".

    6) Press Enter or any key at that screen and the windows setup screen will appear and follow instructions from there

    Originally posted by davidche:
    ok thanks for the answers, now,... a renew of questions.

    1. Is reformatting used when my com is infected with pests(virus and spyware)??

    2. B4 i reformat am i supposed to back up the com first?

    3. Does refourmatting garuntee me with no pests later on?
    1. Only when its impossible to remove the viruses/spywares completely. Some people reformat because after a few months, most windows PCs will slow down due to the number of applications installed. Reformatting your computer resets it to the state when you first bought it.

    2. Yes, you are recommended to backup all important data as all data is removed or overwritten when you reformat the PC. Even if you have files on another partition, its best if you can spare some time to backup those as well. Theres a slight chance that the other partition is affected by the reformatting and causing you to lose files.

    3. Reformatting only resets your computer, meaning all the viruses you have now will be cleared, along with your files and installed applications. Reformatting is not vaccination. You should install antivirus applications and firewall straight after formatting, before you get connected to the net.
