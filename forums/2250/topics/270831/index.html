    Originally posted by the_unknown:
    anyway to find out whether it is a graphics card problem or purely settings problem? cos i just bought this 2nd-hand card...
    To see if it's a settings problem, just mess around the settings. They usually have a manager for it, either at your system tray (near the clock) or in Control Panel. If it's neither, right click on your desktop and select Properties. Select the Settings tab and play around the settings.

    Hardware issues is sometimes obvious. If your PC can't start and has a beeping error which matches graphics card error, then it's most likely the card problem.

    There will also be other occasions where the graphics card will produce errors of some sort or no errors but the display is just odd. It isn't so obvious until someone try out the card on another system.
