    I think the answer is pretty obvious, regardless what course you are in, which would be Acer Aspire 4930G.

    Reasons is simple:

    • Apple Macbook is definitely out of question since its for design course
    • All prices, from what I see, is about the same (ranging from $1200 to $1400 w/o GST) other than HP Pavillion which is $1649 (also w/o GST) which is justified due to 3 years warranty (other models only provide 1 year warranty, extending warranty will add about maybe another $200 to $300, so in the end pricing would be about the same for all the models)
    • Acer Aspire 4930G have much better specification (better graphic, more RAM, more hard disk space) compared to the rest of the models given and about the same price too. Why settle for less for about the same price, especially when you are gonna use it for 3 years?

    Therefore, my recommedation would be Acer Aspire 4930G. However, do note that whatever notebook you decides on, please extend the warranty to cover 3 years (which is the duration of time you needed to complete your diploma).

    No matter how well you protect your laptop, it will definitely give you problems (e.g. casing problem, screen problem, motherboard spoil, ram spoil, graphic card spoil) that require you to visit their service centre.

    If you only buy 1 year warranty and its expired, good luck to you, its gonna cost more than that $200-300 bucks that your saved for not extending the warranty to 3 years and this is especially true if you are unlucky and need to visit the service centre frequently (for like 2-3 times per year) or if your motherboard/graphic card spoil/Screen spoil.

    Y_Shun's Notebook guideline

     - Lightweight [recommend: 12~13inch because screen resolutions are still 1280x800]
    A decent 14" laptop will weight around 2kg~
    However, bringing a laptop + books will be a large amount of weight for you and your bag! Especially if you travel to school by bus... imagine carrying a bag of over 2kg during the peak hours, its not light, mind you!

    Ignore if you do not need to bring books. [RP students] 

    -  Graphics Card [recommend: NVDIA, ATI; hate: Intel!]
    During your poly time, there are sure to be 1to2hrs of free time, during this free time, it will be time for you and your classmates to have mini-lan sessions, especially for guys....so a good graphics card is necessary to play games such as Left4Dead, COD etc etc.

    - Battery Life
    If theres an option to get higher battery life, probably consider for it only if its within your budget. You wouldn't want to hunt around for power socket.

    - Matt Screen
    Find matte screen computers like Fujitsu/Toshiba because glossy screens usually makes it harder for your eyes with reflections on it.

    Lastly...its design!

    - Find a cool stylish design notebook for yourself and take good care with it. This thing will be your pencil box for years to come, so take good care for it :D

     

    Remember to purchase 3years warranty. :)
