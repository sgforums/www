    15 May 07, 20:13
    Originally posted by MooKu:
    I don't understand leh.

    Why do I have two IP addresses?
    Or is one of them some gateway address or something..... I'm using a router.
    Gateway address is for you to configure your router.

    There's another IP address known as the internal IP address. You can tell it by clicking on your connection (near the clock), select the Support tab. You will see IP Address: X.X.X.X, where X are numbers. That's the one assigned by your router, and is the internal IP address. Internal IP addresses are used on local networks only. So let's say you have a laptop and a PC and you want to share files, you can just connect to your laptop without ever needing the Internet.

    The external IP address is what we are talking about. It's the one assigned by your ISP (Pacnet, Singnet, Starhub, M1, QMax, iCell). They are the ones that get logged by servers. Recall the threads hijacking? Jason knew because it was logged down by servers. It's also traceable, in the above link given to missqi.
