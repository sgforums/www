    If you want to shave off $50, get yourself a HD4850 instead of the 5750. About the same performance for both of them, except that the 5750 has DX11 support and uses the 40nm process (smaller = more efficient)

     

    Get yourself a nice power supply, for that set-up, a 400W from a reputable brand will be good. Want to be cheapo or not enough cash? The Vantec ION 2+ is a good budget PSU, about $80 for the 500W PSU. That said, if you have extra moolah, spend it on power supplies from Antec, Cooler Master, OCZ, Corsair etc. Won't go wrong.

    Cooling fans? I highly recommend it. The stock fan is EXTREMELY LOUD. If you bought to HD4850 and saved that $50, get the Cooler Master Hyper 212+. Good budget heatsink plus fan combo. Also, aftermarket cooling solutions keep the CPU cooler, and that usually results in a longer lifespan.

    Sound card? The E53 has a very good 'high-end' built in sound (ALC889A), cannot beat Creative X-fi lah, but X-fi is $100++.
