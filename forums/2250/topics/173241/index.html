    Too confirm whether your RAM module is faulty or not, swap it with another module that's OK, and see if it hangs up.

    If swapping RAM modules still produces hang ups, then you should look closely at your motherboard for swollen capacitors. Look at your graphic card too for swollen caps. If there is none, open up your power supply to see if caps in there are leaky, swollen, or ruptured.

    Voltage control issues within your PC usually causes these sort of BSOD.
