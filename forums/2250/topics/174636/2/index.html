    Any CD (Both commercial and non-commercial) that contains any data can be read by your drive E (CD-Rom Drive) except DVD. CD-Rewritable Drive can read whatever the CD-Rom Drive reads + CD-Rewriteable.

    Switch off your system and main sockets. Re-connect all the wires of the CD-Rom Drive and try again.

    Test it using a VCD or Audio CD.

    If it doesn't work, I'll need to find out more about your CD-Rom Drive:

    1. Is the light on your CD-Rom Drive always lit?
    2. Does it produce and strange sounds when reading?
    3. ABOUT how old is your CD-Rom Drive?

    To copy/duplicate a disc using only one drive, fire up your CD burning software, and under the types of CDs to burn, choose Copy Disc (Or some labels it as "Make a backup"). It will then copy the data in your F drive and eject the disc, asking you to insert a blank disc to burn.

    By the way, Audio CD, Video CD, CD-R, CD-rom and Recordable CD are all made of the same type of disc.
