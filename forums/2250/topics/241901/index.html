    Both hardware and software will cause a system to hang.

    For hardware, generally, they cause your blue screen or you just can boot up to Windows. For blue screen errors, it's usually an incompatible driver (make sure you select correctly), or is an updated driver (just do a roll back) that is in conflict. Or in one of the cases here, the driver wasn't installed properly, and copying and paste the driver in the system32\drivers folder would solve the problem.

    Software wise, there's lots of reasons. It could be a bug in the software, using too much system resources, viruses, a very fragmented drive, too many startup programs and services, etc.

    A lag during surfing - is either Internet speeds or inadequate system resources. A defragmentation of hard disk, cleaning up of malwares and adding RAM will generally solve the problems. If it doesn't, and you are surfing using a tabbed browser, try closing unused tabs. Too many tabs can take up precious memory resources.

    Hang during boot-up, hints at software problems. Usually, it's the boot file that is corrupted, and you just need to rebuild everything and it will be solved. If it gives you a blue screen immediately Windows is loading up, drivers or hardware are in conflict. This usually occur in people who have graphics and sounds on board. Some require them to be disabled manually in order for external devices to work, otherwise, it will get into trouble. Drivers that cause problems, boot into Safe Mode to determine.

    Also, it could be too many startup programs and services, which could be disabled. Sometimes, they can overwhelm a system, even a brand new one. It's worth to take a look at them and disable unnecessary programs and services.
