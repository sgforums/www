    Your computer a Pentium 3, early Pentium 4 or Anthon system? It helps if you can provide the manufacturer and model number. Not to worry though since you can search for the 5400rpm drive over wt the second-hand market. Simlim may be a promising place to get one otherwise you can try eBay or buy form people who are willing to sell their old units.

    If you want to use the newer 7200rpm IDE or SATA drive on your old system and wants stability at the same time you can get SATA/IDE controller cards. These cards plugs into the older PCI (2.xx) slots with onboard SATA/ IDE connectors that in turns connects to the hard disk. So instead of data being accessed through te motherboard IDE/SATA connectors, the hard disk communicates via data exchange through the PCI buses.
