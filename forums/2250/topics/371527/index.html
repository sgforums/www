    Budget?

    at least core 2 duo for now, or if u want can wait for the p55 with the core i5

    for gaming pc also need to get a true power powersupply(PSU), a 450w true/real/pure power psu is diff from a standard rated power psu.

    mobo just get a normal one with 6 or more sata if u are running a few hdd. also look at the mobo before buying, some are badly designed that the sata potrs are covered by the graphics card, or the pci express slots are so close to each other that u can only run single slot cards in crossfire. p45s are quite popular now for mainstream systems. x58s are for the gaming section

    graphics card can look for those with nonref. cooling, esp if u L> high performance cards

    sometimes shop will tell u to crossfire, like buying 2 4770 and cf, but u need to get a mobo which can have their pci express slot running both x16 when on crossfire, if not both running @ x8 might hinder performance, but that means the mobo will be ex

    graphics card these few months like nothing outta the ordinary, i tink ati still more bang for the buck

    rams get the normal ddr2-800 @ 6-6-6-18 or ddr2-800 @ 5-5-5-12 will do, unless u are going to squeeze out all the performance, go for those ddr2-1066. in short, get the ram with fast core clock and low timing

    dvd drive just get those with writer , 30-40 dollar if i not wrong
