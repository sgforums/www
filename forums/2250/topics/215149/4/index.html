    For those who signed up for Singnet:

    1. Go to any of the hotspots.
    2. Connect to Wireless@SG network.
    3. Launch your favourite internet browser and type in any URL. You will be re-directed to log in.
    a) Singnet customers: type in your Singnet userid and password. For example, mayi@singnet, password, ***********
    b) Singtel mobile customers: type in your mobile number, followed by @stm. Password is your IDEAS password. If you have not signed up for this service, sign up first.

    For those who signed up for QMax:

    http://wsg.qmax.com.sg/portal/wlogin.aspx

    Click on the above link to log in.

    For those who signed up for iCell:

    1. Go to any hotspots.
    2. Connect to Wireless@SG network.
    3. Launch your favourite internet browser and type in any URL. You will be re-directed to log in.
    4. Enter the log in details.

    Some FAQ after the Wireless@SG clinic in Woodlands.

    Q1: Is this free?
    A1: Yes, it's free, but for a limited time period. This free broadband will be free until 31 December 2009.

    Q2: Does it cover HDB flats?
    A2: No, unless you are staying near major shopping centres such as Toa Payoh Hub. In such places, they are being covered extensively, so you will be able to receive the wireless signals.

    Q3: Is it everywhere?
    A3: No again. The operators have agreements with IDA and other retailers to set up the Wireless@SG access points.

    Q4: Does it cover MRT stations?
    A4: Technically, no. However, you may be able to receive wireless signals if you still have not cross the gate.

    Q5: How do I login?
    A5: Connect to the Wireless@SG network, surf to any webpage. You will be prompted to login. Type in your username and password to login. If you have forgotten your password, please follow the instructions on the webpage to reset it.

    For Singtel mobile customers, please use your IDEAS username and password provided to you when you sign up the line with Singtel.

    For Singnet broadband users, please call 1610 to reset your password. For Singtel mobile customers, please call 1626 to reset your password. It's much more faster and straight-forward than to do a reset via the web.

    Q6: It doesn't re-direct me to the login page.
    A6: Please clear your browser's history, cache and cookies and try again.

    Q7: If I'm a foreigner, can I register too?
    A7: Yes. Connect to the Wireless@SG network, surf to any webpage. You will be re-directed to a login page. On the login page, you can click to sign up. Please use your passport number to sign up. Your password will be sent to you via SMS. Overseas charges apply, depending on which mobile phone company you signed up with.

    Q8: Is it secure?
    A8: No. It's an opened network; anybody can connect to it. Please use a firewall when surfing using the Wireless@SG network. If you need to connect back to your company, please use the VPN provided by your company to connect back to the company. Singnet, iCell and QMax will not be responsible for any data loss if you failed to do so.
