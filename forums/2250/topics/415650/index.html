    "I'll need both the domain name as well as web hosting services" Yes.

    "If I signed up for a (Domain Name & Web Hosting) package, say 1 year, does that mean my Domain Name & Web Hosting services will be terminated after 1 year should I decline to extend the services?" Yes. Do note that you're free to transfer to other web hosts and domain name registrars at any time should you be unsatisfied by your current service providers.

    "I wish to first build a simple, cost effective and presentable business website (<10-12 pages) on my own and the ability to update my website as and when I desire." Consider using either a blog or a Content Management System (Wordpress, Joomla, e107 etc). On most web hosts, which are running cPanel, there's a click-install feature called "Fantastico" for quick-and-easy installation of common web applications. http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fantastico_(web_hosting)

    The only differences between free and paid web hosting services are the quality of the services and the availability of support. If you're on a free web hosting service and your website goes down one day, don't expect to receive any form of immediate support from your web host.

    Ultimately, the decision to choose between either is this: Would the additional downtime of your website and lack of support cost you more than a paid web hosting service in terms of lost sales? For a small online business with a daily revenue of $1,000, even a downtime of 6 hours would result in a loss of $250 in revenue. It might not be a smart idea to go with the free option when a paid service typically cost under $20 a month. On the other hand, if you're just exploring and experimenting with the web, feel free to start out with a free web hosting service.

    Another thing to note is the location of the servers. If your website is targeting visitors from a particular geographical area, it would be best to employ a hosting service of which servers are in the same area, if your budget permits, to reduce loading times for the visitors you're targeting.

    As for domain names, .com's typically cost $15 to $25 a year depending on the domain name registrar you register with. As for .sg's and .com.sg's, it's typically available at $50 per year, and the latter would require proof of business registration. You may want to read more about it here: http://vooju.com/faq_domain.html
