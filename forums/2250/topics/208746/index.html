  • ndmmxiaomayi
    6 Sep 06, 10:56
    You can't hibernate because there is not enough hard disk space on your laptop. Hibernation uses the same amount of hard disk space as your RAM.

    In your case, you have 1GB of RAM, which means 1GB of hard disk space will be used for hibernation. This will leave less than 1% of hard disk space left, making hibernation impossible. Windows need you to have at least 5% of the hard disk to be free to run properly, which means you need at least 5GB of hard disk space, assuming that there is only C drive.

    Please remove any unnecessary programs, backup your files in an external media to free up disk space.

    After freeing up hard disk space, your laptop should be able to hibernate and standby again.

    Turn off your internet connection. Your network card could be set to wake on lan. Or do this:

    To stop your computer from waking up when your internet / network connection is active, go into Control Panel > System > Hardware > Device Manager, then find the device that controls your connection (e.g. Network Adapters or Modems). Right click on the relevent device and go to Properties > Power Management, then de-select "Allow this device to bring the computer out of standby".
