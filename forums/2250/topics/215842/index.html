    That's a newer processor core you are using but the chipset may not be the latest for the mobo (865P). If you want more features (esp HD TV and much up-to-date sound support), you may want a newer chipset. 1-2 gig of DDR-2 ram from a trusted manufacturer won't harm - that's in my configuration for the next build. But with the hard disks and sound card add on i trust that you will want to use at least a better psu. 450 watts is fine but get a 'branded' one. I do not know about graphics card but most of them new ards are power hungry. Even if you add a 500 watts or higher PSU i will say it will only make your build future-proof. That PSU is definitely one thing you have to work on.

    Some ATI cards are more affordable and you may want to look into the cards' performances especially when an everlier post indicated that some games do not play well on ATI as compared to nVidia. Some games were tested on either card and proven to work fine or better desired. Also games based on different engines works differntly with ATI/ nVidia. A few things to take note of is the removal of embbeded nVidia grpahics drivers if you were to switch to a different card type and the fact that AMD and ATI are an item now. For me to choose my next card may well be an ATI but until my next build i will hav to be keep a bre ast of the lateest news with the 2 graphics card chip vendors.
