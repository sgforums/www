    Boyboyb

        I am not sure the many other methods (like Browser plugins) out there that may suit you more,  but since I do not install any plugins,  I suggest this method:

    • Open the website, :>  www.keepvid.com
    • Copy your Youtube address (and exclude whatever it is after the "&" sign) and paste it into the blank space in www.keepvid.com
    • Click "download"
    • Click "Run" or "Allow" whenever a dialog-box appears saying whether do you want to authorise your computer to run the application
    • After the process,  the website will show many options, including ">> Download MP4 <<"   and  ">> Download MP3 <<".   Choose the mp3 that you need (i choose "mp3" if you want songs only,  not the video)
    • Click "Run this time" or "Run" whenever a dialog-box appears saying whether do you want to authorise your computer to run the application
    • You can click the ">> Download MP3 <<" now to download and save your mp3 onto your computer now  (don't anyhow click "Save",  choose a appropriate folder first,  preferably onto the desktop if you want to do a quick one)
    • After you downloaded several songs that way,  you would want to start burning them onto a CD,   so here I use iTunes to do my stuffs,   although I have many other programs,  this is the one I prefer
    • If you don't have iTunes you can go to http://www.apple.com/itunes/ to download iTunes
    • In ITunes, pull the MP3s that you have converted into the Main empty space
    • Create a new playlist by going to "File" menu and click "New Playlist"
    • Rename the new playlist to whatever you want
    • Go back to the Main empty space window and select those relevant songs that you want,  and hold down your mouse and pull them into your new playlist
    • After you finish with that, right-click mouse on your new playlist on the left and select "Burn Playlist to Disc"
    • When the dialog box pops-up, click "Audio CD" and "Gap Between Songs" according to your relevant needs,  and insert an empty new CD upon prompting,  and your job is done.    (only choose "MP3 CD" if you do not want the CD to be played on most normal CD players)

    Maybe CD-RW can one lah,  it is just that for CD-ROM is confirm 100% can,  so that's why I suggest CD-ROM.

    It is not the size of your song that your CD-Player cannot play.  It should be because you did not burn it in an Audio CD format thus you cannot play it on your CD-Player.

    Also, if you closed the session (or sealed the session) after burning CDs, you will not be able to burn any more songs into a CD again.  You must confirm everything that you want to burn,  and you cannot do any changes to it again (example, in a CD-ROM, you cannot delete anything, cannot rename anything, cannot add a new file)

    Therefore, whether you use iTunes or Realplayer,  you must do it properly in order of the songs that you want,  before you click "Burn".   Treat this piece of CD-RW that you burn as an experience lah.

    An Audio CD does not calculate it's size by the file size of your songs (eg. 1.36MB) ;  an Audio CD calculates by the length of a song that can be fitted into a Audio CD.  The original file-size of your MP3s does not matter at all.  Therefore, an Audio CD can keep about 80 minutes of songs.
