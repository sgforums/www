    It also depends on the finishing of the keypads as it was meant to be during production/ design. If the surface started off as matt after prolonged period of contacts (with the fingers) and wear, the 'rough' uneven surface shall be smoothen and thus the gloss thereafter. If the keypads were meant to be even/ glossy to start with, the wear after a while may not be that obvious.

    Generally but not always keypads with smoother glossy surface will cost more as the inner walls of the mold has to be polished to maintain the gloss of its molded product. Its either that or after treatments to make the molded products look glossy.

    Some generic plastic material comes with limited colors and feel as compared to another with paintwork applied but will not show obvious gradation in wear as compared to plastic with spray over paintwork. Depending on the environment, handling of the end user etc, the rate of wear may vary and the results will usually mean baring the base plastic material and showing a contrast.

    We must also understand that our hands may sweat and its a process that can rapidly cause certain decorative agents to peel and loses its adhesiveness from the surface after some time.
