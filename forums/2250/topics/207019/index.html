    Iomega ships 320GB eSATA hard drive

    Iomega has begun shipping its first external hard drives equipped with an eSATA port for direct connection to a PC's Serial ATA bus, the storage specialist announced this week.

    No eSATA port on your PC or Mac? Don't worry, Iomega's bundling a suitable PCI add-in adaptor. The drive also has a regular USB 2.0 port too. eSATA connects at 1,500Mbps, rather more than USB 2.0's top speed of 480Mbps and even Firewire's to-line rate of 800Mbps.

    How fast the drive is, is another matter. Iomega's offering a 320GB unit based on a 7,200rpm drive with 2MB of cache memory.

    The drive comes bundled with suitable cables and back-up software for both Mac and Windows users. The package retails for $240 in the US. Iomega didn't say if or when the drive will ship in Europe. Â®



    IE7 'near-final' version released

    Will be ready by the end of the year

    By Nick Farrell: Friday 25 August 2006, 08:53

    SOFTWARE GIANT Microsoft has released a 'near final' beta of its super soaraway internet browser IE7 for punters to have a play with.
    Internet Explorer 7 Release Candidate 1 replaces the beta 3 version and has all the features that Vole will include in the final product, though it's only available in English for now. Additional languages will be launched in September, including the first Spanish and French versions.

    The new build apparently has improvements in performance, stability, security, and application compatibility. Support for CSS has been enhanced and there is an auto-uninstall feature to help punters who've got earlier betas on their machine to get RC1 up and running.

    Microsoft seems pretty chuffed with the progress its browser is making and thinks it will be ready for a full version by the end of the year.
