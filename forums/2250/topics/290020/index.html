    We are only restricted to some pornographic websites as the government wants to that it does not stand for that.

    Singapore did have widespread piracy concerns in the past but we have kept it under control. Piracy in computer related software had in the past helped us to gain knowledge that would otherwise be rather unaffordable for many. However our society is more affluent now and Singapore wants to be knowned to protect intellectual property rights and being an anti-pirate assists in the formulation of Free Trade Agreements. Thus most forms of piracy widespread.

    We hardly get knockdowns (illegal or otherwise) due to our stance against piracy and we don't have brands like Nike setting up sweatshops in our country as labour costs are considerably higher.

    No we do not have police knocking on our doors due to illegal softwares on our PCs. Businesses do however get into trouble when someone blows the whistle on them. Whistleblowers get rewarded by software developers
