    You could Google some netiquette online.

    DIY computer - means you built it yourself or you choose the parts of a PC yourself and get someone to build for you.

    It does come with warranty and support, but in bits and pieces. That means, hard disk support in one company, motherboard support in another, etc. How long that warranty and support will last, will all be listed in the warranty card itself.

    Typically, a hard disk warranty lasts anything from 1 year to 5 years, though there are a few that are slightly longer.

    Motheboard warranty is around 1 to 3 years, hardly longer than this.

    CD/DVD drive warranty is around 1 year.

    Graphics card warranty is also 1 year.

    Keyboard/mouse is up to the individual company's decision.

    Monitor warranty is anything from 1 year to 3 years.

    You also have to differentiate between the different types of support, some are on-site (meaning they go to your house to service), some you have to carry it yourself to the service centre.

    All terms and conditions are listed in all the warranty cards. You have to read carefully, and in some cases, sent them to the company in question.
