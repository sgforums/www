    what kind of ext hdd would you like to get?

    1. 3.5 inch hdds 
    2. 2.5 inch hdds

     

    3.5 inch hdds are generally cheaper, than their 2.5inch siblings, however, they are have heavier and bulkier casings and they normally require a power source for them to run.

    2.5 inch hdds are generally more expensive. however they are portable and they require only a usb port to power it up.

    There are plenty of brands of hdds -- segate, maxtor, toshiba ect.

     You can either get these hdds and then get a casing to go with it or get pre-formatted ones from various manufacturer. The price difference is not really that much.

    The best place i recc to see lots of varieties of different kinds of hdd would be challenger.

    My advice will be to stay away from Maxtor. Maxtor used to produce great hard disks in the past (in fact all my old PCs use Maxtor HDDs) and they last long. The newer ones I built no longer use Maxtor but Seagate or Hitachi.

    Yes. You can install games/ put files/ ect. Not anyknown requirements, except I would recc having a folder for each game you install. If not their dll files will be all over the place.

    It would function like a thumbdrive (plug and play) just that it has a larger capacity.

    If you will not bring your external hdd around, i would recc getting the 3.5 inch external hdd. The difference between a 3.5 and a 2.5 inch hdd is that the 2.5 is smaller than the 3.5 and it requires only a usb port. The 3.5 requires a power source. However the 3.5 inch hdd is cheaper. A 80gb 2.5'' hdd would be more expensive than a 80gb 3.5'' hdd at same storage capacity.
