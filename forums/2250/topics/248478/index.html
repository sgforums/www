    Hmm.. You have four instances of firefox.exe?

    Generally, an instance of an application (eg. firefox.exe) is captured on your Task Manager for every attempt made to launch that application. So, that means something(s) on your computer is trying to launch your browser (for three times) even though it was already launched. As a result, Task Manager will just "pool" these processes together and "share" the RAM.

    As to what actually caused this, I have no idea. It may be malware, or it might be driver conflicts. But, since you are using mIRC, Bit Torrent, and somewhat of an online gaming platform, I would suggest you to scan your computer for potential malware. If nothing turns up, try updating all your programs to resolve any potential "conflicts" in your system.
