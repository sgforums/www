    Hard disk failure may be attributed to different reason but more often than not it's just wear and tear.You must know that the hard disk is actually a platter spinning very fast while the head basically reads the information on the surface of the platter.Over time, the magnetic film on the surface may be scratched by the head and there you have it, a hard disk failure.

    As for Abao's question, use the hard disk manufacturer diagnostics tool to run a check on the hard disk instead of Pcwizard, if you friend is worried, you can ask him to backup the data first.
