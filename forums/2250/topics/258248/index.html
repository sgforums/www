  • token141
    2 Jun 07, 15:19
    Just go to SIM LIM Square and look for a wireless router that supports 802.11a/b/g...you can get a simple one that cost less than S$80. There are various brands such as SMC.

    It should come with a manual that teach you how to set up..basically, you connect your current broadband modem to the wirless router (instead of to your PC now) via a network cable. Once you defined the SSID (an identifier for your laptops to identify the wireless network) and protection such as WEP/WPA (password to share between the wireless router and your laptops so that others like your neighbours cannot use it), that's all. You turn on your broadband modem and your wireless router and you have a wireless LAN at home.
  • token141

    One more thing. If you still have a desktop that needs wired access, i.e., it does not have wireless capability, look for a wireless router that comes with a few wired network ports. Typically most wireless router have that.

    So you can connect the desktop to the wireless router via a network cable and your laptops can connect via wireless.
