    Ok, you have to subscribe to Webroot Spysweeper in order to remove the spyware.



    For this part, open registry editor.

    Go to Start, Run. Type in regedit in the textbox.



    Find HKEY_CURRENT_USER, click on the plus sign.

    Find Software, click on the plus sign.

    Find Microsoft, click on the plus sign.

    Find Windows, click on the plus sign.

    Find CurrentVersion, click on the plus sign.

    Find ruins. Delete that.

    Do the same for all the spywares shown in the picture (the picture before the registry editor).

    If you are unsure, use Microsoft Antispyware. Editing the registry is risky if you don't know how. Either that, use Spybot S&D. See the advisory thread on spyware.

    For this part:



    Restart Computer in safe mode. Press F8 before the Windows logo shows. Select safe mode. Find the entries one by one and delete them. You have to print out your picture to help you.

    Again, use Microsoft Antispyware if you don't want to spoil your computer. Spybot S&D also offers removing those spyware.

    Originally posted by Gordonator:

    As long as you enable cookies, google/yahoo problem will be there. Just clear your cookies before you shutdown. Working in safe mode is just to prevent things from going wrong.

    For IE, go to Tools, Internet Options. Click Delete Cookies.

    Then Delete Files. Check the Delete all offline contents checkbox also. Then click OK. Click on Clear History.

    After all this is done, click OK.

    For Firefox, if you are using v1.5 and above, just press Ctrl+Shift+Del. It will clear all your cookies and cache.

    For the ruins spyware, use Spybot S&D. Use their cleaning utility to clean off. After that, restart computer in Safe Mode. Find the folder where this spyware is installed. Check your registry also. If it is back there, delete it. Restart computer again. Do a full system scan. If it is not there, then problem should be solved. If it comes back a few days later, write in to Straits Times for help. and don't visit that particular website again.
