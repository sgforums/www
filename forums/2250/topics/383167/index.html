    And 8GB isn't bad, just that 8GB + Socket 1136 means that the chip will revert to Dual-channel operation. The difference between dual and triple is that dual accesses 2 DIMMS at once, and triple accesses 3 at once. This increases the amount of data that can transferred simultaneously, and decreases latency. Think of it as a (R)AID 0 for RAM, except with 3 drives instead of 2. To enable triple channel, populate the triple-channel slots. Or install DIMMs in multiples of 3. I'm guessing you have the DX58SO or a similar board with 4 DIMM slots. Removing a DIMM from the other-coloured slot might work, but isn't guaranteed if the DIMMs aren't part of a kit.
