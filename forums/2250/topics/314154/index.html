Acer service is an Ace

  • ckh@mls

    An ace ball cannot be returned, so is Acer service. I bought an Acer PC end January and there was a problem optical disc writer. It couldn’t write to UDF ruining my CD-R and CD-RW discs, making them useless.

    On Mar 14, I called helpdesk which was like more than 20 minutes waiting in line to speak with a human being. They couldn’t give me a solution and requested some time to investigate. The next day I got a call that an engineer would come to site to check. The appointment was to be an embarrassing week later.

    At the appointment date, I waited and waited but nobody showed up; not even a courtesy call. In the night, I put in an email at their website to complain for the no show.

    Nothing happened the next day, no calls or emails. In fact nothing happened, not until when I called their helpdesk 5 days later. It was only then they FOUND OUT THAT THEY DID NOT ATTEND TO ME. This revelation triggered off the usual ‘Dear John’ apologies and a top-priority re-appointment but there was no mention of what if I hadn’t call and what my loss was for all the time wasted.

    I told them firmly that I wasn’t going to accept all these ‘Dear John’ apologies. The last authority I spoke with promised me her immediate boss was to call me but he didn’t, obviously because he didn’t make that promise.

    At this top-priority re-appointment, an engineer came and spent more than an hour testing the optical disc writer but failed to find a solution. The other option of replacing the disc writer couldn’t be carried out as he didn’t have any with him. In the meantime, I got email addresses of his superiors and sent them complaint emails but they chose to ignore and not reply. So another appointment was set to replace the drive. The drive was replaced with another of the same make and model. After more than half an hour of testing the problem still remains. He said he had to pass this on to their technical department.

    Later he called saying that a service pack software upgrade would solve the problem. I did and ruined some more CD-R and CD-RW. A couple of days later he called to check and another appointment was fixed. He came and tested ok using his discs but failed using mine. As he was more prepared he replaced the drive with another after the failure, a different make and model. This time it solved the problem but my dead discs remained dead.

    This clearly shows poor service and responsibility. They did not solve the problem on their own. In fact they didn’t know of such problem that the OS had a software problem or their original installed drive had a compatibility problem. I was their R&D guinea pig who had to sacrifice my own time and discs for their benefit. What difference or benefit do we find in a branded Acer PC and a no-brand self-assembled one? The next question is why buy Acer? Is it because Acer Serves Aces?
