Good news for Starhub broadband subscribers

  • Poignant
    17 Mar 10, 00:06

    StarHub upgrades broadband
    By Wendy Lim

    USERS who surf the web on the go will soon get faster Internet access from StarHub.

    The telco will be progressively upgrading its mobile broadband network to the dual carrier frequency technology which will allow for data download speed of up to 42.2Mbps, double the maximum speed on the current network.

    'StarHub is excited that it will soon be able to manage even larger volumes of data traffic and continue to support the highest HSPA speeds available in Singapore, to meet the growing demand for broadband access on the move,' said Peter Cook, Starhub's vice-president of integrated network engineering on Tuesday.

    With the implementation of the network upgrade, Starhub has become the first mobile operator in Southeast Asia to deploy the dual carrier technology.

    The upgrade to its mobile broadband network is expected to be fully completed by the third quarter of this year, said a statement from StarHub.
