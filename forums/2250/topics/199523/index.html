    Nah.. don't care so much about wikipedia or what...
    Lemme just summaries for you a bit..

    Basically you heard of brick and mortar company before?
    It is a normal shop that is built with concrete and patrons will visit their shop to buy things.

    Now, what is click and mortar?
    It is a virtual shop that only uses a few clicks to purchase items.
    And this is what mainly internet shops does...

    However, ebay doesn't need inventory, which is purely click-only company. It is a direct C2C business...
    Note: B2B is Business to business
    B2C is business to consumer
    C2C is consumer to consumer

    Anyone can just sell their items in ebay, and anyone can bid for it.
    Ebay earns a little profits during transaction.

    Ebay business strategy is mainly on its free C2C service.
    Since it is free, anyone can sell anything.
    Although the profit is little, the clients are a lot.

    Ebay had some fraud free transaction system.
    It's let users leave a comment after the transaction.
    It will auto delete an auction after some days to reduce the laggyness of the whole server....
