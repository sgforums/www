    My view...

    I prefer DIY, reason being I get to customize it the way I want it to be. Generally, it's cheaper, unless the system is top-end. In that sense, a manufacturer will be able to help you save more bucks as they get the components directly from a distributor.

    If there's any reason why I choose pre-built PCs, it's because of convenience. Recovery is in a hidden partition, press a few buttons and done. Some softwares bundled are the full version, which saves money buying. Drivers are readily available, just go their website and download. You don't have to do a lot of preliminary steps to find out what is suitable, what is not, then get the right stuffs.
