    Athlon is mostly for desktops and older laptops.
    Think in terms pentium 4 processors in the older laptops

    Turion is a mobile technology developed by AMD after the seperon. These processors have better battery and power usage and are more suited for laptops.
    THink in terms of the pentium M processors, Centrion which are all for laptops.

    Speed wise both should be the same. If you compare in terms of Ghz.
    Newer versions, if you count by release date it would be Turion.

    But Turion and Athlon processors have now been updated, to Turion 64x2 and Athlon 64x2 processors. (Think core duo).

    Thus in short, one is a general processor while the other is more for portable processors.

    Ahem, duo core doesn't mean 64 hor.

    The 64 there means it's a 64-bit processor.

    This also means 2 things

    1. More RAM could be added (provided it's supported by mobo)

    2. You can use a 64-bit OS (XP 64-bit, Vista 64-bit, Linux 64-bit, etc)

    What works in 64-bit WILL NOT work in 32-bit, but what works in 32-bit MAY work in 64-bit.
