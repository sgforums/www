    I'll be straightforward with you - get a new motherboard. Virtually all motherboards on the market now support PCIe graphics cards. Yours is only compatible with AGP graphics cards which are simply not worth their current retail price as they are almost phased out and provide a lower performance as compared to their PCIe counterparts of the same price range. To illustrate my point better with an example, a $200 AGP graphics card would lose out in performance as compared to a $100 PCIe one. My advice to you would be to save up a little more over the next few months and get an entirely new system instead.

    If you're insistent on getting a new graphics card for your current system and are currently using an on-board one, all I can say is, spend as little as possible for it by getting an entry level one such as FX5500 which would set you back for around $60, because as I have mentioned above, it's definitely not a good idea to spend too much on an AGP graphics card at this time.
