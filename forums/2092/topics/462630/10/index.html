In light of these prices, some Singaporeans have been tempted to buy cars from across the Causeway instead. Checks with car dealers in Malaysia found that a brand new 1,600cc Toyota Altis sedan can cost S$70,000 less than in Singapore. The car can be bought at RM$107,121 ($44,633) from a car dealer in Malaysia. In Singapore, it costs $116,688, including COE. A Malaysian car dealer revealed that one to two Singaporeans will show up every month to buy cars. This practice, however, is illegal. Most of the buyers are said to own buinesses in both Singapore and Malaysia. According to Raymond Tang, Honorary Secretary of the Singapore Vehicle Traders Association, most of these businessmen pass the Malaysian-registered cars to their employees and use them to commute between the two countries. A check with the Land Transport Authority confirmed that Singaporeans and Permanent Residents are not allowed to own or use foreign registered cars here.     Pinewood Iskandar Malaysia Studios Pinewood Studios to grow Malaysia's film industry Pinewood Iskandar Malaysia Studios targets to train 900 people by December to supply its studios in Johor. Nusajaya, Johor: Adventures on a grand scale including technically advanced films like 'Life of Pi' will soon be filmed at Pinewood Studios' state-of-the-art facility in Malaysia's southern state of Johor. Pinewood Studios is building a new integrated studio facility in Iskandar Malaysia. Mr Michael Lake, CEO of Pinewood Iskandar Malaysia Studios, said: "This is an initiative of Khazanah Nasional. They wanted to build the film industry in Malaysia. After a lot of studies they realized the best way to do this is was to create a large film studio to anchor the industry in Malaysia, and this is about attracting international productions especially into Iskandar. "They went out and did a deal with Pinewood to license their name and to involve Pinewood in the management of the facility. So it gave us straight away an international recognition for the studios." Since the movie-making business has its ups and downs, to keep overheads in check, the facility will be have about 50 staff - mostly deployed in the television and post-production areas. The 50-acre Pinewood Iskandar Malaysia Studios will have state-of-the-art film stages, TV studios and post-production suites. It will have two 12,000 sq ft television studios. There will also be five film stages. The largest stage, at 30,000 sq ft, will have an indoor water tank for productions involving work on or under water. "That will be the only one in Asia Pacific and that's to film those projects that are set at sea. 'Life of Pi' is a prime example. With this tank we can film that production here in Iskandar." And training is already underway to prepare locals for the creative industry. Mr Lake said: "Training is a very important part of what we are doing. We're building these studios in a green field area where there's been no film industry before. "So one of the key things for us is attracting talent but most important of all, is building a local talent force. So we've done that in conjunction with the Iskandar Regional Development Authority. "We created a training programme. In fact, we've just graduated our first 147 students that we trained in hair and make-up, wardrobe, costume design, production accounting. "And it was a very smart course, because we took people who had already graduated in the basic skills there. We took in carpenters and trained them as set construction people. "We took somebody who'd been training to do hair for a salon and cross skilled them into the film industry. So it was a very easy start for us to build a strong talent force here. "Our estimation is by the end of December we'll have trained 900 people for the film industry to supply our studios." Pinewood Iskandar Malaysia Studios says plans are also underway to provide internship opportunities for local film-major students when University of Southern California (USC) operates an international branch in nearby Edu-city. And with film companies flying into Iskandar for production work, apartment rentals, hotel rooms and a range of other services all stand to benefit. Edward Kollmer, general manager of Thistle Hotel in Johor Bahru, said: "It's had a great impact. Our business is seven days a week. Initially business was always strong on weekends. Some good examples of this would be Legoland opening in September 2012 and it has increased our business at the weekends, in particular. "Also, we've seen developments within the Iskandar project with regards to Educity and we've seen a lot of overseas organizations come to JB...they use our facilities and our hotels to develop their originations. So it's had a fantastic impact. "Our business year-on-year has grown and with the sustainability of the plan has continued to grow moving forward."
