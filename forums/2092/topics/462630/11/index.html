    More SMEs exploring new frontiers 12 Aug 2013 SINGAPORE'S corporate big guns have long had a presence in some of the world's riskier locales but now smaller firms are following them into these new frontiers. Regions like South America, Africa and the Middle East were always considered just too hard, too culturally different, too dodgy, or too dangerous for firms without the resources of a blue chip. But the confines of the local market and the stiffer regional competition have prompted bosses to hit the road less travelled. There are an estimated 55 Singapore firms with a presence in Africa, almost 130 in Latin America, and 300 in the Middle East. These small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) are flying the Singapore flag in these new emerging markets and selling services and products in a range of sectors, from food and consumer electronics to logistics and energy. Mr Cody Lee, director of the Singapore Business Federation's (SBF's) global business division, said the association's foray into such markets started in 2004. The SBF has seen growing interest among its 19,000 members for the eight to 10 trade missions a year that it organises to emerging markets outside of Asia. Nigeria and Tanzania, among the fastest-growing economies in Africa, have attracted the most interest from companies here, added Mr Lee. http://www.iesingapore.gov.sg/Venture-Overseas/Browse-By-Market/Africa-Middle-East/Ghana/News/mc/News/2013/8/More-SMEs-exploring-new-frontiers
