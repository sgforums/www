    Will your loved ones get the critical financial support they will need?

    Death is a sensitive matter, but one which you should come to terms with sooner rather than later. The family you leave behind will need financial support to tide them through the transition period, from basic necessities to the children's education. If you're not financially prepared, your loved ones may find themselves in a vulnerable situation.

    What you leave behind and the means of transfer in the event of untimely death need due consideration. Next Saturday, wealth management consultant Bernard Lum from the Wealth College will tell you why estate planning is important and the factors to consider in passing on financial assets. He will also discuss how Singapore law can affect your estate plan and why wills are important.

    Being prepared can make a meaningful difference to the ones you leave behind. Manogarann Ayakannu from Life Insurance Association will tell you how to ensure your loved ones are able to carry on with life without you. He will discuss the different types of life insurance that can secure the financial needs of your loved ones when you are no longer around. Manogarann will also discuss how yours and your family's needs change through the life stages and what you can do to have the right insurance protection in place.
