    A. TREND

    A trend is a direction used to indicate the pattern of the price.

    There are 3 different types of trends.

    Uptrend : Series of higher highs, higher lows, and higher closes.

    Downtrend : Series of lower highs, lower lows, and lower closes.

    Sideways : Prices trading at broadly similar highs and lows.

    CHANGE OF TREND

    As the price flutuates greatly, a change of trend may occur.

    Uptrend to Downtrend

    (i) First signal is occurrence of a LOWER low.
    (ii) Downtrend confirmed by the occurrence of a LOWER high.

    Downtrend to Uptrend

    (i) First signal is occurrence of a HIGHER high.
    (ii) Uptrend confirmed by the occurrence of a HIGHER low.

    Why do prices RISE in an uptrend?

    o There are two activities in a market at any one time Â– buying and selling. There are two groups of traders Â– buyers and sellers. They oppose each other in the market, and price will move up or down depending on the strength of either buyers or sellers.

    o If there is bullish news in the market, there are more buyers than sellers. The excess buyers then compete for the limited supply of sellers.

    Result : prices are pushed up.

    o As prices rise even higher, those who held the view that the market was going down eventually give up this belief and join the buyers, thus forcing prices up and creating an uptrend.

    Why do prices FALL in a downtrend?
    o If there is bearish news in the market, there are more sellers than buyers. The excess sellers then compete for the limited supply of buyers.

    Result : prices are push down.

    o As prices fall lower, those who held the view that the market was going up eventually give up this belief and join the sellers, thus forcing prices down and creating a downtrend.

    B. RETRACEMENT OR CORRECTION

    A retracement or correction is a temporary price movement (up or down) against the prevailing trend. There are many possible explanations why such an event may occur.

    Why do prices retrace or correct down in the uptrend?

    After prices have risen significantly, those who have bought decide to sell out to take profit.

    At the same time, some of those that sold earlier at lower levels may find it even more attractive to sell again at the higher levels.

    Result : Prices start to fall or correct/retrace.

    Why do prices retrace or correct up in a downtrend?

    After prices have fallen significantly, those who have sold decide to buy back to take profit.

    At the same time, some of those who bought earlier at higher levels may find it even more attractive to buy again at the lower prices.

    Result : prices start to rise or correct/retrace.

    Why do markets CONGEST or go SIDEWAYS?

    o Due to lack of factors to drive a market consistently in one direction, prices drift up and down.

    Result : prices trade in a range.

    C. SUPPORT & RESISTANCE

    SUPPORT

    Price area where a falling market encounters buying which drives prices up.

    Support Level

    There are many methods to forecast the support level in a market. Using price history alone, the support level in the future is the most recent major low price in the chart.

    Break of nearest support

    Once the nearest support is penetrated, the previous lower low becomes the support.

    RESISTANCE

    Price area where a rising market encounters selling which turns prices down.

    Resistance Level

    There are many methods to forecast the resistance level in a market. Using price history alone, the resistance level in the future is the most recent major high price you can find in the chart.

    Break of nearest resistance

    Once the nearest resistance is penetrated, the previous high becomes the next resistance.

    Strength of a Support/Resistance Level

    Strength of a support/resistance level depends on how long it has taken to establish the support/ resistance. The longer it takes to form, the stronger the support/ resistance level.

    REVERSAL OF ROLE OF SUPPORT & RESISTANCE

    Support turn resistance

    o In a downtrend, the recent low point which is a support level, when penetrated, assumes the role of resistance as price retraces UP.

    Resistance turn support

    o In an uptrend, the recent high point which is a resistance level, when penetrated, assumes the role of support as price retraces DOWN.
