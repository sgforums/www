    As any investor would know, it's about risk management and studies.

    1) Don't invest cause you heard someone said it was a good buy. Study thoroughly any one asset before buying the asset. Simply put, even if in the short-term prices fluctuates and price falls, in the long-term prices will recover and even move up, if the fundamentals of the Company remains strong and does not change.

    2) Be wary of market sentiments as it reflect irrational investors' behaviour. Even the best Company's share prices falls because irrational investors starts selling due to the perceived risk in the share market, even though the fundamentals remains. Buy at the right time.

    3) Manage your risk by selling put options or keeping money in Companies with a recession proof business such as consumer staples or gold miners.

    A lot of traders are trading with no money. 

    Basically in Singapore, you have a three-day period before you need to pay the stock. It work like this: 

    Day 1: price is 650 cents, you buy 30 lots  = $19,500

    Day 2: price is 652 cents, you hold

    Day 3: price is 655 cents, you sell 30 lots = $19650.

    The gain is $100 (exclude trading fees)

    You need to pay up on the third day, but you have already sell the stocks, so you gain $100 dollars without having real money to invest.

    The above is a 3-day trade. Some people do a 1-day trade. Others, you can also borrow money from the broker firm to trade,then you need to pay up within 20 days.

    Trading and investments are two different things. The former is a job; the later is a investment.
