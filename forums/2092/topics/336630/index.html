    There are numerous types of protection you might want to subscribe to. Unfortunately most of us have limited spending power and are forced to choose 'the one' that seems the most important. There isn't a most important thing to protect, it really depends on your priorities. Some of them can be :-

    1) Protecting against hefty hospitalisation bills. This can come in the form of hospitalisation insurance that should cover your hospital expenses should you fall ill.

    2) Protection against critical illnesses. These insurance policies are typically tied up to cover death. They pay out a certain lump sum (you decide that lump sum) should misfortune befall you.

    3) Protection against death of a partner. This type of protection is very typical in mortgage insurance, covering the loan payments of the house should something happen to your partner.

    These are the ones that would be more applicable to you. There also insurance policies that include a savings component together with death/critical illness protection. These are known as endownment policies. But i don't think that is what you are looking for.

    Tan Kin Lian's blog offers alot of information on the different types of insurance. Do a google search and you would be able to find his blog.
