  • Pallova2
    2 Nov 09, 12:27

    It is basically a hit and takedown fight. Contenders will gain points by hitting significantly (full contact) at the legal areas or executing takedowns through throws and sweep which resulted to an unresisted fall to the opponent.

    Even though it does not restrict the kind of martial art competing but it is a standing type fight... Any grappling is allowed with the objective of bringing the opponent down...Once there is a takedown (successful or not) the game is reset before it resumes...

    We want to create a platform for all martial art to compete with each other in a game that is very high on safety without compromising the impact and the range of technique from various styles... At the end of the day, it is still just a game nothing more than that...which is accessible by the masses that have conditioned themselves to a certain extent but not all the way into professional fights.

    You may regard this as an entry level kind of tournament...

  • Pallova2
    3 Nov 09, 11:40
    Originally posted by Sgforum King:

    how many points for takedown?

    Takedown is divided into 2 section...

    1st section: Contender manage to bring the opponent down in an immobilized position unable to put up any stance (sitting on the buttocks, fall flat on the ground and all four points i.e. both hands and legs in a prone position on the ground)... 2 points to the contender

    2nd section: After executing the takedown, the contender is still in a commanding stance (i.e. can still follow up with another attack). 1 point will be awarded to the contender.

    Hence a successful takedown will receive the full 3 points...
