    I would like to add... the fact that the general public viewed silat as a mere flashy performance with no real attacks or defense value is due to the fact that most silat master a generation ago made it as such. They preferred the art to evolve into a show piece, even developing music to accompany silat trainings and choreographed all the moves so that it can be appreciated by people during weddings. No surprise that most silat has lost its essence and when silat sparring tournament were started back in the early eighties many do not know how to compete properly.
