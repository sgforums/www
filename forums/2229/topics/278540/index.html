    I am tire of my bunk mates asking me whether tai ji is practical or not so now i am telling the whole world it is!!

    A lot of people view tai ji from outside the open door. The first thing that they observe has always been the slow movement of tai ji. One of my friends once commented " The movement is so slow, I cannot see how u can use a slow movement in a fast fight." Well who told u to intercept a fast fist slowly. There is a saying among practitioners: if u dont move i dont move, but if u move i move faster. The next question they ask is "So why the slowness."

    When u first start to drive do u drive at 80km/hr straight away? Okay being unfamiliar with the art is not the answer. How many of us really knows the full potential of our body or how to use it. Now punch. Which muscle group are u using? Where are u aiming? Where do u place yr hand after that? These are the minor factors that u need to cultivate into behavior. Yes u can practice at high speed but are u able to engage all the respective muscle group. Are u sure yr placement of hands or limbs will be correct? Will u be training correctly.

    Tai ji is known as a soft art, an internal art. There are certain moves that help to invoke qi.

    This is just a noob view of why tai ji is practice slowly. So to the avid practitioner if i am wrong. Sorry
