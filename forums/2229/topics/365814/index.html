  • ASKL!

    Hey. I personally took TKD before and feel that its a very 'weak' martial art. If you seek violent types, take up muaythai. TKD does not cost much though, it teachs you self defence mainly. And every two level you need to change a belt, the belt is $4 if price did not change. The tape inbetween the 2 levels are free. As for shoes, it is optional.

    The uniform may cost up to 100 if you want a good quality one, again if the price did not change. A normal one would be 60 or so. And for the guards, i didn't use them anyway throughout my life in TKD. And there is also the grading fees( for you to go to the next level) They are reasonable prices, but the fee will bite you if you fail your grading as you need to repay to go to another one. And if i'm not wrong grading only happens 4 times a year, so if you fail even one time (or even none) it's very hard to progress up the ranks(unless double promotion[save cost, time]).

    Anyway, if you wanna take up martial arts, you should consider intrest and not the cost, as i know a few places which are willing to teach free. And if your really interested, you should do more research. Cheers!
