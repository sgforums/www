    The Ghosts Must Be Crazy!  - Coming Soon!!! 06 January 2011.

    Genre: Hormedy (Horror + Comedy)


    Starring: Mark Lee, Henry Thia, Dennis Chew, Chua Enlai, John Cheng, Wang Lei, David Bala

    Synopsis:

    “The Ghosts Must Be Crazy” is the new upcoming movie of J Team Productions. There are 2 stories in the movie , ”The Day Off” and “Ghost Bride”.

    Directed by Boris Boo (director of “Phua Chu Kang The Movie”), “The Day Off” is about Ah Nan (John Cheng) and Ah Lei (Wang Lei) going for their annual in-camp training and they meet a very “on” Platoon Commander. They know that they are going to suffer this time round so they decide to cheat for MC. However, the Platoon Commander does not buy their idea and chases them away.

    The Platoon Commander launches a series of rigorous trainings and everyone is complaining. Ah Nan and Ah Lei decide to play a trick on their Platoon Commander as a form of revenge. As they about to start their revenge, a series of strange things happen……..

    The second story of the movie is directed by the first time director, Mark Lee, a comedian and host familiar to most households. “Ghost Bride” is about a man Ah Hui (Henry Thia) who is unlucky in love. He meets a stranger, Ah Hai (Mark Lee), who promises him fortune and blessings if he seeks help from 'brothers and sisters' in the netherworld. Ah Hui does so, and ends up finding a beautiful jade bangle inside a red packet. Soon after, Ah Hui strikes the lottery, but spends it all lavishly. Just when he is down in the dumps again, Ah Hai offers him another solution and something strange happens…

    28 Nov – Apart from leading the cast of Singapore's horror comedy "The Ghosts Must Be Crazy", Singapore Radio Channel Y.E.S. 93.3FM's DJ Dennis Chew will also sing the theme song in Mandarin, loosely translated as 'Meet The Ghosts'.

    Composed by Benny Wong with lyrics written by Jack Neo, the theme song will kick-start the movie together with the opening credits.

    "The Ghosts Must Be Crazy" consists of two stories which are "The Day Off" directed by Boris Boo and "Ghost Bride" by actor-turned-filmmaker Mark Lee.

    "The Day Off" takes place in a military camp where a group of misdemeanor recruits encounters awry experiences during the night of an attempted prank onto their Commander. It stars Chew together with comedian-emcee Chua En Lai as well as 'getai' artistes Wang Lei and John Cheng.

    Meanwhile, "Ghost Bride" explores the Chinese ritual of ghost marriage starring Mark Lee and veteran comedian Henry Thia.

    "The Ghosts Must Be Crazy" is slated to open in Singapore on 6 January 2011 while a Malaysian release is yet to be secured.
