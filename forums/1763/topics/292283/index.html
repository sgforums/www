    For the film buff in all of us, the Asian Festival of 1st Films (AFFF) is not to be missed! This year, out of 500 entries from across 50 countries, 21 films and documentaries have been nominated across 9 award categories. An additional 6 films have been selected in the out-of-competition section. The nominated films will be screened, from 27th Nov till 3rd Dec, at Golden Village Vivo City cinemas, The Arts House and House at Dempsey Hill. The festival culminates in a grand award ceremony graced by international celebrities on 4th Dec 2007.

    The only festival of its kind in the world, the AFFF celebrates the excellence of first-time producers, directors, writers, cinematographers, actors and documentary makers from the Asia Pacific diaspora. Held in conjunction with the Asia Media Festival and Asian Media Market, AFFF boasts of hosting eminent personalities from the global film industry who will be part of the selection committee and jury to select the winning films and documentaries. Past winning films have went on to be screened and won awards at the likes of Cannes, Sundance, Berlin and other noted Film Festivals around the world.

    The festival nominees along with their synopses can be found at http://www.asianfirstfilms.com/nominatedfilms.html.

    The nominated films and their categories are:

    Best Film: Beautiful, Dharm, The Touch of Fate, Weed

    Best Producer: Dharm, The Lucky Miles, Vanaja, Weed

    Best Director: Kabul Express, Vanaja, A Touch of Fate, Weed

    Best ScreenPlay/Script: 5 Moments of Infidelity, Keeping Watch, Owl & the Sparrow, Weed

    Best Cinematographer: Family on the Sky Lake, Keeping Watch, The Last Lumberjacks

    Best Actor (Male): Batzul Khayankhyarvaa (Khadak), Wu Zhong Tian (The Touch of Fate), Mai Zi (Weed)

    Best Actor (Female): Julia Foenander (Beautiful), Haden Kuo (Keeping Watch), Pham Thi Han (Owl & the Sparrow), Mamatha Bhukya (Vanaja)

    Best Documentary: Khee Jin (Feet Unbound), Diane Michael (Lockout), Idit Avrahami (The Boy Who Died of Fear), Yu Guangyi (The Last Lumberjacks), Irena Leica S Cruz (USS Cooper - Return to Ormoc Bay)

    Best Director of Documentary: Hu Linping (Family on the Sky Lake), Sheetal S Agarwal (Gearing Up - The Fire Within), Maya Newell (Richard), Mridul Chowdhary & Sujan Mahmud (The Migrant), Nadine Naous & Lena Rouxel (To each his Palestine)

    The opening film, Khadak screens on the 27th of Nov at Golden Village Vivo City and the schedule for the festival can be found at http://www.asianfirstfilms.com/filmschedule.html. Ticketing information can be found at http://www.asianfirstfilms.com/ticketing.html.
