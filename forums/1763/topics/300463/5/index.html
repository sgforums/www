    From today Straits Times

    Ah Long triumphs despite bad notices

    Jack Neo's latest comedy triumphs over Jay Chou's basketball movie, but Stephen Chow reigns supreme at the box office
    By Boon Chan, MEDIA CORRESPONDENT

    FACED with the Stephen Chow-Jay Chou challenge over the Chinese New Year weekend, Singapore writer-director Jack Neo held his own with his best-ever box-office weekend showing of $1.47 million for Ah Long Pte Ltd.
    Hong Kong funnyman Chow's CJ7 still came out tops with $2 million but Neo beat Kungfu Dunk, which came in third with $1.41 million.

    CJ7, a family drama about a poor boy and an alien pet, the Jay Chou star vehicle-cum-basketball action flick Kungfu Dunk and loanshark comedy Ah Long Pte Ltd all opened last Thursday.

    Ah Long's achievement came despite mixed reviews from the press. Some critics had panned it heavily. The movie has television star Fann Wong playing a loan shark and popular comedian Mark Lee as her effeminate husband.

    Neo said: 'I'm very happy with the results. That's the fun thing about movies. There are lots of surprises.

    'The most important thing is word of mouth. After all, you would trust your friends more than anyone else.'

    What was surprising was the relatively poor showing of Kungfu Dunk. The movie was heavily promoted and Chou toured the region to publicise the movie.

    Despite the cool critical reception, hardcore fans of Neo found plenty to like in Ah Long.

    Housewife Lan Meilan, 68, said: 'I loved the dialect dialogue best. It was well-spoken and very funny. I felt very happy after watching it.'
