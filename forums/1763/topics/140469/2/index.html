  • le cruz
    31 Jul 05, 10:46
    This movie is pretty good, it certainly one of the best wuxia movies released in recent years. The cinematography is top-notch, and the action direction (by Lau Kar Leung who also plays Fu Qingzhu in the film) is just superb. The duels here are gritty, exciting, superbly executed and captured on film, and most of all believable (no flying humans ala Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon). The locations were great, and I loved the way the camera moves to show to show the beautiful landscapes. Other eye candy also comes in the form of Charlie Young, Korean actress Kim So Yeun, and Chinese actress Zhang Jingchu. Overall, this is quite a good-looking movie.

    My gripe with this film is that certain scenes seemed a little rushed, and certain plot points not very well explained. The numerous instances of love-at-first-sight between the characters were also where i felt where the movie began lose it.

    But hey, if I were a damsel in distress, I guess I might also instantly fall for Donnie Yen's heroic Chu Zhaonan. He is just so cool. And his weapon, the You-Long (Dragon) Sword, must be coolest weapon conceived since Bu Jing Yun's 'Jue Shi Hao Jian' in Feng Yun. The other 6 swords are pretty interesting as well. Each has its own strength and history. Check out the website for further details. http://www.sevenswordsthefilm.com/english/sevenswordsthefilm.html

    IMO, the movie is well worth the $9.50 ticket price I paid. So go watch it!
