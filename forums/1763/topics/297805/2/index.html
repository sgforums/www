    I'd give it a 3 out of 5.

    It is quite a nice show, but after watching Iron Man, one can't really help but expect more from this.

    Well, at least, this time, it's not a 'miracle' (Spider-Man saved by Green Globin, Iron Man saved by Pepper Potts) that saved the day, but pure brute force and power from The Incredible Hulk himself. I found the fight scenes with the Abomination a little short, though. But I dig the 'days without incident' timer. Overall, it's a better movie than Ang Lee's version. The story is less convoluted (saving me the hassle of trying to figure out what the movie was about as experienced in Ang Lee's version).

    Throughout the movie, there was a constant mention of 'super soldier serum' and Nick Fury's name (printed on a file) can be spotted near the beginning of the film - definitely clues that there WILL be an Avengers movie.
