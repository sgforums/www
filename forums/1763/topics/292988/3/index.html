    I don't understand the people who say they hated the movie because "it left too many questions unanswered."

    What questions? "Where did the monster come from?" Let's say Abrams did have a scientist in a labcoat pop up irrationally and tell us "Oh, the creature came from Uranus, and it's also invulnerable to our weapons because of a sub-positronic antimatter forcefield as you may have noticed, thanks for watching." How would that improve the viewing experience at all? Certain sci-fi films (Independence Day for example) are built around that kind of silly expositionary technobabble, but Cloverfield is clearly not that kind of film. You may not like the kind of film Cloverfield is--an intimate survival narrative and "road story"--but don't fault it for being what it is, and in my opinion, doing it really well.
