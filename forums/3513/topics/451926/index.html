RS Components Announces New Distributor Agreements ....

    Adds new test and measurement products from Agilent including InfiniiVision 2000 and 3000 X-Series oscilloscopes and handheld digital multimeter

    SINGAPORE, May 3, 2012 /PRNewswire-Asia/ -- RS Components, the trading brand of Electrocomponents plc (LSE:ECM), the world's leading high service distributor of electronics and maintenance products, today announced new distributor agreements with Agilent Technologies (Agilent) in Southeast Asia.  With the new agreements, RS Components will add Agilent's InfiniiVision 2000 and 3000 X-Series oscilloscopes and handheld digital multimeter (DMM) to its extensive portfolio of test and measurement products in Malaysia, Thailand, the Philippines and Singapore.

    RS Components has been appointed as the authorised distributor for Agilent's new InfiniiVision 2000 and 3000 X-Series oscilloscopes in Southeast Asia.  The X-Series oscilloscopes incorporate breakthrough technology that allows advanced capabilities to be included while keeping the product affordable. The oscilloscopes offer mixed signal capability, software applications, and upgradability in line with changing customer requirements.  One of the major advances allows the devices to have the fastest update rates in their class, with up to 1,000,000 waveforms per second.  Another well-received improvement is the ability to upgrade the devices post-sale, enabling engineers to purchase today with the option for added capabilities as their performance needs evolve. 

    RS Components has also been appointed the master distributor for Agilent's high performance handheld DMMs in Singapore and the Philippines.  These handheld DMMs are designed for faster, more accurate measurements to minimise false readings.  Features on some of the models include a built-in torchlight for low-light situations, improved user interface, increased shock protection, brighter and higher contrast OLED (Organic LED) display technology, non-contact voltage detection and visual alerts for noisy environments.  RS Components is also the authorised distributor for the handheld DMMs in Malaysia and Thailand.

    Serene Tan, Head of Product Management, Asia Pacific, RS Components, said: "We are excited to extend our relationship with Agilent in Southeast Asia and add their new range of test and measurement products to our already extensive product offerings. We believe that these new products will meet greater demand from engineers for better and more reliable tools in tough and rugged industrial environments.  Customers in Southeast Asia can now look forward to our latest test and measurement offerings to address their daily work challenges." 

    Lee Choon Seng, Agilent's Regional Channel Manager for EMG Korea-South Asia Pacific said: "Agilent and RS Components share a common goal to accelerate growth in the test and measurement market in the region.  We are excited to offer the products through RS Components' strong presence and wide reach to relevant customers in Southeast Asia.  We are also confident that the high level of service and the trust in the RS Components brand will benefit customers."

    Customers can find out more about InfiniiVision 2000 and 3000 X-series oscilloscopes and the handheld DMMs at www.rs-components.com.

    About RS Components

    RS Components and Allied Electronics are the trading brands of Electrocomponents plc, the world's leading high service distributor of electronics and maintenance products. Through operations in 32 countries and 17 warehouses, the Group serves 1.6 million customers worldwide and distributes 550,000 products ranging from semiconductors and optoelectronics to power tools and protective clothing. The product and service portfolio supports the entire product lifecycle from R&D through pre-production to maintenance and repair. The Group ships more than 46,000 parcels on the same day the orders are received.

    Electrocomponents is listed on the London Stock Exchange and in the last financial year to 31 March 2011 had revenues of GBP1.18bn.
