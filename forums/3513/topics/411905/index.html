PublicAffairsAsia Announces Speakers for 2010 Corporate Affa

  • Helen.zhang

    HONG KONG, Spet. 27 /PRNewswire-Asia/ -- PublicAffairsAsia has announced the speakers and themes for the 2010 Corporate Affairs Forum which is being held at the Marina Mandarin Hotel in Singapore on November 30, 2010.  Full information is available at the forum's website at http://www.corporateaffairsforum.com

    Through this exclusive PublicAffairsAsia event, C level managers and senior PA and communications staff will engage in peer-to-peer learning with speakers and corporate affairs practitioners including Ambassador Frank Lavin, former US ambassador to Singapore, Tim Sutton, chairman of Weber Shandwick Asia Pacific, Steve Okun, AmCham chairman and vice president of public affairs at UPS, Mark Runacres, former British deputy high commissioner to India, and many, many more elite industry and corporate speakers.

    Commenting on the conference, PublicAffairsAsia's Executive Director Craig Hoy said: "Asia's diversity is its strength, although this also raises significant challenges for corporations engaged in the region. As a senior corporate executive operating in this region it is vital that you and your team remain abreast of the strategies for effective engagement with governmental, NGO, regulatory and industry bodies in the region."

    Issues to be addressed at this high level gathering of C Level executives and public and corporate affairs practitioners include:

    • Practical do's and don'ts for localising corporate affairs strategies in Asia Pacific;
    • Understanding the nuances of developing corporate public affairs and government relations programmes;
    • Greater understanding about the development, scope and impact of coalition groups in Asia Pacific;
    • Mid-term analysis on the development of ASEAN+ and its likely impact on the corporate affairs practice in the region;
    • Insight into practical ways to defend and develop corporate reputation across diverse developing markets; and
    • Greater understanding of the impact of corruption and the approaches in place to remove the problem.

    PublicAffairsAsia restricts numbers at its events and conferences to ensure interactivity and exclusivity.  PAA is offering offer partner rates (AmCham, EuroCham, Business Software Alliance, Weber Shandwick and IMA) from just US$1200 and full delegate rates from US$1600 for this full-day, executive level conference. It is also pleased to offer group discounts of up to 15%.

    More information about the conference can be found at http://www.corporateaffairsforum.com/ or by requesting a copy of the brochure from events@publicaffairsasia.com

    Understanding the importance of effective corporate and government affairs can drive your bottom line results and deliver your corporate goals; and we hope you, and your colleagues, will attend this year's CA Forum in Singapore.

    The event is sponsored by Weber Shandwick and Henry Hale Maguire, with AmCham and EuroCham becoming partners for the high-level gathering. PR Newswire Asia becomes the lead promotional partner at this event.
