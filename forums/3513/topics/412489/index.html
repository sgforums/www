    Those are complaints iPhone users report hearing from the people they're speaking to on their Bluetooth headsets. But now, i.Tech, a leading manufacturer of Bluetooth headsets, is resolving the problem of diminished sound quality once and for all with its new Special Edition line of Bluetooth headsets.

    "iPhone users have written on blogs and discussion forums that they cannot be heard clearly by the people they're talking to when on a Bluetooth headset with an iPhone," said Wendy Cho, owner of i.Tech Dynamic Ltd. "i.Tech's iPhone Special Edition headsets raise the bar for conversation audio quality for iPhone users. Now, the talk experience for iPhone users on Bluetooth is nearly equivalent to a live conversation."

    The trio of new i.Tech headsets, engineered especially for iPhone users, dramatically improve the audio quality of conversations over Bluetooth. New iPhone Special Edition models of the i.Tech Clip Naro 601, VoiceClip 604 and Clip II mini 606 include TalkApp software, developed by i.Tech exclusively for i.Tech headsets, that minimizes distortion and ensures voice capture replicates the speaker's original voice level.

    In addition, the Special Edition headsets include a feature called BatteryApp, which adds a battery status indicator to the user's iPhone display to show how much battery life remains on the i.Tech headset. Installation is automatic when the i.Tech headset and iPhone are paired for the first time.

    The iPhone Special Editions are already on the market; MSRPs are the same as that of the standard edition Clip Naro 601, VoiceClip 604 and Clip II mini 606 headsets.

    i.Tech's iPhone Special Edition headsets are compatible with iPhone 4, iPhone 3GS and iPhone 3G. The Clip II mini is compatible with Bluetooth Version 2.0, and the Clip Naro 601 and VoiceClip 604 are compatible with Bluetooth Version 2.1 + EDR Class 2.
