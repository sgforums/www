GuideMeSingapore Releases E-commerce Guide for Entrepreneurs

    Leading Singapore business startup portal www.GuideMeSingapore.com publishes a comprehensive guide on starting an e-commerce venture in Singapore

    SINGAPORE, Sept. 24, 2012 /PRNewswire/ -- Entrepreneurs looking to expand their business through e-commerce can learn the necessary steps and other essential information with GuideMeSingapore's new series of guides on setting up an online business in Singapore.

    The five-part series provides a comprehensive overview of the e-commerce industry in Singapore and its growth opportunities. It also considers the regulatory and competitive landscape in order to guide entrepreneurs on the issues they must consider when they start a new venture in this segment. The series provides step by step instructions on performing appropriate research, creating a business plan, incorporating the e-commerce venture and establishing the operational infrastructure necessary for success.

    In addition, the guides describe the regulations that govern online business activities, as well as a summary of applicable Singapore income tax laws that an e-commerce business would need to comply with. For entrepreneurs looking to acquire some financial help for their online business idea, the series concludes with a guide on how a Singapore company can secure additional funding through loans, investments, and government assistance schemes.

    "Globalization and technology are changing the way we live and do business, and these trends have made e-commerce one of the fastest growing segments worldwide," said Ms. Jacqueline Low, Chief Operating Officer of Janus Corporate Solutions, a leading Singapore company incorporation services firm that owns GuideMeSingapore.com.

    "Many of our clients who establish a presence in Singapore want to use the internet as a sales channel," she explained. "Our guides cover all the important aspects that one needs to know before venturing into e-commerce, especially potentially complicated issues such as taxes and laws. However, one reason that many entrepreneurs choose to set up a company in Singapore is because of the large support system from both the public and private sector, including government-backed financial assistance schemes targeted at start-ups," continued Ms. Low.

    "When you combine the attractive tax system and business infrastructure in Singapore with the flexibility and unlimited reach of e-commerce, the possibilities for growth are endless," she concluded.
