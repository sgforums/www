More Perks for Visitors of Asia's Best Design Fairs in Bangk

  • Helen.zhang
    24 Sep 10, 10:33

     

     

    BANGKOK, Sept. 22 /PRNewswire-Asia/ -- Thailand's invitation to Bangkok International Gift Fair and Bangkok International Houseware Fair (BIG+BIH) October 2010 is not only a great business opportunity and inspiration, but also offers a long list of privileges for international buyers.

    BIG+BIH fairs are widely acknowledged as Asia's best lifestyle design event that found favor with design and quality enthusiasts from all over the world. The lifestyle industry in Thailand is recognized for its creativity, quality, and business integrity. With these combined, the fairs offer a highly unique ambiance.

    BIG+BIH are organized by the Department of Export Promotion, Ministry of Commerce for the 30th time.  It gains immense support from private sector, especially Thai Lifestyle Product Federation. Products to be featured at the fairs are a vast array of gifts, premiums, home decorative, artificial flowers and plants, handicrafts, household items, spa products, home textiles, toys & games, stationery, and other design objects.

    Buyers visiting the fairs can expect to receive a warm welcome and satisfying experience. From the moment they arrive at Suvarnabhumi Bangkok International Airport, they will be greeted and led through the Fast Track immigration line where they will be informed of a list of exclusive benefits, including free shuttle bus service, free use of internet, discounts and privileges at participating restaurants, hotels, package tours, as well as other products and services.  Approximately 100,000 international and local buyers are expected to visit the fairs.  All are recommended to register at www.thaitradefair.com.

    BIG+BIH October 2010 will be held under the theme Soul and Style of the Orient at the Bangkok International Trade and Exhibition Center (BITEC), which is a short driving distance from Suvarnabhumi airport and various hotels.  The doors will be opened for business and trade negotiations during October 19-22 from 10:00 am until 06:00 pm and for general public during October 23-24 from 10:00 am to 09:00 pm (local time).
