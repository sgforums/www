Bankers Launch Innovative Retreat for Burned Out Executives

  • Helen.zhang
    13 Jun 12, 10:56

    SINGAPORE, June 12, 2012 /PRNewswire-Asia/ -- This year, thousands of jaded executives will ponder the 10-million-dollar "What Should I Do With My Life" question against a high-stress backdrop of restructuring, global volatility and diminished bonuses. In response, enter The Legacy Process started by a group of bankers and ex-bankers, which aims to help individuals' transition to a life of meaning by pursuing their passions and living sustainably. 


    (Logo: http://www.prnasia.com/sa/2012/06/12/20120612134021956469-l.jpg ) 
    (Photo: http://www.prnasia.com/sa/2012/06/12/2012061213412551664.html ) 

    "We can choose to do work which nourishes us rather than depleting our spirit. That is what The Legacy Process is about," said Legacy Process Director, Mark Leahy, a veteran debt capital markets banker who also owns an organic micro-vineyard in rural Australia. 

    The Legacy Process provides coaching and runs a week-long residential Legacy Retreat held in the bucolic environs of the Mornington Peninsula, Australia. 

    It uses a unique holistic approach of physical, mental, emotional and spiritual work based on principles of psychotherapy, meditation and financial resource management to help participants get clarity on what they want to do with their lives. This is coupled with practical advice on how to bridge the gap between reality and where they want to be. 

    Guest speakers will share their own experiences of moving on from a purely corporate life to found charities, set up green businesses, explore and adventure. 

    "If you empower yourself to chase your dreams with passion and unleash your real energy, you really start living again, but more meaningfully. Speaking from personal experience, it's an amazing discovery that is also very infectious!" said Legacy Process advisor Noor Quek, founder of NQ International Pte. Ltd. and President of Breast Cancer Foundation Singapore. 

    With a maximum of 15 persons per Legacy Retreat, participants are guaranteed intimacy and personal attention. 

    The price for the week-long retreat starting 27 August 2012 is US$13,680 per head (representing a special 30% launch discount, normal price US$19,800) including all accommodation, food and therapies. 10% of profits go to charity. 

    ABOUT THE LEGACY PROCESS: 

    Legacy Process Pte. Ltd is a Singapore-based company which helps executives who are looking for a more meaningful and sustainable way of living. It offers coaching and residential retreats in Australia run by leading experts in the fields of psychotherapy, meditation and financial resource advisory with an average of over 30 years of experience in their respective fields. Its advisory board includes established global entrepreneurs and banking veterans who bring to the table their real-life experiences of aligning work with their passions. You can find out more about our team at www.legacyprocess.com/about-us.html.
