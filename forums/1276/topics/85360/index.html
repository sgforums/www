    Personally, I don't support the show.

    There are several factors.

    'A' the pull factor:
    They uses artistes to go through dangerous/so-called-dangerous stunts every year. They try to motivate the attraction of the game through the lucky draw systems. More money and manpower could be used to better and if the audience really need waking up calls from the media then it only means something is wrong with the culture in Singapore.

    'B' the push factor:
    They send messages to people who had donated before to request for more donations which I think is an unhealthy habit. If someone has paid before, cancelled the line and another user has actually picked up the same line, wouldn't it be considered harassment?

    'C' the gain factor:
    The management system regulates most of its revenue to helping most of the extreme cases of poverty and kept more than 50% of the money gained in its reserve every year instead of expending more to assist more people. The NKF has more than oftenly oversee the importance of volunteer work and often used paid manpower for majority of its activities.

    'D' the alternative factor:
    NKF's aim every year is only to help people with kidney failures but there are many more people suffering from different problems that needs help. Thus, the NKF show became a yearly project that overshadowed many other charities.
