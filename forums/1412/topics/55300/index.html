    Disney Monopoly Box Game

    Average rating: *****
    ~ Own All the Magic~
    The classic board game gets a Disney version. And they went all out. Instead of the traditional properties, you get to buy Disney movies that range from classics like "Snow White" and "Bambi" to modern films like "Tarzan" and "Toy Story." The railroads have become famous transportation from the films like Aladdin's carpet and Cruela's car (hope you drive better then she does.) The houses and hotels have turned into cottages and castles. Scrooge McDuck is on all the money, and the tokens are detailed sculptures of classic Disney heroes and heroines.
    Of course the rules are the same as always, so if you know the game, you'll know immediately how to play this one. However, it's perfect for the die-hard Disney fan like me. Being able to buy and trade some of my favorite movies just makes this all that much more fun. I was a little surprised at some of the placements for the movies. For example, "101 Dalmatians" is in the Boardwalk spot, a place I figured for "Snow White," "Beauty and the Beast," or "Lion King" (which did get the Park Place spot.)

    If you're looking for a slightly different edition of Monopoly, this is the one to get. It's lots of fun for Disney fans of all ages.

    This classic game of luck, strategy, and determination is easy to grasp for children as young as 6 years old, yet it's fun for adults and older siblings too. This Disney-themed version features such classic Disney characters as Cruella De Vil, Bambi, Dumbo, the Little Mermaid, Snow White, Buzz Lightyear, Peter Pan, and Tarzan. By drawing cards, players move their game pieces around the board, hoping to eventually accumulate all their pieces at the final destination--home sweet home. Sorry is known as the game of "sweet revenge," since players can send each other's pawns back to the starting line, thus forcing one another to lose ground and begin all over again. This kind of frustration may be hard for children under age 8 to handle. In fact, young ones typically crumble into tears of outrage when their pawns are cavalierly sent back. The only recourse is to teach children how to plot their own revenge, which makes them feel as powerful as superheroes.

    DISNEY MUSIC GAME SET





    For true fans of Disney movies, it's the Disney Music Game. Following the Cranium and Trivial Pursuit mode of play, players choose question cards and answer questions about songs from Disney movies. A mouse-eared "Melody Maker" plays Disney songs while players guess who performed them, what movie they're from, and learn other fun facts. A right answer propels a note-shaped plastic token down a happy trail of stills from favorite Disney movies on the slick board. There are 300 playing cards, of which 200 are for grownups. The kids' playing cards have pictures on the back to provide a clue to the correct answer. This game will be frustrating to those families not very familiar with Disney movies; we recommend it for die-hard Disney fans only. Requires three AA batteries (included).
