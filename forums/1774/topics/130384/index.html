    -------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Shinn is a hurt kid, he puts on an outer shell to look tough and strong. He wants to become godly powerful so he can stop his own pain and the pain of others around him. When you sit and watch how he acts he seems like a complete and utter jerk, with no back story you'd probably be right, but with the back story he's the complete and utter reverse.

    In the more recent episodes involving Rey and Stellar you see Shinn's true personality comming through, he's really a sweet guy, he wants to look after those weaker then himself but at the same time knows he's too weak to do it. So he wants to get stronger as mentioned above.

    The only problem with Shinn is when he lets his pain "take him over". He becomes so bitter and hateful that he outright thinks everyone else is scum and below him. Only he knows best and only he can see the "right path". Simply put he was left on his own and doesn't see others understanding him. They miss some "obvious fact" in his opinion.

    A good example of this is with Athrun slapping Shinn. Shinn knew them people would just be exploited again and so went and "solved" the problem. To him "A means is just a way to get to an end". If he has to kill someone to save someone he cares for them he's happy to do it because to him people dying is just how life goes. Athrun has the opposit view to Shinn "Fix the problem without hurting anyone if you can".

    Since all we've seen in SEED is the later view when Shinn came along he clashed with pretty much everyone except Rey. If you watch episode 20 and how Shinn is you can see he suddenly changes as he goes to ZAFT, he put up all his barriers and started to force himself to "be the best".

    Shinn is much like Duo in a way (I figure he's the best example), he's willing to become death it's self if he can use it to save others.
