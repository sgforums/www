    What you have read, of the above, do not teach or imply the divinity of Mary. They are part of what the Church teaches as the Marian dogmas. I know that many of the concepts are difficult to digest and understand, especially when as a Protestant, we have already uphold certain bias and perspectives already. Do keep an open mind in attempting to comprehend them.

    The marian dogmas are briefly:

    1) The perpetual virginity of Mary
    2) The Immaculate Conception
    3) The Assumption
    4) The veneration of Mary as Queen of heaven
    5) Mary as Mother of God (theotokos)

    The Catholic church hold that Mary, even after giving birth to Jesus, remained a virgin till her demise from the earth.

    As a Protestant, our tendency to reject this doctrine holds from a few issues. One is our propensity to uphold the primacy of Christ in our worship, by putting all others to a secondary status. We want to show that Mary, however, blessed and highly favored she is, was simply a human vessel which God used. That's all.

    However, there are a few objections to the Protestant view. Besides, Martin Luther himself, the father of the Reformation, subcribed to the Catholic view.

    1) When the angel Gabriel appeared to Mary, and announced to her that she will bore a son, who will be Savior to her people, note that at that time, she was already bethrothed to Joseph. Note also that Gabriel's first announcement did not include the supernatural mode in which she will conceive. That comes later. Now, it is very natural, when someone comes to you, and tell you that you will have a child (esp. when you are already engaged), that you will assume that the child would arrive via natural means, that you will get married to your fiance, and then have marital relations, in which you will conceive that child.

    But notice what Mary responded? She told the angel Gabriel that how is it possible that she can conceive..since she is a virgin? Of course she was then still a virgin. But she is going to be married to Joseph. Why the discrepancy? Only then did Gabriel told her the supernatural manner in which she will conceive the child.

    Thus the implication from Scripture that there was a vow she had made to God, a vow of virginity before God.

    2) Is it natural to remain a virgin after marriage? Hmm..it is not normal and typical, but nothing disproves the notion that it cannot be done. It is still a normal functioning family, with Joseph leading and taking care of the family. Marriage do not presume conjugal relations. Besides, according to Catholic teaching, Mary is a type of the Ark of the Covenant. Just as the Ark is set apart for God's use (noone can touch it and live), to contain the word of God and the glory of God, Mary is the Ark, used by God to contain the living Word of God made flesh. Thus it is a natural consequence of that sanctification to even set apart her body for holy use.

    3) Another common objection by Protestants would be the use of the word "firstborn". The bible uses the word "firstborn" in describing the child Jesus - Mary's firstborn. Protestants would always assume that the word "firstborn" would presume many more children. But that is an assumption which has no warrant at all. To the Jewish people, one's first child is the firstborn, regardless of whether or not there would be anymore children. The child who opened up the womb would be the Firstborn. Thus the word does not disprove anything.

    4) The bible also used the word "brethren" and "brothers" to describe many of Jesus' acquaintances. Now, the word brother, or brethren, has many meanings in the Bible. Not just the conjugal, per se. Brethren can speak of cousins, relatives, fellow Jew, fellow Christian, etc etc. This still does not disprove conclusively the Catholic view. Note that everytime these alleged brothers of the Lord were named, they were so called in relation to Jesus - "brethren of the Lord". "the brothers of Jesus", etc etc. Nowhere can we find Mary's sons, or Mary's children. In relation to Mary, there is always only one son - Jesus Himself.

    5) Now, let's assume that Jesus had brothers. They would all be younger than He was, right? When the family went to Jerusalem for the Passover feast, as depicted in the Gospel of Luke, only Jesus the boy, was present. He was already 12. Now, in Jewish custom, they do not leave any of their children behind when participating in the Feast. But in Luke's account, it is apparent that only the boy Jesus was present with them. This could mean that if he indeed has a brother, there would be at least a 12 year age gap. It is obvious during that time, Mary has not other children.
    Now, most of Jesus' acquaintances, as well as cousins, such as James and John, Judas (not Iscariot), etc, who were labelled "brethren" in the bible, were of similar age with Jesus. Besides, Jesus died at around 32. He started His ministry at age 30, estimately. This would mean his next younger brother would be 18 (12 years difference). Obviously, many of Jesus' "brethren" were not of such tender age then.

But we can read in Scripture incidents whereby Jesus was receiving advice from his "brethren", which implied they were of older age. This cannot be, if they were biological brothers - as Jesus was the oldest, the firstborn. (I cannot recall the account firsthand. But trust me on this. Check it up. Use a concordance even.)
