    Just thought of sharing a personal testimony with you guys:

    I first got to know this guy A when i was serving in the ministry. He was new to us and looked troubled. So on our way back after cell, asked him to call to chat. And we did. From that night we called perhaps once a week and talked for hours. Then it intensified in a week when we talked every night till 2am or 3am n the morning. when it got to that point, i realised that i had falled for this person. I asked myself why and I knew he was someone i could talk to. He cared much for his family and i know he will care for his own. He's also very understanding, told him some 'dirty linen' about my family but he did not judge me. so one faithful night when we were talking, i decided to ask him how he felt (want to know if this is going anywhere). He asked where i think this is going and i expressed that i had some positive feelings about him. He said that it was just as friends that we've been talking. I was devastated!!

    Even after that night, i could not get over it. Days, weeks and months, i'll lament to my friends how i feel for him and how he did not reciprocrate. Actually, he did not clearly say that he did not like me, more like it's not working that way. So i cling on (heart dun die la).

    But through the months, God was speaking to me about letting go. He used the story of Abraham and Isaac. How the former was going to sacrifice his only son because of his love for God. Thankfully our God is not a tyrant, it was merely a test for Abraham, if he could trust God wholeheartedly and he did! I tried to let go but a few days later, i took it back again.

    God continued to speak to me until one fine day, during worship, i saw a picture of myself hanging on to a twig at the side of a steep cliff. I knew what it was about. I have the impression that God was telling me to let go, of this whole feelings and relationship and submit to Him. But i argued that if i did, what would i be left with? He said to me, just let go and you'll see. Look at what you are holding on to, it's a twig. It will break in time (how can it hold me up). So i did let go and fell into the palm of God's hands.

    Having surrendered, I didn't want to think more about it. I told a close mutual friend that i'll be honest with him that day and close the chap of my life. I told myself even if i did not get married in future, it'll be all right. God's love was so strong in my life.

    without my knowledge, one of my girlfriends checked out the chalet with him. She spent the whole afternoon talking to him.
