    okay. i've just finished reading The Code Book by Simon Singh.. well, have to say it got pretty technical.. all about encryption and how the codebreakers frustrate their efforts... and vice-versa...

    codes from ancient times to now.. to the modern day of how public keys (like the ones in our computer systems) work, and what are the weaknesses.. how codebreakers in the past were linguists and around the Second World War, mathematicians and statisticians were brought in to break the code of the Enigma Machine and the efforts of the British when they built a huge mechanical computer to do it... to the evolution of a programmable computer...

    the race is now on to build the first the quantum computer which will revolutionise and shift the balance of power back to the codebreakers, but will also give the encryptors a powerful tool which may allow them to build the truly unbreakable code....

    quite a good read... but it isn't as good as Simon Singh's former effort, Fermat's Last Theorem or Fermat's Enigma..

    only those with interests in trivia, history and encryption need read, i think it'll bore the heck out of the rest
