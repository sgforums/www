    Hunters of Dune is the 7th in the Dune series (not counting the 6 prequels).



    A book a couple of decades in the writing after the passing of Frank Herbert. He passed on shortly after finishing the 6th book, Chapterhouse: Dune.

    The book picks off from where the 6th ended, a rag-tag lot of people who steals a no-ship and escapes the Bene Gesserit home planet of Chapterhouse just before the forced merger with the hated Honored Matres.

    As the group of people make their escape, battles for the control of the Old Empire rage on while out there, unknown to many or perhaps mentally blocked out by the vicious and cruel Honored Matres, the Enemy awaits.

    Not in the scale of the original author, his son has big shoes to fill and attempts admirably from his father's notes of this book.

    Still, a good read for those who have followed the epic series. 3 1/2 out of 5.

    The next instalment, Sandworms of Dune, will be released in August 2007.

    On a summer night in 1949, seven-year-old Henry Day is kidnapped by changelings and renamed Aniday. For the rest of the story, he lives among them, another unaging child of the forest, at the borderland between the real world and myth.     THE STOLEN CHILD is a tale told by two narrators in interwoven chapters. The first excerpt, from chapter one, is told from the perspective of the changeling Henry, looking back as an adult and remembering the night that he first assumed the child's identity. He begins by making a confession and asserting his guiltlessness over the whole story.

    The second excerpt begins the tale of Aniday, thirty years after that fateful summer, remembering what happened to him on the night he became the stolen child.
