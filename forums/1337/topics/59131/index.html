  • kopiosatu
    15 Oct 03, 12:44
    Originally posted by choco B:
    Erm OT here but... what's the difference between folk and classical guitar, in terms of the guitar itself, music, and styles of playing?

    And which one should a beginner learn?
    well... ppl have this misconception that a classical guitar is the 'lousier' guitar and its suitable for beginners...

    well it IS cheaper... but a good classical guitar can cost up to 5-6K and it offers the sweetest sound and its easier to depress the strings, somehow it feels softer.

    u have classical, flamenco, yadayadayada... those u use the classical/spanish guitar. Classical music u have to pluck the strings, u can't use a spectrum/pick. Its a little more rigid than the other ways of playing, because no matter how much feelings u put into the piece, you're still playing the piece... but a good classical guitarists knows how to control the tempo, the tone and the volume of the notes...

    generally when playing the guitar... there's a whole bunch of styles... i can't remember all of them, especially the electric guitar side... there are different methods and techniques to do scaling. Eric clapton has his own style, Yngwie Malsteen has his own style, so does, Slash, Van Halen and many others... the top guitarists are the best at a particular style / technique

    for folk/acoustic guitar... well u just listen to MTV unplugged. that's about it.
