    Hmm...From what i can tell, it's best to start with a classical guitar...
    Why so u ask?
    So as to train ur accuracy and hand movement...
    No doubt the fretboard of a classical is wider, but in such cases, it provides u with a strong foundation in ur left hand movement. This is consequential because as u progress along in guitar playing, a strong control and accuracy is needed. U may disagree with what i say but my shi fu did improvised this method on me. I'm a teacher myself and i know that this is true cause students of mine had become excellent guitarists...
    What's more, i like that creamy tone of a classical.
    But when push comes to shove,i would rather be handling an electric guitar as many techniques can be put into use.

    P.s The chords F and G aren't hard to hit. U gotta try to stretch even harder,at the same time having no preconcieved notions of how hard the task is and just let the movement develop organically...
