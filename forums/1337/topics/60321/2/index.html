    Dear Diary,

    For a moment, I thought all was gone. I thought I should give up on my 'A' levels. I thought I shouldn't come for anymore papers.

    Today is Day One, the first day of the Big As. This morning, I had a Mathematics paper and an excruciating Economics 3-essay paper in the afternoon.

    The Mathematics paper.. already minused so many marks. I don't know why, suddenly I totally forgot how to do all those. I felt so useless this morning. I should know how to do all those! I'd been doing them for years! It's ridiculous for me not to be able to do the questions. I felt so stupid.

    I really thought there wasn't anymore hope. I'd pinned all my hopes on Math paper 1, since it was all on pure math, while Paper 2, with statistics and all.. I could never excel at that.

    I was feeling pretty hopeless for the afternoon paper too.. There was so much more I should study. But then I remembered, in the morning, a classmate whom I wasn't exactly close to, she came up to me and told me "Don't give up okay? Hang in there."

    There are people who have put their hopes on me.. My seniors, my tutors, my friends..

    So I went ahead after the Math paper... into the library and concentrated on preparations for the next paper.

    I had only prepared half of all that we were supposed to study. I couldn't care anymore. I believed it was better if I continued to focus on what I'd prepared.

    And so, as I sat down in front of the exam paper in the afternoon, I prayed so hard for the questions to be on what I'd studied.

    Thank God. They ALL came out! The 3 chapters I had really really studied for! And so I went ahead, planning my essay before putting it into actual words...

    I didn't manage to finish the paper. But I have really tried my best. Really. Maybe. But I really did all I could do... But I think my essays were badly written.. I lacked practice under such conditions. And I wrote too slowly.

    But all is not lost.. I still have a chance to score at least a C if I do well in my MCQ, data response and case study.

    Tomorrow's GP... I can do it!

    Dear Diary,

    Finally, after 18 years of life on Earth, I can say proudly: I have a dream.

    I used to be terribly fickle-minded about what I wanted to do when I stepped into the working society.

    Doctor, nurse, politician, lawyer, stockbroker...

    They kept changing. Until I began to distrust myself. Whenever I thought of something I would become, the thought that it would soon go away would come to my mind. And it really did.

    When I recently felt that I should pursue a career in music, I was worried it would go away again. But as the days go by, I'm becoming more certain of the path I should take.

    I want to teach. I want to teach the world music. I want to spread music around, because I think it's one of the most beautiful things mankind has. And I believe music is not just for pleasure, but has its other purposes too.

    I've always wanted to learn music, but I regret not insisting on it when I was young. Now I know I must not let the regret continue...

    Hey, just to offer some experience for reference and inspiration

    I'm a full-time vocal instructor now with a establish school in Singapore. I wouldn't say I spent alot of time before earning my music qualifications, most of them are half-past 7 types.

    Neither did I spent alot of time in my studies, but I manage to scratch through all these years from primary to uni.

    You will have to cherish the times of being a student; it;s when you are officially free to do the things you want, provided you give a reasonable academic report. I never regret studying a totally irrelevant course in NUS to what I'm doing now. Many people don't understand; it's during then I dabble alot in music creation, performances, and even performing arts management. One thing in mind, I was determined to work in the music scene and I fully utlised my time to juggle between study and music-related work(ok, I might even say less on studies).

    Yes, you do need a bit of right connections, so start looking now! Be it contemporary or classical, there are people who can give you the head-start.

    Because of my experience in various faculties, including the performing art itself, management, education and administration, I immediately got my first job after exams as operation manager of school. Currently, I've shifted to full-time teaching cum HOD.

    Ok enough of my life-story. I just hope it's inspiring enough for you. You don't need a big miracle. I was an average Singapore student under the stiff primary-->secondary-->JC-->NS-->NUS education system. But you can make it.

    Just remember my signature; FOCUS.
