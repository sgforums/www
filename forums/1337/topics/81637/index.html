    Mass producing copies on CD-Rs is one of the poorest yet most economical way. CD-Rs employ the use of dyes to have their data "burnt" in, thus they have a higher chance of encountering read problems.

    The music CDs that are industry standard are stamped. Only the original CD is laser cut. The metal film encapsulated within the CD is stamped or imprinted with a metal press before the plastic is layered on.
