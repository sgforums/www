    A conductor works with the orchestra. What I'm trying to say is, how a piece of music will turn out actually largely depends on the conductor.

    This is because of the influence of the conductor's interpretation of the piece of music. And to a large extent, the conductor's performance on the podium affects how immersed the players are in the piece of music, and in turn how well they will be able to bring out the essence of a piece of music.

    Conductors aren't there just to keep tempo. Their work is not just what you see on the stage during a performance, but rather also the preparation work done before the performance.

    In a piece of music, there are instances where there are certain tempo changes that an orchestra may not be able to handle without a conductor, such as umm accelerandos and ritards...
