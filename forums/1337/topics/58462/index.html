    Opus.

    From my music dictionary...

    opus, pl. opuses or opera

    It's Latin for "work" and is a term used for a musical work or musical composition and generally used to indicate, by number, the order of a composer's works, beginning with the first (opus 1) and continuing to the last, or to distinguish compositions that have the same title.

    In practice, however, opus numbers frequently refer not to the order of writing but to the order of publication. Also, some composers assign opus numbers only to those compositions they consider important, leaving the less important (in their view) unnumbered.

    A single opus may include one or several compositions; in the latter case the pieces are usually related in some way, such as being published at the same time, or being written as a series. For example, Beethoven's opus 18 consists of six string quartets; they are referred to as Quartet op. 18, no. 1; op. 18, no. 2 etc.

    The word "opus" is often abbreviated op. or Op. and the plural form, opp.

    opus - A creative work, especially a musical composition numbered to designate the order of a composer's works.

    movement - Music. A self-contained section of an extended composition.

    Take for example:

    Beethoven - Piano Sonata No.13 in E-flat Op. 27
    1. Andante etc.
    2. Allegro molto e vivace
    3. Adagio con espressione
    4. Allegro vivace - Presto

    This piece is composed by Beethoven, and his 13th Piano Sonata composed played in the E-flat scale. Op.27 can mean that this compostition is his 27th composition.

    Sonata is a composition for one or more solo instruments, one of which is usually a keyboard instrument, usually consisting of 3 or 4 independent movements varying in key, mood, and tempo.

    As you can see this composition/piano sonata has 4 movements. It is like a book, one of the masterpiece by an author with different chapters. Hence this sonata is like a book with 4 chapters.

    The 1st movement is played Andante, which means that the 1st movement in this sonata is played rather slowly. (Faster than adagio, but slower than allegretto)

    The 2nd movement is played allegro molto vivace, meaning this piece is played fast and very vivaciously. Allegro means fast, faster than allegretto, but slower than presto. molto means very, vivace can mean vivacious...

    Cheers.
