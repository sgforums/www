  • yjkeh
    22 Feb 12, 10:11
    1) Can't tell you much about chances. This is pretty much manpower dept considerations that not much people are privy to. 2) The training is residential. You will be doing PT in the morning, then classroom lessons in law and police procedures in the afternoon and PT/ Police Self Defensive Tactics Lessons in the evening again. It's free and easy after 6pm as the trainers work on a 9 to 5 basis. Book in on Sunday night and Book out on Friday evening. Won't kenna tekan much. It's nothing like the army culture since police officer work in a more civil environment and therefore all the trainers treat the senior police officers with respect. No cutting botak. Overseas training will be a trip to Nepal for about 2 weeks. Climb Mountain. Before that, there should be a trip to OBS. 3) Smartphone is allowed during training phase. HTA allows camera phone and everything. No such restrictions when you pass out as well, otherwise you can imagine the trouble at police stations when members of public carry such phones into the station.
