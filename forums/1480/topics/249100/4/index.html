    I agree with pitot. Whether or not they carry their service revolver depends on their department within the CNB, but if you're a frontliner most definitely you will carry your revolver with you almost all of the time.

    Their training is actually with police officer/senior police officer trainees, ie either 6 months [cpl/sgt] or 9 months [inspector/asp]. Learn the same thing, with a bit extra thrown in.

    Legal powers is different also. Police are empowered to enforce all laws under the sun. CNB's powers is more to misuse of drugs act-related offences. They have a lot of powers relating to the said act. In fact, more than the police.

    But of course, if a robbery has taken place, they can detain the perpetrator. But the arresting party and subsequent in-charge case will be the Police.

    And yeap, their working hours are irregular, cos you'll never know when they will be conducting operations. They are a very, very, very secretive bunch. But for a good reason lah.

    For your last comment, perhaps they are similar to the CID in terms of:

    1) no need for uniforms
    2) can carry service revolver back [depends on department, though]
    3) irregular hours, but only when there is impending major operation.
