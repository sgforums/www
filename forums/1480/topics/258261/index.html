    3 Jun 07, 02:00
    They consist a very wide spectrum of duties.

    Roughly similiar set up, with similiar rules in the various divisions. Lots of them juggle multiple roles with regards to VSC works at different stages of time and periods of their lives.

    So it can be kinda difficult to tell what is the general information you really need to know.

    There is admin, patrols, plain clothes, standby drivers, 2 wheelers, warrant teams, dispatch drivers, NPCOs, Charge Rooms & Organising Committees.

    So it quite a mix sometimes.

    There are some who are also regular volunteers with the temples, RC, Civil Defence, children homes & shelters.

    There are teachers from schools, prison services, dentist, taxi drivers, lecturers and some more...

    The recent more prominent example is a auditor, currently attached to CAD.

    When we say attached, it means that the auditor belongs to a division, but sort of also part time with the CAD.

    They go rank and file. Except for some cases. Time and again, VSCs does not follow the ranking processes. They keep changing it and have their own rules.

    I have nothing good to say about it. So dont press me for details.

    Generally, most VSCs recruits, after completing their training, are awarded Cpl (V) rank.

    There are some like ex-regulars, PNS or ex VSCs are awarded their last rank in service when they joined VSC.

    There was previous other ranking procedures that some recruits, after a years service, and their qualifications has degrees, are promoted to Insp(V).

    New ranking procedures now in place, that those who 'displayed' exceptional leadership qualities *snort* are put up for acting Inspector rank and promoted officially to Inspector(V) rank after a year service as acting inspector.

    Soooo....you may be a corporal of a year, a 14 year serving staff sergeant, with some proven track record and good educational qualifications....and you can be put up for acting inspector rank for a year and get your inspector rank.

    Or....you can be contented to be a corporal for 14 years and do whatever you truly like to do, instead of hankering after the rank.

    PS. They have more reasons not to put u up for a promotion then to actualy put you up for one. Easily from: Your hours not enough for one particular year in the past 7 years, to no IPPT due to injuries so you cannot be promoted or you didnt do well in your shooting. Or the best ones......you didnt do your VSC job well enough...(make a guess how you can do your job well, but because you dont angkat your senior officers or make a show of how 'fantastic you did your job'.
