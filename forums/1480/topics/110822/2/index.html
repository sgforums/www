    Arming of British police hasnt really changed since 9/11.

    They have differing armouries depending on region or constabulary.

    While the standard street policeman/woman will not be armed, there are a fair amount of Firearms Officers.

    The specialist Firearms Officers routinely train with the SAS, and even within the London region there will be weapon variations between local units [ie. Thames / River police and Met / SO19 / Anti-terrorist branch].
    Within the Met, so19 train D/RPG's, AS, SB, and others [bodyguards, air marshals].

    Weapons spotted have been the standard MP5 variants [LE variant with SA only and 10 rnd mags, as well as standard models], Glock's [usually 17], Browning [phased out], Sig's, AUG LE variants and G36's [both carbine and rifle variants]. MP5K is also used.
    Marksmen have been seen with the AI [incidently recently saved from receivership] L96's.
    The Smith & Wesson .38 Model 10 revolver is still issued [usually only Special Branch].
    [LE variants are all usually semi-auto only, with low capacity magazines]

    I say they havent changed much since 9/11 mainly because people forget that the British Isles has lived through terrorism before, for 30-40 years during the Irish troubles [which are not yet over] - a fact that seems to have been forgotten by alot of people [perhaps most of all by Americans..] - but has armed the British services with probably more active experience than anyone else in the world [indeed they are often asked overseas to lend their experience to others combatting terrorism].

    As for actual shooting, police tend to be trained for centre of body mass shots and not using anything that would likely go beyond the targets body..
    [depending on situation obviously]

    [the police actually also offer training to certain sectors of the public, but its pretty expensive] - and the preferred weapons are the mp5 and the glock.
    Taser's are now also employed.

    While not strictly police, the MOD police have been issued the H&K MP7.
