    Hey mineohmine, I'm not a female but I can tell you how the rest of my female squad mates are coping. Physically wise, if you can run 5km in 32 mins, you are definitely fine. Running during PT is always pegged to the slowest runner unless its IPPT or intervals where you do your best. There's also time to do your own training at night. Being on the heavier side is ok as long as u are not obese and can pass your IPPT. And the training is not as tough as in the army. Its more mental than physical cos you have to stay awake for long hours and attend classroom lessons on top on your PT. During your 9 months here, you will cover the basic competencies of being a police officer, that is how to shoot, your defensive tactics, lifesaving, police officer basic course and neighbourhood police ground response force course. Then you will also cover law lessons to prep you for your first posting as IO. Then at the end, you will undergo leadership training, 3 weeks each locally and in Nepal. Then you pass out about 2-3 weeks after that.
