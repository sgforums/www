    Hi Ashieee, from what I have been gathering, the procedures are like this 1) Selection Assessment which includes: i) Psychometric Test ii) Physical Assessment iii) Verification of Documents 2) Dialogue Session with the Assistant Director (Manpower) 3) A Psychologist Interview 4) A Panel Interview 5) Medical Checkup 6) Offer Letter I do not know much about whether grades matter but I would think it is more for your pay calculations and determination of your entry rank. I think they depend heavily on the selection assessment and also the activities that you have been heavily involved in to determine your suitability.

  • yjkeh
    29 Feb 12, 05:26
    hi samsam22, I'm currently studying overseas. So I have to travel back and fro during my holidays for the interviews which explains why I had my dialogue session before the selection assessment. I'm graduating this coming July though. Anyway regarding the dialogue session, it's basically a 1 to 1 session with a manpower officer. Before it, you will get a briefing about the job scope and what to expect. During the interview, the officer will ask you questions about your motivations to join, what and why are you studying that particular course in uni etc etc. It's probably to gauge your interest in the job and let you ask more questions about the job.
