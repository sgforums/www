    1) As a Junior Officer is there a prospect in SPF? i mean can i go far?
    Yes, if your CO is a good one but most likely is a No cos u will end up stuck in NPC for 3-5 yrs unless u know people who can rope u over. The longer u stuck in NPC = more likely to be posted out but also = more likely people dun want u cos u only trained in NPC duties and nothing much. Want to have prospect must quickly go officehours or get transferred to be IO...then u can jump and jump almost anywhere....

    Rule of thumb :
    1) 2 yrs in NPC then quickly request to go be IO or go officehours. 1 yr then quickly source for the next job....if u wait for MPO to do his/her job...u can wait until u get ur next rank also still there....

    2) Do ur best but dun try too hard, else u will fall down hard...have seen many such cases...they are indeed good but step on too many fellow officers' foot, therefore ganna condemned and the rest is for u to find out.

    2) Can a JO become a SO ?
    Previously Yes. Now is NO...strictly NO...unless u got super ultra good supporter like a strong Commander or DY Commd or someone who in Cantoment. Max rank is SSI2 which will take u 15-18 yrs at least unless u high flyer then perhaps just 12 yrs can get there. Those who can do so and can afford to do so, resign after they get their SSgt...which is say....abt 5 yrs. From my circle of friends, Sgt->SSgt on the average is 4yrs but Cpl to Sgt is very fast cos there are things u can't do as a Cpl but Sgt can, wat i won't reveal.

    3) I heard tat SPF sponsors officers to study degree in Justice Studies. I am from a poor background so i dun have the means to go to UNI although i got the grades. Will a normal diploma holder be eligible?
    AFAIK, they sponsor u go temasek poly study some 'elligible for use in SPF but worthless outside DIPLOMA'.
    No, they do NOT support Diploma Holders to go Uni but they do support 'A' Level Holders.

    I am an ex-police officer.
    With regards to ur question:
    1) Yes, there is a prospect in SPF as a JO, but u must be prepared to work in investigation and get a degree to be promoted to SO. If not, you will lose out to the degree holders JOs.
    3) U can try apply after working a few yrs, think they will take into account the number of years of service in the force. Many sssgt/ssgt are taking the degree/diploma in justice studies.

    My advice to u: if u wanna be a PO, stick to being a PO. Narcotics' officers job is very different from PO. To maximise your prospect in SPF, i would advise u to get a degree while studying part-time and then work in investigation. Once u shine in investigation and armed with a degree, prospects in SPF is good.
