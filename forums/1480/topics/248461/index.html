    SINGAPORE: Two Police Coast Guard officers were killed in the line of duty on Friday night when their boat collided with a speed boat carrying illegal immigrants.
    Police said two Coast Guard boats, each manned by four officers, were performing ambush duties off Tuas.

    At about 9.30pm, a speed boat with six persons on board intruded into Singapore waters and the two police boats gave chase.

    In the course of the pursuit, one of the boats collided with the vessel and both capsized.

    The other police boat immediately called for assistance and carried out a search and rescue operation.

    Two officers from the capsized boat were rescued, with minor injuries.

    But the other two officers, Station Inspector Mohd Khalid Muhamad and Staff Sergeant Heah Khim Han were missing.

    An intensive search was carried out and their bodies were found at 8.15am on Saturday morning.

    One of the illegal immigrants also died in the incident, while three others were rescued.

    41-year-old Station Inspector Mohd Khalid Muhamad was buried at the Choa Chu Kang Muslim cemetery on Saturday afternoon.

    He had joined the Singapore Police Force in 1986.

    29-year-old Staff Sergeant Heah was with the Police Force since 2001.

    - CNA/so

    Two Police Coast Guard (PCG) officers had been killed and an unknown number of illegal immigrants gone missing after the boats which the PCG officers and the illegal immigrants were in respectively collided at the sea off Tuas last night. The two PCG officers killed in the line of duty are Station Inspector Mohd Khalid Bin Muhamad and Staff Sergeant Heah Khim Han. Their bodies were retrieved this morning at about 8.15am after an intensive search and rescue operation involving the PCG, naval divers, Maritime and Port Authority of Singapore, as well as the Royal Malaysia Police (Marine Police) throughout the night. Two other fellow crew mates on board the same PCG boat had been rescued. Three illegal immigrants had also been rescued, together with the recovery of a body of a fourth illegal immigrant.

    Two PCG boats, manned by four officers each, were performing ambush duties in Singapore Territorial Waters (STW) off Tuas on 13 Apr 07. At about 9.30 pm, a speed boat with about six persons on board illegally intruded into STW. The two PCG boats gave chase. In the course of the pursuit, there was a collision between one of the PCG boats and the intruding vessel. As a result, one of the PCG boats as well as the speed boat capsized. The remaining PCG boat immediately summoned for assistance and proceeded to conduct search and rescue.

    Two of the PCG officers from the capsized PCG boat were rescued from the sea. They suffered minor injuries. The remaining two officers went missing. Their bodies were recovered this morning at about 8.15am. Three persons on the intruding vessel had also been rescued and the body of one of them was recovered.

    The two PCG officers killed in the incident are Station Inspector (SI) Mohd Khalid Bin Muhamad and Staff Sergeant Heah Khim Han. SI Mohd Khalid Bin Muhamad joined the SPF in 1986 and S/Sgt Heah Khim Han joined the SPF in 2001.

    Investigations are in progress and the search operation for the remaining missing persons is still ongoing.

    The Commissioner of Police, Mr Khoo Boon Hui, is saddened by the death of the two officers, "I am deeply saddened by the loss of our two dedicated officers who had sacrificed their lives for the security of Singapore. My officers and I send our condolences to the families of our two heroes and we will do everything possible to assist them to overcome these very traumatic and difficult times."
