    Hi xuande, 

    I hope I can address you as an applicant still waiting which I share an opposite stand as compared to you. I went thru all stages already not knowing the outcome and still stuck in a decision whether to take up the offer if everything turns successful. Few years being an engineer has bore me down both public and Pte sector (public is paying well, Pte has good exposure) deep down I want to be extraordinary and being police I feel I can do that, but I know it's not an easy job either....I like the police work but i have some uncertainty regarding the work life balance and spending time at home with family. I will not sugar the scope of a pass out SPO---the first year or so will be tough, adjusting to shift work and learning a whole new skill set. As an IO, if you are investigating a case and your subject gets arrested on the weekend, you may have to go back on the weekend to record his statement and settle his bail. As a Team Leader, you may get recalled to lead security operations during public holidays such as National Day, Christmas, even Chinese New Year, on top of your normal duties. While there are a lot of leave, missing out on a special public holiday with a loved one is sometimes not really replaceable even with 3 or 4 days of leave.
     
    But this is the sacrifice all police officers understand and are willing to make. The dynamic and uncertain nature of this job is what makes it exciting. The impact police make on our fellow citizens’ safety and security is what makes it meaningful and worthwhile. We see a side of Singapore that only police officers know of, a side that we work day and night to protect our countrymen from.

    The wait for the result is tormenting and if you want to play bastard or If I were you, I will take up the engineer job while entering to wait for the process of police since it is so long anyway.. Too many factors to consider anyway, if u r working then dont have to quit and continue waiting.. If u are not, go for the job first and wait for police..

    and if you are confident speaker, show leadership, calm speaker you can pass the interview at ease..

    Till now I can't decide either...

    Well, during my SA I took a peek and saw the number of names on the list, and based off information from this forum (6-10trainees per intake, roughly 9 intake per year, and one SA every 2weeks-1month), the numbers are quite depressing in terms of success rate (i.e. 95% will not be selected for various reasons). Of course, this number is highly inaccurate, because it does not include people who decided to withdraw, higher number of intakes, different number of trainees per intake, different number of applications at different times of the year, and various other reasons. Ultimately, I think the important thing is to know that they will be assessing you as an individual; that is your suitability and fit to being a police officer; and not in comparison to other applicants. Based on this fact itself, I would not trust that estimation that I've made. So like what many others have suggested thus far, just do your best :)
