    Hi guys, it has been a while since I've been here. I've just passed out from HTA sometime back. Anyway, I was in the same predicament as you all a year ago, checking the forum and career@gov everyday. Haha. If this is of any help, everyone who applied to be a SPO have to go through the same wait (except the scholars I guess). The same applies to me. No doubt the wait can be agonizing but hang in there but I think MPD (manpower department) is doing their best to choose the best officers around to join the force.

    Anyway to help those who are still not sure about the processes:
    1. Assessment - the advice will be to get enough rest the day before because the test can be quite draggy. It will last about the whole day. (Pass/ Fail stage)

    2. AD dialogue- the dialogue is a 2- man panel asking you questions pertaining to why you join SPF. It will be good if you know how SPF works because it shows that you have passion about it. It will last about 10-15 min. (Pass/Fail Stage)

    3.Psychology Assessment - A psychologist from PPSD (Police Psychologist Service Department) will talk to you and also clarify certain questions you answered during the first stage. And probably some of your background. It will last about 1 hour. (Non fail stage)

    4.Board Interview - It will be a panel of 7 officers including 3 Cmdrs/Directors from any division/department. It is quite similar to AD dialogue but some of the questions can get quite sticky. Also, they might ask some questions which are awkward for you. I guess all they want is to test your reaction. It will last around 30-40 min. (Pass/Fail stage)

    5.Medical- It will be at Raffles Medical and they will take your ht and wt and also blood pressure, and BLOOD (ok, I dont like needles, so i hated it) . They will also have a checklist to check on ur posture etc. Like those health check up in primary school. I went on a non peak period, so it lasted around 1hr. (not sure if it is pass/fail stage, but heard rumours about people failing it)

    My whole process lasted around 6 months after I graduated.

    Bond signing
    Vital will contact you upon your clearing of medical to sign bond. You needed 2 sureties. If I did not remember wrongly, parents can be your sureties as long as they earn about a certain sum a month. (Have to check with vital). 

    After which they will tell you your reporting date, which squad you will be in and who is your course manager. They will give you your course manager number as well. Your course manager will also contact you. 

    Pre-training 
    You will be call to go for an orientation and tour. They will also settle some adminstrative stuffs. 

    Things to bring for training 
    - A few white shirts and black pants (in case they cant issue you uniforms due to shortages)
    - White socks 
    - Polishing cloths
    - Hangers
    - Clothes peg
    - 3M stick on hanger ( in case your bunk doesnt have any) 
    - Shampoo/Shower gel
    - Extention wire ( up to you ) 
    - Electric water kettle (if you dont take tap water. water cooler can be quite far) 
    - Smart phone is allowed

    The rest will be issued in due time. ( within the week you enlist.) 

    Training
    If you can get through till this stage, you should be able to get through training. Training is fun and enjoy the fullest. Trust me. 

    Anything can PM me. Hahah, I'll try my best to help and check this site frequently.
