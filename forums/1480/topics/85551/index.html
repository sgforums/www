    Before you decide to enagage a lawyer and incur hefty legal fees, should consider the following :

    Availability of documentary proof that you have intention to buy the handphones from him that he has intention to sell them to you. i.e. any written or implied agreements. Agreements involved an offer made by one party and acceptance by the other and with consideration (involving benefit & detriment)

    For a breach of agreement case to occur, the same must occur of either express and/or implied terms, or terms imposed by statute such as in your case the the Sales of Goods Act.

    If the deal that you were going to make does not fall expressly or implicitly into the scope of the Sales of Goods Act, it is difiicult to take legal action based on 'Breach of Agreement' since you can't establish whther there was any agreement in the 1st place.

    1. Is there a written agreement?
    YES = Breach of Trust aka C.B.T.
    NO = Cheating.

    2. Whichever the ACT is, make sure you have the other party's particulars,
    date of transactions, stipulated cash amount & stipulated number of goods
    units before stepping into the police station. Otherwise, they'll tell you go
    find out the details first.

    3. Alternatively, you can seek a lawyer and head to Small Claims Tribunal and
    fight it out in there. Provided you can get the other party to go along.

    4. Irrationally, you may also get a few of us friends and beat the living
    daylights out of his brains. (NOT ADVISABLE)
