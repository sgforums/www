    Originally posted by LazerLordz:
    Guards trained PO.Lol.Anyway OT aside, what rank does a SPO hold?Inspector?
    Hi...

    SPO are officers with the ranks of Inspector and above.

    The entry rank will be dependent on a few factors such as the degree that one has. Holders of a 2Upp Hons degree and above from a recognised univeristy will be Assistant Superintendent (ASP) while those with other degrees will be Inspector (INSP).

    Interesting thing to note is that while in the SAF and SCDF, other ranks will only salute ranks of commissioned officers for SAF ( 2LT and above )and senior officers of ranks LTA and above for SCDF, in the police force, a Station Inspector ( SI) is warranted a salute from the junior ranks. This SI rank is something like a warrant officer.

    Originally posted by cornyfish2000:

    Another interesting thing is that while SAF Officer Cadets , regardless of service status, i.e. NSF or regular will have to put on the white bars during trainee days and most probably go through the same syllabus, it is quite different for SPF Senior Officer Trainees.

    Regular Senior Officer Trainees do not put on white bars during their trainee days. They have a black shoulder epaulette with silver strip.

    OCTs are mostly cross-over from SAF BMTC although in recent batches there are some Police NSF in the OCT course. They wear the white strips as mentioned and are known as OCTs. They have a different course as compared to the regular senior officers trainees.
