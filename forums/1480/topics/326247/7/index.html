    Dude,

    The VSC is an organisation that will understand your 'real life' concerns. It remains a volunteer organisation even though it is uniformed. This means the VSC will understand work and family have to come first. The same in training. However, you can imagine there are certain elements you MUST attend. Not passing IPPT will mean you are not allowed to join any division and stay in training until you pass. For shooting for instance, obviously you must attend and pass before they will give you a gun.  Simple right? So if you miss it, you must come back at a later date. For other classes which are less critical, if you miss it, there will not be any catchup, you just have to study the notes from your classmates, so you will still pass the exam. Please note however that each course has a minimum required attendance %, which is reasonable but it means you have to be sensible too about how many classes you can miss.

    The key for me was to study the schedule, make sure you don't miss exam dates (miss those and that is a real pain) and book work trips when there are no exams or key classes to attend.
