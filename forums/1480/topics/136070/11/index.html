    Originally posted by Fistandantulus:
    How does the attachment to a patrol unit work? Does the VSC stay with the team for a long period of time or does he/she gets to attached to the "current working unit" when the VSC reports in for work?
    The practise differs from divisions to divisions.

    You are assigned to a VSC team that is rightfully suppose to follow the schedule of the corresponding regular team. Be it for a full shift or 1/2 a shift.

    But as Volunteers has their own work schdule and commitment. It is not possible to totally follow your assigned team schedule thus your 2nd description will apply.

    There is not to say that VSC did not delibrately allow it to be so.

    Lets say, you are a Friday team member, thus in Team 5. You got quite a lot of buddies (regulars/ns) which is Regular Team 2 working on Sat night.

    You want to join them. You can. Just have to inform the liasion officer/MPO that you are coming to be reporting for VSC duty with Regular Team 2 for night shift on that Sat night. Try to minimise incoveniences by informing your LO/MPO earlier cos they need to make arrangement for your logistics.

    I also forgot to add.

    Ex-Regulars/PNS are allowed to join the VSC and start serving the VSC with their last active rank.

    Eg, you are a Regular Staff Sergeant. You resigned from the Regular service and join the VSC.

    The MPO processing your application will then also apply for you to start your VSC service with your last rank. So you can become SSGT(V) instead.

    Or

    You are a National Service Police Inspector now. When you joint the VSC, you can join the VSC and start off as Inspector(V).
