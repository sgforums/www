    if u get posted to be a NPCO.(which is most likely) u will be working shift. it is unlikely for ur first post to be a admin staff unless u wish to sign on as a CSO(Coporate Support Officer). for sergeant without NS, ur pay wun even reach 2k, for sergeant wif NS liability completed, ur pay will be slightly above 2k.

    Police is a force for the nation. its not just a job. its a responsibility that u will take up in protecting urself, ur family, ur friends, and ur nation. think twice before taking up this career and do not regret it halfway down the road.

    if u feel Police is not the right career for u. u may consider SCDF, SPS, CNB, ICA, ISD or other government agencies job if u are eager to work in a government agency.

    your pay will be subjected to an increment every year, before it hits the ceiling [wage limit for that particular rank]. sometimes, if you stay stuck at a certain rank for too long, eventually the ceiling will be raised a bit higher. so you might end up being a staff sergeant, but with senior staff sergeant pay. something like that.

    and if you go to specialist vocations, you'll be entitled to skill allowances as well...

    though they all receive more money for doing less. Just ask any NPCO around.

    Originally posted by MohamedF:

    So, main question is, how's the job...? Testimonials from JOs...
    mate, if you get to convert and become a JO, you'll be almost definitely posted out to a land divison, doing frontline work. it is ANYTHING but desk bound.

    sure, sometimes you have to man npp/npc counters and lodge reports, but more often than not you'll be out on the streets lah.

    the job's okay; it's just that you have to deal with loads of pressure: from our public, as well as from within.

    but all in all, you'll definitely find the job to be an eye-opener. you'll get to see things that most people wouldn't see in their entire lifetimes.

    to quote you, and speak in similar terms, if life were a video game, after joining the spf, your experience points will rocket sky high, and you'll be experiencing level upgrades on an almost regular basis.

    you might want to hold your horses though. maybe, if you could, try to get into npco course after your 3 months' training, after which you'll be out on the streets. experience the life for yourself, then make your decision. no worries mate, there's no rush at all.

    or, after collating enough information and you feel like this is the job for you, go on and apply.

    here's wishing you all the best, and you can always give me a pm if you need to know anything.
