    SINGAPOREANS GETTING FATTER
    MORE Singaporeans are overweight, and affluence and lifestyle changes are to blame.

    A survey by the Ministry of Health in 1992 found that 21 per cent of the population were overweight and 5 per cent obese. Another survey in 1998 found that 24.4 per cent were overweight and 6 per cent obese.

    Last year, however, the World Health Organisation proposed stricter criteria to measure obesity in Asians. This was because an Asian who has the same Body Mass Index (BMI) as a Caucasian has more body fat, says Dr Tey Beng Hea, who heads the Weight Management Programme at Alexandra Hospital.

    With the new criteria, the 24.4 per cent classified as overweight in 1998 were reclassified as obese, which makes the total figure for obese Singaporeans 30.4 per cent. Dr Tey says more Singaporeans are overweight because their lifestyles have become more sedentary. 'People have also gone from low-fat, low-sugar diets to high-fat, high-sugar ones,' he adds.

    The BMI is used to determine obesity. To get your BMI, divide your weight in kilogrames by the square of your height in metres. An Asian male is overweight if he hits 23 on the BMI and obese if he hits 25.

    You can use the waist-hip ratio to check if you have too much abdominal fat. Divide your waist measurement by your hip measurement to get the ratio.

    Men who have a ratio of more than 1 and women who have a ratio of more than 0.85 might be more prone to Type II diabetes and hypertension.

    Some common clubbing myths:

    Myth #1
    "Dancing will help me burn the calories off."
    You can shimmy and shake all you want, but dancing once or twice a week even for an hour only burns off about 250 calories. Barely enough to work off those strawberry daiquiri calories.

    Myth #2
    "Fingerfoods don't count."
    Think again. You may just be picking on small quantities of food, but bar nuts, a few chicken wings, and some butter-covered popcorn contain about 275 calories per serving.

    Myth #3
    "Supper helps to soak up the alcohol."
    Who can resist tucking into some char kway teow, or wanton noodles at the end of a night of clubbing. But by 4am in the morning, your body's metabolism slows down and once you get to bed, no way is your body burning all those calories that you just consumed.

    Instead of regular beer (which contains about 171 calories per mug), drink Lite beer which contains about 100 calories per mug.
    Order a wine spritzer which only has 41 calories as opposed to red wine, which has 120 calories.
    A mixed drink with sprite or coke contains between 140 to 150 calories per can, while a club soda or seltzer contains hardly any calories.
