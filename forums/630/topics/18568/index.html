    I'm not sure about you people, but lately I have been quite confused about this friends concept.

    Some time ago I read in a magazine this article by a sociologist about different types of friends: acquaintence, platonic, close, best.

    No matter what your own definitions about these different groups of friends are, have you wondered how people progress through the different stages, if at all? Or how about regressing in stages e.g. people drifting apart, former close friends becoming platonic friends instead.

    I thought I was a nice enough person. But sometimes I find that when some close friends click with others more, and they become closer while I stay put. In other words, I miss out on the bonding (for want of a better word.) In the past, this might be because I have not done my part as a friend, but I really don't think this is an issue now. Physical proximity has to do with it I suppose, just like being posted to different camps in the army.

    I'm not sure I'm complaining. I guess I'm just a bit disappointed that this has to happen. I can't label it as being betrayed, because is certainly is not, but when this sort of things happen, it just goes to remind myself how badly I am at assessing at how close I am to people, or that I'm expecting too much from what there never was. At its worse, it can take away the motivation to open your heart to new people.

    On the other hand, I think you should only spread your trust and sincerity that thin. In other words, quality and not quantity.

    Confusing.
