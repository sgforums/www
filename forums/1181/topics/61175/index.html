LAN EVO - Parts and tunes

    Saw a few posts on queries regarding this topic. Thought i compile and put one together. This is a compilation and not exactly from personal experience. So you may need to take it with a pinch of salt and trial and error for yourself to see what suits you.

    Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution III GSR (CE9A)
    Specifications
    engine: 1997cc 4-cyl in-line DOHC 16-valve intercooler turbo (4G63)
    transmission: 5-speed manual
    drivetrain: full-time 4WD
    max power: 270 bhp at 6000 RPM
    max torque: 228 ft lbs at 3000 rpm
    weight: 1260 kg (or 2778 lbs)

    Summary of tunes
    N tune - like A tune only do not have the sticker

    A tune - the fastest, but can be said drifty, the misfiring system (bang bang rules), used by Kyouichi in Initial D, one of the most stock looking A tune car

    B tune - grip and handling

    C tune - more to speed and acceleration because of carbon fibre parts

    Engine/internals
    A tune - from about 310PS to 350PS, have misfiring system
    B tune - useless without misfiring
    C tune - useless without misfiring

    Wheels
    A tune - Mitsubishi (Enkei) - very drifty, bad in wet
    B tune - Enkei white 5-spoke
    C tune - Enkei grey multi-spoke
    no tune - RS Watanabe black 8-spoke - drifty but still controllable
    no tune - RS Watanabe gold 8-spoke - good grip on dry but bad in rain

    Muffler
    A tune - Initial D Kyouichi/Kyle type muffler - HP and acceleration - all tracks
    B tune - Fujitsubo - average, nothing special - all tracks
    C tune - Creative Sports straight flow exhaust - 3rd and 4th gear more powerful than A tune, other gears weaker - Akina, Happo, Iro

    Rear spoiler/wing
    A tune - Mitsubishi - drifty
    B tune - HKS GT wing - stability, wide turning radius, good for high speed corners
    C tune - Creative Sports partial carbon fibre wing - lightweight but stable, good for low speed corners

    Front bumper
    A tune - Mitsubishi
    B tune - HKS - handling
    C tune - Mitsubishi

    Side window mirrors
    A tune - Mitsubishi
    B tune - Mitsubishi
    C tune - Creative Sports aero mirror - lightweight

    Stickers
    A tune - Initial D Emperor sticker - slight increase in accel
    none for other tunes

    Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution IV RS (CN9A)

    Lancer Evolution IV RS Specifications...
    Engine: 4-cyl in-line DOHC 16-valve intercooled turbocharged 2.0 liter
    Transmission: 5-speed manual
    Trivetrain: Full-Time 4WD
    Max power: 280 hp at 6500 rpm
    Max torque: 260 lb-ft torque at 3000 rpm
    weight: 2976 lb (1350 kg)

    Summary of tunes
    N tune - best in everything but may drift a bit

    A tune - best accel

    B tune - best speed

    C tune - cornering

    D tune - stable

    Engine/internals
    A tune - should have 300PS
    Other tunes unknown

    Wheels
    A tune - stock - average grip, average in rain, average start - all course
    B tune - Enkei white 5-spoke - excellent grip, average in rain, good start
    C tune - Enkei grey multispoke - good grip, good in rain, good start
    D tune - Valdi Sport gold 6-spoke - avg grip, excellent in rain, bad start
    no tune - Enkei white 6-spoke - excellent grip, good in rain, excellent start - all courses

    Muffler
    N tune - Mitsubishi single - avg accel, bad steer, good start, 1-2 good 3-5 avg - all courses
    A tune - Initial D Hawk/Seiji type single pipe muffler - avg accel, avg steer, excellent start, 1-3 excellent 4 avg 5 bad - last 3 courses
    B tune - HKS - good accel, good steer, good start, 1-4 good 5 avg - all courses
    C tune - Fujitsubo - good accel, excellent steer, avg start, 1-3 avg 4 bad 5 good - first 3 courses
    D tune - Bozz Speed dual muffler - excellent accel, excellent steer, good start, 1-3 good 4 excellent 5 avg - last 3 courses

    Rear spoiler/wing
    N tune - Mitsubishi - avg accel, avg steer, good in rain
    A tune - Initial D lightened Evo IV spoiler - good accel, good steer, avg in rain
    B tune - HKS GT wing - excellent accel, excellent steer, bad in rain, unstable at high speed, AVOID RAIN, great steering
    C tune - Valdi Sport Evo V-type spoiler - good accel, good steer, excellent in rain, great in the rain, stable at high speed, good steering
    D tune - Bozz Speed - excellent accel, good steer, avg in rain, not as light as B. very stable at high speed, OK in the rain

    Front bumper
    N tune - Mitsubishi - good accel, avg steer, stable
    A tune - Mitsubishi - same as N
    B tune - HKS - excellent accel, excellent steer, very unstable, super lightweight, get's "odd" at high speed, crap in rain
    C tune - Valdi Sport - excellent accel, good steer, unstable, lightweight, sometimes gets strange at high speed, OK for rain
    D tune - Bozz Speed (Evo IV bumper + additional spoiler lip) - avg accel, good steer, very stable, Great at high speed due to the extra down force, great in rain

    Stickers
    A tune - Monster Sport sticker - +1.5% accel
    A tune - Initial D Emperor sticker - +1.5% accel

    all of the information are from RedLine12k

    Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution VII GSR (CT9A)

    Specification
    engine: 1997cc 4-cyl in-line DOHC 16-valve intercooler turbo
    transmission: 5-speed manual
    drivetrain: full-time 4WD
    max power: 280 PS at 6500 RPM
    max torque: 282.5 ft/lbs at 3500 RPM
    weight: 1400 kg (or 3086 lbs)

    Summary of tunes

    A tune - grip, but perhaps handling better than B tune

    B tune - grip, much faster than A tune

    C tune - accel and speed with tons of carbon fibre parts, but handling may be crazy

    Engine/internals
    N tune - unknown
    A tune - unknown
    B tune - unknown
    C tune - unknown

    Wheels
    N tune - Mitsubishi - average, fairly well in dry or rain
    A tune - Ralliart grey multispoke
    B tune - Enkei white 5-spoke - have grip in both dry and wet
    C tune - Monster Sport gold 6-spoke - fast but handles bad
    no tune - Volk Racing - enough said. sucks in handling but fast
    no tune - Enkei multispoke - drifty

    Muffler
    N tune - Mitsubishi - fast launch and accel, average on upper gears
    A tune - Ralliart - normal start and accel, weaker 3rd and 4th gear, 5th better than Mitsubishi
    B tune - Creative Sports
    C tune - Monster Sport - OK start, 3rd to 5th gear very powerful

    Rear spoiler/wing
    N tune - Mitsubishi - average on dry and rain
    A tune - Valdi Sport GT wing
    B tune - Creative Sports GT wing
    C tune - Monster Sport Evo VII spoiler with carbon fibre spoiler - lightweight

    Front bumper
    N tune - Mitsubishi
    A tune - Valdi Sport
    B tune - Creative Sports
    C tune - Monster Sport (Evo VII bumper + carbon fibre spoiler lip)

    Rear bumper
    N tune - Mitsubishi
    A tune - Mitsubishi
    B tune - Mitsubishi
    C tune - Monster Sport carbon trunk - lightweight

    Hood
    N tune - Mitsubishi
    A tune - Valdi Sport that looks like Evo VIII's hood
    B tune - Creative Sports GT-type hood
    C tune - Monster Sport carbon fibre hood - lightweight

    Hope this has helped.
