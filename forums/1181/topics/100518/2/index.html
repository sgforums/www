    I believe what Leon is saying is that there is a performance difference for a car in entry and exit if it is turbo or NA. Turbo relies on a component which runs off the exhaust gases (more gas, more pump) to pump air into the engine. When you slow down for entry, there will be less exhaust gases. Less air is pumped in as the component is slowed down. Therefore there will be less combustion and therefore less power. NA cars will not suffer so much since air can still enter naturally.

    However at exit, the driver will be stepping on the accelerator. Therefore there will be an increase in exhaust gases. So with more exhaust, the turbo will be faster and therefore pump more air into the engine. NA air intake depends on speed of the car. At exit, speed will be quite low so not much air intake. So NA cars suffer at this point.
