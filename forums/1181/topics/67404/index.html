    Whoa hold up there. Let's not argue too much.

    As I mentioned before, AT gives optimum shift points for fuel efficiency. Therefore when AT shifts, it is usually pretty gentle. MT can be pretty rough as the shifts are dependent on driver's skill. But since you have control over the shifts, you are able to control the power.

    In the game, I have seen some drivers use AT before and I do notice that when it shifts, it is pretty gentle. Also since you cannot control when it shifts, you do not have the option of dragging the gear. Since you cannot drag the gear, you cannot allow the power to build. MT will definitely allow you to get to the top gear faster than AT which also means you can be faster but that means you need to be able to shift precisely at those points. AT and MT optimum shifts only differ by about 200 - 300 rpm. While the AT can shift precisely at those points, the question is can you? So actually I think ID is trying to copy real life. One example is like when you're nearing a turn. Since you are still accelerating, AT would shift you to the next higher gear. This gives more speed and less power which isn't what we want when we tackle a turn. In MT, you can just drag the gear.

    In short (in the game), AT is good if you really do not know how to shift at all but your footwork (braking and accelerating) is good. MT would be better if you really know how to shift.

    Before I post my comments on this topic, I like to say hi to all here, especially HIRA. LOL.

    Anyway, gear shifting of the AT and the MT is different in Initial D, if you look close enough. Sure, some of the shift points are correct, but when it comes to 4th gear or 5th gear shifting, the differences are there. In Ver.2, cars in 4th gear shifting in 5th gear is different in the MT and AT. AT shifts earlier, if you notice. MT shifts in 5th gear at Tuchizaka at 129-132, AT shifts earlier.

    So, a way to get the gear shifting times, is by seeing where the acceleration begins to drop, so that you shift better. This will waste money, but getting a FT AT car to get the right shift times for a FT car is to me even more wasteful. Findings in Ver.3 is that gear shifting will get different as you tune up.
