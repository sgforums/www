    I did not follow the anime or manga for ID but I do know that it should not be called 'misfiring' in real life. The term 'misfiring' is used to describe a timing problem in the engine firing sequence of the pistons.

    Basically the problem is instead of the piston firing properly at the correct time to add more power, it is firing at the wrong time and produces power which acts against the other pistons. Main symptom of this problem is power loss, if the problem is not so severe. You'll just feel that you don't seem to be getting the correct power for the rpm. More severe cases would involve the car jerking regularly, in this case, stop the car and call a tow truck.

    For the EVO3 in the game, the reason why there is a 'lighting up' at the exhaust is because of the ECU. The ECU has been set up so that it is delivering a very, very rich content of fuel air mixture into the engine. This would give more power which would translate into better acceleration. This does not help in higher top speed as there is a limit to how much fuel air is burnt in the engine.

    At the start since the car is still stationary, there is not much need for power. Therefore most of this fuel air mixture is not burnt in the engine and is passed out through the exhaust. Since most of the oxygen has already been used up in the engine, this exhaust gas will not flare up while passing through the exhaust pipe. Until it reaches the outside environment (out of the pipe) where there is more oxygen then it just flares up.

    This is not very good for the engine, in terms of temperature and stress. It's just like having the Nitro system. But Nitro is worse since they're pumping a very high percentage of oxygen into the engine. I think it's about 90%. That's why there is a constant bluish flame coming out of the exhaust pipe. The unused oxygen just fires up.

    Edit: As for whether this sytem gives the EVO3 better acceleration in the game, I don't know.

    Basically, the misfiring system is actually called Anti-Lag System. Misfiring was a BS term created in initial d. In this system, petrol is pumped into the exhaust and ignited, to keep the turbine spinning. That's why there are the sparks coming out of the muffler. This prevents turbo lag and gives a higher exit speed. As Impreza WRX said, this is bad not only for the exhaust system, but also for the turbocharger.
