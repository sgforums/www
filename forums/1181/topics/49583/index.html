    hi hi ppl ~ i'm a dc2 tune D driver . . . my time on akina downhill dry is 2"55'xx . . . well u just need to know the map and your car well enough to know how fast u can enter a hairpin at and how well your car can turn at different hairpins . . . main hairpins to take note of are:

    - hairpin before the S curve (the S curve is a good place to cut time by speeding up since u don't need to shift gears, but if u fail this hairpin, u will take time to speed up and lose alot of time)

    - hairpin before the 5 consecutive hairpins (there is a fairly long straight to speed up after this hairpin)

    - 5 consecutive hairpins (before the 1st hairpin, your speed should be about 165km/h and you can enter the 1st at about 130km/h - make sure u start braking then turning early ! and the rest about 125km/h ... )

    - hairpin before the long straight towards the end of the stage (can be completed at 5th gear with speed at 150km/h+, u can speed up quickly and cut down time easily)

    - hairpin before finish line (entering this hairpin at a high speed, u should let go of the accellerator and turn hard to the right and then start accellerating again)

    well hope this general information helps!

    27 Jun 03, 11:31
    The FD can hit very respectable times on Akina. You cannot turn using the FF method or a DC2 method since the FD Rx7 is FR and has a different turning radius.

    I do a 2:56 using the FD Spirit and the method i use to 'conquer' the turns is by 'rocking' the car towards the outside of a turn and then using the rebound of grip to throw the car into the normal cornering direction.

    Eg: Approaching a right turn
    1) I keep extreme right
    2) About 3-4 car lengths i make a dive to the left
    3) Shift down, slight brake, release accl
    4) Once i am 3/4 into the left, i then turn right attacking the right corner. (i turn slightly right , then when i get my car pointing in the apex of the corner, i make a full right into the turn).

    Not easy putting down in words but i hope this helps.
