    Yeah that's the car. Too bad it's not customizable. The good thing about this WRX is the strong 3rd gear, make use of it in Irohazaka. I know it seems faster to go through the turn at a higher speed. But usually you'll suffer a little knock or bump. For the WRX it is faster for you to brake and slow down to 3rd gear to make the turn without knocking. Not all of the turns require you to slow down though. There are some turns in the 2nd section that allow you to just drift through at 105 km/h. So just drive into the turn, let go of the gas and just slide through. These turns are usually marked with yellow lines and there are 3 turning left and 2 turning right. If you think you're going a little bit too fast, try tapping the brake a few times and turning at the same time instead of stepping on it.

    The other part is where you can jump over the grassy part in section 3. Don't screw this up. Just need practice to remember where to jump. There is another tricky S-shaped double hairpin (after the grass jumping part, section 4) which you need to enter at 3rd gear. Pump the gas and you can get through at 95 km/h.

    Eh heh, sorry made a mistake in my previous post. You should play Boost On to stand a chance against those cars on home turf, not Boost Off.

    The ditches are not supposed to make you faster in terms of acceleration. They sort of act like those train tracks forcing your car to move along a certain line preventing your car from swinging in/out too much. So if you use those ditches properly, you should be able to take those turns at a much higher speed than normal. This means your average speed on the track should be faster.

    You know the part with the 4 consecutive hairpins in the 3rd section for Akina down? Yeah you can use the ditches on the side you're turning to help you turn at a higher speed. It's not easy though as the ditch is just next to the 'barrier' so you might knock around like a pinball if you're not careful.

    Other ditches are on the first 2 hairpins of the 1st section of Akina down. They're on the opposite side of the turn. These 2 ditches end just before the hairpin. They sort of allow you to turn earlier for the hairpin giving you more distance. It's because when one side of the car is in the ditch and you try to turn, the front wheels will come out first while your rear wheels are still in the ditch. Because of this, your car sort of turns on the spot. Another ditch you can use is at the hairpin just before the 3rd section. It's on the side you're turning to.

    It seems Akagi also has ditches you can use. I'm not sure whether the ditches help or not but if you play Time Attack, you can see on the analysis screen for Akagi that they take note of whether you enter ditches or not. It's the same for Akina.

    I also assume that you got beat by the DC2 in Akagi. I think I saw a DC2 get 2.19 before on Akagi. So I think more training is in order. Are you driving an EVO or WRX? I'm not sure if this advice can apply to both but for a WRX, yeah that's correct you can take the last 4 hairpins at 158.

    Before entering the 1st hairpin, need to enter at about 165, tap the brake slightly, do a full turn and when you're nearly out of the hairpin, counter steer and do a full turn in the other direction. Do not try and maintain a neutral direction. You must full turn the wheel from 1 end to the other very fast. No need to brake but you will need to pump the accelerator gently unless you can confidently maintain 1/4. You should nicely slide your way through. Same for the next 2.

    Oh I recommend training in the rain then playing in dry. You'll find sliding around very easy after wet conditions. Plus you can go challenge in rain. Next time challenge the DC2 in Akagi uphill wet. Heh Heh.

    Edit: This is for the WRX. Please try and adjust accordingly for EVO.
