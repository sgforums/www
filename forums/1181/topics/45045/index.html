    To beat the Cappucino, the most important thing is not to slow down during the turns by hitting the walls. During the first turn on the first lap, what you can do is before the turn approaches, release accelerator, depress brake gently, shift down to 4th gear, navigate the turn, accelerate and shift back to 5th gear. You can do it conservatively (ie: brake harder) and come out of the turn at around 155kph or be more adventurous and take the corner at over 165kph. Try it for the 2 major corners and if you do not hit the walls, you will be able to beat the cappucino all the time.

    Good luck and hope this helps.

    If you look at the road surfaces for this track, you'll notice that it is quite uneven. One of the major turns in the track is actually slanted. If you can see the camera view of the car from outside, you will see that the car will actually have 1 wheel of the ground while taking the turn. The FC is front wheel drive. You lose partial acceleration if either of your 2 front wheels lift off the ground while going over the uneven surface. Tracks like Myogi and Akina are very flat so you'll have normal acceleration.
