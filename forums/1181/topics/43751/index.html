    Using a MR2 requires a very different racing line from other cars .

    Main reason for different racing line? The name "MR-2" is pretty self explanatory. The car is a MR car, being that MR stands for Midship-Rear wheel drive. What makes this car so wierd that it deserves such a wierd name? For one, MR2's engine is at the back, where the boot is. Those who watched Initial D 3rd Stage will see Kai's father tuning the engine at the boot. When the engine's at the back, it also means that the center of gravity of the car is shifted to the middle, and not like other drivetrains (FR, FF, 4WD). MR cars are unique in this way.

    Having said that, this car drifts differently from any other car (even its younger sister, the MR-S). This car offers pickup faster than any other. (I don't know how it works in Initial D) Which is why sports car around the world (Ferrari F50s, Lamborghini Murcielago, Porsche) all have their engines at the back. Even the F1 cars have their engines at the back.

    If you understood what i said above, You'd know the reason why MR2 requires a different racing line. When the car drifts, all the weight is shifted to the back of the car, meaning your ass will literally try to swing forward (thus the huge countersteering required for the MR2). Which makes this car very very weak in Akagi. The MR2 is almost impossible to control properly in Akagi wet. The MR2 requires a certain skill to use properly. For high speed tracks like Usui and AKagi, the MR2 will have many disadvantages, but when it comes to the one and only low speed track in the game, Irohazaka, I belive that a good MR2 driver can topple even the Best Spirit-R/Intergra efforts. We'll just have to see if anyone will bother to train up for it.
