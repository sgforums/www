    Maybe it's a bit difficult to visualize the movement of the vehicle in powersliding and drifting since they look quite the same. In the game before the race starts, sometimes there is a picture of Takumi drifting the car. Look at it closely, the car is almost perpendicular to the road.

    When you normally turn a car left, the left wheels (front and back) travel a shorter distance than compared to the right wheels. Powersliding is a sliding version of this. This is how all of the turns in ID is taken.

    Drifting is when you get the car almost perpendicular to the road and actually smoothly follow the curvature of the turn. In this case, the rear wheels (left and right) will actually travel a greater distance than the front wheels (left and right). This is because the front wheels are on the inside of the turn and the rear wheels are on the outside of the turn. The closest to an actual drift I can think of is to use a RWD car and drive on Myogi. If you do a drift correctly, your view should be actually looking over the mountain side and not really be in line with the road. Also computer drivers on Iro perform minor drifts for the turns.

    So yeah, there is no drifting in ID (except for Myogi and com drivers).
