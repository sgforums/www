  • fudgester
    19 Jan 06, 18:01
    I was planning to sell a couple of textbooks from previous modules which I took. So I posted messages on IVLE, offering to sell those books.

    One guy SMS-ed me, saying that he was interested in buying one of my books. He asked me about the condition of the book. So I told him that it was still in good condition and the covers were wrapped in plastic. It had some highlighting and pencilled annotations, but those are quite minimal. Pretty much what you would expect of a second-hand book, methinks.

    However, just because of the highlighting and annotations, the guy decided to back off. He said that he wanted (I quote him) 'a totally clean book'.

    Sheesh man... if you want a 'totally clean book', why bother buying second hand?

    The point I'm trying to highlight (no pun intended) is that you can't really buy a second hand book and expect it to be brand new. It's only to be expected that there will be a few highlighted lines, some pencilled notes, and so on.

    Of course, there will be second hand books that really look and feel brand new, but they are hard to find. The retail price of the book is $38++, and I'm only charging $20. That's pretty much what everyone else is charging for that book in the 'Used Textbooks' Forum in IVLE. Heck, I was open to negotiations. I would have given it to him for $15 if he asked.

    I say a few highlighted lines and pencilled notes are a small price to pay for a discounted price. I don't see why anyone really needs the book to be totally clean.

    Even then, if for some reason you need a totally clean book, go and get it at full price from the Co-Op. Don't even think about getting it second-hand.
