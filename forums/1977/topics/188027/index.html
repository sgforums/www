    i have many to say ...

    1) has anyone questioned LTA the technical specs of our roads? i am sure there are standards to adhere to regarding friction specs against tires in dry/wet conditions, ability of road surface to drain water thru (not over, over surface draining causes water sheeting and is very dangerous).

    2) has anyone noticed that LTA may have a few sub-contractors maintaining and building roads? although LTA are the primary body doing the QC, based on what are these procedures? as drivers, dont you all think more and more bumps are surfacing due to dodgy works?

    3) has anyone noticed along long stretches of E-ways, different contractors have done the surfacing (maybe even the underlays) in different ways (different materials). has anyone noticed that the roads with different surfaces have different reactions to tires? next time when you travel from city to woodlands and it rains, please take note certain sections do not encourage drain off as well as other sections.

    4) has anyone questioned that the increased number of accidents along E-ways may be linked to the poor quality of the roads ability to drain water during rainy days? watersheeting effect causes tires to have no grip on road, especially when you do an E-brake, the tires tend to have a floating effect on top of the water sheet.

    readers and motorist alike please do think about the above, we do not wish our friends and relatives to die on the road anymore.
