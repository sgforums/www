    This & that
    Why this... and why that

    HEADLIGHT HAZARD: There has been a dramatic increase in the use of Xenon (blue-white) headlights on vehicles. I am sure all drivers have encountered these blinding headlights.

    Many times, I have been blinded by a vehicle turning out from a lane. I also get blinded when the light hits my side-view mirrors. Singapore is well lit in most areas, so there is no need for these lights.

    TAN POH CHENG (MS)

    MORE EXPO PARKING LOTS NEEDED: Besides adding more space to the Singapore Expo, I hope more parking spaces will also be added.

    The ERP parking system should also be used instead of payment machines, which result in long queues when trade shows end.

    TONG SIEW PUI

    HOT-ZONE ENTIRE CITY: The plan by Philadelphia officials to turn the city into the world's largest wireless hot spot should give Singapore something to look at ('Philadelphia to become huge wireless Internet hot spot'; ST, Sept 3). Singapore can well afford the cost of US$10 million (S$17 million) cited. Even if it is US$50 million, we should do it.

    The rate Singapore telcos charge (21 cents per minute) for hot spots is way too high. Singapore should consider hot-zoning the entire city as part of infrastructure-building, much like the roads and train tracks. This will help Singapore become an information hub.

    YAP POW LOOK

    LIGHTER CATCHES FIRE: A few months ago, I bought a pack of lighters. On Tuesday morning, I was at the kitchen sink when I heard a loud 'swoosh' sound and found my kitchen cabinet burning on the inside.

    One of the lighters stored there had popped its metallic top and exploded. I was lucky because I managed to put out the fire before the other lighters caught fire too. Does any government agency check these lighters before they are put on sale? Is there a quality mark we should look out for?

    NORAINNI ABDUL RAHMAN (MDM)

    CATER TO NEEDY ELDERLY: I was touched by the efforts of Ms Fion Phua and other volunteers ('Free household items: Anyone? Anyone?' (ST, Sept 20).

    The elderly who live on social welfare could well need the items but may be unaware of the giveaway because many are illiterate. Perhaps charitable organisations can help reach out to them.

    CHEW HWEE LING (MISS)

    WHY IS IC NOT ACCEPTED?: My friend, who was asked to report to the police station, was asked to get a visitor pass. He handed over his identity card in exchange for the pass but was told that only a credit card or driving licence would do. His ATM card was also rejected.

    Why is this so? Not everyone has a credit card or driving licence.

    CHUA AIK HONG
