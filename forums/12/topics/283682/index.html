    From what you say, it seems like he has a cause of action under the tort of trespass to a person. It seems a case of battery

    Typically, he'll have to prove 3 things, directness, interference and unlawfulness.

    He can also leave it to the police or he can report it and file this suit against them. It's up to him. But its best that he consults a lawyer before taking any action. Get it quick because the limitations for civil actions regarding intentional torts is typically shorter than those for unintentional torts.

    By the way, get him to retrieve a copy of his police report. It will cause unhappiness in the station for those in-charge of the reports, but it's essential that his civil action does not contradict his police report.

    Certain things to look out for are provocation and self-defence by either party. Also, if your friend was challenged to a fight and he agrees, meaning he expressly or implictly gives consent, he's done too.

    Basically, it is typically easier to prove a civil rather than a criminal action as the standard of proof is lower in civil action. Go for it if he really thinks it worth it.
