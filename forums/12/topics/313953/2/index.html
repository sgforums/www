    Arabic is their official language, although English alone should help you get by. When I was there (only for a short while though), language wasn't much of a problem because it's probably one of the most cosmopolitan places in the Middle East - then again, I'm working for an IB where the lingua franca is English anyway. I suppose you'll get by fine in your working environment if you are hired by a MNC.

    In some ways, it's a little like Hong Kong - or Japan (actually, Japan is worse than Dubai if you're concerned about the language issue from my experience) - where English is the official language with Cantonese being their lingua franca. While not being able to speak Cantonese isn't going to hinder your work in HK, it certainly isn't going to enable you to fully assimilate into their culture.

    That said, the younger generation in UAE is pretty savvy and well-versed with English, so the language issue really depends pretty much on what you get up to and the kind of people you'll be associating with in the process.

    Hope that helps.
