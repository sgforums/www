    TS,

    I empathize with your situation, and I have so many things to say to you that I don't know quite where to begin.

    Firstly, I would like to say that I share similar traits as you, in that I have lots of misplaced pride (which I have swallowed and shitted out of my system on a periodic basis hopefully). Secondly, I admire your steadfast in love, and I hope it lasts.

    Your predicament sends a big shudder down my spine, and serves a timely reminder of the fact that one should always positively love and validate the woman one loves.

    Secondly, jojo's advice on how to validate a woman, and the various things that you can/need to do are good, but sadly your situation is more dire than that. When a woman tells you calmly in the face that you're not in her heart, it is worse than a crying or mad woman. It signifies that her heart has closed to the present you.

    To address your most immediate concern first, you said you don't understand why she is allowing another guy to wrap his hands around her. Sure none of us guys like to see another man wrapping his arms around the woman we love. In fact, it's sickening to even think about it, let alone see it happening. And you mentioned that why is it that she is so sensitive to you having another girl in the house, and yet she's "loosening" up to other men?

    The answer is plain, you 2 were a couple then, and of course it's natural to be angry and upset about another girl in the house. But she has officially cut all ties with you, and hence she has nobody to account to. Let not that cloud your judgement. Sure, it hurts, but it's over, pal. It's over.

    In fact, she has the freedom to hug/make love to other men whom she thinks is worth the while, and there's nothing you can do about it, or should do about it. That's the true meaning of a breakup. Please don't go around saying "i don't want her to be cheated" and all that crap, because you no longer have a License to do that.

    Okay, that sounds depressing and deflating, but I think that could be the start of a new beginning.

    When you think about all the happy times you had together, think also about all the sacrifices she has made to make you happy. And you realize it's probably happy one way street.

    The start of a new beginning is to change yourself for the better. Six months of self-reflection is no big deal, compared to 4 years of hurt on her part.

    Here is where the steadfastedness comes in, pal.
    She has given you a time period, 6 months, to come back if you wish, and woo her all over again.
    Well, I wouldn't say whether she is sincere in that or not.
    But like jojo said, please look at the situation from your ex-gf's point of view. She's hurting too much (and has even slapped you) and 6 months is a short period for her to heal.

    If you go back after 6 months, and find that she's still in another man's arms, then you can really let go, and just wish her all the best.
    If you go back after 6 months, and found her still hurting, maybe she will tell you to come back after another 6 months.
    If you find her in a more receptive mood after 6 months, no doubt you have hope. And although she may say that you have to start all over again, that's not quite possible. Everything you've done for her is not forgotten like this.

    Is she worth the 6 months wait?
    Let not anyone tell you it's worth it, or it's a waste of time, or it's wimpish or whatever. You ask yourself, is she worth it? You said she completes you, so is that perfect girl worth waiting?

    Every time you think of the injustice of her not remembering both your good times and sweet memories, tell yourself she's actually suffering during this time too.

    Hope that helps, pal.
