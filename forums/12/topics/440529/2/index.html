    Well, as for cash management, it's very good to save money, but I think you might be too tough on yourself. spending only $1.50 and eating biscuit everyday? That's not healthy nor sustainable.

    There are 2 ways of saving money.

    1) Sit there and scrimp on whatever meagre pocket money your parents give you.

    or

    2) Go earn your own money. I believe it should be school holiday for you now. Go find a holiday job. You should be able to make close to $1000 in 1 month. And it would be more justified for you to spend your hard-earned money.

    and lastly, word of advice. Try not to buy any big ticket items yet. While something might seem very "worthwhile" (e.g. fishing rods) at the moment, you might regret spending the money in the future, when you could have used it for something better.

    Maybe you would like to learn driving when you turn 18, go for a backpacking holiday with friends when you graduate, getting a girlfriend, buy a car/motorcycle etc Just some suggestions.

    Take some time, and figure out where and what you would be 5 years down the road, and den evaluate whether its worth it to make the purchase.
