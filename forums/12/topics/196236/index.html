    I don't think I need to give any more advice than what has been already been churned out by others on this thread, so I'll just address the abovementioned.

    When she says she doesn't know, it could really be a genuine case that she doesn't know(as in she might not be prepared for a relationship at this point in time, she isn't sure if you're the one for her, among a list of other countless possibilities) - hence, do not try to read between the lines when there is, in all likelihood, nothing there to begin with. In other words, she'd probably have given the same reply had someone else(barring someone whom she has a strong sense of revulsion for) done what you did, which also suggests her seemingly negative response was not reserved exclusively for you(perhaps, she doesn't even know her reaction had sowed the faintest notion of rejection in your mind!).

    Clearly, the fact that she still makes the effort to initiate a conversation with you after the incident is a clear indication it wasn't a outright rejection. I suppose only time will tell if anything comes of this, but I see nothing to suggest she actually rejected you.
