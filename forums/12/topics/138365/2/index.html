    Work = Power x Time

    Work = Knowledge x Money

    Money =Work / Knowledge

    Just a side note.... your formula is flawed. When it is postulated that Time=Money, it contemplates that the longer you spend on something, the higher your expenses or, as the case may be, opportunity cost will be.

    On the basis of the foregoing and applying Money = Expenses or Opportunity Cost to your formula, we have:

    Expenses =Work / Knowledge

    Thus, as Knowledge decreases, Expenses increases, regardless of how much Work is done. New conclusion is the less you know, the more you have to pay to get things done.
