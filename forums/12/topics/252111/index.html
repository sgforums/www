    There are more than 101 way to ask a question, but it's wheather did you know the correct steps of answering the question.

    Maths is like a skill, if you didn't practice, i'll turn rusty. Keep adding Oil, don't let it rust. Try to spent at least 30~60 minute a day tackling some maths questions.

    Do it slowly, and steadily.

    Although Mid-Year exams are important, but the ultimate goal is to do well for your O Level. Nevermind if you didn't do well for Mid-Year Exam, remember that the ultimate goal is O Level. DON'T be disheartened by your friends' comment, you must keep your mindset positive at all time.

    Even though you may have some disappointment on your grades, but make that be your lesson learned. Never never make the same mistake twice, this is what my maths teacher told me.

    When you faced a very challenging maths question, challenge yourself to see if you can solve the problem or not. If you got the wrong answer, then try again. If wrong, then maybe you can seek help from your teacher or your peers. Perhaps you can post the questions here at sgForums and everybody will help you.

    Remember this, Energizer's slogan is "Never say Die".

    While last-minute mugging can get you through the earlier stages of one's education, this is not my recommended mode of study for tertiary level. That you have found yourself short in handling your exams is a good indicator of your inadequacy. Good that you have discovered this early coz it means you have time and opportunity to change your study strategy.

    The key to handling tertiary studies is consistency and regular studies. Seting aside time to revise your work everyday will be the best. And the best thing about this is that if you do it on a daily basis, you don't have to study for hours! In fact a few minutes per subject will be sufficient coz you do it everyday! Just go through the topics covered in the lecture & tutorial each day to make sure you understand them. You don't have to master them. At least make sure you can follow them. Intensify your revision only nearer the exams so you can focus more fully on them. The key is : FAIL TO PREPARE, PREPAR TO FAIL! Good luck!
