    I have been doing treatments in yunnam haircare since 2002. While I get to hear bad and good views about Yunnam, I still continue my treatments in Yunnam as the treatments are effective to me. My advice to those who are considering whether to come to Yunnam treatment is that you need to consider whether spending more that 10-20k on your hair is worth it to you.

     

    For me, I had very sensitive scalp (reddish and itchy) so I decided to seek help from Yunnam. However, in the few years of treatment in Yunnam, I heard and talked to many customers. Many thought that they only need to do treatment for one year and they could stop all treatments in Yunnam. But in the end, they regret as they need spend more on their treatment to maintain their hair condition.

     

    Yunnam’s treatment and products are not cheap. Don't be fooled by their promotion of spending less than a hundred buck for one treatment. One treatment ranges from 150-250 bucks and their package comes in 20 treatments ( about 3000-5000bucks). In the first year of treatment, you are likely to spend more than 10k as you will need to come for treatment at least once a week. In the second year, slightly less than 10k as you will need to come for treatment less frequently ( once a month).

     

    I have seen customers stopping due to high cost and returning yunnam to continue in a year time as their hair problem returned after ceasing their treatment in yunnam. In my opinion, Yunnam’s treatments are effective only in controlling hair problem. Once your hair problems are controlled, you need to constantly maintain to prevent your hair problem from returning to haunt you.

     

    So with the high cost and long-term treatment, my advice is whether you can afford the cost and whether it is worth it. I have seen university students who are still studying seeking help from yunnam and ended up paying installments ( few hundreds per month) to maintain their hair problem. I felt sorry for those students as the reason for them continuing is because they do not wish to see the thousands of dollars savings they spent, go to waste (their hair problem came back again).

     

    So to those students who are considering whether to seek help from yunnam, I would advice them to seek help from doctors or if they really want to seek help from yunnam, please consider the high cost required.

    Here's my take so far. Before i go on, I suffered from hair loss due to many factors except genetic inheritance (as all my family members do not have this problem). So to keep things simple and hassle free, i always go to the barber and say get me a crew cut No .1 (the shortest in the range). The new hairstyle has made my life easier and less worrisome about not looking good with balding problem. A basic principle in life is there is always 2 side of the coin - one is the problem, the other is the solution.

    Anyway, i started having hair loss mainly due to stress and the lack of attention to proper diet. Of course, our weather made things worse. All in all, the multitude of factors added to the situation of mine - uncontrollable hair loss. I had wanted to visit Yun Nan or Beijing 101. However, deliberation over the issues and knowing of the fact that there is no free lunch in this world - i decided against it - to avoid the huge costs and also the hassle of never-ending treatment.

    I visited and consulted medical professionals (doctors and specialists - not Traditional Chinese Medicine (aka TCM)) and from a medical science perspective, there is no known permanent cure to hair loss. Medical prescribed as mentioned earlier in this thread will only help to maintain the situation in the best condition that our hormones are able to adjust. As such, medication will maintain the current state of hair loss condition and in some instances slightly improve (meaning more hair growth and less hair loss). However, the medication has to be sustained and cannot be stopped, else the condition will return. There are some known side effects and one of them is the loss of sexual drive (manz.. this is worse than hair loss, and is one reason why i choose not to opt for it.).

    So after gathering of facts and much consderations, I opted for a solution (flip side of the coin) by settling down comfortably with neater hair cut (how messy can it get with crew cut no.1) and without being hassled by maintenance. Anyway, i am almost 40. No big deal as life goes on and I think I am happier given that I have found the solution to my problem.

    Cheers, JY
