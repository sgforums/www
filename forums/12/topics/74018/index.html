    You rang?

    Okay, I don't know what the rules are in Singapore, so I'm writing upon the assumption that they adopt the same general rules used in Australia.

    Counsellors are not subject to client-counsellor privileges - they're not protected by confidentiality rules like doctors or lawyers. However, they are not obliged to report if it doesn't involve abuse or harm to life and limb.

    In this case, I'd need to know more details. Who referred the kid to the counsellor and what was the working arrangement? It might be that by the working arrangement, the counsellor may be obliged to report the theft. If no such condition exists, I would not report it, and instead aim to have the kid return the stolen item, and take a look at the family's financial situation.
