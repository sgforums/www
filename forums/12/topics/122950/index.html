    A recent Time magazine issue looks at the Science of Happiness and in one related article, the magazine looks at the relation between money and happiness.

    One paragraph has this to say:

    "Paradoxically, it is the very increase in money [...] that triggers dissatisfaction. As material expectations keep rising, more money may engender only more desires. [....]As men and women move up the economic ladder, most of them almost immediately stop feeling grateful for their elevated circumstances and focus on what they still don't have. Suppose you lived in two-bedroom house for years and dreamed of having three bedrooms. You finally get that three-bedroom house. Will it bring you happiness? Not necessarily. Three bedrooms will become your new norm, and you'll begin to long for a four-bedroom abode."

    Bottomline....don't let money control your happiness. Be content with what you have because when you pass on from this world, you won't be bringing along all those dollar notes with you.
