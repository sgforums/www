    This is one of life's biggest paradoxes IMO and in fact, it applies to more than just relationships.

    If you're prepared to fail and not commit yourself fully, you'd in all probability reproach yourself for not having done more should things go wrong(and needless to say, chances of things not working out would invariably be higher in this instance). Then again, if you went all out and still failed, you'd still find it hard to bear the brunt of your failings.

    Hence, this begs the question : would you really want to lead a stable and indifferent life whereby you neither experience the extreme ecstasy nor despair? Or would you prefer to give everything in all your pursuits so that you live a life with as few regrets as possible(at the expense of encountering many more downs than if you were to adopt a passive approach, of course)?
