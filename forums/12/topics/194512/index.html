    I think a prevalent trait that I've observed among Asians in general(basically those brought up in an Asian culture) - and I'm not just talking about girls - is the lack of want to take an initiative in forging inter-personal relationships(and I don't just mean boy-girl ones). Having studied and worked in many parts of the world myself, I do feel that is often a major obstacle which prevents strong bonds from being formed.

    Back to your issue . If this(the part where you lament she doesn't take much initiative to start a conversation or ask you out and you had to do everything) hadn't been described in an Asian context, then it'd probably mean she has no interest in you. That said, I don't think her body language(or lack thereof) should put you off just yet - as many have already said on this thread, the fact that she has been willing to go out with you should leave you with some hope. Then again, how and what you're prepared to do with this 'hope' also depends on you - are you prepared to be the one initiating things every time for the foreseeable future, for instance? In essence, I think you need to reassess your objectives(ie.how far you're prepared to go to win her over) in this endeavour and re-strategise your moves to attain what you want. Nevertheless, just keep in mind that her body language may not indicate a disinterest in you.

    Personally, such a problem has never - and in all likelihood, will never - plague me simply because I've never believed in any sort of a relationship where both parties are not prepared to move on to "the next level" mutually(so basically, I wouldn't even see myself having a future with someone who doesn't want/bother/dare to take the initiative to initiate something with me ).
