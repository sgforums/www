    You must be going through quite a bit. You sound frustrated. Maybe you are feeling that nobody (your family and school mates) understands what you are going through and that doesn't make it easier.

    I gathered a few things from your post:
    1) You have a interest to gain new knowledge which propels you towards new things. This makes picking them up much easier
    2) Since you like new things, it's not mastering them but analysing them that you do
    3) Hence it's much easier for you to grasp them, which accounts for the shorter time you need to absorb the info
    4) Your love-hate relationship with your classmates stem from the fact that they are unable to understand how you put in little and do so much better
    5) They have mistaken your honest confession as a sign of pride. Thus they hold back any information from you
    6) They may look up to you but find it strange that you go to them for advise on topics and all. In fear (of losing out) they may clam up and shut you out.
    7) Since your parents have not taken the path you are now on, they really have no idea what you are going through.

    Your abilities aside, if you want to talk about making friends, you must be on the same level as them. No one likes to have someone smarter than them to be friends. Friends make you feel good about yourself and not the other way round. If you want genuine friends, you've to be one yourself. You are doing great at school and that's fantastic. Will you be wiling to share it with them and not fear that you have lost?

    Primarily, tertiary education is to equip you with basic skills and more importantly the ability to analyse situations. What you face in the working world may be different from what you have studied. It's a process and not an end. You ability to analyse will put you in a better state, cos that's what employers will look for. But your relationship skills should be as strong cos no matter how smart you are, you still need to work with them. And you need to care for those you work with and not make them objects alone.
