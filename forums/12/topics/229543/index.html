    Ultimately... you can make money doing anything... as long as you are good enough at it.

    If you want to do game design, become one of the best, and you'll always be hired.

    The world outside actually looks more at competence than qualifications.

    I have friends who start in one degree, but then end up working in entirely different jobs.

    So as a mental test of job suitability, try this: Close your eyes and imagine... what would it feel like to do the kind of work you're aspiring to do, day after day.

    See it vividly as if you are there, and really get the feeling for it.... THEN imagine yourself doing that job for another 5 to 10 years...

    The question Now is... do you still feel the passion for it?

    If the answer is YES, then this might be the right work for you.

    It's best if you can make money doing what you love. Even if you start out earning less at first... because in the end, it's your PASSION that will push you to excel and drive your income through the roof.
