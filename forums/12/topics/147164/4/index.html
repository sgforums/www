    I think the key is to communicate your vision and fears to the guy to help him understand, not misperceive you.

    If I may equate physical to sex, then you really don't want a premature/early r/s to be motivated and fed by physical lust, sex and desire. You don't want the guy to date you because he's motivated by sex to get a good satisfaction while you unwillingly give in to his needs and suffer in silence for fear that if you don't give in, he'll break up.

    What you want to cultivate and develop (to see a loving r/s) is love that is non-sexual in nature because through that would you be able to build and have trust, confidence and love in the guy to settle down with.

    A r/s is shaky when in the early stages, physical sex dominates (not non-sexual love).

    A r/s is solid when non-sexual love gets tested and grows deep in the early stages. It's something for two lovebirds to comfortably settled down together with, to do and enjoy anything/everything together (be it buying a flat, physical sex, lust, etc that would spice up the loving r/s else it becomes dull).
