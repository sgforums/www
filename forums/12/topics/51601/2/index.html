    hi cunfused 2000,

    A few things that i'd like to offer... for a start, many guys (and gals alike) take a closer look at their relationship before they sign on the dotted line. It's especially frightening when relationships around you do not work out well or reports on divorce rates have been steadily climbing up. We start to question if this is what we are getting ourselves into.

    I was told that love has 3 components: passion, intimacy & commitment.

    We can quickly identify with passion, the racing heartbeat, the world slowing down when you are with your significant other and all seems bright as day. Perhaps that's what you are feeling with Ms S. I reckon you must have felt that when you first knew you gf. The thing about passion is it can quickly come and quickly go. Give yourself 6 months or a year and the heartbeat will go back to normal.

    Intimacy is like the anchor. The more you know about your SO (sig other) and the more you complement her, the easier it is to sustain it. Common values and beliefs play a big part. The more you know about the other and agree with that, the greater the chances of sticking it through. For me, i started out talking to my husband on the phone. We would talk for hours. It's at those times that i found out about his values, which is family first. I was looking for a man like that and i knew that since he treasured his parents and siblings, he will take good care of ours.

    the last and often mistaken as least is commitment. It almost sounds like a dirty or taboo word. After i got married, a friend enquired about our state of affairs. He's been married for years and have 2 children. So he told me something that i'm holding on to... we marry the one we love. after the wedding bells and all, we learn to love the one we married. It's not a chore, but realise that we have committed to the life or better or worse, in sickness or in health, till death do us part. Getting married may be the easy part, but keeping it together takes a lifetime

    Perhaps it's for you to know what you really want in a spouse, not just the practicalities (i have a 5-room flat, no car but all the loans you've talked about), we're talking about personality, values, etc. It's important to know who you are too. It is a good time to take stock and make a decision, an informed one and one you'll hold on to.
