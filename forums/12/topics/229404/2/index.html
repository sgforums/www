    I think you must accept rejections slightly more positively.

    I see rejection this way:

    Rejections are very painful but are they are parts and parcels of everyday life.
    It hopefully leads us to the one we have been waiting for.

    It is impossible that each time you go into a shop and you definitely buy the thing you want. The shopowner may be disappointed by your rejection of his goods, but the next customer may buy or the next shop could offer what you want. Just make sure you know what you want. Rejection is just parts and parcels of life.

    When i got rejected, my thoughts are:
    1. She thinks I am lousy.
    2. She might be lousy, because she don't see my virtues.
    3. Both are OK, but we have differences or due to some circumstances.

    Since the reasons for rejection is subjective and usually personal. You should never be affected by it to the point that commit suicide is comtemplated.
    But we should reflect on why things don't work.

    If you have family and friends that are concerned about you, you should be quite OK. You should feel good about yourself.

    Don't start a relationship by expecting to fulfill your dream of marriage and children by 30. You will frighten every gal you meet.
    Just treat them like normal guy friends. Don't have too much expectation too soon, you also won't feel rejected this way.

    But starting to wait at 8.30 when the time is 10.00 while does indicate you cares, is a little bit excessive.
    The opposite party may feel a little bit controlled. If before marriage, things are like this, after marriage.......We all need to breathe.

    Finding the right person is most important, not finding the right time (if there is such thing as the right time to marry.)

    Life deals you quite nice cards. Take up some sports to take your thoughts of out of this suicide issue for your parents' sake.
