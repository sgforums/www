    Let's just say this.... I see you as a borderline case. Not really good enough to proceed to Sec 4 but not bad enough to get retained. Ultimately, you're going to have to ask yourself this question: are you going to work hard from now on? A lot of damage has been done to your academic standing, and you really need to put in a lot of effort here.

    I was something like you once.... I really heck cared the first half of Sec 3. When I realised I was near the bottom of my class, I bucked up and put in the ultimate effort. Even during the Dec holidays I would study like siao (believe me, I studied the recommended 3-4 hours during the Dec holidays). My Sec 3 foundation was very bad, and all that effort was needed to correct it.

    If you truly believe that your foundation is very bad, and all-out revision over the holidays won't help, then it would be better to retain. Otherwise, trust me, your Sec 4 will be hell. You really, really need all those Sec 3 concepts to do Sec 4. Might as well retain one year, be a super pro in Sec 3 stuff, and your Sec 4 will be much easier. Of course, this still isn't a licence for you to slack in Sec 4.

    My tuition student failed his Sec 2 streaming and was given the choice of whether to promote or retain. He chose to get promoted, and he's now suffering like hell. In my view, he made a bad mistake by not retaining because he can't even do very elementary mathematics like simple linear equations ( ax + b = c ) and similar triangles. No joke. I ended up drilling all those basic Sec 1 and Sec 2 stuff into his head instead of teaching him Sec 3 topics. It's really not possible to teach him Sec 3 stuff when his foundation is so weak.

    I don't suppose your situation is as bad as my tuition kid, but ultimately it's your decision.

    Honestly speaking, when it comes to handwriting, I used to have terrible handwriting too. But remember, that you are writing not for yourself to see, but for the Cambridge markers to see. If you keep reminding yourself, sooner or later you will pressurise yourself to write neater, until you reach the biting point whereby you do not have to compromise speed for neatness. It comes with practice, especially if you do Social Studies.

    Careless mistakes. Sometimes it will save a lot of time if you read the question and do your working properly. Sometimes, esp for A maths, you wont have the time to re-check. So when you are doing your working, why not recheck at the same time? It wont take too long, simply recalculate on your calculator.

    It is always a better option to promote. You did well enough to make it to Sec 3, you can definately make it thru your O levels. You don't have to mug, I.D. your problem topics/chapters, and work on them. No point mugging over a topic that you did well, right?

    All the best!
