    Hi TS,

    You have difficulty securing a job?

    I tendered my resignation 3 weeks ago and my 1 month is due next week. Up till now I have had 4 interviews and 1 already offered. I am going for my final interview for my 3rd interview tomorrow. The amount of resumes I sent; I cannot remember.

    Have you been eagerly looking through news papers? apply to jobstreet. Amongst my 4 interviews, 2 of them were through agency. 1 was from newspaper and 1 was from company web page.

    What I can advise is that:
    (1) Know the industry well that you're going to enter. Unless you're thinking of entering an industry you've never done before. Still you still need to know something from that industry.
    (2) If you can do what you're best at then do that.
    (3) Enter firms with famous names. It'll facilitate a better chance when you leave for your next job. Also it'll be very great if you can expand your job scope with your new job. Or become specialise on it.
    (4) If you're in the technical stream, stay with technical. Don't stray to operation unless you decided to change line.
    (5) Stay at least 1 year on the job.
    (6) Stress is every where. It's part and parcel of working life. If you cannot handle stress in the last job then you'll be hopping from job to job.
    (7) I conducted interviews before and I look at the attitude of the interviewee first before anything else. Many of the interviewers also do that.
    (8 ) Never bad-mouth your previous company that you left.

    There're many more others can add to the list. But the bottom line is
    Have a Positive Attitude!

    Good luck to your job hunting!
