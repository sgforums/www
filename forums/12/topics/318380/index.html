    "unable to control my actions and thoughts clearly."

    The problem lies here. You are not controlling your thoughts. But you can't stop thinking anyway. But you can control your mind.

    Thoughts are internal dialogue. Your mind is speaking, saying something, telling You something, that is thought. Most are instructive, telling You what to do, and some instigating, like the kind you are complaining about.

    You can try this experiment. Try labeling your thoughts. Identify the traits of a particular thought and label it according to its characteristics. Say like angry thoughts, label it Grumpy. Happy thoughts label it Happy, or Felicity or such. When thoughts are anoymous they run wild unbriddled, when identified they become silent. You want to keep the bad tempered thoughts quiet, not the happy or neutral ones. Lable them, then watch out for them. You will find they do not appear when you watch out for them. But it will start yakking when You are unaware. This is normal, experienced Buddhist monks always have thoughts interrupting their meditation, they just dismiss it and continue meditation. Identify the bad thought and stop it. This way you can stop unwanted thoughts, but allow any other thoughts free reign of your mind.

    See if this works.
