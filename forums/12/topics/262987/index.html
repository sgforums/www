    Hey girl,

    First off, worrying about it will just increase your stress levels and that's really bad for your overall health.

    I myself found my hair getting thinner especially at the parting near the hairline. When i consulted a dietician, she told me i was lacking in Omega-3 oil. Since then i've been taking Usana's Omega-3 fish oil gel capsules and also their multivitamins. Currently, after a month, there's a vast improvement and though my hair is not luciously thick, at least noone will comment saying i'm balding or whatever.

    Also, i nourish my hair every week by applying Olive oil to the entire head and massaging it in. This is done before bed and left throughout the night, clipped up. Then i wash out my hair with a mild shampoo in the morning. Though it is really gross to touch your oily hair at night and stuff, this treatment leaves your hair healthy and shiny. If you are hardworking, once massaging the oil in, soak a small towel with hot water and wrap your hair in a turban-like fashion. Leave till towel is cool. Repeat at least 5 times. This is good especially in early stages.

    Another rule is to NEVER brush your wet hair! This will jerk the hair follicles out as wet hair is at its' weakest. Instead, use a wide toothed comb to comb out once, and then let hair air-dry. As far as possible, use a brush only on dry hair, that has those plastic points in its teeth. This will act like a massage on the hair.

    It is important not to use too many styling products. Best to have a short, no-fuss hairstyle during this period of pampering your hair. Rebonding/straightening/perming is out of the question as they do the worst damage possible to hair.

    Lastly, hair is a woman's crowning glory. Do think before you do/apply anything to your hair.
