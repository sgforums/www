    Just some cost factors:

    Raising a baby in sg may not be easy, but it may not be hard either. It depends on hpw much one is willing to sacrifice.

    Good cots can be purchased at 80 plus; while a branded one may cost hundreds. A child doesn't need a good one, as long as the cot is play safe, sleep safe.

    Babies grow very fast; clothes should be minimal at the initial stage to prevent 'wastage'; at most have to wash pretty frequently.

    Diapers can be used in the nights, so as to save costs; cloth ones can be used in the daytime. There are even diaper liners to line the cloth so as make washing easier; e.g. if the baby poo, the stain will be mostly on the liner, rather than on the cloth diaper. Liners can be thrown away or re-used after washing. Liners are sold in 100s per box, price is less than 10, I think it's a few dollars.

    Washing can be done when the baby is sleeping.

    There are day care centres who take care of babies, alternatively, nannies are also available. Day care centres normally offer cheaper pricing to working mothers, while for nannies, a monthly salary of roughly 600 - 700 SGD.

    I have a friend who asks her neighbour for help; her neighbour upon seeing her situation, agrees to help at 150 SGD a month.

    Milk - If adhere by nutritious meals etc, a mother can give breast milk up to at least 6 months - saving on milk powder. At 8 months, the baby can take liquid food like porridge and fish slices; but have to be cooked till they are very soft and easily digestiable.

    Stuff like stroller, toys etc - there are always cheap ones out there, just depends on whether one is willing to look.

    Alternative finance, there are always helplines and organisations to turn to for counselling, help on single parents who have chilldren before marriage and also finance.

    For schooling, schools don't stop or blacklist children born in single families, even if the parents are not married. Every child enlisted is given the same opportunity.
