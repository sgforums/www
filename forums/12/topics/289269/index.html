    The problem is that you have nothing to look forward to in life! There must be a balance. If you have things to look forward to, then you will be able to cope with the downsides of life.

    You do not mind going to work even if it is a torture, if you know that you will have a wild time over the weekend.

    If you have nothing to look forward to then the torture of work will be a serious torture because the torture is all you have!

    How can anyone, not just you, live life that way? A long lasting continuous no ending torture?

    You see your friends enjoying life at school and you assume that they do not find it a torture. You think that they do not find it a torture because it is fun for them.

    Think again. That might not be true! The real reason might be that they can cope and because they can cope, they are able to have some fun within the torture.

    That is to say, they have many things, outside of school to look forward to and so they can cope with the torture of school better and because they can cope with the torture of school better, they start to enjoy it!

    So, I think that you cannot solve your problem because you are looking for the wrong solution! You are trying to make a torturous situation acceptable by trying to make school less of a torture, you will fail!

    It will always be a torture. You cannot do anything about that!

    What you can do, however is to find things that you enjoy to do. You can organize, plan, look forward to doing things that you enjoy doing and just clench your teeth and do the necessary to finish school and pass.

    If you do not solve this problem, you will have problems next time when you start working. When you find it tough going, you resign. If you do that, you will not be able to put in enough years to gain the necessary experience.

    Whereas, if you are able to "compensate" by finding things to look forward to, your life will be more meaningful and you will be able to cope with the torture of school and next time, work, better.
