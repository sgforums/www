    1. Communication is two ways. it isn't just TELLING her how you feel when she isn't/doesn't want to divulge her own feelings back.

    Never say you are the ugliest, poorest, shortest, skinniest, most stupid, etc, etc. REMEMBER: No human is perfect. They have flaws and they won't show it out that easily unless its pretty obvious.

    Also take note there are less fortunate people trying to survive out there in this world made cruel by humans. Those disabled, mentally challenged, elderly, and others who seemed to be struggling with their lives are doing their best to make out the best with what they have to live in this world. Many of them know how to take life easy as they do NOT expect many things. They live simple and happy. Its something we all can learn from them.

    Try to go out with people who are more joyous, happy, nice and those who gave you very good impressions. These people (even if they have problems of their own) can at least show a positive and influencing side to the people around them. Observe how they act, and learn from them, but try not to imitate them too much, unless you want to be branded a "Mark II" of someone...

    Insecurity is one issue that has a major factor on low self-esteem.

    How do you act when a task is given to you? Say... a project? Do you panic at the deadline given? Or do you fear you may not have enough resources to support your stuffs? Or better, you fear no one is there to lend you a hand when you need it? Take a moment to think how you act when every situation comes to you and then ask your friends how they respond in the same situation.

    It also has to do with your upbringing and the enviornment you were brought up. Families and friends have a huge influence on who you are now. Many of us do not have an ideal family upbringing, but that does not stop us from being the good person we are capable of. It is through self awareness and acknowledgement that we have areas to improve which makes us one step closer to being a good person as a whole.
