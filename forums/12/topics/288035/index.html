    UniSIM is Singapore's first private university with MOE approval to award its own degrees. In this respect, it ranks equally with all Singapore universities (3 public ones) which come under MOE's control.
    As for foreign universities - there are plenty from Australia, Britain and USA. Most of these depends on local educational setups to run their programmes and the degrees are awarded by the foreign universities. They do not come under MOE's control. PSB, MDIS, MIS, SHRI, SMA and many others offer such foreign university programmes. And so does SIM Global which runs RMIT, UOL, OU, etc.
    Whether lectures, tutorials, courseworks, etc. - it all depends on the university's approach. For UOL, it is unseen written exams for most papers (unless practical ones!). For RMIT, you have combination of courseworks and exams.
    Unlike on-campus full-time study, you can take your time to do the programme, work or do other things!
    Some programmes don't even require you to attend courses if you are able to study and learn on your own! Some UOL programmes fit into this scheme which is ideal for people who are disciplined, capable and can learn with minimum help or mostly self-help. This is quite an uphill task! It used to be the case where many went thru UOL External Programme on their own bootstrap and really earned their degrees by their own efforts! These days, so many educational outfits are actually conducting classes for these UOL self-learning programmes.

    If you ask me, I rather put my money in UniSIM. It is still young but in time to come, it will gain the recognition that SIM has. America's best and top universities (Ivy League) are private universities. So, don't worry.

    Whether you study full-time on-campus or part-time off-campus, the difference is only the 'student' life. There is no difference whether you study Statistics in UniSIM, RMIT, NUS or whatever. The same with Accounting - you will learn debits and credits all the same!!!!

    Of course, those who studied full-time on-campus will have more opportunities and time to be involved in campus activities and to socialise with one another.
    But do not think for one moment that the degree obtained is any better! At the end of day, it is you - the student that makes the difference. If you are good, any university you go is no problem but of course, if you are that good, you can choose to go to top university - not the second stringers!
