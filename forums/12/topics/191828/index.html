    Why do women like jerks?

    Right, seriously, I'd disagree with some things others have suggested here, but ultimately, you have to decide for yourself - and live with your decision for better or worse.

    Without knowing who you love more(your ex when you were with her or your current girlfriend at this point in time), I'd say what your ex has done has surpassed what most reasonable girls would ever do. The fact she was reluctant to sell the engagement ring to raise money to repay your debt, in my view, does not so much represent the sentimental value of the ring to her as much as her love for you. Moreover, I think the fact she actually bothered to lend you a helping hand(and to even do so behind your back) despite you two already not being together("clean break" or not, it's quite clear both you and your ex have established a tacit and implicit understanding that your relationship is over) merely reinforces that point as far as I can see.

    No disrespect to your current girlfriend, but I suppose there's some reservation about her which has prompted you to even consider getting back with your ex to begin with - quite simply, you do not know how much hardship your current girlfriend is, or will ever be, prepared to go through with you. Subconsciously, whether you would want to admit to it or not, you know this is playing on your mind somewhat and - I could be wrong here though - someday when things go wrong(be it a tiff or whatever) with your current girlfriend, you're bound to draw comparisons between her and your ex. Should this be the case, then I think you should quite readily acknowledge at the back of your mind that it's still your ex whom you love more and against whom all your other girlfriends(be it your current one or future ones if things don't work out with the current one ) will be benchmarked against.

    At the end of the day, my only advice is to make a decision with your heart - if it's your ex you truly love, but remain unsure simply because you wouldn't like to break up with your current girlfriend because she has done nothing wrong and has been nice to you over the 3 years you've been together, then go back to your ex. It might sound cruel and harsh to your girlfriend, but I suppose you know better than most that's for the best in the long run. However, if you are only pondering this issue out of gratitude for your ex, then you're obviously better off staying in your current relationship. In essence, you should make a decision based on what you feel is best for yourself and disregard the interests of both women in the process - this may sound cruel, but I suppose it's the only way for you to truly find an answer to your question.

    Nevertheless, just remember to bear in mind that whatever you decide to do, it's a decision you'll have to live with...

    Good Luck!
