    Trust me, the health insurance, is minimal compared to your day to day expenses. Most internationals in Melbourne/Sydney spend typically $350/wk, although the majority I know receive $450-$500. Everyone likes to deny how much they really spend in order to project the typical "uni-life-is-so-tough-and-boring-i-m-just-a-poor-frugal-hardworking-student" image.

    If you don't plan your expenses properly and you blow your budget, that's when kingdom come.

    I'll give you a rough breakdown:

    Accomodation - $100-$150/wk rent alone depending
    Bills (HP+Internet+Electricity+Water+Gas) - $50/wk
    Food - $100/wk +/- $50 depending how often you eat out

    That's $300 right there.

    When I was 18< and taking the bus, I spent $30/wk on transport.

    Now that I'm >18 and driving, I spend $30/wk on petrol - and I'm driving a really fuel economical car - if you buy R33,34,S15 like most of the other internationals and my friends, its going to cost you at least $60-70/week

    You have approx $350 right there.

    Budget carefully, this is a major life switch - factor in things like bills still have to be paid when you come back to Singapore for holiday - your rent/internet etc. anything that is under a contract.

    Besides studying, you would also want to have a life. That's gonna cost $$ as well.

    IMHO - there's no such thing as wu hua in an overseas education. It's a luxury, not a necessity - and your parents are under no obligation to give you one either - most of those who are/have received one acknowledge this privilege

    The best thing you can do to "repay", so as to speak, your parents is to go into the top universities (regardless of which country) and do your best. Don't think it's a stride through the park because it sure ain't. Singaporeans tend to over inflate their self worth. If you're entering a Go8 uni in Australia, you're going to be competing amongst some of the very best that the States have to offer - of course, there will be ALOT of crappy internationals, but those who aren't uni material tend to drop out, or get by so marginally that really, you don't notice.

    Having said that, for Australia, the general consensus is that the best universities belong to the Go8 - UNSW, Monash, Melbourne, Sydney, Adelaide, UWA, UQ, ANU

    There are some minor differences here and there - e.g. some people say UNSW engin is better blah blah blah - but seriously, factor in where you'd rather be - as in, Melbourne, Sydney, Perth, Adelaide, Queensland, Canberra

    Melbourne is BORING to me - I reckon Sydney pwns, and Queensland seems to be a really happening place though I've yet to do any "real living" there aside from being a tourist.

    EDIT: FYI: I received my pre-tertiary education in Australia - I actually really wanted to go to CA/UK/US but because of my liability (aka the DUTY ) to the nation, I could not choose to pursue my tertiary education beyond AU - I really recommend going to CA if you can - I've spent time there and it is AWESOME! Calgary might be abit .. though
