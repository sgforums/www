    To be fair, I suppose it cannot be easy to be going through what you're going through now, but forgive me for being blunt, marriage is something that you shouldn't even consider at this point in time(nevermind the 9 years, it still wouldn't be right even if you had been together for 90 years if you two ever came to such conclusions!).

    As some have pointed out, there're so many possible reasons as to why your bf feels the way he does - be it because he has fallen for someone else, awakened to the reality that you're not the one he wants to spend the rest of his life with(even if that has taken 9 years on his part to realise, but to be fair, these 9 years are very special times in anyone's lives as people will invariably have a change in mindset of goals in lives - which in turn would affect the kind of partner they want - as they move from adolescence to adulthood), or maybe, he is just bored with the sort of routine you're going through and nothing more - and without knowing your bf and you personally, it is indeed quite hard for anyone to advise which category your bf falls under. Hence, I wouldn't like to speculate on the reason for your bf feeling the way he does because my guess would only be as good as yours...

    Nevertheless, I suppose you ought to only change if you think what he says makes sense(and you genuinely want to change) and not make those changes exclusively for him because he wants it and you want to salvage the relationship - it might work for the time being, but there is absolutely nothing to safeguard your future(as you might be tempted to think). If anything, it might actually be a cause of contention later on in your relationship(whether you're married to him or not) and might spark off more dissent. Basically, I don't believe anyone should change himself/herself solely to accomodate his/her partner - if anything, he should learn to accept you for who you're and if he can't, then I think it might be better you let this relationship go. As a last resort(and cruel as this may sound), I think you'd be better off living with 9 years of a relationship gone sour than a wrecked marriage that would haunt you later on.

    Then again, of course no one is in a better position to find out why your bf has had this sudden change of heart than you - perhaps, a heart-to-heart talk with him to iron things out might do wonders if this was simply a case of him getting bored with the mundane routine you've gotten up to for 9 years. At least, he has been honest enough with you to tell you what he feels - and hopefully, the chance he has provided for you two to work something out will prove sufficient to salvage your relationship...
