  • one36
    14 Apr 10, 06:36

    do u know why u fear ?

    generally, fear can either be real or baseless.  if the fear is real, try to pinpoint what could be the cause of the fear.  take precaution or manage it to eliminate the cause so that the fear will be removed. 

    if the fear is baseless, this is mostly due to our imagination or lack of knowledge.  in this case, talk to your coach or seniors and let them help you understand.  find out how best you can do to protect yourself.  take small steps in conquering your fear. 

    just to give an example - before jumping off 10 storeys, jump off 1st storey first, then 2nd storey, ... progressively.  build up your confidence and gauge how far you can go and how much your body can take.

    seek out martial arts community (forum) where there will be more people who can give you advice.

    hope it helps.

    Maybe the type of martial arts you learn> to you fall down is no good, but to some,is a false move in order to go for the kill.

    Most martial arts teach "stand-up" fighting, they dun really teach "ground" fighting and are for "points system" during tornement.In real life, do you think one will not fall down?

    Example: Muay thai teach you knee the ribs due to many restrictions. If me, i knee my opponent's private parts. Aikido tell you go in, twist the hand, make ur emeny fall, if me, i grap the hand (do 180 degree turn), put his elbow above my shouder, i break his arm.

    I have friends that are instructors in martial arts, but what they teach during class are not what they use in real life, reason being> In real pratical world, is use the easiest,fastest,most lethal way to KO ur opponent.

    Many will teach how to excute a technic, but never show/teach how or what to do in order for you to let you opponent to give you the chance to use that technic.

    During tornement or real fight is timing issue, reaction, practice & practice.
    An example: Last year, got a competition, is Muay Thai VS TKD in singapore.
    TKD is black belt 4 Dan(iirc),36 yrs old instructor, 76kg. 
    Muay thai opponent only learn 6 months, age 27, 72kg.

    TKD guy use double turn back thrust of right leg, Muay thai guy avoid.(Timing)
    TKD guy jump up, come down, fast 180 degrees turn to left leg kick, Muay thai guy avoid.(Reaction)
    TKD guy again jump up, mid air turn right leg kick, Muay Thai guy bend down then do a high sweep kick.TKD guy fall flat on face(Timing, Reaction + practice).

    Another SG chinese guy (heard he learn 5 years shang shou).
    Facing a TKD brown belt guy (Indian).(Tis guy practice hard, good conditioning)
    This TKD guy is a clever guy, use brain to fight.Chinese guy charge in> Indian guy act fall down, but he back thrust using heel kick the chines guy chin, Chines guy KO.
    (Timing, practice)

    Learn martial arts is only learn the style, to really use it is HAVE to practice.
    In real world, is using brain & how to use the fastest leathal way to KO opponent.

    Read up books on variouse martial arts, you will notice some technic from martial arts A is use to counter martial arts B.
