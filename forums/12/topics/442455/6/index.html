    i need help with problems regarding my neighbour as well. it's a really long story, i'll try to summarize it in one sentence:

    "they like to spray bleach into my house, but we don't have solid evidence of them in the act"

    if you have time to spare, you may like to read on:

    i've stayed at my house since i was born, i'm already 20. then, my old neighbour (whom have not once complained about my family's behaviour during their stay) sold their house last year. the new flat owner rented the entire unit to several families, whom apparently aren't locals.

    initially, they started off cooking curry almost every hour of the day. Mind you, it's not nice smelling curry like my other malay neighbour makes. it's super pungent and unpleasant to the nose. like the curry got burnt and what's cooking is the aluminum from the wok. i suspect they do not flush after using the washroom as well, since there seem to be an accumulation of ammonia smell coming from the bathroom's drain. besides that, when hanging out the clothes using bamboo poles, they LOVE to use theirs to hit ours, which is rather gross. in addition, there is noise every night after midnight. it's not continuous-- we can easily call the police for that. it sounds like people using the washing board to wash clothes, and then random thumping noises. we stay above them, doesn't make sense for us to hear their walking/ running etc. idk but it feels like they have been using bamboo poles to hit their ceiling. they also like to burn incense, the stick form in their house, and have a pot of incense at the stairs between our 2 levels. the smell is overwhelming, but we understand. for religious reasons we tolerate that.

    BUT the previous actions mentioned above, we do not understand.  i don't think we deserve all these nonsense when we didn't even cause disturbance to them in any way. even if we did, they could have come up and speak to us, right? there's only my mum at home usually, how much trouble can she create? so, we spoke to them nicely several times. the wives who stay at home in the day don't speak english, and just answers "no, no, no", to whatever we say. fine, we wait for their husbands to come home. we spoke to them several times, okay good, they understand where we are coming from, say they'll try to restrict their actions (eg. when cooking close the kitchen windows a little, try to keep the bathroom clean, dun poke the bamboo poles too high when keeping their laundry...)

    AND IT COMES BACK WITHIN ONE WEEK. AND WORSE OFF EACH TIME. NEW "PATTERNS" APPEAR. AND THE NUMBER OF PEOPLE STAYING THERE INCREASES.

    i applaud them for their creativity, seriously. from incense burning, they upgrade to air freshener. the cheapo smelly kinds. spray out of their window. okay, i have no evidence of that but i can hear the nozzle sprays loudly when i stand by the window and the smell diffuses into my house. and next thing is all windows in my house closed, all fans turned on, main door opened to let the smell out. and the cooking smell appears with increasing frequency, everytime my mum puts out our laundry. and the noise levels up to door slamming, and table dragging etc. they blame it on their kids. 

    it's getting out of hand. the neighbour 2 storeys below us got affected as well. we moved on to writing to the flat owner through post (not sure if he saw the letters). we went to HDB (to check if the rental is illegal, since the owner does not stay in the house, and it's rented to a few foreign families), then to town council for the daily incense burning along the corridor, it's a fire hazard especially when there are many kids in my block. then to the police for the noise. then also to the meet the peoples' session to speak to the MP.

    BUT TO NO AVAIL. the people sent to handle this case so happens not to do their job competently. we understand, they do have their limits.

    the people from hdb cant possibly catch them for illegal renting, when they came we peeked at the neighbour, they refused to open the door. after awhile, we see a man, supposedly the flat owner coming, and then the person from hdb came again. ah, the owner stays in that unit! like real. but we dont have evidence of him not staying there! we told the hdb officer, he insisted on his observations.

    the people from town council. HO HO HO. for the incense, yes, it's a sensitive issue. but what they did was to stick a weak piece of paper on the wall above the incense pot between our 2 storeys. it looks familiar. i have the exact same one at home, just that it's not pasted on the wall. i think every year during the chinese tomb-sweeping festival every household gets that.

    the police, i know, the noise, you cant really catch it, it's on and off. we don't expect you to send someone to stay overnight with us to check it out. maybe just get someone to issue a simple letter? and they just told us that they're too busy. yes it's a trival matter compared to catching serial murderers. but in singapore, really? in a neighbourhood police post, really?

    then they upgrade again. this time to spraying bleach during meal times. i'm really at a loss what to do. my mum's affected emotionally, so thinks that they're opposing her deliberately.

    i understand we must try to accept people's differing ways of life. but shouldn't they also try to be more open minded and change their habits a little?

    any smart people out there can offer me some suggestions to tackle this issue? or are we over reacting in the sense that all these are common?
