    You may have disagreements now, but there may be a point in your life later when you feel your time with her is limited.. so if there's anything you can do to improve the relationship, now's a good time to start. I can't stress more on this

    For me, my dispute with my mum got so bad that I moved out with my then-boyfriend (now my fiance). Only recently, when my mom found 2 lumps in different parts of her breast, did I realise how silly it was to hold on to all those quarrels .. it wasn't just a problem with her... it was also me. All I needed to do was open up. I was VERY nervous when I moved back with her, fearing a reprise of the past.. but all she had to say was, "Welcome home." and that 5-year old barrier just dissolved.

    Try taking more initiative to communicate / bond with your mom, eg :

    - update her about small events of your life without her prompting..
    - ask HER more frequently about what's she's up to/ her current interests, just sit n listen if and when you have the time..
    - make small gestures like offering to buy food home for her.
    - If you don't have the time, try to end the conversation on a positive note..
    - if she nags, just brush it off laughingly and try to gear the conversation towards any of the above points.

    Force yourself to view the situation from a different angle. Dun see it as she's wasting your time / trying to make life hard for u / all her fault or it will show in your body language.. when mums feel like you care, they will trust you more.. and tend to nag less

    If you're wondering why she's like that, it's most likely because she is still struggling to accept the changes in you starting from your teen years.. It's quite a common thing. If U keep avoiding/ignoring/being unpleasant with mum, things will really snowball. So guide her along patiently, help her to help you.

    Best of luk 2u & ur mum
