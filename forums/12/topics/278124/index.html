    Comprehension is the understanding and interpretation of what is read. To be able to accurately understand written material, need to be able to

    1) decode what they read;
    2) make connections between what they read and what they already know; and
    3) think deeply about what they have read.

    One big part of comprehension is having a sufficient vocabulary, or knowing the meanings of enough words.

    Note: Your C6 score in English probably is one factor you don't do well in Comprehension.

    Readers who have strong comprehension are able to draw conclusions about what they read Â– what is important, what is a fact, what caused an event to happen, which characters are important. Thus comprehension involves combining reading with thinking and reasoning.

    If vision is not the problem,
    you can,

    Use outlines, maps, and notes when you read.

    Read stories or passages in short sections and make sure you know what happened before you continue reading.

    Ask yourself, "Does this make sense?" If it doesn't, reread the part that didn't make sense.

    Read with a buddy. Stop every page or so and take turns summarizing what you've read.

    As you read, try to form mental pictures or images that match the story.

    Don't jump and answer the question at the first reading.
    Think for a while.
    Do a second take.

    Put the box on the table by the window in the kitchen
    is an ambiguous sentence.
    It could mean any of the following:

    1. Put the box onto the table that is by the window in the kitchen.
    Put the box / on the table by the window in the kitchen.

    2. Take the box that is on the table and put it by the window in the kitchen.
    Put , the box on the table / by the window in the kitchen.

    3. Take the box off the table that is by the window and put it in the kitchen.
    Put (the box on the table by the window) in the kitchen.

    Read slowly and think.
