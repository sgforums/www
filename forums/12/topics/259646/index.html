    Hmm first of all, you WILL have to go to court, divorce is a legal process, it is not just a matter of signing documents so simple.

    Btw , could you state your mum's reasons for getting a divorce? More details would help.

    The four allowable reasons are:

    1. Adultery
    2. Unreasonable Behavior
    3. 3 year separation with consent
    4. 4 year separation without consent

    This are basically the 4 reasons the court will accept for granting a divorce.

    The court will decide the ancillary matters before the divorce is finalized, ie, division of matrimonial assets (House, Car, properties etc) and custody of children (You). The court will look at the contributions of the parties in the division of assets. From your above explanation, where your dad has not contributed to the house or the family, he will probably get very little if any money from the sale of the house.

    The monies from the sale of the house will first go towards paying back the amount oweing to CPF, then the profits if any shall go back to you.

    Your dad will also have to pay maintenance to support both your mum and yourself.

    No matter what, you will need a lawyer to draw up the relevant forms etc. I really think you should get some legal aid. If your monthly income is < $800, the legal aid bureau will be able to give you substantially subsidized legal advice.

    Do take a look at their webby

    http://app.minlaw.gov.sg/lab/info.asp
