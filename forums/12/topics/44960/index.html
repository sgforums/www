    Everyone has different relationship, has different love experience. Some people had never experience the sweetness of a BGR before and often wonder what is it like to be in one.

    Hey, you never been in a relationship before - how do you know it is sweetness and not bitterness? You can't judge a relationship by the number of smooches they did in public, neither you can judge by how sweet his/her words with one another.

    Lonely? Or pure influence from media and friends?

    Throughout your entire life - Love is divided into 2 different category. One is fated, the other is mutual fate. Astrologically speaking, fated refers to regrets, your marriage, and other relation that you truely feel for them. Mutual fate (Free-willed) refers to relation you don't have to enter.

    How do you know whether which relationship you don't have to enter? My words are: How well do you truely know yourself? What do you seek in a partner?

    This hunk/babe that you have fallen for - it could be a girl-next door or a nerdy guy - do you think he/she is suitable for you? Liking and suitability are two different thing altogether.

    Ok finally you landed yourself into a relationship - what's next?

    Everyone relationship has its own unique life cycle - shaped by environmental external factors and the two parties, driven purely by their effort in it. When a relationship is born, at the point of time both of you agree to be together, both of you have signed a invisble contract - which state the risk involved of having a relationship. You both agreed, therefore never question why is there hurt when things happened, unless you do the same for every happiness you experienced.

    Every relation has their reason to be there: mostly its learning of cosmic lessons. Nobody is born into this world knowing how to chase, love or even maintain a relation. Everyone has to start from somewhere - the trick is how fast you learn the trade. Some people only need one relation to gain as much maturity/lessons/etc as others who may need a couple to as many as twenty or even more.

    So people, how many do you need?
