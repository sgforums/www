    Heartbroken,

     

    I suggest you start with finding out for yourself what is a'meaningful life'. You can't start running a race if you do not know where is the end point. Once you have a goal, then you can start making plans on how to achieve that goal. Finding out about yourself and your goal/s is a process of life everyone goes through. Most people do it a few times in their life. There is no shortcut to this. If you don't do it, you'll find yourself going through life aimlessly. A meaningful life is not what others or society tell you is about. You have to find out and decide what it is yourself. Age in on your side. Widen your horizon. Get to know people. Try new things. Seek new experience.

    Yes, low self esteem might be an issue. However, don't be fooled into thinking that being well-dressed, having the 6-pack lean body, earning big bucks and being popular with the opposite sex are the answers. If this is the case, many people in the world have serious issues. People who have healthy esteems understand their strengths and weaknesses, know what their goals are, and have good relationships/supports. You don't need to be rich, good-looking and well-dressed to be happy, although I acknowledge that those attributes definitely helps.
