    I've got people I love who are smokers, too.
    But alas, it is their choice to smoke, ultimately, not ours.

    We can only offer our support to them.

    Some suggestions:

    • Maybe you can talk to her one day: sit down with her and tell her that you're not judging her or anything, but say that you worry about her health and that her smoking makes you afraid that she is compromising her health.
    • Take it from your experiences. Say you smoke too, but you realise that you harming yourself will be hurting her as well, hence you want to quit for the both of you.
    • Offer to quit together with her, and say that you'll be quit buddies and give each other emotional and moral support.
    • Give her something to look forward to. Tell her that because you think this is a good idea to make you guys even closer by quitting together, that you are planning a surprise to celebrate the 10th week of quitting. Every week of being off the cigarette successfully, bring her out somewhere nice (no need to be somewhere pricey, but make it meaningful, fun or romantic) and tell her how much you appreciate her love and support.

    I think the point is that you shouldn't try to be too hard or full on when it comes to making her quit or saying hurtful things like "You so smelly when you smoke" or "Smoking is wrong you know!", and try to offer understanding and support.

    Ultimately it is her choice, but there are many ways you can help her to make that decision to quit.

    Once you have quit, you will look back and think, why did I even bother to think twice before quitting? Its true!

    Good luck.
