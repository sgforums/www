    Generally, contracts in Singapore do not have to be in writing although it is always good to have it written down on paper. But the issue here is that this transaction is made between friends (i.e non-strangers), the law does not recognise social/domestic transactions that do not show an intention to create a legal relationship - it is likely that this "promise" is not legally enforceable. So you may not have a legal basis to sue him or commence litigation in Court.

    Secondly, if he is already declared bankrupt (by law), there will be other creditors (eg. banks) that are after him. They will get a share of his assets first, before you.
