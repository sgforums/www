    "If misery loves company, misery has company enough."
    - Henry David Thoreau

    In the book "Love in the Time of Cholera", there is a particularly memorable line, one that remained etched in my heart until today:

    "... he looked at her and saw her naked to her waist, just as he had imagined her. Her shoulders were wrinkled, her b reasts sagged, her ribs were covered by a flabby skin as pale and cold as a frog's."

    The protagonist had endured fifty-one years, nine months, and four days of unrequited love before he finally had the opportunity to profess again his love for the woman he had loved since his youth. When she finally accepted him after a series of vehement rejections, when their love was finally consummated after a chain of fateful setbacks, his love still remained true but the subject of his love since youth was no longer the same.

    You can devote as many years as you are blessed with to the love of your life, but you must be made painfully aware of the fact that though your love may still remain true after those years, the person whom you love would never be the same again as she whom you started loving before those years.

    In the end, you may realise that all your life you have been in love with just an image. Nothing more.
