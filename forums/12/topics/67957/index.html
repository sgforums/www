    How many times, when we fell in love, we felt this uncontrollable need to have the person? Face it, its like a virus; you can't stop your love from multiplying. You feel that this extraordinary person makes you feel good when he/she is around and his/her presence lingers even if he/she vanish from your sight.

    You couldn't contain yourself. You did the expressing.

    Your worst nightmare happened - he/she rejected you.

    So what? We hide in your favourite corner and weep over your failure. We refuse meetings from our good buddies and prefer to remain alone for a moment.

    In the midst of our reflecting; 'how many times have we actually smile and respect the good decision of the other person?'

    The kind of love we give - is it the kind of love they want?

    In real love you want the other person's good,
    In romantic love you want the other person

    Cheers

    I agree with Yunhaier .

    "In real love you want the other person's good,
    In romantic love you want the other person "
    Loving someone is to support her unconditionally.
    Love her without thinking of anything in your mind.
    And definitely not trying to impress her with the things you do.

    Very often, we always think that what we do is a token of love
    but is it what the other party is feeling? Probably not.
    If the other party doesn't feel the love, it is not considered love.

    Love doesn't mean that you can do anything for her and think that she will be happy.
    It is only in the mind of the giver and not the receiver.
    Love is doing something that she can feel it in comfort and not pressure.

    A good relationship makes us feel energized.
    And a bad one make us feel stress out and tired.
