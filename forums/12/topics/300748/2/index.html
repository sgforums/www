Only you would know. Like they say, 'nobody knows what goes on behind closed doors'. Including myself and the other good people in the forum. You only know the answer. And you have to find that answer within yourself.

He was upfront to tell you that he needs 2 years to make his money. He could have not said anything at all. Maybe he is buying time or maybe he really is trying. However I agree with the guys in the forum that you should NOT pay his way for him. However IF he is NOT leeching on you, and asking you to only be patient with him because he cannot afford to give you the lifestyle you want, that is a different story.

    I'm saying all this from my perspective. Here's why I symphatise for 'R'. I was broke for 3 years. My wife and I lived hand to mouth. We got married, had a kid, but still no money in the bank. No savings/investments etc. Now my wife didn't pay my way, but she did rough it out with me. Don't get it wrong, my wife has a good paying job. Just not enough to pay for 2. All this happened in 2003. It's 2008 now. My financial situation has improved. But it would not have been possible without her 'support'. Sure, she complained a lot along the way. But she hung on. Because she loved me. And she knew I loved her. Put it this way, Not all of us have the luxury of financial freedom. But as long as you try to get there, shouldn't you be given some credit for it?     All this said, you have already told him that it is over. I just hope that the next relationship you find, you will find happiness. And please....please...do not rush into so much commitment so quickly. Enjoy your partner's company. Enjoy the relationship. Be monogamous of course. But don't start sharing the bills (Or taking over the bills in your case) so quickly.

    If however, this relationship does work out, you have to put your terms down. on the flipside, you'd have to hear out what his terms are too. And if his terms is to give him time, you'd have to give him time...not $$.
