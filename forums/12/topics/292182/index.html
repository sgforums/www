    Originally posted by joilin:

    It all depends on whether there's method to the madness. While I was at univeristy I worked through a number of part-time jobs, making sure each one was different from the last, and for a short period after graduating I continued the strategy to widen my breadth of experience. It's worked in my favour because the potential employers that I wanted to work with all had a focus on developing their staff, and because of their development focus, they recognised my strategy when reviewing my work history. As such, it became a self-sorting process because I wasn't interested in working for someone who was going to pigeonhole me.
