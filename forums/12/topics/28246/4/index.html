    Woo, glad to see pple so interested in music though Jrock aint my cup of tea. Grunge and rock chills my world. Well I am a vox in my band so i pretty know nuts about drums but hanging around and jamming with my drummer, i can offer my 3 cents worth.

    Firstly, as someone mentioned, learn your basics. its impt. knowing the theories is one thing, applying them is another.

    Second, know what u wan. obviously, for a beginner, if u gonna listen so widely from Blink 182 to some chinese band drummer, u aint gonna get far. Try something that u really like, practice it, loosen ur limbs, once u get the hang of ur limbs moving individually and able to pull thru a song pretty well, only then its time to listen widely and learn other styles.

    Thirdly, yeah jam. My band also started jamming and make mistakes, learn, thats why we get a chance to play in public nowadays. we are glad we make mistakes.

    Fourthly, passion.self explanatory.

    Fifthly, creativity. As a drummer, many noes the theories but how do we differentiate a good drummer and an average one? the creativity. Basically, a drummer who can express what the song wants to express is a good drummer. Creativity doesnt mean playing more but simply to play in a way that will bring out the essence of the song. Example, you can hardly use the style of Jazz to play Rock, it simply does not fit the songs. I believe that a song is a human body. Just like a human body, there are many layers, from the organs, to the bones, to our outer body. A song also needs to be treated the same way. Instruments must build layer by layer. When u play, think how best can you build the body.

    Lastly, peserverance. nobody becomes a damn good drummer overnite. practice and if after 10 years u still cant make it, then at least it has been a hobby..:p

    Hope that helps although it seems that senseless yakking to me...
