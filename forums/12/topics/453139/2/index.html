    Of course, its discouraging. But can we do anything about that? No. Because there will always be bullies that give us a hard time.

    One way to avoid that is going thru interviews. At the interview, you can ask them back to find out what kind of bosses you will be working with. And find out about their HR Policy whether it protects staff to express themselves. Also before joining that company, try asking any of your friends working there how its like.

    Once you start working there, its hard to turn back. Maybe you think the easy way out is to switch jobs. But chances are, you may end up jumping from a hot pot to a frying pan. You haven't build up your career where you are and suddenly you decide to leave in 6 months or one year, you hardly learn anything. New employers will look over your resume and find that you are a job hopper. When they interview and ask you what you learn in your previous job, you can't say much because you only worked there for 1 year.

    I attended interviews and often they ask me why I only worked there for 3 years? I tell you, even when I switch job few times and each job is within 3 years, they see me as a job hopper. If you switch job every 1 year or 2 years, surely they will focus on that.

    So before one accepts a job, do your homework and find out more about them before you accept the job.
