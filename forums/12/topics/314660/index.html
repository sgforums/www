    deja_vu88, I'm also partially hearing-impaired. I have lost the hearing ability of a few frequencies/pitch/tone since young. :)

    I agreed that being partially hearing-impaired does has it's own disadvantages and sometimes it's also quite frustrating trying to figure out what the other person is talking. However, I also learnt to overcome this weakness by being more outspoken. Since I can't quite make out what the other person is trying talking sometimes, I learnt to ask OPEN-ENDED questions to others and myself. You see, most questions encountered in a normal conversation are usually limited to a certain number of replies. So what I did was only to figure out briefly what the other person's reply was and I'll follow up with another opinion+question. I was able to figure out the reply briefly cos I wasn't completely deaf....I could still listen to a song but I would never be able to figure out what the singer is singing without reading the lyrics.


    Frankly speaking, I have tried to explain my hearing distress to many of my close friends and sometimes new friends. But they were all in disbelief of my hearing-impairment. Haven't found a single person who could have the empathy to understand and doesn't discriminate against my hearing handicap. Anyway, also thanks to all these people that caused me to find strength within myself to overcome this deficiency.

    I'm not wearing any hearing aids and have never wear any of them and will never wear any of them. I totally dislike the idea of wearing them and I'm happier when my world is more quiet than normal people.

    It's not easy to live in this world without the sense of hearing awareness. But I believed there are always ways and methods that I could find within myself to overcome all these obstacles and live a normal life like anyone else. And I would also encourage you to do the same too.


    Good Luck and all the best to whatever decision you decided to take. :)
