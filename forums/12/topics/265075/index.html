    This is a classic example of the "It's me, not you mentality". In my book, the Classic Examples of Relationships with us Teenagers, i clearly outlined the symptoms of mentality among us teenagers to young adults we face. Amongst these is this classic example. She cites the lack of communication as the cause of her desire to be in the relationship. Ask yourself the following :

    1. Out of ten, how much do you rate your communication with her?

    2. Out of ten, how much do you rate her communication with you?

    The perfect ratio should always be more than 1. If it's less than 1, then i'm afraid that the relationship shouldn't be pursued further. Why? You may ask...

    Simply put,

    She is finding excuses to get out of the relationship. She yearns to seek "greener pastures". She wants to find better experiences. She is bored with you. She finds you more of a roadkill and ur irritating the heck outta her.

    Apply whichever is applicable.

    She just wanna exploit the lack of communcation as the "Get Out" clause in the r/s.

    First of all, give her some time. Try and remove her from your mind and thoughts. In my book, i state the different post relationship acitivities that you can consider when forgetting about the ex. Give yourselves a week and tell yourself not to contact her. If she still loves you, she will contact u first. 95% of all relationships i surveyed where the girl initiated a cooling off period first doesnt contact the guy at all first. and almost 100% of the 95% have a short lived patching up.

    Move on and find someone else better.

    --Author of the Classic Examples of Relationships with us Teenagers
