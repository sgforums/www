  • airgrinder

    If you're getting into personal financial consulting better make sure you do it good and do it well. Both the banks offer a basic pay on top of your com. You can do it extremely well and jump to be their private banking relationship managers (RMs) (handles customers with at least US$1mil net worth) or you can jump ship to other banks when they're head hunting. A large number of a local bank's private RMs jump to a foreign bank last year, causing a near destabilisation in the private banking arm. They brought a large number of their customers away too.

    Also a number of officers from a foreign bank jump to a local bank in late 2005, causing the foreign bank to be overworked in that particular dept. The foreign bank had to issue a warning through MAS to the local bank not to "dig" their staff away.

    People who started in financial consulting but did not do well are generally "not well received" by some banks and FAs. One can still get a job, but usually not something managerial, where the career could be to a VP or SVP.
