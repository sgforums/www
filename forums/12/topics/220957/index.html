    You're obviously in no state to get married as things stand(from your perspective, at least), so I suppose the least you could do is postpone your marriage plans until you've straightened your thoughts?

    While I don't think I'm in a position to give you any advice relating to your predicament, I think there're a few things you ought to seriously consider. Afterall, a marriage is for a lifetime.

    If you genuinely think your "crush" is "The One"(for whatever reason unbeknown to me), then perhaps it would be worthwhile to give this a shot regardless of the fact he's attached. The point is, if his current relationship was built on a sound foundation and he doesn't have the slightest romantic interest in you, there's no hope of you ever breaking them up. On the other hand, if your conjecture proves right(or at least, you wish that to be true) and the both of you indeed view each other as "The One"(admittedly remote chance of that being the case in a realistic world), then obviously a lifetime of happiness is worth the transient misery you're currently confronted with.

    That said, of course this would be a gamble as there's no doubt irreparable and irrevocable damage would be done to your present relationship if you so choose to pursue your unspoken course of action. At the end of the day, it's only you and you alone who will be in any position to weigh up the risk assessment and decide which is the more viable option for you - on one hand(if you stick with your current boyfriend), you could be left wandering "what if" for life; on the other hand(should you break up with your current boyfriend), you might look back in years to come(assuming your relationship with your crush doesn't work out as you fantasise) on passing up a secure future which you're comfortable with...

    To be fair, I don't think it's ever easy for anyone to choose between his/her dreams and what needs to be done in reality and while I'm not encouraging/discouraging either course of action, I hope I've at least put things in a sense of perspective for you to better consider what you should do. Last but not least, it'd certainly help if you assessed the whole situation rationally(ie.you should be dead certain it isn't just a mere crush that is giving you serious thoughts about breaking up with someone whom you've spent 10 years with. Then again, neither should you make up your mind and commit a lifetime to your current boyfriend solely on the exclusive premise that you've been with him for 10 years and feel duty-bound to do so) before acting.

    Good luck whatever course of action you choose to pursue.
