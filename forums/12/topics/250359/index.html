    When writing a GP essay, to at least get a B or a C standard essay, always follow this rough structure:

    Point of view on issue.

    What are your reasons for supporting the issue?

    Then for each reason, use this outline:

    - Topic sentence (a short sentence which says quickly what you want to say like "Cars should be banned because they pollute the air" )

    - Elaboration (eg "Pollution of the air causes health problems which may result in higher medical costs for the public" )

    - A 'closing-up' statement (eg "Due to the possible air pollution resulting from cars, we should ban them" )

    Then after you have elaborated and listed all your reasons, just state a conclusion (eg "In conclusion, cars should be banned because they bring great harm to our society and offers little or no benefits in return" )

    Also, try to stick to simple words that are clear but concise in transmitting your meaning to the reader. Instead of having sentences like "Cars are the omnipresent scourge that should be purged from the very society we live in", try writing "Cars, whose presence can be felt everywhere, is a source of harm to our society and should be removed"

    When writing a piece of writing, remember that it must sound logical. Flair can come in as an accessory to decorate your writing but logic must always come first. With a logical and concisely written piece of work, you cannot go very wrong with it.

    As for PW preliminary ideas, just use the above guideline with the topic being the topic you chosen and the reasons are just the ideas you have for that particular topic.

    For GP comprehension, always look out for topic sentences. Often, a chunk of words can just be summarised into the topic sentence. Highlight them and you should get a pretty good idea of what the whole passage is talking about. Do not fret if others get it faster than you. The most important thing is to understand it yourself.

    Also, if you feel that your vocabulary is a bit weak, try having a habit of reading newspapers everyday. Pay extra attention on the "Insights/Review" section. This section have commentary articles which usually highlight a pertinent issue and offer relevant examples which you can use in your GP essays. The other articles are also worth to read. If really in doubt of any new word you do not understand, try going to google and "define: {insert unknown word here}" like "define: quagmire". If all else fails, just fall back to your trusty dictionary.

    You are still in J1 and it is not too late to start getting used to practising good writing. Work on the basics first and make small but stead progress towards a logical, fluent, coherent and concise piece of writing before going on to higher levels like adding a bit of your individual flair and balancing your arguments with opposing views.

    and yes of course, when answering questions, ask yourself, what do the person asking want to know, or rather, if you asked that question, what do you want to know.
