    Hi,

    sorry to hear of the development. I am not sure what to say. I understand the hurt and the pain that you are experiencing now. Its certainly something that you dont even wish it on your worst enemies.

    I guess for the time being the only thing to do is really to ensure that you continue to remain healthy by sleeping enough hours and eating properly. Once you have these 2 things sorted out your body and mind will allow you to continue to fight for what you want - financial freedom and to rebuild whatever you can of your relationship. So staying healthy is now even more important than ever.

    Hope you get a job soon, that will not only provide you with the much needed $ but also gives you some distractions and at the same time something to focus on so that your mind will not continue to wander. What may be damaging for your emotional health at this moment is if you do not having something worthwhile to focus on.

    So dont let your mind wander about and go into an argument within your own head about the possibilities and what you would have done different and the maybes and the regrets etc. You recognise your error. You have asked God for forgiveness, you have repented now forgive yourself and not continue beating yourself to death. Like we discussed before, there is a difference between recognising repenting regreting and not allowing yourself to be forgiven and keep beating yourself. The former is healthy the latter not. The latter is something that we use to actually make ourselves feel "good" by punishing ourselves with guilt and pain. Sure we will always feel bad. That is only natural but the question is for how long and how do we handle this regrets? If we allow it to grow and grow without learning any lessons from it without repenting and forgiving ourselves, it may consume us bit by bit. If we dont take an active step to deal with this, it becomes sort of like a compensatory mechanism that we use to make us feel "good" in the sense that we know that we have done wrong and deserves to be punished and so we punish ourselves and in doing so, we kinda feel good bec we have not escape the justice of being punished. sorry I wish I can describe it better but I hope you know what I mean.

    Anyway, some people do not allow themselves to move beyond the punishment stage. This will not bring any lasting change but as you may recognise in some people they made a mistake years before and they are still beating themselves up over and over and over. In time, you have to move on. Recognise the fact that we make mistakes, small and big ones! some are not so costly others are so expensive that we can ill afford to make but we are humans and we do make mistakes. regrets without forgiving yourself ie the beating yourself up will often not lead to any long term change within yourselve so it is not a good thing to do.

    So the first thing to do is to do something constructive with your time and your energy. This will then help you not to allow your mind to wander and wonder. With something useful and beneficial to do and focus your mind on, you will be able to move on and recover better.

    Concerning counseling, it will be great if your wife will agree to go for counseling to help her sort out her emotions and all the confused tots. Good if you can go together but not necessary. what she needs right now is to somehow clear the load that is in her heart and mind. The same goes for yourself, if you can go talk to someone as well. Call the numbers like the Samaritans and ask them if they know of any counseling services that you or your wife can attend.

    In the mean time, keep in touch with your wife. dont be pushy. show her by your own actions and the change in yourself (genuine change) that you are taking responsibilities for your FUTURE. Remember all this has to come from within you and motivated by your genuine desire to take hold of your future and that will power you on. Otherwise, if your motivation is purely her alone, and when she does not respond like we discuss the last time, you will become discouraged and will stop thereby plunging yourself deeper into depression.

    I know its alot to do and it does take a heck of a lot of energy to keep up. That is why you need to regulate your vegetative function of sleeping and eating right. That will be the baseline to provide you with that foundation to do what you need to do to sort out your current state!

    It is unfortunate that as this is a written forum it has all the limitation of the written word. As such, I hope that what I have set out above is not too muddled and is of some use to you. I hope the prinicples I have set out isnt too vague and also please dont expect yourself to bounce back and recover immediately. It will take time for you to cry your own heart out and to sort out your own emotions and thoughts. So dont give yourself unrealistic expectations and pressure. take it easy and move at a pace that you are comfortable with.

    Take care.
