    I'm doing a design course in Temasek Polytechnic now- if you would look around, you can easily find out who I am.

    Anyway- the designing industry may be very different from what you think it is about. Yes, designing is about creating. But it is also about meeting deadlines, sacrificing your design to please the client, countless sleepless nights.

    All these will wear people down, if you don't have passion in it.

    My lecturer said this to me:
    "There are three groups of people in the designing industry. One group has passion and talent- they are easily recognisable, and they are those "elites".

    Another group has talent, but no passion- they waste their talents away. The last group has passion, but no talent- they have to work doubly hard to reach the mark of the first group."

    You need to be really passionate about what you're doing.

    As for the million dollar question of "money or interest":

    What is your goals in life? Is creating and revolutionising of a higher priority than having good food, driving in a BMW, living in a private housing?

    Originally posted by blu_sky:
    Times have changed. which century are you still living in?

    In this age that we are living, there are designers making big bucks. They are making it big even decades ago eg Tommy Hilfiger and Christian Dior (in the 40s and 50s). In this age of opportunities, even a famous hairstylist can earn more than a mediocre lawyer or accountant. Dont believe? Ask our local stylist, David Gan.

    The number of high-earners for such careers are typically smaller than those for traditionally high-earning ones. I'm not saying that you can't earn big bucks doing design, I'm just saying the chances are more limited.

    The chances are higher for you to become a consultant for doctors, or to make partner for lawyers or accountants than for you to make it big as a designer and ean big bucks.

    IMHO, the main reason designers don't earn as much is because companies are generally unwilling to take the risk. Design is a rather unquantifiable (revenue matching wise) and subjective. It can make or break the product and ultimately the company. There have been cases whereby companies have gambled and won. Case in point, Apple v Creative. Creative came up with the concept of a HDD-based Mp3 player 1st. Apple just made it look desireable.

    But, money does govern and limit businessess and so every dollar is monitored to maximize it's benefit. It's with those unquantifiables, the indeterminates that companies are a little fearful. R&D, advertising and design fall into that category. Fortunately though, with Apple's success, companies tend to be more receptive to design these days. So there is still hope.
