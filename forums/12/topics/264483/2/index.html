    Suicide is choice, like everything else. Yes, it doesn't solve many problems. It hurts your kin, and taints your own memory. The only thing it does is for the person; for him/her, the pain is over. But this pain is forced onto others.

    However, to call a person "stupid" because he chose to end his life is uncalled for. These people have lost their way, and it is the responsibility of the people around him to bring him back. Comments like "Why are you so stupid?' and "You so dumb go hurt yourself for what?" are extremely hurting to one who is already suffering. Comparing his suffering to the suffering of others (like kids in Africa, starving people) will possibly make him feel worse and drive him off the edge. I would know because this is what many have done to me before. Shocking a person with harsh words is a gamble that should not be made casually, because the odds of losing are very high.

    Empathy, unconditional sympathy and kindness are the ways to remove the cancer of suicidal thoughts. Be kind to someone who has lost love, and he will see that he has not lost any at all. Be gentle with someone who has been hurt, because it will remove the pain in the least painful way. Be caring to those who need care, because they will then be able to care for themselves.
