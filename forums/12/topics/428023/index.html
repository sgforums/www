    I have taught NT, NA and Exp classes. 

    There is often only ONE factor separating all the students in these 3 streams.

    "Whether they studied harder when they were at a younger age." (PSLE)

     

    And once they enter their respective streams in secondary school, the 'self-fulfilling prophecy' kicks in. They're lumped together with either 'the cream' or the 'no-hopers'. This makes it especially difficult for those who've (so to speak) 'learned their lesson' and want to study harder to achieve more in their education.

    I've had to go down to Cantonment 5 times already, to speak up for 4 different students who needed my help at the police station there. All of them were NT kids.

    I've also had the privilege of teaching a young lady who returned to repeat her Sec 3NT year after being expelled. She turned her head on right-ways and ended up being of the top 5 students nationwide in her N(T) levels last year.

    I've never been ashamed of any of my NT students. They're bright, fun and have a more selfless side to them than many of the Exp kids I teach. 

     

    The ONLY problem I've had with MANY of my NT kids is the LAZINESS that many of them hold onto. (it persists for most until 4 months before their N-levels). 

     

    If your friend can get rid of any 'Laziness' he has inside him and pushes himself, then trust me... in a few years time and with hard work, the playing field will be LEVEL.

    University degree or not. 

    CJ
