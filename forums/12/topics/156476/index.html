    What everyone has said so far is very relevant and true.

    To really burn fats and see results, you'd have to burn more calories, and eat less of them.

    Essentially this means, more exercise, less food.

    To lose weight you need a balance of Cardio and Weights .. around 60:40 - 70:30 depending. Cardio will really burn your fat, but weights will build muscle that in turn consumes and burns my calories whenever you exercise. It also helps give you a leaner and more toned look. I'd say a good balance would be 4 days of cardio and 3 days of weights, alternating. I did post alot about this in another thread, but what I've just said is a very summarised version.

    There is no short cut method to weight loss. Infact, the term should be replaced with weight management because this is a lifelong commitment to good health.
