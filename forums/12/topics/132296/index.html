    16 May 05, 00:13
    Practice makes perfect. If you find it difficult to keep your eyes on the road and navigate at the same time, then drive only when your father or someone familiar with directions is beside you so that you could focus on the road conditions first. If a "vehicle commander" or navigator is not available, it may help if you'd check up the street directory and plan your route first using as many familiar roads as possible.

    Drive around Singapore more often and familiarise yourself with the roads, if you tend to slow down when reading road signs try to stick to the left lane in case you get horned by impatient drivers behind you. With time, you should become more confident such that you could just glance at the abundant road signs and be able to quickly plan your route while keeping alert on the road conditions.

    Even experienced drivers may still face problems navigating, so keep the street directory handy. Drive safely, and don't lose your head trying to impress the girl sitting beside you.
