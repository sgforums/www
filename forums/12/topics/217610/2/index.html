    I would recommend against renting over the Internet. The photos usually don't give you a good idea of what the place is really like, and you'll need to check out the neighbourhood personally - the last thing you want to do is rent the place and get stuck with noisy neighbours. I would suggest that you get temporary accommodations in early February at the latest, and then personally check out potential places to rent.

    Right now I would suggest that you just do your research on the net to get a feel of what prices are like. In the meantime, get your paperwork together! You will need a statement from the bank - it doesn't have to show figures, but it must include a statement to the effect that you (or whoever's backing you) have definitely got enough cash to cover the rent for the duration of your stay. Basically, any documentation that shows that you're a good bet for your landlord will work in your favour.

    Brunswick and Coburg are all right neighbourhoods, although I personally prefer Brunswick - it's slightly closer to Melbourne Uni.

    You've definitely made the right choice in avoiding the halls. if you want to get serious about your work, you don't need the distraction of having a couple of hundred students around you.
