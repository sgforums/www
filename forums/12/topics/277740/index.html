    alba it does sound to me like a panic attack, and if stress is getting to you as per the above persons post it would make sense. it doesn't neccesarily require a doc or drastic measures, just take the time to reduce your stress - there are a lot of things that can help - just a few suggestions:

    - Reduce caffeine intake if you drink lots of coffee or tea or soft drinks - caffeine can be a major factor when it comes to stress and panic attacks and if you drink a lot of tea, coffee or drinks like Coke which contain high concentrations of caffeine it bumps up your blood pressure significantly

    - Reduce the stressful situations you get into when you can, if doing something unneccesary stresses you out try not to do it, eg, taking a particular route to work, if theres a less stressful route that adds maybe 10 minutes to your journey time take that instead

    - If you smoke try to reduce the amount, I know that most smokers believe it calms them but Nicotine is a double edged sword, it acts both ways, it may take a few days or weeks to be noticable but significantly reducing the amount of cigarettes you smoke per day could have a big effect

    - Panic attacks and stress are predominantly psychological, if you can try to avoid getting to exploding point in your mind the chances are you won't have a panic attack and you'll feel a lot better. Things like counting to ten when you feel stress coming on, taking a short walk etc could help

    - If you can, take a holiday, get away from the things that stress you out and relax, you might find coming back to things with a more positive and relaxed mindset can help you get rid of it altogether.

    If it happens more regularly it would be wise to see a doctor, if nothing more than that chest pain can be very serious and when it's not obviously caused by something non-serious it should always be checked out by a doc. same as when you get whacked on the head a bit too hard.
