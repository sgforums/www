    Ok, LDR once again. In your situations, it's very difficult to see much future in your relationship because the odds are terribly overwhelming. You can only know if this relationship if for you ONLY after you have lived together for a period of time. Love is great, feeling is strong - but we must not let that influence and blind us to our risky future. You have to anticipate problems way before it arrivals and hits you in the face because your relationship is so fragile - you need to shield them from the hostile wind and build up a steady foundation before you expose them to 'experience'.

    You can attempt this relationship and your objective of this move has to be: Minimize possible risk. Culture difference can be respected and learned, but as for language, it's truely a feat on the strength of your relationship. There are some 5 pointers that you have to have before you can consider this relationship:

    I) I hope financially, you are independent and no problem with that because if that wasn't the case, I advise you to drop this relationship. Too much problems thrown in a relationship can instantly kill it before you can find ways to salvage.

    II) Mentally and emotionally you have to be 10 times more understanding and stronger. Temper has to be curb. Empathy place with intiative and decision making. Your mind and heart must be prepared and trained to see that this relationship WILL NOT be a smooth going one (like all true love). Rewards in this relationship can only be felt AFTER you spend mutual effort in maintaining and upkeeping this relationship.

    III) You have little choice; to understand culture, you have to begin by studying their language. You can go to my ex-school (www.ikoma.com.sg) Lack of communication or mis-communication are the top killers in relationship. See, when you wrote an email to her saying that this relationship may be a little difficult and everything, she became defensive and blasted back at you. THIS IS MIS-COMMUNICATION. Imagine if you were to have real life issues with her but you can't find a common ground to talk things out... this could mean certain death. You may want to say something, but she interpret them differently or vica vista.

    IV) An immediate jump to marriage is a no-no. I suggest if a title is critical, get engaged instead. In this way she could stay over for 3 - 6 months to get use to the life in here WITHOUT people talking. If you are taking foreign language class in say, Ikoma - there are many Japanese people there. At least when you can taking the class and she waiting for you to finish, she will get to know people of her own country - this minimize home sickness and establish networking.

    V) There is no room for 'what-if' WHEN YOU HAVE DECIDE TO PURSUE this relationship after considering Phrase I and II - be positive and ALWAYS employ the 'right' way to handle your own relationship. Drop previous bad habit (e.g. Break-patch, cold war, violence if applicable, etc). Both of you must share this common vision in your relationship before you can move to greater heights. Constantly encourage one another or even do little things to make the day happier.

    Personal: If I had my chance of that LDR... the yunhaier today would be so much different.......
