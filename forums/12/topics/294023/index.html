    Usually kids around age 2 is ready for some schooling.

    But not the full day ones, most parents put them in half days for a few days per week.

    It is only to get them used to the curriculum so they are used to the environment and will have an easier transition to full fledge schools like Kindergarten.

    Most likely, 2 year old curricullums are not as structured.
    And the best part about it is that they will have other children to interactive with.
    Children learn more from other children.

    Where I am, 3 years old is pre-school, and 2 year old are preliminaries/toddlers.

    It is actually better for your child to go for some school to start.

    In fact, the hardest part about letting a young child go to school is not with the child.
    Most parent or primary care givers will experience separation anxiety and will be hard to let her/him go the first few weeks.

    I've seen many adults cry at the first day of school.

    But don't worry, most children at that age will adapt well. It really depends on how the teachers transition them over, and the personality of a child.

    So choose the school carefully, what is most important is that the children in that school should be happy and enjoy going there.

    So yes, if financial is not a problem for you, it is better for your child to go.
    Children at this age are like sponges.
    You will be surprised how fast your child picks up skills.

    Although, what I cannot tahan is that they also picks up sickness from other kids real easy.
