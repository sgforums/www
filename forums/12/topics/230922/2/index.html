    Hi LOLS,

    I used to have severe acne too, that's why your thread attacted me.

    But it's clear now, and I have some best practices I'd like to share with you:

    1) Avoid the oily and heaty foods like prata, char quay teow, banana fritters, fast food, fries etc...

    Although the doctors say it doesn't make a difference, I swear to you it does. If you ask a TCM (Traditional Chinese Medicine) doctor, he'll tell you such foods produce pathogenic heat which increases sebaceous secretion and worsens acne.

    I have a friend who never believed what I told him and continued to eat all the fried and oily stuff.... today, 8 years later, he still has bad acne. And he's way out of puberty too.

    2) Intake cooling, greaseless, healthy foods like salads, veggies, fruits, tomato, cucumber, celery... make it a habit to blend your own fruit juice and drink that as a complete meal.

    This means you'll have to find a healthier alternative for a lot of the things you eat. A bit hard to find, but not impossible. There are always healthier choices available.

    3) Use a good face wash. And don't scrimp on using it. My friend bought this expensive $60 face wash and squeezes less than a dime each time.... NOT EFFECTIVE. Just get a $10 one and use it in copious amounts.

    4) Use acne medication, those you can get from a skin specialist.

    5) Don't squeeze. You can soak a small towel in hot water and place it on the pimple to draw the sebum to gather into a head. It'll take maybe two days of application every 3 hours. Once it's in a head (yellow dot of puss) then it's safer to squeeze, but I recommend you just let it burst by itself when you wash it.

    6) Use a facial mask to draw out the sebum at night. I've had good success with this brand called BLACKMORE's. Available at most pharmaceutical outlets.

    7) Drink genuine herbal cooling tea. The kind you buy from a chinese herbalist. Maybe Eu Yan Sang. This really helps to relieve pathogenic heat. OR, you can just blend 5 tomatoes and 3 sticks of celery and drink that.

    Cheers,
    Lance

    1. pour some boiled water (the one that's just off the stove and really hot) into a bowl of size slightly larger than your face,

    2. position your face above the bowl and cover your head with a towel (so the steam goes to your face instead of evaporating into the surroundings).
