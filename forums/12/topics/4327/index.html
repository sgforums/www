    Dear Atti,

    In Singapore, to be a trained social worker there is actually a subject under social work in NUS arts fac(for those who are interested to be a social worker). Basically being a social worker is not just being a counsellor so simple and doesnt mean that the person will recprociate what you are doing for them. It takes alot of patience,care & concern for the client.(We called them our clients) Besides human relationship is complicated.

    Moreover, you can be a social worker in different areas eg: children(Singapore Children Society), adolescents (Orphanage, Singapore Boys'/Girls' Home), elderlys (Old folks home), hospitals (to provide financial aids for those who are needy), policies setter (eg: CDCs) blah~ blah~

    Learn about of theories before you can really go and work as social worker... :-)

    Dont worry, if you are unable to go into NUS to study social work. There are alot of university overseas which offer pyschology course and social work courses as well...

    Good luck to those who are interested!
