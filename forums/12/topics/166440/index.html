    Hi Pup,

    First of all, a big hug to you. I've gone through this before, & at the time, I'd thought that my parents were contradicting themselves. But after I'd come out to work, I realised how difficult it is to earn money. You're milked to the fullest for every cent the company pays you, more often than Not.

    So parents wish to instill in you what they've learnt. To them, $180/- is too big a sum to splurge on computer games, when later in life, you probably will get sick of your PS2 set. They're also worried that you may splurge in future on unnecessary things instead of stuff you really need, or saving for a rainy day.

    Even up till now, after earning my own money, my parents still nag at me about buying jewellery (according to them, I should save up to buy a pair of 2ct total solitaire earrings instead of buying tiny little things that people can't even see). To appease them, & now that I understand why they nag at me like this, I've stopped buying that many, that often.

    When you come out to work for real, & you can't rely on your parents anymore financially, you'll understand why your dad is so upset now. Learn to look beyond the scolding, & the good intentions underneath. And when your dad has cooled down, tell him that in future, you'll discuss these expenses with him before going ahead without approval, but he has to listen to what you have to say.

    FeowFeow
