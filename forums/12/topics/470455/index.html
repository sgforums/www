    Permanent residence permit

    The requirements for obtaining a permanent residence permit are:

    • The applicant has attended a course in Icelandic for foreigners.
    • There are no circumstances which may cause the applicant to be deported.
    • Secure financial circumstances.
    • The applicant has held the same types of residence permits for the four years preceding his/her application and that the provisions for issuing such a residence permit still apply.
    • The applicant does not have any outstanding cases in the criminal justice system wherein he/she is a suspect or has been charged with a punishable act.


    A permanent residence permit becomes invalid when the individual has resided in another country continuously for over 18 months.

    Estimated processing time for permanent residence permit applications is 90 days from the date the Directorate has received adequate documentation. If supporting documents are missing or inadequate, the processing time will take longer.

    Photograph: The applicant will be photographed when applying for a permanent residence permit and shall bring his passport.

    Application for a permanent residence permit must be submitted at least one month before the current permit expires.

     

    The following documentation must be submitted: 

    1. An original application form for a permanent residence permit, completed and signed by the applicant.
    2. Photocopy of the applicant’s passport/travel document. The passport must still be valid.
    3. Certified copies of the applicant’s tax returns for the previous four years. Those who submit their tax reports electronically to Internal Revenue Directorate (RSK) can send a certified copy in pdf form to the Directorate by e-mail to busetuleyfi@utl.is This e-mail address is being protected from spambots. You need JavaScript enabled to view it
    4. Certificate from the applicant’s Icelandic language course. The applicant shall provide original documentation showing that he/she has completed 150 hours of Icelandic language studies, or taken a test attesting his/her proficiency in the Icelandic language at an accredited institution.
    5. A signed employment contract (if applying for unlimited work permit).
    6. Paychecks for the last three months/proof of support.
    7. Social Services Certificate. The applicant must submit a certificate from the applicable municipal services, indicating whether or not he/she has received any financial social support for the past four years

    When processing of the application is completed, the applicant will be advised by written notification.

     

    Permanent residence permits for children under the age of 18:

    The only requirements other than the application form (signed by parent/legal guardian) is a copy of the child’s passport.

    Children who have been issued a permanent residence permit may be employed without a work permit until the age of 18. Upon turning 18 years old, the individual shall apply for a permanent work permit if he intends to work.
