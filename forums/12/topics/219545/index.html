    First, you probably should have poster this in the Singaporeans Overseas forum.

    I can only advise you about UK living allowance. Where exactly in the UK will you be based. Yes the southern parts of the UK is in general more expensive but expenses will vary quite a lot depending on where you are.

    Now, for London, you should expect to spend at least 100 pounds per week for a bedsit (student standard!). I see that you have been advised to go for 3 month leases so expect to pay a premium for short term rental. try Gumtree to get an idea of the rental involved. http://www.gumtree.com/

    On top of rental, you have to pay:

    1) water -- Usually dirt cheap. Usually less than 60 pounds per year per person (should be much much lower!)

    2) Electricity -- Quite expensive. expect to pay on average about 20 pounds per person per month. Less in summer more in winter.

    3) TV licence -- about 130 pounds per year. If you live in a block of flats you might be able to get away with not paying but the penalty if caught can be a fine of up to 1000 pounds. (remember they need a warrant to enter your property! Dun invite them in or give them reason to enter!)

    4) Since you are not a student, I -think- you have to pay council tax. It will depend on the property you are living in.

    If you eat out, McD value meals cost about 4 - 5 pounds each, a kebab and chips about 4 pounds. If you buy groceries and cook at home, budget about 30 pounds per week though you can certainly spend less - i know someone who uses just 10 pounds a week on groceries!

    All in all I think your 2000 (about 630 pounds/month) will just (or barely) be enough! the coy obviously did their sums.
