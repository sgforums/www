    Poly life is slower, but not truly easier than JC life.

    Indeed, a poly dip holds more weight than an A-lvl cert in general, but it is not truly that much better, if you cannot get into a uni.

    To be honest, remaining in JC or switching to poly will not make you regret. It is not doing enough to make a turnabout from your current situation that will be sure to make you regret.

    If you want to remain in JC, you will have to accept by now that you will have to put in a lot of effort and have adequate discipline. From your one year experience, you should know what is expected of you.

    If you want to switch to poly, you will need to know what degree courses you will want to take up when you enter uni. It will be pointless for you to take up a diploma course which you have no interest in, or does not lead to the degree course which you want to take up.

    If you think poly is slackier, than you are greatly mistaken. A lot of people do not have adequate discipline in poly, and stray off, due to the many temptations and less pressure in poly life. As a result, they do not do well for their diploma. In poly, you will have to self-discipline, as there is less peer pressure in most (but not all) faculties as compared to in JC.

    Summing it up, here are the questions you should ask yourself:

    1) Can I instill discipline in myself if I were to continue my studies in JC?

    2) What degree course do I wish to take up?

    3) If I were to continue in JC, what should I do and what should I NOT do?
