    Genital herpes is very common and once infected, you can shed the virus and pass on the disease even when you have no symptoms. Your doctor has prescribed you acyclovir or other members of the same drug family. That will heal the blisters but will not cure it. Long term suppressive therapy (take the medicine twice a day) can prevent relapse and reduce the risk of transmission.

    Guys, you won't get it from an infected person through social contact. If she has genital herpes, it is unlikely that you will get it through kissing either. It is difficult to advise what to do in our society. If a girl tells you she has herpes will you not drop her like a hot potato? If she tells you she has herpes after you get married what will you do? Stick with her but never have sex? Forget about children? Or tell her to take acyclovir all her life (will easily cost you $200 a month) and wear a condom all the time? Even so, there is always a small risk of transmission
