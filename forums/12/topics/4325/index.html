    Well, mabbe I'm not too qualified to answer the questions posted in this forum yet, seeing that I'm only a junior member. But let me take a whack at it...

    Everyone has had some form of infatuation and rejection at any one point in their lives. After getting burnt so many times, I can see why you're a tad bit afraid to get back into the dating game.

    Firstly, why don't you try a simple enough test? It might be for fun.. but it could build up your self-confidence. Take those little online personality tests that people mail to each other. Hmm... www.emode.com seems to work pretty well. Then send invitations to all the girls you know.. regardless of whether or not they're interested or if you're interested in them.
    See what sort of girl you're compatiable with personally... that way, at least you have a rough guide.

    Sometimes, guys tend to like girls who are totally not their type. You can go out with them almost everyday.. get a little bit disillusioned by "pretending" that you're both together. Then you realise that she's just treating you as a friend. Bummer. It may surprise you to know that the most seemingly incompatiable person according to you, might actually be that special someone for you.

    Next up, if all these girls are actually treating you as their friend. Well, that's GREAT! I mean... for females to have male friends that they trust and confide in.. is an honour which seems to get harder to earn as you grow older. Just be their friend, with time... some of them, might just end up turning around and saying that..."Hey! why can't I like XXX? I mean.. he's always been there for me." That's kinda how a geeky bugger like myself managed to end up with 2 of my girlfriends.

    When you decide to tell a girl that you like her.. it takes a whole lot of guts and pondering before you actually do it... it is NOT romantic nor does it make sense to do it on a spur of the moment.

    Speaking from experience, when I tell girls that I like them, usually I'm ready to put everything that I've had with them up to this point in time on the line.. ie, I'm willing to lose their friendship and I am ready to accept the fact that nothing will ever be the same again. (Ladies: Betcha didn't know guys thought so much about it now did you?) That way, if everything turns out badly, you're prepared... and if it turns out great.. well, happiness then.

    I've had this girl that I've had a soft spot for ... for over... hmm.. 12 years? I know I know.. it's not possible.. but then, yeah.. I have.. and we're terribly close friends. She knows that I like her in a sense... but I refuse to do anything about it, because I know that I'm not the guy for her. That's a bit sad sometimes when you think about it... but when you do "love" someone.. you want what's best for them, not what's best for you.

    Oh my.. I seem to be talking a lot. I think I'll end off soon...

    How to widen your social circle? Come to the forums, go to IRC, network through friends... pretty common sense stuff. I guess the only advice I can give is not to go out with every girl on the basis of wanting to be in a relationship with her. Just enjoy her company and you'll slowly you'll realise what you REALLY want in a girl.

    Geez.. Long Post. Sorry folks.
