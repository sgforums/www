    I guess i wanna give my 7 cents worth on such issues....

    I think enuff has been said, you are not even sure of your feelings...how to maintain such relationship even if you are together?

    I also wanna say that people nowadays get into a relationship easily, far too easily. This is common in teenagers but is not surprising within adults too. I will just tok about teenagers and young adults since most of us are around that age range.

    Most relationships started off becos I like you, you like me, i think i understand you, i think you understand me. Thats IMPORTANT i must say, but is that all?

    Most relationships started in early stages of your life like from 14 to even 20 do not last, aint I right? Some do last until marriage (yes they do, I have frens who were together from sec 4 till today married). but i would like to propose that these cases are exceptions rather than a norm. then u may question why?

    This is not a case of just maturity, age does not determine maturity but it pretty much influence us. WHo can sincerely tell me that they already knew what they want in life, in a life partner when they are just 16? Not many of us i think. at that age, most of us want our bf/gf to be nice looking, sweet...

    There are a few things i think we need to consider b4 stepping into a relationship:

    Emotional maturity : If we are not sure and cant handle our emotions well, cant show maturity in our emotional life, then we shld have 2nd tots abt having to deal with another person's emotions.

    Social maturity : at our young age, all we meet are our frens in sch or church or club or relatives...do we really know who want as our life partner? have we seen enough to know what we want?

    Financial maturity: taking your parents' money to go dating? not too right i think. nevermind i can work part time. are u sure? you cant even take care of urself, u still need ur parents to provide for you, can u really take care of another person?

    THere are many other more considerations. Some may just say, hey all we want is a relationship, doesnt matter if it doesnt last long, if it does, well i am glad. Relationships that started off like this mentality will most likely fail as the mentality is just to 'try it out'.

    I am not stating that this is a 100% foolproof plan to a nice and everlasting marriage. there is none. but with a bit more consideration done, you can increase that chance of a long lasting relationship.

    they say love is blind, it is true. Thus my advice is, before you get blind, open your eyes, look around and look at things objectively and if you are dead sure that you and the other party are focussed on making this relationship work, then sure go ahead and jump right in. Love is affairs of our hearts and our hearts dun think rationally, but why not take a little time to analyse love in a rational way before you enjoy your romance.
