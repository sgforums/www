    17 Oct 06, 13:39
    If you are going to have more than 100 people, gathering around the pool site might be an issue.

    Perhaps you can consider only inviting family members and closed friends only to the solemnisation if you insist having the solemnisation at the pool site. After the solemnisation, you can move on to another venue for your "c0cktail" party (buffet lunch) where you can invite the rest of your friends to.

    How about just hire a hall where you can accommodate a big turn-out? The set up will be simple... you just need a big long table for all the food and drinks etc and chairs along the hall for your guests to sit whenever/wherever they want.

    Most of the people will probably stand and chat with friends most of the time anyway. If possible, have some space where you can turn it into a dance floor for people to dance.

    To minimise chaos, it's best to have a program you can stick to (your guests don't have to see this program, just your MC/DJ on that day to make the appropriate announcement) at the c0cktail party including:

    From to - Arrival of guests (Drinks to be served on arrival)
    From to - Arrival of bride & Groom (MC to introduce the new husband and wife etc)
    From to - Lunch time (not including desserts yet)
    From to - Champagne pouring and toasting time (if you're planning to do it)
    From to - Speech (Father of the bride/Best Man/ Groom/Bride)
    From to - Cake cutting time
    From to - Dancing or other entertainment
    From to - Desserts/coffee/tea time
    From to - Dancing or more entertainment etc
    From to - Conclusion of the party

    At the conclusion of the party, if you and your partner are planning to leave before your guests, you can always get the MC to get all your guests to form a big circle with you and your partner inside the circle. Once everyone is there, you and your partner can start thanking your guests by going around the circle (starting at the same point with one going clockwise direction while the other the anti-clockwise direction).

    These are just some of my ideas.... Anyway, happy planning and congratulations on your impending nuptial. All the best!

    The problem with catered lunch.

    Is that they never have enough drinks! Drinks always runs out before your party really started.

    And make sure the bozz of tat catering company is always on hand.

    You dont want to get into a arguement with some teenage waiter with his: Bozz only tell us to bring this amount of drinks. Bozz busy elsewhere.

    I tell you, anytime I hear that, I will never ever utilized those kinda caterers anymore. And there is such in multiples in Singapore.

    Make sure they standby 4 more containers of drinks.

    Food wise, get a tasting done first. And check your guest for allergies, restrictions and get them to give you boxes to 'ta pao'. And always delay their time to collect back their utensils. You dont want them to clear the food way before your last guest has his fill of the food and drinks.
