  • glossy_matte

    I have a good buddy, lets name her J. J is slightly plump, hates exercise, hardly anyone calls her pretty. But recently, she went for a makeover and somehow she looks slightly slimmer and much prettier. Male friends have told me she looks better. As a friend, I should feel happy for her but somehow I feel so damn jealous and even bear petty hatred towards her.

    Friends have always compared the both of us and usually I tend to get the compliments. I'm slim, and when I asked a male friend how J looks as compared. He answered "Almost as slim as you already". Somehow I get this urge to shed even more - I exercise and go to the gym regularly. I feel angry even to lose out.

    I hate myself for this and I've tried taking 10 deep breaths and think on the bright side and try to convince myself that she is my friend and I should be glad for her. But the devil me keeps coming back! And now whatever she has, I'll get/become better. I know this kind of competition is totally wrong! But I can't stop getting jealous.

    Will someone please tell me what can I do? I treasure this friendship and I don't want to lose it just because of my jealousy.

    You know. I've felt the same way once. Friends usually comment on me looking better, etc, until he changed a bit.

    In a way, i'm telling myself that "I want to be noticed! Look at me! Why are you looking at him?" Sounds pretty bad, but its true. Its a case of low-self esteem(for me back then anyway).

    I asked myself, "Hey, do i want a friend, or do i want to be noticed?". I realised i wanted both. And the only way to do it was to develop a high self-esteem, or to get the attention i need through other ways.

    Perhaps i was too used to having attention when i was in my younger years, kept winning chess tournaments. Since my secondary school, poly doesn't treat Chess as a serious CCA, i've stopped winning, and perhaps i'm not too used to the lack of attention now.

    Even now, i post in the hope that someone will quote me, and say "x2" or "I believe _Aaron_ is right". Its a strange thing isn't it?

    Another aspect of looking at it could be girls just want to win each other. They compete and compete, just for the fun of it. You know you shouldn't be so worked up about it, but if its doing you good, then why not continue?

    Just smile when friends compliment her, and tell yourself that you will be better, in a friendly way. Purge the demon(devil) inside you. This is just the ugly side of you that you will have to live with.

    Or you could, adopt a heck-care attitude. Living with this can really make life easier i feel.

    Pray. Pray for forgiveness, and feel the inner peace within.
    Meditate, let your mind clear, and focus on a favourite phrase or line.

    Other than taking deep breathes, you could also grab a drink, take a stroll, think of happy thoughts to get yourself out of the situation.

    Whatever you choose, just don't go overboard... Don't try to slim yourself by eating only 1 meal a day, you get the idea. Guess i rambled too much again...
