    Since young, I had the burning desire to live and work overseas. I went to US (Wisconsin) pursuing a degree right after my '0' Levels, but due to NS duties, I had to return after a few years.

    I loved USA, the exciting lifestyle and culture, as well as the lovely weather (even the winters with an average of -30 degrees). Also as a Singaporean Chinese, I am rarely a victim of racial remarks.

    Now that i'm 32, NS is no longer a liability for me, yet I'm tied down by other commitments. I am also getting married next year and we bought a new condominium slated to complete by mid 2015. Of course, it was a huge decision...but I am quite regretful now. 

     

    Do I really have to give up my dreams of leaving Singapore?

    I have considered the options of migrating to NZ but with an average income tax of 20-30%, lack of CPF, and higher living expenses (in terms of commodities like cigarettes, food etc.), it becomes apparent that I cannot fund the housing in SG unless I make like SGD$10,000 a month. I am no doctor or lawyer, currently just a humble marketing professional in middle management. My savings are also depleted, with about $100K left for my marriage, car and renovation expenses.

    I have seriously thought about my options:

    i.) Forgo the stamp duty and deposit paid upfront for the house (around $150K), and incur more expenses as the bank loan is already in effect. Too extreme I guess?

    ii.) Find an good paying expat job that sends me and my wife abroad, to UK or US? Hmm..I was never a "lucky" person.


    So...I believe many Singaporeans are facing similar dilemmas. Any useful advice would be great! Thanks.
