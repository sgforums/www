    'Stead'? I thought only kids use that term.

    I seriously suggest that you DON'T pop the question. Many guys make the mistake of popping the question too early, and end up regretting. Guys like me.

    Like I've mentioned before, girls only want to be good friends with SOME guys. But you might be thinking, "good friends will eventually become lovers, right?" Wrong. That only happens in TV dramas. To girls, good friends are good friends. There's no 'sparks', or attraction at all.

    To put it in simple terms, oranges(boyfriend-type) attract girls. Apples(good friend-type) do not. Why? Simply because they're different.

    In most cases(90%), the guy thinks that he is an orange, but in fact he is an APPLE.

    I'm not going to teach you how to become an apple, because it's too complicated for you right now. So stick to the old advice....Let her know you better first! When she gets to know your personality and character better, she'll be able to make a decision on whether you'll make a good boyfriend. That will increase your chances, but the decision is still hers.

    Hmm so your friend told you that he likes her too. What are you planning to do? Give up on the girl and let your friend woo her instead? You'll be a great fool if you do that.
