    Summer, I'm not a legal expert, but let me put my opinion down.

    If the grounds for divorce is due to your mum's unreasonable behavior (e.g adultery, neglect kids, gamble and needs family to keep paying her debts), then your dad may be able to pay less or even nothing to your mum. You and your siblings can be witness to your mum's bad behavior, and your dad's sacrifices to keep the family going.

    If the insurance is paid by your dad, check if he can prove it (e.g payment is by GIRO from your dad's bank account). Then check with his lawyer, if it is possible to contest for ownership transfer of policy to his name without any transfer fee paid to your mum (other than the amts paid by your mum+interest).

    Write down your feelings and reasons in a letter about why you think your mum should not get any money from your dad's assets (e.g neglected the family, dad has been paying off your mum's debts, payment of exhorbitant sums of money to your mum will cause your family financial difficulties...etc). Do not lie, be honest and factual about the reasons. That way, even if you cannot appear in court, the letter will express your views.

    Sign the letter, and give it to your dad's lawyer. Btw, the letter may or may not be useful legally as you are below legal age, but children's views can provide strong evidence to sway the judge's opinion in a family court.
