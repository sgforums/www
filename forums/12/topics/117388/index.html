    In Third Law of Love states: though together, we each chose our current relationships for our personal and specific reasons.

    There is a prevailing fantasy that somehow there is some commonality between us and our partners that is more important than our personal reasons for being with them. Some of us undoubtedly believe that there are some 'special' reasons for being together that are more important than our personal feelings and issues. I am not denying that there are some couples that comes together and achieve great things in life, but you can also bet that each of them is getting each and every one of their personal needs and desires fulfilled at the same time.

    Your relationship is for YOU, not your partner or for those other people in your life. If it wasn't giving you something that you wanted, you wouldn't be there.

    The exchange of Love for Love is the simplest needs the relationship had to produce. We always hear people saying 'takes two to clap.' and how unrequited love ends up one sided. The reason is simple, the other party requires nothing from the one loving him/her. If principles were strong, he/she wouldn't want the money nor the sex either. And in the simplest state: love wasn't brew and exchange, there is no way you could end up a relationship because he/she cannot fulfill whatever needs/desire you are seeking.

    Love is inmeasurable.

    There is a exchange of subconscious mutual benefits. A relationship could take place.

    She gives sex.
