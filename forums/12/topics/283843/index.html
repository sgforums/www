    Since your parent died without a will, he/she died inestate and the assets wil be distributed according to the rules of inestacy as backed by the Interstate Succession Act provided u meet their provisions.

    In this case, your late parents assets are distributed as follows..

    1/2 goes to your father/mother

    the rest goes to you or split amongst your siblings..

    Dun worry..engage a lawyer as the Executor of that Estate...u will sure get money..however, if you have siblings...your remaining parent will have the discretion to allocate the remaining half of the assets in whichever ratio he/she wants...so if you have 2 siblings and he/she doesnt like u..he/she can allocate the remaining assets 1:4:5 with the "1" being u and you or your lawyer can't do anything about it since it's by law...
  • <Precious>
    5 Oct 07, 09:30
    Much depends on the types of assets and how they are held in law. If the house is held by both your parents as joint tenants as opposed to a tenancy-in-common, upon the cessation of one joint owner, the property will go the surviving joint-tenant who then becomes the sole owner.

    CPF monies will go to the person who had been nominated by the deceased parent. In your case, probably the surviving parent must have been nominated by the deceased.

    Insurance is the same as CPF rules - depends on who the nominee is.

    Cash assets will be distributed in accordance with the rules of intestacy. It is probable that there is little if any cash assets or you should have been notified of the share you are entitled already.......
