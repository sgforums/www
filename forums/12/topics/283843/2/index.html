    Hi

    From what I recall, and gone thru, the money in the cpf will be distributed as
    fair as possible. CPF board will perform this task.

    My father died when I was 13, leaving behind a small sum of CPF shared
    among my brother, sister, mum and me.

    CPF board split the money this way, my mother will get 40%, and the rest of
    us 3 siblings get 20% each. We had no arguments, well... how to argue
    anyway.

    Since I was 13, I was not entitled to draw any money until I reach 18.
    When I turned 18, cpf sent a cheque to me and just deposit it into
    any bank account. It took them about month after my birthday to send me
    the cheque, but I was not so bothered.

    I am 26 this year, so the whole issued started 13 years ago.

    When your parent died, were you already 18? Also, CPF board will only
    recognise legitimate family members. So if you unfortunately do not, in any
    way, carry some form of legal papers regarding to your parent, you may not
    be entitled to a single cent.

    Otherwise, your parent has already made a will that is to pass every asset
    and money to partner in law or someone else.

    Best of luck to you
