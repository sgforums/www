    I think before you take another step forward, you should come up with an idea of how many more tries you will be willing to give to the SPF interview. Then stick to that number. There are many different hurdles we all must overcome in our own lives (be it repeated rejections at interviews, relationships or in chasing our own dreams). While it is good to be persistant in chasing our dreams, it is equally as important to know when to say 'enough' and to look for alternatives.

    We know far too little about the entire world to limit ourselves to just one dream.

    So keep your options open, set yourself a limit for the number of tries/time/effort you are willing to sacrifice to chase this particular dream...and if you still get nowhere by that time, then dream a new dream. Remember to explore other options while you're chasing this one particular 'dream job'. 

    I used to think that being a Marine biologist would be the perfect job for me....given my love for underwater marine life and passion for certain aspects of it. But while chasing that dream, I stumbled upon another which was even better than the first....and I wouldn't have if I hadn't been trying to be a marine biologist.

    Some dreams are stepping stones to bigger dreams still unrealised.

    Good luck with your search though.Try not to identify a 'failure' of a particular situation to a person (even if that person was being an as$ and failed you on purpose)....

    I'm not exactly 'religious' but in his own way, i guess his failing you is a part of the bigger plan for you. So take it in your stride alright? =)
