    This is the stage in life when you would start pondering about the meaning of your life. Nothing wrong with that. This is also when you would realize there's a gap between your ideals and the reality. Nothing wrong with that as well. But instead of simply going through the daily drudgery aimlessly, think about how what you're doing it now could help you achieve what you want in the future.

    You said you're interested in skydiving, but are you taking up any sports-related CCA currently to prepare yourself for that? You like animals? How about becoming a veterinarian in the future? That would require a good knowledge of biology and chemistry. Once you know your way, you'll find that life is more than just a walk in the dark.

    Last but not least, don't make judgements about your classmates in haste. Get close to them, and you may realize that some of them might not be so different from you after all.

    Hey TS,

    I was in a similar situation as you when I was young.There are a few things I want to say.

    1. Your are your own best friend. There won't be someone who can truly understands how you feel other than yourself. Start to love yourself for who you are and improve yourself one step at a time.

    2. Join an interest group in school e.g. band and expand your social circle. Learning to socialise is an essential skill that you will need in your life. 

    3. Activities like mountain climbing, scuba diving can be learned later when you have the financial capacity to take care of yourself.

    4. Set yourself objectives and goals e.g. I want to complete 10km run in 2010 or I want to improve my average score in one subject by 10 marks by year end. Work towards your goals and even if you fail, its ok as long as you know you have done your best and made some improvements.

    5. Glad that you have interest in doing volunteering work. Get some friends and visit SPCA, talk to the staff there and explore the possibility of you doing volunteering work. there. Visit their website and send enquiry emails about volunteering to them.

    'You have a choice to walk towards your dreams or sit on it. It's your life. It's your choice.'

    Good Luck my friend.

    Actually, the root of your problems is due to your lack of self-love, which is a very common problem for both adults and youths. But I think you may be too young and have not enough life experiences to understand that, so I shall not explain it here.

    But about your case, it'll help you a lot, if you spend time doing things that you enjoy, and will make you happy. As long as you cannot find time and squeeze some time out every week to do things that truly make you happy, you'll continue to be miserable. That's the way life is. If you choose not to do anything to change your life, your life will very rarely change for the better on its own.

    I suggest putting aside expensive hobbies for the time being till you're able to afford them. Your parents are not obligated to spend such money. Besides, frankly, you'll still be lonely and unhappy even if you have the money to take up such hobbies, as that's not the root cause of your current misery.

    Volunteering at SPCA is a noble activity, and they definitely will welcome you, provided you genuinely wish to help. And you'll probably get to know more people with similar compassion for animals in need. There is a senior lady in charge there who seems unfriendly at first, but if you can prove your sincerity, she'll reveal her warm side.

    Once you start doing things that you enjoy, you'll slowly find your life more meaningful, and good things can then happen in your life, and you'll then look forward to your future. Have fun, my friend! :)
