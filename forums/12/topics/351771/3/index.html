    it seems like a vicious cycle

     

    How to get over someone?

    You don't.

    When you walk along the beach enjoying a peaceful sunset, even in the midst of the crowd, you will remember him.

    When you are sitting alone at home, listening to some music, you remember your time with him.

    You try to mix around with others, go into different relationships, but you will either compare him to others or hold yourself back, because you remember your deeply shared experiences with him.

    You try to get drunk, but at a drunken state which gives you happiness, it only triggers your memories and makes you weep even harder, at the loss of him.

    We humans can never forget. The way we can never forget alphabets. We can never 'unlearn'. The human mind does not function like a computer, where the delete button will wipe away files or wiped away by using a magnet on the harddisk.

    Memories will always stay with us. More so if it was deeply etched through trial and tribulations.

    But it is within our power to control such memories. It takes only determination. Determination to convince yourself why you must not linger on such memories. We know what is good and bad for ourselves. We cannot bring life to the dead. That's why we must give ourselves a reason to reject those memories when it surfaces.

    Time heals not by forgetting, but heals by making your realizing why you can no longer bring the dead alive. Those hopes of a return to the good times in your memories of him are dead and inevitably gone forever. Embrace and enjoy those memories in your vulnerable moments, but know that it has a door marked 'NEVERMORE'. Smile at them and know that when one door closes, another one opens. This is a reality many happy couples know. And you can be one of them too, if you let yourself free instead of being crippled by your memories.
