    Recovering from a break up is a process, usually classified into denial, depression, anger, acceptance and for some, indifference.

    Denial - Your relationship has fallen apart. Your partner leaves you and you harbour thoughts of reunion. You make excuses for the breakdown and think he is coming back.

    Depression - 2 months later, he's still gone from your life and you realise he is not coming back. This is the stage where you should start getting out and exercising to release pent up feelings. And begin the healing process. 

    Anger - Is the final stage. You'll start wondering what you saw in him to begin with and start hating him for wasting your time going through the pain. You recall the bad moments in your failed relationship and start blaming yourself and maybe your partner. This is also the stage when you start throwing away pictures, notes and messages.

    Acceptance - You stop reading his horoscope. You start appreciating the good from what you guys had, smile at old memories and you're happy when you hear he has found someone new. You stop caring whether or not he comes back.

     

    Chin up and you'll be fine. When one door closes, another one opens.
