    Hey serenewwjd... You'd noticed that most of the nice folks who responded will tell you the same thing - focus on your studies. This is probably the most genuine and possibly the simpliest advice anyone can give.

    Having said all that, young people with raging (and changing) hormones do get involved in all these lurvy-durvy stuff - nothing unusual, part of growing up. The problem comes about with the MANAGING of your emotions.

    When we are in love (or infatuation or whatever word you want to use), our judgement is impaired. We either become super sensitive or super agitated - meaning, what we see and think may not be accurate.

    In our relationship (platonic or otherwise) we need to place ourselves in the other fellas shoes. Some of the other boys that you like, may like you too, but may not place you as part of the priorities at this point of their life, ie, some like you, but may feel that the studies are more important, others may like to fish a little bit more. Your account of all the boys are a little long, but I guess I get the picture.

    The point is, emotions in teenage years (may extend to young adults) is experimental at best - we experience all kinds of new emotions and sometimes it is difficult with all these changes. In order not to get confused - set your priorities right. As yourself what you want to do in RGS? Do well, or muck around? If your answer is (and I think my guess is correct) to do well acadamic, you need to understand that having bgr problems is the last thing you ever need at this point in time. You might also need to ask yourself some questions: why the need to have a bf? is it to satisfy the need to feel loved? or to be part of the in-crowd (ie your class mates who also have bfs).

    Trust no one - even if some of these boys are your church friends. This is one of the biggest misconceptions - that church people are all saints. Every individual in church have their own level of spiritual maturity (some masquerade it very well). Others do not have control of their own emotions. Just because one individual may be a pastor's son, or a leader's son, does not make them spiritual individuals.

    Online friends? I'd be very very cautious on this one. There are scums that prey on pretty young things like yourself. Chat if you have to - do not reveal your name, address, identity or even agree to meet them anywhere, no matter how attractive they say they are. Consult your church leaders on this one, please!

    May I recommend you a book: 7 Habits for Effective Teenagers by Sean Covey. My recently-turned-teenage son read it when he was 9 and it helped him tremendously and he is so focused that it scares me.

    I hope what I've given your are some good fatherly advice and are helpful in your decision making process.
