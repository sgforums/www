If she decides to preserve her own life, it means her choice is to SACRIFICE her child to keep hers. She won't be able to take the consequence of such a decision and would rather chose to sacrifice herself. This defies logics. This is emotional decision-making (or Emotions > Logics ).

    As from your point of view, you are weighing between a wife or a child. And obviously, you would save your wife because you made your marriage vow to your wife, not your child. She will be the only person who will walk through your life with you, not your children. This is logical. This is rational decision making. (or Logics > Emotions)

    I give you a simple illustration:

    Say if your wife is terminally ill and wouldn't have much time left. One day, came a drunken yunhaier with a gun, pointing menacingly at the defenceless two of you.

    'Only one of you is allowed to leave here alive.'

    If I leave it to the both of you, chances are, your wife will come forth (despite your resistance) and allow herself to be killed because after all, she don't have much time left. (Rational decision making)

    BUT

    If I personally ask you to make the decision, would you allow your wife to be shot, even if she doesn't have much time left? (Emotional decision making)
