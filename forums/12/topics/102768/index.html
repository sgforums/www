    To go overseas to study, your parents will need to give the govt a bond of usually $100k or more depending on your parent's income can go up to the $millions also depending on the financial status of your parents.

    If you were to break the bond, ie not come back to Singapore when you finish your studies without a good reason(ie can be for work) but you still have to come back to serve NS, you will lose the bond, plus your citizenship status as well as be marked as being on the wanted list. ie you will be charged and put into jail.

    Pros about finishing your degree first, there isn't a break btw studies and army and back to studies...

    Cons about finishing your degree first, esp if you're doing an IT degree, you will not be up to date on the latest programming and after 2 1/2yrs some new programme might be out already or you would have forgotten most of what you have learnt if you do not revise or keep up with the business trends. etc

    Cons about finishing army first, you might find it hard to go back to studying again... a lot has changed in life... which leads to the pros of finishing NS first, you will be more mature, usually more motive and know what you want to do in life and what kind of career path you would like to take on and learn in the future...

    For most families, the burden of the bond can be quite hard esp if they're only middle class. Because I believe the bond is held on to the govt. ie money is kept with the govt and the parents cannot touch it till the bond is released so as to secure a payment. I like to call it the zero coupon bond issued by your parents to the govt which has a full annuity value added to it!
