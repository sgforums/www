    To start with, I think u current generation of young people have changed from what i notice it is not the olden times any more in the 80s. Girls tend to look at a guy's economic situation before falling in love is the current complaint now. to say, i dont agree on this fullheartedly nor disagree. it had existed since the days of the merdeka but was not widespread because the society was different back then. In my time, people were rather simplistic in thinking. A few with different ideas made it big.

    U have enjoyed the fruits of labour your forefathers who helped shape up singapore. I can understand the current economic climate but do not let it induce a paradigm shift- a change from one way of thinking to another. It's a revolution, a transformation, a sort of metamorphosis. It just does not happen, but rather it is driven by agents of change. There was one from the 80s to 90s. During the 80s and before people lived in kampongs and were the ones who migrated to HDB estates. At the time the thinking of te majority was to grab the opportunities of a rising Singapore. Everyone had equal chances. During the kampong days , people were not so well to do and as a result ties with friends and relatives were stronger. But we also had problems in society like drug addiction among youths and gangsterism. In those days u could survive with O levels and educate your children by working as a taxi driver. We didnt have internet and most of us stayed at home. We had more chances to socialise around in the correct way unlike now where socialising involves clubbing and other activites. In those days entertainment meant playing soccer ,sepak takraw , watching WWF on TV or meaningful activities inthe neighbourhood CC. A select few who chose to stray got entangled in the void deck culture secret society/KTV trends. They were very active in those days.

    Somehow in the 90s people tend to think differently. Thats the golden decade when more and more singaporeans became richer quicker, became more fashionable and old ideas were dying out. Remember the property boom in the mid 90s? Selling of HDB flats can land u as much as 50K in the bank account. More people started driving cars and quality of life had improved dramatically especially in the early 90s. The clubbing and corporate scene was vibrantly emerging. By the mid to late 90s most people could afford to go to tertiary education. the mass introduction of the personal computer and the internet during this period have impacted both personal and business environments, and is a catalyst for the next Paradigm Shift in the millenium .But along comes with the paradigm shift some first signs of mild negative effects

    It was also during this time that economy started to come into problems. After the 2000 things changed to what u find in the current time now. I dont know
    why this happened or why the Singapore government had changed. gone were those days when Mps would walk with residents. What i see now from some websites where singaporeans air their views, there is widespread discontentment and resentment towards the governtment particularly among males. But it is good to know that people now are more open minded unlike before. this is the paradigm shift that u wish to see but not the expense of love and moral values. But as always have faith, love and conviction always in your hearts and importantly be truthful to yourself and others around u.

    John
    San Francisco, USA
