    I agree, if you are not schooling, then go to a polyclinic and get a referral to any of the hospital with a neuroscience unit or counseling centre. The psychiatris will first go through with you your history and then do a diagnosis on whether you are suffering from depression. Medicine may or may not be required. Bec you are referred you will be entitled to subsidised rates.

    They have counsellors there too. eg like in NUH's neuroscience department, they have psychiatrists and psychologist (counsellors) so you can see both.

    The thing about depression is that the sooner you seek assistance the easier it is to resolve it. so please be encouraged to seek assistance. plenty of people do it and in fact 1 in 5 will get a sever bout of depression sometime in their lives. so its nothing unusual.

    For private psychiatrist in places like raffles hospital/geneagle/Mt E and other private clinics, the charges that I know of can be anywhere from $80-150 per visit and counselling can range from $50-150/hr. Some of these practitioners will vary their rates according to your ability to pay. Yes help is not cheap but from people whom I know of, they see it as a something worthwhile even with the cost bec without normal level of feel good, your quality of life and your performance in relaitonship and even in work and making money is affected so it becomes more expensive not treating it!

    So if you cant go the private way, go through the public route and you will get medical assistance at subsidised rates.

    Take care
