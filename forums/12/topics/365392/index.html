    A wise man once told me, you can alter fate but you cannot change destiny.

    Destiny for one is finite. Just like our alotted lifespan. No matter how healthy you attempt to live your life, within that century, you will return to dust.

    But fate is alterable. Fate is a condition, situation and environment that you live, shaping, controlling and directing you into one direction. While you are still alive, you can break out of such control by using the power of your mind - to think , anaylse and work out a rational decision to avoid what 'Fate' has determined.

    For example, in this financial crisis, many will be out of jobs and staring at empty dinner tables at home. Money had become scarce. You presume there is no job, and it is your 'fate' to starve. But if you think rationally, you will know that is not true.

    Money don't disappear, but had only been hoarded up. But not by everyone. There is still money in the financial market, and jobs still abound, made more so by leaders who have a responsibility to see that their citizens do not starve.

    Therefore, if you actively seek out jobs, correct your mistakes and retune your personality after each rejection, no matter how mean, you will not starve. It is only temporary. 

    Once confidence returns, the world's economy will surge. There is an additional few US trillions floating in the market, with resources going cheap. Those dollars are opportunities and bound to trickle down, but only for those who dont give up to presumed 'fate'.

    Some believed that 'fate' controls one. Some believe in the philosophy of 'what is yours will be yours, but what is not yours will never be yours'. No point banging your head onto the wall.

    I had tried and proven such philosophy wrong with success several times, but....at high costs to myself and had come to believe that when you challenge fate, you will have to make sacrifices...

    Still, one have to survive, if not for himself, then for his loved ones, and must weigh the  priorities and get it right on how far one should go.
