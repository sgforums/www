    Dear Galtwenty,

    I suppose deep down in us, everybody misses the good old days when we were young & didn't have a care. I do miss those old days too, when I didn't have to worry about $ & I was still innocent about what work life was really like. Friends & I used to think about what we'd do, what ambitions we have, etc. How I laugh at it now, & I smile whenever my young nephew voices his thoughts, because that was how I used to be...

    However, although I miss the old days, I don't wish to turn back the clock. The lessons I've learnt & the hurdles I've crossed made me what I am today. I know what to do when the going gets tough, etc. It's better than staying stagnant at a level which isn't very much.

    A young girl I'm mentoring brings back sweet memories of when I was like her, fresh, innocent, etc. But she's struggling, & I well remember her struggles. I don't want to be in that position again.

    FeowFeow
