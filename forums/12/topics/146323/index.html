  • Gedanken
    8 Aug 05, 08:37
    A lump can be one of the following:
    - a cyst
    - an infection in the breast
    - changes in the ducts and milk-producing glands
    - a benign or malignant tumour (benign = harmless, malignant = harmful)

    Most lumps (about 80%) are harmless, but there's no sense in taking any chances, is there?

    Go see a General Practicioner about this - if he or she can't sort it out on the spot, he or she will be able to refer you to a specialist.

    If you do need a test carried out, it will be either a mammogram or ultrasound scan. Both are non-invasive (no entry of anything through skin), and it will take a few days to get the results. If the doctor wants to be really sure that the growth is not malignant, you'll need to do a biopsy where a tissue sample is taken out of the lump for analysis. The results will also take a few days.

    As everyone else has already said, it's better to play safe. Go set up your appointment ASAP!
