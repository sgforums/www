    Step back and look at the parameters of the situation, and the course of action is pretty straightforward.

    First, the title of the thread already shows that you have closed off options. Why should it be what's right or what's accepted? Are the two mutually exclusive? I would suggest that an adjustment of both would result in a meeting point in which it can be both right and accepted.

    Let's look at your bottom line. You want the marks. Simple as that. Who you get the marks with and how it gets done are independent considerations, so treat them independently when you're working out your plan.

    Next, the strongest point you have on your side is that you are basing your conclusions on what these two guys have DONE, while the girls are basing their approach on what they have SAID. Obviously the stronger evidence is in your favour, so squeeze the girls on that one. You already have proof that these two slackers are dead weight - if they want to bring the guys back in the team, they have to CONVINCE you that things will work out this time. There's no need to get emotional when discussing this point with them. Be cool, calm and logical, and use every bit of your experience with these guys to rebut whatever the girls say, and place the emphasis on ACTIONS, not words.

    Ideally, by now you should have the girls in a corner. Point out that you've given them every opportunity to make their case, but there's nothing that they've done that guarantees protection of the bottom line - the marks. Make sure you bring it back to the marks because you need to keep this business-like.

    At this point, you have two options. Either shut the whole discussion down or give the girls a way out. The former option is ill-advised, since it could well result in damage to the group's morale. What about a way out for them, then?

    Reiterate the point that this is business, not personal. At the end of the day each of you will take marks away from this, and that's the only thing that counts here.

    In light of that, the first issue to be addressed is where the team is at currently, and what needs to be done. Remind the girls that what the two slackers do is much more important than what they say, so if they want back in, they're going to have to perform some actions that demonstrate their intentions. Set aside a few tasks that they will need to do to earn probationary status in the team. For example, if the bikinis haven't already been packed, give them a clearly set time to finish the job. If they don't do it, they can't convince you that they're a good bet and that's the end of the issue. The objective of this is to shift the responsibility of the outcome from yourself (in making the decision) to them (in proving their good intent). If they fail, the blame falls squarely on them.

    If they do pass and get back in the team, you're going to have to lay it out for them. They've obviously got work practice problems that you will have to talk to them about, and you're going to have to put measures in to address these problems. Also, if they do complete the task, get back in and screw it up again, at the very least you'll have cut your losses by getting soem work out of them.

    From the sounds of it, your team isn't doing much project management. From what you've described, it sounds like tasks are assigned to each team member on a lassiez faire basis, and no strict timelines are being defined. You need to build it into your team processes that everybody knows exactly what they're supposed to be doing and by exactly when, and these assignments have to be done in the presence pof the whole team so that it doesn't become an individual argument. Make it clear to these guys that if they fail to meet their deadlines, they're out because their failure will be at everybody's expense.

    In summary, get the bums to earn their place back in the team by SHOWING that they're serious instead of just saying it, and institute measures to keep them under control. If they fail, it give you clear grounds to chuck them out again without further need for debate.

    The girls seem to be thinking with God-knows-what, so you're going to have to be the one to set things on the right path. Make sure that everything you do is logical and ties in to the bottom line and that they know it - it's business, not personal. Besides, if you cannot make a clear connection between your actions and the bottom line, you're wasting your time and energy. On the basis of past history, the two guys are on very thin ice, and if they want back in they're going to have to agree on the stipulated conditions WHICH ARE SPECIFICALLY DESIGNED TO ENSURE THAT THE JOB GETS DONE AND THAT THE WHOLE TEAM ACHIEVES THE BOTTOM LINE. I also suggest that if the girls want the guys back in the team so badly, they have to put their money where their mouth is and act as guarantors to ensure that the guys meet their commitments - they're the ones who want the bums back, so it's their job, not yours.
