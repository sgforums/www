    Before I begin, I just wanna say this: 5 months to marriage is playing fire with your love life. There are a significiant number of couples out there who can't even maintain a BGR for 5 months - I truely doubt your friends are truely ready. Why rush marriage if you plan to spend the rest of your life as husband and wife - it doesn't matter if you both spend a little more time as bf/gf right?

    Ok, regarding your concern, its normal. Woman, after their romance-filled-dream teenage age, would look for qualities more than just simple I-love-you, you-love-me. In fact, many come to realise that to make a relationship function well, other important elements has to be fulfilled as well.

    You loved him no doubt, and you are right about the part where there is no comparison and you begin to wonder if he is truely the right guy for you. There could be better guys out there ya? My advice is that: drop this thought. Love is mutually exclusive. In simple - when you truely love someone, others don't matter because they are magically programmed to be smaller than your current partner. Don't look at the dyamanic environment around you - look at what you want. Is he the guy you want? Or rather, the qualities he display? If yes - that's enough for this aspect. You love him - you don't love what he could offer (although this affects the kind of relationship you will be in, but like all other things in life: we embark the ship, we stay until the ship sinks and we have no choice but to leap)

    There is no financial security? Fair enough - throw yourself a decent amount of years to gain whatever financial security you both need. 5 years? 10 years? All that doesn't matter - what you ought to do is not to look at your relationship in figures and sums. Lead a each new day as it is, while working towards your objective for each other. TREAT HIM ACCORDING TO WHAT HIS STATUS IS TO YOU - a boyfriend.

    P.S: You are worrying over nothing my dear angel. There are times when we have to keep things simple, and perform what we have to do each day and so be it. This would be the time to do that, first. Until when your vision of your relationship is clearer, probably you can move your consideration to the next level. Enjoy your relationship and work hard in it - the rewards will befall if fate grants them.

    Cheers

    Ask yourself this:-
    Is he the one you can grow old with?
    Do you see yourself loving this person 40-50 years down the road?
    Do you see him as the father of your child?
    Can he be a good father, provider and lover?

    He should ask himself the same question about you too.
    A marriage is a match when both party have their life goals aligned.
    It is about 2 people from different background living and sharing their lives together as a team.

    Note: Marriage is not like baking a cake. You must not base your decision upon the length of your b/g relationship.

    About Financial Security:-
    What kind of financial security are you expecting?
    Will you love him less if he is poor?
    If financial security is what you base your marriage decision on. I can assure you, you will be dissapointed.
    In a marriage, it takes 2 to acheive financial security. A rich man can be broke (financially) if he marries the wrong woman( a big spender).
    Remember if you base your marriage on financial condition, financial reason will most likely also be the basis of your divorce.

    Your last question:-
    Future husband vs boyfriend. what is so different about the 2?
    Treat your boyfriend like how you would treat your husband.
    Ask yourself this :-
    Do you want your boyfriend to treat you differently after you get married ?
    Are you going to change into another person after you get married ?

    Reason: If your boyfriend cannot treat you good now, he will not treat you any better after you are married to him. Same goes to you.

    Lead a happy life. Don't start taking each other for granted. Once you do, it's not worth it anymore.
