    First off, gonna have to disclaim all errors stated within this message! This is not a legal advice! They are entirely based on my personal and not professional experience & knowledge; it is still your responsibility to research the authencity of my statements!

    Get the details of the other driver. Call up your insurance company, they'll advise you to send in your car for inspection of damage.

    Your insurance company will proceed with the claims, and inform you about what to do accordingly. Usually this will happen within the day or 2nd.

    If it was a hit and run, you need to at least have the *correct* number plate. Go to your nearest police post, and make a police report. You will also need to state that you would like the police to charge him with criminal actions. The police will then advise you if they will charge him. If not, they may or may not advise you if it is plausible to bring legal proceedings towards him in your personal capacity (ie: sue him), but ultimately you will need to find your own lawyer to advise you on this.

    It takes about a week for them to track down the dude, and they will then send you a courtesy letter regarding what they have charged him with.

    Ok, all that out of the way, so exactly what happened??

    Dear BaoBao,

    I've asked around, & this is what I've managed to find out regarding what you can do to have your grievance addressed. Draft a formal complaint letter to the Head of Investigations Traffic Police, and list down in chronological order what happened. Although I know it's gonna be difficult Not to be emotional, please at least appear civil & calm in your letter, & Not go in with your guns blazing (you'll know why in the next para).

    After listing everything in chronological order, ask questions, again in a calm & civilised manner, & ask for them to revert to you within the next 5 or 7 or 14 days. THEN, cc. your letter to the Commissioner of Police, & Ministry of Home Affairs.

    Usually, this will spur them into action. I hope this will help you to at least get some justice or deeper investigation into what happened, & some closure.
