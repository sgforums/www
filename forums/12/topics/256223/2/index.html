    We are blinded by our emotions.

    This is a stage whereby everything revolves around how the other person makes you feel.

    This is not a mature form of love and is built on a weak foundation; a co-dependence on each other to fill up the emotional void we all feel from time to time.

    Stage 2: Comfort zone

    As we progress further into the relationship, the infatuation lessens and we move on to something real.

    There is a reduced feeling of being Â“in loveÂ”.

    If the relationship is to progress any further, there must be mutual respect and admiration.

    Not an admiration for their contribution to you. But the contribution to themselves.

    The understanding of their strength and weakness and all their faults led to the admiration of the person as a whole.

    Many people will bail out from the comfort zone.

    The smart ones leave their emotions aside and bail when they realize that there are far more faults than good points in the other partyÂ’s character.

    The immature ones leave the relationship because they make the mistake of thinking the relationship is in trouble because they are no longer feeling the infatuation.
