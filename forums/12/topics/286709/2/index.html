    The prescribed punishment must be reasonably equivalent to the harm caused to the victim, taking into account the manner in which the harm is expressed.

    When he say: ''i'm going to splash hot water into your face'' did you apprehend an immediate fear of physical harm?

    If so, then he would have assaulted you.

    However, taking into account the fact that you responded in such a way as to cause some harm (or attempt to cause), it would be a persuasive basis for an inference of non-apprehension of fear.

    Was your battery (a contact with another, whether or not amounting to hurt or physical pain, with an intention of causing such contact) a reasonable response to such a threat of violence, taking into account the injury caused by you and the tone of voice and manner of body position and gesture of your victim?

    If not then
    a) You will be culpable in causing harm to your boyfriend
    b) liable to pay for any medical expenses as required

    Was the response an effective (to the mind of the victim) way of preventing such a threat from being carried out? Or was the response a reckless act which would potentially accelerate the violent party into carrying out his aforesaid threat?

    If so, then your act of slapping him was

    a) Reckless as to endanger own life
    b) A case of mutually-intended assault
