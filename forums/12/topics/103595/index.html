    Favouritism.

    This concept does not just apply to parent-child.

    Let me put an analogy here.

    You're a 8 year old girl who has just gotten her first doll. You're happy with it, and you keep on playing with it over and over. Then your parents get you another doll. You stop playing with your first doll as much, as your new doll is prettier and better. Then you get more and more dolls.

    While it is debatable that the first doll will always hold a special significance, do the other dolls not lose out to their predecessor because each of them are newer and better?

    Lets link this to parents.

    Parents have their first child, they spoil and pamper them, and treat them very well. A few years later another baby comes. Their attention will be shifted to the new baby for several reasons. The new baby definitely needs more care and attention, while the other doesnÂ’t. Hence the first child may feel neglected by their parents.

    Depending on which child gives them more joy, they may tend to favor that child more. However, I do feel that love cannot be truly explained, much less the love a parent has for a child. I am of the view that parents do love each child equally, though they may hide that in their heart when that child causes them hurt. We all do that.

    A bit lengthy, but thats my view on this.
