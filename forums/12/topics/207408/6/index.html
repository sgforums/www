    Hey, just giving my views.

    If you're 16 and retained does that mean you're taking your 'O' Levels this year or next? If its this year, it's time to start chionging. if its next year, there's still time for improvement but you definately need to start now. EVERYONE hates studying - we all feel sleepy when we mug. But that's no excuse for not trying. If you lack the discipline and determination now, you will lack it no matter what you do in the future. It's best you face up to that fact now and stop giving yourself excuses.

    Even if you do poorly for the 'O's, you could go to ITE and that is certainly not a dead end. Skilled workers are always needed and you can weigh your options from there. Pooling funds and starting your own business (in bikes since that's where your interests lie) is not a bad idea. You can learn the trade and save up while working for someone else first.

    Joining the army will seem like a enticing option but i urge you to think twice about it. The first 2 contract renewals for NCOs are very difficult (not talking about commissioned officers here) even though the money is relatively good. If you fail to renew your contract, you will be cast out of the army with NO marketable skills. Its far worse than losing your job outside if you ask me.

    Someone said you could be a hawker. Why not? It's respectable, good work and it certainly can give a damn good living.

    The service industry needs fewer qualifications than most places and certainly interacting with people is much better than the monotony of other types of work.

    Point is, it's not the end of the road. Everyone can give advice but it's up to you to try. (You spend a hell lot of time on this forum. Perhaps its time to cut down till you get your studies sorted out).
