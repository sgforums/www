In a modern world like this is, not many people would bat an eyelid about sexual activities between couples, especially those involved in a relationship. However, too much focus on sexual intimacy in the budding of a relationship poses a big problem. That is the understanding of each other.

    However, how many married couples have had great sex life before they were married and then after getting married, only to have it go downhills and their marriage on the rocks?

    Countless examples. So the problem doesn't lie with the sex. It lies with understanding each other. And this process takes time. In marital surveys, the number one complaint is the feeling of not being understood by one's partner. When a couple in a new relationship focus on the sexual intimacy, no matter how loved one may feel right now, it will eventually fade and go downhills once conflict arises. And if they do not practice how to handle conflict and understand what turns each other on and off (not the physical aspect!), and what each other's quirky habits are, problems will arise in future.
