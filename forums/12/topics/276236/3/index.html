    some people learn driving faster than others. "talent" you can say, just like those young english footballers.

    don't be afraid to make mistakes, it won't be the end of the world even if your car somehow stalls in the middle of junction and cause the biggest traffic hold up in the history of singapore.

    anyway, try to learn from a driving school instead of a private instructor. you might be able to save money, but the failure rate is very very high, and you'll end up spending $150+ to book another test date, not to mention warm up drives before the test itself. Just how much can you save?

    I'm 18, and I got my class 3 driving licence in the space of two months. i enrolled in SSDC on the 2nd of march this, started my first driving lesson the very next day, completed all 4 stages listed in the student handbook in 2 and a half weeks, passed my final theory in april, and got a test date on 16th may.

    voila. spent just $1.7k, including all the theory tests + enrollment fees everything.

    but be prepared to spend more if you're a slow learner. everybody's different.
