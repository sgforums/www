    Of course it certainly doesn't 'hurt' to try one more time, but like a drop of dew into a pool of lake, the ripple effect surely doesn't only limit itself to that one drop, or that one particular try for the matter of fact - it's a chain effect.

    In fact, most of the time, a typical man would tell himself he should give himself another try, using the reason you have given above and as MCsquare has mentioned: Only the individual will determine the worth of persistence. This is where he would probably decide to invest more and when he failed the second time round, this time, with the increased liability he has incurred, he would either attempt to pursue a 'break even' or leave with the trauma of greater lost.

    Do you gamble? Because yunhaier is a gambler and in gambling, especially when one is on the losing streak, it's difficult to command our emotions to cut loses, especially when one harbour the thought of 'breaking even'. In the end? You probably lose more.

    Why?

    Because it doesn't hurt to try one more time.

    P.S: The cosmic lesson is often not about hardwork because if it's the lack of hardwork that is missing from the equation, then he would probably have succeeded. It's about dealing with rejection and how to channel those energies to evolve oneself.

    Cheers
