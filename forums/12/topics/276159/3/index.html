As a matter of fact, it's precisely during the wooing stage (i.e. the stage before you formalize the relationship and both parties, implicitly or explicitly, understand they have a commitment to each other as more than just friends) that one should really observe and trust your gut instinct on where this relationship (or lack thereof) could potentially be heading. It's precisely also during this phase where you'd most need to let nature take its course IMO - for most, the concept of having nature delivering a verdict on the destiny of a relationship could stem from anything ranging from whether there's chemistry between the pair (i.e. for talking, common interests, etc) to whether one's partner actually remotely fits the stereotype of what one wants in a partner. In truth, in such instances, much as many may not share my views, I actually do not sympathise with the women who think they have been led on by guys and if anything, it's the guys who have done right by them. The reason being, most of my mates who pull the plug at this point very rightly cites potential lack of communication and incompatibility as long-term obstacles that could potentially rock the relationship - to a very large extent, I have to agree with that for there're obviously some traits and characteristics which you cannot enter into a relationship without them being clearly established (regardless of how much you'd like to give them the benefit of the doubt).

    Obviously, I'm in complete agreement with you that, should the abovementioned issues be redressed, then the ball should be on the guy's court to seal the deal and formalise the relationship (be it through formally asking for the woman's hand or simply holding her hands and sealing the deal with a kiss). And that's where the wooing stops and dating begins...

    On a sidenote, I think it's pretty hard for a guy to be romantic to a woman who falls in the above category for ultimately, things just do not flow well enough (in most cases) for the bloke to feel appreciated enough or be given the encouragement to offer the brand of romanticism the lady craves. If both parties decide to hold back, what ultimately gives? After all, as with any other types of relationship, it takes two hands to clap.
