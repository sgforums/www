    Originally posted by bismarck:
    suggestions ?
    In case you didn't quite got what I've said the other time, this is a summarised version...

    1. PLAN and schedule the areas and topics to be covered by you on a routine basis (eg. daily <- That's the most practical and idea time frame I can suggest)

    2. Be DISCIPLINE and CONSISTENT in the above plan. Do NOT think of the other 'unnecessary stuffs'. Stay in FOCUS of your OBJECTIVE(S).

    3. Do NOT think of or invite other 'troubles' or things which could keep you OCCUPIED and hence depletes you of TIME and CONCENTRATION.

    4. Schedule proper time for short breaks and rest. ESPECIALLY SLEEP at the end of the day. See these as REWARDS and you DESERVES them. AVOID giving yourself other rewards like chatting over the phone, playing computer games, surf net, watch TV or such... These activities tend to hook you on your time. You CAN'T afford to take the CHANCE. These or such bigger rewards should only come after your EXAMS' OVER. REMEMBER. You're NOT depleting yourself of these... You're just postponing them for MORE IMPORTANT things (your EXAMS)... SET your PRIORITY RIGHT. The way I see it.... You can either choose to ENJOY now and SUFFER, be regretful about it LATER... Or you can choose to 'SUFFER' now and ENJOY yourself after the exams... EIther way, you'll get to 'suffer' and 'enjoy'. The only difference is one comes with a good result and the other DON'T....

    Regards.
