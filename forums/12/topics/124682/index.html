    People can be so cruel and judgmental. They base their opinions on looks, behaviour and often, with the young crowd, what their peers think. I can only ask you to start trying not to let their lack of understanding of you affect your reason for living. If these people don't even bother to get to know you before they judge you, are they worth even your consideration of their opinions?

    If you are quiet and shy by nature, it's possible for others to perceive you as "stuck up". It's really difficult, if you are very introverted, to open yourself to others rightaway, I can understand that. There are many unkind people in this world, but there are really nice ones too. Keep trying, step by step. Try to sit with classmates everyday at the canteen instead of sitting alone, even if you feel they are looking at you strangely. It gets easier as the days go by and it allows your classmates to get to know you alittle at a time.

    If you aren't sure what to do for projects, try asking the group leader, for a start, "I really want to help but I'm not sure what to do. I'd really appreciate it if you could point me in the right direction?" Something along those lines. If you face a problem doing your bit of the project, ask someone, "I've done this (tell them what you've done or what research you've come up with). I'm not sure if it's right. Could I just get your opinion on this?" Chances are you'll get the help you need. Do your best to ignore the looks you're getting.

    To some, other people's opinions of them matter alot. Popular kids or those who have no problem mixing with the "in crowds" can be the most cruel of the lot. But these are also the ones who will have the most difficulty understanding the more quiet and shy ones amongst them bec they have never been put in that position. You have a friend, your religion and you have your family. That's really great. Other friends will come along gradually. If not in school, then as you go along in life. For me, for instance, it's not the quantity of people whom I know or how popular I am. It's the quality of the company I choose to keep and the crowd of friends, however small, whom I know I can trust, confide in and rely on. What's the point of having a huge book of friends who are only superficial?

    Hang on in there, ok? Try talking to a counsellor, if you have one in school. It helps.
