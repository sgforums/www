    Hello,

    I went through that before, I'm still in the process of recovery and I can tell you it's not easy -- I still get attacks every now and then, though less frequently.

    You know, I believed that I was weak, helpless and well, damaged in every sense because of my inability to function like a "normal" person, because of the sheer number of attacks that I suffered from. I kept feeling that there was something wrong with me.

    Coming from someone who has been there, you have you actively learn to love yourself, build up on that a step at a time, take an appreciative step back and look how far you've come since babyhood. Things like that. Kick those negative thoughts, but if they do linger, find an outlet. I used to write all those thoughts down in a diary when they repeated in my head, I wrote accounts of the emotionally abusive memories too. It helped, because after a while, you run out of things to write, and you realise, those problems are just ghosts from the past. Trust me, it takes a while before you get it all out of your system, but write whatever comes to mind, and when you're out of the danger zone, look back at your older entries and try to piece together what's bothering you. You'd find that most of the entries repeat, and you might observe repeating patterns of negativity.

    Just identify the patterns and thoughts, and go about trying to solve them. Avoid hurtful situations if you can afford to, like if socialising stirs an attack, avoid extensive socialising. You are not obliged to entertain anyone if you don't feel like it, and sometimes a simple 'No' can make your day.

    The idea is to break the cycles, and learn to appreciate yourself better.

    Hey, it's not the end of the world. (: Sometimes, we get into these ruts when we didn't receive help earlier, or recognise that we're driving into a ditch. The road to recovery isn't easy, but if you've got support, love and determination, you'd make it.

    I was referred to a counsellor once in sec school, it didn't help me much because I wasn't ready to receive any help. I got this far pretty much on my own, and with the help of my bf. Not saying that talking to a professional doesn't help, but make sure you genuinely want help before you go seeking it.

    Take things a step at your time, I'm sure you'll bounce back.
