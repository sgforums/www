    Wear a cap or something that reasonably covers your head/face when traveling on a train because the CCTV footage in the local train station will be checked.

    If you're looking for food that's free, try looking in grocery stores, they often give away free samples. Try the bakery section, for free cookies or cake, or whatever or if there is one in your area try Costco. Grab a cart and tell the people checking that your mom's waiting for you or whatever. You can also try going through the customer service area, but go to the bathroom before trying the main shopping area. And look like you actually have a purpose. Don't hang about.

    Do not bring your cell phone (unless you can change your number, or the SIM card), debit or credit cards with you, as these can be used to trace your location. If you need to make call to someone, borrow a phone or use a payphone. If you need to purchase something, pay with cash.

    If you don't have a place to stay, you'll have to find shelter elsewhere. Try finding a reasonably safe spot under a bridge, in an alcove, in an abandoned building somewhere, or perhaps in a public building that stays open 24/7. Public libraries, churches, college buildings, airports, and train stations are all possible options
