    Paul may have advised Christians against marrying non-Christians, but it is still legally possible for Christians to marry non-Christians and many have done so. And there are some churches that do allow such unions to be witnessed by the brethren within the church, but the ones I know of are Catholic.

    The form of marriage aside, I'd think the potential problems in the substance of the marriage deserve more attention. Unions like these are often strained because the Christian -- even if he or she would not mention it -- would always face pressure, by nature of the faith, to convert his or her non-Christian partner. Furthermore, when kids come into the picture, thorny issues arise as the parents may often clash with differing opinions on the upbringing of the kids.
