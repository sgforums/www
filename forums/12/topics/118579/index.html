    First of all, I'll be addressing this in parts.

    Originally posted by BakaDead-:
    Is our ability to compromise and forgive decreased? Or is the corporate ladder too harsh and cruel in reality for us to climb?
    No, such behavior have been already present in the past except they probably didn't glorify it. Difficulty is a matter of perspective and ability. The hierarchy of the corporate ladder is a pyramid where most people will fall at the lower levels. Few will ascend.

    Originally posted by BakaDead-:
    After all, our education do prepares us to be employee but can anyone handle it if reality slapped you with the truth?
    Education only prepares you with skills, and most people are unable to become entrepreneurs because they lacked the courage, luck, vision, connections and most importantly the finances to support them. So in the end, they will decide to take an open opportunity where they take minimal risks. Ability to handle reality differentiates between people.

    Originally posted by BakaDead-:
    Ultimately, i dunno what i want in this life.. I want to do things i truly enjoy and even more of quality living. Instead of studying.. do you think i can travel slowly around the world, taking my laptop with me, recording my phrase of life and living my days doing odd jobs which i feel is more meaningful?
    There is a financial instability in this type of lifestyle and you must be prepared to be able to weather long periods of unemployment. Furthermore, it might affect your social lifestyle as you will always be on the move. Thus, you will have to accept leaving loved ones behind as you venture. Other things will include illnesses, and other problems. It is an insecure lifestyle that I would not recommend.

    Changing your perspectives might be the key to achieving happiness.
