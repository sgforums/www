    heh im an fds man too, but please don't think RP life is shiok, i had that conception when i got my posting was like "woohoo!"

    but the truth is FDS always have manpower problems, i dont know about other bases, but my base its common to stand for 8 hours straight, with sbo rifle. its not physically xiong, but actually mentally. i ever kenna 1 man checkpoint stand 11 hours straight. (my friend has did 24 straight, checkpoint jumping) you can hiong and sit down of course, but if u get caught = extra. repeated offence SOL.

    for airbases, rp's still do night duty, so you can finish duty at 1800 and wake up at 0200, its hard to wake up in the middle of the night, for FDS the entire NSF days your body clock is screwed.

    for my base a weekend mount of 72 hours its common to clock up to 28-30 hours, so you rest almost the same hours you do duty. but rest time also counts in the time to walk back to the HQ and send ammo and arms, and bathe. etc so it eats into your rest time.

    its a vocation that you are easily charged, screw up with the ammo sign in out = charged. lost round (which has happened) DB.

    let unauthorsied people into base = charge, its a seems like a simple mistake, but its easy to screw up. after a few hours standing there its easy to drift off.

    but of course dont think im saying its some super elite vocation, the fact is we are overtrained guard dogs. after 3 months of a pretty tough course, all we do is stand, i think its depressing too at times.

    but poster think on the bright side, when we come back for reservist we get it good by just standing there. instead of chionging around in the forest. just tong for your 2 years.
