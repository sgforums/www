    kennyrogers, I find it hard pressed that a problem like the one confronting your relationship could actually consume a relationship as extensively as yours between two adults at your age.

    I'm by no means trying to trivialise your problems. Rather, I think insecurity is a massive problem in your relationship and that, above all else, could very well prove the undoing of your relationship. It is also no coincidence that, through reading your post, I realise how fortunate I am that I have never been in any sort of a relationship with a deeply insecure woman.

    To put things in perspective, a true romance should pretty much be dictated by how much both parties are willing to give, rather than wanting to receive. Insecurity consuming a person is often a terrible anomaly others can do very little about. While the tolerance threshold for insecurity may very much vary from person to person, it goes without saying once an individual's prescribed threshold has been exceeded (which is obviously the case in yours), questions and doubts with regard to the insecure person's commitment would inevitably surface. When things descend to such a pathetic state, I'm not sure if you could actually have any sort of reasonable quality relationship - which is exponentially compounded when you consider a marriage is a relationship for a lifetime - with your mate. In essence, it's high time you evaluated the status quo and decide if this is what you want for the rest of your life, rather than pinning your hopes on some miraculous elixir to fix your mate's insecurity in time to come.

    While I concur with jojobeach's assertion that women are, by and large, insecure creatures, I wouldn't go so far as to make a case for putting up with certain forms of insecurity which are exhaustively uncalled for and overboard. It's perfectly fine that your woman may need you to validate her self-worth from time to time. However, I'm not so sure if you'd still want to be in a relationship with a woman who resorts to seeking other guys' company when she feels lonely and perpetually draws excessive comparisons between how her friends' beaus are treating them and how you're treating her. I don't think it's an exaggeration that confident folks would hardly ever fall remotely close into this category to begin with - if anything, on closer scrutiny, you may very well realise those who are insecure are the ones who can't seem to accept their lot in life and are eternally - and unjustly - worrying about all and sundry which serves little purpose besides unsettling those around them (which you obviously find yourself a victim to).

    In your case, I wouldn't so much fault your girlfriend(not that she's blameless of course!) for causing the woes currently afflicting you for you must have known her to be insecure from the outset. If anything, it's high time you woke up to the realization of you having committed the cardinal sin of wooing someone whose levels of insecurity far exceed your tolerable threshold and decide if you can see a future with such a person. After all, the worst thing you could do now is carry on with the present in the hope that something will change for the better in the future because a blissful marriage isn't exactly something you want to live in hope for.
