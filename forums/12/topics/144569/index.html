    Thanks for the account. You sound pretty mature for a 17-year-old.

    Based on your side of the story, it does sound like your ex-girlfriend can be quite temperamental. Temperamental people are usually not easy to get along with, but she must have some pretty attractive qualities for which you are willing to put up with her flaws and accept her for what she is.

    From your analysis, you do seem to have a good grasp of understanding her character and you may have shown a basic level of trust when she went somewhat incommunicado for more than a week. However, both your accounts did not really touch on how you actually broke up. It may be a touchy topic, but was it she or you who initiated the break-up? Or was there any explicit mention of "break-up" at all -- are you two just going through this "cold war" phase? Such details might help us (as well as yourself) understand the situation better.

    One more thing: About yourself. It is understandable if you flared up as well when facing her outbursts leading to the break-up, but are you a temperamental person like her too? Did your dates often end up in unhappy fights before the break-up? Suppose you got back together again, do you think you could keep yourself in check this time, and not fight with her when she flares up again?

    All in all, it is usually not difficult for ex-lovers to come together again, per se -- all both parties needed to do was to recapture the emotions that brought them together in the first place. BUT the problem lies in answering:
    Why they broke up,
    Why they should come back together after splitting, and
    Why history wouldn't repeat itself and they wouldn't split again.

    I may be wrong, but for now I'd say it is finding these three answers that makes the whole process of patching up so difficult.
