    Mutual Respect

    For starters, the individual must know himself or herself well first. It is vital that the individual's character and personality is maintained when a relationship grows. There is no need for you to have exact views on all issues as your partner. Having respect for what your partner stands up for is more important than being agreeable when not.

    Best of Friends

    Your partner is your lover and also your best chum! Treat your partner as your best friend and do things that you would do with your best friends with him or her too. If you enjoy a certain activity with your best of friends, do it with your partner too.

    Unconditional Love

    A small dose of unconditional love will go a long way. Do not expect that your partner reciprocates what you give out. Do not keep a checklist of what you have shared, but rather, it is more important to concentrate on showering your love.

    Understanding

    Know how your partner feels; whether by listening or by observing his actions. Hold back touchy issues when it is obvious that he has had a bad day in the office. Instead, offer to give a back rub or cook a good dinner instead. When you see that your partner has received a commendation about his work ability, share the joy too!

    Compromise

    Give an inch, and you may well get a mile. In a relationship, it needs both parties to click and a small compromise on each other's part will help to increase mutual understanding. There may be instances when both parties have to work late, but do still work out a schedule to spend time together by adjusting activities that have less priority. Compromise will mean that you are able to see things from your partner's viewpoint and this will only help to strengthen the relationship in the long term.
