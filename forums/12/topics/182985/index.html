    Hi Nikar,

    This may take some time and effort.

    Let's look at your sister's behaviour this way:

    A --> B --> C

    B represents her temper tantrums; A represents the antecedents (or possible triggering events that spur off her anger); C represents the consequences (or reinforcements).

    Firstly, what are the As? What would usually sparkle off her temper tantrums? Your family did the right thing by moving the home PC to the living room, because that is equivalent to moving some possible triggers of her temper tantrums. Are there other triggering factors that your family can work on removing or moving? For example, if your younger brother is usually the person who would spark off her anger (even though he is not at fault), then advise or made arrangemetns for your younger brother to avoid her physically, i.e. maintain distance, or even move temporarily to a relative's place, etc. Don't see this as accomodating her, because you are not. See it as wanting to prevent her nonsense behaviour from happening.

    Secondly, there must have been a lot of Cs in the past, that is, she must have been reinforced in the past. For instance, when she kicks up a fuss, all her family members react over it. I am sure all of you showed that you are upset. I am sure she may get some things going her way when she throws her tantrums. Now, this is the difficult part of it all - IGNORE her when she's throwing a temper tantrum. Pretend nothing is happening. Easier said than done, I know, but if nobody pays any attention to her, she would soon realise there is no point throwing her weights around because nobody notices. Don't give her what she wants too, when she throws her tantrums. For example, if she's kicking up a fuss about your brother taking one more piece of chocolate. Just let your brother continue with that piece of chocolate. No need to ask him to suddenly give it up because sister is getting angry. It is VERY important that she must eventually know that throwing temper tantrums is not the way to get things her way. After all, the world does not revolve around her. Now, her temper tantrums are likely to escalate with all the ignorance, because she would need to try harder to get your family members' attention, reaction, or what she wants. However, if all of you can pull through with your ignorance, there would come a time when her temper tantrums reach a peak, and then after which she would give it up. It's very hard to endure, let me tell you first. But if all of you succeed, the returns are worthy.

    OK. From what I am hearing from your sharing, I must say your sister has been "rewarded" for the past 18 years when she throws her tantrums. That means, whenever she throws tantrums, she is often rewarded by her family members' attention, annoyance, or get her way. (Remember, negative attention, like scolding her, is also attention. You would still be acknowledging her anger when you respond negatively towards it.) It could be that this bad habit of hers is so in-depth that it would be highly difficult for her to reach her peak before such negative behaviour reduces. So if her tantrums become truly unbearable, I suggest you discuss with your parents to get a family counsellor. Get the family counsellor to work with all of you. You said she is likely to be nicer to strangers, so let a stranger give her a very clear message about how wrong her behaviour has been. Also, her behaviour is likely to be labelled as potentially abusive and may one day get her into trouble with the law. (Yes, abusing your own sibling is also against the law. BUT please, you or your family members should NOT be the ones telling her this. Let an outsider do it. If her negative behaviour subsides, then it wouldn't be a need for this message to be brought to her, since after all, you are one family.)

    Now, we also want to encourage her to adopt the right behaviour. So, let's say B is "Not throwing temper tantrums". So you need to work on all the Cs (reinforcements) that you can to encourage her not throwing her temper tantrums. For example, when she is talking to anyone calmly (because there was no need for her to feel jealous or angry), tell her politely how much you all love to talk to her when she talks calmly. She may not be purposedly being nice, but encouraging her when she's NOT being nasty would work better. Coupling this, with the above ignorance when she throws tantrums, she would soon realise that being nice would earn her all the attention she wants. Also, she would also eventually learn that if she wants to get what she wants, she HAS to be nice.

    Pretty hard work, but for the love of your own sister, it's worthy. If not, get professional help. Hope the above helps, somehow.
