    Dear TS, honestly. I'm not sure how long have you been in retail, but let me tell you something. It's mostly the same everywhere, because the "3C methodology" is the most lucrative method of all.

    So long as you're willing to lie through the gaps of your teeth, you'll be able to make incredible amounts of sales.

    Yes, it's like betraying yourself and going against your conscience. However, this is a choice you have to make. "Lie to get more money" or "Be honest and lose some sales".

    And i'll be honest with you, i choose the latter. Cause i've never liked to "sell myself out". I believe that once you've "lost yourself" you lose everything because you've lost all that you once stood for.

    I used to be a promoter for shoes for the holidays cause i'm waiting for school to start. And i work at a rate of 5.50 an hour. And i get 50 cents commission for every pair i sell.

    My job has a decent pay, but it's the commission that makes it a nice paying job. And i actually forgo a few sales because i refused to lie about the product i was pushing.

    A few customers with flatfoot actually approached me and asked me for a reccomandation. But i knew that my brand wasn't that good for people with flatfoot and i actually asked them to buy another brand (Ecco, if you were wondering.)

    I just felt that the commission isn't worth the pain the customers has to endure if she bought it.

    She actually went on to buy a pair of Ecco. But when she came to me, i noticed that the pair she bought was a little loose and i asked her if she felt if it was a little too loose. And she actually realised her mistake and went to get it changed.

    Yes, i didn't earned a single cent from her, but i actually felt so much better knowing that she actually was happy with her purchase.

    Maybe it's because i'm young,  and the problem of financial burden hasn't dawned on me. But at this point in time of life, i absolutely refuse to "sell myself". And i wish this never changes.

    Hope this helps you. Cheers
