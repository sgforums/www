    I'm afraid there's no kenging your way out. When I went for my vocational interview, a sargeant asked me if I wanted to go to Commandos. I asked him if I had a choice, and he said no. I thought that maybe if I had a bad attitude they wouldn't want me, so I said "What the f*ck are you asking me for then?". The next week I got my letter saying that I was selected. As it turns out, that attitude was exactly what they were looking for.

    I went for the inital pilot selection tests as well. A few months before I got my red beret, some recruiter said that I passed and asked if I wanted to report at PLAB for initial training. By that time I was too close to the beret already, so I said, "Don't call me, I'll call you" and hung up on her.

    Anyway, if you are going into Commandos, welcome to the fold. Don't go looking at it as being siong - you'll have plenty of tall stories to tell over a beer by the time you're done! Keep your cool, make a point to get right into the experience, and you will be fine.
