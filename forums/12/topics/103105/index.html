    hello beautiful mind.... i can help.

    i went to Latrobe, Swinburne and RMIT ! so i think i qualify to answer your questions...

    a foreign education is an experience. something that will be with you for life. it can change your perspective of people, the world and your life.

    you can see and experience new things that others do not have the opportunity.....at a price.

    dont think of money as top priority if it isnt a concern; it can be earned later. the chance, you may never have.

    RMIT background.. it was a poly and still very much an ARTS uni. not as recognised as melbourne university, swinburne or latrobe in traditional subjects such as Business. for arts related, yes definitely. (personal opinion but not far from everybody who has been there...)

    Your business program, if you go to aust, try Melbourne University or Monash University. they are hard to get in but very recognised especially melb uni.

    when you get there, make sure you do your best but warning: it wont be easy in the first year as you will have to get used to life there and learn a whole new education system...

    if you need more help, send me a PM. i can advice you on accommodation, unis and agents who can help you..i was just as clueless when i went there so i understand that feeling..
