    Hi All,


    My name is Eric and I am 22 years old.

    I am sharing my problem which I have been facing sometime.

    I have no friends. I find it very hard to make friends. I feel very lonely and sometimes keep wondering what is life for. 

    I am studying in university currently. I dun have any friends there as well and feel very lonely. I find the people there are very choosy when making friends. They tend to prefer those smarter people. I am a average student.  I am now dreading group projects as I dun know anyone. I started to skip lessons gradually and there is a slight drop in my grades. Whenever I see other group of friends being happy together in campus, I feel sad that I do not have any friends to share laughter with. Are university students supposed to be self-centered and selfish people? I tried talking to my lecturer mentor but he tend to avoid that topic. This makes me feel that even lecturers do not care for their students.

    I am sad that for university graduation, there will be no-one who will want to take a group photo with me. This may sound silly, but it will make one feel pain when others are taking group photos happily while you are a loner there.

    I used to have some friends back when I was in primary, secondary, jc and army. However, after each phase ended, those friends also stopped contacting me. I tried my best to keep contact. I always be the first to initiate the conversation. But, they tend to finish off the conversation in a few words. Like they dun seem to be interested to talk. I feel like I'm being a dog going after people, wagging my tail. Eventually, I stopped contacting them. Are friends from each phase (pri, sec etc.) meant to separate after that phase is over?

    In my handphone, there are more smses that I have sent to others. Rather, people smsing me is barely to zero.  During occasions like New Years Day or Christmas, its always me who wish others first. There was never a occasion where one person wished me greeting first. Its really sad thinking about it. Even in Facebook, nobody tends to like or comment on my posts. Eventually, I stopped posting.Even in Facebook chat, nobody chats with me. Its always me trying to chat with others. When I look at photos in Facebook whereby friends happily gather together, it has made me shed tears many times.

    I love to go to cinemas and watch movies. But, nobody wants to go with me. Nobody calls me to go gatherings or outings with them. I always stay at home in weekends due to this. My social life is very pathetic and disappointing. 
    I find this world is very selfish. 

    Does having no friends make one a loser?
    All these loneliness has beginning to make me hate to go outside and always stay at home.

    If this goes on, I dunno how I am going to cope in my job in the future being a loner.

    I wonder why I was born and why I have to go through all these sufferings.

    I wish that I can change all this and get some friends to mix with and have a better social life.

    Looking forward to your advices guys.

    I have the same experience as you, even though I'm already 28 and working. 90% of the SMS I've received are advertisements too. Before I continue, I'm assuming you're not one of those weirdo doing strange things behind the classroom, like chewing your hair or acting like a complete himbo (compare bimbo, or if you're an animal, jumbo). Because if you're too weird, you can't blame people for avoiding you.

    The ugly truth is that people have to look out for themselves. No one is obliged to take care of you (except your parents, when you're younger anyway). Anything more you get from others are bonuses. You have to learn to love yourself and being with yourself first, cos it's the only person who can take care of you even if the whole world crumbles in ashes and you're still alive somehow.

    The uglier truth about the happiness and laughter you see on people's faces when they are together in a social group? They ain't always genuine. Especially in Asian country like Singapore, where we prefer to fit in socially, rather than being too individualistic. If you think about it, it's not that admirable.

    There are two potential paths you can take from here:

    1) Be a social chameleon. Learn to socialise with people, even if they don't click well with you. Put on a facade if you need. With enough practise, you'll get used to it and will have enough skills to manage bigger social circles. Of course, don't demoralise yourself by questioning the genuinity of social connections made. This is a useful skills to have once you start working.

    2) Be yourself. Find people who are like you to hangout with. Lesser but closely-knitted friends. This is where quality matters more than quantity. These are the people who won't judge you and accept you as who you are. Find activities you would like to do when you're alone.

    Social skills wise, I suggest you find your style/brand, so people can identity with you or remember you easily. It's ok to be abit quirky sometimes. Eg. I'm bloody good at making lame statements, and may sometimes start singing in the public, when with my friends.

    Another thing to learn is not to take things too personally. Most people don't warm up to each other that easily. It's not just a "Happy New Year" sms that people will start contacting with you all over again. Some people might not find that they could click well with you, hence they do nothing more than just return the courtesy. Also, there could be a case of being too over-friendly too fast. Don't get too sticky!

    If you're keen in boardgames or karaoke, just pm me. I usually meet up some friends on a monthly basis for that.
