    Some tips on how to survive in the first stage of army life, BMT:

    BMT:
    1) make friends, make friends, make friends.

    Start immediately. Try not to make enemies. Friends will help us get things done, reinforce each other's knowledge of lessons, defend us against backstabbers, and generally raise our morale. They may become our pillars of strength. Having friends means BMT will be alot more enjoyable. Very important.

    Help everyone, don't treat anyone badly, even if they may not treat you well.

    2) Just do as you as told.

    Instructors detest smart alecks, who assume things can be done their way, that their way is better. Army doesn't work that way. One thing you have to learn is to: not be individualistic. Just do things the way you are told, the way you are taught. No room to be individualistic in BMT.

    You will have learn things quickly, and do things quickly the way you are told. Never assume.

    3) Understand that Army culture is different from civilian life.

    The moment you step foot in camp, understand that you are entering a different world. This is not to make you feel stress, but to tell you to expect that at the start of BMT, instructors will be especially hard on all the recruits. Everyone, not just you. They will use all sorts of colourful language on all of you, they will harass all of you. This is done to establish control, so that recruits will understand that in Army, you have to obey orders. Don't fight this. Roll with it.

    Understand that instructors don't have anything personal against you. When they use vulgar language on you, when they punish you, dont take it personally. Generally, it is done to teach you about something. Just relax about it, man. Don't hate them, this only wastes energy and distract you what you need to do, what you need to learn. Instructors will begin to let up a little once they see that the recruits are tranforming to suit the system, when they learn army discipline, the way to think and the way to do things in army.

    BMT will be tough at the beginning, but as you begin to understand the system and make more friends, things will get better.

    4) Be strong, there's nothing to be afraid of.

    The transition from civilian life to army life may take a little getting used to. One my first day of BMT, I thought to myself, "how can I live like this for the next 2.5 years?" But once I learnt that my mindset had to change, that I was no longer a civilian but a soldier in training, then i began to understand what i was going through is necessary. So you will learn to change your mindset, the sooner the better.

    Also know that treatment of soldiers nowadays is strictly regulated and monitored. Instructors may make life difficult for you, but they will also take care of you physically. Good instructors will take care of you emotionally and mentally. But not every instructor is good, so you have to take care of yourself. Make friends, they will help you get through it all.

    -------

    End with this cliche - many people have got through it, don't be afraid that you cannot make it.
