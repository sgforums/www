    as far as i know there are codes of conduct for psychiatrists and licensed counselors.....like promise of confidentiality....
    That's what I thought too... But after I went for a part-time counsellor course, I learnt to my surprise there is no such thing as 100% confidentiality... Let me explain why...

    First, before a counselling session, the counsellor MUST tell the client there is NO promise of confidentiality and the client must accept before the session proceeds.

    Confidentiality is maintained with respect to the SAFETY of the client, even though the client may hold it against the counsellor.

    The counsellor cannot be with the client 24/7, so for cases where the client's or others' lives may be endangered, information CAN and MUST be revealed to the ppl involved...

    Classic example, client is suicidal, so immediate family members must be informed in case client decides to ask family members for some sleeping pills and they give him/ her unwittingly...

    Or client shares that she intends to take a chopper and kills her father who has abused her sexually since young, information must be divulged to the relevant authorities.
