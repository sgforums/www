    Hey TS,

    I think you really need to settle your wife down for a talk. Preferably, find a moment in your life when you are not quarreling, and are level headed. Perhaps, after a good meal, or something good. Find a good premise to begin with.

    Tell her how you feel. That despite the friction and quarrels, divorce is something that you would refuse to consider in the first place. And try to talk to her and find out why she's always "proposing" a divorce whenever you quarrel.

    This talk is not a confrontation, a real heart to heart talk. Avoid using words like "You always say", "Threaten", "Anyhow." Be thoughtful about the words you use. Women are sensitive creatures.

    There could be many reasons why she reacts in such a manner. Perhaps she's feeling insecure. Perhaps you're not spending enough time with her. Or maybe she thinks you're having an affair ( I hope not).

    Quarrels are unavoidable, and neither are they the source of your problems. Be committed to have "good" fights with her, where both of you don't threaten to walk out, and are resolute in working things out together.

    Cheers mate!
