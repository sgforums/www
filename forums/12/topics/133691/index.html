    Dear Malcolm,

    If there's 1 word that raises my hackles, it's the word "victim". I believe whether or Not you're a victim is by CHOICE. You can choose to wallow in self-pity, indulge in "Why me?" syndrome & drink yourself silly. Ultimately, who's gonna end up paying except yourself?

    Of course you're sad when a relationship ends, you're Not alone, MILLIONS of people in the world go through break-ups everyday. If we had a million drama queens everyday walking around swigging alcohol, withdrawing from people, we'd have ZILLIONS of drunk hermits by now.

    I've gone through break-ups, & even WORSE, believe me, much much WORSE. But I've refused to be a victim & I refuse to let others think I'm a victim. I don't need others to pity me, I only need the support of those who're close to me. I will NOT allow the people who shattered me to see what they'd done. In fact, if they can hurt me like this, it shows they don't give a damn about me. Then why should I ruin my life over people whom I shouldn't care about?

    Wake up, will you?

    I know this is harsh, but the word "victim" is so loosely used, it irritates me. Victim of rape, acceptable. Victim of murder, acceptable. Victim of robbery, acceptable. Victim of a breakup? What a joke.
