    You can report to MOM, but not like they will do anything.

    To accuse your store manager of discrimination is difficult, but you can just say you "THINK" he is instead of accusing.  Then bring up your extra long hours and colleagues who don't have to do it.

    The other thing is, you are within your rights as an employee to reject work that is not within the scope of your employment contract. I.e. you are a clerk, but your boss demands that you wash the toilet as well (which is not what a clerk does).  If your contract doesn't state that you have to wash the toilet, you don't have to. 

    When you tender your resignation, you HAVE to give notification as per your contract.  So does your employer, if he asks you to leave.  If you are both mutually obligated to give 1 month's notification, he has to pay you 1 month's salary if he fires you on the spot, Or, you have to pay him 1 mth's wages if you insist of leaving immediately. 

    So do check out MOM's website regarding disputes if you think you still want to lodge a complaint.

    http://www.mom.gov.sg/employment-practices/disputes-and-claims/Pages/advisory-services.aspx
