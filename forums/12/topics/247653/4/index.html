    Now you believe eh?

    Ok I remember the last time I got hooked to video games or something that takes up most of my time in a day. It was hard for me to get over it. Basically I did not feel the importance of other things over what I was addicted to: spending more time with family and friends, self-reflection, sleep, etc, etc.

    I asked myself, "Why am I addicted? Is it really that important to me? Or am I using it as an excuse to escape from something? Or just simply I got nothing of value to do?"

    When I found out that I have better things to do, more important things to look out for and more valued stuffs to focus on, I slowly remove the time that I am engrossed in to do more of what is beneficial and better for me and everybody around me.

    So let me ask you: what do you value more than your addiction? Family? Friends? Sleep?

    Find it, rediscover the values and prioritise again. You will find new and better meaning to your life than simple addiction to what you think you are addicted to.
