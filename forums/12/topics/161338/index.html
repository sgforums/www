    I think most of you are confused on the concepts of insurance, so allow me to explain... If the policy's insured is a minor, by that we mean under the age of 16, then of course the insured CANNOT BE AN APPLICANT/ POLICY OWNER, but his or her immediate family member. ( For the singles, its their parents, and for the married, its their spouse) Therefore, given that the insured suffers a slip disc and is declared partially disabled, the benefits goes to the APPLICANT. The benefits ALWAYS GOES TO THE APPLICANT, and it doesnt matter if the applicant has been paying for the premiums or not.... However, if the policy insured is above 16yo, then when buying the policy, the parent can choose to be the applicant or allow the child(insured) to be the applicant. Either way, again the benefits goes to the applicant.. The person who pays the premiums is not taking into consideration upon distribution of the benefits.
