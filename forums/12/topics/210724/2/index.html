    I'm going to go out on a limb here and propose that they key issue here is the attitude that you take to your condition.

    As others here have noted, you suffer no lack of communication skills - your post is clear and well-articulated. As you have noted yourself, your speech impediment undermines your confidence despite your positive qualities, and if you take this as a starting point, everything else can be seen to follow it.

    Getting away from the abstract, I put forward the case of a close friend of mine. She lost her hearing at the age of four owing to a childhood disease, and in fact was not diagnosed as being hearing-imparied for the next five years. By that time, teachers had mistaken her hearing impairment as an intellectual disability.

    Here's the damdest thing - she never took any notice of it, at least not apparently. She became student president at pre-university, and went on to finish her honours degree, and on top of that has gone on to do anything that any able-bodied person would do and then some. The clincher is that for years I'd wanted to date her, but do you think she was ever short of attention? Hell no - I never got to get a word in edgewise.

    To this day we remain close friends. She's been with her partner for seven years and I'm engaged to someone else, but even today I'll freely admit that her outlook on life is wonderfully attractive. She's never let anything stop her, shallow-minded detractors be damned - that's just downright sexy in my book.

    At the risk of doing a Tony Robbins, the bottom line is that you have a choice - focus upon your shortcomings and let them cripple you, or ignore them and make best use of what you do have. It's up to you.
