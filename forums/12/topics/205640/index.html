    I experienced this during a period in my life as well. Now that I think back, I believe there are several contributing factors

    - stubbornness: unwilling to accept that I was fully responsible for the mistakes I made.

    - naiveity: Lack of experience in handling the tasks given to me. Also never bothered to ask people when I was unsure, and made assumptions which turned out to be wrong.

    - Snap decisions: I didn't bother to think though the task thoroughly and calmly.

    - Overconfidence/Lack of confidence: prior to this cock up period in my life, I was enjoying some degree of success in what I did, and that built up my pride such that it got in the way of learning. Even as I realised that what I was doing was not working, I resisted change and refused to humble myself to learn from others. So basically, deep down my confidence was shaken, and that made me kanchiong spider. But because of pride, I refused to accept that I was screwing up. This is a recipe for disaster.
