    When a girl tells a guy that his "courting is not enough" for her to accept his love, it usually means she has no love for him. There are two possible reasons why such an excuse is used:

    1. She does not find him an ideal mate, but she does not want to lose him as a friend -- a potential "best guy friend" -- either.

    2. She could be waiting for "something" to move her into loving him. While it is possible for such a relationship to work, the odds are not high. The problem usually is not in how hard it is to get the "something"; but the possibility that when that "something" is eventually gotten, the girl would realise it isn't what she actually wanted, and the guy would still end up with no love. Or that the girl could lose her patience and go to another guy instead.

    If you don't wish to fight the odds by changing yourself yet you can't give her up, you could give her some time instead to date a few so-called "happening and romantic" guys first, let them maybe break her in along with her heart, and she just might come to realise the advantages of being with "boring and unromantic" guys. That is, if you don't mind playing the waiting game of course.

    Nevertheless, it is heartening to hear that you have bravely tested the waters and have identified her incompatibility as a mate, thus saving yourself from some potential big-time hurt. Perhaps the next challenge is to ask yourself whether you can continue to be "just a friend" after having been rejected more than once.

    Therefore she has chosen an alternative: attempting to shape her given variables to convince her heart to accept you into a relationship. Her psyche would probably react like this:

    'Ok fine, I think he's a good guy. I don't know if I love him anot (or even so, not till that extend), but I don't mind giving him a chance. But I must have that reason for myself to give him that chance.

    Advice?

    There's no such thing as zero risk in relationship: there's no relationship without tears as well - it's all about decisions and the path we opt for. If you have come so far, just to give it up, you got to tell yourself that you won't ever look back and regret.

    You could also TRY to fill-in-the-blank with what's missing currently and create a relationship with her. It could be difficult to define each other's love definition, individual expectation, etc - however, we will learn two very important skills: compromising and evolving.
