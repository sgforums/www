    Do you agree that there are two sides to a person - for example a person can be sometimes negative and positive ?  Or do you think that one side is always dominant in a person ?  That is, a person is predominantly a sad person or predominantly a happy person ?  

    Would anyone wish to remain sad anyway ?  Or is sadness a result of circumstances which are less fulfilling ?

    I realise that I keep falling back into depression.  Am alone most of the days.  Not sure if this is my nature or due to my circumstances.  I am not always sad, though.  There was a period of my life when I was with happy people and engaged in meaningful work.  I was happy then.

    If I believe that I am a naturally sad person, then would it be counter-productive to change myself to be happier ?  Maybe I should accept the sad side of me ?  Society celebrates happiness and admires happy people.  However sadness is the other side of the same coin.  Sadness should not be despised upon nor avoided.  Can it be avoided anyway if it is part of you ?   Many great artists are melancholy people.  It could just be the way a person (brain) is wired.

    I am a person with much conflict.  The more I seek fulfilment, the further I am from it.
