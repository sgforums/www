    Read this topic, and seemed like something i've always wanted to tell ppl.

    When u r in uni, all u think is aceing the exams and getting the highest grade as possible. But in truth, what u learn in uni is just a fraction of what is needed in the working life. Sure, a good grade gives u a good start, but its not the absolute thing.

    Maybe im not the best person to comment, as I've only been working for 7 years. Hopefully ppl with more experience can comment. But for the past few years, i've been conducting many interviews etc, so i wish to share out some experience in it.

    When i interview ppl, i dont really look for the absolute best. Instead, i tend to look for those that can fit into the current working team culture and environment. Being the smartest candidate with the best grade doesnt guarantee u a job - instead, u need to appeal to the interviewers' needs. I also look for candidates with a good attitude, pleasant personality, and able to handle pressure well - i usually grill them with tough questions, just to see how they respond, and not really to listen to the answers. Many candidates tend to answer rudely or fumble or be angry - big no no. Dun be rude to the interviewer! ... Once i had a candidate asking me to give him a cup of water before the interview. Hahaha! I should hv just kicked him out of the room immediately.

    Another thing i wish to share is that once i got out from my uni (a very very long time ago), i immediately discovered that watever i learned in uni was completely insufficient. Everything was much more advanced, and i had to learn it on the job. Tat's when i realised that the knowledge is not as important as the other soft skills u learn during ur uni days, things like doing extensive research, writing well, problem solving and analysis, etc. ... In fact, the module that contributed the most to me was something totally unrelated to my field of studies, but thought me how to debate issues with others, and form my own viewpoints and defend them.

    In other words, dont worry too much abt the grades, and most definately dont concentrate on it until there's nothing else to show off the employer except ur grades!
