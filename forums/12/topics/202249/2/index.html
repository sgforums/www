    To the threadstarter:

    I have flat foot + heart murmur + elevated blood pressure + acute tinnitus + astigmatism. And I'm PES B.

    Type all those terms in Google if you have no idea what they mean.

    Just because you're flat-footed and asthmatic does not mean that you will be instantly downgraded to a lower PES status. It also has to do with the degree of your medical condition. If your medical condition isn't all that bad, then you will be considered combat-fit. Personally, my own flat foot didn't hinder my normal physical activities; I just can't stand straight for extended periods of time. As it stands, PES B means that they won't post you to the ultra-siong vocations.

    You're going to be in the army; live with it. Finding ways and means to weasel your way out of the training and regimentation. Otherwise, I can tell you that you will always have a negative view of the army, and the Pygmalion Effect will come into play.

    'What is the Pygmalion Effect?' you may ask. It is when something bad happens because you think it will happen. Since you keep thinking that you should try to weasel your way out of the army, you will never have a good army experience.

    You should instead be learning more on how to cope with it both mentally and physically.
