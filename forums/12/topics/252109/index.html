    There are pros and cons to both sides.

    Those not so well to do may have to struggle with daily expenses, while the rich ones have to carry unecessary burdens or expectations. From personal experience, being born in a rich family does not necessarily mean a better life. For example, one will live a highly protected lifestyle with little chance of making 'real' friends or perhaps gaining life experiences.

    Wherever you go, you would be under scrutiny from the public. Especially if you have generations of family reputation to protect and uphold. Parents may have the thinking "Here are the resources for you to study, all you need to study and nothing else" and expects you to surpass them.

    Did one ask to be born in such circumstances? Why cant I go out with friends of 'different class', browse shopping malls together, enjoy a plate of hawker char kway teow alfresco style? Or perhaps get a part time job at McDonalds? I may not earn much but at least I can have a sense of fulfillment and knowing the value of the money I earn.

    This life is suffocating. Until now, I do not know what happiness is. In my mind, happiness is to be free from all this, social constraints and all that. But when that time comes, all I can do is make the most of what I have.
