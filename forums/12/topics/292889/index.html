    If you are still studying, I would advise you not to change your job.

    Firstly, you need the paycheck.
    Secondly, sometimes, a new job may be overwhelming that you will find it hard to get used to it, and thus, your studies will suffer as a result of it.

    However, if you can find another job with a higher pay and less responsibilities than your current job, by all means and change it.

    I guess the last thing that you would want to happen to you is that your new paycheck cant compared to your current one and you have additional responsibilities which will make your studies suffer.

    Biomedical field is more towards R&D. Lab-based work. The sales part is like Pharmaceutical Management.

    Bachelor degree -> Lab technician/junior research scientist. (basic analysis)

    Master degree -> Senior lab technician(research) research scientist, supervisory position.

    PhD -> Research. Now Singapore only have very few of PR(Private Researcher); Those who research based on their individual findings. If you can get to this level, it's remarkable.

    Discovering the blockbuster drug are the Pharmaceutical specialization. Similar field with the BMS, but different league. BMS is more towards Life Sciences, while Pharmaceutical is about Drugs.
