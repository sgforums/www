    Learn to accept reality and survive it. What you experience is quite common in many companies. So, moving on may not solve your problem. Having said this, however, does not mean that you should not move on. There are more friendly companies out there, but it may be a long search. Can you afford the search? This depends on your financial needs as well as your patience and job interest.

    For a lot of people, they just accept life as it is. For some, they job hop each time they reach a point where they cannot take it anymore. I have a number of friends who change job every so often. But most important of all, learn to relax even when everything is falling apart. At the worst, you get sacked or the company folds up.

  • Agony Aunt 123
    9 Nov 04, 16:59
    Hi Pure Milk 28

    Company Politics are everywhere
    Where there are people, there are politics

    Birds of the same feather, flock together

    The best is to remain neutral
    Singapore is so small , and even if we should leave this workplace,
    we may bump into familiar figures later in another office, you know.

    Causes of politics are different ......
    Some politics started out of people's ego of challenging
    one another , envy, jealousy, pride, competition , insecurity etc. for their own
    personal gains .... alot of human conflicts arise because almost everyone
    desires to be a winner

    However, I believe that it takes two hands to clap..
    so, no single side is perfect ...each has its own reasons for doing so ...
    As human beings' behaviour is likened to animals, it's only their
    natural tendency to behave like this

    It's up to us to perceive the politics in the office ...
    Most freshies, fresh grads, or people who have just joined the
    workplace couldn't tolerate ... but after sometimes ...they have to gradually
    learn to accept this as reality ...

    For example :
    I came across many freshie grads ( poly & U ) being treated unfavorably by
    their senior colleagues, especially those of 40 years and above age range
    and who do not have diploma & degree themselves. In our heart, we do feel
    for all these freshies, rite? We tend to ask : Why all these 40 something years old people have to be like that??? ( But not all 40 something years
    old people are like that lah)

    We can't expect our colleagues to be like our friends and family members ...
    Occasionally, if we are blessed, we may come across colleagues who
    value relationships with us , ya ... , and whom we desire to meet everyday in the office!

    Ok, one last thing :
    If we don;t like office politics, don;t be an office politician ourselves also, ya.
    Do unto others as what we would like them to do back to us - that's fair
    because not everyone of us can still turn the other cheek after we have been slapped the other side, ya.

    Take gd care!

    Agony Aunt 123
