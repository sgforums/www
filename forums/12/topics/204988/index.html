    Consider these:

    Course A

    1) Are you in a hurry to complete the degree? How much can you save for the difference of 1.5 yrs?

    2) Which organisation are you intending to apply for jobs... If you are looking for Government sectors then better choose Course B

    Course B

    1) How recognised can it be than local university... unless you are talking about top overseas university e.g. Cambridge etc...

    2) Longer duration normally equal to more effort, money and time being involved... can you afford it?

    Apart from the above, the are many factors that you need to consider like what are the modules in each of these courses... and are they what you want to learn or interested to learn... How much help are they of help to you in your future careers...

    Most importantly is that you much spend more time looking into the two courses and also into more other courses offered by other universities... and don't jump into it... coz the degree is going to stay with you for the rest of your life...
