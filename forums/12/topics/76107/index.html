    With reference to my posting, well I'm studying computing and logic and thus enthralled about how to make a decision given such ambigiuous situation and scenario.

    Like in many examples I already stated, there are so many possible outcomes and possible decision to pick. How do a guy know when is a gal's want is a "want" and not a "don't want" and vice versa.

    By stating "if u know...", that is assuming that a guy knows some information but this information is an unknown variable or parameter since the gal never says anything about this. Then we're back to the chicken and egg problem -- problem is bcos a guy doesn't know he has to guess and not bcos a guy knows something (gal's preference or thinking or opinion or mentality), then he can make a good guess and decision.

    That's the assumption that guys already know certain information or mathematically speaking called premises which are known to be true. Which enables them to make a decision.

    For example, if we say "if you know she likes you, then make a move..." The problem is a guy doesn't even know if she likes him (and she'll never say it), so how to decide if a move is appropriate? The condition is that "if you know she likes you" and this is transitivity in mathematics and logic.
