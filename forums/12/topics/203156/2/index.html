    Most of you may set to think that failing modules in Polytechnic is not a big deal and that students can always repeat the module the next semester.

    Just to share to you what I have seen in my own eyes when I was in poly. I sincerely hope that those who think that failing modules isnt a big deal to reconsider this thought again. I have friends whom have failed one or two modules during their course of study in poly. They thought that it was of no big deal but only to realise that they have in fact ruined many great job opportunities on the way too that could have been offered to them only if they didnt fail any modules.

    I am not here to demoralise those who have modules failed but my intention is to bring out the reality. Sorcing good grades not only helps you in your future higher education pursue but also grooms you future. Dont you want to have a good family and sustainable income to live a comfortable life? Althought Singapore do grant credentials to private university like SIM with its private degree courses from UOL & RMIT, the problem remains that how much does the employer values this certificate as? Will you be ended up walking a big round but yet achieve nothing?

    Nothing is too late if you want to change it RIGHT now... the decision is yours and do make good use of it. Good chance rarely comes twice.
