    New project to integrate new immigrants into the community to be launched.

    Posted: 12 January 2010 2157 hrs

    SINGAPORE: A new project to integrate new immigrants into the community will be launched in March.

    Called the Singapore Citizenship Journey, it will be piloted in three grassroots constituencies, Tanjong Pagar, Sembawang and West Coast. About three to four hundred participants will be involved.

    New citizens will take part in the project that includes interactive online elements, historical site visits and grassroots sharing sessions.

    The English language can be used a medium for integration and the council is hoping to tap into the language's potential by encouraging more immigrants to take up English courses.

    A new website will be launched to help match members of the public to courses that suit their schedule, learning needs and budget.

    These efforts are part of the National Integration Council's efforts to create a public, private and people network to support social integration.

    11 organisations have taken up S$500,000 from the S$10 million community integration fund to develop community projects involving local and new Singaporeans.

    The council, announced in February 2009, was formed to help new immigrants here to integrate.

    The council gathered 120 of its members to seek views on how to create a vibrant public, private and people network to support social integration at a closed door dialogue session this evening.

    Community Development, Youth and Sports Minister Dr Vivian Balakrishnan who is also the Chairman of the National Integration Council said: “After someone comes in and interacts and even after you got the facility to communicate, there is still a need for an emotional bond to be built. An emotional bond to be built amongst people, amongst citizens, as well as an emotional bond between the citizen and this place which we call Singapore.” - CNA/vm
