'Rise above gender perspective' : Lim Hwee Hua

  • Clivebenss

    Apr 9, 2011 - Straits Times

    By Teo Wan Gek

    THE chief of the People's Action Party's women's wing on Saturday urged Singaporeans to rise above the gender issue when assessing the party's new slate of candidates.

    Mrs Lim Hwee Hua was responding to a question on criticisms of the ruling party's new faces, much of which has been been directed at two women - Ms Tin Pei Ling, 27, and Ms Foo Mee Har, 45.

    She said on the sidelines of an event at nex Mall: 'I certainly hope that people will rise above the gender perspective."

    Mrs Lim, who is Minister in the Prime Minister's Office and Second Minister for Finance and Transport, said the candidates should be assessed on whether they have the conviction to serve, and their competencies and abilities.

    She expressed a hope that people would not target candidates because they are women, adding that 'that would be very sad if it were true.'

    SM Goh: It was a ‘tongue-in cheek’ comment
     April 3rd, 2011 |  Author: Editorial

     

    Senior Minister Goh Chok Tong, who reportedly told local media that People’s Action Party new candidate Ms Ting Pei Ling was seeing a “trauma specialist” has now qualify (hours later) that the comment which he made on Facebook was a “tongue-in cheek”.

    Obviously referring to the “frosty reception” Ms Tin was receiving from Netizens, Mr Goh was reported by ChannelNewsAsia yesterday to have said that Ms Tin has been “traumatized” and that he was with her when she saw a ‘trauma specialist’.

    The revealations prompted Netizens to speculate that Mr Goh was merely using it as an excuse for the PAP to remove Ms Tin from the candidacy on “medical reason”, seeing now that Ms Tin would be a total liability to the party.

    However, this speculation has now been debunked and peeved Netizens should expect a $15,000 a month “Parliamentary Stomper” after the General Election, via the coat-tails of Mr Goh.

    Referring to his initial posting, Mr Goh (in his new comment hours later) accused the media of failing to appreciate his “tongue-in cheek” post:

    MParader (Mr Goh)
    Pei Ling just called. Media wants her comments on my post. They [the media] have failed to appreciate my tongue-in cheek post. True, she has been thru baptism of fire. My use of medical metaphor is becos Fatimah is a trauma specialist. No need for media to chase PL for comments. She is strong n working hard.— Goh Chok Tong

    He explained that he had used a “medical metaphor to describe “Fatimah” as she is a trauma specialist.

    According to the Parliament of Singapore website, Dr Fatimah (who is an MP at Marine Parade GRC) is currently a consultant with the Department of Emergency Medicine at the Singapore General Hospital.

    Since Ms Tin Pei Ling was first introduced to the public as the PAP’s youngest candidate on 28th March, thousands of Netizens have called for Ms Tin to be fielded in an SMC to “prove her mettle” and have vowed to vote the entire Marine Parade GRC team out should the PAP decides to allow her to “piggy-back” into Parliament via the coat-tails of Mr Goh.
