    Dec 16, 2010 - Straits Times

    NEW PAP CENTRAL EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE

    Mah absent from PAP's CEC

    THE ruling People's Action Party (PAP) on Wednesday announced the make-up of its highest decision-making body for the next two years, a period which will include the upcoming general election.

    The line-up of the 18-member CEC is the same as in 2008, except that Law and Home Affairs Minister K. Shanmugam has replaced Mr Mah Bow Tan.

    Mr Shanmugam became a Cabinet minister in May 2008, five months before the 2008 party elections.

    All the CEC members in the side report were elected during a PAP conference on Nov 29, with the exception of Mrs Lim Hwee Hua and Dr Vivian Balakrishnan, who were co-opted after the election. They scored the 13th and 14th highest number of votes.

    Meeting on Wednesday, the CEC co-opted another four members - Mr Gan Kim Yong, Dr Ng Eng Hen, Ms Indranee Rajah and Mr Teo Ser Luck.

    The party's other internal appointments for the 2010 term remain largely the same, with one notable change: Mrs Josephine Teo takes over from Mr Lee Yi Shyan as head of the sub-committee for external relations. The sub-committee handles the party's links and interaction with other political parties from around the world.
