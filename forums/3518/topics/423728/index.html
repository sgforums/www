GE: PAP to field at least 20 new candidates

  • Clivebenss
    25 Feb 11, 22:44

    By Evelyn Choo | Posted: 25 February 2011 2239 hrs

    SINGAPORE : PAP Chairman Lim Boon Heng has said his party plans to field at least 20 new candidates in the upcoming General Election so that they can gain experience working on the ground while the older ministers carry on.

    He hopes the new candidates will be ready in five to ten years to take the country forward.

    Mr Lim, who is also the MP for Jurong GRC, spoke on the sidelines of a community event at his constituency on Friday.

    "We have a list of possible candidates, including reserves. And as to the actual positioning of who stands where, there is some idea about what we want to do. But we do not take this as the final list. The final list is confirmed only on Nomination Day," the chairman of the People's Action Party (PAP) said.

    "If you recall in 2001 in Jurong GRC, the slate was decided hours before Nomination Day. So it can happen to any of the seats we are contesting," Mr Lim added.

    On possible candidates, two new faces were spotted on Friday evening - 29-year-old Alex Yam and 35-year-old Desmond Lee.

    Both have been serving as grassroots leaders in Jurong GRC for about a month.

    Mr Yam is currently coordinating the new media activities of the various resident committees of Jurong, while Mr Lee oversees active ageing events.

    On current MPs in his Jurong team, Mr Lim commended Ms Grace Fu - who is the Senior Minister of State for National Development and Education - on her work in Yuhua over the past five years, saying she has done well.

    He said her ward saw the most significant change in terms of being carved out as a Single Member Constituency.

    Responding to the re-drawing of electoral boundaries, Mr Lim called the report "logical" and "sensible", as there are changes in population.

    - CNA
