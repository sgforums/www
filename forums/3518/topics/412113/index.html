    Temasek Review, 1 October 2010

    Former PAP minister Dr Lee Boon Yang has decided to retire from politics for good and will not be contesting in the next general election.

    The 63 year old veterinarian first enter politics in 1984 as MP of Jalan Besar ward which has since be “expanded” to form a GRC.

    He claimed that he joined politics to “serve” the people:

    “When I entered politics, it was not a career move. Politics is about serving the people and helping people to improve their life and realising their dreams.”

    Dr Lee is the second PAP heavyweight to retire in the next general election, the other being former transport minister Yeo Cheow Tong. He will be joining Keppel Corporation to sit on its board of directors after leaving politics, a standard “exit route” for former PAP ministers to keep their mouths shut in “retirement” like former Deputy Prime Minister Tony Tan who is now the Chairman of Singapore Press Holdings.

    While the younger ministers have retired to make way for new leaders, there are no signs if PAP strongman Lee Kuan Yew is going to retire anytime soon.

    At 87 years of age, Lee is probably the world’s oldest living minister and MP. Though he admitted he is not doing much work lately except “forecasting”, he costs Singapore taxpayers more than $3 million dollars a year.

    Lee has been dropping subtly to the press recently that he is not quite ready to retire. He quipped famously that he will “decline” if he stops working.

    Soon after returning from a tour of Russia and Ukraine, Lee had a fall. He was admitted to SGH on Wednesday for a chest infection. His current status remains unknown.
