    Reform Party candidates to swop battlegrounds

    By Andrea Ong

    Reform Party's Alec Tok (right) shakes hand with former Reform Party member Tan Tee Seng (left, face obscured) as Jeannette Chong-Aruldoss and Hazel Poa look on during a walkabout at Redhill Market in Radin Mas SMC and Mountbatten SMC. -- ST PHOTO: JOSEPH NAIR

     

    THE Reform Party (RP) on Sunday said it plans to reshuffle its pack - swopping candidates for the Pioneer and Radin Mas single member constituencies (SMCs).

    Pioneer will be contested by party chief Kenneth Jeyaretnam instead of theatre director Alec Tok, who will go to Radin Mas.

    Mr Tok, 45, speaking to reporters during a walkabout in Radin Mas on Sunday, said the switch made sense because of the different voter profiles.

    Residents in Pioneer are largely English-speaking and middle class, while those in Radin Mas tend to be older, poorer and Mandarin- or dialect-speaking.

    'The party will have to consider who can communicate better to two different sets of demographics. He who can communicate to the electorate in their language should obviously be the choice.'

    Radin Mas looks to be one of the most hotly contested wards, with four opposition parties saying they will contest it.
