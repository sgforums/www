    If we are not happy with the state of affairs or if we prefer a change, it is then our choice to do the right thing and to make the change rather than pointing fingers at PAP.

    PAP has made sure that the climate here is not conducive for the opposition parties to flourish. This is quite normal for a ruling party wanting to stay in power absolutely. But absolute power corrupts absolutely. It is the responsibility of the citizens to ensure there is check and balance in a democratic system.

    Fear is a normal human feeling. So far PAP has been able to play the fear card very well. We are now a more mature society. I think the element of fear will gradually be fading away.

    In the past, although our opposition parties did not give PAP a real challenge, but I can observe that they were able to apply pressure to influence some government policies. Without the presence of oppositions, we would in a worst situation today.

    Singapore is now at a turning point. LKY will soon be a figure of the past. I can anticipate a period of great change ahead of us.
    Singaporeans must realize what their priorities are. We have to get the priorities right in the first place. To do so, we have to:

    1. looking at both short term and long term;
    2. moving away from blaming and accountability, but learning to think about the roots of the problems we are facing;
    3. avoiding reacting to what are happening around you, but trying to create new opportunities;
    4. thinking beyond having, but embracing being and becoming.

    The coming election is of paramount important to all. The outcome is going to map the direction of the post-LKY era of Singapore. A Singapore without check and balance, a Singapore without a multi-party system is not a real democracy. Singapore will certainly be a more gracious country without PAP dominating in almost every aspect of their lives. Stronger opposition parties will force the PAP government to be more consultative and equitable, to be more caring and sincere.

    I am confident that the people of Singapore know what to do because it is not going to affect them alone but also the destiny of their children.
