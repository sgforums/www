    SINGAPORE - In what appears to be a step closer to the General Election, some 860 void decks or precinct pavilions have been reserved for use as polling stations.

    Of these, 660 have been reserved as backups, including for locations that had been earlier identified for use as polling stations such as schools and community clubs, said the Elections Department on Friday.

    The department was responding to MediaCorp's queries. Its spokeswoman added that each of these locations will be reserved until a better alternative - for instance a school, CC or new void deck - is available.

    The spokeswoman said: "(The) Elections Department will predetermine the location for the polling stations and reserve the specific void deck (or precinct pavilion) with HDB. The reservation is long-term, to ensure that the site is available anytime an election is called."

    The presidential election is due by next August and the General Election is due by February 2012.

    Meanwhile, the department has also requested quotations for the supply, subscription and maintenance of commercial off-the-shelf software. These are specialised software that can be used with little or no modification.

    Last month, the department issued a tender document for the purchase of at least 1,000 mobile broadband devices and SIM cards, as well as video collaboration services, which will be used at electoral premises. These would enable election officials to make video calls to one another.

    The department has also bought 8,500 trays that can be used to hold voting slips, beefed up its hotline and e-mail system, and arranged a telephone etiquette course for staff. ALICIA WONG
