    By Sharon See | Posted: 27 March 2011 1323 hrs

    SINGAPORE: Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong said Singaporeans should think carefully and calmly about the long-term future during the upcoming General Election.

    He said this on the sidelines of a community event in Ang Mo Kio on Sunday.

    "Whatever emotions that are aroused during that period, calm down, detach yourself, think carefully before you vote - which is why we have a Cooling-off Day before Polling Day. So I think in that same spirit, we really should take things in our stride," he said.

    In response to a question from a reporter on whether there has been too much hype over the election, Mr Lee noted that newspapers are "getting a bit breathless".

    "And when it comes, it comes. And before it comes, know that it's on the horizon but no need to get breathless," he added.

    -CNA/ac

    Mar 27, 2011 - Sunday Times

    PM Lee's first comments on PAP candidates

    By Rachel Scully, Multimedia Journalist

    "IT IS very daunting for them to stand in front of so many journalists and answer questions one after another', said Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong.

    While speaking to the media on the sidelines of the People's Action Party Community Foundation's 25th anniversary celebrations, Mr Lee expressed empathy for the new PAP candidates that were unveiled the past week.

    When questioned on why the PAP announced its candidates for East Coast GRC on Thursday ahead of Nomination Day, Mr Lee said he supported the decision as it allowed residents to get know the new candidates better.

    The news came after Senior Minister S. Jayakumar and Speaker Abdullah Tarmugi made public their intention to retire.

    Minister in the Prime Minister's Office Lim Swee Say - from Holland-Bukit Timah GRC - and Senior Parliamentary Secretary for National Development, Mohamad Maliki Osman - from Sembawang GRC - will move to the constituency.
