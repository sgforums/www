"More than happy" to stand in single ward, says Grace Fu

  • Clivebenss
    7 Nov 10, 15:53

    By Joanne Chan | Posted: 07 November 2010 1545 hrs

    SINGAPORE: Senior Minister of State for National Development and Education Grace Fu said she was "more than happy" to stand for election in a single ward should her party require her to.

    She added she had been studying and learning from more experienced party members in preparation for the upcoming General Election.

    Ms Fu, who's in the Jurong GRC, was asked about the speculation that she would be contesting in a single member constituency (SMC).

    The PAP team in Jurong GRC was uncontested in the 2006 General Election.

    Ms Fu said: "We are told to expect the unexpected. So we can be fielded, we may not be fielded.

    "We may be fielded in an SMC, we may be fielded in a GRC. Whether we are fielded in an SMC or we're fielded in a GRC, we have to expect the instruction from the party".

    Ms Fu, who spoke to reporters on the sidelines of a community event on Sunday morning, joined grassroots leaders and residents in an annual cleanup of Yuhua estate.

    She also planted a tree to celebrate Clean and Green Singapore.

    At the event, Ms Fu also announced that four blocks in the estate would be getting lift upgrading.

    They are blocks 209, 210, 211 and 214 Jurong East Street 21.

    Ms Fu said the Yuhua estate was relatively old, having been built in 1982. She added that there were many elderly residents in the area.

    As such, the lift upgrading programme would be beneficial to the elderly who are less mobile.

    -CNA/wk
