    Breaking News – Nomination Day for General election set on 1st September

    AUGUST 25, 2015 BY ONLINECITIZEN IN GE2015 · 0 COMMENT
        

     

    Following the dissolving of parliament, President of Singapore, Mr Tony Tan Keng Yam issued the writ of election at 4 pm today, that the Nomination Day will be held on 1st September 2015, Tuesday.

    Secretary General of People’s Action Party, Lee Hsien Loong has announced on his facebook page that the Polling Day will be on 11th September 2015, Friday. Meaning 10th September 2015, Thursday would be the Cooling Off Day.

    The parliament was just dissolved by Mr Tan at 3pm.

    This will be the 13th General Election to be held in Singapore.

    A total of 13 Single Member Constituency and 16 Group Representation Constituency would be contested in the upcoming General Election with a total of  89 Parliamentary seats up for grabs.

    10 political parties will be taking part in the election and there is expected to be no walkovers.

    There are currently 3 constituencies held by the opposition Workers’ Party.

    The parliament is dissolved exactly one month after the Electoral Boundary Review Committee released the modified electoral map on 24 July.

    1ST SEP FOR NOMINATIONS, 11TH SEPTEMBER WILL BE POLLING DAY

    Submitted by farhan on Tue, 25/08/2015 - 4:32pm

    The office of President Tony Tan Keng Yam has released the Writ of Elections at 4PM today, in which he declared that nominations for political candidates would be held on 1st September. In the same hour, the Elections Department of Singapore also released its Press Release on General Elections 2015, which confirmed that polling day would be held on 11th September.

     

    Advertisement

    The returning officer for GE2015 will be Mr Ng Wai Choong, CEO of the Energy Market Authority.

    Potential candidates will need to pick up their nomination papers from the office of the Returning Officer at Elections Department, 11 Prinsep Link, Singapore 187949, between 9 a.m. and 5 p.m. from Monday to Friday and between 9 a.m. and 1 p.m. on Saturday. These papers would have to be filed with the Elections Department at selected Nomination Places between 11 a.m. and 12 noon on Nomination Day.

    Election deposits per candidate will be fixed at $14,500, or 8% of the total allowances payable to a Member of Parliament in the preceding calendar year.

    On 11th September (Friday), Singaporeans would be granted a public holiday to cast their vote.
