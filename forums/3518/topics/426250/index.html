    By Alvina Soh | Posted: 26 March 2011 2158 hrs

    SINGAPORE: People's Action Party chairman Lim Boon Heng said the pace of introducing new candidates for the coming General Election is based on several factors, including strategic considerations.

    "The pace of the announcements of our candidates will depend on what kind of tactical decisions we have to make," Mr Lim said.

    "When it suits us to announce ahead, we will do so. But when we think that we may need to tactically delay, we will do so."

    Mr Lim, who is also the deputy chairman of the People's Association, was speaking to reporters on Saturday at a team-building programme at the Outward Bound School on Pulau Ubin.

    Joining him were some 85 grassroots leaders.

    The programme, launched last month by the People's Association, aims to forge closer bonds between the participants.

    The target is to reach out to 4,000 grassroots leaders this year.

    -CNA/wk

    GE: Key PAP candidate to be named
    By Hoe Yeen Nie | Posted: 26 March 2011 2051 hrs

    SINGAPORE: A potential member of the People's Action Party's core leadership team may be unveiled as early as next weekend, and the candidate will likely stand in Marine Parade GRC.

    This was revealed by Senior Minister Goh Chok Tong, in a posting on the constituency's Facebook page.

    Mr Goh wrote that he is planning for someone to succeed him "in leading the GRC one day", and that he has asked Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong to send him "a strong candidate who has the potential to be part of the fourth gen (generation) core team".

    He added that the candidate will be named next weekend.

    Mr Goh has been updating the Facebook page regularly since his first post on March 18.

    On Friday, he paid tribute to retiring Marine Parade MP Matthias Yao, and hinted at a possible new candidate, 27-year-old Tin Pei Ling.
