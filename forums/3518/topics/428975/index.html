    Apr 27, 2011 - Straits Times

    Objection to Sim Ann's candidacy overruled

    By Jessica Cheam

    THE Singapore Democratic Party's Holland Bukit Timah team had said in a hand-written note put up on the Greenridge Secondary nomination centre notice board that People's Action Party candidate Sim Ann is still in the civil service. They said she had 'identified herself as 'unemployed', adding that this was a 'misrepresentation'.

    The whole SDP team had signed the note, and asked the Returning Officer to declare her candidacy 'null and void'.

    However, the Returning Officer at the centre said he has considered both of SDP's objections and has decided to overrule them.

    The objection was put up just before the 12.30pm deadline for objections.

    SDP's Holland-Bukit Timah candidate Vincent Wijeysingha told The Straits Times that the SDP was not given any reasons why their objections were overruled.

    Ms Sim, 36, was with the civil service for the last 12 years, working in several ministries.

    She began volunteering at the grassroots level in 2002 and became a member of the Bukit Timah Citizens' Consultative Committee in 2003.
