    PE: S'poreans to start receiving poll cards for PE

    SINGAPORE: 2.27 million Singaporeans will start receiving their poll cards for the Presidential Election from Thursday.

    Voters should receive their poll cards within three days of Nomination Day, which was on August 17.

    For the second time this year, Singaporeans will be headed to the polls. This time, it is to pick the country's head of state.

    The Elections Department is emphasising the dignity of the office at stake. As such, it is pulling out all the stops to make sure the voting and counting process on August 27 will go smoothly.

    Besides sending out more election officials to facilitate the polls, the Elections Department is also hoping to shorten the time it takes for voters to cast their vote.

    It said that along with the crucial identification documents like NRIC or passport, voters should also bring their poll cards to the polling stations to facilitate checks by election officials so as to shorten the waiting time.

    Those who do not receive their poll cards in the mail for any reason can now print it out for themselves.

    The Elections Department has introduced a new service on its     website which allows voters to print out their poll cards.

    Voters can also head to the community centres or the Elections Department to get help in printing out their poll cards.

    On Polling Day, voters will be issued a ballot paper at the polling stations.

    The ballot paper will have all four candidates' names, photos and symbols printed on it in black and white. Voters should mark an "X" in the empty box next to their choice.

    Measures have also been put in place to ensure that the votes of the blind will be kept secret. A stencil with cut-out boxes will help them mark the ballot paper on their own.

    There will be 781 polling stations across the island - located at schools, community centres and void decks.

    The votes will be counted at 162 counting centres when voting closes at 8pm on Polling Day.

    The last time Singaporeans went to the polls to choose a president was 18 years ago in 1993. Results of that election only came out in the early morning of the next day, at 3.30am. 

    While the Elections Department is hoping Singaporeans will not have to wait that long this time around, it is still expecting it to be past midnight before the results are announced, that is if there is no recount needed. 

    In the 1993 election, there were 1.6 million voters and two candidates. This time, there are four candidates and 2.27 million votes to be counted.

    Channel NewsAsia will be broadcasting a special coverage of the Singapore Presidential Election Results from 10pm on August 27.

    CNA/al
