National issues more important or constituency issues?

  • Thethinker6
    29 Apr 11, 15:27

     

    It is interesting that in the post nomination speeches, the PAP tend to focus on constituency issues while the opposition tend to focus on national issues.  Which is correct?

    In the 1970s there were only national issues and no constituency issues. Our forefathers were very focused on the good of the whole Singapore. It was only till housing estate upgrading came along, then constituency issues stole the limelight during elections.  Are constituency issues really that important?

    In some big countries, a single constituency is already bigger than the whole of Singapore.  Then constituency issues are important.  In Singapore constituencies are very small and very interrelated with each other. One constituency's issues will most likely affect the other constituencies as well.  We live in one constituency, and we work in another constituency, while traveling through constituencies each day.  We have relatives who stay in other constituencies and have gatherings in yet other constituencies as well.  Thus the good and the bad of constituencies are shared rather than wholly enjoyed by only the locals.

    Therefore what is the use of a $0.6 billion upgrading in your constituency?  They may promise you a new shopping center, a new hospital, new school, new stadium, new jogging path, and even a man-made lagoon.  But people from other constituencies will come to use your shopping center, hospital, school, stadium, jogging path and lagoon too.  You most probably would end up using other constituency's shopping center, hospital, school, stadium, jogging path and lagoon as well.  So what is the big deal?

    And the good news end there.  How is the billion dollar upgrading financed? GST. Who will build that upgrading? Foreign labor. How will the town's living cost become? Much higher living cost. Will your son/daughter be able to buy a HDB near your flat? No, because the HDB prices rise much higher because it becomes a more beautiful estate. Will you actually be able to enjoy the estate? No because you work long hours each day, and you would go overseas for vacation anyway.  What can go wrong? Well you have just sent back to parliament a group of politicians who do not listen to you regarding national issues.

    The fact is that a covered walkway will not make you any happier if living costs and medical cost continues to go up uncontrolled. A lift upgrading will not make you any happier if foreigner labor continues to threaten your job. A new hospital is of no use if you cannot afford to use it. A new school is also of no use if your kids cannot get in due to more foreigners.  Shopping center will not help if it is jam-packed with people all the time, and so are the buses and MRT.  Therefore national issues affects our daily lives much more than constituency issues.

    Why then did the PAP focus on constituency issues? Perhaps it is because they messed up the national issues and thus would not like people to think about it.  Why did the opposition focus on national issues? Because there are serious and urgent national issues that require the attention of everyone.

    Therefore in this election, I hope everyone can be selfless and focus more on the national issues.  Our forefathers did that for us to bring prosperity to us.  We need to do that for the next generation so that the future Singapore will be more citizen focused rather than money focused.  Don't sell away the bright future of Singapore for merely a covered walkway or a lift upgrading.

    Depends.

    The majority of votes that goes to the MIW are actually older generation who tend to have benefitted from the constituency upgrades. They tend to have less issues with the current day-to-day outcomes and more equipped to live with less.It is easier for them to associate with the constituency upgrades as seeing-is-believing.

    The most discomforting set of outcome from this is that they may not be able to comprehend the resultant impact made in the Parliament. On the contrary, only the educated and informed Singaporeans will tend to take national issues to heart as they can understand figures and statistics. It is rather difficult to bring to the attention of the older generation the statistical studies which show the downward curve we are heading.

    These older generation do not see the swing as these are figures and does not relate to them unless someone spend more time and more effort explaining the outcomes of these figures.

    Thus, based on neurological examination, material visual and audio information is always easier to digest that immaterial statistical information.

    You need very good speakers to translate these immaterial statistical information into something which they can easily associate with and there is always difficulties in justifying the source.
