PAP assessing which MPs should make way for new blood

  • Clivebenss
    12 Feb 11, 17:34

    By Hetty Musfirah Abdul Khamid | Posted: 12 February 2011 1624 hrs

    SINGAPORE: The People's Action Party (PAP) is assessing who among the current MPs should step down to make way for new candidates in the next General Election, which must be held by February next year.

    PAP chairman Lim Boon Heng said this when asked to elaborate on comments last month by Senior Minister Goh Chok Tong that the party was at the tail end of interviewing candidates.

    Mr Lim, who is also the Minister in the Prime Minister's Office, was speaking at the official opening of a new childcare centre at the Jurong GRC on Saturday.

    29-year-old Alex Yam, who has been a PAP member since 2002, was among grassroots leaders accompanying Mr Lim during the event.

    When asked if he is likely to stand as a GE candidate, Mr Yam expressed surprise and said he will leave the decision to the Prime Minister.

    Mr Lim gave little away, saying grassroots leaders are moved around constituencies to help them gain exposure.

    He also revealed that most of the potential election candidates are young and some have limited exposure to grassroots activities.

    Mr Lim said that it is also no secret that PAP typically renews a quarter to a third of its candidates at every election, adding that the process of choosing candidates is still an ongoing one.

    "When SM Goh mentioned that we are at the tail end....it means that the leadership is now looking at the existing MPs, looking at the people who are potential candidates and then assessing who should step down and who should be brought in," said Mr Lim.

    And as far as his Jurong GRC is concerned, Mr Lim said: "Ever since Dr Ong Chit Chung passed away, we have to get ready....so we do meet regularly from time to time to make sure that different things are prepared....update things we do, improve on some of the preparations and so on. So I think we are as ready as we can for the election, and just waiting for Prime Minister to tell us when."

    Separately, Mr Lim also gave his comments on the PCF kindergarten in Bedok that has threatened to impose fines on parents who are late in picking up their children after class.

    He said the 22 PCF Little Wings centres located in the western part of Singapore have also seen such similar cases but it has not become a big issue. So the centres are not yet exploring the idea of imposing similar fines on parents.

    "We should not be carried way with this issue about fines. It's actually also about having communication with parents, for them to understand the constraints of operating a centre," said Mr Lim.

    "So I think we prefer to continue to engage parents who are late and find out the reasons, and where possible, we will strike up some mutually acceptable solution," he added.

    Mr Lim said the government can provide more support for working parents. And he hopes the upcoming Budget can address this.

    One possibility is to provide more subsidies in infant care, he said.

    Mr Lim said: "I think we can subsidise more in infant care, because I think for parents, really, when the child comes and both parents are working, who looks after the kid? And it is not always convenient to have grandparents (look after the kids).

    Some grandparents may also be working.....So, the setting up of infant care centres is important, but infant care is labour-intensive, it's expensive. So, I would be in favour of increasing subsidies for infant care."

    Finance Minister Tharman Shanmugaratnam will deliver the Budget on 18 February.

    - CNA/ir
