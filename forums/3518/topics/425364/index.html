    Mar 17, 2011 - Straits Times

    Low Thia Khiang believes a GRC win is crucial for opposition

    By Jeremy Au Yong & Kor Kian Beng

    WORKERS' Party (WP) chief Low Thia Khiang is mulling a 'high-stakes' decision: to stay in Hougang or contest a group representation constituency (GRC) at the next general election.

    Responding to questions from The Straits Times at his Meet-the-People session last night, the opposition stalwart and MP for the Hougang single-seat constituency disclosed that he had been thinking about leading a GRC bid even before the 2006 polls.

    'Contesting a GRC is an option open to me,' said Mr Low. 'And if I do take up that option, I will explain on Nomination Day the reasons.'

    He stressed, however, that the decision is not one he or the WP takes lightly because it is 'a serious matter and the stakes are very high'.

    Speculation that Mr Low, who turns 55 in September, might leave Hougang for a GRC was reignited last weekend by remarks he made during a walkabout in East Coast GRC.

    He stressed that a GRC win was crucial if the opposition hoped to grow and renew its leadership.
