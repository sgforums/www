    GE: Losing Aljunied "not end of world", says MM Lee
    Posted: 30 April 2011 2049 hrs

    SINGAPORE: Minister Mentor Lee Kuan Yew has said it would not be "the end of the world" if the People's Action Party (PAP) loses Aljunied GRC in this election.

    Mr Lee was speaking to reporters on a wide range of issues about this election, including cost of living and the younger generation, at the Radin Mas branch after a meeting with party activists and PAP candidate Sam Tan on Saturday.

    Asked about the possibility of the PAP losing Aljunied, Mr Lee said "losing one GRC does not mean the end of the world".

    On the higher cost of living, Mr Lee said that the PAP has helped to resolve the problems by sharing the benefits of growth with citizens.

    He said that was possible only because of a capable government and finance minister.

    To give more to citizens, Mr Lee said the country needs to grow and not raid its reserves.

    He cited the many initiatives that have helped Singaporeans cope such as Workfare, Grow and Share bonus and utilities rebates.

    Mr Lee also took aim at the opposition's real motives.

    Pointing to the Workers' Party's aim of capturing Aljunied GRC, he said every party sets out to win a majority to form the government.

    "Let's have no hypocrisy about this," he said.

    - CNA/ir
