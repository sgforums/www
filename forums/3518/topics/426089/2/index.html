    Mar 28, 2011 - Straits Times

    Big guns' retirement led PAP to name East Coast slate early

    Party does not want to take supporters by surprise, PM explains

    By Zakir Hussain

    THE PAP took the unusual step of announcing its East Coast GRC line-up early because the retirement of two heavyweight MPs there was 'quite a big move', Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong said on Sunday.

    'On the one hand, we don't want to give any points to the opposition for nothing. On the other hand, we don't want to take our own supporters by surprise,' he told reporters on the sidelines of a community event in his constituency.

    'We owe it to our own supporters and especially to the team in the GRC to know in good time what's going to happen, whom they'll be working with, and to have the new people able to bond and know the conditions in the GRC before they are fielded there.

    'From that point of view, there's an advantage in letting it be known early who's coming into that constituency. Where to set the balance between surprise and preparation, that will vary from GRC to GRC.'

    His comments came as Senior Minister S. Jayakumar, East Coast GRC's outgoing anchor minister, introduced labour chief Lim Swee Say to residents there on Sunday morning.

    Professor Jayakumar and Speaker of Parliament Abdullah Tarmugi, who have been MPs there since 1980 and 1984 respectively, announced last Thursday that they will not contest the next election.
