Amy Khor says ready to contest in SMC or GRC

  • Clivebenss
    11 Nov 10, 16:18

    By Lian Cheong | Posted: 10 November 2010 2007 hrs

    SINGAPORE : Minister of State for the Environment and Water Resources, Amy Khor, has said she is ready to contest either in a Single Member Constituency (SMC) or a Group Representation Constituency (GRC) in the next General Election, and it is up to the prime minister and party leaders to decide.

    Speaking at a National Environment Agency event, she said competition is healthy for Singapore's political landscape.

    Dr Khor said: "I welcome a contest. I think it's good for the political development of Singapore. Also, I think it's good for the residents. They get to exercise their choice and in fact through a contest, residents get to know the candidates better, get to know what the parties stand for.

    "I stand ready to contest (in a) GRC or SMC, but that really is the prerogative of the prime minister and party leaders. Exactly where I will be fielded also is their prerogative.

    "But I suppose what I would like to say is as Mayor of the South West district, I think I do have a good feel of the ground - not just in my constituency in the GRC but also in the district because we do serve residents throughout the district in terms of either social assistance and employment assistance and even community bonding."

    - CNA/al
