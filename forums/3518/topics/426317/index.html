GE: Charles Chong to stand in Joo Chiat SMC

  • Clivebenss
    27 Mar 11, 16:05

    By S Ramesh | Posted: 27 March 2011 1601 hrs

    SINGAPORE: The People's Action Party's candidate for the Joo Chiat single member seat will be veteran MP Charles Chong, who is currently an MP in the Pasir Ris-Punggol GRC.

    This was announced by Joo Chiat's incumbent MP Chan Soo Sen, who also announced to his activists that he would be retiring from politics.

    Mr Chan said with this introduction, Mr Charles Chong will join the Joo Chiat team to take part in its walkabout and cycle-about, various grassroots events, as well as General Election Committee meetings.

    The incumbent MP also paid accolade to his residents, grassroots leaders, activists, and comrades for the opportunity to work together for the past 15 years.

    He said it has been both fruitful and unforgettable.

    Mr Chan said: "We witnessed the many changes in Joo Chiat during this period. Many new businesses and new residents have moved in. We participated in the development of Joo Chiat community together, anticipating and solving problems. These were challenging at times.

    "However, we developed strong bonds in the process. We have evolved a unique Joo Chiat community spirit that genuinely emphasises neighborliness and participation. This may mean taking a little more time to achieve results. However, with participation, there is commitment and ownership to what we do."

    -CNA/ac

    Mar 28, 2011 - Straits Times

    PAP taking no chances in Joo Chiat

    Veteran Charles Chong steps in as Chan Soo Sen confirms retirement

    VETERAN MP Charles Chong will represent the People's Action Party in Joo Chiat during the next general election - a sign that the PAP is taking no chances in the largely upper-middle-class ward.

    The incumbent, Mr Chan Soo Sen, 54, confirmed what party sources had indicated last week: that he will be retiring.

    Mr Chong, 57, a five-term MP, was introduced at the Joo Chiat PAP branch on Sunday by Deputy Prime Minister Teo Chee Hean, who described him as a candidate of 'considerable ability' with a 'proven track record'.

    'I'm confident that with Charles here, residents will continue to be well looked-after,' said Mr Teo, who was Joo Chiat's MP from 1992 to 1997, before Mr Chan took over.

    On Sunday, Senior Minister Goh Chok Tong said Mr Chong was 'the best man for Joo Chiat'.

    'He's very amiable. He can get to know people very quickly. Within a week or so, I think he will be able to establish rapport,' he said, after a walkabout in an area of Joo Chiat that has been merged with Marine Parade GRC.
