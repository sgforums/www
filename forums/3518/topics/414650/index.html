    Opposition Reform Party or RP said today that it has withdrawn its application to join the Singapore Democratic Alliance or SDA.
     
    In a statement, RP says it will continue to explore other ways to collaborate with veteran MP Chiam See Tong and the Singapore People’s Party or SPP.
     
    The statement comes in the wake of the fallout between Mr Chiam and his former close aide, Desmond Lim. 

    Both men had disagreed over whether to let RP join the SDA.

    This led to a feud over whether Mr Lim is still the Secretary General of the SDA, as both camps seek to oust the other from power. 

    The incident is reminiscent of the spat between Mr Chiam and his ex-protégé, Chee Soon Juan, which led to Mr Chiam leaving the Singapore Democratic Party. 

    RP’s Secretary General Kenneth Jeyaretnam says his party is still working on forming a "dream team" with Mr Chiam in the upcoming general elections. 

    This, he says, could come in the form of a new alliance, in place of SDA. 

    When asked if he would welcome Mr Chiam into RP, Mr Jeyaretnam says he’s open to the suggestion. 

    "We’d certainly welcome Mr Chiam, coming into the Reform Party. Whether he would wish to do so or not, that’s another matter. But we would certainly offer him a very senior position in our Central Executive Committee were he to come in"

    Singapore’s next General Election must be called by February 2012.
