    No big hongbao budget

    PM  Lee tempers expectations ahead of  Feb 22 statement 
    By Kor Kian Beng The Sunday Times

    PRIME Minister Lee Hsien Loong yesterday cautioned Singaporeans against measuring the pace of the economic recovery by looking at the property market.

    He also tempered any expectations of a mound of goodies in the coming Budget, saying it 'may be too early to hope for a big hongbao'.

    The Budget statement will be made on Feb 22, he said.

    He was speaking to reporters after attending the official light-up ceremony of Chinatown's Chinese New Year celebrations last night.

    Looking cheerful and jovial as he spoke about the economic recovery, Mr Lee highlighted the need to strike a balance between making people feel confident they can improve things and ensuring they do not assume all problems are gone.

    'And you can't just look at the property market and say 'wow, it's gone up so much and next year will go up even more' and get carried away, whether carried away with enthusiasm or carried away with panic,' he said. 'I think we have to treat these things with detachment and calmness.'

    PM Lee cautions S'poreans against getting carried away with economy's recovery
    Posted: 23 January 2010 2217 hrs

    SINGAPORE : Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong has cautioned Singaporeans against getting too carried away with the recent recovery in the economy.

    He noted that Singapore is not expecting a very vibrant and strong recovery.

    Mr Lee said that although the government wants people to feel that things are better, at the same time, Singaporeans cannot assume that all problems have passed.

    Mr Lee was speaking to reporters at the Lunar New Year light-up ceremony in Chinatown on Saturday evening.

    On the buoyant property market, Mr Lee said that Singaporeans cannot assume it will continue going up.

    He added that the government is confident that things are under control.

    On whether Singapore will achieve 3 or 4 per cent economic growth this year, Mr Lee said it will depend on how well things turn out.

    He was also asked about the Economic Strategies Committee report.

    Mr Lee did not want to give details but said Finance Minister Tharman Shanmugaratnam will release the report on February 1. - CNA/ms
