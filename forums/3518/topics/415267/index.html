    By Imelda Saad | Posted: 13 November 2010 2144 hrs

    SINGAPORE: Minister Mentor Lee Kuan Yew has said that whether he stands in the next General Election or not will depend on his health and the Prime Minister's decision.

    Minister Mentor Lee has been taking care of Tanjong Pagar constituency since it was a single-member ward in 1955.

    Tanjong Pagar is now a Group Representation Constituency (GRC) covering a vast part of central Singapore including areas like Moulmein, Tanglin and Tiong Bahru and is helmed by six PAP MPs.

    It is one of the big GRCs, with a voter base of 148,141 at the last General Election in 2006.

    Now at 87 years of age, Mr Lee said his decision to stand in the next General Election will depend on two things.

    He said: "If I'm fit, I'm prepared to stand but whether I stand or not, it's up to the Prime Minister."

    Minister Mentor Lee and his PAP team may face competition in the polls due by February 2012.

    The Reform Party has indicated it will contest Tanjong Pagar if it has enough candidates.

    "Welcome. We'll contest them," said Mr Lee.

    Since Tanjong Pagar became a GRC in 1991, it has never been contested.

    The last time Tanjong Pagar was contested was way back in 1988 when it was still a single-member constituency.

    Then Mr Lee had faced off with independent candidate Gnaguru Thamboo Mylvaganam.

    Mr Lee had won about 80 percent of the valid votes.

    Minister Mentor Lee remains confident about his team's success.

    He said: "You think they'll win in the next election? You can sense the crowd, you can feel the ground. I don't have to answer you."

    With changes to the Parliamentary Elections Act, the six-member Tanjong Pagar GRC may be hived off as the new laws will pave the way for more smaller GRCs.

    On the likelihood of any team member in the Tanjong Pagar GRC being asked to helm a single-member constituency, Minister for Communications, Information and the Arts Lui Tuck Yew said: "My team is looking forward to it."

    Earlier Saturday at a community event, Mr Lee urged residents to hold on to their property.

    He said part of Singapore's success lies in its home ownership policy where every Singaporean has the opportunity to own a home, which will appreciate in value.

    Minister Mentor Lee said: "So all those who own houses, I urge you not to listen to the estate agents...and go and sell your flats because that's a stupid thing to do. You may sell it and not get a rental flat for a long time and you cannot gain from a rental flat. Any improvement to a rental flat goes to the government, so please remember that."

    One way the value of public housing is enhanced is through upgrading, such as the S$1 million project launched on Saturday.

    The project will see covered linkways connecting eight blocks of flats to the Tiong Bahru MRT station.
