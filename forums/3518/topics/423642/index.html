    Feb 24, 2011 - Straits Times

    by Lyn Chan

     

    THE electoral boundaries for the next General Election were released on Thursday.

    Looking at previous elections, the release of the Electoral Boundaries Report usually indicates the nearing of a general election.

    What happens next?

    Dissolution of Parliament

    The general election must be held within three months of the dissolution of Parliament.

    The last three elections saw Parliament dissolving between a day and almost seven weeks after the release of the report.

    Nomination Day

    Nomination Day must come between five days and a month after the President has issued writ.

    Based on the past three elections, Nomination Day took place after Parliament was dissolved.

    The minimum period between Nomination and Polling Day, typically nine days, has been extended to 10 days to make up for the Cooling-Off Day.

    Polling Day must be held not earlier than the 10th day and not later than the 56th day immediately following the Notice of Contested Elections is published after Nomination Day.

    Cooling-Off Day

    Cooling-Off Day has been implemented to give voters some time to mull over the issues raised during the election before making a trip to the polls.

    During these 24 hours, candidates are not allowed to campaign.

    However, party political broadcasts are allowed.

    Polling Day

    Previous polls were held between 17 days and six months after the release of the Electoral Boundaries Report.

    Based on past experience, Singaporeans can expect to go to the polls by Aug 24.
