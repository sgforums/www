    Yam Ah Mee to step down as PA chief

     

    He is retiring from public service, says exit not linked to Palmer saga

     

    Mr Yam said he intends to join the private sector but has not finalised his plans. -- ST FILE PHOTO

     

    By KARAMJIT KAUR

     

    PEOPLE'S Association (PA) chief executive director Yam Ah Mee will step down later this year when he retires from public service.

       He told The Straits Times last night that his term as a public service leader would expire then, and the move had been planned some time ago.

       The Public Sector Leadership scheme, which started in 2000, places limits on how long top civil servants can serve in key posts.

       This ensures a continuous flow of talent to the top.

       Mr Yam, 56, added that his departure was not related to the recent case involving former MP and Speaker of Parliament Michael Palmer and ex-PA staff member Laura Ong. He was responding to a Shin Min Daily News report which quoted unnamed sources saying his impending exit was linked to the saga.

       Mr Yam said: "The rumour is totally unfounded. I want to clarify that I am not resigning and my decision to retire from the Administrative Service has nothing to do with the incident.

       "I can't imagine where they got the information from."

       On Dec 12, Mr Palmer announced at a media conference that he had quit from his public posts after admitting to an affair with a PA staff. Later the same day, PA identified the woman as Ms Ong. This led to observers asking if employee confidentiality had been breached.

       It prompted PA deputy chairman Lim Swee Say to say a few days later that the association had thought long and hard on whether to identify Ms Ong to the media.

       Mr Lim, who is also a Minister in the Prime Minister's Office, said then that he had discussed the matter with Mr Yam and other senior staff.

       Mr Yam said last night that his move from PA was planned before the Palmer-Ong saga broke out. He intends to join the private sector but has not finalised his plans.

       Prior to joining PA, he was chief executive of the Land Transport Authority, and before that, the Ministry of Transport's deputy secretary.

       Mr Ang Hak Seng, currently chief executive of the Health Promotion Board, is touted to take over as PA's new head.

       Mr Yam, who shot to fame in the 2011 General Election as returning officer, will assume the same role for the Punggol East by-election on Jan 26.

     

    Top of the news, The Straits Times, Friday January 11 2013, Pg A10
