    Firstly, i would like to introduce to you about bowling balls. They comes in many different brands, weights and colours. They can weight from 6 pounds(lightest) to 16pounds(heaviest), and price ranging from $100 to $400 including drilling and inserts. The brands available in singapore are Ebonite, Brunswick, Storm, Hammer, RotoGrip. Ebonite and Hammer are "bothers" company, so is the case for Storm and RotoGrip.

    For fit wise, there are 3 kinds. Conventional, finger tip and spinners. The house balls that you use at bowling centres are all polyester ball drilled with the conventional grip, that means you can insert your fingers up to the 2nd joint. this provides more grip and control. "Spinners" fit are similar to the conventional fit, but i am not too sure as i never bowl spinner before. Mayb the spinners can share some info about their fit? the last one will be Finger tip, this is the grip that you should choose if you intend to learn playing Hook, that means if you want your ball to travel in a curve instead of just going straight. In his fit, the fingers are just drilled to the depth of your first finger joint. this is to enable you to create more revolutions with the ball, which is needed to make the ball hook!

    Next, i will talk about the kind of bowling balls in the market. There are basically 4 kind, polyester, reactive, pearlised and particle. they can also be categorised into beginers, entry performance, mid performance and high performance ball. the price is as follows:

    Polyester -$100 to $140(special editions cost even more)
    entry performance -$190 to $230
    Mid performance -$240 to $$290
    High performance -$300 to $400

    Polyester is the most basic bowling ball, the material is the same as the house balls you used in bowling centres. this is the ball that is recommended for most beginers. This ball does not react much with the lanes and pretty much go where you send them, ie it goes straight. advance bowlers use this ball for shooting spares too. So for beginers, it is a good choice to start with, and eventually when you move up to higher performance balls, your first polyester ball can stil be used to shoot spares!!

    As for reactive, pearlised and particles ball, they have special coverstocks(the surface of the ball) that enable it to find friction on the lanes so that the ball actually hooks and not go straight. however, do not expect to be able to play hook just by buying a performance ball, you stil need to learn how to properly release the ball in order to for it to hook. It is just like computer, the ball is the hardware, and your release technique is the software, in order to hook the ball, both software and the hardware must work hand in hand!!!

    So after you decide the style of bowling you want, your budget and performance level you want, and verify that the pro shop is indeed professional. You can make the decision to buy the ball.

    So wat is the right weight to choose? It really depends on individual builts and strength, but a safe guide will be to the weight of the house ball that you are comfortable with +2 pounds! This is because when the ball is drilled to your hand specifications, it will provide a better grip and hence you can handle a heavier ball than your usual house ball. so if you always bowling with a 10 pound house ball, it would be safe to buy a 12 pound ball.

    You will usually have to choice of whether you want to use thumb blocks and finger inserts anot. It is individual preference, but mostly recommended for hook bowling, as it is more comfortable and give a more consistent release.

    After your ball is drilled, test the feel of the ball, the distance between your thumb and fingers should be such that it is comfortable and not too tight, unless that is wat you prefer. learn to keep your thumb straight and not grip the ball too tightly, relax your thumb and fingers, the ball should not drop off too easily, or else it means that the the holes are drilled too big. Lastly, remember to get a see saw(ball carrier) from the shop, it should be included.

    Buying a performance ball

    When you intend to buy a performance ball, there will be more things to look out for. It is always good to read about the ball you intend to buy from its official website, find out more. don't just listen to the pro shop one sidedly, as i have mentioned before, many pro shops are not as professional as it seems and should be. alot are just interested to clear watever stocks they have on hand.

    factor 1) Pin distance

    The pin is the spot on the ball with a coloured dot, usually yellow colour, about 1 cm in diameter. this is actually the spot which marks the position of the core inside the ball. The centre of gravity(cg) is the heaviest spot on the ball, marked by another dot or mark. Ask the ball driller where these spots are if you not sure, and if they can't answer you, get out of there!!!! the guy obviously dunno much abt bowling ball, let alone ball drilling.

    the distance between the pin and the cg is very important. for spinners, you should choose a pin-in(0-1inch) ball, or 180 degree oppisite. if you are playing hook, try not to buy a pin-in ball, at the very least the pin and the cg should be more than 1 inch appart. this is because a pin in ball will not hook much.

    factor 2) ball mapping

    this part is much more complicated and takes alot of basic knowledge in order to understand ball mapping. It is also not easy to tell the ball driller wat you want the ball to do. But it is definitely possible. you can tell the ball driller to make your ball go long and strong, or early and strong, or wat have you. in order to do this you need to have very good knowledge of bowling already of cos. In this case, if the ball driller do not know how to give wat you wan, then it simply means he is not good at ball mapping.

    for others who dunno much about wat you want, if you already have a ball, bring it along when you buy the new ball, the ball driller will have to look at your old ball, analyse your ball track, measure your positive axis point(PAP) and ask you want you want to do with your new ball. If he did not even bother about your old ball and simply proceed to drill, stop him, dun buy the ball from him, because PAP is a major influence on ball reaction, and if he doesnt even care where your pap is, it means he either can't be bothered, or he is a lousy ball driller. so do you trust your new $300+ performance ball with such a ball driller?? you judge yourself.

    Coverstock
    anyway, i saw someone with the question abt ball erratic behavior. So mayb i can try to explain why this is so and explain more abt coverstock. For beginners to understand more easily, you have to understand why a ball hooks. A ball hooks only when it finds friction. Different lanes may have different amount of oil and oiling pattern at different times, so your ball may hook alot when the lane is dry, but a few hours later when u return to bowl again, you find that the ball hook much lesser. This is most prob due to that the centre just oiled the lane again, so your ball finds less friction, and therefore hook less. so i shall expalin now the different type of coverstocks

    polyester- min friction, goes very straight.

    reactive-reactive coverstock is special mixture of urethene with added resin that makes the ball tackier and hence increase friction, therefore increasing the hook potential.

    pearlised-pearlised are reactive coverstock that is hardened to make the ball slide through the heads of the lane. so that they retain more energy for the backend. specially used for heads break down(when oil at the heads is carried further up the lane by balls).

    particle- particle coverstocks are reactive coverstocks that have added microscopic particles on the surface. This creates very higher friction and is especially useful in very oily lane. The hookiest coverstock of all three kind.

    Od cos there are always changes, now they have new coverstocks called particle pearl, that ensure alot of energy is retained and have a very strong backend.

    The trend now is towards reactive and also more shiny pearlised ball actually, as in tournament condition where every player use high end particle balls, these balls absorb so much oil at the heads such that within 1 game, the heads will become very dry and breakdown, and from then on, the use of particle ball will not be suitable as it will read the lane too early and may hook too much and lose too much energy at the backend.

    ok...i tell you, drilling balance hole has 3 uses:
    1) bring the ball to legal standards.

    2)slightly modify ball reaction, taking weight out of ball by drilling balance hole alters slightly the RG of the ball, so ball reaction can be altered slightly by deciding how much weight to take out, and for the same amount, whether to drill shallow with bigger holes or drill deep with smaller holes.

    3) alter your ball tracks on your ball, by moving the balance hole position up or down with respect to the centre line of the ball will shift your tracks, or rather the tie knot of your ball tracks on your ball up or down. So for example if your ball tracks flare over your finger hole, you can drill a balance hole upwards from the centreline, and your your ball track will automatically shift downwards and keep clear from your finger hole.

    As for mass bias, it is the spot with mass closest to the surface of the ball, the placement of this spot will influence if your backend reaction is strong or even, you can even place it in reverse for those with extremely high rpm to make the backend not so strong, so that your ball would not over hook.
