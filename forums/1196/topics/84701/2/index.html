    coolger03: Not all period cramps are caused by endometriosis. Some are, but not all. The common type of menstrual cramp is caused by the body's overproduction of a chemical (prostaglandins) that makes your uterine contract excessively, thus causing cramps.

    If you suspect something is wrong IamAngeline, or just want peace of mind, then you should go to a doctor to get a pelvic exam.

    Otherwise, regular painkillers like Panadol are ineffective, and you just increase your intake every month as your body gets used to the dosage. You should ask your doctor for drugs like Ibuprofen or Naproxen. These drugs reduce the production of prostaglandins.

    And then there are the therapies for cramps, e.g. heat applied to abdomen, diet, etc..
