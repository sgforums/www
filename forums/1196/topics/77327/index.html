    NKF files writ against SPH

    The National Kidney Foundation (NKF) is suing Singapore Press Holdings (SPH) over an article in The Straits Times (ST).
    .
    The NKF filed a writ of summons in the High Court on Friday, following a lawyer's letter sent to ST on April 19 asking for an apology and a retraction.
    .
    ST's failure to comply has "increased" the injury done to the charity and its CEO, Mr T T Durai, said an NKF spokesperson. The writ was filed against SPH and a reporter who wrote an article, "The NKF: Controversially ahead of its time?" which, the NKF said, implied it had mismanaged donors' money and was defamatory.
    .
    An SPH spokesperson told Today after it received the letter, the company had asked for time to examine the matter and prepare a response. It then received the writ on Saturday.
    .
    "We will be looking into this and are unable to comment on the course of action at this point." Â— Derrick A Paulo
