    I myself still eat beef because I have not become a Vegetarian yet. So what does Buddha say about meat eating? There are certain conditions that we must look out for before eating the meat. According to the suttas, all Buddhists should eat the 'Three-pure meat', 'pure' here in my understanding means karmically purer.

    "Three-pure meat" has the following conditions: The animal was not specifically killed for you*; you did not witness the killing; and you did not hear the killing. There is plenty of meat and fish (not live) in the markets that is "three-pure meat".

    * This includes those live seafood you ordered from restaurants! So try not to order live seafood yourself.
