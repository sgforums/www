    THe best way to u turn is to make sure:
    1. ur eyepoint is correctly looking at the place u wanna go..

    2. keep ur eyepoint there till ur bike is going in that direction.. avoid the temptation to look at the curb or otherthings at the side like trees or instructor bike etc...

    3. cover ur rear brake wif ur right leg slightly braking thru the whole u turn...

    so remember this few points and practise makes perfect. ;o)

  • Noah
    23 Jun 02, 00:52
    Hi Snow,
    so you're on the oad again har ?
    For your problem ..... I share my experience lah, whether effective for you or not, you decide lar ....

    1. Dun half clutch. Engage the engine fully. Start with first gear, and switch to 2nd when you have gained momentum (started moving). All this time dun touch the clutch lor .... Because with half clucth, if you dun have euff biting point, the bike loses power and balance, and you tend to put your leg down to stabilise yourself. By not clutching, all the engine power is transmitted, you just control the speed with the throttle. You will not lose balance unless your speed too slow until cannot maintain. Dun worry, if properly done, this technique can negotiate decent U-turn. I everyday have to do U-turn and the turn I have to make is quite steep. This method works best for me. You see for youself if you can use this.

    2. If speed too fast, tap the rear brakes. Dun jam it, but tap and feel your speed. Still going too fasthen increase pressure on the rear brakes, until you are comfortable.

    3. The seemingly insignificant but most important thing. Yes, eyeline. You must look ahead to where you wanna turn. It has this "strange, but true" impression on me ... I mean what can you doo just y looking ... but it seems like certain things really need eye-power ... aha hha

    Best of luck !

    [QUOTE]Originally posted by snowball72:
    how to U-properly??

    my half clutch control kanasai. when the bike turn halfway, bike no power liao.. start to wobble

    release the clutch lever slightly to transmit more power to rear wheel. If the engine feels like knocking and dying off, just pull in the clutch, open the throttle and release the clutch again

    suppose to u-turn den liao lane change to left. but either cannot make a good u-turn or is str u-turn into the leftmost lane. den after dat can't lane change liaoz.

    try leaning your bike ( without leaning your body as well ) as much as u can by pushing the handlebars and keeping your upper body as straight as possible to maintain your balance ( transfer your weight through your left foot to the foot pedal ).
    However, if your speed is really too slow, u wont have enough leverage to lean your bike, in this case just maintain good eyepoint and steer slightly using the handlebars

    hope this helps
