    27 Oct 02, 10:39
    my advice is:

    1) first to wash, the chain. Use kerosene or diesel or any fancy chain washing cleaner from watever fancy brands, although the former is recommended. After you finish washing the chain with a soft brush and cloth, proceed to ride your bike slowly ard the carpark to fling off the excess kerosene.

    2) Proceed to wash your bike top-down. If your swingarm is dirty with the grease and such, use kerosene dapped onto cloth and wipe it off. Rims, swingarms, anything. Use soap to rinse that oily film off. Rinse off all soapy substances with water.

    3) Let dry. Proceed to wax the paintwork parts. Let the wax dry up. Here's a dilemma, you wan shine or comfort? If you wan shine, wax your bike under hot sun. Else, do it in shade at ur own comfort. Next proceed to wipe all parts that doesnt need to wax and polish with autosol/brasso for those chrome parts. After polishing the chrome parts, apply a layer of wax for more longer lasting shine. (dun wipe off first). Proceed back to the paintwork waxed, wipe off the wax with a clean soft wiping cloth. After its done, the chrome parts waxed shd be dry too, wipe the wax off too.

    4) put under sun for awhile, so the chain dru up easier. When the chain is really dry, apply chainlube/oil and let it rest for some time to allow the wax/oil to settle in on the chain.

    5) drool.
