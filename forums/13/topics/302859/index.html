    To correct some misconception.

    Even if you are above 21. You are buying a bike under installment. You will still need a garantor. The only difference is that whether the bike shop trusts you. If they dont, then they will rather you bring along a outside outside garantor.

    Generally, some bike shop act as your garantor if they are helping you take the loans from either Mitsui or some other Loan Companies. Thats why they have this thing about repossessing your bike when you dont pay up and agreements issues and so on.

    Ans: Yes, you can do the above in your name. You will need gaurantors.

    And do take note, that you need to also be aware of additional finances needed for the following:

    a) Insurance Premium. Peg at S$1k minimum IN CASH if its not covered with the installment plan you take from the bike shop.

    b) Road Tax, COE, ERP Unit, Transfer Fee, Agremeent Fees, Rack & Boxes & even a deposit if they are not covered by the installment plan. Around S$2k.

    c) A tank of Petrol; a bottle of chain spray; bike wash items; raincoat & pants; cash card, bike parking coupon & your season parking. (take note that season parking starts in the beginning of the month at some location, so you might be better off using parking coupons and then applying for giro for season parking for the new month.) This should take up at least S$100/- which should stabalise to about $60 a per month.

    d) Also do standby rainy days monies, where you might encounter summons. servicing, changing of engine oils and regular maintenance.
