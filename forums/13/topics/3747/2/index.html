    actually i think this description of cruisers only apply to the bigger cruisers, 2A and above. i dont think it would apply much to the phantom if u were to compare it to other 2B bikes, especially the phantom150.
    i dont think its too noisy. my phantom 150 sounds similar to roadsters like the rxz, and not as throaty as 2B racers. i've heard the phantom200, i actually think it sounds nicer than than the 150, a nice deep rumble, very cruiser-like. the 150 sounds like a normal roadster. no offence to scrambler riders, but i think scramblers are noisier, with their high-pitched engine sound.
    donno bout the 200, but the 150, i definitely dont think its slow. obviously will lose to most racers in terms of both pick-up and top end, but against other roadsters and cruisers of its class, can put up a decent fight. and i think the weight and low center of grav makes it feel more stable when cruising at high speeds along the e-way.
    as for handling, i admit the turning angle very wide. and leaning lower to turn tighter will just end up scraping the pedal. happened to me a few times. and its heavy compared to racers and cruisers, significantly so. but if u willing to sacrifice these, you get a comfortable bike, nice sitting position. up to u lor.
