    Words from someone :

    I saw it at the Bangkok Motor Show.

    It looks pretty much like the Phantom 150. It's riding position is also pretty much the same--semi-chopper.

    The specification of the engine isn't disclosed yet. But it's 4-stroke, air-cooled and mono-cylinder.

    Some improvements over the Phantom 150 are as follows:
    1. Electric starter.
    2. Tubeless tires.

    Following have NOT been improved:
    1. Lack of trip meter.
    2. Lack of center stand.

    I have a feeling that the new Phantom isn't as fast as the old one over the 100km/h range, but should be comfortable under 100km/h.
