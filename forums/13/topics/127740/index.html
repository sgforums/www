    always look where u are going... confirm wont make a wide turn (thats wad u r doing) or collide with vehicles on the other lanes

    this is normal.. everyone makes wide turns at the beginning.

    (1) look where u r turning 1st, turn left, look left.
    (2) half clutch open throttle and let ur bike move off
    (3) u feel the biting point (or if u feel u need to move faster), quickly shift to gear 2 for higher speed and quickly accelerate to the middle of the lane

    ppl fall or make wide turn cos they dont dare to turn for fear of falling or they take too much time to change gear. by the time they finish changing gear, they notice they're already going into the outer lane...

    or u can ask ur instructor to negotiate the bend and u request to be his pillion.. i think he'll be more than willing to help

  • contrarian
    21 Apr 05, 22:56
    brake to slow down before you enter the turn, and look at the exit of the turn when you are entering it.

    once you enter the turn, do NOT apply the front brake. if too fast and you feel you're being pushed outwards, either in lean more or if no choice then apply some rear brake (gently).

    tip: if you can feel your bike being pushed outwards, you're fast enough to lean inwards more. at slower speeds you won't need to bank so much.

    try to maintain steady throttle through the turn to avoid wobbling.

    once you turn past the apex of the corner, you can open the throttle slightly. this will help to get your bike upright.
