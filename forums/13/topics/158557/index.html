    Some tips, I pick up during my 5 years riding,
    1.) Defensive Riding.
    2.) Kill all ur bad bike habits. Yup, even squeezing between vehicles during red light.
    3.) Ride with an open mind. So wat if the car try to race u, so wat the car cut ur lane, keep cool and forget it, no point getting angry with such actions. As mentioend, bike is always on the losing end.
    4.) Ride with eyes open big big. What is more dangerous than riding a motorbike? Riding without paying attention to the road. U can easy avoid situations by alot of stupid riders, drivers or pedestrians by just paying more attention to the road.

    The above points are hard to pick up, especially for young ppl. But once you pick up, you will have less stressful time on the road and not to mentioend, less encounter with Uncle Sam.

    Originally posted by hisoka:
    yeah but very irritating sometimes feel like knocking some down to tell them dun cut queue in front of me
    Ha!
    If all bikes form up, left, right, left, right, two by two, like they are taught during training, the queue at traffic lights will be super long.
    Further more, do you notice the first car waste 1 second of the green light before moving off, and the second car takes 2 seconds, and the third car.......this is precisely the reason why Singaporean drivers developed the habit of honking immediately when the light turns green.
    I don't like squeezing between cars, and I don't while traffic is moving.
    But when traffic stops, and there is sufficient space between vehicles, I will move as far ahead as I can. Too many times, while following properly behind vehicles, I get 2 red lights before I clear the junction.
    Now whenever all vehicles have stopped. I squeeze up to the front.
    It is not a good thing to do, but heck you clear that congestion faster, and you breath in less fume.
