    Yes, playing with the clutch will help in improving the bike's acceleration. But do remember that the clutch plate will wear out faster becos of the friction

    In SPs, the power will kick in at higher RPM. I guess the same also goes for other 2 strokers (TZM, KRR), although they might have broader power range. Try to shift gear at around 7000-8000 RPM. You should get enuff acceleration, and no longer need to play with the clutch.

    My problem is a bit different, it's the 1st gear that's troubling me. It feels quite heavy when the bike starts to move, for example after a traffic light. Takes a while to increase the RPM from 1500 to 3500, then it will accelerate normally. Anybody has the same experience?

    Originally posted by LexZEUS:
    Do you feel that your front brake is more powerful than rear brake?
    Yes. 1st I thought it's only my bike. But since you have the same prob, I guess it's common in SPs.
    Just to add on, front brake will always be more powerful than rear brake. It has larger disc (double front discs for big bikes), and sometimes more powerful calipers. But bear in mind that your leg is much more powerful than your hand. So the power implied to the brakes should equal each other. But somehow the rear brake in SP feels just like a drum brake

    Originally posted by LexZEUS:
    What does engine brake mean?

    Originally posted by Arrow19:
    engine braking is the time when u close ur throttle (dun pull in clutch) the bike will haf like auto braking.. that is engine braking la...
    Try to drop the gear at around 5000-6000 RPM, and release the clutch immediately. The RPM will shoot up, and you'll feel something is holding back the bike. That's engine brake. It's useful when you have lousy brakes, or when you're riding fast and need to de-accelerate quickly.
    Gear ratio also plays a part. Dropping from 6 to 5 at 6000 RPM won't give you significant engine brake, but try to drop from 3 to 2 at 6000, and your bike will jerk

    Based on my experience, just closing the throttle won't give you enuff engine brake if you're riding a 2-stroker. You still have to drop the gear. For a 4-stroker, closing the throttle will give you some engine brake, and some more when you drop the gear.
