    2 or 4 stroke refers to the how many strokes is needed for one engine cycle (oor whatever it is called). In a 2 stroke, one engine cycle is usually like: piston goes down, air comes in, fuel comes in, piston goes up, compresses and mixes air and fuel, spark plug sparks, and conbusts the fuel air mixture pushing the piston down and propelling the bike forward.

    2 stroke --> One *bang* everytime the piston goes up and down (hence 2 strokes). as in down-up-bang-down-up-bang.

    4 stroke -> One bang every 4 strokes. eg. down-up-down-up-bang-down-up-down-up-bang-etc etc.

    2 stroke is less reliable and will generally not last as long as a 4 stroke. But it is easier to repair when comething does go wrong since it is less complicated. For the same engine size, has more oomph than a 4 stroke.

    4 stroke is more reliable but produces less oomph.

    Illustration: Honda NSR 125 is a 2 stroke. Honda CG125 is a 4 stroke.
