    lemme try on the push-start/battery thing:

    the batt is charged by the alternator ("magneto" + "coil" or whatever they call it), which is connected on the crank. AC current is converted to DC to charge the battery and power the CDI.

    if your bike is the kick-start type, each action you kick is actually charging that battery for that tiny bit and powering the CDI for generating that spark. once your engine kicks into life, it's a self-sustaining process. however, to ensure that your other stuff like lights and indicators can work, a battery is needed to power it. why? because the power generated by the alternator is dependent on the engine rev. the purpose of the batt is to store extra current when the alternator produces more electricity than the bike requires, and this is used when you turn on the lights etc.

    electric start uses a starter motor to start. so if your batt goes completely flat, you're screwed. if you press the starter and nothing happens, could be that either the battery is flat or the starter motor is screwed. test out the battery first. if the battery is fine (lights etc), can still push start and head for your mechanic to service the starter motor (or starter carbon or electricals).

    hope this helps!
