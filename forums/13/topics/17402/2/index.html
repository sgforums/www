    Originally posted by roger:
    i have tried a sp before and i am riding a krr now. here are some of my opinions.

    on questions regarding the frame, u will not feel the frame difference when riding either bikes. the krr frame is composed of tubes and the sp one is of a twin spar aluminium type. it makes no difference whatsoever to top speed or cornering abilities.

    on the fairing issue, the krr fairing does not fully cover the engine and therefore makes servicing less of a hassale. the sp fairing covers almost the entire engine and
    can make degreasing the engine a headache. therefore the sp's engine tend to get dirtier easily.

    as for the finishing, the sp's decals are not long lasting and tend to peel off if rider, pillion or luggage accidentally scuff against it. when ridden often in bad weather, i doubt the SP stickers will last long.

    the krr (green or silver version) have less stickers and does not face this problem.

    performance wise, its more or less the same. i can say that the sp does not have problems keeping up with the krr.

    your question on the 0-100km/h is highly irrelevant in singapore. even if the bike take 3 seconds to reach 100, is it of any use to you? i doubt u can use this acceleration in singapore.
