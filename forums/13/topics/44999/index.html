    Just like to contribute some.

    First I've riding my TA200 for nearly a year without much mishap, except skipped off the road due to the original damn lousy IRC tires (got it changed soon after that)

    All bike chain needs constant lubricating due to Singapore wet / dry weather and proper monmitoring of it tension. Too tight a chain is not suitable for pillion as it needs to stretch more.

    Don't throw clutch while shifting gear, this will cause the bike to jerk and to cause unnecessary tension on the chain. I always change gear, throttle abit before slowly letting the clutch go. Hehe..., my chain has never been adjusted since I bought the bike. But regular engine servicing is a must.

    Then coming to the famous phantom sound....I got it smoothen out by pouring in engine additive. now the bike is smooth and quiet. I can tell the diff when riding next to a phanton on the road.

    Well, always take care the bike well and the bike will take care of you while you are on it.

    Happy riding!
