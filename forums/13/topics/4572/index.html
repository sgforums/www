    what i learnt today, i try make it short and simple

    the reason you feel a loss in power when you switch to a power filter (KNN etc), or if you remove your airbox. is because at Â¼ and Â½ throttle, there is too much air, and too little petrol in your carburator.

    this causes your engine to run lean (which means, not enough petrol to burn)

    your petrol is shot into your carburator through a needle and a main jet. the size of your main jet determines how much fuel is burned.
    2T does not enter the carbs through the main jet.

    when you switch to using pre-mix, you are increasing the thickness of your petrol, because now, there are 2 things flowing through the main jet, petrol and 2t.

    assuming your mixture is 30:1, you are actually pumping 3.33333333333% less petrol into your carburators (which if you had paid attention earlier, causes your engine to run lean).

    so if you do not have an air box, if you removed your 2t pump. and you are still using your original main jet, you are actually burning less petrol than is needed for your bike.
    this is why you lose pick up.

    basically, the same theory applies whenever you modify the airflow of your bike.
    if you kuitoh your pipe, that means your exhuast is going to flow better, which also means there is going to be more air in your carburator.
    if you change your entire pipe, there is going to be EVEN more air in your carburator.

    a simple solution is to just reduce the airflow into your carburator, but this will reduce your top end power, because at full throttle, the engine will be running to rich (means too much petrol and/or carburator tio flood)

    however, this is all mototiams do

    the real solution is to buy a bigger main jet as well as longer needle.

    happy modding.
