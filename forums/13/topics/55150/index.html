    Originally posted by TZR:
    Go and die! *BISH*
    what he said is true to a certain extend.
    the action of having your rims dirtied by chain lub is call 'fling' of the lub. a couple of ways to solve this problem.

    1) get scott-oiler - a device that drips engine oil directly on your chain. flow can be controlled. do a search on the web for it.

    2) use chain lubs that doesnt 'fling easily. one very very good example is Motorex. its available in MotorX which is opp BikeTech.

    fyi, please be careful if you wanna use wd40 to clean the rims. WD40 will harden/spoil your chain so never lub your chain with it.

    what i would do:

    1) clean chain with diesel
    2) clean rims with diesel
    3) shampoo them
    4) wash off the shampoo
    5) dry chain and rims
    6) spray on Motorex chain lub

    mods: no advertising or hard feelings meant.
    just my 2 cents worth
