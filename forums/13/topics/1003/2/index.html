    Hi!

    Atti: the reason why production of starlet was discontinued is because the car couldnt meet increasing emission standards.

    About whether a car will be unstable at 100km.....I think it is more than just the type of car. Any (most) cars can reach 100, its one of the benchmark sprint time that the industry uses to rate the acceleration of cars (another being 0-160kmh)
    Anyway as long as the road is straight and bumpless and windless, stability isnt much of an issue. But because there are no such roads in Singapore, varying degrees of the above affect varying types of cars with varying types of running condition.

    Has anyone ever followed a car and see it bounce and bounce after hitting a bump? Dont condem the brand! Its probably a worn out damper thats the culprit.

    As for continental cars being heavier and more stable, it is true that continental cars are heavier than their asian counterparts (much like the continentals and their asian counterparts oops). Therefore the cars would be more stable - in a straight line.
    But heavier cars have more momentum so its gonna be more difficult to change the direction of a continental car in an emergency. Of course modern driving aids help.

    Just my 2 cents worth....cheers!
