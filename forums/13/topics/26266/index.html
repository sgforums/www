    mmmmmkay, let's discuss this... but i must first state that i'm not too sure where the 2T goes to for a 2 stroker.. i see a tube leading from my 2T reservoir to the carb, and another one going into the crankcase...

    digress a little: for 4 strokers, it won't harm the engine. your engine is lubricated no matter what happens.. in fact, when your bike runs out of petrol and it just dies, no issue at all since your piston(s) are more thoroughly lubricated by the 4T in the crankcase (petrol "washes" off the 4T on the cylinder walls/piston rings).

    for 2 strokers: don't think it will, but i'm assuming that the stupid tube that runs into the crankcase actually does some work. besides, if 2T is fed into the float bowl, even when your bike runs out of petrol, 2T will still atomize/evaporate and enter your cylinder (when your engine is ABOUT to die, the piston is still moving, so airflow is there). maybe perhaps not that much. which leads to a discussion about how the vapour pressure of a substance is influenced by the presence of "impurities" (that is, petrol), but that's something i can't really contribute on...

    Running on a single ring is not going to cause any major problems
    (hopefully), but unless you're racing it then stick to the twin ring set-up.

    The two rings will give better low rpm sealing, and so more power, so
    offsetting and added friction.

    A single ring will give better high rpm performance as thanks to the high
    combustion chamber pressure the single ring seals well enough, so that a
    second ring is not needed, and would be just going along for the ride.
