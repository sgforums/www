    the way i see it.

    u need to go for revision. and ASK the instructor for detailed instructions on the entire route.

    alot of people attend revision having the mentality that they just take the bike and ride around. what they don't know that it is the best time to clear up any doubts.

    1. dunno where your blindpots are? - ask for instructions on turns which the instructor feels where students always screw up

    2. fail to look out for traffic - look further. you might think the car approaching you is far enough for u to make a move. but the tester doesn't think so.

    3. change lane abruptly - i'm not sure if SSDC teached you lane change properly. the angle you change your lane depends on how much road distance you have.

    4. incorrect position - please remember the rules when stopping at any stop/waiting point. these are the BASICS.

    5. unecessary stopping - probably poor judgement from junctions. what you deem as "safety stops" might be unecessary stopping to the tester.

    but the most important thing is that you go for more revision and ASK the instructor, best if he can go into detail. that's the only way to almost guarantee a smoother test where they won't fault u for the most minor things.
