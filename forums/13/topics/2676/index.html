    nope, you cant.
    if i'm not wrong (if i am and someone knows better welcome to correct me), its true that kevlar is basically made of fabric, but it is highly densitive woven fabric. as such, cutting thru a thin piece of kevlar is the same as cutting, for example, a shirt that is hundreds of pieces of cloth thick.
    it all depends on how dense the kevlar is. at its most dense, it is hard, and that is used to make the army helmets, that no longer feels like fabric but rather like solid. the bulletproof vest is less dense, hence feels like very thick shirt. and the racing suits are less dense, to make it more flexible, and anyway heh heh dont tear when buang can already in gp nobody shoot pistol at u!
    anyway, no you cant find kevlar at army surplus shops in beach road. kevlar is supplied straight to industries requiring their use. hence u need one good lobang at industries using them. best bet is places like S'pore Technologies labs as sakaratulz mentioned.
