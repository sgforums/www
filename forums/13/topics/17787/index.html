    Heh heh , i always likened riding as a cross-between driving and cycling. They work on the same principles . The former has clutches but 4 wheels , the latter 2 wheels but no clutches , so if you are good in either of them it helps alot.

    I love cycling when young , so when it came to cornering , handling a two-wheel you tend to be more steady.

    One practical advice about nervousness is to not want to pass so badly. Sometimes the harder you push yourself , the worse you perform. I realised i passed easily for every pract after i became very unmotivated about getting a licence. ( hehe that was when i helped to amputate a young lad's leg)

    So try to ride naturally as in your body and bike flow into one.

    You will find that all the reflexes come naturally after a while and you don't consciously have to think about blindspot/clutch in etc. Also during each lesson , think that you don't mind failing , then when you pass , it comes as a very pleasant surprise.

    Remember my TP , i was super relaxed , no pressure at all cos i thot i will confirm fail one, then surprisingly , i passed first time. 19 out of 80 pple.
