    few things i did when i took my tests that really helped me.

    i adjusted the idle of the bike to a higher rev. i find this helps prevent the bike from dying suddenly.

    adjustment of clutch to my preference with a further biting point. also prevents engine dying during mis clutch incidents.

    always always check your blind spots. an extra flick of your head to the side will save u plenty of points.

  • viciouskitty74
    7 Jan 09, 13:48

    You dont have to be scared,  cos testers on road are sometimes blocked view by big cars and vans.

    Your important portion to look out for is the circuit.

    Ask your instructors and have them show the map of the circuit and what is the progression from the move out point of the circuit to the road.

    Then ask about where at the map you are suppose to turn your heads or the portion most needed to change lane and what happens when there is cars there.

    Then practise turning your heads at the points where the instructors tells you to.  Even if you are not really looking, the motion of turning your head will show the tester that you are careful.
