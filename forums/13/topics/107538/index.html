Synthetic or Mineral Based oil for your bike??

  • Old Bird
    16 Dec 04, 09:10
    I don't go for synthetic oils simply because I do not ride like a MotoGP rider. Am not doing redline gear changes in between traffic lights.
    Synthetic oil is designed for engines that produce extremely high rpms and generate alot of heat. Racing engines cannot use mineral based oils coz the oil cannot handle the extreme heat and will break down losing its lubricity.

    Engine oil holds the carbon and dirt and metal filings that is generated by the combustion chamber and gearbox drive train gears and also the byproducts from the clutch plates.

    Contrary to the belief... a good oil turns black fast. It is doing its job of trapping the carbon particles in suspension. Drain that out... and you drain all the potential harmful waste from the engine.

    I change the oil in my bikes at every 5000 km and the oil filter at every other oil change. And with our climate... no need the 10W40 stuff. 20W50 is best for local use.

    Synthetic or mineral based - what is your preference??

    Ride smart. Ride safe.
