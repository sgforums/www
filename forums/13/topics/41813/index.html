    Some mechanics will tell u that you can actually increase the rpm a little after each run in ... i.e. 1st run in ... rpm 4 ... 2nd run in ... rpm 5 and so on ... But different bike different thing ... different mechanics also say different thing ...

    But the main thing to look out for during running in is not really always the dragging and the speed limit thing (though many always emphasize on this area). Another important aspect is that they get a good run-in through the gears ... i.e. you engage all the gears often ... therefore normally during running in it is good to go along normal roads and not expressways ... so that you always get to shift through all the gears ...
