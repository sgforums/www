  • Magiskter
    25 Dec 01, 13:09
    Actually the kind of coolant used by the bikes raced in MotoGP or SBK is just plain distilled water. No antifreeze/coolant additive is used at all. Coolant spilled on tracks are just as slippery as an oil spill so its banned at most tracks. Professionals agree that just plain distilled/de-ionised water is good enough for use in weather that don't see ice/snow forming. Coolant additives are used for the purpose of making sure ur coolant don't freeze up in winter. Plus certain types of coolant do hv a lubricating effect on moving places.

    If u hv a aluminium engine block in ur bike, look for coolants that state they're "alloy/aluminium friendly". Moreover, it is true that tap water contains large amounts of minerals that will cling onto parts of ur cooling system & will lower or damage their cooling capability. De-scaling or flushing ur system would then be neccessary. Do take note. Use only distilled/de-ionised water when refilling or topping ur ur radiator fluid levels. As far as I know, this is insisted in both Honda & Yamaha servicing manuals.
