    When you pull in the clutch.

    You are releasing the connection from the engine to the wheel. thus power is not transmitted to the wheels.

    that means, your speed of your bike will be uncontrolled because of the terrain.

    Eg. you pull clutch in going down a slope. becos of gravity, you are faster going down a slope and there is no control by the engines to regulate your speed.

    You might also hit a stone and made a dead stop and fly. But if you still has your clutch out and your enginer power transmitted to the wheel. You will hit a stone and most likely run over the stone and be safely on your way again.

    When you pull in the clutch, you disengage the engine.

    You clutch in and select the gear.
    Gear 1 = Push off
    Gear 2 = Low speed, acceleration
    Gear 3 = Acceleration, mid range speed (L)
    Gear 4 = Acceleration, mid range speed (H)
    Gear 5 and above = Cruising.
    You actually move with your clutch, with the proper gears engaged
    The trottle only provides the power.
