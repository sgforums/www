  • octega
    19 Nov 04, 10:45
    1.) Look Straight. (NEVER, look down onto the plank)
    2.) Do not use the front bake at all. Keep your right hand on the trottle at all times.
    3.) Use the rear brake for braking
    4.) Concentrate on the clutch and the trottle. If you feel like falling off, trottle up, use the acceleration to keep you in balance. Once you feel that you are safe, quickly, maintain half-clutch, trottle down, and tap the rear brake to slow down.
    You have to do this beri quickly and repeatly until plank is cleared and pratice makes perfect!

    The other way is to ride throught the plank normally, you will be penalise, atleast you didn't fall off the plank for immediate failure.

    this is what i did:

    1. first i check back then correct my positioning and aiming b4 moving off

    2. when moving, sit up straight, knees touching the tank and feet pressing down on the foot rest. Focus was also emphasised on the handlebar

    3. i was looking at the plank when i was about to go up

    4. to go up to plank, throttle more, and when was on it, i just release throttle(while at half-clutch) and let the bike move on its own.Of course i had to increase speed on and off to stay in balance. At this point i was actually like concentrating more on the balancing part.

    5. I feel that looking far may help in balancing, i was also looking at the slalom cones when i was on the plank, however there were some occasions where i was looking straight and far but my handle bar did not seem to be able to coordinate well with my eyes....it was moving left and right/ so occasionally i would look down at the plank while my head was upright.

    So these were basically what i did. The ratio of me falling down to going through smoothly was about 2:10. Hopefully my experience will also be beneficial to those who are also facing difficulties in this obstacle...CHEERS!!
