    i will choose (A) also. 3M came up with a tyre liquified rubber which u can squeeze the liquid into the tyres...especially for brand new tyre. let the liquid flow freely inside the tyre and in the event when the tyre is punctured, the liquid will flow and fill up the hole...then solidify. The process will lose some air...just go to the nearest petrol station to top up the pressure....and the tyre is working again.

    But so far, this technology is new, and no feedback of anyone using yet. But bike shops can cater for filling the holes up for $10 or so...its better than getting a new tyre.

    but of course, if the punctured hole is too big, then a new tyre is inevitable...
