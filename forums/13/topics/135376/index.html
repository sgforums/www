    Btw, Phantom 150cc is 2-stroke (and thus requires 2T oil) and Phantom 200cc is a 4-stroke (doesn't require 2T oil).

    In terms of speed and pickup, Phantom 150cc is better as it shares the same engine as NSR150, but it is older.

    Whereas Phantom 200cc is more fuel efficient and doesn't emit much white smoke (or any, I ain't very sure) but its acceleration is not as good as its 150cc counterparts.

    I am one of the uncles who ride a Phantom.
    Its definitely not for racing.
    And I like it.
    Yes it is very low maintenance.
    No problem since I bought a new one 3 years ago. Only changed rear brake pad.
    Though it is time I change the spark plugs, I have not done so, because I never had trouble starting the bike all these 3 years.

    Only advice I can give regarding Phantom is to get one from an authorised Honda agent that gives after sales service.
    Avoid the shops that parallel imports the Phantom.
    Phantoms come with strange noises, that gets you uncomfortable, but is usually no problem, but an authorised agent would be helpful there.
