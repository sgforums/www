    Seems like a pantang topic.

    If you ride, ride, and ride, all the time, it is only logical that you will die on the road.
    But there is no harm, in riding safely, being alert and defensive all the time, and to die of old age first.

    Remember your safety helmet's best protection is in reminding you of your vulnerability each time you put it on.

    Ride within your ability, and the capability of your bike.

    Always learn and improve, everyday there are lessons to be learnt on the road.
    Reading other vehicles movements, always asking what if that driver......... and being mentally prepared. Always be mindful of proper braking techniques. (Last year a 10 day old rider killed himself because of improper braking, his friend crashed, he wanted to stop but crashed himself and died.)
    Always be cool headed.
    There is little margin for error, therefore always be alert. Total concentration at all times. Total awareness of the situation around you.
    Riders are better people than drivers, because of the constant state of awareness the rider is in. Drivers are stressed up when they drive, they are influenced by the day's happening, their mind on their problems, and they become neurotic people while in their cars/vehicle. Beware of them and avoid them.
    One more thing, look far ahead, remember your riding instructors mantra. Singapore riders and drivers, always look as if they are walking, they look down just ahead of them. They therefore always come close to a stalled lorry/bus, and then make a lane switch because they did not see the lorry/bus well in advance.
    SBS drivers are taught the 12 vehicle rule, ie look 12 vehicles ahead of you. Spot any obstruction and be prepared to make a lane switch. It is a good rule to follow.

    Ride well.
