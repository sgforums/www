    Originally posted by stylomilo:
    2 stroke bikes must add 2T and 4T right?
    there are different tanks to add into? engine oil refers to 2T and 4T right? sorrie, still quite blur~
    Yes. As design of the 2 strokes engine, 2 strokes bikes need 2T oil to lubricate the piston block and rod.
    2T oil is store in different tank, will mix with petrol in carburetor to be "suck" in to the to combustion chamber. The petrol will burn and the 2T oil will stay in the combustion chamber.

    The excess 2T oil will be "push" out via the exhaust pipe and become white smoke.

    http://w3.one.net/~jschust/animations.html

    Engine oil (or 4T oil) use to lubricate the engine, including gearbox, clutch, etc....

    4 strokes engine no need additional oil to lubricate the combustion chamber, the lubrication is done by engine oil.

    That also explain why you see only white smoke from 2T bike only.

    TA200 has bigger engine block and having fins/blades for air flow to cool the engine.

    TA150 has small engine block as it's design, a radiotor/coolant need to cool the engine. The bike look nice with the radiator too.

    Carburetor is the device used to control the throttle, the amount mixture of petrol and air enter the combustion chamber. Tuning basically adjust the ratio of the air/fuel mixture.

    As for multiple carburetor/multiple piston engine, the balancing between each carburetor/piston needed to be carefully adjust, to make sure all piston have the equal amount fuel, and generate a well balance power, the engine will running smoother and effecient.
