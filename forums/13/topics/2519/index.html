    Firstly, you must understand that the front brakes are very much more effective in comparison to the rear brakes. Also, what your instructor meant was that if your front wheel gets locked, you're definitely gonna skid and fall. In contrast, if the rear wheel is locked, your bike will fishtail but it can still be pulled back.

    I would advise you to apply more of the front brakes(don't pull the lever all the way in though) and only a little of the rear brakes.

    Don't worry, the S4 front double disc brakes are very effective so squeeze that lever harder. With the correct technique, you'll find the e-brake a breeze soon enough.

    Originally posted by Noah:
    Still cannot get the clutch and the throttle right ... Haizz ... It's going to be really long ...
    Hey, don't be disheartened pal. Almost everyone encounters this problem while upgrading to 2A.

    I faced this problem too initially so let me share with you how I overcame it. Whenever I'm on the bike, say waiting for the lights to turn green or waiting for my turn to do the courses, I would play with the throttle. Get used to the responsiveness of the throttle. Try to make the rpm rise as slowly as possible. Once you get the hang of it, the various courses will become child's play . One more thing, when you close throttle, close it slowly as well just like how you open throttle. By doing this, the engine brake won't give you that much of a headache.

    Good luck.
