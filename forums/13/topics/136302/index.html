    atom, are u aiming to get ur class 2A or 2 later on?

    If yes, my advice, get a ah-peh bike to ride first.
    Ah-peh bikes:-
    1.) Honda CG-125
    2.) Suzuki GN-125
    3.) Yamaha, RXK, RXZ (earlier models)
    4.) etc, etc.

    Why?
    1.) It is cheaper. In terms of bike price, insurance, etc.
    2.) If u buang, u dun feel the pinch. can't be said if u buang a SP, and the fairing goes everywhere.
    3.) If worst come to worst, u want to scrap, also dun feel the pinch also.
    4.) It is more fuel economical.

    My experience.
    When I got my class 2B, my mind was already set to get a 400cc bike. So I rode on my dad's honda CG-125.

    Yeh, I look ugly, no doubt. If u happen to see a gorilla riding on a bike during 2000 o 2001. Thats me. But thats only for 1 year. After which, I got my class 2A, scrap my class 2B bike and got myself a 400cc bike. Now, I have passed my class 3 planning to take up Class 2.

    So just bear wif it for awhile then u got a powerful bike to ride liao.

    The few common 2B ferring bikes to date are quite limited.

    Yamaha TZM / TZR (older version of TZM) : Mostly are thrashed and a few of them are illegally modified with through pipe (means if caught by TP means $500 to the govt).

    And the price is more exp compared to KRR and SP, even though the bike is quite old. But said to be the fastest amongst all 2B bikes.

    Honda NSR150SP : Alot of them also already thrashed liao, very few with favourable engine condition. Looks nicer than a KRR (at least to me), price higher than a KRR generally (depending on vehicle plate), but cheaper than TZM. Stock bikes can go up to 180 kph (or less? I ain't sure, somewhere around there). And Honda parts are generally cheaper.

    Kawasaki KRR150 : Light bike, thus its pickup is slightly better. (Used to ride a KRR) Relatively cheaper and newwer than SP, 2nd hand bike can get at less than 3K. Cheapest than TZM and SP generally. But it also means the bike has no resale value.

    Honda CBR150 : 4-stroke machine, pickup slowest compared to the above. A light mahine too. But it's definitely more fuel-efficient than the rest.

    Aprilia RS125 : Has new and 2nd hand bikes. First hand bike is very exp, can get a brand new Super 4, 2nd hand is very cheap, but the engine and outlook conditionmay not be favourable anymore. Nice bike with nice posture to corner, but pickup is quite slow compared to TZM, SP and KRR.

    Italian bike, aka high maintanence bike, even a mirror by itself costs $80. Not recommended if you have a budget.
