    i think the guy typo lah, not change engine, it is change engine oil.

    ok 2 stroke bikes require 3 types of oil. 1 is 2T. 1 is 4T/Gear Oil. the last one is petrol (hahaha)

    2T:-
    it lubes your piston and prevents it from jamming. basically piston jam = $$$. use good 2t, fully synthetic types. semi-syn is ok if you don't chiong that much (that means don't always run here run there all 100++kmh).

    4T/Gear Oil:-
    in 2 stroke bikes, because only the gear box requires lubrication, some ppl claim that only gear oil shld be used. this isn't very true, but we shall ignore this for the moment.

    basically wat it does is lubricate your gears and engine as well as take away heat from there.

    there are 3 types of 4t. mineral, semi syn and fully syn.

    when you 1st buy a bike, the shop will put mineral oil into your engine. after running 800km, you should change the oil, becoz it is a new bike, it will have collected alot of debris in the oil and will become very thick. thick = bad coz cannot flow very well = cannot perform wat it is meant to do = bad.
    continue using mineral oil at this point.
    at 1.6k (or 2k, not much diff really), change your oil again. this time, switch to fully synthetic 4t or gear oil.
    then u just periodically change your oil from here on. fully syn oils usually last a good 3-5km.
