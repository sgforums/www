    http://sbo.co.za/tech/m99strokes1.html
    2 stroke engines use this process called "scavenging" to recharge the cylinder with a new air/fuel mix everytime. it's due to the engine design of not having valves like a 4-stroke engine (but the piston and crankshaft in a 2 stroke act as valves). as such, it's highly possible that unburnt air/fuel passes out the exhaust pipe with every engine revolution. this is the main emission issue regarding 2-strokers. hope this helps.
