    For me, if i get a 2nd hand bike, I'll first check:

    1) Front/Rear Brake Discs(rotorS) - whether warped
    2) Front/Rear Brakes/calipers - working or not
    3) Front/Rear shocks/suspension
    4) Front/Rear Tyres - How much life left
    5) Chain/sprocket - How much life left
    6) Alignment of handlebar with front wheel
    7) Steering cone - too stiff?

    Trust me, you won't want to change the front brakedisc as they costs about $600++ for one piece. Then go ahead with the engine and bike run test:

    1) Check if difference in bike idling sound when clutch in - determines if housing is loose and needs gasket/housing replacement

    2) Rev to the Max gradually, to determine max rpm - this will tell if exhaust system is clogged with carbon residue, also checks if valves are working (will most probably be maxed at 10,000rpm on a stock milo pipe *yucks* and 12k-13k rpm if fitted with an arrow/rs/chamber pipe system)

    3) Lastly, whether I'm comfortable with the bike itself. Take it for a spin maybe?

    All of the above, of course, is subjected to whether or not permission is granted.
    You can get your hands on parts easily from Ideal motors (aprilia agent) or from most motorshops around UBI. *not sure why people always like to spread the legend bout `parts hard to get', that's when the bike was firstly introduced to SG*
    And pricing of the bike parts are somewhat similar to cagiva's. The bike won't blow a hole in your pants if you keep your ass on your seat and not BUANG. And if you're not so UNLUCKY as to pick one in a FUGGed up condition.
