    hey man, i've been looking at the same bike also . v-twin engine configuration gives added power compared to single cylinder bikes, and it's 4-stroke, so less maintenance issues. 17l fuel tank too...

    but it has some cons i think.

    v-twins are known to guzzle more petrol, so fuel efficiency wise it may not be so cheap to ride.

    it's damn expensive. $9.5k OTR was what i was quoted. not a good choice if you think you wanna upgrade to 2A or class 2.

    this bike is damn new, press-release came out in like 2001. it's almost impossible to find a second-hand one. it may be difficult to sell, because it's a rare bike, no one dares to buy it, so you might lose money if you change bike. linked to this is that it may not be easy to find a reliable mech to service it, or find parts that the bike needs specifically. and besides, it's like a pilot project. bound to be some flaw in the design that they'll correct with the later years'. imagine if the fairing breaks, go where and find???

    no kick-starter. some people won't see this as a con, but after hearing so much about failed electric starters, esp on italian bikes and my personal experience on SSDC's honda bikes, i think it'll be way safer to have a kick-starter.

    last of all, the power of the bike is suspect. it may be a v-twin, or however powerful that honda claims it to be, but it's after all a 125cc 4-stroke bike, and it's about 150kg in dry weight. so it's debatable as to whether the v-twin will compensate the effects of the small engine size and its weight.

    don't mean to discourage you or paint such a bleak picture man. i like the bike too, just that these issues are on my mind when i think about whether the 9.5k is worth paying. would be happy to hear any good comments about the bike! i'll happily stand corrected! hahaha

    Re: Honda Varadero XL125cc

    I owned the bike and the following are the comments I have for the bike.

    1. fuel consumption : full tank approx $17.00 (95 unleaded)

    2. peformance : don't expect to "cheong" with aprilla, nsr at traffic light, no fight.
    However, smooth ride all the way to 120km/hr with pillion (no vibration). And easy riding in city.

    3. engine : smooth, quiet, minimum vibration.

    4. handling : easy to handle, but may be a little heavy for lady rider. No offence please. Front and rear
    disc brake, braking is immediate and responsive.

    Don't expect head turns as most have mistook the bike for the 1000cc varadero. Thus, no big surprise
    on the outlook.

    ay man.. i was considering this bike as well... but the power really puts me off. its 15bhp is about all the smallish v-twin can give, for a 150kg (dry-weight) frame. not wishing to intimidate you with figures, but it'll be hurt in pick-up. however, two riders of the bike have given me good reviews, regarding stability, fuel consumption and ease of ride. oh yeah, and if you're thinking of adding more power by mods, not really feasible. one owner i talked is a dirtbike/motard mech, he spent quite a packet on an after-market straight through exhaust, modified the airbox and rejetted the bike. the result? "nothing fantastic, don't expect too much..."

    er.. actually, this bike banks in on the typical new biker's desire to ride a shiny large bike as soon as possible... but the added weight of the frame with the small engine means it's not going anywhere in a hurry. it's also designed for the UK "learner legal" market, where the max power is 15bhp.

    regarding parts, i won't worry so much if i were you, but cosmetics wise, i would. it's unlikely that it would require any major parts change in the first 2 years, being a new bike, if you maintain the bike regularly. probably after then, when the market for it picks up, you're going to see more parts for it. but do think about cosmetic things like fairings. er.. not cursing you, but it's easy to get damaged fairings... accidents and stuff. not easy to find... oh yes, accessories are expensive. go to the UK honda website, they have a list of accessories there. a PLASTIC exhaust undercowling costs $260.

    as for whether you should buy a new bike as your first bike etc, i won't touch on that. just think what you want out of your bike. cheap, economical transport? then it's the bike for you. a full tank is 17litres, and can go up to i quote "450+km" but if you're looking for something fun and fast to ride when you need the pickup, it's probably a bad choice. just drop me a line if you wanna know more about it. btw, $7k sounds quite impossible, especially OTR, everywhere i've gone to quotes $9.5k OTR, at least. kuang tai lee at ubi even quoted me $10.4k!

    so which shop quoted you $7k? care to share? don't worry, don't think lines of people are forming to "snatch" the bike from you...
