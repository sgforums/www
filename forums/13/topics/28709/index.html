    You can perform 3 things.

    1) Improve power. This would mean things like spark plug, certain pipe. Would also improve general bike performance but very expensive.

    2) Change gear ratio. Bigger rear sprocket or smaller front pinion both increases pick-up. More commonly used by ppl.

    3) Improve mechanical efficiency. Things like high-grade chain, sprocket. This is very minimal and not normally recommended.

    Originally posted by scorpione_phantom:
    Thanx for your info.. but don't understand why is there a change in the rpm??? can elaborate more??
    In lay mens' term...

    RPM = power
    Pick-up = Torque
    Speed = angular velocity.

    Recalling that power = (torque)x(angular velocity). If you increase torque while maintaining power, angular velocity will decrease.

    This is why when you change rear sprocket to bigger to increase pick-up your top speed will decrease.

    There is an increase in RPM(power) because you need to increase power to achieve same speed(angular velocity) as before. Torque is determine by gear and sprocket ratio and is fixed.
