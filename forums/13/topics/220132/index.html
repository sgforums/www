    first,you will have to be 18 years old and above...with have no demerit points accumulated...
    then go to a riding centre such as SSDC, BBDC or CDC...
    go to their counter, say you want to register for class 2B.they will then do a eye test for you on the spot and fill up your particulars.after the process is completed, you are officially a student at that school.

    as a student, you can book practical lessons. each school has their different way of doing practical and theory lessons. so when u registering as student at the counter, the customer service officer will explain to you the steps.

    it is normally about 20 plus dollars per practical lesson. most of the time, you can book your practical lessons online.

    Just a slight correction. You _can_ have some demerit points (that is, if u have a driving licence). To be accurate, you cannot have more than 12 demerit points before you take your class 2B practical test.

    The whole process is usually between 3 - 6 months. I have not heard of anyone taking less than 3 mths actually. Budget spending somewhere between $600 and $1,000 in total for all your lessons and traffic police practical test.

    You can enrol online or in person at any of the 3 centres. Living in Kallang, the CDC one is obviously the closest. Just bear in mind that in the initial lessons, the bike instructors are a bit strict and "quai lan" - it takes some getting used to if you're not used to being yelled at! haha

    good luck mate.

    Things to get:
    - PSB approved helmet (it's a blue sticker at the back) and no tinted or chrome visors!
    - Gloves (aka durian gloves. U can most likely get from the canteen for $1; but buy from hardware stores cheaper.)
    - Good pair of jeans and shoes (good jeans to save ur skin and shoes with good grip) no shorts or slippers allowed
    - Sunblock (afternoon lesson will burn u) or long sleeve shirt
    - IC sized coloured photo
    - Alot of patience!!

    Ok that's pretty much cover it. CDC uses Yamaha YBR125 for it's 2b class while the other centres uses Honda CG125 (both are bout the same, cept the Yahama's faster & lighter). I never been to CDC before and I heard that it has the smallest circuit AND the strictest intructors. But in the longrun, all centres are pretty much the same. Good luck.
