    Interesting topic........

    Ok people I've search this forum but there is still 1 thing lacking in it......I know you guys out there have a few years experience of riding under your belt, so why not share safety tips.

    For example, never ride your bike less than 50% of the lane....try to ride about 75% of the lane. Reason is that if you are riding at abt 75% of the lane size, drivers have no choice but to change lane in order to overtake you. If you are riding below 50% of the lane size, some idiot dirvers will just overtake you side by side forcing you to the kerb.

    If possible, buy riding helmets that is with d-ring not the clip like our porch. Although it is easier to clip our strap than to put in into the ring type, it is the most dangerous as in the event of an accident, the clip tend to break making your helmet flew off from your head!

    Please on normal beam instead of using pole light. For better visibility.

    Wear full covered gloves. Reason, if we go riding, ccol wid tend to blow making our knuckles slightly numb. In the long run it will affect our hands.

    Try to roll yourself instead of sliding on the road when you collide your skid your bike. This is to minimise your injury.

    Do not use bloody tinted visor. Although it looks cool, but you will have a hard time riding in the night.

    Try to pump your tyre about 10 to 20% more than the recommended. Our body weight plays apart.

    Never use cheap tyres. Try to get as much information on tyres as possible before changing it. Tyres are the thing that divide between the bike and the road surface.

    That's about all for the moment....;p
