    er... doing the u-turn is almost exactly like doing the crank course.. that's assuming that you stop at the junction lar.. wobbling is caused by incorrect acceleration, mostly too much or too little. too much, you'll be struggling to control the direction, and the panicky braking you'll apply then doesn't help much either. too little, you'll be struggling to balance the bike (think narrow plank when your bike almost "see-chia"). i'd say try to improve your clutching and throttling when moving off, and if you master the figure 8 and crank course, there should be no problems... what i do whenever i'm waiting for people in front is that i push the bike backwards (a little), and then release clutch and throttle.. so i get familiar with the bike's biting point and characteristics. wears out the clutch plates i know.. but erm.. your concern is to pass right? besides.. they service their bikes also.. so why not?? oh please check behind you when you do this, and don't do it during the test hor...
