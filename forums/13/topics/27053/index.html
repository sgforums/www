    This is a tricky subject, but one I've approached many times.
    These products offer increased mileage, prolonged engine life etc etc.
    Look on the packaging and all thats in the stuff is a big dose of Teflon. Teflon is made by ICI and on their recommendation should never be placed inside a motor. Of course this doesn't stop people from advertising the stuff, furthermore it is said to bond with metal thereby reducing friction, again a complete lie. There is no product that can bond with metal and do what they claim.

    Teflon only remains suspended in the fluid its poured into, and the reason the engine quietens down (really only noticeable in older vehicles with noisy valvegear) is the fact that as Teflon heats up it expands massively, filling up the loose tolerances. This is okay for older vehicles but on new twin cam multivalve engines with tiny oilways this can be highly dangerous in a high power environment.

    This can also damage turbo bearings etc due to the fact that oil is used to cool engine parts too.....the teflon takes up valuable
    oil space, the more oil the better. Motorup etc is 90% oil and 10% Teflon, you wouldn't get me putting it in my bike/car.

    If it was that good then F1 teams around the world would use it, instead they choose a Synthetic high grade oil (Mobil 1, Castrol RS etc). It is true though, NSR250's need a better oiling system.....maybe it would stop a few crank failures.. But it remains to be said, if a NSR is looked after with preventative maintenance and good quality two stroke oil it will last many years.
    I'm sure this will spark debate..
