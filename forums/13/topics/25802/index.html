    Details on Gilera Runner 180 SP are as follows.

    Engine = Single Cylinder 2 stroke
    Dry weight = 107 Kg
    Displacement = 175.8 cc
    Basic POL Requirements = 2T, 4T and Coolant
    Oil tank capacity = 1.8 Litres
    Gear Change = Automatic CVT with torque server
    Fuel tank capacity = 12 Litres
    Consumption = 21 kmpl (ECE cycle)
    Max. power (non kat version) = 21 bhp/8,000 rpm
    Max. speed = 120 kph

    As you might know that the Gilera Runner 180 SP is basically a scotter class motorbike. So you might expect similar handling for a bike with "trans-city" commuting design. You can't compare an NSR 150 SP with this bike. They would both behave very differently at bends, at different speed.

    With Gilera's scooters, you might want to pay more attention on its automatic gear change technology. Namely the CVT, I believe is called "Constantly Variable Transmission". You get an almost infinite gear optimised for any speed.
