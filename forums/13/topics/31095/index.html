    Difficulty is mostly during Lesson 1 while getting used to the bike. Requirements at various stations are more stringent than 2B. For instance, you have to stay on narrow plank for >8 secs rather than 6 sec for 2B, e-brake to go 40km/h and under 13m and of course, the bumpy course. That takes a bit of getting used to. After accustomisation, piece of cake. Best of luck!

    haha... ya runner... mi got my 2A in Aug tis year but did not upgrade immediately... take my time to look ard first... as for the S4 i decide to delay till Feb then take as i going holiday in Jan!

    as for 2A classes... i took at ubi... they using yamaha zeal so the height quite comfortable for my height...

    prac 1 i tink its slope, narrow plank and slalom... the engine brake at 2A is must heavier so u cannot close trottle immediately like u do for 2B... but trust mi... you will get the hang of it very soon into the lesson... juz maintain the trottle instead of immediately closing it...
    slope not much of a prob juz tat engine brake is heavier...
    narrow plank is much easier than 2B as the wheels are larger in nature so much more easier to balance... no prob wif the 8sec...
    slalom is a little tougher as the body of 2A bike is bigger so u need to swing the bike more...

    prac 2 is on figure 8, crank course, ebrake and bumpy course....
    figure 8 must maintain trottle so u need to use rear brake more to slow down at bends... crank course the same...
    ebrake is the same as 2B but at higher speed of 40km/h... same technique as 2B...
    alot say bumpy course is a killer but personally i felt bumpy course is the easiest!!
    u will need to maintain half stand posture and put ur knees on the dent of the tank to maintain the bike... open and close trottle as u move along it...

    prac 3 is an assessment of all... juz take note that u need to do alot of safety checks... its not a matter of skills more but ur safety checks will cause u ur TP if u did not do adequately... as 2A after all u been on the road for 1 year before going for it hence they will go for ur safety checks instead... remember ur blind spots, junction checks etc...
