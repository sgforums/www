    which is why we bikers ourselves should strive to know more technical stuff about our bikes, so that we don't get chopped.

    when was the last time you changed your chain and sprockets? or cleaned/lubricated them for that matter?

    look at the teeth of your sprocket. if they're fine, they should be symmetrical. if they're worn out, they'll look very curved and sharp. go through the chain to check for loose links or anything like that.

    finally, get a rag and some kerosene (if you're using o/x-ring chains) or petrol/diesel (for plain normal metal chains), clean it thoroughly. clean your front and rear sprockets at the same time. then lube your chain well.

    see if that gets rid of the sound. perhaps it is just a dirty chain that needs cleaning.

    hi there... diesel works best for the normal metal chains, cheaper than petrol.. but only for plain metal chains..

    for o-rings or x-ring chains, it's not that good to use diesel, because the effects on the rings are not that known. petrol WILL destroy the o/x-rings... the best alternative would be to get kerosene from hardware stores. cheapest, confirm will not harm the chain. just don't smoke when you're cleaning the chain.. and don't store the kerosene bottle under your seat or your bag..
