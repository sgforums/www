    Originally posted by Ducati996:
    eh anybody can tell me diff between tubed and tubeless tyres?
    actually this has been asked before, but never mind. tubed tires use an inner air-tube that is air-tight, with a harder outer rubber shell that goes on the road. think bicycle tire. usually found with spoked rims.

    tubeless tires are just the shell, with no inner tube, the sidewalls of the tubeless shell forms an air-tight seal with the walls of the rims, sealing the air in. the inner layer of the rubber shell is coated with a protective layer that seals around a puncture. these are usually found on cast/sports rims.

    pricing difference i dunno, but tubeless tires have the advantage of not losing air pressure suddenly in the case of a puncture. if you hit a nail and your tubeless tire punctures, it will not blow out like a tubed one.. it will just leak air gradually, probably enough for you to ride to the nearest tire shop to get it patched. in my opinion safer. there're tire repair kits for tubeless tires too. heard from a friend it sells cheaply at mustafa centre.

    most spoked rims cannot use tubeless, because the points where the spokes join the rim are not air-tight. there are some that can, but mostly too expensive for us to think about.
