    It was actually a bunch of doctors that got the government to make helmets compulsory. They noticed that many of the motorcyclists that are stretchered in die of head injuries. They could have broken bones and become crippled or amputees, but it is usually the head injury that do them in.

    Helmet don't save lives.
    At best, it makes you to be aware of your vulnerability everytime you put it on.

    You tumble and land on your head.
    Your head slam on to a vehicle.
    Your head bounce on the road.
    A heavy vehicle runs over your torso/head.
    You break your neck.
    Any one of these and you're dead.

    Good padding in your helmet is very important.
    When you fly, and your helmeted head hit something and stop suddenly, your brains going to get squashed by the momentum. The padding could provide the braking effect to the head so that the brains won't bunch up in your skull.
    Its a very slim chance, but hey! its better than nothing.
