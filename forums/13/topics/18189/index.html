    hi there, heard about the learner plate system in other countries like UK. in singapore, operating any vehicle on the road without a valid license is an offence. the old system was that you could just slap on a "L" plate and ride until you're confident enough to take the test.

    currently, there are 3 driving/riding centres. bukit batok driving centre (BBDC), singapore safety driving centre (SSDC), and comfort safety driving centre (CDC). SSDC is located at yio chu kang, near the MRT station, just behind the civil defence camp. CDC is located at ubi. don't have the numbers, try their websites.
    www.ssdcl.com.sg www.bbdc.com.sg www.cdc.com.sg

    as for the theory lessons, it's not that bad, but i cannot remember the exact required standard. i think if you get 5 or less answers wrong, out of 50 or 60, you pass the test. for riding, only a single test, the riding theory test, is required, unlike driving.

    if you wish to practise without a license, the driving centres offer this service, with the condition that you'd have attained at least a certain standard in their syllabus. this varies for driving centre to centre, so i'm not that sure.

    just enrol and start the lessons, practising without a license is not advisable for public roads, as it is illegal. and you cannot rent a bike without a valid license and one year's experience in riding for that license class. practice sessions (called "self-practice" in SSDC) don't cost more than $11 or $12 per session (100 minutes). it's not worth the risk, as the penalty for riding/driving without a license is a fine and a 2 year ban from learning/driving for all vehicle classes. will be worse if you hit someone...

    hope this helps.

    enrolment: $70
    5 circuit lessons: 5 x $22
    3 road lessons: 5 x $32
    RTT enrolment: $6
    PDL application: $20/25 (can't remember)
