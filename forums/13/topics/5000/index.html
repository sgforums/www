    Below is the explaination from the senior. Mixing is unavoidable else you drain the fuel tank...... No much problem on mixing different brand and octane level....

    From: "ultra_classic35"
    Date: Thu Aug 29, 2002 1:23 pm
    Subject: Misconception of Octane..

    I had never heard of such things as dangers of higher octane.
    Basically, octane is a chemical in the fuel to control the rate of
    burning of the fuel. The higher the octane, the slower it burns.
    People always get misconception that higher octane means more power
    which is wrong. If your bike only needs only a 92 or 95 octane level,
    there is not reason why you need to pump a 98 octane fuel or more.
    One is just wasting there money. But instead, higher octane than
    neccessary will actually cos the engine to run slightly slower.
    Remember the purpose of octane is to slow down the rate which the
    fuel burns.

    But one should never never use a octane level lower than the
    recommemded by the manufacturers. Lower octane causes preignition and
    detonation, which is the worse thing an engine could ever gets.As you
    know that late model bikes are designed to have high compression, 9:1
    or sometimes 10.2:1 or even higher. The higher the compression, the
    easier the fuel will ignite as they are compressed with air. If the
    fuel ignites before the spark plugs fires, it will cos preignition as
    the word describes which will cos the combustion chamber to run at
    very high temp. Cosing damages and breaking parts. Same as
    detonation. Over here where the Nascar racing engines are running so
    high on compression that the use octane like 110 or higher.

    General rule of thumb, use the fuel that has the octane level
    recommended by the manufacturers. There is no need to pump octane
    level more than is neccessary. Unless you had lots of money to spent.
    But using a fuel of higer octane will not cause any engine damages or
    anything like that. One must remember that if you modify your bike's
    engine and there is a increase in compression, you might need to
    raise the octane level of the fuel. Never modify your engine till you
    can't use normal gas at the pump. I had seen guys here doing it here
    and when they pump gas at the station, the had to add in octane
    booster to prevent preignition. It's a pain in the ass but unless you
    are one hell of a rider....

    Hope this info helps those who wants to know more about octane.

    Eric Yeo
    Arizona USA
