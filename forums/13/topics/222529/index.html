    Class 2 is a breeze for me.
    No diff from Class 2A Except for timings for the courses.

    I also have a problem with the bumpy course, just can't get the hang of it.
    So, after the e Brake, I move off as per normal, clutch in, and roll over the first three bumps, then I release the clutch slowly and trottle gently for the rest of the bumps. Your body position must be perfect, and you must maintain the correct eye line, to keep your balance.

    Mounting the Fazers at CDC was also quite a challenge, I have to stand on my toes and quickly mount before the bike slant and collapse.

  • mancha
    5 Dec 06, 02:33
    My Class 2A was a learning experience, getting used to the weight and the power, but Class 2 was really enjoyable, as I already know the routine.
    I enjoy showing off at the fig. 8. Its all in your wrist (trottle control) and eyeline. At the plank, I just lock my sights on the tip of a cone, my hands goes on auto mode. Same for the shalom, my sight locked on the tip of the end cone, and my face always facing that direction, but my body and machine doing the sway round the pylons. For eBrake, I do my 1,2,3 method, 1 squeeze front brake, 2 rear brakes, 3 clutch in.
    In all the correct sitting posture and looking ahead is very important.
