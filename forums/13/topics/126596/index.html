  • kopiosatu
    12 Apr 05, 20:19
    right foot step on the main stand

    left hand hold the handle bar.

    right hand grab the crash bar

    rest ur weight on the right foot that is stepping on the main stand

    at the same time pull the crash bar

    look to your right and shift ur body weight to the right, in a motion like you are gonna step off and walk on ur right foot.

    You don't use strength, u use body weight and swing.

    with practice you will find it easy.

    Firstly I hope you don't make sweeping statements like the instructors...
    If you can't mount the bike on the main stand, you can't pass the TP test.

    One thing I'd add to the technique described by the others:

    When you kick down the main stand with your foot, you will reach a point when the two parts of the main stand will touch the ground. Once this happens, the bike should be stable.

    This should be the point your resting point before you look half-right and swing it in one smooth motion. It is not a function of strength, it's the momentum.

    One other tip is to take a deep breath and exhale when starting to pull it from this position.

    For Class 2B bikes, I can put the bike on the main stand just with biceps and arm.
    For those who are smaller built, I suggest using your thigh muscles.
    Bend your right knees slightly (foot steppin on the mainstand), and jerk at the crash bar by straightening your right leg, at the same time using your body weight to pull up at the crash bar.
    Practice. How many times do you park during a lesson?
    Not much.
    Don't worry. In time you will get the hang of it.
    On the TP riding test, you must park your bike on the mainstand when you finish.
