    In view of the recent surge of motorcycle accidents, I thought it might be useful for the more experienced riders here to contribute some safety tips to the newbies.

    As a matter of fact, for me, before I got my license, I did a lot reading up and asking around on the common causes of accidents and how to avoid them. To date, this knowledge has saved me several times.

    Learn from the mistakes of others, you might not have the chance to make all of them yourself.

    Let me start first.

    1. Try not to rely on the rear brakes too much 'coz your bike will fishtail once the rear wheel locks. Get used to using more of the front brakes and a little of the rear brakes while braking.

    I sincerely hope this will be beneficial to everyone, especially the newer bikers.

    I believe a good maintenance schedule to keep your machine at prime working condition is important to reduce potential for accidents that might occur due to machine failure like piston seizing, chain snapping etc.

    Also it is important to read up and practise on defensive riding. Some good articles on riding can be found here at: http://www.msgroup.org/discuss.asp

    One way to reduce one's risk of having accidents is to avoid riding early in the morning between 1 to 6am. I believe one will tend to travel much faster than normal as traffic is light and there are greater hazards posed such as motorists who might be fatigued or drunk during this time. Most of the accidents I have read in the papers usually occur during this period.

    Another thing is to avoid lane-splitting when riding. Moving to the front when the traffic is at a standstill is ok but still exercise caution and care. However, never travel in-between lanes while on the move as you might just be in the blind spot of the driver at your sides. There is nothing wrong with taking up a lane for your own but do not hog the right-most lane.

    Also keep a safe distance between you and vehicles in front of you and always brake earlier but lightly squeezing the front brake lever to signify that you are stopping. This is to give cars behind you ample notice to have sufficient reaction time to stop their vehicles as they will require a greater distance to stop compared to a bike.

    Lastly, I believe one should wear proper attire while riding. Granted it does not look cool but I think it is better than having road rash or skin grafting. I think the basic requirement should be to wear a SNELL or DOT approved helmet, gloves, jeans and shoes. I notice people spend quite a lot on changing the aesthetics of their bikes or attempting to boost performance at the expense of their own safety gear.

    Hope this helps.

    Tip #10 - "Ride with Anticipation"

    When you see a car waiting on the left side of the road, chances are, the driver might want to do an illegal u-turn (taxi drivers do this very often); Ride with the thought that the driver might make a u-turn anytime. Try to slow down and horn if you think it's necessary.
