    it is a teaching in motorcycle mechanics to run in your bike, i mean the subject and not the uncle at the motordiam.

    running in your bike for first hand is to let the engine components in your engine to "sit" into place. some parts might still be too unseasoned and cause additional friction to the new engine which is non-repairable. any strange sounds or problems experienced while running in should be highlighted to the mechanic.

    more info can be found in the local library.

    I agree with feng125 on the topic of running in. The engineers and designers of your bike definitely knows more about it than the rider.

    Thinking logically, when an engine is brand new, the various parts in the engine wear and polish themselves to the correct operating clearances during run-in. This can be seen during the first oil change when there are a lot of metal fragments in the oil but less and none during subsequent oil changes. Anyway, all bike and car manuals do state a running-in procedure for a brand new vehicle.
