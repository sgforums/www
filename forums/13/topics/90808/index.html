    But dun worry, it's just your 1st lesson, you'll get better once you get the hang of it. The best way to troubleshoot your problem is to ask the instructor. That's what they're there for.

    Do you lean your body towards your turning direction? I saw some newbies are afraid to lean their body when turning. They turn the handle bar, but when the bike starts to lean, they use their bodies to counter-balance (to keep the bike upright), because they're afraid the bike will fall. I'd say that'll make turning pretty damn heavy

    Unlike cars, bike wheels are aligned in a relatively straight line, even when turning. The shape of the tire causes the bike to turn when it leans to the left or right. So leaning is compulsory, but I'm sure you already know that. If that's not the cause of your problem, as I said, ask the instructor

    hey,

    Steering a bike is one of the fundamentals to riding a bike.

    The easiest way to steer a bike is like this:

    to turn left ... push the handle bar on the left away from your body...

    to turn right... push the handle bar on the right away from your body...

    don't lean your body into the corner....

    as the bike leans over during the turn ... keep your body as upright as possible....

    try it.... it works.... it's called countersteering.....

    cheers....

    countersteering...

    you push your right handle bar, ur bike will lean to the right naturally.

    pro riders use lean width method when turning, that's when they bring the bike to its maximum cornering angle and use their body weight to shift the CG to counter the centrifugal force.

    every bike has a maximum angle it can corner. so u use ur body weight for different situations. let's say u need make a u-turn, u will lean inwards of the turn while keeping ur bike straight so that u can negotiate a tight turn without losing the stability of ur bike.

    because when u push your bike lower for the turn, u need more speed. u-turns are low speed, so u use ur body weight to turn for u. i forgot the name is technique... lean... something.
