    Hi!

    haha.. I see that everybody here like to play colour..huh...
    Anyway, my bike is Red, not blue.

    As how to remove the rubber off the exhaust:

    1. Thin Layer: Autosol can do the trick but very shack..

    2. Thick layer =>
    Before moving off for a ride, spray oil(chain oil or any oil) onto the pipe. Once the pipe get hot, the rubber will melt again and the rubber will flow down and help to thin down due to the oil.

    Upon coming back from the ride while the pipe is still very hot. Do it fast.

    a)Get a chain oil or any oil and spray onto the rubber layer. Now the rubber should be soft due to the heat. Spray at will and spray generously
    The purpose of the oil is to act as a buffer layer to prevent contact of the rubber to stick to the pipe.

    b) Get a newspaper and crush it into a ball, use it like a cloth and wipe the rubber off. You might need to repeat the procedure several time in order to thin down the rubber layer which means U cannot do it after one trip.... unless U trottle it till the pipe get hot again.

    c) Mostly what are left are thin layer of rubber, Then use plenty of Autosol to put it to shine!!!
    -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
    Warning: Try not to use any knife or sharp object to scrap it off.. The only problem that U will have is a pipe full of scratches...

    Enjoy the ordeal.... hahaha!!!!
