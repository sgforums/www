    Originally posted by totalduh:
    Hey, anyone can explain the thing about tubed and tubeless tyres??
    Hi there,

    The important difference between tubed & tubeless tires lies in fact that tubeless tires has a inner rubber tube within the walls of the tire. Tubed tires don't.

    When a tubeless tire gets an air leak(ie. due to a nail puncture) the inner air pressure will cause the puncture to seal itself up, allowing the rider to locate a workshop & hv it patched up or tire replaced. Tubed tires will continue to leak air & that's the downside of using a tubed tire.

    Which is also why other than older models or types of bikes like a cruiser/chopper, most bikes now come with tubeless tires as stock.

    Hope this helps. Cheers!

    Just me, Magiskter.
