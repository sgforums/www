    After reading your post that you may be looking for a new car soon, I would like to offer my suggestion. I am a Mercedes and BMW fanatic. Sorry! I don't fancy sport car since I don not think they are safe and practical enough.

    Yes, a BMW is certainly an excellent choice provided that you have the budget for that type of car. The Z3 is nice, but the body is not rigid enough. As a result, the body flexes so much that the handling is a dissapoinment by BMW standards. In addition, over the long-term, you will hear many squeaks and rattles, since the body will flex even more as the car gets older. I don't know about you, but it annoys me like hell if I constantly hear those rattling in the car.

    The Audi TT is a beauty, but I wouldn't buy that car for another year or two. The reason is that they found out that the car has too much aerodynamic lift at the rear-end of the car at high speed. There have already been a serious accident reported where the TT rear-end lifted so much during high speed travelling at the German Authobahn that the car flipped over, and the driver was killed. As I read, Audi has recognized the problem and is now fixing it by adding thicker stabilizer bar and a rear spoiler to reduce rear-end lift.

    If you really like BMW, I would go for the new 323i 2 door coupe or the 4 door sedan. The six cylinders 2.5 litres 192hp engines is a "sweet sweet" thing. It runs and sounds like a jet engine at high rpm. Basically, the engine sounds so "sweet" that you constantly would want to rev the engine to its limits. It never feel stress and sound like you can run over the engine speed limits. Ohhh lala! I am drooling here as I am typing. HAHA! And, it gives good value for the money since it comes with so many high-tech stuff in the car. I think the 3 series coipue or sedan gives the best sporty handling and good ride while giving good practicality. The 328i is nicer but I think the very slight power advantage it has is not worth the extra $10,000 over the 323i(here in Canada).

    Hope my advice is helpful to you!

    Sorry to read that you BMW was totaled. Hope that your sis will recover from her injury and shock soon.

    Mercedes....mmmmm! What can I say! It's the best engineered car, aside from BMW, you can buy in the market these days. It's a benchmark for other cars out there to compare. It's a real masterpiece.

    Don't buy the year 2000 SLK yet. I read that the SLK will have some revisions in the year 2001. Mainly, there will be some minor facelifts to the body and the bumper. More importantly, they will replace the noisy 4-cylinder supercharged engine with a new 3.2L V6 218hp engine that is much much more smoother and quieter than the 4-cylinder engine. The SLK is far far more rigid than the BMW Z3. Also, like any other Mercedes, the safety features incorporate into the car is immensed - many many times safer than any other convertibles out there. However, as you know, like any other Mercedes products, it's more of a touring car than a sport car. Meaning, it's car more for cruising in a relax manner than a car that you throw around the bend.

    If you can't wait, and you must have a "sport" Mercedes, I would recommend the
    year 2000 CLK 320. There have already been some changes to it for the year 2000, namely revised bumper skirts, additional turn signal indicators on the side view mirrors and better looking wheels. I think in Singapore, you have CLK 200, CLK 230 and CLK 320. I would certainly avoid the CLK 200 and the CLK 230. It's powered by a 4-cylinder engine. The CLK 200 engine is sluggish and noisy. The CLK 230 supercharge engine is very powerful but noisy. The CLK 320 engine is powerful and smooth. But that depends on how much money you want to spend. The CLK 320 is more expensive than the CLK 230 and CLK 200. If you do not want to spend over board, I say settle for the CLK 230 if you do not mind about the noise. But, again, it's no sports car. Unlike BMW, it feels very isolated just like any other Mercs. But it does gives an excellent high speed stability and composure.

    If you do not mind about 4 door sedan, a C-Class is a good choice. But don't buy it yet, since the new C-Class will be here in 2001. The C-Class offers the typical Mercedes sense of solidity and reliability. Again, it does not have the handling of a BMW, but it does seem to build to last a long time. Also, compare to BMW 3 series, it does give slightly better high speed composure and stability.

    One last thing I would like to add is that what ever Mercedes model you choose to buy, no cars out there can compare to the Mercedes sense of solidity. You will know what I mean if you have driven one.
