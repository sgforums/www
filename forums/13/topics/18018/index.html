    Originally posted by Ah^Huat:
    If you leave ur choke on, wat will happen is that it opens ur carb bigger to take in more air, more air flow = more petrol being pumped into carb and then into the block region.
    Sorry... correction...
    turning on the choke will actually reduce the air intake into the carb, thereby richening the fuel-air mixture (more fuel = rich mixture, more air = lean mixture).
    This helps in starting a cold engine and speed up the heating process. But when the engine has reached normal running temperature, rich mixture is no longer needed and the engine will stall if it doesn't get enuff air.
