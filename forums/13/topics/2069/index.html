    oh yah.. as for buying new, almost all shops give 5% as interest. it's "flat add-on" interest. don't tell the ah-pek at the bikeshop that, he'll look at you with a blur face.

    here's how it works:
    the OTR is the final price (assuming ethical, honest business procedures on the part of the shop).

    lemme explain what happens in a theoretical example. if you decide to pay only $1k, and the OTR is $6k, you're loaning $5k.

    for interest, it depends on how long you wanna take to pay back. if you wanna take 1 year, it's 1 x 5%, 2 years, 2 x 5% and so on.

    assume you wanna pay in 2 years:

    your monthly instalment would be
    [$5000 + $500 (10% interest)] divided by 24 months...

    so, the longer you take to pay back, you pay more interest.

    points to note:

    some shops demand a minimum downpayment, that is expressed as a percentage of the OTR price, like say 30% or 20% upfront. varies from shop to shop.

    some orh-tiam are dishonest. when you buy a second-hand bike, they'll tell you, first 2 years 6%, 3rd year 12%. which is effectively 8%. so be sure that they state their terms clearly.

    for second-hand bike from a private seller, pls refer to the post titled "super4 good or not?", i stated stuff quite clearly. "COI", as we know it, the sub-rider thing, works on trust. NOT safe unless it's your friend you can trust.
