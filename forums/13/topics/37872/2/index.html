    Riding on the bike, or driving a 4-wheeler car or light truck, I will always study the road in front and behind.

    I will anticipate the situation in front, by looking at the situation 4 cars ahead - when possible, or at least 2 cars ahead. I will anticipate what is happening to that driver first, and will determine how he will react that will affect the other cars in front of me, so that I will take EARLY PRECAUTION.

    I will look at the immediate vehicles behind me - to see if the guy behind me is breathing down my neck, persistently getting too close to my tail for comfort. I will check that this idiot will not get too close to me in any e-situation that will endanger my property (bike, car or truck). When on a bike, I will try to speed up and overtake the other vehicles in front; or when driving a 4-wheeler, I will move out of the way to let that guy pass. If situation is heavy traffic, then I will press on my brake or turn on the hazzard light to warn that idiot off my back, and keep his distance.

    Many situation can always be hypothetically dreamed up as possible worse case scenarios for riders or drivers.

    But the word is ANTICIPATE - by reading the road situation AHEAD of you (2 or 3 or 4 vehicles ahead), and be alert to the guy BEHIND you. Watch the behavior or driving style of the vehicles in front and behind you, and prepare your own riding habit in response.

    So far, it has serve me well.
