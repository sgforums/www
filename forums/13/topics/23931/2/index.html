    anyway the pgm4 is really an old bike.. and old technology... totally for enthusiasts.. unless u can get hold of the honda rs version.. i think its still a wiser idea to get a four stroke 400cc to prepare for the bigger class 4 strokers...

    pgm4 is really cool, rs250 is really fast, but after a year, u will think the pgm4 is starting to look old and bulky and the rs250 will feel slaggy and no power... then thats when u start looking for greener pastures like the 1000cc class or 600cc class...

    a highly mod rs250 has 70bhp? and to get more out of it u gotta spend big money
    the pgm4 has 50+ bhp? and to get more u gotta sell your house, make ur galfren work as a karaoke gal and maybe weekdays u urself work at desker road... if u get what i mean...
    whereas why not just get a gsxr600 or r6 or f4i and u instantly get 90bhp stock!
    and if u are hungry for more... go get the bigger stuff like gsxr1000 or r1 or 954 and u get 135bhp... 144bhp for the gixxer1000 so the point is.. if the year was 1994 the pgm4 or the rs250 would kick ass... but its 2002 a good 8 years already, and the kr150 should be able to kick the asses of the 250s if u spend just a bit of money

    for me i just slept thru my class 2a phase and waited for class2 then got my weapon of choice... saved lots of money in the process.. think about it!

  • MS
    21 Jul 02, 19:48
    250 and 600 are mainly made for track. I personally rode a RS250 and GSXR600 on track. I must say a RS250 has superb handling and definately suited for 'slower tracks' (Tracks with more tight corners). I managed to hit 1'55" on the RS250 stock while GSXR600 only 2'00". The main reason is due to engine brake and power. But I must say both are fantastic bikes and I enjoy the aggressive handling.

    Of course those pple who prefer the more straight up posture (NSR250 and R6) may find the GSXR600 and RS250 a chore to handle.

    Mainly depends on the rider preference. No such thing as the better bike.
