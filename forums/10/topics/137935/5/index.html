    HDB should be enjoying a huge economy of scale in the HDB flat building program, yet the HDB flats are sold at a price that is far higher then a similar size unit of private homes built in Malaysia - with land prices based on commercial values at no subsidised costings, nor the building program having the economies of scale similar to HDB; (and without considering the difference in the exchange rates) .

    Someone will argue that land is plentiful and cheap in Malaysia, however, HDB flats are build on land that have been taken over at a fraction of the open market valuation of land in Singapore.

    The value of land sold in Singapore is generally influenced by the Government, through URA's periodical release of land parcels for sale to the public and private sectors.

    Land prices have reached astronomical proportions, to the extent that for the prices to climb down to more realistic values, it will undermine the bank loans of previous mortgages, and will affect the general accounting valuations of all assets of individuals and corporations in Singapore.
