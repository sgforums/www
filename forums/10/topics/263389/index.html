    Dr. Vivian Balakrishnan was held up as one of the promising younger ministers or new blood and was appointed by ex-PM Goh Chok Tong to be in charge of remaking Singapore.

    During the Annual Conference on Remaking of Singapore in 2002 at Suntec, I as one of the audience present raised the question : "Minister, the major issue of great concern to citizens at this point is the rising costs of living. What do you intend to do in the remaking of Singapore to address this problem?"

    His reply: "Costs have many components. There are the fixed cost and variable cost. The government has no control over variable cost like imported goods and services."

    Instead, in one of his public speeches, he retorted : "Do you have a solution to rising cost of living?"

    So being one of our promising younger ministers and being in charge of remaking of Singapore, he was without doubt given the opportunity to do something to reverse or modify some past policies which caused rising costs of living in the face of dropping wages and structural unemployments e.g. double charging and profiteering by HDB and many GLCs on lands and assets which were already paid by citizens through all sorts of creative accounting.

    He simply avoided the issue and denied problems by asking people instead to give him solutions.

    Has he any record to show that he has succeeded in remaking Singapore or the economy to date?

    Now being the minister in charge of community development is he prepared to take up issues brought up by the youths of today or is he playing safe to safeguard his own career.

    If he is the minister in charge of community development surely when PM Lee decided to donate his salary increment to charity after feeling remorse about the uproar created by the public over the ministers' million-dollar pay again, he should have demonstrated his spirit of community bonding by donating his undeserved salary increment to charity as well.

    What kind of ministers do we have today, really?
