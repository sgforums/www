A practical solution to our low-wage workers' problem.

  • robertteh
    13 Dec 07, 14:06
    Many ministers have tried to look into solutions to our low-wage workers' problems.

    From workfare to welfare to the latest financial aids of granting additional S$45,000 to be distributed to the poor via the political agency of CCC it looks like there is no practical solution in sight.

    The low-wage workers include cleaners, sale personnel, security guards, construction workers, whose salaries are low - less than $800.00 pm.

    This group of workers could not survive as what they earn are not enough to pay for transportation, utility and medical plus three meals.

    On the other hand the ministers, senior civil servants, CEO/GM's, of GLCs were constantly given increments and upgrading and today many of the employees in these categories were not being paid exceeding 15-150 times of wage of low-wage workers due to policy (to retain talents) and not due to actual market forces.

    So it is quite fair and practical for companies instead of squeezing the low-wage workers by passing some of the excessive current or future increments of these fortunate ministers, senior civil servants, CEOs/GM/directors of GLCs to such low-wage workers. Many of our ministers and senior civil servants and CEOs/GMs/directors of GLCs were being over-paid.

    In any recession instead of talking about cutting the wages of low-wage workers the government should re-distribute the excessive increments and bonuses to the high-wage workers to the low-wage categories so as to make the multiple less skewed. The real and practical solution should be to cut back the over-generous salaries of senior management instead of the low-wage workers.

    To build a compassionate and caring and competitive society it is time to adopt such a policy and practice to return to competitiveness again not by constant tax and fee increases. Otherwise the rich will only become richer and the poor poorer.

    Let the new post-65 MPs and younger ministers accept this as a challenge to their talents and leadership.
