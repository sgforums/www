    Mahathir blames 'parsimonious' S'poreans for rows
    PUTRAJAYA - Malaysian Prime Minister Mahathir Mohamad blames a 'parsimonious' attitude by Singapore for the numerous disputes that have rocked bilateral relations in the past several years.

    'We have tried our best to downplay the differences, but the fact remains that there are things that are done by Singapore which are detrimental to our interests,' he said yesterday.

    'It is ridiculous...how can you buy anything at the same price as in 1927?' he said, referring to the price of water supplied to Singapore which Malaysia wants to raise from three sen to RM6.25 (S$2.87) per 1,000 gallons.

    'They buy at three sen and they sell to their own people at very high prices, making huge profits for themselves, and they sell to ships at S$20.'

    Singapore says it sells only a small quantity of water to ships - about a million gallons per day - which is less than 1 per cent of its total water demand.

    It also says water sold to ships does attract high tariffs but this is to discourage this kind of demand.

    Dr Mahathir recited a litany of other complaints against Singapore, including the Pedra Branca issue and quarrels over pension funds for Malaysians working in Singapore.

    'We have nothing against Singaporeans, they are actually Malaysians and many of them have got their parents here - even the leaders have got their parents here,' he said.

    'But they behave in a way that is quite unfriendly, they are very parsimonious.

    'Even if they raised (water payments) to S$3, it's not going to leave a dent in their finances. They have huge reserves of US$80 billion but they quibble over three sen and it makes it difficult for us to carry out our very friendly kind of relationship with them.'
