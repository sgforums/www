    Simple explanation - South Korea has embraced Democracy and liberalize their political system, dismantling any possibilities of political control by any one political party over the civil service, security forces, government agencies, and government linked companies.

    The South Korean Government is not afraid of PRIVATE WEALTH being a political threat to their government or to any political parties.

    In South Korea, the Government encourages PRIVATE CAPITAL to take the lead in strategic industries - unlike the pseudo Singapore version of privatisation of strategic services that is largely government funded and government appointed management of the newly formed pseudo-privately registered businesses.

    Free market is not merely a lip service in South Korea but truly embraced, and resulting in South Korea being the leading Asian country with the widest digital coverage provided by Private Enterprises, serving the highest number of users in broad band services at the lowest costs.

    This has resulted in a large number from a generation of tech-savvy youngsters connected to a huge world wide web of information and technology, and advancing their education processes at a higher level than any other Asian countries.

    This highly disciplined and enthusiastic tech-savvy generation feeds into the South Korean high tech industries and manufacturing support services, and resulting in many innovative products that are world beaters.

    South Korea is leading in the large vessel shipbuilding, innovative high tech product research and development, leading also in nearly all highly competitive heavy, medium and consumer industries comparable to the best from the East and the West.

    This transformation came about as a result of the sudden death of a tight-fisted martial rule by the late President Park Chung Hee.

    His sudden demise by assassination from the head of his security service, shook the foundation of the government and South Korean society.

    The sudden and violent death of this strong and omnipotent ruler, resulted in South Korea having an opportunity to revamp their political system and structure, after decades of restrictive controls that prevented any display of initiatives and enterprise.

    Singapore has seen - and continue to see - the monopolistic domination of the POWER OF ONE, which fear Singaporeans being influenced by ideas that will place Singaporeans beyond the influence and control by the POWER OF ONE.

    Supreme control is the keyword for the pervasive hand of the Singapore Government in all fields and perspective of Singapore events and experiences.

    This has extended to the point where any new product that show great potential will result in some government agencies attempting to usurp the representation rights to the ' sunrise' product ' - by a carefully managed effort to either challenge the rights to the franchise, or to make an attempt in reverse-engineering to find a new approach to redesigning the patented product.

    Yet the common Asian trait exist from the influence of Confuscian philosophy in the respect towards authority, and the necessity for society to have defined law and order.

    This resulted in the Korean rule under a strong man for many decades, as well as the present experience in Singapore.

    Korea had the strong rule of the late President Park Chung Hee. while Singapore had over 45 years of LKY - as a PM, then as SM, and currently as MM.

    With the assassination of the Korean Park Chung Hee, it allowed Korea to blossom to its full potential; while Singapore is still floundering under the insistent influence from the Rule by the POWER OF ONE.

    Chaebols formed by the Korean Government ? You may have got some wrong information on this topic, as the Korean Chaebols are mainly Private Family owned Companies.

    These Private Korean Companies were encourage to grow aggressively by Park Chung Hee, who directed his Government to work closely with these Private Korean Companies so as to compete globally.

    This approach was modelled after the large Japanese businesses that are completely owned and operated by Private Japanese Families. These Zaibatsus are involved in manufacturing, trading, banking, and with their activities covering all marketing levels and axis.

    While the large Korean population provide a good domestic base for her manufacturing industries, the output capacities are all geared for export to earn foreign exchange.

    The economy of Korea during the rule of Park Chung Hee was much like a Command Economy, similar to the Communist model - (Park was a Socialist before he came into prominence in the Military); and ironically, the economy only took off after the assassination of Park Chung Hee by his trusted Head of the Korean Intelligence.

    His demise allowed a fresh beginning for South Korea, and resulted in liberalisation of its political, social, cultural and economic development.
