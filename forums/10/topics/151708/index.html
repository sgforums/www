Outsourcing of medical services

  • oxford mushroom
    8 Sep 05, 15:46
    "A radiology group in Bangalore, India, has promised it can study X-rays and send a diagnosis within 30 minutes, Health Minister Khaw Boon Wan said yesterday. And it can do it cheaper too.

    Mr Khaw said he saw no reason why Singapore should not pass on routine radiology work to cheaper places and concentrate on high-end work instead."

    (Straits Times 8 Sep: http://straitstimes.asia1.com.sg/sub/singapore/story/0,5562,339446,00.html?)

    Is this the way forward? Outsourcing diagnostics can certainly be cheaper and achieve better turnaround time. However, whilst labs in Singapore are accreditated by international bodies (Singapore was blowing its trumpet just few days ago about acquiring College of American Pathologists accreditation), the same cannot be said of low-cost diagnostic centres in India.

    The Minister's idea is for the X-rays (and probably other lab tests in future) of Singaporeans who want a cheap service to be outsourced to India. Paying and foreign patients who are willing to pay for higher quality will have their tests done in Singapore at a higher price. Subsidized patients with difficult conditions will continue to be handled locally at higher cost, but with time more of these can be outsourced as the quality of Indian labs rapidly improve.

    From the experience of others who have gone down this route, one implication is that hospitals will not want to train younger radiologists who do the bulk of the simpler cases. In any case, the easy cases have all gone to India anyway, so hospitals will just hire qualified radiologists to handle the complex cases. Secondly, in time, India and China will have so many cases that they will train increasingly more radiologists whilst we will lose them, as increasingly complex cases can be outsourced. Eventually, all of diagnostics will be outsouced (it has happened in the US).

    The writing is on the wall: if you are a young doctor considering specialisation, there is no future in diagnostic radiology unless you are prepared to accept a lower pay or work in India, where the cost of living is lower. This will eventually happen to other disciplines that can be easily outsourced, such as telepathology for diagnostic pathology services.
