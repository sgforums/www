    I left Singapore in 1994, well before foreign talents became a debated issue. However, if I was to have left more recently, it would be the pull factor of Australia rather than the push factor from Singapore that would have driven my decision.

    Still, let's say for example that you have a point and that FTs are a cause of migration out of Singapore. That point in itself defeats the "second-class citizen" argument against migration, does it not? The first-class Singaporean citizen sees the second-class foreign talent coming in, making good money and having a nice life. Why, then should the Singaporean not want to take a stab at being a comfortably and happy second-class citizen somewhere else?

    Over more than half a century, Australia has received waves of immigrants, starting with the Greeks and Italians, then the Vietnamese, then the Southeast Asians, Eastern Europeans and Africans. Over that period there have been a number of lean periods, none of which resulted in any backlash towards the immigrant population.

    I'm sure at this point somebody's going to mention the Cronulla riots as an instance of racial disharmony in Australia, but that had nothing at all to do with the foreign talents issue. The impetus there was that a mob of Lebanese youths had finally worn out their welcome at Cronulla by beating up a lifeguard and harrassing women at the beach for years - even Lebanese women have outright said that these youths were offensive.
