    Rolls Royce to set up aircraft engine assembly facility in S'pore
    By Satish Cheney, Channel NewsAsia | Posted: 20 November 2007 2125 hrs

    SINGAPORE : Rolls Royce will be setting up Asia's first ever facility to manufacture engines for large commercial aircrafts in Singapore.

    To be located at the Seletar Aerospace Park, the new plant will cost S$320 million.

    Rolls Royce wants to establish a 'factory of the future' when it comes to aerospace engines. It is confident of running one at the Seletar Aerospace Park.

    Rolls Royce is not new to Singapore's aerospace industry. Since 1995, Singapore Airlines has been a major customer of its engines.

    Other customers include SilkAir and the budget carriers operating out of Singapore.

    Right now, Rolls Royce accounts for more than 10 percent of Singapore's aerospace output.

    Rolls Royce is expecting to hire at least 330 staff here to work on engines for the Boeing 787 and Airbus A350. Currently, it has about 1,000 employees in Singapore.

    The company said that even though other Asian countries may have lower capital costs, Singapore is better poised for the aerospace sector in terms of skilled labour and infrastructure.

    Jonathan Asherson, Regional Director, South East Asia, Rolls Royce International, said: "Another criteria was the US dollars, in the mitigation against the exchange rate. And also being close to our customers, and Asia is a hugely growing part of our business."

    The aerospace industry in Asia is booming, with the number of aircraft in the region set to double by 2015.

    Singapore is keen to tap into the growth, and the Economic Development Board said it will be announcing more investment deals for the Seletar Aerospace Park over the next few months.

    Manohar Khiatani, Assistant MD, Industry Development, Economic Development Board, said: "There'll be opportunities to attract engine component manufacturers who supply Rolls Royce... systems suppliers who provide systems for aircraft engines, as well as our local supporting industries eventually, for the component activities."

    The new Rolls Royce plant is expected to be up and running by the end of 2009.

    Asia accounts for about 27 percent of the revenue at Rolls Royce.

    Meanwhile, Rolls Royce also announced another engine facility for corporate aircrafts in the Commonwealth of Virginia, USA. - CNA/ms

    ExxonMobil breaks ground in Singapore for largest petrochemical complex
    Nov 5, 2007

    SINGAPORE (AFP) Â— US energy giant ExxonMobil on Tuesday began building the world's largest petrochemical complex in Singapore, which is expected to be ready by 2011.

    The petrochemical complex, which will be ExxonMobil's second plant in the city-state, would be capable of producing one million tons of ethylene and 1.3 million tons of polyethylene annually.

    It is also capable of producing 450,000 tons of polypropylene and 300,000 tons of specialty elastomers, the company said.

    ExxonMobil executives declined to say how much was invested in the petrochemical plant, but industry experts estimate that a project of such size could require investments of 2.0 billion US dollars.

    Regional managing director Kwa Chong Seng said the new complex "will strengthen ExxonMobil's supply capabilities to serve the growing needs for petrochemical products in Asia and globally."

    "The new Singapore project will employ ExxonMobil's latest proprietary technologies, and will have the capability to process a broad range of feedstocks and convert them into higher value products," he said at the groundbreaking ceremony.

    ExxonMobil's new petrochemical complex will be located on Jurong Island, which houses Singapore's flourishing multi-billion dollar chemicals industry.

    The US energy firm's decision to build the complex in Singapore is a further boost to the island-nation's position as a regional chemical industrial base for the world's leading players.

    Output from the chemicals industry is now worth 74 billion Singapore dollars (51 billion US), Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong said.

    National Oxygen to invest at least S$40m in Singapore, region
    By Rachel Kelly, Channel NewsAsia | Posted: 20 November 2007 2009 hrs

    SINGAPORE : Industrial gas manufacturer National Oxygen (NOX) expects to invest at least S$40 million to expand in Singapore and the region.

    It revealed this on Tuesday, as it marked the completion of its third air separation facility on Jurong Island.

    NOX is the Singapore unit of a Japanese firm, and has been operating here for the past 25 years. It is seeing steady growth in its business.

    The company is one of the leading industrial gas manufacturers in the region, specialising in production of oxygen, nitrogen and argon.

    However, it is keen to expand. It plans to plough more capital into investments and increase manpower.

    Shigeto Umatani, Managing Director, National Oxygen, said: "We are planning to invest not only in Singapore, but in Malaysia Indonesia and Batam.

    "We are going to start with a new refilling station for downstream business in Batam, Indonesia, and we are planning to set up a new on site gas plant in Malaysia. We are also going to set up a new carbon dioxide plant in Malaysia. These projects will be completed by the end of next year."

    NOX expects to spend a total of S$40 million on these projects. In Singapore alone, it has so far invested a total of S$175 million.

    It booked a revenue of S$87 million last year, and this is expected to grow, as the third plant in Jurong kicks into full gear.

    NOX said gas manufacturing will stay on a uptrend, as the economy grows.

    Mr Umatani said: "I think for the past three or four years, Singapore's economy has been expanding very rapidly - between, I guess, 6-8 percent per year.

    "This year's economic growth is over 8 percent and I think, I'm sure, this growth will stay for the next couple of years, and in that sense, we believe that there is strong growth in this business (gas manufacturing) for the next few years at least."

    With the numerous projects NOX has in the Singapore industry, the company is hoping to attain a one third total of market share in the budding sector. - CNA/ms

    Neste to build US$814m S'pore biofuel plant
    November 30, 2007, 3.20 pm (Singapore time)

    HELSINKI - Finnish Neste Oil said on Friday it will build the largest biodiesel plant in the world in Singapore, investing 550 million euros (US$814.4 million).

    'The plant will have a design capacity of 800,000 tonnes each year, making it the largest facility producing diesel fuel from renewable feedstocks anywhere,' Neste said in a statement.

    The plant will use mostly palm oil as its raw material, but it can use also other feedstock.

    Neste started its first biodiesel plant, with an annual capacity of 170,000 tonnes, earlier this year in Finland and its second NExBTL biodiesel unit is scheduled to start operations in 2009.

    The company said construction of the Singapore plant will begin in the first half of 2008, and the facility is scheduled to be completed by the end of 2010. -- REUTERS
