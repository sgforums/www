    22 Apr 06, 18:29
    ST Forum On-line

    April 22, 2006
    Criticisms of young participants at MM Lee's dialogue are unfair. Don't we want forward-thinking, inquisitive minds?

    I am shocked at the way many readers reacted towards the forum "Why my vote matters" featuring a panel of young Singaporeans and Minister Mentor Lee Kuan Yew.

    Criticisms of young participants at MM Lee's dialogue are unfair. Don't we want forward-thinking, inquisitive minds?

    The harsh criticisms levelled at the ten young participants have gone out of hand. Yes, perhaps they seemed somewhat overly-enthusiastic in the manner in which they questioned MM Lee, but I thought this is what the government wants of the young generation of Singaporeans - to be forward-thinking and inquisitive minds.

    At school, we are taught to speak up in class, to constantly ask intelligent questions and not just blindly follow what we are being taught. If this is what the Ministry of Education wants to inculcate in the future leaders of Singapore, why dampen the challenging spirits of the young participants of the forum?

    The way several fuming readers relentlessly knocked at the young participants made me feel rather obfuscated. It is ironic how the older generation of Singaporeans constantly lament that youths today are ignoramuses when it comes to politics, yet they become incensed when we show our utmost concern on the political issues of our country.

    Even if some people feel that the young Singaporeans were disrespectful to MM Lee in the forum, I believe that everyone has his own views. Perhaps we should all try to respect each other's opinions.

    Maybe Singapore isn't ready for such a provocative forum, but I think that the ten participants have performed well with much courage, and none of them should be sorry for anything they have said in the forum.

    Ms Sim Shin Wei
