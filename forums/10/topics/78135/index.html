  • StMichael
    1 Jun 04, 20:47
    Are teachers masochists? I suppose you are talking about the teachers with a passion for teaching? I guess those of us who takes upon teaching as a calling sees things differently.

    I have been teaching in Sunday School for many years, and in a primary school for 5 over years (some of them as Head Pastoral Care). Sometimes it gets frustrating when pupils just don't see the importance of what they are doing. Sometimes it's hardbreaking when you find them not appreciating your efforts. Many times you have to mark till late in the night. And if anything goes wrong, the parents are quick to believe their 'perfect' son and blame the teacher.

    But the rewards are great when pupils come to you to tell you that you've made a difference in their lives...years after they left you. It is wonderful having the parent greet you at the hawker centre and insisting that you not pay for the food served (I never went back to that hawker centre again!!!). It is wonderful to see your table piled up with cards and crafts made by the children. It is wonderful to listen to the laughters of the class during the comedy presentation for English class.

    Masochistic? Perhaps only for certain teachers to whom teaching is their ENTIRE life. They are the crazy teachers who spend hours preparing small little crafts for each child for a 1/2 hour lesson. Or mark through 3 compositions a week. Sometimes I really wonder if there's a reason why these teachers are stil 'Miss' at their age. Now these are masochistic!!!

    It's wonderful to be teaching again, after a break of a year!!!
