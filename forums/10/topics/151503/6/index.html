    Gay army couples get married quarters
    Ben Townley, Gay.com UK

    The Ministry of Defence has confirmed lesbian and gay couples serving in the armed forces will be given married quarters after the introduction of civil partnerships later this year.

    In a ruling set to delight same-sex couples within the armed forces, the MoD said it will allow those who take advantage of the new laws to have access to the quarters currently only available to married heterosexual couples.

    The move is the latest in a string of pro-gay changes, which began in 2000 when the government was forced to drop a ban on gay people.

    Since then, sections of the armed forces have targeted lesbians and gay men for recruitment.

    Most recently, soldiers took place in a Pride parade for the first time, using the Manchester Pride celebrations to encourage more members of the gay community to sign up.

    A spokesperson for the MoD told the press that the changes would see an extension of rights to same-sex couples.

    "We will be extending the same entitlements, allowances and benefits to same-sex couples who choose to register a civil partnership recognised in law that are granted to married couples," he said.

    The move is set to anger religious and conservative groups that are opposed to the Civil Partnership Act.

    They say the new laws, which offer legal recognition to lesbian and gay couples for the first time, are in opposition to marriage.

    Already, many are criticising the MoD for the changes, arguing that unmarried heterosexual couples should also have access to the benefits on offer.

    These claims have been slammed by gay campaigners who say the new legislation will be a vital step towards equality for same-sex couples.
