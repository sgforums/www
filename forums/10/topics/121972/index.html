    by the government. If the government stands by its decision in
    denying the request, it has to provide convincing answers to the
    main reason why it is rejected: why the huge gap in the cost of the
    upgrading with Chiam's estimate of $2 million vs HDB's $12 million.
    If however, in the interest of democracy, the government changes its
    mind and allows Mr Chiam to go ahead, and provided the residents vote
    for it, it will have a bigger problem on its hand if Mr Chiam
    succeeds in upgrading the lifts at just $2 million when it cost 6
    times that amount for HDB: have the government and affected HDB
    residents been suckers all this while?

    their funds prudently?

    Of course, if Mr Chiam fails in his attempt to limit the cost to $2
    million, he will then have to answer to his constituents and a
    possible outcome may see him losing his stronghold in the next
    General Election.
