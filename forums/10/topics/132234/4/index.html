    How in the world would you know? Given the lack of genuine transparency in these government organisations, as well as the fact that, as we know, Singaporeans are almost never told the truth about anything that reflects negatively on the government, it is nearly impossible for anyone to accurately gauge exactly how much of a contribution these organisations have made to Singapore.

    For example, there is really no reliable way to verify whether all those corporate tax breaks, cuts in CPF contribution rates and other incentives given to foreign MNCs have really translated into adequate benefits for average Singaporeans. Indeed, considering the stagnant employment and salary levels we're seeing, as well as the ongoing financial problems that many Singaporean families are facing, one might have good cause to suspect that all is not well.

    There is also no reliable way to confirm whether or not all our taxpayers' money is being properly managed by these government agencies. On the contrary, I believe many international commentators have remarked that many Singapore govt agencies have produced very poor returns on their investments to date.
