Is Tharman convinced by Chiam's words of scrapping the Streaming idea or snatching Chiam's credit?

  • BillyBong
    6 Oct 06, 08:46
    Unfortunately, everyone has been caught up in a 'rat race' of sorts.

    Even the school children have been unwittingly sucked into this blackhole.

    Streaming has its benefits and it detractors. To some extent, i'm all for streaming, as it provides the foundation for competition. And it is through competition that everyone learns to improve their capabilities.

    However, we tend to forget that streaming breeds elitism, and because MOE has failed to rein in the problem, it persists among the children and their parents. Students who do not perform as well based on paper grades tend to lose focus and get easily demoralised.

    The whole system then degrades into a vicious cycle: the only way to get out is to be good at it; Thus students who cannot meet the perceived high standards gets marginalised into normal / technical streams later on. The fact that EM3 was abolished served notice to this fact, but it has not changed the incorrect mindset of people.

    We have been trained to develop an 'all-rounded' education, but have completely forgotten how to be 'well-rounded'. We mimic other systems and people who are successful, without realising that it has become an empty goal.

    The true purpose of streaming has therefore become completely warped without realization.
