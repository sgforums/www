    Well... I'm sure that many of you have heard of George Bush's speech on late morning Wednesday (Singapore time). His speech touched on economics, the War in Iraq, and security issues.

    I won't need to repeat what the Straits Times have mentioned (I'm assuming that most of you respect ST enough to read it. I hardly do.) like the economics and the War in Iraq... but I have noticed that ST missed out a particular and very important point in Bush's speech.

    I actually taped down the speech (yes, I'm a very bored person) from 10.30am onwards while I was in school in the meantime and I remember very clearly that Bush was at one point trying to promote his Patriot Act.

    What is the Patriot Act? It is just a bill filled heavily with legal language that just says that in times of great need, terrorist suspects will have all of their rights revoked immediately (nudge nudge, wink wink) except for their right to own firearms (which is very VERY strange indeed).

    What I want to say is that by ommiting the Patriot Act, ST is actually trying to hide the fact that we have such a law and to avoid any sort of uproar from the locals who think that the Patriot Act is wrong, think about our ISA and start to protest (which is actually quite unrealistic because the bulk of our population have absolutely no guts at all).

    Is this the sort of media that we want to live with? It is common knowledge that our newspapers are all state-run "running dogs" of the authorities and there's virtually nothing we can do about it.

    Some people will have the *OUT OF POINT* sign popping in your head when you read this threat, and perhaps even more will ask "What's your point?" before clicking on the 'Back' button on their browsers. But, I guess what I'm trying to do is to leave a thought in your mind.

    Just a thought.
