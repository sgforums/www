    When a management audit is conducted of transactions in the private-sector companies such a listed company or management corporation, the normal practice is to examine accountable and transparent work processes plus calling of quotations, calling of tenders for projects, shortlisting procedures, jointly conducted interviews and selection of staff and decisions and deliberations by the board or committee appointed to carry out and oversee such functions.

    In the civil service, there is supposedly a Public Service Commission to look after fair and independent of civil servants following such procedure.

    Very often we hear of remarks in public speeches that so and so civil servants were selected by the MM Lee or the PM and ministers.

    I just wonder if the PM has followed such independent panel's accountable and transparent financial and management audited recommendations in selecting senior civil servants e.g. chief of police, army, airforce, navy, chief justice, senior judges, permanent secretaries etc to safeguard against likelihood of nepotism.

    Has citizens the right to ask for evidences to show proper procedures are being followed eg. tenders/advertisements, deliberations and reports of selection committee etc. Under what circumstances will the government conduct a management audit to verify such practices are being followed with report of Management Audit printed for circulation.

    The government should follow such procedures to prevent corruption, nepotism through setting of personal example. It is not a matter of whether MM, SM or PM has the power to appoint or recommend such offices.
