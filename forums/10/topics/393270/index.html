    LEGISLATION was introduced in Parliament yesterday to make a number of major changes to the political system. If all goes according to plan, the revisions should become law by next month.

    The legislative move comes some 10 months after Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong announced his plan to provide for more diverse views in Parliament.

    The changes pave the way for greater opposition representation in Parliament, via an increase in the allowable number of Non-Constituency Members of Parliament (NCMPs) and the entrenchment of the Nominated MP (NMP) scheme.

    Once implemented, non-ruling party MPs would fill at least 18 seats, or one-fifth of the House.

    Other key changes include a cooling-off day to ban election campaigning on the eve of Polling Day, and allowing political websites to engage in online election advertising.

    To effect these changes, amendments to the Constitution, the Parliamentary Elections Act (PEA) and the Presidential Elections Act were tabled - by Deputy Prime Minister Wong Kan Seng in the first case, and Law Minister K.Shanmugam in the second and third.

    Read the full story in Friday's edition of The Straits Times.
