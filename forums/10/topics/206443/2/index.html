    I find the xenophobic characteristic displayed by some of my fellow Singaporeans worrying. They speak as though living in prosperity in Singapore is a right, not something that we need to fight for. The same characteristic that I found among groups of youths in many well to do western countries. I cannot help but remembered how hard our forefathers worked when they first migrated to Singapore from their hometown, be it in China, Malaysia or India. And today, we the descendants of those who had fought so hard to prosper, are now demanding our government to keep out others with the same spirit of our forefathers so that we can have things easy!?!? What is the Ethos of us Singaporeans? Kiasuism? Was our state not built with sweat, blood and courage of our forefathers such as ricksaw pullers, coolies, construction workers. And instead of continuing the spirits that our forefathers displayed, we, who grew up in relative comfort compared to the majority of the rest of the world, we act as if we have inherited the right to be rich and prosper without a fight. Sure, jobs are not as readily available as they were during the 80s, but we won't starve to death as long as we are willing to work. If we want to raise to the top, we shouldn't demand people to give it to us, we should fight for it with our true ability! The state had already provided us with great subsidies in terms of education, and that is our greatest capital!
    When we talk about foreign talents, two pictures spring to mind, the "experts" picture and the "construction worker" picture. The experts possess the skills that Singapore lack of while the "construction workers" took the jobs that Singaporeans are refusing to do. Without these foreign people coming in, we will not have enough population to renew the labour force and economical growth will be hampered.
    In terms of culture, one must remembered that the greatest part of our culture is its diversity and its tolerance. Refusing "foreign element" from entering into our culture would be the greatest betrayal to our culture.The current racial mix of Chinese, Malay and Indian are incidental, they will not stay the same forever and rejection of new immigrants will only plant the seed for future racial disharmony.
    The NS may seem like unfairness to Singaporean males, but utility should override fairnes and there are measures such as extra pay that reduce the degree of such unfairness.
    Foreign influx may appear frightening to some, but we were once foreign to this island too.
