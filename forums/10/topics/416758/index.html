    Send Govt your Budget wish list

    By Cheryl Lim

     

    SINGAPOREANS and businesses are invited to tell the Government what they hope to see in the next Budget.

    There are also more channels through which they can offer their views on issues relating to public spending.

    Both general feedback and suggestions on specific aspects of public expenditure are welcome, the Ministry of Finance (MOF) said yesterday. MOF, together with Reach, launched the feedback exercise yesterday.

    The Budget 2011 website - www.singaporebudget.gov.sg - features a feedback section.

    Other channels open this year include Facebook, Twitter and discussion boards on Reach's Budget microsite.

    More traditional channels include e-mail, reach@reach. gov.sg, and toll-free hotline 1800-3535555. Suggestions and views are also accepted by post.

    At the People's Action Party's biennial conference last Sunday, Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong had hinted at the possibility of goodies in the Budget, as in previous years of high growth.

    'When the Budget comes, with some luck, we should have some good news,' he had said.

    As part of the outreach activities, there will be an online Budget quiz, plus a Budget Challenge with a parliamentary-style debate to increase awareness of the Budget process and public finance.

    The quiz will be divided into two sections, each comprising questions on Singapore's fiscal, economic, tax and social policies.

    No limit is placed on the number of attempts made and prizes will be awarded to the top scorers in each round of the quiz.

    The Budget Challenge gives junior college students and those in the last two years of their integrated programmes the chance to experience what it is like to be a Member of Parliament, with them being able to engage in 'cut-and-thrust' parliamentary-style debate.

    Each team will represent a ministry and propose policy initiatives to be debated in a parliamentary setting. Prizes will be awarded to the top three teams.
