    Example 1
    In 2006, if after rebates etc and your net taxable income is $60,000,
    the income tax you need to pay is $2,700 (8.75%). If you spend $800 a
    month on needed goods and services, the GST paid for 5% will be $480
    Total=$3,180 in Tax paid.

    In 2007, if after rebates etc and your net taxable income is $60,000,
    the income tax you need to pay is $2,600 (8.50%). If you spend $800 a
    month on needed goods and services, the GST paid for 7% will be $672
    Total=$3,272 in Tax paid.

    From $3,180 to $3,272, the effective increase is 2.89%.

    Example 2
    In 2006, if after rebates etc and your net taxable income is $400,000,
    the income tax you need to pay is $61,650 (21%). If you spend $4,000 a
    month on needed goods and services, the GST paid for 5% will be $2,400
    Total=$64,050 in Tax paid.

    In 2007, if after rebates etc and your net taxable income is $400,000,
    the income tax you need to pay is $58,700 (20%). If you spend $4,000 a
    month on needed goods and services, the GST paid for 7% will be $3,360
    Total=$62,060 in Tax paid.

    From $64,050 to $62,060, the effective decrease is -3.11%.

    Example 3
    In 2006, if after rebates etc and your net taxable income is $25,000,
    the income tax you need to pay is $187.50 (3.75%). If you spend $800 a

    Total=$667.50 in Tax paid.

    the income tax you need to pay is $175 (3.5%). If you spend $800 a month

    Total=$847 in Tax paid.

    Here is my take on this GST issue. Please give me your thought about this.

    If we look at the history of taxes, be it corporate tax and individual tax rates, there has been a significant drop of taxes being charged. This can give you a better idea about it.

    Individual tax rates
    http://www.iras.gov.sg/ESVPortal/iit/iit-se-a1.1.15+tax+rates+for+resident+individuals.asp

    Corporate tax rates
    http://www.iras.gov.sg/ESVPortal/ct/ct_b.2.2_what+are+the+tax+rates.asp

    As you can see from the rates above, both tax rates has dropped significantly, with indivdual drop from the first $7500 at 2% to the first $20,000 at 0% and corporate tax drop from 40% to the current 20%.

    Now lets look into the system of taxation for individuals. For those who knows a little bit about tax would realise that it is a system to tax the rich more then the poor. In another words, the government according to this tax system wants to shorten the gap between the poor and the rich.

    Another minor example would be the government gave rebates on utilities for those who stay 3 room flat and below. Help the poor and let the rich pay for the utilities.

    With the GST introduced, the general impact will be minimal to the company as they can choose to register as a GST trader. Therefore, the government is very much trying to promote a better environment to for the company to grow and become competitive especially when the gov is saying that there might be a decrease with the corporate tax further. With this in mind, in an environmentally friendly for company would push the economy to greater heights and thus giving the people more job prospects and better financial backing in the long run. With this in place, the government must train and educate everyone to make sure everyone can reap profit from this in which they are currently doing. I shall not touch on the issue with inflation and such for now.

    Thus, the main impact on GST will definitely be to the consumers - Citizen of Singapore. This is very different from the direction that the government would like to foster - tax the rich and not the poor. This is because GST affects everyone! From the poor to the rich. I would agree that the GST will definitely affect the poor more than the rich in terms of percentage on the spendings of necesities. But if we were to take a step back and see, the rich definitely spends more than the poor and therefore, they will definitely pay more GST to the government.

    The main primary concern now is that the poor, with the rising price (due to inflation) and the increase of GST might just be another blow to them. They might even suffer as their financial strenght is already limited. Now, the government has promised to help them. Should they be able to help them effectively will be the primary factor to make this entire system works. For now, we could only suspect and question on how well are they able to perform in this area.

    In my conclusion, with the GST increase is to help the corporate world grow and people get more jobs (better pay?). As the poor will suffer the most, the government has promised to help them. The question is how well can they help especially when there is an inflation.
