    Originally posted by Chelzea:
    tourist can claim back gst
    On the contrary, tourist do pay GST.

    If a tourist buys something from a non-GST registered business,
    he would have paid some GST on the goods (even if the receipt don't
    state the GST tax). It's because these non-GST registered business
    purchase goods from GST registered suppliers, the suppliers would
    have already charge GST on the goods, then the GST on the goods
    is passed on to the consumer, in this case the tourist.

    The only relief is that the tourist doesn't pay the final value added tax
    charged by the non-GST registered business. (e.g. if non-GST business
    purchase product (cost $100 from GST registered business) for $105,
    sells it for $155, non-GST business can't charge GST for the $155,
    but tourist has already paid $5 of hidden GST.)
