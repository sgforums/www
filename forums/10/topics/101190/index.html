    If I understand the law correctly, what use to be the case is this - The wife can easily migrate to Singapore on the husband's merit. But the husband cannot migrate to Singapore on the wife's merit.

    This means, if you marry a Singapore husband, you probably get the citizenship regardless of anything else. But if you marry a Singapore wife, you still need to prove you are capable or rich, with similar comparison to another applicant who has no family ties here (I.e. zero points for having a family here).

    The law has since change to a point system where family ties in Singapore improves ones chance of migrating to Singapore. I do not know if there is still uneven bonus points for a local husband versus a local wife. My understanding is that this point system is not transparent.
