    PM Lee and all the ministers who spoke strongly about a bright future ahead have not answered the most important question asked by many Singaporeans - will there be a future if they keep sticking to their own views and brushing aside people's views as can be seen in so many debates and problems ?

    NKF is one. Nicol Highway's collapse and public's demand for accountability and transparency is another. GRC election law is yet another. Brushing aside younger people's views or perceptions of leaders' arrogance in one more. Temasek's losses of citizens' monies in Shin Corp (paper loss $1.4 billion now) and many overseas projects is another.

    If the government continues to run the country in the same self-centred manner how could it bring about progress and prosperity. People will be turned off by arrogance and narrow-minded leaders. On the other hand, changes where finally take place even after people's repeated pressures only took place after long delays resulting in much damage done e.g. elitist academic education streaming system. (no credit to government or Shamugaratnam because of this long delays)

    If there is no accountability and transparency in governing the most important areas and all the errors and mistakes are conveniently forgotten without making those in charge responsible, how are the people to agree with their confidence that there will be a great future ahead.

    The boss of Taiwan IT Giant Acer Mr. Stanley Shih revealed his secret of success to CNA Mr. Yang Ming in CNA interview with him this morning:-

    Stanley Shih as a young man believed to be successful, he would need:

    (1) To build an international Brand Name

    (2) To build new products needed by the world.

    These two ideas led Acer all the way to become a top IT MNC providing many jobs for Taiwan.

    Our leaders believed:

    (1) Meritocracy (A few elites are recruited to government to administer public services.

    (2) Legalistic wrangling for control of populations to keep laws and order.

    In this interview he disclosed that he depended on buidling up his team to review and take action to apply the above-mentioned two ideals. He was able to develop a team of staff who could carry on achieving these two ideals and take actions to overcome many practical problems and challenges. He did not pass the ownership of Acer to his own son but to his team who was welll versed in such governing philosophy to build Acer's competitiveness.

    So where are the major differences between Stanley Shih and our leaders' governing philosophy?

    One is proactive aimed at building capabilities for the economy to meet outside competitions with real focus on knowledge application the other on controls to prevent chaos and dissidents from giving their views.

    Singapore could not upgrade its economy because it has lacked leadeship in mass empowerments and practical knowledge application as envisioned by government, people and industrial leaders in Taiwan.

    The above-mentioned merely confirmed one point that our future as spoken of by PM Lee is not dependent on past assumptions as these past assumptions might not work as well as pro-active pro-technology and pro-competition living system like that of Acer. If our leaders have adopted Acer two practical goals and consistently applied them today we would no doubt have built a team of Acer-type ministers who would provide more jobs and upgrade the economy for all.
