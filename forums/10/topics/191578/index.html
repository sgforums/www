Is it a concern that there are more Christians among the MPs

  • robertteh
    20 May 06, 01:33
    There may be more MPs who are Christians than other religions as a result of education or evolution of our society but that does not necessarily mean a bad thing or follow that Christians or Christianity are dominating the government.

    As long as the constitution or government's basic philosophy is multi-religious or secular, Singapore should at least be in stable hand.

    There is a need to differentiate values and religion in refering to religion or regilious group within the government. The value system among the Christians might be strong or outspoken but as long as such values are good for society, and coincide with all the good value system of the state, there is no reason to fear the upholding of those values whether coming from Christians or non-christians.

    It is for the government and society to mature to a higher level of social mores i.e. higher morality, fairness, equity, practising EQs and desirable social and cultural values. If religions can serve as complement to such social and cultural ballast of a country, they should be welcome to grow on their own strengths without inhibitions or discrimination against any one. Singapore can remain a multi-religious country with people of different beliefs living in harmony and mutual respect.

    If with improvements in education and opening up of society more people may turn to religions that offer higher values of living and higher morality standards. This trend is good for the people and should be a good part of governance.

    A government devoid of values or morality on the ground that it has to be totally secular may also be too detached and uncommitted to values and that may produce even greater danger.
