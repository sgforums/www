Lesson one - Do not make promises which cannot be delivered.

  • robertteh
    14 May 06, 21:13
    (as posted to FBU on 14.5.2006 in reply to forumer's parable about tree of integrity)

    Be sincere and honest in making promises,

    Do not make promises that cannot be delivered, (e.g. Swiss Standard of living)

    For whatever the reason if promises are not kept

    It will be worse to try to explain failure

    So lesson one for good government ministers

    Do not make promises that cannot be delivered,

    To try to explain promises that were not delivered,

    Will cause more pain like rubing salt into wound,

    Broken promises cause bad faith

    Which undermine the root of integrity tree,

    So learn the lesson and be more humble in correcting

    Past policies and assumptions that did not work

    Do not try to explain them away,

    Take ownership of problems caused by wrong policies,

    That is the mark of true leadership

    Otherwise the tree of integrity will be blown away by strong wind.

    Originally posted by king108:
    Fellow Robertteh..We got Liu Pei here meh?
    A warfare strategist like Zhu Ge Liang would shake his head at seeing many of our idling resources laying to waste. He would have advised a good plan to use them to upgrade Singapore economy to value-added technology-driven model.

    When ministers talked about economic restructuring, there was only behind the scene talking with no sign of any such strategic positioning to utilise our strengths to the fullest.

    There is no realizable plan, scheme, detailing and finalizing to launch such a program as what Dr. Goh Keng Swee or Wesemius would have done if they are still around.

    Many past announcements of business hub, IT hub, technology highway, international branding, remain fragmented and not put into strategic positioning as Stanley Shih has done for Acer.

    There has been too much attention given to concepts about leadership and education but not real strategic economic planning and consistent implementation. Even when IR is to be implemented, there will be lack of coordination and spin-off effects to benefit the whole economy if there is no clearly thought-out strategy to use our locational and human resources to the fullest.
