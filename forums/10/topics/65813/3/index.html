    PanAsia raises newsprint prices for Asia

    PUBLICATION paper supplier PanAsia Paper announced yesterday that newsprint prices to its Asian export markets will increase to US$50 (S$84.20) per tonne from US$40 currently.

    Fuelling this rise is strong demand in the region along with rising costs of production, it said. Projected economic growth in Asia is expected to result in greater advertising spending, newspaper pagination and newsprint consumption.

    PanAsia chief sales officer Hannes Skisaker said: 'The global supply and demand balance for newsprint is improving. Since the beginning of the third quarter of last year, we have seen an increase in demand for our products.'

    Similarly, cost factors are pushing prices upwards. Raw materials, such as energy and waste paper are becoming more expensive, while the decline of the US dollar has made transactions within Asia relatively dearer.

    'After a prolonged period of depressed pricing for newsprint products, we sincerely hope our customers understand our need to raise prices as overall business conditions improve,' said Mr Andre van Hattum, president and chief executive of PanAsia.

    PanAsia currently markets about 500,000 tonnes of newsprint and other products from North America, Europe and Oceania.

    The price rise will be effective for shipments made from April.
