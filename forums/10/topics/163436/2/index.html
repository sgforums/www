    Before anyone goes on an NTU-bashing spree, let's get some facts right.

    The Star (malaysian tabloid) stated that the AEON chair could cost as much as S$2200/-.

    Note the modal terms they use in 'could cost' and 'as much as'.

    The actual cost online costs US$599/- which works out to abt approx S$1000/- on a 1.67 conversion rate. And you can bet NTU will sniff out the best deal and ask for the best discount. See below for an example of the cost.

    http://www.bizrate.com/buy/products__cat_id--25010300,keyword--herman%2520miller%2520aeron%2520chair.html

    Ultimately, this is just another classic case of sensationalising news to suit the papers.

    There is an obvious difference of S$1200/- which means nothing to The Star since they conveniently stated a 'price range' carefully hidden their lines.

    Still, as a side note, i doubt the professors or students (whoever the chairs are for) will not fully appreciate the bio-design of the chair, making it a less than worthwhile investment.
