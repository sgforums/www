    Having stayed in AMK for a long time and shifting to Bukit Batok recently, I discover a major flaw in the ERP system. First of all, lets just say that the volume of cars is almost the same on both AYE and CTE (seriously... its almost the same). AYE do not experience the same kind of jam like on CTE, and here is why.

    The major contributing factor is the number of flyovers (upsloping roads) we have on CTE. Starting from SLE, we have (a) Lentor Flyover (b) AMK Ave 5 Flyover (c) AMK Ave 3 Flyover (d) AMK Ave 1 Flyover (e) Braddell Flyover (f) PIE Flyover. Travelling on the CTE in the morning, you will discover a bottleneck at the foot of all these flyovers, and it gets worse as we are nearing the CBD. But once, we pass the PIE Flyover, movement is smooth.

    On the other hand, there isn't any flyovers on AYE, therefore no bottlenecks.

    Coupled with the 2 ERPs which acts like a force field making cars slow down when approaching, the jam gets worst.

    All these flyovers can be likened to water dams, speed of water slows down when hitting a dam and will continue its same speed after clearing the dam.

    So the answer to the jam on CTE is not ERPs, but rather a major education on road users that they do not need to reduce their speed from 90kmh to 60kmh when approaching the apex of the slope!!!

    Just my 2 cents worth...

    It is these Speed Cameras that act like a valve that throttle the flow of the traffic, which also has been the cause of multiple car pile up during the bumper-to-bumper traffic.

    ndsef:

    It's not so much of the uphill and downhill layout of CTE that causes the jam. Even if it is, the costs for restructuring that segment of the expressway will be extremely complicated since that segment is pretty much like how the North South MRT tracks meets the East West tracks at City Hall and Raffles Place. Planning and work as well as the money to rebuild CTE would take MANY years and too many hard earned peanuts.

    But that's not my point.

    I would say the major contributing factor to the heavy traffic (in a jam.. u don't even move..) is really the space in between exits starting from CTE's Bukit Timah Road exit all the way to Braddel's exit. Not to mention, the traffic coming in from PIE which by the way is not just situated to close to the Braddel exit but also comprises of only ONE lane. And to top everything off, we throw in a Speed Camera (which i doubt serves any purpose since there isn't any space to go above 70 km/h during peak hour). Situating two exits so close to each other only causes a huge slow down in traffic. With 6 exits tightly packed in this stretch (Bukit Timah, Jln Bahagia, Upper Serangoon, PIE and Braddel).. the end result is a chain of stoppages. So the 3rd and 4th lanes on the CTE at that stretch will perpetually be full of people slowing down either to exit, to change to faster lanes or just because they're stuck.. then as you drive pass jalan bahagia exit up the final ramp before the speed cam, u start to see the cars infront of you break.. JUST because it is a subconcious mindset to do so when you see the speed cam even if you are travelling at only 60-70km/h (which is about the fastest you can go at those times) So one person breaks.. the rest of us will as well.. so there you go... the conclusion: Heavy and Slow traffic as you come out of the tunnel.

    Despite all that, CTE is still the preferred choice by most people staying anywhere between Serangoon all the way to Yishun.. the other routes up north from south already have pretty heavy traffic (Upper Serangoon road, Farrer -> Lornie -> Braddel -> Upper Thompson). Putting up a gantry may ease the strain on CTE but it's only going to transfer that amount of traffic onto the Braddel and the Upper Serangoon roads... And unecessary move in my opinion because the traffic on CTE may be slow moving.. but atleast its still moving.

    I suggest either we wait to see how the new "braddel express route" works out OR.. Do something about the Speed Cam (maybe switch it off during peak hours) OR build a parallel route that runs under CTE with only exits at Yio Chu Kang and beyond and at the Havelock area bypassing the whole entire stretch..

    thoughts?
