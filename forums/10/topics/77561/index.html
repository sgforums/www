  • SGglobalist
    28 Apr 04, 14:04
    I just had a conversation with a friend, who is 50 and trying to find a job.

    She said that it is really hard to find a job now in Singapore due to her age. And that people have been turning her down, even though she has had much experience in her field. Some have even gone so far as to ask for the age, and when she told them she was 50, they said she was 'too old'.

    I do not think that companies have the right to use age as an excuse to refuse employment or terminating an employee. There are people who are over 50 and have excellent health and can perform as well as a 30 year old.

    I think there should be laws to stop this age discrimination. An older person does not make him/her an inefficient worker. A lazy worker makes an inefficient worker! There are going to be many older people in Singapore in years to come, we could need the extra workers due to the poor birth rates. Furthermore, labeling the older people as disfunctioned, useless, and inefficient by discriminating aginst them based on their age has backlashes too.

    If society makes you feel old and tells you that you are not wanted and you have nothing else to contribute how would you feel? I think there could be a rise in physicological truma, and how about suicide, they might feel so unwanted by society that they could end up thinking about it. If credit card debts keep on rising, discriminating against the old will make it harder for them to pay back what they owe.

    I think there needs to be laws regulating how companies hire their employees. I am not asking to control every single move a company makes but to make sure that it is done fairly and appropriately. Maybe it could be like Canada, where no companies can ask for a person's age when interviewing them.

    So what do you all think?

    Do you think that there is age discrimination in Singapore?

    If there is, how should we stop this?
