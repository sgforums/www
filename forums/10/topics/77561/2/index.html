    Thailand hero, I think you are missing the point. The point is some people have the experience and the expertise but due to their age they are discriminated from that job. They are looked down by employers who want to pay less, make more profit, and not to have to worry about medical expenses etc.

    I agree with shutterbug that the government is not doing anything much to help those that already inhabit this land. But I think the government has hit the nail on the head by concentrating also on birth rates. However, I think there is a greater need to help those already here, so that they will not be left behind. We cannot always just think about the future and not care about the present.

    I know some people, like shutterbug that wants to leave ASAP, but others here have family, memories, and a bond with this country. So what should be done? Those that have they mind set on leaving do not care for this country anymore, maybe dealing with this discrimination might help stir some people to work harder for the better of society.

    I think it is wrong for the government to leave employment issues to the hands of employers, if what one reader said is correct, where there has already been an issue and the gov said that it is up to the companies. I think there is a limit on how much any government should leave companies to their own devices, government control and intervention is always needed. So what can we do? dealing with this is crucial to our future as singaporeans, and people who work in Singapore.

    What should we do? lets deliberate further.
