    ok, i'd explain in a different way and hopefully you can understand this time ...

    suppose there is no GST at all:

    1) cost of goods will be $100
    2) selling price of goods will be price with GST ($120) less GST amount ($5.71) = $114.29
    3) profit will be $114.29 - $100 = $14.29

    GST is a tax on the consumer and should not in any way eat into your profit margin of $14.29.

    let's see what happens when we have GST:

    1) cost of goods = $105
    2) selling price = $120
    3) profit = $120 - $105 = $15.

    profit when there is no GST = $14.29
    profit when there is GST = $15

    so you see, when there is GST, you effectively earn an extra $0.71 for nothing! that cannot possibly be right and the reason is because the extra $0.71 is meant for the govt in the form of GST. so returning $0.71 back to govt is indeed correct ... not $5.71
