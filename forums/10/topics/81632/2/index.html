    Let's bring this discussion to a close so that we don't keep going round and round the mulberry bush.

    Firstly, it's irrational to link drugs and weapons. Thought this point is obvious, but some people just don't get it. Supporting a more liberal drug policy does not mean supporting a more liberal policy towards arms. There is nothing illogical or inconsistent about wanting a more liberal policy towards drugs and continuing to support our strict stance against weapons possession.

    Next, there is the common argument that substance abuse is bad because not only does it harm the user, it also affects his family and society at large. It is therefore justifiable to imprison substance abusers.

    The above argument is fallacious because many actions of humans affect others adversely but we do not imprison these people for such actions.

    As for the justification that substance abusers should be imprisoned because drug taking hurts the abusers, my contention is that the cure is worse than the disease because of the very real repercussions of imprisonment. At the bottomline, we should respect the right of the individual to live his life so long as he does not directly harm others.

    Finally some people think it is a big deal that drug users should be punished because they have broken the law by possessing drugs and you can't consume drugs without first possessing them. This way of thinking overlooks the fact that we have consciously chosen to criminalise drug possession and consumption. But is it rational to do so in the first place is the question I raised.
