    Over in Singapore, we do have a sexuality education programme, but not a sex education programme. I was one of the earlier teachers trained in the programme, but it was for primary schools. Those teachers in secondary schools have a different set of training for the same programme.

    Pupils' parents are allowed to opt their children out of it. And frankly speaking, if you were to ask me, the quality of it varies from school to school depending on the teacher handling the programme. In some schools it's virtually non-existent. In some schools, we merged it into the Health Ed or P6 Post-PSLE programme.

    It's no longer just the purvey of the Science teacher. She can only teach the labelling (the various organs, functions and names), but will not go into how you do sex. In fact, even the sexuality education programme does not. It deals more with handling your sexuality. But the question always get asked during the lesson - "How do you do 'it'"? And the teacher has to answer with a straight face using diagrams.

    Heh...and they tend to select married teachers, both male and females, to conduct this course. No marks for guessing why...although in our modern society it doesn't mean that you have no experience if you are not married.
