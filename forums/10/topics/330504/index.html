    Transport fare hikes debated
    By Jamie Ee Wen Wei
    Some 150 people turned up to hear six people - including former Workers' Party (WP) candidate Dr James Gomez - delve into the issue. -- PHOTO: ALPHONSUS CHERN
    FRISBEES flew in the park, and martial arts exponents raised their arms. Nearby, words flew from the speakers' mouths, their arms raised occasionally.

    The hot-button issue on Saturday at the Speakers' Corner in Hong Lim Park was the public transport fare hike announced last Friday.

    Some 150 people turned up to hear six people - including former Workers' Party (WP) candidate Dr James Gomez - delve into the issue.

    The protest, organised by socio-political blog The Online Citizen (TOC), was the second there since rules on public demonstrations were relaxed on Sept 1.

    National University of Singapore (NUS) law undergraduate Choo Zheng Xi, the chief editor of TOC's website, spoke first. He called the transport fare hikes a 'recurring nightmare'.

    Dr Gomez argued that the transport woes were exacerbated by the the 'large volume of people brought into the country'.

    Financial analyst and local blogger Leong Sze Hian said the 'transfer' rebate cost would be borne by commuters making direct journeys. He said: 'Who are the ones who take direct services? The poor!'

    Earlier in the week, the TOC had released to the media a 10-page report, with recommendations to improve the transport system. On Saturday, 50 copies were given out. Many in the crowd were professionals and university students.
