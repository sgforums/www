    Audit is a farce. The company pays the auditor fees to audit the accounts hence there is the element of conflict of interest. The company is free to change auditor as they pay them fees. The auditor will try his best to pass the accounts as true and fair so that they can work on the next company, but there is the stick of legal liability from the public if they screw up. Worst still these companies often offer more lucrative consulting contracts to influence these auditors. The problems of auditing industry can be seen in US where there are so many financial scandals.

    In an audit, it is easier for companies with big clout to doctor accounts or bend rules. MP Davinder Singh was saying the level of the corporate governance is not as high as the western counterparts. Some Boards have many family members on the boards and are more likely to make decisions that are detrimental to the public investors and benefit of family members.

    If you are talking about Goverment Sector, the level accountability and tranparency is much lower.The GIC who manages our reserves had 3 traders who were fined for insider trading. This was discovered by the Japanese Authorities and informed MAS. Initially they were only fined by MAS and GIC refused to sack them. Later they were merely removed from the operations in Japan but retained in GIC. I think the Japanese authorities gave singapore face by not prosecuting the case in Japan. I am not familiar with Japanese Laws but in US if you commit insider trading, it is a criminal offence. Martha Stewart, a celebrity went to jail because of insider trading. To let off insiders who trade on price sensitive information lightly sends a wrong signal on accountability and tranparency.

    Its important to have a system of check and balances, or you may find gold taps and all sorts of nonsense. The argument of not opening GICs to audit due to fear of foreign speculator attacks is a weak one. The president in his private capacity should then be able to audit the accounts. Why was an ex-president denied an internal audit of the accounts?
