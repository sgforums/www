    PAP Govt, through the civil service and GLCs, are the one who will going to
    benefit tremendously by this CPF cut as they employed about 40% of Singapore
    workforce.

    Due to this simple fact, it will create a depressionary pressure on the
    economy if such "savings" are not re-injected into the economy. It would
    mean that instead of having budget deficit, PAP govt may just have budget
    surplus during economic downturn. Due to the increase in GST, govt revenue
    at half time already increased by 5%!

    Sometimes it makes one wonder whether such CPF cuts is necessary or not, or
    isit for the purpose of saving GLCs from the red, instead of helping to
    retain MNCs from relocating?

    My guess is that foreign MNCs will sooner or later, shift out of Singapore
    and there is nothing we could do to prevent this from happening. Thus
    whether such across the board CPF cut is necessary will remain to be seen.

    AFter the massive round of "asset enhancement" back in 1990s, we have been
    forcefully put on the path of no return. It is impossible to pay for your
    mortgage with your CPF in full if the CPF is cut to 30% when they have
    increased your contributions to Medisave and Special accounts.

    Imagine that a household with total income of $4000 will not be able to pay
    their mortgage for a four room flat that cost $200,000 when the 30% CPF will
    only puts in less than $700 in ordinary account. This is cost a great middle
    class crunch and it will create a deterrence effect to young couples
    planning to marry.

    The social-economical impact of the 6% cut in CPF will be too wide to be
    estimated.

    As we are faced with STRUCTURAL economic problems, retaining sectors that
    are STRUCTURALLY unsound to our economic model would be unwise. And since it
    is "STRUCTURAL" in nature, a blunt and crude method like a cut of 6% of CPF
    across the board is not a good method at all. There are still companies
    making money and such CPF cut would mean a "windfall" given by Govt at the
    expense of the workers.

    CPF should not be used as a economic policy instrument at all. It may be
    very tempting and easy to use but it is ineffective to address specific
    problems happening at specific area.

    PAP govt should let the Labour Unions at various economic sectors to play
    the important role of managing and fighting for workers' welfare and
    preventing them from being "exploited". Allowing companies that are making
    money to have a cut on workers' pay, be it CPF or otherwise, is an
    exploitation!

    That is why I think the Labour Chief should step down as he doesn't know
    what he is talking about when he urge for CPF cut, as much as possible!

    Employers' and employees' welfare and benefits should be balanced up,
    especially so during economic downturn when unscrupulous employers will
    always tend to utilize such situation to exploit employees even when they
    are making money. The check and balance should be provided by the labour
    unions.

    Govt is just taking an easier way out for employers to make a statutory cut
    on the CPF so that the power of labour unions in exercising checks and
    balance would be marginalized. This should not be so.

    If the PAP govt really thinks that in some sector, there should be drastic
    cut, then let the employer and employee negotiate for it. Even though I
    strongly oppose PAP govt using CPF cuts as policy instruments, I think at
    the very worst, it should be done in a way that more flexibility should be
    exercised so that welfare of workers (especially those in profit making
    industries) should be safe guarded.

    My proposal would be, take out the six percent and turn it into a variable
    component. For civil service and GLCs, this variable component should not be
    cut as by doing so, they will put on deflationary pressures on local pte
    consumptions and expenditure. For foreign MNCs, let the unions go and
    negotiate. That is the best out of the worst thing the govt should do.
