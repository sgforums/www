    It seems that you are very interested in the Mig29 and F16 comparison. There was this excellent article in the Air Force Monthly a while back (approx 2 to 3 years ago) precisely about comparing these 2 planes. Of course some things have changed since then including the avionics, radar capability and of course we also know that the SMT version of Mig29 has an enlarged fuel tank in the formed of an enlarged dorsal spine.

    Anyway, in short, the conclusion (based on purely air to air combat since you are most interested in these area and I assumed you are not comparing them on strike roles), is that Mig29 is slightly more superior in slow speed manoeuvrability (around 400 kts), but in the high subsonic, transonic region, F16 is more superior. I think this also translate to F16 vs F18. I have spoken to my RSAF pilot friend about his experience fighting the Hornets, well he indicated that they usually do not slow down to fight them, the Vipers are the most potent when they have a lot of energy and their pointability being not as good as Mig29s or the Bugs / Superbugs (due to their better Alpha handling). However, with the introduction of 4 gen off-boresight WVR missiles like Python4, ASRAAM and AIM-9X, the gaps have closed so much that dogfighters today or in the future does not necessary depend on the pilots skills. It really goes down to technology and who has the first looks. It is when looks can kill really.

    Thus, in an F5 vs Mig29 situation, and lets put BVR engage aside, if both are piloted by equally skilled pilots and both are carry off-boresight missiles, given say the F5 has either AIM-9X or Python 4, plus both have HMS, I think the outcome is equally matched. I think technology here play the key role, ie. the off-boresight capabilities of the missiles, the track rate of the seeker, HMS capabilities, radar capabilities and maybe a bit of tactics and a lot of luck !!!!!
