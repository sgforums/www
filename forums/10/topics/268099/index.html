    Why must we put the responsibility on the SMRT staff to help in such cases? Can't we as fellow passengers and citizens also chip in?

    Yes, the SMRT staff has the responsibility to help the injured passenger, which I believe they did to the best of their limited abilities. However, the call for them to take down the particulars of the errant passenger is really not practical. Firstly, they cannot possibly take down the particulars of every passenger who knocked into another, an incident which must occur several hundred times a day. Secondly, they have no legal right to do so (to the best of my knowledge), and the passenger can simply refuse to give his particulars, in which case the SMRT would have no legal recourse.
