  • SingaporeTyrannosaur
    26 Jan 10, 01:11

    The problem is with Singapore putting all its political eggs in one basket.

    Ultimately like it or not, the PAP has to admit that in the long run it is in the best interest of Singapore and the PAP to have a opposition, and a credible one at that. Unfortunately Singapore is so far off from having a true democracy that one wonders wht the future holds.

    Ultimately, one-party-states lead to intellectual inbreeding and groupthink. This is because the entire system lacks any real internal checks and balances, not to mention as opposed to recruiting from people of diverse political opinion, one party tends to recruit those that would agree with them, leading to selection pressure over the long run, to create a party that with far less vitality then the original generation of leaders, favouring adherence to party guidelines as opposed to actually finding solutions.

    What this means is that it is a VERY big question, that lacking a credible opposition, or not allowing Singaporeans sufficent political space to breathe, one wonders how exactly the nation will turn out after this current generation of leaders pass on and the next take over. Our MM will not be around forever, and one wonders how things will play out. Will there be a power struggle? Nobody knows.

    One can cite the example of China as an example of "sucessful", one party rule. But before one gets to far one needs to realize that China, due to singular party rule and adherence to a "cult" of infailablity of the party (Mao in particular), eventually created the cultural revolution that caused plenty of deaths, suffering, and turmoil in the nation. The party itself was forced to reconsider a lot of it's internal policies.

    The thing is, can Singapore afford such a mistake? Are we on the brink of making one? Will our policies, driven relentlessly with no real opposition to act as a check and balance lead to a state where we quite literally plunge our soceity over the edge like China's during the cultural revolution? Nobody knows.
