    "last election 66.6% voted them" ?

     

    You should consider the following details and comparing the Singapore Election Results with that achieved by countries which are far bigger in population size and across larger land mass.

     

    Singapore has a ‘Total Population of 4,401,400 Singaporeans – from 0 to 65 years and over (July 2006 est)’ (*1) 

    In Election 2006, the ‘Total Valid Votes accepted was 1,123,000 out of a Total Voting Electorate of 1,222,884 that actually participated - from an electoral roll of 2,147,840 citizens’ (*2) 

    The total 1,123,000 votes accepted as valid represent only 27% of Singapore total population of 4,401,400 or only 52% of the registered voters of 2,147,840.

    From the total of 1,123,000 valid votes counted - the PAP received only 747,860 votes, and these valid votes given to the PAP was only 34.81% of the registered list of 2,147,840, or only 16.99% of the total population of 4,401,400.

     

    ‘In the Indonesian Legislative Election 2009 – Indonesia had a Registered List of Voters that numbered 171,265,442 citizens; and the Total Votes countered was 104,099,785 votes’ (*3) 

    The valid votes of 104,099,785 is 60.78% of the registered voters of 171,265,442 Indonesian citizens.

    Considering that the ‘Indonesian Population is 240,271,522 – from 0 to 65 years and older (July 2009 est) (*4) - the valid votes counted will represent 43.32% of the total population compared to Singapore pathetic 16.99%.

    This large involvement of the Indonesian population is more remarkable when the entire election process is conducted across the large expanse of Indonesian territory consisting of over 1000 large and small islands.

     

    In the ‘Japanese 2009 Election - it saw a Total of 70,244,972 voters which represented a 69.28% turnout’ (*5)  from a Total ‘Population of 127,078,679 Japanese – from 0 to 65 years and over (July 2009 est)’ (*6) 

     

    The pathetic size of the total population of Singaporeans that were involved in the electoral process cannot possibly be seen as a clear mandate to the PAP.

    This is the kind of election scenario that the PAP has engineered through its typical methods at gerrymeandering the process, so as to give themselves a false legitimacy at each election since stepping into political power in 1957.
