    8 Mar 05, 09:41
    lwflee,

    Your posts seem strangely out of sync with the rest; like a voice in the wilderness!! The original post is too broad ranging to provide focus. I think your points are more focussed on the economy and about shifting our economy towards the services sector.

    IMO, the most important element for success is quality of expertise, be it in finance, tourism, legal, consultancy, medical, education, entertainment, etc. (in any case, it is probably true for most enterprises). To be frank, I don't believe our domestic pool of talent is sufficient in quality and numbers to compete with players in North America and Europe. We would need to attract top talents to come and set up shop and to strengthen local service enterprises. We should also leverage upon our knowledge of the region and build niche markets.

    The authorities are probably doing the right things with various incentives, regulatory framework, promotions and foreign partnerships. However, as with earlier phase of industrialisation, I expect the GLCs will eventually dominate the scene. One could also expect more government service establishments to be eventually privatised and seize the opportunities. Pearlie has suggested the authorities should leave the businesses entirely to the private sector. I rather suspect we do not have sufficient pool of talent in the private sector to make it happen.

    I sympathise with the complaints of some of the other posters about coping with rising costs. Unfortunately, this is an inevitable consequence of economic growth. The government has to deal with the social issue of growing disparity in income and dissatisfaction. More has to be done to ensure the fruits of economic success are also shared by the vast majority of middle and lower income Sporeans.
