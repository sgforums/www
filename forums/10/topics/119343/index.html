    The government is elected to discharge certain duties and responsibilities to its citizens. There appear to be a dire lack of understanding of such duties over the years in that provisions of essential services are being passed off to non-government bodies or GLCs etc under schemes of corporatization etc which final objectives only served to reduce such duties and responsibilities to citizens.

    At one time, security in any apartments or housing estates are covered by the government. Over the years not just security but a whole host of other local and municipal responsibilities have been progressively transferred to private management corporations. Even in areas of enforcements of fire regulations and traffic controls, government nowadays has less to do and more to charge and tax.

    Transport, medical, utilities and education one by one are being passed off through corporatisation etc to GLCs or even NTUC enabling government to profit from expenditure savings and surpluses. The original tax allocations were not passed back to the citizens.

    The tendency to corporatize and pass off government responsibilities and duties to citizens in Transport, medical and utilities etc is persistent. Minister Khaw Boon Woon is thinking of doing the same thing by corporatizing certain specialist medical treatments to private consultants which will no doubt result in another round of medical cost increases and lowering of service level.

    It is suggested that each time any ministry is thinking of passing off duties to GLCs or private sector to save on its own costs, a portion of the taxes attributable to such services shall be put into an endowment pr capital funds from which any shortfall, inefficiencies or extraordinary expenditures can be jointly funded from such reserved expenditure witheld or owed to the people.

    It is like sinking fund in the management corporations to be used for specific situations which can be defined objectively by panels of experts.

    This inequity is confirmed by MediaCorp's CEO's latest revelation that one of the reason why MediaCorp cannot survive or stay profitable is that the licence fees on TV/radio are collected and kept by the government and the whole TV stations have then have to recover costs of operations purely from end users. Quality of the TV programs if affected is clearly caused by government's single-minded determination to avoid discharge of its duties and responsibilities to citizens and to create surpluses which at the end of the day only jack up costs of living to citizens and making the economy uncompetitive.

    Lands acquired using tax payers' fund should not be sold at market prices again to citizens for basic housing and essential services. Land rentals should not be charged for utilities, education, medical or even LTA services. Only a certain percentage of state lands should be permitted to be sold at market prices to private sector for commercial and relevant development otherwise the costs of living and doing business in Singapore will keep going up.

    Hospital and medical services should not be corporatised just to retain good doctor. People pay taxes to fund part of infrastructural medical costs through GST, personal and corporate taxes and medishield and all kinds of schemes.

    They should not be short-changed in any surreptitious corporate schemes because of the above-stated reasons.
