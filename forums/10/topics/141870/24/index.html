    NKF hires law firm as it considers action against former board

    Legal team looking into contracts with Forte, Protonweb

    By Michelle Quah
    April 11, 2006
    The Business Times

    (SINGAPORE) The National Kidney Foundation (NKF) has hired one of Singapore's top law firms, Allen & Gledhill (A&G), to look into possible legal recourse against the charity's former board of directors, sources have told The Business Times.

    BT understands that a team from A&G - led by Senior Counsel K Shanmugam, head of litigation and dispute resolution - has begun work into whether the NKF's new board has grounds for action against the former directors.

    A BT source said: 'If you remember, what (accountancy firm) KPMG found in their special investigation of NKF last year were basically management lapses and a lack of transparency, rather than specific crimes. That makes criminal action against the NKF's former employees difficult. So the new board is looking at civil action first.'

    Attempts to reach Mr Shanmugam yesterday were unsuccessful.

    BT understands the A&G team is looking at the two contracts entered into by the old board with Forte Systems and Protonweb Solutions several years ago. The two deals involved NKF payments to the companies even though they were said to have failed to deliver some services.

    The Protonweb deal, worth $4.5 million, saw payments being made by the NKF to Protonweb even though the call centre's services were delayed and there was a shortfall in the number of hours delivered. In the case of the Forte contract, which cost the NKF $3.3 million, the software was not delivered on time and Forte was said to have failed to meet the contract specifications.

    Both companies were owned by Pharis Aboobacker, said to have been a friend of the then NKF CEO, TT Durai. The former board was made up of members such as the then chairman Richard Yong and board member Matilda Chua. Mr Durai was not a board member.

    The NKF's new board, led by chairman Gerard Ee, said at the time the KPMG report was released that it was considering action against the charity's former executive committee members in relation to these contracts. 'The terms of these contracts were unusual, and the attitudes of the exco members to the poor performance of these contracts were extraordinary,' the NKF's new board said then.

    Mr Ee, speaking to BT yesterday, confirmed that his board was looking at the avenues they have for legal action against the former board members of the charity, and that the two contracts are areas they are looking into.

    'Our solicitors, Allen & Gledhill, are looking into whether we have any actionable grounds, and we'll wait to act on their advice,' he said. 'We'll announce our course of action at the appropriate time.'

    Latest Update from CNA

    Former NKF CEO TT Durai charged

    SINGAPORE : Former National Kidney Foundation (NKF) CEO TT Durai has been slapped with two charges of corruption.

    He is accused of submitting false invoices to mislead the NKF.

    One charge reads that 57-year-old Durai had, between December 2003 and January 2004, deceived the NKF into making a $20,000 payment to a company for providing interior design consultancy service for various dialysis centres in 2003.

    The other alleges he deceived the NKF, in September 2003, into paying $5,000 to another company as consultancy for hiring a senior manager for NKF.

    In both cases, no such services were rendered.

    For each charge, Durai faces a maximum fine of $100,000 or five years in jail, or both. He is out on bail of $50,000.

    A former assistant manager of the NKF's purchasing department, 43-year-old Ragini Vijayalingam, faces 11 corruption charges.

    She is alleged to have received cash and gifts amounting to more than $10,000, between September 2003 and July 2005, from a medical supplies company for helping it to secure contracts and future business dealings with NKF.

    Former NKF Board Member Matilda Chua, 37, faces two charges of falsifying company accounts with intent to defraud.

    She is alleged to have falsified the accounts of Global Net Relations (GNR), of which she was a director, in December 2001 and December 2002.

    A $300,000 discount given to a company for the purchase of shares from GNR was classified instead as advisory fees and loan repayment to two individuals.

    Each offence carries a jail term of up to seven years, or fine, or both

    Chua is out on bail of $20,000.

    The three have had their passports impounded.

    Former NKF Board Chairman Richard Yong Kun Da and Treasurer Loo Say San face one charge each under the Companies Act.

    This is for failing to exercise reasonable diligence as directors of NKF.

    If found guilty, they face a maximum fine of $5,000 or a jail term of one year, or both.

    The Commercial Affairs Department says based on evidence, it will not be filing criminal charges against anybody else besides the two.

    The cases will be mentioned again on April 25. - CNA/de
