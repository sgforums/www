    Channel NewsAsia - 36 minutes ago

    Committee of Inquiry’s report will be useful for other countries too

    SINGAPORE: The final report by the Committee of Inquiry, set up to investigate the escape of Jemaah Islamiyah leader, Mas Selamat Kastari, will serve as critical knowledge for other countries as well.

    Mas Selamat escaped last Wednesday from the Whitley Road Detention Centre.

    He has been held under the Internal Security Act since 2006, as he had allegedly plotted to crash a hijacked plane into Changi Airport.

    Deputy Prime Minister Wong Kan Seng announced the formation of the independent committee on 2 March.

    The committee will likely submit a report in a month or so.

    Retired Judge, Goh Joon Seng heads the 3—member panel.

    Also on the panel — former Commissioner of Police, Tee Tua Ba and Deputy Secretary of Security at the Home Affairs Ministry, Choong May Ling.

    Experts said this is an authoritative panel and the members are logical choices.

    They noted that the retired judge would be able to address legal issues, while the ex—police chief will have knowledge about law enforcement.

    Experts said the committee will likely study areas like how he managed to escape from the detention facility, the initial moments when he disappeared and the centre’s security infrastructure.

    The panel will also likely make recommendations on how to prevent such an incident from happening again.

    Experts also added that other countries would read this final report closely to compare it with their present security practices.

    Using that report, other regional authorities can tighten their own detention procedures.

    Associate Professor Kumar Ramakrishna, member of the Head Centre for Excellence for National Security at the S Rajaratnam School of International Studies, said: "The importance of providing such an authoritative analysis and investigation would be certainly to provide what would amount to the definitive view and official view on the subject of this detainee’s escape.

    “It also would help to quash a lot of public speculation and answer a lot of questions which may be in people’s minds.“

    He added: “I think the commission’s work will be very important and useful to not just within the government but to analysts outside, as well as to our counterparts in the region as well.”

    In another development, authorities in Johor are assisting Singapore to track down Mas Selamat.

    Johor’s Police Chief, Mokhtar Shariff said his department has distributed photos and details of the wanted man, to its personnel manning all the entry points in Malaysia.

    He added that the Malaysian law enforcement officers have also "been instructed to arrest the fugitive on sight."

    The Police Chief said that “Singapore had made an official request for assistance from Johor."

    INTERPOL, the world’s largest police organisation, has already elevated a security notice on Mas Selamat to Red Notice, with an arrest warrant. —CNA/vm
