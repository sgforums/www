    Feb 28, 2008  
    DPM apologises for security lapse, explains how detainee escaped
    By Ng Kai Ling
    A SECURITY lapse at the Internal Security Department's Whitley Road detention centre allowed Mas Selamat Kastari, the leader of the Singapore Jemaah Islamiyah (JI) network, to escape on Wednesday afternoon.

    Minister of Home Affairs Wong Kan Seng said in Parliament on Thursday that Mas Selamat was taken out of his cell to go to the family visitation room to wait for his family.

    There, he requested to go to the toilet and then escaped.

    Mr Wong, who is also Deputy Prime Minister, said that the incident should not have happened and he is 'sorry that it had'.

    He added that security at the centre has since been stepped up.

    MP Teo Ho Pin earlier asked: Why, since Mas Selamat is a security threat, did the authorities take four hours to inform the public of the escape?

    Mr Wong explained that this is because the agencies assessed at that time that there was no imminent danger to the public, and that the focus then was to lock down the immediate vicinity of the detention centre.

    He added that there was no information that the escapee has any plan that threatens public security.

    He emphasised that the priority now is to arrest the Jemaah Islamiyah leader and no efforts will be spared.

    All checkpoints are on high alert in case the escapee tries to leave the country.

    Mr Wong urged the public to stay calm and warned that anyone who renders assistance to Mas Selamat is commiting an offence.
