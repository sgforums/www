    Legal Action: Tamil Article Published in "The Hammer"

    In November 1995, the (Workers' )Party and the whole of its Executive Council found itself the object of two defamation suits filed by five PAP Tamil MPs and eleven members of the Organising Committee of the Tamil Language Week arising from an article published in the Party organ, "The Hammer". The Plaintiffs' complaint in both suits was that the article implied that their efforts to promote the Tamil Language had been less than sincere.

    Judgment: Tamil Article Published in "The Hammer"

    By the said judgment given at the High Court on the 30th November 1998, Jeyaretnam, A Balakrishnan and Workers' Party were collectively and severally ordered to pay ten of the plaintiffs in the said suit a total sum of $265,000 for damages and costs to be taxed.

    The Worker's Party's appeal against the said judgment was dismissed on 21st April 1999. By then the total sum had snowballed to close to half a million dollars, inclusive of legal costs.

    1999: Winding Up Petition Against the Party

    In September 1999, the plaintiffs commenced bankruptcy proceedings against Jeyeratnam and initiated a winding up petition against Workers' Party, which had been unable to pay the sum. That action invited international attention and criticisms and was widely reported in both the local and international media. Eventually the plaintiffs did not proceed the action to the end.
