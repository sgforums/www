    By Jackson Sawatan

    SINGAPORE, Sept 28 (Bernama) -- Singapore has revoked the circulation and distribution approval to the Far Eastern Economic Review (FEER) magazine for failure by its publisher to comply with conditions under the republic's Newspaper and Printing Presses Act (NPPA).

    The conditions required FEER, a magazine published by Dow Jones & Co Inc, to appoint a representative authorised to accept service of any notice or legal process on behalf of the publisher, and submit a security deposit of S$200,000.

    "FEER had not complied by the Sept 11 deadline, nor has it complied till today, despite a reminder," the Ministry of Information, Communication and the Arts said in a statement.

    With the revocation, which took effect Thursday, it will become an offence for any person to sell or distribute the magazine in the republic, or import it or possess it for sale or distribution.

    The ministry said: "It is a privilege and not a right for foreign newspapers to circulate in Singapore. If any foreign newspaper fails to comply with the law, including the NPPA, they cannot expect to enjoy this privilege".

    Singapore gazetted FEER as "a declared foreign newspaper" on Dec 26, 1987 "for interfering in the domestic politics of Singapore" and later classified it as an offshore newspaper following an amendment to the NPPA.

    In December 2004, FEER changed from a weekly to a monthly publication and, as a result, it ceased to be an offshore newspaper and was allowed to circulate in Singapore without having to comply with the conditions for offshore newspapers.

    The magazine, with a circulation cap of 10,000 copies, however remained "a declared foreign newspaper" but in August, the ministry said: "It is an anomaly for FEER, which is a declared foreign newspaper, not to be subjected to the conditions that apply to the other declared foreign newspapers".

    Following that, the government issued notice to FEER to comply with the conditions under the NPPA by Sept 11.

    FEER is currently being sued by Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong and his father, Minister Mentor Lee Kuan Yew, over an article pertaining to opposition leader Dr Chee Soon Juan, which was published in the July issue of the magazine.
