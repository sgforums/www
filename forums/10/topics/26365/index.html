  • CX
    15 Sep 02, 21:52
    its a bit hard to study inside, unless, like they said, u get an office job or something like that...

    but even there, life inside just sweeps u away and your best laid plans gets chucked aside... it takes a lot of discipline to keep to your plans.

    and besides, unless u go out of your way to be lam-nua, its not likely that u will get too rusty... its all about exercising your brain, not necessarily doing homework. read a book in your free time, keep updated and talk to friends outside... that all contributes to enriching u and preparing u for life outside.

    its actually good to take a break away from studying for awhile... it refreshes u and it makes u want to work harder after that cos of all the time u lost.

    the army as a choice of career, is often overrated... they pay o.k, but its not for everybody... but NS as an experience, is often under-rated. u just have to take it the right way and try to get the most out of it.

    and here's some tips for u:

    nobody gets used to being dirty or life out in the field! but u learn to take care of yourself and make yourself comfortable so its not so bad...

    a groundsheet over muddy ground is actually more comfortable to lie on than rocky ground. grass is the best, but there are usually insects. bring a trashbag. they're waterproof. your groundsheet is not.

    mosquitoes are everywhere, but u learn how to avoid getting bitten (a little repellant over the ears so they don't buzz over them and a little on your face and neck... not too much on your forehead cos your sweat will wash them into your eyes and THAT HURTS. and finally, a layer all over your uniform: sleeves, collar, helmet and camo scarf.)

    and last of all, have some self-respect... everybody gets tired, but try to control yourself... nobody appreciates having a bitching pussy in the section. u only end up pissing people off and STILL tired...

    u'll bore yourself stupid if all u think about from day one is ORD... try doing what i do: wait for meal times its more predictable (at least while in camp...) and u feel that time is passing...
