    As with a recent poll taken of 100 people by the straits times, all of the 100 polled did not want the govt out of office, but merely censured for certain acts that have not gone down well with the people.

    The handling of the IRs was highlighted as a decision that went ahead despite a significantly high negative response from the people.

    Another irk was the manner in which the RC and incumbent PAP MPs managed residents during elections. Yusof from the hotly contested Aljunied GRC described his visit from his MP whom 'he didn't know' as cold and 'dissatisfactory'. The MP (who is not named) gave him a quick handshake, thrust a bottle of newater into his hands, and left. It was all very well rehearsed and executed.

    Sylvia Lim on the other hand, spent several minutes talking to Yusof, not just 'shaking his hands' but was 'sincere'. Hence the WP got his vote.

    Top of the polls was:

    1. a desire to have alternative voices in parliament to act as a check on the govt

    2. disapproval of ruling party tactics such as votes-for-upgrading strategy

    3. the view that ruling party candidates were unapproachable.

    Most of the people even concurred that the majority of Ruling party candidates were seen as 'stand-offish' and 'out of touch' with ordinary people.

    Judging by the rate this goes, people may one day yearn to have a 50-50 coalition govt, PAP-opposition run, with PAP running the national issues while the opposition handling the affairs of the people.
