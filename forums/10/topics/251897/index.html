    When cost of land is purposefully kept ARTIFICIALLY HIGH - it affects the selling price of the homes, shops, offices, schools, hospitals, etc.

    With such high costs, the shop owners must charge higher costs to pay for the rentals or bank loans.

    As workers, we will need a minimum higher wage so as to cover our mortgages and leave some money to pay for our daily necessities, our children's education, and the family's future.

    The Government can affect this cost input if it is willing to be LESS GREEDY in setting the minimum price that it is prepared to dispose of the land for development; as well as to remove unnecessary burden in high development charges and stamp duties on the cost and type of development that is planned for the plot of land.

    The other factors that can influence our Cost of Living are also the cost of Essential Services that are dictated by the Government - even though these Essential Services are now provided by "Private Companies" - these Companies are basically Government Owned Companies that are registered as Private Business Entitites.

    These Companies are involved in Electricity Generating, Electricity Supply, Water Production and Sewage Treatment, Water Distribution, Garbage and Sewage Collection.

    Temasek Holding is the major shareholder of Electricity Generating and sells Electricity at a fixxed price to all the Electricity Supply Companies that add only an extra cent or two on the high supply price.

    This means that Temasek is making profit by the minute as we use the computer, the airconditioner, the lightings, the household appliances - and with the money not even going back to the Government Finance Ministry, but into the Profit and Loss Account of Temasek Holdings (probably to be lost in another UNACCOUNTED Shin Corp disaster).

    Is it not supposed to be managing Singapore Reserves by making our reserves earn profits OVERSEAS ?

    The other Government Private Owned Companies providing essential services are similarly earning profits from Singaporeans, and are not returned or reported in the Finance Ministry Annual Report - unlike in the past when PUB, PWD and ENV were responsible for these essential services.

    With the COST of Living in Singapore NOT dictated by Market Forces, but by the wilful profit goals set by the Government aka the Ruling Party, Singaporeans are paying a higher price than necessary that make our Cost of Living higher than our neighbors.

    As the Cost of Living spiral out of control, and gets higher, this will affect the Standard of Living, as we can afford less quality when we must spend more on the essentials to keep ourselves alive.

    Cost of Living is not affected by our wages - even when our wages will affect the cost to our employer, who will recover this cost with a higher selling price; in the same manner that this Government will recover the money given to Singaporeans by charging a higher price for all the essential services that are in their monopolistic control.
