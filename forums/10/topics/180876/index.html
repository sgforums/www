    In foreign policy, there are some issues on which a state has to take a stand, as sitting on the fence would be detrimental or useless to the state's interest compared to siding with one or the other. This is the case with the Taiwan issue. Simply put, if we want to do business in China, we have to side with them over Taiwan. China is too big a fish to snub for the likes of a small fry like Taiwan.

    It's true that we do not want other countries to interfere in our politics. But neither do we interfere in the politics of Taiwan nor China. We do not tell them what they should do; we merely give our comments and state our stand on the matter as clearly as possible. I think our handling of the matter has been excellent so far, giving us quite a bit of credit in the region regarding this issue. Just look at the uproar over PM Lee's visit to Taiwan some months back, and see how it shows that what we do is taken seriously by both parties. Hence our leaders cannot afford to sit back and watch the show, but must handle the matter carefully, which so far we've managed to do pretty decently.
