    it's not difficult to see the difficult times we are in and the potential economic benefits that the casino can bring. the problem is, for as long as anyone can remember, we have been told one thing only to find out that it actually means another. cultural representation in the GRC sense turns out to be a buy one get three free scheme. honesty in leadership costs us millions every month. so i'm not sure when they claim to be bringing in the whale for us, they are not actually feeding off us small shrimps.

    in any case, i think our situation can be likened to that of a highly paid technician whose boss has decided to employ someone cheaper. one way is to enhance our skillsets and productivity so that we justify our higher pay. but there's a fundamental limit of how much we can set ourselves apart from the rest of the field because if you look at our competitors, we're essentially of the same stock - same strengths so to speak.

    the other way is to become bosses ourselves. if we have the technical know-how (which we don't), we can develop and market our own products and and employ our competitors instead, thus turning a competitive disadvantage into a competitive advantage.

    the problem is we have not reached that level to become bosses ourselves and i feel this is the real jump we must make now in order to reach the next rung that the developed world currently sits. most of the developed nations are there because they have the technology / branding to create premium products well sought after all over the world.

    there's a lot of hope directed at our young ones who will supposedly become more creative and achieve what we couldn't. however, regardless of how much idealism we inject into them, when they step into the reality of society, they would still be faced with the same bureaucratic inertia that is preventing dreams from being realised. ultimately, a pragmatic society like ours have little place for dreams and whatever hopes we may cherish or talents we possess are to be constrained within the limits of the choices available to us.
