    euphratis, consider this:

    When you retire, say at about 60, you'll get no more money coming in as salary, yet you'll have living costs of maybe $1500 a month, assuming that the house is paid up by then and you haven't set your bank loan terms at 65 years.

    OK, so your annual expenditure (for just one person, not counting dependants) is $18,000 a year. Okay, let's say $300 comes from CPF every month - that's still $14,400 you need. Where's the money going to come from?

    For argument's sake, say you've got investments and you're depending on the dividends to live on. We'll be generous and call it 8%, which ought to be a good balance between performance and security. At that rate, you'll need $180,000 in capital. That's assuming that you've got the house paid up, say one that's worth $350,000. On top of that, you've got your living expenses while you're working, say at the set $18,000 a year for 30 years - that's $540,000.

    In sum, by the time you're 60, you'll need to have earned a total of $1,070,000. And that's if you're frugal and earning something like $36,000 a year - no overseas holidays, no big parties, no real fun.

    So have a think about it - do you really want to wait until you're 45 to start thinking about these things? Better get a lot of sedatives, because you're going to have a lot of sleepless nights.

    I'm not disagreeing with what you're saying about the government - I'm just saying that you need to make sure that your resentment doesn't keep you from facing the reality described above.
