    One could point out that the current situation was made a lot worse then it ought to be by the heavy-handed population control policies of the PAP. Some people did point out in the era of the baby boomers that the birth rate would naturally come down as the country becomes developed, and that heavy-handed population control policies would not be such a good idea.

    Unfortunately this observation ended up being almost prophetic. The fact that we had our (quietly forgotten) eugenics era and stuff like the graduate mother's scheme probably didn't help things.

    Additionally the current measures to boost the population always run into conflicts of interests... the government wants people to have more babies but are unwilling to encourage or create the societal and economic conditions to encourage such a thing. 

    The current massive influx of FTs is just a stopgap measure, ultimately if the root conditions are not met, even the FTs themselves will not be having more children (assuming that they even stay), ultimately this leads to a country with an open door policy where the population is constantly topped up by foreigners hoping to make a quick buck off the favorable policies while the locals shoulder the burden of that. It's going to be a vicious cycle.

    Unfortunately I do not see any viable solutions to the situation, namely because our government will be unwilling, or unable to really do what it takes to resolve the issue... probably because it involves taking steps that might affect their own interests.
