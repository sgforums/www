    An undischarged bankrupt being a Registered Director of a Company is an offense - clear and simple.

    For a search fee of $5.00, this can be verified at the Registrar of Companies and Businesses, at International Plaza, 5th Floor, Anson Road.

    A bankrupt is not allowed by the Official Assignee's office to operate any bank account - (especially Current Account), except a Savings Account and only with the approval of the OA's office.

    If a bankrupt is not registered as a Director of a Company, but is allowed to operate a Current Account by the Registered Directors of the Company, the bankrupt has also committed an offense.

    The Police can act on the instruction of the Official Assignee's Office, and it remains with the Official Assignee as the initiator of any prosecution action taken against the bankrupt.

    The bankrupt can face a fine of $10,000 and jail sentence if the Official Assignee decide to act on any proven public complaint towards an undischarged bankrupt.

    You are right that the Registrar of Companies and Businesses will take some time to update their records. However, you can approach the Counter to have them trace any outstanding updates for any particular company that you are specially interest in.

    Bankrupts are NOT ALLOWED to sign or operate any Current Account - period, this is clearly stated in the Bankruptcy Act.

    The Police will not step into a Civil Matter between two parties, but will act if a Criminal Offense had occurred and will depend on the nature and severity of the case (as in mass public cheating).

    Unfortunately, from own experience, the Police will more then likely refer to the Official Assignee's Office, who will then seek further advise from the Attorney General's Chambers concerning prosecution.

    Your Police Report can be submitted to the Official Assignee's Office for further IMMEDIATE and URGENT investigation on the matter.

    You can demand to see the Case Officer responsible for managing the affairs of the particular Bankrupt, as with his NRIC number given to the OA's Reception Counter, his entire file can be retrieved and the Case Officer identified.
