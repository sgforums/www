    PAP MP Koo Tsai Kee: SAF has been “practising” productivity as there are no foreigners in SAF

    March 3, 2010

    In one of the most stunning and outlandish remarks made to date by a PAP MP on the Budget, Mr Koo Tsai Kee seems to have forgotten to put on his thinking cap in his eagerness to please his superior Deputy Prime Minister and Defence Minister Teo Chee Hean.

    Speaking in Parliament yesterday, Tanjong Pagar MP Koo Tsai Kee who is also the Minister of State for Defence heaped generous praises on DPM Teo as being the “best man” to lead the National Productivity and Continuing Education Council because of his long years in the Singapore Armed Forces (SAF).

    Mr Koo claimed that SAF has been “practising productivity all this while” because “there are no foreign workers or foreign talent in the SAF, which is made up entirely of Singapore soldiers.”

    “The SAF, despite its manpower constraints, has been able to face up to a widening range of security threats in recent years under Defence Minister Teo….And I am not saying this because he’s my boss,” he added.

    With due respect to Mr Koo, one need not be a MP or Minister of State for the matter to realize the simple fact that foreigners will not be employed by SAF or any armies in the world for security reasons unless they are mercenaries and any Singapore male who has served two years of National Service will be able to attest to the amount of wastage which is going on within SAF.

    It is an oxymoron to deduce that SAF is “productive” just because there are no foreigners on its payroll.

    As for his “trust” in his superior, it appears to be misplaced too. DPM Teo told CNA a few days ago that Singapore has made “progress” previously in its productivity drive:

    “We have made good progress in the past in our productivity drive and that is why we have been able to move our economy forward and have good jobs for many Singaporeans. But this is a constant effort and we have to renew and re-double our efforts,” he was quoted as saying.

    However, according to data from the U.S. Department of Labor, Singapore’s productivity has been dropping for the last three years with the steepest decline in 2008 at 6.6 per cent. It averaged a miserable 0.7 percent increase per year for the last decade, the lowest among developed nations. How such a dismal performance can be misconstrued as “good progress” is anybody’s guess.

    Singaporeans should not place too much hope on either DPM Teo or Mr Koo to spear-head Singapore’s productivity drive to reach its lofty target of 3 percent increase per year for they still seem clueless of the reality on the ground.
