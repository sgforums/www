Prime real estate market by issuing 120 leasehold

  • robertteh
    3 Oct 05, 13:09
    Government does not need to control so much lands. If lands are not so tightly controlled under 99 years' lease, so by extending leasehold to 120 years, government will still have many lands and maintain its land stock to meet various purposes.

    Once this mental block is removed, Singapore 99 years' leaseholds will boom one more round as people become more receptive to buying leaseholds being primed up by 120 years to make people confident about leaseholds.

    The whole market will react positively if a new concept about real estate is created by good thinkers and planners - really thinking out the box.

  • robertteh
    3 Oct 05, 14:32
    Originally posted by TooFree:
    How you arrived at this figure of 120?
    99-year leasehold is a little too short as can be seen in project like Mandarin Garden, Gillman Height, Farrer Court etc where after another 10-20 years the market values begin to drop or unable to appreciate despite good locations etc.

    120 years will create a certain practical buffer against bankers' and financial institutions' rule of thumb lending policies and will continue to lend to leasehold purchases even for older estates. 120 year tenure in a way will extend the investment opportunities to one-to-two generations. 99-year tenure is too thin to extend to two generation. There is a lack of sustainability in 99 year leasehold to get more sustained benefit of such leasehold.

    Once government starts to issue new 120-year leasehold the signal to the whole real estate market will be very strong with 99-year leaseholders having greater confidence in continuing their purchases of leasehold and the whole property market will be boosted.

    No matter what we do economically, real estate investments will continue to fuel hopes of upward mobilities for the populations. Past factors happened once in the 1970s - 1980s. These factors are purely opportunistic and economic. New opportunities for another round of real estate boom is unlikely based on economics alone. The same neighbouring countries supporting our real estate boom no longer exist. It is time to think out of the box and get Singaporeans back to confidence in their own real estates one more round.

    This is only one way for sure. Government through MRT and in time to come IR will also tap on new potential to enrich its coffers in order to continue to enjoy future surpluses through various land-sale schemes.

    Whether the solutions to people's economy vibrancy is through real hard works of earning as much wages or possible or through property booms, it may attract different schools of thought but in the real world, IR will push up land values around towns and citizens living close by such areas and MRT will continue to enjoy some benefit.

    I think solutions to our economic competitiveness should be multi-pronged, with real estates being the primary factor and government should continue to support this factor to allow citizens to continue to live from any appreciations of property values.
