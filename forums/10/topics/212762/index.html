    Some Europeans are into Holocaust denial. That would be inconsistent with the idea of free speech.

    At the bottomline, we need to ask whether or not we believe in free speech within limits of the law. If an utterance was made that broke no laws of the land but it annoys some people, should the utterance not have been made?

    I would think that the space for free speech should always be maximised rather than curtailed. After all, there are so many different people with so many different beliefs. Any statement made is bound to displease this or that person. Should we all therefore just shut up and say nothing?

    If somebody says something that is offensive to you, you need to think about how to react. If what was said was false, refute it, or ignore it. If what was said was debatable, set out your case to say why you think your views are right. If what was said was true, learn from it.

    In the marketplace of ideas, why should religion be exempt from debate and criticism?
