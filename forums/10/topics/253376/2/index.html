    JJ wrote:
    On this matter, I believe FT is necessary.

    Some forummers oppose FT. But that argument simply doesn't accord due credit to FT for helping Singapore develop into what it is today. We have always had FT. Maybe there are more of them these days and hence they may be more visible. But they are vital for any open capitalist society like Singapore.

    Let's face it. One way or other, there are many jobs that are far better done by FT. I am sure there are some jobs occupied by FT that could be done by locals. That is an unfortunate by-product of govt policies. But by and large, Singapore as a whole benefits from the presence of all these FT.
    Whether a policy is good or bad is not the simplistic generation as often made out to be.

    Often while a policy may seem good but when carried to excess without control it will become bad. The key criterion to any policy often is in the implementation.

    For example it is a good policy to avoid welfarism as that will make people over-dependent on the state resulting in laziness and deterioration of the work ethics and causing loss of economic competitiveness.

    However by going to the opposite of taxing and recovering all possible costs of government services, it will drive up costs of living and bankrupt the citizens' saving and retirements and eventually the state may still have to look after the hapless retirees and aged population unable to lead a normal life.

    Today we are suffering from the reverse of welfarism with the citizens being heavily taxed with direct and indirect taxes of all kinds by the state.

    Our domestic sector of the economy is unable to grow because of extremely high costs of essential government services ranging from housing to transportation and utility.

    Meritocracy is good but we are now practising meritocracy narrowly to the extreme which only benefit a few elites and this has becomes bad.

    Foreign talents may be good but again we have a government which does not understand the full implication of over-liberalizing employment of foreigners like tripartism when carried to the extreme will lead to large-scale abuse and destablise society causing discontented and disllusioned citizens and eventually unrest.
