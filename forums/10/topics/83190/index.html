    Personally, I think owning a car in Singapore, is not a cheap and easy thing to do. Firstly, one needs to ensure one can support a car. Once financial ability is confirmed, and the car is bought, one needs to take every effort to prevent / avoid having one's car scratched.

    The popular thinking and opinion that owning a car is covenient, is a misconception. Because with it, comes parking space hassles, parking coupon time running out when visiting clients or friends, and then there's the current issue of having it scratched by some schizo maniac.

    Several years ago, my car got scratched, and the culprit though reported to the Police, nothing was done about it. Until recently last year, the culprit who scratched my car cut the throat of an NUS proffessor and was diagnosed to be suffering from severe schizophrenia. This is my real experience with car-scratching individuals. They're mostly mad people.

    Next, my solution is to park in well lit and exposed lots, where general public can see my car, and in corner lots . In covered multi-story carparks, I park near adjacent flats where residence can see the carpark and is bright - as well as in corner lots.

    I believe if you park carelessly, such as near pick up trucks, vans and lorries, these illiterate drivers will fling their door open so hard and wide, it bang against the side of your car, causing an ugly dimple to form. Or if you park so close to the neighboring vehicle that the driver of the other parked car find hard to enter his vehicle, he may scratch your car in frustration and out of revenge. REMEMBER, people ( or motorists ) in Singapore are VERY vengeful. Generally, with the costs of living and owning a car in Singapore so high, these sort of behaviour is inevtiable. So to live with it, we all need to adopt certain extreme measures to avoid these sort of disappointments, and heartaches.

    I always take my time when looking for a "safe" parking lot. I park far away from where I am going to if I have to, just so that my car can be safe from sick people, in heartlands.

    In this regard, forget convenience of owning a car. Because when you park conveniently, and without consideration of such probabilities, chances are, one day your car WILL be scratched.

    Believe me, I've been driving for more than 20 years, and even when you are careful as a motorist, you cannot be sure to stay out of accidents - just because others are wreckless on the road.

    So owning a car in Singapore, and driving it is stressful. Not convenient. Why do I own a car than?? Because I don't wanna be amongst those buried underground should some maniac manages to sneak a bomb into an MRT train some day.
