Sembawang and Temasek losing $685 millions?

  • robertteh
    3 Apr 06, 14:02
    Is it true that Sembawang Corporation or Temasek again lost heavily to the tune of $685 million due to certain delays in vessel conversions.

    It is economic commonsense that all our decision makers running GLCs and Temasek have to have that uncanny extraordinary ability to feel the market directions as well as the strong hands-on implementation ability to contain quality-time-cost aspects of managing such companies.

    Have they ever learnt enough that in running Sembawang Corp and investing people's monies in such industrial and technology knowledge applications, all these key personnel have to posssess that business acumen equivalent to the best in the industry.

    Unfortunately, based on assumptions about scholars and academic talents too many GLCs are exposed to this kind of risks without anyone seemingly in control over such diverse major decisions in running the GLCs.

    Are the ministers going to tell us how they are going to safeguard our surpluses so that they are not wiped out by such poor decision making as seen in these innumerable fiascos.

    In the face of the latest investment losses by Sembawang and Temasek, it is time again to reexamine our past policy assumptions that our ex-civil servant highflyers are fit to run the GLCs.

    These people have been constantly praised as people who know how to make decisions.

    Ministers should tell the people whether they can still entrust the running of GLCs to the ex-army captains and all the ex-MPs and ex-ministers by pushing them to take up posts in these companies after retirements.

    Is the kind of naive underbidding as has happened in this instance under some form of psychological crucible test to see if these scholars might sacrifice shareholders and public monies just to secure projects of certain nature to win names and reputations. Or do they have the attitude that the monies belong to the public and therefore do not regard them as at risk. If they make wrong decision, do they have to take the rap as happened in the private sector.

    How do our engineering GLCs compete with companies like INTEL if Chip design is revolutionalised every three years with values multiplied per function increasing by 100 times over 10 years.

    If our MM/SM/PM do not understand such matters, being not trained to succeed in such businesses before how do we trust them to invest our monies in all the businesses with this kind of repeated mistakes.

    The EDB and finance minister have on records implemented many economic restructuring exercises since 1980s. What happened to achieving our target of value-adding and technology-based new economic model as announced? What have they achieved by way of new technology start-ups to catch up with other Asian tigers since the Asian Financial crisis and subsequent prolonged recessions of 1997-2002.

    What is needed badly is more practical implementation abilities and not all the scholar-managed GLCs for show. How will the scholars compete with China and India or are they mentally giving up and regarding them as too competitive and we cannot do anything to compete.

    Sadly, at every crisis, ministers are found to be in the habit of pushing away problems to the people with some finger pointing only at workers and not their won lack of implementation abilities. Workers have been subjected to all the relearning and retraining going on at SPRING and EDB all the past twenty years and yet the leaders are still slamming them for not doing their part. So who can lead better, workers who should try harder and perhaps not the leaders.
