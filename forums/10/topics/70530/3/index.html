    cookiecutter, I can see the efforts being done, however one interesting point to ponder is, why only now? Even in the heady days of the early/mid-90s, a lot of economists had highlighted the issues of cost as well as what they saw as overdependence on Foreign Direct Investments from MNCs in Singapore. They predicted that if we insisted on following the formula, the good days will not last that much longer as our regional competitors had a lot more going for them in this respect. And its not just the foreign analysts who say that, in a wide-ranging article published around the same time, the Singapore Democratic Party highlighted these points and much more, including the need for more entrpreneurs, education reform etc. It hardly rated a mention in the local press then, only for many of the same points to resurface (repackaged as government ideas) when "Remaking Singapore" was announced almost 10 years later. (Disclaimer: As usual, I'm not a member of any political party, just a Singaporean who believes in reading more than propaganda)

    Another thing which the analysts had noted was that a lot of the costs derived was from land costs which was set by the government. Absolutely nothing has been done in this respect when we're talking about cutting costs in the past few years, despite the slump in property prices, land in Singapore is still regarded as over-valued. And of course, this issue rates nothing more than a passing mention in the local mouthpieces. Before anyone starts going on about "land-scarce" Singapore, ask why are there so much unused office space, unoccupied HDB flats, unrented apartments. The government has no qualms about inflicting pain on Singaporeans to cut costs, yet it seems amazingly reluctant to inflict pain on itself for that reason.
