    It is typical LKY style when dealing with Foreigners, especially with the British which he has vast experience due to his years in Cambridge. His response will be quite different when the same question is posed in an Asian setting.

    It very much depends on the audience that his reply is intended, never take him completely at his words - especially from someone that is expert in moving goal posts.

    Singapore's arrival as a First World status was never an accident, not when Goh Chok Tong intended for Singapore to achieve a Swiss Standard of Living by the time he steps down - as was his stated goal when taking office in 1990.

    Other then the external facade of a First World - with the seemingly high standards of living as reflected in modern transportation, high quality of buildings, high standards of education, high quality of living conditions, and what else that are viewed superficially - we should also look deeper into the substance of the First World that Singaporeans are supposed to be enjoying.

    Are the substance of the modern transportation tangible and what costs do Singaporeans pay or absorb with their wages ?

    What price do Singaporeans pay for the Real Estate or Properties that they own ?

    What values and qualities has the seemingly high standard of education produced in the graduates from the various levels of educational institutions ?

    What price do Singaporeans pay for the seemingly high standards of living condition that we are supposed to enjoy ?

    How do all these factor into the Quality of Life of Singaporeans ?

    The First World Status that Singapore has been bestowed was a result of the LARGE RESERVES that the Singapore Treasury has accumulated, and which elevated the PER CAPITA INCOME of Singaporeans to be second in Asia when compared to Japan.

    However, the average PER CAPITA INCOME is not reflective of the average Singaporean income.

    Although the income has been regularly and annually pushed upwards, it is hardly based on any real productive output similar to the Swiss or the Japanese, both of which has a large capacity to be innovative and technologically creative and productive.

    Are we truly a First World material as matters stand, or are we living in a Virtual Reality of a First World - which like a house built from a deck of cards, seems to collapse at the slightest sneeze from the environment ?
