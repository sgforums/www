    Find other solutions that help make work pay better but don't sacrifice workers' CPF savings, says the Manpower Minister.

    Dr Ng Eng Hen was responding to a proposal by the labour movement to exempt low-wage workers from CPF contributions.

    It's among a list of ideas submitted by the National Trades Union Congress (NTUC) to the Ministerial Committee on Low-Wage Workers.

    Dr Ng said the committee, which he chairs, shares the NTUC's aim of raising workers' take-home salary.

    But exempting those earning less than $1,000 from CPF contributions would sacrifice the workers' long-term aspirations for home ownership, Medisave and retirement income.

    Besides, the government is not always able to help top up these workers' CPF savings, especially in years of budget deficits.

    Dr Ng was speaking to reporters after the launch of the 6th Singapore Learning Festival on Thursday. - CNA/ir
