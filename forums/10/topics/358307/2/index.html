    Another interesting issue with relation to hawker centres

    In Sengkang, a new hawker centre cum wet market is being built. The authorities invited tenders (new strategy) to manage the place.

    Kopitiam bid $500,000.00 per month to manage the place. Basically, means the authorities sit there doing nothing, and they get an extra $500,000.00 per month as rental from Kopitiam.

    Noting that there are about only 100 stalls within the hawker centre/wet market, the crazy bid above translates to a price of $5,000.00 per month for each hawker or wet market stall! That is the price of a stall in a coffeeshop in a good location!

    Residents wrote in to their MPs unhappy about this. If a rental of a stall is $5,000.00 per month, I think you know what sort of food prices you are going to get.

    The authorities responded back highlighting that the private firm should know what they are doing, and that if prices are too high, the market demand and supply will come into play. So residents living there should not be worried.

    Basically, what they meant is, they want to continue taking the $500,000 per month easy income, and they don't care what is going to happen to the residents. Let market forces decide.

    sigh...
