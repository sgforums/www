    Let me try to start properly.

    What my previous beliefs were - as I've said, predominantly idealism. The belief that people are good, that people will work together and co-operate. The belief that technology will solve our problems. That if you've got the technology, by all means use it.

The need for deeper context, the realisation that knee-jerk reactions are not helpful. The need to know more.

    2004. I have not yet found any solutions, if at all there can be any solution. But I will try to spread the word. And here I am.

    Now for the problems that will be happening to the people here, in this place, in this time.

    Hmm. Where do we start?

    Maybe we'll start where I started.

    Perhaps we'll start with a very simple calculation.

    Total up all your (or your family's) outstanding debts - housing loan, car loan, renovation loan, hire-purchase, if any.

    Divide by your annual household income (perhaps yours + your spouse, or if you're younger, your father's and perhaps mother's income).

    Multiply to get in terms of %.

    So it's :

    total debt / total income x 100%

    Got it?

    Now let's compare to averages. For the Singaporean national average, it's about 178%.

    What does this mean?

    One way to look at it is that it means that the average Singaporean will take 1.78 years to pay off all his debt if he puts aside 100% of his annual salary to pay it off.

    Of course that's not possible, so the re-payments are stretched over time.

    Is it healthy to be in such debt? No. Are most working-class people in debt? Yes.

    Is this a healthy situation? Probably not.

    What's a good number for this debt-service ratio? 0.00%. But few people have the ready cash to instantly pay up for the big-ticket items.

    The exact ratio to be "comfortable" with is highly subjective. For some people it may run up to 300%, 400% or more - pretty dangerous grounds.

    IMHO, anything over 100% is already quite shaky.

    From a personal, family debt issue, one then realises that hey, "I'm not alone!" It is a nation-wide problem. It is not only a Singapore problem, it's the same in many other so-called "developed" countries.

    From here on, it's not just "developed" countries, "developing" countries are staggering under debt as well.

    So are companies. Very large companies.

    The total amount of debt in the world is staggering. It is just as staggering for individuals as it is for SME's, MNC's, and entire countries.
