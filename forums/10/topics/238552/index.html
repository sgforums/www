    Are Singaporeans overpaying for HDB flats?
    Assuming I took a 200k home loan from HDB @ 2.6% interest for 30 years, I would need to pay $800.68 monthly. This would work out to be:

    Monthly Payment: $800.68 Total Paid: $288244.59 Total Interest: $88244.59
    Need to have CPF(33%): $1201.02 Monthly Pay: $3639.45

    At the end of 30 years, I would probably be left with little amount in cpf. Not to forget that cpf contribution from employers will drop when you are above 50.

    Assuming that the HDB flat appreciates in value and I sell it off, I would need to pay a levy to get a 2nd subsidized HDB flat or I can choose to buy a resale flat which most likely will cost just as much (assuming same flat type). It seems wiser to stay in the flat for 30 yrs and then try to downgrade when I am about to retire. However, will banks be willing to allow loans to purchase a 30yr HDB flat?? This could mean that I may not be able to sell off the flat and downgrade. The options left would be to take up reverse mortgage or pledge the flat to HDB for retirement payout.

    hmm.... any comments?
