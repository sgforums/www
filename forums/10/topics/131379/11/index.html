Every action and every consequence of the enactment of a law has its own spot on the Plane, so each has a definite amount of individual harm and social harm which it causes. Likewise, each person has a posiiton on the Plane too, accounting for the relativity of morality, since each person has a different position, and this point is called the person's Morality Threshold. The four corners of the Plane are: Suicide/Invisible Harm, World Annihilation/Invisible Harm, Skin-deep World Annihilation, Skin-Deep Suicide. None of the extremes are good. In fact, the ideal morality point is the centre point of the Plane. if an action or an effect is located between a Threshold and the Edge of World Annihilation, it is considered moral by the person in question; if it lies between the Threshold and the Edge of Suicide, it's immoral. We assume of course that the person fights for his/her own survival.

    The point of course is, how does the law determine what is a crime, and how does it weigh the strength of punishment?
