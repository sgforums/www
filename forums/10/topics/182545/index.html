    Chee Soon Juan claimed this was a political vendetta plotted by the ruling party (one of the PAP MPs is a NUS professor; Mr S.R. Vasoo). The PAP has however denied the accusation and insisted the offense was real. Chiam See Tong urged Chee to stop the accusations and get back to real business.

    However, Chiam was not supported by most of the party and the Central Executive Committee voted to get rid of him as general secretary and replace him with Chee. Supporters of Chiam quickly left the party and formed the Singapore People's Party in 1994.

    As if this was not enough, the party even decided to strip Chiam See Tong's membership in the party. Had he lost his membership, under the current law, he would also have to lose his seat in parliament.

    This invoked unhappiness among opposition supporters in Potong Pasir.

    Chiam took up this issue to the courts which ruled in favor of him as it was illegal to do so.

    Hence between 1993 and 1997, Chiam kept his seat in parliament officially as a SDP member. In the 1997 elections, he took over from Sin Kek Tong as the secretary-general (leader) of the Singapore People's Party.

    A defamation suit is the best way to declare your innocence and claim damages to right any discredit to your reputation. Legally, it is the only recourse to address a 'wrongful' statement made against you; note that i place 'wrongful' in inverted commas as the determination and presentation of the case can be warped to suit your own ends.

    The NKF was probably the one clear example of how legal defamation suits were used to con the public into thinking that allegations of funds mismanagement and unethical use of public monies were wholely 'untrue'. Of couse, tks to another GLC in SPH, all the lies were exposed and futher auditing by KPMG revealed the disgusting truth of a system built based on a similar platform as our Ruling Party.

    In such cases, of defendants vs the state or state-owned enterprise, it is the intimidating fear of david vs goliath. Money talks and in such cases, money buys you the best lawyers. And the margin for a fair and balanced trial is almost completely eroded the moment you cannot compete with a bottomless cash reserve of million-dollar ministers.

    Better to settle out of court then risk total monetary ruin, even if you possibly have a case.

    As such, how can anyone truly believe WHO IS TELLING THE TRUTH? Even if the plaintiff is completely and unreservingly exonerated?

    Who can trust a judgement or court settlement based almost entirely on financial pressure?

    As the saying goes: if you argue correctly, you're never wrong....
