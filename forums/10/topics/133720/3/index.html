    One of the earliest origins for surname Zhang was during the rule of the first legendary emperor Huang-di (Yellow Emperor) in Chinese mythology. Hui, the fifth son of Huang-di, was attributed to be the inventor of the bow -- an important weapon in the Chinese military arsenal -- and his descendants were bestowed the surname of Zhang (character representing to stretch a bow).

    For the surname Shen with the water radical, one early origin could be traced to the Ji clan, who claimed descent from China's first legendary emperor Huang-di (Yellow Emperor) in Chinese mythology. After the death of King Wu (Zhou Wuwang) in the Zhou Dynasty, young King Cheng (Zhou Chengwang) ascended the throne and his uncle Fourth Prince was appointed regent. The son of deposed King Zhou (Shang Zhouwang) sought revenge for his father and incited a rebellion against the young king. The rebels were crushed by General Ji Zai, and the general, who also happened to be King Cheng's uncle Tenth Prince, was bestowed the state of Shen for his meritorious deeds. Shen was conquered by the state of Cai during the Spring and Autumn period, and Ji Zai's descendants fled to the state of Chu, adopting the surname of Shen after their homeland.

    To make it up, maybe I could throw in a legend in Chinese mythology about Bo Yi, the founder of the Ying clan which was the origin of Huang I chose to quote:

    Legend has it that Emperor Zhuanxu -- the successor of the first legendary emperor Huang-di (Yellow Emperor) -- had a granddaughter named Nuu Xiu. One day, Nuu Xiu was weaving at home when a swallow flew in through the window and dropped an egg in front of her. Seized by an unknown impulse, Nuu Xiu used her mouth to catch the egg and swallowed it into her stomach. She miraculously became pregnant and gave birth to Da Ye, who was the grandfather of Bo Yi.

    The reason why there are many people from the Fujian province bearing the surname Huang could be attributed to one of the pioneer fathers of Fujian, Huang Tang. At the end of the Eastern Jin Dynasty, a major faction of the Huang clan led by Huang Tang, moved from Jiangxi to colonise and develop the relatively backward Fujian, and the settlement of many Huang families there may have resulted in Huang being a popular surname in that province.

    Jiang (representing river) shared similar origins with the surnames Qin and Huang (representing yellow) in the Ying clan. The Ying clan's origin could be traced to a noble Bo Yi who, as earlier mentioned, served the legendary emperor Shun. The state of Jiang was founded in the early years of the Zhou Dynasty by yet another faction of the clan. During the Spring and Autumn period, many small states were conquered and annexed by the dominant state of Chu and Jiang was one such state. The citizens of the former sovereign state then took up the surname of Jiang in remembrance of their destroyed homeland.
