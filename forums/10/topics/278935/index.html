    A budget deficit occurs when a government spends more money than it takes in.
    An accumulated deficit over several years (or centuries) is referred to as the government debt(or public debt). Public debt is the part of a country's debts that is owed to creditors who are citizens of that country.

    Public debt from excess borrowing from other creditors becomes the taxpayersÂ’ debts.

    Another thing that shouldn't be overlooked is the incentive behind clearing public debts. This doesn't apply to dictatorships like this regime though.

    Take the US for instance. Considering the fact numerous creditors (through the securities issued by the federal government) which the federal government (and its agencies) is indebted to are actually American firms, the need to service the national debt may not actually be as pressing. In truth, it might actually prove beneficial to the securities' holders that the government holds a sizable debt - through issuing securities - with such firms to stimulate economic growth and employment (through the prosperity of the creditors). A couple of Democrat presidential administrations (Franklin Roosevelt's public spending policies have its root in this) have actually utilised this principle in some form, shape or way.

    Ironically, the 'demise' of a debt issuer often benefits the holder (tangibly and intangibly) and this pretty much holds at both governmental and corporate levels. This is clearly born out by the fact that a couple of prominent financial institutions' vast improvement in performances (most notably Goldman Sachs) in the aftermath of the burst of the dotcom bubble in the late 1990's.
