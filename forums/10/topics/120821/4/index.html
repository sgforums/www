    Sec 22 and other sections have other responsibilities and/or power for the president. So I don't think out presidency is a zuo bo job. However, dont't leave out the '..in accordance to article..' at the end of many clauses, it shows how controlled and limited the president's powers are. That's legally speaking.

    Politically speaking, the president may not have much choices when it comes to approving budgets, bills or appt of public servants. This is, currently, the fact of Singapore politics. I think whether that will change or not depends alot on whether there is a collective demand from the people. Right now, I don't seem to feel or see much of that, but that's not to say the people is to blame. Sometimes the Govt. may set policies or make changes that naturally will arouse people to think that it has to do with politics or keeping PAP in power.
