StraitsTimes' uneven reporting of SGP issues - change needed

  • blueheeler
    23 Jun 07, 09:30
    Today's newspaper sings praises for S'pore's ranking as one of the world's top 20 'most liveable cities' (World section, page 40). Notice the many photos on that page, and the emphasis on the things here that helped S'pore win that accolade.

    A few days ago, S'pore was listed as one of the top 20 most expensive cities in the world for expats, and it was reported in a slim, photo-less column. There was little analysis about why things are so expensive here (S'pore is more expensive to live in than New York, and also S'pore was ranked 17th last year but 14th this year). I expect a S'porean newspaper worth its salt would launch an investigative piece on the rising costs in S'pore and the effects on its people and economy, but after what happened to mrbrown's column in Today newspaper (see http://blueheeler.wordpress.com/2006/07/07/another-sad-day-for-press-unfreedom-in-spore/, and http://blueheeler.wordpress.com/2006/08/21/another-cherry-on-top-please-pm-lee/), I have more faith in a reformed sex-addict in a wh-orehouse than our newspapers' ability to show all sides of the news and dig for answers.

    How I wish someone somewhere in the world would be kind enough to fund a non-govt-linked local newspaper/website here in S'pore (such as www.malaysiakini.com in Malaysia) so to give us fresh perspectives and alternatives to the local news currently dished out to us.
