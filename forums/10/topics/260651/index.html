    A Sinister Development
    The development charge increase is obnoxious in that again, the government is taking more money from the people. While ostensibly the developers pay, is there any doubt that the ultimate paymaster is the home buyer? Is there any doubt that the majority ultimately suffers?     The sinister aspect of the development charge is that it would have the effect of restricting supply by reducing the number of redevelopments of old estates or condos in en-bloc sales/purchases. Restricting supplies is never a good way to cool an over-heated market and in fact, has the opposite effect in the long run.

    Surely a better policy would be to ensure that all redevelopments increase the built up area, and increase the living space.

    It could even be multi-pronged. An increase in development charge if the living space is reduced and a reduction of the development charge if the developer plans to increase the living space after redevelopment.
