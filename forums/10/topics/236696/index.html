    It sounds like English but only those living in Singapore can know the real meaning.

    When you are paid "Peanuts" you expect $600K per year
    "Peanut" salaries must be $600K per annum and above
    The rest of the world considers "peanut" salary as equivalent to the cost of a bag of peanuts.

    "Help" is take, like increase in GST is to "Help" the poor (take from the poor)

    "Poor" can be rich, like increase in GST is to help the "poor" (help the rich by cutting Corporate tax)

    Foreign = Local and Local = Foreign when it is convenient like:

    Invite Foreign workers in so Locals have jobs (Invite foreign workers in so "Foreigners" have jobs)

    This thread is open for contributions of other words with uniquely Singaporean meanings.
