    It depends if there are any fine prints on the Home Page of the Website, and if there are any WARNINGS in those fine prints.

    Most defense against such 'cheap' attempts by websites screaming infringement of copyright laws, is that the fine prints are not prominent enough, and that the warnings are not prominent or clearly made known.

    Warning notices must be clear and prominent, especially in places that invite one to use the facilities; if none exist, or if it does exist but placed without due prominence or significance - and designed to be lost - it will be likely that one can get away from this charge.

    This is similar to past disputes between car owners and car park operator or carpark owner, over the insignificant warnings printed on car park coupons, or the non-prominent warnings at the entrance of car parks, in which the Operator and the Owner of the Building will disclaim responsibility of damage or loss of property from the cars that utilise its parking facilities.

    Incidentally, extracting prints from the Singapore Street Directory for other then private use is prohibited - as the copyright is quite prominently made in its early pages leading to the directory proper.

    You will be infringing on copyrights if you make any copies of extracts from the Singapore Street Directory for your wedding invitation card, company advertisements, sale of property, etc.
