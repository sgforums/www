    We need to reflect on the meaning of marriage.

    The purpose of a civil marriage is to facilitate two adults coming together to form a household. It involves the recognition of a relationship in the eyes of the law and allows the sharing of property and the disbursement of such property when death or divorce occurs. Traditionally, marriage is understood as occurring between a male and female.

    There is really no rational reason why same sex marriages should be ruled out, apart from homophobia that has its roots in religious thinking. Since a civil ceremony is, or should be, devoid of religious considerations, same sex marriages should therefore be allowed in Singapore.

    Before this can happen however, prejudice against homosexual relationships need to be eradicated.
