    They are talking cock again

    I don't think that singapore any different from any other part of the world. Moreover singapore electricity market is not as deregulated as most of the develped countries. The demand is very much stable and predicable.

    1) Most, if not all of the Natural Gas purchase for electricity production are on long term contracts, Take-or-pay contracts. So the producer of the gas can ensure a minimum income even if the gas is not used. Furthermore this contacts are sign over long term of 10 to 20 years. Future price increases for NG are taken into consideration in contract as a periodical increment of prices.

    2) If the generator sign this NG contracts, they already know before hand how much they are going to generate over the NG gas contract period.

    3) Singapore Power market operates on vesting contract, where retailers sign mid to long term contract with the generator; and pay the balance if they over use their quota; again this is to ensure fixed cost.

    So the increase in electricity tariff is rubbish.
