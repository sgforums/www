Will SPRING ever accept local management excellence?

  • robertteh
    5 Jul 08, 00:44

    Many problems have already arisen in ministry after ministry due to lack of hands-on process management to apply proper timely supervision to attain highest standards and results in their major or core tasks e.g. MHA, MINDEF, MND, MOM, etc.

    Solutions to the many problems encountered are simple. Just put up core tasks to be monitored daily and hourly and get them passed every day with all deviations tracked hands-on to the desired grades judged by the most senior with copies sent to the minister in charge.

    The sets of databases will ensure standards are kept to the highest possible and attainable. Such hands-on process management offers many facets of control, processing, comparison against prescribed databases which generate results.

    Such process management was sent to SPRING sometime ago but was turned away because it is a local product not of international repute to warrant another look.

    Ask SPRING whether it has something better today as compared with such process management capable of passing prescribed standards in the form of databases everyday?

    It is a shame ministry after ministry do not respect local management excellence even where it has already been proven successful in reducing the management fee from $1.00 per share value to $0.20 per share value saving for the particular environment huge expenditure while increasing efficiency as posted in another thread.

    Minister for HA should have no such problems at check points or courts or prisons in overseeing certain critical duties again once he is prepared to be humble to apply process management as introduced to SPRING in the public interest even if it is not his own or government's idea.
