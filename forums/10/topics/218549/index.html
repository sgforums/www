    Man hurt in public bus brawl
    Horrified passengers watch as elderly man battered in fight
    By Andre Yeo

    November 04, 2006

    IT was a sickening bus ride.

    In front of several horrified passengers, an elderly man was being battered by another passenger in an SBS Transit bus.

    The assault was so vicious that the man's spectacles broke and blood oozed from above his eyes.

    John (not his real name) witnessed the incident and called The New Paper hotline.

    He had boarded SBS Transit service 265 on Monday in front of the NTUC Fairprice outlet at Ang Mo Kio Avenue 8. He was on his way home.

    He lives in Ang Mo Kio Avenue 5.

    It was about 1pm. After he took a seat, he heard a commotion.

    The 22-year-old student said: 'There were about 15 passengers in the bus. They were mostly elderly.

    'I did not understand what the commotion was all about as it was in Hokkien.

    'I don't know who started it, but there was an exchange of words between a man in his 40s and the old man.'

    John said the old man, who looked to be in his 70s, was seated three seats behind the driver.

    VIOLENCE

    He doesn't know when the exchange of words began.

    Then it turned violent.

    John said: 'He punched the old man several times on his face until his glasses broke. His shirt tore and he started to bleed from the top of the left eyebrow.

    'His shirt had blood stains. The bus driver then separated them.

    'The younger man then left and the driver called for help on a radio receiver next to the driver's seat.'

    The other passengers then alighted.

    SBS Transit's director (corporate communications), Ms Tammy Tan, confirmed the incident.

    She said the bus was moving along Ang Mo Kio Avenue 8 when the driver heard a commotion.

    He pulled up by the side of the road to check on the situation.

    Ms Tan said: 'The bus captain (driver) immediately went to check on the elderly passenger who first declined assistance.'

    She said he then changed his mind and decided to seek medical help.

    Ms Tan said the bus captain immediately called for an ambulance which took the elderly passenger to the Singapore General Hospital (SGH).

    Said Ms Tan: 'A check with the police showed that the elderly passenger has yet to lodge a report with the police.

    'It is not possible for us to lodge a report given that our bus captain did not witness the alleged assault.'

    A Singapore Civil Defence Force spokesman told The New Paper they tended to the elderly victim who suffered a cut on the left eye brow and took him to SGH.
