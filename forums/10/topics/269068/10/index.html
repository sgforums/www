    For the benefit of everyone, please note that a professor is a title conferred by a university. A PhD holder, who chooses to lecture and perform research at a specific university is automatically given this title. There are several levels such as assistant professor (starter), associate professor (intermediate) and a full professor.

    A PhD holder not working at a university is considered a doctor in parenthesis only and not strictly a 'professor'. There are some exceptions to this: Professor S Jayakumar (Law Minister) and Professor Tommy Koh (Ambassor-at-large), who are both full-fledged professors despite not being 'employed' at any specific university. I believe they are under a special arrangement that permits them to retain their professorship in exchange for a minimum number of hours giving lectures and performing other duties etc.
