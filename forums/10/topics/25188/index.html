    Simply put, I feel the education system places too much emphasis on grades. The general perception, contributed by society at large, parents and the whole education system, is that if you don't score straight As and I do, that makes me a better person than you. Period. Nothing else matters.

    Education is not simply about grades, its also moulding the mind, the character of the young. The system imparts kids with knowledge for exams, teaches them how to spot questions for exams, and then promptly forgets about it after stepping out of the exam hall, so they can cram their brains with more material for the next exam. The system teaches us not to be thinkers, just followers. There must be more emphasis on teaching us to think, to solve problems, not to just do something because "the book says so".

    For example, in one of my subjects in my Aerospace Engineering course in Aust, we had just 3-4 lessons for it in the entire 15 week semester. 1st week, we were given an outline to a problem, to design an aircraft using a set of specifications on a single piece of A4 paper, plus a second A4 sheet detailing what aspects of the design to focus on and what to ignore. We're put into groups of 9-10, chosen by the lecturer instead of letting us choose our own groups with ppl we know because, in industry, we don't get to choose who to work with. The remaining 3 lessons we were not taught anything, instead it was used to do presentations on our progess(2 presentations) and the final presnentation. Lecturers from the entire department and selected industry figures were on hand to listen to us present our design and the floor was open to questions from anyone when a group finished its presentations. We had to design every aspect of the plane, from wings, landing gear, tail, cockpit, engine selection and draw out the design on CAD/CAM. Nothing was taught to us, we were told to use whatever we've learnt in our previous years to design. Lecturers were available for questions, otherwise we're on our own. Grading is done by a combination of your teammates evaluation of you and the lecturers reading of your final report. Can anyone see subjects taught like this in Singapore schools? I can't. Neither can the Singaporeans friends I've told this to.

    Finally, the education system I feel has to teach kids not only to become knowledgable, but also on how to be a good person. Grades aren't everything. Everytime I come back to Singapore for holidays, I see students from the so-called top schools, behaving like louts without consideration for those around them. And this, I'm afraid, is where the Singaporean education system has failed. All too often, we produce good students, but lousy people.
