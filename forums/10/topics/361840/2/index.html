    A lot of people also know that a part of the "good" performance is through capital injection.

     

    Someone from another forum mentioned how it works with a simple example:

     

    Assuming you have an initial amount of $1000 and you invest the whole amount into stocks.

     

    At the end of 1 year, you lost 50% to $500.

     

    At this point, you inject capital into your account with another say $1000.

     

    Your account is now $1500.

     

    Voila, your losses have been reduced to just 33.33% ($500 of $1500).

     

    If you inject even more capital, your losses is minimized even more, so you can safely tell people you lost a small x% of your capital.
