    From the strait times webby:-

    Opec won't raise output despite high oil prices

    BALI (Indonesia) - The Organisation of Petroleum Exporting Countries (Opec) has no plan to raise output despite record oil prices, the group's president said on Friday.

    'We have no plan to raise production further because the current rise in oil prices is due to non-fundamental factors,' Opec president Purnomo Yusgiantoro told reporters on the sidelines of an oil industry conference in Bali.
    Advertisement

    Mr Purnomo, who is also Indonesia's energy minister, predicted oil prices would fall below US$50 a barrel early next year, saying that elections in Iraq planned for January are expected to be smooth. Demand is also expected to ease in the Western Hemisphere during spring, he added.

    Oil prices touched US$53 in New York on Thursday - a new all-time high - due to tight supply and the perception that output in the Gulf of Mexico is still hampered by repair work due to Hurricane Ivan, as well as strikes in Nigeria and Norway.
