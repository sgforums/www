    There is nothing like a scandal involving porn films claimed on Commons expenses to focus attention on the pay and perks enjoyed, sorry earned, by our public servants.

    Even before Jacqui Smith, the Home Secretary, was forced to apologise for her husband's blue movies there had been calls for the whole system to be overturned. The committee on standards in public life which has promised to report on MP pay and expenses by the end of the year is under pressure to do something radical.

    Whenever MPs come under attack over pay they are quick to argue that compared to executives in the private and public sector they are not particularly well paid.

    With the G20 leaders in the country we thought it was worth getting a snapshot of how much the highest paid presidents and prime ministers around the world earn. For comparison's sake all earnings have been converted into dollars. It also shows basic annual salary only, not the expenses claimed on top.

    So, where does our own Gordon Brown stand?

    1. Lee Hsien Loong - Singapore
    Salary in dollars - $2.47 million
    Salary in local currency - S$3.76 million

    2. Donald Tsang Yum-Kuen - Hong Kong
    Salary in dollars - $516,000
    Salary in local currency - HK$4 million

    3. Barack Obama - United States
    Salary in dollars - $400,000

    4. Brian Cowen - Ireland
    Salary in dollars - $341,000
    Salary in local currency - €257,000

    5. Nicolas Sarkozy - France
    Salary in dollars - $318,000
    Salary in local currency - €240,000

    6. Angela Merkel - Germany
    Salary in dollars - $303,000
    Salary in local currency - €228,000

    7. Gordon Brown - UK
    Salary in dollars - $279,000
    Salary in local currency - £194,250

    8. Stephen Harper - Canada
    Salary in dollars - $246,000
    Salary in local currency - C$311,000

    9. Taro Aso - Japan
    Salary in dollars - $243,000
    Salary in local currency - Y24 million

    10. Kevin Rudd - Australia
    Salary in dollars - $229,000
    Salary in local currency - A$330,000

     

     

    -----------------------------------------

    Note: Their data is a bit old. Singapore ministerial pay has since be reduced a little, with our PM pay as $3.04 million

    http://www.salary.sg/2008/civil-service-bonus-2008-and-pay-cut-for-ministers/

    These are the new salaries:

    • President $3.14m (down 19%)
    • Prime Minister $3.04m (down 19%)
    • Ministerial Grade MR4 $1.57m (down 18%)
    • Entry Superscale Grade SR9 $353,000 (down 12%)
    • Allowance for Members of Parliament $190,000 (down 16%). Note that most MPs hold a full-time job and some are also directors in companies.

    Converted to USD at rate of 1.5 SGD to 1 USD

    • President USD$2.09m (down 19%)

    • Ministerial Grade MR4 $USD1.05m (down 18%)

    • Allowance for Members of Parliament USD$126,667 (down 16%). Note that most MPs hold a full-time job and some are also directors in companies.

    It is really unfair to criticize our politicians of "sucking" money from their people and leave them to poverty which is certainly untrue. Compare yourself to countries like Iraq where the late Saddam Hussein decorated his quarters with gold toilet seats while his people struggled to make ends meet. That is despeicable. However in Singapore, the story is different. Yes the lay person may not be very well-off, but we are not several from adverse poverty just like China experiences in some part of their country. Zimbabwe under Mugabe was a sorry sight too. We play our politicians well to encourage them to do a good job and be less prone to corruption.

    Ask yourselves, if the job does not pay well would you be motivating yourself to work hard to be a politician? Just because the source of revenue for the government comes out of your pockets, you are feeling sore about their hefty pay-packets. Why not complain that private corporations are raking in bonuses by the millions? Yes by comparison our country's politicians do earn several times compared to other countries but every country's political system differs. How much do you hear of corruption in Singapore as compared to overseas? Look at Chen Shui Bian or even those nominated to take posts in the Obama administration's treasury. Several of them fail to make the mark due to questionable tax histories and such.

    Our labour pool is rather small and attracting talents to join the government is no easy task. Ask yourself, if the pay of a politician is $400k (the equivalent of Obama's) whilst as a doctor you can rake in $600k. What would your choice be? Our pay has to be competitive compared to what other alternatives these people who choose to be politicians have. Dont be hypocritical when you say the politicians are greedy. Dont you wish you could have a bigger house, drive a bigger car or earn a larger wage? Arent these traits of greed? Greed and selfishness is everywhere a human phenomena, an innate nature in fact. Father of economics Adam Smith recognized this as a fundamental driving force behind the market system. By the pursuit of self-interest people promote an end (benefit to society)which was never part of their intention that makes today's market economies possible. To scorn justified pay packets is to condemn the market system and meritocracy. Ask yourself would you rather a system whereby your hard work does not merit an equivalent portion of monetary reward.
