    And yes soul_rage I agree that amidst the current financial crisis we do see the US government extending helping hands to their auto-makers and large financial institutions and hence you conclude that there is no true free market, which I agree to a certain degree. All I can say is that the market is like a kite. Leave it to fly until it runs into trouble then bring it back. If you restrict too much of its freedom, you cant see its beauty; on the other hand give it too much slack and it runs amok. Bhagwati once said that the 'invisible hand is flawed, but the visible hand is frail.' I agree with this thats why I suscribe to capitalists and meritocratic ideals. Nonetheless when really confronted with a crisis as large as this, the 'visible hand' may intervene on grounds that there is a larger picture to care about. It is debatable whether such mega-corporations should have been allowed to have such large socio-economic influence to begin with, but i believe the US govt is doing the right thing to give suitable rescue in light of a greater purpose to revive the economy.
