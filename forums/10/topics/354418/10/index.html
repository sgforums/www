    To soul_rage, you have good ideas in terms of running the transport company as a non-profit organization. The trouble is how are you going to ensure you align the objectives of workers, managers and such with what you are trying to achieve? The concept of principle-agent problem is happening all around us. From your realtor to the people you lead in your company, there is always a problem of aligning one person's objectives with the others. Realtors are paid a percentage of the sale value of a house to encourage them to maximize the value they help their clients get (this mechanism despite good, is still flawed but its not the time to discuss this). Monetary incentives, whether you like it or not is often what motivates people to do certain things, particularly in jobs. Which is why i am pro-competitive market system because it creates the right benefits in the right places to make sure the objectives of principle and agents are aligned.

    Similarly, if you are chairing the board of this non profit organization and are tasked with the necessity to achieve the above mentioned goals how are you going to attain them while maximizing your own utility?

    Keeping costs as affordable as possible for the general population

    This is a problematic statement. How are you going to know if the costs is already 'as affordable as possible'. It is imperative for you to set profit motives for your managers running the corporation for you in a way such that they would be motivated to keep costs down for you. If you run as a non-profit organization and pay your managers fixed wages, how are you going to ensure they go the extra mile to keep costs down when status quo earns them the same amount of pay? Alongside that, the company as a whole has no incentive to keep costs down because if they lower costs, their revenue goes down correspondingly. What good would they do them if they spend so much effort lowering costs and the benefits are accrued to the people?

    I know the above once again sounds callous and $$$ driven but it is true. This is why managing your staff in a company and getting the most out of them is such a problematic thing and a topic economists have revisited time and again to understand the mechanisms of office life.

    To speakup

    I quote from wikipedia a definition of non-profit organizations

    "Whereas profit organizations exist to earn and distribute taxable business earnings to shareholders, the nonprofit organization exists solely to provide programs and services that are of public benefit.
