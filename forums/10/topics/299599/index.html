    Foreigners, not babies, boom
    Marginal increase in births, record number of new S'pore citizens this year

    SLIGHTLY more than 30,000 Singaporean babies were born in the first 10 months of this year, just one per cent higher than the same period the year before, according to the National Population Secretariat.

    The 30,168 new babies are joined by a record number of foreigners who are calling Singapore home.

    The whole of last year saw 13,209 foreigners taking up Singapore citizenship and 57,310 taking up permanent residency. On Aug 19, Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong said: "Over the next decade, we have a unique opportunity to transform Singapore. Together, let us make this truly a City of Possibilities and a home for all of us!"

    This includes more foreigners; about 14,000 of them took up the invitation to call Singapore home. At the first-ever national ceremony on Aug 17, 155 foreigners swore allegiance to Singapore as they received their citizenship from Deputy Prime Minister Wong Kan Seng.

    The National Population Secretariat told Channel NewsAsia that it does not set targets on new Permanent Residents (PRs) and citizens. It added that "the high number of new PRs and Singapore citizenships granted could be due to the broadening of the criteria for citizenship applications in 2004, as well as the many economic opportunities that Singapore offers".

    Favourable economic conditions have been cited for the increase in the number of foreigners here.
