    Mas Selamat escape not only is a serious lapse of our security, it has long term implication on our security, safety and in foreign investment.

    Some of you forumites asked, what Selamat escape got to do with WKS?. Everything I said. Why?

    1) For those who have studied public governance, management, corporate affair etc, you will know this - accountability flows downward but responsibility flows upward.

    In other words, the Gurkha who failed in his/their duties should be accountable for his/their act but responsibility for such blatant, unimaginable incident should be shouldered by the leader. In this case, the authority, ie WKS or the PAP government. Why?

    WKS or the PAP government may not physically guarded over Mas Selamat. But they are responsible for the formulation, implementation, and most importantly, the approval of the ISD security plans. The responsibility should not lies solely on the shoulders of some ISD officer or Gurkha officer. The ISD officier or Gurkha are merely the executer and not the executive.  

    2) Because every month we pay millions of dollar for the PAP government to do a good job. We don't pay peanut but good money and we expect good return on our investment.

    To add salt to the wound, LKY appeared on the media to say we are not infallible. As if we are a bunch of kids, not knowing the better of it. Infallible. Why not infallible in the first place? Because they screwed up on the security in the first place.

    LHK also appeared to say "what to do, it has happened".  To give such statement is as good as not giving it in the first place.

    If we are paying them top dollars for the job, we should expect better than these.

    Is this all that our leaders are capable of doing in a crisis? Is saying sorry so hard?

    All the boosting about Singapore being number one in this and that, attracting top investment from here and there, are being unravelled and turning into thin air. INVESTOR WILL RUN IF MAS SELAMAT IS NOT CAUGHT. IT WILL BE WORSE THAN THE SARS AFFAIR.

    Yet no one has spoken out on this issue. The opposition is quiet, the public is quiet. Why? Are we so numb that we are scared of voicing issue –issue that concerns our very own life?
