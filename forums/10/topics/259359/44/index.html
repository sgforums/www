    Migration can mean taking up permanent residency or citizenship in a country.

    I wonder if you know the difference between those two.

    Permanent residency means you are not a citizen of that country but you get some benefits ( about 60% - 80 %of the citizens in Australia) and you cannot vote.

    Citizenship means you are a citizen of that country.

    Migration can go in the above two ways but it always has to go thru permanent residency first.

    You can't just jump into a country and expect to be its citizen in a month you land there - no country in the first world status allow that at all - NONE AT ALL.

    Permanent residency acts as a trial period - it is up to you whether you want to renew it, get citizenship or go back to your own country - you can even extend it if you prove to the country you migrated that you have been contributing in someway. You can even acquire citizenship after a said period of permanent residency .

    So some Singaporeans may migrate as permanent residents and stay permanent residents in a foreign country BUT THEY STAY SINGAPOREAN, while some may choose to take up citizenship there and surrender the Singaporean citizenship . And we have some who will choose to return to Singapore.
