    Singaporeans open to more foreigners coming to boost population numbers
    By Hwee Goh, Channel NewsAsia

    SINGAPORE : Singaporeans seem open to having more foreigners here to boost population numbers.

    But they do expect these people to sink roots and commit to becoming Singaporean.

    Advertisement

    Our reporter speaks to Singaporeans about a point raised in Friday's Budget that Permanent Residents will be encouraged to become citizens.

    It is all to do with a falling birth rate that is now one of the lowest in the world.

    Not enough of babies are born here each year, so it is likely the shortfall will have to be replaced through immigration.

    So the government said it needs to give PRs an incentive to become citizens.

    How that is done is not clear but observers say currently PRs have enough perks to keep them as PRs, so it may mean revoking some of these perks to make it more attractive for them to become citizens.

    BG George Yeo, Trade and Industry Minister, said: "I think we should continue to welcome PRs into Singapore really we need them in the economy. What we want is to make sure that citizens get a leg up over PRs, they are special, they are part of the Singapore core."

    Right now, citizens have the right to vote, enjoy housing and upgrading subsidies, and government CPF top-ups.

    They also have to do national service.

    First generation PRs do not have to do NS, but their children, who are also PRs, do.

    They also pay higher school fees.

    Locals did not seem concerned about these differences.

    "No matter any country you come is OK as long as you stay in Singapore and have babies and become Singapore citizen is OK," said one Singaporean.

    "Whether these people are transient or not I suppose now the society is so globalised that it does not really make a difference whether or not they are true Singaporeans so long as they blend in," said another.

    Analysts tell Channel NewsAsia that this issue is a new spin to the foreign talent debate - the government is saying not only do they need their skills, now they need their babies as well!

    Dr Ng Eng Hen, Acting Manpower Minister, said: "We are wanting babies for babies sake!"

    BG Yeo said: "We welcome girls marrying foreign men and their husbands becoming citizens. we welcome Singaporean men marrying foreign ladies and these ladies becoming citizens and hope their children will take up citizenship and do their national service."

    Tharman Shanmugaratnam, Acting Education Minister, said: "It's not just augmenting the population, it's about the quality of the city, you need a certain diversity, different perspectives, different backgrounds rubbing against each other, testing each other and motivating each other."

    More details are expected by August as a high-level committee deliberates just what incentives are needed for PRs to become citizens. - CNA
