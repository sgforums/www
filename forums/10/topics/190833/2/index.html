    What do you understand by that? Are the operating costs paid by the government and thus through tax payers?

    Unfortunately, the Singapore transport system IS ALREADY NATIONALISED when all the Eleven PLUS One Privately Operated Bus Companies were asked to shut down.

    These included : Singapore Traction Co, Tay Koh Yat Bus Co., Green Bus, Blue Bus, Yellow Bus, Hock Lee Bus, Almagamated Bus Service, United Bus Company, Paya Lebar Bus Co, Bedok Bus Co, Changi Bus Co, Jurong Bus Co. - which made up of 11 Chinese owned, and one British-owned bus companies.

    When these private bus companies asked the government for fare increases, the Government will insist that investments have to be made on new buses before any fare increases can be approved.

    This cause a 'chicken and egg situation, as to which should come first', which is a far cry from the present arrangements that seems to have LTA 'controlling' all transport fares increases.

    Without the assurance of fare increases, and being cash strapped, most private bus companies were soon required to close down; allowing for the Singapore Bus Services (SBS) to come into being, and subsequently followed by CCS, and TIBS.

    The present bus companies are all Government Linked, with their initial capital paid-up being made available from public funds, but under the guise of private or public corporations - some of which are public listed.
