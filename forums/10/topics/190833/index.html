    NOL and SIA have reported recently their lower earnings due to the higher fuel costs despite turning in better performance all round. The local transport companies should also be facing the same problems but should they be allowed to increase the fares again?

    Last year, they chose to increase the bus fares despite an increase in profits. This year, with the fuel prices skyrocketing and facing a loss in earnings, what are the chances of these corporations absorbing the cost? For sure, they are going to submit their application and the PTC (public transport council) will approve it.

    What i am trying to say is, in times of gloom and pressures from global conditions, we understand the need to increase fares to cope and sustain the business. But they have used up the goodwill by asking citizens to pay during good times for the purpose of profitability. This is PUBLIC transport, we are talking about, not a private-run enterprise.

    By the way, just do away with all the extravaganza and useless additions to cut down the operating expenses. What we need are just the basics, getting from point A to B in a timely and safe manner. The extra comforts are good if free but we never ask for it in the first place.

    By the way, will the PTC readjust the fares downward if the fuel cost goes down? Sadly, i don't think so. There is only one way for prices to go and that is up, Up, UP!!

    Annual Fare Review Process
    The PTC has put in place a framework to cap overall fare increases in small, regular steps. From 2005, the annual fare adjustment will be based on the new formula recommended by Government appointed Fare Review Mechanism Committee (FRMC) as follows:

    Maximum Fare Adjustment = 0.5 CPI + 0.5 WI - 0.3%

    Where

    CPI = Change in Consumer Price Index over the preceding year

    WI = Change in Average Monthly Earnings (Annual National Average) over the preceding year, adjusted to account for any change in the employer's CPF contribution rate

    0.3% = The productivity extraction based on a sharing of productivity gains achieved by PTOs
