    On 25 Sep 2007,
    Health Ministry unveils S$2b infrastructure masterplan
    SINGAPORE: Singapore's medical landscape is set to be transformed by 2015 with a S$2 billion infrastructure masterplan.

    Under the masterplan will be new facilities, including a new general hospital in Jurong and a centre to manage addiction problems.

    .
    .

    Health Minister Khaw Boon Wan said: "When we underestimate demand, the result is overcrowding at hospitals, as we now experience at Tan Tock Seng Hospital. If we over-estimate demand and over-supply, we end up with under-utilised assets - a costly outcome.

    "Between over-supply and under-supply, I would prefer to slightly under-supply than to over-supply as this will put pressure on ourselves to intensify usage and minimise over-consumption."

    And on that basis, the Ministry revealed details of its masterplan.

    The new Khoo Teck Puat hospital in Yishun will open in 2010 and more general hospitals are planned, including one in Jurong which will open before 2015.
    But today (6 days later):
    Hospital in Jurong on hold
    Tenders called now will mean higher building costs: Khaw

    THE hospital in Yishun will come up as planned in three years, but the Government will take its time to build the one in Jurong.
    Health Minister Khaw Boon Wan, speaking at a community event in Yishun yesterday, said rising construction costs in recent months would swell building costs of Yishun's Khoo Teck Puat Hospital by 10 to 20 per cent.

    He told reporters that in hindsight, he should have started work on the hospital two or even three years earlier.

    But since the Government had committed itself to opening that hospital by 2010, it would make good on its promise, he added.

    'But it does mean that for Jurong hospital, the western hospital which I am planning, I will take my time,' he said.

    There was no point heading into the whole construction frenzy when the tender price is too high, otherwise 'the high cost will eventually be borne by everybody, by society, by the Government and by patients', he said. [quote]
