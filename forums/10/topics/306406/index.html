    I just can't put my finger down to it, but I think just brandishing the sterotypical title branding people from China as possessing a certain characteristic or causing X social problems just isn't the way to go. Furthermore, can we really place the source of such instances of social dysfunctionality upon policy dictated by our government? Or should we consider a broader view, that the world is changing and borders that once secured comfort zones for whole populations of people are steadily becoming the thing of the past? We can't deport the Chinese, we can't just kick them out, it just does not work that way, eveyone knows that. A reactionist attitude towards this issue, which has been favored by certain politicians, would result in a backlash in our relationship with the main land and hence having wider implications for our purse strings. Rather, let us place the onus on ourselves, upon how we as a society deal with such problems.

    Recently my aunt, who slought her way through life, died suddenly. Within days of her death, a china woman took her place and as she did not leave a will, obviously the china woman reaped the fruits of her labor. I was disgusted and angry, as were my relatives for you can imagine the emotional hurt that all of us felt when we heard of the news. Naturally, we linked the situation to the larger picture, and many held the view that this was yet again another instance of china women being cheap. Hell, she was old enough to be his daughter. But that is not the point. I wondered, if there was stricter policy to prevent the entrance of chinese nationals, could my aunty's toil been rightly preserved? Or for a fact, was stricter policy even possible given the loopsided nature of international relations?


    I do not know. But one thing I know is that if we remove the veil of nationality and sterotypical characterization, there still remains the human instinct for survival based upon our own social situations. As such, we as individuals band together for a common cause, with a common culture should agenda to embalish such behavior as culturally unacceptable, for the survival of our tribe, for the survival of us as individuals. Brash accusations and brandishments just would not do, they are farcical and useless. Rather, if one from our soceity should embody such a transgression upon his or her own kind, he or she should be outcasted. Only with such a mental edge can we as a society preserve our diversiveness and social fabric.
