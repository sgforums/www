    Well, there's some truth about excessive exercise and stressful exercising on top of our daily stressful pre-occupation.

    While GETTING some form of exercise helps reduce fat and cholestrol in our system, it is also important to be aware of sress levels in WHATEVER we choose to do.

    I always ask myself before doing ANYTHING;"Am I happy with what I'm going to do? Do I really enjoy doing this? Do I really want to, need to, or instead, have to do this?"

    Note, 'want to' is very different from 'need to', and 'have to', in doing things.

    In 'want to', you are at your own liberty and freewill and are generally happy or pleased to do a certain activity, hence there's very little or NO STRESS at all. In 'need to', the activity you choose (or in this case 'required' or 'expected') to do, is something you cannot don't do. This automatically uncurs some degree of stress depending on what the matter is related to. But in 'have to', you really have no choice but to do a certain activity whether you like it or not, happy about it or not, and whether you can do it or not. This is utter STRESS.

    So usually, for exercise, it should fall under the 'need to' criteria, as all of us NEED to try and stay healthy or at least reasonably fit. But of course, there are people who 'want to' exercise excessively because they enjoy it, or are training for a certain purpose (such as bodybuilding) - this then although creates positive stress, is also harmful as well because excess in anything is damaging; our bodies aren't made to be continuously under strain.
