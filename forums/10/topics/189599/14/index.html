    In a legal suit, there is usually one right and one wrong. This is too simplistic. In real life, there is often no absolutes but a mixture of right and wrong with both parties contributing one part of the problem.

    Singapore's democracy framework ..eg its one-man-one-vote election system has been altered to suit the power in power to perpetuate its control and power.

    The basic and fundamental rights of citizens are in danger of being superseded by new meanings interpreted legalistically to suit narrow interest of the power that be.

    There is no more rule of law when the significance of justice, fairness and equity are being made less objective and more subjective. Innuendos have taken over as important part of evidence in defamation suits.

    Whoever is in political power will be able to interpret new innuendos as almost every utterances can be interpreted as innuendos. How does the judiciary going to go about in administration of laws and its legal system when we are fast reaching a point where distinction between right and wrong has become quite blurred.

    It now looks like the government can deprive citizens of their rights and entitlements for estate upgrading depending on loyalty to the political party in power. There is no absolute right or wrong now about citizens' rights too.

    So it would be quite farcical to carry on talking about mistakes in forms or who is liars etc as bigger mistakes committed depending on who committed those mistakes may not be mistakes but excused or justified on some shifting subjective ground.
