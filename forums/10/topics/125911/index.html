    ICA arrests China national with forged S Passes

    By Joanne Leow, Channel NewsAsia

    SINGAPORE : The Immigration and Checkpoints Authority has arrested a man from China with forged documents in his possession.

    He is believed to be part of a syndicate producing and selling forged S Passes to immigration offenders.

    An S Pass is a new category of work pass intended to meet industries' needs for middle level skilled manpower, especially specialised workers and technicians.

    This is the first time ICA has come across forged S passes.

    He faces up to seven years in jail and a fine.

    The forged S Passes had coarse and distorted Micro-line text when viewed under a magnifying glass.

    The colour tone and font size and type of the forged cards were also different from genuine S Passes.

    The Police said landlords should verify their workers and tenants particulars via The VERIFI and iCHECK services available at ICA's website: http://www.ica.gov.sg

    The Manpower Ministry has a system to allow the checking on the validity of work passes at their website: http://www.mom.gov.sg

    ICA officers have also arrested a Malaysian man at the Woodlands checkpoint when they found contraband cigarettes hidden in his car speakers.

    The total amount of taxes and duties involved is estimated to be some $1,600 - and the man faces a fine up to 20 times that amount. - CNA
