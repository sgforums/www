    In the good old days when the government looked after people, when you could keep less than $500 in your POSB account, and when hospital fees were heavily subsidised, the prime concern of hospitals was to cure sick people quickly and cheaply.

    Now, there is a conflict of interest between the patient, or client's interests, and the hospital's interest in making profit as a business. As such, hospitals may be tempted to give unnecessary expensive treatments and medicines to rich people, and to not treat people who cannot afford to pay.
