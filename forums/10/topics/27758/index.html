    A few random thoughts:

    In my opinion, officers decide WHAT needs to be done, and WOSEs focus on HOW it should be done. It is the exactly the same as the duties of the executives and the middle management of a corporation. Problems arise when an officer decides what should be done without stopping to consider if it can be done practically. This is very common because young officers do not think to consult their NCOs before making decisions. You get stupid plans that cannot possibly work and WOSEs trying to make them work "by magic".

    Units operate like families. The officer is the father and the NCO under him (PS for a PC, CSM for an OC, etc.) is the mother. For example, the father decides that the children need new clothes and gives the mother the money for it. The mother then scolds them, makes them eat their lunch, scolds them until they get on the bus, scolds them while they try on their clothes, buys the clothes, then scolds them all the way home. In the same way, the PC decides that the men need more marksmanship training and obtains time at the range, ammunition, medical support, transport, etc. The PS then fucks the men upside-down until they fall in, fucks them left-right-center all the way to the range, fucks them inside-out while they shoot, fucks them some more, then fucks them until they get back and clean their rifles.

    Most of the work of an officer is behind the scenes, like obtaining resources from higher ups (like ammunition in peace and artillery fire in war), planning and decision making. This work is "upward". What I mean by this is that the officer mostly deals with the people above him. In contrast, most of the work of an NCO is in the face of his men. His work is mostly "downward", dealing with people below him. Just like dad and mom.

    Hope someone finds these enlightening.
