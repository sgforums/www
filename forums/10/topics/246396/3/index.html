    Originally posted by OO_OO_OO:
    so if i go beat the fuck out of the guy who said that, the police wont take action too?
    Depends on the injuries that the other guy sustained. If it results in a fracture, emasculation, loss of sight/hearing, dismembering of any joints, permanent disfugration or any other criteria that would allow the case to be classified as voluntarily causing grevious hurt, sec 325 cap 224, then you will be placed under arrest.

    If not, then it will fall under voluntarily causing hurt, sec 323 cap 224. In which, you would know by now, would require a magistrate's complaint to allow the police to have full investigative/arrest powers.

    One reason why voluntarily causing hurt requires such a procedure is because it is needed to filer out the less serious cases from the ones which are more severe, but do not fall under sec 325 cap 224. As you can see, there is no in-between. Either you get injured real bad or you don't.

    And technically, voluntarily causing hurt happens all the time. Even if you were to playfully hit your friend on the arm, then that would be voluntarily causing hurt too. As would be a slap to the face.

    If an arrest is made everytime a call is made for cases of alleged assault, can you imagine what would happen? People will be saying cops jump the gun too early and are arrest-happy. And our lockups will overflow.

    However, if it happens in view of the cops themselves, then they have full powers to effect arrest.

    Assaulting a civil servant will only apply if the person is in the midst of carrying out his duty, sec 354 cap 224 [penal code].

    Using criminal force to deter a public servant from discharge of his duty.

    Whoever assaults or uses criminal force to any person being a public servant in the execution of his duty as such public servant, or with intent to prevent or deter that person from discharging his duty as such public servant, or in consequence of anything done or attempted to be done by such person in the lawful discharge of his duty as such public servant, shall be punished with imprisonment for a term which may extend to 2 years, or with fine, or with both.

    So, for example, if a student hits his teacher, this law will be applied. The law can be applied to other civil servants too, but the catch is that they have to be in the midst of carrying out their duties when they were assaulted.
