    The quota of women in medicine in NUS was started in 1979 due ot the fact that at that time, after women got their qualifications as a doctor, around 1 out of every 5 would stop work due to some reasons or another while for men, only 1 out of 20 would stop. Thus, it was deemed inefficient to award more quota for women as in the end Singapore would not have enough doctors coming out from NUS. So there was no discrimination.

    But be rest assured, this quota has been scrapped i believe this year because they found that the attrition rate between of women doctors have fell to an acceptable rate compared to men's rate.
