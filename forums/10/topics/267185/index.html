Proposed World Peace Day

  • robertteh
    10 Jul 07, 22:15
    For thousands of years mankind has been at wars.

    After the second world war, mankind should have learned the truth about wars, that is there is no victor.

    So whether a war is started by the need for national or economic security, greed or ambition, the second world war conclusively proved one truth - the world is too small to be engaged in conflict. It will be destroyed as witnessed by the release of the first atomic bomb on hundreds of thousands of innocent people in Nagasaki.

    But men are as obsessed today as ever with their own self-importance that they must prove something superior about themselves and not see the futility of wars.

    The Japanese people and their leaders are still far from remorseful about their own "Asian Co-prosperity" war. They try to justify their last war as a war of self-defence.

    Now earth warming, ecological pollution and depletion of oil and other natural resources are further bad news that the small earth we live in has only a limited life and even without war mankind has not many good years ahead to live for.

    So now let us examine and re-examine what the almighty want of us in this earthly life of ours. Did he really ask people to fight it out or engage in conquering others or our neighbours?

    One would have thought from reading of bible or koran that the clear god message : "Love thy neighbours" even if god at the same time has said other things like "Do not sin".

    Do denizens of the world have a higher or more noble reason to disagree with such basic wisdom. If they do then they have to tell us whether god was wrong about the first advice.

    If they treasure and trust their own almighty whoever he might be, surely they will be more patient with their neighbours even if they don't love them.

    Let us all declare as "World Peace Day" the anniversary of second world war and tell the Israelis and Palestinians on this day there is no purpose to fight on but there is god's purpose to live in love and not hatred.
