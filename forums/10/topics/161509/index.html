Similarities in Hotel New World & Nicol Highway Negligence?

  • robertteh
    6 Nov 05, 22:23
    The following are findings were given in "REPORT OF THE INQUIRY INTO THE COLLAPSE OF THE HOEL NEW WORLD" (DATED 16.2.1987:-

    (1) Building Control Authority (DCB, BCB and BAB) in charge ...failing to detect structural drawings and plans being prepared by unqualified person.

    (2) Neither RC calculations nor other RC drawings had been lodged with the Chief Building Surveyor as at date of certification by piling layout plan on 16.5.1968 and these were submitted only 7 months later on 12.12.1968

    (3) Based on his calculations, Dr. Buttling came to the following conclusions about the capacity of the piles of the building:-

    (a) The calculated ultimate bearing capacity for a single pile of an assumed length of 80 ft (24.4 M) was 400 KN. Since the RC drawings and the RC calculations show that the design load intended by the designer on the pile was 200 KN, the factor of safety for the piles was therefore 2.

    (4) The piles were arranged haphazardly.. One of the piles failed to pass the design load test at 400 KN with the panelists commenting that this pile might have been short-pile thereby compromising its carrying capacity.

    (5) The soil profile shows a layer of fill material of about 1.5 M thickness at the surface. Below this is a layer of marine clay which at the location of the building is about 2 M thick. ..A marine clay layer is about 18 M.

    It is very clear that the marine clay layers at Nicol Highway were very much more extensive and dewatering of groundwater for 30M deep open cut should have alerted DCB and BCB to check on the temporary shoring works to be constructed at Nicol Highway where the collapse occurred.

    When marine clays are so extensive and the open cut so deep, the surrounding road and bridges would subside and the subsidence should have been estimated so that bigger shoring structures could be designed to prevent total collapse of the roads and bridges nearby.

    For a smaller project like Hotel New World there were already so many structural designs, calculations and supervisions found compromised. Even piles were found to be haphazardly arranged and short-piled thereby compromising the original design load carrying capacity.

    One wonders why for major and much bigger works involving whole of surrounding roads and bridges and safety of public, the critical structurals calculations were not submitted to LTA and subsequently to competent departments (DCB, BCB) for structural clearance after the Hotel New World experience.

    Has the minister in charge at least asked for the objective pre-planning and calculation checks on safety aspects as he should know that contractor might not have acted in the interest of the public in order to save costs. Has Hotel New World report submitted to the President been debated by parliament or digested by the cabinet or has it been overlooked with no one looing into fuller implementations as mentioned therein? Obviously the RC drawings and calculations and certifications by QP have not been thoroughly checked by the competent department nor asked for by the minister and overlooked.

    The Hotel New World's front page report to the President stated:

    We have the honour to refer you to the Instrument of Appointment of Commission of Inquiry dated March 22, 1986 with the following terms of reference :-

    (a) to determine the cause of collapse ..
    (b) to make recommendations for such appropriate measures that can be taken to prevent a similar occurrence.

    Had Hotel New World's recommendations been taken seriously and all departments been made responsible to implement them with minister overseeing the implementation do you think there will be another collapse like Nicol Highway collapse killing the lives of several and at least one prior request for transfer and subsequent suicide.

    Should the minister be made responsible?
