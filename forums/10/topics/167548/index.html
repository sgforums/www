  • Gedanken
    7 Dec 05, 15:46
    I don't find any prolem with the death sentence per se, but what is it about its being mandatory that gives it more deterrent effect than a non-mandatory death sentence? Even if the sentence is not mandatory, if the case is proven the sentence will most likely be passed, so anybody planning to commit the crime will still know what they're in for.

    For certain crimes, the nature of the case pretty much determines if the convict's life should be forfeit. For example, there's no reason why someone who's murdered someone in cold blood should be given the benefit he knowingly denied someone else. Such a sentence may be passed if the death penalty exists but is not mandatory.

    On the other hand, there's more harm in an inflexible mandatory system than in one where judges are trusted to consider and accommodate for the mitigating circumstances. No system of law is perfect, and as such the only sensible thing to do is to provide leeway for dealing with cases that are not run-of-the-mill.
