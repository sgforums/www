    Have your say in Singapore's Budget 2007

    The Government is inviting members of the public to SMS their feedback

    Dec 10, 2006
    AsiaOne

    What do you wish to see in the 2007 Budget? Here's your chance to "speak up" and give feedback on what you want to be included in it - by simply sending an SMS on your mobile phone to 9 SpeakUp (97732587) from tomorrow to Jan 15.

    This is another initiative under REACH, or Reaching Everyone for Active Citizenry @ Home (www.reach.gov.sg), which was set up in October 2006 as part of the restructuring plans for the Feedback Unit to move beyond gathering public feedback, and to become the lead agency for engaging and connecting with citizens.

    The new unit signifies its renewed commitment to develop and promote an active citizenry through citizen participation and involvement, said a statement from the Ministry of Information, Communications and the Arts today.

    To guide the public in giving their views for Budget 2007, they can respond to the general questions listed below:

    What do you hope to see in the 2007 Budget Statement?
    What do you hope would be included in the GST off-set package?
    How do you think the Workfare payment should be distributed? For example, what percentage should be in cash and what percentage should go into CPF?
    How do you think the Government should alleviate the impact of the GST increase on the lower-income, on the middle-income?
    What is the ONE thing you hope to see in the 2007 Budget Statement?

    Users are encouraged to keep to a 160-character limit, including prefixes and spaces in the text of the SMS. They will get an acknowledgement note after the SMS is sent.

    Apart from SMS, members of the public can also contribute their views on the 2007 Budget Statement through the following channels:
