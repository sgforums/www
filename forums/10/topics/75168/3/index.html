    here's my perspective. i graduated from sec sch last year. decideding whether to go to a good poly course i've been eyeing for a long time, or to a good jc was a real pain. i eventually went the poly route. reasons being, in poly, i learn what i liked, and felt could do better. poly is not as stressed and high paced as a jc. with a diploma, i could go and work immediatly after ns or graduation, and save up money for a degree overseas.

    i spoke to a few of my r/c flying buddies. they're all managers in their own respective trades. they were telling me they'd rather hire a diploma holder than a degree holder. i was told that, in their eyes, both are almost equally qualified. the only reason is that degree holders cost more to hire and are typically more "big-headed". yeah i know this is only a small percentage of ppl, but it gives you an idea of what some ppl think nowadays.

    all along, my father has always told me to go to a jc, as it is better. recently, i met up with some of my old classmates. they kept telling me that they could not cope with their jcs. i asked if they considered going to a poly. they simply said, no choice, parents force me to go jc. kinda feel sad for them to suffer for their parent's outdated mindsets.

    and the case that good people go to jcs only is not true. in fact, its the opposite. from what i see in my school, many ace students choose to go to a poly(including my gf and me). most who go to a jc are those whose L1R5 is at the 18-20 range. their reason was mainly because with their points they could not enter a poly choice of their course, so they went to jc. even the course i'm in have many many single-digit scorers.

    in my personal opinion, polytechnics are not a class below jcs. they are in fact on par, and in some cases, better than a jc. the main reason some ppl still think so is due to outdated thinking. no doubt that a degree would certainly be better than a diploma, but going to jc doesn't mean an easier route to uni.(only 75% of middle-level jc students make it to uni, from JAE statistics).
