    Kayveas takes a pot shot at Gerakan

    New Straits Times

    Fresh from his war of words with the MIC, Datuk M. Kayveas has now turned his attention to Gerakan.

    The PPP president accused Gerakan of "playing to the gallery" in proposing a merger of all 14 Barisan Nasional component parties.

    He said he agreed with Prime Minister Datuk Seri Abdullah Ahmad Badawi that parties in the BN were not ready for a merger.

    "When the PPP proposed a merger with Gerakan a few years ago, it disagreed.

    It can't even merge two parties and yet is suggesting that the BN components become one party," he said at the prime minister's and Muslim ministers' Hari Raya open house on Saturday.

    Kayveas was commenting on Gerakan Youth chief Datuk Mah Siew Keong's suggestion last week that all 14 BN component parties be consolidated.

    Kayveas said if Gerakan was serious about a merger of the 14 BN parties, it should lead the way by making the first move.

    "If Gerakan can start the ball rolling, people will see it as a do-able concept."
