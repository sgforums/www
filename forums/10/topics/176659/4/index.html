    2 Feb 06, 16:16
    Originally posted by Gazelle:
    talking about HDB, what I dont understand is why is there stil a household income ceiling of $8000?

    When HDB first started the Homeownership for the people scheme. it knew its role as a government housing authority and knew that it task is to sell public housing at a low affordable rate of $6,500 for 3-room standard flat and $7,800 for 3-room improved flats in the early 1970s.

    Later after the government started talking about high pays for ministers it has conveniently forgotten that it was a public housing authority and began to raise the price of 3-room flat to $150,000.

    At such inflated price, HDB was no longer selling low-cost housing but it has kept to its past low-cost public housing income ceiling condition.

    How ironic that HDB cannot even sell off its stock of flats any more except through the resale market way of auctioning or tender it is still sticking to the old income ceiling and low-cost housing conditions.
