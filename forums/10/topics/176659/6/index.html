    Classic Singapore Ills

    ===============================
    In her 40s, back with a degree - but no job

    Feb 3, 2006
    The Straits Times

    I WAS elated to be able to join my family for the recent Chinese New Year reunion dinner after missing the past two years' to pursue a degree in Australia.

    Although I am happy to be back in Singapore, I faced an uphill task re-entering the workforce, due to my age. I am in my 40s.

    Employers are reluctant to recruit older candidates because they believe such workers are not healthy and would cost them in medical expenses. I find such discrimination grossly unfair.

    As the population ages, Singapore needs to keep older workers employed for longer. Older candidates should be assessed and recruited according to the same criteria as younger ones.

    If this issue is not addressed soon, older job seekers would become disillusioned and leave Singapore to take up jobs overseas. It would be a shame to lose talented, able and healthy workers to other developed countries due to this age discrimination.

    Why should we be penalised when we have taken up the Government's challenge to upgrade our skills and qualification?

    Sharon Lee Soh Leng (Ms)
