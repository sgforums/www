    Firstly, who is in charge of Singapore to have caused this "massive techno-economical jam" ?

    Secondly, does it takes any "guts to implement new tech" - or does it take entrepreneurial vision and business acumen to chart new directions ?

    Thirdly, how did the stupendous conclusion come about that it is "the people expects spoon-feeding in learning new tech" ?

    Was it not due to the lack of "guts to implement new tech" to cause the "massive techno-economical jam" in the first place ?

    Should the people make the first move to learn new tech - when there is no "guts to implement new tech" ?

     

    After the economic downturn or crash of 1987, 1997, and again during 2002 through 2004 - a total of three Economic Review Committees were formed, and each of this Committee had made recommendations to reduce the huge amounts of contributions to the personal accounts in the CPF, so as to release the entrepreneurial energy to make Singapore less dependent on the big corporations, and have a second tier economic-industrial network that will provide employment for the majority.

    Singapore's economy although seemingly a capitalist model - is no better then a modified form of 'command economy' with direct government policy interference in determining national economic directions - that see the government hand moving resources into directions that it sees as the future.

    The government involvement get even deeper when it participate in direct competition in the market place of entrepreneurs, by using public money to set up Government-Linked Companies to compete with private entrepreneurs.

    Can the government be successful entrepreneurs ?

     

    Are Singaporeans unwilling to learn new tech ?

    Or has it not been a fact that the PAP Government treat Singaporeans as human resource - to be manipulated and directed into directions that will meet with the vision and goals set up by the Government ?

    How often has it been said by applicants to the various tertiary institutions that the course that they applied for had insufficient seats available, but there was an unusual large number in another faculty to produce "the manpower needed by industry" ?

    If Singaporeans need spoon-feeding - does the commentator count itself to be a Singaporean - and similarly need to be spoon fed to see the situation that is so clearly obvious ?

    THE FRENCH FOREIGN LEGION

    ENLISTMENT REQUIREMENTS


    • to be between the age of 17 and 40 years old (Parental or legal tutor authorization is required for minors),
    • to hold a valid official identity card,
    • to be physically fit for duty wherever he may be needed,
    • knowledge of the French language is not necessary because it will be acquired during the contract.
    Enlistment Procedures:
    The first contract is for five years

     

    To enlist in the French Foreign Legion, all volunteers must present themselves in any recruiting office in France. Travel costs and the eventual obtention of a visa are borne by the candidate and no help whatsoever can be accorded by the French Foreign Legion.
    If successful, after preliminary medical check-ups, the candidate will be transfered to the selection center of the Legion Headquarters in Aubagne (15 kms from Marseille) where he will undergo medical, IQ and physical fitness tests.
    If the candidate is successfull in passing the selection, he will be asked to sign a five years unconditional contract to serve wherever the French Foreign Legion needs him.
    The unsuccessfull candidate is immediately returned to civilian life to rejoin his country of origin without any financial aid.
    The Initial Contract
    For four months the young legionnaire will receive a basic military instruction at the 4th Foreign Regiment located in Castelnaudary after which, he will be posted to a regiment, depending on his capabilities and the needs of the French Foreign Legion.
    Promotion through the ranks will depend upon his physical capacities as well as his IQ, service record and leadership abilities.

    1. LUC LEGER - A rhythmic race, alternating 20 meters in 1 minute and 20 meters in 2 minutes (Make at least 7 landings).

    2. PULLUPS - make at least 4 pullups

    3. ROPE - Climb 5meter rope at least once.

    4. ABDOMINAL MUSCLES - do at least 40 situps
