    Top public sector officers to get 4% to 21% pay rise in January
    13 December 2007 1838 hrs (SST) 1038 hrs (GMT)

    SINGAPORE : Top public sector staff will have a second round of salary adjustments on January 1 next year. Their annual salaries will rise between 4% and 21%.

    The group, comprising of 351 people, includes administrative officers, as well as political, judicial and statutory appointment holders.

    This exercise is a continuation of the government's announcement in April this year to adjust salaries in phases for the public sector. The pay will also be more closely tied in with individual performance.

    Salaries in the public sector have lagged behind those of the private sector, despite the first phase of adjustments announced in April. To make public service pay more competitive, further revisions will be made based on two salary benchmarks.

    Those under the MR4 benchmark, such as entry level ministers and senior permanent secretaries, will see their annual salaries increase by 21% to S$1.94 million, comprising a 14% increase in monthly salaries and a rise of some two months in performance bonuses. The revision will include a 7% imputed pension for those at the more senior level.

    Public service officers under the SR9 benchmark for entry level Superscale Grade will also see their annual income go up by 4% to S$398,000 to come up on par at 100% of the benchmark.

    Allowances for Members of Parliament will rise 4% to S$225,000 a year since their packages are pegged to the entry Superscale Grade.

    Salaries of those in the MR4 grade are pegged to two-thirds of the median salary among the top eight earners in six professions, namely multinational corporations, bankers, lawyers, accountants, local manufacturer and engineers, while the SR9 grade is pegged to the annual salary of the 15th top earner, aged 32 years old, in the same six professions.

    The Minister-in-charge of Civil Service and Defence Minister, Teo Chee Hean, said in a statement that the latest adjustment follows the April announcement.

    He said the public sector salaries move with the market and that private sector salaries have increased significantly. Mr Teo said the civil service needs to follow suit in order to attract and retain good people.

    The average resignation rate for the Administrative Service for the last three years was 3%.

    This year, 13 officers have left the service to date. This brings the resignation rate to 5.5%.

    The Public Service Division said the attrition rate has risen but has not reached serious levels.

    But it added that it should be proactive to retain able officers in the public service

    These revisions will cost the government S$27.6 million a year, of which S$17.5 million will be for the political, judicial and statutory appointment holders.

    The Public Service Division said the latest changes will help public sector pay reach the stated target of 77% of private sector benchmarks by the end of this month.

    The government said it will monitor the benchmark movements and make further adjustments by end-2008 to 88% of private sector benchmarks.

    Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong had said in April that he will donate his wage increases for the next five years to charity.

    Mr Lee will donate $500,000 to charity next year. This is based on the difference between what he would have received in 2007 and the salary level before the April 2007 revisions. The same principle will apply for his earnings in 2008. - CNA /ls
