Should we have an Anti-Age Discrimination Law

  • relaxx1corner
    16 Jun 04, 17:34
    Australia has just passed the law whereby employer cannot discriminate on the basis of age. Should Singapore passed a similar law too?

    Employers will no longer be able to discriminate on the basis of age under laws passed by Parliament yesterday.

    Labor and the Australian Democrats yesterday backed the Age Discrimination Bill 2003 in the Senate despite the government rejecting their amendments in the lower house.

    Under the laws, it would be illegal for employers to discriminate on the basis of age.

    Access to goods and services, including health and medical services, education and accommodation would also be protected.

    The Human Rights and Equal Opportunity Commission will also be given powers to deal with age discrimination complaints.

    The government rejected a number of amendments including the extension of the laws to cover voluntary work and the setting up of an age discrimination commissioner.

    Brian Grieg (AD, WA) told the Senate it was disappointing the Government had not backed the amendments.

    "It's long overdue in Australia and we've taken the view that it's better to have a weak prohibition against age discrimination then to have none at all," Senator Greig said.

    Advertisement
    Advertisement
    Joe Ludwig (ALP, Qld) called on the Government to revisit the laws at a later date.

    "Labor's amendments would have given younger and older people greater protection in the workplace," he said.

    Parliamentary secretary Judith Troeth said the amendments were unnecessary.

    "The Government's objective is to encourage and foster attitudinal change without imposing unnecessary and unrealistic restraints on decision making in business and other sectors of public life," Senator Troeth said.
