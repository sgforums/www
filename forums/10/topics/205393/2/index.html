    Whatever infrastructure that is built, the hardware that is put in place will need an efficient software to operate this hardware to a high standard of practical benefits.

    Building the canal at the Istmus of Kra in Thailand has been a 329 year old dream - since first brought up by an intelligent Thai King to link the Gulf of Thailand on the East, with the Andaman Sea on the West.

    Unfortunately, the construction will be mega dollar project that will only see benefit for the oil trade, and its delay was due to the inexpensive oil that did not provide any incentive to spend on this mega-dollar project.

    However, with current oil prices soaring to levels not seen before in the last fifty years, there will be some serious rethinking from major beneficiaries for a shorter route that can accomodate larger vessels carrying oil - that is currently restricted in traffic volume by the narrow Malacca Straits.

    The construction of this canal at the Isthmus of Kra will require a project time frame of at least forty yeras.

    Can Thai Politics sustain a venture of this duration, and not stretch it further in time and in higher costs over-run ?

    Even after the canal is built, will Thai efficiency and disciplined industriousness make the canal operation to be smooth, safe, and speedy for the super large tankers that will be envisaged to carry a larger volume of super-high priced oil ?

    All these said, we will need to tone down our own smugness and find out the reason for Maersk returning to Singapore, having walked out of Singapore and after taking the major stake in Tanjong Pelapas Port in Johor.
