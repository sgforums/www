    Without telling the people back in 1970s, a certain Goh Keng Swee doctrine was implemented quietly which assumed that by pushing up salaries and prices Singapore will forcibly price out the lower grades of industries and upgrade to value-adding higher technology ones. Under that doctrine, it was also assumed that by pushing up HDB flat prices to get people mortgaged to their property for 30 years, Singaporeans will be kept to work harder to sustain Singapore prosperity.

    This doctrine has probably backfired causing high costs without corresponding upgrading of jobs or industries. The high cost part of the doctrine was probably auto-piloted without anyone checking it or making correction thereby causing untold problems. It is too big a policy to be easily checked by anyone.

    The result today is Singapore has lost all the economic competitiveness in the domestic sector of the economy granted that foreign investments and GLCs might still do well because they can charge high fees like SPH and SBS and recover all costs under one power of monopoly or another.
