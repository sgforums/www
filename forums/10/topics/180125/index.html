It is federal because each individual state has its own state government with some significant degree of autonomy, answering only to the central federal government only on major issues such as foreign policy and defence. It is a consitutional monarchy because the head of state is the King (Yang di-Pertuan Agong) whose seat is rotated between the various Sultans of each state.
