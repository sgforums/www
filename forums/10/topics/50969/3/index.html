    New Straits Times (Oct 1, 2003)

    At The Dewan Rakyat: RM2.5m for water ads well spent, says Toyad

    Oct 1: THE RM2,513,404.72 spent by the Government via the National Economic Action Council on advertisements on the Malaysia-Singapore water issue has resulted in more people being aware of the disadvantaged position Malaysia is in.

    It has also been successful in garnering support from Singaporeans and the international community alike, Deputy Foreign Minister Datuk Dr Leo Michael Toyad said in a written answer to a question by Datuk Wan Junaidi Tuanku Jaafar (BN-Batang Lupar).

    He said the advertisement campaign from July 13 to 18 gave the people a true picture of Malaysia's situation against the baseless allegations from Singapore.

    "Many parties are now aware and understand our position and our rights," he said.

    He said the Government had on July 30 decided to refer the dispute to a tribunal, which would be in accordance with Malaysian laws, and not to the International Court of Justice.

    He said this was specified under the 1961 and 1962 water agreements entered into by both countries.

    He also said the Government had taken steps to stop the purchase of treated water from Singapore.

    "The Johor Government is now building a water treatment plant in Semanggar and water pipelines to connect the plant to consumers, scheduled to be completed by the end of next year," he said.

    He also said the proposed bridge project to replace the outdated Johor Causeway was to accommodate the rising number of vehicles.

    "The bridge will ease traffic flow and also open the waterway in the Straits of Tebrau which has been blocked all this while." Dr Toyad said a portion of the Causeway would be demolished and the new bridge built in a curved design to allow small ships to pass.
