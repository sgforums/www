    For a parliamentary system, or democracy to work efficiently and effectively, there has to be safeguard against tyranny by those in power through legalistic controls and gerrymandering. Of the three realms of government, now only the executive has power with judiciary and legislative playing second fiddles.

    This aspect of our governing system is an undesirable part of our system. This part has to evolve to a more satisfactory balance and check system.

    In our case, the basic laws relating to the proper coordination and smooth functioning of our democracy or parliamentary democracy has been overriden by a strong executive through all kind of legalistic gerrrymandering of existing laws which effectively render the constitution a dead document.

    Now the executive is the over-domineering branch of government which runs all areas of the government to its wishes and desires to serve the narrower agenda of the party in power. The system is losing its objectivity, accountability and transparency. People are losing their rights to offer views and opinions resulting in many problems.

    In our system, the various parties are not working together but are contending with laws passed by the more powerful executives. We have no way to check on gerrymandering of laws from GRC to Sedition Act and self-approval of ministers' inflated salaries and retirement gratuities. More are coming like upgrading of HDB estates priority to citizens who are loyal to the government.

    Under this sort of circumstance, it is not quite a good idea to give the leaders additional powers to appoint outside parties to hold executive powers like the MMs or SMs. There will be more gerrymandering with more powers to the few in power leading to corruptions and nepotism which is the greatest danger of all.
