    I think it is good to encourage our students to study hard and ultimately receive uni education wherever one can and wherever financial means permits.

    One should always improve himself or herself educationally. Better still go for lifelong education.

    However, university education or degree-acquisition education, should not become blind pursuit as there exist in life many more challenges.

    In the past, the educational system appears to be too academic and general aimed at passing examinations with the highest grades without being too concerned with applying what one has learnt in works and business.

    Students try to go to uni to acquire degrees e.g. laws, business adm, accountancy etc without regard to future needs in job market or our economic competitiveness.

    It will be wasteful to educate the population in this manner - education for education or degree sake.

    Many working adults today still believe in blindly educating themselves to obtain degrees for their own sake with many turning to uni to acquire degree just to have better self-esteem although they may not even need those knowledge to be more successful in whatever they are doing.

    The relevant question to ask of our educators should be: do we equip students with knowledge that they can apply after the uni? Should our students be equipped with implementation of their learned or acquired knowledge in works or business start-ups ? Judging from the examination questions and syllabuses it will be found the education is mainly concerned with passing of examinations rather than relevance of competency in works or applications.

    We are a Confucian society no doubt and as parents we usually wish our children to go for education and proud to have our photographs taken of them wearing the mortar boards.

    Yet, the greater challenge in the new technology age, is not about education for education sake or paper chase but how to compete in technology application to raise standards of living.

    That is why I have posted to Feedback Unit since 2002 against such blind pursuit of academic general education to broad-based practical learning where knowledge learned can be applied even in class to stimulate interests in practical application in real future jobs and businesses.

    Broad-based education system is being practised by Finnish and Swiss people equally smaller countries, which has benefited their people excel in wider areas of "Knowledge application approach".

    Some ministers have gone to these countries recently to try learn about broad-based education and knowledge application. Have they anything to report or recommend? What changes can be made to upgrade our academic general education to broad-based education approach urged by the people.

    If we are too slow to change we might soon find that we are producing more and more highly or overly qualified people who become unemployed. Such look back to the last two deep recessions, to see how hopeless was our education.

    If we want more students to become our future knowledge workers then we have to start at "O" level or even primary level. Then we can say our future generation will be able to start up new technology applications.

    I hope our EDB will not keep talking about attracting foreign talents or producing more scholars. It should holistically go for knowledge application, researches and innovation so that the students are ready and well prepared to seize opportunities in all these challenges.

    I think we should educate people for ther future jobs or business start-ups through practical applications and innovations and not just lip service.

    Our ministers have not done enough in such areas of knowledge application aspects. It is time to find new challenges and open up and empower the masses.

    More need to be done to open up new opportunities through knowledge application and empowerments of the people.

    There seems to be restraint or conservativeness in seeking new ideas and challenges to make greater progress. Our ministers' constant talk to build a value-adding and technology-driven economy will not happen until there is real change in education.

    We ought to have more action- and result-oriented ministers who are able to make quick changes to solve fundamental problems caused by past policies which are mainly too narrow in conceptions and not open to mass participation. The new ministers need to be nimble to seize new opportunities in technology and knowledge application fronts.

    The ministers in charge of EDB, MOM, MOE. MTI should be action-type of ministers and try to justify past policies and become more rhetorical and conceptual. So far very little has been made to upgrade the economy after so many economic restructuring exercises.

    It is no use trying to appear on TV to outtalk or outwit journalists or students to gross over shortcomings.

    It will be counter-productive trying to control the younger generation in their interactions in blogs with all sorts of top-down conceptualisation of sedition act.

    Try new venues to get people more committed to building social and cultural vibrancy which will better stabilise our country.

    Or else the stability will be at stake. It will be paranoid to keep worrying about people's criticism of government policies or wrongs. By starting on knowledge application approach in education government will learn better how to empower people to make a fresh good start. Have confidence in your leadership to open up and all will be well.

    In the past leaders have been talking about concepts: (a) FT (b) investment creation (c) academic education (d) meritocracy. So far our leaders are losing in big ways in empowering and motivating people.

    Today we need to identify and get people to accept greater challenges: (1) promoting practical knowledge application at all levels and (2) innovate and prepare through more broad-based practical education to create new technology start-ups like what the Taiwanese did with the promotion of IT back in the early 1970s.

    We have produced many scholars who have become yesmen in cabinet not knowing how to come up with overall plan to enable the average citizens to compete and do well.

    During the period of my work in various fields, I have found that many engineers have forgotten their formulas or knowledge due to lack of practical application.

    I have found many non-graduates are able to excel in what they are doing through exposures in applying knowledge even in one or two specific fields of whatever they have learned. There is no reason students cannot be better schooled and trained through knowledge application training to prepare them ahead of life.
