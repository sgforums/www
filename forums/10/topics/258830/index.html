    S'pore starts new LKY water prize

    PAYING tribute to Singapore's founding father, the PUB, on Monday, unveiled the Lee Kuan Yew Water Prize.
    To be awarded in March next year, the inaugural award, recognises individuals or organisations who have made a difference to humanity by helping to solve the world's water woes.

    It will be the highlight of the city state's first International Water Week - a series of events featuring the indusry's heavy hitters, showcasing cutting edge technologies and water management techniques.

    As Singapore's 'contribution to develop and encourage' innovative water solutions, the $300,000 prize is not limited to breakthroughs in technology, but is also open to groundbreaking policies and programmes across the globe, said Environment and Water Resources Minister, Dr Yaacob Ibrahim.

    Dr Yaacob was speaking at the opening of the IWA (Internation Water Association) Leading Edge Conference at the Swissotel on Monday morning.

    With its focus on cutting edge technologies, the prize will be a 'unique contribution' to helping spur new innovation in the field, said IWA executive director, Mr Paul Reiter.

    The three-day conference which ends on Wednesday, brings together some 300 delegates from over 40 countries and includes some of the biggest players in the global water industry.

    'It's a gathering of the best of the best,' said Mr Reiter.

    Among them is Professor Perry McCarthy, winner of this year's Stockholm Water Prize - the water industry's answer to the Nobel Prize.
