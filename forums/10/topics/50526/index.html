    Syed Hamid criticises Singapore book on water talks, 3 months after release

    By Walter Fernandez

    After a lull of several months, Kuala Lumpur has started a war of words over water again.

    Malaysia has accused Singapore of injecting fiction into its account of the water talks.

    Malaysian Foreign Minister Syed Hamid Albar was referring to a book published back in March.

    The book sets out Singapore's position on the issue and is largely a compilation of Singapore's Foreign Minister S Jayakumar's statement to Parliament in January as well as the documents and diplomatic correspondence made public at that sitting.

    According to Bernama, Syed Hamid described the release of the book, whose contents were online since January, as underhanded and warned it could mar ties.

    So the Malaysian government is coming up with its own publication on the dispute to dispel what Syed Hamid called the "the half-truths and the misleading statements."
