    Based on the ST report reproduced below, it would seem that the priority of the police is to catch the guilty and not to prevent crime or murder. Have they lost their senses?

    27 Oct 2005
    Eyewitness to a tragedy
    JALAN BUKIT MERAH MURDER

    Retiree Liew Meng Choon, 65, was sitting in his usual place at the top of the staircase at the end of a short path from Block 132, Jalan Bukit Merah, when he witnessed the tragic events unfold on Tuesday evening

    6pm: He sees the 26-year-old wife coming out of a taxi. She appears normal, talking into her mobile phone, but then goes to the coffee shop to use the public phone. The conversation is short, about 40 seconds, and she then goes up to her flat.

    She knocks on the door of her second-storey flat and her husband lets her in. Shortly after that, an argument breaks out between the couple. Neighbours told Mr Liew later that they heard the woman crying out for help.

    ..............

    8.45pm: The wife's boyfriend calls the police. He tells them that
    she has sent him an SMS asking him to call 999 as her life is in
    danger. He tells the police she is in Block 132, Jalan Bukit Merah,
    but fails to give the unit number.

    9pm: The first team of police officers arrives and goes door to
    door. When the officers reach the second-floor unit, they see a man
    armed with two knives sitting in the living room.

    Officers see a handbag and a woman's shoe but no sign of the woman.
    When the man notices them, he threatens them and slams the door shut.

    The police then try to persuade him to put down the knives and to
    let them in, but fail. Singapore Civil Defence Force officers are
    then called in to climb up to the windows at the rear of the flat to
    prevent him from escaping.

    The Special Operations Command is also called in. By this time, the
    crowd of onlookers is more than 200-strong.

    10pm: Police officers cut the lock of the metal gate with special
    cutters but do not attempt to break down the door. Instead, police
    negotiators are called in and, in line with police strategy, the
    man's relatives are quickly found and taken to the unit to persuade
    the man to give up.

    12.40am: After the man's sister and mother fail to persuade him to
    open the door, his son and daughter, who have been playing around the
    staircase landing, are asked to try to get him out.

    Crying pitifully, their pleas work and the man opens the door.
    Police rush in and find his wife strangled to death in the bedroom.
