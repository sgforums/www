    let's imagine, a massive earthquake of magnitude 9.0 occurs in the South China Sea, creating a tsunami that speeds westwards towards Sg.

    now a look at a map will reveal that the southeastern tip of Peninsular Malaysia shields us to some extent. if a tsunami really were to hit us, what do we have on our eastern coast? the first to get hit would be Changi East Airbase, and Changi Naval Base. we'll lose lots of RSN ships and F-16s, and sadly quite a few SAF servicemen as well.

    thankfully, the Changi East area is otherwise not densely populated.

    even if the worst occured and the tsunami were to come further in, it would encounter the flat tarmac, airliners and buildings of Changi Airport. here the economic impact will be huge, and people will die as well - but the airport buidings are obviously strongly built, and will protect the people inside.

    even if the airport will have to close, we won't be totally cut off - we could reopen Paya Lebar and Seletar to commercial traffic.

    we would definitely suffer. but we wouldn't be totally knocked out. life will go on.
