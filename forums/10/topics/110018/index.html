    A friend of was told of a lucky Singaporean's close shave from that tsunami disaster on the morning of 26.12.2004. Let me recount this incident;-

    That morning, he and his two young children were playing on the shallow sandy Kamanda beach which stretched very far out to the sea.

    For some reasons, his children felt disturbed by the changing tide which for one moment receded completely and the next rising up swiftly.

    Because of his children complaints and his concern for their comfort or safety, the father decided to bring them back to the hotel.

    From the hotel, he observed for some moments the swaying of the tide one moment receding completely into the distance and the next rising up quickly to nearly engulf many beach goers.

    No one seems to notice such telltale little signs of something calamitous which was about to happen.

    Somehow, he felt uncomfortable and decided to leave the hotel early. Hours later, back in Singapore airport, he heard the Tsunami calamity that claimed many lives. Luckily for him, he had not ignored the telltale sign or the pleading of his children.

    Jan 4, 2005
    Quake west of Luzon would pose a threat

    SINGAPORE was spared the tsunami onslaught as there is no direct 'line of sight' for the tidal waves to travel from the undersea earthquake epicentre to the island.

    Quake west of Luzon would pose a threat

    The epicentre was in the Indian Ocean and the Republic is sheltered by the island of Sumatra. However, the situation would be different if the earthquake had occurred somewhere to the west of the Philippines.

    The Philippines has its share of volcanic eruptions and earthquakes (the resort town of Baguio was hit by an earthquake some years ago).

    Should a major undersea earthquake strike, say, west of Luzon Island, the resultant tsunami waves would race down the South China Sea, like the North-east Monsoon, to hit the east coast of West Malaysia and Singapore shores. The subsequent destruction of Changi and the East Coast seafronts would be unimaginable.

    Can the Civil Defence authorities let us know whether they have now included this scenario in their disaster planning?

    Phoon Boon Pin
