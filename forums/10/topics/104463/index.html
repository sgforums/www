    By his latest revelation, MM Lee indirectly or directly admitted that he did not realise learning both English and pushing learning of mother tongue to the hilt of making a good passing as a university admission criteria would indeed cause dilution of both English and Chinese.

    Today, he finally has to rectify that fundamental error of judgment.

    When I had to switch from Chinese to English education after secondary level some years ago, for economic reason, I found it extremely difficult (not so smart) to learn English and so had to forego Chinese (including reading of Chinese newspaper) so as to concentrate 100% on learning English.

    In the same way, if we are to compel the child to learn both he will most likely not learn both well.

    Such compulsion would also seriously hamper mastery or effective learning of science and technological diluting excellence needed to produce an highly knowledgeable work force of tomorrow.

    In theory, it is desirable to learn both. In practice forcing on children to learn both will be at the expense of either of them and also of science and technological mastery.

    So, do we want education for all round knowledge acquisition and application or do we to go overboard with learning two languages with penalty to create problems for children in other areas of excellence?
