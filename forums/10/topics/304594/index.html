    JONATHAN WEST: I think it's very important to know more about China - what will they export, what will they import, what is the government's policy, what is it aiming to do? And to find a relationship with China that is advantageous. There are products that Australia makes uniquely well, that it makes uniquely well at certain times of the year, that is the sort of thing that China is seeking to import. Seeking to compete with China on the basis of cost, on the basis of being in the same market that they're in where labour cost is critical, is a losing game. China's great advantage is not, as we often think of it, low cost, unskilled labour. Their great advantage is low cost, skilled labour. They graduate nowadays between 500,000 and one million engineers a year. Now, in Australia we graduate about 5,000. Those engineers are paid, to emphasise, one fifteenth of what we are paid.
