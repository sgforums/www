    One of the first things Ong did when he became the Singapore President was requesting a government White Paper to list down in clear and specific terms, the responsibilities of a President.

    When the White Paper was produced, he took to question, among other things, one such responsibility listed: protection of Singapore's financial reserves. According to press reports, Ong wanted to know what he was supposed to protect in his Presidential capacity, and requested for a full inventory of government assets and reserves from the government. The inventory list was never produced, as the authorities dawdled, claiming insufficient resources to compile such a list.

    Ong became a little frustrated and made public statements about being prevented by ministers and bureaucrats from carrying out his Presidential duties. That move riled more than a few feathers within the government, and the latter withdrew their support for Ong to carry on a second term as the President, citing his ill health.

    Ong insisted to have been given a "clean bill of health" by external medical professionals, and initially intended to run for a second term. However, the wife came down with cancer and her deteriorating health was one of the reaons that prompted Ong to relinquish his Presidential duties to spend more time with her. He made his decision public during a press conference before the 1999 Presidential elections and Nathan was sworn in after a walkover victory.
