No more subsidies for foreigners at polyclinics

  • TooFree
    12 Nov 05, 14:48
    The Health Ministry will stop providing subsidies to foreigners at the polyclinics from January next year.

    It will also reduce the subsidies given to Permanent Residents.

    Currently, foreigners are subsidised like citizens, paying only $8 for basic consultation fees.

    With the new regulation, foreigners will likely pay about $16, while Permanent Residents can expect to pay $12.

    Foreign patients make up about 5 per cent of the patient-load at polyclinics.

    The Health Ministry says it's not appropriate to subsidise foreign workers as it's the employers' responsibility to do so.

    Health Minister Khaw Boon Wan first indicated plans to revoke the subsidies more than one month ago.

    The change will affect 540,000 work permit holders and 80,000 employment pass holders here. -CNA

    Serious guys, I feel that this is a good move for all Singaporean ... but don't you find it lacks compassion for low-income foreign workers or PRs?
