    Ok, Qitai, I will do this on a personal favour.

    The MNC will do this to reduce taxes. For example, MNC will always choose a place with very low tax to be its base of revenue collections. It will open up an operation in Singapore which may have higher taxes. Thus, to avoid being taxed too high in Singapore, through internal pricing, it will say the MNC's base provides certain raw material or services and charge this into the operation in Singapore. Now, it would become a cost factor to the Singapore's operations, which is not necessary so if it purchase the raw material DIRECTLY from the suppliers. But due to the "centralize" policy, it has to buy from the MNC's base, giving it a profit determined solely by the MNC alone!

    Thus through such schemes, MNCs literally "transferred" earnings out of the Singapore's operations.

    In HDB's case, the govt could well give HDB the one time off endowment fund to start off, lending to HDB flat buyers, earning interests from them, then use these earnings to really subsidize the flats it sold. But no, this will not do because it will not show that HDB is "losing money" in giving subsidies to Singaporeans!

    I suspect the "Capital Expenditure" on Land is the "premium" HDB is asked to pay govt for use of land that HDB itself might have acquired throught he Land Acquisition Act. This premium is the result of the "price difference" between acquired price and market price. HDB is deemed merely as "proxy holder" of these land on behalf of the Govt. When it uses them, it has to pay the Govt. This is why it must use "MARKET SUBSIDY" concept to sell the idea that HDB is in deficits because it is giving "subsidies"! And to illustrate this point, the "subsidies" are shown as "govt grants" to HDB! But they didn't tell the whole story; HDB may be "losing money" but the Govt is not! In fact, wouldn't one be curious about HDB's claim that it has so much reserves accumulated from the past even when it is "losing money" in providing the "market subsidies"?

    This is like saying the MNC's Singapore operations are losing money but the MNC with its base, are not losing money from Singapore's operations by means of these internal pricing!

    Goh Meng Seng
