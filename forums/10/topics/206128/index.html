Are Maids Here To Work As Maids Or As Clerks?

  • dragg
    21 Aug 06, 11:35
    extracted from straits times forum.

    RECENTLY, my parents tried to hire a maid for the family. The maid was new to the job, with no experience working here.
    She had to sit for a written English test at the Ministry of Manpower, which she attempted nine times but failed.

    She was then not allowed to work in Singapore and was sent back to Indonesia - just because she failed her test as she was unable to understand some words.

    As a result, my parents had to go through all the trouble of selecting another maid.

    What is the actual purpose of having tests like this? Is it not sufficient to just understand some simple words for conversation?

    Is it really necessary for the maids to be able to read a lot of English words? So long as they are able to converse in English, why do they even need to read up that much?

    When my parents interviewed her in English, she was able to understand them and was also able to show her understanding of English in her answers.

    The only problem was that her ability to read English words was limited. And because of that, she failed her test.

    Are maids here to work as maids or as clerks? They could slowly learn how to read while working for the family.

    Another thing - if there is no language barrier between the family and the maid, why should the maid not be hired?

    In our case, being a Malay family, we would have had no problems conversing with her in Malay or Bahasa Indonesia.

    Jefferey Loh Ridwan Loh
