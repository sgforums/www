    The data gathered in this paper suggest that increased religiosity in society does not correlate at all with a more stable or harmonious society in both social and economic factors. The top five ranked nations, Sweden, Denmark, Japan, Norway and Holland are all noted for their secularism, and strong values in a stable society indicated by low rates of homicides, suicides, incarceration (number of people put in jail), STD rates, life satisfaction index, marriage duration and abortions, and economic equality.

    The United States, on the other hand, has a very strong culture of publicly declared religiosity (no American politician could hope to be elected to office unless they were publicly declared Christians, or were Jews), and has indications of high social and economic instability at the same time.

     

     Has "religion" any relations to the "social / economic dysfuntion" of any society ?

    While the author attempts to compare the "intensity of being religious" to affect the results in the statistics of the social ills observed, there are erroneous conclusions in the claims made. 

    Firstly, how was the conclusion made in determining that - "the top five ranked nations, Sweden, Denmark, Japan, Norway and Holland are all noted for their secularism" ?

    Is "secular"  intended to be a distinct separation of Church and State; or allowing the Church to  co-exist without the need for any patronage from the State ?

    If by "secular" it is meant that the general population are non-religious, then nothing can be more wrong as seen from the gems that correct the wrong claims made as displayed below :-

    ‘One fifth of Swedes chose to describe themselves as believing in God, while the majority (78 percent) of the population belongs to the Church of Sweden – the Lutheran Church that separated from the State in 2000’ (*1); 

    ‘In Denmark, the Evangelical Lutheran Church is state supported, and accounts for 83 per cent of Denmark’s reglious affiliation’. (*2); 

    ‘Shinto and Budhism are Japan’s two major religions, and have been co-existing for several centuries – with most Japanese considering themselves Buddhist , Shintoist or both’ (*3). 

    ‘The Lutheran Church of Norway is the state church with 86 per cent of Norwegians being Lutherans, 3.5 per cent being Protestants, and 1 per cent being Catholics’ (*4) 

    ‘In the Netherlands (Holland) - the largest group of her population consist of 26.6 per cent being Roman Catholics, while the rest is distributed over a multitude of Protestant Church in the Netherlands forming 16.8 per cent as Protestants, and 43.4 per cent as Christians – with no religious affiliation forming 42.7 per cent of her citizenry’(*5). 

     If the above shows the flaw in the claim to secularism in the top five nations, then this will lead to the second flawed claim in the conclusion that the intensity "of secularism and social values will be seen in the low rates of homicides, suicides, incarceration (number of people put in jail), STD rates, life satisfaction index, marriage duration and abortions, and economic equality".

    Was it correct to compare the low indices in social ills found in these top five nations compared to the USA - when it is obvious that population size will affect the statistics with an increased potential to higher frequency of social ills occurring compared to a smaller size population ?

    Is it correct to place the cause of social dysfunction being caused by higher rate of religiousity ?

It is true that humans have always relied on religion until very recently, but only because civilization was still very much in flux until the 21st century in a select few nations when its inhabitants have decided for themselves that God was no longer needed.

    When the Homo Sapien becomes more learned with scientific and philosophical discoveries and thinking, he should be less superstitious and will develop a higher intellect to seek the abstract quality in a God that is worthy of his developed intelligence.

    Will the Homo Sapien ever reach the point in its intellectual development to decide that he will no longer need a more Superior Being ?

    Societies and Religions are the hallmark of the intellect of the Homo Sapien - can it be anything less ?

    Social stresses appear in a society of Homo Sapiens not due to poor planning but due to environmental factors beyond the control of the Homo Sapiens.

    Weather can play havoc to agriculture produces that result in increased breakdown of the social system unless there is a strong communal bond in self-help that will need supplementing from higher authorities.

    Is the modern civilisation that we know today abandoning God ?

    If it is so, how should one conclude the increase numbers of new found religious groups that are not from the main stream churches, and prefer to worship in small groups in the outdoors, or in rented ballrooms, or rotating through members' homes ?
