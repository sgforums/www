    2 Jun 05, 19:50
    Originally posted by Guyfawkes80:
    Areour rights being monitored by any international bodies like UN?

    Just a question.
    To answer your question, let's us examine our citizens' rights. Do they possess for example any of the 7 basic rights (posted under "Defintion of True Democracy") of citizens:-

    (1) Do we still have the "One Man One Vote" right, given the fact that only 40 % of eligible voters can cast their votes vote under the handicapped GRC-controlled voting process ?

    (2) Right of Protection against prosecution for political participation.

    (3) Basic personal rights e.g. freedom of speech without being sued for defamation.

    (4) Right to observance of Rule of law, fairness and equity in enforcement of election or constitutional rights of citizens.(ministers getting within boundary of election booths not even regarded as an offence)

    (5) Rights to seek protection by an independent constitution sub-court open to observers from UN agencies for matters relating to political participation by citizens.

    (6) Right to seek protection by an independent constitution sub-court against conflict of interest among the executives - e.g. self-approved bonuses or highest salaries (equated inequitably to the highest private sector) and life-long annuities (S$1,000 million) to 3-term MP/Ministers/Political Office holders.

    (7) Safeguard against prosecution of political opponents e.g. speeches made during election campaigns.
