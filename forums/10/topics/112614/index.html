    i used to feel rather unfair and hard done by the CCA point systems in schools.

    Having a cca like soccer would definitely earned you less points than a member of a uniform groups. Soccer competitions in schools are held mostly once a year and thats perhaps the only opportunity for us to gain points.
    as for uniform group members,there are countless courses and points accredited
    for the ranks attained.activities like SYF (singapore youth festival) also benefits them.

    A member of the soccer team spends as much time or more on their CCA as any member of other CCAs.The opportunity to gain points are fewer for such CCAs and I believe it is the case for many other CCAs as well.

    This puts the students in a difficult situation of choosing a CCA that can earn them more points or a CCA that they feel passionately for.

    Such a point system should be made fair to all students regardless of what their CCA's are.There must be equal opportunities to gain points when we place all the CCAs side sby side and compare them.

    CCA points affect students their futhur studies,surely it must not be overlooked.

    For 2 yrs,we trained 3times a wk,5times when competition draws near.the tournament came and gone.I got 3points in total.One each year for being a member of soccer and one for representing my school.

    What the students want at the end of the day is a system that can give them due credit for the effort they have put in.
