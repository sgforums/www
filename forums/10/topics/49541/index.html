    The decision by many to have smaller families not doubt comprise of many complex and differing considerations.

    Of course, the most basic and foundamentals ones would include the cost of having a large family, personal prefences, and the appropriate housing needs of a large sized family as compared to a smaller one.

    I believe the arguments with concern towards the cost of having a large family would have been very familiar with many of us here.

    However, I would like to highlight the point that it might also be prehaps, the decreasing size of the availible housing units that may be actually' forcing' family sizes to shrink too.

    Consider a typical young couple who just got married.

    While they may not req a large unit in the early phase of their marriage, four or five years down the line, chances are that the pair would have had children of their own.

    Assuming that the couple's first appartment isa 4 room HDB flat, the available space would be already been very streched for the two parents and the two children.

    Where would there be room for the third child to come in?

    Now of course if we were to look solely at this statement, the argument would be very lame indeed.

    However, if the relevant authorities could give some special financial assistance for couples who have decided to have larger families at least some limited financial assistence to get a larger 5-room unit for their first purchase, the move would probably lend a very helpful hand for such family planning.

    Though there will be the usual black sheeps who will try to take advantage of the scheme if oneis available, I certainly believe that the authorities would have a hard time to track down family units who had participated in the scheme, but later decided against participation.

    The periodic reacquiring of the loaned sum may thus be done by the authorities, and no harm would be done anyway.

    The positive benefits of such family friendly assitance would not only play a significant role in helping large family startups to be easier, but may also be seen as a parallel extension of the Baby Bonus scheme revealed by the Prime Minister some time back in a bid to encourage population growth.

    Incidently, the spirit of such schemes would also go hand in hand with MCDS' rigourous promotion of extended family contacts.
