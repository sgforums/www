    The current constitution (Singapore) is too general and vague.

    For example, there is no specific definition of citizens' rights, no redress or remedy for any infringement of such right like the habeas corpus under the constitution.

    There is no governmental structure spelling out the respective roles and functions of different components within the government.

    Hence, the constitution in the present form is unable to assure the citizens of system integrity, accountable and transparent processes to prevent overlapping of roles among the executive, judiciary and legislative components of government resulting in compromises on system integrity.

    Above all there is no assurances of true and uninhibited political participation by the citizens in political or election process nor upholding of the one-man-one-vote election process, essential to the vibrancy and progress in any democratically elected system of government.

    For the time being, people may be content with bread and butter issues rather than the weighty issues of rights and integrity of the governing system.

    We are a young country which is still in the process of experimenting with our political system through trial and error. There is a need to grow and mature our very own political and government system in a timely manner. If we fail to do so, we might one day end up with tyranny and abuse of power which is one of the greatest factors for instability in any systems.

    Therefore with this perspective in mind, I am asking this critical question at this juncture of our development Â– should we study the existing constitutions and its shortcomings and make relevant amendments and improvements as well as to prevent any possibility of abuse by any future parties in power.

    The vagueness and generality in the existing constitution have already resulted in laws and regulations being passed like the summary power of arrest of citizens and detention without trials and granting of estate upgrading to incumbent party's loyalists and doctoring of citizens' one-man-one-vote entitlements under the GRC election process.

    Our constitution is looking to be more like an old tattered piece of clothe worn just to avoid nakedness but unable to provide protection from the element and warmth to the wearer.

    Once an objective, fair and equitable constitution has been established, it will strengthen the government in winning wholeheared support of the people. We can proceed with evolving a constitution from overall perspective as stated in the foregoing to avoid any narrow approaches which were narrowly based on pragmatism or monies making by the government.

    Above all the constitution must be regarded as compact between the government and the people and kept sacred and not easily be undermined with impediments to the larger objectives and goals of promoting total growth and vibrancy which could come about through mass participation by citizens.

    People want to elect the government to serve the people's needs and aspirations and deliver the greatest benefits of the greatest numbers and not to give itself more and more legalistic controls to itself to stay permanently in power. If we want greater progress, we must be prepared to win it by working together as a people and go beyond the needs and aspirations of first stage of nation building to embrace larger goals and objectives in perspective such as the following:-

    (1) To safeguard One Man One Vote principle so that only the best individuals are elected into political office complete with accountable and transparent check on power of executives by the people.

    (2) To ensure continual multi-racial representation but not at the expense of tilting the level playing field in favor of the party in power. (Alternative solution is possible e.g. 20% of seats of parliaments can be set aside to allow minority racial seats to be allocated to minority winning the highest percentage of votes among themselves as counted in the one-man-one-vote election return)

    (3) To safeguard the Basic rights of all citizens irrespective of race, culture, religion, freedoms of speech, political beliefs, right to stand for election or right to make criticisms of policies or practices of government in power.

    (4) To refer all issues and disputes among politicians to independent courts empowered to adjudicate all matters impinging on constitutional matters.

    (5) Establish Rule of law, justice, fairness and equity of all citizens and between citizens and government without fear or favor to the government in power.

    (6) To fundamentally encourage through the constitution further growth of social, cultural and economic vibrancy, entrepreneurship and creativity of citizens.

    In my view there are enough powers given to the government within our system to carry its duties fully and effectively. In the new era, success will be dependent more on how to mobilize and motivate all citizens towards nation building. To do so, it is essential if not critical to grant, preserve and uphold relevant rights and entitlements of citizens so that the citizens will be motivated to come forward to serve their nation in whatever capacity they are equipped to do so without any constraints.

    Such a constitution will be proactive and open up greater avenues for citizens to take a larger part in nation building through all cultural and economic interactions among all citizens. Such larger objectives and goals could be incorporated so as to engender cooperations and interactions beneficial to nurturing and developing creativity and entrepreneurship.

    Singapore's democracy is still young and it needs to be further nurtured and developed into one which will motivate and encourage all to participate in nation building.

    It will be too premature to put the blame for any demonstrations and rallies seen in certain countries on democracy and conclude fallaciously that democracy is the cause for chaos and is unsuitable for us or Asians.

    The fact is democracy has triumphed over the dark forces, social injustices and brought about significant progress of the human race over the past four hundred years

    Democracy has faults and defects but its totality of accountable and transparency governance processes, including the one-man-one-vote and capability at balancing of self-centred autocracy with objectivity, justice and equity has produced the final good and greatest benefits to the greatest number. Without democracy or fair and equitable political and election processes, the world could have been worse off. There could have been more tyranny and bad experiences due to dictatorship, autocracy or pseudo-democratic practices which benefit only those in power.

    In the final analysis, a nation is only as good as the quality of its people. If people want to make further progress in the new era of technology and globalization, the first precondition must be to put its own governing process in order one which will embrace all to come forward to contribute to total progress with accountability and transparency irrespective of belief, cult, race, religion or political views or inclinations.
