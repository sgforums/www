  • robertteh
    3 Jun 07, 01:06
    World leaders have tried many times to resolve the conflicts between the Israelites and Muslim nations for ages.

    Many peace agreements have negotiated with the help of leaders like the presidents of United States etc.

    Yet the conflicts remain as intractable as ever.

    Why?

    Political Leaders clearly could not produce results, as they are not trusted by the people in conflicts.

    Obviously if we are to re-examine past efforts, there has been a singular lack of success in such past and current diplomatic peace missions.

    Something apparently have gone fundamentally wrong with such peace efforts and undertaking.

    I would propose that instead of resolving such conflicts among politicians, of all nations involved, all parties involved should pause for a breather and let the conditions be cultivated such that ordinary citizens with social status and influence associated in non-political arenas elect their own representatives of peace to interact for a change.

    Totally new and neutral and non-political ordinary sincere peacemakers could emerge in due course to build social and cultural bridges among nations.

    Political leaders are not suitable to intercede in such peace efforts as they are viewed with suspicions by both sides as likely to have hidden agendas behind all the efforts and negotiations.

    US for example in proposing any changes to current balances of power will not be trusted by the Muslim nations.

    US will also tend to use consciously or not economic, military or political pressures to bear on their negotiations whereas ordinary non-political ambassadors will be more trusted if they approach such objective based on people-to-people friendship to forge common social, cultural and educational or even community-building objectives.

    Through exchanges of such nature, a lot remain to be and can be better accomplished by ordinary active citizens involved in building of cultural and community welfare projects among such nations.

    United Nation has become politicised today and is not too suitable for such efforts.

    A different format of peace will have to be found if nations in conflicts are to resolve their differences.
