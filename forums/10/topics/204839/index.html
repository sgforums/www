    This problem of "missing water" is common amongst Third World Countries, where budget constraints result in poor management and supervision, leading to pilferages, breakdowns and breakages to the main delivery system.

    Even in a compact place like Hong Kong, illegal water and electricity tappings into the respective utilities mains are common; and with the population living in dense concentrations within a small space, such small illegal connections get lost in the myraid of pipes and cables.

    Singapore Government is fortunate to have Singapore as a small country, with a much smaller population - when compared with Hong Kong; and with most slums being eliminated, and the people moved into newly built flats - all of these factors have allowed the Singapore Government to keep a lid on such mismanagement of "missing water", and "missing electricity".

    The purposeful drive to eliminate slum areas of Bukit Ho Swee and China Town areas in Singapore, was partially to achieve social progress and to manage public accounting in revenue collections.
