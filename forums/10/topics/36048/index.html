    Blast at oil and gas facility in New York

    A massive blast ripped through an oil and gas facility off Staten Island in New York on Friday, triggering a major blaze, local television reported.

    One eyewitness described hearing a huge explosion, while television footage showed thick black smoke belching out of the facility.

    There were no reports of any casualties.

    A New York Fire Department spokesman said it was not immediately clear what had caused the blaze.

    US Coast Guard officials said the facility was owned by Exxon Mobil, the world's largest oil company.

    Initial reports from fire officials described the barge as carrying propane.

    Burning oil from the facility appeared to spread into the Arthur Kill waterway, a busy shipping thoroughfare between Staten Island and New Jersey state.

    Several residents living up to three miles away told local television that the explosion was violent enough to shake the windows in their houses.

    New York, like the rest of the United States, is currently on a state of high alert for a possible terrorist attack.

    Meanwhile, oil prices spiked up by almost a dollar a barrel on world markets on news of the massive blast.
