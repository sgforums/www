    To lower would be the decimation of our poor, down to a population size of 1.5million. The other 2million will have to starve and die out. It will be a majority of the poor and middle classes who will suffer, the very those who cannot afford to leave the island.

     

    Our economy is fully integrated to the world, from the mama shop at the HBD void deck to Bill Gates' Microsoft. To lower costs, it will have to be a chain reaction from the top to the bottom in our world.

     

    Take for a simple example of a taxi company. The company can lower their rental of a taxi by a few hundred dollars, but if they were to lower the rental for an entire fleet, it would be hundreds of thousands of dollars.

     

    The company needs every dollar to service their loans, and provide the necessary infrastructure to support the taxis - building, maintenance, staff, etc.

     

    When they lower the rental of taxi, they would not be able to service their loans, as well as to build up reserves to buy a new fleet when the current fleet of taxis are worned out.

     

    The taxi company will then fold, and thousands of taxi drivers will be out of job and starve to death. Extrapoliate that onto the entire economy, we will all be swatting flies and dying by the roadsides, akin to failed African states.

     

    Our govt can attempt to lower costs and cuts taxes, cut down on social spending, depriving funding for sectors such as education and healthcare, and don't earn revenues from property prices, rents, land sales, utilities, etc, etc, but can there be any assurance that private operators will not pocket the differences instead of using it to build up their private companies and keep people hired?

     

    Lowering costs is not the answer, for money is growing and printed at a tremendous rate each time a resource is harvested. Improved fertilization. Modern land efficient use and resource processing methods. Double, triple yielding crops. Renewable agri-fuel. Sustainable environment friendly recyled plastics instead of wood, recyclable ferrous oxides, etc, etc, etc.There are more money in this world today then 10 years ago.

     

    It is only natural companies grow to earn such money. And based on market theory of supply and demand, people and growing population growth will pay more for exclusive and critical goods, and the cycle of high costs will continue to spiral.

     

    But there are ways for companies to beat high costs or high operating costs. Quality or Quantity is the solution.

     

    For example, a hawker must serve exceptionally good food, so good that he can price it at $12 for a plate of fried rice, and people will willingly pay for it, even if his target market is small. He will still survive. This is quality.

     

    Or he can sell his fried rice at $1.50, and i am sure people will queue up to buy it, and he will survive too. This is quantity.

     

    Our population was 1.5million in 1965, Today it is 3.5million exclulding 1.5million foreigners.  There are many ways to earn money from this increase of pop growth, example-KFC's $888 ang pow promotion, etc.

     

    Demand will offset highcosts. But if there is no demand, low costs will not help anyway. It will only be a lingering death for companies. Thus the need to spend. People are hoarding money, and not a case of no money.

     

    As for workers who face high costs of living, due to competition, raising salaries is not a solution. Nor is the lowering of business costs another solution for it will only destroy business enterprises and creates more unemployment.

     

    Working in companies or public sector assures one of an assured paycheque every month. Thus, the worker must either upgrade to earn more, or be satisfied with the amount and live within his means.

     

    The other solution would be to become an entreprenuer, or learn to be one, to beat high costs of living. Complaining is no use. Solutions and actions are needed.

     

    High costs of living will never end, but it can be beaten and kept in check.
