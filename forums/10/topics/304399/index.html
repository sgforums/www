    Dear Deathbait,

    I'm sorry I did not make myself clearer.

    I did not mean to say self reflection is bad.

    Bear in mind the "why don't you look at yourself" is used as an emotive appeal for empathy and understanding. To work, this requires and depends on sufficient parallels in the subject party. The purpose is to to adjust a negative emotional mindset that may be blocking an intellectual appeal or to create a mindset where the intellect may resolve it in a biased manner in accordance with your intent.

    It was not used appropriately against the anecdotal evidence because as in example 1. the reader could empathize with the stabbed person's family.

    You can argue that you did not intend that effect or that meaning. I accept that. I am not arguing that it was what you meant. I'm highlighting the effect.

    This tool can also be abused, as in to imply to bystanders that the other person is incapable of empathy, which is a basic human trait vital to our own protections. This dehumanizes the other person, changes the subject, does not look like an outright criticism, confuses bystanders etc. To accomplish this successfully, you require to draw parallels that the entire crowd of bystanders can emphatize with.

    It can also serve to obfusicate the issue by creating the empathic link then purposely creating confusion as to what conclusion you are taking us towards. In the meantime, action is put on hold. Used this way it's intended effect is to delay action.

    Your use had the 3rd effect on me and seemed pointless. I was bringing it to your attention in case it wasn't what you intended.
