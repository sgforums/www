    My advice to you is:
    Let your girl understand that P4 is an interesting year because this is the year they raise the bar on learning. If she's been achieve over her previous 3 years, let her understand that there WILL be a drop in marks as she need to adjust to the higher level of learning. Tell her the purpose of schooling is: not, not to make mistakes, but it is to make all the mistakes and learn along the way. Tell her not to be demoralised by the number of corrections she need to do because everyone's doing correction now.
    P1 boy...? let him dream a while. Get into his mind and see what's he thinking inside. Find out what challenge him. Use these as a leverage to encourage him more. Never, ever compare him negatively against his sister. You may praise his strong points over his sister's to boost his confidence a little. Assign small amount of work to him and see if he reacts responsibly by doing them. Reward him once in a while. And when he gets a little more mature, explain the purpose of schooling to him.
    Your younger child has a typical 2nd child syndrome. One with a slightly more lackadasical attitude (otherwise called boh chap). So the challenge is to slowly nudge him to do a little more, taking baby steps along the way.
    I know these for a fact 'cos my two sons are the same - older boy, totally motivated and focused, young boy, relak attitude. I can still managed to get them to work hard in school.

    There will always be overzealous teachers... some of these needed talking to especially if their methods are wrong. Otherwise see the head of student welfare in the school.

    I strongly believe in caning, up to a point. Anyone else who also believe in hard punishment please note that there is another skill to master - that of being at peace with oneself. In English, it means, when caning, don't get angry. Very tough thing to do. Because you need to convince yourself the purpose of the caning is a punishment for an offence and NOT to vent anger.

    I cannot emphasize enough that as parents (especially fathers) we need to spend enough quality time with our children. My sons and I do lots of things together, including building advance Lego sets, play Xbox games, watch MTV, chit chatting about our day, share funny stories, etc.

    Some books that I'd found useful for my sons:
    Seven habits of Effective Teenager by Sean Covey
    Who move my cheese for teens by Spencer Johnson
    Mind Mapping for kids by Tony Buzan
    (titles may not be 100% accurate...)

    My older son has gone through all of the above and have found them to be helpful. I am trying to work on my younger son with at least Who move my cheese this year. He's finish a book entitled "Public speaking in the classroom" recently.

    Children are for us to nurture, if we don't do a good job, THEY suffer....
