    You know what, you are not far from the truth. I think at the upper levels, most schools have lost the mission to educate. It had evolved to a ranking issue. Note, too, that MOE is probably aware of it and had taken steps to rectify the situation. Whether successful or not, I don't know. It's like the 5-day week thing. CCA is now on Fridays. Has it now become more stressful on Friday? Will parents now see Saturdays as a free day for more tuitions? Other than the exams period, weekends in my household is meant for fun and recreation.
    I am not defending the system per se, I think that MOE is NOT really doing a good job, but I think they are trying. With parents and sometimes the kids being so vocal, sometimes teachers are stuck between the devil and the deep blue sea. If the syllabus is too simple, they will be accused of slacking in their work, if it is too tough, then they will be accused of being elitist. If they conduct extra lessons, parents will complain that it is too much, don't conduct, parents will say that they do not care.
    Glad I am not a school teacher.

    I wish for all to note that I am NOT pro-government or any it's representatives. I am just stating that there are other forces at place - the largest one being the parent and the family unit. I am an advocate of good parent/child relationship. To me it is the first line of defense for the child's growing up process. Any parent reading this should ask themselves some questions:
    1. Did I communicate enough? Note that communication is NOT nagging or scolding - it is just chit-chatting and trivial stuff.
    2. Did I encourage the child to communicate enough? This is not reporting to you what homework he has, it is how has his day been? What games did he play during PE? What funny stories he heard today?
    3. Was there a high level of blaming and finger-pointing when things go wrong?
    4. Did you encourage care and concern within the family unit?
    5. Are you realistic to your child's performance?
    6. Did you take time to pursue with your child things that interest him?

    I can go on... but don't fully blame the system. Look inwards, under your roof... there are things that will interest you....
