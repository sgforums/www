    Share a little story of mine about 10+ years ago.

    I was running a small Music CD shop and due to the cost of Cds are quite expensive and to avoid too much balance stock, i normally dont order alot. But sometime it is hard to predict the sales, so for some titles, they will sell out very fast. Some customer dont mind order it from me and wait, but their main concern is at least able to look at the cover before ordering.

    So what i did was some of the popular titles i would scan the cover from original copies and print them out. When i sold out my last copy of original, i would temporary use the printed one until my new stock arrived.

    One fine day, my shop was raided by CID and music companies rep. After 2 hours of searching by the reps, they could not find any pirated music CD, while i was chatting away with the CID that accompany them. Those Reps still insist on searching until the CID insist that there are no more place to search and it is obvious there are no pirated stuff, a little arguement between them and finally the CID raise his voice to declare End of Raid, no more searching.

    They brought back 500 pieces of original CDS and 20 printed covers. 3 months later they send me a summon to go to court and charge me for the 20 printed covers which i have all the original CDs(among the 500 pcs). I spoke to my lawyer and decide to fight the case seems the charges seems dumb and any person with the right mind would find me guilty.

    After a few discussion between the lawyers, my lawyer told me it might not be advisable to fight the case, would be better to admit guilty. I was like WTF? why?.

    He told me all the 20 scans i have their are logos from the music companies. They are able to come up with 7 charges base on that 20 logoes. They are going to reduce to 1 charge if i plead guilty. If i dont, i have to prepare for a fight which does not gurantee any winning.

    During that time, 1 pirated CD was fine $200-300, so he thinks for my case it would be maybe 50-100 since it is not a CD, it is just a logo. Assuming i was lucky, the judge will fine me for $50 for each logo, total = 20 x $50 = $1,000

    lawyer fees = $2000, and also i have to compensate the music companies for a total of $4,000. Total Damage = $7,000 and that would be the best situation.

    If i would fight the case, lawyer fees = $20,000 and no gurantee that i would win, and base on previous cases, chances of winning is definitely less the 1/2.

    So as a owner of a small music shop, looking at the figures, i decide to plead guilty. Losing $7,000 is better then losing $20,000 for sure plus court fine if i lose.

    So i plead guilty and guess what, the judge fine me for $500 for each logo. 1 whole pirated CD is $200-300, 1 Logo = $500? My lawyer was stunned by the fine too.

    in the end my damage = $14,000. $10,000 fine, $4,000 compensate music companies. My lawyer decided not to charge me any lawyer's fee since he was very sorry with the outcome.

    I learn something from this incident, poor people dont have the means to prove they are innocent at certain situation. So if you suay suay kana, then thats too bad.

    The legal system is fair? greatest lie ever.
