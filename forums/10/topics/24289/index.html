    Language is a dynamic process. It is continuously growing, changing, incorporating new words and grammer rules according to local circumstances while some old ones grow out of fashion and die out.

    I think that Profanities in language is part of this process. It is ridiculous to try to curb it of force language to develop along a "cleaner" path...

    Think about it: all languages share certain similarities as long as profanities are concerned. Body parts for instance: sexual anatomies are always used in English, Chinese dialects, Tamil, Spanish, Italian and French and Russian. Incest is a common theme in all of the above as well. Faeces is another common theme.

    So whats the big deal? It is not so much the point that it is articulated at all but it is rather interesting to see that all cultures have more or less the same standards of what is considered vulgar and profane.

    I think its good news. It shows that humankind has evolved a more or less common standard of what they consider vulgar and socially unacceptable and expressing them as signs of displeasure and insult shows that they do not agree with any of the above choice of profanities, and not an acceptance of them.
