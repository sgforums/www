    Many people said opposition parties are just for show in Singapore and the main politics player is PAP. Do you feel Singapore need strong opposition parties?
    Uh... I don't know what you guys think of this. But this idea has been cropping up in my head.

    I have observed the political development of many SE and E Asian states. They are all remarkably similar in a way that the ruling party that won the state independence from the colonial rulers often stay in power for very very long. Look at Arroyo, Megawati, Suharto, Chiang Kai Shek and Gandhi families. They eventually fell under political opposition, but not after staying in power for decades.

    Indonesia and Taiwan used to be almost one-party states, very much like Singapore now. But as time passed and the older generation of founding fathers passed on, democracy gradually flourished as politicians scrambled to fill in the political vacuum left behind by the titans.
