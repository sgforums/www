    No matter how MM Lee explains it, his GRC gerrymandering is most undesirable and will not benefit Singapore or lead to its ongoing progress.

    One-man-one-vote system allows the best individuals to come forward to stand for election with no hindrance whatsoever.

    Racial balance could easily be conceived through party manifesto, leadership ability and a host of other measures including good incentive scheme such as reserving of certain top ministerial posts for such minority candidates.

    With GRC, the individual best candidates will be decimated and checked as many of them will be hindered by the obstacle of having to convince fellow minority candidates to stand together with him which reduces our chance of finding or empowering the best candidates to serve.

    MM Lee was stumped when asked why he must increase the size of GRC which clearly showed that he was unable to justify it except for perpetuating his party's interest at the expense of one-man-one-vote which serves the best interest of the nation.

    MM Lee also did not explain well why he needs so much controls since PAP was not in danger of losing. He was not convincing in trying to say that he needs to consider the interest of the minority who will have problem in getting voted in by the majority-Chinese voters.

    When he was told that in earlier elections, a number of minority PAP candidates did get in, he said that that was when PAP was still strongly supported by the people.

    Therefore MM Lee by his own admission has revealed that GRC has been conceived to boost the PAP at that time when PAP was losing support.

    Conclusion: MM Lee could not justify GRC was for the good of Singapore but for PAP's own interest. Singaporeans will have to rethink the GRC if they want to throw up the best candidates to lead the country.
