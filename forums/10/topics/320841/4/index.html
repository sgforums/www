    In 1965, MM Lee cried when we got kick out of the Msian federation. He realized the enormity of the deed. We had nothing except a small port to depend on. Even our own asset, the People were largely uneducated.

    He fought long and bitterly hard against race supremacists so that we singaporeans may live in a mutli racial society free from discrimination. With that kick, and without economic support, he despaired and feared leading our people to their doom. 

    But those tears were only momentarily. With the late Dr GohKS and Rajaratnam, his team set about the world to bring investments in, to provide jobs for the largely uneducated people. But such revenues from businesses were not enough for social spending.

    Those gleaming towers, spotless MRT, fanciful schools with world class education standards today you see is the hardwork of MM Lee's team and our previous generation. It is thru their CPF contributions, tax contributions combined with other trade revenues that made Singapore what it is today.

    Very often govt will tax the rich to help the poor. While Spore does not cripple the rich with taxes till they don't invest to create jobs, our most middle class bears taxes daily thru GST. But then such gst comes with free will - if you don't need  goods, you don't need to pay any gst. And such gst costs a mere few cents on staples, but such mere cents goes towards helping social expenditure to help the poor thru education to escape poverty, and health care subsidies to help them get medical aid.

    With a huge CPF fund, the govt invests it prudently for long term, so as not to use more taxation to provide for social spending.

    We may be paying more, but such monies are used to help others. We Singaporean are a generous lot. We may grumble, but we donate generously to other nations, so we too can help our own.

    Before anyone accuse the govt of 'profiteering', do show which pockets the 'profits' went into.
