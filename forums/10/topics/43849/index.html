  • kenhor
    3 May 03, 22:43
    I think the main problem that we face in our economy is that we have embarked on a niche that is very difficult to sustain.

    In the 60s and 70s, we were the manufacturing base for MNCs. In the 80s, we were the port and transhipment hub. In the 90s, we are the regional HQ for MNCs and a service hub.

    Each of these models of economic growth in the last 4 decades depended on foreign investment as well as the fact that Singapore is a better place to do business than our neighbours.

    But now, with China and India sucking up all the FDI and Malaysia and Philippines able to compete in manufacturing and services, we have little to differentiate. Indeed, brining in FT to either fill in the skills gap or to keep the salaries/costs low is only a short term measure.

    Its is important to note that we are keeping our economic model intact after 40 years. We still are trying to attract FDI and act as a transhipment point. The latest is the FTA with USA. That reinforces the goverment's commitment to the model, not to change to any other model of development.

    We will sink or swim depending on the models success. Already, our neighbour has changed from primary commodities, to manufacturing to services and now to focus on local SMIs to power their economy. All that in the same timeframe as us. We have to do something on a macro level to change our mindset or else no matter what we do, we are stuck at the same economic model, and not the masters of our own destiny.

    Short term goals for Singapore is to continue to attract foreign investments in a variety of form, whether in service, manufacturing, etc. This will require an environment supportive of foreign talent, in order to attract the larger more significant investments.

    Long term goals should be to encourage local enterprises to form and to:
    1. Feed into the existing business framework
    2. Replace some, if not all, the existing foreign framework and ultimately to export these to the region and the world
    3. Create new form of businesses

    We have been successful in the past achieving the short term goals set. Each year we were able to bring in more foreign investments. While we were patting out backs, we failed to see that we have wasted those years by not looking after our long term goals.

    It is time, we separate the two and have separate home teams working on them. Placing them together will simply lead to a status quo. The long term goal team should be given the flexibility to achieve their goals without hindrance and concerns of short term realities.

    I have worked mostly with MNCs and one very obvious trait of such companies is that they have one and only one goal and that is to achieve profits for their parent company back home. There is no loyalty to the local country where they operate. If you do not give them what they want they will move and leave. The few cases recently in the shipping business is clear for all to see.

    To think that they have Singapore interest at heart is being naive. My constant hope for the government is to realize the need to put more emphasis to build a large pool of world class businesses that will one day go overseas and bring back the dough.

    I know some will say that enterpreneurs should come from the people and should need no assistance from the government. My answer is simple, if with government assistance we can bring sucess quicker, then why not. Say what you want but fortune 1000 companies achieve their current position with a lot of help from their home governments. Sanctions, quotas and whatever are used to ensure these successes.

    P.S. I have read recently government is already placing our brains at the service of local SMBs. This is a good start and I urge the government not to stop at that only.

    Cheers
