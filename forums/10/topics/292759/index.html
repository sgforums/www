  • robertteh
    21 Nov 07, 20:04
    Dr. Tan Cheng Bock once asked PM Lee: Are you not being reactive.

    We do not know whether PM Lee understood what Dr. Tan had in mind in referring to his reaction to criticisms as reactive.

    Here is the definition of Reactive and Proactive as given by Stephen R. Covey, author of Seven Habits of Highly Effective People:-

    REACTIVE LANGUAGE
    ...........................
    There is nothing I can do.

    That is just the way I am.

    He makes me so mad.

    They won't allow that.

    I have to do that.

    I can't.

    I must.

    If only.

    PROACTIVE LANGUAGE
    .............................

    Let's look at the alternatives.

    I can choose a different approach.

    I control my own feelings.

    I can create an effective presentation.

    I can choose an appropriate response.

    I choose.

    I prefer

    I will.

    Just use the above-stated list to check the responses to people's feedbacks on various problems.

    Just look at how our ministers react to feedbacks treating them as whining or complaining and see for yourself how many of them are being proactive in problem solving.

    I would say most of their responses could be classified as "We know better" "We have the mandate" "We have to do that". "There is no choice because we are a little red dot without resource", or "We must".

    How often did we ever come across their responses as "I choose with proper study of alternatives", "I can" or "I will look into and let you know"

    See for yourself how proactive or positive are our ministers.
