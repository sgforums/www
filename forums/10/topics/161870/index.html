    There is such a thing called loyalty, For the past many years, our government has forgotten about loyality to its own citizens.

    There would be a lot of talks about open economy, competitiveness thereby invitations to all foreign companies and foreigners to share our limited businesses and jobs.

    Has the government ever once considered that citizens have needs and expectations. They need opportunities and exposures which were given to foreign companies and foreigners. If foreigners can come in the name of competitiveness and take away anything, what would be the value of being citizens? Should citizens be loyal to government if government is not prepared to be loyal to its people.

    At least if government really wants to keep the economy open so that it will be more competitive, there should also be preserving or some basic safeguard against abuse by employers of some opportunities for citizens,

    Do we have any ministers looking into how to create and preserve some opportunities for its own citizens and businesses.

    People have given loyality to government but government has not returned loyalty to the people. It is time to change past pro-foreign policies to demonstrate to citizens that government while it wants to keep the economy open and competitive will not sacrifice the people's interests.
