    It's better to let the individual decide on what their investments should be themselves. Better to let the individual choose if they want to be in the CPF scheme (for individuals like Gazelle and lionnoisy) or they want to invest the money themselves.
    CPF is a touchy subject. Don't forget we're discussing annuity, where most elderly live in an era where dependence on the govt was like a mother b.reastfeeding her infant. Many others are understandably ignorant, believing the misguided claims that CPF will cover them in their twilight years.

    Our generation could probably be relied to get better value than the paltry 2.5% with our monies, but the same cannot be said of our super seniors, who may have become totally dependent on the govt for guidance.

    On situations where the majority cannot be relied upon to make decisions, the govt may step in. This approach is acceptable, since it will cost even more taxpayer's monies to manage this catagory of people later on, should they find themselves in financial turmoil without any means of recovery.

    In other situations, such as means testing to alleviate the problem of over-crowded hospitals, it's a fair to say that our mentality and that of the majority may 'abuse' their 'rights' and deprive more deserving patients of a bed. That the govt decided to step in is probably welcomed.

    This case is decidedly different. We are talking about the insurance industry, which has played an important role in shaping family development and investments for the last 20 years. We cannot feign ignorance now, nor can the majority claim they have little or no knowledge with the intricacies and legalities of insurance-care products. The idea that the govt may choose to intervene is simply premature at this stage.
