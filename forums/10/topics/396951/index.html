Suharto's daughter

  • Veggie Bao
    18 Apr 10, 23:27

    During the last dying days of the Suharto regime, the people were fed up, and the credibility and moral authority of the ruling family (can be said so) was diminishing day by day.

    Eventually, the regime did something.

    Suharto's daughter was appointed Minister of Social Affairs.

    She was pictured visitting poor people, orphanages, dishing out aids, visitting hospital patients, and so on.

    But eventually, it didnt help.

    People were fed up by the misdeeds, and the power monopoly of the Suharto family.

    This reminds me of some similar case in some other countries.

    There is a country where a certain charismatic ruler has been in power for so long, and that he entrenched power to his family.

    Because of the prominence and vulnerability of his family, something clever was done.

    The daughter was put forward, she wrote some articles in the state media. Usually, she would write how she is just an ordinary person, with faults and weaknesses, and that she is always striving for truth, justice, and compassion. She is also a firm believer of social solidarity.

    Now, why would the state media avail such generous space to her?

    There are countless other columnists who can write about a wide range of subjects.

    From what I read, there is nothing special about what she wrote. It feels more like a blog or a personal commentary. Usually talking about her personal experiences or some things in her family, or brothers, or parents.

    This made me sad in some way.

    It means it is an indication that the situation has reached the Suharto's family level.
