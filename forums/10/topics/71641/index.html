    While increasing maternity leave is a good idea, doing just that might fly back in their faces if that is not backed up with refinements in the Employment Act as well as the the government deferring the costs of such a move.

    As have already been pointed out, increasing maternity leave means more pain for employers who lose pregnant staff for longer periods, who might have to pass more work to the other staff, or going to the trouble of hiring and training contract replacements. This might lead employers to be more reluctant to hire married females, or worse female workers as a whole (as far as possible).

    An extension in mandatory maternity leave should be backed up with changes to the employment act to ensure mothers to be are not unfairly dismissed by unscrupulous employers. While difficult to enforce, it would serve as a deterrent to employers from dismissing pregnant staff, as well as a confidence-building measure for working parents to be

    Time for the government to start protecting the workers and working mothers.
