    After 4 decades of national service conscription, many male Singaporeans have accepted it as part of their lives. The idea of changes to this conscription law is expected to draw mix reactions from the public. However this conscription policy is not cast in stone. We should not allow any policies to be untouched if we see flaws in it. Singapore's security cannot be taken lightly. I have highlighted how our conscription policy can be detrimental to our nation's security and social order instead. Not only does it lessen our defense capabilities, it has also weaken our social cohesiveness and economic competitiveness.

    There are many opinions from forummers about my views of our national service conscription, and my suggestions of changes to be made.

    One of the main concern is that abolishing conscription will deplete and weaken our defence force. I cannot disagree more. Using the "option" approach will enlarge and strengthen our defence force instead. Firstly, in my proposition, adult females citizens and permanent residents will have to do national service. Therefore it will provide an opportunity for them to enrol themselves with any of the defence forces, including the military. If our women folks are real tough talking patriots, then this will be an good opportunity for them to walk the talk and join the army. For permanent residents and foreigners-turn-citizens, it is a chance to prove their faith and loyalty to this country, and show that they are equally prepared to defend it with their lives, just like Singapore-born Singaporeans. Secondly, if all that I have been reading from forums and the press about how supportive and proud our male Singaporeans are about SAF, then I would expect the rate of "opting out" from the military force to be minimal. The combine result will be an enlarged defence force. In addition, it will be a defence force that is made up of highly motivated and committed personnel, as oppose to "reluctant soldiers" being conscripted into the military as what it is now. Anchored by professional personnel, our defence force must be more stronger than before.

    There are people who opposes abolishing conscription because they feel that it is unfair to them having completed their 2/2.5 years of NS and/or 13 years of reservist liability. However, not all will agree. We will probably get different opinions from those who are currently doing NS currently, and those who have yet to do so. However it is unacceptable not to change a policy if it is proven to be flawed. We should not allow it to continue and do irreversible damages to our next and future generations.

    You're being overidealistic here, and as a result, I find your arguments flawed.

    1. First of all, you say that conscription should be abolished, but in the next breath, you say that females and PRs should do NS (highlighted in blue). Aren't you being self-contradictory here? Isn't making females and PRs do NS forced conscription? By the way, second-generation PRs have to do NS, in case if you didn't know.

    2. How on earth do you know that most Singaporeans would not choose to opt out of NS if they were given that option? The only supporting evidence that you present for this is the impression you get from reading the forums and the press. That's not hard evidence, boy.

    Call me pessimistic, but trust me, most of us DON'T want to do NS if we were given that option. We would rather go on to university right after JC and leave the problem of NS to someone else. If our boys currently doing NS were given the option, the huge majority would choose to leave immediately and enter the workforce or go into further studies.

    3. In light of my point (2), how on earth are you going to get 'an enlarged defence force' made up of 'highly motivated and committed personnel'? With so many people taking up the option of not doing NS, you'll just have a depleted personnel strength.

    In addition, you still haven't answered one of my questions in an earlier post: how are you going to account for the economy? If you say that we would have 'an enlarged defence force' using your suggestions, Singapore's economy will take a huge beting in the process. Currently, the full strength of the SAF is around 350,000, a figure where NSmen make up the vast majority (about 250,000). If the defence force is enlarged to, say, 400,000 regulars (as per your suggestion of an 'enlarged defence force'), we would have a severe dent in available manpower. So who's going to teach the kids, treat the sick, run the industries and construct the buildings?

    Besides, as rich as Singapore is, we simply don't have the resources to maintain and upkeep such a large professional army. We already spend about 5% of our GDP on a largely conscript defence force, which is proportionately more than what the US spends (around 3.5%). And we're not fighting any wars in Iraq or Afghanistan. So how much more do you think we're willing to spend?
