  • Reggi
    25 Aug 02, 22:58
    Am relatively new to this forum, and found it interesting that there seems to be a large interest here on the possible outcome of a Singapore-Malaysia war.

    Personally, I have done my part serving in the SAF. Let me share my 5 cents worth of what I think is the SAF's role - that of being a doomsday device.

    If we are attacked, the SAF will punch into the enemy (e.g. Malaysia) with its superior firepower (esp air force and armour), cohesion of its combined arms as well as its technological edge (e.g. C3 - command, control and communications). With our early warning systems we'll probably be even doing a pre-emptive stike.

    Given the SAF edge in combat readiness (if our reservists think our guys are bad, check out how well the Malaysians are faring when their mechanised equipment, planes and ships have problems retaining minimum combat operativeness), early success in initial engagements seems very likely.

    But our goal of a swift and decisive victory is not easily reached. The enemy have the strategic depth which Singapore lacks. Once they pull in, the situation may very well end up in a war of attrition. How long can we afford to mobilise our NSmen, who are simultaneously playing important roles in our economy? How many NSmen can we afford to lose? Essentially, the activation of the SAF in war comes with a terrible price.

    The SAF acts as a force of deterrence. When attacked, we possess the ability to retaliate - to hit back and do so much damage as to outweigh any possible gain the attacker might have in starting the conflict.

    A earlier model of deterrence was that of Singapore being a 'poisoned shrimp' causing whoever who eats it a serious case of stomachache. But we have come far since then, and now possess the firepower to do some really serious damage.

    Hence the SAF is like a doomsday device. When attacked, we go *boom!* - we hit you hard, we get hit hard, everyone gets hit. But get this fact straight - when the war starts, everyone loses.

    Hotheads on both sides of the fence should be aware of this fact, this price to be paid by both sides if hot-air rhethorics and military adventurism get carried away too far one day.....
