    Dear friends,

    Thanks for contributing into this discussion, its really good to see everyone speaking up! I've been discovering alot of interesting discussions on this forum and i'm surprised i've not found it earlier! Alas a place to discuss country matters with others who really care!

    I've deliberately chosen a more provocative word 'racist' to stir up emotions of fellow singaporeans in hope of hearing your views about this matter. It turned out much better than i expected to be honest. I was prepared to get flamed big time for my remarks.

    Yes i questioned, are singaporeans Racists? I asked myself this question and I can put my hand on my heart and say No we can't be classified as racists as backed by most people who have replied.

    Ignorant, selfish, naive and proud, (and a whole lot more) we are all guilty of it. Lets not kid ourselves, havent we heard of ugly singaporeans misbehaving overseas? how about singaporeans littering the minute they step out of singapore into JB or china?

    I am criticising singapore because singapore means alot to me. I want the best for our country and we cannot get too complacent about it. Whats the use of us cheering ourselves and ignoring the facts that really matters? I must also point out that being critical is distinctively different from cynical attitudes.

    I have met tooooooooooooo many cynical singaporeans. Its such a pity...

    There are so many factors involved in this discussion, i try not to deviate from the main points too much or it won't end. I shall summarise on my points.

    Singapore is a multi-cultural society. We pride ourselves to live together in harmony, its something very special and we should embrace it. Dont let it go down the drain.

    We should be more tolerant of others, be more open minded and accept other's cultures, its a fact we have to live with so make the best out of it. (Singapore will become more and more culturally diverse and immigration is here to stay)

    Edcuation is important. Not just academic excellence, but even socially, about how we should treat our neighbours, how we treat other cultures. This kind of education starts from the homes, from a young age, not from moral education in primary school.

    If we are willing to take a tiny step back, or be alittle patient, i'm sure alot of misunderstandings can be sorted out. For example, one of the earlier posts refering to the malay soldiers name calling the 'chinese people', to be honest, i dont see whats wrong with that. Perhaps they have no other way to call chinese? I dont think they mean any harm, simply because they have no idea they were being offensive in the first place. If we didnt take those to heart but instead join in with the lads there, i have no doubts the whole platoon will glue together well.

    There are always bad sheep in every society. We cannot generalise the entire race/culture/religion/country from our bad experiences with them. China has 1.2billion population. If they have just as many proportion of bad sheeps as singaporeans, their bad sheeps alone could possibly be the entire singaporean population. So lets not jump in to criticise others so easily, it does no good for ourselves, we will only look ugly doing so.

    Lets get stronger and prevent ourselves from getting cheated. That should be the way forward. We are too straigth bat, if we continue to be ignorant, we will never face up to the PRC in the future. There will be more and more incidents of singaporeans getting cheated, played out, etc and its our fault for not living up to face the real world.

    All these social problems cannot be rectified quickly. Its a mindset and will take generations to change. I think our political climate has attributed to much of these social dilemmas. We are obedient and yet complacent. We have the creature comforts and we get too proud. We think we are 1st world and have the skills in business but we end up picking up the wrong side of the bargain when we deal with other countries.

    Am i over critical? Perhaps, but i will act and improve, not just whine away. Singapore has a fantastic future ahead, lets make it rock!
