    I think whether we like it or not, the paper chase is here to stay... Its a matter of getting used to it, living with it and making your choices. People who have taken both paths have both found their niche.

    For your info novellum, studying is never about memorising and regurgitation, whether in a poly, a jc or a uni. I feel sorry for the people you know who do that.

    I don't necessarily agree that its more important to be hardworking because we're in an economic crisis. Its exactly because of this, and the fact that its an employer's market out there now that qualifications will matter more.

    A potential employer will never know if u are a dilligent individual just by looking at u through 3 rounds of interviews. They'll want to know what u have to offer on paper before u even get a chance to start being hardworking!

    Hence, qualifications and relevant experience will be the things that opens doors in the immediate aftermath of one's graduation, not virtues and "whats inside" 'cos they can't see it and quantify it. Its after this initial stage that what you are will bring you to the next level.

    And I'm sure u're aware that positions offered by companies often come with minimum pre-requisite for applicants. A position for an engineer will unlikely be open to an ITE grad unless they've had an overwhelming bounty of relevent experience while a fresh engineering grad *might* stand a fighting chance... but of course, the position will go to someone with BOTH experience AND qualifications...

    AND precisely because its an economic depression now, grads are lowering their expectations. so, there's more people fighting for the few jobs available which brings me right back to the point that the Boss gets to pick and choose the best so dare to dream if u think they'll pick one with lower qualification and less relevant experience but promises that he/she will work "vely hard"... yes, sure, a positive attitude counts... but many factors are in consideration as well...

    Find a job u like and work forever??? Dare to dream... why on earth do u think so many people have been laid off recently? 'cos they don't like their job??? job security is not what it was before... it has been estimated that in this new globalised economy, economic fluctuations will result in people having up to 3 or more careers in 1 lifetime. And because we all need to eat, there is no longer the luxury of choosing only the jobs u like anymore, but the ones which u are most qualified for and most likely to get and keep. (if that also happens to be what u like to do, good for you...)

    And so your friend *wants* to go for a degree... so encourage them instead of being such a wet blanket! Everybody's entitled to choose u know... It may not be the right choice to you, but then again, how do you know?

    You decided to be happy with your diploma, so let others choose when they want to be happy.
