    For the first time in 3 elections, a credible party emerged from the shadows, silent and bidding, and when they sprang up suddenly, created enough panic that even 'lao jiaos' like Goh CT and Lee HL fumbled with their campaign speeches.

    The threat of the WP must have been so enormous that for once there was genuine fear that 1 GRC might fall. And not just fall, concede to a party which may just have the quality and goods to deliver a better town council, better hope and a better future for the people.

    So fearful that the Ruling Party absolutely lost all focus in their original campaign agenda, or maybe they never had a solid one to begin with, that they concentrated their tried and trusted tactic of character assassination on James Gomez, hoping to sink the fast-riding WP ship with the speed of the titanic.

    Unsurprisingly, it may have backfired; the people are wiser now, less gullible and more educated. Piecing together snippets of a CCTV and CNA footage and drawing a speculative conculsion was hardly detective's work. There was less malice in James Gomez's foolish blunder than JBJ's criminal accusation at MM Lee in the 98 election.

    What price has the Ruling Party paid this time?

    The 7% increase in Low Thia Khiang's SMC indicates a strong REJECTION of SGD100mil worth of taxpayer's goodies, and a firm rejection of the despicable methods to 'buy' hougang voters. Eric Low's character hardly endears him to the constituents. And Low Thia Khiang's firm assertion that the upgrading money was in fact the people's monies being used unscrupulously for one party's gain; it brings back sad comparisions to the NKF saga.

    I'm glad aljunied showed strong support for Sylvia Lim and her team, even if she missed the 7% cut needed to win majority.

    Our people are slowly maturing into a society with higher moral standards and gracious ethics.

    Sadly, it is predominant only in OPPOSITION wards, where the people that matter have truly EARNED their votes.
