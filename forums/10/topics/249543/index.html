    Is the FT being imported those that come in to fufill special niches that Singaporeans cannot/ don't want to fufill or are they imported because they are cheaper to run then the Singaporean?

    Local package or not, FTs do not have to worry about many of the other things your average Singaporean has to contend with that applies only to locals. This means that when they get their pay, they really get paid instead of getting their salary siphoned for other purposes. Even if the FT and the Singaporean earn the same, the Singaporean is already at an disadvantage.

    There's no way the Singaporean can compete except to sell himself cheaper and work much harder for the same amount of work. Given all this, why not become the FT instead of being a local worker?

    The question I believe is this: Is Singapore a people magnet at the expense of it's own people?

    Attracting FT is not a difficult task, but recruiting local talent is more difficult given one has to make internal changes to policies, living standards, and systems of thought... things which apparently the powers-that-be are unwilling to fiddle with. So instead of encouraging... lets say more creative talents to blossom in Singapore by loosening the restriction on media laws and the like and accepting all forms of creativity (and not just the sterile forms that agree with the status quo) as valuable, we decide to take the easy path out by importing creative talents from other countries who already had the advantage of their more uncontrained upbringing in a more liberal country and the like.

    In fact, it seems some want to have their cake and eat it to. We want to excel in sports but make no real move to nurture such talents in our young, driving home the point that one needs to go through the route learning path in order to become anything. It's laughable our sports school would have rejected Fandi and yet we wonder how come we need to import FTs to make up for this lack of talent.

    It is not a game of musical chairs, more of poision ball... who wants to be a local worker when prospects as an FT are so much better?

    Do I have anything against FTs? Not really I don't mind them being around. The real problem is that the local is basically fighting with one hand tied behind his back when it comes to trying to survive alongside the FTs. The fault is not with the FT for being talent, but for some systems that are taking the short route out to our talent "issues" by trying to import talents instead of generate it.

    Instead of having an FT policy that acts as a way of enriching our soceity and injecting talents that we do not have to help us grow in this area... it seems we have crossed way over into importing whatever we have just to make up for the lack, and yet not taking any concrete measures to make sure steps are in place for the local resources to be developed.

    At the end of the day, can our own FTs really trive, supposing they decide to settle down, as valued added Singaporeans? Given our systems I think their children will end up being pretty much like us, and the talent orginally imported would have been squandered.

    It's great the FT team helped us to win the Tiger Cup, but I wonder if their children, assuming they had their parent's sporting talent, would truly be able to follow in their parent's legacy in our kind of system and soceity? Or will they, after years of grappling with our kind of educational system find themselves just like another local... being told that they don't have enough sporting talent and that the next generation of FTs need to be imported to make up for this "lack"?

    Or we importing FTs to enrich ourselves, or just as a one-generation stopgap that we must carry out for who knows how long at the expense of the locals?
