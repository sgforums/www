    Our politicians appear to have been suffering some kind of megalomania - the need to prove their supremacy and do big things.

    As long as our median wages of lower 80% of the populations have suffered drastic drop in their wages when compared with early 1990s, and property values have dropped thirty percents from the peak their leadership cannot be said to be a success.

    Reason - too much conceptualisation by too many idea men. The real talents wanted by the people to lead them out of this morass is dynamism, action and implementation abilities which are mostly left to the middle and lower rung with the top missing in action.

    In a soccer game, common sense would tell anyone that it is not strikers or forwards that score goals but in our system we have so many forwards without the midfielders to back them.

    Ask any ministers what had happened to our many economic restructuring to value-added technology-driven model and how are they going about to create a cosmopolitan and inclusive society they will give you a blank stare.

    If we are to set up something alongside the MPs system of government called CDA, Town Council or Mayors, we should ensure that there is no overlapping of roles and duties with that provided by the MPs, the government departments like PA, Ministry of Youth, and Community Development and ministries.

    Singaporeans among the middle to lower income groups have been going through a tough time from 1970s with citizens retrenched, wages dropping and MNCs and many businesses pulling out due to high costs of operations etc.

    As a government, it must be aware of such high-cost problems and our gradual losses in economic competitiveness.

    In such a scenario, a caring and entrepreneurial government will ensure that the taxes paid and surpluses belonging to the people are prudently spent in bolstering the country's competitiveness and not splash them on concrete arches, nicer bus stops, more tearing down of buildings to build newer ones.

    It should also not try to increase ministries and departments in the like of more CDAs, PAs, MYCD, Town Councils and Mayors when the such functions can be served by existing PAs, HDB, and MPs.

    Can one imagine how much of tax payers' monies are being splashed on all such unessential projects that only look good, suppose to improve communication of problems and feedbacks and community development when as can be seen in election 2006 were actually only built to serve the ruling party in increasing its own support.

    Therefore I consider that our leaders have lost touched with the ground, failing to understand the unemployment problems affecting thousands and the high costs which cause deep-seated problems to its own people.

    The tendency to use the billions to splash on big projects and creating more Mayors to look good from the outside which do not actually create technology start-ups and jobs and lower our costs of living is a wrong trend.

    Billions are being lost in poorly evaluated overseas projects in an impulsive manner which could easily be channelled back for technology job creations, long-term economic re-structuring or setting up of endowment low-risk fund to earn returns to lower our high costs of utility and other essential services.

    Minimum government can do is to put our surpluses where the mouth is and we will become a properous country in no time.

    We have so many scholars now joining the government becoming MPs and ministers. Why not let them look at such tendency to splash taxpayers' hard-earned monies on concrete arches and flower boxes and channel them to re-position the whole economy, lower costs of living and create more jobs.
