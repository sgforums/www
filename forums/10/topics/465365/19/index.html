    Instead of complaining about the current shortfalls of our governmental policies, why not listen to the alternatives/suggestions of our opposition parties and how viable those suggestions are? 

    My main issue with the Workers' Party is that they seem to go along and echo the complains of various sectors of our populace without actually giving a concrete/viable plan to how they are going to actually run the bloody nation. 

    Never should you oppose the Singaporean government (PAP) for the sheer sake of opposing, nor should you blindly support PAP either.

    Having travelled to other nations around Southeast asia for holidays/community service projects, Singapore has been good to me, my family and my friends. Perhaps the policies are not to the people's best interests, but they are still sound and are capable of providing a roof over our heads, enough food to survive and an education system which allows for studious people to succeed if they have the will and desire to do so. 

    Even so, such an argument that we 'Should be glad for what we have' is not a good argument for debate either. There certainly are issues and policies that can be changed for the better, and things that can certainly be improved upon.

    Looking the issues with other nations and their politics, one can certainly say that there will forever be a section of the populace who will feel "hard done"/"injustice" done towards them and their fathers, while another section would feel glad for what the government has done for them. 

    The best party that can be in charge of Singapore should be the party that is in charge of Singapore. What I fear is that with the voting of more opposition members and parties, what we get is not a better Singapore but one that is divided over her own policies and becoming just another nation that becomes a joke of a government (All talk and no work done). As much as we can hate on our current government, I think we should all give credit where it is due and give criticisms when it should be given. Voting in opposition members for the sake of hating on the PAP, instead of voting for the person who would truly benefit your community and Singapore as a whole would be the absolute wrong move, and do no benefit but for your own egoistic needs. 

    It also closely resembles our current societal attitudes, the "ME" attitude of the 21st century. I would believe that quite a number of people on Singaporean forums are of the newer generation, the more technologically savvy generation (I myself included). Everything should be for my OWN BENEFIT, if something does not benefit me or my friends/family, then it should be changed to suit my OWN NEEDS. And if things do not go MY WAY, then I will complain to high heavens instead of buckling down and finding a solution on your own. (Sorry, but even while reading this I feel like it's overly simplistic and that I'm merely generalising an entire section of Singapore into a certain category, but I do strongly feel that most of us can relate to such attitudes in ourselves to a certain/great extent!) Sad...
