    In today's Straits Times (Insight Pg 33), Chua Mui Hoong suggested that Lim Boon Heng could have "slayed the sacred cow of withdrawal at age 55".

    What she meant was that the option to withdraw CPF money up to a limited amount could have been removed by Lim but instead, he chose to only up the retirement age.

    She also went on to say those who complained about the age increase are mostly those from the "low to median income" group. These people are very keen to use the money accumulated over the years but ironically, these are also the very people who need the money over the long term.

    That is what the CPF's system is for:

    1) Partial and limited withdrawal at age 55 and
    2) Monthly withdrawal at age 62

    Withdrawal at age 55 ensures that Singaporeans may enjoy the fruits of their labour but at the same time, leaving an amount for retirement.

    The withdrawal at age 62 ensures that Singaporeans may live beyond their money.

    She also mentioned that it's a "fact of the world" that people are living longer, thereby justifying the age increase.

    According to a survery by the UN (Human Development Report 2006), Singaporeans' life expectancy at birth in 2004 is 78.9. Those retiring within the next 20 years shall still, on average, continue to die in their 70s.

    Why the need to raise the retirement age? By doing so will only mean that these Singaporeans shall die before they can fully enjoy their CPF savings.

    Party propaganda once again. She, as a reporter is supposed to write objectively but now she is writing to further the party's cause and justify Lim's "good decision". The age increase looks inevitable.
