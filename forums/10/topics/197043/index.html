    This HDB upgrading program - under whichever IUP or MUP program - is a sheer waste of surplus funds.

    In certain neighborhood, construction works that have been completed were torn down and modification works made; while work sequence were seen to be poorly disorganised with contractors having to return to continue work that could have been performed previously at one instant; and with some works that have been poorly designed despite the good intent.

    While some PAP wards had seen upgrading within 15 years since the estate were raised, some other PAP wards seem to see no works being done since the estate was lived in more then 20 years ago.

    It begs the question, who is in charge of the upgrading program ?

    Is such program decided and managed at the Town Council level, or is it decided at the Ministry of National Development level ?

    If Town Council has the responsibility to decide and managed such upgrading works - within their Town Council's own surplus budgets or otherwise - why should the Ministry impinge on the rights of the Town Councils by passing Ministerial directives and guidelines that restrict the Town Councils ?

    If this is not political wayang taking place, and perpetuated by the Ruling Political Party to indirectly prevent Opposition Town Councils from being more effective in meeting the needs of its own constituents.
