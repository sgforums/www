    Here's a definition since you asked for it:

    "Political Corruption", as a term by itself, to date is still being discussed without much headway. There are several schools of thought on this, continually arguing over the definition in order to justify their convictions.

    I propose then, in order to get started on this topic, without having to waste time on definitions, that we use the simpler definitions afforded in dictionaries.

    Political, as an adjective to the the noun, Corruption. Corruption by Politicians so to speak. Then it leaves us to define Corruption.

    Corruption, very simply put is (according to dictionaries) - "dishonesty and illegal behaviour by people in power or authority"

    Put together, it will be reasonable to define for this argument, Political Corruption as Dishonest and/or Illegal Behavious by Politicians who are in authority.

    Now, when MM Lee declared that our leaders were incorrupt, it should be seen in this context:

    Singapore Leaders are not guilty of "illegal" behaviour according to OUR law.

    The reason why we dont think this counts for much, is because Singapore's Law is passed by only one body without a referendum from the people. The Parliament which is dominated by PAP.

    If I was a leader of a country, and I made every kind of law [and people had no power to complain] to protect my own ambitions and gains, I would most certainly be incorrupt by definition. There is no law that I've broken and hence not illegal.

    Thus the stated definition of corruption is found lacking.

    In view of all this, and addressing your very first motivation to start this thread, what we are effectively saying is that, there is nothing to be in awe of such a statement. It doesnt count for anything.
