    Don't rely on tired formula'

    The New Paper
    3 March 2005

    East Coast GRC's Mr Tan Soo Khoon again stirred the hornet's nest.

    Think last year's 8.3 million tourist arrivals and $9.6 billion in tourism receipts is worth shouting about?

    Think again, he says.

    The tourism sector's contribution to GDP has actually fallen by half from 6 per cent in 1993 to the current 3 per cent.

    Mr Tan's call?

    Don't ape what others do. Come up with original ideas. Capitalise on our unique strengths.

    And he added that relying 'on the same tried and tired formula by simply repackaging it under a new slogan' won't work

    Already, he argued, Singapore's had two golden opportunities snatched away by its regional competitors.

    One, we lost the Formula One Grand Prix event to Malaysia.

    Result: They get an estimated RM$1 billion ($428m) injection into their economy each year.

    Two, we lost the Disneyland 'super theme park' to Hong Kong.

    The result: Millions of extra tourists, thousands of extra jobs headed Hong Kong's way.

    What we're left with, he says, is 'neither-here-nor-there' attractions like Haw Par Villa, and Sentosa's 'artificial beaches'.

    Oh, and as he puts it, 'one ageing musical fountain'.
