    Good points, all. Still, there's more to it.

    The point isn't simply whether we can bathe or not. In general,

    1.) Before bookout, NSmen do try to smell good *if it's feasible*. They may bathe, apply deodorant/powder, wear new uniform, wear civies etc.

    We *do make the effort wherever we *can.

    2.) However, sprucing up isn't always possible. Sometimes you don't want to shower because you don't want to sabo your mates during area cleaning (by not being available to clean and by leaving soap suds etc). Sometimes there are simply too few cubicles for too many people. Sometimes the showers are down. Etc, etc, etc.

    The point is, smelling nice isn't always possible in the SAF. We don't like it, but we *can't do much about it*.

    3.) Often, sprucing up is futile. Even after showering, you may still sweat quite a bit. Sometimes/often (more so with recruits/trainees), you'll kena corporal punishment. Often you'll have to walk a long distance to the gate, and then to the bus-stop/MRT station. If you're wearing civies, it's bad enough. Imagine how sweaty you'll be in no. 4. Often you'll book out when it's hot- it's just a question of *how hot. 

    In any case, you'll probably pick up at least a mildly unpleasant odour.

     

    We don't want wild applause for defending our country etc etc. But we *do hope for some modicum of empathy and respect, if not understanding. We want others to at least *try to put themselves in our shoes and see where we're coming from, not least because that's what we'd do for them.

    Seeing things from all perspectives does not mean accommodating the other perspective. Just to be clear, i won't say that Fryderyk HPH is doing that. But i do want to stress that reasonableness =/= blind accommodation- esp if one side is being unreasonable.

    To illustrate. I might understand why the Taliban chop off people's hands for stealing, or why they force women to wear burqas etc. I understand that their extreme interpretation of their otherwise benign religion makes them think that way. BUT that doesn't mean that i accommodate them by agreeing that mutilation is a good punishment for theft, or that a woman should be forcibly deprived of her freedom.
