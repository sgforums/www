    Much of what you say is sensible, with one *big exception. I don't think all guys only "act" for those 2 years.

    Granted, unwillingness to do NS does contribute to the "chao keng" problem. Yet, even if ppl dislike the job, many will still do their best- if for no other reason than to get it over with instead of semula again and again.

    However, this is still beside the point. The point is that even the most "diehard" NSmen can't realistically be expected to attain the same standard as professionals. NSmen are not just soldiers, but *citizens also. That in no way detracts from their contributions. It simply means that NSmen cannot devote their lives to soldiering. As a consequence, many of them lose their skills through atrophy. Precious time is wasted maintaining a standard, or at least keeping it from all going to hell. That time could be spent on professionals and helping them advance to better standards.

    As sgdiehard said so appropriately: "you will be really dellusioned if you think you are doing a job that some regulars should do." I cannot agree more.

    Therefore, whether NSmen, facing low morale and unhappiness, "act" or not is not material. What matters is that we can't expect them to be as good as regulars, because we can't blame them for being citizens instead of full-time soldiers.
