    I haven't fire yet but got touch before...so these are my views.

    The SAR21 is heavier than the m16...about 1kg more but it felt lighter becoz it's compact and short compare with the M16. Which is longer and very "ting teh" at times.

    The SAR21 is bullpup designed.....meaning the magazine is at the butt. This can be odd for older soldiers who are used to the M16. In the M16, you click press the button with your index finger and the Magazine come out. The SAR21 button is at the back so you need t use the other hand to press. Very odd position :/

    The scope is cool. You see clearer and it's easier to aim. Dun need to aglin any front sight and rear sight coz there's just a crossshair in the scope. They also cum with rubber covers to cover the scope if not use.
    I ask those who use before. They say raining the scope sometimes "fog out" or if got water it blur the vision. So if not using better cover the scope to protect.

    Cocking the weapon need some getting used to. The charging handle is relatively difficult the pull the bolt back cause the handle is twistable and rather flimsy. It's also rather stiff when I tried out with the display set but it could be due to the lack of oil in the bolt. (m16 sometimes also like that)

    One thing that I concern about the rifle is whether does it have a "combat loading" function. You see, the M16's bolt will locked back when the last round has been fired. So you just need to change the magazine and ease the spring so the bolt will slam back with the new round. They don't teach these in BMT due to safety resons with units will teach.

    I spoke to the demostrator about it, he say that SAR21 also has comabt loading function but it's hard to simulate without a round. hmmm.....

    I also scared the SAR21 will make you deaf. The chamber is near to your face so it'll surely be very loud when you fire.
