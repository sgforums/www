    In a public sector design and build contract, the main contractor for Nicole Highway MRT circle line should have undertaken the following:-

    (1) Submitting all design development in detailed shop drawings with evaluations and soil investigation tests to the requirements of LTA.

    (2) Compliance with all site and soil conditions in carrying out the works.

    (3) Warranting the design to meet all conditions, adequacy, suitability and integrity.

    LTA should have obtained all these shop drawings and soil reports and method statements and endorsed its consent before proceeding with works.

    Did report show presence of prevalent marine clay. How much quantities of water will be drained out from the surrounding and how dewatering will affect the subsidence of surrounding roads and bridges causing the whole roads and bridge structures to weigh in our the retaining wall below the excavated site.

    If design and build contract is to be administered according to the contractual terms and conditions then there is no possibility of overlooking the need to strengthen the retaining wall sufficiently to prevent massive loading from the subsided roads and bridges or other structures.

    The inquiry needs to be holistic and look at the big picture as well as the small picture at foremen and supervisors' level to get to the truths. Regards.

    Forumites might still remember the Hotel New World collapse, where 33 persons were killed when the whole building collapsed due to malpractices and lack of supervision of building processes.

    The Minister promised to leave no stone unturned and take thorough measures to prevent another accident. A committee of inquiry was also set up to establish the cause of the collapse and recommend preventive measures to avoid future occurrences.

    MOM/MND/LTA/BCA were charged with responsibilities to prevent similar accidents or collapses as recommended by Committee of Inquiry whose report was widely hailed as the final solution to check on sporadic site safety problems.

    Since Hotel New World collapse, have we improved our worksite safety. There were many more structural collapses such as collapses of veranda in Toa Payoh factory and many more.

    In view of repeated work site and building failures, isn't it time again to re-examine the efficiency and effectiveness of our BCA/LTA/MOM/MND in overseeing and preventing further accidents.

    Will public projects like MRT lines come under strict supervision? Perhaps, doable work processes should be set up in Department Report (Enforcement)/Work Processes as suggested in "HOW TO DO IT" posting. Without doable processes, can policies or measures really be implemented consistently and successfully. Just my humble opinion. Regards to all.
