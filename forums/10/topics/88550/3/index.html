    Hi there Malarica,

    First of all thank you for your Good Question.
    Secondly, thanks to all forumites for their views.

    Thirdly. this is my answer:

    Most of the time, i have been a silent observer and i have been thinking, for i have certainly leaarned alot from the previous threads but i don't know how the forumites think as to such specific questions, so hence my thread.

    It was indeed revelating to see the exchange, from this i realised i can achieve a few things:

    1. get more information and views from passionate and learned individuals in the forum;
    2. allow this information exchange to all interestred forumites;
    3. invoke some awareness in all our forumites with exchange and discussion and debates on respective views;

    In my view, Singaporeans generally lack passion with regards to their political destiny. This passion (or rather the lack of) has allowed the ruling PAP to drive the country to its present state today.

    Now look here, i am not discounting what the PAP has done before in the leadership of the nation till early 1980s. However, the continued hegemony over parliament by the PAP is in my opinion going to drive the country to ruin.

    The nation building PAP members did well. BUT most of them are gone.
    DID they make lives difficult for people? No.
    Did they achieve for Singapore? YES
    What about the present PAP members and the Swollen cabinet of theirs?
    What have they achieved for Singapore?
    What can they promise for Singapore's future?

    They claim that we are no good and we need foreign talent, we must welcome competition with foreign talent and compete with the world players and in the world market. We must move out of our comfort zone.

    But they claim to be the brightest and the world's best and nobody can compete with them that is why they deserve the world's highest salaries amongst political leaders. They dislike and are intolerant of compeition, everyone knows i need not say more.

    Are they leading by example? Our so called mighty leaders?

    They brought the country to a crossroads then they claim to open up and try to remake Singapore yet they have the cheek to ask us for ideas to undo the wrangle they made.
    How many ideas actually get implemented?
    If they are the ones having the salary and the power and they dont have ideas, what are they there for?

    When they attempted to remake Singapore, they claimed that no stone will be left unturned.
    After 1200 man years spent by the Remaking Singapore committee, our PM then DPM said: Its not that no stones were left unturned, its that we carefully put the stones back in place.

    In my humble opinoin, if life does not seem to be getting better for most people, it is time to check the PAP government seriously.
    The high costs and pressure cooker society is taking a toll on the people and their livelihood their sense of civic mindeness and their future. everyone for himself, talk about nationhood and nation beilding?

    I wholly agree with RobertTeh for his remark that for the Govt to be loved it must first love the people. It must realise that It was elected (however remote in history when that happened) to SERVE THE PEOPLE.
    BY THE PEOPLE, FOR THE PEOPLE.

    Does the present PAP government love the people? or do they love themselves? You'll have to decide for yourself.

    Remember:

    Singaporeans when they grow old will not assured of State welfare for them despite their massive contributions in direct taxes or indirect costs.

    Any retirement income by way of compulsory CPF savings which is difficult to achieve by most Singaporeans will be whittled down by high land cost and cost of living.

    If all of these were to happen by way of the "choice" by the people, i submit to the rule of the majority but how often has "Choice" been on the Menu for singaporeans?

    The choice will only come if there is a challenger, the challenger may not come forward if the people prove to be too meek and obedient for the challenger to feel that there is a worthwhile cause.

    For the able and the educated feel that if they can still survive and maybe prosper, why find trouble, why challenge the incumbent?
    If cannot tahan already, just pack up bags and leave. Leave the rest to fend for themselves. This is happening already.

    For good opposition to emerge, the people must signal at the coming GE that they want an opposition and they want it bad. Its the chicken and egg thingy.

    Passion is lacking in all stratas of the Singapore society, from the leaders to the people. Its passion that we lack. its passion that i am trying to invoke. I apologise if any quarrels were caused amongst the forumites. Its my little attempt to make it all happen for Singapore.

    I am only Singaporean and i am small but i nurse a hope.

    The GRCs institutionalise a multi racial parliament. The surveys and trends tabulated by the government indicated that if the previous SMCs electoral system is not changed,minority candidates will gradually have little chance to win any seats because of the ppl's tendency to vote along ethnic lines.

    However a GRC system can also be a double edge sword.While presently they work to the advantage of the PAP and appear to be the opposition's grave yard in the short term, in the longer run they may actually enable the opposition to win more seats faster than was possible when they have to fight it out, one-to-one in SMCs.

    Eg in a 6 member super GRC an opposition team can anchor it with two charismatic leaders one of whom is an ethnic minority + 4 other team members one of which could preferably be a women.

    With a underlying 20% discontended voters, the opposition need to capture another 15% minority votes + 15% of the middle ground + 1% luck to succed.
    GRCs are not as invincible as they seem. All the opposition need are credible candidates of high calibre.
