    Let's not forget two blatant myths the fascist media distorted which give as accurate a reflection of a warped reality as having people believe Santa Claus actually delivers presents to kids every Christmas.

    Firstly, there were those headlines that the PAP was not returned to power on nomination day. While it's true that the PAP had a theoretical chance of not garnering 50% of the seats in parliament, the myth fails to account for the fact that no other single party could have even chalked up 37 seats(to rival the number PAP had been assured on nomination day), even accounting for the theoretical chance that the PAP lost all 47 contested seats. In essence, under the practice of a parliamentary democracy, how could you effectively be 'out of power' if you still held the most seats in a coalition government?

    Second myth - the PAP had a 66.6% mandate. This is utter nonsense. The fact of the matter is PAP won 66.6% of the valid votes cast, and not 66.6% of the total votes cast. Evidently, the figure does not account for the substantial share of invalid votes and needless to say, had this portion been factored in the statistics, their mandate is actually lower than 66.6%! Quite simply, how people who haven't been in favour of you(although not necessarily against you) could be cast aside and omitted from one's "mandate" is beyond me...

    Well, welcome to the wonderful world of Singapore politics with a 'first world' government and a 'first world' media...
