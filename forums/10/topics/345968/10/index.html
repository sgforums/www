    I used to worked for oil company, just share a little that I know. Singapore prospers not from oil refinery but the whole petroleum industry. In a nut shell, Singapore, as one of the busiest ports in the world, a major oil refining center, the key chemicals production and distribution center, supported by ship building and repair, financial and other auxiliary services, such as insurance, is not easy, I am not saying not possible, but not easy for any other country in the region to emulate.

    Besides capacities, the 3 refineries in Singapore used to be owned by 6 major oil companies, Shell, Esso, Mobil, Caltex, BP, SPC. Its efficiency enable Singapore to refine oils, from light crude from Saudi to heavy crude  from Indonesia, giving higher yields than local refineries, i.e. for every barrel, our refineries produce much more value than other refineries. We refine crude from Saudi, Indonesia...they have their refineries, but not always for the right crude, giving the right product, at the right place, the right time.

    Singapore can distribute refined products, from gasoline to chemical feedstocks much easier than any other refineries in the region, the chemicals plants in jurong island is a major market.

    Petroleum suppliers, refiners, traders, tankers owners use Singapore as a major one stop service center for their business that stretching from europe to Asia, and from Singapore, you can stretch to australia.

    If you miss the Suez Canal, you need to ago round the whole Africa continent. Kra Canal allows you to bypass only the Malayan pennisular, you save one day shipping time. If you spend 7 to 10 days sailing all the way from Europe or the Middle East to the Far East. How will the 1 day make any difference? especially so if you actually bypass an important rest point for refueling, repairing or even picking up or unloading some cargo.

    Economically, it is never favorable to build the Kra Canal.

    Politically, if Singapore continues to be friendly to China, Japan, ...and the oil producing countries, I believe China and Japan would prefer to handle the Singapore government than the Thai, no?

    to sum up, if you travel 8 hours on a higher way from Singapore to Penang, you won't want to take a 30 minutes short cut on a muddy road, to bypass KL, where you can have a good rest, good food, shopping....

    Kra Canal is the 30 minutes muddy short cut. Only AndrewPKYap loves going in the mud and throw mud at Singapore.
