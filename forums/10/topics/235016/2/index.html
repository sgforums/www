Indeed the PRC did try to finish off the nationalists with a series of coastal attacks in the 1950s, but suffered huge losses. The PRC government wants reunification with Taiwan as a subset of the PRC political structure.

    Taiwan (Republic of) is a short-lived state born in 1895. It was the first republic in East Asia and was declared by Governor Liu Ming Quan. There were two major reasons for its existence. The Taiwanese have moved to Taiwan (Formosa at that time) since the Ming Dynasty, and have enjoyed de facto self-governence for hundreds of years. Unfortunately, Japan's increasing aggression towards the Qing Dynasty forced them to cede Formosa to become Japan's first overseas colony. The local leaders were fed up of mismanagement by the mainland and opposed Japanese rule, so they declared an Republic, althought it was crushed by the Japanese in less than a year. This rebellious spirit remains active in Taiwan today.

    The Chinese nationals (PRC,ROC) want reunification with each other, but under their own respective power structure. The Taiwanese nationals (especially from southern Taiwan like Tainan and Kaohsiung) are suspicious of mainlanders of any political affliation.
