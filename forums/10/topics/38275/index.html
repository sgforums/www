    You have made very relevant points that shows your perceptive observations of current events.

    At times I wonder if the Government realise that Singapore was a great trading post, made possible by the tremendous enterprise of the migrant communities that formed Singapore - even during the Colonial Days.

    The indomitable and ambitious spirit of entreprenuerships from the PRIVATE Merchants, Tradesmen, Artisans, Laborers and Unskilled Workers have created dreams that led to the foundation of Singapore.

    With or Without the Government, Singapore will be Singapore, as Hong Kong has become Hong Kong even without any interference from Government policies and regulatory inputs.

    The Singapore Government had interfered with the laissez-faire system established since the founding of Singapore, and has brought us one full circle to events in the historical past - and in the process doused the flame of enterprise and entrepreneurship which is currently being lamented.

    Since the 1950's, Singapore was serviced by more then six to eight bus companies that were ALL PRIVATELY owned. The bus fares were strictly regulated to prevent unwarranted price hikes that affect the cost of transportation and enriching the Private Owners.

    Without the price hike reviews, and with tight fare controls, the bus operators were caught between the pressures to upgrade their operations with newer buses, and to keep their costs down.

    By 1970's, the Government of Independent Singapore, decided that it was not efficient to have bus companies owned and operated Privately; and forced the various companies to amalgamate or to be closed.

    Subsequently, the entire bus transport system was basically NATIONALISED, for the simple reason of efficiency and less wastage of resources, that was to result in higher standards with better transport costs controls resulting in affordable fares.

    The full circle has now returned, with more bus companies appearing (supplementary services) to support the three main stream companies (SBS, Translink, CCS).

    Although newer buses have appeared, the bus fares have seen tremendous hikes, when compared to those charged before, and when compared to similar size operation and standards as found in Hong Kong. (It is ironical that the Privately operated Bus Companies were prevented from any fare hikes in the 1950's).

    This is only one part of the full circle experience, as the same reasons were given to remove the many Privately Owned Taxi services; and the full circle now returning with many taxi companies being formed today but all State Owned through GLCs.
