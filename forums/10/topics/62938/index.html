Foreign Talent At Ministerial Level

  • eze
    29 Nov 03, 20:22
    I would like to suggest the benefits of expanding our wealth of local expertise in government, to include foreign talent at the Ministerial level. This is so that we can ensure we get the best brains from global markets who will be competitive and experienced enough to represent Singapore in the economic and social policy processes. This way, in times of economic policy making, we will ensure that we are making strict decisions based on the criteria with which we suggest our workforce should be-- i.e. meritocracy, capability, IQ, highly trained, experience, and willing to work for market driven salaries. If, we agree that foreign talent will help our industries and sectors gain competitiveness and vibrancy. Why should it not help the process of government? How do we know now that we have the best brains in Government in a globally competitive way? How do we know the decisions are best, when it is not a gobally open environment? Inasmuch, I feel we should reach out to communities and news environments overseas to appoint the best educated and most experienced for government. The process should be as globally open as possible.

    If a migrant foreigner is prepared to give up his Citizenship from his country of origin, and take up an oath of Singapore Citizenship, he is as much a Singaporean as any Singaporean whose ancestors are immigrants as well.
