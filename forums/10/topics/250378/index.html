    SINGAPORE, April 24 (Reuters) - SMRT Corp. (SMRT.SI: Quote, Profile , Research), Singapore's state-controlled metro operator, on Tuesday posted a 80.3 percent rise in quarterly earnings, thanks to a lower corporate tax rate which offset higher operating expenses.

    SMRT, which also operates buses and taxis in the city-state, reported S$36.2 million net profit for the three months ended March 31, its fiscal fourth quarter.

    The firm posted 2006 net profit of S$135.4 million, compared with S$103.4 million a year ago.

    Full-year profits beat a consensus net profit forecast of S$121.9 million from 11 analysts polled by Reuters Estimates.
