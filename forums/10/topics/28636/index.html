    Having GLCs to operate strategic national services certainly is acceptable by any means, and operate it profitably is preferred.

    However, when the GLCs become pervasive and also threaten the minimal service sector that used to be attended to by individual families (from NTUC to Cheers), this should be cause for alarm.

    A significant part of our annual surplus seems to be derived from Government revenues from internal production and internal consumption, without any further significant manufacturing development that can be exported (compared with Taiwan or Hongkong).

    There has to be a limit to this growth, and our present economic problems seem to be due to this pervasive dominance of GLCs in every nook and corner of the economic activity of SGP.
