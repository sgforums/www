    Perhaps you do not understand how auditing works.

    Let me quote an example from engineering quality control. Say a process line produced 500 parts in a batch. The QC personnel will have to check the batch before sending to customers/other depts/etc/whatever. The QC people will not be able to check each and every part. They'll just check a fix quantity of parts based on probability of their pre-defined acceptable tolerance. It will be too costly (both time, manpower, money) to inspect each of the 500 pieces.

    In auditing, it's the same. Auditors work on a pre-defined, legal acceptable framework. They'll check all that is required based on the requirements pre-set in the industry. Be it auditing NKF, mcdonalds, genting international, keppel corporation, the practises and framework is not unsimilar.

    After the Gold Tap incident where the papers decide to back their reporters and take the case to court, a full auditing team was put in place with the intention to "dig out dirt" from NKF. Over 50 auditors were assigned to audit full time into NKF's accounts. That's how they got the stuff out. Consider a normal annual audit process where a team, say 5 people were put to the task for a 2 weeks? And this team of 5 is most likely also handling other companies' accounts at the same time. And, they dun check each and every txn, they randomly check certain txns, done by the internal auditors.

    (Extracted from www/managefranchise/blogspot.com )

    Most businesses are today still managed in an ad hoc manner resulting in complexities and uncertainties which lead to idling, delays, inefficiencies, losses, scandals or malpractices.

    Despite the Asian Financial Crisis, many businesses continue to be managed in an ad hoc fire-fighting manner with senior management only concerned with making decisions leaving implementation to the subordinates.

    When troubles happen, the senior management has many escape routes one of which is to conduct a post-mortem Management Audit to determine the cause or causes of problems to punish a scapegoat.

    It may be asked : why a management audit and not hold the senior management of responsibility to take preemptive or corrective or advance actions instead of leaving issues and problems to chance occurrence before taking actions.

    Process management like OSP Process Management is aimed at preventing problems and solving them in advance before their occurrence, viz:-

    (1) Establish complex and conceptual goals and tasks in Follow-through lego-like component work processes.

    (2) Objective supervision, Clear definitions of roles, duties and quality-time-cost control.

    (3) Coordination and knowledge application.

    (4) Effective Communication and feedbacks.

    (5) Logical work processes, measurement of performance, corrections.

    (6) Objectivity, accountability and transparency.

    (7) Clearly defined Filing, retrieving of records.

    ( Motivation of workers in knowledge application.

    (9) Effective Departmentalisation and delegation.

    As the saying goes, it is easy to find faults as anyone can make mistakes.

    Processes set up in follow-through sequence and integrated into daily work routines will avoid the errors and mistakes of the kind as discovered belatedly by the Auditor General.

    Being set up in attainable component work processes the whole organization will gel their works with coordination, teamwork and knowledge application at every level.

    The traditional management or financial audit might be the most effective in finding faults but will such audit lead to preemptive solutions, efficiency and prevention of malpractices as found in NKF and many others.

    Once tasks are established in cause-effect lego-like component work processes the management and lower rungs will be connected into a whole allowing regular and consistent actions to be taken to realize result-oriented performance with hands-on control, comparison, processing and corrections to attain organizational efficiency and productivity.

    In the globalization age, it is critical to empower staff to achieve higher level of efficiency through coordination, teamwork and knowledge application by all staff.

    Therefore, there is an overriding need to change the current mindset of management from one which is based on fault finding to one which will be more hands-on and proactive as that will be the only way to solve many of the problems reported by the Auditor General.

    Process management will also be most effective in upgrading efficiency, accountable good corporate governance and results.

    If the solutions are proposed after the events or facts are found out credit will not be given to such finding for the same finder was himself probably unaware or unable to prevent its occurrence in the first place in most situation.

    If your organization is thinking of spending thousands on conducting a management or financial audit surely it will be more beneficial to devote some time to process management as the latter while being more cost effective will be the more creative way to upgrade efficiency instead of fault findings without contributing to final solutions of problems.

    So there is an alternative solution to all those problems mentioned by the Auditor General. Problems are not that complex and overwhelming when tasks are laid out in objective component work processes and processed through forms and work forms via a call centre so that everyone will be able to work with coordination and teamwork and knowledge application to achieve results.
