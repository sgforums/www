    Circuit Breaker Rules

    After the Dow Jones Industrial Average plunged 508 points in the Oct. 19, 1987, crash, government officials had a question: What could be done to prevent a replay?

    The response was two major new rules implemented in 1988. The first established circuit breakers that would halt trading for an hour if the Dow Jones Industrial Average plunged 250 points, and for two hours if it fell 400 points. The second clamped a collar on the ability of program traders to dart between stocks and stock index futures when the Dow industrials rose or fell more than 50 points, an effort to reduce the volatility such arbitrage trading was said to cause.
