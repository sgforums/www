    PM Lee should take a more proactive planning approach instead of just relying on concepts and policies to lower costs and increase our economic competitiveness as shown in one such model as follows:-

    HOW TO INCREASE ECONOMIC COMPETITIVENESS AND LOWER COSTS AND IMPROVE LIVING STANDARDS

    Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs) are the embryo life-blood of a national economy. Through nurturing capital formation, technology start-ups and looking after the growths and health of small and medium enterprises, a country can go a long way in upgrading its own economic competitiveness, resilience, and vibrancy.

    That is why perhaps, many countries are in the forefront of encouraging business and technology start-ups in their own respective Silicon Valleys.

    However, many governments tend to take short-cut and prefer to woe the bigger and more established enterprises and MNCs with incentives forgetting to look after their own domestic sector SMEs.

    They would even practise a tax-and-recover system of economic management which double-charge on lands and all essential services which drives up business costs thereby curtailing their own peopleÂ’s entrepreneurship and SMEs.

    SMEs are vulnerable to turbulence, ups-and-downs in the economy. They are the sources of new capital formation, applications of knowledge and internal resources. They need to be supported in order to compete and grow so as to create more business start-ups.

    It makes sense for the government not only to support their own SMEs with incentives but wherever possible to create special procurement policies to allow them to put to better use the idling internal resources of lands, capital, material, commodities, advertising, promotion and market penetration.

    TYPICAL PROBLEMS FACING SMEs.

    SMEs typically face the following problems:-
    1. Higher start-up costs.
    2. Fuzziness of management.
    3. Lack of process management.
    4. Lack of transparency and accountability.
    5. Lack of capital and resources.
    6. Lack of total management know-how.

    DEVELOPING NEW OPPORTUNITIES

    Government should therefore make in their total economic plans, adopt a more proactive policy to promote the growths and health of its own domestic SMEs including automating work processes to capitalize on SMEsÂ’ strengths in ideas, experimentations, creativity, standardization, specialization, objectivity, coordination, integration and information management.

    CURRENT BUSINESS MODELS

    Unfortunately, so far the efforts seem to be expended in window-dress performance and limited to getting SMEs to go for look-good SOP or ISO certification or financing deals rather than upgrading their management excellence to true cause-effect empowering work processes to motivate workers and to tap on their practical knowledge application and technological competitiveness.

    Without empowering the staff in practical knowledge and creativity in every field the enterprise will remain conceptual putting up with a great deal of ad hoc training and re-training without producing the needed mass knowledge applications and results.

    Look-good marketing ISOs, employed by many SMEs were unable to upgrade mass participation by workers in knowledge application as witnessed by business malpractices and failures during the Asian financial crisis of 1997.

    RE-INVENTION AND SETTING NEW DIRECTIONS

    There is a critical need for businesses to standardize tasks to enable staff to perform them with true efficiency and productivity as follows:-

    1. STEPS AND CORE ACTIVITIES
    2. ADMININSTRATIVE MANAGEMENT
    3. OPERATIONAL MANAGEMENT
    4. PARAMETERS (TIME- COST-QUALITY)
    5. QUANTITATIVE MEASUREMENT
    6. CONTINUOUS STANDARDIZATION
    7. INTEGRATING OVERALL TASKS.
    8. PROCESSING TASKS IN DOCUMENTATION
    9. CORRECTIVE ACTIONS ON VARIANCES

    For example, in marketing, SMEs could excel in the following areas :-

    (1) Capitalizing on advantages of your products and services.
    (2) Implementing market penetration through joint economy of scale.
    (3) Standardizing processes for implementation in regional operations.
    (4) Follow through implementation of tasks in steps to reach results from coordination, knowledge application.

    SMEsÂ’ automation of work processes are supported by departmentalization of diverse tasks hence:-

    1.(General) 2.(Human Resource) 3.(Revenue) 4.(Accounts) 5.(Marketing) 6.(Services-all outsourced activities) 7.(Facility) 8.(Enforcement) 9. (Production) 10.(Compliance).

    WORK FORM (PRODUCTION)

    All departmental core activities will be efficiently managed with hands-on controls, processing, comparison and corrective actions as set up in the OSP Process File List.

    Core production tasks are easily set up in sets of core activities to be processed at scheduled intervals with feedbacks and corrective actions taken at all levels.

    WORK FORM (ACCOUNTS)

    For example, Work Form (Accounts) monitor all accounts, revenues and payments with hands-on coordinated processing through forms and work forms to prevent any leakage or malpractices. Call centre ensures integrated work processes to attain results from systematic quality-time-cost controls, objective and timely supervision, certification of works through 2-way communication task-lines as elaborated.

    WORK FORM (SERVICE - TECHNOLOGY/R&D)

    All the enterpriseÂ’s services e.g. from housekeeping to security, M&E, logistics, R&D, project management etc are set up as core activities to be processed at scheduled time intervals with coordination and knowledge application.

    WORK FORM (MARKETING)

    Marketing tasks are no longer complex or ad hoc but consistently performed core activities e.g. customersÂ’ needs/values, equipment/material/manpower changes, price/cost, A&P - 4 P (product differentiations or technology excellence) to bring about competitiveness.

    WORK FORM (REVENUE)

    The Revenue Department will be able to oversee funds, investments, loans, cash flow and profitability and revenue and take corrective actions to prevent leakages and malpractices due to closed individual ad hoc control and self-centredness.
