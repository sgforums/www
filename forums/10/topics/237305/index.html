    What is leadership? Is it talents, insight, foresight or all the ivory tower assumptions ?

    How do people judge leadership ? How can vision, foresight, insight be put to work in the daily work place?

    What kind of leaders should citizens choose ? Should leaders be people possessing academic talents or experiences in good corporate governance, passion to serve with moral uprightness or nobility of spirit?

    It would appear that one critical quality which is most urgently needed is the ability to carry out plans and visions and mobilize people in major upgrading or improvements.

    But more often than not people seem to be concerned only with presentations or academic ability without recognition of true implementation abilities.

    Despite many years of emphasizing on leadership, scholastic attainments, it was to be found that today we have a rather uninspiring domestic economy which consequently has to rely heavily on foreigners to prop up the whole economy.

    What happen to all our own talents or do we have no talents?

    The Asian Financial crisis has exposed self-centredness as the ultimate cause of financial instability.

    Why is it so easily forgotten and why are we still promoting individualism and self-centredness resulting in malpractices in all the NKF going on in so many organizations.

    opportunities to prove their exceptional leadership qualities ? Sad to say, apart from giving look-good presentations and technocratic statistical digests, very few of the academic leaders have measured up to a high standard worthy of note or commendations.

    Taking into account all the above-stated causes of our problems, the next phase of leadership therefore should not be leadership as these are too conceptual and unattainable and are the root causes of malpractices going on in many of our organizations.

    The real challenge is to work towards teamwork and results-oriented hands-on work processes which can involve and motivate all in doing their parts to upgrade total success of all.

    There appears great obstacles to overcome because conceptual leadership and many of its assumptions have been implanted in our minds for too long so much so that people have accepted them as real.

    So it is obviously a major issue whether in our fast-paced technology age will leadership by individuals make such a big impact and what happen if we cannot find the proverbial leaders needed for success.

    What we actually need most may be simple down-to-earth broad-based technology and knowledge application by the masses.

    Therefore we should strive in every work place to lay down our tasks in attainable work processes which will allow staff to take over and exercise their broad-based knowledge application and teamwork.

    We will need such new-age implementation leadership based on pro-active work processes which will give us a better chance to solve our "20 major major government policy errors" as posted to the Feedback Unit since 2002.

    We can keep talking about benefits of investing in grandiose plans like biomedical researches, but if the actions are dependent on one or two individuals how could we excel and achieve results.
