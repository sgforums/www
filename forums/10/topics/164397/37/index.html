    Originally posted by Rexdriver:
    You just made my point with your example. The death penalty is used as a deterrent, which you asserted was but a secondary motive...
    Wrong. Remember the context that the AOA was implemented in. Back then organised crime was still rampant, and tt was not uncommon to hear of armed robberies and kidnap attempts on businessmen like CK Tang. The implementation of the death penalty for possession and sale of arms in 1973 was not for the purpose of deterrence, but to physically reduce the frequency of armed crime by literally killing off offenders while working to curb the import of arms from Indonesia and Malaysia. At this point in time, the death penalty was no deterrent to crime- since criminals had as much chance to die in a shootout as to swing from a noose, many died in gun battles with the police during the course of their crimes. Also discovered were that most of the crimelords involved were repeat offenders- everytime the police foiled a robbery they would retreat to a offshore kelong and recoup their losses for another attempt.

    The death penalty as a deterrent to armed crime remained ineffective until gun trafficking was stopped in the 80s, due to it being made impossible and unprofitable to bring weapons into the country.
