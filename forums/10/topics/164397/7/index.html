    Guys, guys, guys, let's get a bit of perspective here, shall we?

    The Singaporean government's doing its job. The majority of the Singaporean people don't want drugs in the country and support the death penalty as a method of achieving this objective. By upholding the death sentence, the SG government is doing exactly what it's supposed to do.

    On the other hand, the Australian government also has its job to do, which is protecting the lives and interests of Australians overseas (regardless of their ethnic origins). Which government can afford to be seen to be washing their hands of one of their own citizens who's in trouble overseas?

    IMHO the Howard government took its best shot already by suggesting that the death sentence be commuted in exchange for all that Nguyen knows about the drug smuggling ring. That has been the only suggestion so far that goes to achieving Singapore's higher goal, namely the reduction of drug traffic.

    On the other hand, if that plan was to be carried out, it also means a death sentence for Nguyen - only it will be a drug ring that kills him, not a government-appointed hangman.

    But that's not where the problem lies. The problem lies in everybody else sticking their noses in.

    Who's making noise about the Singaporean government treating Australia "with contempt"? The opposition. Who's calling for boycotts and calling the other side names? Private citizens. Who's sensationalising it? The newspapers.

    Let's get a grip here, people. What's flying about in the media is nowhere near the entire truth. There are plenty of Aussies who think that Nguyen was very foolish to agree to the gang's demands, and now it's tough luck that his gamble didn't pay off. However, that kind of view just doesn't pay well in terms of newspaper circulation numbers.

    Once you filter out the political grandstanding, hyperbole and other forms of crap, what you're left with is two governments in a very difficult position, a gang of drug smugglers who caused all this trouble, and one guy who was either dumb or desperate enough to be the pawn.

    Nufff said.
