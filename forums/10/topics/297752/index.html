    11,000 households picked for expenditure survey

    Mon, Dec 17, 2007
    The Straits Times

    GOVERNMENT officials will come a knocking on 11,000 doors in the next fortnight.

    But the Department of Statistics officers just want to know how households are spending their money.

    From Dec 24 to Jan 7, the officers will visit the selected households in Choa Chu Kang, Jalan Berseh, Jurong West, Seletar, Serangoon North, Tampines, Tanjong Pagar, Toa Payoh and Woodlands.

    The survey results will eventually help the government re-look the Consumer Price Index and plan economic and social policies.

    This is part of an even larger survey - to be done over a year. The results will go to updating the basket of goods and services used in computing the Consumer Price Index, which is a key way people measure inflation.

    The officers will have identity cards and letters of authorisation. But if you have doubts, call the hotline on 1800-437-0708, from 8.30 pm to 10 pm.
