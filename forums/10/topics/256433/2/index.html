    CASE survey shows milk prices up, sugar prices down
    By May Wong, Ng Baoying, Channel NewsAsia | Posted: 01 June 2007 2023 hrs

    SINGAPORE: The price of condensed and evaporated milk as well as certain brands of fresh milk, has gone up.

    This is according to the Consumers Association of Singapore (CASE) which conducted a price survey, following concerns by consumers.

    CASE checked four major supermarket chains - Carrefour, NTUC Fairprice, Giant, and Sheng Siong - and found that condensed milk prices have gone up by about 10 to 45 per cent.

    Leading the pack was Nestle Milkmaid High-Calcium condensed milk which now costs about 50 cents more.

    Also up by about the same amount is Nestle Carnation Full Cream evaporated milk.

    CASE also found that the 1 litre packs of Marigold, Magnolia, Farm House and Daisy fresh milk, now cost between 10 and 55 cents more.

    But when it comes to sugar, prices have gone down or remained the same.

    For those who are looking to cut costs, CASE suggests choosing supermarket housebrands which are cheaper. - CNA/yy
