    You raised a few interesting points, o_x. My opposition to the annuities is almost entirely on economic grounds.

    If the younger generation doesn't know how to save, why do you think the government confiscating their money will teach them to save? Economically, if young people thinks the government is saving for them, they will reduce their own private saving. The government pushing back the CPF payout date, is only too clear an indicator it is not actually saving the money. The government is very worried now it cannot pay out the money. It has actually spent everything on public works like Biopolis.

    About governments thinking they have unlimited economic resources, that is also explainable by economics. The government is divorced from profit and loss unlike private enterprise. Whatever its performance, it can continue to tax away. It cannot allocate private resources efficiently. Therefore they come up with new pathologies to justify their existence as planners and managers.
