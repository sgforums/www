    Is Supriya an Indian citizen?

     

    Member of Parliament (MP) Supriya Sule may have to answer for the whispers that she is a citizen of Singapore.


    The Bombay High Court on Friday ordered the Maharashtra government and the Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA) to decide on an application asking why her Indian citizenship should not be cancelled in view of her dual citizenship.

    Petitioner Mrunalini Kakade claimed that Sule is the director of Laguna International Pvt Ltd and a permanent resident of Singapore.

    Kakade sought that Sule ceases to be an Indian citizen ‘for these and other reasons’. Sule would then be liable for disqualification as an MP.

    Supriya Sule
    The Pune resident contested the parliamentary election from Baramati constituency against Sule, who was elected MP.

    In an application to the MHA dated June 22, 2009, Kakade stated that since Sule had voluntarily given up citizenship of India, she should cease to be an Indian citizen.

    Kakade pointed out that Sule was a member of the Rajya Sabha from 2007 till she was elected from the Baramati.

    In her affidavit, Sule revealed she has a property in Singapore. “Under the provisions of law in Singapore, she cannot purchase property unless she is a permanent resident,” Kakade submitted.

    Kakade has filed an RTI plea seeking information on Sule’s status in Singapore. Her permanent resident identification number was declared following that.

    The MHA told Kakade that its ministry was investigating the matter and that her application was sent to the State Government. Kakade, unhappy with the MHA’s handling of the matter, wrote to them saying that such an inquiry should be made through a committee.

    The MHA replied that unless the state and its investigating agency provided them with a report, no action could be taken. “There appears to be dilly-dallying on part of the MHA and the State Government,” Kakade claimed.

    When contacted Sule said she did not want to comment as the matter was sub-judice.
