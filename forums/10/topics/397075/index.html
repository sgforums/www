    Teo Chee Hean aspired to build immigrant society for all races

    April 19th, 2010 | Author: Our Correspondent

    With Singapore’s demographics being altered radically over the last few years due to the relentless influx of foreigners, PAP leaders have been emphasizing the importance of racial and religious harmony in recent weeks, the latest being Deputy Prime Minister Teo Chee Hean.

    Speaking at a community event in Pasir Ris yesterday, DPM Teo called on Singaporeans to strive to be part of the “common space” they share with one another.

    “Each community in Singapore must be allowed to have its own space to practise its culture, traditions and beliefs. But we must subscribe to a wider common space among all of us, where we come together as Singaporeans, regardless of what our race, language or beliefs are,” DPM Teo was quoted as saying in the Straits Times.

    He noted Singapore’s history as an immigrant society and said that this is “the kind of Singapore that we want to build, where people find their own space and their comfort level and they feel at home here.”

    PAP leaders have often used the fact that Singaporeans’ forefathers are immigrants too to exhort them to accept more foreigners into Singapore.

    The PAP’s liberal immigration and pro-foreigner policies have become increasingly unpopular among Singaporeans and could cost them some votes in the next general election.

    According to a recent Wall Street Journal editorial, the relentless influx of foreigners into Singapore has depressed the wages of ordinary Singaporeans, increased the cost of living and led to an overall decline in the quality of life.

    Despite the widespread unhappiness on the ground, the PAP has continued to insist that foreigners are “essential” for Singapore.
