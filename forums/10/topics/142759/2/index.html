    MPs urge Singaporeans not to stop giving to charities because of NKF saga
    By S Ramesh, Channel NewsAsia

    SINGAPORE : Set up an independent committee of inquiry to investigate the NKF and put in place a more structured regulatory system for charities.

    These were just some of the calls made by MPs when debating the NKF issue on Wednesday.

    Dr Amy Khor, mayor of Southwest CDC said: "Although we should not go on a witch-hunt, the public disquiet about the many un-answered questions on the NKF must be fully addressed with no stones unturned. Otherwise this could lead to a very cynical public with deep mistrust for charities and hence, lower philanthropy participation and giving."

    Chiam See Tong, MP for Potong Pasir said: "The best form of transparency is to have independent checks on the finances and affairs of the body."

    MPs also want the salaries of key officials of larger Voluntary Welfare Organisations (VWO) and charities made public.

    "The annual remuneration of top executives be disclosed in salary bands the ways listed companies are now required to do. Paying competitive salaries is an acceptable fact of life but providing such information will ensure that IPCs will remove doubts in the minds of their most important stakeholders, the donors," said Tan Soo Koon, MP of East Coast GRC.

    "At every union meeting I attended, there were many angry words, frustrations and above all a sense of betrayal. Many were outraged to find out that in 2003, during SARS, when some of them had no pay leave and worked reduced hours to keep their jobs, the CEO of NKF was paid twelve months bonus and all in the name of charity," said Halimah Yacob, MP of Jurong GRC.

    The NKF's efficiency was another issue brought up, with Bukit Timah MP Wang Kai Yuen comparing it with another organisation, the Kidney Dialysis Foundation (KDF).

    "The staff costs of NKF amounted to $27 million in 2003 while the total staff costs of KDF amounted to a very small $573,000 which is still a tad less than the 600,000 dollars TT Durai drew as NKF CEO. I am here to look at the lessons we could learn from this debacle of how the largest and most successful VWO in Singapore lost the confidence of majority of Singaporeans overnight."

    Despite some very strong words against the way the NKF had been managed, the MPs recognised the good work done by the Foundation.

    And they are confident this would continue, which was why they say calls for transparency must never overshadow the NKF's main objective, and that is serving those who need kidney dialysis.

    So they urged Singaporeans not to stop giving to charities including the NKF, saying that the cause must be separate from TT Durai, the man.

    The debate on the NKF issue continues on Thursday
