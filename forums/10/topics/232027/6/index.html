    Thai Foreign Ministry Not Worried About Thaksin's Trip To Singapore E-mail this news to a friend Printable version of this news,by D. Arul Rajoo,
    BANGKOK, Jan 15 (Bernama)    --http://www.bernama.com/bernama/v3/news_lite.php?id=241384
    BANGKOK, Jan 15 (Bernama) -- The Thai Foreign Ministry is not worried about deposed prime minister Thaksin Shinawatra's visit to Singapore and meeting with the republic's Deputy Prime Minister, S. Jayakumar.

    Acting Foreign Minister Sawanit Kongsiri said the Singapore government had told the Thai government that the visit and meeting were not politically motivated but were private.

    "We are not worried, it's not political. But if there is anything to do with politics and can affect the national security, we will be worried and will take the necessary measures," he told reporters here today.

    The Singapore Foreign Affairs Ministry said yesterday that the meeting between Thaksin, who was ousted in a military coup on Sept 19, and Jayakumar was purely social and private.

    He was in the republic on a private four-day visit but had reportedly left for Japan.

    Thaksin, who sold his family-owned Shin Corporation to Singapore's Temasek Holdings in a controversial deal last year, went to Singapore after the Foreign Ministry revoked diplomatic passports held by him and his wife, Potjaman, on Jan 10.

    Sawanit confirmed that the revocation would take affect from Jan 25.

    As the military junta that ousted him has continuously denied him the opportunity to return to the kingdom, Thaksin now has to use a normal passport to continue his travel abroad.

    He spent most of his time travelling between London, Beijing and Hong Kong after the coup, with short trips to Bali, causing anxiety among the military junta which accused him of involvement in political undercurrents back home.

    Former prime minister Chuan Leekpai told the local media that Thaksin could travel anywhere he wanted as he had the wealth, including from the 77 billion baht sale of Shin Corporation.
