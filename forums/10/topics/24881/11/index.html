    KL beefs up air power with new US fighter jets

    KUALA LUMPUR -- Malaysia plans to buy US Superhornet FA-18F fighter jets to upgrade its air power, Defence Minister Najib Razak said on Saturday in a national news agency report.

    It will also buy radar systems and guided missile launchers from the United States, Bernama news agency quoted him as saying.

    Mr Najib did not say how many Superhornets Malaysia will be buying, or how much the purchase will be worth. He said defence officials of the two countries were finalising details and hoped to conclude a deal by the end of this year.

    Malaysia has made several major defence purchases this year, spending about RM6 billion (US$1.6 billion) on European and Russian-made hardware ranging from rifles and missiles to its first submarines.

    Government officials said the purchases were part of plans that were put on hold in 1997 by the Asian financial crisis.

    On Saturday, Mr Najib said Malaysia's neighbours should not be nervous about the upgrade.

    'Every time we buy something, the people always link it to an arms race. But when others buy, nobody says anything,' Bernama quoted him as saying.

    Malaysia's air force currently has 18 Russian MiG-29 fighters, eight FA-18D Hornets, seven F-5 Tigers and seven British-built Hawk jets. -- AP
