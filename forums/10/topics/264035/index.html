    "Said Zahari, in his book, recalls the strike he had led at Utusan Malaysia to champion press freedom, freedom of speech and democracy which culminated in him paying a heavy price for his action -- spending 17 years in detention, "said (MY Information Minister Datuk Seri Zainuddin Maidin)Zainuddin.
    KUALA LUMPUR, July 16 (Bernama) -- The New Economic Policy (DEB) has succeeded in producing a big number of Malay professionals, many of whom have made a name for themselves in major cities in the world like London and Dubai, said Information Minister Datuk Seri Zainuddin Maidin.

    Zainuddin said that since the NEP was implemented, many Malays had become doctors, pilots and engineers who are not only serving the country but also making it big abroad.

    "It is said that some of them earn as much as RM50,000 a month in big cities like London and this has made them decide not to return to Malaysia but to continue with their careers in those places because of the lucrative income," he said.

    Recalling the early days of the NEP, Zainuddin said the western media objected to it when it was introduced by the late Tun Abdul Razak in 1971 and to this day it continued to get the same reaction from such media.

    "If there was no NEP, we will still be talking about poverty distribution but today we are talking about unfair distribution of wealth," he said when launching the book "Dalam Ribuan Mimpi Gelisah" and its English version "The Long Nightmare" authored by a former detainee, Said Zahari, at the Dewan Bahasa and Pustaka (DBP) here today. Both versions are published by Utusan Publications & Distributors Sdn Bhd.

    Zainuddin said there are people in the country who have forgotten that thousands of children of farmers and fishermen got to further their studies locally and abroad and became professionals, which changed the identity spectrum of the Malays, all made possible because of the NEP.

    He also said that it gave added strength when a person could see things outside of the ideology he or she holds and be able to give views based on facts gathered from exposure, observation and experience.

    On Said, who was once Editor-in-Chief of Utusan Melayu, Zainuddin said he was a true blue-blood of the newspaper.

    "Said Zahari, in his book, recalls the strike he had led at Utusan Malaysia to champion press freedom, freedom of speech and democracy which culminated in him paying a heavy price for his action -- spending 17 years in detention, "said Zainuddin.

    Said, who is now 79 years old, was under detention in Singapore for 17 years since 1963 under the Internal Security Act. He had once led the Partai Rakyat Singapura.

    The book is a description of the struggle Said and his wife underwent during that period.

    Said, who was present, said he wanted Malaysians, especially the younger generation, to read the book to get to know the many aspects of the nation's history which might not be available in other books.

    "In my view of the history of political developments and the struggle for independence, there are moments in history that have gone unrecorded and untold," he said.

    -- BERNAMA(Pl note this is the MY government news agency)
