    In the first place why are the shopkeepers clamouring for corridor spaces and giving so many half-cooked excuses like not having enough space to do their business as sensationalised by CNA and the newspapes.

    It is because when HDB or Town Council failed to prevent the first shop in any estate from starting the encroachment. Many private shopping centres have controlled their corridors very well and shopkeepers are not asking for permission to occupy the fire escape routes which often are the corridors.

    Once a particular shop started the encroachment there will be a tendency for all the neighbouring shops to do likewise as then it becomes an issue business rivalry fighting for passers-by for impulse business.

    When the situation reaches this stage the HDB, Town Council and MPs would be unable to stop all the rest from wanting the same treatment.

    If HDB or Town Council or the MPs have leadership as claimed they could easily understand such a human psychology. If so they should be able to hold meetings with all shopkeepers and let them share the real problems at issue that is if all agree not occupy the corridors then there will be no necessity for any of the rest to seek encroachments to keep up rivalry of those who use corridor space.

    This common-objective of not starting the rivalry for corridor advertisement or display to win more passers-by is deep rooted in human psychology and MPs do not understand such an issue.

    If they do then not only will HDB estates be as pleasant as good-class private shopping centre where such encroachments are well controlled shopkeepers will find no need in practice to extend their trading frontage to corridors to win more customers because when no body encroaches the corridor there is no need for the rest to ask for same privilege.

    Some people will defend the "occupy corridor" practice of HDB/Town Council on the ground that the shopkeepers are unable to move their equipment in and out daily like the wet-market hawkers.

    The wet market hawkers' need for occupying corridor spaces are is basically affected by the same root-cause - rivalry for business of passers-by.

    If no body starts such a rivalry in a row of shophouse there will be no need for any neighbouring shopkeepers for wanting to ask for such occupation of common areas. Such a problem which has grown big-scale will eventually subside over time.

    It is total understanding of the problems which holds the key to all. Now the situation has become so bad that many shopkeepers especially the coffee shops were so heavily and financially committed to occupying corridors that many of them have invested on purchases of fridges and shelves and structures of all kinds and even paying a licence fee to HDB.

    It will take some gigantic effort to make all revert to operating within their originally allotted and leased shop space within their units boundaries.

    If this problem is still not resolved in an in-depth manner there will be more fires and probably more deaths.
