    I'm really tired of see this repression and propaganda line being thrown around.

    When i was growing up, there was no Young or Kid PAP that i had to join. I don't have to go through any indoctrination classes or said Hail Lee Kuan Yew to his picture every morning.

    I initially thought PAP was a repressive organisation, until i started reading books (Actual paper made books, not the internet or the ones with many pictures in them)

    Then i started reading China articles and works from Chinese authors. ( They have a very different way of looking at the world compared to the West)

    Then i started understanding why that the PAP is a great deal better than the other choices out there. Because it's able to plan in strategic terms, not react in a knee jerk manner in most democracies.  We're not weighted down with external debts like our neighbours.  (Malaysia has done a good job of this too, despite my prejudices)  

    I don't worship the PAP, it's still inefficient and have its problems. But i find the idea that multiple parties will automatically promise a better government laughable because keeping the population satisified and happy is a pipe dream, human greed can never be satisifed. We will always want more and better stuff.

    Do i think that the PAP always make good decisions? No.

    But what defines a good decision? The one that makes people happy? Or the one that demands sacrifices to create a more sustainable future?

    Do i open the storehouses so that people can have full meals for the forseeable future? Or do i ration it out just in case of a major natural disaster?

    We pick and poke on the mistakes that the PAP made and always ignored the things they've done well. Looks at our commodities, there's inflation yes but did it go out of control during the high oil and grain prices? Did our food prices skyrocket?

    In the work place, what we hate the most of is politics. Yet somehow we expect it to work in the massive workplace of Singapore.
