    Belgium
    -> 20+ days leave (unsure about the accumulation part)
    -> Public Holidays: 13
    -> Pay similar to Singapore, except in EURO dollars. For eg. a person who earns S$2000 here will get E$2000 there
    -> Union to protect the workers

    Germany
    -> 30 days leave that can accumulative endlessly (there is no such thing as only can accumulate for 2 years)
    -> Public Holidays: 15
    -> Pay similar to Singapore, except in EURO dollars, AFTER Taxation. For eg, a person who earns S$2000 here will get E$2000 there
    -> Union to protect the workers
    -> Others: Train transport at half price if buy 1-year validity ticket. Work-life balance. Goes home on time most of the time.
    -> Germany allows employee to work out staggered work hours as long as their work permits. This reduce traffic jams and allows people flexibility to manage their time.

    Japan
    -> 26 days leave (can accumulate for 2 years)
    -> Public Holidays: 15 (if bank or govt employees: +3 more)
    -> Basic pay higher than Singapore. If Overtime, executives below managerial level gets OT pay.
    -> Union to protect the workers

    Singapore
    -> 14 - 18 days leave (assuming private companies). Only can accumulate for 2 years. Legally, a company can grant you only 7 days vacation and get away with it.
    -> Public Holidays: 11
    -> Pay is among the lowest in developed countries. If Overtime (MOST of the time), no overtime pay. No off-in-lieu. Expected to come back during weekends, 24 hours if required.
    -> No union to protect the workers. No protection for executives against unfair treatment. Individual is supposed to hire a lawyer to address unfair treatment
    -> Still a mentality to want the worker to sit there from morning to night regardless of productivity
    NOTE: Check your employment contract. Chances are, there's a clause whereby it allows the company to terminate your service without any valid reason so long as it gives you 1 month notice in advance. (This clause has a serious implication, in that a company can terminate you even if you are a good performer, so long as it gives a month's notice)

    The above are just comparisons of what I have gathered so far.
