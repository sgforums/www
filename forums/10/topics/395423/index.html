    Govt will need to continue search for more women to join politics: Lim Hwee Hua

    SINGAPORE: The government will need to continue to search and persuade more women to join politics and the effort has been encouraging with more women in Parliament now.

    Minister in the Prime Minister's Office, Mrs Lim Hwee Hua, said this after a forum on women's leadership role in the New World.

    The panel also discussed challenges that women face in the business world and what could be done to address them.

    Also taking part in the forum were prominent women such as entrepreneurs Merry Riana and Genecia Luo.

    Another issue raised was whether Singapore should have a quota to ensure more women in the political arena.

    Lim Hwee Hua, Minister, Prime Minister's Office, Second Minister for Finance & Transport, said: "The application of a quota in Singapore is not quite as useful because women are not being denied the opportunity to as some of the participants also raised.

    “More importantly, women don't want to feel like they are not sure why they are actually chosen for those positions whether it was because of the quotas or their own merit."

    Mrs Lim also responded to a question from Channel NewsAsia on the walkabouts of the opposition parties at various constituencies.

    She said: "I suppose they are preparing for the General Election. And in a way trying to work the ground so that they can at the General Election truly say that they've been working the ground unlike the rest of us who have been working the ground.

    "I think it reflects the expectations of the electorate. People do expect you to know the ground and to have done things for the people and to be able to demonstrate that you're committed to being there and not just for the General Election itself."

    - CNA/vm
