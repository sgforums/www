    DonÂ’t buy from illegal hawkers
    - Dec 29, 2006
    AsiaOne
    The National Environment Agency (NEA) and the Singapore Police Force (SPF) have issued a joint statement urging the public not to purchase items, like party novelties, food or drinks from illegal hawkers who usually peddle their wares in crowded areas or mass-public participation mega-events like the forthcoming Marina Bay Countdown.

    In its last operation during the "Celebrate Christmas" concerts, NEA officers issued 80 tickets against those hawking items like spray foam, party novelties, food and drinks. Their wares were also seized.

    Illegal hawkers, if convicted, may be fined up to $1,000 and; in the case of a second or subsequent conviction, fined up to $4,000 or jailed up to 3 months or to both.

    NEA officers will continue to work with the Police to curb illegal hawking along Marina Bay and other areas for the upcoming New Year countdown celebrations.
