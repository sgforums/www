    ST latest news 7 Aug 2007

    Aug 18 marks citizenship day: DPM Wong
    AUGUST this year will have two dates to mark Singapore's nationhood.
    Apart from celebrating the Nation's birthday on Thursday, some brand new Singaporeans will be welcomed with a grand scale national ceremony on Aug 18 to give them their citizenship papers.

    The National Citizenship ceremony is an effort to reinforce the significance of being a citizen here - not only among foreigners who choose to become Singaporeans but also among 'native' Singaporeans too.

    Deputy Prime Minister Wong Kan Seng revealed this on Tuesday, at a National Day Observance ceremony at the Home Affairs Ministry.

    Currently, new Singaporeans are sworn in with smaller scale ceremonies at the constituency grassroots level.

    These will continue - the national-level ceremony will instead be more of a symbolic one with a selected group of new citizens receiving their citizenship certificates.

    But DPM Wong, who oversees the National Population Secretariat, said that the National Ceremony is a first but 'significant' step to creating Singapore's own Citizenship Day.

    Citizenship Days are celebrated in other parts of the world - like Australia and the US - where new citizens are celebrated and welcomed.

    But all this is just part of a barrage of activities and efforts to encourage people to settle here.

    For instance, the Immigration and Checkpoints Authority has come up with a new information package for people considering becoming citizens or permanent residents.

    In a large packet, flyers - chock full of information about the benefits of citizenship and the mechanics of application - are tiered alongside bright pictures of Singapore and the testimonies of four people who have taken up the Singapore citizenship.

    If present citizenship-application trends keep up, Singapore can expect to add about 200,000 permanent residents and 40,000 citizens to its fold in the next five years.

    Read the full report in Wednesday's edition of The Straits Times.
