    The issue is not sexual equality but one of RELATIVE equality.

    For the calculative breed, there will never be a balance that qualifies as such. Stinging issues such as National Service, labourous industries including construction and shipping etc will always remain a firm barrier to 'total equality'.

    But with a broader spectrum of education, a greater awareness of the opposite sex and a reversal of the stereotype mentality that has long branded females as 2nd class to men, attitudes may change over time and a deeper respect may develop between the two sexes.

    Scandinavian countries such as Sweden have been very successful in this aspect and their population has been extremely receptive to the idea of women being granted bigger and more prominent roles in various industries including the civil service. This is to such an extent that it is required by LAW that 1/3 of Swede Parliament comprise female MPs.

    No female visitor feels intimidated nor discriminated in Sweden as a result.

    For Singapore to reach this goal requires an open mindset, one that we are still decades away.
