    For starters, I think it might not be a gd idea to classify Singaporeans arbitrarily into two opposing camps - those who ardently support the PAP, and those who ardently oppose the PAP and support the opposition. Such a mode of classification stifles our political process, as it ignores those who are proactively critical of policies out of sheer objectivity, as compared to those who support or criticize the government from a purely partisan basis. I belief that there is many others who see themselves apart from this rigid dual paradigm, but rather see themselves as Singaporeans concern with the state of our own community, who are critical of the government upon certain issues they find out of necessity and objectivity to be critical, or to applaud the government or the opposition for that matter, when certain policies are enacted that serve the nation well.
