    Or you can consider google for some self-defence tips.

     

    3 principles in self-defence

    1) Anticipation and situation awareness

    Should be aware of places where possible violence may occur. Unless necessary, try avoiding these places. However, if you're there, just be more aware of what's happening around you. Maybe there is a heated argument that may escalate to a brawling, etc.

    2) Walk-away

    Common sense here, if you see something not right, try to move yourself away from the immediate area. However, if you're caught in the situation of a heated argument, try to talk your way out. Remember, the other party may have lost control, but you have to keep cool, it's your safety we're talking about!

    3) Self-defence

    When you are unable to walk-away and the argument has escalated to a fight, then it's time to defend! Try looking out for doors, corridors where they can facilitate your escape. Remember you're doing self-defence, not into sparring competition, so staying long in a fight shouldn't be in any of your options. Techniques should be either restraining your aggressor or repel their assaults. Shouting for help, running away should be within your options. 

     

    Declare, this is in no way encouraging anyone to get involved in any fight. To the best of your ability, avoid violence at all times! Make love not war!
