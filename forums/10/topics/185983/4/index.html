    Khaw: Political excitement ok, but no jokes please

    By Karen Teng

    Health Minister Khaw Boon Wan says political excitement is welcome in Singapore but the election process should not be taken lightly.

    The minister points out that the upcoming General Election should be about choosing credible and capable leaders for Singapore.

    Mr Khaw says he understands younger Singaporeans' yearning for political excitement.

    But he puts his foot down and says Singapore's leaders can't be made the brunt of jokes.

    The minister cited the case of Taiwan where its leaders are ridiculed and laughed at on late night TV shows.

    People want excitement. That's okay, entertainment value and so on, it's a young generation they want issues to be connected. So as outsiders you look at it and laugh, its entertainment value. But the moment Singaporeans start making jokes, things which are not true about MM, PM Lee or about myself are you sure you are doing something right?

    Mr Khaw says politicians who're honest and serious about their responsibilities, should be treated with respect and not ridicule.

    Yes once in a while you want some excitement but at the end of the day you have to go back to how do bring food to the table, do my kids continue to remain uneducated? Then how do you fight the competition? In a modern world today if you have uneducated and low skills, then how do you compete? There're hundreds and millions of such people in China and India waiting to come up.

    Mr Khaw says ensuring Singapore's continued prosperity should be a joint effort between the government and the people.

    He likened the cooperation to the way everyone here worked together to battle SARS.
