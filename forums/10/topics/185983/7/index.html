    The issue of racial quota is just a form of legislation to prevent ethnic enclaves from forming. It is natural human nature to live next to people of the same skin colour, same ethnic group, speak the same dialect etc. But the downside is it eventually degrades into hotspots for trouble and more racial segregation. The moment we start living within our own race, we tend to lose focus with the goals of racial acceptance and become less tolerant of other races and their culture. So in this aspect, i agree with Lee Senior that the current controls should remain.

    His haughty retort of the WP's plan to abolish grassroots is understandable. While he will never admit it, grassroots is definitely a key area where the ruling party recruits potential candidates for elections and young PAP. In this regard, the grassroots committees are therefore NOT apolitical, as the ruling party continually maintains. WP sees it as a 'barrier to entry' for them to work efficiently in their town councils; on the other hand, WP is unlikely to replace it with a neutral committee and chances of it being politically-aligned are just as high.

    On his remarks regarding the issue of fear and how he took Ken Kwek apart during his cross-examination, he is obviously stating his position: if you can't prove it, shut up.

    The aura of induced fear is clearly evident, mostly in part due to the threat of high-handed defamation suits by any who are deemed to have crossed the OB markers of the govt (Catherine Lim nearly found herself in one but her articulate nature and non-confrontational style got her off), but at the same time near-impossible to 'prove' with any hard evidence.

    The manner and conduct of polling stations is one example.

    And his bold assumption regarding 60% confirmed support is based on a stagnant population content with his past achievements and not the current team. He needs to put his confidence (or overconfidence) into perspective.
