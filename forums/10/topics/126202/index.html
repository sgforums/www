    The UK government considered it and actually downgraded it to a Class C drug, all but legalizing it. However, they have a re-think now based on scientific data linking it to psychiatric disease and addiction.

    'An appreciable proportion of cannabis users report short-lived adverse effects, including psychotic states following heavy consumption, and regular users are at risk of dependence. People with major mental illnesses such as schizophrenia are especially vulnerable in that cannabis generally provokes relapse and aggravates existing symptoms. Health workers need to recognise, and respond to, the adverse effects of cannabis on mental health.'The British Journal of Psychiatry (2001) 178: 116-122

    Research by J Van Os et al. at the University of Maastricht in the Netherlands, Cannabis use and psychosis: a longitudinal population-based study (2002), confirmed that cannabis is associated with later schizophrenia and that this is not explained by predromal symptoms. It was estimated that lack of exposure to cannabis would have reduced the incidence of psychosis requiring treatment by as much as 50%.

    I think there are more pertinent issues with the government than legalizing a drug which may lead to more psychiatric patients on the streets.
