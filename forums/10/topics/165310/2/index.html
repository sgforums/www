  • snow leopard
    8 Dec 05, 20:19
    Originally posted by skyline63:
    Bus fare so high !
    in my opinion, the public transport scene has evolved signficantly over the last decade or so because of the MRT. the bus is no longer the primary mode of transport for many people and its role will only diminish with time as MRT routes expand. thus, the economy of scale that the bus company once enjoyed will diminish and with it escalating costs.

    at the same time, bus companies have become mercenary, self-serving entities no longer devoted to public service. the double pressures of a shrinking pie and a greater need to squeeze the bottomline can only mean that they charge more for less ...

    to fundamentally overcome this situation, i think there is a need to relook the role of buses in the future of singapore's transport system. it may be better that some other transport medium be used to complement the MRT as a means of bridging the short distance between home and MRT. we can have better amenities for bicycles or roller blades or that segweg thing provided it can be made inexpensive ... or even tut tuts that are so prevalent in Thailand.

    in addition, rather than buy expensive buses from Europe and paying double for the exchange rate, why not collaborate with Thailand to have our own buses produced in Thailand? Thailand is now an automotive hub with a strong engineering base. it would be win win for both of us ...
