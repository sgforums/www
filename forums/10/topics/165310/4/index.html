    fair enough, however we need to ask ourselves what is the role of the public bus service? to provide a public service at cost or to make handsome profits? if the two are mutually incompatible then perhaps the two functions shouldn't exist in the same company.

    one possibility around the dilemma is this. all profitable routes are to be turned over to private operators. the public bus company only operates the non-profitable routes, thus satisfying its role as a provider of public services without the ambiguity of attempting to fleece from the population at the same time. costs can be recovered through contributions from private bus companies on the basis of their respective revenues. this can be easily automated through the use of the ezlink system.

    but will the public bus company give up its monopoly profits and be contented with just recovering costs? i don't think so ... hence the notion of the need for excessive profits to make up for unprofitable routes is at best another lame duck excuse at monopolising profits ... like so many other stupid excuses we've been told here ...
