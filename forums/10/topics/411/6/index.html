    [quote]Originally posted by Viper52:
    [b]

    The SAR80 is indeed a more sturdy firing platform due to its weight. But the way its designed, I couldn't hold it my BMT OC taught me to hold it like the M-16 we used in BMT(which was unusual but REALLY made the M-16 a much more accurate weapon), which to me negated the extra stability of the SAR80. And while the weight is an advantage at the foxhole part of range, it was a DEFINITE disadvantage at the rundown

    I was from a log unit and had to use the SAR80 for two years. Had to say that i agree with Viper. I found the SAR80 to be very accurate when you are resting your arm on something to stabilise the rifle. I did better with the SAR-80 in the range than with the m-16, in anycase.
    However, it was a bitch to run down the range with, but not due to the weight. it's just that the cross-section of the rifle is almost square, and it has no handle, so it makes it cumbersome to grab hold of (unless you have a hand the size of a gorilla's), and this was one main drawback i found with the SAR-80. This problem kept surfacing repeatedly, even when you are casually handling the rifle, like during guard duty. The M-16 was much handier to use.
    All said though, i found the rifle quite reliable. very little fouling even though we had quite a few extra range sessions close to my ORD, and in my two years of using it, and after all the range sessions, it never jammed once.
    Please keep in mind that i was from a log unit, so the rifle never went running through the mud or water or stuff like that, so my views are, admittedly, a little skewed. My two cent's worth.
