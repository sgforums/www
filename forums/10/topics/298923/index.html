  • robertteh

    Dear forummers,

    Our politicians always talk about our rule of law. Is it true that our law is so good and just that there is rule of law in this country?

    From the way the rich and the elites are getting away from punishment for failing to serve NS to the elite drug offenders not being given the fair punishment under the law, is it not obvious that we do not have a good justice system under the current state of the law.

    A poor prisoner would be punished seriously for a minor crime whereas the rich who can engage the best lawyer like in SPH vs NKF/Durai case is the state of justice of our law at the present moment.

    It appears that our current system of law is too legalistic and judges and police would rather look for superficially good judgment to close a case and unable to serve the need of the people in delivering justice.

    Currently laws are full of legalistic assumptions rather than whether the witnesses are telling the truths against the offenders. So judges being aware are unable to serve the need of justice for the innocent as the case presented by a lying witness look good for conviction. They may do so stay legalistic and unchallengeable especially the other party is a powerful or rich person who may have resources to appeal. So to be safe in his judgment it will be weighted against the innocent poor. There may be too many such cases where the rich are having their way with the judges and this will be how judges and police handle and deliver justice.

    So justice cannot prevail because judges are being too legalistic and working to serve their own need - to be legalistic and following current procedures to avoid problems.

    Is there not a possibility of witnesses laying sometimes to save their own skins ?

    Given such a possibility scenario about our current justice system, where there is really no true justice to begin with how could all believe there is rule of law.

    Do we have justices like the Pao Jin Tien or the renown Justice Pao who will go all out to investigate the case for the innocent?

    Currently an accused may be innocent but he is simply unable to put up with with procedural assumptions of his guilt due to his inability to get evidences or witnesses to defend his own case.

    How many of our judges can openly and publicly say that justice has not been compromised.

    Are our politicians judges and prosecutors capable of granting the fullest assistance in protecting the innocent accused who unwittingly at times are just unable to defend themselves against the weight of discretions power and assumptions on the part who administer the justice.

    So to that degree of uncertainty people can be innocent and convicted of serious crimes punished by death sentence.

    A death sentence can be handed out due to our current uncertainty with witnesses and need to comply with legalistic procedures to avoid controversy.

    Therefore there is a strong and logical case to abolish death penalty or harsh sentences because of too many possibilities of witnesses lying or judges being unable to serve the people's needs for justice.
