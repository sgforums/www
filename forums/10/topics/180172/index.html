    Those who feel that they may be offended or are unable to contribute to this slightly provocative yet sensible discussion please do not read on.

    I, personally, being chinese, have never discriminated against the minorities here in Singapore. Back in Secondary 2, my class used to conduct football friendlies during recess which we would dub "Racist Cup" where we'd pit malays against the chinese. And we actually did it in humour, rather than enimity. So when I was younger, I did not believe in The PAP's mantra of Racial Tolerance, but rather, sought to create Acceptance. In a sense, I did not see other races as a malay, indian or eurasian - but as a Singaporean.

    However, I have grown disenchanted in recent years as I see more and more instances of racism primarily by the largest minority here in Singapore. Where I used to work as a waiter, the older chinese aunties would warn me about them (The largest minority) and I'd often tell them not to be so racist. I could see why, but because they did not offend me or my race, I felt that it was just their culture and if the majority of them want to lag behind in society, so be it. After all, in every society, you need low-end workers as well.

    Now 21, I am looking at things in an absolutely different light. I think I have lost count the number of times I've had my food/service from the Major minority delayed because they prefer to entertain to their own race first. Most of the times I just cancel my order from them. Also, in my camp, the people in charge are "them". Expectedly, the NSFs who are of the same race are given preferential treatment BLATANTLY. Imagine being given more workload while the others slack away just because you're a Chinese? Also, I've noted that they tend to ostracised their "westernised" counterparts for some strange reason. Is it because of jealousy?

    Can someone shed some light on this "phenomenom"? I fear of sharing my opinions because I may be termed a racist. It would be better if you're from the said Race.

    I just hope the PAP would target not just young children but also those in their teens as well as adults about racial harmony. Maybe we'd learn more from it that way.

    Also, stop hiding "ugly" statistics of races in performance charts either socially, economically or politically. Not doing so to me in considered racist. We should be more open in that aspect instead of using paper to cover all the cracks that are surfacing in our society.
