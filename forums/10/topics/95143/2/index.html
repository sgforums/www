    Dear Chun Yong,

    It is really disheartening to read your posting here.

    We are not only voting for someone who is only in charge of our sector (i.e. Town Council), definitely not someone that is just to switch off the lights after the lesson ends. MPs' responsibilities are much more important than that. Though the overwhelming domination of PAP in parliament has practically turn the parliament into something like a "rubber stamp" in law making, but it is still important to have proper parliamentary debates on every issues. MPs are law makers and they are supposed to pass laws that would eventually affect our daily lives.

    To belittle the role of the MP and thus the vote in your hand is to make a mockery out of the democractic process we have at the moment. PAP links the governing of the ward via Town Councils to the selection of MPs, has deliberately sidetrack the original importance of the role of MPs as law makers. Voting is a matter of struggle between social role vs self interests. This link only tries to enhance the considerations on the "self interests" by each and everyone, playing down the importance (supposedly to be a more important role) of the social role of lawmakers. I call this "rule by materialism". Subsequently, to enhance such materialistic self-interests considerations, they added HDB upgrading into the equations.

    Is this healthy for Singapore's long term development? After I read your posting here, it has confirmed my fear that the success of such tactics have done great damage to our fabric of society.

    Goh Meng Seng

    Dear Nelstar,

    There are advantages and disadvantages of taking "hasty" approach. Efficiency comes with a price; At what Price?

    To have a "stable" system and vision, we should not be afraid of people "catching up" with us at all.

    We must realize that we are part of Souteast Asia, not America. We must also realize that our neighbours could well be our strength, not "enemies". For the past decades, we have tried to live and strive out alone, without the need of taking our neighbours into considerations. We are encaged in such an "unfriendly" mindset that the only considerations we have is how fast our neighbours are "catching up"! This is a source of tension and unhappiness sowed in our relationship with our neighbours. This will have to resolve ultimately.

    Whether we like it or not, we are stuck down here, a little red dot in SEA. The vision of Singapore would be a stronger, united ASEAN; ASEAN UNION should be the long term strategic aim, with economic Free Trade Zone as the mid term aim. What we lack as a small nation, we could establish it in economic unison with our neighbours. We lack economic depth and we cannot expect to find it in some far away places like USA!

    I am risking sensitive strategic political views to state it here. The total population masses we have in this ASEAN economic union will make us a formidable entity with great bargaining power, as comparable to China, India, EU or even USA. We should enhance economic development and union with our neighbours instead of taking them as a form of egoistic comparison, which has been done again and again by PAP leadership, so insensitive to the feelings of our neighbours.

    Goh Meng Seng
