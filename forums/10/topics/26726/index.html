    i frankly think u're nuts...

    firstly, mindef is in "paper" mode these days... no paper qualifications, be prepared henta kaki...

    secondly, mindef is especially in scholar mode these days... they take care of the careers of their scholars (and why shouldn't they? they've spent a fortune educating them!) if u're not one of them, be prepared for many frustrating moments when u think that u're just as good as them, but your chance is lost cos u're not one of them. it may or may not be true but it will eat u inside out and piss u off plenty if it ever happens...

    thirdly, unless u're thinking of doing it for the long haul... i.e. straight in from NS, first contract, second contract and work all your life, then retire with a fat bonus, don't do it. u stay in for one contract, u feel tempted to stay on forever... at 30, u really don't wanna risk too much by starting all over, esp if u've got family...

    fourth, u only aim to be an officer. its the only route that pays properly. but because u've only got a diploma, be prepared to henta kaki even if u get there... u may not even get captain. rarely they do, but u never, ever get major.

    fifth, keep your options open... many other places other than commandos can prove just as rewarding in many ways. unless u like pain in massive doses, i seriously don't think its a good idea to go in and end up hating it... the regulars in there are really extreme

    they usually screw their limbs before they're 30... kneecaps, elbows, back, u name it, they've cracked it... its physically very intense but these people have proven that its "mind over matter"... and they'll keep going if they can because their career depends on it and they really like doing it... its beyond my comprehension ...

    once u get in there, this is the procedure:

    BMT - 3 months promote to PTE
    BCT basic commando training - 3 months then promote to CPL
    Airborne Course - earn ur wings after 5 jumps. 2 CF(clean fatigue), 2 CE(combat equipment), 1 NCE (night combat equipment).
    VT vocational training: you have to go for "leader course" to be a 3SG b4 u can be selected to go for OCS.

    for officers, they will leave after these nine months and go to OCS without the triservice term. so i guess that leaves 6 more months b4 they become a cdo 2LT. somewhere in between i think u will have to do the 72 click route march like the rest of them. can't remember if our officer cadets joined us.

    after that i think it's much the same as any other place. come back with a chocolate bar, do your PC tour, then the rest is see how..

    this is quite old information tho... things might have changed by now, and i dun even remember a few things.. so this might not be the most accurate info.

    Well, here it goes...
    1. 10wks of BMT
    2. 8wks of BCT(Basic Commando Training)
    3.*2mths of VT(Vocation Training)
    *For Regulars, they have to go for ATCOR selection before Commando Section Leader Course. ATCOR stands for Acceptance Test for Cdo Regulars... Pass the selection and you'll be accepted as Regulars in the CDO!
    4. After that you attend BAC(Basic Airborne Course)...Potential Officer Cadets will attend OCS after BAC.
    After commissioning as Officers, you have to come back for COCC(Cdo Officers Conversion Course). At OCS, you'll be taught Infantry Tactics...that's why u need the conversion course.

    NB: All Cdos have to be fit and at the same time mentally tough! It's all about mind over matter...How tough it is?? U outta come and find out yourself!
