    CHINESE BANK RESUMES ATM WITHDRAWAL, TRANSFER SERVICES: FSC
    2007-01-07 18:59:44

    Taipei, Jan. 7 (CNA) Automated teller machines (ATM) for Taipei- based Chinese Bank have resumed inter-bank money withdrawal and transfer services starting 7 a.m. Sunday, according to Financial Supervisory Commission (FSC) Vice Chairwoman Susan Chang.

    The FSC announced late Friday that Central Deposit Insurance Corp. (CDIC) would be taking over Chinese Bank -- a member of the Rebar Asia Pacific Group -- beginning midnight Saturday, in the wake of a bank run sparked by the insolvency of the group's two member firms, China Rebar Co and Chia Hsin Food & Synthetic Fiber Co.

    Some clients of the cash-strapped bank complained that they have been unable to withdraw funds despite the FSC's assurance that inter-bank withdrawals and transfer services by other banks' ATMs were operating as usual.

    Chang said this could not occur theoretically, since the ATM system is a data-processing system linking computer facilities of financial institutions to those of the country's central bank on a 24-hour basis, and is a system designed for the real-time transfer of funds across the institutions' deposit reserve accounts at the central bank.

    But, she added that if clients complain they are still unable to withdraw their money, the FSC will deal with such extraordinary situations on a case-by-case basis.(By Y.L.Kao)ENDITEM/diG
