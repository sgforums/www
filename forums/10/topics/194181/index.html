    There has been a standing rule by HDB that prohibits the feeding of stray cats in the void decks, due to the high birth rate of these cats that are left to wander the HDB neighbor, causing a nuisance during the mating season.

    This prohibition was extended to the feeding of wild pigeons, which are well known to dirty the neighborhood with their droppings - and more urgently so, with the current mysteriously uncurable Bird Flu problems.

    The net effect of the feeding of cats and pigeons is that left-over food will be consumed by rodents, crows, dogs and occassionaly monkeys.

    The Town Councils have since taken over the administration of this rule with a gentler touch, but HDB may step in when the situation becomes a serious nuisance to the public.

    The SPCA has its own views about the effects of such severe rules, and believe that a more responsible and caring approach can help to address the concerns raised in the breeding of unwanted pests.

    Six months for feeding cats ? This sounds like an overkill.

    It is the arrogance in the ease of use of legislation to change social behaviour.
