    Finland's coalition government works because "Co-operation among the major parties is traditional".

    The fact that Finland is doing better compared with Singapore shows that PAP style 1 party domination is the the only way to do things.

    It also shows that 2 parties system is not the only alternative to 1 party domination.

    However, coalition government does not work well if there is no "Co-operation among the major parties". In such case, the coalition government works by making deals with individual parties to get the required majority votes.

    If Singapore is moving toward a diversive, inclusive society, coalition government is a viable option. It is also more preferable. The policy change will not be too drastic when new coalition government is formed compared with that in two parties system.
