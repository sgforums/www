    She loses baby after robbers' bashing
    April 12, 2006

    A BUSINESSWOMAN miscarried for a second time in two years after she was attacked in a petrol station toilet last Saturday.

    Madam Shirley Koh, 35, was with her husband, their 4-year-old son and their maid.

    The family stopped at a petrol station in Jalan Tebrau, Johor Bahru, on their way home after shopping at City Square.

    Madam Koh told Malaysian newspapers that she and her maid had taken her son to use the toilet when four or five men in their 20s suddenly rushed in.

    She said in tears: 'They started hitting me. They punched my face, and kicked me in the stomach.'

    She covered her face but the men continued to hit her around her head and neck.

    Madam Koh was seven months pregnant with a boy and, during her ordeal, she heard someone say in Malay: 'She's pregnant, don't kick her.'

    The attackers took her handbag, containing RM1,000 ($440), as well as her ATM and credit cards before fleeing.

    It was too late when her husband, who had gone to the cashier, rushed her to the hospital.

    They lost the baby the next day.

    This is Madam Koh's second miscarriage. Two years ago, her five-month-old foetus suddenly stopped growing.

    Her husband said that they were shaken, but still hoped to have more children in the future.
