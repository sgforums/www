    Originally posted by Atobe:

     

    Is the Singapore Pledge all about EQUALITY only, or something larger then just the word concerning 'equality' ?

    The maiden speech by NMP Viswa Sadasivan in the Singapore Parliament on 18 August 2009 (*1)  had called for the Singapore Government to conduct its policies along the lines of The Pledge - which was crafted as:-

     

    We, the Citizens of Singapore - pledge Ourselves as ONE UNITED PEOPLE - regardless of race, language, or religion -  to build a Democratic Society based on Justice and Equality, so as to achieve happiness, prosperity and progress for our nation.

     

     

    You are right, the pledge is not about equality; it is about HARMONY.  Following this definition, my point was simply that absolute equality across all spectrums is a Utopian dream.  The same can be said of Justice.

    It doesn't mean we throw them out of the window, but rather, we hold on to these ideals as guiding principles. 

    And honestly, I feel that our national pledge really means llittle since all the nouns used are abstract concepts that no one actually bothered to sit down and define.  "Happiness" can be 2 very different thing between you and me, and the way to achieve it requiring differing methods. Maybe the problem was really having too many of those terms...?

    Compare it against the American Pledge of Allegience..

    I pledge allegiance to the Flag
         of the United States of America,
    and to the Republic for which it stands:
         one Nation under God, indivisible,
    With Liberty and Justice for all.



    As you can see, it is actually a pledge of allegiance to the State and elected government and presidency.  The Singaporean pledge, simple put, requires us to live harmoniously with one another.

     

    I think why what LKY said was so "controversial" was that most people either did not know or was afraid of the truth of equality.  That none are truly equal.  Governments can endow upon their citizens equal social, economic, civil and politica/legal rights, but at the end of the day, none are truly equal.  To attempt to enforce equality would be as good as being communists where you strip the bourgeoisie of their wealth and redistribute it to the poor/workers. 

    Another matter which I would say in a rather frank manner is that the constitutional advantages given to the Malays are needed due to the sensitivity of our region.  We really do not want to appear as a "little red dot in a sea of green."  The lack of such an institution may legitimize "Malay-Chinese" polarization of our little corner in SEA. 

    I guess what LKY was trying to say is that, and true our rather oddly Hobbesian characteristics, we ought to strive for sufficient equality to maintain a peaceful and harmonious society.

    We, the citizens of Singapore

Rajaratnam was inspired to craft The Singapore Pledge to be sworn to by its Citizens to build a Singapore that will be totally different and which will ensure 'happiness, prosperity and progress for our nation'.

    To ensure that Singapore will not follow the ways of the UMNO ULTRAS, the citizens of Singapore had considered that even as we will build a Democratic society based on justice and equality - the special interests of the minority races will be protected.

    Was S Rajaratnam's objectives for "equality" based on the Communists ideology of a classless society with all being treated equally in all ways - irregardless of productive efforts and receiving all rewards in equal portions ?

    Or was the "equality" that he wanted for Singapore based on the democratic principle of justice and equal opportunity for all - irregardless of race, language or religion - that allow each and every citizen the opportunity towards happiness and prosperity; in total contrasts to the racist politics of the Malaysian UMNO ULTRAS that demand first and foremost the recognition to the privileges for their majority race in Malaysia ?

    Was it simply to appease the sea of green that this Little Red Dot happened to be accidentally sited by the good fortunes of nature, or was it done to appease our own conscience of justice and equality for all in a democratic society to be built ?

    Was it not done due to the recognition that the Malays as a minority group were less capable to compete in a society that is to be built on equal basis for all ? 

    Is it unequal for the other ethnic groups in Singapore that in order for the minority Malays not to be left behind in the march to progress, that the Constitution was specially redefined to include a Clause that will protect the special interests given to the Singapore Malays ?
