    Protectionism isn't all that bad, it can help salvage the local industry and save jobs too. But protectionism cannot persist indefinitely, it could be use as a measure to protect the local industry, giving them time to reorganise their structure, gain technology to improve on their products.

    Tariffs could be applied to the cheaper imports, which can go into subsidising the local industry, this saves job in the local industry.

    If local producers produce at $10 while foreign country produces at $5, the local government could always levy tariffs from the foreign country at $2.50 per unit. The $2.50 goes into subsidising and sustaining the local industry. Both products will sell at $7.50 in the local market. Yes, you could get it for $5 in another foreign country, but by buying at $7.50, you save the jobs of the local market. Everybody pays abit more, but everybody has a job. Protectionism isn't any different from the Kereitsu's (ç³»åˆ—)  of the Japanese, where they will use Japanese firms products though these products are priced higher than other competitors.
