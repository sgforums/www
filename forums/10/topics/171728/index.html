    I have highlighted this in the Cars Forum before, so thought I might as well post it here too...

    If you have not noticed, read the news quite some time ago, LTA is coming down hard on Fog lamp offenders. Fog Lamps can only be used when (technically speaking) there is fog or poor visibility.     Originally posted by anonymouscoward:
    anyone knows if halogen head lamps are permitted? if you ask me, I find those white, irritating halogen head lamps so much more annoying that fog lamps.
    Actually all head lights are halogen type. I think you are referring to the High intensity discharge lights aka HID. These are factory fitted in most german made cars and Lexus. HID is useful if you drive at night on North South highway, as it lights up the road in front like as if it is day light, good visibility.

    But these HID lights are fast becoming a status related fixtures on normal cars. With aftermarket HID lights at cheap prices, even KIA cars have them now.

    These are really not necessary in Singapore city driving, since our roads are always well lighted. For me, I am sticking to the original "yellow" lights as I feel that they provide better visibility under street lighting conditions.
