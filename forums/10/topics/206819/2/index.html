    National Service doesn't take all that much out of the economy. True our workforce is 40,000 people smaller but honestly the pros more than make up for the cons.

    The SAF has a standing force of around 20,000. If you include NSFs and NSmen, the figure is very much larger. Without NS, Singapore's forces would be no comparison to the 100,000 that our nearest neighbour fields. The added security more than makes up for the pain of going through NS. Never make the mistake of thinking that our neighbours are completely friendly towards us or that the confidence with which we conduct our dealings with them can be maintained without a credible armed force.

    We cannot add forces to the SAF by attempting to have a larger standing army. This is simply due to the fact that it costs very much more to pay an army of regulars as compared to NSFs and NSmen.

    The strength of the SAF lies in the airforce and navy, two wings that have much lower representation from NSFs than the army. Yet this does not mean that we can neglect to develop our ground forces. In the event of a war, they will be needed to take and hold territory to give our commanders strategic depth to work with.

    As a Singaporean male who has to give up 2 years of his life like everyone else, I can safely say that I feel more confident in Singapore for having NS.
