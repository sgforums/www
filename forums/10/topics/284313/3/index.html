    In my opinion, I feel that due to the higher standard of living and the booming economy, many Singaporeans can afford to buy cars. The vehicle population in Singapore has increased rather significantly from 799373 in year 2006 to 832996 in the period of Aug 2007 thanks to the statistics from Singapore Department of Statistics.
    From 2005 754992 to 2006 799973 to 2007 832996, the increase seems to be getting bigger. Hence, jams can be seen everywhere.

    In regards to about the standards of public transport, I have posted a comment and observation under 147 bunching problem at Singapore Buses Forum.

    http://www.sgforums.com/?action=thread_display&thread_id=284124

    In summary, with the unforeseen traffic conditions such as traffic jams, bus bunching are going to get worse. So, if nothing is being done, expect a decline in bus service standards in terms of waiting times.

    Bus bunching means that buses tend to cluster in groups as they travel along the bus route.

    With perfect traffic conditions, in my opinion, bus bunching tend to occur due to the fact of having traffic lights which hamper the smooth travel of vehicles. However, with traffic jams occurring at certain parts of the route, buses will be clustered along the location where traffic jams occur.

    This means that freq of buses will be more irregular. 28 mins, 2 mins, 4 mins, 4 mins in IRIS will be more of a common sight.

    In my opinion, finding another solution into combating the common traffic jams seen at the roads is linked to bus service standards. What we want is the somooth freq of buses.
