    At the forum with Minister Raymond Lim televised today over Channel 5, it is obvious that the Minister was unable to answer to the irate Thomson residents' query : "We don't mind to pay the ERP but what we are frustrated is despite paying the ERP the traffic situation does not improve."

    Minister was unable to satisfy the residents in his reply: "If we do not have ERP, traffic will get worse. That is why we have other measures including COEs and better bus services.     It is so sad that the ERP has been proposed as the ultimate solution to the traffic congestion problem but now the residents have to bear higher and higher costs and unlike being presented as the final solution it has not solve the traffic problem at all despite all the fees being paid.

    The residents in Thomson are right in saying that if we cannot solve the problem now with ERP we might as well let it go back to square one and abolish ERP instead of paying all the monies and causing costs of living to go up and yet traffic situation is getting worse.

    If we go back to the parliament debates many years ago, the ministers in charge of transportation were on record as saying in the initial years that vehicle excise duty was not a good tool to allocate road usage so subsequently suggested introduction of COE which was claimed to be more equitable in doing so.

    After COE was introduced although excise duty was supposedly or promised to be replaced by COE excise duty remained at 170% of OMV for many years which was only slowly reduced to about 130% some years later.

    So this COE constituted some sort of double taxation on the middle class for many years depriving them of disposable income and causing the economy essentially to be slowed down or retarded as a result.

    COE did not seem to solve the problem so minister later went to parliament to propose ERP as a more equitable way to price road usage. The emphasis was laid on road usage as it was said to be the final and ultimate solution to road congestion.

    After the above-stated COE and ERP the middle class citizens were being fleeced by the hundreds of thousands in the work cycle of 30-40 years just to feed ministers' million-dollar salaries.

    Where is the final solution to road congestion. For all you know there may have to be another REP (repeated error pricing) or RIP(road interlocution pricing) in order to finally put to rest the road congestion problem but citizens will have to wait for another decade to hear excuses why four solutions are not enough to solve problem with new excuses such as road congestion requires some other total or holistic final solution.
