    Tripartism on the surface is fine as it is a gentler way to bring about peaceful settlements of disputes between employers and employees without disruption by industrial actions like boycott etc.

    But it has many unspoken underlying meanings or conventions as well about being non-confrontational and non-interfering in the right of the employer to hire and fire employees.

    Given such general labour law, no wonder many employers in Singapore have no hesitation in refusing to pay retrenchment benefits except to unionised employees. In many cases, employees are fired without proper procedures being followed often wrongfully? How many wronged employees will be able except in limited cases involving collective agreement, turn to MOM for redress when his employer refuses to pay his entitled 13th month or other agreed benefits. More often than not the aggrieved employees will have to face arbitrary actions by the employers who regard it as his right to terminate contract by notice without the employee being given a fair hearing or defence except in conducting justice through his own expensive legal process.
