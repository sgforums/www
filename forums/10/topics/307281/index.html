    I think it was more an economic observations.

    Probably along the lines of using a rule of thumb that a labor force of x numbers can create a GDP of y dollars and we foresee that Singapore's economy is growing at z dollars a year. We assume that in the year qqqq we'd be creating z+ dollars which means we should anticipate needing x+ workforce at that time.

    Assuming we're not able to reproduce x+ citizens by qqqq year who are ready to enter the workforce, we'll need to make up the difference with foreign labor so we start to attract them now.

     

    The problem is that economic policies clash with other types of policies all the time. Notwithstanding that applying free market theories in a regulated market usually creates lots of interesting problems too.
