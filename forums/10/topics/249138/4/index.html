    "The Fatwa Committee's view is that these latest amendments under HOTA (in 2004) are permissable, according to islamic religious laws and principles. A Muslim can donate his or her kidney, heart, liver and cornea with consent of one next-of-kin and witnessed by another person" - MUIS Fatwas

    Originally posted by sgquitter:

    You are the one who is confused. For organ donation purpose, brain death refers to brain stem death, the complete and irreversible cessation of all brain stem functions. The criteria for determining brain death include at least 7 clinical tests for various aspects of brainstem function and that has stood the test of time in the medical literature. If a patient satisfies those clinical criteria, there is no chance of recovery because the brainstem is dead and the heart will stop soon after life support measures are discontinued. Local doctors follow the best practice as performed by all reputable hospitals worldwide.

    Of course the media loves a story about cases where the doctors made a mistake when they fail to apply the criteria properly. The most common situation is when a comatosed patient (after road traffic accident etc), is placed under sedation. The criteria for determining brain death requires that anaesthesia must be stopped and a blood test done to confirm that it has fallen below therapeutic levels before the clinical tests for brain death can be applied. If the doctors fail to recognize that sedation can mimic signs of brain death and the need to remove barbiturates and other sedating agents, they can make a mistake in rare situations. However, the media would blow up the rare mistakes into a everyday occurrence. The fault in those instances is with the doctors who apply the criteria, not the criteria themselves.
