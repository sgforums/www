Now if we also put this "converted" Singaporeans into the equation, I can tell you the porportion of actual FT taking away professional jobs is even more significant.

    Just looking at the ratio I've given, it is obvious that the competition is fierce. If only I did a count of the job applicants and look at the profile, I can relate even more accurately the situation. But that's not my job.

    The way I look at it is although the threat of FT is tremendous, it is necessary to keep Singapore very competitive otherwise which a number of companies would already fold up from global competition and otherwise which many MNC would not want to locate their HQ in Singapore. If the government tightens the control on FT, the FT would naturally go elsewhere and we probably would see a drop in competitiveness of Singapore companies.

    Nonetheless, it is important that our government ensures every Singaporean is given at least a starting job where Singaporeans can have a foundation to compete with the FT. And I must say the job of retaining competitiveness vs giving Singaporeans their bread and butter is a tough one. Finding that optimal point is not only difficult but very subjective!
