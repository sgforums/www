    Agence France Presse
    July 26, 2005
    VIENTIANE

    SE Asia cannot afford to isolate Myanmar: S'pore

    ISOLATING Myanmar would not be in Southeast Asia's interests because the military regime has its "gates" open to emerging regional giants China and India, Singapore's foreign minister said Tuesday, July 26.

    Foreign Minister George Yeo said he did not believe that with ramped up pressure and "the wave of a wand, all the problems (in Myanmar) will be solved and everybody will live happily ever after."

    US and EU sanctions on Myanmar's internationally condemned junta have done little to bring real change to the country.

    "But Myanmar has the back gate to China wide open. India, in its own geo-strategic calculation, has decided to keep its side gate to Myanmar open," Yeo said at a news conference.

    "So it must be in the interest of ASEAN to keep our side gate open whatever happens in Myanmar.

    Nonetheless, "Myanmar has decided from very early that it would rather be a part of Southeast Asia than be a part of South Asia and we welcome that," he said.

    Yeo was speaking after foreign ministers of the Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN) announced that Myanmar had taken the unprecedented decision to skip the chairmanship of the grouping next year.

    Myanmar said the decision, which came after months of pressure from the United States and European Union, was to enable it to concentrate on its "democratisation process".

    Is it in the interest of ASEAN to stay engage with Myanmar; or is it in the interest of SINGAPORE to have ASEAN staying engaged with Myanmar ?

    With so much investments made by the Singapore Government in Myanmar, through all the Government Linked Companies, it is little wonder that our Singapore Foreign Minister George Yeo is so persuasive to convince fellow ASEAN Countries not to abandon Myanmar.

    Is it so bad for Myanmar to lean closer to China and / or India ?

    If there is active and positive bi-lateral relationships between Myanmar and India, and Myanmar and China, it can only mean that there is lesser leverage for the Singapore Government to apply to protect its own investments.

    The more progressive and openly democratic countries of ASEAN - (such as Indonesia, Malaysia, Philippines, and Thailand) - are openly critical of Myanmar's internal policy that does not respect the "People's Will" in the last election - (when it was confirmed that the Opposition had won an overwhelming majority); and that the Military Junta continue to hold all the reigns of power and refuse to loosen the hold on the Opposition candidate - Aung San Su Chi.

    It remains only the un-Democratic countries in this region to support the concept of 'non-interference' in one another's internal affairs, and advocate the continued engagement of Myanmar.

    Should an issue of conscience appear, these un-Democratic countries in ASEAN will merely discuss the issues from a spectators' point of views.

    The ASEAN countries will not interfere nor pressure the Myanmar Government, who will customarily reject all persuasions to free the leader of the Opposition Group, and for the Military Junta to step down.
