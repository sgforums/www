    Agreed with the orginal author and the bit about non-profitable talent being wasted in Singapore.

    Lets have, say... someone who is vastly talented in the military field... or maybe someone who is brimming with ideas for communication studies and advertising. But with average results in other fields.

    But both the courses/scholarships that suit them (For example, the DSTA scholarship and say, NUS Communications Studies course) are "4A2S"-type... meaning that you won't get anywhere near them without a near-flawless A-level cert.
    I'm sure that you have heard of DSTA scholars who really don't have the interest (and know nuts about) military matters... They only seek to get the scholarship to improve their CV and for the money. They don't make up all the DSTA scholars, but there are enough of them to be noticeable (I know some of them personally). Ditto with the Comm Studies course (I know that, sister graduated from it)

    As a result, those with the interest and the specialised ability don't get anywhere near these courses/scholarships. And Singapore loses another talent.

    What we need to do is to remove that damned "excellent academic results" requirement from the specialised scholarships and in-demand courses. Sure, the so-called intelligent fellows will bitch and moan (because their 4A's, plus a B3 in GP) just doesn't cut it anymore with a "4B's, with an A1 in GP" fellow when competing for, say... a place in an English-specialised course.

    But that's the only way we can get commited people who are interested and talented in that field into the course... Not some ticket-punching rote-learning 4A bugger with the intention of getting a better CV and NO personal interest (aside from required mugging) in mentioned high-demand course or scholarship.

    Same thing goes with the DSTA scholarship... If the 4A2S scholar wannabe wants to get the title and money, but cannot tell the difference between a tank and APC, etc. then he should NOT get the scholarship. Instead, it should go to someone with the interest and the ability.

    Trust me, I've met some of these types of people who actually admit to "going for the name/money" as opposed to being truly commited to the path. Not all of the high-flyers are like that... but sadly, enough of them are.
