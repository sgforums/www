    It seems that the Malaysians are testing Singapore's resolve to resort to military deterrence by pressing on these issues. They are probing into water, to see how deep the SAF runs. And recently, they have "Requested" or rather is taunting the SAF to move.

    They hope to trigger any military response from the SAF, in order to observe and in future hopefully deal with. They feel they have nothing to lose at the moment since any response from the SAF would violate their "national sovereignty" and earn Singapore condemnation from the international community.

    If Singapore could, the SAF should be holding large scale exercises on home soil to flex its military muscles to show that it has the resolve. Yet due to the land and space constraint, it cannot do that. Large scale exercises tend to offer some combat experience, instill confidence, and raise morale.

    A foreseeable move by the MAF would be likewise. They may do what the SAF cannot. They may hold large scale exercises to intimidate the SAF, and try to nullify Singapore's Military deterrence. At the same time, they would attempt to impose their political will, on Singapore. Succeeding in that, would indirectly imply that Malaysia IS S.E.A's regional leader.

    WHY: The Malaysian Govt is fearful. Not to mention sick and tired of being 2nd best. They feel out of control of regional politics and now seek to correct that. The time is right, regional countries including Singapore are slowly recovering from the economic downturn. Recovering but no doubt still hurt. They should have acted earlier, but perhaps they couldn't. In anycase, their time is now.

    It seems that the top of their agenda is creating a submissive and manageable Singapore. And their first attempt is taming Singapore's lion, the SAF. Without a doubt, they are hunting the SAF, attempting to corner it, and hopefully tame it by demoralising it, and starving it of public confidence. It is a dangerous game, taunting lions. They had better be prepared to kill the lion should they fail.
