    SINGAPORE'S Temasek Holdings has been very much in the news in recent weeks with the high-profile multi-billion-dollar purchase of Shin Corp and its offer for P&O.

    While many of Temasek's deals are long term in nature, it is nonetheless disheartening to learn that for the last five years, its total shareholder return was a paltry one per cent a year, against 2.7 per cent for the Straits Times Index.

    As a stakeholder in the country's hard-earned reserves, I find this most disappointing as its performance is way below that of many public companies here.

    Surely, the rate of shareholder return must form part of the key performance indicators for Temasek's senior executives. The return on investment must at least match those achieved by the average public company.

    Jeffrey Ho Loon Poh
