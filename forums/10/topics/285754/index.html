    CPF was first started by the British Colonial Government, and the Local Self-Rule Government formed by the MIW in 1957 - {only after the transition of several governments formed by other political parties} - continued with this practise to fund its own administrative works and development plans.

    It is almost 50 years that the CPF interest remained unchanged until the Second Minister of Finance make the present changes to the CPF in 2007.

    Fifty years, the Government had access to cheap CPF funds - this is an undenial fact, and past MPs have similarly made such an observations, with the MPs from the MIW consistently retracting their statements - perhaps under pressure from the Party Whip.

    It is common sense, and does not take rocket science calculations to figure out that the CPF based on the present structure cannot work.

    The amount taken out of circulation from CPF ANNUALLY by even a small number of retirees will have made a huge dent into the CPF account; a dent that new participants in the CPF program cannot possibly make up.

    With a declining population, it becomes even harder to fill up the dent created by the withdrawals of the retirees.

    It is not surprising that this MIW Government has continuously advocated the holding back of FULL PAY OUT of the CPF to retiring Singaporeans, which will amount to the breaking of the social contract promised by the MIW Government to Singaporeans since the 1950s.

    What more can be said of the MIW ?
