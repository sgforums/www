    newater isn't desalinated waste water. its waste water that has gone through reverse osmosis (micro-filtration) and therefore uses very little energy.

    the only way to purify sea water to a satisfactory level is distillation, which is basically boiling off the sea water and condensing the steam to get water. the energy required to boil off such large amounts of water is way too much for this method to be viable. and certain impurities which have a lower boiling point than water would also end up in the distilled water.

    so the reason newater is used is both because it uses very little energy (almost no energy at all is used in the purification process itself) and the water is cleaner.

    also, salt cannot be filtered out of sea water.

    if you know some chemistry you'll know that salt water is a solution, and the salt in the water cannot be filtered out at all. you have to boil it off and condense the steam.

    salt water is a solution, whereas waste water is a mixture, hence the impurities in waste water can be filtered out.
