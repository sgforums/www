Each of these kept a substantial deposit at the B. I.     They made agreements on all the major financial problems of the world, as well as on many of the economic and political problems, especially in reference to loans, payments, and the economic future of the chief areas of the globe.

    The B. I. S. is generally regarded as the apex of the structure of financial capitalism whose remote origins go back to the creation of the Bank of England in 1694 and the Bank of France in 1803.

It was set up rather to remedy the decline of London as the world's financial center bv providing a mechanism bv which a world with three chief financial centers in London, New York, and Paris could still operate as one.

    The B. I. S. was a vain effort to cope with the problems arising from the growth of a number of centers. It was intended to be the world cartel of ever-growing national financial powers by assembling the nominal heads of these national financial centers...
