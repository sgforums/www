    Before the ERP was introduced, the then Minister of Transport - Mah Bow Tan - had promised that Road Tax will be brought down to earth - as soon as the ERP is extensively implemented to regulate the flow of traffic and the growth of the car population.

    Besides the implementation of the ERP, there is also the C.O.E. that allowed the authorities a revenue generating way of regulating the growth in the number of new cars.

    Yet with both successfully in place, the road tax had seen only a minor adjustment of no more then 15 percent reduction, when it had been increased by regular increments of 50% and 75% that finally end up with a road tax that is 150% from the fees charged during the period towards mid-1980's.
