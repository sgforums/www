    after reading the thread about Singapore's elitist education system, i decided to start thread because i want to voice out my comments, on something else about the education system, which would have been off topic in that thead (hence this disscussion)

    im sure most of you would have heard about the CCA (known formerly as ECA) points system applied in JCs, polytechnics (not sure) and secondary schools

    in the tertiary education system, they have the PEARLS points
    PEARLS stands for:

    Participation
    Enrichment
    Achievement
    Representation
    Leadership
    Service

    technically, when you take part in a cca, you get more points for the more you do
    i suppose this is to award those who are more active in their CCAs, and give them a "chance" to compete with the more academically inclined peers

    however, i feel that this system is flawed, and is doing more harm then good

    e.g.
    i have a friend who is a tennis player
    for the school's first team or not, that remains undetermined for now (he's still JC1)
    the tennis team trains (very hard, i would add) at least 3-4 times a week until 7-8 pm
    he complains that even though he puts in a lot of effort for his cca, he does not feel that the school acknowledges his sweat and toil
    according to the PEARLS system, he will only get 2 points overall if he continues his efforts in tennis, but fail to get into the first team.

    the main thing that im trying to say is not that the PEARLS system should award more points or whatnot, but rather the bigger picture, of whether it is doing its job appropriately

    right now, such a CCA system awards students who achieve top places in national or international competitions
    also, under the "leadership" category, it awards points to those who hold titles in leadership positions such as chairman or captain

    how about those who constantly put in effort, but do not have the ability to be one of the best?
    in an average sized cca, only one or two will be selected to become the "heads"
    how about the rest of the team who put in effort as well?
    maybe this is not "convincing" enough, i shall elaborate later

    i believe the CCA points system was set up to "level the playing field" among the students, some of whom do not have the same academic (the 3Rs: read, remember, regurgitate) talents of their fellow students
    this was supposedly to let those students "catch up" with the other students, and promote the idea of being "all-rounded" students

    what it did however, was just add obstacles to the already challenging rat race
    instead of helping those caught behind, this system raises the standards for those on top
    for them to even more differentiate themselves among the rest of the "scholars", CCA was nothing more then just to make themselves appear more "brilliant" then other hopefuls

    originally, national placings were put in place to encourage participation
    of course the schools responded well, they felt that it was a pretty good idea to introduce competition
    however, it worked too well
    schools with commemorable records in the CCA department used that as an advertisement, to encourage budding sportsmen to join them
    this does not seem to work both ways
    in institutions where the a particular CCA is well known for winning countless medals, only the best are allowed to remain in the CCA
    in other words, they have trials for all the players, and only the better ones are allowed to remain
    it would seem, that the drive for students to continue now comes from how many medals they can win, not their desire to acheive
    where is the passion?

    note: i have decided to end this post here, as incoherent as it seems, as i am having difficulty organising my thoughts
    it would be much easier to reply to posts...
    so please comment
    ty
