    Rexdriver's got a point there. A number of factors contribute to reducing the drug problem, not just the death penalty.

    IMHO there's no problem with the death penalty per se, but the mandatory aspect of the sentencing is something that needs to be revised. In this particular case, the drugs were not headed for Singapore, and that raises a fair question of the proportion of the punishment to the crime. It would be fair to keep the death penalty, but with a system that is flexible enough to accommodate the circumstances and that not resort to a default stance of guilt before innocence is proven.
