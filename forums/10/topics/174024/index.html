    Singapore lawyers to review death penalty
    Jan 13, 2006, 11:58 GMT

    SINGAPORE, Singapore (UPI) -- Singapore`s Law Society said Friday it planned to review the country`s controversial use of the death penalty and make recommendations to the government.

    Society President Philip Jeyaretnam said a committee of lawyers would study the legal issues to explore whether the death penalty should be left to judges` discretion or even abolished, The Australian reported.

    The death penalty is mandatory in Singapore for certain crimes, including murder and drug trafficking.

    The December hanging of a 25-year-old Australian, Van Tuong Nguyen, for drug trafficking caused an international outcry, backed up by local civil rights groups who want mandatory capital punishment abolished.

    The eight-member legal review committee could take up to nine months to complete its work, Jeyaretnam said.
