    Wife says sorry, but...
    Owner demands $18,000
    By Crystal Chan

    November 23, 2007

    THEY caught the person who scratched their Mercedes Benz on video. The next day they found an apology note on the windscreen. It was supposedly written by the culprit's wife.

    Mr Ang Shuenn Guan with the CCTV system he fixed up in his parents' flat. Picture: FRANCIS LEE
    But that was not good enough for businessman Ang Shuenn Guan, 37, and his wife.

    They made a police report, but were later told that the culprit would not be charged. Instead, the man received a stern warning from the police.

    Now they are thinking of hiring a lawyer to try to recover the cost of spray-painting their car. They bought the car last year for $220,000.

    Another neighbour also had his luxury car scratched by the same person.

    The Angs said that they found a 30cm scratch mark on their car in an open carpark near Block 636, Hougang Avenue 8, on 11 Apr this year.

    They made a police report and then spent $4,500 to install a closed-circuit camera at Mr Ang's parents' unit. His parents live on the third floor while his flat is on the seventh.

    The culprit struck again a month later. He was captured on camera but the Angs did not know who he was. They then asked other car owners in the neighbourhood.

    One of them, Mr Lim Chin Seng, said his black BMW had also been scratched. He, too, installed a video camera in his car to nab the culprit.

    On the police advice, the Angs lodged a magistrate's complaint on 5 Jun. The vandalism was considered an act of mischief and police cannot arrest the culprit or investigate the case without a magistrate's authorisation.

    On 11 Jun, the couple filmed the man in the act.

    When confronted, he initially denied it. But they claimed that he confessed after being told he had been caught on camera.

    Mrs Ang, a clerk, said: 'He immediately apologised and wanted to settle the matter privately. We didn't want to drop our case as we had evidence.'

    The Angs made their fourth police report the next day and handed the footage to the police.

    Earlier that morning, Mr Ang found the note on his car windscreen. The couple contacted the wife to ask for compensation of about $18,000 - $5,400 to spray-paint and $13,000 for renting a Mercedes S280 for 11 days.

    They showed us quotations for the spray-painting and rental.

    Mrs Ang said: 'She said she couldn't afford that amount, so it was a deadlock.'

    The Angs put off the spray-painting.

    But the culprit did pay Mr Lim's repair bill.

    Last month, the police wrote to the Angs saying that the culprit had been issued a stern warning.

    SHOULD HE BE PROSECUTED?

    When contacted, a police spokesman said they decided to do this after consulting with the Attorney-General's Chambers. They declined to comment on why he was not charged.

    Lawyers said this could be because the incident appeared minor.

    Lawyer Singa Retnam said: 'The incident involves private property so he could have been let off to avoid wasting resources on a prosecution.'

    But lawyer Kirpal Singh feels car owners should be entitled to legal protection, especially if they leave their cars in a public place.

    When contacted, the culprit's wife said: 'I'm trying to settle this matter privately and the police have already investigated.

    'I'm getting a lawyer to write to the Angs. I don't wish to talk about this incident anymore.'
