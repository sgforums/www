    Finally, after nearly 40 Years of Public Hankering, the Government has relented in their insistence of having the Second Language as an IMPORTANT CRITERIA for admission into a Tertiary Institution.

    2nd language barrier to enter university lifted
    By Sandra Davie

    A SACRED cow in education was slaughtered on Saturday when the Education Ministry announced that students no longer need to include the grade for their mother tongue subject when applying for a university here.

    The surprise move, which will take effect from this year, means that students who have difficulty with the mother tongue language will not be disadvantaged when they apply to enter the National University of Singapore (NUS) or Nanyang Technological University (NTU).

    But they must still score at least a D7 or a pass in Chinese language B to qualify for admission.

    Before, even if language laggards did better overall, they could lose out to others by being forced to count mother tongue when their grades were converted into admission scores.

    This issue has been a bugbear of students and parents and is said to have driven many students abroad to do their university studies because they could not meet the language requirement.

    Neither NTU nor NUS would say how many students had failed to get in under the mother tongue requirement, but they said there were several each year.

    New admission criteria at a glance

    Minimum requirement

    Old: Two A-level passes, General Paper and mother tongue. Polytechnic students needed a relevant diploma.

    New:Minimum A-level criteria will not change but polytechnic students can use a diploma in any discipline.

    Mother-tongue language and American Scholastic Assessment Test (SAT)

    Old:Mother-tongue language grades, SAT and A levels counted for admission.

    New: Mother tongue and SAT no longer needed. But for this year and next year, applicants may still use their SAT scores if these will boost their chances of admission.

    Two-tier admission framework


    Old: Applicants shortlisted by a total score based on their three best A-level subjects, General Paper and mother-tongue grades.

    New: Mother-tongue is no longer required but may be included, if the student does well, to boost his admission score. In addition to the score based on the A-level results, there will also be a faculty score using additional criteria that a faculty can set. It may include portfolios, reasoning tests or interviews.

    Special admission criteria


    Old: Universities set aside a few places for exceptionally talented students, for example, winners of international Olympiad competitions.

    New: A university may pick up to 10 per cent of its enrolment according to independent criteria that may include other activities a student is involved in, for example volunteer work or entrepreneurial activities.
