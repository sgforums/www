    Speaking about Nurses... does anyone here seriously think it takes just 2 years to train a professional soldier?

    But plenty of useful stuff can still be learnt and done in 2 years, it's not as if there's any reason to believe to NSF nurse will be totally incompetent... and given a time of crisis where there are many injured, anybody would prefer a person with any level of knowledge in nursing then people with no knowledge at all.

    Combat medics are most expendable in the line of fire - they know only advanced first aid and with a bit of experience and luck - they can give advanced treatment properly.
    Usually the ones with the least qualifications and skills required are sent out frontline.

    Combat Medic Specialists (frontline mostly health professionals with actual qualifications) - they supervise the combat medics and they have to risk the line of fire to supervise but I doubt that will happen.

    Army nursing officers are less expendable - they know advanced first aid and they know how to anticipate things as they are happening especially when the combat medics are bringing in the wounded they treated. i.e. which organ the bullet hits , triaging, infection control etc . They also usually assist the army surgeons in operating theatres.

    Army medical officers are the least expendable - they know the most.

    So that is why combat medics go into the secondline together with the frontline troops, not SAF nursing officers or Medical Officers - they stay at the back with those mobile medical treatment units.
