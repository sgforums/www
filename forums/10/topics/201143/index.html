    "Mr HDB save Sg fr Communists" - ironically, during the Japanese Occupation of Singapore, Lim Kim San was arrested and tortured by the Japanese Kempetai - secret police - on suspicion of being pro-communist and pro-British.

    Is it not even more ironical that during the same period of Japanese Occupation, MM LKY was working for the Japanese Military as an interpreter, and President S. R. Nathan was said to be working as a secret agent for the Kempetai ?

    Wars certainly leave very untidy endings.

    Lim Kim San's understanding of the local business and contracting process, allowed him to resolve Singapore's housing problems for the mass of middle-low income Singaporeans.

    During his administration of HDB, the design team was able to create designs that resulted in HDB flats being built with such efficiency that it was reputed to complete one HDB unit every 45 minutes.

    Those flat were sold at the low costs to Singaporeans that it made HDB famous, and allowed many young Singaporeans - during the early 1960s and 1970s - were able to complete their housing loans in about ten years and still have sufficient savings in the CPF for retirement.

    The success of Lim Kim San's housing policy, allowed many Singaporeans to own low cost HDB flats to be sold later at three to four times higher then the original purchase price - and about a one-third lower to the price of Privately built flats called "condominiums".

    This allowed many Singaporeans to make windfall profit from the sale of HDB flats, and helped many Singaporeans to upgrade to privately built flats or terrace house in the 1970s and early 198s.

    Unfortunately, Lim Kim San could not stop the "politically motivated agenda" of taking a portion of the wealth made by Singaporeans from such HDB re-sale, and the wealth in the CPF accounts that they possessed during the late 1970s and through 1980s which allowed them to upgrade their homes.

    During this period, the prices of HDB flats began to make gradual increases, while the unit size began to shrink in floor area - and with price increases continue climbing despite land being acquired with compensation at a fraction of market valuation, building material and construction costs based on the lowest competitive bids.

    We have a situation today that younger Singaporeans could not afford to own an HDB Flat until they have a career that pays them a minimum sum of S$2,000 a month, and having saved enough for the minimum deposit for a housing loan, and being in a housing debt for the next twenty years of his working life.

    With the present short-term employment contract promoted by the Government and the NTUC, how will the mortgage be served in between the lull period of career change ?

    Like the other founding fathers of Singapore, Lim Kim San was also reputed to be "a quiet person distinterested in politics", and when accepting his role to contribute to Singapore and Singaporeans - he entered into Government service with full commitment to the responsibilities given to him - with little time for POLITICS.

    Will the political landscape for Singapore be different - if those other Founding Fathers - all known to be quiet and disinterested in politics - have been more politically active in the Party Politics, instead of leaving the political manouvres and fixxing to LKY ?
