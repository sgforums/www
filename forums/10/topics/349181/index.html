    I certainly don't praise their policies.

    But I still respect their ministers. Just as I respect Mr Chiam See Tong and Mr Low Thia Khiang for their work towards making Singapore a better country.

    It's foolish and narrow-minded to throw yourself behind only one political faction. I supported the PAP's policies towards inviting foreigners, even though I'd think that it could have been better regulated. I was repulsed by the PAP 'show of solidarity' when multiple PAP MPs took on Mr Low when he questioned the effectiveness of the rescue package.

    Sure, I have to admit that PAP's governance has been pretty authoritarian for quite a while. But this is kind of like Morton's Fork: I'd vote for the lesser of two evils. Because right now, I don't have much faith in the Opposition's ability to govern Singapore effectively. And personally, I feel that the Opposition, if they manage to win the majority vote in the next election, would still end up imitating much of PAP's current policies.

    I don't actually care whether it's a PAP government or a WP government or a SDP government. As long as their policies make sense, I'm all for them.

    So you could say that right now, I'm a sincere supporter of the PAP. Not because I agree with the need for multi-million-dollar salaries for ministers or the way they conduct their diplomatic relations with other countries. But because at the present, the PAP's still the best party for Singapore.
