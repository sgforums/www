    Yes..trying living in UK.

    Just passed by the job centre yesterday. Looking for a new graduate to work as office administrator...salary 10-12k GBP per annum. That translates into less than S$3000 a month (and no 13-month bonus in UK). From that salary, you have to pay income tax, council tax and national insurance, which will take up at least 20% or more of your salary, depending on where you live. Every time you buy something, the VAT (equivalent of GST) is 17.5%.

    Each time you take the bus, the local fare starts at 1.70 GBP (about S$5) one-way. Every time you take the MRT in London, the fare is 3GBP or S$8.80. Don't even ask about taxi, you cannot afford it.

    What about housing? If you rent a studio flat with one bedroom in London, you can expect to pay 800-900 GBP (S$2,300-2,600).

    How do you live on such a salary? People talk about high costs of living in Singapore...come to London and you will thank your lucky stars you are in Singapore.
