    I believe that the question here is rather not how much we have to learn, but how similar we are to Taiwan. Perhaps we can draw parallels in terms of our political development. The Kuomintang lifted martial law and Lee Teng Hui ushered in a new era of democratic reforms, finally resulting in the recent passing over of power to the DPP. A gradual release in an authoritarian regime can be observed, and perhaps the government in Singapore is trying to enforce similar changes, put in such a fashion that it is gentle and does not have an overly drastic effect in the state of things at present. The floodgates of change are opening, what with Education reforms, liberalisation of the Telecoms Market, the inevitable liberalisation of the arts scene and media, and the changing political climate. Even the erection of the Speakers Corner, which some may regard as a pointless pursuit, but which i feel is nevertheless a truly symbolic gesture as to the opening up of Singapore. Perhaps we will witness a paradigm shift in the political climate of Singapore, like what Taiwan has experienced.
