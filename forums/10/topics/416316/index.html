    By Jonathan Peeris, Channel NewsAsia | Posted: 17 August 2009 1511 hrs

     
     
    Photos 1 of 1

       
     

     

     

    SINGAPORE: Singapore Exchange's mainboard-listed BBR Holdings has secured a S$104.2-million contract from the Housing & Development Board (HDB) to build seven blocks of flats in Yishun Neighbourhood 4.

    This is the company's first HDB contract and work is expected to begin next month.

    The project will be carried out in 10 phases and is expected to be completed by 2012.

    The work comprises 864 homes, a Child Care Centre, a roof garden, communal amenities and site works, as well as contingency works.

    With this latest win, BBR said on Monday its order book for civil engineering and building projects stands at S$396 million, with projects predominantly spread out in Singapore and Malaysia.
