    Subject: KEY ISSUE IN RECENT OUTBREAK OF DENGUE

    Date: Jul 1 3007

    TO: The Editor, TODAY,

    Dear Sir,

    The recent outbreak of dengue could have been prevented in view of the
    previous outbreak which has occurred only about a year ago.

    The key issue raised by Minister in charge is that people have been
    spreading mosquito by their inconsiderate habits of various kind. But is
    this assumption proven. No doubt there may be cases of negligence among the
    people but was the latest outbreak caused by such habits?

    I feel that the key issue here is how the ministries and the government
    should coordinate and take serious preemptive measures to deal with a
    problem of such nature.

    It is pertinent perhaps at this stage of evolvement to a more inclusive and
    caring society that various ministries and the government as a whole should
    tackle issues and problems holistically. In the past, it would appear that
    there has been manoerving of issues and problems with legalistic wrangling
    of laws and regulations on the assumptions or presumptions that someone is
    at fault and to pass certain duties and responsibilities to citizens.

    Over the years, we have already seen too many such laws and regulations
    passed on presumptions of such nature with penalties imposed on citizens for
    its own negligence and inefficiencies from HDB's own poorly designed
    windows, lack of structural checking on buildings to illegal immigration
    where old lady landlords were presumed guilty of habouring illegal to
    mosquito breeding attributable to insufficient preemptive measures by NEA.

    It is my humble view that government should take on the larger role of
    looking into all problems holistically and solve them by preemptive measures
    instead of legalistically passing more laws and regulations to presume
    responsibilities by citizens to solve problems.
