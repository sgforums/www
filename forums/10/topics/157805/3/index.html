    i think that in this case the opposition will just have to face up to the harsh reality that if they cannot field enough capable candidates to counter the incumbent, then they are simply not as competent.

    If the opposition were to be competent, and they can prove it, there would not be a shortage of talents joining their ranks. So, the number of candidates the opposition is allowed to field in any constituency should not be an issue. I for one wouldnt mind either. but the fact is that the people of singapore do not see the need to come up with an opposition to the PAP. This could be due to:

    1) Singaporeans' political apathy.

    2) Singaporeans are contented with the situation as it is.

    Im not going to elaborate on the 1st, its quite obvious.

    Given the current stability in Singapore, there is no serious need for change. By "serious need for change", i mean "if we dont change the govt, we are going to suffer in endless poverty with no hope in sight for several generations to come". Obviously Singaporeans today have much to lose if they were to have a sudden change of government. The newcomers lack experience and might not be able to maintain Singapore's current economy which, despite stagnating in recent years, is not at rock bottom.

    People tend to want to change whole governments only when they feel that they have nothing to lose. The people of Russia turned to communism because the Tzar failed them terribly n they were desperate.

    ok, u might ask why then arent the british, americans etc not sitting back but constantly trying to change their govts, since their nations are alot stronger economically than singapore. The important difference here is that Singaporeans have a different attitude. We tend to be more practical, more money-minded and less concerned about politics.

    In short, Singaporeans resist change, and to them there is not enough reason for change yet.

    I guess we could also blame it on the bad luck of the opposition, because they are trying to advocate change in a nation which resists it. Perhaps they should just continue to build up grassroots support, avoid getting sued and wait for the opportunity to present itself.
