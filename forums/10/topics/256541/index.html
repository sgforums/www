    27 May 07, 08:54
    MOH ready to reveal details on revamped ElderShield within weeks
    By May Wong, Channel NewsAsia | Posted: 26 May 2007 1819 hrs

    SINGAPORE: Health Minister Khaw Boon Wan has said he will reveal details of the revamped ElderShield before the end of next month, while hinting that a total of three insurers may offer the scheme.

    Currently, only two companies provide ElderShield, a severe disability insurance scheme to help Singaporeans pay for long-term care especially during old age.

    The reformed scheme is set to be implemented end-September.

    Mr Khaw was speaking at a dialogue session with some 300 grassroots leaders to get public feedback on the proposed ElderShield changes.

    One request that often came up during Saturday's session was to shorten the 90-day deferment.

    Currently, a policy holder has to wait 90 days after being certified as disabled before he could receive the ElderShield payout.

    This is to minimise fraud.

    Mr Khaw said: "We can take a look to see if 90 days are overly long. But, I think, in practice the insurers have been flexible case by case. They do approve cases well within 90 days, shorter than 90 days."

    One proposed change to ElderShield is to increase the monthly payout for policy-holders from $300 to $400 over five years.

    Other proposed changes are extending the payout period to six years and allowing people to buy add-on schemes for more coverage.

    As of end last year, about 750,000 people have ElderShield policies offered by NTUC Income and Great Eastern Life.

    The two existing insurers as well as Aviva have thrown in their bids to be providers.

    Mr Khaw said: "There are some details to be tidied up but I think within a few weeks, I should be able to settle the exact form of what the reform will look like and also to announce the result of the tender. We have two insurers at the moment for ElderShield and I think a third insurer is likely to crop up with this reform. Three insurers are not bad."

    The Minister also said that after reforming the ElderShield, he wants to work with insurance companies to make their contracts easier to understand.

    Insurance contracts, he said, should be written in simple languages so that people will know what they are buying and the kind of benefits they will get.

    Mr Khaw said he would work on this in the second half of this year. - CNA/ir
