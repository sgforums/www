Common Fallacy About GST

  • maurizio13
    18 Nov 06, 12:11
    Most people have this misconception that so long as this business doesn't charge GST, they won't be paying GST for their purchases.

    This thought is WRONG.

    A GST registered business is able to charge GST (Output Tax) for their Sales and get refund GST (Input Tax) for what they paid for.

    A Non-GST registered business is NOT able to charge GST and NOT able to get refund GST.

    Example 1:

    In this example all are GST registered business except the Consumer.

    [M]anufacturer ---> [W]holesaler ---> [R]etailer ---> [C]onsumer

    Sequence of events: Manufacturer sells to Wholesaler sells to Retailer, then Consumer. (Say they are in the furniture business)

    -------Sales----Input Tax---Output Tax

    [M]---$100----$0------------$5

    [W]---$200----$5-----------$10

    [R]----$300----$10---------$15

    [C]----$315

    All the GST charged refundable, except the final GST paid by consumer, being
    $15.

    Example 2:

    All scenario the same except that [R]etailer is NOT GST registered business.
    Therefore [R]etailer can't charge (Output Tax) GST or get refunds (Input Tax) for GST paid.

    -------Sales----Input Tax----Output Tax

    [M]---$100-----$0------------$5

    [W]---$200-----$5-----------$10

    [R]----$300-----$10----------$0

    [C]----$310 and above

    The Retailer ends up paying $10 of GST but is not able to get a refund for it.
    So ultimately he will sell his goods above $310. But with all the other GST
    (utilities, petrol, rental, advertising, etc) he has to pay to bring the final
    product to you, the final cost you have to pay for GST is definitely way
    above $10.

    EVEN IF YOU BUY FROM NON-GST REGISTERED BUSINESS, YOU WILL STILL BE PAYING GST, BUT IT IS HIDDEN IN THE COST WHICH DOESN'T SHOW UP IN YOUR RECEIPT.
