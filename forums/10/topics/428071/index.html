    http://satayclub.net/2011/04/mah-bow-tan-rejects-debate-over-hdb-prices/

     

    National Development Minister Mah Bow Tan has rejected opposition calls for a debate over the PAP’s public housing policy, insisting that the ruling party has ‘nothing to hide’ and that its position is ‘well known’.

     

    Mr Mah has come under intense fire from the opposition regarding the spiralling cost of HDB flats. The Workers’ Party has called for housing prices to be pegged to to the median rate of income, claiming that housing has become unaffordable because the HDB instead pegs the prices of flats to those in the open market. Mr Mah called the WP proposals ‘irresponsible’.

     

    Separately, National Solidarity Party leader Goh Meng Seng – who will be contesting the coming general election in Mr Mah’s Tampines GRC – has challenged Mr Mah to a public debate on the issue.

     

    Mr Mah rejected the suggestion, saying that there was ‘no need for a debate’. He insists that public housing is already affordable, and that the HDB is operating at a loss. He says there is no way for the prices of HDB flats to be brought down any further without implementing cuts in other areas, such as education and defence.

     

    WP leader Low Thia Khiang, who is MP for Hougang, replied that the only reason why the HDB was operating at a loss was because it buys its land from the Singapore Land Authority, a government statutory board. Land accounts for a significant proportion of the costs involved in building HDB flats, resulting in a situation that Mr Low called “taking money from the left pocket and putting it in the right pocket”. He has also challenged Mr Mah to reveal the exact cost of the land it has purchased from the SLA, something which Mr Mah and the HDB have not been willing to do.
