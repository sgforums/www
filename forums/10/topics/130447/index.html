  • robertteh

    Legalistic government is one overrelying on legal processes to maintain legal control and win like in legal cases. It's basic philosophy is to win at all costs legally like in any court case against all opponents and defend against any queries or complaints of any kinds.

    Problem solving is not its primary objective. Legalistic government is aimed at winning all cases, debates, arguments legally for the government's positions or policies, sustain controls and perpetuate in the hold of power.

    Such legalistic government will not concern itself with alternative views and solutions as its make-up or system is designed to win on legality and not lose any case.

    Such legally oriented system aimed at winning legally to stay in power, maintain controls of populations, perpetuation of high costs and building of surpluses to benefit limited legally-oriented objectives.

    Such legalistic government will not be inclined to considerations of non-legal matters like moral, social or sin issues.

    So people cannot expect legalistic government to admit mistakes as it is against their primary objective to lose any case or disputes. Whatever the outcome, legalistic government will justify its doings one way or another instead of admitting mistakes. Even if any mistakes is admitted it will be done very casually or lightly and not in court or legally.

    The deadliest sins can be legally justified as correct eg. legally self-approved highest pay (in the name of preventing corruptions) when the final outcome is the same - those who are paid high are still as corrupted except in different ways or otherwise justified.
