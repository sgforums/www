    Chinese sovereignty over Taiwan dates back to 1661. Then Cheng Chen Kung, leader of Ming rearguard, captured Taiwan with the ambitions to use it as a base to overthrow the Ching Dynasty. Taiwan was designated as a prefecture within the mainland province of Fujian in 1683. For 212 years after that year Taiwan formed part of the Chinese empire ruled from Beijing. Chinese sovereignty over Taiwan then was recognised by America, Russia, Britain, France and Japan in various treaties such as Treaties of Tientsin, Treaty of Shimonoseki. In the 1943 Cairo Declaration, UK and US acknowledged that Manchuria, Formosa and the Pescadore Islands had been an integral part of China in 1895.

    The inaction of the PRC over Taiwan after the KMT retreated there in 1949, however doesn't vitiate it's claim to sovereignty. And Taiwan's claim of independence is therefore untenable because of the primacy of sovereignty under international law.
