    Workers' Party unveils manifesto, plans to contest 3 single wards & 4 GRCs
    By Farah Abdul Rahim, Channel NewsAsia

    The Workers' Party has unveiled its party manifesto and in the process also said it is going to contest in three single wards and four Group Representative Constituencies in the next General Election.

    It is the first opposition party to release its manifesto ahead of the next General Election due by the middle of next year.

    The party document details its policies from healthcare to labour, and contains proposals like unemployment insurance for those out of a job.

    It also calls for the office of president to be reverted to its former ceremonial position and for one central agency to provide public transport for all.

    The manifesto has not been updated since 1994.

    Workers' Party Secretary-General, Low Thia Khiang, said: "Time for the renewal, that's the main reason and certainly the elections, that's another reason. We look at the issues seriously, explore policy options on many issues, state our positions and give our policy statements on certain government issues."

    The Workers' Party said that in the next election, it is targeting the single wards of Hougang, Nee Soon East and Joo Chiat as well as 4 GRCs - Sembawang, East Coast, Ang Mo Kio and Aljunied.

    Foreign Minister George Yeo, who is leading the PAP team in Aljunied, says he welcomes the challenge.

    He said: "They wanted to compete in Aljunied the last time, although there were some problems with forms. I expect them to come back. They have been canvassing the ground sporadically. I think they will come back and I am not surprised."

    The Workers' Party said it is aware the Democratic Progressive Party (DPP) also has its eyes set on East Coast GRC.

    Mr Low said he intends to talk to the DPP to prevent a three-cornered fight. - CNA/ir

    Dear oxford mushroom,

    You remind me about the reporter from New Paper who wrote quite sarcastically on this perspective but he did not raise his doubts or questions during the press conference.

    First of all, we are not in the position to have all the financial data of the govt to do a simulation on the impact on the proposal we have made. However, I could assure you that the Manifesto when looks in totality, is sustainable.

    There are "subtle" suggestions on cutting spending in Defence and be prudent on this particularly, not to initiate arm race in this region. Defence spending alone in Singapore's budget takes up about 20% or more. The second biggest spending I think is education.

    For Healthcare portion, the newspaper only highlighted "subsidies" but did not mention or emphasize about the medical insurance or hospital insurance that we proposed to help finance the healthcare cost. On top of that, we actually suggested that private hospitals should be included in the scheme as well, so that citizens could use their Medisave on private healthcare providers.

    As for the transport cost section, New Paper reporter did not mention the most important part of our proposal: Public Transport provider should not be profit orientated but they should cover their cost and depreciation cost. Thus we are not asking the govt to subsidize the whole operation of the public transport system but merely on the initial set up cost of infrastructure and equipment. In fact we have suggested that transport companies should be allowed to utilize the land they own to generate rental income to cross subsidize operation costs.

    I guess you will need to read the whole Manifesto to get the whole idea. We are responsible and prudent enough to put up ideas that are not about all spending and tax cuts. GST, whether you like it or not, is a regressive taxation on the population; basically it means that it put a heavier burden on the lower income group. To take away GST from daily necessity would dampen such tendency. We are prudent enough not to suggest the total abolishment of the GST but takes balance approach in this issue.

    Thus I think you will have to get a copy of our Manifesto, read it in totality before you can come to the conclusion whether we are irresponsible in our approach.

    Goh Meng Seng
