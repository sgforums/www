    Any decision that affects a change in direction that affects any Singapore's 'Structural Policies' will be made from the PM's Office, who will make its decisions to every issue that is presented on a plate of Issues of the Day for decisions to be made.

    From the details of the main issues that are thoroughly researched by the Employed Talents, the Prime Minister has to develop his 'PERSONAL' understanding of the issues presented, based on a 'PERSONAL DATA BANK of EXPERIENCE' from which decisions are evaluated and made.

    Where 'PERSONAL EXPERIENCE' is lacking, more detailed studies will have to be developed, and until the 'COMFORT LEVEL' is reached for the issues that are to be decided.

    With technologies and opportunities being available in fleeting periods of the 'competitive cycles' that are open for grabs by every country that is competing in technological supremacy and progress, can Singapore continue to depend on such highly centralised, Top-Down approach of management ?

    With Government Linked Companies competing with Private Enterprises for growth, and justifying its own existence by performance, there have been many instances where the muscle of the GLCs will weigh down on the SMEs - to take on major stakes in new technologies.

    Private enterprises have often been stifled by the lack of 'entrepreneurial venture capital' , as Singapore Banks are conservative, and will not venture into this area when their loans are not insured by any Government fundings or tax write-off programs.

    Despite the Government's effort to nurture local enterprise, more often then not, it is targetted at sectors in which the GLCs can have a stake - ending up in a situation where the 'money flow is returned to the original pockets' .

    'Sun Power' has been tried in Singapore, and is used to power the night lightings at the bus stops all over Singapore.

    The problem with 'Sun Power' is with the harvesting and storage of the energy.

    Large amount of open space is needed to have the solar cells and solar panels to absorb the sunlight and convert it into usable energy, which need to be stored.

    While some have claimed that all the roof tops of HDB flats can be utilised for the installation of Solar Cells and Solar Panels, a solution will have to be found on the efficient storage of the solar energy for usage by 'the many' and over a 'long period of time'.

    Not only has this 'Little Red Dot' a big problem with land space, it also has a big problem with territorial waters as well.

    'Hydro-Power' will have to depend on some expanse of sea space to be able to tap the powers of the sea current in order to convert its energy for use on land. Again, the problem also is with the storage of converted energy for subsequent use.

    Ultimately, Private Enterprise will need to have easy access to research and development funds from Private or Public Venture Capital, which will have to be insured by Government approved budget plans.

    This approach will ultimately help to develop new technologies for Singapore to make a mark in this highly competitive and rewarding field.
