    Originally posted by want to know:
    What must the ITE guy do to get into uni.
    About three to four months ago, our Straits Times featured some very successful persons from ITE, who went up the ladder of academia and achieved even a Master's Degree in Mechanical Engineering.

    If you are really good and perform consistently in ITE, graduating from ITE with good grades, I believe there is opening for you at the Polytechnics in Singapore.

    From the local Polytechnics, your results will bring you onward to one of the two Universities in Singapore, or alternatively you can study overseas.

    Many overseas Universities give high regard to the Polytechnic training in Singapore, and will shorten their Degree course by a year (depending on your choice of study).

    Don't lose heart if you have landed in ITE; it probably make life easier for you to cope.

    Albert Einstein - the father of atomic science - was a late starter, and probably would have gone to ITE had it existed during his time.
