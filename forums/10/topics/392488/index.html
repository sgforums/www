ST fails to mention that author of its article on HDB flats

  • Rock^Star
    3 Mar 10, 08:54

    Written by Our Correspondent

    Last Saturday, the Straits Times published a highly debatable commentary written by two NUS professors Tu Yong and Yu Shi Ming on HDB flats still being affordable in Singapore:

    The aim of the article is to show that HDB flats are still affordable in Singapore by making comparisons to housing prices in Hong Kong, London and Sydney.

    One of TR columnists Ng Kok Lim had written an article rebutting the points mentioned in it to reach the conclusion that there is no empirical evidence to suggest that public housing is affordable in Singapore by any standard. (read article here)

    Though Straits Times included a footnote at the bottom of the article that both authors are from the Department of Real Estate, National University of Singapore, it omitted the fact that Professor Yu Shi Ming sits on the board of HDB as well. (source: HDB Infoweb)

    Surely readers ought to know this important fact and be allowed to assess the merits of the article independently on their own after taking Professor Yu’s relationship with HDB into consideration?

    Is this another honest mistake or a deliberate omission on the part of the Straits Times? Either way, it does not reflect well on the credibility and professionalism of the paper.

    Minister for Communications, Information and Arts Rear-Admiral Lui Tuck Yew said not too long ago that the mainstream media remains a “trusted” source of news in Singapore.

    We leave it to readers to judge for themselves if they can really trust the Singapore media.

    Source: http://www.temasekreview.com/2010/03/03/st-fails-to-mention-that-author-of-its-article-on-hdb-flats-being-affordable-sits-on-hdb-board/
