    Dear yuu_kajima,

    I am a strong believer of real peace--not the peace all governments promote by setting up Armies to threaten each other. THAT is merely a "keep at bay by means of violence" method. I do not believe in it.

    I think the 'real peace' that you believe in only exists in an ideal world where people care about one another and only use things that belong to them. However, the world that we live in is not ideal. People do steal from one another, people do kill their fellow human beings and in a bigger scale, countries do attack one another (remember Iraq's invation to Kuwait).

    Governments do not like to go to war (in general) because it simply costs too much, in terms of lives, money, properties, time and lots of other things. However, that doesn't mean that they do not need armed forces to defend their countries. After all, you have to defend what is yours, right? How can you do that without an armed forces and a well-trained one at that? Are you going to leave the country at the mercy of other countries?

    Furthermore, armed forces do not only serve to defend the country from external attack. They also defend the country from internal attack. What I mean by internal attack is incidents within the country itself that threaten the stability and the security of the nation, e.g. riots, strikes.

    Therefore, armed forces are essential in the existence and well-being of a country, not to mention the peaceful lives of the people who live in the country.
