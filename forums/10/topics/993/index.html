    It's a scary thing, to br sure. Believe me, I was there and it wasn't even WORTH debating if you were there too. Every day, I woke up, asking myself the same question: Why the hell am I still alive? What am I doing here? WHY? WHY? WHYWHYWHY? I am a strong believer of real peace--not the peace all governments promote by setting up Armies to threaten each other. THAT is merely a "keep at bay by means of violence" method. I do not believe in it. I don't have to go into the topic of the utilisation of time since, although we all know that politics leave no questions for the free mind, we can sure use the 2-2and a half years for self-enrichment, working etc.. The one thing I can't understand is that--why do we have to be treated like that? We are living, breathing, thinking individuals like all of the officers and sergeants. Why must we be at their mercy, at their beck and call, and be punished for the slightest things? Why camn't we go through a more Humane training in the course of BMT? The officers are almost allways sadistic. They see pain in our eyes and they can actually smile and torture us relentlessly. Imagine running down 4-storeys with 50+ sweating, flustered men in 20seconds. Undo-able? Precisely. They use that as an excuse to extend their authority and power. The sight of the sadistic joy and the childish gleam of the joy of abusing power I see in their eyes sickens me to the core. I don't relish serving NS. Indeed, I think most don't. But the sgts. amd officers can opt to vent their anger on us. We, unlike them, are like playthings. When you go through the process, your mind stops. It stops to think, because it knows that there is no amswer to the problem. I know. I spent 2 months forgetting the nightmare and another 1 month getting my brains to work. Even today I still get nightmares of the ordeal. No one can TELL you the process and expect anyone to understand. But at least, respect us as humans, PLEASE!
