    It's rather interesting to read all the posting in this topic. Several people are rather aggressive while some are rather sincere in expressing their views. I have to say that none is totally wrong, but nobody is completely right either.

    National Service (NS) is a very important aspect of national defence. Here, we call it Total Defence (TD) which consists of five equally important realms. These five realms constitutes activities collectively helping the nation to progress and propel beyond its present state. None of them should be seen as less important than any other. Nation building is a never-ending process. Rome was not built in a day. People need to be mobilised to render their service to build a better society.

    NS is part of military defence. It is statutory that all Singaporeans have a duty to protect the nation. All physically fit persons are legally binded to serve NS.

    Because of our small population, each individual is an important asset to the country. Most people need to take up multiple roles in our society.

    First, we have to go through NS, which is followed by reservist to the age of 50 and 40, for officers and other ranks respectively. Here, we see men coming together for a common objective: to protect our land, our home.

    Second, the reservists, which form the main body of our armed forces, are also professionals and workers, each armed with knowledge and skills, and making contributions to the society in another way, economic defence. These people, who can be seen packing buses and MRT trains every morning and evening, work for the development of the nation. Their diligent work contributes to our strong economic growth. Without them, there is no talk for a national fiber/coaxial network providing cable television and making singapore an intelligent island. There is no talk for a second terminal at Changi Airport, let alone a third. There won't be even talk for SAF scholarships and fast promotions of SAF regulars which usually accompany large pay increments.

    Third, many individuals are also contributing, on their own accord, through social services. Volunteers in hospitals, old folk's homes, and even neighbourhood police posts. Social defence, is what we call it.

    Fourth, there are people doing charity and welfare work. Some may also be parting some of their income for charity. These people hope to bring help and hope to the less fortunate people in our society.

    The list is nevertheless not exhaustive. What we need to understand here is that at different time, we play different roles. In Singapore, everyone has enough burden to take care of himself, his family, the society and nation. We, as fellow citizens, have responsibilities towards ourselves and the society. We need mutual SUPPORT of each other, in all aspects of TD. To acheive it, we must have mutual RESPECT for each other.

    In peace,
    Frankie.
