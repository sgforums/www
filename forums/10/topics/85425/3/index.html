    Somewhere along the lline, our system of government got changed.

    Government's principal dutes and responsibilities include (a) creating jobs, (b) housing, (c) education, (d) security and (e) medical needs etc as contained in parties' manifestos and election campaigning speeches.

    Due to limitations caused by our own unique one-party system, we must have forgotten these prestine duties and responsibilities to the people. Over time, our economy is now run so as to be be efficient, be rich, build reserves etc resulting in high costs.

    There is no careful examination of how government charges for lands or services. The argument that land costs is chargeable has to be re-examined in this light. Lands were acquired by government cheaply or at little costs under Land Acquisition Act. It does not need to amortize lands like the private landlords. Charge the highest rates for commercial usage by all means. But where the people or tax payers are concerned lands have been paid for using tax payers' monies. Charging land rentals for hospitals, utilities and other essential services needs re-thinking to avoid a double-charge somewhere.

    If Singapore is to become competitive again, the first thing to do now is to debate openly on such major policy decisions. Right or wrong, such policies must be agreed upon before anything else will work.

    If government wants to be competitive and efficient, it is necessary to resolve all such issues with people before levying near-market (called subsidised) prices for housing and essential services. More ideas of others are necessary to make for change.

    1. When HDB flats price is not set based on cost but on a household

    "HDB flats are priced below their respective market values, to

    of Singaporeans to afford at least a 4-room flat. Those with higher
    income can consider buying the larger HDB flats."

    of Singaporeans to afford at least a 4-room flat"? Why not "80% of
    Singaporeans to afford at least a 5-room flat"?

    Why the government choose an affordability based pricing scheme
    instead of a cost based pricing schem? What is the rationale behind
    the decision?

    2. Does the government realize that this affordability based scheme
    is taking more money than necessary for cost recovery from the
    people's savings that they need for children education, medical
    care, and retirement?

    3. Does the government realize that this affordability based scheme
    turn previously well affordable HDB flats into back breaking
    financial burden for those hard hit by the economic restructuring
    and could not find jobs with the matching pay?
