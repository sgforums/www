    Among the hottest topic in Singapore nowadays is the rising income gap in Singapore.

    Our Gini coefficient is one of the highest in the world.

    Earlier this year, Finance Minister rebutted to Sylvia Lim's concerns about the high income gap in Singapore in Parliament by saying, "That's a good question but it can't be helped. Do you want to increase jobs first to improve the economy?"

    I'm pushing for an egalitarian society between social classes as a high income or social gap will lead to disunity in our society. The rich and elites will become too powerful so that they can hire and fire employees at their own discretion, causing high turnoverrates, therefore decreasing productivity.

    So offer your views on how we can decrease the income gap in Singapore?

    Do the Thaksin way by imposing a debt moratorium on the lower income group in Singapore? This was what he did to the rural areas of Thailand as he encouraged rural enterprises to spring up at better advantages. He improved GDP, yet also improved Gini coefficient. Praise to him!
