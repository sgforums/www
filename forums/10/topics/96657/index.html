    SIA flight diverted to UK airport after bomb threat

    MANCHESTER -- A Singapore Airlines passenger jet was diverted to Manchester Airport in northern England on Monday after a bomb threat, police said, adding that it may have been a hoax.

    The Frankfurt to New York flight landed safely at 11 am (1000 GMT) without a military escort, Greater Manchester Police said.

    Advertisement

    The pilot of Singapore Airlines flight SQ26 declared a 'full emergency' mid-flight, a spokesman for Manchester Airport said. The Boeing 747-400, bound for New York's JFK airport, was carrying 293 passengers and 19 crew.

    'Early indications are that it could be a hoax but we cannot confirm that one way or another until full checks are carried out,' a spokesman for Greater Manchester Police said.

    'We got a security warning from the German security authorities,' said Peter Menkel, an official with Singapore Airlines in Frankfurt. He said he did not have details, but added that 'it had nothing to do with a passenger on board - it was external.'

    In Germany, federal police spokesman Klaus Ludwig said a bomb threat against the flight was made in a call to police in the south-western German city of Tuebingen.

    It was the fourth commercial flight diverted as a security precaution in Europe in the last eight days.

    One was diverted to Stansted airport north of London, while another was diverted to Shannon in western Ireland and a third to Amsterdam's Schiphol airport in the Netherlands.

    All three flights resumed their journeys after searches concluded there was no threat to security. -- AP

    KUALA LUMPUR - Malaysian police are investigating a third case in 12 months of suspected sabotage involving a Malaysia Airlines (MAS) plane after wires were severed in an Airbus A330 aircraft.

    Police said yesterday that the plane was grounded at Kuala Lumpur's international airport on Tuesday after ground crew found some electrical wires inside the aircraft had been cut. The plane was being prepared for a flight to Bangkok.

    Assistant Commissioner Abdul Rasman Hussin said the police were not treating the sabotage as terrorism but suspected someone inside MAS.

    'No, there's no connection with terrorism,' said the deputy chief of the Criminal Investigation Department.

    'We are trying to get evidence but it is difficult. It is not a security breach. We suspect an inside job and we're trying to narrow down (a list of suspects).'

    He added that the police were combing through lists of all MAS staff with access to aircraft.

    MAS said it had stepped up security again after the latest incident but declined to comment further.

    AC Abdul Rasman declined to give details on the severed wiring.

    The Star newspaper said yesterday the cut wires were under an instrument panel below the flight captain's seat. The flight systems had indicated a malfunction.

    Two MAS planes were grounded last year after wires were found to have been tampered with - an Airbus bound for Perth, Australia, in October and a Boeing jet bound for Bombay, India, in November, Malaysian media reported.

    Police detained and questioned 14 MAS maintenance employees after the first incident late last year.

    But police said yesterday that they had yet to identify a suspect. -- Reuters, AP, The Star/Asia News Network
