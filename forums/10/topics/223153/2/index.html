    I managed to find the cost of producing 1 Kwh of electricity under inefficient means, through the use of mobile power generators using diesel.

    Diesel is a cheap component from distillating a barrel of oil. 44 gallons are produced from 1 barrel of oil, of which 9.2 gallons are diesel. We will assume that diesel and gasonline & jet fuel are priced the same and the cost of 1 barrel of oil is USD 60

    http://www.energyquest.ca.gov/story/chapter08.html

    Cost per gallon of diesel:
    (USD 60 / 44 gallons) = USD 1.36/gallon of diesel (SGD 2.12 / gallon of diesel)

    Diesel engine-generator sets operated at their best efficiency point can produce between 3 and 4 kilowatthours of electrical energy for each litre of diesel fuel consumed, with lower efficiency at part load.

    1 US gallon = 3.785 litres

    Therefore SGD 2.12 / gallon of diesel is equivalent to 3.785 litres.

    We take the average of "3 and 4 kilowatthours of electrical energy for each litre of diesel fuel consumed", we get 3.5 Kwh / litre.

    For the SGD 2.12 you pay for 3.785 litres of diesel, it will produce:

    3.785 litres X 3.5 Kwh/litre = 13.25 Kwh

    Therefore 1 Kwh of electricity generation based on inefficient method is:

    SGD 2.12 / 13.25 = 16 cents/Kwh

    Under very inefficient means of electricity generation, we can still achieve 16 cents/Kwh. What does this tell us about the 21.64 cents / Kwh Singapore Power charges Singapore citizens?
