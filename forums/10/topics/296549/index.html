    Malaysians 'better than S'poreans in Chinese'

    The Straits Times

    A language expert from China says the standard of Chinese used in Malaysia is higher than in Singapore.

    Mr Yang Hong Qing, who heads the school of languages and literature in Peng Cheng University in Xuzhou, said Chinese Malaysians are better at their mother tongue than their Singaporean counterparts because they are emotionally and intellectually more enthusiastic about the language.

    Moreover, Singaporeans seldom use Chinese, he said, according to the Malaysian Chinese daily, Sin Chew Jit Poh.

    As such, raising language standards in Singapore cannot not be achieved overnight.

    Mr Yang said he also believed that the level of the Chinese language in Malaysia is high because it gives Chinese Malaysians a sense of identity.

    Most Chinese Malaysians, including the young, take pride in learning the language, he said.

    By contrast, young people in Singapore do not accord it similar importance and resort to using English.

    This, he added, is a reason Chinese-language standards are slipping in the Republic.

    He noted that despite government efforts to reverse the situation through campaigns and scholarships, there has been no change in Chinese-language standards in Singapore.

    Although Malaysia's standards cannot match those in China, where Chinese is the mother tongue, they are still good, he noted.

    He also pointed to the wide use of the Chinese language in the Chinese business community and the proliferation of Chinese-language newspapers as examples of the vitality of the language in Malaysia.
