    in the case of our society, i think 2% is just the tip of the ice berg. but even as we deem those 2% as 'hopeless' with or without the casino, it just makes it so much more convenient for them to find a place to lose money as opposed to travelling up north.

    more importantly, what would be the effect on the next 20% to 50%? this group of people who wouldn't otherwise take the trouble to travel to gamble might just end up gambling once you take away the travelling hassle.

    $100 might make people think twice before trying their luck but once they've entered, perhaps won once or twice and experienced the thrill, you can be sure they'd be back for more. and that $100 barrier soon becomes in the eyes of a gambler in the making, $50, then $25 ... and when he loses, all the more he'd want to win back ... human psychology ...

    bottomline is no one can guarantee that $100 is good enough deterrence and putting the blame on the gambler is only half right (and half wrong) especially when that blame comes from the very person setting up the gambling table.
