    The integration of BUSINESS, ACCOUNTING and COMPUTERS
    -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    What is the nature of a business?
    ANS:
    An organisation that provides a service or product to
    the client and that the transaction may give itself a profit.

    What is the difference between a service-based business and a product
    based business?
    ANS:
    1) Service-based business can be customer-service oriented.
    Eg. Providing information over the phone, delivering of goods, repair of
    equipment

    2)A product business usually has an inventory that receives stock
    from suppliers. It also has an operations side that sells the stock,
    assemble/modify stock for use. Eg. restaurants, insurance, retail,
    fish & meat, marketshop-house. Sometimes it provides services not
    related
    to the business, eg. Asking the shopowner for an exchange of coins.

    How does accounting relate to business?
    ANS:
    Accounting records information of expenditure, credit, debit,
    capital and liabilities. It shows a business's performance.
    This information is useful to the business organisation and to
    government bodies that it must report to. Eg. Company Income tax Body.

    What is Computing?
    ANS:
    The input and process of information by a dedicated device and its
    delivery
    and presentation to the user. Eg. Computer, Cash register, handphone
    etc.

    How has computers affect accounting?
    ANS:
    Traditionally, accounting is done by calculators and notes
    written on recordbooks. Computers is a way to deliver
    the information to another location. It also processes business
    information
    to provide brief, accurate reports.

    How is computing used in any business?
    ANS:
    1) Administration/Sales
    2) Engineering/Production
    3) Critical data

    1) In any service-based business, one can see extensive usage of
    computers eg. in electronic cash-registers, a whole armada of
    office-workers
    behind computer desks and phonecalls.

    2) Computing affects eng/production with these tools:
    Rapid prototyping, PLC/SCADA, electro-mechanical automation.
    These tools are used to test product design and manufacturing
    processes.

    3) The use of phones, recorders, radiosets especially to request for
    exact information. (in scenes like emergency rooms, underground ops,
    supplychain)

    Electrical Engineering (EE)
    ----------------------------------
    What is EE?
    ANS: It is an applied science, a study in some polytechnics and has
    a profound influence on urban life.

    What does it involve?
    ANS: It starts off with power delivery, distribution, and also
    components,
    systems and information technology.

    How is it an applied science?
    ANS: Alot of modern day products and devices uses electricity. How
    they are safe and reliable involves design and economic usage of parts.
    This is a role taken up by electrical engineers.

    What does a study of EE involve?
    Electrical Engineering graduates need to study
    basic electrical circuits, electronics, design, power protection, PLCs
    and mechanical interaction.

    What does an electrical engineering project involve?ANS:
    It could be a Programmable Logic Relay project or a electronics
    circuitboard
    or a computer software or a product-material research/study.
    The tools used could be statistics, interviews, and documentation.
    The mode of presentation can involve website displays that show
    'live' product performance.

    How is EE related to ELECTRONICS eng.?
    Electronics focuses on op-amps, transistors, diodes, design and
    performance
    of TVs, COMPUTERS, hardware and software.
    EE focuses on INSTALLATION and SAFETY INSURANCE such as
    lighting appliances, datacable fixtures etc.

    What is PLC?ANS:
    PLC stands for programmable logic controller. It is a tool
    used to control traffic lights, MRT trains, robots, etc.
    It is often linked with SCADA: Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition.

    That is a tool often used in factories to supervise manufacturing
    processes.
    Programs are written into the PLC machine through a PLC controller.
    The machine then processes the program and shows the results.

    Where is EE most profound?
    ANS:
    It is a science that involves other disciplines. If we look at around
    us,
    alot of our lifestyle is made possible through EE.

    For eg. To have a proper apartment, materials must be made
    to fit and proper for use in accordance with housing regulations.
    The wires chosen must be of correct rating and the mechanical
    parts(eg. armatures, housings, cableboxes) must fit exactly.

    Their behaviour must relate to each other in such a way
    that there is a form of 'distinguishment'. This distinguishment
    is for safety purposes. Eg. Enough gap inside a socket-housing

    Airconditioners are made through electrical and mechanical
    eng. but are repaired by people well-versed in gases and fluids.

    What skill is needed to understand EE?
    ANS: Understanding of relationships of parts and how they
    are presented to the user in a cautious way.

    What is javascript?
    ANS: It is a language used on web-browsers. It processes codes
    written by programmers. Together with advance languages,
    they can tell our computer what to do and what to display.

    What has javascript got to do with PLC programming?
    IMHO they are both the same in rules and methods.
    Both involve a use of a dedicated device to process
    the rules(PLC machine and the Browser).

    Both involve the usage of rules to get
    1) User variables/input
    2) User expectations.

    Whereas PLC programming/SCADA runs stored programs
    to control remote lights, machines, sensors located far away, the same
    behaviour
    goes for web-browsers: local browsers running codes stored
    in servers and remote PCs in other countries.

    Their only difference is that
    1) PLC/SCADA serves the organisation requirements and is founded on
    "CONTROL"
    2) Web-browsers/handheld netdevices serves the user interests and is
    founded on "FREEDOM"

    How the two relate to each other is best found in
    Space Satellites. There over 24 man-made satellites in outerspace
    rotating
    around Earth. They deliver information to and fro. When a
    satellite receives continental information, it activates its PLC
    program-turning
    on or off the appropriate switches-then the signals are transmitted to
    another
    continent. The local device(browser or monitor) thus presents the
    information.

    Is there a possibility of a webbrowser running a server code to
    activate: say my TV/RADIO/SHOWER and lights?
    ANS:All this is very possible and is actually happening. Such a system
    is very expensive
    but i think BILL GATES'S HOUSE got such a thing.

    Is it profitable to sell such a system?
    Unless it becomes a way of life, IMHO i think it would produce only
    marginal profits.

    1)Together with advance pollution control techniques, it would reduce

    Nature of quality engineering & management
    -------------------------------------------------------
    What is quality?
    Quality is about providing customer satisfaction such as getting good
    reviews of service/product and reducing complains and also the running
    of
    smooth operations within the company.

    I have seen companies stating they are quality class. What does it mean?

    It means their company has the procedures and measures
    to run their business according to a set of rules.

    What are these set of rules?
    The ISO 9000 series is a popular one. usually conformance to these
    rules provide the company with an award. In the US, the award is know as
    MALCOLM BALDRIDGE and in Japan, the DEMING prize.
    In Singapore, the Productivity & Standards Board(PSB) administer these
    rules
    for local companies.

    Why should quality be related to engineering?
    1) Safety. A well designed engineered product passes the safety test.
    It is used and sold extensively in world-markets.
    2) Performance: Solid performance shows the dedication of the people
    involved.

    How does engineering relate to Management?
    Management serves to guide the organisation as a whole.
    Engineering prepares the service or product for sale and use.
    Management makes sure this product gets to the right person
    and at the right time and price.

    How is quality related to management?
    A good quality management programme improves employee morale.
    Reduced costs. More bonuses.

    Can an organisation survive by a quality mark?
    No. It must continously improve its ideas, retrain people and improve
    methods
    for survival. It needs the right people to take on the work.
