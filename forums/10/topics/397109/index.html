    It appears that the floodgates will be opened wider to Indian professionals to work in Singapore if India were to have its way.

    An Indian Financial Daily reported two days ago that India is seeking greater access for its professionals to work in Singapore in the coming review of the of the comprehensive economic cooperation agreement or CECA signed between the two countries in 2005.

    The report claimed that “Singapore has already got a lot of concessions as part of India’s free trade agreement with the ten member Asean countries.”

    In return, Singapore has recognized some Indian medical colleges such as the All India Institute of Medical Sciences and Christian Medical College in Vellore as part of CECA.

    Medical doctors from these colleges can now practice in Singapore as doctors without additional local qualifications.

    There are increasing number of Indian doctors working in Singapore in recent years, most of whom are deployed in unpopular specialities within public hospitals and polyclinics.

    An official from the Indian Commerce Ministry was quoted as saying that India wants a “proper mutual recognition agreement in place to provide for both countries recognizing each others’ degrees in given areas.” and to seek greater access for services.

    India’s request is likely to be accepted by the pro-foreigner PAP government which will result in a greater inflow of Indian expatriates into Singapore.

    PAP strongman Lee Kuan Yew recently urged Singapore to accept more qualified immigrants and used the example of an Indian student succeeding in Singapore to illustrate his point.

    The news was not published in the state media which is busy of late trying to promote “integration” between Singaporeans and the immigrants.
