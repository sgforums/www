    One might be forgiven for suspecting that, given his activities, LKY might never have had time to take an IQ test.

    Taking a stab at making an estimation, I worked upon the assumption that LKY was the intellectual equal of other world leaders in 1959. The leaders I took into consideration were:

    Eisenhower - 69 years old
    McMillan - 65 years old
    Nehru - 70 years old
    Ben Gurion - 73 years old

    leading to a rounded average of 69 yeqars of age. I had considered adding Nasser top the list, but his presidency by coup d'etat made the addition spurious. Upon the assumption that all these leaders were of equal intellectual ability, we may assume that LKY had a mental age of 69 in 1959.

    Given that the equation for IQ is (Mental age / Chronological age) X 100, LKY's IQ would have been 192.

    Before we read too much into this, this figure is admittedly flawed:

    1) we are assuming that LKY was equal to the other world leaders at this point - this is unlikely to be the case since there is no specific comparison to determine that equality

    2) the political conditions in different countries tap into different aspects of their leaders' abilities, so the fact that these are leaders in itself is inadequate

    3) the list of leaders is far from comprehensive

    4) any IQ score of 155 or above is not a statistically reliable figure

    Without better information to go on, however, this was the closest thing I could use for a calculation.

    From my experience in abilities testing, and given that the ideas in LKY's autobiography are indeed his, I would expect that his IQ score would be in the range of 140 to 150.
