    MM Lee once stated the reasons behind implementing a conscripted army. Once the Brits left, defence was left to ourseleves. We had 3 options. 1 was to sign a defence treaty with neighbouring nations, for example Malaysia and Indonesia, stating that in times of war, THEIR armies would defend US. This method is not unproven as Gambia and Senegal have a similar treaty that has been put to the test before. It held up. But seriously, would you like Singapore to always be at a dis-advantage by having neighbouring countries defend us?

    The second option to provide protection to this small nation would be to have an almost entirely mercenary army. Whether they would hold key positions was never contemplated, as this option was thrown out the minute it was brought up for obvious reasons.
