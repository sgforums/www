    Posted on April 8th, 2009 under The Good Balita

    Around 5,000 new jobs would be made available to Filipinos in Singapore’s Sentosa island, one of the city state’s main attractions and the host of the newest integrated resort, Resorts World which opens in 2010.

    Philippine Labor Secretary Marianito Roque who visited Singapore recently said, he was able to get openings in Sentosa for 5,000 Filipinos.

    The bulk of the hiring will be coming from Resorts World at Sentosa, which operates the integrated resort, which announced that it would be hiring around 20,000 people by the middle of the year.

    The US$4 billion integrated resort is developed by Malaysia’s Genting International.

    The mammoth resort will occupy over 49 hectares of land and will be the home of Universal Studios, Singapore, Southeast Asia’s first and only Universal Studios theme park and it will feature 24 attractions, with 18 rides exclusively designed for the park. The park will be divided into six zones - including Superhero City, Egypt, New York, The Lost World and Hollywood.

    The integrated resort will also have six world-class hotels offering a total of 1,840 rooms for accommodation. Each hotel is designed with a different theme, catering to both the leisure and business visitors.

    The casino, with a floor area of 15,000 sqm will be located beneath Maxims Residences, which is an all-suite hotel with a magnificent view of Singapore’s Southern waterfront.
