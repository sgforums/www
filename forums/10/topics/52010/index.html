    I am not sure if the Government is "NOT to be blamed for all that is happening with the current situation" in Singapore for the lack of entrepreneurship.

    To start with, although the Government has always advocated that "Market Supply and Demand" shall be the criteria for the allocation of national resources - including labor, the government's hand is always at play to manipulate the market mechanism.

    Students are guided or allocated by the quota system into the various streams of technical and academic at the secondary levels, and the sciences and general studies at the tertiary institutions.

    The citizens below 45 years old are NOT allowed to apply for any hawker licences, and are required to take on menial works (a.k.a factory hands) when they do not qualify for administrative work.

    Those who are below 45 years, and unable to get their own licences, will "borrow" the licences of older folks - who are not the real stall holders.

    Any enterprising ideas from any individuals, who form a company, will soon find themselves facing the onslaught of a GLC breathing down their back - as in the automotive workshops facing the might of NTUC Comfort Automobile Workshops, General Automotive Services, and also from Singapore Technologies Automotive - all with major workshops in the Sin Min Area; or the private Prime Supermarkets facing the might of NTUC Co-operative Supermarkets and Neighborhood stores.

    Even the consulting sector of professional engineers and architects are also facing the competition from Government Linked Companies such as those from PWD, PSA and now HDB; let alone some other Government Linked Companies competing with the private Building and Construtcion Companies, and Private Home Developers such as City Development and Far East Corporations.

    The Government will have the upper hand, as it is the one who draft the laws and legislate the same into Law, and come up with regulations to move the population in the direction that it sees fit to suit their Master Plan.

    Is the Government blameless in the present inertia in the population being entrepreneurial ?
