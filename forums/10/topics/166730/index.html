    Just to clear everybody knows that outsourcing isn't cheap, depending on what you farm out it can be very expensive to implement and only corporations which huge financial muscle will do it, and they only realise savings incrementally over a long period not immediately.

    sometimes there is more to life than just dollar and cents. Sure outsourcing can reduce cost but the effect of that is that you end up selling out some of your faithful employees to some agency and many of these employees could be your most productive workers may not like this sort of arragngement.

    for example say PIGHEAD corporation outsourced their helpdesk business manpower requirements to an outsourcing company or HR agency, in effect what has happened is that the bulk of their employees have to join the HR agency or leave. The agency would then supply these same people back to the original company PIGHEAD where they previously worked for at the same pay. The status of these workers maybe previously from permanent now becomes contract staff. As long as there is work the contract staff is safe, if there is a recession and PIGHEAD stops some production or dramatically slashes costs those staff will be axed and since they are on contract there is rarely any benefits.

    Once outsourced the impact for PIGHEAD is that they no longer have any obligation to pay their original employees bonuses, insurance, training etc the HR agency takes care of that and bills PIGHEAD accordingly ... And every year to reduce PIGHEAD outsourcing costs they will ask the HR agency to lower their fees, the agency can agree or not agree. If they agree it means the agency earns less so they either for that year pay no increments to their "contract staff" because they earn less or they take on another account say DOGHEAD corporation and leverage by using those same employees manage it.

    And the following year maybe PIGHEAD AND DOGHEAD ask the agency to reduce cost, again the agency agrees so no increment again or they get a new account called CATHEAD and again leverage those same employees now working 200% working 2 accounts to now handle a third account. And following year again another account to manage So on so forth, is a slave job. These employees normally leave after a few years because there so much sh!t to handle, when they leave inexperieced people come in are farmed out often immediately to replaced those who left, they are left to fend for themselves OJT as a result production can drop.

    Outsourcing is a dirty word. The people who lose out the most often are those at the bottom of the food chain. The top guys never lose anything. Be very careful what you wish for.

    ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

    Oh yes one last thing, the companies will keep asking for fees to be lowered. The more fees are lowered the more companies save overtime. There will come a time where the HR agency will feel they just cannot lower fees anymore. They can stop whereby all the staff will be axed and the companies will find another agency who can do it.

    Or the agency can open another office in india, china, thailand or even malaysia and source cheaper manpower there and may get equally or similiar calibre workers then internally farm the work here to there thereby taking advantage of the currency exchange and increase bottomline. Those who lose their jobs to cheaper overseas workers have to scramble for whatever leftover scraps locally maybe even at lower pay or be prepare to get axed.

    So now you know how jobs move geographically and how good outsourcing really is.
