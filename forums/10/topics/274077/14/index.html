    Science (as I have mentioned before) is one of the best tools available to mankind in studying natural phenomenon. But to try and apply it to other less "naturalistic" areas like politics, ethics, religion, social values, etc would most likely bring about less than satisfying results.

    The great philosopher Bertrand Russell said it best:

    "Ethics contains no statements, whether true or false, but consists of desires of a certain general kind, namely such as are concerned with the desires of mankind in general - and of gods, angels, and devils, if they exist. Science can discuss the causes of desires, and the means for realizing them, but it cannot contain any genuinely ethical sentences, because it is concerned with what is true or false (i.e. naturalistic explanantions) "

    "I conclude that, while it is true that science cannot decide questions of values, that is because they cannot be intellectually decided at all, and lie outside the realm of truth and falsehood. Whatever knowledge is attainable, must be attained by scientific methods; and what science cannot discover, mankind cannot know."

    Full text at: http://www.solstice.us/russell/science-ethics.html

    Note the last part where he says:
    "what science cannot discover, mankind cannot know."
