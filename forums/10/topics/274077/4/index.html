    We need to bring 2 main arguments into perspective, which I see is the gist of this thread on Science

    1) Things that Today's Science can explain
    2) Things that Today's Science cannot explain

    Notice my use of the word "Today"

    For (1), even if Science can explain NOW, we may find out that it is incorrect sometime down the road later, because Science is all about research and trials. Discoveries being announced to the world is done only after some intense trial and error has been performed (and subject to the commercial and pragmatic limitations of society), but that doesn't mean that it is 100% error-free. We all know there are many announced discoveries that were proven to be incorrect sometime later.

    For (2), things that Science cannot explain, doesn't mean it cannot be done, or it doesn't exist. For example, ghosts. Who dare say with 100% certainty that ghosts do not exist because science cannot prove it? Also, many of the great researchers of the past had to struggle with their repeated failures, before finally being able to prove or perform something.
    So if they had given up inventing the light bulb, for instance, would the believers of Today's Science then say that its not possible to create the light bulb?

    Science is THE way to explain MANY things, but its important for one to keep an open mind in order to understand things beyond Science's explanation.
