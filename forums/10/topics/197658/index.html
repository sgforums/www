    Islamic seminary in US to bridge religion-culture gulf

    Jun 28, 2006
    The Straits Times
    EVERY seat in the auditorium at the University of Houston was taken. The two American-Muslims onstage spoke in Arabic, with flawless accents.

    Sheikh Hamza Yusuf, 48, and Imam Zaid Shakir, 50, are converts to Islam and had spent years in the Middle East and West Africa.

    The two men are creating the first Islamic seminary in the United States where they hope to train a new generation of imams and scholars who can reconcile Islam and American culture. The California seminary, called the Zaytuna Institute, is in its fledgling stages.

    Sheikh Yusuf believes that if more Muslims were schooled in Islam's diverse intellectual streams and had a holistic understanding of it, they would not be so susceptible to the message that suicide bombers are martyrs.

    Though the two do not denounce any particular school of thought, their students say they are challenging the sometimes intolerant influence of sects such as Wahhabism and Salafism.

    Sheikh Yusuf and Imam Shakir teach that Islam is open to a diversity of interpretations and to beware of 'fanatics' who pluck Islamic scripture out of context and say: We are going to tell you what God says on every issue.

    'That's not Islam,' Sheikh Yusuf says. 'That is psychopathy.'

    Hundreds of Muslims come to Zaytuna for classes on Prophet Muhammad, the Quran and the Arabic language. There is also a female scholar who teaches only women. -- NEW YORK TIMES
