    Ex-presidential candidate Tan Jee Say turns May Day protest into 'political rally'

     

    Change the Govt: Tan Jee Say


    HIS plan to help workers?

       Change the Government, said former presidential candidate Tan Jee Say yesterday at Hong Lim Park. He was not against foreign workers in Singapore, but blamed current policies for allowing them in.

       "Do not vote PAP out of gratitude because gratitude is about the past, not for the future," he said.

       "Vote for the future, not the past."

       Mr Tan, who also stood and lost at the 2011 General Election in Holland-Bukit Timah GRC as a Singapore Democratic Party candidate, described the Population White Paper as a plot against Singaporeans.

       Ten others spoke about their concerns ranging from human rights to bread-and-butter issues before Mr Tan.

       Speaking on infrastructure, he said places such as Hong Kong built infrastructure first before bringing in more people, but the exact opposite was done here.

       "The only way... is to change the Government," he told the crowd.

       "The fact is the opposition today is more ready than PAP was in 1959."

       He said the opposition now has seven PhD holders.

       "By the next general election in 2016, more will join us, like many of you here in the audience," he said.

       He said it was possible for different opposition parties to form a coalition government.

       He said: "Go for it Singapore, your life will taste much better under a 'rojak' coalition."


    News, The New Paper, Thursday, May 2 2013, Pg 3
