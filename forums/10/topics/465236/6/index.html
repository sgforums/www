    ESM Goh on Hong Lim Park protest

     

    'Speakers too political, one-sided, emotional'

     

     

    HAPPY that people are debating the population issue, but unimpressed by the rhetoric of the speakers.

       That was the reaction from Emeritus Senior Minister (ESM) Goh Chok Tong (right), when asked on Facebook what he thought of the protest at Hong Lim Park against the population White Paper.

       Mr Goh wrote: "I was happy that Speakers' Corner is serving its purpose. Also pleased that Singaporean are debating this population issue because it is about our future. But our future goes beyond just population figures."

       He added: "Cannot say that I think much of speakers' rhetoric. Too political, too one-sided, appealing to emotions only and not shedding light on important issues."

       A crowd, variously estimated to be anything between 1,000 and 5,000 had gathered on Saturday evening to listen to 12 speakers, including opposition figures.

       They rejected bringing in more foreigners to sustain economic growth, and argued that Singaporeans should have been consulted more widely on the White Paper.

       Though some spoke out against xenophobia, most said they feared Singapore will no longer be home for Singaporeans and that the infrastructure cannot cope even with the current population, let alone 6.9 million.

       Drawing loud cheers at the end, former presidential candidate Tan Jee Say accused the ruling People's Action Party of bringing in new citizens to shore up its share of votes.

     

    News Watch, The New Paper, Monday, February 18 2013, Pg 8
