    Frustrated by foreign talent

     

    HE turned up at Hong Lim Park yeaterday to protest against the current immigration policies.

       Mr Edmund Loke (right), 38, a machine operator, was seen holding up a placard with the words "Save Singapore".

       He said: "I am affected by the influx of foreigners every single day.

       "When I take public transport to work in the morning, it is too crowded and everybody is just squeezing. The buses are packed."

       He is also frustrated at work.

       "When I get to work, my workplace has more foreign talent than Singaporeans," he said.

       "Because of them, my pay is so low."

       The machine operator said he earns $1,300 a month.

       He then asked how foreigners could integrate when they are so many.

       "The infrastructure is not there," he said.

       "They (the Government) have finally admitted this, but do they listen to the public?"

       Mr Loke said he did not see any improvement to his situation after 2½ years.

       He also said he was afraid for the future and his job security.

       He said: "More and more foreigners come in, and they are willing to accept such low pay.

       "I am uncertain about how secure my job is."


    News, The New Paper, Thursday, May 2 2013, Pg 3
