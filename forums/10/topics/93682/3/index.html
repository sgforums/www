    I am sure you've been to Singapore before, so when you get pass the Causeway you will see some highrise, they are the first blocks of HDB flats you encounter. Those are the Woodlands checkpoint are not that new.
    HDB came about because LKY had to tackle the housing issue quickly. It was an affordable way to give the masses a roof over their heads. As it evolves, the housing provided by HDB are categoriesed by 2 rooms (1 bedroom, 1 hall, the kitchen and toilets are obvious). 3 rooms, 4 rooms, 5 rooms (4 and 5 comes with dining room), Executive (4 bedrooms, hall, dining room, maybe balcony) and lastly Maisonette (as executive but two stories). Beyond this, is HUDC, which is a HDB-developed condominum project.
    All else for housing are private developments, condos will have tennis courts, swimming pools etc.
    Here's not to say that people living in HDB flats cannot afford condos, it is just a matter of choice. My HDB unit is cost more than a condo, but with at least twice the floor space of a similarly priced unit.
