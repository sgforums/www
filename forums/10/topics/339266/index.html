Being a ethical businessman

  • Worldlybusinessman
    3 Dec 08, 07:10

    Ethics is a pledge of future success in business.An ethical businessman is a person who meets his obligations and guarantees his word. I used to meet people like that — they always kept their word and promises and that helped them acquire good reputations in business circles. The essential component of future success for any organization is good business and the reputation of its employees; this attracts new clients and partners.

     

    Ethics is a component of success everywhere in life, not only in business, because morality is the basis of everything.  But, still, ethical businessmen are very rare in our country; in most cases businessmen will do anything to get money.The problem is that business ethics don’t really exist in Singapore. Maybe after 50 or even 60 years our country will enter a new phase in its development and such concepts like ethics, morality and conscience will be essential in business affairs. I think that a businessman is ethical if he can sleep free of guilt at night and has gotten his good reputation with good deeds

    The recent minibond issue is one of the ways of unethical behaviour after dozens of investors were left in the lurch.

    i do know of overseas businesses which devote to environmental friendlyness and charity. But there is none in our country. Why is this so?

    As a business, bth young and old buy your products and it will be a good idea to project your business has having a "social cause" to buyers, especially the young. In the western world, indeed the old people who are more well off than the young due to their years savings and pension, they are actually my returning customers because i did make up a good reputation with them.

    I know people who, when they started doing business, sometimes did illegal things in order to survive, but now that they have succeeded they donate big money to charity. One example is Microsoft, Which learnt the hard way, due tot heir supposed monopolisitic practices as viewed by some in the community, Gates indeed donated large amounts of money to charity.

     

    There are indeed some ethical businessmen in singapore, but they have all their businesses elsewhere.

    Ho can we project our country, apart from the superficially outside appearance of  1st world to the one of a truly first world place to do business in terms of ethics?
