    so we have arrived at this question,

    What does GCT mean by "First World Standard" ? How do the policies announced by our government fit in ?
    In his 2001 National Day Rally speech, then PM GCT stated that:

    ''Our economy grew faster in the 90s than in the 80s, despite the 1997 Asian financial crisis (7.7% per annum, as against 7.3%). I was also quietly satisfied that we realised our vision of reaching the 1984 Swiss standard of living last year. In 1984, we had set this as a target to be achieved by 1999, but we missed it by one year because of the crisis.''

    Was reaching an economic growth rate of 7.7% in Year 2000 sufficient justification for claiming that Singapore has reached the Swiss Standard of Living pegged at 1984 ?

    Is the Swiss Standard of Living so intangible that it lasted momentarily in a percentum expression only ?

    What had GCT in mind when he mentioned the Swiss Standard of Living for Singaporeans to work towards to ?

    In an interview that GCT gave to Asiaweek magazine printed in 2001, he explained:

    ''In 1984, the government said it wanted Singapore to be a developed country by 1999, with living standards equivalent to Switzerland's in 1984. The OECD recently reclassified Singapore as a "more advanced developing economy." Are you on track for 1999?

    In 1984, when we talked about the Swiss standard of living, I was using a simple measure of per capita income. By 1999, we would like to achieve the 1984 per capita income of Switzerland. Are we on track? If you look at most of the economic indicators, the answer is yes.''

    Unfortunately, the economic downturn in 1987 disrupted the progress, and another one in 1997 shook Singapore to the core before the speech quote and the Asiaweek interview were both made.

    Should we look at the SWISS Standard of Living merely in economic numbers or in the totality of the SWISS Life - its politics, social, cultural, intellectual, - that make up the Swiss way ?

    All that is mentioned in GCT's National Day Rally Speech of 2001 was addressed to Singaporeans to emulate and conform, somehow they were not quite applicable to the Government, its Ministers, and the Civil Servants.

    Such is the half-measures of our Political Leaders towards making structural change is what is hampering our movement forward - as can be seen in LHL's famous remarks of ''all stones were turned over, inspected, and placed back as they were best left alone'' - when he head the Economic Restructuring Committee after the 1997 downturn.

    Can Singapore achieve a SWISS Standard of Living that is recognised as equal to any country in the First World - when the Singapore Political System is locked in a Third World mould ?

    Can economic progress be lasting when political policies hampers the development of political, social and cultural creativity that is essential to a thriving and living economy ?

    The SWATCH watch was the Swiss answer to all the SEIKO, CASIO, Citizens, and ALBA - can Singapore emulate the private enterprise and initiative of the Swiss to recover from the debilating challenge of the Japanese watch industry that knocked out the Swiss historical leadership in this area ?

    Such a creative initiative from the Private Sector can only come about when the Political System encourages the Individual to be responsible for all issues - big and small.

    Hong Kong is another example of Private initiative and creative enterprise that continue to maintain the Hongkongers reputation of being survivalists in nywhere in the World.

    It is the Political System that moulds the People.
