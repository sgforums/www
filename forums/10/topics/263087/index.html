    Was looking at the actual 2005 revenue from taxes in the 2007 MOF's budget (Ministry of Finance), I cross referenced it to IRAS's (Inland Revenue Authority of Singapore) collection in 2005. Some of the figures from MOF when cross referenced to IRAS is accurate, while other figures did not tally.

    There were no differences between the IRAS's and MOF's figure for Betting Taxes, Stamp Duty and Estate Duty.

    The differences between the IRAS's and MOF's figure for Corporate & Personal Income Tax, Property Tax and GST adds up to $5,651,744.

    How is it that when IRAS collects one amount for a particular tax category, but when it is received by MOF, there is a difference.

    I wonder if anybody could provide any reasonable explanation for the discrepancies?

    Source: Inland Revenue Authority of Singapore


    Source: Ministry of Finance


    Corporate and Personal Income Tax:

    IRAS = $4,326,564,000 + $7,340,767,000 = $11,667,331,000

    MOF = $11,663,203,937

    IRAS - MOF = $11,667,331,000 - $11,663,203,937 = $4,127,063 (difference)

    Property Tax:

    IRAS - MOF = $1,829,177,000 - $1,828,934,461 = $242,539 (difference)

    GST:

    IRAS - MOF = $3,816,618,000 - $3,815,335,858 = $1,282,142 (difference)

    Total Differences = $4,127,063 + $242,539 + $1,282,142 = $5,651,744



    Betting Taxes:

    IRAS = $1,222,246,000 + $278,340,000 = $1,500,586,000

    MOF = $1,500,386,176

    IRAS - MOF = $1,500,586,000 - $1,500,386,176 = $176 (difference due to accuracy adjustments)

    Stamp Duty:

    IRAS - MOF = $966,677,000 - $966,677,030 = $30 (difference due to accuracy adjustments)

    Estate Duty:

    IRAS - MOF = $80,687,000 - $80,687,333 = $333 (difference due to accuracy adjustments)
