    Originally posted by dragg:

    Cost of generating electricity is between $0.09 - $012 per kilowatt-hour. The current Power Supply billing for electricity is $0.17-$0.21 per kilowatt-hour depending on oil prices inclusive of networking costs (partly exempted for extra-high-tension supply) and land/infrastructure costs already paid for by the taxpayers some years ago.

    A more reasonable electricity charge would be in the region of $0.12 - $0.15 in the current oil-spike situation which will still allow the utility companies to cover costs of operation with some profits without giving inflated high profits to the government. The government should have granted a credit on the land and fixed infrastructure assets to the utility companies upon handing over the facilities to them or corporatising PS as a private entity.

    In view of such creative accounting practices being rampant, I am inviting you to send this post to as many friends and fellow citizens to ask for a rebate on the current utility charges to reverse the unwarranted land and fixed asset/networking charges.
