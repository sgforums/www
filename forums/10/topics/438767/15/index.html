    Although I have never voted for the PAP, it doesn't mean I will never vote for them.

    In the 90s I was staying in Kim Keat constituency, Ong Teng Cheong was the MP, if it was a single ward (not GRC, Toa Payaoh that time always walkover) and even if JBJ stood there, I would find it hard not to vote for OTC. He was a really good MP and took personal care of the residents needs. How to throw this kind of MP out?

    So very subjective, that I said I did not vote for OTC as President. That was national issue, I didn't want the PAP to dominate the landscape even the presidency. 

    So it boils down to both local and national issues. Basically speaking I am against giving 1 party too much power, even those days when the PAP was supposedly better, but then again the local issue comes about.

    Nationally now I am against many policies of the PAP, so I would vote against them, however if I am in a single ward and my MP was like OTC, I might vote for them. (In fact I am in a single ward, but my MP is nowhere close to OTC, so I voted for the other guy, even though I thought he was a lightweight).
