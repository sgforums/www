    I read with a lot of interest (and maybe angst and injustification) of the recent topics in the Speaker's Corner. As of yet, I really feel for the poor Singaporeans in the lower/middle strata in Singapore that experienced little income growth despite Singapore registering an impressive 7% GDP growth in 06. partly because i volunteer during my free time and i have an opportunity to look at facets of Singapore many don't have a glimpse to as yet.

    Topics like lowering 1% corporate tax to offset the 2% raise in gst and the growing poor in SG. Sure, we can lament that policies albeit unpopular may be ridiculous and even ineffective (in our own right), that's very much we can all do. my 2c worth though.

    I've read some reasons as to why the poor isnt receiving the help even if they should through the Workfare scheme etc. because they don't have the proper avenues to such know-how. Sadly in our meritocratic society, we can only rely on volunteers to reach out and bridge the information gap and passionate social workers but are experiencing burnt out and job hopping to more lucrative cashfields.

    Perhaps, we can gather more views and suggestions from here to come up with a concrete plan to reach out to the "unreachables"? anyone from the floor?

    -an exasperated singaporean.
