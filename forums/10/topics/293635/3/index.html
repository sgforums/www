    It all comes down to a personal value system, and whether you are able to migrate or not.

    Consumer prices will continue to rise on a slightly steeper trend than other nations with a higher degree of self sufficiency. (e.g. have to buy sand and water). You can bite the bullet or pack up and go.

    Many people prefer the western culture, where life/culture is pro family. Some prefer the buzz and faster paced local culture. I kow for fact, in Australia, in companies run by Australians, the general attitude is, at 6pm your boss will tell you to go home and have a life.

    Just for an example to illustrate the difference in culture:

    Its like a worker has to watch his kid's soccer game at school. He skipped work for a few hours on tuesday afternoon. A project dateline a week later, was delayed maybe a day or 2.

    The boss can react in 1 of 2 ways

    1 "Why weren't you at work tuesday afternoon? Is your son's soccer game that impt?"
    2 "Why the project had to be delayed? How did your son do at the game btw"

    Reaction 1 would probably be what you get here in SG. Although that may be changing.

    of course there are other factors to consider, this is just an illustration.

    Working culture aside, PRs and Citizens get preferential treatment in terms of employment. Meaning, a company has to justify to the government that it cannot find a Citizen or PR to do the job, before approval to hire a foreigner.

    It also comes down to effort put into work vs creature comforts. Some poeple may think, "I live in a cosmopolitan city/nation, why do I still have the rice-bowl mentality?" I remember a loacl bank using a golden rice bowl in a current ad poster.

    Sure there is always a sacrifice or a down side to uprooting and moving on to another nation. Then again, if the benefits out weigh the downside, and there are work-arounds (e.g. avoid highly crime prone areas). many would think, why not move?
