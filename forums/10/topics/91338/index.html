    I agree with you: all the stations here tout to play the "best music", which in their own opinion is just the mainstream popular music, or Pop as we know it. So far, we only have Symphony 92.4 to play classics.

    The reason why we do not have stations specialising in any genre is our small population. The radio audience is already a largely fragmented pie, with 5 major chinese radio stations, 5 major english radio stations fighting for audiences. With 4 million people (assuming they all listen to radio at all!), it is hard enough for the mainstream stations to gain advertising, not to mention stations like Symphony. In fact, radio in Singapore is also subsidised by the Media Development of Singapore, through the radio licences that people pay.

    A solution would be a catch-all station, one that inter-mixes all the genres, potentially appealing to a wider breadth of audiences. Certain time-slots could be dedicated to exclusively a genre. But most likely, such a station would be under public service, rather than a profit generating media corporation.
