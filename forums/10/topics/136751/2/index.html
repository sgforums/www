    How to streamline the economy? Here we go..

    1) Reduce and sell off many of Temasek Holdings' GLCs to local SMEs and MNCs as Temasek has shown that it is ineffective in controlling too many different companies in different markets at the same time. In addition, Temasek Holdings have encouraged a monopolistic culture in the private sector- which is always an unhealthy sign. Opening up the market will help in dissolving the monopolistic way that the private sector and the govt has always been run.

    2) With less govt interference in the private sectors, local SMEs and international MNCs will be the ones who will be providing the services of the private sector. Meaning that, anyone who owes a business, no matter what nationality he is or how big or how small his business is will have a level playing field in terms of competition. That means that he or she, in order to compete will need to improve his/her products/commodities/services. This means he or she also needs to hire good people in order to accomplish that goal.

    Aka, that means more employment.

    3) Intense competition always mean good things for both the companies and the consumers. Prices will be cheaper, quality will improve, service will be better as companies will be locked in a battle to attract, retain new and regular customers or consumers. Unlike in a monopolistic environment where people will be retained regardless of performance, people working in a competitive private sector will have to be on their toes because of the intense working environment they are in.

    If they work hard, they get their bonuses, they get their basic wage packages. if they work intenstively hard, they get even more bonuses and higher wages and they will need to work very hard to retain their top positions. It also works for those who underperform.

    Those who lag will be paid less. They have less bonuses, less wages to bring home etc. Those who are nothing but disrupters, resisters, or extremely lazy will find themselves unemployed because the companies they work don't stand for such laziness. And of course to ensure that these people get employed again, it must be in such a way that while they don't get too unhappy, they also won't be let go when they are too old. Its best to let go of underperformers while they are still in their 20s or early 30s.

    4) Local SMEs which succeed in the local markets will, in time, have chances to expand overseas. Singapore already have a few, like Tiger Beer, Creative, SIA, 77th Street and Breadtalk. But under a more liberalised private sector, and with a clear, transparent system imposed by a new govt, new more open SMEs will expand into other parts of Asia, even in the West and South America. These SMEs will be better brands than the current ones and if the new SMEs succeed, then automatically Singapore will also be in the limelight. In this case, it isn't the govt who gets all the glory- instead it will be the Singaporean citizens who will be the main ones as they are the ones who helped these SMEs succeed overseas.

    5) A last thing to add: The people itself will benefit in many ways. I have mentioned it before many times. Nonetheless, I will go through it again.

    i) More money in the pocket for the people as govt will transfer a huge percentage of the money earned from selling of GLCs back to the people. After all GLC money is taxpayers money. Taxpayers' money should go back to the people sooner or later.

    So lower income families and lower middle class citizens will get the bulk of the money the govt will gain. Not just S$1000 for a whole year but S$8000 for the 1st 8 months(for the selected (really)lower income families) and S$3000 for the last 4 months.

    ii) But as time passes, and as the liberalized private sector improves and the economy improves and with employment rates up, less cash bonuses or tax breaks will be handled out- especially to the richer middle class families. Of course, tax breaks will still be continued for the bottom 10% to the bottom 30% and cash handouts will be given. But people will be expected to work hard to get more bonuses. See Point 3.

    iii) With also more money in their pockets, people will be encouraged to use the money to improve their lives. People always need money to spend: food, clothes, a HDB flat, kitchen appliances, luxury electronic appliances(LCD, Plasma TV, DVD player, MP3 players, DVD-R video camera recorders, 3G cellphones), fashion etc. In return, there will also be more retail shops- from the big brand names of both international and local fame to the mum-and-dad HDB cheap shops downstairs. All these means a better shopping experience for all as there are more choices for a more affordable price.

    And did we say that families can now take a break for a holiday- with more money- as well?

    Again, as I said, this is all in theory. IRL, things may or may not happen as described above. But again, we cannot say whether can it succeed unless we give it our best shot. And still it sounds better than the current PAP system where everyone tries to get by and yet are quite unhappy with both life in the office and at home.

    The reason on why I wrote that because I believed in a few main things:

    1) Economic freedom and political freedom goes hand-in-hand. When there's creative freedom in your workplace, eventually you will transfer that into your home and your family. So if you feel more happier with your work in a new working culture and regime, you naturally wanna share with your family. And when you get more money from your bonuses from your company, you wanna share with your family.

    And with more money in your pockets and wallets, you also sense a belonging to the country as it shows that it recognizes and appreciates your real talents, your hard work, the results you get and the effort. You score points and money not by kissing ur bosses' rear end everyday, but by your own hands. That, by itself, creates a lot of positive feelings from within.

    From there, politics take over. Elections you will vote to show that you want a govt which can not only continue to run the country- not control- in an effective manner, but also care for the citizens of the country as well. Eventually most of your family members and your relatives will vote because they care as well.

    2) Working culture in Singapore is still about the family-run, monopolistic culture. The bosses only know how to kiss up people they like and kick down people they dislike. People who are the best ass kissers are the ones promoted while those who prefer to be honest and work hard are relegated to the tiny corners of the office to rot.

    It sucks the hell out of a job and is extremely de-motivating to anyone who works in such a culture. What's the point of working in such an environment? Sure the pay is good but if you feel that you hate your fellow colleagues, the bosses doesn't like you and you work for nuts and yet get nothing, then what's the point?

    The working culture in S'pore usually sucks and that's why by liberalising the private sector, we mostly probably will see the 1st few positive steps in re-instating a more open working culture that motivates people more than the exact depressing opposite.

    3) Just read Jack Welch's book, read some US-published magazines and read about the current business scenes in the US and around the world. See how much the people there and internationally have so much choices and so much economic freedom. Everytime in Newsweek and Time, I read about a new young prospective CEO from South Korea or China or the US and everytime I wonder why can't Singapore have one?
