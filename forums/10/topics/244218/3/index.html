    24 Mar 07, 14:02
    At the height of the last recession, we were constantly reminded of the virtues of a "flexible" wage system and how we should have KPIs and move away from a seniority-based system.

    In the last GE, I vaguely remembered an opposition member's suggestion of pegging our minister's pay to 10X the average salary of the lowest 10% of our working class...or something along that line.

    I personally don't think that is a bad idea... but with some refinement.

    1stly, KPIs. As a government, much as the aim is to increase our GDP, it must now also aim to close the gap between the rich and the poor. Just as all organisations use their reward systems to help achieve their organisational goals. these changes as organisational goals changes. Likewise, the remuneration system for the top echelons of our civil service must change to meet this new need.

    2ndly, flexible wage system. How do we fit these KPIs to a fixed and variable component of a flexible wage system? Well, to attract and retain the able to the top echelons, the fixed component needs to be fair, but certainly not exorbitant to the extent of being indecent! Perhaps, the current salary that these people draw can be "defaulted" as the fixed component. Any intended increases should go to the variable component, but tied to the KPI of closing the gap between the rich and the poor. This way, the population can see the "soul" of the people at the top working for them, and will be less resistant to any increase to their remuneration.

    Above is a rather simplistic suggestion, but I think it captures the essence of what the government has been telling its people. When we see them doing what they tell us to do, the support comes naturally.

    I remember in one parliament seating, when an opposition member asked the manpower minister what the governments' long term target was in terms of the unemployment figure, the opposition member was flabbergasted at the go-around he was given and turned to the speaker of the house and said...he did not answer my question! The people are told to have KPIs and have their pay pegged to it. But when we ask for the unemployment KPI, we are not given an answer.

    So, perhaps, this is a good time to show that they can walk their talk.

    Just my thoughts.
