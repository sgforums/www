It should all be done on a basis of equality and if as a meritious student from the poly is poor, there are sufficient scholarships and bursaries available to him. This goes even for uni undergrads.
    No one in S'pore is deprived of a place in poly of uni just cos he/she is poor. And what sort of an argument is that universities are 'too exp' for poly students? Thats rubbish. Poly students pay just as much if not more for their courses. So it all comes down to ability.

    But don't expect the govt to save places for you if you yourself don't make the mark. I refuse to accept that fixed quotas should be set aside for poly grads = this smacks of affirmative action and flies in the face of meritocracy.
    I mean, if the person in question is floundering already in poly, why should he be given a place in the university? Isn't he overstretching his ability?

    So, places to those with merit. Financial status is a non-issue here cos the gove will provide - as long as your results are good enough. And lets stop all this 'who is better' bickering.
