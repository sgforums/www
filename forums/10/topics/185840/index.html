  • BillyBong
    11 Apr 06, 18:22
    Performance bonus is quote simply: quantum bonus (fixed lump sum) + variable wage bonus.

    It can be presented in various forms but the above is the general concept.

    Quantum bonus is a fixed value, usually the 13th month for every employee, while the variable wage is the profit sharing scheme based entirely on annual performance.

    Of course, such a move is good as it rewards those consistent performers who meet their set targets.

My take on our pro-employers faulty wage system shafted down our throat is like this:

    CPF

    Currently, the employers' contribution is 13%, now think....if you were an employer, with a budget to pay a security guard $1000 per mth and knowing you would have to contribute 13% of $1000 = additional $130 ie $1130, would not that exceed your budget? So, the next best thing to do is to suppress your budget further to help 'absorb' the CPF contribution right?
    In other words, pay the worker less so that : pay+CPF=$1000 nett. Now that makes economic sense! CPF have done a great job and suckers like us think our employers are helping us to pay for our leasehold properties.

    Performance Bonus

    one word : arbitrary. There is no clear system to determine a tranparent scoring system that fairly grade each worker. From my former experience it is a seniority based system, tainted in secrecy, one sided and it encourage wayang in the pressence of the reporting officer. (here i am referring to occupations that do not have figures to justify or measure, for instance, sale figures) Companies usually fix a certain budget regardless of the number of staff, so only a small % of staff gets it. No need to perform ... senoir staff will take credits for your ideas.

    Variable Component
