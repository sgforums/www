    A thought just occurred to me.

    A while back, when these issues first began to heat up I remember reading a political analyst's view on how the negotiations were being conducted. Basically what he said was that Malaysia and Singapore were approaching the issues from 2 very different angles. Whereas Singapore was all formal and to the letter, Msia had always preferred to be less formal and more ad hoc in its approach. I remembr thinking then... 'What the f**k?? More ad hoc?? More informal?? We're talking about governments here not the local town hall meeting...'

    Fast forward to today.

    The same things are again plaguing both countries. And I have a view to offer. If any of u out there are diplomats then please share your views...

    Msians have always had a culture of being a bit more, for lack of a precise term, "easy going" when dealing with friends or a friend of a friend. What I mean is, if you're familiar with the guy or he's in a higher position or equal to you, then the rules wont strictly apply to you. Its a way of life i think. You will go easy on the guys you know but be strict to a stranger that comes to you off the street. Some of you out there may be repulsed by this idea and equal it to cronyism or even accuse it as being corruption. Granted, in large doses or when assisting illegal activity, it is obviously wrong. Im not here to debate the correctness of these actions. However, to them, this is a sign of respect and friendliness. After all, we're suposed to be friends and whats the bending of a few rules between friends.

    Therefore Singapore's strict insistence on following everything to the letter strikes Msia as being too rigid and frankly, in their view, is not how friends deal with each other. Friends should be more easygoing and not let 'rules' come too much in the way of things. Maybe thats why they keep getting so angry. It because Singapore does not understand how they perceive things. Singapore is, of course, right in following everything to the letter. All im saying is maybe its precisely this approach thats pissing the Malaysians off. Im not arguing for Singapore to give in to Malaysia. All im saying is maybe a failure to understand where the other side is coming from is the cause of all our problems...

    A japanese friend once told my dad that they likened Malaysia to a big stadium with lots of sports. And the Japanese always made sure they kept every possible sport uniform in their lockers cause today the official sport would be soccer but tomorrow it might be tennis and the next day it might be ping pong. He was referring to the frequent rule changes that occur in Malaysia...and I think he was right...The best example i can give is the constant sway between fousing on the National language and English. They've alternately focused on Malay then English quite a few times i believe...although this time they appear to be serious.

    Sometimes I think the legislators in Malaysia just change the rules, wait to see what happens then if they dont work...change em till they do. Being ad hoc seems to be a way of life and it might be good to remember that when dealing with Malaysia
