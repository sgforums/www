    Dear Qitai,

    It is possible, why not?

    In Taiwan, the election system is divided into two; localized representation and National representation.

    This means that on the localized front, each party provide their own list of candidates and fight for the restricted number of representations allocated to each respective township/constituncy. On the other hand, the individual party will also provide a "National" list of candidates whom will be allocated on the aggregate votes each party gets from the poll; there is a limit in the number of seats allocated to this "National representation".

    This means that even if certain constituencies are not being contested, the voters there still could vote, to choose the National representatives.

    It would mean that nobody would be left out in the electorate process; this is the true spirit of citizens taking ownership of the country, via voting.

    Goh Meng Seng
