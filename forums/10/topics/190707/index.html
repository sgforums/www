    With the Ruling Party garnering only 66.6% from the contested wards, there remains the other wards with 32 Parliamentary Seats that were uncontested.

    The " overwhelming suppport " claimed is against the actual count of votes that reflect a decline - (when compared to the 2001 results) - for the Prime Minister's own standing in his leadership of the Party, and as the Prime Minister of Singapore, and in his anchor role for his GRC.

    From the records of the Workers' Party, the following average of votes won in each election will show that there remains a consistent number of supporters for the Opposition in the contested wards.

    GE 1972 24.5% average of all valid votes in 27 single seat ward
    GE 1976 30.7% average of all valid votes in 22 single seat ward
    GE 1980 29.7% average of all valid votes in 8 single seat ward
    GE 1984 41.3% average of all valid votes in 15 single seat ward
    GE 1988 36.5% average of all valid votes in 5 GRCx3 and 14 single seat
    GE 1991 39.7% average of all valid votes in 2 GRCx4 and and 5 single seat
    GE 1997 40.6% average of all valid votes in 1 GRCx5, 2 GRCx4 and 1 single seat.
    GE 2001 40.6% average of all valid votes in 2 single seat wards
    GE 2006 39.7% average of all valid votes in 2 GRCx5, 1 GRCx6 and 4 single seats.

    The percentages shown are the average of valid ballots received by the Workers' Party, and in the wards which they have fought for; and despite the increase in the number of candidates, the Workers' Party has managed to maintain their average over the last 8 General Elections.

    The 2006 average results showed the Workers' Party holding to their average score despite having 20 candidates - with two groups in a 5 member GRC, and one group in a 6 member GRC.

    The earlier years showed a smaller number of candidates being fielded, especially with the smallest fielding of only two candidates in GE 2001.

    Why should we doubt the ability of the candidates from the Opposition to run Singapore ?

    MM LKY and his first generation of founding fathers - had zero experience when they took over the reins of government, and with MM LKY breaking down in 1965 - when Singapore was ousted from Malaysia; leaving Dr Toh Chin Chye and Dr Goh Keng Swee to run Singapore without LKY.

    Everyone of us have an innate sense of organising, multi-tasking, systematic and disciplined thought process, a good dose of forward looking ability, and a clear mind of the goals ahead for Singaporeans and Singapore.

    Given the responsiblity, Singaporeans have the ability to quickly learn the ropes and fill into the larger size shoes - as can be seen in the many young and old Singaporeans having the ability to learn on the job, improvise, brain storm, and move ahead with diligences and care.

    It is no different in governing a country, in which the Leader or Leaders will have to quickly organise a team of visionaries and clear headed thinkers, to brainstorm on the many issues in taking over and running the Government.

    A new system will need to be set in place, where non-sensitive government information is shared amongst all Members of Parliament - from both sides of the ailse.

    This is to allow intelligent debate to be made; and where a shadow cabinet is formed so as to ensure stability for Singapore whenever there is a transfer of powers in a change of political party to form the government.
