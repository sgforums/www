    Mah Bow Tan: We are a “land-locked” city state

    Just when Singaporeans are crying out for help to bring down the sky-rocketing prices of HDB flats, National Development Minister Mah Bow Tan talked about Singapore’s “biodiversity” instead.

    During a speech made at the launch of the “Forest of Giants” yesterday, Mr Mah claimed that Singapore has been “successful” in balancing development and conservation. 

     ”…we are a very small city state, land locked; development is important for us, but at the same time, we have to make every effort to conserve what we have. So, this balance, this tussle between development and conservation is even more acute in a city state like ours,” he was quoted as saying in Channel News Asia.

    It is not known how relevant the topic of conservation is to ordinary Singaporeans who are increasingly priced out of the public housing market.

    Mr Mah has come under fire lately from disgruntled home buyers for failing to control HDB flat prices which hit a record high last year though he admitted that he was caught “off-guard” by the phenomenal growth in prices.

    Despite evidence showing otherwise, Mr Mah is adamant that HDB flats remain affordable to Singaporeans and castigated home buyers for being “fussy” and having “unrealistic” expectations.

    Mr Mah’s nonchalant demeanour appeared to suggest that he remained unfazed by the rising chorus of discontent from the ground.

    He is paid about $2 million dollars a year, or more than 4 times the annual salary of U.S. President Barack Obama and is expected to get another 8.8 percent pay hike this year.

    With the property market booming and “good times” returning, Mr Mah can well afford to dwell on “biodiversity” and “conservation for now.
