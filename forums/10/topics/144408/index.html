    LAND TRANSPORT AUTHORITY (LTA) PRESS RELEASE
    Revised ERP rates at Dunearn Road Gantry and implementation of ERP on Northbound CTE
    26 July 2005

    Traffic conditions on the northbound CTE have been deteriorating since 2000. Even after the stretch of the expressway between the PIE and Ang Mo Kio Avenue 1 was widened, congestion continued to persist especially during the evening peak hours. The average travel speed from 6.30pm to 8.00pm is below 40 km/h, which is well below the optimal speed range. As such, ERP will be introduced to alleviate congestion on the expressway. Motorists heading north can consider alternatives, including travelling at a different time, using public transport, or using alternative routes such Thomson Road and Upper Serangoon Road.

    ERP Rate Review Criteria

    LTA reviews the traffic conditions on the expressways and roads in the Restricted Zone (RZ) and Radial Roads where the ERP system is in operation, every quarterly and for the June and December school holidays.

    Following each review, the ERP rates will be adjusted where necessary. This is to achieve optimal traffic flow on our expressways and roads, by maintaining a speed range of 45km/h to 65km/h for expressways and 20km/h to 30 km/h for arterial roads.

    Next ERP Rate Review

    The next revision of ERP rates will take place in November 2005 for the fourth quarterly ERP Rate Review for year 2005.
    For full details and price variations, refer to : http://www.asiaone.com.sg/a1news/a1_20050726_001.html
