    Statistics have shown that crime rates for the first half of the year had increased. The crimes committed by teenagers (mainly 14-16 years old) had shown increment; theft in schools, public area etc; vandalising and fighting.

    Schools ought to enforce stricter rules and regulations to aid in disciplining the students and teaching them moral values at the same time as well.

    It's now very common to see school students, mainly from secondary schools, sporting fanciful and coloured hairstyles. A poll had shown that almost 6 out of 10 teenagers engage in smoking activities, today.

    The basic etiquette of a student wearing his uniform had long disappeared. Students no longer tuck in their school uniforms and wearing baggy school uniforms are not scarce either.

    Schools should educate their students not only academically, but instilling moral values as well. More advertisement and talks regarding the negative effects in smoking should be conducted for the good sake of our future leaders of Singapore.

    Parents expressed serious difficulty in handling their children. In addition, teenagers nowadays are much open-minded; it's not surprising to read from the newspaper that a 15-year-old girl is no longer a virgin.

    As almost half of a teenager's day is spent in school, schools have the responsibility to mould these students into someone with good character, values and discipline.

    It is time for the MOE and school staffs to start enforcing discipline, or few years later, policemen will be asked to be Discipline Masters in schools! (similar to most schools in the US and Britain)
