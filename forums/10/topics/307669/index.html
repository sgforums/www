    Growth Dividends for S'poreans

    SINGAPORE: The government is to give Growth Dividends to all adult Singaporeans, to be paid out in two instalments in April and October this year.

    This was announced by Finance Minister Tharman Shanmugaratnam in his Budget Statement on Friday.

    A lower-income Singaporean living in a three-room HDB flat or smaller, will receive a Growth Dividend of $400. This is on top of the $250 in GST Credits that he will be getting this year.

    The majority of Singaporeans, who live in other HDB flats and do not have high incomes, will receive a Growth Dividend of $300 (on top of $200 in GST Credits that he will get this year).

    Older Singaporeans, those aged 60 and above, will get more. Most older Singaporeans will receive one and a half times what other Singaporeans will receive.

    Those with annual incomes more than $100,000 will receive a Growth Dividend of $100.

    Those who have served and are currently serving national service will get an additional $100 of Growth Dividends, to recognise their contributions to the nation.

    As before, Singaporeans can donate their Growth Dividends to charity.

    The Growth Dividends will cost the government $865 million this year.
