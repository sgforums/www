    Several reasons why guns are still in use...

    1.) Note with main-gun launching systems. Missiles have delicate electronics... meaning that the powerful recoil of a heavy tank gun can knock the sensitive calibration out of the window.

    2.) Missiles are expensive... as CX has explained.

    3.) Missiles can be decoyed or destroyed in mid-flight... a 120mm sabot round will ignore flares, chaff and possible Vulcan-type anti-missile miniguns to score on the bad guy.

    4.) Missiles are limited to HEAT and HESH type warheads. HEAT penetration capability depends on diameter of the shaped charge, direction of the shaped charge, and number of warheads... meaning that there is going to be a point where the warhead is just too thick to be practical. The sabot round penetration power depends on velocity and angle of strike. And the tank gun allows you to use HEAT and HESH too!

    5.) Missiles bring less good-stuff (warhead payload) to the bad guy. That's because they need space for propellant (which needs to be hauled by the missile in flight). The tank shell (once in flight and un-saboted) is 100% full of happiness and joy headed for the bad guy.

    Sidenote: Btw... HEAT and HESH don't perform well in rifled barrels... The spin actually diminishes their pentration power. So you need free-floating driving bands when shooting HEAT and HESH... whcih sometimes still get stuck when fired in a fouled barrel! That is why the Americans chose to use 120mm smoothbores in their M1A1 and M1A2's. The Brits stuck with rifled barrels in their Challenger 2's, preferring the longer range and accuracy (debatable).

    Why missiles then?

    The one really good point for missiles: They can be adjusted in mid-flight. Which means two things... You can still get the target despite a lousy initial aim, and you can run away if the missile is fire and forget!

    15 Oct 02, 09:35
    According to what I've researched, HEAT and HESH can be weakened if the projectile is spinning...

    For HEAT, the shaped-charge HEAT jet needs time to form... If the projectile is spinning when the shaped charge detonates, the jet (which is mostly gas) tends to scatter more. For HESH, the same thing happens, spreading the same amount of HE over a larger area and reducing the explosive power/armour area affected (thus reducing chances of a big piece of spall being detached from the interior).

    Smoothbores are supposedly less accurate than rifles. Same principle as muskets versus rifles... spinning-stablises the (solid) round and makes it go further.

    And yes, I was talking about firing shells from smoothbores, not missiles when I referred to range. That M256 120mm Rheinmetall gun on the M1A1 Heavy Armour (served in Gulf War) happens to have a very nice targeting computer with wind, temp, etc. the whole works... thus enabling a nice first-shot strike at long range. And the fins also help out in stablising the round (you're talking about a long thin heavy metal dart... think medieval arrow.)

    Finally, my posts ain't about proving anyone wrong... We're all mature here, and I'm not trying to propose a whip-it-out-and-slam-table competition. So relax mate!
