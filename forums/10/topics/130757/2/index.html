    Originally posted by kopiosatu:
    what's the point of comparing?

    what matters now is that why the current bugger doesn't seem to do anything. I don't even know if he's still alive.
    The Position of President of Singapore is now different from those days in Sheares or Yusok Ishak. It is an elected position commanding its own constitution authority.

    The president owes a duty even though he was not democratically elected by the people to discharge his responsibility to the people of Singapore. He can carry on what ex-president Ong Teng Cheong has started but failed to accomplish - ask for major assets e.g. foreign and GLC investments to be valued once a year.

    This is the type of elected president Singapore needs. PMO has 6 ministers and many senior civil servants set up to look after GIC and oversee asset surpluses costing taxpayers millions.

    I am sure even if PM has not instructed anyone to keep updated records of assets, the president on his own sense of duty can do so without further asking. To this day, what OTC has asked for was not wrong except that the tasks of valuating all assets or major assets might take time.

    OTC's intention and objective were truly admirable and correct. The incumbent party was short-sighted not to see the positive contribution of the elected president's office for their future generation's sake. The party wants to win over the young educated and elites. Can the party walk tall among the people they are claiming to lead as their sheeps ? Prove to the young educated elites that the party is pro-Singapore and will always do things for Singapore's interest in any event party and state interests are at odds or differ.

    So I ask: the current president to carry on his contributions in his own way i.e. to establish his office as elected president for future generation's sake so that the assets can be guarded against possible abuse and the 2nd key can be properly used to open the asset box for assistance to singaporeans when the crisis comes.
