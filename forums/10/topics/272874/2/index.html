    Every hard working individual who migrated here, and who had an iota of entrepreneurial imagination and daring had made it good - largely due to the latitude of political and economic space that prevailed under Colonial Rule.

    Tan Lark Sye saw the opportunities in rubber while being a laborer and worked to establish his own company. Not forgetting the other personality named Tan Kah Kee, and some other famed Chinese family names from the Chan's, the Lee's, and the See's - all having made their mark as the Straits Chinese.

    As much as the Tan's featured prominently in the Chinese migrant community, the Indian migrants also have their famous sons in the likes of Naraina Pillai, and another famous personality in P. Govindasamy - both of whom seized the opportunities that came their way and grew with the open economy promoted by the Colonial Government.

    There were also the famed Arab personalities that settled in Singapore and provided us with their names to famous present day landmarks - Aljunied, Alkaff, and Alsagoff.

    All had large families and life was as rewarding as it was challenging - even when they live under Colonial Rule - with no financial or special support to local traders, nor will they compete directly with the local traders.

    Jobs were bountiful as Singapore thrived during the days from Stamford Raffles till the outbreak of WW2 - the economic progress being due to the latitude of space made available by the hands-off approach of the Colonial Government.

    After WW2, it provided new impetus to get rich as opportunities abound in the many reconstruction works that had to be carried out by the Colonial Government.

    Can the same be said of Singapore since self-government under ambitious politicians with their own personal agenda to take over the seat of Power ?

    Every ridiculous reasons were given for the ''Family Planning-Stop at 2'' program similar to the impetuous reasons given in support of NKF - despite the voices of caution and restrain in matters that bore long term consequences.

    The early voices of caution were dismissed and the Family Planning Program was implemented with religious fervor, and continued into the late 1970s.

    It was only around the mid-1980S that it dawned on the self-proclaimed talented elites that NSF recruitments for the SAF was experiencing a critical progressive decline in available MAN-power.

    Were the job situation in the mid-1980S any better then in Colonial Times, with the Singapore economy hurtling towards the 1987 economic downturn ?

    During the mid-1980S, the Private Business Community were reeling with the purposeful drive by the Singapore Talented Elite to increase workers' wages, and the Business Community had called on the Self-acclaimed Elites to make a U-Turn to their misguided wage policies.

    The famous ''NO U-Turn mentality'' of the Talented Elite was the subject of some intellectual discourse by none other than Sim Wong Hoo.

    Even LKY had admitted that on hindsight perhaps the alternative voices could have been given some latitude in space in the scheme of things during the 1960s - but then, everyone was groping around without experience; and someone had to make a decision to move, even if it is a wrong decision.

    Now we are saddled with a declining population as was accurately predicted by the alternative voices of caution during the 1960s - cautionary voices that objected to the all encompassing and indiscriminate implementation of the Family Planning Program.
