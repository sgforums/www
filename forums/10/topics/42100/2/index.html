    It is always tough to understand the purpose of everything or anything that is being asked of us to do, when we are stuck in active NS.

    From charging up and down the hills, or one clearing enemy position from one hill top to the next; or sweeping big or small stones with big or small brooms, stand-by beds, washing toilets, cutting grass - they may all look stupid or crazy.

    Once you are out of the two year or for some the extra half year, and when you look back, you will be wondering how you manage to have survived through all the bullying, the stupidity of all the muscle and bone breaking charging, running and marching.

    Believe this, when you are alone in a trouble spot - like in Cambodia, when riot suddenly burst out without warning, you will appreciate every bit of training that you could still remember.

    Such a situation can easily burst out even in a Tokyo subway, or in New York City, or even in Hamburg, or Rome.

    In life, things will never work out the way you dream about, it is how you shape your mental attitude to changed environment that will make you a better person.

    Shape the broom to make it sweep better.

    Show that you are enjoying the pain that is given out to you, and soon enough either you break the instructor, or he breaks your spirit.

    It is a challenge to find out your threshold, and if you live through it, you will have alot to talk about when you finished your two or half year more of NS.

    When you have so much to talk about, it will not be such a waste of time.
