    5. Social services - charities, community development, NGOs.
    6. Security
    7. Cleaning services.
    8. Advertising industry:- design, production, events.
    9. Media services
    10. Legal services

    12. Postal services
    13. Research and developement
    14. Euducation/office supplies
    15. Household repairs - sanitation and electrical

     

    B. Industries that will face a slowdown in a crisis:-

     

    1. Banking:- but will revert to its traditional role and deal less in exotic financial products.
    2. Logistic services including private transportation
    3. Standard Aviation services
    4. Marine and Port services.
    5. Entertainment services
    6. Construction
    7. Engineering

     

    C. Industries that will remain stagnant


    1. Property
    2. Insurance
    3. Vehicle trade inclusive of repairs.
    4. Retail
    5. Non-essential item production - snack foods, restaurants, foodcourt, etc
    6. Electronic manufacturing industry
    7. Tourism
    8. Investments

     

    D. Industries to watch out for:-

     

    These industries will be the ones which will lift economies out of the crisis and will be tech-driven, but still under development, underwraps and yet to be mass marketed.


    1. Products ( Scientific ) that will improve our health
    2. Products that will be energy efficient, that are cheap and readily adopted by masses.
    3. Products (Gadgets) that will improve our productivity
    4. Exploration with emphasis on finding raw resources, inclusive of planetary bodies.

     

    This list is by no means exhaustive, but the point is - ensure you go for courses which will enhance your employablity in this current crisis.
