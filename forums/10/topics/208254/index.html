PROPOSAL TO REVAMP THE CONSTITUTION

    The Constitution usually sets out the basic rights of citizens and defines the role of government and its component branches.

    To be competitive, it should not just define the rights of citizens, but protect the rights against interference by government in power.

    It should define larger aspects of nation building and set up the larger roles of the government and put up appropriate safeguard to prevent abuse and motivate all citizens to participate in social and cultural and all aspects of nationhood.

    A forward-looking constitution will lead a to more competitive nation building. This requires the spelling out of roles, duties and responsibilities of government and creating the necessary conditions for motivating and empowering social, cultural and moral growth of society and promotion and nurturing of enterprises and citizenship.

    A more general constitution on the other hand may lead to conflicts and disputes among component of government leading to complexities and uncertainties and instability.

    There should be a proper balance between the components and providing for the necessary conditions supportive of development of active citizenship.

    While the government has the duty to establish law and order and enforce them to attract investments and stable growth, it has the larger roles to play to bring about economic competitiveness and vibrancy with the participation of its people.

    Any lack of definition of roles may lead to abuse and over-controls, over-regulations and over-legalistic passing of laws and enforcement resulting in autocracy and stifling of creativity and enterprises of society.

    Should the constitution serve the larger long-term interest of the state or the people - to the greatest good for the greatest number?

    The answer is obvious isn't it? Government is elected to serve the people to look after their interests and wellbeing through :

    (1) administration,
    (2) justice,
    (3) maintaining democratic election processes
    (4) implementing policies and decisions in clearly defined objective work processes with accountability and responsibility seen to be promoting citizens' social, cultural and economic vibrancy and competitiveness.

    Specifically the constitution would establish three branches of government: (a) An effective executive, (b) Independent legislature, (c ) An independent judiciary. Each of the three branches of government should be supported by a non-aligned civil service in the interest of all citizens.

    When such a constitution is established, there will be cooperations, coordination and efficiency and all the government policies and decisions will then gain the fullest support and participation of its citizens in nation building ?

    The existing constitution is too general leaving the executive branch too much discretionary power to pass laws and regulations to help itself in avoiding accountability and curtail people's right to speak up, and participate in governing policies, appointments of government etc.

    Read more about the current shortcomings e.g. "20 major policy errors of governemnt" as posted to the feedback unit.

    If the government wants to build a cosmopolitan and inclusive society then it must go beyond pro-exectuive top-down leadership-centric pretentious and hypocritic window dressing and statistical presentations.

    There is a need to recognise that any form of legalistic wrangling of laws and regulations like the laws of election GRCs will be counter-productive and abhorent to the long-term goal and interest and will lead ultimately to instability.

    There is obviously a need to balance between law and order and empowering of citizens' collective talents, coordination, knowledge applications and innovation in every field, the key to the people's vibrancy and competitiveness.
