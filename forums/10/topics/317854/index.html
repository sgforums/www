Project WiTHIN (On Self-Esteem and Anorexia Nervosa)

  • Project WiTHIN
    17 May 08, 18:29

    Hello everyone!

    Project WiTHIN is an awareness project that seeks to spread awareness about anorexia nervosa, as well as to encourage students, especially teenage girls, to be more confident and comfortable with their own looks and bodies, looking beyond the physical construction of their bodies, and into their mind, heart and soul. Through research, we also realised that family members and friends play an integral role in motivating a teenage girl to value her character over mere exterior traits or characteristics.

    We were featured on Youth.SG (Link: http://www.youth.sg/content/view/3442/69/) last year. Also, The Straits Times has published articles detailing our project, on Mind Your Body, 5th September 2007 (Link: http://www.projectthin.com/5Sept2007.jpg/) and The Sunday Times, 28th October 2007 (Link: http://www.projectthin.com/28Oct2007.png/) respectively. Recently, on 14th May 2008, we were featured in Lian He Zao Bao under the ‘Popcorn (Bao Mi Hua)’ section.

    Around the world, more and more teenage girls are increasingly desirous of changing their physical appearances. It has been suggested that globalisation has led Singapore teenage girls to inherit Western notions of beauty, as well as the underlying prerequisite: thinness. Evidently, 70% of Singaporean women who took part in a survey by DOVE strongly purported that: “I wish the media did a better job in portraying women of diverse physical characteristics”.

    There are three major causes of the lack of awareness of anorexia and self-esteem issues amongst Singaporeans.

    1. The media. Singaporean teenage girls are disillusioned by the perception that the extent of thinness is indefinitely correlated with the degree of beauty.
    2. The proliferation of new media has seen the rise of pro-anorexia communities on the internet, where teenage girls openly discuss starvation techniques and influence individuals to hop on the bandwagon.
    3. We have also discovered that no organisations have tackled the problem of anorexia per se. Even though Unilever (DOVE) has developed the BodyTalk workshops to encourage re-evaluation of perception towards beauty, the themes of the workshops are often too wide, placing lesser emphasis on the problem of anorexia.

    Do you agree with us that these are the reasons that are causing the increased incidence of anorexia nervosa in Singapore? What other initiatives do you think we should implement? Share your views with us!
