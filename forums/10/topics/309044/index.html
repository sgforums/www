    Singapore's minister of state for the ministry of education and manpower, Gan Kim Yong, told the country's parliament yesterday that strong economic growth had led to record highs in local employment.

    Mr Yong made the assurance in response to a question from Madam Ho Goek Choo about how employment of local people could be enhanced, according to TodayOnline.com.

    Although the number of foreign workers in Singapore is at all-time high, with 61 per cent of all jobs going to a non-national, the resident unemployment rate hit a record low of 2.3 per cent in December, Mr Yong noted.

    He added that there were 92,100 more local people in employment last year, which is two or three times the annual average increase.

    "Over the last few years, our experience has shown that we have been able to adapt quickly to changes in the economic environment and we have continued to diversify our sources of foreign workers," the minister said.

    A recent report revealed that in Singapore's manufacturing sector, which accounts for a quarter of the economy, production rose by 6.9 per cent in 2007 on the previous year.
