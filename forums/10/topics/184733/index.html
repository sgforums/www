  • Atobe
    2 Apr 06, 14:21

    In the recent Mandarin forum on Channel 8, broadcast on Saturday - amongst the panel of participants were some selected Singaporeans from all walks of life - including Jack Neo.
    Mr Jack Neo, Film Director, says: "I want to ask the Prime Minister whether you think Singaporeans have enough money?"

    Mr Lee says: "I think many Singaporeans have a feeling that they don't have enough money. Why?
    I don't know. You are an expert in the matter, so you should know better." - CNA/de
    How could the PM know if Singaporeans have any feeling that we don't have enough money, when his needs are all taken care of as a PM, and with a joint income with his wife that will total over $2 Million a year at least, and with him never experiencing what poverty and tight family budget is about throughout his life?

    Was there an answer given to that Jack Neo Question ?

    But he stressed that discussions on politics must be taken seriously, not as jokes, as in the case in some countries in the West .

    Speaking at a Chinese current affairs forum organised by MediaCorp TV Channel 8, he said that countries can become unstable if political figures are not given basic respect and acceptance.

    Said Mr Lee, "Politics needs a proposition and an opposition. Disagreements and different opinions are acceptable, but we must treat the subject seriously. We can keep it informal, like today's discussion; this is informal." - CNA /ct
    Is this not an irony that PM LHL will speak of "politics must be taken seriously, not as jokes - when in Singapore, POLITICS as practised by the Ruling Political Party IS A JOKE ?

    Is it surprising that PM LHL will demand that political figures need to be "given basic respect and acceptance" - when the Ruling Political Party has never given any respect nor acceptance of Opposition Parties and their leadership - with his father (MM LKY) giving the best virtuoso performance in deriding JBJ ?

    Does PM LHL truly believe that "Politics needs a proposition and an opposition" , or is this mere rhetoric ?

    The manner in which the Ruling Political Party treated WITHOUT ANY RESPECT the Workers' Party Manifesto clearly shows the standard of politics that the Ruling Political Party is capable of - and if that itself is not a JOKE, what possibly can the future hold for Politics in Singapore ?
