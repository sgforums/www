    extracted from straits times.

    I REFER to the letter, 'Are Singaporeans ready to defend country?' (ST, Aug 12), by Mr Lee Chuan Yaw.
    I am 20 and a third sergeant serving national service in the field defence squadron in Paya Lebar Air Base.

    I remember when I was training in the School of Infantry Specialists (Sispec), one of our platoon commanders, Warrant Officer Lam, told us this during a lecture: if every man protects his family, ultimately they would protect the whole nation.

    While there may be a few instances of Singaporeans trying to evade national service, there are also many others who would be ready to pick up a rifle and head for the front line to protect their families.

    In fact, at Sispec I met many warrant officers who loved the country so much that they would do just that.

    There were commandos and guards, and I was greatly inspired by them because I have a family and a girlfriend and I want to protect them too.

    I can safely say that Singaporeans can trust in our soldiers and these officers to lead our soldiers into battle.

    I would like Mr Lee to know that I am willing to fight and die for Singapore if there is ever a need to.

    I will always remember the last line of the Singapore Armed Forces pledge, which states: 'I will defend Singapore with my life.'

    Dominic Leong Hui Han

    NATION ABOVE ALL
