    By S Ramesh | Posted: 30 December 2011 1600 hrs

    SINGAPORE: The committee set up to review political salaries submitted its report to the Prime Minister on Friday afternoon.

    This was confirmed by its chairman, Gerard Ee, on Friday.

    The government says it is studying the report.

    Channel NewsAsia understands that the report is expected to be made public in early January next year.

    It also understands from several Members of Parliament that the report is expected to be debated in Parliament soon.

    Next month could see two sessions of Parliament - one on January 9 as announced by the Clerk of Parliament and a second sitting on January 16.

    The committee to review political salaries was set up after Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong announced the decision to do so in his speech at the swearing-in ceremony of the new Cabinet at the Istana on May 21 this year.

    - CNA
