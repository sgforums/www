    Seriously I don't think the anti-piracy activities will benefit companies such as Microsoft, Adobe, and Macromedia in the long run. It will instead, tip in favor of Linux, and Sun Microsystems and other freeware companies. Why?

    Let's see the minds of the people who buys pirated stuff: "I will never buy original stuff. So expensive!!". So even if piracy was eradicated, they would never ever convince / persuade these people to buy their original goods, as what they want are goods as cheap as possible. Well, maybe initially they will buy those original goods, but I can say, 5 - 10 years from now, if Linux, a freeware OS, becomes even more user-friendly, many people will flock to it as it is nearly free. And oh of course, StarOffice. It is free to download, a small fee if you want it by post. When they become more powerful in features, you will know what will happen.

    Another thing: Right now, the average age of having an advanced IT knowledge is getting smaller and smaller. However, if the government's wish is to educate all students on IT, I think the wish would be dead. To enable the students to have a greater knowledge of IT means that the students should have the necessary programs installed IN THEIR COMPUTER, such as Adobe and Macromedia. However the parents of the students do not understand the importance of the programs and even if they do, they would have no money to buy. Look, Adobe and Macromedia products have unrealistically high prices. How are the students to get them into their computer? They have absolutely no way to do it except buying pirated CDs.

    I am not a avid supporter of pirated CDs, but when piracy was totally eradicated, this will be the destiny: Big Companies will lose money, Freeware Companies will gain revenue. However, this destiny can be avoided only if the companies reduce their prices, not by little, but by lots.
