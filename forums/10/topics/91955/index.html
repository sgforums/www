    The high profile reporting will result in more attempts to do so.

    Copycat suicides
    Suicide rates are influenced by publicity about suicide of famous people, and even the fictional suicide of a character in a popular drama can raise the suicide rate temporarily.

    Copycat suicides are defined as duplications of suicides due to repeated accounts or depictions of the original suicide on television and in other media. The well-publicized suicide serves as a model, in the absence of protective factors, to the next suicide. They occasionally spread, like wildfire, through a school system, through a community, or in terms of a celebrity suicide wave, nationally.

    Therefore it is customary in some countries that media do not report suicides, except in special cases.

    In the book, Suicide Clusters (Boston/London: Faber and Faber, 1987), by Loren Coleman, many specific examples of copycat suicides are given.

    A new culture needs to be fostered with regards to courtesy on the MRT lines - not just to reduce the number of accidents on the train service, but as a general improvement to the quality of experience for all the commuters concerned.

    People like to stand near the edge of the platform, even when a train is a few minutes away. The waiting lines drawn on the platform itself only serves as a marker for people to cloister around, since it is also a direct indication of where the doors of the train would be in open-air stations.

    The stubborn-mindness of the bee-hive collective usually causes all to throw caution to the wind, and crowd even closer to the train doors even before the train comes to a complete halt. The train could suddenly accelerate, and cause people to be sucked into the tracks should they go too near (Benoulli effect). Also, blocking the train doors prevent alighting passengers from making their way out of the train itself, and forces them to elbow their way through the relentless crowd. Such inconsideration leads to stress for everybody concerned.

    A better solution needs to be found to this bad culture on our train service. A good early education on coutesy not only helps save lives (you would probably not fall into the tracks so easily if you avoid standing near the edge of the platform), but also promotes an easier commuting experience for all the passengers concerned. Given the extreme cost of private transport in Singapore, trains are an inevitability in life. Let's make it a better experience for everyone.
