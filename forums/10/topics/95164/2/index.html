    To: Sibeisuaykia,

    There are possibly two ways of awarding talents (generalisation) assuming that there are such a thing as talents:-

    (1) Recognize that people are different and some have more talents than others. President or an independent panel be advised to appoint specific person of calibre to be ministers or senior civil servants (salaries may be higher than ministers') needed by Singapore based on his past track records and qualifications.

    Singapore can have all the talents it wants on a case-by-case basis and there should be no objection from anyone to pay such people higher pays on substantiated ground. By adopting such balanced or objective approach, Singapore can still attract many talents it wants or needs without opening the floodgates to award everyone higher pay based on positions. (Democracy at work) or;

    (2) Make general statements or all kinds of policies based on talents or meritocracy, pay everyone equal based on positions, (assuming all are talents) and uplift all salary scales to retain that one or two talent, create promotions and increase pays of all positions across the board.

    Singapore might be following the (2) approach for too many years resulting in everyone being presumed to be talents.

    At the end, we are over-doing things. In the Asian Financial Crisis and last two recessions, our so-called talents were too helpless to do anything and could not come up with plans to overcome high costs affecting our competitiveness.

    All non-talents too are regarded as talents and awarded promotions or higher salaries presumed or premised on such talent or meritocracy policies to retain or attract talents resulting in our high costs.
