    If US President is to peg his pay to Bill Gate or other chief executives in US corporation, US president should be paid US100 million a year or more.

    CEOs and others in the private sector are not apple-for-apple comparison with that of public sector. Reasons are many such as the following:-

    (1) Corporate skills are much more wider-ranging in nature. Ministers placed in charge of corporations may flop in their jobs and cause corporations to lose monies or collapse.

    (2) Many private sector's CEOs and bosses are entrepreneurs using their own funds (not tax payers' monies) to generate wealths or salaries for themselves. They can pay any amounts to themselves if they want to.

    (3) Ministers and MPs have gone back to private sectors but could not actually justify their capability to earn the same salaries as paid while working as ministers.

    (4) Stock brokers and property developers were subjected to economic downturns and their salaries and earnings have gone down by recent stock crashes. Did their ministers take this fact into account in working out comparisons with private sector's salaries.

    (5) The only rationale for giving higher salaries to ministers and Singapore is to avoid corruptions. Is this working effectively ? What about the correspondingly pay increases across the board for the entire civil service. Such a policy of paying salaries to equate to private sector drove up Singapore costs affecting its economic competitiveness.

    (6) Any business entity whose funds are contributions from members such as clubs, mcst and associations are easier to manage because funds or monies are of no problems as mentioned by senior civil servant Ngiam Tong Down after his retirement and taking up a new position as CEO of HDB Corp. Being easier and not worried about pay cheque, civil servants are not in the same categories as equivalent positions in the private sector. There is no rationale in assuming that civil servants or ministers are doing more difficult jobs.

    Paying high salaries to ministers means that we are going to get good ministers who can solve our present problems with lack of economic recovery by local and smaller businesses.

    Here are some real problems that need to be tackled by all ministers to be worth their world's highest pays.

    Business or economic policies or plans currently are not benefitting the local or small businesses. Many measures might have been introduced to help SMEs but as usual they don't help because of conditions and rules.

However, there is a general tendency to promote businesses based on size or track record. This means that new entrepreneurs cannot enter the market.

    Pro-foreign investment and pro-foreign employment policies did not work during the Asian Financial Crisis and last two recessions in creating jobs or preventing downturns. Policies that are aimed at 100 % compliance with all regulations without discretionary or flexible open categories will continue to stifle growths.

    What rationale is there for continuing with such pro-foreign policies. Appropriately planned in master business plan or strategy, to spin the economy with deregulations and more openning up of closed doors (to small timers and SMEs), all the local companies and small businesses will do well again and the whole population of 3.5 millions will be motivated to work the economy.
