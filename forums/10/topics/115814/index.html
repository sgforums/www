    WP seeks feedback on casino

    THE Workers' Party is seeking feedback on the casino proposal as it intends to have the issue debated in Parliament, the party said in a statement yesterday.

    The views will help the opposition party in 'formulating its policy position on the casino and gambling in general', said the statement by second assistant secretary-general James Gomez.

    Describing it as a 'unique opportunity' for Singaporeans to be involved in policy formulation, it added: 'They will have an opportunity to air their viewpoints on the type of Singapore we and our children want to grow up in.'

    The statement said various arguments have been put forward by Singaporeans on the casino proposal, from the economic benefits arising from tourism to the social fallout caused by gaming.

    It also noted that the Government invited potential investors to submit concept plans by the end of this month for an integrated resort that could feature a casino - and that it was still undecided whether to proceed with the idea.

    The Government has said that it wants to hear all views before making up its mind and that it will not rush into making a decision.

    The Workers' Party statement said the March 5 public consultation exercise will be held at its headquarters at 216-G Syed Alwi Road, #02-03, from 2pm to 5pm.

    The statement added that the public can also send views via e-mail to pcc@wp.org.sg or in writing to PO Box 15, Toa Payoh Central Post Office, Singapore 913101.
