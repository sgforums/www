  • Atobe
    5 Jun 06, 11:45
    CPF require a minimum sum of $90,000 for your Retirement Fund, if your CPF account has more than this amount, the surplus will be paid out by installment at the age of 56 years and 58 years.

    The Retirement Fund of $90,000 will start its payout when you reach the age of 60 until the fund runs dry - then it is good luck to you, as you are on your own.

    Much will depend on what you have done with the balance amount that exceeded the minimum sum of $90,000 and paid out in two installments at the age of 56 and 58.

    To all those who are still below thirty, get a long term insurance program - either life, endowment, or medical when the Premiums are still low; those who past forty will have to be contented that they will pay lesser than those in the fifties; while for the fifties and above, good luck with the Premiums.

    More could have been done by CPF and the Government - if they can pool all the Retirement Fund into one sinking fund National Insurance Scheme, that will balloon the amount that can be made available to each Singaporean until their last day.

    Left to individual resource, the scope is limited.
