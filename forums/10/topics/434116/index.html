    Chan Chun Sing: People belong to the country because they do things for the country

    July 11th, 2011 | Author: Temasek Review

    Despite an earlier backlash over his comments made at a YPAP dialogue recently, unpopular PAP Youth Minister Chan Chun Sing continues to taunt young Singaporeans that they are ‘not doing more’ for their nation.

    In an interview with Straits Times Insight yesterday, Mr Chan said it is the ‘responsibility’ of the younger generation to understand why certain policies are formulated to begin with, as well as the pros and cons of alternatives.

    He believes that ‘a population which wants the Government to do more to engage them must themselves also engage.’

    ‘The larger issue is sense of ownership. People don’t belong to their country because the country gives them things. They belong to the country also because they do things for the country, he added.

    Singaporeans have done many things for their country such as serving National Service, paying income taxes, producing new ‘economic digits’ to keep the GDP growing and keeping their CPF monies in the PAP coffers till they step into their graves, but what has the PAP regime given them?

    Mr Chan has been making ‘waves’ in cyberspace lately with a series of outrageous remarks such as calling on Young PAP members to reclaim cyberspace from the ‘lunatic fringe’ in snide reference to PAP critics.

    Being a former high-ranking general in the army, Mr Chan is hampered by his ‘top-down’ approach and ‘robotic’ style of communication in his efforts to reach out to young Singaporeans who dislike his condescending style.
