    Even one case of maid abuse is one case too many

    I FEEL sick every time I read about a case of maid abuse here and I applaud The Straits Times for highlighting these cases and the plight of maids.

    I further applaud the courts for the tough stance they take regarding maid abuse cases.

    These people have sacrificed so much to come here to earn a small amount of money to help take care of their families back home, yet many of them are treated so badly.

    Maids are human beings like the rest of us, yet some employers treat their pets better than they treat their maids.

    Sure, the abusers are a small minority of employers but, on the other hand, the cases that go before the courts are likely just the tip of the iceberg - many more abuse cases must be occurring, with maids suffering in silence, not knowing what to do.

    In our modern society how can anyone treat someone else inhumanely? Abuse is abuse, whether it is physical, verbal, psychological or not allowing the maid to eat nutritiously.

    There can be no excuse for such abuse, yet many of the accused claim that they are 'depressed', as if this gives them the right to be abusive. Not only are some employers abusive, but we have also probably all witnessed children physically or verbally abusing their maid too.

    Clearly, some people are not fit to employ a maid and should not be allowed to do so.

    While employers have the ability to research into the background of their prospective maid it would appear sensible that some form of checking also be carried out in regard to the employer; to confirm whether the employer and his/her family are fit to employ and take care of a maid in a humane and caring manner.

    As well as jail terms and financial penalties, employers convicted of maid abuse should receive psychological counselling and also be banned from ever hiring a maid again.

    This may sound tough but employers must realise that hiring a maid entails a great deal of responsibility and if they are not capable of being responsible and humane towards their maid then they should not be allowed the privilege of hiring a maid.

    I reiterate that I know that maid abuse here occurs in only a small minority of cases but in today's civilised society even one case is one case too many.

    Bruce William Swales

    Apr 3 Straits Times
