    Singapore made ourselves relevant on the world stage only insofar as our ability to provide a place of safety and tranquility - an oasisi - in this region of political, economic and ethnic instability.

    When this region - especially with our immediate and main neighbors in Indonesia, Malaysia, Thailand, Vietnam and Philippines - flourish and reach the same level of political, economic and ethnic calm and progress, Singapore will have to find a new niche to make ourselves relevant on a new chess game on a new chess board.

    Singapore's unique advantage can easily be emulated when more reasonable and dynamic persons take over the helms of our neighboring countries, and with their citizens exposed to being educated in a global environment, it will not be too difficult to find qualified talents in the same vein as our own talents that consider themselves to be Elites.

    If one look at the second tier of political and economic leadership in the governments of Indonesia, Malaysia, Thailand, Philippines and Vietnam - they are young and brilliantly educated individuals in their mid-40s and are the brains behind the many policies pronounced by their respective leaders at the Front facing the Citizens.

    Give and take another 10 to 20 years, these countries will be infuenced by the push and pulls of the different countries in a ASEAN combined efforts to achieve the declared Regional Goals of economic and political progress from the present status quo.

    Singapore has this window of opportunity to develop our political institutions that will make it independent from the influence of any SINGLE POLITICAL ENTITY - be it a Political Party or a Political Personality.

    There must be an institutionalised process that is transparent and independent from political influences that give more advantage to any single party over the others.

    The citizens of Singapore must be given the same recognition of responsibility and political ability as deserved as Citizens of a declared First World Country - as is the case of the Swiss Citizens, who enjoy political responsibility and respect from the Government.

    The Swiss Government DO NOT assume that it has the Right to decide and determine what is good for the Swiss Citizens.

    Anything that affects the Swiss Citizens immediate and future political, economic, or social conditions are referred to the Swiss Citizens for their decision in an open and transparent referendum.

    The Swiss Citizens will never see a similar Annuitysituation being decided by the Swiss Government and have it forced down their throat as a 'fait accompli'.

    Only a self-serving government will beleive that they have a right to decide all that is good and right for the Citizens that put them in office.
