Work life Balance achievable to just a myth?

  • mulan
    29 Aug 04, 18:31
    Hi,

    Like many I am ecstatic over PM Lee's announcement of a 5-day work week a concrete endorsement to work life balance.

    But I can't help but feel sceptical about the success of pushing for work life balance when the actual implementation is solely reliant on the people in positions of authority in different organisations -- namely our bosses. There are just too many unenlightened bosses out there who believe that productivity and/or customer service will be adversely affected if workers are to work only for 5 days a week.

    What about work load? The past 2 years have been lean and tough years for Singapore economically. As a result, it has been largely an employers' market. Head counts in companies have been cut resulting in 1 person shouldering the workload originally meant for two or more persons and yet drawing just 0.75 person's pay. Staff have been openly threantened with the sack if one dared to complain or not cooperate. Intimidation to the tune of "you can get off the train if you don't wish to ride along" have been just veiled attampts of even more blantant threats. The the economic recovery is yet to be filtered to all levels of the society and unfortunately employers are still king -- the need to motivate, keep staff happy so that they will remain with the company or the mantra of "when the staff is happy the customers will be happy" have become forsaken management fad... like EQ. Bosses couldn't care less as long as there is a long queue of applicants vying for your position.

    My guess is many will feel compelled by societal pressure of some sort to pay lip-service to implementing "Work Life Balance" in their companies/organisations. But in realty they neither believed in it nor is interested to take real concrete actions to materialise this to the benefit of their staff, to their mental and physical health, to their families which should ultimately translate into benefits to the company in terms of great staff loyalty, less absentism, greater effort on the part of staff to do their best for the good of the company, etc... After all if you are not happy or not performing, there are still many eyeing for your job!

    The amount of workload and most importantly how staff oriented is the management is an important variable in the whole equation of making work life balance possible. Sadly, this variable is key but too subjected to the discretion of humans.

    Perhaps, we should make it possible and legal to publicly name and shame bosses who are obstacles to making work life balance possible for staff just like criminals are. For aren't they, bad and slave-driver type of bosses no different from criminals. Overworked staff inevitably carry unfinished work home affecting time for families to bond and that can be one of the many causes of the increasing numbers of marriages breaking down and children neglected or abused and/or becoming delinquents. Many who have had it bad in the office by day couldn't help but bring back the bad vibes home causing misery to all at home -- spouses and children to drift apart emotionally. And of course not to forget many who fell ill succumbing to pressure and the barrage of insults or humiliation one has to face at work and from bosses. So, aren't these kind of bosses doing harm to the society the same way criminals are.

    Please let me know what you think.

    Just some of my thoughts.

    To: Mulan,

    Your comments are quite valid and substantiated by may own actual work experiences.

    Everything that are spelt out in the labor laws are too general. Labour laws are enacted to achieve government social and political agendas and aims. These agenda and aims once written into the laws are hardly changed to meet ground level conditions.

    Various employers are able to short-change employees because there are too many loopholes they could exploit.

    For example, it is very easy for an employer to sack or get rid of workers by contractually terminating their employments as provided for under the contract. The government will not do anything to help a distressed worker whose employment is terminated by way of this contractual clause or by notice as is normally called.

    Employer who want to short-change workers can withold their bonus or 13th month supplement without accountability as of now. Employees who are discriminated will have to sue the employer to get justice as ministry of manpower will say that this is the entitlement or right of employer under present legislation.

    Lastly when employees are given extra workloads due to cut-back of staff, the remaining employees have to bear all the blunt. This aspect of poor labor practices are not handled at all by the MOM at this moment. That is why employees tend to be victimized at this moment and I agree with you on this point.
