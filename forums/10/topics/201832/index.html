Something more uplifting: what do you LIKE about S'pore?

  • blueheeler
    25 Jul 06, 17:05
    So, I've been accused of being thankless when I point out the cracks in the upper tiers of S'pore's powerbase and the flaws in their policies.

    So, to show my appreciation to our little red dot, I'll make a start with stuff that I'm HAPPY about in Singapore:

    1. Clean dependable tap-water ALWAYS. S'pore is the only place in Southeast Asia where I brush my teeth with tap-water.

    2. Reliable power supply. Elsewhere in SEAsia, the norm is for power to come on-and-off ever so often.

    3. Personal safety. I've never felt unsafe anywhere in S'pore after dark, even in seedy Geylang or rowdy Boat Quay. Even in '1st' world countries, there are usually no-go areas after dark.

    4. Being fluent in English. It truly is the international language, and thanks to MOE for teaching me how to speak/write it fluently.

    5. Having a 2nd language. Many of my Aussies, English, American friends are fluent only in English. So they are amazed that I can speak not only English, but Mandarin, chinese dialects, Malay... Thanks to MOE again.

    Anyone to continue?
