    He tries to save a few minutes, but ends up with... 60 STITCHES
    Singaporean robbed of passport, IC, two handphones and $320 cash in JB
    May 15, 2007

    HE took a shortcut and paid dearly for it.

    In trying to shave off a few minutes from his travelling time, Mr Din (not his real name) almost lost his life.

    The Singaporean was robbed and attacked by two men in Johor Baru last Wednesday and received 60 stitches for slashes on his back and head, reported Berita Harian.

    Recalling the dreadful incident, MrDin, 38, said it happened around 7pm when he was riding through a narrow deserted lane to the immigration checkpoint on his motorbike.

    Said Mr Din: 'I thought taking the short-cut would be safe but after going into the lane, I felt like I was being followed.

    -- Picture: BERITA HARIAN
    'Suddenly I was struck with what I thought was a long piece of wood. I turned around and I saw a man looking at me.

    'We struggled and this time I was hit on the head with a metal bar. Then I realised there was not one but two men attacking me.'

    He described how the two of them took out a folding knife and started slashing his back and even put the knife to his neck.

    He then realised his life was at risk.

    'They asked me if I wanted to live or die?

    'They said if I wanted to live I'd better hand over all my belongings,' he said while adding that he believes one of them was a foreigner.

    'Although I gave them my money, they were not satisfied. So they snatched my bag, passport and my identity card and fled.'

    In addition, Mr Din also lost two handphones worth $500 and $320 in cash.

    STARTED BLEEDING

    He tried to chase the duo but his injuries were starting to hurt badly.

    There was a lot of blood flowing down his back and his head.

    He tried to seek help from passers-by but they paid no attention to him.

    But one man stopped to help.

    'Luckily there was one person who took me to a nearby police station so that I could make a police report,' he added.

    He was then taken to the Sultanah Aminah hospital for treatment.

    The Malaysian police later informed his wife who rushed over to bring him back home.
