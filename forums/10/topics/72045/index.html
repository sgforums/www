    to think again, it is a hatemail, but not really dumb - on second thoughts.

    the threadstarter did make a generalization, which implies that while the elitist schools do produce such "haoliao" ,"xialan" student, there are also exception like one that replied. how true such i statement is we wouldn't know, though we do know that a few of such "xialan students" from the elite schools had did things to put their schools in a slightly bad name.

    the (elitist) system is both good and bad like anything else, a double-bladed sword. the system drives, motivates (whichever way they use is not of concern here) the students to do their best, for themselves or the school. on the downside, not every teenager is hard-working, free of family problems, free of depression etc and even lesser are as intelligent. in this system, only the best would get to the top - those who are intelligent, who can work-hard, physically and psychologically healthy, or i might even include socially and spiritually healthy etc - only the best get to the top.

    ***please note that these should be taken no more than generalizations.

    Thus are many other reasons why there are masses remaining in the lesser schools. Sometimes, students who "didn't make it" are despised. The reasons why teenagers can't make it doesn't necessary mean that they are unintelligent, unhealthy etc...and they are "worthless to the society". Note the inverted commas. Once I was in a conversation with 2 girls who were from the elite schools. Suddenly, we went into the topic concerning our education system. Both of them unanimously remarked that students who "didn't make it" were "stupid", "unintelligent"...while i was able to make them understand my point that student falling short of the mark are due to many reasons...they maintained that whatever reasons for the "failure", arguing proves to be pointless. Because such people are "useless to the society".

    They were unable to see that people not making the mark, doesn't imply that they would become useless. To put it in a more humane way, everyone is human, even if unintelligent, even when on can't attend a top school. Economically, socially, everyone is integral to the society...by drawing lines, applying labels only serve to create conflict. From conflict, anything can happen.

    My point is of bringing up the conversation I had with the 2 students, is to say that:
    1. whether or not one attends top schools is not important. Neither are people who are not as endowed/wealthy any less useful.
    2. people deserve respect, intelligent or not, rich or not, healthy or not.
    3. practice restraint when applying labels, especially when the label is potentially hurtful or even derogatory like "stupid", "useless"
    4. the elitist themselves should be aware that not everyone is as fortunate as they are - of being wealthier, brought up in loving families, more intelligent...
    5. be sensitive to other peoples feelings. even when practicing restraint doesn't make you any money, oneself becomes a better person, more likable, more approachable. it can open many doors [without knobs]
    6. if you can, understand that there are people who are borned into troubled families which greatly affects a person's ability to perform - Freudian psychology. note that being aware of and to understand are 2 different things.

    Finally, people tend generalize...read not too much into it; it is what it is - a generalization.
