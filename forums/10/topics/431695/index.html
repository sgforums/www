    NTUC to focus on better employment for Singaporeans
    By Joanne Chan | Posted: 01 June 2011 1433 hrs

    SINGAPORE: Singapore's Labour Chief Lim Swee Say said on Wednesday that the Labour Movement is reshaping itself to be more pro-worker and will strive for better employment for citizens.

    Mr Lim said this year's general election have shown that full employment isn't enough to satisfy the aspirations of Singaporeans.

    He added that Singaporeans want better paying jobs and career advancement where low-wage workers want higher wages, mature workers want decent jobs, back to work women want to get their careers back on track and PMETs want faster career advancement.

    He said Singapore must find the right balance between being pro-business and pro-worker.

    With the current situation of full employment, Mr Lim said the balancing point should be adjusted towards helping Singaporeans find better employment.

    He said an idea thrown up during initial discussions is to link access to foreign labour to productivity gains and the hiring of mature workers.

    Another idea is to have a dependency ceiling for PMETs (professionals, managers, executives, technicians).

    Mr Lim said some companies have foreigners as a vast majority, limiting career advancement for Singaporean PMETs.

    However, he stressed that these ideas are not cast in stone, and the labour movement wants to engage the public in debate to reflect their concerns.

    This represents a new focus for NTUC - greater transparency over policy formulation.

    Mr Lim said that many Singaporeans may be familiar with the various policies, but do not know the rationale behind them.

    In reshaping the labour movement, Mr Lim also announced new office holders.

    Mr Ong Ye Kung has been promoted to Deputy Secretary General, replacing Madam Halimah Yacob.

    Ms Cham Hui Fong takes over from Mrs Josephine Teo as Assistant Secretary General.
