    In other countries, if you are appointed Minister for Home Affairs, you will be appropriately qualified based on professional experiences because your appointment will have to be scrutinised by the parliamentary committees of one expertise or another.

    To be appointed to his job, Greenspan was scrutinized by no less than the Congress who will ask relevant questions of his experiences.

    Those who are appointed as chief of CIA or FBI will similarly be scrutinized for solid experiences and successful operations in tackling major crimes.

    Down here, it is a matter of elitism and connections. As one ex-lecturer from NUS some Linsey has pointed out, it is not what you know that determine one's rise in the corporate ladder but who you know. Obviously a certain protectionism is operating here for all top jobs. If you are known to the MPs or PMs and one of the known elites, you will be invited to tea or eventually fill the minister or other top job.

    So after so many years we still do have have people with sufficient solid experiences to handle economic restructuring to value-adding technology driven model which create jobs or transport problems or a Warren-Buffet system of investing people's surpluses to help in lowering costs and saving of our surpluses for retirements.

    This kind of government has caused us many lost opportunities due to promotions of ignoramuses to positions of incompetence as pointed out in the Peter Principle.

    Now our costs of living are rising, with all citizens getting poorer by the days and months due precisely to this kind of governing.

    There is no dedicated professionalism being practised except for talking big and denying problems.

    If this is not complacency and failures what is complacency?

    Who is the chief of all such complacency?
