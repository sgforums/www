    Many say PM Lee addressed difficult issues confronting Singaporeans
    Channel NewsAsia - Monday, August 20SINGAPORE: Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong's speech at the National Day Rally on Sunday has been described as substantive.

    Mr Lee brought up various issues in his most important address of the year � from new measures to narrow the widening income gap to specific initiatives to help the ageing population, to new initiatives in education.

    Many in the audience said the Prime Minister took the bull by the horns and addressed some of the most difficult issues confronting ordinary Singaporeans.

    "I think every citizen is involved in today's speech. He's really touched everyone - young, elderly, middle-aged, whoever,� commented community leader Sarjit Singh.

    A junior college student, Goh Li Jie, said, "Now with three universities, (we have) tight schedule with limited scope. But with a fourth university� chances of us getting in (are) quite high."

    To NTUC assistant secretary-general Halimah Yaacob, "I think the most important message he has put across is that Singaporeans need to work longer and that the government will do its best to support Singaporeans in their effort by making sure there are jobs available."

    Gerard Ee, chairman of the Council for the Third Age, agreed. "If your lifespan is expected to go to 80 and beyond, that is 20 to 25 years to the traditional retirement and that is a very long time to live doing nothing. So those with opportunities to do so should be meaningfully engaged."

    Among the audience, for the first time, were opposition MPs.

    Chiam See Tong, MP for Potong Pasir said, "The CPF one per cent increase in interest rate will be welcomed by people. But (for the) delay in drawing down the CPF money from 62 to 65, I think the government will have to do a lot convincing the people."

    Most MPs believed the speech has put all the signs in place, so it is now their turn to explain the details to their constituents.

    "These things are not easy to digest and I think Singaporeans will need time to digest. There are positive steps but (they) will need a lot of explanation," said South West Community Development Council Mayor Amy Khor. - CNA/ac
