    One practical way in which the LTA can improve road safety is by improving traffic light sequencing. The sequencing in Singapore is dreadful (you get a green light then another set of lights 200 metres ahead turns red immediately). In fact, I don't think there *is* any sequencing in Singapore, yet other developed countries have it for decades.

    What do I mean by sequencing? I mean intelligent programming of lights so that if you travel at the speed limit you will encounter a continuous run of green lights. With sequencing, traffic in general reaches the destination faster, and the average number of vehicles on the roads decreases. Add to that a decrease in frustration, and fewer cars running red lights. Without sequencing LTA is responsible for a huge waste of commuters time.

    Further improvements to sequencing

    * reduce prolonged green arrows in off peak periods. Such green arrows help nobody when traffic is light, and only serve to waste the time of traffic trying to go proceed directly through intersections

    * giving pedestrians higher priority - increase the frequency of pedestrian lights, so that pedestrians don't give up waiting and jay-walk instead

    * also on pedestrians, don't assume they have all the time in the world to cross three sides of a cross intersection because the 4th doesn't have a pedestrian crossing, and don't assume pedestrians have all the time in the world to navigate barriers and overhead passes. The intersection of Nicoll Highway and Middle Road is a good case where pedestrians simply give up and jay walk instead.
