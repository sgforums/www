A Letter to Steve Chia

  • caramon.
    7 May 06, 17:02
    Hi Steve,

    I hope you don't mind me addressing you by your first name when we do not know each other. Let me cut to the chase and state that I have emailed you to express my appreciation and gratitude for your efforts and blood-and-sweat over the last few years, culminating in the recent Elections 6th May in which you won a 39.6% of votes in CCK.

    I have heard that you plan to quit politics if you fail to win a MP seat this time round. Let me first state that I understand your frustrations, pains and distress that might have led you to make such an "all-or-nothing" decision. And that whatever your choice you will still have the sincere support of an ordinary Singaporean like me.

    However, I really feel that it would be a huge pity for an established and talented person as yourself to leave the current political scene as it is, after all the efforts that you have put in over the last few years. 5 years ago, you were relatively unknown, even within the circles where express sentiment and sympathy for the Opposition was strong. Today, your name rests on the tips of tongues of people like me and my peers, as a symbol for intellectual and gentlemanly politics.

    Who am I, then? What demographic do I represent? Let me introduce myself shortly. We just never had a chance to vote for you as most of us live in GRCs which were never contested.

    We understand the sacrifices you have made in the course of politics, including the pain and shame of having your personal and private affairs brought out into public, scrutinised under the microscope, and then riddiculed for it. But let me assure you that I, and many of my peers, feel that such activities of yours do not reflect on your ability and capability to represent and speak for us. What matters to us is your passion, your courage, your commitment and your ability to speak up and argue about meaningful issues and matters which are questionable and dodgey.

    And in this respect, Steve, you have excelled. You have done far more than you should have done, paid a heavier price than what many of us will ever have to pay, and we appreciate that. And we would really hate to see you go, to see you leave, and to see a good, courageous and intelligent man like yourself leave the political scence, just right at the moment as the bubbling cauldron that is the Singapore political arena is starting to boil over!

    An alternative could be for you to join the Worker Party, for reasons which are obvious. I will leave the reasoning and motivations and benefits of this move to you. Or you could stay in your current party and continue the good fight which you have been fighting and are fighting. It doesn't matters.

    What matters is that you are a hero, the unsung hero of ordinary Singaporeans without a voice like myself, by choice, by fear or by circumstances. And know that if we do ever have a chance to vote for you, our voices will be resounding and travel far and wide.

    On a parting note, I wish you well in whatever you do and your endeavours ahead. All the best, Mr Chia. You will be remembered whatever you do.

    Best Regards,
    XXX
