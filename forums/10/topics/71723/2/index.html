    Regarding the consistent walk-overs the PAP has claimed, it is probably due to the sad state of the opposition in our country.

    Firstly, opposition members are usually stero-typified against, as they are believed to be marked members, and are usually targetted against (political attacks), and hence shuns people from joining the opposition, preventing an expected swelling up in the numbers, in direct proportion to the dissatisfaction with the government.

    Also, the continual remarks by certain political figures putting down the opposition as "unnecessary", and "irrelevant", or "are trying to sow discord", have certainly not helped matters. People are wrenched away from the alternative viewpoints, as biased media reports portray the opposition in a bad light, while continually giving the PAP prime news space (perhaps they can help it, perhaps they can't; after all, it now is the main political party in Singapore).

    If we want a robust and free opposition in Singapore, the only way to do so is to get more activists who are truely aware of Singapore's wants and needs to join the opposition, provide a true voice of reason, and more importantly, solutions to the problems facing our country. That is the only way for the opposition to regain it's credibility in utilitarian Singapore.
