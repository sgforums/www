    It is actually very difficult to determine which aircraft is better, unless the different aircrafts fight each other in a war & the better one will be determine from the kill ratio. For example, the Israeli's F-15 proved itself superior to Syrian's Mig-24 because the former are able to shot down 83 Syrian's fighter, & yet the Israeli had zero F-15 losses from that particular war.

    However, there are some modern research done to determine, or at least, to predict, the ability of different aircrafts without having to see them fighting in an actual war. The following are 2 examples:

    1) This is what I see from the following website: http://www.fas.org/man/dod-101/sys/ac/f-15.htm : "Simulations conducted by British Aerospace and the British Defense Research Agency compared the effectiveness of the F-15C, Rafale, EF-2000, and F-22 against the Russian Su-35 armed with active radar missiles similar to the AIM-120 Advanced Medium Range Air-to-Air Missile (AMRAAM). The Rafale achieved a 1:1 kill ratio (1 Su-35 destroyed for each Rafale lost). The EF-2000 kill ratio was 4.5:1 while the F-22 achieved a ratio of 10:1. In stark contrast was the F-15C, losing 1.3 Eagles for each Su-35 destroyed."

    2) Here are some info obtained from Pioneer Magazine, Apr. 1999 issue : "Studies show that the Eurofighter's only real competitor is the F-22. These studies also indicate that the F-22 can defeat the latest Russian Su-27 Flanker in 91% of beyond-visual-range engagements. The Eurofighter would succeed in 82% while the Rafale would survive in only 50% of such encounters. What is significant, however, is that the Eurofighter is 50% less costly to produce than the F-22."

    The above examples may use different methods & opponent aircraft, however, if those research are accurate, it can be predict that EF2000 are overall more superior than Rafale.
