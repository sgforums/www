    update for ya guys:

    Russia: French company to help SU-30MK fighter win Malaysian tender

    The French company Tales is involved in development of on-board equipment for the SU-30MK multifunctional fighter that Russia is planning to put up for a Malaysian tender, Tales representative in Malaysia and Brunei Francois Dupond said on Friday. The fighter will be put up jointly by the Rosoboronexport state-owned arms trader, Sukhoi aircraft corporation and Irkutsk Aircraft Production Association (IAPO), Dupond told Interfax-Military News Agency.

    Tales has a huge experience in co-operation with Russian developers and producers of avionics, the representative said. The company's experts took part in development of on-board equipment for the MIG-AT trainer, as well as for the SU-30MKI Flanker fighter, which Rosoboronexport and IAPO are exporting to India.

    The Malaysian variant of SU-30MK will have the cabin's information and control field designed on fifth-generation plane technologies, Dupond said. The minimum of liquid crystal displays will provide the maximum of data for the pilot, which will allow the crew to keep its attention on the main task - piloting and conducting combat operations. The second peculiarity of the SU-30MK avionics is application of laser gyroscopes that, unlike regular gyroscopes, guarantee high accuracy of aerial navigation.

    Dupond did not specify the volume of works that Tales would be responsible for in the course of the SU-30MK design, saying only that his company's participation in the project would be very broad.
