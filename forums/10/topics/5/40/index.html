    Just saw an article I thought those of us interested in the recent MBT development will be interested to know:
    ------------------------------------------
    United Defense, with subcontractors BAE Systems and TRW, has demonstrated the first simultaneous defeat of two live threats by the Integrated Army Active Protection System (IAAPS) on a US combat vehicle, under a US Army Survivability Technology Integration Programme contract.

    The integrated suite has successfully conducted multiple demonstrations of end-to-end system performance, including: detection, classification, and selection of the appropriate countermeasure; tracking and computation of the fire control solution; and defeat by electronic warfare soft kill or active protection hard kill.

    The suite has successfully demonstrated defeat of two live threats fired at a single vehicle at the same time without the use of armour as the primary means of defeat. The next phase in demonstrating IAAPS capabilities involves defeating incoming threats while on the move.

    "The successful intercept of two incoming, simultaneous threats has never been achieved previously on a US ground combat vehicle," said Val Horvatich, General Manager of United Defense's Advanced Development Centre. "The ability to actively protect ground combat systems from hostile threats using systems other than armour is key enabling technology for the Army's Future Combat Systems."

    The Integrated Army Active Protection System (IAAPS) is currently integrated on a ground combat vehicle and is undergoing testing. The suite includes two types of passive sensors, electronic warfare soft kill countermeasures, and an active protection hard kill system composed of launcher, radar and a deployed countermeasure.

    The IAAPS program, which is an Army Science and Technology Objective, has successfully demonstrated an integrated survivability system on a ground combat vehicle, which is designed to increase vehicle survivability while allowing for a decrease in armour and, therefore, vehicle weight.

    Source: http://defence-data.com/current/page14669.htm
