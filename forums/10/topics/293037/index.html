Singapore PM: Asia Pacific leaders worried about oil prices

    Leaders from 16 Asia/Pacific nations were worried soaring oil prices would prove a growing burden that could hinder economic growth and hurt the poor, Singapore's prime minister said Wednesday.

    Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong said leaders from the 10-nation ASEAN bloc and their counterparts from Australia, China, India, Japan, New Zealand and South Korea had discussed the issue at their East Asia Summit here.

    "It's a matter of concern for many of the countries," Lee told a press conference after the talks, noting the soaring prices would pose particular challenges to major oil-importing countries and the region's poorer states.

    "This is a burden that is growing, particularly a burden among low-income groups," he said, adding that high oil prices could also lead to higher inflation and cost of living.

    World oil prices surged to a record peak above 99 dollars per barrel on Wednesday on the back of the falling US dollar and tight global crude supplies, traders said.

    New York's main contract, light sweet crude for January delivery, rocketed to an historic 99.29 dollars.

    In London, Brent North Sea crude for January delivery jumped to an all-time pinnacle of 96.53 dollars per barrel.
