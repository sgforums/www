    Whistleblower: One who reveals wrongdoing within an organization to the public or to those in positions of authority

    The thing about whistleblowing is that the term often brings with it a certain amount of publicity and satisfied vindictiveness. It isn't just about bringing up an issue but about BLOWING up an issue. (Hence WHISTLE blow, like policeman.)

    The big danger with whistleblowing is how it first invokes fear in the society. You don't do something not because you understand it is wrong, but because you are afraid to get caught.

    Secondly, it empowers an individual with the right to threaten, and invoke punishment. It is something that can be so easily abused it isnt funny. This is a very dangerous step because it is just a thin line away from a witch hunt.

    Thirdly, it builts distrust amongst society. Yesterday you were the bystander. Today you are the whistleblower. Will you be the whistle-blowEE tommorrow?

    Of course, sometimes it is RIGHT to whistleblow. E.g. If you see your neighbour abusing his child, and you inform the police... and the police investigates and if there is evidence, your neighbours gets convicted. But it gets a bit out of hand if you inform the police, then inform the RCs, then inform the press - before the investigation takes place.

    Ideally, It would be great to correct wrongs through education, example, encouragement, err.. suitable deterrents. And only when these have completely failed.. well, we move on. But. -shrugs- looks like our goverrnment is looking at whistleblowing as a cue to responsible citizenship nowadays.
