    This murky status of overlapping territorial claims was finally resolved in an international review during the United Nations sponsored Third Law of the Sea Convention under the chairmanship of Singapore's Professor Tommy Koh - lasting from 1980 to 1982.

    The Law of the Sea Convention provide the framework for maritime disputes to be resolved, as well as clarify the many maritime issues related to sovereign ownership to maritime areas involving accompanying rights to undersea wealth. It also provide clarification to multiple issues arising from the doctrine of 'Freedom of the seas''.
