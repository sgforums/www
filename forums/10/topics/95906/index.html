    This is my earlier writing on PM Lee's visit to Taiwan

    Clear and Present Danger of Taiwan War

    According to a report by the Australian Defence Minister, the Chinese
    officials are all talking about an eminent WAR against Taiwan. And
    these are not just talks only. In preparations for war, the Chinese
    arm forces have made many military maneuvers over the past months
    with the most recent one, openly declared as a mock up amphibious
    assaults on Taiwan mainland. This military exercise will be enhanced
    by a live firing of ballistic missles from a missile base in China.
    It will be the most important military preparations on the Chinese
    side for a total showdown with Taiwan. Anyone who is familiar with
    military operations, these are the signs of "pre-ops" preparations.
    You will have military exercises again and again before you really go
    to war. If we track Chinese military exercises for the past 12
    months, it is eminent that they are really preparing for war. All the
    elite forces have been positioned in the southern part of China.
    China has cleared most of the diplomatic obstacles with other
    regional rivals like India and Russia. Thus, it could concentrate on
    the one front that matters most to it.

    Taiwan's Ah Bian has planned to make big changes and amendments
    (almost creating a totally new one) to its constituitions in 2006.
    This is the fundamental reasons for China's determination to choose
    arm conflicts as a mean to end the 5 decade old stalemate over
    Taiwan.

    The most important question we want to ask is, what will happen to
    Singapore? If US decided to get involved in this war, what's our
    role? Same role as in Korean and Vietnam wars? We must bear in mind
    that we are dealing with a potentially big military power that have
    ballistic missiles capabalities. If we get involved and US tried to
    use us as a tap to tighten on China's supplies (basically oil), we
    might have to face ballistic missiles flying over our heads! And if
    in the event that we acted as a supply depot for US marines and
    aircraft carriers, our oil refineries and installations would be the
    first targets from a strategic point of view. IN the past, Korean and
    Vietnam didn't have such long distance capabilities to disrupt the
    supply lines of their enemies. But in modern warfare, China possesses
    such capabilities.

    What stand should we take in the event of such military showdown? We
    aim for Swiss standards of living, we should take the Swiss stand of
    Neutrality?

    Goh Meng Seng
