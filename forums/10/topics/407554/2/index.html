    Aug 29, 2010

    PM'S NATIONAL DAY RALLY

    Award to recognise NSmen

    By Lee Jia Xin

    The $9,000 for NSmen who are citizens only - and more for commanders - will go into their CPF and Post-Secondary Education Accounts in tranches. -- ST PHOTO: EDWIN KOO

     

    NATIONAL Servicemen stand to get $9,000 each in a move to show that citizens come first amidst resentment towards foreigners who do not have to serve NS.

    Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong on Sunday night announced a 'National Service Recognition Award' at the National Day Rally.

    The $9,000 for NSmen who are citizens only - and more for commanders - will go into their CPF and Post-Secondary Education Accounts in tranches.

    Mr Lee said this is a recognition for the contributions and sacrifices of NSmen who give up two years full-time, and then return for reservist while trying to build a career and build a family.

    Permanent Residents who have done their NS will get the payout only when they become citizens.

    At any one time, there are 55,000 active servicemen - about 20,000 regulars and more than 30,000 full-time national servicemen.

    There are about 300,000 operationally-ready National Servicemen.
