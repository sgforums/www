    NDR speech gets thumbs up but key concerns remain

    By Faris – August 31st, 2010

    Many Yahoo! Answers users feel PM Lee’s National Day Rally addressed all their key concerns but some felt more had to be done to study the long-term impact of foreign immigrants into the country.

    That sums up the views of over 130 Answers users who  responded to a question on Yahoo! Answers, “What are your thoughts on this year’s National Day Rally?”

    In his National Day Rally speech on Sunday, PM Lee Hsien Loong addressed four key issues — the economy, immigration, education and the Singaporean spirit. He also announced that the government would slow down the intake of immigrants to 80,000 instead of 100,000 this year.

    And although some respondents welcomed the government’s announcement, others remain concerned over the policy’s long-term impact.

    User Cocoon, said that “Suddenly I feel like a foreigner in my own country. Too many of them in too short a time. Maybe it is time that they should stop taking in new immigrants.”

     Answers user, Lawrence said more had to be done to educate immigrants on the local culture and to assimilate them into Singapore’s multi-racial community.

    “What I am afraid is the loss of our cultural identity with too many foreigners coming into our country without the proper cultural education,” he said.

    “What many Singaporeans are facing is cultural displacement as foreigners insists on speaking their own language and forming sub-cultures within our borders. Just wondering who is assimilating who and what?… Thankfully, I am bilingual but what about those people who don’t speak Chinese like our Malay and Indian brothers and sisters?”, he added.

    Other also felt PM Lee failed to address marginalised groups such as the less fortunate and handicapped.

    “The less fortunate still don’t get better attention and to share the glory of Singapore’s success,” said user valiant.

    “Unemployed Singaporeans and their future not mentioned. To upgrade and land a job, or retire, etc. Job creation for this group of people never mentioned. This is helpful for those who are currently unemployed or those who will be unemployed when they are over 40,” said CPFer.

    However, there were also those who felt the speech addressed the key concerns and fears Singaporeans grapple with daily.

    True Repentance called it “one of the best National Day Rally speeches in years”. He applauded PM Lee for highlighting ”the Singapore Spirit that was dreamt of and driven by our forefathers”.

    Another user, ME, said his fears were addressed.

    “Many of my negative thoughts turned positive after (listening to) Prime Minister, Mr Lee Hsien Long’s speech,” he said.

    “I used to question: ‘Is the influx of foreign workers and immigrants really necessary?’, ‘Will Singaporean have lesser rights in future?’, ‘Will the over-crowded train problem be solved?’ etc. There is some clarity now and I guess we will be able to see the light at the end of the tunnel,” he added.

    Another user, Bibik, said, ”It (the speech) was well-delivered… he sounded very sincere in his plans to help further develop and improve Singapore, especially in overseeing and addressing the concerns of his fellow Singaporeans. I am particularly touched when he spoke about making plans to ensure that where education is concerned, those not academically inclined would have a chance to succeed.”

    Mjbhagwa agreed, saying, “I really appreciate the thoughts and the plan he put forward on MRT expansion as well as mitigation steps on rising property costs, especially HDB housing.”

    Another user williamwl, who says he is a Permanent Resident, also said Singapore citizens should be more appreciative of what they have.

    “No government is ever ideal, just like there is never an ideal employer in every work place,” he said.

    “However, if there’s more pro than cons, and everyone has a safe roof over their head, safety in the society, a job with decent income to bring up the family, that’s good enough… I am just another immigrant , like those mentioned by your PM in his speech… but an appreciative one. What about you?”
