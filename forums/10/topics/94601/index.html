    JAKARTA (Indonesia) - Former general Susilo Bambang Yudhoyono took an unassailable lead on Thursday in Indonesia's presidential elections, official data showed, as the defeated incumbent president prepared to make her first public address since the polls.

    With 104 million of the estimated 120 million votes counted, Mr Bambang had 61 per cent to President Megawati Sukarnoputri's 39 per cent, according to the General Election Commission.

    Despite this, Ms Megawati said she would not concede until official results were announced. That is scheduled to take place on Oct 5, but given the quick pace of vote counting could happen earlier. Mr Bambang said he would not formally claim victory until the tally was complete.

    Ms Megawati was scheduled to deliver an accountability speech to Indonesia's top legislative body, the People's Consultative Embassy, later on Thursday.

    The speech, which has been long scheduled, will be the taciturn leader's first public appearance since Monday's election, which passed off peacefully and has been free of allegations of irregularities.

    Mr Bambang has not left his house on the outskirts of Jakarta, where aides say he is considering possible Cabinet lineups and new government policies, since the ballots were cast.

    Analysts have called on him to move quickly to establish his reformist credentials and reassure the business community by naming a new attorney general and finance minister.

    Indonesia is saddled with almost 40 per cent unemployment and rampant corruption, and has been hit by three terror attacks by Al-Qaeda linked militants in the past two years. -- AP
