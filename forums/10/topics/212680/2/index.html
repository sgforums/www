    In his twilight years, MM LKY seems to have a bad tendency to reminisce, perhaps to make himself relevant; as the truth of what he had said refers to the past - during the Mahathir's and Habibie's period in office - NOT THE PRESENT.

    It has been three years since Badawi and Bambang have taken over the leadership; and with Bambang being the third Indonesian President since the small minded Habibie was unceremoniously voted out of office.

    Prior to Bambang's term, we have seen the blind President Wahid Abdulrahman and Sukarnoputri both turning to Singapore for morale support and economic co-operation.

    Singapore had 3 years of good and quiet relationship with Badawi, and more than 9 years of improved relationship since the ruckus with Habibie.

    Why did MM LKY dig up old historical issues - and on international stage that smudge the image of our neighbors - by stirring up the painful historical events of Singapore's relationship with our two bigger neighbors ?

    Why did MM LKY use their internal domestic ethnic issues that has NO BEARING to describe the PRESENT NEW relationship between Singapore and our bigger neighbors ?

    Did MM LKY update himself with the new events in Malaysia and Indonesia, or is he still living in his old memories of contentious ethnic issues of fighting for a small economic pie ?

    Both Malaysia and Indonesia have changed their domestic policies towards the Chinese - although seemingly small steps, it is nonetheless a far improvement from the darker days that MM LKY remembers during the 1960s through the mid-1990s.

    MM LKY has got it wrong on both counts - he has not updated himself with the current policies of Malaysia and Indonesia; and he has not considered that the new Malaysian and Indonesian leaderships have not co-erced Singapore's Leadership in the same manner as past leaders of Malaysia and Indonesia.

    In Indonesia's case, even under the 30 years of Suharto's rule, LKY seems to have forgotten his warm relationship with Suharto, with glowing words of admiration mutually given.

    Since Suharto's unfortunate downfall, MM LKY has not been short of critical, condescending and patronising comments towards an 'Old Friend'.

    Mahathir's caustic remarks of Singaporean leaders being a 'fair weather' friend seems to ring sharp and loud as any bell can ring.

    With friends like LKY, do the leaders of Malaysia and Indonesia need any enemies ?

    Do we need to look deeper for the toughness of Malaysian and Indonesian Politicians shown towards the Singapore Government under the leadership of LKY - whether he is the PM or MM ?

    MM Lee to visit France, US from Sep 30 to Oct 22
    By Asha Popatlal/Melvin Yong, Channel NewsAsia | Posted: 29 September 2006 2000 hrs

    SINGAPORE : Minister Mentor Lee Kuan Yew will be going to France for a 10-day visit from September 30 to October 9.

    While there, he will attend the JPMorgan Chase International Council Meeting and call on French President Jacques Chirac.

    He will be accompanied by Mrs Lee.

    Mr Lee will proceed to the United States, where he will be visiting New York, Washington DC, Dallas and Las Vegas.

    In New York, he will be speaking at the Woodrow Wilson International Center for Scholars Award Gala and receive an award for public service.

    As Special Advisor, he will also attend a Citibank Board Meeting.

    In Washington DC, the Minister Mentor will meet with US President George W Bush, Vice-President Richard Cheney and other senior administration officials.

    Mr Lee will then proceed to Dallas where he is due to give a lecture.

    He returns to Singapore on October 22.

    Meanwhile, responding to a Channel NewsAsia query, the press secretary to the Minister Mentor said Mr Lee has written to Malaysian Prime Minister Abdullah Badawi.

    The letter is presently with the Singapore High Commission in Kuala Lumpur, ready to be personally conveyed to Mr Abdullah.

    On Thursday, Singapore's High Commissioner to Kuala Lumpur was asked by Malaysia to explain recent remarks made by Mr Lee.

    During an international forum in Singapore two weeks ago, the Minister Mentor had said ethnic Chinese minorities in Malaysia and Indonesia are being marginalised.

    Prime Minister Abdullah had described the comments as unjustified and said that Kuala Lumpur was awaiting a response. - CNA/ms
