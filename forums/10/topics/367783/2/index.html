    What long term effects of Newater can there be when it is basically just H20? No matter where it comes from there's nothing else in it unless something went wrong with the water reclaiming process.

    It's a bit like saying that if I create water in a lab that is chemically, and purely water and nothing else, drinking it is somehow dangerous compared to drinking water that was "naturally made" in the skies... despite the fact that chemically and functionally, both sets of water are functionally identical.

    Additonally the very non-newater tap water we have is derived from non-potable sources, and filtered through "unnatural" technologies... we don't seem to have any issues with it.

    Basically barring any massive failure in the Newater process drinking Newater is about as boring as drinking chemically pure water... if anything the problem is that it is too pure and we are not getting any minerals from it.

    We have to be rational about our critiques.
