    I heard ( and saw on TV ), with very little surprise that foreigners will be encouraged to help Singapore with the shortfall in babies.

    Also, they are encouraged to bring over their dependants in a bid to attract more foreigners here to set roots. Looks to me, we're being consigned to the attic like old furniture.

    What have you all to say now? Or rather, what can we say? But that's the way it is here?

    I mean look, the gov is concerned we don't have kids to take care of us when we grow old, or that we get old and sick and become liabilities. Then fine, but why can't we have our CPF at retirement age ( before 62 ) to take care of diabetic conditions and high blood pressure ( which is so popular and common in SG ) so that it does not get worse by the time we reach 62?

    I have a friend who has a diabetic condition, and high blood pressure to look after. Monthly medical fees are on average $300. She's way above 55, but below 62, so CPF board says she will only be given about $300 per month once she reaches 62 but meantime, she has nothing to tie her over. I mean, right now, while she is old and cannot get a job ( thanks again to SIngapore's employment preferences ) to finance the medical cost to treat the condition, why can't the government have a heart to let her have her money to control her ailments before it gets out of hand at 62, while her son who just recently started a business is better able to roll finances to grow? In a situation like this, who can think of having babies???

    The government thinks there is nothing much about bearing children and bringing them up, but haven't they thought about young parents who also have aging and ailing parents to look after?? Medical costs for old parents, plus the costs of having a baby and much more down the line.

    I think our government haven't done a reality check.
