    I suppose when our leaders first told the people about the dangers of welfarism 30-40 years ago as practised in the west, they were correct.

    When any ministry or department is inefficient or costly, there is no one to check on them and the leaders allow them to increase fees to cover back any cost increases.

    The state is practising no longer welfare-avoidance but monopolistic charging of fees and recovering full costs of services and market values. Not only that, many services were privatised to reduce spending but in many cases, they are accumulating surpluses like NKF without accountability to people.

    That is why many statutory boards are making excessive profits from people like what HDB has been doing.

    Every department is a cost centre. Making monies are no longer enough. They are making more and more monies and accumulate them as surpluses while the private sector economy practically cannot survive and people are struggling to make ends meet.

    Unless there is a balance between avoiding welfarism and profiteering, Singapore will not be able to return to the old-day vibrancy and entrepreneurship.
