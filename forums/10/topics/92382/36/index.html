    WHAT KIND OF LEADERS DO WE NEED TODAY?

    What is leadership ? What kind of leaders do people do we need for today's business organizations or enterprises? What qualifies must leaders possess?

    In my opinion, leaders are people who not only must possess sufficient intelligence, qualifications they must possess most of all the fire, passions and highest prerequisites to motivate people and mobilize them to new challenges in today's society.

    Above all else, leaders should be committed to implementation efficiency, and accountable and transparent corporate governance to motivate all the reach goals and objectives.

    Leaders should continually sustain, develop and nurture the social, cultural and economic vibrancy of the whole enterprise to keep up with competitions and survival.

    In particular, they should take proactive actions e.g :-

    (1) Identify new challenges, set directions to reach aspired goals and objectives.

    (2) Set high standards by personal examples so as to preserve the morale necessary for mass participation and competitiveness.

    It is especially crucial for leaders to lead the people by suffering together with the people and not expect only the people to bear short-term pains and sacrifices while the leaders are free from such struggles.

    For the above-stated reasons, I would like to urge all leaders and elites and people who are given opportunities not to manage tasks by assumptions of the past.

    They should seriously consider that being leaders they are the people who are given opportunities to serve and must serve with passions and implementation capabilities and not assumptions about their own high-cost and elitist pro-administration and pro-foreign investment and job-creation policies which have proven futile and unhelpful to majority of the population.

    They should set new directions for the whole population and aim at accomplishing the greatest good or the greatest numbers.

    Only when leaders can see the difference they can make claims about their talents and abilities. New ministers who have not proven themselves should refrain from introducing new policies that are not fully tested or supported by tbe people.

    People now expect ministers to get real and answer to problems about the dropping of wages and increases of costs. They should not mull over such problems.

    In the economic sphere, they should govern holistically with entrepreneurship and competitiveness - coordination and practical knowledge application to take advantage of new and constantly upgrading technologies.

    At the same time, they must unite people and not harp on matters or polices which merely cause hardship without progress or divide the people.

    PRACTOCAL LEADERSHIP QUALITIES - IMPLEMENTATION ABILITIES

    Leaders must be able to target on implementation excellence or empower the people through the collective actions of the rank and file.

    To do so successfully over time, they must practise accountability and transparency to manage the organization with a good set of corporate governance.

    In summary, leaders must possess the following abilities which are to be proven in actual work performance:-

    (1) Passion,
    (2) Ability to Inspire and Motivate followers,
    (3) Ability to get people to work with coordination and apply knowledge.
    (4) Ability to translate complex goals and ideas into implementation processes to bring about competitiveness, technology start-ups and jobs etc.
    (5) Invest monies and perform tasks with Accountability and Transparency
    (6) Collective leadership by core team to make major decisions,
    (7) Sense of integrity free from theoretical assumptions or controls that stifle mass participation in knowledge application.

    PATIENCE AND DETERMINATION

    Very often however leaders may become too complacent and only concern themselves wih assumptions and fail to sustain the undertaking with sufficient patience and determination to motivate all in efficient implementation.

    It would be disastrous to concern themselves with ideas, assumptions and stop getting the people to sustain their fighting spirits and share rewards to do so throught the people.

    People are not machines and will resist unfair or inequitable practices.

    HUMILITY AND RELIABILITY

    There is an overriding need today for leaders to come fully equipped implementation processes and work practices in lowering costs and creating jobs.

    In leading from behind, leaders must serve the people with humility of servant, reliability, sense of justice, empathy to attain social and economic wellbeing of all.

    HONOUR AND INTEGRITY

    Leaders are now too obsessed with money, and they might now be too involved in situations of coflicts in contracts or self-reward or self-promotion or assumptions.

    SOCIAL AND CULTURAL VIBRANCY

    Leaders must cultivate in-depth social, cultural and economic vibracny of people to develop and maximise mass participation through interactions to reach excellence.

    BROAD-BASED EDUCATION AND KNOWLEDGE APPLICATION

    They should constantly equip them with skills and knowledge application abilities through broad-based practical education.

    USUAL MISTAKES - JUSTIFICATIONS AND DENIALS

    Finally, leaders should be humble and not resort to shallow argumentations to put up look-good justifications and policies which tend to delay or deny their solutions.

    IMPLEMENTATION ABILITY THE KEY

    Above all, leaders should be committed to objective selfless implementation processes above party or personal interest so as to empower all to reach for results with minimal petty controls and self-centred assumptions.

    It is not enough that the leaders themselves are efficient or able - the whole team must be so. To produce organizational efficiency, they deliver team efficiency, coordination and knowledge application.

    Leaders shoudd not be merely concerned with their own views, statistics or presentations or with only the bottomline, evenues or surpluses.

    They must sustain the whole organization, plans for continuing expansions, job creation or lowering of costs of basic services to his people.

    They must be able to utilise limited available resources e.g. use of empty properties, vacant lands, idle capitals for expansion and survival through innovations, entrepreneurship, application of knowledge and value-add competitiveness.

    When leaders must take consistent and focused actions to meet these unknown with certainties without being hindered by any forms of self-centred or intuitive policies or assumptions.

    Finally when over time, leaders must take steps to overcome human problems and uncertainties to keep the team vibrant and desist from taking credits and "let go" so that the team will produce team efficiency and productivity to attain larger results and objectives.

    Extract from the Singapore Consitution


    Article 12 Equality

    (1) All persons are equal before the law and entitled to the equal protection of the law.
    (2) Except as expressly authorized by this Constitution, there shall be no discrimination against citizens of Singapore on the ground only of religion, race, descent or place of birth in any law or in the appointment to any office or employment under a public authority or in the administration of any law relating to the acquisition, holding, or disposition of property or the establishing or carrying on of any trade, business, profession, vocation or employment.
    (3) This article does not invalidate or prohibit
    (a) any provision regulating personal law; or
    (b) any provision or practice restricting office or employment connected with the affairs of any religion, or of an institution managed by a group professing any religion, to persons professing that religion.

    Article 13 Prohibition of Banishment and Freedom of Movement

    (1) No citizen of Singapore shall be banished or excluded from Singapore.
    (2) Subject to any law relating to the security of Singapore or any part thereof, public order, public health or the punishment of offenders, every citizen of Singapore has the right to move freely throughout Singapore and to reside in any part thereof.

    Article 14 Freedom of Speech, Assembly, and Association

    (1) Subject to clauses (2) and (3)
    (a) every citizen of Singapore has the right to freedom of speech and expression;
    (b) all citizens of Singapore have the right to assemble peaceably and without arms; and
    (c) all citizens of Singapore have the right to form associations.
    (2) Parliament may by law impose
    (a) on the rights conferred by clause (1)(a), such restrictions as it considers necessary or expedient in the interest of the security of Singapore or any part thereof, friendly relations with other countries, public order or morality and restrictions designed to protect the privileges of Parliament or to provide against contempt of court, defamation or incitement to any offence;
    (b) on the right conferred by clause (1)(b), such restrictions as it considers necessary or expedient in the interest of the security of Singapore or any part thereof or public order; and
    (c) on the right conferred by clause (1)(c), such restrictions as it considers necessary or expedient in the interest of the security of Singapore or any part thereof, public order or morality.
    (3) Restrictions on the right to form associations conferred by clause (1)(c) may also be imposed by any law relating to labor or education.

    Article 151 Restrictions on Preventive Detention

    (1) Where any law or ordinance made or promulgated in pursuance of this Part provides for preventive detention
    (a) the authority on whose order any person is detained under that law or ordinance shall as soon as may be, inform him of the grounds for his detention and, subject to clause (3), the allegations of fact on which the order is based and shall give him the opportunity of making representations against the order as soon as may be; and
    (b) no citizen of Singapore shall be detained under that law or ordinance for a period exceeding 3 months unless an advisory board constituted as mentioned in clause (2) has considered any representations made by him under paragraph (a) and made recommendations thereon to the President.
    (2) An advisory board constituted for the purposes of this article shall consist of a chairman, who shall be appointed by the President and who shall be or have been, or be qualified to be, a Judge of the Supreme Court, and two other members, who shall be appointed by the President after consultation with the Chief Justice.
    (3) This article does not require any authority to disclose facts the disclosure of which would, in its opinion, be against the national interest.
    (4) Where an advisory board constituted for the purposes of this article recommends the release of any person under any law or ordinance made or promulgated in pursuance of this Part, the person shall not be detained or further detained without the concurrence of the President if the recommendations of the advisory board are not accepted by the authority on whose advice or order the person is detained.
