    Hi, TKHAN,

    The state of democracy that currently exists in more advanced countries has probably been quite well summed up by Pilamaster in the previous post as:

    1) Federal Democracy (as practiced by USA and to a lesser extent UK, AU)

    2) Central parliamentary Democracy (practiced by Eastern Europe and Russia)

    3) Socialist Democracy (practiced by germany and I guess Scandinavia)

    4) Direct Democracy (practicd by Swiss)

    These democratic countries may be practising varying degrees of democracy but as a whole, these are democratic systems in terms of one-man one vote, equal rights of citizens, non-prosecution for anyone taking part in electoral process etc.

    These countries have achieved significant progress and prosperity or advancements in social, cultural or economic terms. No other system has yet stood up to the superiority of democratic system of government as posted previously under this topic.

    Those states that claim to practise other systems cannot quite offer such strongly based openness, accountability and transparency as exist in these democratic countries. Asian values, family values upheld for sometime have since the Asian Financial crisis been found self-oriented or self-centred which could have brought about malpractices at the end.

    Civilization over the past four centuries has achieved mcuh progress under democratic system of government. Democracy has triumphed over pure socialism, communism, autocracy, dictatorship, guided-democracy, and pseudo-socialism or pseudo-democracy.

    It signifies better scientific progress through mass participation, openness made possible under democracy. As a result, people who are free to vote and practise government accountability and transparency attained higher standard of living, economic, educational and social advancements after adoption of such system of government.

    Some latest democratised countries might have experienced practical difficulties due to certain exceptional and temporary circumstances but given time and fine-tuning, their people, these newly democratised countries will attain their goals and objectives and aspirations as compared with non-democratic or pseudo-democratic ones because democracy is more reliable in the longer term than ad hoc beliefs or practices.

    The 7 points of True Democracy are the practical extracts (therefore they exist as facts and not fiction) of democratic government worth practising particularly by those on the threshold of democracy as mass participation of people will bring about progress and it will help to avoid "U" turn or justifications.

    Singapore has tried to follow such a system but is probably affected by self-justifications. Citizens can see and will feel the goodness of democracy only the beneficiaries cannot see these reasons. So, there is nothing doubtful about the 7 points of TRUE democracy as they are worth practising and working hard for.

    They can stand up to scrutiny but not those in favour of other theories not yet proven by civilization and who are protective of status quo or entrenched in one-party interests.
