    Hello Thinkloud2000,

    The topic "Definition of True Democracy" arose because there exist many problems with autocratic governance. I don't have to go through the 20 problems with autocracy as posted as they were well discussed previously.

    I agree with your view and those of Elfred that some other countries have worse problems. According to such view we can accept that autocracy has achieved a certain degree of success. Many people will not disagree with such view.

    But we must remember one thing: success at what price to the people. Do all people succeed as well as the scholars and the rich?

    I do not wish to go through the lengthy discussions again which policies of autocratic governance have caused problems and in what way they have caused problems to many people. I will confine to answering to your question: Why this topic as it seems too idealistic to ask for democracy or accountable and transparent management.

    The main reason for starting this topic is that there existed many problems with autocracy as the ideas of a few men are not sufficient to run a competitive economy. Many solutions or policies under autocracy are too narrow only serving the interest of perpetuation of the rule not overly concerned with citizens' problems which are often sidelined with justifications and excuses. There is an absence of alternative views. The past policies could only achieve so much and the economic competitiveness stagnated up to a certain point.

    The second reason is: autocracy is not a system or one that is sustenable. I have given you the reason for saying so in the previous post and replies to Elfred.

    Even though democracy alone will not solve all problems, it offers a certain clearly defined political structural framework well tested in history towards which people could strive for improvements and continuous improvements.

    Mistakes made by any elected leaders may be corrected in due course. Yes, there may be frustrations in solving certain problems due to the need to accommodate alternative views and allow checks and balances. Yes, important changes will take time to gain acceptance. There is a risk of discontent being expressed in street demonstration and labour unrest too.

    However, look at the bigger picture. It does not mean that just because of freedom of expressions and choices there will necessarily be civil unrest or chaos as our leaders and news media under their control want all to believe. Many autocratic or dictatorial countries have turned to democracy and are having initial or teething problems. However that does not mean democracy has failed.

    Democracy existed over the past many centuries. Many non-democratic countries are working towards establishing democratic electoral processes to ensure that there are no more perpetual abuses or mismanagement under autocratic or dictatorial rules as seen in the past.

    Many people might not be aware that if there is no accountable and transparent participative governance involving all citizens to take part in freely expressing their views and scrutinise performance the situation may be worse. So it is important for Singapore to evolve its political system towards democratic model which allow mass participation to take place to bring about the knowledge applications and contributions by all.

    Given a more educated workforce, there is a need to change to embrace all views and suggestions. Democracy is a long winded process but by suitably institutionalizing the good features of socialism, leadership in our case into the democratic framework as posted in the 7 points of practical democracy, the big mistakes under one-man domination and autocracy can be avoided without unrest.
