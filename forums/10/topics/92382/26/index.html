    HOW TO DECENTRALIZE TOP-DOWN TO ACCOUNTABLE TEAMWORK PROCESSES

    According to Mark Twain: Â“The secret of getting ahead is to get started. The secret of getting started is breaking your complex and overwhelming tasks into small manageable tasks and then starting on the first one.Â”

    It is in the human nature to be self-centred and manage their tasks in an ad hoc manner. Many people would go about tasks based on undefined conceptual or presumed self-ability, leadership quality without being concerned with accountability or implementation work processes or teamworks.

    Self-centredness has been identified as the primary cause of malpractices by the Asian Financial Crisis and the mammoth corporate shenanigans in the aftermath.

    Today despite the many corporate malpractices as unveiled by Enron or World-com collapses management practices remain self-serving and conceptual. Too university management courses are teaching conceptual ad hoc and self-centred management approaches: e.g. Neurological systems, Management-by-objective, Taylorism, FAYOL, Standard Operating Procedures (ISO), Organization theory etc. Most of these concepts are too centred on personal abilities, abstract and cannot be applied to actual work performance to attain results.

    Today, the fast-paced globalised knowledge-based environment has made it increasingly difficult for a few men at the helm to oversee every major decision or their implementation. The abilities of a few are too slow and inept to adapt and reach to changes in all fields from human psychological motivation to technological renewals and upgrading. Problems get bogged down or locked in while everyone will be pretending all is well with policies or decisions when inefficiencies are affecting all in slow attrition.

    On the other hand, it is increasingly proven that complex problems are performed in established work processes with coordination and knowledge application by all staff as demonstrated in many computerized business applications. Everyone is capable of leading, being motivated and appy knowledge irrespective of the level of management not just the top management personnel.

    Leadership and talent or its allied meritocracy are simply too self-serving and individualistic to produce such process-organizational efficiency or economic competitiveness.

    Therfore all the conceptual general management are insufficient to produce competitiveness and are making no difference to material results except for look-good public relation.

    Such general conceptual management concentrate on decision making often at the expense of implementational coordination corrections and adaptation and teamwork.

    Results on the other hand will not be realized until everyone in the whole team is just as capable in making good decision and implementing them with coordination and knowledge application.

    Hence traditional approach in management fails to delegate or empower the team and consequently unable to meet with the demands of changing conditions or technological environment in the new age.

    Newer management approach will have to increase implementation emphasis by establishing follow-through process steps such as : (1) Study of Ideas, (2) Enquiry, (3) Securing Contracts and (4) Contract Administration (Operations).

    The first three steps are macro-level decision steps while the last step, namely contract administration (esablished often in project management core activities) which will allow proper follow-through of tasks in structured departments to coordinate all functional departments as follows:-

    ]1.(General) 2.(Human Resource) 3.(Revenue) 4.(Accounts) 5.(Marketing) 6.(Services-outsourced activities) 7.(Facility) 8.(Enforcement) 9. (Production) 10.(Compliance).

    Although the functional departments names vary in practice there is no reason not to structure them functionally along the above-stated lines for all individual businesses for the purpose of maintaining integrity of process and follow-through departmental delegation and two-way communication, coordination and knowledge application the major part of process management.

    Whether a business is governmental or non-governmental, overall functional departments are as a whole functionally the same as stated in the foregoing from the point of attaining follow-through organizational efficiency or economic competitiveness.

    CORE ACTIVITIES AND PARAMETERS

    Complex tasks can now be clearly structured and performed by all departments with in-built coordination, consistency and focus (primary factor of success) to realize economic competitiveness.

    A typical department can be set up for processing in attainable work processes thus:-

    1. Department Report (defining 3-4 core activities)
    2. Assign 2-3 quality-time-cost parameters to each core activity.
    3. Process detailed tasks derived from each core activity sequentially in time-based knowledge-application work forms.

    Monitoring of tasks and reporting systems are now self-regulated by generated quantifiable assessments:-: "1" for non-compliance of parameter, "2" for compliance and "3" for excellence.

    TRACKING OF PERFORMANCE THROUGH CALL CENTER

    With tasks so standardized the organization is on the way to establishing a call center to oversee tasks to attain coordination and knowledge application by all in a two-way communication.

    Variances or "1s' as communication to the call-centre are self-corrected by all departments.

    Level 1: Management Report (call centre)
    Level 2- Department reports Â– oversee operations giving variance feedbacks.
    Level 3 - Work forms - sequential knowledge application.
