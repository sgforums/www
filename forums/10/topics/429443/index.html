    GRC 'myth'

     

    GRC has already been implemented for 13 years yet it remained a contentious issue because it was viewed by many as unfair and only benefited the ruling party.

     

    However I think Single Member Constituencies (SMC) system can also guarantee minority representation in parliament by some modification on its implementation

     

    This is how it works :

     

    1. The Elections Department would specify the required number of minority representatives in the all SMC constituencies according to the percentage of the minority community in the total population.

    1. The Election Department will designate which are the constituencies as minority represented only constituencies.

    2. Political parties could only nominate a minority candidate to contest in these designated constituencies. So only minority representatives will guaranteed be elected into parliament.

    3. These minority represented SMC should be rotated among all the constituencies in every General Election.

    The advantage of the said rotation is that a MP will not be staying in a constituency for too long. The electorate will find it beneficial and be more then happy to accept the scheme because PM or other heavy weight ministers will one day come to serve them in their constituency

     

    Likewise this system could also be applied to ensure the desired gender representation.
