    I have come across a number of people whose views relating to other races and religions are frighteningly bordering on racism. As a Chinese and non-Muslim, I want to reassure particularly Singaporean Malays that these people are the minority.

    The following are my personal views but I hope there will be others who will support me:

    1. On employment, I am completely against employment based on racial or language abilities if that is not absolutely required for the job. Obviously if I want a Chinese language teacher, I will select candidates who have a good command of the language. But if I am looking for a security guard or production engineeer, I support laws that will make discrimination on the basis of race or language illegal.

    2. On the issue of religion, I believe we should be more sensitive to the religious teachings and practices of Muslim Singaporeans. Relating to the issue of dogs in public vehicles, the current ban should stay. However, we can and should explore possible alternatives/compromise with the Muslim community, such as the possibility of transporting pet dogs confined to approved cages and enclosures so that there is no chance of dog saliva staining taxi seats.

    3. On national service, I support putting Muslims/Malays on an equal footing with other races with regard to assignment to various vocations. Assessment of security risks should be based on individuals, not an entire race. It is not healthy to have Chinese boys serving NS mainly in the army and Malay boys in the police. There should be greater integration during national service.

    4. On education, I support strengthening of compulsory teaching on race and religious relations. There should be greater cooperation between mosques and schools to organize joint activities for students of all races and religions. In schools, there must be greater integration of all races. Students of different races may be separated for language classes but must have other lessons together.

    5. On the legal front, I do support the use of the Sedition Act to stamp out racist remarks. I know many do not agree with me but having read the remarks posted by one of the bloggers who has been charged, I think the risk is real and cannot be ignored. I hope in time, we will not need such laws as it is a disgrace that we need them in society. Unfortunately, at the moment, I think we do.

    6. I also urge Muslim leaders to reach out to heartlanders in the neighbourhood and invite them to participate in non-religious, social activities in the mosques. Is it possible to expand the activities of the mosques beyond the religious to incorporate social concerns in the community as well? Can mosques work with community centres to organize activities and classes for the community?

    7. I urge Chinese Singaporeans in particular to cultivate friendships with Malay Singaporeans (how many Malay friends do you have?). I certainly hope to be invited to participate in the Eid celebrations every year by my Muslim friends.

    I believe building strong relationships with Muslims in our community is the best way to combat racism and the risk of terrorism.
