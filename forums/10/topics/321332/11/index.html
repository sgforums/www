    I don't think Singapore is a hot destination for people. Most Chinese see Singapore as more of a stepping stone: a place where they can learn English in an environment where most people can speak Mandarin, so that they can later move to the USA and take up citizenship there. Some people say that the Chinese who come here are actually the worse ones. Apparently, the best go to the US, the second-tier stay in China and only the lousy ones come here.

    As for Singaporeans migrating overseas, as a Singaporean living in Australia, I must say that life here is very stressful unlike in Australia. Over in Australia, the minimum annual leave is 28 days. In Singapore, it's only 7 days. In Australia, road sweepers and cleaners can afford a holiday in France or England. In Singapore, the same type of people will be lucky to even visit Bangkok once in their lifetime. In addition, in Singapore, you are always pressed to meet tight deadlines at work while in Australia, you can have all the time in the world to finish your work as taking things slow is part of the Australian culture. And of course, Australian bosses are generally more family friendly than Singaporean bosses. Just look at the number of Singaporean women who have been sidelined in the company just because they get pregnant. While I currently do not plan to give up my Singapore citizenship, and I have already served my NS, I think that we still have a long way to go if we want to convince more locals to stay.
