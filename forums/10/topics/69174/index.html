    Is this the time for us to find better paying jobs overseas, since our own domestic employment situation is poor ?

    The present situation in Singapore is similar to the Filippinos during the early 1980s - and those from Indonesia and Vietnam from early 1990s - when their economies were shattered by political incompetency and lack of economic planning.

    The warning cards were already on our table since the EARLY 1980s, when businesses were all complaining about the high costs of doing businesses in Singapore - such as stubborn insistence of annual wage increases, high CPF contribution to achieve a 40 percent mark, high land prices that resulted in high cost of developments and subsequent rentals and property prices - that affected all down stream costs.

    The Government had claimed that THEY KNOW WHAT IS BEST FOR SINGAPORE.

    How can they NOT know everything ?

    They insisted on having ALL THE AVAILABLE and LIMITED SINGAPORE TALENTED PERSONS on their side, and every angles were supposed to have been covered.

    Still we had the experience of the 1987 crash, and had almost recovered by 1991 - by then the Government returned to set the pace of wage increments in the Civil Service that affected the wages paid to Commerce and Industries.

    CPF contribution was pushed back to 40 PERCENT again.

    New fiscal policies were introduced with high COE prices that affected commercial vehicles, followed with ERP being introduced; while tax on fuel remained relentlessly high.

    URA land sale prices were kept continuously high to maintain the 'false value of the bubble' that the Banking Community need, so that past property financing plans are not jeopardised.

    Some would claim that these comments are with the benefit of hindsight. However, these were already the complaints that were voiced by nearly all sectors of Singapore Life - from ordinary housewife, hawkers, Small and Medium Enterprises to large Corporations; home buyers to industrial developers; entrepreneurs and established businessess planning new investments had begun to pick alternative sites with lower overheads.

    These were already the writings on the wall, that were ignored.

    Even the Opposition Party had written books about it, and offered solutions that were derided - but now we see Government adopting ideas that were already suggested more then TEN YEARS AGO.

    Fortunately, those who are going overseas have found jobs based on the HIGHLY TRAINED SKILLS of these Singaporeans - as middle or high level managers, consultants, specialists.

    We have yet to experience husbands and wives leaving the family to seek menial jobs overseas - as laborers, maids, farm hands.

    Let us not fall into the silly and cheap political cliches created by the senior members of the Ruling Party, who had contradicted themselves by coining this label for these Singaporeans as QUITTERS; when at the same time these same Political Leaders had failed in their economic planning had also encouraged Singaporeans to 'SPREAD THEIR WINGS'

    Many Singaporeans had 'spread their wings' since the late 1970s, more during 1980s, AND even MORE during the 1990s.

    Those who DARE, WINS.

    So are those who 'SPREAD THEIR WINGS' now 'QUITTERS' ?

    Originally posted by FireIce:
    u sure they are "talents"?

    That statement says it all, FireIce - probably more than you intended. A good part of the time, the ones who leave are treated as "talents" with the inverted commas.

    These people leave Singapore and go to environments in which they can perform to their maximum potential, instead of being hampered by people who either have some inferiority complex or want to protect their rice bowls instead of being truly competitive.

    These are the people who want to have the freedom to do what they see to be the right thing, instead of following the yes-sir-no-sir-three-bags-full mentality that pervades right from the beginning of the education process. Unfortunately, Singapore is the kind of place where thinking for yourself makes you a pariah.

    The strangest thing is that these are exactly the people that Singapore needs right now in its current stage of development. The growth of the economy has resulted in a population that can no longer afford to play the game of producing goods that are cheaper than anyone else's. Right now, they need people who can think outside of the square, but with the atmosphere of hierarchical compliance, they have done themselves in. Recognising this need, the government instead decided to launch into its "stayers and quitters" nonsense. The message they effectively delivered was "Singapore - love it or lump it". Fine - faced with such a choice, many have chosen to lump it, and it's the government's poor judgement to have offered such a challenge instead of trying to coax these people back.

    So, yes, in other parts of the world, they're talents. It's only in Singapore that they're "talents".

    If it's the latter case, that question doesn't really add up. The migration process usually requires that applicants be able to demonstrate their ability to support themselves, either by gaining employment or by setting up viable businesses (their previous business track record is also taken into consideration).
