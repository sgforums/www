    Hi All..

    I came across this forum while reading my daily ST headlines. And being a singaporean living overseas, can't help but check out this topic.

    Just thought i give my 2 cents' worth. I have been living in Amsterdam for 4.5 years. Reason for moving: Boyfriend is dutch. The thing is, the grass is not greener on the other side, nor is it browner. Admittedly, I've always wanted to 'get out' of singapore, not because of the govt. but because of the lifestyle (everyone chasing the 5 Cs, after designer clothes, etc, etc.). To me, there's more to life than chasing the materialistic lifestyle.

    Life is different here in Amsterdam, but not necessary better (or worse). Holland has its own problems with foreigners. The cost of living is quite high. It's expensive to own a house, though getting a car is cheap. There's lots of cultural activities here, but public transport is expensive and unreliable.

    What I'm trying to say is that there are 2 sides to every story. Being here, I'm quite proud to be a singaporean. Singapore has come a long way in a short 39 years. Most other countries have a much longer history (centuries!). We are smaller in land area than Luxembourg, but not less important. The govt. did what it had to do to build up the country. Decisions have to be made, and they may not always be popular with the people.

    That said, I do think it is perhaps time for a change. The education system needs to be revamped, and work culture/ethics as well. So instead of condemning, why not give suggestions? It's either improve or approve...

    Owning a house here is not easy. Most people rent and even then, there is a long waiting list. We recently bought our first apartment and that cost Â€200,000 for 700 sq. feet. HDB flats in singapore are bigger than that.

    Transport is also expensive and definitely not efficient. Ever since moving here, I miss the efficiency i've taken for granted in singapore.

    Cars are cheap of course, compared to singapore. But traffic jams are quite terrifying. Having a 35km long traffic jam is quite normal.

    There are also problems with the medical facilities. The waiting list is long. People have to wait 3-6 months for an operation.

    Every country has it's own problems. One of the main issue now in Holland is the tightening of immigration laws. There is a so-called problem of the migrants not integrating into the society. Racial discrimination still exists, despite it being known as a tolerant place. These are issues that may not be reported in ST or other news, and thus make the country seems better. If you're here as an expat (via a job in a MNC), then life is quite different, and easier. But if you move here for any other reason, then life can be quite difficult. Expats are always treated differently no matter which country they are from.
