  • robertteh
    17 Apr 06, 15:17
    The MP is currently being paid $12,000.00 pm for doing a part-time job which he needs not have to perform but our MPs are being described as great talents and people with all kinds of ability beyond human or performance-rated proportion.

    He can still keep his normal job without resigning when he is elected as a MP.

    As part-time salary or allowance of $12,000.00 pm is simply too high to pay the MPs. Don't forget we the taxpayers still have to pay the MPs for life at about $8,000.00 pm after two terms.

    In the case of an opposition MP, he has to do more works to produce checks and balances on the government. A backbencher MP does not have too much to worry as his works are preempted and covered by the autocratic leaders who except them not to ask too much questions.

    An opposition MP is also under the risk of being sued for defamation and targeting by the party in power.

    Opposition MPs would deserve to be paid more than the backbencchers who hardly attend meetings or raise questions in parliaments.

    More likely since our MM Lee and PM Lee are so sure about their leadership and convinced of the righteuousness of their policies, backbencher MPs do not really have to query their leaders. All they need to do is to be able to speak in favour of their leaders' policies and be supportive.

    MPs' job scopes consist of attending meet the people sessions about four times per month, Parliament once or twice per quarter, and some walkabouts.

    If we divide $12,000.00 into say 6 attendances, MP is being paid $2,000.00 for each attendance. Where in the world can one receive $12,000 pm for a part-time sinecure job as our MPs many of whom only become active to appear on TV during the election time.

    True, an MP needs to do some researches of problems and issues but how many of them really have done great works from their so-called researches. I think some forumers have done more than our MPs in bringing up real issues facing their constituents.

    It is not worth paying $12,000.00 pm to our MPs (except opposition MPs) to carry out a part-time job which they need not perform in many cases except to be yesmen. I think to be realistic, we should pay our MP about $500.00 per attendance oe $3,000.00 per month with savings going to an endowment fund to help lower the costs of living currently faced by all citizens.

    If certain leader or ministers have proven their worth such as having succeeded in turning around the economy during 1997-99 or 2000-2002, with upgrading to a value-adding model, we should pay such ministers $1,000,000.00 pa but this should not be taken as precedent to pay all the rest of ministers who have experienced highway collapses or high medical or housing costs.

    Do not forget that beside the minister's salaries, tax payers still have to pay them to the tune of $750,000.00 pa for the rest of his life after two terms.

    Those who have not yet proven results like Dr. Goh Keng Swee has should not be paid automatically $1,000,000.00 pa. If we really must bribe our ministers so as to prevent bribery, then this is not the kind of Minister we want. We only need to pay them sufficiently high to be worth of trouble to leave his job to serve the nation. If in the private sector someone has made 10 times our minister's salaries, let it be so our ministers are not risking their own capital or life saving like Bill Gate or Li Ka Shing and cannot be compared with such corporate bosses in the private sector as recommended by MM Lee.

    His comparison is not apple-to-apple and cannot stand closer scrutiny.

    All our Ministers have are paid million-dollar salaries should be regularly made to defend his performance based on acceptable objective yardstick and not by intuitions of the MM or PM alone. Ministers who have created NKF or repeated high costs or highway collapses are not talents and should be told plainly they have not performed by someone else as PM will probably find it hard to tell them so.

    To create a more accountable and competitive government, our leaders should start changing their assumption that all their chosen men or women are talents. If they are, they must be independent and objective measurement yardstick, justification based on "I say what I like" rhetorics or presentation.
