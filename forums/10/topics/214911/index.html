    ============================================
    Parents, please be more responsible in raising your kids
    October 13, 2006

    THERE have been many reports recently about students getting beaten, their haircut problems and so on.

    These have led to negative feedback from parents.

    I believe these complaints create the impression that schools and teachers are treating these students unfairly.

    MORE ILL DISCIPLINE

    But could it be that there are really more ill-disciplined and disrespectful students in Singapore today?

    Where is the respect that they should give teachers?

    Take the haircut report in The New Paper. Why make a fuss during exams, saying schools should not disturb them?

    They should have had their hair cut before the exam.

    This is a joke - I thought students are supposed to adhere to the regulations of education.

    Passing exams does not mean the student is 'good' if the student is not well-trained in his/her character.

    Many people think schools are meant to train students in terms of academic achievement.

    But schools are there to impart knowledge and guide students as they grow up.

    Students should not be given the impression that the schools are there only to teach them to pass exams.

    I am not a teacher and I am not connected to the education system in Singapore. I have made these comments because parents today need to be more responsible in bringing up their children.
    - Saberobin Tan
