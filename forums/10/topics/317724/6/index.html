    Statistically, married couples live longer. The odds are higher that a spouse will live to a ripe old age than a single person will. Take a couple who are both 60: The probability that at least one spouse will reach 95 is 45%. For a single man, the odds of reaching that age are 22%; for a single woman, 30%.
