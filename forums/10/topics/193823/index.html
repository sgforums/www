Do we have ministers who can restructure the economy?

  • robertteh
    3 Jun 06, 18:02
    After two recent severe economic recessions of the 1997-1999 and 2000-2002, we have heard from the Minister of Finance of formation of Economic Restructuring Committees and their recommendations.

    What have happened to the works of these two ERC. Anyone answerable? Have we reached the target of creating new technology start-ups to manufacture international products and create international brand names as done by Acer of Taiwan over the period 1970s - present.

    Let's face it, we need creative and entrepreneurial ministers in this larger area of government not so many lawyers to impose more controls over the populations to ensure law and order.

    Learn from Stanley Shih of Acer of Taiwan who recently was interviewed by CNA on how he built up Acer. He said this: When I was a young graduate, I began to dream of (1) How to manufacture international products needed by the world and (2) How to build International Brand Names to market the products.

    If only we have such ministers who know how to build these dreams we would have done well. Right now, we have none despite claim by our leaders of having engaged many scholars over the years to restructure the economy to "value-adding" technology-driven new economy. Nothing like this has happened yet and there is no regular report made to citizens that our ministers or someone with the passion is doing so.

    Then why are we paying them million dollars in salaries and bonuses to do routine maintenance?
