    Hello, all forumers,

    A reader's letter as published on 24.12.2004 in TODAY newspaper is reproduced for your information:-

    "The JTC seems to recognize, in its move to lower industrial rentals (Dec 23) that the operating environment is still fragile and rental costs remain high and uncompetitive for businesses. This is good news.

    However, HDB seems to take the opposite view that business rentals in HDB estates - which were previously granted rebates for a limited period - are not high.

    Shop keepers and ordinary people would disagree with the HDB's assumption that the business operating environment has improved for them.

    Is there a more consistent practice with regard to rental assessments for industrial premises and non-industrial premises?

    Why the discrepany between the JTC and HDB's interpretations of rental levels and market conditions?

    By merely granting the rebates and withdrawing them at the first sign of external recovery, our business-operating environment will remain artificially high and difficult for most domestic business recovery.

    Singapore should and must maintain more consistent and more equitable cost assessment policies to help businesses be competitive in the coming years, instead of doing so only after more crises.

    For a start, HDB should try to interpret the situation as JTC has done to be more proactive in helping businesses to survive.

    Robert Teh Kok Hua"

    Haven't enough been said about high costs of doing business and living in Singapore by forumers. What changes have been made by top leaders so far despite all their claims of openness and accountability or changing mindsets. Are mindset of leaders ever changed? Your views are welcome.
