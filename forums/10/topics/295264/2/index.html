LKY is aware of such a leadership criteria but he has only paid lip service to such criteria and instead once he was secure in his power he went on the reverse of governing only to serve his own or his party political agendas at the expense of this leadership principle.

Based on assumption about the danger of welfarism he single-minded resorted to taxing and recouping all costs of essential services resulting in ever-rising costs of living and our gradual loss of economic competitiveness.

Is this the sign of greatness at all by any definition?

    So everything has gone wrong with the same erstwhile great leader because of self or self-centredness and arrogance.

    If he had not let power go to his head from the 1970s we might have gone on to achieve greater economic competitiveness from developing our own mass talents in every sphere of nationhood.

    Instead he relied on hypocrisy and justifications to deny problems to no avail even in the face of all the wrong doings as exposed in NKF and subsequent audits of many departments.

    I have to agree with you on this one - no leader is perfect.

    Great leaders are people who have accomplished great deeds or goals against all odds relative to their circumstances to fulfill the specific expectations of their people.

    In our case in the circumstances back in 1960s we needed and had LKY who possessed the right passions and determination to maneuver over many distracting forces to win or help win independence from the colonial master and went on build up the basic infrastructures and certain basic needs for the people.

    To that extent up to 1970s, LKY has been a great leader period.

    From 1970s he was a changed man as I have elaborated. He let power go to his head and became arrogant always trying to talk about his leadership and his past glory instead of focusing in solving many problems.

    So to me because of such self-glorifications and self-interests he had sacrifice the larger good of society and our economy stagnated for many years.

    Now with the recent world recovery he seized the opportunity quickly to talk about his past success again and again being too self-centred and protectionistic of his own gains and positions.

    This kind of leadership to me is not great at all. He should just retire and let Singaporeans throw up their own new-age leaders without interference from behind the scene. We do have many potential good leaders who are much more passionate and capable than him who understand the needs of the people and who will no doubt given the opportunity win over the people to develop and nurture mass participation and practical knowledge application of all citizens (not just limited to scholars) to serve the greatest benefits of the greatest number.

    People must use their keen perception to spot such new leaders who could serve them not necessarily people who are so-called scholars or high-flyers.
