    In the case of a club or membership organization, club constitutions always say that members will enjoy equal rights and privileges. If anyone is discriminated, the member has a right to start a class action or take his own action on the club for breach of the constitution or collective contract.

    In the case of Potong Pasir, residents should not accept discriminatory practices by the HDB or Town council. They will have the right to upgrade the estates based on equality of treatment under the Town Council Act.

    In private condominium the Strata Title Board has ruled on many occasions that residents are accessible to all such rights of upgrading or improvements irrespective of how the management justifies the decisions to upgrade different areas at different times. It has ruled that any reserving of parking lots for any particular group of residents is illegal.

    Residents of Potong Pasir should consider starting a class action to get back their rightful entitlements. Only when people can show that they intend to protect their right will the government finally listen and treat them with equality to entitlements as the rest in Toa Payoh or nearby estates in upgrading of their estates.

    If the HDB or town council are to rely on technicality such as voting for upgrading the residents should show them that this is only detailed decision making by residents and their rights to availability of the common fund for common areas upgrading in equality of all is not affected by such technicality.
