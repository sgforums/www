    Personally, I have been grateful to the PAP for the great governance and leadership for Singapore. I know they are an efficient party. However, in some countries, power is held on for too long in some families and trouble starts to surface. (there are already some, s'poreans, you know them.) I serve Singapore, so ultimately I put Singapore above PAP.

    My concern is that, many MPs in government are not battle-tested. Not wishing to risk defamation, but I feel in theory they can feel free to implement policies without inquiring the unhappiness or views of the people because they will never get elected out. They can play a wild card although they have been very careful. I always felt Singapore resembles the system in ancient China where you sit for the examinations and get a post in the government. Its a known fact that many PSC scholars score straight As and S paper dist, go into the government, go through a walkover election and get a post.

    I feel that, now that we have a mature educated population, stable political climate and economic growth, it is a good time to open up the political environment in Singapore. Its a good opportunity now. If Singapore is not ready in 20-30-40 years, then when???

    The PAP always say the Opposition failed to field good candidates. I hope the political system can be more open. With that, more educated people will join an opposition and provide an effective check to the ruling party. Personally, I hope there can be a strong opposition in the next GE. I mean, if people speak up against flaws, why must they be seen as purely troublemakers?
