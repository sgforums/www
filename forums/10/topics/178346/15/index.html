    Currently studying in melbourne and keeping close to latest news on the election in Australia.

    Personally, I could feel being an australian (even i am a "pure" singaporean) after engaging with the different views of the two main parties i.e. the coalition and the labor. It is about being able to engage in the future of the country and knowing that you are holding onto something powerful - a cast of vote.

    It is time for singaporeans to think about the future of our nation. The future of a nation is not simply restricted to the economic well-being, but rather the political atmosphere. As i read through the Singapore parliamentary reports, and compare it with Australian federal parliament where the two parties exert their pressure onto each other, I yearn greatly for the day where Singapore could reach a two party system where the incumbent will have to fight for survival, not just being talented over whatever they are doing, but also endure the political onslaught during the election. Looking at the current list of Post 65 PAP members, I wonder how many of them are capable in achieving the latter.

    Oh well, maybe it is time to change our constitution, from a nation "Singapore" to Temasek Holding and be listed under Nasdaq, if Singaporeans are not willing to give the opposition a chance in the next election. Once the GRC syndrome is overcome, talented personnel who aspire to be a politician will think twice during the PAP tea invitation, as he or she will have more options.

    Lastly, with regards to the idea of opposing as being an opposition party is flawed. An opposition party does not necessary needs to oppose the incumbent on every single issue. I believe that an opposition party as an alternative party of possible different views and beliefs. Just take a look at this year australia election where "me-tooism" are part of the election compaign, the opposition labor party seem to be on its way of breaking the coalition hope of attaining the fifth term in government.

    Let's embrace the term alternative over opposition in the next election.
