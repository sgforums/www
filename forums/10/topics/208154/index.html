    Singapore paper criticizes China after reporter jailed for alleged espionage

    SINGAPORE (AP) - A Singaporean newspaper whose reporter has been jailed by Chinese authorities on espionage charges expressed support for its employee and criticized Beijing on Friday, saying the country's judicial process should be more open.

    Ching Cheong, a Hong Kong-based China correspondent for the Straits Times in Singapore, was sentenced Thursday to five years in prison after being convicted of selling secrets to Taiwan, a self-ruled island that Beijing claims as its territory.

    A Beijing court found Ching guilty of selling unspecified "state secrets and intelligence" to an unidentified Taiwanese foundation that is really a spy agency, China's official Xinhua news agency said. China and Taiwan split in 1949 amid civil war and are believed to spy actively on each other.

    The newspaper said the process by which Ching was prosecuted should have been more transparent. Ching was detained during a visit to the southern Chinese city of Guangzhou in April 2005 and stood trial earlier this month in a one-day closed proceeding.

    "Judicial opaqueness is the first of the old-style institutional staples which a modernizing China will want to get to grips with as the nation moves to centre stage in world affairs," the newspaper said in an editorial. "We acknowledge this is a work in progress, but it is an important one."

    The newspaper also urged the central Chinese government to look into what it called a "lack of clarity and certainty" in the country's interpretation of secrecy laws and what constituted violations of national security.

    "Journalists cannot be second-guessing security officials and operating on the basis that their first mistake shall be their last," the editorial said.

    Ching's conviction came during a government campaign to tighten media controls that has seen dozens of journalists jailed.
