    Johor Ready To Consider Senai As Regional Cargo/Logistics Hub


    BATU PAHAT, June 6 (Bernama) -- The Johor State Government is ready to give due consideration to proposals that the Senai Airport be made a regional cargo and logistics hub, Menteri Besar Datuk Abdul Ghani Othman said Tuesday.

    Any suggestion to make Senai airport as a cargo and logistics hub to serve not only the country but regionally, will be given the consideration by the state government, he said.

    He was speaking to reporters after visiting the Modern Agriculture Project at the Air Hitam Veterinary Institute here with state Exco members, Tuesday.

    The Menteri Besar was asked to comment on a news report today that Senai Airport Terminal Services (SATS) was planning to buy a 1600-hectare piece of land belonging to Lee Rubber Co to develop the cargo centre.

    SATS, the operator of the Senai Airport, is owned by corporate big wig Tan Sri Syed Mokhtar al-Bukhary. He also owns the Port of Tanjung Pelepas (PTP) in Johor.

    Abdul Ghani said the plans to develop Senai as a regional cargo centre actually began five years ago and was based on several factors including that it would be capable of providing the competition to its neighbour, Singapore.
