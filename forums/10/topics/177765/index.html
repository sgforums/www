    The recent WP manifesto regarding scrapping the HDB Ethnic Group quota system makes sense as the policy is flawed and works against the owners of HDB flats. I do understand the fact that the policy was originally implemented to prevent the formation of racial enclaves and to promote ethnic integration in HDB housing towns. Its all good and fine. However, its a great idea build with unfairness towards flat owners whose block of flats have been marked as ineligible for sale say to chinese, indians, or malays.

    It doesnt make sense to penalise a HDB owner who is looking to sell his property in the open market and finds out that he can't say sell to the chinese for example. As the chinese make up the majority, a block of flats marked as not being eligible for chinese buyers could see a massive drop in its market value. So why should the owner bear such a massive financial loss on his HDB flat's market value when the guy across the road with a similar size unit might be able to achieve a premium; as his block of flat does not have any maximum quota against a single ethnicity. The policy strikes me as a totally unfair system.

    Solution:
    If thegovernment insist on maintaining the HDB ethnic group policy; in all fairness owners of HDB flats should be reimbursed by the government for their financial loss when the units are not eligble to be sold to a particular ethnic group. The compensation formulae should take the Open Market Value of the HDB flat LESS the lower price at which the flat is sold as a result of quota of ethnic buyers in that particular block of flats.
