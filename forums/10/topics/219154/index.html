    I saw the news tonight, some students in Ngee Ann developed a system that is able to track both SMRT's and SBS's buses. The students also integrated the ability to track the buses into mobile phones. The students believe that they are able to install this system into the various bus stop in Singapore and let passengers know when the bus will come.

    Sound like a great idea? Yes, of course it is, for the commuters.

    This way, they can claim that they need money for the equipment they installed at the various bus stop islandwide and have another bus hike. Then, they need money to maintain these equipment. And they can also say that they have less space for advertisement, which reduce their revenue to cover their operation cost. Then all these factors add up to a fare hike.

    Don't be mistaken, forumers. I'm not against the students' invention, in fact, I'm looking forward for it. I just don't want the commuters to use this invention as an excuse to introduce another round of fare hike.
