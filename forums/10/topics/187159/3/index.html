    I think you have a very shallow understanding of what law suits are about. People do not only sue because the other party has acted illegally (though in Singapore, legality can be a very nuanced and uncertain concept). People sometimes use law suits as a tactic.

    One, defamation trials can be very very expensive to defend because ofthen there are many complex technical and evidentiary issues to deal with. To illustrate, England's longest trial, lasting about TEN years, was a defamation trial. Look up "McLibel".

    Two, the damages awarded in Singapore fro defamation is, imo, out of all proportion to whatever harm the words concerned has caused. Look up mr Tang's case.

    So the fact that someone has decided to sue does not neccessarily mean that he thinks he has a good case. It could mean that he thinks he can scare the other party into submission because maybe he has more resources to throw around.

    Further, you seem to confuse having a good cause of action as somehow meaining that he is 'right' to sue. Surely you cannot be so naive? Again, I would suggest you look up Mr tang's case.
