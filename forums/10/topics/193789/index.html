    Originally posted by Rock^Star:
    OK gd point. Has anyone ever wondered why our courts assign absolute power to the judge instead of having a jury? Also, ever discovered that our courts were once with jury, similar to the UK?

    Robertteh, care to explain?
    A government normally refers to the executive branch of the government. All over the world, parliamentary democracy has defined government as (1) Executive (2) Legislature (3) Judiciary with each having its own role and responsibility for the larger purpose of government the whole country with equity, fairness, justice and equality of citizens before the law.

    There is a common practice to separate the role of executive, legislature and judiciary in order to be fair, just as to law and fact not to allow any executive to govern all in one.

    Owing to a loophole in our Constitution, in Singapore, our government has been given certain discretionary power to pass laws to restrict the rights of citizens as to what is moral, what is public order and what is interest of the public as posted in "Definition of True Democracy".

    So our government has indirectly over time restricted citizens' constitutional rights including their rights to be heard as to law and facts in a trial with equity, fairness in our court.

    Our government by abolishing the jury trials in serious cases is leaving every issue of laws and fact to the judge who in turn is appointed by or with the sanction of the executive who is in control of the prosecution.

    The judge is supposed to have fair and is now given a free hand to decide on the laws and fact. But the judge also knows full well that his career path and remuneration is determined by and with recommendation of the executive.

    So when a judge is hearing a political defamation suit who will he pay attention to in hearing of law and facts.

    The reason for abolishing of jury system as we have heard from our politicians is that sometimes the jury might be swayed or tempted by good defence argument to free criminals based on emotive presentation by smart defence lawyers.

    But is it not equally true that the judge may well listen to over-assertiveness of the prosecution directly or indirectly influenced by the executive thereby blurring our executive, judiciary to the greater detriment of government and justice.

    By abolishing the jury system we are removing a critical element in the justice system - right of the accused to be heard as to his fact by a more neutral or independent jury best able to grant him justice - paving the way for the judge to listen to wishes of the ruling party given in pre-announced policies and other politically motivated pronouncements.

    In our autocratic system, our leaders are already acting as judge and jury so what will be the greater dangers to society? Will the greater danger be lawlessness and disorders caused by politically defamatory or inflamatory speeches, when the executive has abolished jury trial of facts. In any cases of political defamation against the opposition politicians, in the absence of any fact-hearing panel, could we expect the trials to be fair, equitable as to laws and facts.

    Who will have influence the facts - the judge who has to worry about his career after what has happened to past judges who got transferred after their giving of verdict the other way.

    In Singapore with the last bastion of fair hearing of fact or defence - the judge is the only person who can be relied upon to be fair but how fair can he be when he has to worry what will happen to his career which is determined by the executive.
