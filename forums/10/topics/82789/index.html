    SINGAPORE : The Singapore Government is restoring the monthly pay cuts taken by political, statutory and judicial appointment holders as well as Administrative Service officers over the past four years.

    The restoration will be done in two steps on July 1 this year and January 1 next year.

    A statement from the Prime Minister's Office says pay is being restored because the economy has recovered and is growing again.

    The first pay cut of up to 10 percent took place in November 2001 because of the highly uncertain economic outlook after the September 11 terrorist attacks.

    The second cut, again of up to 10 percent, was in July 2003 after the SARS outbreak.

    The cuts affect not just ministers and senior civil servants but also the President, judges and Administrative Service officers.

    On top of the restoration, the Government is also changing the way public sector salaries are adjusted.

    To keep public sector pay competitive, it has been automatically benchmarked to selected salaries in the private sector since 1994.

    But in the 10 years since then, adjustments were only made in four years, because economic conditions have been unstable and private sector pay has been more variable then expected.

    So from now adjustments will no longer be automatic.

    The Government will still track private sector pay, but it will also take into account the broader state of the economy each year. - CNA
