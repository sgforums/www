    SINGAPORE: Singapore's sovereign wealth funds - the Government of Singapore Investment Corp (GIC) and Temasek Holdings - have performed better than global equity markets last year.

    Finance Minister Tharman Shanmugaratnam said this in a written reply to a parliamentary question from a Member of Parliament.

    He said: "Investments by GIC and Temasek have inevitably been affected, like those of other global investors.

    "However, their overall value has fallen by less than the decline in global equity markets, as they (GIC and Temasek) maintain diversified portfolios and had taken precautionary actions early in the (financial) crisis to reduce their exposures to the equity markets."

    The finance minister, however, did not give details on the extent of losses suffered by GIC and Temasek.

    Key global markets took a severe beating last year, with the MSCI World Equities Index falling by 42 per cent.

    - CNA/ir
