Only when this primary framework is practised over a period of time will it be possible to have democracy firmly rooted as part of their own culture. Only then will it be possible for people to prevent manipulations of democracy and enjoy lasting peace prosperity and progress.

    PROPOSED OVERHAUL OF CURRENT CONSTITUTION,

    The current constitution (Singapore) is too general and vague. There is no true democracy with proper framework for protection of citizens' rights and integrity of one-man-one-vote election process.

    In the present form it can be easily manipulated like introduction of GRC to enable any ruling party to stay in power or to suit its own narrow party interest.

    All that the ruling party is interested in is to exercise all sorts of administrative control of the judiciary and military and legislature and it will be able to do as it like even paying themselves millions of salaries or life pensions without any basis or consent of the people.

    The government can easily get their own legal officers or judges to interpret the laws and the constitutions to circumvent loopholes or grey areas to do all its biddings.

    The answer is obvious: people in Asia do not understand perhaps because of their own culture enough of how democracy really work in the west which have enjoyed true progress and peace to facilitate development of its people.

    There is no tolerance by democracies in Asia of criticisms of government with most of the government resorting to fixing of political oppositions or alternative shadow government.

    Even if there is some forms of tolerances they will be exceptions or hypocrisy in dealing with people's feedbacks of problems.

    The constitution is the basic document that spell out the basic rights of citizens, defining the duties and roles of government and their responsibilities to safeguard social, cultural and economic vibrancy particularly in the following areas:-

    (1) One Man One Vote election process to ensure that all who are eligible are able to enter his name for nomination to be a candidate for election.

    (2) To ensure multi-racial representation, a certain number of seats of parliaments can be reserved for contest among minority candidates.

    (3) The rights and duties of citizens are spelled out as inviolable with suitable protection irrespective of race, culture, religion or tenets including freedoms of speech, political beliefs, right to stand for election or right to make criticisms of policies or practices of government in power without fear of being sued for remarks or criticisms of government in the course of election.

    (4) The roles and duties and responsibilities of components of governments i.e. executives, legislature, judiciary and civil service are spelled out clearly to avoid conflict or usurpation of power by anyone to lay down a foundation for establishment of rule of law.

    (5) Establish Rule of law, justice, fairness and equity in conduct of laws without fear or favor to the government in power.

    (6) To Establish a low-cost independent panel open to observers from UN agencies to oversee election process and political participation by citizens.

    (7) To Establish powers in appropriate agencies under the supervision of an independent panel for indictment or Investigation and adjudication of conflicts of interests among members of the government.

    It is recommended that the existing constitution be amended so as to ensure that we will eventually evolve into a true democracy where citizens are not being defensive in playing their part in the governing of the country and they are being encouraged to take an active part to safeguard the quality of government with proper scrutiny and feedbacks of whatever policies or actions taken by the government to ensure that they would exercise their sacred votes during election without fear or favor or compromise.

    Such a constitution will be proactive and open up greater possibilities to have a country well run by the government which will truly serve as the government by the people and for the people in the widest perspective of nation building.

    Through such a proactive constitution people can then develop into a cohesive social and cultural and economic entity able to contribute to creativity, entrepreneurship or participations in election, giving feedbacks without fear or favor.

    Singapore's democracy is still young and it has not fully developed and matured.
