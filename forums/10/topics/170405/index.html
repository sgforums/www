More Shopping centres, more Land sales, Less jobs

  • robertteh
    22 Dec 05, 22:27
    With so many economists and scholars employed in the government, can't they realise their major mistakes in pushing land sales.

    By pushing major land sales by the URA, they are not helping to create jobs at all.

    The shopping areas are simply overcrowded. More land sales at Orchard Road and CBD will only lower the success of existing shopping centres due to over-supply. Shopping per capita is now too high. It will be a zero sum game by pushing for more shopping centres to be built and existing shopping centres will face lesser demand and lowering of productivity.

    The same major mistake is happening in pushing more property land sale. Why keep pushing for more and more vacancy in property occupancy? It is another zero sum game. More residential lands flooding the market will lead to greater vacancies and greater depressed pricing. No wonder Singapore property indices are declining for the 7 straight years as compared with HongKong and other Asian Tigers.

    Instead of pouring more monies into concrete which is sucking monies into unproductive use, they should find ways to generate more jobs by fueling technology start-ups. Go and talk to all the entrepreneurs and get them to invest on easy loans from the idle monies with easy rules....and relaxed conditions....More jobs will be created instead of more concrete arches and more spending on concreted sales...
