Education - Broadbased vs Creative learning

  • goh meng seng
    17 Oct 04, 19:57
    Many people, including those high flyers in MOE thought that we need "Creative Learning" or Creative Teaching" for schools inorder to cultivate "innovative minds".

    I have done a little bit of research on Finland's education system, which is regarded as a country far more advance in cultivating the Innovative Economy.

    Surprisingly, they are not talking about "Creative Learning" or "Teach Less Learn More" thing. Their emphasis is on Broadbase learning.

    This is the extension from an understanding of how the Innovative Economy evolves. There are two spectrum of innovations; social innovations as well as technological innovations. What we have been told is that R&D in technology is important, high tech is important, but the fact is, high tech without social innovative evolution, you couldn't possibly create anything valuable to sell in the world market.

    There are bi-directional influences of both social and technological innovations. Starbuck or bubble tea is a form of social innovations, which may or may not need technological innovations. A new technological innovation must be integrated into social innovation to make sense and be marketable.

    Thus, they conclude that only through broadbase learning, they could cultivate the awareness on both sides, the social and technological needs for innovations to take place.

    Are we heading in the wrong direction by stressing in some "Creative Learning" by putting up funny exam questions?

    Goh Meng Seng

    I quote:

    4.1. Standard Education As Basis For Professional Knowledge

    "The society is responsible for supplying standard education, a function that cannot be transferred to enterprises. Standard education must convey new generations the cultural inheritance which serves as a basis for nations to build their future on. The vast quantity of information available and the continuous increase in new information accentuate, on the one hand, the importance of subjects that help analyze information and, on the other hand, learning to acquire necessary information by oneself. Instead of giving answers and demanding memorization, it is important to encourage curiosity and teach pupils and students to find information at their own initiative. These objectives already make it necessary to offer broad-based standard education, but it is important also for the reason that when working life is quickly changing, people must have the aptitude to change professions and to utilize lifelong learning. Narrow and specialized basic education will lead to problems, because the changes in working life will crumble the value of this kind of education fast."
