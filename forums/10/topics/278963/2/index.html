    Yes, while 'Low Heat Transmission Glass' can be used to cut down heat transmission INTO the building without cutting down light reaching into the building - it does not help to reflect heat to the neighboring environment.

    An 'Analysis and simulation of curtain wall reflection glare' conducted by the Department of Architecture, National Taiwan University of Science and Technology has highlighted this seldom considered point during the construction of modern buildings featuring glass facade that will surely reflect light to the neighboring environment.

    While the extensive use of ''new technology'' glass in this South Beach Project can help to reduce the amount of energy use within its building, it may result in the higher use of energy in neighboring buildings built earlier based on designs from different environmental criteria.

    Green Buildings need not necessarily feature glass panels, and can feature a whole panapoly of basic features that help in creating an immediate comfortable and energy efficient space for its tenants and also to its neighboring environment.
