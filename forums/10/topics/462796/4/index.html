    REFORM PARTY'S STATEMENT ON MICHAEL PALMER’S RESIGNATION

     

     

    The Reform Party notes PAP MP for Punggol East, Michael Palmer’s resignation this afternoon. Marital infidelity and breakdown is sad whatever the circumstances and our thoughts are with the respective families.

    The Reform Party urges the PM to call a by-election as soon as possible in order that the people of Punggol East may exercise their democratic right to be represented in Parliament.

     The Reform Party was one of the strongest and most vocal advocates at the time of the Hougang vacancy and fully supportive of Mdm Vellama’s case for a  judicial review of the by –election laws. The Party’s views on the need for timely by –elections are a matter of record. Please see the links to our previous press releases on this subject below.

    The residents of Punggol East should not be deprived of the ability to choose what they consider to be the most attractive ideology or the candidate that will be most effective in holding the government to account. Furthermore in GE 2011 we were prevented from being able to contest any SMC, even in areas where we had been conducting outreach for some time.  For these reasons we are strongly considering contesting this seat if and when a by-election is called.

    Finally we note the PM’s comment in his letter accepting Mr. Palmer’s resignation that the affair was particularly serious because it involved a PA staff member.

    The control of grassroots organizations by the PAP is just one of the ways the PAP uses state resources to affect and influence voters.

    Some of the others are state control of the media, obstacles to the growth and funding of alternative parties, the harassment of individuals through defamation suits, the control of the Elections Department by the Prime Minister’s office, and threats to withhold upgrading from Opposition wards. 

    In Putin’s Russia these types of actions have been referred to as tantamount to electoral fraud. In Singapore they weaken the PAP’s claim to have the people’s mandate.

    Kenneth Jeyaretnam

    Secretary-General
