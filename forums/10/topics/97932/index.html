    There is a difference between a small place like Singapore and a large country like USA.

    There is definitely a trade off between civil liberty and the law that bans guns. Two spectrum of considertaions. To ban guns is to prevent small disputes between individuals turning into dangerous gun fights that may hurt innocents as well. Even in Singapore now, small disputes could turn into big fights creating casualties. The second consideration is to preven small arms to be used by robbers or any syndicates to commit crimes. Laws banning guns would probably be effective in achieving the first objective, but there is a trade off in the second; those victims of criminals would not be able to protect themselves against these criminals effectively.

    For a big country like USA, there are people who live in isolated farms or ulu ulu places. Unlike in city areas, police officers could not reach you within minutes of call for help. Thus, personal defence and safety has to fall on each individuals. Having a gun could even out the odds against robbers. This is true.

    However, in Singapore's context, we live in a ver compact style. With such density of residential population, pschological and physical stress may result in more frictions among the population. Thus, banning guns would be the wiser move. Even if criminals possess guns, it is not easy to escape from their crime scenes in such a compact city. You go bang bang, the whole neighbourhood will know. Unlike in USA, if you go bang bang in the ulu ulu place, nobody knows.

    The harsh law on gun possession definitely has its effects on criminals. Criminals are not stupid people. If money is what they are after, no need to use gun that could endanger their own life if they get caught.

    This has explained why there are less crimes committed with guns in Singapore, though we cannot completely eradicate the possibility.

    However, in the context of a terrorist threat, the ground rules might have changed. It is not money that they are after but innocent lives and blood that they are after. Thus, the odds against the potential victims will increase. Whether releasing the rules on guns possession will help, is yet to be examined.

    Goh Meng Seng
