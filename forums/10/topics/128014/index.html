    Speaking of credibility, the leaders have been talking about plan to increase the population from 1990s but failed. The last year of the dragon saw the highest birth then population growth came back down.

    Then came the double whammy to the population growth in the form of migration of the educated due to erroneous elitist education policy.

    Thousands are migrating every year to Australia, USA, Canada, even China ..Net population will continue to be insignificantly small.

    The post-war baby boomers who started businesses and set up factories during the 1970s and 1980s are retiring at 55-60 in large number from 2001 to 2006. These founding entrepreneurs were not being helped due to high-cost, pro-foreign business policies and government going into businesses to compete against them. Therefore the economy is going to slow down further.

    Past economic strategy was to (1) set up Jurong to industrialise --1960s (2) graduate from labor-intensive to capital-intensive --1970s (3) upgrade to value-add sectors -- 1980s.

    The economy since 1980s were heavily dependent on foreign investments without pursuit of any parallel plan to build own capabilities. One hundred and one reasons will be given not to help the locals and SMEs. Under such short-sighted economic planning, any value-adding plans would only be nothing but ad hoc or random.

    Yet, without an overall economic master plan to leverage our strengths and counter our weaknesses, the economy is basically directionless. The local businesses are suffering and suffering without much help from the government.

    As I see it, Singapore past policies are causing the economy to flounder badly during the last two recessions. Other Asian economies are recovering faster than our economy. The standard of living of the people are dropping back to the 1970s due to unemployments, lower salaries and asset bubble bursts.

    South Korea has gone through the same Asian Financial crisis and recessions. It has come up on top with better manufacturing growths. Its own MNCs are churning out large number of world-beating products and keeping up the economic race.

    The local businesses are dying and MNCs are progressively moving out to less costly destinations.

    Leaders are not consistent and focused in implementing a creative economic master plan to provide directions to help all to compete in world market. They are truly running out of ideas despite all the claims of their own talents.

    The above-stated reasons i.e. loss of economic planning strategy etc, are precisely why we need to go for increasing the population growth, more participation by youths in politics etc. If the fundamental errors as posted are not corrected our leaders will have to do all the unnecessary things like trying to get more babies or set up a casino.

    Where is the true leadership and talents among them to grow or nurture more entrepreneurs or overcome our loss of economic competitiveness due to high costs created by them or simply lack of ideas.

    Now that the economy is having a "back to the black" correction in 2004 after two prolonged recessions of 1996-1999 and 2001-2003, the leaders will be quick to claim credit for the recovery when actually such a recovery is the cyclical up-and-down of world economy.

    More ideas are needed to revive the present economy. The first task is to come up with a vibrant economic master plan and produce a strong team to carry out the plan if Singaporeans are to maintain their asset values or earn a better living.
