    Annan urges Japan to express remorse to China, S. Korea
    U.N. Secretary-General Kofi Annan said Wednesday that Japan could warm its soured relationships with China and South Korea if it expressed remorse and regret for its acts during World War II, following the example of Germany.

    Japan's relations with the two countries should be improved, Annan said in a meeting with Takenori Kanzaki, leader of Komeito, at a hotel in Tokyo.

    The leader of Komeito, the junior coalition partner of the ruling Liberal Democratic Party, responded that he will make efforts to improve relations with the two countries.

    Japan's relations with China and South Korea have been strained primarily by Prime Minister Junichiro Koizumi's controversial visits to Yasukuni Shrine in Tokyo, which honors Class-A war criminals of along with the war dead. (Jiji Press)
