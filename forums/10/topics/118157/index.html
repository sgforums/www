About Speaking Mandarin, and Commerce

  • ShutterBug
    17 Feb 05, 19:06
    Let me share with you all, my experience. I am not against the Chinese language, Mandarin, as I am a Chinese myself - but more accurately a Teochew with a little mix of Portugese.

    Some months ago, I made a phone call to one of the many auto accessories shops in Singapore, and I enquired about the brands, models, and prices of their car audio products. A girl on the other end of the line asked me in Mandarin if I can speak Mandarin. I replied in Mandarin that I can only speak a little, but am more comfortable speaking English. She simply said that NOBODY there can speak English, and suggested that I get someone who speaks Mandarin to make the enquiry for me.

    Tell me, is this customer service in Singapore, or China? Is this customer service, or has the Speak Mandarin Campagne done more than what it was expected to do?

    I have experienced on many occasions, when I step into a shop, a petrol kiosk, buying a movie ticket, and even at government offices, that I am greeted and spoken to in Mandarin. Has the standard medium of communication in Singapore for commerce business and trade been changed to Mandarin instead of English?? Or was it because out of convenience, the sales person, service provider, or officer, speaks to me in Mandarin because he/she does not know how to communicate in English?

    Whatever the reason, I know that in the service industry, service providers should never assume the customer prefers to speak Mandarin. In fact, service providers are supposed to make customers feel comfortable - by speaking whatever language the customer prefers.

    So now I see buses with large bold letters pasted on the rear ends that shouts "Speak Good English". Where's the co-relation? I don't see it!

    It doesn't necessarily mean that when you are comfortable in Mandarin, others around you should too.
