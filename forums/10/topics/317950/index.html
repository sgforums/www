  • Atobe

    Singapore maybe small, and may not have the space for more primary industry work in animal and agricultural sector.

    However, there are many farms owned by Singaporeans overseas in Indonesia, Malaysia, Myanmar, Thailand and Vietnam - either on a wholly owned basis or joint-venture with locals.

    In such investments, Singapore will bring finance and technical expertise to modernise farming techniques and increase yields, as well as the marketing experience to the global market.

    Over the last ten years, the Singapore Government has played 'catch-up' to the Singaporean private enterprises, who have actually led the way into China and India - and also into Australia and South Africa. Many Singaporean private enterprises have got into agricultural investments to provide upstream raw materials needed for their industrial facilities, which prolong the shelf life of the agricultural products in being marketed to the global market.
