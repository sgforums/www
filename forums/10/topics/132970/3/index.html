    Let's take a step back, and look at this scenario objectively:

    If you do your job well in the EYES OF YOUR EMPLOYER (not yours), they would have no reason to retrench, much less fire you, and if the company was downsizing, they'd still give you a great recommendation letter that would put you up for employment somewhere else. May not be as great but hey, you still have a job.

    You carry on making your CPF downpayments but somewhere along the way you meet your partner in life and you decide to buy a house. You deplete your CPF almost completely to pay it off. Is it worthy immediate investment ?

    CHOY! but Singaporeans lifestyle isn't all too perfect, people fall sick and undergo medical treatment, once again you pay out of your CPF. Just like you pay for your family's and their other misc fees such as school fees.

    And you expect to have CPF left?

    CPF IS sufficient for a reasonable lifestyle that you had (e.g. if you earned 15k/mth u roughly have 5k/mth now, if you earned 3k/mth, u roughly have 1k/mth now) provided you do not waste your CPF away.

    Since Singaporeans are so fond of drawing and paying out of their CPF, what is the next best step?

    I feel that upon reaching 21yrs of age, everyone should open a retirement fund whereby 10% of their monthly salary is deposited. This over 44yrs (65 std) will grow into a tidy sum through guaranteed deposits. 43% of salary gone now (10%+33%). 57% left. Save up 27% for immediate usage should there be a need (including insurance; I CANNOT STRESS THIS ENOUGH! Life insurance is very important! The odds of accidents happening are 1 in a million, but everyone dies, note LIFE INSURANCE and not accident insurance, I mean take both policies out but in proportion, along with your car,house blah blah insurance). 30% is left for immediate spending. 30% is enough to live within your means. Some people i know spend only 20% of their salary a month. I know everyone needs pampering so that 10% is for the weekly restaurant outing or family trip to the beach.

    The reason why Singaporeans often run dry is they buy unnecessary stuff (even me), I feel that the govt is blamed wrongly in this case, although I do not like their way of looking through rose tinted glasses. So what, you think Singapore will have a better future in your hands? If you can't even manage your own retirement fund, you expect that you will do a better job with the lives of 4million?

    The model I provided above is just one that I constructed through nights of careful thoughts and observation.
