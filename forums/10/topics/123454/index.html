    From 1965, the government has been preaching prudence, diligence and hardworks.

    Initially, such policies of hardwork, prudence etc were working. Monies were spent wisely on education and other basic needs like medical and hospitalization and utilities.

    Then came elitism called meritocracy in the 1970s. Government believe that they are the talents or elites with non-stop upgrading of civil servants to all kinds of sinecure superscale positions with double- and triple- promotions driving up the costs of running the government across the board.

    In order to maintain such high costs, all basic services were charged at market prices with corporatisation schemes of all kinds to farm out services to private companies who then charge citizens at market prices. As a result, much of the Taxes become accumulated as surpluses.

    The whole economy began to go down and become uncompetitive because such budgetary surpluses of S$2 billions per annum and further off-budget surpluses by individual statutory boards like HDB, JTC, URA etc amounting to another S$1-2 billions per annum sucked away monies from the economy.

    It is not possible to make the economy competitive as no matter how the government tries to promote entrepreneurship or value-adding economic strategy, its own domestic economy simply cannot make monies and citizens cannot survive in a place with such high-costs.

    Unless urgent actions are taken to address such high costs due to corporatization schemes, double charges on land and triple charges on vehicles and avoidance of extravagant spending, it is of no use talking about building an open or all-inclusive society, calling on youths to participate in justifications, promoting more elitism or new elitism or asking people to sacrifice more fee increases.

    To: stupidissmart & Iamgoondu,

    From the exchanges of views, I cannot help noticing:

    (1) Singapore government has always been very miserly in spending on people's medical, education, housing, utilities, transportation by recovering every cent through a system of creative accounting.

    It will keep pointing out to the people the need to avoid welfarism. It acquires lands at nominal costs paid for by taxpaers yet sell flats built on such acquired lands at market prices to the citizens. Indirect taxes through double-charge on lands through HDB and triple-charge on vehicle through LTA impose great burdens on people and businesses alike. Yet, it would continue to resort to all kinds of corporatisation schemes to recover more costs.

    (2) However through years of denying spending on essential services which people need for living, big surpluses are accumulated into billions by both fiscal budgeting and off-budget surpluses by various statutory boards.

    So monies are available for spending. If the departments don't spend the surpluses or budget allocations, it will be presumed that the allocations are not needed so every department will try to spend any allocations or surpluses.

    Coming to think of it, this kind of situation requires the leadership of the PM and whole cabinet to avoid getting into.

    It may be the result of paranoid holding back of spending in essential services or corporatization schemes of all kinds that HDB, LTA, JTC, Town Councils and all departments have obtained big surpluses and will now have to spend them for obvious reasons that if they don't spend them, the monies will have to go into more surpluses or future budget allocations may get reduced.

    The latest posh community clubs and air-conditioned bus interchanges and many more unreported projects are just continuing unclear thinking by the cabinet on the entire budgetary process, allocations of funds, and lack of guidances to ministries on fiscal policies and implementation.

    Economists' traditional theories on 'spending for the sake of spending to stimulate economic activities are questionable. Billions were spent on big projects during last two recessions in this manner (for the sake of spending) without apparent effect to revive the economy.

    There should be more discussions and feedbacks on how surpluses should be spent to avoid clinging on of such fallacious views of the past resulting in indiscriminate spending on unproductive concrete which produce little or no return to lower costs of living of citizens.
