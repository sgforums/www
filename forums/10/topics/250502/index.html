    Well, it sure is difficult to find much information on the income distribution of Singapore! I have some stats on income inequality, but they are outdated.

    According to the Human Development Report 2006, based on 1998 stats (why do they not have updated stats??), Singapore's Gini coefficient is 42.5, where 0 rep zero inequality (ie everyone's income is equal) and 100 rep full inequality (richest owns all). This is much higher than the other countries listed above Singapore in the HDI with the sole exception of HongKong, although I admit this figure is prob no longer accurate since the stats almost 10 years ago.

    Nevertheless, they do give a clear indication that Singapore IS heading towards an unhealthy distribution of income. Perhaps policies implemented should focus on distributing more income, without going to the extent of affecting econ growth (coz if economy isn't growing, there isn't any point in discussing income equality in the first place), and without leaning towards the view that perfect equality is good (and feasible).
