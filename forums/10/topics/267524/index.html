  • <Precious>
    12 Jul 07, 12:58
    When the tenancy agreement was signed, it was for a 2-year period with a $2K rent payable by the tenant to the landlord. Unless, there is also another clause in this same agreement that provides for the landlord to adjust the rent payable at any time in his discretion, the landlord is strictly bound by the terms of that tenancy agreement for the 2-year period.

    As such, the landlord's subsequent rental revision to $3K will amount to a breach of contract and could be sued as such.

    However, there may be other details which may have to be evaluated before the entire matter could be more accurately assessed. PM me if you are interested to find out more!
