    Expert witness says old NKF generated huge surpluses from health screenings
    SINGAPORE:

    An expert witness says none, or only 4 cents of every dollar raised by the old NKF went to the charity's beneficiaries and programmes in the past.
    British accountant David Jeremy Lee gave this testimony in the ongoing civil suit between NKF and its former board members.
    In court on Monday, lawyer for third party defendant Alwyn Lim, questioned Mr Lee on why the figure he came up with was so low.
    Mr Leong Hee Tean said accounting firm KPMG, who was commissioned to audit the charity by the new Board, put the figure at 10 cents for every dollar raised.
    Mr Lee said the figures were different as KPMG had a wider scope in its investigations.
    Mr Lee had been engaged by NKF to examine the charity's income and expenditure.
    He said the free health screenings provided by the foundation under former CEO TT Durai were highly profitable, and the old NKF had treated the costs of these health screenings as a disbursement of donor's monies to beneficiaries, which was misleading.
    Mr Lee pointed out that the health screenings had generated a surplus through Lifedrop donations from attendees.
    Another witness, the former deputy director of integrated communications said he discovered that almost all the figures released in publicity materials by the old NKF were inaccurate.
    The materials reported that 56 cents of every dollar had gone to help patients and beneficiaries. - CNA /dt
