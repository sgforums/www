    Safety and crime rate is only one out of the many different criteria by which the quality of life in any particular city can be judged. And it is not such an overwhelmingly crucial element that a high crime rate would render a city unliveable. In any case, a high crime rate does not necessarily imply that one would definitely, eventually, become a victim of crime oneself, just as a high rate of traffic accidents does not mean that one would be knocked down by a car one day.

    In fact I believe that with prudence and caution one can always prevent oneself from becoming a victim of a crime or traffic accident. I lived in London for three years and one day I learned from the news that a fatal shooting took place not more than 200 metres from the house I lived in. I found it no cause for alarm, and nor do I find it necessary for anyone to cause such hysterics over some news reports of violence in the cities. In fact I think if you look closer at the statistics and experience the life in the city you'll find that violence is the exception rather than norm.
