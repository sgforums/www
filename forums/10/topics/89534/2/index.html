    There is no real answer to your investment question - how to invest the S$10 K to produce big profits.

    It is because the risks for every business is different. Every decision is relative to its particular risk of that business.

    So it boils down to the person's investment strategy how to identify business opportunities (it comes knocking if at all only once after a long long time). Singapore has the opportunity to do it once. For the second time, it has got to try harder.

    The art of war is a more reasonable and doable strategy except that it has many imponderables. To equip one with strengths in human resource, supplies and equipment is its basic strategy and to utilise one strengths so acquired is the next complementary step.

    In addition, one has to identify enemy's or competitors' strengths and weaknesses and come up with one's own plan to overcome and overwhelm with one strength while avoiding competitors's charge.

    A simpler solution will be to formulate IDEAS + MAKE ENQUIRIES (build supplies and resources, assess competitors) + SECURE CONTRACTS + WORK OUT DOABLES TO OBTAIN RESULTS.

    If the right idea is identified using one's own acumen and instinct plus data, go at it consistently to reach goals in 3 steps as mentioned. Regards, Happy investing!!
