    Nyet... if the Japs had invaded Russia instead of SE Asia, they would have had to face the Siberian divisions - the divisions which are well-equipped and well-suited to the cold. After the Soviet spymaster Richard Sorge assured Stalin that the Japs would not invade Russia, Stalin diverted those Siberian divisions to fight the Germans. It was those Siberian divisions which proved to be the turning point - no way could the Germans handle all that firepower being thrown at them.

    Besides, in 1939, border skirmishes actually did occur between the Soviets and the Japanese Army in Manchuria. The brilliant general Georgy Zhukov managed to drive back the 6th Japanese Army with brilliant mobile armour tactics. The battle experience gained by the Siberian divisions in this battle proved to be useful in their counterstrike against the Nazis.

    Besides, these skirmishes convinced the Japs to not attack the Soviets and to risk going to war with the Americans instead.
