    Daddy you talk too much financial terms only some ppl can understand. I translate for you with a simple example... John buys a house for $100,000 in America. Every year he pays $10 in instalments for the next 10 years. The loan company sells the loan to a bank which put under assets $100,000. Now, somebody in the bank has a bright idea. Why not let people speculate on whether John can repay back the full loan in 10 years? So they break the 10 year loan up and sell pieces of it to other people like this. Grade A John Derivative - loan repayments by John in the first two years. Grade B John Derivative - loan repayments by John in year 3-5 Grade C John Derivative - loan repayments by John in year 5-10 Since its common sense that the longer the time frame, the more higher the chance of John defaulting on the loan. Its good since everyone makes money from poor John and people that buy the securitised mortage credit backed derivatives. But what happens when John and millions of people like him default on their loans? Everyone down the line that made money off John gets burnt with the exception of the loan company that directly lent the money to John and quickly packaged it off for sales. SUMMARY The bottomline is that lots of people made money and lots of people got burnt. There are hundreds of small loan companies, most banks also offer credit directly. Its very unlikely that anyone profited tremendously, so just take it as a pinch of salt. :lol:
