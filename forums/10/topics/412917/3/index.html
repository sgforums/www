    Corrupt Database Caused Prolonged Site Downtime

    Temasek Review went offline from Monday 2200 hours to Tuesday 2200 hours due to a corrupted database caused by a failed upgrading of our wordpress.

    We had originally scheduled an upgrading of our wordpress on Monday 2200 hours and it was expected to be completed within an hour.

    However, the upgrading did not went well due to the incompatibility of our present customised theme and certain plugins. The failed upgrading created a serious security breach on our site, allowing any registered users full access to our administrative backend. Our tech had to shut the site down immediately. We later learnt that the failed update had corrupted the database. Since our servers are sync in real time, our mirror server suffered the same fate.

    As it was already midnight, we were unable to grab hold of the programmer who customised the theme for our site to work with support personnel from our service provider to restore the corrupted database (a corrupted database).

    Our service provider originally suggested restoring the site from the previous day’s database but we decided against it as hundreds of posts would have been lost. We decided to salvage the corrupted database and try to restore it as fully as possible and this was achieved today with our programmer and service provider working in tandem.

    Fortunately for us, the large chunk of data corrupted were subscriber’s information and previous site archives from 2008. Other than that, all data remains intact.

    If you are a registered subscriber to our site and you find that you are no longer able to login with your existing username, try executing a password retrieval and if it fails, then please re-register yourself. Contributors, Authors and Editor’s data are all intact.

    We would like to take this opportunity to thank our host RTG (Asia) Network and our programmer for their professional service, for restoring our database and modifying the theme to suit the latest wordpress version.

    Additionally we would also like to apologise to our readers for this unexpected downtime and thank you for your patience and understanding.
