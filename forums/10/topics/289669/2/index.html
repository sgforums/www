    Below the governing system there exists many details which need to be considered by respective operators covering such details from approvals of processes and resources and setting up of facilities etc which need to be in compliance with issues of safety, efficiency, fairness and relevant micro-level decision processes but these are often delegated. Unless of course our leaders are not able to delegate them to the lower levels and are too overly concerned with micro-management or nannying.

    The most important and critical governing process is the overall functions and duties as spelled out in the constitutions and administering of these functions and roles. Good leaders should be capable of delegating these lower-level operational details to the respective rungs and not deviate from their major policy and implementation set-ups. If they cannot delegate them as are often the case then they are not efficient in delegation and governing that is all.

    For a sample of such delegated management processes : see www.managefranchise.blogspot.com for illustration or even details..

    So we should not have to have MM Lee overly concerned with challenging the SIA union chief on the pilot wage issue if he has succeeded in delegating all the such detailed operational issues to the proper operational level.

    The fundamental governing framework of democracy should concern with the election and structure of government, establishment of laws, rights and obligations of government and citizens and facilities for proper balanced functioning of the three governing bodies known as judiciary, legislative and executive of the nation.

    In our case our government overshadow the first two i.e. the judiciary and legislative and that may be the reason why they are being caught with too much detailed issues and have to handle every issue themselves in which case I agree with you that the government will be slowing down their competitions at the operational level.

    So however busy or central is our island or global are our ports or facilities, we should not expect the government to look after them every day. The detailed operations are often delegated to the respective organizations and private sectors to look after them through regulations and operational set-ups by delegations somewhere systematically without involvement of any ministers unless of course the ministers do not know how to set up the delegated processes as sampled in www,managefranchise.blogspot.com.

    There is no short-cut to having our ports bypassing government though when it comes to more critical and important issues of course but these are usually also well attended to by their respective boards or operators so there is no concern of the government to look over the shoulders of every entity to look after such mundane issues.

    For example the modification of terminal facilities for arrival of A380 Airbus needs not be dealt with by the government or ministers.
