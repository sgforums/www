    The company has not committed a breach of statutory law as far as I know.

    Singapore does not have any legislation that explicitly forbids discriminatory employment practices; so long as an advertisement is not so blatantly racist e.g. "Chinese/Malays/Indians do not bother applying because we do not like to hire Asians", the company is theoratically not under any other legal obligation besides making sure they're not violating other acts such as the Sedition Act and so on.

    The closest we have to so-called "enforcement" of fair employment practices is the Tripartite Guidelines for Employment Practices (TAFEP). Check out their website for mroe info. Whether a company chooses to follow the guidelines stipulated is entirely up to their discretion. Many companies are paper signatories to TAFEP but in reality, violate the regulations they pledged to adopt i.e. wayang. The only thing that truly discourages a company from going overboard with discrimination is the negative publicity they will get from the public and the media (but as you should know, the Singapore media is err...)

    This is not America. The offending advertisement is perfectly within the bounds of the laws set by a government that will not pass an anti-discriminatory bill into legislation for fear of affecting business competitiveness.
