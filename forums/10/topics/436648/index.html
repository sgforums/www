To heal the social divide

  • NiceChap
    22 Aug 11, 22:32

    There are so much hullabaloo about the coming Presidential Election.

    Even after the GE, why some people are still resenting the ruling party. As long as their basic fundamental needs such as education, housing, transportation and medical cost are not satisfactorily met, these people ,especially the middle income and low income group, will not listen to the anybody including the presidential hopefuls affiliated to the ruling party. As such, all the presidential hopefuls tried to distant themselves from the ruling party. How successful in their shaking off the PAP image remains to be seen.

    Before GE 2011, many people were asked to take CPF cut and bite the bullet during economic downturn. When times were better, prices kept rising while their pay remained stagnant. Foreigners were welcomed with open arms. All their dreams of having affordable basic needs were shattered. The situation was aggravated when the parliament announced astronomical pay and bonuses for their ministers and president. That was the last straw that broke the camel’s back. Until now, this has been and will still be an issue even after the presidential election. The government tried to appease the people rage with workfare package just before the GE but the people were not persuaded. So who else did the people turn to for their plight: the opposition.

    These people started to depend on the opposition to speak up for their grievances. During the GE, upgrading of HDB flats was used as carrot and stick  to the chagrin of the people. The ruling party even redrew the boundaries of the GRC hoping to beat the opposition which the people deemed was their only hope and voice. By hook or by crook they must have the opposition to look into their welfare. Anybody that tries to stifle the opposition will be vehemently met with criticism as seen very recently between the clash of Paya Lebar CCC and Mr Chen Show Mao. They just want a level playing field in politics.

    To heal the social divide would take an enormous task. It is not as simple as overnight reverse engineering as most problems are deep seated. Like it or not, the opposition is here to stay.
