    Guide to the Singapore GE 2011 Part 1

     

    As per 2006 GE, there are currently 9 Single Member Constituencies (SMC) and 14 Group Representation Constituencies (GRC) - 5 GRCs of 6- seats and 9 GRC of 5-seats, which gives a total of 84 seats.
     
    To rule effectively, the ruling party will have to control two- third of the available seats- 56.
     
    One of the GRCs- Jurong GRC- has 4 MPs due to the death of one of the MP. Calls have been made for a by- election to be held for the vacant ward- Bukit Batok, but unfortunately, it was sidelined by the EXTRAORDINARY government.
     
    No of choices for Singapore voters
     
    For walkovers, there are no choices available for the voters.
     
    For 1 vs 1 contest, there are 3 choices available for the voters. They are: 1) PAP, 2) Opposition party and last but not least, 3) spoilt vote.
     
    For 3- cornered fight, there are 4 choices available for the voters. They are: 1) PAP, 2) Opposition party, 3) another Opposition party or Independent, and last but not least, 4) spoilt vote.
