    Sylvia Lim rebuts Ministry of National Development on behalf of Hougang MP Low Thia Kiang

    The Chairman of the Workers’ Party, NCMP Ms Sylvia Lim has written a reply to rebut the letter by Minister for National Development’s Press Secretary entitled: “Ministry: MP Low wrong on lift upgrading” which was published on Tuesday.

    Ms Lim took issue with MND’s reply which seems to imply that the grassroots advisor was more appropriate than the MP to announce the LUP plans because he was able to gather residents’ input and marshal support for the plans.

    The PAP grassroots representative for Hougang Mr Eric Low sparked a public backlash when he unshamedly claimed credit for bringing LUP to Hougang two weeks ago which led many Singaporeans to question why the announcement was not made by the MP for Hougang, Mr Low Thia Kiang instead.

    Writing on behalf of Hougang MP Mr Low Thia Kiang who is currently out of town, Ms Lim wrote that Mr Low’s letter was not about claiming credit for LUP:

    “He (Low) has stated that he is prepared to work with the government’s appointees for the benefit of his constituents, and indeed has met the grassroots advisor several times over many months to give input on the LUP plans for Hougang.”

    She added sarcastically that the LUP was introduced “to rectify a design flaw in public housing which does not cater for an ageing society. Given this imperative, the public interest should trump politics.”

    According to Ms Lim, Mr Low was “compelled” to write the letter to Straits Times Forum because HDB’s initial reply to Mr Yusuf who had written in lampooning HDB and PA for not following protocol seems to insinuate that Mr Low was unwilling to work with the government.

    Mr Eric Low had earlier revealed that he enjoyed a cordial working relationship with Mr Low Thia Kiang and the two even had coffee together after the last meeting on the LUP.

    Ms Lim also rebuked MND’s claims that LUP Is funded from budget surpluses which Opposition MPs are not responsible for generating.

    She wrote that the budget surpluses are hardly the effort of the government alone, as they include significant contributions from the public through taxes, levies and stamp fees, which the government simply reaps.

    Ms Lim ended the letter by berating MND for not respecting the people’s choice of their MP:

    “Finally, MND also appears to interpret the General Elections as an event where the only outcome which matters is who forms the government. This shows the respect the government has for the people’s choice of Member of Parliament, who is vested with the Constitutional mandate to represent the constituency.”

    The PAP won 82 out of 84 seats contested during the last general elections. The two opposition wards are Hougang and Potong Pasir which were denied critical estate upgrading by the PAP government because they did not express “support” for the government’s plan.

    Upgrading was constantly used as a carrot to entice residents to vote for the PAP though being taxpayers, they are entitled to it. Senior Minister Goh Chok Tong had promised a $100 million upgrading plan for Hougang and Potong Pasir if the PAP candidates won.

    Though Mr Eric Low lost to Mr Low Thia Kiang, he still lingered around in Hougang as the “grassroots adviser”, an euphemism for PAP losers.

    The PAP government has drawn online flak for choosing to work through their unelected representatives instead of the opposition MPs who are voted in by the residents.

    Unless more opposition MPs are voted into parliament the next election, this age-old “practice” promulgated by the PAP is likely to continue.
