  • robertteh
    1 May 05, 21:12
    It is not that the government has no monies. The problem is caused by our own unique system of government - every department is a cost centre and is expected to break even or make monies.

    Under the cost-centred system of government, the department that makes a deficit will increase their fees and charges on the citizens. This is equivalent to some sorts of creative accounting where gains or surpluses will be transferred to the central consolidated fund and deficits will be recovered from the citizens. When HDB makes the monies from land acquisition it transfer the surpluses to the central consolidated fund. The moment HDB or their offshoots town council make a deficit it will quickly increase fees and charges. JTC, HDB and LTA etc make S$14 billions over 10 years - a lot of monies - but these are retained and not passed on to citizens in lower costs.

    This has been the problem with our top-down management. Such cost-centred government or Creative accounting is undesirable - it kills the goose that lays the golden eggs depriving businesses of competitiveness - but nobody is able to do anything. There is no overall deficit but the government under creative accounting will keep increasing their fees or charges. So it is a one-way process: head I win tail you lose.
