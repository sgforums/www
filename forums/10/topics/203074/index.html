  • Gedanken
    3 Aug 06, 14:02
    Read both volumes ages ago.

    I'd be lying if I said I didn't like some parts of the books. They do explain why and how a lot of things in modern Singapore came to be, and there was some sound and pragmatic thinking behind many of the decisions made by the original PAP leaders like Rajaratnam and Goh Keng Swee. I was particularly amused by the description of EW Barker's nervousness when signing the agreement to split with Malaysia.

    That said, in the second volume, one cannot help thinking that there was a trace of self-aggrandisement, what with claims of being the origin of the "evil empire" tag for the Soviet Union and all. Still, it is an autobiography, so why not?
