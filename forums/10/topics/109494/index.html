    Clarification from NTUC Income as reported in 27 Dec ST Forum Page

    ----------------
    Dec 27, 2004
    We're not selling cars, says NTUC Income chief
    I REFER to the report, 'NTUC Income to sell cars' (ST, Dec 25).

    The article is misleading. We are not selling cars. We are not promising discounts.

    Here is the correct situation:

    Last month, we asked our policyholders if they are interested in participating in collective purchase of vehicles and properties. About 1,000 of them expressed interest.

    Two weeks ago, we held a briefing for the interested policyholders to discuss several issues, including collective purchase, parallel import, warranty, certificate of entitlement, loan and insurance. Two hundred policyholders attended this seminar.

    At the briefing, we invited a few dealers to present their offer of a discount for certain car models.

    A few days later, your senior correspondent Christopher Tan wrote and asked me to comment about our proposed car cooperative. I did not know what he was talking about. I replied that we were not forming a new car cooperative.

    Later, he clarified that he was interested about our collective purchase briefing. I referred him to my colleagues who were responsible for this project.

    In the ST report, he said that 'the usually chatty Mr Tan was evasive about the bulk car purchase plan'. I find this remark to be unjustified and mischievous. In any case, we do not consider our activity to be worth reporting. This is why we did not invite your journalist to cover our event.

    If your paper wishes to report this event, you should at least ensure that your report is reasonably accurate. Your headline was particularly misleading.

    You have caused a lot of unnecessary trouble for us. I ask you to have my reply displayed prominently in your paper.

    Tan Kin Lian
    Chief Executive Officer
    NTUC Income

    CHRISTOPHER TAN REPLIES:

    We reported factually what happened at the presentation NTUC Income made to prospective car buyers. Getting them to buy cars through Income is, in our view, getting into the car-selling business. That was the gist of our report.
