    SINGAPORE : Minister Mentor Lee Kuan Yew has rebutted criticism by Workers' Party chief Low Thia Khiang that the People's Action Party is not a First World government.

    In a statement issued by his press secretary, Mr Lee said that if the PAP did not have the capabilities of a First World government, Singapore would not have risen up to the First World standards, as measured by the World Bank, the World Economic Forum, the International Institute for Management Development and Transparency International.

    In his statement, Mr Lee also reiterated that the PAP would respond at the level the opposition chooses to fight for votes.

    Mr Lee said that when the Workers' Party was headed by JB Jeyaretnam, it "played by the standards of street fighters".

    So the PAP had to respond in kind, said the Minister Mentor.

    Mr Lee said, "The Workers' Party under Low Thia Khiang must take this opportunity having got rid of Jeyaretnam to begin the process of getting the WP to First World standards."

    Responding to this, the WP has made the issue of a First World government a key platform of their election campaign.

    The two other key issues are accountability and hope for the future, although the party declined to elaborate.

    Responding to Mr Lee, WorkersÂ’ Party Chairman Sylvia Lim said there is more to being a First World nation than economic rankings.

    She claims Singaporeans score poorly when it comes to happiness.

I think that as far as the government is concerned they should try and give people a life which they find meaningful, so there is some concern on that front as well." - CNA /ct
