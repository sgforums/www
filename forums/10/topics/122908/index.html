    Dear All,

    It seems that my own experience with EZ Link is not unique. The following
    letter to ST forum has confirmed another over charge:

    ST, April 5, 2005

    Is fare updating system reliable for loop services?

    LAST Saturday, at about 11.25am, my wife and I took an SBS Transit service 9
    from Bedok Interchange to Eastpoint in Simei for lunch.

    When the person in front of me alighted, I noticed that his ez-link card
    showed a deduction of $1.53. Instinctively, I sensed that something was
    amiss and checked the deduction for my card when I alighted. The amount also
    showed $1.53.

    As the distance from Bedok to Eastpoint is not very great, $1.53 seems a bit
    too expensive.

    The next day, I went to the ez-link website to check the deductions on my
    card. Not surprisingly, a deduction of $1.53 was made on April 2 at 11.43am
    on my wife's card as well as mine.

    Not convinced that the fare was $1.53, I went to the SBS website to check.
    It calculated my fare to be $0.83.

    I noticed that SBS service 9 is a loop service to Airport Cargo Road.
    Perhaps the auto fare updating system could have mistaken the bus to be at
    the opposite bus stop at the point when I alighted.

    The SBS website showed that the fare from Bedok to Eastpoint (opposite bus
    stop) to be $1.53. This raises a number of questions:

    How many passengers have been overcharged while taking SBS service 9 all
    this while?

    Is this a frequent occurrence on loop services?

    How reliable is the auto fare updating system for loop services?

    I would like to suggest a few ways to overcome this problem:

    Incorporate into the system a means to countercheck the fare with the time
    taken for the entire journey. It would have been impossible for me to have
    travelled from Bedok Interchange to Airport Cargo Road to Eastpoint in a
    span of 20 minutes.

    Link the system to the bus odometer to countercheck the fare with the
    distance travelled.

    Chua Khee Seng
