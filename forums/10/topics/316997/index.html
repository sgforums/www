    SINGAPORE: A Jakarta court has turned down an appeal by Temasek Holdings and ruled that the Singapore investment company must sell or reduce its stakes in two Indonesian telecom companies.

    The Central Jakarta District Court ruled on Friday that Temasek must either sell its stake in Indosat or Telkomsel, or halve its shareholdings in both companies. The court also said that Temasek has 12 months to do so.

    Earlier, Indonesia's anti-trust watchdog, the KPPU, had said that Temasek must cut its holdings within two years. In addition, Temasek must also pay 15 billion Indonesian rupiah (US$1.6 million) in penalties.

    Last November, the KPPU said Temasek had violated cross-ownership regulations in the country's telecoms industry by its indirect ownership of Indosat and Telkomsel.

    Temasek owns substantial stakes in SingTel and fully owns Singapore Technologies Telemedia. In turn, SingTel owns 35 percent of Telkomsel, while ST Telemedia's control of Asia Mobile Holdings gives it a 40 percent stake in Indosat.

    Responding to the latest ruling, Temasek said in a statement that it is deeply disappointed by the verdict, adding that it will appeal against the district court's decision under the laws of Indonesia and continue to seek legal redress.

    Goh Yong Siang, Temasek's managing director, strategic relations, said: "The facts are Temasek has no shares in Indosat and Telkomsel, and plays no role in their business decisions and operations. Telkomsel, in particular, is majority owned by PT Telkom, which in turn is majority owned and controlled by the Indonesian government.

    "Both Telkomsel and Indosat are regulated businesses, operating within the guidelines of the Indonesian Telecommunications Regulatory Authority or Badan Regulasi Telekomunikasi Indonesia (BRTI). It is therefore not possible for Temasek to engage in any monopolistic or anti-competitive practices in the Indonesian mobile telecommunications market." - CNA/ir
