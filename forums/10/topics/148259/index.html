Use of Medisave for expensive procedures

  • oxford mushroom
    18 Aug 05, 03:18
    There were a number of letters to the Straits Times about extending the use of Medisave for expensive procedures like a PET scan.

    The MOH spokesman reply was "..With regard to the specific request to use Medisave for PET Scan, the Ministry of Health would also like to clarify that expensive treatment does not equate better treatment..."

    It is sad that the person replying is not even a doctor. The PET scan in question is often needed to help doctors assess if a chemotherapeutic regime is having the desired effect. The PET scan would be able to tell oncologists if it is safe to reduce the course of chemotherapy, which as we know, has very serious side effects. Without it, doctors will have to give further unnecessary treatment that may well kill the patient. Procedures like PET scan and many drugs which have been shown scientifically to be valuable are not available to most Singaporeans mainly because they are expensive.

    MOH keeps harping on this line: " Hence there is a need to guard against over-servicing of subsidised patients by doctors, and treatment decisions should be based on medical need and peer-review by specialists. "

    But in truth, hospitals are expanding services in cosmetic surgery to the well-heeled whilst cancer patients are denied treatment that can improve lifespan by months to years because they are not 'cost-effective' and not a 'cure'.
