    I agree that Singapore soccer has probably won the tiger cup by luck and not true excellence.

    If we are to watch the game carefully, most of the Indonesian players can dribble the ball and charge at the goal but not our players. The rest of regional or world teams will be hard to beat.

    Let's take a closer look at how to train and win any games:-

    (1) Fitness - ? hours of training per day
    (2) Coordination - ? connectivity and long pass
    (3) Targeting at goal - increase and realize opportunity

    Assign parameters to each so that the whole team will reach excellence in fitness, coordination and shooting.

    What are team did not reach are fitness, coordination and shooting abilities.

    Hours & Intensity are parameters that may be adopted for training of fitness. Just like golf, a world class player must hit more than 2,000 balls a day. There is no if or but.

    Number of long passes and keeping in possession when adopted as parameters for 2nd Core Activity Coordination, will lead to control of game versus the goal scores or goal possibility.

    Keep training schedule to the above-mentioned focused on the above simple doables daily and weekly. Create entertaining routines as well and incentives and motivation to sustain training to reach set Fitness and Coordination.

    The keeping of scores can be done daily of attainment of parameters: "1" -non attained "2" attained and "3" excellent.

    The team is well informed of the schedule and every team member will then work with consistency and focus and see for themselves their attainment of schedules, consistency, parameters and target. Then the team will sustain their fitness and coordination to reach high level of excellence.
