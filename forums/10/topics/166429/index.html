    Since 2002, many people have given feedbacks to the government's Feedback Unit to use our internal abundant resources such as empty lands, vacant factoris and many educated unemployed to get out of our economic recessions and overcome many problems.

    Up to now, we have not heard of JTC implementing another major value-adding or new technology start-up strategy but all the scholars in EDB are trying to claim credit for sending scholars to world universities.

    There is no rocket science in trying to create value-adding industries or technology start-ups. Just follow what Taiwan has been doing in going into computerizations as their major push into science and technology. They did it in a total master plan employing holistic manpower and educational strategy.

    At least we can use the empty lands or factories to put these resources to good use, like allowing the unemployed to occupy them on short leases to produce agri products that will substitute our food and livestock import bills. NTUC can easily create more of our own sources of food supply.

    Neither have we heard of any upgraded Economic Master Plan being implemented consistently to occupy our many unemployed new graduates or retrenched workers.

    Top down autocracy cannot work well and has not been working well. Top down leaders do not produce economic competitiveness as the people below are not well motivated or exposed to come up with good products or services.

    We are only seeing some ministers busy about multi-tasking the cleaners to do a better cleaning job. What else?
