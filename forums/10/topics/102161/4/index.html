    I am really grateful for what all my teachers have done for me while I was in the schooling system. They provided me with an excellent shelter from the real world outside, and given me a chance to lean and discover myself - all the while managing all that enormous bulk of administrative work. I can still remember when I was in Secondary one, I would stay in the Media Resource Library to play after school, and the teachers were still writing furiously away at their desks after 7 pm (when I finally had to leave the place because the attendant was closing the floor off).

    To those who think teaching is easy, try doing relief teaching for one day, and you will realise that many students have no interest in study, and look forward to play time after the examinations more than getting the excellent grade that will push them towards professional jobs. You have to sort out bullies who pick on others, give a listening ear to your charges facing emotional problems (raging hormones and all...), write out lesson plans (which I think are useful only for inspection, because the schedule gets knocked out of whack anyway), maintain discipline in the class, mark papers, set examination questions, conduct remedial lessons, go for CCA meetings, and more! In addition to that, you do not just take three or four classes for one subject, you have a form class, of which you have to take charge for their overall welfare.

    All these can only be fulfilled by a person with passion for teaching, and a love for young people (I've met many teachers with that, [rant] and really felt sorry they had to deal with spoilt brats who loved to throw 5 cent coins at other people [end rant]).

    The fact that students greet teachers before every lesson is testimonial to their personal sacrifices they give to these students. No other profession can match the personal development these teachers do to their charges. Being in contact with these young people for almost half the day, they are the ones who truly shape the thinking of the next generation.
