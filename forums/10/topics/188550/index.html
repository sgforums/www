    Still remember the journalist who wrote a long letter to PM Lee to complain about his unfair dismissal on account of an article he wrote which the special editor refused to publish.

    He pleaded how being an international renown journalist he was summarily dismissed by his superial special editor on account of an unfavorable article he drafted for publication and how he was overruled by a less experienced editor.

    He pleaded with PM Lee was his explanation which showed that the special editor was being over-sensitive about his views in that article.

    It appeared that our press is totally overshadowed and controlled by the authority vested in that special editor. There was no news that that journalist was subsequently given a fair hearing by the PM. It is not known if he was reinstated or compensated for such treatment through a proper hearing.

    No wonder none of the 10 journalists having dialogue with MM Lee dared to counter the vehement points made by MM Lee about Singaporeans' fears to speak up on their views about government.
