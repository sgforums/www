    So far the government's performance has always been assessed by its own ministers and MPs with the state-controlled press and TV media giving look-good presentation in a one-sided manner.

    To be more objective and credible in assessing government's performance, all such assessments should be carried out by an independent panel.

    For example, an Independent Government's Performance Index can be set up as follows:-

    PEOPLE'S GOVERNMENT PERFORMANCE INDEX (PGPI)

    SAMPLE QUESTIONS: ADDED AT REGULAR INTERVAL IN TANDEM WITH NEW GOALS, TASKS AND POLICIES.

    (PGPI-1) Whether LTA has improved its safety standards in 2006 (after Nicol Highway collapse or overhead-bridge barricade accidents) ?

    VOTE: Reply to forum thread: PGPI-1 - (1, 2 or 3)
    (representing: 1-parameters not attained; 2=satisfactory ; 3=well done)

    TOTALISATOR: aggregated votes divided by total of maximum (3 points) in percentage for each question.

    (PGPI-2) Whether MOM has served Singaporean workers with higher priority than foreign workers : Vote: (1/2/3)

    (PGPI-3) Whether NTUC has served employees with higher priority over employers: Vote: (1/2/3)

    (PGPI-4) Whether Ministers have become more proactive in creating employment opportunities for citizens or whether they are trying to avoid the problem by urging the unemployed "not to be choosy", "go for retraining" or harping on constant "no free lunch" excuses: Vote: (1/2/3)

    (PGPI-5) Whether government has learned from the NKF case and moderated on its tax-and-recover pro-surplus policies at the expense of providing reasonably priced basic services or creating economic competitiveness to relieve unemployments.: Vote : (1/2/3)

    (PGPI-6) Whether the government is better standard of accountability and transparency at ministries, departments, statutory boards, Temasek, GLCs and NGOs after the NKF saga ? Vote: (1/2/3)

    (PGPI-7) Has the government demonstrated leadeship qualities such as the following:-i

    (1) Passion,
    (2) Ability to Motivate followers,
    (3) Ability to get people to work with coordination and apply knowledge.
    (4) Ability to Translate goals and objectives into implementation processes to accomplish goals e.g. bring about competitiveness, technology start-ups and jobs etc.
    (5) Invest monies and perform tasks with Accountability and Transparency
    (6) Collective leadership by core team to make major decisions,
    Vote: (1/2/3)

    (PGPI-8 ) Has the government made adequate efforts to lower its costs of essential government services e.g. medical, utilities, housing, transportation, education, etc, etc Vote: (1/2/3)

    (POPI-9) Have government succeeded in efficiently executed major projects e.g. MRTs, infrastructureal developments, Growth Triangle, IR within cost/time targets.

    (PGPI-10) Is there any significant relaxation of controls over the intellectual space ?

    (PGPI-11) Are there sufficient and effective implementation of safe Work site practices after the previous major safety accidents e.g. Hotel New World collapse, Nicol Highway Collapse ?

    (PGPI-12) Has government delivered the promised Swiss standard of living?

    (PGPI-13) Has government succeeded in its widely publicised "Asset enhancement scheme"?

    (PGPI-14) Has government succeeded in getting citizens to invest in Unit trust investment?

    (PGPI-15) Has government maintained a desirable strategic Foreign relation consistent with its overall interest in a hostile environment for matured and balanced national wellbeing and security e.g. based on people-to-people relation and social and cultural mutual respect.

    (PGPI-16) Has the government succeeded in its economic restructuring to a knowledge-based value-adding technology-driven model?

    (PGPI-17) Has government while seeking to avoid welfarism gone to the other extreme with its tax-and-recover-all-cost-up-to-market-value high-cost policies e.g. Double-charging on lands (paid for by taxes) or triple charges on vehicles (retaining excise duty, after COE and ERP)

    (PGPI-18 ) Has the Meritocratic education policy over-emphasised on the education of elites while neglecting development of broad-based practical education and practical knowledge application and entrepreneurship.

    (PGPI-19) Is the government on the way to realizing its vision of Reaching World Cup soccer league within the 2010 deadline?

    (PGPI-20) Has government succeeded in preserving the values or growth of the citizens' retirements through the CPF scheme?

    (PGPI-21) Has government become obsessed with paying the highest salaries to its own number while neglecting nurturing integrity, honesty, values, humility, accountability?

    (PGPI-22) Is there sufficient accountability and transparency in respect of investments of citizens' monies in overseas investments.

    (PGPI-23) Has the government succeeded in improving the standards of living of its citizens as a result of wage-restructuring for its public sector introduced under the Goh Keng Swee Doctrine?

    (PGPI-24) Has the government taken sufficient steps to nurture the domestic sector of economy while promoting foreign talents and investments.

    Is a system of benchmarking the minister's salaries to the highest paid bosses or CEOs in the private sector based on apple-to-apple comparison?

    (PGPI-25) Has government's entry into business brought about greater economic competitiveness, vibrancy or entrepreneurship to the economy?

    (PGPI-26) Is there any sincerity or progress in building a more cosmopolitan and inclusive society?

    NOTE: On 12.11.2005 Today's Newspaper carried an article on page 6: "New "Satisfaction";; Index for service which states:

    "A BENCHMARK to evaluate service standards in the retail and F&B sectors will be established by the end of next year.

    The Workforce Development Agency says it will call for proposals on the national service indicator by next month..."

    It looks like WDA has still not yet put up a more neutral and objective government performance index despite the past publications of presumed performance indices.
