    Now to the topic. What is the criteria used in the selection of SAF officers?

    OCS (Officer Cadet School) takes in 4 intakes a year. April (Delta, Foxtrot, Sierra and Tango), June (Bravo and Echo), Sept (Alpha, Sierra, Tango) and Dec (Charlie)

    Thus, roughly abt 1000 people make it to OCS a year. How are they selected? BMT rankings. Top 10% of each cohort (no matter which company or platoon, COHORT). The prerequisite is that you must be either a 'A' Level student or a poly student.

    The catch in this is that as the Jan BMT intake is the largest (and has most of the 'A' level pple..), the top 10% has the most numbers, which is why the April OCS intake is the largest at 4 companies, which is a maximum of 144 cadets). Thus if u're in the Jan BMT, u arguably have more chance than a guy from Apr BMT as there will be more numbers and places available in OCS.

    What is u get posted to SISPEC? Well...after BSLC, the top 10% can jump over to OCS too. Sometimes, mono intake pple jump over too. Nothing extraordinary.

    Ok, it's not exactly so clear cut. Delta company in OCS is known as the scholar company because all the potential PSC/SAF/SPF overseas scholars are there. Generally, if yr grades qualify u for the scholarship, u should make it there (assuming u're not really VERY screwed-up). My batch-boy flunked SOC in BMT, still made it there.

    Of course, if u choose to sign on as an officer in BMT, they'll give u a lee-way, top 30%. Still, i know countless friends who got chopped as they failed to make the grade.

    Is the system foolproof? Sad to say, no. While OCC has one of the highest OOC rate in S'pore due to injuries, there are lots of "cannot make it" cases too. Lots of others who should have made OCS but failed to as well. No system is perfect and till now, no one can find a better system.
