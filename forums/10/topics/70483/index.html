    My views?? It's not so much content censorship and regulation that has hindered artistic expressionism but rather, the lack of support and sponsorship.

    In Singapore, the regulations on art performances is limited to the annual submission of scripts to (was it MITA?? or NAC?? can't recall) the authorities for approval before performance and this has been extended to once in 3 years instead of annually in the lates censorship review committee report. The reason for this regulation to be in place is so that we can sift the obscene acts from the artistic ones. Remember the incident where a guy snipped his pubic hair in public and drank urine? If Beatrice Chia's production with the title f*cking & shopping can be put up and even win awards, i don't see a problem in regulations placed on artistic expression.

    The arts arena in Singapore takes a super long time to grow and in some cases, arts organisations sputter out of life and died because of the lack of support from local community and sponsorship. It's a natural bias that exists in Singaporeans that anything foreign is good but local productions are deemed poor. Just look at and compare the ticket sales of productions like Les Miserables with local musicals / plays. While international acts have the standards and garner almost 100% attendance for all their shows, good-quality local productions are considered lucky if they could score a 60% and above attendance on weekend shows.

    Given this kind of unsupportive, Pinkleton-syndrome type of environment, can you blame the arts community for losing enthusiasm and creativity in artistic expression?
