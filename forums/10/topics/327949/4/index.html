    4sg,

    I disagree with your point that overseas Singapore accumulated poor skill sets and as such will be at a disadvantage when they return to Singapore.

    Since Jan this year, the Singapore Goverment has been organising overseas meetings among Singaporeans who have been working overseas in UK and US and the meetings has been taking place in cities like London, New York and cities in Australia.

    I was fortunate enough to be attending one of these meetups and met many Singaporeans in London who are in very well known companies in UK.  After the gathering we were also invited for lunch and I got a chance to network with the overseas Singaporeans based in UK.  I can assure you that if you are in excellent financial companies like Lehman Brothers, Goldman Sachs or Citigroup, you are in a much better position than most Singaporeans working in banks. 

    You have no idea the degree of competition required in order to obtain a job in such an organisation because you have to compete with the top students from Cambridge and Oxford and the talented foreigners from the Ivy League Unis in the US for a job.  When you inside such an organistion, you have to demonstrate assertiveness, courage to speak out, and express yourself like a gentlemen.  This is not something that can be done without hard work and perseverance and especially cannot be learnt and taught in Singapore schools.

    Therefore I personally feel that Singaporeans who are working overseas represent Singapore in their own way.  I must admit that I was initially disappointed with being labelled losers when I first heard about it.  However I am quite sure the truth will prevail eventually, and the fact is that if you do live and work in Singapore, you will have limitations unless you are in a field thoroughly encouraged by the Goverment.
