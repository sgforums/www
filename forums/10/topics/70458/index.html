    It is natural for ppl to keep high hopes after hearing some form of good news from a company possibly bent on hiring you.

    But this is the working world. Companies now have the pick of the crop. If a company informs you that your interview has been successful, it does not = that you're been hired. Keep that in mind. That's the first thing.

    Number 2: Dun stop sending out resumes to other potential job offers. Just because one company calls you up with favourable news, it does not mean you have been hired. Keep sending out those applications. By doing this, you're taking care of yourself and not putting all your eggs in one company. After all, another job offer may come that offers you much more. At this point, you have shaped your position from one of having no choice to one which you can choose.

    Number 3: Sometimes you have to decide whether to take a job or hold out for a better one. For example, u may have sent out 2 applications with a preference for one of them. If the one you prefer has not sent you a response while the other has, you have to decide whether to take it first or reject it and hope the other comes calling. This is a gamble. And to my knowledge, dun gamble too often coz eventually u'll lose.

    I hope my limited experience can assist you in your job search. It's a cruel world out there, no one plays by the rules and verbal agreements mean nothing till it's a signed black and white. Read your contracts carefully and sign the dotted print only after you're scanned between the lines.
