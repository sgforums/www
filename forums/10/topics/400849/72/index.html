    Risks remain for borrowers: MAS

     

    The property cooling measures introduced in Singapore in recent years have curbed the growth in home loans. But some households may remain financially vulnerable, the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) said yesterday.

       Demand for homes from investors and speculators has dropped, going by the proportion of borrowers who take up a second or subsequent housing loan, MAS said in its annual financial stability report.

       But it warned: "More sluggish economic growth, less favourable labour market conditions and less bullish sentiment could lead to a turn in the property cycle, possibly at the same time that interest rates rise.

       "Such a scenario could lead to a concurrent erosion of household net wealth and higher debtservicing burdens. Overleveraged households would be more vulnerable to such shocks."

       It noted that mortgage loans account for about three-quarters of household debt and said close monitoring of Singapore's property market continues to be needed.

       MAS also said that as developed economies recover and withdraw their easy monetary policies; there could be considerable risks for Singapore.

     

    PARTICULAR CONCERN

     

    Of particular concern is the build-up of private and public sector debt in recent years, MAS said.

       The scaling back of stimulus measures by developed economies could lead to debt servicing burdens in Asia rising sharply.

       But MAS noted that corporate and household balance sheets are healthy in aggregate and the banking system has held up well under stress tests.

       Still, it said the banking sector has to guard against a potential rise in bad credit, especially in regional operations.

     

    News, The New Paper, Wednesday, December 4 2013, Pg 4
