    What I am worried about is class injustice and the opression of the masses by the rich.

    If this situation persists, an unhealthy situation will develop.

    Already, Singapore has one of the world's highest Ginni coefficient (the coefficient which measures rich-poor gap)

    This is a sad condition.

    And I do worry about the opression of the masses by the rich, class injustice, and neo-feudalism developing gradually but steadily.

    Home, shelter, is a basic human necessity. It is imperative to protect the fair and just provision of this goods, more so than the urgency to establish anti-monopoly bodies or regulatory bodies to watch certain industries.

    One thing which makes the housing situation unhealthy is that......it seems not insulated from speculation.

    Why is that ?

    Try to track the housing price and compare it to stock price.

    Try to track it and compare it throughout time period.

    They correlate stock market price, and they fluctuate greatly over time. This proves that there is seepage despite the efforts by authorities, from the speculative market.

    There is no way, if price is simply a function of supply and demand, by genuine home buyers and home sellers, that it would fluctuate so much, or correlate with the stock market. It is not a genuine supply and demand by real home buyers and sellers, but from speculators.

    Speculators are capitalist people. They would be landowners, in feudal terms they are the landlords, the aristocrats, the upper-classes.

    While the masses are the peasants, with no property, no ability to own property.

    There is no way the peasants can defeat the landlords, because the landlords keep accumulating and leveraging their capital. They push up and they control the goods, and the masses just have to contend to being at their mercy and have to foverer be landless peasants.

    HDB policies should be reviewed, in fact there should be a massive review on the whole housing situation.
