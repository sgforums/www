    Whether a law is necessary or not depends not just on the act itself but on the particular circumstance in which the act or presumed offence is committed.

    If the act is very serious, it should be punished more severely. Murder is punished with death penalty so is drug trafficking involving certain quantity of heroin.

    If the act is made in highly publcized media like the national newspapers or televisions, I agree that the racists should face severe penalty.

    In the case of private blogs, and internets even if they are accessible to public which are mostly scantily read by not more than 20 per day, are they so serious as presumed by the government.

    What I am pleading is not racism is not serious or should be condone. What I am pleading is for the government before introducing any more draconian controls like ISD-type presumptuous laws, be more objective and consider the rationality and circumstances as well.

    Has the government so far given any facts or figures to show that the particular offending blogs were widely read with high traffic.

    Is it government's duty to consider each case according to merits, circumstances and reality of the situation before jumping to conclusions of any kind like in this particular case.

    There is no need to jump to the conclusion that any racist words uttered are racist and will cause immediate harm or damage. Why leave so much presumptions for the government to use big axe on citizens based on presumptions as in this case. Government should be more objective and not go back to the old days of passing all kinds of laws on presumptions.

    If an old woman let out her flat carelessly to a foreigner without work permit, should she go to jail on presumption that harbouring illegal immigrant is seriouis.

    If one or two high-rise window drop off its hinge because HDB failed to introduce proper workmanship or standards in construction of flats should government be so rash as to conclude that all the flat owners deserve to be punished by heavy fines for not maintaining windowns. This kind of presumptuous laws are being passed by our government making everyone liable for acts they did not commit to be guilty of draconian punishments to suits government convenience.

    Our society is not facing big racism problems because of one or three racists. Our country does not become a poor country because of one family sleeping in HDB car park or void deck as published in the newspaper today.

    There is a need to be objective in taking actions on people. Let the new leaders be different from the old and not make the same mistakes of overreacting to presumed problems and refusing to listen to good views of the people like in the NKF case.     "anon"]robertteh,

    Please stop overgeneralizing the whole issue.

    1. Charges are against racism not against different points of view

    There are many sites on the net against singapore government and its policies, but the government has not banned them, or prosecuted the owners. It's only racist sites that are being brought down. This can not be seen as an all out war against freedom of expression.

    You are free to express your opinions, but that does not mean you can say anything you like. You are still responsible for your comments. Insults, false accusations, etc. will get you in trouble.

    Note that we all have a duty to our fellow beings under the law of torts. People seem to forget this very often. This duty takes precedence over your right to free expression in cases where you cause emotional distress to people through your expressions.

    2. Blogs are not private!!!

    As long as you are open to the 'worldwide web', a blog is not private, and serves as a stage to promote your views to an audience. It becomes a virtual public assembly. Please note that for a real public assembly in Singapore you need to get police permission. Yet to make a virtual public assembly you don't need police permission. So one could argue that Singapore has become more lenient after the advent of the internet. (There were at least a 100 visitors to the some of the racist blogs by very conservative estimates.)
