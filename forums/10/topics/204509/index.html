    In free trade, one has to look at a country's comparative advantage, ie. what one country produces most efficiently, that country shall export that good.

    We can produce T-shirts and compete with China, but we will be on the losing end as our opportunity cost of producing T-shirts is higher. We could have used the available resources to produce goods that we are more efficient at producing, say semi-conductors.

    Ultimately, it would be cheaper to specialise in making semi-conductors and trade for T-shirts, rather than manufacturing both goods ourselves.

    It is true that local businesses that compete with foreign firms in such industries may lose out, as consumers will choose lower-priced goods.
