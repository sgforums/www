Let's throw in some ideas on what we want for Singapore

  • fymk
    19 Oct 04, 17:55
    Let's throw in some ideas instead of singling out people. I just want ideas to improve singapore as it is.

    I will do my bit to suggest :

    Abolish the million dollar or near million dollar pay of ministers and divert those cash into some funds for the underprivileged. The pay is really ridiculous for the size of the country and I don't see them doing much except raise prices. Reduce the number of ministers. Singapore don't need so many .

    Healthcare - concentrate on community support services for the aged in each GRC /SMC - community nurses that travel about checking up on the aged. Instead of crowding them into one huge retirement village. Think of ways to retain nurses as well instead of some cursory recognition thing one week per year or waste money on adverts on how great nursing is - everyone knows it is not that great .

    Schools should impose volunteer services in social studies on students to let them see the other side of a world they are sheltered from, instead of relying on bloody social studies textbooks to tell them to smile and be courteous.

    Abolish streaming from primary school and make them start streaming when the kids are 14 years old into science/arts/commerce/vocational training. Open up opportunities for young students to attach themselves to research in NUS/NTU to bring out new talents or give them a pseudo work attachment in something they are interested in. Talented ones do university subjects when they are around 16years old if they have the apititude (they do it in australia) .

    Sports funding - abolish foreign talent scheme except for bringing foreign coaches in to coach our OWN. Not some china pr to win a gold of the olympics.

    Send something to overseas scholars or even overseas students who give the ministry of foreign affairs notice about their address, to make them aware their country remembers them - a card will do ( Brunei does it for their sponsored students). It goes along way.

    Instead of outsourcing for foreign talents, send our own out for training and an attachment. Exchanges of information is always good. Protect jobs of our own people first .
