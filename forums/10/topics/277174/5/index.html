    The speed camera has caused the smooth traffic flow into a ''slow-stop-and-go'' crawl that produce a backed up chain effect, causing a multiple repeated built-up of time that each car stop and pick-up again, leading to the massive jams caused by the slow down before the speed cameras.

    Expressways are designed to allow a high volume of traffic to move vehicles to their destinations safely at the safest speed reasonable based on the traffic conditions.

    Slow driving speed is not necessarily safe - as frequent bumper-to-bumper collision that lead to pile-up can occur more frequently at crawling speed too.

    The Traffic Police should change their attention on catching speedsters, but to actually manage traffic situations along Expressways.

    Speed limits should be adjusted and road discipline should be intelligently enforced that require slow or slower drivers to observe lane discipline, and for cars 'NOT to hog the lead position beyond 3km' and for such lead cars to stay from the lead position for another 1 km to 3 km - so as to allow other cars from the rear to overtake the lead cars.

    Intelligent traffic and speed management, as well as road or expressways designs - will need to guide the Traffic Planners and Managers in approaching the traffic situation in Singapore.

    This is but only one part in solving the traffic problems, and other essential parts will need to be included to complete the jig-saw solution.
