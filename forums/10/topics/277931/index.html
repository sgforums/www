    Fraser & Neave names ex SingTel CEO chairman

    Reuters - Wednesday, September 5 - Fraser & Neave , a Singapore conglomerate whose interests include beer and property, said on Wednesday that the former chief executive of Singapore Telecommunications would be its next chairman.

    The company said in a statement that Lee Hsien Yang, the younger brother of Singapore Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong, will replace Chairman Michael Fam once Fam has retired on Oct. 15. Lee will be a non-executive director of the firm as of Thursday. Fam stepped down as chief executive of Fraser & Neave in January.

    Lee, a son of the city-state's first Prime Minister Lee Kuan Yew, unexpectedly announced his resignation from SingTel last year and left the job he had held since May 1995 on April 1.

    Last December, Fraser & Neave sold 14.9 percent of the company to state investor Temasek, run by Ho Ching, the prime minister's wife, for S$900 million.

    Shares of Fraser & Neave, which were suspended from trading on Wednesday, rose as much as 1.8 percent on Tuesday after the local Business Times newspaper had cited unnamed sources saying that Lee would join the conglomerate.

    Fraser & Neave and Dutch brewer Heineken own Tiger beer maker Asia Pacific Breweries . The conglomerate recorded S$3.9 billion in sales in the in the nine months to June 30
