    Should MRT and other public essentials be run by the Govt?

    Countries like Taiwan and South Korea have public transportation that are
    govt owned.

    e.g. Taiwan

    They have a good MRT system that is just as good as Singapore, but they do not raise prices regularly like Singapore because its govt owned.



    Taiwan's public Utilities board have not raised electricity rates for 26 years and the cost of eletricity per KWH is half of Singapore's.

    Privatization may not make the operators better as they are usually monopolies and have little incentives to improve.

    In Korea, the transport system is run by the govt. Korea has a more advanced Transport system than Singapore, where the arrival times are downloadable with a PDA or phone, whereas Singapore is only playing catch up with their trial project Iris.

    Being govt owned also means that Koreans enjoy cheaper public transport than Singaporeans despite the fact that Seoul on average has a higher cost of living than Singapore.


    Their MRT fare is integrated with the bus fare system


    For example, it cost only 800 won ($1.30) to for the First 10 km, Regardless of how many times one changes from MRT to bus to another feeder bus
