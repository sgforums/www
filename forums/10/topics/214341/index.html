In press-controlled Singapore, what do newspapers survive?

  • robertteh
    9 Oct 06, 19:38
    In order to survive in such an anti-press controlled society, the Singapore press media has no choice but to walk on tight rope - on the one hand, it needs readership, on the other, it needs to comply with diverse unspelt wishes of its political masters.

    So what must the press media do ?

    It is to be noticed that clever journalists in order to make news interesting and wanted by the people often engage themselves in controversies among the people.

    They would report on domestic people's petty issues like the anti-social behaviours of people, unreasonable attitudes of citizens, long queue for Vietnamese wives, university professor's courtship with a China girls, complaints of citizens against fellow citizens for any wrongful conduct or lack of neighbourliness.

    So I will not be surprised to read a lot of news nowadays in our newspapers about the tiff of our leaders with Mahathir and many inconsequential hot debates on people's having many many different views of problems so as to distract from the main issues.
