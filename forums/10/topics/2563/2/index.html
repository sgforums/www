    I don't think Singapore will be able to afford the UCAV on a large scale basis to have any significant impact in its armed forces. It would more likely use UAVs for recon etc. Also, Singapore does require a large number of UAVs. UAVs are lost or written off VERY easily. Probably 'cos they're harder to control, especially if you lose contact with it, although current SIngapore UAVs are programmed to return on their own if contact is lost, but it is still very easy to lose a UAV. Another reason is that very often, you need continuous info of your enemy/battlefield. To do that, basically you need a large number of UAVs (some sending feedback, some on their way/returning, some on standby and some in maintenance)

    also, I doubt it necessary for a supersonic UAV. A UAV should be stealthy. If the enemy find your UAV, you can say byebye to it already, 'cos most fighters today are supersonic and SAMs are even more so. (unless the enemy has yet to scramble planes and does not have SAMs aimed at you, which is highly unlikely once the UAV is found) Once you're UAV is found in that sector, most likely you cannot put anymore UAVs around there because the enemy will be on their guard there, so the best option is still to be stealthy and not speedy.

    A UAV also beats a satellite in that you need several satellies up just to ensure that you can give 24hr coverage of the enemy(at least 3 sats i think). Otherwise, you'll only have info when the your sat is overhead the enemy. A UAV is there when you need it.
