    Originally posted by wonders?:

    I guess culture to different people means different things..

    Dialects is our culture identity, its part of our culture being chinese singaporean, do we want to be faceless international citizens or uniquely Singaporean?

    Dialect is indeed a cultural identity. However, in my humble opinion, I think it is very important to think carefully whether it is better to craft a wider and more common cultural identity, and let some other cultural identity fade away, or not.

    In my opinion, Mandarin can become people's cultural identity, and mother tongue, and the communication between parents and children and so on within the family.

    I think culture is not static, and we human beings have some destiny or control, and we can shape and mold our future and our culture.

    I think now we are still in generational transition, and it is important to think very carefully, is it better to go all the way to mold a new, common, wider cultural characteristic, or maybe do it half-way, to try forging that, while maintaining the past distinctive characteristics, in other words try putting our foot on both ships.

    In my opinion, it is better to firmly and steadily move in a clear and decisive direction. Do a little sacrifice now, we are the sacrificing generation.

    If you look at the world statistics on languages, actually a lot of languages fade away, become disused, become absorbed, the users use another language, and so on. Actually it is not so special or unique. I think we need not fear a disuse of a certain language, but try to think carefully whether it is better to adopt a certain other language.

    With regards to the language proficiency, actually one of the problems that Singapore has right now, is the low level of both English and Mandarin proficiency. There are many people who speak bad English, and bad Mandarin. This condition should be found a remedy.
