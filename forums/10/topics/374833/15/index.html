    I will attempt to answer your question with some form of justification.

    I am basing my rating of the govt on the oath brought up in our pledge

    Democratic Society (4/10)

    Democracy is still a huge issue with Singapore today. We are still afraid of questioning the Govt face to face, we are short of options in our Govt, we are even afraid to vote for the opposition as some of us are afraid of backlash from the Govt

    but from another point of view, they have started to relax their controls on the internet, etc (which I believe its more a case of knowing they have no choice, rather than they are willing to), therefore instead of 3/10, I am giving the govt 4/10.

    Justice (7/10)

    Justice is quite good. The courts are very impartial when it comes to normal cases. However, for every case against the govt, it's almost always the govt wins. The PAP enjoys suing opposition members, and they almost always enjoy victory.

    Equality (3/10)

    Seeing people like DPM Wong who can still be there when the greatest terrorist of our history escape from prison.

    Seeing people like Ho Ching who can still be there when our funds lost 40% of its value.

    There is no equality between organizations and individuals.

    • Govt does not provide a safety net for white collar workers above $2k monthly pay (we are always on our own), if unfair dismissal, you are on your own
    • Govt does not prevent age discrimination.
    • White collar workers can lose their jobs even if they are performing (read your contract. Organizations can ask a person to leave giving 1 month in lieu of salary, no reasons need to be given). No job security

    Unspoken discrimination preference given to foreign talent vs local talent. Enough said.

    3/10 in my opinion. After all, Equality is an aspiration, NOT an oath, according to MM Lee

    Happiness (5/10)

    Are we happy? I don't know about you. But with escalating costs and suppressed wages, this is 5/10 for me. I am neither happy nor extremely upset. I just merely found a better way of life. Obviously for me, I will always want to score an 8 or 9 out of 10 in all my exams, including the examination of happiness.

    Prosperity (7/10)

    Economic progression is good. Our govt is doing well in surviving the credit crisis, though we were one of the 1st to go into recession.

    Give it due credit for being a great corporation. However, give it due credit for going into recession so quickly as well, plus the loss of value in our funds in GIC and Temasek.

    Progress (5/10)

    Progress is broken into economic progression and social progression for me. Economic progression is good (See prosperity), but social progression is almost ZERO for me. The govt has been focusing too much on economic progress, believing that economic progression will cure all other aspects of issues.

    However, its not the case. Too many people get left behind, life gets tougher in Singapore due to rising costs

    (8 pts out of 10) for economic progression. (2 out of 10 for social progression), average to 5/10

     

    TOTAL: 5.2/10.

    Singapore scores well in the economy and in the courts, but everything else is "JUST PASS", or "Have to do better"

    In Chinese Literature, there are two well-known stories about changing life. The first one is Liao Fan's Four Lessons. And another one is Yu Jing Yi Meets the Kitchen God.
    The main difference in both stories lies in the person they meet in process of changing their life. Mr Liao Fan met a Master Yun-Gu, a great and well-known Zen Master of his times. Master Yun-Gu told Mr Liao Fan that life can be changed even if it's predestined. (In Buddhism, it's our mind, speech and body action that determine our life). Mr Yu Jing met the Kitchen God whom he had worship every day and also to whom he complaint about his misfortune.

    Comparing the both stories, you'll find that both of them are scholars that are well in Chinese literature but can be said as unfortunate people. Anyway, misfortune can be change to fortune. Bad luck can be changed into good luck. Everything is within you. From the two stories, we also learn that "repentance" is the main factor for changing life. Learning without repentance won't give you real benefit.
