    Apr 27, 2007
    AsiaOne

    The Far Eastern Economic Review (Feer) magazine has filed an affidavit seeking permission to engage a Queen's Counsel (QC) to represent it in a defamation suit.

    Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong and Minister Mentor Lee Kuan Yew are suing the magazine's publisher and its editor.

    Mr Peter Low of Peter Low Partnership, lawyer for Feer, filed an affidavit to the High Court this week. Mr Low said this is because it could not get a Senior Counsel (SC) to act on its behalf and pointed to the complex nature of the case.

    Senior Counsel are the local equivalent of the QC, who are top lawyers from Britain. Singapore has 45 SCs.

    The hearing for the application to the High Court for a Queen's Counsel is set for May 10.

    However yesterday, a Senior Counsel Harry Elias of Harry Elias Partnership told The Straits Times that he is Â“ready, willing and ableÂ” to represent Feer, if no SC came forward.

    Â“As SC, it is incumbent on us to say we are ready to help...I would argue the case as I would for any other client, without fear or favourÂ”, said Mr Elias.

    Mr Low, who applied to engage QC Gavin James Millar represent Feer, declined to comment.

    Review Publishing and Feer editor Hugo Restall are being sued by PM Lee and MM Lee for an article which the magazine published last July.

    The article questioned whether Singapore deserved its reputation for having a "squeaky-clean government". PM Lee and MM Lee said the article had set out to disparage them.

    In his application, Mr Low said that QC Millar had successfully argued several high-profile defamtion cases in Britain. He also sought to have Mr Millar to represent Feer in its current bid to throw out the defamation suit.

    Feer, a monthly magazine, has already failed twice to dismiss the case and the hearing for its third and last attempt will be held in July.
