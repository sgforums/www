    Fewer than half the households in Singapore are on broadband, even though the Republic was one of the first countries in the world that was wired up nationwide for high-speed Internet services.

    The figure is lower than the 66 per cent in Hong Kong, where a 10Mbps service costs HK$148 (S$32) a month. A 10Mbps service costs $88 in Singapore. (Straits Times 3 Nov)

    If the government will allow foreign ISPs to come in, will our charges be lowered? Are we practising protectionism to keep our prices inflated?
