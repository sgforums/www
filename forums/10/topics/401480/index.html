    Singaporean charged in court for “threatening” PAP MP Lee Yi Shyan

    TEMASEK REVIEW

    In a sign that the ruling PAP is becoming more and more unpopular with the people, a relief cabby was charged in court today for threatening his Member of Parliament Lee Yi Shyan in March this year.

    52 year old Mr Teo Kian Seng allegedly reminded Lee of Yio Chu Kang MP Seng Han Tong’s torching incident and told him that he hoped “history will not repeat itself” during a meet-the-people session.

    Mr Teo told the media that he had gone to see Lee over a summons his mother had received from the Town Council for causing “obstruction” along the corridor.

    He did not mention the outcome of the meeting or why he made the statement to Lee. It was not revealed who made the police report – Lee or one of his grassroots leaders. 

    If convicted, Mr Teo faces a jail term of up to two years and/or a fine.

    Since the Seng Han Thong incident, PAP MPs have become unusually “sensitive” to verbal “threats” from residents. Seng’s attacker, also a cabby Ong Kah Chua, has been remanded at IMH at the President’s “pleasure” to this very day.

    A garang-guni man was sentenced to six months imprisonment last year for threatening to beat up his MP Denise Phua over the REACH hotline.

    A few weeks later, a 17 year old mentally challenged youth was arrested immediately by the police for slamming a chair against the door during a meet-the-people session with PAP MP Lim Hwee Hua.

    He was probably let off with a warning since he was never charged in court.

    Singaporeans are reminded to be extra gentle, polite and careful with their choice of words when they meet their PAP MPs. Please keep your heads low and speak softly in their presence.

    In other developed countries, MPs are merely servants of the people. It seems to be the other way round in Singapore.
