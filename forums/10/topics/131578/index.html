What's Going On?

  • ShutterBug
    11 May 05, 17:17
    Today, this morning I went to an HDB branch office to renew my monthly parking.

    There I was attended to by this China woman who tried her best to communicate with me in English, but eventually gave up and asked me if I can speak Mandarin with her. I agreed, and concluded the transaction.

    I left the HDB branch office, feeling troubled, asking myself in my head; what is going on? Aren't we multi-lingual? Aren't we largely bi-lingual?

    In fact, yesterday while ordering a drink at a foodcourt, the lady how took my order over the counter asked me what would a like to drink in Mandarin, and then turned to her colleague and spoke in hokkien, and another colleague in English. I replied in English that I would like 3 cold barley drinks.

    What is going on?

    I can understand if I'm in China and people communicate in Mandarin. The other question is; aren't employees in HDB and any other organization supposed to be able to communicate in both English and Mandarin?

    This brings to mind my awareness that there are a great many high school and university grads who can't compose a decent formal letter. Let alone a decent thesis in proper English.
