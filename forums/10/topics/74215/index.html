    I have been away from Singapore for 4 years now. Left to backpack around China and now I am somewhere near the Atlantic - Ireland. I resented SIngapore when I left for not being true to its identity - multi-racial. I felt that some races were given more advantages, and preferances than others. I wanted to know why Chinese New Year songs were played on radio on chinese new year and no Deepavali or Hari Raya songs on the relevant festive days. Why is there a Speak Mandarin campaign and not Malay or Indian or any other languages? Why are chinese and hong kong residents given special rights to enter singapore? No one has yet answered me.

    Travelling around the world has only taught me that people see Singapore as a CHINESE country. I hated having to explain that it is multi-cultural because everyone seems to know LKY is Chinese and somehow they got the idea that Singaporeans speak Chinese and that we are ALL Chinese. I also love my asian identity and again, it seems that chinese is equals to asian. I am still thinking of ways to shake that damning idea that people have.

    What I do now is that I go on to explain to people the racial mix that we have in Singapore. That we mostly speak English on top of our respective mother tongues. That SIngapore was part of Malaysia and traditionally, the Malays were the natives and that our national anthem is in Malay. I feel good saying that, I really do.

    Last year I was back in Singapore for a short break. I was in two taxis where the drivers were Indian. Both voiced their discontent against the government that "some people are more equal that others i.e the chinese community". One had very strong words. Both felt that Singapore felt less like home.

    A close friend of mine was posted to SPH for her school holidays last year by her Poly. She was apparently approached by one lass at work that said, "I'm very happy you are here. You are the first non-chinese we have, you know! Personally, I also don't have any non-chinese friends!"

    Why is this all happening? How can this all be improved? Will it ever improve? What does the government have to say about this?
    What do people have to say about this?

    For me, I'm not going home till Singapore can feel like home. Which saddens me cos there's where my heart is.....

    To answer a question asked, I am half Malay, half white (in no order of preference). I am proud to be Asian, something about being ethnic and cultural that makes me proud.

    Since I've been travelling, I am also a world citizen in a way - I am Buddhist in Tibet, Hindu in Calcutta, Catholic in Ireland and Muslim in Tunisia.

    Anyway, I feel that the whole more equal than others thing has got to be sorted out in Singapore. It cannot be ignored that silently, non-chinese races are feeling resentful. I do not know how long more people can smile and pretend that these issues do not exist because it will bound to come up soon enough. I do not want to think of Singapore as living in multi-racial "tolerant". I want Singapore to be in multi-racial harmony because that is what makes it special. In times of world chaos, I think that it is absolutely wonderful to be able to live with one another co-dependently. Times are changing. Alot more people are inter-marrying now. I think alot of families are made up of people from different cultures. Correct me if I'm wrong but I believe in my grandparents generation, some time during the war, many chinese kids especially girls were given up for adoption to none chinese families. My maternal grandmother was one. And I know another lady in that similar situation. I just feel that there is no trust that exist like that anymore. I believe that alot of those who gave their kids away in that situation gave them to people they trusted which I don't believe in an instant exist anymore between races.

    I do not blame the Chinese race per say on this issue. Yes, I do blame the PAP because who else can I blame? And for that, I don't vote for them. Something more substantial has to be done - more than just talks and seminars that are all show. We cannot afford racial disharmony in Singapore - we are too small a country for that. We need to recognise that firstly, this problem of discontent exist. That there are companies out that that only emplot chinese races. That there are Singaporeans out there that have got absolutely no Malay or Indian or Eurasian friends. That children in schools are not mixing well racially.

    When asked recently about what it means to be Singaporean - I said, "To grow up with a Malay mum, a Caucasian dad and a Chinese grandmother. To go to school and see Mrs. Siva, the Maths teacher wearing her Sari and a pendant with a picture of Sai Baba on it. To go to English class and Miss Suriati is there wearing a hijab waiting for us to get to our seats. To have Mr. Singh allow us play basketball during P.E. To visit Indian friends as a family during Deepavali and Christian friends at Christmas. To give Hari Raya goodies to non-Muslim neighbours on Hari Raya morning. To remember vegetarian and Halal food when inviting friends over. Those are some of the things that make me Singaporean."
