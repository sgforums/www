    A Mercedes-Benz made in Germany, is quite different from the same model being assembled in South Africa, or in Malaysia, or in Vietnam.

    Similarly, a vehicle designed by Mercedes-Benz and built by Ssangyoung in South Korea, may have all the hallmarks of Mercedes-Benz, and even bear the same trade marque; unfortunately the ride is different, and the engine performance is not as smooth as the real thing in the real Mercedes-Benz.

    OEM products are different from House Brands - both products are manufactured according to some production specification.

    How are House Brands able to sell at much lower costs than the better known brands that spend more on marketing ?

    The obvious savings achieved by House Brands product is that it does not need to spend as much on advertising and marketing.

    However, at the end of the day, the House Brands can achieve lower costs either through a bigger volume of guaranteed purchase, or that the lower costs are achieved by tweaking the contents of the products, so as to make the product lower.

    Even business such as NTUC Fairprice - that is supposed to serve Social Objective of ''keeping prices'' stable and costs low - will have to justify their continued existence via the ''Bottom Line''.

    How else does one explain for the bumper cash reserves that allow NTUC to build a spanking new office tower block in the heart of the prime district in downtown Singapore ?

    Even NTUC Fairprice cannot be in the supermarket business for any simple altruistic reason only.
