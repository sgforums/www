    SINGAPORE : The Consumers Association of Singapore (CASE) said it had already raised the issue of StarHub Cable TV's price hike to the Competition Commission, and would leave it to them to investigate the matter.
    CASE President Yeo Guat Kwang was speaking to reporters during the Committee Against GST Profiteering walkabout in Hougang on Sunday.

    He said CASE was not against price increases, but companies must justify such increases.

    Mr Yeo said: "Lately we see organisations like SCV making adjustments not in terms of percentage, but in terms of multiplying factor. This is something we are quite concerned about, because it is important for the service provider to come up with clear explanations.

    "We are not against periodic adjustment per se. As businesses, we know and understand that when costs go up, businesses must make adjustments. The question is transparency, and how positive businesses are actually doing it in terms of giving consumers clear information.

    "If the price adjustment is not in gradual terms or small percentages but in multiplying factor like two or three times, I think businesses should respect the choice of consumers."

    He called for such businesses to give consumers a choice to opt out of contracts even if the businesses have done nothing illegal.

    Mr Yeo said: "Although legally the companies and service providers have the right to do so, I hope they will consider the sentiments of consumers. They should give consumers the right to opt out." - CNA/de
