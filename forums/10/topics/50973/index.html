    Why is this so?

    Because of our past history with Malaysia. During the days when Singapore was merged with Malaysia. Every state has a chief minister (the state governor and now promoted to the position as a Prime Minister) and a ruler, or the Yang di Pertuan Agong (which is merely a symbolic representative of the people of the state).

    Why the need of a symbolic representation is a matter of face, I think. Anyway, this is how our president came about who was then Yusof bin Ishak. But Yusof bin Ishak was always be looked down on for the Dubar or the Conference of Rulers as he was not born of the royal blood and was considered a commoner by Sultans of the Malaysian state.

    The only true friend who really considered Yusof equality of status was our ally's Sultan of Brunei who was not part of Malaysia. Because the Sultan of Brunei thinks he was being looked down on when initially he was joining Malaysia, he did not like his status among the other Sultans.
