    In economics, demand that is more than supply, prices will go up or supply that cannot keep up with demand, prices will go up.

    In Singapore, almost everything is imported. So, if the imported prices increase, the prices in Singapore will go up. The only way to reduce prices in Singapore is to buy from the chepeast source of imports or to buy inferior imports or substitute imports to as to reduce the prices in Singapore.

    Another way, it is to make Singapore dollar stronger so that singapore can still buy the same amount or slightly amount of the higher price imports. Singapore dollars can only become stronger if there are greater demand for Singapore dollars (eg attract more tourists to spend in Singapore or exports more) or less supply of Singapore (eg imports less or fewer Singaporeans go for overseas trips to spend).

    Alternatively, the government can subsidize the imports but this will require a lot of money and it will be difficult for the government to sustain on a long-term basis when the imports prices keep rising.

    Also, the consumers can choose to buy less imports and use more local goods and services but this might not be possible as Singapore imports almost everything and some goods cannot be substituted.

    Theoretically, the above analysis is correct. But, it is incorrect for anyone who does not have the money to keep up with the sharply increase in prices !!!.
