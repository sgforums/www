    On carrots, I think there must be "something to gain or lose" at the constituency level, or else it makes no sense to vote by constituencies. And I do think there is reason to have constituencies... so that MPs can reach out more readily to the people, and be more focused in doing so.

    But should lift upgrading be the carrot? I somehow feel that there are carrots that should be given out impartially (in which case they no longer remain carrots... but that's separate issue), especially those given out by stat boards and government ministries directly, such as lift upgrading.

    Within a constituency, the voted in political party may then compete on fair grounds against other constituencies and political parties... using the resources they can gather (perhaps by adjusting conservancy fees, fund raising activities, etc.) and provide upgrades / improvements to the areas specific to within that constituency (including lift upgrading if they want, but that decision should not be due to "HDB did not approve our application, because we are SDA not PAP", but perhaps because they simply want to do more for the people).
