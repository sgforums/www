    Sylvia Lim is the new NCMP


    The Workers' Party has accepted the offer to take up the Non-Constituency MP (NCMP) seat in the next parliament

    It has named chairman Sylvia Lim as its choice. The 41-year-old lecturer was part of the WP team that contested in Aljunied GRC in this year's General Election.

    The others were Mr Goh Meng Seng, Mr James Gomez, Mr Mohd Rahizan Yaacob and Mr Tan Wui-Hua.

    The team gathered nearly 44 percent of the valid votes, the best performing loser in the election.

    The announcement of the choice for NCMP was made at a news conference after an hour-long meeting at the party headquarters in Syed Alwi Road on Tuesday night.

    The Elections Department confirmed that it had written to the Workers' Party on Sunday asking it to name its NCMP.

    Under the Parliamentary Elections Act, the NCMP offer would be extended to the team which polled the highest percentage of votes among the unsuccessful opposition candidates.

    In this case, the Workers' Party team obtained the highest percentage of nearly 44 percent.

    The law stipulates that the number of NCMP will be three, minus the number of Opposition MPs elected.

    For this general election, since two Opposition MPs have been elected, there will only be one NCMP. - CNA/ir
