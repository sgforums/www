    These people usually dun plan to stay in singapore and so it is hard to justify how much of research effort is really put back into the singapore economy.

    Actually hor, to be objective, the research info and results do stay in Singapore.

    From my 1-year stint in Institute of Micro Electronics (IME), what I observed is that as much time is spent on doing research as on preparing presentations, writing papers. This is to 'store' the information within Singapore, and to apply for patents under Singapore's name. Eg, for my FYP, my main supervisor comes from Taiwan, my other supervisors are from China and India. My main supervisor goes overseas very frequently to give presentations on our project, and the project is currently underway for a patent. But the documentations are still in Singapore; their work is owned by IME.

    That way, even if these foreign researchers leave, and new ones come, they can very easily get into the same/similar project with all these documentations. The patents do stay, the results get shared worldwide.
