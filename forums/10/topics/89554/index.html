    Leverage on our strengths.

    Our strengths are:

    (1) Multi-racial, multi-religious and Multi-cultural country where people of diverse race and cultural background exist in harmony and understand one another.

    (2) Our past economic success - except for the high cost part.

    (3) International standing - tentatively successful except we tend to offend our neighbours with our high-profile pronouncements of all kinds.

    (4) Our military hardware and civil defence software.

    We have tested our neighbours' (Like Mahathir's) attitudes towards us before with our legalistic and high-profile "We are not afraid of you" pronouncements but this did not work. Abdullah the new Malaysian PM has already extended his warm gesture towards us. Let's us build on this window of opportunities and take the initiative to offer certain flexibility in respect of certain issues like water or causeway replacement based on realistic long-term interests of our nation.

    If we are the first one to suggest and could form another follow-up delegation to iron out details, relations with Malaysia should improve. Legal minds may be too constrained/ Negotiators should not be full of legal jargons as our neighbours may not take our approach too well. Keep legal assertions as the final resorts when there is no alternative except to insist on arbitration. They should know our legal rights when we exchange correspondences or drafts. Keep legalities a bit to the background during negotiations.

    As for our relationship with China, they are learning to respect our position. Last time our old administration has struck a certain common understanding with Mao and Deng and they saw our needs and aspirations as a multi-racial country. May be now is the time to build such mutual trust. If we go in and poke finger at their sensitive policies, without in-depth understanding of their major goals and policies, we may end up not the wiser.

    Our strengths is that China may find our people trustworthy to do certain things but to help them build up their infrastructures and commerce. To use our strengths, it is crucial to exercise our foreign policies towards China with quiet low-profile helpfulness and mutual respects.

    Our international relations should be exercised with suppleness and quiet diplomacy with willingness to benefit our neighbours through two-way technical exchange programs. Essentially this calls for a Swiss quiet diplomacy approach. Swiss has not lost out in any international conflicts to date and I don't see we will lose out if we are able to do the same.
