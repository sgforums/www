Singapore slips in broadband stakes

  • oxford mushroom
    9 Jul 05, 05:12
    Straits Times 5 July reported on the failure of Singapore to capitalize on our early adoption of broadband technology. A decade after, less than half of HDB homeowners have taken to broadband. The reason is clear: speeds are low and costs are high, compared to our counterparts in US, Korea and Hong Kong. The blame was placed squarely on Singtel, who has been given such a monopoly that they can dominate the market and stifle competitors. Because Singtel owns the infrastructure, other ISPs have to pay them a huge chunk of every dollar they earn.

    Finally, someone dares to voice the truth....as one forum writer stated, Singaporeans are having a raw deal from broadband.
