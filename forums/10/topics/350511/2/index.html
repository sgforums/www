    Bank Of Italy Denies Saccomani's Remarks On ECB - Source

     

    ROME -(Dow Jones)- The Bank of Italy denied Friday that one of its senior officials had said the European Central Bank is considering buying government bonds.

    Earlier Friday, Bloomberg News reported that Fabrizio Saccomani, director-general of Italy's central bank, had suggested the ECB was "considering several options."

    He made the remarks when asked whether the ECB would follow the Bank of England, which Thursday announced a "quantitative easing" policy including the purchase of GBP75 billion in gilts and other securities.

    No such statement was made, a source at the Bank of Italy said.

    The ECB's statute prohibits the central bank from buying government bonds from member states directly, although purchases on the secondary market are allowed.
