Has he really attracted those with rare passionate convictions advance ideas and entrepreneurship or those with proven practical implementation ability.

    Obviously IQ representative of academic talents alone is not necessarily a quality of good leaders. Without EQ, highly qualified elites would be just plain flowers on the wall for decoration.

    MM Lee should have found out for himself that if he wanted performance and delivery of result he could not rely on academicians alone.

    He should look for the few little Thaksins or Lim Kim Sans who might not be so highly qualified but who are otherwise the rare group with implementation abilities very much harder to find as compared with the educated elites.

    So who are the true talents fit to be our new leaders who could lead a more educated people in today's highly competitive globalized environment?

    Can we attract them with higher salaries bigger bonuses and life-long pensions as MM Lee has assumed in all his policies?

    There is a need to look at other more important factors e.g. environmental factor which may be even more important in attracting talents.

    Why is it that MM Lee was unable to find more men with outstanding implementation skills like Lim Kim San or Dr. Goh Keng Swee in the later years from 1970s onwards.

    Is it because Singapore does not have any more capable people who could measure up to the calibre of men like Lim Kim San or Dr. Goh Keng Swee.

    I would think that not all people will be attracted to million-dollar salaries. Talents do not necessarily draw to monies. It is a two-way process in attracting talents as the talents too will have to decide for themselves whether the prevailing conditions will allow them to contribute their abilities or talents to a job well done.

    Potential candidates do not respond lightly to praises from MM Lee or being spotted or invited for tea. Good men will not necessarily measure their calling in monies.

    There are many other pertinent factors which MM Lee does not bother with for his own limited purpose. Remember Kong Ming (Zhu Ker Liang) had to be persuaded thrice with great show of deference before he finally accepted the offer of post of state counsellor to Liu Biu.

    Real talents also do not work by currying favors of others or superiors. They usually are convicted to their belief or philosophy and will be people who are extremely proactive with independence of thought and actions.

    Given our autocracy and self-centred authoritarian rule where one-man ideas is obeyed without question and the way many good and capable people were sidelined, demoted or forced to premature retirement how could he have attracted the true talents from among the very limited talents we have.

    Therefore under such circumstances MM Lee will have to offer million-dollar salaries presumably as the only pragmatic solutions to attract talents but has he succeeded in this supreme and all-important task critical to our survival as a little red dot.

    Today owing to his own failure in attracting talents what he has attracted might well be the mediocre people who dare not change the autocratic unworkable policies of the past but who would carry out orders or curry favor as the way to gain position of power for their own good.

    To attract really good people to govern the country the authoritarian rule has to change. More ground work will have to be done and not just million dollar salaries before true talents could be attracted to come forward to serve.

    So who at the end must change? If Singapore is to continue to prosper and talents will come forward there has to be total change of the politics and political environment to welcome and encourage team work and appreciation of excellence.

    Today we could see some good men staying low-profile even within the cabinet to avoid getting themselves into trouble.

    I am asking only for change or reconsideration of all the hype about attracting talents with money or elitism. There is a need to let good people be identified of those who are truly passionate and working diligently to serve the people to produce the greatest benefits for the greatest number.

    If MM Lee insisted his talent hunting was still effective and pragmatic and his million-dollar incentivizing of good people to join as ministers as correct let him first prove that his ex-ministers have indeed the ability to lead in private sector after they left service.
