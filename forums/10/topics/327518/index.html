    Academic excellence are evidence of one's mental strength and the perseverance as well as the desire to gain knowledge, to understand how things work and the world around us.

    While knowledge will never cease, one is often given a certificate as a reward for his hard mental work once he shows aptitude for a specialist subject, which will be good enough to secure a job in the economy, based on his specialisation and be paid according to market rates of supply and demand.

    Sporting excellence are evidences of not only one's physical strength, it is also evidence of his mental strength to overcome the limits believed by everyone as facts of human or national limitations.

    While sporting activities are generally believed to make one fit, it carries much more values that are gained from engaging in it, such as sportsmanship behaviour, responsibility and pride in achievement, etc. It is a youthful exhuberance with many life's experience gained and should be celebrated, for we adults were young once too.

    It is not enough that our nation should be satisfied with academic excellence, for alone without sports, the human body will be subject to health and even mental issues later in life.

    Thus the need to give equal balance to social funding and national support into our youth through the elevation of sports to greater glory such as the olympics, where our young may know there are no physical or mental limits with regards to the human body, if we tried hard enough.

    Although some of the winners are imports, there is no need to be ashamed of ourselves, for they are only temporary measures to give hope to our own youthful citizens that they too, being human and equal, can equally excel, if not better than them in time.

    We as a nation aspires towards overall excellence, and sporting glory is one aspiration that will bind us and unite us all as one, sharing with our national sportsmen the agony of defeat, or the truimph of victory, for it was our support, encouragement, and funds that help these winners. ( even if they had lost, for to participate alone is already a win, a glory some nations cannot even achieve to meet the time trials).

    So do stand tall, be proud. We singaporeans, every citizen, had come a long way and have much to smile. Come what may, we use our brains and brawns to survive and never say die.

    Congrats to our Singapore Olympic team and officials that made a difference in the Beijing 2008 Olympics!
