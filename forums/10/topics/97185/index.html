    I would say a person's quality of life is pegged to his attitude towards life itself.

    If he is unhappy all his life, there is no way we can say that this person is enjoying himself.

    A misnomer would be to link the quality of life to the number of material gains a person has managed to achieve. That is the quality of one's material comforts. Whereas these material comforts can improve someone's quality of life, it is not the entire picture unto itself. Certain professionals are successful in their jobs, earn the high pay, yet have abysmal relationships with others; he might be a total jerk on a train by bashing through the crowd without allowing passengers to alight, for instance.

    Improving one's quality of life involves a huge sphere of many factors, of which we are myopic to: we believe cash is the key to one's quality of life. Spiritual, relationships, personal fulfillment (goals) are but some of the other facets of one's life. Neglecting them is akin to leaving a hole within oneself, a deficiency weighs down on our minds.

    There are many ways to measure QoL, depending on each person's value system. Below is a sample of MercerÂ’s study of 39 key quality of life determinants, grouped in the following categories:

    Political and social environment (political stability, crime, law enforcement, etc)
    Economic environment (currency exchange regulations, banking services, etc)
    Socio-cultural environment (censorship, limitations on personal freedom, etc)
    Medical and health considerations (medical supplies and services, infectious diseases, sewage, waste disposal, air pollution, etc)
    Schools and education (standard and availability of schools, etc)
    Public services and transportation (electricity, water, public transport, traffic congestion, etc)
    Recreation (restaurants, theatres, cinemas, sports and leisure, etc)
    Consumer goods (availability of food/daily consumption items, cars, etc)
    Housing (housing, household appliances, furniture, maintenance services, etc)
    Natural environment (climate, record of natural disasters)
