    SINGAPORE : Singapore has been ranked the third most competitive economy in Asia, behind Hong Kong and Taiwan.

    That is according to a survey conducted by the Institute of Policy Studies (IPS) and Nanyang Technological University (NTU).

    And among ASEAN member countries, Singapore topped the list - ahead of Malaysia and Thailand.

    But experts warn that Singapore must keep a close eye on costs and focus on expanding its services sector - or risk falling behind.

    The IPS-NTU competitiveness survey covers four main areas and takes into account 79 sites - the 10-member Association of Southeast Asian Nations, 34 Chinese cities and 35 Indian states.

    While Singapore shone in the key assessment areas of government, institutions, social and economic environment, it lagged as far as business costs were concerned.

    Experts say that Singapore should look to its services sector to derive greater value-added growth as competition from Chinese cities such as Guangdong and Shanghai was intensifying.

    Tan Khee Giap, Associate Professor, NTU, said, "Our potential competitors are Hong Kong, Taiwan, Guangdong, Zhejiang, Jiangsu, Shanghai, (and) Beijing. These are the big cities and provinces in China. But in the ASEAN 10, we lead almost in every category, including government, institutions, economic environment and social environment.

    "But when it comes to business environment, we are equal to Malaysia and the point I want to make is that we cannot cut our wages to match Malaysia. Our competitiveness is not in cutting wages.

    "We can move up the manufacturing ladder, but there is an extent that we can do and in the longer term, I still think services will give Singapore the biggest spin-off where our standard of living can be maintained."

    In the ASEAN category, Malaysia and Thailand came in second and third respectively, but it was Vietnam which surprised, by beating Philippines and Indonesia to the 4th place.

    According to the World Bank, which sponsored part of the survey, the gap was starting to narrow.

    Peter Stephens, Singapore Representative, World Bank, said, "...the role of innovation and creativity and entrepreneurship going forward...these are areas that the government focuses heavily on and that there is a lot of discussion on. And for Singapore to continue to lead the pack and be the successful and prosperous economy that it is, these are areas where there is going to need to be increased attention."

    Among Indian states, Maharashtra was ranked 8th in the government and institutions segment. - CNA/ms

    September 6, 2006

    Taiwan maintained its global ranking and scores on US-based Business Environment Risk Intelligence's (BERI) second Business Environment Risk Report for 2006. In the report, Taiwan received 72 points on the Profit Opportunity Recommendation (POR) score and ranked 6th globally -- identical to its performance on BERI's first report of 2006. Out of the 50 countries surveyed for the test, Taiwan ranked right behind the countries of Switzerland, Singapore, Netherlands, Japan, and Norway.

    In the overall rankings, Taiwan remained at third place among Asian countries, behind Singapore (Global Rank No. 2, score of 78 ) and Japan (Global Rank No. 3, score of 75); Taiwan placed ahead of China (Global Rank No. 17, POR score of 61), Malaysia (Global Rank No. 19, score of 60), South Korea (Global Rank No. 21, score of 59), Thailand, the Philippines, Vietnam, and Indonesia.
