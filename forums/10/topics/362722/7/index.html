    When the term "Foreign Talent" was first created, it was meant to be interpreted as those foreigners that have the "talent" in "high end skills and experience" that are not found amongst Singaporeans.

    Examples of such skills not found amongst Singaporeans then - would include such talents in international finance that require financial traders with the knowledge of trading in special financial instruments, as well as the legal minds with the expertise in crafting of the legal documents that are globally binding - all of which were intended to help achieve the PAP goal for Singapore to be a Financial Hub.

    Under a cloak of official silence, the growth of foreign talent seems to have pervaded and is no longer in the upper rarefied end of the exclusive world of high finance.

    Quietly, a silent revolution seem to have been started by the PAP.

    Silently, an influx of new migrants have been allowed into Singapore to take on jobs that would otherwise have been available to Singaporean school leavers.

    The realisation of this insidious invasion suddenly became obvious to Singaporeans when we begin to notice "foreign talents" coming into direct contact with us - the common folks - with greater frequency.

    This group of "foreign talents" no longer come from Europe or the USA, and are no longer the Caucasian Foreign Talents - with the highly skilled missing talent not found amongst Singaporeans.

    The silent invasion over the last five years have been the result of the easy entry of young citizens from Bangladesh, China, India, Malaysia, Myanmar and even South Korea - all being given PR status to occupy the middle and lower wage positions that surely would have been available to young Singaporeans.

    Some of these young foreigners have been in Singapore on Student Pass - to study in our Polytechnic and University, and upon graduation had applied for PR status to obtain employment.

    Walk into Changi Airport, one will encounter girls from China or Malaysia manning the airport check-in counters, or the service counters at the Airport Food Courts; while girls from Myanmar trained in Singapore Polytechnic or University are employed to supervise the Bangladeshi technicians who are employed to maintain the airport facilities; and with Indian engineers employed in the airport design office to crunch the engineering numbers.

    Walk into any Government Linked Companies, and an army of the same types of "foreign talent" can be found in the various positions classified at "low and middle" wage levels.

    The silent invasion is no longer unnoticeable as the numbers begin to grow, and foreign voices can be heard loudly, working in restaurants and food centers - coming into direct contact with Singaporeans in greater frequency.

    Should Singaporeans be alarmed at this sudden realisation ?

    Does the PAP have a more cynical agenda behind this cleverly disguised policy that was cunningly crafted and explained to Singaporeans at the beginning, and after five years we have now seen the real consequences of the "Foreign Talent" policy ?
