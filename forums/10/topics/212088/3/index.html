    Originally posted by LazerLordz:
    We need to amend the PM term to two terms. Since we've more or less taken the Westminster doctrine and tweaked it to kingdom come, one more amendment shouldn't hurt.
    It is a good proposal to limit the PM to two terms so that there will be no need to keep passing laws legalistically just to hang on to or fighting for power.

    The incumbent should allow people with new ideas and abilities to rise up and review all past and existing policies for at least one term after he has served a maximum of two terms.

    After two terms and lapse of one term to test out new ideas and talents if the ex-PM is still in demand and has proven himself as worthy of continuation, he could then stand again for the PM post. In this way we could avoid autocracy or dictatorship which is too narrow and self-centred for long-term good or stability of any country.

    If PM Lee wants to change, as he has told people to do so, he should work towards decentralising the political power concentrated in a few to a larger group of ministers and professionally objective and responsible dare-to-speak-up back benchers and capable oppositions who can also contribute in polishing up policies and checking on self-centredness practices.

    Good MPs, good ministers and good ex-PM should also be allowed to serve again after a lapse of one term since we are a small country where human resource is limited and should be put to good use.

    If PM Lee really wants people to make demand for change and regards change as good as the old system so far is too narrow and limited in mobilizing people towards mass participation and vibrancy, then he should consider the above-stated suggestion.
