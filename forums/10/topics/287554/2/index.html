    Let me relate a contrasting story today. It might make no sense but here it goes.

    I met my Norwegian contacts for many rounds of beers. They appreciate what we have here even when we have erm "no democracy". Whats happening there is, they have social welfare for all and they have the same population as us. However they have many homeless people despite the welfare. These homeless people choose to lead their lives that way as they would have more money for drugs. In their society, criminal offences like drugs trafficking, murder, rape etc warrants a less penalty like corruption or general corporate crimes. Don't ask me why but thats what i was told. They have different "precedences" there and my contacts wish that there was less "democracy" as in what we have in Singapore. Such thing perpetuate there as their politics is 50-50 and no political parties want to offend if you would like. I was convinced that the ruling party(PAP) is doing a very great job.

    I had to take a cab and had a cabbie who was very much into politics. Somehow i kinda instigated him into talking about politics. I remembered that many years ago, cab rental rates were about $90 per day. Today, i sat in a Skoda and his rental is about $110 per day and diesel rates are of course much more than it was when he first started out. He started to KPKB about the government not caring. However i realised that he is worse off than before. Somehow i felt that competition is bearing fruit as the benificiaries are consumers. However, who gives a damn about the taxi driver ? Every form of price increase is a bad word to consumers. What hit me was that the government managed to make the taxi driver worse off and make the consumer feel worse off. Something is wrong is it not ? I leave you people to decide whats happening today. I'll come back and hope that there is a fruitfull discussion.

    Is everybody worse off ? Is it due to the government ? Will we better off as a "true democracy" ? We need to answer these questions. If we feel that we are getting a rough deal, how do we need to make it better ?
