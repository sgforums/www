    Will the new one be related to the field you studied or totally unrelated or perhaps partially related?
    ______________________________________________________________

    The working world is not just restricted to degrees. What qualifications you need will solely depend on your field. Generally, in Singapore, a degree is what you need. If you intend to work overseas, then the answer is no. Particularly if you are in very technical fields such as information technology or engineering. These fields have exams to take, of which they are not easy to pass. On average, one needs at least 75% to pass them. The more specialized the field is, the higher the expectations.

    As per usual, nothing in this world is ever absolute. What you learn today, may change tomorrow. Pluto has long been regarded as a planet (granted, it's been disputed, but most textbooks put it as a planet, we shall take it as it is), and now Pluto is no longer a planet.

    Since nothing is ever absolute, then it creates problems. I studied this today, I have to study something else tomorrow. But the basic principles still remains. Take for example genetics. No matter how much it changes, no matter what discoveries we make, the four bases will still be ATCG (adenine, thymine, cytosine and guanine). ATCG forms the basis for genes.

    The only way is to learn. Not learn to cope, but to relearn. As long as the basics doesn't change (not that they are absolute), the advanced topics will be built on top of these basics. How things shouldn't be important. More importantly, how you want to deal with it.

    No point having the government to lay out the ground rules and forcing you, neither does changing the entire education system helps. Learning should come from motivation, not cold hard rules. Our government has implemented 6 years of compulsory education to improve literacy of the population, but that doesn't mean that all of them will bother to learn. Rules will be rules, whatever that goes will depend on the person himself/herself.
