    Short answer? Yes.

    Lacking natural resources, all the country has to offer is the services of its people. If the cost of living continues to rise (pricing Singaporean labour out of a competitive world market), and if Singapore does not keep up with world standards, a collapse is entirely possible.

    Is it likely? I hope not. For the first 20 years of Singapore's independence, the government did a brilliant job because it understood Singapore's position as a newly-industrialised country that could produce quality goods at a competitive price, especially with a population that was willing to shut up and do what it was told. Over the next 20 years, they lost the edge because they failed to recognise the need for the country to evolve in line with the economy. Now we've got panicked efforts to make Singaporeans more creative and enterprising, but at the same time, the advantage given to GLCs make the playing field far less than even. Right now it's up to the government to quit yanking on the leash, and instead to let Singaporeans go out and do their thing in a less strictly-regulated environment.
