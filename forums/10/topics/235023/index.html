    Thailand has introduced legislation for compulsory licensing of a number of drugs, bypasing the patent rights of the manufacturer. Whilst this benefits the patients, it also means that pharmaceutical companies would not invest in the country. Such a policy runs contrary to making the biomedical industry a pillar of Singapore's economy and will not work here.

    Drugs are protected by patents for as long as 20 years...there are strict rules about parallel imports. If you are certain that a drug is genuine and is available in a foreign country, there is nothing to stop you from buying it there. If you end up with adverse effects from poor quality drugs or contamination, of course you may not be able to seek redress but that's your choice.

    You can purchase for your own needs overseas. But if you try to buy in bulk and import it to Singapore, the pharmaceutical companies have the right to sue you. In addition, you cannot sell the drug to the public without a license and if anyone suffers harm as a result, you are liable, just like the Slim10 case.
