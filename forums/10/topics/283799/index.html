    It's not a new concept. In Sociology, I believe this line of thought is known as the Conflictarian Perspective (Correct me if I'm wrong). Where because of the socio-economic situation of your parents, you would find it hard to rise beyond that distinct social class. In Singapore, they'll say that with meritocracy, we have a chance to elevate our social status, based upon the 'merits' we contribute to society, irregardless of our standing social-economic condition. In other words, there's a presence of Social Mobility, upon which many welfarist based states are criticized for sorely lacking off.
