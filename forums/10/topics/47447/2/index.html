    In the education system? Where do we start? For starters

    - Reduce the emphasis on using exams to assess students. Exams are an anachronistic and unrealistic way to assess a student's aptitude, and while I do not advocate scrapping them totally, I believe its high time we increased the importance of in-semester work, assignments and projects to assess a student's performance rather that having a high-pressure 2-3 hour exam assess the student's progress over 6 months
    - Accept the fact that every student is an individual and not to be molded into a mindless production line robot. Conformity is good, but the level expected of Singapore's students breeds mindlessness, like that we've seen in a lot of students.

    The through train system proposed by the Education Ministry, looks like a step in the right direction at first glance, but look deeper into it. Its obvious that while the best students will go through what will obviously help in their development, the others will continue to be molded into the the production line robots the Singapore school system seems so good at producing. Call me cynical, but this plan can be said as producing advanced controllers for production line robots, instead of the old system of using a few high-QC certified production line robots to controlling the others.

    While I accept that not all schools or students can accept the through-train system, the MoE has, by using the through-train plan, proclaimed it has reformed the system. But it has in fact, glossed over and covered up some serious flaws (as well as its gearing for economic situations that are no longer present) in the education system that have become obvious as the global economy gone south in the past few years..

    Instead of making the system less elitist, we've gone the exact opposite way.

    The problem we face is not only confined to Singapore. The issues exist for every country in the world

    a) What is our role?
    b) Is our role/economic model sustainable in the future?
    c) Can our resources/people rise up to support the role/model.

    Our role in the global economy was fully defined in the 60s-90s. However, we are now facing a fact that the role is now undermined by globalization, that being a middleman will not last in the future. Hence we have to change the role.

    Then, we have to see if our present capabilities can support that. Sadly, we can't even do that (being a middleman) with our current population (which is our only resource) so defineing a new role will be exceedingly difficult.

    Philippines and Malaysia has a huge advantage .. it can copy us as it has a 2 to 4 year lag to our development. It can wait for us to iron out the bugs and then steamroll us by being bigger much faster and cheaper than what it took us to do.

    a) Education
    b) Transhipment
    c) Services
    d) IT
    e) Tourism
    f) Call Centre
    g) Hospitality
    h) Health
    i) Finance

    Our government has stuck in the mentality of of doing what is popular and profitable currently and hope no one else can do it. The recent tourism and biotech campaigns show this. But it won't be long before our lack of resources in those areas will cause the sector (and the economy) once again to fail.

    Let me give you a real example. I spent like 3 weeks in the USA. I invited many of my colleagues to visit Asia for their holidays. Indeed, there is a SIA promotion for a very good fare with accomodation (6 nights) to come to Singapore. The problem is .. what the heck can some tourist do in Singapore for 7 days and 6 nights?

    So we will end up benefitting the Malaysians, Thais and the Indonesians. So our wonderful promotion will not be able to kick start our tourism industry. So most of the money used for incentives will be lucky if we break even.
