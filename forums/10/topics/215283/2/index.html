    INDONESIA is unlikely to ratify an Asean anti-haze agreement any time soon even though President Susilo Bambang Yudhoyono has said that it would.
    Environment ministers from neighbouring countries, who met in Pekanbaru about a week ago to discuss the problem, had requested that Jakarta ratify the treaty.

    But it now appears that the treaty will remain stalled in Parliament here.

    In fact, a member of the parliamentary commission for the environment said MPs had actually decided to postpone the process before Dr Yudhoyono called Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong on Oct 12 to assure him that action would be taken.

    'The pledge had caught us by surprise,' said Mr Alvin Lie, a member of the commission which is leading the debate on the ratification.

    He told The Sunday Times yesterday: 'All the factions in the commission, including those from the President's own Democrat Party, had agreed earlier that there should be certain steps taken by the government before the ratification process would resume.'

    Chief among these would be a firm commitment by the Yudhoyono administration to act on other cross-border environmental issues involving Indonesia's Asean neighbours.

    These included Malaysia's alleged role as the biggest buyer of illegally-logged timber from Indonesia and illegal fishing in Indonesian waters by Thai fishermen.

    In an article in the Koran Tempo daily, Mr Lie had also tied the ratification to a non-environmental issue: ongoing talks between Singapore and Indonesia over an extradition treaty.

    Mr Lie has spoken about the need for a 'balance of benefits'. The decision to postpone the ratification was based on the commission's view that Indonesia already has several existing laws that can deal with the haze problem, he said.

    'Hence, we do not need to be afraid of pressures from other countries,' said Mr Lie....

    (Straits Times, 22 Oct)
