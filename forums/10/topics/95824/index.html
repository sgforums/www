    As a country with very diverse culture, religion and races, it is necessary for us to setup an Equal Opportunities Commission to ensure that citizens are not discriminated by any inappropriate means, be it race, langugae, religion, gender, political affiliations, nationality etc etc, especially in the job advertisements, employment practices...etc.

    The Commission will be tasked with the power to investigate and censure employers that discriminate their employees or jobseekers by any of these means. The Commission will be tasked with the power to carry out social justice by bringing errant employers or companies to justice if by means of thorough investigations, found them to be guilty of discriminatory practices.

    Goh Meng Seng

    This concept is not an "original" idea from me. It is practiced here in Hong Kong. The head of the panel is a retired judge.

    There is a committee or panel to deal with cases of such. It is precisely due to the sensitivity of discrimination practices that we need to set up such a commission. In Singapore's context, the panel will naturally consist of minority representatives and Chinese. Preferrably, these people should have some form of law training. There will be a group of civil servants who will assist in investigations and data collection.

    As I have said, if the company really deal solely with China, then it is a legitimate reason as language ability will become a necessary skill. However, if any Malay or Indian could speak very Fluent Mandarin, I don't see why he or she should be deprived of the job opportunity!

    But believe me, there are employers who are just using language skill on a discrimination basis. Thus, it is up to the commission to investigate and decide on "grey areas" like this! That's precisely why such commission is necessary.
