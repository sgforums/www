    Originally posted by teekh:
    dalai lama also got eat meat one FYI...
    If you read the book by the Dalai Lama "Freedom in Exile", the choice of diet was briefly mentioned. If I remember correctly, in Tibetan Buddhism, meat is not strictly forbidden simply for practical reasons, because it is difficult to grow sufficient fruits, vegetables and grains in the high altitude and dry terrain (they were living among the Himalayan mtn range). Hence, the people have to be practical about it, and the monks are allowed to eat some meat, so that they would be able to get enough nutrients. In the book, he also related about how he wanted to go totally vegetarian, which he did for a few months, but then his health suffered much. His followers could not bear to see him in poor health, so he was kind of forced to include some meat in his diet.

    As to those 'monks' who flaunt their expensive watches and eat at expensive restaurants, I'm not sure if they are Buddhist monks at all.
