    Dear Uncle,

    First of all, in life, sometime you have to think in miscule, otherwise you find it difficult to understand certain problem, why think big when you are not big. I see many Uncles talk big, but earn little ya. So try to think small first before you embark on big things if you can afford to do so.

    I am still young, but for those uncles or aunties who had lives here for decades, ask yourself, is there a time when cost of living goes down??? no right? it is alway rising, not only in Singapore, but all over the world, it just that we are bit faster because of our ever fast changing economy and adaptation. The govt takes cost of living as a cane to it any laymen on the street, if cost of living remain the same or lowered, peoples tends to laze off, become complacent and have no morale to work hard or improve themselves, just like some 3rd world countries, the cost of living do increase, but not much, therefore people there are not so hardworking and do not have the sense of improvement so as to earn higher paid. Many Singaporeans who travelled use to nag and complain about such countries poor and slow services. But that is the way of their life, no morale, no kick, no cane to beat them on.

    Any layman on the street, will knows that a plate of chicken rice in singapore cost average $3 now, if tomollow, the price goes up to $4, what happens??? that layman will sense that cost of living is up, he would grumble and curse alots, but life still goes on.

    On the hindsight, this layman knows that he needs to get higher paid to off set  inflation, and to do that, he needs to be better educated and trained with better skills to offer himself to the market at a higher paid, he also needs to save and be wary of his finanical situation, in another words, he will move on and improve himself to work harder, smarter and more skillful in order to get a better job with higher paid, better position, higher paid increment, higher promotion etc etc just to match it. It is a continous improvement progression, and that is what the govt wants, by increasing the cost in secondary products (with the primary being essential goods which most are capable of fulfilling), people will be motivated to move toward achieving those goods. And that, on the whole, move the whole country economy, skills, education, improvement and productivity to the next level of standard of living.

    So, to put it simply, by using the cost of living as a cane, people will move, and the carrot will be improvement and better living conditions.
