    Tuesday February 21, 2006, 5:26 pm

    CANBERRA (AFP) - Australia has decided to shut Singapore Airlines out of the lucrative trans-Pacific routes to the US while the cap on foreign ownership of national airline Qantas will remain at 49 percent, Transport Minister Warren Truss says.

    Truss said that Australia viewed an open skies policy as a national goal and was willing to negotiate access to the Pacific route "but only when it is in the national interest.

    "In terms of the Singapore Airlines' request for access (to Australia-US routes), the government has decided not to grant them access at the present time," he said.

    Qantas, which earns as much as 20 percent of its profits from its Sydney-Los Angeles services, had argued that it would be disadvantaged if state-backed airlines such as Singapore Airlines were allowed to fly the route.

    In announcing the completion of a lengthy government review of aviation policy, Truss said that it found that policy settings which have been in place since 1999 were appropriate.

    He said although Qantas had lobbied hard for the government to lift restrictions on government ownership, this would not be granted.

    "The government has decided not to amend the Qantas Sale Act in that regard," Truss said.
