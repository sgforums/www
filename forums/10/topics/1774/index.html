    Ducati1996: I do agree that the US is a busybody at times, but I think your analysis of US involvement in Vietnam was flawed. I think had the US not got involved, the fall of Vietnam, and all of SE Asia to communism would be very real. It is now agreed for a fact that the National Liberation Front(more pupularly known as the Viet Cong) of Vietnam was just short of initiating Phase 3 of a "peoples revolution"(ie. the start of subversion in urban centres after completing Phase 2 - comunization in the rural areas) by the time the US sent troops to South Vietnam in 1965. It would then be a matter of time before Cambodia and Laos fell, and thereafter Thailand Malaysia and Singapore, which already had active communist movements. Of course, Indonesia under Sukarno was already overtly communist. The Domino Theory may be easy to be scoffed by the Left in the West, but in 1965 in SE Asia, it was very real. US involvement might not have stopped the communists in IndoChina, but it was vital to stopping communism in SE Asia because

    1) It slowed the revolution in IndoChina by 10 years, and significantly weakened Vietnam's(and to a certain extent: PRC and USSR's) ability to sponsor similar movements throughout SEA, by forcing them to focus efforts in Vietnam in the case of the latter 2 countries.
    2) It actively awoke the US to the threat of communism in SE Asia, and committed the US to fighting it in countries like Malaysia and Thailand
    3) It made regional governments wake up to the seriousness of the threat

    Their tactics, policies may have not been exactly totally successful or morally aboveboard, but the underlying intentions, were no doubt good.

    I do agree however that the US does piss me off with their "holier than thou" attitude, and pokes their noses where it don't belong.

    As to China today, I feel that letting Taiwan go would be in the best interests of both countries, but I just feel that after 50+ years of claiming Taiwan is a part of China and accepting the status quo, both sides(minus the Taiwanese independence advocates) would be unable to accept a climbdown without seeing it as a defeat. Which brings me to one more question: What gives Taiwan the right to claim to be the legit China(incl. the mainland) when it feels like it, and declare independence when it feels like it?

    China is here to stay, politically, economically and militarily. We'll have to live with it and work with it. There are some who can't accept it, in the West and in Asia. But they'll wake up to that eventually.

    Consider the track record of the communists in Vietnam: Even as the Vietnam war was was rumbling, they were running communist movements in Laos and Cambodia by sponsoring the Pathet Lao and Khmer Rouge respectively. They succeeded in Laos in 1970 and Cambodia in 1975, and when they found the Khmer Rouge government they installed was not to their liking, they simply invaded Cambodia in 1979 and installed a government more malleable to their wishes. All this by a nation depleted by 40+ years of fighting the Japanese, French and Americans.

    Now consider the scenario if the US had not gotten itself involved. Vietnam becomes communist in 1965-66. Cambodia and Laos follow in the next 2 years or so with the new(undepleted) Vietnamese regimes help. With the US washing its hands off fighting communism in SE Asia, Sukarnos opponents will not get the support they need to overthrow him, Thailands and Malaysia's(including Singapore's) communists get the support from the USSR, China and Vietnam(remember both the Soviets and Chinese supported Vietnams communists DESPITE the Moscow-Beijing rift). At this moment, Thailands and Malaysias governments are still weak politically economically and militarily and the communists actually had support within the local populace then. How long would a concerted communist subversion effort taken to overthrow these 2 new democracies?

    With US involvement in SE Asia, by the time the Vietnamese had invaded Cambodia, the economies of SE Asia was on a more even keel, governments stronger politically and militarily, ASEAN was more established than before to form a united front against the communists in IndoChina to prevent the Vietnemese and their proxies from running the show completely in Cambodia, which would have IMO for certain allowed them to turn attention to the rest of SE Asia

    The above scenario are all just ifs and buts, and might not happen even without US involvement. But I don't fancy the chances of it not happening.
