    Does controlled press means better faster decisions or prolonging bad decisions?

    The government often argues that a controlled press allows the government to execute good decisions quickly without the need to build a consensus. However, the sword cut both ways. It also means a bad decision can be covered up, critics can silenced and a distorted coverage of the policy can be propagated just to minimize embarrassment. Without a press to check the government, bad policies can be continued to make the bad look good

    28 Nov 05, 15:21
    The assumption that we should trust the government to make a better decision is based on 2 grounds.

    1. The government is privileged to see the larger picture and acts as an arbiter of different self-interest groups
    2. Resources are available to tap various sources of research and make a good decision.

    The government sometimes fails because

    1. Specifics goals, benchmark of performance are set for civil servants, They sometime relentlessly pursue these goals ignoring how their decisions may affect the public in an adverse way.

    2. Policies need feedback - the ground man always experience the problems of the implemented policy first. Humans are immensely complex and what really appears to work on paper fails in practice.

    I donÂ’t advocate the kind of crazy paparazzi press in Hong Kong, but a more liberal press lubricates social change. There should be space for public criticism of government policies to rise or we risk being stagnant and oblivious to the rapid social changes brought about by internet and globalization.
