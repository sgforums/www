    PAP can't be autocratic on any issue - it has to sit for 'exams'

    Sun, Aug 26, 2007- AsiaOne

    Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong said the People's Action Party (PAP) cannot afford to be autocratic on anything.

    He said this in response to a comment raised at the Young NTUC dialogue in Ang Mo Kio yesterday.This is because the party has to sit for "examinations", or elections, every five years, he said.

    The PAP needs to win the support of the electorate and get a consensus on issues. But while the majority's agreement is required on major issues, such as the integrated resorts, it cannot be done for everything, The Straits Times reported.

    PM Lee quoted the example of taxes and said this issue cannot go through the same process. The Government has to decide and make an announcement in the Budget.

    [color=brown]But on smaller issues, Mr Lee agreed that the Government should not step in too quickly and allow the people to discuss and come to their own conclusions.[/color]
