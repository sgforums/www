    "As you can no doubt imagine, we often say in despair, 'What's the point of war ? Why, oh, why can't people live together peacefully ? Why all these destruction ?'

    "The question is understandable, but up to now no one has come up with a satisfactory answer. Why is England manufacturing bigger and better airplanes and bombs and at the same time churning out new houses for reconstruction? Why are millions spent on war each day, while not a penny is available for medical science, artists or the poor? Why do people have to starve when mountains of food are rotting away in other parts of the world ? Oh, why are people so crazy ?

    "I don't believe the war is simply the work of politicians and capitalists. Oh no, the common man is every bit as guilty; otherwise, people and nations would have rebelled long ago! There's a destructive urge in people, the urge to rage, murder and kill. And until all of humanity, without exception, undergoes a metamorphosis, wars will continue to be waged, and everything that has been carefuly built up, cultivated and grown will be cut down and destroyed, only to start all over again!"
