    Fruit platters go, but street lights will stay
    By M. Nirmala

    CUT waste? Cut out the fruits then.

    One government department accepted this idea from a civil servant who felt that serving fruit at its meetings was a waste of money.

    The savings: $132 a year.

    The Public Service Division said it used to serve fruit during monthly meetings for directors of personnel as the sessions last about 2 1/2 hours. But now it will just serve drinks.

    The suggestion was among 1,200 received by the Cut Waste Panel set up three months ago to look into suggestions from the public to trim fat in the public sector.

    Yesterday, it released details of the ideas accepted and rejected and those already being carried out by government agencies.

    One writer thought it unnecessary for one government department to take out newspaper advertisements to congratulate another.

    The writer targeted the Singapore Tourism Board, which said it would stop the practice unless there are 'exceptional grounds'.

    Another writer suggested turning some unused motorcycle lots in Telok Blangah into car park lots instead, and that will be done too.

    But a power-saving idea had to be rejected.

    A writer felt street lighting was a waste and recommended halving the power bill by switching off every other lamp on all roads.

    Today's cars come with good headlights so that should do, the writer felt.

    Not so, said the Land Transport Authority.

    Alternate lighting would create an imbalance to uniform illumination on the roads - something called the 'zebra effect', it said.

    'The contrast between the bright and dark areas will cause tiredness to motorists. This is a safety risk,' it said.

    So street lights will stay, but the LTA will look at using lower-wattage and energy-efficient lamps.
