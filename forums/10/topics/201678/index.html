    SINGAPORE (Dow Jones)--A research collaboration between Johns Hopkins University, and Singapore's Agency for Science, Technology and Research or A*Star is likely to end soon, with the joint facility at Singapore's biomedical center, Biopolis, to close in a year, the Straits Times reported quoting researchers and officials.

    The newspaper said four local students who were awarded doctorate scholarships to study at the university in Baltimore were also told last month the research division won't fund their studies.

    An A*Star spokesman told the newspaper the decision was taken by the leadership of the U.S. university and the state funded agency to replace the current "operating model of collaboration" with a "new model of partnership" that is still being developed

    However John Hopkins University had a different view.

    A spokesman for the university said it had done its part to recruit faculty and graduate students under its agreement with A*Star. The university maintains that its Singapore partner has not kept up its end of the deal in meeting its "financial and educational obligations".

    "Although Johns Hopkins University has attempted to be as collegial as is possible during this very difficult phase, the displacement of outstanding junior faculty recruited from throughout the world, and the intense disruption of graduate student education, cannot be underestimated or dismissed," the spokesman told the newspaper.

    Johns Hopkins came to Singapore in 1998 to carry out research, education and patient care activities. It also set up a medical center here, designated as Johns Hopkins Singapore's clinical arm for patient care and clinical research, which is still expanding, the newspaper said.

    Singapore is trying to attract investment in the biomedical sector in an effort to diversify its manufacturing sector from a bigger reliance on electronics.
