    How are judges appointed? In the United States, confirmation of the President's nominees to the federal judiciary by the United States Senate is required. In many of the states, judges must be directly elected or at least periodically be subjected to elections where there is no opponents. But if voters express their disapproval, the judge cannot continue in office.
    The judges for the High Court and Court of Appeal in New Zealand are appointed by the Governor-General on the nomination of the Attorney-General. The custom is that the Attorney-General mentions these appointments after he or she has determined them at Cabinet. The Attorney-General usually consults with the President of the law society, the Chief Justice, the President of the Court of Appeal and the Solicitor-General.
    Should there be a formal screening mechanism when appointing Chief Justices' in Singapore? Should the public have a say in their retention? What qualities do you look for in a perspective judge? Would it make any difference if he or she had been convicted of an offence such as a driving offence? These issues go to the heart of the integrity of the justice system and the test of public confidence in the administration of justice.

    Ten years later, in 2004, it was announced that a completely new Monorail System is to be introduced, and will be connecting Sentosa with the main island of Singapore.

    It seems that the earlier decision of those keen-eyed Directors, appointed to the Board of Sentosa Development Corporation, is now vindicated.
