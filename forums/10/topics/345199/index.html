    What is so bad about Singapore being an exporter of high tech products? Look around, there are so many things which are basically rooted in 1950s era technology. The opportunities for real returns from investments in applied research are very real.

    All wealth is derived from production. The state of the American economy proves that a service dominated economy cannot support long term growth. The idea that wealth can be magically created by offering financial services not rooted in production has been shown to be a Ponzy scheme.

    Japan, Israel, China, they're all spending on applied research because that is the precursor to new industrial activity. Even though we have been wasting a disproportionate amount of money on basic research, it is still not too late to redirect our resources to more productive channels.

    Most importantly, we need a paradigm shift, we need to do away with the old rentier mindset, we need to do away with the elitism and we need to fund promising innovations and see it through to manufacturing and export. Arms, power plants, solar panels, water recycling systems, foldable mini-scooters, no taboos, everything is fair game. Look at how Russia banked heavily on its arms industries to pick itself up after the breakup of the USSR. Everything needs to be directed at building our own industries, the time has come. If we accept that wealth must come from production, what can we else can produce? This is what a knowledge based economy is all about. It seems somehow the effort to build up KBE capacity got sidetracked by the easy money but now that that's all over we should redouble our efforts to build up KBE capabilities.
