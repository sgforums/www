    Someone in another site has asked the question why so few of our elites are speaking up for the people in our society.

    Apart from ex-civil servant Ngiam Tong Dow, ex-speaker Tan Soo Khoon, ex-Income-CEO Tan Kin Lian who else? Not many.

    An average joe sorry to say has been so brainwashed consciously or sub-consciously for many years from teens to adulthood by government-controlled media indoctrination that they can no longer tell the difference between right and wrong, justice and evil, oppression and victimisation etc etc. Just because of constant headlines screaming the defamation suit and all the propaganda about telling of lies against people like CSJ etc many have been made to believe the untruth.

    Many elites too many not realize the transformation of grey matter. Fear creeps gradually and irretrievably into their whole being but this will not be admitted as abnormal or cowardice.

    Let us call a spade a spade for a start. I feel there is an certain illness afflicting the whole society and the solution is to tell the truth as we see them even the truth about ourselves even when we may be hurt by such truth. We need to find an antidote to rid or the climate of fear and begin to act normal as healthy citizenry and not shy away from speaking up and telling the truths. Why merely pretend to forget or ignore the wrong doings and exist without morality or sense of right and wrong just to go through the merest and dry biological existence.

    It is time to ponder over such basic issues and start asking ourselves why is it so difficult to speak our mind about right or wrong?

    For a start let us see what is so difficult to call a spade a spade like what Ngiam has done. Why be so afraid to tell the truth or express our feeling about certain policies which may be wrongful or working against the common interest of the people.

    To me I feel it is important we as people must know how to speak up our mind honestly without fear or favor of course without defaming any one's character to the best of our ability.

    We simply have to pull ourselves together and sacrifice a bit to commit ourselves to at least play a small part. Tell the truth and speak up without fear or favor or defaming anyone and uphold the first principle of morality as citizen.
    If the more educated cannot speak for us too then what is going to happen to the whole society? Surely by saying nothing things are not going to improve or get better such as more accountability and openness in the governing of the country. The elites may well enjoy a better material life and get ahead in his own career etc but is he finally at peace with himself and society if society comes across many crisis like NKF or Selamat again and again.

    Ngiam Tong Dow, Tan Kin Lian, Tan Soo Koon etc belong to the rare breed but they were tolerated to serve a certain purpose - to showcase certain degree of tolerance which is merely for show or hypocrisy. I wonder if Ngiam realizes he is merely serving a hidden agenda which he himself may or may not even realise.

    To me, this is quite a phenomenon in our little red dot. We have many unsavory culture. Yellow culture, is sexiness. Pink culture may be worshipping of foreigner. Blue culture we have them too. Now we may have a new culture - "kou cher dou shen" or "just manage to survive without principle".

    The more common idiom may be "do not trouble trouble unless trouble troubles you".

    Someone I know of was noted for being acquiescent too not in the habit of speaking his mind regarding wrongful act of government people are talking about like ministerial salary issue or rising costs of living.

    However one day his property was frozen by urban redevelopment conservation scheme and he became frantic and seeking help from every one and became more sober.

    So the moral of the story - most people who are compliant of government wrong doings or oppressive action do not act until he himself becomes a victim. Beware of such individuals and get them to understand and realize their own citizens' duties and judgmental flaws.

    Collectively when every one we know begins to exercise his one ounce of righteousness and uprightness about right and wrong and will speak up on the truth like Ngiam is trying his very best to do we all the ordinary citizens will be a force to reckon with much more powerful than a strong opposition party.

    If every one we know has the sense of right and wrong we will as a people at least in expressing displeasure of wrong doings will be powerful to check on wrong doing as has happened and any hidden agendas and hypocrisy.

    The States Times then will have no choice but to follow the morality or culture of such a upright people.

    When the time comes we will then find pleasure as active citizens and the role we could play without expecting any rewards for ourselves.

    For now strong civic consciousness is rare. When we are only slowly getting a bit of community in this forum and it is not too bad for a start. But we need more of such spirit and continuing maturity and growth as a culture if we want a better government and the citizens to be counted.

    The first generation that was involved in politics had a shared vision in battling the Colonial Government with their haughty racist attitude in governing Asians.

    It was surprising that during the 1950s, the personalities that formed the first cabinet was a disparate lot - coming from different backgrounds and all not born in Singapore, as some were from the Malayan States, others from India and Indonesia.

    All were vocal and spirited in their cause, and none less than LKY - and all were tolerated by the British Colonial Government during those days, and more so than an independent Singapore towards her own Citizens.

    It was reported that in the first LKY Cabinet, he had formidable personalities that were all able to debate and argue policy matters with him, and consensus came about by such means of brain-storming and verbal jousting of ideas. With so many strong charactered persons in a Team, someone or everyone has to give way to reach consensus or the government will be paralysed.

    Those who were reputed to give LKY a run for his money as PM - were Dr Toh Chin Chye as DPM, Dr Goh Keng Swee, Mr S Rajaratnam, Mr E Barker, Mr Jek Yoon Tong, Mr Ong Pang Boon, and Mr Yong Nyuk Lin.

    Dr Toh Chin Chye, who was the DPM in LKY's first cabinet, and was also the Chairman of the PAP - after being sidelined to become a very vociferous backbencher - had said the following to the Youth of Singapore after he retired:

    “I would say the generation of the ‘50s and ‘60s took the plunge into politics without ever calculating the costs of the risk and the benefits to be gained. They were driven by ideology. Today’s generation has no culture and averse to taking political risk. Really, an interest in politics is very necessary for the future. But I cannot blame the present generations, because they see the heavy-handed response by the government to dissenting views, even though they know that these matters involve their daily lives.

    So the result is that we have produced a younger generation who are meek and therefore very calculating. They are less independent-thinking and lack in initiative. It does not bode well for the emergence of future leaders in politics and business. Robots and computers can be programmed or if you like, can be trained. But the trouble, of course, is that computers lack soul and what we need in Singapore is soul. Because it is soul that makes society.”

    It would have been interesting to read the memoirs of Dr Toh - as he is known to be an honest person, possessing a high standard of dignity, and respect towards even his political opponents.

    His pride has probably prevented him to bow to the pressures of having to dilute or modify the narration his own total experiences during his long tenure in politics. With his DPM position in the Cabinet and a Chairman of the PAP, he is standing shoulder to shoulder to LKY in terms of responsibility even though he is shorter than LKY by at least a quarter.

    It was Toh who held the cabinet together when Singapore was booted out of Malaysia, and LKY submitted his resignation paper to the Chairman; who together with Dr Goh Keng Swee, Mr S Rajaratnam, and other senior party members decided to reject LKY's letter.

    Dr Toh's departure from politics was an unhappy affair, when he was asked to stepped aside under a 'dubious' program of self-renewal, which saw him giving up his Cabinet position, and soon after also his Chairmanship of the Party - and only to become a Back Bencher in Parliament. His reservations and instinct towards the 'self-renewal' program that was on LKY's political agenda created sharp division in the Party, with bits and pieces spilling out into the press. It now seems that Dr Toh's concern has seen its fruition with LKY's continued tenure into the foreseeable future, when the entire first generation MPs are all long gone.

    It would have been insightful if Dr Toh had been able to put his memoirs into print, and allow generations to learn of his experiences and also the events during those critical periods towards Self-Government, then into Malaysia, the period when Singapore was unceremoniously booted out of Malaysia, the struggles in finding a way into the future for Singapore with the many internal Cabinet debates on dubious policies, and the manner that the Government is runned; and more importantly his views to his working relationship with LKY.

    Dr Toh is known to be a man with a high standard of integrity and dignity, with respects towards others even his most raucous political opponents.

    Will a man that has such strong values bow to the dictates imposed on him by his Oath to protect States Secrets that was taken during his term as DPM in cabinet, and with no time limit to such an Oath ? Will he write his memoirs with one hand tied behind his back, and the truth to be modified to suit secrecy laws ?

    Can Singapore hope for its future generations to learn anything from the past, when truth has to be suppressed by Secrecy Laws that has no time bar ?

    In a slight modification to the question posed by Senior Civil Servant - Mr Ngiam Tong Dow : ''Is the interest of the Ruling Political Party bigger than that of Singapore and Singaporeans ?"
