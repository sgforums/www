    Originally posted by fairlady_xoxo:
    They are considered Private Uni....only 3 Local Unis (Allow you to use CPF to pay fees)

    NUS, NTU, SMU
    UniSIM has university status like the other public-funded universities. In the main, UniSIM caters to the adult working population rather than full-time students who are fresh school-leavers.

    The authorities and many politicians forgot this, and thus tend to refer to another fourth university.
    There is room for more universities whether public or private. The most important thing is not the number but the quality.

    If you go to OZ, you find that every states have several universities - both state and non-state. By state, its mean the 'official' and fully state-funded university. Each state usually has one or maybe more in the more-populated states. Take Western Australia - its entire population is less than SG. But it has 5 universities, only one is considered state - UWA.
    Only the other hand, Victoria has 10 universities, population same as SG, and only a few are considered state universities.
    So, you see why it is easy to get into an Aussie university! There are so many and the population of OZ is small. In WA, foreign students even out-numbered local ones!
