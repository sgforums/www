  • yoohoo
    8 Apr 00, 21:54
    NUS came up with the new 5-point system during last semester (1st Semester of 1999/2000). Some people were better off while others were unaffected.

    The main purpose of this new system is suppose to let students "do better". Eg: In the past, if you get an "F" grade (zero point), u need to have an "A" grade (4 points) to average out to give a "C" grade (2 points).

    However with this new system, u are required to have only a "B+" grade (4 points) to get the same average of a "C" grade (2 points).

    I observed that those who had done relatively not so well will see an improvement over their CAP score while whose who had done well will not be affected as much as the former.

    So, what is your opinon on the new 5-point system?

    [PS: BTW, only the current 2nd year students (98/99 intake) are affected. This is because the 1st year students received their results for the 1st time. The 3rd year students are still adopting the old 4-point system]

    there is no gain for year 2s when it comes to honours. It fact there is a loss. The upgrading of grades and pts does not compensate the upgrading of criteria for honours offering. This is especially so for those who qualify for honours slightly above the cutoff mark under the old system. What it means is that those who qualify under the old system fail to qualify under the new. One reason for this is that not all grades are being upgraded. Thr lower grades are but not the upper grades. The main benificiaries are those who did not do too well (eg ave of Cs). Those who did average of Bs and above only benefit from a improvement in the outlook of the transcript for some grades but are worse off for the borderline honours cases as they may not qualify.
