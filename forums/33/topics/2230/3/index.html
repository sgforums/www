    hi yohoo,

    CAPM is one model of asset pricing, and what some research tries to do is to find if there's a better model that will model stock returns.

    Spreads, straddles etc are hedging strategies using combinations of stocks and options. Options are another area for finance research. But what I was referring to was more like using momentum strategies (buying past winner stocks i.e. stocks which had made good returns and selling past losers), contrarian strategies (buying past losers and selling past winners), etc.

    January effect is simply the phenomenon whereby you can make greater profits in January compared to other months of the year. One explanation for this (in the case for countries with capital gains tax) is because people sell off their stocks which have made losses in December to minimise their tax liabilities, and then buy them back later in Jan.
