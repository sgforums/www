    By the way, regarding the point about being "condemned" bcoz of poor English results, I have a few comments of my own.

    At 1st, I also thought that English should not be the stumbling block towards higher education. Many of my friends who had excellent grades for their 'O' level opted to go poly instead of JC cited their mediocre command of English as the reason. Many of them did not do badly for their 'O' level English, it is the GP in JC which frightens them.

    Anyway, that was a few years ago. Now, my opinions have changed. Through these years, I had realised that English is really important in the current society structure. During my University years, I needed to do up countless reports & presentations. As my English wasn't that fantastic, hence I really had a difficult time finding the appropriate phrases & vocabulary to express myself. If you have fantastic content & analysis, but without a proper command of English to express all these, your report may be regarded as just an average piece of work. However, if you can only come up with a a sufficiently good analysis, but you excel in the wording & organisation of your report, good grades are of high possibility. What I trying to put across here is not that content is unimportant, but that a strong command of English is very necessary to put forth whatever points you want to make.

    So actually, using English to "screen" students during the 'O' & 'A' levels is to make sure that those who manage to go up the academic ranks have the adequate English proficiency to deal with the future academic challenges. Nonetheless, there are exceptions whose English is not particularly strong but are still able to excel in whatever they do. But, I guess this only applies to those who are considered experts in their fields of speciality, & not to the average man-on-the-street.

    My 1-cent worth...
