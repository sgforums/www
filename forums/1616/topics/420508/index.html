    Dogs Really Love Home Cooked Cooking – Make Their Choice Foods At Home

     

    16.01.2011 | Author: Ben Jauntery

     

    In the light of increasing evidence against the harmful nature of commercial dog food, many owners are now pondering over the dilemma of what is best for their pet. In this scenario, they are faced with two choices either to turn to home cooked dog meals or upgrade to costlier dog foods.

    Compared with what we humans consume, dog food is not much distinct. It also has all the nutrients that our body needs. It is also a balanced diet and it also is seasoned for flavor. In fact a complete analysis of dog foods reveal that it is 2/5th parts protein and remaining equal parts of carbohydrate and vegetables. However, take a look at cheap dog foods they come with grain in them and are packed with carbohydrates or vegetables.

    In the department of meat, you can use any meat as all are high in protein. However for easy digestibility try turkey meat. A meal of turkey meat, rice and carrots is a perfectly balanced and healthy diet for your dog. Other meats that are equally good are ground beef and brown rice for a high carbohydrate and protein diet.

    To make these doggie delights one need not use fresh veggies, instead the frozen type shall suffice. Dice them up, boil them well and season them with a little salt and garlic. This should serve as the base to add in the meat and carbohydrate parts. Dogs generally do not have a preference over the type of carbohydrate they consume, however, depending on choices potatoes or rice can be used.

    A commonly held misconception is that dog food cooking is tough. Truth is that it is as easy as making food for yourself. Best of all you can cook in bulk for a week and refrigerate it so that your dog can enjoy the benefits of homemade food for the entire week.

    With dogs that are attuned to commercial foods, it may take at least a week to switch them over to home cooked food. Otherwise, with a sudden change, your dog may not accept the food and in worst cases it may suffer from bouts of diarrhea. By the week?s end, your dog will be eating home food without any qualms.

    Home food is also very healthy for your dog. A dog completely on a homemade diet is prone to lesser allergies as this food has all natural proteins unlike the commercial varieties that are high on artificial proteins which, is the biggest contributor to dog allergies.

    Snack items for your pet can also be prepared right at home using the large database of dog food recipes found on the World Wide Web listings. Now you too can give your dog a good snack at anytime of the day.
